# Rocco Raccoon’s Bubble Beat

Rocco was a musical raccoon with a stripey tail and bright, curious eyes. He loved to make music everywhere he went. Today, he padded into the bathroom with his little drumstick—really a soft, safe wooden spoon. The bathroom smelled like bubble soap and minty toothpaste. The tiles were cool on his feet, and the mirror was shiny like a lake.

“Tap-tap-tap!” Rocco tapped the spoon on the rim of the empty bathtub.

Drip. Drip. The faucet made a lazy beat. “I can join that beat,” Rocco whispered. “Drip, drip, tap! Drip, drip, tap!”

His sister, Ruby Raccoon, stepped in with a towel. “I need to wash my paws,” she said. She turned on the water. Whoosh! The rush of water made a loud sound.

Rocco’s ears perked. “Whoosh is a big sound,” he said. He tapped faster. Tap-tap-tap-tap!

Ruby jumped. “Too loud, Rocco,” she said, rubbing her ears. “Please stop. I need some quiet.”

Rocco felt a hot bubble of anger puff in his chest. “But I want to play my song!” he said. His tail flicked. Tap! The spoon bonked the soap dish by mistake. Clink! The soap dish slid, and a bar of soap thumped to the floor.

Ruby stepped back. “Hey! Watch where you tap. I need space,” she said, holding up a paw. “Personal space.”

Rocco froze and hugged his spoon. He took a slow breath. He looked at Ruby’s face. Her eyebrows were scrunched. The bathroom felt too loud and messy.

Rocco remembered what Mama Raccoon taught them: “When you feel mad, stop and breathe. Use gentle words. Look with your eyes. Listen with your ears.”

He puffed out a long breath. “Hooo,” he said. The warm anger bubble got smaller. He looked around. The floor was a little slippery from the soap. The tub rim was crowded with toys. Ruby needed space at the sink.

“I’m sorry, Ruby,” Rocco said. “I didn’t mean to scare you. I will give you space.” He picked up the soap and put it back, nice and neat. He stepped away from the sink.

Ruby’s face softened. “Thanks, Rocco,” she said. “I want to hear your music. Can we take turns?”

“Take turns?” Rocco tilted his head. “How?”

Ruby pointed. “First, I wash my paws. Then you play a song. Then I brush my teeth. Then you play again. We can make a bathroom band, but in turns, and with gentle sounds.”

Rocco smiled. “Okay! We can keep a beat together.”

Ruby began. Squish-scrub, squish-scrub. The soap made bubbles that popped like tiny kisses. “Squish-scrub, squish-scrub,” she hummed.

Rocco listened. He counted softly. “One, two, three, four.” He watched her elbows and paws so he would not bump her. He stood on a bath mat that felt soft like a cloud. He kept his spoon still, hugging it to his chest. He was practicing coordination—eyes looking, ears listening, body waiting.

“All clean!” said Ruby. She shook her paws. Shake-shake. “Your turn to play.”

Rocco stepped to the tub. He made a gentle beat on the rim. Tap… tap… tippity-tap. He matched the faucet drip. Drip… tap… drip… tap. He swayed his tail in time. He kept his spoon away from the soap dish. He kept his feet far from Ruby’s toes. He was moving with care. He was coordinating his body.

Ruby smiled and swayed with the beat. “Nice and soft. I like it.”

“My turn,” Ruby said after a little while. She lifted her toothbrush. Scritch-scritch, scritch-scritch. Rocco listened again. He counted. “One, two, three, four.” He noticed her cheeks puff when she rinsed. Swish-swish.

Rocco whispered, “After your turn, I can add the towel beat.” He pointed to the towel rail.

Ruby nodded. “Yes, but only soft touches.”

When Ruby was done, she stepped back and said, “Your turn!”

Rocco tapped the towel rail very gently with his finger, not the spoon. Ting… ting… ting. The sound was tiny and sweet. He added a soft pat on the bath mat with his foot. Pat… pat. He moved slow and careful, like a conductor. He kept his tail curled close so it did not knock anything. He felt proud. He was using his whole body to keep everyone safe and happy.

Mama Raccoon peeked in. “I hear a bathroom band,” she said, smiling. “What good coordination! You are looking, listening, and moving together. And I see you giving space and taking turns.”

Rocco’s chest felt warm and light. “I got mad before,” he said. “But I stopped, I breathed, and I used gentle words.”

Ruby nodded. “We made a plan. We took turns. Rocco kept my space safe.”

Mama clapped softly. Clap, clap. “That is wonderful anger care and boundary care,” she said. “And the music is lovely.”

Rocco tapped one last gentle beat. Tap… drip… ting… pat. He bowed with his spoon across his tummy.

Ruby giggled and bowed too. “Bathroom band is done.”

Rocco put the spoon away. He wiped a little splash from the floor. He looked at Ruby. “Thank you for the plan,” he said.

“Thank you for listening,” Ruby said. They bumped paws softly—tap.

Rocco whispered, “When we look, listen, and move with care, we can play together anywhere.”

The End.
