# Buzzy Beaver Builds a Bright Machine

In a cozy house on a quiet street, there was a basement lab. It smelled a little like clean soap and fresh wood. Soft lights hummed. Buttons blinked in rainbow colors. In the middle of the room stood Buzzy, a busy beaver with bright eyes and a wide, helpful tail.

Buzzy wore tiny goggles and a neat blue apron. “Today is a big day,” Buzzy said. “We will make the Color Smile Machine! It will paint happy pictures for everyone.”

Buzzy had a plan on a big paper. The paper crinkled when Buzzy touched it. “Step 1,” Buzzy read, tapping the paper. “Build the frame. Step 2. Mix safe, bright paints. Step 3. Test it with a smile.”

Buzzy hurried to lift a long, shiny pipe. “Oof! Heavy,” Buzzy puffed. The pipe slipped and clanged on the floor. “That was loud,” Buzzy whispered, ears twitching.

From a shelf, a small light blinked red. Buzzy’s tail thumped softly. “I want to do it all by myself,” Buzzy said. “I am busy. I can do it.”

Buzzy tried again. The pipe wobbled. The jars of paint rattled. Blue paint almost tipped.

A gentle voice floated from the stairs. “Need a paw?” It was Dot, a kind little duck in a polka-dot scarf. She hopped down, webbed feet going pat-pat on the steps.

“We can help too!” chirped Pip and Pop, two tiny mice with paint-splattered whiskers. They squeaked and waved little brushes.

Buzzy’s cheeks felt warm. “I wanted to do it alone. But… it is very hard.” Buzzy took a slow breath. “Yes, please. I need help.”

Dot smiled. “Teamwork time!” she quacked.

First, they stretched. “Reach up high,” said Dot, lifting her wings. “Roll your shoulders,” said Buzzy, rolling gently. “Wiggle, wiggle,” squeaked the mice, giggling. They sipped cool water. “Glug-glug,” sang Pip. “Water helps us work,” Pop nodded.

“Healthy bodies, happy helpers,” said Buzzy. “Now, jobs for everyone.”

Buzzy tapped the plan. “Dot, can you hold the pipe steady?” Dot tucked it under her wing. “Steady as a rock,” she said.

“Pip and Pop, can you bring the little screws?” Buzzy asked. “Yes!” piped Pip. “Screws coming!” Pop squeaked. They scampered with a tiny box that jingled softly.

Buzzy turned a smooth wrench. Click, click, click. “This feels good,” Buzzy said. “We are doing it together.”

They mixed paints in clear bowls. The paints swirled—red like a shiny apple, blue like a cool lake, yellow like warm sunshine. “It smells like sweet berries,” Dot said, sniffing the red. “And like lemons!” said Pip, tapping the yellow.

Buzzy read labels out loud. “Safe paint. Washable. Non-toxic.” Buzzy smiled. “Good for friends. Good for paws.”

“Snack break?” Pop asked. Buzzy nodded. They washed hands with bubbly soap. Swish, swish. They ate crunchy carrot sticks and crisp apple slices. “Crunch!” said Dot. “Munch!” said Pip. “Yum and healthy,” Buzzy said, tail thumping happily.

Then came the switch. The big, friendly button glowed green. “Are we ready?” Buzzy asked. Feet shuffled. Tails swished. Hearts felt bouncy.

“We’re ready!” everyone cheered.

Buzzy pressed the button. Whirr… whirr… clunk.

The machine sputtered and painted a tiny dot. Then it stopped. The light blinked yellow.

“Oh no,” said Buzzy. Buzzy’s ears drooped. “Did I make a mistake?”

Dot looked at the plan. “Let’s not give up,” she said softly. “We can learn.”

“Check the steps,” said Pip.

“Look with your senses,” said Pop. “See, hear, smell, touch gently.”

Buzzy listened. Whirr… clunk. “I hear a rattle,” Buzzy said. Buzzy looked. A hose was loose and wiggly. Buzzy touched it carefully. “Loose,” Buzzy said. “We can fix this.”

Dot held the hose. Pip and Pop fetched a clip. Click! Buzzy tightened it. They tried again.

Buzzy pressed the button. Whirr… whirr… swish!

Out came a soft spray of colors. The machine painted a big smile on a blank paper. Red, blue, yellow, and green danced together. It looked like a rainbow hug.

“We did it!” Dot quacked.

“We learned and fixed it,” squeaked the mice.

Buzzy’s eyes shone. “We did it together,” Buzzy said. “Teamwork made it work.”

They made art for the wall—sunny suns, happy trees, and friendly fish. The basement lab smelled like clean water and a little like lemons. Music played softly from a tiny radio, and their feet tapped along.

When they were done, they cleaned up. Brushes went in a cup. Bowls washed with suds. The floor got a gentle sweep. “Clean space, clear mind,” Buzzy said. “Healthy habit!”

They stretched again and sipped more water. “Ahhh,” said Dot.

Buzzy hung the big plan on a hook. Buzzy looked at the team—Dot, Pip, and Pop. “Thank you,” Buzzy said. “Asking for help was smart. Listening and sharing made us strong. Learning made us better. Healthy habits kept us going.”

Dot nodded. “Teamwork is a super tool.”

Pip giggled. “Teamwork makes bright art!”

Pop clapped. “Teamwork makes big smiles!”

Buzzy patted the Color Smile Machine. The light glowed green, soft and calm. “I started busy,” Buzzy said, “and I finished happy—with friends.”

The End.
