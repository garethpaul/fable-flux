# Bubbles of Many Colors

Pip was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a wiggly waddle. One sunny morning, Pip slid across the bathroom tiles. The tiles felt cool and smooth under his little feet. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. Plink, plink, plink went the water on the shiny sink. The bathroom smelled like minty soap and warm towels.

“Today,” Pip said, “I will practice brushing my beak and washing my flippers!” He loved practice. Practice made him better, and better felt great.

On the counter stood a tall mirror. Pip waved. “Hello, Pip!” he giggled. The mirror Pip waved back, of course.

Pip turned the faucet. Swish! Water rushed and sparkled. He squeezed the soap. Squeeeak! Bubbles puffed out like little white clouds. “Hello, bubbles!” he laughed. He dipped his flippers in the water—splash! It felt tickly and warm.

Then Pip had a big idea. “Let’s have an imagination parade!” he said. “All friends welcome!”

He blinked at the soap bottle and the brush cup. With a giggle and a wiggle, Pip imagined them as friends. Pop! In his mind, the blue soap bottle became Bluey Bubble, tall and smooth. Pop! The green toothbrush became Greeny Brush, short and bendy. Pop! The soft towel became Tulla Towel, fluffy and gentle. Pop! Even the rubber duck became Dax Duck, squeaky and bright. They were all different. Different sizes, different shapes, different sounds.

“Welcome, friends!” Pip said. “We will practice washing and brushing together.”

Bluey Bubble spoke in a deep, calm voice. “I am strong and soapy.”

Greeny Brush wiggled. “I am small, but I can clean tiny spots!”

Tulla Towel whispered softly. “I am warm and cuddly.”

Dax Duck squeaked, “I am squeaky and fun!”

Pip clapped his flippers. “We are all different. Different is good! Let’s listen to each other and help.”

Pip dipped Greeny Brush into a tiny cup. “I am practicing brushing my beak,” Pip said. He brushed gently. “Top, bottom, side, side.” Scritch, scritch, scritch. The minty taste was tingly. Pip wrinkled his beak. “Mint is zippy!”

Greeny Brush hummed, “Small strokes, Pip. Gentle and slow.”

“I am listening,” Pip said. He went slow. Scritch, scritch, pause. “This feels better!”

Bluey Bubble bubbled. “Now wash flippers.” Pip squirted one drop. Plop! “Just a little,” Bluey Bubble said. “We can share.”

Pip rubbed his flippers. Swish, swash, swoosh. Bubbles grew big and round. They were like tiny moons. Pip held one close. “You look like a pearl!” The bubble popped—pop!—and he giggled.

Dax Duck bobbed in the sink. “Squeak, squeak! Let’s splash!” he cried.

Pip started to splash big splashes. Sploosh! Splash! Water sprinkled the mirror. Water sprinkled Tulla Towel. Water sprinkled the floor. The bathroom sounded like rain.

Tulla Towel drooped. “Oh dear,” she whispered. “I am wet and chilly.”

Pip blinked. “Oh,” he said softly. He looked at his friends. Bluey Bubble was slippery. Greeny Brush was tipped over. Dax Duck was bumping the drain.

“I didn’t listen,” Pip said. His tummy felt squiggly. He didn’t like that feeling. “I got too splashy. I need to make amends.”

Pip took a deep breath. He held Tulla Towel gently. “I’m sorry, Tulla. I will help you dry.” He patted the counter. Pat, pat, pat. He wiped the mirror. Wipe, wipe. He put Greeny Brush back in the cup. Tap. He set Bluey Bubble upright. Stand tall. He moved Dax Duck to a safe spot. Plop.

Tulla Towel warmed up. “Thank you, Pip. That was kind.”

Bluey Bubble bubbled softly. “Saying sorry is brave.”

Greeny Brush chimed, “And practicing gentle is smart.”

Dax Duck squeaked, “We can still have fun!”

Pip smiled. His tummy felt smooth again. “I will practice listening,” he said. “When a friend says stop, I stop. When a friend needs help, I help. We are different, but we all belong.”

He turned to the mirror. He saw a penguin with a minty-clean beak, shiny flippers, and kind eyes. He saw his friends—tall, small, fluffy, squeaky. All different. All special. All together.

“Now,” Pip said, “let’s try a quiet splash. Plip.” He tapped the water. “Plip.” Dax Duck plipped too. “Plip.” The sink made tiny ripples that looked like rings on a pond.

Bluey Bubble sang in a slow bubble voice, “Different colors, different shapes, different sounds. We learn together.”

Pip nodded. “I like our differences,” he said. “They make our bathroom team strong!”

They made a little plan:
- Pip would practice gentle brushing every day.
- He would listen to his friends’ voices.
- He would share the soap and the space.
- If he made a mistake, he would make amends.

The bathroom felt cozy now. It smelled minty and warm again. The faucet went drip, drip, drip, like a sleepy drum.

Pip wrapped himself in Tulla Towel. The towel hugged him like a cloud. “Thank you, Tulla,” he whispered.

Tulla Towel murmured, “You’re welcome, Pip.”

Pip waved at the mirror. “See you next time, friends. Today we learned: Different is wonderful. Practice helps us grow. Listening keeps us kind. Making amends fixes mistakes.”

His friends shimmered in the shiny sink. Dax Duck gave one last squeak. Bluey Bubble winked. Greeny Brush stood tall and proud.

Pip waddled out, clean and happy. His heart felt big and bright.

The End.
