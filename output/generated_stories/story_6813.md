# Panda Poppy on the Rooftop

Poppy was a patient panda with soft, fuzzy fur. She liked to sit on the sunny rooftop garden. The rooftop smelled like fresh leaves and sweet flowers. Bees hummed. The wind whispered, whoosh, whoosh. Poppy loved to breathe in, slow and calm. She would pat her round belly and smile.

One bright morning, Poppy heard a tiny squeak. “Help!” squeaked a little voice.

Poppy turned her round ears. She listened. She heard the squeak again, near the tall planter. “I am coming,” Poppy said softly. “I will take my time.”

She waddled over, paws going tap-tap on the warm tiles. There, between two flower pots, was a little red kite. It was stuck under a wiggly rope. A squirrel named Pip stood nearby, tail twitching. His eyes were big and wet.

“My kite!” Pip cried. “I tried to pull it, but I can’t. I’m too small.”

Poppy sat down, calm and steady. “We can help your kite,” she said. “Let’s think.” She took a deep breath. “We will be patient.”

Pip sniffled. “I am scared of heights,” he whispered, looking over the edge. Far below, cars sounded like tiny “beep-beep” toys. The rooftop felt very high.

Poppy nodded. “I feel a little wobbly too,” she said. “Heights can be scary. But we can be brave together. Being brave means trying, even when we feel scared.”

Pip rubbed his nose. “Do you think I can help?”

“Yes,” Poppy said. “I believe in you. We can do this step by step.”

Poppy touched the rope. It was rough under her paw. She tried to lift it, but it didn’t move. “Hmm,” she hummed. “I need a plan. I will use the watering can to hold the rope up.”

She waddled to the watering can. It was cool and shiny. Water splashed, plip-plop, as she moved it. “Pip, can you hold the handle?” Poppy asked.

Pip stood tall. His small paws shook, but he held the handle. “I will try,” he said.

Poppy smiled. “Good trying. You are brave.” She slid the spout under the rope. “On three,” she said. “One… two… three!”

They lifted. The rope rose a little. The kite wriggled but didn’t come free.

Pip’s ears drooped. “It’s not working,” he said.

Poppy took another slow breath. “It is okay. We will try again in a new way. We can learn. We can wait. We can be patient and keep trying.”

Pip’s tail stopped twitching. He nodded. “Okay.”

Poppy looked around. She saw a soft cushion near the rooftop chair. “We can make a safe spot,” she said. “If the kite pops out fast, it can land on the cushion.”

They slid the cushion close to the planter. The fabric felt squishy and warm in the sun. Poppy’s heart felt warm too. She was using her calm, and it felt good.

“Now,” Poppy said, “I will lift the rope again. Pip, you can gently pull the kite string.”

Pip licked his lips. “I can do gentle,” he said to himself. He held the string. His paws felt the smooth line. He pulled, soft and slow.

Poppy lifted. Pip pulled. The rope moved higher. The kite wiggled, wiggle, wiggle—and then—pop! The kite slid free and landed on the cushion with a soft thump.

“We did it!” Pip squeaked. He jumped and clapped his paws. His eyes shone like little stars.

Poppy laughed, a happy rumble. “Yes, we did it together. You were brave and careful.”

Pip hugged his kite. “I was scared,” he said, “but I still helped. I can be brave.”

Poppy nodded. “You can. I was unsure too, but I trusted myself. I knew we could try, wait, and try again.”

A tiny voice chirped from the other side of the rooftop. A small bird flapped its wings. Its foot was tangled in a thread by the garden fence. “Cheep-cheep!”

Poppy and Pip hurried over. The bird’s feathers felt soft like clouds. The thread was thin and tight.

Poppy spoke gently. “Little bird, we will help.” She looked at Pip. “We learned a lot. Let’s use our plan again.”

Pip stood taller. “We can make it safe. We can be patient.”

They put the cushion under the bird. Poppy used two fingers to hold the thread. Pip used tiny scissors from the garden box. Snip-snip! The thread came loose.

The bird hopped, then lifted off. Its wings whooshed the air, brushing Poppy’s nose. “Cheep! Thank you!” it sang as it flew into the bright, blue sky.

Pip grinned. “We helped twice!”

Poppy placed a paw on her chest. “I feel proud,” she said. “I trusted myself. Patience and courage helped me help others. When we believe in ourselves and stay calm, we can do big things.”

Pip nodded. “I believe in me too.”

They sat on the rooftop and shared a juicy apple. Crunch, crunch. The wind was gentle. The sun was warm. The kite fluttered happily in the air, red and bright against the sky.

Poppy smiled at Pip. “Remember,” she said, “you are brave. You can help. You can try. You can be patient with yourself.”

Pip smiled back. “And I can say, ‘I can do it.’”

“Yes,” said Poppy. “You can do it.”

The End.
