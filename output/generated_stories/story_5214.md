# The Quiet Clicks of Milo Mouse

Milo was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a cozy home office under a big wooden desk. The desk smelled like pencils and paper. The keyboard made tiny click-click sounds, and the computer screen gave off a warm, gentle glow.

Each morning, Milo squeaked, “Good morning, Desk! Good morning, Chair! Good morning, click-click keys!” He liked the calm and the quiet. He liked the soft hum of the computer, like a purr. It made him feel safe and still.

One busy day, the home office was extra noisy. The printer chugged. The phone buzzed. Papers rustled. A cup tapped the saucer. Tap, tap! Buzz, buzz! Chug, chug! Milo’s whiskers twitched. His small ears felt full of sounds.

“Oh my,” said Milo. “So many noises! My tummy feels jiggly.”

He took a slow breath in. He let a slow breath out. He remembered something kind: “When I feel wiggly, I can be calm,” Milo told himself. “I can use my words. I can be honest.”

Milo peeked out and saw his friend, Dot the desk lamp. Dot glowed a friendly yellow. “Hello, Milo,” said Dot. “You look worried.”

Milo nodded. “I feel busy in my head,” he said. “Everything is loud. I want to be calm.”

Dot dimmed her light to a softer glow. “Let’s try a calm game,” she said. “Smell a flower, blow a cloud.”

Milo smiled. He closed his eyes. He sniffed in slowly. He blew out gently. Sniff in. Blow out. His body felt softer, like a warm blanket. He opened his eyes and said, “Thank you, Dot.”

Then, a tiny thump-thump! came from the shelf. It was Pip the paper clip, bouncing near a stack of papers. “Help! I slipped!” Pip squeaked.

Milo scampered up a book, pat-pat, pat-pat. He reached Pip and held him steady with his little paws. “I’ve got you,” said Milo. “Let’s be calm and safe.”

Pip wiggled and giggled. “Thank you! I was scared. I made a mess too.” A few papers had slid to the floor like quiet snow.

Milo looked at the papers. He felt a pinch in his tummy. “I touched the papers too,” he said. “I was trying to help, but I made some fall.” He took a breath. “I want to tell the truth.”

Just then, the door opened, and kind footsteps came in. It was the Person who worked at the desk. The Person picked up the papers and smiled. “What a little tumble,” they said in a gentle voice.

Milo stood up tall and squeaked, “I am sorry. I bumped the papers when I helped Pip. I wanted to be helpful, but I made a mess.”

The Person knelt down. “Thank you for being honest, little friend,” they said. “Honesty helps me understand. We can fix it together.”

Milo’s heart felt warm. His whiskers wiggled with relief. “Can we make a plan?” he asked. “I want the office to feel calm again.”

“Yes,” said the Person. “Let’s use calm voices and kind steps. We can put papers in a neat stack. We can move the cup away from the edge. We can set the phone to quiet.”

Together they worked in a gentle, quiet way. The papers went swish-swish into a tidy pile. The cup slid clink to the middle. The phone made one last buzz, then a soft hush.

Milo listened. The room sounded peaceful again. Click-click went the keys. Hummmm went the computer. Dot shone with a warm, low light. Pip rested in a small dish, snug as a bug.

Milo felt proud. “We did it,” he said. “We talked. We told the truth. We were calm.”

The Person smiled. “You were brave and kind, Milo. You used calm breaths and calm words. You asked for help. That is smart.”

Milo’s tummy felt steady now. He curled up on a little square of soft felt that smelled like clean paper. He told a short story to Pip and Dot, in a gentle voice.

“Once there was a mouse,” Milo began, “who felt busy in his head. He smelled a flower. He blew a cloud. He told the truth. He asked for help. And the office became calm again.”

Pip clinked happily. Dot glowed. The Person listened with a quiet smile.

Milo finished, “The end.”

Dot whispered, “That was a good story.”

Milo nodded. “Calm helps me think. Talking helps friends understand. Telling the truth helps hearts feel light.” He yawned a tiny yawn. “I can be calm, I can communicate, and I can be honest.”

The office hummed like a lullaby. Milo closed his eyes, warm and cozy. He felt safe, kind, and calm.

And that was the quiet, happy end of this busy day.

The End.
