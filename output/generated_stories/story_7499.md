# Buzzy Basement Cleanup with Cubby

Cubby the bear cub loved to help. He had soft brown fur, bright eyes, and a cheerful little smile. One rainy afternoon, Cubby clomped down the wooden steps to the busy basement with his mama. The basement hummed and buzzed. The washing machine swished, swish-swish. The dryer hummed, hmmmmm. Boxes stacked high smelled like old paper and crayons. There were shelves with tools, and a big table with art supplies.

“Mama, it is noisy and busy down here,” Cubby said, wiggling his round ears.

“It is,” Mama Bear agreed. “But we have a problem to solve. Our recycling is all mixed up with trash. We need to sort it out.”

Cubby looked around. There were crinkly plastic bottles rolled under a chair. Glass jars clinked together in a box. Paper and cardboard were in a pile on the floor. Banana peels and apple cores sat in a little green pail, and a stinky smell made Cubby wrinkle his nose.

“Pee-yew!” Cubby giggled. “That smells like old bananas.”

“That’s our compost,” Mama Bear said. “Food scraps go there to make dirt. But look—everything else is mixed together. Can you help me sort it all?”

Cubby puffed his chest. “Yes! I can help.”

Mama Bear set out four big bins. One was blue, one was green, one was yellow, and one was brown. She stuck big picture labels on them: a bottle for glass, a bottle for plastic, a paper for paper, and a leaf for compost.

Cubby tapped each bin with his paw. “Blue is glass. Green is plastic. Yellow is paper. Brown is compost.”

“Great memory,” Mama said. “Now, let’s sort.”

Cubby picked up a clear bottle. “This is plastic. It feels light and a little crinkly.” He squeezed it. Crinkle-crinkle! He dropped it into the green bin. Plop!

He found a shiny jar. “This is glass. It’s smooth and heavy.” He dropped it into the blue bin. Clink!

He grabbed a cereal box. “This is paper. It is flat and a little rough.” He slid it into the yellow bin. Swish!

He held up a banana peel with two claws. “This is food. It is slippery and smelly.” He put it in the brown bin. Squish!

“Good sorting, Cubby,” Mama said. “When we recycle, we care for our Earth. We make less trash. We save trees, water, and energy.”

Cubby’s eyes grew wide. “We are helpers for Earth?”

“Yes,” Mama smiled. “We are Earth helpers.”

Cubby felt proud. He wanted to be the best helper. He looked under the table. He saw a shiny can rolling in dust. He picked it up and put it in the green bin.

“Wait, Cubby,” Mama said gently. “That can is metal. It goes in the blue bin with glass here, because our town recycles metal with glass.”

Cubby’s ears drooped. “Oh no. I made a mistake.”

Mama rubbed his shoulder. “Mistakes help us learn. Try again.”

Cubby lifted the can. It felt cool and hard. He listened. Tap-tap. “Metal goes with glass in our town,” he said. He put the can in the blue bin. Clink!

He smiled again. “I can learn. I can try.”

They kept sorting. The dryer hummed, hmmmmm, and warm air puffed like a soft hug. The washing machine swished, swish-swish, like waves. Cubby liked the sounds.

“Listen, Mama,” he said. “The basement sings while we work!”

Mama laughed. “It does.”

Cubby found a sticky yogurt cup. “Plastic,” he said, but he made a face. “It is gooey.”

Mama gave him a cloth. “Let’s rinse sticky things, so they can be recycled.”

They washed the cup together with warm water. The soap smelled like lemons. Cubby sniffed. “It smells bright!” He shook the cup. Drip-drip. Then he put it into the green bin. Plop!

He found a sparkly picture from old wrapping paper. “Paper!” he cheered. But Mama shook her head kindly. “This paper has glitter. Glitter can make it hard to recycle. Let’s save it for art.”

Cubby placed it on the art table. “Art paper,” he said. “We can make a new card!”

They finished the piles, and the bins stood full and neat. Blue glowed with jars and cans. Green sat snug with clean plastic. Yellow was full of paper and cardboard. Brown held peels and cores, ready to turn into soil.

Cubby clapped. “We did it! The busy basement is tidy.”

Mama looked around. The shelves were clear. The floor felt smooth under their paws. The air smelled fresh now, not stinky. “We solved the problem,” she said. “You helped so much.”

Cubby puffed his chest again. “I am an Earth helper.”

Mama nodded. “Yes. And we learned a sorting rhyme. Say it with me.”

Together they chanted:
“Glass and metal, clink clink blue.
Plastic light goes green, that’s true.
Paper, cardboard, yellow bright.
Scraps in brown, out of sight!”

Cubby giggled and danced. His paws thumped softly on the floor. Thump-thump. “I will remember our rhyme.”

Mama pointed to a little poster they made with pictures and colors. “We’ll keep this here in the basement. We can teach our friends, too.”

Cubby imagined showing his friends. He felt warm and happy. “I can teach. I can care. I can recycle!”

Mama gave him a hug. “That is the lesson. When we sort and recycle, we take care of our home and our Earth.”

Cubby looked at the bins one more time. They were neat and just right. He whispered, “Thank you, Earth.”

The rainy day ended, and the basement felt calm. The hums were softer now. Cubby and Mama climbed the steps, hand in paw, ready for a cozy snack.

The End.
