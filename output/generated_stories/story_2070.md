# Leo's Big Night Adventure

Leo the little lion cub sat on the soft guest room bed. His golden fur was fluffy and warm. Tonight was his first time sleeping in Grandma's guest room all by himself. The room smelled like lavender and felt cozy.

But Leo felt worried. The room was darker than his own bedroom at home. Strange shadows danced on the walls from the tree outside the window.

"I don't know if I can do this," Leo whispered to himself. His little paws clutched the blue blanket.

Then Leo heard a funny noise. "Drip, drip, drip." It came from somewhere in the room. Leo's ears perked up. His heart beat a little faster.

"What should I do?" Leo thought. "Should I call for Grandma? Or should I be brave and find out what it is?"

Leo took a deep breath. "I am a brave lion cub," he said out loud. "I can figure this out myself!"

Leo climbed down from the big bed. His paws touched the soft carpet. Step by step, he followed the dripping sound. It led him to the corner of the room.

There was a small plant in a pretty pot. Water was dripping from its leaves onto a plate below. "Oh!" said Leo. "It's just the plant! Grandma must have watered it today."

Leo felt proud of himself. He had solved the mystery all on his own! But then he heard another sound. "Scratch, scratch, scratch."

This time, Leo wasn't scared. "I can find out what this is too," he said with confidence.

Leo looked around the room carefully. He used his sharp lion eyes. The scratching came from near the window. Leo walked over and peeked behind the curtain.

A small tree branch was scratching against the glass when the wind blew. "That's not scary at all!" Leo giggled. "It's just saying hello!"

But Leo noticed something else. The window was open just a tiny bit. Cool air was coming in, making the room chilly.

"I'm cold," Leo said. "I could call Grandma to close the window. But I think I can do it myself!"

Leo stood on his tippy-toes. He pushed the window closed with his paws. "Click!" The room became warmer right away.

"I did it!" Leo cheered quietly. He felt so proud and strong.

Leo climbed back into the cozy bed. He pulled the soft blanket up to his chin. The room didn't feel scary anymore. It felt safe and comfortable.

"I was worried about sleeping here," Leo said to himself. "But I figured out all the sounds. I made the room warmer. I can take care of myself!"

Just then, Grandma peeked into the room. "How are you doing, little Leo?" she asked softly.

"I'm doing great, Grandma!" Leo said with a big smile. "I heard some noises, but I found out what they were all by myself. And I closed the window when I was cold."

Grandma's eyes sparkled with pride. "What a smart, brave lion cub you are! You used your thinking skills and took care of yourself."

"Yes!" said Leo. "When I have a problem, I can think about it and try to solve it myself first. That makes me feel strong and proud."

Grandma gave Leo a gentle kiss on his forehead. "Sweet dreams, my brave little lion."

Leo snuggled into his pillow. He felt happy and confident. He had learned that he could handle new situations by being calm, thinking clearly, and trying his best.

The guest room felt perfect now. Leo closed his eyes and fell asleep with a smile, knowing he could take care of himself.

The End.
