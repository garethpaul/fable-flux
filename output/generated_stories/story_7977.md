# Milo's Big Mistake

High up in the tall oak tree, Milo the monkey lived in the most wonderful treehouse. It had rope swings, a cozy reading nook, and windows that let in the warm sunshine. Milo loved his treehouse, but today he felt very sad.

"Oh no, oh no," Milo whispered, looking at the broken bird feeder hanging outside his window. Yesterday, he had been swinging too wildly and accidentally knocked it down. The colorful wooden feeder now had a big crack right down the middle.

Mrs. Robin landed on the windowsill, her small head tilted to one side. "Milo, what happened to our feeder? My babies and I are so hungry, and all the seeds spilled out."

Milo's tummy felt twisty. He wanted to hide under his soft blanket, but he knew that wouldn't help anyone. Taking a deep breath, he looked at Mrs. Robin with his big brown eyes.

"Mrs. Robin, I need to tell you something important," Milo said, his voice shaking a little. "I broke your feeder yesterday when I was swinging. I'm really, really sorry."

Mrs. Robin's feathers ruffled. "Oh my! But Milo, why didn't you tell me yesterday?"

"I was scared you'd be angry with me," Milo admitted, his tail drooping. "But hiding the truth made me feel worse and worse. I want to make it right."

Mrs. Robin flew closer. "I appreciate your honesty, Milo. But my family needs food. What can we do?"

Milo scratched his head, thinking hard. "Maybe we can work together to fix this! I have some wood and paint in my treehouse. Could you teach me how to build a proper bird feeder?"

"That's a wonderful idea!" chirped Mrs. Robin. "I know exactly what birds need - a roof to keep the rain out, small holes for the seeds, and a perch to stand on."

Together, they gathered supplies. Mrs. Robin perched on Milo's shoulder, giving gentle instructions. "Make the hole a little smaller, Milo. Perfect! Now let's add some bright colors so other birds can see it too."

Milo carefully painted red stripes and blue dots on the new feeder. His hands got messy with paint, but he didn't mind. "This is fun! I'm learning so much about what birds need."

"You're a quick learner," said Mrs. Robin warmly. "And thank you for being honest with me. That took courage."

When they finished, the new feeder was even better than the old one. It had two feeding spots and a tiny roof decorated with rainbow colors. Milo hung it carefully outside his window.

"There!" Milo said proudly. "And Mrs. Robin, I promise to be more careful when I swing."

Soon, Mrs. Robin's babies and many other birds came to eat. Their happy chirping filled the treehouse with music. Milo felt warm and proud inside.

"You know what, Milo?" said Mrs. Robin. "Making mistakes is okay. What matters is being honest about them and working to make things better."

Milo nodded, watching the birds enjoy their feast. "I learned that telling the truth feels much better than hiding it. And working together made everything more fun!"

As the sun set, Milo curled up in his reading nook, listening to the gentle sounds of his feathered friends. He had learned that honesty, even when scary, always leads to better solutions.

The End.
