# Beep’s Big Morning

Sunlight peeked through the curtains in a cozy bedroom. The blanket felt soft and warm. A little clock said, “Tick-tock, tick-tock.” On the rug sat Beep, a helpful robot dog. Beep’s metal ears twitched. Beep’s tiny tail light blinked blue.

“Good morning!” Beep said in a gentle, happy voice. “Time to start our day!”

Mina, a small child with bright eyes, yawned. “I’m sleepy,” said Mina. “Can you do everything for me, Beep?”

Beep wagged its light-tail. “I can help, Mina. But you can do many things by yourself. Doing things yourself helps you grow strong and proud.”

Mina listened. The room smelled like clean sheets and a little like lemon soap. A soft breeze moved the curtains. “Okay,” Mina said. “What do I do first?”

“First,” said Beep, tapping its paw to play a friendly chime, “let’s make your bed. I will show, and you can try.”

Beep held one corner of the blanket with a tiny grabber. “Fold to the middle,” Beep said. “Smooth it flat.”

Mina took the other corner. The blanket was fluffy and warm under her hands. “Fold to the middle,” she repeated. She smoothed the blanket. Swish, swish.

“You did it!” Beep said. “How do you feel?”

Mina smiled. “I feel big!”

Next, Beep blinked a green light. “Time to put on clothes. You choose. Choosing is a big step.”

Mina opened the drawer. T-shirts smelled fresh. “I want the blue shirt with the sun!” she said.

“Good choice,” said Beep. “Blue like the sky we all share.”

Mina pulled the shirt over her head. It felt soft on her skin. She wriggled into pants and wiggled her toes into socks. One sock was inside out. “Oops,” Mina said.

“You can fix it,” Beep said kindly.

Mina turned the sock right side out and pulled it on. “I did it!” she said, bouncing.

“Wonderful,” said Beep. “Now your cozy bedroom needs a tidy.” Beep opened a small drawer with a soft whirr. “We put toys in the basket. Books on the shelf. Clothes in the hamper. You can do it, and I can remind.”

Mina picked up blocks. Clack, clack, clack. Into the basket they went. She patted her teddy’s head and set it on the pillow. She slid books onto the shelf. One book had a picture of Earth on the cover.

“What is that?” Mina asked.

“That is our planet,” said Beep. Its screen showed a tiny picture of Earth, blue and green. “People all around the world wake up, get dressed, and tidy their rooms, just like you. We are all helpers in our homes. When we take care of our things, we help our family and our world.”

“Our world?” Mina asked.

“Yes,” said Beep. “When we put toys away, they last longer. When we turn off lights when we leave, we save energy. Saving energy is good for Earth. We are global citizens. That means we care for people and places all over the world, even from our cozy bedroom.”

Mina looked at the light switch. Click. Off. Sunlight was bright enough. “I am a global citizen,” she said softly.

Beep chirped. “Now, let’s complete your task list.”

“What is a task list?” asked Mina.

Beep projected a friendly list onto the wall, glowing like a rainbow. It showed small pictures:
- Make bed
- Get dressed
- Tidy room
- Brush teeth
- Water plant
- Say hello to the world

“We already did three,” said Beep. “Next: brush teeth.”

Mina giggled and picked up her tiny toothbrush. Minty foam tasted cool and tingly. “Brush, brush, brush,” she sang.

Beep hummed along, ticking for two minutes. “Done!” Beep said.

Mina rinsed and smiled at her shiny teeth. “What next?”

“Water plant,” said Beep, pointing to a small green plant on the windowsill. The leaves felt smooth and cool. “Plants need water to live. People everywhere care for plants. Caring is a way to help our big home, Earth.”

Mina poured a little water. Drip, drip, drip. The soil smelled like rain. “I helped!” she said.

“Last,” said Beep, “say hello to the world.”

Mina stood by the window. She waved out to the bright morning. “Hello, world!” she called. “I will be kind. I will help. I will try to do things by myself.”

Beep sat beside her. “That is the best hello.”

Mina looked at the glowing list. All boxes were checked. “Beep,” she said, “I did my tasks! I did them mostly by myself.”

Beep nodded, tail light blinking gold. “You were independent. That means you tried first and asked for help when you needed it. You completed your tasks. You cared for your room and your plant. You saved energy. You thought about our big world.”

Mina hugged Beep. Beep was cool and smooth, with a happy little whirr.

“How do you feel now?” Beep asked.

“I feel proud,” said Mina. “I can do it!”

Beep’s voice was warm. “Today you learned: I can try. I can finish. I can care. Being independent helps you, your family, and our world.”

Mina smiled at the tidy room, the soft bed, the bright plant, and the sunny window. The cozy bedroom felt even cozier now.

“Ready for a new day?” asked Beep.

“Ready!” said Mina.

The End.
