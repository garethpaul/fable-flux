# Nutmeg’s New Neighbor

Nutmeg was a small, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail. One rainy afternoon, Nutmeg scampered in through a tiny window to the guest room. The room smelled like clean soap and warm blankets. The rug was soft under Nutmeg’s paws, and the bedspread was as blue as the sky.

“I’ll wait here until the rain stops,” Nutmeg said, patting her little bag of acorns. Drip, drip, drip went the water from her fur onto the wooden floor.

Just then, the closet door creaked. Creak, creeeak. Two shiny eyes peeped out. A little toy robot rolled forward. Its wheels went whirrr, whirrr. It had a friendly face screen that blinked.

“Hello,” beeped the robot. “I am Rollo. Who are you?”

Nutmeg puffed up her tail. “I’m Nutmeg. I’m a squirrel. I’m just staying for the rain.”

Rollo’s screen flashed. “I like quiet. I like neat lines and tidy piles. Acorns are round and roll-y. Will they roll everywhere?”

Nutmeg hugged her bag. “My acorns are important. They are my food.”

Rollo’s eyes blinked again. “I do not understand. I like square blocks. I do not like roll-y things.”

Nutmeg felt a little sting in her heart. “I feel worried,” she said softly. “Maybe I should leave.”

The rain banged harder on the window. Pitter-patter became patter-PAT. The room grew cooler and smelled like wet leaves. Nutmeg shivered.

“I do not want you to be cold,” Rollo said. “But I also do not want acorns rolling under the bed.”

Nutmeg took a deep breath. “Maybe we can solve this. I am resourceful. I can try.”

“This is a problem,” Rollo beeped. “We can find a solution.”

Nutmeg looked around. She saw a small basket by the dresser. It held extra socks. The basket was woven and warm-looking.

“Could we put my acorns in that basket?” Nutmeg asked. “They won’t roll. They will stay neat.”

Rollo rolled closer. Whirr. “A basket is like a safe nest. Yes.”

Nutmeg tipped her bag carefully. Clink, clink, clink. The acorns dropped into the basket. They made a nice, gentle sound, like tiny raindrops.

Rollo’s screen lit up with a smile. “Order restored.”

Nutmeg smiled too. “Thank you for helping. The room feels safe now.”

They listened to the rain together. The guest room light was soft and golden. The blanket smelled like sunshine and lemon. Nutmeg’s fur began to dry and fluff.

“I like to stack blocks into towers,” Rollo said. “Would you like to see?”

Nutmeg nodded. “I like to build nests. Maybe I can help.”

Rollo rolled to a corner rug. There were red, yellow, and blue blocks. Rollo stacked one, two, three. Up went the tower. Nutmeg placed a block gently on top with her tiny paws.

“It wobbles,” Nutmeg whispered.

Rollo steadied it. “We can try again. We can be resilient.”

They tried again. One, two, three, four. The tower stood tall. Nutmeg clapped her paws. “We did it!”

“Team success,” Rollo beeped. “You are gentle.”

Nutmeg’s tummy rumbled. “May I eat one acorn?”

Rollo nodded. “Yes. The basket keeps them still.”

Nutmeg cracked an acorn. Crunch, munch. It tasted nutty and sweet. She offered Rollo a block to hold. Rollo pressed it to his chest with a soft click.

“I am learning,” Rollo said. “Acorns are food for squirrels. Blocks are fun for robots. Different needs.”

Nutmeg’s ears twitched. “Yes. We like different things. That is okay.”

They found more ways to share the space. Nutmeg lined up shiny buttons she found in a drawer. Click, click, click. Rollo loved the neat lines. Nutmeg made a tiny nest out of a washcloth and a scarf. It was warm and soft. Rollo kept the wheels quiet on the rug to help Nutmeg rest.

A gust of wind shook the window. Boom. The tower toppled. Blocks tumbled everywhere. Thud, thud.

Rollo’s screen flickered. “Oh no.”

Nutmeg sat up. “We can fix it. Let’s try again.”

They picked up blocks together. Rollo hummed a calm tune. Mmmm. Nutmeg used her paws to steady the base. They rebuilt, slower this time. The tower stood taller than before.

“I feel proud,” said Rollo.

“I feel brave,” said Nutmeg.

The rain softened. Pitter, patter, pause. A shy sunbeam slid across the floor, warm like toast. Nutmeg looked at the bright window.

“It’s time for me to go,” she said.

Rollo rolled close. “Will you come again?”

Nutmeg nodded. “Yes. We can build and share and learn.”

Rollo blinked happily. “I like learning. I learned tolerance. That means I can be kind and patient with things and friends that are different.”

Nutmeg smiled. “And I learned resilience. When the tower fell, we tried again. I also made a new discovery: a basket keeps acorns safe!”

They touched paw to wheel. Tap.

As Nutmeg climbed to the window, she looked back at the guest room. It felt warm, tidy, and friendly. She felt safe and seen.

“Good-bye, Rollo!”

“Good-bye, Nutmeg!” Rollo beeped. “Different is okay. Sharing is great.”

Nutmeg leaped into the soft, damp air. The world smelled fresh and new.

The End.
