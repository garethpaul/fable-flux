# Pippa the Penguin’s Wiggle-Day

Pippa was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a tiny orange beak. One sunny morning, she waddled into the guest room. The guest room was quiet and cozy. A soft rug tickled her feet. A small window let in warm light. Dust sparkles danced in the sun.

“Hello, room!” Pippa chirped. “I’m here to move and learn.”

On a little desk, Pippa saw paper, a short pencil, and a big, comfy chair with bouncy cushions. The chair made a friendly squeak when she touched it.

“Squeak!” said the chair.

“Hee-hee!” giggled Pippa. “I like you too.”

Pippa’s tummy felt fluttery with excitement. She wanted to explore the whole room by herself. She wanted to move her body and see what she could do. She wanted to try writing, too.

She took a deep breath. The room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. The curtains swished softly. “Swish, swish,” they sang.

“I will make a Wiggle-Day,” Pippa decided. “I will move, try things, and write my own little list.”

Pippa waddled to the rug. “Waddle, waddle,” went her feet. The rug felt fluffy like clouds. She bent her knees and did a penguin stretch. “Stretch up tall,” she said. She reached her flippers to the ceiling. She looked left, then right.

“Good morning, neck!” Pippa giggled. “Let’s move!”

She made up a fun game. She called it the ABC Move Game. For A, she did an Air-flap. She flapped both flippers gently. “Flap-flap!” For B, she did a Bounce. She bent her knees and did tiny, safe bounces on the soft rug. “Boing, boing.” For C, she did a Circle step. She waddled in a slow circle and looked all around.

Pippa smiled. “Movement makes my body happy,” she said. “I feel strong!”

Her eyes found the desk again. On the desk was a little notepad. The paper was smooth and white. The pencil was short, just right for her flipper-hands. Pippa climbed into the chair by herself. The cushion made another squeak. She felt proud. “I did it on my own,” she whispered.

Pippa pressed the pencil to the paper. “I will write my Wiggle-Day list,” she said. She made a line and another line. The pencil went scratch-scratch. She drew a round shape that looked like a little penguin face. She smiled and tried some letters. They were wiggly and happy, just like her.

“Dear list,” Pippa said out loud, “A is for Air-flap. B is for Bounce. C is for Circle step.” She wrote short marks next to each word. “This is my plan,” she whispered. “I can make my own plan.”

She hopped down and tried her moves again. Flap. Bounce. Circle. She added a new one for D. “D is for Door-tiptoe!” She tiptoed to the door and back. Her feet went tip-tip-tip on the wood floor. The floor felt cool. “Tiptoe makes me quiet and strong,” she said.

Then she thought about E. “E is for Explore!” Pippa explored the guest room with careful steps. She looked under the bed. It smelled like lavender. She felt the blanket. It was soft and fuzzy. She listened to the clock. “Tick-tock, tick-tock,” it went.

“I am curious,” Pippa said proudly. “I look, listen, and smell. I learn with my whole body.”

Pippa waddled to the window. She watched a bird outside. The bird hopped on a branch. “Cheep-cheep,” sang the bird.

Pippa waved. “I’m moving too!” she told the bird. “I have a plan!”

She went back to the desk and wrote more lines. “E is for Explore,” she said, writing little E marks. She added F. “F is for Freeze!” She stood very still like a statue. No wiggle. No wobble. Just quiet. She felt the beat of her heart going thump-thump. Being still was part of moving, too.

“Freeze helps me rest,” she said softly.

Suddenly, the chair looked extra bouncy. “G is for Gentle jumps,” Pippa decided. She made gentle jumps on the rug so she stayed safe. “Jump-jump,” she laughed. Her flippers spread wide for balance. Her cheeks felt warm and happy.

She checked her list. It was getting longer. She felt brave and proud. She did all the moves by herself. She listened to her body. When she felt a little tired, she added H.

“H is for Hug-breath,” she said. She hugged herself and took a deep, slow breath. In through the beak. Out nice and slow. Her chest rose and fell. She felt calm and strong at the same time.

“Pippa,” she told herself kindly, “you made a Wiggle-Day. You moved. You explored. You wrote your own plan. You did it!”

Just then, the door opened a crack. Someone peeked in. “Pippa? How are you?” asked a gentle voice.

Pippa waved her list. “I’m great! I made my Wiggle-Day list. I moved in safe ways. I tried writing. I explored the guest room. I did it by myself!”

The voice smiled. “I’m proud of you. How do you feel?”

Pippa wiggled with joy. “I feel strong, curious, and calm,” she said. “Movement helps my body learn. Writing helps my brain remember. I can make a plan and try new things.”

She placed her list on the desk, right in the warm sunlight. The pencil made one last scratch-scratch as she drew a big happy star.

“Tomorrow,” Pippa whispered, “I will try more letters and more moves.”

The End.
