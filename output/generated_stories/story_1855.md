# Pepper the Parrot Says “Thank You”

Pepper was a creative parrot with green, gold, and blue feathers that sparkled like tiny paint drops. Pepper loved to sing, “Squawk! Idea time!” and make art with anything colorful. One sunny morning, Pepper hopped into a warm kitchen. The air smelled like cinnamon and butter. The oven hummed like a happy bee. Pots clinked. A wooden spoon tapped-tap-tapped a bowl.

Mama Lina was cooking. She wore a bright apron with flowers from her home far away. She hummed a soft song in another language. “La-la-lá,” she sang, rolling dough in little circles. The song felt like a cozy hug.

“Good morning, Pepper,” said Mama Lina. “Today we make sweet rolls from my grandmother’s recipe. In my country, we say gracias to the hands that help.”

“Gra-ci-as,” Pepper echoed, trying the new word. “What does it mean?”

“It means thank you,” Mama Lina smiled. “Gratitude is saying thank you with your words and your actions.”

Pepper puffed his feathers. “I want to help! I can be a kitchen artist!”

Mama Lina nodded. “We have chores. Small jobs make big help. You can sort berries, stir gently, and keep the counter tidy.”

Pepper flapped. “I can do it! Squawk!” He pecked open the berry basket. Red raspberries, shiny blueberries, and sweet blackberries tumbled out. They smelled like sunny gardens.

Pepper started to sort, but the berries were round and bouncy. Plop! A blueberry rolled under a chair. “Oh no!” Pepper chirped.

“It’s okay,” said Mama Lina kindly. “Try again. Be patient.”

Pepper took a deep breath. He scooted the blueberry back with his beak. He put red with red, blue with blue, and black with black. “I’m doing it!” Pepper cheered. Then—oops!—his wing bumped the bowl. Berries scattered like tiny marbles across the warm floor.

Pepper’s heart sank. “I messed up,” he whispered. His feathers drooped.

Mama Lina knelt and touched Pepper’s shoulder. “Everyone makes mistakes. We keep going. That is persistence.”

“Per… sis… tence,” Pepper said slowly. He puffed up again. “I can try again.”

Together they picked up berries. The floor was a little sticky. Pepper wiped it with a soft cloth. Swish, swish. “Clean and shiny!” he said.

“Thank you for fixing it,” said Mama Lina. “That is gratitude too.”

Next came stirring. The wooden spoon felt heavy in Pepper’s claws. The dough was soft and squishy. It smelled like warm milk and sweet sugar. “Stir slow,” Mama Lina said. “Listen.” The dough made a gentle gloopy sound. Glop. Glop.

Pepper stirred slowly. He counted, “One, two, three, stir!” He made a little song, “Glop, glop, thank you, plop!” His tail feathers danced. He felt proud. Then his wing slipped—splish! A tiny splash of dough landed on his beak.

Pepper giggled. “Tasty!” He licked a dot. “Mmm. Cinnamon!”

“You are learning,” Mama Lina said, smiling. “Now we roll the dough and tuck in the berries. This recipe is special to my family. In my country, we share food and say thank you to our elders.”

Pepper blinked. “I will say thank you to your grandma too,” he said. “Thank you, Grandma Recipe!”

They rolled the dough into little circles. Pepper used the back of his claw like a tiny brush to make swirls. “Art!” he chirped. “Berry dots, dough swirls, happy rolls!”

When it was time to put the pan into the oven, warm air brushed Pepper’s face. It smelled like a sweet hug. The oven hummed. “We wait now,” said Mama Lina. “Waiting is part of cooking.”

Pepper bounced. Waiting was hard. He wanted to peek. He wanted to taste. He sang his waiting song. “Wait, wait, I can wait! I can be patient. I feel great!” He wiggled but stayed put. He looked around the kitchen. The counter was messy again—berry stains, flour dust, and a sticky spoon.

“I can do a chore,” Pepper said. He wiped the counter in slow circles. Swish. Swish. He washed the spoon. Splash, splash. He stacked the bowls. Clink, clink. “I did it!” Pepper said.

Mama Lina looked around. “Thank you, Pepper. You helped so much.”

Pepper stood tall. His chest felt warm and light. “I am helpful,” he said. “I can do hard things.”

Ding! The oven chimed. Mama Lina opened the door. A sweet cloud of cinnamon rolled into the kitchen. The rolls were golden and soft, with berry jewels peeking out. “Beautiful,” she said. “Just like my grandmother made.”

Pepper’s eyes sparkled. “May I serve?” He put two rolls on a little plate. He added a tiny card he made from a napkin. On it he drew a heart and wrote, “Thank You” and “Gracias.”

He handed the plate to Mama Lina with a little bow. “Thank you for teaching me,” he said. “Thank you for your song. Thank you for your recipe. Thank you for trusting me when I messed up.”

Mama Lina’s eyes were shiny. “And thank you, Pepper, for your help, your trying, and your big heart.”

They tasted the warm rolls. Soft, sweet, and berry-bright. Pepper closed his eyes. “Mmm,” he hummed. “My tongue is dancing!”

Pepper flapped happily. “Today I learned gratitude means saying thank you with my words and my chores. If I keep trying, I can do it. I can feel proud and kind.”

Mama Lina nodded. “Yes. Gratitude, persistence, and self-confidence make strong wings.”

Pepper spread his wings wide. “Squawk! Strong wings! Thank you!”

The End.
