# Splash the Dolphin's Colorful Day

Splash the dolphin loved the creative art room at the ocean school. The bright room had big windows that let sunshine dance on the walls. Colorful paints sat in neat rows. Brushes of all sizes waited to create beautiful pictures.

"Good morning, Splash!" said Ms. Seahorse, the art teacher. "Today we're painting pictures of our favorite ocean friends."

Splash clapped his flippers together. "Yay! I love painting!" he squeaked happily.

Splash picked up a big brush and dipped it in bright blue paint. Splash, splash, splash! He painted wavy lines across his paper. Then he chose yellow paint for a sunny fish. Orange paint made beautiful coral. Red paint created dancing seaweed.

"This is so much fun!" Splash giggled as he painted.

But Splash was having so much fun that he forgot something very important. Paint dripped from his brush onto his flippers. Blue paint splashed on his nose. Yellow paint got on his tail. Soon, Splash was covered in colorful paint from head to fin!

"Oh my!" said Ms. Seahorse. "Splash, you're very messy. What should you do when you get paint on yourself?"

Splash looked down at his colorful flippers. The paint felt sticky and gooey. "I don't know," he said quietly.

"When we get messy, we need to clean up," Ms. Seahorse explained gently. "It's important to wash our hands and bodies to stay healthy and clean."

"But I like the pretty colors!" Splash said. The paint made him look like a rainbow.

Ms. Seahorse smiled. "The colors are beautiful, but paint isn't good for our skin. It can make us feel icky and even make us sick if we don't wash it off."

Splash's friend Shelly the starfish swam over. "I always wash my arms after painting," she said, showing her clean pink arms. "It feels so nice and fresh!"

"Really?" asked Splash. He touched his sticky nose with his painted flipper. It did feel a bit yucky.

"Come on, Splash," said Ms. Seahorse. "Let's go to the washing station. I'll show you how to clean up properly."

At the washing station, warm water bubbled up from the ocean floor. Ms. Seahorse showed Splash how to rub his flippers together with gentle soap. The soap smelled like sweet sea flowers.

"Scrub, scrub, scrub!" sang Splash as he washed his flippers. The colorful paint swirled away in pretty spirals.

"Now your nose," said Ms. Seahorse. Splash rubbed his nose gently. The blue paint washed away.

"My tail too!" Splash said, swishing his tail in the bubbly water. Soon all the paint was gone.

"How do you feel now, Splash?" asked Ms. Seahorse.

Splash wiggled his clean flippers and touched his fresh nose. "I feel wonderful! So clean and smooth!" he said with a big smile.

"That's because keeping clean is a healthy habit," explained Ms. Seahorse. "When we wash our hands and bodies, we take good care of ourselves."

From that day on, Splash remembered to wash up after art time. He learned that being responsible meant cleaning up when he got messy. He still loved painting with bright colors, but now he loved the fresh, clean feeling afterward even more.

"Clean flippers, happy dolphin!" Splash would sing as he washed up each day.

And Ms. Seahorse was very proud of how responsible Splash had become.

The End.
