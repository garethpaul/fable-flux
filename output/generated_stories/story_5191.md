# Panda Pippa Finds Her Calm

In a quiet house, down a squeaky stairway, there was a small basement lab. The light was soft and yellow. The air smelled like warm soap and sweet glue. Little fans went whir, whir, whir. Beep-beep lights blinked like tiny stars. On a big table sat jars of buttons, friendly tools, and colorful sticky notes.

This was the favorite place of Panda Pippa. Pippa was a patient panda who loved to help. She wore a tiny blue apron with a heart patch. Her paws were gentle. Her eyes were bright with curiosity.

Today was Fix-It Day. Pippa was a volunteer helper. Neighbors brought broken things to the basement lab, and Pippa tried her best to fix them, one by one.

Mrs. Duck waddled in with a toy car. “It goes click-click but not vroom,” she quacked.

“Let’s see,” Pippa said softly. She held the toy close. She listened. She turned a screw. “I hear the click. We can help it vroom.” She smiled. “We will go slow.”

Pippa set a timer that went tick, tick, tick. “Time helps us plan,” she told Mrs. Duck. “We do one job, then the next.”

Next, Mr. Mole came with a cracked frame. “My picture fell,” he sniffed.

“We can fix it,” said Pippa. “We will be careful.” She gave Mr. Mole a warm cup of tea and a comfy seat.

Then, a loud clatter! A box tipped over. Buttons bounced like rain. Clink, clink, clatter! Pippa’s ears twitched. Her heart thumped fast. Her cheeks felt hot.

“Oh no,” she whispered. “So many buttons.”

Her paws wanted to pinch tight. She felt a big, hot bubble inside. That hot bubble was anger. It grew and grew.

Pippa remembered a poster on the wall. It said: “Stop. Breathe. Talk. Solve.” The letters were bright rainbow colors.

She put a paw on her chest. “Stop,” she said in a tiny voice.

She took a slow breath in. Sloooow in. Then out. Whoooo. She felt the air touch her nose, cool and sweet. Again: sloooow in, whoooo out. Her shoulders softened. The hot bubble got smaller.

Mrs. Duck waddled close. “Are you okay?”

Pippa nodded. “I felt mad. I will use my calm plan. I will talk.”

She looked at the messy buttons. “I feel upset when the box falls,” she said, “because I want to keep the lab safe and neat. Can we please pick up the buttons together?”

“Of course,” said Mrs. Duck.

“I can help too,” said Mr. Mole.

Their paws and wings went plink-plink as they picked up buttons. The fans hummed. The timer ticked. Together felt better.

Soon the buttons were back in the jar. Pippa tied on the lid. Twist, twist. She smiled. “Thank you for helping. Teamwork is kind.”

“Your calm helped my heart,” Mr. Mole said. “I felt worried. Now I feel okay.”

Pippa’s cheeks cooled. The hot bubble was gone. “When I get angry,” she said, “I stop, breathe, and use peaceful words.”

Now it was time for the toy car. Pippa checked the battery. Click. She wiped a little dust. She added a tiny drop of oil. The wheels whispered, shh-shh. “Try it,” she said.

Mrs. Duck put the car down. “Vroooom!” It rolled smooth and happy.

“Thank you,” she quacked. “You were patient.”

Next was the picture frame. Pippa dabbed a thin line of clear glue. It smelled sweet and a little strong. She pressed the wood edges together. “We wait,” she said, setting the timer. “Waiting gives glue time to hug.”

While they waited, a young bunny peeked in. “What is this place?” he asked, eyes big.

“This is our basement lab,” said Pippa. “We fix and learn. We use curiosity to ask, ‘How does it work?’ Would you like to watch?”

“Yes!” the bunny said. His ears wiggled.

Pippa showed him the beeping lights. “These tell us, ‘I am on.’” She showed him the soft brushes. “These help dust go away.” She showed him the rainbow poster. “This helps feelings.”

The timer went ding! The glue was dry. Pippa held up the frame. “All better,” she said.

Mr. Mole put his picture inside. It showed him smiling with his grandma. He patted the frame. “Thank you,” he said softly.

Just then, another wobble! A stack of papers slid. Skrrrrt! One sheet tore. The hot bubble jumped back into Pippa’s tummy. Her paws felt tight.

“Stop,” she whispered. She put a paw on her chest. Breath in, sloooow. Breath out, whoooo. She told herself, “I can be calm.” Then she spoke. “The papers slipped. I feel upset. Can someone help me pick them up, please?”

“I can!” said the young bunny. He used careful little paws. “We can tape the torn one.”

Pippa’s smile grew warm. “Thank you. That was peaceful talking.”

Together they taped the paper. Smooth, smooth. The fans still hummed. The lights still blinked. The lab felt safe again.

Pippa looked at her friends. “Today we learned many things,” she said. “We learned to plan with time. We learned to be curious and ask how things work. We learned to volunteer and help. We learned to talk with kind, calm words when we feel mad.”

Mrs. Duck nodded. “We can all try: Stop. Breathe. Talk. Solve.”

Pippa drew a little heart on a sticky note and put it on the poster. “My calm heart,” she said.

Everyone waved goodbye. The toy car went vroom, the frame was strong, the papers were neat, and the button jar was tight. Pippa turned off the lights. Click. The lab felt cozy and quiet.

Pippa patted her chest. “I am proud,” she whispered. “When anger comes, I can find my calm.”

The End.
