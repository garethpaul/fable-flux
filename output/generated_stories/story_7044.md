# Fox Kit’s Paint-Box Morning

The sun peeked over the trees, warm and golden. A curious fox kit named Flick hopped down the garden path. His paws were soft on the cool dirt. Birds sang, “Cheep, cheep!” The air smelled like wet leaves and fresh grass.

Flick’s tail swished. “Today is a make-something day,” he said. “I feel it in my whiskers!” He trotted to the little garden shed. The door was old and green. It creaked, “Eeee-oooo,” as he pushed it open with his nose.

Inside, the shed was cozy and dusty. Light shone in stripes across wooden shelves. There were jars of buttons, pots of paint, and brushes with fluffy tops. There were little seeds in paper packets and a stack of empty flower pots.

Flick’s eyes got wide. “So many things! What can I make?” he whispered. He breathed in the smell of paint and wood. It tickled his nose. “Achoo!”

“Bless you,” said a friendly spider, lowering down on a silk thread. “What are you doing, little fox?”

“I want to make something,” said Flick. “But I don’t know what yet.”

“That’s okay,” said the spider. “First, look. Then, listen. Then, try.”

Flick liked that. He made a little routine. “Look, listen, try,” he repeated. He looked around. He listened to the soft drip of a leaky tap. He heard the wind hum outside. Then he tried.

He dipped a brush into blue paint. “Swish,” went the brush. He made a big blue circle on a scrap of cardboard. He dipped again into yellow. “Dot, dot, dot,” went the brush. He made sunny dots inside the circle.

Flick smiled. “It looks like a sky cookie!”

A beetle with shiny green wings crawled out from behind a pot. “Tap, tap,” went its tiny feet. “What are you making?” the beetle asked.

“I’m not sure yet,” said Flick. “I’m just trying.”

“That is creative,” said the beetle. “You are using your ideas.”

Flick’s chest felt warm. His tail made a happy wiggle. “Thank you,” he said.

He found a box of chalk. He found a bundle of yarn. He found a little wooden spoon. He held the spoon like a drumstick and tapped the pots. “Ting, tong, ting!” The sounds danced in the air. Flick giggled.

“I can paint and make music,” he said. “I can do both!” He felt brave and proud. He was expressing himself.

Then he saw a cracked flower pot. It looked sad and plain. Flick touched it with his paw. It was cool and rough.

“I will make you bright,” he told the pot. “I will give you a happy face.”

He painted big yellow petals around the pot. He added orange swirls and tiny blue stars. He glued buttons like tiny eyes. He tied a red yarn bow at the top. The pot was no longer plain. It was cheerful and silly.

The door creaked again. Mama Fox peeked in. Her fur was soft and shiny. “Hello, Flick,” she said. “It is our tidy-up time soon. What are you doing?”

Flick’s ears twitched. “I’m making! I’m painting and tapping and trying.” He showed Mama the sky cookie picture. He showed her the cheerful pot.

Mama’s eyes sparkled. “You made with your heart,” she said. “Tell me about your art.”

Flick took a deep breath. He used his words. “The blue circle is the morning sky. The yellow dots are bird songs. The cheerful pot is for growing happy seeds. I wanted it to smile.”

Mama nodded. “Thank you for telling me. I can hear your ideas. That is good communication.”

The spider swung nearby. “Look, listen, try,” it reminded.

Flick nodded. “I looked. I listened. I tried. And I made something new!”

Mama said, “We can add one more step. Look, listen, try, share.” She pointed to the garden. “Let’s put your cheerful pot by the door, where everyone can see.”

They carried the pot outside. The sun warmed Flick’s fur. The breeze ruffled his ears. They placed the pot on the step. It looked like it was smiling at the world.

A hummingbird zipped by. “Zzz-zip!” It paused and peered. “Nice colors!” it chirped.

Flick’s heart went hop-hop. “Thank you,” he said. His self-esteem grew like a little sprout.

Mama smiled. “Now it is tidy-up time.”

Flick went back to the shed. He washed the brushes. “Swish, swish,” in a jar of water. He wiped the table. He put the chalk in the box and the buttons in the jar. He wrapped the yarn into a neat ball. He followed his routine: make, share, tidy.

When the shed was clean, Flick and Mama sat on the step. The pot glowed in the sun. Flick whispered, “I feel proud. I can make things. I can tell about my ideas.”

Mama hugged him. “Yes. Your voice and your ideas matter. Creative-expression helps your inside feelings shine outside.”

Flick wiggled his toes. “Tomorrow, I will make music for the garden,” he said. “Ting, tong, ting!”

Mama laughed. “I can’t wait to listen.”

Flick looked at the cheerful pot and the blue-and-yellow picture. He breathed in the sweet smell of grass. He listened to the soft garden sounds. He felt calm and happy.

He said softly, “Look, listen, try, share.” He smiled. “I did it.”

The End.
