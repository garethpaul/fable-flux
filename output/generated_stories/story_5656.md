# Tiko the Tortoise Makes Soup

In a warm kitchen that smelled like bread and sunshine, a wise tortoise named Tiko blinked his gentle eyes. The kettle hummed. The window let in soft light. The wooden spoon felt smooth in Tiko’s careful paw.

“Today,” Tiko said slowly, “we will make vegetable soup. We will do it together.”

His friends came to visit. Pip the puppy wagged his tail. Lina the lamb bounced on tiny hooves. Buzz the little bee zipped in with a happy hum. Everyone liked Tiko’s kitchen. It was cozy. It was safe. It felt like a hug.

Pip sniffed the carrots. “Can I taste?” he asked.

“Soon,” said Tiko with a smile. “First, we work as a team.”

Lina’s ears wiggled. “What is a team?” she asked.

“A team means we help each other,” Tiko said. “We take turns. We listen. We use gentle bodies and calm voices.”

Buzz landed on the spoon. “Bzz-zz! I can help,” he said.

Tiko nodded. “Yes. Everyone can help.”

Tiko set out a bowl, a pot, and a little basket of vegetables. The tomatoes were red like tiny suns. The carrots were orange and crunchy. The peas were green and round, like little beads.

“Step one,” Tiko said. “We wash the veggies.”

Pip rushed to the sink and turned the water on too fast. Splash! Water jumped out and wet the floor. Pip’s eyes went big. “Oh no!”

Tiko took a slow breath in. He let a slow breath out. “Let’s pause,” he said kindly. “We can use self-control.”

Pip copied Tiko. In… out… His tail slowed. “I feel calmer,” Pip whispered.

“Good,” said Tiko. “Now we turn the water on slowly.” Pip nodded and turned the knob with gentle paws. The water made a soft whisper sound.

“Step two,” Tiko said. “We chop and pluck.” He gave Lina the peas. “You can pop peas from the pods.” Pop! Pop! went the peas into the bowl. Lina giggled. “This is fun!”

Buzz buzzed near the herbs. “I will pick the tiny leaves,” he said. The herbs smelled fresh, like the garden after rain. Buzz moved carefully. “Bzz… one leaf, two leaves.”

Tiko held the knife. “I will cut the carrots. Slow and safe.” Chop, chop, chop went the carrots, steady as a clock. Pip watched and waited.

“I want to stir,” Pip said, tail twitching again.

“You will stir,” Tiko said. “We take turns. First, we add the veggies.” He lifted the pot lid. Warm steam kissed their faces. It smelled like comfort.

Lina helped pour in the peas. Plink, plink, plink. Buzz dropped the herb leaves like tiny green stars. Pip carried the chopped carrots with careful steps. His paws stayed slow. He was proud.

“Now we stir,” Tiko said. He handed the wooden spoon to Pip. Pip’s eyes shone. He stirred in a circle. Swish, swish. The soup whispered and bubbled. “This is our team soup,” he said softly.

Buzz hovered near Pip’s ear. “Bzz, your stirring is gentle.”

“Thank you,” Pip said.

Lina stood close. “I want a turn too.”

Pip looked at the spoon. He took a breath. He smiled and handed the spoon to Lina. “Your turn.”

Lina stirred. Swish, swish, swish. “It smells so good,” she said. Tiko tasted a little drop. “Needs a pinch of salt,” he said. He let Buzz point to the salt jar. Pip sprinkled just a tiny pinch. They all watched the tiny grains fall like snow.

Tiko clapped his paws. “Teamwork is tasty,” he said.

While the soup bubbled, they cleaned up together. Pip wiped the little water puddle. Squeak, squeak went the cloth. Lina put the peapods in the compost bin. Plop. Buzz dusted flour from the counter with a tiny brush. “Bzz—clean and bright!”

The timer dinged. Tiko lifted the lid. Whoosh! A warm cloud puffed up. The smell was cozy and sweet. He ladled soup into bowls. Pip’s bowl had a silly carrot smile. Lina’s had a pea heart. Buzz had a tiny thimble cup.

They sat at the little table. The wood felt warm and smooth. The steam tickled their noses.

“Let’s share thanks,” said Tiko.

“Thank you, team,” Pip said. “Thank you for letting me stir.”

“Thank you, Pip, for waiting your turn,” Lina said. “Thank you for the herbs, Buzz.”

“Bzz—thank you for safe chopping, Tiko,” Buzz hummed.

Tiko nodded. “I am grateful for all of you. We listened. We took turns. We calmed our bodies. That is teamwork.”

They tasted the soup. Sip. Ahh. It was perfect. It tasted like kindness and hugs.

Pip wagged. “I learned to breathe slow when I get excited.”

Lina nuzzled Tiko’s shoulder. “I learned that taking turns makes room for everyone.”

Buzz spun a happy loop. “And I learned that little helpers make a big difference.”

Tiko smiled, eyes crinkling like tiny moons. “Together, we did something wonderful.”

They finished their soup and washed the bowls. The kitchen glowed. It smelled like herbs and happy hearts. Pip placed the spoon back in its drawer with a soft click.

“Tomorrow,” Tiko said, “we can bake bread. We will do it as a team.”

“Yes!” said Pip. “Yes!” said Lina. “Bzz-yes!” said Buzz.

They all took one more deep, calm breath. In… out…

“We are a great team,” said Tiko.

The End.
