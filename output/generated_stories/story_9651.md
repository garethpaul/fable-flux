# Toby's Rooftop Race

High up on the sunny rooftop of the community center, a wise old tortoise named Toby watched the children play. His green shell sparkled in the warm sunlight, and his kind eyes twinkled as he saw kids from all over the neighborhood having fun together.

"Come on, everyone!" called Maya, a little girl with bouncy curls. "Let's have a race across the rooftop!"

The children lined up excitedly. There was Carlos with his bright red sneakers, Zara wearing her purple headband, and little Ben who loved to hop like a bunny. They all looked so happy and ready to run.

Toby smiled and slowly walked over to join them. "May I watch your race?" he asked in his gentle, deep voice.

"Of course, Toby!" the children said together. They loved the wise tortoise who always had the best stories.

"Ready, set, GO!" shouted Maya.

The children took off running across the soft rooftop surface. Their feet made pattering sounds like raindrops. Carlos was very fast, but suddenly he tripped and fell behind. He looked around and saw that no one was watching him.

Carlos had an idea. While the others kept running toward the finish line, he quietly took a shortcut behind the big air conditioning unit. No one could see him there. He ran quickly and popped out right near the finish line.

"I won! I won!" Carlos called out, jumping up and down.

But Toby had seen everything with his wise old eyes. The tortoise slowly walked over to Carlos while the other children cheered.

"Carlos," Toby said softly, so only the boy could hear. "Can you tell me about your race?"

Carlos felt his cheeks get warm and red. He looked down at his bright sneakers. "I... I ran really fast," he said quietly.

Toby nodded slowly. "I saw you fall down. That must have been scary."

"Yeah," Carlos whispered. "I thought I couldn't win anymore."

"What did you do then?" asked Toby gently.

Carlos felt butterflies in his tummy. He knew Toby was very wise and always told the truth. "I... I took a shortcut behind the big box," he admitted. "I didn't really win fair."

Toby smiled warmly. "Thank you for telling me the truth, Carlos. That took courage. How do you feel now?"

"My tummy feels funny," Carlos said. "I want to tell my friends the truth too."

"That's what honest friends do," said Toby. "And you know what? Your friends will still like you. Everyone falls down sometimes."

Carlos took a deep breath and walked back to his friends. "Hey everyone," he called out. "I need to tell you something. I didn't really win the race. I took a shortcut when I fell down."

The children looked surprised, but Maya smiled big. "That's okay, Carlos! Want to race again? We can all help each other if someone falls."

"Yeah!" said Zara. "Racing is more fun when we're all honest!"

Ben hopped up and down. "Let's all race together!"

Toby watched as the children lined up again, this time with Carlos in the middle where his friends could help him if he stumbled. The warm sun felt wonderful on everyone's faces as they ran together, laughing and cheering for each other.

"Being honest feels much better than winning unfairly," Carlos said to Toby after the race.

"You're absolutely right," said the wise tortoise. "Honest friends have the most fun together."

As the sun began to set over the rooftop, all the children sat around Toby, feeling happy and proud. They had learned that telling the truth, even when it's hard, makes friendships stronger and games more fun.

The End.
