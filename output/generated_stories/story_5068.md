# Kody's Big Cleanup

Kody the koala loved to play in Grandma's cozy guest room. The soft blue walls made him feel peaceful, and the fluffy white bed was perfect for bouncing. But today, something was very wrong.

"Oh no!" Kody gasped, looking around the messy room. Crayons were scattered across the wooden floor like colorful confetti. His juice box had spilled, making a sticky purple puddle on the pretty rug. Pillows were everywhere, and Grandma's favorite book lay open with torn pages.

Kody's fuzzy ears drooped. "I made such a big mess," he whispered sadly. His tummy felt all twisty inside.

Just then, he heard Grandma's gentle footsteps coming down the hallway. "Kody, dear, are you ready for lunch?" she called.

Kody wanted to hide under the bed, but he knew that wouldn't help. He took a deep breath, just like Grandma taught him when he felt worried.

"Grandma," Kody said softly when she appeared in the doorway. "I'm really, really sorry. I made a terrible mess in your special room."

Grandma's kind eyes looked around the room. She didn't look angry, just a little sad. "Oh my, this is quite a mess, isn't it?"

"I was playing and forgot to be careful," Kody explained, his voice shaky. "I broke your book and spilled my juice. I feel awful inside, like my tummy is full of butterflies."

Grandma sat down on the edge of the bed and patted her lap. Kody climbed up for a warm, eucalyptus-scented hug.

"Thank you for telling me the truth, sweet Kody," Grandma said, stroking his soft gray fur. "Everyone makes mistakes. What matters most is what we do next."

Kody looked up with hopeful eyes. "What can I do to make it better?"

"Well," Grandma smiled, "let's think about this together. What do you think needs to happen?"

Kody thought hard, tapping his little black nose. "I could clean up the crayons! And maybe we could fix your book with tape?"

"Those are wonderful ideas!" Grandma said. "And what about the spilled juice?"

"I could get paper towels and wipe it up!" Kody said, feeling more excited. "I know where you keep them in the kitchen!"

Together, they made a plan. First, Kody carefully picked up each crayon, counting them as he put them back in the box. "One red, two blue, three yellow," he said, making it into a fun game.

Next, Grandma showed him how to dab the juice stain gently with warm, soapy water. "Press softly, like you're patting a baby bird," she said. Kody giggled as the purple stain slowly disappeared.

For the torn book, Grandma got special clear tape. Kody held the pages while she carefully mended them. "It's like giving the book a band-aid," Kody observed.

Finally, they fluffed the pillows and arranged them neatly on the bed. The room looked beautiful again, and it smelled fresh and clean.

"Wow!" Kody said, spinning around happily. "We fixed everything together!"

"You worked so hard to make things right," Grandma said proudly. "That's what making amends means - fixing our mistakes and learning to do better."

Kody felt warm and proud inside, much better than the twisty feeling from before. "I'll remember to be more careful next time I play in here."

"And if you make another mistake," Grandma winked, "you'll know just what to do."

From that day on, whenever Kody made a mess, he remembered how good it felt to make amends and clean up responsibly.

The End.
