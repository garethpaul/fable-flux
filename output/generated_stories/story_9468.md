# Robo's Rooftop Routine

High up on the tallest building in town, a shiny blue robot dog named Robo lived on a special rooftop. Every morning, Robo followed the same happy routine. First, he would stretch his metal legs and wag his antenna tail. Then he would water the pretty flowers in the rooftop garden. Finally, he would write in his special notebook about all the wonderful things he saw.

"Beep beep! Good morning, sun!" Robo said cheerfully, looking at the bright yellow sunshine. His LED eyes sparkled like tiny stars.

But one Tuesday morning, something felt different. The wind was whistling louder than usual - "Whoooosh!" - and Robo's notebook pages were flapping wildly in the breeze.

"Oh no!" Robo barked in his friendly robot voice. "My routine feels all mixed up today!"

Robo tried to water the flowers, but the strong wind blew the water drops everywhere. "Splash, splash!" The water went on the walls, on his shiny fur, everywhere except the flowers!

Then Robo tried to write in his notebook, but the wind kept turning the pages. "Flip, flap, flip!" His pencil couldn't stay on one page long enough to write even one word.

Robo's ears drooped down sadly. "My routine isn't working," he whimpered. "What should I do?"

But then Robo's curiosity kicked in. His ears perked up tall and straight. "Wait! Maybe I can find a new way to do my routine," he thought bravely.

Robo looked around the rooftop with his bright LED eyes. He spotted a cozy corner behind the big air conditioning unit where the wind couldn't reach.

"Aha!" Robo barked excitedly. "I'll move my routine to the safe spot!"

First, Robo carefully carried his watering can to the sheltered corner. The flowers there were protected from the wind too. "Glug, glug, glug!" went the water as it gently soaked into the soil. The flowers seemed to smile and say "Thank you!"

Next, Robo brought his special notebook to the quiet corner. The pages stayed perfectly still. He could finally write! With his pencil gripped carefully in his mouth, Robo wrote: "Today I learned that routines can change, and that's okay!"

As Robo wrote, he heard a small voice say, "Help! Help!"

Robo's ears twitched. He looked around and saw a tiny yellow bird struggling in the strong wind.

"Don't worry, little friend!" Robo called out bravely. "Come to my safe corner!"

The little bird flew over and landed safely next to Robo. "Thank you, Robo! You're so kind," chirped the bird.

"I learned something important today," Robo told his new friend. "Sometimes our routines need to change, but that doesn't mean they're broken. We just need to be curious and find new ways to do the things we love!"

The bird nodded happily. "And you were brave enough to try something different!"

From that day on, Robo kept two special spots on the rooftop - his sunny spot for calm days and his cozy corner for windy days. His routine stayed the same, but now he had the perfect place for any kind of weather.

Every evening, Robo would write in his notebook: "Routines help us feel safe and happy, but it's okay to change them when we need to. Being curious and brave helps us solve problems!"

And whenever little birds needed help, they knew exactly where to find their helpful robot dog friend.

The End.
