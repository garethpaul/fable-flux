# Koala Kori’s Gentle Song

In a comfortable nursery with soft rugs and sunny windows, lived a calm koala named Kori. The nursery smelled like clean blankets and warm oatmeal. The rocking chair creaked a little, and tiny bells on the shelf chimed when the breeze came in. Kori loved quiet hugs, slow sways, and the cozy hum of the room.

One morning, the nursery felt extra busy. It was Chores Day. Blocks needed to be put away, books wanted to line up on the shelf, and crayon cups needed sorting. Kori rubbed their fuzzy ears and smiled. “I can help,” Kori said in a gentle voice.

As Kori reached for the blocks, a tiny tap-tap-tap drifted through the air. Tap-tap. Tap-tap. Kori’s big round ears wiggled. “What’s that sound?” Kori asked, curious.

From under a blanket basket, a little mouse popped out. “Hello! I’m Mip,” squeaked the mouse. Mip wore a leaf like a hat and held two wooden spoons. Tap-tap! “I like to make music!”

Kori blinked calmly. “Music is nice,” Kori said. “But we are doing chores right now.”

Mip nodded, then tapped the spoons again. Tap-a-tap! Tap-a-tap! The sound bounced off the rug and made the bells on the shelf jing-jing-jingle. Kori’s belly felt fluttery. The blocks wobbled. A tower tumbled down. Clack-clack-clack!

Mip gasped. “Oh no! I made a mess.”

Kori took a slow breath. In. Out. “It’s okay,” Kori said softly. “I felt surprised. Let’s think.” Kori’s eyes were kind. “We can fix the mess.”

Mip’s whiskers drooped. “I’m sorry, Kori. I didn’t mean to knock your blocks.”

“Thank you for saying sorry,” Kori said. “I accept your apology.” Kori smiled a calm smile. “I am curious about your music. Maybe we can make music and do chores at the same time.”

Mip’s eyes shined. “Really? How?”

Kori thought. The nursery held many sounds. The broom swished. The rocking chair creaked. The bells jingled. Even the window hummed when the wind whispered. Kori patted the rug. “Let’s listen,” Kori said. “Shhh. What do we hear?”

Together they listened. Swish-swish. Creak-creak. Jing-jing. Hum-hum. Kori’s ears relaxed. “We can make a song with chores,” Kori said. “A helping song.”

Mip bounced. “A helping song!”

Kori picked up the blocks, one by one. Plop. Plop. Plop. “Plop-plop-plop,” Kori sang in a soft voice.

Mip tapped the spoons gently. Tap… tap… tap… “Tap… tap… tap…”

The broom swished in a steady beat. Swish-swish. The bells answered. Jing-jing. The rocking chair kept time. Creak… creak… creak…

Kori giggled. “This sounds nice. Let’s keep it gentle.”

Mip nodded. “Gentle music for gentle chores.”

They made a pattern. Plop, plop, plop. Tap. Swish-swish. Jing. Creak. The room filled with friendly sounds. The sun made warm squares on the floor, and dust sparkles danced like tiny stars.

Kori sang a simple tune.
“Pick up slow,
Place just so.
Tap so light,
We’re doing right.”

Mip added a rhyme.
“Swish and sweep,
Books in a heap?
No, no, no!
On shelves they go!”

Kori laughed, eyes twinkling. “Good words, Mip.”

Together they moved and made music. Kori sorted crayons by color. “Red, blue, green,” Kori sang. Plink, plink, plink into the cup. Mip tapped to match. Tap, tap, tap. They put soft toys in the basket. Thump-thump, thump. Mip added a tiny trill on the spoon handles. Trrr-rrring!

Soon the nursery felt calm and tidy. The blocks stood tall on the shelf. The books lined up in a rainbow. The rug lay flat and cozy.

Mip looked around and squeaked, “We did it! And we made music too.”

Kori nodded. “Music helped us work together. Your tapping sounded happy when it was soft.”

Mip’s whiskers twitched. “I learned to listen. Loud tapping can surprise. Soft tapping can help.”

“I learned too,” Kori said. “I learned to be curious instead of upset. I listened to your idea.”

Mip took off the leaf hat and held it like a tiny bowl. “I want to make amends,” Mip said. “Can I dust the shelf I shook?”

Kori’s heart felt warm. “Yes. Thank you.” Kori handed Mip a small, fluffy duster. It felt like a cloud. Mip dusted in slow circles. Swish-swish. The bells jingled a gentle thank-you. Jing-jing.

When they were done, Kori and Mip sat by the sunny window. The room smelled like lemon polish and fresh air. Kori hummed a lullaby. Mmm-mmm-mmm. Mip tapped a tiny beat. Tip-tip.

Kori said, “Music can help us calm down. Music can help us do chores. Music can help us be friends.”

Mip smiled wide. “Music helps me say, ‘I’m sorry,’ and ‘I can help.’”

Kori nodded. “We can accept each other. Your tapping is part of you. My quiet is part of me. We can make space for both.”

They made a final soft song together.
“Listen, learn, and take your time,
Gentle beat and gentle rhyme.
Curious hearts will find a way,
To help, to sing, to save the day.”

The nursery glowed with peace. Kori felt cozy and proud. Mip felt helpful and kind.

And when Chores Day came again, they knew just what to do: make a helping song, listen with care, say sorry when needed, and accept each other’s ways.

The clear lesson of the day: Music can guide our hands and our hearts. It helps us listen, be curious, make amends, and work together with a smile.

The End.
