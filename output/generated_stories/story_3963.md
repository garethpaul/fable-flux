# Bucky Beaver’s Bubbly Bathroom Day

Bucky was a busy beaver. He loved to build, fix, and help. Today, he had a very important job in the bathroom.

“Good morning, bubbles!” Bucky said as he turned on the warm water. The water went whoosh and made soft steam. It smelled like fresh soap and minty toothpaste. The mirror got a little foggy. Bucky smiled at his foggy face.

“I will make my body strong and clean,” he said. “Wellness means I take care of me!”

He set out his toothbrush, his tiny comb, a soft towel, and a little cup. He also had a step stool. “Safety first,” he said, tapping the stool. “No slips!”

Just then, he heard a small hiccup sound. “Hic!” It came from the bathtub.

Bucky peeked over. His little rubber duck, Dot, bobbed in the tub. Next to Dot sat a tiny bug friend named Pip. Pip was a shy water bug with big eyes.

“Hi, Dot! Hi, Pip!” Bucky waved. “Are you okay?”

Pip wrinkled his nose. “Hic! My tummy feels funny. I ate old crumbs I found by the drain. Now I feel icky.”

Bucky’s whiskers twitched. “Oh dear. Eating old crumbs is not wellness,” he said gently. “Let’s help your tummy.”

He gave Pip a tiny sip of warm water in a little bottle cap. “Sip, sip,” Bucky said softly. “Warm water can be kind.”

Pip took a sip. “Thank you,” he whispered. “Hic—oh! I think the hiccup stopped.”

Bucky smiled. “You are brave, Pip. Now, let’s clean the bathroom so it is healthy for everyone.”

Bucky put on small yellow gloves. They made a squeaky sound. “Squeak!” He giggled. He turned off the water so it would not waste. “We save water. That is wellness, too.”

He looked at the sink. Glittery toothpaste dots sparkled on the counter. Bucky looked at the floor. A tiny puddle shined near the tub.

Bucky took a breath. “I see a mess. I made some of it,” he said honestly. “I was in a hurry last night.”

Dot bobbed and quacked softly. “Quack?”

“I will tell the truth,” Bucky said. “I will clean it up.”

He wiped the toothpaste dots with a damp cloth. Wipe, wipe, wipe. The sink smelled like lemon now. He put down a bath mat so feet would be dry and safe. Pat, pat, pat.

“Pip, can you help me pick up the tiny toys?” Bucky asked.

Pip nodded and scooted along the edge of the tub, pushing a toy boat closer. “I can help!” he said. The boat made a soft clink.

“Thank you for helping,” said Bucky. “Helping others makes hearts warm.” He touched his chest. “I feel warm here.”

Next, it was time to brush. Bucky sang, “Brush, brush, brush your teeth, morning, noon, and night.” He brushed in little circles. Swish, swish. The paste was minty and cool. His mouth felt tingly and clean. He spit into the sink and rinsed.

“Pip, I will teach you a wellness trick,” Bucky said. He took a deep breath in through his nose. “Smell the mint.” He breathed out slowly. “Ahhhh.”

Pip breathed in and out. “That feels calm,” Pip said, smiling.

Bucky washed his hands with warm water and soap. He rubbed his paws. “Front, back, between, thumbs.” The bubbles smelled like oranges. He counted to twenty. “One, two, three…” The bubbles popped like tiny drums. Pop, pop, pop!

He dried his paws on the soft towel. Rub, rub. “Clean hands help us stay well.”

Then Bucky heard another sound. “Sniffle.” It came from the trash can. Bucky peeked in and found a small note. It said, “I spilled the cup. I felt scared. I am sorry. —Your friend, Tiny Turtle.”

Bucky looked at the small puddle by the tub. He nodded. “Tiny Turtle made the spill and felt scared. I am glad he told the truth.”

Dot bobbed. “Quack!”

Bucky said, “Honesty helps us fix things. We can help Tiny Turtle next time.”

Just then, Tiny Turtle peeked around the door. His shell was shiny, and his eyes were wet. “I’m sorry I ran away,” Tiny Turtle said in a small voice. “I thought you would be mad.”

Bucky knelt down. “Thank you for telling the truth,” he said kindly. “I’m not mad. We already cleaned it together. Next time, say, ‘Please help me.’ We can do it as a team.”

Tiny Turtle sniffled, then smiled. “Okay. Can I help now?”

“Yes,” said Bucky. “Please put the comb in the cup.”

Tiny Turtle picked up the comb and set it in place. Click! “I helped!” he cheered.

“You did,” said Bucky. “Helping others and being honest make our bathroom safe and happy.”

Bucky looked around. The mirror was clear. The towel was folded. The floor was dry. Pip’s tummy felt better. Dot floated peacefully.

Bucky stood tall on his stool. He took one more deep breath. “Today I learned: Wellness is brushing, washing, breathing calm, saving water, and telling the truth. Wellness is also helping friends.”

Tiny Turtle nodded. “And asking for help,” he added.

Pip waved. “And sipping warm water when my tummy is icky.”

Bucky grinned. “We did great, team.”

They all smiled in the clean, sweet-smelling bathroom. The room felt bright and cozy. Their hearts felt warm and light.

The End.
