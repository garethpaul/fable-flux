# Sunny Steps and Gentle Hops

Bunny Ben was a determined rabbit with soft gray fur and big, brave eyes. He lived in a sunny backyard where the grass smelled fresh and sweet. The sun warmed his whiskers, and tiny daisies tickled his paws when he hopped. Today, Ben had a big plan.

“I will make a Hoppy Path!” Ben said, puffing his chest. “A path for everyone to hop and play.”

He picked up smooth stones with his little paws. Clink, clink, clink. He lined them up in the grass, making a zigzag path. The stones felt cool. The air smelled like lemons from the neighbor’s tree. Ben’s ears wiggled with joy.

Ben’s friends came to see. Tilly the turtle slowly walked over. Patch the puppy wagged his tail. Lina the ladybug buzzed and landed on a leaf.

“What is this?” asked Tilly.

“It’s a Hoppy Path,” Ben said. “My grandma made one when she was little. She called it the Hoppity Line. It is our family tradition. We hop together and take turns. We cheer for each other. We stay safe. We never push.”

Ben stood tall. “I want to share our tradition with you.”

Patch barked, “I want to go first! I am fast! Zoom!” He bumped the stones with his paws.

Ben’s whiskers drooped. “Wait, Patch. We take turns. That is part of the tradition.”

Patch said, “But I am excited!” His tail thumped against a flower pot. Thud.

Lina clicked her tiny wings. “Maybe we can all go at once!”

Ben took a deep breath. The breeze brushed his ears. He remembered Grandma’s words: Kind voice. Calm body. Clear rules.

He said softly, “Friends, let’s talk. I feel worried when we go too fast. Stones can roll. We might fall. We need a plan.”

Tilly blinked her gentle turtle eyes. “I like plans.”

Patch’s ears lowered. “I didn’t mean to bump your stones, Ben. I just want to play.”

“I know,” Ben said, nodding. “Thank you for telling me. Let’s fix the stones together.” They pressed the stones into the soft earth. Push, push. The stones sat snug and steady.

Ben tapped a paw on the first stone. “Here are the rules from my family tradition. One: We take turns. Two: We cheer for each hopper. Three: We hop with bunny knees bent, and eyes on the stones. That is for safety. Four: If there is a problem, we stop, talk, and find a solution together.”

Tilly smiled. “I can go slow and steady.”

Patch wagged. “I can wait and cheer!”

Lina buzzed. “And I can say, ‘You can do it!’”

Ben’s heart felt warm. “I will go first to show you. Watch.” He bent his knees, wiggled his tail, and hopped. Plunk, plunk, plunk. The stones felt firm under his paws. He landed and bowed. “Ta-da!”

Patch clapped with his paws. “Go, Tilly!”

Tilly stepped on the first stone. Her shell made a tiny tap. Tap, tap. She moved slow. On the third stone, she wobbled. “Oh!” she gasped.

Ben raised his paws. “Freeze.” Everyone stopped. The yard grew quiet. A bee hummed by. Ben spoke in a calm voice. “Tilly, do you want a helping paw?”

“Yes, please,” Tilly said.

Ben stood next to her. “We can do it together. Bend your knees. Look where you want your foot to go.” Tilly took a breath. She stepped and smiled. “I did it!”

Patch barked, “My turn!” He bent his knees like Ben did. He hopped, hop-hop-hop! On the last stone, he slipped on a leaf. Skid!

“Freeze,” said Ben again, gentle but firm. “Leaf alert.” He moved the leaf out of the way. “Safety check,” he said. “We can look for leaves, sticks, or mud. Then we hop.”

Patch tried again. Hop, hop, hop! He landed with a happy wiggle. “I did it! Thank you for helping me be safe.”

Lina zoomed up and down the path, tiny feet tapping. “I did a little bug hop!”

They all laughed. The sun felt warm on their backs. The smell of fresh grass was sweet. A bird sang, “Cheer-cheer-cheer!” and they cheered back.

Then a new problem came. Two squirrels, Squeak and Squeal, ran in. “Out of the way!” Squeal cried. “We want to go now!” They bumped past Patch, and one stone rolled, clatter-clack.

Ben’s tummy fluttered, but he stood tall. Courage filled his chest like a deep breath. “Friends,” he said, “we have a rule. We take turns and we stay safe. When we push, someone can fall. We can share the fun without rushing.”

Squeak paused. “We were just excited.”

Ben nodded. “I get excited too. Let’s fix the stone together, then we can make a turn list.” He smiled and held out his paw. “You can help. That is part of the tradition.”

They all pressed the stone back. Push, push. Ben drew a line in the dirt with his paw. “Here is the waiting line. We cheer from here.”

Squeal giggled. “Cheer spot! I like it.” The squirrels bounced to the line.

They took turns. They cheered. “You can do it! Slow and steady! Bend your knees!” Everyone hopped safely. Everyone had fun.

At the end, Ben looked around. The sun made everything golden. His friends were smiling. His heart felt strong. “Today we shared my family tradition. We used kind voices. We made a plan. We stayed safe. When there was a problem, we talked and found a solution together.”

Patch wagged. “That’s called conflict resolution,” he said proudly.

Ben grinned. “Yes. We can solve problems with our words and our hearts.”

They all touched the last stone together. Warm, warm, warm.

The End.
