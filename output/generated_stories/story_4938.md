# Benny Beaver Learns to Listen

Benny Beaver was always busy, busy, busy! He loved to build things with his strong teeth and flat tail. One sunny morning, Benny climbed up to the quiet attic of an old farmhouse to find wood scraps for his latest project.

"Perfect!" Benny said, looking around at all the wooden boxes and old furniture. "I can build the biggest dam ever!"

He immediately started gnawing on a wooden chair leg. *Chomp, chomp, chomp!* The sound echoed through the peaceful attic.

"Excuse me," came a tiny voice.

But Benny was too busy chomping to hear. *Chomp, chomp, chomp!*

"Um, hello there," the voice said again, a little louder.

Benny kept working, his tail slapping excitedly against the dusty floor. *Thwap, thwap, thwap!*

"PLEASE LISTEN!" squeaked the voice.

Finally, Benny stopped chomping. He looked around the quiet attic with his bright brown eyes. "Who said that?"

"Down here," whispered the voice.

Benny looked down and saw a tiny gray mouse sitting on a pile of old quilts. The mouse looked very sad.

"Oh! I'm sorry, little friend. I didn't see you there," Benny said, sitting back on his haunches. "I was so busy building, I wasn't paying attention."

The mouse's whiskers twitched. "My name is Mia. This is my home, and you're chomping on my favorite reading chair."

Benny felt his furry cheeks turn warm with embarrassment. "I'm really sorry, Mia. I should have looked around first and asked if it was okay."

Mia's tiny black eyes sparkled. "It's alright. But maybe next time you could listen with your ears before you start working?"

"You're absolutely right," Benny said, nodding his big head. "My mama always tells me I have two ears and one mouth, so I should listen twice as much as I talk."

Benny sat very still and quiet for a moment. He could hear the soft *tick-tock* of an old clock, the gentle *whoosh* of wind through the roof, and even Mia's tiny heartbeat.

"Wow," Benny whispered. "When I stop and listen, I can hear so many wonderful sounds. I never noticed before because I was always making noise myself."

Mia smiled, showing her tiny white teeth. "The attic is full of beautiful sounds when you take time to listen. Would you like me to show you around? Maybe we can find some wood scraps that I don't need."

"That would be wonderful!" Benny said. "But this time, I'll listen first and chomp second."

Mia led Benny around the cozy attic, showing him old wooden crates that were empty and broken fence pieces that nobody used anymore. As they walked, Benny practiced listening carefully to everything Mia said.

"Thank you for teaching me to listen, Mia," Benny said as he gathered the wood scraps. "I learned that when I stop, look, and listen, I can be a much better friend."

"And I learned that busy beavers can be very kind when they take time to listen," Mia replied with a giggle.

From that day on, whenever Benny wanted to build something new, he always remembered to stop, take a deep breath, and listen first. He made many new friends this way and never accidentally chomped on anyone's favorite chair again.

The End.
