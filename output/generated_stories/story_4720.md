# Polly Speaks Up

Polly the Parrot sat quietly in her travel cage in the back seat of the family car. Her bright green feathers sparkled in the sunlight streaming through the window. The car hummed along the smooth road, and Polly watched the trees whoosh by.

"Are we almost there?" asked little Emma from her car seat.

"Almost, sweetie," said Mom from the front. "We're going to visit Grandma Rose at the care home today."

Polly's orange beak opened, then closed. She wanted to say something, but she felt too shy. You see, Polly had a wonderful idea, but she wasn't sure if anyone would listen to a parrot.

The car turned into a parking lot. Polly could see a big building with lots of windows and a garden full of colorful flowers.

"I wonder if Grandma Rose will like the cookies we baked," said Dad, turning off the engine.

Polly's heart fluttered with excitement. She took a deep breath and finally spoke up. "Squawk! I have an idea! I have an idea!"

Emma giggled. "What's your idea, Polly?"

Polly felt brave now that someone was listening. "I can sing songs for the people inside! I know lots of happy songs that might make them smile!"

Mom turned around with a big smile. "Polly, that's a wonderful idea! I'm so glad you told us. The residents would love to hear your beautiful voice."

"But what if they don't like my singing?" Polly worried, ruffling her bright red tail feathers.

Dad reached back and gently touched her cage. "Polly, you'll never know unless you try. And I think you have the most beautiful voice in the whole world."

Inside the care home, the hallways smelled like fresh flowers and warm soup. Polly's cage sat on a table in the sunny common room. Many elderly people gathered around in their wheelchairs and comfortable chairs.

"Hello, everyone!" chirped Polly. "I'm Polly, and I'd like to sing for you!"

Grandma Rose clapped her hands together. "Oh, how delightful! We would love that, dear Polly."

Polly felt her chest puff up with confidence. She began to sing "You Are My Sunshine" in her clear, sweet voice. The room filled with her cheerful melody.

Soon, everyone was singing along! Some people clapped. Others swayed in their chairs. Mr. Frank, who usually sat quietly in the corner, was tapping his foot and grinning from ear to ear.

"More! More!" called out Mrs. Chen, her eyes twinkling with joy.

Polly sang song after song. "Twinkle, Twinkle, Little Star," "If You're Happy and You Know It," and even "Happy Birthday" for Mrs. Lopez, who was celebrating her 85th birthday that day.

After an hour of singing, Polly felt wonderfully tired but so, so happy.

"Thank you, Polly," said Grandma Rose, tears of joy in her eyes. "You've made our whole day brighter."

As they walked back to the car, Emma hugged Polly's cage gently. "Polly, I'm so proud of you for speaking up about your idea!"

"Me too," said Mom. "Because you were brave enough to share your thoughts, you made so many people happy today."

Polly's feathers practically glowed with pride. "I learned something important today," she chirped. "When I speak up about my ideas, wonderful things can happen!"

Dad started the car and smiled in the rearview mirror. "And Polly, we're grateful you shared your gift with everyone. You're already planning to come back next week, aren't you?"

"Absolutely!" squawked Polly happily. "Speaking up feels amazing!"

The End.
