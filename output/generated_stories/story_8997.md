# Gigi the Giraffe Says Please

Gigi the giraffe was tall and thoughtful. She lived next to a sunny backyard with bright yellow flowers, soft green grass, and a little birdbath that went plip-plop in the warm light. The air smelled like sweet oranges and fresh mint. Bees hummed. The sky felt big and blue.

Gigi peeked over the fence and waved her long, gentle neck. “Good morning!” she said in a kind voice.

Milo, a small boy with a red hat, looked up and waved. “Hi, Gigi! Want to play veggie café?”

Gigi’s eyes sparkled. “Yes, please!” she said. She loved good words like please and thank you. They felt smooth and warm, like sunshine on her fur.

Milo set up bowls on the picnic table. He put out rainbow snacks: orange carrots, green peas, purple grapes, and yellow bananas. “We can serve healthy food,” he said. “It helps us grow strong.”

Gigi sniffed. “Mmm. The carrots smell crunchy. The bananas smell sweet.”

A breeze wiggled the wind chime. Ting-a-ling! It sounded like tiny bells giggling.

“First,” Milo said, “we make a menu.” He drew big pictures. “Carrot sticks. Pea pops. Banana bites. Grape circles.”

Gigi nodded. “Let’s add kind words to the menu,” she said. “Please. Thank you. May I. No, thank you.”

Milo smiled. “A word menu!”

Soon, neighbors came to the sunny backyard café. A ladybug landed on the table. “Hello,” buzzed a friendly bee. Even the goldfish in a clear bowl swished, as if to say, “Hi!”

“Welcome!” Gigi said. “What would you like?”

The bee hovered over the grapes. “Grapes! Gimme grapes!”

Gigi tilted her head. “Let’s try our word menu,” she said gently. “May I have grapes, please?”

The bee thought, then tried. “May I have grapes, please?”

Gigi placed a tiny grape circle on a leaf. “Thank you for asking with kind words.”

The bee zipped in a happy loop. “Thank you!” it buzzed. “They are juicy!”

Next came a hungry squirrel. It reached for five banana bites at once.

Milo held up his hand. “Wait, please. We share in a kind way.”

Gigi added, “We also respect bodies. Ask before you take. Personal space matters.”

The squirrel blinked. “May I have two banana bites, please?”

“Yes,” said Gigi. “Two is fair. Thank you for asking.”

Crunch crunch went the squirrel. “Yum!”

Just then, a big crow landed near Gigi’s tail. “HEY! MOVE!” it cawed, stepping very close.

Gigi felt a wobble in her tummy. She took a slow breath. The sun felt warm on her back. “I like space,” Gigi said calmly. “Please stand back. That keeps me safe.”

The crow blinked, surprised. “Oh. Sorry. I didn’t know.”

“Thank you for listening,” Gigi said. “May I help you?”

The crow nodded. “May I have peas, please?”

Milo scooped peas into a tiny cup. “Here you go.”

Everyone smiled. The backyard felt calm again. The wind chime went ting-a-ling, like a happy yes.

A little snail slid up the table. It pointed its eyes at the carrots. “Um,” said the snail softly, “may I have a carrot, please?”

Gigi lowered her head to listen. “Thank you for using your voice,” she said. “Here is a tiny carrot curl.”

“Thank you,” whispered the snail. It nibbled, slow and happy.

Soon, a sign blew off the fence. It flapped onto the grass. Milo picked it up. “Oops,” he said. “Litter is not kind to our yard.”

Gigi nodded. “We take care of our place. That is being a good neighbor.” She lifted the sign with her careful lips and set it in the recycling bin. “Please stay tidy,” she read. “Thank you.”

Milo clapped. “We did civic responsibility!”

The café got busy. Voices filled the sunny air.

“May I have water, please?” buzzed the bee.

“Yes. Sip, sip,” said Gigi.

“No, thank you,” said the squirrel to the grapes. “I am full.”

“Good listening to your tummy,” Milo said. “Healthy food and healthy words!”

A tiny toddler peeked through the gate with a grown-up. The toddler reached toward Gigi’s soft neck.

Gigi smiled and took a step back. “Please ask before touching,” she said kindly.

The grown-up asked, “May we pet you, Gigi?”

Gigi nodded. “Yes, you may. Gentle hands, please.”

The toddler giggled and patted slowly. Gigi’s fur felt warm, and the child’s hand felt small and careful. “Thank you,” said the toddler.

“You are welcome,” said Gigi. Her heart felt big and light, like a kite.

When the sun began to yawn, Milo stretched. “Café is closing,” he said.

Gigi looked around. The table was neat. The bowls were empty. Friends waved goodbye.

“Let’s share our word menu one more time,” Gigi said. “Please. Thank you. May I. No, thank you. Excuse me. I need space.”

“Kind words help,” Milo said.

“They help us eat well, share well, and care well,” Gigi added. “They make our backyard bright.”

The wind chime sang a soft ting-a-ling goodnight. The air smelled like clean grass. Gigi smiled up at the sky.

“I learned today,” she said, “that words are yummy for the heart. Please and thank you are like fruit. They help us grow kind and strong.”

Milo nodded. “And we take care of our yard and each other.”

Gigi waved her long, gentle neck. “Goodnight, friends.”

“Goodnight!” everyone called.

The backyard felt peaceful and proud. Gigi’s thoughtful heart was full.

The End.
