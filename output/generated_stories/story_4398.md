# Momo the Helper Monkey

Momo was a brave little monkey with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived with his family in a cozy house where Mama worked in her home office. The office had a big wooden desk, a comfy blue chair, and shelves filled with colorful books.

One busy Monday morning, Mama sat at her desk looking very worried. Papers were scattered everywhere like fallen leaves, and her computer screen showed lots of important work.

"Oh dear," Mama sighed, rubbing her forehead. "I have so much to do today, but everything is such a mess!"

Momo swung down from his favorite perch on the bookshelf. His little heart felt sad seeing Mama so stressed. "What's wrong, Mama?" he asked in his sweet, squeaky voice.

"I need to finish this big project for work," Mama explained. "But I can't find my important papers, my desk is cluttered, and I don't know where to start!"

Momo's ears perked up. He wanted to help! But instead of jumping in right away, the smart little monkey remembered what Grandpa Monkey had taught him: "Always listen first, then make a plan."

"Tell me more, Mama," Momo said, climbing onto her lap. "What papers do you need?"

Mama smiled at her thoughtful little monkey. "I need the blue folder with the drawings, the red notebook with my notes, and my special pen that writes in purple ink."

Momo listened carefully, nodding his furry head. Then he closed his eyes and made a plan in his mind. "I know! I can help you find everything and organize your desk!"

"That would be wonderful, sweetie," Mama said, giving him a gentle hug.

First, Momo looked under the desk. He found the blue folder hiding behind the wastepaper basket. "Found it!" he cheered, his tail wiggling with excitement.

Next, he searched the bookshelf, moving books carefully with his nimble fingers. Behind a thick dictionary, he discovered the red notebook. "Here's number two!" he announced proudly.

The purple pen was the trickiest to find. Momo looked high and low, sniffing with his little black nose. Finally, he spotted it rolling under the printer. He stretched his long arm and grabbed it. "Got it, Mama!"

But Momo wasn't finished helping yet. He saw that Mama's desk was still messy. "Can I help organize too?" he asked politely.

"Of course!" Mama said. "What's your plan?"

Momo thought carefully. "We can put all the pens in this cup, stack the papers in neat piles, and put the books back on the shelf!"

Working together, they sorted everything. Momo's quick hands helped stack papers while Mama decided what went where. Soon, the desk looked clean and organized, with everything in its proper place.

"Momo, you're such a wonderful helper!" Mama said, scooping him up for a big hug. "You listened to understand what I needed, made a smart plan, and worked so hard to help me."

Momo beamed with pride, his cheeks turning slightly pink. "I like helping you, Mama! It makes me feel happy inside, like warm sunshine in my tummy!"

Now Mama could work peacefully at her organized desk, and Momo felt proud knowing he had made a real difference. He learned that the best way to help others is to listen carefully, think of a good plan, and work together with love.

From that day on, whenever someone needed help, Momo remembered to listen first, plan second, and then lend a helping hand.

The End.
