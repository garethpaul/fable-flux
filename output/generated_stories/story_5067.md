# Koala Kori Tries Something New

Kori was a calm koala with soft gray fur and a gentle smile. One sunny afternoon, Kori visited a friend’s house. “You can nap in the guest room,” said Mia, a cheerful mouse with bright eyes. “It smells like lemon and clean sheets!”

Kori stepped into the guest room. The curtains were light and fluttery, and the room felt cool and quiet. A small blue fan hummed softly. The blanket on the bed was fluffy and warm. On the bedside table sat a glass of water with tiny lemon slices and a little plate of crunchy carrot sticks.

“Mmm, it smells fresh,” Kori said, taking a deep breath.

Mia pointed to a small basket. “I put some new things in here. They help me feel calm and healthy.”

Kori peered into the basket. There was a lavender pillow pouch, a pair of soft ear covers, and a tiny green bottle. The label said, “Eucalyptus.” Kori’s ears perked up. “I don’t know these things,” Kori whispered. “I always nap on my own blankie.”

Mia smiled kindly. “You can try them if you like. Or you can use your blankie. It’s your choice!”

Kori felt a little tight in the tummy. New things can feel strange. “No, thank you,” Kori said quickly. “I don’t need any of that.” Kori pulled the old blankie from a backpack and curled up on the bed.

But the lemon water looked sparkly. The blanket felt a little too warm. The fan sounded a bit too buzzy. Kori turned this way and that way. “I can’t settle,” Kori sighed.

Mia peeked in. “Kori, are you okay?”

“I wanted a good nap,” Kori said, rubbing sleepy eyes. “But I feel wiggly.”

Mia nodded. “Sometimes new rooms feel different. When that happens to me, I try one new thing at a time. It helps my body feel well.”

Kori blinked. “One thing at a time?”

“Yes,” said Mia. “A tiny try. If you don’t like it, you stop.”

Kori thought and thought. Being open to new things can be scary. But being stuck felt yucky too. “Okay,” Kori said slowly. “I can be open-minded. I will try one tiny thing.”

Mia’s whiskers wiggled. “Great! Would you like the ear covers? They make the buzzing softer.”

Kori touched the soft ear covers. They felt like cloudy marshmallows. “I will try,” Kori said. Kori slid them on. The fan’s hum turned into a gentle “whooo.” Kori’s shoulders dropped. “Oh,” Kori said. “That is nice.”

Next, Kori pointed to the lavender pouch. “What about this?”

“That is for smelling,” Mia said. “Just a little sniff.”

Kori held the pouch and took a tiny sniff. The smell was soft and sweet, like purple flowers in the evening. Kori’s tummy felt less tight. “I like it,” Kori whispered.

Mia smiled. “Would you like a sip of lemon water? It can be refreshing.”

Kori tasted the water. It was cool, with a tickly, happy zip on the tongue. “Zing!” Kori giggled. “That woke me up a little.”

Kori looked at the green bottle. “Euca… lyptus?”

“It’s a plant smell,” Mia said. “I put one drop on my wrist and breathe in slow. But only if you want to.”

Kori thought about being open-minded. “I can try one tiny drop.” Mia put a dot on Kori’s wrist. Kori took a slow breath in and out. The smell was crisp, like fresh leaves after rain. Kori felt clearer and calm.

“I said no before,” Kori told Mia softly. “I didn’t listen. I was not open. I am sorry.”

Mia patted Kori’s paw. “Thank you for saying sorry. That was kind. You’re trying new things, and that is brave.”

Kori climbed back onto the bed. The blanket now felt cozy. The ear covers made the room gentle. The lavender and eucalyptus smells wrapped Kori like soft air. Kori’s eyes grew heavy. “This guest room is nice,” Kori murmured.

“Rest well,” Mia whispered, dimming the light.

When Kori woke up, the sun had moved, and the room glowed gold. Kori stretched and smiled. “That was a good nap.”

Mia came in with a soft step. “How do you feel?”

“Calm and happy,” Kori said. “Thank you for sharing your wellness basket. I learned that trying new things, one by one, can help. And when I’m not sure, I can be open-minded.”

Mia nodded. “And you made amends by saying sorry and by listening. That keeps friendships strong.”

Kori picked up the basket and placed it neatly back on the table. “I will remember to be open next time,” Kori said. “Maybe I’ll even bring a tiny basket to share with you.”

Mia clapped. “I would love that!”

They both giggled. The guest room smelled like flowers and clean sheets. The fan whispered. The lemon water sparkled. Kori felt peaceful inside and out.

Kori looked at Mia. “Being open-minded helped my body and my heart.”

“It helps wellness,” Mia said. “Little tries can do big things.”

Kori nodded. “Little tries, big calm.”

The friends hugged, happy and warm.

The End.
