# Bubbles Says “I’m Sorry”

In a quiet study corner, soft light made warm puddles on the floor. A little lamp hummed. Colorful maps showed oceans, mountains, and many flags. Crayons, paper, and a big seashell pencil cup waited on a small desk. The air smelled like crayons and clean paper.

Bubbles, a playful dolphin, was visiting for the day. He wiggled his tail and clicked with happy chatter. His smooth gray skin sparkled like tiny raindrops. “Eee-eee! Let’s learn about the world!” he squeaked. He loved to splash and play, but he also loved to learn new things.

On the desk was a map of the world. “Look,” said Bubbles, pointing with his fin. “There is the big blue ocean where I live. So much water!” He pressed his fin a little too hard. The map slid and bumped the seashell pencil cup.

Clatter! The cup tipped. Pencils rolled like tiny logs. A bright green crayon snapped in two. A glue stick plopped onto the floor. Bubbles’ eyes went wide. His heart thumped like soft drumbeats in his chest.

“Oh no,” whispered Bubbles. He felt hot and wiggly. “I didn’t mean to. I was excited.” He wanted to hide under the desk. He wanted to jump back into the sea. He wanted the mess to go away.

A gentle voice came from the doorway. “I heard a clatter. Is everything okay?” It was Teacher Aya, with kind eyes and a calm smile. She wore a scarf with little fish from many countries on it.

Bubbles looked at the broken crayon. He looked at Teacher Aya. His voice was very small. “I made a mess. I… I’m sorry.”

“Thank you for telling me,” said Teacher Aya. Her voice was soft like a blanket. “Saying ‘I’m sorry’ is brave. It shows a strong heart.”

Bubbles felt a tiny warm spark in his chest. Brave? Could he be brave and playful at the same time? He took a breath. “I bumped the cup. I will help fix it.”

“That is a good plan,” said Teacher Aya. “Let’s clean up together.”

They picked up pencils. Tap, tap, tap went the pencils as they slid back into the cup. Bubbles held the broken crayon pieces. The wax felt bumpy and a little sticky. “I broke it,” he said. “Can it still color?”

“Let’s try,” said Teacher Aya. She wrapped a small paper band around the pieces. Snap, snug. “Now it is two crayons. See? One problem, two tools.”

Bubbles smiled. “Two! I like that.” He tested them on the map’s edge. Scritch, scritch. The green made a bright, happy line. He giggled and did a dolphin click. “Eee-eee! It still works!”

Teacher Aya nodded. “When we make a mistake, we can fix it, learn, and keep going. That is called resilience.”

Bubbles rolled the word around like a marble. “Re-sil-ience,” he said slowly. “It feels strong.”

“It is,” said Teacher Aya. “And your apology was kind. When we say ‘I’m sorry,’ we help hearts feel better.”

Bubbles looked at the map again. “Can we keep learning about the world?” he asked. “I want to know where other dolphins swim and where children live.”

Teacher Aya pointed to the map. “Here is a warm ocean where dolphins leap. Here is a cold sea with big ice. Here is a busy city. Here is a quiet village. People say hello in many ways. In my country, we say ‘Salaam.’ In others, they say ‘Hola,’ ‘Bonjour,’ or ‘Ni Hao.’ And when we make a mistake, we can say ‘I’m sorry,’ or ‘Lo siento,’ or ‘Pardon,’ or ‘Duìbùqǐ.’”

Bubbles tried the words carefully. “Lo see-en-to. Par-don. Dway-boo-chee.” He giggled. “My mouth feels wiggly!”

“It’s fun to try,” said Teacher Aya. “It shows care for others.”

Bubbles puffed a tiny water mist from his blowhole—just a little, like a sneeze. He glanced at the desk. “Oops,” he said, and quickly wiped the drops with a soft cloth. “I’m sorry, desk. I will keep you dry.”

Teacher Aya smiled. “You are learning to notice and fix. That builds self-confidence.”

Bubbles felt tall, even though he was a small dolphin in a small study corner. “I can learn. I can say sorry. I can keep trying,” he said. His voice was stronger now.

They colored the map together. Blue for oceans. Green for forests. Yellow for sunny deserts. Red for warm roofs. Bubbles listened to the scritch-scratch of crayons and the gentle hum of the lamp. He smelled the waxy crayons and the faint salt on his own skin. The room felt peaceful again.

When they finished, Bubbles held up the map. “Look! Our world is bright,” he said. “I want to be kind in every place.”

Teacher Aya nodded. “That is the best adventure. Kind words and brave hearts can travel anywhere.”

Bubbles turned to the pencil cup. He patted it gently. “I’m sorry I bumped you,” he told it with a grin. “Thank you for still holding our pencils.”

Then he looked at Teacher Aya. “Thank you for helping me fix my mistake.”

“You did it,” she said. “You apologized. You helped. You learned. That is a big win.”

Bubbles clicked happily. “Eee-eee! Next time I will touch the map softly. And if I forget, I will say ‘I’m sorry’ and try again.”

The End.
