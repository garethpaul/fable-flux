# Benny Beaver's Moving Day

Benny Beaver woke up early in his cozy dining room home. The morning sun streamed through the window, making the wooden table sparkle like gold. Today was a very special day - Benny was going to rearrange all his furniture!

"Good morning, dining room!" Benny said cheerfully, stretching his little paws high above his head. "Today we're going to move and dance and make everything perfect!"

Benny looked around at all the heavy chairs and the big wooden table. His tail twitched with excitement. "This is going to be so much fun!" he squeaked.

First, Benny tried to push the biggest chair. He put his tiny paws against the smooth wood and pushed with all his might. "Nnnngh!" he grunted. The chair didn't budge even a little bit.

"Hmm," Benny said, scratching his furry head. "Maybe I need to warm up my muscles first!"

Benny began to march in place, lifting his knees high. "Left, right, left, right!" he chanted. His little heart started beating faster, and he felt warm and tingly all over. Then he did jumping jacks, his arms and legs moving like a happy star.

"Much better!" Benny puffed, feeling stronger already.

He tried the chair again. This time, it moved just a tiny bit! "Hooray!" Benny cheered. But then he realized he needed to move it much, much further.

Benny's whiskers drooped. "This is harder than I thought," he sighed. He felt frustrated and wanted to give up. The chair seemed so big, and he felt so small.

But then Benny remembered what his mama always said: "When something feels too hard, take a deep breath and try a different way."

Benny took three deep breaths. "Whoosh, whoosh, whoosh," went the air in and out of his nose. He felt calmer already.

"Maybe I can push it little by little," Benny said thoughtfully. He pushed the chair just one inch. "One!" he counted. Then another inch. "Two!" Soon he was counting and pushing, counting and pushing.

"This is like a fun game!" Benny giggled. His muscles felt strong from all the exercise, and his heart felt happy from counting.

After moving the chair, Benny felt so proud! His arms and legs felt tingly and strong. "Exercise makes me feel like a superhero!" he declared.

Next came the small table. Benny did more jumping jacks to warm up, then pushed it carefully across the smooth floor. It made a satisfying "scritch-scratch" sound as it moved.

When Benny finished arranging everything, he stood in the middle of his dining room and spun around slowly. The late afternoon sun made everything glow orange and cozy.

"Look how beautiful everything looks!" Benny said, his eyes sparkling with joy. "And look how strong my body feels!"

Benny's muscles felt pleasantly tired, like after a good day of playing. His heart was still beating a little fast, and he felt proud and accomplished.

"Thank you, body, for being so strong!" Benny said, patting his arms and legs. "Thank you, dining room, for being my perfect home! And thank you, exercise, for making me feel so wonderful!"

As the stars began to twinkle outside, Benny curled up in his newly arranged dining room. He had learned that exercise made him stronger, that taking breaks helped when things felt hard, and that his body could do amazing things when he took care of it.

"Tomorrow," Benny yawned happily, "I'll do more moving and stretching. My body loves to exercise!"

The End.
