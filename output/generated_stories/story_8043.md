# Panda Bao Says Sorry

Panda Bao was a patient panda. Bao loved two things: soft music and bouncy balls. Today, Bao padded into the musical practice room. The room was warm and cozy. It smelled like polished wood. Golden drums sat in a row. Shiny bells tinkled on a shelf. A big window let in sunshine.

“Hello, room,” Bao whispered. “I am ready to practice.”

Bao picked up a small drum. Tap-tap, tap-tap! The sound was gentle, like rain on leaves. Bao smiled. “I can keep a beat,” Bao said. “Beat means the steady thump that helps music move.”

Bao heard a squeak. It was little Mei the mouse. Mei wore tiny sneakers. She pushed a bright red ball. “Hi, Bao!” squeaked Mei. “I’m practicing sports. I’m learning to dribble.”

“Dribble means bounce-bounce with control,” Bao explained. “Can I hear your dribble?”

Mei nodded. Bounce. Bounce. Bounce. The red ball tapped the floor like a quick drum. “I can count in two languages!” Mei said proudly. “Listen! ‘One, two, three!’ and also ‘Uno, dos, tres!’”

Bao’s ears perked up. “That is language appreciation. We like words from many places,” Bao said. “Words help us share and care.”

Just then, Kit the kitten peeked in. Kit wore a blue scarf and hummed. “Is this the music room?” asked Kit. “I want to sing. I can sing ‘hello’ in many languages. Hello! Hola! Nǐ hǎo!”

“Come in,” said Bao. “We can make music, words, and sports all together.”

Bao placed the small drum on a stand. Tap-tap, tap-tap. Mei dribbled the ball. Bounce-bounce. Kit sang softly. La-la, hola, nǐ hǎo! The sounds blended like a friendly soup. The beat was steady. Bao felt proud.

Then, something whooshy happened. Mei bounced the ball extra high. “Oops!” The ball zoomed—boing!—and bumped Bao’s drum. The drum wobbled. The drum clattered to the floor. CLANG!

The room got very quiet. Bao’s heart thumped. The shiny drum now had a tiny dent. Bao’s eyes felt wet.

Mei froze. Kit’s tail puffed. “Oh no,” whispered Kit.

Mei looked down. Her whiskers drooped. “I didn’t mean to,” she squeaked. Mei took a little breath and remembered what Bao always taught. Be patient. Use kind words.

Mei rolled the red ball to Bao. “Bao,” she said, “I am sorry. I bounced too high. I bumped your drum. Are you okay?”

Bao held the drum gently. Bao took a slow breath in. Bao let a slow breath out. Bao felt the warm room again. Bao felt the soft drum skin under panda paws.

“Thank you for your apology,” Bao said. “Apology means saying sorry and trying to make it better.”

Mei nodded. “I am sorry,” she said again. “Lo siento,” she added softly. “That is ‘I’m sorry’ in Spanish.” Mei looked up. “How can I help?”

Kit stepped closer. “Duìbuqǐ,” Kit said. “That is ‘sorry’ in Chinese. I can help, too.”

Bao’s ears lifted. The words sounded like little hugs. “Your words are kind,” said Bao. “Let’s fix the drum. Then we can practice safe sports here.”

They found a soft cloth. Wipe, wipe. Bao smoothed the drum. The tiny dent did not stop the sound. Tap-tap. It still sounded like rain on leaves.

Bao smiled at Mei. “Next time, we can set a line for dribbling,” Bao suggested. “A safe space. Sports need rules.”

Mei nodded fast. “Yes! A dribble line.” She placed a strip of blue tape on the floor. “I will stay behind the line,” she said. “I will keep control.”

Kit purred. “I can sing a gentle beat word to help: ‘Soft, soft, soft.’ In Spanish: ‘Suave, suave, suave.’ In Chinese: ‘Qīng, qīng, qīng.’”

Bao tapped the drum. Tap—soft. Tap—soft. Mei bounced the ball within the blue line. Bounce—soft. Bounce—soft. Kit sang the gentle words. The three friends smiled.

“Listen,” said Bao. “Music, words, and sports can play together. We can use our bodies, our voices, and our hearts.”

Mei looked at Bao. “Thank you for being patient,” she said. “I learned to say sorry, and I learned to make it better.”

Bao nodded. “Saying sorry is brave,” Bao said. “We also fix what we can, and we learn new ways.”

They practiced a little song to remember:

Bao tapped the drum. Tap-tap.
Mei bounced the ball. Bounce-bounce.
Kit sang:

“Sorry means I care.
I stop. I fix. I share.
Hello, hola, nǐ hǎo, too,
Kind words help me and you.”

The room felt bright again. The sun made the bells shine. The red ball thumped softly like a sleepy heartbeat. Bao felt calm. Mei felt proud. Kit felt cozy.

At the end, Bao said, “What did we learn today?”

Mei raised a tiny paw. “I learned to say sorry and to play safe with a line.”

Kit swished a tail. “I learned new words in two languages. I learned that music and sports can be friends.”

Bao hugged them both. “I learned that patient hearts help friends grow,” Bao said.

They packed up. The drum rested on its stand. The red ball sat by the blue tape. The friends waved.

“Good-bye! Adiós! Zàijiàn!” they called.

They left the musical practice room, ready for tomorrow’s gentle beat.

The End.
