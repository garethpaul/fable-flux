# The Little Song in the Guest Room

In a sunny house, there was a cozy guest room with soft pillows, a big window, and a tiny plant on the sill. The curtains were light and swishy. When the wind blew, they made a quiet whoosh. In this room lived a singing bird named Pip.

Pip was small and bright. His feathers were the color of lemons, and his eyes sparkled like morning dew. Pip loved to sing. He sang when the sun woke up. He sang when the moon said good night. He sang little songs like, “Tweet, tweet, tra-la-la!”

One day, Nana came to visit. Nana smiled and patted the pillows. “What a lovely guest room,” she said. “I will rest here.”

Pip fluttered near the window. “Hello, Nana! I’m Pip. I love to sing in this room.”

Nana laughed softly. “Hello, Pip. I love your friendly face.”

Morning came. The room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. The sunlight felt warm on Pip’s feathers. Pip puffed his chest and began, “Tweet, tweet, tra-la-la!” His voice filled the room like bubble music.

Nana rubbed her eyes. “Oh my,” she said. “Your song is sweet, Pip, but I am still sleepy. My eyes feel heavy. Could you sing a little later?”

Pip stopped mid-note. His heart felt squishy. “But I always sing now,” he said. “This is my morning song.”

Nana nodded kindly. “I understand. Your song makes you happy. Rest makes me happy. Maybe we can find a way that works for both of us.”

Pip blinked. He had never tried that before. “A way for both?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Nana. “It is called a compromise. We listen to each other. We find a plan together.”

Pip looked at the clock. Tick-tock. He looked at the window. The sky was bright but not too bright. He took a deep breath. He liked to learn new things. “I can try,” he said. “I can grow.”

Nana smiled. “Let’s think.”

They both sat very still. The curtains made a soft whoosh. The pillow smelled like lavender. Pip could hear Nana’s calm breath. In and out. In and out.

Pip said, “Maybe I can sing softly first.”

Nana said, “Maybe I can rest for ten more minutes.”

Pip thought. “I can chirp quietly while you rest. Then, when you wake, I can sing my big song outside the door.”

Nana clapped her hands softly. “That sounds like a good plan. We are coordinating. We are working together.”

Pip smiled. His tail feathers wiggled. “We are compromising. We are sharing the room.”

Nana set a little timer. It ticked like a tiny beetle. Pip made soft chirps, “twee…twee…” The soft sounds felt like warm blanket sounds. Nana closed her eyes and rested.

When the timer went ding, Nana opened her eyes and stretched. “Ahh,” she said. “I feel rested.”

Pip fluttered to the doorway. He peeked out into the hall. The light there was bright and wide. “Now it is time!” he cheered.

Nana stood and followed him to the doorway. “I am ready to listen,” she said.

Pip took a big breath. He sang, “Tweet, tweet, tra-la-la! La-la-la, tweet!” His voice sounded bright and happy. It bounced down the hall and back into the guest room like gentle echoes. Nana swayed to the beat. Her slippers made a soft shuffle on the rug.

After the song, Nana giggled. “That was lovely. Thank you for waiting.”

Pip felt proud. His chest was warm. “Thank you for resting,” he said. “We made a plan together.”

Later that day, they played a game. Pip stacked soft blocks near the bed. Nana helped by holding the base steady. “Coordination,” Nana said. “I hold. You stack. We do it together.”

Pip nodded. “Together is better.”

When the tower wobbled, Pip took a breath. “I can try again,” he said. “I can learn.” He moved one block to the middle. He tried once more. The tower stood tall.

Nana gave a happy hum. “That is a growth mindset. You keep trying. You learn from little wobbles.”

Pip chirped. “I can grow. I can listen. I can share.”

That evening, the guest room was quiet and cozy again. The lamp glowed like a candle. The plant smelled fresh and green. Pip settled on the window sill. Nana tucked in with a soft blanket.

Pip whispered, “Nana, will we use our plan tomorrow?”

Nana nodded. “Yes, dear Pip. We will compromise again. You sing softly first. I rest a little. Then you sing big by the door. We coordinate and care.”

Pip smiled. “That makes my heart sing.”

Nana smiled back. “And that helps me rest.”

They both felt happy. They both felt heard. In the gentle light, Pip hummed a tiny good-night tune, “hmm…hmm…tweet…” It was as soft as a feather.

The next morning, they used their plan again. It worked! Pip sang softly. Nana rested. Then Pip sang big by the door. Together, they made the guest room a friendly place.

Pip learned that compromise is kind. When we listen and share, everyone can smile. Pip felt brave and proud. He could grow, he could try, and he could work with a friend.

The End.
