# Ollie's Garden Helper Routine

Ollie the otter loved to splash and play all day long. He lived near a beautiful balcony garden filled with colorful flowers, green vegetables, and buzzing bees. The garden belonged to Mrs. Chen, a kind lady who watered her plants every morning.

"Splash, splash, splash!" Ollie would jump in his little pool, sending water droplets flying everywhere. Sometimes the water landed on Mrs. Chen's tomatoes. Sometimes it splashed on her pretty petunias.

"Oh my!" said Mrs. Chen one morning, looking at her droopy flowers. "My plants need just the right amount of water, not too much and not too little."

Ollie heard Mrs. Chen talking and felt sad. His whiskers drooped down. "Did I hurt the pretty flowers?" he asked himself, looking at the wet leaves.

Mrs. Chen noticed Ollie looking worried. "Hello there, little otter," she said gently. "I see you love to play in the water."

"I do!" said Ollie, his dark eyes sparkling. "But I think my splashing made your plants too wet. I'm sorry!"

Mrs. Chen smiled. "You know what, Ollie? I have an idea. Would you like to help me take care of my garden?"

Ollie's tail wiggled with excitement. "Yes, yes! But how can I help?"

"Well," said Mrs. Chen, "plants need water every day, just like you need food every day. We could make a special routine together."

"What's a routine?" asked Ollie, tilting his furry head.

"A routine is when we do the same helpful things at the same time each day," explained Mrs. Chen. "It helps us remember important jobs."

Mrs. Chen showed Ollie a small watering can. "Every morning after breakfast, we can water the plants together. You can help me remember which ones need water."

Ollie clapped his little paws. "That sounds fun! When do we start?"

"Tomorrow morning," said Mrs. Chen. "But we need to practice being gentle with the water."

The next morning, Ollie woke up early. The sun was painting the sky pink and orange. He remembered his new job! "Time for our routine!" he called to Mrs. Chen.

Together, they walked around the balcony garden. Mrs. Chen showed Ollie how to touch the soil. "If it feels dry, the plant is thirsty," she explained.

Ollie learned to pour water slowly and carefully. "Gentle drops for the flowers," he said, watching the water trickle down. "Not big splashes like when I play!"

Day after day, Ollie and Mrs. Chen followed their morning routine. Ollie learned which plants needed more water and which needed less. The tomatoes grew bigger and redder. The flowers bloomed brighter and prettier.

"Look!" said Ollie one morning, pointing with his paw. "The sunflowers are as tall as me now!"

Mrs. Chen hugged Ollie gently. "That's because we take good care of them every single day. Our routine helps the plants stay healthy and happy."

Ollie felt proud and happy. He had learned that routines help important things get done. He still loved to splash and play, but now he had a special job too.

"Thank you for teaching me about routines," said Ollie. "Now I'm a garden helper AND a playful otter!"

From that day on, Ollie never forgot his morning routine. The garden grew more beautiful each day, and Ollie learned that helping others felt just as good as playing.

The End.
