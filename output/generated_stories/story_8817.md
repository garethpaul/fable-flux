# Ellie’s Big, Brave Brush

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and bright, curious eyes. She lived in a playful living room full of cozy pillows, soft rugs, and a big sunny window. On the floor was a big sheet of paper, smooth and white like a fluffy cloud. Little cups of paint sat nearby—red like strawberries, blue like the sky, yellow like sunshine, and green like fresh leaves. The room smelled like crayons and soap, and the music from a tiny radio hummed softly.

Ellie loved art. She held a chunky paintbrush in her trunk. It felt tickly and wet. Dip, swish, swish! went the brush. Splish, splash! went the paint. Ellie giggled. “I will paint my feelings,” she said. “I will paint my ideas too.”

Mama Elephant peeked in with a warm smile. “What a colorful plan, Ellie,” she said. “Remember to ask for help if you need it.”

Ellie nodded. Her heart felt big and brave. She started with blue swirls. “This is a windy day,” she whispered. She added yellow dots. “This is warm sunshine.” Then she made a big red circle. “This is my brave heart.” She patted the paper gently with her foot. The rug was squishy under her toes. The room felt safe and happy.

Just then, Ellie’s cousin, Max the monkey, hopped into the living room. Max loved to move and wiggle. “Hi, Ellie!” Max chattered. “Let’s make a super fast painting!” He grabbed a brush and whooshed it across the paper. Swoop! Splat! The paint splashed everywhere—on the paper, on the rug, and a tiny drop on Ellie’s ear.

Ellie blinked. She felt surprised. Her cheeks felt warm. “Oh,” she said softly. “I was making slow swirls. I was telling a story with colors.”

Max bounced. “Fast is fun!” he said. “Look! Boom, zoom, whoosh!” He scribbled green zigzags right over Ellie’s red circle.

Ellie’s tummy felt twisty. She wanted to speak up, but her voice felt small. She looked at her brave red circle, now hidden by zigzags. She took a deep breath. She remembered Mama’s words: Ask for help if you need it.

Ellie said, “Max, I like that you are fast. But I need slow right now.” Her voice was gentle, but strong. “I am painting a story. Can we take turns? Can I have space for my swirls?”

Max froze and tilted his head. “Take turns?” he asked.

“Yes,” Ellie said. “Art can be slow or fast. My art is slow. Your art is fast. Both are good. I need to say what I need.” She put her trunk on her chest and felt her brave heart.

Max scratched his head. “I didn’t know,” he said. “I just wanted to play.” He looked at the paint on Ellie’s ear. “I’m sorry, Ellie.”

Ellie smiled. “Thank you for saying sorry,” she said. “Would you like to make a fast page and a slow page?”

Mama Elephant came back with a soft towel. She dabbed Ellie’s ear. “I heard good words,” she said. “Ellie, you used your voice. Max, you listened. That is smart and kind.”

Together, they set out two big sheets of paper. One had a little sign: Fast Art! The other had a sign: Slow Art. Max clapped. “I will zoom over here,” he said. “Swoop! Swoop!” Ellie laughed. She felt the twisty feeling melt away.

Ellie dipped her brush. Swish, swish. She made gentle waves of blue again. She added yellow dots that looked like tiny suns. She drew her brave red circle back, even bigger this time. “Hello, brave heart,” she whispered. “I see you.”

Max made green zigzags on his paper. “Zippy zigzags!” he shouted, but not too loud. He looked over at Ellie. “How is your story?” he asked.

“It is growing,” Ellie said. “This blue is the windy day. This yellow is warm sunshine. This red is my brave heart. And this,” she added a soft green curve, “is a leaf that listens.”

“A leaf that listens?” Max asked.

Ellie nodded. “It means I listen to my feelings. I also listen to you.”

Max smiled. “I will listen too.” He made a small blue swirl on the corner of his fast page. “This is my calm swirl,” he said.

They painted and painted. The radio hummed. The living room smelled like fruity paint and crayons. The sun warmed their backs. The rug was still squishy, but now it had newspaper under it—Mama’s good idea. “Artists make a plan,” Mama said. “That is using knowledge.”

When they were done, Ellie and Max held up their pages. Max had zigzags and stars and speedy lines. Ellie had swirls and dots and a big red circle that glowed. Both pictures were bright and beautiful.

Ellie said, “I feel proud. I used my voice. I asked for space. I learned that slow is okay.”

Max said, “I feel proud too. I learned to listen. I learned that fast is okay, and slow is okay. Art is for feelings and ideas!”

Mama tapped her trunk. “What did we learn?” she asked.

Ellie said, “Art helps me share my story. I can speak up kindly when I need something.”

Max said, “I can ask, ‘Is this okay?’ before I paint on your page.”

Mama smiled. “That is self-advocacy and knowledge. You are thoughtful artists.”

They hung the pictures by the sunny window. The colors glowed like a rainbow. Ellie’s heart felt calm and strong. Max’s feet stopped wiggling for a moment as he stared at the art. The living room felt full of soft light and happy whispers.

Ellie whispered, “Thank you, art.”

Max whispered, “Thank you, words.”

The End.
