# Panda Poppy’s Treehouse School Day

Panda Poppy lived in a cozy treehouse that smelled like clean bamboo and sweet tea. The treehouse had soft rugs, bright paper lanterns, and a round window that showed the sunrise. Poppy was a very patient panda. She loved to learn, and she loved to share.

One sunny morning, Poppy clapped her paws. “Today I will make a little school in my treehouse,” she said. “We will learn about our big world. We will plan. We will listen. We will try together.”

Her friends climbed up the ladder. There was Mei the tiny monkey, Tiko the turtle, and Lila the ladybug. Their feet made tap-tap-tap sounds on the wooden floor. A gentle breeze brought the smell of pine and flowers.

“Welcome, friends!” Poppy smiled. “Please come in. Take a seat on the soft rug.”

Lila buzzed, “What will we learn today?”

Poppy held up a colorful map. “We will learn about greetings from different places. We will learn to say hello with care. We will also plan a snack that uses foods from many cultures.”

Mei’s eyes sparkled. “I like to say ni hao!”

Tiko nodded slowly. “I say jambo with my cousin.”

Poppy placed little cards in a neat row. Each card showed a flag and a word. “We will practice together. We will be patient. We will take turns.”

They began. Poppy said, “Hello.” Mei said, “Ni hao.” Tiko said, “Jambo.” Lila said, “Bonjour,” in a tiny whisper. Poppy listened, smiled, and clapped for each friend.

“Your words are beautiful,” Poppy said. “Each hello is like a warm hug.”

Mei bounced up and down. “Can I go again now?”

Poppy put a gentle paw on Mei’s shoulder. “Let us follow our plan. We take turns so we can hear every voice. Planning helps us learn.”

Mei took a deep breath and nodded. “Okay. I can wait.”

Next, Poppy rolled out a big paper schedule. It had pictures: a clock for time, a basket for snack, a drum for music, and a book for story. “This is our plan for the day,” she said. “We will work as a team. This is called coordination. We move together like a song.”

Lila pointed to the basket picture. “Snack time soon?”

“Yes,” Poppy said. “We will make a rainbow snack. We will try rice crackers from one place, sweet mango from another, and hummus from another. We will taste and talk.”

The friends washed their paws. The water felt cool and splishy-splashy. The soap smelled like oranges. Poppy laid out little plates. The crackers made a soft crunch, the mango was juicy and bright, and the hummus was smooth and creamy.

Tiko took a tiny bite. “Mmm. New foods can be friendly.”

Mei giggled. “The mango drips on my chin!”

Poppy wiped Mei’s chin with a napkin. “Trying new things is part of learning,” she said. “We can be brave and curious.”

After snack, Poppy brought out small scarves. “We will do a hello dance,” she said. “When you hear a word, wave your scarf and make a kind move.”

Poppy called, “Hello!” The scarves swished like soft rain. “Ni hao!” The scarves fluttered like butterflies. “Jambo!” The scarves bounced like happy frogs. “Bonjour!” The scarves twirled like tiny tops.

Everyone laughed. The treehouse echoed with little steps and gentle swirls. The lanterns swayed and made soft rustling sounds.

Then, oops! Mei jumped too fast and bumped the basket. Crackers spilled. Hummus tipped. A little splat landed on Poppy’s paw.

Mei’s eyes grew round. “I’m sorry,” she whispered. Her tail drooped.

Poppy took a slow breath. She felt sticky hummus on her fur. Then she smiled softly. “Thank you for saying sorry, Mei. Accidents happen. I forgive you.”

Mei’s eyes brightened. “I will help clean.”

Poppy nodded. “That is part of our plan too—helping. We learn from mistakes.”

They worked together. Tiko held the dustpan. Lila brushed crumbs into it with tiny wings. Mei wiped the floor. Poppy washed her paw. Soon the treehouse looked neat again, and it smelled fresh like pine.

Poppy gathered her friends. “What did we learn today?”

Mei raised her hand. “We learned to wait our turn and follow the plan.”

Tiko added, “We learned to try new foods and say hello in many ways.”

Lila chimed in, “We learned to move together. That is coordination.”

Poppy nodded. “And we learned about forgiveness. When we make a mistake, we say sorry. We help. We try again.”

The sun made warm golden shapes on the rug. Poppy rolled up the schedule and tucked it away. “Education means learning every day,” she said. “We learn with hearts, with hands, and with minds. We learn about the world and about each other.”

Mei smiled. “Can we do treehouse school again tomorrow?”

Poppy hugged her friends. The hug felt cozy and safe. “Yes,” she said. “We will make a new plan. We will listen and share. We will learn with love.”

They waved their scarves one more time. “Hello, world!” they cheered.

The End.
