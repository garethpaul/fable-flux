# Buzzy Beaver’s Big Dinner Helper

In a cozy log cabin by a gentle river, a busy beaver named Buzzy padded into the dining room. The room smelled warm and yummy, like baked apples and sweet corn. The long wooden table felt smooth under Buzzy’s little paws. Plates, cups, and shiny forks waited in a jumble.

Buzzy’s tail thumped—thump, thump! “I want to help,” Buzzy said, whiskers twitching. “I can set the table all by myself!”

Mama Beaver smiled and wiped her paws on a checkered cloth. “That’s brave, Buzzy,” she said softly. “Our family dinner is special. We share our traditions, and we all help. Would you like to try?”

Buzzy nodded. “Yes! I can do it. I am a strong helper.”

Papa Beaver came in carrying a warm pot that smelled like herbs and carrots. Steam curled up, making little white clouds. “Careful, Buzzy,” Papa said. “The pot is hot.” Papa set it far from Buzzy’s paws.

Buzzy took a deep breath. “I can set the plates,” Buzzy told the room, voice small but bold.

Buzzy lifted a plate. It was cool and smooth. “One… two… three…” Buzzy counted gently, lining them around the table. But then, oh no! A fork clinked to the floor. “Clink!” It made Buzzy jump.

Buzzy’s ears drooped. “Maybe I can’t do it,” Buzzy whispered, heart thumping like a tiny drum.

Mama knelt beside Buzzy and patted the big, flat tail. “Everyone drops forks sometimes,” she said. “Bravery is trying again.”

Buzzy took another breath. “I will try again.” Buzzy picked up the fork and placed it next to a plate. “Fork on the left?” Buzzy asked, eyebrows scrunching.

Mama smiled. “In our family tradition, we put forks on the left and spoons on the right. We also place a tiny twig with each napkin to share where we come from.”

Buzzy’s eyes lit up. “A twig tradition! I want to do the twigs!”

Papa brought a bundle of sweet-smelling twigs from the riverbank. They smelled like rain and sunshine. “Here you go, helper,” he said.

Buzzy folded napkins, soft like clouds, and tucked a twig in each one. “Tuck, tuck, tuck,” Buzzy sang. Soon the table looked friendly and neat.

Then Buzzy noticed something else. “Mama likes the blue cup,” Buzzy said, paw on chin. “Papa likes the striped one. I like the tiny cup with stars.” Buzzy wanted to put the star cup in the biggest spot, right in the middle, so everyone could see it.

Papa peeked. “That is a fun idea,” he said. “But Mama likes her cup next to her chair.”

Buzzy’s whiskers wiggled. “What if we share?” Buzzy asked. “We can put my star cup in the center with flowers inside, like a little river star! Then everyone can see it.”

Mama clapped softly. “That is a smart compromise,” she said. “We can use your idea and keep our seats the same.”

Buzzy’s chest felt warm and bright. “I had a good idea,” Buzzy whispered to the twigs. “I can do this.”

Buzzy tip-tapped to the kitchen and bravely lifted the small star cup. The cup was smooth and cool. Buzzy filled it with tiny yellow flowers that smelled like honey. Back in the dining room, Buzzy set the star cup in the center. The flowers smiled up at everyone.

“Beautiful,” Papa said.

Just then, the door creaked and Beaver Grandma and Beaver Grandpa shuffled in, paws full of a sweet pie. The pie smelled like cinnamon and love. “Hello, little helper,” Grandma said. “Are we ready?”

Buzzy looked at the table. Plates lined up. Forks on the left. Spoons on the right. Napkins with twigs tucked just so. The star cup with flowers in the center. Everything looked bright and welcoming.

Buzzy’s heart fluttered. “I did it,” Buzzy said, cheeks warm. “I set the table!”

Grandpa’s whiskers twinkled. “I see brave paws here,” he said. “And careful eyes. And a kind heart.”

Buzzy gulped. “I was scared when the fork fell,” Buzzy admitted. “But I tried again. I asked for help. And we made a compromise.”

Mama hugged Buzzy gently. “That shows self-confidence,” she said. “You believed you could learn. You were brave to try. And you listened to others, too.”

Everyone sat down. The chairs made little squeaks. The pie went “mmmmm” in the air. Papa scooped carrot stew. The bowls felt warm in Buzzy’s paws. They all held paws around the table.

Grandma spoke softly. “In our family, we share food, stories, and little twigs to remember our river home.”

Buzzy held up a twig. It smelled like the quiet river at night. “I want to share my story,” Buzzy said. “I was a busy beaver today. I made mistakes. I tried again. I made a plan with my family. I can help. I can learn.”

Everyone smiled.

Buzzy sat taller, tail thumping a happy beat. “I believe in me,” Buzzy said.

Mama nodded. “And we believe in you, too.”

They ate, they laughed, and the flowers in the star cup seemed to glow.

The lesson was clear: Be brave, try again, and believe in yourself. Ask for help and make room for others’ ideas. When we work together, our hearts feel big and strong.

The End.
