# Brave Bunny in the Garden Shed

The morning sun peeked into the garden shed through a dusty window. The air smelled like fresh soil and wooden boxes. A tiny drip-drip from a leaky tap made a gentle song. In the corner, a determined rabbit named Pip twitched his soft nose and listened.

“I can do it today,” Pip whispered to himself. His ears stood tall. His fur felt warm and cozy, and his heart beat a brave little thump-thump. “I can reach the crunchy carrot basket.”

The carrot basket sat on a high shelf. It was full of orange carrots and green tops. Pip loved carrots, but he loved something even more today—believing in himself.

He hopped closer and looked up. “Oh,” he said softly. “It is high.” His tummy fluttered like a butterfly. For a moment, Pip wanted to hop away and hide under the rake. Then he took a slow breath in and out.

“Breathe in like a flower. Blow out like a candle,” Pip said. He had learned this to help his body feel calm. “I can try. I can learn. I am brave.”

On the floor sat things the gardener used: a little stool, a soft coiled hose, a pair of gloves, and a stack of seed packets. Pip’s whiskers tickled as he sniffed each one. The gloves smelled like grass. The stool felt smooth. The hose was squishy and cool.

Pip made a plan. “First, I will make a path,” he said. “Strategy time!” He nudged the stool under the shelf. He pushed the gloves to the side so they would not slip under his feet. He tapped the stool with his paw. “Tap, tap. Is it steady?” The stool did not wobble.

He tried to hop up, but his back legs felt wiggly. “Oh no,” he squeaked. “What if I fall?” His ears drooped.

Then Pip remembered his own kind voice. “I can talk to myself like a friend,” he said. He put a paw on his chest. “Pip, you are learning. You can try again. You can move slowly.”

He took another flower-candle breath. He wiggled his toes. He felt his legs get strong. Hop! Pip landed on the stool. His paws made a tiny thup sound on the wood.

From the window, a gentle breeze brushed his fur. The shed smelled like carrots and sunshine dust. Pip smiled. “Hello, calm body,” he said. “Hello, brave heart.”

The basket was still a little too high. “Hmm,” Pip hummed. “New plan.” He looked around and saw the seed packets. The packets were thin but stiff. He slid two under one leg of the stool to make it just a bit taller and level. Tap, tap. He tested it again. No wobble.

“Smart thinking,” Pip told himself. “That is good strategy.”

He stood tall and reached up. His paw touched the edge of the basket. “Almost!” he giggled. He felt proud. He wasn’t at the top yet, but he had come so far.

Just then, a tiny spider peeped from a web in the corner. “Hello, Pip,” she said in a whispery voice. “You are careful and brave.”

“Thank you,” Pip said, eyes bright. “I am trying one step at a time.”

“One silk at a time is how I make my web,” the spider said. “You are doing the same.”

Pip nodded. He bent his knees a little, counted, “One… two… three,” and made a gentle hop. His paw hooked the basket handle. The basket wiggled. Pip froze and breathed. Steady. Steady. He eased the basket closer, inch by inch, until it rested on the shelf edge.

With both paws, Pip tugged. The basket slid into his arms. He lowered it onto the stool, then to the floor. Plop! The carrots shone bright and orange like tiny suns.

Pip’s ears popped up with joy. He did a happy little hop-hop-hop. “I did it!” he cheered. He took a small carrot and crunched. Crunch, crunch. It tasted sweet and fresh. He chewed slowly and felt warm from nose to tail.

He saved some carrots for later and washed his paws under the drip-drip tap. The cool water tickled. “Taking care of my body helps my brave,” he said. He ate a leaf of carrot top, green and crisp, and stretched his legs. Long stretch. Big yawn. His body felt strong and well.

The spider lowered beside him on a silver thread. “What did you learn, Pip?” she asked.

Pip smiled and patted the stool. “I learned to be kind to myself,” he said. “I used a plan. I moved slowly. I listened to my breath. When I felt scared, I said, ‘I can try.’ And I did! One step at a time.”

The spider nodded. “That is good web-building for the heart.”

Pip tucked two carrots into a tiny sack made from an old glove finger. “Next time,” he said, “I will try something new again. I am small, but I am strong inside.”

The sun made a warm square on the floor. Pip curled up in the light, feeling safe, proud, and calm. Outside, birds chirped. Inside, the shed hummed with quiet peace.

Pip closed his eyes and whispered, “I believe in me.”

The End.
