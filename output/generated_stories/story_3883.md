# Milo Mouse’s Morning in the Guest Room

Milo was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright button eyes. He lived in a cozy house with a sunny guest room. Every morning, Milo followed his little routine. It helped him feel calm and ready.

“Good morning, sunshine,” Milo whispered as light peeked through the curtains. The room smelled like clean sheets and a hint of lavender. The blanket felt warm on his paws.

Step one, Milo thought. “Stretch.” He reached up high, wiggled his whiskers, and took a deep breath. He loved the soft rustle of the curtains as the breeze said hello.

Step two. “Make the bed,” he said. He patted the tiny pillow and smoothed the blanket. Pat, pat, smooth, smooth. It made a gentle swish sound. “All tidy!”

Step three. “Brush, brush,” Milo sang. He brushed his teeth with a tiny toothbrush near the little sink in the guest room. “Minty!” he giggled, tasting cool bubbles.

Step four. “Good morning, world.” Milo opened his window just a little. He could hear birds singing, “Tweet-tweet!” He could smell toast from the kitchen and spices from the pantry. He loved how the house felt busy and friendly.

Today, the guest room had visitors. On the floor sat a red suitcase with bright stickers. The stickers showed a tall tower, a snowy mountain, a sandy beach, and a shiny city. “Wow,” Milo said. “Stories from far away!”

There was also a soft scarf with little elephants, a tiny drum with a warm wooden smell, and a book with curvy letters on the cover. Milo’s whiskers wiggled with wonder. “So many treasures from around the big world,” he whispered.

Knock, knock went the door. In came Anya the cat, wearing a gentle smile and a bell that chimed, “Ting-ting.” With her came Sori the squirrel, who held a little notebook. Their cousins were visiting, and they had brought the suitcase.

“Hello,” Milo said. “Welcome to the guest room. My name is Milo. Would you like to share about your treasures?”

“We can,” Anya purred. “But first, what do you do every morning, Milo? You look so ready.”

“I have a routine,” Milo said. “I stretch, make my bed, brush my teeth, and say good morning to the world. It helps me feel safe and kind.”

Sori nodded. “We have morning things too. In my family, we clap a rhythm and say thank you for the day.”

Anya lifted the drum. “In my grandma’s home, we sing a soft song in another language.”

Milo’s eyes grew wide. “Can you teach me?”

Just then, a little mouse cousin named Pip rushed in. Pip was excited. Pip grabbed the scarf and spun with it. “Whee!” Pip squeaked. The scarf fluttered like a butterfly—and almost fell into a cup of tea!

“Careful, Pip,” Anya said kindly.

Pip frowned. “I want to play now!”

Milo took a slow breath. Routine helps, he reminded himself. “Let’s make a plan,” he said in a gentle voice. “First we do our morning routine together. Then we learn about the treasures. Then we play.”

“But I want to play first,” Pip said, crossing little paws.

Milo nodded. “I hear you. Playing is fun. My plan helps everyone feel calm. When we follow steps, we all get a turn. After we do the steps, playtime will be extra fun.”

Anya added, “And my drum will be safe.”

Sori said, “And the scarf will stay clean.”

Pip looked at the scarf, then at Milo. “Okay,” Pip said softly. “What is step one?”

“Stretch,” Milo said. They all reached up high. The bell went ting-ting, and the scarf felt soft and warm against Pip’s paws.

“Step two, make the bed,” Milo said. Together they patted the pillow—pat, pat—smoothed the blanket—smooth, smooth—and smiled at the neat bed.

“Step three, brush, brush,” Sori sang. They took turns at the tiny sink. Bubbles popped. “Minty!” Pip giggled.

“Step four,” said Anya, “good morning, world.” They cracked the window and listened. Tweet-tweet from the birds. Ting-ting from Anya’s bell. A far-off hum from a bus. The world felt big and close.

Now Milo said, “It is treasure time.” Anya gently tapped the drum. Dum, dum, dum. The sound was warm and round, like a hug. “In my grandma’s home,” Anya said, “we use this drum to start the day.”

Sori opened the book. “These letters are from far away,” Sori said. “They say, Good morning, friends!” Sori traced the curvy shapes with a careful paw.

Milo wrapped the elephant scarf around everyone’s shoulders like a circle. “It feels friendly,” Pip whispered. The scarf smelled a little like spice and sunshine.

“Now we play,” Milo said. They clapped Sori’s rhythm: clap-clap, tap-tap, clap. Anya sang a soft morning song. Milo hummed along. Pip danced, gentle and careful, holding the scarf edges with respect.

When they finished, Pip hugged Milo. “Your plan helped me,” Pip said. “I felt mad at first, but then I felt calm.”

Milo smiled. “Plans and routines help us take turns, keep things safe, and learn new things. They help our hearts feel steady.”

Anya purred. “We shared about our homes. Your routine made room for our songs.”

Sori nodded. “We can do this again tomorrow.”

Milo looked around the guest room. The bed was neat. The drum was safe. The scarf was folded. The window was open just a bit, and the air smelled sweet and clean.

“Good morning, world,” Milo said once more. “We are ready.”

And the world seemed to whisper back, “Good morning, friends.”

The End.
