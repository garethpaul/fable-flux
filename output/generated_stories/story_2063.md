# Fawn Finds Her Feelings

In a warm kitchen filled with golden light, a gentle deer fawn named Fawn stood on a soft rug. The oven hummed. A pot on the stove bubbled quietly. Cinnamon and apples scented the air. Fawn’s ears twitched. Her small hooves made little tap-tap sounds on the floor.

Fawn’s friend, a fluffy bunny named Pip, bounced in. “Hi, Fawn!” Pip chirped. “Let’s bake apple muffins!”

Fawn’s nose wriggled. “Yum,” she said. “I like apples.”

Pip hopped to the counter and clinked bowls together. “We will need flour, milk, eggs, and apples,” Pip said. The metal spoon clanged against the bowl with a happy ring. The warm kitchen felt safe and cozy.

Fawn looked at the shiny mixer. It whirred loudly when Pip turned it on. “Wrrr-rrrrr!” The sound made Fawn’s chest feel tight. Her ears went flat. She took a tiny step back.

Pip smiled. “Ready to pour?” Pip asked.

Fawn wanted to be brave. She wanted to help her friend. She took a breath and poured the milk. The mixer started again. “Wrrr-rrrrr!” The sound buzzed inside Fawn’s head like a big bee. Her tummy felt twisty. Her eyes felt hot.

Pip did not notice. Pip was humming. “Mix, mix, mix!”

Fawn’s hooves slid on the rug. She tried to stand still, but her legs felt wobbly. She wanted to say something, but her voice felt stuck.

The oven ticked. The cinnamon smell grew richer. The window let in sun. It should have felt nice. But inside, Fawn felt a storm of feelings. Her heart beat fast. Her ears were too full of sound.

Fawn stopped and put a hoof on her chest. She closed her eyes for a moment. She remembered what Mama Deer had taught her. “Name your feeling,” Mama had said. “Then choose what you can do.”

Fawn whispered to herself, “I feel scared. I feel too loud inside.”

“Fawn?” Pip turned the mixer off and looked over. “Are you okay?”

Fawn took a slow breath. In. Out. She touched her chest again. “My feelings are big,” she said quietly. “The mixer is too loud for me.”

Pip’s ears drooped. “Oh,” Pip said. “I didn’t know.” Pip hopped closer and looked worried. “I want you to feel safe. What can we do?”

Fawn felt a little lighter. She had used her voice. She had told her friend. That was brave. “Maybe,” Fawn said, “we can make it quieter. Or we can take turns. Or I can have quiet ears.”

“Quiet ears?” Pip blinked.

Fawn pointed to a small basket by the door. Inside were soft ear covers that Mama Deer had made. “These help me,” Fawn said. “They make sounds softer.”

Pip nodded. “Let’s try them! And we can also turn the mixer low.”

They worked together. Pip turned the knob. “Wrr…” The mixer purred softly now. Fawn slipped on the ear covers. The sound became gentle, like rain in leaves. The storm inside Fawn began to calm.

“Is this better?” Pip asked.

Fawn smiled. “Yes,” she said. “Thank you for listening.”

Pip grinned back. “Friends listen,” Pip said. “I’m glad you told me.”

They chopped apples. Fawn loved the soft thock-thock of the knife on the board. The apples were cool and crisp. Juice ran down Fawn’s hoof. It felt sticky and sweet. She giggled. Pip giggled too.

They sprinkled cinnamon. The warm smell hugged them. Fawn felt happy now. The kitchen did not feel too loud. It felt just right.

When the muffins baked, they sat on the rug and watched the oven window glow. Pip asked, “How did you know what you felt?”

Fawn thought. “I put my hoof on my heart,” she said. “I listened to my body. My tummy was twisty. My ears went flat. That told me I felt scared.”

Pip nodded. “And you told me. That was good.”

Fawn smiled. “Yes. I used my words. I said, ‘The mixer is too loud for me.’ And we made a plan.”

Pip wiggled happily. “That is good deciding,” Pip said. “We can make plans together.”

The timer beeped. “Beep, beep!” Pip opened the oven with a soft whoosh. Warm air puffed out, like a cozy blanket. The muffins were golden and round. They set them on the counter to cool. Fawn leaned close and smelled cinnamon, apple, and a tiny hint of butter. Her mouth watered.

They waited. Fawn hummed a little tune. At last, Pip said, “Ready to taste?”

Fawn nodded. They each took a muffin. The top was a little crisp. The inside was soft and warm. “Mmm,” Fawn said. “This tastes like hugs.”

Pip laughed. “It tastes like friendship.”

They ate and smiled. Fawn felt proud. She had listened to her feelings. She had used kind words. She had asked for what she needed. Pip had listened and helped. Together, they had made a good choice.

Fawn looked at Pip. “Thank you for being my friend.”

Pip touched Fawn’s hoof. “Thank you for telling me your feelings,” Pip said. “Next time, we will make a plan again.”

Fawn nodded. “Next time, I will put my hoof on my heart, name my feeling, and use my words.”

Outside, a soft breeze moved the leaves. Inside, the warm kitchen glowed. Fawn felt calm, safe, and loved.

The End.
