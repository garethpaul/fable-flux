# Gigi’s Dinner-Time Plan

Gigi the thoughtful giraffe loved her cozy dining room. The room smelled like warm cinnamon rolls and sweet mango slices. The round table had a soft blue cloth, and the chairs looked like little clouds. On the shelf, baskets held napkins, spoons, forks, and colorful cups. A tiny chalkboard showed Gigi’s neat list: “Set table. Share space. Use kind words.”

“I like when dinner is calm,” Gigi whispered, her long neck bobbing happily. “I can make a plan!”

Gigi took a deep breath and hummed a quiet tune. She opened a basket. “Napkins first,” she said. Her hooves placed napkins in a gentle circle. “One napkin for me. One napkin for my guests.”

Knock knock!

Gigi’s tall ears flicked. “Come in!” she called.

Her friends stepped into the dining room. Momo the monkey swung in with a bundle of bananas. Tia the tiny turtle carried a jar of crunchy carrots. Poppy the parrot fluttered down with a bright red bell pepper.

“Welcome!” said Gigi. “Tonight we share dinner and stories.”

“It smells yummy,” said Momo, sniffing the air. “Can we sit now?”

“Almost,” Gigi said kindly. She tapped the chalkboard with a soft smile. “First, we set up together. Then we eat. We will use kind words and share space.”

Momo wiggled. “I like to sit by the window. I need lots of room to swing my tail!”

Tia blinked slowly. “I like to sit near the floor. I feel safe down low.”

Poppy fluffed her feathers. “I like to sit by the bright lamp. I like light, light, bright light!”

Gigi felt a tiny twist in her tummy. Three friends, three different needs. The dining room suddenly felt small. Her heart thumped. “What if we can’t all be happy?” she thought.

She placed her hoof on her chest. “Breath in… and out,” she whispered. She noticed her feelings. Her tummy felt tight. Her neck felt warm. “I feel worried,” she said softly, “and that is okay.”

She looked at her list: Share space. Use kind words.

Gigi smiled. “Friends,” she said gently, “we can all be comfy. We can make a plan together.”

“How?” asked Tia.

“We listen to each other,” Gigi said. “We take turns. We find a way.”

Momo nodded. “I can try.”

Poppy tilted her head. “I can try too!”

Tia’s eyes grew soft. “I will try.”

Gigi used her long neck to point to each seat. “The window chair can be Momo’s. The lamp chair can be Poppy’s. And I will put a soft mat by my chair, low and cozy, for Tia.”

Tia looked at the mat. “Oh! That feels safe.”

“But the table is high,” Momo said, scratching his head. “Tia can’t reach.”

Gigi tapped her chin. “We can bring the small stool.” She opened a basket and pulled out a little step stool and a tiny tray. “Tia’s tray can be right by my chair. We can pass her food.”

Momo clapped. “That works!”

Poppy chirped, “Bright light for me, window for Momo, cozy mat for Tia! Squawk-tastic!”

Gigi’s heart felt light again. She placed plates, forks, and cups. “Let’s keep it tidy,” she said. “Things have special homes.” She pointed: “Cups here, forks here, napkins here.” The baskets helped everyone find what they needed. Organization made the room feel calm and friendly.

They all took their places. Gigi poured warm carrot soup. The steam smelled sweet and soft. Momo peeled bananas. Poppy sliced red pepper into crunchy strips. Tia arranged carrot coins on her tray.

Momo swung his tail a little too wide. It knocked a spoon. Clatter! The spoon fell to the floor.

Momo’s eyes went wide. “Oh no! I made a mess.”

Gigi took a slow breath. “It’s okay,” she said warmly. “We can fix it together.” She handed Momo a towel from the basket. “This is our clean-up helper.”

Momo wiped the floor. “Thank you for being patient,” he said.

Tia smiled up at Momo. “Thank you for cleaning.”

Poppy leaned closer to the lamp, then blinked at Gigi’s long neck. “Gigi, your neck is very tall. Does it block the light?”

Gigi laughed softly. “Sometimes it does.” She scooted her chair back just a little. “How is that?”

“Perfect,” Poppy chirped. “Thank you for moving.”

They ate and talked.

Momo said, “I like to wiggle.”

Tia said, “I like to go slow.”

Poppy said, “I like bright light.”

Gigi said, “I like a plan.”

“We are different,” Gigi said, “and that is okay. We can be kind to each other. We can make space for each other. That is tolerance.”

After dinner, Gigi clapped gently. “Let’s tidy up together.” She hummed her tune. “Plates go in the sink. Forks in the silver basket. Napkins in the wash basket.” Everyone helped. It felt calm and easy. The dining room looked neat again.

Tia’s shell gleamed in the lamp. “I felt safe tonight.”

Momo’s tail curled happily. “I felt welcome.”

Poppy ruffled her feathers. “I felt seen.”

Gigi’s heart felt warm. “I felt proud. We listened. We were kind. We made room for each other.”

They all hugged. The room smelled like cinnamon and clean soap. The table shone. The chalkboard now said: “We did it!”

Gigi whispered, “When we respect each other’s needs, dinner feels good.”

Momo, Tia, and Poppy said together, “We can be different and still belong.”

The End.
