# Millie Mouse Solves the Morning Mix-Up

Sunlight peeked through the soft curtains of the comfortable nursery. A warm, milky smell floated from a little cup, and the floor rug felt fluffy like a cloud. Millie Mouse wiggled her whiskers and stretched. “Good morning, nursery,” she squeaked kindly. “I am ready for a smart, calm day!”

Millie liked to help. She liked to think with her head and her heart. Today, she wanted to get ready all by herself.

First, she checked her “Morning Map.” It was a picture chart on the wall with four simple drawings: a toothbrush, a shirt, a bowl, and a book.

“Brush. Dress. Breakfast. Read,” Millie said, tapping each picture with her tiny paw. “One, two, three, four. That makes sense!”

She picked up her little blue toothbrush. “Hello, brush,” she giggled. “Time to make my teeth sparkle.” She squeezed a pea-sized dot of toothpaste. “Not too much, not too little. Just right.” The minty smell tickled her nose. Swish, swish, swish. “Top teeth, bottom teeth, little teeth, big teeth,” she sang. Bubbles foamed. She spit into the tiny sink and rinsed. “All clean!”

Then Millie looked at the dresser. Two shirts lay there: one red, one green. She touched both with her paw. The red one felt soft like a peach. The green one felt scratchy like hay.

Millie paused. “What do I need today?” she asked herself. She looked out the window. Sunlight made warm dots on the floor. She took a deep breath. Warm air. “If it is warm, I will pick the soft, light shirt.” She smiled and slipped on the red shirt. “That is logical thinking,” she said proudly. “I asked a question. I looked for clues. I chose.”

Her tummy gave a gentle grumble. “Breakfast time!” On the little table were three bowls. One smelled like sweet oats with cinnamon. One smelled like berries. One was empty.

“Oh!” Millie said. “Which one is mine?” A small note sat nearby with a scribble: M. Millie touched each bowl carefully. The oats were warm. The berries were cool. The empty bowl felt lonely.

Millie thought. “My Morning Map shows a bowl, not two. And the note says M. I am Millie. I will choose one bowl and eat with calm, healthy habits.”

She closed her eyes and breathed in slowly. Cinnamon smelled cozy. Berries smelled bright. “My tummy wants warm,” she decided. “Warm will feel good.” She picked the oat bowl.

“Chew, chew, chew,” Millie said softly. “Small bites. Sit up tall.” The spoon made a tiny clink. The oats tasted creamy and kind. She dabbed her whiskers with a napkin. “All done,” she said, patting her belly. “Thank you, breakfast.”

Just then, Millie heard a sound: tap… tap… tap. A round, red ball had rolled under the bookshelf. Little Hedgehog, her shy friend, peeked in from the doorway. “Um… Millie?” he whispered. “My ball… it’s stuck. I’m scared to reach. It’s dark under there.”

Millie nodded. “It looks a little dark,” she agreed. She felt a flutter in her chest, too. The space under the shelf was small and shadowy. Millie placed a paw on her heart. “Let’s use calm and think.”

She looked around. “We need light, and something long.” She spotted a small flashlight on the toy shelf and a wooden ruler near the art box. “Aha!” Her whiskers perked. “Plan: first, shine the light to see. Second, use the ruler to roll the ball out.”

Hedgehog watched with wide eyes. “That sounds smart,” he said.

Millie clicked the flashlight. A soft white circle lit the floor. “Hello, little ball,” she said gently. “I see you.” The red ball glowed like a cherry.

She slid the ruler under the shelf. “Push slowly,” she whispered. Scritch… scritch. The ball wiggled, then rolled out with a happy bup!

“You did it!” Hedgehog cheered. He hugged his ball and then looked at Millie. “You were brave.”

Millie smiled. “I felt a little scared,” she admitted. “But I took a breath. I made a plan. That helped me be brave.”

Hedgehog nodded. “I want to try next time.”

“You can,” said Millie kindly. “We can think it through together.”

Millie checked her Morning Map again. Last picture: a book. She chose a story about shapes. “Circles roll. Squares stack,” she read aloud. “Triangles point.” Hedgehog sat beside her, ball tucked under his arm. The pages felt smooth, and the pictures were bright.

When they finished, Millie put the book back on the shelf. She clapped softly. “Today I used logical thinking to solve the morning mix-up. I brushed my teeth with care. I chose a comfy shirt. I picked a healthy breakfast. I made a plan to help a friend. I stayed calm and brave.”

Hedgehog smiled. “I learned to look for clues and make a plan, too.”

Millie nodded. “That is independence,” she said gently. “You can do many things by yourself when you think step by step.”

They both took a deep breath in… and out. The nursery felt warm and safe. The curtains swished. Somewhere, a clock ticked steady and slow.

Millie placed a gold sticker on her Morning Map. “Good job, me,” she whispered. “Good job, us.”

The End.
