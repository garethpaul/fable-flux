# Benny Bear's Honest Morning

Benny Bear was a fluffy little cub with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. Every morning, Mama Bear would call out, "Time to brush your teeth, Benny!"

This morning was no different. "Benny, sweetie! Come brush your teeth so we can have breakfast!" Mama Bear's voice echoed from the kitchen.

Benny was playing with his favorite toy blocks in his room. The colorful towers he had built looked so wonderful! "Just five more minutes," he thought to himself. But deep down, his tummy felt a little twisty. He knew he should go to the bathroom right away.

"Coming, Mama!" Benny called back, but he didn't move from his blocks.

Minutes passed. Mama Bear called again, "Benny? Are you brushing your teeth?"

Benny's heart beat faster. He looked at his blocks, then toward the bathroom. The white tiles gleamed in the morning sunlight streaming through the window. He could smell the minty toothpaste from where he sat.

"Yes, Mama!" Benny called out, even though he was still sitting with his toys.

But saying those words made Benny's stomach feel even more twisty. His ears drooped a little. Something didn't feel right about what he had just said.

Benny sat quietly for a moment, thinking. He remembered when Mama Bear had taught him about telling the truth. "Honest little bears are happy little bears," she had said, giving him a warm hug.

Suddenly, Benny realized what was making his tummy feel so uncomfortable. He hadn't been honest! He had told Mama he was brushing his teeth when he was really still playing.

Benny's little paws trembled as he stood up. He walked slowly to the bathroom, his soft footsteps padding on the cool tile floor. The bathroom smelled fresh and clean, like the lavender soap Mama Bear used.

He picked up his small blue toothbrush and squeezed a tiny bit of strawberry toothpaste onto it. As he began brushing, making little circles just like Mama had taught him, he heard her footsteps coming down the hall.

"Benny? How are you doing in there?" Mama Bear asked gently.

Benny stopped brushing. His mouth was full of foamy toothpaste, but he knew what he had to do. He spit carefully into the sink and took a deep breath.

"Mama," he said in a small voice, "I need to tell you something."

Mama Bear appeared in the doorway, her kind eyes looking concerned. "What is it, little one?"

"When you asked if I was brushing my teeth, I said yes, but I was really still playing with my blocks. I'm sorry I wasn't honest." Benny's voice was quiet, but he felt a little bit better already.

Mama Bear knelt down and wrapped Benny in the biggest, warmest bear hug. "Thank you for telling me the truth, Benny. That took courage, and I'm very proud of you."

"My tummy felt all twisty when I wasn't being honest," Benny said, snuggling into Mama's soft fur.

"That's your heart telling you what's right," Mama Bear explained. "When we tell the truth, even when it's hard, we feel much better inside."

Benny finished brushing his teeth, and together they walked to the kitchen for breakfast. His tummy felt light and happy now, and he had learned something very important: being honest always feels better than hiding the truth.

The End.
