# Rocco’s Closet Concert

Rocco was a musical raccoon with bright eyes and nimble paws. He loved to make music with anything he found. He liked to tap spoons. He liked to shake buttons in a jar. He liked to hum sweet little songs.

Rocco had a special place. It was a closet hideaway. The closet smelled like clean shirts and warm soap. The floor was soft with a fuzzy rug. On the shelf, there was a tiny lamp that made a golden glow. Inside the closet, Rocco kept his treasures: a tin can drum, a ribbon bell, and a shoebox guitar with rubber-band strings.

“Time for my closet concert,” Rocco said, wiggling his striped tail. He plucked the rubber bands. Twang! Twang! He shook the ribbon bell. Jing-a-ling! He tapped the tin can. Tink-tink!

Rocco’s little sister, Rina, peeked in. Her nose twitched. “Can I play too?” she asked in a squeaky voice.

Rocco smiled. “Yes! You can be my helper. You can wave the scarf like a dancing cloud.”

Rina climbed into the closet hideaway and sat on the fuzzy rug. She waved the scarf. Whoosh, whoosh. It felt smooth and soft in her small paws.

Rocco cleared his throat. “A-one, a-two, a—” He stopped. The rubber-band strings were loose. They made a sad sound. Plop-twang. His ears drooped.

“Oh no,” Rocco said. “My shoebox guitar doesn’t sound good. My concert will be ruined.” His tummy felt tight. He scrunched his nose. He wanted to stomp his feet.

Rina blinked. “It’s okay,” she said softly. “We can fix it.”

Rocco took a deep breath. In, out. The closet smelled like lavender. The golden lamp felt warm on his fur. He remembered a helpful plan: stop, breathe, think, try.

He said it out loud. “Stop. Breathe. Think. Try.”

Rocco tapped his head gently. “What can I do?” He looked around the closet. He saw clothespins, string, and a strip of tape. He plucked a rubber band again. Plip-plip. His whiskers twitched with an idea.

“I can tighten the strings,” he said. “I can use clothespins to hold them tight.”

Rina clapped. “Click, click!”

Rocco worked slowly. He wrapped a rubber band and clipped it with a clothespin. Clip! He tried another. Clip! He strummed. Twang-ting-ting! The sound was better, but one band still buzzed. Buzz-buzz.

Rocco felt a grumble inside. “I’m getting frustrated,” he said, frowning.

Rina touched his paw. “Let’s take a wiggle break,” she suggested.

They wiggled their shoulders. They shook their paws. They took three puffy breaths. In… out. In… out. In… out.

Rocco smiled a little. “Thank you, Rina. Now I can think again.”

He looked at the buzzing band and the strip of tape. “Maybe,” he said, “the band is too wobbly. Tape can keep it still.” He pressed a tiny piece of tape to the edge of the box. Press, press. Then he strummed. Twang! The note rang clear and bright.

“Yay!” Rina squealed. “Music!”

Rocco grinned. “Problem solved!”

Just then, Mama Raccoon called from the hall. “Little ones, it’s quiet time.”

Rocco peeked out of the closet. “Mama, we are having a concert. But our music might be loud.”

Mama’s whiskers lifted in a smile. “I love your music, Rocco. But your baby brother is napping.”

Rocco paused. He wanted to play big, loud songs. His paws tingled to drum. But he remembered another helpful plan: use words, make a plan, take turns.

He nodded. “Let’s talk,” he said to Mama. “We can make a quiet concert now and a big concert later.”

Mama tapped her chin. “That sounds fair. What is your plan?”

Rocco thought. “We will use soft sounds. No tin can drum. Just soft humming, gentle twangs, and the scarf. After nap, we can have a loud concert in the living room.”

Rina added, “And we can invite you!”

Mama agreed. “Deal,” she said. “Thank you for your kind plan.”

Rocco felt proud. He shook Rina’s paw. “We made a good plan.”

They started the quiet concert. The closet lamp glowed. The scarf whispered. Whoosh, whoosh. Rocco hummed a breezy tune. Mmm-mmm. He strummed softly. Ting… ting… The sound was like raindrops on a window. The fuzzy rug tickled their toes.

When the baby woke up, Mama said, “Nap is over!”

Rocco and Rina moved to the living room. Rocco tapped the tin can. Tink-tink! Rina jingled the ribbon bell. Jing-a-ling! They played their big, happy song.

Mama clapped. “Bravo!”

Rocco bowed. His heart felt warm. “Today I learned three big things,” he said. “When I feel stuck, I stop, breathe, think, and try. When I feel mad, I take a wiggle break. And when I need something, I use words and make a plan.”

Rina nodded. “We can solve problems together.”

Rocco smiled at his sister and hugged her. “Yes. Music is fun. And solving problems is music for the mind.”

The End.
