# Oliver Owl's Special Lab Friends

Deep beneath Mrs. Henderson's house, in a cozy basement lab filled with bubbling beakers and colorful bottles, lived Oliver the wise old owl. His big yellow eyes sparkled as he worked on his special experiments every day.

"Hoot, hoot!" Oliver said happily as he stirred a purple mixture. "Today I'm making soap that smells like flowers!"

Oliver loved his quiet lab, but sometimes he felt a little lonely. The basement was always so still and silent.

One morning, Oliver heard tiny squeaking sounds coming from behind his bookshelf. "Who could that be?" he wondered aloud, tilting his feathered head to listen carefully.

Out scurried three little mice - a gray one, a brown one, and a white one with pink ears. They looked very dirty and their fur was all messy.

"Oh my!" gasped Oliver. "You little mice look like you need help!"

The gray mouse named Pip spoke first. "We're sorry, Mr. Owl. We know you probably don't want dirty mice in your clean lab."

Oliver's heart felt warm. He remembered what his grandmother owl had taught him: "Everyone deserves kindness, no matter how they look."

"Nonsense!" hooted Oliver gently. "Everyone is welcome here. But I can see you might feel better if you were clean and fresh. Would you like some help?"

The brown mouse, Dot, sniffled. "We've been living in the old pipes. We don't know how to get clean."

The white mouse, Rosie, added quietly, "We promise we're nice mice. We just don't have a place to wash up."

Oliver listened carefully to each mouse. His wise old heart understood exactly what they needed.

"I have just the thing!" Oliver announced. He flew to his shelf and brought down three tiny bottles of his special flower soap. "This soap is perfect for little paws and whiskers!"

Oliver filled three small bottle caps with warm water from his lab sink. The water sparkled and smelled like sunshine.

"Here you go, my new friends," Oliver said kindly. "Take your time and get nice and clean."

The three mice looked at each other with surprise and joy. No one had ever been so kind to them before!

Splash, splash, splash! The mice washed their paws, their whiskers, and their tails. They used Oliver's soft cotton balls to dry off. Soon their fur was fluffy and shiny, and they smelled like beautiful flowers.

"We feel so much better!" squeaked Pip, twirling around.

"And we smell wonderful!" added Dot, sniffing her clean paws.

"Thank you for being so nice to us," whispered Rosie.

Oliver smiled his biggest owl smile. "You know what? I've been lonely in this lab. Would you three like to be my helpers? We could do experiments together!"

"Really?" all three mice said at once, their eyes bright with excitement.

From that day on, Oliver's basement lab was filled with happy sounds. The mice helped measure ingredients, and Oliver taught them about staying clean and healthy. They learned that washing their paws before experiments kept their work safe and tidy.

Every evening, they would sit together sharing stories. Oliver learned that being different on the outside didn't matter - what mattered was having a kind heart and being a good friend.

"You know," Oliver said one day, "the best experiment I ever did was learning to listen and be kind to everyone I meet."

The three mice nodded happily, their clean whiskers twitching with joy.

The End.
