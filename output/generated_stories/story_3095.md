# Polly's Painting Problem

In the cozy study corner, bright green Polly the parrot perched on her special easel. Colorful feathers covered her wings like a rainbow. Her tiny claws gripped a paintbrush.

"Today I'll paint the most beautiful picture!" Polly squawked happily. She dipped her brush in red paint. Swish, swish went the brush across the white paper.

But oh no! The red paint dripped down like tears. It made a big messy blob instead of a pretty flower.

"Squawk! This is all wrong!" Polly flapped her wings. The paintbrush flew across the room. It landed with a soft thud on the carpet.

Polly's bright eyes filled with tears. "I can't paint! I'm just a silly bird!" She tucked her colorful head under her wing.

From the bookshelf, wise old Owl hooted softly. "What's wrong, little friend?"

"I wanted to paint a flower, but it looks like mud!" Polly peeped sadly. "Real artists don't make messes like this."

Owl's big round eyes twinkled. "Even the best artists make messes when they're learning. Would you like to try again?"

Polly peeked out from under her wing. "But what if I mess up again?"

"Then we'll try a different way," Owl said gently. "Sometimes the best paintings come from trying many times."

Polly took a deep breath. The air smelled like fresh paper and colorful paints. She hopped down and picked up her brush with her beak.

"This time, I'll try smaller dots," Polly said to herself. She dipped just the tip of her brush in yellow paint. Dot, dot, dot. Three tiny yellow circles appeared on the paper.

"Look! Little suns!" Polly chirped with excitement.

Next, she tried green paint. Instead of big swooshes, she made tiny lines. Up and down, up and down. Soon she had painted grass!

"I'm doing it!" Polly danced on her perch. Her colorful tail feathers swished with joy.

For the flower, Polly remembered her mistake. This time, she pressed the brush very gently. She made one small red circle, then added tiny pink petals around it.

"Beautiful!" hooted Owl. "You didn't give up, and look what happened!"

Polly stepped back to admire her painting. It showed a bright yellow sun, green grass, and a lovely red flower with pink petals. It wasn't perfect, but it was hers.

"It's different from what I imagined," Polly said, tilting her head. "But I like it even more!"

"That's the magic of trying again," Owl explained. "When we feel frustrated, we can take a breath, think of a new way, and keep trying."

Polly hung her painting on the study corner wall. Every time she looked at it, she remembered how good it felt to not give up.

The next day, Polly painted again. This time, when the paint dripped, she didn't throw her brush. Instead, she said, "Oops! Let me try a different way."

Soon, other animals came to paint in the study corner too. Rabbit painted purple carrots. Squirrel painted blue acorns. Mouse painted enormous cheese wheels.

"Everyone paints differently," Polly realized. "And that's what makes it special!"

From that day on, whenever Polly felt frustrated, she remembered her flower painting. She would take a deep breath, think of a new way to try, and keep going.

"Feelings are like paint," Polly would tell her friends. "Sometimes they get messy, but we can always clean up and start fresh!"

The End.
