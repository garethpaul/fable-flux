# Bouncy's Big Treehouse Cleanup

Bouncy was a small kangaroo joey with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in the most wonderful treehouse, high up in a big oak tree. The treehouse had wooden walls that smelled like fresh pine and windows that let in golden sunshine.

Every morning, Bouncy loved to hop around his treehouse home. *Boing, boing, boing!* went his little feet on the wooden floor. But today, something was wrong.

"Oh no!" Bouncy gasped, looking around. His treehouse was very messy! Toys were scattered everywhere. Books lay open on the floor. His favorite red ball was stuck under a pile of blocks.

Bouncy's ears drooped down sadly. "How did everything get so mixed up?" he wondered aloud. He tried to hop to his toy box, but *CRASH!* He bumped into a tower of cups.

Just then, he heard a gentle tapping at his treehouse door. *Tap, tap, tap.*

"Hello, Bouncy!" called a sweet voice. "It's me, Rosie!"

Bouncy opened the door to find his best friend, a little rabbit with fluffy white fur and pink ears. Rosie's nose twitched as she sniffed the morning air.

"Hi, Rosie!" Bouncy said, but his voice sounded sad. "I wanted to play, but my treehouse is too messy. I don't know how to fix it."

Rosie hopped inside and looked around with her bright eyes. "Wow, it is pretty messy in here," she said kindly. "But don't worry! I have an idea. Let's clean it up together!"

"Together?" Bouncy's ears perked up hopefully.

"Yes!" Rosie clapped her small paws. "Two friends can solve any problem! You're good at hopping high, and I'm good at organizing small spaces."

Bouncy felt his heart fill with happiness. "Really? You'll help me?"

"Of course! That's what friends do," Rosie smiled warmly.

They made a plan. Rosie would sort the toys into groups while Bouncy used his strong legs to hop up and put things on high shelves.

"Let's put all the balls here," Rosie said, making a neat pile. Her whiskers twitched with concentration.

"And I'll put the books up there!" Bouncy said, bouncing high to reach the tall bookshelf. *Boing!* Up he went, placing each book carefully.

As they worked together, they sang a happy cleaning song: "Pick up, put away, we're cleaning up today! Friends helping friends, that's the very best way!"

The warm sunshine streamed through the windows, making their fur glow golden. The treehouse began to smell fresh and clean again.

Soon, everything had a special place. The balls sat in their basket, the books stood straight on their shelf, and the blocks were stacked in a colorful tower.

"We did it!" Bouncy cheered, bouncing with joy. His whole treehouse sparkled and shone.

"We make a great team," Rosie said, giving Bouncy a gentle hug. "Your hopping and my organizing worked perfectly together!"

Bouncy looked around his beautiful, clean treehouse. "Thank you, Rosie. I couldn't have done this without you. You're the best friend ever!"

"And you're mine too," Rosie replied. "Now we have a wonderful place to play!"

They spent the rest of the morning playing happily in the organized treehouse, sharing toys and laughing together. Bouncy learned that big problems become small when friends work together, and that helping each other makes everything more fun.

From that day on, Bouncy always remembered to keep his treehouse tidy, and whenever he needed help, he knew his dear friend Rosie would always be there.

The End.
