# Panda on the Rooftop Beat

Pip was a patient panda. Pip lived in a tall building with a flat rooftop. The rooftop was Pip’s favorite place. The sky felt big. The breeze felt soft. The sun felt warm like a hug on his fur.

One bright morning, Pip climbed the little steps to the rooftop. He held a small drum made from a tin and a stretchy top. Tap-tap. Pip loved to dance. He wanted to learn a new dance move. It was a gentle spin with a hop at the end.

“I can do it if I practice,” Pip said to himself. His voice was calm. He took a deep breath in. He let it out slowly. The breeze smelled like clean laundry and fresh rain.

Tap. Tap. Tap-tap. Pip played a slow beat and tried the spin. His paws slid. His hop was small. He wobbled and landed on his bottom.

“That’s okay,” Pip said with a smile. “Practice takes time.” He stood up and brushed off his soft fur. “Try again.”

Pip tried again. Tap-tap. Spin. Hop. Oops! He bumped a flower pot. The little flowers shook and sprinkled tiny drops of water on his nose. It felt cool. Pip giggled. “Hello, flowers. I will be gentle.”

Just then, a pigeon fluttered down with a flappy swoosh. “Coo-coo! What are you doing?” the pigeon asked, tilting her head.

“I’m practicing a dance,” said Pip. “I tap, I spin, I hop.”

“I am Pippa the pigeon,” she cooed. “I like dance too. Can I watch?”

“Yes,” said Pip. “You can watch, and you can help by counting.”

Pippa puffed her feathers. “I can count to three. One, two, three!”

Pip nodded. “Great! When you say three, I hop.”

They started. Tap-tap. Pippa counted, “One, two, three!” Pip spun and hopped. He still wobbled, but not as much.

“Good try,” Pippa said. “You are brave.”

“Thank you,” Pip said. “I will keep practicing.”

They heard tiny paws skitter. A mouse peeked out from a planter. “Squeak! I am Min. It is noisy,” said Min, rubbing her ears.

Pip put down his drum. “Oh. I did not know it was too loud for you, Min,” he said kindly. “Thank you for telling me.”

Pippa nodded. “We can talk and choose a calm way.”

Pip smiled. “Let’s find a peaceful solution. I can drum softly. Tap… tap…” He showed a quiet beat, like raindrops.

Min’s whiskers twitched. “That is gentle,” she said. “Squeak, thank you.”

“You’re welcome,” Pip said. “I want everyone to feel good on the rooftop.”

Pip tried again with soft taps. Pippa counted, “One, two, three!” Pip spun and hopped. He wobbled a little. He took a breath. He did not rush. His heart felt steady like his beat.

Clouds floated by like big cotton puffs. A warm smell of soup drifted up from a window below. A wind bell tinkled, ting-ting, like tiny stars.

“I still need practice,” Pip said. “Practice helps my body learn.”

Pippa flapped. “I can show my wing swirl!” She twirled in a circle and laughed. Min clapped her tiny paws.

Pip watched. “Nice twirl,” he said. “Can you show me slowly?”

“Of course,” said Pippa. She moved one step at a time. “Wing up, turn, wing down.”

Pip copied. “Paw up, turn, paw down.” He smiled. “Slow practice helps.”

A gust of wind whooshed. The wind blew Pippa backward. “Eep!” She wobbled toward the edge. Pip felt a jump in his tummy. He took a brave step and held out his soft paws.

“I’ve got you,” Pip said calmly. Pippa flapped and steadied. “Thank you,” she cooed, her eyes wide.

“You’re safe,” said Pip. “We can practice away from the edge.” He pointed to the middle of the rooftop.

“That is a smart and peaceful choice,” Min said. “Squeak!”

They moved to the middle. Pip placed the drum on a mat so it would not slide. He smiled at his friends. “Let’s talk about our plan.”

Pip spoke slowly. “I will tap softly. Pippa will count. Min will clap at the hop.”

“Good plan,” Pippa said.

“Good plan,” Min squeaked.

They tried again. Tap… tap… “One, two, three!” Clap! Pip spun. He bent his knees. He hopped and landed with both paws together. His feet felt firm. His body felt balanced. He did it!

“I did it!” Pip cheered. He felt warm and proud. Pippa danced in a circle. Min squeaked and laughed. The wind bell sang, ting-ting-ting.

Pip bowed. “Thank you for helping me. Practice helped me learn. Talking helped us be kind. We found a calm way together.”

Pippa nodded. “You were patient.”

Min added, “You were brave and gentle.”

Pip smiled at the bright sky. “When we practice, we grow. When we talk, we understand. When we choose peace, we all shine.”

They danced a little more with soft taps, slow turns, and happy claps. The rooftop felt safe and sweet, like a warm cookie smell in the air.

As the sun began to rest, Pip packed his drum. He hugged his friends. “Tomorrow, I will practice again,” he said.

“We will cheer,” Pippa and Min chimed.

Pip looked out at the soft orange sky. His heart felt calm. He whispered, “Practice makes me brave and strong.”

The End.
