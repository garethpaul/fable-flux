# Tiko the Tortoise and the Pantry Plan

Tiko was a wise tortoise with a smooth, shiny shell. He lived in a cozy house with a bright, tidy pantry. The pantry smelled like cinnamon and warm bread. Jars clicked softly. Paper bags rustled. Little labels smiled with neat letters.

One sunny morning, friends came to visit. There was Mina the mouse, Pip the parrot, Lala the lamb, and Kito the kitten. They peeked into the pantry. Their eyes grew wide.

“Look at all the snacks!” Pip squawked, flapping. “Crunchy crackers! Sweet mango chips!”

“I smell cinnamon!” said Lala, bouncing.

“Let’s eat now!” meowed Kito, tapping a box.

Mina tugged a ribbon. “I want to try everything!”

Tiko blinked slowly. “Friends, welcome. In this pantry, we follow directions. We share. We are fair.”

“Fair?” asked Mina. “What is fair?”

“Fair means everyone gets what they need,” Tiko said gently. “We take turns. We listen to the plan.”

Pip tilted his head. “What plan?”

Tiko pulled out a little chart with pictures. “This is the Pantry Plan. First, we wash hands. Then, we sit. Then, we choose one snack each. We wait for everyone. Then we taste. After tasting, we talk about flavors from around the world.”

“From the world?” Lala whispered. “Like far-away places?”

“Yes,” Tiko smiled. “These snacks come from many places. We can learn how friends everywhere enjoy food.”

Kito tapped his paws. “But I am hungry now!”

“I know,” said Tiko. “Following directions helps everyone have enough. It keeps tummies and hearts calm.”

They washed their hands: splash, swish, drip. They sat on soft mats with colorful dots. Tiko opened boxes and bags, one by one. The pantry smelled like oranges, cocoa, and toasted rice.

“Here we have rice crackers from Japan,” Tiko said. “Crunchy!”

Crunch, crunch, went the crackers.

“Here are dried mango slices from the Philippines,” Tiko said. “Chewy and sweet.”

Chew, chew, went the mango.

“Here is cocoa nib trail mix from Ghana,” Tiko said. “Nutty and chocolaty.”

Munch, munch, went the mix.

“And here is cinnamon bread from our town,” Tiko added. “Warm and cozy.”

Mmmm, went everyone.

Mina’s whiskers twitched. “I want two mango slices.”

Kito’s tail swished. “I want the biggest piece of bread.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “I will take extra crackers.”

Lala’s ears drooped. “What if there is not enough for me?”

Tiko listened. He felt a small worry in his tummy. He wanted everyone to feel happy and safe. He tapped the chart. “Friends, remember the plan. One snack each first. Then we check. That is fair.”

But Mina grabbed another mango slice. Kito reached for the biggest bread. Pip pecked more crackers. Lala held her hooves and whispered, “I will wait, like the plan says.”

Tiko’s wise eyes grew soft. “Let us pause,” he said. “Close your eyes. Take a big breath in. Smell the cinnamon.” They all breathed in. “Let it out, slow.” They breathed out.

Tiko spoke kindly. “When we take more before others choose, someone gets less. That does not feel fair. Fairness means we think of everyone, not just ourselves.”

Pip folded his wings. “I forgot,” he said. “I was excited.”

Kito licked a crumb. “I was hungry.”

Mina looked at Lala’s worried face. “I did not want to make you sad,” she squeaked.

Tiko nodded. “Thank you for listening. We can fix it. Here is the solution. We measure with our eyes and our hearts. We make equal shares first. Then we taste together. After, if there is more, we can ask and take turns.”

“How do we make equal shares?” Lala asked.

Tiko placed the snacks on a clean board. He used a small string to guide cutting. “We divide each snack into the same number of pieces as friends. Four friends, four pieces. Watch.”

Snip, snap, he divided the bread. Crack, he broke the crackers. He counted mango slices: one, two, three, four. He scooped trail mix into four little bowls. Each bowl looked the same.

“Now,” Tiko said, “we follow directions. One piece or one bowl per friend.”

Mina took one mango slice and smiled. Kito took one piece of bread and purred. Pip took one cracker and bobbed his head. Lala took one bowl of mix and wagged her tail.

They all waited. Tiko watched. “Everyone ready? Taste!”

They tasted together. Crunch, chew, munch, mmmm. The flavors danced: sweet mango sunshine, cocoa warmth from Ghana, crisp rice from Japan, and cinnamon from home.

“This mango is like a sunny hug,” said Mina.

“This bread is like a warm blanket,” said Kito.

“These crackers are like tiny drums,” said Pip.

“This mix is like a cozy campfire,” said Lala.

Tiko smiled. “People in many places enjoy different foods. When we share fairly and follow directions, we all get to learn and enjoy together.”

They finished their first tastes. Tiko looked in the bags. “There is more,” he said. “Who would like a second taste?”

Mina raised a paw, but waited. Kito raised a paw, but waited. Pip raised a wing, but waited. Lala raised a hoof, and smiled.

Tiko nodded. “We will take turns.” He gave one extra piece to each friend in turn. Everyone said, “Thank you.”

At the end, the pantry was neat again. The air still smelled like cinnamon and smiles. Lala rubbed her cheek on Tiko’s shell. “It felt good to be fair,” she said.

Pip chirped, “Following directions helped my tummy feel calm.”

Kito purred, “Sharing made the snacks taste better.”

Mina whispered, “I liked learning about foods from far-away friends.”

Tiko tucked the chart away. “Fairness is caring about everyone. When we follow directions, we show respect. And when we learn about the world, our hearts grow big.”

They all nodded. Their tummies were happy. Their hearts were calm.

“Thank you, Tiko,” they said together.

“You are welcome,” Tiko said. “Remember: Be fair. Follow the plan. Care about the world.”

The End.
