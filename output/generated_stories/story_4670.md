# Chirpy's Swimming Song

Every morning, Chirpy the little blue bird loved to sing beautiful songs from her perch by the bathroom window. Her voice was sweet like honey, and she could trill the most wonderful melodies.

But there was one thing that made Chirpy's feathers ruffle with worry. In the big white bathtub below, she could see the shiny water sparkling in the morning light. All the other birds in her family knew how to swim and splash. Her mama, papa, and big sister Melody could dive and paddle like graceful dancers.

"Chirpy, come join us for our morning swim!" called Mama Bird from the tub. "It's a family tradition that goes back many, many years."

Chirpy's tiny heart went thump-thump-thump. "I... I don't know how," she chirped quietly. Her voice was so soft, like a whisper in the wind.

Papa Bird's eyes twinkled kindly. "Every bird in our family learns to swim, little one. Your grandmama taught me, and her grandmama taught her. Would you like to try?"

Chirpy looked at the water. It looked so deep and scary. What if she sank like a stone? What if she couldn't do it right?

"I'm too little," Chirpy said, tucking her head under her tiny wing.

Melody flew over and perched beside her sister. "I was scared too when I first started," she said gently. "But Papa taught me a special song that helped me learn. Would you like to hear it?"

Chirpy peeked out from under her wing. She loved songs more than anything in the whole world.

Melody began to sing: "Kick your feet, flap your wings, swimming is the song water sings! One-two-three, splash with me, soon you'll swim as well as we!"

The song made Chirpy's heart feel warm and brave. Maybe she could try, just a little bit.

"Will you help me?" Chirpy asked in her sweetest voice.

"Of course!" said Papa Bird. "We'll start very slow, just like learning a new song. First, we practice one note at a time."

Papa Bird showed Chirpy how to dip just her tiny toes in the warm, bubbly water. It felt nice and tickly! Then he taught her how to move her wings in small, gentle circles.

"Now sing Melody's swimming song while you try," suggested Mama Bird.

Chirpy took a deep breath and began to sing: "Kick your feet, flap your wings..." As she sang, she moved her little body just like the song said. Splash! Splash! Splash!

Day after day, Chirpy practiced. She sang the swimming song every morning. Her family cheered and clapped their wings as she got better and better.

Soon, Chirpy could paddle all around the bathtub! She could even do little dives, just like her sister Melody.

"Look at me!" Chirpy sang happily, spinning in circles in the water. "I'm swimming! I'm really swimming!"

"You did it by practicing every day and never giving up," said Papa Bird proudly. "Now you can share our family tradition too."

From that day on, Chirpy loved swimming time just as much as singing time. She learned that trying new things could be scary at first, but with practice and family support, she could do anything!

And whenever new little birds visited, Chirpy would teach them her special swimming song, sharing the wonderful tradition with everyone.

The End.
