# Milo's Kind Heart in the Laundry Room

Milo the monkey loved helping his mama with chores. Today, he swung into the warm, cozy laundry room where the washing machine hummed a gentle song. The air smelled fresh and clean, like sunshine and soap bubbles.

"Mama, can I help fold the towels?" Milo asked, his brown eyes sparkling with excitement.

"Of course, my sweet monkey," Mama smiled. "But first, let's wash our hands. Clean hands help keep our clothes clean too!"

Milo splashed his little paws in the warm, soapy water. He loved the tickly feeling of the bubbles between his fingers. After drying his hands on a fluffy towel, he was ready to help.

As they folded clothes together, Milo heard a tiny sound. "Squeak! Squeak!"

"What's that noise, Mama?" Milo asked, tilting his head to listen better.

They looked around the laundry room until Milo spotted something small and gray behind the dryer. It was a little mouse, and she looked very scared!

"Oh my!" said Mama. "That's Rosie Mouse. She must be lost."

The little mouse was shivering and had tears in her tiny black eyes. "I can't find my way home," Rosie squeaked sadly. "I was looking for food crumbs, but now I'm all turned around!"

Milo felt his heart fill with kindness. Even though some animals might be afraid of mice, Milo wanted to help. "Don't worry, Rosie," he said gently. "We'll help you feel better."

"But Milo," Mama whispered, "we need to respect that Rosie might be scared of us too. Let's give her some space and think of a kind way to help."

Milo nodded thoughtfully. He remembered his special notebook and colorful crayons that he kept on the laundry room shelf. "I have an idea!" he said.

Carefully, Milo drew a simple map on a piece of paper. He drew the laundry room, the hallway, and the little hole in the kitchen wall where mouse families lived. He used bright colors to make it cheerful and easy to follow.

"Here, Rosie," Milo said softly, placing the map on the floor at a respectful distance. "This map will show you the safe way home. The yellow line leads to your family!"

Rosie's whiskers twitched with surprise and joy. "You drew this just for me? That's so kind!"

"Everyone deserves to feel safe and loved," Milo said with a warm smile.

Mama watched proudly as Milo also placed a small piece of cheese near the map. "And here's a snack for your journey," he added.

Rosie picked up the map in her tiny paws. "Thank you, Milo! Your kind heart and helpful map will get me home safely. You're the most compassionate monkey I've ever met!"

As Rosie scurried away following the colorful map, Milo felt wonderful inside. His chest felt warm and happy, like when he got extra hugs.

"I'm proud of you, Milo," Mama said, giving him a big squeeze. "You showed compassion by caring about Rosie's feelings. You showed respect by giving her space. And your writing and drawing helped solve the problem!"

Milo beamed with pride. "Helping others feels really good, Mama. Even when they're different from me."

From that day on, whenever Milo helped in the laundry room, he always remembered to keep his heart open to others who might need kindness. And sometimes, he would find little thank-you notes from Rosie, drawn in tiny mouse writing!

The End.
