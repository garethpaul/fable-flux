# Tortoise Tim Sets a Small, Big Goal

Tortoise Tim was a wise tortoise with a smooth, shiny shell. One sunny morning, he slowly walked into the laundry room. The room hummed and whirred. The washer swished like the sea. The dryer hummed like a happy bee. Warm, soapy smells floated in the air. Soft towels were piled like little clouds.

Tim blinked his gentle eyes. “Today,” he said softly, “I will set a goal.” He liked goals. Goals helped him try, step by step.

“What is your goal, Tim?” asked a tiny voice. A sock puppet peeked out of a basket. It was a little striped sock with button eyes and a stitched-on smile.

Tim smiled back. “Hello, Stripe. My goal is to fold one big towel all by myself, and to put it on the shelf. I will go slow. I will do my best.”

Stripe clapped his little sock hands. “That is a good, clear goal!”

The washer sang a beep. “Beep, beep!” it said. “Good luck, Tim!”

The dryer whooshed warm air. “Whooosh! I believe in you,” it hummed.

Tim felt calm. He liked that everyone spoke kindly. “Thank you,” he said. “I will start small. First, I will pull the towel out. Then, I will smooth it. Then, I will fold it in half. Then, I will fold it again. Last, I will put it on the shelf.” He nodded. “Five little steps.”

Stripe said, “Five is a neat number!”

Tim slid close to the warm dryer. He reached his short, careful legs into the soft, warm towels. He found a big blue towel. It smelled like soap and sunshine. “Step one,” he whispered. “Pull the towel out.”

He tugged. The towel was heavy. Tim puffed a tiny puff. “Huff.” He did not rush. He did not fuss. “Slow and steady,” he said. With one more gentle pull, the towel flopped onto the floor with a cozy “fwump.”

“Step two,” said Tim. “Smooth the towel.” He pressed his flat feet and his smooth shell to the towel to make it flat. The towel felt warm on his belly. “Mmm, warm like a cuddle,” he said. He smiled. Stripe giggled.

Then Tim said, “Step three: fold in half.” He pushed one side to the other. It didn’t match. One corner stuck out like a little ear.

Stripe tilted his head. “It’s okay,” he said softly.

Tim nodded. “It is okay to try again.” He breathed in. He breathed out. “In. Out.” He tried again, slow and steady. This time the corners met.

“Step four: fold again.” Tim folded the towel one more time. He used his nose to nudge the edge. He used his strong little legs to press it flat.

“Step five: put it on the shelf,” said Tim. He looked up. The shelf was a little high. He felt a tiny worry in his tummy. The shelf seemed tall like a fence.

Stripe saw his friend pause. “Do you want help?” Stripe asked.

Tim nodded. “Yes, please,” he said. “Asking for help is smart. Thank you.”

Stripe leaned down from the basket. The dryer hummed low. “Teamwork,” it sang.

With a gentle push from Stripe and a careful lift from Tim, the folded towel reached the shelf. It sat there neat and snug. Tim’s eyes sparkled. His goal was done!

“You did it!” cheered Stripe. “You followed your steps. You kept trying.”

Tim felt proud and warm inside, like cocoa. “Thank you for your help, Stripe. Thank you, washer and dryer, for cheering me on.” He looked at the other towels. “I will set a new, small goal,” he said. “Now I will fold two small towels. I will keep my steps. I will go slow. I will be kind to others and to myself.”

Just then, a tiny mitten rolled out of the basket. It whispered, “I am scared. The washer is loud.”

Tim lowered his head to the mitten. “I hear you,” he said kindly. “The washer does go swish-swish. Would you like to listen together and count to five?”

The mitten nodded. Together, they listened. “Swish, swish,” went the washer. “One, two, three, four, five,” counted Tim in a soft voice. The mitten relaxed. “Thank you,” it said. “You are respectful and gentle.”

Tim smiled. “We all do better when we feel safe.”

Then Tim and Stripe folded two little towels. They used the same five steps. When a corner went crooked, Tim said, “It is okay. We can improve.” They tried again. The towels became neat squares.

Stripe cheered, “Practice makes us better!”

Tim nodded. “Yes. Goals help us try. Steps help us see what to do. Practice helps us improve. Being kind shows respect for others.”

At last, the laundry room was calm and tidy. The shelf held the big blue towel and two small towels, all folded with care. The warm soapy smell drifted like a happy song. The washer and dryer hummed softly, “Well done, well done.”

Tim took a deep breath. He felt peaceful. “Today I set a goal,” he said. “I followed my steps. I asked for help. I practiced. I was kind. I did it.”

Stripe gave a little bow. “Wise and steady, that’s you.”

Tim chuckled. “And brave and helpful, that’s you.”

They smiled at each other. The day felt bright in the gentle, warm room.

The lesson was clear and simple: Set a small goal. Make simple steps. Try, try again. Ask for help. Be kind and respectful. With patience, you will grow.

The End.
