# Ruby Rabbit's Rooftop Adventure

Ruby Rabbit sat on the tall rooftop, her soft brown ears drooping down. The morning sun felt warm on her fluffy fur, but she felt sad inside.

"I can't do anything right," Ruby whispered to herself. She looked at the pile of colorful blocks scattered around her. "I wanted to build a tower, but it keeps falling down!"

Just then, a cheerful blue jay landed nearby. "Hello there, little rabbit! I'm Jay. Why do you look so upset?"

Ruby's pink nose twitched. "Hi Jay. I'm Ruby. I've been trying all morning to build the tallest tower on this rooftop, but every time I stack the blocks, they tumble down. I must be really bad at building things."

Jay tilted his bright blue head. "Hmm, let me see your blocks. Maybe we can figure this out together!"

Ruby showed Jay her blocks. Some were big and heavy, others were small and light. Some were round, and some were square.

"Tell me, Ruby," said Jay, "how did you try to build your tower?"

Ruby picked up a big round block. "Well, I started with this one because it's my favorite color - purple!"

"That's a beautiful block!" said Jay. "But I'm thinking... what do you notice about its shape?"

Ruby looked closely at the round block. She rolled it gently with her paw. "Oh! It's round, so it rolls around. It's not flat like the square blocks!"

"Exactly!" chirped Jay excitedly. "You're using your thinking skills! What do you think might work better for the bottom of a tower?"

Ruby's ears perked up. She picked up a large, flat square block. "Maybe this one? It won't roll because it's flat!"

"Great thinking, Ruby! Now, what about the next block?"

Ruby thought carefully. The warm breeze ruffled her whiskers as she examined each block. "I think... maybe another big flat one? To make the bottom strong?"

"You're becoming quite the builder!" said Jay.

Ruby felt a little spark of excitement in her tummy. She carefully placed one flat block down, then another flat one on top. The tower stayed up!

"It's working!" Ruby squeaked with joy. "But what comes next?"

"What do you think?" asked Jay with a wink.

Ruby looked at her remaining blocks. "Maybe... smaller blocks on top? So the tower doesn't get too heavy?"

"Ruby, you're amazing at figuring things out!" said Jay.

Block by block, Ruby built her tower. She used her biggest, flattest blocks at the bottom. Then medium blocks in the middle. Finally, she placed the smallest blocks at the top, finishing with her favorite round purple block right at the very top.

"Look, Jay! I did it!" Ruby hopped up and down with excitement. Her tower stood tall and proud against the blue sky.

"You certainly did!" said Jay. "And do you know what the best part is?"

"What?" asked Ruby, her eyes sparkling.

"You figured it out all by yourself! You used your smart thinking brain to solve the problem. You didn't give up, even when things were tricky."

Ruby felt warm and proud inside. "I guess I am good at building! I just needed to think about it differently."

"That's right, Ruby. Sometimes when things don't work the first time, it doesn't mean we're bad at them. It just means we need to try a different way!"

Ruby hugged her beautiful tower gently. From that day on, whenever something seemed too hard, she remembered her rooftop adventure and how good it felt to figure things out for herself.

The End.
