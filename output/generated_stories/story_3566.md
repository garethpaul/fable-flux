# Milo's Helping Heart

High up in the tallest oak tree lived a little monkey named Milo. His treehouse was cozy and warm, with soft leaves for his bed and colorful flowers decorating the windows. Milo loved swinging from branch to branch, but today something was different.

"Oh no!" cried Mrs. Rabbit from below. "The storm last night knocked down all our food stores!"

Milo peeked down through his treehouse window. He could see many forest friends looking worried and sad. Baby birds were chirping hungrily in their nests, and the squirrels were searching frantically for their scattered acorns.

"I want to help," Milo said to himself, his heart feeling heavy seeing his friends in trouble. But then he frowned. "I'm just a little monkey. What can I do?"

Milo sat on his favorite branch, swinging his tail back and forth. The warm sun touched his fuzzy brown fur, and he could smell the fresh morning air. Suddenly, an idea popped into his head!

"I may be small, but I'm good at climbing!" Milo exclaimed, jumping up with excitement.

He scampered down the tree trunk, his tiny hands gripping the rough bark. "Mrs. Rabbit! Mr. Squirrel! Everyone!" he called out. "I want to help you find food!"

"That's very kind, Milo," said Mrs. Rabbit, her soft ears drooping. "But we've looked everywhere."

"I can climb higher than anyone!" Milo said with a big smile. "Let me check the treetops!"

Up, up, up Milo climbed, higher than he'd ever gone before. His little heart beat fast, but he felt brave and determined. At the very top of the trees, he found something wonderful.

"Look! Look!" Milo shouted down to his friends. "There are berries up here! Lots and lots of sweet, juicy berries!"

The other animals cheered below. But there was still a problem – how could they reach the berries so high up?

Milo thought hard, scratching his head with his tiny finger. Then his eyes lit up with another idea!

"I'll pick the berries and drop them down to you!" he called.

For the whole morning, brave little Milo worked. He carefully picked red berries, purple berries, and golden berries. His small hands got sticky and sweet, and his tummy rumbled, but he kept working. One by one, he gently tossed the berries down to his grateful friends.

"Catch!" Milo giggled as he dropped berries to the baby birds.

"Here come more!" he laughed, sending berries to the hungry squirrels.

When the sun was high in the sky, all the forest animals had full bellies and happy hearts.

"Thank you, Milo!" called Mrs. Rabbit. "You saved the day!"

"We couldn't have done it without you!" squeaked the squirrels.

Milo felt warm and glowing inside, better than he'd ever felt before. Even though he was tired, his heart felt light and happy.

"Helping others feels wonderful!" Milo said, swinging back up to his treehouse. "Even when something seems hard, I can always find a way to help."

As the stars began to twinkle that evening, Milo looked out his treehouse window at all his friends playing happily below. He had learned something very important: no matter how small you are, you can always make a big difference when you use your special talents to help others.

From that day on, whenever someone in the forest needed help, they knew they could count on Milo, the little monkey with the biggest helping heart of all.

The End.
