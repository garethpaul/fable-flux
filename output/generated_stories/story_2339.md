# Koa the Koala’s Rooftop Plan

Koa the koala woke up on a gentle, breezy morning. He stretched his soft gray arms and blinked his calm, sleepy eyes. From his cozy nest, he climbed onto the flat rooftop garden above his home. The roof felt warm from the sun, and the air smelled like fresh leaves and tiny flowers. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” and a kind wind whispered, “Whooo.”

Koa smiled. “Good morning, sky,” he said in his quiet voice. He loved the rooftop. He could see puffy clouds, hear distant giggles from the street, and feel the soft tickle of grass patches under his paws.

Today, Koa had a wish. He sat on a smooth tile and hugged his knees. “I have a big goal,” he said to himself. “I want to build a little wind flag that dances in the air.”

Koa took a calm breath. “I will make a plan,” he whispered. “A plan helps a goal come true.”

He closed his eyes and listened to his heart go thump-thump, slow and steady. Then he opened his eyes and spoke kindly to himself. “Step one: find a stick. Step two: find ribbon. Step three: ask for help. Step four: take breaks and stay safe.”

Koa felt proud. “I have my steps,” he said. “I can do this.”

He padded across the rooftop. The sun warmed his fur. A stripey cat named Miso lay in a sunny patch, purring. “Hello, Koa,” Miso meowed. “What are you doing?”

“I have a goal,” said Koa with a small smile. “I will make a wind flag. It will swish and swoosh. I made a plan with steps.”

Miso flicked her tail. “That sounds fun,” she purred. “Do you need help?”

“Yes, please,” said Koa. “I need a stick.”

They looked together. Koa peered under a bench. “I see a long twig,” he said. He touched it. “It is smooth and not sharp.” He held it up. The wind tickled the twig. “Step one, done,” Koa said softly.

“Now ribbon,” Miso meowed, hopping toward a planter. A bright scarf hung on the railing, left by their friend Lina. It fluttered shhhh-shhhh in the breeze.

Koa blinked. “I must ask first,” he said. “Goals are kind when we ask.”

He and Miso carefully climbed down one step to Lina’s open rooftop door. They knocked, tap-tap. Lina peeked out with big eyes and a bigger smile. “Hi, Koa! Hi, Miso!”

Koa spoke in his peaceful voice. “Lina, I have a goal to make a wind flag. May I please use your scarf as ribbons? I will cut two small strips. I will leave the rest. Is that okay?”

Lina nodded. “Thank you for asking so kindly,” she said. “Yes, you can use two strips. I can help you cut.”

Koa’s heart felt warm. “Thank you,” he said.

Back on the roof, the sun was bright. A cloud slid by like a fluffy boat. Lina snipped, snip-snip, two short ribbons—one blue, one yellow. Koa tied them carefully to the top of the twig. His paws moved slow and gentle. The ribbons fluttered, flutter-flutter, making soft rustle sounds.

Koa held up the flag. The wind caught it, whoosh! The ribbons danced like happy fish. Koa giggled. “It works!”

Then the wind grew stronger. The flag tugged, tug-tug, and the knot slipped. The ribbon drooped. Koa felt a little pinch in his tummy. “Oh,” he whispered. “A problem.”

He closed his eyes and took three calm breaths. In, out. In, out. In, out. “When I feel stuck, I can go back to my plan,” he said. “And I can add a new step.”

He opened his eyes. “New step: make a stronger knot.”

Koa looked at Lina and Miso. “Can we think together?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Lina. “Let’s use a double knot.” She showed Koa slowly, loop-loop, pull. Miso placed a paw to hold the twig steady.

Koa tried. Loop-loop, pull. The knot snugged tight. He tugged gently. The ribbon stayed. “Thank you,” he said.

They tested again. The wind swished the ribbons, shhhh! The colors flashed like little sunbeams. Koa’s ears wiggled with joy. “My goal is coming true,” he said.

They placed the wind flag in a planter of soft soil to keep it safe. Koa checked the last step. “Take breaks,” he read from his tiny chalk note on a tile. “And stay safe.”

“Let’s have water,” Lina said. She poured cool water into small cups. The cups felt smooth and cold in Koa’s paws. They sipped. The water tasted fresh.

Miso stretched and purred. “Your plan was calm and clear,” she said. “You asked nicely. You fixed the knot. You did it!”

Koa stood by the fluttering flag. He felt tall inside. The breeze brushed his cheeks. Birds chirped nearby. The rooftop smelled like mint leaves and warm stone. The flag whispered, “Swoosh-swoosh,” like it was singing.

Koa spoke softly, as if the sky could hear. “Today I had a goal. I made small steps. I asked for help. I stayed calm. I took breaks. My goal came true.”

Lina smiled. “We can set new goals too!” she said. “Maybe a tiny garden next.”

Koa nodded. “One step at a time,” he said with a peaceful grin.

And when the sun began to rest and the sky turned peach, the little wind flag still danced. Koa felt happy and safe. He knew he could make plans, use kind words, and enjoy the fresh air with friends.

The End.
