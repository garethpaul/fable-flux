# Ellie's Rooftop Morning

Little Ellie the elephant calf woke up as the warm sun peeked through her bedroom window. She stretched her tiny trunk and wiggled her big ears. "Good morning, sunshine!" she said to herself with a happy smile.

Ellie lived in a special building with her mama, and their home had the most wonderful rooftop garden. Every morning, Ellie loved to go up to the rooftop to start her day in a healthy way.

"Mama, may I go to the rooftop for my morning routine?" Ellie asked politely.

"Of course, my dear," Mama Elephant said, giving Ellie a gentle trunk hug. "Remember to take your time and listen to your body."

Ellie climbed the stairs slowly, one step at a time. She wasn't in a hurry because she knew that taking care of herself was important and couldn't be rushed.

On the rooftop, the fresh morning air tickled her trunk. She could smell the sweet flowers in the garden and hear the cheerful birds singing their morning songs. The soft breeze felt cool against her gray skin.

"First, I need to drink some water," Ellie said to herself. She walked to her special water bowl and took long, refreshing sips. "Glub, glub, glub! My body needs water to feel strong and happy."

Next, Ellie found her favorite sunny spot on the rooftop. She sat down carefully and closed her eyes. "Now it's time to breathe deeply," she whispered. Ellie took slow, deep breaths through her trunk. "In... and out... in... and out." The breathing made her feel calm and peaceful.

After her breathing time, Ellie stood up and began her gentle exercises. She lifted her trunk high up to the sky, then down to touch her toes. "Stretch, stretch, stretch!" she giggled. She moved her body slowly and carefully, never rushing or pushing too hard.

"One, two, three!" Ellie counted as she took small steps around the rooftop garden. Walking felt good on her feet, and she enjoyed looking at all the pretty flowers - red roses, yellow sunflowers, and purple violets.

A little butterfly landed on Ellie's trunk. "Hello, little friend," Ellie said softly, being very still and patient. She waited quietly until the butterfly was ready to fly away. "Thank you for visiting me," she whispered.

As Ellie continued her morning routine, she felt grateful for her healthy body. She touched her strong legs, wiggled her flexible trunk, and flapped her big ears. "I'm so lucky to have a body that can move and play," she said happily.

When Ellie felt tired, she knew it was time to rest. She found a comfortable spot in the shade and sat down quietly. "My body is telling me to take a break, and I should listen," she said wisely.

After resting, Ellie felt refreshed and ready for breakfast. She walked slowly back to the stairs, taking one last look at the beautiful rooftop garden.

"Mama, I finished my morning self-care!" Ellie announced proudly when she reached the kitchen.

"How wonderful, Ellie! How do you feel?" Mama asked.

"I feel strong, calm, and happy! Taking care of myself makes me feel good inside and out," Ellie said with a big smile.

Mama hugged Ellie tight. "You're learning such an important lesson, my dear. When we take care of our bodies with patience and respect, we feel our very best."

Ellie nodded happily, ready to enjoy a healthy breakfast and a wonderful day ahead.

The End.
