# Ruby Rabbit's Big Discovery

Ruby Rabbit loved her basement lab. It had shiny tables, colorful bottles, and lots of interesting gadgets that beeped and hummed. Every morning, Ruby would hop down the wooden stairs to work on her experiments.

"Good morning, lab!" Ruby said cheerfully, her fluffy tail wiggling with excitement.

But today was different. When Ruby flipped the light switch, nothing happened. The basement stayed dark and quiet. All her machines were silent.

"Oh no!" Ruby whispered. Her heart started beating fast like a tiny drum. The darkness felt scary and cold.

Ruby's ears drooped down. She wanted to run back upstairs where it was bright and safe. But then she remembered something important.

"Wait," Ruby said to herself. "I am brave. I can figure this out."

Ruby took three deep breaths, just like Mama Rabbit had taught her. "In... and out... in... and out... in... and out." Her heart slowed down to its normal rhythm.

"Now I need to listen carefully," Ruby said. She stood very still and moved her long ears around like little antennas.

She heard a soft humming sound coming from the corner. It sounded like the big generator that powered all her lab equipment.

Ruby felt along the wall with her paws. The stones were cool and bumpy under her fur. Step by step, she made her way toward the humming sound.

"There you are!" Ruby said when she found the generator. She felt around until she found a big red button that had popped out.

Ruby pushed the button back in with a satisfying *click*. Suddenly, all the lights came on! The machines started beeping happily again.

"I did it!" Ruby cheered, doing a little bunny hop dance. "I solved the problem all by myself!"

Ruby learned that when something scary happens, she could stay calm and use her senses to help. She made this her new routine: breathe deeply, listen carefully, and feel her way through problems.

From that day on, Ruby felt more confident in her lab. She knew that even when things went wrong, she had the courage and skills to fix them.

"Being brave doesn't mean I'm not scared," Ruby said to herself proudly. "It means I can be scared and still do what needs to be done."

Ruby spent the rest of the morning working on her favorite experiment - making rainbow bubbles that smelled like strawberries. Every time a bubble popped, she remembered how good it felt to be brave and solve problems on her own.

When Mama Rabbit called down for lunch, Ruby hopped up the stairs with a big smile.

"How was your morning in the lab?" Mama asked.

"I had an adventure!" Ruby said. "The lights went out, but I stayed calm and fixed everything myself. I'm getting braver every day!"

Mama Rabbit gave Ruby a warm hug. "I'm so proud of you for being brave and not giving up."

Ruby's whiskers twitched with happiness. She had learned that courage wasn't about never being afraid - it was about facing her fears and trying her best.

The End.
