# Milo Mouse’s Calm Car Ride

Milo was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, gentle eyes. He wore a tiny blue scarf that smelled like home. Today, Milo climbed into a family car with his friends, the Maple family. The car was warm and cozy. The seats felt smooth, and the air smelled like orange snacks and vanilla hand soap.

“Click,” went the seat belts.

“Vroom,” hummed the engine.

Milo sat in a little mouse bed by the window. He could see the road and the trees. The sun made golden dots on the seat. It felt like warm butter.

“Are we ready?” asked Mama Maple. Her voice was calm and steady.

“Yes!” said Max and May, the Maple twins.

Milo took a small breath. “I am ready too,” he said softly.

They were going to Grandma Maple’s house for Sunday Snacks. It was a family tradition. Every Sunday, they shared fruit slices, sweet bread, and stories. Milo loved the stories most. He loved how Grandma’s voice sounded like a quiet song.

As they drove, the road became busy. Horns beeped. A siren wailed far away. Max wiggled. May kicked her feet.

“I don’t like the beeps,” said May.

“The noises are loud,” said Max, covering his ears.

Milo felt his whiskers shake. He felt a little jumpy inside. His tiny heart went thump-thump.

He remembered something Grandma Mouse had taught him. “When the world is noisy,” she had said, “we can be quiet inside.” She called it Calm Mouse Breath.

Milo sat up and spoke kindly. “Friends, I know a calm trick. Would you like to try?”

Mama Maple glanced in the mirror. “That sounds helpful, Milo. Please teach us.”

Milo nodded. “First, put your hand—or paw—on your tummy,” he said.

Max and May placed their hands on their tummies. Milo placed his paw on his tiny mouse belly.

“Now, smell the flower.” Milo sniffed in slowly through his nose. “And blow the candle.” He blew out softly through his mouth.

Max tried. In. Out. May tried. In. Out.

The siren faded. The car hummed. “Vroom” turned into a soft “mmm.”

“I feel better,” said May, smiling.

“My tummy is gentle now,” said Max.

Milo smiled. His heart felt slow and warm. “That is Calm Mouse Breath,” he said. “My grandma taught me. It is our little tradition.”

“I love that tradition,” said Mama Maple. “Thank you for sharing.”

The traffic stopped. Red lights glowed. The car waited. A big truck was stuck. The driver waved his arms. Cars honked. It felt like a big tangle.

“We may be late,” said Mama Maple.

Max frowned. “I want snacks now.”

May crossed her arms. “I want Grandma.”

Milo felt a tiny worry. He wanted Grandma too. He wanted the sweet bread and the quiet song. He held his scarf and smelled the vanilla. He remembered: calm helps us think and speak.

Milo spoke softly. “We can use calm words. We can tell Mama what we need.”

Max nodded. “Mama, I feel wiggly.”

May added, “Mama, I feel sad.”

“Thank you for telling me,” said Mama Maple. Her voice was still calm. “We will get there soon. For now, let’s follow car rules. We sit. We buckle. We use quiet voices. That helps everyone be safe.”

Milo took another calm breath. “Smell the flower. Blow the candle.”

Max and May followed his directions. In. Out. In. Out.

They waited. The truck moved. The red lights turned green. The car rolled. The wind whooshed softly by the window. Milo watched the trees flicker—green, green, green.

“Since this is a long ride,” said Mama Maple, “let’s share a Sunday car tradition. Would you like a story?”

“Yes!” said the twins.

Milo’s whiskers twitched with joy. “Can I tell my grandma’s mouse story?” he asked.

“Please do,” said Mama Maple.

Milo began, “Long ago, in a tiny field, a little mouse learned to be calm. When the rain was loud, he listened to the patter. When the wind was strong, he tucked into his nest. He breathed in like a flower. He blew out like a candle. And he found a peaceful way.”

Max whispered, “I can hear the rain,” and tapped his fingers softly.

May closed her eyes. “I feel cozy like the nest.”

Milo added, “When friends felt upset, the mouse shared his calm. He used kind words. He listened. He followed directions to stay safe. And together, they found peaceful solutions.”

The car turned onto a sunny street. “We’re almost there,” said Mama Maple.

Milo peeked out. He could see Grandma’s house. The door was open. The porch smelled like cinnamon.

They parked. “Click,” went the seat belts. “Creak,” went the door. “Crunch,” went the gravel under feet and mouse paws.

Grandma Maple stood smiling. “You made it! Come in, my dears.”

Milo ran to her tiny mouse chair by the fruit bowl. “Grandma, I shared our Calm Mouse Breath in the car!”

Grandma bent close. Her eyes twinkled. “That is our tradition,” she said. “When we are calm, we can hear, we can think, and we can love.”

Max said, “Smell the flower.”

May said, “Blow the candle.”

Mama Maple hugged them. “Thank you, Milo, for helping us use calm and kind words. That made our trip safe and sweet.”

Milo’s heart felt bright. He tasted warm sweet bread and cool orange slices. He heard soft family voices. He smelled cinnamon and vanilla. He felt the cozy Sunday tradition wrap around them like a blanket.

Milo whispered, “Calm helps us talk. Calm helps us listen. Calm helps us find peaceful answers.”

Everyone nodded.

The End.
