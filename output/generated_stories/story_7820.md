# Splash the Dolphin's Laundry Day Adventure

Splash the dolphin loved to play and swim everywhere! One sunny morning, he swam through the open window of Mrs. Chen's house and found himself in a very strange room. There were big white boxes that hummed and buzzed, colorful clothes hanging on lines, and the sweet smell of soap bubbles in the air.

"Wow!" squeaked Splash, wiggling his fins excitedly. "What is this magical place?"

Just then, Mrs. Chen walked in carrying a basket of wet clothes. She gasped when she saw Splash splashing in her laundry sink!

"Oh my! A dolphin in my laundry room!" she said, setting down her basket. "How did you get here, little one?"

Splash's cheeks turned pink. "I... I swam through your window. I was so curious about the bubbles and sounds! But now I think I made a big mess." He looked around at the puddles of water everywhere.

Mrs. Chen smiled kindly. "It's okay to be curious, Splash, but swimming into someone's home without asking isn't the right thing to do. This is my private space."

Splash's tail drooped. He knew Mrs. Chen was right. "I'm sorry," he said honestly. "I should have asked first. That would have been the right thing to do."

"Thank you for telling the truth," Mrs. Chen said warmly. "That shows you have a good heart. But now we have a problem - how do we get you back to the ocean safely?"

Splash thought hard, moving his fins as he planned. "I need water to swim, but I also need to clean up this mess I made. That's the honest thing to do!"

"You're absolutely right!" Mrs. Chen agreed. "Let's work together."

Splash got to work right away. He used his strong tail to push the mop bucket around, helping Mrs. Chen clean up all the water. His movements were graceful and careful - he made sure not to knock over anything else.

"I have an idea!" Splash said, his eyes sparkling. "Can we fill your washing machine with water? Then I can swim through the house in small hops from one water container to another!"

Mrs. Chen clapped her hands. "That's very resourceful thinking, Splash!"

Together, they made a plan. Mrs. Chen filled buckets and containers with water, creating a path from the laundry room to the window. Splash practiced small jumps, moving carefully from container to container.

"Remember," Mrs. Chen said gently, "next time you're curious about someone's home, what should you do?"

"Ask permission first!" Splash said proudly. "And respect people's boundaries. Their homes are private spaces."

"Exactly right!" Mrs. Chen smiled.

With a happy splash, Splash jumped from the last bucket through the window and back into the sparkling ocean. He waved his fin at Mrs. Chen.

"Thank you for teaching me about being honest and respectful!" he called out. "And thank you for helping me get home safely!"

Mrs. Chen waved back. "Thank you for being truthful and helping clean up, Splash! You're always welcome to visit - just ask first next time!"

From that day on, whenever Splash was curious about something new, he always remembered to ask permission and respect others' boundaries. He learned that being honest, even when you make mistakes, is always the right choice.

The End.
