# Polly Plans a Safe Splash

Polly was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a soft, sunny voice. She loved to make art and sing new songs. One morning, Polly hopped into the bathroom with a paintbrush made from a feather and a tiny cup of water. She wanted to paint bubbles on the mirror.

“Squawk! Bubble art day!” Polly chirped.

The bathroom was shiny and full of sounds. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. Swish went the shower curtain. The floor felt cool on Polly’s feet. The soap smelled like oranges. Polly loved the bathroom, but she knew it could be slippery.

Polly paused and took a deep breath. “Safety first,” she told herself. “Think, think, think.”

She looked around. The floor had small puddles from last night’s bath. The rug was bunched up and wrinkled. The plug was near the sink. The step stool was wiggly.

Polly tapped her beak. “What is safe? What is not safe?” she asked.

Just then, her friend Tilly, a tiny turtle with a shiny shell, peeked in. “Hi, Polly! Can I help?” Tilly asked in a soft voice.

“Yes, please,” Polly said. “I want to paint bubbles on the mirror, but I want to be safe.”

Tilly nodded. “Let’s use our thinking hats,” she said.

Polly straightened her feathers. “First, the floor is wet. That is slippery.”

Tilly looked at the towel rack. “We can dry it,” she suggested.

Together they pressed a towel on the puddles. Squish, squish went the towel, and the floor felt better.

“Next,” Polly said, “the rug is bunched. That can make us trip.”

Tilly held one corner while Polly smoothed it flat. “All flat!” said Polly.

Polly pointed to the plug near the sink. “Water and plugs do not mix,” she said in a firm voice.

Tilly nodded. “We keep water away from plugs,” she repeated.

Polly and Tilly moved the little cup to the other side, far from the plug. Polly smiled. “Good choice.”

Polly stepped onto the stool. It wobbled. “Oh!” she squawked and hopped down. “This is not safe.”

Tilly pressed the stool with her tiny foot. “It wiggles.”

Polly thought. “We need a sturdy base.”

They placed the stool on the flat rug and checked the feet. Polly tested it again. No wobble now.

“Great teamwork,” Polly said. Her feathers puffed with pride.

“Can we tell others?” Tilly asked. “We can help our friends be safe too.”

“Yes,” Polly said. “That is kind and helpful. That is community service!”

Polly and Tilly made a little safety song to sing while they worked.

“Dry the puddles, smooth the rug,
Keep the water from the plug.
Check the stool, nice and tight,
We are safe and we feel right!”

Polly dipped her brush in the tiny cup of water and made soft swirls on the mirror. The swirls looked like bubbles floating up. Tilly clapped her tiny feet. Tap, tap!

The bathroom smelled fresh and citrusy. The mirror was cool to Polly’s brush. The lights were bright and warm. Polly hummed and painted, and Tilly hummed too.

Soon, their friend Max the Mouse peeked in. “Can I play?” Max asked. He wore a red scarf and had curious whiskers.

Polly smiled. “Yes, Max, but we have safety rules,” she said in an assertive but kind voice. “Please walk, do not run. Keep your scarf away from the water. And if you see a puddle, tell us.”

Max nodded. “Okay!” he said. “I will be careful.”

A moment later, Max pointed. “There is a tiny drip by the tub.”

“Thank you, Max,” Polly said. “Great noticing!” She patted a towel on the drip. “You are using your eyes and your brain. That is critical thinking.”

Max beamed. “I can help more,” he said.

Polly handed Max a dry cloth to wipe the mirror frame, far from the water. “Teamwork keeps us safe,” Polly said.

Soon, their bubble art was complete. Big circles, little circles, and swirly lines decorated the glass. Each friend added their own style. Polly’s bubbles had sparkly dots. Tilly’s had tiny turtle shapes. Max made zig-zag bubbles. They were all different, and together, the mirror looked joyful.

“I love how different we are,” Polly said. “Different is good.”

“It makes our art special,” Tilly added.

Just then, the faucet started to drip faster. Drip-drip-drip! “That sound is telling us something,” Polly said. “Let’s think.”

Max said, “Maybe the handle is not tight.”

“Let’s ask for help,” Tilly suggested. “A grown-up can fix it.”

Polly nodded. “Asking for help is safe.”

They called for Mr. Owl, the wise neighbor. Mr. Owl tightened the handle gently. “Good job noticing,” he hooted. “You kept the water away from plugs and kept the floor dry. That is very safe.”

Polly stood tall. “We were careful, and we were kind,” she said.

Mr. Owl smiled. “You also helped your community by stopping drips and making art that brings smiles.”

Polly looked at her friends. “Safety first, fun always,” she said.

They sang their safety song one more time, loud and proud:

“Dry the puddles, smooth the rug,
Keep the water from the plug.
Check the stool, nice and tight,
We are safe and we feel right!”

They all laughed and felt warm and happy inside. The bathroom was clean, bright, and safe. Their bubble art gleamed on the mirror.

The lesson was clear: Think first, look around, speak up kindly, and help others. That is how we stay safe and make our world better.

The End.
