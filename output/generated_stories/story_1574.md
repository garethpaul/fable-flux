# Benny Builds with Brave Ears

Benny was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and big, brave ears. He had a special place in his house. Down the creaky stairs, under the warm kitchen, there was a basement lab. It smelled a little like cardboard and fresh wood. Tiny lights blinked. Tools rested on a neat table. A fan hummed gently, like a friendly bee.

Benny rubbed his paws together. “Today, I will build my big idea,” he said. His voice sounded bouncy in the quiet room. “I will make a carrot-catcher cart! It will roll and lift. It will help carry carrots to Grandma Bunny.”

He had a plan on a big paper. He drew simple shapes with a blue crayon. A circle for wheels. A square for a basket. A line for a handle. He tapped the paper. “Step one. Build the base,” he said.

Clink, tap, clink. Benny picked up little wooden sticks and tiny screws. He smelled the clean wood and heard the soft tick of the clock. He moved slowly. He looked at his plan again and again. “Check the plan,” he whispered. “I can do hard things.”

His friend, Miri the mouse, scurried in. “Hi, Benny! It smells like new wood. What are you making?”

“A carrot-catcher cart,” Benny said. His eyes shone. “I’m building it so I can help Grandma. My goal is to finish before snack time.”

Miri nodded. “That is a good goal,” she squeaked. “Do you want help?”

“Thank you,” Benny said. “But I must practice. I want to learn each part. I want to build it the right way.”

Miri smiled. “I will cheer for you,” she said, sitting on a spool of string.

Benny began. He pressed two sticks together. “Glue, please,” he said to himself. Squeeze. The glue smelled sweet and sticky. He waited for it to dry. “Practice means slow and steady,” he reminded himself. He hummed, “Build, build, build,” while the fan hummed too.

“Step two. Add the wheels,” Benny said, reading his plan. He pushed a little wheel onto a shiny axle. Squeak! The wheel turned. He pushed the second wheel. Squeak! It turned too. He smiled. “I did it!”

He tried the cart base on the floor. Roll, roll. It moved a little, then stopped. “Oh,” Benny said softly. His ears drooped. “It is not ready.”

Miri clapped. “Try again. Practice makes strong,” she said.

Benny took a deep breath. He checked the plan. He saw a small note he wrote: “Make sure the wheels are even.” He touched one wheel. Wobble. “Aha,” he said. “I see.” He fixed the wobble with a tiny washer. Click. He tried again. Roll, roll, roll. Smooth!

“Step three. Build the basket,” Benny read. He cut soft cardboard. Snip, snip. He folded the sides. Rustle. He glued the corners and held them tight. “One bunny, two bunny, three bunny,” he counted while it dried.

Just then, he saw a shiny button on the table. It was bright and red. Next to it, a note said, “Emergency fan. Only press if you need help.” Benny liked the shiny button. He wanted to press it. His paw hovered.

Miri watched him. “It is pretty,” she said, “but is it part of your plan?”

Benny pulled his paw back. “No,” he said. “My plan says basket, handle, test. The button is not part of my build. I must use honest paws. I do not press things I do not need.” His chest felt warm. He smiled at his choice.

“Good integrity,” Miri said with a squeak. “You are being true.”

Benny kept going. “Step four. Add the handle.” He slid a smooth stick into place. Tap, tap. He tightened a tiny bolt. He wiped a drop of glue with a cloth that smelled like lemon. The cart looked real now, like a little helper ready to roll.

“Step five. Test the cart,” Benny said. He put a plush carrot in the basket. “Ready?” he asked.

“Ready!” Miri cheered.

Benny pushed the handle. Roll, roll. The cart moved across the floor. The wheel squeaked happily. The basket stayed strong. The plush carrot wiggled but did not fall out. Benny’s ears stood tall. “It works!” he laughed.

He tried a real carrot. It was cool and crisp in his paw. Crunchy smell filled the room. He placed it gently in the basket. The cart rolled again. Roll, roll, roll. It worked even better.

Footsteps sounded on the stairs. Thump, thump. Grandma Bunny peered in with a kind smile. “Hello, my brave builder,” she said. “What is this?”

Benny beamed. “It is my carrot-catcher cart. I set a goal. I followed my plan. I practiced. I was honest. I did not press the shiny button. And now I can help you carry carrots.”

Grandma Bunny touched the cart. “It is sturdy,” she said. “And you built it with care.”

Benny lifted the handle. “Let’s try it!” he said.

Together, they rolled the cart up the stairs. It was not too heavy. The wheels were even and strong. Outside, the garden smelled like fresh earth. Benny placed carrots into the basket. Plop, plop, plop. The cart stayed steady.

Grandma Bunny hugged him. “You met your goal,” she said softly. “You built with patience and practice. You showed integrity. You helped us both.”

Benny’s heart felt big and bright. “I can build,” he said. “I can practice. I can be honest. I can set goals and meet them.”

Miri clapped from the porch. “Hooray for Benny!”

Benny’s ears wiggled with joy. He looked at his plan. He put a check mark by each step. Check, check, check. The sun warmed his fur. The cart glowed in the light.

Lesson learned: When we make a plan, practice our steps, and do the right thing—even when no one is watching—we can build wonderful things that help others.

The End.
