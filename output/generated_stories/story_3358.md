# Otter Ollie’s Balcony Garden Day

Otter Ollie was a playful otter with bright eyes and quick paws. He lived with his family in a cozy home. Outside the living room, there was a small balcony garden. The garden was full of pots and plants. Green mint leaves tickled the air. Sweet strawberries glowed red. Basil smelled warm and peppery. A tiny lemon tree wore little yellow hats.

Every morning, Ollie padded out to the balcony. He sniffed the mint. He touched the soil. He listened to the bees buzz-buzz by the flowers.

“Good morning, plants!” Ollie sang. “Time to stretch and sip!”

One sunny day, Ollie’s friend, Miko the mouse, came to visit. Miko’s nose wiggled. “Mmm,” he squeaked. “Your garden smells like a hug.”

Ollie grinned. “It smells like breakfast,” he said. “Want to help me with our healthy morning habits?”

“What’s that?” asked Miko.

Ollie clapped his paws. “Healthy habits are good things we do every day. We drink water, we move our bodies, we eat colorful foods, and we rest.”

Miko’s eyes sparkled. “I want to learn!”

First, Ollie filled a tiny watering can. The water made a soft glug-glug sound. Sunlight sparkled on the drops like little stars. “Plants need water,” Ollie said. “We do too.” He poured a little into two cups. The cups were small and blue.

They took a sip together. “Cold and fresh,” said Miko. “It makes my body feel happy.”

Next, Ollie stretched his paws up, up, up. “Reach to the lemon hats!” he giggled. He bent down and touched his toes. He wiggled side to side like a fish. The balcony boards felt warm under his feet.

Miko copied him. “Stretch, wiggle, wiggle!” Miko laughed so hard his whiskers shook. “Moving is fun!”

Ollie nodded. “My grandma taught me this,” he said, soft and proud. “She calls it our Morning Garden Dance. It is our family tradition.”

“What is a tradition?” asked Miko.

“It’s a special thing we do again and again,” said Ollie. “We share it with people we love.”

Miko smiled. “Thank you for sharing it with me.”

They tiptoed to the strawberry pot. A big red berry peeked out. It looked like a tiny heart. Ollie pointed. “We pick together,” he said. “We wash, then we share.”

They carried the berry to a bowl. Water splashed as Ollie rinsed it. The berry felt cool and bumpy. He sliced it with a safe, soft knife. The inside was bright and juicy.

“Let’s make a color plate,” Ollie said. He picked mint leaves, one basil leaf, and a small lemon wedge. The mint smelled like candy. The basil smelled like pizza. The lemon smelled zingy, like sunshine.

Miko sniffed. “My tummy says yes.”

They sat on a little mat. “First, we breathe,” said Ollie. “In through the nose. Out through the mouth.” They took slow breaths. The breeze tickled their faces. A bee hummed hello.

Ollie handed Miko a berry slice. “Chew, chew,” he said. “Taste the sweet.” Miko bit. “Mmm,” he squeaked. “Juicy!”

Ollie nibbled the mint leaf. “Cool and green,” he said. He touched the basil to his nose. “Spicy leaf!” He dabbed a tiny bit of lemon on his tongue. “Tingle!”

Miko tried the lemon too. His eyes went wide. “Oo! Zippy!” Then he giggled. “I like zippy.”

Suddenly, Ollie noticed a droopy pot in the corner. The soil looked dry and dusty. The plant’s leaves curled like sleepy fingers.

“Oh no,” Ollie said. “Our parsley looks tired.”

Miko’s ears dipped. “How can we help?”

Ollie took a deep breath. “We can take initiative,” he said carefully. “That means we start helping right away.”

He touched the soil. “It is dry. Let’s give water.” He filled the can and poured slowly. The dirt drank with a soft slurp. He turned the pot so the plant could see the sun. He picked off a brown leaf gently.

Miko patted the pot. “Wake up, parsley. We care for you.”

Ollie smiled. “Caring is a healthy habit too.”

They waited. A breeze came. The leaves lifted a little. The parsley stood taller, just a tiny bit.

“It’s working,” whispered Miko.

Ollie nodded. “We will check again later.”

A soft voice came from the doorway. It was Nana Otter. She wore a blue scarf with little fish. “I see two good helpers,” she said. “What are you doing?”

Ollie stood tall. “We did our Morning Garden Dance. We drank water. We ate colors. We cared for parsley. I shared our tradition with Miko.”

Nana smiled. “My heart feels full.” She clapped a gentle rhythm. “Shall we end with our Thank-You Song?”

Ollie beamed. “Yes!”

They sang together, soft and simple:
“Thank you sun, thank you rain,
Thank you leaves, again, again.
Thank you friends, near and far,
Healthy habits are our star.”

Miko swayed. His tail curled in joy. “I like this song,” he said.

Nana handed them each a tiny napkin. “Remember,” she said, “healthy habits are little steps we take every day. We drink water. We move. We eat colors. We help. We rest.”

Ollie looked at Miko. “Want to come back tomorrow?”

Miko nodded. “Yes! I will bring my own cup. And I will share my family’s apple clap. It is our tradition!”

Ollie clapped. “I can’t wait.”

They cleaned up the bowls. They put the watering can away. Ollie peeked at the parsley. It was a bit brighter now.

“Good job,” he whispered. “See you soon.”

The sun felt warm. The mint smelled sweet. Two friends felt proud.

The End.
