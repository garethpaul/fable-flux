# Ollie's Garden Day

Ollie the otter lived on a sunny balcony garden. His home was filled with colorful flowers, green plants, and buzzing bees. But today, Ollie felt grumpy and tired.

"I don't want to do anything!" Ollie huffed. He flopped down next to the tomato plants. His whiskers twitched with frustration.

The morning sun felt warm on his fur, but Ollie didn't notice. The sweet smell of lavender filled the air, but he didn't care. He just felt cranky.

Mrs. Robin landed on the balcony railing. "Good morning, Ollie! What's wrong? You look upset."

"I'm tired and angry," Ollie grumbled. "I stayed up too late playing last night. Now I feel awful!"

Mrs. Robin tilted her head. "Oh my! It sounds like you need some self-care, little friend."

"Self-care? What's that?" Ollie asked, sitting up.

"Self-care means taking good care of yourself," Mrs. Robin explained. "It helps you feel better when you're tired or grumpy."

Ollie's ears perked up. "How do I do that?"

Mrs. Robin smiled. "Let's discover some ways together! First, let's make a strategy - a plan to help you feel better."

They walked to the herb garden. Mrs. Robin pointed to the mint plants. "Smell these green leaves, Ollie."

Ollie took a deep breath. The fresh, cool scent made him feel a little calmer. "That smells nice," he said softly.

"Good! Deep breathing is self-care," Mrs. Robin chirped. "Now, let's water the plants. Moving your body helps too."

Ollie picked up the small watering can. The cool water splashed gently on the flowers. Red geraniums, yellow marigolds, and purple petunias sparkled with water drops.

"This feels good," Ollie said, his anger melting away. "The flowers look happy!"

"You're helping them grow," Mrs. Robin said. "Volunteering and helping others is also self-care. It makes our hearts feel warm."

Next, they sat by the strawberry plants. Mrs. Robin showed Ollie how to eat slowly and mindfully. The sweet, juicy berries tasted delicious.

"When we eat good food slowly, we take care of our bodies," Mrs. Robin explained.

Ollie nodded, feeling much better. His tummy was happy, and his anger was gone.

"What else can I do for self-care?" Ollie asked eagerly.

"Rest is very important," Mrs. Robin said. "Let's find a cozy spot."

They made a soft nest under the big sunflower. The yellow petals created a gentle shade. Ollie curled up and closed his eyes for a few minutes.

When he opened them, Ollie felt refreshed and peaceful. The garden sounds were beautiful - bees humming, leaves rustling, and water trickling from the fountain.

"I feel so much better!" Ollie exclaimed. "I'm not angry anymore!"

Mrs. Robin nodded proudly. "You discovered that self-care works! Remember your strategy: breathe deeply, move your body, help others, eat well, and rest."

Ollie jumped up excitedly. "Can I help you find worms for your babies? That would be volunteering!"

"What a wonderful idea!" Mrs. Robin laughed.

They spent the afternoon working together in the garden. Ollie felt happy, calm, and proud. He had learned that taking care of himself helped him take care of others too.

As the sun set, Ollie made a promise. "Every day, I'll remember to do self-care. Then I can be the best otter I can be!"

Mrs. Robin smiled. "That's very wise, Ollie. Sweet dreams!"

Ollie snuggled into his cozy bed, feeling grateful for his new discovery. Self-care wasn't selfish - it was smart!

The End.
