# Ollie Otter's Clean Treehouse Day

High up in the tall oak tree, Ollie Otter lived in the most wonderful treehouse. It had wooden walls that smelled like fresh pine, windows that let in golden sunshine, and a cozy reading corner with soft pillows. But today, something was very wrong.

"Oh my whiskers!" squeaked Ollie as he looked around his treehouse. Dirty dishes were piled high in the sink, making a sour smell. Muddy pawprints covered the wooden floor. His favorite books were scattered everywhere, and his bed was a jumbled mess of tangled blankets.

Ollie's friend Bella Bunny poked her fluffy head through the window. "Good morning, Ollie! Can I come play?"

Ollie's cheeks turned pink with embarrassment. "Um... maybe later, Bella. My treehouse is too messy for visitors."

Bella's nose twitched as she smelled the stinky dishes. "Yuck! What happened here?"

"I've been having so much fun playing that I forgot to clean up," Ollie admitted, hanging his head low. "Now it's too big of a mess, and I don't know where to start."

"Don't worry!" chirped Bella. "My mama always says that cleaning is easier when friends work together. Can I help you?"

Ollie's brown eyes lit up. "Really? You'd help me even though I made this mess?"

"Of course! That's what friends do," Bella smiled. "But first, let's make a plan. My mama taught me that everything has its place."

Together, they looked around the treehouse. Bella pointed to different areas. "See? Books go on the bookshelf, dishes go in the kitchen, and clothes go in the closet. Easy!"

"You're right!" Ollie clapped his paws together. "Let's start with the smelly dishes. They're making my nose wrinkle!"

Ollie filled a basin with warm, soapy water that smelled like fresh lemons. The bubbles tickled his paws as he scrubbed each dish until it sparkled. "Look how shiny they are now!" he giggled.

While Ollie washed dishes, Bella gathered all the scattered books. "These books want to go home to their shelf," she said in a funny voice, making Ollie laugh.

Next, they worked together to sweep the muddy pawprints. Ollie held the dustpan while Bella used the broom. "Swoosh, swoosh!" went the broom as it pushed all the dirt away.

"Now for the bed!" announced Ollie. They pulled the blankets tight and fluffed the pillows until everything looked neat and cozy.

Finally, they opened all the windows wide. Fresh, clean air filled the treehouse, carrying the sweet scent of flowers from the garden below.

"Wow!" gasped Ollie, spinning around his clean treehouse. "It smells so good, and everything looks beautiful! I feel proud and happy."

"You should feel proud," said Bella. "You took responsibility for your mess and worked hard to fix it."

Ollie nodded thoughtfully. "I learned something important today. When I keep my home clean every day, it's much easier than letting it get really messy."

"And cleaning can be fun when friends help each other," added Bella.

From that day on, Ollie made sure to wash his dishes right after eating, put his books back on the shelf after reading, and keep his treehouse tidy. And whenever his friends visited, they always said the same thing: "Ollie, your treehouse smells so fresh and looks so nice!"

Ollie would smile proudly and say, "A clean home is a happy home!"

The End.
