# Toby's Big Mistake

Toby the tortoise loved living in the busy basement. Tools hung on the walls. Boxes were stacked everywhere. The concrete floor felt cool under his green shell. Best of all, there was lots of space to play ball games!

Every morning, Toby rolled his red rubber ball around the basement. *Bounce, bounce, bounce!* The sound echoed off the walls. He loved how the ball felt smooth in his flippers.

Today, Toby wanted to try something new. "I'll bounce my ball really, really high!" he said excitedly.

Toby pushed the ball down hard with both flippers. *BOING!* The ball shot up like a rocket. It flew higher than ever before. Toby watched with wide eyes as it soared through the air.

*CRASH!*

The ball knocked over Papa Rabbit's special toolbox. Screws and nails scattered everywhere. They made tiny *ping* sounds as they hit the floor. Toby's tummy felt heavy with worry.

"Oh no!" Toby gasped. His shell felt tight with fear. "What have I done?"

Papa Rabbit came running down the wooden stairs. His footsteps went *thump, thump, thump.* When he saw the mess, his ears drooped sadly.

"My tools," Papa Rabbit said quietly. "I need them to fix Mrs. Mouse's leaky faucet today."

Toby wanted to hide in his shell. But he knew that wouldn't help. His wise grandma always said, "When you make a mistake, you must make it right."

"Papa Rabbit," Toby said in a small voice. "I'm very sorry. My ball made this big mess. It was an accident, but it's still my fault."

Papa Rabbit's ears perked up a little. "Thank you for telling the truth, Toby."

"I want to help fix this," Toby said. "Can I help you pick up all the pieces?"

Papa Rabbit smiled. "That would be very kind."

Toby moved slowly but never gave up. His little flippers weren't very fast, but he kept working. One by one, he picked up each screw. *Clink, clink* went the metal pieces as he dropped them in the box.

"This one goes here," Toby said, placing a tiny nail in its special spot.

Papa Rabbit watched Toby work so hard. "You're being very helpful, Toby. Thank you for making amends."

"What does that mean?" Toby asked, still picking up screws.

"Making amends means fixing something when you've done wrong. You said sorry, and now you're helping clean up. That's how we solve problems peacefully."

Toby felt warm inside. His shell didn't feel tight anymore. Working together felt much better than hiding.

After they finished, Papa Rabbit had an idea. "Toby, would you like to learn how to bounce your ball safely?"

"Yes, please!" Toby said.

Papa Rabbit showed Toby a clear space away from the tools. "If you play here, your ball won't hit anything important."

Toby practiced in his new spot. *Bounce, bounce, bounce!* The ball went high, but now it was safe. It felt even more fun because he wasn't worried about breaking things.

"Thank you for teaching me, Papa Rabbit," Toby said. "And thank you for forgiving me."

"Thank you for making things right," Papa Rabbit replied. "That's what good friends do."

From that day on, Toby always played ball in his special safe spot. He learned that when you make a mistake, the best thing to do is say sorry and help fix it. Making amends made everyone feel better.

And whenever other basement friends made mistakes, Toby would help them learn about making amends too. Because that's what wise tortoises do.

The End.
