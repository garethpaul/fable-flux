# Benny Beaver's Big Game Day

Benny Beaver lived in the busiest basement you could ever imagine! His cozy home was filled with wood chips that smelled like fresh pine trees, colorful craft supplies scattered on tiny tables, and the gentle sound of water dripping from the pipes above.

"Tap, tap, tap!" went Benny's little paws as he worked on his newest project. He was making a special hockey stick from a smooth piece of driftwood he had found by the stream.

"This will be perfect for the big game tomorrow!" Benny said to himself, his fluffy tail swishing with excitement.

But as the day went on, Benny's tummy started to feel funny. He had never played hockey with the other woodland animals before. What if he wasn't good enough? What if he made mistakes?

"Maybe I should just stay home," Benny whispered, his voice getting smaller and smaller.

Just then, he heard familiar voices coming down the basement stairs. It was his best friend Rosie Rabbit and Oliver Owl.

"Knock, knock! Benny, are you down there?" called Rosie in her cheerful voice.

"We came to see how you're doing!" hooted Oliver gently.

Benny's ears drooped. He wanted to hide, but instead he took a deep breath. "I'm here," he called back. "Come on down!"

When his friends reached the bottom of the stairs, they could see all of Benny's wonderful crafts - tiny boats made from bark, colorful leaf paintings, and his beautiful new hockey stick.

"Wow, Benny! You made all of this?" gasped Rosie, her pink nose twitching with amazement.

"Your hockey stick looks amazing!" Oliver said, his big round eyes sparkling.

Benny felt his cheeks get warm. "Thank you, but... I'm scared about tomorrow's game. What if I'm not good at hockey? What if everyone laughs at me?"

Rosie hopped closer and put her soft paw on Benny's shoulder. "Benny, do you remember when you were scared to show us your first wooden boat?"

"Yes," Benny nodded slowly.

"But when you told us how you felt, we helped you feel better, didn't we?" Oliver reminded him kindly.

"And look how wonderful your crafts turned out when you kept practicing!" Rosie added with a big smile.

Benny's tail began to perk up. "You're right! When I told you I was worried, you helped me feel brave."

"That's what friends do," Oliver said warmly. "And hockey is just like crafting - the more you practice and talk about it, the better you get!"

"Will you help me practice right now?" Benny asked, feeling a little spark of excitement in his chest.

"Of course!" his friends said together.

They spent the rest of the afternoon in the basement, using rolled-up socks as a hockey puck. Benny practiced moving the sock with his new stick while Rosie cheered and Oliver gave helpful tips.

"Great job, Benny! You're getting better every try!" Rosie clapped her paws.

"Remember to keep your eyes on the puck," Oliver suggested gently.

The next day at the big game, Benny felt nervous butterflies in his tummy again. But this time, he remembered what his friends taught him.

"I feel a little scared, but I'm excited to try my best!" Benny told his teammates before the game started.

"That's perfectly okay!" said the other animals. "We're here to have fun together!"

Benny played his heart out, and even though he didn't score any goals, he had the most wonderful time. Every time he felt worried, he talked to his friends, and they encouraged him to keep trying.

"I'm so glad I told you how I felt," Benny said afterward, his eyes shining with happiness. "Talking about my feelings made everything better!"

The End.
