# Ellie's Brave Night

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray skin and big brown eyes. She lived in the most comfortable nursery you could imagine. The walls were painted sunny yellow. Fluffy clouds floated across the ceiling. A cozy rocking chair sat by the window.

Every night, Ellie slept peacefully in her special bed. It had the softest blankets and the fluffiest pillows. The nightlight glowed like a warm star in the corner.

But tonight was different. Ellie heard something that made her little trunk tremble. "Sniff, sniff, sob," came a tiny sound from across the room.

Ellie lifted her big ears to listen better. The sound came again. "Sniff, sniff, sob." It was coming from the toy box in the corner.

Ellie's heart beat fast. The nursery felt darker somehow. The shadows looked bigger. "Should I go see what's wrong?" she wondered. Her trunk curled up nervously.

The crying continued. It sounded so sad and lonely. Ellie took a deep breath. "Someone needs help," she whispered to herself. "I need to be brave."

Slowly, Ellie climbed out of her cozy bed. Her feet felt cold on the soft carpet. Step by step, she walked toward the toy box. Her trunk shook a little, but she kept going.

"Hello?" Ellie called softly. "Is someone there?"

The crying stopped. Then a tiny voice answered, "Yes. It's me, Buttons."

Ellie looked into the toy box. There sat a small teddy bear with one missing button eye. Tears rolled down his fuzzy brown cheeks.

"What's wrong, Buttons?" Ellie asked gently. She reached out her trunk to pat his soft head.

"I fell into the toy box," Buttons sniffled. "I can't climb out. I've been stuck here all day. I'm scared and hungry."

Ellie's big heart filled with kindness. "Don't worry," she said. "I'll help you."

But the toy box was deep. Ellie wasn't sure how to get Buttons out. She thought hard. Then she had an idea!

"Hold onto my trunk," Ellie said. She lowered her long trunk into the toy box. "I'll pull you up."

Buttons grabbed onto Ellie's trunk with his little paws. Ellie pulled gently but firmly. Up, up, up came Buttons until he was safe on the carpet.

"Thank you, Ellie!" Buttons cried happily. "You saved me! You're so brave and kind."

Ellie felt warm and proud inside. "I was scared at first," she admitted. "But I knew you needed help."

"You were scared but you helped anyway?" Buttons asked with wonder.

"That's what being brave means," Ellie explained. "It's not about not being scared. It's about doing the right thing even when you are scared."

Buttons hugged Ellie's trunk tight. "Will you be my friend?"

"Of course!" Ellie smiled. "And next time you need help, just call my name."

From that night on, Ellie and Buttons were the best of friends. Ellie learned that being brave made her feel strong and happy inside. And whenever other toys in the nursery needed help, they knew they could count on brave, kind Ellie.

The nursery felt warmer and cozier than ever. The nightlight seemed to glow brighter. And Ellie slept peacefully, knowing she had been brave when someone needed her most.

The End.
