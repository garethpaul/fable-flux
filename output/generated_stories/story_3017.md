# Ollie's Big Swimming Lesson

The warm sunshine sparkled on the little blue kiddie pool in the backyard. Ollie the otter splashed happily in the shallow water, his whiskers twitching with excitement.

"Look at me swim!" Ollie called to his friend Benny the bunny, who sat on the grass nearby. Ollie paddled in tiny circles, but something didn't feel quite right.

Benny watched with big, worried eyes. "Ollie, you're swimming in circles! Real otters swim straight and fast."

Ollie stopped paddling and felt his tummy get heavy with sadness. "I try and try, but I can't swim like the big otters on TV. They glide so smoothly through the water."

"Maybe you're just different," Benny said gently, his soft ears drooping.

"Different isn't good enough," Ollie sighed, floating on his back. The warm sun felt nice on his wet fur, but he still felt disappointed.

Just then, Mrs. Robin landed on the pool's edge with a gentle flutter. "What's wrong, little otter?"

"I can't swim properly," Ollie explained, his voice small and quiet. "I keep going in circles instead of straight lines."

Mrs. Robin tilted her head thoughtfully. "Have you tried practicing a little bit each day? Even I had to learn to fly better when I was young."

"Really?" Ollie's dark eyes grew wide with surprise.

"Oh yes!" chirped Mrs. Robin. "At first, I could only flutter from branch to branch. But I practiced every single day, and now I can soar high above the trees!"

Ollie felt a tiny spark of hope in his chest. "Will you help me practice?"

"Of course!" said Mrs. Robin kindly. "And Benny can cheer you on!"

The next morning, Ollie woke up early. The grass was still wet with dew, and the pool water felt cool and fresh. Mrs. Robin perched nearby with helpful tips.

"Try moving your paws more slowly," she suggested. "Feel the water push against them."

Ollie took a deep breath and tried again. This time, he moved his little paws more carefully. He still went in a small circle, but it was a bigger circle than before!

"I did it a little better!" Ollie squeaked with joy.

"That's improvement!" cheered Benny, hopping excitedly on his hind legs.

Day after day, Ollie practiced. Sometimes he felt frustrated when he still swam in circles. But Mrs. Robin reminded him to be patient with himself.

"Learning takes time," she would say. "Every day you're getting a little bit better."

On the fourth day, something magical happened. Ollie pushed off from one side of the pool and swam almost straight to the other side!

"Look! Look!" he called out, water dripping from his whiskers. "I swam straight!"

Benny clapped his paws together. "You did it, Ollie!"

But the best part came next. Little Squeaky the mouse appeared at the pool's edge, looking scared.

"I want to learn to swim too," Squeaky whispered. "But I'm so small and afraid."

Without hesitation, Ollie paddled over to his tiny friend. "I can teach you! I know exactly how it feels to be scared. We can practice together!"

Ollie remembered how patient Mrs. Robin had been with him. Now he could share that same kindness with someone else.

"Start with just putting your paws in the water," Ollie said gently. "We'll go very slowly."

As the warm afternoon sun shone down on them, Ollie realized something wonderful. Learning to swim better wasn't just about becoming faster or stronger. It was about being patient with himself and then sharing that patience with others.

"Thank you for teaching me that practice makes progress," Ollie told Mrs. Robin.

"And thank you for showing Squeaky the same kindness I showed you," she replied with a proud smile.

From that day on, Ollie's pool became a place where friends helped friends improve, one splash at a time.

The End.
