# Miri Mouse Makes a Picture

Miri was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She lived in a playful living room with bouncy pillows, a colorful rug, and a window that let in warm, golden light. The room smelled like fresh cookies from the kitchen, and the floor felt warm under Miri’s tiny paws.

“Today feels like a making day,” Miri squeaked. Her whiskers twitched with excitement.

On the low table, Miri found a box of chunky crayons, some paper, a glue stick, and a basket of safe, soft scraps—bits of ribbon, shiny buttons, and fuzzy pom-poms. The crayons smelled waxy and sweet. The paper felt smooth and cool.

“I want to make a big picture for everyone,” Miri said. “I will use my own ideas!”

Miri picked a crayon and drew a bright yellow sun. She added a blue sky, a pink cloud, and a rainbow with extra colors. She sang a little song as she worked. “Scribble, scribble, I can try. Colors dance and colors fly!”

Her friend Pip, a tiny brown mouse with quick feet, peeked over a pillow. “What are you doing, Miri?” he asked.

“I am making a picture,” Miri said. “It is my idea. I want it to be new.”

Pip sniffed. “New can be hard,” he said. “Maybe copy the picture in the book?” He pointed to a big picture book on the floor.

Miri looked at the book. It showed a perfect house with square windows and a tall, straight tree. Miri felt a small twist in her tummy. “I like that picture,” she said. “But I want my own picture.”

Pip nodded slowly. “Okay. I will watch.”

Miri tried something different. She took a ribbon and made a curly line for a river. She glued soft pom-poms for puffy bushes. She pressed a shiny button in the middle. “This is the sun’s wink,” she giggled.

Pip clapped his tiny paws. “That looks fun!”

Just then, Coco the cat padded in, purring like a little drum. “Mmm, what is this?” Coco asked, her whiskers tickling the air.

“It is my picture,” Miri said. “I am using my own ideas.”

Coco swished her tail. “I like the wink,” she purred. “But the river is usually blue.”

Miri looked at her curly ribbon river. It was green. She felt shy for a moment, like a small cloud was over her head. Then she took a big breath. “I know rivers are often blue,” she said. “But today, my river is green. It’s a grassy river. It is my picture.”

Coco blinked slowly. “That is creative,” she said.

Miri smiled and kept going. She wanted to try something new. She rolled a crayon under her paw and made soft dots. Tap, tap, tap. The dots looked like tiny fireflies. She used the glue stick to make sticky stars from torn paper edges. They felt a little bumpy.

Pip leaned closer. “Can I help?” he asked.

“Yes,” Miri said kindly, “but let’s be honest and use our own ideas. No copying the book, okay?”

“Okay,” Pip said. “I will make a tiny path with buttons.” He set the buttons in a wiggly line. Click, click, click.

They worked and hummed. The room was filled with color and the soft sound of purring and little mouse paws. The air smelled like glue and crayons. The window light made the buttons sparkle.

Soon, Miri stepped back. “Oh!” she said softly. “I like it.”

Just then, Grandma Mouse came in with a warm cookie tray. “What are my little artists making?” she asked, smiling.

Miri’s ears grew warm. She looked at the picture and then at the big book on the floor. She wanted Grandma to be proud. For a moment, she thought, Should I say it’s from the book?

Miri put a paw on her chest. “Grandma,” she said, “this picture is my own idea. Pip helped with the button path. We did not copy the book. We tried new things.”

Grandma Mouse’s eyes shone. “Thank you for being honest,” she said. “That is brave and kind. And look at this innovation! A ribbon river, a button path, and a winking sun. You used your own minds.”

Miri felt light, like a kite in the sky. “I like making things my way,” she said.

Grandma set the tray down. The cookies were warm and smelled of cinnamon. “Creative-expression is a beautiful thing,” she said. “When you use your ideas, the world gets new colors.”

Coco purred louder. “And new rivers,” she added.

Pip smiled. “And new paths!”

Miri taped the picture on the wall. The green river curled, the button path wiggled, and the sun’s button winked. The living room looked even more playful now.

Miri nibbled a cookie and licked a crumb from her paw. “Today I learned something,” she said. “I learned I can make art my own way, tell the truth about it, and try new ideas. That makes me feel proud and free.”

Grandma Mouse hugged her. “That is the lesson,” she said gently. “Be honest. Be creative. Try new things. You can do it yourself, and you can share.”

Miri’s whiskers danced. “Tomorrow,” she said, “I will make music with pots!”

Everyone laughed. The room felt warm and bright. The picture on the wall seemed to glow.

The End.
