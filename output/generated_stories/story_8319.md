# Fawn’s Fair Share in the Home Office

In a cozy home office, a gentle deer fawn named Fawn tiptoed onto a soft rug. The rug felt warm and fuzzy under her tiny hooves. Clickity-clack! The keyboard made cheerful little sounds. Sunlight glowed on the desk, and a little fan hummed, whirr, whirr, whirr.

Fawn peered at a basket of crunchy snacks on the shelf: apple slices, carrot sticks, and tiny nuts. “Mmm,” she whispered. Her tummy gave a happy rumble. “I love good food. It helps me grow strong.”

On the wall, there was a chart with bright stickers. It said: Snack Schedule. Monday was Mouse’s day. Tuesday was Bunny’s day. Wednesday was Fawn’s day. Today was Wednesday. Fawn smiled. “It’s my turn today!”

Just then, Mouse and Bunny scurried in. Mouse had bright eyes and quick paws. Bunny had soft ears and a gentle hop. “Good morning!” said Mouse. “Good morning!” said Bunny.

“Good morning,” Fawn said. “Today is my snack day.”

Mouse looked at the basket. “I am hungry now,” he squeaked. “Can I have the apples?”

Bunny wiggled her nose. “I want carrots, please.”

Fawn blinked. She wanted to be kind. She wanted to share. But she also wanted to be fair. Fawn took a deep breath. She listened to the soft hum of the fan and the clickity-clack of the keys. “We need to be just,” Fawn said slowly. “We have a rule on the wall. The rule helps us be fair. Today is my day.”

Mouse’s whiskers drooped. “But my tummy is grumbly.”

Bunny’s ears flopped. “Mine too.”

Fawn felt a tug in her heart. She wanted to help her friends. But she also wanted to follow the rule. Justice means fairness for everyone, she thought. Justice means the same rules for all.

Fawn nodded. “Let’s do this,” she said. “We will follow the rule and still be kind.”

“How?” asked Mouse.

Fawn pointed to the chart. “It says Wednesday is Fawn’s day. So I get to choose. I choose to share some.” She lifted the basket carefully. “We will each have a little, and we will save the rest for later.”

Mouse’s eyes brightened. “A fair share?”

“A fair share,” Fawn said. “Fair means the same and kind.”

Bunny clapped softly. “Hooray for fair!”

Fawn placed three plates on the desk. Clink, clink. She washed her hooves with a small wipe. It smelled like lemons. “Clean hooves before snack,” she said. “That keeps us healthy.” Mouse and Bunny cleaned their paws too. The wipe felt cool and smooth.

Fawn carefully counted. “One, two, three apple slices for me, one, two, three for Mouse, and one, two, three for Bunny.” She did the same with carrot sticks. Crunch! Crunch! Crunch! The carrots sounded like tiny drums. The apples were sweet and juicy. The nuts were crunchy and small, so Fawn gave just a few to Mouse and Bunny. “We chew slowly and safely,” she said.

Mouse smiled. “This tastes good.”

Bunny wiggled her ears. “Healthy food helps us be strong.”

Fawn nodded. “Yes. Good food helps our bodies and our brains.”

As they munched, a little wind from the fan tickled their faces. The office smelled like paper, pencils, and sweet apples. Then a shadow fell across the desk. It was Badger, peeking in the door. Badger looked shy. “Hello,” he murmured. “I am new here. May I sit?”

Fawn gave a warm smile. “Yes. You may sit with us.” She pulled a soft cushion closer. “Welcome. We accept new friends.”

Badger’s eyes softened. “Thank you.”

Mouse whispered, “Do we have enough snack for Badger?”

Fawn looked at the basket. “Let’s see.” She checked the chart again. It still said Wednesday: Fawn. Fawn took another breath. “Justice means we follow the rule. It is my day to choose. I choose to share a little with our new friend too.” She gave Badger two apple slices and one carrot stick. “We share so everyone gets a taste.”

Badger nibbled. “Crunch. Thank you.” He sighed with relief. “I was worried no one would want me.”

“We want you,” Bunny said. “We accept friends.”

Mouse nodded. “We play fair.”

They finished their snack. Fawn put the lids on the containers and saved the rest in the fridge box with a label: Saved for Later. “We keep things tidy,” she said. “That is kind to everyone.”

Badger raised a paw. “What if tomorrow I am hungry?”

Mouse scampered to the chart. “Tomorrow is Thursday. That is my day.” He puffed up proudly. “I will choose fair shares tomorrow.”

Bunny added, “And Friday is my day. I will be fair too.”

Fawn smiled. “We have a rule, and we keep it. That is justice. Justice helps us feel safe. Justice helps us trust each other.”

They all nodded. The fan hummed. The keys said clickity-clack one more time. The sunlight made a warm golden square on the rug, and Fawn curled her little legs and rested there.

“Today,” Fawn said softly, “we learned to be fair. We followed the rule. We shared healthy food. We welcomed a new friend.”

“And we feel happy,” Bunny said.

“And full,” Mouse giggled.

“And accepted,” Badger whispered.

Fawn’s heart felt light. “Justice is fair. Nutrition makes us strong. Acceptance makes us friends.”

They all smiled together in the cozy home office. It felt safe. It felt kind. It felt just.

The End.
