# Pippa the Parrot’s Sunny Helping Day

Pippa was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of blue on her wings. She lived in a sunny backyard filled with warm light, soft grass, and the sweet smell of oranges from a little tree. The sky was big and blue. Bees hummed. Leaves whispered. Pippa loved to make things and help friends.

“Good morning, sun!” Pippa chirped. “Let’s make today a helping day.”

Pippa hopped along the warm stepping stones. Her feet felt toasty. She saw a small puddle sparkling like a tiny mirror. “Shiny!” she squawked with a giggle. She dipped her beak and felt cool water. “Ahh!”

Then Pippa heard a soft snuffle. “Oh no,” came a tiny voice. It was Benny, a fuzzy brown bunny. His ears drooped.

“Hello, Benny,” Pippa said gently. “I hear you. What’s wrong?”

“My carrot rolled under the tall bush,” Benny sniffled. “I can see it, but I can’t reach it. I’m hungry.”

Pippa felt her heart flutter with care. She imagined how Benny felt. “You feel sad and hungry,” she said softly. “I want to help.”

Benny nodded. “Yes, please.”

Pippa looked around with her bright, curious eyes. She had an idea. “Let’s work together. I will use this twig like a tiny hook.”

She picked up a thin twig with her beak. She slid it under the bush. Scritch, scratch. The leaves brushed her feathers. “Almost there,” she said. “Can you cheer, Benny? Cheering gives me courage.”

“Go, Pippa, go!” Benny cheered, clapping his paws.

Pop! The carrot rolled out. Benny’s eyes lit up. “You did it!”

“We did it,” Pippa said. “Teamwork!”

Benny took a small bite. Crunch. “Would you like a nibble?” he asked.

“Thank you for sharing,” Pippa said, tasting the sweet carrot. “Mmm!”

Next, they heard tiny squeaks. “Help! Help!” Three little chicks stood near the birdbath. Their feathers were soft and yellow like dandelions. The water shimmered in the sun.

“What happened?” Pippa asked, tilting her head.

“Our leaf boat got stuck,” peeped the smallest chick. A big leaf floated in circles, far from the edge. The chicks looked worried.

Pippa breathed in warm air and smelled wet stone. She thought about the chicks. “You feel worried and stuck,” she said kindly. “We can help.”

Pippa flapped her wings slowly. “Let’s be a team,” she told Benny and the chicks. “I will lead, and everyone can help.”

Benny stood tall. “Tell us what to do, Pippa.”

Pippa found a long, bendy straw the children left from a picnic. She dipped one end in the water and blew softly. Whoosh. Gentle ripples moved across the birdbath. The leaf boat drifted a little.

“Chicks, can you peep softly to keep calm?” Pippa asked. “Benny, can you shade the water with your ears so the glare is not too bright for me?”

“Peep, peep,” sang the chicks in a calm, steady rhythm.

Benny stretched his big ears, making a cool shadow. “Shade ready!”

Pippa aimed the straw again. Whoosh, whoosh. Ripple by ripple, the leaf boat floated back to the edge. The smallest chick hopped with joy. “You saved our boat!”

“We saved it together,” Pippa said with a smile. “Thank you for peeping and shading.”

The chicks fluffed up. “You are a good leader,” said the biggest chick. “You listened to us.”

Pippa felt warm pride in her chest. “A leader listens and cares,” she said. “I try to feel what you feel. That is called empathy. When we understand feelings, we can help better.”

Just then, a soft whine drifted from the garden gate. “Um… excuse me,” said Moxie, a little gray puppy with paws too big for her. “I’m learning to dig a hole for a flower. But I made a mess.” Moxie’s nose had dirt on it. Her tail drooped.

Pippa looked at the muddy spot. She thought about how Moxie might feel. “You feel worried and a little embarrassed,” Pippa said kindly. “It’s okay. We can fix it.”

Moxie’s tail wagged. “Really?”

“Really,” said Pippa. “Let’s make a plan.”

Pippa took a deep breath. She felt the sun on her feathers and the breeze on her cheeks. “Benny, you are good at gentle digging. Chicks, you can pat the soil flat with your soft feet. I will carry the small trowel.”

Together, they worked. Benny scooped neat, small bits. Scrape, scrape. The chicks patted. Tap, tap. Pippa lifted the tiny trowel with her beak. Moxie brought water in a little can. Splash, splash. Soon the hole was just right. They planted a bright marigold. It smelled peppery and happy.

Moxie smiled a big puppy smile. “Thank you for helping me. I feel better.”

Pippa nodded. “We helped because we care. When we notice feelings, we can choose kind actions.”

They all sat under the orange tree and shared cool shade. The breeze tickled their ears. Bees buzzed by like tiny songs. Everyone felt calm and proud.

Benny said, “Today I learned to cheer and to share.”

The chicks peeped, “We learned to stay calm and to listen.”

Moxie wagged. “I learned it’s okay to ask for help.”

Pippa fluffed her feathers. “And I learned that a good leader uses empathy. I stop. I look. I listen. I think about how others feel. Then I help.”

They all touched paws, wings, and little feet. “Teamwork!” they cheered.

The sun began to sink, turning the sky soft orange. The marigold glowed. The backyard felt warm and safe.

Pippa chirped, “When we care, we can help. When we help, we can lead. When we work together, we all feel happy.”

Everyone smiled. Their hearts felt light.

The End.
