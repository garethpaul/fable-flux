# Buckle-Up Buddies

The family car purred like a sleepy cat. The seats were soft and warm. Sunlight made tiny stars on the window. A curious fox kit named Flick sat in his car seat, tail tucked like a fluffy scarf.

Flick’s ears twitched. He loved to watch everything. He saw trees, clouds, and a lady walking a bouncy dog. He also saw his little sister, Dot, in her car seat. She hugged a stuffed rabbit named Binky.

“Let’s go on a pretend trip!” Flick chirped. “We can drive to Rainbow Mountain!”

Dot clapped. “Binky likes rainbows!”

Mama Fox smiled in the front. “We are going to Grandma’s. But your imagination can go anywhere.”

Papa Fox drove. The tires hummed, hum hum hum, like a lullaby.

Flick leaned toward Dot. “Can I try Binky?” he asked.

Dot pulled Binky close. “No. Binky is shy.”

Flick felt a poke inside his chest. It was a small sad feeling. He wanted to hug Binky so he could pretend Binky was the Rainbow Mountain guide.

Flick took a breath. He looked at his paws. “Okay,” he said softly. “Binky can stay with you.”

Mama Fox looked in the mirror. “Thank you for asking, Flick. And thank you for hearing Dot’s no. That shows respect.”

Flick’s sad feeling felt smaller. He still wanted a guide. He looked out the window. “Hello, Cloud Captain,” he whispered to a puffy cloud. “Will you guide us?”

In his mind, the cloud wore a tiny hat. “Aye-aye!” the Cloud Captain said in a whispery whoosh.

“Dot,” Flick said. “Let’s pretend I have a cloud friend. He will show us the safe way. We can be Buckle-Up Buddies.”

Dot giggled. “Buckle-Up Buddies!”

Papa Fox turned on the air vent. A cool breeze kissed Flick’s nose. It smelled like pine and a little like lemon from the car cleaner. Flick wriggled happily.

“Cloud Captain says, ‘Stay in your seats,’” Flick reported. “He says, ‘We can wave with fingers, not with feet.’”

Dot wiggled her toes. “Feet stay cozy,” she said, tucking them under her blanket.

A red light turned the car’s hum to a quiet stop. Outside, a big truck rumbled. It made Flick’s tummy buzz. The sound was loud. Flick put his paws over his ears.

Mama Fox’s voice was gentle. “Flick, are you feeling bothered by the noise?”

Flick nodded. “The truck is growly.”

Mama cracked his window a tiny bit. Fresh air slid in. “You can say, ‘I feel scared. I need quiet,’” she said.

Flick tried the words. “I feel scared. I need quiet.”

Papa Fox smiled in the mirror. “Thank you for telling us. We can help.”

Dot held up her blanket edge. “Snuggle corner for Flick?” she offered.

Flick took the blanket edge. It was soft and warm like sunshine on fur. His belly felt calmer. “Thanks, Dot,” he said. “That feels good.”

The light turned green. The car hummed again. Flick sat up straight. “Cloud Captain says the road is a wiggly snake. We must hold the rules with gentle paws.”

“What are the rules?” Dot asked, eyes wide.

Flick counted on his toes. “Rule one: Ask before touching. Rule two: Listen to the answer. Rule three: Use kind words. Rule four: Keep your body safe in your seat.”

Dot nodded. “Rules are friends.”

They drove past a playground. Flick saw a fox pup holding a shiny sticker. The sticker sparkled like tiny stars. Flick wanted to look closer. He wanted to touch it. He felt the wanting push at him like a little wind.

He took a deep breath. “Dot,” he said, “sometimes I really, really want something. My tummy gets tight. I can ask, but I must be okay if the answer is no.”

Dot tapped Binky’s ear. “No touch Binky. But you can look.”

Flick smiled. “Looking is nice.”

The car turned. The seatbelt snug hugged him. He listened to the click-click of the blinker. He smelled something yummy. Fresh bread! Grandma’s house was near.

“Cloud Captain,” Flick said, “what is our friendship mission?”

The cloud’s whisper filled his mind. “Mission: Make space and share space.”

Flick nodded. “I can make space when Dot needs Binky. And Dot made space for my feelings when the truck was loud.”

Dot beamed. “We are good buddies.”

They pulled into Grandma’s driveway. The gravel crunched under the tires like tiny crackers. Papa parked. The car got quiet.

Mama turned around. “Before we get out, let’s check in. How does everyone feel?”

Dot said, “Happy and hungry.”

Flick thought. He felt warm, like a cozy blanket inside. “I feel proud,” he said. “I asked before touching. I heard a no. I used kind words. I told my feelings.”

Papa Fox unbuckled the belts. “That is big friendship work.”

Grandma opened the door. “Hello, my little foxes!” She smelled like cinnamon. “I have bread. And I have stickers.”

She held out a sheet of shiny star stickers. “Would you like one?” she asked.

Flick’s eyes sparkled. “Yes, please!” He pointed. “May I choose the blue star?”

“Of course,” Grandma said, smiling. “Thank you for asking.”

Dot chose a pink star. “Binky says thank you too,” she whispered.

Flick pressed the blue star on his car seat strap. It shone like a tiny rainbow mountain guide.

“Cloud Captain approves,” Flick said with a grin.

Mama hugged them both. “Friends ask. Friends listen. Friends care.”

Flick took Dot’s paw. “Buckle-Up Buddies forever.”

The End.
