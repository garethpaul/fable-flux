# Rooftop Rainbow Raccoon

On a warm evening, a musical raccoon named Riff padded up a tall stairway to a rooftop. The sun was pink and orange. The air smelled like fresh rain. Pigeons cooed, “Coo, coo,” and a soft breeze tickled Riff’s whiskers.

Riff carried a small drum, a bright paint set, and a tiny guitar. “Tonight,” Riff sang softly, “I will make rooftop art! I will sing, I will paint, and I will share.”

Riff set a big, blank paper on the floor. He dabbed a brush in blue paint. Swish, swish! Then yellow. Dab, dab! Riff tapped the drum—tap-tap-tap!—and hummed, “La, la, la.” Colors danced. Sounds sparkled. The rooftop felt happy.

Soon, neighbors peeked over the edge. A kind cat named Miso hopped up. A sleepy turtle named Tuck scooted over. Fireflies began to blink on and off like tiny stars.

“Hi, friends!” Riff said. “I’m painting a song and singing a picture!”

Miso’s eyes grew wide. “Ooh, art party!”

Tuck smiled slowly. “I like soft songs,” he whispered.

Riff kept painting and playing. But his tummy made a little rumble. He had been practicing all day. He had not had a snack or water. He had not stretched his paws. His paws felt tight. His head felt fuzzy. The brush slipped—splat! A big blob of brown fell on the bright yellow sun he had painted.

Riff froze. “Oh no.”

Miso tilted her head. “It still looks nice.”

Tuck blinked. “It looks like a cloud now,” he said.

Riff’s cheeks felt hot. “I wanted it perfect,” he whispered. “I promised a shiny sun.”

Riff tapped the drum too fast—rat-a-tat-tat!—and the sound felt bumpy. He strummed the guitar—zing!—but it sounded sharp and squeaky. The pigeons flapped and flew to the edge.

Miso stepped closer. “Riff, are you okay?”

Riff looked at his messy paws. He wanted to say, “I’m fine.” But inside, his tummy rumble said, “I am not fine.” Riff took a slow breath. He listened to the breeze. He felt the warm roof under his feet. He remembered: honesty matters.

“I need to tell the truth,” Riff said. “I am hungry and thirsty. I am tired. I did not take care of me. I thought art had to be perfect. Now I feel wobbly.”

Miso nodded. “Thank you for telling us.”

Tuck opened a tiny bag. “I have crunchy carrots,” he said. “Crunch, crunch?”

Miso held out a little water bottle. “Sip, sip,” she said with a smile.

Riff’s eyes got shiny. “Thank you. That is very kind.” He washed his paws in a little bowl. Splash, splash. He stretched his arms and tail. He rubbed his cheeks. He wiggled his toes. He took three slow breaths. In, out. In, out. In, out.

The sky turned violet. The first star peeked out.

Riff felt calmer. His tummy was happy. His head was clear. He looked at the brown blob on the yellow sun. He looked at his friends.

“I will be honest with my art, too,” Riff said. “Art is not about perfect. Art is about feelings. Art is about trying and sharing.” He touched the brown blob. “This blob can be a cozy nest. The sun can be a sunset.”

Miso purred. “I like cozy.”

Tuck nodded. “I like honest art.”

Riff dipped his brush in orange. Swish, swish. He added gentle lines. He dabbed pink and purple. Dab, dab. He tapped the drum softly—tap… tap… tap—and hummed a calm tune, “Mm-mmm, la-la.”

“Would you like to help?” Riff asked.

Miso dipped a tiny brush in white and painted little sparkles. “Twinkle, twinkle,” she sang.

Tuck pressed a sponge to the paper. “Puff, puff,” he said, making soft clouds.

Riff strummed the guitar with care—bing, bing, bing. The sounds were warm and round. The colors looked cozy and kind. Fireflies floated close and made the painting glow. The pigeons returned and cooed a gentle, “Coo.”

Riff smiled. “Thank you for your compassion,” he said. “You cared about me. You shared. You helped.”

Miso licked a paw. “You told the truth. That helped us help you.”

Tuck yawned. “Self-care makes sweet art.”

Riff signed the painting with a little paw print. He sang, “Art is trying. Art is sharing. Art is caring.” The rooftop felt like a hug.

Riff looked at the children down below and waved. “Friends,” he called softly, “when you make art, take care of you. Drink water. Have a snack. Stretch your body. Tell the truth about how you feel. If you make a blob, that blob can become a nest, or a cloud, or a new idea. Art is your heart on paper, sound, or dance.”

Miso added, “Be kind to yourself.”

Tuck added, “Be kind to others.”

Riff nodded. “And be honest. Your art will shine.”

They all sat together and watched the violet sky turn deep blue. The painting dried. It showed a soft sunset, a cozy nest, twinkly stars, and gentle clouds. It showed care, truth, and love.

Riff strummed one last warm chord. “Good night, rooftop,” he whispered.

The End.
