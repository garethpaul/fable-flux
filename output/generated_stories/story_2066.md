# Daisy the Brave Little Fawn

On a sunny morning, a gentle deer fawn named Daisy wandered onto Mrs. Chen's beautiful balcony garden. The colorful flowers smelled so sweet, and the green leaves looked so tasty! Daisy's tummy rumbled loudly.

"Oh my!" said Daisy, looking at all the wonderful plants. "These flowers are so pretty, and those lettuce leaves look delicious!"

But there was a big problem. A tall wire fence surrounded the entire garden. Daisy could see the yummy vegetables through the gaps, but she couldn't reach them. She tried stretching her neck as far as it would go.

"Hmm," she said, feeling frustrated. "I can almost touch that carrot, but not quite!"

Daisy sat down and thought hard. She could have just walked away, but her tummy was very hungry, and she really wanted to help herself. "I need to think of a way to solve this problem," she said out loud.

First, Daisy tried jumping. She hopped and bounced, but the fence was too tall. Her little legs couldn't jump that high.

"That didn't work," she said, brushing off her spotted fur. "But I won't give up!"

Next, Daisy tried squeezing through the fence gaps. She pushed her nose through one opening, then tried to fit her head. But the spaces were too small for even a little fawn.

"Ouch!" she said, pulling her head back out. "That's too tight!"

Then Daisy had a creative idea. She noticed some fallen branches near the balcony corner. "Maybe I can use these to help me!" she said excitedly.

Carefully, Daisy picked up a long, thin branch with her mouth. She poked it through the fence and tried to pull a lettuce leaf closer. The branch was too short and kept slipping.

"I need to ask for what I need," Daisy said bravely. She had learned that sometimes you have to speak up for yourself.

Just then, Mrs. Chen came out to water her plants. She saw the little fawn trying so hard to reach the vegetables.

"Oh my goodness!" said Mrs. Chen with a kind smile. "Are you hungry, little one?"

Daisy looked up with her big brown eyes. Even though she felt shy, she knew she needed to be brave and ask for help. "Yes, please," she said politely. "I've been trying to reach some food, but I can't get through your fence. Could you help me?"

Mrs. Chen's heart melted. "Of course, sweet fawn! I admire how hard you tried to solve your problem all by yourself. That shows you're very smart and determined!"

Mrs. Chen picked some fresh lettuce leaves and carrots from her garden. She opened the little gate that Daisy hadn't noticed before and placed the vegetables in a small bowl outside the fence.

"There you go!" said Mrs. Chen. "You were so clever to try different solutions, and very brave to ask for help when you needed it."

Daisy munched happily on the crisp vegetables. "Thank you so much!" she said between bites. "I learned that it's okay to ask for help, and that trying different ideas can lead to good solutions!"

From that day on, Mrs. Chen always left some vegetables out for Daisy. And Daisy learned that speaking up for herself and being creative with problem-solving were very important skills.

The End.
