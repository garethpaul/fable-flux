# Rascal's Musical Hideaway

In a cozy bedroom closet, behind the hanging clothes and shoe boxes, lived a little raccoon named Rascal. His fluffy gray fur had black rings around his bright eyes, and he wore a tiny red bow tie. Rascal loved music more than anything in the whole world.

Every day, Rascal would play his small wooden drums in his special hideaway. *Tap-tap-tap!* *Boom-boom-boom!* The closet walls made his music sound extra special, like he was in his very own concert hall.

But one morning, something was wrong. Rascal couldn't find his drumsticks anywhere!

"Oh no!" he said, looking under his soft pillow and behind his acorn collection. "Where are my drumsticks? I can't make music without them!"

Rascal felt his tummy get tight and his paws start to shake. He was getting very frustrated. He wanted to stomp his feet and cry, but then he remembered what his mama had taught him: "When you have a problem, take a deep breath and think of a solution."

Rascal took three deep breaths. *In... out... in... out... in... out.* He felt a little better.

"I need to ask for help," he said to himself. "But who can I ask?"

Just then, he heard voices outside the closet. It was Emma, the little girl who lived in the room, and her big brother Jake.

"I borrowed your crayons yesterday," Jake was saying. "Want to trade? I'll give them back if you let me use your puzzle."

"That sounds fair!" Emma replied. "You can use my puzzle, and I get my crayons back."

Rascal's ears perked up. They were making a trade! Maybe he could try that too.

He peeked out from behind a winter coat and saw Jake holding two small sticks. They looked exactly like his drumsticks!

"Excuse me," Rascal said in his politest voice, stepping out of the closet.

Emma and Jake looked surprised but not scared. They had seen Rascal before.

"Hi, Rascal!" Emma said with a smile. "What's wrong? You look sad."

"I lost my drumsticks," Rascal explained, his voice a little wobbly. "I think Jake might have them. I really need them to make my music."

Jake looked down at the sticks in his hands. "Oh! I found these yesterday and thought they were just regular sticks. I was going to use them to build a fort."

Rascal felt frustrated again, but he remembered to breathe. "Jake, those are very special to me. Could we make a trade? I have something you might like."

"What do you have?" Jake asked, curious.

Rascal scurried back to his hideaway and returned with a shiny blue marble. "This is my favorite marble. It sparkles in the light! Would you trade my drumsticks for this marble?"

Jake's eyes lit up. "Wow! That's beautiful! But I really wanted to build my fort..."

"What if," Emma suggested, "Rascal plays a special song for us with his drums, and then Jake can use some other sticks from outside for his fort?"

"That's a great idea!" Rascal said, clapping his paws together.

Jake smiled and handed over the drumsticks. "I'd love to hear your music, Rascal!"

Rascal felt so happy and proud. He had used his words, stayed calm, and found a solution that made everyone happy.

*Tap-tap-tap! Boom-boom-boom!* Rascal played the most joyful song, and Emma and Jake danced along.

"Thank you for helping me solve my problem," Rascal said. "I learned that when I'm frustrated, I can take deep breaths, use my words, and work together to find an answer!"

From that day on, whenever Rascal had a problem, he remembered to breathe, speak up for himself, and look for ways to make everyone happy.

The End.
