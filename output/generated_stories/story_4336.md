# Gigi’s Big World in a Small Room

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with soft, spotty fur and kind, curious eyes. She lived in a playful living room full of bright pillows, a rainbow rug, and a window that let in warm sunshine. The room smelled like fresh crayons and clean laundry. Music from a tiny radio drifted softly through the air.

“Good morning, world,” Gigi said, stretching her long neck. She could almost touch the ceiling with her nose. “What can I learn today?”

On the coffee table sat a puzzle of a big, round Earth. Every piece was a different color. Gigi tapped the box with her hoof. “Let’s build the world,” she whispered.

Her friends came to play: Tiko the tiger with striped pajamas, Bree the bunny with floppy ears, and Manny the mouse with a tiny blue scarf. They all smiled and giggled as they hopped onto the rainbow rug.

“What’s that?” asked Bree, her nose wiggling.

“It’s our planet,” said Gigi. She spoke slowly and kindly. “People live all over it. We are all neighbors, even if we are far away.”

Tiko blinked. “How can we be neighbors if we can’t see them from the window?”

Gigi smiled. “We can still care. Caring makes us neighbors.”

They opened the puzzle box. Pieces clacked and clicked like little seashells. Gigi sniffed the cardboard smell. “Mmm, new puzzle.”

They started to build. Gigi found a blue piece. “This looks like ocean,” she said. “Oceans are big baths for the Earth.”

Bree found a sandy piece. “This looks warm,” she said, pressing her cheek to it. “Maybe it’s a desert.”

Manny found a green curvy piece. “A forest,” he squeaked. “I hear birds when I look at it.” He closed his eyes. “Chirp, chirp!”

They placed the pieces together. Click. Click. Click. The picture grew. A big, friendly Earth appeared on the table.

“Let’s add people,” Gigi said. She brought out a basket of tiny paper friends she had made. Each had a different smile, different clothes, and different skin tones. Some wore hats. Some wore scarves. Some had curly hair. Some had straight hair. All had bright, kind eyes.

“This is Ayo,” said Gigi, placing a figure near the desert. “He loves to sing.” She sung a soft note. “Laaa.”

“This is Mei,” said Bree, placing one near a mountain. “She likes to paint with watercolors.” Bree pretended to paint with her paw.

“This is Lila,” said Manny, placing a figure near the forest. “She plants trees.” He patted the table like patting soil.

Tiko looked at the ocean. “Who lives here?”

“Fish,” said Gigi, giggling. “But people visit on boats.”

They added more figures. They added tiny paper foods too—rice bowls, bread loaves, fruit baskets, and a pot of soup. The table looked like a friendly feast.

“Everyone is different,” said Bree.

“And everyone belongs,” Gigi replied.

Just then, the radio played a song in a language they didn’t know. The words were gentle and round. The rhythm made their toes wiggle. Tiko clapped. “I don’t know the words,” he said, “but I feel happy.”

Gigi swayed her long neck like a dancing tree. “We can listen. We can learn. We can enjoy new songs. That’s part of being a good neighbor to the whole world.”

Manny pointed to the window. Raindrops began to tap, tap, tap on the glass. The room smelled like wet grass. “What if someone far away needs help?” he asked.

Gigi thought. Her tail swished slowly. “We can help from right here,” she said. “We can share. We can use our voices. We can be careful with our things so the Earth stays healthy.”

“How?” asked Tiko.

Gigi brought a box labeled Help Kit. Inside were crayons, paper, stickers, and a little jar of coins. “We can make cards to cheer someone. We can learn a kind word from another language. We can save coins to share with a clean-water project. We can recycle paper and turn off lights to care for our planet.”

They got to work. Bree drew a big sun. “For anyone who needs a smile,” she said.

Manny wrote Hello in big letters and then practiced saying hola and jambo and ni hao. He giggled at the new sounds. “Hi, neighbors!” he squeaked.

Tiko carefully put two coins into the jar. Clink. Clink. He hugged it with his striped arms. “This is for water,” he said softly.

Gigi sorted scrap paper into a blue bin. “Re-use, recycle, reduce,” she said like a gentle drumbeat. “This is civic responsibility. We help our home. Our home is this room, our street, and also our big Earth.”

They taped their cards around the puzzle Earth. The living room felt warm and bright, like a hug. The rain slowed. A pale sun peeked out and slid golden light across the rug.

Gigi looked at her friends. “What did we learn today?”

Bree raised her paw. “We learned that people are different and wonderful.”

Manny squeaked, “We learned we can be kind to neighbors, even far away.”

Tiko grinned. “We learned to help and to care for our planet.”

Gigi nodded, her eyes shiny. “Yes. That is global citizenship. We are part of one big, beautiful world. We listen, we share, and we care.”

They stood together, paws and hooves touching. Gigi spoke softly. “I promise to be a good world neighbor.”

“Me too!” said Bree.

“Me three!” squeaked Manny.

“Me four!” roared Tiko, but gently.

They laughed, and the room filled with happy sound. The puzzle Earth glowed in the sunlight, and the tiny paper people seemed to smile back.

Gigi wrapped her long neck around her friends like a comfy scarf. “Even in our playful living room,” she said, “we can do big, kind things.”

The End.
