# Beep’s Gentle Game Time

Beep was a helpful robot dog with soft silver fur that warmed like a cozy blanket. His tail made a happy whirr-whirr sound when he wagged it. Beep lived in a comfortable nursery full of bright pillows, a basket of balls, and a little window where sunbeams made golden squares on the floor. The room smelled like clean cotton and apples.

“Good morning, friends,” Beep chirped in a kind, beepy voice. “Who wants to play gentle sports?”

Mia and Sam clapped. “Me! Me!” said Mia, hopping on her toes. Sam hugged a blue pillow and smiled. “I want to kick the soft ball,” he said.

Beep rolled out a soft, squishy soccer ball. It felt like a puffy cloud. “We will warm up first,” Beep said. “Warm-ups help our bodies feel safe and strong.” He bent his robot knees. “Stretch, stretch, touch the floor. Wiggle fingers. Wiggle toes.”

Everyone stretched. The floor felt cool on their fingertips. The sun felt warm on their cheeks. Mia giggled. “My toes are tickly!”

“Now, gentle kicks,” Beep said. “Tap the ball with your foot. Tap, tap. Take turns. We care for each other.”

Mia tapped. Sam tapped. Beep wagged his tail with a soft whirr. “You are doing great,” he said. “This is exercise. Exercise helps our hearts thump-thump in a happy way.”

Just then, a new friend peeked in. It was Lina, holding a small teddy. Her eyes looked shiny like little ponds. “Hi,” she whispered. “Can I play?”

“Of course,” Beep said kindly. “Welcome, Lina.”

Mia nodded. “You can kick after me.”

Lina smiled a little. “Okay.”

They played. Tap, tap, tap. The ball rolled over the fluffy rug. It made a hush sound on the fibers. Beep counted turns. “Mia, Sam, Lina,” he said. “Everyone gets a try.”

But when it was Sam’s turn again, he felt big jumpy feelings. He wanted a long turn. He kicked the ball too hard. Thump! It bumped Mia’s shin. Mia’s eyes filled with tears. “Ouch,” she said in a tiny voice.

The nursery felt very quiet. The sun squares looked still on the floor. Sam’s face turned pink. “I didn’t mean it,” he said. His voice shook.

Beep stepped close. His paw pads were soft, like little pillows. “Let’s take gentle breaths,” he said. “In… and out.” Beep made a soft whooosh sound. Mia breathed in and out. Sam breathed in and out. Lina hugged her teddy and breathed too.

“Sam,” Beep said in his calm beepy voice, “your kick was too strong. We keep our friends safe. What can we say?”

Sam looked at Mia. “I’m sorry, Mia,” he said. “Are you okay?”

Mia sniffled. “My leg still hurts a little.”

Beep nodded. “Thank you for telling us, Mia. Our feelings matter. Our bodies matter.” He looked at Sam. “Would you like to help Mia feel better?”

Sam nodded fast. “Yes!”

Beep brought the small cool pack from the self-care basket. It smelled like mint. “We can take care,” Beep said. He placed the cool pack gently on Mia’s shin. Mia sighed. “That feels nice.”

Lina whispered, “We can also use soft kicks.”

“Good idea,” Beep said. “Sports are for fun and care. We can play safely with kind feet and kind words.”

Sam looked down. “I got too excited,” he said. “Can you show me the gentle way?”

Beep smiled. His eyes glowed a peaceful blue. “Yes. Watch.” He tapped the ball so it rolled slow like a sleepy turtle. “Gentle kicks keep friends safe.”

Sam tried. Tap. The ball moved softly. Mia smiled. “That was gentle!”

Lina tapped next. Tap. The ball rolled to Mia. Mia tapped back. Tap. The room filled with quiet thumps and happy giggles.

Beep added a new game. “Compassion Circle,” he announced. “We take turns. When you tap, say a kind word.”

Mia tapped. “Kind,” she said.

Sam tapped. “Care,” he said.

Lina tapped. “Share,” she said.

Beep tapped with his paw. “Safe,” he said.

They played Compassion Circle, saying kind words: “Gentle.” “Friend.” “Listen.” “Help.” Each word felt warm, like a snug blanket around the room.

Later, Beep said, “Water break.” He poured small cups. The water was cool and splashy. He passed out orange slices that smelled bright and sweet. “This is self-care,” Beep said. “We rest, drink, and eat to help our bodies.”

Mia took a sip. “My leg feels better.”

Sam looked at Mia. “Thank you for telling me how you felt,” he said. “I will use gentle kicks.”

Mia smiled. “Thank you for saying sorry.”

Lina hugged her teddy. “I like Compassion Circle,” she said softly. “It makes my heart feel calm.”

Beep wagged. Whirr-whirr. “When we listen and help, we show compassion. Compassion is caring about how someone feels and trying to help.”

They played one more round. Tap. “Kind.” Tap. “Gentle.” Tap. “Share.” Tap. “Safe.”

When it was time to clean up, Beep sang a tidy song. “Pick up balls, fold the mats, pat the pillows, just like that.” The mats felt smooth. The basket made a little squeak as the ball dropped in. Everyone worked together.

Before quiet time, Beep asked, “What did we learn today?”

Mia said, “We can say how we feel.”

Sam said, “We can say sorry and try again.”

Lina said, “We can play gently and share turns.”

Beep nodded. “And we practice compassion. We use peaceful words. We care for bodies with exercise and rest. Sports can be kind and fun.”

The nursery glowed with soft light. The air smelled like oranges and clean cotton. Beep curled up by the window, tail humming. Mia, Sam, and Lina snuggled on the pillows, cozy and safe.

“Good game, friends,” Beep whispered.

“Good game,” they whispered back.

The End.
