# Roo’s Rhythm of Care

Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright, curious eyes and springy feet. Every afternoon, Roo bounced to the musical practice room. The room smelled like clean wood and shiny metal. Soft light danced on drums, bells, and little xylophones. Today, Roo said, “Hop, bop, don’t stop!” and giggled.

“Hello, Roo,” said Miss Melody, the music teacher. She wore a green scarf with tiny leaves. “Today we play and learn. We move with care, and we care for our things.”

Roo’s ears perked up. “Care? Like hugs?”

“Yes,” said Miss Melody. “Care for our feelings, care for our friends, and care for our world. That is called conservation. We use only what we need, we keep things tidy, and we help the earth.”

Roo’s tail twitched with excitement. “I can do that!”

First, they warmed up. Tap, tap, tap went Roo’s little drumsticks on the practice pad. The sound was soft, like rain on a leaf. “Tap-tap-tap,” Roo said in rhythm. “I feel calm.”

Miss Melody smiled. “Great coordination, Roo. Hands together, feet steady. Breathe in, breathe out.” Roo put a paw on his chest and took a slow breath. The room felt cozy and safe.

Next, Roo saw a row of colorful shakers. “Can I try them all?” Roo asked.

“We will use only what we need,” said Miss Melody gently. “Pick one shaker, and then put it back. That’s conservation in our room. We save, we share, and we don’t waste.”

Roo picked a blue shaker that sounded like tiny shells. Shake-shake. Roo thought about the beach. He could almost smell salty air and feel warm sun. “This one makes me happy,” Roo said. “My belly feels warm.”

“That is emotional awareness,” said Miss Melody. “You notice how you feel. When we notice, we can choose kind actions.”

Roo nodded. He shook in time: shake, rest, shake, rest. His feet made a soft thump on the floor as his tail swished. Thump-thump, swish-swish. Roo laughed. “My body loves the beat!”

Miss Melody clapped. “Let’s make a music routine. Routine helps us remember and keeps our room clean.”

She pointed to a simple sign:
- Play
- Pause
- Put back
- Wipe
- Lights off, water off

Roo read the words slowly with her. “Play. Pause. Put back. Wipe. Lights off, water off.”

They started. Roo played the drum: tum-tum. He paused and listened to the quiet. The quiet sounded like a soft blanket. Then he put the drum back on its shelf. He used a little cloth to wipe the drum gently. “All clean!” Roo said.

Miss Melody turned on the sink for a second and then off again. “Hands need a quick wash. We save water. That is conservation too.”

Roo washed fast—splash, rub, rinse—and turned the water off. “I did it!” He felt proud. His chest felt big and warm.

Soon, Roo saw a friend, Tilly the tiny turtle, struggling with a tall stack of music sheets. Papers rustled like leaves. “Oh no!” Tilly cried. “They might fall!”

Roo took a breath. “I want to help,” he said. “But I feel wiggly.” His feet bounced in place.

Miss Melody whispered, “Pause and choose.” Roo closed his eyes for one breath. He felt the wiggles slow down. He reached with careful paws and held the bottom of the stack while Tilly steadied the top.

“Thank you, Roo,” Tilly said. “You are strong and gentle.”

Roo smiled. “We can share and save. Let’s use only the pages we need.” Tilly nodded. They counted ten pages. The rest went back into the folder. “We saved paper!” Roo cheered.

Miss Melody beamed. “That helps trees and turtles and joeys too.”

They played a duet: Roo on the blue shaker and Tilly on a tiny triangle. Shake-ting, shake-ting. The sound was bright, like stars. Roo’s hops matched the beat. Hop-shake, hop-shake. “I am using my body with control,” Roo said. “That’s coordination!”

After the song, Roo looked around. Some lights were still on, and a little fan hummed. “We can save energy,” Roo said. He reached for the switch. Click. The extra lights went off, and warm sunlight filled the room. “The sun is bright today.” Then he turned the fan to low. “Just enough,” he said.

Miss Melody nodded. “You are a conservation hero. You choose ‘just enough’ and not ‘too much.’”

Roo felt a happy flutter. “My heart feels light,” he said. “I can hear the quiet again.” The quiet smelled like lemon polish and soft dust on wood.

At cleanup time, Roo sang the routine song:
“Play, pause, put back,
Wipe-wipe, that’s our track.
Lights off, water off too,
Care for the earth—me and you!”

Tilly clapped, ting-ting. Miss Melody joined in. The room looked neat. The shelves shone. The instruments rested like sleepy friends.

“Today I learned,” Roo said, “to care for my feelings with a pause, to move my body with control, and to care for our world by using only what we need.”

Miss Melody gave Roo a leaf sticker. “You grew your green heart today.”

Roo held the sticker close. “I feel proud and calm,” he said. “Tomorrow, I will follow our routine again.”

Miss Melody smiled. “Little choices make a big difference.”

Roo bounced once, softly. “Hop, bop, don’t stop—caring!”

The End.
