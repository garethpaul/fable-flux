# Bubbles Across the Big Blue World

Splash! Splash! A playful dolphin named Dori peeked out of the bathtub. The bathroom was warm and steamy. The mirror was foggy. Tiny drops of water tick-tick-ticked from the faucet. Dori loved bath time. She flicked her tail and made gentle waves. “Hello, bubbles!” she giggled. “Let’s play!”

Dori wasn’t alone. A yellow duck named Dot bobbed on the water. A green washcloth frog named Flip floated nearby. “Ready for a bubble adventure?” Dori asked.

“Quack-yes!” said Dot.

“Ribbit-yes!” said Flip in a soft voice.

Dori took a deep breath. The air smelled like sweet soap—like lemons and flowers. “Today,” Dori said, “we will travel the world… right here in the tub!”

Dot blinked. “But… how?”

“With our imaginations,” Dori whispered. “And with bubbles!”

She dipped her fin into the water and swirled, swirled. Round, shiny bubbles popped up. Dori blew on them—puff, puff—and they grew big and round like little moons.

“Look,” said Dori, pointing her fin. “This bubble looks like a globe. Do you see? Blue like oceans, white like clouds.”

Dot leaned close. “I see! I see!”

Flip nodded. “I see too.”

“Let’s visit places with our hearts,” said Dori. “We can learn how friends far away care for the water we all share.”

The tub water felt warm like a hug. The bubbles smelled like clean rain. Dori wiggled her tail. “First stop: a sunny beach!” She bumped a bubble, and it floated up. In her mind, the bathroom light became the sun. The towel became a sandy shore.

On the “beach,” children laughed. “Hola!” Dori said, practicing a cheerful hello. “On this beach, friends pick up trash to keep the water safe.”

Dot paddled. “Why?”

“Because the ocean is home to many,” Dori said. “To fish, to turtles, and to dolphins like me.” She scooped a pretend candy wrapper from the water and set it on the side of the tub. “We can help too. Little hands can be big helpers.”

Flip reached with his washcloth arm. “I can help.” He lifted a pretend straw and laid it by the soap dish. He smiled. “Helping feels good.”

“Next stop,” Dori said, nudging another bubble, “a busy city with tall buildings.” The bathroom tiles sparkled like windows. “People here take quick showers to save water.”

Dot tilted her head. “Quick?”

“Quick like—quack-quack-quick!” Dori giggled. “They turn off the water while they brush. Listen.” She clicked the faucet off. The drip-drip stopped. The room became quiet. “Saving water is kind. It is kind to our big blue world.”

Flip whispered, “I like the quiet. It sounds like listening.”

Dori nodded. “Listening to the planet.”

She nudged a bubble to the mirror. It made a soft pop. “Now, let’s visit a snowy place.” She shivered and wrapped a washcloth around her back. “Brrr!” The cool glass of the mirror felt like ice. “People here keep rivers clean so the water under the ice stays fresh.”

Dot snuggled close. “How do they keep it clean?”

“They put trash in bins. They use less soap in rivers,” said Dori. “And they share warm smiles. Smiles keep hearts warm.”

Dot grinned. Flip grinned too.

“Last stop,” Dori said, swishing her tail, “a wide, wide ocean—all the oceans! We are all connected.” She tapped the tub water. “This water goes to that water. This bubble meets that bubble.”

Dot looked thoughtful. “So what we do here… matters there?”

“Yes,” said Dori softly. “We share one world. We share one big blue home.”

Suddenly, Dori spotted a problem. A tiny mountain of foam was sliding toward the drain. A toy boat was stuck. “Oh no,” she said. “The boat is trapped!”

Dot gasped. “Can we help?”

“We can,” said Dori. “We will be gentle and smart.” She took a slow breath. “We turn the water off, so it doesn’t rush.” Click. The faucet was off. The water calmed.

Flip paddled to one side of the boat. Dot paddled to the other. “On three,” said Dori. “One, two, three!” They nudged the toy free. The boat bobbed safely. They cheered, “Hooray!”

“That was good teamwork,” Dot said, proud.

“And good responsibility,” Flip added. “We helped and we used less water.”

Dori’s eyes sparkled. “We also used creative hearts. We traveled far with bubbles and care.”

The fog on the mirror began to fade. The bath water felt cooler now. Bath time was ending. Dori splashed softly and looked at her friends. “Before we finish, let’s make a promise.”

Dot sat very still. Flip listened.

Dori spoke clearly. “I promise to turn off the tap when I don’t need it. I promise to put trash in the bin. I promise to be kind to water and kind to friends, near and far.”

Dot said, “I promise to help keep our tub clean.”

Flip said, “I promise to use soap carefully—just enough!”

Dori smiled. “These small promises make a big difference for our big blue world.”

They hummed a happy tune together. The tune sounded like waves and wind. Dori drew a heart on the foggy mirror with her fin. Inside the heart, she drew tiny dots like stars. “Friends everywhere,” she said. “Friends who need clean water, just like us.”

Dot touched the heart with his little beak. Flip patted it with his soft cloth hand. They all felt warm and proud.

“Adventure over,” Dori said, “but caring continues.”

Dot yawned. “That was a good trip.”

Flip nodded. “A kind trip.”

Dori winked. “And tomorrow, we can visit again—with bubbles, with art, and with love.”

They dried off with fluffy towels that smelled like sunshine. The bathroom shone. The faucet was quiet. The boat was safe. The bubbles were gone, but the promise stayed.

And somewhere, in beaches and cities and snow, friends near and far smiled, because little helpers were helping.

The End.
