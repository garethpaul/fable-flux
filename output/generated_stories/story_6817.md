# Pip's Big Mistake

High up in the old oak tree, Pip the mouse lived in a cozy treehouse. The wooden walls were warm and brown. Sunlight peeked through tiny windows. Green leaves rustled softly outside.

Pip loved his treehouse home. He had a tiny bed made of soft moss. His acorn collection sat on wooden shelves. Everything was perfect and neat.

One sunny morning, Pip heard crying below. He poked his little pink nose out the window. Down on the ground, a baby bird sat alone.

"Chirp, chirp!" cried the little bird. "My nest fell down! All my pretty shells are gone!"

Pip's whiskers twitched. He wanted to help. But then he saw something shiny near the bird. Beautiful rainbow shells sparkled in the grass.

"Those would look perfect in my collection," Pip thought. When the baby bird hopped away to find food, Pip scurried down the tree trunk.

His tiny paws grabbed the pretty shells. They felt smooth and cool. Pip stuffed them in his cheeks and climbed back up to his treehouse.

"These will make my home even more beautiful," Pip squeaked happily. He arranged the shells on his shelf. They glittered like tiny rainbows.

But soon, Pip heard more crying. The baby bird was back, looking everywhere for her shells.

"Where are my special shells?" chirped the bird sadly. "My mama gave them to me. They're all I have left of my old nest!"

Pip's tummy felt funny. His ears drooped down. The baby bird sounded so sad. Her little voice cracked when she cried.

Pip looked at the shells on his shelf. They were beautiful, but they weren't really his. His whiskers trembled. He felt sorry and ashamed.

"I took something that wasn't mine," Pip whispered. "That was wrong. I need to make this right."

Pip's little heart beat fast. He was scared to admit his mistake. But he knew what he had to do.

The kind mouse gathered all the shells in his tiny paws. He climbed down the tree trunk carefully. His legs shook a little.

"Excuse me," Pip called softly to the baby bird. "I have something that belongs to you."

The baby bird turned around. Her eyes grew wide when she saw her shells.

"You found them!" she chirped with joy.

Pip's ears turned red. "Actually, I took them. I'm very sorry. I thought they were pretty and wanted them for my collection. But that was wrong. These are yours."

Pip placed all the shells gently in front of the baby bird. "I'm sorry I made you sad. Can you forgive me?"

The baby bird looked surprised. Then she smiled. "Thank you for bringing them back. That was very brave and honest."

Pip felt much better. His tummy stopped feeling funny. "Would you like to be friends?" he asked.

"Yes!" chirped the baby bird. "My name is Sunny."

"I'm Pip. Would you like to see my treehouse?"

Sunny clapped her little wings. Together, they climbed up to Pip's cozy home. Pip showed Sunny his acorn collection and moss bed.

"Your home is wonderful!" said Sunny. "And you're a very kind mouse for making things right."

From that day on, Pip and Sunny were the best of friends. They played together every day in the treehouse. And Pip learned that saying sorry and making amends felt much better than keeping things that weren't his.

The End.
