# Tiny Steps in the Sunny Yard

Toby was a shy turtle who lived in a cozy shell. His shell was smooth and green like a leaf. He loved the sunny backyard. The grass felt cool on his feet. The flowers smelled sweet. Bees buzzed softly, “Bzz, bzz,” and a bluebird sang, “Tweet-tweet!”

Toby liked to watch from the shade. He peeked out from behind a marigold. He saw Bunny hop fast. He saw Squirrel zip up a tree. He saw Puppy dash and bark, “Woof!” Toby pulled his head in a little. He felt small and slow.

“I wish I could be fast,” Toby whispered. His voice was as soft as a breeze.

Bluebird fluttered down and tilted her head. “Hello, Toby,” she sang. “What are you thinking?”

“I am slow,” Toby said. “I want to play. But I am shy. And I am not fast like Bunny.”

Bluebird smiled. “Being you is special,” she said. “You are careful. You are patient. You are strong in your own way.”

Toby took a tiny breath. The warm sun touched his shell. He thought and thought. “Maybe I can practice,” he said. “Maybe I can get a little faster. Maybe I can make a plan.”

Bluebird nodded. “A plan is a smart start.”

Toby looked around the sunny backyard. He saw the garden path, smooth and sandy. He saw the big rock near the daisies. He saw a shiny red bucket by the fence.

“I will try tiny steps,” Toby said. “I will practice from the marigold to the rock. Then from the rock to the bucket. I will rest and drink water. I will use my strong turtle legs.”

He told his plan to Bunny, Squirrel, Puppy, and Bluebird. “I am shy,” he said, “but I want to try.”

“We can cheer,” said Bunny, thumping softly. “Thump, thump!”

“I can count,” said Squirrel. “One, two, three!”

“I can bring water,” said Puppy. “Slosh, slosh!”

“I can sing,” said Bluebird. “Tweet-tweet-tweet!”

Toby felt warm inside. “Thank you,” he said. “Please be patient with me.”

He stood at the marigold. The grass tickled. A butterfly brushed his nose like a feather. “Ready,” said Squirrel. “One, two, three—go!”

Toby took one step. Then another. His shell felt heavy, but the sun felt good. He could smell the sweet flowers and the fresh dirt. “Slow and steady,” he said quietly. “Slow and steady.”

“Thump, thump!” Bunny cheered.

“Tweet-tweet!” Bluebird sang.

“Woof-woof!” Puppy wagged.

Toby reached the big rock. He smiled a shy little smile. “I did it,” he said. He took a sip of water. He rested in the shade.

“Good plan,” Bluebird said. “You are improving.”

Toby nodded. “Now to the red bucket,” he said. “I will try again.”

Squirrel counted, “One, two, three—go!”

Toby moved his feet. Step, step, step. He listened to the buzzing bees. He felt the warm sun and the soft breeze. He talked to himself kindly. “I can do tiny steps. I can take my time.”

He reached the red bucket. Puppy splashed a little water, and Toby giggled. “That was fun,” Toby said, surprised.

Bunny hopped in a circle. “You are faster than before!”

Toby stood taller. “I got a little better,” he said. “I am still slow, but I am stronger. I am me.”

Bluebird nodded. “We all move in different ways,” she sang. “Fast or slow, you can grow.”

The friends wanted to play a game. “Let’s make a trail,” said Squirrel. He placed leaves like stepping stones. Bunny set a flower at the end. Puppy set the bucket in the middle for water. Bluebird drew a line in the sand with her tiny feet.

Toby looked at the trail. “I can do this,” he said. “I will follow my plan. Tiny steps. Rest. Water. Try again.”

They started. Bunny hopped fast and then waited. Squirrel scurried and then clapped. Puppy trotted and then sat. Toby moved steady and calm. He tapped each leaf with his little toes. Tap. Tap. Tap.

Sometimes the wind blew. Sometimes a bee buzzed close. Toby felt shy for a moment and pulled in. Then he remembered his plan. He peeked out, took a breath, and tried again.

At last, Toby touched the flower at the end. The petals were soft like a pillow. He smelled their sweet, sunny smell. The friends cheered, “Hooray!”

Toby’s shy smile grew big. “I did it with my plan. I did it with patience. I did it with friends.”

Bluebird fluffed her feathers. “You improved,” she said. “You practiced. You were kind to yourself. That is brave.”

Toby looked at Bunny, Squirrel, and Puppy. “We are all different,” he said. “Bunny is fast. Squirrel climbs. Puppy plays. I move slow and steady. We can all play together.”

The sun slid warm across the grass. The bees hummed a sleepy song. Toby felt proud and peaceful.

He whispered, “Tiny steps help me grow.” Then he added, “Tomorrow, I will practice again.”

The End.
