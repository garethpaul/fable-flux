# Rizzo's Shed Song

Rizzo was a musical raccoon with soft gray fur and bright curious eyes. He wore a tiny red scarf and loved to make music. One sunny morning, Rizzo padded to the garden shed at the edge of the yard. The shed smelled like fresh wood and warm earth. A tiny window let in a golden square of light. Dust sparkles danced in the air like tiny stars.

Inside the shed lived old music treasures. There were wooden spoons that went clack-clack, tin cans that went bing-bong, and a small drum that went tum-tum. Rizzo’s grandma had taught him to make music with everyday things. She called it the Family Shed Song. “We make music, and we clean up too,” Grandma always said. “That is our tradition. Music and care go together.”

Rizzo tapped the drum. Tum-tum! He sang, “Hello, hello, it’s music time!” He shook a jar of buttons. Chik-chik! He smiled so big his whiskers twitched.

Just then, his friend Bella, a bright blue bird, fluttered in and perched on a rake. “Hi, Rizzo! Can I sing too?” she chirped.

“Yes!” said Rizzo. “Let’s make a shed band!”

Bella pecked a tiny bell. Ding! Rizzo swished a broom across the floor. Swish-swish! The shed smelled like hay and soap. The floor felt cool under Rizzo’s paws.

Another friend, Milo the mouse, peeked from behind a flowerpot. “I can clap,” squeaked Milo. Clap-clap!

Rizzo felt happy and excited. He hopped and tapped and sang. “Music time, music time, friends and fun and sunny shine!” They played and giggled until they were all a bit out of breath.

Then Rizzo saw the pile of spoons on the floor. He saw the bell, the drumsticks, the buttons, and the broom, all scattered. He heard Grandma’s voice in his heart. “We make music, and we clean up too.”

Rizzo looked at Bella and Milo. They were ready to play more. Rizzo’s tummy felt a little squiggly. He liked to play. But he also wanted to do what Grandma taught him.

He took a deep breath. He smelled the sweet wood and the green leaves outside. “Friends,” he said softly, “I forgot our family rule. When we play, we put things back. That is our tradition. It’s my job to care for the shed.”

Bella tilted her head. “Oh! I didn’t know,” she chirped.

Milo’s ears wiggled. “We can help,” he said. “Friends help friends.”

Rizzo felt warm and proud. “Thank you,” he said. “First, spoons back in the blue bucket.” He held the bucket and listened to the clink-clink as the spoons went in.

“Bell goes on the hook,” Bella sang, and she gently placed it. Ding!

“Buttons go in the jar,” Milo squeaked, dropping them in. Plip-plip!

Rizzo brushed the floor. Swish-swish! The broom made soft sounds, and dust sparkles danced again. The shed looked neat and cozy. The tools smiled from their places. Rizzo’s heart felt calm and strong. He knew he was being responsible.

“Grandma says,” Rizzo explained, “we share our tradition with friends. We make music, and we care for our things. That is how we show love.”

Bella fluffed her feathers. “I like your tradition,” she said. “Now I know the rule!”

Milo grinned. “Me too. Clean-up can be part of the song.”

Rizzo nodded. “Let’s sing the Clean-Up Verse!” He tapped the drum. Tum-tum. He sang in a clear, gentle voice:

“Play and sing, then tidy too,
We care for me, we care for you.
Spoons and bells and buttons bright,
Back in place, all snug and right.”

Bella joined with a lilting “La-la-la!” Milo clapped in time. They felt the rhythm in their bodies: step, tap, sweep, and smile. The shed smelled fresh and safe. The sunbeam felt warm on their faces.

When they finished, Bella whispered, “Rizzo, you were brave to speak up. You helped us learn.”

Rizzo’s whiskers lifted. “Thank you,” he said. “Being responsible means I remember to care, even when I want to keep playing.”

Milo looked at the neat shelves. “Now there’s room to dance!” he squeaked.

Rizzo laughed. “Yes! And now our music sounds even better.” He picked up two wooden sticks, with permission, and tapped a gentle beat. Tap-tap. Bella sang soft notes. Milo clapped softly. Their song floated like butterflies in the sun.

Soon it was time to go. Rizzo checked the room. “Bell on the hook?” he asked.

“Ding!” said Bella, touching it once.

“Spoons in the bucket?” asked Rizzo.

“Clink!” said Milo as he tapped the lid.

“Floor all clean?” asked Rizzo.

“Swish!” said the broom, leaning proud and tall.

Rizzo smiled. “We did it together. We shared my family tradition. We were responsible. And we had fun!”

On the way out, he closed the door gently. Click. The shed looked peaceful. Rizzo felt peaceful too. He knew Grandma would be proud. He knew his friends were proud. And he was proud of himself.

As they waved goodbye, Rizzo sang one more time, “Music and care, friends who share!” Bella and Milo sang with him, their voices warm and bright.

And that day, the shed kept their song safe, along with their promise to be responsible, every time they played.

The End.
