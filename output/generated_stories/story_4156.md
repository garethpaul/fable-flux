# Tilly Tortoise Thinks It Through

In a warm kitchen smelling like toast and cinnamon, a wise tortoise named Tilly woke up slowly. The sun painted the floor in golden squares. The kettle hummed a happy song. Tilly stretched her little legs and smiled.

“Good morning, kitchen,” Tilly said softly. Her voice was gentle, like warm tea. “Today, I have a big goal.”

She looked at a chart on the fridge. It had stickers shaped like leaves. The top said: “Tilly’s Goals.” Under it, three simple pictures showed her plan. Picture one: a little plant. Picture two: a shiny water drop. Picture three: a tidy bin.

“I will grow a plant, save water, and make less trash,” Tilly said. “I can do it if I think it through.”

She bumped her shell against a small watering can. “Oops! I must be careful,” she giggled. The can felt cool and smooth. She liked how the handle fit her little leg.

Just then, the faucet dripped. Plip. Plop. Plip. The sound made tiny ripples in the sink. Tilly tilted her head. “Is that wasting water?” she asked herself.

She did not rush. She did not guess. She used her thinking steps.

“Step one,” Tilly said. “Look and listen.” She watched the drops. She listened to the plip-plop song.

“Step two,” she said. “Ask a smart question.” Tilly asked, “What can I do to stop the drip?”

“Step three,” she said. “Try a good idea.” She reached up and turned the handle tighter. The faucet squeaked. The drip slowed…then stopped. The kitchen grew quiet and calm.

Tilly smiled. “I did it! I saved water.” She put a leaf sticker on her chart and felt proud. Her heart felt warm, like the toast smell.

Next, she scooted to the table. A seed packet waited in a little bowl. The packet rustled like dry leaves in fall. “Hello, seeds,” Tilly whispered. “I will help you grow.”

She peeked into a small pot with soft dirt. It was cool and crumbly under her little toes. “How much water do you need?” Tilly asked the seeds. “Not too much. Not too little.”

She used her steps again. “Look and listen.” She touched the soil. “It feels dry.”

“Ask a smart question,” she said. “What will help the seed without wasting?”

“Try a good idea.” Tilly filled the watering can with just a little water. She poured slowly, sip-sip-sip, like the seed was drinking tea. The water made a gentle dark circle in the soil. The smell of wet dirt rose up, fresh and earthy.

“Perfect,” Tilly said. “I can check again later.” She placed the pot on the sunny windowsill. Sunlight warmed her shell and made her sleepy for just a second.

Then she noticed the bin by the door. It was full of boxes, bottles, and a banana peel. “Oh dear,” Tilly said. “This is messy.”

She read the simple labels she had made with bright crayons: “Recycle,” “Compost,” and “Trash.” The words were big and friendly.

Tilly tapped her chin. “Let’s think it through.”

“Look and listen,” she said. She looked at the items. The banana peel felt soft and squishy. The bottle felt hard and smooth. The box felt light and papery.

“Ask a smart question,” she said. “Where does each thing go?”

“Try a good idea,” she said. She put the banana peel in “Compost.” She put the bottle and the box in “Recycle.” Only a tiny wrapper went in “Trash.”

“Less trash is best,” Tilly said. “We can keep the Earth clean.”

The kettle clicked. Steam swirled in little clouds. Tilly poured warm water into a cup for her mint tea. The mint smelled bright and happy. She sat at the table and thought about her day.

Thump, thump. A small knock came from the door. It was Pip the sparrow, tapping on the window with his beak. “Tilly!” chirped Pip. “Can I have some water? My birdbath is dry.”

Tilly nodded. “Yes, Pip. We can share, and we can save.”

She filled a tiny lid with water and set it near the window. She did not fill it to the top. “This is enough for now,” she said. “We can check and add more later.” Pip sipped and fluffed his feathers. “Thank you!” he chirped.

Tilly looked at her chart. She had saved water. She had planted a seed. She had sorted the bin. She smiled and added two more leaf stickers. The row of green leaves made her feel strong and steady.

Pip tilted his head. “Tilly, how do you do so well?”

Tilly laughed softly. “I take little steps. I think before I act. I set a goal. I ask smart questions. I try good ideas. And I check again.”

Pip hopped on the sill. “That sounds wise,” he said.

“It is,” said Tilly, “and it is kind to our Earth.”

The warm kitchen glowed as the sun moved across the floor. The seed pot sat quietly, cozy in the light. The faucet was still. The bins were neat. Tilly’s heart felt calm.

She whispered to the seed, “Grow strong.” She whispered to the sink, “Thank you for water.” She whispered to herself, “I can do it, step by step.”

And she did.

The End.
