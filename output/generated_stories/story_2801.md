# Kenny Koala's Car Ride Lesson

Kenny Koala sat quietly in his car seat, watching the trees zoom by outside the window. His soft gray fur was fluffy and clean, and his big round eyes sparkled with curiosity. The family car smelled like Mom's lavender air freshener, and the gentle hum of the engine made Kenny feel sleepy.

"Are we there yet?" asked his little sister Kira from the seat next to him.

"Not yet, sweetie," said Mom from the front seat. "We still have an hour to go to Grandma's house."

Kenny's tummy started to rumble. *Grumble, grumble, GRUMBLE!* It was getting louder and louder. He felt hungry, but he remembered the yummy snacks Mom had packed in the cooler.

"Mom, can I have a snack?" Kenny asked politely.

"Of course, honey! What would you like?" Mom replied, glancing in the rearview mirror with a smile.

Kenny looked at the cooler between the seats. He could see colorful packages peeking out. There were his favorite chocolate cookies, some salty chips, and... wait! There were also apple slices, carrot sticks, and a small container of yogurt.

His tummy rumbled again, and Kenny reached for the chocolate cookies. They looked so good with their shiny wrapper crinkling in his paws.

"Hold on there, Kenny," said Dad gently. "Remember what we talked about? What makes our bodies strong and healthy?"

Kenny paused. He remembered their talk about healthy foods. His paws felt the smooth, cool apple slices in their container. They smelled fresh and sweet, like the apple tree in their backyard.

But the cookies looked so tempting! Kenny's mouth watered thinking about the sweet chocolate chips.

"I really want the cookies, Dad," Kenny said honestly. "They look so yummy!"

"I understand, buddy," Dad said with a warm voice. "Cookies taste good, but what do you think would give you more energy for playing with Grandma's dog, Buster?"

Kenny thought hard. He touched his tummy and remembered how he felt after eating too many sweets last week. His tummy had hurt, and he felt tired instead of playful.

"The apples?" Kenny asked, though he still wanted those cookies.

"That's right!" said Mom encouragingly. "And you know what? If you eat your healthy snacks first, you can have one cookie after lunch at Grandma's."

Kenny smiled. That sounded fair! He opened the container of apple slices. *Crunch, crunch, crunch!* The apples were crisp and juicy. The sweet juice ran down his chin, and he giggled.

"These are really good!" Kenny said, surprised. "They're sweet like cookies, but they make my tummy feel happy, not heavy."

"I want apples too!" said Kira, clapping her small paws.

Mom passed back more apple slices and some carrot sticks. *Crunch, crunch!* The whole car filled with the happy sounds of healthy munching.

"I'm sorry I almost chose cookies first," Kenny said to his parents. "I know you want me to be strong and healthy."

"Thank you for saying that, Kenny," said Dad proudly. "And thank you for making a good choice. That shows you're growing up!"

Kenny felt proud and energetic. His tummy was satisfied, and he felt ready for anything. When they arrived at Grandma's house, he had plenty of energy to run and play with Buster in the yard.

"Good choices give us good energy!" Kenny told Grandma as they played fetch.

And from that day on, Kenny always remembered to choose healthy snacks first, saving treats for special times.

The End.
