# Pebble, the Mindful Parrot

Pebble was a small, creative parrot with green feathers that shimmered like leaves after rain. He lived with Nana on a peaceful front porch. The porch had a rocking chair that squeaked softly, a flower basket that smelled like sweet oranges, and a wind chime that sang tiny songs when the breeze came to play.

Every morning, Pebble liked to make things. He stacked smooth stones into tiny towers. He drew wiggly pictures with chalk on the wooden floor. He even arranged leaves into shapes that looked like little boats.

One sunny day, Pebble felt very busy inside. His feathers fluffed up. His heart went thump-thump-thump. He wanted to do everything at once.

“I will build a tall tower!” Pebble chirped. “And draw a big sun! And teach the wind chime a new song!”

Pebble grabbed a stone. Clink! He placed it on another stone. Wiggle, wobble—crash! The stones tumbled down.

“Oh no,” Pebble sighed. “I can fix it. I will do it fast.”

He hurried to draw a big yellow sun. Scritch, scratch—his chalk snapped in two.

Pebble’s wings drooped. “I can’t do anything right,” he squawked, louder than he meant to.

Just then, Nana stepped onto the porch with two cups of warm peppermint tea. The tea smelled fresh, like garden leaves. Nana’s slippers made a soft hush-hush sound on the boards.

“Good morning, Pebble,” Nana said gently. “I hear your big feelings.”

Pebble blinked. “My tower fell. My chalk broke. My heart is too bouncy.”

Nana sat in the rocking chair. Squeak, creak, squeak. “Sometimes our hearts feel like jumping frogs,” she said. “Let’s help your frog heart rest. Do you want to try a mindful pause?”

Pebble tilted his head. “What is a mindful paws? Do I need tiny parrot gloves?”

Nana chuckled. “Not paws, little friend. Pause. It means we stop, breathe, and notice. We pay kind attention to one thing at a time.”

Pebble shuffled closer. The porch boards felt warm under his feet. “Okay. Teach me.”

“First,” Nana whispered, “let’s listen. Close your eyes if you like.”

Pebble closed his eyes. The sun touched his feathers. He heard the wind chime sing, ding-ding-ding, like tiny silver bells. He heard bees hummm at the flowers. He even heard the rocking chair, squeak, creak, squeak.

“What do you hear?” asked Nana.

“I hear music in the air,” Pebble said softly.

“Now,” Nana said, “let’s smell. Breathe in through your beak.” She took a slow breath. “What do you smell?”

Pebble breathed in. “Peppermint tea,” he said. “And orange flowers. And wood that smells like sunshine.”

“Good,” Nana smiled. “Now feel. Put your wing on your chest. Is your heart still hopping?”

Pebble touched his chest. “It’s hopping slower,” he whispered.

“Let’s take three gentle breaths together,” Nana said.

They breathed in and out. In… and out. In… and out.

Pebble felt his body soften. His feathers smoothed down.

“Now,” Nana said, “let’s speak with kindness. What do you want to tell your heart?”

Pebble thought. “Dear heart,” he said softly, “we can go slow. We can do one thing at a time.”

Nana nodded. “That is peaceful communication with yourself. It helps your inside voice be a good friend.”

Pebble smiled, a tiny parrot smile. “I like being a good friend to me.”

He turned to his stones. “Hello, stones,” he said kindly. “I will build a small tower first. Then maybe a tall one later.”

Pebble placed one stone. Clink. He waited. He breathed. He placed another. Clink. He noticed how cool and smooth they felt. He listened to the soft sounds. Clink. Clink. Soon a little tower stood strong.

“I did it,” Pebble whispered.

Next, he picked up the broken chalk. He rubbed the pieces gently in his toes. “Hello, chalk,” he said. “You can still draw.” He made a small sun with short, happy rays. Scritch, scratch, scritch. The yellow lines looked warm and bright.

A breeze came, ruffling his feathers. Pebble turned to the wind chime. “Hello, wind,” he said. “Would you like to sing with me?”

He took another mindful breath and hummed, “La-la-la,” softly. The chime answered, ding-ding-ding. Pebble listened, then hummed again, la-la-la, a little slower. The chime sang back. It felt like a kind conversation, with space and smiles.

Just then, a little squirrel peeked up on the porch step. Its tiny paws pitter-pattered. “Is it safe to sit?” the squirrel squeaked.

Pebble nodded. “Yes. We are going slow today. Would you like to listen with us?”

The squirrel sat near the tower, nose twitching. Together, they breathed in the minty air and out into the sunny day.

Nana clapped quietly. “Pebble, you practiced mindfulness. You listened, smelled, felt, breathed, and spoke kindly. How do you feel now?”

Pebble lifted his wings and twirled once, softly. “I feel calm. My frog heart is resting.”

“And what did you learn?” Nana asked.

Pebble looked at his tower, his sunny drawing, and the smiling squirrel. He took one more gentle breath. “When I slow down and notice, I can be kind to myself and others. I can do one thing at a time. That makes my day feel bright.”

Nana winked. “That’s wellness, little bird.”

Pebble hopped onto the rail and watched the garden sway. The wind chime sang a soft lullaby. The porch felt peaceful and full of friends.

Pebble whispered to the breeze, “Thank you.” Then he said to himself, “I can choose calm. I can speak with kindness.” His heart agreed, steady and glad.

The End.
