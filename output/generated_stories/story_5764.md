# Rooftop Helper, Beep!

On a sunny morning, a helpful robot dog named Beep trotted onto the rooftop garden. The sky was bright blue. The air smelled like mint and tomato vines. The wind made the chimes go ting, ting, ting. Beep’s little metal paws clicked on the tiles. Click, click, click. His tail-light blinked happy yellow.

“Good morning, rooftop!” Beep beeped. His voice was warm and friendly.

Beep loved to help. He watched the plants. He watched the birds. He watched the people who came to water and play. Today, the rooftop felt busy. The wind was strong. The watering can rocked. The big sun umbrella wobbled and flapped. Flap, flap!

Mina, the gardener, held her hat. “Oh dear,” she said. “The umbrella is broken. The shade is gone. The plants will get too hot!”

The tomato leaves drooped. The mint looked thirsty. A little boy named Leo squinted. “It is too bright,” he said. “My eyes!” He put his hands over his face.

Beep’s ears—little round dishes—turned toward the umbrella. He saw a bent stick. He saw torn cloth. He heard the fabric whisper, swish, swish, in the wind. Beep’s help-o-meter blinked. H-E-L-P lit up on his chest.

“I can help,” said Beep. “Beep, beep!”

“How?” asked Mina. “We need shade. But the umbrella is broken.”

Beep wagged. “We can make shade. We can craft!” His tail-light flashed green for go.

Mina smiled. “Crafting? On the rooftop?”

“Yes!” said Beep. “We will fix and make. We can use what we have.”

Beep looked around. He saw a box. He smelled cardboard. He felt smooth cloth. Inside were old scarves, wooden sticks, clothespins, and a small roll of string. There were also colorful paper triangles and a safe, round-edge child scissors. The wind made the scarves flutter like soft flags.

“Let us make a shade tent,” said Beep. “We will make it together.”

Leo clapped. “I can help!”

“Me too,” said Mina. “Let’s take initiative. That means we start and try.”

Beep nodded. “We start now.”

First, they made a plan. Beep tapped his paw and drew a little map on a piece of cardboard. “Two sticks here,” he said. Tap, tap. “Two sticks there. Cloth on top. Clips to hold. String to tie.”

“I can hold the sticks,” said Leo. He stood tall. The wind whooshed by. Whoosh! He giggled. “It tickles my hair.”

“I will tie the string,” said Mina. “I will tie it tight and safe.”

“I will measure,” said Beep. He measured with his tail-light. It blinked red, then yellow, then green when the sticks were just right. “Perfect!”

They began. Beep pressed his soft magnet paws to the metal railing so the sticks would not slip. Mina tied gentle knots. Leo handed clothespins. Click. Click. Click. They stretched a big blue scarf. It looked like a sky river. They added a green scarf. It looked like a leaf blanket. The scarves fluttered. Flup, flup, flup.

“The wind is strong,” said Mina. “Will it blow away?”

Beep listened. The wind hummed. Hummm. He felt the pull. He thought. “We need more. We need pockets and weights.”

“Pockets?” Leo asked.

“We can craft pockets from the paper,” said Beep. “We fold, fold, fold. We tape. We tuck small stones in.”

Mina nodded. “Good idea.”

They folded the paper triangles. Fold, fold. Press, press. They made little pockets and taped them to the edges of the scarves. Tap, tap. They found smooth rooftop pebbles and put them in. Plunk, plunk. The shade tent felt heavier. It did not flap so much now.

“Smart crafting,” said Mina. “You used your brain and your paws.”

Beep’s cheeks glowed. “Thank you.”

Leo crawled under the new shade. “Ahh,” he said. “It is cool. The light is soft.”

Beep crawled in too. He smelled mint and warm bread from a nearby kitchen vent. Mmm. He heard the chimes go ting, ting, ting, but softer now under the scarves. He felt proud. His help-o-meter shone bright.

Suddenly, a tiny bird landed on the railing. Cheep! Cheep! It hopped and looked at the tent.

“The bird is curious,” said Leo.

Beep wagged. “We can make a small water dish for birds too.”

Mina clapped. “More helpfulness!”

They found a shallow lid. They rinsed it and filled it with cool water. Splash, splash. They put it in the shade, near the mint. The bird hopped closer. Sip. Sip. It fluffed its feathers and sang. Cheep-cheep-cheep!

“The plants will be happier in the shade,” Mina said. She touched a tomato leaf. It felt less hot. She smiled. “Thank you for taking initiative, Beep. You saw a problem and you started a plan. You used crafting to make a solution.”

Beep’s tail-light blinked happy rainbow. “Helping feels good,” he said. “When we help, we care. When we start, we can fix. When we craft, we make new things.”

Leo nodded. “I can help too. I can hand things. I can hold things. I can try.”

“Yes,” said Beep gently. “Helping can be big or small. Every little help matters.”

They sat together under the cozy shade. The wind blew soft. Whoosh…shhh. The mint smelled fresh. The chimes sang. The rooftop felt calm and kind.

Mina poured water for the plants. Glug, glug. “Teamwork,” she said.

Beep looked at the sky. He felt warm inside. He had been helpful. He had used his idea. He had crafted with his friends. The rooftop was better because they all helped.

Beep wagged. “Beep, beep! Helper team!”

Leo laughed. “Helper team forever!”

The End.
