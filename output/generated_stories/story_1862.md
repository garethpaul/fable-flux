# Melody Mouse and the Feelings Song

In a cozy, quiet attic filled with dusty boxes and old treasures, lived a kind little mouse named Melody. She had soft gray fur, bright black eyes, and tiny pink ears that twitched whenever she heard interesting sounds.

Melody loved exploring the attic, but lately, she had been feeling something strange in her tummy. It felt like butterflies dancing around, making her feel worried and confused. She didn't know what this feeling was or what to do about it.

One sunny morning, as golden light streamed through the small attic window, Melody heard a beautiful sound. *Ting, ting, ting!* It was coming from an old music box tucked behind a pile of books.

"Oh my!" squeaked Melody, her whiskers twitching with curiosity. "What a lovely sound!"

She scurried over to the music box and carefully opened its lid. A tiny ballerina began to spin, and the most wonderful melody filled the air. But as Melody listened, those butterfly feelings in her tummy grew stronger.

"Why do I feel so funny?" she whispered to herself, her little paws pressed against her belly.

Just then, she noticed something magical. As the music played different notes, she felt different things! When the music was high and light, she felt happy and bouncy. When it was low and slow, she felt calm and peaceful. When it played fast, she felt excited!

"The music is showing me my feelings!" Melody exclaimed, her eyes growing wide with wonder.

She began to move with the music, letting her body show what she felt inside. When she felt happy, she danced in little circles. When she felt sad, she moved slowly and gently. When she felt excited, she hopped up and down!

"I have so many different feelings," Melody said, talking to the spinning ballerina. "Sometimes I feel happy, sometimes worried, sometimes curious, and sometimes peaceful. And that's okay!"

The ballerina seemed to nod as she spun, and Melody felt much better. Those butterfly feelings in her tummy weren't scary anymore – they were just her emotions saying hello!

From that day on, whenever Melody felt confused about her feelings, she would visit the music box. She learned that when she felt angry, the music helped her stomp her feet until she felt better. When she felt scared, gentle melodies helped her breathe slowly and feel brave again.

Melody even made up her own feelings song:

"When I'm happy, I can dance!
When I'm sad, I move real slow.
When I'm angry, I can stomp!
All my feelings help me grow!"

Soon, other attic creatures heard about Melody's special music box. A shy spider named Webster and a friendly cricket called Chirp came to learn about feelings too. Melody taught them her feelings song, and they all danced together.

"Feelings are like music," Melody explained to her new friends. "They come and go, and each one is special. The important thing is to notice them and let them move through us like a beautiful song."

The quiet attic became filled with the sounds of friendship, music, and laughter. Melody had learned that understanding her feelings made her feel much happier and helped her make wonderful friends.

Now Melody knew that all feelings – happy, sad, angry, or scared – were perfectly normal and okay to have.

The End.
