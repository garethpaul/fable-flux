# Leo Learns to Take Turns

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright curious eyes. One sunny morning, Leo padded into the pantry. The pantry was cool and smelled like apples and oats. Shelves were full of crunchy carrots, shiny red apples, little boxes of cereal, and jars of peanut butter. Leo’s tummy gave a happy rumble.

“Oh! So many yummy foods,” Leo said, his whiskers twitching. “I’m hungry!”

Just then, his friends appeared at the pantry door. There was Tessa the tiny turtle, who moved slowly but smiled big. There was Max the monkey, who liked to hop and chatter. And there was Bella the bluebird, who sang sweet songs.

Max bounced. “Bananas! I see bananas!”

Tessa looked up. “I would like a carrot, please.”

Bella fluttered. “I would love some oats to make warm porridge.”

Leo felt his tummy rumble again. He saw a big jar of peanut butter. He loved peanut butter on apple slices. He thought, I will take the jar now, and I will eat first!

He reached his paw toward the jar. But Max reached for the banana at the same time. Tessa started to move toward the carrots. Bella flapped by the oats. Everyone reached and moved and grabbed at once. Hands, paws, wings, and little turtle feet all bumped together!

“Ouch,” said Tessa softly.

“My wing!” chirped Bella.

“My banana!” Max squeaked, holding on tight.

Leo pulled the jar close to his chest and felt hot inside. He looked at his friends. They looked sad and squished. The pantry did not feel happy anymore. It felt noisy and crowded.

Leo took a deep breath. He listened. The pantry hummed quiet. He smelled apples again. He heard Bella’s soft “cheep.” He saw Tessa blink slow. He felt his heart slow down.

Leo remembered what Mama Lion always said: “We take turns. We share. We care.”

Leo put the peanut butter jar back on the shelf. He stood tall, even though he was still little. “Friends,” he said in a brave, calm voice, “we are all hungry. But we bumped and bonked. That did not feel good. Let’s try taking turns.”

Max stopped bouncing. “Taking turns?”

Leo nodded. “Yes. Taking turns means we wait. We let one friend go first. Then the next friend. Everyone gets a turn.”

Tessa smiled a tiny smile. “That sounds kind.”

Bella fluttered down to a lower shelf. “Who will go first?”

Leo looked around. He saw Tessa’s slow feet. He saw Bella’s small wings. He saw Max’s wiggly hands. Leo’s tummy still rumbled, but he wanted to be kind and brave. “Tessa can go first,” he said gently. “She moves slow, and she waited so nicely.”

Tessa’s eyes sparkled. “Thank you, Leo.” She reached slowly and carefully. “I would like a crunchy carrot, please.” She picked one bright orange carrot. Crunch! She took a small bite. “Mmm. Sweet and crunchy. Carrots help me grow strong.”

Bella clapped her little wings. “My turn next?”

Leo nodded. “Yes, Bella. You may go next.”

Bella picked a bag of oats. “I will make warm porridge,” she chirped. “Oats give me energy to fly.” She smelled the oats. “They smell toasty.”

Max rubbed his tummy. “Is it my turn now?”

“It is,” Leo said. “Go ahead, Max.”

Max reached for a bunch of bananas. He peeled one with a happy squeak. “Bananas are soft and sweet. They help me jump and play.” He took a bite. “Mmm!”

Leo’s turn finally came. He felt a soft happiness inside. He reached for the jar of peanut butter and a shiny red apple. He sliced the apple into little moons. He dipped a slice into the peanut butter. “Apples are juicy and full of goodness,” he said. “Peanut butter gives me strong muscles.” He crunched. “Yum!”

They all sat on the pantry floor together. The cool tiles felt smooth. Sunlight made warm puddles on the floor. They listened to the tiny hum of the fridge and the gentle drip of the sink. They chewed and chatted.

“I like taking turns,” Tessa said between small crunches.

“It makes my belly and my heart feel good,” Max added.

“It sounds like a song,” Bella sang softly. “Take turns, show care, there is enough to share.”

Leo smiled. He felt proud and calm. His friends were happy. No one was bumped. No one was left out.

Then Leo heard a soft sniffle. In the corner, a little field mouse peeped out. “Hi,” squeaked the mouse. “I am shy. May I have a turn, too?”

Leo’s heart felt warm. “Yes,” he said kindly. “We can make room.” He scooted over and pointed to a tiny basket of berries. “These are sweet and full of vitamins. They will help you feel strong.”

The mouse’s eyes shone. “Thank you.” He took a few berries and smiled with purple lips.

When everyone finished, Leo stood up. “Friends, what did we learn?”

Max waved his banana peel toward the trash. “We learned to take turns!”

Tessa patted her shell. “Taking turns keeps us safe and happy.”

Bella sang, “Taking turns shows compassion. We care for each other.”

Leo nodded. “Yes. Taking turns helps us share food that helps our bodies grow. Taking turns helps our hearts be kind.”

They cleaned up together. Max put peels in the bin. Tessa stacked bowls. Bella brushed crumbs. Leo closed the peanut butter jar and wiped the table.

As they left the pantry, Leo felt brave and light. He had listened. He had waited. He had shared. His friends smiled and sang.

From that day on, when their tummies rumbled, they remembered the pantry lesson: We take turns, we share good food, and we care for each other.

The End.
