# Benny Beaver’s Gentle Beat

Benny was a busy beaver with soft brown fur and a wide, flat tail. He liked to tap, tap, tap on everything. One sunny afternoon, Benny trotted into the musical practice room. The room was quiet and cozy. The wooden floor smelled sweet like fresh trees. Sunlight made warm squares on the rug. The shelves held shiny drums, tiny bells, and wooden sticks. A big green plant sat by the window and waved a leaf, as if to say hello.

“Time to play!” Benny said. He clapped his paws. “Tap-tap-tap! I will make the biggest, fastest music!”

Benny grabbed two drumsticks. He hit the drum hard. Boom-boom-bang! He shook a rain stick loud. Rattle-shhh, rattle-shhh! He stomped his tail. Thump, thump, thump! The sounds were big. The sounds were fast. The sounds were everywhere.

But then Benny heard a small sound. “Eeeek.” The big green plant’s leaves trembled. A tiny dust cloud swirled from the shelves. A little bell fell and went ding… then quiet.

Benny stopped. His heart went boom-boom-boom inside his chest. “Oh no,” he whispered. “Did I scare the room?”

From the corner, a soft voice spoke. It was Ms. Lark, the music teacher. She wore a scarf with blue birds on it. Her smile was kind. “Hello, Benny. I like your energy,” she said. “Your music is strong. But the room, the instruments, and the plant need gentle care.”

Benny blinked. “Gentle care?”

Ms. Lark nodded. “We say thank you to our space. We show gratitude. We say thank you to our instruments, too. When we care for our room and our planet, our music grows calm and clear.”

Benny looked at the drum. He patted it softly. “Thank you, drum.” He turned to the plant. Its green leaves were shiny in the sun. “Thank you, plant, for clean air.” He whispered to the room, “Thank you, room, for keeping us safe and warm.”

Ms. Lark smiled. “Let’s try again. First, we breathe. In through the nose… slow… and out through the mouth… slow.”

Benny tried. In… he smelled the sweet wood and the soft, earthy plant. Out… his shoulders relaxed. His tail lay still. He felt calm, like a quiet river.

“Good,” said Ms. Lark. “Now, use gentle hands. Listen for the soft sounds.”

Benny picked up a small bell. Ding… a tiny star of sound floated in the air. He touched the drum with one paw. Tap… tap… tap… It felt like rain on a leaf. He shook the rain stick very softly. Shhhh… it sounded like a breeze in trees.

The plant’s leaves stopped shaking. The dust cloud went away. The room felt happy.

Benny grinned. “The room likes gentle music!”

Ms. Lark nodded. “Yes. When we are calm and thankful, we make room for everyone to enjoy. That is gratitude.”

Benny saw a mess on the floor. Paper scraps. A broken leaf. A sticky spot. “I can help,” he said. He picked up the paper. He put it in a bin that said “Recycle.” He carried the broken leaf to the compost bin. He wiped the sticky spot with a cloth.

“Thank you for cleaning, Benny,” said Ms. Lark. “That is environmental care. We keep our space and our planet clean.”

Benny felt warm and proud. “I am happy to help.”

He looked at the instruments. “Thank you, drum, for your deep voice.” He rubbed the drum gently. “Thank you, bell, for your tiny sparkle.” He set the bell on a soft pad. “Thank you, rain stick, for your shhhh like rain.” He put it back on the shelf, safe and straight.

Ms. Lark clapped softly. “Now play your song.”

Benny took a deep breath. In… out… He began. Tap… tap… ding… shhhh… thump… pause… tap… He listened and left space between sounds. His tail made a soft beat on the floor. Thum… thum… like a heart. The room hummed along. The plant’s leaves swayed, happy as a meadow.

When he finished, Benny was smiling wide. “That felt good,” he said. “My music was calm, and I said thank you.”

“It was beautiful,” said Ms. Lark. “You solved the problem with gratitude and care. You learned to be gentle. You helped the room, and the room helped you.”

Benny nodded. He put the sticks away. He turned off the light. He touched the door and whispered, “Thank you, door.” He waved at the plant. “Thank you, plant.” He patted his drum one more time. “Thank you, drum.”

As Benny left, he felt light like a leaf on a stream. He thought, I can be busy and calm. I can be strong and gentle. I can say thank you and care for my world.

And the musical practice room stayed tidy and sweet, ready for more soft songs and grateful hearts.

The End.
