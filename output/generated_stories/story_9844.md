# Polly's Paint Problem

In a bright, colorful art room filled with paintbrushes, crayons, and paper, lived a beautiful green parrot named Polly. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds in the sunshine streaming through the windows. Polly loved to create art with her talented beak and claws.

"Squawk! Time to paint!" Polly chirped happily as she fluttered to her favorite easel. The art room smelled like fresh paint and new paper - her favorite smells in the whole world.

Polly was working on a special painting of a rainbow forest when her friend Benny the bunny hopped into the room. His soft gray fur was covered in mud from playing outside.

"Hi Polly!" Benny called out. "Can I paint with you? This looks so fun!"

Before Polly could answer, Benny jumped right onto her painting table. His muddy paws left brown prints all over Polly's clean workspace. Then he grabbed her favorite blue paintbrush without asking.

"Wait, Benny!" Polly squawked, but Benny was already dipping the brush into the red paint. Drip, drop, splash! Red paint splattered everywhere - on Polly's rainbow painting, on her clean feathers, and all over the table.

Polly felt her heart beating fast. She loved sharing, but this didn't feel good. "Stop, please!" she said, spreading her colorful wings wide. "You're making a mess of my special painting!"

Benny looked surprised. "But I just wanted to help make it prettier," he said, still holding the dripping paintbrush.

Polly took a deep breath, just like her teacher had shown her. "Benny, I like playing with friends, but I need you to ask before using my things. And look - your muddy paws are getting my clean art supplies dirty."

She pointed with her wing to show Benny the muddy prints and paint splatters. "When we keep our art space clean, we take care of our environment and make better art together."

Benny's ears drooped. "Oh no! I'm sorry, Polly. I didn't mean to mess up your beautiful painting."

"It's okay," Polly said kindly. "Let's fix this together! First, let's clean you up so you don't spread more mud around."

Polly showed Benny how to wash his paws in the special art sink. The warm, soapy water felt good and smelled like lavender. "Clean paws make clean art!" Polly sang.

Next, they carefully cleaned the paintbrushes and wiped down the table with soft, colorful cloths. "Taking care of our art supplies means they'll last longer and work better," Polly explained.

"Now," said Polly, "would you like to paint with me? I have an extra canvas right here, and you can use the green paint - but please ask first!"

"May I please use the green paint, Polly?" Benny asked politely.

"Yes, you may!" Polly chirped happily. "And thank you for asking!"

Together, they painted side by side. Benny created a beautiful garden scene while Polly finished her rainbow forest. The art room filled with the happy sounds of friends creating together - the gentle swish of paintbrushes, quiet humming, and soft giggles.

"This is much more fun when we respect each other's space," Benny said, admiring both paintings.

"And when we keep our environment clean and safe!" Polly added, looking at their tidy workspace.

From that day on, Benny always asked before using Polly's art supplies, and he made sure to clean up first. Polly learned that saying "no" or "please stop" was okay when someone crossed her boundaries. Together, they created the most beautiful art while taking care of their special creative space.

The End.
