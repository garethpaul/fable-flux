# Sunny and the Balcony Garden Helpers

Sunny was a small, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail. He lived in a cozy nest near a balcony garden. The garden was full of green leaves, tiny flowers, and little pots of herbs. The air smelled fresh, like mint and wet soil. Bees hummed softly. A little wind tickled Sunny’s whiskers.

Every morning, Sunny peeked over the balcony. He loved to count the tomatoes and sniff the basil. “Good morning, garden,” he chirped. “I am Sunny. I like to help.”

One sunny day, Sunny heard a tiny cry. “Help… help,” said a little voice. Sunny’s ears perked up. He followed the sound to a pot of strawberries. A small ladybug was stuck under a curled leaf.

“Oh no!” said Sunny. “Are you okay?”

“I am Lila,” said the ladybug. “My wing is stuck to the leaf. I can’t fly.”

Sunny looked closely. The leaf was sticky with sweet sap. Lila’s wing was trapped like a ribbon in syrup.

“I can help,” said Sunny. He took a breath. He remembered his goals: think first, then act; be gentle; and ask friends for help if needed.

Sunny tried to lift the leaf with his tiny paws. “Hmm,” he said. “This is sticky.” He paused and thought. “I need a tool.”

He scampered to a pot of rosemary and found a small dry twig. “This can be my helper stick,” he said. He slid the twig under the leaf and lifted slowly. “One, two, three… up we go.”

“Thank you,” said Lila, fluttering her wings. “You were patient. You didn’t rush.”

Sunny smiled. “Helping takes care.”

Just then, a soft sob came from the tomato vines. “My tomatoes are too heavy,” whispered the vine. The vine leaned over the edge, and two small tomatoes dangled like red bells.

Sunny put a paw on his chest. “We can help,” he said. He looked around. “We need a plan. We need teamwork.”

Lila landed on Sunny’s ear. “I can fly and look for string,” she said.

Sunny nodded. “Good idea. I will gather soft leaves. We can make a gentle sling.”

They got to work. Sunny collected big, soft basil leaves. He hummed, “Pick and place, slow and safe.” Lila buzzed around the railing and found a long piece of fallen twine near a flower pot.

“I found string!” Lila called.

Sunny clapped his paws. “Great teamwork. Now we must be patient.”

Sunny tied the twine, but his first knot slipped. The tomatoes wobbled. “Oh!” he said. He took a deep breath. “It’s okay to try again.” He tried a new knot, slow and careful, and tucked the basil leaves under the tomatoes to make a soft seat.

“How does that feel?” Sunny asked.

“Supported,” sighed the vine, lifting gently. “Thank you for helping me.”

A bee buzzed by and said, “Buzz-buzz, can I help too?”

“Yes, Bee,” said Sunny. “Please tell the others to be careful around the tomatoes.”

“Buzz-buzz, I will,” said Bee. He zipped around, guiding the other insects away from the busy spot.

The balcony felt calm and happy. The herbs swayed and the flowers smiled in the sun. Then Sunny heard a scratch-scratch-scratch. A little snail was stuck on a dry patch of the railing. Its shell was dusty, and it could not slide.

“I am Pip,” said the snail. “I am thirsty, and the path is rough.”

Sunny sniffed the air. “We need water,” he said. He looked up at the sky. The sun was warm. He looked at the watering can near a chair. “That can is too big for me,” he said. “But I can think of another way.”

Sunny padded to a leafy plant full of drops from the morning mist. He shook a leaf gently. Plip, plop! Tiny drops fell onto the railing. Lila flew to another leaf and did the same. Together they made a small wet trail.

“Try now, Pip,” said Sunny.

Pip slid forward with a happy sigh. “It’s smooth. Thank you for thinking of me.”

Sunny felt warm inside. He had a goal for the day: help others in the garden. He had used his mind, his paws, and his friends. He had been patient and kind.

The afternoon breeze cooled the balcony. The mint smelled sweet, and the tomatoes glowed red. The vine stood tall with its new sling. Lila flew in happy circles. Pip rested under a shady leaf. Bee buzzed a soft song.

Sunny sat on the railing and looked at his friends. “We helped together,” he said. “When we help others, the garden feels good.”

Lila landed on his nose and giggled. “Helping is a team job.”

Pip peeped, “And patience makes it safe.”

Bee added, “Buzz-buzz, and a good plan makes it smart.”

Sunny nodded. “Yes. Helping-others, teamwork, patience, and good thinking. These are our garden rules.”

The sun began to set, and the sky turned peach and gold. Sunny stretched his paws. “Today, we set a goal to help, and we did it,” he said. “I am proud of us.”

“Me too,” said Lila.

“Me three,” said Pip.

“Buzz-buzz, me four,” said Bee.

Sunny wrapped his tail around himself and whispered to the garden, “Good night, friends. Tomorrow we can help again.”

The garden glowed softly. The air smelled like basil and earth. The balcony was quiet and safe, full of friends who knew how to help, wait, and work together.

The End.
