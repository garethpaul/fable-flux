# Spark the Robot Dog’s Garden Game

In a comfortable nursery with soft rugs and sunny windows, a helpful robot dog named Spark wagged his shiny tail. His tail made a gentle whirr-whirr sound. His nose light blinked blue when he was excited. Today, Spark had a plan.

“Beep-bark! Let’s play outside,” Spark said. His voice was warm and friendly.

Mia and Ben sat on cushions, building a block tower. They looked at the bright window. They could hear birds chirp-cheep outside. They could smell fresh air when the window cracked open.

“I want to run fast,” said Mia. She jumped up and clapped. “Zoom, zoom!”

“I want to water the flowers,” said Ben. He picked up a tiny toy watering can. “Sprinkle, sprinkle!”

Spark’s ear-lights blinked. “We can do both,” he said. “But first, we plan. That keeps our bodies calm and safe.” His speakers hummed a happy tune.

Outside, the garden was green and soft. The grass felt tickly on their toes. The sun felt warm on their faces. A little breeze whispered shhh through the leaves. Spark sniffed the air. “Beep! Smells like wet earth and sweet flowers.”

Mia ran in a big circle. “I am a rocket!” she shouted.

Ben knelt by the marigolds. “I am a rain cloud,” he said, pouring a little water. “Drip, drop.”

Soon, Mia ran close to Ben and splashed his shoes by accident. Splish! Ben frowned. His eyebrows made a tiny mountain. “Hey! I’m watering.”

Mia stopped. She wiggled her fingers. “I want to run,” she said. Her cheeks felt hot.

Spark blinked a yellow light. “Pause,” he said gently. “Let’s take a breath.” He sat and lifted a paw. “Smell the flower.” They breathed in. “Blow the dandelion.” They breathed out. Mia and Ben did it with him. In and out. The warm sun and the slow breathing made their bodies feel calm.

“Good job,” Spark said. “We have a little problem. We both want to use the same space. We can solve it.”

“How?” asked Ben, wiping a little mud from his hands.

“We can make a Garden Game,” said Spark. His tail whirred. “We take turns. We move our bodies with control. We help each other. That is cooperation.”

Mia nodded. “Okay.”

Ben nodded, too. “Okay.”

Spark tapped his paw on the grass. “First, we mark a running lane.” He put down soft ribbon from his belly pouch, making a curvy path. “This is the Zoom Path.”

Mia smiled. “Zoom Path!”

“Second, we mark the Water Spots,” Spark said, placing bright stones by the flowers. “This is the Sprinkle Zone.”

Ben touched a stone. It felt smooth and cool. “Sprinkle Zone!”

Spark wagged. “We set a timer,” he said, showing a little light on his collar. “Beep means switch. Mia runs on the Zoom Path. Ben waters in the Sprinkle Zone. Then we trade. That is a compromise. We both get a turn.”

“Okay,” said Mia. Her voice was bright but calm.

“Okay,” said Ben. He took a steady breath. “Smell the flower, blow the dandelion.”

“Ready,” Spark said. “Three, two, one… Beep!”

Mia ran on the curvy path. Her feet went pat-pat-pat. She kept her arms out like airplane wings. She watched the ribbon line. She did not bump the flowers. “I can steer my body,” she sang.

Ben watered the marigolds. Drip, drop, drip. He checked the soil. He waited for the water to sink in. “I can pour slow,” he said. He kept the water away from Mia’s path.

“Beep!” went Spark’s collar. “Switch!”

Mia handed the watering can to Ben with two hands. “Your turn,” she said.

“Thank you,” Ben said. He passed Mia a soft beanbag. “You can toss this while you run.”

Mia tossed and caught. Plop, catch! She moved her eyes, hands, and feet together. She laughed. “I’m practicing coordination!”

Ben watered the mint leaves. He smelled their fresh, cool scent. “Mmm,” he said, smiling. He checked the bright stones and only watered the plants in the Sprinkle Zone.

Spark watched with his friendly blue light. “You are both doing outdoor activity with care,” he said. “You are moving your bodies, using your eyes and hands, and taking turns.”

A butterfly fluttered by, light as a whisper. Mia slowed and pointed. “Look!”

Ben paused, too. They both stayed still. The butterfly landed on a marigold. Its wings looked like tiny orange flags. Mia and Ben smiled and stayed quiet. Spark whispered, “Good self-regulation. You paused your game to be gentle.”

“Beep!” The timer sang again. They switched once more. They ran and watered. They tossed and caught. They breathed when they felt wiggly. They smiled when they felt proud.

Soon, Spark said, “Last beep!” He wagged. “Time to cool down.” He led them in slow stretches. Reach up to the sun. Touch toes to the grass. Wiggle fingers like leaves.

Back in the comfortable nursery, they washed hands. The water was warm, and the soap smelled like lemons. They curled on the soft rug. Their legs felt sleepy and strong.

“I liked running,” said Mia. “I liked taking turns, too.”

“I liked watering,” said Ben. “I liked the plan.”

Spark’s light glowed soft. “Today we learned outside can be fun and safe,” he said. “We moved our bodies. We worked together. We made a fair plan. When we feel wiggly or upset, we can pause, breathe, and find a compromise.”

Mia and Ben nodded. Their smiles were big and calm.

“Beep-bark,” said Spark. “Tomorrow, we can try a new Garden Game!”

The End.
