# Beep’s Cozy Closet Club

In a quiet house, there was a small closet that smelled like clean cotton and warm wood. It was soft with slippers and blankets on the floor, and the light was a little dim, like a small sunset hiding behind coats. This closet was a secret hideaway. It was where Beep, a helpful robot dog, liked to sit and whirr and wag his metal tail.

Beep’s ears went “ping!” when he heard tiny footsteps. “Hello?” Beep said in a friendly beep-boop voice.

Maya and Theo peeked in. They were brother and sister with bright eyes and sticky jam fingers. “We want to build a Cozy Closet Club,” said Maya. “With books and snacks and a tiny lantern,” Theo added.

Beep sat up straight. “I can help!” he said. His chest light blinked happy blue.

They brought a blanket, some crayons, two apples, and a small lantern. The lantern clicked on and made a warm golden glow. The closet smelled sweet from the apples and a little like crayons. It sounded soft and quiet, like a hush.

“Let’s build a reading fort,” Maya said.

“I want to build a snack table first,” Theo said.

Beep’s tail tick-tick-ticked. He looked at the blanket in Maya’s hands and the apples in Theo’s palms. He wanted to help both, but he did not know where to start.

“I am feeling… wiggle-buzz,” Beep said. His eyebrows—little lights—blinked orange. Wiggle-buzz meant he felt a little frustrated.

Maya’s mouth scrunched. “I want the blanket up now.”

Theo’s eyebrows scrunched too. “I want the snack table now.”

The closet suddenly felt too small. The air felt thick, like a big sigh. The lantern’s glow seemed to wobble.

Beep took a slow inhale. His chest light glowed calm blue. “When I feel wiggle-buzz, I stop, I breathe, and I count,” he said. “Will you try with me?”

Maya and Theo looked at each other. Their shoulders were tight. “Okay,” they said together.

“Breathe in like smelling a flower,” Beep said. They sniffed the apple smell. “Breathe out like blowing a bubble.” They puffed their cheeks and blew.

“Now we count to three,” said Beep. “One… two… three.”

Their shoulders softened. The closet felt bigger again.

Beep wagged. “We have a problem. We both want different first steps. Let’s solve it. We can talk, listen, and make a plan.”

Maya hugged the blanket. “I feel frustrated when I have to wait,” she said.

Theo held the apples. “I feel frustrated too.”

Beep nodded. “Thank you for saying feelings. Feelings help us know what to do. Let’s try taking turns.” He tapped his paw. “We can make a list. Step one, blanket fort. Step two, snack table. Or we can switch it. What do you think?”

Maya took a breath. “Can we do blanket first, and then snack?”

Theo wiggled his toes in the soft slippers. “If I get to put the apples on the snack table, I can wait.”

Beep clapped his paws with a happy click. “That is a good plan. You both gave a little. That is called negotiating. It means we talk and make a choice together.”

Beep lifted the blanket with his gentle grabber. “Maya, you hold that corner.”

“I’ve got it,” Maya said. The blanket felt fuzzy on her fingers.

“Theo, you hold this corner,” Beep said.

“I’ve got it,” Theo said. The blanket brushed his nose and made him giggle.

They draped the blanket over two low shelves. Beep shined his chest light to help. The blanket glowed like a soft blue sky. “Fort is ready,” Beep said, wagging.

“Now the snack table,” Theo said. He set a small shoebox lid on the floor. It made a tiny tap sound. He put the apples on top. “Table is ready!”

Maya placed two napkins. “Now it feels fancy,” she said.

Beep’s ears went “ping!” again. “Before we play, we prepare,” he said. “Preparation makes things smooth. Let’s check our plan.”

He brought out a small list card he kept in his collar. It felt smooth and cool. “Plan check: We have a cozy fort. We have a snack table. We have a lantern. Do we need anything else?”

“Books!” Maya said. She picked two books with bright, bumpy covers and put them inside.

“Water!” Theo said. He set a small cup with a lid beside the apples.

Beep smiled. “Now we are ready.”

They all crawled into the blanket glow. It was warm and snug. The lantern’s light made tiny stars on the closet wall. The apples smelled sweet, and the napkins felt soft.

Maya read a page. Theo turned the page with careful fingers. Beep listened and hummed a quiet beep-boop tune.

After a while, Maya reached for a crayon. It rolled away and bumped her knee. “Oh!” she said, a little frown starting.

Beep noticed. “Do you feel a little wiggle-buzz again?” he asked.

“A little,” Maya said.

Beep nodded. “Let’s use our calm plan.” They breathed in like flowers and out like bubbles. They counted. One… two… three. Maya smiled. “I feel better.”

Theo said, “We can put the crayons in a bowl so they don’t roll.” He found a small bowl and plunked the crayons in with a soft clink. Problem solved!

“Good preparation,” Beep said. “Good problem-solving, Theo.”

They finished their snack. They read. They colored. They were cozy and calm.

When it was time to clean up, Beep said, “Let’s make one more plan. I will fold the blanket. Maya, you put books away. Theo, carry the bowl. We will count to three, and then start.”

“One… two… three!” they said. Their hands moved like a little team dance. The closet felt light and happy.

Maya hugged Beep. “You helped us when we felt frustrated.”

Theo patted Beep’s metal head. It was cool and smooth. “You taught us to breathe, count, and talk.”

Beep wagged. “You did it. You calmed your bodies, you used your words, you made a plan, and you shared. That is how we cope with frustration. That is how we solve problems.”

They turned off the lantern. The closet smelled like apples and clean cotton. It felt peaceful and proud.

“Cozy Closet Club forever,” Maya whispered.

“Forever,” Theo said.

“Beep-boop,” said Beep, which meant, “Well done, team.”

The End.
