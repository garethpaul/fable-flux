# Benny the Busy Beaver’s Sunny Backyard Plan

Benny was a busy beaver with bright, shiny teeth and a fluffy, flat tail. He lived near a sunny backyard where birds sang, flowers danced, and a tiny creek went trickle-trickle. The grass felt soft like a green blanket. The air smelled sweet like strawberries. Benny loved to make neat plans and help his friends.

One sunny morning, Benny opened his little notebook. “Today I have three things,” he said. “One, fix the wobbly garden fence. Two, gather sticks for the play fort. Three, bring carrot snacks to my friends.”

He tapped his tail happily. “I will do it step by step!” he said. Benny liked to be on time, and his notebook helped him remember.

Just then, he heard, “Chirp-chirp! Help!” It was Bella Bluebird on the fence. A piece of her blue yarn had gotten stuck in a rose bush.

Benny blinked. He looked at his notebook. Fence, sticks, snacks. Then he looked at Bella’s worried eyes. “Helping comes first,” he said. “I can be kind and still be on time.”

Benny waddled over. “Hold still, Bella,” he said gently. He nibbled the thorny vine very carefully. Snip. Snip. Snip. “There! You’re free!”

Bella flapped her wings. “Thank you, Benny! I was scared. You were calm and kind.”

Benny smiled. “We take turns. Today I help you. Another day, you can help someone else.”

Bella chirped, “I can help now! I’ll watch the time. When the sun moves to the tall sunflower, we do the next job.”

Benny nodded. “That’s a helpful plan.”

They went to the fence. It was a little wobbly and went creak-creak when the wind came. Benny pressed his paws on the wood. “I hear a creak here,” he said. He felt the rough wood and smelled the piney smell. He chewed a smooth peg from a fallen branch. Tap-tap with his tail. Push-push with his paws. The fence stood straighter.

“Nice work!” said a soft voice. It was Tilly Turtle, slow and steady, wearing a tiny red bow. “I move slowly,” Tilly said. “Can I help too?”

Benny nodded. “Everyone helps in their own way. Slow can be careful.” He smiled with tolerance, knowing friends are different.

Tilly carried small pebbles, one by one, to make a firm base. “Plop. Plop,” went the pebbles. Bella chirped, “The sun is almost at the tall sunflower!”

Benny checked his notebook. “Fence is done,” he said. “Next, gather sticks for the play fort.”

They walked to the shady tree near the sunny backyard. Sticks lay in a crisscross. Benny loved sticks! He smelled the warm wood and leaves. “Let’s sort them,” he said. “Long sticks here. Short sticks there. Curvy sticks for the roof.”

A rustle came from the tall grass. Out popped Max Mole with tiny glasses. “I dig, dig, dig,” he said. “But I can’t see very well in the sun. Can I still help?”

Benny patted Max’s paw. “Yes. You can feel the sticks. Your paws are gentle.” Benny showed him how to find smooth sticks by touch. Max smiled and hummed, “Mmm-hmm, smooth!”

Bella counted softly, “One, two, three,” putting short sticks in a neat pile. Tilly made a steady stack with long ones. Benny lifted curvy sticks and set them down, swish-swish. The pile grew.

A cloud slid over the sun. A breeze smelled like mint. Bella chirped, “When the sun peeks out again, it will be carrot snack time!”

Benny looked at his notebook. “We are doing great. We helped Bella. We worked together. We are on time!”

Then a tiny squeak came from the sandbox. Pip the Mouse was stuck in a deep scoop. “Help! The sides are too slippery!”

Benny hurried over. He put his tail down like a little ramp. “Climb up, Pip,” he said. Pip’s whiskers twitched. Pat-pat went Pip’s tiny feet on the soft tail. Up he went!

“Thank you!” Pip squeaked. “I thought I was too small to ask for help.”

Benny knelt and smiled. “Asking for help is brave,” he said. “We take care of each other.”

The sun peeked out, warm and bright. Bella chirped, “Snack time!”

Benny opened his basket. Crunchy carrots! He passed them around. “One for each friend,” he said. The carrots snapped and crunched. Max giggled. “Crunch-crunch!” Tilly chewed slowly. “Mmm,” she said. Pip nibbled happily. Bella pecked tiny bites.

Benny looked around the sunny backyard. The fence stood tall and safe. The stick fort looked cozy like a nest. His friends smiled. The day felt full and good.

“We did it,” Benny said. “We helped, we waited, we listened. We were on time, and we were kind.”

Bella flapped. “We are a team!”

Tilly nodded. “Slow or fast, we all can help.”

Max tapped the ground. “I can help by feeling.”

Pip squeaked, “And by asking!”

Benny put a paw on his notebook. “Today I learned that helping others comes first. When we plan and take turns, there is time for kindness and for jobs. We respect how each friend is different, and that makes our team strong.”

The friends sat together, feeling the warm sun and the soft grass. A butterfly floated by. The creek went trickle-trickle. Benny closed his notebook with a happy tap.

“Tomorrow,” he said, “we can help someone new.”

The End.
