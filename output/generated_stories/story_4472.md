# Ellie’s Pantry Plan

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and big kind eyes. One rainy morning, patter-patter drops tapped the window. Ellie’s tummy gave a little rumble-rumble. “I would like a snack,” she said.

Mama was taking a nap. The house was quiet. Ellie looked at the kitchen door. It was closed. But the pantry door was open just a little bit. The pantry smelled like warm oats, sweet cinnamon, and crunchy crackers. Ellie tiptoed in. Her feet went thump-thump on the floor, but softly.

Inside, the pantry was cozy like a tiny room. Shelves stood tall like friendly trees. Jars clinked when she bumped them. “Oops, sorry jars,” Ellie whispered. She peered up. On a high shelf were bananas and oats. On a middle shelf were raisins and a little jar of honey. On the lowest shelf was a stack of bowls.

“My tummy wants a snack,” Ellie said. “I can do this. I can make my own simple snack.” She took a deep breath. It smelled like oats and a little dusty flour.

Ellie remembered what Mama always said, “Prepare first, and then begin. Making a plan helps you win.” Ellie smiled. “I need a plan.”

She sat on the floor with a paper and a chunky crayon. Scritch-scratch, she drew a list with pictures.

“Step one: wash hands,” she said. She padded to the sink and used warm water and gentle soap. Bubbles felt slippery and fun. “Whoosh!” she rinsed and dried on a towel.

“Step two: gather things.” Ellie looked around. The bowl on the lowest shelf was blue like the sky. “Perfect!” She found a wooden spoon. It felt smooth and warm. She pointed her trunk up at the bananas. “Hmm, they are high.” She looked for something safe.

In the corner was a small sturdy step stool. It had rubber feet that didn’t slide. “Step three: be safe.” Ellie set the stool on the floor. She checked it with her foot. It didn’t wobble. “Good!” She climbed one step. Her heart went thump-thump. She reached with her trunk and gently took one banana. “Got it!” She giggled.

She climbed down slow and steady. “Step four: measure.” Ellie looked at the oats. The box was on a lower shelf. She hugged it close. “You are not heavy,” she told it. The oats whispered inside, shhh-shhh.

Ellie opened the box and poured some into the blue bowl. Plip-plop, the oats made soft sounds. “Not too much,” she said. “Just enough.” She sprinkled a tiny bit of cinnamon. “A pinch is little,” she reminded herself.

“Step five: add goodness.” Ellie peeled the banana. The peel went sss-ssst. The banana smelled sweet. She mashed it with her spoon. Squish-squash! She added a small handful of raisins. “Hello, little raisins,” she said. “In you go.”

She looked up at the honey jar. It was on the middle shelf. “I can reach if I use the stool again,” Ellie said. She checked the stool. Still steady. She climbed and took the honey with both little paws and her trunk. She climbed down, slow and safe. “Step six: a tiny drizzle.” She tipped the jar just a little. Drip… drip… golden drops. “That’s enough,” she said with a proud smile.

Ellie stirred the bowl. The spoon went round and round. The oats and banana and raisins danced together. The smell was cozy. Her tummy said, “Mmm!”

She tasted a small bite. “Yum!” she said. But then she paused. “I should make a place to eat.” She remembered another lesson. “Prepare the space, too.”

Ellie found a small mat. It was soft and green. She placed it on the floor near the window where the rain said, patter-patter. She brought her bowl and a napkin. She put a cup of water beside it. The cup chimed a tiny clink when it touched the mat.

She sat and took another bite. Warm banana and sweet raisins made her smile. “I did it all by myself,” she whispered. Her ears fluttered happily.

Just then, Mama peeked in. “Ellie, are you okay?” Mama asked softly.

Ellie nodded. “Yes, Mama. I made a snack. I washed my hands. I used the stool safely. I measured. I cleaned as I went.” She held up the napkin and showed the neat pantry. The jars stood straight. The box of oats was closed. The counter was wiped. Only a few tiny oat bits were in the bowl.

Mama’s eyes shone. “I see you prepared. I see you were careful. I see you solved the problem by making a plan.”

Ellie’s heart felt big and warm. “I made a list,” she said. “Step one, wash hands. Step two, gather things. Step three, be safe. Step four, measure. Step five, add goodness. Step six, a tiny drizzle.”

Mama smiled and sat beside her. “That is wonderful, my clever calf. You were self-reliant. You used your own ideas. You crafted your snack with care.”

Ellie took another bite. “It tastes like proud,” she giggled.

Mama laughed. “It tastes like banana and love.”

After Ellie finished, she put the spoon and bowl in the sink. Swish-swish, she washed them with warm bubbles. She dried them and put them back on the low shelf. She folded the mat and set it away.

Ellie patted her chest. “When I make a plan, I can do many things,” she said.

Mama nodded. “Prepare first, be safe, and try. You can do so much.”

Ellie looked at the pantry. It now smelled like sweet banana and cinnamon. She felt calm and brave. Outside, the rain slowed to a soft drip-drop.

“I am ready for next time,” Ellie said. “I can make a snack again. I can craft a little note, too!”

She drew a small picture list and taped it on the pantry wall at her height. The paper showed a hand with soap, a bowl, a stool, a spoon, and a smiley face at the end.

“This is my Pantry Plan,” Ellie said proudly. “It helps me help myself.”

Mama hugged her gently. “What a good plan.”

Ellie smiled. “I can do it.”

The End.
