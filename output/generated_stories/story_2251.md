# Melody Maps and Mouse Steps

In a playful living room, soft rugs felt like clouds under tiny feet. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Toys made a friendly mess, and a little radio hummed happy tunes. A kind mouse named Miri lived behind a cozy book about colors. Miri had bright eyes, soft gray fur, and a tiny blue scarf that smelled like lavender.

One morning, Miri peeked out. “Good day, room,” she squeaked. She loved music. When the radio played, her whiskers wiggled and her tail twitched. She wanted to dance. But the floor was a maze of blocks, crayons, and bouncy balls. A shiny bell sat on top of a wobbly stack of pillows. Miri had a big plan: ring the bell to play along with the song.

Miri took one step. “Eep!” A crayon rolled. She froze and listened. The radio sang, la-la-la, and the air smelled like oranges. A breeze from the window made the curtains wave hello.

“I can do this,” Miri told herself. “I am kind, I am brave, and I can make a smart plan.” She felt her heart go pat-pat-pat. She took a deep breath through her tiny nose. Calm. Courage.

Miri looked around with careful eyes. “Strategy time,” she whispered. “First, I will make a path.” She pushed a soft teddy a little to the side. She nudged a block into a neat line. Tap, tap. “This is my mouse road,” she said.

She listened to the beat of the music. Thump, thump, thump. “I will step on the beat,” she decided. “Step on thump, wait on la.” She tapped her paw. “Thump-step. La-wait.” She made a little melody map in her mind, like dots that matched the song. She smiled. “This is my plan.”

Miri started again. Thump-step onto the rug square. La-wait beside a ball. Thump-step over a crayon. La-wait behind a pillow. “You’re doing it,” she told herself. “Good job, Miri.” Her tail made a happy swoosh.

A tall tower of blocks leaned and wobbled. “Oh!” Miri stopped. “New plan,” she said softly. “I will go around, not through.” She traced a safe curve with her paw. “Curve on la, cross on thump.” The radio sang laaaa, and she moved slow and steady, like a quiet drum.

Suddenly, the bouncy ball rolled toward her. Boing, boing! Miri’s ears perked. “Freeze,” she said, holding still. The ball passed, and she let out a giggle. “Nice pause,” she whispered. “Pauses are part of the plan.” She felt proud. Her tummy felt warm, like cocoa.

The wobbly pillow stack stood like a mountain. On top, the shiny bell glowed. Ding, it seemed to promise. Miri looked up. “This part needs a new strategy,” she said. She touched the bottom pillow. It was very squishy. “If I climb straight up, I might slip.”

She listened to the radio. The song changed to a soft, slow tune. Sway-sway. Miri nodded. “I will make steps.” She found two small books. Slide, slide. She tucked them at the pillow’s edge. “Step one, step two,” she said. She tested them with her paws. Firm. “Good.”

She wrapped her tiny scarf tighter. “Courage,” she told her heart. “We are brave and careful.” She began to climb. Step on thump. Stop on la. Step on thump. Stop on la. She hugged the pillow like a friend. The pillow smelled like soap and sunshine. Higher and higher she went.

At the top, Miri balanced. The bell sat close. Her whiskers tickled. She hesitated. “What if I can’t reach?” she whispered. Then she remembered her plan. “Long tail for balance. Low knees. Gentle stretch.” She breathed in, counting with the beat. One, two, three. She breathed out. “I can do this.”

Miri stretched her paw and—ding! The bell sang a sweet, clear note. It rang like a star. The sound danced around the living room. Miri giggled, then rang it again with the music. Ding-ding, la-la! The radio and the bell made a tiny band. Miri swayed. She felt strong. She felt smart. She felt proud.

She climbed down the steps the same careful way. Step on thump. Stop on la. When her paws touched the soft rug, she did a happy spin. “I did it!” she cheered. “My plan worked!”

She swept her mouse road back into tidy shapes. She put the books neatly by the pillows. “Kind mouse, kind room,” she said. The radio ended with a soft hum. Miri sat, sipping a pretend cup of tea from a toy set. The tea smelled like strawberries in her imagination. She wrapped her scarf like a hug.

“I learned something,” Miri said. “When I have a big job, I can make a plan. I can move slow and steady. I can listen to the beat. I can pause and try again. That is strategy. Strategy helps me be brave. Strategy helps me believe in me.”

She patted her chest. “Good job, Miri.” Her eyes shone. The living room felt extra playful now, like it was smiling back.

Outside, a bird chirped. Inside, the bell gave one last tiny ding, like a wink. Miri curled beside the color book and hummed along with the quiet. She felt safe, smart, and ready for the next song.

The End.
