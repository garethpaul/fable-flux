# Benny the Brave Beaver Builds Something New

Benny the beaver sat in his cozy study corner, surrounded by colorful books and soft cushions. His little wooden desk was covered with pencils, papers, and building blocks. Today felt different - today he wanted to try something very special and very scary.

"I want to build the tallest tower ever!" Benny announced to his teddy bear friend, Mr. Buttons. His fluffy brown tail twitched with excitement as he looked at the pile of wooden blocks.

Benny had watched his big sister build amazing towers, but he had never tried it himself. His little paws felt shaky as he picked up the first block. "What if it falls down?" he whispered. "What if I'm not good at it?"

Mr. Buttons seemed to smile encouragingly from his spot on the reading chair.

Taking a deep breath that smelled like the fresh pine wood of his blocks, Benny placed the first piece on his desk. *Click!* It made a satisfying sound. Then he carefully added a second block on top. His heart beat fast like a little drum - *thump, thump, thump.*

"One, two!" Benny counted proudly. "I did it!"

But when he reached for the third block, his paw bumped the tower. *Crash!* The blocks tumbled down, making a loud noise that echoed in the quiet study corner.

"Oh no!" Benny's eyes filled with tears. His cheeks felt hot and his tummy felt twisty. "I can't do it! It's too hard!"

Benny wanted to give up. He pushed the blocks away and crossed his little arms. But then he remembered what his mama always said: "Brave beavers don't give up. They try again!"

"Maybe I need to make my body strong first," Benny thought. He stood up and did five jumping jacks, his feet making soft *thud* sounds on the carpet. Then he stretched his arms up high like a tall tree and took three big, deep breaths.

"Okay, blocks," Benny said in his bravest voice. "I'm going to try again, and this time I'll be extra careful!"

This time, Benny moved more slowly. He placed each block with both paws, making sure it was steady before adding the next one. When his tower wobbled a little, instead of getting upset, he took a deep breath and adjusted the blocks gently.

"One, two, three, four!" Benny counted as his tower grew taller. His face glowed with pride as the afternoon sunlight streamed through the window, making his blocks sparkle like jewels.

When he placed the fifth block on top, Benny stepped back and clapped his paws together. "I did it! I built a tall tower!" His tail wiggled with pure joy.

Just then, his mama peeked into the study corner. "Wow, Benny! What a wonderful tower! You must have been very brave to keep trying."

Benny beamed. "I was scared at first, and I got frustrated when it fell down. But I did my exercises, took deep breaths, and tried again! Being brave means trying even when you're scared, right?"

"That's exactly right, my brave little beaver," Mama said, giving him a warm hug that smelled like vanilla cookies.

From that day on, whenever Benny felt scared to try something new, he remembered his tower-building adventure. He would do his exercises, take deep breaths, and remind himself that being brave doesn't mean not feeling scared - it means trying anyway!

The End.
