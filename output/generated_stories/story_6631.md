# Benny Beaver's Big Mistake

Benny Beaver loved to build things. He had big front teeth that were perfect for chewing wood. He had a flat tail that went *thwap-thwap* when he was excited. Today, Benny was visiting his friend's house and staying in the guest room.

"This room needs fixing!" Benny said, looking around with his bright brown eyes. The wooden chair looked wobbly. The wooden desk had scratches. "I can make everything better!"

Benny's tail went *thwap-thwap* on the soft carpet. He was so excited to help! Without asking anyone, he started chewing on the chair leg with his sharp teeth. *Crunch, crunch, crunch!*

"Almost done!" Benny said happily. But then... *CRASH!* The chair fell over with a loud bang.

Benny's friend Sarah came running. "Oh no! What happened, Benny?"

Benny's ears drooped down. His tail stopped moving. "I... I wanted to fix your chair. But I made it worse."

Sarah's mom came to see what was wrong. When she saw the broken chair, her face looked sad. "Benny, this chair was very special. It belonged to Sarah's grandma."

Benny felt tears in his eyes. His tummy felt heavy and sick. "I'm so sorry! I didn't know it was special. I just wanted to help."

"But Benny," Sarah's mom said gently, "you should always ask before you touch things that don't belong to you. That's how we stay safe and keep things safe too."

Benny nodded slowly. "I understand now. I should have asked first. But how can I fix what I broke?"

Sarah's mom smiled a little. "Well, what are you really good at, Benny?"

"Building things!" Benny said, his tail giving a tiny *thwap*.

"Then maybe you can help us build something new," she said.

Benny thought hard. His little beaver brain worked and worked. Then his eyes lit up! "I have an idea! I can build Sarah a new chair! A special beaver chair!"

"But this time," Sarah's mom said, "we'll work together. And we'll use the right materials."

The next day, Benny came back with his dad. They brought smooth, safe wood pieces and child-friendly tools. Benny showed Sarah how to sand the wood until it felt soft like silk. Sarah helped paint it bright yellow like sunshine.

"This is fun!" Sarah giggled as she painted. "And it's safe because we're doing it together!"

Benny carefully used his teeth to make pretty designs in the wood. But this time, he asked permission first. "Is it okay if I make a flower shape here?"

"Yes!" Sarah clapped her hands.

When they finished, the new chair was beautiful. It was the perfect size for Sarah. It had smooth edges and pretty flower designs.

"Thank you for helping me understand," Benny said to Sarah's mom. "Now I know to always ask before I touch things. And I know that working together is safer and more fun!"

Sarah sat in her new chair and hugged Benny. "This chair is even more special because we made it together!"

Benny's tail went *thwap-thwap-thwap* with happiness. He had learned that being helpful meant being safe and asking permission first. And he had learned that making mistakes was okay, as long as you tried to make things right.

From that day on, Benny always asked before he tried to help. And everyone loved having such a thoughtful, safety-smart beaver friend!

The End.
