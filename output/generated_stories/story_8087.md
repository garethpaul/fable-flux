# Ellie Saves the Sparkly Bits

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft, gray ears and kind eyes. She loved the creative art room. It smelled like crayons, fresh paper, and a little bit like glue. Sunlight made bright squares on the floor, and a rainbow of paint pots sat on a low shelf.

“Today, I will make a big, bright picture,” Ellie said. Her trunk wiggled with happy wiggles.

Ellie picked up shiny paper, sparkly stickers, and a big bottle of glitter. She opened the glitter, and a tiny cloud of silver dust floated up. It tickled her nose. “Ah-choo!” she giggled.

Ellie started to squeeze. Glitter sprinkled like magic snow. It made the table shine. It made her toes shine too! Then she reached for new paper and snipped, snip, snip with scissors. Pieces fell to the floor like colorful leaves.

Ellie hummed a little song. But soon, her pile of supplies got smaller. The trash bin got bigger and bigger. Crumpled paper poked out. Long ribbons made a spaghetti nest.

Her friend, Pip the mouse, peeked in. “Squeak! Ellie, the bin is full,” Pip said, sniffing. “What will we do with all this?”

Ellie’s ears drooped. “Oh,” she said softly. “I used so much. Maybe... too much.”

Miss Junie, the art helper, walked over. She wore a paint-splashed apron and a warm smile. “Ellie, your art is bright. But look at the trash. Can we think of a plan to use less?” she asked gently.

Ellie’s heart felt heavy, like a stone. “I did not mean to waste,” she whispered. “I want to be kind to our world.”

Miss Junie nodded. “That is called sustainability—taking care of things so they last. We can use a smart strategy, a step-by-step plan.”

Ellie’s ears lifted. “A plan?”

“Yes,” Miss Junie said. “Step one: Pause. Look at what we have. Step two: Sort. What can we reuse? Step three: Share. Be generous. Others can use some too.”

Ellie took a big, slow breath. She liked that plan. It felt calm and clear.

“Pip,” Ellie said, “let’s pause.” They looked. There were paper scraps, ribbon curls, clean bottle caps, and a little spilled glitter.

“Let’s sort,” Ellie said. They made neat piles: Reuse, Recycle, and Trash. The paper scraps and ribbons went in Reuse. The clean bottle caps went in Recycle. The sticky, messy bits went in Trash.

Pip clapped his tiny paws. “Squeak! This is fun.”

Ellie smiled. “Now we share.” She used her trunk to fill little baskets. “One for Maya. One for Tom. One for you, Pip.” She added shiny scraps and soft ribbons. “These are gifts. Let’s make art together.”

Maya the bunny hopped in. “Ooh, baskets! Thank you, Ellie!” Tom the turtle peeked out of his shell. “I can make a robot,” he said.

They all sat at the big table. The room hummed with happy sounds—snip, snip; tap, tap; swish, swish. Paint smelled sweet, like summer berries. Paper felt smooth. Ribbon felt silky.

Ellie looked at her glitter bottle. “I will sprinkle carefully,” she said. She pinched just a tiny bit. The glitter twinkled like stars in the night. “Pretty,” she whispered.

Miss Junie nodded. “You are using less and still making it lovely.”

Ellie felt proud. Her chest felt warm, like sunshine. She had made a kind choice.

Soon, Maya said, “I need a circle for a moon. Do you have one?”

Ellie checked her Reuse pile. “Yes,” she said, offering a shiny circle. “Here you go.” Maya beamed.

Tom needed two small wheels. Ellie found two little bottle caps in the Recycle bin. “Ask an adult to wash them,” she said. “Then wheels!” Tom grinned.

Ellie’s own picture grew: a big green tree with a smiling bird. She used a brown paper strip for the trunk. She made leaves from tiny green triangles. She glued a ribbon nest for the bird. She added only one star of glitter at the top. “Just enough,” she said.

Pip sniffed the air. “It smells like ideas,” he giggled.

Miss Junie hung the pictures on a string. They fluttered like flags. “Friends,” she said, “look at what your smart plan did. You made art. You used less. You shared with care.”

Ellie’s tail swished. “We did it together,” she said.

Miss Junie gave a thumbs-up. “That is a growth mindset. You saw a problem, you learned, and you tried again.”

Ellie touched the full trash bin. Now it was not full. The Reuse basket was ready for tomorrow. The room looked cozy and calm.

Ellie whispered, “I will remember: Pause. Sort. Share. Use just enough. That is how we care for our world.”

Pip squeaked, “And it’s fun!”

Everyone laughed. The sunlight made their pictures glow. Ellie felt light as a feather and strong as an elephant, all at once.

She looked at her tree. The little bird seemed to sing, “Thank you.” Ellie listened. She heard the soft swish of ribbons, the quiet click of bottle caps, and the happy hum of friends creating together.

Ellie smiled. “Art can be kind,” she said.

The End.
