# Polly's Colorful Solution

Polly the parrot loved bright colors and making beautiful things. She lived with the Johnson family in their cozy house, and her favorite room was the dining room with its big sunny windows.

One morning, Polly flew into the dining room and saw something that made her green feathers droop. The white walls looked so plain and boring! The family was having breakfast, but even they seemed a little sad.

"Good morning, Polly!" said little Emma, but her voice wasn't as cheerful as usual.

"Morning, morning!" Polly squawked back. "What's wrong, Emma?"

Emma's mom sighed. "We wanted to make our dining room more colorful for Emma's birthday party next week, but we don't know how. Everything looks so plain."

Polly tilted her bright red head. She could see the problem clearly - the room needed more color and creativity! Her mind began to buzz with ideas.

"I can help! I can help!" Polly chirped excitedly, flapping her beautiful wings.

But how could a parrot make a room more colorful? Polly thought and thought. Then she remembered something special about herself - she was very creative and loved to make art!

First, Polly flew to Emma's art supplies. She carefully picked up a paintbrush with her strong beak. Dip, dip, dip went the brush into bright yellow paint. Then Polly flew back to the dining room and began painting cheerful sunflowers on the wall!

"Oh my!" gasped Emma's mom. "Polly, what are you doing?"

"Making beauty! Making joy!" Polly called out as she painted.

The sunflowers looked wonderful, but Polly wasn't finished. She got blue paint and added a pretty sky. Then red paint for ladybugs, and green paint for grass. Each stroke of the brush made the room brighter and happier.

Emma clapped her hands. "It's beautiful, Polly! But how did you learn to paint?"

Polly puffed up her chest proudly. "I watched, I tried, I practiced! Everyone can be creative!"

Soon, the whole family was helping. Emma's dad brought more brushes. Emma painted purple butterflies. Her mom added pink flowers. Even baby brother made handprints in orange paint!

The dining room filled with the sweet smell of paint and the sound of happy laughter. The walls came alive with a magical garden full of colors that sparkled in the sunlight streaming through the windows.

"This is amazing!" said Emma's dad. "Polly, you solved our problem by being creative!"

Polly felt warm and proud inside. "When we use our imagination and try new things, we can make anything beautiful!"

By the end of the day, the dining room was transformed. It looked like a rainbow had burst inside! The family gathered around their colorful table for dinner, and everyone felt so much happier.

"Thank you, Polly," said Emma, giving her feathered friend a gentle hug. "You taught us that being creative can solve problems and make everything more wonderful."

Polly snuggled close to Emma. "Remember, little one - when something seems hard or boring, use your creativity! Paint, draw, sing, dance, or build. There's always a colorful solution waiting inside your imagination!"

From that day on, the Johnson family's dining room was the most joyful room in the house, and they all remembered Polly's important lesson about the magic of creative expression.

The End.
