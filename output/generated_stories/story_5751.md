# Kiki Koala’s Pantry Plan

Kiki was a calm koala with soft gray fur and bright, gentle eyes. One rainy afternoon, Kiki tiptoed into the pantry. The pantry smelled like warm oats and sweet honey. The shelves were tall and brown, and jars lined up like shiny buttons.

“Hmm,” Kiki hummed softly. “I would like a snack.”

Kiki’s ears twitched. She heard the patter-patter of rain on the window. She felt her tummy rumble. Her nose wiggled as she sniffed a ripe banana scent.

On the floor sat a big red stool. On the top shelf, way, way up, was a jar of crunchy crackers. They were Kiki’s favorite. The jar twinkled in the light. Kiki smiled.

“I can reach it,” she whispered.

Kiki put one paw on the stool. The stool wobbled. The shelves looked very high. Kiki felt a tiny flutter in her chest.

Kiki paused. She remembered what Mama taught her: “We keep our bodies safe. We ask for help. We wait. We make good choices.”

Kiki took a deep breath in and a slow breath out. “Safety first,” she said to herself.

Just then, Kiki heard a little squeak. It was Benny the mouse, peeking from behind a bag of flour. His whiskers twitched. “Hello, Kiki!” Benny squeaked. “What are you doing?”

“I want the crunchy crackers,” said Kiki. “But they are very high. The stool is wobbly.” Kiki touched the stool with her toe. It rocked and made a tiny creak.

Benny’s eyes grew round. “That looks not safe,” he said. “If you climb, you might slip. Crash!”

Kiki nodded. She could almost hear the crash in her head. She did not like that sound. “You are right,” she said calmly. “I want a snack, but I want to be safe. And I want to be respectful. These crackers are for sharing, not grabbing.”

Benny sniffed the air. “I smell bananas too,” he said with a smile. “Maybe we can find a safer snack.”

Kiki looked around. She saw a basket on a low shelf. Inside were yellow bananas and red apples. She also saw a small note from Mama. The note said, “Snacks on the low shelf are safe to reach.”

Kiki’s heart felt warm. She picked up the basket and set it on the floor. “Thank you, Mama,” she said softly.

Benny clapped his tiny paws. “Hooray! No climbing. No falling.”

Kiki peeled a banana. The peel made a soft zip sound. The banana smelled sweet and sunny. She took a small bite. “Mmm,” she said. “Would you like some banana?” she asked Benny.

“Yes, please,” Benny said, his whiskers wiggling. Kiki broke off a tiny piece for him. “Thank you for asking and sharing,” Benny said. “That is very kind.”

Soon, the pantry door opened. It was Mama Koala. She stepped in and smiled. “Hello, Kiki. Hello, Benny. I see you found the safe snack shelf.”

Kiki nodded. “Mama, I wanted the crackers on the top shelf. But the stool was wobbly. I remembered your safety rules. I did not climb. I asked for help in my heart. I looked for a safe choice.”

Mama’s eyes shone with pride. “That was a smart and safe plan. Thank you for keeping your body safe. Thank you for respecting our pantry rules. And thank you for sharing with Benny.”

Kiki’s ears perked up. “Mama, can we have crackers too?”

“Of course,” said Mama. “Let’s do it safely together.”

Mama held Kiki’s paw. She also looked at Benny. “First, we make a plan,” Mama said. “Step one: ask a grown-up. Step two: use a sturdy step with non-slip feet. Step three: use two hands. Step four: move slowly. Step five: put things back where they belong.”

Kiki repeated, “Ask, sturdy step, two hands, move slowly, put back.” She liked the steps. They felt calm and clear.

Mama brought a sturdy step stool with rubber feet. It did not wobble. It felt strong and steady on the floor. “Listen,” Mama said. She tapped it. “Thump, thump. Safe and steady.”

Kiki climbed one step. Mama held the jar with her. Together, they lifted it down. The glass felt cool and smooth. The lid made a click when Mama opened it.

“Crunch, crunch,” went the crackers. Benny nibbled a tiny crumb. “Yum!” he squeaked.

Kiki smiled. “We did it the safe way. We worked together.”

Mama nodded. “You used peaceful problem-solving. You waited. You asked. You respected the pantry. You kept everyone safe.”

Kiki felt proud and calm. The rain outside softened to a gentle drip-drop. The pantry smelled cozy, like bananas and crackers and hugs.

Kiki took a breath and said, “My plan for next time: If something is high, I will not climb. I will ask a grown-up. I will choose a safe snack I can reach. I will share. I will be calm.”

Benny held up his tiny paw. “I will wait my turn and say please and thank you,” he squeaked.

Mama hugged them both. “Those are wonderful plans.”

They finished their snack and cleaned up together. Kiki put the banana peel in the bin. Mama put the jar back on the top shelf. Benny wiped a little crumb with a napkin.

Kiki waved to the shelves. “Good-bye, crackers on top. See you with Mama.”

Safety felt good. Respect felt kind. Peace felt warm.

The End.
