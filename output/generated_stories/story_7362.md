# Ella’s Little List

Ella was a gentle elephant calf who lived in a comfortable nursery. The nursery smelled like warm soap and sweet bananas. Soft blankets were stacked in neat piles. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. A clock on the wall went tick-tick, tick-tick.

Every morning, Ella wiggled her big, soft ears and smiled. “Good morning, room,” she said in her tiny trumpet voice. “What should I do first?”

Miss Nia, the kind caregiver, tapped a chart with colorful pictures. “Good morning, Ella. Let’s follow our little list. A routine helps our day feel calm and happy.”

Ella blinked at the chart. It showed a toothbrush, a tiny towel, blocks, books, snacks, and a cleanup basket. “A little list?” Ella asked.

“A routine is like a friendly path,” Miss Nia said. “We do one step, then the next.”

Ella liked the sound of a friendly path.

Step one: Brush! Ella dipped her elephant toothbrush in a pea-sized dot of toothpaste. Minty bubbles fizzled on her tongue. Scrub-scrub went the brush. Swish-swish went the water. “Ahhh!” Ella giggled. Her breath felt cool like morning air.

Step two: Wash face and paws. The towel was soft and fluffy. Pat-pat-pat. Ella liked the warm water on her paws. Drip, drop, plink went the tiny drops into the sink.

Step three: Make bed. Ella tugged her blanket with her trunk. “Pull, tug, smooth,” she sang. The blanket spread like a quiet lake. She patted the corners. “There,” she said proudly.

Tick-tick, tick-tick, went the clock. Ella felt peaceful. Her tummy hummed a happy song.

Then she heard a small sniffle. Her friend Pip, a shy porcupine, sat by a basket of blocks. “I can’t find the red block,” Pip whispered. “I’m sad.”

Ella looked at the chart. Play time was next. She had a plan. “Routines can help us,” she said softly. “First, we look. Then, we sort. Then, we build.”

Ella and Pip knelt on the soft rug. The rug felt springy under Ella’s knees. “Look,” Ella said, using her eyes like bright flashlights. “Sort,” she said, making piles. “Build,” she said, stacking one, two, three.

Clink, clack, clunk went the blocks. Pip smiled. “There’s the red one!” Pip cheered.

They built a tall, wobbly tower. Ella placed a blue block on top with the tip of her trunk. “Boop,” she said. The tower stood strong. Pip’s quills wiggled with joy.

Ding! The snack bell rang. Miss Nia called, “Snack time! Wash, sit, nibble.”

Ella checked the chart. “Wash first,” she told Pip. They washed their paws together. Swish-swish, dry-dry. Then they sat. Crunch went the apple slices. Slurp went the milk. “Thank you,” Ella said. She loved using kind words as part of her routine.

After snack, it was cleanup time. Some little ones wandered away. Toys were scattered like stars. Miss Nia smiled. “We can help the whole room. That’s community service. A clean room helps everyone.”

Ella stood tall. “Friends, let’s make a clean-up train!” she called. “Pick up, put away, pat-pat the shelves.”

Choo-choo, they sang. Pick up, put away, pat-pat. Ella showed a little bunny how to place books with the spines out. “Like this,” she said gently. “You can do it.” The bunny beamed and copied her.

The room began to shine. The shelves smelled like lemon. The floor felt open and safe. Miss Nia clapped softly. “Wonderful work. You followed the routine and helped others.”

Later, it was quiet time. Lights dimmed. Ella curled up with her blanket. She felt sleepy, but a tiny worry tickled her heart. “Miss Nia,” she whispered, “I’m a little too warm. May I have a cooler blanket?”

“That is great self-advocacy, Ella,” Miss Nia said. “You told me what you need.” She brought a light, breezy blanket. It felt like a cool cloud. “Better?” she asked.

“Better,” Ella sighed. Her eyes felt heavy and calm. She loved that she could ask for help.

When rest was done, the chart showed art time and then goodbye. Ella dipped a brush into sunny yellow paint. Swish-swish, swish-swish. She painted a big happy sun and, underneath, a little list with tiny pictures. “This is my routine,” she told Pip. “It helps me finish my jobs.”

Pip nodded. “Routines are friendly paths,” Pip said proudly, remembering the words.

As the day ended, parents came to the door. The room buzzed with soft chatter and warm hugs. Miss Nia handed Ella a small card with her very own picture list. “You can use this at home,” she said.

Ella pressed the card to her heart. “I will brush, wash, make bed, play, snack, clean, rest, art,” she recited. “I can do my tasks. I can help. I can ask for what I need.”

On the way out, Ella spotted a dropped scarf. She picked it up and gave it to a friend. “Here you go,” she said kindly. Helping felt like sunshine in her chest.

The clock went tick-tick, tick-tick. Ella waved her trunk. “Goodbye, room. See you tomorrow for our friendly path.”

That night, Ella used her little list. Step by step, she felt proud and peaceful. Routine made her day smooth and bright. She completed her tasks, helped her friends, and spoke up kindly when she needed something.

And that is how a gentle elephant calf learned that routines are like cozy hugs for the day: they guide you, help you finish, and make time to help others too.

The End.
