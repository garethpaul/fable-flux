# Pippin's Splashy Sorry

Pippin the penguin loved bath time! Every morning, he would waddle into the cozy bathroom with his yellow rubber duck and his favorite blue towel. The bathroom had warm, fluffy rugs that felt soft under his webbed feet, and the mirror always fogged up in the most wonderful way.

"Splash, splash, splash!" Pippin giggled as he filled the bathtub with bubbly water. The bubbles smelled like sweet strawberries, and they tickled his black and white feathers.

But today, Pippin was in such a hurry to play that he forgot something very important. His little sister Penny was still brushing her teeth at the sink.

"Pippin, wait!" called Penny. "I'm not finished yet!"

But Pippin didn't listen. He was too excited about his imaginary pirate ship adventure. "Ahoy, matey!" he called out, pretending his rubber duck was a brave pirate captain.

WHOOSH! Pippin turned on the water so fast that it splashed everywhere. Big drops of water flew through the air like tiny raindrops. Some landed on Penny's clean pajamas, making them all wet and cold.

"Oh no!" cried Penny. Her bottom lip started to wobble. "My pajamas are all soggy now!"

Pippin stopped splashing. He looked at his little sister's sad face and felt a funny feeling in his tummy. It wasn't a good feeling at all. It was the feeling you get when you know you've made a mistake.

"Penny, I... I'm sorry," Pippin said softly. His cheerful voice became gentle and caring. "I should have listened when you asked me to wait."

Penny sniffled. "Now I'll be cold, and Mama will wonder why my pajamas are wet."

Pippin thought very hard. How could he make things better? Then his eyes lit up with an idea!

"Wait right here!" Pippin waddled quickly to the linen closet. His flippers made soft patting sounds on the tile floor. He came back carrying a fluffy, warm towel that smelled like sunshine.

"Here, Penny," he said kindly. "This towel is super soft and warm. You can wrap up in it like a cozy burrito!"

Penny's face brightened a little. "Really?"

"Really! And look," Pippin added, "we can share bath time! You can be the captain of your own ship, and I'll be your first mate. We can sail to Bubble Island together!"

Penny clapped her flippers together. "That sounds wonderful! But next time..."

"Next time, I'll listen first and ask if it's okay to start," Pippin promised. "Being kind means thinking about others, not just myself."

They spent the rest of bath time playing together. Pippin helped Penny make a bubble beard, and Penny showed Pippin how to make the water swirl like a whirlpool. They took turns being the captain, and they both listened carefully to each other's ideas.

When Mama Penguin peeked in, she saw both her little ones giggling and playing nicely together.

"What good penguins you are," she smiled. "You're sharing so beautifully!"

As they dried off with their fluffy towels, Penny hugged her big brother. "Thank you for saying sorry, Pippin. And thank you for making bath time fun for both of us."

Pippin hugged her back. "Thank you for forgiving me, Penny. Being kind feels much better than being in a hurry."

From that day on, Pippin always remembered to listen first, share nicely, and think about others' feelings. And bath time became even more fun because they played together!

The End.
