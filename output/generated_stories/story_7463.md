# Toby's Big Day Preparation

In the cozy corner of the living room lived a shy little turtle named Toby. His shell was green with yellow spots, and he had the sweetest brown eyes. Toby loved his quiet spot behind the big blue couch where he could watch the family play.

Every morning, Toby watched the children get ready for school. They rushed around looking for backpacks, shoes, and lunch boxes. Sometimes they forgot things and had to run back inside.

"Oh no!" little Sarah would cry. "I forgot my homework!"

"Where are my soccer cleats?" her brother Jake would shout.

Toby pulled his head into his shell when they got loud and frantic. All that rushing made him feel worried and scared.

One evening, Toby heard Mom talking to the children. "Tomorrow is picture day at school," she said gently. "Let's get everything ready tonight so we're not rushing in the morning."

Toby peeked his head out. "Get ready tonight?" he whispered to himself. "What a smart idea!"

He watched as the family calmly laid out clean clothes. Sarah brushed her hair and put her brush on the table. Jake polished his shoes until they sparkled. Mom packed their lunches and put them in the refrigerator.

"This feels so peaceful," Sarah smiled. "I'm not worried about tomorrow anymore."

Toby's eyes grew wide. Being prepared made them feel calm and happy! He wanted to try this too.

The next morning, Toby decided to prepare for his own big day. He was planning to venture out from behind the couch to explore the sunny spot by the window.

First, Toby cleaned his shell with a soft cloth he found. Then he practiced walking slowly and steadily across the carpet. He even rehearsed what he might say if someone noticed him.

"Hello," he whispered quietly. "My name is Toby. I'm friendly."

When the family rushed downstairs for breakfast, they moved calmly and smiled. Sarah had her backpack ready. Jake wore his shiny shoes. Everything was prepared from the night before.

"Look how peaceful our morning is!" Mom said happily.

Toby felt brave and ready too. Slowly, he walked out from behind the couch. Step by step, he made his way toward the sunny window spot. The warm light felt wonderful on his shell.

"Oh my!" said Mom, spotting him. "Look at this beautiful turtle!"

Toby felt his heart beating fast, but he remembered his practice. He took a deep breath and stayed calm.

"Hello," he said in his small, clear voice. "My name is Toby. I live behind your couch. I've been watching how you prepare for your days, and it helped me prepare for mine too."

Sarah knelt down gently. "Hi Toby! You're so brave to come out and talk to us."

"I learned that when I prepare and practice, I feel much calmer," Toby explained. "Just like your family does."

From that day on, Toby became the family's preparation helper. Every evening, he would remind them to get ready for the next day. And every morning, he would venture out to his sunny spot, feeling confident because he was always prepared.

"Being prepared helps us feel calm and brave," Toby would say. "When we plan ahead, we can enjoy each day with peaceful hearts."

The End.
