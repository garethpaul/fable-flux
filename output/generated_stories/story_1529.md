# Ellie's Big Basement Mistake

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved playing in Grandma Mouse's busy basement. The cozy space smelled like warm cookies and old books. Colorful boxes lined the walls, and soft rugs covered the floor.

"Good morning, Ellie!" squeaked Grandma Mouse, carrying a basket of freshly folded laundry. "Please be careful down here today. I have lots of work to do."

Ellie nodded her gray head. "I will, Grandma Mouse!"

But as Ellie played with her favorite red ball, she got very excited. She trumpeted loudly and spun around in circles. Her trunk swished through the air, and her big feet stomped on the soft rugs.

CRASH!

Ellie's trunk accidentally knocked over a tall stack of Grandma Mouse's clean dishes. The white plates scattered across the floor with loud clanking sounds.

"Oh no!" gasped Ellie, her ears drooping low.

Grandma Mouse rushed over, her whiskers twitching with worry. When she saw the broken pieces, her small shoulders sagged.

"My special dishes," she whispered sadly. "I was going to use these for my tea party today."

Ellie felt a heavy feeling in her tummy. Her friend looked so sad, and it was all her fault! She wanted to run away and hide, but instead, she took a deep breath.

"Grandma Mouse," Ellie said softly, her voice shaking a little. "I'm very, very sorry. I broke your dishes because I wasn't being careful."

Grandma Mouse looked up at the gentle elephant calf. "Thank you for telling me the truth, Ellie."

"I want to make it better," Ellie said, kneeling down carefully. "Can I help clean up? Can I fix this somehow?"

Grandma Mouse's eyes brightened. "Well, we can clean up together. And maybe you can help me with something else too."

Ellie and Grandma Mouse picked up every piece, putting the broken bits in a special box. Then Ellie used her trunk like a gentle broom to sweep up the tiny crumbs.

"Now," said Grandma Mouse, "I have an idea. Would you like to help me make new decorations for my tea party?"

"Really?" Ellie's eyes sparkled. "Even after I made such a big mistake?"

"Everyone makes mistakes, dear," Grandma Mouse smiled. "What matters is that you said sorry and wanted to help fix things."

Together, they made beautiful paper flowers. Ellie used her trunk to carefully fold the colorful paper, and Grandma Mouse showed her how to make them bloom. The basement filled with the happy sounds of their laughter and the crinkle of paper.

When they finished, the tea party table looked even more beautiful than before.

"Thank you, Ellie," said Grandma Mouse, giving the elephant calf a warm hug. "Your help made everything better. And these flowers are much prettier than my old dishes!"

Ellie felt warm and happy inside. "Thank you for forgiving me, Grandma Mouse. Next time I'll remember to be more careful."

"And I'm grateful you're such a good friend," Grandma Mouse replied. "You showed me that making mistakes isn't the end of the world. What matters is how we fix them together."

As they sat down for their tea party, surrounded by beautiful paper flowers, Ellie learned that saying sorry and helping to make things right felt much better than running away from problems.

The End.
