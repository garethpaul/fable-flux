# Fiona's Big Feelings

Fiona the gentle deer fawn stepped carefully into Grandma's guest room. Her small hooves made soft clicking sounds on the wooden floor. The room smelled like lavender and sunshine.

"I want to play with my blocks NOW!" Fiona said loudly, stamping her tiny hoof.

But Grandma was on the phone in the corner chair. She held up one finger, which meant "wait please." Grandma's voice was quiet and gentle as she talked.

Fiona's chest felt hot and tight. Her brown ears twitched with frustration. She wanted to build a tall tower RIGHT NOW! Waiting felt impossible.

"GRANDMA!" Fiona called out, even louder this time.

Grandma looked over with kind eyes but shook her head softly. She pointed to the phone and whispered, "Just a few more minutes, sweetie."

Fiona's feelings grew bigger and bigger inside her small body. She felt like a balloon ready to pop! Her white spots seemed to wiggle with frustration.

"This is NOT FAIR!" Fiona huffed. She was about to stomp both hooves when she heard something.

Grandma was saying, "Yes, I understand you're worried about the doctor visit tomorrow."

Fiona's ears perked up. Someone else was worried? She listened more carefully.

"You're being very brave," Grandma said softly into the phone. "I'll be right there with you."

Fiona realized Grandma was helping someone who felt scared. Just like when Fiona felt scared about thunderstorms, and Grandma always helped her feel better.

Fiona took a deep breath. The air tasted fresh and clean. She counted to three, just like Grandma had taught her. One... two... three...

Her big feelings started to feel smaller.

Fiona noticed the soft blue blanket on the guest bed. She climbed up carefully and wrapped it around herself. The blanket felt warm and cozy, like a gentle hug.

She watched the dust particles dance in the sunbeam streaming through the window. They sparkled like tiny stars. Fiona began to feel calmer.

"Thank you for calling," Grandma said into the phone. "Everything will be okay."

Grandma hung up and turned to Fiona with a warm smile. "Thank you for waiting so patiently, my dear fawn."

"Was someone sad?" Fiona asked, her voice much quieter now.

"Yes," Grandma nodded. "My friend was nervous about visiting the doctor. Sometimes grown-ups need comfort too."

Fiona felt proud that she had waited. "I felt really frustrated," she admitted. "But then I listened and took deep breaths."

"That was very wise," Grandma said, sitting on the bed beside Fiona. "When we have big feelings, we can take deep breaths and think about others too."

"Can we build blocks now?" Fiona asked hopefully.

"Of course!" Grandma laughed. "And thank you for being so respectful while I helped my friend."

Together, they built the tallest, most beautiful block tower ever. Fiona felt warm and happy inside. She had learned that waiting wasn't so bad when she remembered to breathe and think about others.

"I'm proud of how you handled your frustration," Grandma said, giving Fiona a gentle nuzzle.

Fiona smiled. She felt proud too. Big feelings weren't scary when you knew what to do with them.

The End.
