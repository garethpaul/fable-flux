# Milly Mouse and the Big Laundry Day

Milly Mouse lived in a cozy corner of the warm laundry room. She loved the smell of clean soap and the gentle hum of the washing machine. The laundry room was her favorite place in the whole house.

Every week, Mrs. Chen would bring big baskets of dirty clothes. She would sort them into piles - whites here, colors there, delicates in a special basket. Milly watched from her little hole in the wall.

But this week was different. Mrs. Chen looked very tired. Her shoulders drooped as she carried the heavy baskets. "Oh my," she sighed. "So much laundry to do, and I have to cook dinner for the family too."

Milly's tiny heart felt sad seeing Mrs. Chen so worried. "I want to help!" squeaked Milly softly. But she was just a little mouse. How could she possibly help with such big tasks?

That night, Milly had an idea. She scurried around the laundry room, looking at everything with her bright black eyes. The soap smelled like fresh flowers. The fabric softener felt smooth on her tiny paws. The warm dryer made gentle whooshing sounds.

"I may be small," Milly said to herself, "but I can still do something!"

The next morning, Mrs. Chen came downstairs with another heavy basket. But when she opened the laundry room door, she gasped with surprise.

All the socks were paired up in neat little rows! The small items that had fallen behind the washer were lined up on the counter. Even the lint from the dryer was cleaned up in a tiny, perfect pile.

"How wonderful!" Mrs. Chen exclaimed, clapping her hands together. "Someone has been helping me!"

Milly giggled quietly from her hiding spot. She felt warm and happy inside, like sunshine in her tummy.

But Milly wasn't finished. Each day, she found new ways to help. She pushed dropped buttons back onto the counter with her nose. She used her sharp teeth to remove tags that needed cutting. She even learned to sort the coins that fell from pockets into a special jar.

The laundry room became the most organized place in the house. Mrs. Chen finished her work faster and had more time to play with her children. She would hum happy songs while folding clothes.

One evening, Mrs. Chen sat down near Milly's corner. "Thank you, little helper," she whispered. "I don't know who you are, but your kindness makes my days so much brighter."

Milly's whiskers twitched with joy. She peeked out just enough to see Mrs. Chen's grateful smile.

From that day on, Milly made completing her helpful tasks the most important part of every day. She learned that even the smallest helper could make the biggest difference. When everyone works together and finishes their jobs, the whole family feels happier.

Mrs. Chen continued to leave tiny crumbs of cheese near the washing machine - her way of saying thank you to her mysterious little helper.

And Milly? She kept helping every single day, because she learned that completing tasks and caring for others filled her heart with the greatest joy of all.

The End.
