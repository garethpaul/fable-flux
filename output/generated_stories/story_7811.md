# Bubbles in the Laundry Room

Dizzy the dolphin was not in the sea today. He was in a warm, bright laundry room! Sunlight made little squares on the floor. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer went, “Thump-a-thump.” The room smelled like clean soap and warm towels.

Dizzy flicked his tail and giggled. “I love the bubble song,” he said, listening to the washer. He wiggled his fins. “I have a big idea. Today, I will make bubble art!”

Dizzy set a goal. “First, I will gather safe things. Then, I will ask before I use them. Finally, I will make my art,” he said. He took a deep breath, brave and ready.

He looked around. There was a little basket of old socks, a box with clothespins, and a stack of empty cardboard boxes. Dizzy wanted to use the shiny soap bottle too. It was colorful and tall.

“Can I use the soap?” he called. Mrs. Maple, the kind grown-up, poked her head in the door. “Hi, Dizzy,” she said with a smile. “Soap is for the washer, not for play. That’s a boundary. But you can use these safe things.” She passed him a bowl of water, a small drop of kid-safe bubble mix, and some big soft sponges shaped like stars.

Dizzy nodded. “Thank you for telling me the boundary. I will stay safe,” he said. He set the shiny soap bottle back and felt proud. He was brave and careful.

Plop! Dizzy dipped a star sponge into the bubble mix. “Squeeze, squeeze,” he sang. Bubbles grew and popped—pop, pop, pop! He blew gently through a clothespin ring. A rainbow bubble floated up. It looked like a tiny planet with pink and blue swirls.

“Wow,” Dizzy whispered. “I want to make a Bubble Laundry Parade!”

He made a plan and said it out loud. “Goal one: make bubble shapes. Goal two: decorate the laundry baskets. Goal three: show my parade.”

He worked step by step. He dipped, blew, and stamped bubbles onto a big piece of craft paper Mrs. Maple had taped to the floor. The paper crinkled under his belly fin. It felt cool and smooth. “Sssshh, crinkle, crinkle,” it went.

“Hello, Dizzy!” said Buttons the cat, tiptoeing in. “What are you making?”

“I’m making bubble art,” Dizzy said. “Would you like to help?”

“Yes!” Buttons purred. She reached for the paint shelf.

Dizzy lifted his fin. He spoke kindly and clear. “My idea is to use sponges and bubble mix only. That is my boundary for this art. No paint today.”

Buttons paused, then nodded. “Thank you for telling me. I can follow your art rules.” She picked up a moon sponge. “May I print moons?”

“Yes,” Dizzy said. “Thank you for asking. I feel happy when friends respect my plan.”

They worked together. Dizzy made star bubbles. Buttons made moon prints. The room smelled like soapy rain. The washer sang, “Whirr-whirr,” like a gentle drum.

Soon, Tiko the tiny turtle peeked in. “Can I make big splashes?” he asked, eyes wide.

Dizzy looked at the floor. It was a bit wet already. He took a brave breath. “I like splashes, Tiko, but I don’t want slippery floors. My boundary is little splashes in the bowl only.”

Tiko smiled. “Okay. Little splashes.” He pat-patted the water. “Plip, plip.”

Then something went wrong. A bubble floated toward the “Start” button. Pop! The washer stopped. The room grew quiet. Dizzy’s eyes got big.

“Oh no,” he said softly. “We need to fix this.”

Dizzy felt a flutter in his tummy. He did not run away. He was brave. “We can solve it,” he told himself. “Step one: ask for help. Step two: try again.”

“Mrs. Maple?” Dizzy called. “A bubble made the washer stop. Can you help?”

Mrs. Maple came in and looked kindly at them. “Thank you for telling me right away,” she said. She pressed a button. “Beep!” The washer hummed again. “Whirr.”

Dizzy let out a happy “click-click,” the sound dolphins make when they are glad. “I learned to ask for help,” he said.

“Now, let’s finish your parade,” Mrs. Maple smiled.

Dizzy tied clean socks together like flags. Buttons clipped them with clothespins. Tiko carried a tiny sign made from a cardboard square. It said, “Bubble Parade!”

They marched around the laundry baskets. Dizzy led with a fin high. “Bubble art! Bubble art!” they chanted softly. The dryer gave a drumbeat: “Thump-a-thump!”

Mrs. Maple clapped. “What a creative parade! Dizzy, your plan worked. You used safe tools, you spoke your boundaries, and you were brave when there was a problem.”

Dizzy’s cheeks felt warm with pride. “I made art in my own way,” he said. “That is creative expression. I also kept us safe.”

Buttons purred, “I liked hearing your idea.”

Tiko nodded. “I liked following the plan. It helped me.”

Dizzy looked at the bubble paper, shining with tiny rainbows. He said, “My new goal is to make a Bubble Parade poster for the wall. Tomorrow, I will draw it with crayons.”

Mrs. Maple smiled. “I can help you hang it.”

Dizzy swished his tail and laughed. The laundry room smelled like warm towels again. The washer hummed a happy song. Dizzy felt calm and strong.

He said, “Today I learned three things: I can be brave and ask for help. I can say my boundaries and listen to others’. And I can make my own art, step by step.”

Everyone cheered. “Hooray for Dizzy!”

The End.
