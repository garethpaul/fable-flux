# Pippa Panda’s Calm Craft

In a comfortable nursery, soft and warm like a hug, Pippa the patient panda sat at a bright little table. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. The room smelled like clean crayons and fresh soap. A cozy fan hummed, “whrrr.”

Pippa’s round ears perked up. Today was Craft Day! She had shiny paper, safe scissors, a glue stick, and a blue ribbon that felt smooth and cool. She wanted to make a paper kite for the window. The kite would float on a string and make the room feel brave and happy.

“I can do this,” Pippa whispered. “Slow and steady.”

Ms. Star, the gentle teacher, smiled. “Remember our calm plan, Pippa. If it gets tricky, we stop, breathe, and try a new way.”

Pippa nodded. She drew a diamond shape. Scratch, scratch went the crayon. She cut along the lines. Snip, snip went the scissors.

But the paper slipped. Snip! Oh no. A corner came off.

Pippa’s tummy felt tight. Her paws felt hot. “My kite is broken,” she said, and her eyes felt watery.

“It’s okay,” said Ms. Star. “What do we do first, when we feel upset?”

Pippa put her paw on her belly. “We stop,” she said softly.

She closed her eyes. She took a slow breath in. Smell the flower. She let it out. Blow the candle. In and out. In and out. The fan hummed with her. Whrrr… whooo.

Her tummy softened. Her paws cooled down. “I feel calmer now,” she said.

Brave Pippa looked at the paper. “I can fix it with crafting.” She found the missing corner under her chair. She dabbed glue—squish, squish—and pressed the corner back. The glue felt cool and sticky. She held it still and counted, “One, two, three, four, five.” The paper stuck!

“Good strategy,” said Ms. Star.

Pippa smiled a small smile. “Thank you.”

She glued colorful strips for a tail. She tied on the blue ribbon. Tug, tug. The ribbon slipped out.

Pippa’s eyebrows scrunched. “It won’t stay!”

Her chest felt tight again. Her feet wanted to stomp. But she remembered. “Stop.” She placed her paw on her heart. “Breathe.”

Smell the flower. Blow the candle. She felt the cool air in her nose. She felt her shoulders drop.

“What’s another way?” Pippa asked herself. “A new strategy.”

She looked around and found tape. She felt the smooth roll and heard the sticky rip. Rrrrip! She taped the ribbon gently to the back of the kite. Press, press. It held!

“I did it!” she cheered. Her cheer sounded like tiny bells in the sunny room.

Across the table, a soft bunny plush sat in a basket. Pippa gave the bunny a pat. “We are brave crafters,” she told it.

Now it was time to draw clouds on the kite. Pippa picked up a white crayon. Swish, swish. The clouds looked fluffy like her fur. She added a small sun. Circle, dot dot for eyes, smile. “Hello, Sunny,” she giggled.

Last, she wanted to hang the kite on the window. She stood up tall. The window felt cool when she touched it. But the sticky hook was high. Too high.

Pippa felt a thump in her chest. “I can’t reach,” she said. “What if I drop it?”

Ms. Star came close. “This is a brave moment,” she said. “What are your safe choices?”

Pippa looked around. There was a little step stool with rubber dots. It looked steady. “I can ask for help,” she said, “and I can use the stool.”

“Great plan,” Ms. Star said. “I will spot you.”

Pippa wiped her paws on a soft towel. “Clean paws help sticky hooks stick,” she said. Healthy habits! She climbed the stool. Tap, tap went her feet. She held the kite close, like a hug. She took one more slow breath. Smell the flower. Blow the candle.

“I am brave and calm,” she whispered.

She pressed the hook on the window. Press, press. It stuck! She hung the kite. The ribbon tail tickled her nose. It smelled like clean paper and a tiny hint of glue.

Pippa climbed down. The kite swayed in the gentle fan wind. Whrrr… swish… swish.

“You did it,” said Ms. Star. “How did you solve your problems today?”

Pippa thought. “When I felt mad, I stopped and breathed. I tried new strategies: glue, tape, and a stool. I asked for help. I kept going, even when it was hard.”

Ms. Star nodded. “That is patience and bravery.”

Pippa’s heart felt warm. She gave herself a small panda hug. She sipped her water. Cool, clear, ahh. Then she cleaned up her table—cap on glue, scissors in the cup, scraps in the bin. Rustle, clink, plop.

She looked at the bright kite in the sunny window. “When I feel frustrated,” she said, “I can be calm, brave, and try a new way.”

The kite nodded in the fan wind, as if it agreed.

The End.
