# Binky Bear Solves the Case

Binky was a friendly bear cub with soft, fuzzy fur and bright, curious eyes. Today, Binky was in Mama’s home office. The room smelled like fresh paper and warm tea. The computer hummed, the printer clicked, and sunbeams made little dust sparkles dance in the air.

“Mama, can I help you work?” Binky asked, standing on tip-paws.

“You can be my helper,” Mama Bear said with a smile. “I need to send a very important letter.”

Binky loved to help. He liked the smooth feel of sticky notes and the soft swish of pens on paper. He also liked the big, comfy office chair that spun around. Wheee!

Suddenly, Mama looked around. “Oh dear,” she said. “My special red stamp is missing. I need it to send the letter.”

Binky’s ears perked up. “A missing stamp? This is a job for Detective Binky!” He put a sticky note on his forehead like a badge. “I will be resourceful,” he said proudly. “I will use what I have to solve the case.”

First, Binky looked with his eyes. He peeked under the keyboard. He peered behind the stack of folders. He sniffed gently, and the room smelled like lemon cleaner and paper. No stamp.

Binky thought, Think, think. What can I use? He rolled the office chair to the bookcase. “Zoom!” he whispered. “I will check the high shelf.” But Binky was small. He could not reach.

He looked around. He saw a safe, sturdy footstool. “A tool!” Binky cheered. He pushed the stool with a little grunt. “Uff!” He climbed up, holding on with his soft paws. He looked carefully. “Hmm. No stamp. But I see Mama’s hole-punch and paper clips.”

Binky climbed down. He did not give up. “I will try a new idea,” he said. He took a long ruler and a flashlight. Click. The light made a warm circle on the floor.

He shined the light under the desk. “Hello?” he said to the dust bunnies. “Have you seen a red stamp?” He saw a paperclip, a crayon, and a little toy car. No stamp.

Binky heard a rustle. Flap, flap. He turned and saw Squeaky the mouse peeking by the trash bin. “Hi, Squeaky,” Binky said kindly. “Did you see a red stamp?”

Squeaky twitched his whiskers. “I saw something red roll under the printer,” he squeaked.

“Thank you for telling me,” Binky said. “I will check. And I will be fair and gentle.” Binky believed in justice. He did not blame anyone. He just wanted to find the stamp.

Binky crawled to the big, warm printer. It hummed, “Bzzz.” He lay on the soft rug and shined his light. “Aha!” he said. He saw the red stamp. But it was too far for his paw.

“What can I use?” Binky whispered. He looked at his tools. He had the ruler. He had paper clips. He had string from the drawer. He smiled a big, bright smile. “I will make a grabber!”

Binky slid paper clips onto the end of the ruler. Click, click. He tied the string tight. Knot, knot. He made a little hook. “This is my helpful hook,” he said.

He put his cheek on the cool floor and reached the ruler hook under the printer. “Steady,” Binky said. He felt calm. He felt brave. Hook, hook. Clink! The hook touched the stamp.

“I did it!” Binky cheered softly. He pulled the string slow and gentle. The red stamp slid, slid, slid and popped out. Plop!

Binky picked it up. It smelled like ink and cherries. “Mama! I found it!”

Mama Bear clapped her paws. “Oh, Binky! How did you do that?”

“I looked. I listened. I asked. I made a tool,” Binky said. “I was resourceful!”

Mama smiled and gave Binky a warm hug. “You were also fair. You did not blame Squeaky. You asked with kind words. That shows justice. And you listened to his idea. That shows open-mindedness.”

Squeaky peeked out again. “I am sorry,” he squeaked. “I bumped the stamp when I ran by. I was scared of the printer.”

“It’s okay,” Binky said. “Thank you for telling the truth. We can all be careful.”

Mama stamped the letter with a happy thump. Thump! “Now the letter is ready,” she said. “Teamwork saved the day.”

Binky looked around the office. The sun felt warm on his fur. The printer smelled like warm paper. The chair went wheee when he gave it a spin. He felt proud.

“Mama,” Binky asked, “can we make a special spot for the stamp so it doesn’t get lost again?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “What can we use?”

Binky thought. He found a small box. He put soft tissue inside. He drew a red stamp picture on the lid. “This is the Stamp Home,” he said. “It will live by the printer.”

Mama nodded. “That is a smart plan.”

Binky waved to Squeaky. “Do you want a little snack?” he asked.

Squeaky’s eyes shone. “Yes, please.”

They shared crunchy apple slices. Crunch, crunch. They all laughed.

Binky said, “Today I learned to use what I have, try new ideas, and be fair and kind.”

Mama said, “That is resourcefulness, justice, and open-mindedness.”

Binky smiled. “Detective Binky is on the case, always ready to help!”

The End.
