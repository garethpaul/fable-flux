# Benny's Big Brave Moment

Benny the rabbit twitched his soft brown nose as he hopped down the creaky wooden stairs. The basement lab smelled like old books and something sweet. Colorful bottles lined the shelves, glowing softly in the dim light.

"Wow!" whispered Benny, his big eyes growing wide. "Professor Owl's lab is amazing!"

But Professor Owl had told him very clearly: "Wait upstairs, Benny. I'll be right back with your carrot juice."

Benny's fluffy tail wiggled with excitement. A big red button on the tall table caught his eye. It was shiny and round, just perfect for pressing. His paws itched to touch it.

"Just one little press," Benny said to himself. "What could happen?"

He stretched up on his tippy-toes. His heart beat fast like a tiny drum. The button felt smooth and cool under his paw.

CLICK!

Suddenly, purple smoke puffed out everywhere! It smelled like grape candy but made Benny's eyes water. Beakers bubbled and fizzed. A funny whirring sound filled the air.

"Oh no, oh no!" squeaked Benny. His ears drooped down. "I wasn't supposed to touch anything!"

The purple smoke grew thicker. Benny couldn't see the stairs anymore. His little heart pounded. He felt scared and sorry all at once.

"Professor Owl!" he called out in his tiny voice. "Help! I made a big mistake!"

Heavy footsteps came down the stairs. "Benny? Where are you?" Professor Owl's deep voice sounded worried.

"I'm here!" Benny called, waving his paws in the purple cloud. "I touched the red button! I'm sorry!"

Professor Owl's big shadow appeared through the smoke. He pressed a blue button, and whoosh! A fan started spinning. The purple smoke began to clear.

Benny sat on the floor, looking very small. His whiskers trembled. "I didn't listen," he said quietly. "I was supposed to wait upstairs."

Professor Owl's kind eyes looked down at the little rabbit. "Benny, what happened here?"

Benny took a deep breath. Being honest felt scary, but he knew it was right. "I saw the shiny red button. I wanted to press it so badly. I didn't stop and think. I just did it." His voice got even smaller. "I'm really, really sorry."

Professor Owl sat down on the floor next to Benny. "Thank you for telling me the truth. That took courage."

"Courage?" Benny's ears perked up a little.

"Yes," smiled Professor Owl. "Being brave enough to say sorry when we make mistakes is very important. And you called for help right away. That was smart."

Benny felt a warm feeling in his chest. "I was scared you'd be angry."

"I'm proud of you for being honest," said Professor Owl gently. "But next time, what could you do when you feel like touching something?"

Benny thought hard. "I could take deep breaths and count to ten?"

"Excellent! And?"

"Ask first before touching anything!"

"Perfect!" Professor Owl clapped his wings. "Now, would you like to help me clean up? Then I'll show you how this machine actually works."

Benny's whole face lit up. "Really? You trust me to help?"

"Of course! Everyone makes mistakes, Benny. What matters is that we learn from them."

As they cleaned together, Benny felt proud and happy. He had been brave enough to tell the truth, and Professor Owl still wanted to be his friend.

"Next time," Benny said, "I'll be brave enough to wait and listen, just like I was brave enough to say sorry today."

Professor Owl smiled. "That's the bravest thing of all."

The End.
