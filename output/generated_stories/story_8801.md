# Poppy Panda’s Shed Surprise

Poppy was a patient panda with soft black-and-white fur and bright, gentle eyes. One sunny morning, she padded across the yard. The grass was cool on her paws. Birds sang, “Tweet-tweet!” and a breeze smelled like fresh leaves and rain.

Poppy stopped at the garden shed. The shed was small and wooden, with a squeaky door. “Eeeek,” said the door. Inside, it was cozy and smelled like soil and mint. Sunbeams made dust sparkle like tiny stars.

Poppy smiled. “Today is a good day to be outdoors,” she said. “I can help in the garden!”

On the shelf, she saw a little rake, a green watering can, a soft brush, and a bright yellow bucket. There were kid-sized gloves, too. Poppy put them on very carefully. “One glove, two gloves,” she said. “I am ready.”

From the corner came a tiny voice. “Hello?” It was Miri the ladybug, red with seven black dots. She waved a tiny leg. “I like your shed,” she said.

“Thank you, Miri,” said Poppy. “I will be gentle. I will share the space. Would you like to help?”

“Yes, please!” said Miri. “Let’s make the garden happy.”

Poppy picked up the rake. She took a deep breath. The rake made soft scratch-scratch sounds in the dirt outside the shed. “I rake slowly,” said Poppy. “I am careful.” She made neat lines where the carrots grew. She moved around little worms and said, “Excuse me, friends.”

“You are respectful,” Miri said. “Thank you for watching where you step.”

Poppy nodded. “Being outdoors means we care for living things.”

Next, Poppy filled the yellow bucket from the rain barrel. Drip, drop, plip. The water was cool on her paws. She poured the water into the green can. “Glug-glug,” said the can. Poppy giggled. “You sound thirsty!”

She walked to the flowers. “Good morning, daisies,” she said. “Here is a drink.” She tilted the can and counted, “One, two, three little pours.” The water sparkled in the sun. The daisies bobbed like they were dancing.

Miri flew round and round. “Hooray!” she cheered. “You are so helpful.”

Poppy felt warm and proud. Her tummy hummed like a happy song. “I like being outside,” she said. “It makes my body strong and my mind calm.”

Then she heard a rustle in the shed. Poppy peeked inside. A small red ball was stuck under a broom. “Oh!” said Poppy. “Someone might want this.” She waited and listened. She was patient. In a moment, Benji Bunny hopped by the door. His ears twitched. “Have you seen my red ball?” he asked.

“Yes,” Poppy said. “It is under the broom. Let’s move it together.” Poppy held the broom. Benji gently tugged the ball. Together, they counted, “One, two, three—pull!” The ball popped free and rolled with a soft thump.

“Thank you!” Benji said, smiling. “You are very helpful.”

“You’re welcome,” said Poppy. “Would you like to play a garden game?”

Benji’s eyes shone. “Yes!”

Poppy looked at the tools. “We will be responsible,” she said. “We will put tools back after we use them, and we will keep the path clear.”

They made a game called Treasure Garden. Poppy placed smooth stones in a circle. “This is our boat,” she said. “We sail to Island Shed!”

Miri buzzed as the captain. “All aboard!” she squeaked.

Poppy and Benji hopped from stone to stone. The grass tickled their toes. The sun made their shadows long and silly. They pretended the rake was a tall mast and the watering can was a bell. Ding-ding!

When the wind blew, Poppy sniffed the air. It smelled like rosemary. “Storm coming!” she said, giggling. She held an old blanket like a sail. “Hold tight!” Benji laughed and held one corner. Miri zipped to the top and called, “Steady, crew!”

Soon the “storm” passed. They landed at Island Shed. Poppy bowed. “Thank you, team. Now we tidy up.” She folded the blanket, set the rake on its hook, wiped the can, and put the bucket upside down so it could dry. She brushed the dirt from the floor. Swish-swish. The shed looked neat and happy.

Benji placed the ball in a basket. “Putting things away keeps everyone safe,” he said.

Poppy nodded. “And it helps us find them next time.”

Miri fluttered down. “You were patient, you were helpful, and you respected the garden,” she said.

Poppy felt her heart glow. “Being outdoors is fun,” she said. “I can move my body, use my imagination, and take care of the world.”

The sun grew lower. The air felt cool like a soft hug. Poppy waved to her friends. “See you tomorrow for more outdoor play,” she said.

“See you!” called Benji.

“See you!” buzzed Miri.

Poppy closed the squeaky door. “Eeeek,” it said. She smiled. “Good night, shed. Thank you for the adventure.”

She took a deep breath of the evening air and whispered, “Today I learned: Play outside, help others, respect living things, and clean up when you are done.”

Her paws felt tired in a good way. Her heart felt peaceful. Poppy knew she would come back to the shed again, ready for another kind day.

The End.
