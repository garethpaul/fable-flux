# Milo the Brave Monkey's Pantry Adventure

Milo the monkey loved to help his family. He had soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. Today, Mama Monkey asked him to get bananas from the tall pantry shelf.

"The bananas are way up high, Milo," Mama said. "Can you help me reach them?"

Milo looked up, up, up at the pantry shelf. It was very tall! The yellow bananas sat in a basket at the very top. They smelled sweet and delicious.

"I'm too small," Milo said quietly. His tail drooped down. "I can't reach that high."

But then Milo remembered what Papa Monkey always said: "Brave monkeys find a way!"

Milo looked around the pantry. He saw boxes, cans, and jars on the lower shelves. His eyes sparkled with an idea.

"I can build something!" Milo said out loud. "I'll make steps to climb up!"

First, Milo tried to stack soup cans. *Clank, clank, clank!* They rolled away. That didn't work.

"Hmm," Milo said, scratching his head. "I need to think more carefully."

He looked again. This time, he saw sturdy cereal boxes. They were flat and wide.

"These look stronger," Milo said. He carefully placed one box on the floor. *Thump!* It stayed put.

Then Milo added a second box on top. He pressed down gently to make sure it was steady. The boxes felt solid under his paws.

"One more should do it," Milo said bravely.

He placed a third box very carefully. Now he had steps! But they still weren't quite tall enough.

Milo didn't give up. He spotted a small stool nearby. 

"Perfect!" he said. He pushed the stool next to his box tower. Now he could climb from the stool to the boxes, then reach the bananas!

Milo took a deep breath. His heart beat fast, but he felt excited too.

"I can do this," he whispered to himself. "Brave monkeys try new things."

Step by step, Milo climbed up. First onto the stool. Then onto the first box. It held him! Then the second box. Still steady! Finally, the third box.

Now Milo could reach the banana basket! He stretched his arm up high and grabbed the handle carefully.

"Got them!" Milo cheered. The bananas smelled even sweeter up close.

Slowly and carefully, Milo climbed back down. Step by step, holding the basket tight. When his feet touched the floor, he felt so proud!

"Mama! Mama!" Milo called, running to the kitchen. "I got the bananas!"

Mama Monkey's eyes grew wide with surprise. "How did you reach so high, little one?"

"I built steps!" Milo said proudly. "I used boxes and a stool. I was scared at first, but I didn't give up!"

Mama Monkey hugged Milo tight. "You were so brave and clever! You thought about the problem and found a solution."

"It felt scary," Milo admitted. "But I remembered to be brave."

"That's what real courage is," Mama said softly. "Being scared but trying anyway. And you were so patient, thinking through each step."

Milo beamed with pride. His chest puffed out as he stood tall.

That evening, the whole monkey family enjoyed the sweet bananas together. Milo told Papa Monkey all about his pantry adventure.

"You built something amazing today," Papa said. "Not just steps, but confidence too!"

From that day on, whenever Milo faced something that seemed too hard, he remembered his pantry adventure. He learned that being brave, thinking carefully, and not giving up could help him solve any problem.

The End.
