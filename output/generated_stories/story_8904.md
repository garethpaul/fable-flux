# Nutkin's Big Helper Day

Nutkin the squirrel lived in a cozy oak tree next to Mrs. Chen's balcony garden. Every morning, he loved to watch the colorful flowers dance in the breeze and smell the sweet herbs growing in bright pots.

One sunny Tuesday, Nutkin heard a tiny voice crying. "Help! Help!" squeaked the voice.

Nutkin's fluffy tail twitched as he looked around. There, stuck between two heavy flower pots, was a little field mouse named Pip.

"Oh my!" said Nutkin, scrambling down to the balcony. "Don't worry, Pip. I'll help you!"

Nutkin pushed against the big pot with all his might. "Oof!" he grunted. The pot didn't move even a tiny bit. It was much too heavy for one small squirrel.

"It's no use," sniffled Pip. "I'll be stuck here forever!"

"No, you won't," said Nutkin firmly. "Sometimes the first try doesn't work, but that doesn't mean we give up! Let me think of another way."

Nutkin sat on his haunches and tapped his chin with his tiny paw. He looked at the smooth pots, the wooden deck, and the watering can nearby. Suddenly, his eyes sparkled with an idea!

"The water!" he exclaimed. "Water makes things slippery!"

Nutkin carefully tipped the watering can and let a small stream of cool water flow around the pots. The water made the deck wet and slippery.

"Now try to wiggle out, Pip!" called Nutkin encouragingly.

Pip tried to squeeze through, but he was still stuck. The little mouse's whiskers drooped sadly.

"Hmm," said Nutkin, not giving up. "What else can I try?" He noticed some smooth, round pebbles in a nearby planter. "Maybe these will help!"

One by one, Nutkin rolled the small pebbles under the pot's edge. Each pebble lifted the pot just a tiny bit higher. It was slow work, and Nutkin's paws got tired, but he kept going.

"You're working so hard to help me," said Pip gratefully. "Thank you for not giving up!"

"Friends help friends," puffed Nutkin as he rolled another pebble into place. "And sometimes helping takes time, but that's okay!"

After placing six pebbles, there was finally enough space! Pip squeezed through the gap and tumbled out safely onto the soft moss.

"Hooray!" cheered both friends, dancing around the balcony garden.

Just then, Mrs. Chen came outside to water her plants. She saw the two little animals and smiled warmly. "Well, hello there, little helpers!" she said gently. She sprinkled some sunflower seeds on the deck for them to share.

As they munched the tasty seeds together, Pip said, "Nutkin, you're the best friend ever! You didn't give up, even when it was hard."

Nutkin's chest puffed up with pride. "When something seems impossible, we just need to try different ways and be patient. There's always a solution if we keep trying!"

The warm sun shone down on the balcony garden as the two friends planned their next adventure. Nutkin had learned that helping others feels wonderful, and that being patient and trying different ideas can solve even the trickiest problems.

From that day on, whenever anyone in the garden needed help, they knew exactly who to call – Nutkin, the most resourceful and caring squirrel around!

The End.
