# Polly's Messy Art Corner

In a sunny backyard filled with colorful flowers and buzzing bees, lived a creative parrot named Polly. Her bright green feathers sparkled in the warm sunshine, and her orange beak was always busy creating wonderful things.

Polly loved to make art! She had paintbrushes, crayons, paper, glue, and sparkly stickers scattered all around her favorite corner of the yard. But there was one big problem - everything was so messy that Polly could never find what she needed.

"Oh no!" squawked Polly one bright morning. "I want to paint a beautiful flower, but where are my paintbrushes?" She flapped her wings and looked around frantically. Her art supplies were everywhere! Paintbrushes were stuck in the grass, crayons were rolling under the garden bench, and her special glitter was spilled all over the stone path.

"Squawk! This is too hard!" Polly cried, feeling frustrated. The warm sun felt nice on her feathers, but she couldn't enjoy it when everything was such a mess.

Just then, Polly's friend Benny the bunny hopped over. His soft gray fur twitched as he sniffed the sweet smell of nearby roses.

"What's wrong, Polly?" asked Benny kindly, his pink nose wiggling.

"I can't find anything!" Polly explained. "My art corner is too messy. I want to create, but I spend all my time looking for things instead of making beautiful art!"

Benny's ears perked up. "I have an idea! What if we organize your art supplies? My mama taught me that everything should have its own special place."

Polly tilted her colorful head. "Organize? What does that mean?"

"It means putting things where they belong," Benny explained patiently. "Like putting all your paintbrushes together in one spot, and all your crayons in another spot."

Polly thought about this. It sounded like a good idea! "But how do I decide where everything should go?" she wondered.

"Let's think together," said Benny. "What do you use most often?"

Polly fluffed her bright feathers as she considered. "Well, I use my paintbrushes and paints a lot. And I love my crayons too!"

"Perfect!" chirped Benny. "Let's find containers for them."

They found an old flower pot for the paintbrushes and a small wooden box for the crayons. Polly carefully placed each paintbrush in the pot, feeling proud as she watched them stand up straight and neat. The crayons fit perfectly in the little box, making a satisfying clicking sound.

"This feels good!" Polly squawked happily. "Everything looks so tidy!"

Next, they organized the paper in a flat basket and put the glue and stickers in a small tin can. Even though it took some time, Polly felt excited watching her art corner transform.

"Now when I want to paint," said Polly, "I know exactly where my paintbrushes are!" She demonstrated by easily picking up a brush from the flower pot.

The next morning, when the golden sunshine warmed the backyard again, Polly wanted to create a picture of the beautiful butterfly she saw. Instead of searching everywhere, she simply walked to her organized art corner. Everything was right where it belonged!

"This is wonderful!" Polly sang as she painted. "Being organized makes creating art so much more fun!"

From that day on, Polly always put her supplies back in their special places. She learned that taking time to organize made everything easier and more enjoyable.

The End.
