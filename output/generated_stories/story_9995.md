# Bubbles Builds a Bridge

In a quiet attic, where the air smelled like old wood and cinnamon, a playful dolphin named Bubbles peeked out of a big blue toy trunk. The attic was soft and still. Dust sparkles floated like tiny stars. Sunlight made warm stripes on the floor.

“Click-click!” went a lamp. “Creak,” sang the floor. Bubbles clapped her fins. “Whee! Let’s play!”

Two friends were there too: Pip the plush penguin and Nib the shy toy mouse. On the floor lay colorful blocks, a little bell, and a folded blanket.

Bubbles swam through the air—just like magic—and giggled. “Let’s build a bridge!” she said. “A big, brave bridge from the trunk to the window nook!”

Pip waddled and nodded. “Yes! We can do it. I will stack blue blocks.”

Nib took a small breath. “I can carry little blocks,” he squeaked. “I am small, but I can help.”

They began. Tap, tap. Stack, stack. The blocks felt cool and smooth. The blanket felt soft and warm. The attic smelled dusty and sweet. Outside, a bird chirped, “Cheep-cheep!”

Bubbles placed the first block. “Plop!” Pip added two more. “Thunk, thunk.” Nib carried a tiny red block and set it down. “Click.”

Soon, the bridge grew tall and wide. “We are brave builders,” Bubbles sang. “We can do big things!”

But then—oops! Bubbles hopped too fast. Her fin bumped the tower. The blocks began to wobble. “Wibble-wobble—CRASH!” Down went the bridge into a bright, clattery pile.

Pip’s eyes filled with tears. “Oh no! My blue blocks!” he cried.

Nib squeaked and hid behind the bell. “It’s too hard. I’m scared.”

Bubbles felt her tummy twist like a knot. “I’m sorry,” she whispered. “I was too fast.” Her fins drooped. The attic felt quiet and heavy.

Pip crossed his little wings. “You knocked it down.”

Bubbles took a brave breath. “You’re right,” she said softly. “I made a mistake. I feel sad. Can we fix it together?”

Pip sniffed. He looked at the colorful mess. He took a slow breath too. “I feel mad,” he said, “but I still want a bridge.”

Nib peeked out. “I want to help,” he whispered. “But I am scared it will fall again.”

Bubbles nodded. “Let’s solve this. We can talk. We can listen. We can try a new plan.”

She tapped the floor gently. “Step one: We go slow.” Tap.

Pip wiped a tear. “Step two: We take turns.” Tap.

Nib stood a little taller. “Step three: We make a strong base.” Tap.

“Good plan,” said Bubbles. “I will start with the big, flat blocks. Pip, you can check if they are steady. Nib, you can bring the light blocks for the top.”

They began again. Slow and steady. Tap, tap. Press, test. “Is it wiggly?” Bubbles asked.

Pip pressed the sides. “No wiggles. Good job.”

Nib carried a tiny yellow block. “Here you go,” he said, smiling a little.

Bubbles looked at Pip. “I will move carefully. If I go too fast, please tell me.”

“I will use gentle words,” said Pip. “I will say, ‘Slow, please.’”

Nib added, “And I will say, ‘I’m scared,’ when I feel worried.”

They worked as a team. When the blocks leaned, they paused. “Slow, please,” Pip reminded.

“Thank you,” Bubbles said. She moved her fin gently. “I hear you.”

When Nib’s paws shook, he took a big breath in and out. “I’m scared,” he said.

Bubbles smiled kindly. “You are brave, Nib. Let’s do it together.” She steadied the block so Nib could place it. Click.

Little by little, the bridge grew again. Wide base. Firm sides. Light blocks on top. The blanket became a soft path. The bell sat at the end like a shiny reward.

At last, Bubbles tapped the final block. “Click!”

They all looked. The bridge stood tall and strong. Sunlight kissed the top, and it glowed like a rainbow.

Pip rang the bell. “Ding!” He laughed. “We did it!”

Nib touched the blanket path. “It feels cozy,” he said. “I feel proud.”

Bubbles felt warm and brave. “Thank you for talking with me,” she told Pip. “Thank you for telling me your feelings. I learned to listen.”

Pip smiled. “I learned to use gentle words.”

Nib nodded. “I learned to take brave breaths.”

They swam—waddled—scurried across the bridge together. The attic did not feel quiet now. It felt full of happy sounds. Clap, ding, giggle!

Bubbles looked at her friends. “We had a problem,” she said. “We solved it with kind words, a new plan, and courage.”

Pip added, “And we finished our task!”

Nib squeaked, “Together!”

They cuddled on the blanket path, watching the dust sparkles dance in the sun. The bridge stood strong beside them, a colorful sign of teamwork and care.

Lesson: When something goes wrong, we can stop, talk, listen, and try again. With courage, kind words, and a good plan, friends can fix problems and finish what they start.

The End.
