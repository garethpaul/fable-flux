# Ollie's Garden Helper Day

Ollie the otter loved splashing in his little pool on the sunny balcony garden. The warm morning sun tickled his whiskers as he floated on his back, watching the fluffy white clouds drift by.

"Good morning, Ollie!" called Mama Otter from behind the tomato plants. "Today is garden chore day. Would you like to help me take care of our beautiful plants?"

Ollie's eyes sparkled with excitement. "Yes, yes! I want to help!" He scrambled out of his pool, water droplets glistening on his brown fur like tiny diamonds.

Mama Otter smiled. "Wonderful! First, let's water the flowers. They're very thirsty this morning."

She handed Ollie a small, bright red watering can. It was just the right size for his little paws. "Hold it carefully with both paws," Mama said gently.

Ollie gripped the watering can tight. The water inside made a gentle sloshing sound as he walked slowly to the colorful petunias. "Like this, Mama?"

"Perfect! Now tip it gently so the water comes out like soft rain."

Ollie tilted the can, and cool water streamed out in a silvery arc. The flowers seemed to dance as the water touched their petals. "They look so happy!" Ollie giggled.

"Plants need water every day, just like we need food," Mama explained. "Taking care of them is an important job."

Next, Mama showed Ollie how to pull out the small weeds that were trying to steal food from their vegetables. "See these little green plants that don't belong? We need to gently pull them out."

Ollie's nimble paws worked carefully around the lettuce. "This one?" he asked, pointing to a tiny weed.

"Yes! You're learning so well," Mama praised.

The soil felt cool and crumbly between Ollie's toes. He loved the earthy smell and the way the dirt tickled his paw pads. Each weed he pulled made him feel proud and helpful.

"Now for the best part," Mama said with a wink. "Let's pick some fresh strawberries for lunch!"

Ollie's whiskers twitched with excitement. The strawberries hung like little red jewels among the green leaves. "Can I pick them all by myself?"

"Of course! Choose the ones that are bright red and feel soft but not squishy."

Ollie carefully selected each strawberry, placing them gently in a wicker basket. "One, two, three!" he counted proudly. The sweet strawberry scent made his mouth water.

When they finished all their garden chores, Ollie felt tired but very happy. His fur was a little dirty, and his paws were muddy, but his heart felt warm and full.

"Thank you for being such a wonderful helper today," Mama said, giving him a big, fuzzy hug. "Because you helped with the chores, our garden will grow strong and beautiful."

Ollie beamed with pride. "I did it all by myself! Well, almost all by myself," he added with a giggle.

"You certainly did! When we take care of our responsibilities, everything around us grows happier and healthier," Mama explained. "And the best part is, you learned how to do important jobs that help our whole family."

As they sat together sharing the sweet strawberries, Ollie felt proud knowing he had helped make their garden beautiful. Tomorrow, he decided, he would do his chores again—all by himself!

The End.
