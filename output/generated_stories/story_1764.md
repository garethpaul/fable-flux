# Jump-Start Joey's Sunny Choices

Joey was a joyful kangaroo joey with springy feet and a soft, warm pouch. He lived in a sunny backyard with green grass, bright flowers, and a gentle breeze that smelled like lemon and leaves. Birds sang sweet songs, and the sun felt like a cozy hug.

One morning, Joey popped up and stretched. “Good morning, sun!” he giggled. His tummy made a little rumble-rumble.

Mama Roo laughed softly. “Good morning, Joey. Your tummy says it’s breakfast time.”

Joey’s eyes twinkled. On the table sat a shiny bowl of rainbow fruit, a plate of crunchy carrot sticks, and a big cup of cool water. Next to it was a tiny bowl of crispy crackers.

Joey sniffed the sweet strawberries. “Mmm. I want crackers!” he said.

Mama Roo knelt down so her eyes met his. “Let’s make a healthy choice first,” she said gently. “Fruit gives you jump power, and water helps you go-go-go. Crackers can be a later treat.”

Joey rubbed his chin. “Jump power sounds fun.” He looked at the crackers, then at the juicy blueberries. He took a deep breath and listened to his tummy. “Okay. Fruit first, water sip, then maybe crackers later.”

“That’s a smart plan,” Mama Roo said. “You made a good decision.”

Joey chomped on strawberries. Squish! Sweet juice dripped on his chin. He crunched carrot sticks. Crunch, crunch! He sipped cool water. Ahh! His tummy felt full and happy.

After breakfast, the backyard sparkled. Bees buzzed softly. The grass felt tickly under Joey’s toes. His best friend, Wally the wombat, waddled through the gate, carrying a red ball.

“Hi, Joey!” Wally grinned. “Ball time?”

Joey bounced. “Yes! I have extra jump power!”

They played “Bounce and Roll.” Joey boinged high, and Wally rolled the ball with his paws. The ball made a thump-thump sound on the ground. The flowers waved as the wind whispered, “Whee!”

After a while, Wally panted, “I’m thirsty.”

Joey remembered his water. His cup sat by the porch, beads of cold drops sliding down the sides. He looked at Wally, who licked his dry lips. Joey’s heart felt warm.

“You can have a big sip,” Joey said, offering the cup.

Wally sipped. “Ahh, thank you! That was kind.” Joey took a small sip too. The water tasted fresh, like morning air.

They played again until they heard a tiny scratch-scratch. A little blue lizard peeked from under the flower pot. Its eyes were shiny like raindrops.

“Hello, little friend,” Joey said softly. “Do you want to play too?”

The lizard blinked and flicked its tail. Joey listened. He listened to the wind, to Wally’s quiet breaths, and to the lizard’s tiny steps. He had an idea.

“Let’s play a game we all can do,” Joey said. “We can play ‘Gentle Garden Helpers!’ We will pick up fallen leaves and make a cozy pile for the lizard to hide in.”

Wally nodded. “I can roll the leaves. My paws are good at that.”

Joey bounced lightly to the big maple tree. Leaves made crunchy, papery sounds. The lizard followed, tick-tick-tick on the stones. Joey and Wally worked together. They talked softly and listened to each other’s ideas.

“Let’s make the pile here, near the flowers,” Wally suggested.

Joey thought. “Yes. The sun is warm here, and the shade is close too.”

They made a soft leaf bed. The lizard snuggled in and looked very pleased. Joey’s chest felt big with happy.

After helping, Joey’s tummy rumbled again. He and Wally sat on the grass. Mama Roo brought out a little snack plate. There were apple slices, cucumber rounds, and the tiny bowl of crackers.

Mama Roo smiled. “Let’s make a smart snack choice. What goes in your body to help your body?”

Joey paused. He listened to his tummy and to Mama’s gentle voice. He looked at the colorful plate. “I choose apples and cucumbers first,” he said. “Crunchy green and sweet red. Then a small cracker.”

Wally nodded. “I choose the same!” They crunched together. Crunch-crunch. Munch-munch. The cucumbers tasted cool, like raindrops. The apples were juicy and made their cheeks sticky-sweet. They each took one cracker at the end. Yum.

When they finished, Joey noticed only one apple slice was left. He looked at Wally. He looked at the cozy lizard in the leaf bed. Joey thought, then smiled.

“Wally, would you like the last apple slice?” he asked.

Wally tilted his head. “Do you want it?”

Joey took a breath. “We can share! Half for you, half for me.”

Mama Roo clapped softly. “That’s good negotiating and generous, too.”

They split the slice. It made a little snap sound. They both giggled.

The sun slipped lower, and the backyard smelled like warm grass and flowers. Joey felt calm and proud. He had made good choices. He had listened to his tummy, to his friends, and to his mama. He had shared. He had helped.

Mama Roo gave Joey a snuggly hug. “How do you feel, my joyful joey?” she asked.

Joey nestled close. “I feel strong and kind,” he said. “Fruit and water give me jump power. Sharing makes my heart warm. Listening helps me choose.”

Mama Roo smiled. “That’s today’s lesson.”

Joey bounced once, softly. “Tomorrow, I’ll make healthy choices again.”

The wind whispered, “Yes,” and the little lizard peeked out, as if to agree.

The End.
