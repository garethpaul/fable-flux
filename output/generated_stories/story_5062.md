# Ping the Panda's Special Welcome Gift

Ping the panda lived in a cozy guest room at Grandma's house. He had soft black and white fur and kind, gentle eyes. Ping loved to make things with his paws.

Today was special! Ping's cousin Lily was coming to visit. She would sleep in the guest room with him. Ping wanted to make something wonderful for her.

"What can I make for Lily?" Ping wondered aloud. He looked around the guest room. There was a small desk with colorful paper, scissors, and glue. His tummy rumbled softly.

Ping had an idea! "I'll make a special snack holder for Lily," he said with excitement. "She can keep healthy treats in it!"

Ping gathered the supplies. He picked up bright yellow paper that felt smooth under his paws. The scissors made a happy "snip-snip" sound as he cut carefully.

But oh no! The paper tore. "Oops," Ping said softly. He felt disappointed, but he didn't give up. Patient Ping took a deep breath.

"I can try again," he told himself. "Good things take time." Ping chose green paper this time. It reminded him of fresh bamboo leaves.

Slowly and carefully, Ping cut out a rectangle. Then he folded the edges up to make a little box. The paper crinkled pleasantly as he worked.

Next, Ping decorated the box. He drew pictures of apples, carrots, and grapes with his crayons. The red crayon smelled waxy and felt bumpy in his paw.

"This will hold Lily's healthy snacks," Ping said proudly. "She'll love having fruits and vegetables to munch on!"

Just then, Ping heard footsteps in the hallway. "Ping! I'm here!" called a cheerful voice. It was Lily!

Ping's cousin bounced into the guest room. She had curly brown hair and a big smile. "Hi, Ping! I missed you so much!"

"Welcome, Lily!" Ping said warmly. He held up his creation. "I made this special snack box for you. It's for keeping healthy treats!"

Lily's eyes sparkled with joy. "Oh, Ping! It's beautiful! I love the fruit pictures you drew." She hugged the patient panda tight.

"Look inside," Ping said shyly. He had filled the box with apple slices and baby carrots from the kitchen. The apple slices smelled sweet and fresh.

"Mmm! These look delicious," Lily said. She crunched on a carrot stick. "Thank you for thinking of healthy snacks. And thank you for making this with your own paws!"

Ping felt warm and happy inside. "I wanted to show you how much I care," he explained. "Making things for people we love is special."

Lily nodded. "Can you teach me how to make one too? Then I can make a gift for someone else!"

"Of course!" Ping said excitedly. "We can make them together. Crafting is more fun when we share it!"

The two cousins spent the afternoon cutting, folding, and decorating. They made snack boxes for Grandma, Mom, and Dad. The guest room filled with the sounds of happy giggles and crinkling paper.

"I learned something today," Lily said as they cleaned up. "Taking time to make something special shows people you care about them."

Ping smiled his gentle panda smile. "And eating healthy foods gives us energy to create even more wonderful things!"

That night, as they snuggled in their beds, both cousins felt proud of their handmade gifts and their full, happy hearts.

The End.
