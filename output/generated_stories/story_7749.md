# Ellie’s Big Helper Heart

In a comfortable nursery with soft rugs and sunny windows, a gentle elephant calf named Ellie wiggled her ears. The room smelled like clean blankets and warm milk. Stuffed animals sat on little shelves. Blocks clinked gently in a basket. The clock made a soft tick-tock sound.

“Good morning, Ellie,” said Nana Nori, the kind caregiver, smoothing a blue blanket. “Today, everyone has a small job. Jobs help us take care of our nursery. That is responsibility.”

Ellie’s eyes grew wide. “Re-spon-si-bi-li-ty,” she said slowly, tasting the big word like a sweet banana. “What is my job?”

Nana Nori smiled. “Your job is Block Helper. You make sure the blocks go back in their basket after playtime.”

Ellie swished her small tail. “I can do it! I am the Block Helper!”

Her friends clapped. Pip the puppy wagged. Momo the monkey squeaked. Lulu the lamb gave a tiny baa. “We have jobs too!” said Pip. “I am Water Cup Watcher.” “I am Blanket Straightener,” said Lulu proudly. Momo bounced. “I am Book Buddy!”

Ellie felt warm and excited. She liked her job. She liked the idea of helping. The blocks were bright and smooth, red, blue, and yellow. She could feel the cool wood on her trunk.

Playtime began. Ellie built a tall tower. Tap, tap, tap. The blocks clicked together. “Look!” Ellie cheered. “Tall like a tree!” Momo clapped. Pip barked softly. Lulu nuzzled the soft rug.

Then the snack bell chimed—ding-a-ling! Everyone looked at the bowls of apple slices. The apples smelled sweet. Ellie’s tummy rumbled. Nana Nori called, “Snack time! And after snack, we tidy our jobs.”

Ellie looked at her tower. The blocks glittered in the sun. “I will put them away later,” she whispered. “Just one quick snack.”

She hurried to the table. Crunch, crunch went the apples. Slurp went the water cups. Pip drank carefully. Lulu dabbed her chin. Ellie smiled. “Apples are so juicy!”

After snack, Nana Nori said gently, “Time to do our jobs.” Lulu straightened blankets. Momo stacked books in a neat line. Pip checked water cups and wiped a little drip.

Ellie looked at the blocks. The tower still stood in the sunlight. She felt sleepy from the warm room. “Maybe I can play just a little more,” she said. Ellie tapped the tower again. Tap, tap.

Momo tilted his head. “Ellie, are you putting away your blocks?”

“Later,” Ellie said. “The tower is so pretty.”

Then Nana Nori rolled in the nap mats. “Nap time soon,” she sang softly.

Ellie’s tower wobbled. Wibble wobble—CRASH! The blocks spilled all over the floor. Clack clack clatter! A red block slid under Pip’s paw. “Yip!” Pip hopped back. A blue block bumped Lulu’s mat. Lulu’s eyes grew big.

Ellie’s tummy felt twisty. Her ears drooped. “Oh no,” she whispered. “I forgot my job. I was the Block Helper. I did not help.”

Pip looked worried. “I almost slipped.”

Lulu whispered, “My nap mat is bumpy now.”

Momo sighed. “I can’t walk past the blocks without stepping on them.”

Ellie felt a little tear prick. She took a deep breath and listened to the quiet tick-tock. The room smelled like apples and warm blankets. She remembered Nana Nori’s words: Jobs help us take care of our nursery.

Ellie lifted her trunk high. “Friends, I am sorry,” she said, voice soft but brave. “I forgot my job. I will fix it. I want to be responsible.”

Nana Nori knelt beside her. “Thank you for your honest words, Ellie. You can make it right.”

Ellie nodded. “I will.” She picked up a red block. She picked up a blue block. Clink, clink. “Basket, please,” she said to herself, helping her remember. “Blocks belong in the basket.”

Momo hopped closer. “Can I help?”

Ellie looked at the big spill and then at her friends. She thought about fairness. “Yes, but it was my job, so I will do the most. You can help a little.”

“That is fair,” said Lulu. Pip wagged. “I’ll roll the blocks toward you.”

Together, they made soft music. Clink. Clack. Slide. Drop. Ellie worked the hardest. She felt the smooth wood and heard the happy sound of blocks finding home. Her heart felt lighter and lighter.

Soon, the floor was clear. The basket was full. Ellie pressed down the last block gently. “There,” she said with a smile. “Safe and tidy.”

Pip tested the floor. “No more slipping!”

Lulu patted her mat. “Smooth again.”

Momo gave Ellie a tiny high-five. “You were responsible and kind.”

Nana Nori hugged Ellie around her shoulders. “You remembered your job, you fixed the problem, and you let friends help in a fair way. That is responsibility. That is empathy.”

Ellie’s ears wiggled happily. “Being responsible keeps everyone safe. It helps our nursery feel cozy.”

They curled up for nap time. The room was quiet and calm. The clock went tick-tock. Ellie smelled clean blankets and felt warm and proud. She whispered, “Tomorrow, I will do my job right away.”

Lulu murmured, “Me too.”

Pip yawned, “We help each other.”

Momo whispered, “That’s fair.”

Ellie closed her eyes. She felt sleepy and glad, like a soft song in her heart. In the comfortable nursery, everyone rested, safe and snug, because they each took care of their jobs, and they cared for one another.

The End.
