# Tiko Finishes the Song

Tiko was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a small, happy trunk. Today was music day. The musical practice room smelled like polished wood and lemon soap. The floor was shiny. Sunlight made warm squares on the ground. Instruments sat in neat rows—tiny triangles, jingling tambourines, wooden sticks, and a big drum that looked like a round, sleepy moon.

Tiko wiggled his toes. “I have a list,” he said softly, patting a small card his teacher gave him. “Step one: warm up. Step two: practice the new song. Step three: help clean up.”

He took a deep breath. His trunk felt tingly with excitement. “I can do it. One step at a time,” he whispered.

First, the warm-up. Tiko puffed his cheeks and hummed, “mmm-mmm.” He tapped the floor with his foot—tap, tap. He shook the jingle bells gently—chika-chika. The sound sparkled like tiny stars. Tiko smiled. His ears flapped with the rhythm.

“Hello!” chirped Lina the little lark, fluttering to a music stand. “I want the bells!”

Tiko looked at his list. “I am warming up now,” he said kindly. “You can use the bells after I finish this step.”

Lina’s feathers ruffled. “I want them now.”

Tiko took a breath. His tummy felt wobbly, like jelly. He remembered the steps. “Warm up, practice, clean up,” he said to himself. Then he turned to Lina and said, “I can share after I finish. Would you like the triangle while you wait?” He held up the shiny triangle.

Lina blinked. “Okay,” she chirped. She took the triangle and went, “ting… ting!” The clear sound was like a raindrop in a pond. Tiko’s tummy felt calm again. He finished his warm-up. Step one: done.

“Good job, me,” Tiko said with a tiny giggle.

Now, step two: the new song. Tiko picked up the wooden claves—tap, tap—to keep the beat. The new song was called Little River. It had a soft part like whispering water and a loud part like happy splashes.

“Tap-tap, tip-tap, tap,” he practiced. He counted, “One, two, three, four.” The claves felt smooth and warm in his hands. He could smell the wood, sweet and light.

Just then, Rizo the raccoon rolled in a big drum. “Make way!” he said. “I want to play the drum now!”

Tiko’s heart bumped a little faster. The drum was part of the loud splashy part. Tiko needed it later in the song, not yet. He looked at his list again. “Practice the new song,” he read. “I am on this step.”

Rizo tapped the drum with his paw—boom! The sound was big and round, like thunder but not scary. “Isn’t it great?” he grinned.

“It is great,” Tiko said gently. “I will need the drum when I reach the loud part. Could you wait until I count to twenty? Then it will be your turn.”

Rizo’s whiskers twitched. “Twenty is a big number.”

Tiko nodded. “We can count together.” He held up a soft, strong foot. “Ready?”

Rizo huffed, but his eyes softened. “Okay.”

“1, 2, 3…” Tiko tapped the claves softly as they counted, “4, 5, 6…” Lina chimed tiny “ting!” notes on the triangle. The sound felt like a friendly clock. “19, 20!”

Tiko smiled. “Thank you, Rizo. Now we play the loud part together!”

Rizo’s face lit up. “Together!”

Tiko counted, “One, two, ready, go!” He tapped the beat—tap, tap, tap. Rizo boomed the drum—boom, BOOM! Lina added bright “ting!” notes. The room filled with music—soft like water, then loud like splashes, then soft again. Tiko’s chest felt warm and proud. He was doing it. He was finishing the song.

Just then, a tiny mouse peeked from behind a music stand. “H-hello,” she squeaked. “I am Mimmi. I want to learn, but I am small.”

Tiko’s ears drooped kindly. “All sizes can make music,” he said. He remembered his list. Practice the new song. He had almost finished. He could be generous and still complete his task.

“Would you like to help with the last soft part?” Tiko asked. He offered Mimmi the jingle bells. “Shake them slowly, like little raindrops.”

Mimmi’s eyes sparkled. “Really? For me?”

“For us,” Tiko said. “We play together.”

They began. Tiko tapped the claves quiet—tap… tap… Rizo made a gentle drum heartbeat—boomp… boomp… Lina sang a sweet “la-la,” and Mimmi shook the bells—chika… chika… The music sounded like a comfy blanket. Tiko felt calm and happy. He reached the end of the song and lifted his trunk high. “We did it!”

Everyone smiled. Lina fluttered. Rizo clapped his paws. Mimmi bounced on her tiny feet.

Tiko looked at his list. Step two: done. He beamed. “Step three: help clean up.”

He started gathering instruments. “Rizo, can you roll the big drum back?” he asked. “Lina, can you hang the triangle on the hook? Mimmi, can you place the bells in the box?” Tiko put the claves away and wiped the shiny floor where water had dripped from a flower vase. The room smelled like lemon again.

They finished together. Tiko checked his card. All steps done. He felt strong and steady inside, like a tall tree.

Teacher Panda peeked in and smiled. “I heard beautiful music, and I see a clean room. Tiko, you followed your steps. You were patient, you solved problems with kind words, and you shared. That is how we make music—and peace.”

Tiko’s ears flapped happily. “One step at a time,” he said. “We can finish our tasks and help friends too.”

Lina chirped, “I liked waiting with counting.”

Rizo nodded. “I liked playing together.”

Mimmi squeaked, “I liked that I could help.”

Tiko smiled. “Me too,” he said. “When we finish our tasks and use gentle words, everyone shines.”

The End.
