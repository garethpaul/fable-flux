# Hoots and Hearts in the Practice Room

In a bright musical practice room, sunlight danced on shiny drums and golden bells. A row of tiny pianos tinkled when a breeze slipped in. The room smelled like fresh wood and lemon polish. In the corner, on a tall music stand, sat a wise old owl named Olwyn. His feathers were soft gray and brown, and his eyes were big and kind.

“Good morning,” hooted Olwyn. “Today we make music, and we listen to our feelings.”

Little friends came in with gentle footsteps and excited giggles. Pip the mouse wore a tiny red scarf. Lila the lamb carried a small drum. Benji the bunny held a bright blue shaker. Each friend had a big smile. But there was also Strum the squirrel, clutching a shiny guitar, tail flicking, eyes darting.

Olwyn tapped a triangle. Ting! “We will play together,” he said. “We will share, take turns, and use our listening ears. If a feeling gets big, we will pause and breathe.”

Everyone nodded. The room filled with happy sounds: tap-tap, ding-ding, shake-shake. But suddenly, Strum strummed very loud. RANG-RANG-RANG! Benji’s ears drooped. Lila covered her woolly ears. Pip squeaked and dropped his scarf.

“Strum,” said Olwyn softly, “your guitar is strong and bright. Can you play soft-soft, like a butterfly?”

Strum’s tail twitched. “I want to be heard,” he said. His voice wobbled. “I feel hot and tight in my tummy.”

Olwyn blinked gently. “Thank you for telling us your feeling. That tight feeling is important. Let’s all take a slow breath together. In…and out.” The friends breathed in, then out. The room felt calmer.

Olwyn asked, “What does your feeling want to tell you, Strum?”

Strum looked at the shiny floor. “It says, ‘I want a turn.’ It says, ‘I want people to clap for me.’”

Pip raised a tiny paw. “I like clapping,” he squeaked. “We can clap for you.”

Benji nodded. “And we can share turns.”

Olwyn smiled. “That is civic responsibility. We think about others, and we help our group.”

Strum took a small breath. “I can try soft-soft. But can I have a solo later?”

“Yes,” said Olwyn. “We will make a plan. Playing together means planning together.”

They made a chart with big, friendly pictures: a drum, a shaker, a guitar, a bell, a triangle. Olwyn pointed with one feather. “We will go in order. Everyone gets a turn. We keep our room tidy, we listen, and we take care of each other.”

The music began again. Tap-tap. Shake-shake. Ting-ting. Strum strummed soft, like raindrops on leaves. The sound was warm and cozy. Lila smiled. “Like a lullaby,” she said.

But then, a squeal! The shaker slipped from Benji’s paws and rolled under the piano. It rattled like a little snake. Benji’s eyes grew shiny. “Oh no! I lost it!” His nose twitched fast.

Olwyn fluttered down near him. “What feeling is here now, Benji?”

Benji sniffled. “I feel sad. And worried. My chest feels heavy.”

Olwyn nodded. “Let’s name it. Sad and worried. When we name a feeling, it feels less lonely. Friends, how can we help?”

Pip crawled under the piano. “I can look!” he chirped. Lila held a bell to light the space with sparkly reflections. Strum kept the room calm with a soft, steady strum—hum-hum-hum, like a gentle hum to soothe a friend.

“Found it!” piped Pip. He pushed the blue shaker out with both tiny paws.

Everyone cheered. Clap, clap, clap! Benji smiled. “Thank you!” He hugged the shaker. “I feel better. My chest is lighter now.”

Olwyn tapped the triangle. Ting! “This is community. We keep our room safe. We help each other. We clean up, too.”

They picked up fallen sticks and straightened music pages. They placed instruments back on their shelves when they finished. The room felt peaceful and bright.

Then came Strum’s solo. The friends sat in a circle, listening with open ears and open hearts. Strum took one more slow breath. “I feel…excited,” he said. “And a little shy.”

“That’s okay,” said Lila. “We are your friends.”

Strum began to play. Plink-plink, strum-strum, hum-hum. The notes sounded like wind in trees and like sunshine on water. He played soft, then a little louder, then soft again. His tail relaxed. His eyes were bright. When he finished, the room was quiet, like a hug.

Then the friends clapped, not too loud, not too fast—just right. Clap, clap, clap.

Strum grinned. “Thank you for listening. My tummy feels calm now.”

Olwyn hooted gently. “You listened to your feelings, and you shared them. You respected our plan and our friends. That is emotional awareness and responsibility.”

Pip squeaked, “Feelings are like music! Loud or soft, fast or slow.”

Benji added, “We can say our feelings, not stomp or shout.”

Lila said, “We can make a plan and help our friends.”

Olwyn opened his wings. “Here is our song for today: Name your feeling. Breathe slowly. Make a plan. Share and care.”

They all sang together, voices sweet and small: “Name, breathe, plan, share and care.” The words floated like confetti in the sunny room.

As the day ended, the instruments rested. The friends waved goodbye. The practice room was tidy, the air warm with lemon and music. Olwyn looked around with soft eyes and hooted, “When we know our feelings, we make room for everyone’s song.”

The End.
