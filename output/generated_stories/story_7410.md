# The Little Song in the Seatbelt

Chirpy was a small, bright-yellow singing bird. Chirpy loved to sing in the family car. The car smelled like vanilla snacks and fresh apples. The seats were soft and warm. The windows showed trees whooshing by. Chirpy’s favorite place was the back seat, right by the window. Chirpy chirped, “Tweet-a-dee! Tweet-a-doo! Car rides, I sing for you!”

One sunny morning, Chirpy hopped onto the seat. Mama Driver buckled her seatbelt and smiled in the mirror. “Ready to go, Chirpy?”

“Ready!” Chirpy sang. “I will sing a traveling tune!”

But Chirpy felt the seatbelt on the seat. It looked like a long gray snake. “Hmm,” Chirpy wondered. “Do I need that? I want to hop and spin. I want to stand by the window and sing!”

Chirpy slid past the seatbelt and started to hop. “Tweet! Tweet! Look at the clouds!” Chirpy pressed close to the window.

Mama Driver said in a calm voice, “Chirpy, please sit down and use your seatbelt.”

Chirpy puffed tiny feathers. “But I can’t dance if I’m buckled. I want to twirl! I want to see everything!”

The car rolled forward. The sun made warm stripes on the seat. Chirpy hopped again. Suddenly, the car slowed at a red light. Chirpy felt a bump and slid a little. “Eep!” Chirpy peeped, heart thumping. The seat felt a bit scratchy under tiny feet. The bump felt scary.

Mama Driver’s voice was gentle but firm. “Chirpy, that little bump is a warning. Seatbelts keep bodies safe, even tiny bird bodies.”

Chirpy’s feelings felt tangled like string. “But I want to sing and move,” Chirpy said softly. Chirpy’s tummy felt wiggly. The bright joy felt dim.

A big brother voice came from the front. “Chirpy, can we make a plan?” It was Sam. His backpack smelled like crayons. “We can solve this together.”

Chirpy blinked. “A plan?”

Sam nodded. “First, we keep you safe. Second, we help you sing. Third, we make it fair for everyone. That is justice. Everyone gets safety and a turn.”

“Justice,” Chirpy repeated, rolling the word around like a round seed. Chirpy was curious. “How do we do that?”

Mama Driver signaled and pulled the car to a quiet stop. The engine hummed like a sleepy bear. She turned and smiled. “We can use our calm voices and take turns sharing ideas. That is conflict-resolution. We listen. We speak kindly. We find a solution together.”

Chirpy sat on the seat, tail feathers quivering. “I want to be safe. I also want to sing and move.”

Sam held up a soft scarf from his lap. It smelled like laundry soap and sunshine. “We can make a cozy bird spot. The seatbelt goes over the cozy. You can wiggle your toes and flap a teeny bit, but you stay buckled.”

Mama Driver nodded. “And we can open the little window a tiny crack. Fresh air can help you feel calm. That is self-care. We take care of our bodies and feelings.”

Chirpy’s eyes sparkled. “Can I still sing?”

“Yes,” said Sam. “We can make a song about safety! We all sing together.”

Chirpy felt hope rise like warm air. “Okay,” Chirpy said. “Let’s try.”

Sam helped gently. He folded the scarf into a soft nest shape. Mama Driver showed how the seatbelt clicked. “Click!” It sounded like a tiny door closing. The belt held Chirpy snug, like a friendly hug.

Chirpy took a small breath. The tiny window let in a cool breeze that smelled like pine trees. Chirpy wiggled toes and felt okay. “Tweet,” Chirpy sang, quiet at first. “Tweet-a-dee, safe with me.”

Sam clapped softly. “Good start! How about: Buckle up, we care. Buckle up, we share.”

Mama Driver added a verse. “Buckle up, we try. If we feel big feelings, we breathe and don’t cry.”

Chirpy chirped louder. The song filled the car like warm light. Chirpy felt safe and still had room to sing. The worry untangled inside.

Soon, the car turned and rolled into a parking spot at the park. Mama Driver turned off the engine. “We did it,” she said. “We solved the problem by listening and making a plan.”

Chirpy puffed up with pride. “I stayed buckled! I sang my song! I took care of my feelings.”

Sam smiled. “And we were fair. Everyone was safe. Everyone got a turn to give ideas.”

Chirpy hopped in the snug belt and whispered, “Thank you for helping me. When I’m curious, I can ask. When I feel wiggly, I can breathe. When we disagree, we can talk and make a plan.”

Mama Driver nodded. “That’s the lesson. Use calm words. Listen. Choose safety. Care for yourself. That is real bravery.”

Chirpy sang a final line, sweet and clear: “Click goes the belt, calm goes my heart. We solve together; that’s the smart part!”

The End.
