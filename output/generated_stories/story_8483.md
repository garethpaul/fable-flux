# Luna's Lullaby Night

Little Luna was a gentle deer fawn with soft brown spots and the biggest, kindest eyes in the whole forest. Tonight, she was staying at her grandmother's cozy bedroom for the very first time. The room smelled like warm cookies and lavender flowers, and moonlight danced through the pretty lace curtains.

But Luna felt wiggly and worried. Her heart was beating fast like a little drum. "Grandma Doe," she whispered, "I can't sleep. Everything feels different here. The bed is too soft, and I miss my own tree hollow."

Grandma Doe's gentle hooves clicked softly on the wooden floor as she walked over. Her silver fur sparkled in the moonlight. "Oh, my sweet little fawn," she said with a warm smile. "I understand completely. When I was your age, I felt the same way sleeping somewhere new."

Luna's ears perked up. "Really, Grandma? You felt scared too?"

"I did indeed," Grandma Doe nodded. "But my grandmother taught me something very special that always helped me feel calm and peaceful. Would you like me to share it with you?"

Luna's tail wiggled with curiosity. "Yes, please!"

Grandma Doe sat on the edge of the cozy bed, making the soft quilt rustle gently. "Close your eyes, little one, and listen carefully."

Luna squeezed her eyes shut tight. She could hear the wind whispering through the trees outside and the gentle tick-tock of the old wooden clock.

Then, Grandma Doe began to hum the most beautiful, gentle melody Luna had ever heard. It sounded like water babbling in a stream, like leaves dancing in a summer breeze, like the softest rain on flower petals.

"This is our family's special lullaby," Grandma Doe whispered. "My grandmother sang it to me, and her grandmother sang it to her. Now I'm sharing it with you."

As the sweet music filled the room, Luna felt her shoulders relax. Her breathing became slow and deep, like when she napped in sunny meadows. The melody wrapped around her like the warmest, softest blanket.

"The song teaches us to breathe slowly," Grandma Doe explained gently. "In and out, like the gentle waves on a quiet pond. When we feel worried or excited, we can remember this peaceful feeling."

Luna opened her eyes and smiled. "I feel so much better, Grandma. My heart isn't racing anymore. Can you teach me the words?"

"Of course, my dear." Grandma Doe's eyes twinkled. "But first, let's practice the calm breathing together."

They breathed in slowly through their noses, counting to three, then breathed out softly through their mouths. Luna felt like she was floating on a cloud.

"Now," said Grandma Doe, "whenever you feel worried or can't sleep, you can hum our special lullaby and remember this calm, peaceful feeling. The music will always be with you, just like my love."

Luna snuggled deep into the soft pillows. "Thank you for sharing our family's special song, Grandma. I feel so calm and safe now."

As Grandma Doe hummed the gentle lullaby one more time, Luna's eyelids grew heavy. The beautiful music filled her dreams with dancing butterflies and sparkling streams.

From that night on, whenever Luna felt worried or restless, she would remember her grandmother's special lullaby and the calm, peaceful feeling it brought to her heart.

The End.
