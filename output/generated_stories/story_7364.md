# Piko the Parrot’s Finish Line

Piko was a small, creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of blue on his tail. He lived on a sunny balcony garden. The garden smelled like mint and sweet tomatoes. Tiny bells chimed in the breeze. Bees hummed. Leaves made soft shhh sounds.

One morning, Piko flapped his wings and said, “Today I will paint a welcome sign for our balcony garden!” He wanted the sign to say, “Hello, Friends!” for birds, butterflies, and neighbors who waved from their balconies too. Piko loved big ideas. He loved colors. He loved to start.

He hopped to his tiny paint table. “Red, yellow, blue!” he sang. He dipped his brush. Splash! Red dots. Swish! Yellow swirls. “Oh! I also want to make a fruit snack,” Piko chirped. He flew to the little bowl of strawberries. “Mmm, sweet!” He pecked one, then two.

He looked back. The sign was half-colored. “I will finish soon,” Piko said. Then he saw a shiny watering can. “The mint is thirsty! I will water it now.” He poured a little. Drip, drip, drip. “The tomatoes too!” Drip, drip.

A butterfly named Lila landed on the railing. Her wings were orange and soft. “Hi, Piko,” Lila said. “What are you doing?”

“I am doing… everything!” Piko laughed. “Painting and snacking and watering and—oh! I forgot the blue paint!”

Lila tilted her head. “Do you feel busy?”

Piko puffed his feathers. “Yes. My tummy feels wiggly. My head feels floaty. I start, and then I fly away.”

From the next balcony, a kind neighbor named Mr. Omar waved. He wore a hat with a tiny world pin. “Good morning, gardeners!” he called. “I love how your garden helps the bees. We all share the same sky!”

Piko smiled. “I want to help. I want to finish my sign. But my thoughts hop like little frogs.”

Lila fluttered close. “Let’s try a mindful moment,” she said softly. “Breathe with me.”

Piko stood still. He put his claw on his chest. He breathed in slowly. In… one, two, three. He breathed out slowly. Out… one, two, three.

“What do you notice?” Lila asked.

“I hear bells,” Piko whispered. “I smell mint. My tummy is calm.”

“Good,” Lila said. “Now let’s make a plan. One thing at a time. First, then, next.”

Piko nodded. “First, finish the sign. Then, clean the brush. Next, water the mint. After, snack time!”

“Perfect,” Lila said. “I will be your helper. I will remind you kindly.”

Piko looked at his half-finished sign. He took a slow breath. “First, finish the sign,” he told himself. He dipped his brush in blue. Swish, swish. He added a bright sky. He dotted little hearts. He painted “Hello, Friends!” in big, friendly letters.

Mr. Omar clapped softly. “Wonderful, Piko! Your sign will make everyone feel welcome. That is global kindness. We are neighbors everywhere.”

Piko felt warm and proud. “Then, clean the brush,” he said. He swirled the brush in the water cup. Swirl, swirl. He wiped it dry.

“Next, water the mint,” Lila reminded.

Piko poured just enough. Drip, drip, drip. He touched a leaf. It felt cool and bouncy.

“After, snack time!” Piko cheered. He nibbled a strawberry. “Mmm. Sweet and juicy.”

The sun moved a little. A cloud made the balcony feel soft and shady. Piko hung the sign on the railing. The bells chimed. The sign swayed. “Hello, Friends!” it said to the whole wide world.

A tiny ant line marched by. Piko watched them carry crumbs. “They work together,” he said. “They finish their jobs.” He turned to Lila. “I finished my job too!”

Lila smiled. “How do you feel?”

“Calm and proud,” Piko said. “My heart is steady. My head is clear. First, then, next helped me finish.”

A pigeon landed nearby and cooed, “Coo-oo! Nice sign!”

Piko bowed. “Thank you! Welcome to our garden.” He pointed his wing. “The mint is here. The tomatoes are there. Please share kindly.”

Mr. Omar called again, “You showed understanding, Piko. You listened to your feelings. You made a plan. You finished your task. That helps everyone.”

Piko puffed happily. “When I finish my tasks, I help my garden, my friends, and my neighbors too,” he said. He looked at the sky. “We all share this big world. I can do my part.”

Lila fluttered circles. “Remember our steps: stop, breathe, plan, finish.”

Piko repeated, “Stop. Breathe. Plan. Finish.” He tapped his beak. “I can say it when my thoughts hop like frogs.”

As the day ended, the balcony glowed golden. The air smelled like mint and sunshine. Piko sat by his sign. He felt peaceful. He felt ready for tomorrow.

“Tomorrow,” Piko said, “I will plant new seeds. First, then, next!”

Lila laughed. “I will be here to help.”

Piko looked at the welcome sign and whispered, “Hello, Friends.” He tucked his head into his soft feathers. The bells chimed one more time. The garden hummed with happy sounds.

The End.
