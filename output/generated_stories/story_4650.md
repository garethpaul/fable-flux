# Squeaky’s Warm Kitchen Welcome

The warm kitchen smelled like cinnamon toast and sweet apples. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. Pots clinked softly, and the kettle hummed a happy song. Squeaky, a resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail, stood on a stool by the counter.

“I’m making friendship muffins!” Squeaky chirped. “I want my new neighbors to feel welcome.”

Squeaky read the big recipe card. “Flour, milk, eggs, apples, and a pinch of spice.” He tapped the spoon on the bowl—tink, tink, tink. He whisked—swish, swish, swish.

There came a gentle knock. Three friends peeked in the door. A hedgehog named Hana rolled her basket inside. A rabbit named Rafi hopped in with long ears twitching. A little bird named Luli fluttered to the window ledge.

“Hello!” Squeaky squeaked. “Come in, come in! It’s warm and cozy. We’ll bake together.”

Hana smiled. “Bok,” she said softly.

Rafi wiggled his nose. “Salam,” he said kindly.

Luli chirped, “Hola,” in a sing-song voice.

Squeaky’s whisk paused. He blinked. “Um… hello?” He felt a little twist in his tummy. The words sounded different. He didn’t understand.

Hana pointed to the bowl and said, “Bok,” again, patting her heart. Rafi held up a jar of sweet dates. “Salam,” he repeated, smiling wide. Luli landed on the recipe card and chirped, “Hola,” tapping one tiny foot on each word.

Squeaky’s ears drooped. “I want to be a good friend, but I don’t know those words,” he whispered. The kitchen felt a little too quiet now, even with the kettle humming.

Rafi noticed Squeaky’s worried face. He gently set the dates on the table and put a paw on his chest. “Friend,” he said, slowly. He pointed to Squeaky. “Friend.”

Hana nodded and touched her chest. “Friend,” she echoed. “Bok means hello where I’m from.”

Luli fluffed her feathers. “Hola means hello,” she sang. “I can teach you!”

Squeaky took a deep breath. The cinnamon smell made him feel brave. “I can learn,” he said. “Let’s share words. Let’s share muffins!”

They all smiled. The warm kitchen felt even warmer.

“First,” Squeaky said, “we need to stir.” He handed the spoon to Hana.

Hana held the spoon carefully. “Miješaj,” she said gently, stirring in a slow circle. “That means stir.”

“Mih-shy,” Squeaky tried. “Miješaj.” He giggled. “It tickles my tongue!”

Rafi cracked an egg—crick! “Beid,” he said. “That’s egg in my language.”

“Bade,” Squeaky repeated. “Beid.” He nodded proudly.

Luli sprinkled cinnamon—sprinkle, sprinkle. The sweet spice made tiny clouds. “Canela,” she chirped. “That’s cinnamon!”

“Canela,” Squeaky said, taking a deep happy breath. “Canela smells like hugs.”

They all laughed. The batter looked smooth and creamy now. Squeaky greased the muffin pan. “We’ll make four muffins. One for each friend.”

Hana carefully poured the batter. Rafi placed a date on top of each one—plop, plop, plop, plop. Luli added a tiny apple slice—snip, snip.

Squeaky slid the pan into the oven. The door closed with a soft thump. “Now we wait,” he said. He set the timer—tick, tick, tick.

Waiting felt long. Squeaky’s tail twitched. “What if I forget your words?” he asked. “What if I say them wrong?”

Hana rolled a small napkin and patted his paw. “It’s okay to try,” she said. “Trying is kind.”

Rafi nodded. “We can help each other. We listen.”

Luli sang a little tune. “Hola, hello, bok, salam,” she chirped. “Many ways to say one thing: friend.”

Squeaky smiled. “Friend,” he said, and his voice felt brave now.

Ding! The muffins were ready. Squeaky opened the oven. A warm, sweet wave of canela and apples filled the kitchen. The muffins were golden and puffy. Steam danced like tiny clouds.

They sat at the table. Squeaky placed a muffin in front of each friend. “For you, Hana,” he said. “Hvala,” Hana replied. “That means thank you.”

“For you, Rafi,” said Squeaky. “Shukran,” Rafi said with a smile.

“For you, Luli,” Squeaky chirped. “Gracias,” Luli trilled.

Squeaky blinked. “So many beautiful words,” he said. “They feel like colorful ribbons.”

They each took a small bite—mmmmm. The muffins were soft and sweet, with little chewy bits of date and the warm hug of canela. The kitchen felt bright and safe. The kettle sighed a happy sigh.

Squeaky wiggled in his chair. “I learned hello in three ways,” he said proudly. “Hola, bok, salam. And thank you: gracias, hvala, shukran.” He touched his heart. “Friend, friend, friend.”

Hana squeezed his paw. “We learned your words too,” she said. “Hello and thank you.”

Rafi looked around the warm kitchen. “Different words. One table.”

Luli fluttered her wings. “Different voices. One song.”

Squeaky nodded. “Different friends. One heart.” He grinned. “When we listen, we understand. When we share, we belong.”

They clinked their muffin wrappers together—crinkle, crinkle. “To friendship!” they all said, in all their beautiful words.

The End.
