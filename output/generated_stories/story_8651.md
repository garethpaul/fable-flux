# Pippa Penguin and the Calm-Down Button

Pippa was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a bouncy waddle. She loved to visit Grandpa Gus’s basement lab. The lab was cool and cozy, with soft humming machines, twinkly lights, and the gentle smell of warm cocoa. There were boxes of buttons, bins of gears, and a big clock that went tick-tock, tick-tock.

“Good morning, Grandpa Gus!” Pippa chirped, flapping her little wings.

“Good morning, Pippa,” Grandpa Gus said with a smile. “Today we will use calm minds and curious hearts.”

Pippa clapped. “I like calm minds! I like curious hearts too!”

Grandpa Gus pointed to a small, silver robot on the table. It had a round head and a friendly face. “This is Bibi. Bibi tells stories. But today, Bibi is mixed-up. Bibi talks too fast and forgets the middle and the end.”

As if to show this, Bibi whirred and said, “Once upon a zoom! The end!” Then Bibi beeped and blinked.

Pippa giggled. “That was a very short story.”

Grandpa Gus chuckled. “We can help Bibi. We will use mindfulness and logical thinking.”

Pippa tilted her head. “Mind-ful-ness?”

“Mindfulness means we notice what we feel, what we hear, what we see, and what we smell. We slow down. We breathe. We pay attention,” said Grandpa Gus.

Pippa took a slow breath in. She felt the cool air in her beak. She smelled cocoa. She heard the tick-tock. She saw Bibi’s little lights blink blue, then yellow. “I can do that,” she said softly.

Grandpa Gus nodded. “First, we make a plan. Step one: be calm. Step two: listen to Bibi. Step three: try one button at a time and tell the story of what we do.”

Pippa repeated, “Be calm. Listen. Try one button. Tell the story.”

She waddled closer to Bibi. “Hello, Bibi. I am Pippa. We will help you.”

Bibi blinked. “Hello, Pippa. Once upon a—zip!” Bibi buzzed and stopped.

Pippa put a flipper on her belly. “I feel wiggly,” she said. “I will breathe.” She took a slow breath in and a slow breath out. “Now I feel steady.”

She looked at the buttons on Bibi’s side. There was a red button, a blue button, and a green button. Each had a tiny picture: a start flag, a pause hand, and a turtle.

Pippa whispered, “I will tell the story of what I do. First, I see the red button with a start flag. I think it means start. I will press it.”

Click. Bibi lit up. “Once upon a—zoom zoom—done!” Bibi rushed and finished too fast.

Pippa frowned a little. “That didn’t work. I feel a little disappointed,” she said. “I can breathe.” She breathed in and out. She felt her shoulders relax. “Now I can try again.”

Grandpa Gus smiled. “What is your next step?”

“I will listen,” Pippa said. She leaned close. Bibi’s hum sounded shaky, like a quick drum: brrr-brrr-brrr.

She looked at the blue button with the pause hand. “The hand looks like ‘wait.’ I will try the blue button.” Click.

Bibi’s hum slowed. “Once upon a time, there was a…” Bibi paused, then stuttered, “A… A…”

Pippa nodded. “Closer! That was slower.”

She looked at the green button with a turtle. “A turtle is slow and steady. I like turtles.” Pippa breathed again. In, out. “I feel calm and ready. I will press the green button.” Click.

Bibi’s lights glowed soft and warm. Bibi said, “Once upon a time, there was a cheerful penguin named Pippa. She listened to the tick-tock and the hum-hum. She took a breath. She told her story slowly.”

Pippa’s eyes sparkled. “It works! Bibi can tell a slow story.”

Grandpa Gus clapped. “You used your plan. You noticed your feelings. You breathed. You tried one button at a time. That is logical thinking with a mindful heart.”

Bibi continued in a calm voice, “Pippa and Grandpa Gus had cocoa that smelled sweet. The mug felt warm. The lab lights twinkled like tiny stars. They took one breath. Then they took one step. Then they told one more part of the story.”

Pippa smiled and added, “And they listened to each sound. Tick-tock. Hum-hum. Sip-sip.”

“Tick-tock. Hum-hum. Sip-sip,” Bibi echoed, happy and steady.

Pippa looked at the big clock. “It is time to clean up.” She felt a little sad the fun was ending. She put a flipper on her belly again. “Hello, sad,” she whispered. “You can sit with me.” She took a gentle breath. The sad felt softer.

She told herself a tiny story. “First we put the buttons in the box. Next we wipe the table. Last we say goodbye.” She and Grandpa Gus worked together, step by step. The soft cloth made a swish-swish sound. The buttons clinked, clink, clink, as they went back in their box.

“All done,” said Pippa. “I feel proud.”

Grandpa Gus patted her wing. “You were mindful and logical, and you told a clear story.”

Bibi chimed, “Mindful Pippa helps stories grow.”

Pippa waddled to the stairs. She looked back and waved. “Thank you, Bibi. Thank you, Grandpa. Today I learned a calm-down button is inside me. It is my breath. When I slow down, I can think in steps. Then my stories make sense.”

Grandpa Gus winked. “That is the lesson.”

Pippa took one last slow breath. The lab hummed. The clock tick-tocked. Her heart felt warm and light.

And off she went, ready for tomorrow’s little adventure.

The End.
