# Tiko the Wise Tortoise and the Balcony Garden

Tiko was a wise tortoise who lived in a cozy basket on a sunny balcony garden. The balcony smelled like mint and sweet tomatoes. Soft wind tickled the leaves. Bees hummed, “Bzz-bzz,” and tiny bells on a wind chime sang, “Ting-ting.”

Every morning, Tiko peeked over the basket and greeted the plants. “Good morning, basil. Good morning, flowers,” Tiko said in a warm, slow voice.

One day, Tiko heard giggles and fast footsteps. Two little neighbors, Mina and Jamal, ran onto the balcony with their toy trucks.

“Vroom! Vroom!” Jamal zoomed his truck right between the tomato pots.

“I’m making a leaf soup!” Mina shouted, snapping a basil leaf and dropping it into a cup.

Tiko blinked and felt a little worried. The pots wobbled. The soil shook. A tiny snail on a lettuce leaf pulled into its shell. The wind chime clinked nervously.

Tiko took a deep breath. He remembered: speak calm, speak kind.

“Hello, friends,” Tiko said. “This is our balcony garden. We share it. We care for it. Please walk slowly.”

“But we want to play!” Jamal said, his eyes bright.

“And I want more leaves for my soup!” Mina said, reaching for another basil leaf.

Tiko shook his head gently. “Leaves are living. Plants are growing. We show respect for others, even plants. We ask before we pick. We think of safety. See how the pots wobble? They can fall.”

Just then, a big gust of wind whooshed by. “Whooooosh!” A tomato vine leaned. A pot tipped. Dirt sprinkled like cocoa powder. The snail slid closer to the edge.

Mina froze. Jamal stopped his truck. Their eyes grew wide.

“I don’t want the snail to fall,” Mina whispered.

Tiko nodded. “Me too. Let’s listen and work together.”

They all went still and quiet. They listened to the wind. They listened to the soft creak of the railing. They listened to the tiny drip-drip from the watering can.

“I hear the wind,” Jamal said softly.

“I hear the chimes,” Mina added.

“And I hear your careful hearts,” said Tiko. “Thank you for listening.”

Mina looked at her cup. “I didn’t ask the basil. I didn’t ask you. I wasn’t being respectful.”

Jamal looked at the trucks. “My trucks were too fast. I didn’t think about the pots.”

Tiko smiled kindly. “We can fix this. This is a problem we can solve.”

“How?” asked Jamal.

“First,” Tiko said, “we make a safe play path. Slow feet. Quiet wheels. The path goes around the pots, not between them.”

They used smooth pebbles to mark a little curve by the wall. Jamal rolled his truck along the pebble road. “Vroom… but soft vroom,” he said with a grin.

“Second,” Tiko went on, “we make a picking plan. We ask. We take just a little. We thank the plant.”

Mina stood beside the basil. She placed her hand on her chest. “Basil, may I please take one leaf?” she asked in a gentle voice.

Tiko nodded. “That was respectful. Take one small leaf from the top.”

Mina pinched a tiny leaf. Sniff-sniff. “Mmm, it smells like pizza,” she giggled. “Thank you, Basil.”

“Third,” Tiko said, “we help our snail friend.” He pointed with his nose. “We make a safe corner.”

Mina and Jamal placed a shiny shell and a soft leaf in a corner tray. Tiko slowly nudged the snail onto the leaf. “Safe travels, little one,” he said. The snail peeped out and touched Tiko’s nose with its feelers. It felt like a tiny tickle.

The wind blew again, but this time the pots stood steady. The pebble path kept the trucks away. The snail rested in its cozy corner. The wind chime sang a happy, “Ting-ting-ting.”

Mina smiled. “I feel proud. I listened.”

Jamal smiled, too. “I feel safe. I was careful.”

Tiko’s eyes twinkled. “And I feel thankful. You showed respect for others—the plants, the snail, and me.”

They made a new game. They called it the Garden Helper Game.

- Step one: Tiptoe on the pebble path.
- Step two: Ask before you pick.
- Step three: Say thank you and move slowly.

“Ready?” Tiko asked.

“Ready!” Mina and Jamal chimed.

They played and laughed. Jamal whispered, “Soft vroom.” Mina stirred her pretend soup with one little basil leaf and lots of imagination. Tiko hummed along with the bees.

When the sun began to set, the balcony turned golden. The tomatoes looked like tiny lights. The mint leaves smelled cool and sweet. The wind grew soft, like a blanket.

Mina patted Tiko’s shell. “Thank you for teaching us.”

Jamal waved. “We will remember: respect, listen, and be safe.”

Tiko nodded, slow and wise. “When we respect others, we make room for everyone to grow.”

The balcony garden felt calm and happy again. The plants stood tall. The snail tucked in. The wind chime whispered a gentle good night.

The End.
