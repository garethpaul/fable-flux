# Bubbles the Brave in the Laundry Room

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with a bright smile and shiny, silver-blue skin. One rainy day, Bubbles visited a house with a big, warm, humming laundry room. The air smelled like clean soap. The washing machine went whirr-whirr. The dryer spun with a soft thump-thump. Socks hung like little flags on a line.

“Wow,” Bubbles giggled, flipping his tail. “This room sings!”

Bubbles loved to play, but today he had a goal. He wanted to help wash a big, muddy beach towel. It was heavy and sandy and smelled like seaweed. Helping was important to Bubbles. He wanted to be brave, and he wanted to do the right thing.

Bubbles put the towel in a neat pile. He took a deep breath and said, “I can do hard things. I can be brave.” His heart went pitter-pat, but he felt strong inside.

He began to plan. “Step one,” Bubbles said softly. “Ask for help.” He swam a little circle and called, “Mr. Washer? Ms. Dryer? Can you help me, please?”

The washing machine blinked a little light. Click. “Hello,” it seemed to say.

“Step two,” Bubbles said. “Get the things we need.” He picked up a small scoop of soap. It smelled like lemons and sunshine. He set the scoop by the washer.

“Step three,” Bubbles whispered. “Do the job.” He flapped his fins and nudged the door open. The metal felt cool on his nose. The drum inside was shiny like a silver cave.

Suddenly, he saw a sign that said, “Ask an adult before you use the machines.” Bubbles paused. His eyes grew wide. He wanted to be honest. He wanted to show integrity.

“I will tell the truth,” Bubbles said. “I need help from a grown-up.”

Just then, Mia, a kind, gentle adult with warm hands, walked in with a basket. “Hi, Bubbles,” she said. “You look thoughtful.”

“I have a goal,” Bubbles said. “I want to wash this muddy towel. I made a plan. But the sign says to ask an adult. Can you help me, please?”

Mia smiled. “That is brave, Bubbles. You asked for help. You are careful and honest. Let’s do it together.”

Bubbles beamed. “Thank you!”

They worked as a team. The towel made a soft plop as it went into the washer. Bubbles poured the lemony soap. The room smelled bright and clean. Mia closed the door with a click and pressed the gentle cycle. Water splashed—swish, swish, swish. The washer hummed a steady song.

While they waited, Bubbles felt calm and proud. But then—they heard a tiny cry. “Eee! Eee!” A small sock was stuck under the washer, peeking out like a shy mouse.

Bubbles’ fins tingled. “I’m a little scared,” he said. “It’s dark under there.”

Mia knelt down and shined a small flashlight. “We can be brave together,” she said. “Let’s make a plan.”

Bubbles nodded. “Step one: Look. Step two: Reach. Step three: Pull gently.”

He took a breath. He felt the cool floor under his belly. He listened to the washer’s song. Swish, swish. He slid his fin into the shadow. It was dusty and a little tickly. He wanted to pull back, but he whispered, “Brave means doing the right thing, even when I feel a little scared.”

Slowly, slowly, Bubbles felt the sock. He tugged. The sock wiggled free with a soft pop!

“You did it!” Mia cheered. “That was brave.”

Bubbles laughed, his voice like tiny bubbles. “We did it. We followed our plan.”

They shook the dust off the sock and added it to the laundry. When the washer stopped, they moved the towel to the dryer. Warm air whooshed. The dryer tumbled—thump-thump—like a friendly drum. The room grew cozy and toasty.

Soon, the towel came out soft, warm, and clean. Bubbles nuzzled it. “Mmm, it smells like sunshine.”

Mia folded the towel neatly. “Bubbles,” she said, “you set a goal, you made a plan, and you told the truth. You were brave in a safe way.”

Bubbles felt his heart glow. “I learned something,” he said. “Bravery is not just big. Bravery is doing the right thing, even when I feel nervous. Planning helps me. Telling the truth helps me. Asking for help keeps me safe.”

He looked around the laundry room. The machines were quiet. The air was warm. The socks hung like happy flags again. Bubbles swished his tail and said, “Thank you, laundry room. Thank you, Mia.”

Mia smiled. “Thank you, Bubbles. You made this room shine a little brighter today.”

Bubbles giggled. “I will remember: Be brave. Make a plan. Tell the truth. Reach goals, one small step at a time.”

He gave a playful flip and a splashy laugh, and the lemon-fresh air seemed to sparkle.

The End.
