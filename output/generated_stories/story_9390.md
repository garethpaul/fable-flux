# Rosie Rabbit Gets Ready

Rosie Rabbit lived in the coziest nursery you could imagine. Soft blue walls surrounded her little bed, and colorful toys filled every corner. The morning sun streamed through the polka-dot curtains, making everything warm and bright.

"Time to get ready for playgroup!" called Mama Rabbit from downstairs.

Rosie bounced out of bed, her fluffy white tail wiggling with excitement. She loved playgroup! But as she looked around her room, her smile faded. Her favorite red backpack was nowhere to be seen.

"Oh no!" Rosie said, hopping around frantically. "Where is my backpack?"

She looked under her soft, squishy pillow. Not there. She peeked behind her wooden toy chest that smelled like fresh cedar. Still nothing.

"Rosie, we need to leave soon!" Mama called again.

Rosie's ears drooped. Yesterday, she had thrown her backpack somewhere after playgroup instead of putting it in its special spot by her dresser. Now she couldn't find it anywhere!

She searched through her pile of stuffed animals - they felt so soft and cuddly, but no backpack. She looked in her closet among the clothes that smelled like lavender soap. Still no luck.

"I can't go without my backpack!" Rosie said, her voice getting louder. "It has my snacks and my favorite crayon!"

Mama Rabbit appeared in the doorway, her whiskers twitching with concern. "What's wrong, little bunny?"

"I can't find my backpack anywhere!" Rosie said, stomping her little foot. "I looked and looked!"

Mama sat down on the soft carpet next to Rosie. "When did you last see it?"

Rosie thought hard, her nose wiggling. "Yesterday... after playgroup... I think I just dropped it somewhere."

"Ah," said Mama gently. "Remember what we talked about? Having a special place for important things?"

Rosie's ears perked up a little. "You mean... preparing for tomorrow before I play?"

"Exactly!" Mama smiled. "Let's look together."

They searched the nursery carefully. Finally, Mama spotted something red peeking out from under the rocking chair - the chair with the soft, cushioned seat where Mama read bedtime stories.

"There it is!" Rosie exclaimed, diving to grab her backpack.

But then Rosie stopped. She looked at Mama with big, sorry eyes. "Mama, I'm sorry I didn't put my backpack away properly. And I'm sorry I got upset and stomped my foot."

Mama gave Rosie a warm, fuzzy hug that smelled like vanilla and love. "Thank you for saying sorry, sweetheart. That was very brave of you."

Rosie took a deep breath. "Mama, I want to be better at getting ready. Can you help me make a plan?"

"Of course!" Mama said proudly. "What do you think we should do?"

Rosie stood up tall, her ears straight and confident. "Every day after playgroup, I'll put my backpack in its special spot. And before bed, I'll check that everything is ready for tomorrow!"

"That's a wonderful plan!" Mama clapped her paws together.

From that day on, Rosie always prepared for the next day before playtime. She put her backpack in its special spot, laid out her clothes, and made sure her toys were tidy. Her mornings became calm and happy, and she never felt worried about being late again.

Rosie learned that being prepared made everything easier and more fun!

The End.
