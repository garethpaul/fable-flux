# Pippin Penguin Speaks Up

Pippin the penguin loved his cozy bedroom. The soft blue walls reminded him of the ocean, and his fluffy white pillow felt like a cloud. Every night, Mama Penguin would tuck him in with his favorite fish-shaped blanket.

But tonight was different. Pippin felt frustrated and wiggly under his covers.

"Mama," called Pippin softly. "I can't sleep."

Mama Penguin waddled over with her gentle smile. "What's wrong, little one?"

Pippin pulled the blanket up to his beak. He felt shy about telling Mama what was bothering him. His flippers felt warm and tingly with worry.

"I... I don't know," he whispered, even though he did know.

Mama sat on the edge of his bed. The mattress made a soft whoosh sound as she settled down. "Sometimes when we feel upset, it helps to use our words. Can you try telling me what's in your heart?"

Pippin took a deep breath. The cool air felt good in his lungs. He remembered what Mama taught him about using his voice when something was wrong.

"The blanket is too scratchy," Pippin said quietly. "It makes my feathers feel itchy, and I keep wiggling around."

"Oh my!" said Mama, her eyes sparkling with understanding. "Thank you for telling me, Pippin. I'm so proud of you for using your words."

Pippin felt a warm, happy feeling spread through his chest. "Really?"

"Really," Mama nodded. "When something bothers us, it's important to speak up. That's called self-advocacy - using our voice to help ourselves feel better."

Mama gently removed the scratchy blanket. The soft evening air felt cool and comfortable against Pippin's feathers.

"Now, let's find a solution together," Mama said. She opened Pippin's dresser drawer and pulled out his super-soft yellow blanket. It smelled like lavender and felt like silk.

"This one!" Pippin chirped happily. "It's so smooth and cozy!"

As Mama tucked the new blanket around him, Pippin snuggled down into his pillow. The soft fabric felt perfect against his feathers.

"Mama, I'm glad I told you about the scratchy blanket," Pippin said with a sleepy yawn.

"Me too, sweetie. Sometimes we feel frustrated when something isn't right, but when we use calm words to explain our feelings, we can fix the problem together."

Pippin's eyes grew heavy as he listened to the gentle sounds of his room - the soft tick of his clock and the whisper of wind outside his window.

"Will you remember to use your voice next time something bothers you?" Mama asked softly.

"Yes," Pippin nodded, his voice getting drowsy. "I'll take a deep breath and use my words to ask for help."

Mama gave him a gentle kiss on his fuzzy head. "That's my brave little penguin. Sweet dreams."

As Mama turned off the light, Pippin felt proud and peaceful. His body felt relaxed under the perfect blanket, and his heart felt warm knowing that Mama listened when he spoke up for himself.

"Speaking up feels good," he whispered to himself as he drifted off to sleep, dreaming of swimming in calm, blue waters.

The End.
