# Roo's Sunny Backyard Dance

The sun was warm and bright in the backyard. A joyful kangaroo joey named Roo bounced onto the soft grass. The grass tickled Roo’s toes. A sweet smell of flowers floated in the air. Birds chirped a happy beat.

“I feel wiggly!” Roo laughed. “I want to dance!”

Roo’s mama stood by the garden, watering the mint and little yellow flowers. “Good morning, my bouncy star,” Mama said. “I hear your feet ready to tap.”

Roo nodded and hopped in a circle. Tap, hop, swish! The leaves on the lemon tree rustled like tiny drums. Roo’s tail made a soft thump on the ground. “Listen, Mama! The backyard is making music.”

Mama smiled. “Our family has an old dance we share at celebrations,” she said. “It’s called the Sun-Step. Would you like to learn it?”

Roo’s eyes grew big and bright. “Yes, please! I want to dance the Sun-Step!”

Mama put down the watering can. She clapped a gentle rhythm. Clap-clap… pause… clap. “The Sun-Step starts slow,” Mama explained. “We breathe in, and we breathe out. Then we move our feet in a circle to say hello to the sun.”

Roo took a big breath in. The air smelled like mint. Roo let the breath out slowly. “Ahhh,” Roo sighed, feeling calm and steady. Roo’s legs still wanted to wiggle fast, but Roo held still for one more breath.

“Good self-control,” Mama said softly. “That helps our bodies listen.”

Roo smiled. “I can listen to my body and to you.”

They began. Clap-clap… pause… clap. Mama showed one step, then another. Step-heel, sway-sway, little hop. Roo copied. Step-heel, sway—oops! Roo zoomed too fast and tumbled onto the grass.

“I went zoom,” Roo giggled, cheeks warm and pink.

“That’s okay,” Mama said, patting Roo’s shoulder. “Let’s name our speed. Would you like to try Turtle Pace or Joey Pace?”

Roo pressed a paw to Roo’s chest. “I feel like a joey rocket. But I can choose Turtle Pace first.” Roo made the slowest little steps. Step-heel, sway-sway, little hop. The ground felt steady. Roo’s tail balanced like a soft anchor.

“Beautiful,” Mama said. “Now you know how to slow down. Later we can go faster.”

The gate clicked. Roo’s friend, Wally Wombat, waddled in with a wooden drum. “Hi, Roo! Hi, Mama! I heard dancing,” Wally said. His whiskers twitched with excitement. “Can I play my drum?”

Roo bounced once, then remembered Turtle Pace. “Yes! But Mama is teaching me the Sun-Step. It is our family dance.”

Wally’s eyes were round. “Wow. Can I learn too?”

Mama nodded. “We can share our traditions. When we share, we take turns. First we dance the Sun-Step. Then you can show your drum beat.”

“That’s fair,” Wally said. “I can wait.”

They made a plan together. Roo pointed to a sunny spot. “We will dance here.” Wally pointed to the shade. “I will sit and watch. I will keep my drum quiet for now.”

Roo breathed in, breathed out. Clap-clap… pause… clap. Step-heel, sway-sway, little hop. Roo’s ears heard Wally’s quiet humming. Roo’s nose smelled lemon and mint. Roo’s feet felt the warm grass. Roo’s heart felt proud.

Mama sang a soft line, “Hello sun, hello day.” Roo added, “Hello friends, let’s dance and play.”

“Now my turn?” Wally asked, drumming the air with his paws.

Roo looked at Mama. Mama nodded. “Let’s use our words to make a fair trade,” she said.

Roo turned to Wally. “We danced the Sun-Step. Now you can teach us your drum beat. Can it be soft first?”

“Yes,” Wally agreed. “Soft like a butterfly.” Wally tapped the drum. Tip-tap… tip-tap-tap. The sound was like tiny raindrops.

Roo’s toes tingled. Roo wanted to jump fast. Roo pressed paws together and took one slow breath. “I can wait,” Roo whispered. “Body, we can dance after the soft beat.”

Wally smiled. “Now a little louder.” Tap-tap… TAP. “This is our picnic beat,” he said. “My family plays it when we share food.”

Mama’s eyes shone. “Thank you for sharing your tradition, Wally.”

Roo clapped. “Let’s try a dance that mixes both! We can do the Sun-Step with the picnic beat.”

Wally scratched his chin. “We need a plan. You like to hop high. I like to drum slow. How do we make it work?”

Roo thought. “We can take turns. First slow Sun-Step with quiet drum. Then one round of fast hops with louder drum. Then we breathe.”

“That is good negotiating,” Mama said. “You both shared ideas and made a plan.”

They tried it. Slow: clap-clap… pause… clap. Tip-tap… tip-tap-tap. Step-heel, sway-sway, little hop. Roo felt calm and happy. Fast: TAP-TAP… TAP! Roo hopped high—boing, boing, boing! The leaves shivered and the birds chirped along. Then both friends froze like statues and took a long breath together.

Roo laughed. “My wiggles feel friendly now.”

Wally grinned. “My drum feels friendly too.”

Mama bowed. “What a beautiful dance of sharing.”

The sun slid a little lower, making long, golden lines on the grass. Roo wrapped arms around Wally in a gentle hug. “Thank you for waiting, and for drumming.”

“Thank you for teaching me,” Wally said. “I will teach my family the Sun-Step.”

Roo nodded. “And I will show mine the picnic beat.”

Mama gave each of them a cool mint leaf to smell. “Remember today,” she said. “Dance can help us share, calm our bodies, and make plans together.”

Roo pressed the leaf to Roo’s nose. Fresh and bright. “Dance helps me listen, breathe, and be kind,” Roo said.

“And dance is fun,” Wally added, tapping a tiny final tip-tap.

They all giggled. In the sunny backyard, the friends knew a big truth: when we dance, we can share our traditions, use our words, and choose our pace.

The End.
