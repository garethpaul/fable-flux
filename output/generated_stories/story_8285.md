# Dippy Plans the Basement Bash

Drip, drop, plink! The busy basement hummed with sounds. Boxes rustled. A fan whirred. A washer swished. In the middle of it all, a small blue kiddie pool sparkled under a bright lamp.

“Wheee!” squeaked Dippy, a playful dolphin who loved to splash in his pool. His smooth skin felt cool under the light. He flapped his tail and made tiny waves. Little water drops tickled his nose.

Today was special. Dippy wanted to host a Basement Bash for his friends. There were toys, pillows, and a tunnel made from big cardboard boxes. There was a basket of snacks—apple slices, crackers, and tiny fish-shaped cookies.

Dippy wiggled with excitement. “Let’s go! Let’s start now!” he chirped.

Just then, his friends came down the stairs.

Tina the turtle clunked along in her shiny shell. “Hi, Dippy!” she said softly.

Buzz the bee zipped in circles. “Bzz! Games! Snacks! Bzz!”

Milo the mouse peeked out from behind a stack of books. “Are there cozy spots? I like cozy.”

Dippy’s tail splashed. “Yes! Let’s play everything at once!”

The basement felt even busier now. The washer hummed. The fan went spin-spin. Buzz buzzed. Milo squeaked. Tina clunked. Everyone talked at the same time.

“I want the tunnel!” Buzz zipped toward the cardboard tunnel.

“I want the tunnel too,” said Milo, trying to squeeze in.

Tina looked at the blocks. “Can we build a tower? Slow and steady.”

Dippy’s heart thumped fast. He wanted to make all his friends happy. He tried to do everything at the same time. He pushed blocks, splashed water, and peeked into the tunnel.

But the blocks fell. Splash sploosh! Water sloshed onto the floor. Buzz and Milo bumped heads. “Ow!” they both said.

Dippy felt hot in his cheeks. His tail flicked hard. “Stop! Stop! Everyone stop!” he shouted. His voice echoed off the boxes.

He felt a big angry bubble inside. It grew and pushed at his chest. He wanted to pop it with a loud splash. He wanted to yell again.

Tina blinked. “Dippy, are you okay?”

Dippy closed his eyes. He remembered something Grandma Dolphin once told him. “When your angry bubble grows, pause. Breathe in, breathe out. Then plan.”

Dippy took a slow breath in. He held it. He let it out like a soft whistle. He did it again. In. Out. The angry bubble got smaller. His body felt cooler.

“I was mad,” Dippy said. “The basement is busy. I got mixed up. We need a plan.”

Buzz hovered. “A plan? Bzz?”

Milo’s whiskers twitched. “A plan sounds safe.”

Tina smiled. “A plan helps us take turns.”

Dippy nodded. He wiggled to the whiteboard on the wall. It was dusty and squeaky. He wiped it with a cloth. “We will make a list. First, we plan. Then, we play.”

He drew simple shapes. A square for the tunnel. A circle for the pool. A triangle for the blocks. He made a clock with a big hand and a little hand. He used a bright red marker.

“Listen,” Dippy said. He used his brave voice. It was kind and strong. “We will have three turns. Each turn is two songs long. I will play music. First: Tunnel time. Next: Block time. Last: Splash time. Everyone gets a turn in each spot.”

Buzz buzzed lower. “Two songs? I can wait for two songs.”

Milo nodded. “Two songs is cozy.”

Tina tapped her shell. “I like this plan.”

Dippy added one more rule. “If you want the same toy, say ‘My turn, please,’ and we will wait. No pushing. If we feel mad, we breathe.”

Buzz zipped up. “What if someone cuts in line? Bzz!”

Dippy stood tall on his tail. “I will say, ‘Please wait. It’s Milo’s turn now.’ That is assertive. That means I use my strong, kind words.”

He pressed a button on a small radio. A cheerful song played. The beat went clap-clap, tap-tap. Everyone moved to the tunnel.

“First is tunnel time,” Dippy said.

Buzz zoomed through. “Bzz! My turn!” He finished, then zipped to the back of the line. “Your turn, Milo!”

Milo scampered through, giggling. “Swoosh! My turn!”

Tina went slow. “I’m going steady.” Dippy cheered, “You can do it!”

Dippy felt brave when the line got wiggly. When Buzz tried to slip in, Dippy smiled and said, “Please wait. It’s Tina’s turn now.” Buzz hovered back. “Okay!”

The song ended. Dippy clicked to the next one. “Block time!” They built a big, big tower. Tap, tap, stack. It wobbled. They held their breath. “We need a plan,” whispered Dippy. “Big blocks on the bottom. Small blocks on top.” The tower stood tall. Everyone clapped.

Last, the splash time. The light made the water shine like tiny stars. Dippy let each friend dip a toe or a paw. “My turn, please,” Milo said. “Yes,” Dippy answered. Buzz buzzed close. “I will wait,” he said bravely.

When the final song ended, the basement felt calm. The washer clicked. The fan hummed soft. The whiteboard plan had red marks and happy doodles.

Dippy looked around. No one was bumped. No one was left out. He felt proud and warm. “We did it,” he said. “We made a plan. We took turns. We used kind, strong words. We breathed when we felt mad.”

Tina nodded. “Planning helped us.”

Buzz did a loop-de-loop. “Bzz! Planning is power!”

Milo snuggled on a pillow. “I feel safe.”

Dippy smiled and swished his tail gently. “Next time, we will plan first. Then we will play.”

The End.
