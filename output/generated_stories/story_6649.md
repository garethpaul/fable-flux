# Benny Beaver's Big Guest Room Problem

Benny Beaver was a very busy beaver who loved to build things. He had big, flat teeth perfect for chewing wood, and a wide, paddle-shaped tail that went *thwap-thwap* when he was excited.

Today, Benny was staying in Grandma Beaver's cozy guest room. The room smelled like lavender flowers and had soft, fluffy pillows everywhere. But there was one big problem - Benny wanted to build a dam right there in the guest room!

"I need sticks! I need mud! I need to build NOW!" Benny said, his voice getting louder and louder. His tail started slapping the wooden floor - *THWAP! THWAP! THWAP!*

Benny felt his chest getting hot and tight. He was getting very angry because he couldn't build his dam. He started to stomp around the room, making the pretty picture frames rattle on the walls.

"This is not safe," he huffed. "What if there's a flood? What if the water comes in? I MUST build a dam RIGHT NOW!"

Just then, Grandma Beaver peeked her gray head through the doorway. She had kind, twinkling eyes and whiskers that wiggled when she smiled.

"Oh my, Benny," she said gently. "You sound upset. What's making you feel so worried?"

Benny's tail stopped slapping. He took a deep breath that smelled like Grandma's chamomile tea. "I need to build a dam to keep everyone safe, but there's no river here! I don't know what to do!"

Grandma Beaver nodded slowly. "That sounds like a big problem. Would you like to sit with me and think of a strategy together?"

"What's a strategy?" Benny asked, his voice much quieter now.

"A strategy is like a plan - a smart way to solve problems," Grandma explained. "First, let's breathe together. In through your nose... out through your mouth."

Benny breathed in the sweet lavender smell and felt his chest relax. "That feels better," he said.

"Good! Now, let's think together. Are there any rivers or streams near this guest room?" Grandma asked.

Benny looked around carefully. He listened with his small, round ears. All he heard were birds chirping outside and the tick-tock of the grandfather clock. "No rivers," he said thoughtfully.

"So if there are no rivers, do we need a dam right here?" Grandma asked with a gentle smile.

Benny's eyes grew wide. "Oh! No rivers means no floods! The guest room is already safe!"

"Exactly! You used strategy - you looked, you listened, and you thought carefully," Grandma said proudly. "But I can see you still want to build. Would you like to make something special for the guest room instead?"

Benny's tail wiggled with excitement. "Yes, please!"

Together, they gathered soft pillows and cozy blankets. Benny used his building skills to create the most wonderful pillow fort. It had tunnels to crawl through and a special reading nook that smelled like lavender.

"This is even better than a dam!" Benny laughed, snuggling into his creation. "I used strategy to solve my problem, and I talked calmly instead of getting angry."

"You did wonderfully," Grandma said, giving him a warm hug. "Remember, when you feel upset, you can always stop, breathe, and think of a good strategy."

That night, Benny slept peacefully in his pillow fort, dreaming of all the wonderful things he could build tomorrow.

The End.
