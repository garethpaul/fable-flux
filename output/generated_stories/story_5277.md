# Pippa the Penguin Leads the Art Parade

Pippa was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a happy wobble. Today, Pippa waddled into the creative art room. The room smelled like fresh paper and lemony paint. Sunlight danced on jars of brushes. Colored papers rustled like soft leaves. Snip-snip went the scissors. Tap-tap went tiny feet on the floor.

“Good morning!” Pippa chirped. “Let’s make art together!”

Friends from many places gathered around the big table. There was Mei with a red ribbon, Ravi with a shiny sticker star, Amara with a bright bead necklace, and Luka with a blue cap. Their teacher, Miss Lila, smiled and said, “Today we will make an Art Parade! We will make flags to share our favorite things. We will hang them on a long string and then march around the room.”

Pippa clapped her little wings. “I love parades! I can help,” she said, feeling warm and brave inside.

Miss Lila nodded. “Pippa, you can be our leader. A leader listens, helps, and guides.”

Pippa took a deep breath. She wanted to be a kind leader. She wanted everyone to feel happy and proud.

“First,” said Pippa, “let’s put the papers in a neat line. Then we can share the scissors and glue.” She spoke slowly and clearly.

Mei picked a red paper and said, “I want to make a flag with a dragon, like in my grandma’s stories.”

“That sounds wonderful,” Pippa said. “Let’s draw it together.” Pippa slid a tray of crayons over. The crayons smelled waxy and sweet. Scratch-scratch went the crayons on paper.

Ravi held a gold sticker and whispered, “I want a star for the night sky in my home.”

“Great idea,” said Pippa. “Stars can shine on all our flags.” She gave him a soft brush and some dark blue paint. Swish-swish went the brush. The paint looked like the deep, quiet sea.

Amara tapped her beads and hummed. “I want to draw a drum. I like the beat, boom-boom, at festivals.”

“I can help you draw circles,” Pippa offered. “Round like this.” Circle, circle. They laughed when the circles made a happy, bouncy pattern.

Luka frowned at his paper. “I don’t know what to make,” he said. He looked worried. His shoulders drooped.

Pippa waddled closer and lowered her voice. “It’s okay,” she said gently. “Let’s think together.” She put a wing on her chest. “What do you love?”

Luka sniffed. “I love blue. And I love ships. My grandpa sings about ships.”

Pippa smiled. “Blue ships! Good plan.” She showed Luka how to make a big rectangle for the ship and a triangle for the sail. “Step by step,” she said. “We can do it.”

Luka drew slowly. “I did it!” he giggled.

As everyone worked, the room filled with sounds—snip, swish, tap, and happy giggles. Glitter sparkled like tiny stars. The long string for the flags lay on the table, soft and smooth.

Then a little problem popped up. “Oh no,” said Mei. “We only have one bottle of glue.”

Pippa paused. She looked at the table. She counted the friends with her flippers: “One, two, three, four.” She looked at the glue. “One.”

Pippa used logical thinking. “We can take turns,” she said. “Let’s make a glue plan. Mei first, then Ravi, then Amara, then Luka. While we wait, we can draw more.” She set a small timer shaped like a ladybug. Tick-tick-tick.

“Good plan,” said Miss Lila.

They all nodded. Mei glued her dragon scales, then passed the bottle. Ravi added his shiny star and passed the bottle. Amara glued her drum circles and passed the bottle. Luka glued his blue ship.

Pippa checked on everyone. “How are you feeling?” she asked.

“Proud!” said Mei.

“Calm,” said Ravi.

“Happy,” said Amara.

“Brave,” said Luka.

Pippa felt proud too, but she remembered to be compassionate. She helped wipe a drip of glue from Ravi’s sleeve. She found an extra crayon for Amara. She held the paper steady while Mei cut carefully. She whispered, “You’re doing great,” to Luka.

At last, the flags were ready. Pippa lifted the soft string. “Let’s hang them high!” she said. Everyone reached up. The flags fluttered like colorful wings—red dragon, gold star, bright drum, blue ship.

“Time for our Art Parade,” said Miss Lila.

Pippa stood at the front. Her heart beat thump-thump, but it felt like a friendly drum. “Follow me,” she said. “Step, step, smile, smile!”

They marched around the room. Tap-tap went their feet. Swish went the flags. They sang a simple song: “We make, we share, we care!” The room felt warm and bright.

After the parade, Pippa turned to her friends. “You did amazing work,” she said. “Your flags show your stories. We can learn about each other’s homes and songs.”

Mei pointed to the dragon. “This is from my grandma’s festival.”

Ravi pointed to the star. “This is our night sky.”

Amara tapped her drum picture. “This is our music.”

Luka hugged his ship flag. “This is my grandpa’s song.”

Pippa listened carefully. She felt her chest glow, like a little lantern. She learned new things about her friends. She felt strong, but also gentle.

Miss Lila said, “Pippa, you led with care. You listened, you helped, and you made a plan. That is true leadership.”

Pippa smiled and waddled in a happy circle. “A good leader helps everyone shine,” she said. “We practiced, we shared, and we worked together.”

Everyone clapped. The flags rustled like soft applause.

The End.
