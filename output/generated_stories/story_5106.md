# Ollie Otter's Clean Workshop Adventure

Ollie the otter loved to play in Grandpa's garage workshop. The big room smelled like wood shavings and had shiny tools hanging on the walls. Ollie's whiskers twitched with excitement every time he visited.

"Look at all these fun things!" Ollie squeaked, running his paws over the smooth workbench. But as he explored, his paws got covered in sawdust and oil. Soon, his beautiful brown fur was dirty and sticky.

Ollie found a piece of paper and a pencil on the workbench. "I want to write a letter to Mama!" he said happily. But when he picked up the pencil with his dirty paws, black smudges got all over the clean white paper.

"Oh no!" Ollie cried. The paper looked messy, and he couldn't read what he had written. His paws left dirty prints everywhere he touched.

Grandpa Beaver walked into the workshop and saw Ollie looking sad. "What's wrong, little friend?" Grandpa asked kindly.

"I wanted to write Mama a pretty letter, but my paws are too dirty," Ollie sniffled. "Everything I touch gets messy."

Grandpa Beaver's eyes twinkled. "I think I can help you discover something very important," he said. "Follow me to my special washing station."

In the corner of the workshop was a small sink with warm, soapy water. The bubbles smelled like fresh lemons and made Ollie's nose tickle in a good way.

"Before I work with my tools or write anything important, I always wash my paws," Grandpa explained. "Clean paws help me do my best work."

Ollie watched as Grandpa showed him how to wash properly. "First, we wet our paws with warm water. Then we use soap and rub them together like this - scrub, scrub, scrub! Don't forget between your fingers and under your claws."

The warm water felt nice on Ollie's paws. The soap made slippery, fun bubbles that popped when he touched them. "This feels good!" Ollie giggled.

"Now we rinse off all the soap," Grandpa said. "And dry our paws with this soft, clean towel."

Ollie looked at his paws. They were clean and fluffy again! "Wow! They're so much better now," he said proudly.

"Now you can try writing your letter again," Grandpa suggested, giving Ollie a fresh piece of paper.

This time, when Ollie picked up the pencil, no dirty marks appeared on the paper. His letters came out neat and clear. "Dear Mama," he wrote carefully. "I learned about washing my paws today!"

"That's wonderful writing, Ollie!" Grandpa praised. "When we keep ourselves clean, we can do all sorts of things better - writing, building, playing, and even eating."

Ollie felt very proud of himself. "I want to remember to wash my paws every time I play in the workshop," he said firmly. "And before I eat snacks too!"

From that day on, Ollie always washed his paws before doing important activities. He discovered that being clean made everything more fun and helped him do his best work. His letters to Mama were always beautiful and neat, and his workshop adventures were even more enjoyable.

"Clean paws, happy day!" became Ollie's favorite saying.

The End.
