# Robo-Pup’s Compassion Dance

Robo-Pup was a helpful robot dog with a shiny nose light and soft whirring wheels. One sunny afternoon, Robo-Pup rolled into the guest room. The guest room was cozy and quiet. A soft rug tickled Robo-Pup’s wheels. The curtains were yellow like warm sunshine. A big window let in light that made tiny dust sparkles float and dance in the air.

“Beep-beep!” Robo-Pup said happily. “Time to help!”

On the bed sat a small suitcase. The suitcase was open like a friendly mouth. Inside were tiny shirts, a red scarf, a little drum, and a photo of children smiling in different places around the world. There was a gentle smell of clean cotton and lemon polish. Robo-Pup’s ears clicked. “Visitors are coming,” he said. “We will make them feel welcome.”

Just then, the door creaked. A shy child peeked in. The child’s name was Ami. Ami held a stuffed bear with one ear flopped down. Ami looked at the quiet room and whispered, “Is this room for my cousins? They live far away.”

Robo-Pup blinked his blue eye lights. “Yes,” he said softly. “They come from far, far away. They may feel new. They may feel small. We can show compassion.”

“Com-pash-on?” Ami asked, hugging the bear.

“Compassion means we care,” Robo-Pup said. “We see feelings. We help with gentle hearts.”

Ami nodded. “I want to help! What can we do?”

Robo-Pup listened. The guest room hummed softly with the fridge far away, and the clock made a friendly tick-tock. He wagged his metal tail. “First, let us make a welcome place.”

They smoothed the pillow. They tucked the blanket. Ami placed the red scarf on the bed like a bright ribbon. “My cousins like red,” Ami said. “It makes them smile.”

Robo-Pup’s nose light glowed. “Good thinking,” he said. “Smiles travel across the world!”

On the dresser was a little music player. It had sticky stars on it. “Music helps hearts feel safe,” said Robo-Pup. “What music do your cousins like?”

Ami looked at the photo. “They dance where they live,” Ami said. “They clap and stomp and spin!”

Robo-Pup beeped a happy beep. “Let us make a compassion dance.” He pressed a button. Soft drum beats filled the room: dum-dum, pat-pat, dum-dum. The sound was warm like sunshine on cheeks.

Robo-Pup showed a gentle step. “Step, step, clap,” he said. “This dance says, ‘We see you. We care.’”

Ami giggled and tried it. “Step, step, clap!” The rug felt fuzzy under feet. The drum beat tickled Ami’s tummy. The stuffed bear bounced happily.

“Now,” said Robo-Pup, “we add a kindness move from far away.” He showed a slow bow with paws to the heart. “Bow and smile.”

Ami copied. “Bow and smile.”

They danced around the guest room. Curtain shadows swayed like waves. The lemon smell and drum beats mixed together. Robo-Pup made a soft whirr-spin. Ami clapped and laughed. “This is our compassion dance!” Ami said.

Just then, the front door opened. Voices floated down the hallway. Different words, soft and musical, filled the house. Ami’s eyes grew wide. “They are here,” Ami whispered. Ami held the bear close.

Robo-Pup rolled to the door and spoke gently. “Let us welcome them. Remember: we care. We see feelings.”

Two cousins stepped in with their parent. One cousin held a little shell necklace. The other wore bright socks. They looked around the guest room, quiet and careful. Their eyes were big.

Ami took a small breath and smiled. “Hi. This room is for you,” Ami said. “We made it soft and warm.”

Robo-Pup bowed and tapped his nose light. “Welcome,” he said. “We are happy you are here.”

The cousins smiled a little, but one cousin’s eyes were shiny. “I miss home,” the cousin whispered in a tiny voice.

Ami’s heart felt a gentle tug. Ami placed the red scarf in the cousin’s hands. “This is for you,” Ami said. “Red makes smiles. We can dance together.”

Robo-Pup turned on the music again, soft and friendly. “Step, step, clap,” he whispered.

Ami showed the steps. “Step, step, clap.” The cousins watched. Then they tried it too. Step, step, clap. Bow and smile. The shell necklace clicked like tiny rain. Bright socks swished on the rug. Robo-Pup spun with a happy whirr. The room filled with gentle drum beats and happy breath sounds.

The cousin who missed home began to smile. “We dance like this at home too,” they said. “We clap with open hands.” They showed a new move: open hands to the sky, then down to the heart.

Robo-Pup copied carefully. “Open, open, heart,” he said. “Thank you for teaching us.” He looked at Ami. “This is global-awareness,” he added softly. “We learn from friends from far away. We share and care.”

Ami nodded. “We can learn from each other,” Ami said. “Your dance is beautiful.”

They all danced together: step, step, clap, bow and smile, open hands to the sky, down to the heart. Laughter fluttered like little birds. The guest room no longer felt quiet. It felt warm and kind.

When the music faded, everyone sat on the soft rug. The cousins touched the red scarf and smiled. The parent wiped a happy tear and said, “Thank you. You made us feel welcome.”

Robo-Pup’s tail clicked. “Compassion is seeing feelings and helping,” he said. “You were brave and kind, Ami.”

Ami hugged the cousins. “I liked learning your dance,” Ami said. “We can dance again tomorrow.”

“Yes,” said Robo-Pup. “Every day can be a compassion dance.”

The cousins nodded. “We feel at home,” they said.

The guest room was calm now. The sun made golden stripes on the floor. Robo-Pup listened to the soft tick-tock and smiled with his bright nose light. He whispered, “When we care, the world feels closer.”

Lesson: Compassion means we see how others feel, we listen, we learn from them, and we help with kind hearts.

The End.
