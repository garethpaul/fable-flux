# Milo Mouse Tries It Himself

Milo was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a cozy bedroom at the end of the hall. The bedroom smelled like fresh sheets and warm cocoa. The curtains were blue and fluttered softly when the window was open. A small lamp glowed like a friendly star.

One sunny morning, Milo woke up and stretched. “Good morning, bed,” he said with a squeaky giggle. He patted his pillow. “Good morning, pillow.” His whiskers twitched. He felt ready for a little adventure.

On his shelf sat a big picture book called The Busy Garden. The cover was smooth and shiny. Milo wanted to read it by himself. Beside the book was a tiny jar of buttons that clicked when he shook it, and a little cup of crayons that smelled like wax and rainbows.

Milo picked up the book. It was heavy for a small mouse. “I can do it myself,” he said softly. He carried it to his reading corner—a small blanket nest under the window, with a fluffy cushion. He set the book down, but the pages flopped shut.

“Hmm,” said Milo. He opened it again. The pages swooshed and slipped. “I’m trying,” he told himself. His tail curled with a tiny wiggle. He tried to hold the pages with one paw and turn with the other, but the book tipped and thunked onto the floor.

Milo felt his cheeks get warm. “Oh,” he said. “That didn’t work.” His tummy felt tight, like a knot. He wanted to shout, “Help me!” But he took a small breath. In…the warm cocoa air. Out…nice and slow.

“I can try a different way,” said Milo. He remembered his Mama’s gentle voice: “When you feel stuck, stop, breathe, and think of one small step.” Milo nodded. “One small step.”

He looked around the cozy bedroom. He saw his button jar. Click, click, click. He saw his crayons and his little stuffed owl, who always looked brave. He saw a clothespin on the window ledge, left from yesterday’s artwork.

Milo made a plan. “I will use helpers,” he told Owl. “That’s still me doing it.” Owl smiled with stitched eyes.

First, Milo opened the book to the first page, very slowly. Swoosh. He put the clothespin near the top to hold some pages back. Click. It stayed! The book did not flop shut.

“Smart thinking,” Milo whispered to himself. He felt a little lighter.

He began to read the pictures. “The bees buzz. The flowers swish,” he said, tracing the bright colors with a gentle paw. He used his soft mouse voice to tell the story. “Once there was a busy garden. A little worm wiggled and worked. The worm was brave and patient.”

As he read, the clothespin slipped a tiny bit. Uh-oh. The page started to fall forward. Milo felt the knot in his tummy again. He clenched his paws. He wanted to push the book away.

He remembered, “Stop, breathe, small step.” He took a deep breath. In…the smell of clean blanket. Out…nice and slow. He told himself, “I can do one more try.”

He looked at the button jar. Click, click. “Buttons can help,” he said. He placed two buttons at the corners of the page. The buttons felt cool and smooth on his paws. The page stayed down.

“Thank you, buttons,” Milo said, grinning. He went on with his storytelling, turning the next page carefully. He used three buttons this time and a tiny crayon to hold the edge, just enough. He spoke in a clear voice: “The worm kept going, even when dirt fell. The worm tried a new path. The worm did not give up.”

Milo noticed that his voice sounded proud. It made his ears feel warm in a happy way. The room felt calm and safe, with the lamp glowing and the curtains whispering, shhh, shhh.

Soon, Milo reached the last page. He turned it slowly. The garden in the picture was bright with sun. The worm smiled. Milo smiled too. He had done it himself.

Just then Mama Mouse peeked in. “I heard a brave storyteller,” she said softly.

Milo pointed to the book and the buttons. “Mama, I read my pictures all by myself. I used helpers. I got stuck, but I took a breath. I tried again.”

Mama sat beside him, the blanket warm under them. “That is wonderful, Milo,” she said. “You were independent. You made a plan. You kept calm when it was hard.”

Milo’s whiskers wiggled. “I felt frustrated,” he admitted. “My tummy got knotty. But I said, ‘Stop, breathe, small step.’ Then it felt better.”

Mama nodded. “Feelings can be big. But you are bigger when you use your tools.”

Milo hugged Owl. “I want to try my puzzle next,” he said. “I can do one piece at a time.”

“That sounds like a smart small step,” Mama said.

Milo put the book back on the shelf by himself. He smoothed the blanket nest. He set the buttons in their jar. Click. He felt proud and calm. The bedroom smelled like sunshine now, and his heart felt as warm as cocoa.

He looked at Mama and said, “I am growing. I can try. I can ask for help if I need it, and I can help myself too.”

Mama smiled. “Yes, Milo. Independent and kind.”

Milo whispered to Owl, “We can do hard things, one small step at a time.” Owl said nothing, but the room felt extra brave.

The End.
