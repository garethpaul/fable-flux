# Shelly's Big Feelings Day

The warm sun shone down on the green grass in the backyard. Shelly the tortoise was building a beautiful tower with colorful blocks. She stacked them very carefully, one by one.

"Red block, blue block, yellow block," Shelly said happily. The tower was getting tall and pretty.

Just then, Benny the bunny hopped over. "Wow! Can I play too?" he asked excitedly.

"I'm still building," said Shelly. "Maybe when I'm done."

But Benny was too excited to wait. He reached for a green block from Shelly's pile. "I'll just take this one!"

"No!" said Shelly. "That's mine!" She felt her face getting hot and her shell felt tight.

Benny grabbed the block anyway. CRASH! The whole tower fell down. All of Shelly's hard work was gone.

Shelly felt very angry. Her heart beat fast like a drum. "You broke my tower!" she shouted. She wanted to push Benny away.

But then Shelly remembered what Grandpa Tortoise had taught her. When big angry feelings come, she should stop and take deep breaths.

Shelly pulled her head into her shell. She took three slow, deep breaths. "One... two... three..." The angry feeling started to get smaller.

She poked her head back out. "Benny," she said in a calm voice. "I felt very angry when you took my block without asking. It made my tower fall down."

Benny's ears drooped. "I'm sorry, Shelly. I was just excited to play."

"I understand," said Shelly. "But next time, please ask first. These are my blocks, and I need you to respect that."

"You're right," said Benny. "Can we work together to fix it?"

Shelly thought for a moment. "Yes, but let's make some rules first. We take turns picking blocks. And we ask before we touch something that belongs to the other person."

"That sounds fair!" said Benny, nodding his fluffy head.

Together, they began rebuilding the tower. This time, it was even more beautiful because they each added their own creative ideas.

"Your turn to pick a block," said Shelly.

"May I use the purple one?" asked Benny politely.

"Yes, you may," smiled Shelly.

As they played, Shelly felt proud of herself. She had felt very angry, but she didn't let the anger control her. Instead, she used her words to solve the problem.

The sun felt warm on their backs as they worked together. Birds chirped in the trees above them. The grass smelled fresh and sweet.

"Shelly," said Benny as he carefully placed a orange block. "Thank you for teaching me about asking first. And thank you for not staying angry at me."

"Thank you for listening and saying sorry," said Shelly. "When we use our words instead of our anger, we can fix problems and still be friends."

Their new tower was the tallest and most colorful one yet. It had blocks of every color, arranged in a beautiful pattern that sparkled in the sunshine.

"Look what we made together!" said Benny.

"It's much better than what I could have made alone," agreed Shelly.

As the day ended, Shelly felt happy and proud. She had learned that angry feelings are okay, but she could choose what to do with them. Taking deep breaths, using kind words, and setting boundaries had helped her solve the problem and keep her friendship with Benny.

The End.
