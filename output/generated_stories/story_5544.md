# Miko's Amazing Fix-It Day

The morning sun streamed through the dusty garage window. Miko the monkey swung down from the rafters where he liked to sleep. His nose twitched at the smell of old paint and motor oil.

"Oh no!" gasped Miko. His favorite red wagon sat in the corner with a wobbly wheel. The wheel hung sideways, making sad creaking sounds.

Miko's ears drooped. "How can I help Daddy with chores today? My wagon is broken!"

Daddy was counting on Miko to carry tools around the yard. But the wagon couldn't roll with a broken wheel.

Miko scratched his furry head. He looked around the cluttered garage workshop. Rusty screws scattered across the workbench. Colorful paint cans lined the shelves. Old bicycle parts hung from hooks on the wall.

"Maybe I can fix this myself," Miko said bravely. His tail curled with excitement.

First, Miko examined the wobbly wheel closely. The bolt was loose and rusty. "I need something to tighten this," he thought.

Miko climbed up to the tool rack. His small paws grabbed a shiny wrench. But it was too big! The wrench slipped right off the tiny bolt.

"Hmm," Miko tapped his chin. He spotted a jar full of different sized screws and bolts. "What if I find a better bolt?"

Miko dumped out the jar. Clink, clank, clatter! The metal pieces scattered everywhere. Some were too long. Some were too short. Some were too wide.

Then Miko's keen eyes spotted something perfect. A bright silver bolt, just the right size!

But how could he tighten it without the right tool?

Miko's whiskers twitched as he thought hard. He noticed an old coin on the workbench. "This might work!" he said.

Carefully, Miko fitted the coin into the bolt's groove. He turned it slowly. Click, click, click! The bolt tightened perfectly.

"It worked!" Miko cheered, doing a little monkey dance.

But the wheel still looked wobbly. Miko discovered the wheel needed something to help it spin smoothly.

He found a small tube of thick, gooey grease. "This smells funny," Miko giggled, "but it might help!"

Miko squeezed a tiny drop of grease onto the wheel. He spun it gently. The wheel turned smoothly and quietly now.

"Perfect!" Miko clapped his paws together.

Just then, Daddy walked into the garage. "Miko, are you ready to help with— Oh my! You fixed your wagon all by yourself!"

Miko's chest puffed up with pride. "I used what I found in the workshop, Daddy. The coin worked like a screwdriver, and the grease made it spin better!"

Daddy smiled widely. "You were very resourceful, Miko. You didn't give up when you had a problem. You looked around and found creative solutions!"

Miko loaded the wagon with rakes and shovels. The wheel rolled perfectly across the bumpy garage floor.

"Being resourceful means using what you have to solve problems," Daddy explained as they headed outside. "You turned ordinary things into exactly what you needed."

Miko's tail swished happily. He had learned that with creativity and courage, he could fix almost anything. Even when the right tools weren't available, his clever thinking had saved the day.

The End.
