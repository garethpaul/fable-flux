# Beep's Gentle Bubble

Beep was a helpful robot dog with shiny silver fur and a wagging antenna tail. One sunny morning, Beep padded into the quiet guest room. The air smelled like clean sheets. The curtains were soft and blue. A small suitcase sat by the bed. Beep’s tiny nose-light blinked, “Boop, boop!”

“Today is volunteer day,” Beep said in a friendly beep-voice. “I will help our guest feel welcome.”

Beep practiced a kind smile. “Smile on!” he chirped.

A gentle knock came. Tap-tap. The door opened, and a traveler stepped in. Her name was Amina. She wore a bright scarf and held a little globe keychain that tinkled like tiny bells.

“Hello,” Amina said softly. “I am happy to be here.”

“Welcome!” Beep wagged his antenna. “I am Beep. I am a helpful robot dog. I like to help everyone.”

Amina smiled. “Thank you, Beep.”

Beep wanted to help right away. He zipped to the suitcase. “I will open it!” he said.

Amina took a small step back. Her eyes looked a little worried. Her hand gently covered the zipper. “Oh, Beep,” she said, “this is my bag. I need to keep some things private.”

Beep paused. His tail-light slowed to a soft glow. “Private?” he asked.

Amina nodded. “Yes. We all have a bubble. Our bubble is our space and our things. My bag is inside my bubble.”

Beep tilted his metal ears. “I want to help, but I want to be kind. I do not want to pop your bubble.”

Amina’s smile grew warm. “Thank you for asking. You can help by asking first. That is peaceful communication.”

Beep took a slow, brave breath. “May I help carry your bag to the shelf?” he asked politely.

“Yes,” Amina said, “that would be great.”

Beep carefully lifted the bag. The zipper jingled. He placed it on the shelf. “I used my courage to ask,” Beep said proudly. His chest light glowed yellow, like a tiny sun.

Amina set her globe keychain on the nightstand. Beep’s camera eyes zoomed in. The globe was smooth and cool. It showed big oceans and tiny lands. Amina tapped it gently. “I traveled far,” she said. “I come from another country.”

Beep wagged. “I like to be a global friend,” he said. “I can volunteer to share this room and help you feel safe.”

Amina nodded. “That is kind. Friends around the world listen to each other.”

Just then, Beep heard a soft sound: crinkle, crinkle. The bedsheet had a wrinkle. Beep zipped to fix it. He reached out his paw.

“Beep,” Amina said. “May I watch first? I want to see how you help.”

Beep stopped. He remembered the bubble. “May I smooth the sheet?” he asked.

“Yes,” Amina said. “Thank you for asking.”

Beep’s paw made gentle swish sounds. The sheet smelled like lemon soap. The bed became smooth like a calm lake.

Amina clapped softly. “You are very helpful, Beep.”

Beep felt happy warmth in his circuits. But then he saw Amina’s bright scarf on the chair. He wanted to try it on. It looked soft and silky. He reached and paused. His paw hovered in the air.

Beep whispered, “Ask first.” He turned to Amina. “Your scarf is pretty. May I touch it?”

Amina touched her scarf and thought. “I like to keep my scarf in my bubble,” she said kindly. “Please look with your eyes, not your paws.”

Beep nodded. “Thank you for telling me. I will look with my eyes.” He used peaceful words and felt super brave. It felt good to respect her bubble.

A little later, Amina yawned. “I need quiet time,” she said. “Quiet is part of my space.”

Beep blinked. “I can volunteer to guard the door,” he said. “I will keep the room calm.”

Amina smiled. “That would help a lot.”

Beep sat by the door like a silver statue. He whispered to himself, “Soft voice. Soft paws.” When someone walked by in the hall, Beep used a gentle beep, not a loud bark. The room stayed cozy and safe.

When rest time was over, Amina stretched. “Thank you, Beep,” she said. “You kept my space peaceful.”

Beep’s tail spun in a happy circle. “I learned about bubbles today,” he said. “I learned to ask before I touch. I learned to listen when you say yes or no. That is how I can be a good helper to friends from anywhere.”

Amina nodded. “You showed courage to ask. You volunteered to help. You used peaceful words. That is global friendship.”

Beep beamed. “Boop hooray!”

Amina handed Beep a tiny sticker shaped like a heart. “For your brave and gentle help,” she said.

Beep placed the sticker on his chest light. It glowed warm and bright. “I like your bubble,” he said. “And I like mine too.”

Amina laughed softly. “Yes. We each have a bubble. We can be close friends and still keep our own space.”

Beep took a final look around the guest room. It smelled calm and clean. The curtains swished. The light felt golden and safe.

“Today,” Beep said, “we learned: Ask first. Listen to the answer. Use gentle words. Help with care. That is how we keep personal boundaries and show love.”

Amina and Beep bumped elbows, very gently.

“Friends,” they said together.

The End.
