# Ollie Owl and the Attic of Little Jobs

In a quiet attic, where dust sparkled like tiny stars in the glow of a small lamp, lived a wise old owl named Ollie. The attic smelled like old wood and cinnamon. Soft boxes made gentle thumps when the house sighed. Ollie’s feathers were fluffy and warm, and his eyes were kind and bright.

One morning, Ollie fluffed his wings and looked around. “Hoo-hoo,” he said softly. “I have many little jobs today.”

He pointed with one wing at a list written on a chalkboard. It said: 
1. Sweep the floor. 
2. Sort the story books. 
3. Hang the welcome scarves. 
4. Check the window latch.

“Little jobs, one by one,” Ollie hummed. “Start small. Stay safe. Finish strong.”

Just then, a tiny breeze whispered through the attic. It made the paper lanterns sway. The lanterns were painted with warm colors and little words from many places. One red lantern said “Hola.” A green one said “Bonjour.” A yellow one said “Namaste.” A blue one said “Konnichiwa.” They made soft shadows that danced like friendly birds.

Ollie smiled. “These are our hello words,” he said. “They help us show care for friends from many homes.” He touched each lantern gently. “We keep them high and safe.”

He picked up a small broom. “Step one: sweep.” He put on his soft knee pads and a little dust mask. “Safety first,” he said. “We protect our eyes and our beaks.”

Swish, swish went the broom. Little dust bunnies scooted away. “Excuse me, dust,” Ollie giggled. “We are cleaning today.” The floor started to shine like smooth chocolate.

When the sweeping was done, Ollie checked his list. He made a neat check mark. “One job finished,” he said. “I can do the next one.”

He opened a box of story books. The covers smelled like paper and adventure. Some books had shiny gold letters. Some had soft pictures of children dancing, sharing bread, and singing. Ollie sorted them by size so they would not fall. “Big books on the bottom,” he said. “Small books on top.” He whispered each title, careful and slow, like a song.

Then a tiny voice squeaked from a corner. It was a little mouse named Mina, wearing a bright scarf with tiny stars. “Hello, Ollie,” Mina said. “May I help?”

“Hello, Mina,” said Ollie. “Yes, you can help by staying safe. Please wear your tiny gloves.” Mina pulled on small blue gloves and smiled. “We finish our jobs by staying careful,” Ollie said.

Together they stacked the books. “Gracias,” Mina said when Ollie passed a book. “That means thank you,” Ollie told her. “Merci,” he said back. Mina giggled. “I like hello and thank-you words!”

They checked the list again. Two check marks now. “Next,” said Ollie, “we hang the welcome scarves.” The scarves were soft and bright. They came from many families. Some had little elephants. Some had flowers. Some had lines like rain.

“We will hang them low enough to see,” Ollie said, “but not where we trip.” He climbed a small step stool. “I will use the stool with three feet on the floor,” he said. “You will hold it steady. Team safety.”

Mina held the stool with both tiny paws. “I’ve got it,” she said.

Ollie hung a red scarf. “Hola,” he said. He hung a green scarf. “Bonjour.” A yellow scarf. “Namaste.” A blue scarf. “Konnichiwa.” The scarves whispered in the soft breeze, making a happy rustle, like little waves. The attic looked bright and kind.

“Keep going,” Mina cheered. Ollie smiled. “We are almost done.”

They looked at the list. Three check marks! “Last job,” said Ollie. “Check the window latch.”

The window was old and a bit wobbly. Rain was tapping softly outside. Pitter-patter, pitter-patter. Ollie touched the latch. “It must be snug so wind does not push in,” he said.

But the latch was stuck. Ollie took a deep breath. “When something is stuck, we do not force it,” he told Mina. “We use tools and ask for help.”

He found a small drop of oil. “A tiny drop,” he said. “Safety means just enough.” He placed the oil and wiped the extra. Click! The latch moved smooth and easy. “There,” he said. “Safe and sound.”

Mina clapped her paws. “We did it! We finished all the jobs!”

Ollie made the last check mark. Four neat marks in a row. He felt warm and proud. The attic felt cozy and clean. The lanterns glowed. The scarves swayed. The books stood brave and tall.

Mina looked up. “Ollie, what did we learn today?”

Ollie blinked kindly. “We learned to finish our tasks, one by one,” he said. “We stayed safe with masks, gloves, and careful steps. We used hello and thank-you words from many places. That shows love for many cultures. And we asked for help when we needed it.”

Mina nodded. “I like finishing,” she said. “It feels good, like a hug.”

Ollie wrapped a wing around her. “Yes,” he said. “Start small. Stay safe. Finish strong. And say hello with a smile.”

They turned off the lamp. The attic glowed softly with lantern light and happy hearts. Outside, the rain quieted, and the house felt peaceful.

The End.
