# Rosie Rabbit's Musical Mystery

The morning sun shone through the big windows of the music room. Rosie Rabbit hopped inside, her fluffy white tail bouncing with each step. She loved music time! The room smelled like wood polish and had colorful instruments everywhere.

"Good morning, everyone!" called Mrs. Melody, the music teacher. "Today we'll practice for our spring concert!"

Rosie's ears perked up with excitement. She couldn't wait to play her tiny drum. But as she looked around, she noticed something was wrong.

Little Mouse sat in the corner, looking very sad. His whiskers drooped down, and his small paws covered his eyes.

"What's wrong, Mouse?" Rosie asked gently, hopping over to him.

"I can't reach my triangle," Mouse squeaked quietly. "It's hanging too high on the wall. Everyone else has their instruments, but I can't get mine."

Rosie looked up. Sure enough, the shiny silver triangle hung on a hook that was perfect for bigger animals, but much too high for tiny Mouse.

"Hmm," thought Rosie, tapping her paw on her chin. "Let me think about this."

She looked around the room carefully. There was a small wooden stool near the piano, but it was too heavy for Mouse to move by himself. The other animals were busy with their own instruments and didn't notice Mouse's problem.

"I have an idea!" Rosie said with a bright smile. "But I need to ask Mrs. Melody first."

Rosie hopped over to their teacher. "Mrs. Melody, Mouse can't reach his triangle. May I help him?"

"Of course, dear Rosie," Mrs. Melody smiled. "Helping friends is always the right thing to do."

Rosie thought hard. She was tall enough to reach the triangle, but she wanted to help Mouse feel proud and independent too.

"Mouse," she called, "come stand on my back! Then you can reach your triangle all by yourself!"

Mouse's eyes lit up like tiny stars. "Really? You'd do that for me?"

"Of course!" Rosie said warmly. "Friends help friends."

Carefully, Mouse climbed onto Rosie's strong back. Rosie stood very still, making sure Mouse felt safe. With a happy squeak, Mouse reached up and took his triangle from the hook.

"I did it! I got my triangle!" Mouse cheered, holding the instrument high.

The sweet "ting, ting, ting" sound filled the air as Mouse played his first notes. All the other animals stopped and clapped their paws together.

"Beautiful music, Mouse!" called Bear from across the room.

"Thank you, Rosie!" Mouse said, giving her the biggest hug his tiny arms could give. "You helped me when I really needed it."

Rosie felt warm and happy inside. Her heart felt as light as a feather. "That's what friends do," she said with a gentle smile.

For the rest of music class, Rosie and Mouse played their instruments together. The drum went "boom, boom, boom" and the triangle went "ting, ting, ting." They made the most wonderful music together.

When it was time to go, Mrs. Melody gathered everyone in a circle.

"Today, Rosie showed us something very important," she said. "When we see someone who needs help, we can use our thinking skills to find a way to help them. And when we help others, we make beautiful music together - not just with instruments, but with kindness."

Rosie's ears stood up tall with pride. She had learned that helping others made her feel just as happy as it made them feel.

The End.
