# Benny Bear Prepares Pancakes

In a warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon and sunshine, a friendly bear cub named Benny wiggled his paws. The bright morning light made the honey jars sparkle. The kettle hummed a happy song, and the wooden spoon felt smooth in Benny’s paw.

“I want to make pancakes!” Benny cheered, bouncing on his toes.

Mama Bear smiled and tapped the counter. “Pancakes are fun,” she said softly. “First, we prepare.”

“Prepare?” Benny tilted his fuzzy head. His ears twitched with curiosity. “Can we cook now?”

Mama Bear shook her head gently. “We will, after we prepare. Preparing helps us do things safely and well.”

Benny took a long breath in and let it out slowly. “Okay. Let’s prepare.”

Mama Bear held up a paw. “Step one: listen.”

Benny nodded and looked at Mama’s kind eyes. He kept his paws still. “I’m listening.”

“Good,” Mama said. “Step two: gather what we need. Let’s make a list.”

Benny liked lists. They felt neat, like stacking blocks. Mama said each item, and Benny repeated:

“Bowl,” he said, patting the big blue bowl.

“Spoon,” he said, holding the wooden spoon.

“Measuring cups,” he said, clicking them together like little bells.

“Flour, milk, eggs, and a tiny pinch of salt,” Mama finished.

Benny placed each item on the counter in a row. He liked the way the flour bag crinkled, the milk felt cold, and the eggs were smooth like little moons. He smiled. “We are prepared!”

Mama winked. “Almost. Step three: clear our space.”

Benny looked around. Toys? Away in the basket. Sticky jam? Wiped with a warm cloth. He lined the bowl in the middle. “Now we are prepared.”

“Wonderful,” Mama said. “Step four: slow and steady. We use self-control.”

Benny curled his paws to his chest and whispered, “Slow and steady.” His body felt calm like a quiet stream.

“Let’s begin,” Mama said. “First, flour.”

Benny scooped the flour. A tiny cloud puffed up, soft as snow. “Oops!” Benny giggled. Flour tickled his nose. “Ah-choo!”

Mama laughed kindly. “That’s okay. Preparation helps us know what to do next. Tap the scoop on the bowl to shake off extra flour.”

Tap, tap. The flour fell like white rain. Benny watched carefully.

“Milk now,” Mama said.

Benny held the measuring cup with both paws. “I will pour slowly.” The milk made a soft glug-glug. Benny watched the line rise to the number Mama pointed at. “I did it!”

“Eggs,” Mama said, handing him one.

Benny felt the smooth shell. “Crack?” he asked.

“Soft crack,” Mama said. “Use the bowl edge. Tap-tap. Then open like a door.”

Benny took a long breath. Tap-tap. Crack! He opened it carefully. Plop! The egg slid into the bowl. No shell pieces! Benny’s eyes shone. “I used slow and steady!”

“You did,” Mama said proudly. “Now stir.”

Benny wrapped both paws around the spoon. Swirl, swirl. The batter became creamy and smelled sweet. He felt the spoon get heavy and light as he moved. “This is coordination,” Mama said. “Your paws, your eyes, your muscles—working together.”

Benny grinned. “My paws and eyes are friends!”

The pan warmed on the stove. It made a soft sss as a little butter melted. “Listen,” Mama whispered.

Benny listened. He heard the tiny sizzle, like a quiet song. He smelled warm butter and sweet batter. “Ready?” Mama asked.

“Ready,” Benny said. He poured a small circle. “Like a sun!”

They watched bubbles pop on top. “When you see bubbles,” Mama said, “it’s time to flip.”

Benny held the spatula. His paws felt wiggly. He paused and took another slow breath. “Slow and steady,” he reminded himself. He slid the spatula under and lifted. Flip! The pancake turned and landed with a soft pat.

“I did it!” Benny cheered, bouncing just a little.

“You prepared, you listened, you used self-control, and you coordinated your movements,” Mama said. “That helped you do it.”

They made more pancakes—little moons, tiny stars, and even a bear face with blueberry eyes. Benny used his creativity, placing fruit carefully. “Look!” he said. “A happy pancake!”

When the plate was full, Mama turned off the stove. “Last step,” she said.

“Eat?” Benny guessed.

“Almost,” Mama smiled. “We clean up. Preparation also means putting things away so we’re ready next time.”

Benny nodded. He wiped the counter with warm, soapy bubbles. He stacked the measuring cups. He put the flour back where it lived. He felt proud. The kitchen looked calm and cozy again.

They sat at the table. Benny’s chair was soft, and the plate was warm in his paws. He took a bite. The pancake was fluffy and sweet, with a tiny crunch from blueberry seeds. He hummed, “Mmm!”

Mama poured a little honey. “How do you feel?”

Benny touched his chest. “I feel proud and peaceful.”

Mama kissed his fuzzy forehead. “That’s the power of preparation.”

Benny smiled. “First we prepare, then we do. We listen, we go slow, we work together. That helps me do hard things.”

Mama raised her fork. “Well said, Chef Benny.”

They clinked forks like tiny bells and ate their happy pancakes. The warm kitchen hummed softly. Benny looked at the neat counter, the clean bowl, and the empty flour trail. He felt ready for anything.

“Tomorrow,” Benny said, “I will prepare to make fruit salad!”

Mama laughed. “With the same careful steps.”

Benny nodded. “Prepare, listen, slow and steady, and clean up.”

He took another joyful bite and hugged Mama with syrupy paws.

The End.
