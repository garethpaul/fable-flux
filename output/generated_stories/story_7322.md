# Nutkin's Workshop Adventure

Nutkin the squirrel loved to make things with his tiny paws. Every morning, he scampered down from his oak tree to visit Mr. Tom's garage workshop. The workshop smelled like wood shavings and had shelves full of colorful tools that sparkled in the sunlight.

"Good morning, Nutkin!" called Mr. Tom, who was painting a wooden birdhouse bright blue. "What would you like to make today?"

Nutkin's fluffy tail twitched with excitement. "I want to make a acorn holder for winter!" he squeaked. "But I need to use the small hammer."

Just then, Chippy the chipmunk ran into the workshop. "I'm here to make a pinecone mobile!" Chippy announced loudly. He grabbed the small hammer before Nutkin could reach it. "I need this hammer first!"

Nutkin felt his whiskers droop. The hammer was perfect for his tiny paws, and he had asked for it first. But Chippy was already starting to bang on some pinecones.

"Chippy," said Nutkin in a clear, brave voice, "I was here first, and I asked to use that hammer. Could you please let me have a turn?"

Chippy stopped hammering and looked at Nutkin. "But I'm already using it!" he said.

Mr. Tom set down his paintbrush and smiled. "In my workshop, we take turns with tools," he said gently. "Chippy, how long do you think you'll need the hammer?"

Chippy counted on his little claws. "Maybe... five minutes?"

"That sounds fair," said Nutkin, feeling proud that he had spoken up. "I can wait five minutes. While I wait, maybe I can sand my wood smooth!"

Nutkin picked up a piece of sandpaper that felt rough like tree bark. He rubbed it back and forth on his piece of wood, making it smooth as a river stone. The sound was soft and scratchy, like leaves rustling in the wind.

"Time's up!" called Mr. Tom after five minutes.

Chippy handed the hammer to Nutkin with a smile. "Here you go! Thank you for waiting your turn."

"Thank you for sharing!" said Nutkin. He took the hammer and began tapping tiny nails into his wood. *Tap, tap, tap!* The sound echoed through the workshop like a woodpecker's song.

While Nutkin worked, Chippy used the big scissors to cut colorful string for his mobile. They worked side by side, sharing the workspace peacefully.

"Look!" said Nutkin, holding up his finished acorn holder. It had three small cups perfect for storing his winter nuts. "It's exactly what I wanted!"

"And look at my mobile!" said Chippy, hanging his creation from a hook. The pinecones danced and spun, casting moving shadows on the workshop walls.

Mr. Tom clapped his hands. "You both did wonderful work! And Nutkin, I'm proud of you for speaking up when you needed the hammer. That took courage."

"And Chippy, you did a great job sharing and taking turns," Mr. Tom added.

As the sun climbed higher in the sky, Nutkin packed up his new acorn holder. He felt warm and happy inside, like drinking hot cocoa on a cold day.

"Thank you, Mr. Tom!" called both friends as they headed home.

"Come back tomorrow!" Mr. Tom replied. "There's always more crafting to do!"

Nutkin scampered up his tree, knowing he had learned something important: when you speak up kindly and take turns, everyone can create wonderful things together.

The End.
