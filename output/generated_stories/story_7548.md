# Penny's Bathroom Art Gallery

Penny the penguin waddled into the big white bathroom. Her orange feet made soft patting sounds on the cool tiles. She carried her special art box under her flipper.

"Today I'll make the most beautiful pictures!" Penny said happily. She loved art time in the bathroom where the light was bright and shiny.

Penny opened her art box. Inside were colorful crayons, sparkly stickers, and smooth paper. She spread everything out on the bathroom counter next to the sink.

"What should I draw first?" Penny wondered aloud. She picked up a blue crayon and started drawing ocean waves on her paper.

Just then, she heard a tiny sniffle from behind the toilet.

"Hello?" Penny called softly. "Is someone there?"

A small mouse peeked out. His whiskers trembled. "I'm sorry," squeaked the mouse. "I didn't mean to bother you. I just... I really love art too."

Penny's heart felt warm. "What's your name?" she asked kindly.

"I'm Max," said the mouse. "I watch you make beautiful pictures every day. But I'm too small. No one ever includes me in art time."

Penny looked at her art supplies, then at Max's sad little face. She had an idea!

"Max, would you like to make art together?" Penny asked. "I have plenty of supplies to share."

Max's tiny eyes grew wide. "Really? But I'm just a mouse. And this is your special art time."

"Art is more fun when we share it!" Penny said cheerfully. She carefully placed a small piece of paper on the floor for Max. Then she chose the tiniest crayon from her box.

"This purple crayon is perfect for your size," Penny said, rolling it gently toward Max.

Max picked up the crayon with his tiny paws. "Thank you, Penny! What should we draw?"

"Let's each draw our favorite things," Penny suggested. "Then we can put our pictures together like a big art gallery!"

Penny drew beautiful icebergs and fish swimming in blue water. Max drew tiny flowers and cheese wedges that made Penny giggle.

"Your flowers are so detailed!" Penny said, admiring Max's work.

"And your fish look like they're really swimming!" Max replied happily.

When they finished, Penny had another wonderful idea. "Let's hang our pictures on the bathroom wall! We can make our own art gallery."

Working together, they carefully taped their artwork to the white bathroom walls. Penny reached the high spots while Max placed pictures down low.

"It's beautiful!" Max squeaked with joy. "Our very own gallery!"

Penny stepped back to admire their work. The bathroom walls were now covered with colorful drawings. "You know what, Max? This is the best art time I've ever had."

"Really?" Max asked.

"Really," Penny nodded. "Sharing art with a friend makes everything more special. And now everyone who comes in here will see our beautiful gallery!"

From that day on, Penny and Max made art together every afternoon. They learned that art becomes more magical when shared with others. And the bathroom became the most colorful, happy room in the whole house.

"Art is always better with friends," Penny would say, and Max would nod his little head in agreement.

The End.
