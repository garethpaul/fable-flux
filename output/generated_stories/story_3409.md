# Benny's Big Garden Helper Day

Benny the rabbit wiggled his pink nose as he hopped into the old garden shed. The wooden walls smelled like earth and flowers. Sunlight peeked through dusty windows, making golden spots on the floor.

"Today I want to help Farmer Joe!" Benny said to himself. His fluffy tail twitched with excitement. But when he looked around the big shed, his ears drooped down.

Everything was SO tall! The watering can sat high on a shelf. Garden tools hung way up on the wall. Even the seed packets were stored in boxes Benny couldn't reach.

"I'm too small," Benny whispered sadly. His whiskers trembled. "I can't help with anything."

Just then, he heard a tiny voice. "Psst! Down here!"

Benny looked down. A little mouse poked her head out from behind a flower pot.

"Hi! I'm Squeaky," said the mouse. "You look sad. What's wrong?"

"I want to help in the garden, but I'm too little," Benny sighed. "Everything is too big for me."

Squeaky's eyes sparkled. "Being small isn't bad! Watch this!" She scurried up a rope and pushed a small packet of seeds off the shelf. It landed softly near Benny.

"Wow!" Benny's ears perked up. "You're so clever!"

"We can work together," Squeaky said kindly. "You're bigger than me, and I can reach high places. What do you think?"

Benny felt a warm feeling in his chest. Maybe he COULD help after all! "Yes! Let's make a plan!"

First, Squeaky climbed up and pushed down packets of carrot seeds. Benny caught them in his soft paws. Then Benny used his strong back legs to move the heavy watering can closer to the door. Squeaky squeezed through a crack and turned on the water spigot.

"This is working!" Benny laughed as cool water splashed into the can.

Next, they needed small garden tools. Squeaky found tiny hand shovels on a low shelf. Benny carefully carried them outside in his mouth.

When Farmer Joe came to the shed, his eyes grew wide. "Well, look at this! Someone organized all my small tools and filled the watering can!"

Benny and Squeaky peeked out from behind a sunflower. Benny's heart felt big and proud.

"I DID help!" Benny whispered. "We both did!"

That afternoon, Benny and Squeaky planted carrot seeds together. Benny dug holes with his paws while Squeaky dropped in the seeds. The warm dirt felt good between Benny's toes. Birds sang happy songs above them.

"Thank you for helping me feel brave," Benny told Squeaky as they shared a crisp lettuce leaf.

"Thank you for being my friend," Squeaky replied. "We make a great team!"

As the sun set, painting the sky orange and pink, Benny hopped home with his head held high. His cotton tail bounced with each happy step.

"I learned something important today," Benny said to the twinkling stars. "I don't have to be big to be helpful. I just need to be kind, think smart, and work with friends!"

From that day on, Benny and Squeaky were the best garden helpers ever. And whenever Benny felt too small for something, he remembered that being little could be wonderful too.

The End.
