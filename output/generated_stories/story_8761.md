# Rocco Raccoon and the Bathroom Band

Rocco was a musical raccoon with a fluffy striped tail and bright, curious eyes. He loved to make music everywhere he went. He tapped on pots. He hummed in the yard. He even whistled to the birds. But Rocco’s favorite place to play music was the bathroom.

The bathroom was shiny and echoey. When Rocco clapped, the sound bounced off the tiles. Clap! Clap! Clap! When he tapped the tub, it sounded like a drum. Boom, boom, boom. When he blew across the top of a bottle, it made a soft hooo sound. The mirror fogged up with warm steam, and drops of water made tiny plink, plink noises on the sink. It was like a little concert in there.

One morning, Rocco hurried in with his wooden spoon and a small plastic cup. “Time for the Bathroom Band!” he sang.

His sister, Ruby, called from the hallway, “Rocco, I need to brush my teeth!”

“Just one song!” Rocco said, tail swishing. He tapped the cup on the tub. Tap-tap. He drummed on the wastebasket lid. Tum-tum. He stomped a little rhythm. Stomp-stomp. It felt so good to make music.

But the noise made the lights seem extra bright, and the sound bounced and bounced. Boom! Tap! Tum! Stomp! Rocco felt full of big energy. He went faster and faster. His spoon slipped and clattered to the floor. Clang!

Ruby stepped in and covered her ears. “Rocco, it’s too loud! I can’t think!”

Rocco frowned. “But I want to play.”

Mama Raccoon peeked in with a gentle smile. She smelled like soap and warm towels. “I hear big sounds,” Mama said softly. “What is happening?”

Ruby said, “I need my turn to brush. Rocco keeps playing.”

Rocco’s ears drooped. He looked at the spoon. He looked at the sink. He felt a wiggle in his tummy, a fizzy feeling. “I want my music,” he said, his voice small.

Mama knelt down. “I love your music. And I also love taking turns. The bathroom is for everyone. What can we do?”

Rocco took a deep breath. He remembered a trick Mama taught him. “Smell the flower,” he whispered to himself, breathing in through his nose. “Blow the candle,” he whispered, breathing out slowly. His shoulders relaxed. The fizzy feeling got smaller.

“I can wait,” Rocco said, still a little sad. “But waiting is hard.”

Mama nodded. “Waiting is hard. Waiting is practice. It helps us improve. Let’s make a plan. A music plan and a brushing plan.”

Ruby held up two fingers. “Two minutes for me, then two minutes for you?”

Rocco thought. He tapped the floor softly with one finger. Tap. Not too loud. “Okay. Two minutes. And I can play quiet.”

Ruby smiled. “Thank you.”

Rocco set the spoon down and watched. Ruby brushed her teeth. Scritch-scratch, scritch-scratch. The mint smell was fresh and tingly in the air. Rocco hummed a gentle tune in his head. Mmm-mmm-mmm. He swayed his tail slowly. He looked at the clock on the wall. Tick-tock, tick-tock. He whispered, “Smell the flower. Blow the candle.” He could do it.

When Ruby finished, she said, “Your turn!” She handed Rocco a soft washcloth. “Maybe this instead of the spoon?”

Rocco touched the cloth. It felt squishy and quiet. “A quiet drum,” he said, a small smile growing. He patted it on the counter. Pat-pat. He tapped the side of the tub with his paw pads. Tup-tup. He blew over the cup, just a whisper. Hoo.

Mama clapped softly. “Beautiful. You are improving your music and your waiting.”

Rocco wiggled with joy. He played a quiet song while Ruby washed her hands. He made a soft rain sound with his claws on the shower curtain. Rrrr-rrraaa. He brushed the washcloth over the mirror. Swish-swish. He listened to how the sounds changed. The bathroom felt cozy and calm.

Just then, Papa Raccoon knocked. “Toothpaste time for me!”

Rocco looked at Ruby. He felt the fizzy feeling try to pop back up. He wanted to say, “No, it’s my turn!” But he remembered the plan. He took another breath. Smell the flower. Blow the candle.

Rocco said, “Papa, I can take turns. I will finish my quiet song, and then it’s your turn.” He counted to five on his fingers. “One, two, three, four, five.” Then he put the cup down.

Papa smiled. “Thank you, my musical friend.”

Rocco stood back and listened to the water. Drip, drip, drip. He noticed a new sound he hadn’t heard before: the heater made a low hum, mmmmmm. He hummed along softly. His music felt different now. It was careful. It was kind.

When Papa finished, he said, “Rocco, would you like to lead the Bathroom Band for one more song while I clap?”

Rocco grinned. “Yes! A quiet song.”

He looked at his band: Ruby with the hand towel, Mama with the hairbrush, Papa with gentle claps. Rocco lifted his paw. “Ready?”

They played together, soft and sweet. Swish. Tap. Hoo. Clap. The bathroom filled with warm, happy sounds. No one covered their ears. Everyone smiled.

After the song, Rocco said, “I improved! I can play quiet. I can wait. I can take turns.”

Mama hugged him. “You practiced self-control. You made a plan. That’s how we get better.”

Ruby bumped noses with him. “And your music is still beautiful.”

Rocco felt proud and calm. He put the spoon away and washed his paws. The water felt warm. The soap smelled like oranges. He dried with a fluffy towel. He looked in the mirror and saw a musical raccoon who could learn and grow.

Rocco whispered to himself, “When I breathe and wait, I make space for everyone. That helps me improve.”

Then he did a happy tail swish and padded out of the bathroom, ready for more music and more turns.

The End.
