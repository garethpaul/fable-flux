# Shelly's Big Helper Day

Shelly the turtle peeked her little green head out from under her cozy shell. Today was special - Grandma Turtle was coming to visit! But Shelly felt butterflies dancing in her tummy. She was very shy and always hid when visitors came.

"Shelly, dear," called Mama Turtle from the kitchen. "Can you help me get the guest room ready for Grandma?"

Shelly's eyes grew wide. The guest room was where Grandma would sleep tonight. "But Mama," Shelly whispered, pulling her head back a little, "what if I do something wrong?"

Mama Turtle smiled warmly. "Come with me, sweetie. I'll show you how we can make Grandma feel welcome together."

They walked slowly to the sunny guest room. The yellow walls looked cheerful, but the bed was messy and there were toys scattered on the floor.

"First," said Mama, "let's think about how Grandma might feel after her long trip. What do you think she'll need?"

Shelly thought hard, tapping her tiny claws together. "Um... maybe she'll be tired?"

"That's right! You're being very thoughtful," Mama said proudly. "When we think about how others feel, it helps us know how to help them."

Shelly felt a warm feeling in her chest. Maybe she could be a good helper after all!

"Let's make the bed soft and comfy," Mama suggested. Together, they pulled the fluffy blue blanket tight and fluffed the pillows until they looked like puffy clouds.

"This feels so soft!" Shelly giggled, patting the pillow with her small paw.

Next, they picked up the toys. Shelly found her favorite teddy bear under the chair. "Should I leave Mr. Snuggles here for Grandma?" she asked.

"What a kind idea!" Mama beamed. "But maybe Grandma brought her own special things. Let's put Mr. Snuggles in your room where he belongs."

As they cleaned, Shelly started feeling braver. "Mama, what else might make Grandma happy?"

"Well," Mama said, "Grandma loves flowers. Maybe we could pick some for her room?"

Shelly's eyes sparkled. Even though she was shy, she loved the garden! They went outside and carefully picked pretty yellow daisies and purple violets. The flowers smelled sweet like sunshine.

"These smell wonderful!" Shelly said, sniffing the bouquet.

Back in the guest room, they put the flowers in a small vase by the window. The room now looked bright and welcoming.

Just then, they heard a gentle knock at the front door.

"That's Grandma!" Mama said.

Shelly felt nervous again and started to hide in her shell. But then she looked at the beautiful room they had prepared together. She thought about how tired Grandma must be and how happy the flowers would make her.

Taking a deep breath, Shelly walked to the front door with Mama.

"Hello, my dear Shelly!" Grandma Turtle said with twinkling eyes.

"Hi, Grandma," Shelly said softly. "I... I helped make your room ready. There are flowers and soft pillows."

Grandma's face lit up with joy. "Oh, sweetheart! That's so thoughtful of you. You must have worked very hard to think about what I would need."

Shelly felt proud and warm inside. She had been brave, and thinking about Grandma's feelings had helped her know exactly what to do.

"Thank you for caring about me," Grandma said, giving Shelly a gentle hug.

That night, Shelly learned that when we think about how others feel and try to help them, it makes both them and us feel wonderful inside.

The End.
