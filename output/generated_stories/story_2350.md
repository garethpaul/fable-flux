# Ollie the Otter’s Rooftop Rescue

Ollie was a playful otter with bright eyes and a splashy tail. Ollie loved to explore. One sunny morning, Ollie climbed the stairs to the rooftop garden. The air smelled like fresh mint and warm bread from the bakery next door. A soft breeze tickled Ollie’s whiskers.

“Good morning, rooftop!” Ollie giggled.

The rooftop had flower boxes, little wind chimes, and a small blue kiddie pool. The pool shone like a tiny lake in the sky. Ollie wiggled his paws. “Today, I will plant seeds, fill the pool, and share a snack with friends,” Ollie said. “We will work together!”

Ollie brought a little backpack. Inside were small cups for seeds, a roll of tape, a coil of string, a tiny scoop, and a folded list. Ollie tapped the list. “This is my plan,” he said. “Preparation helps me do hard things.”

Just then, two friends popped up from the stairwell. Pip the pigeon flapped his gray wings. “Coo! Coo! I am ready to help,” Pip said.

Mina the mouse squeaked and held a cloth. “I brought a blanket for our picnic!”

Ollie smiled. “Teamwork time! Let’s set up the pool first.”

They pushed the light pool into a sunny spot. The plastic felt smooth and cool under Ollie’s paws. But the rooftop wind whispered and then whooshed. The pool slid a little. “Whoops,” said Ollie. “The wind is strong today.”

Pip tilted his head. “Coo? Is that a problem?”

“It is a little problem,” Ollie said. “We can be resilient. We can try again.”

They filled buckets from a faucet and poured water into the pool. Splash, splash, sploosh! Water sparkled and sprinkled their cheeks. Mina giggled. “It’s like rain kisses!”

A big gust of wind came. WHOOSH! The pool wobbled and bumped the flower box. Water sloshed out. A tiny plant bent over.

“My plant!” cried Mina. Her whiskers trembled.

Ollie took a deep breath. He put a paw on his chest and counted, “One… two… three.” The breeze smelled like rosemary. The chimes went ting-a-ling.

“It’s okay, Mina,” Ollie said. “We can fix it. We will be brave and try again.”

Pip pecked gently at the plant. “Coo, we will help.”

Ollie scooped fresh soil with his tiny scoop. Mina held the stem so soft. Pip shaded the plant with his wing. Together they tucked the plant back up, snug and safe. Ollie dripped a little water at the base. “There,” he said. “Teamwork helps.”

They still needed to stop the pool from sliding. Ollie opened the backpack and pulled out the string and tape. “Preparation time!”

Mina’s ears perked. “What’s the plan?”

Ollie looked around. “We can make safe ties. We can ask the railing to help.” The metal railing felt cool and firm. Ollie looped the string around the pool handles and tied it to the railing posts. Pip tugged with his beak. Mina pressed the tape to hold the knots. The wind blew again. The pool stayed put.

“Hooray!” they cheered.

They filled the pool higher. Ollie dipped a paw. “Ahh, cool and nice.” Sunlight made little diamonds on the water. Pip sipped and cooed. Mina tapped her toes on the warm roof.

Then clouds rolled in. The sky turned gray. A new wind came, more pushy and loud. WHOOSH! A flower box at the far end tipped. A tumble of dirt and a small green seed packet slid toward the edge. Ollie’s heart thumped. “Oh no!”

“We can do it,” Ollie said, voice calm and brave. “We stay safe. We work together.”

Mina grabbed the blanket. “We can catch the dirt!”

Pip flew fast. “I will hold the packet!”

Ollie ran, paws pat-pat on the roof. He slid the blanket under the dirt. Thump! The soil fell safely on the soft cloth. Pip swooped and caught the seed packet in his beak.

But the box was still wobbly. Ollie’s whiskers twitched. He took another deep breath. “One… two… three.” He used the string and tape again, tying the flower box to the railing just like the pool. Mina smoothed the soil back in with careful paws. Pip read the packet label. “Coo! Carrot seeds.”

Ollie smiled. “Let’s plant them together.” They poked tiny holes. Plink, plink went the seeds. They patted the soil. They watered gently. The rain began to tap-tap on the roof, cool and soft.

The three friends sat under a little awning and shared snacks. Ollie opened the last pocket of the backpack and pulled out extra crackers and apple slices. “I brought more to share,” he said.

“Thank you,” Mina squeaked. “You are generous.”

Pip nodded. “Coo! You thought ahead.”

Ollie munched and watched the rain make tiny rings in the pool. He felt tired and proud. “Today was hard,” he said. “But we kept trying. We had a plan. We helped each other.”

The rain slowed. Sunlight peeked out and made a small rainbow over the pool. The wind turned gentle. The chimes sang a happy song.

Mina pointed. “Our plant stands tall.”

Pip flapped. “Our seeds are safe.”

Ollie wiggled his tail. “We were resilient. We were ready. We were kind.”

They held paws and wings. “Teamwork!” they cheered.

Then Ollie said, “Friends, what did we learn?”

Mina smiled. “When things go wrong, we can breathe and try again.”

Pip added, “We can share and help.”

Ollie nodded. “And we can prepare, so we are safe.”

They looked over the bright, clean rooftop. The pool was steady. The flower box was snug. The air smelled like rain and green leaves. Ollie felt warm inside.

“We did it,” Ollie said softly. “We keep trying. We work together. We give and share.”

The rooftop sparkled. The day felt new again.

The End.
