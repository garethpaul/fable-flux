# Splash the Dolphin's Recycling Adventure

The basement lab was full of interesting sounds. Beep, beep, beep went the computers. Bubble, bubble, bubble went the big water tank in the corner. And splash, splash, splash went Splash the dolphin as she swam in circles.

"Good morning, Splash!" called Dr. Marina as she walked down the wooden stairs. Her lab coat was white and clean. She carried a big bucket that smelled fishy. "Are you ready for breakfast?"

Splash popped her gray head above the water. "Click, click!" she said happily. That was dolphin talk for "Yes, please!"

But when Dr. Marina got closer, Splash saw something that made her sad. The bucket was full of yucky plastic bottles and bags floating with the fish. The plastic looked dirty and smelled bad.

"Oh no!" said Dr. Marina. "These plastic pieces got mixed up with your fish again. The ocean cleaning robot brought them by mistake."

Splash's big, round eyes looked worried. She knew plastic was not good for sea animals like her. Fish and dolphins could get sick if they ate plastic by accident.

"Click, click, click!" said Splash. She wanted to help fix this problem.

Dr. Marina scratched her head. "Hmm, we need to think about this carefully. How can we keep the plastic separate from the fish?"

Splash swam in a circle, thinking hard. Then she had an idea! She used her nose to push three empty containers to the edge of her tank. One was blue, one was green, and one was red.

"What are you thinking, Splash?" asked Dr. Marina.

Splash pointed her nose at the blue container, then at the plastic bottles. Then she pointed at the green container and the plastic bags. Finally, she pointed at the red container and the fish.

"Oh!" said Dr. Marina, clapping her hands. "You want to sort everything into different containers! That's brilliant logical thinking!"

Working together, they carefully sorted everything. Dr. Marina picked up each item and showed it to Splash. When Splash saw a plastic bottle, she would splash toward the blue container. When she saw a plastic bag, she would splash toward the green container. When she saw a fish, she would splash toward the red container.

"Splash, splash, splash!" went the water as Splash moved back and forth. She was using great coordination to point the right way each time.

Soon, all the plastic bottles were in the blue container. All the plastic bags were in the green container. And all the clean, fresh fish were in the red container.

"Now the plastic can go to the recycling center," explained Dr. Marina. "There, people will clean it and make it into new things like toys or clothes. Nothing gets wasted!"

Splash clapped her flippers together. She felt proud that she helped solve the problem.

Dr. Marina gave Splash her clean fish for breakfast. "You know what, Splash? By sorting and recycling, we're taking care of our ocean home. When we reuse things instead of throwing them away, we help keep the water clean for all sea animals."

"Click, click!" agreed Splash as she munched her tasty fish.

From that day on, Splash and Dr. Marina always sorted things carefully. They put plastic in recycling containers, food scraps in compost bins, and kept everything organized. The basement lab became the cleanest, most sustainable lab in the whole building.

And whenever other scientists visited, Splash would show them her sorting game. Soon, everyone was recycling and reusing things, just like the smart dolphin taught them.

Splash learned that even small actions, like sorting and recycling, could make a big difference for the planet. And the best part was, it was fun to do when you worked together as a team!

The End.
