# Pebble the Penguin and the Fair Share Feast

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a wiggly walk. One sunny afternoon, Pebble waddled into the dining room. The room smelled like warm bread and sweet berries. The table was big and round. It was set with napkins, little cups, and a basket full of shiny paper, glue sticks, and colored yarn.

“Today is Craft-and-Snack Day!” Pebble chirped. “We will make placemats and then have a fair feast!”

Pebble’s friends came in: Mia the mouse, Tiko the turtle, and Bibi the bluebird. Everyone was excited. The sunlight made a soft golden glow on the wooden chairs. Pebble clapped little flippers. “Let’s make placemats so everyone has a spot. Then we will share snacks the fair way.”

They all sat at the table. Pebble passed out big sheets of paper that felt smooth and cool. “One for you,” Pebble said to Mia. “One for you,” to Tiko. “One for you,” to Bibi. Pebble smiled. “And one for me.”

They cut yarn with snip-snip sounds and glued paper shapes with sticky, sweet-smelling glue. “I’m making stars!” Mia squeaked. “I’m making waves,” said Tiko, pressing blue yarn into swirls. “I’m making feathery prints,” chirped Bibi, tapping soft blue feathers onto her mat.

Pebble made a placemat with a big heart in the middle. Pebble hummed, happy and proud. Then Pebble noticed the snack bowls on the counter: a bowl of juicy red strawberries, a bowl of crunchy crackers, and a bowl of tiny cheese cubes that smelled creamy and mild.

Pebble brought the bowls to the table. “We will share,” Pebble said. “Everyone gets the same. That is fair.”

Just then, Bibi fluttered her wings and said, “I want a lot! I want all the strawberries!” Bibi scooped a big handful and piled them on her placemat, the sweet smell filling the air.

Mia’s whiskers drooped. “I love strawberries too,” she whispered.

Tiko blinked slowly. “I did not get any yet,” he said.

Pebble’s tummy felt twisty. The room grew quiet. Pebble took a deep breath and felt the cool air. Pebble looked at the bowls and at the placemats. Pebble wanted everyone to feel happy and safe.

Pebble stood tall on tip-claws. “Friends,” Pebble said gently, “we need fairness. Justice means being fair and making sure everyone gets what is right. It is not just about wanting. It is about what is fair for all.”

Bibi looked at her pile of strawberries. She shuffled her feet. “But I was hungry,” she said softly.

Pebble nodded. “I hear you, Bibi. You are hungry. We can help you. But we also help Mia and Tiko. We can fix this together. We can make a fair plan.”

“How?” asked Mia, ears perking up.

Pebble pointed to the placemats. “We made a placemat for each friend. Now we will make little sharing squares.” Pebble held up shiny paper. “We will craft square cards to measure the snacks. Each friend gets two squares of strawberries, two squares of crackers, and two squares of cheese.”

“That sounds neat,” said Tiko, smiling.

They cut small squares. Snip-snip. The paper felt crisp in their hands. Pebble drew tiny pictures on each square: a red dot for strawberries, a tan dot for crackers, and a yellow dot for cheese. The markers smelled a little like fruit.

“Now we count,” Pebble said. “One square for Mia, one square for Tiko, one square for Bibi, one square for Pebble.” They placed the squares in a circle, going around the table. The snacks made soft plop sounds as they were set down.

Bibi watched. Her eyes grew wide. “I took too much before,” she said. “It was not fair. I am sorry.”

Pebble smiled warmly. “Thank you for saying sorry. Saying sorry is helpful. Returning what we took is helpful too.” Pebble gently moved extra strawberries back to the bowl. Bibi helped, her wings brushing the air with soft whooshes.

Soon, each placemat had the same: a bright little pile of strawberries, a crunchy stack of crackers, and a neat row of cheese cubes. The table looked colorful and calm.

Pebble clapped. “Now we all have a fair share. This is justice. We listen. We help. We make it right.”

They ate together. Crunch, munch, chew. The strawberries were juicy and cool on their tongues. The crackers crackled. The cheese was soft and creamy. Everyone smiled.

After the snacks, Pebble had one more idea. “Let’s craft Justice Stars,” Pebble said. Pebble cut out shiny star shapes. “We give a star when someone is fair and helpful.” Pebble handed a star to Bibi. “For saying sorry and helping fix the problem.”

Bibi beamed. “Thank you. Next time, I will ask, ‘Is this fair?’”

Pebble nodded. “That is a good question. Justice asks, ‘Is this fair for everyone?’”

They hung the Justice Stars on a ribbon across the dining room. The stars twinkled in the soft light. The room felt warm and kind.

Mia giggled. “I like fair sharing.”

Tiko patted his shell. “I feel safe and happy.”

Pebble waddled in a circle, laughing. “Today we learned about justice. We used our crafts to help us be fair. We listened, we shared, and we made it right together.”

Everyone cheered. “Hooray for fairness! Hooray for helping!”

Pebble smiled and felt light as a bubble. “When we are fair,” Pebble said, “everyone shines.”

The End.
