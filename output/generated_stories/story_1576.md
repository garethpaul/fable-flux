# Rusty the Robot Dog Takes Turns

Click-click. Whirr-whirr. Rusty the robot dog wagged his shiny metal tail. He lived in a cozy garden shed that smelled like fresh dirt and wood. Sunlight peeked through a little window. The floor felt cool and smooth. Tools hung on hooks, and a small red broom leaned in the corner.

“Good morning!” beeped Rusty in his friendly robot voice. “I am ready to help!”

Outside, children laughed. Mia and Ben ran in with muddy boots. Mrs. Lee followed, holding a basket of flower seeds. “We want to plant a flower bed today,” said Mrs. Lee. “The garden will be bright and happy.”

Rusty’s blue eyes glowed. “I can help! I love to help the garden. Community service is my favorite.”

Mia picked up a little shovel. “I want to dig first!” she said.

Ben grabbed the watering can. “I want to water now!” he said.

Rusty looked at the tools. He wanted to use his soft brush tail to sweep the floor, and he wanted to carry the seed basket with his gentle robot mouth. He wanted to do everything. His gears hummed. Click-click.

Mrs. Lee smiled. “Friends, we will take turns,” she said. “We will share the tools. Taking turns is fair.”

Mia frowned a little. Ben puffed his cheeks. Rusty’s tail slowed. He remembered his honesty chip, the one that helped him tell the truth and do the right thing. “Integrity means doing what is right,” he beeped softly. “Taking turns is right.”

They made a simple plan. Mia would dig first, Ben would water second, and Rusty would sweep and carry seeds after. Then they would switch. “We can go one-two-three,” said Rusty. “We can say ‘My turn, your turn, our turn.’”

Mia dug the soft soil. Scritch-scritch. The dirt smelled rich and sweet. Ben waited and watched, but his feet wiggled. Rusty waited too. Waiting was hard. His motor buzzed. He took a deep breath. “Whirr-in, whirr-out.”

“Is it my turn yet?” Ben asked.

“Almost,” said Rusty. “Thank you for waiting. You are doing great.”

When Mia finished a little row, she handed the shovel to Ben. “Your turn,” she said.

Ben smiled. “Thank you!” He gently watered the soil. Drip-drop. Drip-drop. The water sparkled in the sun. Rusty wanted to water too. He wanted to splash. He waited. He wagged. He whispered, “Taking turns, taking turns.”

Now it was Rusty’s turn to help. He lifted the seed basket with care. “I will carry,” he beeped. “I will sweep.” He trotted into the shed. Swish-swish went his brush tail, sweeping leaves into a neat pile. The shed smelled cleaner. He felt proud.

Suddenly, there was a problem. The little shovel bent with a squeak. “Oh no,” said Mia. “It’s a bit broken.”

Ben looked worried. “We still need to plant seeds. What do we do?”

Rusty set down the basket. “We have a problem,” he said. “We can solve it. We will keep trying.” His persistence light blinked. He checked the toolbox. Click. He found a tiny wrench and some tape.

Mrs. Lee knelt beside them. “We need to be honest,” she said. “Who used the shovel when it bent?”

Mia looked down at her muddy hands. “I did,” she said in a small voice. “I pushed too hard. I’m sorry.”

Rusty nodded. “Thank you for telling the truth,” he beeped. “That is integrity.”

Ben touched Mia’s arm. “It’s okay,” he said. “We can fix it together.”

Rusty held the shovel steady. Mia held the wrench. Ben held the tape. Together they went turn by turn. “My turn to twist,” said Mia. Twist-twist. “My turn to tape,” said Ben. Smooth-smooth. “My turn to test,” beeped Rusty. Tap-tap. The shovel was straight again. Not brand new, but strong.

“Teamwork!” cheered Mrs. Lee. “You kept going. You were honest. You helped the garden. That is community service.”

They returned to the flower bed. Now they took turns planting seeds. Mia dropped seeds into little pockets of soil. Plink-plink. Ben covered them gently. Pat-pat. Rusty watered with the softest drip-drop from his careful mouth. Each turn felt calm. Each turn felt kind.

A tiny breeze brushed their cheeks. Birds chirped. The garden smelled like hope. Rusty hummed a happy tune. “Taking turns is like a dance,” he beeped. “Step, step, share.”

When the last seed was planted, Rusty swept the shed again. Swish-swish. The tools were clean. The floor was neat. The shovel rested on a hook with a small note: “Please be gentle.”

Mia smiled. “We did it!”

Ben nodded. “We helped the garden and we helped each other.”

Rusty wagged his shiny tail. “Yes,” he said. “Today we learned to take turns. We told the truth. We served our garden.”

Mrs. Lee clapped softly. “Look at our work,” she said. “When we take turns, everyone gets a chance. When we are honest, we grow trust. When we help, our whole community blooms.”

Rusty’s eyes glowed warm and bright. “Tomorrow,” he beeped, “we will water in turns again. My turn, your turn, our turn.”

They all looked at the soft, dark soil. They could almost see the flowers waking up under the ground.

“Good night, seeds,” whispered Mia.

“See you soon,” said Ben.

“Rest and grow,” beeped Rusty.

Click-click. Whirr-whirr. The garden shed felt cozy and calm. The day had a happy, tidy ending.

Lesson: Taking turns is fair and kind. Being honest shows integrity. Helping together makes our community strong.

The End.
