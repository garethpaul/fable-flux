# Gigi's Garden Day

Gigi the giraffe loved her beautiful balcony garden. Every morning, she would stretch her long neck up high and look at all the colorful flowers and green plants that needed her care.

"Good morning, little garden!" Gigi said cheerfully. "Today is going to be a wonderful day!"

But as Gigi looked around, she felt a little worried. There were so many things to do! The tomatoes needed watering, the flowers needed checking, and she still needed to wash her hooves and brush her teeth.

"Oh my," Gigi sighed. "How will I get everything done?"

Gigi's friend Benny the butterfly fluttered over. "You look worried, Gigi. What's wrong?"

"I have so much to do today," Gigi explained. "I don't know where to start!"

Benny smiled. "My grandmother butterfly taught me something special. She said the best way to get things done is to make a plan and do one thing at a time."

"Really?" Gigi's ears perked up. "How do I do that?"

"First, think about what's most important," Benny suggested. "What do you need to do to take care of yourself?"

Gigi thought carefully. "Well, I should wash my hooves and brush my teeth to stay clean and healthy."

"Perfect!" Benny clapped his tiny wings. "That's taking care of yourself first. What's next?"

"The plants look thirsty," Gigi noticed. "I should water them before it gets too hot."

"Great thinking!" Benny encouraged. "And after that?"

"I can check on my flowers and maybe plant some new seeds," Gigi said, feeling more confident.

Gigi started with her morning routine. She went to the little sink in her garden and carefully washed her hooves with warm, soapy water. The bubbles tickled and smelled like lavender.

"Scrub, scrub, scrub," she sang happily. "Clean hooves help me feel my best!"

Next, she brushed her teeth with her soft toothbrush, making sure to reach all the way to the back. "Brush, brush, brush," she hummed. "Healthy teeth make me smile bright!"

Feeling fresh and clean, Gigi picked up her special watering can. It was painted with rainbow stripes and had a long spout perfect for her tall neck to reach.

"Time to water my garden friends," she said.

She walked slowly around her balcony, giving each plant just the right amount of water. The soil smelled rich and earthy. The plants seemed to dance as the cool water touched their roots.

"Thank you, Gigi!" the tomato plant seemed to whisper in the breeze.

"You're welcome!" Gigi giggled.

Finally, Gigi checked each flower carefully. She found three new buds ready to bloom and spotted a tiny caterpillar who needed to be moved to a safe leaf.

"Hello, little caterpillar," she said gently, using a leaf to help him move. "Here's a nice spot for you."

As the sun climbed higher, Gigi sat back and admired her work. Her garden sparkled with water droplets, her hooves were clean, and her teeth felt smooth and fresh.

"Benny was right," Gigi said as her butterfly friend returned. "When I made a plan and did one thing at a time, everything got done perfectly!"

"And you didn't feel worried anymore, did you?" Benny asked.

"Not at all!" Gigi smiled her biggest smile. "Taking care of myself first gave me energy for everything else. And doing one job at a time made it all feel easy and fun!"

From that day on, Gigi always started her morning by making a simple plan. She learned that taking time to do things properly made every day better and more enjoyable.

The End.
