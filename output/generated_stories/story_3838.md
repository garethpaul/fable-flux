# Benny Bunny’s Busy Sunny Day

Benny Bunny woke up in a sunny backyard. The grass tickled his paws. Warm sun touched his ears. Birds sang, “Tweet-tweet!” The air smelled like sweet flowers. Benny stretched and smiled. “Today is a big day,” he said. “I will pick carrots, water the flowers, and play with my friends.”

Benny was a determined rabbit. When Benny made a plan, he tried his best. He hopped to his little chalkboard by the garden gate. He picked up a blue chalk. “First, carrots,” he said. He drew a little carrot. “Next, water flowers.” He drew a droplet. “Then, playtime!” He drew a smiling sun.

Just then, his friend Tilly Turtle poked her head from the soft grass. “Good morning, Benny,” said Tilly in her slow, gentle voice. “Will you play tag with me later?”

“Yes!” said Benny. His nose twitched with joy. “I have work first. But I will make time for you. Let’s play after I pick carrots and water the flowers.”

Tilly smiled. “I will come back when the sun is a little higher,” she said, pointing with her tiny foot.

Benny hopped to the carrot patch. The soil was dark and cool. He dug with his soft paws. Scritch, scritch. He pulled up a bright orange carrot. Pop! “One carrot,” he said. He pulled two. Pop! Pop! “Two carrots.” He pulled more. Pop, pop, pop! He was careful. He put the carrots in a neat basket. “I am doing my job,” he said proudly.

Then Benny looked at the flowers. The daisies were white and sunny. The marigolds were bright and golden. The watering can was heavy. Benny puffed. “I can do this,” he said. He lifted the can and poured a tiny rain. Splish-splash. The flowers swayed. “Thank you,” they seemed to say.

Just as Benny finished watering, a gentle breeze brushed his whiskers. His friend Dotty Duck waddled by, her soft feathers shining. “Quack! Benny, will you help me find my missing blue pebble by the pond?” she asked, her eyes worried.

Benny looked at his chalkboard. Carrots: done. Water: done. Playtime: next. “I planned to play with Tilly,” he said. “But friends help friends. If I use my time well, I can help both.”

Benny thought. He tapped his paw and felt the warm ground. “Let’s look for ten minutes,” he said kindly. “Then I will meet Tilly. We can look together too!”

Dotty smiled. “Quack, thank you!”

Benny and Dotty went to the pond. The water was cool and glassy. Frogs croaked, “Ribbit.” Benny sniffed. He looked under a leaf. He looked near a smooth stone. He listened to the soft wind. “Blue pebble, where are you?” he whispered. Then he saw a flash of blue by a clover. “Dotty, look!” he cried.

“Quack! You found it!” Dotty hugged the pebble with her wing. “Thank you, Benny.”

Benny checked the sun. It was a little higher now. “Time to meet Tilly,” he said. “Do you want to come play too?”

“Quack, yes!” said Dotty. “Friends can play together.”

They hurried back to the garden. Tilly was waiting near the marigolds, nibbling a leaf of lettuce. “Hello! I am ready,” she said with a warm smile.

“I am sorry I am a little late,” said Benny. “Dotty needed help. I planned my time and I came as fast as I could.”

Tilly nodded. “Friends help friends. I am glad you came.”

Benny clapped his paws. “Let’s plan our play. First, one game of tag. Then, a quick snack. Then, we put the carrots away together. The sun will still be shining, and we can have story time under the big tree.”

“Quack! That sounds good,” said Dotty.

“Slow and steady is good too,” said Tilly.

They played tag on the soft grass. Benny hopped fast. Tilly moved slow but clever. Dotty waddled and flapped her little wings, laughing, “Quack-quack!” Benny watched the sun and counted. “One minute, two minutes, three,” he said. When they finished the game, they all took deep breaths. The air smelled like mint and fresh earth.

“Snack time,” said Benny. He passed crunchy carrot sticks. Crunch, crunch. Dotty shared little seeds. Tilly shared sweet clover. They ate and giggled.

Then Benny pointed to the basket. “Team time,” he said. Together they carried the carrots to the shade. The basket felt lighter with three friends. They stacked the carrots neatly. “Job done,” said Benny. He drew a big happy check mark on the chalkboard.

At last they curled up under the big tree. Leaves whispered above. Sunlight made dappled patterns. Benny read a small book about kind words. His voice was soft and warm. Tilly closed her eyes, smiling. Dotty rested her head on her blue pebble.

Benny felt proud and calm. “Today I made a plan. I did my jobs. I helped a friend. I made time to play,” he said.

Tilly nodded. “You were determined and kind.”

Dotty quacked softly. “And we did it together.”

Benny smiled. His heart felt like sunshine. “Friends share time. Friends help. When we plan and work together, there is time for work and play,” he said.

The sun glowed golden. The backyard was peaceful and bright. Benny tucked his chalk by the gate. “Tomorrow we can plan again,” he said. “Together.”

The End.
