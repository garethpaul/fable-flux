# Bubbles in the Garden Shed

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with bright, curious eyes. One sunny day, a big rain cloud drifted away, and the world smelled fresh and clean. Bubbles wriggled his shiny tail and squeaked, “Eee-eee! Let’s play!”

But there was no ocean nearby. There was a small house, a grassy yard, and a little garden shed with a creaky wooden door. The door was open just a crack.

Bubbles peeked inside. “Hello?” he called softly.

The garden shed was cool and shadowy. Sunlight peeped through dusty windows in tiny golden lines. It smelled like soil, leaves, and a little bit like sweet apples. Shelves held flower pots, soft gloves, and colorful string. A broom stood tall like a quiet tree.

Bubbles swam through the air the way dolphins do in stories and dreams. He pretended the floor was a calm blue sea. “Splash, splash,” he whispered, flicking his tail. He imagined the rake was a sea dragon, the watering can was a friendly whale, and the broom—oh!—the broom was a tall sea tower.

Just then, a small voice came from a corner. “Please be careful.”

Bubbles turned. A tiny snail with a shiny shell peeked out from a clay pot. “I’m Dot,” said the snail. “This shed is my home.”

Bubbles blushed. “Oh! Hello, Dot. I didn’t know. I will be gentle.”

Dot smiled. “Thank you. You can play, but please don’t knock the pots.”

Bubbles nodded. “I will respect your home.”

Bubbles began a game. “Let’s imagine!” he squeaked. “The floor is the sea. The broom is a lighthouse. The string is a rainbow rope.”

Dot’s eyes sparkled. “I like that.”

Bubbles swam slowly so the air made a soft whoosh. He clicked and hummed a tune. He balanced a little glove on his nose like a circus trick. “Ta-da!”

Dot clapped his snail feelers. “Bravo!”

Suddenly, the creaky door moved. Crrreeeak. A gust of wind puffed in. The wind pushed the watering can. It tipped and rolled. Clatter! The can bumped a stack of pots. The pots wobbled. Wobble, wobble—oh no! One pot slid toward the edge.

Dot gasped. “My nest! My nest is under there!”

Bubbles felt a squeeze in his chest. “We need a plan,” he said. “Let’s solve this.”

He took a deep breath. “First, we keep calm. Second, we work together.”

Dot nodded. “How can I help?”

Bubbles squinted at the wobbly pots. He imagined the broom was not a tower now, but a brave helper. He whispered to himself, “Imagination helps me think.”

“Dot,” Bubbles said, “can you guide me? Tell me which pot is safest to touch.”

Dot looked carefully. “Move the small blue pot first. It’s light.”

Bubbles slid under the shelf like a silver fish. He used his smooth nose to nudge the small blue pot back to safety. Tap. The big pot still wobbled.

Dot pointed with a feeler. “Now the red one. Slowly.”

Bubbles hummed a steady song to keep his movements gentle. “Mmm-mmm-mmm.” He nudged the red pot. Tap. Still wobbly! The biggest pot leaned and leaned, ready to fall.

Bubbles imagined the rainbow rope could hold the pot. He grabbed the colorful string with his fins. “Dot, can you anchor the string around that nail?”

Dot slid to the nail and looped the string with careful twists. “Like this?”

“Yes!” said Bubbles. He wrapped the string around the big pot like a hug. Together, they pulled—slowly, slowly—until the pot settled back in place. The wobble stopped.

Dot sighed with relief. “My nest is safe.”

Bubbles smiled. “We did it!”

But the door still swung open, and the wind whooshed in again. The watering can rattled. “We need to close the door,” Bubbles said, “so your home stays safe.”

Dot looked at the heavy door. “It is too big for me.”

Bubbles thought. He imagined he was a mighty ocean wave. “I can push with my tail,” he said. He swam forward, pressed his tail against the door, and pushed. Crrrk. The door moved a little.

Dot added, “I can help by holding the door latch.”

“Great idea,” said Bubbles. “Helping together makes us strong.”

Bubbles pushed again. Whoosh. The door slid into place. Dot clicked the latch. Click!

The shed became cozy and quiet. Dust sparkles danced in the sunlight. The wind stayed outside.

Dot smiled up at Bubbles. “Thank you for helping and for respecting my home.”

Bubbles bowed playfully. “Thank you for trusting me. And thank you for helping me think. My imagination helped us make a plan.”

Dot nodded. “Imagination is a smart tool.”

Bubbles giggled. “And we used teamwork, too.”

They celebrated with a gentle game. Bubbles pretended to be a soft rain cloud and made “pitter-patter” sounds on the glove. Dot pretended to be a brave boat, gliding along the shelf. They took turns, asked before moving things, and put everything back when they were done.

When it was time to go, Bubbles said, “Good-bye, Dot. I will always be careful in your home. I will always ask first.”

Dot waved a tiny feeler. “Come play again. Bring your big imagination. We can make safe, kind stories together.”

Bubbles swam out into the sunny day, feeling warm and proud. He whispered, “I can imagine. I can respect others. I can help.”

And the garden shed, quiet and tidy, seemed to hum, “Thank you.”

The End.
