# Cleo the Clever Cat Chooses to Help

Cleo was a clever cat with soft gray fur and bright green eyes. Her whiskers twitched when she listened, and her tail curled like a question mark when she thought. Cleo loved music. She loved the soft hum of the piano, the warm boom of the drum, and the shiny ting of the triangle.

One sunny afternoon, Cleo padded into the musical practice room. The room smelled like polished wood and lemon oil. Sunlight made golden patches on the floor. Posters of notes and instruments hung on the walls. There was a big piano, a set of drums, a row of tiny violins, and a basket full of shakers and bells.

“Hello, friends!” Cleo said with a purr.

“Hi, Cleo!” said Bella Bunny, hugging a drumstick. “We have a big music show today.”

Cleo looked around. Everyone was busy. Timmy Turtle was trying to tune a tiny violin. His little hands moved slow. Mia Mouse was carrying a stack of music papers that were too tall. The papers wiggled and slipped. Benny Bear was tapping a drum, but he looked worried. Lila Lamb stood by the piano, flipping pages back and forth.

Cleo’s tail curled. She wanted to play the triangle. She could see it on the table. It was shiny and silver and made a lovely ting. But another thought tickled her whiskers. Her friends needed help.

Cleo took a deep breath. She listened. Tap-tap, rustle-rustle, flip-flip. She heard the sounds of friends who needed a helper. Cleo knew she had to make a choice.

“I want to play,” Cleo whispered to herself. “But I can also help. What is the best choice?”

She closed her eyes for a moment. Her ears stood tall. “I will listen first,” she decided. “Listening will help me choose.”

Cleo walked to Mia Mouse. “Mia, what do you need?” she asked gently.

“Eep! I need to carry these papers to the piano,” Mia squeaked. “But they are sliding!”

“I can help,” Cleo said with a smile. She spread her soft paws and steadied the stack. She walked slowly with Mia. The paper smelled like fresh ink. The edges tickled Cleo’s whiskers. Together, they put the music on the piano.

“Thank you,” Mia said. “You are kind.”

Cleo felt warm inside. “Kindness is a good choice,” she thought.

Next, Cleo padded to Timmy Turtle. The violin made a squeaky ee-eee. Timmy frowned. “I can’t tune it,” he said softly. “I’m too slow.”

Cleo crouched to listen to the string. She tapped it with her claw—ping! “Let’s try together,” she said. “We can listen and turn, little by little.”

Timmy turned the peg. Cleo listened. Ping… piiiing… ping! At last, the sound felt smooth like warm soup. Timmy smiled. “We did it!”

Cleo purred. “Listening is helpful,” she said. “Listening helps me make good choices.”

She heard a thud and a sigh. Benny Bear sat by the drum. “I can’t remember the beat,” Benny said, rubbing his nose. “Boom-boom-bah? Or boom-bah-boom?”

Cleo sat next to him. “Let’s clap it first,” she said. “Clap with me. Clap-clap-clap… pause.” They clapped together. The rhythm bounced like a little ball. Benny grinned. “Oh! I hear it now.” He tapped the drum. Boom-boom-bah! The sound was deep and friendly.

“Thank you, Cleo,” Benny rumbled. “You shared your time.”

Cleo’s whiskers quivered. “Sharing time is generous,” she thought. “It feels good to help.”

Lila Lamb waved from the piano. “Cleo, I need someone to turn pages during the show,” she said. “Will you volunteer?”

Cleo looked at the shiny triangle again. She really, really wanted to play. Ting-ting! But she looked at Lila’s hopeful eyes. She thought about the papers, the violin, the drum. She remembered how good it felt to help.

Cleo put a paw over her heart. “I will volunteer,” she said. “I can turn pages and listen for the right time.”

Lila smiled. “Thank you! Your good listening will be perfect.”

Soon, it was time for the show. The chairs were filled with friends and families. The room smelled like popcorn. The lights were warm and soft. Cleo sat by the piano, paws ready. Her tail curled with care.

Lila began to play. The notes were like raindrops. Cleo watched the music lines run across the page. When it was time, Cleo turned the page—flip! Lila kept playing, smooth and steady. Benny tapped the drum with confidence. Timmy played his tiny violin, sweet and clear. Mia passed out little bells to the children in the front row.

Between songs, the triangle waited on the table. Cleo looked at it. She took a deep breath and made another choice. She whispered to Benny, “Would you like a turn on the triangle for the next song?” Benny nodded, eyes bright. Cleo smiled and handed it over.

The last song filled the room like warm sunshine. At the very end, Benny tinged the triangle—ting! Everyone clapped and cheered. Cleo’s heart glowed.

After the show, Lila hugged Cleo. “You made wonderful choices today,” she said. “You listened. You were kind. You were generous. You volunteered to help.”

Cleo purred loudly. “It felt good to think and choose,” she said. “I learned that listening helps me decide. And helping makes the music better for everyone.”

Mia, Timmy, and Benny nodded. “Cleo, you are a clever cat,” they said together.

Cleo’s tail curled into a happy exclamation point. She looked at her friends and the shiny instruments. “Next time,” she said, “I will listen, think, and choose again.”

And she knew she would.

The End.
