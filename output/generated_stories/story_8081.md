# Ellie's Colorful Discovery

Ellie the elephant calf loved the bright art room at her school. The walls were covered with paintings in every color of the rainbow. She could smell the sweet scent of crayons and hear the gentle *squish-squish* of paintbrushes in water cups.

Today was special - it was International Art Day! All the children would paint pictures of their families and share them with the class.

Ellie picked up a gray crayon with her trunk. She began drawing her elephant family on the big white paper. "Gray elephants are the most beautiful," she thought to herself.

But when Ellie looked around the room, she saw something that made her trunk droop. Maya the monkey was painting her family bright orange. Leo the lion was using golden yellow for his family. Zara the zebra painted black and white stripes.

"That's all wrong!" Ellie said loudly. "Families should look the same! They should all be gray like mine!"

The other children stopped painting. Maya's big brown eyes looked sad. Leo's whiskers drooped. Zara's ears flattened against her head.

"But Ellie," said Maya softly, "my family has beautiful orange fur. That's how we look."

"And my family has golden manes," added Leo, his voice quiet.

Mrs. Owl, their teacher, flew over with her gentle wings. "Ellie, dear, what seems to be the problem?"

"Everyone's painting their families wrong colors!" Ellie trumpeted. "They don't look like my family at all!"

Mrs. Owl's wise eyes twinkled. "Come with me, Ellie. Let's look at our friendship garden outside."

Through the big window, Ellie could see the school garden. There were red roses, yellow sunflowers, purple violets, and white daisies.

"Tell me, Ellie," said Mrs. Owl, "which flower is the right color?"

Ellie thought hard, swinging her trunk back and forth. "Well... they're all pretty colors."

"Exactly!" hooted Mrs. Owl. "Each flower is beautiful in its own special way. Just like our families!"

Ellie looked back at her friends' paintings. Maya's orange monkey family was swinging happily from painted trees. Leo's golden lion family looked strong and proud. Zara's striped zebra family was running across a sunny field.

"Oh!" gasped Ellie. "They really are beautiful! Each family is special and different!"

Ellie walked over to her friends with small, careful steps. "Maya, Leo, Zara... I'm sorry I said your families were wrong. Your paintings are wonderful!"

Maya's face lit up like sunshine. "Thank you, Ellie! Your gray elephant family is beautiful too!"

"We're all different, and that makes us special!" said Leo with a happy roar.

"Let's paint a big picture together!" suggested Zara, bouncing on her hooves.

The four friends worked together on a huge piece of paper. They painted elephants, monkeys, lions, and zebras all playing together in a colorful meadow. Red flowers, blue sky, green grass, and a bright yellow sun smiled down on everyone.

When they finished, Ellie stepped back and clapped her ears together. "Look how beautiful it is when we're all different colors!"

Mrs. Owl hung their friendship painting on the special wall. "This shows that celebrating our differences makes the world more beautiful," she said.

From that day on, Ellie loved seeing all the different colors and shapes of her friends and their families. She learned that being different wasn't wrong - it was wonderful!

The End.
