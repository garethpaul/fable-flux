# Bear Cub Builds a Rooftop Garden

On a sunny morning, a friendly bear cub named Bibi climbed the stairs with a small backpack. Step, step, step! She pushed open a door and felt a warm breeze. She was on the rooftop. The roof felt bumpy under her paws, and she smelled fresh air and a hint of flowers from far away.

“Hello, rooftop!” Bibi giggled. “Today, I will build a tiny garden.”

Bibi opened her backpack. Inside were little pots, a small trowel, a roll of twine, and a bag of seeds. She also had a water bottle that went slosh, slosh. She set everything down and hummed a happy tune.

Just then, a flutter of wings brushed her ear. “Whoosh!” A pigeon landed and tilted its head. “Coo?”

“Hi, Pio,” Bibi said kindly. “I remember you! I’m building a garden. It will be a calm, green place.”

Pio puffed his chest. “Coo-coo! I like seeds. Are those for me?”

Bibi smiled. “These seeds are for planting. But we can share. We can make a feeding spot too.”

Pio hopped closer, but a small squeak came from the corner. “Eee! Watch out!” A little mouse peeked from a pipe. “I’m Mimi. Please don’t step on my path.”

Bibi crouched down. “Hi, Mimi. I didn’t see your path. Thank you for telling me.”

Mimi sniffed the air. “What is that smell?”

“Dirt,” Bibi said, patting a bag. “It smells earthy. I want to plant flowers so bees and butterflies can visit. It will be pretty and peaceful.”

Pio ruffled his feathers. “But I want seeds now.”

Mimi wrung her tiny paws. “And I don’t want my path blocked.”

Bibi felt her tummy wobble. She wanted to build, but she wanted everyone to be happy. She took a slow, deep breath. In, out. The breeze tickled her nose. “We can solve this peacefully,” Bibi said. “We can talk and make a plan.”

Pio and Mimi nodded.

“First,” Bibi said, “let’s listen to each other. Pio, what do you need?”

“Coo,” said Pio. “I need a safe place to eat.”

“Mimi, what do you need?” asked Bibi.

“I need a clear path, so I can run home fast,” Mimi squeaked.

“And I need space to plant,” Bibi said. “We all have needs.”

Bibi looked around the rooftop. The floor was wide and sunny. There were corners with shade and a warm, flat spot near a wall. She tapped her paw on the roof. Thump, thump. She thought hard, her ears twitching. “We can be resourceful,” she said. “We can use what we have.”

She found a long, flat board near an old box. She gently moved it. “This can be a bridge for Mimi’s path,” Bibi said. She placed the board so it made a neat, smooth walkway from the pipe to a safe corner. “Try it.”

Mimi scurried across. Tap-tap-tap went her tiny feet. “It feels nice!” she squealed. “And it’s clear!”

Next, Bibi took a small clay saucer from under a pot. “This can be a seed plate for Pio.” She poured a few extra seeds onto the saucer. “We will make a feeding spot over here, away from the garden, so the plants can grow.”

Pio hopped over and pecked. Peck, peck. “Coo! Delicious and safe.”

Bibi smiled. “Now, the garden can be right here by the sunny wall. We will build it together.”

They worked as a team. Bibi scooped soil into pots. The dirt felt cool and crumbly on her paws. Splish! She dribbled a little water. Mimi held the twine with her tiny hands, helping Bibi tie the pots to the railing so they wouldn’t tip. “I’m good at knots,” Mimi said proudly.

Pio flapped up and brought a tiny twig. “For labels?” he cooed.

“Yes!” Bibi said. She used a bit of twine to tie the twig to a pot. “This pot is for blue flowers. This one is for herbs that smell fresh.”

They planted the seeds. Tap, tap, tap went the trowel. Bibi whispered, “Sleep tight, little seeds. Grow strong.” The sun warmed their backs. A soft cloud drifted by like cotton candy. The city hummed below. Honk, whirr, distant laughter.

When they were done, they sat together. The rooftop felt peaceful. The pots stood in a neat row, Mimi’s bridge was clear, and Pio’s seed plate sat in the shade.

Pio cooed, “We solved our problem with talking and sharing.”

Mimi nodded. “And with smart ideas.”

Bibi’s heart felt full. “That is peaceful problem solving,” she said. “We listened, we were kind, and we used what we had. That is teamwork and resourcefulness.”

Days later, tiny green sprouts peeked up. “Hello, little leaves,” Bibi said softly. “We took care of each other, and now we take care of you.”

Pio brought a shiny button he found. “For decoration.” Mimi added a small pebble. Bibi pressed the button and pebble into the soil as garden treasure.

They sat in the warm glow of sunset. The sky turned pink and gold. The air smelled like new leaves and city bread from a bakery below. Bibi squeezed her friends’ paws and wings.

“We made a peaceful plan,” Bibi said. “We built something good together.”

Mimi squeaked, “When we listen, we find space for everyone.”

Pio cooed, “Teamwork makes a happy roof.”

Bibi nodded. “When we have a problem, we can breathe, talk, and share. That is the gentle way.”

The rooftop felt safe, bright, and calm. The garden swayed softly in the breeze, like it was humming, “Thank you.”

The End.
