# Spark, the Helpful Robot Dog

Spark was a helpful robot dog. He had shiny silver ears, a soft whirring tail, and a friendly beep that sounded like “bee-boop!” He lived with Mia in a cozy house. One day, Mia asked, “Spark, can you help me clean the quiet attic?” Spark wagged his tail and said, “Bee-boop! I am ready to help.”

They climbed the creaky wooden stairs. The attic was very quiet. Dust floated like tiny snow. A small window let in golden light. It smelled like old books and warm cedar. Boxes were stacked in funny towers. A red kite peeked out from one box, and a blue scarf lay on the floor.

Mia smiled. “We need to clean and make a cozy reading corner. That is our plan.” Spark nodded. “A plan helps us be responsible. Step one: Look. Step two: Sort. Step three: Put away.”

“Good plan,” Mia said. “We can do it.”

They began “Step one: Look.” Spark scanned with his gentle yellow light. “Bee-boop. I see puzzle pieces, crayons, and a teddy bear.” Mia giggled. “I see my old drum!”

Spark said, “Now, Step two: Sort. We make three piles. Keep, share, and recycle.” Mia clapped. “Yes! That will help us decide.”

They picked up the puzzle pieces. Clink, clink, clink. Spark’s soft paw made a quiet tapping sound on the wooden floor. “Keep,” Mia said. “We can finish this puzzle later.” Spark placed them in a box. “Bee-boop. Puzzle safe.”

They found the drum. Mia tapped it once. Boom. The attic wiggled with sound. Spark wagged. “Drum is loud. Is it for Keep or Share?” Mia thought. She put a finger on her chin. “I think Share. My friend likes music.” Spark nodded. “Good decision.”

Soon they found a problem. A box of storybooks was too heavy for Mia. She tugged and tugged. The box didn’t move. “Oh no,” Mia said. “I want to finish, but this is hard.” Her face felt warm. Tears prickled in her eyes.

Spark sat beside her and beeped softly. “Bee-boop. It is okay to ask for help. Being responsible means we take care of the job and our bodies. We can make a new plan.”

Mia took a deep breath. The air smelled dusty and sweet. “Yes,” she said. “New plan. We will not push. We will move the books little by little.”

Spark smiled with his light-up eyes. “Step one: Open the box. Step two: Carry a few books at a time. Step three: Take a rest when we need it.” Mia nodded. “We can do it together.”

They opened the box. Rustle, rustle. Mia picked up two books. Spark carried one book gently in his mouth. “Bee-boop,” he hummed as they walked slowly to the Keep pile. They went back and forth. Step, step. Place, place. Rest, sip of water. They kept going. Mia felt proud. “We are taking care of our job,” she said. “We are being responsible.”

Spark wagged. “Yes. We planned. We decided. We are kind to ourselves.”

Then they heard a squeak. A little mouse peeked from behind a scarf. It had tiny whiskers and bright eyes. Mia gasped. “Oh! Hello, little friend.” The mouse looked scared. Spark lowered his head and said softly, “Bee-boop. Peaceful plan. We will help the mouse find a cozy spot.”

Mia whispered, “We can make a tiny nest by the window, away from the boxes.” She found a small basket and a bit of soft fabric. Spark nudged the basket gently. The mouse sniffed and climbed in. “There,” Mia said. “Safe and calm.” Spark beeped happily. “We made a peaceful solution.”

At last, the attic looked different. The floor was clear. The puzzle pieces were in a box. The drum was in the Share pile. The books were on a small shelf. A comfy blanket lay in a sunbeam, ready for reading. Mia’s cheeks were rosy. Spark’s tail hummed.

Mia sat on the blanket and hugged Spark. “We did it! We took care of our space.” Spark said, “Bee-boop. That is responsibility.”

Mia nodded. “We made a plan. We made good choices. We asked for help. We were kind.” She smiled and patted the floor. “Let’s read one book before we go.”

They opened a picture book. The pages smelled like paper and sunshine. Mia read slowly. Spark listened, head on her knee, tail softly whirring. Outside, a tiny breeze moved the attic window. The mouse peeked from the basket and twitched its whiskers, happy and quiet.

When the story ended, Mia put the book back on the shelf. She stood up and looked around. “I feel calm,” she said. “The attic feels calm too.”

Spark said, “Bee-boop. Taking care of our things helps everyone. When we plan and decide, we finish our job. That is being responsible.”

Mia took Spark’s paw. “Tomorrow, we will bring labels for the boxes. That is part of our plan.” Spark wagged. “Bee-boop. We will keep going, one small step at a time.”

They turned off the light, but the attic still felt warm and safe. Mia whispered, “Thank you, Spark.” Spark whispered back, “Bee-boop. You did great.”

They went downstairs, proud and peaceful, ready for a snack and a smile.

The End.
