# Ellie's Fair Music Day

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved music more than anything. Her big ears would flap happily whenever she heard a pretty song. Today was her turn to practice piano in the special music room at school.

The music room smelled like fresh flowers and had sunshine streaming through tall windows. Ellie's trunk wiggled with excitement as she walked to the shiny black piano. Her teacher, Mrs. Robin, had given each student thirty minutes to practice.

"I can do this all by myself!" Ellie said proudly, climbing onto the piano bench. Her little legs dangled as she placed her trunk gently on the keys. The notes made beautiful sounds - *ting, ting, tong!*

Just then, Max the monkey swung into the room. "Hey Ellie! Can I practice now? I forgot it was my turn yesterday."

Ellie's ears drooped a little. She had waited all week for her practice time. "But Mrs. Robin said it's my turn now, Max. You had your time yesterday."

Max crossed his arms. "Come on, just let me go first! I'm faster than you anyway."

Ellie felt her tummy get tight and worried. She really wanted to be nice to Max, but she also knew it wasn't fair. She had followed the rules and waited patiently for her turn.

"That's not right, Max," Ellie said in her gentle voice. "Everyone gets the same amount of practice time. That's what makes it fair for all of us."

Max frowned and sat in the corner. "Fine! But I don't like waiting."

Ellie felt sad seeing Max upset, but she knew she was doing the right thing. She turned back to the piano and began playing her favorite song. Her trunk danced across the keys - *plink, plonk, ping!* The music filled the room with happy sounds.

As Ellie played, she noticed Max listening. His foot was tapping along to the beat! When she finished her song, Max clapped his hands.

"That was really pretty, Ellie!" Max said, his frown turning into a small smile.

"Thank you!" Ellie beamed. "Would you like me to teach you this song when it's your turn?"

Max's eyes lit up. "Really? You'd do that for me?"

"Of course! Friends help friends," Ellie said warmly. "But first, I need to finish my practice time. I want to learn the whole song before I can teach it to you."

Ellie spent the rest of her thirty minutes practicing hard. She pressed each key carefully with her trunk, listening to make sure every note sounded just right. Sometimes she made mistakes, but she kept trying until she got it perfect.

When Mrs. Robin's timer went *ding!*, Ellie's practice time was over.

"Great job completing your practice, Ellie!" Mrs. Robin chirped. "Now it's Max's turn."

Ellie hopped down from the bench and gave Max a gentle pat on the back. "Your turn now! And I'll help you learn that song, just like I promised."

Max grinned as he climbed onto the bench. "Thanks for being fair, Ellie. And thanks for waiting to teach me. You're a really good friend."

As Ellie left the music room, her heart felt warm and happy. She had stood up for what was right, finished her important practice, and still found a way to help her friend. Sometimes being fair meant everyone had to wait their turn, but it also meant everyone got their chance to shine.

The End.
