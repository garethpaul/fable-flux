# Pablo the Parrot's Big Idea

Pablo was a bright red parrot with beautiful blue wings. He lived in a cozy home office with his friend Maria. The office had a big wooden desk, colorful books on tall shelves, and a computer that hummed softly.

"Click, click, tap!" went Maria's fingers on the keyboard.

Pablo loved watching Maria work. She drew pictures on her computer and wrote stories. Pablo wished he could help too, but his wings were too small for the keyboard.

"I want to help Maria," Pablo said to himself. "But how can a parrot help with work?"

Every day, Pablo watched from his perch. Maria looked tired. She rubbed her eyes and yawned. Sometimes she forgot to eat lunch because she worked so hard.

"Maria looks so busy," Pablo thought. "She needs help!"

One morning, Pablo had a wonderful idea. His eyes sparkled with excitement. He flapped his colorful wings and squawked happily.

"I know what I can do!" Pablo chirped.

Pablo flew to Maria's desk. He picked up a pencil with his strong beak. The pencil felt smooth and cool. Pablo began to draw on a piece of white paper.

"Scratch, scratch, scratch!" went the pencil.

Pablo drew a big yellow sun. Then he drew Maria with a big smile. He added hearts and flowers all around the picture. His drawing was wobbly but full of love.

"What are you doing, Pablo?" asked Maria, looking up from her computer.

"I made this for you!" Pablo said proudly. "You work so hard. I wanted to help make you smile!"

Maria's face lit up like sunshine. "Oh, Pablo! This is beautiful! You drew this all by yourself?"

"Yes!" Pablo puffed out his chest feathers. "I used my own idea!"

Maria hung Pablo's drawing on the wall next to her desk. The bright colors made the whole office feel happier and warmer.

But Pablo wasn't finished. He had more ideas bubbling in his head like fizzy soda.

The next day, Pablo learned something new. He watched Maria answer the phone. When it rang, Pablo practiced saying "Hello!" His voice was clear and friendly.

"Ring, ring!" went the phone.

"Hello! Maria's office!" Pablo answered cheerfully.

Maria giggled. "Pablo, you're becoming my little helper!"

Pablo felt proud and happy. His chest feathers fluffed up with joy. He had learned that even a small parrot could help in big ways.

From that day on, Pablo helped Maria every day. He organized her colorful pens with his beak. He sang happy songs when Maria felt tired. He even learned to say "Good morning!" to visitors.

"You know what, Pablo?" Maria said one afternoon. "You taught me something important. You didn't wait for someone to tell you how to help. You used your own smart ideas!"

Pablo's heart felt warm and full. "I learned that I can do important things too, even if I'm small!"

Maria smiled and gently stroked Pablo's soft red feathers. "You're right! When we use our own ideas to help others, wonderful things happen."

Pablo looked at his drawing on the wall. He felt proud of himself. He had used his initiative to help his friend, and it felt amazing.

"Tomorrow I'll think of new ways to help!" Pablo chirped happily.

The office felt full of sunshine, friendship, and the joy that comes from taking initiative.

The End.
