# Ellie's Honest Heart

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved helping Mama in the big, cozy pantry. The shelves were filled with colorful jars of honey, bags of sweet apples, and boxes of crunchy crackers. Everything smelled so good!

One sunny morning, Mama asked Ellie to count the red apples while she organized the high shelves.

"One, two, three..." Ellie counted carefully with her trunk. The apples were so shiny and red, they looked like little rubies. Her tummy rumbled loudly. "Grumble, grumble!"

When Mama wasn't looking, Ellie picked up one juicy apple. "Just a tiny bite," she whispered to herself. The apple was sweet and crunchy. Before she knew it, she had eaten the whole thing!

"Ellie, how many apples did you count?" Mama called from the tall ladder.

Ellie's ears drooped. She looked at the basket. There were only nine apples now, but there had been ten before. Her heart felt heavy like a big rock.

"Um... nine apples, Mama," Ellie said quietly, her trunk touching the ground.

Later that day, Ellie's friends Benny Bunny and Rosie Raccoon came to play. They were excited about the apple pie Mama was planning to make for their picnic tomorrow.

"I can't wait to taste Mama Elephant's famous apple pie!" squeaked Benny, hopping up and down.

"She makes the best pies in the whole forest!" chittered Rosie, her fluffy tail swishing happily.

Ellie's stomach felt funny, but not from hunger. She felt sad and worried. What if there weren't enough apples for the pie? What if her friends couldn't have their special treat because of what she did?

That evening, as fireflies danced outside the pantry window, Ellie couldn't sleep. She kept thinking about the missing apple and her friends' excited faces.

"Mama," Ellie said softly, walking to where Mama was reading. "I need to tell you something important."

Mama put down her book and patted her lap. "What is it, little one?"

Ellie climbed up, her trunk trembling. "I... I ate one of the apples today. When I said there were nine, there were really ten before. I'm sorry, Mama. I should have asked first."

Mama wrapped her big, warm trunk around Ellie. "Thank you for telling me the truth, sweetheart. That took a lot of courage."

"But now there might not be enough apples for Benny and Rosie's pie!" Ellie's eyes filled with tears.

Mama smiled gently. "You know what? Let's go pick some fresh apples from our tree right now. We can make an even bigger pie for everyone to share!"

Under the twinkling stars, Mama and Ellie picked the most beautiful apples together. Ellie felt the cool night air on her skin and smelled the sweet apple blossoms.

The next day, when Benny and Rosie arrived for the picnic, they gasped with joy. The apple pie was enormous and golden brown!

"This is the biggest pie ever!" laughed Benny.

"There's enough for the whole forest!" giggled Rosie.

Ellie beamed with happiness. "I helped pick these apples with Mama. Being honest made everything better!"

As they all shared the delicious pie together, Ellie learned that telling the truth, even when it's hard, always leads to the sweetest endings.

The End.
