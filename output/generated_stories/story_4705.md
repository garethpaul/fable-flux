# Polly Plans and the Car-Trip Fix

Polly was a bright green parrot with a sunny yellow beak. She loved to sing, “Squawk-a-doodle-doo!” and make up songs. Today, Polly sat in the family car. The seats were soft and smelled like warm cookies. The air felt cool from the little vents. The sun made sparkles on the windows.

“Ready for the park?” asked Mia, buckling her seat. Dad started the engine. Vroom! The car hummed like a sleepy bee.

Polly fluffed her feathers. “Park! Park! Trees and breeze!” she chirped. She liked trees. She liked shade. She liked taking care of nature too.

The car rolled down the street. Then… hiccup. Hiccup. The car lights blinked. The humming stopped. The car became very quiet.

Mia looked worried. “Dad, what happened?”

Dad sighed. “We used the battery too much while we waited. The car won’t start.”

Polly tilted her head. “No start?” Her heart fluttered. Mia’s eyebrows scrunched. Dad looked around. The sidewalk was empty, and the sun felt hot.

Mia whispered, “We can’t just sit and waste power. That’s not good for the planet.”

Polly thought hard. She was a creative parrot. She liked to solve problems. She liked to help. She liked to do things herself when she could. Her little claws tapped the seat. Tap, tap, tap.

Polly took a deep breath. She could smell warm air, like toast. She heard a bird far away. Tweet. She looked at the car. What could she do? She remembered something Dad had said. “We save power when we turn things off.”

Polly puffed up. “I can try!” she said. “Self-reliance! Try first!”

Mia nodded. “We can help too. We can all do small jobs.”

Polly hopped to the center. “Let’s make a plan,” she said. “Polly plans!” She held up one wing like a little captain.

Step one, Polly looked for what was on. The radio light glowed. “Click!” Mia turned it off. The little screen went dark.

Step two, the fan blew cold air. It felt nice, but it used power. “Whirr…” Polly touched the button with her beak. “Click.” The fan stopped. The car was quiet again.

Step three, the doors were open. The dome light was on. It made a warm circle of light. “We need the doors closed,” Polly said gently. “Close, please.” Dad and Mia closed the doors. “Click.” The light went out.

Polly listened. The car felt calm. She looked at Dad. “Try now?”

Dad smiled. “You’re a good leader, Polly.” He turned the key. The car tried to start. Rrr-rrr… but it did not start yet.

Polly did not give up. “Try more ideas,” she said. “We can do it.” She felt brave. She felt helpful. She felt the soft seat under her feet. She tasted the last crumb of a cracker on her beak. Crunch.

She remembered water. “Water!” she squawked. “We save water by sipping. We save power by waiting.” She flapped. “Let’s rest the car for a minute.”

They waited. They took calm breaths. In and out. In and out. The car felt cooler with the windows open a little. A small breeze tickled Polly’s feathers. It smelled like grass.

Mia looked around the car. “We have a small solar charger in the bag,” she said. “We use it to charge phones with sunshine.”

Polly’s eyes shone. “Sun power! Conservation!” she chirped. “We can use the sun to help!”

Dad nodded. “Good idea. It won’t start the car, but it can charge my phone so I can call for help if we need it. And it teaches us to use clean energy.”

Polly helped. She held the cable in her beak and passed it to Mia. Mia set the tiny panel on the dashboard where the sun was bright. The panel felt warm. The light blinked. Blink, blink.

Polly felt proud. “We are using what we have,” she said. “We are careful with power.”

Dad tried the key again. Rrr-rrr… vroom! The car started! The humming came back, smooth and happy. Mia clapped. Polly flapped. “Squawk-a-doodle-doo! It worked!”

Dad laughed. “It helped that we turned things off and waited. And we had a smart plan. You led us, Polly.”

Polly stood tall. “We did it together. We tried first. We cared for our car. We cared for the planet.”

On the way to the park, they kept the windows open a little to feel the breeze. The air smelled like pine. The road made a soft shhh sound. Polly sang a new song. “Use what you’ve got. Think, think, think. Save what you can. Blink, blink, blink.”

Mia smiled. “Polly, what did we learn today?”

Polly bobbed her head. “We learned self-reliance. We can try to fix small problems by ourselves. We learned problem-solving. Make a plan. Take steps. We learned conservation. Turn off lights. Save power. Use the sun when we can.”

Dad added, “And we stayed calm and kind.”

Polly snuggled into her seat. She felt warm, safe, and strong. She could hear leaves whispering when they reached the park. The trees waved hello.

Polly whispered, “Small birds can do big things.” She looked at Mia and Dad. “We can help the earth, and we can help ourselves.”

They parked under a shady tree. Click. The car rested. They took their water bottles and sipped. Sip, sip. No waste. They put wrappers in a little bag. No litter. The sun felt gentle on their faces.

Polly stretched her wings. “Let’s play,” she said. “And let’s take care of our world.”

The End.
