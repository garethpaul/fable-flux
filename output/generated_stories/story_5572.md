# Tilly Turtle’s Treehouse Plan

Tilly was a shy turtle. She lived near a big, friendly tree. High in the leaves was a small treehouse with a round window. The wood smelled like warm sunshine. Birds sang, “Chee-chee!” and the breeze made the leaves whisper, “Shhhh.”

Tilly wanted to visit the treehouse. She wanted to look out the round window and see the whole world. But Tilly was shy. She was also slow. The ladder looked tall. Her heart went thump-thump. Her shell felt snug, and she wanted to hide inside.

“I am scared,” Tilly whispered. “What if I can’t climb? What if I slip?”

Just then, her friend Benny the bluebird swooped down. “Hi, Tilly!” he chirped. “Do you want to come up to the treehouse? The floor is smooth like a cookie. The window is shiny like a bubble!”

Tilly took a deep breath in. She let it out slowly. “I want to go,” she said, “but I feel shy. The ladder is high.”

Benny fluttered to the first rung. “We can make a plan,” he said. “A plan helps our bodies feel calm and our minds feel ready.”

Tilly blinked. “A plan?”

“Yes,” Benny said. “Steps, like little seeds in a row. We do one, then the next.”

Tilly listened. The breeze was cool on her cheeks. She smelled pine and sweet flowers. She felt the soft grass under her feet. “Okay,” she said. “Let’s plan.”

Benny tapped his wing on a flat rock like a tiny table. “Step One,” he chirped, “we look and talk. We see each part. We say what we need.”

Tilly looked at the ladder. “I need a slow pace. I need to rest sometimes. I need a friend to cheer.”

Benny smiled. “I can cheer! Chee-chee-YES!”

“Step Two,” Tilly said, her voice a little braver, “we practice on the ground.”

Tilly touched the first rung with her front foot. She pressed and lifted, then put it down again. Tap. Tap. The rung felt smooth and warm from the sun.

“Step Three,” Benny said, “we breathe. In for three. Out for three.”

Tilly breathed in. One, two, three. She breathed out. One, two, three. Her shoulders softened. Her shell did not feel so tight.

“Step Four,” Tilly said, “we climb one rung. Just one.”

She put one foot up. Thump. She held on. Her tummy felt wiggly, like jelly. “I did it,” she whispered.

“Chee-chee-YES!” Benny sang. “Step Five: rest.”

Tilly rested. She looked around. She felt the wood under her toes. She heard the leaves say, “Shhhh.” She tasted the sweet air. She smiled a tiny smile.

“Step Six,” Tilly said, “we check our feelings.”

“How do you feel?” Benny asked.

“I feel shy,” Tilly said, “and a little brave.”

“That is okay,” Benny said. “You can be both.”

Tilly nodded. “Step Seven,” she said, “we climb one more rung.”

Up she went. Thump. Then rest. Then breathe. In for three. Out for three. Her plan was like a gentle song. Climb, rest, breathe. Climb, rest, breathe.

Halfway up, Tilly stopped. “My legs feel tired,” she said. “My heart is fast.”

“That is your body talking,” Benny said. “That is self-care. We listen. Do you want water?”

“Yes, please,” Tilly said. Benny brought a tiny cap full of water. It was cool and tasted like rain. Tilly felt better.

She looked down. “It is high,” she said.

Benny nodded. “It is high. And you are brave.”

Tilly looked up at the round window. It sparkled. “I will use my plan,” she said. “One rung. Then rest.”

She climbed again. Thump. Thump. Thump. At the top, she reached the treehouse floor. The wood felt smooth on her feet. A soft rug tickled her toes. She could smell fresh leaves. She could hear the sky hum.

“You did it!” Benny cheered. “Welcome, Tilly!”

Tilly looked out the round window. The world was green and gold. She saw a river like a ribbon. She heard a far frog go, “Ribbit.” She felt the sun warm her shell. Happy tears tickled her eyes.

“I am still shy,” Tilly said, “but I am also strong.”

Benny nodded. “You made a plan. You listened to your body. You were brave in a gentle way.”

Tilly sat on the soft rug. She took three calm breaths. In for three. Out for three. “Thank you, Benny,” she said. “Next time, I can help a friend make a plan too.”

They had a tiny picnic. Crunchy apple slices. A nutty cracker. A sweet drop of honey. The flavors felt bright and kind.

When it was time to go down, Tilly smiled. “We can use our plan again.”

“Steps like seeds,” Benny said.

“Little steps grow big courage,” Tilly said.

They climbed down slow and safe. At the bottom, Tilly patted the ladder. “Thank you, ladder,” she whispered.

She looked at the treehouse one more time. The round window winked in the sun. Tilly felt shy, and brave, and proud. She knew she could try new things, one small step at a time, with a good plan, calm breaths, and kind friends.

The clear lesson: planning helps us feel ready, acceptance helps us feel okay, and bravery grows when we take little steps and listen to our bodies.

The End.
