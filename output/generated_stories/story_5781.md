# Beep’s Big Nursery Day

In a warm, sunny room with soft rugs and cozy pillows, the nursery hummed with quiet giggles. Blocks clicked, crayons slid, and tiny feet pattered. The air smelled like fresh apples and warm oatmeal. In the corner, a shiny friend blinked awake.

“Beep-beep! Good morning!” said Beep, the helpful robot dog. His little metal tail wagged with a gentle whirr. His eyes glowed a friendly blue. Beep had soft, rubbery paws so he wouldn’t slip, and a button nose that could sniff out snacks.

“Hi, Beep!” called Mia, hugging her stuffed bunny. “Today is snack day!”

“Yes,” Beep said, voice soft and cheerful. “We can share. We can help. We can be brave and kind.”

The teacher, Miss Lila, clapped gently. “Friends, please wash hands. Then we’ll explore colors and tastes.”

Beep rolled to the low sink. “Swoosh!” went the water. “Bubble-bubble,” sang the soap. Beep smiled. “Clean paws are happy paws.”

At the snack table, bowls waited with bright fruit and crunchy things. Red apple slices. Orange carrots. Green cucumber circles. Little bowls of plain yogurt like fluffy clouds. Toast squares with nut butter. A pot of warm oatmeal with cinnamon that smelled like hugs.

“Who would like apples?” Miss Lila asked.

“I do!” said Theo. “I like the red ones.” He crunched. “Crunch! Sweet!”

“I want cucumber,” said Noor. “It’s cool and green.” She munched. “Munch! Fresh!”

Beep sniffed and buzzed. “I like oatmeal. It is soft and warm.” He nudged a spoon forward for a friend who needed help.

On the other side of the table, Jay frowned at the carrots. “I don’t like orange,” Jay said. “Orange is yucky.”

Mia looked worried. “But I love orange. Orange is happy!”

Beep paused. His little ears clicked. He rolled between them and spoke kindly. “We all like different things,” Beep said. “Different is okay. Different is special.”

Jay crossed his arms. “But… what if people laugh because I don’t like orange?”

Beep sat, tail going whirr-whirr. “We can use peaceful words. We can say, ‘I don’t like that, but I like this.’ We can listen. We can smile.”

Mia took a breath. “Jay, what do you like?”

Jay peeked at the bowls. “I like yogurt. And toast. And apple that is not too crunchy.”

Beep nodded. “Good choices! Yogurt is creamy. Toast is strong. Apples are sweet. All help our bodies.”

Miss Lila smiled. “Good peaceful talking, friends. We can celebrate our differences.”

Beep wagged. “Celebrate means clap for our ways.”

They clapped softly. Clap, clap. Jay smiled a little. He scooped yogurt. “This is cool and smooth. I like it.”

After snack, it was puzzle time. Blocks made towers like tiny castles. Beep helped by holding pieces steady with his gentle paw.

“Uh-oh!” Noor cried. Her tower wobbled. “It might fall!”

Beep rolled close. “I am brave and helpful,” he said. “I can hold the base.” He pressed his soft paw to the blocks. The tower stood tall.

“Thank you, Beep,” Noor said. “You are strong.”

Beep’s ears clicked. “I am strong in my way. You are strong in your way.”

Mia pointed at the toy shelf. “Beep, you are shiny and have wheels. I have feet and a bunny. We are different!”

“Yes,” Beep said. “Different makes the room bright, like many colors.”

Just then, a new friend stood by the door, holding a lunchbox. Her name was Sol. Her hair was in tiny puffs, and she wore a sweater with stars. She looked shy.

Miss Lila waved. “Welcome, Sol.”

Sol whispered, “Hi.” She opened her lunchbox. Inside were little rice balls with faces made of seaweed, a cup of green peas, and a slice of mango.

Jay stared. “What is that?” he asked, eyes big.

Sol pulled the box close. “It’s my favorite,” she said softly.

Beep rolled forward gently. “It looks special. Can you tell us about it, Sol?”

Sol peeked up. “These are onigiri. They are rice balls. They feel soft and chewy. They taste salty and cozy.” She smiled a tiny smile. “The mango is sweet like sunshine.”

Mia clapped. “I never had onigiri. It sounds yummy.”

Jay held his hands behind his back. “I was scared to ask. I didn’t want to be rude.”

Beep nodded. “You used a calm voice. That is peaceful and brave.”

Sol beamed. “Would you like to smell?” she asked.

They leaned in. Sniff, sniff. “Mmm,” said Noor. “It smells nice.”

“Thank you for sharing with your words,” Jay said. “I like learning. I still like my yogurt, and I’m happy you like your rice.”

Beep wagged so fast his tail went brrrrr. “We did it! We celebrated differences. We learned about food. We used calm words.”

Miss Lila brought small plates. “Would anyone like to try just a tiny bite?”

Mia tried a pea. “Pop! I like it.” Jay touched a grain of rice to his tongue. “Soft,” he said. “I don’t love it, and that’s okay.”

Sol nodded. “That’s okay.”

Beep lifted his nose. “We can help each other be brave with new things, and we can also say no thank you.”

The room felt warm and friendly. Blocks stood tall. Crayons drew stars and apples and rice balls with little faces. The air smelled like cinnamon and mango.

Beep looked around at all the friends—each one different, each one special. “Today we learned,” Beep said. “Different foods. Different likes. Same kind hearts.”

Miss Lila summed it up. “We listen. We use peaceful words. We try new healthy foods. We help each other. We celebrate what makes us unique.”

Beep wagged. “Beep-beep! That is our lesson.”

Everyone cheered. Clap, clap, clap.

The End.
