# Tashi the Tortoise Fixes the Wobbly Wagon

Tashi was a wise tortoise. He wore tiny round glasses and a bright blue bandana. He moved slowly and thought carefully. Tashi loved to visit the garage workshop. It smelled like wood and orange oil. The floor felt cool. Soft light shone through a little window. Tools hung on the wall like shiny smiles.

One warm morning, Tashi heard clatter and clink. “Oh dear,” he said. “That sounds like a problem.” He peeked into the workshop. On the workbench sat a red wagon with one wobbly wheel. It went wiggle-wobble, wiggle-wobble.

Two friends stood nearby. Pip the puppy wagged and barked. “Let’s fix it fast! Zoom! Zoom!” Bella the bunny thumped a foot. “No, no! Let’s paint it first. Pink and sparkly!” Pip and Bella both talked at once. Their voices got loud. Their faces scrunched.

Tashi took a slow deep breath. In… and out. “Hello, friends,” he said in a gentle voice. “I see a wobbly wheel. I hear big feelings.”

Pip whined, “I want to hurry!”

Bella huffed, “I want it pretty!”

The wagon wiggled again. Wiggle-wobble. It made a tiny squeak. Tashi touched the wooden bench. It was smooth. He listened to the tiny squeak, then to his calm heartbeat. Thump… thump… peaceful and slow.

“Let us try calm,” said Tashi. “Calm helps our brains think. Put a paw on your tummy. Take a soft breath with me.” Pip and Bella copied him. In… and out. Their shoulders dropped. Their noses twitched. The workshop felt quiet and cozy.

“Now,” Tashi said, smiling, “we can use our calm to make a plan.”

Pip tilted his head. “A plan?”

Bella’s ears perked up. “A plan!”

Tashi nodded. “First we fix the wheel so the wagon is safe. Then we paint it together. That is a compromise. We both get some of what we want.” He drew a little picture on a notepad. Step 1: Fix. Step 2: Paint. He showed the drawing. Pip and Bella smiled.

“That sounds fair,” said Pip.

“That sounds fun,” said Bella.

“Good,” said Tashi. “Now we use skill-building. We learn and try. We do things step by step.”

He rolled the wagon gently. The wheel squeaked again. Tashi felt the nuts and bolts. “Hmm.” He pointed to a little bolt. “This is a bit loose.”

Pip bounced. “I can hold the flashlight!”

Bella clapped. “I can find the right wrench!”

Tashi nodded. “Teamwork. Calm hands. Slow and steady.” He handed Pip a small flashlight. Pip flicked it on. A warm yellow circle of light glowed. Bella opened a drawer. It rattled with metal sounds. Clink. Clank. She chose a small wrench, then checked the size. “This one?”

Tashi tried it. “Click!” It fit. “Well done.”

They took a breath together. In… and out. Tashi turned the wrench slowly. “Righty-tighty,” he said in a sing-song voice. Pip hummed, “Righty-tighty.” Bella tapped a gentle rhythm on the bench. Tashi showed them how to keep fingers away from the moving part. “Safety first,” he said kindly.

The wheel stopped wobbling. Tashi spun it. It went round and smooth. “Listen,” he said. They listened. No squeak. Just a soft whirr, like a sleepy bee.

Pip grinned. “We fixed it!”

Bella beamed. “Now we paint!”

Tashi chuckled. “Yes. Step 2.” He set down old paper. It crinkled like leaves. He brought out paint pots. Red, blue, yellow, white, and a tiny pot of sparkle glaze. He also brought two brushes and one sponge.

Bella reached for the sparkle glaze. Pip reached for the blue. Their hands bumped. “Hey!” “Hey!” Their voices grew loud again.

Tashi lifted a gentle hand. “Calm check,” he said softly. “Breath first, then talk.” They breathed together. In… and out. Pip’s tail slowed. Bella’s ears relaxed.

Tashi said, “We can take turns. We can share tools. We can plan the colors. That is another compromise.”

Pip nodded. “I paint the sides blue first.”

Bella nodded. “I add pink swirls after.”

Tashi smiled. “And we both can use sparkle glaze at the end.”

They dipped brushes. The paint was cool and smooth. Swish, swish. The sponge made soft dots. Pop, pop. Pip painted careful blue stripes on the side. Bella made pink swirls like little clouds. Tashi showed them how to mix a tiny bit of white to make pastel colors. “Light colors are calm colors,” he said. “See how soft they look?”

The wagon began to shine. Blue stripes, pink swirls, yellow dots, and a gentle sparkle at the end. It looked happy and safe.

Pip whispered, “I like how we took turns.”

Bella giggled, “I like our calm breaths.”

Tashi patted the wagon. “You used calm to solve a problem. You made a plan. You learned a skill. You shared and made a good choice.”

Pip tugged the handle. The wagon rolled smooth and quiet. No wiggle-wobble. Just a soft whirr. They took it for a tiny ride around the workshop. The light on the tools winked. The air smelled like orange oil and a little like paint. It felt warm and proud.

Pip said, “When I feel rushy, I can breathe.”

Bella said, “When I feel bossy, I can share.”

Tashi nodded. “Calm first. Then plan. Then do. That is wise.”

They all smiled. The wobbly wagon was fixed and beautiful. The friends felt peaceful and strong.

The lesson was clear: Calm helps us think, compromise helps us share, and practice builds our skills.

The End.
