# Dolly the Dolphin's Craft Room Adventure

In a cozy house by the sea, there was a very special basement. It wasn't dark or scary - it was bright and colorful! The walls were painted ocean blue, and everywhere you looked, there were craft supplies. Scissors, glue, colorful paper, ribbons, and sparkly stickers filled every shelf.

This was where Dolly the dolphin loved to spend her time. Dolly was a magical dolphin who could swim through the air just like water! She had smooth, gray skin that sparkled in the light, and the biggest, brightest smile you ever did see.

"Wheee!" Dolly squeaked as she swam through the air around the craft tables. The basement smelled like fresh crayons and sweet glue. Dolly's fins made soft swooshing sounds as she moved.

Today, Dolly was very excited. Her friend Benny the bear was coming over to make friendship bracelets together. But when Dolly looked around the busy basement, she gasped.

"Oh no!" she said, her voice echoing off the walls. "What a mess!"

Craft supplies were scattered everywhere. Paper scraps covered the floor like confetti. Markers had rolled under tables, and ribbons were tangled in knots. The basement looked like a colorful tornado had spun through it!

Just then, *ding dong* - the doorbell rang upstairs.

"That must be Benny!" Dolly said, her heart beating fast like a little drum. She felt worried and embarrassed. How could she invite her friend into such a messy place?

Dolly swam quickly to the door. There stood Benny, a fluffy brown bear with kind eyes, holding a basket of beads.

"Hi, Dolly!" Benny said cheerfully. "I brought my special beads to share!"

Dolly's cheeks turned pink. She looked down at her fins. "Benny, I need to tell you something. I made a big mess in the craft room, and I didn't clean it up. I'm really sorry."

Benny's face was understanding and warm. "That's okay, Dolly. Everyone makes messes sometimes. Would you like help cleaning up?"

Dolly's eyes lit up like little stars. "Really? You'd help me?"

"Of course!" Benny said. "Friends help each other. And cleaning can be fun when we do it together!"

Down in the basement, Dolly and Benny worked as a team. Dolly used her fins to gather the paper scraps, making swooshing sounds as she moved. The papers crinkled and rustled as she picked them up. Benny used his strong paws to untangle the ribbons, humming a happy tune.

"This red marker goes with the red markers," Benny said, sorting carefully.

"And these stickers go in the sticker box!" Dolly added, feeling proud as she organized.

As they cleaned, Dolly learned something important. When she slowed down and paid attention to where she put things, the basement stayed much neater. She practiced putting each crayon back in its special spot and closing glue bottles tight.

Soon, the basement sparkled! Everything had a place, and every place had its thing.

"Now we can make beautiful friendship bracelets!" Dolly said happily.

Benny shared his colorful beads, and Dolly shared her special string. Together, they made the most beautiful bracelets, with blues like the ocean and greens like seaweed.

"Thank you for helping me clean and for sharing your beads," Dolly said, giving Benny a gentle hug with her fins.

"Thank you for saying sorry and for sharing your craft room," Benny replied.

From that day on, Dolly remembered to clean as she crafted, and she always shared her supplies with friends. The basement stayed beautiful, and every craft time was filled with joy and friendship.

The End.
