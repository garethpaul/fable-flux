# Ruby the Robin's Breakfast Plan

Ruby the Robin lived in a cozy nest just outside the dining room window. Every morning, she loved to sing beautiful songs while the family ate breakfast. Her feathers sparkled bright orange in the sunlight, and her voice was as sweet as honey.

But Ruby had a big problem. She was always late!

"Tweet-tweet-tweet!" Ruby sang as she fluttered into the dining room. The family had already finished eating. The table was empty. The chairs were pushed in.

"Oh no!" chirped Ruby. "I missed breakfast time again!"

This happened every single day. Ruby would sleep too long, then spend forever preening her feathers. By the time she arrived to sing, everyone was gone.

Ruby felt sad. Her beautiful songs were meant to make breakfast special, but nobody could hear them.

"I need to make a plan," Ruby said to herself. She perched on the windowsill and thought hard. "But how?"

The next morning, Ruby woke up when the sun was still sleepy. She looked at the dining room. It was dark and quiet.

"Maybe I should wait," Ruby thought. But then she remembered how disappointed she felt yesterday.

"No!" Ruby decided firmly. "I'll be a leader and make good choices!"

First, Ruby made a morning schedule. "I'll wake up early, sing my practice songs, then eat my breakfast quickly," she planned out loud.

Ruby tried her new plan. She woke up with the first ray of sunshine. She practiced her prettiest songs in her nest. Then she ate three small berries - not too many, not too few.

When she flew to the dining room, the family was just sitting down!

"Good morning!" chirped Ruby happily. She began to sing her sweetest song.

"Listen!" said little Emma at the table. "The robin is singing!"

"How lovely!" said Mama, smiling.

Ruby felt so proud. Her plan was working!

But the next day, Ruby faced a new problem. Her friend Chippy the Chipmunk wanted to play.

"Come on, Ruby! Let's gather acorns!" chattered Chippy.

Ruby looked at the dining room. The family would eat soon. But Chippy looked so excited.

"I have an idea," said Ruby thoughtfully. "What if we play for just ten minutes? Then I can still sing at breakfast time!"

Chippy clapped his tiny paws. "That's a great compromise!"

They played quickly, then Ruby flew to the dining room right on time. She sang while Chippy listened from the window.

"You're so smart, Ruby!" said Chippy. "You found a way for both of us to be happy!"

Day after day, Ruby stuck to her schedule. She learned to balance her time between singing, playing, and helping others. When Mama Bird needed help finding twigs, Ruby helped for fifteen minutes, then sang her songs. When it rained and she wanted to sleep longer, Ruby reminded herself how good it felt to be on time.

Soon, the whole family looked forward to Ruby's morning concerts. They would smile and hum along while they ate their breakfast.

"You know what?" Ruby sang happily one morning. "Making a plan and sticking to it feels wonderful! Now everyone gets to enjoy music with their breakfast!"

Ruby had learned that being a good leader meant making smart decisions about time. She discovered that planning ahead helped her do all the things she loved - and made others happy too.

From that day on, Ruby was never late again. Her sweet songs filled the dining room every morning, making breakfast the most magical time of day.

The End.
