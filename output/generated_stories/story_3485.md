# Bubbles the Dolphin’s Bath-Time Boat

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash. Today, Bubbles swam into a bright, bubbly bathroom. The warm air smelled like soap and lemons. The mirror was shiny. The tub was full of foamy bubbles that looked like tiny white clouds.

“Wheee!” giggled Bubbles, flipping his tail. “This bathroom is an ocean of bubbles!”

On the counter sat a box of safe craft things: soft sponges, empty cardboard rolls, tape, and stickers. A note beside the box read, “Please tidy up after crafting.”

Bubbles read the note out loud. “Tidy up. That means be responsible,” he said, puffing a little bubble. He felt proud for remembering the big word. Responsible.

Bubbles peeked at the tub. “I want to make a little boat,” he said. “Then it can sail on the bubble sea!”

The sink hummed softly. The faucet went drip, drip. The towel felt fluffy like a cloud. Bubbles took a deep breath. “I will make a bath-time boat,” he decided. “But I will also keep it neat. I can do both.”

He set a clean towel on the counter to use as a craft mat. “Step one,” he said. “Make a plan.” He tapped his fin to his chin. “What do I need?”

He looked at the box. “I will use a sponge for the boat’s body. A cardboard roll can be the mast. A paper triangle can be the sail. Tape will hold it all together. Stickers can make it pretty.”

Bubbles smiled. He made a choice to keep the sink area dry. “Water stays in the tub. Crafting stays on the towel,” he said. “That is my responsible rule.”

He picked up a soft yellow sponge. It smelled like sunshine. He gently pressed it with his fin. Squish, squish. “This will float,” he whispered. He taped the cardboard roll to the sponge. The tape made a soft rip sound. Rrrip. Then he cut a triangle from a paper towel. Snip, snip. He slid the triangle onto the roll for a sail. He added a star sticker. “Star Boat!” Bubbles cheered.

He looked at the little bits of paper on the counter. “I will keep these in a pile,” he said. He scooped them together. He found the tiny trash can and put the bits inside. “That feels good,” he said. “I am taking care of the bathroom.”

Bubbles gently carried the boat to the tub. The bubbles were warm and smelled sweet. Plop! The boat sat on top. It bobbed up and down. “Sail, Star Boat, sail!” Bubbles sang.

He blew a small puff of air. Poof! The boat glided over the foam. “I am the captain,” said Bubbles. “I will make safe choices.” He turned the faucet a tiny bit to add more warm water. “Just a little,” he said. “Not too full.” He watched the water line. When it reached a safe mark, he turned the faucet off. “Good decision,” he told himself.

Then Bubbles saw a bottle of slippery soap near the edge of the tub. It wobbled. “Uh-oh,” he said. “That could fall.” He moved it to a steady spot. “Being responsible means thinking ahead,” he said proudly.

The boat reached a bubble mountain. The white foam tickled Bubbles’ nose. “Achoo!” he squeaked, giggling. He guided the boat around the mountain with a gentle wave from his fin. “Left turn,” he said. “Slow and steady.”

Soon, Bubbles noticed a small drip on the floor. “Drip, drip.” He looked around. “Did I cause that?” he asked himself. He checked the tub. A tiny bit of water had splashed over when he made a big wave.

Bubbles took a deep breath. “I can fix this,” he said. He grabbed the corner of the towel on a hook and dabbed the drip. Dab, dab, dab. “All clean!” He smiled. “Taking care of spills is part of being responsible.”

When bath-time sailing felt complete, Bubbles made one more choice. “It’s time to tidy up.” He lifted Star Boat from the tub and placed it on the towel to dry. He emptied the tiny trash can into a bigger can. He put the tape back in the box and closed the lid. He wiped the counter with a soft cloth. Swish, swish. He checked the floor again. Dry!

Bubbles looked at the mirror. His eyes sparkled. He felt calm and proud. “I did it,” he said. “I had fun, I made good choices, and I cleaned up.”

Just then, a little rubber duck bobbed near the faucet. “Quack?” it squeaked.

Bubbles giggled. “Yes, Duck. We can sail tomorrow too. We will plan, we will choose, and we will care.”

He placed Star Boat on the windowsill to dry, with the sail open like a smile. He hung the towel neatly. He made sure the water was off and the soap was safe.

Bubbles floated one last time in the warm foamy tub, then hopped out with a happy splash. “Being responsible helps everyone,” he said. “It keeps the bathroom clean. It keeps me safe. And it makes fun last longer.”

The bathroom smelled fresh. The counter shone. The tub was tidy. Star Boat rested, ready for another day.

Bubbles waved his fin. “Good job, Captain,” he told himself. “Today we learned: Make a plan, make good choices, and clean up when you are done.”

He smiled a big dolphin smile, feeling warm and proud and ready for a new day.

The End.
