# Milo Monkey's Mindful Bedtime

The soft yellow nightlight glowed in Milo Monkey's cozy bedroom. His fluffy blue blanket was pulled up to his chin, and his favorite stuffed banana lay beside him. But Milo's brown eyes were wide open, staring at the ceiling.

"I can't sleep," Milo whispered to himself. His furry tail twitched under the covers. Outside his window, he could hear different sounds - a car honking, a dog barking, and the wind whooshing through the trees.

"Too noisy," Milo said, covering his ears with his small hands. "Too bright. Too scratchy." The pajamas felt funny on his fur, and even his soft pillow seemed too lumpy.

Mama Monkey peeked through the doorway. "What's wrong, my brave little monkey?" she asked gently, her voice warm like honey.

"Everything feels different tonight," Milo said, sitting up. "The sounds are too loud, and my pajamas feel weird, and I can't make my brain stop thinking!"

Mama Monkey sat on the edge of his bed, making it dip slightly. "Would you like to try something special with me?" she asked. "Something that might help your busy mind feel calm?"

Milo nodded, his ears perking up with curiosity.

"Let's try mindful breathing," Mama said softly. "Close your eyes and imagine you're smelling your favorite flower."

Milo squeezed his eyes shut. "Like the yellow daisies in our garden?"

"Perfect," Mama smiled. "Now breathe in slowly through your nose, like you're smelling that sweet daisy."

Milo took a deep, slow breath. "Mmm," he hummed. "It smells sunny and happy."

"Now breathe out slowly through your mouth, like you're blowing the daisy seeds to make a wish," Mama instructed.

Milo breathed out with a gentle "whoosh." His shoulders relaxed a little.

"Let's do it again," Mama said. "This time, notice how your tummy moves up and down, like a balloon filling with air and then letting it out."

They breathed together - in with the daisy smell, out with the wish-blowing. Milo felt his furry chest rise and fall. The car sounds outside seemed quieter now, like background music instead of noise.

"How do you feel?" Mama asked after five breaths.

"Calmer," Milo said, surprised. "My tail stopped twitching."

"That's the magic of mindful breathing," Mama explained. "When we pay attention to our breathing, our minds can rest. Even when things around us feel different or overwhelming, we can find peace inside ourselves."

Milo opened his eyes and looked at his room. The nightlight still glowed yellow, but now it seemed cozy instead of too bright. His pajamas still felt different from his daytime clothes, but different didn't have to mean bad.

"Can we try one more thing?" Mama asked. "Let's do a body scan. Start with your toes and notice how they feel."

Milo wiggled his toes under the blanket. "They feel warm and relaxed."

"Now your legs," Mama guided.

"Heavy and sleepy," Milo giggled softly.

They moved up through his body - his tummy felt soft, his arms felt loose like cooked spaghetti, and his face felt smooth and peaceful.

"Even when our world feels noisy or different, we can always come back to our breathing," Mama said, tucking the blanket around him. "Your breath is always with you, like a best friend."

Milo smiled sleepily. "I like having a breathing friend."

"Sweet dreams, my mindful little monkey," Mama whispered, giving him a gentle kiss on his forehead.

As Mama tiptoed out, Milo closed his eyes and took another slow, peaceful breath. The sounds outside were still there, but they felt far away now. His breathing was close and comforting.

"In like a daisy," he whispered to himself. "Out like a wish."

Soon, Milo was fast asleep, dreaming of floating on clouds made of gentle breaths.

The End.
