# Joey Jumps to Save the Drips

Joey was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright, bouncy eyes. He loved to hop, clap, and sing little songs. One sunny morning, Joey hopped into the bathroom. The floor tiles felt cool on his feet. The air smelled like bubble soap and fresh mint toothpaste. He heard a sound—plip, plop, plip.

“Hmm? Who is making that dripping song?” asked Joey, tilting his head.

“Plip, plop, plip,” answered the sink.

Joey peeked closer. A tiny drop fell from the shiny faucet. Another drop followed, and then another. Joey saw the drops splash like tiny diamonds in the sink. The sound was gentle, but it did not stop.

“Water is precious,” Joey whispered. “Mom says water helps plants, animals, and people, too. We do not waste it.”

He felt a little flutter in his tummy. Could he fix it? He was just a small joey. He took a brave breath and put his paw on the faucet handle. “I can try,” he said.

Joey turned the handle a little. The faucet squeaked. The drip slowed, but it did not stop. “Plip… plop…” came the tiny song.

Joey’s ears twitched. He wanted to run and call for help. But he remembered Mom’s story from last night. Mom had told him a story about a clever wombat who cared for the forest by fixing small things early. “Small paws can make big help,” Mom had said.

Joey smiled. “I can be like the wombat,” he told himself. “I can be brave and careful.”

He looked around the bathroom. He saw a soft towel, a step stool, a little cup for rinsing, and a basket with a brush and a comb. The window let in warm light. Dust sparkles danced in the sunbeam, like tiny fairies cheering him on.

Joey pulled the step stool to the sink. Clunk. He climbed up. Thump, thump went his little heart. He wrapped the towel around the faucet to keep his paws from slipping. He tried again, turning the handle just a tiny bit more. Squeak. He waited. He listened.

Silence.

Then—plip.

“Oh!” said Joey. “Almost!”

He was not scared now. He felt brave. He felt strong. Joey turned the handle back a tiny bit, then forward a tiny bit, very gentle. “Left a little, right a little,” he sang softly. “Save the water, every little bit.”

The drip slowed. He could still hear a soft, tiny tap, like a mouse whisper. He looked at the drain and had an idea. Joey placed the little cup under the faucet to catch any more drips. The cup made a happy clink.

“I will not waste you,” Joey told the water in the cup. “I will use you later to water Mom’s fern.”

Just then, a bigger drip gathered at the tip of the faucet. Joey leaned close. He tightened the handle one last tiny turn. Squeeeak.

Silence.

No drip. No plop. Only the hum of the bathroom fan and the far-off chirp of a bird by the window.

“I did it!” Joey cheered. “Hip, hop, hooray!”

He hopped down from the stool and clapped his paws. His heart felt warm and proud. He had been brave. He had been careful. He had saved water.

Joey looked around again. “What else can help our Earth?” he wondered.

He saw the light glowing over the mirror. “I am done in here,” he said. “The light can rest.” He stretched up and flipped the switch. Click. The bathroom became soft and cozy. Golden light from the window was enough.

He felt the towel. It was damp from his paws. Joey spread it neatly over the rail so it could dry. “I can use you again,” he said to the towel. “That is good for the Earth.”

Just then, Mom peeked in. “Hello, my joyful joey,” she said with a smile. “What are you up to?”

Joey stood tall. “I heard drips,” he said. “I was brave. I used the stool. I turned the handle gently. I caught the last drips in the cup. I will use them for the fern! I saved water.”

Mom’s eyes shone. “That is wonderful,” she said. “You cared for our home. You cared for our Earth. You were safe, brave, and very smart.”

Joey felt cozy inside. He held up the cup. The water looked like a tiny pond, clear and bright. “Can we water the fern now?” he asked.

“Yes,” Mom said. “And tonight, will you tell me the story of Joey who saved the drips?”

Joey giggled. “Yes! I will tell it with big hops and funny sounds. Plip, plop, squeak!”

They walked to the fern by the sunny window. Joey poured the water slowly. The fern leaves trembled with joy, like little green hands waving thank you.

Joey touched the leaves and whispered, “We take care of you. We save water. Small paws can make big help.”

That night, Joey told his story. He told how he listened, how he tried, how he did not give up. He told how he used what he had—the stool, the towel, the cup. He told how being brave and careful kept the water safe. Mom clapped and hugged him tight.

The clear lesson grew in Joey’s heart: When we care for water, we care for our world. We can be brave, try our best, and use only what we need.

The End.
