# Kori Koala’s Kind Table Talk

Kori the calm koala sat in a bright dining room with a sunny window and a big wooden table. The room smelled like warm eucalyptus tea and fresh fruit. The chairs were soft, and the table felt smooth under Kori’s gentle paws.

“Good morning, table,” Kori whispered with a smile. “Thank you for holding our breakfast.”

Kori liked to thank things. It was a way to care for the world. Caring is called conservation. Conservation means we keep things safe and use only what we need.

Mama came in with a friendly hum. “Good morning, Kori. Are you ready for breakfast?” she asked.

“Yes, please,” said Kori in a soft voice. “I’m hungry, but I will take just enough.”

Mama placed a small bowl of oats, a handful of berries, and a cup of water. The oats were warm and smelled like cinnamon. The berries were bright and juicy. The water was cool and clear. Kori’s tummy rumbled.

Kori took a tiny taste. “Mmm,” Kori said. “This is just right.” Kori ate slowly, listening to the little clink of the spoon and the tiny tap-tap of paws on the table.

Suddenly, there was a quick knock. Tap tap tap!

“Come in,” Mama called.

Zippy the zebra rushed in, hooves clacking on the floor. “I’m so thirsty!” Zippy said, breathing fast. Zippy grabbed Kori’s cup and gulped the water.

Kori blinked. Kori’s chest felt tight for a moment. That was Kori’s cup. Kori took a slow breath and put a paw on the table to feel calm.

“Zippy,” Kori said kindly, “that was my water. I feel upset when you take my things without asking. Please ask next time. That is my boundary.” Kori placed a paw on the cup to show it was special.

Zippy’s ears drooped. “Oh,” Zippy said. “I forgot to ask. I’m sorry.”

Mama nodded. “Thank you, Zippy, for saying sorry. It’s fair to ask before using someone’s things. That is justice. Justice means fair and kind rules for everyone.”

Kori’s calm voice returned. “You can have some of my water if we pour it into another cup. We can share in a fair way.” Kori smiled.

“That’s kind,” said Zippy. “I will get my own cup.” Zippy trotted to the cupboard and took a small cup. “May I pour some?”

“Yes,” said Kori. “Let’s pour just what you need. We do not want to waste water. Water is precious.”

Together they poured a little water. Drip, drip, drip went the careful stream. Zippy took a sip. “Ahh,” Zippy said. “Thank you. This tastes like a cool river.”

Knock, knock, knock!

In came Tilly the turtle, slow and steady. “Good morning,” Tilly whispered. “May I join you?”

“Yes,” said Kori. “There is a place for you.” Kori pulled out a chair with a soft squeak. Tilly sat and smiled.

Tilly opened a big bag. Piles of napkins, plastic forks, and paper plates fell onto the table. “I brought lots!” Tilly said. “We will never run out.”

Kori looked at the pile. It was too much. Some would be used, but many would be thrown away. That would make trash. Trash can hurt our world.

Kori touched Tilly’s flipper gently. “Tilly,” Kori said in a calm voice, “thank you for being prepared. But this is a lot. We only need a little. Taking only what we need helps our Earth. That is conservation.”

Tilly blinked. “Oh. I wanted to help.”

“You are helping,” said Kori. “We can use washable plates and cloth napkins. Then we can wash and use them again. We can save the extra things for a big party another day.”

Mama nodded. “That is a smart plan. Reuse is kind to the Earth.”

Zippy clacked a hoof. “I like cloth napkins. They are soft!” Zippy dabbed a mouth with a soft napkin. “Cozy!”

Tilly gathered the extra napkins, forks, and plates. The pile got smaller. Kori placed out just three cloth napkins and three spoons. The table looked neat and calm.

Then Zippy reached for Kori’s berries. “Mmm, berries!”

Kori held up a paw. “Zippy, please ask before taking my food. My plate is my space.”

Zippy stopped. “May I have one berry, please?”

Kori looked at the bowl. “Yes. One berry is fair. We will also save some berries for later.” Kori gently rolled one berry to Zippy.

“Thank you,” said Zippy, smiling.

Tilly clapped softly. “I like how we ask. It feels safe. It feels fair.”

Kori felt warm inside. The dining room was bright and peaceful again. The tea smelled sweet. The wooden table felt strong and friendly.

“Kori,” Mama said, “you showed many good things today. You used words to keep your boundary. You shared in a fair way. You took only what you needed. That is good for people and good for the planet.”

Kori nodded. “Little choices help a lot. We can ask before we take. We can share fairly. We can reuse and not waste. We can protect water, food, and trees.”

Zippy lifted the small cup. “To caring for the Earth!” Zippy said.

Tilly raised a spoon. “To fair and kind rules!”

Mama lifted her tea. “To asking and listening!”

Kori raised a paw. “To conservation, to boundaries, and to justice,” Kori said softly. “We can do it every day.”

They all took a sip, a bite, and a deep happy breath. The dining room felt safe, kind, and clean. Outside, a bird sang a cheerful song. Inside, Kori smiled and patted the table.

“Thank you, table,” Kori whispered again. “We will take care of you, and you help take care of us.”

The End.
