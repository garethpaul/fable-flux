# Porch Paints and Gentle Hooves

The sun was soft and golden on the peaceful front porch. The boards felt warm and smooth. A gentle deer fawn named Della stepped carefully onto the porch, her tiny hooves making soft tap-tap sounds. She sniffed the air. It smelled like pine trees, sweet tea, and a little like crayons.

On the porch was a low table with a basket of art things. There were leaves, chalk, paper, yarn, and a little cup of water with a brush. Birds sang from the oak tree. A breeze tickled Della’s ears and made the wind chimes sing, ting-ting-ting.

Della’s friend, a small squirrel named Pip, zipped up the railing and sat beside her. His tail curled like a question mark. “What should we do?” Pip asked, his eyes bright.

Della looked at the basket. She felt a little shy. “I want to make something,” she said softly. “But I don’t know what.”

Pip bounced. “Let’s make porch art! We can paint!” He looked around. “But… there is only one brush.”

Della blinked. “We can share. We can take turns,” she said. Her voice was warm and calm.

Pip nodded. “Taking turns is fair. Who goes first?”

Della’s ears twitched. She thought. “We can tap the brush on the table. One, two. If it taps one, you go. If it taps two, I go.”

Pip giggled. “Okay!”

Tap. Tap. “Two!” Della smiled, but her belly did a little flip. She felt unsure. “What should I paint?”

Pip leaned close. “Close your eyes,” he said. “Listen. What do you hear?”

Della closed her eyes. She heard the birds singing, the leaves whispering, and the wind chimes ting-ting-ting. She smelled the pine and the sweet tea. She felt the warm sun on her back. A picture grew in her mind. “I hear a song,” she whispered. “I see swirls.”

She dipped the brush in water and then in chalk dust she rubbed from a blue stick. She swirled the brush on the paper in soft blue circles. Swish, swish. “It looks like wind,” she said, smiling.

“Now me!” Pip cheered. Tap. “One! My turn.” He rubbed yellow chalk into a little dish and added a drip of water. He dotted the page with tiny suns. Dot. Dot. Dot. “These are the warm spots,” he said. “Warm like your back.”

They took turns. Della painted a gentle brown path for their hooves and paws. Pip added green leaves with small bites in them, like squirrel snacks. When the brush needed a rest, they used their paws and a small leaf as a stamp. Press. Lift. Press. Lift. The porch rails watched quietly, and the wind chimes sang.

Soon the cup tipped a little. “Oh no,” said Pip. “The water is almost gone.”

Della looked around. “We can be resourceful,” she said. “We can use what we have.” She dipped the brush in a tiny puddle on the porch step. “This can help.” The puddle made the colors softer and dreamy.

A tiny rabbit named Mina hopped up, nose twitching. “What are you making?” she asked.

“A porch picture,” Della said proudly. “We are sharing and taking turns.”

Mina looked at the basket. “There is only one brush,” she said. Her whiskers wiggled. “Can I help?”

Della thought. “Yes,” she said kindly. “We can make more tools.” She picked up a feather from under the chair. “This can be a brush.” She tied it to a stick with a short piece of yarn. Pip grinned and tied a row of leaves to another stick. “Leaf brush!” he said.

They tried the new tools. The feather brush made soft lines like whispers. The leaf brush made bold, bumpy shapes. Mina used a sponge made from a folded napkin. Dab. Dab. Dab. Little clouds appeared.

They took turns with each tool. They waited. They watched. They said, “Your turn,” and “My turn,” and “Thank you.” When someone had an idea, they shared it. When someone felt stuck, they listened.

“I want to paint the sound,” Della said. “How does ting-ting look?”

Mina smiled. “Maybe like shiny dots!”

Della dabbed silver chalk dust with the feather brush. Ting. Ting. Tiny bright spots appeared.

Pip tilted his head. “How does quiet look?”

Della closed her eyes again. She breathed in, slow and soft. She thought of shade, and cool steps, and still air. She used a bit of gray and made gentle, hush-hush strokes. “Quiet looks like soft gray,” she said.

At last they sat back. The paper was full of swirls, dots, leaves, suns, and soft paths. It looked like a song you could see. The porch felt happy. Even the wind chimes seemed to smile.

Mina clapped her paws. “We made this together!”

Pip nodded. “We used our ideas. We used what we had.”

Della’s heart felt warm. “We were creative,” she said. “We were resourceful. And we took turns.”

They taped the picture to the porch wall with careful paws. The sun touched the swirls and made them glow.

Della looked at her friends. “I learned that ideas can come from sounds and smells,” she said. “I learned that sharing and waiting makes room for everyone’s ideas.”

Pip grinned. “And I learned a feather can be a brush!”

Mina giggled. “And quiet can be a color.”

They sat together, sipping cool water and watching their art flutter a little in the breeze. The porch felt peaceful, and their hearts felt proud.

The End.
