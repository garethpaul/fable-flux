# Brave Bunny Shares the Drum

In a sunny, playful living room, a determined rabbit named Rumi hopped from rug to rug. The rug was soft and squishy. The air smelled like warm pancakes, and a little breeze made the curtains dance. Toys smiled from baskets. A clock tick-tocked like a friendly drum. Rumi’s ears stood tall. Today felt special.

Rumi’s family was getting ready for Story and Share Night. Mama Rabbit placed a round, small drum on the couch. The drum had bright red sides and a smooth, shiny top. It came from Grandma’s home far away. Mama smiled and said, “Rumi, this is our taiko drum. Tonight, we will share our songs, our stories, and our traditions.”

Rumi touched the drum. Tap-tap. It sounded like a gentle heartbeat. Rumi’s paws tingled. “I want to share,” Rumi said. “I am brave. I can do it.”

Mama nodded. “Sharing our traditions helps friends learn and love who we are.”

Rumi practiced a little rhythm. Tap, tap, ta-TAP! Rumi swayed and whispered, “I can share. I can share.” But then Rumi looked at the door and felt a wiggle of worries. What if friends laughed? What if the drum was too loud? Rumi’s ears wobbled.

Papa Rabbit noticed and knelt beside Rumi. “Being brave does not mean no worries,” Papa said softly. “Being brave means you try, even with the worries.”

Rumi took a deep breath. In… out… The living room smelled like sweet tea. The drum felt smooth under Rumi’s paw. “I will try,” Rumi said.

Ding-dong! The doorbell sang. Friends came in with smiles and snacks. Mia the mouse carried a tiny blue scarf. Benji the bear hugged a honey jar. Lila the lamb held a small bell that chimed, ting-ting.

They all sat on the soft rug. Pillows puffed up like clouds. Mama said, “Welcome! Tonight, we share who we are. We listen with kind ears. We look with kind eyes. We clap with kind paws.”

Mia went first. She held up her blue scarf. “My grandma taught me a sky dance,” she said. She swirled the scarf. It whooshed like wind. Everyone clapped. “Thank you for sharing,” said Papa.

Benji showed his honey. “We eat this during our big family day,” he said. He gave each friend a tiny taste. It was sweet and golden. “Mmm,” said Rumi, licking lips. “Your tradition is yummy!”

Lila rang her bell. Ting-ting! “We ring this for blessings,” she said, smiling shyly. The sound was soft and bright. Everyone listened. “Beautiful,” Mama said.

Then it was Rumi’s turn. The drum rested in Rumi’s lap. Rumi’s paws felt warm and a little shaky. “I am Rumi,” Rumi began. “This is our taiko drum. My grandma taught me a rhythm. It tells a story of strong hearts.”

Rumi tapped gently. Tap, tap, ta-TAP. The drum spoke back, round and warm. Rumi tapped again, a little braver. TAP, ta-TAP, tap-tap! Rumi’s feet wiggled. The room felt full of music. Friends watched with wide, kind eyes.

Suddenly, the drumstick slipped! Thump—it rolled under the couch. Rumi’s cheeks felt hot. The room got quiet. The worries came back, like tiny bees. What if everyone laughed?

Lila scooted close. “It’s okay,” she whispered. “We can help.” Benji lifted the couch just a little. Mia reached her tiny paw. “Got it!” she squeaked, holding the drumstick high.

Rumi felt the worries float away. “Thank you,” Rumi said. “I will try again.” Rumi took another breath. In… out… Tap, tap, ta-TAP! TAP, ta-TAP, tap-tap! The rhythm was steady and strong, like a brave heartbeat. Rumi smiled. The friends smiled too. The living room seemed to hum with joy.

When the song ended, everyone clapped and stomped their feet. Clap-clap! Stomp-stomp! “That was wonderful,” Mama said. “I felt the story in my chest.”

Benji said, “Your drum sounds like thunder and hugs.”

Mia twirled her scarf. “I want to learn,” she said.

Lila rang her bell softly. “Me too,” she added.

Rumi’s ears stood tall again. “I can teach a little,” Rumi said. “We can trade! I can learn your sky dance. I can taste your honey. I can ring your bell.”

They took turns. Mia swirled her scarf while Rumi tapped a gentle beat. Benji shared another sweet drop. Lila rang ting-ting between the drum taps. The room filled with sounds, colors, and smiles. It felt like a warm blanket of friendship.

When it was time to rest, everyone cuddled into the pillows. Papa said, “What did we learn today?”

Rumi raised a paw. “I learned that sharing our traditions is brave,” Rumi said. “When we share, we understand each other. We respect each other. And we feel close, like family.”

Mia nodded. “I learned to listen with kind ears.”

Benji added, “I learned new sounds and new tastes.”

Lila whispered, “I learned to be brave too.”

Mama hugged Rumi. “Cultural appreciation means we enjoy and respect what makes each of us special.”

Rumi hugged back. The drum sat quietly, warm from playing. The living room felt safe and bright. Rumi smiled and said, “I am brave. I can share. We can share together.”

The End.
