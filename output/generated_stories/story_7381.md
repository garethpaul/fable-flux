# Penny Penguin's Peaceful Garden Day

Penny Penguin waddled onto her favorite balcony garden, her orange beak gleaming in the morning sunshine. The small space was filled with colorful pots of flowers, herbs, and tiny vegetables. She loved spending time here every day.

"What a beautiful morning!" Penny said, stretching her flippers wide. She could smell the sweet lavender and feel the warm sun on her black and white feathers.

But today felt different. Penny noticed her tummy felt fluttery, and her mind was jumping around like a bouncy ball. She had so many things she wanted to do in her garden!

"I want to water the flowers AND plant new seeds AND pick tomatoes AND..." Penny stopped mid-sentence. She felt dizzy thinking about everything at once.

Penny sat down on her favorite little stool next to the mint plants. She remembered what her grandmother had taught her about feeling overwhelmed.

"Take three deep breaths, Penny," she whispered to herself. "Smell the mint. Feel your feet on the ground."

Penny closed her eyes and breathed in slowly through her beak. The mint smelled fresh and cool. She breathed out slowly, feeling her shoulders relax. She did this two more times.

"Ahh," Penny sighed, opening her eyes. "That feels much better."

Now Penny could think more clearly. She looked around her garden with calm, focused eyes instead of rushing thoughts.

"What does my garden need most today?" she asked herself quietly.

She noticed the tomato plants looked thirsty - their leaves were drooping slightly. The flower pots near the railing seemed dry too. But the herbs looked happy and green.

"I think watering comes first," Penny decided. "Then I can make a plan for the rest."

Penny got her small watering can and filled it with cool water. As she gently poured water on each plant, she paid attention to how the soil looked and smelled. She watched tiny water droplets sparkle on the leaves.

"Thank you for the water," she pretended the tomatoes were saying. This made her giggle.

After watering, Penny sat back down on her stool. "Now, what else would make me and my garden happy today?"

She thought carefully. "I could pick some herbs for Mama's dinner. And maybe I could read my favorite book here in the sunshine."

Penny realized she didn't need to do everything at once. She could choose what felt right for today.

When her little sister Pip came outside, Penny was sitting peacefully, reading her book with a small basket of fresh herbs beside her.

"Penny, you look so calm and happy," Pip said. "Can you teach me how to feel peaceful like that?"

Penny smiled warmly. "Of course! First, we sit quietly and take three deep breaths. Then we notice what we can see, smell, and hear in our garden."

The two penguin sisters sat together, breathing slowly and listening to the gentle sounds of birds chirping and leaves rustling in the breeze.

"I feel much calmer now," Pip said softly.

"When we slow down and pay attention to right now, we can think better and feel happier," Penny explained. "And it's okay to ask for help when we need it, just like you asked me."

As the sun climbed higher, Penny felt proud. She had turned a scattered, worried morning into a peaceful, productive day by taking time to breathe, notice her feelings, and make thoughtful choices.

"Tomorrow I'll remember to start with deep breaths," Penny said to herself, smiling contentedly in her beautiful balcony garden.

The End.
