# Joey Roo Helps the Backyard

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey. He had soft brown fur and big springy feet. One sunny morning, Joey Roo hopped into the sunny backyard. The grass smelled sweet. A warm breeze tickled his ears. Birds sang, “Tweet, tweet!” and the sun felt cozy on Joey Roo’s back.

“Good morning, backyard!” Joey Roo said with a big smile.

He looked around. Oh no! The wind had blown paper cups, a red ribbon, and snack wrappers all over the lawn. The gate was open, and the garden hose lay in a twisty tangle. A small ant line had to walk around a cup mountain. The bird bath was low on water. Joey Roo put his paws on his chest.

“This is our shared place,” Joey Roo said. “We all use it. So we take care of it. That is being a good helper in our home and for our friends. That is civic responsibility!”

Just then, his friends hopped and fluttered over. Pip the parrot flapped down to the fence. “Squawk! Messy, messy!”

Mimi the mouse peeked from a flower pot. “Eek! I can’t find my tiny path,” she squeaked.

Buzzy the bee buzzed near a crumpled wrapper. “Bzz, I need flowers, not plastic.”

Joey Roo nodded. “Let’s help. We can clean up. We can make a plan. We can do it together.”

Pip puffed his feathers. “I like to give orders! I will be the boss. You all do what I say.”

Mimi’s whiskers drooped. “I don’t like being bossed,” she said softly.

Joey Roo took a deep breath. He wanted everyone to feel heard. He remembered: When we share a space, we share choices, too. That is part of being responsible together.

“Friends,” Joey Roo said, “let’s use teamwork. We can talk. We can listen. We can make a fair plan.”

Pip tilted his head. “Squawk? A fair plan?”

“Yes,” Joey Roo said. “We can each pick a job. We can help each other. We can take turns.”

Buzzy buzzed a happy “Bzz-yes!”

Joey Roo clapped his paws. “First, let’s sort the trash.” He pointed to two buckets. “Blue bucket for recycling. Green bucket for trash.”

Mimi’s eyes sparkled. “I can carry tiny bits!” She picked up a shiny wrapper and dropped it in the green bucket. “Plink!”

Pip grabbed the red ribbon with his beak. “This can be used again,” he squawked. He placed it on a “use-again” rock. “Thump!”

Buzzy zipped to a cup near the ant line. “Excuse me, ants,” he buzzed kindly. Joey Roo gently lifted the cup. “Your path is open now,” he said to the ants.

As they worked, Joey Roo used kind words. “Thank you, Pip. Good sorting, Mimi. Nice buzzing, Buzzy!”

They reached the twisty hose. Pip tugged one way. Mimi tugged another way. “It’s stuck!” they groaned.

Joey Roo watched their faces. Pip looked grumpy; Mimi looked worried. Joey Roo said, “Let’s try a new way. I will hold the middle. Pip, you pull slowly. Mimi, count to three.”

Mimi squeaked, “One, two, three!” Pip pulled gently. Joey Roo steadied the hose. “Swoop!” The tangle came free.

“We did it!” they cheered.

Next, Joey Roo turned to the bird bath. The sun made the empty bowl feel warm. “Pip, would you like to help fill this? Clean water helps birds. It helps our backyard friends.”

Pip nodded. “I want to help,” he said. He felt proud to be a helper, not a boss. Joey Roo turned the faucet a tiny bit. Water sang, “Glug-glug.” Pip watched, then called, “Stop!” when the bowl was full. The water sparkled like tiny stars.

Mimi pointed to the open gate. “The gate is open,” she said. “Leaves might blow in.”

“Let’s close it,” Joey Roo said. He hopped over and clicked the latch. “Click!” He made a little sign from the red ribbon and a cup lid. With Mimi’s tiny paw prints and Buzzy’s pollen dots, the sign said, “Please close the gate. Thank you!”

“That’s creative and helpful,” Buzzy buzzed. “Bzz-beautiful!”

They finished picking up the last wrappers. The air smelled fresh. The grass felt soft. The yard looked tidy and bright. Joey Roo took a deep, happy breath.

Just then, a small gust of wind whooshed by. “Whoosh!” A paper leaf danced toward the gate. It bumped the new sign. The gate stayed shut. The leaf slid to the side. “It works!” Mimi cheered.

Pip looked at Joey Roo. “I liked our plan,” he said. “I felt listened to.”

Mimi nodded. “I felt safe.”

Buzzy buzzed, “I felt helpful!”

Joey Roo’s heart felt warm. “We took care of our shared space,” he said. “We helped our friends. We used kind words. We worked together. That is civic responsibility.”

They all smiled. The birds sang again. The sun glowed golden. Joey Roo picked up a small seed packet from the “use-again” rock. “Let’s plant flowers,” he said. “Flowers help bees and make our backyard pretty.”

They made little holes. Pat, pat, pat. They dropped in seeds. Plink, plink, plink. They covered them with soft dirt. Swish. Then Pip brought a tiny cup of water. “Sip, sip,” the ground drank.

Joey Roo patted the earth. “We care for our place today,” he said, “and we care for it tomorrow.”

Pip, Mimi, and Buzzy all said together, “We are helpers!”

Joey Roo looked at the clean, sunny backyard. He felt proud. “We can do small things to help everyone,” he said. “That is how we live together in a happy place.”

The wind blew gently. The ribbon sign fluttered. The bird bath sparkled. The seeds rested under the warm dirt, ready to grow.

Joey Roo smiled. “Thank you, friends,” he said. “Let’s check the yard again later.”

“Squawk-okay!” “Eek-yes!” “Bzz-yes!”

They hopped, squeaked, and buzzed off for a snack, knowing they had done a big, good thing together.

The End.
