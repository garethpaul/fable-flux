# Pippin the Penguin Makes a Plan

Pippin was a cheerful penguin with a bright yellow scarf. He waddled down the stairs, step, step, step, into the basement lab. The lab smelled like lemon soap and warm cocoa. Little lights blinked. Beep-beep! A soft fan hummed. Whirr. The floor felt cool under Pippin’s feet.

“Hello, lab!” Pippin said. “Today I will make a smoothie and a new bubble blower!”

On the big table, Pippin set two things. On the left were red strawberries and leafy spinach. On the right was a shiny bubble wand and a bottle of bubble mix. Pippin clapped his flippers. “Yay!”

Then Pippin heard a cough. “Ahem.” It was Tock the Timer, a little round timer with a smiling face. “Tick-tock, Pippin. You only have time for one big project,” Tock said, wiggling his hand.

Pippin blinked. His tummy made a tiny rumble. He wanted a smoothie. He also wanted bubbles. He felt a little tug in his chest. “Oh no,” he said. “I want both!”

From a shelf, Dr. Dot the Dots-and-Spots Robot rolled out. Her buttons glowed blue. “We can help you decide,” she beeped kindly. “What do you need, Pippin?”

Pippin took a deep breath. He smelled the sweet strawberries. “Mmm. I need a healthy snack. My tummy is hungry.”

He touched the bubble wand. It was smooth and cool. “And I want to play with bubbles! I love bubbles.”

Tock ticked. “Tick, tick, tick. One big project.”

Pippin felt worried. He puffed his cheeks. He looked at the floor. Then he remembered what Mama Penguin always said: “Use your words. Make a plan. You can talk it out.”

Pippin lifted his head. He stood up tall, as tall as a small penguin could. “I can try to talk and make a deal,” he told himself. His heart felt warm and brave.

He looked at Tock and Dr. Dot. “Can we make a plan together?” Pippin asked. “I want a smoothie. I also want bubbles. How can we do both?”

“That is called negotiation,” Dr. Dot said with a cheerful beep. “We listen. We share. We make a fair plan.”

Pippin nodded. “I will share first,” he said. “I feel hungry, and smoothies help my body. They help me feel strong.”

Tock nodded. “Your wellness matters. Tick.”

Dr. Dot whirred. “And bubbles help your joy. Play time matters, too.”

Pippin flapped his flippers. “Yes!”

“Here is an idea,” Tock said. “What if you make a small smoothie now and small bubbles after? Two small projects fit in one big time.”

Pippin tilted his head. “Hmm. That sounds good. But I also want to help clean the lab. It is good to be tidy.” He pointed to a few tiny crumbs by the blender. “Can we make time for that?”

Dr. Dot blinked her blue lights. “Let us try this: Step one, make a quick, healthy smoothie. Step two, clean as we go. Step three, take a bubble break. Step four, tidy up. Is that fair?”

Pippin listened to his tummy. Grumble. He looked at the bubble wand. He looked at the crumbs. He felt calm. “Yes,” he said. “That is fair. Thank you. We made a plan!”

Pippin washed the berries. Splash, splash. He added spinach leaves. Swish. He poured cool milk. Glug-glug. The blender went bzzzz! The smoothie was pink with little green specks. It smelled like summer.

He took a sip. “Mmm!” The smoothie was sweet and fresh. His belly felt happy and strong. He smiled. “Wellness win!”

As he made the smoothie, Pippin wiped the table. Wipe, wipe. He put peels in the bin. Plop. “Cleaning as I go,” he sang. “I am neat and I am smart!”

Tock smiled. “You are right on time.”

Next, Pippin dipped the wand into the bubble mix. He blew gently. Puh! Bubbles floated up, shiny and round. They looked like little rainbows. “Pop! Pop!” Pippin giggled as two bubbles touched his beak. He took three deep breaths. In… out… in… out… in… out. “Bubble breaths help me feel calm,” he said softly.

Dr. Dot clapped her robot hands. “You followed the plan. How do you feel?”

Pippin stood tall again. “I feel proud. I used my words. I listened. I made a fair plan. I took care of my body and my space. I had fun, too!”

Tock chimed, “Ding!” The time was up. The lab was tidy. The smoothie cup was empty. The bubble wand was clean and dry.

Pippin looked around. The lights still blinked. Beep-beep. The fan still hummed. Whirr. The basement lab felt cozy and bright.

He patted his chest. “I can do hard things. When I want two things, I can talk, listen, and make a plan. That is negotiation.” He smiled a big penguin smile. “I am confident!”

Dr. Dot nodded. “And you stayed well, inside and out.”

Pippin waved his flippers. “Thank you, friends,” he said. “Tomorrow we can make a new plan!”

He waddled back up the stairs, step, step, step, with a happy heart and a calm mind.

The lesson: Use your words. Listen to others. Make a fair plan. Take care of your body and your space. You can do it!

The End.
