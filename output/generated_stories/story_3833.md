# Ellie Shares the Shovel

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, she trotted to the old garden shed behind the house. The shed smelled like fresh soil and sweet wood. Sunlight made little dust sparkles dance in the air.

“I want to plant seeds,” Ellie said, swishing her little tail. “I will grow big, bright flowers!”

She pushed the shed door with her round forehead. Creak! The door opened. Inside, Ellie saw a shiny red shovel, a small green rake, and a bucket that smelled like rain. She touched the shovel with her trunk. It felt cool and smooth.

“Perfect,” Ellie whispered. “I can dig a hole for my seeds.”

Just then, her friends peeked in. Pip the puppy wagged his tail. Mia the mouse twitched her whiskers. Buzzy the bumblebee hummed a soft, happy tune.

“Hi, Ellie!” barked Pip. “Can we help?”

“I want to rake the soil,” squeaked Mia.

“I can buzz and check the flowers,” hummed Buzzy.

Ellie looked at the shiny shovel. She hugged it close with her trunk. “I found it first,” she said. “I need it.”

Pip’s ears drooped. Mia’s whiskers wiggled in worry. Buzzy’s hum got quiet.

Ellie marched to the dirt patch by the shed. She began to dig. Scrape, scrape. The shovel cut the soil. Ellie’s feet sank a little in the soft earth. She smiled. “This is fun.”

But the hole was big, and the soil was heavy. Ellie puffed. “Hoo—ahh.” Her trunk felt tired. Her ears got warm. The sun tickled her back.

Pip stepped closer. “I can fetch water,” he said gently. “My tongue says the air is dry.”

Mia held up the little rake. “I can smooth the edges,” she squeaked. “We can be a team.”

Ellie clutched the shovel. She did not want to let go. “I started,” she said. “I should finish.”

Buzzy hovered and sniffed the air with his tiny bee nose. “Friend hum,” he buzzed softly. “When friends help, work is light.”

Ellie dug again. Scrape, scrape. The dirt clumped in heavy lumps. The shovel slip-slid. Ellie’s trunk shook. She sighed. “This is hard.”

Pip set his paw on Ellie’s foot. His paw was warm and soft. “We want to help,” he said. “We like helping you.”

Ellie looked at her friends’ faces. Pip’s eyes were kind. Mia’s nose twitched with hope. Buzzy’s wings made a gentle breeze that cooled Ellie’s cheeks. Ellie’s chest felt tight. She did not want to hurt her friends’ feelings. She also did not want to let go of the shovel.

Ellie closed her eyes. She took a slow breath. In, out. She thought, If I share, the work will be easier. If I share, we can all smile.

Ellie opened her eyes. “Let’s solve this together,” she said. “We can take turns with the shovel. We can make a plan.”

Pip’s tail thumped. Mia clapped her tiny paws. Buzzy hummed a happy tune.

Ellie said, “First, I dig five scoops. Then Pip digs five scoops. Then Mia smooths with the rake. Buzzy checks the hole and hums when it is ready. We share. We take turns.”

“Good plan!” yipped Pip.

“Good plan!” squeaked Mia.

“Good plan!” buzzed Buzzy.

Ellie counted, “One, two, three, four, five!” She passed the shovel to Pip. The metal was cool in Pip’s paws. He dug. Scrape, scrape. He grinned, and his tongue flopped out.

“Now me!” said Mia. She raked the edges, shh, shh, shh, making the hole neat and round. Little pebbles clicked like tiny bells.

Buzzy hovered low. “The soil smells rich,” he buzzed. “The hole feels deep enough. Hummm.”

Ellie felt light and happy. The work went fast. The friends laughed. The sun felt warm, and the breeze smelled like grass.

Soon the hole was ready. Ellie poured seeds from the bucket. They tickled her trunk like tiny raindrops. Pip fetched water. Slosh, slosh. He poured gently. Mia patted the soil smooth. Buzzy hummed a soft song, like a lullaby for seeds.

Ellie looked at the neat little mound. “We did it,” she said. “Together!”

Pip barked, “We shared!”

Mia squeaked, “We solved the problem!”

Buzzy buzzed, “We are good friends.”

Ellie’s heart felt big and warm. She touched the shovel and smiled. “Sharing made this easier. Sharing made this fun. When we share, everyone gets a turn. When we share, we all win.”

They closed the shed door. Creak, click. The garden smelled sweet and wet. The friends sat in the soft grass and watched a ladybug crawl over a leaf.

“Tomorrow,” Ellie said, “we can water the seeds again. We can share the watering can, too.”

“Yes!” said Pip.

“Yes!” said Mia.

“Yes!” buzzed Buzzy.

Ellie flapped her ears. “Thank you for helping me,” she said. “I like our plan. I like our team.”

The friends bumped noses and paws and trunks. They felt calm and proud. The sun dipped low, and the sky turned peach and pink.

Ellie whispered to the sleepy garden, “We share. We care. We grow.”

The End.
