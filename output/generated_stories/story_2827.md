# Cubby’s Pantry Parade

Cubby was a friendly bear cub with soft, fuzzy fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a snug den where the pantry was full of yummy smells. The pantry had neat shelves, tiny jars that clicked when the lids turned, and baskets that whispered when little paws brushed by.

Every morning, Cubby had a special routine. He loved routines. Routines made him feel safe and calm. He would hum, “Step one, wash paws. Step two, check shelves. Step three, snack and clean.” He sang it softly like a little song.

One sunny morning, Cubby padded into the pantry. The cool floor felt smooth under his paws. He sniffed the air. “Mmm,” he said. “I smell honey, oats, and apples.” His tummy gave a happy rumble.

Cubby wiggled his whiskers and began his task. “Step one, wash paws.” He washed with warm, soapy water. Splish, splash. “Step two, check shelves.” He looked at the jars. He lined them up: honey, oats, berries, nuts. Click, clack, click. “Step three, snack and clean.” He planned to nibble an apple slice and sweep up crumbs.

Just then, Cubby heard little feet. Tap-tap-tap. Two tiny field mice peeked in. One wore a blue ribbon. The other had a shiny pebble necklace.

“Hello!” said Cubby, smiling. “I’m Cubby. Welcome to my pantry.”

“I’m Pip,” said the mouse with the ribbon.

“I’m Dot,” said the one with the pebble. “We are hungry. May we share a snack?”

Cubby’s ears twitched. He liked to share, but he also liked his routine. “Yes,” he said, “We can share. But we follow the routine. We wash paws, we check shelves, then we snack and clean.”

Pip nodded fast. “Okay!”

Dot wrinkled her nose. “Do we have to do it that way? I think we should snack first!”

Cubby took a slow breath. He remembered being open-minded. “Hmm,” he said. “Let’s think together. My routine helps us stay safe and tidy. If we snack first, there may be crumbs. The floor might get sticky.”

Dot looked down. “I don’t like sticky,” she said softly.

Cubby smiled. “How about this? We keep the routine, but we can make it fun. We can make it a Pantry Parade! We march, we sing, and we work together.”

Pip clapped her tiny paws. “A parade!”

Dot’s eyes sparkled. “I like parades.”

Cubby raised his paw like a leader. “Step one: wash paws.” He hummed a little drum beat. Pip and Dot giggled and splish-splashed their tiny paws in the warm water. “Soapy bubbles, scrub-scrub,” sang Cubby.

“Step two: check shelves,” Cubby said. He made his voice deep and brave. “Jars in a row!” He and the mice lined up the jars. Click, clack. Pip counted, “One honey, two oats, three berry jams!” Dot made the baskets go swish, swish. They all cheered when the labels faced forward.

“Step three: snack and clean,” Cubby said. He sliced an apple. Crunch! The apple smelled sweet. He set out nut crumbs and a tiny spoon of honey. “We share fairly,” Cubby said. “Each friend gets the same.”

Pip nodded. “Fair is friendly.”

Dot peered at the honey. “I like a little, not a lot.”

Cubby’s ears perked. “That is helpful to know. We can be open-minded. We can make choices that feel right for each of us.” He gave Dot a tiny drizzle and Pip a tiny drizzle too. Then he saved a little extra for later, just in case.

They munched together. Crunch, crunch. Lick, lick. The pantry smelled like apples and warm honey. Their tummies felt happy and full.

When the snack was done, Cubby held up a small broom. “Now we clean,” he said. “We take turns. That is fair.” He brushed the floor. Swish, swish. Pip gathered crumbs with a little dustpan. Tap, tap. Dot wiped the shelf with a soft cloth. Wipe, wipe.

Dot smiled. “This is like a dance.”

Cubby laughed. “Yes! We are a team. That is called coordination. We move together, and we help each other.”

They finished the Pantry Parade with a bow. Cubby looked around. The shelves were tidy. The floor was clean. The jars were lined up like little soldiers. His heart felt warm and proud.

Pip whispered, “Your routine is smart.”

Dot said, “I thought I wanted to snack first. But washing and checking first made it better. Thank you for listening to me and for the parade.”

Cubby patted their tiny heads gently. “Thank you for sharing and helping. When we follow a good routine, we feel safe. When we are open-minded, we can make the routine fun. When we are fair, everyone smiles.”

Pip and Dot waved goodbye and scampered out, their feet going tap-tap-tap.

Cubby put away the broom. He sang his song again, soft and happy. “Step one, wash paws. Step two, check shelves. Step three, snack and clean.”

He took a deep breath. The pantry felt calm and cozy. The jars gleamed. Cubby felt ready for the day.

The End.
