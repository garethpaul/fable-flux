# Spark the Robot Dog’s Cozy Bedroom Mission

The cozy bedroom was warm and sunny. Soft curtains swished like whispers. A little lamp glowed like a tiny moon. A helpful robot dog named Spark blinked his bright blue eyes. Beep-beep! His metal nose wiggled. His smooth paws clicked on the wooden floor.

“Good morning, Spark,” said Mia, patting his shiny head. Her hand felt the cool, smooth metal. “Today, I want to do my bedtime jobs. I want to do them by myself.”

Spark wagged his little metal tail. “Woof-beep! I can help you remember,” he said in a gentle, friendly voice. “You can do it, Mia.”

Mia smiled. She felt brave and curious. “Let’s make a plan,” she said. Spark’s belly screen lit up with a soft hum. A picture list popped up: brush teeth, tidy toys, pick pajamas, choose a bedtime book.

“I can do it!” Mia said. She stood tall in her fuzzy socks. The socks were purple and felt like little clouds.

“First: tidy toys,” Spark beeped. He pointed his nose toward the floor. Blocks and stuffed animals made a colorful mountain. Mia’s eyes got big.

“That’s a lot,” she whispered. Her tummy felt squiggly.

“You can do it one by one,” said Spark. “Small steps. I will count.”

Mia took a deep breath. She picked up a red block. Clack! Into the box it went. Then a blue block. Clack! Then a soft bear. Snuggle, into the basket. Spark counted, “One… two… three!” His tail clicked happily.

“I’m doing it!” Mia said. She felt proud. Her shoulders relaxed. The room smelled like clean blankets and sweet soap. She liked that smell. It felt like calm.

“Next: brush teeth,” Spark said. He trotted beside her to the little sink in the corner of the cozy bedroom. The faucet water went shh-shh. The toothpaste smelled minty and fresh. Mia squeezed a tiny dot on her toothbrush.

“I can brush all by myself,” Mia said. She moved the brush in little circles. “Up and down, round and round,” Spark sang in a soft beep-song. Mia giggled and brushed. Bubbles tickled her tongue. She spit and rinsed. Drip, drip. She wiped her mouth with a soft towel.

“All clean!” she said, showing a big smile.

“Shiny chompers!” Spark cheered.

“Now: pick pajamas,” he beeped. Mia opened her drawer. Swish. Inside were star jammies, rainbow jammies, and dinosaur jammies. She touched the fabric. The star jammies felt smooth and cool. The dinosaur jammies felt cozy and warm.

“I want the dinosaur ones,” Mia said. “They feel snuggly.” She changed by herself. The soft cloth made a whisper sound as she pulled the shirt over her head. She wiggled and giggled.

“Good choice,” said Spark. “You listened to your body. That is independent.”

Mia’s eyes shone. “I am independent,” she said. The word felt big and special in her mouth.

“Last: choose a bedtime book,” Spark said. He opened the book basket with his paw. Flip, flip. Mia looked at the covers. Animals, trucks, and a book about the moon. Her fingers tapped the moon book. She liked the silver picture. It looked shiny and cool.

“I will read the moon book,” she decided. She climbed into bed. The blankets felt soft as marshmallows. Spark hopped onto the rug and sat beside the bed, his eyes twinkling like little stars.

Mia held the book. She opened to the first page. “I can start reading,” she said. “I will try.”

“You try first,” Spark agreed. “If you need help, I can help.”

Mia sounded out a little word. “M…o…o…n.” She grinned. “Moon!”

“Yes!” said Spark. “Curiosity helps our brains learn.”

Mia turned the page. The paper made a quiet crinkle. She looked at the pictures and told the story in her own words. “The moon is big and bright. It helps the night feel cozy,” she said.

When she came to a hard word, she paused. Her eyebrows scrunched. “Spark, can you share a hint?”

Spark wagged. “It starts with s,” he said softly. “S…ky.”

“Sky!” Mia said. “Thank you for sharing a hint. I can do the rest.”

They finished the book together. Mia closed it with a gentle thump. She felt calm and sleepy. The lamp glowed warm and gold. The curtains sighed. Spark’s belly made a soft purr-hum.

“Mia,” Spark asked, “how do you feel?”

“I feel proud,” Mia said. “I cleaned up. I brushed my teeth. I picked my pajamas. I read my book. I did my jobs by myself. I asked for help when I needed a hint. I can be independent.”

Spark nuzzled her hand. “Yes. You were curious, you tried your best, and you completed your tasks. That is strong and smart.”

Mia hugged Spark’s smooth head. “Tomorrow, I can try new jobs,” she whispered. “I can share my skills with my little brother, too.”

Spark’s eyes blinked slow and happy. “Woof-beep. We can make a new plan together. Small steps, big hearts.”

Mia yawned a tiny yawn. “Good night, Spark.”

“Good night, Mia,” said Spark. He dimmed his lights. The cozy bedroom felt safe and snug, like a warm hug. Mia closed her eyes, smiling.

Tonight, Mia learned she could try, finish, and shine—one small step at a time.

The End.
