# Gigi the Giraffe and the Honest Switch

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with big gentle eyes and very long eyelashes. She loved words. She loved how they felt in her mouth: please, thank you, hola, jambo, kindness. Gigi liked to practice new words every day.

One rainy afternoon, Gigi visited the basement lab at the community center. The lab was cool and quiet. Lights blinked. Buttons clicked. The air hummed softly, like bees saying hello. It smelled like clean soap and warm popcorn from snack time. On a big table sat a bright box with glowing letters that said, “Word Helper.”

“Hello, little lights,” Gigi whispered. “Today I will learn new words.” She smiled and swished her tail.

Down the stairs came Mr. Mole, the kind lab helper, wearing tiny glasses. “Hi, Gigi,” he said. “I must take these books upstairs. Then I’ll help you with the Word Helper.”

“I can wait,” said Gigi. “I will read labels.” She loved labels. She read carefully, moving slowly so her long neck did not bump anything. “Start. Stop. Record. Listen.”

Mr. Mole nodded. “What a careful giraffe. I’ll be right back!”

When Mr. Mole left, Gigi heard a soft beep-beep-beep. The Word Helper screen flashed. A green light turned yellow. “Oh,” Gigi said. “Is that okay?” She took a deep breath and listened. The hum sounded louder, like brrrrrrrr.

Gigi’s heart thumped. “I want to help,” she said to herself. “I want to do the right thing.” She remembered the little sign on the wall: “If you see a problem, tell a grown-up.” But Mr. Mole was upstairs.

Gigi looked closer. A tiny slip of paper peeked out from under the box. She gently pulled it free with her lips. The note said, “If light turns yellow, press the Honest Switch to pause.” It showed a picture of a small blue switch with a smiley face.

Gigi spotted it! A little blue switch sat near the side. But another switch, a shiny red one, was bigger.

“The red one looks fun,” Gigi whispered. “But the note says the blue one.” Gigi felt a tug inside. She could press the big red switch and pretend it was right. No one would know. Or she could be honest and follow the note, even if it was small and not shiny.

She put a hoof on her chest. “Integrity means telling the truth and doing what is right, even when no one is watching,” she said softly. “I will choose the blue switch.”

Gigi stretched her long neck and pressed the blue switch with her gentle nose. Click!

The hum softened to mmmmmm. The yellow light turned calm. The screen showed a smiling face and the word “Paused.”

Gigi sighed with relief. “Good choice,” she whispered. She felt warm and proud inside.

Just then, a little mouse named Mina peeked in. “Hi, Gigi! I’m here to volunteer. I can carry tools.” Mina wore tiny gloves and a big smile.

“Hi, Mina,” said Gigi. “I think I paused the Word Helper. I read the note and used the Honest Switch.”

Mina’s eyes sparkled. “That shows integrity! You followed the rules.”

Footsteps pitter-pattered on the stairs. Mr. Mole returned with more books and a tired smile. “Thank you for waiting! Oh! The Word Helper is paused. What happened?”

Gigi took a deep breath. “The light turned yellow. I found a note. It said to press the Honest Switch. I did. I wanted to help the right way.”

Mr. Mole’s whiskers twitched happily. “Gigi, that was thoughtful and brave. You showed integrity. You told the truth and followed directions. Thank you.”

Mina held up her tiny hands. “We can help more! We can clean the buttons. We can sort the word cards by color.” She bounced. “I want to volunteer!”

Gigi nodded. “I will help, too.” She took initiative. “I can read labels while you dust. Then we can practice words together.”

They worked as a team. Swish went Gigi’s tail as she read: “Green. Blue. Pause. Play.” Tap-tap went Mina’s cloth on the buttons. Mr. Mole checked wires, humming a happy tune.

“Listen,” said Gigi. “Please. Gracias. Merci. Thank you. Xie xie. Asante.” The words felt like music, soft and sweet.

Mina giggled. “Words are friends! They help us be kind.”

At last, Mr. Mole flipped a safe lever. “All fixed! Ready to learn?” He looked at Gigi. “Would you like to start us off?”

Gigi stood tall. “Yes, please.” She pressed Play with her nose. The machine chirped, “Hello, friends!” A rainbow of letters danced on the screen. The room smelled like crayons and clean air. The hum sounded like a happy purr.

Gigi looked at her friends. “I am glad I was honest,” she said. “I feel peaceful.”

Mr. Mole nodded. “Integrity keeps the lab safe and our hearts calm.”

Mina clapped. “And volunteering makes work light!”

Gigi smiled. “And taking initiative helps us start doing good.”

They practiced more words together. Kindness. Truth. Help. Please. The words floated like bubbles, bright and gentle. Gigi felt proud, not for being perfect, but for being honest and helpful.

When it was time to go, Gigi said, “Today I learned that integrity is doing the right thing, even when no one sees you. It makes the world safer and kinder.”

Mr. Mole waved. “Thank you, Gigi and Mina. You made the lab better with your honesty and your helping hearts.”

Gigi walked up the stairs with Mina, step by careful step. The rain had stopped. The air smelled fresh. Gigi whispered, “Thank you,” to the sky.

The End.
