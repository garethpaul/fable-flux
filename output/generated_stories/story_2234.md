# Benny Beaver’s Break-and-Boost

Benny Beaver had a cozy home office. His desk was made of smooth wood that smelled like fresh trees. His chair was soft and squeaky. Click, click, click went his little keyboard. Benny was a very busy beaver.

“I must finish my big plan,” Benny said. His tail thumped the floor—thump, thump—when he felt excited. He loved to build things, and today he was drawing a big bridge on his screen.

But after a while, Benny’s eyes felt tired. His shoulders drooped. His paws felt slow. “Oh no,” he sighed. “I’m not doing a good job. Maybe I’m not good enough.” His tail went still.

Outside, the wind whispered. A ray of sunshine hugged the window. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” But Benny did not feel cheery. He frowned at his plan. “It’s too hard,” he muttered. “I can’t get it right.”

Just then, his friend, Dot the little blue bird, fluttered to the windowsill. Tap-tap went her beak on the glass. “Hello, Benny!” she chirped. “What’s that long face?”

Benny opened the window. A cool breeze tickled his nose. “Hi, Dot,” he said softly. “I’m trying to finish my bridge plan, but I keep messing up. Maybe I’m not good at this.”

Dot tilted her head. “Hmm. Your office smells like fresh wood and warm tea,” she said. “But it sounds like your body needs a wiggle and your heart needs a hug.”

“A wiggle and a hug?” Benny asked.

“Yes!” Dot nodded. “When work feels hard, we can move our bodies. We can take a tiny exercise break. Moving makes our brains bright and our hearts brave.”

Benny looked at his screen. The lines were squiggly. His plan felt jumbly. He took a slow breath. “Okay,” he said. “Let’s try.”

Dot clapped her wings. “Wiggle time!”

Benny stood up from his squeaky chair. He stretched his arms high. Stretch, stretch. He touched his toes. Tap, tap. He rolled his shoulders. Round and round. He hopped in place. Hop, hop, hop!

“Count with me!” Dot chirped. “One, two, three, four, five!”

Benny’s cheeks felt warm. His heart went pitter-pat in a happy way. He felt the rug soft under his paws. He smelled his lemon tea on the desk. He giggled. “This is fun!”

“Now,” Dot said, “say kind words to yourself.”

Benny blinked. “Kind words to myself?”

“Yes,” Dot sang. “I am strong. I am learning. I can try again.”

Benny put his paw on his chest. “I am strong,” he said. “I am learning. I can try again.”

His tail gave a brave thump. “Thump!”

Benny sat back down. Click, click, click went the keys. He drew one line. He checked it. He drew another line. He smiled. “I’m going slow,” he told Dot. “Slow and steady.”

“That’s persistence,” Dot chirped. “You keep going, even when it’s hard.”

Benny nodded. He took a tiny sip of tea. Sip. He took a deep breath. In and out. His plan started to look neat and tidy. His eyes felt bright again.

Then—uh-oh!—a line went too long. It poked off the page. “Oops,” Benny said. “I made another mistake.”

Dot fluttered closer. “What will you do now?”

Benny felt the worry try to crawl back in. His tail began to droop, but he caught himself. He put his paw on his chest and whispered, “I am strong. I am learning. I can try again.”

He stood up and did five tiny hops. “One, two, three, four, five!” He smiled. “Exercise helps me feel brave.”

He sat down and tried again. Click, click. He fixed the line. He checked his plan. He kept going. Slow. Steady. Patient.

At last, Benny leaned back. He stretched his arms with a happy sigh. “Dot! I did it. My bridge plan is ready!”

Dot twirled in the air. “Cheep-cheep hooray! How do you feel, Benny?”

Benny’s eyes sparkled. “I feel proud,” he said. “I worked hard. I took a break. I moved my body. I used kind words. I kept trying.”

Dot nodded. “That’s self-esteem! It’s the good feeling inside when you know you can learn and grow.”

Benny smiled big. His tail thumped a happy drum. “Thump, thump, thump!”

He saved his plan. Click. He closed his notebook. Snap. The room felt bright and sunny. The soft rug warmed his paws, and the lemon tea smelled sweet.

Benny looked at Dot. “Next time I feel stuck, I will do three things: move my body, say kind words, and try again.”

Dot chirped, “That’s a smart plan.”

Benny waved from the window. “Thanks, Dot.”

“Anytime!” Dot sang as she flew away.

Benny turned back to his desk. He patted the smooth wood and whispered, “I can do big things, one small try at a time.”

Lesson: When work feels hard, take a little exercise break, speak kindly to yourself, and keep trying. You can learn and grow. You can feel proud of you!

The End.
