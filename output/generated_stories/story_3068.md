# Gigi's Special Guest Room Adventure

Gigi the giraffe lived in a cozy house with her family. She had a very long neck that reached up, up, up to the ceiling! Today was extra special because Grandma Giraffe was coming to visit. Mama had asked Gigi to help prepare the guest room.

"Gigi," said Mama with a warm smile, "Grandma will sleep in the guest room tonight. Can you help me make it perfect for her?"

Gigi nodded her long head eagerly. "Yes, Mama! I want Grandma to feel happy and comfortable."

They walked into the guest room together. The morning sunlight streamed through the soft yellow curtains, making everything glow like golden honey. Gigi could smell the fresh, clean scent of the lavender sheets Mama had just washed.

"First, let's make the bed nice and cozy," Mama said, showing Gigi how to smooth the blankets. "Grandma likes her pillows fluffy, just like clouds."

Gigi carefully used her long neck to fluff the pillows, making them bouncy and soft. "Like this, Mama?" she asked, pressing her nose gently into the pillow. It felt squishy and wonderful!

"Perfect!" Mama praised. "Now, what do you think would make Grandma feel extra welcome?"

Gigi thought carefully, tapping her hoof on the wooden floor. She remembered how Grandma always told stories about the old days. "Oh! Grandma loves her photo album with pictures of when you were little, Mama!"

"What a thoughtful idea!" Mama said, giving Gigi a gentle nuzzle. "You're thinking about what makes Grandma happy. That's called empathy - understanding how someone else feels."

Together, they placed Grandma's favorite photo album on the nightstand. Gigi also remembered that Grandma liked to drink chamomile tea before bed, so they put a pretty teacup and saucer nearby.

"Grandma's feet get cold at night," Gigi said, remembering how Grandma would rub her hooves together. "Should we put extra socks in the drawer?"

"You're being so caring and helpful," Mama said proudly. "Yes, let's add some warm, fuzzy socks."

When Grandma arrived that evening, her eyes twinkled like stars when she saw the guest room. "Oh my!" she exclaimed, clapping her hooves together. "This room feels so welcoming and cozy!"

Gigi's heart felt warm and fuzzy, just like the socks they had prepared. "I helped make it special for you, Grandma! Mama taught me to think about what would make you happy."

Grandma bent her long neck down to give Gigi the biggest, most wonderful hug. "You showed such kindness by thinking of my feelings," she said softly. "When we work together and care about others, we create something beautiful."

That night, as Grandma settled into the perfectly prepared bed, she called out, "Thank you, my dear Gigi! This is the most comfortable guest room ever!"

Gigi smiled as she drifted off to sleep in her own room. She had learned that when you listen carefully, follow directions, and think about how others feel, you can make someone's day extra special. Working together with Mama had made the task fun and easy, and sharing in Grandma's happiness made Gigi's heart feel full of joy.

The End.
