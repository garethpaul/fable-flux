# Kody the Koala Learns to Reach High

Kody the koala loved helping Mama in the kitchen. His soft gray fur was always clean, and his black button nose twitched when he smelled yummy food. Today, Mama asked him to get honey from the pantry.

"The honey jar is on the top shelf, Kody," Mama said with a smile. "Can you reach it for our morning toast?"

Kody waddled into the pantry on his short legs. The shelves were tall and filled with colorful boxes and jars. He looked up, up, up at the golden honey jar sitting way at the top.

"I can do this!" Kody said, stretching his arms high. His fuzzy paws reached and reached, but the jar was too far away. "Hmm, that didn't work."

Kody tried jumping. Hop! Hop! Hop! His round belly bounced, but he still couldn't reach the honey. The jar seemed to sparkle in the light, calling to him from above.

"Maybe I need to try a different way," Kody said to himself. He remembered what Mama always told him: "When something is hard, take a deep breath and think of new ideas."

Kody took a slow, deep breath. The pantry smelled like cinnamon and vanilla. He looked around with his bright black eyes. On the bottom shelf, he spotted a sturdy wooden box.

"I wonder if this box could help me," Kody said. He pushed the box with his paws. It was heavy, but he pushed slowly and steadily. Push... push... push. "Almost there!"

The box slid across the floor with a soft scraping sound. Kody placed it carefully under the high shelf. Then he climbed on top with his careful koala paws.

"I'm higher now!" Kody said happily. But when he reached up again, the honey jar was still too far away. "That's okay. I just need to keep trying."

Kody looked around again. He saw another box on a middle shelf. This one was smaller and lighter. "Two boxes might work better than one!"

Very carefully, Kody climbed down and got the second box. He placed it gently on top of the first box. The boxes felt solid and safe under his paws.

"Now I'm even taller!" Kody said. He climbed up slowly, holding on tight. Step by step, paw by paw, he went higher. When he reached the top, he stretched his arm toward the honey jar.

His paw touched the smooth glass! "I can almost reach it!" Kody said. He stretched just a little more, being very careful not to fall. His paw wrapped around the jar handle.

"I did it! I got the honey!" Kody cheered. He climbed down slowly, holding the jar close to his fluffy chest. The honey was warm from the sunlight and smelled sweet and golden.

Mama was waiting in the kitchen. "You were in there for a while, Kody. Did you have trouble reaching the honey?"

"At first it was hard," Kody said proudly. "But I didn't give up! I tried jumping, then I used one box, then I used two boxes. It took patience, but I figured it out!"

Mama hugged Kody tight. "I'm so proud of you! You showed great patience and kept trying new ideas. That's how we learn and grow stronger."

As they spread the golden honey on their warm toast, Kody smiled. He learned that when things are difficult, taking time to think and try different ways can help him succeed.

The End.
