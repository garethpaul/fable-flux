# Penny the Parrot's Colorful Confidence

In a bright art room filled with paints and brushes, lived Penny the Parrot. She had beautiful green feathers and a curved orange beak. Penny loved to create art, but today she felt very sad.

"I'm not good at anything," Penny sighed. She looked at her messy painting on the easel. The colors had run together into a muddy brown blob. *Drip, drop, drip* went the paint onto the floor.

Penny's friend Max the Mouse came into the art room. He was carrying a small soccer ball under his tiny arm.

"Hi Penny! Want to play soccer with me?" asked Max cheerfully.

"No thank you," said Penny, hanging her head low. "I'm terrible at sports. And I'm terrible at art too. Look at my painting!"

Max looked at the painting. The colors swirled together like a chocolate milkshake. "It looks like you tried really hard," said Max kindly.

"But it's all wrong!" squawked Penny. She accidentally knocked over a jar of water with her wing. *Splash!* The water spilled across the table.

"Oh no! I'm sorry, Max. I mess up everything I touch," Penny said. Tears started to form in her bright black eyes.

Max climbed up onto the art table. The room smelled like fresh paint and crayons. Sunlight streamed through the big windows, making rainbow colors on the wall.

"Penny, everyone makes mistakes when they're learning," said Max gently. "I remember when I first tried to kick a soccer ball. I missed completely and fell on my tail!"

Penny giggled a little. "Really?"

"Really! But I kept trying. And you know what? Making mistakes helps us get better," Max explained. "Your painting shows you're brave enough to try new things."

Penny looked at her painting again. Maybe the swirled colors did look kind of interesting. Like a stormy sky or ocean waves.

"I'm sorry I was so grumpy, Max," said Penny. "You're a good friend for trying to cheer me up."

"That's okay," smiled Max. "Friends help each other feel better."

Penny took a deep breath. The art room felt warm and safe. She could hear children laughing outside in the playground.

"Maybe... maybe I could try playing soccer with you?" asked Penny quietly.

"That would be great!" said Max, bouncing the little ball. "And maybe later, you could teach me how to paint?"

Penny's feathers ruffled with excitement. "I'd like that! Even if we're not perfect, we can have fun trying."

They went outside to the grassy playground. Penny tried to kick the ball with her claw. She missed the first time, but she laughed instead of getting upset.

"Good try!" cheered Max. "Try again!"

This time, Penny gently tapped the ball. It rolled slowly across the grass. "I did it!" she squawked happily.

Later, back in the art room, Penny started a new painting. This time, she painted slowly and carefully. She made a picture of her and Max playing soccer together.

"This painting tells a story," said Penny proudly. "It shows that friends help each other learn and grow."

Max clapped his tiny paws. "It's beautiful, Penny! And you know what? You're good at lots of things. You're good at being kind, at trying hard, and at being a wonderful friend."

Penny's chest puffed up with pride. She felt warm and happy inside. "Thank you, Max. I learned something important today. When I make mistakes, it doesn't mean I'm not good enough. It means I'm learning!"

From that day on, Penny tried new things with confidence. She knew that with practice, patience, and good friends, she could improve at anything.

The End.
