# The Fawn Who Shared the Spoon

In a warm kitchen, a gentle deer fawn named Fern stood on a soft rug. The room smelled like cinnamon toast. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. The kettle hummed, and tiny bubbles sang.

Fern wiggled her ears. “Today, I will make pretend soup,” she said. “It will be rainbow soup! It will taste like smiles!”

Fern set small bowls in a line. Clink, clink. She tapped a wooden spoon on the counter. Tap-tap-tap. She dreamed big dreams with her big brown eyes. She imagined green pea planets and carrot comets and noodle rainbows.

Her friend Pip, a tiny field mouse, peeked from a teacup. “Squeak! I want to stir too,” Pip said.

Fern smiled. “Yes! We can both stir. We can use our imaginations.”

Just then, another friend, Tilly the bluebird, fluttered to the windowsill. “Tweet! I will sing the soup a song,” she chirped. The kitchen felt even warmer with music.

Fern lifted the wooden spoon. “This is the Magic Sharing Spoon. It helps us take turns,” she said. “We stir, we count to five, and then we pass it on.”

Pip nodded. Tilly trilled. It felt fair, and it felt fun.

Fern dipped the spoon into an empty pot. She did not need real water or real vegetables. She used her mind. Swish-swish-swish. “I imagine rainbow noodles,” she whispered.

Pip’s whiskers twitched. “I imagine star peas!” he squeaked.

Tilly sang, “Tweet-tweet! I imagine sunshine lemon drops!”

The kitchen filled with pretend smells and pretend steam. Fern could almost taste sweet lemon and warm carrots. Her tummy made a happy rumble.

Then the door creaked open. In walked Grumble, a grumpy raccoon with tired eyes. He had been playing outside and was muddy and hungry.

Grumble sniffed. “Where is the soup? I want it now,” he said.

Fern blinked. “We are making pretend soup,” she said softly.

Grumble frowned. “That is not real. I will take the spoon and make real soup.” He reached for the Magic Sharing Spoon.

Fern held it close. “We take turns,” she said. “We count to five. It is fair.”

Grumble huffed. “I am bigger. I get the spoon.” He tugged. Pip squeaked and hid. Tilly fluffed her feathers.

Fern felt a swirl of feelings. Her heart beat fast. She did not want to upset Grumble, but she wanted fairness. She took a slow breath. She looked at the sunny squares on the floor. She remembered the sharing rule.

Fern said in a calm voice, “Grumble, in this kitchen we are kind. We are fair. We use our imaginations and we take turns. That is justice. Justice means fair for everyone.”

Grumble’s paws slowed. “But I am hungry,” he said, softer now.

Fern nodded. “I hear you,” she said. “First, we can make a quick real snack. Then we can use the Magic Sharing Spoon together. We will follow the turn rule.”

Pip peeked from the teacup. “We can share apples!” he squeaked.

Tilly sang, “Tweet! I can peck crumbs for all.”

Fern set a little plate of apple slices on the table. Crunch. Crunch. Grumble took a bite. His eyes warmed. “Thank you,” he murmured.

Fern smiled. “You are welcome.” She held out the spoon. “Now we will try again. I will stir and count to five. Then Pip, then Tilly, then you, Grumble.”

Grumble blinked. “Me too?”

“Yes,” said Fern. “We are all part of the game. Our ideas matter.”

Fern stirred. “One, two, three, four, five.” She passed the spoon to Pip. Pip’s whiskers danced as he stirred. “One, two, three, four, five.” He passed it to Tilly. Tilly sang and stirred. “One, two, three, four, five.” She passed it to Grumble.

Grumble held the spoon. The kitchen was quiet. He looked at the shiny wood. He took a breath. “I imagine… soft cloud potatoes,” he said, shy at first. He stirred. “One, two, three, four, five.”

Fern clapped her hooves. Pip squeaked with joy. Tilly twirled.

The pretend soup smelled more wonderful than ever. It smelled like apples and sunshine and clouds. They all dipped their empty spoons in the empty pot and tasted with their imaginations.

“Mmm,” said Fern. “It tastes like friendship.”

“Mmm,” said Pip. “It tastes like stars.”

“Mmm,” said Tilly. “It tastes like songs.”

Grumble smiled, really smiled. “It tastes like being fair,” he said. “Thank you for the turn. I felt heard.” He wiped his muddy paws and put the spoon gently back on the counter.

Fern’s heart felt warm, like the kettle’s song. “We used our imaginations,” she said. “We were fair. We were kind. We shared. That is justice.”

They cleaned up together. Swish went the sponge. Pat went the towels. The kitchen shone. The golden sun squares moved across the rug as the day went on.

Grumble said, “I am grateful for friends who share.”

Fern said, “I am grateful for your cloud potatoes.”

Pip giggled. Tilly hummed a happy tune.

Fern looked at her friends. “When we imagine together, everyone belongs,” she said. “When we are fair, everyone feels good. When we share our ideas, we make something new.”

They all nodded. Their hearts felt full. The warm kitchen felt like home.

The End.
