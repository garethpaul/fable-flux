# Tiko the Tortoise Makes a Plan

In a quiet study corner, a wise tortoise named Tiko sat at a small wooden desk. The desk felt smooth under his little feet. A warm lamp glowed like a tiny sun, making soft yellow light on his paper. Tiko liked the smell of sharpened pencils. It smelled like fresh trees after rain.

Tiko had a goal. He wanted to learn the shapes on a big, colorful chart. There was a circle like a cookie, a square like a box, a triangle like a slice of cheese, and a star that looked like a sparkle. Tiko touched his shell and took a deep breath.

“I can do this,” he whispered. “One step at a time.”

He picked up a blue pencil. It felt cool in his hand. He tried to draw a circle. The pencil squeaked a little, but his circle looked more like an egg.

“Oh,” Tiko said. His voice was calm, but his cheeks felt warm. “This is hard.”

From the doorway, friends peeked in. Pippy the parrot fluffed her green feathers. Malik the mouse twitched his whiskers. Zara the zebra foal tapped a hoof softly on the rug. They were all different, with bright eyes and kind smiles.

“Do you need help?” asked Pippy in a gentle voice.

Tiko blinked slowly. He liked his friends. He also wanted to learn to help himself. He remembered his goal: learn shapes on his own. He also remembered wellness: take breaks, breathe, and be kind to your body and mind.

“Thank you,” Tiko said. “I want to try by myself first. I will ask if I need help.”

“That’s a good plan,” said Zara. “We can sit nearby if you like.”

“Yes,” Tiko said. “Let’s make a deal. You can sit with me. If I try three times and still need help, I will ask. And if I need a stretch, we all stretch, okay?”

Malik nodded. “Deal!” His whiskers wiggled with a happy tickle sound. Pippy added, “We’ll be quiet helpers.”

Tiko made a little chart on his paper. He drew three tiny boxes and put a dot in the first box.

“Try one,” he told himself. He drew another circle. It was wobbly. He smiled a little. “Better.”

He drew a square. One side went slanty. He didn’t feel mad. He remembered wellness. He set down the pencil, rolled his shoulders, and took a slow breath in and out. The lamp hummed a soft song. The room felt cozy and safe.

“Try two,” he said, making a second dot in his chart. He drew a triangle. Pointy and neat! He clapped his little feet on the floor. Tap tap!

Malik whispered, “Nice triangle.” Tiko’s heart felt warm, like a cup of tea.

He looked at the star on the big chart. So many points! His tummy fluttered. He closed his eyes and pictured a calm lake. He opened his eyes and smiled.

“Small steps,” he said. “Goal-setting time.” He wrote in big letters: Today: Circle, Square, Triangle. Tomorrow: Star.

Pippy chirped softly, “Good plan, Tiko.”

Tiko tried the circle again. “Round and slow,” he said. The pencil made a soft shhh sound. The circle looked rounder. He giggled. He drew the square with slow lines. He sang a tiny tune, “Line, stop. Turn, line, stop.” The square turned out straight. He made a third dot on his chart.

“I did my three tries,” Tiko said. He looked at his friends. “Now I will ask for help with the star. Can we make a trade?”

“What kind of trade?” Zara asked with a smile.

Tiko thought. “I will show you my slow-breath trick. You show me a star trick.”

“That’s fair,” said Malik. “We agree.” Pippy nodded. “Good negotiation!”

Tiko showed them: “Breathe in like smelling a flower. Breathe out like blowing a candle.” They all breathed together. It sounded like wind in tall grass.

Zara showed a star trick: “Make a little triangle, then another, then another, around and around.”

Tiko tried. One triangle, then another. The star sparkled on the page. He traced it softly. “I did it with help,” he said, “after I did my best by myself.”

He rubbed his neck. “Stretch time?” he asked.

“Stretch time!” everyone chimed. They reached up high, touched their toes, and shook out their arms. The rug felt soft. The lamp hummed. Tiko’s shell felt warm and steady.

Before bed, Tiko looked at his paper. Circle, square, triangle, and a new star. He made a little heart in the corner.

“What did we learn today?” Pippy asked.

Tiko smiled. “I learned to trust myself. I can make a plan. I can try, rest, and try again. I can ask for help after I do my best. We can make fair deals that help everyone. And we can take good care of our bodies and feelings.”

Malik squeaked, “That’s self-reliance and wellness!”

Zara added, “And smart negotiation.”

Tiko nodded. “Tomorrow, I will learn more. One step at a time.”

The lamp clicked off. The study corner felt calm and happy. Tiko curled up with a peaceful sigh.

The End.
