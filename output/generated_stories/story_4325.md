# Spark, the Robot Dog Builds a Dream

Clink, clank, whirr! In a sunny dining room with a long table and soft chairs, a helpful robot dog named Spark wagged his shiny tail. His tail made a gentle tink-tink sound when it moved. The morning light made rainbow spots on the wall. The room smelled like warm toast and sweet oranges.

“Good morning, Spark,” said Mia, a small child with curly hair and bright eyes. Mia’s dad, who had dark glasses and kind hands, smiled and tapped the table. “Good morning, team,” he said. “We have a mission.”

Spark perked up his metal ears. “Mission?” beeped Spark in a happy, gentle voice.

Mia hopped into a chair. “Today is Build-A-World Day!” she cheered. “We need a big, special center on the table for our pretend city. But we don’t have a kit.”

Spark looked at the empty tabletop. He heard the soft hum of the fridge. He felt the cool wood under his paws. “No kit?” he asked.

“No kit,” Mia said, with a small frown. “Just us. And things from the dining room.”

Spark’s lights blinked. “We can use imagination,” he said. “Imagination is the best tool.”

Mia’s frown flipped into a smile. “Yes! We can build with what we have.”

Spark sniffed with his little sensor nose. He smelled crayons and paper. He heard a bird chirp outside. He saw napkins, spoons, a fruit bowl, boxes, and safe, clean jars.

“Let’s make a plan,” Spark said. “Step one: Look. Step two: Think. Step three: Build.”

Mia nodded. “I can do that. I can be the designer.”

Dad clapped slowly. “I’ll be the cheerleader,” he said. “You two can lead.”

Mia opened a drawer and found colorful napkins. “These can be roads,” she said. “Soft roads.”

Spark gently nudged napkins with his nose, lining them in long stripes. Swish, swish, swish. “Roads ready,” he beeped.

Mia found wooden spoons. “These can be bridges,” she said. She set them across the napkin roads. Tap, tap.

Spark spotted the fruit bowl. “The big orange can be a sun dome,” he said. He rolled the orange to the center. It went bump-bump-bump and stopped. The room filled with a bright citrus smell.

Mia giggled. “And the bananas can be curved slides!”

Spark tilted his head. “Slides need a base.” He looked at two empty boxes. “Boxes can be towers.”

Mia stacked the boxes with careful hands. “I’m a builder,” she said. “I can do it by myself.”

The boxes wobbled. Mia’s eyebrows scrunched. “Oh no.”

Spark stepped close. “Take a breath,” he said softly. “We can try again. Try slow. Try steady.”

Mia put one hand on the table and one on the box. She set it down gently. The wobble stopped. “I did it!” she said. Her smile felt warm, like sunshine.

Dad cheered. “Go, team!”

Next, Spark saw clean, plastic cups. “These can be houses,” he said. “All shapes, all sizes.” He set them near the napkin roads. Some were tall. Some were short. Some had stripes. Some were clear. “This is a diverse city,” Spark said. “Different is good.”

Mia drew little paper doors and windows. She glued them on. “Everyone is welcome here,” she said. “Friends of all kinds.”

Spark’s ears whirred. “We still need a special center.”

Mia looked around. She saw a round placemat. She felt the woven circles under her fingers. “This can be our learning circle,” she said. “A place to share ideas.”

They set the placemat in the middle. Spark added a tiny bell from the shelf. Ding! “This is the idea bell,” he said. “When it rings, we listen.”

Mia touched the bell. “Ding!” She spoke in a calm voice. “Idea: We make a library tower.”

Spark’s eyes lit up. “What can we use?”

Mia thought. She looked. She smiled. “Coasters! They stack like books.” She stacked flat coasters: one, two, three, four. The tower grew. Her hands were steady. Her face looked proud.

Dad nodded. “You are leading with your mind. That’s self-reliance,” he said. “You are trusting yourself.”

Suddenly, the banana slide rolled off the box. Thunk! It fell to the floor with a soft thud.

Mia’s shoulders drooped. “Our slide is broken.”

Spark sat and wagged. “We can solve it,” he said kindly. “What can we try?”

Mia tapped her chin. She looked at a cloth napkin ring. “This can hold the banana,” she said. She set the ring at the edge of the box and tucked the banana into it. The slide stayed. She grinned. “I fixed it!”

Spark beeped happily. “Problem solved.”

They added crayons as tiny cars. They drew flowers on paper and tucked them by the roads. They made a little park with a green plate. They placed a small teddy at the library tower.

Mia looked at the whole table city. The sun dome glowed orange. The napkin roads were smooth. The bridges were strong. The houses were many. The idea bell was ready. The room was full of color and sweet smells and soft sounds.

Mia held Spark’s warm metal paw. “We did it,” she whispered.

Spark blinked. “You imagined it. You built it. You solved problems. You did it yourself.”

Dad put a hand on Mia’s shoulder. “What did we learn?” he asked.

Mia thought. She smiled. “I learned that imagination can build big things. I learned I can try, try, try. I learned that friends can be different and still belong. I learned to listen to ideas.”

Spark wagged. “And we learned that tools can be anywhere—in our minds, in our hands, and on our table.”

Mia rang the bell. Ding! “Let’s play in our city,” she said.

They laughed and played in their dining room world, happy and proud, letting their ideas grow.

The End.
