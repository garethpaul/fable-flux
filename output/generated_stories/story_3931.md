# Koko's Safe Craft Day

Koko the koala loved spending time in Mama's home office. The big wooden desk had so many interesting things - colorful pens, shiny paper clips, and a warm computer that hummed softly. Today, Koko wanted to make something special.

"Mama, can I make a picture for you?" asked Koko, pointing to the craft supplies on the shelf.

"Of course, my little one," said Mama, looking up from her computer. "But remember, we must be safe when we craft. What do we do first?"

Koko thought carefully. "We wash our hands!"

"That's right!" Mama smiled. "Clean hands keep our crafts clean and keep germs away."

Koko waddled to the little sink in the corner of the office. The warm, soapy water felt nice on his fuzzy paws. He scrubbed and scrubbed, making tiny bubbles. "All clean!" he announced.

Back at the craft table, Koko saw scissors, glue, and colorful paper. His eyes grew wide with excitement. He reached for the big, sharp scissors.

"Wait, Koko!" said Mama gently. "Those scissors are for grown-ups only. They could hurt your little paws. Let's use these special safety scissors instead."

Mama handed Koko small, round-tipped scissors with bright yellow handles. "These are just your size, and they won't cut you if you're careful."

Koko felt the smooth plastic handles. They fit perfectly in his paws! "Thank you, Mama. Safety first!"

"Now, let's do some stretches before we start," said Mama. "Crafting uses our hands and arms, so we need to warm them up!"

Koko giggled as he and Mama reached their arms up high like tall trees. They rolled their shoulders like wheels. They wiggled their fingers like raindrops falling. Koko's whole body felt loose and ready.

"I feel strong and ready to craft!" said Koko, bouncing a little.

As Koko carefully cut colorful shapes, Mama showed him how to hold the scissors properly. "Point them away from your body, and cut slowly," she said. "Good crafting takes time."

Koko cut out a big yellow sun, fluffy white clouds, and green grass. The paper made soft crunching sounds as he worked. The glue smelled sweet and felt sticky on his paws.

"Mama, look!" Koko held up his picture. "It's our office with sunshine coming through the window!"

"It's beautiful, Koko!" Mama hugged him tight. "You followed all the safety rules. You washed your hands, used the right tools, stretched your body, and worked carefully."

Koko felt proud and happy. His picture showed the cozy office where he felt safe and loved. "Can we hang it on the wall?"

"Absolutely!" said Mama. "Right where I can see it while I work."

As they cleaned up together, putting supplies back in their special places, Koko felt grateful. "Mama, I like being safe when I craft. It makes everything more fun!"

"That's the best lesson of all," said Mama, giving Koko another hug. "When we're safe, we can enjoy our activities without worry."

Koko looked at his beautiful picture hanging on the wall. The bright colors made the whole office feel cheerful. He had learned that being safe wasn't about saying "no" to fun - it was about finding the right way to have fun.

"Tomorrow, can we craft again?" asked Koko.

"Of course," said Mama. "Safety, stretching, and all!"

Koko smiled, already dreaming of his next safe crafting adventure.

The End.
