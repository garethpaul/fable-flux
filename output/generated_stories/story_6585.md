# Mellow's Porch Plan

Every morning, a kind mouse named Mellow sat on the peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like warm wood and sunshine. The boards felt smooth under Mellow’s tiny feet. Birds sang, “Chee-chee-chee!” and the breeze said, “Whoosh.”

Mellow loved the porch. He loved seeing the sky turn pink. He loved the little flower pots lined up like a parade. Most of all, he loved helping. Mellow had a routine. He called it the Porch Plan.

“First sweep, then water, then check the seeds,” Mellow would say. He tapped his whiskers and smiled. “A plan helps me do each thing, one by one.”

One day, Mellow opened the tiny broom closet on the porch. “Oh!” he squeaked. The broom was missing! A leaf pile danced around the door. Cups tipped on their sides. A crinkly snack bag rolled, flip-flip, across the step.

“This is not peaceful,” said Mellow. He felt a little twist in his tummy. “I need my routine.”

Just then, his friends padded up the porch steps. Hopsy the rabbit wiggled her nose. “It smells like crunchy leaves,” she said. Tiko the turtle blinked slowly. “I heard the wind make a mess,” he said gently. “We can help.”

Mellow nodded. “Thank you. Let’s follow the Porch Plan. First we sweep. But the broom is gone.”

Hopsy twitched her ears. “I will hop and look high!” Tiko tapped his shell. “I will look low and slow.” Mellow whispered, “I will listen.”

They worked together. Hopsy peeked behind flower pots. Tiko checked under the bench. Mellow stood very still. He heard a scratchy, swishy sound. “Listen,” he said. “Swish-swish.” He tiptoed to the railing and peeked.

There was the broom, stuck between the porch steps and a bush, tickled by the wind. “Found you!” Mellow cheered. He looked at his friends. “Teamwork helps.”

Now the plan could start. “First we sweep,” said Mellow. He held the broom. “I will sweep the big leaves.” Hopsy clapped her paws. “I will sweep the little ones.” Tiko smiled. “I will hold the dustpan steady.”

Swish, swish, sweep. The broom made soft sounds like brushing fur. The leaves smelled like fall, a little spicy and sweet. The friends moved together. Mellow pushed, Hopsy gathered, Tiko scooped. Their bodies made a rhythm, like a gentle song. They stayed in step so they did not bump. That is coordination.

“Next we sort,” said Mellow. He pointed to three small bins. “Trash, recycle, and compost.” The bins made little bumps when he tapped them.

Hopsy held up the crinkly snack bag. “Trash?” she asked. Mellow nodded. “Yes, that goes in trash.” Tiko held a paper cup. “Recycle.” Mellow smiled. “Yes!” He lifted a handful of dry leaves and a banana peel. “Compost,” he said. “Compost feeds the garden.”

They felt proud. Their porch was cleaner, and the earth would be happier. “This is environmental care,” Mellow said softly. “We care for our home and our world.”

“Third we water,” Mellow said. He filled a tiny watering can. Splash, trickle. The water smelled fresh. Hopsy counted, “One, two, three,” as she watered three flower pots. Tiko watered the seed tray slowly, so the seeds did not float away.

Drip, drip, drip. The soil turned dark and cool. The flowers lifted their faces. A yellow petal brushed Mellow’s nose. He giggled. “It tickles!”

“Last we check the seeds,” said Mellow. He knelt by the tray. “Hello, little seeds,” he whispered. “We will see you soon.” He drew a small picture chart with the days of the week. Monday had a sun. Tuesday had a cloud. “Each day we do our plan,” he said. “Then we grow strong habits, just like seeds grow into plants.”

A gust of wind puffed again. A napkin fluttered onto the step. “Oh no,” said Hopsy. “More mess?”

Mellow took a breath. He patted his chest. “It’s okay. We have a routine.” He smiled and pointed. “First we sweep.” The friends laughed and did the steps again, but faster this time. Their bodies knew the dance.

When they finished, the porch looked bright and tidy. The bins were in a neat row. The broom was hung up so the wind could not grab it. The watering can sat on its saucer. Mellow’s tummy felt calm and warm.

Tiko blinked happily. “Your Porch Plan works,” he said. Hopsy wiggled. “It feels good to help together!”

Mellow nodded. “Routine helps us know what to do. Coordination helps us move together. Caring for our porch helps the earth. We can do it every day.”

He made a little sign and taped it by the door:
Porch Plan
1. Sweep
2. Sort: trash, recycle, compost
3. Water plants
4. Check seeds

The sign rustled softly in the breeze. The porch felt peaceful again. The birds sang, “Chee-chee-chee!” The breeze whispered, “Whoosh.” Mellow and his friends sat on the step, sipping cool water and smiling at the clean, sun-warmed boards.

Mellow said, “When we have a plan, problems feel smaller. When we work together, we can fix them.”

Hopsy and Tiko nodded. “Let’s do it again tomorrow,” they said.

“Yes,” said Mellow. “Every day.”

The End.
