# Pip's Beautiful Welcome

Pip was a bright green parrot with rainbow feathers on his wings. He lived with the Martinez family, who loved him very much. Today was special - Grandma Rosa was coming to visit from Mexico! She would stay in the pretty guest room with yellow walls and soft blue curtains.

"Pip, can you help make the guest room beautiful for Grandma Rosa?" asked Mama Martinez. "She's bringing special decorations from her home in Mexico."

Pip fluffed his colorful feathers with excitement. "¡Sí! Yes! I want to help!" he squawked happily.

Pip flew into the guest room and looked around. The bed had crisp white sheets, and sunlight streamed through the window. But something was missing. The room felt empty and plain.

"I know!" chirped Pip. "I'll make beautiful art for Grandma Rosa!"

Pip found some colorful paper and crayons. He tried to hold a crayon in his beak, but it was too hard. The crayon slipped and fell to the floor with a small *thunk*.

"Oh no," Pip said sadly. His bright eyes looked worried. "I can't draw like people do."

Pip tried again. He picked up a red crayon with his foot, but his drawing looked like squiggly lines. It didn't look beautiful at all. Pip's head drooped down.

"This is too hard," he sighed. "Maybe I'm not good at making art."

Just then, Papa Martinez walked into the room. He saw Pip looking sad next to the messy paper.

"What's wrong, little friend?" Papa asked gently.

"I wanted to make beautiful art for Grandma Rosa," Pip explained. "But I can't hold crayons like people. My drawing looks silly."

Papa smiled warmly. "Pip, you ARE an artist! Remember how you sing beautiful songs? And look at your amazing feathers - they're like a rainbow!"

Pip tilted his head, thinking. "But that's just... me."

"Exactly!" said Papa. "The best art comes from being yourself. What makes you special, Pip?"

Pip thought hard. Slowly, his eyes began to sparkle again. "I can sing! I can speak Spanish and English! I have pretty feathers!"

"¡Perfecto!" said Papa. "Perfect!"

Pip felt excited again. He had an idea! He flew to his favorite perch and began to sing a beautiful song in Spanish that Grandma Rosa had taught him over the phone: "Las mañanitas, las mañanitas..." His voice was sweet and clear.

Then Pip carefully arranged his molted feathers on the white paper, making a colorful pattern. Red, blue, green, and yellow feathers created a beautiful design.

When Grandma Rosa arrived, she gasped with joy. "¡Qué hermoso! How beautiful!" she exclaimed, clapping her hands.

Pip sang his Spanish song for her, and Grandma Rosa's eyes filled with happy tears. "Pip, this is the most wonderful welcome! Your song reminds me of home, and your feather art is more beautiful than any painting."

Pip puffed up his chest proudly. "I learned that I don't have to be like everyone else to make something special!"

"That's right, mi nieto," said Grandma Rosa, which means "my grandchild" in Spanish. "When we try our best and use our special gifts, we can do amazing things."

From that day on, Pip knew that being different made him special, not less. When things seemed hard, he remembered to try again in his own special way.

The End.
