# Robo-Pup’s Treehouse Workout

The morning sun tickled the leaves. The treehouse smelled like fresh wood and warm pancakes. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” from nearby branches. Inside the cozy treehouse, a helpful robot dog named Robo-Pup wagged his shiny tail. His tail made a soft whir-whir sound.

“Good morning!” said Robo-Pup in a kind, beepy voice. His eyes blinked gentle blue lights. “Today is exercise day. Let’s move our bodies!”

Robo-Pup loved to help friends stay strong and healthy. He had a soft metal nose, smooth silver paws, and a big, bright heart. He also had a little speaker on his collar that could play happy music.

Robo-Pup looked around the treehouse. Mats were on the floor. A jump rope hung on a hook. A small poster said: “Move, Stretch, Smile!”

Just then, two friends climbed up the ladder. Mia came first. She wore red sneakers. Her cheeks were rosy. Behind her came Ben. He wore a blue cap. His cap had a tiny star. Their shoes made quiet tap-tap sounds on the wooden floor.

“Hi, Robo-Pup!” Mia said, hugging him. “We want to exercise!”

“Great!” Robo-Pup beeped. “We will warm up. Then we will hop, jump, and stretch. Exercise keeps hearts happy and bodies strong.”

Mia smiled. “I like to jump like a frog.”

Ben looked down. “I’m good at jumping too,” he said, but his voice was small.

Robo-Pup played a gentle beat. Thump-thump, thump-thump. “Let’s start with big arm circles,” he said. “One, two, three!” The treehouse filled with giggles and swishy air sounds as their arms went round and round.

After warm-ups, Robo-Pup set out three cones. “We will have a small obstacle course,” he said. “Hop to the first cone, bear-walk to the second, and tiptoe to the third. Ready?”

Mia nodded fast. Ben gulped. “Uh-huh,” he said, but he held his stomach.

“Go!” beeped Robo-Pup.

Mia hopped, “Boing, boing!” Ben started to hop too, but he tripped and fell on his knees. His cap slid over one eye. The floor felt cool on his hands. His eyes felt wet.

“Are you okay?” Mia asked.

“I’m fine,” Ben said in a shaky voice. He stood up. He looked at the mat. He felt a twisty feeling inside.

Robo-Pup blinked softly. “It’s okay to go slow,” he said. “Exercise is about trying. Let’s breathe in… and out.”

They tried again. Mia finished the course and cheered. Ben tried to bear-walk, but his arms felt tired. He wanted to stop. He looked at the jump rope and had a not-nice idea.

“If I say I finished, Robo-Pup won’t make me do it again,” Ben thought. He tiptoed around the cones and whispered, “I did it.”

Robo-Pup tilted his head. His ear panels hummed. “Ben,” he said gently, “my sensors saw you skip the bear-walk. Are you being honest?”

Ben’s face felt hot. He stared at his shoes. The treehouse was quiet, except for a faint breeze, hush-hush through the leaves.

Mia touched Ben’s arm. “It’s okay to tell the truth,” she said. “We can help.”

Ben sniffed. “I was tired. I didn’t want to say it,” he whispered. “I said I finished, but I didn’t. I’m sorry.”

Robo-Pup wagged. “Thank you for being honest,” he said warmly. “Honesty keeps trust strong, like a sturdy ladder. Now, let’s be fair, too. Justice means fairness. We will make a plan that is fair for everyone.”

“How?” asked Mia.

Robo-Pup’s eyes glowed. “We will try the course again, but we will change it so it fits each of us. Fair does not always mean the same. It means everyone gets what they need to do their best.”

He moved the cones closer. “Mia, you can try frog jumps and a long bear-walk. Ben, you can do gentle bear-steps and slow tiptoes. We will cheer for each other.”

Ben took a deep breath. He felt the cool air in his nose and the warm air out. “I can try,” he said.

They started. “Boing!” went Mia’s frog jumps. Ben placed his hands down carefully. “Step-step,” he said softly, moving like a small bear. His arms felt a little wobbly, but he kept going. Robo-Pup played happy music. The thump-thump beat made the floor vibrate lightly.

Mia clapped. “You’re doing it, Ben!”

Ben smiled. “I am doing it!”

They reached the last cone. “Tiptoe time,” said Robo-Pup. “Quiet as a mouse.”

They tiptoed—tap-tap, whisper-step—until they touched the cone. “We did it!” Mia cheered.

Robo-Pup spun in a circle, tail whirring. “Excellent! Exercise helps our bodies grow strong. Being honest helps our hearts feel light. Being fair helps everyone shine.”

Ben lifted his cap and wiped his forehead. He felt proud. His tummy felt calm. “Thank you for helping me,” he said. “I told the truth, and we made a fair plan.”

Mia high-fived him. “Let’s stretch,” she said.

They reached for the sky. They touched their toes. The treehouse smelled like pine and sunshine. Their bodies felt warm and happy.

Robo-Pup said, “Let’s say our lesson: Move your body, tell the truth, and be fair.”

They all said it together. “Move your body, tell the truth, and be fair!”

The birds chirped along. Cheep-cheep!

Robo-Pup turned down the music. “Today, we learned that exercise is for everyone. We can go fast or slow. We try our best. We are honest about what we need. And we make fair plans so all friends can join.”

Mia nodded. “Exercise makes me giggle.”

Ben nodded too. “Exercise makes me brave.”

Robo-Pup wagged. “And honesty and justice make us good friends.”

They looked out the treehouse window at the bright, blue sky. The leaves rustled like soft applause. Their hearts felt strong, kind, and steady.

The End.
