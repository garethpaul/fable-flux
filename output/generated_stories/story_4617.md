# Daisy Fawn's Garden Helper Day

On a sunny balcony garden filled with colorful flowers, little Daisy Fawn stretched her spotted legs. The morning air smelled sweet like roses and fresh mint. Her mama had asked her to help water all the plants today.

"I want to help!" Daisy had said excitedly. But now, looking at all the flower pots and hanging baskets, she felt very small.

"There are so many plants," Daisy whispered, her brown eyes wide. "What if I can't do it all?"

The watering can seemed enormous next to her tiny hooves. Petunias drooped in their pots. Tomato plants looked thirsty. Herbs waited patiently in their containers.

Daisy tried to lift the big watering can, but it was too heavy. Water splashed everywhere except on the plants.

"This is too hard!" she sighed, sitting down among the marigolds.

Just then, a wise old butterfly landed on her nose. "Hello, little fawn. You look worried."

"I promised to water all these plants, but there are too many! I'm too little," Daisy said sadly.

The butterfly's wings sparkled in the sunlight. "Have you tried doing just one plant at a time?"

"One at a time?" Daisy tilted her head.

"Yes! See that little basil plant? Start there. Then move to the next one. One step, one plant, one sip of water."

Daisy looked at the small basil plant. It didn't seem scary at all! She carefully filled a tiny cup with water and gently poured it into the soil.

"I did it!" she exclaimed as the basil leaves perked up. The plant seemed to whisper "thank you" in the breeze.

Feeling braver, Daisy moved to a pot of cheerful sunflowers. Cup by cup, she gave them cool water. Their yellow faces seemed to smile at her.

"This feels good," Daisy said, her confidence growing. She could smell the fresh, clean scent of watered earth.

Next came the strawberry plants with their tiny white flowers. Then the lavender that smelled like Mama's favorite soap. Each plant got its own special drink of water.

As Daisy worked, she noticed how peaceful she felt. Her breathing was calm and steady. She listened to birds chirping and bees buzzing happily around the flowers.

"One more plant," she said to herself, approaching the last pot of cherry tomatoes. "You can do this, Daisy!"

When Mama Deer stepped onto the balcony, she gasped with delight. Every single plant looked happy and refreshed. Water droplets sparkled like tiny diamonds on the leaves.

"Daisy! You watered every plant! How did you do it all by yourself?"

Daisy's chest puffed up with pride. "I did one plant at a time, Mama. The butterfly taught me that big jobs are just lots of little jobs put together!"

Mama nuzzled Daisy's soft fur. "You learned something very important today. When we break big tasks into small pieces, we can accomplish amazing things."

Daisy looked around at all the healthy, happy plants. She had done something really important! The garden looked beautiful, and she felt proud and capable.

"Tomorrow, can I help plant new seeds?" Daisy asked eagerly.

"Of course, my little garden helper," Mama smiled. "One seed at a time!"

As the sun climbed higher, Daisy settled down for a rest among the flowers, feeling proud of her hard work and excited for tomorrow's new adventure.

The End.
