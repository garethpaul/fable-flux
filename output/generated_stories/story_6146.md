# Ollie Otter Learns to Share the Fun

Ollie Otter loved playing in his cozy living room. The soft carpet felt wonderful under his furry paws, and colorful toys were scattered everywhere like a rainbow had fallen from the sky.

"What should I play with first?" Ollie wondered aloud, his whiskers twitching with excitement. His eyes sparkled as he spotted his favorite red ball bouncing gently in a sunny corner.

Just then, his little sister Penny waddled into the room. Her tiny paws made soft patting sounds on the carpet. "Ollie! Can I play too?" she asked, her voice sweet like honey.

"I want to play with my ball first!" Ollie said quickly, scooping up the bright red ball and hugging it tight. It felt smooth and warm in his arms.

Penny's bottom lip began to wobble. "But I want to play with the ball too," she whispered.

Ollie clutched the ball tighter. "No! I had it first!" He could feel his heart beating fast, and his fur felt all prickly with worry that he might have to give up his favorite toy.

Mama Otter heard the commotion and glided into the room, her gentle smile warming the space like sunshine. "What's happening here, my little otters?"

"Penny wants my ball, but I was playing with it first!" Ollie explained, his voice getting louder.

Mama Otter sat down on the soft carpet and patted the space beside her. "Come here, both of you. Let's take a deep breath together."

Ollie and Penny snuggled close to Mama. She smelled like lavender and love. "Breathe in slowly," Mama said, taking a big, peaceful breath. "Now breathe out. Feel how calm that makes you?"

Ollie felt his shoulders relax as he breathed. The prickly feeling in his fur started to go away.

"Now," Mama said gently, "how do you think Penny feels when she can't play with the ball?"

Ollie looked at his sister's sad face and felt a warm, gentle feeling in his tummy. "She feels... sad?" he said quietly.

"That's right. And how would you feel if someone wouldn't share with you?"

Ollie thought for a moment, remembering when his friend wouldn't let him play with the toy boat last week. "I felt really sad and left out," he admitted.

"Taking turns means everyone gets to have fun," Mama explained. "When we share, we show respect for others' feelings."

Ollie looked at the red ball in his paws, then at Penny's hopeful face. Slowly, a smile spread across his whiskers. "Penny, would you like to play with the ball first? I can count to ten, then it's my turn!"

Penny's face lit up like a star. "Really? Thank you, Ollie!"

Ollie handed over the ball, and Penny giggled with joy as she bounced it gently. The sound of her laughter made Ollie's heart feel warm and fuzzy.

"One, two, three..." Ollie counted slowly, watching Penny play. When he reached ten, Penny happily rolled the ball back to him.

"My turn!" Ollie said cheerfully, catching the ball. After counting to ten again, he rolled it back to Penny.

They played this way for the whole afternoon, taking turns and laughing together. The living room filled with the happy sounds of bouncing, giggling, and counting.

"Taking turns is actually more fun!" Ollie realized. "We both get to play, and we're playing together!"

Mama Otter beamed with pride. "When we take turns and show respect for others, everyone feels happy and included."

As the sun set through the living room window, Ollie and Penny curled up together, tired but content. Ollie had learned that sharing made playtime twice as wonderful.

The End.
