# Kora the Calm Koala Helps the Backyard

Kora the calm koala woke up to warm sunshine on her soft gray fur. She stretched slowly and blinked. The sunny backyard smelled like sweet grass and lemony flowers. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” A light breeze brushed Kora’s round ears.

Kora loved the backyard. She loved the tall sunflowers, the tiny ants marching, and the shiny blue birdbath. Today felt like a good day to help.

Kora climbed down from her favorite low branch. Her paws touched the cool dirt. “Good morning, backyard,” she whispered. “How can I help you today?”

Just then, a little bunny named Bibi hopped fast, thump, thump, thump. Bibi’s ears wiggled. “Kora! Oh, Kora! There is a big mess.”

Kora took a slow breath in and a slow breath out. “Let’s look,” she said softly.

They walked to the center of the yard. Oh no! The birdbath was tipped over. Water splashed the grass. A small pile of sticks and leaves blocked the tiny garden path. A paper bag fluttered and flapped, “fuff-fuff,” by the fence.

Sparrow Sam zipped down in a swirl of feathers. “We can’t drink!” he chirped. “The birdbath is sideways!”

Tiny Ant marched up, antennae twitching. “Our path is blocked! We can’t go to our picnic crumb.”

Bibi’s nose twitched. “And that bag is scary. It keeps going fuff-fuff!”

Kora felt her heart go thump, but she stayed calm. She placed her paw on the ground. It felt cool and steady. “We will solve it together,” she said. “We will use our thinking. We will be kind. We will help our backyard.”

Bibi took a deep breath too. “Okay. What do we do first?”

Kora looked and listened. She used her eyes, ears, and nose. She noticed the water shining on the grass. She heard the bag flapping. She smelled the lemony flowers. “Let’s make a plan,” Kora said. “Step one: Make the birdbath safe. Step two: Clear the path. Step three: Stop the flappy bag. We can do it as a team.”

Sparrow Sam chirped, “I can help with the birdbath! I am small but fast. I can bring little pebbles.”

Tiny Ant lifted a leaf tip. “We can carry bits of mud to make it steady.”

Bibi puffed out her chest. “I can push gently with my strong bunny legs.”

Kora smiled. “I will guide and lift a little. We will count together. And we will be careful and peaceful.”

They all got to work. Bibi pressed her paws. Kora placed her soft pads under the rim. Sparrow Sam dropped tiny pebbles with quick “plip-plip.” Tiny Ants pat-patted mud, “tap-tap.”

“One, two, three,” Kora said calmly. “Lift a little.”

The birdbath moved. The base found a flat place. It wobbled once and then sat, nice and straight. The water rippled and glittered in the sun like tiny stars.

Sparrow Sam dipped his beak. “Mmm! Thank you!” he sang.

“Step one done,” said Kora. She felt proud, but not rushed. “Now, the path.”

The pile of sticks and leaves looked big. Bibi’s eyes grew wide. “It looks like a mountain!”

Kora nodded. “It is big, but we are many. We can sort. Sticks to the left. Leaves to the right. We can make a cozy bed for bugs.”

Tiny Ant smiled. “We like cozy!”

They sorted together. Kora picked up thin sticks that smelled like rain. Bibi gathered soft leaves that felt like little pillows. Sparrow Sam tugged at light pieces. Tiny Ants carried the tiniest bits, one by one.

Soon, the path was clear. The line of ants could march again. “Thank you,” said Tiny Ant. “We accept help, and we give help too. That is good.”

Kora nodded. “Helping each other makes our backyard happy.”

A “fuff-fuff” sound reminded them of the last problem. The paper bag bumped the fence. A shy snail peeked out from a shady rock. “Hello,” said the snail slowly. “I am Slo-Slo. The bag makes me hide. But I want to slide to my clover.”

Kora knelt to the snail. “We see you, Slo-Slo. We accept that you move slowly. That is okay. We will make it calm again.”

“How do we catch the bag?” Bibi asked. “It keeps running away!”

Kora’s eyes softened. She watched the wind push the bag. It flapped toward the fence, then back toward the lilac bush. Kora thought, slow and steady. “We will use the wind. We will not chase fast. We will guide it gently.”

She pointed with a paw. “Bibi, can you stand by the lilac? Sparrow Sam, can you tug the string on the bag when it floats near? Tiny Ants, can you hold a leaf like a little wall? Slo-Slo, you can cheer us on.”

“Cheer,” said Slo-Slo, smiling.

They waited. The breeze puffed. The bag sailed toward the lilac. “Now,” Kora whispered.

Bibi held out her soft belly like a cushion. Boop! The bag bumped and slowed. Sparrow Sam grabbed the string with his beak, “nip,” and let it drop closer. The Tiny Ants lifted a leaf, “up, up,” and the bag settled on top.

Kora patted the bag flat. “We will reuse this,” she said. “It can carry sticks to the compost.”

They filled the bag with dry sticks and carried it together. The backyard felt quiet and bright. The sun warmed everyone. The birdbath shimmered. The path was open. Slo-Slo slid to clover with a happy “mmm.”

Bibi smiled. “We did it! We fixed all the problems.”

Kora’s calm eyes twinkled. “We did it together. We used our minds. We were kind. We accepted each friend’s way. We chose peaceful solutions. That is community service. That means we help our whole place.”

Sparrow Sam sang a sweet song. Tiny Ants danced a tiny dance. Slo-Slo made a slow happy wiggle.

Kora looked around the sunny backyard. It felt safe, clean, and bright. “When we see a mess, we can help,” she said. “When we see a friend, we can listen. When we use calm thinking, we find good answers.”

Bibi nodded. “I can help at home too. I can pick up toys. I can share. I can be calm like you.”

Kora hugged Bibi gently. “Yes,” she said. “Helping hands and paws make our world better.”

The sun glowed warm on their fur and feathers and shells. The backyard hummed, “thank you.”

The End.
