# The Patient Panda’s Bubble Plan

Poppy was a patient panda with soft black-and-white fur. She loved to hum and wiggle her round ears. One sunny morning, Poppy tiptoed into the bathroom. The tiles felt cool on her paws. Drip, drip, drip went the silver faucet. The mirror was shiny like a small lake.

Poppy smiled. “Good morning, sink! Good morning, soap!” she said.

She had a plan. Today she would teach her friend, Tiny Turtle, how to be clean and healthy. Poppy liked to learn new things, and she liked to share. Sharing knowledge made her heart feel warm, like a cozy blanket.

Tiny Turtle peeked in from the doorway. His shell was green and speckled. He looked curious, but a little shy. “Hi, Poppy,” Tiny Turtle whispered. “Do I have to wash? I am slow. Washing takes so long.”

Poppy nodded kindly. “I am slow too, Tiny,” she said. “Slow is okay. We can be slow together. We can be patient. Let’s make it fun!”

Tiny Turtle blinked. “Fun? In the bathroom?”

Poppy grinned. “Fun with bubbles!”

First, Poppy turned on the tap. The water made a soft whoosh and felt warm and tickly on her paws. “Step one,” she said gently. “Wet your hands.” Tiny Turtle stretched his little legs and let the water run over his claws. “It feels like rain,” he giggled.

“Step two: soap time!” Poppy pumped the soap. Squish! A puff of soap smelled like lemons. “Mmm, it smells sunny,” said Poppy. Tiny Turtle sniffed. “Sunny soap!” he laughed.

Poppy held up her paws. “We scrub front and back. We scrub between our fingers. We scrub our thumbs. We scrub our wrists. We sing while we scrub. That helps us be patient.”

“What song?” asked Tiny Turtle.

Poppy started to sing in a happy, bouncy voice:
“Bubbles up and bubbles down,
Scrub, scrub, scrub around!
Front and back and in between,
Happy hands are shiny clean!”

Tiny Turtle sang too. Their voices echoed softly in the bathroom. Echo, echo. The bubbles grew big and foamy like little clouds. Poppy counted slowly, “One, two, three... all the way to twenty.” Tiny Turtle counted with her. Counting felt like a game.

Poppy winked. “That is knowledge, Tiny. We count to help our brains and our hands. Knowledge helps us stay healthy.”

They rinsed their hands. Whoosh! The bubbles slid away. Tiny Turtle watched the water sparkle and twirl down the drain. “Goodbye, bubbles!” he said.

Poppy handed him a soft, dry towel. “Pat, pat, pat,” she said. Tiny Turtle patted his hands dry. The towel felt warm and fuzzy. He smiled. “My hands feel happy.”

Just then, Tiny Turtle looked worried. “But what if I forget? What if I do not know when to wash?”

Poppy tapped her chin. “Hmm. That is our problem. Sometimes we forget. Let’s make a plan!”

They thought together. Thinking made a quiet, comfy feeling in the bathroom. Poppy pointed to the mirror. “We can make a picture plan,” she said.

She found a small board with magnets. She drew simple pictures: a sun for morning, a plate for before eating, a potty for after using the toilet, and a soccer ball for after play. Next to each picture, she drew tiny bubbles and a smiling sink.

“This is our Bubble Plan,” Poppy said. “We wash:
- in the morning,
- before we eat,
- after we use the potty,
- after we play.”

Tiny Turtle clapped his little feet. “I can follow pictures! Pictures help my brain remember.”

Poppy nodded. “That is using knowledge. And we can help each other. That is friendship.”

Tiny Turtle looked at his shell. “Sometimes my shell gets dusty,” he said. “What do I do?”

Poppy showed him a gentle sponge. “Step by step,” she said. “Warm water, a tiny dot of soap, soft circles.” She dipped the sponge. Swish, swish. Tiny Turtle felt the soft, warm circles on his shell. “It feels like a hug,” he said, eyes happy.

They rinsed with a small cup. Splash! The water made tiny rainbows on the shell. Poppy hummed their bubble song, and Tiny Turtle swayed from side to side. “I like this,” he said. “I feel fresh. I feel brave.”

Suddenly, Tiny Turtle sneezed—achoo!—a tiny, squeaky sneeze. “Oh!” he said. “What now?”

Poppy smiled. “We use a tissue,” she said. “We catch the sneeze. Then we throw the tissue away. Then”—she pointed to the sink—“we wash again!”

Tiny Turtle nodded. “Because we care for our friends,” he said. “We do not share germs. We share songs and smiles.”

Poppy beamed. “Yes! That is the lesson.”

They washed one more time, counting together. The bathroom smelled fresh, like lemons and rain. The mirror shone. The towels were fluffy. Poppy felt proud. Tiny Turtle felt proud too.

“Thank you, Poppy,” Tiny Turtle said. “You were patient with me.”

Poppy patted his shell. “Friends help friends learn. When we know better, we do better.”

Tiny Turtle looked at the Bubble Plan on the mirror. “I can do this,” he said. “I can be clean. I can be kind. I can remember.”

Poppy nodded. “We did it together.”

They left the bathroom, paws and claws clean, shells shiny, and hearts full. The problem was solved with patience, knowledge, and friendship—and lots of bubbly fun.

The End.
