# Moonlight on the Rooftop

On a soft, warm night, a clever cat named Clover climbed up to the rooftop. The shingles felt smooth and a little warm under her paws. The air smelled like fresh rain and sweet flowers. The moon hung like a bright white cookie in the sky. Stars twinkled like tiny bells.

Clover loved the rooftop. She could see the whole neighborhood—little lights in windows, a dog snoring below, and a tiny breeze that made her whiskers wiggle. She purred and whispered, “Hello, moon.”

Clover had a shiny red ribbon tied to her collar. She loved to swish it and watch it dance. Tonight, she wanted to make a surprise gift for her friend, Piper the pigeon. Piper liked to land on the rooftop and coo stories about the clouds.

Clover had an idea. “I will make a little flag for Piper!” she said. She looked around. She saw chalk, a small stick, and a blue bottle cap near the chimney. She could be creative!

Clover tied her ribbon to the stick. Swish! The red ribbon flapped like a tiny flag in the cool wind. She used chalk to draw a big cloud smile on a gray shingle. She placed the blue cap like a shiny button in the middle. “This is for Piper,” Clover purred. “A cloud flag!”

Soon, Piper landed with a flutter. “Coo, coo! Hello, Clover,” said Piper. “Oh! Is that a special flag?”

Clover felt proud. She puffed up her fur and said, “Yes! I made it for you.” She felt a little tickle in her tummy. The flag looked pretty, but Clover had tied the ribbon too tight. The knot pulled on her collar. It made a snap! The ribbon slipped, and the flag leaned and wobbled.

Piper blinked. “It’s lovely, Clover. But it looks a bit wobbly.”

Clover did not want Piper to think she made a mistake. She wanted to look clever. She said, “Um… the wind did it. The wind made it wobbly.” Her voice was small. Her ears bent back.

Just then, a soft black kitten named Bean climbed onto the roof. Bean’s paws made tiny pat-pat sounds. “Hi! I like flags!” Bean squeaked. Bean bounced near the flag and accidentally bumped it. The stick fell with a clack. The blue bottle cap rolled and rolled and rolled—plonk—down near the edge of the roof.

“Oh no,” said Clover. Her tummy felt oops-y. Piper cooed, “It’s okay, Bean. We can fix it.”

Clover stared at the fallen flag. Her chest felt tight. She knew she had not tied the knot right. She remembered her words: “The wind did it.” But she knew that was not true. Her whiskers drooped. She felt a little heavy inside.

Piper tilted her head. “Clover, are you alright?”

Clover took a slow breath. She listened to the night—the soft hum of crickets, the whisper of the breeze, the little pat-pat of Bean’s paws. The moon looked kind and bright.

Clover said, “I need to tell the truth.” Her voice trembled, but she kept going. “I tied the ribbon wrong. I said the wind did it, but it was me. I didn’t want to look silly. I am sorry.”

Piper blinked, then smiled with warm eyes. “Thank you for telling the truth,” said Piper. “That was brave.”

Bean nodded so hard his ears wiggled. “Honest! Honest! I like honest!”

Clover felt lighter, like a balloon. “Will you forgive me?” she asked.

“Of course,” said Piper gently. “I forgive you. We all make mistakes. Honesty helps us fix them.”

Bean bounced. “Let’s fix the flag! Let’s be creative!” He found a second stick—a short twig near the chimney. Piper picked up the blue cap with her beak and dropped it by Clover. Clover licked the ribbon and made a new, bigger loop. She showed Bean how to hold the stick steady with his paws. Piper flapped her wings to make a little breeze to test it.

Clover tied the ribbon in a bow with two big ears and a small tail. She giggled. “Bunny-bow!” She used chalk to draw little dots like stars around the cloud. Bean added a tiny heart. Piper set the blue cap in the center and tapped it gently—click!

The flag stood tall. It fluttered happily. The ribbon whispered, swish-swish, in the night air.

Piper cooed, “Now it is strong and beautiful.”

Clover’s eyes shone. She felt proud, but this time it felt warm and true. “Thank you for helping me,” she said. “And thank you for forgiving me. Telling the truth felt a little scary, but now I feel good inside.”

Bean leaned against Clover. “Honesty hug!” he giggled. They all laughed.

They sat together on the rooftop and listened to the crickets sing. The moonlight made the chalk cloud glow. The ribbon waved hello to the stars. The night smelled like rain and cookies from a nearby window. Clover’s purr rumbled like a tiny drum.

Piper said, “Clover, you are a clever cat. You used creativity to make the flag. You used honesty to make your heart feel light. And we used forgiveness to stay good friends.”

Clover nodded. “When we tell the truth, we can fix things. When we forgive, we can smile together.”

Bean yawned. “And when we are creative, we can make shiny cloud flags!”

They all smiled at the pretty flag. The rooftop felt safe and soft under their paws and claws. The night felt friendly and bright.

Clover whispered, “Hello, moon. Thank you for this honest night.”

The End.
