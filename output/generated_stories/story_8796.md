# Milo Mouse and the Big Garden Mess

Milo Mouse lived in a cozy corner of Farmer Joe's garden shed. He loved the smell of fresh dirt and the sound of rain pattering on the wooden roof. Every morning, Milo would peek out from his tiny door to watch the beautiful garden wake up.

One sunny morning, Milo scurried outside to collect seeds for breakfast. The warm sunshine felt wonderful on his soft gray fur. He could hear birds chirping and bees buzzing happily around the colorful flowers.

But when Milo reached his favorite sunflower patch, he gasped. "Oh no!" he squeaked. All the sunflower seeds he had been saving were scattered everywhere! The wind had blown them all around the garden.

Milo's whiskers twitched with anger. His little paws clenched into fists. "This isn't fair!" he shouted, stomping his tiny feet. "I worked so hard to collect those seeds!"

He felt his heart beating fast and his face getting hot. Milo was so angry that he wanted to kick the nearest flower pot. But just as he lifted his paw, he heard a gentle voice.

"Milo? Are you okay?" It was Bella Butterfly, floating down from a nearby rose bush.

"No, I'm NOT okay!" Milo said loudly. "My seeds are ruined! Everything is terrible!"

Bella landed softly on a leaf near Milo. "I can see you're very upset," she said kindly. "When I feel angry, I like to take three deep breaths. Would you like to try that with me?"

Milo didn't want to, but Bella's voice was so gentle. "Okay," he whispered.

"Breathe in slowly through your nose," Bella said, demonstrating with her tiny butterfly body. "One... two... three... Now breathe out slowly through your mouth."

Milo breathed in, smelling the sweet garden flowers. Then he breathed out, feeling a little calmer. They did this three times together.

"How do you feel now?" Bella asked.

"A little better," Milo admitted. His paws weren't clenched anymore.

"Good! Now, can you tell me what really happened to your seeds?"

Milo looked down at his paws. "Well... I guess I didn't put them away properly last night. I left them outside, and the wind scattered them." He took another deep breath. "It wasn't anyone else's fault. It was mine."

"That was very honest of you to say that," Bella smiled. "And you know what? I have an idea! What if we turn this into a fun outdoor adventure? We could search for your seeds together and plant new ones!"

Milo's ears perked up. "Really? You'd help me?"

"Of course! And while we search, we might find other wonderful things in the garden too!"

So Milo and Bella spent the morning exploring every corner of the garden. They found some of Milo's seeds hiding under leaves, discovered a family of friendly ladybugs, and even found some new flower seeds that had blown in from the neighbor's garden.

As they worked together, Milo realized something important. "Bella," he said, "I'm glad I didn't kick that flower pot when I was angry. Taking deep breaths really helped me think better."

"That's right, Milo! When we feel angry, we can always stop, breathe, and think before we act," Bella said. "And being honest about our mistakes helps us learn and grow."

By afternoon, they had planted a whole new row of beautiful flowers. Milo felt proud and happy. He had learned that anger was okay to feel, but there were good ways to handle it.

"Thank you for helping me today," Milo said to Bella. "Tomorrow, I'll remember to put my seeds away safely!"

The End.
