# Ollie Otter's Bathroom Adventure

Ollie Otter loved water more than anything in the whole world! His soft, brown fur sparkled when it got wet, and his little black nose twitched with excitement whenever he heard the sound of running water.

One sunny morning, Ollie woke up and stretched his paws high above his head. "What a beautiful day for a bath!" he squeaked happily. He scampered on his short legs to the bathroom, where the white bathtub gleamed in the morning light.

But when Ollie reached the bathroom door, he heard splashing sounds coming from inside. "Splash! Splash! Giggle!"

Ollie peeked around the corner and saw his little sister Luna already in the bathtub. Her gray fur was covered in soapy bubbles, and she was playing with her favorite yellow rubber duck.

"Luna!" Ollie called out, his voice getting louder. "I want to take my bath NOW! Get out!"

Luna looked up with sad, round eyes. Soap bubbles dripped from her whiskers. "But Ollie," she said softly, "I just started washing my fur. I need more time to get clean."

Ollie stamped his little paw on the cool tile floor. "But I want the bathtub! I was here first yesterday!"

Just then, Mama Otter appeared in the doorway. Her warm brown eyes looked at both of her children. "Ollie, what's happening here?" she asked gently.

"Luna won't share the bathtub!" Ollie huffed, crossing his arms over his fluffy chest.

Mama Otter sat down on the bathroom stool and patted her lap. "Come here, Ollie. Let's talk about being respectful."

Ollie climbed onto Mama's warm lap. Her fur smelled like lavender and sunshine.

"When we respect others," Mama explained, "we think about their feelings too. Luna was here first today, and she needs time to finish her bath. How do you think she feels when you tell her to get out?"

Ollie thought for a moment. He watched Luna quietly washing behind her ears with a soft washcloth. "Probably sad," he whispered.

"That's right," Mama said, giving Ollie a gentle squeeze. "And while Luna finishes her bath, what could you do to help our family?"

Ollie's eyes lit up. "I could help with chores!"

"What a wonderful idea!" Mama smiled.

Ollie hopped down and looked around the bathroom. He saw towels on the floor from yesterday's bath time. "I can pick up these towels!" he announced proudly.

As Ollie folded the soft, fluffy towels, he felt happy and helpful. The towels smelled clean and fresh, like spring flowers.

"Ollie," Luna called from the bathtub, "thank you for waiting! I'm almost done washing my tail."

"You're welcome, Luna!" Ollie replied, feeling warm inside. "Take your time!"

A few minutes later, Luna stepped out of the tub, all clean and shiny. "The bathtub is ready for you now, Ollie!"

"Thank you for sharing!" Ollie said, giving his sister a big, fuzzy hug.

As Ollie slipped into the warm, bubbly water, he felt proud. He had been respectful, helped with chores, and now everyone was happy.

"Being respectful feels even better than a warm bath," Ollie thought to himself, though the bath felt pretty wonderful too!

From that day on, Ollie always remembered to wait his turn and think about others' feelings. And the bathroom became a place where both otters could enjoy their baths peacefully.

The End.
