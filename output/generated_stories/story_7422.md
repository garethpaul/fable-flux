# Fawn's Fair Solution

Little Fawn loved visiting Mama's home office. The cozy room smelled like lavender candles and had soft carpet that felt wonderful under her tiny hooves. Today, she was playing quietly with her favorite wooden blocks while Mama worked at her big desk.

"Click, click, click," went Mama's computer keys. Fawn built a tall tower with her colorful blocks, being very careful not to make too much noise.

Suddenly, the door burst open. "ROAR!" shouted Bear Cub as he stomped into the office. His heavy paws made loud THUMP sounds on the floor.

"I want to play too!" Bear Cub declared, grabbing some of Fawn's blocks without asking.

"Those are mine," Fawn said softly, her brown eyes wide with surprise. "I was using them."

"I don't care!" Bear Cub growled, knocking down Fawn's beautiful tower. The blocks scattered everywhere with loud CRASH and CLATTER sounds.

Fawn felt her heart beating fast like a little drum. Her eyes filled with tears, but she remembered what Mama taught her about staying safe and being brave.

"That wasn't fair," Fawn said, standing up tall even though she was much smaller than Bear Cub. "You knocked down my tower and took my blocks without asking."

"So what?" Bear Cub huffed, crossing his furry arms.

Mama looked up from her computer. "What's happening here?" she asked gently.

Fawn took a deep breath. The air still smelled like lavender, which helped her feel calmer. "Bear Cub took my blocks and knocked down my tower. I was playing nicely and quietly."

"Is that true, Bear Cub?" Mama asked kindly but firmly.

Bear Cub's face turned red under his brown fur. He looked down at his big paws. "Maybe," he mumbled.

"How do you think that made Fawn feel?" Mama asked.

Bear Cub peeked at Fawn's sad face. "Probably not good," he said quietly.

Fawn nodded. "It made me feel sad and scared. But I don't want to fight. Can we find a fair way to play together?"

Mama smiled proudly. "That's very wise, Fawn. What ideas do you have?"

Fawn thought for a moment, twirling one of her soft ears. "We could take turns! Bear Cub can build with half the blocks while I use the other half. Then we can switch!"

"That sounds fair," said Mama. "What do you think, Bear Cub?"

Bear Cub's eyes lit up. "Really? You'd share with me even though I was mean?"

"Yes," Fawn said with a gentle smile. "Everyone deserves a second chance to be kind."

Bear Cub carefully picked up the scattered blocks. "I'm sorry I knocked down your tower and took your blocks. That wasn't nice."

"Thank you for saying sorry," Fawn replied. "Now let's build something amazing together!"

They divided the blocks fairly. Bear Cub built a sturdy bridge while Fawn created a pretty castle. The office filled with happy giggles instead of angry sounds.

"Look, Mama!" Fawn called softly. "We made a whole town!"

"I'm so proud of both of you," Mama said warmly. "Fawn, you stood up for yourself and found a fair solution. Bear Cub, you listened and made things right."

As they played together peacefully, Fawn felt proud and happy. She had been brave, stayed safe, and helped solve the problem fairly. The lavender-scented office felt cozy and peaceful again.

"This is much more fun than fighting," Bear Cub admitted, carefully placing a block on their shared creation.

"It always is," Fawn agreed, her gentle heart full of joy.

The End.
