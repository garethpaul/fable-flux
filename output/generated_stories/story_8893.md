# Fiona's Fair Feast

In a cozy dining room with warm yellow walls and a big wooden table, lived a gentle deer fawn named Fiona. She had soft brown spots on her back and the kindest eyes you ever did see. Fiona loved helping her mama set the table for their special family dinners.

One sunny morning, Fiona's mama announced, "Today we're having a friendship feast! I've invited families from all around the forest to share a meal with us."

Fiona clapped her little hooves together. "Oh, how wonderful! I can't wait to meet everyone!"

Soon, the dining room filled with delicious smells. There was sweet honey bread from the Bear family, spicy acorn soup from the Squirrel family, and fresh berry salad from the Rabbit family. The table looked like a rainbow of colors and flavors.

When the guests arrived, Fiona noticed something that made her tummy feel funny. Mr. Raccoon and his family were sitting at the far end of the table, away from everyone else. They looked sad and lonely.

"Why are the Raccoons sitting so far away?" Fiona whispered to her mama.

Her mama sighed softly. "Some of the other families think raccoons are too messy to sit close to them."

Fiona's heart felt heavy. She watched as little Rascal Raccoon played quietly by himself while the other children laughed and shared stories at the other end of the table. His striped tail drooped, and his tiny paws fidgeted with his napkin.

"That doesn't seem fair," Fiona said, her voice shaking a little. "Everyone should feel welcome at our table."

Fiona took a deep breath and stood up on her wobbly legs. Her heart was beating fast, but she knew what was right.

"Excuse me, everyone," Fiona said in her clearest voice. "I think we need to make room for all our friends together."

The dining room grew quiet. All eyes turned to the little fawn.

"The Raccoon family brought the most beautiful nut cookies," Fiona continued, walking over to Rascal. "And look how carefully they washed their paws before dinner. Just like all of us!"

She gently took Rascal's paw. "Would you like to sit next to me? I'd love to hear about your favorite games."

Rascal's eyes lit up like tiny stars. "Really? You want me to sit with you?"

"Of course!" Fiona smiled. "Friends sit together."

Slowly, other young animals began to move closer. Bella Bunny hopped over and said, "I love your mask markings, Rascal! They look so cool!"

Sammy Squirrel chattered excitedly, "Can you really wash food in the stream? That's amazing!"

Soon, all the children were sitting together, asking questions and sharing stories. The grown-ups watched and began moving closer too, their hearts warming at the sight.

Mrs. Raccoon wiped a happy tear from her eye. "Thank you, Fiona. You made us feel truly welcome."

As they all shared the feast together, Fiona learned that the Raccoons told the funniest jokes, knew the best hiding spots, and were actually very clean and careful eaters. The dining room filled with laughter and new friendships.

"I'm so proud of you," Fiona's mama whispered, nuzzling her little one. "You showed everyone what fairness looks like."

Fiona's spots seemed to glow with happiness. She had learned that being fair means making sure everyone feels included and valued, no matter how different they might seem.

From that day forward, every feast in their dining room was a true friendship feast, where every family had a special place at the table.

The End.
