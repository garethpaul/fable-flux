# Kiki the Calm Koala’s Cozy Bedtime

Kiki the calm koala loved her cozy bedroom. The room smelled like fresh eucalyptus leaves, and her soft blanket felt fluffy like clouds. A tiny night lamp glowed warm and golden. The window showed a sprinkle of stars.

“Good evening, room,” Kiki whispered, patting her pillow. “I’m ready for a kind and healthy night.”

Kiki liked to take care of her body and her heart. She had a bedtime routine. It made her feel safe and sleepy.

First, she stretched her arms up, up, up. “Hello, sky,” she said. She touched her toes and wiggled her fuzzy ears. “Hello, toes.”

Next, Kiki brushed her teeth. Squish, squish went the toothpaste. Swish, swish went the brush. Bubbles tasted minty and cool.

“Two minutes,” Kiki sang, watching the sand in her tiny timer. “Brush, brush, brush. Rinse, rinse, rinse.” She looked in the mirror and smiled. “Clean and bright!”

She washed her paws with warm water and a drop of lemon soap. The suds felt slippery and silly. “Squeaky clean paws,” she giggled.

Kiki glanced at her bed. Her favorite book lay on top, and her stuffed kangaroo, Roo, waited with a soft stitched smile.

From the hallway, Kiki heard rustling. Her little cousin, Koko, peeked in, holding a crinkly bag of sugar cookies.

“Kiki,” Koko whispered, “want a cookie? They’re crunchy and sweet!”

Kiki sniffed the air. Sugar sparkled. Her tummy gave a tiny growl. But Kiki remembered her healthy habit. “I don’t eat cookies right before bed,” she said gently. “They make my body too bouncy.”

Koko pouted. “But just one?”

Kiki took a calm breath. “I promised myself no bedtime cookies,” she said with honesty. “Promises are important.” She smiled kindly. “We can save them for breakfast time? Or after our morning walk?”

Koko blinked. “You won’t tell on me?”

Kiki shook her head. “I will tell the truth if a grown-up asks. But right now, I can help you make a good choice.”

Koko looked at the crinkly bag. He crinkled it less. “What if we do something else?”

Kiki’s eyes twinkled. “We can do my bedtime routine together! It helps my body rest.”

Koko’s ears perked. “Okay.”

Kiki shared her soft brush and a tiny dot of paste on Koko’s brush from the bathroom cup. “We can take turns,” she said. “We share kindly.”

They brushed. Swish, swish. Bubbles tickled. Koko giggled. “Minty!”

Back in the cozy bedroom, Kiki showed Koko how to stretch like a sleepy star. They reached and swayed, slow and gentle. The night lamp glowed warm. The room hummed quiet and safe.

Koko’s tummy rumbled again. “I’m still a little hungry,” he whispered.

Kiki nodded. She listened with care. “I feel that sometimes too,” she said. “Let’s choose a calm snack.” She opened a small basket by the bed. Inside were apple slices in a little container and a bottle of cool water.

Kiki offered Koko an apple slice. “Crunch, crunch,” he said, smiling. “This tastes fresh.”

Kiki drank a small sip of water. “Sip, sip,” she said. “Water helps my body rest.”

There was a soft knock. Aunt Nara peeked in. “Little koalas, it’s bedtime,” she said kindly. “What’s going on?”

Koko looked at Kiki. He took a breath. “I brought cookies,” he said honestly. “Kiki helped me choose apples instead.”

Aunt Nara’s eyes were warm. “Thank you for telling the truth,” she said. “That shows integrity. And thank you, Kiki, for helping with kind ideas.”

Kiki shrugged softly. “We can be different and still be kind,” she said to Koko. “You like cookies. I like apples now. Both of us can make good choices.”

Aunt Nara smiled. “Cookies can wait for another time. I’m proud of your healthy habits and your honesty.”

Kiki climbed into bed. The blanket hugged her like a gentle cloud. Koko tucked Roo beside her. The room smelled like lemons and clean sheets.

“Story time?” Koko whispered.

“Short and sweet,” Kiki said. She opened her book and read in a soft voice about a moon that hummed lullabies. Koko leaned on the bed, eyes fluttering. The night lamp made tiny gold dots on the wall.

When the story was done, Kiki closed the book. “Good night, Koko,” she said. “Thank you for trying apples. That was brave.”

Koko hugged her. “Good night, Kiki. Thank you for being calm.”

Aunt Nara turned the lamp low. “Sleep well, little stars.”

Kiki took a deep, slow breath. In, out. Her body felt clean, cozy, and cared for. Her heart felt light. She had kept her promise. She had told the truth. She had been gentle with Koko’s feelings.

She whispered, “Healthy habits help me rest. Honesty makes me strong. Kindness makes us close.”

The stars outside winked. The cozy bedroom sighed with quiet. Kiki snuggled Roo and drifted to sleep with a soft smile, ready for tomorrow’s new day.

The End.
