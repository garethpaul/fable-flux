# Jump, Tap, and Save the Sound

Kippa was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and springy feet. Today, Kippa hopped into the musical practice room. The room smelled like clean wood and lemon polish. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Drums, shakers, and a shiny triangle all waited quietly.

“Hello, instruments!” Kippa giggled. “I’m ready to play!”

Coach Lila, a kind koala with a soft voice, waved from a corner. “Hi, Kippa. Today we practice music and care. We make sounds, and we also take care of our things and our world.”

Kippa’s ears perked up. “Care? Like hugs?”

“Like conservation,” Coach Lila said, tapping the triangle so it rang, ting! “Conservation means we use only what we need, we put things back, and we don’t waste.”

Kippa nodded. “I can do that!”

Kippa bounced to the shakers. They were bright like sunshine and felt smooth and cool. “Shake-shake-shake!” Kippa sang. The sound was like rain on leaves. It made Kippa want to jump higher and higher.

Coach Lila smiled. “Great beat! Now, let’s make a choice. Which shaker will you use? One is enough.”

Kippa looked at the shakers. There were three. Kippa’s paws reached for all of them. “I want all!”

Coach Lila held up a gentle paw. “Let’s breathe. In… and out. One shaker is enough for this song. That’s self-regulation. You can do it.”

Kippa put two shakers down and held one tight. “I choose the green shaker,” Kippa said. “One is enough.” Kippa shook, tap-tap, swish-swish, and felt proud.

Next, Kippa hopped to the drum. It was big and round. The top felt like soft paper. “Boom-boom!” Kippa tapped with careful paws. The drum sounded like a heartbeat. Kippa smiled and swayed.

“Listen,” Coach Lila said. “Can you drum and shake in a pattern? That’s coordination.”

Kippa blinked. “A pattern?”

“Like this: shake-shake, boom. Shake-shake, boom.” Coach Lila clapped softly: clap-clap, pause. “You can do it.”

Kippa tried. Shake-shake, boom. Shake-shake, boom. Feet tapped on the warm floor. Ears wiggled with the beat. “I did it!” Kippa laughed.

Coach Lila pointed to a small sign with a green leaf. “When we play, we also think about our planet. We keep the room neat. We save power. We use water and paper carefully. That’s conservation.”

Kippa saw a light switch by the door. “The sun is bright,” Kippa said, peeking at the sunny squares. “Do we need the big lights?”

“Good question,” Coach Lila said. “What do you think?”

Kippa looked around. The room was bright from the sun. “I think… we can turn off the lights.” Kippa hopped over and clicked. The lights went off. The room stayed warm and sunny. “We saved energy!”

“Great decision,” Coach Lila said. “You chose well.”

Kippa felt a happy flutter. The music felt even nicer now. Kippa picked up two drum sticks. “I want to hit super loud!” Kippa said, lifting the sticks high.

Coach Lila touched Kippa’s paw. “Let’s check in. Loud can be fun, but too loud can hurt ears and the drum. Take a slow breath. In… and out. Choose your level.”

Kippa breathed in. The lemon-polish smell tickled Kippa’s nose. Kippa breathed out. “I pick medium,” Kippa said. “Tap, not smash.”

Tap-tap-tap. The drum hummed warmly. The shaker whispered swish. The triangle sang ting! Kippa felt calm and strong.

Soon, other joeys peeked in. “Can we hear a song?” they asked.

“Yes!” Kippa said. “But we will share and care.” Kippa made a plan. “I’ll use one shaker. You use the triangle. We take turns on the drum.”

The joeys nodded. “We will keep the lids on the boxes,” one said, pointing to the basket of shakers. “And we will put sticks back.”

Kippa grinned. “That saves our things. That saves the planet, too!”

They began. Shake-shake, boom. Ting! Shake-shake, boom. Ting! The room filled with soft, happy sounds. The joeys swayed. The sunlight felt like a hug.

After the song, Kippa saw a water bottle tipped on its side. “Oh!” A drip, drip, drip was falling onto the floor.

Coach Lila raised her eyebrows. “What should we do?”

Kippa thought. “We don’t waste water.” Kippa lifted the bottle and twisted the cap tight. Drip, drip… stopped. Kippa wiped the little splash with a cloth. “Saved it!”

Coach Lila clapped softly. “That was careful and kind.”

Kippa looked around. “What next?” The drum sticks were on the floor. The shaker basket was open. The triangle beater was under a chair.

“We tidy!” Kippa said. “We put things back so they don’t get lost. We can use them again next time.”

The joeys cleaned as a team. Kippa bounced, picked up, and sorted. The basket clicked shut. The sticks rested on the drum. The beater went beside the triangle. The room looked neat, and it felt peaceful.

Kippa turned to the light switch. “The sun is still bright. Time to go. I will turn the lights off again.” Click. Quiet. Safe. Saved.

Coach Lila gave Kippa a sticker with a tiny green leaf. “You made good choices. You used one shaker, you picked a sound level, you saved water and energy, and you put things back. That is conservation, decision-making, self-regulation, and coordination.”

Kippa pressed the sticker to a soft gray notebook. “I learned that caring for music means caring for our world,” Kippa said. “I can choose to save, not waste.”

Coach Lila nodded. “Small choices make a big difference.”

Kippa hopped toward the door, heart light as a song. “Shake-shake, boom,” Kippa whispered, and smiled. “I will care today, and tomorrow, too.”

The End.
