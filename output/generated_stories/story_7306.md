# Nutkin's Sharing Solution

In a cozy house basement, there lived a clever squirrel named Nutkin. The basement was always busy and full of wonderful things. Old boxes made perfect climbing towers. Shiny pipes ran along the ceiling like jungle gyms. The concrete floor felt cool under Nutkin's tiny paws.

Nutkin loved exploring every corner of the busy basement. He could smell the earthy scent of stored potatoes. He could hear the gentle hum of the washing machine. But today, something exciting was happening!

Three little mice had discovered a pile of colorful yarn balls near the old workbench. The yarn balls were perfect for rolling and playing. But there was a big problem.

"I want the red one!" squeaked the first mouse, grabbing tightly.

"No, I saw it first!" cried the second mouse, pulling hard.

"It's mine! Mine! Mine!" shouted the third mouse, tugging with all her might.

The three mice were pulling the yarn ball in different directions. Their tiny faces were getting red and frustrated. The beautiful red yarn was starting to unravel and tangle.

Nutkin's fluffy tail twitched as he watched. "Oh dear," he said softly. "This isn't working very well."

Nutkin scampered down from his perch on a cardboard box. His sharp claws clicked on the smooth floor. He had an idea growing in his busy squirrel brain.

"Friends," Nutkin called out in his gentle voice. "What if we tried something different?"

The three mice stopped pulling. They looked at the wise squirrel with their bright black eyes.

"What do you mean?" asked the first mouse, still holding the tangled yarn.

Nutkin's whiskers twitched with excitement. "What if we take turns? Each mouse could play with the yarn ball for a little while. Then pass it to the next friend!"

The second mouse tilted her tiny head. "But what if the other mice take too long?"

Nutkin thought carefully. His bushy tail swished back and forth. "We could count together! Count to ten, then switch. That way everyone knows when it's time to share."

The third mouse's eyes grew wide with wonder. "That sounds fair! Can we try it?"

Nutkin helped them set up their new system. The first mouse rolled the red yarn ball while everyone counted: "One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten!"

"My turn!" giggled the second mouse. She caught the yarn ball and rolled it in happy circles.

"One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten!"

"Now me!" laughed the third mouse, clapping her tiny paws together.

Soon all three mice were having wonderful fun. They discovered that taking turns made the game even better! They could watch each other's creative moves. They could cheer for their friends. They could rest between turns.

"This is much more fun than fighting," said the first mouse, smiling widely.

"And nobody feels sad or angry," added the second mouse.

"Thank you, Nutkin!" said the third mouse. "Your idea worked perfectly!"

Nutkin felt warm and happy inside his furry chest. "Sometimes the best solutions come when we work together," he said proudly.

From that day on, whenever the basement got busy with playing friends, everyone remembered Nutkin's sharing solution. They learned that taking turns made everything more fun for everyone.

The End.
