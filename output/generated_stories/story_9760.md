# Beep’s Dinner-Time Teamwork

Beep was a helpful robot dog with shiny silver paws and a soft, wagging tail that glowed blue. Beep lived with Mia and Max. Every evening, Beep’s job was to help in the dining room. The dining room smelled like warm bread and sweet apples. The table felt smooth, and the lights made everything look cozy and bright.

“Dinner in ten minutes!” called Dad. His voice was warm. A timer on the wall went tick-tick-tick.

Beep’s ears blinked. “Woof-beep! Ten minutes means it’s time to plan,” Beep said in a friendly robot voice. “We can work together!”

Mia bounced up and down. “I want to put out the napkins!”

Max shook his head. “No, I want to do napkins. I’m faster!”

Their smiles faded. Their eyebrows squished. The room felt tight and prickly, like a balloon too full.

Beep sat between them and wagged. “Let’s take a breath. In…and out.” Beep took a calm, soft whirr-breath. Mia and Max copied. In…and out. The prickly feeling began to float away.

“Tick-tick-tick,” said the timer.

“We have a little time,” Beep said. “Let’s make a plan we all like. That’s called cooperation.”

Mia looked at Max. “We can share jobs.”

Max looked at Mia. “We can take turns.”

Beep opened a small panel on its side. A tiny light blinked like a star. “Here are the jobs: napkins, forks, cups, and apples. We have ten minutes. We will each choose one job now, then switch if we still want to help more.”

Mia raised her hand. “I pick napkins!”

Max nodded. “Okay. I pick forks.”

Beep wagged. “Great start. I will roll cups to the table with my gentle paw-wheels. After that, I will wash the apples.”

They hurried to work. Mia touched the napkins. They felt soft like clouds. “One napkin for Dad, one for Mom, one for Max, one for me,” she said, placing each one carefully. The napkins were bright and colorful, and they made the table cheerful.

Max counted forks. “One, two, three, four.” The forks made a happy tinkle sound on the plates. He lined them up straight, like little shiny soldiers.

Beep rolled cups with a quiet click-click. The cups were cool and smooth. “Cup here, cup there,” Beep sang. The hum of Beep’s motor sounded like a low kitten purr.

“Tick-tick-tick,” said the timer.

Dad peeked in. “Five minutes left, team!”

Mia and Max looked at the fruit bowl. Only two apples were shiny. Two were still dusty.

“I want to wash the apples,” Mia said.

“No, I do,” said Max.

The tight feeling came back. Mia crossed her arms. Max frowned. The room felt heavy.

Beep’s tail light turned soft green. “Pause,” Beep said gently. “We can solve this with kind words. What is the problem?”

Mia said, “We both want the same job.”

Max nodded. “Yes.”

Beep asked, “What do we each need?”

Mia whispered, “I want to help more.”

Max said, “I want to help more, too.”

Beep beeped softly. “We can share. Mia can wash two apples. Max can dry two apples. Then switch. That is cooperation. We also keep an eye on time.”

Mia smiled a little. “Okay.”

Max smiled back. “Okay.”

They went to the sink together. The water felt cool and splashy. It sounded like tiny rain. “Swish-swish,” Mia washed one red apple and one green one. Max used a soft towel. “Pat-pat,” he dried them until they were shiny like little suns.

“Switch!” Beep chimed.

Max washed the last two apples. Mia dried them with gentle pats. They laughed when water drops tickled their fingers.

“Tick-tick—ding!” sang the timer.

Dad came back in. “Time’s up! How did it go?”

Beep sat tall, tail glowing bright blue. “We used cooperation. We made a plan. We took turns. We watched the time. The table is ready!”

Mom walked in and sniffed the air. “Mmm, it smells like apples and warm bread. The table looks beautiful!”

Mia puffed with pride. “I did napkins and drying.”

Max grinned. “I did forks and washing.”

Beep added, “And I rolled cups and helped the team.”

They all sat down. The chairs made soft scritch sounds on the floor. Dad passed the bread. Mom poured water. The apples crunched with happy bites. Crunch, crunch!

Dad smiled. “When we share jobs, dinner is ready on time.”

Mom nodded. “And it feels good to help together.”

Mia looked at Max. “I liked taking turns.”

Max nodded. “Me too. It made the work go fast.”

Beep wagged. “Teamwork makes time feel friendly. Cooperation helps us solve little problems with kind words.”

They clinked cups gently. “To our team,” said Dad.

“To our team,” everyone echoed.

Beep’s tail glowed like a tiny moon. “Woof-beep. Helping together is the best.”

The End.
