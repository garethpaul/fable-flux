# Nutkin's Big Helper Day

Nutkin the squirrel lived in a cozy oak tree outside Mrs. Chen's house. Every morning, he would peek through the window of the guest room and wave his fluffy tail at the visitors staying there.

One sunny Tuesday, Nutkin heard soft crying coming from the guest room. He pressed his tiny ear against the cool glass window and listened carefully.

"I miss my mama," sniffled a small voice. "I don't understand what anyone is saying here."

Nutkin's heart felt warm and worried at the same time. Inside the room, he could see a little girl with dark braids sitting on the big blue bed. She was holding a stuffed elephant and wiping her eyes with the back of her hand.

"Oh my!" squeaked Nutkin. "She needs a friend!"

Being a very resourceful squirrel, Nutkin thought hard about how he could help. He scampered down the tree trunk, his claws making tiny *tap-tap-tap* sounds on the bark.

First, Nutkin gathered the prettiest things he could find: a smooth red leaf, three shiny acorns, and a purple flower that smelled like vanilla. He arranged them carefully on the windowsill where the little girl could see them.

When she noticed the colorful gifts, her tears stopped. "Oh! How beautiful!" she said in her own language, which sounded like music to Nutkin's ears.

But Nutkin wanted to do more. He remembered seeing Mrs. Chen's granddaughter draw pictures with crayons. Maybe pictures could help them talk to each other!

Nutkin found a piece of chalk near the garden path. Using his tiny paws, he drew a simple smiley face on the dark stone under the window. Then he drew a heart. Then he drew what he hoped looked like a squirrel and a little girl holding hands.

The girl pressed her face against the window and giggled. "You drew pictures for me!" she said, clapping her hands together.

Even though Nutkin couldn't understand her words, he understood her happy voice and bright smile. He chattered excitedly and did a little dance on his hind legs.

The girl opened the window. "Hello, little squirrel," she said softly, trying out the English words she had been learning. "Thank you for... for being kind."

Nutkin's chest puffed up with pride. He had been brave enough to try helping, even when he wasn't sure how. He pointed to himself and said, "Nutkin!" Then he pointed to her with a questioning chirp.

"Sofia," she said, pointing to herself. "My name is Sofia."

From that day on, Nutkin visited Sofia every morning. He would bring her small treasures and draw pictures in the dirt. Sofia would practice her English words with him and teach him words in her language too. She learned that "ardilla" meant squirrel, and Nutkin learned that kindness sounds the same in every language.

Mrs. Chen was amazed to see Sofia laughing and chattering happily by the window each day. "That little squirrel has such a helpful heart," she told Sofia's parents.

When it was time for Sofia to go home, she drew a picture of herself and Nutkin and left it on the windowsill. Nutkin kept it in his nest as a reminder that being helpful, brave, and kind can make wonderful friendships bloom.

The End.
