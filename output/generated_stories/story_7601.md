# Fawn's Soft Voice, Strong Choice

In a cozy bedroom, a gentle deer fawn curled on a soft leaf-print rug. Moonlight peeked through the curtains and made silver shapes on the wall. The room smelled like pine needles and clean blankets. A little night lamp glowed warm and golden. The fawn hummed a tiny song and nudged a fuzzy pillow with a nose as pink as a wildflower.

On the shelf sat stuffed friends: Owl, Bunny, and Squirrel. They watched with button eyes as the fawn lined up picture books. The pages whispered when turned. Swish, swish. It was almost bedtime, and the fawn loved a bedtime story.

“Tonight I will choose,” the fawn said in a small, brave voice. “I want to read ‘The Kind Meadow.’”

Mama Deer poked her head in the door. Her steps were quiet, like soft grass. “Story time,” Mama sang. “Let’s tuck in, my little leaf.”

The fawn climbed into the nest of blankets. They were warm and cuddly, like sunshine. But just then, a squeak came from the corner. Squeak! Squeak! Little Mouse popped out from a toy basket, dragging a tiny acorn car.

“I want the bouncy music box!” Mouse squeaked. “Play it now, please!”

Owl hooted gently from the shelf. “Hoo-hoo, I was hoping for a shadow show.”

Bunny’s ears wiggled. “I want extra snuggles,” Bunny whispered.

The fawn’s heart did a small flip. So many wants! The fawn felt small under so many wishes. The room felt too busy, too loud. The fawn tucked hooves under the blanket and took a deep breath. In. Out. The blanket smelled like lavender. Calm floated in.

Mama Deer sat on the bed and smoothed the fawn’s fur. “You can speak up, little one,” Mama said softly. “Use your kind voice.”

The fawn nodded. “I can? Even if others want different things?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Your voice matters too.”

The fawn looked at Mouse, Owl, and Bunny. The fawn heard a thump-thump in a brave chest, like a tiny drum. “I have something to say,” the fawn said. The voice was soft, but steady. “I feel squished when lots of voices talk at once. I want to choose the story first. Then we can take turns. That feels fair.”

Owl blinked. “Hoo?” he asked.

“First my book, ‘The Kind Meadow,’” said the fawn, “then the music box for a little dance, and then extra snuggles. We can do a shadow show tomorrow.”

Mouse’s whiskers twitched. “But I want bouncy now!”

The fawn put a hoof on the blanket and tried again. “I hear you, Mouse,” the fawn said kindly. “You want bouncy. I want quiet story first. My body needs quiet. I am saying what I need.” The words felt like standing tall in soft grass.

Mama Deer smiled. “That is self-advocacy,” she said. “You are asking for what you need, using gentle words.”

Mouse squeaked softer. “You listened to me. I can wait a little.”

Owl ruffled his cloth wings. “A plan is wise,” Owl hooted. “I can hoot quietly for the story.”

Bunny hugged the pillow. “I like turns,” Bunny said.

The fawn’s shoulders relaxed. The room seemed warm again, not too busy. Mama opened the book. Her voice was smooth, like a stream. The paper smelled like fresh wood. The fawn listened and felt safe. The story told of animals sharing the path and taking turns at a berry bush. The fawn smiled. It felt just right.

When the story ended, Mouse held up the music box. “Bouncy time?” Mouse asked.

“Yes,” said the fawn. “Bouncy time for just one song.” The music box sang tinkly notes, like raindrops on leaves. The fawn tapped tiny hooves. Mouse twirled. Owl hooted softly in time. Bunny giggled and clapped paws.

When the song finished, the fawn took another breath. “I need quiet again,” the fawn said, calm and clear. “I am ready for snuggles now.”

Bunny hopped into the fawn’s arms. Mama wrapped them both in the blanket. The fabric was smooth and cozy. The lamp’s golden glow made the room look like warm honey. Owl tucked in on the pillow. Mouse parked the little acorn car beside the bed.

“Thank you for telling us your needs,” Mama whispered. “Thank you for listening to others, too.”

The fawn felt proud, like a small star brightening the dark. “It is fair when we take turns,” the fawn said. “It is kind when we listen. I can use my voice to say what I need. And I can help others get what they need too.”

Mouse nodded. “You helped me wait. Now I can help you rest,” Mouse said, tiptoeing so quietly.

Owl added, “Peaceful plans make bedtime smooth.”

Bunny snuggled close. “Soft voices feel safe,” Bunny murmured.

The fawn yawned a tiny, squeaky yawn. “Thank you,” the fawn whispered. “Tomorrow we can do the shadow show first. That is fair.”

Mama kissed the fawn’s forehead. “You found a peaceful solution,” she said. “You took care of yourself and cared for your friends.”

The fawn closed sleepy eyes. The moonlight drew silver leaves on the wall. The night hummed like a gentle lullaby. Safe. Warm. Heard.

Tonight, the fawn learned: my voice is small and kind, and it is strong. I can say what I need. I can listen to you. Together, we can make fair and peaceful plans.

The End.
