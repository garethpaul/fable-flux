# Leo’s Kind Craft in the Guest Room

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny afternoon, Leo padded quietly into the guest room. The room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. A soft breeze tickled the white curtains. The bedspread felt smooth under his paws, and the carpet was warm and fuzzy.

Mama Lion whispered, “Grandma is coming to visit tonight. Let’s make the guest room feel extra special.”

Leo’s ears perked up. “I can help!” he said, puffing his small chest. He wanted to be helpful and brave.

Mama smiled. “That is kind, Leo. Grandma loves surprises.”

Leo looked around. The room was neat, but plain. On the little table, there was a basket of colorful paper, safe glue, soft yarn, and round stickers. Next to it sat a tiny bell and a lavender sachet that smelled like flowers.

Leo took a deep breath in. He smelled lavender. He let the breath out slowly. “I can do this,” he whispered. This was Leo practicing mindfulness—breathing to feel calm and strong.

Just then, a flutter of soft wings brushed the curtain. “Oh!” peeped a tiny gray mouse from behind a pillow. “I’m Mimi. I’m lost. I came in with the breeze.”

Leo blinked. “Hello, Mimi. You’re safe. This is the guest room,” he said kindly. “I am making a surprise for Grandma. Do you want to help?”

Mimi’s whiskers twitched. “I want to help, but I am small. I’m scared I can’t do it.”

Leo nodded. He remembered how he sometimes felt small too. “We can help each other,” he said. “Helping is brave.”

He padded to the table. “Let’s craft a welcome,” he said. The word felt warm in his mouth, like hot cocoa. “We can make a banner, a card, and a little bell ribbon.”

Mimi’s eyes shone. “What is a banner?”

“It’s a long, happy sign,” said Leo. He held up the paper. The paper felt smooth and cool. He cut triangles with safety scissors, snip, snip, snip. Mimi stuck on round stickers, pat, pat, pat. The stickers were red, yellow, and blue. They looked bright like candy drops.

“Good job, Mimi,” Leo said. “You are helping.”

Mimi smiled. “I am helping!”

Leo used the yarn to tie the triangles together. He tied slowly. Loop. Pull. Loop. Pull. He took another deep breath in, then out. “Slow is okay,” he told himself. “I can do hard things.”

They hung the banner over the bed. It swayed a little and made a soft whisper sound. The room looked cheerful now.

Next, they made a card. Leo drew a sun with a crayon. The crayon smelled a little waxy and felt strong in his paw. Mimi added tiny heart stickers. “Grandma will see our love,” Mimi said.

“She will feel welcome,” Leo said.

Then Leo saw the tiny bell. “Let’s make a bell ribbon for the door,” he said. He threaded the bell onto a piece of yarn. Tingle-ting! The sound was light and happy. Mimi held the yarn steady with her tiny paws while Leo tied a bow. The bow was not perfect. It was a little crooked.

Leo looked at the bow. His ears drooped. “It’s not perfect,” he said softly.

Mimi touched his paw. “It is kind,” she squeaked. “Kind is better than perfect.”

Leo breathed in. He smelled lavender again. He breathed out. “You are right,” he said. “Helping is what matters.”

Just then, they heard a small sneeze. “Ah-choo!” A blue butterfly had landed on the lampshade and sneezed from the dust. “Pardon me,” the butterfly fluttered. “I need a soft landing spot.”

Leo thought. “We can help,” he said. He folded a square of paper into a tiny tent. Mimi placed it by the lamp. The butterfly settled onto it, wings whispering. “Thank you,” it murmured. “You are helpers.”

Leo’s chest felt warm. Helping made him feel brave inside.

Footsteps padded down the hall. Mama Lion peeked in. “Oh my!” she said. “The room looks happy.”

Leo pointed. “We made a banner, a card, and a bell ribbon. Mimi helped. We helped the butterfly too!”

Mama Lion hugged him. Her fur smelled like sunshine. “You used your careful breaths. You crafted with kind hands. You helped others and worked together.”

The door bell ribbon jingled softly, tingle-ting, as Grandma arrived. “Hello, my dears,” Grandma said, her voice like a cozy blanket. She stepped into the guest room and saw the banner and the card. Her eyes shone. “This is beautiful. I feel so welcome.”

Mimi peeked out and waved a tiny paw. The butterfly fluttered a hello. Leo stood tall. “We wanted you to feel loved,” he said.

Grandma patted the bedspread. “I do feel loved. Thank you for your help. You are a brave little lion, Leo, and a kind friend.”

Leo’s heart felt big. He looked at Mimi and the butterfly. “We can help others, even when we are small,” he said. “We can take calm breaths, try new things, and make things with our hands.”

Mama Lion nodded. “Helping-others makes a room—and a heart—bright.”

Leo listened to the soft bell, smelled the lavender, and felt the fuzzy carpet under his paws. He smiled. “I like being a helper,” he whispered.

The lesson was clear in Leo’s heart: Helping-others, using calm breaths, and trying your best can make someone’s day shine. Crafting together builds self-confidence and joy.

The End.
