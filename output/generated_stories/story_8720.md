# Polly's Rainbow Pantry

Polly the parrot loved bright colors almost as much as she loved talking. Her feathers were emerald green with splashes of blue and yellow. Today, she flew into the big pantry where all the food was kept.

"Oh my!" squawked Polly, looking around with her bright black eyes. "This pantry looks so boring and gray!"

The pantry shelves were filled with plain brown boxes and white containers. Everything looked the same. Polly tilted her colorful head and had a wonderful idea.

"I know! I'll make this pantry as colorful and diverse as a rainbow!" she chirped excitedly.

Polly began to explore. She found red apples that smelled sweet and crisp. "Hello, beautiful red apples!" she said politely. "You're so shiny and round!"

Next, she discovered orange carrots that felt bumpy and cool. "Orange carrots, you're so bright and cheerful!" Polly praised them.

Then she spotted yellow bananas that were soft and curved. "Yellow bananas, you smell so tropical and yummy!" she sang.

Polly found green lettuce leaves that rustled like paper when she touched them gently with her wing. "Green lettuce, you're so fresh and crisp!" she whispered.

She discovered purple grapes that hung in clusters like tiny jewels. "Purple grapes, you're so sweet and juicy!" she exclaimed.

Finally, Polly found blue blueberries that rolled around like little marbles. "Blue blueberries, you're so small and perfect!" she giggled.

"Now," said Polly thoughtfully, "how can I arrange all these beautiful, different foods to show how wonderful diversity is?"

Polly carefully moved the fruits and vegetables, using her strong beak and clever claws. She arranged them by colors, making a beautiful rainbow pattern on the shelves.

"Red apples here, orange carrots there, yellow bananas in the middle," she sang as she worked. "Green lettuce on this shelf, purple grapes hanging nicely, and blue blueberries scattered like stars!"

As Polly worked, she made sure not to waste anything. She used old newspaper to line the shelves so the food would stay fresh longer. "We must take care of our Earth by not wasting food," she reminded herself.

When the other animals came to the pantry, they gasped in amazement.

"Wow, Polly!" said Benny the bear. "It's so colorful and beautiful!"

"Each color is different, but they all look wonderful together!" added Rosie the rabbit.

"Just like us!" squeaked Max the mouse. "We're all different colors and sizes, but we're all special!"

Polly's chest puffed up with pride. "That's exactly right!" she squawked happily. "Every fruit and vegetable is different, just like every animal and person is different. But when we put all our differences together, we make something beautiful!"

The animals learned that they could take only what they needed and share with others. They also learned to compost the peels and scraps to help plants grow.

"Diversity makes everything more beautiful and interesting," Polly explained to her friends. "And when we take care of our food and our Earth, everyone benefits!"

From that day on, the pantry stayed colorful and organized. All the animals worked together to keep it clean and waste-free. They celebrated their differences and learned that everyone had something special to offer.

Polly smiled as she looked at her rainbow pantry. "Different is beautiful," she sang softly. "And together, we can take care of our world!"

The End.
