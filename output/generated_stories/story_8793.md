# Pip the Mouse and the Missing Seeds

In a cozy corner of an old garden shed lived a kind little mouse named Pip. The shed smelled like fresh earth and sunshine, and it was filled with wonderful things like flower pots, watering cans, and bags of seeds.

Every morning, Pip would scurry around the shed, helping to keep it tidy. He loved the way the morning light streamed through the dusty windows, making everything sparkle.

One sunny day, Pip discovered something terrible. The bag of sunflower seeds that Farmer Joe had been saving for spring planting was torn open, and seeds were scattered everywhere!

"Oh no!" squeaked Pip, his tiny heart beating fast. He felt angry and worried. Who could have done this?

Just then, he heard rustling sounds from behind the tool box. Out popped his friend Squeaky, another mouse from the shed. Squeaky's cheeks were puffed out, and there were seed shells around his whiskers.

"Squeaky!" called Pip. "Did you see what happened to Farmer Joe's seeds?"

Squeaky's eyes grew wide. He quickly swallowed what was in his mouth and shook his head. "No, no! I don't know anything about seeds!" he said, but his voice sounded funny.

Pip felt his anger growing hot in his chest. He wanted to shout, but he remembered what his mama had taught him about taking deep breaths when feeling mad.

"Squeaky," Pip said gently, taking three slow, deep breaths. "I can see seed shells on your whiskers. It's okay to tell me the truth."

Squeaky's ears drooped. He looked down at his tiny paws. "I... I was so hungry, Pip. I found the bag and I couldn't help myself. I ate some seeds, and then the bag tore open!"

Pip could see that Squeaky felt very sorry. Instead of staying angry, Pip's kind heart felt sad for his friend.

"Squeaky, I understand you were hungry," Pip said softly. "But these seeds are special. Farmer Joe was saving them to plant beautiful sunflowers that will feed many birds and bees. When we take things without asking, it can hurt others."

Squeaky's eyes filled with tiny mouse tears. "I didn't think about that! I just thought about my empty tummy. What can we do?"

Pip's whiskers twitched as he thought. "Well, being honest was the first step. Now we need to make things right!"

Together, the two mice spent the whole morning carefully picking up every single seed from the shed floor. They found an old envelope and gently placed each seed inside.

When Farmer Joe came to the shed that afternoon, he found the envelope with a tiny note that read: "Sorry about the seeds. We cleaned them up. - The Shed Mice"

Farmer Joe chuckled warmly. "Well, I'll be! These little mice are quite honest and helpful." He left some special mouse food near their corner as a thank-you gift.

That evening, as Pip and Squeaky shared their treat, Squeaky said, "Thank you for helping me be honest, Pip. I feel so much better now!"

"Being honest isn't always easy," Pip replied, "but it always makes things better in the end. And now Farmer Joe knows he can trust us to help take care of his shed!"

From that day on, whenever Squeaky felt tempted to take something without asking, he remembered how good it felt to tell the truth and make things right.

The End.
