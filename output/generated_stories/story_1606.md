# Squeaky-Clean Squirrel in the Family Car

Sunny the squirrel was small, clever, and very resourceful. One bright morning, Sunny scampered into the family car. The car smelled like warm sunshine and fresh seat fabric. The seats were soft, and the steering wheel looked big and shiny. Sunny’s tiny paws made soft tap-tap sounds on the floor mats.

“Hello, car!” Sunny chirped. “Today, I will learn something new.”

The car doors clicked as Mama and little Jo climbed in. Jo was three and held a snack cup full of crunchy crackers. Crunch, crunch! Sunny peeked from under the seat and waved. Jo giggled. “Hi, Sunny!”

Mama smiled. “We’re going to the park. First, let’s clean up our hands. Clean hands keep us healthy.”

Sunny perked up. “Clean hands? Healthy? I want to learn!”

Mama took out a small bottle of hand gel and a pack of soft wipes from the door pocket. The gel smelled like lemons—zingy and bright. “Before we snack, we wash or clean our hands,” Mama said. “Germs are tiny and invisible, but washing sends them away.”

Sunny thought. “I don’t see germs,” he said, twitching his whiskers.

“You can’t see them,” Mama said gently. “But they can make tummies hurt. Clean hands are strong hands!”

Jo held out small hands. Squish went the gel. Rub-rub-rub. “Fronts, backs, between fingers, and thumbs,” Mama sang in a happy tune. “Rub until they’re dry!”

Sunny copied Jo. “Fronts, backs, between fingers, and thumbs!” He rubbed his tiny paws together. Warmth spread over his fur, and the lemony smell made his nose wiggle. “Mmm! My paws smell like a sunny tree!”

After Jo’s hands were clean, Mama passed the snack cup. Crunch! “May I have one?” Sunny asked politely.

“Yes,” said Jo. “Clean paws, clean snack!”

Sunny took a tiny cracker. “Crunch!” He smiled. “Clean tastes better!”

As the car began to hum, the seat belts clicked. “Click!” Sunny watched, wide-eyed. “Safety first,” Mama said. “Seat belts keep us safe.” The car rolled along. The air from the vents felt cool on Sunny’s whiskers. Whoosh. Trees waved outside, and Sunny felt happy and calm.

Halfway to the park, Jo dropped a cracker. Plop! It fell on the car seat. “Uh-oh,” Jo said, reaching for it.

“Wait,” Sunny said, holding up a paw. “We do not eat food from the seat,” he said wisely. “Seats can be dirty. Germs may live there.” Sunny remembered the lemon smell and Mama’s song.

Mama nodded. “Sunny is right. We throw it away and take a clean one. Thank you, Sunny.”

Sunny puffed up with pride. “I am a helper squirrel!”

Soon, Jo’s fingers got sticky from fruit. “My hands are icky,” Jo said with a frown.

Sunny looked around. He was resourceful, so he made a plan. He found the wipes, tugged one out with a soft shhhp, and held it up. “Here you go, Jo. Wipe, wipe, wipe!”

Jo giggled as the wipe felt cool and wet. “Wipe, wipe, wipe!” Jo sang with Sunny. “Fronts, backs, between fingers, and thumbs!” The sticky feeling went away, and Jo smiled again.

The car stopped at the park. “We are here!” Mama said. The sun was warm and bright. Birds chirped. Cheep-cheep! Jo and Mama unbuckled. “Before we play, let’s check our hands,” Mama said. “When we leave the car, we can pick up dust and crumbs.”

Sunny hopped onto the seat edge. He felt a crumb on his paw. “Oh!” He brushed it off. “Time to clean!” He held out the gel. Mama helped Jo and Sunny again. Squish. Rub-rub-rub. Lemon scent danced in the air.

They played at the park. Sunny scurried up a slide and down again—whee! Jo laughed and clapped. The sand felt gritty between Jo’s fingers. The swings creaked. The breeze tasted like sweet grass.

After playtime, their hands were sandy. Sunny looked serious. “We must wash at the sink,” he said. “Water and soap make bubbles. Bubbles chase germs away!”

They went to the park sink. The water made a soft rush—whoooosh. Sunny wet his paws. The soap smelled like flowers. He rubbed until bubbles covered his paws like tiny clouds. “Fronts, backs, between fingers, and thumbs!” he sang. Jo sang too. They rinsed and shook. Drip, drip!

Back in the family car, they sat with clean hands and happy hearts. The seats felt cozy. The air smelled fresh. Sunny wrapped his tail around himself and sighed with joy.

Mama smiled. “Sunny, you were resourceful. You found wipes. You reminded us not to eat dropped food. You helped us sing the washing song.”

Sunny’s eyes shone. “Clean hands keep us healthy, strong, and ready to learn,” he said proudly. “Before snacks, after play, after the potty, and when they feel icky—we wash or wipe!”

Jo nodded. “Clean hands, happy tummy!”

They all laughed. The car hummed softly as they drove home. Sunny looked out the window and felt proud. He had learned, he had helped, and he had shared the lesson.

Lesson: Clean hands help us stay healthy. We wash or wipe fronts, backs, between fingers, and thumbs. We do not eat food that falls on seats or floors. Being clean helps us feel good and ready to learn and play.

The End.
