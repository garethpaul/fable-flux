# Pablo Parrot’s Paint-and-Play Day

Pablo was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a speckled red tail. He loved to flap, sing, and make art. His favorite place was the creative art room. It smelled like orange crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight made soft squares on the floor. Paints sat in little cups, and brushes stood like tiny trees.

“Good morning, room!” squawked Pablo. “Squawk-tastic day!”

Each day, Pablo followed his routine. First, he stretched his wings. Next, he took three calm breaths. Then he looked at his chart: Paint, Snack, Sports Time, Story Time, Clean Up. He tapped the chart with his beak. “I can do it, bit by bit,” he sang.

Pablo dipped a brush and painted a big blue circle. “This is a ball,” he chirped. “Bounce, bounce!” He added orange lines like sunshine. He painted a green field and a white goal. “Sports are fun!” he said. “Sports help me move my body and my feelings.”

His friend, Mia the mouse, peeked in. “Hi, Pablo,” squeaked Mia. She wore tiny sneakers. “I want to play soccer after snack.”

“Squawk-yes!” said Pablo. “We can paint our plan first!”

They painted a picture map: a start line, a goal, and a water break spot. Pablo loved making a story map with pictures. “First, we warm up. Next, we kick. Then, we cheer,” he said. “Last, we share a story.”

Soon, Benny the bunny hopped in, ears flopping. “I love sports! Let’s go now!” he shouted. He grabbed the painted ball picture with a big paw.

Pablo’s feathers ruffled. The paper crinkled. “Gentle, please,” said Pablo. “I worked hard on that. I want my art to be safe.”

Benny blinked. “Oh. I was excited. Sorry.”

Pablo smiled. “Thank you for the sorry. I like soft hands with my art. That is my boundary.” He pointed to a small sign he had painted: “Please Ask Before Touching.”

Mia nodded. “Boundaries keep our things and feelings safe.”

They had snack. Crunch, crunch went carrots. Sip, sip went water. Pablo wiped a little carrot crumb from his beak and checked the chart. “Now it’s Sports Time!”

They moved to a soft mat in the art room. The mat smelled clean, like lemons. Pablo clapped a rhythm. “Warm up! One, two, flap! One, two, stretch!” They giggled as they moved. Warm muscles felt cozy.

Benny rolled a soft foam ball. “Kick time!” he said. He kicked too hard, and the ball bumped into Pablo’s paint cups. “Uh-oh!”

Splat! Blue paint wiggled toward the edge of the table. Drip, drip.

Pablo’s heart fluttered. “My art!” he squeaked. He took a deep breath. “I feel worried,” he said softly. He looked at Benny. “Please stop and ask next time. That is my space.”

Benny’s ears drooped. “I forgot to check. I’m sorry. How can I help?”

Pablo fluffed his feathers down. “Thank you. Let’s clean together.” They wiped the paint. Swish, swish. Mia brought paper towels. Soon, the table was clean, and the paints were safe.

Pablo smiled again. “Sports can be safe when we follow rules and listen to bodies and spaces.”

They tried again. Pablo set a boundary line with blue tape on the floor. “Kick on this side,” he said. “Art stays on that side.”

Mia clapped. “Great idea!”

Benny nodded. “I can follow the line.”

They played. Benny kicked gently. Tap! The foam ball rolled. Mia stopped the ball with her little foot. Pablo flapped and cheered, “Go, team! Go!”

They took a water break at the spot on their map. Cool water felt nice on tongues. Pablo watched their happy faces. “Sports help us get strong and learn to wait, listen, and share,” he said.

After sports, Pablo checked the chart again. “Story Time!”

They sat on a soft rug. Pablo told a story about a brave ball who learned to roll slow and roll fast. He used different voices. “I am the ball,” he squawked in a tiny voice. “I roll with care and I roll with flair!” The friends laughed. The room felt warm and friendly.

Then Pablo shared his feelings. “Today, I had a problem. The ball bumped my paints. I felt worried.” He took a calm breath. “We found a solution. We cleaned up. We made a line. We asked before touching. That kept our art and our game safe.”

Benny smiled. “I learned to check your sign and ask.”

Mia added, “I learned that routines help us have fun. Warm up, play, drink water, and rest.”

Pablo puffed with pride. “I learned that sports are more than running. Sports are teamwork, care, and listening to boundaries.”

They cleaned up together. Brushes went into cups. Balls went into bins. Tap, tap, click. The art room smelled like soap and orange crayons again.

Pablo looked at the chart one last time. “We did it, bit by bit,” he sang. He wrapped a wing around his friends. “Thanks for playing and listening.”

“Thanks for painting and sharing,” said Mia.

“Thanks for setting the line,” said Benny.

Pablo nodded. “When we care for each other’s space, we all have room to play.”

The End.
