# Rosie Rabbit Learns to Listen

Rosie Rabbit loved spending time on the peaceful front porch of her cozy burrow home. The wooden boards felt warm under her soft brown paws, and she could smell the sweet flowers blooming in Mama's garden nearby.

"Rosie!" called Mama Rabbit from inside. "It's time to help with chores!"

But Rosie was busy watching a colorful butterfly dance around the purple petunias. She wiggled her pink nose and pretended not to hear.

"Rosie, dear!" Mama called again. "Please come help me fold the clean towels!"

Rosie's long ears twitched, but she kept watching the butterfly. "I'm too busy right now, Mama!" she called back.

A few minutes later, Papa Rabbit stepped onto the porch. His gray whiskers sparkled in the sunshine. "Hello, little one. Would you like to help me water the vegetable garden?"

Rosie shook her head quickly. "No thank you, Papa. I'm watching this pretty butterfly!"

Papa smiled gently. "That butterfly is beautiful, Rosie. But listening to your family is important too."

"I am listening!" Rosie said, though her eyes stayed on the butterfly.

Just then, Grandpa Rabbit hobbled onto the porch with his walking stick. He sat down in the creaky rocking chair next to Rosie.

"What a lovely day," Grandpa said softly. "Perfect for sitting and thinking."

Rosie glanced at Grandpa. His kind eyes twinkled as he rocked back and forth. The chair made a gentle "creak-creak" sound that was somehow very soothing.

"Grandpa," Rosie asked, "why does everyone want me to do chores? Can't I just watch butterflies?"

Grandpa's ears perked up. "Well, little bunny, let me tell you a secret. When I was young like you, I thought chores were boring too."

Rosie's ears stood straight up. Now she was really listening!

"But then my mama taught me something special," Grandpa continued. "She said that when we help our family, we show them we love them. And when we listen carefully, we might discover something wonderful."

"Like what?" Rosie asked, scooting closer to Grandpa's chair.

"Well," Grandpa whispered, "your Mama needs help folding towels because she's making a surprise picnic lunch for our family. And your Papa wants help watering the garden because the tomatoes are almost ready to pick for our sandwiches!"

Rosie's eyes grew wide. "A picnic? Really?"

"Really," Grandpa nodded. "But they wanted it to be a surprise. Good thing I listened when they were planning it this morning!"

Rosie felt her heart flutter with excitement. She had been so busy not listening that she almost missed something wonderful!

"I want to help!" Rosie said, jumping up. "I want to listen too!"

She bounded inside and found Mama in the laundry room. "Mama, I'm sorry I didn't listen. Can I still help with the towels?"

Mama's face lit up with a warm smile. "Of course, sweet pea! Thank you for coming to help."

Together, they folded the soft, clean towels that smelled like sunshine. Then Rosie helped Papa water the red tomatoes and green lettuce in the garden.

Later, as the family enjoyed their surprise picnic on the front porch, Rosie felt proud and happy. The sandwiches tasted extra delicious because she had helped grow the vegetables.

"Thank you for teaching me to listen, Grandpa," Rosie said, giving him a big hug.

"When we listen with our hearts," Grandpa said, "we discover the best surprises of all."

The End.
