# Ellie's Lullaby Problem

Little Ellie the elephant calf sat in her cozy nursery. Soft moonlight came through the window. Her blanket felt warm and fuzzy. But Ellie could not sleep.

"Mama," called Ellie. Her voice sounded worried. "I can't remember Grandmama's special lullaby."

Mama Elephant walked over with gentle steps. Her trunk touched Ellie's forehead softly. "What's wrong, little one?" she asked.

Ellie's big eyes filled with tears. "Tomorrow is Baby Cousin's first bedtime. I want to sing Grandmama's lullaby like she did for me. But I forgot the words!"

The special lullaby was very old. Grandmama's grandmama had sung it first. Every elephant baby heard it on their first night. It was their family's most important tradition.

"Hmm," said Mama. She sat down next to Ellie. "Let's think together. What do you remember?"

Ellie closed her eyes tight. She tried very hard. "It goes... 'Sleep little baby, under the...' Oh no! I can't remember what comes next!"

Mama smiled warmly. "Let's use our thinking skills. What did Grandmama always point to when she sang?"

Ellie thought and thought. "The stars! She pointed up at the stars!"

"That's right!" said Mama. "So maybe it goes 'under the stars'?"

Ellie's trunk wiggled with excitement. "Yes! 'Sleep little baby, under the stars!' But what comes after that?"

"Close your eyes again," said Mama. "What did you feel when Grandmama sang?"

Ellie remembered the gentle rocking. She remembered feeling safe and loved. "I felt like... like I was floating on clouds!"

"Wonderful thinking!" said Mama. "Maybe the next part talks about clouds?"

Ellie jumped up and down. "I think it goes 'Dream on soft clouds, gentle and white!'"

Mama clapped her trunk against her knee. "You're doing great! Now think about how the song ended. What did Grandmama always say last?"

Ellie sat very still. She remembered Grandmama's warm trunk hugging her tight. "She always said we loved me!"

"Perfect!" said Mama. "So maybe it ends with 'We love you so, sleep through the night!'"

Ellie sang the whole lullaby together:
"Sleep little baby, under the stars,
Dream on soft clouds, gentle and white,
We love you so, sleep through the night!"

The melody sounded just right. It felt warm and special.

"But Mama," said Ellie, "what if I forget again tomorrow?"

Mama had a wonderful idea. "Let's practice together every night this week. And we can teach it to Papa too. When we share traditions with our family, they stay strong."

Ellie smiled big. "And when Baby Cousin grows up, I'll teach the lullaby to her babies too!"

"That's exactly right," said Mama. "Traditions grow stronger when we share them and help each other remember."

The next evening, Ellie sang perfectly for Baby Cousin. Her voice was clear and sweet. Baby Cousin's eyes grew sleepy and peaceful. The whole family listened with happy hearts.

After the lullaby, Uncle Elephant said, "Thank you for keeping our tradition alive, Ellie. You thought so carefully to remember it."

Ellie felt proud and warm inside. She had solved her problem by thinking step by step. She had shared something special with her family. And now their tradition would continue for many more elephant babies to come.

From that night on, Ellie never worried about forgetting again. She knew that when families work together and share their traditions, the important things always stay safe in their hearts.

The End.
