# Panda Pao Waits and Grows

Panda Pao lived on a sunny balcony with pots of soil, green leaves, and tiny flowers. The balcony garden smelled like fresh mint and wet earth. Bees hummed. A soft breeze tickled Pao’s black-and-white cheeks.

“Good morning, garden,” Pao said. “I will take care of you.”

Pao’s favorite pot held little carrot seeds. Pao wanted big, crunchy carrots. Pao rubbed the smooth pot and said, “Grow, please.” Pao felt a wiggle of excitement in their tummy. “I want carrots now!”

Mama Mei Panda came out with a watering can. “Good morning, Pao,” she said. “Plants grow slow. We can help them with water, sun, and kind hands.”

Pao nodded. “I will try to wait.”

Pao took a deep breath. In, out. The air smelled like basil. Pao felt calmer.

Pao filled the tiny watering can. Drip, drip, drip. “Just a little drink,” Pao said to the seeds. The soil turned dark and cool. Pao touched it with one soft paw. “Moist,” Pao whispered. A ladybug crawled across a leaf like a shiny red dot.

“Can I dig and see the carrots?” Pao asked.

Mama Mei shook her head gently. “We need to be honest with the garden. We do not poke or pull. That would hurt the baby plants.”

Pao looked at the soil. Pao wanted to see. Pao wanted now. Pao felt the wiggle again. It was big. Pao put both paws on their belly. “Hello, wiggle,” Pao said. “I see you.”

Mama Mei smiled. “That is self-regulation, Pao. You noticed your big feeling. Now try a calm tool.”

Pao nodded. “I can count for calm.” Pao looked at a bright yellow flower and counted petals: “One… two… three… four… five.” The wiggle got smaller. Pao blew out a soft, long breath. “I can wait.”

To be kind to Earth, Pao saved water in a little bucket from yesterday’s rain. “We reuse,” Pao said. “We take care.” Pao carried the bucket slowly, slowly, so no water spilled. The water made a tiny song when it poured: plink, plink.

“Good choice,” said Mama Mei. “Sustainability means we use only what we need.”

Pao checked the compost jar. It smelled a little stinky and a little sweet. Banana peels, tea leaves, and carrot tops were inside. “We feed the soil,” Pao said proudly. Pao scooped a small bit and mixed it in. The soil felt crumbly and rich.

A small bird landed on the railing and chirped, “Peep!”

“Hello, Bird,” said Pao. “This is our balcony garden. Please be gentle.”

Pao planted two more seeds. “One for Bird to look at,” Pao said, “and one for me.”

The sun moved across the sky. Pao waited and watched. Waiting is hard. Pao wanted to touch the pot and dig. Pao’s paw lifted. Pao stopped. “I want to dig,” Pao said honestly.

Mama Mei nodded. “Thank you for telling the truth. What can you do instead?”

Pao thought. Pao hummed a soft tune. “Hum, hum, hum.” Pao wiggled toes. Pao squeezed paws and then let them go. Squeeze… release. Squeeze… release. The big want felt smaller.

“Let’s check the leaves,” Mama Mei said. They looked for bugs that might bite. “We can move them kindly,” she said.

Pao found a hungry snail under a pot. “Hello, Snail,” Pao said. “Please eat this lettuce leaf, not the baby carrots.” Pao placed the snail in a tray with a spare leaf. The snail’s shell was smooth and cool.

They picked up tiny fallen leaves and put them in the compost. They recycled a crinkly paper cup into the blue bin. “We take care of our home,” said Pao.

Day by day, Pao kept a gentle routine:
- Water a little in the morning.
- Save extra water.
- Sing to the seeds.
- Count for calm.
- Check for bugs with kind hands.
- Tell the truth about big wants.

One misty morning, Pao saw something green. “Mama! Mama! Sprouts!” Little green hairs poked up like tiny umbrellas. Pao’s heart felt warm. “Waiting worked!”

Pao wanted to pull a sprout. Pao’s paws tingled. Pao noticed. “Hello, tingles. I can wait.” Pao took three slow breaths and counted to five again. Pao smiled. “I am patient.”

As days passed, the sprouts grew into feathery tops. Orange tips peeked under the soil. Pao was careful. Pao only touched the leaves to say hello. Finally, Mama Mei said, “Today, we harvest.”

Pao held the green tops and wiggled gently. Pop! Out came a small, bright carrot. The smell was sweet and earthy. Pao’s eyes shone. “I did it!”

Pao looked at Mama Mei. “I want to eat it all myself.” Then Pao paused. “I feel grabby. I will be honest.”

Mama Mei hugged Pao. “Thank you for your honesty. Let’s share. We can also save some seeds to plant again.”

They washed the carrot with saved rainwater. Crunch! Pao took a bite. It was juicy and crisp. Pao shared a piece with Mama Mei and placed the peels in the compost jar. Pao patted the soil. “Thank you, Earth.”

Pao looked at the balcony garden. Bees hummed. Leaves swayed. The bird chirped. Pao felt proud and calm.

“I learned to wait,” Pao said. “I can notice my big wants. I can breathe, count, and choose kind actions. I can tell the truth. I can care for Earth.”

Mama Mei smiled. “That is self-regulation. That is sustainability. That is honesty. And that is love.”

Pao hugged the watering can. “I am a patient panda,” Pao said. “And I can grow, too.”

The End.
