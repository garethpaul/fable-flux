# Panda Bao’s Brave Little Beat

Panda Bao was a small, patient panda with big gentle eyes. Today, Bao stepped into the musical practice room. It smelled like polished wood and warm tea. Sunlight slid across the floor in bright stripes. Drums, bells, and little flutes lined the walls like shiny stars.

Bao took a deep breath. “I feel a little wobble in my tummy,” Bao whispered. “But I can be brave.”

Bao’s friends were there too—Lulu the lamb, Tiko the turtle, and Mina the mouse. Coach Kora, a kind crane with soft gray feathers, flapped her wings and smiled.

“Welcome, musicians,” Coach Kora said. “Today we will share a song from our families. We will take turns. We will listen. We will be brave.”

Bao’s ears perked up. Share a family song? Bao loved the old drum beats Mama Panda taught at home. The beats sounded like a strong heartbeat: dum, da-dum, dum. But sharing in front of friends felt new and a little scary.

Lulu stepped first. “My grandma hums this,” Lulu said. She plucked a small harp. Ting-ting-ting! Her song was soft like a lullaby. Everyone listened. Everyone smiled.

“Thank you, Lulu,” Coach Kora said. “We take turns. Who will be next?”

Tiko lifted a bamboo flute. “Slow and steady,” he said. He blew a clear, gentle note. Whooo. The sound curled like steam from a cup. Bao felt the wobble in the tummy calm a little.

“Thank you, Tiko,” said Coach Kora. “Mina?”

Mina hopped to a tiny drum no bigger than a saucer. “My auntie taught me this tap-tap.” She tapped tap-tap-tap, then paused, then tap-tap-tap again. It felt like raindrops on leaves. The room smelled sweet and safe. Bao’s paws tingled.

Coach Kora turned to Bao. “Bao, would you like to share?”

Bao’s heart thumped. The big drum in the corner looked tall and bright. Bao touched it. The leather was smooth and warm under the paw. “I want to share Mama’s beat,” Bao said, voice small. “But I feel shy.”

Coach Kora nodded. “Being brave does not mean not feeling shy,” she said. “Being brave means doing a kind, true thing even when you feel shy.”

Bao looked at friends. Lulu gave a tiny nod. Tiko smiled his slow smile. Mina squeaked, “You can do it, Bao!”

Bao took another breath. In, out. Bao lifted the drumstick. “One moment,” Bao said. “I will wait for my turn.”

Coach Kora smiled. “It is your turn now, Bao.”

Bao stood tall. “This beat is from my family,” Bao said. “Mama says it is the heartbeat of the bamboo forest.” Bao tapped softly first: dum… da-dum… dum. The sound was deep and warm. It made the floor hum under Bao’s feet.

Bao felt brave. Bao pressed a little stronger: DUM… da-DUM… DUM. The drum filled the room. Lulu’s ears perked. Tiko’s eyes shone. Mina swayed side to side.

“Listen,” Bao said between beats. “Mama says the beat helps us breathe when we are scared.” Bao tapped and whispered, “In… out… in… out.” The drum matched the breath. The wobble in Bao’s tummy melted like snow in sunshine.

When the beat ended, the room was quiet and bright. Then everyone clapped their paws, hooves, and wings.

“That was beautiful,” Coach Kora said. “Thank you for sharing your tradition, Bao.”

Bao’s cheeks felt warm. “I was scared, but I did it,” Bao said. “I was brave.”

Lulu bleated gently. “Your drum felt like a hug.”

Tiko nodded. “I could feel the bamboo forest.”

Mina squeaked, “Can we try your beat too?”

Bao’s eyes sparkled. “Yes! We can take turns playing it.” Bao showed the pattern: “Soft-soft, strong. Soft-soft, strong.” Each friend took a turn. Lulu went first: dum… da-dum… dum. Then Tiko: dum… da-dum… dum. Then Mina, tiny but steady: dum… da-dum… dum.

When Mina finished, Bao clapped. “Good taking turns!” Bao said. “Good listening!”

Coach Kora fluttered close. “Let’s make a friendship song,” she said. “We will mix our traditions.”

They made a circle. Lulu added her ting-ting-ting. Tiko blew a gentle whooo. Mina tapped her raindrop rhythm. Bao kept the brave bamboo beat. Together the sounds danced around the room. The drum felt like a strong heart. The harp sparkled like morning sun. The flute floated like a soft cloud. The little drum clicked like happy feet.

Bao smiled so wide the cheeks almost squeaked. “This is our song,” Bao said. “We are sharing. We are kind. We are brave.”

After the music, Bao poured warm tea from a tiny pot. The steam smelled sweet and grassy. Everyone sipped and sighed.

Coach Kora spoke softly. “What did we learn today?”

Bao lifted a paw. “I learned that bravery is doing a good thing, even when I feel shy.”

Lulu added, “I learned to listen and wait for my turn.”

Tiko said, “I learned that sharing traditions makes new friends.”

Mina squeaked, “I learned that small drums can be strong!”

Coach Kora nodded. “You were all brave. You took turns. You shared your family songs. That makes your hearts big.”

Bao looked at the big drum and felt proud. “My tummy wobble is gone,” Bao said. “When it comes back, I will breathe and I will try.”

The friends packed the instruments with gentle hands. The practice room felt warm and bright. As they waved goodbye, Bao tapped the brave beat once more: dum… da-dum… dum.

“I can be brave,” Bao whispered. “And I can help my friends be brave too.”

The End.
