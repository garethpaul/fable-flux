# Poppy Panda’s Calm Dance Plan

Poppy the panda lived in a cozy treehouse that smelled like sweet bamboo and warm tea. Her treehouse had soft rugs, twinkly paper lanterns, and a big round window. Birds sang, leaves whispered, and the air felt cool and green. Poppy was patient. She liked to sit, breathe, and think before she acted.

One sunny morning, Poppy clapped her paws. “I will make a dance show tonight!” she said. “I want to teach a new dance. The Spin-and-Grin!” She wiggled her fluffy tail. She loved to share and to learn.

Poppy opened her little red notebook. Inside, she kept her dance ideas. She took a deep breath, slow and steady. “First,” she said softly, “I gather knowledge.” She smiled. “Knowledge helps me know what to do.”

She listened to the leaves. Swish, swish. She tapped the floor. Tap, tap. She felt the breeze on her nose. Cool, cool. “These sounds can be the beat,” she said.

But when Poppy tried to spin, she bumped into a stool. Thunk! The stool fell. Her music shell rolled away. Clatter! Poppy’s face felt hot. Her tummy felt tight. “Oh no,” she said. “I feel mad.”

Poppy sat on her soft rug. She put one paw on her belly and one paw on her heart. “I am patient,” she whispered. “I will count and breathe.” She breathed in for one, two, three. She breathed out for one, two, three. The hot feeling cooled. The tight feeling softened.

“Now I will try a calm plan,” Poppy said. “I can use what I know.” She opened her notebook again. She drew a simple map of her treehouse floor. She marked where the stool should go. She marked a circle for spinning. She made a list in baby steps:
- Move the stool.
- Pick up the shell.
- Practice slow spins.
- Add the grin.

She smiled. “I can rely on myself. I can learn and try.”

Poppy moved the stool. Scrape, scrape. She picked up the music shell. It felt smooth and cool. “Thank you, shell,” she said. She set it on a soft scarf so it would not roll away.

Poppy tried a slow spin. Whoosh! Her paws felt steady. She tried again. Whoosh, whoosh. “Better,” she said. “I am learning.”

Just then, a little squirrel named Suri peeked in the window. “Hi, Poppy! I heard music. Can I watch?”

“Yes,” said Poppy with a warm grin. “I am making a dance. I had a bump, and I felt mad. But I used calm breathing. I used my knowledge to make a plan.”

Suri’s tail twitched. “Sometimes I feel mad too,” she whispered. “My tail gets knotty.”

Poppy nodded. “That happens. Feelings are okay. We can learn what helps.” She showed Suri her list. “This is my plan.”

Suri’s eyes grew wide. “A list! Smart! Can I help?”

“You can help me practice,” Poppy said. “We will count, breathe, and spin.”

They stood together. Poppy said, “Breathe in, one, two, three. Breathe out, one, two, three.” They breathed. The air smelled like leaves and tea. The lanterns glowed softly. A bird chirped, peep-peep.

“Now, slow spin,” said Poppy. They spun small, gentle circles. Their paws made tiny taps on the rug. Tap, tap, tap.

Suri giggled. “I did it! My tail is not knotty now.”

Poppy clapped. “You used knowledge too. You tried breathing and baby steps.”

Soon, more friends came. Toto the turtle thumped up the ladder. Lina the ladybug zipped in through the window. “We heard there is a dance!” said Toto.

“Yes,” said Poppy. “Tonight we share the Spin-and-Grin. But first, I must solve one more problem. We need music.”

The friends looked around. The shell was quiet. The wind was soft. “What can we do?” asked Lina.

Poppy put a paw on her chin. “We will learn from what we hear.” She listened. Swish, swish went the leaves. Peep, peep went the bird. Tap, tap went Toto’s shell when he moved. “We can make a band with the sounds we know!”

Poppy gave each friend a simple job. “Toto, tap your shell, slow and steady. Lina, buzz gently, like a hum. Suri, shake these dry leaves softly. I will clap a calm beat.” They practiced. Tap, hum, shake, clap. The sounds felt smooth and friendly.

Poppy drew one more tiny list:
- Start with breathing.
- Count the beat.
- Spin slowly.
- Add a grin.

When the sun was low and gold, the treehouse felt warm and ready. Lanterns twinkled like little stars. Friends gathered on the floor. Poppy stood tall and peaceful. “Welcome to our Spin-and-Grin,” she said. “Tonight we share a dance. We learned with calm hearts and clear plans.”

They began. “Breathe in,” Poppy said. “Breathe out.” Tap, hum, shake, clap. Poppy spun, slow and steady. She showed the grin—big and kind. Suri spun too, tail smooth and happy. Toto tapped with pride. Lina hummed like a tiny breeze.

Everyone smiled. The dance felt safe. The dance felt fun. The dance felt like learning.

When they finished, the friends cheered. “Hooray for Poppy! Hooray for the plan! Hooray for calm!” The treehouse glowed with joy.

Poppy bowed. “Thank you, friends. Today I learned that knowledge helps me. I learned I can rely on myself. I learned I can calm my anger with breathing and baby steps. When I share what I learn, we all grow.”

Suri hugged Poppy. “Your patience helped me be patient too.”

Poppy nodded. “When we know more, we do better. When we are calm, we can learn. When we make a plan, we can solve problems.”

They all spun one more tiny Spin-and-Grin. Soft music, warm lights, happy hearts.

The End.
