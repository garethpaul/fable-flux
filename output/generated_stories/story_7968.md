# Benny Bunny Tries Again

Tap-tap-tap went the little keys. Click-click went the mouse. Benny Bunny sat in a small, cozy home office. The room smelled like warm paper and crayons. Sunlight made a bright square on the floor. Benny’s ears stood tall.

“I will write my story today,” Benny said. “I will make a song for it too!” His whiskers wiggled with excitement.

Benny was a determined rabbit. He loved to tell stories. He loved to sing and hop. He wanted to make a story that children could hear, sing, and hop along with. He had a notebook, a tiny drum, and a shiny ukulele.

Benny took a deep breath. He opened his notebook. He began to tap the keys and hum. “La-la, hop-hop,” he sang softly.

But the words did not come. His paws felt clumsy on the keys. His ears drooped. “Oh no,” he said. “This is hard.”

Benny tried again. He tapped slowly. “Once there was a bunny…” He stopped. He could not think of the next part. He sighed. “Maybe I should just rest.”

He looked out the window. The sky was blue. He heard birds go tweet-tweet. He smelled fresh air. He stood up and stretched his legs. “A small exercise break might help,” he said.

Benny hopped in place. Hop, hop, hop! He swung his arms and rolled his shoulders. He touched his toes. He counted. “One… two… three… four… five!” His heart felt warm. His body felt awake.

Back at the desk, Benny smiled. “I will try again.” He sang a little tune on his ukulele. Strum, strum, strum. “La-la, hop-hop, never stop,” he sang. The words sounded sweet. He tapped the keys and wrote the line.

Suddenly, the drum rolled off the shelf. Boom! It hit the floor. The sound made Benny jump. His paw slipped. He bumped the keys and closed his notebook. His song stopped. “Oh no!” cried Benny. “I made a mess.”

He picked up the drum. He patted it gently. “I am sorry, little drum,” he said. “I will be more careful.” He put the drum in a safe place. He made-amends by sweeping up the dust and tidying the cords. Now the room felt neat and calm again.

Benny sat down. “I can do it,” he whispered. “I can keep going.” He took one slow breath in and one slow breath out. In… out…

He began to tell the story out loud first. “Once there was a bunny who loved to hop and sing,” he said. He tapped the desk softly, like tiny footsteps. Tap, tap, tap. “The bunny had a big idea! The bunny wanted to tell a story with a song.”

Benny listened to his own words. They sounded good! He smiled. “I will sing the idea,” he said. He strummed. “Hop, hop, little feet. Clap, clap, keep the beat. Try and try, you will see, you can do it—1, 2, 3!”

He wrote the song line by line. He added sounds. Clap! Tap! La-la! He hummed and tapped. His ears wiggled to the rhythm. He felt happy. The story grew. He wrote about the bunny’s first try, and the second try, and the exercise break that helped. He wrote about saying sorry to the drum, and cleaning up, and trying again.

Then Benny hit a new bump. The ending was missing. His nose twitched. He felt a tiny worry in his tummy. “What if I cannot finish?” he asked.

Benny remembered his own song. He took three hops in place. Hop, hop, hop. He clapped. Clap, clap! “Try and try,” he said. “I will not give up.”

He stood and walked around the room. The carpet felt soft under his paws. He looked at a picture on the wall. It showed a bright moon. “Aha!” said Benny. “The bunny can sing under the moon!”

He hurried back to the desk. He wrote the ending: “The bunny sings under the moon. Friends clap and hop. The story is done. The song is fun. Try and try, and soon you win.”

Benny read the whole story aloud. His voice was warm and proud. He sang the song all through. Strum, strum. La-la. Clap, clap. He could feel the beat in his chest. It felt like a happy drum.

He smiled and whispered, “I did it.” He placed the notebook on the shelf, neat and safe. He patted the drum again. “Thank you for waiting,” he said.

Benny opened the window and let the breezy air in. He felt calm, tired, and glad. “Persistence helped me,” he said. “When I got stuck, I took care of my body with exercise. I made-amends for my mess. I told my story with music. I tried and tried, and I did not stop.”

Then Benny sang softly to the sunlit room, “Hop, hop, little feet. Clap, clap, keep the beat. Try and try, you will see, you can do it—1, 2, 3!”

And he could.

The End.
