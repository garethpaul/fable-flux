# Ruby Rabbit's Big Bathroom Problem

Ruby Rabbit lived in a cozy burrow with her three little brothers. Every morning, they all needed to use the bathroom at the same time. The small bathroom had one sink, one mirror, and one special step stool.

"I need to brush my teeth first!" squeaked Benny, the youngest bunny.

"No way! I'm the biggest!" shouted Max, pushing past everyone.

"I was here first!" cried little Oliver, hopping up and down.

Ruby watched her brothers argue. Their fluffy tails twitched with frustration. The bathroom felt crowded and noisy. Nobody was getting ready for school.

"Stop!" Ruby said firmly, thumping her strong hind foot. "This isn't working!"

All three brothers looked at their smart big sister. Ruby's whiskers twitched as she thought carefully.

"We need to talk about this," Ruby said. "Let's have a family meeting."

The four rabbits sat in a circle on the soft bathroom rug. Ruby could smell the minty toothpaste and hear water dripping from the faucet.

"Everyone wants to use the bathroom," Ruby said kindly. "But we can't all fit at once. What should we do?"

"I could brush my teeth super fast!" offered Benny, wiggling his pink nose.

"Or we could take turns," suggested Oliver thoughtfully.

Max crossed his fluffy arms. "But I don't want to wait!"

Ruby nodded. "I understand, Max. Waiting is hard. But what if we made waiting fun?"

She hopped up excitedly. "What if each rabbit helps the family while waiting? That's called community service!"

"What's that?" asked Benny, tilting his soft ears.

"It means helping others," Ruby explained. "While one bunny uses the bathroom, the others can help Mama make breakfast or tidy the burrow."

The brothers' eyes grew bright. This sounded much better than arguing!

"Let's try it," Ruby suggested. "Benny goes first because he's smallest and fastest. Max, you can help Mama set the table. Oliver, you can organize our school books."

"What about you, Ruby?" asked Oliver.

"I'll make sure everyone gets their turn," Ruby said proudly.

They tried Ruby's plan. Benny hopped into the bathroom first. He brushed his tiny teeth quickly, humming a happy song. Meanwhile, Max carefully placed bowls on the breakfast table. Oliver stacked their school books neatly by the door.

"My turn!" called Max when Benny finished.

Max washed his face and combed his fur. Oliver helped Mama pour orange juice. Benny folded their pajamas.

When Oliver's turn came, he brushed his teeth extra carefully. Max and Benny helped pack their lunch boxes together.

Finally, Ruby had her turn. The bathroom was peaceful and quiet. She could hear her brothers laughing in the kitchen. Her plan was working!

At breakfast, Mama Rabbit smiled proudly. "You solved your problem together! You negotiated, took turns, and helped the family."

"What does negotiated mean?" asked Benny, munching his carrot.

"It means you talked about the problem and found a solution everyone liked," Ruby explained.

Max nodded enthusiastically. "And we all helped! That felt really good."

"Tomorrow we can do the same thing," suggested Oliver. "But maybe I can go first?"

"We can take turns being first," Ruby said wisely. "That's fair for everyone."

The rabbit family finished breakfast together, feeling happy and proud. They had learned that talking, sharing, and helping others made everything better.

From that day on, the bathroom was never crowded or noisy again. The four rabbit siblings had discovered that working together was much more fun than fighting.

The End.
