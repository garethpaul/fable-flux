# Momo's Messy Mistake

Momo the monkey loved to play. His brown fur was soft and fluffy. His long tail helped him swing and climb. Today, he was playing in the big laundry room.

The laundry room smelled like fresh soap bubbles. Warm, clean towels sat in neat piles. The washing machine hummed a gentle song.

"Wheee!" Momo swung from the clothesline. Clean shirts and socks hung all around him.

CRASH! Momo's tail knocked over a basket. Clean clothes tumbled everywhere. White socks mixed with colorful shirts on the floor.

"Oh no!" Momo said. His big brown eyes grew wide.

Mama Bear walked into the room. She had been folding clothes all morning. Her paws were tired from all the hard work.

"Momo, what happened?" Mama Bear asked. Her voice sounded sad.

Momo's tummy felt funny. He wanted to run away and hide. But he remembered what Papa always said: "Brave monkeys tell the truth."

"I was swinging," Momo said quietly. "I knocked over your clean clothes. I'm sorry, Mama Bear."

Mama Bear's face looked tired. "These clothes were clean and folded. Now they need to be washed again."

Momo saw how sad Mama Bear looked. His heart felt heavy. He had made a big mess of her hard work.

"I want to help," Momo said. "Please show me how."

Mama Bear smiled a little. "That's very respectful, Momo. Let's work together."

First, they picked up all the clothes. Momo sorted the white socks from the colored shirts. His little paws worked carefully.

"Good job separating the colors," Mama Bear said. "That keeps the clothes looking nice."

Next, they put the dirty clothes in the washing machine. Momo helped pour the soap. It smelled like fresh flowers.

"Why do we wash clothes?" Momo asked.

"Clean clothes keep us healthy," Mama Bear explained. "They help us feel good and look nice for others."

While the clothes washed, Momo helped sweep the floor. The broom was big, but he tried his best. Mama Bear showed him how to make neat piles of dust.

"You're learning to respect our home," Mama Bear said kindly. "When we take care of our things, they take care of us."

The washing machine finished with a happy beep. Together, they moved the wet clothes to the dryer. Momo carefully placed each sock and shirt inside.

"Can I write a note?" Momo asked. "To say sorry?"

Mama Bear gave him paper and a big crayon. Momo drew a picture of himself helping. He wrote "SORRY" in wobbly letters.

"This shows you care," Mama Bear said, hugging him tight. "That's what respect means - caring about others and their feelings."

When the clothes were dry, they folded them together. Momo made small, neat piles. His tongue stuck out as he concentrated hard.

"Look!" Momo said proudly. "I can fold too!"

"You learned something important today," Mama Bear said. "When we make mistakes, we can fix them with respect and hard work."

Momo nodded. His chest felt warm and happy. He had turned his big mistake into something good.

From that day on, Momo always asked before playing in the laundry room. He helped Mama Bear every week. He learned that respecting others' work made everyone feel happy.

"Being respectful feels good," Momo told his friends. "It makes hearts feel warm and homes feel nice."

The End.
