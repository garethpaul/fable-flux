# Rocco's Clean Car Concert

Rocco the raccoon loved music more than anything. He carried his tiny tambourine everywhere he went. Today, he was riding in the family car with Mom and Dad Bear. The leather seats felt smooth under his fuzzy bottom.

"Look at my paws!" Rocco said happily. His little paws were covered in sticky mud from playing outside. "I made music in the puddles this morning!"

Rocco started shaking his tambourine. *Jingle, jangle, jingle!* The bright sound filled the car. But when he clapped his muddy paws together, he left brown smudges on the clean car seat.

"Oh my!" said Mom Bear, looking in the mirror. "Rocco, your paws are very dirty."

Rocco looked down at his paws. They were brown and grimy. He touched the car window and left a muddy handprint. "Oops," he whispered.

Dad Bear pulled the car over safely. "Rocco, we need to talk about keeping clean," he said gently. "When we're dirty, we can make messes that are hard to clean up."

"But I like being muddy!" Rocco said. "It's fun!"

"Playing in mud IS fun," Mom Bear agreed. "But look around the car. What do you see?"

Rocco looked carefully. His muddy pawprints were everywhere. On the seats. On the windows. Even on his tambourine! The car smelled like wet dirt instead of the nice pine scent it usually had.

"I made everything dirty," Rocco said quietly. His ears drooped down. "I'm sorry."

"It's okay, little one," Dad Bear said kindly. "We all make mistakes. But this is a good time to learn about staying clean."

Mom Bear reached into her purse. She pulled out some wet wipes that smelled like fresh lemons. "These will help us clean up," she said.

Rocco watched as Mom Bear gently wiped his paws. The cool, damp cloth felt nice on his fur. "That tickles!" he giggled as she cleaned between his tiny fingers.

"When we keep our paws clean, we show respect for others," Dad Bear explained. "Clean paws mean we don't make messes in shared spaces like our car."

"Or at other people's houses," Mom Bear added. "Clean paws show we care about keeping things nice for everyone."

Rocco nodded. He was learning something important. "Can I help clean the car?" he asked.

"Of course!" Mom Bear gave him a fresh wipe. Together, they cleaned all the muddy spots. Rocco worked carefully, wiping each smudge until it disappeared.

When they finished, the car looked shiny and clean again. It smelled fresh and pine-scented once more. Rocco's paws were clean and soft.

"Now I can play my tambourine without making messes!" Rocco said happily. He shook his instrument carefully. *Jingle, jangle, jingle!* The music sounded even prettier in the clean car.

"That's right," Dad Bear smiled. "Clean paws make for better music-making."

As they drove home, Rocco sang a little song: "Clean paws, clean paws, that's the way to go! Clean paws show we care for others, this I know!"

Mom and Dad Bear clapped along. The car filled with happy music and laughter.

From that day on, Rocco always washed his paws before getting in the car. He learned that being clean wasn't just about looking nice – it was about showing respect and kindness to others.

The End.
