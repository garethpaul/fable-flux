# Ollie the Otter’s Rooftop Ready-Day

Ollie the otter loved to wiggle and giggle. He lived with his family in a cozy house with a flat rooftop. The rooftop was warm from the sun and smelled like fresh air and flowers from the neighbor’s garden. On the wall, a little bell went ting-ting when the wind blew. Ollie called it the singy bell.

One bright morning, Ollie scampered to the stairs. “Rooftop time!” he chirped, tail swish-swish. He wanted to splash in his tiny rooftop tub, draw chalk shapes, and listen to the singy bell.

Mama Otter called, “Ollie, first we get ready. We prepare our bodies and our things.”

Ollie paused on the step. “Prepare?” he asked, head tilted.

“It means we take care before we play,” Mama said, smiling. “That’s self-care. We help our body feel good and safe.”

Ollie blinked. “Okay! Rooftop ready-day!”

They went to the bathroom. The sink water went whoosh. “Brush, brush,” Mama sang. “In Spanish we say cepillar. Can you say it?”

“Seh-pee-yar,” Ollie tried. He giggled at the new word.

“Great job,” Mama said. “We brush teeth to keep them happy and strong.”

Ollie brushed in little circles. Minty bubbles tickled his tongue. “It’s tingly!” he laughed.

Next, they washed hands. “Wash, wash,” Mama sang. “In French we say laver.”

“Lah-vay,” Ollie said. Warm water felt like a hug on his paws. The soap smelled like oranges. He sniffed. “Mmm. Oranges!”

“Clean paws are good for play,” Mama nodded. “Now let’s pick a hat and sunscreen.”

Ollie chose a blue hat with a tiny fish on it. The sunscreen was cool and squishy. Mama dotted it on his nose. “Dot, dot,” she said. “In Swahili we say jua for sun.”

“Joo-ah,” Ollie whispered, touching his hat. He felt safe and ready.

They packed a small bag. “We prepare,” Mama said. “Water bottle, snack, towel.”

“Apple slices,” Ollie said. Crunch, crunch in his mind. He licked his lips.

On the rooftop, the breeze went shhhh. The bell went ting-ting. The sky was big and blue, with puffy clouds like marshmallows. Ollie set his tiny tub near a sunny patch. The water splashed plip-plop as he wiggled his toes.

“First, a quick stretch,” Mama said. “Stretch arms up. In Japanese we say nobiru.”

“No-bee-roo,” Ollie said, reaching high. His shoulders felt loose and happy.

He hopped into the tub. “Splash-splash!” Cool water kissed his fur. He giggled. “Hello, toes.”

After splashing, Ollie drew with rainbow chalk. He made a sun, a fish, and a big letter O. He traced it with his paw. “O is for Ollie. In English, O. In Spanish, O too!” He smiled. “I like words.”

Suddenly, a gray cloud puffed over the sun. The breeze got stronger. The singy bell went ting-ting-ting! Ollie shivered. “Brrr. I feel chilly.”

Mama peeked from the door. “Time to care for your body again,” she said kindly. “What do we do?”

Ollie looked at the bag. “Towel!” He wrapped himself up. The towel was soft and warm. He took a sip of water. “Sip, sip.”

“Smart choice,” Mama said. “You prepared, and now you feel better.”

Raindrops began to patter. Pit-pat, pit-pat on the rooftop. The chalk lines blurred into dreamy colors. Ollie frowned a little. “My fish is swimming away.”

Mama nodded. “The rain is painting, too.”

Ollie listened. The rain sang a soft song. “Drip-drop,” he whispered. “In Hindi we say baarish for rain.”

“Baa-rish,” Mama repeated, smiling. “What’s the plan, Rooftop Ready Ranger?”

Ollie thought. He touched his hat. He looked at the door. He felt his tummy. “Snack, then inside,” he said. “Dry, warm, and safe.”

They went downstairs. Ollie placed his wet hat on a hook. “We dry things,” he said. “We care for our stuff.”

He munched apple slices. Crunch, crunch. He felt full and calm. He took deep breaths. In and out. The house smelled like cinnamon and clean fur. He heard the rain tapping the roof, like little paws dancing.

Mama brought a small notebook. “Let’s make a list for next rooftop day,” she said. “Preparation helps us play longer and feel good.”

Ollie clapped. “List!” He spoke each word slowly. “Hat. Sunscreen. Water. Towel. Snack. Stretch.”

Mama added one more. “Feelings check. We ask our body, ‘How do you feel?’”

Ollie touched his chest. “Body, how do you feel?” He waited. “I feel cozy.”

They drew pictures by each word. A hat with a fish. A sun with dots. A blue droplet for water. A fluffy towel. A shiny apple. Long arms stretching. A little heart for feelings.

The rain stopped. Sunlight peeked in, warm and golden. Ollie smiled. “We cared. We prepared. We learned new words!”

Mama hugged him. “That’s self-care, language love, and smart planning.”

Ollie wiggled with joy. “Tomorrow, rooftop again?”

“Tomorrow,” Mama said. “And every day, we listen to our bodies, use kind words, and get ready.”

Ollie looked at the window. He whispered, “Thank you, rain. Thank you, sun. Thank you, words.” His eyes felt sleepy-soft.

He curled up with his towel, smelling oranges and cinnamon. The singy bell gave one last tiny ting from the breeze.

Ollie yawned. “Rooftop ready-day was the best,” he murmured.

The End.
