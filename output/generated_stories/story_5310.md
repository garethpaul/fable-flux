# Rocco’s Gentle Song

Rocco was a small raccoon with bright eyes and a soft, striped tail. He loved music. He wore a tiny blue scarf and a tiny drum. Tap-tap, hum-hum! Rocco liked to play happy songs.

Rocco lived in a comfortable nursery. The nursery had a big window, fluffy rugs, cozy blankets, and baskets of toys. Sunlight made the room feel warm and golden. The air smelled like clean laundry and apples. A clock ticked softly on the wall: tick, tock, tick, tock.

Every afternoon, the children in the nursery had Quiet Time. They cuddled with their stuffed animals and listened to soft stories. Miss June, the teacher, whispered, “Quiet Time helps our bodies and minds rest.”

Rocco wanted to help. “I can play a lullaby,” he said. “I can make a soft song for Quiet Time.”

He practiced all morning. He laid out his instruments by himself, one by one. First, his tiny drum. Then, a small wooden flute. Then, a shaker that sounded like gentle rain. He checked each one. “Ready,” he said proudly. Rocco liked being prepared. It made him feel strong and independent.

When the clock said Quiet Time, Rocco stood up. He took a deep breath. He looked at the children snuggling under blankets. He looked at Miss June smiling. Rocco lifted his drum.

But just then, Baby Leo started to fuss. His face got red. He waved his hands. “No drum! Too loud!” he cried. His lip trembled. He squeezed his ears with his fingers.

Rocco froze. His heart thumped. He didn’t want to scare anyone. He wanted to help. He felt a little sad, and his tail went droopy.

Miss June knelt beside Rocco. “We have a problem,” she said softly. “Leo feels the drum is too loud. We need to find a peaceful way to fix this. Can we use our words and listen?”

Rocco nodded. He took another deep breath. “Leo,” he whispered kindly, “I didn’t know it was too loud. Thank you for telling me. What sound feels gentle to you?”

Leo sniffled. “Shhhh,” he said. “Like the wind.”

Rocco listened carefully. “You like wind sounds,” he repeated. He put the drum down. He spoke to the whole room, “Friends, I want to make Quiet Time cozy. Can we talk and choose together?”

The room was very still. The children looked at Rocco. Maya raised her hand. “I like soft flute,” she said. Ben hugged his bear. “I like tiny rain,” he added.

Rocco smiled. “Thank you for sharing. I hear you.” He pointed to his instruments. “We can try a soft flute. We can try tiny rain. We will see what feels best.”

Rocco prepared again. He rolled up a blanket and placed it under the drum to make it extra quiet, just in case. He put the shaker in a small basket to keep the sound gentle. He licked his lips. His mouth tasted like apples from snack time. He felt calm.

“Ready,” he whispered. He held up one paw. “I will play slowly. If it is too much, please tell me with calm words. I will listen.”

He brought the wooden flute to his mouth. He blew a soft, airy note. Hooo… like a whisper. The sound floated like a warm breeze through the nursery. The curtains swayed. Leo relaxed his hands. His shoulders dropped. “I like that,” he murmured.

Then Rocco picked up the shaker and moved it very gently. Shhh… shhh… It sounded like soft rain on a window. Ben smiled and hugged his bear tighter. Maya yawned.

Rocco watched everyone’s faces. He listened to their breathing. In… out… in… out… He matched his music to the calm rhythm. He felt proud. He had used peaceful words. He had listened. He had prepared and changed his plan. He was helping.

Miss June whispered, “Rocco, you found a solution with peaceful communication. You kept your friends safe and calm.”

Rocco whispered back, “I asked, I listened, and I made a new plan.”

He finished with a gentle hum, low and sweet. Mmm… mmm… The room smelled like soap and warm blankets. The sunlight was soft and sleepy. One by one, the children closed their eyes. Leo held his tiny blanket and smiled.

After Quiet Time, the children sat up slowly. They looked rested and happy. Leo toddled over to Rocco. “Thank you,” he said. “Your wind song was nice.”

Rocco’s tail lifted high. “Thank you for telling me what you needed,” he said. “Your words helped me help you.”

Maya clapped softly. “Rocco, you are prepared and kind,” she said. Ben nodded. “You let us choose,” he added.

Rocco packed his instruments by himself. He wrapped the drum in a blanket. He placed the flute in a soft case. He put the shaker in the basket. He felt independent and careful. He made a little sign for later that said, “Soft Sounds for Quiet Time.” He drew a cloud, a raindrop, and a tiny wind swirl.

Miss June smiled. “Today we learned something important,” she said. “When we have a problem, we can use peaceful words. We can listen to each other. We can prepare and change our plan. That is how we help.”

Rocco touched his blue scarf and nodded. “I will remember,” he said. “Peaceful words make peaceful music.”

The End.
