# Polly's Caring Morning

High up on a sunny rooftop, a bright green parrot named Polly stretched her colorful wings. The morning sun felt warm and cozy on her feathers. Polly loved starting each day the same special way.

"Good morning, world!" Polly chirped happily. She fluffed her beautiful red and blue tail feathers and began her daily routine.

First, Polly cleaned her bright orange beak with her tiny foot. "Clean beak, happy me!" she sang to herself. Then she smoothed down all her green feathers, making them shine like emeralds in the sunlight.

Just as Polly was about to practice her favorite songs, she heard a small, sad sound. "Sniff, sniff."

Polly looked around with her bright black eyes. There, in the corner of the rooftop, sat a little gray mouse. The mouse looked very upset and was rubbing his tiny pink nose.

"Hello there, little friend," Polly said gently, hopping closer. "My name is Polly. What's wrong?"

The mouse looked up with watery eyes. "I'm Pip," he squeaked softly. "I'm lost and can't find my way home. I'm also very hungry and thirsty."

Polly's heart felt warm with caring feelings. She wanted to help Pip feel better. "Don't worry, Pip. Let me take care of you," she said kindly.

First, Polly flew to her special water dish and carefully carried some fresh, cool water to Pip in her beak. "Here, drink this. You'll feel much better," she cooed.

Pip drank the water gratefully. "Thank you, Polly. That tastes wonderful!"

Next, Polly remembered the sunflower seeds she had been saving for later. She brought them to Pip and watched as he nibbled happily. His whiskers twitched with joy.

"Now," said Polly, "let's find your home. What does it look like?"

Pip described a small hole near some red flowers. Polly flew high above the rooftop and spotted the exact place Pip had described, just one building over.

"I can see your home!" Polly called down excitedly. "Climb on my back, and I'll fly you there safely."

Pip was a little scared at first. "I've never flown before," he whispered.

"Don't worry," Polly said softly. "I'll be very gentle and keep you safe. Hold on tight to my feathers."

With Pip holding on carefully, Polly flew slowly and smoothly to the next rooftop. She landed right next to the little hole by the red flowers.

"Home!" squeaked Pip joyfully. "Thank you so much, Polly! You're the kindest friend I've ever met."

Polly felt her heart glow with happiness. "I'm glad I could help you, Pip. Taking care of others makes me feel good inside too."

As Pip scurried safely into his home, Polly flew back to her rooftop. She realized something wonderful – helping Pip had made her morning routine even more special.

From that day on, Polly added something new to her daily routine. After cleaning her beak and smoothing her feathers, she always looked around to see if anyone needed help or kindness.

"Taking care of myself AND others makes every day brighter," Polly sang happily as she watched the sun climb higher in the blue sky.

And whenever Pip visited the rooftop, he and Polly would share seeds and water together, the very best of friends.

The End.
