# Oliver Owl's Healthy Day Plan

High up in the quiet attic of an old farmhouse lived Oliver Owl, the wisest bird in the whole neighborhood. His soft brown feathers rustled gently as he perched on a wooden beam, watching the golden morning sunlight stream through the dusty window.

"Hoot, hoot," Oliver yawned, stretching his big wings wide. But as he tried to fly down to greet the day, something felt wrong. His wings felt heavy, his tummy rumbled loudly, and his bright yellow eyes felt sleepy and tired.

"Oh my," Oliver said to himself, looking at his reflection in an old mirror. "I don't feel very well today. I've been staying up too late reading books and forgetting to eat my healthy foods."

Just then, Oliver heard tiny voices coming from below. Three little mice had climbed up into the attic, looking worried and sad.

"Oliver Owl! Oliver Owl!" squeaked the smallest mouse named Pip. "We need your help! We feel tired and grumpy every day, and we don't know why!"

Oliver's wise eyes twinkled as he realized they all had the same problem. "Well, my little friends," he hooted gently, "I think we all need to learn about healthy habits together!"

Oliver fluffed his feathers and stood up tall. "Let me tell you about my special Healthy Day Plan. First, we need good sleep. Our bodies need rest to grow strong, just like flowers need nighttime to bloom."

"How much sleep?" asked Pip, his tiny whiskers twitching with curiosity.

"Little ones like you need lots of sleep - about 10 to 12 hours each night! And wise owls like me need 8 hours," Oliver explained, making a soft hooting sound that made the mice giggle.

"Next," Oliver continued, spreading one wing toward the window where they could see the bright sun, "we need to eat colorful, healthy foods. Red apples, orange carrots, green lettuce, and purple berries! These rainbow foods give our bodies energy and help us feel happy."

The mice's eyes grew wide with excitement. "Like a rainbow on our plates!" squeaked the middle mouse named Dot.

"Exactly!" Oliver hooted proudly. "And third, we need to move our bodies every day. Flying, running, jumping, and playing help keep our hearts strong and our minds sharp."

Oliver demonstrated by doing a little owl dance, flapping his wings and hopping from one foot to the other. The mice clapped their tiny paws and giggled with delight.

"But how do we remember to do all these things?" asked the biggest mouse named Max.

Oliver's eyes sparkled with wisdom. "We make a plan! Every morning, we can say: 'Today I will eat healthy foods, move my body, and get good sleep tonight.' When we make a goal and stick to it, we become healthy and strong!"

From that day forward, Oliver and his mouse friends followed their Healthy Day Plan together. They ate crunchy vegetables, played fun games in the attic, and went to bed early each night. Soon, Oliver's feathers became shinier, his eyes brighter, and his wings stronger than ever. The mice had more energy to play and explore.

"Thank you, Oliver!" the mice cheered. "We feel wonderful now!"

Oliver smiled warmly. "Remember, little ones, healthy habits help us feel our best every single day. When we take care of our bodies, we can take care of others too!"

The End.
