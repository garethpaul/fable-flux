# Rocco Raccoon's Backyard Band

The sun was warm and bright in the sunny backyard. Birds chirped in the tall trees. The grass smelled sweet and green. Flowers nodded in the breeze. In the shade by the fence, a little raccoon named Rocco tapped his striped tail on a bucket.

Tap-tap. Tap-tap-tap.

Rocco was a musical raccoon. He loved sounds. He loved rhythm. He loved making songs with anything he could find. Today, he wanted to play a happy song for his friends.

Rocco looked around the yard. There was a blue plastic bin full of recyclables. It glinted in the sun. Bottles clinked together. A metal can made a soft ring. A cardboard tube felt smooth under his paws. “These can be instruments!” Rocco said, his whiskers twitching with excitement. “We can make a backyard band!”

Mina the mouse popped out from under a leaf. “I want to play,” she squeaked.

Gus the grasshopper rubbed his legs and chirped, “Me too!”

Ellie the robin hopped down from a branch. “Let’s sing,” she trilled.

Rocco smiled and passed out the clean, empty recyclables. “Mina, here’s a little bottle. Gus, take these two caps to click. Ellie, this shiny can can be your drum.” He held a long cardboard tube like a horn. “And I’ll play this!”

They gathered in a sunny patch. The air smelled like fresh dirt and flowers. A butterfly fluttered by. Rocco lifted the tube. “One, two, three, play!”

Clack! Clink! Boom! Honk!

The sounds crashed together. Mina squeaked too fast. Gus clicked too slow. Ellie tapped the can very loud. Rocco blew his tube hard. The noise felt big and messy. Rocco’s ears flattened. His chest felt tight. His tail drooped.

“This sounds wrong,” he said softly. His tummy felt twisty. His face felt hot. “I feel… frustrated.”

Mina held her bottle close. “I feel worried,” she whispered. “Am I doing it wrong?”

Gus stopped clicking. “I feel jumpy.”

Ellie tilted her head. “I feel a little loud,” she admitted, fluffing her feathers.

Rocco took a slow breath in, like smelling a flower. He blew out, like blowing a dandelion. In. Out. In. Out. “I feel frustrated,” he said again. “But I can calm down.” He put a paw on his chest. “My feelings are big, but they can change.”

The friends nodded and breathed with him. In. Out. In. Out. The backyard felt gentle again.

Rocco picked up the cardboard tube. “Let’s try a plan. We can practice. We can take turns. We can cooperate.”

“What does cooperate mean?” asked Mina.

“It means we work together,” said Rocco. “We listen to each other. We help each other.”

Ellie pecked the ground softly. “And we can use soft and loud voices on purpose.”

Gus clicked his caps quietly. “Practice time!”

Rocco smiled. “First, we set a beat. I will tap my tail, soft and steady.” He swished his tail: tap… tap… tap… tap. “Mina, match my beat with your bottle. Soft taps.”

Mina tapped gently. Tap… tap… tap… tap. Her eyes grew bright. “Oh! I can do it!”

“Great,” said Rocco. “Gus, click on every other tap.” Tap… click… tap… click.

Gus’s clicks sounded like tiny pebbles. “This feels good.”

“Ellie,” Rocco said, “you can be the drum at the end of each four taps.” Tap… tap… tap… tap—BOOM.

Ellie puffed with pride. “Boom!” she sang softly, and then laughed.

They tried again. The sounds came together like a friendly hug. Tap… tap… click… tap—BOOM. Tap… tap… click… tap—BOOM. Rocco lifted the tube and hummed a simple tune. It was smooth and sweet, like warm honey. The sun sparkled on the bottles. The can drum glowed. The caps clicked like little crickets.

But then a new feeling bubbled up in Rocco’s chest. He saw Ellie’s wings droop. Mina’s taps slowed. Gus’s clicking got shaky. Rocco stopped. “How do we feel?” he asked.

“I feel tired,” said Mina.

“I feel thirsty,” said Gus.

“I feel a little left out,” Ellie said softly. “I want a turn to lead.”

Rocco nodded. “Thanks for telling me. I feel proud that we shared our feelings. Let’s take a water break. Then we can take turns being the leader.”

They sipped cool water from a leaf cup. The water felt cold and nice. The breeze tickled their fur and feathers.

“Ellie, you lead first,” said Rocco. “Count us in.”

Ellie lifted her beak. “One, two, three!” She tapped a gentle new beat. The others listened and followed. Then Mina led with tiny taps. Then Gus led with click-clicks. Each time, they practiced, listened, and cooperated.

When it was Rocco’s turn again, he heard something special: his friends. Their sounds fit together like puzzle pieces. His heart felt light. His tail swished in a happy wave.

They finished with a big, soft “BOOM,” and the backyard became quiet. They smiled at each other.

Rocco touched the recyclables. “We turned these into instruments,” he said. “We can help the earth by reusing things.” He put the items back in the blue bin. “And we can help our hearts by naming our feelings.”

Mina squeaked, “When I feel worried, I can take a breath.”

Gus chirped, “When I feel jumpy, I can ask for a break.”

Ellie added, “When I feel left out, I can say so and ask for a turn.”

Rocco nodded. “When I feel frustrated, I can breathe, make a plan, and practice with my friends.”

They all did a happy wiggle. The sun felt warm on their faces. The band had found its song—together.

The End.
