# Brave Cub on the Rooftop Race

On a sunny morning, a friendly bear cub named Bibi climbed the stairs with a soft pat-pat. The steps were cool under her paws. She reached the rooftop and blinked in the bright light. The rooftop felt big and open. A gentle breeze brushed her fur. She could smell warm tar and fresh air. Clouds looked close, like fluffy pillows.

Bibi held a small red flag. “Today, I practice sports,” she told herself. “I can do it.” She felt her heart go thump-thump.

Lines of chalk made a tiny race track around pots of flowers. Yellow marigolds glowed. A kite-tail streamer flapped, tap-tap, against the wall. Bibi set the red flag in a cup. She placed three orange cones in a row. “Step one,” she said. “Set the cones. Step two, run around. Step three, cheer for me!”

Her friend, a sparrow named Pip, landed on the railing. “Chirp! Good morning, Bibi! What are you doing?”

“I’m having a rooftop race,” Bibi said. She wiggled her toes inside soft sneakers. “I want to get better. I want to feel brave.”

Pip tilted his head. “Chirp, chirp! I can count laps!” He puffed his tiny chest. “I will be the coach!”

Bibi smiled. Her tummy felt fluttery. “Okay,” she said. “I will try my best.”

“On your marks,” Pip chirped. “Ready… set… go!”

Bibi ran. Her paws pattered. The breeze kissed her nose. She went around cone one. She went around cone two. At cone three, she slipped a little. “Oof!” She wobbled and bumped a pot. The clay pot made a dull thunk, but did not fall.

“I’m not good,” Bibi whispered. She looked down. Her ears drooped.

Pip flapped beside her. “Chirp! Wait. Stop and think. What happened?”

Bibi took a deep breath. The air smelled like mint leaves from a small plant. “I went too fast at the turn,” she said. “My foot slid.”

Pip nodded. “Good thinking. What could you try?”

“I can slow at the turn,” Bibi said. “I can make small steps. Step, step, step.”

“Smart plan!” Pip chirped. “You are using your brain. That is skill-building!”

Bibi lifted her head. Her cheeks felt warm, but her eyes were bright. “Okay. I will try again.”

“Lap two!” Pip called.

Bibi ran. Pat-pat-pat. She reached cone one. “Small steps,” she told herself. She slowed a little. Step, step, step. Around cone two. She breathed in, out, in, out. Around cone three, she made tiny bear steps. “I did it!” she said, grinning.

“Chirp! Great job!” Pip sang.

Bibi felt a little spark in her chest. It felt like a sunbeam. “I can learn,” she said.

“Lap three!” Pip called.

Bibi ran again. The wind whooshed gently past her ears. At the last turn, a kite-string on the floor made a squiggly line. Bibi paused. “Hmm,” she said. “This is in the way.”

She pointed. “If I step on it, I may slip,” she said.

“What can we do?” Pip asked.

Bibi looked around. “We can move it,” she said. She picked up the string and wrapped it around a hook. She made a simple knot. “There,” she said. “Safe track.”

“Logical thinking!” Pip chirped. “You solved a problem.”

Bibi ran the last turn. Her paws felt steady. Her breath was puff-puff, but happy. She crossed her chalk finish line and waved her red flag. “I did my race!”

Pip spun in the air. “Chirp! Time for a medal,” he said. He plucked a yellow petal from a marigold on the ground and placed it on Bibi’s head like a tiny crown.

Bibi giggled. The petal felt soft and cool. “Thank you, Coach Pip,” she said.

“How do you feel?” asked Pip.

Bibi pressed a paw to her chest. “I feel proud,” she said. “I had a wobbly moment, but I kept trying. I used steps. I used my brain. I got better.”

The door creaked open. A gentle voice floated up from the stairs. It was Grandma Bear. “Bibi, snack time! I have cool water and sweet apple slices.”

Bibi’s tummy rumbled. “Yum!” She waved to Grandma. “I’ll be right there!”

Before she went, Bibi picked up the cones and stacked them. She brushed the chalk lines smooth with her paw. She counted aloud, “One, two, three,” as she put things away. The rooftop felt calm and neat.

Pip hopped to her shoulder. “Chirp! What did we learn today?” he asked.

Bibi smiled. “We learned I can try, think, and try again,” she said. “When I make a plan, I get stronger. When I slow down and use small steps, I stay safe. I am proud of me.”

Pip nodded. “That is self-esteem!” he chirped.

Bibi took a sip of cool water downstairs. It felt fresh, like tiny bells in her mouth. She crunched an apple slice. Crunch, crunch. She looked back toward the rooftop stairs and whispered, “Thank you, brave heart.”

Tomorrow, she would run again. She would make new plans. She would cheer for herself and others. But today, she hugged Pip and smiled big. She knew she could learn new skills, one careful step at a time.

The End.
