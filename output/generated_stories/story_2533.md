# Polly's Wellness Discovery

Polly the parrot loved her colorful feathers and bright yellow beak. She lived in a cozy living room filled with soft cushions, bouncy balls, and her favorite wooden perch by the sunny window.

One morning, Polly woke up feeling grumpy. Her beautiful green feathers looked dull, and she felt tired even though she had just slept all night.

"Squawk! I don't want to play today," Polly said to her friend Max the teddy bear, who sat quietly on the soft blue couch.

Max's button eyes seemed to look at her with concern. "What's wrong, Polly?" he seemed to say in her imagination.

Polly fluffed her feathers crossly. "I feel yucky and my tummy hurts a little. Nothing is fun anymore!"

She had been staying up late watching the pretty lights outside her window and eating too many sunflower seeds. She hadn't been drinking enough water or moving around much either.

Polly's owner, Emma, noticed something was different. "Oh Polly, you seem sad today. Let me help you feel better," Emma said gently, her voice warm and caring.

Emma sat down on the fluffy carpet next to Polly's perch. "I think I know what might help. Let's discover some wellness secrets together!"

First, Emma filled Polly's water bowl with fresh, cool water. "Drinking water helps our bodies feel strong and happy," she explained. The water sparkled in the morning sunlight streaming through the window.

Polly took a small sip. The cool water felt refreshing on her beak. "Mmm, that does taste good," she chirped softly.

Next, Emma opened the window to let in fresh air. A gentle breeze tickled Polly's feathers, and she could smell the sweet flowers from the garden outside.

"Fresh air helps us breathe better and feel more awake," Emma said, taking a deep breath. "Try it, Polly!"

Polly stretched her wings wide and took a big breath. The fresh air filled her lungs and made her feel a little more energetic.

Then Emma suggested they do some fun exercises together. "Let's dance and move our bodies!" she said, putting on cheerful music.

Emma wiggled her arms like wings, and Polly began to flap hers too. They hopped around the living room, stepping on the soft carpet and laughing together. Polly's heart started beating faster, and she felt more awake.

After their dance, Emma gave Polly some healthy apple slices instead of too many seeds. "Good food gives us energy to play and learn," she explained.

The crispy apple was sweet and juicy. Polly's tummy started feeling much better.

Finally, Emma helped Polly understand about good sleep. "When we go to bed early, our bodies can rest and grow strong for tomorrow," she said, making Polly's perch extra cozy with a soft blanket.

Polly realized she had made some mistakes. She looked at Emma with her bright black eyes. "I'm sorry I was grumpy this morning. I didn't take good care of myself."

"That's okay, Polly. Now you know how to feel better!" Emma smiled and gently petted Polly's now-shiny feathers.

From that day on, Polly remembered her wellness discoveries: drink water, breathe fresh air, move her body, eat healthy foods, and get good sleep. She felt happy, energetic, and ready for new adventures every day.

"Taking care of myself helps me take care of my friends too!" Polly squawked joyfully, her feathers gleaming in the afternoon sun.

The End.
