# Milo the Monkey Builds a Brave Breakfast

In a warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon and sunshine, a courageous monkey named Milo wiggled his toes on the soft rug. The morning light made golden squares on the floor. The kettle hummed a happy song, and the clock tick-tocked like a gentle drum.

Milo puffed out his little chest. “Today, I will make my own breakfast,” he said. “I am brave. I am careful. I can do it!”

His tail curled with excitement. He imagined himself wearing a chef’s hat. In his mind, he heard a crowd say, “Go, Chef Milo!” Imagination made him smile. It felt warm, like cocoa in a mug.

But when Milo looked around, he saw a tall shelf, a wobbly stack of bowls, and a big bag of oats. The spoon drawer squeaked. The milk carton felt heavy and cool.

Milo took a deep breath. He remembered: brave does not mean fast. Brave means safe and steady.

“Step one,” he said softly, holding up one fuzzy finger. “Wash hands.” He turned on the tap. The water was warm and whooshy. Soap made bubbles like tiny clouds. “Scrub, scrub, scrub,” he sang. The bubbles smelled like lemons and made his hands squeaky clean.

“Step two,” Milo said. “Get a bowl.” The bowls were high. Milo did not climb. He used his imagination to solve the problem. He pictured a sturdy helper: a small wooden stool. “I will build a safe step,” he said.

Milo pushed the little stool near the counter—push, push, scrape. He checked with his paws. “Sturdy,” he said. He climbed up slowly—one foot, then the other. The bowl felt smooth and cool. “I did it!” he cheered, tail swishing.

“Step three,” he said. “Oats!” The oatmeal bag was big and crinkly. “Crinkle, crinkle,” went the paper. Milo used two hands. He poured a little. “Oops!” A few oats sprinkled like soft rain on the counter.

“It’s okay,” Milo whispered. “I can fix little spills.” He brushed the oats back into the bowl with a tiny sweep. Problem, then solution. That felt good.

“Step four: Milk.” The fridge sighed when he opened it. Cool air kissed his nose. The milk carton was cold and heavy. Milo hugged it with both arms. “I am strong,” he said. He poured s-l-o-w-l-y. Glug, glug. The oats floated like little boats. He giggled.

“Step five: Mix.” Milo held a wooden spoon. Tap tap. Swirl, swirl. The oats made a cozy whisper. He imagined he was stirring a pond for ducks. “Quack-quack, round and back,” he sang.

Then Milo looked at the stove. It was shiny and hot. Heat shimmered like tiny waves. He stopped. He remembered the family rule: “Ask for help with hot things.”

“I can be brave and ask,” Milo said. He called, “Mama?”

Mama Monkey padded in with soft steps. She wore a red apron with yellow bananas. “Good morning, Chef Milo! I smell cinnamon and courage,” she said, smiling.

Milo pointed to the stove. “I built a safe step. I used both hands. I cleaned a spill. Now I need help with the hot part.”

Mama nodded. “That is smart independence. You did so much on your own, and you knew when to ask.” Her voice was warm like toast. “I will handle the heat. You can sprinkle and stir.”

The pot went on the stove—clink. The flame made a gentle whoosh. Milo watched carefully, standing behind the line of tape on the floor that Mama called the “safety line.”

“Now,” Mama said, “a pinch of cinnamon?”

Milo pinched the brown dust. It smelled like hugs. He sprinkled it into the pot. “Snow for my oatmeal boats,” he whispered.

They waited and stirred together. Bubble, bubble. The steam felt like a soft kiss on Milo’s cheeks. He imagined the bubbles were tiny volcanoes on a breakfast island. His eyes shone. “My imagination helps me think and build!” he said.

When the oatmeal was ready, Mama turned off the stove. The pot clicked to quiet. Milo carried his bowl to the table all by himself. He added banana coins—plop, plop, plop—and a drizzle of honey that looked like sunshine.

He tasted a spoonful. Warm. Sweet. Cozy. “Mmm,” he hummed. His tummy felt happy and calm. “I built this breakfast with my ideas and my careful paws.”

Mama sat beside him. “You solved problems step by step. You used your imagination to find ways, and you stayed safe and kind to yourself.”

Milo nodded. “When I imagine, I can see new paths. I can build a plan. I can do many things, and I can ask for help when I need it.”

After breakfast, Milo wiped the table with a soft cloth—swish, swish. He put the spoon in the sink and the oats back on the shelf with Mama’s help. The kitchen smelled like cinnamon and clean.

Milo placed his small stool back where it lived. He patted it. “Thank you, helper,” he said.

Mama hugged him. “Your brave heart and bright mind make a great team.”

Milo’s tail made a happy curl. He felt strong and calm. He felt proud. He whispered, “I am a builder. I am careful. I am kind. My imagination helps me grow.”

And in the warm, sunny kitchen, Milo the courageous monkey smiled at the golden squares on the floor. He saw a new picture in his mind: today’s brave breakfast, tomorrow’s big ideas. With imagination, he could build them, one safe step at a time.

The End.
