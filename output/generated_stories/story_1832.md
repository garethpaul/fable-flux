# Paco the Pantry Parrot

In a cozy kitchen pantry, jars clicked softly and spices made friendly smells. Cinnamon was sweet. Pepper was tickly. Bags of rice rustled like tiny rain. On the top shelf lived Paco, a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a curious beak.

“Peek! Peek! Pantry play!” Paco chirped. He loved words. He loved sounds. He loved making art with labels, lids, and safe, clean stickers that the family kept for crafts.

Paco had a big goal. “Goal: Make a Pantry Picture!” he said, tapping the air with his wing. “A picture of a rainbow bowl!”

He flapped carefully down to the middle shelf. He lined up round lids like colorful moons. He placed blue tape like a river. He stuck soft paper circles like puffy clouds. He hummed, “Tap-tap, stick-stick, swoosh!”

The pantry door creaked. “Eeee!” A little mouse named Miri peeked in. She wore a tiny ribbon. Her nose wiggled. “Hello, Paco,” she squeaked. “It smells like cinnamon hugs in here.”

“Hello, Miri!” Paco squawked happily. “Look! Pantry Picture!”

Miri clapped. “It is lovely. Can I help?”

Paco puffed with pride. “Yes! Team work. Careful, careful.”

Miri nodded. She wanted to join. She wanted to be fast. She darted like a quick crumb. “Zip!” Her tail swished. Her little paws bumped a stack of plastic cups. Clatter-clatter! The cups rolled into the blue tape river. The lids slid. The paper clouds floated down.

Paco’s feathers drooped. His picture was messy now. His heart felt hot and tight. “Oh!” he cried. “My picture!”

Miri froze. Her eyes were wide and shiny. “I’m sorry,” she whispered. “I went too fast. I made a mess.”

The pantry was quiet. The tickly pepper smell felt too big. Paco’s wings trembled. He wanted to squawk loud.

He took a big, slow breath. In…and out. The cinnamon hug smell came back. Paco remembered a word he loved: peace. He remembered his goal: Make a picture and have fun.

He put a wing on his chest. “I feel upset,” he said, using calm words. “My picture fell. I worked hard.”

Miri nodded. “I hear you,” she said softly. “You feel upset. I made a mistake. I’m sorry.”

Paco blinked. The word “sorry” floated like a gentle balloon. He tasted the soft word on his tongue. “Sor-ry,” he said, slow and kind. He liked how the sounds rolled. He loved language. He loved learning new words and sharing them.

He looked at Miri. She looked small and sad. Paco remembered another word he loved: forgive.

He said it out loud, warm and clear. “I can forgive you.”

Miri’s ears lifted. “You can?”

“Yes,” said Paco. “You did not mean to break it. You want to help. We can fix it together.”

Miri’s whiskers wiggled with hope. “Thank you. How can I help?”

Paco tapped his beak and made a plan. “New goal: Fix the picture. New steps: Slow. Talk. Count.”

He pointed with a feather. “We will count to three before we move. We will say what we will do. We will be gentle.”

“Okay,” said Miri. “Count and talk.”

Paco smiled. “Words help. Words are friends.”

They began.

“Step one,” Paco said. “Let’s pick up lids. One…two…three.” He lifted a red lid. Miri lifted a green one. Clink, clink. The sound was friendly now.

“Step two,” said Miri. “Let’s make the blue river smooth.” She touched the tape with careful paws. “I will move slow.”

“Good words,” Paco chirped. “Slow and steady.” Swoosh-smooth went the tape, like a quiet stream.

“Step three,” Paco said. “Clouds up high.” They placed the soft paper circles. Fluff, fluff. The pantry smelled like paper and spice and peace.

They talked as they worked.

“I feel calm,” said Miri.

“I feel proud,” said Paco. “Thank you for helping. Thank you for saying sorry.”

“Thank you for forgiving me,” said Miri. Her eyes were bright again.

Paco fluffed his feathers. “Forgive is a strong word. It makes space in the heart.”

Miri giggled. “Forgive makes room for friends.”

At last, the picture was even better. The lids made a rainbow bowl: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple. The blue tape river curved into the bowl. The clouds floated above. It was bright and happy.

“Ta-da!” Paco sang. “Pantry Picture!”

They both clapped. The claps sounded like tiny drums against the jars.

The pantry door creaked again. The warm kitchen light peeked in. Paco and Miri stood side by side, smiling.

Paco said, “We met our goal. We worked together. We used gentle words. We forgave.”

Miri nodded. “We moved slow. We counted. We talked. We fixed the problem.”

Paco turned to the jars and the spices like an audience. “Friends,” he chirped, “if someone makes a mistake, we can pause. We can use our words. We can listen. We can forgive. Then our hearts feel light, like paper clouds.”

The cinnamon seemed to smile. The pepper tickled just a little.

Miri took a tiny bow. “I will remember to slow down. I will say what I will do.”

Paco took a big bow. “And I will remember to breathe and speak kindly.”

They looked at their rainbow bowl and felt warm and bright inside. They had learned together. They had made art together. They had found peace together.

The End.
