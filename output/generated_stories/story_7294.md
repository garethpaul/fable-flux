# Leo the Lion Cub and the Whirling Laundry

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright, curious eyes. One rainy day, he padded into the laundry room. The room smelled like clean soap and warm towels. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer made a gentle, “Hum, hum.”

“Ooh,” Leo said, nose twitching. “This room feels warm and safe.”

He peeked into a basket full of socks, tiny shirts, and a blue towel. He imagined the towel was a magic cape. “I am Captain Calm!” he roared softly, tying it around his neck. “I help bodies and feelings feel good.”

But then the washing machine made a loud, “THUMP-THUMP!” Leo’s ears went back. His heart beat fast. “Oh! That sound was big,” he whispered.

Leo took a slow breath in through his nose. He remembered what Mama Lion taught him. “When things feel big, we can make our bodies feel small and calm,” she had said.

Leo pressed his paws to his chest. “In… two… three. Out… two… three.” The soapy smell tickled his nose. The warm air from the dryer brushed his whiskers. The thumping slowed down, and so did his heart.

Just then, a sock popped out of the basket and bounced on the floor. It was a tiny striped sock with a little lion face stitched on it. “Hey!” the sock squeaked. “I’m Soxy! I don’t like loud noises.”

“Me too,” said a fuzzy mitten, peeking out. “I’m Mitty. When it’s too loud, I get grumpy.”

Leo sat down on the soft rug. “Let’s talk,” he said. “We can use our words. We can be kind.”

Another THUMP rolled through the room. Soxy curled up. “I want the noise to stop!”

Mitty crossed her fuzzy arms. “I want my turn in the dryer now.”

Leo blinked. This was a problem. The laundry room was busy. The machines had jobs to do. Soxy wanted quiet. Mitty wanted a turn. Leo wanted everyone to feel okay inside their bodies. He put his paw on his chest again. “Captain Calm can help,” he whispered.

He looked at the machines. The washer door had a shiny window. The dryer had a soft, warm glow. Leo imagined they were big gentle giants. “Hello, Washer and Dryer,” he said. “We need a plan.”

The washer went, “Whirr?” The dryer hummed, “Hmmmm.”

Leo nodded. “First, we take care of our wellness. That means helping our bodies feel good. Let’s all practice calm breaths together.”

He showed Soxy and Mitty how to breathe in like smelling a flower and out like blowing a bubble. “Smell the flower,” Leo said, sniffing the soapy air. “Blow the bubble,” he whispered, puffing softly.

Soxy took a tiny breath in. “Smell the flower.” Mitty blew out. “Blow the bubble.” The room felt softer, like a warm towel hug.

“Next,” Leo said, “we practice patience. Machines take time. Bodies need time too. We can make a cozy waiting game.”

“What game?” asked Mitty.

“Imagination game!” Leo grinned. He pulled the blue towel-cape around all three of them. “This towel is a calm cave. While we wait, we can listen for quiet sounds.”

They sat together. The washer hummed. The dryer whispered. Drops of water went, “Plip, plip.” Leo said, “I hear the plip-plips. What do you hear?”

“I hear a tiny tick-tock,” Soxy said, pointing with a stripe. “That’s the clock!”

“I hear Leo’s heartbeat,” Mitty said. “It sounds steady.” She smiled. “That makes me feel safe.”

After a while, the THUMP came back. Soxy flinched. Leo put out a paw. “Let’s use negotiation. That means we talk and make a plan so everyone gets a turn.”

He turned to the machines. “Washer, could you pause for a little stretch break so Soxy can rest?”

The washer blinked its light and slowed to a stop. “Whirr… click.”

Leo smiled. “Thank you, Washer. Soxy, let’s take three calm steps together.” They stood and stepped: “Step… step… step.” With each step, they breathed. The room felt peaceful again.

Mitty tugged Leo’s cape. “Can it be my turn in the dryer now?”

Leo nodded. “Yes. After we put the wet clothes in, we set a timer. When it goes ding, it’s someone else’s turn.” He looked at Soxy. “Is that okay?”

Soxy wiggled. “Yes, if we keep breathing and have the calm cave.”

Leo helped scoop the wet clothes. The smell of clean rain filled the room. He pressed the big round button. The dryer started, “Hum, hum,” like a purring cat. Leo set the timer. “Ding in five,” he said.

They waited in the calm cave, listening. Mitty smiled as the warm air puffed. “I feel cozy.”

Soxy swayed side to side. “I feel brave.”

When the timer went ding, Leo clapped his paws. “We did it! We used calm breaths, we practiced waiting, and we made a plan together!”

The washer beeped. The dryer clicked. The laundry was done. Leo folded a tiny towel—well, he tried. It looked like a wrinkly cloud. He giggled. “Folding takes practice,” he said. “I can try again.”

Mitty patted him. “Practice makes strong.”

Soxy cheered. “And calm makes brave!”

Leo wrapped them both in the blue towel and gave a soft lion cub roar. “Today I learned: Wellness means caring for our bodies and feelings. We can breathe, ask for what we need, and share with others. That’s how everyone feels okay.”

He looked around the warm laundry room. It smelled sweet. It sounded gentle. It felt safe.

“Captain Calm,” Leo said, “mission complete.”

The End.
