# Toby's Treehouse Clean-Up

High up in the big oak tree, Toby the tortoise lived in the most wonderful treehouse. It had round windows that let in golden sunlight and a cozy reading corner filled with soft cushions. Toby loved his treehouse very much.

One sunny morning, Toby woke up and stretched his green shell. "What a beautiful day!" he said, looking out his window. But as he walked around his treehouse, he noticed something that made him frown.

"Oh my," Toby said sadly. "My treehouse is very messy!"

There were empty juice boxes on the floor, crumpled papers everywhere, and old banana peels in the corner. The treehouse smelled yucky, and flies buzzed around the trash.

Toby's friend Bella the bunny hopped up the tree ladder to visit. "Toby!" she called. "Are you home?"

"Come in, Bella," Toby said quietly. He felt embarrassed about his messy home.

Bella wrinkled her pink nose when she came inside. "Toby, what happened here? It doesn't smell very nice."

Toby looked down at his feet. "I've been throwing my trash on the floor instead of putting it in the trash can. I was too lazy to clean up, and now my beautiful treehouse is dirty and smelly."

"Oh, Toby," Bella said gently. "But you can fix this! You're the wisest tortoise I know."

Toby thought for a moment. His grandmother had always told him, "Take care of your home, and it will take care of you." He knew what he had to do.

"You're right, Bella. I need to clean up and take better care of my treehouse. Will you help me learn how to do it properly?"

"Of course!" Bella said, clapping her paws together.

First, they picked up all the trash. Toby carefully sorted everything into different piles. "The banana peels can go in my compost bin," he said. "They'll help my garden grow!"

"And these juice boxes can be recycled," Bella added, holding up the empty containers. "They can be made into new things!"

They put the paper in the recycling bin too. Soon, the treehouse floor was clean and shiny again.

"Now," said Toby, "I want to make sure this never happens again. I'll put a small trash can in every room, and I'll empty them every day."

Bella helped Toby make colorful signs that said "COMPOST," "RECYCLE," and "TRASH." They hung them above three different bins.

"This way," Toby explained, "I'll always know where everything goes. And I'll be helping the Earth too!"

They opened all the windows to let in fresh air. The treehouse began to smell like flowers and sunshine again.

"Toby," Bella said, "you've learned something very important today."

"Yes," Toby nodded wisely. "I learned that taking care of my home and the Earth is my responsibility. When I put trash where it belongs, I keep my home clean and help the planet stay healthy too."

From that day on, Toby kept his treehouse spotless. He composted his food scraps, recycled his containers, and always put trash in the right place. His treehouse became the most beautiful and sweet-smelling home in the whole forest.

And whenever young animals visited, Toby would teach them about taking care of their homes and the Earth, just like Bella had helped him learn.

The End.
