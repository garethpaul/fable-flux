# Bounce's Basketball Day

Bounce the kangaroo joey loved his mama's balcony garden. The colorful flowers smelled sweet, and the green leaves tickled his nose when the wind blew. Today, something new caught his eye - a bright orange basketball sitting by the tomato plants.

"Mama, what's this round thing?" Bounce asked, poking the ball with his tiny paw.

"That's a basketball, little one," Mama Kangaroo said, watering the sunflowers. "It belongs to the children next door. The wind must have blown it over here."

Bounce's eyes grew wide with excitement. He had seen the neighbor children bouncing balls before. *Bounce, bounce, bounce!* It looked like so much fun!

"Can I try?" Bounce asked, his tail wiggling with joy.

"Of course," Mama smiled. "But be gentle with the plants."

Bounce tried to dribble like he had seen the children do. *Thump!* The ball bounced too hard and knocked over a small pot of herbs. Dirt scattered across the wooden deck.

"Oh no!" Bounce's ears drooped. "I made a mess!"

Just then, he heard voices from the next balcony. "Hey, have you seen our basketball?" called a girl's voice.

Bounce felt his tummy flutter. He wanted to keep playing, but he knew the ball wasn't his. He looked at Mama, who gave him an encouraging nod.

"Um, hello!" Bounce called out bravely. "I think your ball is here!"

A boy and girl appeared at the railing between the balconies. "Oh, there it is!" the girl said. "I'm Maya, and this is my brother Jake."

"I'm Bounce!" the joey said, his confidence growing. "I was trying to play basketball like you, but I knocked over Mama's herbs. I'm sorry about your ball being over here."

"That's okay!" Jake said. "We're still learning too. Want us to show you how?"

Bounce's heart jumped with happiness. "Really? Yes, please!"

Maya demonstrated how to gently bounce the ball. "Start small," she said. "Like this - *pat, pat, pat* - not too hard."

Bounce tried again, this time much gentler. *Pat, pat, pat!* The ball stayed low and didn't disturb any plants.

"I did it!" Bounce cheered, hopping up and down.

"Great job!" Jake called. "You're a natural hopper!"

For the next hour, the three friends practiced together across their balconies. Bounce learned to dribble softly, and Maya and Jake learned some of Bounce's special kangaroo moves.

When it was time to return the ball, Bounce carefully tossed it back to his new friends.

"Thank you for teaching me," Bounce said. "And thank you for being patient when I made mistakes."

"Thank you for being honest about finding our ball," Maya replied. "Want to play again tomorrow?"

"Yes!" Bounce exclaimed.

That evening, as Mama tucked him into his cozy pouch, Bounce felt proud and grateful.

"Mama, I learned that sports are more fun when you share them with friends," he said sleepily.

"And what else did you learn?" Mama asked gently.

"That talking to people - even when you're nervous - can lead to wonderful friendships. And saying 'thank you' makes everyone feel good."

Mama kissed his forehead. "Those are very important lessons, my little athlete."

As Bounce drifted off to sleep, he could hardly wait for tomorrow's basketball practice with his new friends.

The End.
