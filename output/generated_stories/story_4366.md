# Koala Kiki’s Quiet Closet

Kiki was a calm koala with soft, silver fur and twinkly eyes. She loved hugs, eucalyptus leaves, and quiet places. Her favorite spot was the closet hideaway, a tiny nook with a soft rug, twinkle lights, and a basket of pillows. It smelled like clean cotton and a little like eucalyptus tea. When Kiki sat there, her heart felt slow and steady, like a gentle drum. Thum, thum. Thum, thum.

One sunny morning, Kiki whispered, “I’m ready for a calm day.” She patted the rug and took a deep breath in… and out. Then she heard footsteps.

“Is anyone home?” chirped Poppy the parrot, fluttering in with bright green feathers. “I want to play pirates!”

“Hello, Poppy,” Kiki said softly. “I like pirates. But I’m in my quiet closet right now.”

Poppy peeked in. “Can I come in too? I will squawk softly. Squa—” Poppy lowered her voice. “Squawk.”

Kiki smiled. “Yes, you can come if we use calm voices. This is a calm space.”

Poppy nodded and folded her wings. The twinkle lights glowed. The rug felt cozy under their toes and talons.

Soon, Taha the tiger cub padded down the hall. “Grrr-eat news! I found a bouncy ball!” he roared, the sound booming like thunder.

Kiki put a paw on her chest. “I need calm,” she said. “Please use a quiet voice in the closet.”

Taha blinked and took a breath. “Okay,” he whispered. “I can try.” He set the bouncy ball outside the door and tiptoed in. “This place smells nice,” he said.

Kiki nodded. “It smells like clean and tea. This is my hideaway. We can share if we are calm.”

They all sat. Poppy hummed a tiny tune. Taha curled his tail. Kiki closed her eyes and counted clouds in her mind: one, two, three.

Then the door creaked. “Hello!” sang Mina the mouse, wearing a tiny backpack with stickers from all over the world—stars from the night sky, a maple leaf, a little globe with blue oceans. “I have stories from far-away places!”

Kiki opened her eyes. “Welcome, Mina. We are using calm voices. We also ask before we sit close.”

Mina nodded. “May I sit next to you?”

“Yes,” said Kiki. “Thank you for asking. That is my boundary. I like to know before someone is close.”

Mina smiled and sat on a pillow. “In some places, people greet with a hug. In others, they wave. It’s different, and that is okay.”

“I like that,” said Kiki. “We can accept differences and still be fair.”

They began a gentle game. Poppy told a soft pirate tale. Taha rolled the ball slowly with his paw, back and forth at the door. Mina shared crunchy rice crackers, one for each friend.

“Fair shares,” said Taha.

“Fair and kind,” said Kiki. “Thank you.”

Suddenly, Zuzu the zebra burst in. “Tag! You’re it!” Zuzu tapped Kiki and giggled, her hooves clacking on the floor.

Kiki’s ears twitched. Her calm felt wobbly, like jelly. She took a deep breath. In… and out. “Zuzu,” she said gently, “this is my quiet closet. I need calm here.”

Zuzu froze, her stripes zigging with surprise. “Oh! I didn’t know. I wanted to play fast.”

“It’s okay to like fast,” said Kiki. “But in this space, we go slow. That is my boundary.”

“How do I do calm?” asked Zuzu.

Kiki’s eyes twinkled. “Let’s try the Koala Calm: smell a flower, blow a feather.”

They all pretended to hold flowers. “Smell… mmm.” Then they pretended to blow a tiny feather. “Foooo.” The air felt cool. Their shoulders sank down. Their faces softened.

Zuzu’s voice dropped to a hush. “That feels nice.”

Poppy whispered, “Squa—k.” She giggled and covered her beak.

Mina unzipped her backpack and showed a small fabric map. “Look,” she said, pointing at tiny pictures. “Some families live where it’s very loud outside. Some live where it’s very quiet. People have different needs.”

Kiki nodded. “We can be fair and accept each other. We can ask what someone needs.”

Taha raised his paw. “Kiki, what do you need?”

Kiki smiled. “I need calm and space to think. I also like to share when friends ask first.”

Zuzu asked, “May I sit by you?”

“Yes,” said Kiki. “Thank you for asking.”

They listened to the soft buzz of the twinkle lights. The closet felt warm and safe. Poppy whispered a lullaby from the rainforest. Taha rolled the ball one last slow roll and then set it still. Mina passed the last cracker around. Zuzu traced a stripe on her leg and smiled.

Kiki said, “We can play fast outside later. In here, we choose calm.”

“Fair,” said Taha.

“Accepting,” said Mina.

“Calm as can be,” chirped Poppy.

“Boundaries respected,” said Zuzu, proud.

Kiki breathed in the cotton-clean air, and out with a happy sigh. Her heart drum was steady again. Thum, thum. Thum, thum.

“Today,” Kiki said, “we learned that calm helps our bodies, asking before we sit close shows respect, and fair shares make friends feel good. People in the big, wide world like different things, and that’s okay. We can listen, ask, and be kind.”

Everyone nodded. The closet hideaway glowed with quiet joy. They hugged if they wanted, and waved if they preferred. Then they tiptoed out to play in the bright day, ready to be calm when needed and fast when fair.

The End.
