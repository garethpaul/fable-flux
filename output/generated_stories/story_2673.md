# Nutmeg Plans the Big Click

Nutmeg was a small, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail. Nutmeg lived in a cozy home office with a big window, a humming computer, and a neat little desk just Nutmeg’s size. The desk smelled like pencils and paper. The keyboard felt bumpy and clicky under tiny paws. The chair was soft and spinny—whee!

Today was a big day. Nutmeg wanted to make a very special video for friends. Nutmeg would teach how to draw a star. Stars were Nutmeg’s favorite. “I will click the red record button,” Nutmeg said. “I will show the steps. I can do it!”

Nutmeg hopped into the spinny chair and looked around. The floor had a jumble of crayons. A cup of markers lay on its side. A piece of paper was crumpled by the trash can. The camera was not on the little stand. The computer screen was sleepy and dark.

Nutmeg’s tail twitched. “Oh,” said Nutmeg softly. “I just want to start now.” Nutmeg poked the keyboard. Tap, tap. The screen stayed dark.

Nutmeg felt a bubble of frustration rise up, like a hiccup. The bubble felt tight. “I want it to work now,” Nutmeg whispered. A little frown crept in.

Nutmeg took a slow breath. In, out. The chair made a tiny squeak. “When I feel a big feeling, I can pause,” Nutmeg said. “I can prepare.”

Nutmeg looked at the room again. “Preparation means getting ready before I start,” Nutmeg told the friendly plant by the window. The plant did not answer, but it looked very green.

“Step one,” said Nutmeg. “Make a plan.” Nutmeg opened a small notebook and drew simple boxes:

- Turn on the computer
- Put the camera on the stand
- Pick paper that is not wrinkly
- Sharpen pencils
- Test the sound
- Practice the steps

Nutmeg smiled. “I can do it, little by little.”

First, Nutmeg pressed the silver button. The computer woke up with a soft whirrrr. The screen glowed like a morning sky. “Hello!” chirped the computer.

Next, Nutmeg found the camera under a scarf. “Aha!” said Nutmeg. The camera felt cool and smooth. Nutmeg clicked it onto the small stand. Click! It stood tall and steady.

Nutmeg picked up the cup of markers and set them upright. Tap, tap, tap. The crayons went back into their box in a neat rainbow—red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple. The box smelled waxy and sweet. “All cozy,” said Nutmeg.

A soft pencil was dull, so Nutmeg used the sharpener. Scritch, scritch. Little curls of wood fell into a cup. Nutmeg listened to the tiny sound and smiled. “Sharp and ready!”

Nutmeg chose a new sheet of smooth paper. It felt cool and crisp. “Perfect.”

“Now test the sound,” said Nutmeg. Nutmeg clapped tiny paws. Clap, clap! The camera blinked a green light. On the screen, a bar jumped up and down. “It hears me!” Nutmeg laughed. “Hello, friends!” Nutmeg’s voice was bright and clear.

Nutmeg took another slow breath. “Now I will practice the steps,” Nutmeg said. “Practice is part of preparation.”

Nutmeg drew a star. “Step one: draw a little line down. Step two: draw a little line up. Step three: cross. Step four: cross again. Step five: close the star.” The pencil made a soft shhh sound on the paper.

But the star looked a little wobbly. Nutmeg’s ears drooped. “Oh no. It is not like I hoped,” Nutmeg said. The frustration bubble came back. It felt tight again.

Nutmeg placed a paw on the table and counted to three. “One… two… three.” Nutmeg took a sip of cool water. “I can try again,” Nutmeg whispered. “When I feel stuck, I can pause, breathe, and go step by step.”

Nutmeg practiced again. Shhh, shhh. The lines were smoother. Nutmeg practiced one more time. Shhh, shhh. The star looked bright and pointy. “I did it,” Nutmeg cheered. The tight bubble went away. Nutmeg felt warm and proud inside, like sunshine.

“Now I am ready to record,” said Nutmeg. “I prepared my tools. I practiced my steps. I calmed my big feelings.”

Nutmeg pressed the red record button. Beep! “Hello, friends,” said Nutmeg with a happy smile. “Today I will show you how to draw a star. First, we make a little line down…”

Nutmeg spoke slowly. The camera light blinked. The pencil made soft shhh sounds. The paper was neat. The markers waited. Nutmeg’s tail swished in a happy rhythm.

When the video was done, Nutmeg pressed stop. “Beep.” Nutmeg watched the video. The sound was clear. The picture was bright. The star was lovely. Nutmeg clapped. “It worked!”

Nutmeg cleaned up the desk, put the cap on the markers, and stacked the paper. Everything had a place, and everything went to its place. The room felt calm. The chair did a small, quiet spin.

Nutmeg looked at the little notebook and added a new box: “Share the video.” Nutmeg checked the box with a tiny star. “Next time, I will use my plan again,” Nutmeg said.

Nutmeg stood by the window and felt the warm sun on whiskers. The home office hummed softly. Nutmeg whispered, “Preparation helps me do my best. When I feel frustrated, I can pause, breathe, and try again. I can take initiative and get ready.”

Nutmeg’s bright eyes sparkled. “I am a resourceful squirrel,” Nutmeg said, and smiled.

The End.
