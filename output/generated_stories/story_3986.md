# Ellie's Big Bathroom Problem

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved bath time. She loved the warm, bubbly water and the way her gray skin sparkled when it was clean. But today, something was very wrong in the bathroom.

"Oh no!" Ellie gasped, her trunk wiggling with worry. The bathtub was completely full of colorful toys belonging to her forest friends. There were Rabbit's rubber carrots, Bear's wooden boats, and Monkey's bouncy balls floating everywhere.

Ellie's mama had told her it was time for her bath, but how could she fit in the tub with all these toys? She felt tears starting to form in her big, kind eyes.

"I need my bath," Ellie said to herself, "but these aren't my toys. What should I do?"

Just then, Rabbit hopped into the bathroom. "Hi Ellie! Are you going to play with our toys?" Rabbit asked, his whiskers twitching excitedly.

Ellie took a deep breath and remembered what her mama taught her about talking through problems. "Rabbit," she said gently, "I understand you want to play, but I really need to take my bath now. Could we find a way for both of us to be happy?"

Rabbit's ears drooped a little. "But we were having so much fun! Bear and Monkey are coming back soon."

Ellie's trunk touched her chin as she thought hard. Then her eyes lit up with an idea! "What if we work together?" she suggested. "I could help you move the toys to a special play area, and then after my bath, I could help you set up an even better game?"

"Really?" Rabbit perked up. "You'd help us play?"

"Of course!" Ellie smiled. "We could make it even more fun together."

Soon Bear and Monkey arrived. At first, they looked sad about moving their toys. But Ellie spoke kindly to each of them.

"Bear, what if we move your boats to the sink? They could sail in their own special harbor," Ellie suggested, her voice warm and friendly.

Bear's eyes sparkled. "That sounds amazing! My boats would love their own harbor!"

"And Monkey," Ellie continued, "your bouncy balls could have more room to bounce in the hallway. We could even set up a bouncing course!"

Monkey clapped his hands. "Yes! A bouncing course would be the best!"

Working together, all the friends carefully moved the toys. Ellie used her strong trunk to lift the heavy boats, while her friends carried their special treasures. The bathroom filled with the happy sounds of cooperation - splashing water, giggling friends, and excited chatter.

"Thank you, Ellie!" said Rabbit. "You helped us find an even better way to play!"

"And you listened to what we wanted too," added Bear.

As Ellie finally slipped into her warm, clean bath, she felt proud and happy. The water felt wonderful on her skin, and she could hear her friends playing joyfully in the hallway.

"Talking together and sharing ideas made everyone happy," Ellie thought, blowing bubbles with her trunk. "When we listen to each other and work as a team, we can solve any problem!"

From that day on, whenever the friends had a disagreement, they remembered Ellie's gentle way of talking through problems. They learned that the best solutions come when everyone works together with kindness and creativity.

The End.
