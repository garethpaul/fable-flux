# Nuts for Everyone!

Sunny the squirrel lived in a cozy treehouse high in a tall oak. The floor was smooth wood. The walls smelled like fresh bark. A small window let in warm, golden light. Sunny liked to plan. Sunny had a little map, a tiny pencil, and a nut basket with neat labels.

One bright morning, Sunny woke up and stretched. “Today is Sharing Day,” Sunny said, tail twitching with excitement. “Friends from near and far are coming to the treehouse!”

Sunny looked at the map. “I will plan so everyone feels welcome,” Sunny said softly. Sunny drew a big circle with the pencil. Inside the circle, Sunny wrote three words out loud, saying each one slowly: “Plan. Fair. Share.”

“First, plan,” Sunny said. Sunny counted nuts. “One, two, three, four...” The nuts were different and colorful. Brown acorns, shiny chestnuts, and stripey pine nuts filled the basket. They smelled nutty and sweet.

“Second, fair,” Sunny said. “Everyone should get a nut they like.”

“Third, share,” Sunny said, smiling.

Knock knock! The treehouse door thumped gently. Sunny opened it. Friends smiled in the sunlight.

There was Mei the mouse, wearing a tiny blue scarf. There was Ayo the robin, red chest puffed with pride. There was Leila the ladybug with shiny red wings. There was Rafi the raccoon, paws clean and ready to help. And there was Timo the turtle, slow and steady, with kind eyes.

“Welcome, friends!” Sunny chirped. “Come in! You are safe and welcome here.”

The friends stepped in. The floor felt warm. The air smelled like apples and leaves. From the window, they heard wind whispering, “Whoooosh.” The treehouse creaked softly, like a happy song.

Mei asked, “What is Sharing Day?”

Sunny’s eyes sparkled. “It is a day to practice being good friends to the whole world,” Sunny said. “That is called global citizenship. It means we care about people and animals from many places. We listen. We share. We are fair.”

Ayo tilted his head. “How do we do that?”

Sunny pointed to the circle on the map. “Plan. Fair. Share.”

“First, we plan what we need,” Sunny said. “We need snacks, water, and a cozy place to sit.”

“I can bring water!” said Rafi. He padded to the little jug and poured carefully into tiny cups. Drip, drip, drip.

“I can make seats,” said Leila. She found soft leaves. “Sit here, please,” she said, patting them gently.

“I can count cups,” said Timo. “One, two, three, four, five.” His voice was calm and slow.

“Thank you,” said Sunny. “We are a team.”

Sunny set the nut basket on the table. “Next is fairness,” Sunny said. “We all like different nuts. Let’s listen.”

Mei sniffed. “I like small, crunchy acorns.”

Ayo chirped, “I like shiny chestnuts!”

Leila said, “I like tiny pine nuts.”

Rafi said, “I like any nut, but I want to make sure everyone has some first.”

Timo smiled. “I will wait. I am patient.”

Sunny nodded. “We will each take one nut we like. Then we will see what is left. That is fair.”

Sunny clapped softly. “Ready?”

Each friend took one favorite nut. Crunch, crunch went Mei. Peck, peck went Ayo. Leila nibbled, and Rafi smiled but held his nut. Timo hummed a happy tune while he waited.

Sunny looked in the basket. “We still have many nuts,” Sunny said. “Now we can share more.”

Then a little gust of wind blew in. The window rattled. Two tiny chipmunks peeped at the door. They looked shy and a bit hungry.

Sunny opened the door wide. “Hello! You are welcome,” Sunny said gently.

The chipmunks whispered, “We came from far away. We are new here.”

Sunny’s heart felt warm. “You are part of our world,” Sunny said. “We care for neighbors near and far.”

Mei scooted over to make space. “Sit with us,” she said.

Ayo poured two more cups of water. Drip, drip.

Leila gave each chipmunk a soft leaf seat. Rafi split his nut in half and smiled. “Here,” he said. “I can wait a little more.”

Sunny thought, then spoke. “We had a plan. Now we will make a new plan so everyone has enough. That is good planning, too.”

Sunny drew a new circle on the map. “First round: everyone gets one nut. Second round: we take turns. If someone is still hungry, we share again.”

Timo nodded. “That is fair.”

Together, they followed the plan. Crunch, peck, nibble, munch. The room filled with sounds and happy faces. The nuts smelled toasty. Water tasted cool and fresh.

When the basket was almost empty, Sunny said, “Let’s save a few for tomorrow. Planning for the future helps everyone.”

The chipmunks smiled. “Thank you for being fair and kind,” they said.

Sunny looked around the cozy treehouse. Friends from different places sat together, listened, and helped. Sunny’s tail swished with joy.

“Today we were good global citizens,” Sunny said. “We planned. We were fair. We shared with friends, old and new.”

Everyone cheered, “Hooray!”

Sunny added, “When we care for others, near and far, the world feels like one big treehouse.”

They all laughed, and the tree outside hummed in the soft wind, “Whoooosh.”

The End.
