# Ollie Otter’s Study Corner Craft

Sunlight slid across the floor, warm and soft like a cozy blanket. In a quiet study corner, a little lamp glowed. Paper rustled. Colored pencils lay in a small cup. Scissors, glue, and a clock with a friendly tick-tock waited on the desk.

A playful otter named Ollie popped his whiskery face over the chair. “Sploosh! Today I make a time poster!” he giggled, wiggling his smooth tail. He loved to splash and play, but today was special. He wanted to learn how to plan his time.

His friend Pip, a tiny mouse with bright eyes, peeked from a little basket. “Hi, Ollie! What’s the plan?” Pip squeaked, twitching his nose. The air smelled like crayons and fresh paper.

Ollie lifted a big sheet of paper. “I will craft a plan to read, to draw, and to clean up,” he said. “Tick-tock clock will help!” The clock went tick… tock… in a calm, steady voice.

Pip clapped. “Let’s make it together!”

Ollie drew three boxes. He labeled them with big, round letters: Read, Craft, Clean. His paws were a bit wiggly, and the letters leaned and bumped. “Oops,” Ollie sighed. “My letters are bumpy.”

Pip smiled. “Bumpy is okay. We can accept bumpy letters. They are yours.” He touched the soft paper. “Feel it? Smooth and strong.”

Ollie stroked the paper. “Smooth,” he said, smiling. “I accept my letters. They are otter letters!”

They set the timer on the clock. “Ten minutes to read,” Pip said. Tick-tock, tick-tock. Ollie opened a book. The pages smelled like dust and sunshine. He traced the pictures with his paw. “A boat! A moon!” he whispered.

But then Ollie spotted the glitter. It shimmered like tiny stars in a jar. “Ooooh, shiny!” He reached for it.

Pip squeaked, “Wait! Reading time first. Craft time next. That’s our plan.”

Ollie puffed his cheeks. “I want glitter now.” His tail thumped the chair. The clock kept tick-tock calm. Ollie looked at the boxes he drew. He took a deep breath and listened to the steady ticking. “I can try,” he said. “First read. Then glitter.”

They cuddled into the chair. Ollie read one page, then two. He sounded out a few small words. Pip pointed and nodded. “Good try. You’re growing,” Pip said.

Ding! The clock chimed softly. “Reading time is done,” Ollie said proudly. “Now craft time!”

He squeezed glue. It smelled sweet and sticky. He sprinkled glitter. It fell like slow silver rain. “Oooh,” Ollie murmured. He cut shapes with careful snips. Snip, snip. The scissors clicked. His paws wobbled, and a star came out lopsided.

“My star is not even,” he frowned.

Pip patted his paw. “Let’s accept the lopsided star. It is your star. It is trying star. We can make another, too.”

Ollie blinked. His whiskers twitched. “I can learn,” he said. He held the paper steady. Snip, snip. The next star was a little straighter. “Look! Better!” He felt warm and tall inside.

Ding! The clock chimed again. “Clean time,” Pip said.

Ollie looked at the shiny glitter on the floor. He made a little face. “Cleaning is not my favorite,” he whispered.

Pip nodded. “Not favorite is okay. We can accept feelings. We can still clean.”

Ollie took a deep breath. He sang a tiny song. “Sweep, sweep, sweep, glitter to the heap!” The broom made hush-hush sounds. The desk smelled like lemon when they wiped it. The cup of pencils clicked cheerfully as they went back in order.

“All clean!” Ollie said. His fur felt fluffy with pride.

He glued his time poster to the wall in the study corner. It sparkled with stars, both lopsided and neat. The boxes said Read, Craft, Clean. “This is my plan,” he said. “I followed it!”

Pip grinned. “You did more than follow it. You accepted your letters and your stars. You accepted your feelings. You tried again. That is a growth mindset.”

Ollie tilted his head. “Growth mindset means I can learn with practice,” he said slowly. “I can try, even when it is hard. I can make a plan and follow it.”

“And,” Pip added, “you can accept yourself and others, just as they are.”

Ollie nodded. He thought of his bumpy letters, his lopsided star, and his not-favorite cleaning. He felt kind to them, and kind to himself. “I accept me,” he said softly. “I accept you, too, Pip, small paws and all.”

Pip giggled. “Thank you, Ollie.”

They sat quietly. The clock went tick-tock. The lamp hummed. The paper felt smooth under their paws. The study corner was bright and calm.

Ollie whispered, “Next time, I will plan snack time too!”

Pip laughed. “And splash time!”

Ollie smiled wide. “Yes. With time and care, we can do many things.”

He looked at his poster one more time. “Accept. Try. Plan. We can grow.”

The End.
