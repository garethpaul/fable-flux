# Bluebird’s Safe Song Practice

Bluebird fluttered into the musical practice room. The room smelled like polished wood and fresh lemon soap. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. Drums sat in a neat row. A shiny triangle hung by a ribbon. A big round clock ticked softly on the wall.

“Tweet-a-dee! Good morning!” sang Bluebird. Her feathers shimmered like little raindrops. “Today I will learn a long song. That is my goal!”

She looked at a chart on the wall. It had bright pictures and simple words.
- Warm-up
- Practice notes
- Rest
- Practice rhythm
- Drink water
- Sing the whole song

Bluebird puffed up with pride. “I made a plan. I will follow my plan. I will be safe and on time.”

From the corner, the gentle music teacher, Miss Bell, smiled. She wore soft shoes and a jingling bracelet. “Good plan, Bluebird,” Miss Bell said. “Safety first.”

Bluebird nodded. She flew to a little hook and put on her tiny ear covers. They were small and soft, like two clouds. “Ear safety,” she chirped. “My ears are important.”

“Tie your music scarf, too,” Miss Bell reminded her. “We don’t want it to flap into the drums.”

Bluebird tied her small scarf in a neat bow. It felt smooth and snug. She checked the floor. No toys. No loose strings. The path was clear.

Tick-tock went the clock. Bluebird set a timer with a gentle bing. “Ten minutes for warm-up,” she said. “I can do it!”

She flapped her wings slowly. Flap, flap. “Breathe in. Breathe out.” She hummed low and soft. Mmm-mmm. The hum felt like warm soup. “This feels good,” she said. “Safe and slow.”

After the timer sang a tiny bing, Bluebird checked her chart. “Next: Practice notes.” She opened her music. The paper smelled clean and crisp. The notes looked like funny dots. She sang, “La, la, la.” Some notes were high like the sky. Some notes were low like the ground.

“Oops,” said Bluebird as her wing reached toward the drum. “That drum is big. I will give it space.” She moved one small step back. Tap, tap went her feet. She felt proud. “I remembered safety.”

Miss Bell clapped quietly. “Good thinking. Safe space helps you sing and move.”

Bluebird set a new timer. “Five minutes to rest.” She sat on a soft perch. She closed her eyes and breathed. She sipped cool water from a tiny cup. The cup felt chilly on her beak. “Rest time helps my body,” she whispered.

Then came rhythm. Bluebird picked up a light wooden stick. “Tap, tap, tap.” The sound was bright, like raindrops on a roof. She counted, “One, two. One, two.” She swung her wing slowly, keeping steady time.

A bouncing basketball rolled past the doorway from the gym next door. Thump, thump. Bluebird peeked out. Two kids were practicing gentle passes with a coach. The ball made a happy sound. Thump, thump, thump.

Bluebird smiled. “That is coordination,” she said. “Like my rhythm. Like a sport! They keep eyes on the ball. I keep eyes on the notes. We all stay safe.”

She turned back to her music. She kept her space clear. She kept her ear covers on. She held the stick with both wings so it would not drop. Tap, tap. “I am careful,” she said, “so I can do my best.”

Now it was time to sing the whole song. The clock hands pointed to the right place. Bluebird checked her plan. “I did each step. I am on time.” Her tummy felt fluttery but brave.

Miss Bell gave a little nod. “Ready when you are.”

Bluebird stood tall. She felt the soft floor under her feet. She smelled the lemon soap again. It made her feel fresh and awake. She took a slow breath. In and out.

“Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo,” she sang. The notes slid like smooth ribbons. “Up the hill, down the hill,” she sang softly, then strong. She moved her wings in a gentle sway to keep the beat. She did not rush. She did not wobble. She followed her plan.

Suddenly, the ribbon on the triangle swung a little in a breeze from the vent. It swished near her face. Bluebird paused. “Pause for safety,” she said. She waited for the ribbon to be still. Then she began again, calm and ready.

At the last note, Bluebird held the sound sweet and clear. “Tweet!” The note floated like a bubble and popped with a happy finish.

Miss Bell clapped. The bracelet jingled like tiny bells. “You did it! You reached your goal.”

Bluebird’s eyes shone. “I did it by being safe, by managing my time, and by practicing my steps. Safety first makes singing fun!”

She looked at the chart and drew a gold star next to “Sing the whole song.” She felt warm and proud. The basketball thumped again next door. Bluebird waved her wing. “I will try a sport later with a coach,” she said. “I will wear the right gear. I will make a plan. I will be safe.”

Miss Bell nodded. “That is a smart idea.”

Bluebird packed up. She hung up her ear covers. She folded her scarf. She checked the floor again. Clean and clear.

“Today I learned,” Bluebird sang softly, “that safety helps me reach my goals. When I make a plan and take my time, I can do big things!”

The End.
