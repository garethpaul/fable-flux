# Benny Beaver's Big Dinner Plan

Benny Beaver loved to build things with his strong teeth and busy paws. Every day, he built dams and houses by the pond. But today was special - it was his family's big dinner party!

Benny looked around the dining room. The long wooden table was empty. The chairs were pushed in. Everything looked so plain and boring.

"Oh no!" Benny said, wringing his little paws. "Our guests will be here soon, and the dining room doesn't look special at all!"

His mama beaver walked in, carrying a stack of plates. "What's wrong, Benny?" she asked, setting the dishes down with a gentle clink.

"I want to help make our dinner party amazing," Benny said, his voice getting smaller. "But I don't know how to make things pretty. I only know how to build with sticks and mud."

Mama Beaver smiled warmly. "Benny, you're so good at building. Maybe that skill can help us here too!"

Benny's ears perked up. "Really? But this is inside, not outside by the pond."

"Why don't you try?" Mama encouraged. "I believe in you!"

Benny looked at the empty table again. Slowly, an idea began to grow in his mind like a tiny seed. "Maybe... maybe I could build something for the table?"

"That sounds wonderful!" said his sister Bella, hopping into the room. "Can I help too?"

"Well..." Benny hesitated. He wanted to do it all by himself to prove he could help. But then he remembered how much fun it was to work together when they built their dam.

"Okay!" Benny said with a big smile. "Let's take turns! I'll gather materials first, then you can help me arrange them."

Benny scurried outside to the garden. He collected smooth river rocks, colorful fallen leaves, and small branches. The leaves crackled under his paws, and the rocks felt cool and smooth.

Back in the dining room, Benny carefully placed the rocks in a line down the center of the table. "Now it's your turn, Bella!" he said.

Bella arranged the bright red and yellow leaves around the rocks like little flowers. "This looks beautiful, Benny! Your building skills really do work inside too!"

"My turn again!" Benny said excitedly. He stood the small branches up like tiny trees between the leaves. Soon, they had created a lovely forest scene right on their dining table!

Papa Beaver walked in just as they finished. "Wow! This looks incredible! Who made this beautiful centerpiece?"

"Benny did!" Bella said proudly. "He used his building skills to make our table special!"

Benny's chest puffed up with pride. "I wasn't sure I could help at first," he admitted. "But Mama believed in me, and Bella and I worked together by taking turns."

When their guests arrived, everyone gasped at the beautiful table. "It's like having dinner in an enchanted forest!" said Mrs. Rabbit.

As they all sat down to eat, Benny felt warm and happy inside. He had learned that his special skills could help in new ways, and that working together made everything even better.

"Thank you for believing in me," Benny whispered to his mama.

"I always will," she whispered back. "You just needed to believe in yourself too."

The End.
