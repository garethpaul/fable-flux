# Joey's Workshop Discovery

Little Joey the kangaroo was bouncing around his family's garage workshop, his fluffy tail wiggling with excitement. The workshop smelled like wood shavings and paint, and sunlight streamed through the dusty windows.

"Boing, boing, boing!" Joey hopped from corner to corner, exploring everything with his big curious eyes. His mama was working at her workbench, fixing a broken chair.

"Mama, what's this?" Joey asked, pointing his tiny paw at a dusty old box tucked behind some tools.

"I don't know, sweetie," Mama Kangaroo said with a smile. "Why don't you discover what's inside?"

Joey's ears perked up. He loved discoveries! He carefully pulled the box closer with his paws. It felt rough and bumpy, and when he opened it, dust tickled his nose. "Achoo!"

Inside the box, Joey found something wonderful - old notebooks, colorful pencils, and even some crayons! But everything was covered in thick, gray dust.

"Ew, yucky!" Joey wrinkled his nose. The pencils felt gritty, and the notebooks had dusty fingerprints all over them. "Mama, these are too dirty to use!"

Mama Kangaroo hopped over to see. "Oh my! These belonged to your big sister when she was little. She loved to write and draw stories."

"But they're so messy," Joey said sadly, holding up a dusty red crayon. "I can't write with dirty things."

"What do you think we should do?" Mama asked, her eyes twinkling.

Joey thought hard, tapping his paw on his chin. "Maybe... maybe we could clean them?"

"What a smart discovery!" Mama said proudly. "Cleaning things before we use them is very important. It keeps us healthy and makes everything work better."

Together, they filled a bucket with warm, soapy water. Joey dipped a soft cloth in the water and carefully wiped each pencil and crayon. The water felt warm and bubbly on his paws.

"Look, Mama! The colors are so bright now!" Joey giggled as he cleaned a purple crayon. The dust came right off, revealing beautiful, vibrant colors underneath.

They wiped down the notebooks too, and Joey discovered that the covers had pretty pictures of flowers and rainbows that were hidden under all that dust.

"Now let's wash our paws," Mama said. "Clean paws make the best art!"

Joey scrubbed his paws with soap and warm water, watching the dirty water swirl down the drain. His paws felt fresh and clean.

"There!" he said, shaking his paws dry. "Now I can write!"

Joey opened the first clean notebook and picked up a bright blue crayon. He drew a big circle for the sun, then added wiggly lines for sunshine. Under it, he carefully wrote his first letter - a wobbly but proud "J" for Joey.

"I'm writing, Mama! I'm really writing!" Joey bounced up and down with joy.

"You made such a wonderful discovery today," Mama said, giving him a warm hug. "You found these art supplies, and you discovered that cleaning them first made them so much better to use."

Joey nodded enthusiastically. "And clean paws make better writing too!"

From that day on, Joey always remembered to clean his art supplies and wash his paws before creating. He filled notebook after notebook with colorful stories and pictures, and every time he opened that special box, he smiled, remembering his very first discovery in the workshop.

The End.
