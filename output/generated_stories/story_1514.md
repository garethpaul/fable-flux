# Bunny's Big Basketball Game

High up in the tall oak tree, Bunny had built the most wonderful treehouse. It had bright yellow walls, a cozy reading corner, and best of all - a tiny basketball hoop hanging from the ceiling!

Bunny LOVED playing basketball. Every morning, he would hop around his treehouse, bouncing his orange ball. *Bounce, bounce, bounce!* The sound echoed through the wooden walls.

"I'm going to be the BEST basketball player ever!" Bunny declared, throwing the ball toward the hoop. *Whoosh!* It sailed right over and bonked against the window.

Today was special. Bunny's friends were coming to play basketball in his treehouse. He was so excited that his fluffy white tail wiggled like crazy!

*Knock, knock, knock!*

"Come up!" called Bunny.

Squirrel, Owl, and Mouse climbed up the rope ladder. Their eyes grew wide when they saw the basketball hoop.

"Wow, Bunny! This is amazing!" squeaked Mouse.

"Let's play a game!" hooted Owl wisely. "But first, let me explain the rules—"

"No time for rules!" interrupted Bunny, grabbing the ball. "I already know how to play!"

Bunny started dribbling fast. *Bounce, bounce, bounce!* He ran around the treehouse, bumping into his friends.

"Bunny, wait!" called Squirrel. "Owl is trying to tell us—"

"I don't need to listen!" Bunny shouted, throwing the ball hard. *CRASH!* It knocked over a lamp. *THUD!* It hit the wall and bounced back, bonking Bunny right on his nose.

"Ouch!" Bunny rubbed his pink nose. The ball rolled under his reading chair.

Owl fluffed his feathers gently. "Bunny, in basketball, we need to listen to each other. That's how we play safely and have fun."

Bunny's ears drooped. He felt his heart beating fast from running around so much. "But I just wanted to play!"

"We want to play too," said Mouse kindly. "But listening helps us play better together."

Bunny took three deep breaths, just like Mama Rabbit had taught him. *Breathe in... breathe out...* His heart slowed down, and he felt calmer.

"I'm sorry," Bunny said softly. "I was so excited that I forgot to listen. Will you teach me the rules, Owl?"

Owl smiled. "Of course! First, we take turns shooting the ball. Second, we cheer for each other. Third, we use gentle throws inside the treehouse."

Bunny nodded, his ears perking up. "I can do that!"

They played the most wonderful game! Bunny listened carefully when Squirrel suggested they count to ten before each shot. He cheered loudly when Mouse made a basket. When he felt excited again, he remembered to take deep breaths.

"Great listening, Bunny!" praised Owl as Bunny made a gentle shot. *Swish!* The ball went right through the hoop.

"This is so much more fun when we listen to each other!" laughed Bunny, his tail wiggling happily.

As the sun set through the treehouse windows, the friends sat in a circle, sharing apple juice and crackers.

"Thank you for teaching me," Bunny said. "Listening made our game better, and nobody got hurt!"

"That's what friends do," said Squirrel. "We help each other learn."

From that day on, Bunny always remembered to listen first, breathe when he felt too excited, and play gently with his friends. His treehouse became the happiest place to play basketball in the whole forest!

The End.
