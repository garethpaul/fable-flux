# The Brave Build in the Busy Garage

The sun peeked in through the little window of the garage workshop. Dust danced like tiny stars in the warm light. Tools hung on hooks—bright red hammer, shiny silver wrench, and a blue tape measure that made a soft zip-zip sound.

In the middle of the room sat a clever cat named Coco. Coco had soft gray fur, bright green eyes, and a curious nose that wiggled when she was thinking. Coco loved to build. Today, she wanted to build a rolling cart to carry blocks and books.

“I can do it,” Coco purred. “I am brave. I am calm. I can build.”

Coco looked at her plans—a simple picture with big shapes: a box, four wheels, and a handle. She tapped the paper with her paw. “First, the box,” she said.

She picked up the wood pieces. The wood smelled sweet, like warm toast. “Hammer time,” Coco whispered. She held the little nails steady. Tap, tap, tap. The sound echoed in the garage. The box began to take shape. Coco smiled. “I am doing it.”

Next, Coco reached for the wheels. The wheels were round and smooth. She rolled them on the floor. Whirr, whirr. The sound made her whiskers twitch with joy.

Suddenly—clatter! A tall stack of metal pans wobbled and fell. Bang! Clang! Crash! The loud noise made Coco jump. Her heart pitter-pattered fast. Her tail puffed up like a dandelion.

Coco froze. She felt a little scared. The garage seemed bigger and louder than before. The shadows looked long. The hammer looked heavy.

“I don’t like that sound,” Coco whispered. “Maybe I should stop.”

She sat down and wrapped her tail around her paws. She took a slow breath. In... and out. In... and out. She remembered a calm song her friend sang. “Breathe in brave, breathe out slow,” she hummed softly.

Coco looked at her picture again. She remembered why she wanted the cart: to carry blocks, to share books, to help friends. Her ears perked up.

“I can be brave,” Coco told herself. “Brave does not mean no fear. Brave means I try, even when I feel a little scared. I can be calm. I can build.”

She looked around the workshop. “I need it to be safe and quiet,” she said. Coco picked up the fallen pans one by one. Clink, clink. She stacked them neatly. She swept the floor with a soft shh-shh sound. She moved the wobbly box to a steady shelf. “There,” she said. “Better.”

Coco rubbed her paws together. “Back to the wheels.”

She held a wheel up to the box. “Hello, wheel,” she giggled. “Let’s be friends.” She lined up the hole and gently turned the screw. The silver wrench made a click-click sound. “Lefty loosey, righty tighty,” she said in a sing-song voice. “Righty tighty.” Click! The wheel felt snug.

One wheel, two wheels, three wheels, four. Coco rolled the cart a little. It wiggled. “Hmm,” she said. “It needs to be straight.”

Coco took another breath. “Stay calm, think slow, fix small.” She loosened a screw just a little—lefty loosey. She checked the other wheel—righty tighty. The cart rolled smoother. Whirr... whirr... whirr. Coco’s eyes sparkled.

Next was the handle. Coco picked up a smooth wooden stick. It smelled like fresh trees. She sanded the end with a sandy-scratchy sound. Scratch, scratch, scratch. The handle felt soft and safe. She attached it to the box. Tap, tap. Click, click.

“Test time,” Coco said. She put three bright blocks in the cart. Red. Blue. Yellow. She pulled the handle. The cart rolled gently across the floor. Whirr. The blocks jiggled, but they did not fall. Coco’s whiskers wiggled. “I did it!” she cheered.

Just then, the wind whooshed through the window and made a loose sign flap. Flap-flap-flap! Coco’s fur puffed again. She felt the tiny scare come back.

She stopped. She put her paw on her chest. “Hello, little scare,” she said kindly. “I can be calm.” She took a slow, brave breath. The sign flapped less. The sound was not so big now. Coco walked to the window and closed it gently. Click. The room felt quiet and cozy.

Coco smiled. “I was brave,” she said. “I did not run away. I used my calm. I used my plan. I fixed the problem.”

She added a soft cushion to the cart—plop! She drew a star on the side with blue chalk. It left a dusty, cheerful mark. She tied a yellow ribbon on the handle. The ribbon felt silky and bright.

Coco pulled her new cart around the garage workshop. “Roll, little cart,” she sang. “Roll and help.” She placed books inside and stacked blocks on top. The cart moved steady and strong.

Coco sat beside her finished cart and purred. The room smelled like wood and warm sun. It sounded like soft wheels and quiet breath.

She looked at the reader with a happy grin. “When loud things happen,” she said, “we can be brave. We can breathe. We can fix little things. We can keep going. Brave building needs calm hearts.”

Coco patted the cart. “I am a clever cat,” she purred. “And I am brave.”

The End.
