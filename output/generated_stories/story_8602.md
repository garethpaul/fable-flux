# Dotty the Dolphin’s Nursery Band

Dotty was a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a smile as wide as a sea shell. One sunny morning, Dotty peeked into a comfortable nursery. The room felt warm and cozy. Soft rugs covered the floor. Sunlight made sparkles on the wall. Colorful blocks sat in baskets, and friendly stuffed animals lined a shelf. Best of all, there was a little music corner with a tiny drum, a xylophone with rainbow bars, a gentle shaker, and a small piano that went plink-plonk.

“Oh! Music!” Dotty chirped, clapping her flippers. “I want to make a song!”

Dotty swam—yes, even on land she could wiggle and bounce—over to the xylophone. She tapped a red bar. Ting! She tapped a blue bar. Tong! She tapped them all. Ting, tong, ting, tong! The sounds made her giggle.

“Hello, Dotty,” said Mr. Owl, the soft toy who watched from a rocking chair. In the nursery, toys could whisper when someone listened with care. Mr. Owl’s voice was low and kind. “What are you playing today?”

“A song!” Dotty said. “I want it to sound like the ocean. Whoosh, swish, splash!”

Dotty tried again. Ting-tong-CLANG! She hit three bars at once. “Oops,” she said, wrinkling her nose. “That sounded crunchy, not splashy.”

She tried the little drum. Thump, thump. “Hmm,” Dotty said. “I want it to be smooth and gentle.” She tried the shaker. Sha-sha-sha. “Better,” she whispered. “But I don’t know how to make it into a song.”

She frowned. Her tail drooped. “Maybe I can’t do it.”

Mr. Owl rocked and hummed a tiny tune. “Hoo-hoo. Do not give up, Dotty. Music is a friend that grows with practice. Start small. Try one sound. Then add another. Listen with your heart.”

Dotty took a slow breath. She put a flipper on her chest. In and out. She smiled a little. “I can try,” she said. “I can take initiative! I will make a plan.”

She picked up the shaker. “This will be my ocean waves.” Sha… sha… sha… very soft. She closed her eyes and imagined the sea. She heard seagulls far away. She smelled soapy clean blankets and a tiny hint of orange from a diffuser in the room. The rug felt fluffy under her belly. Sha… sha… sha…

“Good start,” Mr. Owl whispered. “What comes next?”

Dotty opened her eyes. “Drops of water!” She touched the xylophone, just one bar. Ting… ting… ting… “Little sparkles!” she giggled.

Then Dotty added the drum. Thump… thump. “This is my heart,” she said. Thump… thump. “Boom-boom, boom-boom.”

She tried to put them together.
Sha… sha… ting… thump… ting…
Sha… sha… ting… thump… ting…

“Hmm,” she said, tilting her head. “It still feels bumpy.”

“Try a steady beat,” Mr. Owl suggested. “Keep the thump the same.”

Dotty practiced a steady drum. Thump… thump… thump… thump. She nodded. “Now the shaker can ride on top,” she said. Sha-sha… sha-sha… sha-sha. Then she lightly tapped the xylophone. Ting… ting… ting…

She tried again, more slowly.
Thump… thump… thump… thump…
Sha-sha… sha-sha… sha-sha…
Ting… ting… ting…

Her tail gave a happy wiggle. “It sounds like waves and drops!” she cried.

But when she tried to go faster, her flipper slipped. CLONK! The drum boomed, the shaker fell, and the xylophone made a tinny clatter. Dotty’s eyes grew wide, and she felt hot in her cheeks.

“I messed up!” she said.

Mr. Owl blinked kindly. “That is part of learning. Musicians practice. Start slow, smile, and try again.”

Dotty took another breath. She picked up the shaker. “I can practice,” she said. “Slow and steady.” She counted softly, “One, two, three, four.” Thump… thump… thump… thump… Sha-sha… sha-sha… sha-sha… Ting… ting… ting…

Poppy the plush penguin scooted closer. “Can I listen?” she asked.

“Yes!” Dotty said. “And you can help.” She handed Poppy the shaker. “You do the waves.” Poppy shook gently. Sha-sha… sha-sha…

Dotty kept the drum steady. Thump… thump… thump… thump. Then she tapped the xylophone like tiny raindrops. Ting… ting… ting…

The nursery felt full of soft sound. The sunlight looked extra golden. The air felt light, like a hug. Even the blocks seemed to smile. Dotty grinned. “We made a song!”

Mr. Owl hummed along. “Hoo-hoo, it is lovely. What will you call it?”

Dotty thought. “Ocean Lullaby,” she said.

Poppy clapped her little wings. “Play it again!”

They practiced their song three more times. Each time, Dotty listened. Each time, she made small changes. A softer shaker here. A slower drum there. Ting… ting-ting… ting. The song became smoother, like calm water. Dotty felt proud. She had used her idea, taken the first step, and kept going.

At the end, Dotty bowed. “Thank you for listening,” she said. “I learned something today.”

Mr. Owl nodded. “And what did you learn, dear dolphin?”

Dotty smiled. “Music is like the ocean. It flows when I listen. When I practice slowly and keep a steady beat, my song grows. If I make a mistake, I can try again. I can take initiative and make my own music.”

Poppy chirped, “And making music together is fun!”

Dotty hugged her friends. The nursery felt cozy and calm. The little instruments waited, ready for more songs tomorrow. Dotty whispered, “I can’t wait to practice again.”

The End.
