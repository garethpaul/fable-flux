# Cubby Cleans the Workshop

In a sunny forest, there was a small garage workshop beside a cozy den. Inside, wooden shelves smelled like pine. Little tools clinked and clanked. A soft breeze carried the scent of leaves and fresh dirt through the open door.

A friendly bear cub named Cubby padded in on his big paws. “Good morning, workshop!” he said. His nose twitched. “Hmm. It smells dusty.”

Cubby loved helping. He liked fixing and sorting. Today, he wanted to make a bird feeder from old wood. He looked around. Cans rattled. Boxes teetered. A squeaky wheel rolled by and bonked his paw.

“Ouch,” Cubby said. He rubbed his paw and frowned. “This is very messy.”

Papa Bear walked in with a warm smile. “Hi, Cubby. Busy day?”

“I want to make a bird feeder,” Cubby said. “But I can’t find anything. There’s too much stuff.”

Papa nodded. “Let’s clean together. We can take care of our things. We can take care of our world, too.”

Cubby’s ears perked up. “Our world?”

Papa touched a box full of crumpled paper and plastic. “When we sort and reuse, we help the forest. We use less and waste less.”

Cubby puffed his chest. “I can do that!”

They started. Cubby opened a bin. It was full of old bottle caps. “These are tiny moons!” he giggled, shaking them. The caps tinkled like little bells.

Papa pointed to four big buckets. Each had a picture on it. One had a bottle. One had a can. One had paper. One had a happy arrow circle for reuse. “We put things in the right bucket,” Papa said.

Cubby tried. He dropped a crinkly bag into the bottle bucket. Clink! It did not fit.

He huffed. “This is hard.”

Papa’s voice was gentle. “It’s okay to feel mad. Take a bear breath with me. In… and out.”

Cubby breathed in deep. He smelled oil and orange soap. He breathed out, slow and warm. His shoulders felt softer.

“Let’s look again,” Papa said. “Is the bag a bottle?”

Cubby shook his head. “No. The bag is plastic. The picture is a bottle.”

“That’s right. Where does it go?”

Cubby found a bucket with a picture of a bag. “Here!” He dropped it in. Swoosh. “I did it!”

He smiled and got back to work. He found smooth wood. He found a metal can. He felt rough, bumpy, shiny, and soft things with his paws. He listened to clinks and thumps and rustles. It felt like a music song.

A little field mouse peeked in. “Squeak! Can I help?” she asked.

Cubby blinked. He liked helping. He also liked space to think. He put a paw to his chest. “Yes, you can help by handing me caps, one at a time,” he said. “But please don’t climb on the tools. That is my boundary.” He pointed to a bright red toolbox.

The mouse nodded. “Okay! I will stay by the caps.”

They worked. The mouse handed a cap. “Here.” The cap was cold and smooth.

“Thank you,” Cubby said. “That feels nice and cool!”

Soon, Cubby found a broken jar. “Oops,” he said. The glass glittered with tiny lights. He felt worry in his tummy. “I don’t like this,” he whispered.

Papa stepped in. “Glass goes in a special box. We keep paws safe.”

Cubby wanted to toss it fast. But he stopped and took a bear breath. In… out. “I can try again,” he told himself. He used thick gloves. The gloves felt snug and soft inside. He moved the glass slowly. Tink… tink… safe.

“Good choice,” Papa said. “You were careful and brave.”

Cubby’s frustration bubbled again when he could not find the hammer. He stomped a paw. “Where is it?”

The mouse pointed. “Squeak! Under the rag.”

Cubby lifted the rag. The hammer was warm from the sun. “Found you!” He laughed. “Thank you for showing me,” he told the mouse. “Next time, I will look with calm eyes.”

“Calm eyes,” the mouse echoed, smiling.

At last, the workshop looked different. The floor was clear. The buckets were sorted. The air smelled fresh and a little like oranges. Sunlight made bright squares on the clean floor.

“Now the bird feeder,” Papa said.

Cubby used the wood. Tap, tap, tap went the hammer. He tied string—loop, pull, snug. The feeder was simple and neat. They filled it with seeds that smelled nutty.

Outside, Cubby hung the feeder on a low branch. Rustle, rustle. A bluebird flew down. “Tweet!” The bird pecked a seed.

Cubby wiggled with joy. “We did it! We cleaned. We sorted. We reused. We helped the birds and the forest.”

Papa hugged him. The hug was warm and safe. “You cared for your space. You cared for your world. And you cared for yourself when you felt mad.”

Cubby nodded. “When I feel mad, I can take a bear breath. I can try again. I can ask for help. I can say my boundaries kindly.”

He looked at the tidy workshop. “Clean space, happy place,” he said. He patted the recycling buckets. “Right home, right bucket.”

The mouse squeaked, “And seeds for friends!”

Cubby laughed. “Seeds for friends.”

They listened to the soft chirps and the gentle wind. Cubby felt proud, calm, and kind.

The End.
