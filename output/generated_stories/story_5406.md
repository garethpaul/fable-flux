# Benny Beaver’s Thank-You Pancakes

Benny was a busy beaver with bright teeth and a big, thumpy tail. He loved to help. Today, Benny stood in a warm kitchen. The sun made golden squares on the floor. The air smelled sweet, like butter and maple syrup. The kettle sang a soft whistle. Pitter-pat went the rain outside, tap-tap on the window.

“Today, I will make thank-you pancakes!” Benny said. His whiskers wiggled. “I want to say thank you to my friends. They help me every day.”

Benny looked around the kitchen. The table felt smooth under his paws. The bowl was cool and shiny. He got flour. He got eggs. He got milk. He got blueberries. He got a tiny pinch of cinnamon. He set everything in a neat line. “I can do it,” he said, puffing with pride. “I can do it by myself!”

He cracked an egg. Plop! It slid into the bowl. “Oops,” Benny giggled. A tiny shell fell in with a tick-tick. He fished it out carefully. He poured the milk. Glug-glug. He whisked the bowl round and round. Swish, swish, swish. The batter smelled cozy, like morning hugs.

Benny turned on the stove. Click, whoosh. The pan got warm. Little bubbles danced in the butter. He poured a small circle. Sizzle! The sound was happy, like tiny claps.

But then, the batter grew too fast. Flop! A pancake folded on itself. The next one flipped too high. Whee—splat! It landed on the counter.

Benny’s tail drooped. “Oh no,” he whispered. “Maybe I can’t do it.”

Just then, a gentle knock came at the door. Knock-knock.

“Come in!” Benny called.

In came Tilly Turtle, slow and steady. She wore a bright red bow. “Mmm, it smells yummy,” said Tilly, smiling. “What are you making?”

“Thank-you pancakes,” said Benny. “But I keep messing up.”

Tilly touched Benny’s paw. “It’s okay to need help,” she said softly. “Teamwork makes things easier. I can read the recipe slowly for you.”

Knock-knock. In came Pip the Rabbit with springy ears. “I can help stir,” Pip said. “I’m good at gentle stirring.”

Knock-knock. In came Gus the Goose with a neat white apron. “Honk! I can flip,” Gus said. “I flip pancakes like a pro.”

Benny took a deep breath. The warm kitchen hummed with friendly voices and soft music from the kettle. “Thank you,” he said. His heart felt warm, like a fresh blanket. “Let’s work together.”

Tilly read in a calm voice. “First, stir the dry things. Then add the wet things. Slow and steady.”

Pip held the bowl with two paws and stirred in tiny circles. Swish, swish. “I can do soft swirls,” Pip said, smiling.

Gus waited for little bubbles to pop on top. “Now,” he said. Flip! The pancake turned over and landed just right. “Honk! Perfect.”

Benny watched and tried again. His paws felt more sure. He poured smaller circles. He waited for the bubbles. He counted, “One, two, three.” He flipped. Flip! The pancake landed with a soft pat.

“You did it!” said Tilly.

“You did it!” said Pip.

“Honk! You did it!” said Gus.

Benny smiled so wide his whiskers tickled his cheeks. “I did it,” he said. “We did it together.”

Soon the table was full of pancakes—round, fluffy, and golden. Blueberries peeked out like tiny purple stars. Steam curled up in little ribbons. Benny put butter on top. It melted like yellow snow in the sun. He poured a slow ribbon of syrup. Drip, drip, drip. The sweet smell filled the room.

Benny folded his paws. He looked at his friends. “Thank you, Tilly. Thank you, Pip. Thank you, Gus,” he said. “You helped me. I am grateful.”

“You’re welcome,” said Tilly. “You were brave to ask.”

“You kept trying,” said Pip. “That shows strong heart.”

“Honk,” said Gus. “And your flips were fantastic.”

They ate together. Chew, chew. Mmm. The pancakes were warm and soft. Syrup made their lips sticky. They giggled and licked their fingers.

When they were done, Benny looked at the messy counter. There was flour like snow and a drip of batter on the floor. His eyes got big. “Uh-oh,” he said.

Tilly nodded. “We can clean up. It’s our job, too.”

Pip got a cloth. Wipe, wipe. Gus washed bowls. Splash, swish. Benny swept with his tail—whoosh, whoosh. The kitchen became neat again. It felt calm and bright.

Benny took a breath. His chest felt full of light. “Today I learned something,” he said. “I learned to say thank you. I learned to ask for help. I learned I can do new things when I keep trying. Teamwork makes it better. And cleaning up is part of the job.”

Tilly smiled. “Gratitude makes hearts happy,” she said.

Pip nodded. “You believed in yourself.”

Gus fluffed his feathers. “We all helped, and that feels good.”

Benny placed one last pancake on a small plate. He wrote “Thank you” on it with blueberries. He handed it to his friends. “This is for you,” he said softly. “I am grateful for you.”

Outside, the rain stopped. Sunlight sparkled on the window. Inside, the warm kitchen glowed. Benny’s tail thumped a happy beat.

And when the kettle sang again, they all sang, too.

The End.
