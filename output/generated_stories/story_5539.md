# Momo’s Music Fix-It Day

In a bright musical practice room, a courageous monkey named Momo swung in on the door handle with a happy squeak. “I’m ready to make music!” Momo said. The room smelled like lemon polish and fresh wood. Sunlight danced on shiny instruments. Drums waited in a neat row. A piano smiled with black and white teeth. Little bells sat in a basket like stars.

Momo’s tail curled with excitement. “Today, I will make a new song,” Momo whispered. “I will try something new.”

Momo tapped a drum. Boom! The sound felt big and bouncy. Momo plinked the piano. Plink, plonk, plink! The notes were like raindrops. Momo shook a bell. Ting-a-ling! It sparkled like sunshine.

But when Momo tried to play the big guitar, the string went twang and then… snapped! The sound popped like a bubble. “Oh no!” Momo gasped. The guitar looked sad. The song stopped before it could start.

Momo’s ears drooped. “How can I play now?” Momo asked. The room felt too quiet. The drums were fine, the piano was fine, but the guitar was not fine. Momo had a big problem.

Momo took a deep breath. In, out. “I am brave,” Momo said softly. “I can think. I can try. I can make a plan.” The words felt warm, like a hug.

Momo looked around the room. What could help? Momo noticed a basket of ribbons, a roll of tape, some rubber bands, a box of pencils, and a soft scarf by the window. Momo’s eyes sparkled. “I can try something new! I can use what I have.”

Momo picked up a rubber band and stretched it. Twang! It made a funny little sound. “Hmm,” Momo said. “This sounds like a tiny guitar string.”

Momo wrapped one rubber band around an empty tissue box and plucked it. Twung! Momo smiled. “Listen!” Momo said to the quiet room. “I made a new sound!”

Momo added more rubber bands across the box. Pling, plong, plang, plong! Each one made a different sound. “This is my new guitar,” Momo said proudly. “It is a tissue-box guitar!”

Momo’s tail swished. “I can keep going. I can try more.” Momo took a pencil and two spoons. Tap-tap on the spoons made a tiny drum sound. Tap-tap on the pencil made a wood block sound. The room began to sing again.

Just then, Teacher Tori, a kind turtle, peeked in. “Hello, Momo,” said Teacher Tori in a soft, calm voice. “I heard a string snap. Are you okay?”

“I was sad,” Momo said, “but I am trying a new idea. Listen to my tissue-box guitar!”

Teacher Tori listened. Pling, plong, plang! The turtle’s eyes twinkled. “That is clever,” she said. “You used what you had. You are thinking with your brain and your brave heart. That is called innovation—making new ideas to solve a problem.”

Momo grinned. “Innovation!” The word felt big and bouncy, like the drum. “I can solve my problem in a new way.”

“But,” said Momo, looking at the broken guitar, “I still wish we had a big, smooth sound.”

Teacher Tori nodded. “Let us think together. What else can make a long sound?” She looked around. “What do you notice?”

Momo listened. The heater hummed. The window made a tiny breeze. The scarf fluttered with a soft whoosh. “The scarf!” Momo said. “It can sing!”

Momo tied one end of the scarf to a chair and held the other end tight. Momo plucked the scarf like a string. Vvvvvooom. It made a low, gentle sound. Momo giggled. “It sounds like a whale!”

Teacher Tori clapped slowly. “Beautiful. You are testing and learning.”

Momo thought some more. “What if we put the scarf over the tissue-box guitar?” Momo laid the scarf across the rubber bands. The sound changed. Pluff, plaff, ploom! It was soft and cozy.

Momo pressed the scarf tighter. The sound grew deeper. Momo loosened it. The sound grew lighter. “I can change sounds by changing how things feel,” Momo said. “I am experimenting!”

“Exactly,” said Teacher Tori. “You are using careful steps. You try, you listen, you change, and you try again. That is critical thinking.”

Momo’s smile grew wide. “Now I can make my new song.” Momo counted. “One, two, three.” Drum: boom-boom. Tissue strings: pling-plong. Scarf string: vvvvooom. Bells: ting-a-ling. Piano: plink-plonk.

The room filled with a brave, new tune. It was warm like a blanket and bright like morning. Momo felt proud and calm. “I did it,” Momo said. “I solved the problem in a new way.”

Other friends peeked in—the mouse, the duck, and the bunny. “Ooooh,” they said. “We want to try!”

Momo nodded. “Let’s play together. Let’s make our own instruments. We can be kind. We can share. We can listen and learn.”

They all built little instruments—cup drums, straw flutes, and spoon shakers. Soon, the musical practice room buzzed and jingled and sang. They made a big, friendly band.

When the song ended, everyone clapped. Teacher Tori said, “What did we learn today?”

Momo put a hand on the brave chest and said, “When something breaks, we can breathe, think, and try new ideas. We can use what we have. We can test and change our plan. That is innovation, innovation, and critical thinking.”

Teacher Tori smiled. “Yes. You are courageous and smart. New ideas can solve problems.”

Momo hugged the tissue-box guitar. “I will remember,” Momo said. “I can make music, even when things go wrong.”

The sun shone brighter. The room felt happy again.

The End.
