# Toby's Big Feelings

Toby the turtle sat in his cozy study corner. His green shell felt warm under the soft reading lamp. Colorful books lined the shelves around him. A fuzzy blue rug tickled his feet.

"Time for homework!" said Miss Rabbit, his teacher. She placed a worksheet on Toby's little desk. "Practice writing your letters, please."

Toby picked up his yellow pencil. His hand felt shaky. The letters looked so hard! He tried to write the letter 'A' but it came out wobbly.

"This is too hard!" Toby whispered. His face felt hot. His heart beat fast like a drum. 

He tried again. The pencil slipped. His 'B' looked like a squiggly worm.

"GRRRR!" Toby growled. His shell felt tight and angry. He wanted to throw his pencil across the room!

"Toby, are you okay?" asked Benny Bunny from the next desk. Benny had kind brown eyes and floppy ears.

"NO!" Toby snapped. "These letters are stupid! I can't do it!" His voice was loud and rumbly.

Miss Rabbit hopped over quietly. "Toby, I can see you're feeling angry. When we feel mad, our bodies tell us. What does your body feel like?"

Toby touched his shell. "Hot and tight," he said softly.

"Let's try something special," Miss Rabbit said gently. "Close your eyes and take three slow breaths with me."

Toby closed his eyes. "Breathe in slowly like you're smelling flowers," Miss Rabbit said. "One... two... three."

Toby's shell felt a little looser.

"Now breathe out like you're blowing bubbles," she continued. "One... two... three."

Toby opened his eyes. The angry feeling was getting smaller.

"Better?" asked Benny with a warm smile.

Toby nodded. "A little."

"Writing letters is tricky," said Benny. "I mess up too! Want to practice together?"

"Really?" Toby asked. He thought Benny was perfect at everything.

"Really! Look at my 'C' - it looks like a broken cookie!" Benny giggled.

Toby smiled for the first time. "Mine looks like a sad moon!"

They both laughed. The study corner felt cozy and safe again.

"Let's try one letter at a time," Benny suggested. "We can help each other."

Toby picked up his pencil again. This time, his hand felt steady. "Okay, let's start with 'A'."

"Go slow," Benny whispered. "Like you're drawing a mountain with a bridge."

Toby drew carefully. His 'A' wasn't perfect, but it looked like a real letter!

"You did it!" Benny cheered quietly.

"I did!" Toby felt proud and happy. His shell felt light and comfortable now.

They worked together for the rest of the time. When Toby felt frustrated, he remembered to breathe slowly. When Benny made mistakes, Toby helped him too.

"Look!" Toby showed Miss Rabbit his paper. He had written five whole letters!

"Wonderful work, Toby! You managed your angry feelings and finished your task. How do you feel now?"

Toby's eyes sparkled. "Happy! And proud! Benny helped me, and I helped him too."

"That's what good friends do," Miss Rabbit smiled. "And remember - when big feelings come, we can always breathe and ask for help."

Toby hugged his new friend Benny. The study corner felt like the best place in the world.

The End.
