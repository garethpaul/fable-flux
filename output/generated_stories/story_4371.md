# Koa the Koala Learns About Sharing

Koa the koala loved visiting the bright, colorful art room at the community center. The walls were covered with rainbow paintings, and shelves held jars of sparkly glitter, smooth paintbrushes, and boxes of chunky crayons. The sweet smell of fresh paper and the gentle sound of children creating always made Koa feel peaceful.

Today, Koa arrived early and saw something wonderful - a brand new set of magical rainbow markers sitting on the round table. They shimmered in their clear container, with colors that seemed to dance in the morning sunlight streaming through the big windows.

"Oh my!" Koa said softly, his gray fuzzy ears perking up. "These look amazing!"

Just as Koa reached for the markers, three little rabbits hopped into the art room. Their names were Ruby, Rosie, and Rex, and they were all very excited.

"Look at those beautiful markers!" squeaked Ruby, her pink nose twitching.

"I want to use them first!" said Rosie, bouncing up and down.

"No, me first!" called Rex, his whiskers wiggling with excitement.

The three rabbits began to argue, their voices getting louder and louder. Koa watched quietly, his calm brown eyes observing everything. He could see that all the rabbits really wanted to create beautiful art, just like he did.

"Wait, friends," Koa said in his gentle voice. "I have an idea."

The rabbits stopped arguing and looked at the wise koala.

"These markers belong to everyone in the art room," Koa explained, touching the container softly. "That means we can all use them together. Would you like to hear my plan?"

The rabbits nodded eagerly, their ears standing straight up.

Koa smiled warmly. "We can take turns! Each of us can choose two colors for ten minutes, then we pass them to the next friend. That way, everyone gets a fair chance to create something special."

Ruby clapped her small paws together. "That sounds fair!"

"But what if someone uses them longer than ten minutes?" worried Rosie.

Koa pointed to the big, friendly clock on the wall that had numbers and a smiling face. "We can watch the clock together. When the big hand moves to the next number, it's time to share with the next friend."

Rex's eyes lit up. "And we can even help each other with our drawings!"

"Exactly!" Koa said, his voice warm like a cozy hug. "Sharing makes everything more fun."

The four friends spent the morning creating the most beautiful artwork together. Ruby drew a garden full of flowers, Rosie made a picture of her family, and Rex created a superhero bunny. Koa painted a peaceful eucalyptus tree with leaves that seemed to whisper in an imaginary breeze.

When each friend's turn was over, they happily passed the markers to the next person. They even shared ideas and helped each other choose colors.

"This is so much better than arguing," said Ruby, admiring everyone's artwork.

"When we share fairly, everyone gets to be happy," agreed Rosie.

As they cleaned up together, wiping the table with soft cloths and putting the markers back in their special container, Koa felt proud of how they had solved their problem.

"Remember, friends," Koa said gently, "being fair means making sure everyone gets a chance. When we share and take turns, we all win."

The rabbits hugged Koa goodbye, promising to remember the lesson about fairness. As Koa walked home through the sunny afternoon, he felt warm and happy, knowing that kindness and fairness always make the world a brighter place.

The End.
