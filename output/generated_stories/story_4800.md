# Eeko Listens With His Big Ears

Eeko was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray skin and very big, floppy ears. He lived with his family in a playful living room full of colorful pillows, a low drum, a basket of books, and a soft rug that felt like warm grass.

One sunny afternoon, the living room smelled like sweet mango slices and cinnamon tea. The window let in a breeze that made the curtains whisper, shhh. Eeko’s tail swished. His toes wiggled on the rug. He wanted to play, play, play!

Mama Elephant called, “Eeko, please listen.” Her voice was warm like a hug. “Today we will dance and share music from our friends around the world. But first, we put toys away and make a safe space.”

Eeko heard Mama’s words, but the drum looked so fun. “Boom-boom-boom!” he tapped with his little trunk. The drums thumped like thunder. He laughed. “Listen to me, Mama!”

Mama smiled gently. “We will all listen to each other. Listening keeps us safe. Listening helps us learn. Eeko, first, we tidy. Then we drum together.”

Eeko’s ears twitched. He wanted to keep drumming. He did not want to tidy. He pushed a pile of blocks with his foot. The blocks rattled and clattered. “Clink, clank!” A tower wobbled and toppled. A small car rolled under the table.

Papa Elephant peeked in with a tray. “Snacks are ready. But wait,” he said, looking at the messy floor. “We need clear paths so no one trips. Big feet, little wheels—oops!” Papa wiggled his big toes and chuckled.

Eeko frowned. He felt a tiny twist in his tummy. He loved snacks. He loved to dance. He didn’t want anyone to trip. His ears drooped. “I’m sorry,” he whispered. “It is hard to stop when I’m excited.”

Mama touched his head with her trunk. “Thank you for your words. That is responsible. Let’s try a listening game.”

“A game?” Eeko’s ears perked up.

“Yes,” Mama said. “It is called Ears First, Feet Next. We listen. Then we move.”

Papa clapped softly—pat, pat. “I say ‘Eeko, please.’ You say ‘I’m listening,’ and show me with your ears and eyes.”

Eeko stood tall. “I’m listening.” He opened his ears wide and looked at Papa.

Papa smiled. “Eeko, please pick up the red blocks.”

Eeko looked around. He saw bright red blocks near the couch. “I heard you,” he said. He picked them up and set them in the basket. Plop, plop.

“Eeko, please roll the blue car to me,” Mama said.

Eeko knelt and peered under the table. The rug tickled his knees. “I hear you!” He rolled the blue car. Vrrrr. Mama caught it with a giggle.

“Eeko, please place the drum in the center,” Papa said.

“I hear you,” Eeko sang. He carried the drum carefully and set it on the soft rug. Boom—just one gentle beat.

The floor was clear. The room felt calm and open. “Good listening,” Mama said. “That was very responsible and safe.”

Papa set down the tray. “Now, cultural music time!” He showed a small shaker with tiny beads. “This maraca is from our friends who dance with bright ribbons.” Mama held a scarf with bright patterns. “This cloth shows shapes from faraway mountains.” Papa lifted a little bell. “This bell sounds like a sunny morning.”

Eeko’s eyes sparkled. “We can learn from everyone,” he said softly.

“We listen to their music and stories,” Mama said. “Listening shows respect and helps us understand. We accept different sounds and dances.”

Papa rang the bell. Ting! The sound was clear and shiny. Eeko listened. It felt like a smile in the air. Mama shook the maraca. Shaka-shaka. It sounded like friendly rain. Eeko gently tapped the drum. Boom, boom. It felt like a warm heartbeat.

They danced between the pillows. The rug felt bouncy. The bell jingled. The maraca whispered. The drum thumped. Eeko’s ears flapped with joy.

Suddenly, Eeko felt the drum slide. It nudged a pillow. The pillow wobbled near the table leg. Eeko remembered: clear paths keep us safe. His ears tingled. “Stop and listen,” he told himself.

He froze and took a deep breath. “Mama, Papa, please listen,” Eeko said. “The drum is too close to the table. We can move the pillow back.”

Mama and Papa stopped and listened to Eeko. “Good noticing,” Papa said. “You kept us safe.”

Together they moved the pillow. They made space again. Eeko smiled. “My ears helped,” he said proudly.

After the dance, they sat for snacks. The mango was sweet and juicy. The cinnamon tea smelled cozy. Eeko licked a sticky drop from his trunk and giggled. “Listening made dancing better,” he said. “I learned new sounds. I kept the room safe. I helped.”

Mama nodded. “Listening shows care.”

Papa patted Eeko’s shoulder. “And responsibility.”

Eeko’s ears felt warm and happy. He looked at the drum, the bell, and the maraca. “I like learning from many places,” he said. “I can listen, be careful, and enjoy together.”

That night, Eeko tucked the instruments into the basket. “Good night, sounds,” he whispered. He paused, ears open, and listened to the soft hush of the curtains and the quiet ting of the bell settling. His heart felt calm.

Eeko smiled. “I am a good listener,” he said. “When I listen, I learn. When I listen, I keep us safe. When I listen, I show love.”

The End.
