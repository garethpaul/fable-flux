# Leo’s Quiet Roar

In a sunny backyard, a brave little lion cub named Leo padded onto the soft grass. The warm sun kissed his fur. The air smelled like fresh leaves and sweet flowers. A gentle breeze whispered, shhh, through the tall sunflowers. Birds chirped, peep-peep, from the fence.

Leo blinked his bright eyes. “Today, I have a big goal!” he said. “I want to balance my favorite red ball on my nose.”

He set the shiny red ball on the grass. He wiggled his whiskers. He took a deep breath. “I can do this,” he whispered.

Leo nudged the ball with his nose. The ball wobbled. Plop! It rolled off and thumped onto his paw.

“Oh,” Leo said, with a small frown. His tummy felt fluttery. His tail twitched. He tried again. He pressed his nose up, very carefully. The ball sat… sat… then slid. Bonk! It touched the ground.

“I keep trying, but it keeps falling,” Leo sighed. His ears tilted down. He wanted to roar, a big loud roar. But he remembered his mama’s words: “When you feel wiggly or worried, find your calm. Calm helps your brave.”

Leo sat on the sunny step and listened. He heard bees buzzing, bzzzz. He felt the warm stone under his paws. He smelled mint leaves in a pot by the door. He placed a paw on his belly and another on his chest.

“In… two… three,” he breathed softly. “Out… two… three.” His shoulders relaxed. His tail stopped twitching. The fluttery feeling slowed.

A butterfly fluttered past. “Hello, little friend,” Leo said with a smile. “I’m finding my calm.”

He stood up. “New plan,” he told himself. “Small steps. I can do small steps.” He set a tiny goal: “First, balance for one slow count.”

He rolled the red ball back. “Ready, nose?” he giggled. He took one calm breath. He tipped his nose up. The ball sat. “One,” he whispered. Plop. It fell.

“That’s okay,” he said kindly to himself. “I did one. I can try again.”

He tried again. In… two… three. Out… two… three. “One… two,” he counted. Plop. The ball fell, but Leo’s smile stayed.

“I’m getting better,” he said. “I will keep going.” He felt proud. His brave was growing.

A soft cloud drifted by. The sun felt warm like a hug. The grass felt cool under Leo’s paws. He listened to the birds: peep-peep-peep. He gently licked his nose to make it less slippery. “Smart idea,” he told himself. “I can help myself.”

He set another goal. “Today, I will make it to three.” He marked a tiny circle on the ground with his paw. “This is my practice spot.”

Leo tried again and again. Sometimes the ball fell on “one.” Sometimes it stayed for “two.” Each time he breathed and smiled. “Calm helps my body. Calm helps my brain,” he said. “I can be patient.”

Soon, Mama Lion peeked from the kitchen door. “I see a brave cub,” she said softly. “What is your plan, Leo?”

“My plan is small steps,” Leo replied. “I breathe in, I breathe out. I count. I keep going.”

Mama nodded. “That is a strong plan. Your calm is your strong.”

Leo felt a warm glow in his chest. He set the ball again. He breathed. In… two… three. Out… two… three. Up went his nose. The ball sat still and shiny.

“One,” Leo whispered. “Two… three!”

The ball wobbled, but Leo kept his breath steady. He felt the smooth roundness. He felt his paws steady on the grass. He softened his eyes. He kept going.

“Four… five,” he whispered.

He lowered the ball into his paws and laughed. “I did it! I did five!”

Mama clapped gently. “I am proud of your calm, your self-reliance, and your persistence,” she said. “How do you feel?”

“I feel peaceful,” Leo said. “My calm helped my brave. My small goals helped me do a big thing.”

Leo looked at the red ball. “New goal,” he said with a grin. “Next time, I will try for six.”

He took one last calm breath, in and out. He listened to the bees, bzzzz. He smelled the mint. He felt the warm sun. He rubbed his cheek on the soft grass. “Thank you, calm,” he whispered.

Mama smiled. “When you want to roar big, try breathing small,” she said.

Leo nodded. “I can remember: stop, breathe, count, try.” He patted the ball. “I can do hard things when I am calm.”

He curled up on the sunny step, happy and tired in a good way. The backyard hummed gently. The sunflowers swayed. The butterfly landed on the red ball like a tiny flag of victory.

Leo closed his eyes for a moment and smiled. “Calm first,” he whispered. “Then brave.”

The End.
