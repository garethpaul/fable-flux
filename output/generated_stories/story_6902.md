# Ollie's Fair Share Discovery

Ollie was a playful otter with sleek brown fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved exploring every corner of Grandma's quiet attic. The wooden floorboards creaked softly under his webbed paws as he wandered around.

"What treasures will I find today?" Ollie whispered excitedly.

The attic smelled like old books and lavender. Dusty sunbeams danced through the small window, making everything look magical. Ollie's whiskers twitched with excitement as he spotted something interesting.

In the corner sat three old toy boxes. One was big and overflowing with colorful toys. One was medium-sized with just a few toys inside. The smallest box looked almost empty.

"That's not fair!" Ollie said, scratching his head. "Why does one box have so many toys while the others have so few?"

Ollie read the dusty labels on each box. The big box said "Tommy." The medium box said "Sarah." The tiny box said "Little Emma."

"Poor Emma," Ollie said sadly. "She hardly has any toys to play with."

Ollie's heart felt heavy. He knew it wasn't right for one child to have so much while another had so little. His mama had always taught him that sharing was caring.

"I need to fix this," Ollie decided firmly.

He opened Tommy's overflowing box. *Clink, clank!* Out tumbled toy cars, blocks, puzzles, and stuffed animals. There were so many toys that some spilled onto the floor.

Carefully, Ollie picked up some of the extra toys. A soft teddy bear, colorful building blocks, and a pretty puzzle with butterflies. He gently placed them in Emma's nearly empty box.

"There! Now Emma has more toys to play with," Ollie said happily.

But then Ollie noticed Sarah's box could use more toys too. He took a few more items from Tommy's collection - a toy train and some crayons - and added them to Sarah's box.

"Perfect! Now everyone has a fair share," Ollie said, clapping his paws together.

Just then, he heard footsteps on the attic stairs. *Creak, creak, creak.*

"Oh no! Someone's coming!" Ollie whispered. He quickly hid behind an old rocking chair.

Grandma appeared at the top of the stairs with three children. Tommy looked about eight years old, Sarah was maybe six, and little Emma was only four.

"Let's see your toy boxes," Grandma said kindly.

Tommy opened his box first. "Hey! Some of my toys are missing!" he said, frowning.

Emma opened her box and gasped with delight. "Look! I have new toys! A teddy bear and blocks!"

Sarah smiled when she saw her box. "I got new toys too! Thank you, Grandma!"

But Tommy crossed his arms. "That's not fair! Those are MY toys!"

Ollie felt worried. Had he done something wrong?

Grandma sat down on an old trunk. "Tommy, you had many, many toys. More than you could ever play with at once. Emma and Sarah had very few. Sometimes, sharing our extra things helps others feel happy too."

Tommy thought for a moment. He looked at Emma hugging her new teddy bear and Sarah building with the blocks.

"I guess... I guess I did have too many toys to play with," Tommy said slowly. "And Emma looks really happy with that teddy bear."

"It makes me feel good inside when my friends are happy," Sarah added.

Tommy smiled. "You know what? I'm glad we all have toys to play with now. That IS more fair."

Ollie peeked out from behind the rocking chair, his heart full of joy. He had helped make things fair, and everyone was happy!

As the family went back downstairs, Ollie whispered, "Being fair and sharing makes everyone smile. That's what justice feels like!"

The End.
