# Hoot's Little Wellness Plan

In a playful living room full of soft pillows and bright blocks, a wise old owl named Hoot perched on the back of a cozy couch. Sunlight made warm squares on the carpet. A bouncy ball thumped gently against a chair. The room smelled like fresh apples and clean soap.

Hoot blinked his big golden eyes and fluffed his soft feathers. “Hoo-hoo,” he said kindly. “Today, we learn how to take care of our bodies and our hearts.”

A small child named Pip sat cross-legged on a fuzzy rug, hugging a teddy bear. Pip’s cheeks were rosy, but a tiny frown puckered their lip. “I want to be strong and happy,” Pip whispered. “But sometimes I don’t know what to do.”

Hoot tilted his head. “That is a good problem to solve. We can make a little plan. A plan is a set of small steps. Small steps help us grow.”

Pip’s eyes got wide. “Small steps?”

“Yes,” Hoot said, tapping the couch with one gentle wing. “Small steps are easy to try. Let’s pick three. Move our bodies, feed our bodies, and rest our bodies.”

Pip squeezed the teddy. “Okay! That sounds like a game.”

Hoot smiled. “First step: move our bodies. We can do a wiggle dance.”

Music trickled from a tiny speaker like warm honey. Pip stood up. Hoot hopped down to the rug. “Wiggle, wiggle, wiggle,” Hoot sang. Pip giggled and spun in slow circles. The rug felt soft under bare toes. The air whooshed by Pip’s ears. Teddy bounced in Pip’s arms.

“Let’s set a little goal,” Hoot said. “A goal is a promise to try. Our goal: dance for one happy song.”

Pip nodded. “I can do that!”

They danced until the song ended with a cheerful ding. Pip took a breath. “I did it!” Pip’s chest rose and fell, warm and proud.

Hoot touched a wing to his heart. “Listen to your body. It says, ‘Thank you for moving me.’”

“Thank you, body,” Pip said, patting their tummy. Pip smiled, and the frown was gone.

Hoot blinked. “Second step: feed our bodies. Would you like a snack?”

Pip’s tummy made a tiny rumble. “Yes, please!”

They walked to a small table by the window. A bright bowl held crunchy carrot sticks, sweet apple slices, and a cup of cool water that sparkled in the light. Pip picked up a carrot. Crunch! It sounded like tiny twigs snapping. The apple tasted juicy and sweet, like a little sunshine. The water felt cool and smooth, like a river.

“What is our goal?” Hoot asked softly.

Pip thought. “Try two colors of snack and drink water.”

“Wise choice,” said Hoot. “You are kind to your body.”

Pip ate orange and red, then took a big sip. “I did it again! I feel strong!”

Hoot’s eyes twinkled. “Third step: rest our bodies. After we move and eat, we also need calm.”

Pip looked around. The playful living room was quiet now. The bouncy ball sat still. The pillows looked like friendly clouds. Hoot spread his wings and flapped once, very softly. “Let’s make a cozy nest.”

They stacked pillows into a soft fort. Pip crawled inside with Teddy. Hoot tucked in beside them, warm and feathery. The room smelled like apple and clean clothes. “We will take three slow breaths,” Hoot said. “Our goal: breathe in and out three times.”

Pip placed a hand on their tummy. “In,” Pip whispered, filling up like a balloon. “Out,” they whispered, letting the air flow like a gentle wind. One… two… three.

“How do you feel?” Hoot asked.

“Calm,” said Pip. “Like a soft blanket.”

Hoot nodded. “You moved. You ate well. You rested. You met your goals. That is wellness. Wellness means taking care of your body and feelings.”

Pip peeked out of the pillow fort. “Can I make my own plan?”

“Yes,” Hoot said. “Let’s make it simple and bright.”

Hoot found a big paper and a blue crayon. Pip held the paper steady. Together they drew three happy pictures: a dancing stick figure, a rainbow snack plate, and a sleepy moon with tiny stars. Beside each picture, Hoot wrote small words.

Move: one song.
Munch: two colors.
Rest: three breaths.

Pip clapped softly. “I can try these every day!”

Hoot’s voice was warm. “You can, and you will. If you forget, that is okay. Try again. Small steps help you grow strong, healthy, and brave.”

Pip hugged Teddy. “I feel proud.”

“You did the work,” Hoot said. “Your body and your heart thank you.”

The sun made gold shapes on the wall. The playful living room felt bright and safe. Pip stood tall like a little tree. “Hoot,” Pip said, “tomorrow we can dance two songs!”

Hoot chuckled, low and happy. “That is a fine new goal.”

Pip grinned. “I can do it.”

“Yes,” said the wise old owl. “You can.”

The End.
