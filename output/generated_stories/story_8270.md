# Roo Writes in the Busy Basement

Tap-tap, thump-thump! A joyful kangaroo joey named Roo bounced down the wooden steps to the busy basement. The air smelled like warm cardboard and crayons. Lights hummed softly. Boxes were stacked high, and tables were covered with paper, glue, and markers. Children and grown-ups moved around like busy bees.

Roo’s ears perked up. “Hello! I want to help,” Roo said, tail swish-swish on the floor.

“Hi, Roo,” said Mia, a friendly helper in a bright yellow vest. “We are making Welcome Bags for new neighbors. We need notes to put inside. Would you like to write?”

Roo’s eyes sparkled. “I love to write! I will write happy notes.”

Mia pointed to a small table. “Here is a writing station. Pens, crayons, and paper are ready.”

Roo hopped to the table, heart thumping with joy. The pens clicked. The paper felt smooth. Roo picked a purple crayon and drew a tiny sun. Then Roo wrote, slow and neat, “Hi! You are welcome.”

Roo smiled. “This is fun!”

But the basement was very busy. A fan whirred. Tape went ripppp! Boxes bumped. A boy named Theo zoomed by with a cart. “Make way!” he shouted. The cart squeaked and rattled.

Roo wobbled and made a wiggly line. “Oh no,” Roo whispered. “My letters got squished.”

Roo tried again. “Hello, friend!” But a loud thud made Roo jump. The letters turned loopy-loo. Roo’s tail drooped.

Mia came over. “How’s it going?”

Roo held up the paper. “I want to write kind notes, but it is too noisy. My letters look wiggly. I feel frustrated.” Roo’s ears folded.

Mia nodded. “That is a real problem. Let’s think.”

Roo took a deep breath. In-aaaand out. “I can solve this. I can use my words.”

Theo rolled by again with the squeaky cart. Roo hopped in front carefully and held up a paw. “Theo, can we talk?”

Theo stopped. “What’s up?”

Roo said, calm and kind, “I am writing welcome notes. The squeak-squeak and zoom-zoom make my letters wobbly. Can we make a plan so we both can work?”

Theo blinked. “I have to move the bags. But I can go slower near your table. I can push the cart on the rug so it won’t squeak as much.”

Roo smiled. “Thank you. I can also move my table closer to the soft corner by the blankets. It is quieter there.”

Mia clapped softly. “Great negotiation! You both shared, you both listened, and you found a plan.”

Together, they pushed Roo’s little table to a cozy corner with a big rug and a soft lamp. The fan sounded like a gentle breeze. The tape went rip less often here. Roo wiggled with relief.

Roo picked up a blue marker. “Dear Neighbor, you are brave.” Roo added a star and a smiley face. The letters were neat and clear. Roo giggled. “This feels good.”

A little girl named Lila came by, holding empty bags. “I can’t think what to write,” she said, shoulders slumping.

Roo patted the chair. “You can sit with me. Let’s think of kind words. We can use our senses.”

Roo closed eyes. “What do we hear when someone is kind?”

Lila listened. “Soft voices. ‘Welcome!’”

Roo wrote, “Welcome! We are glad you are here.”

“What do we see?” Roo asked.

“Smiles,” Lila said. “Waves with hands.”

Roo drew a smile and a waving paw. “What do we feel?”

“Warm hugs,” Lila whispered.

Roo wrote, “This bag is a hug in a bag.” Lila giggled.

Soon, a small stack of notes grew: “You matter.” “We can help.” “You are safe.” Roo added little hearts, suns, and paw prints. The markers smelled sweet like berries. Paper rustled happily.

Theo rolled by on the rug, quiet as a whisper. “How are the notes?”

Roo held one up. “Look! We wrote with love.”

Theo grinned. “Perfect for the bags.” He placed each note into a welcome bag. Swish, plop, swish, plop.

A new problem popped up. They were running out of paper. Only three sheets were left, and five bags were waiting.

Roo tapped a paw and thought. “We can share,” Roo said. “Let’s fold each paper in half and write two small notes on each. We can also write on both sides.”

Mia nodded. “Smart solution!”

They folded and wrote tiny, kind messages: “Hi, friend!” on one side, “We’re here!” on the other. Lila drew tiny stars in the corners. Roo’s neat letters danced across the page.

At last, the five bags were full. The basement felt warm and calm. Everyone smiled. Mia rang a little bell. Ding!

“Team,” Mia said, “thank you for volunteering today. Your writing will make new neighbors feel welcome.”

Roo’s chest felt big and happy. “We wrote with care. We solved problems with our words. We helped together.”

Theo gave a thumbs-up. “Your notes make the bags special.”

Lila hugged Roo. “You taught me how to write kind words.”

Roo hugged back gently. “Writing is like a hug you can read.”

Mia handed Roo a sticker shaped like a pencil. “For your great writing, smart negotiation, and kind volunteering.”

Roo stuck it on proudly. “I learned that when it’s busy, I can use my words, make a plan, and keep writing. Kind notes can help people feel safe and seen.”

Everyone cheered. The lights hummed softly. Roo packed up the markers, stacked the papers, and waved a happy paw.

“See you next time!” Roo called, hopping up the steps, tap-tap, thump-thump, heart full of joy and kind words.

The End.
