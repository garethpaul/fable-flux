# Nutkin's Big Building Project

Nutkin the squirrel loved to build things. He had built a cozy nest in the oak tree, a bridge from twigs, and even a tiny slide from smooth bark. Today, he wanted to build something very special.

"I know!" Nutkin chittered excitedly, his fluffy tail twitching. "I'll build the most amazing fort ever!"

Nutkin scampered through the house looking for the perfect spot. The kitchen was too busy. The living room had too much furniture. Then he found it – the bathroom! It was quiet, had smooth tiles, and best of all, nobody was using it right now.

Nutkin gathered his building materials: toilet paper rolls, a few towels, and some cups from the counter. He started stacking and arranging, his little paws working quickly.

"This will be the tallest tower in the whole house!" he said proudly, balancing a cup on top of three toilet paper rolls.

Just then, he heard footsteps. Oh no! Someone was coming to use the bathroom!

"Nutkin?" called Sarah, the little girl who lived in the house. "What are you doing in there?"

Nutkin's heart beat fast like a tiny drum. He could hide his building project and pretend nothing happened. But that wouldn't be honest, would it?

"Um... I'm building a fort," Nutkin said quietly through the door. "I'm sorry, Sarah. I know I should have asked first."

Sarah opened the door gently and peeked inside. Her eyes grew wide when she saw Nutkin's creation.

"Wow, Nutkin! That's really cool!" she said, kneeling down to look closer. "But I really need to brush my teeth for bedtime."

Nutkin's ears drooped. His beautiful fort would have to come down. "I understand," he sighed. "I'll clean it up right away."

"Wait!" Sarah said, her face lighting up with an idea. "What if we work together? Maybe we can find a way for both of us to be happy."

Nutkin's tail perked up. "Really? How?"

Sarah tapped her finger on her chin, thinking. "Well, I only need the sink area to brush my teeth. What if you moved your fort to that corner by the bathtub? Then we could both use the bathroom!"

"That's a great idea!" Nutkin squeaked with joy. "We can compromise!"

Together, they carefully moved Nutkin's fort piece by piece to the corner. Sarah even helped by handing him the toilet paper rolls.

"You know what would make this fort even better?" Sarah asked, her eyes twinkling.

"What?" Nutkin asked curiously.

"A flag!" Sarah reached for a small washcloth and helped Nutkin attach it to the highest point of his fort.

As Sarah brushed her teeth, she watched Nutkin play in his new fort. The soft washcloth flag fluttered gently in the air from the bathroom fan.

"Thank you for being honest with me, Nutkin," Sarah said through her toothpaste bubbles. "And thank you for being willing to compromise."

"Thank you for helping me build an even better fort!" Nutkin replied happily. "I learned that being honest and working together makes everything more fun!"

From that day on, Nutkin always asked before starting his building projects. And Sarah always tried to find ways they could both be happy. The bathroom fort became their special place to share stories and laugh together.

The End.
