# Pippa Penguin on the Rooftop

Pippa was a cheerful penguin with a bright yellow scarf. She lived in a tall city with shiny windows and gentle breezes. One sunny morning, Pippa waddled up the stairs to the rooftop garden. The rooftop felt cool under her little feet. The sky smelled like clean rain and flowers. Birds chirped, “Tweet, tweet!” and the wind whispered, “Whooo.”

Pippa smiled. “Good morning, rooftop!” she sang. She watered the plants with a small blue can. Splash, patter, splash! The leaves glittered with tiny drops. Pippa loved helping the garden. She loved helping friends too.

Soon, friends arrived. Bella the bunny hopped. Tito the turtle clacked on the tiles. Mei the mouse scurried with a squeak. “Hi, Pippa!” they said.

“Hi!” said Pippa. “Let’s take care of the garden together.”

They picked up tiny bits of paper from the corner. They put bottles in the blue bin and apple cores in the green bin. “We are helping the Earth,” Pippa said. “Little bits make a big difference!”

“Little bits make a big difference,” everyone echoed.

Then two pigeons fluttered down. Flap-flap! “Coo, coo!” They were messy and friendly. One pigeon pecked Pippa’s scarf. “Coo? Share scarf?” it asked.

Pippa giggled. “I can share a cuddle,” she said, patting its soft feathers. “But my scarf keeps me warm. I need it.”

The pigeon cooed, “Okay,” and cuddled Pippa’s wing instead.

Soon more friends came. Loopy the puppy wagged her tail. Swish-swish! “Play tag?” Loopy barked.

Pippa looked at the plants. Her tummy felt a little tight, like a small knot. She had watered, cleaned, and cuddled. She had not had a rest or a sip of water. Her feet felt tired. Her beak felt dry.

“I love to play,” Pippa said, “but I need a short break. My body says, ‘Rest, please.’”

Loopy paused. “Okay! I can wait,” she barked, wagging slower.

Pippa sat on a soft mat. She took deep belly breaths. In… and out… like waves. She sipped cool water. Sip, sip. She nibbled a crunchy carrot stick. Crunch!

Bella sat beside her. “Is your tummy knot better?” asked Bella.

“Yes,” Pippa said. “Thank you for asking. I am listening to my body.”

Tito nodded. “That is good self-care,” he said slowly.

Just then, Mei tugged Pippa’s flipper. “Help me carry this big watering can?”

Pippa looked at the heavy can. Her wings felt wobbly. “I can help after I finish my rest,” she said kindly. “Right now I need to be gentle with my body.”

Mei blinked. “Okay,” she squeaked. “I will use the small can for now.”

The sun moved across the sky. The rooftop grew warm and smelled like mint and tomatoes. A cloud drifted by like a fluffy sheep. Pippa stretched and stood up. “I feel ready,” she said. “Let’s play and help!”

They played a calm game called Breeze Tag. “Touch a leaf, then tag a friend,” Pippa said. They moved slowly so the plants stayed safe. The leaves tickled their paws and flippers. Everyone giggled.

Loopy barked, “Best game!”

After the game, Pippa saw a corner with dry soil. “The marigolds need a drink,” she said.

Mei held the small can. “I can do this one,” said Mei.

“Thank you,” Pippa said. “I will carry the empty pot to the bin.” Tito carried the lid. Bella clapped.

A tall gull landed with a loud “Squawk!” It strutted and said, “I like big noise and fast flaps! Let’s flap fast! Move, move!”

Pippa smiled. “We all like different things,” she said. “I like gentle games. The plants need calm. You can flap over there.” She pointed to the far side where there were no pots.

The gull blinked, then grinned. “I can flap over there. Thanks!” It whooshed to the open space and flapped happily. Whoosh-whoosh!

Pippa felt proud. She set a boundary with kind words. She kept the garden safe. She kept her body calm.

When the sun began to set, the sky turned peach and purple. The air smelled like sweet basil. Everyone sat in a circle. Pippa shared orange slices. “I can share my snack,” she said, “and I can also save one for me.”

“That is generous and smart,” said Tito. “You are caring for us and for you.”

Pippa nodded. “When I rest, drink water, and use kind words, I feel strong. Then I can help my friends and the Earth.”

Bella hugged Pippa gently. “You listened to your body, and you told us what you need.”

Loopy wagged. “We listened too!”

The pigeons cooed. The gull gave one last whoosh and waved a wing from the far side. The garden plants looked happy and tall. The rooftop felt peaceful and cozy.

Pippa wrapped her scarf snug and smiled. “Today I learned: I can be kind to others, and I can be kind to myself. That is self-care. I can share, and I can say, ‘Not now,’ or, ‘Over there,’ to keep me and the garden safe.”

Everyone cheered softly, “Hooray for self-care! Hooray for caring for the Earth!”

They watched the first star sparkle. Ting! Pippa took a deep breath. In… and out… Her tummy felt soft and calm. She felt ready for tomorrow.

The End.
