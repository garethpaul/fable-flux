# Miko's Magic Writing Adventure

Miko the monkey loved to swing and play all day long. His fur was brown and fuzzy, and his tail was extra long for swinging from tree to tree. But there was one thing Miko didn't like to do - wash his hands before eating.

"Miko, time for lunch!" called Mama Monkey from the dining room.

Miko swung down from his favorite branch and ran straight to the big wooden table. The dining room smelled like sweet bananas and crunchy nuts. His tummy rumbled loudly.

"Wait, little one," said Mama Monkey gently. "Show me your hands first."

Miko held up his hands. They were covered in dirt and sticky tree sap from climbing all morning. His fingers were muddy brown.

"Oh my! Those hands need washing before we eat," Mama said with a smile.

"But I'm hungry NOW!" Miko said, bouncing up and down. "Washing takes too long!"

Mama Monkey had an idea. She pulled out a special notebook and a big crayon from the dining room drawer.

"Miko, would you like to learn something magical?" she asked.

Miko's eyes grew wide and curious. "Magic? Really?"

"Yes! But first, let's wash those hands so they're clean and ready for magic."

Miko thought about this. Magic sounded very exciting. He followed Mama to wash his hands with warm, soapy water. The bubbles felt tickly and fun. He scrubbed between each finger until they were squeaky clean.

Back at the dining room table, Mama opened the notebook to a fresh, white page.

"Now for the magic," she said, placing the crayon in Miko's clean hand. "This is called writing. Watch!"

Mama showed Miko how to hold the crayon. She helped him make a big letter 'M' on the paper.

"That's the first letter of your name!" Mama said. "M for Miko!"

Miko stared at the wobbly line he had made. It looked like a mountain with two peaks. "I made that?" he asked, amazed.

"You did! And clean hands make the best writing. Dirty hands would make smudgy marks."

Miko looked at his clean hands, then at the letter on the paper. He felt proud and excited.

"Can I make more letters?" he asked.

"After lunch," Mama said. "Clean hands, good food, then more writing magic."

As they ate their delicious lunch, Miko kept looking at his letter 'M' on the paper. He had discovered something wonderful - he could make marks that meant something special!

"Mama," Miko said, chewing his banana carefully, "will you teach me to write my whole name?"

"Of course! And remember, we always wash our hands first. Clean hands help us do our best work."

From that day on, Miko always washed his hands before eating and before writing. He learned that taking care of himself helped him discover new and exciting things. Soon, he could write his whole name: M-I-K-O.

The dining room became Miko's favorite place to practice his new writing skills. And he never forgot that the best discoveries come when we take good care of ourselves first.

"Clean hands, clear mind, beautiful writing!" became Miko's special saying.

The End.
