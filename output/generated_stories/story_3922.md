# Cubby Helps in the Warm Kitchen

Cubby was a friendly bear cub with soft, fuzzy paws and a curious nose. One sunny morning, he padded into the warm kitchen. The air smelled like sweet berries and fresh bread. The oven hummed softly, and a pot bubbled on the stove. The floor felt cozy under Cubby’s feet, and golden light shone on the wooden table.

“Mama, can I help with chores?” Cubby asked, his eyes bright.

Mama Bear smiled and wiped her paws on a towel. “Yes, Cubby. Helping is kind and brave. But we must be safe. Safety first.”

Cubby puffed up with pride. “I can do it!”

Mama Bear pointed to the stove. “The stove is very hot,” she said. “Hot things can hurt. We keep paws away.”

Cubby nodded. “Away from hot.”

Mama Bear pointed to a sharp butter knife on the counter. “Sharp things can poke. We do not touch without Mama.”

“Not without Mama,” Cubby repeated.

Mama Bear gave Cubby a little apron and a soft cloth. “You can wipe the table and sort the spoons. That is a helpful chore. We will use our safety rules.”

Cubby’s nose twitched with excitement. He liked the smell of the wooden spoons, warm and a little nutty. He liked the smooth feel of the table under his cloth. Swish, swish went the cloth. Clink, clink went the spoons.

“I am good at chores,” Cubby said. “I am big!”

“You are growing,” Mama Bear said. “Being careful shows you are smart and strong.”

The pot on the stove went bubble, bubble, bubble. Cubby looked over and tiptoed closer. Steam rose like a tiny white cloud. It smelled like carrots and herbs. His tummy gave a happy rumble.

“I want to stir!” Cubby said. He reached out a paw.

Mama Bear gently held his paw. “Let’s stop,” she said in a calm voice. “What is our rule?”

Cubby paused. He looked at the steam, at the shiny pot, at Mama’s caring eyes. “Hot things can hurt,” he said softly. “Paws away.”

“That’s right,” Mama Bear said. “We can be independent and still ask for help. Being independent means trying, and being open-minded means listening and learning. Let’s find a safe way.”

Cubby’s ears perked up. “A safe way?”

Mama Bear set a sturdy stool far from the hot stove, where Cubby could watch but not reach. She placed a cool wooden spoon in his paw. “You can pretend to stir in this cool bowl,” she said, giving him a bowl of cool water and a few carrot slices. “Swish the spoon and practice.”

Cubby grinned. He swished the spoon gently. Swish, swish, plop! A carrot slice fell with a splash. He giggled. “I am practicing! It is fun!”

“You are learning,” Mama Bear said. “That is very safe.”

Next, Mama Bear brought a basket of clean cloths. “Would you like to fold these?” she asked.

“I can fold!” Cubby said. He pressed his paws on the warm cloths. They smelled like sunshine and soap. He folded one carefully. It looked like a crumpled pancake.

Mama Bear chuckled kindly. “Open your mind, little cub. Try a new way.” She showed him how to fold in half, then in half again. “Like a book,” she said.

Cubby tried again. Fold, pat, fold, pat. “Like a book,” he whispered. This time it looked neat. “I did it!”

“You did,” Mama Bear said. “Chores can make us feel proud.”

Just then, the kettle began to whistle. Wheeeeee! Cubby jumped. He took a step toward it.

Mama Bear raised a gentle paw. “Stop, think, stay back,” she said. “Safety first.”

Cubby stopped. He took a small step back. “Stop, think, stay back,” he repeated. “I remember.”

“Good job,” Mama Bear said. She turned off the stove and moved the kettle with an oven mitt. “We use tools to be safe.”

Cubby looked around the warm kitchen. It glowed with light. It smelled like tea and soup. He felt calm and happy.

“Mama, can I do another chore?” Cubby asked.

“You may,” said Mama Bear. She opened a low drawer and pulled out a soft brush and a little dustpan. “You can sweep crumbs under the table. Keep your paws on the floor. No climbing on chairs, please.”

“No climbing,” Cubby said. He brushed, brush, brushed the crumbs. They made a tiny rustle. He scooped them into the dustpan. “All clean!”

Mama Bear clapped softly. “Safe, helpful, and independent. You did it.”

Cubby tilted his head. “Can I try a new chore tomorrow?”

“Yes,” Mama Bear said. “We will try new things with open minds and safe paws.”

Cubby’s tummy rumbled again. “May I taste the soup?”

Mama Bear nodded. She poured a little soup into a cool bowl and waited. She touched it with her paw. “Warm, not hot,” she said.

Cubby took a tiny spoonful. “Mmm,” he said. “It tastes like cozy.”

Mama Bear smiled. “Today you learned our safety rules: Keep paws away from hot. Do not touch sharp things. Stop, think, stay back. Ask for help. Use tools. Try new ways with an open mind.”

Cubby hugged Mama Bear. “Safety first,” he said proudly. “I can help and be safe.”

“Yes,” Mama Bear said. “When we are safe, we can do our chores, learn new things, and feel strong.”

Cubby looked around the warm kitchen, breathing in the gentle steam and bread smell. He felt brave, careful, and loved.

“I am a helper,” he whispered.

“You are,” Mama Bear said. “And a safe one.”

The End.
