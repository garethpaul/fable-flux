# Squeaky’s Big Table Plan

Squeaky the resourceful squirrel peeped in through the dining room window. The morning sun painted warm squares on the floor. The long table smelled like toast and honey. Chairs stood like tall trees, and a shiny spoon glimmered like a little moon.

“My tummy is rumbling,” Squeaky said, patting his round belly. “I will find a good snack!”

He wiggled through a tiny crack and landed softly on the rug. His tail poofed like a feather duster. He heard footsteps. Tap, tap, tap.

Mia and Max, two siblings, ran into the dining room. Mia held a red ribbon. Max held a blue ball. They both stopped and stared at Squeaky.

“Hi, little squirrel,” Mia whispered. “You’re fluffy like my pillow.”

“Hi!” said Max. “Do you want to play?”

Squeaky’s ears perked. “I like to move, and I like to plan!” he chirped. “But first, I need a snack. Then, we can play.”

Mia pointed at a bowl of apples on the table. “We want to play Picnic Parade under the table,” she said.

Max shook his head. “No, I want to play Rolling Rivers with my ball.”

Their voices grew louder. “Picnic Parade!” said Mia.

“Rolling Rivers!” said Max.

Squeaky blinked. His tummy gave another loud rumble. “Hmm,” he said. “Two ideas. One table. We need a plan.”

Mia crossed her arms. Max bounced his ball. The room felt tight and cranky.

Squeaky took a deep breath. He remembered what he learned in his tree home: when tails get tense, make a calm plan.

“Let’s sit,” Squeaky said. He pattered across the rug, tail swishing. “We can make a choice together.”

Mia sat. Max sat. Squeaky held up three tiny fingers. “We can:
- Try Mia’s game first, then Max’s game.
- Mix both games into one.
- Or pick a new game that uses both a ribbon and a ball.”

Mia looked at her ribbon. Max looked at his ball.

Squeaky nodded. “A good plan is fair. A good plan feels kind.”

He pointed at the table. “Listen to the sounds,” he said. The room hummed with the gentle tick-tick of a clock. A soft breeze slipped through the window and smelled like soap and toast. “We can take turns. We can move our bodies. We can all have fun.”

Mia sighed. “I want to go first.”

Max frowned. “I want to go first, too.”

Squeaky tapped his chin. “Let’s move to decide!” he said, smiling. “We will do a Wiggle Vote. We wiggle for ten counts. Whoever stops wiggling last goes first. Ready?”

Mia giggled. “Ready.”

Max grinned. “Ready!”

“Wiggle, wiggle!” sang Squeaky. He wiggled his hips. He wiggled his tail. Mia wiggled her fingers. Max wiggled his knees. They counted together. “One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten!”

Mia stopped first. Max stopped last. Squeaky clapped his little paws. “Max goes first. Then Mia. That is our compromise. We all helped decide.”

Max nodded. “Okay, I can wait for Mia’s turn.”

Mia nodded, too. “Okay, I can wait for Max’s turn.”

“Good choice,” said Squeaky. His tummy rumbled again. “But snack first. I need energy to play.”

Squeaky looked around. The chairs were tall. The apples were far away. He sniffed. He smelled sweet apples and sticky honey. He needed a plan.

“Movement time,” he said. He hopped to a chair leg. He climbed up, little claws clicking. Click-click-click. He leaped to the seat, then to the table. His tail swooshed for balance. “I did it!”

Squeaky rolled a small apple toward the edge. “Heads up!” he called. Mia held her hands out. Max held his. Squeaky guided the apple down the table leg like a tiny slide. The apple plopped into their hands with a soft thump.

“Teamwork!” said Mia.

“Good plan!” said Max.

They cut slices with help from Dad, who poked his head in. “Nice sharing, everyone,” Dad said, smiling. “Carry on.”

After the snack, Max went first. “Rolling Rivers!” he said. He gently rolled the blue ball under the table. The ball swished over the rug like water. Squeaky scampered after it, paws tapping. Tap-tap! He dodged chair legs like little bridges. Mia held the red ribbon like a finish line. “Splash!” she cheered when Squeaky crossed.

Then it was Mia’s turn. “Picnic Parade!” she said. She spread a small napkin and lined up apple slices. Squeaky marched with tiny steps, left-right, left-right. Max drummed on the table leg with soft beats: dum-dum-dum. The room smelled like fresh apples and sounded like a happy band.

They played both games. They moved their bodies. They felt calm and proud.

Squeaky stretched with a yawn. “We did it,” he said. “We made a plan. We made a fair choice. We took turns.”

Mia smiled. “Compromise means we share and we listen.”

Max nodded. “And we can decide with kind moves, like the Wiggle Vote.”

Squeaky’s tail curled into a comfy C. “When we work together,” he said, “everyone wins.”

The sun made golden spots on the floor. The spoon still shone like a tiny moon. The dining room felt warm, like a hug.

Squeaky waved his paw. “Thank you for playing,” he said.

“Thank you for helping,” Mia and Max said.

They all smiled, full of apples and good ideas.

The End.
