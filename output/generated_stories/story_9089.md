# Benny Beaver’s Bright Idea at the Dinner Table

Benny Beaver was a very busy beaver. He loved to build, fix, and make things better. One rainy afternoon, Benny trotted into the dining room. His tail thumped softly on the floor. Tap, tap. He sniffed the air. It smelled like warm carrot soup and crusty bread. His tummy gave a happy rumble.

Mama Beaver called, “Dinner time, Benny!”

Benny washed his paws with warm, soapy water. He sang, “Scrub-a-dub-dub, soap makes suds!” The bubbles smelled like sweet apples. His paws felt clean and tingly.

Benny hurried to his chair. He saw a tall stack of napkins, a big bowl of soup, and shiny forks and spoons. The candles on the table flickered. “Ooo,” Benny said. “So sparkly!”

Mama smiled. “We will not use the candles tonight, Benny. They are just for looking. Safety first.”

Benny nodded. “Safety first!”

He climbed into his chair, but his tail bumped the chair leg. Wobble, wobble! The spoon rattled. The soup swished. Benny’s eyes grew wide. “Oops!”

Mama steadied the bowl. “Slow and steady, dear.”

Benny sat still. He picked up his spoon. He wanted to scoop fast. He loved soup. It smelled like carrots and cozy hugs. But the steam was hot. The steam tickled his nose. He pulled back. “Hot!” he said. “Too hot.”

Mama blew gently over the bowl. “We wait,” she said. “We blow. We sip slowly.”

Benny blew. “Fooo.” He watched the steam float away like tiny clouds. He took a small sip. “Mmm!” His tummy felt warm and safe.

He looked around the dining room. He noticed the napkins were tall and tippy. He noticed the pitcher was near the edge. He noticed his chair was wobbly. His beaver brain began to buzz. “I want to help our table be safe,” he said. “I want to make a bright idea!”

Mama tilted her head. “A bright idea?”

Benny nodded. “Yes! An idea that keeps us safe and healthy.”

He hopped down carefully. Tap, tap went his tail. He pushed his chair in. “Chairs go close, not out,” he said. “Then no one trips.”

He found small wooden blocks in the toy basket by the door. He liked to build dams with them. He set two blocks under the back legs of his chair. “No wobble!” he said. He gave the seat a gentle wiggle. Steady and strong.

Mama watched. “Clever, Benny.”

Benny moved the pitcher away from the edge. “Pitcher goes in the middle,” he said. He moved the napkins into a low, wide basket. “Napkins sit low and safe.”

He looked at the candles. The little flames danced. “Candles can be pretty,” he said, “but they can be tricky.” He gently blew them out. Puff! The room smelled like a tiny campfire. He smiled. “We can use the glow lamp instead.” He clicked on a small, soft lamp. It made the room feel warm and calm.

Mama clapped softly. “What a smart beaver you are.”

Benny got his mitts—the oven mitts with red apples on them. He put them on his paws. “Hot things need mitts,” he said. He carefully moved the bread basket. The crust felt warm and crunchy through the mitts. “Safe and yummy.”

He went back to his seat and sat tall. He folded his napkin on his lap. “I am ready,” he said. “I will eat slow. I will chew well. I will sip water, too.”

Mama set out cups. “Small sips,” she said. The water was cool and made a tiny clink as it touched the glass.

Benny took a small sip. “Ahh.” He grinned. “My mouth feels happy.”

They began to eat. Benny scooped small spoonfuls. He blew. He tasted. He chewed. Crunch, crunch went the crusty bread. Slurp went the soup. He used his napkin to dab his chin. He felt proud and calm. His tummy felt good.

Suddenly, he heard a little patter of feet. Baby Bella Beaver toddled in. She reached for the shiny spoon near the edge. “Oooh!” she said.

Benny held up a gentle paw. “Stop, Bella. Look with your eyes, not your hands. The spoon can fall.”

Bella blinked. She looked at Benny. She looked at Mama. Mama nodded. “Listen to your brother. Safety first.”

Benny smiled. He bent down so he was eye level with Bella. “We keep things in the middle,” he said. “We sit to eat. We go slow. We blow on hot food.” He blew a gentle puff to show her. “Fooo.”

Bella giggled. “Fooo!”

Benny moved the spoon to the center. “Safe and sound,” he said.

He looked around the dining room one more time. The chair was steady. The napkins were low. The pitcher was in the middle. The soft lamp glowed. Everyone was sitting. Everyone was calm. It felt good—like a big, warm beaver hug.

Mama lifted her cup. “To Benny’s bright idea,” she said. “You used your eyes, your brain, and your heart. You made our dinner safe, and that is healthy for our bodies.”

Benny lifted his cup, too. “Safety first,” he said. “Then slow, then kind.”

After dinner, Benny helped clean up. He wiped the table with a damp cloth. It smelled lemony and fresh. He washed his paws again—scrub-a-dub-dub. He put the blocks back in the basket. He pushed in the chairs. He felt proud, like a builder who finished a strong, safe dam.

Mama gave him a gentle squeeze. “What did we learn today?”

Benny beamed. “We learned to look before we do, to keep hot and sharp things away, to sit while we eat, to chew slow, and to keep things in the middle. We learned that being safe is a smart habit!”

Mama smiled. “And smart habits keep us healthy.”

Benny’s tail made a happy tap, tap. “I like my bright ideas,” he said. “Safety first, always.”

The End.
