# Bear Cub Bounce in the Treehouse

Sunny Bear was a friendly bear cub with soft, fuzzy fur and bright, curious eyes. His treehouse stood high in a big, leafy tree. The wood smelled like warm pine. A gentle breeze made the leaves whisper, shhh-shhh, and little birds chirped, peep-peep, nearby.

“Today is a move-and-groove day!” Sunny Bear said, clapping his paws. “I want to exercise. I want to stretch, hop, and run!”

Sunny climbed the ladder—step, step, step—and reached the treehouse floor. It felt smooth under his paws. He took a deep breath. The air smelled like sweet apples from a basket in the corner.

Sunny rolled out a soft, blue mat. “First, I stretch,” he said. He reached up high, high, high. “Hello, sky!” He touched his toes. “Hello, toes!” He twisted side to side. “Wiggle-wiggle!”

Knock, knock!

Sunny opened the little door. His friends were there: Pip the chipmunk, Dot the duckling, and Tilly the tiny turtle.

“Hi, friends!” Sunny said. “Let’s exercise together!”

Pip squeaked, “I can run super fast!”

Dot flapped her wings. “I can flap-flap and hop!”

Tilly smiled slowly. “I can go steady and strong.”

Sunny nodded. “We will all move. We will all have fun. We will all be fair.”

They stepped inside. The treehouse smelled like pine and apples and a little bit like sunshine. The floor creaked, creak-creak, in a friendly way.

“First game,” Sunny said. “The Bounce-and-Count!”

He drew a big circle on the floor with a piece of chalk. “We take turns. We bounce and count to five. Fair turns for everyone.”

Pip hopped in first. “Boing, boing, boing!” he bounced. “One, two, three, four, five!” He giggled and fell on the soft mat. “Hee-hee!”

Dot went next. “Hop, hop, hop,” she peeped. “One, two, three, four, five!” Her little feet made soft thumps on the wood. Thump-thump!

Then Tilly’s turn. Tilly moved slow. “Boop,” she bounced just a little. “One… two… three… four… five.” Sunny and friends clapped, clap-clap-clap! Tilly beamed. “I did it!”

Sunny hopped in last. “Boing! One, two, three, four, five!” His ears felt warm, and his heart went thump-thump in a happy way. Exercise felt good.

“Next,” Sunny said, “the Ladder Lift!” They lined up by the small rope ladder inside the treehouse. “We climb one at a time. Then we cheer!”

Pip zipped up and down—zip-zip! Dot flapped and stepped—flap-step, flap-step! Tilly climbed slow and steady—grip, step, grip, step. Sunny waited and cheered for each friend. “Go Pip! Go Dot! Go Tilly!”

When Tilly reached the top, she puffed, “I did it!” Sunny smiled. “We all did it.”

Now they wanted a big race. Pip’s whiskers twitched. “Let’s race around the treehouse! I can do ten laps!”

Sunny looked at the small space. The treehouse was cozy, not big. If they all ran fast, they might bump. Bump-bump was not safe or fair.

Sunny rubbed his chin. He heard the leaves whisper, shhh-shhh. He smelled apples again. He had an idea.

“Problem to solve,” Sunny said. “We need a safe race. We need a fair race.”

“How?” asked Dot.

“Hmm,” said Tilly.

Sunny pointed to the chalk. “We make a track circle. We go one at a time. We count our laps. We cheer! After each friend runs, we switch. That is fair.”

Pip’s tail twitched. “But I want to go fast now!”

Sunny nodded kindly. “I know. Waiting is hard. But fair is sharing turns. And safety first. We can also add a fun challenge!”

“What challenge?” Dot asked.

Sunny drew little pictures on the track. A star at one spot. A heart at another. A smiley face at another. “When you pass a star, do two tiptoe steps. When you pass a heart, flap your arms. When you pass a smiley, do a tiny spin!”

Pip grinned. “Okay! That is fun!”

They started. Pip ran first. Pitter-patter, pitter-patter. “One lap!” he called. He tiptoed at the star—tip-tip. He flapped at the heart—flap-flap. He made a small spin at the smiley—whee! He did three laps, then slowed. “I’m done.” His cheeks were pink.

Dot went next. Hop-hop, peep-peep. She tiptoed, she flapped, she spun. “Two laps!” she chirped. “I had fun!”

Tilly went third. She moved gentle and steady. “Lap one,” she said softly. Tiptoe, flap, spin. “Lap two.” The friends cheered, “Go Tilly!” Her smile was big.

Sunny went last. He felt the floor under his paws and the breeze on his face. Tiptoe, flap, spin. “Three laps!” he said.

They all sat on the mat to rest. Their hearts went thump-thump, happy and tired. Sunny passed out apple slices. Crunch! The apples were sweet and juicy. Dot sipped water. Pip fanned himself. Tilly patted her shell.

“That was great exercise,” Sunny said. “My body feels strong. My heart feels happy.”

Pip nodded. “I liked the track and the turns. Fair is fun!”

Dot peeped, “I liked the tiptoes and spins. Exercise can be silly!”

Tilly said, “I liked going steady. I did my best.”

Sunny smiled. “We stretched. We bounced. We climbed. We ran. We solved a problem. We were fair.”

He looked around the bright treehouse. The sun made golden spots on the floor. The air was warm and kind. “Let’s remember our lesson,” Sunny said. “Exercise helps our bodies and our hearts. We can solve problems with calm ideas. And we can be fair by taking turns and cheering for friends.”

Pip raised a paw. “Tomorrow, can we do more?”

Sunny laughed. “Yes! We will move and groove again. Together.”

They put away the mat, wiped the chalk marks, and waved to the chirping birds. The leaves whispered, shhh-shhh, like a happy song.

Sunny Bear felt proud. He hugged his friends. “Thank you for being fair. Thank you for trying. Thank you for exercising with me.”

“Thank you, Sunny!” they all said.

The End.
