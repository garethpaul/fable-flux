# Melody in the Car Seat

The family car was shiny and blue. It smelled like orange slices and clean seats. Sunlight made tiny stars on the windows. Inside the car sat a small, bright bird named Melody. Melody was a singing bird with soft green feathers and a tiny yellow beak. She rode in a comfy travel cage, buckled in with a click. The seat belt made a gentle hug around her cage.

“Chirp-chirp! La-la-la!” sang Melody. Her voice sounded like warm tea and happy bells.

In the front seat sat Mama, holding the steering wheel. In the back seat sat twins, Sam and Sana. They wore their seat belts too. Sam held a toy truck. Sana hugged a blue bear.

“We are going to visit Grandma!” said Mama. “We will take turns with music. We will use kind words and keep safe.”

Melody wiggled her little claws and took a deep breath. She loved to sing. Singing helped her feel calm. Singing was her self-care. “Chirp-chirp! I sing when I’m happy. I sing when I’m scared,” she whispered.

The car hummed down the road. Trees swished by. The air smelled like rain. Melody sang a soft song, like a quiet river. Sana smiled. “I like the soft song,” she said.

Sam scrunched his eyebrows. “I want my truck to vroom,” he said. He made a loud vroom-vroom sound. It sounded like thunder in a tin can.

Melody tried to sing louder. “Laaaa-laa—”

“VROOOOM!” shouted Sam.

“Sam, please lower your voice,” said Mama. “We share the space. We keep everyone safe by staying calm.”

Sam crossed his arms. “I want my turn!”

Melody’s heart beat fast. Her feathers felt prickly. She wanted to sing, but the loud vroom made her tummy twist. She took a slow breath in and out. In. Out. Self-care, she thought. Calm helps me.

“Sana,” said Mama, “do you have an idea?”

Sana patted her blue bear. “We can make turns,” she said. “Melody sings for one song. Then Sam does quiet vrooms for one song.”

“That is fair,” said Mama. “Fair means justice. Everyone gets a turn and is treated with care.”

Sam frowned. “But I want a long turn.”

Melody looked at him. “Sam,” she chirped gently, “my ears are small. Loud sounds hurt me. Can we find a way that is safe for me and fun for you?” She opened her wings a little. “I feel scared when it is too loud.”

Sam’s eyes softened. “You feel scared?”

“Yes,” said Melody. “When it is quiet, I feel safe. When it is safe, I can sing a happy song for everyone.”

Sam looked out the window. The sky was bright and blue. He took a breath, just like Melody. In. Out. “I can try a quiet vroom,” he said. He held his truck close and made a tiny vroom, like a sleepy bee.

“Thank you for caring,” said Mama. “That is kind. That is just and safe.”

Melody chirped a cheerful tune. “Chirp-chirp, la-la!” Her song sounded like sun on water. Sana clapped softly with her fingertips.

After one song, Melody stopped. “Now Sam’s turn,” she said. “I will rest my voice. Rest is self-care.”

Sam smiled. He made gentle vroom sounds, smooth and soft. Sana added a whispery “whoosh” like wind. Mama tapped the steering wheel, tap-tap, like a drum far away.

But soon, a new problem came. A red car zoomed past and honked loudly. HONK! Melody jumped. Her feathers puffed up. “Eeep!” she squeaked.

“I do not feel safe,” said Melody. “I need a pause.”

Mama nodded. “Safety first. We can all take a quiet moment. We will use our calm breaths.”

Everyone breathed together. In. Out. The car felt softer again, like a blanket.

“Melody,” asked Sana, “do you want help?”

“Yes, please,” said Melody. “Can you cover part of my cage with the small blanket? It makes a cozy shade.”

Sana gently placed the tiny blanket over the top, leaving a little window. “How is that?” she asked.

“It is perfect,” said Melody. “Thank you.”

“Now,” said Mama, “let’s make a plan so everyone is included.”

Sana held up her fingers. “One: Melody sings one song. Two: Sam does quiet vrooms one song. Three: We all breathe if things feel too loud. Four: If someone feels scared, we pause and help.”

“That plan sounds fair,” said Mama. “It keeps us safe and includes everyone.”

They tried the plan. Melody sang a gentle rainbow song. Sam made soft road sounds. Sana added a tiny “ding” like a bike bell. Mama smiled and drove safely.

At a stoplight, the car was calm and happy. The air smelled sweet again, like oranges. Melody felt warm inside. She had used her voice to say what she needed. She had listened to Sam too. They found a way that was fair.

“Thank you, friends,” said Melody. “Today we learned to solve a problem with kind words. We listened. We took turns. We kept safe. We cared for our feelings. That is justice for hearts.”

Sam nodded. “I can make quiet vrooms.”

Sana patted her bear. “And I can help with cozy shade.”

Mama said, “And I can guide with gentle rules.”

Melody chirped one last soft note, like a kiss of light. “We are a team,” she sang.

The End.
