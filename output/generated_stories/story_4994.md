# Rooftop Rescue with Rolo the Robot Dog

Rolo was a helpful robot dog. He had shiny silver paws that clicked on the tiles. He had big soft ears that glowed blue. His nose made little beeps when he sniffed. Beep-beep! Rolo loved to help.

One sunny morning, Rolo trotted onto the rooftop garden. The rooftop smelled like flowers and warm bread from the bakery below. The wind was gentle and felt like a hug.

“Good morning!” said Mia, waving. She was small, with curly hair and bright eyes. She was wearing a yellow hat.

“Good morning!” said Sam, who liked tools and wore a red cap.

“Good morning!” Rolo beeped, wagging his little tail light. “How can I help?”

Mia pointed to a big cardboard rocket they were building for Space Day at school. It was tall and wobbly. It had shiny stars glued on the sides. But the rocket door was crooked, and the flag on top kept falling down.

“It’s not working,” Mia said with a sigh.

“It keeps falling,” Sam said, frowning.

Rolo sat down and wiggled his ears. “We can fix it. We can work together,” he said. “Cooperation means helping each other and listening.”

Mia and Sam nodded.

Rolo tapped his nose. Beep! “First, we follow directions. Let’s read the plan.”

Sam held up a paper with big pictures. “Step one: make the base strong. Step two: attach the door. Step three: put the flag on top.”

Mia clapped. “Okay! Step one.”

They all knelt by the cardboard base. The tile felt warm on their knees. Rolo held the base steady with his paws. Mia pressed tape along the edges. Rip! Stick! Sam pressed the corners. Push, push.

“Good job, team,” Rolo said. His ears glowed blue.

A gust of wind swished by. Whoosh! The rocket wiggled.

“It’s okay,” said Rolo. “We are strong together.”

“Step two,” Sam said. “Attach the door.”

Mia picked the door up. It was a little heavy. “I can do it,” she said.

“I can do it better,” Sam said, grabbing one side.

Mia pulled back. Sam pulled too. The door bent. The glue dripped. Plop.

Rolo beeped. “Pause.”

Mia and Sam looked at Rolo.

“Let’s try cooperation,” Rolo said softly. “We can share. We can take turns. We can be gentle.”

Mia took a deep breath. “Okay. Sam, you hold. I will glue.”

Sam nodded. “Yes. I will hold very still.”

Rolo smiled. “I will count. One… two… three. Gentle hands.”

Mia squeezed a little glue—squish—and pressed the door. Sam held it steady. Rolo beeped a happy sound. “We are following directions!”

The door stayed straight. Mia grinned. Sam grinned too.

“Step three,” Sam read. “Put the flag on top.”

The flag was blue with a gold star. The pole was long and wiggly. The top of the rocket was high.

“I am small,” Mia said.

“I am tall,” Sam said. “I can do it.”

Mia felt a little sad. She wanted to help.

Rolo noticed. His ears glowed soft. “We can all help,” he said. “Sam, you hold the pole. Mia, you hand him the ribbon. I will steady the rocket.”

“Okay,” Mia said, smiling again.

Sam stood on a small step stool. The wind blew stronger now. Whoosh! The rocket swayed. Rolo pressed his paws on the base. “Steady, steady,” he said.

Mia held the ribbon. It felt smooth and cool. “Here you go, Sam.”

Sam reached up. His hands shook a little. “It’s wobbly,” he said.

Rolo spoke gently. “Let’s use calm voices. Breathe in… and out.”

They all breathed together. In… and out. The wind felt kinder. The rooftop was quiet for a moment. Even the pigeons cooed softly. Coo, coo.

“Try again,” Rolo said. “Slow and careful.”

Sam set the flag on the tip. Mia tied the ribbon in a bow. Loop, swoop, pull. The bow looked neat and snug.

The flag fluttered. Flap, flap. But it stayed on.

“We did it!” Mia cheered.

“We did it together,” Sam said.

Rolo wagged. “That is cooperation. We listened. We shared. We took turns. We followed directions.”

Just then, a small voice peeped from the door to the roof. It was Lina from next door. She had a green scarf and big shy eyes.

“I want to play too,” Lina whispered. “But I am scared of heights.”

Rolo trotted over and sat beside her. “It is okay to be scared,” he said. “We can be kind. We can help.”

Mia walked over. “You can hold my hand,” she said.

Sam nodded. “You can press the star button.” He pointed to a paper button they had taped on the rocket.

Lina smiled a tiny smile. She held Mia’s hand. Her hand felt warm. She walked slowly, step by step.

“Ready?” Rolo asked.

“Ready,” Lina said.

“Three… two… one… blast off!” they all shouted.

Lina pressed the star button. Beep! The flag fluttered high. The cardboard rocket did not move, but in their minds, it soared. They laughed. They clapped. The rooftop felt bright and safe.

“I am not so scared now,” Lina said.

“That is brave,” Rolo said.

Mia hugged Lina. Sam gave a thumbs-up. The wind brought the smell of fresh bread again. Their tummies rumbled. Grumble, grumble.

“Snack time!” Mia giggled.

They sat in a circle, sharing crackers and apple slices. Crunch, crunch. Rolo beeped a happy tune. Beep-bee-beep.

“We learned something today,” Mia said.

“Cooperation,” Sam said. “We help each other.”

“And we listen and follow directions,” Lina added.

Rolo nodded. “We are kind and patient. We can do more together than alone.”

They looked at the rocket, shining with stars in the sun. It was strong and steady on the rooftop.

“We did it,” they said together.

The End.
