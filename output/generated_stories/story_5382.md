# Nutmeg's Quiet Corner Plan

Nutmeg was a little, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail. She loved to learn. She kept a tiny study corner under a desk in the sunny room. There was a soft leaf rug, a stubby pencil, and a stack of smooth acorn caps she used like buttons. The corner smelled like warm wood and clean paper. Sunbeams made lines of gold on the floor.

“Today I will learn my shapes,” Nutmeg chirped. She sat up tall. “Circle, square, triangle!”

Just then, her friend Pip the chipmunk zipped by. “I found a shiny marble!” Pip squeaked. The marble rolled across the floor with a little click-click. It bumped Nutmeg’s pencil. Plop! The pencil fell and rolled under a book.

Nutmeg’s whiskers drooped. “Oh no. My pencil.” She took a deep breath. “It’s okay, Nutmeg,” she told herself. “I can fix this.”

She used her clever paws to fish the pencil out. She smiled and patted the soft leaf rug. “It’s all right, Pip. Let’s be gentle in my study corner.”

Pip nodded and whispered, “Okay,” but his tail still swished and swished.

Nutmeg opened her shape book. The pages felt smooth and cool. “Circle,” she said. She traced a round cookie shape with her paw. “Mmm, circle like a cookie.” Her tummy gave a tiny rumble.

Her cousin, Willa the rabbit, hopped in next. Thump-thump went Willa’s feet. “I made carrot tea!” Willa said proudly. The cup was small and warm, and the tea smelled sweet and earthy.

Nutmeg loved Willa’s tea. But Willa set the cup too close to the book. A drop splashed—plip!—on the page. A warm spot spread over the corner of the triangle picture.

Nutmeg blinked. Her heart felt tight. She wanted to say, “No!” But she paused. She put a paw on her chest. In… and out… she breathed.

“It was an accident,” Willa said softly, ears drooping.

Nutmeg nodded. “I know. Accidents happen.” She looked at her soggy page and then at her friends. “My study corner is a quiet place. I like it tidy. I also like you. Let’s find a way that helps us all.”

Pip peeked at the tea. “We can put drinks on a mat,” he suggested.

Nutmeg’s eyes brightened. “Yes! A tea mat.” She blinked at her stack of acorn caps. “And what if we make quiet jobs? I can be the Reader. Pip can be the Marble Holder. Willa can be the Tea Watcher.”

Willa’s ears lifted. “I can do that!” she said. She moved the cup onto a coaster Nutmeg made from a round leaf.

Nutmeg smiled. “Thank you.” She wiped the tea drop with a soft cloth. The page was a little wrinkly now. “It is okay,” Nutmeg whispered to the book. “Wrinkles mean we are learning.”

She drew a big triangle. Her pencil made a friendly scritch-scratch. “Triangle like a roof!” she laughed.

Then a soft patter-patter came. It was Drizzle, the shy mouse. Drizzle stood at the edge of the study corner, holding a tiny crumb. “Can I learn, too?” Drizzle squeaked, very quiet.

Nutmeg remembered that Drizzle sometimes chewed on paper when nervous. Nutmeg cared about her book. She also cared about Drizzle. Nutmeg wiggled her whiskers in thought. How could she make space for Drizzle and keep her book safe?

She looked at her acorn caps again. “I have an idea.” She took a smooth scrap of cardboard from the recycling box. It felt firm under her paws. “Drizzle, this is a nibble board. When you feel worried, you can nibble here, not on the book.”

Drizzle’s eyes shone. “Thank you,” she squeaked. “I feel better with a nibble board.”

Pip whispered, “Good idea, Nutmeg.” Willa nodded and set the tea safely on the mat.

They all sat in the warm light. Nutmeg pointed. “Circle like the sun.” The sunbeams felt cozy on their fur.

“Square like a window,” Drizzle said, nibbling the board instead of the page. The nibble made a little tick-tick sound.

“Triangle like a carrot slice,” Willa added, smiling. The tea steamed gently, and the room smelled sweet.

Pip held his marble in still paws. “Marble holder is a quiet job,” he said with pride.

Nutmeg’s heart felt calm and full. She looked around her study corner. It was still tidy. It was also friendly. Everyone had a job. Everyone had a way to be safe and well. “We found a way,” Nutmeg said. “We can be different and be together.”

Soon it was time for a stretch. Nutmeg led a breath. “In like a circle,” she said, breathing in. “Out like a long rectangle,” she said, breathing out. They giggled as they stretched their tiny arms. Their bodies felt good and rested.

Pip asked, “Nutmeg, is it okay if I come again tomorrow?”

Nutmeg nodded. “Yes. We will follow our quiet plan. We will help each other. We will accept each other, just as we are, and keep improving.”

Drizzle smiled. “I feel safe here,” she said. Willa added, “Me too.”

Nutmeg tucked her book away. She patted her soft leaf rug. “My study corner is a place for learning, for caring, and for being ourselves.”

And the sunbeams glowed a little brighter.

The End.
