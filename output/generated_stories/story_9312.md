# Benny Beaver's Bathroom Building

Benny Beaver loved to build things. His big orange teeth were perfect for chewing wood. His flat tail made a wonderful *slap-slap* sound when he was excited.

Every morning, Benny brushed his teeth in the bathroom. The white tiles sparkled. The mirror was shiny and clean. But today, something special was happening.

"Today I'm building a dam!" Benny said to himself. "Right here in the bathtub!"

Benny had collected twigs from the yard. He had smooth pebbles too. Everything was ready for his pretend dam. He filled the bathtub with just a little water. *Splash, splash!*

But then his little sister Bella waddled in.

"I want to build too!" Bella squeaked. Her tiny paws reached for Benny's twigs.

"No!" Benny said quickly. "These are MY building sticks! Go away!"

Bella's brown eyes got watery. Her bottom lip trembled. "But I want to help," she whispered.

Benny grabbed all his twigs. He hugged them tight against his furry chest. "I planned this all by myself! It's not fair if you take my things!"

Bella sat down on the bathroom floor. A big tear rolled down her cheek. "I just wanted to play," she sniffled.

Benny kept building. He stacked his twigs carefully. *Click, click, click.* But something felt wrong. The bathroom was too quiet. Building alone wasn't as fun as he thought.

He looked at Bella. She was drawing pictures in the steam on the mirror. Her little drawings showed two beavers building together.

Benny's tummy felt funny. Not sick-funny. Sad-funny.

"Wait," Benny said slowly. "Maybe... maybe I wasn't being fair."

He thought hard. Mama Beaver always said sharing was important. She said working together made everything better.

"Bella?" Benny called softly. "Do you still want to help?"

Bella's face lit up like sunshine. "Really? You mean it?"

"Yes," Benny nodded. "But we need to take turns. And we both need to be patient. Building takes time."

Bella clapped her tiny paws. "I can be patient! I can practice!"

Benny smiled. "Okay. Let's plan together. You can place the pebbles. I'll arrange the big sticks. We'll make the best dam ever!"

They worked side by side. Bella was very careful with the smooth stones. *Plip, plop* into the water they went. Benny showed her how to weave the twigs together.

"This is harder than I thought," Bella said, concentrating hard.

"That's okay," Benny said kindly. "I had to practice lots too. You're doing great!"

Soon their dam was finished. Water pooled behind their creation. It looked wonderful!

"We did it!" Bella cheered.

"WE did it," Benny agreed, emphasizing the word 'we.' "It's much better than what I could have built alone."

Mama Beaver peeked into the bathroom. "What beautiful teamwork!" she said proudly. "You both learned something important today."

"I learned that sharing is fair," Benny said. "And that being patient helps everyone have fun."

"I learned that practice makes things better!" Bella added. "And that planning together is the best!"

They hugged each other tight. Their dam sparkled in the bathroom light. Outside, they could hear real water flowing in the creek.

"Tomorrow, let's build an even bigger one!" Bella suggested.

"Together?" Benny asked.

"Together!" they both said happily.

The End.
