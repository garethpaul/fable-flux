# Fawn’s Friendly Snack Circle

In a cozy, sunlit nursery, a gentle deer fawn named Fawn rested on a soft rug. The rug felt fuzzy under Fawn’s tiny hooves. Sunbeams made warm squares on the floor, and the room smelled like fresh crayons and clean blankets. Colorful pillows were piled high, and a low table held little baskets of snacks.

“Good morning, Fawn,” sang Teacher Nia, her voice soft like a lullaby. “Today we have a friendly snack circle!”

Fawn’s big brown eyes blinked. “Snack circle?” Fawn asked. “Is it a circle that eats snacks?”

Teacher Nia laughed kindly. “We sit in a circle and share snacks from many families and many places. We can taste, talk, and learn.”

Fawn’s ears flicked with excitement and a tiny bit of worry. “I am small,” Fawn whispered, nose twitching. “I want to be brave and try one new snack today.”

“That is a wonderful goal,” said Teacher Nia. “A small goal is still a big step.”

Friends tiptoed in and sat on cushions. The room filled with soft sounds: wrappers crinkling, cups clinking, happy whispers. Fawn’s heart beat thump-thump with curious joy.

Zara the zebra set down a bright bowl. “These are coconut rice balls,” Zara said proudly. “They are sweet and soft.” The sweet, toasty smell floated like a hug.

Miguel the monkey brought tiny corn cakes. “These are arepitas,” he chirped. “My Abuela makes them.” The warm corn smell was cozy, like a blanket on a chilly morning.

Leila the lamb held up a plate of cucumber stars. “My Baba cuts them in shapes,” she said. “Crunch, crunch!” The green smell was cool and fresh.

Aki the alligator carefully carried a little jar. “Seaweed flakes,” Aki said. “They taste like the sea.” The scent was salty and tickly.

Teacher Nia clapped softly. “So many good foods! Let’s make space, listen with our ears, and be kind with our words.”

Fawn’s tummy gave a shy rumble. “One new snack,” Fawn reminded themself. “I can do one.”

“Which one calls to you?” asked Teacher Nia.

Fawn sniffed the air. Sweet coconut. Warm corn. Cool cucumber. Salty sea. Everything smelled like stories. Fawn felt a bubble of excitement. “I will try… the corn cake,” Fawn said. “It smells like sunshine.”

Miguel smiled wide. “Here you go!” He placed a tiny arepita on a napkin shaped like a leaf.

Fawn touched it with a hoof-tip. It was warm and soft. “Hello, little cake,” Fawn giggled, then took a small bite. Mmm! The taste was gentle and friendly. Fawn’s tail gave a happy wiggle. “I did it!”

“Bravo,” said Teacher Nia. “You met your goal.”

Fawn’s ears perked. “I want to make a new goal,” Fawn said. “I want to try two new snacks!”

“That’s brave,” said Zara. “Would you like a coconut rice ball?”

Fawn sniffed. Sweet like a storybook. Sticky like a cloud. Fawn took a tiny bite. “It tastes like a soft snowflake,” Fawn said, eyes sparkling.

Leila offered a cucumber star. “Crunch?”

Fawn looked at the shiny green edges. “I will save that for later,” Fawn said kindly. “I want to listen to Aki’s food now.”

Aki held out a tiny pinch of seaweed flakes. “This is from my family’s kitchen,” Aki said. “It is crispy and salty.”

Fawn touched the flakes to a tongue-tip. Crunch! The flavor was a little wild, like a windy beach. Fawn’s eyes grew round. “It tastes like waves! I think I like it.”

The friends clapped softly, so the clapping sounded like rain on leaves.

Teacher Nia smiled. “We all bring foods from our families. Our foods are different, and that is special. When we taste and listen, we learn about each other.”

Fawn felt warm from nose to tail. “Our snacks are like a rainbow,” Fawn said. “Many colors. Many smells. Many stories.”

Zara nodded. “My Lola says coconut is good for energy.”

Miguel added, “Corn helps us feel full so we can play.”

Leila said, “Cucumber helps us feel fresh and strong.”

Aki grinned. “Seaweed has yummy goodness from the sea.”

Fawn looked around the circle. “Your foods help your bodies in different ways,” Fawn said. “That is good nutrition!”

Teacher Nia brought out a little chart with stars. “Let’s mark our goals,” she said. “Fawn tried three new snacks today!”

Fawn blinked. “Three? I tried corn cake, coconut ball, and seaweed flakes. That is more than my first goal. I grew my goal!”

“Yes,” said Teacher Nia. “You started with one, and you did more when you felt ready. That’s smart goal-setting.”

Fawn finally reached for a cucumber star and took a brave nibble. Crunch! Cool! “I did four!” Fawn laughed. “My circle is full of flavors.”

Everyone cheered. The nursery felt extra cozy, like a big, soft hug. Fawn’s heart felt big with pride and friendship.

“Today I learned,” Fawn said, “that foods from many cultures are yummy and special. When we share and try new things, we learn about each other and grow strong.”

Teacher Nia nodded. “Different is wonderful. Together, we make a bright and tasty world.”

Fawn licked a little coconut from a whisker and smiled. “Tomorrow, I will listen, share, and try again.”

The End.
