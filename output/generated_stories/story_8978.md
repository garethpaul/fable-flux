# Benny Bear's Big Problem

High up in the tall oak tree, Benny the bear cub loved his cozy treehouse. The wooden walls were painted bright yellow like sunshine, and colorful cushions made it the perfect place to play. But today, Benny had a big problem.

"Oh no!" Benny said, looking at his favorite red ball stuck way up in the tree branches above his treehouse. "How will I ever get my ball back?"

The ball had bounced too high during his morning play time. Now it sat on a branch that looked very far away. Benny's little paws couldn't reach it, even when he stood on his tippy-toes.

Benny sat down on his soft blue cushion and thought hard. "I need to think about this," he said to himself, tapping his furry chin with one paw. "What could help me reach up high?"

First, Benny tried jumping. "One, two, three... jump!" But even his biggest jump wasn't high enough. The ball stayed stuck.

Then Benny looked around his treehouse. He saw his wooden blocks, his picture books, and his toy telescope. "Hmm," he wondered, "could any of these help me?"

Benny picked up his telescope and looked through it. The ball looked bigger and closer, but he still couldn't touch it. "That's not quite right," he said.

Next, Benny had another idea. "What if I stack my blocks?" He carefully piled his red, blue, and green blocks on top of each other. But when he climbed on top, the tower wobbled. "Whoa!" Benny said as he stepped down safely. "That's too wobbly."

Benny sat down again and really thought hard. He closed his eyes and imagined different ways to solve his problem. "What reaches up high?" he asked himself.

Suddenly, his eyes opened wide. "I know!" Benny exclaimed. He looked at his long toy fishing rod in the corner. "This reaches far!"

Benny picked up the fishing rod and carefully extended it toward his ball. The soft hook on the end was perfect for gently catching things. "Almost... almost..." he said, concentrating hard.

With a gentle poke, the ball rolled off the branch and bounced down into Benny's waiting paws. "I did it!" he cheered, hugging his red ball tight.

Just then, Mama Bear climbed up to the treehouse. "I heard you cheering, Benny. What happened?"

"My ball got stuck way up high," Benny explained excitedly. "But I didn't give up! I tried jumping, then blocks, then I really thought about it. I used my fishing rod to get it down!"

Mama Bear smiled proudly. "You used your thinking skills to solve the problem all by yourself. That's wonderful!"

"It felt good to figure it out," Benny said, bouncing his ball gently. "When I have a problem, I can stop and think of different ways to fix it."

"That's right," Mama Bear said, giving him a warm hug. "Your brain is very good at solving problems when you take time to think."

Benny spent the rest of the day playing happily in his treehouse, proud that he had solved his problem all by himself. And whenever his ball bounced high again, he knew exactly what to do.

The End.
