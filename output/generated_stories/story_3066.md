# Sunny's Closet Club

Sunny was a resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail. She lived with a kind family in a cozy house. Sunny loved to make little plans and help others. Her favorite place was a small closet hideaway in the hallway. It smelled like clean socks and cinnamon soap. It was warm and quiet. Sunny called it “The Closet Club.”

Inside the closet, Sunny kept paper, crayons, stickers, a tiny whistle, and a soft, old scarf. She also kept a notebook for writing kind notes. Sunny liked to write. She wrote slowly, with care, so every word felt gentle.

One breezy morning, Sunny peeked out of the closet and heard voices. The children were getting ready for a backyard soccer game. “We need teams!” said Mia. “I want to be captain!” said Sam. Their voices got louder. Sunny’s ears twitched. The air felt tight, like a knot.

Sunny hurried back into the closet. She took a deep breath. She could smell the wooden shelves. She could feel the soft scarf under her paws. “I can help,” she whispered. “I can use words. I can be kind and strong.”

Sunny opened her notebook. She wrote, “Let’s listen and share. We can make fair teams. Everyone can play.” She added a smiley face and a tiny soccer ball. With a hop, she carried the note out to the hallway and nudged it toward Mia’s shoe.

Mia picked it up. “A note!” she said. Sam leaned in. “Let’s listen and share… everyone can play.” Sam’s cheeks softened. Mia smiled. “Okay,” she said. “Let’s listen.”

Sunny’s heart warmed. She felt brave. She scampered back to the closet and put on the soft scarf like a little coach. She blew her tiny whistle. Peep! The sound was small but clear.

“Who did that?” asked Sam, surprised.

Sunny peeked out. “It was me,” she said in a small, steady voice. “I am Sunny. I have an idea.”

“Hi, Sunny,” said Mia softly. “What’s your idea?”

Sunny’s tail swished. “We can make a list. We can write names. We can take turns being captain. That is fair. And we can cheer for each other. That feels kind.”

Sam nodded slowly. “I like cheering,” he said. “And I like turns.”

Mia clapped. “Let’s do it!”

Sunny brought out her notebook and a crayon. The paper felt smooth under her paw. “First, we write names,” she said. “We speak one at a time.” She looked at Sam and Mia. “Can we try that?”

“Yes,” they both said.

As they wrote, a small thump came from outside the door. It was Max, the neighbor’s puppy. He had big paws and a gentle face. He looked worried. He carried a tangle of old ribbons. His ears were droopy.

“Hi, Max,” said Sunny. “Are you okay?”

Max whined softly. “I want to play,” he said, “but my leg feels tired, and I am slow. I don’t want to make anyone mad.”

Sunny’s heart felt tender, like a warm cookie. She stepped closer. “Thank you for telling us,” she said. “Your feelings matter.” She looked at Mia and Sam. “How do you think Max feels?”

Mia crouched down. “I think Max feels worried,” she said.

Sam nodded. “Maybe he feels left out.”

Sunny smiled. “We can help. We can make a spot for Max. We can volunteer to be helpers. We can be kind.”

“I will be a helper!” Mia said. “I can run with Max.”

“I will be a helper too,” said Sam. “I can pass the ball to Max.”

Sunny wrote: “Helpers today: Mia and Sam.” She added a ribbon sticker. Max wagged his tail. The ribbons in his mouth wiggled like colorful snakes.

“Thank you,” Max said, tail thumping. “I feel better.”

Outside in the yard, the grass smelled sweet and sunny. The ball felt bouncy under paws and shoes. The children laughed. “Pass to Max!” called Sam. “Great try!” said Mia. Sunny stood by the edge with her scarf. She cheered. “Go team! Kind words, kind hearts!”

Then a new problem came. Two older kids, Jake and Lina, walked by the gate. “We want to play,” said Jake. “But we only play on the big team.”

Sunny took a breath. She felt a little shake in her tail, but she stood tall. She was small, but she could be brave. “We have a plan,” she said, her voice calm. “We take turns. We cheer for all. If you join, you follow our kind rules.”

Jake crossed his arms. “Why?”

Sunny looked at him gently. “Because feelings matter,” she said. “We want everyone to feel safe. We want everyone to have fun. You matter too.” She held out her notebook. The crayon letters were simple and clear.

Lina read the list. She smiled. “I like it,” she said. “Jake, we can try.”

Jake sighed, then nodded. “Okay. I can try.”

They played. They passed. They cheered. The soft wind brushed their faces. The ball thumped on the grass. Max made a small goal. Everyone clapped. “Great job, Max!” shouted Mia. Jake high-fived him. Max’s tail wagged like a happy drum.

After the game, Sunny led everyone back to the closet hideaway. It was snug and cozy. The cinnamon soap smell wrapped around them like a hug. Sunny opened her notebook.

“Let’s write thank-you notes,” she said. “We can volunteer to help again tomorrow.”

They wrote together. “Thank you for sharing.” “Thank you for cheering.” “Thank you for trying.” Sunny’s paw moved slowly and carefully. Her words felt warm.

Sunny looked around. “What did we learn today?”

Mia raised her hand. “We learned to listen.”

Sam smiled. “We learned to help.”

Jake said softly, “We learned to be brave and kind.”

Max barked, “We learned that my feelings matter.”

Sunny’s eyes sparkled. “Yes,” she said. “Empathy is listening, caring, and helping. When we use kind words and fair rules, everyone can play.”

They stacked the notes, tied them with Max’s ribbons, and put them on the shelf.

Sunny tucked her scarf away and blew one last gentle whistle. Peep!

The Closet Club was quiet and warm. Their hearts were, too.

The End.
