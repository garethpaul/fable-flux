# Splash the Dolphin Says Sorry

Splash the dolphin loved to visit the underwater art room at the bottom of the coral reef. The room was filled with colorful paints made from sea flowers, brushes made from soft seaweed, and canvases made from smooth shells.

Every morning, Splash would swim through the round doorway, his gray skin shimmering in the gentle light that filtered down from above. "Good morning, everyone!" he would click and whistle cheerfully.

The art room was always busy with sea friends. There was Shelly the shy seahorse who painted tiny, delicate pictures. Ollie the octopus used all eight arms to create amazing sculptures. And Finn the little fish made beautiful patterns with his colorful scales.

One sunny Tuesday, Splash was feeling extra playful. He zoomed around the art room, spinning and splashing. "Look at me! Look at me!" he called out, doing loop-de-loops near the ceiling.

SPLASH! His big tail accidentally knocked over Shelly's easel. Her beautiful painting of purple sea anemones went tumbling through the water.

"Oh no!" squeaked Shelly, her tiny voice barely a whisper. Tears began to form in her big, round eyes.

Splash stopped spinning. He looked at the ruined painting floating in pieces. His stomach felt heavy and uncomfortable, like he had swallowed a rock.

"It's okay," Splash said quickly, swimming away. "It was just an accident." But deep down, he knew that wasn't enough.

For the rest of the day, Splash couldn't concentrate on his own art. The bright yellow paint didn't seem as cheerful. The soft brushes felt rough in his fins. Every time he looked at Shelly, she seemed sadder and smaller than before.

That evening, Splash swam home slowly. His mother, Marina, noticed his droopy dorsal fin. "What's wrong, my little splash?" she asked gently.

Splash told her everything. "I hurt Shelly's feelings, Mama. I said it was just an accident, but I didn't say sorry. I feel yucky inside."

Marina nuzzled her son with her smooth nose. "When we hurt someone, even by accident, the right thing to do is apologize. It shows we care about their feelings."

"But what if she doesn't want to be my friend anymore?" Splash worried.

"A real apology comes from the heart," Marina explained. "It's about making things better, not just making yourself feel better."

The next morning, Splash swam to the art room early. He had worked all night on something special. When Shelly arrived, Splash was waiting with a new painting he had made just for her.

"Shelly," Splash said softly, "I'm really sorry I knocked over your painting yesterday. I was being careless, and I hurt your feelings. That was wrong."

He showed her the painting - a beautiful picture of purple sea anemones, just like the one that was ruined, but with a little seahorse swimming happily nearby.

Shelly's eyes grew wide with wonder. "You made this... for me?"

"I wanted to make up for what I did," Splash said. "Can you forgive me?"

Shelly smiled her first real smile since yesterday. "Of course! Thank you for saying sorry, Splash. And thank you for this beautiful painting!"

From that day on, Splash always remembered to think about others' feelings. He learned that saying sorry doesn't make you smaller - it makes your heart bigger.

And whenever accidents happened in the art room, all the sea friends knew exactly what to do: apologize, make things right, and keep being kind to each other.

The End.
