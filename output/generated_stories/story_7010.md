# Tilly Turtle on the Rooftop

Tilly was a shy turtle. She lived in a cozy garden. One sunny day, a fluttering sign blew by. It landed on a wide, flat rooftop. The sign said, “Rooftop Garden Help Needed. Bring kindness!”

Tilly peeked up. She could smell mint leaves in the breeze. She could hear birds chirp, “Cheep, cheep!” The sun was warm on her shell. Tilly felt small, but curious.

“I am shy,” Tilly whispered. “But I can be kind.”

She took a deep breath. “In,” she said. “Out,” she said. Then she walked to the stairs. Tap, tap, tap went her little feet. The steps felt cool and smooth.

At the top, the rooftop garden was bright and busy. Pots of flowers lined the edges. Bees hummed. The wind made soft whooshing sounds. In the middle was a little fountain that went, “plink, plink.”

A tiny sparrow hopped near a droopy tomato plant. “Oh dear,” the sparrow chirped. “The plant needs help.”

Tilly tucked her head a little. “I can help,” she said softly. “I am small, but I am kind.”

The sparrow tilted its head. “Hello, I am Pip. Are you here to help the rooftop?”

“Yes,” Tilly said. Her voice was small, but steady. “I am Tilly.”

Pip smiled with his eyes. “Welcome, Tilly!”

Tilly touched the soil with her toes. It felt dry and dusty. “This plant is thirsty,” she said. “It needs water.”

She looked at the fountain. She looked at the plant. The watering can was big and shiny. It looked heavy.

“I can try,” Tilly said. She pushed the can a little. Scrape, scrape. It did not move far.

Tilly felt her cheeks grow warm. She wanted to hide inside her shell.

Pip chirped gently, “It is okay, Tilly. You tried. That is brave.”

Tilly took another deep breath. “In. Out.” She looked around. There was a small cup beside a flower pot.

“I can use the small cup,” she said. “I can take little sips of water. Little by little.”

She dipped the cup in the fountain. Plink. The water was cool on her feet. She carried the cup to the plant. Step, step. She poured. Drip, drip, drip.

She went back and forth. Plink. Drip. Plink. Drip. The soil turned dark and soft. The leaves lifted a little.

“You did it!” Pip sang. “That was kind and clever.”

Tilly smiled. Her chest felt warm. “I can help,” she said again, a little louder.

Just then, a gust of wind whooshed by. A row of tiny seedlings wobbled. One tiny seedling tipped over.

“Oh no!” Pip chirped.

Tilly hurried over. She saw the tiny seedling on its side. She touched it gently with her cool, smooth foot. The stem felt soft and bendy.

“I will be careful,” Tilly whispered. “Gentle hands. Gentle heart.”

She scooped a little soil with her cup. She made a small hill beside the stem. She patted the soil softly. Pat, pat, pat.

The seedling stood up. Its baby leaves waved. Tilly’s eyes shone. “You are strong,” she told the plant. “We can be strong together.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “You are strong too, Tilly.”

A bumblebee buzzed near a yellow flower. Bzzzz. “Hello,” the bee hummed. “Thank you for the water. The flowers taste sweet today.”

Tilly laughed, a tiny turtle laugh. “You are welcome.”

Later, a cloud covered the sun. The air felt a little cool. Tilly saw a crack in a clay pot. A little vine leaned toward the edge.

“I do not know how to fix a pot,” Tilly said. Her voice shook. She wanted to tuck in again.

Pip chirped softly, “What do you think we can do?”

Tilly looked. She felt the crack with her foot. She thought. “I can move the vine to a safer pot,” she said. “I will need to be slow. I will need to be kind.”

She found a small, snug pot close by. She made a spoon with a leaf and her cup. Scoop, scoop. She lifted the vine with care. The soil smelled rich and sweet like rain.

Tilly set the vine in the new pot. She tucked soil around it. Pat, pat. She gave it a small sip of water. Drip, drip.

The cloud drifted away. The sun peeked out. Warmth spread over Tilly’s shell. She stood tall. She did not hide.

Pip chirped loud and bright, “Hooray for Tilly! Kind hands. Brave heart.”

Tilly felt brave and calm. “I can be kind,” she said. “I can try. I can keep going, even when it is hard.”

The sparrow hopped in a happy circle. The bee hummed a sunny tune. The fountain sang, “plink, plink, plink.”

Tilly looked at the rooftop garden. She smelled mint. She heard bees. She felt the warm sun. She smiled.

“I came here shy,” she said. “I helped with kindness. I learned I can try, and try again. I can care for others. I can care for myself too.”

Pip nodded. “Kindness helps plants grow. Kindness helps hearts grow.”

Tilly’s heart felt big. “I will come back tomorrow,” she said. “We will help again.”

That night, Tilly went down the stairs. Tap, tap. She felt tired in a good way. She felt strong inside.

Lesson: Be kind. Be gentle. Try little by little. When things are hard, take a breath and keep going. Kindness makes gardens grow. Kindness makes us grow too.

The End.
