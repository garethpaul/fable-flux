# Ellie’s Soft Steps

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a warm, round belly. She lived in a bright, comfortable nursery with pastel walls and sunny windows. The room smelled like fresh soap and warm blankets. There were soft carpets, squishy blocks, and tiny shelves with picture books. Birds chirped outside, and a clock made a quiet tick-tock sound.

Every morning, Ellie padded in with soft steps. “Good morning,” she hummed, swishing her little tail. Her friends were there too—Milo the mouse, Pippa the panda, and Tutu the turtle. Ms. Star, the kind teacher, smiled and said, “Today we will build a cozy house with blocks.”

Ellie’s heart fluttered with happy excitement. She loved building. “Let’s make a tall, tall tower,” she said, her eyes bright. “I can carry the big blocks with my trunk.”

Pippa clapped her paws. “Yes, please!” Milo squeaked, “Me too!” Tutu nodded slowly. “We can do it together.”

They began. Ellie picked up a smooth yellow block. It felt cool on her trunk. She placed it carefully. Tap. Pippa added a blue one. Milo brought a tiny red block. Tutu slid in a long green block like a bridge. The tower grew and grew.

Then came the wobbly part. The tower leaned. Ellie felt a little tight twist in her tummy. “It’s not straight,” she said. She reached out fast.

“Wait,” Milo squeaked. “I’m still holding this one.”

Ellie didn’t hear. Her ears were full of her rushing thoughts. She tugged the block. The tower shook. Wobble-wobble—CRASH! The blocks tumbled down with a clatter. Pippa jumped. Milo’s whiskers drooped. Tutu’s head tucked into his shell.

Silence. Tick-tock. Ellie’s cheeks felt hot. Her tummy felt heavy. She whispered, “Oh no.”

Milo’s eyes were shiny. “I worked hard,” he said softly. Pippa hugged her round belly. “I did too.” Tutu peeked out. “It scared me.”

Ellie looked at her friends. She saw Milo’s tiny shaking paws. She heard Pippa’s slow breath. She noticed Tutu blinking and blinking. Ellie felt a warm, gentle feeling grow in her chest. She thought, How do they feel? They feel sad and scared. I made them feel that way.

Ms. Star knelt beside them. “This is a big feeling moment,” she said in a soft voice. “Let’s use our calm steps. Breathe in like you smell a flower. Breathe out like you blow a bubble.”

Ellie took a deep breath in. She smelled the clean blankets and a hint of oranges from snack time. She breathed out and imagined a shiny bubble floating up, up. Pop. Her shoulders dropped. Her heart slowed. “I can be gentle,” she whispered to herself. “Soft steps. Slow trunk.”

Ms. Star nodded. “Good self-control, Ellie.”

Ellie looked at her friends again. “I’m sorry,” she said, with her voice low and kind. “I pulled fast and made the tower fall. I can slow down next time. Are you okay?”

Milo sniffed. “I was scared. Thank you for asking.” Pippa patted Ellie’s knee. “I feel better when you use your soft voice.” Tutu inched all the way out and smiled. “You noticed how we felt. That helps.”

Ellie’s ears perked. She felt brave. “Let’s try again,” she said. “This time, we will use kind leader steps. We listen. We share. We go slow.” She tapped her trunk lightly on her chest and then pointed to everyone. “I can carry the big blocks. Milo can place the tiny ones. Pippa can check if it’s straight. Tutu can steady the base.”

Milo squeaked, “That’s a good plan!” Pippa added, “We can say, ‘Ready?’ before we move.” Tutu nodded, “Cooperation.”

They started again. Ellie lifted a block and asked, “Ready?” Milo peeked up. “Ready.” Pippa lined up the corners. “Looks straight.” Tutu held the bottom with his sturdy shell. The blocks clicked softly together. Click. Tap. Slide. The tower stood tall and strong.

“Look!” Ellie said, her eyes wide. “We did it!” The tower glowed under the sunny window. Everyone cheered. Ms. Star clapped gently. “I see a team that listens and cares. Ellie, I noticed your soft steps and your kind plan. That is leadership with empathy.”

Ellie blushed and smiled. “It feels good to help everyone feel safe.”

They added a little door and a tiny roof. Milo put a red flag on top. Pippa drew a smiley sun on a paper and taped it to the side. Tutu hummed a happy tune. The nursery felt warm and peaceful. The clock went tick-tock, but now it sounded friendly.

Later, they sat on the soft carpet and shared orange slices. The fruit was juicy and sweet. Ellie said, “When I slow down and look at faces, I can see how my friends feel. Then I can make a kind choice.”

Milo nodded. “I like when you ask, ‘Are you okay?’” Pippa grinned. “I like when we say, ‘Ready?’” Tutu said, “I like steady breaths.”

Ms. Star smiled. “Today you learned to notice feelings, use calm breaths, say sorry, and make a plan together. That is empathy, self-control, and teamwork.”

Ellie tucked her trunk around her friends in a gentle hug. “We are a caring team,” she said.

The friends looked at their tall tower. It didn’t wobble at all. It stood strong—just like their kind hearts.

The End.
