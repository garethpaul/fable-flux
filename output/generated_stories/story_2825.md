# Kora Koala and the Fair Share

Kora was a calm koala who liked to sit in a comfy chair in the home office. The chair was soft and warm, and it smelled like fresh paper and a little bit like eucalyptus tea. The window let in bright morning light. Tap-tap-tap went the keyboard. Hummm went the little fan. Kora liked the quiet sounds.

On the big desk were pretty stickers, shiny paper clips, and a jar of smooth crayons. Kora blinked her gentle eyes. “I wonder,” she said, “who gets which stickers today?”

She was helping to pack envelopes for the neighbors. Inside each envelope went a sticker, a crayon, and a tiny note that said, “You are important.” Kora felt happy to help. It felt right.

“Kora,” said Mama from the doorway, “can you finish these ten envelopes while I make lunch?”

“Yes,” said Kora. Her voice was soft and sure. “I can do it.”

Kora’s ears twitched. She liked doing good work. She counted the envelopes. One, two, three… ten. She lined them up in a neat row. She peeked into the sticker box. There were exactly ten star stickers. “Perfect,” she whispered.

Just then, the mail slot went clink! A breeze whooshed the curtains. A squirrel named Squeaks popped his head in the window screen. “Hi, Kora!” squeaked Squeaks. His nose twitched. “Oh! Shiny! Can I have a sticker? Or two? Or three?”

Kora looked at the row of envelopes. She looked at the sticker box. Her heart made a tiny thump. “I want to be kind,” she thought. “But I also want to be fair.”

“What does fair mean?” Kora wondered out loud. She scratched her ear. “Fair means everyone gets what they need, not just the first one to ask.”

Squeaks bounced. “Please? I really, really like stars!”

Kora took a slow breath. She felt the smooth paper in her paws. The room smelled like pencils and toast. “Squeaks,” she said calmly, “I have ten envelopes and ten stickers. One sticker belongs in each envelope. If I give you two, someone will not get any. That would not be fair.”

Squeaks drooped. “Oh,” he squeaked softly.

Kora’s curiosity woke up. “Is there a way to help Squeaks and be fair to everyone?” she wondered. Her whiskers wiggled with thinking. She peered into the drawer. Tap-tap. She found some plain paper circles and a sparkly pen.

“I have an idea!” Kora said. “I can draw star shapes on these circles. They won’t be the same as the shiny stickers, but they will still be special.”

Squeaks’ eyes grew wide. “Really?”

Kora nodded. “Yes. And we will still keep one shiny star for each envelope, so it stays fair.”

Kora got to work. Scratch-scratch went the pen. She drew careful stars. She colored them yellow and gold. She added tiny dots that looked like little lights. Squeaks watched, quiet and curious. He loved the sound of the pen. He loved the way Kora stayed calm.

When she finished, Kora held up two hand-drawn stars. “For you,” she said. “And this shiny star stays for the envelopes.”

Squeaks clapped his little paws. “Thank you, Kora! You were fair and kind.” He pressed his nose to the screen. “You used your own idea!”

Kora smiled. “That is called being resourceful. I used what I had.”

Tap-tap-tap went the keyboard again. Mama came back with a peanut butter sandwich and a little cup of water. “How did it go?” she asked.

Kora showed the neat row of ten envelopes, each with a shiny star peeking out. “Everyone will get what they need,” Kora said. “That is justice. It means we treat people right and make fair choices.”

Mama’s eyes crinkled. “I’m proud of you, Kora. You were self-reliant. You stayed calm. You solved the problem on your own.”

Kora took a small bite of sandwich. The peanut butter was sweet and sticky. She giggled. “And I used my curiosity to find a new way.”

Squeaks waved his paper stars. “I learned something too,” he said. “Next time, I will ask, ‘Is it fair?’ before I take.”

Kora nodded. “That is a good question.”

They all listened for a moment. Hummm went the fan. The window let in a soft afternoon breeze. The home office felt peaceful.

Kora sealed the last envelope. Smack! The sticky part made a funny sound. She lined them up for the mail carrier. “All done,” she said.

Mama put a paw on Kora’s shoulder. “What did we learn today?”

Kora sat up tall. “Justice means fair share,” she said. “We make sure everyone gets what they should. We can be kind and fair at the same time. We can think of new ideas when we have a problem. And we can do it calmly.”

Squeaks nodded fast. “Fair and kind!” he squeaked.

Kora looked at the bright row of envelopes. She felt warm and proud. “I can do hard things,” she whispered. “I can do the right thing.”

The mail slot went clink! The envelopes slid away, ready to bring smiles to the neighbors. Kora waved. “Good journey,” she said.

The home office was quiet again. Tap-tap. Hummm. Kora sipped her water and hugged her chair. Justice felt good. Helping felt good. Learning felt good.

The End.
