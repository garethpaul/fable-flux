# Benny Beaver's Fair Play Day

Benny Beaver loved playing in the cozy nursery with all his woodland friends. The nursery had soft, fluffy carpets that felt like clouds under his little paws, and colorful toys scattered everywhere like a rainbow.

One sunny morning, Benny waddled into the nursery carrying his favorite wooden blocks. "I'm going to build the biggest dam ever!" he announced, his tail slapping excitedly on the floor.

Little Rabbit Ruby hopped over with her soft pink ears bouncing. "Can I help, Benny? I love building too!"

"No way!" Benny said, hugging all his blocks close. "These are MINE! I need ALL of them for my super special dam!"

Ruby's whiskers drooped sadly. She sat in the corner, watching Benny stack block after block. Soon, Benny had used every single wooden block in the nursery.

Squirrel Sam scampered down from the bookshelf. "Benny, could I borrow just two blocks? I want to make a tiny house for my acorn collection."

"Absolutely not!" Benny replied, his brown fur puffing up. "I'm using every single block!"

Sam's fluffy tail drooped as he walked away with empty paws.

Benny kept building and building. His arms started to ache, and his back felt sore from bending over so much. "Ow, ow, ow," he groaned, rubbing his tired shoulders. The dam was getting wobbly because he couldn't hold all the blocks steady by himself.

Just then, Mrs. Owl flew in with her wise, golden eyes twinkling. "Benny, dear, you look very tired. And I notice Ruby and Sam look quite sad. What's happening here?"

Benny looked around the nursery. Ruby was sitting alone, staring at her empty paws. Sam was curled up sadly by the window. Meanwhile, Benny's dam kept falling down because he couldn't manage all those blocks alone.

"I wanted to build the best dam ever," Benny said softly, "but it keeps falling down, and my body hurts from working so hard."

Mrs. Owl nodded wisely. "Sometimes when we try to keep everything for ourselves, we end up with more work and less fun. What do you think would happen if you shared?"

Benny's round eyes grew wide as he thought. Slowly, he waddled over to Ruby. "Ruby, would you like to help me build? We could make it even better together!"

Ruby's ears perked up immediately. "Really? Oh yes, please!"

"Sam!" Benny called out. "Want to help too? You're so good at balancing things!"

Sam's tail fluffed up with joy as he scampered over.

Working together, the three friends built the most amazing structure ever! Ruby's gentle paws helped stack blocks smoothly, Sam's quick movements kept everything balanced, and Benny's strong tail helped steady the whole thing.

"This is so much better!" Benny laughed, feeling his sore muscles relax. "And it's way more fun too!"

"Plus, we finished much faster," Ruby giggled, doing a little hop-dance.

"And now we can all play together!" Sam added, doing a happy backflip.

Mrs. Owl smiled warmly. "When we share fairly and work together, everyone wins. Benny, how do you feel now?"

Benny's whole face lit up. "I feel happy inside! Sharing made everything better – my body feels good, my friends are happy, and our building is the best ever!"

From that day on, Benny always remembered that being fair and sharing made playtime more fun for everyone.

The End.
