# Bouncy Bubbles and a Big Forgive

Kip is a joyful kangaroo joey. He has bright, bouncy eyes and a soft, warm pouch. One sunny morning, Kip hops into the bathroom. The tiles are cool on his toes. Drip, drip, drip goes the tap. “Time for bubbly fun!” Kip cheers.

He squeezes the soap bottle—squish! Pink soap smells like strawberries. He makes a mountain of bubbles. They pop, pop, pop! Kip hums a little song. “Wash, wash, wash, clean paws, clean nose!”

Kip’s friend, Tilly Toad, peeks from the bathtub. “Ribbit! Can I play too?” she asks.

“Yes!” Kip says, smiling. “We can share. Sharing is kind.”

Tilly hops onto the bath mat—splat! Her feet are wet and a little muddy. The mat gets soggy. The mirror fogs like a cloud as warm water runs. Kip giggles. “It’s okay. We will wipe it later.”

They play bubble hats. They make bubble beards. The soap smells sweet, and the water feels warm and tickly. “Look at me!” says Kip. He puts bubbles on his ears.

Suddenly, Tilly sees a shiny red bottle on the counter. “Ooo! Shiny!” She reaches for it.

Kip hops closer. “Wait, Tilly. That’s Mama’s lotion. We must ask first. We show respect.”

But Tilly is excited. She squeezes the bottle—squirt! Lotion splashes everywhere. It lands on the mirror. It drips on the floor. Slippery, slippery!

Kip’s smile fades. He slips—whoa!—and lands on his tail with a thump. “Ow,” he says softly. His eyes get watery.

Tilly’s mouth makes a small “o.” “Oh no,” she whispers. “I didn’t mean to. I am sorry.”

Kip feels mad for a tiny moment. His heart feels tight, like a knot. The soap smell is now mixed with the strong lotion smell. The floor is messy. His tail stings.

Kip takes a slow breath. In, out. He remembers what Mama says: “Justice means we fix the wrong. Respect means we care. Forgiveness helps hearts feel light.”

Kip looks at Tilly. Tilly’s eyes are big and worried. A little lotion dot is on her nose. Kip thinks, She didn’t mean to hurt me. She is my friend. We can make it right.

Kip nods. “Tilly, I felt upset. I fell. But I hear your sorry.” He puts a paw on his chest. “I forgive you.”

Tilly blinks. “You forgive me?” Her voice is small.

“Yes,” says Kip, with a gentle smile. “Forgiveness means we let go of the mad and keep the friend. But next time, please ask. That shows respect.”

Tilly sighs with relief. “I will ask next time. I want to be a respectful friend.”

Kip stands and wiggles his tail. “It still hurts a little,” he admits. “Let’s make it right. That is justice. We can clean the mess together.”

“Together!” Tilly says. Her voice is bright again.

They get a towel. The towel is fluffy and smells like sunshine. Swish, swish, they wipe the floor until it is not slippery. Wipe, wipe, they clean the mirror until it shines. The red bottle goes back in its place. Kip pats it gently. “We accept that some things are not for us,” he says. “We can accept ‘not now’ and still have fun.”

Tilly nods. “We can play with safe things. We can ask first.”

Kip turns off the tap—click. Silence, except for tiny pops of bubble foam. He squeezes a little soap into Tilly’s hands. “Here, this is for play,” he says. “Let’s make bubble hearts.”

They make bubbles again. The bathroom smells sweet and clean. The tiles feel warm from the sun. Kip’s heart feels light, like a balloon. Tilly laughs. “Thank you for forgiving me.”

“You are welcome,” Kip says. “Forgiveness makes room for smiles.”

Mama Kangaroo peeks in. She sees the shiny mirror and the tidy floor. “What happened here?” she asks softly.

Kip hops over. “We had a spill. I fell. Tilly said sorry. I forgave. We cleaned up. We practiced respect and justice.”

Mama smiles and gives them both a gentle pat. “I am proud. Forgiveness is brave. Respect is kind. Justice is making it right. Acceptance is saying, ‘It’s okay to learn and grow.’”

Tilly beams. “We learned!”

Kip holds out a clean, dry towel. “Group hug?” The towel is warm and snuggly. They hug and giggle.

“Next time,” Tilly says, “I will ask about shiny bottles.”

Kip nods. “And I will tell you which ones are safe. We can help each other.”

They finish with a final bubble hat parade. Pop, pop, pop go the bubbles as they bow. The bathroom glows with clean mirrors, happy friends, and calm hearts.

Kip looks at Tilly and says, “Today we learned: When someone says sorry, we can forgive. We show respect by asking and listening. We practice justice by fixing the mistake. We practice acceptance by being patient as we learn.”

Tilly claps. “That is a good lesson.”

Kip grins. “And now, snack time!”

They hop out, paws and toes squeaky clean, hearts full of kindness.

The End.
