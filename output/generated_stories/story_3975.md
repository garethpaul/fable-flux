# Bucky Beaver’s Bubbly Bathroom Day

Bucky the busy beaver woke up to the soft drip-drop of water. Plip. Plop. Plink. He wiggled his flat tail and smiled. “Good morning, bathroom!” he said. The bathroom smelled like minty toothpaste and warm soap. Sunlight peeked in and made the bubbles in the tub sparkle like tiny stars.

Bucky loved to be helpful. He loved to be healthy, too. Today, he had a plan. “Wellness first!” he sang. “I take care of me. I take care of we.”

He padded to the sink and turned the tap just a little. “Just a small stream,” he whispered. “We save water. That helps our river.” The water made a gentle shhh sound. Bucky wet his toothbrush and squeezed a pea-sized dot of toothpaste. “Only a little,” he said. “Little is just right.”

He brushed in soft circles. “Brush, brush, brush,” he hummed. “Top teeth, bottom teeth, tongue too.” The mint tingled. The foamy bubbles tasted cool and sweet. He spit into the sink and giggled, “Patoo!” Then he turned off the tap. “Click! Saving water is caring for Earth.”

Knock, knock. Bucky’s sister, Bella, tapped on the door. “Bucky, is it my turn?”

“Almost, Bella,” Bucky called. “I am quick as a beaver!” He looked at a little sand timer on the shelf. The sand slid down. “One more minute,” he said. “We take turns. Taking turns is kind.”

Bucky wiped the sink with a small towel. He picked up a toy boat from the floor and put it in the basket. He opened the window just a crack. Fresh air whooshed in, smelling like pine trees. “Fresh air keeps us well,” he said. “And it dries the room.”

Bella stepped in with a hop. “My turn!”

Bucky smiled. “Your turn! I’ll help.”

They washed hands together. “Wet, soap, scrub,” said Bucky. They sang a scrub song. “Tops and bottoms, in between, clean, clean, clean!” The soap smelled like oranges. Their paws made fluffy white clouds. The bubbles went pop, pop, pop! They rinsed and dried with a soft towel. It felt warm and snuggly.

“Thank you,” said Bella. “I feel fresh!”

Bucky nodded. “Clean hands help keep us from getting sick. Wellness is feeling good and staying strong.”

Bella pointed to the tub. “Bubble bath?”

Bucky thought. “Yes, but a little. We save water.” He put in just a small splash and filled the tub halfway. “Half is plenty!” he said. The water was warm and comfy. It made tiny waves that tickled their toes.

They shared bath crayons. “We take turns with colors,” said Bucky. “Red for you. Blue for me.” They drew fish, trees, and a smiling sun on the tub wall. “I am making a river,” said Bucky, coloring swirls. “Our river is happy when we keep water clean.”

Bella drew a beaver dam. “Our home!” she said. “Let’s draw a trash can too.”

Bucky cheered. “Great idea! Trash goes in the bin, not in the water.” He picked up a tiny paper wrapper from the floor and dropped it into the little bathroom bin. “That is civic responsibility,” Bucky said proudly. “It means we help our town by doing our part.”

The timer ticked. “Ding!” Bucky smiled. “Bath time is almost done.”

He pulled the plug and watched the water swirl away. “Goodbye, bubbles.” The tub went glug-glug. He rinsed the bath crayons off the wall with a cup. The colors melted into rainbows and then washed clean. “We clean up the art when we’re done,” he said. “We can make more tomorrow!”

They stepped onto the mat—squish, warm and soft. Bucky patted himself dry and brushed his fur. He felt cozy and calm. He put his toothbrush back in the cup, bristles up. He hung the towels neatly. He checked the floor for drips and wiped them. “Safe and tidy,” he said. “No slippery spots.”

Bella looked around. “The bathroom is sparkling!”

Bucky took a deep breath. The room smelled like oranges and mint and fresh air. The window breeze whispered hello. The mirror shone. The sink was dry. The bin was closed. The light was bright but gentle.

Bucky tapped the light switch. “Off when we leave,” he said. “That saves energy.” He held the door for Bella. “After you.”

Bella skipped out. “Thank you, Bucky!”

Bucky smiled. His heart felt big and warm. “Wellness means I care for my body, my family, and my world,” he said softly. “I brush, wash, and rest. I share, clean, and save.”

Mama Beaver peeped around the corner. “I see shiny teeth and tidy paws,” she said. “I see a clean bathroom too. What great helpers!”

Bucky puffed with pride. “We took turns. We saved water. We cleaned up. We made art and then washed it away.”

Mama hugged them. “That is healthy living,” she said. “And that is caring for our community and our Earth.”

Bucky nodded. “Wellness first. I take care of me. I take care of we.”

They all smiled. Their home felt peaceful and bright.

The End.
