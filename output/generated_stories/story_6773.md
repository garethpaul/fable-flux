# Suri the Squirrel and the Kitchen Kindness

Suri the squirrel lived in a cozy nook by a big, warm kitchen. The kitchen smelled like toast and cinnamon. Sunlight made the floor glow golden. The kettle hummed a happy song. Pots and pans rested on hooks like shiny moons.

Every day, Suri visited the kitchen to help tidy the crumbs. “I am a helper,” Suri squeaked. “I am resourceful!” She used her fluffy tail like a duster. Swish, swish! She lined up tiny crumbs and nuts and put them in a little basket.

One bright morning, Suri scampered in and saw a plate of muffins on the table. Blueberry muffins! They were round and soft. Steam rose like little clouds. “Mmm,” said Suri. “They smell warm and sweet.”

Mama Bear lived in the house and baked with care. She wore a blue apron and sang, “Stir, stir, love and cheer.” Today she had baked muffins for her cubs, Bella and Ben.

Suri’s tummy rumbled. “Just one sniff,” she said. She climbed a chair and leaned close. The blueberries were shiny and purple. Her whiskers wiggled. “I can move one muffin to the basket,” she thought. “It will not hurt. I am faster at cleaning with a snack.”

But Suri paused. She remembered Mama Bear’s words from yesterday. “We ask before we take. We respect other people’s things.” Suri put her paws behind her back. “Respect means we care,” she whispered. “Respect means we ask.”

Just then, Bella and Ben skittered in with tiny footsteps. “Muffins!” they cheered. Their noses twitched. Their eyes sparkled.

Suri hopped down. “Good morning,” she said. “I like your muffins.”

“Thank you,” said Bella. “Mama Bear made them for us to share at snack time.”

Suri’s tummy rumbled again. Rrrrrrumble. Ben giggled. “Your tummy is singing!”

Suri felt her cheeks grow warm. “I wanted to take one,” she said softly. “But I didn’t ask. I am trying to be respectful.”

Bella nodded. “Thank you for telling the truth,” she said. “Honesty is kind.”

Ben looked at the plate. “Maybe we can be fair,” he said. “We can make a plan.”

Mama Bear came in, wiping her paws. She smelled like flour and vanilla. “Good morning, helpers,” she said. “I heard muffin talk.”

Suri stood tall on her tiny toes. “Mama Bear,” she said, “may I have a muffin later? I almost took one. I am sorry. I want to wait and ask.”

Mama Bear’s eyes were warm like cocoa. “Thank you for asking, Suri,” she said. “That is respectful. And thank you for being honest.”

Ben tugged on Mama’s apron. “Can we make the sharing fair?” he asked. “We can split one muffin now for Suri and save the rest for snack time.”

Mama Bear smiled. “That is a thoughtful idea. Fair means everyone is considered.” She looked at Suri. “Would half a muffin now be fair?”

Suri thought and tapped her paw. “Yes,” she said. “Half is fair because the muffins are for the cubs. I can wait for more later.”

Mama Bear cut a muffin in half. The muffin was warm. Blueberries popped with purple juice. The smell was cozy and sweet. Suri took her half and held it close. “Thank you,” she said.

They all sat together. The kettle sighed. Steam curled like ribbons. Suri took a tiny bite. “Mmm,” she hummed. “Soft and sweet.”

Bella licked a purple fingertip. “I like how you asked, Suri,” she said. “You respected our muffins.”

Suri nodded. “And I told the truth,” she said. “My tummy was loud.”

Ben giggled. “It was very loud!”

After the small snack, Suri got an idea. “I can be resourceful,” she said. “I will help more to show I care.” She swept crumbs into neat lines. She stacked napkins in a little tower. She wiped a tiny spot on the table with a soft cloth.

Bella and Ben joined in. Swipe, stack, wipe! The kitchen shone. The warm light danced on the clean table. Mama Bear clapped her paws. “What wonderful helpers! When we respect each other, we work well together.”

At snack time, Mama Bear brought out the plate again. Everyone received one muffin. Suri took the smallest one. “This is fair,” she said. “I had a half before.”

Bella pushed her muffin a little closer to Suri. “You can pick the muffin with the biggest blueberry,” she said kindly.

Suri smiled. “Thank you, Bella. That is generous. I feel happy.”

They ate and laughed. Blueberry juice made silly purple smiles. The kettle sang. The kitchen felt extra warm, not just from the oven, but from everyone’s kindness.

When the muffins were gone, Suri wrapped one crumb in a napkin. “This crumb is a reminder,” she said. “Respect means asking before taking. Honesty means telling the truth. Fairness means making a plan that feels right for everyone.”

Mama Bear nodded. “You discovered a big idea in our cozy kitchen,” she said. “Respect makes hearts warm.”

Suri tucked the crumb into her little basket. “I will remember,” she said. “I will be respectful, honest, and fair—every day.”

The End.
