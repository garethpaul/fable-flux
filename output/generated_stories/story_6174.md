# Bubbles Around the World

Benny the Busy Beaver liked to help. He helped stack sticks. He helped brush leaves. Today, he was helping in the laundry room. The laundry room smelled like warm soap. The machines hummed softly. Swish, swish. Hum, hum. Bubbles floated like tiny moons.

“Today I will wash and fold,” Benny said, patting his flat tail. “I am busy and I am careful.”

He opened a basket. Inside were socks. So many socks! Long socks, short socks, tiny socks, rainbow socks. Some had stripes. Some had polka dots. Some had little flags. Some had stars and moons.

Benny took a deep breath. He put his paw on his chest and said, “In… and out.” He liked to be mindful. He liked to go slow. It helped him do a good job.

“Hello, socks,” Benny chuckled. “Let’s go on a clean, bubbly ride.”

He poured in soap. He turned the dial. The washer started. Swish, swish. Benny watched bubbles spin and spin. The window fogged up. He drew a smiley face with his paw. The smiley face smiled back.

While the socks swam, Benny looked at the basket again. There was a little note inside. It said, “From friends around the world.” Benny blinked. “Wow,” he whispered. “These socks came from many places.”

Ding! The washer stopped. Benny opened the door. Warm steam puffed out like a friendly cloud. “Mmm, smells like lemon and sunshine,” Benny said. He lifted the socks into the dryer. Thump, thump. He pressed start. Whirr, whirr. The dryer hummed a cozy song.

When the dryer dinged, Benny pulled out a soft bundle. He sat cross-legged on the floor and began to match pairs.

He picked up a tiny blue sock with a white moon. “Hello, little sock,” Benny said. “Where are you from?”

A soft voice whispered, “I am from Turkey.” The sock wiggled. “In my home, people dance at weddings. We clap. We spin. We sing.”

Benny’s eyes grew wide. “What a joyful dance!” He clapped slowly, clap… clap… clap, and smiled.

He picked up a red sock with a green stripe. The sock said, “I am from Kenya. In my home, we tell stories under the stars. We listen to the night sing.”

Benny closed his eyes. He listened. He could almost hear crickets chirp and leaves whisper. “I love stories,” he said.

He picked up a white sock with tiny maple leaves. “I am from Canada,” it said. “I like snow and warm cocoa.”

Benny rubbed the sock on his cheek. It was soft and warm. “I like cocoa too,” he giggled.

He found a bright yellow sock with little suns. “I am from Brazil,” the sock sang. “We have big parades. We dance and shake-shake-shake!”

Benny shook his tail. Shake-shake-shake! He laughed so hard his whiskers wiggled.

He found a purple sock with gold dots. “I am from India. We make sweet treats for festivals,” it chimed.

Benny imagined the taste. “Mmm, sweet and spicy and kind,” he said.

He found a green sock with a shamrock. “I am from Ireland. We tell jokes and play music,” it giggled.

Benny tapped the floor with his paw. Tap-tap-tap. “I hear the music,” he smiled.

He found a blue-and-white striped sock. “I am from Greece. We look at the sea and share food with friends.”

Benny took a slow breath. In… and out. He imagined blue water. “That feels calm,” he said.

He found a rainbow sock with sparkles. It whispered, “I am me.” The sock glowed. “I like to be bright. I like to be different.”

Benny held the rainbow sock gently. “Being different is good,” he said. “Different is special. Different is beautiful.”

He looked at all the socks. Each one was unique. Each one had a story. Benny felt a warm feeling in his chest. “We are all part of one big world,” he said softly. “We are different, and we belong.”

But then, Benny saw a problem. He had one blue moon sock from Turkey, and one red-and-green sock from Kenya. He could not find their pairs. He looked under the table. He looked behind the basket. He looked in the dryer. No pairs!

Benny felt a tiny worry. His tail twitched. He put his paw on his belly. “In… and out,” he whispered. He listened to the hum of the machines. Hum, hum. He felt the warm floor. He smelled clean soap. His worry got smaller.

He had an idea. “Socks do not have to match to be friends,” he said. He held up the blue moon sock and the red-and-green sock. “You are both special. You can be a pair today.”

The socks giggled. “We can be buddies!” they cheered.

Benny matched more socks. Some were pairs. Some were happy mix-and-match buddies. He folded them into neat, cozy rolls. He made a little mountain of colors. It looked like a tiny rainbow hill.

He placed the socks back in the basket with the note. He added a new note: “From Benny, with care. Different is wonderful.”

The door opened. A friend peeked in. “Thank you, Benny!” they said. “These socks look so happy.”

Benny beamed. “They told me stories,” he said. “They taught me that we all have our own songs. We can listen. We can share. We can celebrate.”

He took one more breath. In… and out. The laundry room hummed. The lemon-soap smell wrapped him like a hug. Benny felt proud and peaceful.

“Today I learned,” he said, “that being different makes our world bright. When we listen kindly and take slow breaths, we can understand each other. We can be friends, even if we don’t match.”

The socks rustled in the basket, like a tiny clap. Benny waved his tail. “See you on the next adventure,” he whispered.

The End.
