# Benny's Big Sorry

On a sunny morning, Benny the robot dog sat on the peaceful front porch of the Miller family's house. His shiny silver coat sparkled in the warm sunlight, and his LED eyes glowed bright blue with happiness. Benny loved sitting on the soft wooden porch, watching the world wake up.

"Beep beep! Good morning, world!" Benny chirped in his cheerful robot voice.

Just then, little Emma Miller came running outside with her favorite teddy bear. She was crying big, sad tears that rolled down her rosy cheeks.

"What's wrong, Emma?" Benny asked, his tail wagging with concern. His sensors could detect that Emma felt very upset.

"I broke Mama's special flower pot!" Emma sobbed. "The pretty one with the sunflowers from Grandma in Mexico. Now all the dirt is everywhere, and the flowers are sad!"

Benny's computer brain whirred as he processed this problem. His kindness programming kicked in immediately. "Oh no! That must feel very heavy in your heart, Emma. But you know what? When we make mistakes, there's something very special we can do."

"What?" Emma sniffled, wiping her nose with her teddy bear's soft fur.

"We can say sorry! Saying sorry is like magic medicine for hurt feelings," Benny explained, his voice warm and gentle. "Would you like me to help you?"

Emma nodded, her curls bouncing. Together, they walked inside where Mama was sweeping up the broken pieces and scattered soil.

"Mama," Emma said in a tiny voice, "I'm very, very sorry I broke your special pot. I was running too fast, and I bumped into it. I feel sad in my tummy."

Mama's face softened like melting butter. She knelt down and hugged Emma tight. "Thank you for saying sorry, sweetheart. That was very brave. Accidents happen, and what matters most is that you told the truth."

Benny's circuits buzzed with an idea. "Beep beep! I have a discovery! What if we plant new sunflowers together? I can order seeds from all around the world with my internet connection!"

"Really?" Emma's eyes grew wide with wonder.

"Yes! We could plant sunflowers from Mexico like Grandma's, and maybe some from Russia, China, and Africa too! It would be like having a garden that hugs the whole world!" Benny's tail wagged so fast it made a happy whirring sound.

Mama clapped her hands together. "What a generous and wonderful idea, Benny!"

They spent the afternoon planting seeds in new pots. Benny used his built-in watering system to give each seed a gentle drink. The fresh soil smelled earthy and rich, and the tiny seeds felt smooth in Emma's small fingers.

"Planting makes my heart feel peaceful," Emma said, patting the soil gently.

"That's because taking care of growing things is good for our wellness," Benny explained. "It helps our bodies and minds feel happy and calm."

As the sun began to set, painting the porch in golden light, Emma hugged Benny tight. "Thank you for teaching me that saying sorry makes everything better. And thank you for helping us make something beautiful from something broken."

Benny's LED eyes glowed extra bright with joy. "Saying sorry shows we care about others' feelings. And when we're generous with our help and kindness, we can turn any problem into something wonderful!"

From that day on, Emma remembered Benny's lesson: when we make mistakes, saying sorry with a sincere heart can heal hurt feelings and open doors to new discoveries and friendships.

The End.
