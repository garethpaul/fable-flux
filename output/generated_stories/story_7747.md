# Fawn's Big Feelings in the Attic

Little Fawn loved to explore. One sunny morning, she climbed the creaky wooden stairs to Grandma Doe's quiet attic. The dusty air smelled like old books and lavender sachets. Sunbeams danced through the small window, making everything sparkle.

"Wow!" whispered Fawn, her big brown eyes wide with wonder. She saw boxes full of treasures, old quilts, and shiny picture frames.

But then Fawn spotted something special - a beautiful music box with a tiny dancing ballerina. Her heart jumped with excitement. She reached for it with her small hooves.

CRASH!

The music box tumbled to the floor and broke into pieces. The little ballerina's arm snapped off, and the pretty melody stopped forever.

Fawn's tummy felt all twisty. Her chest felt tight and heavy. Tears filled her eyes as she looked at the broken pieces.

"Oh no, oh no!" she cried. "What did I do?"

Fawn felt scared and sad at the same time. She wanted to run away and hide. Her little heart was beating fast like a drum.

"Maybe... maybe no one will notice," she whispered to herself. She pushed the broken pieces under an old blanket with her hoof.

But hiding didn't make Fawn feel better. In fact, she felt worse! Her tummy hurt even more, and she couldn't stop thinking about the broken music box.

Fawn sat quietly in the dusty attic, listening to her feelings. She closed her eyes and took deep breaths like Mama Doe taught her.

"I feel scared," she said out loud. "I feel sad. And I feel... sorry."

Suddenly, Fawn heard gentle hoofsteps on the stairs. Grandma Doe appeared at the top, her kind eyes twinkling.

"Hello, little one," Grandma Doe said softly. "I heard a crash. Is everything okay?"

Fawn's heart pounded. She looked at the blanket hiding the broken music box. She looked at Grandma Doe's gentle face.

Taking a big, brave breath, Fawn said, "Grandma, I need to tell you something important."

With shaky hooves, Fawn pulled away the blanket. "I broke your music box. I was curious and wanted to see it, but I was too excited and knocked it over. I'm really, really sorry."

Tears rolled down Fawn's soft cheeks. "I tried to hide it because I felt scared, but hiding made my tummy feel worse."

Grandma Doe smiled and sat down next to Fawn. "Oh, sweet little one, thank you for telling me the truth. That took courage."

"But I broke something special," Fawn sniffled.

"Yes, you did," Grandma Doe nodded. "And you felt scared and sad about it. Those are normal feelings when we make mistakes."

Grandma Doe gently hugged Fawn. "But you listened to your feelings and chose to be honest. That shows you're growing up and learning to be responsible."

Together, they carefully picked up the pieces. "Maybe we can fix it together," Grandma Doe suggested. "And next time, what could you do differently?"

Fawn thought carefully. "I could ask before touching special things. And if I break something, I should tell the truth right away instead of hiding."

"That's exactly right," Grandma Doe smiled. "You've learned something very important today about feelings and being brave."

Fawn felt the heavy feeling in her chest float away. Being honest felt so much better than hiding.

The End.
