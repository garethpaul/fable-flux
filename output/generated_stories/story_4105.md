# Benny Bunny Breathes and Helps

Benny was a determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a cozy bedroom with a warm yellow lamp, a fluffy blue rug, and a little basket of books by his bed. The window let in morning light that felt like a hug. His blanket smelled like sunshine and clean laundry. Today, Benny had big plans.

“I will clean my room. I will make my bed. I will help my friends,” Benny said proudly, puffing out his chest. He wanted to do it all. He wanted to do it now.

Benny looked at his floor. Blocks were everywhere like colorful little mountains. Crayons peeked out from under his pillow. His favorite drum sat in the corner, quiet and still. Benny’s nose wiggled with excitement.

“I can do it!” he said.

He started fast. He grabbed books and stacked them high. He scooped crayons and dropped them in the wrong box. He tried to pull his blanket tight, but it went crooked. The stack of books leaned. The crayons rolled out. The blanket slid to the floor with a soft whoosh.

Benny’s ears drooped. His chest felt tight. His cheeks felt hot. “Oh no,” he whispered. “It’s too much.”

Just then, his neighbor, Mia Mouse, knocked on the door. Tap tap. “Benny, can you help me make cards for the park clean-up?” she squeaked. “We want to invite friends to volunteer!”

Benny liked helping. He liked volunteering. But his room was a mess. His tummy felt fluttery, like tiny birds flapping. He wanted to say yes. He also wanted to finish his room.

Benny remembered something his grandma had taught him. “Bunny Breaths,” she had said. “When your heart feels busy, breathe slow. Smell the flower. Blow the candle.”

Benny closed his eyes. He put one paw on his belly. He took a slow breath in through his nose, sniffing like a bunny. He smelled his clean blanket and a hint of soap. Then he breathed out slowly through his mouth, like blowing out a candle. In… and out. In… and out. His belly rose. His belly fell. The fluttery birds in his tummy calmed down.

Mia waited, ears twitching. “Benny?”

Benny opened his eyes. He felt steadier, like a rock by a stream. He was determined, but now he was calm too. He practiced being assertive, just like his teacher had taught him. “Use your strong, kind voice,” she had said.

“Mia,” Benny said in a clear, gentle voice, “I want to help you. Volunteering is important. I also need to finish my room. I can help after I clean. Can we make cards later this morning?”

Mia smiled. “That is a good plan. I will come back after snack time.” Tap tap went her tiny feet as she left.

Benny breathed again. In… and out. He looked at his room with soft eyes. “One thing at a time,” he told himself. “I can do this.”

He made a little plan out loud, like a song. “First the bed, smooth and flat. Then the books, nice like that. Crayons next, in the box. Then the blocks, clack clack clox.”

He tugged the blanket slowly, feeling the warm, fuzzy fabric under his paws. Smooth. He patted the pillow. Soft. He stacked the books with care and listened to the gentle shhh of pages sliding. He picked up crayons and heard the tiny tap, tap, tap as they dropped into their box. He marched the blocks into their bin. Clack clack. His ears perked up. His chest felt roomy again.

When he was done, his room looked bright and happy. Sunlight made shapes on the rug. Benny smiled so big his whiskers tickled his cheeks.

Tap tap. Mia returned with shiny paper and colorful markers. She also brought Mr. Owl’s flyer that said, “Park Clean-Up! Come help our trees and flowers!”

“Let’s be mindful as we make the cards,” Benny said, remembering his breaths. “Slow and careful. One stroke at a time.”

They sat on the fluffy rug. Benny breathed in and smelled the sweet ink of the markers and the soft cotton of his blanket. Mia giggled at the tiny squeak the marker made on paper. Squeak, swoosh, squeak.

“Dear friends,” Benny said, sounding brave and kind, “please come volunteer at the park. We will pick up trash and help the flowers. We will be gentle with the earth.”

Mia nodded. “We will bring water. We will wear hats. We will share smiles.”

They made card after card, not rushing. When Mia wanted to write too fast, Benny whispered, “Bunny Breaths.” In… and out. Mia’s whiskers relaxed. The letters looked neat and friendly.

Soon, they were done. The cards looked cheerful with little hearts and green trees. Benny stood tall. “Let’s deliver them,” he said firmly but kindly. “We can ask our friends to join us.”

As they handed out the cards, Benny used his assertive voice again. “Will you come help the park? We will work together. We will make it clean and lovely.” His words were clear and polite.

Their friends smiled. “Yes!” said Tilly Turtle. “We love helping.” “Me too!” chirped Pip Sparrow.

That afternoon, everyone met at the park. They picked up wrappers with careful paws. They said thank you to the trees. They breathed the fresh air. Benny felt proud. He had used Bunny Breaths. He had been kind and strong. He had finished his room. He had helped his friends. He had volunteered to care for his home.

On the way back, Benny whispered, “When my heart feels busy, I breathe. I choose my next best step. I speak up with kindness. I help where I can.”

Mia squeezed his paw. “You did all three,” she said. “Mindful. Helpful. Brave.”

Benny smiled. Back in his cozy bedroom, he curled under his clean blanket. It smelled like sunshine again. He took one more slow Bunny Breath. In… and out.

The End.
