# Brave Bubbles in the Blue Bathroom

Benny was a friendly bear cub with soft, fuzzy ears and a button nose. He lived with Mama Bear in a cozy den. Their bathroom was painted sky blue, with silver stars on the ceiling that twinkled when the light was on. The floor felt cool and smooth, and the room smelled like sweet soap and peppermint toothpaste.

Every night, Mama Bear would say, “Bath time, Benny!” and turn the shiny silver tap. Water rushed and sang, splish-splash, splish-splash, into the tub. Tiny bubbles rose and popped with a gentle snap.

But tonight, Benny stood at the door and held his breath. His paws felt wiggly. His tummy did a tiny flip. “Mama,” Benny whispered, “I don’t want bubbles. I don’t want bath time.”

Mama Bear knelt to his level. “Tell me what you feel, honey,” she said softly.

Benny’s eyes were big. “The water is loud. The bubbles are too many. I feel small,” he said. He touched his chest. “My heart is thumpy.”

Mama Bear nodded. “Thank you for telling me. That is brave already.” She looked at the tub. The water swirled, and steam made little clouds. “We can try something new,” she said. “Would you like to help?”

Benny blinked. “Help?” His ears perked up.

“Yes,” Mama Bear said. “We can make a brave plan.”

Benny took a tiny step into the bathroom. The bath mat felt soft like moss. “Okay,” he whispered.

Mama Bear turned the tap down so the water sang quieter. “First,” she said, “we listen.” She closed her eyes and hummed. “Do you hear the water say hello? Splish... splash...”

Benny listened. The sound was not a roar now. It was a sleepy stream. “Splish... splash,” he echoed, and his shoulders relaxed.

“Second,” Mama Bear said, “we choose one brave thing to try.” She held up a small blue cup. “Would you like to pour one cup of water into the tub to say hello back?”

Benny took the cup. It was smooth and cool. He dipped it in the sink, filled it, and poured. The water made a soft ploop. “Hello, tub,” Benny said. “I am Benny.”

“Very brave,” Mama Bear smiled. “Third, we make it fun.” She showed him two bath toys: a yellow duck and a tiny boat with a red flag. “Which friend will help you?”

Benny picked the duck. “Quack,” he said in a squeaky voice. He placed the duck on the water. It bobbed and wiggled. Benny giggled. “It tickles my eyes!”

Mama Bear added a drop of gentle soap. The bubbles grew slowly, like little clouds in the sky. “We can have small bubbles today,” she said. “You tell me when it’s enough.”

“That’s enough,” Benny said when the bubbles reached the duck’s shoulders.

“Good choice,” Mama Bear said. “You are the boss of your brave.”

Benny touched the water with one paw. It was warm like a hug. He felt his heart slow down. “It’s nice,” he said, surprised. He tried the other paw. Then he let his toes dip in. The bubbles kissed his fur with tiny pops. Pop, pop, pop!

“Quack,” Benny said again, braver now. “Duck says, ‘Come sail!’”

Mama Bear held his paws as he stepped into the tub. The water hugged him up to his belly. He took a big belly breath in and out. “I did it,” he whispered.

“You did it,” Mama Bear echoed. “How does your body feel?”

“Warm. Soft. Happy,” Benny said. He made the duck swim around the boat. “Open mind,” he added slowly, remembering something Mama had said once. “That means trying new things.”

Mama Bear smiled. “Yes. You were open-minded, and you took care of your body. That’s wellness—keeping your body clean and your heart calm.”

Benny swished the water and made gentle waves. He listened to the tiny pops and the soft slosh. He smelled the clean soap. He saw the steam clouds float up and fade. He felt brave in his belly and light in his paws.

“Can I wash my fur?” Benny asked. Mama Bear gave him a soft washcloth. Benny rubbed his arms, his neck, behind his ears. The cloth felt squishy and warm. “I am strong and brave,” he sang, making up a bath song. “Splish, splash, bubbles flash!”

When it was time to rinse, Benny held the cup. “I can pour,” he said. The water trickled like rain on leaves. “That feels nice,” he giggled.

Mama Bear wrapped him in a big, fluffy towel that smelled like sunshine. “I am proud of you,” she whispered.

Benny’s eyes were bright. “At first I was scared,” he said. “Then I listened. I tried one brave thing. I made it fun. I kept my body clean. I did hard things.”

Mama Bear nodded. “That is a brave plan we can use anytime.”

Benny patted his clean fur. It felt silky and soft. He looked at the tub and waved. “Good job, tub,” he said. “You were gentle today.”

As they brushed teeth, the peppermint taste tingled on Benny’s tongue. He laughed, “Minty! It’s like tiny snowflakes dancing!”

Mama Bear turned off the light. The silver stars on the ceiling glowed softly. Benny yawned a happy bear yawn. “Next bath,” he said, “I will bring the boat and the duck. I will be brave again.”

“Brave and open,” Mama Bear said.

“And well,” Benny added, patting his chest.

They hugged. Benny felt calm, clean, and proud.

The End.
