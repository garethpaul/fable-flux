# Ollie's Musical Adventure

Ollie the otter loved to splash and play, but today was special. His teacher, Mrs. Robin, had invited him to the music room to learn about instruments from around the world.

"Welcome, Ollie!" chirped Mrs. Robin as she opened the shiny wooden door. The room smelled like fresh flowers and old books. Colorful instruments hung on the walls - drums from Africa, flutes from Ireland, and bells from India.

Ollie's whiskers twitched with excitement. "Wow! Can I touch everything?" he squeaked, reaching his wet paws toward a beautiful golden drum.

"Wait, Ollie," said Mrs. Robin gently. "First, we need to wash and dry your paws. These special instruments need to stay clean and safe."

Ollie's tail drooped. "But I want to play NOW!" He felt frustrated bubbles growing in his tummy.

Mrs. Robin smiled kindly. "I understand you're excited. Let's take three deep breaths together. In... and out... In... and out... In... and out. Better?"

Ollie nodded, feeling the frustrated bubbles float away. "Yes, thank you."

"Now," said Mrs. Robin, "let's follow the steps together. First, we wash our paws at the little sink."

The warm, soapy water felt nice on Ollie's paws. He scrubbed carefully, watching the soap bubbles dance. "This is actually fun!" he giggled.

"Step two - dry your paws with this soft towel," instructed Mrs. Robin.

Ollie patted his paws dry. They felt clean and fluffy.

"Step three - we'll learn about each instrument before we play it," said Mrs. Robin. "This helps us show respect for different cultures."

She pointed to the golden drum. "This djembe comes from West Africa. People there use it to tell stories and celebrate."

"It's beautiful," whispered Ollie, his eyes wide with wonder.

"Step four - we play gently and carefully," said Mrs. Robin, showing Ollie how to tap the drum with his palms.

BOOM-tap-tap! The drum sang a deep, happy song. Ollie's whole body wiggled with joy.

Next, Mrs. Robin showed him Irish tin whistles. "These make music that sounds like birds singing," she explained. When Ollie blew gently, sweet notes floated through the air like butterflies.

Then came the Indian bells. "Ring them softly," instructed Mrs. Robin. The tiny bells chimed like raindrops on leaves.

"I'm following all the steps!" Ollie said proudly. "And I'm learning so much!"

"You're doing wonderfully," praised Mrs. Robin. "When we follow directions, we can enjoy things safely and learn about the amazing world around us."

Ollie practiced each instrument, remembering to be gentle and respectful. When he felt frustrated because the flute was tricky, he took deep breaths just like Mrs. Robin taught him.

As the lesson ended, Ollie helped clean up, carefully placing each instrument back in its special spot.

"Thank you for teaching me to follow directions," said Ollie, giving Mrs. Robin a gentle hug. "I learned about music AND about being patient!"

"You're very welcome," said Mrs. Robin. "Remember, following directions helps us learn new things and keeps everyone safe and happy."

Ollie splashed home, humming the melodies from around the world, excited to practice his new skills tomorrow.

The End.
