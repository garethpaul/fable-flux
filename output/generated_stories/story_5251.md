# Coco’s Bedtime Big Goal

Coco the courageous monkey lived in a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls and a fluffy green rug. His bed had banana-print sheets that smelled like fresh laundry. A small night-light glowed like a friendly star. On the shelf, shiny medals and ribbons clinked softly when the window breeze whispered in.

Coco loved sports. He loved to bounce, run, and stretch. His big goal was taped above his bed in crayon letters: “Be kind. Try my best. Play fair.” Every night he read it out loud.

One evening, Coco wiggled into his pajama pants. “Tomorrow is Team Pillow Race!” he cheered, tail swishing. The Team Pillow Race was a bedtime game. Everyone hopped on pillows and slid across the rug like little sleds. Coco wanted to win. He wanted to hear the medals clink for him.

Mama Monkey tucked him in. “What’s your goal, Coco?”

“Be kind. Try my best. Play fair,” he said with a brave smile.

“Good,” Mama said. “Your heart is the best medal.”

The next night, friends came over for a cozy sleepover. There was Tippy the tall giraffe, soft and careful. There was Dot the tiny ladybug, bright red with happy dots, and Bo the sleepy sloth, slow but strong. The room smelled like cocoa and warm banana muffins. The night-light made tiny stars on the ceiling.

Coach Bear, Coco’s stuffed bear with a whistle on a ribbon, sat at the end of the bed. Coco picked him up. “Coach Bear says we start soon!”

“Yay!” Tippy clapped, hooves tapping the rug. Dot zipped in little loops. Bo stretched and yawned a slow, “I’m ready.”

Coco felt his brave heart thump. “I’m fast,” he said. “I might win.”

Dot hovered by her pillow. “I’m small. My pillow is big,” she said, tiny voice wiggly.

Tippy lowered his long neck. “I’m tall. My legs might bump,” he worried.

Bo blinked. “I go slow. Will you wait?” he asked softly.

Coco’s tail flicked. He wanted to race fast. He wanted gold stars and clinks. He looked at his goal on the wall. Be kind. Try my best. Play fair.

He took a breath. The room smelled sweet and safe. “We will help each other,” he said. “We respect each other’s ways.”

“How?” asked Dot.

Coco thought, feeling the rug’s tickly threads under his toes. “We set team goals,” he said. “Goal one: We listen. Goal two: We make space. Goal three: We cheer for differences.”

Tippy smiled. Bo sighed happily. Dot’s wings hummed like a tiny song.

They lined up on their pillows. Coco’s banana pillow was just right. Tippy’s long pillow made room for her legs. Bo’s pillow was squishy for slow glides. Dot had a small, soft washcloth pillow so she would not sink.

“On your pillows, friends!” Coco called. “Coach Bear says—ready, set—glide!”

They pushed off. Swish! Coco slid fast. The rug felt smooth and cool under his hands. Dot zipped but wobbled. “Help!” she squeaked, her washcloth pillow tilting.

Coco could speed ahead. He could win now. His brave heart thumped. He looked at Dot’s tiny eyes. He looked at the words on the wall. Be kind.

“I’ve got you!” Coco said. He slowed, steering with his tail. He put a gentle finger on Dot’s pillow to steady it. “You can do it.”

“Thank you,” Dot buzzed. Her wings settled. She smiled, brave and bright.

Tippy’s legs bumped the toy box with a bonk. “Oops! My knees!” she said.

Coco waved. “We make space!” He moved the toy box aside, the wooden lid cool and smooth. “Go, Tippy!”

Bo came last, gliding slow and steady. “I’m coming,” he said, voice like a warm blanket.

Coco saw the finish line—a line of socks by the door. He could jump and win. But Bo was still far. Coco remembered Goal three: We cheer for differences.

“Team cheer!” Coco called. “Go, Bo! Slow and strong!” Dot chimed, “Go, Bo!” Tippy sang, “Go, Bo!” Their voices made the room feel cozy and bright.

Bo smiled and glided over the sock line. Swish. Everyone finished together. Coco’s medals on the shelf clinked as if they were clapping.

Coach Bear got a hug from all. Coco felt warm and proud. Not just proud-fast. Proud-kind.

Mama Monkey peeked in, the hall light soft like honey. “How did the Team Pillow Race go?” she asked.

Coco held up Coach Bear. “We set goals,” he said. “We listened. We made space. We cheered. We respected each other.”

Dot buzzed, “Coco helped me.”

Tippy nodded. “We celebrated how we are different.”

Bo yawned a big happy yawn. “I finished with friends.”

Mama smiled. “That sounds like a win for hearts.”

Coco looked at his wall. He picked up a crayon. Carefully, he added a new line to his big goal: “Respect others, always.” He read it out loud, each word like a soft drumbeat. “Be kind. Try my best. Play fair. Respect others, always.”

He looked at his friends. “Tonight, we all win.”

Tippy wrapped her long neck in a gentle hug. Dot landed on Coco’s ear, whispering, “Thank you.” Bo patted Coco’s shoulder with a warm, slow hand.

The night-light glowed. The room smelled like cocoa and clean sheets. The rug felt soft under sleepy toes. Coco’s brave heart felt big and calm.

“Goodnight, team,” Coco whispered. “Tomorrow we can make new goals.”

“Goodnight,” everyone said.

Coco cuddled Coach Bear and closed his eyes, smiling. He had a medal in his chest now, made of kindness and respect.

The End.
