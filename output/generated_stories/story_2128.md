# Nutkin's Helpful Day

Nutkin the squirrel stretched his fluffy tail in his cozy treehouse. The morning sun filtered through the leafy branches, making golden patterns on his wooden floor. Today felt special, but Nutkin wasn't sure why.

"Tweet! Tweet!" called Robin from outside. "Nutkin, we need your help! The big storm last night made such a mess in the forest!"

Nutkin peeked out his round window. Twigs and leaves were scattered everywhere. Some of the smaller animals looked worried and confused.

"Oh my!" said Nutkin. "That does look like a big job." He felt a little overwhelmed. There was so much to clean up!

But then Nutkin remembered what Grandpa Squirrel always said: "Big jobs become small when you take them one acorn at a time."

"I can help!" Nutkin called down to Robin. "But let me make a plan first."

Nutkin sat at his tiny wooden table and thought carefully. He needed to stay calm and organized. He drew pictures on a piece of bark to remember his tasks:
1. Clear the main path
2. Help gather scattered nuts
3. Fix Mrs. Rabbit's broken fence

"One task at a time," Nutkin said to himself, taking a deep breath. The fresh morning air smelled like pine and rain.

First, Nutkin scampered down to the main forest path. He moved branches carefully, one by one. When he felt like rushing, he reminded himself to slow down. "Steady paws make safe work," he whispered.

Little Mouse was crying nearby. "I can't find my acorns anywhere!" she squeaked.

"Don't worry," said Nutkin kindly. "I'll help you look after I finish this path." He showed Mouse his bark plan. "See? Helping you is my second task."

Mouse smiled. "You're so organized, Nutkin!"

After clearing the path, Nutkin helped Mouse search under every leaf and log. His sharp eyes spotted her acorns hidden under a fallen branch. The smooth nuts felt cool in his paws.

"Thank you, Nutkin!" Mouse hugged his fluffy tail.

Finally, Nutkin found Mrs. Rabbit by her broken garden fence. The wooden posts lay scattered like pickup sticks.

"This looks too hard to fix," sighed Mrs. Rabbit.

"Not if we work together," said Nutkin. "I'll hold the posts steady while you tie them with vine."

Working together, they rebuilt the fence piece by piece. Nutkin's paws were getting tired, but he felt proud seeing each post stand tall again.

"There!" said Mrs. Rabbit. "My garden is safe again, thanks to you!"

As the sun climbed higher, Nutkin looked around the forest. The path was clear, Mouse had her acorns, and Mrs. Rabbit's fence stood strong. He had completed every task on his list!

"Nutkin!" called all his forest friends together. "You helped everyone today!"

Nutkin's heart felt warm and full. "It felt good to help," he said. "And breaking big jobs into small tasks made everything easier!"

That evening, Nutkin climbed back to his treehouse. He felt wonderfully tired and happy. Through his window, he could see the neat, tidy forest below.

"Tomorrow, I'll make another plan to help," Nutkin yawned contentedly. "One task at a time always works!"

He curled up in his soft nest of leaves, proud that he had stayed calm, completed all his tasks, and helped his friends. The forest was peaceful again, and Nutkin had learned that being organized and helpful made every day special.

The End.
