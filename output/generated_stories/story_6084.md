# Nutkin's Colorful Surprise

Nutkin the squirrel loved exploring. Today, he scampered through the open window of a cozy home office. His fluffy tail twitched with excitement as he looked around the bright room.

"Wow!" Nutkin squeaked. The office was full of wonderful things. There was a big wooden desk, shelves lined with colorful books, and best of all—art supplies everywhere! Paintbrushes stood in tall jars like tiny trees. Tubes of paint sparkled like rainbow gems in the morning sunlight.

Nutkin's whiskers tickled with curiosity. He had never seen so many beautiful colors before. Red like autumn leaves, blue like the sky, yellow like sunshine, and green like his favorite acorns!

"I want to make something pretty too," Nutkin said to himself. He grabbed a paintbrush with his tiny paws and dipped it in bright purple paint. The brush felt smooth and cool.

Splash! Nutkin painted a wobbly line on the white paper. "This is fun!" he chittered happily. Soon he was painting everywhere—on the paper, on the desk, and even on his own fluffy fur!

Purple paw prints appeared across the important papers. Blue drops splattered on the computer keyboard. Yellow paint dripped down the chair legs like melted butter.

"Oh no!" Nutkin gasped when he saw the colorful mess. His heart felt heavy like a stone. The office looked like a rainbow had exploded!

Just then, he heard footsteps. "Sarah's coming!" Nutkin whispered. Sarah was the kind lady who worked in this office. She always left nuts on the windowsill for him.

Nutkin wanted to hide, but he remembered what his mama always said: "Honest squirrels tell the truth, even when it's scary."

When Sarah walked in, her eyes grew wide. "Oh my!" she said, looking at the paint everywhere.

Nutkin took a deep breath. His little heart went thump-thump-thump. "I'm sorry, Sarah," he squeaked in his tiniest voice. "I wanted to make art like you do. But I made a big mess instead."

Sarah looked surprised to hear Nutkin talk, but then she smiled warmly. "You wanted to create something beautiful? That's wonderful, Nutkin! But let's learn how to paint the right way."

"You're not angry?" Nutkin asked, his dark eyes hopeful.

"Everyone makes mistakes when they're learning," Sarah said gently. "The important thing is that you told the truth. Now, let's work together to clean up and then I'll teach you about art!"

Nutkin's tail swished with joy. Together, they wiped up the paint with soft cloths. The cleaning cloths smelled fresh like flowers. Sarah showed Nutkin how to hold the brush properly and how to keep paint only on the paper.

"Art is about expressing your feelings," Sarah explained. "What makes you happy, Nutkin?"

"Acorns! And friendship! And sunny days!" Nutkin chirped.

Sarah helped him paint a picture of all his favorite things. They painted golden acorns, smiling faces, and a bright yellow sun. Nutkin's painting wasn't perfect, but it was full of love and joy.

"Thank you for teaching me," Nutkin said, admiring their artwork. "And thank you for forgiving my mistake."

"Thank you for being honest," Sarah replied. "That's what real friends do."

From that day on, Nutkin visited Sarah's office every week. They painted together, shared stories, and created beautiful art side by side. Nutkin learned that making mistakes was okay, but telling the truth and making things right was what mattered most.

The End.
