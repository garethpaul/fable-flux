# Pebble the Penguin and the Cozy Guest Room

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright, shiny eyes and a happy waddle. One sunny afternoon, Pebble visited his cousin, Poppy, who lived in a house with a soft, cozy guest room. The guest room had a big window with warm light, a fluffy green rug, and a little basket of toys that made a gentle clack when Pebble touched them.

“Oh, this room feels like a hug,” Pebble said, rubbing his flippers together. He loved the smell of clean blankets, like fresh snow and soap.

Poppy waddled in with a smile. “Pebble, let’s play! The guest room is our special play space today,” she said.

Pebble saw a shiny red truck on the rug. It had smooth wheels and a silver bell that went ting-ting. “I want the truck!” Pebble chirped. He rushed over and scooped it up.

“I was going to play with the truck,” Poppy said softly, her beak wobbling. She reached out a flipper. “Can we take turns?”

Pebble hugged the truck close. “But I saw it first,” he said. His voice sounded tight, like a stuck door. He plopped down on the rug and rolled the truck back and forth. Ting-ting. Ting-ting.

Poppy’s eyes grew shiny. “That hurts my feelings,” she said. “I wanted to share with you.”

Pebble felt a pinch in his tummy. He looked at the window and saw his reflection: one happy eye and one worried eye. The guest room felt less cozy now. It felt quiet and heavy.

A gentle knock came at the door. Aunt Penny, Poppy’s mom, peeked in. “Hello, little penguins,” she said in a warm, sandy voice. “I heard quiet. Is everything okay?”

Poppy sniffled. “Pebble took the truck, and he won’t share.”

Aunt Penny waddled in and sat on the edge of the bed. The blanket made a soft swish. “Hmm,” she said kindly. “This is a good time to practice our sharing plan. In this guest room, we have special house rules. We take turns, we use kind words, and we care for the toys so guests feel welcome.”

Pebble looked down at the shiny truck. He liked the ting-ting sound. He also liked Poppy. He did not like Poppy’s shiny eyes. “I… I don’t want to give it up,” he whispered. His flippers felt warm and jittery.

Aunt Penny nodded. “It’s hard to wait. Waiting is a big skill. But we can make it easier. We can set a timer. Pebble can have the truck for two minutes. Then it’s Poppy’s turn for two minutes. While we wait, we choose a waiting toy.”

Pebble’s tummy pinch softened. He liked plans. “Okay,” he said. “Timer, please.”

Poppy wiped her eyes. “Thank you,” she said. “I can wait if I know my turn is coming.”

Aunt Penny set a little kitchen timer on the bed. It tick-tick-ticked like tiny footsteps. “Pebble, you have two minutes,” she said. “Poppy, would you like the blue blocks while you wait?”

“Yes,” Poppy said, stacking blue blocks with soft thumps. “I’ll make a garage!”

Pebble rolled the truck in a circle around Poppy’s blocks. Ting-ting. He made a vroom sound that was really more like a happy hum. The timer ticked, and Pebble felt calm. He knew the plan.

Tick. Tick. Tock! The timer chimed, ding-ding.

Pebble took a big breath. He held the truck out to Poppy. “Your turn,” he said. His voice was lighter now. Poppy smiled, wide and bright.

“Thank you, Pebble,” she said. She drove the truck into the blue block garage. Ting! “Do you want to open the door for the truck?” she asked.

Pebble clapped. “Yes!” He lifted a block like a door. The truck rolled in with a soft rumble. They both giggled.

Aunt Penny smiled. “I see kind words, sharing, and a plan. That’s conflict-resolution,” she said. She tapped her beak gently. “When there is a problem, we use calm voices, we listen, and we make a fair plan. That is being responsible and kind.”

After a few turns, Pebble said, “Poppy, can we build a road?”

“Yes!” Poppy said. They lined up pillows for hills and used a scarf for a river. The truck tinged over the bridge, and Pebble made splashy sounds. The guest room felt warm and friendly again.

Soon, Aunt Penny brought a small basket. “Time to tidy,” she said. “When we are done playing, we put toys back. That is responsibility too.”

Pebble nodded. He carefully placed the truck in the basket. Poppy stacked the blocks inside with quiet clicks. Pebble smoothed the rug with his flipper. Swish, swish.

“I’m sorry I didn’t share at first,” Pebble said. “I felt grabby. Next time, I will use our plan.”

Poppy gave him a gentle penguin hug. “Thank you,” she said. “I felt sad, but now I feel happy. We solved our problem together.”

Pebble felt light as a snowflake. “We did it! Share, listen, plan, and care,” he sang. Aunt Penny laughed and clapped.

As the sun made a golden square on the rug, Pebble and Poppy sat side by side. The guest room smelled like clean blankets and hot cocoa from the kitchen. Pebble smiled. “Sharing makes the room cozier,” he said.

“And our hearts too,” Poppy added.

They wiggled their feet and giggled. Pebble thought, I can be kind. I can share. I can solve problems. That felt like a superpower.

The End.
