# Ollie Otter’s Table Time Turn-Taking

Ollie was a playful otter with bright eyes and a wiggly tail. He loved to explore, splash, and learn new things. Today, Ollie was at home in the dining room. The wooden table was smooth and shiny. The chairs were cozy and soft. Sunlight made warm, golden squares on the floor. Ollie could smell warm bread and sweet berries. His tummy rumbled. “Mmm,” he giggled. “Lunch time!”

Mama Otter set a basket on the table. “We will make tasty sandwiches,” she said. “We will learn about taking turns and showing respect.”

Ollie bounced in his chair. “I can do it! I can do it!” he said. His paws pat-patted the table with excitement.

Grandpa Otter waddled in with a funny hat and a big smile. “Hello, little splasher,” he said. “I brought a book about table manners.” He held up a small, colorful book. The cover showed happy animals sitting and smiling at a table.

Ollie sniffed the bread. It smelled warm and soft. He touched a tomato slice. It felt cool and smooth. He heard the quiet hum of the fridge and the tiny tick-tock of the clock. Everything felt calm and happy.

Mama Otter put out the food. “Bread, berries, cheese, and lettuce,” she said. “We will pass each bowl around the table. We take turns. We wait. We listen. That is respect.”

Ollie’s whiskers twitched. He reached for the berries right away. His paw swooped, and a berry rolled off the plate. Plop!

Grandpa Otter chuckled. “Oops! Let’s try again. First, we ask. Then, we wait.”

Ollie pulled his paw back. He took a deep breath. “Can I please have some berries?” he asked.

Mama Otter smiled. “Yes, Ollie. Thank you for asking with kind words. I will pass them to you when it is your turn.”

The bowl went to Grandpa first. Grandpa took a few berries. “I will take a little, and leave plenty for others,” he said. “That is respect, too.”

Then the bowl came to Mama. She took a few. “Thank you,” she said. “Now it is Ollie’s turn.”

Ollie’s eyes sparkled. He took a small scoop. “Thank you,” he said.

“Wonderful,” Mama said. “We showed respect. We took turns. We used kind words.”

Grandpa opened the little book. He read in a gentle voice. “At the table, we listen with our ears, look with our eyes, and wait with our paws.” Ollie listened. He felt proud. He liked learning new things.

Next came the cheese. Ollie wanted lots and lots. He reached fast again. His paw bumped the plate. The cheese wobbled.

Ollie stopped. He remembered the book. He pulled his paw back and folded it in his lap. He asked, “May I please have some cheese?”

Mama nodded. “Yes, Ollie. Thank you for waiting.” She passed the plate. “How much do you need?”

Ollie thought. He looked at Grandpa and Mama. “I will take a little, and leave plenty for others,” he said, copying Grandpa’s words. He took two slices. He felt warm and happy inside. Respect felt good.

They built their sandwiches together. The bread was soft. The lettuce was crunchy. The cheese was creamy. The berries were juicy and sweet. “Crunch, munch, slurp,” went the sounds. Ollie wiggled with joy.

Grandpa smiled. “You know, respect is not just for food. Respect is for people, too. We respect bodies, feelings, and words.”

Ollie tilted his head. “What does that mean?”

“It means we look at someone’s face. We listen when they talk. We wait for our turn to speak,” Mama said.

Ollie nodded. “I can try.” He looked at Mama’s face. “Mama, how is your sandwich?” he asked.

Mama’s eyes shone. “Thank you for asking and waiting. My sandwich is yummy! How is yours?”

Ollie waited one beat. “It is yummy and crunchy,” he said.

Grandpa turned a page. “Respect helps us learn. When we listen, we get knowledge.” He pointed to a picture in the book. “See this? This shows sharing. This shows taking turns with a spoon.”

Ollie picked up the jam spoon. He held it tight. He wanted more jam right away. Then he saw Mama waiting. He saw Grandpa watching kindly. Ollie felt the spoon in his paw. It was cool and smooth. He took a deep breath and smiled. “Grandpa, would you like the spoon?” he asked.

“Yes, thank you,” Grandpa said. “That was very respectful.”

Ollie felt proud again. His chest felt light, like a little bubble. He liked this new feeling.

When their tummies were full, Mama brought a small puzzle to the table. “Let’s learn and play,” she said. “We will take turns putting in pieces.”

Ollie loved puzzles. He grabbed a piece, then paused. “Grandpa, would you like to go first?” he asked.

Grandpa winked. “What a kind offer. Thank you. I will put in the corner.” Click went the piece.

Mama went next. Then it was Ollie’s turn. He placed a blue piece. Click. Everyone clapped softly. The puzzle showed a bright river with otters floating paw-in-paw.

“Look,” Mama said. “The otters are holding paws. That shows respect and care.”

Ollie smiled. “Respect means waiting, listening, and being kind,” he said. “It helps us share. It helps us learn.”

Grandpa closed the book with a gentle thump. “You learned a lot today, Ollie.”

Ollie nodded. “I like learning. I like taking turns. I like respect.” He hugged Mama and Grandpa. Their fur felt warm and soft.

The sun made the room glow. The table was tidy. The plates were clean. Ollie’s heart felt full.

“What did we learn?” Mama asked.

Ollie held up a paw. “We ask. We wait. We share. We listen. That is respect.”

Mama and Grandpa cheered. “Yes!”

Ollie giggled. “I can show respect every day,” he said. “At the table, at play, and at school.” He imagined sharing toys, waiting his turn on the slide, and listening to his teacher. He felt ready to try.

They stacked the chairs. They washed the plates with warm, soapy bubbles. Swish, swish. Ollie hummed a little tune. He looked at Mama and Grandpa and said, “Thank you for lunch. Thank you for teaching me.”

“You’re welcome, Ollie,” Mama said. “We learn together.”

Ollie’s whiskers twitched. “Respect helps everyone feel good,” he said.

“Yes,” Grandpa said. “Respect makes a happy home.”

Ollie smiled a big, bright smile. “I will remember.”

The End.
