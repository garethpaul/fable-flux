# Benny Beaver’s Warm Kitchen Helpers

Benny was a busy beaver with bright teeth and a big, happy tail. He lived near a little house with a warm kitchen. The kitchen smelled like cinnamon toast and sweet apples. The oven hummed softly. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor.

One chilly morning, Benny scurried inside to help his friend, Grandma Dot. She wore a blue apron and soft slippers. Pots clinked, water bubbled, and bread crackled as it cooled.

“Good morning, Grandma Dot!” said Benny. “I want to help! I am a busy beaver!”

Grandma Dot smiled. “Good morning, Benny. I love helpers. Today we can bake apple muffins for the neighborhood. That is volunteering. We give treats to others.”

Benny’s tail thumped-thump-thumped with excitement. “Yes! I want to help everyone!”

Grandma Dot patted his paw. “We will do it with a calm routine. We will improve by doing one step at a time.”

Benny nodded fast. “One step! I can do ten steps! Or twenty!”

His tail thumped even louder—thump, thump, THUMP—and spoons rattled in a cup.

Grandma Dot giggled. “Let’s practice self-regulation. That means we slow down our bodies and feelings. Try my warm-kitchen breath.”

“How?” Benny asked.

“Smell the cinnamon,” said Grandma Dot, breathing in. “Blow the muffins cool,” she said, breathing out.

Benny sniffed the sweet air—mmm!—and blew soft puffs, “Fooo.” His shoulders relaxed. His tail slowed to a gentle tap-tap.

“Good job,” said Grandma Dot. “Now our routine: wash paws, gather tools, measure, mix, and clean as we go.”

Benny washed his paws. The warm water felt like a tiny hug. He gathered a bowl, a spoon, and a muffin tin. The metal felt smooth and cool.

“Measuring time,” Grandma Dot said. “We will improve by checking our scoops.”

Benny scooped flour. Poof! A white cloud floated up and tickled his nose. “Achoo!”

“Bless you,” Grandma Dot said. “Let’s try again. Level the scoop.” She showed him how to scrape the top flat.

Benny tried. The flour was even. “I did it!” he cheered.

“Mixing time,” said Grandma Dot. Benny poured milk that went splish, cracked eggs that went crack, and sprinkled cinnamon that went shhh. He stirred in circles. The batter made a soft, sticky sound—slop, slop, swirl.

Benny felt excited again. “I want to stir fast!” He whirled the spoon. Batter splashed. A dot landed on his nose.

“Oh dear,” said Benny. “I made a mess.”

Grandma Dot winked. “That happens. We can improve. What helps you slow down?”

Benny touched his nose, licked the sweet batter, and smiled. “Warm-kitchen breath!” He smelled the cinnamon. He blew like cooling muffins. His body calmed. He stirred slowly. The batter made neat swirls.

“Lovely,” said Grandma Dot. “Now we share the batter evenly.”

Benny held the scoop. He wanted to dump it all in one cup. But he remembered: improve with small steps. He counted softly, “One for you, one for you,” as he filled each spot. Plop, plop, plop.

“Into the oven,” said Grandma Dot. The oven hummed, and the kitchen grew even warmer. The smell got bigger and sweeter, like a hug for the whole room.

While they waited, Benny’s tail twitched. Waiting was hard. He looked at sticky spots on the counter. “I can volunteer to clean,” he said. “I can help now.”

He got a cloth, warm and damp. Wipe, wipe. He washed the spoon. Swish, swish. He stacked bowls. Clink, clink. Cleaning felt good. His heart felt big.

Grandma Dot smiled. “You are improving, Benny. You are following a routine, using self-regulation, and volunteering to help.”

The timer dinged. “Muffins!” Benny said.

They opened the oven. A wave of sweet, apple-cinnamon air rolled out. The muffins puffed tall and golden. The tops were bumpy and shiny.

“Remember,” said Grandma Dot, “warm-kitchen breath.” They waited a moment. Benny breathed in the smell and blew gentle cooling air.

They placed muffins in a basket with a red cloth. Benny carried it carefully. Tap-tap went his tail, slow and happy.

They knocked on neighbors’ doors. “Fresh muffins!” Benny said. “We are sharing!”

Neighbors smiled. “Thank you! You are kind helpers.” Benny’s cheeks felt warm and proud.

Back in the kitchen, Benny sat at the table. He took one muffin. He waited for it to cool. He took small bites. The muffin was soft, sweet, and a little crunchy on top. He closed his eyes and sighed, “Mmm.”

Grandma Dot poured milk. “What did you learn today?”

Benny thought. He put a paw on his chest and breathed in and out. “I learned to improve by doing one step at a time. I can calm my body with warm-kitchen breath. I can follow a routine. And I can volunteer to help others.”

Grandma Dot nodded. “That is a big lesson for a busy beaver.”

Benny smiled. “I am still busy. But now I am busy and calm.”

They clinked their cups. The kitchen hummed, the sun made golden squares, and the warm smell of cinnamon floated like a happy song.

The End.
