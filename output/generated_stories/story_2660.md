# Roary’s Ribbon Dance

The sun was warm in the sunny backyard. The grass smelled sweet and tickly. Birds sang, “Tweet-tweet!” A brave little lion cub named Roary padded out, his paws soft on the ground. His golden fur sparkled in the sunshine.

Mama Lion set a basket on the picnic table. Inside were colorful ribbons—red, blue, green, and gold. They shone like tiny rainbows. “Today,” Mama Lion said with a smile, “I will teach you our family’s ribbon dance. It is our special tradition.”

Roary’s ears perked up. “A ribbon dance? That sounds fun!” He puffed out his chest. “I am brave. I can do it!”

Mama Lion tied a blue ribbon to Roary’s paw. She tied a gold ribbon to her own. “We will learn step by step,” she said gently. “We build a skill by practicing. We go slow. We breathe. We try again.”

Roary nodded. He wanted to learn. He wanted to be strong and brave and share his family’s tradition.

Mama Lion hummed a soft tune. “Sway, swish, circle.” She swayed her body. The gold ribbon made a whoosh sound in the air. It drew a shiny loop that sparkled in the sun.

Roary copied her. “Sway, swish, circle,” he said. He swayed. He swished. He tried to circle. But his blue ribbon tangled around his little paw.

“Oops!” he squeaked. The ribbon pulled tight. “I can’t do it!” His tail drooped. His face felt hot. He felt a bubble of frustration in his tummy.

Mama Lion knelt down and touched his paw. “Learning is a skill,” she whispered. “Skills take practice. It is okay to feel upset. Let’s take a brave breath together.”

Roary breathed in. The warm air smelled like sunny grass. He breathed out. The bubble in his tummy felt smaller.

“Good,” Mama said. “We try again. This time, slow.”

Roary stood tall. “I am brave.” He moved slowly. Sway. Swish. Circle. The ribbon moved like a tiny river. It made a soft shhh sound. Roary smiled.

Then the bird sang louder. “Tweet-tweet!” Roary looked up. His ribbon bonked his nose. “Ow!” He frowned. “This is hard.”

Mama Lion nodded. “Yes, it is hard. New skills can be hard. But your brain grows when you practice. Let’s break it into little steps.”

She showed him. “First, just sway.” They counted, “One, two, sway.” The ribbon waved like a blue flag. Roary giggled. “That feels nice.”

“Next, add swish.” Mama swished her ribbon gently. Roary swished too. The ribbon brushed the air like a paintbrush. Swish, swish.

“Last, a small circle.” Mama drew a tiny circle. Roary made one too. The circle glowed in the sun.

Roary clapped his paws. “I did it! Tiny steps!”

Mama smiled. “Yes. We build skills with tiny steps and big patience.”

Roary tried the whole dance. Sway, swish, circle. Sway, swish, circle. The ribbon flowed smoothly. But then his foot stepped on his ribbon, and he tumbled onto the soft grass. Plop!

Roary’s eyes filled with tears. “I want to stop,” he whispered.

Mama Lion gave him a gentle hug. Her fur was warm and soft. “It is okay to rest,” she said. “Let’s count to five and shake out the grumps.”

They sat together. They counted, “One, two, three, four, five.” They shook their paws. Shake-shake-shake. Roary felt the grumps wiggle away.

“I will try again,” Roary said in a small, brave voice.

Mama Lion’s eyes shone. “That is bravery. Brave means trying, even when it feels hard.”

Roary stood up. He looked at the ribbon. He took one brave breath. He remembered the steps. “Sway, swish, circle.” He moved slowly and listened to the soft whoosh. He felt the sun on his back. He smelled the sweet grass. He heard the birds cheer, “Tweet-tweet!”

Roary added a new part Mama had told him about. “Tap, tap,” he said, tapping his paws on the ground. His ribbon made a golden-blue ring in the air.

Mama Lion clapped. “That’s our tradition! My mama taught me. Her mama taught her. Now I teach you.”

Roary beamed. “And one day, I will teach my cubs!” He felt proud inside, like a warm, glowing light.

They danced together across the sunny backyard. Sway, swish, circle, tap. Sway, swish, circle, tap. Their ribbons painted the air. The colors made loops and rainbows. The breeze felt cool on their cheeks. The ground felt soft under their paws.

Roary stumbled one more time, but he stopped, breathed, and tried again. Each try felt easier. His body remembered. His paws knew where to go.

At last, Roary finished with a big circle and a happy roar. “Raaah!” The ribbon twirled like a star.

Mama Lion hugged him tight. “You did it. You built a new skill. You were patient. You were brave. You shared our tradition.”

Roary smiled up at the sky. “When things are hard, I can breathe, take tiny steps, and try again.”

“Yes,” Mama said. “That is how we grow.”

The End.
