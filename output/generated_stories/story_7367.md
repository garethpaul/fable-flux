# Ellie’s Tidy Trumpet

In a warm, comfortable nursery, a gentle elephant calf named Ellie woke up to soft sunshine peeking through the curtains. The room smelled like clean blankets and sweet soap. Her blue blanket felt cozy against her soft, gray skin. Ellie yawned, stretched her little trunk, and smiled.

“Good morning, Ellie,” hummed Nana Nuru, the kind caretaker. Her voice was like a lullaby. “Today we have a very simple plan. First, make your bed. Next, tidy your toys. Then, snack time! After that, story time.”

Ellie’s ears flapped with excitement. “Snack time! Story time!” she trumpeted in a tiny, happy squeak.

“Yes,” Nana Nuru chuckled. “But remember, we do first things first. We complete our tasks. We follow directions. That shows responsibility.”

Ellie nodded. She liked big words when they felt gentle and safe. “Re-spon-si-bi-li-ty,” she repeated, tapping her trunk.

Nana Nuru pointed to a colorful chart on the wall. It had simple pictures: a bed, a toy basket, a bowl of fruit, and a book. “We will go step by step,” she said, tapping each picture. “Bed, toys, snack, story.”

Ellie looked at her bed. The sheet was crinkled like a little ocean. Her blue blanket was half on the floor. “I can do that,” Ellie whispered. She stood on her soft, squeaky mat. The mat felt bouncy under her feet.

She pulled the sheet smooth with her trunk. Swish. She lifted the blanket. Whoosh. She patted the pillow. Pat, pat. She made neat corners the way Nana had shown her.

Nana Nuru watched with a warm smile. “You’re doing it, Ellie,” she said. “You are completing your first task.”

Ellie felt proud. Her heart made a soft thump-thump of joy. “Bed is done!” she chirped.

“Wonderful,” said Nana. “Now, what comes next?”

Ellie looked at the chart. “Toys,” she said. She looked around the nursery. Blocks were sprinkled like candy colors on the soft, fuzzy rug. A stuffed giraffe peeked from under a chair. Little cars rested beside a book, ready to zoom.

Ellie wanted to play with the cars. “Vroom, vroom,” she whispered, giving one a gentle push. The car rolled and clicked. It sounded fun.

Nana Nuru shook her head softly. “Remember our plan. First we tidy, then we play. We follow directions to finish our jobs.”

Ellie paused. Her trunk wiggled. She took a tiny breath. “Okay,” she said. “I can tidy.”

She picked up the blocks and dropped them into the basket. Clack, clack, clack. She hugged the stuffed giraffe and set it on the shelf. “There you go, Gigi,” she giggled. She lined up the little cars in a row, wheels touching wheels. “Ready, little friends,” she whispered.

One book was open on the floor. The pages were like smooth leaves. She closed it gently—flutter—and put it back on the low shelf.

“Listen,” said Nana Nuru. The room was quiet. Ellie heard only a soft hum from the window and the tiny tap of her feet. “A tidy room has a calm sound,” Nana said. “That is the sound of a job well done.”

Ellie smiled. Her tummy felt light and happy. “Toys are done,” she said.

Nana Nuru clapped softly. “You followed directions. You finished your tasks. You showed responsibility.”

“Snack time now?” Ellie asked, eyes bright.

“Yes,” said Nana. She brought a little bowl with soft banana slices and sweet apples. The fruit smelled fresh and juicy. Ellie sat on a cushion with polka dots. “Mmm,” she said, taking a bite. “Squishy banana. Crunchy apple.”

“After snack comes story time,” Nana reminded her. “You earned it.”

Ellie’s eyes sparkled. “I like stories,” she said between bites.

When the bowl was empty, Nana Nuru showed Ellie the last picture on the chart—a book. “We did each step in order,” Nana said. “This is how we complete tasks.”

Nana chose a book with a shiny elephant on the cover. She sat in the rocking chair. Ellie climbed into her lap, warm and safe. The chair creaked in a friendly way: creek, creek.

As Nana read, Ellie listened. The words were soft and sweet. The pictures were bright like crayons. Ellie could smell a light orange scent from Nana’s sweater, and she could feel the tickle of a page on her trunk.

Soon, Nana paused. “Ellie,” she asked, “How did we get to this nice story time?”

Ellie thought. “I made my bed. I tidied my toys. I followed directions. I finished my jobs. That’s re-spon-si-bi-li-ty,” she said proudly.

Nana kissed the top of Ellie’s head. “Yes. When we do first things first, we feel calm and happy. It keeps our space safe and cozy.”

Ellie looked around the nursery. The bed was smooth. The toys were resting. The room felt peaceful, like a soft hug.

“I like completing tasks,” Ellie said. “It makes my heart feel neat.”

Nana Nuru smiled. “That’s a lovely way to say it.”

Ellie snuggled close and gave a tiny trumpet of joy. “Toot,” she whispered.

They finished the story together, rocking and smiling in their comfortable nursery. Ellie’s eyes grew sleepy, warm and heavy like a blanket. She felt proud, peaceful, and very loved.

From that day, Ellie checked the picture chart each morning. She made her bed. She tidied her toys. She followed directions with her gentle trunk and her brave, big heart. And every time, she enjoyed snack and story time, knowing she had completed her tasks.

The lesson stayed in her mind like a song: First we work, then we rest. We follow directions. We show responsibility. And our world feels safe and happy.

The End.
