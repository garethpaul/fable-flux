# Gigi's Big Problem

Gigi the giraffe loved playing in the sunny backyard. The warm sunshine felt good on her spotted coat, and the green grass smelled fresh and sweet. Today was perfect for playing with her friends!

"Good morning, everyone!" called Gigi as she walked over to the sandbox where Benny the bunny and Rosie the raccoon were playing.

But when Gigi got closer, she saw something was wrong. Benny was sitting in one corner of the sandbox, looking sad. Rosie was in the other corner, her arms crossed and her face grumpy.

"What's happening?" asked Gigi gently, bending her long neck down to see her friends better.

"Rosie took my favorite red shovel!" said Benny, his voice wobbly. "I was using it first!"

"No, I wasn't!" said Rosie, stomping her little paw. "I found it in the sand, so it's mine now!"

Gigi's heart felt heavy seeing her friends upset. She took a deep breath and thought carefully. *When I feel worried, I need to think before I act,* she reminded herself.

"Hmm," said Gigi thoughtfully. "This is a tricky problem. Let me think about this." She sat down slowly, her long legs folding under her. "Can you both tell me what happened? Benny, you go first."

Benny sniffled. "I was digging a castle, and I put my shovel down to get some water. When I came back, Rosie had it!"

"I see," said Gigi. "Rosie, what do you remember?"

Rosie's voice was still angry. "I saw a red shovel in the sand with no one using it, so I picked it up! Finders keepers!"

Gigi nodded slowly. The warm sun helped her think clearly. "I understand now. Benny, when you left your shovel, Rosie didn't know it belonged to you. And Rosie, you found a shovel and thought it was free to use."

Both friends looked at Gigi with big eyes.

"So nobody was trying to be mean," continued Gigi. "This was just a misunderstanding. But now we need to find a solution that makes everyone happy."

"But I want the red shovel!" said Benny.

"Me too!" said Rosie.

Gigi smiled kindly. "Let's think of some ideas together. What could we do?"

Benny thought hard. "Maybe... we could take turns?"

"That's a great idea!" said Gigi. "Rosie, what do you think about sharing?"

Rosie looked at the shovel, then at Benny's sad face. Her heart felt softer. "I guess... taking turns could be fun. But I want to go first since I have it now."

"That sounds fair," said Benny, brightening up. "How long should each turn be?"

"How about we count to twenty for each turn?" suggested Gigi. "That way it's fair for everyone."

"I can count to twenty!" said Rosie proudly.

"Me too!" said Benny.

Soon both friends were giggling and taking turns with the red shovel. They built the most amazing sand castle together, with towers and bridges and a moat around it.

"Thank you, Gigi," said Benny. "You helped us solve our problem!"

"Yes!" agreed Rosie. "Sharing is actually more fun than I thought!"

Gigi felt warm and happy inside. "When we have problems with our friends, we can always talk about it and find a solution together. Thinking before we act and listening to each other helps everyone feel better."

As the friends played together in the sunny backyard, Gigi smiled. Sometimes the best solutions come when everyone works together with kind hearts and thinking minds.

The End.
