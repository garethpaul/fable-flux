# The Thank-You List

In a cozy study corner, a curious fox kit named Finley liked to explore. The study corner was warm and bright. Sunlight made golden puddles on the rug. The air smelled like paper, pine pencils, and a little bit like warm tea. Finley’s fluffy tail swished as he looked around. Books sat in neat rows. A clock tick-tick-ticked softly. A big jar of crayons sparkled with colors.

“I want to make a Super Star Poster!” Finley said. His whiskers trembled with excitement.

Mama Fox peeped in. “That sounds fun,” she said. “Let’s follow directions so it works well.”

Finley nodded fast. “Yes! I can do that.” He loved to figure things out.

On the study table, Mama laid out a simple set of steps on a small card:
1. Read the steps.
2. Gather tools: paper, glue, crayons, stickers.
3. Draw, cut, and glue.
4. Clean up.
5. Make a Thank-You List.

Finley read the steps aloud in a careful, tiny voice. “Read. Gather. Make. Clean. Thank.” He tapped each word with his paw. “Easy!”

He picked up a shiny pair of safe scissors. Snip-snip! The sound was crisp, like little bird beaks. He reached for the glue. Squish. The glue was cool and sticky. He giggled. “Glue feels silly!”

“Step two?” Mama asked kindly.

“Gather!” Finley said. He took blue paper, soft like the sky. He chose green paper that smelled leafy. He picked bright crayons and star stickers that winked like tiny moons. He lined them up in a proud row.

“Step three?” Mama asked.

“Make!” said Finley. He drew a big star and a tiny star. He cut slowly, snip-snip, following the lines. He stuck the stars on the blue paper with a careful pat-pat. He made a comet with a golden tail. He hummed with joy. “Twinkle, twinkle,” he sang.

But then Finley saw a shiny jar on the shelf. It held glitter. The glitter looked like sugar snow. He wanted to use it so much. He stretched his paw.

Mama’s voice was gentle. “What comes next in the directions?”

Finley paused. He looked at the card. He squinted. “Clean up,” he read. He lowered his paw. “Oh. Not glitter now. Glitter comes later if we add it to the plan.”

“That is good thinking,” Mama said. “You checked the steps.”

Finley put the glitter back. He cleaned tiny paper bits into the bin. Sweep, sweep. He wiped the sticky glue spots. Swish, swish. The table felt smooth again.

“Step five?” Mama asked softly.

Finley read the last step. “Make a Thank-You List.” He tilted his head. “What is that?”

“It is a list of who helped you,” Mama said. “We say thank you. Gratitude makes our hearts warm.”

Finley put his paw on his chest. His heart felt soft and bright. He thought hard. He liked to think. He looked around the study corner. He sniffed the tea smell. He listened to the clock’s tick-tick. He watched the sun lines move on the floor.

“Who helped me?” he asked himself. He wiggled his nose. “Mama helped me read the steps.” He wrote, in big careful letters: Thank you, Mama.

He thought again. “The steps helped me remember what to do.” He wrote: Thank you, Steps Card.

He looked at his tools. “The crayons gave me color.” He wrote: Thank you, Crayons. He touched them. They felt smooth and warm from his paws.

He patted the paper. “The paper held my stars.” He wrote: Thank you, Paper.

He sniffed the glue. “The glue stuck things on.” He wrote: Thank you, Glue.

He looked up at the clock. “The clock told me time. I went slow and careful.” He wrote: Thank you, Clock.

He smiled at the sunny window. “The sun gave me light.” He wrote: Thank you, Sun.

He looked at himself in the shiny teapot on the shelf. He saw a little fox face with bright eyes. He whispered, “I used my brain. I stopped and checked. I followed directions.” He wrote: Thank you, Finley’s Thinking.

Mama’s eyes shone. “Finley, that is wonderful critical thinking. You noticed many helpers. How do you feel?”

Finley took a deep breath. The air smelled like pine pencils and a tiny bit sweet. “I feel warm,” he said. “I feel happy. Thank you, Mama.”

Mama hugged him. Her fur was soft and smelled like the forest after rain. “You are welcome,” she said.

Finley picked up his Super Star Poster. He showed it to the window. Light danced on the gold comet tail. He taped his Thank-You List next to it.

“Now I will follow the last, last step,” Finley said.

Mama tilted her head. “What is that?”

Finley grinned. “Say thank you out loud!” He turned to the table. “Thank you, crayons!” He waved at the clock. “Thank you, tick-tock!” He smiled at the sun. “Thank you, sun!” He patted his chest. “Thank you, Finley’s Thinking!” His tail swished with joy.

Mama laughed. “I like your extra step.”

Finley sat in the cozy study corner. He looked at his poster. He looked at his list. He felt calm. He had solved a problem: he had wanted glitter now, but he followed directions and waited. He used his brain to think and choose. He felt grateful for help all around him.

“That is my lesson,” Finley said softly. “When I stop, think, and follow directions, I can do my best. And when I say thank you, my heart shines.”

He put his paws together and whispered, “Thank you.”

The End.
