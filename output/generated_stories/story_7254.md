# Leo Listens on the Porch

On a peaceful front porch, a brave little lion cub named Leo stretched his tiny paws. The porch was warm from the morning sun. A soft breeze smelled like sweet flowers. Birds chirped in the trees. The wooden steps felt smooth under Leo’s furry belly as he lay down to watch the day.

Grandma Lion rocked in a creaky chair. Creak… creak… “Good morning, brave cub,” she purred, smiling. “Today our porch is a listening place.”

“A listening place?” Leo blinked his bright eyes. “But I like to roar!”

Grandma chuckled. “Roars are strong. Listening is strong too. We listen with ears, eyes, heart, and kind words.”

Leo’s whiskers twitched. He liked being brave. Maybe listening could be brave.

Just then, his friend Tiko the monkey swung to the porch rail. “Hi, Leo!” Tiko chattered. “Want to play leaf toss?” Tiko tossed a big green leaf. It fluttered and landed on Leo’s head.

Leo giggled. “Yes! Let’s play!” He shook his mane and the leaf floated down like a butterfly.

They made a leaf game. Rustle, rustle went the leaves. Swish went Tiko’s tail. Tap-tap went Leo’s paws on the wooden floor. The porch smelled leafy and fresh.

Soon, Miri the little bird landed on the railing. Her feathers glittered blue in the sun. “Cheep! I want to play too,” she sang.

“What did you say?” Tiko asked, head tilted.

“Cheep-cheep!” Miri tried again, hopping closer.

Leo frowned. “I don’t understand. Let’s just keep tossing leaves,” he said, tossing another leaf high.

The leaf brushed Miri’s wing. She fluffed her feathers and chirped fast. Her tiny eyes looked worried.

Tiko rolled his eyes. “She’s making so much noise,” he grumbled.

Miri chirped even faster. Cheep-cheep-cheep!

Leo’s ears drooped. “Too loud,” he huffed. He puffed his chest to roar—but then he saw Grandma’s gentle eyes. Grandma put a paw to her own chest and took a slow breath. In… and out.

Leo put his paw on his chest too. In… and out. The warm air filled his nose. His heart slowed. The porch felt quiet again, even with the birds singing. Leo felt brave in a new way.

“Let’s try listening,” Leo said softly. “We can use our ears, eyes, and hearts.”

Leo crouched down to Miri’s level. “Miri, can you show us with your body?” he asked kindly. “Slowly?”

Miri took a deep breath too. She flapped one wing, pointed her beak at the leaves, and then at the porch floor. “Cheep… cheep?” She tapped the rail with her tiny feet and tilted her head toward the yard.

Tiko scratched his head. “She wants… the leaves to go… where?”

Leo watched carefully. Eyes. Ears. Heart. He noticed a small nest tucked under the porch roof. He saw a loose straw on the floor. He heard Miri’s cheep turn soft when she looked at the nest.

“Oh!” Leo’s eyes lit up. “You’re building! You want soft leaves for your nest!”

Miri chirped happily and bounced up and down. Cheep! Cheep!

Tiko’s cheeks turned pink under his fur. “Oh. I thought you were just noisy,” he said, his voice small.

Leo nodded. “We didn’t understand. We can ask with words.” He turned to Miri. “Miri, do you want us to bring soft leaves for your nest?” He spoke slowly and pointed to the leaves, then to the nest.

Miri bobbed her head fast. Yes!

“I’m sorry I didn’t listen,” Tiko said. “Can you forgive me?”

Miri sang a sweet, bright note and nuzzled Tiko’s hand with her beak. Forgiven.

Leo smiled. “Let’s help!” He brushed his whiskers against a pile of soft, fuzzy leaves along the porch steps. They smelled like sunshine and grass. “These feel cozy,” he said.

Together they worked. Leo gathered soft leaves with careful paws. Tiko used his nimble fingers to carry tiny twigs. Miri showed where to place each bit. Tap… tuck… pat. The nest grew round and snug.

Grandma Lion rocked and hummed. “Words can be gentle,” she said. “And listening can be kind.”

When the nest was done, Miri fluttered in. She wriggled and settled, then sang a clear, happy song. The porch seemed to glow with the sound.

Tiko grinned. “We did it! Teamwork!”

Leo stood tall. His chest felt warm and brave. “We listened with our ears. We watched with our eyes. We spoke with kind words. We learned Miri’s way.”

Grandma nodded. “That is strong communication, brave cub. You slowed down, asked, and cared.”

Leo looked at his friends. “If we don’t understand, we can stop, breathe, and ask. We can forgive mistakes too.”

Miri chirped, “Cheep!” and rubbed her head on Leo’s paw.

Leo purred. “Next time we play, we will listen first.”

The breeze whispered over the porch. The chair went creak… creak… The friends sat together, happy and calm, watching sunlight dance on the wooden floor.

Leo whispered, “The porch is a listening place.”

“Yes,” Grandma said. “And your heart is a listening place too.”

The End.
