# Ollie Otter and the Backyard Bubble Plan

Ollie was a playful otter with bright eyes and silky whiskers. He lived with his family in a sunny backyard with soft green grass, warm golden light, and a little blue kiddie pool that glimmered like a jewel. Birds chirped, bees hummed, and daisies tickled Ollie’s paws when he ran.

One morning, Ollie wriggled out of his cozy towel bed. He took a deep sniff. The air smelled like sunshine and strawberries. “Today is Splash Day!” he squeaked, clapping his paws. He grabbed his favorite red ball and wiggled to the door.

Mama Otter smiled and held up a soft washcloth, a small toothbrush, and a bottle of bubbly soap. “Good morning, Ollie. Before Splash Day, we get clean and get ready. That is our plan.”

Ollie bounced. “But I want to splash now!” His whiskers twitched. He could hear water plip-plop in the pool. He could feel the warm sun on his fur. His tummy fluttered with excited wiggles.

Mama knelt and touched Ollie’s nose. “I hear you. Splashing is fun. But clean paws, clean teeth, and a sunscreen swipe keep your body happy. Hygiene first, then play. That is the safe and healthy way.”

Ollie frowned a tiny frown. “Do we have to do all the things now?”

Mama nodded kindly. “Let’s make a plan together. We can take turns choosing steps. We can make it speedy and silly.”

Ollie tilted his head. “We can choose? We can make it silly?” He perked up. “Okay! I choose the silly song step!”

Mama giggled. “Deal. I choose washing paws first.”

They shook paws—negotiation complete. Ollie liked making a deal. It felt fair and friendly.

They walked to the little sink by the back door. The water felt warm and tickly on Ollie’s paws. “Swish, swish,” he said as bubbles grew. The soap smelled like lemon and tiny flowers. “The bubbles are like little clouds!” Ollie laughed.

“Let’s sing the bubble song,” said Mama. “Bubble, bubble, wash-wash-wash. Rinse, rinse, splash-splash-splash!”

Ollie sang with her. He rubbed between his fingers and around his claws. “My paws feel shiny,” he said, holding them up in the sun.

“Your turn to choose,” Mama said.

Ollie tapped his chin. “Tooth time! But can we brush while counting birds?”

“Good idea,” Mama said. They went to the step stool and mirror. Ollie squeezed a tiny dot of toothpaste. It smelled minty and sweet. “Brush the little teeth. Brush the big teeth,” Mama said gently.

“One chirp, two chirps,” Ollie counted between swishes. He watched a blue bird hop along the fence. “Three chirps, four chirps.” The brush made soft scritch-scratch sounds. Spit, rinse, smile—sparkly!

Mama smiled back. “Your turn to choose again.”

Ollie pointed to a small basket. “Preparation time! Let’s pack the play bag.” They put in a clean towel, a water bottle, a snack in a little box, and a sun hat with tiny fish on it. Ollie placed his red ball on top. “Ready, set, prepared!”

“Excellent plan,” said Mama. “My turn. Sunscreen.” She squeezed a small line into her palm. “It’s cool and a little sticky,” Ollie giggled as Mama dotted his nose. The lotion smelled like coconut. She rubbed it on his ears, cheeks, and arms. “We protect our skin every sunny day.”

Ollie peeked at the pool. The water sparkled and made tiny waves. “Can we splash now?” he asked, bouncing again.

“One more check,” Mama said. “Paws clean, teeth brushed, bag ready, sunscreen on. What if the ground gets muddy? How will we keep our play clean?”

Ollie looked at the grass. He thought for a moment. “We can place a clean towel by the pool for sitting. We can rinse our paws if they get muddy. And we can keep the snack box closed until snack time.”

Mama nodded. “You are thinking ahead. That is smart preparation.”

At last, they went to the sunny backyard. The warm air hugged Ollie like a blanket. He dipped a paw in the cool pool. “Ahh!” He tossed the red ball and splashed after it, sending glittery drops into the air. The water felt smooth and fresh against his fur.

After a while, Ollie noticed his paws had a little dirt from the flower bed. He remembered the plan. “Pause for a rinse!” he chirped. He dipped his paws in the clean water bucket they had set nearby and rubbed them. “Swish, swish, clean again!”

Mama clapped. “That is good hygiene, Ollie. You are taking care of your body and your play.”

They rested on the towel. Ollie took a sip of water. “My mouth feels fresh,” he said. He sniffed his paws. “My paws smell like lemon flowers.” He wiggled happily. “I like our plan. I like making deals. I like getting ready.”

Mama hugged him. “When we prepare and keep clean, we feel good, we stay healthy, and we have more fun.”

Ollie nodded. “Hygiene first, then play. Negotiate the steps. Prepare the things. That is our bubble plan!”

The sun drifted lower, and the birds sang a soft evening song. Ollie helped put away the towel and toys. He felt proud, calm, and cozy.

That night, wrapped in his soft towel bed, he whispered, “Clean paws, bright teeth, happy heart.” He smiled, and his whiskers curled like tiny smiles too.

The End.
