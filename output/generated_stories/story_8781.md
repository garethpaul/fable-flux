# Gigi Giraffe Learns to Save Water

Gigi the giraffe loved her morning routine. Every day, she would stretch her long, spotted neck up high and brush her teeth in the bright, sunny bathroom. The white tiles sparkled, and the mirror was so shiny she could see her beautiful brown eyes looking back at her.

But this morning was different. When Gigi turned on the faucet, only tiny drops came out. *Drip, drop, drip, drop.*

"Oh no!" Gigi said, her voice getting louder. "Where's all the water?" She felt her face getting hot and red. She twisted the handle harder. "This isn't fair! I need water to brush my teeth!"

Gigi's friend Benny the bunny hopped into the bathroom. His soft gray ears wiggled when he heard Gigi's upset voice.

"What's wrong, Gigi?" Benny asked gently.

"The water won't come out!" Gigi stomped her hooves. "I'm so angry! How can I brush my teeth? How can I wash my face?"

Benny's pink nose twitched thoughtfully. "Let me tell you something, Gigi. Yesterday, I saw you brushing your teeth with the water running the whole time. The water was flowing down the drain while you brushed."

Gigi's long neck drooped down. "Yes, I always do that. So what?"

"Well," Benny said softly, "when we use too much water, there might not be enough for everyone. The birds need water to drink. The flowers need water to grow. And all the animals in our neighborhood need water too."

Gigi felt her anger melting away like ice cream in the sun. She took three deep breaths, just like her mama had taught her. "Oh, Benny. I never thought about that."

Just then, Papa Giraffe's voice called from downstairs. "The water company is fixing the pipes! Water will be back soon!"

Benny smiled. "This is a perfect chance to learn, Gigi! When the water comes back, I can show you how to save it."

Soon, they heard the pipes gurgling and bubbling. Clear, cool water began flowing from the faucet again.

"Watch this!" Benny said excitedly. He turned on the water and filled a small cup. Then he turned the water off. "Now I can brush my teeth with just this cup of water. See?"

Gigi watched as Benny wet his toothbrush, turned off the faucet, brushed his teeth, then used the rest of the water to rinse. "Wow! You used so little water, but your teeth are still clean and shiny!"

"Your turn!" Benny encouraged.

Gigi carefully filled her own cup with water. She turned off the faucet with a satisfying click. She brushed her big giraffe teeth thoroughly, then rinsed with the water from her cup.

"I did it!" Gigi beamed. "And I feel so good knowing I'm helping save water for all our friends!"

From that day on, Gigi always turned off the water while brushing. She taught her little sister the same trick. When she felt angry about other things, she remembered to take deep breaths and think about solutions, just like Benny had helped her do.

"Thank you for being such a good friend," Gigi told Benny. "You helped me learn something important and feel better too."

"That's what friends do," Benny said with a happy hop.

Now Gigi's bathroom routine was even better than before – because she knew she was taking care of the Earth while taking care of herself.

The End.
