# Koko's Car Ride Song

Koko the koala sat quietly in the back seat of the family car. Her soft gray fur felt cozy against the warm seat. Outside the window, trees whooshed by like green clouds.

"Are we there yet?" asked Koko's little brother Joey from his car seat.

"Not yet, sweetie," said Mama from the front. "We still have a long way to go."

Joey started to wiggle and fuss. "I'm bored! I want to get out!"

Koko watched her brother get more upset. His face turned red, and he began to cry. The happy car ride was becoming sad and noisy.

Papa turned down the radio. "Joey, please try to be patient."

But Joey cried louder. "Waaah! I don't like the car!"

Koko felt sorry for her little brother. She also felt sorry for Mama and Papa, who looked worried in the front seats. The car felt tense and unhappy.

Then Koko had an idea. She remembered the lullaby Mama sang at bedtime. Maybe music could help!

Koko took a deep breath and started to sing softly: "Twinkle, twinkle, little star, how I wonder what you are..."

Joey stopped crying and looked at his big sister. His eyes grew wide with surprise.

Koko kept singing, making her voice gentle and sweet. "Up above the world so high, like a diamond in the sky..."

"That's beautiful, Koko," whispered Mama, smiling in the rearview mirror.

Joey clapped his little hands. "More! More song!"

Koko felt proud that her idea was working. She started another song: "If you're happy and you know it, clap your hands!"

Soon Joey was clapping along. Then Papa started clapping too. Even Mama clapped when it was safe!

"The wheels on the bus go round and round," sang Koko next.

Joey giggled and made the motions with his hands. The car filled with happy sounds instead of crying.

But then Koko realized something. She had been singing for a while, and her throat felt scratchy. When she tried to sing the next song, her voice cracked.

"Oh no," said Koko quietly. "I think I sang too much. My voice is tired."

Joey's smile faded. "No more songs?"

Koko felt bad. She wanted to keep making her family happy, but her voice needed a rest.

"I'm sorry, Joey," said Koko. "I sang so much that my voice is sleepy now."

Papa looked back with kind eyes. "Koko, that was very thoughtful of you to help Joey feel better. Thank you for taking care of your brother."

"You showed wonderful initiative," said Mama. "That means you saw a problem and tried to fix it all by yourself."

Koko felt warm and proud inside.

Then Joey surprised everyone. "I sing now!" he said. In his tiny voice, he started: "Twinkle, twinkle..."

Koko smiled big. "That's perfect, Joey! You can be the singer while my voice rests."

The family took turns singing different songs. When someone's voice got tired, another person would start a new song. They sang about animals, colors, and happy things.

By the time they reached their destination, everyone was smiling and laughing.

"What a wonderful car ride," said Papa as he parked.

"Thanks to Koko's great idea," added Mama, giving her a hug.

Koko felt proud that she had helped her family. She learned that when she saw a problem, she could try to solve it. And when she made a mistake, saying "sorry" made everything better.

The End.
