# Leo’s Safe Song

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, he padded into the musical practice room. The room smelled like polished wood. It felt warm and cozy. Shiny drums stood in a row. A big piano smiled with white keys. Colorful shakers sat in a basket, and a tall music stand waited like a kind friend.

Leo took a deep breath. “Today, I want to learn a whole song,” he said. “My goal is to play safely and finish the ‘Sunny Steps’ song.”

“Goals are good,” said Miss Melody, the gentle music teacher. She wore a blue scarf with tiny music notes. “Safety first, Leo. Then knowledge. Then we reach our goal.”

Leo’s ears perked up. “How do I stay safe?”

Miss Melody pointed to the safety chart on the wall. It had bright pictures and simple words:
- Look with your eyes first.
- Ask before you touch.
- Use quiet paws.
- Keep space for everyone.

Leo nodded. “I can do that!”

He looked with his eyes. The drumsticks were smooth and round. The piano keys were shiny like tiny teeth. The shakers were bright like candy, but not for eating. His whiskers twitched with excitement.

“May I touch the drumsticks?” Leo asked.

“Yes,” said Miss Melody, smiling. “Use quiet paws. Hold them gently.”

Leo wrapped his soft paws around the drumsticks. Tap tap. “That is a soft sound,” he whispered. Tap TAP! “That is a big sound.”

Miss Melody clapped softly. “Good listening, Leo. Our ears tell us what is safe. Big sounds can be scary. Start soft. Find the safe sound.”

Leo nodded. He set the sticks down. “I will start soft.”

Next, Leo looked at the piano. He felt the cool keys. “They are smooth,” he said.

“May I play a key?” he asked.

“You may,” said Miss Melody. “First, sit on the bench with your feet on the floor. No climbing, no standing. We sit to stay safe.”

Leo climbed up carefully, one paw at a time. His tail swished happily. Plink. The piano sang a gentle note. Plink, plink. The notes were like raindrops.

Leo’s tummy felt warm and proud. “I like this.”

Miss Melody showed him a little card with colored dots. “These dots are the notes for ‘Sunny Steps.’ We will learn one step at a time. Knowledge grows slowly, like a tree.”

“Step one,” Leo said. He pressed the red dot key. Plink. “Step two.” He pressed the blue dot key. Plonk. “Oops,” he said with a tiny giggle.

“It’s okay,” said Miss Melody. “We try again.”

Leo smiled. He pressed red, then blue, then yellow. Plink, plonk, plunk. The sounds felt like sunshine and lemon tea. He listened to how loud his paws were. He kept them gentle and safe.

A shiny triangle hung on the wall. It made Leo’s paws itch to touch. He looked with his eyes. He asked, “May I ring the triangle?”

Miss Melody nodded. “Yes. But first, safety.” She showed him the little metal beater. “Hold the triangle by the string. Keep paws away from the sharp corners. Gentle taps make clear sounds.”

Leo held the string. He took a slow breath. Ting! The sound was bright, like a star. “Wow,” he whispered, eyes wide.

“You followed all the steps,” said Miss Melody. “You looked. You asked. You used quiet paws. You learned. Now you can reach your goal.”

Leo’s whiskers wiggled. “My goal is to play the whole ‘Sunny Steps’ song.”

“Let’s make a plan,” Miss Melody said. She drew three stars.
- Star 1: Practice soft sounds.
- Star 2: Learn the notes.
- Star 3: Play the song safely from start to finish.

Leo pointed to Star 1. “I did that!” He played soft drum taps: tip tap tip. He pointed to Star 2. “I’m learning!” He played red, blue, yellow on the piano: plink, plonk, plunk. He pointed to Star 3 and took another deep breath.

Miss Melody counted, “One, two, ready, go.”

Leo began. Tap tap on the drum. Plink on the piano. Ting on the triangle. He watched his paws. He kept space around the instruments. He sat on the bench. He listened to the sounds. When a note sounded too loud, he made the next one softer. His tail swished in time: swish-swish.

The song ended with a sweet ting. The room was quiet for a moment. Then Miss Melody smiled big. “Leo, you did it! You met your goal.”

Leo felt proud and calm. His heart beat steady, not fast. “I did it safely,” he said. “I looked, I asked, I used quiet paws, and I learned the steps.”

Miss Melody hung a small golden star sticker on his chart. “Safety helps us learn. Knowledge helps us reach goals. You were brave and careful.”

Leo nodded. He put the drumsticks back, closed the piano lid gently, and hung the triangle straight. “Putting things away is safe, too,” he said.

As they left the musical practice room, the air felt happy, like a soft song. Leo whispered, “Safety first, then I can learn, then I can do big things.”

Miss Melody smiled. “That is the lesson, little lion.”

The End.
