# Bubbles in the Garden Shed

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with bright, curious eyes and a soft, squeaky giggle. One day, a big storm blew across the beach. The waves went whoosh and the wind went whoo-oo! When the storm stopped, Bubbles swam up the stream that tickled his fins and followed it all the way to a small, cozy garden.

In the corner stood a wooden garden shed. It smelled like fresh soil and warm wood. The door creaked, creak, creak, as Bubbles nudged it with his nose. Inside, the air felt cool and dusty. Shelves held flower pots, shiny garden tools, and a bright yellow watering can. Sunlight peeked through a little window and made dust sparkle like tiny stars.

“Hello?” Bubbles called, splashing his tail on a puddle by the door. Splash-splash.

“Who is there?” squeaked a little voice.

Out from a nest of soft rags came Pip, a small mouse with whiskers that twitch-twitch-twitched. Pip hugged a tiny seed packet. “This is my home,” Pip said. “It is quiet and safe.”

Bubbles smiled. “I am Bubbles! I got lost in the storm. May I rest in your shed for a little while? My fins feel tired, and my heart feels a bit wobbly.”

Pip looked at the puddle by the door and the muddy fin prints. Pip’s nose wrinkled. “I like my floor dry,” Pip said softly. “I feel worried when it is wet.”

Bubbles paused. His playful tail dropped. He looked at Pip’s small paws and the careful little nest. He listened to the tiny squeaks of Pip’s breath. He felt Pip’s worry like a small pebble in his own tummy.

“I hear that you feel worried,” Bubbles said gently. “You like your floor dry. I want to help you feel safe.”

Pip blinked. “You do?”

“Yes,” said Bubbles. “Maybe we can make a plan together.” He took a deep breath and made a soft dolphin whistle, like a lullaby. “What do you need?”

Pip thought. “I need dry floors and a quiet nap. And I need my seeds safe. These are for the garden class. I am teaching the sprouts how to grow.”

Bubbles’ eyes lit up. “You are a teacher? I like to learn! I can be careful. I can stay on this rubber mat,” he said, pointing his fin at a black mat near the door. “No more puddles inside. I can also blow soft bubbles, not big splashes.”

Pip’s whiskers lifted. “That sounds kind.”

Pip looked at his seed packets. He felt Bubbles’ kindness like warm sunshine on his back. “I can help you too,” Pip said. “You can rest here. I can make you a water bowl from the yellow watering can. Then you do not have to make puddles.”

Bubbles smiled wide. “We can help each other.”

They worked together. Pip tugged the watering can—scrape, scrape—to the door. Bubbles dipped his nose in, sip-sip, and made tiny quiet bubbles—blip, blip. Pip swept the floor with a soft broom—swish, swish—while Bubbles held the door open with his fin to let the fresh air in. A sweet smell of wet grass drifted inside.

Soon the shed felt cozy again. The floor was dry. The tools were neat. The sunlight made a warm square on the mat. Bubbles lay there and sighed, “Ahhh.”

Pip smiled. “Now it is time for my garden class,” he said. “Would you like to learn? I teach little sprouts with songs.”

“Yes, please,” said Bubbles, his eyes bright.

Pip held up a seed. “This is a seed. It is small, but it needs love,” he said. “It needs water, light, and gentle care. We do not shout at seeds. We listen to them.”

Bubbles nodded. “Like we listened to each other,” he said softly.

Pip sang a tiny song: “Sip of water, touch of sun, little seed, your day’s begun.” Bubbles hummed along, humming like a boat in calm water—mmm, mmm.

They planted the seeds in small pots. Pat-pat went their paws and fins. Sprinkle-sprinkle went the water. The soil smelled rich and earthy. Bubbles felt calm. Pip felt proud.

Just then, the wind pushed the shed door and it bumped—thump! A pot wobbled. Pip’s eyes grew wide. “Oh!”

Bubbles quickly steadied the pot with his soft fin. “I’ve got it,” he said. “Are you okay?”

Pip nodded, breathing out. “Thank you for helping. You saw I was scared, and you helped right away.”

Bubbles smiled. “I watched your face and listened. That is empathy. It means we feel with each other.”

Pip’s whiskers danced. “And when we talked and made a plan, that was negotiation. We both shared and we both cared. That is how we solve problems.”

They finished planting and sat quietly. The shed felt peaceful. A bird chirped outside. Chirp-chirp. The seeds rested in their pots like tiny dreams.

Bubbles said, “Today I learned to listen, to ask, and to make a plan. I learned that helping you helps me too.”

Pip nodded. “Today I learned that friends can be different and still be kind. When we use empathy, we find gentle answers.”

The sun began to set, painting the window pink and gold. Bubbles wiggled his tail. “When the stream fills again, I will swim home. Thank you for sharing your shed.”

“Thank you for caring about my dry floor,” Pip said. “Come visit for another garden lesson.”

Bubbles giggled. “I will. And I will bring my quiet bubbles.”

They touched paw to fin. Warm, soft, and happy.

The End.
