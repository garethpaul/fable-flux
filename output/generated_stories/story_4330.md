# Gigi Giraffe Builds the Quiet Clock

In a quiet attic full of soft dust and sunshine lines, a thoughtful giraffe named Gigi climbed the last wooden step. Her long neck peeked through beams. Her nose twitched at the smell of old books and cedar. Little motes of dust floated like tiny stars.

“Good morning, attic,” Gigi whispered. She liked to whisper here. The attic liked whispers.

Gigi had a plan. “Today, I will build something,” she said in her gentle voice. “I will build a Quiet Clock. It will help me know when to read, when to clean, and when to rest.” Her big eyes blinked. She felt calm and proud.

On a low table sat her supplies. There were round cardboard circles, yarn, wooden clothespins, a small bell that chimed softly, and a pot of sticky glue. There was also a notebook with simple drawings. Gigi tapped the notebook with her hoof. “I made a list,” she said. “Lists help me use my time.”

She looked out the tiny window at the bright sky. “First, I build,” she told herself. “Then, I tidy. Then, I read. Then, I rest.” She nodded. “One step at a time.”

Gigi set a sand timer. Whoosh—soft sand began to fall. “I will build until the sand is gone,” she planned.

She picked up the biggest cardboard circle. “Hello, circle,” she giggled. “You will be my clock face.” She painted it a warm yellow, like morning tea. The brush went swish, swish. The paint smelled a little like lemons. Gigi smiled. “That smells sunny.”

She glued numbers she had cut from scraps. “One, two, three,” she sang softly, placing each number in a friendly curve. “Four, five, six.” Her neck bent low. Her ears listened to the quiet. “Seven, eight, nine, ten, eleven, twelve.” The numbers looked neat. Gigi’s heart felt cozy.

She checked the sand timer. “Still falling,” she said. “Good job, me. Slow and steady.”

Next, she made the clock hands from two clothespins and yarn. Clip, clip. Pull, pull. The yarn felt soft on her hoof. “These will point to my plan,” she said.

Suddenly, a little moth fluttered by. Its wings were like paper kisses. It bumped the small bell—ding! Gigi blinked. “Oh, hello, Moth,” she whispered. “That bell is too loud for the quiet attic. I will solve this peacefully.”

Gigi looked around. She found a piece of felt. She wrapped it gently around the bell and tied it with yarn. “There,” she said. She touched the bell. It made a tiny, kind chime—ding-a-soft. The moth settled on a beam. The attic felt peaceful again.

The sand finished. “Time to tidy,” Gigi said. She turned the yarn hand to the tidy time. She put the brush in water. Swish, swish, swish. She wiped the table with a soft cloth. “Clean space helps my brain,” she hummed.

She set the sand again. “Reading time,” she planned. Gigi chose a thin book called How Things Fit. She read slowly. “Gears go round,” she read. “Hands point to numbers.” She looked at her clock. “I know that now,” she said, feeling smart.

A soft creak sounded. The window opened a little, and a cool breeze drifted in. It tickled Gigi’s nose. “Achoo,” she giggled. The breeze slid a small screw across the table. Tink-tink. Gigi reached out. “Wait,” she said kindly to herself. “I can fix this, step by step.”

She took three deep breaths. In, out. In, out. In, out. Then she moved the screw into a tiny dish. “Small parts stay together,” she said. “That is good time care.”

When the sand fell again, Gigi turned the yarn hand to “Build More.” She added a ribbon to hang the clock. She stuck on a little pocket for notes. She placed a small picture of a sun for morning, a book for reading, a broom for tidying, and a moon for resting. “Pictures help me remember,” she said.

At the last, Gigi tested the Quiet Clock. She pointed one clothespin to the broom. “Tidy now.” She picked up three things. One—brush. Two—glue. Three—cloth. She sang, “Tidy, tidy, put away. We will build again today.”

She pointed the hand to the sun. “Break time,” she smiled. She looked out at the sky again. It had changed from bright to soft. The attic smelled like paper and lemons and a little bit of fresh air. It felt safe.

Gigi touched the soft bell. Ding-a-soft. She spoke to the attic. “I built a clock. I used my time. I solved small problems with gentle ideas. I can do things myself. And I can learn.”

The moth fluttered once more, like a tiny cheer. Gigi laughed. “Thank you, little friend.”

She hung the Quiet Clock on a beam where she could see it every day. “Tomorrow,” she planned, “I will build shelves for books.” She wrote it on a small card and tucked it into the clock pocket. “Plan, build, tidy, rest.”

Gigi stretched her long neck and felt warm and proud. “I can build with calm hands and a calm heart,” she said. “When I plan my time, I finish my work. When I find peaceful answers, the attic stays happy. I am learning every day.”

She turned off the lamp. The attic glowed softly. The Quiet Clock watched over the room with its friendly numbers and gentle bell.

“Good night, Quiet Clock,” Gigi whispered.

The End.
