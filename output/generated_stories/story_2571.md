# Sunny Song and the Little List

Sunny was a small singing bird with bright yellow feathers and a tiny, happy beak. She lived near a cozy garden shed. The shed smelled like wood and warm earth. Sunlight shone through a dusty window and made tiny sparkles in the air.

One morning, Sunny fluttered to the open door. “Tweet-tee-tee!” she sang. “Today I will help. Today I will finish my tasks!” Her heart felt bouncy and brave.

Sunny hopped onto a wooden shelf. A little paper lay there, with big simple words and pictures drawn in crayon. It was her task list.

- Find the red ribbon
- Water the seedlings
- Sort the buttons
- Hang the new wind chime

Sunny tapped the paper with her beak. “One thing at a time,” she chirped. “I can do it. I will do it.”

The shed was full of sounds. Drip… drip… went a leaky tap. Rustle… rustle… went a bag of potting soil. A friendly breeze whispered through the door and ruffled Sunny’s feathers.

Sunny looked for the red ribbon. She peered into a tin can. “Clink, clink.” Only nails. She opened a drawer. “Creak… creak.” Only yarn in many colors. She peeked into a small basket. “Hello?” she chirped.

“Here I am!” said a soft voice. It was the red ribbon, curled like a tiny snake. “I was hiding under the blue cloth.”

Sunny laughed. “Thank you for telling me!” She carefully picked up the ribbon with her beak. She set it on the shelf next to the list. “Task one, done,” she sang. “Tweet-tee-tee!”

She marked a little dot on the paper. It felt good. Her chest felt warm, like sunshine.

Next was “Water the seedlings.” Sunny hopped to a row of tiny pots. Little green sprouts poked up like baby fingers. The soil looked dry and sleepy.

Sunny lifted a small watering can. “Oh! It’s heavy,” she said. She tried to tip it. “Sloooosh.” Too much water came out.

“Oops!” Sunny chirped. “I need to be gentle.” She took a slow breath. In… out. She steadied the can with her wing and her foot. She made her beak very still. “Tiny sips,” she told herself.

Drip… drip… drip… The water kissed each sprout. The leaves shivered with joy. The smell of wet soil rose up, rich and sweet.

“Thank you,” whispered the seedlings. “We feel awake.”

“You’re welcome,” Sunny said. “Task two, done!” She made another dot on the paper and wiggled with pride.

Now it was time to sort the buttons. A big jar of buttons sat on the workbench. “Tink, tink, tink,” they sounded as she poured them into a shallow tray. Red. Blue. Yellow. Big. Small. Shiny. Matte.

Sunny hummed a sorting song. “Red in the red cup. Blue in the blue cup. Big in the big bowl. Small in the small bowl.” She looked with careful eyes. She moved with slow, steady wings.

“Can I help?” asked a friendly mouse peeking from behind a flower pot. His whiskers twitched.

Sunny smiled. “Yes, we can work together. I will look. You can push.”

The mouse nudged a blue button with his nose. “Boop!” It slid into the blue cup.

Sunny pushed a big red button with her wing. “Tap!” It landed in the big bowl.

They worked side by side. Tap. Boop. Tink. Their bodies moved in a gentle rhythm. It felt like a dance. It felt like a game.

“All sorted!” Sunny chirped. She and the mouse bumped noses. “Task three, done!” She marked another dot. Her dots made a tiny trail of success.

Last was “Hang the new wind chime.” Sunny looked up at the doorway. The wind chime was made of shells and little spoons. It smelled like salt and sounded like a secret ocean when it moved.

Sunny flapped up, but the chime swung away. “Oh!” she said. She tried again, slower this time. She watched the breeze. She felt the air on her feathers. Whoosh… now pause… now whoosh.

“I will move when the wind is quiet,” she told herself. She waited. The breeze softened. The spoons stopped jingling. Sunny reached out with her beak and her tiny foot. Clip! She hooked the chime onto the nail.

“Jingle-jingle!” sang the chime. The sound was bright and happy.

“I did it!” Sunny sang back. “Task four, done!”

She fluttered down to the shelf and looked at her paper. Four dots. A full list. Her heart felt like a big smile.

The mouse clapped his tiny paws. “You finished everything!”

Sunny nodded. “I went slowly. I listened. I watched. I asked for help. I did each thing in order.” She puffed up with calm joy. “That is how I complete my tasks.”

The wind chime sang again as a gentle breeze danced through the door. The seedlings looked green and proud. The buttons sat in neat cups, like colorful candies. The red ribbon waited, ready to tie a gift.

Sunny took a happy breath. The shed smelled like wet earth, metal, and a little bit of ocean from the shells. “Today,” she said, “I learned that finishing feels good. I learned that I can be careful and kind to my work. I learned I can do things by myself, and also with a friend.”

The mouse nodded. “And you learned to move with the wind.”

Sunny giggled. “Yes! That is coordination. That is understanding.”

She hopped to the door and sang a bright, sweet song that floated into the garden. “One thing at a time,” she trilled. “Slow and steady. We can do it.”

The End.
