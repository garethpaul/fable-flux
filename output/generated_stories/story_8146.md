# Kira's Colorful Discovery

Kira the koala loved her mama's home office. It had a big wooden desk that smelled like vanilla. It had soft carpet that felt fuzzy under her paws. Best of all, it had a special art corner just for Kira!

Today, Kira wanted to paint a picture of her family. She had red paint, blue paint, and yellow paint. She had a big white paper and a fluffy brush.

"I'll paint Mama first," said Kira. She dipped her brush in the red paint. Swish, swish! She painted a red circle for Mama's head.

But something looked wrong. Mama's fur wasn't red at all! Mama's fur was gray, just like Kira's.

"Oh no!" said Kira. Her ears drooped down. "This doesn't look like Mama!"

Kira tried again. She painted Papa with blue paint. Swish, swish! But Papa wasn't blue either. Papa was gray too.

"This is too hard!" Kira said. A little tear rolled down her furry cheek. "I don't have gray paint!"

Mama Koala heard Kira's sad voice. She walked over to the art corner. Her footsteps were soft and gentle.

"What's happening, little artist?" Mama asked. She sat down next to Kira on the carpet.

"I want to paint our family," Kira sniffled. "But I don't have gray paint. We're all gray, but I only have red, blue, and yellow!"

Mama smiled. "Let me show you something special," she said. "What happens when we mix colors together?"

Kira wiped her eyes. "Mix colors? I don't understand."

Mama picked up a clean brush. "Watch carefully," she said. She dipped the brush in blue paint. Then she dipped it in yellow paint too.

"Now watch what happens," Mama said. She painted a small circle on the paper. The blue and yellow swirled together. Like magic, they made green!

"Wow!" gasped Kira. Her eyes grew big and round. "It turned green!"

"That's right," said Mama. "When we mix blue and yellow, we get green. What do you think happens when we mix other colors?"

Kira thought hard. She touched her chin with her paw. "Can I try?"

"Of course!" said Mama.

Kira picked up her brush. She mixed red and blue together. Swish, swish! The colors made purple!

"Purple!" shouted Kira, clapping her paws.

Then she mixed red and yellow. They made bright orange!

"I understand now!" said Kira. "Colors can make new colors!"

But Kira still looked puzzled. "How do I make gray for our family?"

Mama thought for a moment. "What color do you see when you mix all the colors together?"

Kira carefully mixed red, blue, and yellow on her paper. The bright colors swirled and danced. Slowly, they became a soft gray color!

"Gray!" Kira squeaked with joy. "I made gray!"

Now Kira painted her family with the special gray she made. She painted Mama with loving care. She painted Papa reading his favorite book. She painted herself with a big smile.

"This looks just like us!" said Kira proudly. She held up her painting. The gray koalas looked happy and real.

"You did wonderful work," said Mama, giving Kira a warm hug. "You learned that sometimes we need to think in new ways to solve problems."

Kira nodded. "I learned that mixing things together can make something new and beautiful!"

She hung her painting on the office wall. Every time she looked at it, she remembered that understanding how things work together can help solve any problem.

From that day on, Kira loved mixing colors and discovering new ones. She learned that the best solutions come from understanding and trying new ideas.

The End.
