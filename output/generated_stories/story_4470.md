# Ellie Finds a Way

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a sweet, curious trunk. One sunny afternoon, Ellie padded into the pantry. The pantry smelled like warm oats, crunchy nuts, and sweet bananas. Shelves stood tall like little forests, full of jars and boxes.

Ellie’s tummy rumbled. “I want a snack,” she said softly. She saw her favorite jar on a high shelf. It was the jar with dried banana chips. The jar sparkled in the light. “Mmm,” Ellie whispered. “Crunchy, sweet, and just right.”

She stretched her trunk up, up, up. Her trunk waved and wiggled. But the jar was still too high. Ellie’s feet shuffled on the cool floor. “I can’t reach,” she sighed.

Ellie looked around. On the pantry floor, there was a small wooden stool. There was also a cloth bag of oats, a basket of apples, and a soft, folded kitchen towel. She took a deep breath. “I can try,” she told herself. “I can think and try.”

Her friend Pip the mouse peeked in from a crack under the door. “Squeak! Do you need help, Ellie?” asked Pip. His whiskers twitched, and his nose smelled like cinnamon.

“Yes, please,” Ellie said. “I want the banana chips. But they are too high.”

Pip looked at the tall shelf. “We can make a plan,” he said. “Let’s think.” Pip’s eyes shone. “What can we use?”

Ellie sniffed the air. She felt the cool stool with her foot. “The stool!” she said. “It is strong and close.”

Pip nodded. “Good idea,” he squeaked. “But be safe.”

Ellie placed the stool near the shelf. She tested it with one foot. The stool felt steady. “I will climb slowly,” she said. She put one big foot on the stool. Then she put her other foot. The stool made a tiny creak, but it did not wobble.

Ellie stretched her trunk again. “Almost,” she whispered. Her trunk brushed the jar. Tap, tap. The jar moved a little. Ellie felt a flutter in her tummy. “I can do this,” she said to herself.

Then Pip squeaked, “Wait! If the jar slips, it might fall.” He pointed to the soft towel. “Let’s make a safe place.”

Ellie smiled. “Good thinking.” She asked, “Pip, will you help me spread the towel on the floor under the shelf?”

Pip grinned. “Yes!” Together, Ellie and Pip pulled the towel open. They spread it like a little soft cloud. It felt smooth under Ellie’s toes.

Ellie climbed back on the stool. She took a deep breath in. The pantry smelled sweet and calm. “Steady,” she told her trunk. She wrapped her trunk gently around the jar lid. Twist, twist. The lid felt tight. Ellie paused and thought. “Righty tighty, lefty loosey,” she said. She twisted the other way. The lid loosened with a soft pop.

“Good job!” Pip cheered, clapping his tiny paws.

Ellie’s ears warmed with pride. “Thank you,” she said. “Now I will bring it down.” She held the jar close to her chest with her trunk. Step by small step, she climbed down from the stool. Her feet felt the floor again. Safe.

But then Ellie heard a little sniffle. From behind the apple basket, a shy voice said, “I wish I could reach the oat cookies. They smell so good.” It was Tilly the turtle. Her shell was shiny green, and her eyes were big and kind.

Ellie smiled. “Hi, Tilly. Would you like to share a snack?”

Tilly peeked out. “Yes, please. But I can’t reach the cookies. They are on the second shelf.”

Ellie looked at the cookies. She thought for a moment. “We can find a way together,” she said. “Let’s make a plan.”

Pip squeaked, “We can move the basket to make space. We can slide the stool. And we can hold the towel again to be safe.”

Ellie nodded. “Good plan. Tilly, would you like to watch the stool with Pip?” Tilly smiled. “Yes! I can watch carefully.”

Ellie moved the stool, slow and steady. She climbed up again and gently took the cookie tin. The tin was light and cool. She held it close and climbed down. “Here we go,” she said.

Tilly’s eyes sparkled. “Thank you! Can we trade? I will share my cookie if you share your banana chips.”

Ellie thought. “That is fair,” she said. “We can share and enjoy together.” Pip clapped. “A perfect trade!”

They sat on the towel like a little picnic. Crunch! went the banana chips. Munch! went the cookies. The pantry felt warm and friendly. Everyone smiled.

Ellie licked a crumb from her lip. “I am proud,” she said. “I used my own ideas. I asked for help kindly. We made a plan. We stayed safe. And we shared.”

Pip nodded. “You solved the problem.”

Tilly added, “You helped me too. That feels good.”

Ellie’s heart felt big and bright. “When something is hard, I can stop, think, and try,” she said. “I can ask friends, and we can make a fair plan.”

They all agreed. In the cozy pantry, with sweet smells and happy tummies, Ellie learned she could rely on herself and include others too.

The End.
