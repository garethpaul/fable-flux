# Brave Little Song in the Living Room

In a playful living room, a small blue bird named Lila loved to sing. The room was sunny and cozy. A soft rug felt fluffy under tiny toes. Colorful pillows sat like rainbow clouds, and a tall houseplant waved its green leaves like gentle hands. Toys made happy clinks and clacks. Lila perched on the back of a couch and hummed, “La-la-la!”

“Good morning, Lila!” said Teddy, a soft brown teddy bear with a stitched smile. He sat on the rug next to a stack of blocks.

“Good morning!” Lila chirped. “Today, I want to sing a big brave song. A strong song! But my tummy feels fluttery.” She held her little wings close. “I feel a bit scared.”

Teddy nodded kindly. “It’s okay to feel scared,” he said. “Feelings are like weather. They come and go.”

Lila blinked her bright eyes. “What if my voice is too small?” she whispered. Her feathers shivered, and she tucked her head under one wing.

Just then, the window let in a warm breeze. It smelled like oranges and fresh soap. From the kitchen came a soft clink of a cup and the sweet crunch of an apple. Lila’s tummy rumbled. “Oh!” she giggled. “I forgot breakfast.”

Teddy patted the rug. “Self-care time,” he said. “Let’s have a snack.”

They shared slices of juicy apple and sips of cool water. The apple was crisp and sweet. “Crunch, crunch,” went Teddy. “Peep, peep,” went Lila, happy again.

“My body feels better,” Lila said. “Food gives me energy. Water helps me sing.”

Teddy smiled. “Now let’s warm up. Breathe in… and out.” They took three slow breaths together. In… out. In… out. In… out.

Lila opened her beak and hummed, “Mmm-mmm.” Her voice buzzed like a busy bee. “Lip brrrrr,” she tried, and her beak tickled. She wiggled her wings and rolled her little bird shoulders. “I am getting ready,” she said. “I can be brave.”

She hopped to the top of a pillow tower. “I will sing here!” But the pillow tower wobbled. “Uh-oh,” said Lila. The tower leaned. “Whoa!” Down came the pillows—plop, flop, flump! Lila fluttered down safely, but her song stuck like a hiccup.

“I can’t do it!” Lila squeaked. Her chest felt tight, and her eyes were wet.

Teddy sat beside her. “That was frustrating,” he said softly. “Let’s name the feeling.”

“I feel mad and sad,” Lila said. “My song fell down. My tower fell down.”

Teddy nodded. “Thank you for telling me. When I feel that way, I try a calm plan. Want to try with me?”

“Yes,” Lila sniffed.

Together they did the Calm Three:
- “One: Hand on heart.” Lila pressed a wing to her chest. Thump-thump, went her brave heart.
- “Two: Belly breath.” They took a slow breath. In… out. Lila felt her belly lift and fall.
- “Three: Kind words.” Teddy said, “I can try again.” Lila whispered, “I am brave. I can try again.”

The room felt quiet and safe. The lamp glowed warm like honey. The rug felt soft under Lila’s tiny feet. The houseplant cheered with a leafy swish.

“Where should I sing?” Lila asked.

Teddy pointed to the couch corner, sturdy and still. “Here is a safe stage. Brave does not mean big and high. Brave can be small and steady.”

Lila stood in the corner. She did one more sip of water. She rolled her shoulders. She smiled at Teddy. “Ready,” she said.

“Go, Lila!” Teddy whispered.

Lila took a breath. “La…” The note was small. She took another breath. “La-la…” Her voice got brighter. She felt her feet on the couch, strong and sure. “La-la-laaaa!” Her song floated like a blue ribbon. It filled the playful room with light. The window breeze danced with her tune. The blocks hummed. The pillows rested in quiet stacks.

She finished with a soft “doo-doo-doo,” and the room was still. Then Teddy clapped his fuzzy paws. “Brave Lila!” he cheered.

Lila giggled. “I did it! I felt scared, and I sang anyway. My brave heart helped me.”

Teddy hugged her gently. “You used self-care. You ate good food, drank water, warmed up, and breathed. You named your feelings and tried again. That is big bravery.”

Lila puffed her chest. “When I feel frustrated,” she said, “I can use my Calm Three. I can ask for help. I can try again.”

The afternoon sun painted gold on the walls. Lila hopped to the window and sang a happy thank-you song. “Brave is not loud only,” she sang. “Brave is caring for me. Brave is trying again.”

Teddy swayed with the tune. The houseplant waved. The living room felt proud and peaceful.

Lila looked at the cozy room and at her kind friend. “Tomorrow,” she chirped, “I will try a new song.”

“And if it’s wobbly,” Teddy said, “we will breathe, snack, and try again.”

Lila smiled. “Yes,” she said. “I am brave, I am kind, and I take care of me.”

The End.
