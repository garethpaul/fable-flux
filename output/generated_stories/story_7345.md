# Joey's Special Bounce

Joey the kangaroo was the happiest little joey in all of Australia. Every morning, he would hop, hop, hop up to the rooftop of the tall building where his family lived. The rooftop was his favorite place because he could see the whole city sparkling in the sunshine.

"Good morning, world!" Joey would call out, bouncing on his strong back legs. His fluffy gray fur felt warm in the morning sun, and his little pouch jiggled as he moved.

But today was different. Today, Joey felt sad instead of happy.

"What's wrong, little one?" asked Mama Kangaroo, hopping up beside him on the rooftop.

Joey's ears drooped down. "Mama, all the other animals hop differently than me. The rabbits hop on four legs, the frogs hop in tiny jumps, and the grasshoppers hop with their long legs. I'm the only one who hops on two big feet like this. I feel... different."

Mama Kangaroo's eyes twinkled with love. "Oh, Joey! Being different is what makes you special. Watch this!"

She began to bounce high, high, HIGH into the air. "Boing! Boing! Boing!" The sound echoed across the rooftop like a happy drum.

"See how high we can jump?" Mama said, landing softly. "The rabbits can't do that. The frogs can't reach the clouds like we can. And grasshoppers? They're much too small!"

Joey tried a little bounce. "Boing!" He went higher than he expected. "Oh! That was fun!"

"Every morning, let's make this our special routine," Mama suggested. "We'll come to the rooftop and practice our amazing kangaroo bounces together."

The next morning, Joey hopped to the rooftop again. This time, he felt excited instead of sad. "Ready for our bouncing practice, Mama!"

"Boing! Boing! Boing!" They bounced together, their feet making happy thumping sounds on the rooftop. The wind whooshed past Joey's ears, and he could smell the fresh morning air.

Soon, other animals came to watch. A little rabbit appeared at the rooftop door. "Wow! You bounce so high! I wish I could do that!"

A tiny frog hopped over. "Amazing! You can see everything from up there!"

Even a green grasshopper chirped, "Your bounces are like music! Thump, thump, thump!"

Joey felt his heart fill with joy. "Would you like to try bouncing with us?" he asked kindly.

The rabbit tried to bounce high but could only hop a little bit. The frog jumped as hard as he could but stayed close to the ground. The grasshopper leaped with all his might but couldn't reach very high.

"We all bounce in our own special ways!" Joey laughed, not making fun but celebrating happily. "Rabbit, your quick little hops are perfect for hopping through gardens! Frog, your small jumps help you swim better! And Grasshopper, your tiny leaps let you land on flowers!"

Every morning after that, all the animals met on the rooftop for their bouncing routine. Each one showed off their special way of moving. Joey learned that being different wasn't something to feel sad about – it was something to celebrate!

"I love being a kangaroo!" Joey announced one sunny morning, bouncing higher than ever before. "And I love that we're all different!"

From that day on, whenever Joey felt different or worried, he would remember his special bouncing routine and all his friends who celebrated what made each of them unique.

The End.
