# Joey Roo and the Cozy Closet Camp

Joey Roo was a joyful little kangaroo joey. His tail wiggled when he was excited, and his ears perked up when he had an idea. One rainy morning, the sky went drip-drop on the windows. “Pitter-patter, pitter-patter,” Joey whispered, listening with a smile.

He hopped down the hall and opened the big closet. It smelled like clean shirts and fresh soap. It was quiet and soft inside. “This is my cozy hideaway,” Joey said. He made a little camp with a blanket on the floor, a pillow, and a tiny lantern that glowed warm and yellow. “Click!” went the switch. Light filled the small space like a hug.

Joey set out his things. He put crayons in a row, a small book with bright pictures, and a snack of apple slices in a tiny cup. He took a deep breath. The closet felt safe, like a nest. “I can make my own fun,” he told himself. His heart felt big and brave.

“Thump-thump,” came soft hops in the hall. It was Kiki, his koala friend. Her fur was gray and soft like a cloud. She peeked in. “Hi, Joey,” Kiki said in a tiny voice. “It’s very rainy. Can I play too?”

Joey looked at his little camp. The closet was not big. He had one pillow and one cup of apples. He felt a little tug inside. He liked his things. He liked his camp. But he looked at Kiki’s eyes. They were hopeful and kind.

Joey took a breath. He put his paw on his chest and listened to his own heart. It said, Be generous. Share. You have enough. Joey smiled. “Yes, Kiki. Come in,” he said. “We can be cozy together.”

Kiki’s face lit up. “Thank you!” she said. She climbed in and squeezed beside him. It was snug, but nice. The blanket felt warm. They could hear the rain whispering outside, “Shhh, shhh, shhh.”

“Do you want an apple slice?” Joey asked.

Kiki nodded. “Crunch,” went the apple. Joey took one too. “Crunch,” he said, giggling. Sharing made the snack taste sweeter.

“Let’s draw,” Joey said. He slid the crayons toward Kiki. She looked at the small row. There were only a few colors. Joey thought, I want the blue. It’s my favorite. He felt the tug again.

He took another breath. Be generous, he told himself. He handed the blue crayon to Kiki first. “You can use blue,” he said.

Kiki smiled big. “Thank you, Joey. You are kind,” she said. Joey felt warm inside, like sunshine. He picked red and drew a big heart. Kiki drew a blue sky and a fluffy tree.

“Tap-tap,” came another sound at the closet door. It was Pip the little possum. His nose wiggled. “Hi,” Pip said. “It smells like apples and crayons. Can I join?”

Joey looked around. The closet felt smaller now. He had no extra pillow. He had only two apple slices left. He felt worried. Can I do this? he wondered. He listened to the rain. “Shhh,” it said. He listened to his heart. You can be generous and smart. You can figure it out.

Joey nodded. “Yes, Pip. Come in. We can make it work,” he said. He folded the blanket in three small squares. He said, “We can share the soft parts. We can take turns with the lantern. We can split the apples.”

Pip squeezed in. “Thank you,” he said. Joey passed one apple slice to Pip and one to Kiki. He did not keep one for himself. His tummy rumbled a little. He felt a tiny pang. Then Pip broke his slice in half. “Here, Joey,” Pip said. “You shared first. I share with you.”

Kiki did the same. “We all share,” she said. Joey took the little halves. “Crunch-crunch,” he said. His tummy felt better, and his heart felt proud.

They played a game. “Let’s whisper a story,” Joey said, turning the lantern low. The light made soft circles on the closet wall. The shirts above them rustled like quiet trees. The floor felt smooth under their toes.

“I start,” Joey said. “Once, there was a brave boat.” He looked at his friends. “Your turn,” he whispered.

“It sailed on a shiny lake,” Kiki added.

“With three friends on board,” Pip said.

They took turns, generous with words. They learned to listen. They learned to wait. They learned to help. When Kiki got stuck, Joey said, “You can do it.” When Pip felt shy, Kiki said, “We believe in you.” Their kind words felt like warm cocoa.

Soon, the rain slowed down. The window went “drip… drop… stop.” The closet felt even cozier now, filled with giggles and gentle breaths. Joey looked at his friends. “I am happy,” he said. “Sharing made our camp better.”

Kiki nodded. “You shared your space.”

Pip nodded. “You shared your things.”

Joey nodded too. “And we shared our ideas,” he said. “When we share, there is more joy.”

They cleaned up together. Joey rolled the blanket. Kiki stacked the crayons. Pip turned off the lantern. “Click,” went the light. They each did their part, using their own paws and their own care. Joey felt proud that he could set up and clean up by himself, and also help his friends.

At the door, they did a soft group hug. It felt like a big, gentle squeeze. “Thank you for being generous,” Kiki said.

“Thank you for being kind,” Pip said.

Joey smiled. “I learned that sharing is caring,” he said. “I can be generous and still be okay. I can trust myself to make a plan. We can all help each other.”

They hopped and padded down the hall together, light and happy, ready for the next small adventure.

The End.
