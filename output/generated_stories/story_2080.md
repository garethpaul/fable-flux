# Delphy the Dolphin Visits the Balcony Garden

Delphy was a playful dolphin who loved to learn. One sunny morning, Delphy splashed in a big blue tub on a high balcony. The balcony had a tiny garden with pots of mint, tomatoes, and bright marigolds. Bees buzzed. Leaves smelled fresh. A wind chime tinkled softly, like tiny bells.

Delphy peeked over the tub and waved a fin. “Hello, garden!” Delphy chirped. “What can I learn today?”

Mina, a little girl with curly hair, opened the sliding door. She held a small watering can and a gentle smile. “Hi, Delphy,” Mina said. “Want to help in my balcony garden?”

“Yes, please!” said Delphy. “I want to help, and I want to share.”

Mina nodded. “We also take turns. We use kind words. We listen with our hearts.”

Delphy took a slow breath. In. Out. The mint leaves smelled cool and sweet. “I am ready,” Delphy said.

Mina showed a chart with flags from many places—little pictures of Earth, a maple leaf, a sun, a star, and more. “Our seeds come from all over the world,” Mina said. “That makes our garden a world garden. We care for each seed, big or small.”

“That is global friendship,” Delphy said. “Like global-citizenship! We care for our big home, Earth, and for each other.”

“Exactly,” Mina said.

Mina’s cat, Pepper, padded over and purred. Pepper brushed against a pot and meowed, “Mrrrp.” Delphy giggled. “Hi, Pepper!”

Mina held up the watering can. “First, we water. We will take turns. I go first, then you, then Pepper can watch.” She winked.

Mina poured slowly. The water made a soft patter on the soil. Delphy waited and watched. The leaves quivered. A warm breeze kissed Delphy’s face. Delphy wanted to splash right away, but Delphy remembered to wait.

“I can wait,” Delphy whispered. “Waiting is kind.”

When Mina finished, she passed the can. “Your turn, Delphy.”

Delphy held the can carefully with both fins. “I will pour slowly,” Delphy said. “Plants drink small sips.” Delphy tipped the can. Patter, patter. “I am helping!”

Mina smiled. “You are being mindful. You are watching, listening, and going slow.”

Suddenly, a little snail peeked from under a marigold leaf. “Hello,” said the snail in a tiny voice. “Please be gentle. This is my home.”

Delphy nodded. “We will be gentle. We share this garden.”

A butterfly landed on the tomato cage. Its wings were yellow and black. They fluttered like soft paper. “I sip nectar here,” the butterfly said. “Thank you for the flowers.”

“We are happy you’re here,” Mina said. “We care for the garden so all friends can visit.”

Delphy’s tub splashed as a gust of wind blew. Water spilled toward the mint pot. Delphy felt worried. “Oh no!” Delphy said. “Did I spill too much?”

Mina took a slow breath and put a hand on her heart. “Let’s be mindful. Let’s look and listen.” They crouched down. The soil was damp but not muddy. The mint still stood tall.

Delphy copied Mina and took a slow breath too. In. Out. “I feel calm now,” Delphy said.

Pepper meowed again and reached a paw toward the butterfly. Delphy gently swished a fin. “Pepper, please give the butterfly space,” Delphy said in a kind but strong voice. “Butterflies are gentle.”

Pepper pulled back and blinked. “Mrrp.” Pepper sat down and watched with quiet eyes.

Mina nodded. “That was assertive and kind, Delphy. You used your strong voice to protect a friend.”

Delphy felt proud and warm inside. “I can speak up kindly,” Delphy said.

They planted tiny seeds from a bright paper packet. The packet showed a little globe. “These are beans,” Mina said. “They grew first in one place, and now they can grow here too. People share seeds across the world!”

Delphy placed each seed in the soil with a soft plop. “We all share Earth,” Delphy said. “We are neighbors, even far away.”

Mina clapped softly. “That is global-citizenship. We learn about others. We help the Earth. We take turns and use kind words.”

A pigeon landed on the railing and cooed, “Coo-coo.” It tilted its head at the chart with flags. Delphy waved. “Hello, pigeon friend. Do you fly to many places?”

“Coo,” said the pigeon, fluffing feathers. “Many roofs, many skies.”

Delphy smiled. “We are all under one big sky.”

When the work was done, Mina brought out three little cups. “Herbal tea for us,” she said. “Mint for me, warm water for you, and a saucer for Pepper.” Steam curled up. It smelled sweet and calm.

They sat quietly for a moment. The wind chime sang. The butterfly twirled. The snail tucked in for a nap. Delphy closed their eyes and listened. In. Out. Tinkle, buzz, coo.

Delphy opened their eyes. “Today I learned to take turns. I learned to be mindful. I learned to speak up kindly. And I learned that friends all over the world share and care.”

Mina touched the globe on the seed packet. “We are global citizens,” she said. “We care for people, plants, and animals, near and far.”

Delphy splashed a tiny happy splash. “I will keep caring,” Delphy said. “I will keep learning.”

The garden glowed in the soft light. The world felt big and friendly.

The End.
