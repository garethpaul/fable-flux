# Pippa Parrot’s Caring Game

In a cozy study corner, under a sunny window, lived Pippa the creative parrot. Pippa had bright green feathers and a rainbow tail. She loved to make things. She loved to learn. Her study corner smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Markers clicked. Scissors snipped. Pages rustled. It was Pippa’s favorite place.

One morning, Pippa fluffed her feathers and said, “Squawk! Today I will make a game we can all play!” She wanted a game that was fun like sports and kind like a hug. She spread colorful papers on her little desk. She drew circles, stars, and hearts. She glued soft leaves that had fallen from a house plant. “Only leaves on the floor,” she said. “We must care for plants. We do not pull.”

Tap-tap went her pencil. Snip-snip went her scissors. “I will call it the Caring Game!” she chirped.

Her friends waddled in. Benny the bunny hopped softly. “Hi, Pippa!” he said, ears wiggling.

Maya the mouse scurried up, tiny paws patter-patter. “It smells like lemon markers,” she squeaked, smiling.

Tiko the turtle came last, slow and steady, shell shining. “I heard there is a game,” he said, blinking.

Pippa beamed. “Yes! We can play in the study corner. The floor is our field. The rules are kind. We take turns. We cheer. We help.” She laid out soft paper circles like stepping stones. She placed a small paper ball in the middle. “We will roll the ball to a friend and say one kind word from our family traditions. We will also move our bodies! Roll, clap, hop, and smile!”

Benny bounced. “Sports! I love to hop,” he said.

Maya clapped. “I can run fast,” she peeped.

Tiko nodded. “I can… try,” he said quietly.

Pippa saw Tiko’s slow feet. She tilted her head. “We will go slow and fast,” she said. “We will make space for every friend.”

They began. Pippa rolled the ball to Benny. It went shhh across the floor. “Benny, your word?” she asked.

Benny caught it with his paws. “In my family, we say ‘Shalom.’ It means peace,” he said proudly.

Everyone smiled. “Shalom!” they said together.

Benny rolled to Maya. Maya squeaked, “We say ‘Namaste.’ It means I see the good in you.” She pressed her tiny paws together. The room felt warm, like sunshine on cheeks.

“Namaste,” whispered Tiko, a little late, but with a big smile.

Maya rolled to Tiko. The ball bumped his shell and rolled away. “Oh!” Tiko gasped, shoulders drooping.

Benny hopped to fetch it. “It’s okay,” he said. “We’re a team.” He placed the ball in Tiko’s hands.

Tiko breathed slowly. “In my family, we say ‘Aloha.’ It means love, peace, and ‘hello,’ too,” he said softly.

“Aloha,” everyone sang.

They clapped three times and did three small hops. One, two, three! The paper circles rustled under their feet. It sounded like leaves in wind. Pippa giggled and flapped. “Now we do the Caring Move! We pass the ball and help a friend move to the next circle.”

Pippa rolled to Benny. He hopped to his circle. Easy! Then Benny rolled to Maya. She scurried and giggled, whiskers twitching. Next was Tiko’s turn again. The ball rolled a little too fast. It bumped his foot.

Tiko’s eyes got wet. “I’m slow,” he said. “I can’t play fast games.”

Pippa’s heart squeezed. She looked at Tiko’s face. She listened to his small voice. She imagined how it felt to be left behind. She thought, “Empathy means I feel with my friend.” She nodded. “Thank you for telling us,” she said softly. “We can change the game to help.”

Benny leaned close. “How can we help?”

Maya held Tiko’s hand. “We can go turtle-slow,” she said.

Pippa smiled. “New rule! We match our friend. Slow when someone needs slow. Fast when someone is ready. We cheer for every try.”

They tried again. Pippa counted with a gentle beat. “Roll… pause… clap.” The ball moved slowly, bump, bump, bump. Tiko placed his foot on the next circle. Benny and Maya kept pace. “You did it!” Pippa chirped.

Tiko’s smile grew. “Aloha,” he whispered again.

They played more rounds. Each turn, a friend shared a tradition. Benny talked about a dance his grandma taught him and showed two hop-hop steps. Maya spoke about her family tea time and how they say thanks for water. Pippa shared a story of painting eggs with plant colors and said, “We use onion skins and spinach. We do not waste. We care for Earth.”

They added a conservation rule to the Caring Game. Pippa held up a tiny jar. “Scrap jar!” she said. “We keep paper bits for art. We turn off the lamp when we finish. We water the plant with our leftover drink.” The friends nodded. Caring for Earth felt good.

Soon, they had a cheering song:
“We roll and we care,
We share and we share!
We move how you need,
Kind hearts lead!”

The study corner was full of soft sounds and bright smiles. The floor felt cool under paws and feet. The lemon marker smell mixed with warm snack crumbs. They took a water break. Pippa handed Tiko the cup first.

“Thank you,” Tiko said.

“You’re welcome,” said Pippa. “I like how you keep trying.”

At the end, Pippa asked, “What did we learn today?”

Benny said, “Peace words make my heart calm.”

Maya said, “When we match a friend, no one feels left out.”

Tiko said, “Empathy feels like a hand holding mine.”

Pippa ruffled her rainbow tail. “Yes. Empathy means we listen, we look, and we care. Sports are more fun when we play together. Sharing our family words is beautiful. And we can help Earth with small choices.”

They stacked the circles, saved the scraps, and turned off the lamp. The study corner glowed with happy quiet.

“We did it,” Pippa whispered.

“Aloha,” said Tiko.

“Namaste,” said Maya.

“Shalom,” said Benny.

“And squawk—thank you,” Pippa laughed.

The End.
