# Melody's Morning Song

On a sunny morning, a little yellow bird named Melody perched on Mrs. Chen's peaceful front porch. The wooden railing felt warm under her tiny feet, and the sweet smell of jasmine flowers filled the air.

"La-la-la! Tweet-tweet-tweet!" Melody sang as loudly as she could. She loved her beautiful voice and wanted everyone to hear it.

Mrs. Chen was inside, trying to rock her baby grandson to sleep. The baby had been fussy all night, and Mrs. Chen was very tired. But Melody's loud singing kept waking the baby up.

"Waaah! Waaah!" cried the baby through the window.

Mrs. Chen gently opened the front door. Her kind eyes looked tired, and she spoke softly. "Hello, little bird. You have such a lovely voice, but could you please sing more quietly? My grandson is trying to sleep."

Melody tilted her head. She had never thought about how her singing might affect others. "But I love to sing loud!" she chirped. "My songs make me happy!"

"I understand," said Mrs. Chen with a gentle smile. "Music is wonderful. In my family, we have a saying: 'Beautiful music shared with kindness touches more hearts than loud music shared without thought.'"

Melody felt confused. She didn't want to stop singing, but she could see that Mrs. Chen looked very tired. The little bird's heart felt heavy as she thought about the crying baby.

"I don't know what to do," Melody whispered, her voice much softer now.

Mrs. Chen sat down on her porch swing, which creaked gently in the morning breeze. "Would you like to learn a special way to sing? My grandmother taught me that the most beautiful songs are like gifts - they should be given at the right time and in the right way."

Melody hopped closer, her bright eyes curious. "What do you mean?"

"Well," said Mrs. Chen, "in the morning when people are waking up, soft, gentle songs help them start their day peacefully. When babies are sleeping, quiet humming can be like a lullaby. And when people are celebrating, that's when loud, joyful songs are perfect!"

Melody's feathers ruffled with excitement. "So I can still sing loud sometimes?"

"Of course!" laughed Mrs. Chen. "But first, let's practice being gentle. Would you like to help me sing a soft lullaby for my grandson?"

Melody nodded eagerly. She cleaned her beak carefully on the porch railing and fluffed her feathers to prepare for this special song.

Together, Mrs. Chen and Melody hummed a gentle tune. "Hmm-hmm-hmm," they sang softly, like a warm breeze through the jasmine flowers.

Soon, the baby's crying stopped. Through the window, they could see him sleeping peacefully, his tiny chest rising and falling.

"You see?" whispered Mrs. Chen. "Your gentle song was exactly what he needed."

Melody felt warm and happy inside. She had learned that her voice was a gift, and gifts should be shared thoughtfully. From that day on, she sang softly in the mornings, loudly during celebrations, and always listened to what others needed.

"Thank you for teaching me," Melody chirped quietly. "Tomorrow I'll come back and sing gentle morning songs!"

Mrs. Chen smiled. "You're always welcome here, little friend."

As the sun climbed higher, Melody flew away, her heart full of joy and her mind full of new understanding about kindness and respect.

The End.
