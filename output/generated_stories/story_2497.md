# Bram the Busy Beaver’s Guest Room Gathering

Bram was a busy beaver with bright teeth and a soft, shiny tail. Today, he was in the guest room at Grandma’s house. The guest room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. Sunlight made the curtains glow warm and yellow.

“Tap, tap,” Bram said as he checked a little list he kept in his paw. “Today is the big welcome day. Friends from many places are coming to stay!”

Bram wanted everyone to feel safe and happy. He puffed a pillow. He smoothed a blanket. He set out cups of cool water. The cups clinked softly. The carpet felt squishy under his feet.

Knock, knock.

Bram opened the door with a smile. “Hello!”

First came Luma the llama from the mountains. She had colorful ribbons in her fur and a gentle hum in her voice. “Hola,” she said softly. “I brought a bright blanket to share.”

“It looks so soft,” Bram said. He touched the blanket. It felt warm and fuzzy. “Thank you. You are welcome here.”

Next came Kito the kitten from a far-away city by the sea. He wore tiny bells on his collar that jingled. “Jambo,” he said with a grin. “I like to dance.”

“Welcome, Kito,” Bram said. The bells sounded like tiny stars.

Then Miko the mouse arrived from a snowy forest. He carried a small picture book. “Konnichiwa,” he whispered. “I like quiet and stories.”

“Welcome, Miko,” said Bram. He spoke softly so Miko would feel calm.

Bram looked at everyone. They all looked different. They spoke different words. They had different likes and needs. Bram’s heart felt big and warm. “We are guests together,” he said. “We can share, we can help, and we can learn.”

Luma placed her bright blanket on the bed. “Would you like to feel it?” she asked.

“Yes!” said Kito and Miko.

Bram paused. He remembered something important. “Let’s ask first,” he said. “Luma, may we touch your blanket?”

“Yes,” she said with a smile.

They touched the blanket. “It is so cozy,” said Miko, rubbing the soft threads. The room felt extra friendly.

Kito’s bells jingled as he jumped. “Let’s have a bell dance!” he shouted.

Miko’s ears folded back. “I like quiet,” he said. “Loud bells scare me.”

Bram took a brave breath. He felt a quick flutter in his tummy, but he stood tall. “We can be kind to each other,” he said. “Kito, can we dance softly so Miko feels safe?”

Kito nodded. “Yes, I can make tiny jingles.” He held his bells gently. The sound became soft and sweet, like rain on leaves.

Miko smiled. “Thank you.”

Luma hummed a mountain song. Bram clapped slowly. The guest room filled with soft music. It felt calm and happy.

Soon, it was time for snacks. Bram set out small plates. “In our home,” he said, “we ask before we take. We listen, and we share. These are our house rules.”

Luma offered dried fruit. “Would you like some?” she asked.

Kito sniffed. “It smells sweet!” He took one piece. “Thank you.”

Miko opened his picture book. “May I read to you?” he asked.

“Yes,” said everyone.

The book showed big trees and bright moons from Miko’s snowy home. “In my forest,” Miko said, “we bow to say hello.” He made a tiny bow.

Kito tried a bow. His bells made a polite ting. Luma bowed too, her ribbons swishing. Bram bowed and felt proud. “We are learning greetings from around the world,” he said. “This is global citizenship. We care about people from many places. We listen, we learn, and we help.”

Suddenly, Kito’s tail knocked into Luma’s blanket. It slipped from the bed. “Oh no!” Kito cried. His eyes grew wide. “I’m sorry.”

Bram knelt and picked up the blanket carefully. “It is okay,” he said. “Accidents happen. Let’s ask Luma what she needs.”

Luma held the blanket close. “This blanket is special,” she said softly. “It was made by my abuela, my grandma.”

Bram nodded. “Thank you for telling us. We will be extra gentle. Kito, would you like to help me fold it?”

“Yes,” said Kito. “May I touch it?”

“Yes,” Luma said, “with clean paws.”

They all washed their paws in the bathroom. The water was cool and splashy. Soap bubbles smelled like lemons. They giggled as the bubbles tickled their noses.

Back in the guest room, they folded the blanket slowly. Swish, smooth, swish. Bram’s heart felt calm and strong.

Miko looked at his book. “May I have quiet time now?” he asked.

Bram nodded. “Yes. Quiet time is a good boundary. We can play a calm game.” He laid out puzzle pieces with pictures of the world. They matched the shapes and said the names: “Mountains. Forests. Seas.”

Luma pointed to her mountains. Kito pointed to his seaside city. Miko pointed to snowy woods. Bram pointed to Grandma’s house. “We are all different,” he said, “and we all belong.”

The sun slid lower. The guest room felt cozy and safe. Bram tucked the bright blanket around the pillows. He looked at his friends. “Today we used courage to speak up. We respected each other’s needs. We shared and learned about our homes. That is global citizenship.”

Everyone smiled. “We listened,” said Miko. “We asked first,” said Luma. “We helped,” said Kito.

Bram nodded. “When we listen, ask, and help, the world feels like one big, kind home.”

They held paws and took a deep, gentle breath together. In, out. The room glowed warm like a hug.

The End.
