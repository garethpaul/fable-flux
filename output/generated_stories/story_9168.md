# Joey's Big Helper Day

Joey the kangaroo joey loved to bounce around the house. His soft brown fur was always clean because Mama washed it every day. Today, Joey bounced into the warm laundry room where Mama was working.

"Bounce, bounce, bounce!" Joey hopped around the washing machine. The room smelled like fresh soap and sunshine.

Mama looked tired. She had big piles of clothes everywhere. Towels sat in one corner. Shirts filled a basket. Socks were scattered on the floor.

"Oh my," Mama sighed. "So much laundry to fold."

Joey stopped bouncing. He saw Mama's sad face. Her shoulders drooped down low.

"I want to help!" Joey said with a big smile. "I can fold too!"

But when Joey tried to fold a big towel, it fell apart. The towel was too heavy for his little paws. It dropped on the floor with a soft thump.

"Grr!" Joey felt angry. His ears turned red. "This is too hard!"

Joey's friend Penny the mouse peeked out from behind the dryer. She had been watching quietly.

"Don't give up, Joey," Penny squeaked in her tiny voice. "Maybe you can help in a different way."

Joey took three deep breaths like Mama taught him. In... out... in... out... in... out. His anger started to melt away.

"You're right, Penny," Joey said. "Let me think."

Joey looked around the warm laundry room. His eyes grew bright. He saw all the socks on the floor.

"I know!" Joey bounced excitedly. "I can match the socks!"

Joey picked up a white sock. He hopped around until he found its partner. "Match!" he called out happily.

Then he found a blue sock. Bounce, bounce, bounce! He searched until he spotted the other blue sock hiding under a shirt.

"Another match!" Joey giggled.

Penny clapped her tiny paws. "You're doing great, Joey!"

Soon Joey had matched all the socks. He made neat little pairs all over the floor. Red socks together. Yellow socks together. Striped socks together.

"Joey!" Mama said with surprise. "You matched all the socks!"

"I wanted to help," Joey said proudly. "I couldn't fold the big towels, but I could do this!"

Mama gave Joey a warm hug. "You took initiative, sweetie. That means you saw what needed to be done and you did it yourself."

"What's initiative?" Joey asked.

"It means being a helper without being asked," Mama explained. "You saw I needed help, and you found your own special way to do it."

Penny scurried over to Joey. "You also stayed calm when you felt angry. That was very smart."

Joey's chest puffed out with pride. "When I got mad about the towel, I remembered to breathe. Then I could think better."

"Exactly right," Mama said. "Deep breaths help us make good choices."

From that day on, Joey always looked for ways to help. He sorted colors when Mama did laundry. He picked up toys without being asked. He even helped Penny reach high places with his bouncing.

"Helping feels good," Joey told Penny one sunny day.

"And you're the best helper friend ever," Penny squeaked back.

Joey learned that taking initiative meant looking around and finding ways to help. When things got hard, he remembered to breathe deeply instead of staying angry. And best of all, helping others made his friendships even stronger.

The End.
