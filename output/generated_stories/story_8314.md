# Fawn’s Rainbow Snack Plan

In a cozy study corner by the window, a gentle deer fawn named Fawn sat on a soft rug. The rug felt warm and tickly under her small hooves. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. A tiny desk held crayons, a notepad, and a cup of water. Fawn breathed in. The room smelled like paper and a little like pine cones from the window box.

“My tummy feels wiggly,” Fawn said. “Snack time soon!” Her ears twitched with a happy flick-flick.

On the wall was a big poster with colorful dots. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple. Fawn traced the colors with her hoof. “I want a rainbow snack,” she said. “Rainbow snacks help me grow strong.”

Mama Deer peeked in. “Hello, little leaf,” Mama said softly. “What are you planning?”

“I want to make a Rainbow Snack Plan,” Fawn said. “Can I learn it here in my study corner?”

Mama smiled. “Yes, let’s learn. Learning tastes good for our minds and our bodies.”

Fawn opened her notepad. She drew six big circles. “Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple,” she said. Her crayon went scritch-scratch on the paper.

“Let’s think of foods,” Mama said.

Fawn sniffed the air, thinking. “Red like… strawberries! They smell sweet and juicy.” She giggled. “They make my nose happy.”

“Good choice,” Mama said.

“Orange like… crunchy carrots!” Fawn tapped the orange circle. “Crunch, crunch!”

“Yellow like… banana slices,” Mama said. “Soft and sunny.”

“Green like… little leaves of spinach,” Fawn said. She wrinkled her nose. “I’m not sure about spinach.”

Mama nodded. “It’s okay to feel unsure. We can try a tiny taste.”

“Blue like… blueberries!” Fawn clapped her hooves. “Blueberries go pop in my mouth!”

“Purple like… grapes,” Mama added. “We can cut them to be safe.”

Fawn wrote tiny pictures in each circle: a strawberry, a carrot, a banana, a leaf, a berry, and a grape. She smiled. “This is my Rainbow Snack Plan.”

Just then, Fawn heard a soft tap-tap at the door. Her friend, a little field mouse named Mims, peeked in. Mims wore a tiny green scarf made from a leaf. “Hi, Fawn,” Mims squeaked. “What are you doing? It smells yummy in here.”

“I’m making a rainbow snack,” Fawn said. “Want to help?”

Mims shuffled his paws. “I only like crackers,” he whispered. “New foods make my whiskers shake.”

Fawn tilted her head. “That’s okay,” she said gently. “You can watch. You can try one tiny bite if you want. We can accept all feelings here.”

Mims smiled a small smile. “Thank you.”

Fawn and Mama set a little tray on the desk. They washed their hooves with a damp cloth. “We keep our study corner clean,” Mama said. “Clean is kind to our tummies and kind to the earth.”

They put strawberry tops into a compost jar. Plink, plink. “The scraps will help plants grow,” Mama said. “That’s environmental care.”

“I like helping plants,” Fawn said. “Thank you, scraps.” She patted the compost jar.

They filled the tray: red strawberries, orange carrot sticks, yellow banana slices, green baby spinach leaves, blue blueberries, and purple grape halves. The colors made a bright, happy rainbow. The strawberries smelled sweet. The carrots looked shiny. The blueberries felt cool and round.

Fawn touched her hoof to her heart. “Body, thank you for telling me you are hungry,” she said.

Mama nodded. “Listening to your body is smart.”

Fawn took a bite of strawberry. “Mmm, juicy! My mouth wakes up.”

She crunched a carrot. “Crunchy! My ears hear the crunch.”

She tried a banana slice. “Soft and sweet. My tongue smiles.”

She looked at the spinach leaf. Her ears drooped a little. “I feel a little worried about this one.”

“It’s okay,” Mama said. “You can try a tiny taste. If you don’t like it, you can try again another day.”

Fawn touched the leaf to her tongue. She blinked. “It tastes like the garden after rain,” she said. “I don’t love it yet. But I can try it again another time.”

Mims watched. His whiskers twitched. “Can I try a blueberry?” he asked.

“Yes,” Fawn said. “Blue is friendly.”

Mims held the blueberry. Pop! He smiled big. “It’s sweet! I feel brave.”

Fawn handed him a carrot stick. “You can nibble, or just smell.”

Mims sniffed. “It smells like crunchy sunshine.” He took a tiny nibble. Crunch. “I can try more later,” he said. “Thank you for accepting me.”

“You are welcome,” Fawn said. “All tastes can grow.”

When they were done, Fawn poured water into little cups. “Sip, sip.” The water was cool and smooth. “Water helps my body.”

They cleaned the desk together. They put peels into the compost jar. They wiped the crumbs. The study corner looked neat and bright again.

Fawn wrote in her notepad: “Rainbow snacks give me energy. Trying new foods is okay. I can listen to my body. I can care for the earth.”

Mama hugged Fawn. “You made a healthy plan. You shared. You cared.”

Fawn’s heart felt warm like sunshine. “Today I learned: Eating many colors helps me grow. It’s okay to try tiny bites. Friends have different likes, and that’s okay. We can keep our world clean and happy.”

Mims wrapped his leaf scarf tight. “I feel accepted,” he said softly. “And brave to try again tomorrow.”

Fawn looked at the rainbow tray. “Tomorrow, we can add red tomatoes,” she said. “And we will compost again!”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Little steps, every day.”

Fawn curled on the soft rug. The study corner was quiet and clean. Her tummy felt full and calm. Her heart felt kind.

The End.
