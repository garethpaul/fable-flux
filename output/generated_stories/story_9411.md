# Miko the Mouse Makes a Rainbow Parade

Miko was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a playful living room filled with squishy pillows, bouncy rugs, and a low table covered in crayons, paper, and stickers. Sunlight spilled across the floor in warm stripes, and the room smelled like fresh laundry and crayons.

One happy morning, Miko stretched his tiny paws. “Today, I will make art!” he squeaked. His whiskers twitched with excitement. He loved to draw and make things. Creative days made his heart feel big and warm.

Miko climbed onto the low table. He felt the cool paper under his paws and heard the soft scratch-scratch of crayons. “I will make a big rainbow,” he said. He picked up a red crayon. It felt smooth and waxy. Then orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple. “Swoosh, swoosh,” he whispered as colors danced on the page.

Just then, his friends peeked in. Buddy the puppy wagged his tail so fast it thumped the floor. Pippa the parakeet fluttered and chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” Tavi the turtle inched along with a gentle smile. Zara the zebra plush wobbled off the couch, her stripes soft and cuddly.

“What are you making?” Buddy asked, nose twitching. He sniffed the crayons. “They smell like sunshine!”

“A rainbow!” Miko beamed. “It will be a Rainbow Parade. We can all make it together.”

Pippa tilted her head. “I like dots. Can the parade have dot-dots?”

Buddy barked, “I like big paw prints!”

Tavi said slowly, “I like quiet patterns. Small squares.”

Zara nuzzled a pink marker. “I like swirls. Swirls feel like dancing.”

Miko’s ears perked. He wanted his own idea to stay special, but he also wanted his friends to feel happy. He took a deep breath. “Let’s do it together,” he said kindly. “We can all add something.”

They gathered close. The rug felt springy under their feet. The crayons made soft tap-tap sounds on the table.

Miko drew a big, curvy road for the parade. “This is our path,” he said.

Buddy bounced. “I want to make a big red bus for the parade!”

Miko paused. He had planned a quiet rainbow, not a big bus. His tail twitched. He felt a tiny pinch inside. He looked at Buddy’s hopeful eyes. Miko remembered his lesson: creative-expression means sharing and trying new ideas. He smiled. “Yes, Buddy. A big red bus can lead the parade!”

Buddy grinned and pressed his paw on a red stamp pad. Thump! A big paw-shaped bus appeared. He added wheels with round stickers. “Vroom!” he giggled.

Pippa fluttered low and dotted the sky with blue and gold dots. “Dot-dot-dot,” she sang. The page sparkled with tiny skies.

Tavi carefully placed little square pieces of colored paper along the road. “Plip. Plip. Plip,” he said softly. The road looked neat and cozy.

Zara swirled a purple marker in gentle circles. “Swish-swish.” The swirls looked like music in the air.

Miko added rainbow flags on tiny sticks. He pressed each flag into place with careful paws. “Flutter, flutter,” he whispered. The flags made him feel proud. He saw everyone’s ideas, shining together.

But soon, the paper felt full. There was one empty corner. “I want to draw a rainbow drum!” Miko said.

“I want a bone-shaped balloon!” Buddy barked.

Pippa chirped, “Dots in the corner!”

Tavi’s eyes blinked slowly. “Maybe a little square bench?”

Zara tapped her marker. “Or a swirl!”

Everyone spoke at once. The room buzzed like a happy beehive, but Miko felt the tiny pinch again. He didn’t want anyone to feel left out. He put a paw on the paper and said in a calm voice, “We can make space for all our ideas. Let’s compromise.”

“What is com-pro-mise?” asked Buddy.

Miko smiled. “Compromise means we each give a little, so we all fit. We can make one corner that holds all our ideas, but smaller.”

They nodded. Miko drew a small rainbow drum. Buddy made a tiny balloon bone tied to the drum. Pippa dotted the balloon string with little blue dots. Tavi added a small square bench next to the drum. Zara swirled a small purple swirl under the bench like a dancing shadow.

They all stepped back. The room was quiet for a moment. Sunlight made the colors glow. The paper felt warm from their busy paws. The Rainbow Parade looked amazing. It had paw prints, dots, squares, swirls, flags, a bus, a balloon, and a drum. It looked like all of them, together.

Miko’s heart felt light. “We did it,” he said softly.

Buddy wagged. “We did it together!”

Pippa chirped, “Dot-tastic!”

Tavi nodded, calm and proud. Zara swayed. “It looks like music.”

Miko rolled up the picture and tied it with yarn. He held it up like a banner. “Rainbow Parade, march!” They paraded around the living room. The rug bounced. The pillows whooshed. They sang, “Color, color, we can share! Try new things and show you care!”

After the parade, they sat in a cozy circle. Miko said, “Today I learned that creative-expression grows when we include friends. We can use our own ideas and listen to others. When we compromise, we make something beautiful.”

Everyone smiled and touched the picture. It felt smooth and strong.

Miko looked at his friends. “I am glad I used my own voice,” he said, “and I am glad I made room for yours.”

They all agreed. The living room felt even more playful, and everyone felt seen and happy.

The End.
