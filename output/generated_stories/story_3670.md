# Benny Beaver's Car Ride Adventure

Benny Beaver was SO excited! Today his family was driving to visit Grandma Beaver. He bounced up and down in his car seat, his fluffy brown tail wiggling with joy.

"Are we there yet? Are we there yet?" Benny chattered loudly, tapping his little paws on the window. *Tap, tap, tap!*

"Not yet, sweetie," said Mama Beaver gently. "We still have a long way to go."

But Benny couldn't sit still! He kicked his feet against the seat in front of him. *Thump, thump, thump!* He sang his favorite song at the top of his voice: "BUILDING DAMS IS SO MUCH FUN, LA LA LA!"

Papa Beaver's ears twitched. He was trying very hard to watch the road safely. "Benny, could you please sing a little quieter?" he asked kindly.

But Benny was having too much fun! He opened his snack bag and crunched his apple slices VERY loudly. *CRUNCH! CRUNCH! CRUNCH!* Then he dropped some pieces on the floor. "Oops!" he giggled.

Baby Sister Beaver started to cry. "Waaah! Waaah!" The loud noises were hurting her tiny ears.

Mama Beaver looked tired. She was trying to read Baby Sister a story to help her feel better, but she couldn't hear her own voice over all of Benny's noise.

Suddenly, Benny noticed something. Mama's shoulders looked droopy. Papa's hands were gripping the steering wheel very tightly. Baby Sister's face was red from crying.

"Oh no," Benny thought, his busy mind starting to work. "Everyone looks upset. I wonder why?"

He looked around the car with his bright, curious eyes. He saw the apple pieces on the floor. He remembered his loud singing and kicking. He thought about how he had been acting.

"Wait a minute," Benny said to himself. "Maybe... maybe I'm making everyone feel bad?"

Benny's little beaver brain was thinking hard. He remembered what Mama always told him: "Think about how others feel, Benny."

"Mama?" Benny said softly. "I think I was being too loud and messy. Is that why everyone is upset?"

Mama Beaver turned around with a gentle smile. "That's very smart thinking, Benny! Yes, when we're all together in our small car, we need to be considerate of each other."

"What does 'considerate' mean?" asked Benny.

"It means thinking about how our actions affect others," explained Papa Beaver. "Like how loud noises can make it hard for me to drive safely, or how they might scare Baby Sister."

Benny nodded thoughtfully. "I understand! I want everyone to feel happy and safe."

"What do you think you could do differently?" asked Mama, helping Benny think it through.

Benny's eyes lit up. "I could sing quietly, sit still so Papa can drive safely, and clean up my mess so our car stays nice!"

"Those are wonderful ideas!" said Papa proudly.

Benny carefully picked up his apple pieces. He sang his favorite song in a soft, gentle voice that sounded like a whisper. He sat still and looked out the window, pointing quietly at the pretty trees and clouds.

Baby Sister stopped crying and smiled. Mama relaxed and read a lovely story. Papa's shoulders felt less tense as he drove.

"Thank you for being so thoughtful, Benny," said Mama. "You made our car ride much more pleasant for everyone."

Benny felt warm and happy inside. Being considerate felt even better than being loud! He had learned that thinking about others' feelings made everyone happier - including himself.

The End.
