# Pebble the Penguin Says "Yes, Please" and "No, Thank You"

Pebble was a cheerful penguin who loved to waddle and sing. Pebble lived in a comfortable nursery with soft rugs, cozy pillows, and bright picture books. The room smelled like warm oatmeal and orange slices. Sunlight made gentle squares on the floor.

One morning, Pebble fluffed their little feathers and hummed, “Good morning!” Pebble’s friends were there too: Mia with her curly hair, Theo with his dinosaur socks, and kind Teacher Rosa who wore a necklace that jingled like tiny bells.

“Today,” said Teacher Rosa, smiling, “we will learn about listening to our bodies and using our strong, kind words.”

Pebble tilted their head. “Strong, kind words? Like please and thank you?”

“Yes,” said Teacher Rosa. “And also words that help us say what we need. That is called being assertive.”

Pebble felt a warm flutter in their chest. “Assertive,” Pebble repeated, tasting the word like a grape. “It means I can say what I need?”

“That’s right,” Teacher Rosa said.

Snack time began. The table was full of colors and smells—crisp green cucumbers, sweet strawberries, soft banana slices, crunchy whole-grain crackers, and a little bowl of cheesy puffs. Pebble’s tummy rumbled. “Rrrr-rrr,” it went, like a tiny purring cat.

Mia reached for the cheesy puffs. “Have some, Pebble!”

Pebble looked at the puffs, then at the cucumbers. Pebble liked cheesy puffs sometimes, but right now Pebble’s body wanted something fresh and crunchy.

Pebble took a breath. The crackers crackled. The strawberries smelled sweet. Pebble felt brave and calm.

“Thank you, Mia,” Pebble said softly, “but no, thank you to the puffs right now. I would like cucumbers, please.”

Mia blinked, then smiled. “Okay! Here you go.” She slid the green slices over. The cucumbers felt cool in Pebble’s flippers. Crunch! Crunch! Pebble’s beak tapped happily. The taste was fresh, like tiny raindrops.

Theo held out a big handful of crackers. “Take lots!” he said, his dinosaur socks wiggling.

Pebble remembered Teacher Rosa’s words. “My tummy says I want just two.” Pebble held up two little feathers. “Two, please.”

Theo nodded and counted, “One, two!” He gave Pebble two crackers. Pebble felt proud, like a small lantern glowing inside.

After snack, it was puzzle time. A big animal puzzle lay on the soft rug. Pebble wanted to put in the penguin piece. It was shiny and blue-black, just like Pebble’s feathers.

Mia reached for the penguin piece too. “I want this one!”

Pebble felt a tiny twist in their tummy. Pebble wanted to grab it fast, but then Pebble took a slow breath. In, out. The rug felt warm under Pebble’s feet. The jingle of Teacher Rosa’s necklace sounded nearby.

Pebble looked at Mia and used strong, kind words. “Mia, I really want the penguin piece. May I have a turn first? You can go next.”

Mia thought for a moment. “Okay,” she said. “You first, me next.”

“Thank you,” said Pebble. Pebble gently pressed the penguin into the blue puzzle sea. Click! It fit perfectly. Pebble’s heart felt light. Mia smiled and took the next piece.

Later, Teacher Rosa brought out the pretend kitchen set. The pots were shiny, and the wooden spoon made a happy tap-tap sound. “Let’s make a healthy soup,” she said. “What should we add?”

Theo shouted, “Cookies!”

Teacher Rosa laughed softly. “Cookies are a treat. Soup needs vegetables to help our bodies grow strong.”

Pebble raised a flipper. “I want carrots and peas, please.” Pebble loved how the pretend carrots looked bright and orange.

Mia added, “And corn!”

They stirred the pot. Pebble sniffed the pretend steam. “Mmm, veggie soup!” Pebble said. “This soup helps our bodies play and learn.”

When it was time to clean up, Theo wanted to keep playing. He held the spoon tight. Pebble felt a little worried. The room buzzed with the rustle of blocks and books. Pebble didn’t want to grab. Pebble wanted to be brave and kind.

Pebble stood tall and spoke clearly. “Theo, I am using that spoon next. When you are done, please pass it to me.”

Theo stopped and looked at Pebble. “Okay,” he said. He stirred one more time and handed it over. The spoon felt smooth and warm in Pebble’s flipper. Pebble smiled. “Thank you.”

Before nap time, Teacher Rosa gathered everyone on the rug. “What did we learn today?” she asked.

Pebble raised a flipper. “I learned to listen to my tummy and my feelings. I can say ‘yes, please’ and ‘no, thank you.’ I can use strong, kind words.”

Mia nodded. “We can take turns.”

Theo grinned. “Veggie soup makes us strong!”

Teacher Rosa’s necklace jingled as she clapped. “Wonderful! Being assertive means you speak up about your needs, in a calm and kind way. That helps you make healthy choices and be a good friend.”

Pebble felt cozy and proud. The room smelled like clean blankets. The sunlight made sleepy shapes on the wall. Pebble whispered, “I am brave. I can say what I need.”

Teacher Rosa tucked a soft blanket around Pebble. “Yes,” she said. “You can.”

Pebble closed their eyes, heart warm as cocoa. Pebble dreamed of crunchy cucumbers, shiny puzzle pieces, and words that were strong and kind.

The End.
