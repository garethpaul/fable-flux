# Joey's Big Boy Bathroom Adventure

Little Joey the kangaroo bounced excitedly around the bathroom. His fluffy tail wiggled with joy as he watched Mama Kangaroo turn on the warm water in the bathtub.

"Bath time, my little hopper!" Mama said with a gentle smile. The water sparkled and bubbled, making soft splashing sounds that made Joey's ears perk up.

"I can do it all by myself!" Joey announced proudly. He hopped toward the tub without waiting for Mama's instructions.

"Wait, Joey," Mama said softly. "Let's go step by step together. First, we need to check the water temperature."

But Joey was too excited. He jumped right toward the tub. "I'm a big joey now!" he called out.

"Stop!" Mama said firmly but kindly. "The water might be too hot for your paws."

Joey paused mid-hop. He looked at Mama's worried face and felt a little confused. "But I want to be independent," he said, his voice getting smaller.

Mama hopped over and gently touched Joey's shoulder. "Being independent means learning to follow important directions first. That keeps you safe and shows respect for others who care about you."

She dipped her paw into the warm water. "See? We test it first to make sure it won't hurt your soft skin."

Joey watched carefully. The water felt perfectly warm on Mama's paw. "Oh," he said quietly. "I didn't know that."

"Now, what comes next?" Mama asked encouragingly.

Joey thought hard, his little nose twitching. "Um... take off my clothes?"

"Exactly right!" Mama clapped her paws together. "You're such a smart joey."

Step by step, Joey followed Mama's gentle directions. He carefully removed his tiny shirt and placed it in the hamper. He listened as Mama explained how to hold the side of the tub while stepping in.

"One paw at a time," Mama guided. "That's it! Perfect!"

The warm water felt wonderful around Joey's legs. It was much better than if he had jumped in too quickly and gotten hurt.

"This is actually more fun," Joey giggled as bubbles tickled his tummy. "Following directions made bath time special!"

Mama handed him his favorite rubber duck. "When we follow directions, we stay safe and learn new things. Plus, it shows we respect the people who love us."

Joey squeezed his duck and watched it float. "Like when you tell me directions, it's because you love me?"

"Exactly, my sweet joey. Directions help keep you happy and healthy."

Joey splashed gently in the safe, warm water. His ears flopped happily as he played with his toys. "Mama, will you teach me more directions tomorrow?"

"Of course!" Mama laughed, her heart full of pride. "There are so many wonderful things to learn when we listen carefully."

As Joey washed behind his ears just like Mama showed him, he felt proud and grown-up in a new way. Following directions didn't make him less independent – it made him smarter and safer.

"I love learning the right way to do things," Joey said, making his duck swim in circles.

Mama smiled warmly. "And I love watching you grow into such a thoughtful, careful joey."

When bath time ended, Joey followed every direction perfectly. He waited for Mama to check that the water was draining safely. He stepped out carefully, one paw at a time. He even helped put his toys away in their special basket.

"Tomorrow, can we have another learning adventure?" Joey asked as Mama wrapped him in his soft, fluffy towel.

"Every day is a learning adventure when you follow directions with such a happy heart," Mama said, giving him a warm kangaroo hug.

The End.
