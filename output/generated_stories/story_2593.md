# Buckle Up, Benny!

Benny was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, brave eyes. He sat in the back seat of the family car with his big sister, Bella. The seats smelled like fresh oranges from their snack, and the sun made warm, golden squares on the soft seats. The car hummed gently, “mmmm,” like a sleepy bee.

Mama Rabbit got in and smiled. “Good morning, my little bunnies,” she said.

“Good morning!” Benny chirped. He wiggled in his seat. He had a big goal today. “Mama, I want to be a Safety Star,” he said. “I want to do every safe thing in the car.”

“That is a wonderful goal,” Mama said. “Safety first.”

Benny reached for his seat belt. Click! It made a happy sound. He pulled the belt snug across his chest and tummy.

“Buckle, buckle, safe and snug. Like a warm and cozy hug!” he sang.

Bella laughed. “I like your song, Benny.”

Benny smiled. “I made a rhyme! Snug and hug.” He liked how the words felt in his mouth. He liked the music of them.

As the car began to roll, the tires whispered on the road. Swish. Swish. Trees outside waved hello. Benny looked at the window and said, “I want to see better.” He put his paw on the window button.

Mama’s ears perked up. “Pause, Benny. Safety check,” she said gently. “Little paws stay away from buttons while the car is moving. Eyes forward. Body back. Seat belt on.”

Benny pulled his paw back. “Yes, Mama,” he said. He felt a tiny twist in his tummy. He wanted to do it right. He took a big, slow breath. In. Out.

He tried his rhyme again. “Buckle, buckle, safe and snug. Little paws don’t push or tug.”

Bella clapped quietly. “Another rhyme!”

Benny felt proud and calm.

Soon, the car stopped at a light. A red light glowed like a shiny cherry. “Stop means stop,” Mama said.

Benny whispered, “Red means stop. Green means go. Yellow is slow, slow, slow.”

“That is good road language,” Mama said. “Words help us be safe.”

Benny looked out the window. On the sidewalk, a small fox kit and his dad stood by the crosswalk. The little fox looked worried. He held a loose backpack, and it dragged on the ground.

Benny pressed his paw to the window, but he did not push the button. “Mama, can we help? The bag is open.”

The light turned red again. Mama pulled the car near the curb and parked. “We can help because we are stopped,” she said. “But first—safety plan.”

Benny nodded. He set a little goal in his mind. Goal: Help kindly. Help safely. Use good words.

Mama looked at him. “What do we do before we get out?”

“Car off. Doors open on the safe side. Hands hold,” Benny said.

“Exactly,” Mama smiled. She turned off the car. Click. The humming stopped. The world felt quiet, with birds chirping sweet notes in the air. The seat leather felt cool under Benny’s paws as he unbuckled. Click. He slid out on the sidewalk side with Mama’s paw in his.

“Hello,” Benny said to the fox kit. “I am Benny. May I help you?”

“Yes, please,” the fox kit said softly. “My books fell.”

Benny crouched down. He held the backpack open. The pages felt smooth and smelled like crayons and sunshine. “One book, two books,” he counted. He smiled at a book with big letters. “A is for acorn,” he read. “I love letters. They tell us things. Letters keep us safe.”

The fox dad nodded. “They do. Thank you for helping.”

Benny used careful words. “It is safe to zip. Zip, zip.” He moved the zipper slowly. Zzzzip! The bag was closed.

Before they crossed, Mama pointed to the big white walking person on the light. “What does that sign say?”

Benny stood tall. “It says walk.” He felt proud of the word. He liked the shape of it in his mouth. Walk. “We will hold hands. We will look left, look right, look left again.”

They all did. Left. Right. Left. The air smelled like fresh rain. A breeze tickled Benny’s whiskers.

“Ready,” Mama said. They stepped onto the crosswalk, paws and feet going tap-tap on the painted lines. The fox kit smiled. “Thank you for helping,” he said.

“You are welcome,” Benny replied. His chest felt warm. Helping felt good.

Back in the car, Benny climbed into his seat. He pulled the belt across his chest and clicked it. Click! “Buckle, buckle, safe and snug. Like a warm and cozy hug,” he sang again.

Mama looked in the mirror. “Benny, you set a goal. You used good words. You were safe and you helped others. I am proud.”

Benny’s ears wiggled. “I am a Safety Star,” he said.

Bella raised her paw for a gentle high-five. “You are a Safety Star and a Word Star!”

Benny grinned. “Safety first. Words help. Helping is kind.” He said the words slowly, so they could sit in his heart.

The car hummed again. Mmmm. The road whispered. Swish. Swish. Benny rested back, snug and happy. He looked at the red, yellow, and green lights as they passed. He could almost hear the lights say their road words: Stop. Slow. Go.

Benny closed his eyes and whispered, “I can be safe every day. I can help in kind ways. I can use strong, kind words.”

Mama’s voice was warm. “Yes, you can.”

The End.
