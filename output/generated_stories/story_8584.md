# Leo's Colorful Day

Leo the lion cub loved making art more than anything else in the whole world. His fluffy golden fur always had tiny specks of paint on it, and his bright green eyes sparkled whenever he saw crayons, paintbrushes, or colorful paper.

Today, Leo bounced into the sunny art room at his school. The room smelled like fresh crayons and sweet glue. Colorful paintings hung on the walls like rainbows, and shelves were filled with every art supply a little cub could dream of.

"Good morning, Leo!" called Miss Rabbit, his kind teacher. "We have a special project today. Everyone will make a friendship poster!"

Leo's tail swished with excitement. He spotted the most beautiful box of 64 crayons sitting on the center table. The box was shiny and new, with every color he could imagine - sunset orange, ocean blue, grass green, and even sparkly silver!

"Mine!" Leo roared softly, grabbing the big crayon box and hugging it tight to his fuzzy chest.

Soon, his friends arrived. Bella the bunny hopped over with her paper. "Leo, may I borrow the red crayon? I want to draw hearts on my poster."

"No," Leo said, turning away. "I'm using ALL the crayons."

Next came Toby the turtle, moving slowly with his paper. "Could I please have the green crayon, Leo? I want to draw my favorite leaves."

"No way!" Leo growled, clutching the box tighter. "These are MINE today!"

Soon, all of Leo's friends were sitting sadly with plain white papers. They only had old, broken crayons that barely made marks. Leo looked around the quiet room. Nobody was laughing or chatting like usual. His friends looked disappointed, and their papers looked empty and sad.

Leo's tummy felt funny - not hungry funny, but worried funny. He looked at his own paper. Even with ALL the crayons, his poster didn't look very happy. It felt lonely, just like his friends looked.

Miss Rabbit sat down beside Leo. "How do you feel when you can't share your favorite things?" she asked gently.

Leo thought about when his big brother wouldn't share his favorite ball. "Sad," Leo whispered. "And left out."

"How do you think your friends feel right now?" Miss Rabbit asked.

Leo looked around again. Bella's ears drooped. Toby had put his head in his shell. "They feel sad too," Leo realized. "Just like I did."

Leo's brave little heart knew what he needed to do. He took a deep breath and stood up tall.

"Friends!" Leo called out in his strongest, kindest voice. "I have an idea! What if we ALL share the crayons? We can take turns and make the most beautiful posters together!"

Bella's ears perked up. Toby poked his head out of his shell. "Really?" they asked.

"Really!" Leo smiled, opening the crayon box wide. "Bella, here's red for your hearts. Toby, here's green for your leaves. Everyone pick your favorite colors!"

Soon the art room was filled with happy chatter and giggles. Friends were trading crayons, sharing ideas, and helping each other. Leo discovered that watching his friends create beautiful art made him feel even happier than keeping all the crayons for himself.

When they finished, their friendship posters were the most colorful, joyful artwork the room had ever seen.

"Sharing makes everything more fun!" Leo announced proudly.

Miss Rabbit smiled. "And sharing makes you a wonderful leader and friend, Leo."

That day, Leo learned that the best treasures are the ones you share with friends.

The End.
