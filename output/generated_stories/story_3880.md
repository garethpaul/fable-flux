# Miri the Kind Mouse’s Little World Tour

In a bright guest room with a sunny window, a kind mouse named Miri woke up to the sound of distant birds. “Chirp, chirp,” sang the birds outside. Miri’s tiny whiskers twitched. She loved mornings. The guest room smelled like clean sheets and warm sunlight. Soft rugs tickled her paws. A big map on the wall showed the whole wide world.

Miri smiled at the map. “Good morning, world,” she squeaked. “Today, I will learn about you.”

On the desk sat a round globe, smooth and cool to the touch. Miri climbed up a stack of books—step, step, step—and hugged the globe. “So many places,” she whispered. The globe spun slowly with a soft whoosh. Blue for water. Green and brown for land. White swirls for clouds.

Beside the globe was a small box labeled “Guest Treasures.” Miri lifted the lid. Inside were tiny things from visitors: a bright red fan, a shiny seashell, a little cloth doll with a colorful dress, and a postcard with a picture of tall trees and snow. Someone had written, “Hello from far away!”

Miri’s heart felt warm. “These are gifts from friends around the world,” she said. “I will have a tiny world party in the guest room!”

She called her friends. “Come, friends! Let’s learn about the world together!”

Pip the squirrel scampered in, tail fluffy. “I hear fun!” he chattered.
Lulu the ladybug fluttered down from a plant. “I see beautiful colors,” she buzzed.
Nina the hedgehog waddled in slowly. “I brought my listening ears,” she said softly.

Miri placed the treasures in a circle on the rug. “Each treasure comes from a different place,” she said. “We will listen. We will taste. We will be kind and take care of our world.”

Pip pointed to the red fan. “What’s that?”
Miri opened it with a soft fwish. “It is a fan from a place that can be very warm. People wave it to feel cool.” She waved it gently. A breeze brushed their faces. “Ahh,” said Nina. “That feels nice.”

Lulu touched the seashell. It felt smooth and salty. Miri held it to her ear. “Hear the ocean?” she asked.
They listened. Whoooosh. The sound was like waves. “The ocean is big,” Miri said. “We must keep it clean. No litter in the water.”
Pip nodded. “No litter,” he repeated.

Next, Miri picked up the little cloth doll. The doll’s dress had bright patterns. “This doll reminds me that people wear different clothes,” she said. “Different is beautiful.” Lulu twirled. “I like colors!” she sang.

Nina looked at the postcard of snow. The picture felt cool just to see it. “Brr,” she giggled. “Some places are cold, and some are hot. All places are special.”
Miri smiled. “Yes. Our world has many homes. We can care for them all.”

Then Miri found a tiny wooden spoon in the box. “A spoon,” she said. “Let’s make a little snack from around the world.” She brought out a small bowl of rice, a slice of mango, and a few crunchy nuts. The guest room smelled sweet and toasty.

“We will share,” Miri said. “We will taste tiny, tiny bites. And we will not waste food.” She placed the spoon in the bowl. “Teamwork!”

Pip held the bowl. Lulu guided with gentle taps. Nina scooped carefully. Miri set a soft napkin for spills. They took turns. Pip tasted rice. “Soft,” he said. Lulu tasted mango. “Sweet,” she buzzed. Nina crunched a nut. “Crunchy!” Miri had a small bite of each. “So many flavors,” she said. “Our world has many tastes.”

After snacks, Miri looked around. “We used a napkin, bowls, and spoons,” she said. “Now we clean up to help our planet.” She rinsed the bowl with a cup of water. “We use just what we need,” she said. “Save water for fish and flowers.” Pip stacked the bowls. Lulu dried with her tiny wings fluttering like a fan. Nina folded the napkin neatly.

Miri pointed to the recycling bin. “Paper here,” she said. “Glass here.” They sorted together. Clink. Rustle. “Teamwork makes our room and our world happy,” Miri said.

The sun moved across the floor. Golden light made warm squares on the rug. Miri touched the globe again. Her paw rested on a faraway place. “I may be small,” she said, “but I can learn about big places. I can be kind to people near and far.”

Nina asked, “How can we be kind to people we don’t know?”
Miri thought. “We can listen. We can share. We can say hello. We can care for the Earth we all share.” She held up the seashell. “When we keep the ocean clean, we help children who live by the sea.” She touched the fan. “When we save energy, we help friends in warm places.” She patted the doll. “When we celebrate different clothes and songs, we make everyone feel welcome.”

Pip smiled. “I can pick up litter at the park.”
Lulu said, “I can plant a flower for bees.”
Nina said, “I can say ‘Welcome’ to new friends.”

Miri’s whiskers wiggled with joy. “And I can keep learning, every day, right here in our guest room. The world is big, but our hearts can be big too.”

They all stood around the globe. They took a deep breath. The room smelled like sunshine and a tiny bit like mango. Outside, the birds sang again. Chirp, chirp.

“Hello, world,” said Miri. “We are your helpers.”

The End.
