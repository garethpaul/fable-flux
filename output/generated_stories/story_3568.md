# Miko's Amazing Ball Game

In a cozy study corner filled with colorful books and soft cushions, lived a little monkey named Miko. His fur was golden brown and fluffy, and his eyes sparkled with curiosity. Miko loved to play, but today he had a big problem.

"Oh no!" Miko said, looking sadly at his favorite red ball. It had a big tear in it and wouldn't bounce anymore. "My ball is broken! How can I play my bouncing game now?"

Miko sat on his favorite blue cushion and felt very sad. He loved to bounce his ball high in the air and catch it. It was his special sport that made him feel strong and happy.

"I could ask Mama for a new ball," Miko thought. "But she's busy working. I want to solve this problem all by myself!"

Miko looked around his study corner with his bright, curious eyes. He saw books with shiny covers, soft pillows in many colors, and his toy box overflowing with treasures.

"Wait!" Miko said, his tail wiggling with excitement. "Maybe I can make a new game! I don't need to wait for someone else to help me."

First, Miko tried rolling a book like a ball. "Thump, thump, thump," went the book across the soft carpet. But it moved too slowly and wasn't bouncy at all.

"That's not quite right," Miko giggled. "But it gave me an idea!"

Next, Miko gathered three soft pillows. He stacked them up high, making a colorful tower. "I can throw my bean bags at the pillow tower!" he said happily.

Miko found his small bean bags - one was purple, one was green, and one was yellow. They felt smooth and squishy in his little hands.

"Here I go!" Miko said. He threw the purple bean bag gently at his pillow tower. "Whoosh!" Down tumbled the pillows with a soft "poof!"

"This is fun!" Miko clapped his hands. "But I want to make it even more exciting!"

Miko's creative mind started spinning with new ideas. He arranged the pillows in different shapes - sometimes in a line, sometimes in a circle. He tried throwing the bean bags from different spots in his study corner.

"I know!" Miko said, jumping up and down. "I can make targets with different points!"

Miko drew colorful circles on pieces of paper with his crayons. He made a big red circle worth one point, a medium blue circle worth two points, and a tiny yellow circle worth three points.

"Now I have my very own sport!" Miko said proudly. "I call it 'Pillow Target Toss!'"

Miko spent the whole afternoon playing his new game. Sometimes he threw the bean bags underhand, sometimes overhand. He counted his points out loud: "One, two, three!"

When Mama came to check on him, she found Miko beaming with pride.

"Miko, what a wonderful game you created!" Mama said. "You used your imagination to solve your problem all by yourself!"

"Yes!" Miko said, his chest puffed out proudly. "When my ball broke, I felt sad. But then I used my creativity to make something new and fun! I didn't need to wait for help - I could do it myself!"

Miko learned that day that when something doesn't work the way we want, we can use our imagination to find new solutions. Sometimes the best games are the ones we create ourselves!

The End.
