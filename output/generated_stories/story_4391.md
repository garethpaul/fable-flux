# Miko's Home Office Helper Plan

Miko was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and quick fingers. He lived in a cozy house with a sunny home office. The room smelled like fresh paper and lemon cleaner. The big desk felt smooth, and the computer hummed a soft, busy song. Click, click, click went the keyboard.

Mama sat at the desk in a rolling chair. “I have a big work call today,” Mama said kindly. “I need quiet, okay?”

Miko nodded. “I can be quiet,” he said. He wanted to help Mama. He wanted to be proud. But his tummy felt fluttery. There were so many things he wanted to do, like build a block tower, draw a rocket, and peel a banana by himself. He looked at the sticky note on the wall that said, “Meeting at 2:00.”

The clock hands moved slowly. Miko rocked on his toes. He looked at the floor. He saw crayons, paper, and a small bell on the shelf. The bell was shiny and sounded like tiny snow when it rang.

Ding!

Mama gave Miko a gentle look. “Sweetie, quiet voice,” she whispered. “Can you wait a little?”

Miko’s cheeks felt warm. He wanted help opening the new box of crayons, but Mama had to work. He took a deep breath in—slow, like smelling a flower—and blew out—soft, like blowing a wish. He remembered a thing Mama had once said: “Use your words. Ask for what you need. Make a plan.”

“I can try,” Miko said to himself. He sat on the fuzzy rug and thought. Thinking felt like putting puzzle pieces together. Piece one: I need help with the crayon box. Piece two: I need a snack. Piece three: I want to build my tower. Piece four: Mama needs quiet.

Miko stood up tall. “I can make a plan,” he said out loud. He found a small notepad on the shelf. The paper felt smooth and cool under his fingers. He picked a blue crayon and carefully wrote little pictures. He drew a clock with a big hand and a little hand. He drew a banana. He drew a tower. He drew himself holding up one finger, then two fingers. He smiled. “This is my plan.”

He tapped the rolling chair softly. “Mama?” he whispered.

Mama turned her chair halfway and smiled. “Yes, Miko?”

Miko held up the notepad. “I have needs,” he said bravely. “I want to open crayons, eat a banana, and build my tower. I can do some by myself. Can we make a quiet signal? And can we make a plan for help?”

Mama’s eyes shone. “I love how you asked,” she said. “That’s self-advocacy, Miko. You told me what you need. Let’s make a plan together.”

Miko’s chest felt warm and proud. “Plan!” he chirped.

Mama pointed to the sticky note. “My meeting is in ten minutes. How about this: before the call, I open the crayons and peel the banana halfway so you can finish. During the call, if you need me and it’s not an emergency, you put your blue note on my desk. That tells me to help when I pause. If it’s an emergency, you touch my elbow. After my call, we build the tower together.”

Miko nodded fast. “Yes! That is a good plan.”

They worked together for one minute. Mama opened the crayon box with a soft rip. She peeled the banana halfway, and it smelled sweet and sunny. Miko took a tiny lick. “Mmm!”

“Thank you for telling me what you need,” Mama said.

“I used my words,” Miko said proudly.

The call started. Mama spoke in a calm voice. “Hello, team.” The keyboard clicked and the mouse went squeak-squeak. Miko sat on the rug and colored. He made a big blue sky and a yellow sun with smiley rays. He built a block tower too, placing each block slowly. Tap. Tap. Tap. The tower grew taller, straight and strong.

After a while, Miko wanted tape for his picture. He paused. He looked at the plan. He drew a small blue square on his note and walked quietly to the desk. He set the note on the corner like a feather.

Mama nodded and held up one finger: wait a moment. Miko took a deep breath and hugged his knees. He counted, “One banana, two banana, three banana.” Soon, Mama put her hand over the microphone. She turned to Miko and whispered, “Tape in the top drawer.”

Miko smiled. “Thank you.” He opened the drawer slowly. The tape felt cool and sticky. He pulled out a little piece—zip!—and stuck his picture to the wall next to the clock. It looked bright and proud, just like he felt.

When the call ended, Mama spun gently in her chair. “You did it, Miko! You followed the plan. You waited. You asked for help in a kind way.”

Miko clapped his hands. “I was independent!” he said. “I did many things by myself.”

Mama hugged him. Her sweater smelled like laundry and lemons. “Yes,” she said. “You planned your steps. You used your words. You were brave and patient.”

They built the tallest tower together. Block by block, it reached up, almost to the desk. Miko placed the last block. Tap! The tower wobbled, then stood tall.

Miko grinned. “I can make plans. I can ask for what I need. I can do things on my own.”

Mama nodded. “That is the lesson, little monkey. When you tell your needs and make a plan, you grow strong inside.”

Miko patted his chest. It felt steady and proud. The home office was quiet and happy. The keyboard rested. The computer hummed like a friendly bee.

“Next time,” Miko said, “I will use my words again.”

“That’s my courageous monkey,” Mama said.

The End.
