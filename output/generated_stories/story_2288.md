# Momo's Big Truth

Momo the monkey swung into Grandma's guest room. The soft blue curtains danced in the breeze. Sunlight made pretty patterns on the wooden floor.

"What a nice place to play!" Momo said, bouncing on the fluffy bed.

On the nightstand sat Grandma's favorite plant. It had shiny green leaves and tiny white flowers. The sweet smell filled the room.

Momo loved to swing and jump. He grabbed the curtain rod and swung across the room. "Wheee!" he called out happily.

But then - CRASH! Momo's long tail knocked over the plant pot. Dirt scattered everywhere. The pretty flowers lay broken on the floor.

"Oh no!" Momo whispered. His tummy felt funny and tight. "What will Grandma say?"

Momo heard footsteps coming up the stairs. His heart beat fast like a drum. He quickly pushed the broken pieces under the bed with his foot.

"Momo, dear, how are you doing?" Grandma called from the hallway.

"Fine, Grandma!" Momo said, but his voice sounded squeaky. He sat on the bed and tried to look normal.

Grandma walked into the room. She had kind eyes and a warm smile. "Have you been having fun in here?"

Momo nodded quickly. But inside, he felt heavy like a rock. The broken plant was still under the bed. The dirt was still on the floor.

"Grandma," Momo said slowly. "I need to tell you something."

Grandma sat down on the bed next to him. "What is it, sweetie?"

Momo took a deep breath. "I was swinging, and I accidentally broke your plant. I'm really sorry. I tried to hide it because I was scared."

Grandma looked under the bed and saw the broken pot. She picked up the little plant gently.

"Oh, Momo," she said softly. "Thank you for telling me the truth. That took courage."

"Are you mad at me?" Momo asked. His eyes felt watery.

"I'm not mad," Grandma said, hugging him tight. "Accidents happen. But I'm proud of you for being honest. That's what brave monkeys do."

Together, they cleaned up the dirt. Grandma showed Momo how to put the plant in a new pot.

"Plants are living things," Grandma explained as they worked. "They need our care and love. When we take care of nature, nature takes care of us."

Momo helped pat down the fresh soil. "The plant looks happy again," he said.

"It does!" Grandma agreed. "And you know what? This plant will grow even stronger now. Just like you grew stronger by telling the truth."

Momo smiled big. His tummy didn't feel funny anymore. "Telling the truth feels much better than hiding," he said.

"That's right," Grandma said. "Honest monkeys are the happiest monkeys."

They watered the plant together. The water sparkled like tiny diamonds in the sunlight. The room smelled fresh and clean again.

"Can I help you take care of all your plants?" Momo asked.

"I would love that," Grandma said. "We'll be the best plant care team ever!"

Momo hugged Grandma tight. He felt proud and happy inside. Telling the truth had been scary, but it made everything better.

From that day on, Momo always remembered: being honest and caring for living things made him feel like the bravest monkey in the whole world.

The End.
