# Benny Beaver’s Bedtime Helpers

In a cozy bedroom with soft lamplight, Benny Beaver wiggled his flat tail on his quilt. The blanket felt warm and fuzzy on his paws. His pillow smelled like clean pine. Outside the window, a gentle rain tapped, tap-tap-tap, like tiny drums.

“I am a busy beaver,” Benny said proudly. “I like to build. I like to help.”

On his shelf, Benny kept a little yellow toolbox for kids. Inside were safe, toy tools: a wooden hammer, soft screws, and a bright flashlight. His mama called from the hallway, “Bedtime in ten minutes, Benny.”

“Okay, Mama!” Benny called back. He thought, I still have time to do one helpful thing before sleep.

Benny looked around his cozy bedroom. His books leaned in a wobbly stack. His stuffed friends—Moose, Bunny, and Duck—were scattered like fallen leaves. And his nightlight, a star that glowed softly, blinked twice and went dim.

“Oh no,” Benny whispered. “My friends need a helper. My room is a little messy, and my star is sleepy.” His heart thumped, thump-thump, not scared, but excited.

He picked up Moose first. “Moose, you sleep on the pillow next to me,” he said. He patted Moose’s soft fur. “You like the pine smell too.” Moose looked cozy and calm.

Benny lifted Bunny and Duck. “You can sit on the bed shelf, safe and snug,” he said, setting them carefully. Bunny’s ears flopped. Duck’s fabric wings felt smooth in Benny’s paw. It made him smile.

He stacked his books by size. Big books on the bottom. Tiny books on top. He wanted to hurry, but the stack wobbled. The wobble made Benny’s nose wrinkle. He felt a little frustrated.

“I can do it with patience,” he told himself. He took a slow breath. In. Out. He tried again, moving slowly, one book at a time. The stack stood straight. “Yes!” Benny cheered softly. “Patience helps.”

Rain whispered outside. Tap-tap-tap. The star nightlight blinked again. Benny clicked on his toy flashlight. A warm beam washed the room in gold. “I will be brave and check the plug,” he said. His tummy fluttered. The plug was near the floor, and the room was a little shadowy.

“I can use courage and be safe,” Benny said. He remembered Mama’s rules. “Ask for help with cords.” Benny called, “Mama?”

Mama padded in, her slippers whispering on the rug. “I’m here,” she said gently. “What do you need, little helper?”

“My star light is sleepy,” Benny said. “Can we check it together?”

“That’s very safe of you,” Mama said, smiling. She bent down and checked the plug. “It was a tiny bit loose. I’ll fix it.” She clicked it in. The star glowed steady and warm. The golden light made the room feel like a hug.

“Thank you, Mama,” Benny said. He felt brave and happy.

“You did the right thing,” Mama said. “You asked for help. That shows courage and safety.”

Benny nodded. He looked around. The floor was clear. The books were neat. Moose, Bunny, and Duck looked comfy. “Our bedroom looks ready,” he said. “But I want to do more. I want to help our building too—our home family.”

Mama tilted her head. “What are you thinking?”

“I can make a bedtime helper plan,” Benny said. He held up his toy hammer and tapped it softly on the air. “Tap, tap. I can lead a quiet clean-up for everyone. A little community service. Small helpers make big happy.”

Mama’s eyes twinkled. “That is a wonderful idea, Benny. What is your plan?”

Benny puffed his cheeks, then let the air out slowly, thinking. “Step one: Put toys in the basket. Step two: Place books on the shelf, big to small. Step three: Check lights with a grown-up. Step four: Soft goodnights to our things.” He grinned. “We can do it every night.”

Mama clapped softly. “Your plan is safe, kind, and patient. I’ll share it with the family.”

Benny felt proud—warm and glowy inside, like the star. He yawned a little beaver yawn. “I can try it right now.”

They worked together. Benny placed a stray puzzle piece into its box. He brushed crumbs into a tiny bin. He wiped a small splash with a soft cloth. When he got stuck on a sticky sticker, his paws felt fussy. “Grrr… sticky!” he said, scrunching his nose.

“Pause and breathe,” Mama reminded.

Benny took a slow breath. In. Out. He tried again with gentle fingers. The sticker peeled off. “Patience wins,” he said, giggling.

At last, everything was tidy. The room smelled like soap and pine. The rain turned into soft hush-hush mist. Benny climbed into bed. He tucked Moose under his chin. Bunny and Duck watched from the shelf.

“Mama,” he whispered, “today I helped my room. Tomorrow I can help more. Maybe I can make a ‘helping list’ for the hallway. We can all use it.”

“That’s community service,” Mama said. “Helping our home and our neighbors. You showed courage to ask for help, safety by checking the light with me, and patience when things were tricky.”

Benny smiled, sleepy and proud. “I am a busy beaver,” he murmured. “I build helpful habits.”

Mama kissed his forehead. The star light glowed warm. The room felt peaceful and safe.

“Goodnight, little helper,” Mama said.

“Goodnight,” Benny whispered. “Tomorrow we help together.”

The End.
