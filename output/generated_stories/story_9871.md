# Penny Panda's Honest Choice

In a busy basement workshop filled with the sounds of *tap-tap-tapping* and *whirr-whirr-whirring*, a patient panda named Penny worked quietly at her wooden table. The basement smelled like fresh wood shavings and glue, and colorful toy pieces were scattered everywhere.

Penny was helping Mr. Bear fix broken toys for the town's children. She loved making things work again with her gentle paws and careful attention.

"Penny," called Mr. Bear from across the noisy basement, "I need to run upstairs for more paint. Please keep sorting these puzzle pieces while I'm gone."

"Of course, Mr. Bear!" Penny nodded, her black ears bobbing. She loved puzzles and was very good at finding where each piece belonged.

As Penny worked, she heard a *clink-clink* sound. A shiny quarter had fallen from Mr. Bear's pocket and rolled right to her feet. The coin sparkled under the bright basement lights.

Penny picked up the quarter and looked around. The basement was so busy and loud that nobody had noticed it fall. She could easily put it in her pocket, and no one would ever know.

"Ooh, shiny!" squeaked a little mouse who lived in the basement corner. "Finders keepers, right Penny?"

Penny held the quarter in her paw, feeling its smooth, cool surface. For a moment, she thought about keeping it. She had been wanting to buy a special bamboo snack from the store.

But then Penny's tummy felt funny - not hungry funny, but worried funny. She remembered what her mama had taught her: "Always do what's right, even when no one is watching."

"No, little mouse," Penny said softly. "This doesn't belong to me. It belongs to Mr. Bear."

The mouse tilted his tiny head. "But he doesn't even know he dropped it!"

Penny sat down and thought carefully. "Just because someone doesn't know something is missing doesn't mean it's okay to keep it. That wouldn't be honest."

When Mr. Bear came back down the creaky basement stairs carrying cans of bright paint, Penny walked over to him.

"Mr. Bear," she said, holding out the quarter, "this fell from your pocket while you were gone."

Mr. Bear's eyes grew wide with surprise. "Oh my! Thank you, Penny. I didn't even realize I had dropped it." He smiled warmly. "You know, this quarter was for buying ice cream for all the children when we finish fixing their toys."

Penny's heart felt light and happy, like a balloon floating up to the ceiling. She had made the right choice!

"You are very honest, Penny," Mr. Bear continued. "Because you did the right thing, would you like to help me choose the ice cream flavors for everyone?"

"Yes, please!" Penny clapped her paws together excitedly.

The little mouse poked his head out from his corner. "Penny, you were right. Being honest feels much better than keeping things that aren't ours."

As they worked together in the busy basement, Penny felt proud and happy inside. She learned that being honest and fair, even when it's hard, always feels better than doing something wrong.

The basement filled with the sweet sounds of laughter and friendship, and Penny knew she had made the very best choice of all.

The End.
