# Robo's Big Heart

In a warm, cozy nursery with pale yellow walls, a shiny robot dog named Robo stood by the window. His silver body gleamed in the morning sunlight. His LED eyes glowed bright blue with kindness.

"Beep beep! Good morning!" Robo said cheerfully to himself. His tail wagged with a gentle whirring sound.

Robo loved his nursery home. Soft teddy bears sat on shelves. A mobile with dancing stars hung from the ceiling. Everything was perfect and peaceful.

But today, Robo heard something different. From the corner came a small, sad sound.

"Sniff, sniff."

Robo's ears perked up. He walked across the soft carpet, his metal paws making tiny clicking sounds. Behind a pile of colorful blocks sat a little wind-up mouse toy. The mouse's red paint was chipped. One of his tiny wheels was wobbly.

"Oh my!" said Robo. "Are you okay, little friend?"

The mouse looked up with watery button eyes. "I'm broken," he squeaked. "My wheel doesn't work right. I can't play with the other toys. They roll and run so fast, but I just wobble."

Robo's heart circuits filled with warmth. He could see how sad the little mouse felt. Being left out was no fun at all.

"Don't worry," Robo said gently. "I want to help you."

"But you're big and strong," said the mouse. "I'm just a broken old toy."

Robo sat down carefully next to his new friend. "Everyone deserves kindness," he said. "Big or small, new or old, perfect or wobbly. We all matter."

Robo thought hard. His computer brain whirred and clicked. Then his eyes lit up even brighter.

"I have an idea!" Robo barked happily.

From his storage compartment, Robo pulled out a tiny screwdriver. Very carefully, he tightened the mouse's loose wheel. Then he found a small piece of soft foam and cushioned the wobbly part.

"There!" said Robo. "Try moving now."

The little mouse took a careful step. Then another. His wheel rolled smoothly! He did a happy little spin.

"I can move again!" squeaked the mouse joyfully. "Thank you, Robo!"

But Robo wasn't finished helping. He called to all the toys in the nursery.

"Everyone! Come meet our friend!" Robo announced.

A bouncy ball rolled over. A stuffed elephant trumpeted hello. A toy train chugged closer. Soon, all the toys gathered around.

"This is Mouse," said Robo proudly. "He's joining our play group."

At first, some toys weren't sure. Mouse looked different with his chipped paint and careful movements.

"He might slow us down," whispered the bouncy ball.

Robo's heart felt heavy. He knew this was important. "Friends," he said in his kindest voice, "being different makes us special. Mouse has wonderful ideas and a gentle heart. When we include everyone, we all have more fun."

The stuffed elephant nodded thoughtfully. "Robo's right," she said. "Let's all play together!"

They played hide-and-seek among the soft blankets. Mouse found the best hiding spots because he moved quietly. They built block towers together. Mouse's careful movements helped make the tallest tower ever!

As the sun set through the nursery window, all the toys sat in a happy circle.

"Thank you, Robo," said Mouse, his button eyes shining. "You showed me that I belong here too."

Robo's LED heart glowed warm and bright. "When we care about others and include everyone, we make the whole world better," he said.

All the toys agreed. From that day on, the nursery was filled with even more laughter, kindness, and friendship.

And Robo learned that the biggest hearts come from showing compassion to others.

The End.
