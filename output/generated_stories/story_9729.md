# Benny the Beaver's Big Bathroom Project

Benny the Beaver loved to build things. He had strong teeth for chewing wood and a big, flat tail that helped him swim and work. One sunny morning, Benny woke up in his cozy dam and stretched his furry arms.

"What should I build today?" Benny wondered aloud, scratching his round belly.

He waddled to his bathroom to brush his big front teeth. As he turned on the water, he noticed something that made him gasp. "Oh no! The water is running and running, even when I'm not using it!"

Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. Splash, splash went the water hitting the sink.

"This is wasting water," Benny said, feeling worried. "Water is very important for all the animals in the forest. I need to fix this right away!"

Benny put on his tiny tool belt and got to work. He climbed up on his tippy-toes to look at the faucet closely. His nose twitched as he sniffed around the pipes.

"I think I need a new washer," he said. Building things always made Benny feel excited and happy.

First, Benny did some stretches to warm up his muscles. He reached his arms up high like the tall trees, then bent down to touch his toes. "Exercise helps me work better!" he chirped.

Then Benny carefully took apart the faucet with his small wrench. Turn, turn, turn went the wrench. His strong paws worked steadily as he removed each piece.

"I can reuse this old washer in my workshop," Benny said, holding up a small rubber ring. "Nothing should go to waste!"

He found a new washer in his toolbox and put it in place. His tail wiggled with excitement as he worked. Building and fixing things was his favorite activity!

"Now I'll put everything back together," Benny said. He worked slowly and carefully, making sure each piece fit perfectly. Click, click, click went the parts as they snapped into place.

When Benny turned the water on again, it flowed smoothly and stopped completely when he turned it off. No more drips!

"Hooray!" Benny cheered, doing a little happy dance. His big tail slapped the bathroom floor with joy. "I fixed it!"

But Benny wasn't finished yet. He looked around his bathroom and had another idea.

"I can make this bathroom even better for the environment," he said.

Benny built a small shelf from recycled wood scraps to hold his toothbrush and soap. He painted it with eco-friendly paint that smelled like fresh pine trees. The green color reminded him of the forest he loved.

Next, he made a sign from bark that said "Turn off the water while brushing teeth!" He hung it right above the sink where he could see it every day.

"There!" Benny said, admiring his work. "Now I'll always remember to save water."

That evening, Benny's friend Ruby Rabbit came to visit. She saw the beautiful bathroom and the helpful sign.

"Wow, Benny! You did such good work," Ruby said. "This will help save water for all the forest animals."

"Building things that help the Earth makes me feel proud," Benny said with a big smile. "And exercising while I work keeps me strong and healthy too!"

From that day on, Benny always turned off the water while brushing his teeth, and he taught all his friends to do the same. He learned that even small actions can make a big difference for the environment.

The End.
