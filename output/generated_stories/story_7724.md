# Roary’s Sharing Parade

In a sunny, playful living room, a brave little lion cub named Roary wiggled his whiskers. “Good morning, toys!” he roared softly. The carpet felt fuzzy under his paws. The window was open, and a gentle breeze brought in the smell of grass and warm sunshine. Birds chirped like tiny bells.

Roary’s friends arrived with happy steps. There was Mia the monkey, who loved bananas and drums. There was Noor the giraffe, tall and gentle, with a scarf of bright colors. And there was Tiko the turtle, slow and thoughtful, with a shiny, patterned shell.

“Hello!” Roary said. “Today, we play parade!”

“Parade!” cheered Mia, beating a small toy drum. “Boom-boom!”

Noor smiled. “In my family, we wear scarves with many colors for festivals,” she said, touching her scarf. “Maybe our parade can be like a festival.”

Tiko nodded slowly. “In my home, we listen to soft music. We can have quiet steps and loud steps.”

Roary’s ears perked up. “I want to see and hear and learn,” he said bravely. “Let’s share our ways!”

They all sat in a circle on the soft rug. The living room smelled like cookies. Sunlight made bright squares on the floor.

“First, let’s get ready,” Roary said. He picked up a basket of dress-up things. Feathers, hats, and ribbons rustled. “We will take turns.”

“Me first!” Mia shouted. She grabbed the drum and a green hat.

Roary took a deep breath. “We need fair turns,” he said kindly. “We say, ‘My turn, your turn.’ We count to three.”

Mia looked at Roary and nodded. “Okay. My turn, then your turn.”

Noor clapped softly. “That is helpful, Roary.”

They began with Noor. Noor showed a gentle dance from her family’s festival. She stepped slow, then bright, lifting her scarf like a rainbow. The scarf tickled the air. Roary watched with big eyes.

“Your dance is beautiful,” said Roary. “Can you teach me?”

“Yes,” Noor said. “Step, step, twirl.”

Roary tried. Step, step, twirl! His tail whooshed happily. Everyone giggled when he wobbled, and Roary giggled too.

Next was Mia. “In my neighborhood, we drum and sing,” she said. “We sing, ‘La-la-la, come and play!’”

Mia tapped the drum. Boom, boom, tap! The beats bounced around the room. Roary clapped his paws. “I want to try!”

“Your turn,” Mia said, handing him the drum. Roary felt the drum’s bumpy top. Tap-tap! Boom! He smiled. “I hear your song,” he said. “It is friendly.”

Then Tiko the turtle showed his slow parade. “In my family, we like quiet walks outside,” he said. “We listen to leaves and the wind.”

Roary’s whiskers twitched. “Outside?” He peeked out the window. The yard was green, with soft grass. “Let’s have a tiny outdoor parade,” he said. “We will be safe, and we will stay together.”

They lined up: Noor first, tall and careful; Mia second, drum ready; Roary third, brave and kind; Tiko last, steady and wise. They stepped onto the porch. Warm sunshine kissed their faces. The grass felt cool on Roary’s paws. He could smell flowers, sweet and gentle. A butterfly fluttered like a little kite.

“Slow steps,” Tiko whispered. Crunch went a dry leaf. “Listen.”

They stood very still. They heard wind whoosh, birds tweet, and far away a dog bark, woof-woof. Mia hummed softly. Noor’s scarf shimmered in the light.

“Now loud steps!” Mia said.

Roary raised a paw. “We take turns,” he reminded. “We had Tiko’s quiet turn. Now it is Mia’s loud turn.”

“Thank you,” Mia said. She tapped the drum. Boom-boom, tap-tap! Noor danced. Roary twirled, his tail swishing. A neighbor lady smiled and waved from her porch. “What a lovely parade!” she called.

Roary waved back. “We are sharing our ways!”

A little boy from next door peeked through the fence. He had a red toy car and shy eyes. Roary padded over. “Hello. I’m Roary. Want to join our parade?”

The boy nodded. “I’m Ben. In my family, we clap and say, ‘Hooray!’”

“Welcome, Ben,” said Noor. “Your way is welcome.”

They made space for Ben in the line. “We help each other,” said Tiko, moving slowly to let Ben in. “That is helpfulness.”

Ben clapped. “Hooray!” Everyone clapped too. Hooray, hooray! The claps sounded like happy rain.

After the parade, they went back to the playful living room. Roary passed out water cups. “Sip, sip,” he said. “We took turns. We shared. We learned.”

Mia wiped her drum. “I learned to be patient.”

Noor folded her scarf. “I learned your drum song.”

Tiko patted his shell. “I learned that quiet can share space with loud.”

Ben rolled his car and smiled. “I learned new friends. Hooray!”

Roary’s heart felt warm, like sunshine on fur. “I learned about many ways,” he said. “Dances, drums, quiet steps, and claps. All our ways are special. All our ways can play together.”

They made a big group hug, soft and cozy. The room smelled like flowers and cookies again. Roary whispered, “Different is beautiful. Sharing is kind. Taking turns makes room for everyone.”

Everyone nodded. “Yes,” they said together.

Roary looked around at his friends. “Today, we were brave, kind, and helpful,” he said. “We had an outdoor adventure and a living room parade. We learned about each other. That is the best treasure.”

They all smiled, paws and hands and hooves and flippers touching gently. “Hooray for all our ways,” they cheered.

The End.
