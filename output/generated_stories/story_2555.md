# Milo’s Music-Time Routine

Milo was a courageous monkey with bright eyes and quick little hands. Every morning, he scampered into the musical practice room with a happy chatter. The room smelled like polished wood and fresh paper. Sunlight danced through the window, and instruments rested like sleepy friends. There was a small drum, a shiny triangle, a gentle xylophone, and a tiny piano with keys that looked like little teeth.

Milo had a special routine. He liked routines because they made him feel calm and strong. He whispered, “Routine helps me do my best.” Then he tapped his list on the wall. It was a big chart with pictures. Milo had drawn the pictures himself.

“Step one,” he said, tapping the first picture. “Wash hands.” He washed with warm, bubbly soap. Splish, splash! His fur smelled clean, like lemons.

“Step two: clean up.” He picked up music papers and put them in neat stacks. Rustle, rustle!

“Step three: warm-up.” He took a deep breath. In, out. In, out. Then he hummed, “mm-mm-mm,” like a sleepy bee. His voice felt warm and ready.

“Step four: writing time.” Milo loved writing. He had a little notebook with a banana on the cover and a green pencil. He wrote down the sounds he wanted to play. He didn’t know big letters yet, so he used lines, dots, and simple shapes. He said, “This dot is a tap on the drum. This line is a slide on the xylophone.” He was independent and proud. “I can do it,” he told himself.

One day, as Milo wrote his tiny sound shapes, his friend Tiko, a curious parrot, flapped in. “Squawk! Can I try the drum?” Tiko asked, eyes wide.

“Yes,” Milo said kindly. “After I finish step four.” He smiled, but he felt excited too.

Tiko saw the drum and could not wait. “Boom!” Tiko tapped it with a big thump. The drum rolled, and papers fluttered like leaves. Milo’s notebook fell open on the floor. A page tore—riiip.

Milo gasped. “My writing page!” His chest felt tight. His eyes felt hot. He wanted to shout, but he took a deep breath. In, out. In, out. He looked at his routine chart. “Stay calm,” he whispered. “Routine helps me do my best.”

Tiko shuffled his feet. “I’m sorry, Milo,” he said softly. “I made a mistake.”

Milo listened. He put his hand on his heart. “I feel sad,” he said. “But I can forgive.” He breathed again and remembered step two: clean up. “Let’s fix it together.”

They picked up the papers. Rustle, rustle. Milo looked at the torn page. The paper felt rough at the rip, but the rest was smooth. He had an idea. He peeled the torn piece away and put it into a blue bin with a circle and arrows. “This is the recycling bin,” Milo explained. “Old paper can be made into new paper.”

Tiko blinked. “New paper from old paper? Squawk! That’s smart.”

“Recycling helps the world,” Milo said. “And it helps me keep my room neat.”

Milo took a fresh page from a stack of used-on-one-side papers. One side had doodles from yesterday, and the other side was blank and clean. “We can reuse this,” he said. He smiled. “See? Routine, writing, and recycling can work together.”

He sat at his little table. “Step four: writing time,” he said again, steady and brave. He drew a dot for the drum, a zigzag line for the triangle, and a long stripe for the xylophone. He spoke as he drew. “Tap, ting, ziiiiing.” The pencil felt smooth, and the paper smelled like warm sunshine.

Tiko watched. “Can I help?” he asked.

“Yes,” Milo said. “You can say the sounds while I write them.” Tiko said, “Boom, ting, ziiing!” They giggled. The sound plan was ready.

“Step five: play time!” Milo tapped the drum gently. Tap-tap. Tiko rang the triangle. Ting! The xylophone sang, ziiiiing. The room filled with friendly sounds. They took turns, and they followed the plan Milo wrote. It felt easy because they had a routine.

After the song, Milo checked the chart. “Last step: clean up and thank you.” He and Tiko stacked the papers, put the instruments back in their spots, and swept tiny crumbs into the bin. The room looked neat again. It sounded quiet and peaceful, like a soft hug.

Tiko said, “Thank you for forgiving me.”

Milo nodded. “Thank you for helping me fix it.” He felt proud and independent. He had stayed calm. He had used his routine. He had recycled. He had written a plan. He had forgiven his friend.

They high-fived—slap!—and Milo stuck a gold star next to “Step four: writing time.”

Milo turned to the window. The sun was still bright. He whispered, “Routine helps me do my best.” His heart felt light, and his tail swished happily.

That day, Milo learned an important lesson: When we follow a kind routine, we can stay calm, write our plans, fix mistakes, forgive our friends, and take care of our world by recycling. Little steps, one by one, help us be brave and strong every day.

The End.
