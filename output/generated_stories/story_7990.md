# Whiskers and the Wobbly Wagon

Whiskers the clever cat loved spending time in Grandpa Joe's garage workshop. The smell of wood shavings tickled her pink nose, and the sound of tools clinking made her orange ears twitch with excitement.

"Good morning, Whiskers!" called Grandpa Joe as he put on his work apron. "Today I need to fix Mrs. Chen's little red wagon. The wheel is wobbly, and she needs it back by lunch time."

Whiskers meowed happily and rubbed against Grandpa Joe's legs. She loved helping with projects!

"Now, let me think," said Grandpa Joe, scratching his gray beard. "First, I need to listen carefully to what's wrong with the wagon." He rolled the wagon back and forth. *Squeak, wobble, squeak!* "Ah, I hear the problem. The wheel is loose."

Whiskers sat very still and listened too. Her ears perked up as she heard the squeaky sound.

"Next," continued Grandpa Joe, "I need to plan my time. Mrs. Chen needs this back in three hours. Let me think about what steps I need to take." He counted on his fingers. "Remove the wheel, find the right bolt, tighten everything up, and test it. That should take about two hours if I stay focused."

Whiskers watched as Grandpa Joe gathered his tools. But then something shiny caught her eye - a red ball of yarn had rolled under the workbench! Without thinking, she pounced after it.

"Whiskers, where did you go?" called Grandpa Joe. "I could use your help listening for any other squeaks."

But Whiskers was too busy batting the yarn ball around the garage. The soft yarn felt wonderful under her paws! She chased it behind boxes and under shelves.

After a while, Whiskers heard Grandpa Joe sigh. "Oh dear, I can't find my small wrench anywhere. I've been looking for twenty minutes!"

Whiskers stopped playing and looked around. There, right next to where she'd been playing with the yarn, was the small wrench! She had knocked it off the workbench while chasing the ball.

"Meow!" she called, running to the wrench.

"Thank you, Whiskers!" said Grandpa Joe. "But now I'm running late. I should have been listening more carefully to where I put my tools."

Whiskers felt sorry. She realized she hadn't been listening to Grandpa Joe when he asked for help. Instead of helping him reach his goal, she had made things harder by playing and not paying attention.

"I have an idea," said Grandpa Joe kindly. "Let's work together. You can be my listening helper. When I work on the wheel, you listen for any strange sounds. Can you do that?"

Whiskers nodded and sat very still. As Grandpa Joe worked, she listened carefully. When he tightened the bolt, she heard a tiny *ping* sound.

"Meow! Meow!" she said, pointing her paw at the wheel.

"Good listening, Whiskers! You're right - that bolt isn't quite tight enough yet." Grandpa Joe gave it another turn until the sound was just right.

Together, they tested the wagon. It rolled smoothly and quietly - no more squeaks or wobbles!

"We did it!" cheered Grandpa Joe. "And we finished just in time for lunch. Thank you for being such a good listener, Whiskers."

Whiskers purred proudly. She had learned that listening carefully and staying focused helped them reach their goal together.

The End.
