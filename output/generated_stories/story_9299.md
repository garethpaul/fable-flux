# Gigi the Careful Builder

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with big, gentle eyes and a long, wiggly neck. One sunny morning, she stepped into the garden shed. The air smelled like fresh wood and clean soap. Sunlight made tiny dust sparkles dance. Gigi smiled. “Today, I will build a birdhouse,” she said softly. “But I will build it safely.”

The shed was full of sounds. Tap, tap from a little hammer on a hook. Swish, swish from a broom leaning in the corner. A soft creak from the door. Gigi’s ears twitched. “First, I make a plan,” she said. She took a big sheet of paper and a red crayon. “A roof, a tiny door, and a perch,” she whispered, drawing careful shapes.

Gigi looked around the shed. There was a box of nails, a shiny hammer, wood pieces, and a little saw. A bright yellow sign on the wall said, “Think Safe, Work Safe.” Gigi nodded. “Safety first.”

She put on her goggles. “These are for eye safety,” she said. The goggles felt snug and made a little squeak. She tied her long scarf behind her so it would not dangle. “Loose things can get caught,” she reminded herself. She slipped on her gloves. “These are for hand safety.”

Gigi placed the wood on a sturdy table. “I need help to hold the wood,” she said. She looked at a clamp with a twisty handle. “Hello, clamp,” Gigi giggled. “You will be my helper.” She tightened the clamp. Twist, twist. The wood did not wiggle.

Gigi picked up the little saw. “Slow and steady,” she said. Scritch, scritch went the saw. The smell of fresh wood was sweet like pencils. A tiny curl of wood fell to the floor. Gigi kept her body still and her eyes wide. “I am safe. I am calm,” she hummed.

Suddenly, she heard a tap at the door. Tap-tap. It was her friend, Pip the robin. “Cheep! What are you doing, Gigi?”

“I am building your birdhouse,” said Gigi. “But I must do it safely.”

“Cheep-cheep! I can cheer for you,” Pip said. He sat on a shelf and watched with bright eyes.

Gigi checked her plan again. “Next, I need nails,” she said. She reached for the nail box, but it was up high. She stretched her long neck. Stretch, stretch. The box wobbled. Wobble, wobble. “Oh!” Gigi froze. She put her hooves back down and took a breath. “This is not safe,” she said. “I need a step stool.”

She found a sturdy step stool. She checked it with her hoof. “No cracks. Not slippery. Good.” She asked, “Pip, can you watch me?”

“Cheep! I will watch,” Pip said.

Gigi climbed slowly. One step. Pause. Two steps. Pause. She held the rail. She moved the box of nails close to her body. “Walk slowly,” she told herself. She climbed down with tiny steps. Tap, tap, tap. Safe on the ground, she smiled. “I did it safely.”

Gigi placed the nails in a small dish so they would not roll. “Little things can be tricky,” she said. “I keep them in one place.”

She began to hammer. Tap. Tap-tap. Tap. She held the nail straight and moved her hoof away. “Fingers back,” she whispered. The hammer made a happy ringing sound. Pip bobbed his head. “Cheep! Nice taps.”

Then, the wood slipped a little. Slide. Gigi stopped right away. “When it slips, I stop,” she said. She breathed in. She tightened the clamp again. Twist, twist. “There. Safer.”

Gigi tried again. Tap. Tap-tap. A nail bent. “Oh dear,” she said. Her ears drooped for a moment. Pip chirped, “Cheep-cheep! You can try again.”

Gigi looked at the bent nail. “I will be patient,” she said. She pulled the bent nail out with small pliers. She put a new nail in, held it steady, and tapped gently. Tap. Tap. Tap-tap. Straight and strong. Gigi beamed. “Persistence means keep trying, even when it is hard.”

Soon, the birdhouse had a roof, a tiny round door, and a little perch. Gigi rubbed the edges with sandpaper. Swish, swish. The wood felt smooth, not scratchy. “Smooth edges are safe for small feet,” she said.

Gigi painted the roof sky-blue and the sides sunshine-yellow. The paint smelled a little funny, like wet leaves. She opened the window wide. Fresh air whooshed in. “Good air keeps us safe,” she said. Pip sniffed the breeze and fluffed his feathers.

At last, Gigi held up the birdhouse. “Pip, what do you think?”

Pip flew in and out of the tiny door. “Cheep! It is perfect. And safe!”

Gigi smiled and wiped a tiny dot of paint from her nose. “I am glad. I used my plan. I used my tools with care. I asked for help when I needed it. I kept trying.”

Pip sang a happy song. Gigi hung the birdhouse on a low branch outside the shed. It clicked softly as it swung. Leaves rustled. The sun felt warm on Gigi’s back.

Gigi turned to Pip and said, “Today I learned: Safety first, then do your best. Go slow, check your tools, and keep trying. That is how we build.”

Pip chirped, “Cheep-cheep! Safety, persistence, building!”

Gigi laughed. “Yes. Safe. Patient. Proud.”

And the little birdhouse became a bright, safe home.

The End.
