# Leo the Lion Cub and the Rooftop Wind

Leo was a brave little lion cub with bright, curious eyes and a soft gold mane that tickled his ears. One warm afternoon, Leo padded up the stair-stair-stairs to the rooftop. The rooftop felt warm on his paws. The sun was a friendly yellow circle, and the breeze made a gentle whoosh-whoosh. From up high, he could see tiny cars and tiny people. He could smell baking bread from the shop below. Mmm!

On the rooftop was Leo’s favorite place: the Big Blue Blanket. Leo used it for resting, for rolling, and for dreaming. Today, he wanted to build something new. “I want to make a shade tent,” Leo said. “Then my friends can sit and read with me.”

He opened his small backpack. Inside were clothespins, a little string, and his blue blanket. He spread the blanket wide. It felt soft and cool. “Ahh,” he purred.

Leo clipped one corner of the blanket to the short fence. Clip! He tied the string to another corner. Tie-tie! He stood back. “Shade tent!” he cheered.

But the wind said, “Whoooooosh!” The blanket flapped like a big blue bird and popped off the fence. Flop! It landed on Leo’s head. “Oof!” said Leo, giggling. Then his ears drooped. “Oh no. My tent doesn’t work.”

Leo felt a little lump in his throat. His paws felt slow and heavy. “I can’t make it,” he whispered. He took a deep breath. The warm air smelled like sunshine and bread. He remembered what Mama Lion always said: “When something is hard, use your head and your heart. Learn, try, and try again.”

Leo sat and listened. The wind made a whispery song. He closed his eyes and felt it on his whiskers. “The wind is strong up here,” he said. “I need a better way.”

“Use ideas,” Mama Lion always said. “Ideas are tools!”

Leo looked around the rooftop with new eyes. He saw two flower pots, a short ladder, and some clothespins. He saw a box of smooth pebbles. He looked at the fence. He looked at the blanket. “I can learn from the wind,” he said softly. “I can work with it, not fight it.”

Just then, his friend, Mina the little dove, fluttered down and landed on the fence. Flutter-flutter. “Coo! Hello, Leo,” Mina chirped. “What are you making?”

“A shade tent,” Leo said. “But the wind keeps knocking it down.”

Mina tilted her head. “The wind helps me fly,” she said. “I fly better when I point my wings with the wind.”

Leo’s eyes lit up. “Thank you, Mina! I can point the blanket with the wind too.”

He stood tall and brave. “Okay, new plan,” he said. “I will try an idea.” He moved the blanket so one side faced the wind, not against it. He tucked one corner under the heavy flower pot. He wrapped the string around the pot. Wrap-wrap. He clipped another corner to the fence. Clip! He placed smooth pebbles along the bottom edge of the blanket. Tap-tap-tap.

“Whooooosh!” sang the wind again. The blanket puffed up like a soft blue bubble, then settled into a nice curve. The tent didn’t fly away.

Leo smiled. “It’s working!” He crawled inside. Shade! It felt cool and calm. It smelled a little like flowers and sunshine. He patted the rooftop. Pat-pat. “Thank you, wind,” he said. “We are friends now.”

Mina hopped inside. “Coo! It is cozy,” she said.

Just then, a tiny squeak came from the ladder. Squeak-squeak. Pip the mouse peeked out, eyes wide. “I’m sorry,” Pip squeaked. “I took your clothespins yesterday. I wanted to hang my leaf art. I forgot to put them back. I was scared to tell you.”

Leo looked at the little mouse. He felt a warm bubble in his chest. He remembered how he felt when the tent fell. He took a breath. “Thank you for telling me the truth,” Leo said softly. “I forgive you, Pip. Next time, please ask.”

Pip nodded fast. “Yes! I will ask,” he said. He held out the clothespins with tiny paws. “Can I help now?”

“Yes,” said Leo. “Let’s make it stronger together.” Pip placed two pins here and two pins there. Clip, clip. Mina adjusted the edge with her beak. Nudge-nudge.

The wind gave a friendly whoosh. The tent held steady. The shade felt cool like a hug. Leo set out three little cups of water from his bag. They clinked softly. He shared with his friends. “To ideas!” Leo said. “To learning! To being brave and kind!”

They sipped. They smiled. They read a small picture book together under the shade. The pages smelled like paper and ink. The world looked blue and calm through the tent.

When the sun began to slip lower, Leo packed up carefully. He rolled the blanket slow. Roll-roll. He put the pebbles back in the box and the pots by the wall. He patted the fence. “Thank you, rooftop,” he whispered. He booped Pip’s nose. He bumped wings with Mina.

Mina chirped, “What did you learn today, Leo?”

Leo stood tall. His tail swished. “I learned to use my ideas,” he said. “I learned to watch, listen, and try again. I learned to work with the wind. And I learned that telling the truth and forgiving make friends stronger.”

Mina cooed. Pip squeaked. The breeze hummed a soft song.

Leo smiled at the wide sky. “When a problem comes,” he said, “I can think, learn, and make something new.”

The End.
