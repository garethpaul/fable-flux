# Buzzy Paws in the Warm Kitchen

In a warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon and honey, a friendly bear cub named Buzzy wiggled his paws. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. A kettle hummed softly. The wooden spoon jar stood like a forest of sticks.

“Mama Bear,” Buzzy said, nose twitching, “can I help stir the batter?”

Mama smiled, her fur soft and shiny. “Yes, helper cub. Helping is kind. Helping takes coordination. That means your eyes, paws, and body work together.”

Buzzy puffed out his chest. “I can do it! My paws are big and strong.”

Mama set a big yellow bowl on the table. The bowl made a clink. She slid a little blue stool close. “First, we breathe. Helping needs steady paws.”

Buzzy hopped up and grabbed the spoon. The spoon felt heavy and slippery. He wanted to go fast. He wanted to stir, stir, stir!

“Zoom!” Buzzy whispered, and he swirled the spoon like a whirly wind.

Sploosh! Batter splashed on the table. Plip! Batter dotted Buzzy’s nose.

Buzzy’s ears drooped. “Oh no.”

Mama’s voice was gentle. “Oops can happen. Let’s try again with self-regulation. That means you slow down your body and use control.”

Buzzy nodded. “How do I do that?”

“We use our bear tools,” said Mama. She tapped her nose. “Look with eyes. Feel with paws. Breathe with belly.”

Buzzy took a deep belly breath. In. Out. The warm kitchen air smelled like vanilla. The kettle hummed its happy song.

Mama placed her paw over his for a moment. “Soft circles. Watch the spoon. Listen for quiet swooshes.”

Buzzy watched the spoon. He moved slow. Swoosh, swoosh. The spoon made soft sounds, like a little river. The batter stayed in the bowl. Buzzy’s shoulders relaxed.

“Good control,” said Mama. “That’s coordination. Eyes see. Paws move. Body stays steady.”

Buzzy grinned. “I can feel it!”

Just then, a tiny egg rolled near the edge of the counter. It wiggled and wobbled.

“Egg alert!” Buzzy squeaked.

Mama stepped back to let Buzzy try. “You can catch it, little cub. Use your tools.”

Buzzy’s heart thumped. He wanted to grab fast. But fast made splashes. He put both paws up, fingers open. He looked at the egg. He bent his knees a little. He breathed.

“Soft, soft,” he whispered.

The egg rolled into his paws. He cupped it gently. No crack! The egg was cool and smooth. Buzzy giggled. “I did it!”

“Respect the egg,” Mama said with a wink. “Respect means you are gentle and kind with things and people.”

Buzzy nodded. “I will respect the egg.”

They finished mixing. Mama set out a tray of little muffin cups. “Would you like to scoop?”

“Yes!” Buzzy said. He scooped a small scoop. He watched the scoop. He breathed. Plop. Batter landed right in the cup. He tried again. Plop. Plip. One tiny drip landed on the pan.

Buzzy wiped it with a cloth. “I help clean too.”

“That shows respect for our kitchen,” Mama said. “Thank you.”

While the muffins baked, the oven made warm whooshes. The kitchen filled with sweet, cozy smells. Buzzy’s tummy rumbled like a friendly bear drum.

“While we wait, let’s do a movement game,” Mama said. “Movement helps coordination.”

Buzzy bounced. “A game!”

Mama made a line with a wooden spoon on the floor. “We heel-to-toe walk on the spoon path. Eyes forward, paws out like wings. Slow and steady.”

Buzzy put one paw in front of the other. He wobbled. He breathed. He looked at the spoon line. Step, step. He made it across!

“I did it, Mama!”

“Now try across and back,” Mama said. “Add a gentle clap. Step, step, clap. Step, step, clap.”

Buzzy tried. His paws and claps wanted to go zoom, but he remembered to breathe. Step, step, clap. Step, step, clap. His paws and claps found a happy rhythm. He felt proud and calm.

Ding! The oven sang.

Mama pulled out golden muffins. The air was warm and sweet. Buzzy waited, paws behind his back. “They look hot,” he said.

“That is self-regulation too,” Mama smiled. “Waiting keeps us safe.”

When the muffins cooled, Mama split one and added a drizzle of honey. The honey shone like sunshine. Buzzy took a bite. It was soft and sweet. He did a tiny happy dance.

“I used my bear tools,” Buzzy said between little chews. “Eyes, paws, body, and breath. I went slow. I was gentle. I respected the kitchen. I moved my body with control.”

Mama hugged him. “Yes. You practiced coordination, self-regulation, and exercise. You helped. You learned. I am proud.”

Buzzy licked a dot of honey from his paw. “Tomorrow, can we play the spoon path again?”

“Tomorrow and today,” Mama laughed. “Two more rounds after our snack.”

Buzzy stood tall on his blue stool and waved the wooden spoon like a tiny flag. “Soft circles, steady steps, happy claps!” he cheered.

And in the warm, cinnamon kitchen, Buzzy’s paws felt ready for every gentle, careful thing.

The End.
