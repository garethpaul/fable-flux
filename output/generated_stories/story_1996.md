# Pippa Penguin’s Planet Plan

Pippa was a cheerful penguin who loved her cozy bedroom. Her bed felt soft like a snow cloud. Her lamp made a warm, golden glow. Stickers of stars and maps dotted the walls. Her favorite blanket smelled like fresh soap. Pippa wiggled her little feet and smiled.

“I want to help the whole big world,” Pippa said. “But I am just one penguin in one room!”

A gentle breeze slipped through the window and made her curtains whisper, swish-swish. Pippa sat up and tapped her beak. “I need a plan,” she said. “A big, smart plan.”

She pulled out a box under her bed. Inside were crayons, paper, and a small globe that felt smooth and cool. Pippa placed the globe on her pillow and gave it a tiny spin. The colors spun—blue seas and green lands. It made a soft whirrrrr sound.

“Hello, World,” Pippa whispered. “How can I help you?”

Her toy friends sat in a row on the shelf: a plush koala, a tiny turtle, and a fluffy bear. Pippa brought them to the bed.

“Team meeting!” she said. “We will plan to help the planet.”

Pippa took a crayon and drew a big heart on paper. “Step one: Listen and learn,” she said. “What does our world need?”

The koala smiled with button eyes. Pippa held it close and said in a koala voice, “Trees help me. Please be kind to trees.”

The turtle felt smooth under her flippers. In a tiny turtle voice she said, “Clean water helps me. Please keep water fresh.”

The bear felt soft and cozy. In a low bear voice Pippa said, “Clean air helps me. Please keep air clear.”

Pippa nodded. “Trees. Water. Air. I can help!” She wrote the words in big, round letters. The paper smelled like waxy crayons.

But then Pippa frowned. “The world is so big. I am so small.”

Her tummy felt wiggly. Her eyes felt wet.

She hugged her blanket and whispered, “I can’t do everything.”

The globe sat quiet on the pillow. Pippa gave it a gentle pat. Spin, whirrr. She took a slow breath. The warm lamp light made her feel calm.

“I will start small,” Pippa said softly. “Small steps can help big places.”

She made a new list with bright colors.

- Plan a cozy bedroom clean-up.
- Save energy.
- Use less and share more.
- Be kind and respectful to people everywhere.

Pippa clapped. “Yes! A plan.”

First, she picked up books and stacked them neatly. Tap, tap, tap. She put toys in a basket. Thump, clink, rustle. She found some paper scraps and put them in a special box. “These can be used again,” she said. “I can draw on the back.”

Next, Pippa padded to her lamp and turned it off for a moment. The room went quiet and dim. She opened the curtains wider. Sunlight poured in, warm and bright. “Hello, sun!” she giggled. “You can help me see.”

She turned the lamp back on just a little later, using the small switch. “I will use only what I need,” she said. “That saves energy.”

Pippa’s tummy stopped feeling wiggly. Her smile grew wide.

Now she checked her water cup. “I will take just a small sip,” she said, “and save the rest for later.” Sip. Ahh. Cool and clean.

She wrote again. “Step two: Help others.”

Pippa picked out a puzzle she didn’t use much. She hugged it and whispered, “You can make someone happy.” She set it by the door to share with a neighbor.

Then Pippa looked at her globe. She placed a tiny heart sticker on each place she had heard about: a forest, a beach, a snowy land, a city. “People and animals live everywhere,” she said. “We show respect by caring for our shared home.”

Her phone made a gentle beep. It was a message from her cousin in a faraway town: “Hi, Pippa! What are you doing?”

Pippa typed slowly, sounding out the words. “I am helping our planet from my cozy bedroom. I clean, I save energy, I share, I care.”

Her cousin sent a happy face. “I will try too!”

Pippa wiggled with joy. “My small steps can travel far,” she said. “Like little footsteps across the globe.”

She made one last plan card with bright blue letters: “Ask for help.” Pippa waddled to the door and called, “Mama?”

Mama stepped in, smelling like warm tea. “Yes, Pippa?”

“I made a planet plan,” Pippa said. She showed the lists. “Can we recycle these papers? Can we walk to the library tomorrow? I want to learn about trees and oceans.”

Mama’s eyes sparkled. “What a thoughtful plan,” she said. “Yes, we can. I’m proud of you.”

Pippa felt light, like a happy snowflake. She hugged Mama tight. “Thank you.”

That night, Pippa snuggled under her blanket. The room was tidy and calm. The globe on her pillow felt cool under her flipper. She whispered, “Good night, World. I am helping you.”

The lamp clicked off. The curtains swished. The stars on her wall glowed softly.

Pippa smiled in the dark. “Small plans. Small steps. Big love,” she said. “We can all help, together.”

The End.
