# Bella Bird's Colorful Discovery

In a cozy garage workshop filled with paintbrushes, wooden blocks, and colorful paints, lived a little yellow bird named Bella. She loved to sing sweet songs while Mr. Tom, the kind workshop owner, built toys for children.

"Tweet-tweet-tweet!" Bella sang happily every morning, perching on the windowsill as golden sunlight streamed through the glass.

But one day, Bella noticed something troubling. Her beautiful yellow feathers were looking dull and gray. Her voice felt scratchy, and her songs didn't sound as pretty as before.

"Oh no!" chirped Bella sadly. "What's happening to me? I don't feel bright and cheerful anymore."

Mr. Tom looked up from his workbench where he was painting a red toy truck. "What's wrong, little friend?" he asked gently.

"My feathers look sad, and my voice sounds funny," Bella explained, fluffing her droopy feathers.

Mr. Tom set down his paintbrush and smiled kindly. "I think I know what might help! But first, let me ask you something. What have you been eating lately?"

Bella thought for a moment. "Just some old crackers I found behind the paint cans," she admitted quietly.

"Ah!" said Mr. Tom. "That's the problem! Just like my paints need the right colors to make beautiful pictures, your body needs the right foods to keep you healthy and bright."

Mr. Tom walked to a small shelf and pulled out a book with colorful pictures. "Look here, Bella. This book shows all the foods that help birds stay strong and beautiful."

Together, they looked at pictures of fresh seeds, juicy berries, and green leafy vegetables. The pages were full of reds, blues, purples, and greens.

"These foods are like nature's paint palette!" Mr. Tom explained. "They give you energy and make your feathers shine."

Bella's eyes sparkled with interest. "Really? Food can be like art?"

"Absolutely!" Mr. Tom laughed. "And I have an idea. Let's go outside and find some of these healthy foods together."

They walked to Mr. Tom's garden behind the workshop. Bella discovered bright red strawberries, dark blue blueberries, and tiny black sunflower seeds. The colors were amazing!

"These look like the paints in your workshop!" Bella chirped excitedly.

"That's right! Nature is the greatest artist of all," Mr. Tom said. "Try some of these nutritious foods."

Bella carefully tasted each colorful food. The strawberries were sweet and juicy. The blueberries burst with flavor. The seeds were crunchy and satisfying.

Within just a few days of eating these healthy, colorful foods, something wonderful happened. Bella's feathers became bright yellow again, even more beautiful than before! Her voice grew strong and clear.

"Tweet-tweet-tweet!" she sang joyfully, her song filling the workshop with happiness.

"You sound magnificent!" Mr. Tom cheered, clapping his hands.

From that day on, Bella made sure to eat a rainbow of healthy foods every day. She even helped other birds in the neighborhood learn about good nutrition by sharing what she had discovered.

"Eating colorful, healthy foods is like painting yourself from the inside out!" Bella would tell her new friends.

And whenever children visited Mr. Tom's workshop, Bella would sing her prettiest songs while Mr. Tom explained how eating well helps everyone grow strong and happy, just like mixing the right colors makes the most beautiful paintings.

Bella learned that taking care of herself helped her take care of others too. Her beautiful songs brought joy to everyone who heard them, and her healthy eating habits inspired all her friends to make good choices.

The End.
