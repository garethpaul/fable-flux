# Ellie's Colorful Solution

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved the bright, cheerful art room at her school. The walls were covered with rainbow paintings, and shelves held jars of colorful paints that sparkled like jewels. The room always smelled like sweet crayons and fresh paper.

Today, Ellie was excited to paint a picture of her family for the art show. She picked up her favorite purple paintbrush with her gentle trunk and dipped it in the pretty purple paint.

"I want to paint too!" said Miko, a little monkey who swung down from the art supply shelf. Miko reached for the same purple paint jar.

"But I was using it first!" Ellie said, holding the paint jar close to her gray, fuzzy chest.

"I need purple for my sunset painting!" Miko chattered, his brown fur bristling with frustration.

Soon, Raj the rabbit hopped over. "What's all this noise about?" he asked, his white whiskers twitching.

"We both want the purple paint," Ellie explained, her big brown eyes looking sad.

"In my family, we always share," said Raj thoughtfully. "But maybe there's another way to solve this."

Just then, Amara the little fox trotted over, her red fur shining in the sunlight streaming through the window. "In my culture, when we have problems, we think of creative solutions together," she said with a smile.

Ellie's ears perked up. "What do you mean?"

Amara pointed to the paint jars with her fluffy tail. "Look! There are red and blue paints right there. What happens when you mix red and blue?"

Miko's eyes grew wide. "They make purple!"

"That's right!" said Raj, clapping his paws together. "And look, there are lots of empty jars we can use for mixing."

Ellie felt her heart grow warm and happy. "So we could make more purple paint instead of fighting over one jar!"

"What a wonderful idea!" said their teacher, Ms. Chen, who had been watching quietly. "This is what we call problem-solving."

The four friends worked together, taking turns to carefully pour red and blue paint into clean jars. The colors swirled together like magic, creating the most beautiful purple paint they had ever seen.

"This purple is even prettier than the first one!" Ellie trumpeted happily, her trunk swaying with joy.

"And now we have enough for everyone," Miko said, dipping his brush into their homemade purple paint.

As they painted side by side, Raj shared stories about his family's traditions, while Amara told them about the beautiful colors used in her grandmother's artwork. Miko showed them how his family painted pictures of the jungle, and Ellie described the special gray paint her elephant family used for celebrations.

"I love learning about everyone's different families," Ellie said, adding purple flowers to her family portrait.

"Me too," said Amara. "Our differences make us special, but working together makes us stronger."

When they finished their paintings, the art room was filled with the most beautiful artwork anyone had ever seen. Each painting was unique and special, just like each friend.

"Today I learned something important," Ellie said, cleaning her paintbrush in the warm, soapy water. "When we have a problem, we don't have to fight. We can think together and find a solution that makes everyone happy."

"And we can learn from each other too," added Miko, hanging up his sunset painting to dry.

From that day on, whenever there was a problem in the art room, the friends remembered Ellie's lesson: think together, share ideas, and create solutions that work for everyone.

The End.
