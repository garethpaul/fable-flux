# Panda Pippa Speaks Up

Panda Pippa lived in a playful living room with bright pillows, soft rugs, and a sunny window. The room smelled like clean soap and a little like oranges. Toys made gentle clinks and clacks. Music hummed from a tiny radio, “la-la-la.”

Pippa was a patient panda. She liked to wait her turn, to listen, and to help. Today, she wanted to build a tall block tower. The blocks were smooth and cool in her paws. “Clink, clack,” went the blocks as she stacked them.

Her friends were there too. Javi the jaguar wore bright green socks. Mei the mouse had sparkly whiskers. Noor the newt had a big, kind smile. They all liked different games, and they all liked to share the space.

“Let’s build!” said Javi. He scooped a pile of blocks.

“Let’s make a rainbow,” squeaked Mei, wiggling her whiskers.

“I can bring the blue,” bubbled Noor.

Pippa nodded. “Yes, please. I will make the base. One, two, three.” She set down three strong blocks. She felt proud and calm. The sun made warm stripes on the floor. A soft breeze puffed the curtains.

Just then, the music got faster. “Tap-tap-tap!” Javi drummed on the blocks. The tower wobbled.

Pippa’s tummy felt tight. She liked the beat, but the tower needed stillness. She took a slow breath in. In through her nose. The air smelled fresh. Out through her mouth. “Whooo.”

“I need still hands for the base,” Pippa said softly. No one heard. The music was bouncy, and everyone was busy.

Mei slid a red block on top. “Pretty!”

Noor pushed a blue block too fast. “Oops!” The tower leaned left.

Pippa felt a little squeeze in her chest. She hugged her soft tail. She was patient, but she also had needs. She remembered a helper rhyme from last night: “Stop. Breathe. Speak up for me.”

She tried again. “Friends, I need slow hands.” Her voice sounded small, like a tiny bell.

Javi kept drumming. “Tap-tap! Tap-tap!” The tower shook.

Pippa closed her eyes. She put a paw on her belly. She took three slow breaths. In… out. In… out. In… out. Her shoulders relaxed.

Then she stood tall. “Friends,” she said in a clear voice, “I need the blocks to be still so I can make the base. Please stop drumming and pushing. Then we can all build.”

The room got quiet. The radio hummed softer. Mei blinked. “Oh! I did not hear you, Pippa. Thank you for telling us.”

Javi paused, his green socks still. “I like to drum,” he said, “but I can drum on a pillow.”

“Good idea,” said Noor. “I can count slow. One… two… three.”

Pippa smiled. “Thank you. That helps me.”

They tried again. The blocks felt steady. “Clink.” “Clack.” Pippa made the base. Mei placed a red block gently. Noor added blue with careful fingers. Javi drummed softly on a squishy pillow. “Thump-thump,” it went in a friendly way.

“Look!” said Pippa. The tower stood tall and straight. It had colors like a rainbow and a strong, wide bottom.

“I like it,” said Mei. “It is tall and calm.”

“It is tall and brave,” said Noor.

Javi grinned. “It is tall and kind.”

Pippa giggled. “It is tall and ours.”

After the tower, it was snack time. The living room smelled like apples and warm toast. Pippa washed her paws at the sink. The water was cool, and the soap bubbles felt tickly. She sang a hand-wash song, “Scrub, scrub, scrub!” Her friends joined in.

At the table, there were crunchy carrots, juicy apple slices, and cups of water that went “glug-glug.” Pippa liked carrots but not the spicy dip. Her tongue felt prickly when she tried it last time.

“Do you want dip?” asked Noor, holding the bowl.

Pippa took a breath. She remembered: “Stop. Breathe. Speak up for me.”

“No, thank you,” she said kindly. “The dip is too spicy for me. I like my carrots plain.”

“That’s okay,” said Noor. “Plain carrots are great.” Mei nodded and munched. Javi said, “I like the dip, but I will keep it on my side.”

Pippa felt warm and safe. Her tummy was happy. She had used her voice, and her friends listened. That felt good.

After snacks, they cleaned up together. Pippa picked up blocks. Mei folded a little blanket. Noor wiped a tiny spill. Javi put the pillow drums back on the couch. Helping hands made the room neat. It smelled fresh again.

Pippa looked around at her friends. They were all different. Javi liked drums. Mei loved sparkles. Noor liked counting. Pippa liked building and slow breaths. Different was good. Different made the day bright.

She clapped her paws. “Thank you for listening to me,” she said. “When I speak up, it helps me. It helps all of us.”

Javi nodded. “When you speak up, I know what to do.”

Mei smiled. “Your words are clear and kind.”

Noor added, “Your voice matters.”

Pippa’s heart felt big and soft. She hugged her friends. The sun was low now, and the room was cozy.

Today’s lesson was gentle and strong: Use your voice to say what you need. Speak up with kind words. Listen when friends speak up too.

Pippa whispered, “I can be patient and brave.”

And the playful living room whispered back, with curtains that swished and pillows that sighed, “Yes, you can.”

The End.
