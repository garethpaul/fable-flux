# Joey's Big Jump Dream

Little Joey the kangaroo loved bouncing around his family's cozy burrow. But today was special - Mama Kangaroo had taken him up to the tall building's rooftop to see the whole city!

"Wow!" said Joey, his eyes wide as saucers. The rooftop felt so high up. He could see cars that looked like tiny toys and people who looked like ants walking on the sidewalks below.

"Look, Joey," said Mama, pointing with her paw. "See all those different neighborhoods? People from all around the world live in our city."

Joey bounced excitedly. "I want to jump to every single one!" he declared. "I'll be the best jumper ever!"

But when Joey tried his first big jump, he only hopped a few feet. He tried again - hop, hop, hop - but he couldn't jump very far at all. His little ears drooped down.

"I'm too small," Joey said sadly. "I'll never be able to jump to all those places like the big kangaroos."

Mama Kangaroo sat down on the warm rooftop and patted the spot next to her. "Come here, little one."

Joey snuggled close. The afternoon sun felt cozy on his soft brown fur.

"You know," said Mama gently, "when I was your age, I felt the same way. I wanted to jump across the whole outback in one giant leap!"

"Really?" asked Joey, looking up with curious eyes.

"Really," Mama smiled. "But then I learned something important. Being small doesn't mean you can't explore the world. It just means you explore it differently."

Joey tilted his head. "How?"

Mama pointed to a little sparrow flying from rooftop to rooftop. "See that bird? She's tiny, but she visits more places than any big animal ever could."

Joey watched the sparrow land on a nearby antenna. "She does go everywhere!"

"And you know what?" said Mama. "We can take the elevator down and walk to those neighborhoods. We can ride the bus to the park. We can meet children from all over the world right here in our city."

Joey's ears perked up. "We can?"

"Of course! Being curious and kind is more important than jumping far. And as you grow bigger, your jumps will grow bigger too."

Joey thought about this. He looked out at all the different buildings - some tall, some short, some colorful, some plain. "Maybe I could start by jumping to that flower pot over there?"

"That sounds perfect," said Mama.

Joey took a deep breath. The cool evening breeze ruffled his fur. He bent his strong little legs and - BOING! - jumped right to the flower pot. The red flowers smelled sweet and lovely.

"I did it!" Joey cheered. "And look - I can see a playground from here where kids are playing together!"

"You sure can," said Mama proudly. "Every small jump teaches you something new."

Joey bounced happily from flower pot to flower pot across the rooftop. With each little jump, he felt more confident. He discovered a butterfly garden, spotted a family having a picnic on another rooftop, and even saw children from different countries playing soccer in the courtyard below.

"Mama," said Joey as the sun began to set, painting the sky orange and pink, "I think being small is actually pretty great. I can notice things that big kangaroos might miss!"

"That's exactly right," said Mama, giving him a warm hug. "You're perfect just the way you are, and you'll keep growing stronger every day."

As they headed to the elevator, Joey felt proud and excited. Tomorrow, he would explore more of his wonderful, diverse city - one small, confident jump at a time.

The End.
