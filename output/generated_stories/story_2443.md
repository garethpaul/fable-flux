# The Clever Cat’s Cozy Corner

In a sunny study corner, a clever cat named Coco curled her striped tail. The study corner was warm and bright. A soft rug tickled Coco’s paws. Stacks of colorful books made a happy rainbow on a low shelf. Crayons smelled waxy and sweet. A small table held paper, scissors, and glue. It was Coco’s favorite place to think.

Today, Coco wanted to make a “World Welcome Poster.” She purred, “A poster that says hello to everyone!”

Her friends came to help. Bibi the bunny hopped in with soft ears. “Hello! I brought carrot stickers,” Bibi said.

Lulu the lamb trotted in with a woolly bounce. “Baa! I brought shiny stars.”

Ravi the raccoon padded in, mask-like eyes bright. “I brought lots of colors!”

Mei the mouse squeaked, “I have tiny bells. They jingle!”

Coco clapped her paws. “Great! Let’s make a poster that welcomes all friends from everywhere.”

They spread their things on the table. Crinkle, crinkle went the paper. The glue smelled a little sweet and sticky. Crayons rolled with tiny thumps. The little bells chimed softly.

Bibi said, “Let’s put big carrot stickers in the middle!”

Lulu said, “Let’s make it all shiny stars!”

Ravi said, “Let’s use every color at once!”

Mei said, “Let’s glue on bells so it jingles.”

Coco blinked. “Hmm,” she purred. “We want it to welcome everyone, not just carrot lovers, or just star fans.” Coco tapped her chin. Tap, tap, tap.

Bibi’s ears drooped. “I really like carrots.”

Lulu’s wool ruffled. “I really like stars.”

Ravi’s tail swished. “I really like colors.”

Mei wrung her tiny paws. “I really like bells.”

The room felt tight and a little quiet. The little friends looked worried. Coco took a slow breath. She smelled the crayons and glue. She listened to the bell’s soft ting. She looked at the bright books. Her whiskers wiggled.

Coco said, “Let’s use our thinking steps.”

“Thinking steps?” asked Mei.

“Yes,” said Coco. “Step one: Stop and breathe.” Everyone took a deep breath in. Smell the crayons. Ahh. They breathed out. The room felt softer.

“Step two: Look and listen,” Coco said. “What do we want?” She pointed to the blank paper. “We want to welcome all friends.”

“Step three: Try ideas and check,” Coco went on. “We can test little pieces.”

Bibi’s ears perked up. “How do we test?”

Coco drew four small boxes on scrap paper. “We make tiny posters first. We see what feels best.”

They tried the first mini poster with big carrots. The orange was bright and cheerful. “Yummy,” Bibi said. But Lulu said, “Friends who don’t like carrots might feel left out.”

They tried the second mini poster with only shiny stars. The stars gleamed. “Sparkly!” Lulu smiled. But Ravi said, “Friends who like soft colors might feel lost.”

They tried the third mini poster with all the colors mixed. “Wow!” said Ravi. But Mei covered her eyes. “It’s a little too busy for me.”

They tried the fourth mini poster with bells all over. It jingled sweetly. Ting-ting! “Nice sound,” Mei said. But Bibi frowned. “Some friends need quiet.”

Coco nodded. “We learned a lot. Carrots are fun, stars are bright, colors are lively, and bells are sweet. But each alone leaves someone out.”

Bibi asked, “What if we mix a little of each?”

Coco’s eyes sparkled. “Let’s think more. Step four: Make a plan that is fair.” She drew a big circle on the big paper. “The middle says ‘Welcome Friends’ in many ways.”

“How do we say it in many ways?” Lulu asked.

Coco pointed to the books. “Let’s look.” She opened a book with kids from many places. They saw “Hello” in different languages. “Hola,” Bibi read. “Hello,” Lulu read. “Namaste,” Ravi read. “Nǐ hǎo,” Mei read.

Coco purred. “Yes! We can write many greetings around the circle. That shows cultural diversity. It shows we welcome friends from everywhere.”

“And the pictures?” Bibi asked.

Coco smiled. “We make small pictures around the words. One carrot here. One star there. One rainbow stripe. One tiny bell with a soft bow.” She tapped the glue. “We can add soft felt under the bell to make a gentle jingle.” Ting… soft and kind.

They worked together. Crayons swished. Glue dabbed. Stickers smoothed. The bell chimed softly. They spoke kindly.

“May I put a star here?” asked Lulu.

“Yes,” said Ravi. “I’ll add a soft color next to it.”

“Can I place a carrot near ‘Hola’?” Bibi asked.

“Good idea,” said Mei. “I’ll tie this small bow to make the bell quiet.”

Soon, the poster glowed with friendly color. The center circle read, “Welcome Friends,” and around it were “Hola, Hello, Namaste, Nǐ hǎo, Jambo, Salaam, Bonjour.” Little pictures danced around the words. The study corner felt warm and bright. It smelled like crayons and a little like cinnamon from Coco’s snack.

They stepped back. Coco asked, “How does it feel?”

Bibi’s ears stood tall. “I feel happy.”

Lulu’s eyes shone. “I feel seen.”

Ravi’s tail swished slowly. “I feel calm.”

Mei rang the tiny bell. Ting… “I feel welcomed.”

Coco smiled, whiskers high. “We used critical thinking. We stopped, we looked, we tested, and we made a fair plan. We compromised so everyone could enjoy the poster.”

They hung the World Welcome Poster in the study corner. When friends walked by, they smiled, waved, and said hello in many ways. The poster did not shout. It sang a gentle, friendly song.

Coco curled up on the rug. “We can solve big problems with small steps,” she purred. “We can think, listen, and share.”

Everyone nodded. They sat together, cozy and proud, in the warm study corner.

The lesson was clear: Use your thinking steps, listen to others, and find a way that welcomes everyone.

The End.
