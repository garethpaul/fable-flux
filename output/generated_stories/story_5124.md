# Polly's Clean Writing Adventure

Polly the parrot loved to write stories with her colorful feathers. She would dip her bright red tail feather in paint and write wonderful tales on big sheets of paper. But Polly had a problem - she never cleaned her feathers after writing!

One sunny morning, Polly flew into the laundry room where Mama was washing clothes. The room smelled fresh and soapy, with warm steam rising from the washing machine.

"Good morning, Polly!" said Mama, folding a soft towel. "What brings you here today?"

"I want to write a new story!" chirped Polly excitedly. "Can I use this room as my writing space?"

Mama smiled. "Of course, but first, let me see your writing feathers."

Polly spread her beautiful tail feathers proudly. But oh my! They were covered in dried paint - purple, green, yellow, and blue all mixed together in messy clumps.

"Polly," Mama said gently, "your feathers are very dirty. Dirty feathers can't write clearly, and they might make you sick too."

"But I love my colorful feathers!" Polly protested. "They make me special!"

Mama filled a small basin with warm, sudsy water. "Clean feathers are healthy feathers, and healthy feathers write the most beautiful stories. Would you like me to show you?"

Polly looked at her reflection in the washing machine door. Her feathers did look quite messy and felt sticky too. "Okay," she said softly.

Mama helped Polly wash each feather carefully. The warm water felt wonderful, and the gentle soap made bubbles that tickled. As they cleaned, the old paint swirled away, revealing Polly's natural bright colors underneath.

"Wow!" gasped Polly, looking at her clean feathers. "They're so shiny and smooth! They feel so much better!"

"Clean feathers are happy feathers," explained Mama. "When we keep ourselves clean, we feel good and stay healthy. Now, shall we try writing?"

Polly dipped her clean feather in fresh blue paint and began to write on a piece of paper. The words flowed smoothly and beautifully across the page.

"Look, Mama! My writing is so much clearer now!" Polly exclaimed. "The letters are neat and pretty!"

"That's because clean tools work better," Mama said. "Just like how we wash our hands before eating and brush our teeth before bed."

Polly wrote a wonderful story about a little bird who learned to keep clean. When she finished, she carefully washed her feather again in the soapy water.

"From now on, I'll always clean my writing feathers," Polly promised. "And I'll take a bath every day too!"

"That's wonderful, Polly," Mama said, giving her a gentle hug. "Clean habits help us feel our best and do our best work."

From that day forward, Polly always washed her feathers after writing. She discovered that being clean made her feel happy, healthy, and ready for new adventures. Her stories became even more beautiful because her clean feathers helped her write clearly.

And whenever young birds came to visit, Polly would share her special discovery: "Clean feathers write the most wonderful stories, and clean habits make us feel wonderful too!"

The End.
