# Melody's Big Problem

Melody the singing bird loved her cozy home in the Johnson family's living room. Her bright yellow feathers shimmered in the morning sunlight that streamed through the big window. She lived in a beautiful white cage near the soft blue couch.

Every morning, Melody sang her happiest songs. "Tweet-tweet-trill!" she chirped. The sweet sounds filled the room like musical sunshine.

But today was different. Today, Melody felt worried.

Little Emma and her brother Sam were playing with their toys on the fluffy carpet. Emma had her favorite dolls. Sam had his wooden blocks. But they were not sharing nicely.

"That's MY doll!" Emma shouted, pulling hard on the toy.

"No! I want to play with it!" Sam yelled back, tugging the other way.

The children's loud voices made Melody's heart feel heavy. The room that was usually filled with laughter now felt upset and angry.

Melody watched from her perch. The soft morning light didn't feel warm anymore. The colorful toys scattered on the floor looked messy and sad.

"I need to help," Melody said to herself. "But how?"

Melody thought and thought. Her little bird brain worked hard. She remembered how beautiful music always made everyone feel better.

"I know!" Melody chirped excitedly. "I'll sing a special song!"

Melody took a deep breath. She puffed out her tiny chest. Then she began to sing the most peaceful, gentle song she knew.

"La-la-tweet, la-la-trill,
When we're angry, we can still
Take deep breaths and count to three,
Then we'll play so happily!"

Emma and Sam stopped pulling. They looked up at Melody's cage. The bird's sweet voice filled the room like a warm hug.

"Sharing makes our hearts feel bright,
Taking turns feels just so right,
When we're kind to those we love,
We're as happy as doves above!"

Emma let go of the doll. Sam sat down on the soft carpet. They both listened to Melody's beautiful song.

"Melody's right," Emma said softly. "We should share."

"I'm sorry I grabbed your doll," Sam said. "Can we play together?"

Emma smiled. Her face lit up like sunshine. "Yes! You can play with my doll, and I'll help you build with your blocks."

Melody felt so proud. Her little heart swelled with joy. The room felt peaceful again. The morning light seemed brighter. The colorful toys looked happy scattered on the carpet.

The children played together nicely. They took turns with the doll. They built a tall tower with the blocks. They giggled and shared their snacks.

"Thank you, Melody!" Emma called up to the cage. "Your song helped us remember to be kind."

"You're the best singing bird ever!" Sam added with a big grin.

Melody fluffed her bright feathers proudly. She had learned something very important today. When people have problems, sometimes all they need is a gentle reminder to be peaceful and kind.

From her perch, Melody could see the whole living room. The soft couch, the warm sunlight, and two children playing happily together. Everything felt just right.

"Tweet-tweet-trill!" Melody sang one more happy song. "Being peaceful feels so good!"

The End.
