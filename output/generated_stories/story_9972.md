# Tiko Sings in the Laundry Room

Tiko was a small singing bird with bright blue feathers and a warm, happy song. One sunny morning, Tiko fluttered into the laundry room to help. The room smelled like clean soap. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer clicked, “Clack, clack.” Warm air puffed out like a soft sigh.

“I will sing while I help!” Tiko chirped. His little feet tapped on the tile. “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo!”

Inside the laundry room, neighbors came and went. Mrs. Owl folded towels. Mr. Squirrel sorted socks. Baby Bunny hopped in circles, pat-pat-pat. Everyone was busy in the bright, cozy room.

Tiko saw a big basket full of tiny socks. “I can match the socks,” he said. “And I can sing a helping song!”

He took a deep breath. “Tweet-tweet, match and meet, little socks go two by two!” His song made Baby Bunny giggle. “Again! Again!” Baby Bunny cheered.

Just then, the washing machine beeped. “Beep-beep.”

“It’s my turn to wash my clothes,” said Mr. Squirrel, holding a small bag of shirts.

But Mrs. Owl blinked her big eyes. “Hoo-hoo, I am next,” she said kindly. “I have my towels ready.”

Baby Bunny’s ears wiggled. “My turn! My turn!” Baby Bunny bounced, thump-thump-thump.

Everyone looked at the single washing machine. Only one load could go in. The machine hummed, ready and waiting.

Tiko paused his song. He felt a little twist in his tummy. He wanted everyone to feel happy. He also wanted to keep helping. He thought, and his feathers fluffed as he used his best thinking.

“We can take turns,” Tiko said gently. “Taking turns means we wait and each person gets a turn. It is fair and kind.”

Baby Bunny’s nose twitched. “But I want my turn now,” Bunny said, with a tiny frown.

Tiko nodded. “I know. Waiting can be hard. Let’s use gentle minds and kind hearts.” He took another deep breath and sang softly, “Wait and share, show you care.”

He looked at the laundry. He used logical thinking. “Let’s make a plan,” Tiko said. “Who has the fastest wash? Small loads go first. Then bigger loads. That way, we all move along.”

Mrs. Owl nodded. “Hoo-hoo, my towels are big. I can wait.”

Mr. Squirrel looked at his bag. “My shirts are small,” he said. “They will be quick.”

Baby Bunny held a tiny bundle. “Mine is teeny,” Bunny whispered.

Tiko clapped his wings. “Great! First Baby Bunny, then Mr. Squirrel, then Mrs. Owl. We will take turns. While we wait, we can help each other. That is community service. We help our neighbors.”

The plan felt cozy, like a warm towel hug. The laundry room seemed brighter.

Baby Bunny put the tiny clothes in. “Plop, plop.” The washer whirred. Tiko sang a patient song. “Swish and spin, we all win.”

While they waited, Tiko helped Mrs. Owl fold towels. “Fold, fold, smooth and neat.” He pecked at a corner and made a neat square. Mrs. Owl smiled. “Thank you, Tiko.”

Mr. Squirrel found lonely socks. “I will help match,” he said. “Helping feels good.”

Baby Bunny’s load finished fast. “Beep-beep!” Baby Bunny clapped. “Your turn, Mr. Squirrel!”

Mr. Squirrel smiled and bowed. “Thank you for taking turns.” He put in his shirts. “Plop, plop.”

As the shirts swished, Baby Bunny helped Tiko carry the clean socks to a basket. The socks smelled like sunshine and flowers. “Mmm,” said Tiko. “So fresh!”

They heard a soft chirp from the hallway. A shy little mouse peeked in, holding a wet blanket. “Excuse me,” the mouse squeaked. “I had a spill. I don’t want to make a fuss.”

Tiko tilted his head. He remembered peaceful solutions. He remembered being open-minded. “Friends,” he said, “can we be kind and think again?”

Mrs. Owl blinked. “Hoo-hoo?”

Tiko spoke softly. “The mouse has an emergency. The blanket is wet and cold. Maybe we can let the mouse go next. It is a caring choice.”

Mr. Squirrel listened. He took a breath. “I can wait a little,” he said. “Kindness first.”

Baby Bunny nodded. “We can share,” Bunny said.

When Mr. Squirrel’s shirts were done, the mouse put the blanket in. “Thank you,” the mouse whispered. “I will be quick.” The washer hummed happily.

While they waited again, they cleaned up the laundry room together. Mr. Squirrel swept. Swish-swish. Baby Bunny put lost buttons in a little jar. Click, click. Mrs. Owl wiped the counter. Wipe-wipe. Tiko sang a tidy tune that sounded like bubbles popping. “Pop-pop, clean up, stop and mop!”

Soon the mouse’s blanket was warm and dry. “Thank you,” the mouse said, wrapping up like a cozy burrito. “You all helped me. I feel safe.”

At last, it was Mrs. Owl’s turn. She loaded her big towels. The washer sang its little beep when it was done. Everyone cheered.

Tiko fluffed his feathers and felt proud. They had made a plan. They had been patient. They had helped the community. They had used peaceful, open minds.

Baby Bunny hugged Tiko. “Taking turns made it fair,” Bunny said.

Mr. Squirrel nodded. “And helping each other made it fast.”

Mrs. Owl added, “Hoo-hoo, and it made my heart warm.”

Tiko sang a final song, soft and sweet. “Share the time, take your turn, kindness is what we all learn.”

The laundry room smelled fresh and sounded calm. The floor was clean. The baskets were full. Everyone smiled.

Today’s lesson was clear: When we take turns, use gentle thinking, and help our neighbors, everyone feels happy and safe.

The End.
