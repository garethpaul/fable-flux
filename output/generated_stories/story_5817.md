# Ready, Set, Rabbit!

Tilly was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, busy eyes. She loved to hop fast and finish things first. Today, Tilly had a big job to do in the garden shed.

Grandma Rabbit gave Tilly a list. “We need to plant seeds before the sun goes down,” Grandma said kindly. “First, sweep the shed. Second, find the seed packets. Third, line up the tools. Then we will plant together.”

Tilly wiggled her nose. “I can do it!” she said. Her ears stood tall. She wanted to show Grandma she could follow directions and use her time well.

The garden shed was cool and smelled like fresh soil and pine wood. Dusty sunbeams floated in the air. Shelves held pots, gloves, and shiny watering cans. A broom leaned against the wall. Tiny seeds rustled in paper packets on a high shelf.

Tilly looked at the list. “Sweep, find, line up,” she said. “Easy!”

Her feet itched to hop. She wanted to start planting right away. “Planting is the fun part,” she whispered. “Maybe I can do that first.”

Tilly grabbed a little shovel. “I’ll just do a tiny plant now,” she said. She dug in a pot. The dry dirt made a soft scritch-scritch sound. But then—oops! The pot tipped. Soil slid like brown sugar onto the floor.

Tilly’s eyes grew wide. “Oh no,” she said. “Now it’s messy.”

Grandma peeked in. “How is it going, dear?” she asked.

Tilly’s ears drooped. “I wanted to plant first,” she said. “But I made a mess. I didn’t follow the list.”

Grandma smiled. “Lists help us use time well,” she said. “When we do first things first, the rest is easier.”

Tilly took a deep breath. She felt a little flutter in her tummy. “I want to try again,” she said. “I can be an on-time, in-order rabbit.”

“Good plan,” said Grandma. “I’ll check in soon.”

Tilly picked up the broom. She held the list in one paw. “Step one: sweep,” she said. “Sweep, sweep, sweep!”

She swept the floor in neat lines. The broom made a soft whoosh-whoosh. Dust puffed like tiny clouds, and then it all went into the pan. Tilly hummed a little tune. “Sweep it up, tidy up, hop-hop-hooray!”

When the floor was clean, Tilly smiled. She smelled the clean wood. She felt proud inside. “Step two: find the seed packets,” she said.

She looked with her eyes, slow and careful. She did not jump around. She checked the shelf with the pots. “No seeds here.” She checked the hook with the gloves. “No seeds here.” She checked the high shelf. “There!” She saw pretty packets with bright pictures—carrots, lettuce, and little red radish dots.

Tilly reached up. She stood on a sturdy box. “I am taking my time,” she said. “I am being safe.” She lifted the packets down. They made a soft papery crinkle. “Seeds found!”

“Step three: line up the tools,” she said. Tilly placed the small shovel, the big shovel, and the rake in a straight line. Clink, clink, clink. She hung the watering can on its hook. She put gloves in a basket. She made labels and stuck them on a shelf: “Seeds,” “Gloves,” “Tools.” She liked the neat labels. They made her feel calm.

Grandma Rabbit came back. Her whiskers twitched with joy. “It looks wonderful,” she said. “You followed directions and saved time. Now everything is easy to find.”

Tilly’s tail gave a happy wiggle. “Now we plant?”

“Now we plant,” Grandma said.

They carried the seeds outside. The evening air felt cool and sweet. Birds chirped soft bedtime songs. Tilly pressed tiny carrot seeds into the soil. Pat, pat, pat. She set little radish seeds in a row. She watered with a gentle pour. Tippy, tippy, tip.

As they worked, Tilly checked the list again. “We did it in order,” she said. “First we cleaned. Then we found seeds. Then we lined up tools. Now we plant. It all fits.”

Grandma nodded. “You managed your time. You stayed on task. You followed directions. You got organized. That is how small steps make a big job feel simple.”

Tilly looked at the tidy shed and the neat garden rows. Her heart felt full and light. The sky turned pink and gold like a warm blanket. She listened to the soft drip of water and smelled the fresh, earthy soil.

“I learned something,” Tilly said. “When I go slow and do first things first, I finish faster. Lists help me. Labels help me. I can be a time-smart rabbit.”

Grandma hugged her. “Yes, little one. You are a determined rabbit who plans, follows, and finishes.”

Tilly tucked the list in a small tin on the shelf labeled “Lists.” She giggled. “Tomorrow, I will make a new list,” she said. “First: check tools. Second: water plants. Third: snack time!”

Grandma laughed. “A perfect plan.”

Tilly hopped once, twice, three times. “Ready, set, rabbit!” she cheered.

And this time, everything felt just right.

The End.
