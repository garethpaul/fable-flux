# Ollie Otter's Green Day

Ollie the otter loved his cozy nursery. The soft yellow walls were covered with pictures of fish and seashells. His favorite blue blanket lay crumpled on the floor next to yesterday's toys.

"Oh my!" said Ollie, looking around. "What a mess I made!"

The morning sun streamed through the window, making dust particles dance in the air. Ollie's whiskers twitched as he sniffed the fresh air coming through the open window.

Mama Otter peeked her head through the doorway. "Good morning, little one! Ready to help make our world a better place today?"

"Yes, Mama!" Ollie clapped his small paws together. "But how?"

"Well," Mama said with a warm smile, "let's start right here in your room. See all these toys on the floor?"

Ollie nodded, his dark eyes scanning the scattered blocks, books, and stuffed animals.

"Instead of throwing them away when we're tired of them, what could we do?" Mama asked gently.

Ollie thought hard, his little nose wrinkling. "Um... give them to friends who need toys?"

"Exactly!" Mama's eyes sparkled. "That's called sharing and reusing. It helps our Earth stay healthy and clean."

Together, they sorted through Ollie's toys. The blocks that were too easy went into a "share" box. The books he'd outgrown joined them.

"Look, Mama!" Ollie held up a torn teddy bear. "Mr. Buttons has a hole in his tummy."

"Should we throw him away?" Mama asked.

"No way!" Ollie hugged the bear tight. "Can we fix him?"

Mama brought her sewing kit. "Of course! Fixing things instead of throwing them away is another way to help our planet."

As Mama stitched, Ollie organized his remaining toys in the wooden toy chest. The smooth wood felt warm under his paws.

"What about these old drawings?" Ollie asked, pointing to a pile of colorful papers.

"The backs are still blank," Mama observed. "What could we do with them?"

Ollie's eyes lit up. "Draw new pictures on the other side!"

"Perfect! That's called reusing, sweetie."

Next, they tackled Ollie's clothes. Some were too small now.

"These can go to baby otters who need them," Mama explained as they folded the tiny shirts and pants.

Ollie felt proud watching the "share" pile grow bigger. "We're helping other families!"

"Yes, and helping Earth too," Mama said. "When we share and reuse things, we don't need to make as many new ones. That keeps our rivers and forests cleaner."

For lunch, they used both sides of their napkins and saved leftover fish for dinner. Ollie even helped wash their dishes instead of using paper plates.

"I'm being a helper!" Ollie giggled as soap bubbles tickled his whiskers.

That afternoon, they delivered the toy box to the otter family next door. Little Luna Otter squealed with joy when she saw the blocks.

"Thank you, Ollie!" she chirped. "These are perfect for building castles!"

Walking home, Ollie felt warm and fuzzy inside. "Mama, sharing feels really good."

"It does, doesn't it?" Mama squeezed his paw. "When we take care of our things and share with others, we're taking care of our whole world."

That night, as Ollie snuggled into his freshly made bed, he looked around his tidy nursery. Everything had a place, and nothing was wasted.

"Tomorrow, can we find more ways to help Earth?" he yawned.

"Every day, little one," Mama whispered, kissing his forehead. "Every single day."

The End.
