# Nutkin's Rooftop Adventure

Nutkin was a clever gray squirrel with a big fluffy tail and bright black eyes. She lived in a tall oak tree next to Mrs. Chen's house. Every morning, she loved to explore the red rooftop that sparkled in the sunshine.

"What a beautiful day to discover something new!" Nutkin chittered excitedly.

She scampered across the smooth tiles, feeling the warm sun on her fur. The rooftop smelled like fresh morning air and blooming flowers from the garden below.

Suddenly, Nutkin heard a tiny voice. "Help! Help me, please!"

She looked around with her sharp eyes. There, stuck between two loose roof tiles, was a small blue bird with a hurt wing.

"Oh no! You're trapped!" Nutkin said, running over quickly.

The little bird looked scared. "I tried to build my nest here, but I got stuck. I'm too afraid to move!"

Nutkin wanted to help right away, but she noticed something important. A big crow was sitting on the chimney nearby, watching them with mean eyes.

"Hey, little squirrel!" the crow cawed loudly. "That's MY rooftop! Go away and leave that bird alone!"

Nutkin's heart beat fast. The crow was much bigger than her. But she looked at the scared little bird and felt brave inside.

"No!" Nutkin said in her strongest voice, standing up tall. "This bird needs help, and I'm going to help her! You can't stop me from being kind!"

The crow flapped his big black wings. "You're too small to tell ME what to do!"

But Nutkin didn't back down. She puffed out her chest and spoke clearly. "Being small doesn't mean I can't be brave! Everyone deserves help when they're scared."

Nutkin carefully used her tiny paws to move the loose tiles. *Scritch, scratch* went her claws as she worked. The tiles felt rough and warm under her paws.

"Almost there!" she told the bird gently. "You're going to be free!"

The crow watched, surprised. He had never seen such a small animal be so brave and determined.

With one final push, Nutkin freed the little bird! "There you go! Your wing looks better already."

The bird flapped her wings carefully. "Thank you so much, Nutkin! You were so brave!"

Something amazing happened then. The big crow flew down and landed nearby.

"I... I'm sorry," the crow said quietly. "I was wrong to be mean. You showed me that being kind is more important than being big."

Nutkin smiled warmly. "It's okay! We can all share this rooftop. There's room for everyone!"

From that day on, the rooftop became a special place where all the birds and Nutkin played together. The crow learned to be gentle, and the little bird built her nest safely nearby.

Nutkin discovered something wonderful: when you speak up for what's right, even if you're small, you can make big changes. She felt proud and happy as she watched all her new friends playing together in the sunshine.

"Speaking up for others feels even better than finding the biggest acorn!" Nutkin chittered joyfully.

The End.
