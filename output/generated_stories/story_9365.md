# Koa the Calm Koala Finds Brave

Koa the calm koala sat on the peaceful front porch of Nana Gum’s little yellow house. The porch smelled like warm eucalyptus tea. A soft breeze tickled Koa’s ears. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” and the wooden boards felt smooth under Koa’s furry toes.

Today was Porch Story Day, a special family tradition. Every week, Nana Gum told stories on the porch, and friends came to listen. Koa loved the stories. But today, Nana Gum had a surprise.

“Koa,” Nana Gum said in her gentle voice, “it’s your turn to share a story today.”

Koa’s tummy did a tiny flip. “My turn?” Koa whispered. “I feel wiggly inside.”

“That’s okay,” Nana Gum smiled. “Feeling wiggly can be the first step to being brave.”

Koa looked down the path. Friends were coming. Pip the tiny possum skipped. Lila the bright lorikeet fluttered. Benji the bandicoot bounced, and Tess the turtle took careful steps. They carried little cups for tea and crunchy leaf cookies for dipping.

“Hi, Koa!” Pip squeaked. “I can’t wait for your story.”

Koa’s paws felt warm and sweaty. “Hi,” Koa said softly. “I’m… I’m thinking.”

Lila landed on the porch rail. “Your porch smells like lemon,” she said, flapping her wings. “I like lemon.”

Benji sniffed the air. “Mmm! Cookies!”

Tess smiled slowly. “I brought a shell pebble for good luck.”

Koa held the pebble. It felt cool and smooth. “Thank you,” Koa said. “I like the way it feels.”

Nana Gum poured tea that smelled fresh and sweet. “Friends,” she said, “today Koa will share a family story. It is our tradition to pass stories from heart to heart.”

Koa’s heart thumped, thump-thump, like a little drum. “What if my voice is too quiet?” Koa asked.

Nana Gum put a warm paw on Koa’s shoulder. “Courage is not a loud roar,” she said. “Courage is a small voice that says, ‘I will try.’”

Koa took a tiny breath. The breeze was cool on Koa’s nose. A leaf brushed Koa’s cheek. The porch boards creaked a friendly creak. “I will try,” Koa whispered.

Pip leaned close. “We’re right here,” he said.

Lila chirped, “I love stories!”

Benji munched a cookie with a happy crunch. “Me too!”

Tess nodded. “Slow and steady.”

Koa began. “This is a story my Nana told me. Long ago, a little koala wanted to climb the tallest gum tree to see the sunrise. The koala felt wiggly, just like me. But the koala took one small step, then one more. The koala said, ‘I will try.’”

Koa paused and looked at friends’ bright eyes. The porch felt safe and warm. Koa’s tummy settled, like a calm pond. “The koala climbed and climbed,” Koa said. “Leaves smelled fresh. Wind sang soft. At the top, the sky turned pink and gold. The koala felt brave.”

Pip clapped his tiny hands. “Pink and gold! Wow!”

Lila fluffed her feathers. “I can almost see it.”

Benji wiggled. “I can almost taste the wind.”

Tess placed the pebble by Koa’s knee. “You are doing it.”

Koa smiled. “The little koala came down and told the story on a porch, just like this one. Sharing the story made the brave feeling grow bigger. The end.”

Nana Gum’s eyes sparkled. “Beautiful, Koa. You shared our tradition with courage.”

Koa let out a happy breath. “I did it,” Koa said. “My voice was small, but it was mine.”

Pip scooted closer. “Sometimes my tummy feels wiggly too,” he said. “When I jump from a low branch.”

“I feel shy when I sing,” Lila added. “But I sing anyway.”

Benji nodded. “I get nervous when I try new cookies,” he said, and everyone giggled.

Tess tapped the pebble. “I move slowly, and that is brave for me.”

Koa’s heart felt warm and wide. “Maybe we can make a new porch tradition,” Koa said. “We can each share one small brave thing.”

Nana Gum clapped softly. “Yes. Courage grows when we share it.”

Pip stood and said, “Today I will try the wobbly step. I will say, ‘I will try!’”

Lila chirped, “I will sing one soft song.”

Benji took a teeny bite of a new cookie with nuts. “Crunch! I tried it!”

Tess lifted her chin. “I will greet a neighbor with a big hello.”

Everyone cheered. The porch sounded like happy bells.

Koa hugged Nana Gum. “Thank you for the story,” Koa said. “Thank you for the courage.”

Nana Gum hugged back. “Courage lives in you,” she whispered. “Traditions help it shine.”

The sun warmed the porch. The tea cups felt smooth and cozy in their hands. The air tasted like lemon and leaves. Friends laughed and listened. They took turns. They shared.

Koa looked at the pebble, at friends, at Nana Gum. “Today I learned,” Koa said, “that courage is trying, even when I feel wiggly. Sharing stories helps us be brave together. And friends make brave feel bigger.”

Pip smiled. “We are brave together.”

Lila sang a soft, sweet note. Benji crunched another cookie. Tess gave a slow, proud thumbs-up.

Koa sat, calm and happy, on the peaceful front porch. The breeze hummed. The boards creaked. Hearts felt light.

And the small voice inside Koa said, “I will try,” and everyone could hear it.

The End.
