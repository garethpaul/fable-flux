# Leo's Colorful Sports Day

Leo the little lion cub walked slowly into the bright art room. Colorful paintbrushes lined the walls, and paper covered every table. Today was special - they were making posters for Sports Day! All the animal friends were excited, but Leo felt worried.

"I'm not good at sports," Leo whispered to himself. His golden fur drooped sadly. He watched Elephant Emma painting a big soccer ball. Rabbit Ruby drew herself jumping high. Turtle Tommy painted himself swimming fast.

"What sport will you paint, Leo?" asked Mrs. Owl, their kind teacher. Her feathers rustled softly as she walked over.

Leo's ears drooped. "I don't know. I'm too slow to run fast like Ruby. I'm not big and strong like Emma. I can't swim like Tommy."

Mrs. Owl's wise eyes sparkled. "Leo, what do you love to do?"

Leo thought hard. "I like to roar! And I love helping my friends when they fall down."

"Those sound like wonderful talents!" Mrs. Owl said warmly. "Let's think about sports that use those skills."

Leo picked up a bright red paintbrush. The soft bristles felt good in his paw. He dipped it in sunny yellow paint. "Maybe I could try cheerleading? I could roar to cheer everyone on!"

"What a fantastic idea!" Mrs. Owl clapped her wings.

Leo's brush moved across the white paper. He painted himself with a big smile, standing tall with pom-poms. His painted roar looked like golden sunbeams coming from his mouth. Around him, he painted all his friends playing different sports.

"Look!" Leo called to his friends. "I'm going to be the loudest, most encouraging cheerleader ever!"

Emma trumpeted happily. "That's perfect, Leo! Your roars always make me feel brave!"

Ruby hopped over. "And you always help us up when we trip. That's what good teammates do!"

Tommy nodded slowly. "Being kind is the most important sport skill of all."

Leo felt warm happiness spread through his chest. His tail swished with excitement. He added more details to his poster - showing himself helping a friend who fell, and cheering for everyone.

Mrs. Owl hung Leo's poster next to all the others. "Class, look at Leo's wonderful discovery. He found his own special way to be part of Sports Day!"

Leo stood tall and proud. His golden mane seemed to shine brighter. "I learned that everyone has different talents. I don't have to be the fastest or strongest. I can use my loud roar and kind heart!"

On Sports Day, Leo wore a bright blue cheerleading outfit. He roared encouragement for every single friend. When someone felt tired, Leo's cheers gave them energy. When someone fell down, Leo was right there to help them up.

"Leo! Leo! He's our guy! If he can't cheer, no one can try!" all the animals chanted together.

Leo beamed with pride. His roar echoed across the playground like happy thunder. He had found his perfect way to shine at sports - by lifting others up and sharing his natural gifts.

That night, Leo painted a new picture. It showed all the different animals doing what they loved best. Everyone was different, and everyone was special.

"Being yourself is the best way to be," Leo said happily, his paintbrush dancing across the paper.

The End.
