# Bubbles Learns to Listen

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a happy smile. Bubbles loved to splash, wiggle, and flip. Today, Bubbles visited a cozy study corner. The study corner had a soft rug like sea foam, a small table, and shelves of books that smelled like paper and sunshine. A little globe sat on the table, blue and green like the ocean and the land. A warm lamp glowed like a tiny sun.

“Eee-eee!” squeaked Bubbles, tail swishing with glee. “Let’s learn and play!”

In the study corner sat Maya and Theo, two friends with crayons, gentle voices, and big curious eyes. They were drawing pictures of places around the world. Maya drew tall mountains. Theo drew sandy deserts. On the globe, they traced a finger from the blue ocean to the green land.

Bubbles swam in the air with pretend waves, making soft splashes with flippers. “Look at me flip!” Bubbles sang, spinning in a circle. The lamp made a warm circle of light on the rug. It felt cozy and safe.

But when Bubbles flipped, the crayons rolled. “Oops!” said Maya, steadying her paper. “We’re drawing, Bubbles.”

Bubbles giggled. “I’m just playing!” The flippers swished again. The globe wobbled a little. The pencils clinked and clicked on the table.

Theo looked up and said kindly, “We like your flips. But please be gentle. This is our study corner. We need quiet hands and quiet tails.”

Bubbles paused and then did one more little flip. The globe tipped—wobble, wobble—then thumped softly back into place. Maya’s mountain picture got a tiny smudge.

Bubbles saw Maya’s tiny frown. The room felt still. The lamp hummed softly. Bubbles took a deep breath and felt the cool air tickle a dolphin nose. “I… I didn’t mean to bump your art,” Bubbles said. “I’m sorry.”

Maya’s face brightened. “Thank you for saying sorry,” she said. “We can all help fix it.”

Theo nodded. “We can use an eraser. And Bubbles, can you help us learn about places on the globe? You are from the big blue ocean!”

Bubbles’ heart felt warm, like the lamp glow. “Yes! I can help. And I can move my body in a quiet way.”

Maya pointed to a big blue patch. “Is this your home?”

Bubbles touched the globe gently with a fin. “The ocean is very big. It touches many lands. That means people everywhere share the same water. We take care of it together.” Bubbles spoke softly, like wave whispers. “We show respect to others by using gentle moves and caring words.”

Theo smiled. “Respect means we think about other people’s feelings.”

Bubbles nodded. “Yes. And we can still move and exercise! Let’s try quiet flips.”

They made a new plan. It was Exercise Time in the study corner. They rolled the rug edges flat. The lamp shone warm. The crayons sat in a cup, not rolling anymore.

Bubbles said, “Let’s do dolphin breaths.” Everyone breathed in through their noses like smelling a sweet flower. They breathed out with slow bubbles—pffff. It sounded soft and cozy.

“Now gentle tail swishes,” Bubbles said, swaying side to side. Maya and Theo copied, swishing like sea grass. The swishes made a whisper sound, shhh-shhh. It felt calm and fun.

“Next, wave arms,” Bubbles said. “Up, down. Up, down.” Their arms went whoosh whoosh, but stayed in their own space. No bumping. No wobbling globe.

They giggled. “This is a quiet ocean dance!” Maya said.

“Let’s add a listening game,” suggested Theo. He tapped the table twice—tap tap. “When you hear two taps, freeze.”

Bubbles listened carefully. Tap tap. Freeze! Everyone held still like statues. They could feel their hearts beating softly—thump-thump—inside their chests.

Tap tap. Move again! Swish, whoosh, giggle. Tap tap. Freeze! Bubbles kept the tail close, respecting the space. It felt good to be careful and kind.

After the exercise, they sat down. The room smelled like crayons and a little bit like apples from a snack bowl. Their cheeks were warm. Their bodies felt strong and calm.

“Thank you for helping us keep the study corner safe,” said Maya.

“Thank you for reminding me to be gentle,” said Bubbles. “I learned to respect your space. I can have fun and still be careful.”

Theo pointed at the globe again. “All around the world, people learn and play. Everyone needs respect.”

Bubbles nodded. “Yes. In the ocean, dolphins swim together. We watch our fins. We look with kind eyes. We say sorry when we splash too big. That helps our pod feel happy.”

Maya fixed her mountain smudge with tiny strokes. “Your apology helped me,” she said.

Bubbles smiled. “Your kind words helped me learn.” Bubbles looked at the study corner. The lamp was steady, the globe was steady, and the friends were steady too.

They made a new sign for the study corner together. It said:
- Quiet bodies
- Kind words
- Gentle moves
- Listen and learn

They drew little pictures: a small ear, a heart, soft feet, a globe with blue and green. They taped the sign on the wall. It looked bright and friendly.

Bubbles did one last tiny flip—just a little “plip”—far from the table. “Respect means we care for each other,” Bubbles said. “When we care, we learn better, we move better, and we feel better.”

Maya and Theo clapped softly—pat pat. “Yes,” they said. “We can do it every day!”

The study corner felt peaceful, like a quiet shore. The friends smiled, ready for more adventures, with gentle bodies and kind, respectful hearts.

The End.
