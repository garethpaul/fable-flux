# Fern the Fox Kit Waits for the Wiggle Spoon

Fern was a curious fox kit with soft orange fur and bright, watchful eyes. Today, she padded into the dining room on quiet paws. The table smelled warm and yummy—like baked carrots, sweet apples, and oat bread. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. The chairs looked tall and shiny, like sleepy trees.

Fern wiggled her nose. “Mmm,” she said. “I’m hungry now.”

Mama Fox set out four small bowls and a big wooden spoon. The spoon had a smooth handle and a round, friendly face carved on top. Fern liked to call it the Wiggle Spoon.

“Dinner is almost ready,” Mama Fox said. “We will take turns and stir the soup three times each.”

Fern’s tail flick-flicked. Taking turns sounded slow. Her tummy made a tiny growl, like a little drum. “Can I stir first? Can I? Please?”

Mama smiled. “We will stir in order. First Papa, then Fern, then Kit Brother, and last me. Slow and steady, gentle and ready.”

Fern bounced on her toes. The spoon looked so shiny and fun. She wanted to grab it now. She reached out—but Papa Fox picked up the spoon and began to stir. Swish, swish, swish.

Fern’s ears drooped. Waiting was hard. The soup bubbled softly—blub, blub—and smelled like carrots and thyme. Steam tickled her whiskers.

“I want my turn,” Fern whispered.

Papa passed the spoon to Fern. “Your turn, little one,” he said.

Fern held the spoon. It felt warm and smooth in her paws. “I can do it!” she said. She dipped the spoon deep and stirred fast—too fast! Soup splashed over the side and made a hot dot on the table.

“Ow!” Fern pulled back. Her eyes got big. “It’s hard,” she said, feeling a little scared and a little sad.

Mama put a napkin over the dot and dabbed it up. “It’s okay,” Mama said in a soft voice. “We try again. Let’s use patience. Patience is like a gentle breath.”

“How?” Fern asked.

Mama breathed in slow. “Breathe in… and out.” She smiled. “Wait and watch. Slow paws. Feel the spoon. Count to three.”

Fern nodded. She took a slow breath. “In… and out.” The warm air smelled like apples. She held the spoon again. She felt the weight, the warm handle, the gentle pull of the soup. She stirred slow. One… two… three. Swish, swish, swish. No splash!

“I did it!” Fern’s tail made a happy curl.

“Good coordination,” Papa said. “Your paws and eyes and breath all worked together.”

Kit Brother clapped. “My turn!” He took the spoon and copied Fern—slow and steady, counting to three. Then Mama stirred last. The soup bubbled happy and ready.

They sat at the table. “Before we eat,” Mama said, “let’s set our napkins and cups.”

Fern jumped up to help. She reached for the stack of cloth napkins. They were soft and smelled like sunshine. She liked how they were not throw-away. “We wash and use again,” Fern said.

“That’s right,” Mama said. “We care for our home and the earth. That is sustainability. We use things again, and we don’t waste.”

Fern spread the napkins carefully—one at each place. She lined up the wooden cups. Her paws moved slow and sure. “This is like stirring,” she said. “Slow and steady.”

“Patience helps with many things,” Papa said. “It keeps us safe and kind.”

They all tasted the soup. It was sweet and warm and just right. Fern smiled big. She felt proud—and cozy.

Then she saw the bowl of oat bread. The slices were thick and golden. Fern wanted the biggest piece right now. Her paws reached out fast.

Mama touched her paw gently. “Remember patience.”

Fern sat back. She took a small breath. “In… and out,” she said softly. “We take turns.”

Papa passed the bread to Mama. Mama passed it to Fern. Fern picked a slice and then passed the bowl to Kit Brother. “Your turn,” she said with a grin.

“Thank you,” Kit Brother said.

After dinner, there were little crumb trails and tiny splashes on the table. Fern wanted to run and play, but Mama brought a small cloth and a wooden brush.

“We can all help,” Mama said. “We clean, and we save. No paper towels today.”

Fern felt a wiggle in her legs. She wanted to go fast. But she remembered the spoon. She breathed in and out. She wiped in small circles with the cloth. Wipe, wipe, wipe. The table got shiny again. Kit Brother brushed crumbs into a bowl for the compost.

“What is compost?” Fern asked.

“It’s a special place for food scraps to go back to the earth,” Papa said. “It helps new plants grow.”

Fern carried the small bowl carefully. She moved slow, eyes watching, paws steady. She felt calm. She felt strong.

When all was clean, Mama hugged Fern. “You were patient and careful. You used good coordination. You helped the earth.”

Fern’s eyes sparkled. “Patience makes things better,” she said. “It helps me stir, share, and clean.”

Papa nodded. “And it helps us enjoy the moment.”

Fern curled her tail around her feet. She could smell a last soft sweetness in the room. The dining room felt warm and bright. Her tummy was happy. Her heart was too.

“I can wait, I can watch, I can try,” Fern said in a small, proud voice. “Slow and steady, gentle and ready.”

The End.
