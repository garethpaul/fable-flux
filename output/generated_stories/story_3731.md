# The Song of the Colorful Clues

Chirp was a tiny singing bird with bright blue feathers and a warm, round belly. Chirp loved the creative art room. It smelled like fresh paper and orange peel soap. It looked like a rainbow, with jars of paint, soft brushes, and glitter that sparkled like stars. The room hummed with gentle music, and Chirp hummed too.

“Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo! I sing while I paint for you!” Chirp sang, tapping the table with a tiny claw.

Today, the art room had a big sign: “Make a Friendship Mural!” The mural would be a giant picture that everyone could add to. There were children with smocks, a sleepy cat named Pudding, and a kind teacher named Miss Lina. On the wall, there was a long white paper waiting for color.

Chirp fluttered down to a soft paintbrush. “I will paint the sky,” Chirp chirped happily.

Miss Lina smiled. “Wonderful, Chirp. Remember, we share and we listen.”

Chirp dipped the brush into blue paint. Swish, swish. It felt cool and smooth. Chirp sang a gentle song, “Blue sky, blue day, sing and paint and play.”

Just then, a child named Milo reached for the same blue paint. “I need blue for my ocean,” Milo said.

Chirp’s feathers puffed. “But I had it first! I want to paint now!”

Milo frowned. “I want to paint now too.”

Pudding the cat gave a soft meow and curled her tail in a question mark. The room grew quiet. Chirp’s tummy felt tight. Chirp didn’t like this feeling. It was like a knot.

Miss Lina knelt down. “We have a problem,” she said softly. “Two artists need the same color. How can we solve it?”

Chirp took a small breath. “Tweet… I don’t know,” Chirp whispered. Chirp wanted to be heard. Chirp wanted to be understood.

Miss Lina nodded. “Let’s understand each other. First, let’s listen. Milo, tell us how you feel.”

Milo looked at his shoes. “I feel sad. I want to make waves. The ocean is my favorite.”

Miss Lina looked at Chirp. “Chirp, tell us how you feel.”

Chirp fluffed a wing. “I feel wiggly and tight. I want to paint the sky. Singing helps me paint.”

Miss Lina smiled. “Thank you. We listened. Now let’s think. What are our clues?”

Chirp blinked. “Clues?”

“Yes,” Miss Lina said. “Clues help us use our thinking. The clues are: You both need blue. The mural is big. We share this room. We want everyone to feel welcome.”

Chirp looked at the long paper. It stretched like a road. Big. Very big. Chirp felt the tight knot loosen just a little.

“Tweet,” Chirp said slowly. “Maybe we can both use blue… but how?”

Milo peeked over. “We could make a sky and an ocean that touch.”

Chirp tilted his head. “Sky can be up… ocean can be down.”

Miss Lina clapped softly. “That is smart thinking. You used your clues and made a plan.”

Chirp and Milo set the paint between them. Chirp dipped the brush. Swish, swish. Milo dipped his brush too. Swish, swish. The sound was like gentle rain. Chirp began to sing again, softer this time. “Share the blue, share the view. Sky above, ocean blue.”

Milo smiled. “I like your song.”

“I can sing slower if you need to think,” Chirp offered. “Does my singing bother you?”

Milo shook his head. “No. Your singing helps me feel calm.”

Chirp’s heart felt warm. Chirp had asked and listened. Chirp felt understood, and Chirp understood Milo too.

They painted together. The blue of the sky met the blue of the ocean. Chirp added a fluffy white cloud with dabs that felt like soft cotton. Milo added a boat with a little red flag. Pudding the cat batted a dry brush and made a tiny tapping sound, tap-tap-tap.

Other children came to the mural. Sunita painted yellow sunbeams that tickled Chirp’s beak with the smell of lemon paint. Jamal made green seaweed that swayed like grass. Maya added purple birds that looked like smiles.

Chirp looked around. Everyone was included. Everyone had space. Everyone had a turn.

Then there was another problem. Jamal needed the red paint for a coral reef, but Milo was using it for the flag.

Chirp remembered the clues. Chirp sang, “Stop and listen, listen and think.”

Chirp asked, “Milo, how long do you need red?”

Milo said, “Just a little more.”

Chirp turned to Jamal. “Can you wait a tiny bit, or can we find another red?”

Jamal held up a pink bottle. “I can mix! Red and white make pink. But I want coral red.”

Chirp fluttered to the supply shelf. “Maybe there is another red.” Chirp used careful eyes. Tap, tap, tap went Chirp’s claws on the shelf. “Here!” Chirp found a second red bottle behind the markers.

“Great thinking, Chirp,” Miss Lina said. “You used clues, you looked, and you helped everyone.”

Soon the mural was full and bright. Sky, ocean, sun, boat, birds, seaweed, and a coral reef that looked like gentle fireworks. The art room smelled like paint and oranges. The music was soft. Hearts felt soft too.

Miss Lina hung a ribbon above the mural that said, “We Understand and Include.”

Chirp sang a happy ending song. “When we listen, when we share, we show each other that we care. Use your clues, take your time, solving problems works just fine.”

Milo pat the brush dry. “Thank you, Chirp.”

“Thank you, Milo,” Chirp chirped back. “We solved the problem together.”

They all stood and looked at the mural. It was big and kind, just like their feelings.

The lesson was clear: We can understand each other by listening, sharing, and using our thinking to solve problems. When we include everyone, the picture is more beautiful.

The End.
