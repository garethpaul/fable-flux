# Bubbles, Breaths, and Brave Little Hooves

Drip, drip, drip. The bathroom was warm and steamy. A gentle deer fawn named Fern stood on a soft bath mat. The mat felt squishy under her hooves. The air smelled like sweet soap. The mirror was foggy and soft like a cloud.

Fern’s big brown eyes watched a tower of colorful bath toys. The tower swayed. “Please stay up,” Fern whispered.

Plink! Plonk! The tower fell. Toys rolled and bumped. One toy hit her hoof. “Ouch!” Fern squeaked. Her heart beat fast. Her cheeks felt hot. Her little tail twitched.

“I tried so hard!” Fern stamped one tiny hoof. “I’m mad!” She wanted to kick the toys. She wanted to shout.

Just then, Fern heard a gentle sound. Swish, swish. Her mama was wiping the mirror with a towel. “I see big feelings,” Mama Deer said softly. “Would you like help?”

Fern puffed out her cheeks. “I’m mad, Mama. My tower crashed.” Her ears drooped low.

Mama Deer knelt beside Fern. “It’s okay to feel mad,” she said. “Feelings are not bad. Let’s try mindfulness. Mindfulness is paying kind attention to right now.”

Fern blinked. “Pay kind attention?” she asked.

“Yes,” Mama said. “We can listen, smell, look, and breathe. Ready?”

Fern nodded a tiny nod.

“First, listen,” Mama whispered.

Fern closed her eyes. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. Tink, tink went a toy against the tub. Swish, swish went the towel. Her breath sounded like a little breeze. “I hear the drips,” Fern said. Her shoulders felt softer.

“Now, smell,” Mama said.

Fern sniffed. Sniff, sniff. “I smell orange soap,” she giggled. “It smells like sunshine.”

“Now, look,” Mama said.

Fern opened her eyes. She saw bubbles in the sink. They looked like tiny moons. She saw the soft bath mat. She saw her toys waiting. “I see sparkly bubbles,” she smiled.

“Last, breathe,” Mama said. “We will do five bubble breaths. Breathe in slow. Breathe out slow, like blowing a bubble.”

Fern put a hoof on her chest. She breathed in through her nose. In... out. In... out. In... out. In... out. In... out. Her belly moved up and down like a boat on gentle waves.

“How do you feel?” Mama asked.

Fern wiggled her ears. “My mad is smaller,” she said. “It feels like a little pebble now, not a big rock.”

“That is mindfulness,” Mama said. “You noticed. You were kind. You breathed.”

Fern looked at the fallen toys. “I want to try again,” she said. “I can be patient.” She picked up each toy and lined them up. Tap, tap, tap. She stacked them slowly. One, two, three. The tower wobbled. Fern paused. She took one bubble breath. Whooo.

The tower stood tall. Fern’s eyes shined. “I did it!” she laughed.

“Bravo,” Mama cheered. “That is resilience. You kept trying.”

Fern looked around. The floor was wet. Some toys were under the sink. “The bathroom is messy,” Fern said. “We can help. We can do community service at home!”

Mama smiled. “Yes. Helping our home helps our family.”

Fern made a plan. “I will wipe. You will hold the trash bag.” She took a small cloth. Swish, swish. She wiped up little puddles. She picked up a wrapper and put it in the bag. She set the toys in a basket. Plunk, plunk, plunk. She sang, “Wipe, wipe, wipe. Help, help, help.”

The room began to sparkle. The mirror shone. The mat was dry. The basket was tidy. Fern felt proud. Warm pride glowed in her chest like a soft lantern.

“Thank you for your help,” Mama said. “How do we keep our calm if things fall again?”

Fern stood tall on her little hooves. “We listen. We smell. We look. We breathe. We take bubble breaths,” she said. “And we keep trying.”

Mama nodded. “And what about anger?”

Fern thought. “Anger is a feeling. It is not a boss,” she said. “I can notice it. I can breathe. I can choose kind actions.”

Mama hugged her. The hug felt like a warm blanket. “That is wise,” she said.

Fern touched the tower and grinned. She whispered, “Thank you, breaths.” She turned on the tap and washed her hooves. The water felt cool. She bubbled the soap. The foam tickled her fur. “Hello, bubbles,” she giggled. “Goodbye, grumps.”

She looked in the shiny mirror. “I am Fern,” she said. “I am mindful. I am brave. I can help.”

The bathroom was calm and clean. The drip, drip had stopped. The air smelled fresh and sweet. Fern and Mama turned off the light. Click.

As they left, Fern patted the basket. “We worked together,” she said. “That felt good.”

Mama whispered, “Yes. We took care of our space, and we took care of our feelings.”

Fern smiled a big, soft smile. “Tomorrow,” she said, “I will build. If it falls, I will breathe.”

The End.
