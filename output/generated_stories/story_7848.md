# Biscuit Discovers the Guest Room

Biscuit was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and a curious nose. One sunny morning, he padded down the hallway and stopped at a door he did not know very well. It was the guest room. The door was open just a little, and a ribbon of cool air tickled his whiskers.

Sniff, sniff, sniff, went Biscuit’s nose. He smelled something sweet, like flowers and clean sheets. He heard a tiny chime, tink-tink, from the window. Light peeked through the curtains and made gentle shapes on the floor.

“I wonder what is in here,” Biscuit whispered. His tummy fluttered. “New places can teach me new things.”

He pushed the door open with a soft paw. The guest room was quiet and cozy. There was a big, fluffy bed with a quilt. The quilt had many colors—red, blue, yellow, and green. The colors were like a garden of squares.

Biscuit touched the quilt. “So soft!” he said. “Hello, quilt.” He giggled. “I am Biscuit. I like to discover.”

On the bed sat three pillows. One pillow was tall and bumpy. “I am Stripey,” it said in a friendly, squeaky voice. One pillow was round and smooth. “I am Dot,” it hummed. The last pillow was small and shiny. “I am Shimmer!” it sang with a tiny sparkle.

Biscuit’s eyes grew wide. “You can talk?”

“We like to help guests feel comfy,” Stripey said. “We can help you discover.”

Biscuit’s ears perked up. “I want to learn.”

He looked around. Beside the bed stood a suitcase with stickers from many places. One sticker showed a snowy mountain. Another had a bright sun, and another had a city with tall buildings. The suitcase sighed softly. “I am Trundle,” it rumbled. “I have traveled far.”

Biscuit sat by Trundle. “What did you see?”

“I met people from many places,” Trundle said. “Some wore bright scarves. Some wore hats. Some spoke softly. Some laughed big. All were kind.”

Biscuit smiled. “Different is good,” he said. “We can learn from different friends.”

The window chimed again. “I am Breezy,” it whispered. “May I show you something?” The curtain fluttered like butterfly wings.

“Yes, please,” said Biscuit. He climbed onto the bed. Stripey, Dot, and Shimmer lined up like a rainbow path.

“Careful paws,” Stripey reminded.

Biscuit crawled slowly. He felt the quilt’s stitches like little roads under his paws. He listened to the gentle tink-tink. He smelled lavender from a small sachet on the nightstand. “Mmm,” he sighed. “It smells calm.”

Shimmer twinkled. “Let’s make a cozy nook for learning!”

“How?” Biscuit asked.

“Together,” Dot said. “We cooperate.”

Stripey puffed up and became a steady bridge for Biscuit to kneel on. Dot rolled to the window and made a soft seat. Shimmer slid onto Biscuit’s lap like a shiny book pillow.

Trundle clicked open. Inside was a small picture book. On the cover were many hands—big hands, small hands, brown hands, tan hands—making a circle. The title was, We All Help.

“Oh!” Biscuit clapped. “A discovery book!”

Breezy lifted the page corners with a tiny wind kiss. Flip-flip. Biscuit traced the pictures with one claw.

On one page, children planted seeds. On another, neighbors shared soup. On another, friends built a birdhouse with a hammer and a smile.

Biscuit read the simple words out loud. “We try. We share. We help.”

He looked at his new friends. “You help me, and I help you. We do it together.”

A soft thump came from the closet. Biscuit tilted his head. “What was that?” He padded over and pulled the door gently. Inside sat a woven basket with two little blankets: one was fluffy and white, the other smooth and patterned with tiny stars.

“Hello,” said the white blanket, yawning. “I’m Fluff.” The star blanket winked. “I’m Starry.”

“We were sleepy,” Fluff said. “But we love story time.”

“Join us,” Biscuit said. “We are learning about helping and differences.”

Fluff curled by Biscuit’s feet. Starry draped over Trundle’s lid like a cape. The room felt warm and safe. Biscuit noticed how each friend was different: Stripey was bumpy, Dot was smooth, Shimmer was shiny, Fluff was soft, Starry was cool, Trundle was strong, and Breezy was light as air.

“You are all special,” Biscuit said. “You are all you.”

A tiny button popped off Shimmer’s corner. “Oh no,” Shimmer squeaked. “My button!”

“We can fix it,” Biscuit said. “We will cooperate.” He looked around and found a small sewing kit on the nightstand. It had bright thread and a shiny needle.

“I can hold the thread,” Dot offered.

“I can keep everyone steady,” Stripey said, puffing up.

“I can blow gently,” Breezy whispered, “so Biscuit’s paws stay cool.”

“I can light the way,” Starry said, catching a little sun on its tiny stars.

Biscuit worked slowly and carefully. In-out, in-out went the needle with a soft shh, shh. Everyone helped. Soon, Shimmer’s button was snug again.

“Thank you,” Shimmer chimed. “Cooperation makes things better.”

Biscuit beamed. His heart felt big. “Today I discovered new friends, new smells, new sounds, and new ways to help. I learned that different friends bring different gifts. We can learn more when we listen and share.”

He closed the book and patted the quilt. The guest room was not strange anymore. It was a place for learning and love.

“I will come back,” Biscuit said, “for more discoveries.”

The room seemed to smile. Tink-tink went the window. Rustle-rustle went the quilt. Click went Trundle, proud and peaceful.

Biscuit took a deep breath. Lavender, sunshine, and a tiny sparkle filled the air. He waved. “Bye for now, friends.”

“Bye, Biscuit,” they sang.

Biscuit padded out, feeling brave and kind.

The End.
