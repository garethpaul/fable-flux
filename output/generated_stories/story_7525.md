# Tortoise Tilly Talks It Out

In a cozy garden shed at the edge of a sunny yard, lived Tilly, a wise tortoise. The shed smelled like fresh soil and sweet wood. Sunlight made warm stripes on the floor. Tilly loved the quiet hum of the day and the soft tick-tick of rain on the roof.

One bright morning, Tilly peeked out from her shell and stretched slowly. “Good morning, day,” she said in her gentle voice. “Let’s be kind today.” She took a sip from a tiny saucer of water. It was cool and clean. She munched a leafy green. It was crunchy and sweet.

Outside the shed, friends were busy. Bees buzzed like soft music. A snail named Sumi slid by, leaving a shiny trail. “Hello, Tilly,” Sumi said. “Can you help in the shed today? We need to tidy and make a rest corner for anyone who feels tired.”

“I would love to,” said Tilly. “A rest corner helps wellness. Everyone needs a calm spot.”

They went inside. The shed was full of tools, pots, and boxes. In the corner, a robin named Rolo hopped and chirped. “I found a box for the rest corner!” Rolo said. He flapped his red chest with pride.

A mouse named Minka tugged a small blanket. “I found a blanket,” she squeaked. “It is soft.” She rubbed it on her whiskers and smiled.

Suddenly, a loud clatter made everyone jump. A frog named Poppy had knocked over a stack of pots. “Oh no!” Poppy said. “I am so bouncy. I didn’t mean it.”

Rolo puffed his feathers. “You are too jumpy!” he chirped.

Poppy frowned. “You are too bossy!”

The shed felt tight and prickly. The warm stripes of sun seemed smaller. Tilly took a slow breath. She felt the air fill her, cool and steady. She let it out, warm and calm.

“Friends,” Tilly said softly, “let’s talk in a peaceful way.” Her voice was like a soft blanket. “We can use kind words. We can listen.”

Rolo and Poppy looked at Tilly. Sumi and Minka stood still and quiet.

Tilly nodded. “First, one friend talks. The other listens. Then we switch. We can use ‘I feel’ words. We can say what we need.”

Rolo tucked his wings. “I can try,” he chirped.

Poppy folded her hands. “Me too,” she croaked.

Tilly smiled. “Rolo, you first.”

Rolo took a tiny breath. “I feel worried when the pots fall,” he said. “I need our shed to be safe for everyone.”

“Thank you,” said Tilly. She turned to Poppy. “Now you.”

Poppy’s voice was soft. “I feel sad when someone calls me too jumpy,” she said. “I need others to remember that jumping is part of me. It is how I move and play.”

Tilly nodded. “You both shared your feelings and needs. That is peaceful communication.”

Rolo looked at Poppy. “I’m sorry I said you are too jumpy,” he chirped. “I can say, ‘Please move carefully near the pots.’”

Poppy smiled. “I can say, ‘I will hop slowly in the shed.’ Thank you for telling me.”

The air in the shed felt warm again, like sunshine on a cozy rock. Sumi clapped with her tiny feelers. Minka squeaked, “Hooray! We talked it out!”

“Now,” said Tilly, “let’s keep making our rest corner. We can celebrate our differences. Rolo can fly high to place things. Poppy can hop to bring light items. Minka can tuck soft things into small spaces. Sumi can make things neat and shiny. And I, with my slow-and-steady shell, can plan where things go.”

They all got to work. Rolo flew up to hang a little bell that made a soft ding when the breeze came. Poppy brought over lavender sprigs. “They smell purple and calm,” she said, and everyone giggled. Minka folded the soft blanket and patted it smooth. Sumi polished a tiny watering can until it sparkled.

Tilly placed a small sign on the wall. It read: Rest Corner. Use Kind Words. Take Deep Breaths. Share Feelings. Ask for Needs.

“Let’s try it,” Tilly said. They sat together. The shed felt peaceful and safe. The bell went ding. The lavender smelled gentle, like a hug in the air. Tilly led a deep breath. “In through the nose, out through the mouth,” she said slowly. Everyone breathed in and out. Their shoulders dropped. Their faces softened.

A cricket peeked in and chirped, “Can I rest here?” His voice shook. “I am tired from singing.”

“Of course,” said Tilly. “This corner is for the whole community.” She gave him a corner of the blanket. The cricket sighed with relief.

Later, as the sun went down, the shed glowed with quiet. Rolo chirped, “Today we learned to listen.”

Poppy added, “We learned to say ‘I feel’ and ‘I need.’”

Minka squeaked, “We helped each other and made a calm place.”

Sumi nodded. “We celebrated our differences.”

Tilly smiled, her eyes kind and wise. “When we talk with care, we take care of each other,” she said. “Peaceful words make a peaceful world.”

They all looked at the little sign and the cozy corner. The garden hummed a gentle good-night song. The bell went ding, and the lavender whispered sweet dreams.

The End.
