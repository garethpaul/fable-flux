# Kody's Big Bedroom Discovery

In a cozy bedroom filled with soft pillows and warm blankets, a calm koala named Kody sat by his window. The morning sun painted golden stripes across his fuzzy gray fur. He loved quiet mornings like this.

Kody had always done the same thing every morning - read his favorite eucalyptus leaf book while sitting in his special corner. The soft carpet felt nice under his paws, and everything was just how he liked it.

But today felt different. His little sister Kira knocked on his bedroom door.

"Kody! Kody!" she called excitedly. "Can I show you something new?"

Kody's ears twitched. He didn't like changes to his morning routine. "I'm reading my book, Kira. Maybe later?"

"Please?" Kira's voice sounded hopeful. "It's about saving trees!"

Kody paused. He loved trees - they gave him his favorite eucalyptus leaves. Slowly, he closed his book and opened the door.

Kira bounced in with a colorful box. "Look! It's a recycling game! We can learn how to help trees and take turns too!"

Kody looked at the bright box suspiciously. "I don't know how to play new games," he said softly. "What if I'm not good at it?"

"That's okay!" Kira smiled. "We can learn together. Being open to new things is how we grow!"

Kody felt nervous butterflies in his tummy. But Kira's excited face made him curious. "Okay," he said slowly. "Show me."

Kira opened the box. Inside were colorful cards with pictures of bottles, cans, and paper. "We take turns sorting things that can be recycled! This helps save trees!"

"I'll go first," Kody said, reaching for a card.

"Wait!" Kira giggled. "Let's take turns properly. You can go first, then me, then you again!"

Kody picked up a card with a plastic bottle. He wasn't sure where it went. "Hmm... is this right?" He placed it in the recycling bin picture.

"Perfect!" Kira clapped. "Now it's my turn!"

They played for several minutes, taking turns and helping each other. When Kody made mistakes, Kira helped him gently. When Kira wasn't sure, Kody shared what he learned.

"This is actually fun," Kody admitted, his eyes growing brighter. "And I'm learning how to help trees!"

"See?" Kira beamed. "Trying new things can be wonderful!"

As they continued playing, Kody felt proud. Even when he didn't get things right the first time, he kept trying. The game taught him that persistence helped him learn better.

"Kira," Kody said thoughtfully, "thank you for sharing this with me. I almost missed out because I was scared of something new."

"That's what being open-minded means," Kira explained. "It's like opening a door to new adventures!"

Kody looked around his cozy bedroom. It still felt safe and comfortable, but now it also felt full of possibilities. The soft morning light seemed even warmer.

"Can we play again tomorrow?" Kody asked.

"Of course! And maybe we can try something else new too!"

Kody smiled, feeling brave and excited. He learned that being open to new experiences, taking turns with others, and not giving up when things were hard made everything more fun. His cozy bedroom had become a place for wonderful discoveries.

From that day on, Kody's morning routine included time for new adventures with his sister.

The End.
