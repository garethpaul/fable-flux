# Bubbles on the Balcony

Bubbles the playful dolphin loved to visit Mia’s balcony garden. It was high above the busy street, with pots of mint, cherry tomatoes, crunchy cucumbers, and bright marigolds. The air smelled fresh and leafy. Wind chimes tinkled, and a tiny fountain splashed, splash-splash, like Bubbles’ own ocean songs.

One sunny morning, Bubbles popped his shiny nose over the balcony rail. “Eee-eee!” he chirped. “Good morning, Mia! Can we play bubble tag?”

Mia giggled and clapped. “Hi, Bubbles! I want to play, but first we help Grandma. It’s watering day.” She pointed to a watering can shaped like a blue whale. “We water the plants, then we can play.”

Grandma stepped out, wearing a colorful scarf. She smiled, her eyes warm. “Good morning, sweeties. Today we also share a little tradition. In my family, we make a mint tea toast to say thank you to our garden.”

Bubbles twirled his tail. “Tea sounds fun, but can we play first? I feel so wiggly!”

Mia shook her head gently. “We have jobs. But we can make a deal.” She held up one finger. “We water together.” A second finger. “We do one round of bubble tag.” A third finger. “Then we make mint tea with Grandma.”

Bubbles blinked. “A deal? What is that?”

Grandma chuckled softly. “A deal is a friendly plan. We talk. We listen. We choose a fair way for everyone.”

Bubbles thought. The fountain splish-splished. The marigolds smelled like sunshine. He nodded. “Okay! A friendly plan. But can we make it more fun? We can sing a watering song!”

Mia grinned. “Yes! We can sing.”

They began. “Sprinkle, sprinkle, little leaves, drink your drops and grow your sleeves!” They giggled as cool water shimmered over the soil. The cucumbers looked bumpy and green. The tomatoes gleamed like tiny red balloons. The mint leaves smelled tingly and sweet.

Bubbles helped by puffing gentle little misty sprays from his mouth, “Pfffft!” The plants shivered happily. He listened when Mia said, “Not too much for the marigolds,” and he nodded. “I hear you.”

When they were done, Bubbles wiggled. “Now bubble tag?”

Mia looked at Grandma. Grandma smiled and touched her scarf. “Yes, little dolphins and little gardeners. One round.”

Bubbles made a bubble with a soft pop. It floated, rainbow-bright. “Tag, you’re it!” he chirped. Mia ran in tiny steps around the pots, careful not to bump them. She reached out and boop! Tapped the bubble. It popped with a gentle kiss sound. They took turns. “Your turn!” “My turn!” They slowed when Grandma said, “Soft feet, please.” They listened and kept the plants safe.

Soon Mia took a deep breath. “We did one round. Now tea time!”

Bubbles blinked again. “But I want one more round.”

Mia frowned a little. “We made a plan.”

Bubbles’ tail drooped. The fountain whispered. Grandma knelt down. “Let’s use our words. Bubbles, what do you need?”

“I need more play,” he said in a small voice.

Mia thought. She put a hand on her heart. “I need to keep our plan. I want to help Grandma.”

Grandma nodded. “You both shared your needs. Now, can we make a new plan that is fair?”

Bubbles took a big breath. He listened to the leaves rustle. “What if… we make the tea first. Then we do one more tiny round?”

Mia tilted her head. “Hmm. I want to help first. I also want to play with you. I can say yes if we make the tiny round very tiny. Like three bubble pops.”

Bubbles counted on his flippers. “One, two, three. I can do that!”

Grandma smiled. “You negotiated. That means you worked together to find a fair plan. I am proud of you.”

They picked mint. The leaves were cool and crisp. Snip, snip. The kettle sang a little song. Steam smelled sweet. Grandma poured warm water into little cups. “In my family,” she said, “we tap our cups and say, ‘Thank you, garden,’ before we sip.”

They tapped. “Thank you, garden,” they said together. Mia took a tiny sip. “Mmm.” Bubbles blew across his cup, “Fffff,” to cool it, then pretended to sip with a happy “Eee!”

Grandma shared more. “We also share fruit with our tea. Try cucumber slices. They are crunchy and good for our bodies.”

Mia crunched. “Crunch! So fresh!” Bubbles nibbled carefully. “Munch-munch! Healthy and yummy!”

After tea, Bubbles made three bubbles. Pop. Pop. Pop. “All done!” he said proudly. He had kept the new plan. Mia smiled and gave him a gentle fin high-five.

Grandma wrapped her scarf tighter against a soft breeze. “Today you learned to talk, to listen, and to make a fair plan. That is negotiation. You cared for your bodies with water, tea, and crunchy food. You shared a family tradition.”

Bubbles twirled in a happy little spiral. “Negotiation feels kind.”

Mia hugged him. “Negotiation helps everyone.”

They looked at the garden. The leaves were shiny with tiny drops. The chimes tinkled. The fountain sang. Their hearts felt calm and full.

The End.
