# Polly’s Spoon Song

Polly was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a little red tuft on top. She lived with a family who loved breakfast time in their sunny dining room. The table was smooth and wooden. The room smelled like warm toast and sweet oranges. Sunlight made the spoons sparkle like tiny moons.

One morning, Polly hopped onto a chair and looked at the shiny spoons. “Squawk! I have an idea,” she said. “I will make music!”

Mama poured orange juice. It made a happy glug-glug sound. Papa buttered toast. It made a soft scrape-scrape sound. The little twins, Mia and Max, giggled. “What music, Polly?” Mia asked.

Polly puffed up her feathers. “A Spoon Song!” she said proudly. She tapped one spoon with another. Ting! Ting! Ting! The sound was bright and tiny, like raindrops on a window.

Max clapped. “Do it again!”

Polly nodded. “I will.” She tapped slow. Ting… ting… ting… Then fast. Tingtingting! She moved the spoons to a glass of water. Plink! The glass sang a new note. Polly tilted her head. “Listen. High note. Low note. We can make a pattern.”

Mia swayed in her chair. “I want to try!”

Papa smiled. “Let Polly show us first. She has a plan.”

Polly stood tall, even though she was little. “Squawk! My plan is simple,” she said, being brave and clear. “We will play together. I will lead. One at a time, then all together.” She felt a flutter in her tummy, but she was assertive. “Please wait your turn.”

Mama nodded. “What a clever leader.”

Polly tapped the table gently with a wooden spoon. Tok… tok… tok… “This is our heartbeat,” she said softly. “We listen. We follow.”

She gave Mia two spoons. “You play rain,” Polly said. Mia tapped soft: ting… ting… ting…

She gave Max a napkin to shake. “You play wind,” Polly said. Whish… whish…

Papa hummed a low, warm note. Mmmmmm. Mama clapped softly. Clap… clap…

Polly listened. She heard the heartbeat, the rain, the wind, the hum, and the claps. She added her own trill. “La-la-la! Squawk-a-la!” It made everyone laugh.

Suddenly, Max banged his spoon too hard. CLANG! The glass wobbled. Polly fluttered. Her heart beat fast. Mia frowned. “Too loud,” she whispered.

Polly took a breath. She remembered to be kind and calm. “Max,” she said, “I like your excitement. But our music needs gentle hands. Can you try softer?”

Max’s eyes were big. “I can try.” He tapped softly. Ting… ting…

Polly smiled. “Thank you. That is tolerant and kind. We all learn.” She looked around. “We can improve our song. Let’s add a new sound.”

She dipped her spoon in the glass. Plink-blub! She blew across the top. Hoooo. The glass made a whisper song. Mia gasped. “Magic!”

“It’s innovation,” Polly said proudly. “A new idea!”

They made a simple pattern. Tok… ting… whish… mmm… clap… hooo… Then again. Tok… ting… whish… mmm… clap… hooo… It felt like waves at the beach. It sounded gentle and bright. The room was full of happy sounds and smiles.

A crumb fell, and the family’s kitten, Noodle, jumped onto a chair. Noodle’s tail swished. Thump-thump. The kitten knocked a fork. Tink! Tink! Polly paused. “Noodle wants to play too,” she said. “We can make space.”

She moved her chair. “Noodle, tap only twice,” Polly asked. Tink… tink. Noodle purred, rr-rr-rr, a soft fuzzy drum.

Polly saw everyone waiting and watching, listening to each other. She felt proud and warm. “We are a band,” she said. “We respect the beat. We take turns. We try new things.”

Then Polly had another idea. “Let’s add a quiet part,” she whispered. “Shhh. Listen.” They all went very soft. The sunlight felt warm on their faces. They could hear birds outside. Chirp… chirp… The quiet made the next part even more special.

“Now a big finish!” Polly called. They played their pattern one last time, a little louder, still gentle and kind. Tok… ting… whish… mmm… clap… hooo… tink… rr-rr-rr! Then stop.

Silence. Then cheers! Mama hugged Polly. “What beautiful music.”

Papa grinned. “You were a strong leader, Polly. You spoke up kindly. You helped us improve.”

Mia bounced. “We made new sounds! Innovation!”

Max nodded. “I learned to play softer. I can do hard things.”

Polly fluffed her feathers. She felt brave, creative, and calm. “Music helps us listen, share, and feel,” she said. “When we try new ideas and speak up with kindness, our song gets better. We can make music anywhere, even in the dining room.”

They all sipped orange juice. It tasted sweet and bright. The spoons looked like little smiles on the table.

Polly tapped a tiny goodbye note. Ting. “Let’s play again tomorrow,” she said.

“Yes!” everyone cheered.

That morning, the dining room became a music room, and the family learned that music is more than sound. It is taking turns, trying new ideas, and being gentle and brave. With listening ears and kind hearts, their Spoon Song shined.

The lesson: Music helps us be assertive, creative, and caring. We can improve by practicing, trying new ideas, and respecting each other’s sounds.

The End.
