# Ellie's Big Brave Day

Little Ellie the elephant calf stood at the bottom of the tall apartment building, looking way, way up at the rooftop. Her trunk wiggled nervously as she watched the other young animals playing games up there.

"I wish I could go up too," Ellie whispered to herself. "But I'm too scared. What if I'm too heavy? What if I fall?"

Just then, her friend Benny the bunny hopped down the stairs. "Hi Ellie! Why aren't you coming up to play with us?"

Ellie's big ears flapped sadly. "I don't think I can make it up there, Benny. I'm just a little elephant, and that's so high up!"

Benny's nose twitched thoughtfully. "But Ellie, you're the strongest and kindest friend we have! Remember when you helped carry all our picnic supplies last week?"

Ellie touched her trunk to the ground, thinking. She did remember feeling proud when she helped everyone.

"Come on," said Benny gently. "Let's take it one step at a time. I'll show you the safe way up."

Together, they walked to the special wide stairs made just for bigger animals. Benny pointed to the sturdy railings. "See? These are here to keep everyone safe. And look how wide the steps are!"

As they climbed slowly, Ellie felt the cool breeze ruffle her ears. "The air smells so fresh up here," she said, her confidence growing with each step.

"That's because we're getting closer to the sky!" Benny replied cheerfully.

When they reached the rooftop, Ellie gasped with wonder. The view was beautiful! She could see the whole neighborhood spread out below like a colorful puzzle. Birds chirped nearby, and fluffy white clouds danced overhead.

"Ellie! You made it!" called out Ruby the raccoon and Sam the squirrel, running over to greet them.

But then Ellie noticed something that made her heart feel heavy. In the corner of the rooftop sat Toby the turtle, all alone. He looked very sad.

"Why isn't Toby playing with everyone?" Ellie asked.

"He says he's too slow to join our games," Ruby explained. "We've been trying to cheer him up, but nothing works."

Ellie's kind heart knew just what to do. She walked over to Toby with confident, gentle steps.

"Hi Toby," she said warmly. "Would you like to watch the clouds with me? I think being slow is perfect for cloud-watching. We can take our time and really see all the amazing shapes."

Toby's eyes lit up. "Really? You think being slow is good for something?"

"Absolutely!" Ellie said with a big smile. "And I think being big is good for giving the best shade." She positioned herself so her body blocked the bright sun for Toby.

Soon, all the friends gathered around Ellie and Toby, lying on the soft rooftop surface. They pointed at clouds that looked like ice cream cones, sailing boats, and fluffy pillows.

"That one looks like a heart!" Toby said excitedly.

"You're right!" Ellie laughed, feeling warm and happy inside.

As the sun began to set, painting the sky in gentle pink and orange colors, Ellie realized something wonderful. She had been brave enough to try something new, and it led to helping a friend feel better.

"Thank you for believing in me, Benny," Ellie said. "And thank you for teaching me that everyone has special gifts, just like Toby."

From that day on, Ellie knew that being brave and kind made her feel stronger than ever.

The End.
