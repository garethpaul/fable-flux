# Sunny the Squirrel and the Sparkly Bathroom

Sunny the squirrel lived in a little house at the edge of a big, green park. She wore a tiny blue scarf and kept her fluffy tail neat and tidy. Sunny liked to help. She liked to fix things. She liked to figure things out. “I am a resourceful squirrel,” she often said proudly.

One rainy morning, the park was quiet. Drip, drop, drip, drop. The rain tapped the windows like a soft drum. Sunny listened and smiled. “Today is a good day to be responsible,” she said. “Today I will do my bathroom chores!”

Sunny’s bathroom was small and bright. The walls were lemon-yellow. The soap smelled like oranges. The towels were soft and green like moss. Squish went the bath mat under her tiny paws. Sunny loved the warm glow of the little lamp and the mirror that made her whiskers look extra shiny.

Sunny opened the door and gasped. “Oh nuts!” she squeaked. There was a puddle on the floor by the sink. Plip, plip, plip. Tiny drops fell from the faucet. Her toothbrush lay on the counter with a little smear of blue paste. A stack of towels leaned like a wobbly tower.

Sunny put her paws on her hips. “I said I would keep the bathroom tidy,” she told herself. “I promised to be responsible.” She took a deep breath. In and out. She made a plan, because plans help small paws do big jobs.

First, Sunny talked to herself, like a coach. “Step one: stop the plip-plip. Step two: wipe the puddle. Step three: put things away. Step four: check my list.”

Sunny checked the faucet. “Hello, leaky!” she said in a friendly voice. She tried the handles. Twist, twist. The drip slowed. Plip… plip… stop. “There!” she cheered. “I can fix little things, and I can ask for help for big things.”

She grabbed a small cloth. Swish-swish. She wiped the puddle. The cloth felt damp and cool. The floor felt smooth and safe again. “No slipping today,” Sunny said. She giggled and wiggled her tail.

Next, she rinsed her toothbrush. Swish in warm water. Bubble-bubble. She put it in its little cup. “Toothbrush lives here,” she said. “Everything has a home.” She wiped the counter with slow, careful circles. Orange soap smell made her nose twitch. “Mmm! Smells clean and sunny.”

Sunny reached for the towels. The wobbly tower leaned. “Oh!” she said, steadying it with both paws. She folded each towel, pat pat, fold fold. She liked the soft rustle and the neat stack. “I’m completing my tasks, one by one,” Sunny sang. “I can do it. I will get it done.”

Just then, she heard a knock. Tap, tap. It was Pip the mouse, wearing a tiny raincoat. “Hi, Sunny!” Pip squeaked. “I brought your mail. Oh wow! Your bathroom looks so bright!”

“Thanks, Pip,” Sunny said. “I’m being responsible today. I am finishing my chores.”

Pip peeked in. “Can I help? I like to volunteer.”

Sunny smiled. “Yes! Thank you for offering. You can shake out the bath mat. But let’s talk about safety. Shake it over the tub, so water doesn’t splash.”

“Good communication,” Pip said proudly. He lifted the bath mat. Shake, shake. Sunny wiped the tub edge. Swish-swish. Together, the bathroom felt even cleaner. They laughed when a tiny drop landed on Pip’s nose. “Boop!” Sunny giggled, tapping his nose gently with her paw.

Sunny looked at her list on the wall. She had drawn pictures to help her remember: a faucet, a towel, a toothbrush, a trash bin. “Trash bin!” she said. “I almost forgot.” She picked up a little empty toothpaste box and dropped it into the bin. Plunk. “Task complete.”

Pip pointed to the mirror. “What about the mirror?”

Sunny nodded. “Good idea.” She sprayed a little cleaner. Spritz! The smell was like fresh lemons. She wiped with round circles until the glass gleamed. When they looked, they saw two happy faces. Pip made a silly face. Sunny did too. They both laughed. “Our smiles are extra shiny,” Sunny said.

Suddenly, the faucet gave a tiny drip again. Plip. “Hmm,” Sunny said. She checked it. “I fixed the handle, but I think the inside needs a tool I don’t have.” She put her paw to her chin. “Being responsible means I try my best and I also ask for help when I need it.”

Sunny picked up the phone and called Mr. Beaver, the friendly handyman. “Hello, Mr. Beaver,” she said. “I tightened my faucet, but it still drips a little. Could you please come check it? I want to care for my home.”

“Of course!” said Mr. Beaver in a warm, deep voice. “You did the right thing. I’ll be there this afternoon.”

Sunny thanked him and hung up. She turned to Pip. “We did so much! We worked together. We talked kindly. We finished our tasks.”

Pip smiled. “And we volunteered and used our voices. That’s good communication.”

Sunny looked around. The towels were straight. The counter sparkled. The floor was dry. The bathroom smelled like oranges and lemons and a little bit like rain. Sunny’s heart felt light and proud. “When I take care of my things, I show responsibility,” she said. “Little jobs, done one by one, make a big difference.”

Later that day, Mr. Beaver fixed the faucet with a gentle clack and a twist. No more plip-plip. “All set,” he said. “You were very responsible, Sunny.”

“Thank you,” Sunny said. She gave Pip a high-five and Mr. Beaver a grateful smile. “Today I learned that being responsible means noticing, planning, doing, and asking for help when needed. I can be a helper at home, and I can complete my tasks.”

Pip nodded. “And I learned that volunteering feels good!”

Sunny hung a tiny star sticker on her list. “Job done,” she whispered. Her bathroom was calm and clean. Drip, drop, went the rain outside, but inside everything was still and bright.

Sunny wrapped her tail around herself like a cozy blanket. “Tomorrow,” she said softly, “I will be responsible again.”

The End.
