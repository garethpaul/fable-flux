# Bubbles Leads the Study Corner Splash

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with bright, curious eyes. Today, Bubbles visited a cozy study corner. The study corner had a soft rug, a little round table, snug pillows, and a basket full of crayons and paper. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. It smelled like paper and fresh crayons. Bubbles clicked and chirped with joy.

“Click-click! Today is a big day,” Bubbles said. “We will learn and make and share.”

On the wall was a simple sign: “Goal of the Day.” The space under it was empty.

Bubbles took a deep breath. “I can be a leader,” Bubbles whispered. “A leader helps friends. A leader listens. A leader makes a plan.”

Flipper, a tiny fish plushie, sat on a chair. Shelli, a small stuffed turtle, leaned on a pillow. A wind-up crab named Cranky clicked softly on the table. Bubbles tapped the table with a fin. Tap-tap.

“Friends,” Bubbles said gently, “our goal today is to make a Sharing Book. We will draw and tell our family traditions. Tradition means something special we do again and again.”

Cranky whirred. “Click-click! I don’t know where to start.”

Shelli whispered, “I feel shy.”

Bubbles smiled. “That’s okay. A leader helps everyone feel safe and brave. We will take turns. We will listen. We will try.”

Bubbles wrote the goal in big, simple letters on a sticky note: “Goal: Make a Sharing Book.” Bubbles stuck it under the sign. The sticky note made a soft pffft sound.

“Step one,” Bubbles said, “we decide our plan. Step two, we create. Step three, we share.”

Bubbles set a timer. It ticked like a tiny clock: tick-tick-tick. “We will work for a little while. When it dings, we share.”

Bubbles passed out paper. The paper was smooth. The crayons were bright and smelled waxy. The colors felt warm in the hand.

Cranky raised a claw. “Leader Bubbles, what is your tradition?”

Bubbles’ eyes sparkled. “My family sings a morning song. We tap our fins on the water. Tap-tap! We sing, ‘Good day, good waves, good hearts!’ Then we say one kind thing to each other.”

Shelli smiled. “My family lights a little lamp at dinner. We say thank you. We pass the bread. The bread is warm, and the butter is soft.”

Cranky clicked. “My family counts the shells on the windowsill. We count together—one, two, three! Then we clap.”

Bubbles nodded. “These are wonderful traditions. Let’s draw them. Use color. Use shapes. I will help.”

Bubbles showed leadership with calm words and kind eyes. “Cranky, you can go first with red and orange. Shelli, you can use green and gold. I will use blue and silver. We will share crayons and space.”

The crayons made soft scritch-scratch sounds. Bubbles drew wavy water and happy fins. “Tap-tap,” Bubbles hummed the song. Shelli drew a tiny lamp with a golden glow. Cranky drew shells like little smiles.

The timer dinged. Ding!

Bubbles clapped fins. “Time to share!”

Cranky looked a bit nervous. “What if it’s not good?”

Bubbles took a breath and spoke gently. “A leader cheers others. Let’s say, ‘You did your best.’ We can also say, ‘I like…’ Ready?”

Cranky nodded. “Okay.” Cranky held up the picture. “This is my counting shells. I feel proud.”

Bubbles said, “I like your shells. They look happy!”

Shelli whispered, “I like your counting. It sounds fun.”

Cranky beamed.

Shelli showed the lamp picture next. The golden glow looked warm and soft. “This is our dinner lamp. I feel calm.”

Bubbles said, “I like your warm light. It feels safe.”

Cranky clicked, “I like your lamp. It looks cozy.”

Shelli giggled.

Bubbles showed the morning song picture. Waves swished across the page. “This is our tapping song. I feel joy.”

Cranky and Shelli cheered, “We like your singing waves!”

Bubbles stacked the pages and punched two holes. The puncher made a chunky chomp. Bubbles tied the pages with blue ribbon. The ribbon felt smooth and cool.

“Now we have our Sharing Book,” Bubbles said. “We met our goal! Good job, team.”

“Leader Bubbles,” Shelli asked, “what does a leader do next?”

Bubbles looked around the study corner. The crayons were scattered. Paper bits were on the rug. The sign still said “Goal of the Day.”

“A leader helps finish and reflect,” Bubbles said. “Step four, we tidy. Step five, we think about what we learned.”

They sang the tidy-up song. “Pick it up, put it back, we can help each friend!” Crayons clicked into the basket. Paper bits swished into the bin. The rug felt smooth again.

Bubbles sat with friends. “What did we learn?”

Cranky said, “A leader makes a plan.”

Shelli added, “A leader listens and helps.”

Bubbles said, “A leader also shares and celebrates. We discovered new things about each other. We shared our traditions. We crafted our book. We reached our goal.”

Bubbles stuck a new note on the wall: “We did it!” The paper made a happy pffft.

Bubbles smiled. “I feel proud of all of us. We can be leaders every day—by being kind, by making plans, and by helping friends.”

The Sharing Book sat on the table, smelling like crayons and ribbon. The study corner felt bright and warm. The friends felt brave and happy.

“Tap-tap,” Bubbles sang softly. “Good day, good waves, good hearts.”

The End.
