# Koda the Koala's Amazing Discovery

Koda the koala loved sitting in his favorite eucalyptus tree. The sunny backyard was warm and peaceful. Birds chirped sweet songs. Butterflies danced in the bright flowers below.

"What a beautiful day," Koda said softly.

Koda noticed something sparkly in the grass. He climbed down slowly, his fluffy gray fur warm from the sunshine. His round black nose twitched with curiosity.

"What could that be?" he wondered.

It was a small, colorful object. It had strange markings Koda had never seen before. The object felt smooth and cool in his paws.

"This looks very special," Koda said. "But I don't know what it is."

Koda felt puzzled. He wanted to learn more, but he was too shy to ask anyone. He sat quietly, holding the mysterious object.

Mrs. Robin flew down from her nest. "Hello, Koda! What do you have there?"

Koda's cheeks turned pink. "I found this, but I don't know what it is. I'm too embarrassed to ask."

"Oh my!" chirped Mrs. Robin. "That looks like something from far away. You should ask Mr. Turtle. He knows about many places around the world."

Koda felt nervous. "But what if he thinks I'm silly?"

"Questions aren't silly," said Mrs. Robin kindly. "They help us learn new things!"

Koda took a deep breath. He walked to the garden pond where Mr. Turtle lived. The old turtle was sunning himself on a warm rock.

"Excuse me, Mr. Turtle," Koda said in a small voice. "I found something strange. Could you help me?"

Mr. Turtle's wise eyes twinkled. "Of course, young Koda! Let me see."

Koda showed him the colorful object. Mr. Turtle smiled widely.

"This is wonderful!" Mr. Turtle exclaimed. "This is a worry stone from Australia! Someone from very far away must have dropped it."

"Australia?" Koda's eyes grew big. "That's where koalas like me come from!"

"Exactly!" said Mr. Turtle. "People there rub these smooth stones when they feel worried. It helps them feel calm and peaceful."

Koda felt amazed. "So this little stone traveled all the way here from my family's homeland?"

"Yes indeed," nodded Mr. Turtle. "The world is full of wonderful connections. This stone shows how we're all linked together, even across great distances."

Koda held the stone gently. It felt warm and comforting in his paws. "I'm so glad I asked you about it. I almost kept it a secret because I was scared."

"Speaking up when you're curious is very brave," said Mr. Turtle. "That's how we discover amazing things about our world."

Koda rubbed the smooth stone. He felt proud and peaceful. "I want to learn more about Australia and other places too!"

"That's the spirit of discovery!" laughed Mr. Turtle. "When we ask questions and share what we find, we learn about the whole wide world."

From that day on, Koda kept the worry stone in his tree. Whenever he found something new or felt curious, he remembered to speak up and ask questions. He learned about many countries and made friends who taught him wonderful things.

The sunny backyard became Koda's place of discovery. He realized that being brave enough to ask questions opened up a whole world of learning and friendship.

The End.
