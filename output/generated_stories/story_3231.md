# The Little Leader in the Lab

In a quiet house on a rainy day, a kind mouse named Miri lived in a basement lab. The lab smelled like warm bread from the toaster and sweet soap from the sink. Lights blinked. Buttons clicked. Little fans hummed, “Whirr, whirr.” Miri liked the soft tick-tick of a clock and the cool, smooth feel of the tiles under her tiny feet.

Miri was small, with bright eyes and a big heart. She loved to help. She swept crumbs with a tiny broom. She tied string bows on rolled-up papers. She listened to the music of the machines. “I can do kind things,” Miri said. “I can help.”

One stormy afternoon, the lights flickered. “Zzzip!” went the lamps. The fans stopped. The machines went quiet. The room felt bigger and darker. The clock ticked alone. “Oh!” Miri squeaked. “The power is off.”

In the corner sat a sleepy plant under a special grow light. Without the light, the plant drooped. Its leaves felt cool and wet. “Don’t worry,” Miri whispered, patting a leaf. “I will help you.”

Miri took a deep breath. She felt a little wiggly in her tummy. “Even if I am small, I can think,” she told herself. “I can try. I can solve problems.”

First, Miri lit a tiny lamp with a safe glow. The gentle light made the lab look warm again. “Better,” she said. “Now I can see.”

She looked around and used her senses. She listened. Drip, drip, went the pipe. She sniffed. The air smelled like rain and rubber gloves. She touched the floor. It was cool and smooth. She looked up. A big battery box sat on a high shelf by the plant’s light. A small sign said, “BACKUP.” Miri’s whiskers twitched. “A backup can help!” she said.

But the shelf was too high. Miri could not reach. She tried a hop. Boing! Not high enough. She tried a stack of blocks. Wobble! Too shaky. Miri sat and hugged her tail for a moment. “It’s okay,” she whispered. “I can think of another way.”

Miri remembered the wagon. It had four squeaky wheels and a little rope. She also remembered the ramp used for rolling boxes. “A plan!” Miri smiled. “I can make a path.”

She pulled the ramp to the shelf. Scrape, scrape. She pushed the wagon under the ramp. Squeak, squeak. She tested the ramp with her paw. “Strong,” she said. She took a slow breath. “I can lead myself with calm steps. Step by step.”

Then she heard tiny footsteps. Tap-tap-tap. Two lab friends came out from under a table: Pip the cricket and Dot the dust bunny. Pip chirped, “Is the plant okay?” Dot murmured, “It’s so dark. I feel shy.”

Miri stood tall, even though she was small. “We can help together,” she said kindly. “Pip, please hold this rope so the ramp doesn’t slide. Dot, can you make the wagon steady with your fluffy body?”

Pip saluted with a cheerful chirp. Dot nodded and puffed up round and soft. Miri smiled. “Thank you. We are a team.”

Miri stepped onto the ramp. It felt a little bouncy. “I will go slow,” she said. “Slow is safe.” She climbed one step. Two steps. Three steps. Her paws felt the wood. Her ears heard Pip’s happy chirp and Dot’s gentle hum. “You can do it, Miri,” Pip sang. “You are brave,” said Dot.

At the top, Miri reached the battery box. It was dusty. She sneezed—“Achoo!” The dust smelled dry and tickly. She brushed the box with the edge of her tail and read the simple picture on it: a plug, an arrow, a light. “I plug the battery into the light,” Miri said. “I can do this.”

Her paws shook a tiny bit. She thought, “I am small, but I am smart. I am careful. I can try.” She lifted the plug. Click. She slid the switch. “Click.”

For a breath, nothing happened. Then—“Bzzzz!” The grow light glowed warm and pink. The plant lifted its leaves like a happy hug. The room felt friendly. Pip chirped loud. Dot spun in a fluffy circle. Miri’s chest felt warm and proud.

Miri climbed down, slow and safe. At the bottom, her friends rushed to her. Pip squeaked, “You did it!” Dot whispered, “You led us. You are a good leader.”

Miri smiled shyly. “We did it together,” she said. “We had a plan. We used our senses. We took turns. We stayed calm. That is problem-solving.”

They gave the plant a tiny cup of water. Drip, drip. The leaves shimmered. The hum of the light sounded like a lullaby. The lab smelled sweet again. The clock ticked in a happy way.

Miri sat with her friends and hugged her tail. “Even small helpers can be leaders,” she said softly. “When a problem comes, I can think. I can ask for help. I can try again. I can be proud of me.”

Pip chirped a song. Dot made a cozy nest. The storm outside slowed to a soft patter. In the warm pink glow, Miri felt brave and bright.

The lesson was clear in her heart: Think first. Make a plan. Work together. Believe in yourself. Step by step, you can fix big things.

The End.
