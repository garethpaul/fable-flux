# Panda Poppy and the Cozy Closet

Poppy the panda loved soft things. She loved soft moss, soft pillows, and soft hugs. In her house, there was a special place: a closet hideaway. Inside the closet were twinkly string lights, a rainbow rug, a beanbag chair, and a basket full of calm-down toys. It smelled like clean cotton and a little bit like lavender. It was quiet and cozy, like a warm hug.

One sunny morning, Poppy invited her friends to play dress-up. “Let’s have a costume parade!” Poppy said. Her friends clapped and cheered.

Milo the monkey swung in, chattering with excitement. “I like loud music and silly dancing!” he giggled.

Bella the bunny hopped softly. “I like whisper songs,” she said in a tiny voice.

Tiko the turtle came slowly with a big grin. “I like things to go slow,” he said, tapping his shell.

They all went to the closet hideaway to choose costumes. Poppy found a sparkly cape. “Ooh, shiny,” she said, rubbing the smooth fabric on her cheek. Bella picked a fluffy tutu. “So soft!” she sighed. Tiko chose a tall hat. “Careful, careful,” he murmured. And Milo grabbed a bright feather boa. “Let’s go fast!” he shouted, and he shook the feathers so they whooshed in the air.

Suddenly, Milo turned the music up, up, up. Boom-ba-boom! Poppy’s ears felt buzzy. The closet lights twinkled and the feathers tickled her nose. “Ah-choo!” she sneezed. The boa flew, and the tall hat slipped off Tiko’s head and bonked the beanbag. Tiko frowned. Bella’s ears drooped. Poppy’s tummy felt tight, like a knot.

“Hey! Too fast!” Poppy said. Her voice came out louder than she meant. “I don’t like it!”

Milo stopped and blinked. “I was just having fun,” he said softly, his tail curling.

Poppy felt hot in her cheeks. Her heart thumped. “I’m mad,” she said. Her paws squeezed into fists. She wanted to shout again.

Poppy remembered the sign on the closet wall. It said: Pause. Breathe. Choose.

She took a slow breath in. She smelled lavender. She let the air out like blowing bubbles. “Pffff.” She put her paw on her chest. “Thump, thump,” it went. She did it again. In… one, two, three. Out… one, two, three.

Her tummy knot loosened.

“I need a calm moment,” Poppy said. She climbed onto the beanbag and hugged a squishy pillow. The pillow was cool and smooth. The lights blinked softly, like tiny stars. Bella sat beside her and held her paw.

Milo shuffled his feet. “I didn’t mean to make you mad,” he whispered. “I have big energy.”

Tiko nodded slowly. “We all like different things,” he said. “Fast, slow, loud, soft.”

Poppy looked around. Her friends had different smiles and different ways. That was special, not wrong.

“I am learning to be patient,” Poppy said, patting her own chest. “When I feel mad, I can slow down.”

“How?” asked Bella.

Poppy sat up and smiled. “I can do belly breaths. Watch.” She put her paw on her belly and took a gentle breath. “In like smelling a flower. Out like blowing out a candle.” Everyone copied her. In… out… The closet felt calm again.

“Thank you,” Poppy said. “Now, let’s fix our problem. We want to play together, but we like different things. We can take turns.”

Milo brightened. “A turn for fast and loud, and a turn for slow and quiet?”

“Yes,” said Tiko. “And a middle turn for medium!”

Poppy giggled. “Perfect. We’ll make a plan.”

She made a simple chart using sticky notes. “First turn: Bella’s whisper song parade. Second turn: Milo’s silly dance. Third turn: Tiko’s slow march. Fourth turn: Poppy’s sparkle show.” She drew little pictures: a musical note, a drum, a turtle, and a star.

“Can we also have a signal?” Poppy asked. “If it gets too loud or too fast, we do the pause sign.” She held up her paw like a stop sign. “Then we breathe three times.”

Milo nodded. “I can do that. I want to play kind.”

They started with Bella’s whisper song. “La la la,” Bella sang softly. The music was gentle, like the hush of leaves. Poppy’s shoulders relaxed. Milo tiptoed and twirled. Tiko marched slow and happy.

Next came Milo’s silly dance. Boom-ba-boom! He wiggled and giggled. When it got too loud, Poppy held up her paw. Everyone paused. Three breaths. In, out. Milo turned the music down a little. They danced again. It was still fun.

Then Tiko led a slow march, one step, pause, one step, pause. The feathers tickled their cheeks. “This is cozy,” said Poppy, smiling.

Last, Poppy did her sparkle show. She swished her cape and made the lights blink like stars. “Ta-da!” she said. Her friends clapped softly—and then loudly. They all laughed.

When they finished, they had a group hug. The closet felt warm and safe. The air smelled like lavender and happy friends.

“I learned something,” Poppy said. “When I feel mad, I can pause, breathe, and choose. I can use words to say how I feel. And we can make a plan together.”

Milo grinned. “I learned that different is good. Fast and slow can both be fun.”

Bella wiggled her nose. “I learned that whisper songs and silly dances can share the same space.”

Tiko nodded. “I learned that listening helps.”

Poppy hung a new sign in the closet: We are different. We are friends. We take turns. We breathe.

They all smiled at the sign. Then they put the costumes away, neat and tidy, and turned off the twinkly lights.

“See you next time in the cozy closet,” Poppy said.

“See you!” her friends cheered.

The End.
