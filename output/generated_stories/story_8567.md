# Rascal's Rainbow Song

Rascal the raccoon loved making music. He had tiny cymbals on his paws and a little drum around his neck. *Ting-ting-boom!* went his instruments as he danced around the bright art room.

The art room smelled like sweet crayons and had sunshine streaming through big windows. Colorful paintings hung on the walls, and there were tables full of brushes, paper, and glittery supplies everywhere.

"I want to make music with the art supplies!" Rascal said excitedly. He picked up paintbrushes and started drumming on the paint jars. *Tap-tap-tap!* Blue paint splashed onto Bunny's drawing.

"Oh no!" cried Bunny. "You got paint on my picture of my family!"

Rascal didn't stop. He grabbed some crayons and shook them like maracas. *Rattle-rattle!* The crayons flew everywhere, landing on Mouse's clay sculpture.

"Rascal, please stop!" squeaked Mouse. "You're making a big mess!"

But Rascal was having too much fun. He climbed on a table and used colored pencils as drumsticks on the art supply boxes. *Bang-clang!* Pencil shavings scattered all over Squirrel's collage.

"That's enough!" said Squirrel, her fluffy tail twitching with frustration. "You're ruining our art projects!"

All the animals looked very upset. Their beautiful artwork was covered in paint splatters, crayon pieces, and pencil shavings. Rascal suddenly stopped playing and looked around. His heart felt heavy when he saw what he had done.

"I... I was just trying to make music," Rascal said quietly, his ears drooping.

"But you didn't ask if it was okay to use our art supplies," said Bunny gently. "And you didn't listen when we asked you to stop."

Rascal sat down on the floor, feeling very sorry. The art room was quiet except for the soft sound of his sniffling. He realized he had been thinking only about his own fun and not about his friends' feelings.

"I'm really, really sorry," Rascal said, looking at each of his friends. "I didn't mean to mess up your beautiful art. I was so excited about making music that I forgot to be careful and kind."

Bunny looked at her paint-splattered drawing. Then she looked at Rascal's sad face. "I can see you're truly sorry, Rascal. I forgive you."

"Me too," said Mouse, her whiskers twitching with a small smile.

"And me," added Squirrel. "But next time, please ask before using things that aren't yours."

Rascal nodded eagerly. "I promise! Can I help clean up? And maybe... maybe we could make art together?"

The friends spent the afternoon cleaning the art room together. Then Bunny had a wonderful idea. "What if we make musical art? We could paint while you play soft, gentle music!"

Rascal's eyes sparkled. He played a quiet, happy tune on his cymbals while his friends painted. The soft *ting-ting* sounds helped them create the most beautiful pictures they had ever made.

"This is even better than making music alone," Rascal said, watching his friends paint peaceful scenes of gardens and rainbows.

As the sun set through the art room windows, the friends admired their new creations. Rascal had learned that saying sorry and meaning it could help fix hurt feelings. He had also learned that the best music happens when everyone feels happy and safe.

"Thank you for forgiving me," Rascal said, giving each friend a gentle hug.

"That's what friends do," smiled Bunny. "We forgive each other and help each other grow."

The End.
