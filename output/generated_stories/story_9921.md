# Melody's Musical Problem

In a cozy closet behind the winter coats, a little raccoon named Melody had made the most wonderful hideaway. She had soft pillows piled high, twinkling fairy lights strung around the walls, and best of all, her collection of musical instruments scattered everywhere.

Melody loved to play music more than anything. She had a tiny drum that went "tap-tap-tap," shiny bells that jingled "ring-a-ling," and a small xylophone with colorful keys that sang beautiful notes.

One sunny morning, Melody woke up feeling excited. "Today I'm going to write my very first song!" she announced, stretching her fuzzy arms and wiggling her striped tail.

She picked up her drumsticks and began to play. "Tap-tap-TAP!" But something was wrong. The drum didn't sound right at all. It made a dull "thud" instead of a happy "tap."

"Oh no!" Melody cried. She looked closer and saw that the drumhead had a big tear right down the middle.

Melody felt her chest get tight and her paws start to shake. "This is terrible! How can I write my song without my drum?" She threw the drumsticks down and stomped her little feet. "Nothing is working! This is so frustrating!"

The bells jingled sadly as she bumped into them. Even the fairy lights seemed to dim as Melody's frustration grew bigger and bigger.

"I give up!" she shouted, plopping down on her pillows with tears in her eyes.

But then Melody remembered something her mama had taught her. "When you feel frustrated, little one, take three deep breaths and think of a new way to solve your problem."

Melody took a big breath in through her nose. "One..." The air smelled like the lavender sachets hanging in the closet. Another breath. "Two..." She could hear birds chirping outside. One more breath. "Three..." Her tight chest began to feel better.

"Okay," Melody said to herself, wiping her eyes. "Maybe I can't use my drum, but what else can I do?"

She looked around her musical hideaway with fresh eyes. The bells caught the light and sparkled. The xylophone's colors looked bright and cheerful. Even her pillows looked bouncy and fun.

"Wait a minute!" Melody's eyes lit up. "I can make drum sounds with other things!"

She picked up a pillow and gave it a gentle pat. "Poof!" It made a soft, squishy sound. She tried patting her hands on her knees. "Pat-pat-pat!" That sounded more like a drum.

Then Melody had an even better idea. She arranged her pillows in a circle and began patting them in rhythm while shaking her bells. "Poof-pat-ring, poof-pat-ring!"

"This sounds amazing!" she giggled, adding the xylophone to her new song. "Poof-pat-ring-ding!"

Soon, Melody was dancing around her closet hideaway, making the most beautiful music she had ever created. It was different from what she had planned, but it was even more special because she had figured it out all by herself.

"I did it!" Melody cheered. "When I felt frustrated, I took deep breaths and found a new way to solve my problem. My song is perfect just the way it is!"

From that day on, whenever Melody faced a problem, she remembered to breathe deeply and look for new solutions. And her closet hideaway was always filled with the happiest music in the whole house.

The End.
