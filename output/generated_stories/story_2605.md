# Ellie’s Big Crafting Hug

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a warm, wiggly trunk. She lived in a comfortable nursery with bright curtains, a cozy rug, and shelves full of paper, crayons, and glitter that sparkled like tiny stars. The nursery smelled like clean soap and orange slices, and the sun made friendly squares of light on the floor.

One sunny morning, Miss Dot, their kind caregiver, clapped her hands. “Today,” she said with a smile, “we will make a giant Friendship Mural! We will craft it together.”

Ellie’s ears flapped with excitement. “Together,” she echoed softly. She loved the word. It felt warm, like a hug.

Around the big table sat Ellie’s friends: Pippa the penguin chick with happy flippers, Niko the bunny with twitchy whiskers, and Tiko the tiny turtle with a shiny green shell. A big roll of paper stretched across the table like a snowy hill.

Miss Dot set out supplies. “We have colored paper, glue sticks, safety scissors, cotton balls, and ribbon. Remember—cooperation means we help and share. We use kind words so everyone’s ideas fit.”

Ellie nodded. She touched the paper with her trunk. It felt smooth and crisp. “Let’s make a sunshine!” she said.

“I want to make a pond!” peeped Tiko.

“I want a rainbow,” whispered Pippa.

“I want big carrots,” giggled Niko.

Ellie’s tummy felt fluttery. So many ideas! The table got busy. Pippa reached for the blue paper. Niko reached for it too. Their paws bumped. “Hey!” said Niko.

Pippa’s eyes grew wide. “I need blue for the rainbow.”

Tiko tugged a glue stick. “I need glue for my pond,” he said. The glue stick slipped. Plop! It rolled off the table and landed with a soft thud on the rug.

Ellie took a deep breath. She remembered Miss Dot’s words: Use kind words. Help and share. “Friends,” Ellie said in her calm, rumbly voice, “we can work together. We can talk about our plan.”

Miss Dot smiled. “Great idea, Ellie. Let’s pause and communicate.”

Ellie pointed her trunk to the big paper. “What if the rainbow goes up here,” she said gently, “and the sunshine sits next to it, warm and bright? The pond can be below, shining like a mirror. And the carrots can grow near the pond, so they have water.”

Pippa’s flippers clapped. “Yes! The rainbow can curve over everything.”

Niko’s whiskers wiggled. “Carrots like water,” he said. “That makes sense.”

Tiko nodded. “My pond can have lily pads.”

Ellie smiled. The fluttery feeling softened to a cozy glow. “We can share the blue,” she said. “Pippa can use some for the rainbow. Tiko can use some for the pond. I can cut sun rays from yellow.”

They made a plan:
- Pippa would cut big arches of color.
- Tiko would make a round pond.
- Niko would snip green stems and orange carrots.
- Ellie would craft the sunshine and glue everything in place.

“Teamwork,” Ellie hummed.

They began. Snip, snip went the scissors. Swish, swish went the paper. The glue smelled sweet, like soft berries. Ellie dabbed glue carefully. “Dot, dot, not a lot,” she sang.

Pippa hesitated. “My red arch is crooked.”

Ellie leaned close. “I can hold the paper steady,” she said. Pippa smiled and snipped again. The arch was smoother.

Tiko frowned at a sticky flipper. “Oops,” he said.

Ellie offered a damp cloth. “Here you go.” Tiko’s eyes sparkled. “Thanks.”

Niko’s carrots kept falling over. “They won’t stand,” he huffed.

Ellie thought. “Let’s talk it out. What do carrots need?” she asked.

“Leaves!” Niko said.

“Glue under the leaves will help them stick,” Ellie said.

Together they pressed the leaves down. Squish! The carrots stayed.

They added cotton-ball clouds that felt soft and fluffy. Ellie’s sunshine got ribbon rays that rustled like quiet wind. Pippa’s rainbow arched across the top like a happy bridge. Tiko’s pond gleamed blue with tiny green lily pads. Niko’s carrots stood proudly near the water.

Miss Dot came to see. “I love how you cooperated,” she said. “I heard kind words. I saw helping hands and sharing.”

Ellie felt big inside, not in size, but in heart. She looked at her friends and said, “When we talk and share, our ideas fit together.”

Pippa nodded. “Cooperation makes it fun.”

Tiko added, “And easier!”

Niko grinned. “And our mural looks amazing.”

They all stepped back. The mural shone in the sun. It looked like a big crafting hug from everyone to everyone.

Ellie wrapped her trunk gently around her friends in a little group cuddle. “Thank you for working with me,” she whispered.

Miss Dot hung the mural on the wall. “This is our Friendship Mural,” she said. “It shows what we can do when we communicate and cooperate.”

Ellie’s ears wiggled happily. She felt proud, calm, and cozy. “Next time,” she said, “let’s make a night sky with stars.”

Pippa, Tiko, and Niko cheered. “Together!”

Ellie looked at the mural one more time. She learned something important: When we use kind words, listen to each other, and share our tools, we can make something beautiful—together.

The End.
