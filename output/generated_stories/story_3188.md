# Cubby’s Turn in the Warm Kitchen

The warm kitchen smelled like sweet bread and cinnamon. The sun made yellow squares on the floor. Pots and pans were quiet on their hooks. A little friendly bear cub named Cubby stood on a soft blue stool, wiggling his toes. He wore a tiny apron with green leaves on it.

“I want to stir! I want to stir!” Cubby said, looking at the big mixing bowl. Mama Bear smiled and patted his paw.

“We will take turns,” Mama Bear said gently. “First, we gather, then we mix, then we taste.”

Cubby’s cousin, Berry, a small bear with a bright red bow, clapped her paws. “I like taking turns,” Berry said. “It feels fair.”

Cubby’s nose twitched. He could smell vanilla, honey, and warm oats. “Mmm,” he hummed. “Oat cookies!”

Mama Bear placed a wooden spoon on the counter. “We will make eco-cookies,” she said. “We use only what we need, and we make no waste. We care for our home and our Earth.”

Cubby nodded. He liked helping the Earth. He picked up a cloth bag and shook it. “No crinkly plastic,” he said proudly. “Just our cloth bag.”

“Good choice,” Mama Bear said. “Now, who helps measure the oats?”

“I do!” Cubby shouted, grabbing the scoop.

Berry stepped back. Her ears drooped a little. “I wanted to help too,” she whispered.

Cubby didn’t hear her. Scoop, scoop, scoop! He poured oats into the bowl. Some oats spilled and made a soft “shhh” sound on the counter and the floor.

Mama Bear touched Cubby’s shoulder. “Pause, Cubby,” she said softly. “Look at Berry’s face.”

Cubby turned. Berry’s eyes were shiny. Her bow drooped. Cubby’s tummy felt wiggly. He didn’t want Berry to be sad.

“I… I forgot to listen,” Cubby said, rubbing his paws together. “I got too excited.”

Mama Bear nodded. “Excited is okay. But our words help. Let’s use peaceful words to share and to take turns.”

Cubby took a deep breath. He smelled the oats and the honey. “Berry,” he said in a calm voice, “I am sorry I didn’t hear you. I feel excited to help. Can we take turns? What would you like to do?”

Berry’s ears perked up. She smiled. “Thank you for asking, Cubby. I would like to pour the honey.”

“Great idea,” Mama Bear said. “Cubby, you can hold the bowl steady. Berry, you can pour slowly.”

Berry picked up the honey jar. The honey slid out in a golden ribbon. Drip, drip, plop! Cubby held the bowl with both paws. He felt proud and calm.

“Good teamwork,” Mama Bear said. “Peaceful words make peaceful paws.”

Cubby giggled. “Peaceful paws!”

When the honey was in, Mama Bear held up the spoon. “Now we mix. Who would like to go first?”

Cubby looked at Berry. He remembered her sad eyes. He remembered his wiggly tummy. He took another deep breath.

“Berry, would you like the first turn?” he asked.

Berry beamed. “Yes, please!” She stirred slowly. Swish, swish, swish. The spoon made a happy sound.

After a few swishes, Berry passed the spoon to Cubby. “Your turn now,” she said.

Cubby stirred next. He felt the thick dough move under the spoon. Squish, squish. It smelled sweet and warm. He passed the spoon back. Stir, pass. Stir, pass. Taking turns made the work easy and fun.

When the dough was ready, Mama Bear set out a big baking sheet with a paper liner. “We use this paper again and again,” she said. “It helps us care for the Earth.”

Cubby scooped cookie dough into small round balls. Berry pressed them gently. Pat, pat. “These look like little moons,” Berry said.

Mama Bear nodded. “And now, we clean as we go.” She handed Cubby a small brush and dustpan. “We don’t want to waste food, and we keep our kitchen safe.”

Cubby looked at the little oat spills on the floor. He felt a tiny grump rise up. “I want to make more cookies,” he said.

Mama Bear crouched down, eye to eye. “Let’s use peaceful words for our feelings,” she said. “What do you feel, Cubby?”

Cubby pressed his paw to his chest. “I feel disappointed. I want to make more cookies now.”

“That is a real feeling,” Mama Bear said. “What could we say to help?”

Cubby thought. He smelled the cinnamon. He heard the oven hum, warm and low. He felt the brush in his paw.

“I can say,” Cubby began slowly, “I feel disappointed, but I can take turns with the cleaning too. Berry, would you like to brush or hold the pan?”

Berry smiled. “I will hold the pan,” she said. “You brush. Then we switch.”

Cubby brushed the oats into the pan. Swish, sweep, swish. Berry held steady. Then they swapped. It felt fair and kind.

Soon the kitchen was tidy again. The oven beeped. Beep! Beep! A warm cookie smell filled the air. Mama Bear opened the oven with a soft whoosh. Golden cookies rested on the tray, tiny moons in a warm sky.

“Time to taste,” Mama Bear said.

Cubby looked at Berry. “You can taste first,” he said.

“Thank you,” Berry said, taking a small bite. “Mmm! Sweet and cozy.”

Cubby took his bite next. The cookie was soft and warm. His heart felt warm too.

Mama Bear hugged them both. “Today you used peaceful words. You listened. You took turns. You cared for your kitchen and your Earth.”

Cubby nodded. “When I feel big feelings,” he said, “I can stop, breathe, and use calm words.”

Berry nodded too. “And we can take turns and clean up together.”

They all smiled. The sun painted bright squares on the clean floor. The kitchen felt warm and happy, like a hug.

The End.
