# Koala Kiki’s Porch Day

Kiki was a calm koala who loved her peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like warm eucalyptus leaves. The wooden floor felt smooth under her paws. A soft breeze tickled her ears. Birds chirped, “Peep-peep!” and the porch swing creaked, “Creeeak,” in a gentle rhythm.

Kiki took a deep breath. “Ahh,” she said, patting her chest. “This is my calm place.”

Her friends came to visit. Benny the bunny hopped up the steps. Mimi the mouse waddled behind him with a tiny backpack. Tito the turtle slowly climbed, step by careful step.

“Good morning!” Kiki said with a smile.

“Good morning!” her friends cheered.

On the porch lay a big, round basket. Inside were puzzles, picture cards, and crayons. A small sign said, “Porch Play — We take turns!”

Kiki clapped softly. “Today is Porch Play Day. We will try new things. We will use our thinking. We will take turns. We will be calm. We can do it!”

Benny’s ears wiggled. “I want to go first!” he said.

Kiki nodded. “Let’s use our turn stone.” She held up a smooth blue stone that felt cool in her paw. “When you hold the stone, it’s your turn.”

Benny took the stone. “My turn!” He pulled out a shape puzzle with a circle, a square, and a triangle. “I can do this fast!”

He tried to fit the triangle into the circle spot. Thunk. It didn’t fit. Benny frowned. “I can’t do it.”

Kiki sat beside him. “Let’s take a calm breath together,” she said. “Smell the flower.” They breathed in. “Blow the bubble.” They breathed out.

Benny smiled a little. “Okay.” He looked again. “Triangle goes in the triangle!” He slid the piece in. Click!

“You did it,” Kiki said. “You looked. You tried. You believed in you.”

Benny beamed. “I can do hard things!”

Mimi held out her tiny paw. “May I have a turn with the stone, please?”

“Yes,” Benny said, handing it over. “Your turn.”

Mimi chose picture cards with faces. One card showed a face with big eyes and a wobbly mouth. “This face is… worried?” she guessed.

Kiki nodded. “What can we do when we feel worried?”

Mimi put a paw on her tummy. “Hand on tummy. Slow breaths,” she said. She breathed in and out. “I can say, ‘I am safe.’”

“That is wellness,” Kiki said gently. “Your body and your feelings matter.”

Tito the turtle finally reached the top step. “Whew,” he said, smiling. “I made it!”

Kiki waved. “Welcome, Tito. Would you like the turn stone?”

Tito took it slowly. He picked a counting game with little wooden leaves. “I am slow,” he said. “Sometimes friends rush me.”

Kiki sat on the creaking swing and patted the seat next to her. “Slow is okay,” she said. “Slow can be smart. Use your thinking.”

Tito set the leaves in a row. “One, two, three…” He paused. “Hmm. Is this four?” He looked unsure.

“Let’s think it through,” Kiki said. “Touch and count.”

Tito touched each leaf. “One, two, three, four.” His eyes lit up. “Four!”

“You did it,” Kiki said, tapping his shell lightly. “You trusted yourself.”

The breeze grew a little stronger. The wind chimes sang, “Ting-ting-ting!” A sweet eucalyptus smell drifted by. Kiki closed her eyes and listened. Her heart felt steady and warm.

Now it was Kiki’s turn. She held the blue stone. “I want to try the tall block tower,” she said. “It might wobble.”

Benny’s nose twitched. “What if it falls?”

Kiki placed a block. “If it falls, we try again.”

Mimi squeaked, “We can help!”

Tito nodded. “We can plan.”

Kiki smiled. “Let’s think. Big blocks on the bottom. Little blocks on top.” She pressed a big block down. “I believe in me,” she whispered. “I can stay calm.”

They built together, taking turns with the stone. The tower grew higher. The porch swing creaked. The chimes tinkled. Their paws and claws tapped the wood. Tap, tap, tap.

Then—wobble-wobble—plop! The tower fell.

Benny gasped. Mimi squeaked. Tito blinked.

Kiki put a paw on her heart. “Hand on heart. Breath in. Breath out,” she said. “It’s okay. I can try again. I trust myself.”

Benny smiled. “We trust you, too!”

Kiki looked at the blocks. “What can we try next?”

Tito raised a slow paw. “We can make a wide base.”

Mimi pointed. “And we can check each layer.”

Kiki nodded. “Good thinking.” They tried again. Big blocks on the bottom. Press, press. Check. Little blocks on top. Press, press. Check.

The tower stood tall and steady, glowing in the warm porch light.

“We did it!” the friends cheered.

Kiki’s eyes shone. “We believed in ourselves. We used calm breaths. We took turns. We used our thinking.”

Benny hopped. “I feel strong inside.”

Mimi hugged her backpack. “I feel safe and brave.”

Tito touched the tower gently. “I feel proud.”

Kiki wrapped her soft arms around her friends. The porch felt extra peaceful now. The wind sang a happy song. The swing creaked a soft, “Good job.”

Kiki said, “Here is our lesson: When we feel unsure, we can breathe, think, and try. We can believe in ourselves. That is self-confidence. When we are calm and take turns, our bodies feel good, and our minds get smart. We can do hard things, together and by ourselves.”

Her friends nodded. They sat on the porch, sipping cool water from little cups and munching eucalyptus crackers and carrot sticks. Crunch, crunch. Sip, sip.

Kiki looked at the sky. It was soft blue with cotton clouds. She felt warm, calm, and brave.

“I believe in me,” she whispered.

“We believe in us,” her friends said.

The End.
