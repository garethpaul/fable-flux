# Miko's Rooftop Rescue

The warm morning sun shone down on the tall apartment building. Up on the rooftop, a brave little monkey named Miko was swinging from the clothesline poles. His brown fur sparkled in the sunlight as he played.

"Wheee!" Miko called out happily. He loved playing up high where he could see the whole neighborhood below.

Suddenly, Miko heard a tiny sound. "Meow! Meow!" It was very soft and scared.

Miko stopped swinging. He listened carefully with his big ears. "Meow! Meow!" There it was again!

Miko looked all around the rooftop. Behind the big air conditioning unit, he found a small orange kitten. The kitten was stuck between two metal pipes!

"Oh no!" said Miko. "How did you get stuck there, little friend?"

The kitten looked up with big, worried eyes. "I was chasing a butterfly," she mewed sadly. "I squeezed through here, but now I can't get out! I'm scared!"

Miko felt his heart beating fast. He wanted to help, but the space was too small for him to fit through. What could he do?

First, Miko tried pulling on the pipes. They wouldn't budge. They were too heavy and strong.

"Don't worry," Miko told the kitten gently. "I'll figure this out. My name is Miko. What's yours?"

"I'm Whiskers," the kitten replied, her voice shaking a little.

Miko sat down and thought hard. He looked around the rooftop for something that might help. There were flower pots, a garden hose, some old boxes, and a long wooden stick.

"The stick!" Miko said excitedly. "Maybe I can use this!"

But when Miko tried to push the kitten out with the stick, Whiskers got more scared. "That's too scary!" she cried.

Miko stopped right away. He realized the stick might hurt his new friend. He needed to think of something gentler.

Then Miko noticed the garden hose. It was soft and flexible. "Whiskers," he called, "I have an idea! Can you grab onto this hose with your teeth?"

Miko carefully fed one end of the hose through the gap. Whiskers bit down gently on the soft rubber.

"Now I'm going to pull very slowly and gently," Miko explained. "Tell me if it hurts, okay?"

"Okay," Whiskers mewed bravely.

Miko pulled the hose bit by bit. Whiskers used her paws to push against the pipes. Little by little, she started to slide through the narrow space.

"It's working!" Whiskers called out happily.

With one final gentle tug, Whiskers popped free! She tumbled onto the rooftop, safe and sound.

"Thank you, Miko!" Whiskers purred, rubbing against his furry arm. "You saved me!"

Miko felt proud and happy. "I'm so glad you're okay! I was worried at first, but I knew if I thought carefully, I could find a way to help."

"You were so brave and smart," Whiskers said. "And you listened when I said the stick was scary. That made me feel safe."

Miko and Whiskers became fast friends. They spent the rest of the morning playing together on the sunny rooftop, being very careful to stay away from tight spaces.

As they played, Miko felt good inside. He had learned that when someone needs help, taking time to think of different solutions can make all the difference. And listening to how others feel is just as important as being brave.

The End.
