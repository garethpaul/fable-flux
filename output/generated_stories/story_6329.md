# Ellie's Big Treehouse Problem

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved her special treehouse. It was painted bright yellow like sunshine and had red polka dots all over. Every morning, she would climb up the wooden ladder with her trunk wrapped around each rung.

"Good morning, treehouse!" Ellie would trumpet happily. The birds would chirp back, and the leaves would rustle in the warm breeze.

But one day, Ellie discovered a big problem. Her friend Kiko the koala from Australia was coming to visit, along with Pedro the penguin from Antarctica and Zara the zebra from Africa. They all wanted to play in her treehouse together!

"Oh no!" Ellie gasped, looking at her tiny treehouse. "How will we all fit? I'm getting bigger every day, and my friends are all different sizes too!"

Ellie sat down and thought hard. She tapped her trunk on her chin - *tap, tap, tap* - the way she always did when she was thinking.

"I need a strategy," she said to herself. "That means I need a smart plan!"

First, Ellie tried squeezing everyone inside the old treehouse. But it was much too crowded! Pedro's flippers got stuck, Kiko couldn't hang properly, and Zara's stripes got all wrinkled.

"This isn't working," Ellie sighed. "But I won't give up!"

Then Ellie had an idea. "What if I ask each friend what would make them comfortable?" she wondered. "Maybe they know things I don't know!"

She called Kiko first. "What do you need to feel happy and safe?" Ellie asked.

"I love to hang from eucalyptus branches," Kiko said. "They smell so fresh and minty!"

Next, she called Pedro. "What makes you feel at home?" 

"I like cool, smooth surfaces where I can slide," Pedro explained. "And I love the color blue like the ocean!"

Finally, she asked Zara. "What would make you feel welcome?"

"I love open spaces where I can run and stretch," Zara shared. "And I enjoy sitting in dappled sunlight!"

Ellie's eyes sparkled with excitement. "I have a new plan!" she trumpeted.

She worked all week, building and creating. She added a eucalyptus branch swing for Kiko that smelled wonderful. She built a smooth blue slide that curved around the tree trunk for Pedro. She created a bigger platform with open railings where Zara could stretch and feel the breeze.

And for herself? She made the floor extra strong to hold her growing weight, with soft cushions that felt like clouds under her feet.

When her friends arrived, they gasped with delight!

"Wow!" said Kiko, swinging happily. "This is perfect!"

"Amazing!" said Pedro, sliding down with a joyful squeak.

"Incredible!" said Zara, stretching in the dappled sunlight.

"But how did you know exactly what we needed?" they all asked together.

Ellie smiled proudly. "I used strategy! I made a plan, and I listened to each of you. I learned that being open-minded means thinking about what others need, not just what I want."

"You thought about friends from all around the world!" said Pedro.

"That makes you a global citizen!" added Zara.

"And you created something totally new and innovative!" finished Kiko.

As they all played together in the perfect treehouse, Ellie felt warm and happy inside. The sun filtered through the leaves, the eucalyptus smelled fresh, and everyone was laughing together.

"The best strategy," Ellie realized, "is caring about others and working together to solve problems!"

From that day on, Ellie's treehouse became known as the most welcoming place in the whole forest, where friends from everywhere could feel at home.

The End.
