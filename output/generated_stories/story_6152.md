# Ollie Otter’s Living Room Parade

Ollie the playful otter wiggled his whiskers and patted his soft, shiny fur. “I’m ready to play!” he cheered. He slid across the smooth rug of the playful living room. The rug felt warm and tickly under his belly. Sunlight made little sparkles on the floor, and the air smelled like fresh crayons.

On the low table sat a box of colorful blocks, a tambourine that went shaka-shaka, and a basket of costumes—feathery capes, silly hats, and a red clown nose. Ollie’s feet went tap-tap-tap with excitement. “Let’s have a parade!” he squeaked.

His friends were there too. Mia Mouse wore a twirly blue ribbon. Ben Bear had a soft, fuzzy vest. Pip Parrot fluffed green and gold feathers. They all smiled. They all wanted to play.

Ollie grabbed the tambourine. Shaka-shaka! “I go first!” he said, bouncing. He shook it fast and loud. Shaka-shaka-shaka!

Mia covered her ears. “Too loud, Ollie,” she said gently. “My ears are small.”

Ben Bear reached for the blocks. “I want to build a tall tower,” he rumbled softly.

Pip Parrot squawked, “Costumes! Costumes! I want a shiny cape!”

Everyone reached at once. Hands and paws and wings bumped. Blocks clacked. The tambourine thumped. Ollie’s tummy felt twisty. He wanted all the things, right now.

Mia took a little breath. “Let’s take turns,” she said. “We can all have fun.”

Ollie’s whiskers drooped. “But I want the tambourine,” he whispered.

Ben Bear nodded. “We can share,” he said. “I can build first, then you can shake.”

Pip Parrot tilted his head. “Different is good,” he chirped. “I like shiny capes. You like music. Mia likes quiet. Ben likes building. We can make a big parade with all our fun.”

Ollie blinked. He looked at his friends. He listened. He took a slow, deep breath. In through his nose. Out through his mouth. The air smelled like orange peels from snack time. His tummy felt softer.

“Okay,” Ollie said. “I can wait. I can take turns. I can be gentle.” He handed the tambourine to Mia. “You choose first.”

Mia smiled and shook the tambourine softly. Shaka… shaka… “Thank you, Ollie,” she said. “That was respectful.”

“What is respectful?” Ollie asked.

Mia tapped the tambourine one-two. “Respect means we care about each other,” she said. “We listen. We use kind bodies. We wait our turn.”

Ben Bear stacked blocks—click, clack, clink. “And we keep each other safe,” he added, placing a block slowly. “Soft hands. Soft voices.”

Pip Parrot fluttered down with a gold cape that whispered swish-swish. “Respect is when we like our differences,” he sang. “We cheer for them!”

Ollie’s eyes grew wide. “I can try,” he said.

They made a plan. Ben built the parade float with blocks: a square base, a tall tower, and a tiny seat for Pip. Mia kept the beat—shaka… shaka… tap. Pip chose costumes for everyone—a silver star hat for Mia, a spotted vest for Ben, a tiny red nose for Ollie, and his own shiny cape.

Ollie waited. His paws tingled, but he hugged a cushion and took another slow breath. “I can wait,” he told himself. “I am strong. I am kind.”

“Your turn, Ollie,” Mia said, passing the tambourine into his careful paws.

He shook it at a gentle pace—shaka, shaka, shaka. It sounded like soft rain on the window. Ollie smiled. “I like this,” he said. “It feels good to be gentle.”

“Parade time!” Pip called. The living room smelled like crayons and oranges and a tiny bit like popcorn from last night. The rug felt bouncy. The tower glittered in the sun.

Ben pushed the block float slowly. “Steady, steady,” he murmured.

Mia marched and tapped her ribbon on her knee. Tap-tap.

Pip perched on the tiny seat and sang, “We are different! We are friends!”

Ollie led with the tambourine, keeping the beat for everyone. Shaka, shaka, tap. He watched his friends’ faces. Mia’s eyes were soft. Ben’s smile was big. Pip’s feathers shimmered. Ollie felt warm and proud.

They paraded around the couch, under the table tunnel, and past the bookshelf mountain. When the tower leaned, Ollie stopped and said, “Let’s slow down.” They all paused. Ben fixed the blocks. “Thanks, Ollie,” he said. “That was respectful and safe.”

After the parade, they sat in a cozy circle on the rug. They took sips of cool water. Drip, gulp, ahh. The room was quiet in a happy way.

Mia said, “What did we learn?”

Ollie raised his paw. “Respect means we listen and we care,” he said. “We take turns. We use soft hands. We cheer for our differences.”

Pip clapped his wings. “And when we wait,” he chirped, “everyone gets a turn!”

Ben nodded. “We had more fun together,” he said. “Our parade was better with all of us.”

Ollie hugged his friends. “I felt wiggly at first,” he said. “But I breathed slow, and I waited. That helped my body be calm.”

Mia squeezed his paw. “That is self-control,” she said. “That is respect for you and for us.”

Ollie’s whiskers twitched with joy. “I like respect,” he said. “It makes the room feel comfy. It makes my heart feel big.”

They put the toys away, one by one. Click, clack, shaka, swish. Then they shared a sweet orange. Peel, peel, peel. The slices were juicy and bright. Ollie passed the last slice to Ben. “Your turn,” he said with a grin.

Ben Bear chuckled. “Thank you, friend.”

Ollie smiled. “We are different. We are kind. We respect each other.”

And the playful living room felt extra cozy, like a warm hug.

The End.
