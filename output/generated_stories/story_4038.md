# Penny Penguin’s Rooftop Concert

Penny was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a bouncy waddle. She lived in a tall, cozy building with a flat, sunny rooftop. The rooftop had soft chalk drawings, a little table, and a view of the sky. Penny loved to sing there. She loved music most of all.

“Today, I will have a rooftop concert!” Penny chirped. She flapped her flippers and smiled. “I will sing, and I will play my little drum.”

Penny waddled to a big blue box. It held her music things: a small drum, two tiny shakers, a triangle, and her song cards. The box was messy. The drum hid under the triangle. The shakers rolled around. The song cards were bent.

Penny frowned a little. “Hmm,” she said softly. “I can’t find the drumstick.”

She looked under the table. Tap, tap. She looked behind the box. Rustle, rustle. She peeked under a blanket. No drumstick.

Her friend, Milo the mouse, popped up from a flower pot. “Hi, Penny! I came to hear your music!” he squeaked. “Squeak-squeak! Are you ready?”

Penny took a deep breath. The wind felt cool. The sun felt warm. “I am not ready,” she said. “I can’t find my drumstick. My box is messy.”

Milo twitched his whiskers. “Maybe we can get ready together,” he said kindly. “Let’s prepare!”

Penny nodded. “Yes. Preparation helps music.”

They sat on the soft rooftop floor. Penny made three neat piles: drums, shakers, and bells. She smoothed the song cards and put them in a little folder. She found a ribbon and tied the two shakers together so they wouldn’t roll away. She put the triangle on a soft cloth so it wouldn’t clang by accident.

Milo clapped. “Good organizing, Penny!”

Penny smiled. “Thank you. Organizing helps me feel calm.”

She still needed the drumstick. Penny closed her eyes. She took a slow, calm breath in. She let it out, like a gentle “whoooo.” She listened. Far away, she heard a bird sing. Near her, she heard the light tinkle of the triangle in the breeze. Then—tap-tap—she heard something by the plant.

Penny opened her eyes. “I hear a tap!” She looked in the flower pot and giggled. “Here it is!” The drumstick had fallen into the dirt.

Milo laughed. “Your ears helped you.”

Penny wiped the drumstick with a soft cloth. “Now I am ready.” She stood tall. “Friends, concert time!”

Two pigeons fluttered down. “Coo-coo! We love music,” they said. A sleepy cat peeked from a window. “Mrrrow? A concert?” A ladybug landed on Penny’s flipper. Tiny feet tickled. “I like quiet songs,” the ladybug whispered.

Penny looked at her organized box. She felt calm and happy. “We will start with a gentle song,” she said. “Then we will play a loud, fun beat. We will share the music.”

Penny tapped the drum softly. Tip-tip. She sang, “Breathe in slow, breathe out slow, let the soft drums gently go.” The wind hummed. The sky looked big and blue. The ladybug swayed.

Milo shook the shakers very softly. Shhh-shhh, like rain on leaves. The pigeons cooed in time. The sleepy cat purred, a low rumble.

Penny smiled. “Now a bouncy beat!” She tapped faster: tum-tum, tum-tum! Milo shook louder: shaka-shaka! The triangle chimed: ting! ting! The rooftop felt alive with sound. Penny’s heart felt light.

Then the shakers slipped from Milo’s paws. They bounced and rolled toward the edge. “Oh no!” Milo squeaked. He looked scared.

Penny held up a flipper. “Calm and ready,” she said gently. She put a small box as a stop near the edge. The shakers bumped the box and stayed safe.

Penny picked them up and tied them back with the ribbon. “This is why we prepare,” she said. “Preparation keeps music safe and fun.”

Milo nodded. “I learned! Organize, then play.”

Penny turned to the friends. “Would you like to share the music?” she asked.

The pigeons each took a shaker. Coo-coo-shake! The ladybug tapped the triangle with a tiny touch: ting! The cat swished her tail in time and purred: prrr-rrr-rrr.

They made a rooftop band. Penny counted, “One, two, three!” Together they played a happy song. It sounded bright and warm. It smelled like fresh air and sun on wood. Penny felt the drumbeat in her belly. Everyone smiled.

When the song was done, Penny said, “Thank you for sharing.” She placed each instrument back in its own spot. Drum in the drum spot. Shakers in the shaker spot. Triangle on the soft cloth. Song cards in the folder.

Milo beamed. “Your music was beautiful. Your plan worked.”

Penny nodded. “Today I learned: Music is more fun when we prepare, organize, and stay calm. Sharing makes the song even sweeter.”

The wind gave a gentle “whoooo,” like a soft cheer. The sky seemed to clap with light. Penny hugged her friends with her flippers.

“Rooftop concert again tomorrow?” asked the cat.

“Yes,” said Penny. “We will get ready, stay calm, and make music together.”

The End.
