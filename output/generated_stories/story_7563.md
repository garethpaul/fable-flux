# Pip's Rainbow Garden

In a cozy closet hideaway behind old coats and boxes, lived a little singing bird named Pip. She had bright yellow feathers that sparkled like sunshine. Her tiny nest was made of soft ribbons and cotton balls.

Every morning, Pip would sing her happy song: "Tweet-tweet-trill, what shall I eat? Tweet-tweet-trill, something sweet!"

But Pip had a big problem. She only wanted to eat sugary seeds and candy crumbs she found on the floor. Her beautiful yellow feathers were getting dull. She felt tired and sleepy all the time.

"Why don't I feel good?" Pip chirped sadly to herself. She looked in the small mirror hanging on the closet wall. Her feathers looked gray instead of bright yellow.

One day, Pip heard voices outside her closet hideaway. Two children were talking.

"Mom says we need to eat colorful foods," said a little girl. "Red apples, orange carrots, green broccoli!"

"Like a rainbow on our plate!" said her brother.

Pip's tiny heart beat fast. "A rainbow? On a plate?" she wondered.

She peeked out of her closet and saw the most amazing thing. The children's lunch had red strawberries, orange slices, yellow corn, green peas, blue blueberries, and purple grapes.

"Tweet! That looks like a beautiful rainbow!" Pip sang softly.

The little girl heard her. "Oh! A pretty bird! Are you hungry, little one?"

Pip nodded her small head. The girl gently placed some colorful foods near the closet door. "These will help you grow strong and healthy!"

Pip tried a tiny blueberry first. It was sweet and juicy! Then she pecked at a piece of orange. It tasted like sunshine! The green pea was crunchy and fresh.

"Mmm!" Pip chirped happily. "These taste wonderful!"

Every day, the kind children shared their rainbow foods with Pip. She learned that red foods helped her heart, orange foods helped her eyes see better, and green foods made her strong.

"I want to share too!" Pip decided. She began collecting healthy seeds from outside and bringing them to other birds in the neighborhood. Soon, all the birds were eating rainbow foods together.

Pip's feathers grew bright and shiny again. She felt full of energy! Her morning song became even more beautiful: "Tweet-tweet-trill, rainbow foods are neat! Tweet-tweet-trill, healthy foods to eat!"

The children clapped when they heard her new song. "Look how bright and happy Pip is now!" they said.

Pip realized something important. When she ate good foods and shared with others, everyone felt better. The whole bird community was healthier and happier.

From her closet hideaway, Pip could see birds from all over the world through the window. She sang her rainbow song loudly so they could all hear. Soon, birds everywhere were learning about eating colorful, healthy foods.

"Sharing good food makes the whole world better!" Pip sang proudly.

Now Pip's closet hideaway was filled with friends. They shared rainbow seeds, told stories, and sang together. Pip learned that taking care of herself and sharing with others made everyone stronger.

Every morning, Pip would look in her mirror and see bright, beautiful yellow feathers. She felt proud knowing she was healthy and helping others too.

"Good food, good friends, good world!" became Pip's favorite song.

The End.
