# Joey Jumps to Fix-It Day

Joey was a joyful kangaroo joey with soft brown fur and a bouncy tail. One sunny morning, he hopped to the garden shed. The shed smelled like fresh soil and lemon soap. The door creaked, “Eee-eee,” as Joey pushed it open.

Inside, there were rakes, pots, and a big box of tools. The village helper day was coming. Joey wanted to help fix the garden toys for everyone. “I can do it!” he said, his ears wiggling.

“Hi, Joey!” chirped a bluebird on the window. “Need a helper?”

“Yes, please!” said Joey. “Let’s fix the wheelbarrow for the park path. It helps everyone keep the park clean.” Joey liked helping the town. It felt good and important.

He found a wobbly wheelbarrow. The wheel was loose. Joey picked up a shiny screwdriver. “I turn, and it tightens,” he said.

He tried to turn the screw. It did not move. Joey pushed. He grunted. “Hnnnng.” The screwdriver slipped and clinked on the floor. Joey’s cheeks felt hot. His tail drooped.

“I can’t do it,” he whispered. His tummy felt twisty. He stomped his foot. “This is too hard!”

The bluebird tilted her head. “It’s okay to feel mad,” she sang softly. “Try a calm-down plan.”

Joey took a big breath in. He smelled clean wood and a hint of mint from a broom. He breathed out slowly. “Whooo.” He put his paw on his chest. “I can try again.”

He remembered his mom’s words: “Stop. Breathe. Think. Try.” Joey said them out loud. “Stop. Breathe. Think. Try.”

He looked around. The shed was warm and golden with sun beams. Dust sparkles swirled in the light. Joey smiled a little.

“Maybe a different tool?” he said. He tried a small wrench. “Clink, clink.” Still stuck.

Joey felt the mad feeling bump back. His nose wrinkled. He wanted to shout. He almost did.

Then he remembered civic responsibility. “This is for everyone,” he said. “The park needs this. I can ask for help. That’s part of helping, too.”

He hopped to the door. “Mrs. Mole?” he called to a neighbor passing by. Mrs. Mole wore round glasses and a bright scarf.

“Hello, Joey,” said Mrs. Mole. “What do you need?”

“I’m fixing the wheelbarrow for village helper day,” Joey said. “But the screw is stuck. I feel frustrated. Can you help?”

Mrs. Mole smiled. “Thank you for asking. Teamwork time!”

They went back to the shed. The bluebird sang a cheer. “Tweet-tweet, team!”

Mrs. Mole touched the screw. “Hmm, it is tight. Let’s try a drop of oil. And a cloth so our paws don’t slip.” She handed Joey a soft rag. It felt warm in his paws. The oil smelled a little like olives.

“Open minds help us find new ideas,” Mrs. Mole said. “Let’s listen and learn from each other.”

Joey nodded. “I can learn,” he said. He dabbed the oil. He wrapped the cloth and held the tool again. “Turn slow,” Mrs. Mole said.

Joey took another deep breath. “Stop. Breathe. Think. Try.” He turned slowly. “Creeeeeak.” The screw moved a tiny bit.

“It moved!” Joey’s eyes sparkled. He giggled and bounced, but just a little, so the tool stayed steady. The bluebird chirped happy notes. “Tweet-tee-dee!”

“Now a little more,” said Mrs. Mole. Joey turned again. “Creak-creak.” The screw tightened. The wheel was firm and straight.

“We did it!” Joey shouted. He hugged Mrs. Mole gently and patted the wheelbarrow. The metal felt cool and smooth. Joey’s tail thumped with joy.

They rolled the wheelbarrow outside. The wheel went “whirr-whirr” on the path. Joey loaded garden gloves, a small rake, and a trash bag. “Let’s clean the park path,” he said. “We can pick up litter and leaves.”

They worked together. Joey raked soft piles of leaves. Mrs. Mole tied up the bag. The bluebird found a shiny bottle cap and dropped it in the bin. “Helping is happy!” she sang.

A little duck waddled by. “Quack, I dropped my cup,” she said. “I’m sorry.”

Joey smiled kindly. “Thank you for telling us,” he said. “Let’s put it in the bin.” They did it together. The duck quacked, “Thanks!” and flapped her small wings.

When they were done, the park path was clean and bright. The sun felt warm on Joey’s fur. The air smelled like grass and sweet flowers. Joey’s chest felt big with pride.

Mrs. Mole clapped. “You were patient and brave,” she said. “You used a calm plan. You asked for help. You helped our town.”

Joey nodded. “I felt frustrated,” he said. “But I took breaths. I tried new ideas. I worked with friends. Teamwork helped. Helping the town feels good.”

The bluebird sang a soft song. “When it’s hard, stop and see, breathe in-out, then try with me.”

Joey looked at the wheelbarrow and smiled. “Tomorrow, I can fix the garden hose,” he said. “If I get stuck, I will stop, breathe, think, and try. I can ask for help. I can be open to new ways.”

He bounced once, twice. “I can do hard things,” he said. “One calm try at a time.”

The End.
