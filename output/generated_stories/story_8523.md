# Miko Tries a New Way

Miko was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and gentle hands. He loved to sit on the peaceful front porch of Nana Pine’s house. The porch smelled like warm wood and sweet oranges. Birds sang. Wind chimes tinkled softly. Miko liked the soft cushion on the porch swing. It felt fluffy under his tail.

One sunny morning, Miko held a smooth blue puzzle. It had wooden animal pieces. “I can do this,” he said. “I am brave. I will try.”

Nana Pine brought two cups of cool lemon water. “Sip-sip,” she said with a smile. “Remember, little one, keep your mind open. New ideas can help.”

Miko nodded. He put the lion piece near the square hole. He pushed. “Hmm.” It did not fit. He pushed harder. “Grr—” The lion still did not fit.

A friendly snail slid near the step. “Hello,” said the snail. “I am Slo.” His shell glowed like honey. “May I watch?”

“Yes,” said Miko. “I want the lion to go in. I push and push, but it won’t.”

Slo blinked slowly. “Maybe try turning it.”

Miko frowned. “I always push. That is how I do it.” He pushed again. The wood made a tap-tap sound. His cheeks felt warm. His tummy felt tight.

Nana Pine sat beside him. “Let’s breathe together,” she said softly. They breathed in and out. In and out. The wind smelled like lemons. Miko felt calmer.

Miko looked at the lion. He looked at Slo. He remembered Nana’s words: keep your mind open. “Okay,” he said. “I can try a new way.” He turned the lion a little. He listened for a soft click. The lion still did not fit.

A bright blue butterfly landed on the porch rail. “Hello! I am Biff,” she sang. “I like to flutter and try new paths.”

Miko waved. “I am trying to fit this lion. Do you have an idea?”

Biff fluttered gently. “Look at the shape,” she said. “It is not a square. It has a wiggly mane. Maybe the lion goes in the lion-shaped hole.”

Miko blinked. He had been looking at the square hole the whole time. He looked around the puzzle. There! A hole with bumpy waves like hair. “Oh!” he said. “Thank you!”

He lined up the lion with the lion hole. He turned it a tiny bit. Click! The piece slid in smooth and snug.

Miko’s eyes shone. “I did it!”

“You stayed open,” Nana Pine said. “You tried new ideas.”

Miko smiled. He felt proud and brave. He took a sip of lemon water. It tasted cool and sparkly on his tongue.

Next, Miko lifted the zebra piece. He went to push it into a round hole. “Wait,” he said to himself. “I will look first.” He traced the stripes with his finger. He searched for a hole with stripey edges. He found it and lined it up.

Just then, a small squirrel named Pip hopped onto the porch. Pip’s tail puffed like a dandelion. “Hi! Can I try a piece?” Pip asked.

“Yes,” said Miko. “We can share.”

Pip grabbed the elephant piece. He tried to force it into a tiny hole. “Go in!” he squeaked. The wood scraped. Pip’s whiskers drooped.

Miko remembered how pushing had made him upset. He spoke kindly and clear. “Pip, let’s be gentle,” he said. “Let’s stop and look. We can breathe.”

They all took a slow breath. Nana Pine smiled. Slo nodded. Biff’s wings whispered, shh-shh.

Miko pointed to the big elephant hole. “Let’s try this one. What do you think?”

Pip’s ears perked up. “I think… yes!” He turned the elephant. Click! It fit. Pip giggled. “We did it together!”

A cloud passed over the sun. The porch felt cool for a moment. Then warm light returned. The wind chimes sang, ding-ding-ding.

Miko picked up the last piece, a tiny star that went on top of the puzzle frame. He tried one way, then another. It didn’t fit. He felt a little stuck again. He wanted to push hard. But he remembered open minds.

“I will try a new idea,” he said. “Friends, do you see something I don’t?”

Slo looked closely. “The star is upside down.”

“Thank you,” said Miko. He turned the star. Click! The puzzle was complete. The picture showed animals dancing under a rainbow.

Nana Pine clapped softly. “Miko, you were brave in a new way today. You were open to ideas. You kept trying. You used kind words.”

Miko’s heart felt light. He looked at his friends. “When we listen and try new ways, we can solve tricky things,” he said. “We can be gentle and strong.”

Biff fluttered around the porch. “Open hearts and open minds help us find new paths,” she sang.

Pip nodded. “And breathing helps when we feel stuck.”

Slo smiled. “Slow and steady can be smart.”

Miko hugged Nana Pine. The lemony breeze tickled his nose. He looked at the finished puzzle and at his friends on the peaceful front porch.

“I learned something,” Miko said. “Being open-minded helps me learn. Talking kindly helps us work together. And trying again makes me strong.”

They all sipped cool lemon water and watched the rainbow picture shine.

The End.
