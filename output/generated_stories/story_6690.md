# Ellie Speaks Up

High up on a sunny rooftop, a gentle elephant calf named Ellie was helping her family with their weekly chores. The rooftop garden needed lots of care, and everyone had jobs to do.

"Ellie, please water the tomatoes," called Mama Elephant from across the rooftop.

"Ellie, sweep the leaves by the fence," said Papa Elephant, pointing with his trunk.

"Ellie, help me move these heavy flower pots," added her big brother Eddie.

Ellie's little trunk drooped. She wanted to help, but everyone was asking her to do different things at the same time. The warm sun beat down on her gray skin as she stood there, confused.

"I... I don't know what to do first," Ellie whispered to herself.

She started to water the tomatoes, but then Papa called again. "Ellie! The leaves are blowing everywhere!"

Ellie dropped the watering can and hurried to sweep. But then Eddie trumpeted loudly, "Ellie! I need help right now!"

The little elephant felt overwhelmed. Her ears flapped nervously as she tried to do everything at once. Water spilled from the can. Leaves scattered in the breeze. The flower pot slipped from her small trunk.

"Oh no!" Ellie cried as dirt scattered across the clean rooftop.

Mama Elephant hurried over. "What happened, little one?"

Ellie's eyes filled with tears. "Everyone wants me to help, but I can't do everything at the same time. I don't know how to say it."

Mama wrapped her warm trunk around Ellie. "Sweet Ellie, it's okay to speak up when you need help. That's called being assertive."

"What does assertive mean?" Ellie asked, sniffling.

"It means using your voice to say what you need," Mama explained gently. "You can be kind and honest at the same time."

Papa and Eddie gathered around. "We're sorry, Ellie," said Papa. "We didn't realize we were all asking you to do different things."

"Yeah, that wasn't fair," Eddie agreed, patting Ellie's back with his trunk.

Ellie took a deep breath. The cool rooftop breeze helped her feel calmer. "I want to help everyone," she said in a clear, brave voice. "But I can only do one job at a time. Can we make a plan together?"

"That's perfect!" said Mama proudly. "What do you think we should do first?"

Ellie looked around the rooftop garden. The plants needed water most. "Let's water the tomatoes first. Then we can all sweep the leaves together. After that, Eddie and I can move the flower pots as a team."

"Great idea!" said Papa. "When we work together and talk to each other, everything gets done better."

The elephant family worked side by side. Ellie watered the red tomatoes, feeling the cool water splash gently. Everyone helped sweep the crunchy leaves into neat piles. Then Ellie and Eddie carefully moved each flower pot, counting "One, two, three, lift!" together.

"This is much more fun," Ellie giggled as they finished the last pot.

"You did something very important today, Ellie," said Mama. "You used your voice to help solve the problem."

"And you were honest about what you needed," added Papa.

"Plus, you helped us work as a team," said Eddie with a big smile.

As the sun began to set over the rooftop, Ellie felt proud and happy. She had learned that speaking up wasn't scary—it was helpful! When she used her voice kindly and honestly, everyone worked better together.

"Tomorrow, if I feel overwhelmed, I'll remember to speak up right away," Ellie said confidently.

The family trumpeted together in celebration, their happy sounds echoing across the rooftop garden.

The End.
