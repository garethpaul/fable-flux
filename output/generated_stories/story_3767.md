# Daisy's Special Apology Garden

Little Daisy the deer fawn sat on her soft pink bed in her cozy bedroom. Her spotted coat felt warm under the fuzzy blanket, but her heart felt heavy. She had done something very wrong today.

"I'm so sorry, Mama," Daisy whispered to her pillow. The afternoon sun streamed through her window, making rainbow patterns on the wall. But Daisy didn't want to look at the pretty colors.

Earlier that day, Daisy had gotten very hungry while playing. She saw Mama's special vegetable garden outside and ate ALL the carrots, lettuce, and tomatoes! Now there was nothing left for their healthy dinner.

Mama Deer peeked her gentle head into the bedroom. "Daisy, sweetheart, what's troubling you?"

"I ate your whole garden, Mama," Daisy said with tears in her big brown eyes. "I was so hungry, and the vegetables looked so yummy and orange and green and red. Now our dinner is all gone!"

Mama's soft hooves clicked on the wooden floor as she came closer. "Oh my little fawn, I understand you were hungry. But what should you have done instead?"

"I should have asked first," Daisy sniffled. "And maybe just taken one carrot, not all of them."

"That's right," Mama said, nuzzling Daisy's spotted neck. "But now, how can we fix this problem together?"

Daisy thought hard. She wiggled her little black nose. "Maybe... maybe I could make you something special?"

"What a wonderful idea!" Mama smiled. "What could you create?"

Daisy's eyes lit up like twinkling stars. "I could make you a picture garden! With all the vegetables I ate!"

She jumped off her bed and ran to her art table. Her hooves made happy tapping sounds on the floor. She grabbed her crayons - orange for carrots, green for lettuce, and bright red for tomatoes.

"Look, Mama!" Daisy said, coloring carefully. "Here are ten carrots to say sorry for the ten I ate. And here's a whole row of lettuce leaves - green and crunchy looking!"

Mama watched as Daisy drew circle after circle for the red tomatoes. "These are beautiful, sweetie. But what else can we do to make things right?"

Daisy tapped her crayon on her chin. "Oh! We could plant new seeds together! Then in a few weeks, we'll have real vegetables again!"

"Perfect!" Mama clapped her hooves together. "And while we wait for them to grow, what should we eat for dinner tonight?"

"We could go to the farmer's market!" Daisy bounced excitedly. "I have coins in my piggy bank. I can buy us fresh vegetables with my own money!"

Mama hugged her little fawn tight. "That's a very grown-up way to make amends, Daisy."

Together, they walked to the market. Daisy carefully picked out carrots, lettuce, and tomatoes. She counted out her shiny coins to pay the kind farmer.

"These vegetables will make a delicious, healthy dinner," the farmer said with a smile.

That evening, as they munched their colorful salad, Daisy felt much better. Her tummy was full of good, nutritious food, and her heart was full of love.

"Thank you for helping me fix my mistake, Mama," Daisy said.

"When we make mistakes, we can always find ways to make things better," Mama replied. "You used your creativity and your caring heart to make amends."

Daisy smiled and took another bite of crunchy carrot. Tomorrow, they would plant new seeds together, and she would always remember to ask before taking food from the garden.

The End.
