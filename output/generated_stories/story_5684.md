# Polly's Writing Adventure

In a cozy home office filled with colorful books and a big wooden desk, lived a bright green parrot named Polly. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds in the morning sunlight that streamed through the window. Polly loved to watch her human friend, Sarah, write stories on her computer every day.

"Click, click, tap!" went Sarah's fingers on the keyboard. Polly would bob her head and squawk happily, "Pretty words! Pretty words!"

But one Tuesday morning, something was different. Sarah sat at her desk with her head in her hands, looking very sad.

"What's wrong, Sarah?" chirped Polly, tilting her colorful head to one side.

"Oh, Polly," Sarah sighed. "I can't think of any good stories to write today. My mind feels all foggy and empty."

Polly fluffed her bright feathers and thought hard. She had an idea! "Polly help! Polly help!" she squawked excitedly.

First, Polly flew to the window and tapped it with her orange beak. "Look outside! Pretty blue sky! Fluffy white clouds!" she called.

Sarah looked up and smiled a little. "You're right, Polly. The sky is beautiful today."

"Now stretch!" commanded Polly, spreading her wings wide. "Like Polly!"

Sarah giggled and stood up, stretching her arms high above her head just like Polly's wings. "Ahh, that feels good," she said, taking a deep breath of the fresh air coming through the window.

"Now walk! Walk! Walk!" Polly flew in circles around the office, her wings making a gentle whooshing sound.

Sarah walked around the room three times, feeling her body wake up. The fuzzy feeling in her head started to clear away like morning mist.

"Feel better?" asked Polly, perching on Sarah's shoulder.

"Yes! Much better," Sarah said, gently stroking Polly's soft green feathers. "But I still don't know what to write about."

Polly's black eyes sparkled with another idea. "Write about Polly! Write about today!"

"That's perfect!" Sarah exclaimed. She sat back down at her desk, but this time she felt happy and excited. Her fingers began to dance across the keyboard: "Once upon a time, there was a very smart parrot who helped her friend feel better..."

As Sarah wrote, Polly preened her feathers proudly. "Good story! Good story!" she chirped.

From that day on, Sarah and Polly created a special morning routine. Every day before writing, they would look outside together, stretch their arms and wings, and take a little walk around the office. Sometimes Sarah felt grumpy or tired, but Polly always reminded her gently.

"Even when we feel yucky, we can feel better," Polly would say in her sweet voice. "Stretch, walk, breathe, then write!"

Sarah learned that taking care of her body helped her mind work better too. And when she made mistakes in her stories, Polly would remind her, "It's okay! Try again! Polly loves you!"

The office filled with the happy sounds of typing, Polly's cheerful chirps, and the rustling of pages as Sarah wrote wonderful story after wonderful story.

"Thank you for teaching me, Polly," Sarah said one sunny afternoon. "You showed me that taking care of myself helps me write better stories."

"Friends help friends!" squawked Polly proudly, doing a little dance on her perch.

The End.
