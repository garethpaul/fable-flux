# Polly Paints an Apology

In a bright creative art room, sunlight danced on jars of paint. The air smelled like fresh paper and sweet glue. Brushes waited in quiet cups. Crayons slept in a rainbow box. A soft hum came from the tall windows.

A creative parrot named Polly perched on a stool. Her feathers were red, green, and blue, like a living painting. She loved to paint, sing, and try new things. “Squawk! Colors, colors!” she chirped happily.

Today was Art Sharing Day. Friends would bring a picture, a clay shape, or a collage to share. Polly felt excited. She wanted her work to be the biggest and the best. Her eyes sparkled as she looked around.

Her friends were busy. Max the mouse rolled clay balls. Tia the turtle dabbed green paint on a leaf. Eli the elephant held a tiny brush very carefully, even though his trunk was big. Their art was different, and that made the room feel like a party of colors.

Polly flapped her wings. “I will make a giant rainbow!” she said. She stretched a long paper across the table. The paper rustled like gentle rain. She dipped a brush in red. Swish, swish! She dipped in orange. Swish, swish!

As Polly painted, her wings brushed the table. Her tail feathers bumped a glue cup. Oops! The glue wobbled and tipped. “Oh!” Polly gasped, but she kept painting.

The glue slipped onto Tia’s leaf art. “Drip… drip… splat.” The leaves stuck together in a clump.

Tia looked up. “My leaves,” she whispered. Her eyes grew shiny.

Polly felt a tiny pinch in her chest. “It was an accident,” she said quickly. “I am making a very big rainbow.” She tried to smile and went back to painting. Swish, swish.

Then Polly swung her tail again. Her feathers brushed Max’s clay balls. The balls rolled off the table. Plop, plop, plop. Max squeaked. “My tower! It fell.”

Eli blinked. “Careful, Polly,” he said in a gentle, deep voice.

Polly’s heart thumped. She looked at her bright rainbow. It was big and bold. But the room felt quiet now, like a song without a beat. She heard a sniffle. Tia wiped a small tear. Max’s whiskers trembled.

Polly’s tummy felt twisty. The colors on her paper looked too loud. “I didn’t mean to,” she whispered. A small voice inside her said, You need to make it right.

She looked at her friends’ faces. She took a deep breath. “Squawk,” she said softly. “I am sorry.”

Tia blinked. “Sorry?” she asked.

Polly hopped down from the stool. “I knocked the glue. I rolled the clay. I did not watch my wings. I hurt your art. I am sorry,” Polly said. Her feathers drooped. “May I help fix it?”

The room waited. Then Max nodded slowly. “You can help me roll the clay again,” he said. “Gentle rolls.”

Tia held up her clumpy leaves. “Maybe we can make a leaf heart,” she said. “It will look different.”

Eli smiled. “I can hold the paper while you help,” he offered. His trunk curled kindly.

Polly’s tummy untwisted a little. She washed her wings in a bowl of water. Swish, swish. The water felt cool. She dried with a soft towel. Pat, pat. “I will move carefully,” she said. “I will use small flaps.”

Together, they began.

Polly rolled clay balls with Max. “Roll, roll,” they said together. Polly sang a soft rolling song. The clay felt smooth and cool under her claws. Max stacked the balls like tiny moons.

Polly and Tia looked at the sticky leaf clump. “We can make a heart,” Polly said. “We can add shiny bits.” She found gold paper and little buttons. Tia dabbed glue with a tiny brush. Tap, tap. The glue smelled sweet. Polly pressed the leaves in a heart shape. Press, press. “It’s different,” Tia said. “I like different.”

Eli showed his tiny brush. “I’m painting dots,” he said. “Would you like to try?” He gave Polly a small corner. Polly dotted gently. Dot, dot, dot. “Small is special too,” Polly whispered.

When they finished, the room felt warm again. The sun made little rainbows on the floor. Their art looked new and bright. Max had a tall clay tower. Tia had a leaf heart with shiny gold and buttons. Eli had careful dots like falling rain. Polly had a rainbow—but she cut it into four pieces. “We can share,” she said. Each friend got a piece of color.

At Sharing Time, everyone clapped. The teacher smiled. “I see teamwork,” she said. “I see gentle hands and kind hearts.”

Polly stood tall. “I made a mistake,” she said in a clear, soft voice. “I said sorry. I helped fix it. We made new things together. Different is beautiful.”

Tia hugged Polly’s wing. “You made amends,” she said.

“What is ‘amends’?” asked Max.

Polly fluffed her feathers. “It means I made it right,” she said. “I used helpful hands and a caring heart.”

Eli nodded. “And we discovered new ideas,” he said. “Leaf hearts. Gentle dots. Shared rainbows.”

Polly smiled. The art room smelled like paper and paint and happy. She felt light, like a feather in the sun. “I will flap small in busy spaces,” she said. “I will look, listen, and make room.”

Her friends smiled back. They were all different. That made their art, and their day, even better.

The End.
