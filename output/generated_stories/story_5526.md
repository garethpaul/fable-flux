# Puddles the Penguin Practices

Puddles was a cheerful penguin with bright, curious eyes and a scarf with many colors. He loved to waddle, hum silly songs, and splash with his flappy feet. Today, Puddles had a big plan in the bathroom.

“I will learn to brush my beak just right,” Puddles said. He puffed up with pride. “I will practice, practice, practice!”

The bathroom smelled like mint and warm soap. The tiles were cool under Puddles’s tummy. Water dripped, drip, drip, into the shiny sink. The mirror showed Puddles’s happy face and rainbow scarf.

Puddles picked up a small toothbrush. It was blue with tiny sparkles. He squeezed a pea of minty paste. “Hello, toothpaste,” he giggled. “You smell like snow and candy canes!”

He tried to brush. Swish, swish, swish. But the brush slipped. A dot of foam landed on his cheek. Plop!

“Oh!” Puddles blinked. “That was tricky.”

Just then, his friends waddled in to say hello. Mimi the mouse had round ears and brown fur. Rollo the rabbit had long, soft ears. Suki the seal slid on her belly. Their voices sounded happy, like bells.

“Hi, Puddles!” said Mimi. “What are you doing?”

“I’m practicing brushing,” Puddles said. “I want to do it well. But it’s hard.”

“That’s okay,” said Suki softly. “We all practice different things.”

“I practice tying my bow,” said Mimi, touching her tiny blue bow. “I had to try many times.”

“I practice hopping quietly,” said Rollo. He hopped, hop, hop, and then tried a whisper-hop. “See? Practice helps.”

Puddles smiled. “Will you watch me try again?”

“Of course,” said his friends together.

They made a little cheer circle on the soft bathmat. The mat felt squishy under their feet and flippers. Puddles took a deep breath. He counted slowly. “One… two… three…”

He put the brush on his beak and made little circles. “Swish, swish, swish.” He went around the top. He went around the bottom. He sang a tiny song to help him remember.

“Little circles, slow and sweet,
Round my beak, front to beak,
Left and right, not too fast,
Practice makes the good things last.”

“You’re doing it!” Mimi squeaked. Her whiskers twitched with joy.

Puddles felt proud. Then the brush bumped his nose. Boop! He giggled, and the brush slipped again. A bubble popped on his scarf. Pop!

“Oh no,” Puddles said. “Maybe I can’t.”

Suki slid closer. “It’s okay to slip,” she whispered. “Try again. We believe in you.”

Rollo nodded. “Let’s play imagination to help. Pretend we are in a Bubble Kingdom!”

“The Bubble Kingdom?” Puddles’s eyes sparkled.

“Yes!” said Mimi. “We are bubble helpers. Our toothbrushes are magic wands. We make bubbles clean and bright.”

Puddles stood tall. “I am Sir Puddles, the Bubble Knight!” He dipped his brush like a sword. The minty smell felt cool in his beak. He listened to the tiny fizz. Fizz, fizz, fizz.

“Swish for the Bubble Queen!” said Rollo.

“Circle for the Bubble Bridge!” said Suki.

“Gentle on the Bubble Babies,” whispered Mimi.

Puddles made little circles again. He moved slowly, up and down, back and forth, left and right. He counted to ten, then to twenty, in his head. The mirror showed a happy penguin working hard. The sink sang drip, drip, drip, like a drum.

He paused to spit. “Ptoo!” The foam swirled away like white clouds. He rinsed with cool water that tasted like snow. Swish, swish. The cup clinked. Clink!

“How do you feel?” asked Suki.

Puddles tapped his beak. It felt smooth and shiny. “I did better!”

“You practiced,” said Mimi. “That’s the magic.”

Puddles looked at his friends. They were all different. Mouse, rabbit, seal, and penguin. Different shapes, different fur, different fins and flippers. All smiling together.

“We all practice different things,” Puddles said. “And we can all help each other.”

Rollo pointed to the colorful towels. “Time to practice drying! Pat, pat, pat, not rub, rub, rub.”

Puddles tried gentle pats. The towel was soft like clouds. Pat, pat, pat. He brushed his scarf and set it straight. He felt calm and proud.

“Thank you,” he told his friends. “You helped me try again.”

“That’s what friends do,” said Mimi.

Suki raised a flipper. “Bubble Kingdom tomorrow?”

“Yes!” Puddles laughed. “And I will practice again. Little circles. Slow and sweet.”

They all did a happy wiggle dance on the bathmat. The lights were warm. The air smelled fresh and minty. Puddles hummed his brushing song.

“Practice helps me learn and grow,” he sang softly. “I can do it, nice and slow.”

He looked in the mirror and saw a brave penguin who didn’t give up. He saw friends who were all different and all kind. He felt strong on the inside.

“I learned something,” Puddles said. “When I practice, I get better. When we share and imagine together, practice is fun.”

His friends cheered. “Hooray for practice!”

The End.
