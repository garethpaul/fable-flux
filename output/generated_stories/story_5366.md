# Nutkin's Big Share

Nutkin the squirrel sat on the dining room windowsill. His fluffy tail twitched with excitement. Today was the big acorn toss game with his forest friends!

"I have the best acorns," Nutkin said proudly. He looked at his shiny collection on the wooden table. "These will help me win for sure!"

Nutkin had spent all week finding the perfect acorns. Some were big and round. Others were small and smooth. He had practiced tossing them into a basket every day.

*Tap tap tap!* Three little knocks came from the window.

"Nutkin! Are you ready?" called Chippy the chipmunk from outside.

Nutkin opened the window. Chippy climbed in, followed by Penny the field mouse and Oakley the young squirrel.

"Wow! Look at all those acorns!" Penny squeaked. Her tiny pink nose twitched as she sniffed them.

"They smell so nutty and fresh," Chippy said with a sigh.

But Oakley looked sad. His ears drooped down. "I couldn't find any good acorns," he whispered. "Mine are all cracked and bumpy."

Nutkin looked at his friend's small pile of broken acorns. Then he looked at his own perfect collection. His tummy felt funny - not hungry funny, but worried funny.

"Don't worry, Oakley," Nutkin said slowly. "We can still play together."

But as they started the game, Oakley's cracked acorns kept missing the basket. They rolled across the smooth dining room floor with soft *click-click* sounds.

"This isn't fair," Oakley sniffled. "I'll never get better at this game."

Nutkin watched his friend's sad face. Suddenly, winning didn't seem so important anymore. His heart felt warm and fuzzy, like when Mama Squirrel hugged him.

"Wait!" Nutkin said, his tail standing up straight. "I have an idea!"

He scurried to his pile of perfect acorns. "Oakley, pick any acorns you want from my collection!"

"Really?" Oakley's eyes grew big and bright.

"Yes! And Penny and Chippy too. We can all share them!"

Soon all four friends were sorting through Nutkin's acorns. The dining room filled with happy chatter and the gentle *clink* of acorns touching.

"This big one feels so smooth!" Penny giggled, rolling a large acorn between her tiny paws.

"And this one is perfectly round!" Chippy added, holding up a shiny brown acorn.

Oakley picked three beautiful acorns. "Thank you, Nutkin! These feel just right in my paws."

Now everyone had good acorns to toss. The game became much more fun! They took turns aiming at the wicker basket on the dining room chair.

*Plop!* Oakley's first acorn landed right in the basket.

"I did it!" he cheered, jumping up and down.

"Great throw!" Nutkin clapped his paws together.

As they played, Nutkin realized something wonderful. Watching his friends succeed felt even better than winning by himself. The dining room echoed with laughter and cheering.

When the game ended, all four friends had made baskets. They sat in a happy circle on the soft rug.

"Nutkin, you're the best friend ever," Oakley said, giving him a warm hug.

"Sharing made the game so much better," Penny agreed.

Nutkin's heart felt full of joy. "When we share, everyone gets to have fun together!"

From that day on, the friends always shared their acorns. And their games became the most fun in the whole forest.

The End.
