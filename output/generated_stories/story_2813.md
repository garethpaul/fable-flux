# Benny Bear's Pantry Adventure

Benny Bear was a curious little cub with soft brown fur and bright, sparkly eyes. One sunny morning, his tummy started to rumble like thunder. "Grumble, grumble, GRUMBLE!"

"Oh my!" said Benny, patting his round belly. "I'm so hungry! I wonder what yummy treats Mama has in the pantry?"

Benny waddled to the cozy pantry, where tall shelves reached up to the ceiling. The shelves were filled with colorful jars, boxes, and bags. Everything smelled wonderful – like cinnamon, vanilla, and fresh bread.

"Let me see," Benny said, standing on his tippy-toes. "I want my favorite honey cookies!"

But when Benny looked around, he couldn't find any honey cookies anywhere. His face scrunched up in disappointment.

"No cookies? But that's all I want to eat!" he said with a little sigh.

Just then, Benny spotted something green and crunchy in a clear jar. "Yuck!" he said, wrinkling his nose. "Those look like weird green chips. I don't want those!"

Next, he saw a bag of small, round, orange things. "What are those bumpy orange balls?" Benny wondered aloud. "They look strange too!"

Benny's tummy rumbled even louder. "GRUMBLE, GRUMBLE, GRUMBLE!"

"Oh dear," said a gentle voice. It was Mama Bear, peeking into the pantry. "What's wrong, my little cub?"

"Mama, I can't find any honey cookies, and everything else looks weird and yucky!" Benny said, his bottom lip trembling.

Mama Bear smiled warmly and knelt down beside Benny. "Sometimes the most wonderful surprises come in packages we don't expect," she said softly. "Would you like to try something new with me?"

Benny wasn't sure, but his tummy was very hungry. "Okay, Mama," he said quietly.

Mama Bear reached for the jar with the green chips. "These are called snap pea crisps," she explained. "They're crunchy and taste like fresh garden peas!"

She handed Benny a small green chip. It felt light and crispy in his little paw. Benny took a tiny nibble.

"Crunch! Oh!" Benny's eyes grew wide. "It tastes like sunshine and gardens! It's actually really good!"

Next, Mama Bear showed him the orange balls. "These are dried apricots," she said. "They're sweet and chewy, like nature's candy!"

Benny touched one gently. It felt soft and squishy. When he took a small bite, sweetness filled his mouth. "Mmm! It tastes like summer!" he giggled.

"You know what, Mama?" Benny said, his tail wagging happily. "I was wrong to say these foods were yucky before I even tried them. They're actually delicious!"

Mama Bear hugged Benny tight. "That's right, little one. When we keep our minds open to new things, we often discover wonderful surprises. Being open-minded means we're willing to try something different, even if it looks unfamiliar at first."

Benny filled a small bowl with snap pea crisps and dried apricots. As he munched happily, he said, "From now on, I'm going to try new foods before I decide I don't like them. There might be lots of yummy surprises waiting for me!"

"That's my brave, open-minded little bear," Mama said proudly.

Benny learned that day that keeping an open mind and being willing to try new things can lead to the most wonderful discoveries.

The End.
