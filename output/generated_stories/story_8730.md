# Penny the Parrot's Big Voice

Penny was a bright green parrot who lived in the cozy study corner of the Miller family's house. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds in the warm sunlight that came through the big window. She loved to paint pictures with her tiny brush and make beautiful songs.

But Penny had a big problem. She was very, very shy.

Every day, the Miller children would come to the study corner to do their homework. They would sit at the wooden desk, pencils scratching on paper. Penny wanted so badly to help them and share her colorful paintings. But whenever she tried to speak up, her voice came out as a tiny whisper.

"Peep," she would say softly. The children couldn't hear her at all.

One afternoon, little Emma Miller was struggling with her art project. She had to draw a rainbow, but she kept erasing and starting over. Her paper was getting wrinkled and gray.

"I can't do it right!" Emma said, tears rolling down her cheeks. "My rainbow looks all wrong!"

Penny's heart felt heavy watching Emma cry. She knew exactly how to make a perfect rainbow. She had painted hundreds of them! Her wings fluttered with excitement, but her voice still felt stuck in her throat.

"I want to help," Penny whispered to herself. "But what if they don't like my ideas? What if my voice sounds silly?"

Then Penny remembered something her mama had taught her long ago: "Being brave doesn't mean you're not scared, little one. It means you do the right thing even when you feel scared."

Penny took three deep breaths. She puffed out her bright chest feathers. She thought of a smart plan.

First, she picked up her smallest paintbrush in her beak. Then she dipped it in red paint and made a gentle curved line on her easel. The paint smelled fresh and sweet.

"What's that pretty red line?" asked Emma, looking up from her messy paper.

Penny's heart beat fast like a tiny drum. This was her chance! She took another deep breath and spoke in her clearest, kindest voice.

"Hello, Emma! I'm Penny. I live here in your study corner. Would you like me to show you how to paint a rainbow?"

Emma's eyes grew wide with wonder. "A talking parrot! And you can paint?"

"Yes!" said Penny, feeling braver with each word. "First, we start with red at the top. Then orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple. Each color needs space to shine."

Emma clapped her hands. "Will you teach me, please?"

Penny felt warm and happy inside. She had been brave, and now she could help! Together, they painted the most beautiful rainbow. Emma's smile was as bright as sunshine.

"Thank you, Penny!" said Emma. "You're the best art teacher ever!"

From that day on, Penny wasn't shy anymore. She helped all the Miller children with their homework. She sang them songs when they felt sad. She painted pictures to make them laugh.

Penny learned that being brave meant caring more about helping others than about her own fears. Her voice became strong and clear because she used it to spread kindness and share her gifts.

The study corner became the happiest place in the whole house, filled with Penny's colorful paintings and joyful songs.

The End.
