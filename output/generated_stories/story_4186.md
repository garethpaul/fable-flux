# Milo Mouse Learns to Wait

Milo Mouse lived with the Johnson family. He was a tiny gray mouse with soft whiskers and bright brown eyes. Today, the whole family was going on a car trip to visit Grandma's house.

"Buckle up, everyone!" called Dad from the front seat. Mom checked that baby Emma was safe in her car seat. Big sister Lucy put on her seatbelt.

Milo squeezed into his special tiny seatbelt that Dad had made just for him. The car smelled like vanilla air freshener and felt warm and cozy.

"Are we there yet?" asked Milo as soon as the car started moving.

"Not yet, little buddy," laughed Dad. "It's a long drive to Grandma's house."

Milo wiggled in his seat. His tiny paws drummed on the window. The trees outside looked so green and pretty, but they were moving so slowly!

"Are we there NOW?" Milo squeaked five minutes later.

"Still driving, Milo," said Mom gently. "Why don't you look for red cars outside?"

But Milo felt wiggly and impatient. His whiskers twitched. His tail swished back and forth. "I want to be there RIGHT NOW!" he squeaked loudly.

Baby Emma started to cry. Lucy frowned and put her hands over her ears. "Milo, you're being too loud," she said.

Milo felt his face get hot. His heart was beating fast like a tiny drum. He wanted to shout and wiggle even more, but he saw that his family looked upset.

"I'm sorry," whispered Milo. "I feel all jumpy inside."

Mom turned around with kind eyes. "It's okay to feel excited, Milo. But when we feel big emotions in the car, we need to use our calm-down tricks."

"What are calm-down tricks?" asked Milo, his ears perking up.

"Let's try taking three deep breaths together," suggested Mom. "Breathe in slowly through your nose... and out through your mouth."

Milo breathed in. The air felt cool in his tiny nose. Then he breathed out slowly. His shoulders felt less tight.

"Now let's count to ten," said Lucy, wanting to help. "One... two... three..."

Milo counted along. His wiggly feeling started to calm down. By the time they reached ten, his heart wasn't drumming so fast.

"I feel better!" said Milo, surprised.

"Good job using your self-control," praised Dad. "Now, let's play a quiet car game. Can you spot something blue outside?"

Milo pressed his nose to the cool window. "I see a blue mailbox!" he squeaked quietly.

"Wonderful! Now find something yellow," said Lucy.

The car game was fun! Milo found yellow flowers, a yellow school bus, and even a yellow butterfly. His mind was busy looking and thinking instead of feeling impatient.

When baby Emma got fussy, Milo whispered a little song he made up: "We're driving in the car today, to see Grandma far away!"

Emma giggled and clapped her tiny hands.

"What a creative song, Milo!" said Mom. "You're being so helpful and patient."

Soon, Milo saw a familiar house with a red door and flower boxes. "Grandma's house!" he cheered.

Grandma came outside with her arms wide open. "My sweet family!" she called.

As they all hugged Grandma, she whispered to Milo, "I heard you were very patient on the long car ride. That shows you're growing up!"

Milo's whiskers twitched with pride. He had learned that big feelings were okay, but he could use breathing and counting to help himself calm down. Car rides were much nicer when everyone felt peaceful and happy.

"Next time I feel wiggly, I'll remember my calm-down tricks," Milo told himself as he snuggled into Grandma's warm hug.

The End.
