# Koby's Recycling Laboratory

Koby the koala loved his special basement lab. It had shiny tables, colorful bottles, and the most wonderful smell of eucalyptus leaves. But today, something was different. His lab was messy with empty bottles, old papers, and plastic containers everywhere!

"Oh my," said Koby, adjusting his tiny safety goggles. "My lab needs help!"

Koby's friend Maya, a little mouse from Mexico, peeked through the lab door. "Hola, Koby! What happened here?"

"I've been making so many fun experiments," Koby explained. "But I forgot to clean up properly. Look at all this waste!"

Maya's whiskers twitched with excitement. "In my family, we have a special saying: 'Un lugar limpio es un lugar feliz' - a clean place is a happy place! Let's sort everything together!"

Koby clapped his furry paws. "That's a wonderful idea! But how do we do it safely?"

"First, we put on our safety gear," Maya said, pointing to the colorful gloves and goggles on the shelf. "Safety first, always!"

They carefully put on their protective gear. The gloves felt smooth and rubbery, and the goggles made everything look crystal clear.

"Now," said Maya, "let's make three special stations. Paper goes here, plastic bottles there, and glass containers over there."

Koby picked up a crinkly newspaper. "This paper feels rough and makes a swishy sound. Where does it go?"

"In the paper bin!" Maya cheered. "When we recycle paper, it can become new books for children to read and write in!"

As they worked, Koby found an old notebook. "Look! I can write down what we're learning today." He carefully drew pictures of recycling bins and wrote "RECYCLE" in big, wobbly letters.

"That's perfect!" said Maya. "Writing helps us remember important things."

They sorted plastic bottles that went "clink-clink" when they touched. The bottles were smooth and light, and some still smelled like the fruit juice that was once inside.

"These bottles can become new toys or even playground equipment!" Koby said excitedly.

When they reached the glass containers, Maya reminded him, "Be extra careful with glass. Hold it gently with both hands."

The glass felt cool and heavy. When they placed it in the special glass bin, it made a soft "ting" sound.

"Glass can be melted down and made into beautiful new bottles or even windows!" Maya explained.

As they finished cleaning, Koby's lab sparkled again. The tables shined, the floor was clear, and everything had its proper place.

"Maya, thank you for teaching me about recycling," Koby said. "I learned that taking care of our space is taking care of our whole community!"

"Sí, exactly!" Maya smiled. "When we recycle and keep things clean, we help our neighbors, our town, and even the whole Earth!"

Koby wrote in his notebook: "Good citizens help their community by recycling and staying safe."

From that day on, Koby always remembered to sort his materials as he worked. He put up colorful signs in different languages that Maya taught him, so all his friends could learn about recycling too.

"Being responsible feels good," Koby said, hugging his clean, organized lab. "And helping our community feels even better!"

The End.
