# Leo the Lion Cub’s Balcony Garden

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright curious eyes. He lived with his family in a cozy home where the breeze smelled like flowers. Outside their door was a small balcony garden. It had pots of mint, basil, tomatoes, and a tiny lemon tree. Little flags from many places fluttered on strings, red, blue, green, and gold. Birds chirped, “Tweet-tweet!” and the sun felt warm like a gentle hug.

One sunny morning, Leo stood on the balcony. He sniffed the mint. “Mmm! Mint smells cool!” He touched the soil. It was dry and crumbly. “Uh-oh,” said Leo. “The plants look droopy.”

Mama Lion stepped outside with a watering can. “Good morning, Leo,” she said. “The plants need help.”

“I can help!” said Leo. His whiskers wiggled with courage.

Mama smiled. “First, clean paws,” she said. “We touch plants with clean paws.” They went inside to wash. Leo sang, “Scrub scrub, bubbles pop!” Warm water splashed. His paws felt fresh and tingly.

Back on the balcony, Leo looked around. “What is the problem?” he asked.

Mama knelt beside him. “Let’s use our senses. Look, listen, touch, and smell.”

Leo looked. The leaves drooped like sleepy eyes. He listened. A bee buzzed, “Bzz-bzz,” near a yellow flower. He touched the soil. “Dry,” he said. He smelled the air. “Sunny and hot.”

Mama nodded. “Good observing, Leo. The problem is the plants are thirsty.”

Leo lifted the watering can, but it was heavy. “I can try,” he said, breathing in and out. “I am brave.” He tipped the can slowly. “Drip, drip, drip.” The soil turned dark and cool. Leo smiled. “They are drinking!”

Just then, a big cloud covered the sun. A gust of wind came whoosh! The little flags danced. A pot wobbled and—oh no!—a small tomato plant tipped over. The soil spilled. A tiny worm wriggled in the mess.

Leo’s ears drooped. “I made a mess,” he whispered.

Mama touched his shoulder. “It’s okay to make a mess when you try. We can fix it. That’s resilience. We keep going.”

Leo took a deep breath. “I will try again.”

They gently scooped the worm back into the pot. “Welcome home, little friend,” said Leo. They set the tomato plant upright and patted the soil. “There, there,” Leo said softly.

Their neighbor, Mrs. Asha, peeked over the railing. She wore a bright scarf with pretty patterns. “Namaste, little lion,” she said kindly. “In my home, we sing to plants. It helps us slow down and be gentle.”

Leo’s eyes lit up. “Can we learn your song?”

Mrs. Asha sang softly, “Grow, grow, little leaves, drink the water, feel the breeze.” Her voice was sweet like honey. Leo hummed along. The wind felt friendlier.

Another neighbor, Mr. Kim, waved from the next balcony. “Annyeong,” he said with a smile. “I use rainwater for my plants. It saves water.” He showed a small bucket that caught drips from the edge of his awning.

“Saves water?” Leo asked.

“Yes,” said Mr. Kim. “That is called sustainability. We use just what we need and help our Earth.”

Leo looked at their empty watering can. “We can save water too!” he said. He placed a clean bowl under the railing to catch tiny drops from the cloud. “Plink, plink,” went the raindrops.

The sun peeked out again. The leaves sparkled. Leo saw a tiny snail on the lemon tree. “Hello, snail,” he whispered. “Please nibble only a little.” He gently moved the snail to a leafy corner. “We share,” he said.

Mama brought a small brush and cloth. “Let’s keep things clean. Hygiene helps plants and paws.” Leo wiped the railing. “Swipe, swipe.” He brushed fallen leaves into a small bin. “Rustle, rustle.” He washed his paws again. “Splash, splash.” Everything felt tidy and safe.

But one tomato plant still looked sad. “What else is wrong?” asked Leo. He crouched down. He noticed the pot had no holes. “The water can’t drain,” he said. “The roots can’t breathe.”

“Good thinking,” Mama said. “You used knowledge.”

They carefully moved the tomato into a pot with little holes. “Poke-poke,” went the soil as they made a cozy space. Leo pressed the soil gently. The plant stood straighter.

In the afternoon, the balcony smelled like mint and lemon. Bees hummed, flags fluttered, and a soft breeze cooled Leo’s nose. “I did it,” Leo whispered. “I kept going.”

Mrs. Asha clapped softly. “You were patient and kind.”

Mr. Kim gave a thumbs-up. “You saved water and helped the Earth.”

Mama hugged Leo. “You were brave and resilient. You tried, you learned, and you tried again.”

Leo puffed out his chest and giggled. “I am a brave little lion cub. I care for my garden.”

He looked at the plants and said, “We will water with care, we will keep clean, we will share, and we will learn from friends everywhere.”

The plants seemed to shimmer in the light. A tiny tomato blushed red. Leo’s heart felt warm like sunshine. He licked his paw and smiled. “When things are hard,” he said, “I breathe, I think, and I try again.”

The balcony garden swayed softly, like it was saying, “Thank you.”

The End.
