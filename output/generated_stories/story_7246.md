# Panda Poppy’s Car Ride Around the World

Panda Poppy was a patient panda with soft black ears and a round, warm belly. Today, Poppy climbed into the family car. The seats felt squishy, the window glass felt cool, and the seat belt clicked with a happy snap. Mama Panda smiled in the front seat. “Ready for a ride, Poppy?”

“Yes!” Poppy said, patting the window. “Where are we going?”

“We’re going to pick up Grandma,” Mama said. “And on the way, we can listen and learn about our big world.”

Poppy’s eyes grew wide. “The whole world? In our car?” The car hummed and the wheels whispered on the road. Vroom, vroom.

Mama touched the radio. “We can hear voices from different places,” she said. “Let’s listen, talk, and be mindful.”

Poppy sat tall, paws on the armrest, and took a slow breath in. He smelled orange slices from his snack bag. “I can be calm,” Poppy whispered. “In, out. I am calm.”

The radio played a friendly voice. “Hello! Hola!” a child said. “I am Lina from Peru.”

Poppy clapped. “Hola, Lina! What is Peru?” He pressed his paw to his heart. “I like your hello.”

Mama nodded. “Peru is a country far away. People there say Hola for hello. Can you say it too?”

Poppy waved at the window. “Hola!” The word felt warm in his mouth, like sunshine. The radio voice giggled and talked about mountains and bright birds. Poppy pictured tall mountains like stacks of pancakes and birds with rainbow wings. He could almost hear wings fluttering. Fwip, fwip.

A car drove by with flags on the window. Poppy pointed. “Look, Mama! Little flags!”

“That’s a sign of family pride,” Mama said. “Families come from many places. We can learn from them.”

The radio changed. “Namaste,” another child said softly. “I am Ravi from India. I like to sit and breathe.”

Poppy placed a paw on his belly. “Namaste,” he said slowly. The word felt smooth, like a pebble. He took a deep breath in and out. “I am breathing with Ravi.” The air smelled like Mama’s tea and orange peel. Poppy felt peaceful.

Mama smiled in the mirror. “When we say words from other places, we show care and respect. That’s global-awareness. We notice the big world and the people in it.”

Poppy nodded. “I can notice.” He noticed the sky, a soft blue blanket. He noticed the beep-beep of a truck. He noticed his own small paws, quiet and still.

They stopped at a light. A family in the next car laughed. The child waved. Poppy waved back with a shy grin.

“Hello!” the child called.

Poppy thought of Lina and Ravi. He cupped his mouth and called, “Hola! Namaste!” The other child giggled and called, “Bonjour!” The light turned green, and both cars rolled forward like gentle turtles.

“What does Bonjour mean?” Poppy asked.

“It’s hello in French,” Mama said. “From places like France and parts of Canada and other places, too.”

Poppy tried the new word. “Bon-jour.” It felt bouncy, like a ball.

The radio voice came again. “Kia ora!” a child from New Zealand said. “I love the ocean.”

Poppy pressed his nose to the cool window. “Kia ora,” he whispered. He heard the word like a wave. Whoosh. He imagined blue water, silver fish, and soft sand between toes. He wiggled his panda toes in his shoes and giggled.

They turned onto a quiet street. Trees waved their leaf-hands. The car smelled like oranges and a little bit like crayons. Poppy’s tummy rumbled. He munched a slice. Slurp.

“Poppy,” Mama said, “how do we show communication in the car?”

Poppy thought. “We take turns. We listen. We use kind words.”

Mama nodded. “Yes. We listen to people from our world. We try new words. We breathe when we feel wiggly. That is mindfulness.”

A little worry puffed in Poppy’s chest. “What if I say a word wrong?”

Mama’s voice was gentle. “It’s okay to try. We can ask, ‘Can you help me say it?’”

Poppy practiced. “Can you help me say it?” He smiled. “That feels brave.”

They parked by Grandma’s house. Grandma Panda stepped out, her fur soft and snowy. She smelled like muffins. “Hello, my loves!”

Poppy hugged her. “Hola, Grandma! Namaste! Bonjour! Kia ora!”

Grandma laughed, her eyes twinkling. “Oh my! What a world traveler in my car!” She buckled in, and off they went again.

As they drove, Poppy shared what he learned. “Lina has mountains. Ravi breathes. The ocean says whoosh. Families have flags. We can say hello in many ways.” He tapped his chest. “I am patient and I listen.”

Grandma patted his paw. “When we listen to the world, the world feels closer.”

Poppy looked out the window. He saw a store with colorful letters. He saw people with different hats and different smiles. He waved. Some waved back. Poppy felt his heart stretch wide, like a big hug.

“Mama,” he said softly, “the world is big. But I can be kind and calm in our car.”

“Yes,” Mama said. “That’s the lesson for today.”

Poppy took one more slow breath. In, out. He let the car’s gentle hum settle in his ears. He whispered, “Hola, Namaste, Bonjour, Kia ora,” like a soft song for the road.

Grandma squeezed his paw. “What a beautiful song.”

Poppy smiled. “I love our big world. I love learning new words. I love listening.”

The car turned toward home, warm and safe. Poppy felt proud and peaceful. He knew he could meet new friends with kind eyes, careful ears, and brave words.

And that is how Panda Poppy went around the world, one hello at a time, from the back seat of the family car.

The End.
