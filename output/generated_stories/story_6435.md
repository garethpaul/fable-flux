# Robo-Pup’s Wiggle Warm-Up

In a comfortable nursery with soft rugs and sunny windows, a helpful robot dog named Robo-Pup blinked his bright blue eyes. His metal ears hummed a happy tune. He wagged his smooth, shiny tail that clicked, click-click, like tiny bells.

“Good morning, friends!” beeped Robo-Pup. “Who is ready to move and play?”

The nursery smelled like fresh crayons and clean blankets. Colorful balls sat in a basket. Little foam blocks made a cozy fort. Outside, a soft breeze whispered at the window.

Maya, a small girl with curly hair, rubbed her sleepy eyes. “I feel slow today,” she said softly. “I don’t want to run.”

Tommy hugged a pillow. “Me too. My legs feel like noodles.”

Robo-Pup tilted his head. “Noodles are good for wiggling! We can start small.”

He tapped his paw, and a gentle chime played. Ding-ding! A banner on the wall said, “Move with kindness!”

Robo-Pup rolled over to the ball basket. He nudged a red ball with his nose. “We will do a warm-up,” he said. “Warm-ups help our bodies get ready. We can feel strong and safe.”

Maya frowned. “What if I can’t do it?”

Robo-Pup blinked. “That’s okay. We go slow. We listen to our bodies. We help each other.”

He pressed his paw on a button on his collar. Soft music filled the nursery, like tiny raindrops. Tip-tap, tip-tap.

“Let’s start with wiggle fingers,” said Robo-Pup. “Wiggle-wiggle!” He wiggled his paw pads. Maya smiled and copied. Tommy giggled and wiggled his toes too.

“Now stretch like a big yawning cat,” Robo-Pup said. “Reeeeach up!” His little antenna rose tall. The sunlight felt warm on their hands. Stretching felt cozy.

“Let’s try marching. Stomp, stomp!” Robo-Pup marched in place. His paws made gentle thump-thump sounds on the rug. Maya’s feet went stomp. Tommy’s feet went step-step. Even the blocks in the fort made tiny rattles.

Just then, Mina, the shy new girl, stood near the window. She held a small paper heart she had made. “I don’t know the moves,” she whispered. Her eyes looked worried.

Robo-Pup trotted over and lowered his head kindly. “You can go at your own speed,” he said. “I will show you. We move to feel good, not to be fast.”

Mina took a breath. “Okay.”

Robo-Pup had an idea. “Let’s craft our own sport game!” he chirped. “We can make it gentle. We can make it fun.”

He opened a drawer with a soft whirr. Whirr-click! Inside were stickers, yarn, and colorful tape. The children sat in a circle on the soft rug. They stuck gold stars on the red ball. They taped yarn on the floor to make a wiggly path. The yarn felt tickly on their fingers.

“What is the game called?” Maya asked.

Robo-Pup’s eyes twinkled. “Wiggle Warm-Up Ball!”

“How do we play?” Tommy asked.

“We roll the ball along the yarn path,” said Robo-Pup. “We cheer for our friends. We move our bodies with big hearts.”

Mina hugged her paper heart. “Can I start slow?”

“Yes,” said Robo-Pup. “Slow is perfect.”

Mina rolled the starry ball. It went swish-swish along the yarn. The room smelled like paper and glue as they cheered. Maya clapped softly. Tommy whispered, “Go, Mina!”

Mina smiled. “I did it!”

“Now try two steps and a reach,” Robo-Pup said. “Step-step, reach!” The children followed. Step-step, reach! Their hands brushed the cool air. Their cheeks got warm and pink. They felt proud.

Suddenly, Maya stopped. “My tummy feels fluttery,” she said.

Robo-Pup nodded. “Let’s take a rest breath. In through the nose. Out like blowing bubbles.” They breathed together. In… out… The music sparkled like tiny bells. Maya’s fluttery tummy felt calm.

“Exercise can be gentle,” Robo-Pup said. “Exercise can be kind. We move, we rest, we try again.”

Tommy picked up the ball and rolled it to Maya. “Your turn,” he said kindly.

Maya rolled it back. “Thanks, Tommy.”

Robo-Pup wagged. “That is compassion! We cheer and help.”

He added one more move. “Let’s try a friendly sport skill: soft kicks.” He set the ball on the rug. “Tap with your toe. Tap-tap!” The taps were quiet and polite. Tap-tap, tap!

Mina tapped once. “I did a sport!” she said with bright eyes.

“Yes,” said Robo-Pup. “Sports are for everyone. We accept all speeds and all steps.”

They made a celebration circle. The children held up their paper hearts. The hearts rustled soft like leaves. Robo-Pup’s tail clicked happy clicks.

“What did we learn?” Robo-Pup asked.

Maya said, “Warm-ups help.”

Tommy said, “Breathing helps.”

Mina said, “Going slow is okay. Friends cheer.”

Robo-Pup nodded. “Exercise is moving with care. Compassion is helping friends. Sports can be gentle and fun. When we move, our bodies feel strong and our hearts feel kind.”

The sun peeked in and hugged the room with light. The nursery felt cozy and bright. The children stretched one more time. Reach up, wiggle down, big smiles all around.

Robo-Pup bowed. “Wiggle Warm-Up Ball forever!”

“Forever!” the children laughed.

The End.
