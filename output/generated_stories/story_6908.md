# Bubbles in the Attic

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a happy flip of the tail. One sunny afternoon, a gentle rain began to patter on the roof of a quiet attic. Tip-tap, tip-tap. Bubbles swam through a wide, watery window of imagination and found himself in the attic, where dust sparkled like tiny stars in the slant of the sun.

“Oooh,” Bubbles whispered, his voice soft like a bubble popping. “This place feels calm.” The attic smelled like old wood and paper. He could hear the soft rustle of dry leaves outside and the creak of the floorboards. It was a good place to think.

On a small table sat a big notebook, a cup of crayons, and a friendly clock that went tick… tock… tick… tock. Bubbles smiled. “I want to write a story,” he said. “I want to plan a fun day!”

He opened the notebook. The paper felt smooth under his fin. He picked a blue crayon and drew a wavy line. “This is me,” he giggled. He picked a yellow crayon. “This is the sun!” He hummed a happy tune as he wrote simple words, sounding them out slowly. “Swim. Smile. Share.”

Just then, a little mouse named Pip peeked out from a cozy box. Pip’s whiskers twitched. “Hi, Bubbles,” said Pip in a squeaky, warm voice. “What are you doing?”

“I’m writing my plan for the day,” Bubbles said. “I want to play, help a friend, and say kind words.”

Pip nodded. “That sounds nice.” Pip climbed onto the table and sniffed the crayons. “Mmm, waxy!” he said with a grin.

Bubbles tapped the page. “First, I will write. Then, I will tidy. Then, I will sing.” He drew three little boxes and put a tiny check mark in the first one. “Step one, write!” he said.

The rain whispered on the roof. Bubbles felt calm. He wrote his name with big round letters. He drew a heart. He felt proud of his careful plan.

Then the friendly clock went tock a little too loud, and Pip jumped. “Oh!” Pip squeaked. His tiny paws skittered, and his tail swished. Pip knocked over the cup of crayons. The crayons rolled and bumped—clink, clonk, clatter—right across the page, making a zig-zag rainbow. A red crayon left a big line across Bubbles’s neat plan.

Bubbles froze. His tummy felt twisty. His face felt hot. “My page,” he whispered.

Pip’s ears drooped. “I’m sorry, Bubbles,” Pip said, voice small and shaky. “I didn’t mean to. I got scared.”

Bubbles looked at the messy page. He took a slow breath. In. Out. He remembered his plan: play, help a friend, say kind words. He remembered a word he loved: forgive.

Bubbles touched the red line with his fin. He could feel the waxy bump. He listened to the rain. Tip-tap. Tip-tap. He looked at Pip’s worried eyes.

“Pip,” Bubbles said softly, “I feel upset. I worked hard. But I know it was an accident.” He put his fin on his heart. “I can forgive you.”

Pip blinked. “You can?” His whiskers wiggled with hope.

“Yes,” Bubbles said, and he gave a gentle smile. “I forgive you.” The twisty feeling in his tummy loosened. His cheeks cooled. He felt lighter, like a bubble floating up.

“Thank you,” Pip said. “I want to help fix it. Can I help you plan again?”

Bubbles nodded. “Yes. We can plan together.” He turned to a new page. He wrote the word PLAN at the top, slowly, carefully. “Let’s make a better plan,” he said. “We can write steps and be careful with the crayons.”

Pip grinned. “Step one: crayons stay in the cup unless we are using them.”

“Good idea,” Bubbles said. He wrote it down. “Step two: one crayon at a time.”

“Step three,” Pip squeaked, “take a deep breath if something goes wrong.”

Bubbles wrote that too. “Step four: use kind words.”

They drew little boxes again. They checked the first box when they put the crayons in the cup. Click. Bubbles felt steady and proud. He was honest with his feelings. He kept his plan. He showed integrity by doing what he said he would do.

Together they wrote: “We will tidy. We will sing. We will share snacks.” Pip added a tiny drawing of a cracker. Bubbles added a wavy smile.

Soon, the attic felt even cozier. The dust sparkles danced. The rain softened to a hush. Bubbles and Pip sang a simple song:

“When plans go oops,
we breathe and try,
we fix with care,
and we don’t cry.
We forgive, we share,
we start again,
with kind hearts, yes,
and a happy friend!”

They finished their plan and gently colored the edges. They put the crayons back, one, two, three, soft and slow. Bubbles looked at Pip. “Thank you for helping,” he said.

Pip nodded. “Thank you for forgiving me.”

Bubbles smiled. “Forgiveness makes room in my heart,” he said. “Now there is room for fun!”

They checked the last boxes—tidy, sing, share. The clock went tick… tock in a friendly way. The attic smelled warm and safe. Bubbles felt peaceful. His plan was finished. His friend felt happy. And his story was written with love.

Bubbles closed the notebook and patted it gently. “Today I learned something big,” he said. “When someone makes a mistake, I can forgive. We can plan again. We can use kind words. That is the right thing to do.”

Pip clapped his tiny paws. “That is a good lesson,” he agreed.

They listened to the quiet attic and the soft, sweet rain. And they smiled.

The End.
