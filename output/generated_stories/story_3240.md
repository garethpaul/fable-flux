# Nutkin's Colorful Solution

Nutkin the squirrel loved exploring the old garden shed behind Mrs. Garcia's house. The wooden walls smelled like sunshine and rain, and dust sparkles danced in the golden light that streamed through the window.

One morning, Nutkin heard soft sniffling sounds coming from inside the shed. He peeked through a crack and saw a little mouse sitting next to some scattered paintbrushes and tubes of paint.

"Hello there," Nutkin said gently, squeezing through the door. "I'm Nutkin. What's wrong?"

The mouse looked up with teary eyes. "I'm Pip," she squeaked. "Mrs. Garcia asked me to paint a beautiful picture for her granddaughter's birthday tomorrow. But look!" She pointed at the messy paint tubes. "All the colors are mixed up and muddy. I don't know how to make them bright and pretty again."

Nutkin's bushy tail twitched as he thought. He looked around the shed carefully, noticing everything - the clean water bucket, the soap bar on the shelf, and some old rags hanging on a hook.

"Hmm," Nutkin said, scratching his chin. "Let me think about this step by step. What do we need to make beautiful colors?"

"Clean paint?" Pip guessed.

"That's right! And what makes things clean?"

"Water and soap!" Pip's eyes brightened a little.

Nutkin nodded. "Exactly! Now, let's think logically. First, we need to clean the brushes so the colors don't mix. Then we can start fresh."

Together, they carried the water bucket to the brushes. Nutkin showed Pip how to swirl each brush in the soapy water until it was clean. The water turned murky brown, but the brushes came out sparkling.

"Now," Nutkin said, "let's think about colors. What happens when you mix red and yellow?"

"Orange!" Pip squeaked excitedly.

"And blue and yellow?"

"Green!"

Nutkin smiled. "You're learning! Let's start with the basic colors and mix them carefully, one at a time."

They squeezed fresh paint onto a clean board. Pip watched as Nutkin mixed just a tiny bit of red with yellow, creating the most beautiful orange she had ever seen.

"Wow!" Pip gasped. "It's like magic!"

"It's not magic," Nutkin chuckled. "It's thinking step by step and being careful. That's called logical thinking."

For the next hour, they worked together. Nutkin taught Pip to clean her brush between each color, to mix small amounts first, and to think about what colors she needed before she started.

Soon, Pip was painting the most wonderful picture - a bright garden with orange flowers, green leaves, and a blue sky with fluffy white clouds.

"I did it!" Pip danced around happily. "Thank you, Nutkin! You taught me that when I have a problem, I can solve it by thinking carefully and taking it one step at a time."

Nutkin's chest puffed with pride. "That's right, Pip. Logical thinking means looking at a problem, breaking it into small pieces, and solving each piece. Plus, helping friends always makes everything better!"

The next day, Mrs. Garcia's granddaughter squealed with delight when she saw Pip's beautiful painting. And Nutkin? He felt warm and fuzzy inside, knowing that helping a friend and teaching logical thinking had made everyone happy.

The End.
