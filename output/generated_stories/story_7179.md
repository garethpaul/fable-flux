# Polly's Wellness Day

Polly the parrot lived in a cozy home office with her friend Sarah. The office had a big wooden desk, colorful books on shelves, and a sunny window where Polly loved to perch. Her bright green feathers sparkled in the morning light, and her orange beak was perfect for chattering.

"Good morning, Polly!" said Sarah as she sat down at her computer. "I have lots of work to do today."

Polly fluffed her feathers and stretched her wings wide. "Good morning! Polly feels stiff," she squawked. Her little bird body felt tight from sleeping all night on her perch.

Sarah looked up from her screen. "You know what, Polly? I feel stiff too. I've been sitting at this desk too much lately."

Polly tilted her colorful head. She had a wonderful idea! "Let's move together!" she chirped excitedly.

"Move together?" Sarah asked with a smile.

Polly hopped down from her perch and began to stretch her bright wings up high, then down low. "Stretch like this!" she called out. "Feel good!"

Sarah giggled and stood up from her chair. She raised her arms up high like Polly's wings, then bent down to touch her toes. "This does feel good!" she said.

Next, Polly began marching in place, lifting her orange feet high with each step. "March, march, march!" she sang in her sweet bird voice. The sound of her tiny claws tapping on the wooden floor made a gentle rhythm.

Sarah marched too, feeling her heart beat faster. "This is fun, Polly!"

But then Polly tried to do a spin and tumbled over. "Whoops!" she squawked, her feathers all ruffled.

"Are you okay?" Sarah asked, worried.

Polly stood up and shook her feathers. At first, she felt embarrassed. But then she remembered something important. "It's okay to fall," she said proudly. "Polly will try again!"

Sarah clapped her hands. "That's right! Practice makes us better."

Polly tried the spin again, this time going slower. She wobbled a little but stayed upright. "I did it!" she cheered, her black eyes sparkling with joy.

"Now let's breathe deeply," Polly suggested. She puffed out her little chest and took a big breath, making a soft "ahhhh" sound as she let it out.

Sarah copied Polly, breathing in through her nose and out through her mouth. "I feel so much more relaxed now," she said.

Polly hopped back onto her perch, feeling proud and happy. Her body felt loose and strong. "Moving makes Polly feel wonderful!" she announced.

"You're so smart, Polly," Sarah said, giving her feathered friend a gentle pet. "You taught me that taking care of our bodies is important. Even when we're busy, we need to move and stretch."

From that day on, Polly and Sarah took movement breaks together every day. Sometimes they would stretch, sometimes they would dance, and sometimes they would just walk around the office. Polly learned that wellness meant taking care of her whole self - her body, her mind, and her feelings.

"Taking care of ourselves is the best gift we can give," Polly would say, preening her beautiful feathers in the warm sunlight.

And whenever Polly made a mistake while moving, she would remember to be kind to herself and try again. Because that's what wellness is all about - practicing, accepting ourselves, and never giving up.

The End.
