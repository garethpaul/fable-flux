# Bubbles in the Balcony Garden

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to explore. One sunny morning, Bubbles swam through a sparkly daydream wave and—whoosh!—popped into a balcony garden high above a busy city. The air felt warm, like a gentle hug. The garden smelled sweet, like mint and tomatoes. Bees hummed, “Bzz-bzz,” and leaves whispered, “shhh-shhh” in the breeze.

“Wow! So many plants,” Bubbles squeaked, flipping her tail with joy. “Hello, little garden!”

Mina, a kind child with bright eyes, stepped outside. She carried a small watering can. “Hi! I’m Mina,” she said, smiling wide. “Are you… a dolphin?”

“Yes! I’m Bubbles,” the dolphin giggled. “I want to help. I love helping. What can we do?”

Mina looked around at the balcony garden. There were tiny tomatoes, curly herbs, and a long vine that tickled the railing. “We have chores,” Mina said. “We water the plants, we sweep the leaves, and we thank the Earth.”

Bubbles clapped her fins. “Thank the Earth? Oh yes! Thank you, Earth!” Her voice was bright and bubbly.

Mina nodded. “We show gratitude. That means we feel thankful. When we water, we say, ‘Thank you, water.’ When we see bees, we say, ‘Thank you, bees.’ We are global citizens. We care for our home, and the whole world, too.”

Bubbles swished closer. “I can help. But I need to say what I can do. I’m good at splashing. I’m not good at holding cans.”

“That’s self-advocacy,” Mina said gently. “You told me what you can do. Thank you for telling me.”

They made a plan. Mina would hold the watering can. Bubbles would make a tiny splash to stir the soil. “Gentle splashes,” Mina whispered. “The plants like soft rain.”

“Soft rain,” Bubbles repeated. She made a soft, shimmery splash. Drip, drop, drip. The tomato plant perked up. A little red tomato glowed like a button.

“Thank you, water,” Mina said.

“Thank you, water,” Bubbles echoed, smiling.

A bee buzzed by, carrying golden dust. “Hello, Bee!” Bubbles called. “Thank you for helping the flowers.”

“Bzz-bzz,” hummed the bee, as if to say, “You’re welcome.”

Next, a big leaf drooped low. “I feel heavy,” the leaf seemed to sigh.

Bubbles blinked. “Can I help that leaf? I can blow bubble breaths.”

“Let’s try,” Mina said. “Innovation means trying a new idea to help.”

Bubbles took a deep, dolphin breath and blew a soft stream of bubbly air. The bubbles tickled the leaf and lifted water drops off its surface. The leaf sprang up, light and happy.

“It worked!” Mina cheered. “That new idea helped.”

Bubbles giggled. “Thank you for letting me try.”

They swept the floor together. Swish, swish went Mina’s broom. Swirl, swirl went Bubbles’ tail, making the little piles of leaves scoot together. A pigeon on the next balcony cooed, “Coo-coo.” A neighbor waved from below. “Nice garden!” they called.

Mina waved back. “We share a sky,” she said to Bubbles. “We share water. We share air. That is why we care together. That is global citizenship.”

Bubbles felt her heart glow warm. “I am thankful for the sky,” she said. “I am thankful for you.”

Mina smiled. “I am thankful for you, too.”

They found a big bucket for compost. “Old leaves become new soil,” Mina explained. “We don’t waste. We give back.”

“That’s gratitude in action,” Bubbles said proudly. “Thank you, old leaves.”

Just then, a small pot wobbled. “Oh!” Bubbles cried. “The wind is strong.” She took another breath. “I need help. The pot is near the edge.”

“That’s good self-advocacy,” Mina said. “You asked for help.” She slid the pot to a safe place and tied it with a soft ribbon to the railing. “Safe and snug.”

Bubbles sighed with relief. “Thank you for listening.”

“Thank you for telling me,” Mina replied.

They rested and shared a snack. Mina ate a crunchy apple. Bubbles imagined a splashy sea-salad of seaweed. Nearby, the tomato plant offered a tiny red gift.

Mina picked it gently. “Dear tomato,” she whispered, “thank you.”

Bubbles added, “We are grateful. We help, and we thank. That makes our hearts big.”

Mina nodded. “Yes. When we feel thankful, we care more. We take good turns. We listen. We try new ideas. We help our home and the world.”

The sun began to set, painting the clouds pink and peach. The city sounded soft and sleepy. Taxis hummed far below. The bees headed home. The balcony garden glowed with quiet pride.

Bubbles looked around. The leaves were clean. The soil was moist. The pots were safe. The compost bucket was full of future soil. Mina and Bubbles held hands—well, hand and fin—and took a deep breath together.

“Thank you, Earth,” they said in one voice.
“Thank you, water.”
“Thank you, bees.”
“Thank you, neighbors.”
“Thank you, friends.”

Bubbles felt a gentle tug, like the sea calling. “I must go now,” she said softly. “But I will remember. I will be grateful. I will speak up when I need help. I will try new ideas. I will care for our big, shared home.”

Mina hugged Bubbles. “I will, too.”

With a sparkle and a splash of dream-light, Bubbles swam back through the wave and into the deep blue sea. The balcony garden was calm and happy, and so were their hearts.

The lesson was clear: Gratitude helps us care, speak up kindly, and work together for our world.

The End.
