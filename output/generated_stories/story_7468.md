# Toby the Turtle Learns to Listen

In a cozy living room filled with soft cushions and colorful toys, lived a shy little turtle named Toby. His green shell sparkled in the morning sunlight that streamed through the big window. Toby loved his safe, warm home, but he was very, very shy.

Every morning, Mama Turtle would call out, "Toby, it's time to clean up your toys before breakfast!"

But Toby would pull his head deep into his shell. "I can't do it by myself," he would whisper. "It's too hard!"

Mama Turtle would always help him, picking up his blocks and putting away his books. Toby felt happy that Mama helped, but deep down, something didn't feel quite right.

One sunny Tuesday morning, something different happened. Mama Turtle had to leave early to help Grandma Turtle in the garden.

"Toby, my little one," Mama said gently, "today you need to listen carefully to the clock. When it goes 'tick-tock, tick-tock' ten times, it will be time to clean up. Then you can have your yummy lettuce breakfast!"

Toby's eyes grew wide. "But Mama, I can't do it alone!"

"Yes, you can," Mama smiled, giving his shell a gentle pat. "You're braver and stronger than you think. Just listen to my words, and listen to the clock."

After Mama left, Toby sat very still. The living room felt big and quiet. He could hear the clock on the wall: "Tick-tock, tick-tock, tick-tock."

Toby played with his favorite red ball, rolling it across the soft carpet. The carpet felt fuzzy under his little feet. He stacked his wooden blocks high, making a tower that reached almost to his chin. The blocks made a satisfying "click" sound when they touched.

Then he heard it: "Tick-tock, tick-tock, tick-tock..." The clock was getting louder!

Toby started counting: "One tick-tock, two tick-tock, three tick-tock..."

When he reached ten, Toby remembered Mama's words. "Time to clean up," he said to himself.

He looked around at his toys scattered everywhere. His tummy felt nervous, like butterflies were dancing inside.

"I can do this," Toby whispered, remembering Mama's gentle voice. "I just need to listen and try."

First, he picked up his red ball. It felt smooth and warm in his hands. He placed it carefully in the toy box. "One toy done!" he said proudly.

Next, he gathered his blocks. They were heavy, but he carried them one by one. "Two, three, four toys done!"

Slowly but surely, Toby cleaned up every single toy. His books went on the shelf, his crayons in their box, and his stuffed animals in their special corner.

When Mama Turtle came home, she gasped with joy. "Oh my! Toby, you did it all by yourself!"

Toby's head popped out of his shell with the biggest smile. "I listened to the clock, just like you said! And I listened to your words in my heart!"

"You managed your time perfectly," Mama beamed. "And you were so self-reliant! That means you could take care of things all by yourself."

Toby felt warm and proud inside. His shell seemed to shine even brighter. "I can do things by myself," he said happily. "I just needed to listen and believe in me!"

From that day on, Toby always listened carefully to instructions and managed his time well. He learned that being self-reliant felt wonderful, and that he was much braver than he ever imagined.

The End.
