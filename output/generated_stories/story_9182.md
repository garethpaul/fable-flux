# Leo Learns to Speak Up

Leo the little lion cub bounced down the wooden stairs to the basement. His golden fur shimmered under the bright lights. The basement was very busy today! 

"Wow!" Leo said, looking around with wide amber eyes.

Uncle Bear was hammering wood. BANG! BANG! BANG! Cousin Rabbit was painting a big blue sign. The smell of paint filled the air. Sister Fox was moving heavy boxes. THUMP! THUMP! THUMP!

"This is so loud!" Leo thought. His little ears started to hurt from all the noise.

Leo wanted to help, but everyone looked so busy. He walked over to Uncle Bear.

"Uncle Bear, can I—" Leo started to say.

"Not now, Leo!" Uncle Bear said without looking up. BANG! BANG! went his hammer.

Leo's ears drooped. He tried talking to Cousin Rabbit next.

"Cousin Rabbit, may I—"

"Sorry, Leo! I'm very busy painting!" Rabbit said quickly, not stopping her brush.

Leo felt sad. His tummy started to feel funny. The loud noises made his head hurt. But everyone seemed too busy to listen.

Leo sat in the corner feeling small. "Maybe I should just be quiet," he whispered.

Then Leo remembered what Mama Lion always told him: "A brave lion speaks up when something is wrong."

Leo took a deep breath. He stood up tall and walked to the middle of the room.

"EXCUSE ME, EVERYONE!" Leo said in his biggest, bravest voice.

Uncle Bear stopped hammering. Cousin Rabbit put down her brush. Sister Fox stopped moving boxes. They all looked at little Leo.

"The noise is hurting my ears," Leo said clearly. "I need it to be quieter, please. And I want to help, but nobody will listen to me."

Uncle Bear's face grew soft. "Oh, Leo! I'm sorry. I didn't know the noise was too loud for you."

"We should have listened better," said Cousin Rabbit, putting down her paintbrush.

Sister Fox nodded. "You were very brave to tell us how you felt, Leo."

Uncle Bear smiled. "Let's take a quiet break. And Leo, we would love your help!"

They all sat in a circle on the soft carpet. The basement became peaceful and calm. Leo's ears stopped hurting. His tummy felt better too.

"What job would you like to do, Leo?" asked Uncle Bear gently.

Leo's eyes sparkled. "Can I help paint? I love the color blue!"

"Of course!" said Cousin Rabbit. "Here's a small brush just for you."

Leo dipped his brush in the smooth blue paint. He made careful strokes on the sign. It felt wonderful to help!

"Thank you for speaking up, Leo," said Sister Fox. "Now we know to check if the noise is too loud."

"And we'll always make time to listen to you," added Uncle Bear.

Leo purred happily as he painted. His golden tail swished back and forth. The basement smelled like paint and felt full of love.

When they finished their work, everyone admired the beautiful blue sign together.

"I'm proud of you for being brave," Uncle Bear said, giving Leo a gentle hug.

Leo smiled big, showing his tiny white teeth. "Mama was right. Speaking up helped everyone!"

From that day on, Leo always remembered to use his voice when he needed something. And his family always remembered to listen carefully to their brave little lion cub.

The End.
