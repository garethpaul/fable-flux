# Benny Bear Cub’s Office Routine

Benny was a friendly bear cub who lived with his family. Today, Benny had a big job to do in the home office. The home office was a cozy room with a soft rug, a humming computer, and a window that let in warm, golden light. The air smelled like paper and pine wood. Click-click went the keyboard. Whirr went the little printer.

Benny wore his tiny blue vest with a pocket. Inside the pocket was a small list. Mama Bear had helped him make it. The list said:
1. Tidy desk.
2. Sort papers.
3. Feed the inbox box.
4. Take a fair break.
5. Share the printer.

Benny smiled. “My routine keeps me strong,” he said. His voice sounded bright like the morning sun.

He pat-patted the rug with his paws and climbed into his little chair. “Step one!” he said. “Tidy desk.” He picked up pencils and put them in a cup. He stacked sticky notes with a soft rustle-rustle. He wiped crumbs with a gentle swish. The desk looked neat, and Benny felt calm.

“Step two: Sort papers,” Benny said. He sniffed the paper smell. “Mmm, like clean books.” He made two piles. “Bills go here. Drawings go there.” He found a crayon picture of a tall tree. “I drew this,” he giggled. He put it in the drawing pile with care.

Just then, a small squirrel popped into the doorway. It was Nixie, Benny’s neighbor friend. “Hi, Benny!” squeaked Nixie. “Can I use the printer? I want to print ten fun pages now-now-now!”

Benny looked at his list. He looked at the printer. The printer hummed softly, like it was resting. Benny took a deep breath. “I am on step two,” he said kindly. “My routine helps me finish. I will share the printer at step five. That is fair.”

Nixie’s tail twitched. “But I want it now!”

Benny nodded. “I know. Waiting is hard. But we take turns. That is justice. Justice means fair for everyone.” Benny pointed to a little sign above the printer. The sign said: “We wait. We share. We care.”

Nixie read the sign slowly. “We… wait. We share. We care.”

Benny smiled. “Would you like to help me with step three while you wait?”

Nixie’s ears perked up. “What is step three?”

“Feed the inbox box!” Benny said. He showed a small box with a happy face sticker. “All new papers go in here, so they do not get lost.”

Together they padded around the room. Plop went a paper into the inbox. Plop. Plop. “This is fun,” said Nixie. “It sounds like popcorn!”

Benny laughed. “Pop-pop-pop!”

When the inbox was full and tidy, Benny looked at his list. “Step four: Take a fair break.”

Nixie blinked. “A break? But I did not do work.”

“Waiting is work too,” Benny said gently. “Our break can be a stretch.” He stood up and stretched his arms high. “Reach for the sun!” he sang. They stretched and wiggled and took three slow breaths. In… and out. In… and out. In… and out. Benny felt his heart slow down. He felt proud. He was following his routine all by himself.

“Now step five,” Benny said. “Share the printer.”

Nixie bounced. “Is it my turn?”

“Yes,” said Benny. “We will take turns. You print one page, then I print one page for Mama, and then you print the next.” Benny tapped the button. Beep. The printer woke up. Whirr-click. Out slid a warm page. It smelled a little like warm toast.

“Oooh,” said Nixie, hugging her page. “It is a leaf maze!”

“Now my turn,” said Benny. He fed one paper in. Whirr-click. Out came Mama’s list with neat lines.

Nixie waited, then printed her next page. Together they counted. “One for you, one for me,” Benny said. “That is fair. That is justice.”

When they were done, Nixie looked around. The desk was tidy. The papers were sorted. The inbox was full and ready. “Benny,” she said, “your routine works. It helped us finish. It helped us share.”

Benny’s cheeks felt warm with happy pride. “Thank you, Nixie. My routine helps me be strong and calm. It helps me be kind.”

Mama Bear peeked into the home office. She smelled like honey tea. “I see clean stacks and happy faces,” she said. “What did you learn today?”

Benny stood tall on his chair. “I learned to follow my routine. It helps me do jobs all by myself.” He looked at Nixie. “And we learned justice. We waited, and we took turns. That was fair.”

Mama smiled. “That is a big bear lesson.”

Nixie waved her leaf maze. “I can wait and share too!”

Benny checked the last box on his list with a tiny star sticker. His paws felt steady. His heart felt bright. The home office hummed softly. The day felt smooth, like the clean desk.

Benny and Nixie did one more stretch. “We wait. We share. We care,” they said together. Benny put his list back in his pocket. He was ready for tomorrow’s routine.

The End.
