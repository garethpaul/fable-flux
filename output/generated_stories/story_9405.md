# Miko’s Color Parade

Miko was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. Miko lived in a playful living room with a bouncy couch, a tickly rug, and a big window that let in warm, golden sunshine. The room smelled like fresh crayons and clean laundry. Little dust motes danced in the light, like tiny stars.

One morning, Miko stretched his tiny paws. “Good morning, room!” he squeaked. He heard a gentle hum from the heater and the soft swish of the curtains. Miko loved to make things. He loved colors, sounds, and shapes.

Today, Miko had a big idea. “I want to make a Color Parade!” he said. “I want to show what I feel inside.” His whiskers wiggled with excitement.

He gathered safe, friendly art things from his craft box: paper, crayons, tape, and stickers. He set them on the low table. Tap, tap went his tiny feet on the wood. Scritch-scratch went the crayons. Miko drew a sunny circle. He drew a happy zigzag. He drew a wavy blue river. He sang a little song, “Color, color, come and play. Bright and bold, let’s lead the way!”

Miko opened a box of buttons he used for pretend coins. Click, clack, click. “These can be parade hats,” he said. He made a tiny sign that read, “Welcome to the Color Parade!” The letters were big and friendly. He put it on the couch like a stage.

Just then, his friend Dot the ladybug flew in and landed on Miko’s ear. “Hi, Miko!” Dot buzzed softly. “What are you making?”

“A Color Parade,” said Miko. “It shows my feelings. Yellow is for my warm tummy. Blue is for my calm breath. Red is for my brave heart.”

Dot smiled. “Can I join?”

“Yes!” said Miko. “Everyone can make and share.”

Miko and Dot taped their drawings on a long string, like flags. The string made a gentle swish as it moved. The pictures fluttered by the open window, and the cool breeze felt like a hug. Miko’s heart felt big and proud.

Soon, Tippy the turtle peeked from under the couch. “What’s all this?” he asked in a slow, cozy voice.

“It’s our Color Parade!” said Miko. “Do you want to add a picture?”

Tippy nodded. He picked a purple crayon. “I like purple,” he said. “It feels like quiet.” He drew a soft purple circle with little stars.

Dot clapped. “It’s lovely!”

Miko beamed. “We are sharing our ideas. That is creative expression! We are saying, ‘This is me,’ with colors.”

They marched around the living room. “Parade, parade!” sang Dot. “Colors, colors!” sang Miko. Tippy tapped his shell like a drum. Thum, thum, thum. The rug felt squishy under their feet, and the air smelled like orange peels from snack time.

Then Miko looked around and blinked. Oh dear. Little paper bits were on the floor. Some crayons rolled under the chair. Tape curls stuck to the rug. “Oops,” said Miko. “We made a happy mess.”

Dot looked worried. “Is a mess okay?”

Miko took a deep breath. “We can fix it. We care for our room. That is being a good helper,” he said. “That is civic responsibility.”

Tippy nodded slowly. “We make art. We also clean up.”

Miko made a plan. “Jobs for everyone! I will pick up paper bits. Dot, please gather crayons. Tippy, can you roll the tape back and place the stickers in the box?”

“Yes!” said Dot.

“Yes,” said Tippy, with a steady smile.

They worked together. Rustle, rustle went the paper into the bin. Click, click went the crayons into their cup. Tippy smoothed the tape with careful little pushes. Soon the living room looked neat and bright again. The couch was tidy, the rug was happy, and the window light made the pictures glow.

Miko felt warm inside. “We did it,” he said. “We made art and we took care of our space.”

Dot fluttered. “I feel proud.”

Tippy smiled. “I feel calm.”

Miko held up a mirror made of shiny foil. He looked at his own tiny face. “I feel brave and strong,” he said softly. “I can make things. I can share. I can help.”

They hung the Color Parade across the window. The yellow circles, blue waves, red zigs, and purple stars made rainbow light dots on the floor. Miko wiggled his nose. “Let’s have a show!”

They invited the stuffed bear, the wooden giraffe, and the toy drum to be the audience. Miko stood tall on the couch stage. “Welcome to our Color Parade,” he announced. “Our colors show our feelings. We can be warm, calm, brave, and quiet. We can share and we can help.”

Dot waved a tiny flag drawing. Tippy tapped a slow beat. Miko pointed to each picture and told a simple story: “This yellow circle is my morning smile. This blue wave is my soft breath. This red zig is my brave try. This purple star is my quiet rest.”

The toys did not move, but Miko felt their happy listening. The room seemed to hum with yes, yes, yes.

After the show, they bowed. Miko’s cheeks felt tingly and glad. “Thank you for coming,” he said.

Dot whispered, “Miko, your art is you.”

Tippy added, “And you took care of the room, too.”

Miko hugged them both. “Today I learned that my voice can be colors. I can show my heart with art. I can help my home stay clean. That makes me feel good about me.”

The sun slid lower, and the golden light turned soft and peachy. The Color Parade fluttered gently, whispering shhh like a lullaby.

Miko snuggled into a couch cushion. “Tomorrow,” he said, “we can make a Music Parade.”

“Yes,” said Dot.

“Yes,” said Tippy.

Miko closed his eyes with a smile. “I am creative. I am kind. I am a good helper.”

The living room glowed, cozy and proud.

The End.
