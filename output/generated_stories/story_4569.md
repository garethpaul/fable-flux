# Paws in a Row

Sunny the bear cub sat on the peaceful front porch. The wood boards felt warm under his furry paws. A soft breeze tickled his ears, and the porch swing creaked, creak, creak, in a gentle rhythm. Birds chirped from the trees, and the smell of fresh pine and sweet berries floated in the air.

“Today, I will do things all by myself,” Sunny said with a proud little puff. His round belly wiggled as he stood up. “I will learn to move my paws in a good, steady way.”

On the porch, a colorful ribbon lay coiled like a rainbow snake. A tiny basket of blueberries waited near the steps. A wooden balance board—two planks and a log—rested beside the swing.

Mama Bear peeked out through the screen door. “Good morning, Sunny,” she said. “What is your plan?”

“I want to carry the blueberries to the porch table,” Sunny said. “And I want to play with my ribbon and try the wobbly board. I want to do it carefully. I want to practice… coor-di-na-tion.”

Mama smiled. “Coordination means your eyes, paws, and body work together. Slow and steady. You can do it.”

Sunny nodded. “I can do it.” He picked up the little basket. The blueberries were cool and smooth. Plip, plop, they rolled softly against one another.

Step. Pause. Step. Pause. Sunny watched his paws. He held his belly tight like a tiny drum. “Eyes, paws, body,” he whispered. “Work together.” He took two steps, and—oops!—the basket tilted. A blueberry rolled toward the edge of the porch.

“Oh no!” Sunny gasped. He set the basket down, took a deep breath, and watched the berry. He crouched low, reached slowly, and caught it with one careful paw. “Got you,” he said, smiling. He put the berry back and clapped once. Clap!

Mama Bear waved from the door. “You solved a problem by slowing down,” she said. “Peaceful and patient.”

Sunny placed the basket on the porch table. “I did it!” he cheered. His chest felt warm and proud.

Next, Sunny picked up the rainbow ribbon. It felt soft and silky. He made it dance in the air. Swish. Swirl. Swish-swish. The ribbon looped like a shiny fish.

“I will make a big circle,” Sunny said. He tried to move his paw in a round path. His eyes followed the ribbon. His feet stood strong on the warm boards. Swish—oops! The ribbon tickled his nose. “Achoo!”

Sunny giggled. “Let me try again.” He took a breath through his nose. In… out… He lifted his paw and watched the ribbon carefully. Slow and steady. The ribbon curved into a glowing circle. Round and round it went.

“I’m doing it!” Sunny laughed. “My eyes watch. My paw moves. My body stays steady. That is coordination!”

He made little circles. He made big circles. He made a figure eight that looked like two blueberry pancakes. Mama clapped. “Beautiful, Sunny.”

Then Sunny looked at the wooden balance board. “This part is wobbly,” he said. “I will be brave and calm.” He put the ribbon away and padded to the board. The log made the plank tip, tip, tip.

Sunny put one paw on the plank. It wobbled. Wobble, wobble. He paused. “Hello, wobble,” he said kindly. “Let’s be friends.”

He put on his thinking face. “How can I stay steady?” he asked. He looked at the porch rail. He looked at his paws. He looked at the board. “I will hold the rail. I will bend my knees. I will look ahead.”

He tried again. One paw. Two paws. Bend. Breathe. Look ahead. The board rocked—tip one way, then tip the other. Sunny felt the movement in his legs, in his feet, and in his belly. “I can match the wobbles,” he whispered.

He moved slowly, heel to toes, toes to heel. Tip… steady… tip… steady. “I’m doing it!” Sunny smiled so big his cheeks felt round as plums.

Just then, a ladybug landed on the rail. “Hello, tiny friend,” Sunny said softly. “Please stay safe.” He paused so the ladybug could crawl along. He waited, calm and kind. When the ladybug flew away, Sunny took two more careful steps. He reached the end of the board and hopped off with a small thump. “I did it!”

Mama Bear opened the door and hugged him. “You used your body wisely. You solved problems with peace and patience. That is coordination and kind thinking.”

Sunny’s ears perked up. He remembered the basket. “May I share the blueberries?” he asked.

“Yes,” Mama said.

Sunny brought the basket to the porch steps. He sat with Mama on the swing. Creak, creak. They popped blueberries into their mouths. Pop! The berries were sweet and cool. The breeze brushed Sunny’s fur. A happy glow spread through him like warm sunshine.

“I learned something,” Sunny said. “When I slow down and pay attention, my eyes, paws, and body work together. I can do things by myself. I can solve little problems in a calm way.”

Mama nodded. “That is a wonderful lesson.”

Sunny looked at his paws. “Paws in a row,” he giggled. “That means I’m ready.”

They watched the trees sway. The porch felt safe and peaceful. Sunny smiled at the world and at his strong, steady self.

The End.
