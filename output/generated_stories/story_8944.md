# Miko's Porch Helper Plan

Miko the monkey loved sitting on his peaceful front porch. The wooden boards felt warm under his fuzzy bottom, and he could smell the sweet flowers blooming in the garden. Every morning, he would swing his long tail and watch the neighborhood wake up.

"What a beautiful day!" Miko said, stretching his arms wide.

But today was different. Miko noticed his friend Bella the bunny hopping by, carrying a heavy basket of laundry. She looked tired and sad.

"Hi Bella!" Miko called out. "Want to sit on my porch and rest?"

Bella stopped and shook her head. "I can't, Miko. I have so many chores to do. My mom is sick, so I have to do all the washing, sweeping, and cooking by myself. It's too much work for one little bunny."

Miko's heart felt heavy seeing his friend so worried. He scratched his head, thinking hard. "There must be a way to help," he thought.

Just then, Carlos the cat walked by, followed by Daisy the duck and Sam the squirrel. They were all heading to the park to play.

"Wait!" Miko called out bravely. He had an idea. "Friends, Bella needs our help. She has lots of chores because her mom is sick. What if we all worked together?"

Carlos tilted his head. "But we don't know how to do chores."

"We can learn!" Miko said with excitement. "And it will be more fun if we all do it together. Nobody should have to do everything alone."

Daisy flapped her wings. "I could help carry things with my strong beak!"

"And I'm great at climbing high places!" added Sam the squirrel.

Carlos purred, "I could help sweep with my fluffy tail."

Bella's eyes filled with happy tears. "Really? You would all help me?"

"Of course!" Miko said, jumping down from his porch. "That's what friends do. We include everyone and help each other."

The friends made a plan. They divided the chores so everyone had a job that matched their special skills. Miko used his strong arms to carry the heavy basket. Daisy picked up scattered toys with her beak. Sam climbed up to dust the high shelves. Carlos swept the floors with his tail, and Bella showed everyone the best way to fold clothes.

"This is actually fun!" laughed Carlos as he swished his tail across the floor.

"And much faster too!" chirped Daisy.

As they worked, they sang songs and told jokes. The sun felt warm on their backs, and the fresh air smelled like soap and sunshine. In just one hour, all the chores were done!

"Thank you so much," Bella said, giving everyone a big hug. "I felt so alone before, but now I feel happy and loved."

"We're all part of one big neighborhood family," Miko said wisely. "When we include everyone and work together, big problems become small ones."

From that day on, the friends met on Miko's peaceful porch every morning. They would plan how to help each other and make sure no one felt left out or overwhelmed.

"Taking care of each other is the most important chore of all," Miko would say, swinging his tail contentedly as he watched his friends play together in the garden.

The End.
