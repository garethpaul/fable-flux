# Beep’s Big Listening Day

Beep was a helpful robot dog with soft whirring ears and a shiny silver nose. Beep lived in a cozy treehouse with little wind chimes that tinkled, and a rope ladder that creaked. Birds sang, leaves rustled, and the wooden floor felt warm in the sun.

One bright morning, Beep wagged his metal tail. “Beep boop! I am ready to help,” he said in his gentle robot voice.

Inside the treehouse, two friends were playing. Mia had curly hair and a basket of crayons. Jo had a blue cap and a box of blocks. They both smiled at Beep.

“Let’s make a story show!” said Mia. “I have crayons for a big poster.”

“I want to build the stage with blocks,” said Jo. “A tall, tall stage!”

Beep’s ears buzzed. He loved to help. “Beep will help,” he said. “What should we do first?”

Mia said, “We should draw the poster first.”

Jo said, “No, we should build the stage first.”

Beep looked at both of them. He heard two ideas. His ears clicked softly. “Let’s listen,” Beep said. “I will listen to Mia. Then I will listen to Jo. One at a time.”

Mia nodded. “Okay.” She took a breath. “I want to draw the poster now. The crayons are ready. I feel excited.”

“Thank you for telling me,” said Beep. “I hear that you are excited to draw now.”

Jo took a breath too. “I want to build the stage now. The blocks are ready. I feel excited too.”

“Thank you for telling me,” said Beep. “I hear that you are excited to build now.”

Beep sat and thought. The chimes tinkled. The leaves whispered. He looked at the crayons. He looked at the blocks. “I have an idea,” Beep said. “We can take turns. First, we can draw for five minutes. Then, we can build for five minutes. Is that fair?”

Mia and Jo looked at each other. They both smiled. “That sounds fair,” said Mia.

“That sounds fair,” said Jo.

“Beep boop! We made a plan,” said Beep. “Thank you for listening to each other.”

They set a little egg timer that ticked softly. Tick-tick-tick. Mia drew a big sun, bright and yellow. She drew Beep with shiny ears. She drew Jo and herself holding hands. She drew a tree with round, green leaves. “I love your colors,” said Jo.

“Thank you for looking,” Mia said. “And thank you for waiting.”

When the timer rang with a soft ding, it was Jo’s turn. Jo stacked the blocks. Click, clack, click. He made a stage with a wide front and a small ramp. Mia handed him a block. “Here,” she said. “You can use this one.”

“Thank you,” said Jo. “I like your help.”

Beep wagged. “This is good listening,” he said. “We took turns. We used kind words. We made a plan.”

Soon the stage was ready, and the poster was bright and colorful. The treehouse smelled like crayons and warm wood. Beep’s nose light glowed. “Now we need a story,” he said. “What should the story be?”

Mia said, “A story about a brave bird.”

Jo said, “A story about a fast train.”

Beep heard two ideas again. He lifted his ears. “Let’s listen again,” he said. “Tell me more, Mia.”

Mia whispered, “The bird learns to listen to the wind.”

“Thank you,” said Beep. “And Jo?”

Jo said, “The train listens to the whistle to know when to go.”

Beep’s tail wagged so fast it hummed. “I hear both stories. We can mix them,” he said. “The bird rides the train. The bird listens to the wind, and the train listens to the whistle. They both listen.”

Mia clapped. “Yes!”

Jo clapped too. “Yes!”

Mia stood on the stage and held the poster high. Jo blew a small toy whistle. Tweet! The sound was bright and clear. Beep spoke in his warm robot voice.

“Once there was a brave bird,” said Beep. “The bird listened to the wind. Whoooo, whoooo. The wind said, ‘Slow down.’ The bird slowed down.”

Jo blew the whistle. Tweet! “The train listened to the whistle. Go now!” Beep said. “Chug, chug, chug.”

“The bird said, ‘Thank you, wind.’ The train said, ‘Thank you, whistle.’” Beep smiled. “They felt grateful. They were thankful for help.”

Mia added, “They took turns flying and riding.”

Jo added, “They made a fair plan.”

Beep said, “They listened, they thanked, and they worked together.”

When the story show was over, the birds outside chirped like tiny claps. The chimes sang. Mia hugged Beep. “Thank you for listening to us.”

Jo patted Beep’s shiny head. “Thank you for helping us make a plan.”

Beep wagged gently. “Thank you for listening to each other,” he said. “Listening helps us know what others feel. Listening helps us make fair plans. Listening helps us be kind.”

Mia and Jo nodded. “We will listen,” they said.

Beep put the poster on the wall. The colors glowed in the afternoon light. The stage stood strong. The treehouse felt calm and happy.

Beep’s ears purred softly. “Beep boop,” he said. “Today we learned to listen, say thank you, and make fair choices. That is a good day.”

The End.
