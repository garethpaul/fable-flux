# Splash the Dolphin's Backyard Adventure

On a bright sunny morning, Splash the dolphin wiggled and giggled in a big blue kiddie pool in Maya's backyard. The warm sun made the water sparkle like tiny diamonds, and Splash loved how it tickled his smooth gray skin.

"Good morning, Splash!" called Maya, running outside with her breakfast toast. "Want to help me clean up the backyard today?"

Splash clicked his tongue happily. "Click-click! I love helping!" But when he looked around the sunny yard, his big dark eyes grew wide. Toys were scattered everywhere! A red ball sat under the swing set, blocks were mixed with sand by the sandbox, and art supplies were spread across the picnic table.

"Oh my!" squeaked Splash. "Everything is so mixed up! Where do we even start?"

Just then, a tiny ant marched by carrying a crumb. "Excuse me," said the ant in a small voice. "I organize things one piece at a time. That's how I keep my ant hill tidy!"

Splash had never talked to an ant before. "Really? You're so small, but you know about organizing?"

The ant smiled. "Being small helps me see details! Start with one area, then move to the next."

"What a smart idea!" said Splash, splashing excitedly. "Thank you, little friend!"

Next, a colorful butterfly fluttered down. "I sort things by colors," she said, her orange and black wings shimmering. "It makes everything look pretty and easy to find!"

Splash clapped his flippers. "You organize differently than the ant, but that's wonderful too!"

A busy squirrel scampered up with cheeks full of acorns. "I put similar things together," he chattered. "All my nuts go in one place, all my leaves in another!"

"Wow!" said Splash. "Everyone has their own special way!"

Maya giggled, watching Splash learn from his new friends. "Should we try their ideas, Splash?"

"Yes! Let's be organized like our helpful friends!" Splash used his nose to gently push the red ball toward other balls. The cool, round surface felt smooth against his snout.

Following the ant's advice, they started with one small area. Maya picked up the art supplies while Splash carefully nudged crayons into a pile with his flipper.

Using the butterfly's color idea, they put all the red toys together, then the blue ones, then the yellow ones. The bright colors looked like a beautiful rainbow in the sunshine.

Like the squirrel suggested, they grouped similar things: all the blocks in one basket, all the balls in another corner, all the sandbox toys in their special bin.

"Look how organized everything is now!" cheered Maya, wiping her forehead. The backyard looked neat and tidy, with everything in its proper place.

Splash splashed joyfully in his pool. "I learned that everyone has different ways to organize, and they're all good! The ant taught me to start small, the butterfly showed me colors help, and the squirrel taught me to group things together!"

"And I learned that asking different friends for help gives us lots of great ideas!" added Maya.

The ant, butterfly, and squirrel all gathered around Splash's pool. "We're different, but we all helped!" said the ant proudly.

"Being organized makes everything more fun!" clicked Splash happily. "And making new friends who think differently makes us all smarter!"

As the warm sun continued to shine on their clean, organized backyard, Splash realized that staying organized was easier when you had good friends and tried different ways of doing things.

The End.
