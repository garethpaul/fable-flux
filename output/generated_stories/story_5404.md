# Leo the Lion Cub Speaks Up

In a cozy kitchen pantry filled with jars and boxes, lived a brave little lion cub named Leo. The pantry smelled like cinnamon and honey, with shelves that reached up, up, up to the ceiling. Leo loved exploring between the colorful cans and listening to the gentle rustle of paper bags.

Every morning, Leo watched the other animals in the house get their breakfast. The big dog got crunchy kibble that went "crunch, crunch, crunch." The fluffy cat got creamy milk that made her purr "mrow, mrow." But Leo? Leo got the same plain crackers every single day.

"I'm hungry for something different," Leo whispered to himself, his little tummy rumbling like distant thunder. But Leo was shy about asking for what he wanted. What if the other animals said no? What if they laughed at him?

One morning, Leo's tummy rumbled extra loud. "GRUMBLE, GRUMBLE!" it said, echoing through the pantry like a tiny roar.

"What was that noise?" asked Bella the bunny, hopping into the pantry with her pink nose twitching.

Leo's cheeks turned red under his golden fur. "Um... that was my tummy," he said quietly, looking down at his paws.

"Are you hungry, Leo?" Bella asked kindly, her soft ears drooping with concern.

Leo nodded slowly. "I eat crackers every day, but... but I smell so many yummy things in here. Sweet apples that smell like sunshine, crunchy carrots that look orange like my mane, and oatmeal that smells warm like hugs."

Bella's eyes grew wide. "Leo, why don't you ask for different foods?"

"I don't know how," Leo admitted, his voice small as a mouse squeak. "What if I use the wrong words? What if nobody listens?"

Bella sat down next to Leo on the pantry floor. "Being brave isn't just about being loud, Leo. Sometimes being brave means using your voice to ask for what you need. And you know so many wonderful words to describe food!"

Leo thought about this. He WAS good with words. He knew "crunchy" and "sweet" and "colorful" and "healthy."

"Will you help me practice?" Leo asked, feeling a tiny spark of courage in his chest.

"Of course!" Bella said, clapping her paws together.

Leo stood up tall, puffed out his little chest, and practiced. "Excuse me, I would like to try some healthy, crunchy carrots, please." His voice got stronger with each word.

"Perfect!" Bella cheered. "Now let's go talk to the others."

Together, they walked to the kitchen where all the animals were having breakfast. Leo's heart beat fast like a drum, but he remembered Bella's words about being brave.

"Excuse me, everyone," Leo said clearly, using his best words. "I would like to try some different, nutritious foods, please. I love words that describe yummy things, and I'd like to taste what I've been smelling."

The big dog wagged his tail. "Of course, Leo! Why didn't you ask before?"

The fluffy cat purred, "We have so many delicious, healthy options!"

Soon, Leo had a colorful plate with orange carrots that crunched, red apples that were sweet and juicy, and warm oatmeal with cinnamon that smelled like a cozy hug.

"Thank you for listening to my words," Leo said gratefully, his tummy finally happy and full.

From that day on, Leo learned that using his voice and beautiful words was the bravest thing he could do. And his breakfast was always an adventure of new, healthy flavors!

The End.
