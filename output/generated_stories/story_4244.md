# Ruby Rabbit's Rooftop Garden

Ruby Rabbit lived on the highest rooftop in the whole neighborhood. She loved her cozy home with its red brick chimney and beautiful view of the city below. Every morning, Ruby would hop out to her special rooftop garden where she grew the most delicious carrots, lettuce, and tomatoes.

"Good morning, little plants!" Ruby would say cheerfully, watering each one with her tiny green watering can. The sun felt warm on her soft brown fur, and the gentle breeze made her whiskers twitch with happiness.

One busy week, Ruby noticed that Mrs. Mouse downstairs looked very tired. Her three little mouse babies were sick, and she had no food in her cupboard.

"I must help!" said Ruby, her heart full of kindness. She gathered all her fresh vegetables and hopped down to Mrs. Mouse's apartment.

"Oh, Ruby!" said Mrs. Mouse, her eyes filling with grateful tears. "You're so generous! But are you sure you can spare all this food?"

"Of course!" replied Ruby with a big smile. "Friends help friends!"

The next day, Ruby met Mr. Squirrel, who had hurt his paw and couldn't gather nuts for winter. Without thinking twice, Ruby gave him half of her stored carrots.

"You're the most responsible neighbor ever!" chittered Mr. Squirrel happily.

Day after day, Ruby continued helping everyone. She shared her food with the hungry pigeons, gave her warm blankets to the shivering cats, and even offered her cozy burrow to a family of field mice during a rainstorm.

But after a whole week of giving everything away, Ruby felt strange. Her tummy rumbled loudly - "GRUMBLE, GRUMBLE!" She felt dizzy and weak. Her usually shiny fur looked dull, and her bright eyes seemed tired.

"Oh my," Ruby whispered, sitting down heavily on her rooftop. "I don't feel very good at all."

Ruby looked at her empty garden and bare cupboard. She had given away all her food and forgotten to save any for herself. Her paws shook, and she felt very, very hungry.

Just then, Mrs. Mouse appeared with a basket. "Ruby! I brought you some soup I made with the vegetables you gave me. You look like you need to eat!"

Mr. Squirrel hopped up too, carrying nuts. "And I saved these for you! You've been so kind to everyone."

Soon, all the animals Ruby had helped came to her rooftop, each bringing something delicious to share.

"Ruby," said wise old Mrs. Owl gently, "helping others is wonderful, but you must take care of yourself too. If you don't eat and rest, you can't help anyone at all!"

Ruby's ears perked up as she understood. "You mean I need to take care of myself first, so I can take care of others?"

"Exactly!" hooted Mrs. Owl. "It's like when you water your plants - you need to keep the watering can full to help your garden grow!"

From that day on, Ruby learned to balance helping others with taking care of herself. She made sure to eat healthy meals, get plenty of sleep, and keep some food stored away. This way, she could continue being generous and responsible while staying healthy and strong.

Ruby's rooftop garden grew bigger and more beautiful than ever, and she had plenty to share with her friends while keeping enough for herself too.

The End.
