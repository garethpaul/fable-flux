# Bolt the Robot Dog Saves the Day

In a busy basement that hummed and buzzed, a helpful robot dog named Bolt wagged his shiny tail. Tiny lights blinked on his metal ears. The air smelled a little dusty, like old books and warm laundry. Shelves were full of boxes, jars, and tools. A soft washer swished. A little fan whirred. It was a busy, busy place.

“Good morning!” Bolt beeped in a happy voice. “Beep, bark! Time to help!”

Mia and Jay, two friends with big smiles, waved. Mia had curly hair and bright blue socks. Jay wore green glasses and a red shirt. They looked different, and they liked different things, and that was great. They were a team.

“Today,” Mia said, “we clean and sort.”

“Today,” Jay added, “we save and share.”

Bolt’s tail lights twinkled. “We can do it! We can be kind to our Earth.”

They opened a box. “Oh!” said Mia. “So many things!” There were paper rolls, buttons, bottle caps, and an old sock. There were plastic spoons and bits of ribbon. The basement smelled like crayons and lemons from the soap.

“What goes where?” Jay asked.

Bolt sniffed with his sensor nose. Beep. Click. Whirr. “We can sort,” he said. “We can reuse. We can recycle. We can make less trash.”

Mia clapped. “Let’s make less trash!”

Jay nodded. “Let’s try!”

Bolt rolled to three big bins. He had made labels with smiling pictures. One bin had a tree. One had a circle with arrows. One had a heart.

“Tree is for compost,” Bolt said. “Food scraps go here. They turn into good dirt.”

“Arrows are for recycle,” he said. “Paper, cans, and some plastic go here.”

“The heart,” he said softly, “is for reuse. We save things and make new things.”

Mia put the old sock in the heart bin. “This can be a puppet!”

Jay dropped bottle caps in the heart bin too. “Wheels for a toy car.”

They worked together. Paper went plop. Caps went clink. Ribbons went swish. The sounds were cozy and busy. They hummed a little song together. Bolt’s tail lights blinked like tiny stars.

Suddenly, the washer beeped and stopped. The basement was quiet. Then they heard drip, drip, drip.

“Oh no,” Mia said. “Water!”

A little puddle was growing. Jay’s eyes grew big.

Bolt tilted his head. “We can be calm. We can try. We can fix small things first.”

He rolled to the puddle. “Safety first,” he said. “Step back, please.” He beeped a gentle beep. He looked under the hose with his camera eye.

“Loose hose,” Bolt said. “We can tighten.”

“I can hold the towel,” Mia said.

“I can turn the knob,” Jay said.

Bolt smiled with his light. “Teamwork!”

Mia pressed the towel down. It felt squishy and cold. Jay turned the knob. Squeak! The drip slowed. Bolt used his small tool paw. Twist, twist. The hose was snug.

The drip stopped. The puddle was saved. They all cheered.

Jay wiped his glasses. “We did it!”

Mia hugged Bolt. His smooth metal felt warm from his little motors. “We fixed it ourselves,” she said.

Bolt wagged. “Self-reliance,” he said. “We try, we learn, we help.”

They checked the bins again. The recycle bin was neat. The heart bin was full of good bits. The tree bin waited.

“I have apple peels,” Mia said. She dropped them in the tree bin. Plop. “They will be dirt!”

Bolt nodded. “Dirt helps plants. Plants help us breathe. That is caring for our Earth.”

Jay held up two jars. One jar had markers that still worked. The other had dry ones. “Can we save these?” he asked.

“We can,” Bolt said. “Dry markers can be dropped off to recycle. Working markers go in the art box. We use what we have first.”

Mia looked around. “We all help in our own way,” she said. “Jay is great with knobs. I am great with towels.”

“And I am great with beeps,” Bolt said, and he made a silly beep-bark. They laughed. Differences made their team strong.

“Let’s make something new,” Jay said, pointing at the heart bin.

They built a tiny garden scooper from a spoon and a cap. They made a little puppet from the sock and buttons. Mia drew eyes. Jay tied a ribbon. The puppet said, “Hello, Earth!” in a tiny voice. Bolt clapped his metal paws. Ting, ting!

“Can we water the big plant?” Mia asked, holding a small watering can.

“We can,” Bolt said. “But we use just what we need.” He showed them a strip of blue tape. “When the water reaches the tape, we stop. That saves water.”

They poured slowly. Drip, drip, sip. The plant’s leaves looked bright and happy.

The basement smelled fresh now, like soap and a little bit of apple. The bins were tidy. The floor was dry. The puppet waved. The plant smiled in green.

Mia took a deep breath. “Today was good.”

Jay nodded. “We sorted. We fixed. We saved. We made.”

Bolt’s tail glowed. “We cared for our Earth. We used less. We reused more. We learned we can try first, and ask for help if it is too big.”

Mia and Jay high-fived. “We are Earth helpers!”

Bolt wagged and said, “Every day, little choices help. Sort your trash. Use just what you need. Share and make new things. Our differences make us a great team, and our care makes Earth happy.”

They turned off the extra lights. Click. The basement grew calm and cozy. The fan purred. The plant rustled softly, like a thank-you.

“Good night, busy basement,” Bolt whispered.

“Good night,” Mia and Jay said.

The End.
