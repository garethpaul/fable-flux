# Kiki Koala’s Quiet Shed Surprise

Kiki the calm koala liked soft mornings. She breathed in. She breathed out. She listened to birds sing, “cheep-cheep.” Today, Kiki padded to the garden shed. The shed smelled like fresh wood and warm earth. Sunlight made tiny dust sparkles in the air.

“I will tidy the shed,” Kiki said gently. “A tidy space helps my heart feel calm.”

The door creaked, “cree-eeak.” Inside were pots, a watering can, a broom, and a little toolbox. Kiki’s fur brushed the cool rake handle. “Hello, tools,” she whispered. “Let’s make this a cozy, safe place.”

Kiki picked up a pot. Clink! She stacked it carefully. She swept the floor—swish, swish. She hummed a slow tune. “Mmm-mmm-mmm.” The tune felt like a hug.

Suddenly, a soft rustle came from behind a bag of seeds. Rustle, rustle. Kiki’s ears perked up. Her tummy felt wiggly. “Oh,” she said, placing a paw on her belly. “I feel a little scared.”

Kiki remembered her wellness plan. “When I have big feelings, I can breathe, name the feeling, and speak kindly.” She put her paw on her heart. “Breathe in… one, two, three. Breathe out… one, two, three.”

“Hello?” Kiki called with a calm voice. “I hear you. I am Kiki. I use peaceful words. Are you okay?”

A tiny voice squeaked, “I’m… I’m stuck.”

Kiki moved slowly. She peeked behind the seed bag. A small green lizard with bright, curious eyes looked up. His tail was looped around a thin string.

“My name is Lolo,” the lizard whispered. “I tried to explore. Now I can’t move.”

“It’s okay, Lolo,” Kiki said. “You are safe with me. Let’s solve this together.”

Kiki took another deep breath. “We will be brave. Brave can be gentle.”

She sat on the soft mat by the door so sunlight warmed her paws. “Lolo, can we try a calm breath together?”

Lolo blinked. “O-okay.”

Kiki showed him. “Smell the flower.” They breathed in slowly. “Blow the bubble.” They breathed out softly. Lolo’s tiny chest rose and fell. His tail twitched less.

“Better,” Lolo said, a little smile growing.

“Now I will untie the string,” Kiki said. “If you feel worried, say ‘stop’ and we will pause.”

“That sounds kind,” Lolo said.

Kiki gently lifted the string. “This string is thin like a noodle,” she said, to keep things light. Snip! She used a little child-safe scissors from the toolbox. The string fell away.

“I’m free!” Lolo chirped, wiggling his toes. He stretched in the warm sun. “Thank you, Kiki.”

“You’re welcome,” Kiki said. “Let’s make the shed safer so this doesn’t happen again.”

They looked around. Kiki noticed a bucket on a wobbly shelf. “Hmm. This could fall. Let’s put it low.” Clunk, the bucket settled on the floor. Lolo found a loose nail on a bench. “This is pokey,” he said. Kiki put it in a small jar. Tap, tap, lid on tight.

They made a “Cozy Corner” with a soft towel and a tiny leaf pillow. Kiki placed a small sign, drawn with a crayon: Quiet Rest Spot. Lolo touched the towel. “It’s soft like a cloud,” he said.

“Let’s have a wellness break,” Kiki said. She poured cool water from the watering can into a cup. “Sip, sip.” The water tasted fresh. Kiki shared a crunchy apple slice she had in her little pouch. Crunch! “Thank you,” Lolo said, licking a tiny drop from his chin.

“Now we check our feelings,” Kiki said. “How does your body feel?”

Lolo closed his eyes. “My tummy feels warm. My tail feels calm.”

“My body feels steady,” Kiki said. “My heart feels cozy.”

They heard a buzzing sound. Bzzz. A bee floated near the open window, bumping the screen softly. Bzz-bzz.

“Oh dear,” said Lolo. “Is the bee stuck?”

Kiki nodded. “We can help, but we will be gentle.” She spoke to the bee in a peaceful voice. “Hello, Bee. We will open the window wider.”

Kiki slid the latch. Click. She slowly pushed. Whoosh! Fresh air whooshed in, smelling like mint and soil. The bee zipped out to the flowers. Bzz-zz-zoom!

“We did it,” Lolo cheered quietly.

Kiki smiled. “Wellness is caring for our space, our bodies, and our feelings. We breathe. We speak kindly. We help safely.”

Lolo stood taller. “I was scared, but I learned to breathe and ask for help. That was brave.”

“Yes,” said Kiki. “Bravery can be soft. Learning can be calm.”

They finished tidying. The shed looked neat and friendly. Sunlight made warm stripes on the floor. Kiki and Lolo sat in the Cozy Corner. They listened to birds, to the wind in the leaves, and to their own slow breaths.

“Thank you for teaching me,” Lolo said.

“We taught each other,” Kiki replied. “That is peaceful communication.”

Kiki closed her eyes for a moment. In, two, three. Out, two, three. Her heart felt light.

When it was time to go, Lolo waved. “See you again, friend!”

“See you, Lolo,” Kiki said. She patted the door. “Good job, shed.”

As Kiki walked home, she whispered her lesson. “When feelings are big, I can breathe, use kind words, and make my space safe. That is wellness.”

The End.
