# Bibi the Singing Bird Solves a Sudsy Mystery

Bibi was a small singing bird with bright blue feathers and a sweet, clear voice. One sunny morning, she fluttered into the warm, whirring laundry room. The air smelled like lemons and soap. The washing machine hummed, “Whummm,” and the dryer tumbled, “Thum-thum-thum.” A soft pile of towels looked like white clouds.

Bibi loved to sing in the laundry room. “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo,” she sang, hopping from the basket to the shelf. She felt cozy, safe, and happy.

Then—splish! A bubble popped on Bibi’s beak. “Oh!” she chirped. “Where did that come from?”

More bubbles floated up, shiny like rainbows. “Pop, pop, pop!” They drifted up from the floor and around the machine.

Bibi blinked. “Hmm. Something is not right,” she said softly. “I will use my thinking beak.” She tapped her beak gently with her wing. Tap, tap.

Bibi remembered what her grandma bird always said: “When there is a problem, stop, look, think, and try.” Bibi took a deep breath. The room smelled like flowers and fresh rain.

“First, I will stop,” said Bibi. She stopped flapping and listened. The washer said, “Whummm.” The bubbles said, “Plip-plop.” The floor made a little “slosh” sound.

“Next, I will look,” said Bibi. She looked at the machine. She looked at the floor. She looked at the shelf with bottles. One bottle had a big cap, and it was open. Bibi read the label slowly. “Soap. Only a little.” She nodded. “Only a little. Hmm.”

“Then, I will think,” said Bibi. She put her head to the side. “If the soap bottle is open, maybe too much soap went in. If there is too much soap, there will be too many bubbles. If there are too many bubbles, the bubbles will spill out.”

She felt proud. “I have a clue!” she chirped. “But I will check.”

Bibi flew down low to see the small soap trail by the machine. Drip, drip, drip. She touched a drop with the tip of her wing. “Slippery,” she said. “It smells like the open bottle. My idea might be right.”

“Hello?” Bibi called. “Is anyone here?” Her voice echoed gently in the warm room.

From the laundry basket, a tiny voice answered, “I am here!” It was Tessa, a little stuffed turtle with soft green fabric. She had been waiting to be washed and was peeking out.

“Hi, Tessa,” said Bibi. “We have a sudsy problem.”

Tessa wiggled. “Oh no! What can we do?”

“We can use peaceful thinking,” said Bibi. “We can work together. We can be careful and kind. And we can fix it without fuss.”

Tessa nodded. “Yes! What is the plan?”

Bibi sang softly, “Stop, look, think, and try.” Then she said, “First, we will make the room safe. Bubbles on the floor are slippery. Tessa, please stay in the basket where it is dry.”

“Okay,” said Tessa.

“Next,” said Bibi, “I will find a towel to put on the floor to soak up the extra bubbles.” She tugged at a towel with her little beak and pulled it down gently—foof! It landed with a soft “whump.” She pushed it over the suds with her wings. The towel drank up the bubbles. Sssslurp.

“Now the floor is safer,” Bibi said. “Then, we need to turn off the machine. But I am a small bird. I cannot turn the knob.”

Tessa thought. “We can call for help,” she said.

“That is a smart idea,” said Bibi. “Asking for help is part of thinking.”

Bibi flew to the open window and sang a clear, bright call. “Tweet-tee-tee! Help, please!”

Footsteps came. Mama walked in, smiling. “Hello, little helpers. What is going on in here?”

Bibi pointed with her wing. “There are too many bubbles. The soap bottle was open. I think too much soap went in.”

Mama looked. She smiled kindly. “You are right, Bibi. You used good clues. Thank you for telling me.”

Bibi’s heart felt warm. “We used stop, look, think, and try,” she said.

Mama turned off the washer. The humming stopped. “Whummm” became quiet. Together, Bibi, Tessa, and Mama spread more towels. They squeezed them in a bucket. Squeeze, squish, splash. The room started to feel dry and calm.

Mama wiped the soap bottle and clicked the cap shut. Click. “Only a little soap,” Mama said. “This much.” She measured with two fingers. “Thank you for using your brain and your voice in a peaceful way.”

Bibi sang a small happy song. “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo, we found the clue and knew what to do!”

Tessa clapped her soft little flippers. “We solved the sudsy mystery!”

Bibi fluttered to the shelf and looked around the room. The towels were folded like puffy clouds again. The air smelled fresh, not too soapy. The dryer hummed softly, “Thum. Thum.”

Bibi turned to Tessa and Mama. “Today we learned to use careful thinking. We looked for clues. We made a plan. We asked for help. We were gentle and safe. That is what good neighbors do all over the world.”

Mama nodded. “That is global citizenship—caring for our home and each other.”

Bibi puffed her feathers with pride. “When there is a problem,” she sang, “we can stop, look, think, and try. We can solve it in a peaceful way.”

She hopped to the window and felt a tiny breeze. It felt like a new song. Bibi smiled. “I will remember this.”

“Me too,” said Tessa, snug in the basket.

Bibi sang one more cheerful line, and the laundry room felt bright and calm again.

The End.
