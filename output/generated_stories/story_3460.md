# Penny Penguin’s Sunny Backyard Parade

Penny was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a bright red bow. One warm morning, she waddled into a sunny backyard. The grass was soft and tickly on her feet. The sun felt cozy on her feathers. Birds chirped, “Tweet, tweet!” A sweet breeze smelled like oranges and clean soap.

“Hello, sunny day!” Penny giggled. “Let’s play parade!”

Her friends were there too. Felix the frog wore a tiny hat. Bella the bunny had long, floppy ears. Max the marmot rolled a drum. A small snail named Nori peeked from a shiny shell.

Penny clapped her flippers. “We can make a parade! Tap, tap, tap! We can march and sing!”

“I can drum,” Max said, boom-boom-boom.

“I can hop,” Bella said, hop-hop-hop.

“I can ribbit music,” Felix said, ribbit-ribbit.

“I can glide,” Penny said, waddle-glide-waddle.

Nori peeked out and whispered, “I am slow. I am quiet.” The snail’s little eyes looked worried.

Penny smiled. “That’s okay, Nori. We will find a place for you too.”

They lined up on the warm patio. The sun made the sidewalk glow. Penny raised a shiny spoon like a baton. “Ready, friends?”

“Ready!” everyone cheered.

Penny called, “One, two, three—PARADE!”

Max drummed fast. Boom-boom-boom! Felix ribbited loud. RIBBIT-RIBBIT! Bella hopped high. Hop! Hop! Hop! Penny waddled and sang, “La-la-la!”

But soon, everything felt mixed up. The drum was too fast. The hops were too high. The ribbits were too loud. Nori tried to follow, but the line zoomed around the mulberry bush, swish-swish-swish, and Nori fell far behind.

“Wait!” Nori called in a tiny voice. “I can’t keep up.”

Penny stopped. Her smile got small. She took a deep breath and listened. The backyard sounded busy: drum-booms, hop-thumps, and big ribbits. She looked at Nori’s shy eyes and slow trail. Penny felt a tug in her heart.

“I want our parade to be for everyone,” she said softly. “But how?”

Felix puffed up. “We need big sounds!”

Max tapped his drum. “I practiced fast beats!”

Bella’s ears wiggled. “I like high hops!”

Penny nodded. “Those are good. You practiced. But we also need to be kind. We can practice being kind too.”

Felix blinked. “Practice… being kind?”

Penny nodded again. “Yes. We can practice tolerance. That means we make room for each friend. We listen. We go slow when someone needs slow. We try new ways together.”

Bella’s ears perked. “We can try again.”

Penny smiled wide. “Let’s make a plan. We can make parts!”

She drew lines in the warm dirt with her spoon. “Max, can you drum a steady beat? Boom… boom… boom. Not too fast.”

Max tried. “Boom… boom… boom.” The sound felt like a soft heartbeat. The drum tickled his paws. “I can do it!”

“Felix,” Penny said, “can you ribbit soft and then loud on the beat? Ribbit… RIBBIT… ribbit… RIBBIT…”

Felix practiced. “Ribbit… RIBBIT…” He giggled. “That’s fun!”

“Bella,” Penny said, “can you hop low and gentle so your feet land on the beat? Hop… hop…”

Bella tried small hops. Her feet made tiny pats. “I can!”

“Nori,” Penny said kindly, “you can be our flag leader. You can hold this leaf flag. We will follow your pace. We will watch your little wiggles. You show the way.”

Nori’s eyes shined. “Me? The leader?” Nori held a green leaf on a twig. It smelled fresh and cool. The snail’s smile grew brave. “I can try.”

Penny clapped. “We can try together!”

They took their places. The sun warmed their backs. The grass brushed their toes. Penny lifted the spoon again. “On Nori’s wiggle… go!”

Nori slid forward slowly. Swish… slide… swish. Max drummed a soft beat. Boom… boom… boom. Felix ribbited soft and then loud. Ribbit… RIBBIT… ribbit… RIBBIT. Bella hopped gently. Hop… hop. Penny waddled and sang, “La… la… la…” in a calm, happy voice.

The parade moved around the lilac bush. It smelled like purple sugar. The leaves made tiny claps in the wind. The friends smiled. They were together. They were steady. They were kind.

Neighbors peeked over the fence. “Hooray!” they cheered. “What a lovely parade!”

Penny saw Nori’s proud eyes. She felt warm and fizzy inside. “This is working,” she whispered.

Then a little bump came—the garden hose lay across the grass like a green snake. Nori paused. “This is hard.”

Penny looked at her friends. “Cooperation time,” she said. “We can help.”

Bella lowered her soft ears like a ramp. “Nori can slide here.”

Max held the drum steady. “I will keep the beat slow.”

Felix hummed a calm ribbit. “You can do it, Nori.”

Penny stood close and sang, “Slow and steady, we can go.”

Nori slid up Bella’s ear, over the hose, and down the other side. Plop! The leaf flag waved. Everyone cheered. “We did it together!”

They reached the end of the yard by the sunny birdbath. The parade stopped in a circle. The water sparkled and went drip… drip… drip. Penny bowed. “Thank you, friends. We practiced our parts. We cooperated. We showed tolerance. We made room for everyone.”

Nori beamed. “I felt safe. Thank you for waiting for me.”

Felix smiled. “I liked the soft ribbits.”

Max nodded. “I can drum steady now.”

Bella wiggled. “Gentle hops are fun!”

Penny’s eyes shone. “When we practice and work together, we all belong. When we are tolerant and kind, every friend can shine.”

The friends hugged. The sun felt like a warm blanket. The backyard smelled like grass and sweet flowers. Their hearts felt big and light.

Penny waved her spoon. “Same time tomorrow?”

“Tomorrow!” they all cheered.

The End.
