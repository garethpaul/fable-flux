# Benny Beaver's Calm-Down Plan

Benny was a busy beaver with big front teeth and a bright orange tail. Today, Benny had a helpful job. He was in the guest room, getting it ready for Grandma. The guest room was quiet and sunny. The curtains felt soft like a hug. The blanket on the bed smelled like clean soap. Benny patted the pillows. Poof! A little dust cloud floated up and made him sneeze. “A-choo!”

“I want everything to be just right,” Benny said. He liked to be a leader. He liked to make a plan. On his little notepad, he wrote, “Plan: 1. Make the bed. 2. Set the lamp. 3. Put out snacks. 4. Welcome Grandma.”

Just then, Benny’s friend, Pip the chipmunk, scurried in with busy feet. “I can help!” Pip squeaked. “I can put the lamp here!”

Pip placed the lamp on the wobbly table. The lamp leaned, then tipped. Clink! The lamp fell over and the bulb rolled under the bed.

Benny felt his face get hot. His ears felt tight. His tummy felt twisty. He wanted to shout, “Pip! No!” His paws curled into little fists.

Benny stopped. He remembered his calm-down plan. He had made it on another day, when blocks fell down. He whispered, “When I’m mad, I stop. I breathe. I count. I use kind words.”

Benny put his paws on his belly and took a big breath in. He felt his belly grow like a bubble. He let the breath out slowly. Whooo. He counted, “One, two, three.” His body felt softer. His voice felt gentle again.

Pip peeked up at Benny. “I’m sorry,” Pip said. “I wanted to help.”

Benny nodded. “Thank you for wanting to help, Pip,” he said in a calm voice. “I feel upset. The lamp fell. I need help to fix it.”

Pip’s whiskers twitched. “We can fix it together,” he said.

Benny smiled a small smile. “Yes. Let’s use a leader plan. I will share the jobs. You can be the lamp helper. I will be the bed helper.”

They knelt on the soft rug. It felt fuzzy under their knees. Together they looked under the bed. It smelled dusty like old books. “I see it,” Benny said. The bulb was round and shiny like a pearl. Pip slid it out with a gentle paw. “Got it!” Pip cheered.

“Thank you,” said Benny. “Now, let’s make it safe. We will use the steady table by the window. It is flat and strong.” Benny lifted the lamp with careful hands. Pip held the cord straight. Click. The lamp stood tall and did not wobble.

Benny took another breath. “Better,” he whispered. His cheeks were cool now. He felt proud.

“Can I fluff the pillows?” Pip asked.

“Yes,” said Benny, “and I will show you how.” He picked up a pillow and patted it softly. “Like this. Gentle pats. Poof, poof.” The pillow smelled like lavender. Pip copied him. “Poof, poof,” Pip said, giggling.

They worked together. Benny led with clear words. “Next, we place the snack plate.” He set out crunchy apple slices and tiny crackers. Crunch, crunch, he tasted one. “Yum,” he said.

Pip nodded. “Yum,” he echoed.

They heard footsteps in the hall. Tap, tap, tap. “Grandma is coming,” Benny whispered.

Suddenly, the blanket snagged on a button and pulled the neat bed into a lump. “Oh no!” Pip squeaked, eyes wide.

Benny felt heat in his face again. His paws wanted to tug fast, but he remembered. Stop. Breathe. Count. He touched the blanket, closed his eyes, and breathed in and out. “One, two, three,” he counted softly. He opened his eyes. “It’s okay,” he said. “We can fix it. Pip, please hold the corner. I will smooth the middle.”

Pip held the corner tight. Benny smoothed the blanket with long, slow strokes. Swish, swish. The bed looked neat again.

Grandma opened the door. “Oh my,” she said, smiling. “The guest room is lovely! It smells fresh, and the lamp is just right.”

Benny’s tail thumped gently on the floor. Thump, thump. “We worked together,” he said. “I got upset, but I used my calm-down plan. I used kind words. I led the jobs so we could fix the problems.”

Pip nodded. “Benny was a good leader,” he said. “He breathed and counted and asked for help.”

Grandma hugged them both. Her hug felt warm like a blanket. “That is wonderful,” she said. “Leaders use calm hearts. When trouble comes, they stop, breathe, and speak kindly. That helps everyone feel safe.”

Benny looked around the cozy guest room. The curtains were smooth, the bed was neat, the lamp glowed softly, and the snack plate looked tasty. He felt proud in his chest, like a little sun.

He turned to Pip. “Thank you for helping,” Benny said. “If we make a mistake again, we will stop, breathe, and fix it together.”

Pip smiled. “Yes! Stop, breathe, and fix. We can do it.”

Benny nodded. “That is our team rule,” he said. “That is our calm-down plan.”

They held out their paws and tapped them together. Tap! Then they sat on the soft rug and shared a cracker, listening to the quiet hum of the lamp and the gentle rustle of the curtains.

Grandma laughed softly. “You two are ready for any adventure,” she said.

Benny whispered, “With calm hearts, we lead the way.”

The End.
