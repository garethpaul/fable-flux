# Polly's Helpful Wings

Polly the parrot loved her bright red and green feathers. She lived with the Johnson family in their cozy house, and her favorite room was the big dining room with its shiny wooden table and sunny windows.

Every morning, the dining room became very messy after breakfast. Crumbs scattered across the floor like tiny pebbles, sticky jam spots dotted the table, and napkins lay crumpled everywhere. Polly watched from her perch as Mom and Dad cleaned up, looking tired and rushed.

"I wish I could help," Polly whispered to herself, fluffing her colorful feathers sadly. "But I'm just a little bird. What can I do?"

One Tuesday morning, Polly noticed something terrible. Mom had hurt her back and couldn't bend down to pick up the fallen toast pieces. Dad was running late for work, and little Emma was too small to reach the high places on the table.

"Oh dear," sighed Mom, holding her back. "This mess will have to wait."

Polly's heart felt heavy. Her family needed help, but she felt too small and unsure. Then she remembered what Grandma Johnson always said: "Everyone has special gifts, no matter how small they seem."

"Maybe... maybe I DO have special gifts!" Polly chirped excitedly.

She spread her beautiful wings wide and flew down to the floor. With her sharp, curved beak, she carefully picked up toast crumbs one by one, dropping them into the trash can. Her small size helped her reach under the chairs where the big vacuum couldn't go.

"Look, Mommy!" squealed Emma, clapping her hands. "Polly is helping!"

Next, Polly discovered she could use her strong feet to grab napkins and carry them to the laundry basket. Her wings could even dust the lower shelves of the china cabinet by flapping gently.

"Polly, you're amazing!" said Dad, smiling proudly. "You found the perfect jobs for your size!"

But then Polly realized something important. She was doing ALL the work by herself, and that wasn't fair to anyone.

"Wait!" she squawked thoughtfully. "We should work together!"

Polly had a wonderful idea. She suggested they take turns with different chores. Dad could wipe the high spots on the table, Mom could sort the dishes from her chair, Emma could put the salt and pepper shakers away, and Polly would handle all the floor crumbs and low dusting.

"That's perfect!" said Mom, her eyes twinkling. "We each do what we do best!"

From that day forward, the Johnson family had the cleanest dining room on the whole street. Polly felt proud and confident as she zoomed around, picking up crumbs with her clever beak. She learned that being small didn't mean being helpless – it meant being special in her own unique way.

The neighbors even started asking for Polly's help with their own dining room cleanups. She became the neighborhood's official "Crumb Captain," and she wore her title with pride, her red and green feathers gleaming in the sunlight.

Every evening, as the family sat around their spotless dining table for dinner, Polly perched happily on her special chair. She had learned that everyone, no matter how small, has important gifts to share. By working together and taking turns, they made their home a happier place for everyone.

"Thank you, Polly," the family would say each night. And Polly would puff up her feathers with confidence, knowing she had found her special way to help.

The End.
