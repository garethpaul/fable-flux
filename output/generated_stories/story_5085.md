# Sunny Songs on the Porch

Every morning, a singing bird named Sunny landed on the peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like fresh soap and warm wood. The sun kissed the white railing. The wind made the chimes sing, “Ting, ting, ting.”

“Good morning!” Sunny chirped. “Tweet-a-dee! Time to sing and help!”

Sunny’s friend, Grandma June, opened the door with a smile. She wore a blue apron and soft slippers that went shuff, shuff on the porch. “Hello, Sunny,” she said. “Today is porch-cleaning day. We have our chores.”

Sunny puffed up with pride. “I can help! I can sing and sweep!” His tiny heart felt happy. Helping felt good.

Grandma June nodded. “Our routine helps us keep the porch safe and nice. First, we tidy toys. Then we water the flowers. Then we sweep.”

Sunny sang, “Tidy, water, sweep! Tidy, water, sweep!”

They began. The porch held a small basket of toys: a red ball, a wooden truck, and a little doll with yarn hair. Sunny used his beak to nudge the ball toward the basket. “Roll, roll,” he chirped. The ball bumped the basket with a soft thump.

Grandma June clapped. “Well done, Sunny.”

Sunny felt the warm sun on his feathers. He smelled the sweet mint from a clay pot. He wanted to sing high and loud. “Tweet-tee-tee!” he trilled, hopping and flapping.

But while he hopped, his wing brushed the doll. The doll fell. “Oh!” The little doll bounced down the porch steps and landed in a tiny mud puddle.

Sunny froze. His tummy felt tight. He looked at Grandma June. “I’m sorry,” he whispered. His voice was small, like a tiny bell.

Grandma June knelt and touched Sunny’s head. “Thank you for your honest words,” she said. “We all make mistakes. When we say sorry, we also fix the problem. That is part of our chore routine too.”

Sunny breathed in the fresh air and let it out slow. “I can fix it,” he said. “What can I do?”

Grandma June smiled. “Good plan. Let’s rinse the doll with warm water, then pat it dry. You can help carry the towel.”

Sunny lifted a corner of the soft towel in his beak. It tasted like sunshine and cotton. Together, they rinsed the doll and patted it, pat-pat-pat, until it was clean. Sunny hummed a gentle tune. “Sorry and fix, sorry and fix,” he sang.

When the doll was safe in the basket, Sunny felt lighter, like a leaf in the breeze.

Now it was time to water the flowers. The leaves were cool and shiny. The soil was dark and smelled rich, like rain. Grandma June poured water from a little can. Swish, swish. Sunny flew from pot to pot, checking each plant. “This one looks thirsty,” he chirped. “And this one is happy!”

Grandma June laughed. “Thank you, Sunny. You are a careful helper.”

Last, it was time to sweep the porch. Sunny loved the sound of the broom. Swish-swish, shuff-shuff. He hopped along, singing his sweeping song. “Sweep the steps, sweep the floor. Helping makes our porch adore!”

Sunny felt fast and excited. He wanted to show how well he could sweep. He darted close to the broom. “Watch me!” he chirped.

But oops! The red ball rolled out of the basket again. It bumped Sunny. He tumbled into a little pile of petals. Pink petals flew up like tiny butterflies.

Sunny blinked. He felt a blush under his feathers. He looked at the mess. He looked at Grandma June. He took a deep breath. “I’m sorry,” he said. “I moved too fast.”

Grandma June nodded kindly. “Thank you for saying sorry. Remember our routine: slow and steady, one chore at a time.”

Sunny nodded. “Slow and steady.” He fluffed his wings, then moved carefully. He nudged the ball back into the basket, then helped gather the petals. Plink, plink, plink, the petals went into the little bin. The porch began to look neat again.

When they were done, the porch shone in the sunlight. The chimes sang gently. The flowers looked bright and proud. Grandma June poured lemonade into a cup that smelled like lemons and sugar. She set a tiny saucer of water for Sunny.

“To good chores,” Grandma June said.

“To a good routine,” Sunny sang. He took a sip of water. It was cool and sweet.

Grandma June sat in her rocking chair. It creaked softly. Sunny rested on the warm railing. “I learned today,” Sunny said. “Chores help us care. Routine helps us remember. And when I make a mess, I say sorry and fix it.”

Grandma June smiled. “That is a big lesson, little friend.”

Sunny lifted his head and sang a bright, happy song. “Tidy, water, sweep! Slow and steady, we keep! Say sorry, make it right. That makes our hearts feel light!”

The porch felt peaceful and clean. The air smelled fresh. Sunny’s song floated into the morning sky, and the day felt just right.

The End.
