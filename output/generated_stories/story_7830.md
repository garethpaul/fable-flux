# Kiki the Calm Koala’s Guest Room Adventure

Kiki the calm koala loved quiet places. One sunny afternoon, Kiki tiptoed into the guest room. The door made a soft click. The air smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. Sunlight made golden squares on the carpet, warm and cozy. Kiki felt safe.

“Oh,” Kiki whispered, “this room looks sleepy.” She smiled and patted the bedspread. It was soft, like a cloud. A big blue suitcase sat by the chair. A friendly lamp watched from the table. The curtains swished gently in the breeze.

Kiki’s round ears perked up. “Let’s play imagination,” she said. “We can go on a pretend trip, right here!” She took a deep breath and felt calm and brave.

First, Kiki checked for safety. “Doors? Clear. Floor? No small bits.” She looked around carefully. She moved shoes to the corner and tucked the suitcase away from the walkway. “Safe path for tiny toes,” she said with a nod. “When we use imagination, we also use care.”

Kiki made the bed smooth with her little paws. “This bed is a boat,” she whispered. “A gentle boat on a glittery lake.” She sat on the edge. “Boat, may I come aboard?” Kiki asked politely.

The bed seemed to say, “Yes, welcome.” The sheets rustled like water. The blanket felt cool on her paws.

“Life jacket,” Kiki said. She used a rolled towel as a pretend safety vest. “Click!” She made the buckle sound. “Even in pretend play, we remember safety.”

A tiny button on the lamp looked like a boat bell. “Ding-ding!” Kiki pressed it, but only once and very softly. “Ring for help,” she said. “We ask for help when we need it.”

Kiki held a folded washcloth like a map. “Where shall we go?” she asked the room. The curtains whispered, “Shhhh, the Windy Islands.” The blue suitcase hummed, “The Shelly Shore.” Kiki tapped her chin. “Let’s discover both!”

“Boat, push off!” she said. She rocked very gently, so the bed stayed safe. “Splash, splash,” she whispered.

Suddenly Kiki saw something under the chair—a shiny button had rolled away. It glowed like a tiny moon. “Discovery!” she said. “But I must be careful.”

She remembered a rule: Don’t put small things near your mouth. Don’t touch things that don’t belong to you. Ask a grown-up. Kiki sat still and breathed in, then out. “I will not touch,” she said calmly. “I will tell a grown-up later.”

Kiki used her imagination again. The button wasn’t a button now; it was the Moon Pebble on Moon Island. “Hello, Moon Pebble,” she waved. “I can look with my eyes.” She felt proud. “I made a safe choice.”

The boat drifted to the Windy Islands. Kiki puffed her cheeks. “Whoooosh,” she made wind sounds. She used a pillow as a friendly sail. “Sail, may I hold you?” she asked. The pillow didn’t mind. “We will be gentle,” Kiki promised.

“Land ho!” she giggled. The carpet was warm and tickly when she stepped down. “Soft sand,” she said, wiggling her toes. She smelled the lemon soap and pretended it was lemon trees. “Mmm, zesty!”

On the “island” she found a basket with guest towels. “Treasures!” Kiki said. She didn’t open the basket. “This is for guests,” she reminded herself. “I can imagine inside.” She closed her eyes. “I imagine seashells, shaped like tiny ears and tiny moons.”

A little creak came from the hallway. Kiki paused and listened. “Listening keeps me safe,” she said. “Is someone there?” The house was quiet again. She felt calm. “It’s okay,” she told herself. “I am not alone. The house is here. The light is here. I am safe.”

Kiki wanted a flag for her island. She looked around. “I can be resourceful,” she said. She found a soft scarf draped over the chair and a paper straw from yesterday’s snack. “May I use these?” she asked the room. “I will be gentle and put them back.”

She slid the scarf through the straw and made a tiny, kind flag. “Flag of Gentle Play!” she cheered. She stuck it in a slipper so nothing got sticky or pokey. “Safe flag,” she said.

Kiki practiced asking for help. “Help, please!” she said to the lamp. Then she practiced giving help. “Thank you for asking,” she answered back with a smile. “Let’s do things safely together.”

Soon, the sunbeam moved across the floor like a sleepy cat. Kiki yawned. Her tummy made a tiny rumble. “Snack time soon,” she said.

Before she left, Kiki cleaned up. She folded the scarf, placed the straw in the bin, smoothed the bed-boat, and put the pillow-sail back. She looked at the Moon Pebble under the chair and remembered. “I will tell a grown-up.”

She stepped into the hall and called, “Grown-up, please help! There is a shiny button in the guest room.” A grown-up came and smiled. “Thank you for telling me, Kiki. That was safe and smart.” The grown-up picked up the button and put it away.

Kiki felt warm inside, like a happy sunbeam. “Today I used imagination,” she said. “I discovered new ideas. I stayed safe. I was resourceful.”

The grown-up hugged Kiki. “You can go anywhere with your mind, little koala—right from a cozy room.”

Kiki nodded. “My mind is my map. My calm is my compass. My kindness is my flag.” She patted the bed-boat. “Thank you, guest room. We had a gentle adventure.”

The lesson was clear: With imagination, we can discover big, bright ideas; with safety and care, we can enjoy them; and with resourcefulness, we can solve little problems—all from a quiet, cozy place.

The End.
