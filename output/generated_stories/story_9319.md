# Rover's Bathroom Helper Adventure

Rover was a special robot dog with shiny silver fur and bright blue eyes that lit up when he was happy. His favorite job was helping children in the bathroom at Little Stars Daycare. He loved the way the warm water sounded when it ran from the faucet - *splash, splash, splash* - and how the soap bubbles smelled like fresh flowers.

One busy morning, three children came into the bathroom all at once. Emma bounced on her toes, Tommy drummed his fingers on the sink, and little Maya stood quietly by the door.

"Me first! Me first!" shouted Emma, rushing toward the sink. "I'm the fastest!"

"No way!" called Tommy, pushing past her. "I was here first!"

Rover's blue eyes flickered with concern. He could see that Maya looked sad and left out. His gentle robot voice made a soft *beep-beep* sound.

"Wait, friends," Rover said kindly. "I know everyone wants to wash their hands, but we need to be patient and take turns."

"But I don't want to wait!" Emma stamped her foot. "Waiting is boring!"

Rover's tail wagged slowly. "I understand waiting feels hard sometimes. But being patient helps everyone feel happy and safe. Would you like to learn my special bathroom song while we wait?"

Tommy crossed his arms. "Songs are silly."

"Yeah," agreed Emma. "And Maya is too little anyway. She takes forever!"

Rover noticed Maya's eyes fill with tears. His heart-shaped chest panel glowed warm and red. "Everyone learns at their own speed, and that's perfectly okay. Maya tries very hard, just like you do."

Rover began to sing in his cheerful robot voice: "🎵 Wash your hands, one-two-three, take your time, patient as can be! 🎵"

Maya giggled softly at the funny song. Even Tommy's mouth twitched into a tiny smile.

"Let's practice being patient together," Rover suggested. "Emma, you can go first, but let's count slowly to ten while you wash. Tommy, you can be my counting helper. And Maya, you can watch and learn!"

Emma stepped up to the sink. As the warm water flowed over her hands, Rover and Tommy counted together: "One... two... three..." The counting made the time pass quickly and felt like a fun game.

"Four... five... six..." Emma scrubbed her hands with the sudsy soap, making lots of bubbles.

"Seven... eight... nine... ten!" they all said together.

"That was actually fun!" Emma said, surprised. "Your turn, Tommy!"

Tommy washed his hands while Emma and Maya helped count. Then it was Maya's turn. She moved slowly and carefully, and Rover praised her for doing such a good job.

"You know what?" said Tommy. "Maya might be little, but she makes the best bubbles!"

"And she's really good at counting!" added Emma.

Rover's blue eyes sparkled with joy. "See how wonderful it is when we're patient with each other? Everyone got clean hands, everyone felt included, and we all had fun!"

The three children looked at their clean, sparkly hands and then at each other.

"Thanks, Rover," said Maya in her small voice. "You helped us be good friends."

"Being patient isn't so hard when we do it together," Emma realized.

"And everyone gets a turn when we're fair," added Tommy.

Rover's tail wagged happily as the children skipped out of the bathroom, chattering about their next adventure. His chest panel glowed bright with pride. He had helped them learn that patience, practice, and fairness made everything better for everyone.

The End.
