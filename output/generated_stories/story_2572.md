# Koala Kori’s Pillow Parade

Kori the calm koala woke up in a cozy bedroom filled with soft things. There were fluffy pillows, a rainbow blanket, a basket of tiny drums, and a small paper fan with bright red dots. Morning light made dappled spots on the rug. The room smelled like clean cotton and warm tea.

Kori stretched slowly. “Good morning, room,” Kori said, patting the pillow. “Today I want to practice coordination. That means working together—my eyes, my ears, my hands, and my feet!”

Kori sat up and listened. The tick-tock clock went click… tick… tock. A gentle breeze whispered through the window. Kori smiled. “I can use sounds and steps. I can count and move.”

Kori set four pillows in a line on the rug. “This is my Pillow Parade,” Kori said. “Step, step, step.” Kori tapped a little drum. “Tap-tap!” The drum sounded like a happy raindrop.

Kori took a breath. “Eyes watch the pillow. Ears hear the drum. Feet step soft. Hands hold steady.” Kori stepped on the first pillow. Squish! It felt cool and bouncy. Kori giggled. “Squish is friendly.”

Step two. The second pillow was silky. “Oh! So smooth,” Kori said. Tap-tap. Kori matched the drum beats with the steps. “One step, two step. I’m in tune!”

On the third pillow, Kori wobbled. “Oh!” Kori’s arms went out wide like tree branches. “I am calm. I am steady.” Kori looked at the pillow, listened to the drum, and took a slow breath. The wobble stopped. Step three, done!

Kori grinned. “Coordination means parts working together.” Kori practiced again, this time counting out loud. “One… two… three… four!” The fourth pillow poofed like a little cloud.

After the Pillow Parade, Kori had an idea. “Innovation time,” Kori said softly. “Let’s make a Rainbow Rhythm Ribbon.” Kori looked around the cozy bedroom. Kori found a long shoelace, a small bell, and the paper fan with red dots. Kori tied the bell to one end of the lace and tucked the fan into a pocket on Kori’s vest.

“Ring-ring!” The bell sounded like tiny stars. Kori swished the lace in the air. “Whoosh!” Kori laughed. “Now my hands can swish, my ears can hear, and my eyes can look. We will move together.”

Kori stood tall on the rug. “Ready, Ribbon?” Kori whispered. Swish, ring, step! Kori twirled the ribbon slowly while stepping across the pillows. Kori matched the ring of the bell to the tick of the clock. Ring… tick… ring… tick. “I can do it,” Kori said, calm and brave.

Kori looked at the paper fan. “This fan is from Grandma Mei,” Kori said softly. “She taught me a fan dance.” Kori opened the fan. Snap! The fan felt cool in Kori’s paw and smelled like sweet paper. Kori bowed. “Thank you, Grandma.”

Kori practiced the fan dance, moving the fan in little circles. “Circle, circle, pause,” Kori sang. Kori added the ribbon with the other hand. Swish and snap, ring and whoosh. Kori’s feet stepped and hopped. “This is coordination! My hands, feet, eyes, and ears work together.”

A small hiccup happened. The ribbon brushed a pillow and the bell rolled under the bed. Tinkle… tinkle… silence.

Kori blinked. “Oh dear.” Kori felt a little twist in the tummy. Then Kori took a calm breath. “I can be resourceful,” Kori said. “I will solve this.”

Kori looked under the bed. It was dark and dusty. Kori used the ribbon lace like a fishing line. “I will guide, not grab,” Kori said. Kori lay on the rug and peeked under. “Hello, bell. I’m coming.”

Kori tapped the drum softly. Tap… tap… tap. The bell glimmered. Kori used the lace to loop around the bell. “Slow… steady… together,” Kori whispered. With a gentle pull, the bell slid out. Tinkle! Kori cheered. “I did it with calm and care.”

Just then, Kori heard a knocking sound—knock knock—from the door. It was Mama Koala. “Kori, are you having fun?” she asked.

“Yes, Mama,” Kori said. “I am practicing coordination. I made a Pillow Parade, a Ribbon, and a Fan Dance. I listened, looked, stepped, and swished. I solved a bell problem too.”

Mama smiled. “That is wonderful. You were innovative and resourceful. How do you feel?”

“I feel proud,” Kori said. “I also feel brave. I learned from Grandma Mei’s fan dance. I want to show you.” Kori stood tall. “I will lead my parade.”

Kori spoke clearly, kind and strong. “Mama, please watch my steps and my dance.” That was Kori being assertive—using a strong, respectful voice.

“Of course,” said Mama. She sat on the bed.

Kori began. Tap-tap on the drum. Step on the pillows. Swish went the ribbon. Snap went the fan. Ring went the bell. Kori counted, breathed, and smiled. The cozy bedroom filled with soft sounds and gentle wind from the fan. The rainbow blanket fluttered like a small flag.

When Kori finished, Mama clapped. “Bravo, Kori! Your body and mind worked together. You listened, looked, and moved with care.”

Kori hugged Mama. “Coordination helps me do things safely and well. When I am calm, I can think. When I plan, I can solve problems. When I practice, I get better.”

Mama nodded. “Yes. And you honored Grandma Mei’s dance. That shows cultural appreciation.”

Kori put the pillows back, rolled the ribbon, and folded the fan. The room felt warm and peaceful.

Kori whispered, “Today I learned a big lesson: Coordination means working together—my hands, feet, eyes, ears, and heart. With calm, innovation, and resourcefulness, I can do hard things.”

The End.
