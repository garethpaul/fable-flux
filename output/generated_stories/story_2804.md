# Kiki the Calm Koala Solves the Case

Every morning, Kiki the calm koala woke up to a soft, golden light in the home office. The room smelled like warm paper and lemon tea. The computer hummed, “Hmmmm,” and the printer clicked, “Clack-clack.” Kiki loved her routine. She stretched, hugged her fuzzy tail, and whispered, “Good morning, desk. Good morning, chair.”

On the wall, a big calendar showed colorful shapes for each day. Monday had a blue circle for “work time.” Tuesday had a green star for “snack time.” Today was Wednesday, with a yellow square for “help time.” Kiki smiled. “I help on help days,” she said.

Kiki’s little friend, Pip the paperclip, jingled from a cup. “Good morning, Kiki!” Pip chimed.

Kiki sipped warm tea. “Good morning, Pip.” She liked to keep things gentle. Calm thoughts helped her think.

Just then, a tiny sound floated from the floor. “Rrrrip.” Kiki looked down. The recycle bin was tipped over. Papers were scattered like white snow. “Oh dear,” Kiki said, placing a paw on her heart. “The tidy office is not tidy.”

Pip gasped. “Who did this? That’s not fair!”

Kiki blinked slowly. “We don’t know yet. Let’s be open-minded. We can be curious and careful. We can find the answer.”

She set a gentle routine: first look, then listen, then think. “Step one: look.” Kiki peeked around the desk. She saw tiny tracks on the dusty mat—little dots and lines going from the window to the bin. “Step two: listen.” She held her breath. From behind the curtain came a faint, “Sniff, sniff… squeak?”

Kiki nodded. “Step three: think.” She tapped her chin. “Someone small. Someone hungry.”

She spoke softly to the curtain. “Hello. I am Kiki. I am calm. Are you okay?”

A shy nose popped out, then whiskers, then a tiny mouse with big ears. The mouse wore a button like a hat. “H-hi,” squeaked the mouse. “I’m Momo. I was looking for a crumb. I was so hungry. The bin fell. I’m sorry.” Momo’s eyes were wet.

Pip bobbed. “You made a mess! That’s not right!”

Kiki breathed in. The air smelled like lemon and paper. She breathed out. “We care about fairness, Pip. Justice means we listen and help make things right.” She turned to Momo. “Thank you for telling the truth.”

Momo pressed his paws together. “I didn’t want to break anything. I was just hungry.”

Kiki patted the mat. “We can fix the mess together. And we can find a better way to get food.”

Momo sniffed. “Really?”

“Really,” said Kiki. “Let’s use resourcefulness. That means we use what we have to solve a problem.”

Kiki looked around the home office. What did they have? Paper, tape, boxes, pens, rubber bands, and a big plastic jar. The printer purred, “Hmmmm,” like it had an idea too.

“I know,” Kiki said. “We can make a snack station for Momo. A safe place. Not the bin.”

Pip jingled with joy. “A snack station!”

Kiki set a new routine: plan, build, test. “Plan,” she said, drawing a simple picture with a crayon. “We need a box, a ramp, and a little tray.”

“Build,” she said next. She used a small box from under the desk. It smelled like cardboard. She taped the lid open. She made a ramp from a file folder and held it with paperclips. Pip was proud to help. Kiki folded a sticky note into a tiny tray.

Momo watched with wide eyes. “It’s so smart,” he squeaked.

“Resourceful,” said Kiki with a smile. “We used what we had.”

Kiki opened the snack drawer. She found a few oats and a tiny raisin. “Only a little,” she said. “Enough to help, not to spill.” She placed them in the sticky-note tray. “This snack station will be away from the bin and away from the cords. Safe and tidy.”

“Test,” Kiki said. She tapped the ramp. “Would you like to try, Momo?”

Momo stepped carefully. Tip, tap, tip, tap. He climbed the ramp and nibbled an oat. “Crunch, crunch.” His whiskers twitched. “It’s perfect!”

Pip clapped against the cup. “Hooray!”

“Now,” Kiki said, “we clean up together, so it’s fair.” She gave Momo a soft brush to push the papers back into the bin. She used a dustpan. Pip held a corner of a paper with his tiny clip. Together they tidied, and the floor looked like smooth snow again.

Momo stood tall. “I made a mistake. I am sorry. I will use the snack station now.”

Kiki nodded. “That is just. You told the truth, helped fix the mess, and learned a better way.”

The sun moved across the rug. The room felt warm and bright. The computer hummed a happy tune. Kiki checked the calendar and drew a small yellow heart on Wednesday. “Help time: complete,” she said.

Momo smiled. “Kiki, you are calm and kind and clever.”

Kiki chuckled softly. “We were resourceful together. Curiosity helped us find the problem. Justice helped us fix it fairly. Open-mindedness helped us listen first. And our routine helped us stay steady.”

Pip jingled. “Can we add ‘snack time’ to the calendar for Momo?”

Kiki grinned. “Yes. A tiny dot each day. A little, not a lot.”

They all sat quiet for a moment, listening to the gentle hum and smelling the lemon tea. Kiki’s heart felt light. The home office was tidy, safe, and kind again.

That evening, Kiki sipped her tea and whispered, “Good job, team.” Momo curled under the box ramp. Pip nestled in his cup. The golden light faded to a soft glow.

Kiki smiled. “When we stay calm and use what we have, we can solve problems. That’s resourcefulness.”

The End.
