# Hops on the Porch, Hugs for the World

The sun was warm, and the air smelled like sweet flowers. A joyful kangaroo joey named Kiki sat on the peaceful front porch, swinging her little feet. The wooden boards felt smooth under her tail. “Thump-thump,” went her happy feet. Birds sang, “Tweet-tweet!” in the tall tree nearby.

Kiki loved her porch. She could see the road, the mailbox, and the bright blue sky. She felt safe and cozy. “What is the world like past my porch?” Kiki wondered. She hugged her small backpack. Inside were crayons, a picture book, and a tiny globe ball that fit in her paw.

Mama Roo brought a cool cup of water with a lemon slice. “Sip-sip?” Mama smiled.

“Yes, please!” Kiki said. The water was cold and tangy. “Mama, what is the big world like?”

“The world is full of friends,” said Mama. “Friends near and far. We can learn about them right here on our porch.”

Just then, the mail carrier, Mr. Bear, came up the steps. His bag jingled softly. “Good morning, Kiki!” he rumbled. “I have postcards from around the world!”

Kiki’s eyes grew wide. “Postcards?” Her ears perked up.

Mr. Bear set down a small stack. The cards smelled like paper and a little like rain. Kiki held one with a bright red maple leaf. “This card is from Canada,” said Mr. Bear. “They say hello with ‘Bonjour’ too.”

Kiki tried it. “Bon-jour!” She giggled. “That sounds like a song.”

The next card showed a tall tower by the sea. “From India,” read Mama. “They say ‘Namaste.’”

Kiki pressed her paws together like she was hugging the air. “Na-ma-ste.” It felt kind and warm.

Another card showed a busy city with bright signs. “From Japan,” said Mr. Bear. “They say ‘Konnichiwa.’”

“Konnichi-wa!” Kiki said. She bowed a little. The porch seemed bigger now, like it stretched across the ocean.

The wind made the chimes go “ting-ting-ting.” Kiki felt a happy flutter in her tummy. “I want to meet friends,” she said. “I want to be a helper. I want to be a good world friend.”

Mama nodded. “That is called being a global citizen,” she said. “We listen, we learn, and we care.”

Kiki opened her backpack and rolled her tiny globe ball. It was smooth and cool. “Round and round,” she whispered. “So many places.”

Just then, neighbors came by the porch. First was Lila and her grandma. Lila wore a bright scarf with tiny stars. “We brought flatbread,” said Grandma. The bread was warm and smelled like toasted seeds. “In our home, we say ‘Salaam’ for peace.”

Kiki took a small bite. “Mmm,” she said. “Salaam!” The word felt like a soft hug in her mouth.

Next, Mateo and his dad rolled by on a bike. They stopped and smiled. “Hola, Kiki,” said Mateo. He had a little drum. “In our home, we tap rhythms.” Mateo tapped, “dum-dum-ta!” The sound bounced off the porch and made Kiki’s toes wiggle.

“Hola!” Kiki said. She clapped, “clap-clap!” Her tail thumped in time.

Then Mei and her mom came with a tiny bag of jasmine tea. “Nǐ hǎo,” said Mei. The tea smelled like flowers and rain. Mei showed Kiki how to wave gently with two hands. Kiki tried it and felt calm and careful.

Kiki looked at her friends. “My porch feels like the whole world,” she said softly.

Mama smiled. “The world can meet right here,” she said. “And we can meet the world with our hearts.”

Kiki took out her crayons. “Let’s draw together!” she said. She spread paper on the porch. The paper was smooth and white like a little cloud.

Lila drew stars and a moon. Mateo drew his drum and a bright sun. Mei drew a gentle mountain. Kiki drew her porch with many smiling friends holding hands. She added the words she learned: Bonjour, Namaste, Konnichiwa, Salaam, Hola, Nǐ hǎo.

Mr. Bear stamped the picture with a little paw print. “This looks like a world poster,” he said.

Kiki looked at the colors—red, blue, gold, and green. She listened to the sounds—dum-dum-ta, clap-clap, ting-ting. She smelled bread and tea and lemon water. Her heart felt big and bouncy, like her best hop.

“I learned,” Kiki said, “that people say hello in many ways. We eat different foods. We sing different songs. We still smile. We still care.”

Mama kissed Kiki’s head. “Yes,” she said. “We are different and the same. We all belong on this big round world.”

Kiki stood up and held the tiny globe. “I will be kind,” she said. “I will listen. I will learn new words. I will share my porch and my heart.”

Everyone cheered. “Hooray for Kiki!” The porch seemed to glow in the soft sun.

Kiki pressed her picture to the porch wall with gentle tape. “There,” she said. “A world poster on my porch.”

She took one more cool sip of lemon water and felt the tangy tickle. “Today, I traveled the world,” she whispered, “right here at home.”

The wind chimed again. “Ting-ting-ting.” Kiki hugged Mama, then waved to her friends as they went on their way.

Kiki curled her tail and smiled. “The world is full of friends,” she said. “I am ready to meet them, step by step, hop by hop.”

The End.
