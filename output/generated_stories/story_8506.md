# Eli the Rooftop Helper

Eli was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and bright curious eyes. One sunny morning, Eli climbed the stairs, step, step, step, to the rooftop garden. The air smelled like fresh mint and warm bread. Bees hummed. A small flag flapped, flap, flap, in the breeze. Eli loved the rooftop. He could see birds glide in the blue sky, and he could hear the city say hello with little honks and laughs.

“Good morning, rooftop,” Eli said in his kind, rumbly voice.

On the rooftop, friends were busy. Mia the mouse watered tiny tomatoes. Tiko the turtle lined up smooth stones. Lala the ladybug read a little book. Eli sniffed. The mint leaves smelled cool and sweet. He touched the warm bricks with his toes. The sun felt like a cozy hug.

Then Eli heard a cry. “Hey! That’s not fair!” It was Pip the pigeon. Pip flapped near a shiny bowl of seeds.

Rex the crow stood tall beside the bowl. “I saw the bowl first,” Rex cawed. “So I take all the seeds.”

Pip shook his wings. “But we share the rooftop. The bowl is for everyone.”

Eli felt his heart thump. He cared about fair. He cared about justice. Justice means everyone gets fair and kind treatment. Eli took a deep breath. He remembered: use gentle words, listen, and stand up for what is right.

Eli walked over. Step, step. “Hello, Rex. Hello, Pip,” he said softly. “Can we talk?”

Rex puffed his feathers. “I am hungry,” he said. “I saw it first.”

Pip pecked the floor. “I am hungry too,” he whispered.

Eli nodded. “Both are hungry,” he said. He closed his eyes and listened to the wind. The wind said shhh. Eli opened his eyes. “We can make a fair plan.”

Rex tilted his head. “A plan?”

“Yes,” Eli said. “On our rooftop, we take turns. We share. That is justice. Fair means no one gets all, and no one gets none.”

Mia the mouse squeaked. “We have a sharing line!” She showed a chalk mark. “We can count seeds. One for Pip, one for Rex.”

Tiko the turtle nodded slowly. “Slow and fair wins the day.”

Rex tapped his beak. “Counting takes time.”

Eli smiled. “We can be patient. We can persist. We keep trying until we get it right.” He tapped the chalk gently with his trunk. “I can help.”

Together, they began. Eli said, “One for Pip.” Pip pecked a seed. “One for Rex.” Rex pecked a seed. The sun warmed their backs. A little breeze tickled Eli’s ears. The seeds made tiny clicks in the bowl. Click. Click. Click.

Halfway through, a gust of wind whooshed. Some seeds skittered across the roof. “Oh no!” cried Pip. Rex hopped back. The seeds rolled under the mint box.

Eli felt worry in his tummy. Then he remembered resilience. “We can try again,” he said. “We can be strong and kind.” He lowered his soft trunk and reached under the box. He felt the dusty floor. He felt round seeds. “I found them!” he said with joy.

Mia brought a little brush. “Sweep, sweep,” she said. Tiko steadied the box. Lala read from her little book, “When we share, we all care.”

Eli laughed. “That is a nice rhyme.” He loved words. Words can help. Words can heal. Eli taught a new word. “Listen: jus-tice.” He clapped slowly. “Jus-tice means fair for everyone.”

“Jus-tice,” Pip tried. “Jus-tice,” Rex tried too. The word felt strong and smooth, like a round stone in the hand.

They kept counting. “One for Pip. One for Rex.” When the bowl was empty, both birds had seeds. Their tummies felt happy.

Rex bowed his head. “Thank you,” he cawed. “I was quick to grab. That was not fair. I will try to be just next time.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “Thank you,” he cooed. “I will speak up when things are not fair.”

Eli felt warm inside. He had stood up for justice with gentle words. He had used his own effort. He had kept going when the wind made trouble. He had helped his friends use fair rules.

Just then, a little cloud slid over the sun. “Shade,” said Tiko with a smile. They all sat on the warm bricks and shared a drink of cool water. The mint smell danced in the air. The flag went flap, flap, flap.

Mia asked, “Eli, how did you know what to do?”

Eli thought. He wiggled his ears. “I listened. I used kind words. I made a fair plan. I kept trying. That is self-reliance. That is persistence. That is justice.”

Lala lifted her book. “Here is another word: thank you.”

“Thank you,” everyone said together. The word sounded like a song on the rooftop.

Rex pecked a leftover crumb and pushed it to Pip. “For you,” he said.

Pip smiled. “For us,” he said, and nudged it back so they could share.

Eli stood and looked at the sky. Birds glided. The city hummed. The rooftop felt safe and good. “Today we learned,” Eli said. “We learned a big word and a big idea.”

Pip and Rex nodded. “Justice,” they said together. “Fair for everyone.”

Eli’s heart felt light as a feather, strong as a drum. He knew that tomorrow he could help again, with his own ears, his own trunk, and his own brave, kind voice.

And the rooftop flag flapped on, telling the sky, flap, flap, flap, “We are fair here.”

The End.
