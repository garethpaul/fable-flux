# Remy's Porch Promise

Remy the rabbit woke up with a big stretch and a bigger smile. The sun felt warm and friendly. The peaceful front porch smelled like fresh mint and clean wood. A breeze tickled his whiskers. The porch swing creaked, creak, creak, in a gentle rhythm.

“Good morning, porch,” Remy said. He liked to talk to the porch like it was a friend. “Today is a healthy-habits day!”

Remy was a determined rabbit. When he made a promise, he tried his best to keep it. He had a little chart on the porch wall. It had tidy pictures he drew himself: a toothbrush, a water cup, a carrot, a broom, and a sunshine for outdoor play.

“First, brush,” he told himself. He tapped his chart with his paw and hopped inside to brush his teeth. Swish, swish, swish went the minty bubbles. “Brush in the morning, brush at night,” he sang softly. “Keep my smile clean and bright!”

He hopped back out to the porch. “Next, drink water.” He lifted his cup and sipped. Glug, glug. Cool water slid down his throat. “Ahh,” he sighed. The breeze ruffled his fur.

“Now, chores,” Remy said with a determined nod. He liked doing chores on the porch. It made the porch feel cozy and loved. He picked up a little broom. Sweep, sweep, sweep went the straw. Pine needles made tiny scratchy sounds as they moved into a neat pile. “Thank you, broom,” Remy whispered. “You are good at your job.”

“Buenos días, porche,” sang a sparrow on the railing. The bird’s feathers flashed gray and white. “Buenos días means good morning,” chirped the sparrow.

Remy’s ears perked up. “Buenos días!” he said back, trying the new words. They felt bouncy and bright on his tongue. “I like how that sounds.”

The sparrow hopped closer. “Soy Pipo,” he said. “I am Pipo.”

“I’m Remy,” said the rabbit. “I’m making healthy choices.”

“Healthy choices are good,” Pipo chirped. “And your porch is very clean!”

Remy beamed, but just then, a small thump came from the step. His little cousin, Dot, scrambled up to the porch. Dot was tiny, with speckles on her ears and big curious eyes.

“Hi, Remy!” Dot squeaked. “Can I help?”

“Sure,” Remy said kindly. “We can wash the veggie basket.”

The basket held crunchy carrots and green leaves from the garden. Remy placed it carefully by the railing. “We will wash, dry, and then snack,” he said.

Dot nodded hard. “I can carry it!” She grabbed the basket and tried to lift. It wobbled. The porch swing creaked. Pipo fluttered up, startled.

“Careful, Dot,” Remy said calmly. “Use two paws. Slow and steady.”

Dot giggled and tugged fast. Whoops! The basket tipped over. Carrots rolled like tiny orange rockets across the porch. A carrot bumped Remy’s paw. Another slipped through the railing and plopped into the soft grass below.

Remy’s chest felt tight. His eyebrows scrunched. “Oh no,” he said. He wanted to shout. He wanted to say, “Dot, you ruined it!”

Dot’s eyes filled with tears. “I’m sorry,” she whispered. “I wanted to help.”

The porch was quiet. Even the breeze paused.

Remy took a deep breath. In, out. He remembered his porch promise: Be kind. Be healthy. Be helpful. He looked at Dot’s trembling whiskers and Pipo’s worried eyes.

Remy put a paw on his heart. “I forgive you, Dot,” he said gently. “I know you wanted to help. We can fix this together.”

Dot blinked. “You forgive me?”

“Yes,” Remy said. “We all make mistakes. Let’s try again—slow and steady.”

Pipo tilted his head. “Perdón means sorry,” he chirped softly. “Gracias means thank you.”

Dot sniffled and copied, “Perdón, Remy.”

“Gracias for helping,” Remy replied. The words felt kind and warm. “Let’s use careful hands.”

They worked like a tiny team. Remy held the basket steady. Dot used two paws, like Remy showed. Pipo pointed with his wing. “Carrot at two o’clock! Another at your paw!”

They picked up carrots. They brushed off dust. They washed them in the bowl by the door. Splash, swish, drip. The water sparkled in the sun. Soon the basket was full again, clean and bright.

“Snack time,” Remy said. He passed around carrot sticks and crisp lettuce. Crunch, crunch! The carrots tasted sweet and happy. The lettuce tasted cool and fresh.

“Salud!” chirped Pipo.

“What does that mean?” Dot asked, smiling now.

“It means to health,” Remy said. “Like cheers for our healthy habits.”

“Salud!” Dot cheered, tapping her carrot with Remy’s.

After the snack, Remy finished his chores. He wiped the porch rail with a soft cloth. Swipe, swipe. He shook the doormat. Flap, flop. He watered the potted herbs. Drip-drop, drip-drop. The porch smelled like mint and sunshine.

Pipo sang a little song. “Buenos días, buenos hábitos,” he trilled. “Good morning, good habits.”

Remy checked his chart: brush, drink water, chores, snack, playtime. All done! He felt proud and steady inside. Dot hopped in a circle. “I learned slow and steady,” she said.

Remy patted her shoulder. “I learned forgiveness,” he said. “It keeps my heart healthy, too.”

The porch swing creaked a happy creak. The breeze started again, soft and kind. Remy stretched and smiled at his friends.

“Every day is a porch promise day,” he said. “We take care of our bodies. We help at home. We say sorry and forgive. We learn new words. And we try again.”

“Sí,” chirped Pipo. “Yes!”

Remy and Dot whispered together, “Buenos días, healthy habits. Salud!”

They sat on the peaceful front porch, listening to birds, feeling the warm wood under their paws, and tasting the last sweet crunch of carrot. Remy’s heart felt light and clean, like the porch after a sweep.

The End.
