# Tilly Turtle’s Warm Kitchen Plan

Tilly was a shy turtle with a soft green shell. She lived in a warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon and sugar. The sun peeked in the window and made golden squares on the floor. The clock on the wall went tick-tock, tick-tock.

Today was a special day. Tilly’s grandma had a tradition. Every year, they made “Friendship Dumplings.” They were small, soft dumplings with tiny smiles made of carrot bits. Grandma said, “We make them to share love and stories.”

Tilly wanted to share the dumplings with her friend, Benny Bunny. But Tilly was shy. She worried about talking. She worried about time. She looked up at the clock. Tick-tock.

“I want to do it,” Tilly whispered. “But what if I’m too slow?”

Grandma Turtle patted Tilly’s shell. “We can make a plan,” she said. “A plan helps time feel friendly.”

Tilly sat at the little table. The table felt smooth under her hands. The kettle sang a soft whistle. Warm air hugged Tilly’s cheeks.

“What is our plan?” Tilly asked, peeking out from behind her shell.

Grandma smiled. “First, we wash hands. Second, we set a timer for mixing. Third, we steam the dumplings. Fourth, we share with a friend.”

Tilly nodded. She liked the steps. Steps felt safe. She got a sticky note and drew little pictures:
- Tiny hands under water
- A bowl with a spoon
- A pot with steam
- Two friends smiling

She stuck the note by the clock.

“Step one,” Grandma said.

Splash, splash. The water was warm. The soap smelled like lemons. Tilly rubbed her fingers. “Clean and ready,” she said with a small smile.

“Step two,” Grandma said. She put a big bowl on the table. Tilly poured in flour. Poof! A flour cloud floated like a little white puff. It landed on Tilly’s nose. “Achoo!” Tilly giggled.

Grandma added water. Plip-plop. Tilly stirred with a wooden spoon. Swish, swish. The dough felt soft and squishy. “We will mix until the timer dings,” Grandma said, turning the little timer. It ticked with the clock. Tick-tick, tick-tock.

Tilly liked the sound. It helped her feel calm. “I can do it,” she said. “I can follow the steps.”

“Step three,” Grandma said when the timer dinged. They made tiny balls of dough. Tilly pressed a tiny carrot smile on each one. The dumplings looked happy. Into the steamer they went. Steam puffed like little clouds. It smelled cozy and warm.

Tilly peeked at the sticky note. “Step four is share with a friend,” she whispered. Her tummy fluttered. She looked at the door. She looked at the clock. Tick-tock.

Grandma touched Tilly’s hand. “Friendship means being brave and kind,” she said. “Benny is your friend. He will be happy.”

Tilly took a deep breath. She felt the warm kitchen. She heard the soft hiss of the steamer. She smelled cinnamon and carrots. “I can be brave,” she said. “I can share our tradition.”

Just then, a gentle knock came at the door. Knock-knock. Benny Bunny stood there with a shy smile and long floppy ears. He held a little basket.

“Hi, Tilly,” Benny said. “I brought time carrots. I mean, thyme carrots,” he giggled. “They smell like the garden.”

Tilly laughed. Her giggle made the room feel even warmer. “Come in, Benny. We have a plan. We are making Friendship Dumplings.”

Benny’s nose twitched. “I like plans,” he said. “They help me not rush.”

“Me too,” Tilly said. “We have four steps. We are on step four: share.”

Grandma lifted the steamer lid. Psssst. Sweet steam rose up. The dumplings smiled with their tiny carrot mouths. Tilly put them on a round plate. Benny set his thyme carrots in a small bowl. The green leaves smelled fresh and bright.

“Let’s eat together,” Tilly said. Her voice felt steady now.

They sat at the table. The dumplings were soft and warm. They tasted gentle and a little sweet. The carrots were tender and herby. Benny’s ears wiggled with joy. “These are yummy,” he said. “Thank you for sharing your tradition.”

Tilly’s heart felt full. “Thank you for coming,” she said. “Friends make brave feelings grow.”

Grandma poured warm tea. The cups clinked softly. They told little stories. Grandma told the story of the first Friendship Dumpling. Tilly told how she made a plan. Benny shared how he waters his carrot patch every morning and every night. “I use a tiny timer,” he said. “Tick-tock helps me remember.”

Tilly pointed to the sticky note with the four steps. “A plan helps me, too,” she said. “It makes time friendly.”

Benny nodded. “We can make a plan to meet again,” he said. “Next time, I will share my carrot-soup tradition.”

Tilly smiled big. She was not hiding behind her shell now. “Yes,” she said. “We can take turns sharing. That is what friends do.”

They cleaned up together. Wipe-wipe. Stack-stack. The clock kept tick-tocking, but no one felt rushed. They followed their plan, and they helped each other.

When Benny hopped to the door, Tilly waved. “Thank you for being my friend,” she said.

“Thank you for sharing,” Benny replied. “See you soon!”

Tilly hugged Grandma. “I was brave,” she whispered.

Grandma hugged back. “You were brave and kind. You made time your friend. You shared our tradition. That is the heart of friendship.”

Tilly looked at the golden squares of sun on the floor. She felt warm and proud. “Friendship is sharing, helping, and making plans together,” she said.

And from that day, Tilly kept her sticky notes by the clock, her heart open, and her kitchen warm for friends.

The End.
