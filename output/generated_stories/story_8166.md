# Rosie Rabbit's Colorful Solution

In a cozy study corner filled with books and crayons, lived a little rabbit named Rosie. She had soft brown fur and the brightest, most curious eyes you ever did see. Rosie loved to draw and paint more than anything in the whole wide world.

One sunny morning, Rosie hopped over to her favorite drawing table. She wanted to make a special picture for the art contest at school. "I'm going to draw the most beautiful rainbow!" she said excitedly, reaching for her box of crayons.

But oh no! When Rosie opened her crayon box, she gasped. "Where are all my colors?" she cried. Only three crayons were left: red, blue, and yellow. All her other beautiful colors - purple, orange, and green - were broken into tiny pieces on the floor.

Rosie's ears drooped sadly. "How can I make a rainbow with only three colors? Everyone else has full crayon boxes. It's not fair!" She felt like giving up, but then she remembered what her mama always said: "When you have a problem, think like an artist!"

Rosie wiggled her pink nose thoughtfully. She picked up her red crayon and made a stripe on her paper. It looked bright and cheerful, like a fire truck. Then she drew with her blue crayon - it reminded her of the sky on a clear day. The yellow crayon made her think of warm sunshine.

"Hmm," Rosie said, tapping her fluffy paw on her chin. "What if I try something different?"

She carefully colored red next to yellow on her paper. Something magical happened! Where the colors touched, they made orange! "Oh my carrots!" Rosie squeaked with joy. "I made a new color!"

Feeling excited, she tried mixing blue and yellow. Green appeared like magic! It was the color of fresh spring grass. Then she mixed red and blue together, and purple bloomed on her paper like beautiful violets.

"I can make ALL the colors!" Rosie shouted happily, doing a little bunny hop dance around her study corner.

She worked and worked, mixing her three crayons in different ways. Soon, her paper was filled with the most wonderful rainbow anyone had ever seen. Each color flowed into the next like a beautiful song.

When Rosie brought her picture to school, her teacher Mrs. Owl looked amazed. "Rosie, how did you make such beautiful colors with only three crayons?"

Rosie's whiskers twitched with pride. "I discovered that when you mix colors together, they make new ones! Red and yellow make orange, blue and yellow make green, and red and blue make purple!"

All her classmates gathered around to see. "That's so cool!" said Tommy Turtle. "Can you teach us how to do that?"

"Of course!" Rosie said happily. "Art is even more fun when we share what we learn!"

Mrs. Owl smiled warmly. "Rosie, you've shown us that creativity isn't about having the most supplies. It's about using your imagination and trying new things!"

Rosie won the art contest, but more importantly, she learned that problems can become opportunities. Sometimes the best solutions come from thinking differently and being creative with what you have.

From that day on, Rosie always remembered: when life gives you just three crayons, you can still paint the whole world!

The End.
