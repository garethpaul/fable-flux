# Nori’s Rooftop Quiet Time

On a sunny day, a gentle elephant calf named Nori climbed the wide stairs to the rooftop garden. The rooftop felt warm under her soft feet. The sky was big and blue. A little breeze tickled her ears. Flowers in pots smelled sweet, like honey and lemons. The city below buzzed softly, like a sleepy bee.

“I love the rooftop,” Nori said, swinging her small trunk. “It is high, it is bright, and there is space to move.”

Nori’s friends were there too. Miko the monkey hung from a rail and giggled. “Let’s play Super Stomp!” he cheered.

Pia the pigeon flapped her wings. “Coo-coo! I like games,” she cooed.

Tara the turtle blinked slowly. “We can exercise together,” she said. “Slow and steady is good for our bodies.”

Nori felt happy. She loved to move her body. Exercise made her legs feel strong and her heart feel glad. “Let’s do stretches first,” Nori said, remembering what Mama taught her. “Stretching is self-care. It helps our bodies be ready.”

They stood in a circle. Nori lifted her trunk up, up, up. “Breathe in,” she said. Everyone breathed in. “Breathe out,” she said, and they all did. The rooftop breeze smelled like fresh water from the fountain. The sun felt warm and friendly.

“Now let’s jump!” Miko shouted.

They hopped and wiggled. They marched and tiptoed. Nori did gentle knee bends. Tara did slow shell twists. Pia fluffed her feathers and flapped. Nori laughed, and her laugh sounded like tiny bells in her own ears.

But then Miko clapped his hands. “Let’s play Super Stomp!” he said again. “We stomp BIG and LOUD!”

Nori looked at the rooftop. She saw the flower pots. She saw a little sign: “Please be gentle. Respect the flowers and neighbors.”

Nori remembered Mama’s words. “Self-care is taking care of you and also caring for others,” Mama had said. “We move our bodies, and we respect things and people around us.”

“I like stomping,” Nori said softly. “But we need to be careful. Loud stomps can scare Pia. They can shake Tara. They can make the flowers fall.” She felt a flutter in her tummy. She wanted to have fun, but she also wanted to be kind.

Miko bounced on his toes. “I want big stomps!” he said, frowning.

Nori felt the flutter grow. Her ears felt warm. She put a gentle hand on her chest. “What is this feeling?” she asked herself. “I feel wiggly and worried. I want fun, and I want to be kind.”

Tara nodded. “Sometimes we feel two things,” she said. “It is okay.”

Pia cooed, “We can find a way.”

Nori took a slow, deep breath. She listened to her breath go in and out, like the ocean. She felt her feet on the warm rooftop. She smelled the lemony flowers. Her body felt calmer.

“I have an idea,” Nori said. “We can play Quiet Stomp. We can still move. We can still have fun. We stomp soft like raindrops. We listen to our bodies. We listen to the rooftop too.”

Miko blinked. “Quiet Stomp?” he asked.

Nori smiled. “Yes. We can make a path with chalk. We can stomp on the chalk dots, one, two, three, but soft. We can count and breathe. We take turns. We respect each other. We respect the flowers. That is self-care and care for others.”

Pia flapped happily. “Coo! I like soft,” she said.

Tara raised her head. “I can go slow on the dots,” she said.

Miko looked at the dots Nori drew with a bright blue chalk. He watched Nori put her feet down gently. Thump…thump…soft as a drum under a blanket. He listened to the sweet quiet. He tried it too. Thip…thip. He grinned. “This is fun!”

They played Quiet Stomp and counted together. “One…two…three…” They breathed in and out. Nori reminded, “Shoulders down. Smiles on. Soft feet.” They moved their bodies and felt strong and safe. The flowers did not fall. The neighbors stayed calm. Pia did not startle. Tara did not wobble.

After the game, Nori said, “Water break!” She poured cool water into little cups. The water felt cold and nice in her mouth. “Drinking water is self-care too,” she told her friends. “It helps our bodies after exercise.”

They sat in the shade. The breeze kissed their cheeks. The city hum sounded like a quiet song. Miko leaned on Nori’s side. “Thank you,” he said. “I felt mad when you said no to big stomps. But now I feel happy. Quiet Stomp was a good idea.”

Nori nodded. “Thank you for listening,” she said. “I felt worried. I took a breath. Then I found a way to help all of us.”

Pia cooed, “We respected the flowers.”

Tara added, “We respected each other.”

Nori smiled. Her heart felt warm and full. “Today I learned: Self-care means moving my body, drinking water, and resting when I need to. It also means caring for feelings and being gentle with others and our space.”

They watched a tiny cloud float by. Nori closed her eyes for a moment and listened to her calm breath. Her body felt strong. Her mind felt quiet. Her friends were close. The rooftop garden felt safe and happy.

“Tomorrow,” Nori said, “we can play again.”

“Quiet Stomp and Stretch!” Miko cheered.

“Coo-coo!” Pia sang.

“Slow and steady,” Tara smiled.

Nori giggled. “Together,” she said.

The End.
