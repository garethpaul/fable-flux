# Tilly Turtle’s Brave Bedtime

Tilly was a shy turtle who loved her cozy bedroom. Her room was soft and warm, like a hug. The nightlight made a gentle glow on the walls. Her bed had a fluffy blue blanket that felt like clouds. A tiny fan hummed, “whirr, whirr,” and the curtains smelled like clean cotton.

Tilly held her favorite stuffed star, Twinkle. She whispered, “Good night, Twinkle,” and tucked her legs and head into her shell. But her belly felt tight. Her cheeks felt hot. Her heart went thump-thump. Tilly was thinking about tomorrow.

Tomorrow, her cousin Toby was coming to visit. Toby was a bouncy turtle who liked to talk and play loudly. Tilly liked quiet play. She was shy and slow to warm up. “What if I feel scared?” she wondered. “What if I don’t know what to say?”

She peeked out of her shell. Mama Turtle sat on the rug, folding soft pajamas. “Mama,” Tilly said in a tiny voice, “I feel wiggly inside.”

Mama looked up with kind eyes. “Wiggly inside? Can you tell me more?” she asked, sitting on the edge of the bed.

Tilly took a little breath and felt the cool air in her nose. “My belly feels twisty,” she said. “My heart is going fast. I think I feel… shy.”

Mama nodded. “Thank you for telling me. That feeling has a name. It is called shy or nervous. Where do you feel it in your body?”

Tilly touched her tummy. “Here. And my cheeks feel warm.”

Mama smiled. “That is emotional awareness. You noticed your feelings and where they live in your body. That is brave.”

Tilly blinked. “Brave?”

“Very brave,” said Mama. “Feelings are like visitors. They come, and then they go. We can talk to them. We can practice what to do.”

Tilly sat up and stroked Twinkle’s soft star points. “Can we practice?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Let’s make a plan. When you feel shy, you can breathe slow. Want to try?”

Tilly nodded. Mama held up a finger. “Smell the flower.” They both sniffed in slowly. “Blow the candle.” They both blew out gently. The fan went “whirr,” and the nightlight made tiny sparkles on the wall.

Tilly giggled. Her belly felt a little less twisty.

“What words can you say to cousin Toby?” Mama asked.

Tilly looked at her toy shelf. She saw blocks, crayons, and a puzzle with a smiling sun. “I can say, ‘Hi, Toby. Want to build a tower?’”

“Wonderful,” Mama said. “Let’s practice with me.” Mama put on a silly voice. “Hello! I’m Toby! I like to jump!”

Tilly hugged Twinkle and tried her words. “Hi, Toby. Want to build a tower?” Her voice was small but clear.

Mama clapped quietly. “You did it! And if you need quiet time, you can say, ‘I need a little break.’ Practice that too.”

Tilly whispered, “I need a little break.” The words felt safe in her mouth.

They practiced again and again—smell the flower, blow the candle; “Hi, Toby”; “I need a little break.” Each time, Tilly felt a little braver. She even made a tiny smile.

In the morning, warm light tickled Tilly’s face. She heard feet on the hallway rug—tap, tap, tap. The door opened, and Toby popped his head in. “Hi, Tilly!” he said loudly. “Let’s play race!”

Tilly’s belly twisted again. Her heart went thump. She remembered Mama’s words. Feelings are visitors. She put a hand on her tummy and took a slow breath. Smell the flower. Blow the candle.

She looked at Toby’s bright eyes. “Hi, Toby,” she said softly. “Want to build a tower?”

Toby blinked. “A tower? Okay!” He bounced to the block basket. Blocks clacked and clinked as they piled them up. Red, blue, yellow—stack, stack, stack.

The tower wobbled. Toby laughed and poked it. “Boom!” It fell with a crash. Tilly jumped. Her cheeks felt warm again. “I don’t like loud,” she whispered.

She remembered her practice. “I need a little break,” Tilly said. She sat on her bed and hugged Twinkle. The blanket felt cool and smooth. Tilly took a breath. Smell the flower. Blow the candle.

Toby watched and sat down too. “Are you okay?” he asked, quieter now.

Tilly nodded. “I felt shy and loud. I am breathing. Then we can build again.”

Toby nodded back. “I can be soft,” he said in a small voice. He picked up a block and set it gently. “Soft,” he whispered. Tilly smiled and added a block. Together, they built a tall, gentle tower.

Mama peeked in and smiled. “I see teamwork,” she said.

When they finished, the tower touched the windowsill. Sunlight slid across the top and made it glow. Tilly’s belly felt calm and warm. Her heart went steady. She looked at Toby. “Thank you for being soft.”

Toby grinned. “Thank you for telling me.”

That night, in her cozy bedroom, Tilly tucked in with Twinkle. The fan hummed. The nightlight glowed. “Today I felt shy,” she whispered, “and I used my plan.” She took one slow breath—smell the flower, blow the candle. “Feelings can come and go. I can notice them, talk about them, and practice what to do.”

Mama kissed her head. “That is the lesson, little turtle. You used emotional awareness, you practiced, and you solved a problem with kind words.”

Tilly smiled in the soft light. “I am shy,” she said, “and I am brave.”

The End.
