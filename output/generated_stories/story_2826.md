# Benny Bear's Pantry Discovery

Benny Bear rubbed his round tummy. "Time for my morning snack!" he said cheerfully. Every day, he ate the same thing - honey and berries. It was his favorite routine.

He pushed open the pantry door with his fuzzy paw. *Creak!* The wooden door sang its familiar song. Sunlight streamed through the small window, making dust sparkle like tiny stars.

"Let's see," Benny mumbled, looking at the tall shelves. "Honey jar, check. Berry basket, check."

But today, something was different. A small brown bag sat next to his usual treats. It had a sweet, nutty smell that tickled his black nose.

"What's this?" Benny wondered aloud. He sniffed the mysterious bag. *Sniff, sniff.* "It smells... interesting."

Just then, Mama Bear walked into the pantry. Her warm brown fur glowed in the sunlight.

"Good morning, Benny," she said with a gentle smile. "I see you found the almonds."

"Almonds?" Benny tilted his head. "But I always eat honey and berries for my morning snack. That's my routine."

Mama Bear nodded. "Routines are wonderful, little one. But sometimes trying new things can be wonderful too."

Benny looked at the bag nervously. "What if I don't like them? What if they taste yucky?"

"That's okay," Mama Bear said softly. "Not liking something new is perfectly fine. But we never know what we might discover if we don't try."

Benny's ears twitched with curiosity. The almonds did smell nice. And Mama Bear's voice sounded so encouraging.

"Will you try them with me?" he asked quietly.

"Of course!" Mama Bear opened the bag carefully. *Rustle, rustle.* The sound was like autumn leaves dancing.

She poured a few almonds into Benny's small paw. They felt smooth and oval-shaped. Some were light brown, others darker.

"They look different from berries," Benny observed. "But that's okay. Different can be good."

He placed one almond on his pink tongue. *Crunch!* It made a satisfying sound between his teeth. The taste was creamy and slightly sweet.

"Mmm!" Benny's eyes grew wide with surprise. "It tastes nutty and nice! It's different from honey and berries, but I like it!"

Mama Bear clapped her paws together. "I'm so proud of you for being open-minded, Benny."

"Open-minded?" Benny asked, crunching another almond.

"It means being willing to try new things and accept that different doesn't mean bad," Mama Bear explained. "You were brave enough to taste something new."

Benny thought about this while he ate. "So I can still love my honey and berries, but also try new foods too?"

"Exactly!" Mama Bear hugged him tight. "Your routine can stay the same, but adding new experiences makes life more exciting."

From that day forward, Benny still enjoyed his favorite honey and berries every morning. But now he also looked forward to discovering new foods in the pantry. Sometimes he found dried fruits, sometimes nuts, and sometimes seeds.

Each new taste taught him that being open to different things made his world bigger and more colorful. And accepting new experiences, even when they seemed scary at first, often led to wonderful surprises.

"Thank you for teaching me about being open-minded, Mama," Benny said, giving her the biggest bear hug.

"Thank you for being so brave and accepting, my little cub," Mama Bear replied.

The End.
