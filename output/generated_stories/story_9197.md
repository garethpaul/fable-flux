# Leo's Garden of Thanks

Leo was a brave little lion cub with golden fur that sparkled in the morning sun. He lived in a cozy apartment with his mama, and his favorite place in the whole world was their tiny balcony garden.

"Good morning, my little sunshine!" Mama Lion called as Leo stretched his paws and yawned. "Would you like to help me water the plants today?"

Leo's eyes lit up like bright stars. "Yes, yes, yes!" he roared happily, though it sounded more like a cute little squeak.

Together, they stepped onto the balcony. Leo loved how the cool morning air tickled his whiskers and how the sweet smell of flowers filled his nose. Their garden wasn't very big, but it was full of wonderful things growing in colorful pots.

"Look, Leo," Mama said, pointing to a small tomato plant. "Our tomatoes are getting red and juicy!"

Leo touched the smooth, round tomato gently with his paw. "Mama, where do tomatoes come from?"

"Well," Mama smiled, "these tomato seeds came from far away, from a place called Italy, where farmers work very hard to grow the best tomatoes in the world."

"Wow!" Leo's eyes grew wide. "So someone far, far away helped make our lunch?"

"That's right, my clever cub. And look here," Mama pointed to their herb pots. "This basil came from seeds that traveled from India, and these carrots came from seeds from France."

Leo sniffed the basil leaves. They smelled fresh and peppery, making his nose twitch in a happy way. "So many friends helped our garden grow!"

As they watered each plant, Mama told Leo about the people who grew the seeds, the truck drivers who brought them to the store, and the store workers who helped them find everything they needed.

"And don't forget," Mama added, sprinkling water on the mint plant, "the rain that falls from the clouds, and the warm sunshine that helps everything grow big and strong."

Leo looked up at the bright blue sky and felt the warm sun on his fuzzy face. "Thank you, sun!" he called out loud. Then he looked at a fluffy white cloud. "Thank you, cloud!"

"That's wonderful, Leo!" Mama purred. "When we say thank you, it makes our hearts feel warm and happy."

Leo ran from plant to plant, his little paws patting the soil gently. "Thank you, tomato farmers in Italy! Thank you, basil growers in India! Thank you, carrot people in France!"

Mama's heart filled with pride as she watched her little cub. "You know what, Leo? I think we should make a special thank-you lunch with our garden vegetables."

"Can we really eat them?" Leo asked, his tummy rumbling.

"Of course! And while we eat, we can think about all the people around the world who helped bring this food to our table."

That afternoon, as they ate their fresh garden salad, Leo closed his eyes and thought about all the helping hands that made their meal possible. The tomatoes tasted like sunshine, the basil smelled like adventure, and the carrots crunched like happy music.

"Mama," Leo said, wiping tomato juice from his whiskers, "I want to say thank you every day."

"That's the best idea ever, my brave little cub," Mama said, giving him a warm hug. "When we remember to be thankful, we remember how connected we are to friends all around the world."

From that day on, Leo started each morning by saying thank you to their little garden, and his heart grew as big and bright as the sun itself.

The End.
