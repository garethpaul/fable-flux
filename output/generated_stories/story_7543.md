# Ella the Elephant and the Busy Laundry Room

In a sunny house at the end of Maple Lane, a gentle elephant calf named Ella visited her neighbors to help. Ella loved to help. Today, she would help in the laundry room.

Ella stepped inside and her big ears wiggled. “It smells like soap bubbles,” she said. The room was warm and steamy. The washing machine hummed. The dryer spun with a soft whirr. A basket overflowed with tiny socks, colorful shirts, and a bright blanket with stars.

“Good morning, Ella,” said Mr. Kim, the neighbor. He smiled and pointed to a list on the wall. “We have a lot to do. We also plan to give clean clothes and blankets to families on our street. Let’s be a team. First, we sort. Then, we wash. Then, we fold.”

Ella’s eyes shined. She wanted to help the whole community. She also felt a little wiggle of worry. The basket looked like a mountain. “That is a lot,” she whispered. Her trunk curled. Her feet shuffled.

Mr. Kim noticed. “We can do it, one step at a time. We can use our words to make a plan.”

Ella nodded. “A plan,” she said softly.

Just then, the washing machine beeped. Beep! Beep!

Ella flapped her ears. “I want to do everything now,” she blurted. She reached for the buttons with her trunk.

“Hold on, Ella,” Mr. Kim said. “We have to follow directions. Look at the list. First, sort.”

Ella’s cheeks felt warm. She huffed a tiny elephant sigh. “I feel frustrated,” she admitted. “I want it to be done fast.”

Mr. Kim crouched so his eyes were kind and close. “Thank you for telling me. When I feel frustrated, I take a slow breath. Want to try?”

Ella took a big elephant breath in. The room smelled like lemon soap. She let the breath out slowly, like a soft whoosh.

“Better,” she said. “Can we make a deal? Can I press one button if I do one job first?”

“That’s good negotiation,” said Mr. Kim. “Let’s make a fair deal. You sort the socks and shirts. Then you may press the start button with your trunk.”

Ella’s eyes brightened. “Deal!” she said. They bumped fist to trunk.

She began to sort. “Socks in this pile. Shirts in that pile. The star blanket in a special pile.” Her trunk brushed the soft cotton. She heard the dryer hum. She felt the floor warm under her feet.

A small boy named Mateo peeked in. “Can I help?” he asked, holding a bag of donated clothes.

“Yes,” Ella said. “We are washing clothes for our neighbors. We are doing community service.” She smiled proudly. “You can hand me socks!”

They worked together. Socks here. Shirts there. Tiny mittens on top. Ella sang a little sorting song. “Pair, pair, pair, we take good care.” Mateo giggled and sang along.

Soon the piles were neat. Ella looked at Mr. Kim. “I did my job. May I press the button now?”

Mr. Kim checked the list. “We need detergent. Two scoops, not more.” He pointed to the measuring cup. “Following directions keeps the clothes safe.”

Ella nodded. “Two scoops.” She poured. One scoop. Two scoops. The powder smelled like fresh rain.

“Now the button,” said Mr. Kim.

Ella’s trunk hovered. “Can I press it two times because I sorted two piles?”

Mr. Kim smiled. “That’s more negotiating! How about this: press once now, and when the dryer is ready, you can press that button too.”

“Deal!” Ella said. She gently pressed start. The machine chugged and swished. Water splashed. Swish, swish, swish. Ella felt proud.

After a while, the dryer beeped. Beep! Beep! Ella’s ears perked up. “My turn!” She pressed the dryer button. Warm air puffed out, smelling like sunshine and soap. Ella felt happy.

They folded shirts small and neat. They matched socks like tiny friends. Mateo held up a green sock and a blue sock. “These are not a pair,” he said.

Ella laughed. “They can still be friends.”

Mr. Kim clapped softly. “You are a great team. Now, let’s pack the clothes for our neighbors.”

Ella helped carry a bag to the porch. She was careful with her big feet. “Helping feels good,” she said.

Mr. Kim brought out a snack. “We worked hard. Let’s eat fruit.”

On the porch, they sat with bowls of crunchy apples, sweet bananas, and bright orange slices. The fruit was juicy and cool. Ella’s trunk curled around a banana. “Mm. This gives me energy,” she said.

“Healthy food helps our bodies,” said Mr. Kim. “It helps us work and stay calm.”

Ella nodded. “When I felt frustrated, I used my breath. I made a plan. I asked for a fair deal. We helped our neighbors. We followed directions. And we ate healthy fruit.”

Mr. Kim smiled. “That is a lot of good things in one day.”

Mateo raised his apple slice. “To teamwork!”

Ella raised her trunk. “To good deals and helping!”

They all laughed. The laundry room hummed softly behind them, still working to help the community.

That night, Ella thought about the busy laundry room. She remembered the warm air, the soft clothes, the beep-beep, and the swish-swish. She snuggled into her star blanket and whispered, “When I feel big feelings, I can use my words and make a fair plan.”

The End.
