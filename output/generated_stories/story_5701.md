# Sparks the Robot Dog Saves Art Time

Clank-clink! Whirr-woof! Sparks, the helpful robot dog, trotted into the busy basement. The basement hummed with sounds. The washing machine went swish-swish. The heater hummed warm and low. A little light blinked red-red by a toolbox. It smelled like soap and warm dust.

“Good morning, basement!” Sparks barked in a friendly robot voice. His metal tail went tick-tick-tick. His nose light blinked blue. He loved helping kids make art and write stories.

Maya and Ben came down the stairs with a box of art things. Maya held colorful paints. Ben held big paper and markers. “We want to make a mural!” Maya said. “We want to write our names and draw a happy garden,” Ben added.

Sparks wagged. “Art time is great time,” he said. “We will make pictures and write words. We will also keep clean and safe.”

They spread paper on a big table. The busy basement felt cozy. The dryer puffed warm air. The cement floor was cool on bare toes. The old pipes made tiny drips: plink, plink.

Maya swirled a brush. “The red paint looks like strawberries,” she said. Ben uncapped a marker. “Sniff! It smells funny,” he said, wrinkling his nose.

Sparks held up a little sign with his paw. It said: Wash, Wipe, Write. “First, we wash hands,” he said. “Clean hands make clean art.” He turned on the sink. Water gushed. Whoosh! He made happy beeps. “Bubble time!”

Maya giggled. Ben sang, “Scrub-a-dub! Scrub-a-dub!” They rubbed their hands with soap. The soap smelled like lemons. “We rinse,” Sparks said. “We dry.” He wagged his towel tail. “Towel woof!” he joked, and they laughed.

Hands clean, they began. Maya dipped her brush. Swoosh, swoosh. Red, yellow, blue. Ben drew a big sun. He wrote S-U-N with a green marker. Sparks stepped close and said, “Good writing! S is curvy. U is like a smile. N is straight then slant.” Ben grinned. “I can write my name too!” He carefully wrote B-E-N. Maya wrote M-A-Y-A in purple. “Beautiful,” said Sparks. “Writing helps us tell our art story.”

Suddenly, oops! A cup of water tipped. Splash! The water ran across the table, and paint started to run. Red rivers! Blue puddles! The paper bubbled. Ben gasped. “Oh no! Our mural!”

The basement felt extra busy now. Drip-drip from the pipes, splash-splash from the table. The washing machine thumped. Maya looked worried. “Everything is messy,” she said, almost crying.

Sparks took a deep breath, and a soft fan whirred in his chest. “It’s okay,” he said kindly. “We have a problem. We will solve it. Art can be messy, and messy can be fixed.”

“How?” Ben asked.

Sparks’ eyes blinked bright. “We follow three steps: Pause, Protect, Plan.”

They all put their hands on their tummies and took a slow breath. “Pause,” said Sparks. The noisy room seemed calmer.

“Protect,” Sparks said. He pressed his paw and a tiny squeegee popped out. Squeak-squeak! He pulled water away from the paper. Maya held the paper edges. Ben placed dry towels under the wet spots. Sparks handed out clean cloths. “Wipe, wipe,” he said. “We keep our space clean. That’s good hygiene.”

“Plan,” Sparks said. “We can use the drips to make art! We will make rain flowers.”

Maya sniffled, then smiled. “Rain flowers?” Sparks nodded. “Yes! Dot the paint. Tap-tap-tap. Then tilt the paper. Let the colors run like rain.” His tail clicked. “We can write labels too. Words help people see our idea.”

Maya dotted red circles. Ben dotted blue and yellow. Sparks tilted the paper gently. The colors slid in soft lines. Drip, drip, trickle. “It looks like petals,” Maya said. “It sounds like tiny rain,” Ben added, listening.

“Now we write,” said Sparks. He pulled out sticky notes. “We can write ‘Flower’ and ‘Rain’ and our names.” Ben wrote F-L-O-W-E-R with Sparks sounding out the letters. “F says fff,” Sparks buzzed. Maya wrote R-A-I-N slowly. “Rrrr, ay, nnn,” she said. Sparks glowed. “Great sounds! Great writing!”

They added more art. Maya stamped green handprints for leaves. Ben made dots with a cotton swab. “Dot-dot-dot!” The dots felt bumpy and looked like seeds. Sparks wiped the table edges, then washed the brushes in warm, soapy water. “Brush bath,” he sang. “Clean tools last longer.”

When they finished, the mural was bright and happy. Rainbow rainflowers danced on the big paper. Each flower had a little label. One said “Flower.” One said “Rain.” Near the sun, Ben wrote “SUN.” Maya wrote “GARDEN.” It smelled like clean soap and a tiny bit like markers. The basement felt calm and proud.

Sparks clapped his metal paws. Ting-ting! “You solved the problem with art,” he said. “You stayed calm. You kept clean. You used writing to share your ideas.” Maya and Ben beamed.

“Thank you, Sparks,” Maya said. Ben patted Sparks’ shiny head. “You make art fun,” he said.

Sparks bowed his robot head. “Art helps us look, listen, and feel. Hygiene keeps us healthy. Writing lets us tell our story. Together, they make magic.”

They washed their hands one more time. Scrub, scrub. Rinse, rinse. Dry, dry. Then they taped the mural on the wall. The colors glowed in the warm basement light.

Maya pointed to the words. “People will know what we made.” Ben nodded. “We turned a spill into a garden.”

Sparks wagged. “That is the art way,” he said. “Make, care, and share.”

The End.
