# Tippy Finds the Sunny Sparkle

Tippy was a gentle deer fawn with soft, speckled fur and bright, curious eyes. One rainy day, pitter-patter drops tapped on the roof. Tippy’s ears perked up. “What is that sound?” she whispered.

She climbed the creaky steps to the quiet attic. The air smelled like old books and pine. Dust floated like tiny snowflakes in the sunlight. “Hello?” Tippy called softly. “I am here to look and learn.”

A tiny squeak answered. “Hi! I’m Dot the mouse,” said a small gray mouse peeking from a box. “What are you doing up here?”

“I want to discover,” said Tippy. “I want to find new things and help if I can.”

Dot’s whiskers twitched. “Me too! I like helping.” Another friend popped up—a yellow canary named Pip. “Tweet! I heard you,” Pip sang. “Let’s explore together.”

Tippy smiled. “Teamwork,” she said. “We can look, listen, and learn.”

They padded and pattered across the wooden floor. The boards felt cool under Tippy’s hooves. Pip fluttered and made a soft breeze. Dot’s tiny feet went tippy-tap, tippy-tap.

Suddenly, Tippy sniffed the air. “Hmm,” she murmured. “I smell something icky.”

Dot sniffed too. “I smell it,” he said, wrinkling his nose.

Pip tilted his head. “Tweet, tweet. I hear a drip.”

They followed the drip, drip, drip to a corner. There sat a big old trunk. The trunk had a rusty lock and dusty lid. A small wet spot shone on the wood. The dust made Tippy sneeze. “Ah-choo!”

“Bless you,” said Dot. “This place needs a tidy.”

Tippy nodded. “First, let’s be safe and clean,” she said. “Hygiene helps us stay healthy.”

She found a little basket nearby with soft cloths and a small brush. “May we use these?” she asked.

Pip peeked into the basket. “Yes! The label says ‘Rags for Cleaning.’”

“Let’s volunteer to help the attic,” Tippy said. “We can wipe and sweep.”

Dot smiled. “We can be helpers for free. That is volunteering.”

They worked together. Tippy wiped the boxes with gentle strokes—swish, swish. Dot swept the floor—scritch, scritch. Pip shook out a cloth—flutter, flutter—and sang a cleaning song. “Wipe, sweep, swish and peep. Keep it clean, tidy neat!”

When they got to the trunk, the drip, drip, drip was louder. Tippy touched the lid. “It’s stuck,” she said. “What is our plan?”

“Teamwork,” Pip chirped. “We solve the problem together.”

“I can push,” said Tippy. “I am strong and careful.”

“I can guide,” said Pip. “I can look from up high.”

“I can wiggle the lock,” said Dot. “My paws are small.”

“Ready?” Tippy asked.

“Ready!” said Dot and Pip.

Tippy pushed. Dot wiggled. Pip watched and called, “A little to the left! Now a tiny lift!” The lid lifted with a soft groan—creeeak! A small splash followed—plip!

Inside the trunk was a cracked jar. Water was dripping from the ceiling onto the jar and out through the crack. That made the icky smell. There were also old soft blankets, a wooden brush, and a shiny magnifying glass.

“Aha,” said Tippy. “We made a discovery! The jar is the problem.”

“Tweet! We can move the jar,” Pip said.

“We can also catch the drip in a bowl,” Dot added.

They found a shallow bowl and slid it under the drip. Plip, plip, plop—the water fell safely into the bowl. Tippy wrapped the cracked jar in a cloth and placed it in a “Fix Me” basket.

Dot brushed the blankets. “These can be washed,” he said.

Tippy held up the magnifying glass. “Look!” she whispered. “When I look through, the dust looks like glitter.”

Pip peered through and giggled. “Sunny sparkle!”

Together, they finished cleaning the corner. The air felt fresher. The floor shone. The drip was quiet now, and the bowl sat safely catching drops. Tippy washed her hooves with a damp cloth. Dot washed his paws. Pip cleaned his beak. “Hygiene helps,” Tippy said. “We clean up after we help.”

They sat by the bright attic window. The rain slowed. A gentle light filled the room. Tippy’s heart felt warm. “We discovered a problem,” she said. “We used teamwork to solve it. We volunteered to help the attic.”

Dot nodded. “We learned to listen, look, and sniff. We learned to be clean.”

Pip sang, “And we found the sunny sparkle!”

Tippy smiled at her friends. “Discovery is like a treasure,” she said softly. “When we explore with care, we find new things. We help others. We keep places clean.”

Dot squeaked, “Let’s tell others to look, learn, and help too.”

Pip chirped, “Yes! Little helpers can make big changes.”

Tippy stood and gave a happy swish of her tail. “Next time, we can make signs,” she said. “They can say, ‘Please keep the attic tidy.’ That is volunteering too.”

They all looked around the quiet attic—now brighter, cleaner, and calm. The pitter-patter had stopped. A soft sunbeam fell on the bowl and made the water sparkle like stars. Tippy whispered, “Thank you, team.”

“Thank you, Tippy,” said Dot.

“Tweet-tweet, team!” sang Pip.

And the attic, clean and still, seemed to smile right back.

The End.
