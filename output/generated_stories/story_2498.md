# Benny Beaver's Helping Heart

Benny Beaver was staying in Grandma's cozy guest room for the weekend. The room smelled like lavender and had soft, fluffy pillows everywhere. Benny loved the way the morning sunshine made pretty patterns on the wooden floor through the lace curtains.

"Good morning, Benny!" called Grandma from downstairs. "Would you like to help me today?"

Benny's tail went *thump-thump* with excitement. He loved helping! "Yes, yes, yes!" he squeaked, rushing down the creaky stairs.

In the kitchen, Grandma was making sandwiches. "Mrs. Rabbit next door is feeling sick," she explained. "I thought we could bring her some soup and help tidy her garden."

Benny's eyes sparkled. "I can help! I'm very good at building and fixing things!"

They walked to Mrs. Rabbit's house, carrying warm soup that smelled like carrots and herbs. Mrs. Rabbit opened the door slowly. She had droopy ears and looked very tired.

"Oh my," she sniffled. "You're so kind to visit."

"Benny wants to help in your garden," Grandma said gently.

Mrs. Rabbit's garden was a mess! Weeds grew everywhere, and her fence had fallen down. Benny rolled up his sleeves. "I'll fix everything!" he declared.

But after an hour of pulling weeds, Benny's paws were sore. The sun felt very hot on his fuzzy head. When he tried to lift a heavy fence post, it was too big for his small paws.

"This is too hard," Benny whispered, feeling frustrated. His tail drooped.

Grandma sat beside him on the soft grass. "Benny, helping others is wonderful, but it's okay to say when something is too much for you."

"But I want to help Mrs. Rabbit feel better," Benny said sadly.

"You already are helping," Grandma smiled. "Look around."

Benny looked. He had pulled many weeds from the flower bed. The colorful flowers - red roses and yellow daisies - could now peek through! Mrs. Rabbit's walkway was clear and clean.

"You've done so much already," Grandma said. "And it's brave to know when to ask for help or take a break."

Just then, Mr. Fox and Ms. Squirrel appeared with tools. "We heard Mrs. Rabbit needed help!" they called out.

"I can show you which weeds to pull!" Benny said excitedly. "And I know where the fence posts go!"

Working together, they finished the garden quickly. Mr. Fox lifted the heavy posts while Ms. Squirrel organized the tools. Benny directed everyone and pulled the smaller weeds.

Mrs. Rabbit came outside with glasses of cool lemonade. "Thank you all so much!" she said, her ears perky again. "Benny, you were such a good helper and leader!"

Back in the guest room that evening, Benny felt proud and happy. The soft pillows felt extra comfortable as he snuggled in.

"Grandma," he said sleepily, "I learned that helping feels good, but I don't have to do everything by myself."

"That's right, dear one," Grandma said, tucking him in. "Volunteering means giving what you can, asking for help when you need it, and knowing your limits. You showed great courage today."

Benny smiled as he drifted off to sleep, dreaming of all the ways he could help others tomorrow - with friends by his side.

The End.
