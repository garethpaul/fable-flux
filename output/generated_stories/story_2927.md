# Olive Owl and the Big Garden Plan

In a cozy garden shed at the end of a sunny yard, a wise old owl named Olive liked to read and write. The shed smelled like fresh wood and sweet soil. Dust danced in warm sunbeams. Little jars clicked when the breeze moved the shelves.

Olive’s feathers were soft and gray. Her eyes were gentle and bright. She wore tiny round glasses. She kept a small notebook and a short blue pencil tucked under her wing.

“Good morning,” Olive hooted softly. “Today feels like a thinking day.”

Just then, a chatter of voices came from outside the shed door.

“I want the tall bed!” chirped Kiki, a tiny canary. “I need room to fly up!”

“No, I want the tall bed,” buzzed Baz the Bee. “The tall bed has the sun I like!”

“I want it too,” squeaked Pip the field mouse. “I can sit on the edge and crunch seeds.”

Everyone squeezed into the shed. The floor felt cool under Pip’s paws. Baz’s wings hummed like a soft zipper. Kiki’s yellow feathers shimmered. But their faces looked tight and worried.

Olive blinked kindly. “Hello, friends. What is the trouble?”

Kiki fluffed her feathers. “We all want the same garden bed. We all want the tall one with the bright sun.”

Baz buzzed, “I need sun for my flowers.”

Pip wrung his tiny tail. “I want a sunny place to draw and snack.”

Olive nodded slowly. “I hear you. You all want the sunny bed. Hoo-hoo. This is a problem.”

The shed got very quiet. Olive could hear the soft tick-tick of rain starting on the roof. It smelled like wet leaves and clean air.

Olive opened her notebook. “When there is a problem,” she said, “we can use a plan. A plan is a strategy. We listen. We share. We write. Then we choose together.”

Kiki tilted her head. “We write?”

“Yes,” Olive said. “When we write, we slow down. We think. We see everyone’s needs.”

Olive drew three little boxes in her notebook. “Let’s write what each friend needs.”

Baz buzzed closer. “I need flowers and sun for nectar,” he said softly. “I share that nectar when I visit blooms all over the yard.”

Olive wrote: Baz: sun, flowers, nectar, sharing.

Kiki chirped, “I need space to practice flying up and down. I sing for everyone in the morning.”

Olive wrote: Kiki: space to fly, sing for all.

Pip sniffed the air. “I need a cozy spot to sit and draw pictures. I can make signs to help everyone find things in the garden.”

Olive wrote: Pip: cozy seat, draw signs.

Olive showed the page. “You all want to use the garden for good things. This garden is for everyone. The world is like a big garden too. We are neighbors, even if we are different. That is called being global citizens.”

“Global what?” Pip asked.

“Global citizens,” Olive said gently. “It means we think about others. We share the earth. We help, not hurt. We solve problems together.”

Kiki blinked. “How do we do that?”

“With listening and a fair plan,” Olive hooted. “Let’s make some choices.”

Olive drew a map of the garden beds. She shaded one tall bed yellow for sun. She shaded a lower bed with light green for soft shade.

“Here are ideas,” Olive said. “Idea one: take turns each day in the tall bed. Idea two: share the tall bed in parts. Idea three: move things to make more than one sunny spot.”

Baz buzzed in a circle. “I like sharing parts. I can have the row with flowers.”

Kiki fluttered. “I can have a small flying lane. I do not need the whole bed.”

Pip squeaked, “I can set a little stool on the edge. I can draw and make signs.”

Olive smiled. “Let’s write our plan so we remember.”

She wrote in big neat letters:

Our Garden Plan
- Baz cares for the flower row in the tall bed.
- Kiki uses a flying lane for practice songs.
- Pip sits on a stool at the edge to draw garden signs.
- We all say, “May I?” before using the space.
- We switch places on rainy days to share light and shade.

“Do we all agree?” Olive asked.

Baz buzzed, “Yes!”

Kiki chirped, “Yes!”

Pip nodded, “Yes!”

Olive added a last line: We are global citizens. We share, we listen, we care.

The rain slowed. The shed felt warm and bright again. They took the plan outside. Pip brushed his paw over a smooth board and drew a big friendly sign: Our Garden Plan — Welcome, Everyone! He drew a tiny bee, a little bird, and a happy mouse holding hands—well, paws and wings.

Baz hummed over lavender and said, “Thank you for the lane, Kiki.”

Kiki sang a sunny note. “Thank you for the flowers, Baz.”

Pip held up another sign. “Thank you, Olive. You helped us write.”

Olive’s eyes shone. “You helped each other. That is the best part.”

They all worked together. Baz visited blooms and shared nectar. Kiki swooped and sang soft happy songs. Pip made signs that said Please, Thank You, and May I? The garden felt full of friendly sounds: buzz-buzz, chirp-chirp, scratch-scratch, and Olive’s gentle hoot.

That night, the garden shed smelled like dry hay and sweet tea. Olive turned a page in her notebook and wrote, Today we used a strategy. We listened. We shared. We cared for our garden like we care for our world.

She looked at the plans on the wall and smiled. “When we think of others,” she whispered, “we all fit.”

The End.
