# Squeaky the Helper Squirrel

Squeaky was a small, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail. He lived in a playful living room full of colorful pillows, soft rugs, and a big window where sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Toys made gentle clacks and clinks when they were moved. The air smelled like fresh crayons and clean laundry. It was a happy place.

One morning, Squeaky heard a tiny sniffle. “Sniff, sniff.” He perked up his ears. “Who needs help?” he asked kindly.

From under a blanket, a little stuffed rabbit whispered, “I feel lost. I can’t find the book about rainbows.”

Squeaky’s heart felt soft and warm. “I can help,” he said. “I like helping. Helping is called volunteering. Volunteering means I choose to help, just because it’s kind.”

He shook his tail and looked around. “Let’s use our words and make a plan,” Squeaky said. “Good communication helps us help better.”

“Okay,” said the rabbit. “I need the rainbow book.”

“Great,” Squeaky replied. “What do we know? The book is colorful. It might be near other books. How do we feel?”

“I feel worried,” the rabbit said. “My tummy feels twisty.”

Squeaky nodded. “Thank you for sharing your feelings. I feel brave and helpful. Let’s breathe.” They took a slow breath in, and a slow breath out. “We can do this together.”

Squeaky scampered to a shelf. He listened. He smelled the sweet paper smell. He touched the smooth covers. “Not here,” he said. He peeked behind a cushion. “Not here, either.” He heard a clatter—clack, clack—from a toy car. “Excuse me, car,” Squeaky said. “Have you seen a rainbow book?”

The toy car beeped cheerfully, “Beep! Check the cozy rug!”

Squeaky nodded. “Thank you for the idea. That is good communication. We listen and share.” He hopped to the cozy rug and lifted a corner. No book. But he found a tiny globe ball, blue and green like Earth.

He held the globe gently. “We live in a big world, and we all can help,” Squeaky said. “People in many places volunteer. Helping is something everyone can do.”

The rabbit peeked out. “Do you think I can help too?”

“Yes,” said Squeaky. “You can use your voice. Let’s call to the book. Sometimes talking helps us think.”

Together they called softly, “Rainbow book, where are you?”

They listened. Only the clock ticked—tick, tock. Squeaky’s tummy felt a little wiggly now. “I feel a bit stuck,” he said. “But I won’t give up. I am smart and strong.” He patted his chest. His self-esteem grew like a small, brave light.

He decided to try a new plan. “Let’s clean as we look. Volunteering can be tidying, too. We can make the room comfy for everyone.”

“Okay,” said the rabbit.

Squeaky began to stack blocks into a neat tower—click, clack. He placed crayons in a cup—tap, tap. He folded a blanket—soft and warm under his paws. As he tidied, the room looked clearer. The sunlight made the rug look extra bright.

A friendly teddy bear waved. “Hi, Squeaky! What are you doing?”

“I’m volunteering,” Squeaky said proudly. “I’m helping our living room and looking for the rainbow book. Would you like to help?”

“Yes,” said the teddy. “I can lift big pillows.”

“Thank you,” Squeaky said. “Let’s share our plan. Teddy, please check under the big pillows. Rabbit, please look in the little basket. I will check by the window. We will tell each other what we find.”

They started. Teddy huffed and puffed and lifted a pillow—whoosh! “Not here,” Teddy said.

Rabbit peered into the basket. “I found a shiny sticker sheet,” Rabbit said. “But no book.”

Squeaky moved to the sunny window. He felt warm sun on his fur. He heard a gentle bird chirp outside. He sat on his back paws and looked behind the curtains. “Aha!” he cried. “I see a corner of something colorful!”

Squeaky tugged carefully—swish, swish. Out came the rainbow book! Its cover was bright red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple. “We did it!” Squeaky cheered. “Teamwork and talking helped us.”

Rabbit’s eyes sparkled. “Thank you, Squeaky! Thank you, Teddy!”

“You’re welcome,” said Squeaky. “When we volunteer, we help our home, our friends, and our whole world. Helping makes hearts feel good.”

Rabbit hugged the book. “My tummy is not twisty now. I feel calm.”

Teddy smiled. “I feel strong.”

Squeaky’s chest felt warm and proud. “I feel helpful. I can choose to help again. Maybe tomorrow we can volunteer to sort crayons by color. We can also share toys with friends who visit. People around the world help in many ways. We can help right here.”

They all sat together on the soft rug. Squeaky opened the book. The pages smelled like fresh paper. “Let’s read,” he said. “After we read, we can put the book back on the shelf. That is part of helping, too.”

They read about bright rainbows and gentle rain. They talked about colors and feelings. They listened and took turns. They smiled at each other.

When the story ended, Squeaky carefully placed the rainbow book on the shelf. He turned to his friends. “Today we learned to use our words, to share our feelings, and to help without being asked. That is volunteering. Helping together makes us strong, kind, and proud.”

Rabbit and Teddy clapped—clap, clap. The living room felt extra cozy and bright.

The End.
