# Gigi the Giraffe Takes a Calm Breath

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with kind eyes and long, gentle legs. She lived next to a sunny backyard with soft, green grass and bright marigolds. The air smelled like oranges from a nearby tree. Bees hummed. A warm breeze tickled Gigi’s ears.

Every morning, Gigi visited the backyard to stretch her neck and say hello to her friends. Today, two friends were there: Pip the puppy and Dot the duckling. They were playing with a red bouncy ball.

“Good morning!” Gigi said, smiling.

“Hi, Gigi!” Pip wagged his tail so fast it swooshed the grass. “We’re playing ball!”

“Quack! My turn!” Dot flapped her little wings. “I want to bounce it high!”

Pip grabbed the ball. Dot waddled closer. Their noses bumped. The ball rolled to Gigi’s hooves.

Pip barked, “It’s mine!”

Dot quacked, “No, it’s mine!”

Gigi felt the air tighten, like a string pulled too hard. The sunny backyard didn’t feel cozy anymore. Pip’s ears drooped. Dot’s feathers puffed. A little storm was blowing in their hearts.

Gigi took a slow breath in through her nose. In… one, two, three. Then a slow breath out. Out… one, two, three. She put a hoof on her chest. Calm spread like warm tea.

She remembered her own rule: Stop. Breathe. Think. Speak kindly.

“Friends,” Gigi said softly, “I see big feelings.”

Pip huffed. “I had it first!”

Dot stamped a tiny foot. “I didn’t get a turn!”

Gigi nodded. “Thank you for telling me. Your feelings matter.” She bent her long neck so her face met theirs. “Let’s use our calm tools.”

“What’s a calm tool?” Pip asked, tilting his head.

“Watch,” Gigi said. “Let’s do Balloon Belly. Hands on tummies.” Pip’s paw and Dot’s wing rested on their bellies. “Breathe in and make your belly big like a balloon.” They breathed in. “Now breathe out and make your belly soft.” They breathed out. The red ball waited quietly by Gigi’s hoof.

Pip’s tail slowed. Dot’s feathers smoothed.

Gigi smiled. “Now we can use our talking tools. We can say, ‘I feel… I need…’”

Dot looked at Pip. “I feel left out. I need a turn.”

Pip blinked. “I feel excited. I need to play, too.”

Gigi clapped her hooves softly. “You both did great. How can we solve this together?”

Pip sniffed the warm air. “Maybe… we can take turns?”

Dot nodded. “And count to five!”

Gigi beamed. “That’s a smart plan. I can be the counter. We’ll keep the game fair.”

The sun shone brighter. The bees hummed again, like music for their plan.

Gigi picked up the ball with her mouth and set it between Pip and Dot. “Pip first. Ready? One… two… three… four… five!”

Pip bounced the ball. It boinged on the grass with a happy thump. He giggled and passed it to Dot.

“Dot’s turn,” Gigi said. “One… two… three… four… five!”

Dot tapped the ball with her little foot. Boing! It bounced low and sweet. She laughed and rolled it back to Pip.

They played and counted. They cheered. The ball made soft bumps and boings on the ground. The orange tree leaves whispered shhh-shhh in the breeze.

After a while, Pip forgot and held the ball a little too long. Dot’s beak drooped. Gigi noticed.

“Pause,” Gigi said kindly. She touched the grass with her hoof and took a breath. “Let’s check our plan.”

Pip looked at the ball, then at Dot. “Oh. I kept it too long. I’m sorry, Dot.”

Dot’s eyes brightened. “Thank you, Pip.”

Gigi nodded. “Saying sorry helps. So does fixing the mistake. Pip, what can you do now?”

Pip rolled the ball to Dot. “You can have two turns.”

Dot quacked with joy. “Thank you!” She bounced it once, twice, and passed it back.

The game felt smooth again, like a song with a steady beat.

When they got a little tired, Gigi said, “Water break!” They sipped cool water from a bowl. The water tasted crisp. The sun felt warm on their backs. Their bodies felt happy.

Pip licked his nose. “Gigi, you helped us. How did you know what to do?”

Gigi tapped her chest. “I used self-regulation. That means I noticed my feelings, I breathed, and I chose kind words.”

Dot tilted her head. “And we helped, too, with our plan.”

“Yes,” Gigi said. “We solved the problem together. That is conflict-resolution. When we disagree, we listen, we breathe, and we make a fair plan.”

Pip wagged. “And we can ask for help.”

Gigi looked around the sunny backyard. “We also took care of our bodies—drinks, rest, and gentle breaths. That is wellness. It keeps our minds and hearts calm.”

The bees hummed a friendly goodbye. The marigolds smelled peppery and sweet. The orange tree rustled softly.

Pip and Dot stood close to Gigi. “Thank you, Gigi,” they said.

Gigi nuzzled them both. “Thank you for trying. You are brave and kind.”

They stacked their paws, wings, and hoof. “Team Calm!” they cheered.

Gigi looked up at the bright blue sky. “Today, we learned a big lesson: When we have big feelings and a problem to solve, we can stop, breathe, use kind words, and make a plan we all like.”

Pip barked happily. Dot quacked along. The backyard felt sunny again—inside and out.

The End.
