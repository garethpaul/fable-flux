# Spark's Honest Adventure

Spark the robot dog wagged his metal tail. His blue lights blinked happily. He climbed up the wooden ladder to Emma's treehouse. Click, click, click went his paws on each step.

"Hi Emma!" Spark barked with his cheerful robot voice. "What are you doing?"

Emma sat on the soft cushions. She held her favorite storybook. "I'm reading about brave knights," she said. "But I lost my bookmark. Now I can't find my place."

Spark's eyes glowed brighter. "I can help you find it!" He loved helping Emma. His nose scanner beeped as he looked around the treehouse.

Under the window, Spark saw something shiny. It was Emma's special butterfly bookmark! But oh no - he accidentally stepped on it. CRUNCH! The pretty wings broke off.

Spark's lights turned dim. His circuits felt all mixed up. Should he tell Emma what happened? Or should he hide the broken bookmark?

"I don't want Emma to be sad," Spark whispered to himself. He quickly pushed the broken pieces under a pillow. "Maybe she won't notice."

"Did you find my bookmark, Spark?" Emma asked with hopeful eyes.

Spark's tail stopped wagging. "Um... no. I didn't see it anywhere." The words made his speaker crackle. Lying felt yucky in his circuits.

Emma looked everywhere. She moved pillows and books. She checked every corner of the treehouse. Her eyes got watery. "It was from my grandma," she sniffled. "It was very special to me."

Spark felt terrible. His happy beeps turned into sad whirs. Emma was crying because of him. The broken bookmark pieces felt heavy in his memory banks.

"Emma," Spark said softly. His voice shook a little. "I need to tell you something important."

Emma wiped her eyes. "What is it, Spark?"

"I found your bookmark," Spark said slowly. "But I broke it by accident. I was scared to tell you. I hid the pieces under the pillow."

Emma reached under the pillow. She found the broken butterfly wings. For a moment, she looked very sad. Then she looked at Spark's dim, worried lights.

"Spark," Emma said gently, "thank you for telling me the truth. That was very brave."

"But I broke your special bookmark," Spark whimpered. "And I lied about it first."

Emma petted Spark's smooth metal head. "Everyone makes mistakes, Spark. But you told me the truth. That's what good friends do."

Spark's lights began to glow again. "I'm really, really sorry, Emma. Can I help fix it?"

Emma smiled. "Let's try together!"

They got Emma's art supplies. Carefully, they glued the wings back on. Emma added colorful stickers to make it even prettier. The bookmark looked different now, but still beautiful.

"It's like a new story," Emma said, holding up their creation. "Sometimes broken things become even more special when we fix them with love."

Spark's tail wagged fast. His happy beeps filled the treehouse. "Thank you for forgiving me, Emma. I learned something important today."

"What did you learn?" Emma asked.

"Being honest feels much better than hiding the truth," Spark said. "Even when it's scary to tell."

Emma hugged her robot friend. "And I learned that mistakes don't break friendships. Honesty makes them stronger."

They spent the rest of the afternoon reading stories together. The fixed bookmark held their place perfectly. And Spark's circuits felt warm and happy, knowing that telling the truth was always the right choice.

The End.
