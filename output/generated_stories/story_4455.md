# Oliver's Art Room Solution

The morning light streamed through the big windows of the colorful art room. Wise old Oliver Owl perched on his favorite wooden easel, watching the children arrive. His soft brown feathers rustled as he stretched his wings.

"Good morning, little artists!" Oliver hooted warmly. His big yellow eyes sparkled with kindness.

Soon, the art room buzzed with happy voices. Red paint splashed on paper. Blue crayons rolled across tables. The sweet smell of glue filled the air.

But then Oliver heard something different. Angry voices from the corner table.

"That's MY purple crayon!" shouted little Emma, her face turning red like a strawberry.

"No, I had it first!" cried Ben, holding the crayon tight in his small fist.

The other children stopped painting. The room grew quiet except for sniffles and upset voices.

Oliver's heart felt heavy. His art room was usually filled with joy, not fighting. He fluffed his feathers and flew down to the corner table with a gentle whoosh.

"Oh my," Oliver said softly, landing between the two children. "What seems to be the trouble?"

Emma's eyes filled with tears. "I was making a purple butterfly, but Ben took my crayon!"

Ben's lip trembled. "But I need purple for my grape picture! I was using it!"

Oliver nodded his wise head slowly. He had seen many problems like this before. "I understand," he said gently. "You both need purple, and there's only one purple crayon."

The children looked at Oliver with hopeful eyes. Surely the wise owl would know what to do.

Oliver thought carefully, tapping his orange beak with one wing. "Tell me, what makes purple so special for your pictures?"

"My butterfly needs purple wings to be beautiful," Emma whispered.

"My grapes can't be grapes without purple," Ben said quietly.

"Both very important!" Oliver agreed. "Let me show you something wonderful."

Oliver waddled to the supply cabinet. His claws clicked softly on the floor. He pulled out a small box with his beak.

"Look here," he said, opening the box. Inside were many purple crayons, purple markers, and purple paint. "We have enough purple for everyone!"

The children's eyes grew wide like full moons.

"But more importantly," Oliver continued, "let's talk about sharing. When we have a problem, we can solve it together instead of fighting."

Oliver showed them how to take turns. "Emma, you use the purple crayon for five minutes. Ben, you can use these purple markers. Then you switch!"

Emma clapped her hands. "That's a great idea!"

Ben smiled big. "We can both make beautiful purple things!"

Oliver watched as the children began working peacefully. Emma carefully colored her butterfly wings. Ben painted juicy purple grapes. The art room filled with happy sounds again.

"Mr. Oliver," Emma said, holding up her finished butterfly. "Thank you for helping us solve our problem!"

"Yeah!" Ben added, showing his grape painting. "Talking is better than fighting!"

Oliver's chest puffed with pride. His feathers felt warm and fluffy. "You're both very smart," he hooted. "Remember, when you have a problem, you can always talk about it. There's usually enough for everyone when we work together."

The other children gathered around to see the beautiful purple artwork. Soon everyone was sharing crayons and helping each other.

As the sun set through the art room windows, Oliver perched back on his easel. The room sparkled with colorful pictures and happy children.

"Every problem has a solution," Oliver whispered to himself, "when we listen, share, and care for each other."

The End.
