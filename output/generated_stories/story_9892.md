# The Panda’s Quiet Pencil

Poppy was a patient panda who loved her cozy treehouse. The treehouse smelled like fresh bamboo and sweet pine. Sunlight made warm patches on the floor. A gentle breeze whispered, “Shhh,” through the leaves.

Today, Poppy had a plan. “I will write a story,” she said softly, patting a smooth, yellow pencil. Her notebook felt cool and bumpy under her paw. “I will write about a brave leaf.”

Poppy took a deep breath in. She liked to breathe in, breathe out, and feel calm. “I can do this, slowly,” she said. “Resilience means I try again when it’s hard.”

Tap-tap. Friends climbed up the ladder. Tiko the tiny turtle peeked in. Bella the bluebird fluttered near the window. Gus the giggly goat poked his head through the door.

“Hi, Poppy!” they sang.

“Hi, friends,” Poppy said with a smile. “I am writing a story. Would you like to join me in the treehouse?”

“Yes!” said Tiko, blinking slowly.

“Yes!” chirped Bella, wings rustling like paper.

“Yes!” bleated Gus, tiny hooves clacking on the wooden floor.

Poppy smiled. “We can be quiet and kind while I write. And then I can share.”

“Okay,” said Tiko.

“I can sing a tiny song,” Bella whispered.

“I can draw a picture,” said Gus, nodding hard.

Poppy pressed the pencil to the page. Scratch-scratch. She wrote: “Once there was a brave leaf who held on in the wind.”

WHOOSH! A big gust came through the window. It tickled Poppy’s ears and made the curtains flap. The pencil rolled off the table. Clack!

“Oh no,” said Poppy. She picked it up and smiled. “It’s okay. I will try again.”

Poppy wrote another line. Scratch-scratch. “The leaf shook, but it did not give up.”

“Look!” cried Gus, hopping. His hooves thumped. Thump-thump!

The pencil wobbled and made a zigzag line across the page. Poppy’s eyes grew wide. Her tummy felt tight. Her heart went boom-boom.

She put a paw on her heart. “I breathe in. I breathe out,” she said.

“I’m sorry,” said Gus, ears drooping. “I got excited.”

“Thank you for telling me,” said Poppy gently. “That was respectful. Let’s try something peaceful. Could you draw on this soft mat? It won’t shake the table.”

Gus nodded. “I can do that!”

Poppy smiled and wrote again. Scratch-scratch.

Bella started to sing. “La-la-la,” she chirped, sweet and light. It sounded like a bell. But then her song grew louder. LA-LA-LA!

Poppy’s pencil slipped again. The line swooped like a roller coaster. Poppy’s paws felt warm. Her cheeks felt hot.

She sat tall and spoke calmly. “Bella, I like your song. It is pretty. But I need a quiet time to write. Can you sing softly, like a whisper breeze?”

Bella blinked and tucked her wings close. “Yes, Poppy,” she whispered. “I can sing soft.” She hummed like a sleepy bee. Mmm-mmm.

Poppy smiled and tried again. Scratch-scratch. “The brave leaf whispered to the tree, ‘I am trying my best.’”

Tiko scooted closer to see. His shell made a gentle scrape on the floor, like sand. “Can I help?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Poppy. “Could you hold the paper steady with your careful turtle paws?”

Tiko stretched his little legs and held the page very still. “Like this?” he asked.

“Perfect,” said Poppy.

Suddenly—plop! A little drop of sap fell from the ceiling. It landed right on Poppy’s page. Sticky! The page smelled sweet and piney.

Poppy blinked. Her story had a sticky dot. She could stop. She could sigh. She could say, “This is too hard.”

But Poppy touched the dot and smiled. “Resilience,” she whispered. “I keep going.”

She drew a round circle around the sap spot. “Now it is the sun!” she said. “The brave leaf is in the sunshine.”

Her friends clapped softly. Clap-clap.

Poppy wrote more. Scratch-scratch. “The wind blew. WHOOSH. The leaf wobbled. But the leaf said, ‘I can breathe. I can hold on.’”

She paused. “Friends, what should the tree say back?”

Tiko smiled. “The tree says, ‘I am here.’”

Bella hummed, “The tree says, ‘You are not alone.’”

Gus added, “The tree says, ‘We can do hard things… together!’”

Poppy’s eyes shone. “That is peaceful and kind.” She wrote the words. Scratch-scratch.

At last, Poppy put down her pencil. “I finished,” she said. “Would you like to hear it?”

“Yes!” her friends said.

Poppy read in a warm, soft voice. “Once there was a brave leaf who held on in the wind. The leaf wobbled and wiggled, but did not give up. The tree whispered, ‘I am here. You are not alone. We can do hard things together.’ And the sun peeked out. The leaf felt strong and safe.”

Her friends sighed happily.

“I learned something,” said Poppy, closing the notebook. “When things are hard, I can breathe. I can ask for help. I can speak kindly. I can try again.”

Gus nodded. “I learned to be gentle in the treehouse.”

Bella smiled. “I learned to sing soft when friends are busy.”

Tiko patted the page. “I learned I can help by being steady.”

Poppy hugged her friends. The treehouse felt warm and peaceful. The wind was soft. The curtains swayed like slow waves. Poppy looked at her pencil and grinned. “We did it,” she said. “That is resilience.”

The End.
