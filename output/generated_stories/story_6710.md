# Daisy's Art Adventure

Daisy the deer fawn loved to explore. Her soft brown spots sparkled in the morning sun as she wandered around the farm. Today, something new caught her big, curious eyes - an open garage door!

"What's in there?" Daisy wondered aloud, her small hooves clicking on the concrete floor.

Inside the garage workshop, she saw amazing things everywhere! Colorful paint brushes hung on hooks. Jars of bright paint sat on shelves - red like strawberries, blue like the sky, and yellow like sunshine. Canvas boards leaned against the wall, and the air smelled like fresh wood and creativity.

"Oh my!" Daisy gasped. "I want to make art too!"

Without thinking, Daisy rushed toward the paint table. Her excitement made her heart beat fast like a drum. She knocked over a jar of blue paint with her nose. SPLASH! The paint spilled everywhere.

"Oops!" she cried, watching the blue paint drip onto the floor. Her ears drooped with worry.

Just then, Mrs. Chen, the kind artist who owned the workshop, appeared. "Hello there, little one," she said gently. "I see you're interested in art!"

Daisy's cheeks felt warm with embarrassment. "I'm sorry! I made a big mess. I just got so excited about all the pretty colors."

Mrs. Chen smiled. "It's okay, Daisy. Sometimes when we get very excited, we need to take a deep breath and think before we act. Would you like to learn how?"

Daisy nodded eagerly, her tail wiggling.

"First, let's clean up together," Mrs. Chen said, handing Daisy a soft cloth. "When we make mistakes, we fix them."

As they wiped up the blue paint, Mrs. Chen taught Daisy a special trick. "When you feel very excited or upset, try this: Stop, take three deep breaths, and think about what to do next. Like this - breathe in slowly... and out slowly."

Daisy tried it. "Stop... breathe in... breathe out... think," she repeated. The worried feeling in her tummy started to go away.

"Much better!" Mrs. Chen said. "Now, would you like to make real art the safe way?"

"Yes, please!" Daisy said, remembering to use her calm voice.

Mrs. Chen showed Daisy how to dip a big, soft brush in paint. "See? We hold it gently and move it slowly across the paper."

Daisy practiced the breathing trick again before she started. Stop. Breathe. Think. Then she carefully dipped the brush in yellow paint and made a big, happy sun on her canvas.

"Look! I'm making sunshine!" Daisy said proudly, adding orange rays around her sun.

"Beautiful!" Mrs. Chen said. "You're using your thinking brain now instead of just your excited feelings."

Daisy painted fluffy white clouds and green grass. Each time she felt excited and wanted to rush, she remembered: Stop, breathe, think. Her painting turned out wonderful!

"I learned something important today," Daisy said, stepping back to admire her artwork. "When I feel big feelings, I can stop and breathe before I act."

"That's called self-control," Mrs. Chen explained. "It helps us make good choices."

Daisy hugged her new painting carefully. "Thank you for teaching me! Now I can make art and keep everyone safe too."

From that day on, whenever Daisy felt excited, worried, or upset, she remembered her special trick: Stop, breathe, and think. And her art became more beautiful than ever!

The End.
