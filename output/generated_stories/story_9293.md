# Gigi the Giraffe and the Safe Home Office

Gigi the giraffe was very thoughtful. She had big gentle eyes and a long, long neck. Gigi loved to sit in her cozy home office. The room smelled like warm paper and lemon cleaner. The window was open, and she could feel a soft, cool breeze on her cheeks. Her chair was soft and squeaky. Her desk was neat with crayons in a cup, sticky notes in a stack, and a little bell that went “ding!”

“Today I will make a Safety List,” Gigi said. “Safety keeps us happy. Safety keeps us strong.”

She took a deep breath. She clicked her pen. Click, click.

Just then, Gigi heard a buzzzz. Her friend BeeBee the bee zipped past the window. “Hi, Gigi!” buzzed BeeBee. “I’m going to play tag in your office!”

Gigi looked around. She saw a long wire on the floor. She saw a hot cup of tea on the edge of the desk. She saw the window screen shaking in the breeze.

Gigi’s heart said, Be careful.

“Wait, BeeBee,” Gigi said gently. “My office is not safe for tag.”

“Why not?” buzzed BeeBee. “I am fast! I am brave!”

“Fast and brave is good,” said Gigi, “but safe is best.”

Gigi pointed with a soft hoof. “See that wire? Someone could trip and bonk. See the hot tea? Someone could spill and burn. See the screen? It could pop out. We must fix these things.”

BeeBee frowned. “But I want to play now.”

Gigi smiled. “We can play after we make it safe. That is fair and kind for everyone.”

BeeBee buzzed in a circle. “Hmm. Okay. I can help!”

Gigi stood up slowly. Her chair squeaked again. Squeak! She made a plan.

Step one: the wire. Gigi picked up the wire. It felt smooth and twisty. She taped it along the wall, sticky-tack by sticky-tack. “No more tripping,” she said.

Step two: the hot tea. Gigi felt the steam on her nose. “Hot,” she said. She moved the cup to the back of the desk and used a lid. “Hot things go back and need a lid. That keeps us safe.”

Step three: the window screen. Gigi checked the corners. Wiggle, wiggle. It was loose. “This is not safe,” she said. “I will close the window and call for help.”

She rang the bell. Ding! Her neighbor Fix-It Fox heard and trotted over with a tool belt that went clink, clink. “How can I help?” asked Fix-It Fox.

“The screen is loose,” said Gigi. “We need it snug to keep little ones safe.”

Fix-It Fox nodded. “Good eye, Gigi. Safety first.” He tightened the screen. Twist, twist. Snap! “All set,” he said. “Now the breeze can blow, and no one will fall.”

“Thank you,” said Gigi. “That was kind and just. Everyone deserves a safe place.”

BeeBee buzzed low. “Can we play now?”

Gigi looked around. The room felt calm. The lemon smell was sweet. The air was cool and gentle. The wire was tucked away. The tea was safe. The window was snug.

“Almost,” said Gigi. “We must make rules. Rules help us be fair. Rules help us be safe.”

Gigi wrote with her pen. Click, click.

Office Safety Rules:
- We walk, not run.
- We keep hot drinks back and with a lid.
- We put wires away.
- We ask for help with tools and windows.
- We clean up when we are done.

BeeBee read the rules. “These are good rules,” she buzzed. “I can follow them. But what if someone does not?”

Gigi nodded. “Then we remind them kindly. We help them learn. That is justice. It is fair and it is kind.”

BeeBee smiled. “I will help too.”

They played a gentle game of “Find the Sticky Note.” BeeBee buzzed to a note. “Found one!” She landed softly. No running, no tripping. Gigi clapped her hooves. Clap, clap. The bell went ding! When they were done, they put the notes back in a tidy stack.

The next day, a little mouse named Pip peeked in. “Can I color?” Pip asked.

“Yes,” said Gigi, “but we follow the rules.”

Pip nodded and sat. He colored with a purple crayon. Scritch, scritch. Then Pip reached for the hot tea.

Gigi held up a hoof. “Stop, please. Hot things can burn.”

Pip blinked. “Oh! I forgot.”

“That’s okay,” said Gigi kindly. “We try again. We keep trying.” Gigi showed Pip the lid and the safe spot. Pip smiled. “Thank you for keeping me safe.”

Every day, Gigi checked the room. She checked the wires. She checked the window. She checked the cups. Sometimes she found a new problem. She did not give up. She worked and asked for help. She was patient and strong. That is persistence.

Soon, all the friends knew the rules. They walked. They cleaned up. They asked for help. The home office was bright and happy. It smelled like lemon and paper and a little like crayons.

BeeBee buzzed by and said, “Gigi, your office is fun and safe!”

Gigi’s heart felt warm. “Safety keeps us happy. Safety keeps us strong. Being fair and kind keeps everyone safe.”

Lesson: We keep trying, we follow rules, and we help each other. That is safety. That is justice. That is how we play and learn every day.

The End.
