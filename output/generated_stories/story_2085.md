# Ollie Otter’s Guest Room Rescue

Ollie the playful otter wiggled through the doorway into the guest room. His whiskers twitched. The room smelled like clean sheets and lemon polish. The carpet felt soft under his webby feet. A little window let in a square of sunshine, warm and bright on the floor.

“Hello, guest room!” Ollie chirped. “Let’s have a tidy-up adventure!”

He loved adventures, even small ones. He looked around. There was a comfy bed with a fluffy blue blanket. There were two big baskets by the wall. One basket had a picture of a bottle on it. The other had a picture of an apple core. A little sign said, “Recycling” with a happy smiley face.

On the desk, Ollie saw crinkly paper, shiny cans, and a plastic bottle. He also saw a little doll with one arm bent, a sock with a hole, and a tiny bird made from folded paper. The bird’s paper wing had a rip.

Ollie picked up the plastic bottle. It felt cool and smooth. “This can go in the recycling,” he said. “I can help the Earth.”

He placed the bottle in the basket with the bottle picture. Clink! It made a gentle sound. Ollie smiled. It felt good to help.

Then he heard a small squeak. He looked down. A dust bunny peeked from under the bed. It was a little puff of fuzz with kind button eyes. “Hi,” it squeaked. “I’m Benny.”

“Hi, Benny! I’m Ollie,” said the otter. “I’m cleaning up. I want the guest room to feel cozy.”

Benny looked worried. “I’m scared of the loud vacuum,” he whispered. “Can you be gentle?”

Ollie’s heart felt warm and soft. He sat down on the carpet, making himself small. “I hear you,” he said softly. “I won’t use the vacuum now. I can sweep with this quiet brush.”

He found a small hand broom. Swish, swish. It made a soft sound like leaves. Benny sighed with relief. “Thank you for listening,” he said.

Ollie nodded. “That’s compassion. I care about your feelings.”

On the bed, the doll’s bent arm wobbled. “Oh dear,” the doll said in a tiny voice. “I’m not okay.”

Ollie climbed up, careful and slow. He touched the doll’s arm. It felt loose. “I can help,” he said. He found a little ribbon in the drawer. He tied the ribbon gently around the arm to keep it safe until a grown-up could fix it. “There,” said Ollie. “You are cared for.”

“Thank you,” whispered the doll.

On the desk, the folded paper bird fluttered. “My wing is ripped,” it said softly. “I can’t sit up.”

Ollie’s whiskers twitched with an idea. He found a strip of paper from the recycling pile. He folded it neatly. He made a tiny brace and taped it carefully. “This is a reuse fix,” he said. “We can make old things new.”

The bird wiggled and sat up straight. “I can perch again!” it chirped.

Next, Ollie saw the sock with a hole. “Hmm,” he said. “You can be a puppet!” He drew two dot eyes near the toe with a washable marker. He slipped the sock over his paw. “Hello, I’m Mr. Sock!” he giggled in a squeaky voice.

Benny laughed so hard he rolled like a little gray cloud. The doll and the paper bird giggled too. The room felt friendly and bright.

Just then, the door creaked. A tall broom leaned inside with a swish. “I am the Big Broom,” it boomed. “I will sweep everything into one pile. Even the little toys. Move aside!”

Ollie stood up tall, but he kept his voice calm. His tail flicked once. “Big Broom,” he said clearly, “please wait. Some things do not belong in a trash pile. The doll is healing. The bird is fixed. Benny is scared of loud sweeping.”

Big Broom paused. “But I want the room clean.”

“We can clean with care,” said Ollie. “That is being assertive and kind. We can sort. We can recycle. We can be gentle.”

Ollie pointed with his paw. “Paper and cans go in this basket. Food bits go in the other. Toys stay safe. And we use quiet brushes for Benny.”

Big Broom lowered its bristles. “I didn’t know,” it said softly. “Thank you for telling me.”

“Thank you for listening,” Ollie replied.

Together, they worked. Swish, swish with the small broom. Tap, clink with the recycling. The sunshine felt warm on Ollie’s fur. The room smelled fresh. The bed looked smooth. The desk was neat. Benny peeked out and did a tiny happy dance.

When they finished, Ollie patted the recycling basket. “We helped the Earth,” he said. “We reused things and kept good things safe.”

Big Broom nodded. “You were brave and gentle,” it said. “You spoke up kindly. You showed compassion.”

Ollie felt a happy bubble in his chest. He looked at his friends. “Today’s lesson,” he said, “is to care about others’ feelings, to speak up with kind words, and to recycle and reuse to help our world.”

Benny beamed. The doll waved her ribbon arm. The paper bird perched proudly.

Ollie wiggled his whiskers and smiled. “A tidy guest room,” he said, “and a kind heart.”

The End.
