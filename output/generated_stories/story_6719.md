# Fawn Fixes Feelings in the Workshop

In a sunny neighborhood, there was a small garage workshop. The air smelled like fresh wood and warm metal. Tools hung on pegs. A broom leaned in the corner. Light blinked through a little window, making bright dusty sparkles.

A gentle deer fawn named Faye stepped softly inside. Her hooves made a quiet tap-tap on the floor. Her ears were big and pink on the inside. Her eyes were wide and kind.

“Hello?” Faye called. “I want to help.”

Mr. Bello, the friendly neighbor, waved from a workbench. “Hi, Faye! I’m fixing bikes for the neighborhood ride. Can you hand me the red wrench?”

Faye sniffed the air. It smelled like lemon soap and rubber tires. She looked at the wall. Many shiny tools! Blue, green, red, and silver.

Her heart started to beat fast. “So many tools,” she whispered. “I want to touch them all!”

She reached for a shiny hammer. Clink! It bumped a box of screws. The little screws rolled like tiny raindrops. Tink, tink, tink!

“Oh no,” said Faye. Her cheeks felt warm. Her tummy felt wiggly. Her legs wanted to run.

Mr. Bello smiled gently. “It’s okay, Faye. We can fix it together. Let’s pause.”

Faye took a big breath in. She smelled sawdust. She breathed out slowly. “I can pause,” she said softly.

Mr. Bello nodded. “We can use a plan. First, we calm our bodies. Then we look. Then we choose.”

Faye put her hoof on her chest. Thump-thump. She breathed in for three. One… two… three. She breathed out for three. One… two… three.

“My body feels soft now,” she said. “I can think.”

“Great,” said Mr. Bello. “Now look. What is the problem?”

Faye looked at the floor. “The screws are everywhere,” she said. “Someone could slip. That’s not safe.”

“That’s right,” said Mr. Bello. “In our workshop, we care for each other and our space. That is civic responsibility.”

Faye nodded. “We share this place, like we share the world,” she said. “We help keep it safe and clean.”

She found a little magnet on a stick. It clicked and ticked as it picked up screws. “Tick, tick, tick!” Faye giggled.

“You found a smart tool,” Mr. Bello said. “That is logical thinking.”

When the screws were back in the box, Faye’s shoulders dropped. She felt light. “Now I can help,” she said.

“Please pass the red wrench,” said Mr. Bello.

Faye looked again. She saw the red wrench. She wanted to grab fast. But she remembered her plan. “Pause. Breathe. Look. Choose,” she whispered.

She reached slowly and carefully. Her hoof felt the cool metal. She smiled. “Here you go.”

Clink! The wrench fit the bike bolt. Mr. Bello turned it. “Perfect.”

A small fan hummed in the corner. It blew a soft breeze. Faye’s fur rippled. “That feels nice,” she said.

Outside, a bird chirped. Children laughed down the street. “We are fixing bikes for everyone,” Faye said. “We are helping our neighbors. That is being a good citizen.”

“Yes,” said Mr. Bello. “Everyone can ride if we all care.”

Faye saw a sign on the wall. It had pictures and words:
- Stop and breathe.
- Ask for help.
- Clean as you go.
- Share tools.
- Be safe.

“I like the sign,” Faye said. “It helps me remember.”

Mr. Bello handed Faye a soft cloth. “Can you wipe the seat?” he asked.

Faye felt a little hurry in her legs. She wanted to wipe fast and be done. But fast made the cloth slip. The seat squeaked and streaked.

Faye paused. She breathed in lemon smell. “Slow is okay,” she told herself. “I can do it step by step.”

She wiped in small circles. Round and round. The seat shined. Faye saw her tiny face looking back. “Hello, me!” she giggled.

“Beautiful job,” said Mr. Bello.

The garage door was open now. A gentle breeze carried the sound of a distant bell. A girl named Lani peeked in. “Is my bike ready?” she asked.

“Almost,” said Faye. “We are checking the bell.”

Faye reached for the bell. Her hoof bumped a paint can. The can wobbled. Glug-glug! A drop of blue paint slid down the side.

Faye felt a pinch in her belly. “Oh,” she said. She wanted to run again. But she remembered her plan.

“Pause,” she whispered.
She breathed in. One… two… three.
She breathed out. One… two… three.
She looked. “Only one drop,” she said. “We can wipe it warm and quick.”

She found a rag and a tiny bit of soap. Swish, swish. The blue spot was gone. The floor was clean.

Lani clapped. “You fixed it!”

Faye smiled. “I fixed my feelings, too,” she said. “When I pause and breathe, I can think and help.”

Mr. Bello rang the bell. Ding-ding! “Bike is ready!”

Lani hopped on. “Thank you, Faye,” she said. “I will ride on the path and share the road. I will wear my helmet.”

“That is safe for you and kind for others,” Faye said. “We all share our world.”

As Lani rode away, Faye put tools back on the pegs. Tap, tap. Everything in its place.

Mr. Bello gave Faye a bright sticker shaped like a tiny globe. “For caring for your feelings, our workshop, and our neighbors,” he said.

Faye placed the sticker on her chest. It sparkled in the sun. Her heart felt calm and strong.

“I can help again tomorrow,” Faye said.

“You can,” said Mr. Bello. “You are independent and thoughtful.”

Faye nodded. “Pause. Breathe. Look. Choose,” she whispered with a smile.

The End.
