# Rilla Rabbit’s Laundry Room Parade

Rilla was a determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She lived with her family in a small house. One rainy morning, the house felt quiet. Drip, drip, went the rain on the window. Hum, hum, went the washing machine in the laundry room.

Rilla’s long ears twitched. “I hear music,” she said. “It sounds like a song!” She hopped to the laundry room and wiggled her nose. The air smelled warm and clean, like soap bubbles and sunshine. The dryer made a low whoosh-whoosh. The washer sang a steady thump-thump.

Rilla smiled. “I want to make music too!” She picked up two wooden clothespins. Click-clack. Click-clack. It sounded like tiny sticks. “I will make a laundry room band,” she said.

Just then, her friends came to play. Tiko the turtle slid in slowly, his shell shiny and green. Mina the mouse zipped in quickly, her tail swishing. Bop the bluebird fluttered and chirped. Zia the zebra, who lived next door, peeked in with a friendly grin.

“Hi, friends!” said Rilla. “Let’s make music and dance!”

Tiko blinked. “I move slow,” he said. “Can I be in the band?”

Mina squeaked, “I move fast! Can I play too?”

Bop chirped, “I can sing high notes!”

Zia swished her tail. “I can stomp and swish. Is that okay?”

Rilla’s ears stood tall. “Yes!” she said. “We are different. That makes our music big and bright.”

They looked around the laundry room. There were baskets and bottles. There was a row of socks on a string. The washer and dryer sang their steady hums.

Rilla tapped the clothespins. Click-clack, click-clack.

Tiko tapped a plastic bucket with a spoon. Tunk… tunk… tunk.

Mina shook a mesh bag full of buttons. Shika-shika-shika!

Bop trilled along with the hum. Tweedle-dee! Tweet-tweet!

Zia stomped gently on a folded towel. Thump, swish, thump, swish.

All together, the sounds filled the room. It was a soft, happy song. Rilla hopped in place, her feet pat-pat on the tile. “Let’s make a Laundry Room Parade!” she cried.

They began to march around the baskets. Rilla led the way. “Step, step, hop!” she sang.

But soon there was a problem. The sock string was low. Zia’s tall stripes brushed the socks. A sock fell on Zia’s nose. “Oh!” she laughed. “I can’t see!”

Tiko tried to turn, but the bucket bumped his shell. Clunk! He felt stuck. “I need more room,” he said.

Mina zipped under a shelf and knocked over a bottle cap. Clatter, clatter! She squeaked, “Too fast!”

Bop flew up high, but the air vent blew a gust. Whoosh! He wobbled. “I need a calm space,” he peeped.

The music stopped. Everyone felt quiet. Rilla’s whiskers drooped. “I wanted us all to play,” she said softly. “But it’s not working.”

Tiko took a slow breath. “We can fix it. We can help each other.”

Rilla nodded. “Yes. We are different. We move different. We need a plan that fits everyone.”

She listened to the room. Hum, whoosh, drip. She looked at her friends. Slow, fast, high, tall. “We can make parts,” she said. “A slow part, a fast part, a quiet part, and a big stomp part. We will take turns. We will help each other with space.”

Her friends smiled.

Rilla set the plan. “Tiko goes first with the slow beat. We all breathe and march gently. Then Mina plays the quick shake. We make a small path for her. Then Bop sings softly. We make the room calm. Last, Zia does the big stomp. We keep the socks safe. I will use hand signs to help.”

Rilla showed her signs: one ear up for slow, two ears up for fast, paws to her chest for calm, paw in the air for stomp. “We can do it!” she said.

They started again.

Tiko’s turn: Tunk… tunk… tunk. Rilla raised one ear. Everyone moved slow. Step… step… pat. They breathed in and out. Calm and steady.

Mina’s turn: Shika-shika-shika! Rilla raised two ears. Everyone made a clear path. Mina zoomed along the basket line. Zia lifted the sock string for her. “Thank you!” Mina squeaked. “I feel safe!”

Bop’s turn: Tweedle-dee… tweet… tweet… Rilla put her paws on her chest. Everyone got quiet. They swayed like soft towels. The room felt peaceful and kind.

Zia’s turn: Thump, swish, thump, swish! Rilla held her paw high. Everyone watched the socks. Tiko gently moved the bucket. Bop flew lower to see. Mina clapped tiny paws. Zia’s stripes danced. “I feel strong and careful,” she said.

At the end, Rilla clicked her pins. Click-clack—stop. They all froze, then giggled. The laundry room felt bright and warm. The rain on the window made a gentle patter-patter, like applause.

“We did it,” Rilla said. “We helped each other. We used our different ways to make one song.”

Tiko smiled. “Slow is good.”

Mina grinned. “Fast is fun.”

Bop chirped, “Soft is sweet.”

Zia laughed, “Big is brave.”

Rilla hugged her friends. “Different is good. Different helps. When we listen and share, we all belong.”

They put the socks back on the string, folded a towel together, and did one more tiny parade, calm and happy.

The End.
