# Toby's Curious Discovery

Toby the tortoise loved his cozy closet hideaway. It was dark and quiet behind the winter coats. He had soft blankets and his favorite smooth rocks to rest on.

"This is my special place," Toby said, patting his shell with his little foot.

One morning, Toby heard strange sounds coming from the other side of the closet wall. *Tap tap tap.* Then *scratch scratch scratch.*

"What could that be?" Toby wondered. His eyes grew wide with curiosity.

The sounds got louder. *Thump thump thump.*

Toby felt his shell get tight. His heart beat fast. "Those sounds are too loud!" he grumbled. "They're bothering my quiet time!"

Toby wanted to yell "STOP THAT NOISE!" But then he remembered what his grandmother always said: "When you're curious instead of angry, you might discover something wonderful."

Toby took three deep breaths. His shell relaxed a little. "Maybe I should find out what's making those sounds," he said softly.

He slowly poked his head out of the closet. The sounds were coming from the room next door. Toby crawled carefully toward the noise.

Through a crack in the wall, Toby saw something amazing. A family of field mice was building a new home! The mama mouse was tapping tiny nails. The papa mouse was scratching wood smooth. The baby mice were thumping as they carried small sticks.

"Wow!" Toby whispered. "They're working so hard!"

But then Toby heard the mama mouse sigh. "I wish we could work quieter," she said in her squeaky voice. "We don't want to bother anyone."

The papa mouse nodded. "We're trying our best, but building a home is noisy work."

Toby felt his heart grow warm. These weren't mean, loud neighbors. They were a family trying to build a safe home, just like he had done with his closet hideaway.

Toby knocked gently on the wall. *Tap tap.*

"Hello?" called the mama mouse nervously.

"Hi there!" Toby said in his kindest voice. "I'm Toby the tortoise. I live in the closet next door."

The mouse family peeked through the crack. They looked scared.

"Don't worry," Toby said with a gentle smile. "I was curious about your sounds. I see you're building a beautiful home!"

The mama mouse's whiskers twitched with surprise. "You're not angry about the noise?"

"At first I was," Toby admitted. "But then I got curious instead. Curiosity is much better than anger. It helps us understand each other."

The papa mouse smiled. "Would you like to see what we're building?"

"I would love that!" Toby said.

The mice showed Toby their tiny home. They had little beds made of cotton and a kitchen with acorn caps for bowls. Everything was perfectly mouse-sized.

"This is wonderful!" Toby said. "You're such good builders!"

"Thank you for being curious about us instead of just being mad," said the mama mouse.

"And thank you for teaching me new words," Toby said. "I learned 'whiskers' and 'squeaky' today!"

From that day on, Toby and the mouse family were good neighbors. When Toby heard building sounds, he would smile and think about his friends working hard. Sometimes he would even help by bringing them soft leaves for their beds.

Toby learned that curiosity opens doors to friendship, while anger closes them. And the mouse family learned that some neighbors are kind and understanding.

The End.
