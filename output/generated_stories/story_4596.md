# Ollie Otter's Big Car Adventure

Ollie the otter wiggled in his car seat as his family drove through the mountains. His whiskers twitched with excitement as he pressed his tiny paws against the cool window.

"Look, Mama!" Ollie squeaked. "Pretty trees everywhere!"

Mama Otter smiled. "Yes, sweetie. We're so lucky to see all these beautiful forests."

But as they drove further, Ollie noticed something that made his tummy feel funny. The trees started looking sick. Their leaves were brown and droopy, and some had no leaves at all.

"Mama, why do those trees look sad?" Ollie asked, his voice getting quieter.

Papa Otter sighed softly. "Those trees are hurt, Ollie. Sometimes people don't take good care of our Earth, and the trees get sick."

Ollie's big brown eyes filled with tears. "But I love trees! They give us clean air to breathe. Fish live in their shade in the rivers!"

"You're absolutely right," Mama said gently. "Trees are very special gifts."

As they drove past a sparkling clean lake, Ollie perked up again. "Oh! That water looks just like our home river!" But then he saw something that made him gasp. Floating in the water were plastic bottles and bags.

"Why is there trash in the pretty water?" Ollie whimpered.

"Some people forget how precious clean water is," Papa explained. "They don't realize that otters like us, and fish, and birds all need clean water to live happily."

Ollie felt very sad. He loved swimming and playing in clean rivers. He loved the fish that swam with him and the birds that sang from the trees. "What can we do, Papa?"

Papa pulled the car over at a rest stop. "Well, little one, we can start by being grateful for what we have and taking good care of it."

Ollie hopped out of the car and ran to a trash can. He picked up an empty water bottle that had fallen on the ground. "Like this?"

"Exactly!" Mama clapped her paws together. "When we're thankful for our Earth, we want to protect it."

A little girl with her family was watching. "Mommy, look! The otter is cleaning up!"

Ollie felt proud and happy. He waved at the little girl with his paw. "Hi! I'm helping take care of our Earth!"

The little girl smiled and picked up a piece of paper that had blown near her feet. "Me too!"

Soon, other families at the rest stop joined in. Everyone was picking up litter and putting it in the trash cans.

"Thank you, Earth, for giving us clean air," Ollie said out loud, looking up at the blue sky.

"Thank you for giving us fresh water," added the little girl.

"Thank you for beautiful trees and animals," said her mother.

As they got back in the car, Ollie felt warm and fuzzy inside. "Mama, I feel so happy when I say thank you!"

"That's because gratitude fills our hearts with joy," Mama said. "And when we're grateful, we naturally want to take care of the wonderful things we have."

Ollie snuggled into his car seat. "From now on, I'll say thank you to Earth every day. And I'll always clean up trash to keep our home beautiful for all the animals around the world!"

Papa started the engine. "That's my thoughtful little otter. When we take care of Earth together, everyone everywhere can enjoy clean air, water, and beautiful nature."

Ollie smiled big, already planning how he'd teach his river friends about being grateful and caring for their home.

The End.
