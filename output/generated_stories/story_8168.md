# Rosie Rabbit's Colorful Bathroom Adventure

Rosie Rabbit loved to paint. Her fluffy white ears bounced with excitement every time she picked up her paintbrush. Today, she had a wonderful idea - she wanted to paint a beautiful rainbow on the bathroom wall to surprise her family!

"This will be the most amazing surprise ever!" Rosie whispered to herself as she gathered her bright paints: red like strawberries, orange like carrots, yellow like sunshine, green like grass, blue like the sky, and purple like grapes.

Rosie dipped her brush in the red paint and started making big, swooping strokes on the white bathroom wall. "Red is so pretty!" she giggled. The paint felt smooth and cool as it spread across the wall.

But when Mama Rabbit came to brush her teeth, she gasped. "Oh my! Rosie, what have you done?"

Rosie's ears drooped. "I wanted to make a beautiful rainbow for everyone to see when they wash their hands," she said sadly. "Don't you like it, Mama?"

Mama Rabbit's voice was gentle but worried. "Rosie, I can see you worked very hard, and your colors are lovely. But painting on the bathroom wall without asking wasn't the right choice. Now we have to clean it up, and that's not fair to Papa and me."

Rosie's nose twitched as she began to understand. "I'm sorry, Mama. I just wanted to make something beautiful. I didn't think about asking first."

"I know you meant well, sweet bunny," Mama said, giving Rosie a warm hug. "Your creativity is wonderful! But next time, let's talk about where the best place would be for your art."

Together, they got warm soapy water and soft sponges. "Scrub-a-dub-dub!" Rosie sang as she helped clean the wall. The soap smelled fresh and lemony, and the water felt warm on her paws.

"You know what, Rosie?" Mama said as they cleaned. "I have an idea. What if we set up a special art space just for you?"

Rosie's ears perked up. "Really? Where?"

"We could put up a big piece of paper on the wall in your room, or get you an easel for the backyard. That way, you can paint beautiful rainbows whenever you want!"

"And I should ask before I paint anywhere new?" Rosie asked.

"Exactly! That shows respect for our home and family," Mama smiled.

When Papa Rabbit came home, Rosie ran to him with her tail wiggling. "Papa! I made a mistake today, but I learned something important!"

She told him the whole story. Papa listened carefully, then said, "I'm proud of you for helping clean up and for understanding, Rosie. And I love that you want to create beautiful art!"

The next day, Papa hung a large sheet of paper on Rosie's bedroom wall. "Now this is YOUR art wall!" he announced.

Rosie painted the most magnificent rainbow, with all the colors flowing perfectly from one to the next. When her little brother Benny saw it, he clapped his paws. "Wow, Rosie! It's beautiful!"

"Thank you!" Rosie beamed. "And next time I want to paint somewhere new, I'll ask first. That's the fair thing to do!"

From that day on, Rosie created many wonderful paintings on her special art wall, and she always remembered to ask before painting anywhere else. Her family loved seeing her creativity bloom in just the right place.

The End.
