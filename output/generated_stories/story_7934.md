# The Kind Mouse’s World Map

In a cozy study corner, a kind mouse named Mila sat at a small desk. The desk was smooth and cool. The lamp made warm, golden light. Papers rustled like soft leaves. Mila’s whiskers twitched with excitement.

“Today I have a big task,” Mila said. “I will make a world map for our snack party!”

She looked at her list. It said: “Make a map. Make flags. Plan a healthy snack from each place. Share and listen.” Mila took a deep breath. The list felt long. Her tiny heart went thump-thump.

“I can do it, one step at a time,” Mila whispered. She picked up a blue crayon. It smelled a little waxy. She drew a big circle. “This is our Earth,” she said. “We all share it.”

She hummed a happy tune and colored oceans blue. Swish, swish. She colored land green. Scritch, scritch. Soon her paws were a little blue and a little green. Mila giggled. “Messy paws mean I’m working!”

Next, she cut paper squares to make small flags. Snip, snip went the scissors. “Each flag is a friend,” she said. “Every friend is different, and that is good.” She drew a red dot on one flag, a star on another, and stripes on a third. Her tail flicked as she focused.

Then Mila looked at her list again. “Plan snacks,” she read. Her tummy rumbled. “Squeak! I will make healthy snacks from around the world.”

She opened a tiny cookbook. Pages smelled like paper and cinnamon. “I can make rice balls for one place, crunchy carrots with hummus for another, and sweet mango slices from far-away lands.” She washed her paws. Splash, splash. She sliced carrots. Crunch! She rolled rice balls. Squish, squish. She peeled a mango. The air filled with a sunny, sweet smell.

But the map still looked plain. Mila frowned. “I want to show kindness from many places,” she said. “How can I do that?”

Just then, her friends peeked into the study corner. Pip the sparrow fluttered in. “Tweet! What are you making?”

“A world map,” said Mila. “I want to learn how friends live, eat, and care in many places. I want to be a helper for our big world.”

Pip tilted his head. “Tweet, I can bring a tiny bowl of oats from my grandma’s country.”

Tara the turtle slowly stepped in. Tap, tap, tap. “I can share a greeting from my cousins,” she said. “They say, ‘Namaste,’ with hands together.”

Lio the lamb bounced and baa-ed, “I can bring crunchy apples from my farm, and I can say ‘Hola!’”

Mila smiled wide. “Thank you! Everyone can share. Everyone can listen. That is global citizenship. We care about our whole world, even if we live in one small corner.”

They all sat on cushions. The lamp made the room glow. “Let’s place flags on the map,” Mila said. Pip gently pressed a tiny star flag near the top. Tara placed a stripe flag on another spot. Lio added a sun flag near the edge.

Mila noticed her list again. “I still need to finish the title,” she said. She wrote, slowly and neatly: “Our Big, Kind World.” Then she drew little hands holding the Earth. Her paws felt steady now. “I am completing my task,” she said proudly.

Pip sniffed the air. “Tweet, I smell mango!”

Mila nodded. “Before we eat, we will say something kind from each place.” She pointed at the map. “In some places, people say ‘Thank you’ like this,” she said, and showed a gentle bow. Tara smiled and put her hands together. Lio waved and said, “Gracias!” Pip chirped, “Merci!”

They tasted the snacks. Crunch went the carrots with creamy hummus. The rice balls were soft and warm. The mango was juicy and bright. “Mmm,” said Tara. “These foods are different, and they are all good for us.” Mila added, “Good food gives us energy to help others.”

Suddenly, the blue crayon rolled off the desk. Plop! The map’s edge smudged. Mila blinked. “Oh dear,” she said. Her ears drooped.

Lio patted her paw. “We can fix it together,” he said.

Mila took a cloth and dabbed the smudge. Pip held the paper still with his tiny claws. Tara used a clean, dry brush to smooth the edge. Stroke, stroke. The map looked neat again.

Mila sighed with relief. “Thank you, friends. When a problem comes, we can pause, breathe, and solve it. We help each other. That is part of being a citizen of the world.”

They stood back and looked at the study corner. The desk was tidy. The map was bright. The flags danced in the lamp light. The snacks were shared and almost gone.

Mila checked her list one last time. “Make a map. Make flags. Plan healthy snacks. Share and listen.” She put a big check mark by each one. “All done!”

Pip chirped, “We learned new words.”

Tara nodded, “We tasted new foods.”

Lio smiled, “We helped fix a problem.”

Mila wrapped her tail around her paws. “And we cared about friends near and far,” she said. “We listened, we shared, and we finished our work. That is what global citizens do.”

They all said together, “Our big world is in our small hands,” and hugged carefully so the map would not bend.

Mila turned off the lamp. Click. The study corner felt peaceful and proud.

The End.
