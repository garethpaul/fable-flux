# Nutkin's Big Building Mistake

The art room smelled like fresh paint and glue. Colorful papers covered every table. Brushes stood tall in jars like tiny trees.

Nutkin the squirrel scampered through the door, his fluffy tail twitching with excitement. Today was the big day! The forest sports festival needed a special trophy stand.

"I can build the best one ever!" Nutkin chattered to himself. His tiny paws grabbed blocks, cardboard tubes, and shiny ribbons.

Stack, stack, stack! Nutkin worked fast. Red blocks on top of blue ones. Cardboard tubes standing tall like tree trunks. He wanted to finish first.

"Slow down, Nutkin," called Mrs. Owl from across the room. "Building takes time and care."

But Nutkin didn't listen. His whiskers twitched as he reached for more blocks. Higher and higher his trophy stand grew.

CRASH!

Everything tumbled down. Blocks scattered across the floor like autumn leaves. The cardboard tubes rolled away. Ribbons fluttered everywhere.

"Oh no!" squeaked Nutkin. His ears drooped. His tail felt heavy.

Worse yet, some blocks had knocked over Bunny's careful painting. Pretty purple flowers now had block-shaped smudges.

"My painting!" Bunny's eyes filled with tears. "I worked so hard on those flowers."

Nutkin's tummy felt funny. Not hungry-funny. Sad-funny. He had hurt his friend's work by rushing.

"I'm sorry, Bunny," Nutkin said in a small voice. "I was going too fast. I didn't mean to wreck your beautiful flowers."

Bunny sniffled. "They took me all morning to paint."

Mrs. Owl hooted softly. "Nutkin, what do you think we should do?"

Nutkin looked at the mess. Blocks everywhere. Bunny's sad face. His own broken trophy stand.

"I should help fix it," Nutkin said. "Can I help make your flowers pretty again, Bunny?"

Bunny's ears perked up a little. "Really? You'd help me?"

"Yes!" Nutkin's tail fluffed up with hope. "And maybe you could help me build my trophy stand? But slower this time?"

Mrs. Owl smiled. "That sounds like a wonderful plan."

First, they cleaned up all the blocks. Nutkin carefully placed each one in its proper spot. The red ones in the red bin. The blue ones in the blue bin.

Then Nutkin and Bunny worked together on the painting. Nutkin held the brush very still while Bunny guided his paw. They painted new purple petals around the smudges.

"Look!" said Bunny. "The smudges look like butterfly wings on my flowers!"

"They're even more beautiful now," Nutkin agreed.

Next came the trophy stand. This time, Nutkin worked slowly. Bunny helped hold blocks steady while Nutkin carefully placed each one.

"One block at a time," Nutkin reminded himself. His paws moved gently. No rushing.

The cardboard tubes went up straight and tall. The ribbons wrapped around like colorful hugs. Together, they built something strong and beautiful.

"It's perfect!" squeaked Nutkin, clapping his paws.

"And it won't fall down," added Bunny with a giggle.

Mrs. Owl nodded approvingly. "When we take time to build carefully and work together, we make something truly special."

At the sports festival, all the forest animals admired the trophy stand. It held the shiny medals perfectly. But Nutkin felt proudest about something else.

"Thank you for helping me," he whispered to Bunny.

"Thank you for saying sorry and helping me too," Bunny whispered back.

The trophy stand sparkled in the sunshine. But the friendship between Nutkin and Bunny sparkled even brighter.

From that day on, Nutkin always remembered: the best things are built with care, patience, and friends.

The End.
