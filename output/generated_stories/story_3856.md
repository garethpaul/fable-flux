# Sunny Soap Bubbles and Brave Monkey Notes

In a sunny backyard, a courageous monkey named Momo loved to swing from a low tree branch. The grass felt cool on Momo’s toes, and the warm sun tickled Momo’s fur. Birds chirped, “Tweet, tweet!” and a soft breeze smelled like fresh flowers.

Momo liked to help. Momo also liked to write little notes. Momo kept a tiny notebook and a bright blue crayon tucked in a leaf pouch. “Writing helps me remember,” Momo said, patting the pouch. Momo practiced kind words like “Please,” “Thank you,” and “I can help!”

One day, Momo saw the backyard friends playing. There was Pip the puppy, Dot the duck, and Bella the bunny. They were building a tiny city in the sand box. “Roads and houses!” Pip barked, wagging his tail. Sand went poof! in the air. It floated onto noses and paws.

“Can I play?” Momo asked.

“Yes!” said Dot. “Quack-yes!”

They scooped and pat-pat-patted the sand. Soon, Pip sneezed. “Ah-choo!” Sand and little carrot bits from a snack were stuck on his paws. Bella rubbed her eyes with sandy paws and blinked. “Ouch, my eyes feel scratchy,” she said softly.

Momo sniffed the air. The sweet flower smell was now mixed with something icky. The shovel handles looked sticky. The water bucket was muddy. Momo felt a little worry in the tummy. “This does not look clean,” Momo whispered. Momo was brave and liked to solve problems. Momo also remembered a note in the notebook.

Momo pulled out the blue crayon and wrote in big, simple letters: “Clean hands. Clean play. Feel good today!” Momo held up the note. “Friends, I have a plan!”

“What’s a plan?” asked Dot.

“It’s a way to fix a problem,” said Momo. “Our hands are dirty. The toys are sticky. We can clean them. Then we can play safely.”

Pip looked at his paws. “They are kind of muddy,” he said.

Bella nodded. “I don’t want scratchy eyes.”

Momo took a deep breath. “We can do this! I will show you. We will wash hands. We will wash toys. We will help each other.”

They all went to the little backyard sink by the garden hose. The water went splish-splash and felt cool like a little rain. Momo turned the soap pump. Squish! Bubbles popped and smelled like lemons. “First, wet hands,” Momo said. “Then soap. Rub, rub, rub! Tops, palms, between fingers, and thumbs. Keep rubbing while we sing!”

Momo sang, “Rub, rub, rub, rub, bubbles bright and clean! Rub, rub, rub, rub, make our hands all gleam!” The friends giggled and sang too. The bubbles shone like tiny rainbows.

Pip wagged his tail so fast that water drops flew like stars. Dot flapped and laughed, “Quack! Clean is fun!” Bella smiled and blinked happily. “My eyes feel better already.”

Momo rinsed. “Now we dry with a clean towel,” Momo said. “Pat, pat.” The towel felt soft and warm from the sun.

“Let’s help the toys,” Momo added. The shovel, the bucket, and the toy cars got a bubble bath in a big tub. “Scrub-a-dub,” sang Momo. “Thank you, toys, for helping us play!” The water turned brown, then clear again. The sun made the clean toys shine.

Dot looked at the sand box. “We made a mess,” she said.

“We can fix it,” said Momo. “We are a team.” Momo wrote another note: “We care for our place.” Momo stuck the note on the fence with a leaf sticker. Together they smoothed the sand, picked up carrot bits, and put trash in a little bin. The breeze smelled fresh again.

A tiny ant crawled by the edge of the sand. Pip almost stepped on it. “Oh! Sorry, little friend,” Pip said, moving his paw.

Momo smiled. “That is compassion,” Momo said. “We care for others, big or small.” Momo wrote a new note: “Be gentle.”

Bella whispered, “Momo, you are brave.”

Momo nodded. “I was nervous to speak up. But when something is not safe, I can be brave and kind. I can use my words. I can write notes to help us remember.”

They went back to playing. Now the sand roads were smooth. The toy cars zoomed. Vroom! The shovel felt nice to hold. Their clean hands made soft pat-pat sounds. The sun warmed their backs. Laughter bubbled like the soap.

When snack time came, Dot said, “Wait! Hands first!” Everyone washed again, singing the little bubble song. The apple slices tasted crisp and sweet. “Clean hands make snacks taste better,” Pip said with a grin.

Momo drew a big smiling bubble on a page and wrote: “Clean hands, kind hearts.” Then Momo hung the page on a string so everyone could see. It fluttered like a happy flag.

“Thank you, Momo,” Bella said. “You helped us help ourselves.”

Momo felt proud and calm. “We did it together,” Momo said. “We can care for our bodies. We can care for our toys. We can care for our friends and our yard.”

They all waved at the tiny ant as it walked away on a clean sand road.

The sun began to set, warm and golden. The backyard looked neat. Their hands were clean and smelled like lemons. Their hearts felt big and light.

Momo packed the notebook and blue crayon. “Today I learned,” Momo said, “that being clean keeps us safe. Speaking up is brave. Writing helps us remember. Helping together makes our place shine.”

Everyone cheered, “Hooray for clean and kind!”

The End.
