# Miko Speaks Up in the Treehouse

Miko was a courageous monkey who lived in a cozy treehouse high in a big green tree. The treehouse smelled like fresh leaves and sweet bananas. Sunlight made warm stripes on the wooden floor. Birds sang, “Tweet-tweet!” and the wind whispered, “Whoosh.”

Every morning, Miko stretched his arms and said, “Good morning, me!” He brushed his teeth, combed his soft fur, and took a big sip of cool water. “Self-care time,” he said with a smile. His tummy felt happy, and his mind felt ready.

Today was “Build and Share Day” in the treehouse. Miko and his friends—Lulu the lemur, Tiko the toucan, and Pippa the porcupine—wanted to build a puzzle path with blocks, string, and bells. They would make a path from one side of the treehouse to the other. When they finished, they would share turns walking the path, ringing the bells, and clapping together.

Miko clapped his hands. “Let’s get started!”

Lulu balanced bright red blocks. “I can stack,” she said.

Tiko held shiny bells with his beak. “I can hang these,” he chirped.

Pippa rolled a ball of soft string. “I can tie knots.”

Miko looked at the plan drawn on a leaf. He knew the plan said, Step 1: Clear the floor. Step 2: Stack blocks in a line. Step 3: Tie string to guide the steps. Step 4: Hang bells at the finish.

“Friends,” Miko said, pointing to the leaf. “Let’s follow the steps.”

They began to work, but everyone moved in different ways. Lulu stacked blocks in a zigzag. Tiko flew around, dropping bells in a pile. Pippa tied knots in the middle of the floor, making loops. The floor became crowded.

Miko tried to place a block, but his tail bumped a bell. Cling-clang! He wobbled. A block slid. “Oh!” he gasped.

Tiko laughed kindly. “It’s okay, Miko.”

Miko’s heart beat faster. The noise felt too loud. The path looked mixed-up, and he could not find space. He took a deep breath, like he had practiced. In, and out. He remembered something important: Self-advocacy means using your voice to say what you need.

He placed his hand over his chest. “I can speak up,” he whispered to himself.

“Friends,” Miko said in a clear voice. “I’m feeling wobbly. I need more space to do my part. Can we pause and make a plan together?”

Lulu stopped stacking. “Oh! Thanks for telling us, Miko.”

Pippa nodded. “I didn’t know you felt wobbly. How can we help?”

Miko smiled. “Thank you for listening. Let’s use the steps on the leaf. First, clear the floor, please.”

They moved the piles. Scrape-scrape went the blocks. Ting-ting went the bells. Swish went the string as Pippa rolled it neatly.

“Second,” said Miko, “let’s make a straight line so we can all reach.” He showed them with his hands. “I’ll place the first three blocks. Lulu, can you place the next three?”

“Sure!” Lulu grinned.

“Third,” said Miko, “Pippa, tie the string along the side so we can hold it and balance. Tiko, can you hang the bells at the end when we finish the path?”

“Happy to!” Tiko chirped.

The room felt calmer. Miko’s legs felt steady. He took small steps, heel-to-toe, placing each block carefully. Tap. Tap. Tap. Lulu copied him. Tap. Tap. Tap. Pippa tied the string snug. Tiko waited, humming a soft tune.

Soon the path was ready. The string made a gentle guide. The bells waited with a quiet promise of music.

“Time to try it!” Miko said. “We will take turns. We will go slow. If anyone needs help, say it with your words.”

Miko went first. He held the string and stepped on each block. He looked forward and felt the wood under his toes—smooth and strong. “Step, step, step,” he said. His tail helped him balance. At the end, he tapped the bells. Ring-a-ling! The sound was bright and happy.

“Your turn, Lulu,” Miko said. Lulu wobbled on the second block. “I feel wiggly,” she said.

Miko offered his hand. “Thank you for telling us what you need. You can hold my hand.”

Lulu held on. Step, step. She made it to the end. Ring-a-ling!

Pippa went next. “My paws are prickly,” she said. “Can I go slower?”

“Yes,” said Miko. “Going slow is good self-care.” Pippa moved slowly and smiled at the bells.

Tiko flew above and landed gently on each block. “I need quiet to focus,” he said softly.

Miko nodded. “Friends, quiet steps for Tiko, please.” Everyone hushed. Tiko reached the end. Ring-a-ling!

They all cheered. The treehouse felt warm and proud. The puzzle path worked because they used their voices, moved with care, and worked together like a team.

Miko’s tummy felt calm. He looked at his friends. “Today I learned that speaking up is brave,” he said. “When I say what I need, I help myself and help my friends.”

Lulu hugged him. “And when we listen, we help you.”

Pippa added, “We made a plan and used coordination.”

Tiko flapped his wings. “And we took care of ourselves with water, rest, and gentle breaths.”

They shared the path many times. They shared the bells. They shared smiles and knowledge, too—how to plan, how to balance, and how to ask for help.

As the sun set and the treehouse turned golden, Miko whispered, “I am proud I spoke up.” The wind sighed, “Whoosh,” like a happy friend.

And every Build and Share Day after that, Miko remembered: Use your voice. Make a plan. Care for yourself. Work together.

The End.
