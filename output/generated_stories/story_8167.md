# Bubble Paint in the Bathroom

Binky was a determined rabbit with soft gray ears and bright, curious eyes. He loved to make art. He liked colors. He liked shapes. He liked to say, “I can try!” when things felt tricky.

One rainy afternoon, Binky hopped into the bathroom. Drip, drip, drip went the water from the faucet. The mirror was shiny. The tiles felt cool under his bunny feet. The air smelled clean, like soap and mint.

Binky carried a small basket. Inside were safe, washable paints, a big brush, a little sponge, and cups for water. He also had a towel and some tape. He set them carefully by the sink.

“I will make bathroom art!” said Binky. “I will make bubbles and rainbows and swirls.”

Binky taped a big sheet of paper low on the wall, right by the tub. He turned the faucet. Swish went the water. He filled a cup with a little water. He dipped the brush in blue paint. He tried to make a round bubble.

But the brush slipped on the wet paper. Squeak! The circle became a wiggly line.

“Oh no,” said Binky. His ears bent just a little. “My bubble is not round.”

He took a deep breath. “I can try again.”

He wiped the brush on the towel. He dipped it in yellow paint and made a sunshine. Dab, dab, dab. The sun looked happy.

Then he tried another bubble. Swirl, swirl. The brush slid again. The bubble looked squishy.

Binky felt a pinch in his tummy. “How can I paint round bubbles if the paper is slippery?” He tapped his foot and thought. The faucet dripped. Plink. Plink. The mirror fogged a tiny bit.

Binky listened to the sounds and looked around. He saw the sponge. He saw a toothbrush cup. He saw the soft bath mat. He smiled a small smile. “I can be creative,” he whispered. “I can try new tools.”

He dipped the sponge in blue paint. He pressed it to the paper. Squish! A round shape appeared. It looked like a big, gentle bubble.

“Oh!” said Binky. His ears stood tall. “That worked!”

He tried again with pink paint. Squish! A bubble. He tried purple. Squish! Another bubble. He made little bubbles with the rim of the toothbrush cup. Dip, press, pop! Circles made soft popping sounds when he lifted the cup.

Binky giggled. “Pop, pop, pop! Hello, bubbles!”

Just then, his friend Tilly, a tiny turtle, peeked in from the hallway. “What are you doing, Binky?” asked Tilly. Her voice was sweet and slow. She wore a small blue bow.

“I am making bubble art,” said Binky. “My brush was too slippery. Now I use a sponge and a cup!”

“Can I help?” asked Tilly.

“Yes!” said Binky. “We can make art together.”

Binky gave Tilly the little sponge. Tilly was slow and careful. She tapped the sponge and made a shy, green bubble. Tap, tap. Binky used the cup to make big circles, then tiny ones with a bottle cap. Dip, press, pop!

They added soap to a bowl of water and blew with a straw. A mountain of bubbles grew. The bubbles smelled like flowers. They sparkled like tiny rainbows.

“Let’s try bubble prints,” said Binky.

“Bubble prints?” asked Tilly.

Binky nodded. “We blow bubbles, then we catch them on the paper.” He held the bowl close to the page. Wiggle, wiggle. Tilly blew softly. Fff-fff. The bubbles floated up and kissed the paper. Kiss, kiss. They left soft, round marks.

Binky clapped his paws. “We did it!”

But then, oh no! A bubble popped on the floor. Plop! A drip of paint slid toward the rug.

“Uh-oh!” said Tilly.

Binky stayed calm. “Problem, solution,” he said kindly. He grabbed the towel and wiped the drip. Wipe, wipe. He put the towel under the paper to catch more drips. He placed the mat farther away. “We keep it safe and tidy,” he said.

They worked together. Binky made blue and purple circles. Tilly made green and yellow ones. Swish, tap, pop! The bathroom sounded like a quiet song. The mirror showed their smiling faces. The air felt warm and soft.

Soon the paper was full of bubbles, big and small. Some bubbles touched. Some bubbles danced alone. In the middle, Binky painted a rabbit and a turtle holding brushes.

“It is us,” said Tilly, smiling.

Binky nodded. “We made bubble art with creative tools. We used care, and we worked as a team.”

He signed his name with tiny paw prints in the corner. Tilly added a little shell shape. They stood back and looked.

“It is beautiful,” said Tilly.

“It is our bathroom gallery,” said Binky proudly. “We solved our problem by trying new ways. We used our hands, our eyes, and our calm breaths.”

The rain stopped outside. The light in the bathroom was soft and golden. The picture shone with color.

Binky washed the brushes and the cup in the sink. Swish, swish. Tilly folded the towel. Fold, pat. They cleaned up every drop. The room smelled fresh again.

Binky touched Tilly’s shell gently. “Thank you for helping.”

“Thank you for sharing,” said Tilly.

They hugged. Binky’s ears tickled Tilly’s cheek. They laughed.

Binky looked at the art one more time. “I learned that creativity means trying new ideas. When one way is hard, I can try another way. With friends, we can make something wonderful.”

Tilly nodded. “And we keep things safe and tidy.”

Binky smiled. “Art, coordination, and kind hearts make bright days.”

The End.
