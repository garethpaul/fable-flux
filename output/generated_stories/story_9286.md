# Panda Poppy Solves a Fair Share

Panda Poppy lived in a small home office with sunny windows and a big leafy plant. The room smelled like fresh paper and warm tea. The desk was smooth and cool, and the computer made a soft hum, like a sleepy bee. Poppy was patient. Poppy liked to listen.

One bright morning, Poppy set out bowls for sorting crayons. “I want to share these crayons fairly,” Poppy said. “Fair means everyone gets what is right.” The word “fair” felt good in Poppy’s mouth. It felt steady, like a firm hug.

Knock, knock! Poppy’s friends came to visit. There was Little Squirrel with quick paws, Tiny Tortoise with a shiny shell, and Bluebird with soft wings. They all liked to draw by the big leafy plant.

On the desk, there was a basket of crayons and a stack of paper. Some paper was new and white. Some paper was used on one side. Poppy smiled and tapped the used stack. “We can draw on the back to save trees,” Poppy said. “That is kind to Earth.” It made the room feel calm, like cool shade.

Little Squirrel reached for the crayons. “I want the big blue!” Squirrel squeaked and grabbed three blues at once.

Bluebird fluffed feathers. “I need blue too,” sang Bluebird. “I want to draw the sky.”

Tiny Tortoise blinked slowly. “I did not get a blue,” Tortoise said, voice soft as a whisper.

The room felt tight, like a knot. The computer hummed louder. Poppy took a deep breath and listened. “I hear you,” Poppy said. “Squirrel wants blue. Bluebird wants blue. Tortoise did not get blue. This does not feel fair.”

Poppy placed paws on the desk, warm from the sun. “Let’s talk,” Poppy said. “We will take turns to speak. One at a time.”

Squirrel’s tail quivered. “I feel excited,” Squirrel said. “I want to draw fast, and I grabbed the blues.”

Poppy nodded. “Thank you for telling us.”

Bluebird tilted a bright eye. “I feel sad,” sang Bluebird. “No blue means no sky.”

Poppy nodded. “Thank you for sharing.”

Tortoise lifted the small head. “I feel left out,” Tortoise said. “I did not get to choose.”

Poppy nodded again. “Thank you for your calm words.”

Poppy looked at the crayons. “Justice means fair for everyone,” Poppy said. “Fair is not grabbing. Fair is sharing and taking turns. Fair is hearing each voice.”

Poppy had an idea. “We can make a crayon plan,” Poppy said, voice gentle. “A plan makes things fair.”

Poppy set a timer on the computer. It made a soft beep. “We will share the blues,” Poppy said. “Each friend gets a turn when the timer beeps. While you wait, you can use other colors. You can also try new shades. Dark green can be a forest. Purple can be a sunset sky.”

Squirrel hugged the blue crayons and then paused. The room felt softer. Squirrel’s nose twitched. “Okay,” Squirrel said. “I can wait for one blue.”

Bluebird fluttered a wing. “I can use white and gray for clouds first,” Bluebird sang.

Tortoise smiled a small smile. “I can draw a pond with green. When it is my turn, I can add blue water.”

Poppy clapped paws. “Good plan!”

Poppy also brought the used paper stack close. “We will draw on the back,” Poppy said. “That saves trees. That is good for Earth. That is sustainability.” The word was big, but Poppy said it slow and kind. “It means we take care so things can last.”

The timer beeped. Beep! Squirrel passed one blue to Bluebird. Beep! Bluebird passed one blue to Tortoise. The crayons felt smooth and warm in each friend’s hand. The paper made a soft swish. The plant leaves rustled, like they were happy too.

As they drew, they talked. Squirrel said, “I feel proud. I can wait my turn.” Bluebird sang, “I feel calm. I got to share.” Tortoise said, “I feel seen. My voice mattered.”

Poppy smiled and sipped tea. The steam smelled like honey. “This is justice,” Poppy said. “We listened. We were fair. We helped Earth by using the backs of paper and caring for our crayons.”

Soon, the desk was full of pictures. Squirrel made a deep blue whale. Bluebird made a big sky with soft clouds. Tortoise made a cool green pond with blue water that sparkled. Poppy used the tiny crayon pieces to draw a leafy tree. “We used all the little bits,” Poppy said. “Nothing was wasted.”

They hung the pictures on the wall with gentle tape. The wall looked bright and kind. The home office felt peaceful and safe.

Poppy gathered the friends close. “What did we learn today?” Poppy asked.

Squirrel raised a paw. “Justice means being fair.”

Bluebird nodded. “We use our voices and listen to others.”

Tortoise tapped the shell. “We take care of Earth by using what we have.”

Poppy hugged them. The hug felt warm like sunshine. “Yes,” Poppy said. “Justice, sustainability, and good communication make our world gentle and fair.”

They cleaned up together. Crayons clicked into the basket. Paper stacked neat. The timer went quiet. The computer hummed softly again.

Poppy looked around the calm room. “We did it,” Poppy said. “We solved the problem by talking, sharing, and caring.”

Everyone smiled.

The End.
