# Splash the Dolphin's Recycling Adventure

On a sunny morning, Splash the dolphin wiggled and squirmed in his special water tank on Grandma Ocean's peaceful front porch. The wooden porch creaked gently in the warm breeze, and colorful flower pots lined the railings.

"Good morning, Splash!" called Grandma Ocean as she stepped outside with her morning tea. The steam curled up from her cup, smelling like sweet mint.

"Good morning, Grandma!" chirped Splash, doing a happy flip in his tank. Water droplets sparkled like tiny diamonds in the sunshine. "What are we doing today?"

Grandma Ocean smiled and pointed to three big bins sitting at the corner of the porch. "Today, we're going to practice our recycling routine. Would you like to help me sort these items?"

Splash's eyes grew wide with excitement. "Yes, yes! I love helping!" He clapped his flippers together, making little splash sounds.

Grandma Ocean picked up a plastic water bottle. "Now Splash, where do you think this goes?"

Splash thought carefully, tapping his fin against his chin. "Hmm... is it the blue bin for plastic?"

"That's exactly right!" said Grandma Ocean proudly. "You've been practicing so well!"

Next, she held up a shiny aluminum can. It caught the sunlight and made pretty reflections dance across the porch ceiling.

"Ooh, ooh! I know this one!" Splash bounced excitedly. "The green bin for metal!"

"Perfect!" Grandma Ocean dropped the can into the green bin with a satisfying *clink*.

Then she picked up an old newspaper. The pages rustled softly in the breeze.

"This one's easy," giggled Splash. "Paper goes in the yellow bin!"

As they continued sorting, Splash noticed his neighbor, Bella the seagull, watching from the porch railing. She looked confused.

"Hi Bella!" called Splash. "Want to learn about recycling with us?"

Bella fluttered down nervously. "I don't know how," she whispered. "It looks hard."

"It's not hard at all!" said Splash kindly. "We can teach you! Grandma Ocean always says that when we work together, everything is more fun."

Grandma Ocean nodded. "Splash is right, Bella. And when we recycle, we help keep our ocean and sky clean for everyone."

Bella's eyes lit up. "Really? I want to help too!"

For the next hour, the three friends worked together on the sunny porch. Splash would call out what bin each item belonged in, Bella would fly the lighter items over, and Grandma Ocean would explain why recycling was so important.

"When we put plastic in the right bin," explained Grandma Ocean, "it gets made into new things instead of floating in the ocean where fish might eat it by mistake."

"And when we recycle paper," added Splash, "we don't have to cut down as many trees!"

Soon, all the recycling was sorted perfectly. The bins stood in neat rows, ready for pickup.

"We did it!" cheered Splash, splashing happily. "And it was so much easier when we all worked together!"

Bella did a little dance on the porch railing. "I can't wait to teach my family!"

Grandma Ocean hugged them both. "You two showed such wonderful integrity by doing the right thing for our planet, even when no one was watching. And by practicing together every day, recycling becomes as easy as swimming!"

As the sun climbed higher, Splash felt proud and happy. He had learned that taking care of the Earth was everyone's job, and it was always better when friends helped each other.

The End.
