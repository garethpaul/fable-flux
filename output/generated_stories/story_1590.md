# Fox Kit on the Rooftop

Tap, tap, tap went tiny paws on the stairs. A curious fox kit named Fenn peeked up and saw a little door that led to the rooftop. A cool breeze blew through the crack. Fenn’s whiskers twitched. He loved new places. He loved to explore.

“I want to see the sky!” Fenn said, tail swishing. He pushed the door open. Whoosh! The rooftop smelled like warm sun and fresh rain. Soft clouds floated like cotton. Far below, cars hummed like bees.

Fenn took one step onto the roof. The floor felt rough and grainy. Little pebbles tickled his paws. He giggled. “This is fun!”

Then Fenn heard a voice. “Hello up there!” It was Miri the pigeon, flapping her gray wings. “Rooftops can be tricky,” she cooed. “Do you know the rules?”

“Rules?” Fenn blinked. “I just want to play.”

Miri landed near him. “We can play and be safe. Safety first, then fun. Do you have a grown-up with you?”

Fenn looked back at the door. It creaked and stayed open, but no grown-up was there. His ears drooped. “No.”

“Let’s call for help,” Miri said kindly. “That shows respect for rules.”

Fenn took a deep breath. “Grown-up! I need help!” His small voice floated on the wind. No answer.

Just then, a gentle head poked up through the door. It was Nana Fox. Her fur was soft and silver, and her eyes were warm. “Fenn,” she said in a calm voice, “I heard you call. Thank you for asking for help. Rooftops are high. We must be careful.”

Fenn’s ears perked up. “Nana, I want to play. I want to see the clouds.”

“We can,” Nana said. “But we will use safety, coordination, and respect. That means we move our bodies together, make a plan, and follow the rules.”

Fenn felt proud. “I can do that!”

Nana showed him a bright orange chalk line along the middle of the roof. “We stay inside the safe line,” she said. “No going near the edge. We keep three points down when we move—two paws and one paw ready, or three paws and a tail. Slow and steady. We listen to each other.”

Miri bobbed her head. “And we help others when they need it.”

Fenn looked at the chalk line. It glowed in the sun. He could hear the city breeze whoosh-whoosh. He could smell bread from a bakery across the street. His heart felt jumpy and happy.

“Let’s practice,” Nana said. “Ready?”

“Ready!” Fenn said.

They started a rooftop game called Step-Step-Stop. Nana tapped the roof: “Step.” Fenn put one paw forward. “Step.” He moved the next paw. “Stop.” They both froze and looked around. No edges close. No loose shingles. All safe.

Fenn giggled. “Again!”

They moved like dancers. Step-step-stop. They kept three points down. They stayed inside the chalk. Fenn felt his body balance. He felt the wind on his ears. He felt strong and smart.

Suddenly, Fenn heard a tiny squeak. “Help!” A ladybug on a leaf was blowing toward the edge. The leaf slid on the pebbles.

Fenn’s eyes went wide. “Nana, the ladybug!”

Nana nodded. “We will help, but we will stay safe. What is our plan?”

Fenn looked at the chalk line. He could almost see the edge. It made his tummy flip. He remembered: safety first, then fun; three points down; stay inside the line; work together.

“I will stay on the line,” Fenn said. “You hold my tail. Miri, you watch the edge.”

“Good plan,” Nana said. She wrapped her paw around Fenn’s tail gently. “I’ve got you.”

Miri flapped to the side. “Edge is near. Slow.”

Fenn moved. Step-step-stop. He reached his little paw toward the leaf. The wind tickled his whiskers. He could hear his own breath. In, out. In, out. “Almost,” he whispered.

“Three points,” Nana reminded.

Fenn pressed his back paws firm. He stretched, just a tiny bit more, and—got it! He pulled the leaf back inside the chalk line. The ladybug shook her tiny wings. “Thank you,” she squeaked. “You helped me, and you were careful.”

Fenn’s chest felt warm. “We made a plan,” he said. “We moved together.”

Nana smiled. “That is coordination. And you listened. That is respect.”

Miri cooed. “You kept everyone safe. That is helping others.”

Fenn beamed. He looked at the wide, bright sky. The clouds looked like pillows again. The sun felt like a hug. “Safety first, then fun,” he said.

Nana nodded. “Let’s enjoy the rooftop. We can sit, we can sing, and we can watch the sky. We stay on the line, and we tell a grown-up where we are.”

They sat and hummed a soft song. The roof warmed their paws. The breeze smelled like flowers now. Fenn leaned against Nana and felt calm.

When it was time to go, Fenn stood up. “Step-step-stop,” he said. They moved to the door together. Fenn turned back and waved to Miri and the ladybug. “Bye!”

“Bye!” they called.

Down the stairs, Fenn said, “Nana, I learned something.”

“What did you learn?” Nana asked.

Fenn said, “Safety comes first. We make a plan. We move slow. We listen and help. That makes fun safe for everyone.”

Nana hugged him. “Yes, little one. That is a smart fox lesson.”

Fenn’s tail swished. He felt proud, safe, and kind.

The End.
