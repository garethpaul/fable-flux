# The Bird Who Sang About Suds

In the quiet morning, a little singing bird named Bella fluffed her bright blue feathers. She lived in a cozy closet hideaway. The closet smelled like clean cotton and cedar. Soft scarves made nests on the high shelf. Shoes lined up like sleepy soldiers on the floor. A tiny ray of light slipped through the crack under the door.

“Good morning, closet,” Bella sang. Her voice was sweet and warm. “Tweet-a-dee! Time to learn. Time to play!”

Bella loved three things: songs, games, and staying clean. She had a shiny toothbrush as tall as her wing, a tiny soap bar shaped like a star, and a pink towel that felt like a hug.

Today was Sports Day in the closet. Socks would be the runners. Shoelaces would be the jump ropes. Scarves would be the finish line. Bella hopped to the shoe line and chirped, “Who wants to race?”

Two socks peeked out from a slipper. One sock was red with little dots. The other sock was blue with stripes. “We do!” the socks said together. “We want to run and have fun!”

Bella smiled, then sniffed. “Oh!” she said softly. The air smelled a little sour, like old cheese. “Friends, are your feet clean?”

The socks wiggled shyly. “We skipped wash time,” said the red sock. “We wanted to play faster,” said the blue sock.

Bella’s eyes were kind. “It is good to play,” she said. “But we wash first. Washing keeps us strong. Washing keeps us healthy. Washing makes play feel happy.”

The socks looked down. “We are sorry,” they whispered.

“It’s okay,” Bella sang. “We can fix it. We learn. We try again.” She opened her little wash kit. The soap star smelled like lemons. The water in the tiny bowl felt cool. The pink towel was soft and warm.

Bella dipped her wing. “Splash, splash,” she said. “Rub, rub.” She showed gentle circles. “Bubbles are buddies. They chase the germs away.”

The socks copied her moves. “Splash, splash,” they said. “Rub, rub.” Bubbles popped like tiny kisses. Pop! Pop! Pop! The blue sock giggled. “It tickles!” The red sock laughed too. “It smells so nice!”

“Rinse, rinse,” Bella hummed. “Dry, dry.” She patted them with the towel. “Clean and cozy,” she sang. “Ready to fly—well, to run!”

The closet felt bright, even in the dim light. The air smelled fresh, like lemons and rain. The scarves swished like flags. The shoelaces jumped like little snakes.

“Now we warm up,” Bella said. “We bend. We stretch.” She showed them small moves. “Arms up!” she chirped, lifting her wings. “Toes touch!” She tapped her tiny toes. The socks stretched and bounced. “We feel strong!” they cheered.

“Ready,” Bella called. “Set. Go!” The socks ran along the rug. Their fabric feet made soft swish-swish sounds. The shoelaces turned into a jump rope. Whip, swish, tap! The socks jumped. One, two, three! They reached the scarf finish line. “Yay!” Bella sang. “You did it!”

The socks hugged. “We feel light and fast,” said the blue sock. “We feel fresh and happy,” said the red sock.

Bella fluffed her feathers. “That is the power of clean,” she said. “Clean hands, clean feet, clean toys. Clean helps us learn. Clean helps us play.”

The closet door creaked a little. A cool breeze slipped in. It smelled like soap and sunshine. Bella’s tummy rumbled. “Snack time!” she chirped.

She brought out tiny apple slices and a cup of water. “Before we eat, we wash our hands,” Bella reminded them. She sang a hand-washing song:

“Water on, soap in hand,
Rub, rub, rub, just like we planned.
Tops and bottoms, in between,
Rinse, rinse, rinse, now we’re clean!”

The socks clapped along, their fabric ends flapping like hands. They washed the way Bella showed them. Then they ate. Crunch! The apple was sweet and juicy. “Yum,” said the red sock. “Delish,” said the blue sock. Bella sipped water and felt the cool trickle. “Ahh,” she sighed.

After snack, Bella set up a tiny learning corner. A shirt button became a counting bead. “One, two, three,” she sang. The socks counted too. “Learning is fun,” they said. Bella smiled. “We learn better when we feel good. Clean helps us feel good.”

They played one more game: scarf soccer. The scarf ball was soft and whooshy. “Kick, pass, share!” Bella called. The socks took turns. They cheered for each other. When the game ended, they were warm and tired. Their breaths were slow. Their hearts felt happy.

“It is tidy time,” Bella said. “We put things away. We wash again.” They cleaned the bowl, the towel, and the floor spot where water dripped. Swish, wipe, done!

The socks looked proud. “We can do it every day,” they said. “Wash before play. Wash before we eat. Wash when we’re done.”

Bella’s song was gentle and bright. “Yes,” she sang. “Clean today. Clean tomorrow. Clean makes strong bodies and brave hearts.”

The closet grew quiet and cozy. The scarves stopped swishing. The shoes stood still. The tiny ray of light grew softer.

“Good night,” the socks whispered.

“Good night,” Bella sang. “Remember: wash, learn, play, and be kind.”

And in the clean, sweet-smelling closet hideaway, everyone rested with happy smiles, ready for another day of songs, sports, and smart, healthy habits.

The End.
