# Poco’s Plan for a Song

Poco the parrot loved the study corner. It was a cozy nook by the window, with a small desk, a soft blue rug, and a basket of shiny pencils. Sunlight came in like warm honey. The air smelled like paper and lemon soap. On the wall hung a calendar with big squares, and a tiny clock tick-tick-ticked.

“Squawk! I want to make music,” Poco chirped, fluffing his green and gold feathers. He tapped his beak gently on a pencil. Tink! “Today I will make a new song.”

Poco hopped onto the desk. He saw a notebook with blank pages. He saw stickers shaped like stars. He heard the clock, tick-tick-tick, and the whisper of the window breeze. He also saw a little drum, a triangle, and a tiny toy piano with rainbow keys.

“I will play everything at once!” Poco said. “More is more! Squawk!”

He flapped his wings, excited. Poco pecked the drum. Boom! He rang the triangle. Ting! He pressed the piano. Plink-plonk-plunk! He tapped the pencil, too. Tink-tink!

Soon the study corner was full of sounds. Boom, ting, plink, plonk, tink! Poco’s head bobbed this way and that way. But the sounds bumped into each other. It felt messy. Poco’s tummy did a funny flip.

“This is too much,” he sighed. “It sounds like a storm.”

Poco sat very still. He remembered something his friend Mia said: “When things feel too busy, take a breath.” Poco closed his eyes. In—slow like a turtle. Out—soft like a feather. In… and out. The tick-tick-tick of the clock sounded friendly again.

“Mindful moment,” Poco whispered. “Listen first.”

Poco opened his eyes and smiled. “Maybe I need a plan,” he said. He looked at the calendar on the wall and the notebook on the desk. “Planning helps music feel happy.”

He drew three big boxes in his notebook with a blue crayon.

Box One: Start
Box Two: Middle
Box Three: End

Poco put a star sticker on the first box. “Start,” he chirped. “Soft like a whisper.” He tapped the triangle very gently. Ting… The sound was bright and tiny, like a sunbeam. Little bubbles of joy popped in Poco’s chest.

He put a heart sticker on the second box. “Middle,” he said. “Steady like a walk.” He placed the small drum on the rug so it would be soft. Boom… boom… boom… The drum sounded warm and slow, like a big friendly hug.

He put a rainbow sticker on the last box. “End,” he said. “Colorful like a smile!” He tried the toy piano: plink-plonk-pling-pling! The notes skipped like happy raindrops.

Poco looked at his plan. “Start with ting. Middle with boom. End with plink,” he said. “That is my song map!”

He took one more deep breath. In… out… “I can do this,” he told himself. “One part at a time.”

Poco began. He stood tall on the desk, feathers smooth. The study corner was calm and bright.

“Ting…” he rang the triangle once. He listened to the sound fade like a tiny bell floating away.

“Boom… boom… boom…” he tapped the drum three times, steady and slow. His claws felt the soft rug. His wings kept a gentle beat.

Then he moved to the piano. “Plink-plonk-pling-pling!” He pressed the rainbow keys in a little pattern. Green, yellow, blue, red. The notes giggled together.

Poco paused. He listened. The sounds fit! His heart felt light. “It works!” he chirped. “My plan makes the music clear.”

He played it again, and this time he added his voice. “La-la-laaa,” he sang softly in the Start, “Ba-ba-ba boom,” he hummed in the Middle, and “Do-re-mi-yaaay!” he sang at the End. The study corner glowed with happy sound.

Mia peeked in from the doorway. “Hi, Poco! That sounds lovely,” she said with a smile. “What did you do?”

Poco fluffed with pride. “I made a plan,” he said. “Start, Middle, End. I listened first. I breathed. I played one thing at a time.”

Mia clapped. Clap, clap. “That is smart planning,” she said. “You were mindful, and you were creative. Your music tells a story!”

Poco nodded. “Music is a friend,” he said. “When I listen, plan, and go slow, the friend sings with me.”

Mia sat on the rug. “Can we try together?”

“Yes!” said Poco. He pointed with his wing. “You do the triangle for the Start. I will do the drum for the Middle. Then we both play the piano for the End.”

They breathed in together. In… out…

Ting…
Boom… boom… boom…
Plink-plonk-pling-pling!

Their eyes sparkled. They giggled. The song felt like sunshine in their chests.

Poco wrote a little note in his notebook: “My Music Plan.” He drew a tiny ear next to it for listening, a tiny cloud for breathing, and three boxes for Start, Middle, End.

He looked around the cozy study corner and whispered, “Thank you, room, for helping me think.”

Then he looked at the reader, as if you were right there. “You can make music, too,” Poco said kindly. “Take a breath. Make a plan. Start soft, go steady, and end with a smile. Your song can tell your story.”

The End.
