# Benny Beaver's Big Problem

Benny Beaver loved his busy basement workshop. It smelled like fresh wood chips and echoed with the sound of his tiny hammer. *Tap, tap, tap!* Every day, Benny built wonderful things with his friends.

Today was extra special. Benny's friends Rosie Rabbit, Tommy Turtle, and Penny Porcupine had come to help build a big toy box for the forest playground.

"This will be the BEST toy box ever!" chirped Rosie, hopping around excitedly.

But when they started working, everything went wrong. Everyone wanted to use the hammer at the same time!

"I need it first!" squeaked Penny.

"No, me!" said Tommy, reaching with his slow but steady flippers.

"I'm the fastest, so I should go first!" declared Rosie.

Soon, all four friends were pulling on the hammer. *Clatter, bang!* Wood pieces scattered everywhere. The basement felt hot and stuffy with all the arguing.

Benny's whiskers twitched with worry. His beautiful, organized workshop looked like a tornado had visited! But then he took a deep breath and thought carefully.

"Wait, wait, WAIT!" called Benny, holding up his fuzzy paws. "Let's stop and think about this problem."

His friends paused, still holding the hammer.

Benny sat down on a soft pile of wood shavings. "When I have a big problem, I like to think step by step. What do we really want?"

"To build the toy box," said Tommy thoughtfully.

"Right!" nodded Benny. "And can we build it if we're all fighting over one hammer?"

"No," admitted Rosie, her ears drooping a little.

Benny's eyes sparkled with an idea. "Let's look around my workshop. What else can we use?"

The friends spread out, searching carefully. The basement was full of interesting tools and materials.

"I found sandpaper!" called Penny, holding up the rough, scratchy paper.

"And I see paintbrushes!" discovered Tommy near a colorful shelf.

"Look! Measuring tape!" giggled Rosie, pulling out the long, yellow strip.

Benny clapped his paws together. "Perfect! Now we can ALL work at the same time. We just need to think about who should do what job."

They sat in a circle on the cool basement floor and made a plan.

"Penny, your quills make you great at detail work. Want to sand the wood smooth?" suggested Benny.

Penny beamed. "Yes! I love making things perfect!"

"Tommy, you're so patient and careful. Could you measure twice to make sure everything fits?"

Tommy smiled slowly. "That sounds just right for me."

"And Rosie, you have such a good eye for colors. Will you plan how we should paint it?"

"Oh yes!" Rosie bounced with joy.

"What about the hammer?" asked Tommy.

Benny thought again. "We can take turns! Five minutes each. I'll watch my special workshop clock."

For the rest of the afternoon, the basement buzzed with happy sounds. *Swish* went the sandpaper. *Snip, snip* went the measuring tape. The friends chatted and laughed as they worked together.

When it was each friend's turn with the hammer, they felt proud and important. When it wasn't their turn, they cheered for whoever was hammering.

By sunset, they had built the most beautiful toy box the forest had ever seen!

"We did it!" cheered Benny, looking at their wonderful work. "And we did it by thinking through our problem and working together."

"Taking turns made it even more fun," agreed Rosie.

"I feel so proud of what we made," said Penny.

Benny hugged his friends. "When we think carefully and share, we can solve any problem!"

The End.
