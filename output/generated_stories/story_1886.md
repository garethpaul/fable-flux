# Kora Koala Makes It Right

The basement buzzed like a busy beehive. Fans hummed. A washing machine swished. The dryer thumped, thump-thump. Boxes stacked like little mountains made tiny paper rustles. Lights glowed warm and yellow.

In the middle of it all sat Kora, a calm koala with soft gray fur and curious eyes. She loved the busy basement. It smelled like clean soap, warm socks, and a hint of crayons. It sounded like work—and play!

Kora had a story circle every afternoon. She called it “Cozy Story Time.” She laid out a fuzzy rug, some pillows, and a big blue blanket. Friends came to listen: Pip the mouse, Dottie the dog, Sunny the snail, and Benny the bunny. Kora would sip warm eucalyptus tea and tell stories that made hearts feel cozy.

One day, Kora found a shiny box on a high shelf. It sparkled like morning dew. A tag said, “Marco’s Marble Maze—Please Ask Before Playing.” Kora knew Marco the raccoon kept his special things neat and safe. He liked lists and labels. He liked to ask and answer.

Kora felt a tingle of excitement. “It would make story time extra fun,” she whispered. She hugged the blue blanket close and listened. Swish, swish went the washer. Thump-thump went the dryer. Her paws felt warm on the rug.

Kora took a slow breath. “I should ask,” she said. But her story friends were already arriving.

“Hi, Kora!” squeaked Pip, whiskers twitching. “What’s today’s story?”

“Something brave!” barked Dottie, wagging her tail. “And kind!”

Kora looked at the shiny box. Her heart made a tiny bump. “We could use the marble maze as a sound for the story,” she said. “Just a little bit.”

She lifted the lid. Click. The glass marbles made a soft clink-clink, like raindrops in a jar. Kora’s tummy did a flip-flop. She knew she hadn’t asked.

Kora began the story. “Once there was a small star that wanted to say sorry—”

Clink! A marble rolled down the maze. Clickety-clack! Everyone giggled. It sounded like tiny feet dancing.

Suddenly, the basement door creaked. Marco the raccoon peeked in, wearing his red bandana. His eyes were careful and kind, but now they were wide.

“My marble maze,” he said softly. “Did you ask?”

Kora’s paws felt cold. The dryer thumped louder. Thump-thump-thump. Her ears felt hot. Her calm heart trembled.

“I didn’t ask,” she said in a very small voice. “I’m sorry, Marco.”

Marco came closer. He knelt by the fuzzy rug. “I care about my maze,” he said. “It was a gift from my grandpa. It helps me feel calm.”

Kora’s eyes filled with warm tears. “I took it because I wanted to make story time special. But taking without asking is not respectful. It was not kind to you.”

Benny the bunny whispered, “Kora is honest.”

Sunny the snail stretched his long eyes. “It takes courage to say sorry,” he said slowly.

Kora put her paws over the marbles. “I want to make it right,” she said. “How can I fix my mistake?”

Marco breathed in, then out. The room seemed to breathe with him. Swish, swish. Thump, thump. The fans hummed softly. He touched the shiny box.

“Thank you for saying sorry,” Marco said. “That helps my heart. Let’s make a plan.”

Kora listened with her whole self. Her paws were still. Her ears were open.

“First,” said Marco, “please put the maze away carefully.”

“Yes,” Kora said. She placed each piece gently, clink, clink, in its home.

“Second,” said Marco, “please ask me next time.”

“Yes,” Kora said. “I will ask. I will wait for your answer.”

“Third,” said Marco, “maybe we can share it together during story time. I can be the Maze Keeper.”

Kora’s heart warmed again. “That is kind,” she said. “Thank you.”

Marco nodded. “We are friends. Friends respect each other.”

Kora turned to her circle. “Friends, I made a mistake. I didn’t ask. I felt excited, but I forgot to be respectful. Saying sorry is brave. Making amends means fixing the hurt. I will show respect now.”

Dottie wagged. “That’s a good lesson!”

Pip squeaked. “We can help!”

Kora smiled. “Marco, will you join our story and use the maze for sound?”

Marco grinned. “Yes!”

Kora wrapped the blue blanket around the group like a small sky. The warm light glowed. The dryer slowed: thump… thump… thump. The scent of clean cotton floated by.

Kora began again. “Once there was a small star who forgot to ask. The star felt bad. The star said, ‘I am sorry,’ and asked, ‘How can I make it right?’ The star listened. The star learned.”

Marco rolled a marble gently. Clickety-clack. It sounded like sparkling starlight. Everyone’s eyes shone.

At the end, Kora turned to Marco. “Thank you for teaching me,” she said.

Marco nodded. “Thank you for listening.”

Kora looked at her friends. “Today we learned to slow down, to ask, to wait, and to fix our mistakes. That is wellness for our hearts. That is courage. That is respect.”

Pip whispered, “And that is friendship.”

The basement felt calm now, even with its busy sounds. Swish, swish. Hum, hum. Kora sipped her warm tea. It tasted like leaves and sunshine. Her tummy felt steady.

Kora smiled. “When we make amends, we grow kinder. When we respect others, our circle feels safe. We can always begin again.”

Everyone hugged, gentle and soft. The story circle felt cozy, brave, and bright.

The End.
