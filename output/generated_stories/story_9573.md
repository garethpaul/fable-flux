# Sunny Seeds on the Balcony

Sunny the squirrel lived on a small, bright balcony garden. There were pots of basil, mint, tiny tomatoes, and cheerful yellow flowers. The air smelled like fresh leaves and a little like pepper from the basil. Bees buzzed softly. The sun warmed the wooden floor. Sunny’s fluffy tail twitched with excitement.

“Good morning!” Sunny chattered. “Today, I will be helpful.”

Sunny peeked over the railing and saw Mia, a little girl, washing her hands in a blue bowl. “Scrub, scrub,” Mia sang. “Clean hands help plants.” She smiled and waved. “Hi, Sunny!”

“Hi, Mia!” Sunny said. “How can I help today?”

Mia pointed to the watering can. “The herbs are thirsty. But not too much water. Just a sip.”

Sunny nodded. “A sip, not a slosh.” He liked learning new words. Sip sounded gentle, like the soft drip of water.

Sunny lifted the small watering can. It felt cool and smooth. He tipped it carefully. Drip… drip… drip. The basil leaves shook and said, “Thank you,” in a leafy whisper. Sunny giggled. “You are welcome, basil!”

Mia clapped. “Great helping, Sunny!”

A tiny wind came. It carried the smell of mint, sweet and sharp. Sunny wrinkled his nose. “Mmm! Mint is minty!” He said the word slowly, tasting the sounds. “Min-ty.”

Mia laughed. “You love words, Sunny.”

“I do,” he said proudly. “Words help. Words tell us how to help.”

A bee buzzed by and bumped a tomato plant. A small tomato fell and rolled toward a little puddle of sticky soil. “Oh no!” said Sunny. He jumped and caught the tomato with his paws. “I got you!” The tomato felt smooth and cool.

“Great catch!” said Mia. “But the tomato is muddy. We need hygiene.” She said the word clearly. “Hy-giene means keeping things clean.”

Sunny blinked. “Hy-giene.” He tried it. “Hy-giene!” He smiled. “Clean is kind.”

Mia brought a soft cloth and a bowl of clean water. “Wash, wash,” she said. Sunny dipped the tomato. Swish! The water looked cloudy, then clear again as he rinsed it fresh. He patted it dry with the cloth. “All clean,” he said.

Sunny looked around the balcony garden. Some leaves had brown spots. Some pots had fallen petals on the soil. A little trowel lay sideways. “I can help more,” Sunny decided. “I can improve the garden.”

“Improve is another good word,” Mia said. “It means make better.”

“Im-prove,” Sunny echoed, tapping his nose. “Make better.”

They worked together. Sunny picked up fallen petals and put them in the compost bucket. He brushed the wooden floor with a tiny broom, swish-swish, and the air felt fresh. Mia held open a small picture book with plant words. She pointed to each word and read it out loud.

“Water,” she said.

“Water,” Sunny repeated.

“Clean.”

“Clean.”

“Help.”

“Help!”

Sunny loved the music of the words. He liked how “help” felt happy in his mouth.

A snail peeked from under a mint leaf. Its shell was shiny and wet. “Hello, Snail,” Sunny whispered. “Please don’t munch all the mint.” The snail blinked slowly and slid away to a lettuce leaf snack Mia had set out. “Thank you,” Sunny said. He was polite and gentle.

Mia brought out a small hand brush. “We can brush soil off the railing. Hygiene helps plants and people,” she said.

Sunny brushed carefully. The dirt tickled his paws. Dust danced in the sunbeams. “Clean, clean,” he sang softly. “Help, help.”

Suddenly, a pot wobbled. A gust of wind had pushed the yellow flower pot to the edge. “Oh!” cried Mia.

“I can help!” said Sunny. He ran fast, his claws clicking on the wood. He wrapped his little arms around the pot and pulled. “I’ve got you!” he grunted. The pot was heavy, but Sunny was strong and smart. Mia steadied it with both hands.

“Teamwork!” Mia said, smiling wide. “We did it.”

Sunny puffed with pride. “Helping feels good,” he said. He stroked the petals. They were silky and smelled like sunshine.

They finished by washing their hands and paws in the blue bowl. “Scrub, scrub,” Mia sang again. “Hygiene helps us stay healthy.”

“Hygiene,” Sunny said, proud of the long word. “Clean paws, kind paws.”

Mia held up the book again. On the last page, it said: “Together we improve.” The picture showed a smiling garden.

Sunny read each word slowly. “To-geth-er we im-prove.”

Mia nodded. “That means we help each other and make things better.”

Sunny looked around the bright balcony garden. The leaves looked happy. The floor was clean. The air smelled minty and sweet. The little tomato glowed like a red marble. Sunny’s heart felt warm.

“I learned new words,” he said. “Hygiene. Improve. Help.” He hugged his fluffy tail. “And I helped my friends.”

Mia patted his head. “You were careful, kind, and clean. You used your words, and you used your paws.”

Sunny smiled. “Being helpful makes the garden happy. Being clean keeps us healthy. Learning words helps me know what to do.”

They sat quietly for a moment. Bees buzzed. Leaves whispered. The sun made a golden blanket across the balcony.

“Tomorrow,” Sunny said softly, “I will help again.”

“Me too,” said Mia.

The End.
