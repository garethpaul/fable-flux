# Jump-Joy Joey Helps in the Shed

Joey was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a big bounce. He loved to hop, hop, hop! One sunny morning, his mama said, “Joey, Mr. Wattle needs help in his garden shed. Would you like to volunteer?”

Joey’s ears perked up. “Volunteer? What does that mean, Mama?”

“It means you help for free,” Mama said with a warm smile. “You give your time and kindness.”

Joey put his little paws on his chest. “I have time! I have kindness! I will help!”

They hop-hopped down the path. The air smelled like sweet grass and lemons. Birds sang a chirp-chirp song. When they reached the garden shed, the door creaked—creeeak!—and a soft smell of soil and wood drifted out. Sunlight made sparkles on dust dancing in the air.

Mr. Wattle, the koala gardener, waved. “Hello, Joey! Hello, Mama Kanga! My shed is messy, and my tools are mixed up. I need help before the kids’ garden day.”

Joey peeked inside. Buckets were upside down. Rakes were asleep on the floor. Pots were stacked like wobbly pancakes. A little radio sat on a shelf, quiet and dusty.

Joey’s tail twitched. “We can do it! I will volunteer!”

Mama nodded. “I’ll water the garden. Joey, you help Mr. Wattle.”

Joey hopped into the shed. His paws felt cool on the wooden floor. “Where should I start?” he asked.

Mr. Wattle sighed, patting a broom. “Let’s sweep first.”

Joey tried to sweep, but the broom was tall and wiggly. Swish-swish! Thump! The broom bonked a bucket. The bucket rolled—clonk, clonk, clonk!—and bumped a big stack of pots. CRASH! Pots tumbled like loud rain.

Joey’s eyes grew wide. “Oh no! I made it worse.”

Mr. Wattle rubbed his nose. “It’s okay, little mate. We will fix it.”

Joey took a deep breath. He felt a tiny worry bubble in his tummy. “I want to help, but it’s hard,” he said softly.

Mr. Wattle smiled. “Helping can be hard. Let’s make it fun! Do you like music?”

Joey’s ears stood tall. “Yes!”

Mr. Wattle tapped the radio. Click. A bouncy tune began—tap-ta-tap, clap-clap! The music filled the shed like warm sunlight. Joey’s paws wiggled. His tail swished. He felt his worry bubble pop!

“Let’s move and sort,” said Mr. Wattle. “When the drum goes boom, we put brooms in the corner. When the guitar goes strum, we stack pots by size. When the claps go clap, we line up buckets. Ready?”

Joey grinned. “Ready!”

The drum went boom-boom. Joey hopped and scooted the brooms—swish—into a neat corner. “Boom means brooms!” he sang.

The guitar went strum-strum. Joey tapped each pot. “Little, little, medium, big!” He stacked them carefully. Clink, clink, clink. “Strum means pots!”

The claps went clap-clap. Joey rolled the buckets into a row. Clonk, clonk. “Clap means buckets!”

He moved with the beat. Hop, slide, scoot. His paws made soft pats on the floor. He smiled so big his cheeks felt warm. Mr. Wattle did a silly shuffle. “Ha! Look at us move!”

“Look at us help!” Joey laughed.

They made a sorting song together:
“Boom for the brooms,
Strum for the pots,
Clap for the buckets,
We help lots and lots!”

Mama peeked in. “What a tidy shed! And what a happy song.”

Joey puffed up with pride. “We are volunteering! We are helping for free! And we are dancing while we do it!”

Mr. Wattle pointed to a tangled line. “One more job, Joey. The hose is all twisty.”

Joey crouched low. The hose smelled like a rainy day. It felt cool and rubbery in his paws. He followed the hose like a path. “This bend goes here,” he said. He hummed the sorting song, and with each hum, he made a loop and a twist. Hummm—loop! Hummm—twist! Soon the hose sat in a neat circle like a green donut.

“You did it!” Mr. Wattle cheered. “Listen.” The radio played a gentle ding. The song was ending. Joey’s heart felt full and bouncy.

Mr. Wattle gave Joey a small, shiny sticker shaped like a star. “Thank you for volunteering,” he said. “You made my shed safe and neat. The kids will find the tools easily.”

Joey traced the star with his paw. It was smooth and a little sticky. “Volunteering feels good,” he said. “I like helping.”

Mama hugged him. Her fur was warm. “You used music and movement to solve a big problem,” she said. “You kept trying, even when it was hard.”

Joey nodded. “Music helped my body move, and moving helped my brain think.”

Mr. Wattle winked. “Would you like to lead the cleanup dance for garden day?”

Joey’s eyes sparkled. “Yes! I will share the song so others can help too.”

He stood tall and sang softly, “Boom for the brooms, strum for the pots, clap for the buckets, we help lots and lots!”

Everyone clapped. Clap-clap! The shed looked bright and calm. The tools stood proud. The air smelled like fresh earth and lemon soap. Joey’s tail thumped in a happy beat.

On the way home, Joey said, “Volunteering is giving kindness with our time. We can help at the shed, in the garden, or at home.”

Mama smiled. “That’s the lesson, Joey. Helping hands—and hopping feet—make big jobs small.”

Joey giggled and hopped high. “I will volunteer again!”

The End.
