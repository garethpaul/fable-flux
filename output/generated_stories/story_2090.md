# Ollie Otter and the Busy Garden Shed

Ollie the playful otter lived by a small blue river. One sunny morning, he padded to the garden shed with soft, wet paws. The shed smelled like fresh soil and warm wood. Sunlight made little dust sparkles dance in the air.

“Today,” Ollie chirped, “I will help with chores!”

He pushed open the creaky door. Squeeeak! Inside, there were brooms, buckets, and shiny tools on hooks. He heard a tiny rustle behind a flower pot.

“Hello?” Ollie said, tilting his head.

A shy mouse peeked out. “I’m Mimi,” she squeaked. “I live here. Please be gentle.”

“I will,” said Ollie with a smile. “I want to sweep, water the plants, and stack the pots.”

Just then, a big rooster named Rafi poked his head in. “Cock-a-doodle-doo! Make way, everyone! I will do all the chores very fast.”

Ollie’s whiskers twitched. He felt a little squished in his heart. He liked helping. He wanted to try.

Rafi grabbed a broom and swished it wildly. Dust puffed up in a gray cloud. Cough, cough! Mimi waved her tiny paws. “Too fast!”

Ollie took a slow breath. He put a paw on his chest. “I feel upset,” he said in a clear, calm voice. “I want to help too. Please share the chores.”

Rafi stopped and blinked. “But I am strong,” he said. “I can do it all.”

Ollie stood tall, even though he was small. “I am strong in my own way,” he said. “I can be patient and careful. I have good ideas. Please listen.”

Mimi nodded. “We can be a team.”

Rafi looked at the dusty floor. He shuffled his claws. “I did not mean to make you feel bad,” he clucked softly. “I just wanted to be helpful.”

Ollie smiled kindly. “Thank you for telling me,” he said. “We can all help. We can take turns.”

Ollie made a simple plan. He drew three shapes in the dust with his paw: a circle, a square, and a triangle.

“Circle is for sweeping,” he said. “Square is for watering. Triangle is for stacking. We will do each one. We will use gentle hands. We will take turns.”

“That is fair,” Mimi squeaked. “I will help by pointing to the tiny corners.”

Rafi fluffed his feathers. “Okay, team,” he said. “Let’s begin.”

First, sweeping. Ollie took the broom and moved it slowly, swish, swish, swish. He hummed a little tune. “Swish and sweep, we do not rush. We take our time. We do not push.”

Rafi watched and waited. That was hard for him. His feet wanted to dance fast. He took a breath, just like Ollie. “I can be patient,” he said. He used his wings to hold the dustpan steady.

Mimi pointed. “There is dust by the toolbox,” she said. “And a leaf by the boots.” Ollie nodded and swept them up. The floor looked neat and clean.

Next, watering. The watering can was heavy. Slosh, slosh. Ollie felt the cool water on his paws.

“I can carry it with you,” said Rafi. “We can do it together.”

“Thank you,” said Ollie. “Let’s pour slowly.”

They tipped the can. Drip, drip, drip. The plants drank happily. Mimi sniffed a mint plant. “Mmm! It smells fresh,” she said. Ollie smelled it too. “Minty!” he giggled.

A tiny spider crawled down on a silver thread. “Hello,” said Ollie. “We will not splash you.” He moved the can to the side, showing compassion to the little spider.

Last, stacking. There were many clay pots. Tap, tap, tap. They sounded like little drums. Ollie lined them up by size. “Small, medium, big,” he sang.

Rafi picked up a very big pot. “I will put it on top,” he said.

Ollie held up a paw. “Wait,” he said clearly. “That feels unsafe. The big pot should go on the bottom. I want us to be safe.”

Rafi paused. He listened. “You are right,” he said. He set the big pot down low. The stack stood strong and steady.

Mimi clapped her tiny paws. “We did it!”

The shed was tidy and calm. The air smelled like mint and clean wood. The sun made a warm stripe on the floor. Ollie felt proud. He had used his voice. He had been patient. He had been kind.

Rafi ruffled his feathers. “I learned something,” he said. “When I rush, I miss things. When I listen, we do better.”

Ollie nodded. “When I speak up, people know what I need,” he said. “I can be gentle and firm. That is called being assertive.”

Mimi smiled. “We shared, we waited, and we cared for even the spider,” she said. “That is good for everyone, near and far.”

Ollie looked around the shed. “We are a team,” he said. “In our garden, in our town, and in our big world, we can use our voices to help.”

They all took a deep, happy breath together. In, and out. The shed felt like a friendly place.

“Thank you, Ollie,” said Rafi.

“Thank you, friends,” said Ollie. “Let’s have mint tea!”

They sat on a little bench. Sip, sip. Warm and sweet. They listened to birds outside. Tweet, tweet. The day felt bright and good.

Ollie’s heart felt light. He had been brave and clear. He had waited his turn. He had cared for others.

And the garden shed shone with love.

The End.
