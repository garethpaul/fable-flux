# Sunny Rooftop, Calm Little Paws

On a warm, sunny morning, a resourceful squirrel named Sprig scampered across a tall, flat rooftop. The rooftop felt smooth and a little warm on his tiny paws. The breeze smelled like fresh leaves and toast from a nearby kitchen. Sprig loved this rooftop. He called it his Sky Garden.

Sprig had a plan. He wore a tiny leaf backpack and carried pieces of string. He hummed a happy tune. “Plan, plan, plan,” he sang. “I can, can, can.” He wanted to tie strings to little cups to carry nuts and berries up from the tree below.

As Sprig set down a cup, a big gull swooped by. “Whoosh!” The wind from the gull’s wings blew Sprig’s cup to the roof’s edge.

Sprig’s tail puffed. His cheeks got hot. “Hey!” he squeaked. “My cup!”

The gull landed with a flap. “Caw! Rooftops are breezy,” the gull said. “Watch out.”

Sprig stomped a tiny foot. His heart beat fast. He felt a bubble of anger push up in his chest. “I worked hard,” he said. “This is my plan!”

Sprig wanted to shout. He wanted to throw the string. But then he remembered something Grandma Squirrel had told him: “When your feelings get big, make your body small. Breathe. Name it. Tame it.”

Sprig put his paws on his belly. He took a slow breath in. He felt the air fill his tummy like a small balloon. He breathed out slowly, like blowing on a dandelion.

“In,” he whispered. “Out.”

He did it again. In. Out.

He whispered, “I feel angry.” He said it softly, like a secret. “I feel angry because my cup blew away.”

His tail stopped puffing. His cheeks cooled. He blinked and felt the breeze again. It smelled like oranges now, sweet and soft.

The gull tilted her head. “You look calmer,” she said.

“I am,” Sprig said. “But I still need help. I am asking firmly. Please don’t swoop so close to my cups.”

“That is clear,” said the gull. “I can fly higher.”

Sprig nodded. That was assertive. He was proud. He did not shout. He used strong, kind words.

Sprig picked up his cup. He looked around his Sky Garden. The roof had warm sun spots and cool shade spots. The edges were windy. The middle was calm. The gravel felt crunchy. The strings felt rough in his paws.

He made a new plan. “I will put the cups in the calm middle,” he said. “I will use a rock to hold each cup.”

He tied the string to the cup. He tied the other end to a sturdy vent pipe. He tested the knot. Tug tug. Strong.

He smiled. “Plan, plan, plan,” he hummed again. “I can, can, can.”

Just then, a door opened with a creak. A small cat named Miso stepped onto the roof. Her paws were soft and quiet. “Hi, Sprig,” said Miso. “I like your cups.”

“Thank you,” said Sprig. “Please walk around them. I am working.”

Miso’s whiskers twitched. She nodded and stepped carefully. But her tail swished, and her paw bumped a string. A cup tipped and berries rolled like tiny marbles. “Plip, plip, plip.”

Sprig felt the hot bubble again. His ears got warm. He wanted to say, “Miso! Look what you did!”

He stopped. He put his paws on his belly. In. Out. In. Out. He named it: “I feel angry.” He added, “I feel worried. I do not want my plan to break.”

Miso’s eyes grew wide. “I’m sorry,” she said. “I didn’t see the string.”

Sprig nodded. He kept his voice steady. “Thank you for saying sorry. Please help me pick up the berries. And please walk on this side.”

“That is clear,” said Miso. She smiled and helped. The berries felt cool and a little sticky. They smelled sweet. Sprig’s anger melted like ice in the sun.

Together, Sprig, Miso, and the gull worked with care. Sprig showed them his plan. He drew a simple map with a chalk pebble: a square for the roof, an X for the calm middle, arrows for the wind. He said, “We will carry nuts and berries up in these cups. We will move slowly. We will watch the strings.”

The gull flew high and watched the wind. “Now!” she called when the breeze was soft.

Sprig and Miso pulled the strings. The cups slid up, smooth and steady. “Zip, zip,” went the string. “Plop,” went the cup when it landed safe.

Soon, the Sky Garden had neat rows of nuts and berries. Sprig’s backpack was empty, and his heart felt full. He wiggled his whiskers. The sun warmed his back. The rooftop smelled like fruit and fresh air.

Sprig looked at his friends. “I did get angry,” he said. “But I used my calm steps. I breathed. I named my feeling. I used strong, kind words. I made a plan. And I asked for help.”

Miso purred. “You were brave and clear.”

The gull flapped softly. “You were a leader.”

Sprig smiled. “When feelings get big, make your body small. Breathe. Name it. Tame it. Then use your plan.”

They sat together and shared berries. The sweet juice tickled Sprig’s tongue. He wiped his paws on a leaf napkin and laughed. The wind was gentle. The rooftop was peaceful. The Sky Garden was safe.

Sprig looked at the cups, the strings, and his friends. He felt proud and calm. He said, “I can take care of my feelings. I can be kind and strong. I can plan.”

And the rooftop felt like home.

The End.
