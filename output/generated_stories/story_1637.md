# Benny Beaver Speaks Up

Benny Beaver loved working in his cozy garage workshop. The smell of fresh wood shavings filled the air, and sunlight streamed through the dusty windows. Every morning, Benny would put on his tiny tool belt and get ready to build wonderful things.

"Good morning, workshop!" Benny would say cheerfully, patting his workbench with his flat tail. He always said "please" and "thank you" to his tools, even though they couldn't talk back. Mama Beaver had taught him that using kind words was very important.

One sunny Tuesday, Benny's friends came to visit. First came Ruby Rabbit, hopping through the garage door.

"Benny!" she squeaked. "I need you to build me a carrot storage box right now!"

Before Benny could answer, in scurried Sam Squirrel. "No way!" Sam chattered loudly. "Benny has to make me a nut-cracking machine first!"

Then Ollie Owl swooped in through the window. "Hoot! Benny must build my reading perch before anything else!"

Benny's whiskers twitched nervously. He wanted to help all his friends, but they were talking so fast and so loudly! The garage felt cramped and noisy. His friends kept interrupting each other and getting louder and louder.

"But I was here first!" Ruby stamped her foot.

"No, I was!" Sam's tail fluffed up big and bushy.

"Hoot! I flew the fastest!" Ollie ruffled his feathers.

Poor Benny felt his tummy rumble. He had been so excited about his friends visiting that he forgot to eat his healthy breakfast of fresh bark and berries. Now he felt dizzy and overwhelmed.

Benny tried to speak quietly, but his friends couldn't hear him over their arguing. He felt smaller and smaller as they got louder and louder.

Then Benny remembered what Papa Beaver had taught him: "Sometimes, little one, you need to speak up clearly so others can hear your important words."

Benny took a deep breath. He stood up tall on his hind legs and used his strong, clear voice.

"STOP, PLEASE!" Benny said firmly but kindly.

The garage became quiet. Ruby, Sam, and Ollie all looked at him with surprise.

"Thank you for listening," Benny said, feeling braver. "I want to help all of you because you're my friends. But first, I need to eat my healthy breakfast so I have energy to work. And we need to take turns talking, so everyone feels heard."

His friends' faces softened. "Oh, Benny," Ruby said gently. "We're sorry we were so loud and pushy."

"We forgot to use our inside voices," Sam added, looking ashamed.

"And we forgot to say please and thank you," Ollie hooted softly.

Benny smiled warmly. "It's okay! Let's start over. Would you like to share my breakfast with me? Then we can make a plan together."

They all sat in a circle on the soft wood shavings. Benny shared his crunchy bark pieces and sweet berries. As they ate, each friend politely took turns explaining what they needed.

"Thank you for speaking up, Benny," Ruby said. "You taught us to be better friends."

After their healthy snack, they worked together. Benny felt strong and happy because he had used his voice. By sunset, all three projects were finished, and everyone felt grateful.

"Using clear, kind words makes everything better," Benny said, cleaning his tools. "And eating healthy food gives me energy to help my friends!"

From that day on, whenever someone was too loud or pushy, Benny remembered to speak up with confidence and kindness.

The End.
