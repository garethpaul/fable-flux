# Benny's Big Recycling Goal

Little Benny the fawn bounced in his car seat as Papa Deer drove down the sunny street. His soft brown spots wiggled with excitement. Today was special - they were going to visit Grandma Doe!

"Look, Papa!" Benny pointed his tiny hoof at something shiny on the road. "What's that?"

Papa Deer slowed down and frowned. "Oh my, that's a lot of trash, Benny. Bottles and cans everywhere."

Benny's big brown eyes grew wide. The pretty road was covered with empty soda cans, plastic bottles, and crinkly wrappers. It made his tummy feel sad.

"Why is there so much trash, Papa?" Benny asked in his soft, squeaky voice.

"Sometimes people forget to put their trash in the right place," Papa explained gently. "But we can help make it better."

Benny pressed his wet nose against the cool car window. He watched more and more litter pass by. His heart felt heavy like a rock.

"I want to help!" Benny said suddenly. "Can we clean it up?"

Papa Deer smiled proudly. "That's a wonderful idea, little one. But there's so much trash. What should we do first?"

Benny thought hard. His little ears twitched as he made his plan. "We need a goal! Like when I want to jump over the big log in our yard!"

"What kind of goal?" Papa asked.

"We could collect all the bottles and cans!" Benny said, bouncing again. "Then we can take them to the recycling place. Grandma Doe told me recycling helps make new things!"

Papa Deer's eyes twinkled. "That's very smart thinking, Benny. Should we start today?"

"Yes! Yes!" Benny clapped his hooves together. "After we visit Grandma, can we come back with bags?"

When they reached Grandma Doe's cozy house, Benny ran to her with quick, happy steps. The wooden porch creaked under his tiny hooves.

"Grandma! Grandma! We saw lots of trash, and I want to recycle it all!" he said breathlessly.

Grandma Doe hugged him tight. She smelled like warm cookies and lavender flowers. "What a caring little fawn you are! Tell me your plan."

Benny explained his recycling goal while they ate lunch. Grandma Doe listened carefully, nodding her gray head.

"I have some big bags in my closet," she offered. "And gloves to keep your hooves clean!"

After lunch, the three deer drove back to the messy road. Benny felt nervous butterflies in his tummy, but also excited and proud.

"Remember, we only touch bottles and cans," Papa said. "Leave the other trash for the grown-ups."

Benny worked carefully. He picked up shiny aluminum cans that clinked together like music. He found clear plastic bottles that crackled in his hooves. Each piece he collected made him feel bigger and stronger.

"Look how much we got!" Benny said, pointing to their full bags. The road looked so much cleaner already.

At the recycling center, a kind worker showed Benny how the bottles and cans would become new things. "Your bottles might become a new playground!" she said.

Driving home, Benny felt warm and happy inside. "Papa, can we make this our special job? Every week?"

"Absolutely," Papa said. "You set a goal, made good choices, and helped our whole community."

Benny smiled his biggest fawn smile. He learned that even little deer could make big differences when they cared enough to try.

The End.
