# Benny Bear's Busy Workshop Day

The morning sun peeked through the dusty windows of Grandpa's garage workshop. Benny Bear rubbed his sleepy eyes and stretched his fuzzy arms high above his head.

"Good morning, little helper!" called Grandpa Bear from across the workshop. Tools clinked and clanked as he organized his workbench. "Ready for another busy day?"

Benny's round ears perked up with excitement. "Yes, Grandpa! I want to help with everything!" He bounced on his little paws, making his brown fur bounce too.

The workshop smelled like wood shavings and motor oil. Colorful tools hung on the walls like a rainbow. Benny loved spending time here with Grandpa, learning how to fix things and build new projects.

"First, we need to fix Mrs. Robin's birdhouse," Grandpa said, pointing to a small wooden house with a loose roof. "Then we'll help Mr. Raccoon with his wobbly wagon wheel."

Benny clapped his paws together. "I can do it all, Grandpa! I don't need any breaks!"

They worked and worked. Benny held nails while Grandpa hammered. He passed tools when Grandpa needed them. His little paws got dirty with sawdust, and his fur collected wood chips.

*Tap, tap, tap* went the hammer. *Whirr, whirr* went the drill. The workshop was full of busy sounds.

After two hours, Benny's tummy started to rumble like a little thunder cloud. His paws felt tired, and his eyelids grew heavy.

"Grandpa," Benny said softly, "I think I'm getting hungry."

But Grandpa was concentrating on fixing the wagon wheel. "Just a few more minutes, Benny. We're almost done!"

Benny wanted to be helpful, so he kept working. But soon his little legs felt wobbly like jelly. His tummy rumbled even louder. *Grumble, grumble, grumble.*

"Oh my!" Grandpa looked up and saw Benny swaying on his feet. "Benny, when did you last eat breakfast?"

Benny's cheeks turned pink under his brown fur. "I... I forgot. I was so excited to help that I ran straight to the workshop."

Grandpa set down his tools and knelt beside Benny. His kind eyes twinkled behind his glasses. "Oh, little one. Taking care of yourself is just as important as helping others."

"But I wanted to be the best helper ever," Benny said, his voice small and tired.

Grandpa scooped Benny up in a warm, gentle hug. "You ARE the best helper, Benny. But even the best helpers need to eat, rest, and take care of themselves. How can we help others if we don't feel strong and healthy?"

They walked to the little kitchen corner of the workshop. Grandpa made Benny a peanut butter and honey sandwich. The bread was soft and sweet. Benny drank cool milk that made his tummy feel happy again.

"You know what?" Grandpa said, sitting beside Benny. "The best carpenters always take breaks. They eat good food, drink water, and rest when they're tired. That's how they do their best work."

Benny's eyes grew wide. "Really? Even the very best ones?"

"Especially the very best ones," Grandpa nodded. "Taking care of yourself isn't selfish, Benny. It's smart. When we feel good, we can help others even better."

After his snack and a short rest, Benny felt amazing! His energy came back, and his paws felt steady again. Together, they finished fixing Mrs. Robin's birdhouse and Mr. Raccoon's wagon.

"Thank you for teaching me about self-care, Grandpa," Benny said as they cleaned up the workshop. "Tomorrow I'll remember to eat breakfast first!"

Grandpa smiled and ruffled Benny's fur. "That's my smart little helper. Remember, taking care of yourself helps you take care of others too."

That night, Benny told Mama Bear all about his important lesson. From then on, he always remembered to eat, rest, and listen to his body's needs.

The End.
