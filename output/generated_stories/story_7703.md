# Leo the Lion Cub’s Teamwork Train

Leo, a brave little lion cub, padded into the playful living room. The big, soft rug felt fluffy under his paws. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Toys waited in colorful baskets. Pillows made cozy mountains. Leo’s whiskers twitched with excitement. Today, he had a big idea.

“I want to build the longest train track ever!” Leo roared in a tiny, happy voice.

His friends came to play. Mei the panda wore a red ribbon. Amir the camel had jingling ankle bells from a family party. Asha the elephant carried a shiny scarf with bright dots. Diego the parrot fluttered and chirped, “Squawk! Team time!”

Leo rolled out the wooden tracks. Click. Clack. Click. He pushed pieces together. “I’ll do it fast,” he said. “I’m brave. I can make it all by myself.”

Mei knelt down. “Can I help?” she asked softly.

Amir nodded. “My grandma says, ‘Many hands make light work.’ We can do it together.”

Asha lifted a pillow. “We can make a tunnel!”

Diego tilted his head. “Squawk! Teamwork, teamwork!”

Leo’s tail swished. He liked being brave. He liked being fast. He did not like waiting. “Maybe I don’t need help,” he said. He pushed more tracks. Click. Clack. Click.

The track bent. It wobbled. It bumped into a chair leg and popped apart. Clack! The pieces scattered. Leo’s ears drooped. He felt a warm puff of air in his chest, like a small growl that wanted to come out. “Oh,” he whispered.

Mei placed a gentle paw on a track piece. “We can try again,” she said. “In my house, we take turns at the table. One person passes, one person pours, and one says thank you.”

Amir smiled. “At my cousin’s picnic, we all stood in a line. We took turns to scoop rice and sauce. It kept the bowls steady.”

Asha swayed her scarf. “At my dance, we follow the drum. One step, then another. We wait, we listen.”

Diego fluffed his feathers. “Squawk! Take turns! Listen! Share!”

Leo blinked. The warm puff in his chest cooled like a soft breeze. “Okay,” he said. “Let’s be a team. We can take turns.”

They sat in a circle on the rug. Leo’s paws felt calm. The rug tickled his toes. He smelled the yummy cinnamon from the kitchen. Somewhere, a clock ticked, steady and quiet.

Leo took a deep breath. “We need a plan.”

Mei raised a paw. “We can make stations. One friend builds straight lines. One friend makes bridges. One friend checks the bumps. One friend decorates.”

Amir tapped his bells. “I can build straight lines. I’m good at making things in rows.”

Asha lifted a pillow. “I’ll make bridges and tunnels with pillows and blocks.”

Mei patted the tracks. “I’ll check for bumps and make sure they click.”

Diego hopped up. “I decorate! Squawk! Signs! Flags!”

Leo smiled. “I’ll be the helper who passes pieces and listens for help.”

They started. It sounded like music. Click. Clack. Tap. Jingle. Flap. The tracks clicked together, smooth and snug. Asha tucked a pillow under a bridge. “Feel this,” she said. “Soft and strong.”

Leo pressed a track. “Good click,” he said. He passed a curved piece to Amir.

“Thank you,” Amir said. He made a wide turn. “This is like the desert road my uncle drove on. Slowly around.”

Mei smoothed a bump. “No wiggle now,” she said. “Perfect.”

Diego stuck a tiny flag by a tunnel. “Hola! Namaste! Nin hao! Salaam!” he chirped. “Many ways to say hello!”

Leo’s ears perked up. “I like all our hellos,” he said. “I like our ways.”

They took turns with the wooden train engine. First, Leo pushed it over the bridge. The wheels hummed softly. Hmmmm. Then he passed it to Mei.

“Your turn,” he said.

Mei smiled. “Xie xie,” she said. “Thank you.” She pushed the train gently through the tunnel. It made a tiny echo. Tuh-tuh-tuh.

Amir took the engine next. “Shukran,” he said, smiling. He guided it around the wide turn. The bells on his ankles jingled. Jing-jing!

Asha wrapped her scarf around a stack of blocks to make a station. “Dhanyavaad,” she said. “Thank you.” She parked the train and made it go, “Chug-chug!”

Diego fluttered and laughed. “Gracias!” He added a paper sign: Team Train. He passed the engine back to Leo.

Leo felt warm and proud. Not the hot, pushy warm. A gentle, cozy warm—like a blanket. “Our train is long,” he said. He looked around. The track swooped under a chair, over a pillow bridge, and around the table leg. It was beautiful.

A small track piece was missing. Uh-oh. Leo crouched low. He listened. Tick, tock. He sniffed. The air smelled like crayons near the art box. He peeked under the couch. “Found it!” he called. Dust tickled his nose. “Achoo!”

Mei giggled. “Bless you.”

Leo clicked the last piece in, but he didn’t push the train. He held it out. “Diego, your turn.”

Diego took a tiny bow. “Teamwork!” he squawked. Everyone clapped.

They all sat close, shoulder to shoulder, paw to paw, wing to trunk. The train went around and around, humming. They listened to the soft sound. They smiled at each other.

Leo said, “I thought brave meant doing it all myself. But brave can mean asking friends, listening, and taking turns.”

Mei nodded. “Together is stronger.”

Amir chimed, “Together is kinder.”

Asha added, “Together is fun.”

Diego flapped. “Together is a team!”

They high-fived and low-fived. The living room felt extra warm and bright. The air smelled like cinnamon and crayons and friendship.

The End.
