# Nutmeg’s Story Day in the Home Office

Nutmeg the squirrel lived in a cozy home office at the top of a sunny house. The office smelled like warm paper and pine pencils. The desk was big and smooth. A soft chair squeaked a tiny hello when someone sat down. A little window let in golden morning light, and the keyboard made click-click sounds like tiny raindrops.

Nutmeg was resourceful. That means she could find smart ways to do things. She kept tiny acorns in a jar, sticky notes on the lamp, and colored paperclips in a small tin. Every day, she helped the home office feel calm and ready. Today, Nutmeg wanted to tell a story.

“Today is Story Day,” Nutmeg said with a happy wiggle of her tail. “I will share our family story.”

She hopped to the shelf and pulled down a little cloth bag. Inside were shiny buttons, a folded leaf, and a tiny bell. “These are my story treasures,” she whispered. “Grandma Squirrel gave them to me.”

Tick-tock went the clock. Click-click went the keyboard. Nutmeg set the treasures on the desk. She took a deep breath. She could smell the pencil shavings. She felt brave.

“First,” Nutmeg said, “I will practice.” She stood tall on the mouse pad. “Hello, friends. My name is Nutmeg. I have a story.”

But no one was there yet. The room was quiet. The lamp hummed softly.

“I need listeners,” Nutmeg said. “Stories like ears.”

Nutmeg made a plan. She was resourceful! She drew a big sign on a sticky note with a green crayon. It said, “Story Time! Come listen!” She stuck it near the window. She tapped the window with her tiny paw. Tap-tap.

Mrs. Ladybug peeked in from the flower box. “A story?” she asked.

“Yes!” said Nutmeg. “Please come.”

Mr. Paperclip wiggled out of his tin. “Click, I like stories,” he said with a little shine.

The Desk Lamp blinked gently. “I will be a warm light,” it said.

Soon, Tiny Dust Bunny rolled out from under the printer. “I listen,” it whispered in a soft, fluffy voice.

Nutmeg’s heart felt full. “Thank you,” she said. “We are ready.”

Nutmeg held up the shiny button. “This button is from Grandma’s red scarf,” she began. “Long ago, when the wind was cold, Grandma wrapped me in her scarf. She said, ‘Stories keep us warm too, Nutmeg.’ We sat by the window. We listened to the rain. Drip, drop, drippy drop. She told me about the first acorn she ever found. It was small but special.”

Mrs. Ladybug leaned closer. “What did she say?” she asked.

Nutmeg smiled. “She said, ‘Share the story, and the acorn grows in hearts.’”

Nutmeg showed the folded leaf. It smelled like the forest after rain. “This leaf is from our family tree,” she said. “Grandpa Squirrel taught me to listen before I speak. He said, ‘A good storyteller uses ears and heart.’ So I listen to the wind, to the clock, to my friends.”

Tick-tock, tick-tock, went the clock. The room felt cozy.

Nutmeg rang the tiny bell. It chimed like a little star. “This bell means it’s time to share,” she said. “Now I will tell a story, and then you can share too. That is our tradition.”

She stood tall again. “Once, little Nutmeg had a big problem,” she said. “Her favorite nut rolled under the heavy printer. She could not reach. She felt worried.” Nutmeg made a worried face. “But she took a breath. She asked for help. ‘Hello, printer,’ she said. ‘Can you move, please?’ The printer hummed. ‘I am very heavy,’ it said. ‘But I can shake a little.’”

Nutmeg giggled. “I used a ruler like a bridge,” she said. “I slid it in and scooped the nut. I said thank you to the printer. I said thank you to my ruler. We all worked together. That is how I solved my problem.”

Mr. Paperclip jingled. “Click-clack! Smart squirrel!”

Mrs. Ladybug clapped her tiny wings. “Brave and kind,” she said.

Nutmeg nodded. “A story is a gift,” she said. “It helps us remember. It helps us learn. It helps us feel close.”

Tiny Dust Bunny whispered, “My turn?” It rolled forward shyly.

“Yes,” said Nutmeg. “We share and we listen.”

Tiny Dust Bunny told a soft, swirly story about dancing in sunbeams and hiding from the vacuum. Mrs. Ladybug told a story about a bright yellow petal and a windy day. Mr. Paperclip told a story about holding papers together so they would not get lost.

Nutmeg listened with her ears and her heart. She smiled. She nodded. She asked gentle questions. “How did you feel?” she asked. “What did you learn?” She spoke clearly and kindly.

When all the stories were done, the office felt warm and happy. The lamp glowed. The keyboard was quiet. The air smelled like paper and a little bit like sunshine.

Nutmeg touched the button, the leaf, and the bell. “Thank you for sharing,” she said. “Today we kept a tradition. We told stories. We listened. We learned.”

“What did we learn?” asked Mrs. Ladybug.

Nutmeg’s tail swished. “We learned that stories help us talk. Stories help us understand. When we listen and share, we grow brave, kind, and smart. We can solve problems, remember our family, and make friends.”

Everyone smiled. The clock said tick-tock, like a happy heart.

Nutmeg put the treasures back in the cloth bag. She felt proud and calm. “Tomorrow,” she said, “we will tell more stories.”

“Yes,” said the lamp. “I will be ready.”

“Yes,” chimed the bell.

And the home office, with its soft chair and sunny window, felt like a small, warm theater for all their voices.

The End.
