# Monty Monkey Builds a Colorful Helper

Monty was a courageous monkey with bright eyes and gentle paws. He loved to help. One sunny morning, he bounced to the garage workshop at the end of the street. The garage smelled like fresh wood and warm oil. Sunbeams shone on jars of screws, paintbrushes, and little wheels.

“Good morning, tools!” Monty giggled. His tail curled with excitement. “Today, I want to build something to help the neighborhood.”

Ms. Dot, the friendly neighbor, waved from the door. She wore big, round glasses. “Hello, Monty! The park sign is old and hard to read. Could you make a new sign? One that is bright and easy for everyone to see?”

Monty stood tall. “Yes! I can do it. I will build a strong sign. I will paint it with art!”

Ms. Dot smiled. “Wonderful. Follow the directions on this paper. It tells each step. Step-by-step helps keep us safe.” She handed Monty a simple list with big pictures and numbers.

Monty read the first picture. “Step 1: Put on safety gear.” He found little monkey-sized goggles and soft ear covers. He slipped them on. “Click!” went the strap. “I am safe,” he said proudly.

He read the second picture. “Step 2: Gather tools.” Monty tapped each tool with his finger and said the names out loud. “Hammer. Sandpaper. Paint. Brushes. Screws. Screwdriver.” The tools clinked and clacked as he placed them on the big table. He liked the neat row. It made him feel ready.

“Step 3: Sand the wood.” Monty held a smooth board. He rubbed it with sandpaper. “Swish, swish,” went the sandpaper. Dust smelled like a pencil. The board felt silky. “Nice and smooth,” Monty said. “My hands tell me it is ready.”

“Step 4: Ask for help with big tools.” Monty looked at a drill. It made a buzzy sound. “Bzzzz.” He took a brave breath. “I will be careful and ask.” He called, “Ms. Dot, can you help me hold the board while I drill?” He was brave, but he was also smart.

“I will help,” said Ms. Dot. “Bravery is following directions and asking for help when needed.” Together, they made two neat holes. “Brrr-rrr,” went the drill.

“Step 5: Paint the sign.” Monty smiled. This was art time! He opened a red paint can. “Pop!” The paint smelled a little sweet. He dipped a brush. “Swoosh.” He painted the letters: PARK. He used big shapes that were easy to see. He added green leaves and a yellow sun. He patted his chest. “I am an artist and a builder!”

Monty looked at the next picture. “Step 6: Let it dry.” Waiting was hard. He wanted to run and show everyone. His tail twitched. He took a deep breath. “I can wait,” he told himself. He set a timer. “Tick-tick-tick.” While he waited, he cleaned the brushes with soapy water. “Swirl, swirl.” The bubbles smelled like lemons.

The timer went “Ding!” The paint was dry and shiny. Monty clapped. “Step 7: Screw the sign to the stand.” He placed the screws. “Righty-tighty,” he whispered, turning the screwdriver. “Lefty-loosey.” He followed the directions slowly. He checked each corner. He tugged the sign. It was strong.

Just then, a little mouse named Pip peeked in. “Squeak! Monty, can I help?”

“Yes, Pip,” said Monty kindly. “You can hold the bag of screws and count. Counting is a skill too.” Pip nodded and counted, “One, two, three, four,” in a tiny, happy voice.

“Step 8: Bring the sign to the park.” The sign was big. Monty puffed his cheeks. “Oof!” It was heavy. He felt a little worried, but he was brave. “I will ask for help again.”

Ms. Dot and Pip each held a side. Together they walked. The sun felt warm. The sign smelled like fresh paint and wood. Birds sang, “Cheep-cheep!” The grass tickled their toes.

At the park, children cheered. “Wow! It is bright! We can read it!” said a little girl. A grandpa smiled. “Thank you, Monty. You helped our community.”

Monty stood a little taller. His goggles glinted in the sun. “We followed the steps. We used our skills. We made art. We were brave. And we worked together.”

Ms. Dot nodded. “Yes. You also cleaned up, waited for paint to dry, and used tools safely. Those are important skills.”

Pip squeaked, “What is next?”

Monty looked at the empty paint cans and the neat tool row. He read the last picture on the paper. “Step 9: Clean and put tools away.” He hummed as he wiped the table. “Helping is also cleaning up,” he said.

When everything was tidy, Monty took a deep, happy breath. The park sign shone in the light. People could find the swings and the slide easily now. A little boy traced the letter P with his finger. “I can see it!” he laughed.

Monty smiled. “Today I learned to follow directions. I built a helpful thing. I made art with colors and shapes. I was brave and asked for help. I helped my community.”

Ms. Dot gave a thumbs-up. “That is skill-building, Monty. Step by step, you grow.”

Monty nodded. “I can do hard things when I take my time and follow the steps.”

He waved to everyone. “Have fun at the park!” His heart felt warm, like a cozy blanket.

The End.
