# Splash the Dolphin Learns to Share

In a cozy nursery filled with soft blue walls and fluffy white clouds painted on the ceiling, lived a very special friend. Splash was a small, gray dolphin who had come to visit the children during story time. The nursery smelled like vanilla candles and felt as warm as a big hug.

Splash loved to play with all the colorful toys scattered around the room. There were red blocks, yellow balls, and green puzzles everywhere. But Splash had a problem – he wanted to play with ALL the toys at the same time!

"Mine! Mine! Mine!" clicked Splash as he gathered toy after toy with his flippers. Soon, he had made a big pile of toys around himself. The soft carpet beneath him was completely covered.

Little Emma, who was only three years old, walked over with her friend Sam. "Can we play too, Splash?" Emma asked sweetly, her brown eyes sparkling with hope.

"No way!" Splash squeaked, splashing his tail. "These are MY toys now!"

Emma's bottom lip began to wobble, and Sam looked very sad. They sat down on the soft, squishy reading cushions and watched Splash play alone.

But something strange happened. Even though Splash had ALL the toys, he wasn't having fun anymore. The blocks felt heavy in his flippers. The balls didn't bounce as high. The puzzles seemed too hard to solve by himself.

"Why don't I feel happy?" Splash wondered aloud, looking around the quiet nursery. The only sounds were the gentle ticking of the clock and Emma sniffling softly.

Mrs. Rose, the kind nursery teacher, sat down beside Splash. Her voice was gentle like a lullaby. "Splash, can you think about how Emma and Sam might be feeling right now?"

Splash looked over at the two children. Emma was wiping her eyes with a tissue, and Sam was staring at the floor. Splash's heart felt heavy, like when storm clouds covered the sunny ocean.

"They look... sad," Splash said quietly. "Did I make them sad?"

"What do you think?" Mrs. Rose asked with a warm smile. "How would you feel if someone took all your favorite things?"

Splash thought very carefully. He remembered how awful he felt when the big waves once washed away his favorite shell. "I would feel very sad and left out," he realized.

Suddenly, Splash understood! Sharing wasn't about giving up his fun – it was about making MORE fun for everyone!

"Emma! Sam!" Splash called out cheerfully. "Would you like to build a castle with these blocks? I think it would be much more fun with friends!"

Emma's face lit up like sunshine. "Really? You want to share?"

"Yes!" Splash giggled, his dolphin smile stretching wide. "I learned that toys are much more fun when we play together!"

The three friends spent the rest of the morning building the most amazing block castle. They took turns, said "please" and "thank you," and helped each other when pieces fell down.

"Thank you for sharing, Splash," Sam said, giving the dolphin a gentle hug.

"Thank you for teaching me that sharing makes everything better!" Splash replied happily.

From that day on, Splash always remembered that the best games happen when everyone can play together. The nursery filled with laughter, friendship, and the joy of sharing.

The End.
