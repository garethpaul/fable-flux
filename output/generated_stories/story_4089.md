# Squeaky’s Pantry Plan

Squeaky the resourceful squirrel lived in a cozy nook by a sunny kitchen window. One morning, Squeaky peeked into the quiet pantry. It smelled like warm bread and sweet apples. The shelves were tall, the jars were shiny, and a soft light made tiny dust sparkles dance in the air.

Squeaky’s tummy gave a little rumble. “I want a snack,” Squeaky whispered. “But I must remember: the pantry belongs to the family. I need to ask, and I need a plan.”

Squeaky took a tiny notebook from a pocket and a short pencil from behind one ear. “Step one: ask. Step two: wait. Step three: share. Step four: say thank you.” Squeaky drew four little boxes and made neat checkmarks to practice.

Just then, Mia, the kind homeowner, opened the pantry door. The door made a soft whoosh, and cool air slipped out.

“Hi, Squeaky,” Mia said with a smile. “Are you visiting the pantry?”

Squeaky nodded and placed a paw on the edge of the shelf, then pulled it back. “This is your pantry, Mia. May I please have a snack? I brought my plan.” Squeaky held up the notebook.

Mia knelt down. “I like your plan. Thank you for asking and for respecting our pantry. That shows good boundaries.”

“What are boundaries?” Squeaky asked, ears twitching.

“Boundaries are kind rules,” Mia explained. “They tell us what belongs to us, what is safe to touch, and when to say yes or no. Boundaries help everyone feel safe and respected.”

Squeaky nodded. “I want to be respectful.” The little squirrel took a tiny step back so Mia could move freely.

Mia pointed to a basket on a low shelf. “This basket is for friends. You may choose from here when you ask first.”

Squeaky’s nose wiggled. The basket smelled like crunchy crackers and dried apple rings. The crinkle of the little paper bags sounded like tiny leaves. Squeaky looked up. “May I have one dried apple ring, please?”

“Yes,” said Mia. “Thank you for asking.”

Squeaky carefully took one ring. “Thank you!” The apple was chewy and sweet. Squeaky’s tail twitched with joy.

Just then, a fluffy neighbor rabbit named Poppy peeked in. “Hello!” Poppy said. “Snacks?”

Squeaky held up one paw. “Poppy, this is Mia’s pantry. We must ask before we take. It’s a boundary.”

Poppy blinked. “A boundary?”

Squeaky nodded. “Yes. We ask, we wait, we share, and we say thank you.”

Mia smiled. “That’s right. Poppy, you may pick from the friend basket if you ask.”

Poppy asked kindly, and soon both friends enjoyed a small snack. Crunch, crunch. The pantry felt friendly and calm.

After eating, Squeaky opened the notebook again. “I want to plan for tomorrow. If I get hungry, I can bring my own snack from home.” Squeaky drew a little acorn and a tiny water drop. “I will pack an acorn and a sip bottle.”

“That’s a smart plan,” Mia said. “Planning helps you keep your own boundaries, too.”

Squeaky looked around and saw a jar of bright red jam on a higher shelf. It sparkled like a ruby. “Ooo,” Squeaky whispered. “That looks yummy.” The little squirrel took one small hop forward, then stopped. “Wait. That jam is not in the friend basket. That jam is not mine.”

Squeaky took a deep breath and stepped back. “My boundary says, ‘I do not take things that are not for me.’” Squeaky smiled and felt proud.

Mia noticed. “I’m proud of you for stopping and checking in with yourself,” she said. “That is strong boundary work.”

Poppy clapped her soft paws. “Good job, Squeaky!”

Squeaky giggled. “Thank you. It helps to have a plan.”

Later, Mia had another idea. She placed a small, green sticker on the friend basket. “Green means go, with permission,” Mia explained. “These are for guests who ask.”

She placed a red sticker on the high shelf with the jam. “Red means stop. Please do not touch unless I offer it.”

Squeaky tapped the notebook again. “Green means go with asking. Red means stop and wait. That is easy to remember.”

Mia added one more shelf with a yellow sticker. “Yellow means ask first and wait for me to help.”

Squeaky and Poppy practiced. Squeaky pointed. “Green?” Poppy said, “Ask, then go.” They pointed to red. Together they said, “Stop.” They pointed to yellow. “Ask and wait.”

With a happy heart, Squeaky packed the small acorn snack for tomorrow. The little pouch felt smooth, and the acorn smelled nutty and good.

Before leaving, Squeaky turned to Mia. “Thank you for sharing your pantry and for teaching us about boundaries.”

“You’re welcome,” Mia said. “I like how you planned ahead and respected our space.”

Squeaky and Poppy waved goodbye. Their paws made soft pat-tap sounds on the floor as they scampered out. The pantry felt warm and peaceful again.

Outside by the window, Squeaky tucked the notebook away and said, “Today I learned that good boundaries are kind. I can ask, I can wait, I can plan, and I can say thank you. That keeps everyone safe and happy.”

Poppy nodded. “Let’s remember green, yellow, and red.”

“Green, yellow, red,” Squeaky repeated. “Respect, plan, and be kind.”

The End.
