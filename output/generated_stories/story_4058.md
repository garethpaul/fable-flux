# Song for the World Tree

In a tall, cozy treehouse, a small, blue singing bird named Lila lived with her friends. The treehouse smelled like pine needles and sweet sap. Sunlight made leaf-shadows dance on the wooden floor. A soft breeze hummed through the branches, like a friendly whisper.

Lila loved to sing. Her voice was clear and bright. “La-la-la!” she chirped. The sound floated like warm honey. One day, a big event was coming. It was the World Tree Concert. Birds from many places would visit. They would bring songs from their homes. Some sang fast. Some sang slow. Some used tiny drums. Some wore pretty feathers and bright scarves.

Lila felt fluttery inside. “I want to sing a welcome song,” she told her friends. “I want to make everyone feel happy and safe.”

Her friend Pip, a chipmunk, clapped his tiny paws. “We can help!”

Mina the squirrel twitched her tail. “Let’s get ready!”

Lila puffed her chest. “I can sing without a plan,” she said. “I will just sing anything.” Her voice wobbled a little.

Pip tilted his head. “Maybe we should prepare,” he said gently.

Mina nodded. “We can make a list.”

But Lila flew in a fast circle. “No time! La-la-la!” She sang a quick song and giggled. The notes bounced around the treehouse. They sounded fine to her.

The next morning, birds from far places arrived. A red bird wore a tiny golden bell. It jingled. A green parrot from a warm island brought a little drum. Tum-tum! A brown nightingale had a soft shawl with tiny stars. It felt silky. The treehouse filled with colors, sounds, and sweet smells of fruit and flowers they carried.

“Welcome,” Lila chirped. “I will sing a welcome song now!”

She opened her beak. She tried to sing words in many styles at once. Fast and slow, loud and soft. Her voice tangled. The notes bumped into each other. “La… la… oh no,” she squeaked. Her tummy felt twisty. She wanted to make everyone smile, but some birds looked puzzled.

The red bird with the bell stepped closer. “It is okay,” he said kindly. “In my home, we practice before big songs.”

The parrot nodded. “We prepare with friends,” she said. “We listen to each other’s music.”

Lila’s feathers drooped. “I did not prepare,” she whispered. “I wanted to. I was excited. But I thought I could do it alone.”

Pip held out a tiny paper. “Here is a list,” he said. “We can still get ready.”

Mina spread a small blanket and set out berries and water. “Let’s plan. We have time if we work together.”

Lila took a deep breath. She felt the cool air in her chest. She heard the leaves rustle. She smelled sweet berries. Her heart slowed down. “Yes,” she said. “Let’s prepare.”

First, they made a plan.
- Listen: “Please share a little of your song,” Lila said to each bird.
- Learn: “Teach me one special thing from your home.”
- Practice: “We will try it together.”

The red bird rang his bell softly. Ding… ding… “We bow before we sing in my home,” he said.

“Oh!” Lila bowed her head. “That is beautiful.”

The parrot tapped her drum. Tum-tum-ta! “We count to three first,” she said. “Uno, dos, tres!”

Lila smiled. “I can count. One, two, three!”

The nightingale wrapped her shawl around Lila’s shoulders for a moment. It felt like a hug. “We begin with a whisper,” she said. “Hmmm…”

Lila closed her eyes. She listened to each friend. She tried the bow. She tried the drum count. She tried the whisper-start. The sounds felt new and special. Her heart felt warm. “Your songs are important,” she said. “Thank you for teaching me.”

They practiced together. Pip tapped sticks on the floor. Click, click. Mina hung shiny leaves to make the treehouse pretty. The sun turned golden. The air smelled like warm wood and berry juice.

“Are we ready?” Lila asked. She felt excited, but this time she felt calm too.

“We prepared,” Pip said.

“We cooperated,” Mina added.

“We shared our culture,” the parrot chirped, her feathers gleaming.

Night came. Little lanterns glowed like fireflies. The treehouse was full. Lila stepped forward. She bowed her head like the red bird taught her. The room grew quiet. She whispered first, like the nightingale. Then she counted, “One, two, three.” The parrot tapped the drum. Tum-tum-ta.

Lila sang her welcome song. It was simple and sweet. It carried the bell’s gentle ring. It used the drum’s steady beat. It held the soft night whisper. Her voice was clear and bright. The song wrapped around the visitors like a warm blanket.

When she finished, the treehouse burst into happy chirps and claps. Ding! Tum-tum! Click, click! Lila’s eyes sparkled. She felt strong and proud.

“Thank you for sharing with me,” she said. “When we prepare and work together, our song is better. When we listen and learn, we show love for each other.”

The birds nodded. They felt seen and welcome.

Lila looked at her friends. “Next time, we will make a plan again,” she said. “We will prepare. We will cooperate. We will celebrate each other’s songs.”

Everyone smiled. The World Tree Concert had begun, and the treehouse felt like home to all.

The End.
