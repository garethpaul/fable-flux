# Benny Bear's Big Feelings

Benny Bear was a fluffy little cub with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, he woke up feeling excited to play with his favorite toy boat in the bathtub. His mama had promised him extra bath time today!

"Bath time, Benny!" called Mama Bear from the bathroom. The warm, steamy air smelled like lavender bubbles, and Benny could hear the gentle splash of water filling the big white tub.

But as Benny walked toward the bathroom, something strange happened. His tummy started to feel funny - like butterflies were dancing inside. His paws felt sweaty, and his heart began beating faster like a little drum. "Thump-thump, thump-thump!"

"What's wrong with me?" Benny whispered to himself, stopping in the doorway. The bathroom suddenly seemed very big and echoey.

Mama Bear noticed Benny standing still. "Come on in, little one. The water is just right - nice and warm!"

But Benny's legs felt wobbly. Yesterday, he had slipped in the bathtub and gotten soap in his eyes. It had stung and made him cry. Now, just thinking about the bathtub made him feel scared and worried.

"I... I don't want to take a bath," Benny said in a small voice, looking down at his fuzzy paws.

Mama Bear knelt down beside him, her voice gentle and caring. "Oh, sweetie, you look upset. Can you tell me what you're feeling right now?"

Benny's bottom lip trembled. He wanted to be brave, but the scared feelings were so big! "My tummy feels twisty, and my heart is beating really fast," he said honestly. "I think... I think I'm scared of the bathtub."

"Thank you for telling me the truth about your feelings," Mama Bear said, giving him a warm hug that smelled like honey. "It's very brave to share when we feel scared. Can you tell me why you feel this way?"

Benny took a deep breath. "Yesterday I slipped and got soap in my eyes. It hurt, and I don't want it to happen again."

"Ah," said Mama Bear, nodding with understanding. "So you're feeling worried because something scary happened before. That makes perfect sense! Fear is a feeling that tries to keep us safe."

Mama Bear sat on the bathroom floor with Benny. "You know what? All feelings are okay to have. When we feel scared, we can take deep breaths together." She showed him how to breathe in slowly through his nose and out through his mouth. "Like smelling flowers and blowing out birthday candles."

They practiced breathing together. Benny felt his heart slow down a little.

"Now," said Mama Bear, "let's think of ways to make bath time feel safer. We could use less soap, put a special mat in the tub so you won't slip, and I'll stay right here with you."

Benny considered this. The scared feeling was still there, but it felt smaller now. "Could we also bring my toy boat? He's brave, and he might help me feel brave too."

"What a wonderful idea!" Mama Bear smiled. "You're being so courageous by talking about your feelings and thinking of solutions."

Slowly, Benny stepped into the bathroom. With the rubber mat under his paws, Mama Bear right beside him, and his brave toy boat floating nearby, the bath didn't seem so scary anymore.

"I did it!" Benny giggled as he splashed gently. "I felt scared, but I was honest about it, and that helped me be brave!"

"You certainly did," said Mama Bear proudly. "Remember, it's always okay to have big feelings, and talking about them helps us feel better."

The End.
