# Miko the Mouse and the Dinner Note

Miko was a kind little mouse who lived behind a small hole in the dining room wall. Every morning, the dining room smelled like toast and jam. The table felt smooth under Miko’s tiny paws, and the sun made golden squares on the floor.

One day, Miko peeked out and saw crumbs all over the floor. A spoon was sticky with honey. A napkin lay crumpled like a little mountain.

“Oh my whiskers,” Miko said softly. “This room needs care.”

Just then, he heard a sniffle. It was Mia, the little girl who lived in the house. She sat at the dining table with her head down. Her crayons were scattered. A big paper lay in front of her, clean and white. Her eyes were shiny with tears.

Miko’s heart felt warm and tight. He climbed onto a chair leg and squeaked, “Hello?”

Mia looked around. “Who’s there?”

“It’s me, Miko,” said the mouse. “I’m a good friend. I listen.” He wiggled his whiskers and sat very still.

Mia blinked. “A mouse who listens?”

“Yes,” said Miko kindly. “What is wrong?”

Mia sniffed. “I’m supposed to write a thank-you note to Grandma. But I don’t know what to say. And I still have to help with chores. It’s all too much.”

Miko nodded. He felt the heavy feeling in Mia’s words. He closed his eyes and imagined being small and stuck with a big, blank paper. He imagined hands tired from picking up crumbs.

“That sounds hard,” Miko said softly. “I hear you. You feel worried and tired.”

Mia nodded. “Yes.”

Miko took a deep breath. The dining room smelled like apples. A tiny breeze moved the curtains. “When I feel stuck,” said Miko, “I start small. One crumb. One kind word. Would you like help?”

Mia wiped her eyes. “Yes, please.”

“First,” said Miko, “let’s make the room smile.” He jumped down and found a tiny broom. Swish, swish. “Can you pick up the napkins?”

Mia stood up. “I can do that.” Crinkle, fold. Crinkle, fold. The napkins made soft sounds as she stacked them.

Miko hummed a gentle tune. He brushed the crumbs into a little pile. The broom tickled the floor. “We are a team,” he said. “Chores can be quick when we help each other.”

Mia smiled. “Teamwork!”

The table looked neat. The spoon shined after Mia washed it. The floor felt smooth again. Miko’s nose twitched happily. “Now the room can rest,” he said.

Mia sat back down. “But the note,” she whispered. “What do I write?”

Miko climbed onto the table and sat near the paper. He looked at Mia’s face. “Let’s write with our hearts,” he said. “Close your eyes. Think about Grandma. What makes your heart feel warm?”

Mia closed her eyes. She saw Grandma’s soft hands. She heard Grandma’s laugh, like little bells. She tasted Grandma’s warm cookies. “Grandma reads me stories,” Mia said. “She hugs me tight. She listens.”

Miko’s eyes sparkled. “Those are good words.” He pointed to the paper. “Say thank you for one thing. Then tell one feeling. Then add one promise. Like this: ‘Thank you for reading to me. I feel happy with you. I will draw you a picture.’”

Mia picked up a purple crayon. Scratch, scratch. The crayon made friendly sounds. “Dear Grandma,” she whispered as she wrote. “Thank you for reading me bedtime stories. I feel happy and safe with you. I will draw you a sunny picture.”

Miko clapped his tiny paws. “Beautiful! Your words are kind and true.”

Mia drew a bright sun with smiling rays. She added a cookie with chocolate chips. “Grandma will laugh,” she said.

Miko smiled. “Your note is a hug made of words.”

Mia looked at the clean dining room. She looked at her note. Her chest felt light. “Miko,” she said, “you helped me with chores and with writing. You understood how I felt.”

Miko nodded. “That is empathy,” he said gently. “Empathy means I try to feel what you feel. Then I can help with care.”

Mia tilted her head. “Can I try empathy too?”

Miko pointed to the tiny broom, tired from all the swishing. Mia giggled. “I think the broom needs a rest.” She set it down nicely. Then she looked at Miko. “You worked hard. Would you like a cozy crumb of cookie?”

Miko’s whiskers wiggled. “Yes, please.” He nibbled a tiny crumb. It tasted buttery and sweet.

The dining room glowed with the afternoon sun. The air smelled clean and warm. Mia folded the note and put it in an envelope. “I feel proud,” she said.

“I feel proud of you too,” said Miko. “We listened. We helped. We wrote with love.”

Mia patted the table. “Thank you, Miko. Today I learned to clean with care, to write with feelings, and to use empathy.”

Miko bowed. “Kind hearts make strong teams.”

They both smiled, and the dining room felt like a happy hug.

The End.
