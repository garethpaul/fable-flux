# Panda Pippa Says “I’m Sorry”

Panda Pippa was a patient panda. She had soft black-and-white fur and warm, gentle eyes. Every afternoon, Pippa padded to the musical practice room. The room smelled like polished wood and lemon soap. Sunlight made squares on the floor. Drums sat in a row. A shiny piano waited. Colorful scarves hung on a hook, ready for dance.

Pippa loved to dance to music. She practiced little steps. Tap, tap, slide. Tap, tap, slide. Her bamboo snack was packed neatly in a small bag. Pippa liked to be prepared.

One day, Pippa opened the door and heard music. Ting-ting from tiny bells. Boom-boom from a small drum. Her friend, Foxy Finn, was there. Finn had bright eyes and quick feet. He hopped and twirled with a red scarf.

“Hi, Finn,” Pippa said, smiling. “I brought my scarf and my bamboo snack. I’m ready to practice.”

“Hi, Pippa!” Finn wagged his fluffy tail. “Let’s dance fast today!”

Pippa took a big breath. “We can dance fast after we warm up,” she said. “Warm-ups help our bodies get ready.”

Finn squeaked a bell. “I want to start now!” He jumped and spun. Whoosh! His scarf brushed Pippa’s nose. It tickled. Pippa giggled.

Pippa set the bell on the table. “Let’s try claps first,” she said kindly. “Clap, clap, pause. Clap, clap, pause.”

They clapped together. Clap, clap, pause. Clap, clap, pause. The sound was crisp and bright. Pippa’s feet made tiny tap sounds on the floor. Finn’s paws thumped with happy energy.

Then Pippa said, “We can count. One, two, three. One, two, three. Counting helps us dance together.”

“One, two, three!” Finn shouted. He dashed to the drum and hit it too hard. BOOM!

The loud boom made Pippa jump. Her bamboo snack rolled out of her bag. It went plop on the floor. The lid popped off. Little bamboo pieces scattered. Pippa’s tummy rumbled. She had planned to eat after dancing.

“Oh,” Pippa said softly. She felt a pinch in her chest. She wanted to be calm. She put her paws on her belly and took a slow breath. In. Out. In. Out.

Finn stopped moving. He saw the bamboo on the floor. He saw Pippa’s face. Her smile was gone. The room was quiet now. Only the piano sat still and shiny. The lemon smell felt stronger, like a reminder to be careful.

“I didn’t mean to do that,” Finn said. His ears drooped. “I got excited. I forgot to be gentle.”

Pippa nodded. “I know you didn’t mean it,” she said softly. “I feel sad. My snack is on the floor. I wanted to eat it later.”

Finn’s cheeks felt hot. He wanted to fix it. He wanted to say the right words. He took a breath like Pippa did. In. Out.

“I’m sorry, Pippa,” Finn said. His voice was clear and kind. “I made a big boom. I scared you. I knocked your snack down. I will help clean up.”

Pippa’s face warmed with a small smile. “Thank you for saying sorry,” she said. “Thank you for helping.”

They worked together. Finn held the bag open. Pippa picked up the bamboo pieces. Tap, tap, tap, they dropped into the bag. Finn wiped the floor with a soft towel. Swish, swish. The room felt calm again.

Pippa zipped the bag. “Next time, we can talk first,” she said. “We can say what we need.”

Finn nodded. “I need to move,” he said. “I feel jumpy when I hear music.”

“I need a plan,” Pippa said. “I feel safe when we prepare.”

They smiled. Pippa pointed to a big paper on the wall. “Let’s make a dance plan,” she said.

Together, they drew simple pictures:
- A sun for warm-up time
- A drum for gentle beats
- Footprints for slow steps
- A star for fast dance at the end
- A snack picture for clean-up and rest

Pippa tapped the drum, soft and steady. Dum, dum, dum. Finn counted. “One, two, three.” They moved their feet. Tap, tap, slide. Their scarves swished, whoosh-whoosh, like wind in trees.

Soon, it was time for the fast part. Finn smiled. “Star time!” he said.

“Ready,” Pippa said. “We warmed up. We counted. We talked. We are prepared.”

They danced quick and light. Spin, clap, slide! Their laughter sounded like bells. Ting-ting-ting! The room felt bright and happy again.

After the dance, Finn looked at Pippa. “Thank you for being patient,” he said. “I learned to say I’m sorry. I learned to talk about what I need.”

Pippa hugged Finn. Her fur was soft and cozy. “Thank you for saying sorry,” she said. “I learned to say what I need, too. Preparation helps us. Communication helps us. Apology helps hearts feel better.”

They sat on the floor. Pippa shared a fresh bamboo piece with Finn. Crunch, crunch. It tasted sweet and green. The piano keys glowed in the sun. The lemon smell felt light and clean.

Pippa and Finn looked at their dance plan on the wall. It made them feel ready for next time.

Pippa said, “Our plan helped us dance. Your apology helped my heart. Next time, we will talk first, prepare, and move with care.”

Finn nodded. “And if I make a mistake,” he said, “I will say, ‘I’m sorry,’ and help fix it.”

Pippa smiled. “That is a good plan.”

The End.
