# The Clever Cat’s Big Build

In a sunny garage workshop, a clever cat named Clover liked to sit on a soft blue mat. The mat smelled like clean soap and warm wood. Tools hung on the wall—silver wrenches, bright screwdrivers, and a friendly red hammer. A little fan hummed, and dust danced in the light.

Clover purred, “I want to build something today.”

She sniffed the air. “Mmm, sawdust and glue.” Her whiskers tickled. She looked around at the wood blocks, the nuts and bolts, and a stack of wheels in a yellow box.

On the workbench was a big sign that read: “Build with care.” Clover tapped it with her paw. “I will. I want to build a little wagon, so I can pull my toys.”

Clover lined up four wooden blocks. “Step one: plan,” she said. “Planning is smart.” She found a pencil and made a simple picture: a box, four wheels, and a string for pulling. She smiled. “I have a plan!”

But when Clover tried to lift the big wooden board for the wagon base, it wobbled. It felt heavy. It smelled like fresh pine and felt rough under her paws.

“Oh no,” Clover whispered. “This is hard.” Her tail twitched. She felt a little scared. What if she dropped it? What if it broke?

Clover sat down and took a brave breath. In, out. In, out. “I can do brave things,” she said softly. “I will be careful and think.”

She looked around. “Strategy time.” She slid a small stool next to the board. “I can lift one end and rest it here.” She pushed gently. The board moved up, scratch-scratch on the stool. “Good,” Clover said. “Smart step.”

She found sandpaper. “Step two: smooth.” She rubbed the board in little circles. Scratch, scratch. The wood became soft and safe. “No splinters,” she said, proud.

Next, Clover picked four little wheels from the yellow box. They were round and cool in her paws. “Step three: attach wheels.” She found tiny screws and a screwdriver with a blue handle. “Lefty loosey, righty tighty,” she sang.

She held the first wheel in place, but the screw rolled off the bench—plink, plink—under a shelf. Clover’s ears drooped. “Oh no.”

She peeked under the shelf. It was dark and dusty. A little spider web shook in the breeze. Clover felt tingly and a little scared. But she remembered her brave breath. In, out. “I can be brave,” she said. She found her small flashlight and clicked it on. Click! A warm circle of light showed the shiny screw. Clover reached in with a magnet stick and pulled the screw out with a happy clink.

“Got you!” she purred. “That was brave and smart.”

Clover tried again. She held the wheel and turned the screw. Righty tighty. Whirr, whirr. It worked! The wheel spun, soft and smooth. Clover smiled and did the next wheel, and the next, and the last. Spin, spin, spin!

“Step four: make the box,” Clover said. She used wood glue that smelled sweet and sticky. She pressed the pieces together and counted to ten. “One, two, three…” Her paws felt warm. Her heart felt proud. She added small nails with the red hammer. Tap, tap, tap. “Gentle taps,” she whispered. “I am careful.”

She tied a red string to the front. She tugged it, and the knot held tight. “Step five: test!” Clover set a soft teddy in the wagon and pulled. The wheels rolled across the smooth floor. Clover giggled. “It works!”

Then she heard a tiny rattle. The wagon made a little wiggle-wobble. “Hmm,” said Clover. “There is a problem.”

She stopped and thought. “When things wobble, we can fix them.” She checked each wheel with her paw. One screw was not tight. “Ah-ha!” She turned the screw a little more. Righty tighty. The wiggle went away.

Clover pulled the wagon again. Roll, roll, roll. No rattle. No wobble. The teddy smiled, and Clover’s whiskers wiggled with joy.

Just then, a big box of blocks slid off a high shelf—thump! The blocks scattered. Clover’s heart jumped. She crouched, ears perked. “That was loud!”

She looked up. The shelf was a little tilted. “This could be not safe,” Clover said. She felt nervous, but she also felt strong. “I can fix it.”

“Strategy time again,” she said. She moved her wagon in front of the shelf and used it to carry the heavy blocks safely to the floor. Roll, roll. She cleared the weight. Then she found a small level—the little bubble inside floated to the side. “Not straight,” she said.

Clover found two sturdy brackets and four big screws. She held the shelf with a clamp. Clamp, snap. She turned each screw slowly. Whirr, whirr. She checked the level again. The bubble moved to the middle. “Straight and safe,” Clover said.

She put the blocks back, one by one. Thunk, thunk. She felt calm and proud. The garage smelled like wood and a little like victory.

Clover looked at her wagon. “I built this with a plan, brave breaths, and smart steps,” she said. She clipped a little bell to the string. Ding-ding! “I did it by myself, and I asked my brain for help.”

She pulled the wagon around the workshop and sang, “Plan, build, test, and fix. That’s the way to do good tricks!”

Clover sat on her blue mat and petted her teddy. “Today I learned: building takes planning, brave hearts, and careful hands. When there is a problem, I can stop, think, and try a new way.”

She smiled and purred. “I am a clever cat who can build.”

The End.
