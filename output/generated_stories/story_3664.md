# Buzzy Beaver and the Tumble Trouble

Buzzy the beaver loved to help. He lived with his family near a cozy house. One rainy day, drip-drop rain tapped the windows. The air smelled soapy and warm in the laundry room. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer spun, “Swish, swish.” Buzzy’s whiskers twitched with excitement.

“I want to help today!” Buzzy said, patting his wide, flat tail on the floor. “I can take initiative.”

Mama Beaver smiled. “That means you start helping all by yourself. You see a need and you try. That’s wonderful, Buzzy. But remember: think first, then act.”

Buzzy nodded. “Think first. Then act.”

The laundry room was full of baskets and soft towels. A big blue basket had clean clothes. A little red basket had socks. A mountain of towels waited, fluffy and warm. Buzzy rubbed one against his cheek. “So soft,” he giggled. He loved the fresh, lemony smell.

Suddenly—clunk, clunk, clunk! The dryer made a loud noise and stopped. The light blinked. The drum would not spin.

“Oh no,” said Mama Beaver. “The dryer stopped. We need dry towels for bath time.”

Papa Beaver peeked in. “I have to fix the roof leak. I can’t fix the dryer right now.”

Buzzy looked at the towels and then at the blinking light. His small heart beat fast. “I can try to help,” he said. “I can use my thinking brain.”

He took a deep breath. In. Out. “Think first. Then act.”

Buzzy listened. No “Swish, swish.” Only quiet. He touched the dryer door. “It feels a little warm.” He looked around the room. He saw a lint trap on top.

“I remember,” Buzzy said. “We clean the lint to help air move. Clean air helps dry.”

He pulled out the lint screen carefully. Fuzzy gray lint puffed up. It tickled his nose. “A-choo!” he sneezed.

Mama laughed. “Good noticing, Buzzy.”

Buzzy picked the lint with two small paws. It felt soft like clouds. He put the lint in the trash. “Clean, clean,” he said. He slid the lint screen back in. He looked at the control buttons. “Do not touch without Mama,” he whispered to himself. “Think. Ask. Act.”

“Mama, may I try to press start?” Buzzy asked.

“Yes,” Mama said. “You thought first. You cleaned the lint. Now we try.”

Buzzy pressed the button. The dryer blinked. “Beep!” But it did not spin.

Buzzy’s whiskers drooped. “It didn’t start.”

Mama patted his shoulder. “It’s okay to try again. We use a growth mindset. We can learn.”

Buzzy nodded. “I can try a new idea.”

He remembered the big blue basket. “Maybe we can dry towels another way.” He looked at the room. He saw a chair. He saw a broom. He saw some clothespins in a jar. They clicked together in his paw. Click, click.

“I have an idea!” Buzzy said. “We can hang the towels. Air can help dry them.”

Mama smiled. “That is initiative, Buzzy. You saw a problem. You chose to try a safe, smart way.”

Buzzy and Mama set the chair near the sink. They stretched a clean string from a hook to the broom handle and tied it low, safe and snug. The string felt rough under Buzzy’s paws. He clipped clothespins to the line. Click. Click. Click.

They hung towels one by one. Drip. Drop. Tiny drops fell into a pan Buzzy set on the floor. Plink. Plink. The room smelled even more lemony now.

Buzzy fanned the towels with a big book. “Woosh, woosh,” he sang. “Dry, dry, little towels.”

Mama opened a small window. Cool air came in. It kissed Buzzy’s nose. “Brr!” he giggled.

Buzzy checked the towels. “Still wet,” he said. “But a little less wet.” He smiled. “Drying takes time. I can wait. I can keep trying.”

He made more lines across the laundry room, low and safe. He used the chair and two sturdy baskets to hold the broom. He tested each knot. Tug-tug. “Strong,” he said.

He sorted the towels by size. “Big ones here. Small ones there.” He spaced them out. “Air needs room,” he told himself. “Room to whoosh.”

While they waited, Buzzy found loose socks in the red basket. He matched them by color and stripes. “Blue-and-blue. Green-and-green. Rainbow and… rainbow!” He cheered when he found a friend for a lonely sock.

The towels began to feel warm and light. Buzzy touched one. “Almost dry!” he said. Pride warmed his chest. He had started helping all by himself. He had used his thinking brain. He had not given up.

Papa Beaver came back, shaking rain from his hat. “What a sight!” he chuckled. “A beaver built a towel dam!”

Buzzy laughed. “It’s a towel line, Papa! We used air and time and clips.”

Papa touched a towel. “Dry and fluffy. Great solution.”

Later, Papa fixed the dryer with a new belt. “All better,” he said. The dryer went “Swish, swish” again.

Buzzy looked at the shiny machine, the clean lint screen, and the neat lines of clothespins. He felt brave and calm.

Mama knelt beside him. “What did you learn today, Buzzy?”

Buzzy thought. He tapped his tail. “I learned to take initiative. I can see a problem and try to help. I think first. I ask for help when I need it. I try new ideas. If it doesn’t work, I try again. That’s a growth mindset!”

Mama hugged him. “Yes. You used careful thinking and you didn’t give up.”

Buzzy hugged back. The laundry room was warm and lemony and full of happy “Swish, swish” sounds. He smiled. “Tomorrow, I will help again.”

The End.
