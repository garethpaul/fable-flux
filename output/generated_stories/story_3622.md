# Joey Roo’s Balcony Garden Plan

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey who loved to bounce. He lived with Mama on a small balcony garden. The garden smelled like mint, felt warm in the sun, and looked bright with yellow flowers. Birds sang soft songs in the morning. The wind made the chimes go ting-ting-ting.

One sunny day, Joey’s nose twitched. “Sniff, sniff! I smell something new,” he said.

Mama looked at the balcony floor. There were empty juice boxes, a crinkly water bottle, and a cardboard roll. “Oh dear,” Mama said gently. “We had a little party, and we made a little mess.”

Joey’s ears perked up. “I can help! I am a big helper,” he said, bouncing once.

Mama smiled. “Let’s make a plan,” she said calmly. “Planning helps us do things step by step.”

Joey clapped his small paws. “Step one! We look. Step two! We sort. Step three! We make art!”

Mama nodded. “That’s a great plan. And we tell the truth if we make a mistake. That is honesty.”

Joey looked at the balcony. He saw a paper bag, the plastic bottle, and the cardboard roll. He also saw a soft, dirty rag. He listened to the city sounds—honk, honk from far away, and the soft whisper of leaves. He took a deep, calm breath. “I am ready.”

“Let’s start with looking,” Mama said.

Joey walked slowly. Tap, tap, tap went his little feet. He picked up the bottle. “This feels smooth,” he said. “Is it recycling?”

“Yes,” said Mama. “Plastic bottles go to recycling. Paper and cardboard do too. The rag is trash.”

Joey nodded. “I will be careful.”

They sorted together. Joey said, “Plastic bottle in the blue bin. Paper bag and roll in the blue bin. Rag in the small trash.”

Joey stopped. He had found a shiny candy wrapper. It looked like a rainbow. He liked it very much. His paws wiggled. He put it in his pocket.

Mama saw him pause. “Joey,” she asked softly, “did you find something?”

Joey’s cheeks felt warm. He looked at his pocket. He looked at Mama. He took a calm breath, like the wind. “Yes,” he said. “I took the shiny wrapper. I wanted it for me. I didn’t ask.”

Mama gave him a gentle hug. “Thank you for telling the truth,” she said. “That is honesty. We can still use it. Wrappers are usually trash, but we can make art with it first.”

Joey smiled. “Art!”

“Now our plan says step three is art,” Mama said. “Let’s make a calm garden sign with recycled things.”

They washed the bottle and the cardboard roll. Splash, splash went the water. Joey giggled at the bubbles. He patted the paper bag dry with the clean corner of the rag.

Mama drew a big oval on the cardboard. Joey said, “It feels bumpy!” He glued the paper bag on the oval. He pressed with his small paw. “Sticky!”

Mama cut the shiny wrapper into little squares. “They look like tiny windows,” Joey said.

“We can make the word ‘Calm’ with the squares,” Mama suggested. “We can plan the letters first. Planning helps us.”

Joey arranged the squares on the oval. C... A... L... M. He moved them slowly. He did not rush. “I am calm,” he whispered. The chimes went ting-ting-ting again.

They glued the squares. Joey added the cardboard roll as a stand. Mama tied the clean ribbon from the paper bag around it. Joey found two leaves from the mint plant. They smelled fresh. He pressed them at the bottom like little green feet.

“Ta-da!” Joey said. “Our Calm Sign!”

They placed the sign by the mint. The balcony garden looked happy. The sun made the shiny squares sparkle. Joey felt proud in his chest. It felt warm and big.

Mama said, “What did we learn today?”

Joey put a paw to his chin. “We made a plan. We followed the plan. We told the truth. We used recycling. We made art. We stayed calm.”

“And how does the garden feel now?” Mama asked.

Joey looked around. The floor was clean. The blue bin was full of good things to recycle. The trash bin was small and light. The flowers glowed in the sun. He took another soft breath. “The garden feels peaceful,” he said.

Just then, a breeze came. It smelled like mint and sunshine. The chimes sang. Joey held Mama’s paw. “I like helping,” he said.

Mama smiled. “Helping, planning, and being honest make our world better,” she said. “And recycling gives old things a new life.”

Joey bounced once, gently. “Today was a good day. Tomorrow, we can plan again!”

The End.
