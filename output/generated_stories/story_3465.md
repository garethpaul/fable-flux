# Bolt's Heart Circuit

In the cozy basement lab, Bolt the robot dog sat on his charging pad. His shiny silver body gleamed under the warm lights. The lab smelled like metal and electricity. But Bolt's LED eyes flickered sadly.

"Beep beep," Bolt whimpered softly. His mechanical tail drooped down.

Today, Bolt was supposed to help Dr. Sarah with her big experiment. But every time he tried to be gentle with the delicate glass tubes, his metal paws were too clumsy. *Crash!* Another tube broke.

"Oh no," Dr. Sarah sighed, cleaning up the pieces. "Bolt, maybe you should just watch today."

Bolt's circuits felt heavy. His heart light dimmed to a faint blue glow. "I want to help," he beeped quietly. "But I keep breaking things. I'm not good at being gentle."

Dr. Sarah knelt down beside Bolt. She patted his smooth head with her soft hand. "I know you want to help, Bolt. Your heart is in the right place. But maybe we need to practice being gentle first?"

Bolt's ears perked up. "Practice? How do we practice gentleness?"

Dr. Sarah smiled. "Let's start small. See this fluffy cotton ball?" She held up something white and soft. "Can you pick it up without squishing it?"

Bolt extended his metal claw very, very slowly. He thought about being as light as a feather. *Whirr-click.* His claw gently touched the cotton. It stayed fluffy and perfect!

"Good job!" Dr. Sarah cheered. "Now try this soap bubble."

Bolt watched the rainbow bubble float in the air. He moved even more carefully, like he was touching a cloud. The bubble bounced off his claw without popping!

"I did it!" Bolt's tail wagged with excitement. His heart light glowed bright green.

"Now," said Dr. Sarah, "let's practice with something that needs our help."

She brought out a tiny toy mouse that had lost its squeaker. "This little mouse is sad because it can't squeak anymore. Can you help fix it with compassion?"

Bolt looked at the small toy. He thought about how sad he felt when he couldn't help. The mouse must feel the same way. His heart light pulsed with understanding.

Very gently, Bolt picked up the mouse. He cradled it in his soft rubber paw pads. "Don't worry, little mouse," he whispered. "We'll make you better."

Working together, Bolt held the mouse steady while Dr. Sarah carefully put in a new squeaker. Bolt's movements were smooth and gentle, like he was holding a baby bird.

"Squeak!" went the mouse when they finished.

"We did it!" Bolt barked happily. "We helped the mouse together!"

Dr. Sarah hugged Bolt's warm metal body. "You learned something very important today, Bolt. Compassion isn't just wanting to help. It's taking time to understand how others feel and practicing the skills to help them gently."

Bolt's heart light sparkled like a star. "So when I practice being gentle, I can show more compassion?"

"Exactly!" Dr. Sarah nodded. "And when we work together with patience and practice, we can solve any problem with kindness."

From that day on, Bolt practiced gentleness every morning. He learned to hold flowers without crushing their petals. He learned to pet the lab cat so softly she purred. And most importantly, he learned that true compassion comes from the heart, grows with practice, and works best when friends help each other.

Bolt became the most helpful robot dog in the whole lab, not because he was the strongest or the fastest, but because he had the biggest, most caring heart of all.

The End.
