# Tilly Turtle’s Sparkly Attic Day

Tilly was a shy turtle who liked quiet places. One sunny afternoon, she climbed the little wooden stairs and peeked into the quiet attic. The air felt cool and a tiny window let in soft light. Dust danced like glitter in the sunbeams. It smelled a little musty, like old books and sleepy blankets.

“Hello?” Tilly whispered. Her voice was soft, like a feather.

In the corner, a tall mirror stood, a bit foggy with dust. Boxes rested under a quilt, and a toy drum sat by a basket of buttons. Tilly smiled shyly. “I will have a quiet play day,” she said.

She set down her tiny backpack. Zip! She took out a small comb, a washcloth, a little soap, and her favorite yellow toothbrush. “I brought my clean-up kit,” she said, patting it proudly. “Clean feels comfy.”

A squeak came from under the quilt. Out popped a fluffy mouse with bright eyes. “Squeak! I’m Mimi,” the mouse said. “I live here.”

“Hi, Mimi,” Tilly said, tucking her head a little. “I’m Tilly.”

Mimi sniffed. “Squeak! The attic is home, but it’s dusty. Sometimes my whiskers tickle. Sometimes I forget to wash my paws.”

Tilly nodded. “Dust makes me sniffle too. I like to wash up. It helps me feel brave.”

Just then, the toy drum wobbled. Out rolled a giggly spider with shiny legs. “I’m Pip the spider!” Pip said, tapping the drum. “Tap-tap! Who wants to play?”

Tilly smiled. “I do, but first… I like to clean. It’s my hygiene time.”

Pip blinked. “Hy-gee-what?”

“Hygiene means keeping our bodies clean,” Tilly said softly. “It keeps us healthy. We wash, brush, and tidy. It makes us feel good.”

Mimi wiggled her whiskers. “Squeak! But I want to play now.”

Tilly felt her cheeks warm. She was shy, but she took a breath. “How about we make a plan?” she said. “We play after we clean a little. We can do it together.”

Pip tapped the drum. “A clean-and-play plan? That sounds fair. That’s good negotiation!”

Tilly nodded. “We can take turns. I wash up first. Then you. Then play!”

They agreed. Tilly took her washcloth. She dipped it in a little bowl of water she found near a plant. Drip, drop. “Warm water feels nice,” she said. She added a tiny bit of soap. Bubbles bloomed and smelled like lemons. Swish, swish. Tilly washed her face, her little legs, and her shell edges. She dried with a soft towel from her backpack. Pat, pat. “Ahh,” she sighed, feeling fresh and brave.

“Your shell looks shiny,” Mimi said.

“Thank you,” Tilly said, smiling. “Now you try.”

Mimi dipped her paws. “Squeak! The water tickles.” She scrubbed her paws and whiskers. “Bubbles feel fun.” She shook them off. “I feel clean and light!”

Pip used a tiny corner of the cloth for his legs. “Tap-tap, scrub-scrub,” he sang. “Clean legs for webby jigs!”

Tilly held up her yellow toothbrush. “Tooth time,” she said. She put on a pea of toothpaste and brushed in little circles. “Brush, brush, brush. Front teeth, back teeth,” she hummed. “Spit in the bowl. Rinse. All done!”

Mimi copied her with a tiny brush from a box. “Brush, brush,” she sang. Pip pretended with a dry twig and laughed.

After washing and brushing, the attic felt different. Tilly opened the window a little. Fresh air smelled like grass and sunshine. The dust danced away. “Let’s tidy together,” Tilly said. “Cooperation makes it fast.”

They worked as a team. Tilly wiped the mirror. Squeak-squeak went the cloth. Now the glass was clear and bright. Mimi gathered buttons into the basket. Click, clack, plink. Pip spun tiny silk ties to bundle loose ribbons. Swish-swish.

“Look!” Tilly said. She could see her face in the mirror now. Her eyes sparkled. Her shell gleamed. She stood taller. “I feel proud. I did a good job.”

Mimi bounced. “Squeak! I feel proud too. My paws are clean, and my whiskers are neat. Thank you for teaching us.”

Pip tapped a happy beat. “Clean drum, clean room, happy boom-boom-boom!”

Tilly’s heart felt warm. She had spoken up, even though she was shy. She had shared her hygiene plan. She had listened to her friends and made a fair plan. That was good negotiation. And they had all helped—cooperation!

“Play time now,” Tilly said with a grin.

They made a game called Sparkle Steps. They took three clean steps, then did a twirl. Step, step, step, twirl! The floor felt smooth under their toes. The attic smelled lemon-bright and airy. They laughed and clapped. Pip did a tiny jig. Mimi squeaked a happy tune. Tilly tapped the drum with gentle hands—dum, dum—feeling confident and calm.

When the sun started to nap, they packed up. Tilly tucked her clean-up kit away. “I’m grateful for friends,” she said. “Thank you for helping clean.”

“Thank you for showing us how,” Mimi said.

“Thank you for the plan,” Pip added. “Now I like hygiene time!”

Tilly smiled at her clear reflection. “Clean keeps us healthy. Clean helps us feel brave. We can wash, brush, and tidy every day.”

They all nodded. “Every day!” they said together.

Tilly climbed down the little stairs, feeling proud and strong. The quiet attic was still quiet, but now it was fresh and shiny, like a smile.

The End.
