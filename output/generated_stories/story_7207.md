# Oliver the Owl's Big Helper Goal

Oliver the Owl lived in a cozy guest room at Grandma Rose's house. His soft brown feathers rustled as he perched on the wooden bedpost, watching the morning sunlight dance through the lace curtains.

"Hoot, hoot!" Oliver called softly. He loved this special room with its fluffy pillows and sweet lavender smell. But today, Oliver felt worried.

Yesterday, he had heard little Tommy crying in the hallway. Tommy's favorite toy truck was missing, and he couldn't find it anywhere. Oliver's big golden eyes had seen everything from his perch, but he was just an owl. How could he help?

"I want to help Tommy find his truck," Oliver said to himself, fluffing his feathers thoughtfully. "But I need a plan!"

Oliver remembered what Grandma Rose always told the children: "When you have a big goal, break it into small steps."

"That's it!" hooted Oliver excitedly. "I'll make a goal with steps!"

Oliver closed his eyes and thought carefully. His first step would be to search the guest room thoroughly. His second step would be to look for clues. His third step would be to find a way to show Tommy where the truck might be.

Oliver began his search, his sharp eyes scanning every corner of the guest room. He looked under the flowery bedspread - nothing but dust bunnies that made him sneeze. "Achoo!"

He checked behind the rocking chair where Grandma Rose read bedtime stories. Still nothing, but he could smell the faint scent of her vanilla perfume.

Then Oliver flew to the tall dresser and peered behind it. His heart jumped with excitement! There, wedged between the dresser and the wall, was Tommy's bright red toy truck!

"Hoot, hoot! I found it!" Oliver whispered happily. But now came the tricky part - how could he tell Tommy?

Oliver thought about his goal again. He needed to be fair and make sure Tommy got his truck back. That would be the right thing to do.

When Tommy came into the guest room that afternoon to help Grandma Rose fold laundry, Oliver had an idea. He gently picked up a small sock in his beak and dropped it near the dresser.

"What's that, Oliver?" Tommy giggled, walking over to pick up the sock.

Oliver hooted softly and flew to perch on top of the dresser, looking down at the space behind it.

Tommy's eyes grew wide. "Are you trying to show me something?"

Oliver nodded his feathered head and hooted again, pointing his wing toward the gap.

Tommy got down on his hands and knees and peered behind the dresser. "My truck!" he shouted with joy. "Oliver, you found my truck!"

Tommy carefully pulled out his beloved red truck, hugging it tight. The truck's wheels still spun perfectly, and its bright paint gleamed in the afternoon light.

"Thank you, Oliver!" Tommy said, gently stroking Oliver's soft feathers. "You're the best friend ever!"

Oliver felt warm and happy inside. His goal-setting plan had worked perfectly! He had broken his big goal into small steps, worked hard to complete each one, and helped his friend in a fair and kind way.

From that day on, Oliver and Tommy were the very best of friends. And whenever Oliver had a new goal, he remembered to make a plan with small steps.

"Goals are like flying," Oliver would hoot wisely. "You go a little bit at a time until you reach where you want to be!"

The End.
