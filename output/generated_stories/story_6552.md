# Fern's Cozy Corner Team

Fern was a curious fox kit with bright eyes and soft, rustly fur. Today, the study corner smelled like warm paper and pine. Sunlight made golden squares on the carpet. A little clock went tick-tick like a tiny woodpecker. Fern’s whiskers twitched with excitement.

“I’m going to make a Picture Book,” Fern said, patting a stack of smooth pages. “It will have colors and shapes and stories.”

On the low shelf sat the art things. There were fat crayons, round stickers, glue that smelled sweet, and a friendly pair of safety scissors. They all waited like quiet helpers.

Fern spread the pages out on the floor. “First, a red circle,” she whispered. She pressed a circle sticker onto the page. It made a soft pop. “Next, a blue square.” The square sat beside the circle like a neighbor.

A soft knock came from the open doorway. Pip, a tiny mouse with bright whiskers, peeked in. “Hi, Fern,” Pip squeaked. “Can I… watch?”

Fern’s tail swished. “You can help,” she said. “We’re making a Picture Book.”

Pip climbed onto a cushion. His little paws made a gentle shh sound on the fabric. “I like circles,” he said. “They look like cheese.”

“Let’s make a cheese moon,” Fern giggled. She drew tiny holes on the red circle with a brown crayon. “Cheese moon!”

Another friend, Sora the sparrow, fluttered to the windowsill. Her feathers rustled like paper leaves. “Cheep! Are you making art? I can bring feathers for texture,” she chirped.

Fern blinked. She felt a small squeeze in her belly. “I… I planned the pages already,” she said softly. “I picked circles and squares.” Her ears tipped back.

Pip watched Fern’s ears. “Are you feeling tight-inside?” he asked.

Fern took a slow breath. “A little. I wanted it to be my way.”

Sora tilted her head. “We can follow your plan,” she tweeted gently. “But maybe one page could be a feather page? Only if you want.”

Fern looked at the crisp pages. She liked her plan. She also liked her friends. The clock went tick-tick. Fern pressed her paw to her chest and breathed in. The air smelled like crayons and pine. She breathed out. The tight feeling softened.

“I want us to make it together,” she said. “We can add a feather page. We can make a cheese moon and a bird nest. We can share.”

Pip’s whiskers wiggled. “Team book!”

Sora flutter-hopped to the carpet. “Cheep!”

They made a plan on a sticky note. Fern drew simple boxes. “Page 1: Shapes. Page 2: Feather page. Page 3: Feelings. Page 4: Team picture,” she said, tapping each box. “We’ll take turns.”

Pip clapped his tiny paws. “That is a good plan. That is a skill,” he squeaked. “A turn-taking plan!”

Fern smiled. “Skill-building,” she repeated, proud of her big new word.

They began. On Page 1, Fern placed shapes and named them. “This circle is cheese moon. This square is a window.” Her voice was calm and happy.

On Page 2, Sora dropped two soft feathers into Fern’s paw. The feathers felt silky, like a whisper. Fern dabbed glue and pressed them down, tap-tap. “Soft,” she said. Sora chirped a gentle song, like warm tea.

On Page 3, Pip drew faces with simple lines. One face had up-curvy eyes. “Happy,” he said. One face had a little rain under the eyes. “Sad.” Fern added a small heart near each face. “Feelings are all okay,” she said. “We can name them.”

Halfway through, Pip’s tail slipped in a dot of glue. “Eep!” His tail stuck to the page. He pulled. The paper crinkled. Pip’s eyes got shiny. “I messed up,” he squeaked in a tiny voice.

Fern felt a quick bump of worry. Then she pressed her paw to her chest again. In, out. Calm. “It’s okay, Pip,” she said softly. “Accidents happen. We can fix it.”

Sora pecked gently at the glue blob. “Cheep-cheep, we can make it a cloud.”

Fern smoothed the crinkle with careful paws. “Yes! A fluffy cloud.” She drew little swirls. Pip’s eyes un-shined. He took a breath. “Thank you,” he whispered. “I felt scared. Now I feel better.”

“At the study corner, we help,” Fern said. “That is what a team does.”

For the last page, they drew themselves. Fern added her fox ears and a bright, friendly smile. Pip drew his whiskers long and proud. Sora colored her wings with tiny strokes. They wrote, in big simple letters: “We all belong.”

Fern held the book close. It felt warm and a little bumpy from the feathers. She could hear the soft crinkle of the pages. “I like our book,” she said. “It has your ideas and my ideas. It is our ideas together.”

Pip nodded. “No one left out,” he said. “Not even the glue blob.”

Sora laughed, a sweet chirp. “Cheep! Inclusion means everyone gets a spot.”

Fern looked at her friends. Her belly felt light and floaty now, like a balloon. “When we share, listen, and take turns, we make something better,” she said. “I felt tight-inside at first. I used my calm breaths. I used a plan. And I chose to include.”

They placed the Picture Book on the low shelf. It glowed in the sun squares. The clock went tick-tick, happy and small.

Fern curled her tail around her paws. “Our study corner is for everyone,” she whispered. “We all belong.”

The End.
