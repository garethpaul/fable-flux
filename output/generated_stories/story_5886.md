# Tilly Turtle’s Picnic Plan

Tilly was a shy turtle who lived with her family. One sunny morning, Mama Turtle called from the dining room, “Tilly, we’re having a picnic in the park today!”

Tilly peeked around the doorway. The dining room smelled like warm toast and sweet strawberries. Sunlight danced on the table. The chairs made little squeaks on the floor. Tilly tucked her head in her shell just a tiny bit. “A picnic?” she whispered. “Outside?”

Mama smiled. “Yes, dear. You can help us get ready.”

Tilly’s heart thumped, thump-thump. She liked quiet places. The big park felt big in her mind. What if it was too loud? What if the grass was too tickly? Tilly looked at the table and had an idea.

“I will practice here,” she said softly.

“How will you practice?” asked Papa Turtle, pouring juice that went glug, glug.

“With preparation!” Tilly said. The word felt brave in her mouth.

She climbed onto a chair. The dining room table became her pretend world. She spread a green napkin and whispered, “This is our grassy hill.” She put a blue plate down. “This is the pond.” She set a yellow cup in the middle. “This is the sun.” She giggled. “It’s shining bright!”

Her big sister, Tasha, clapped. “Good idea, Tilly!”

Tilly made a list with small, neat words. “We need: water, snacks, a blanket, hats, and a plan.” She tapped each finger. “And a quiet spot.”

Mama nodded. “Let’s prepare together.”

They worked as a team. The bread smelled toasty. The strawberries looked red and shiny. The cheese felt smooth and cool. Tilly put each item into the basket, counting softly. “One, two, three.” She added a tiny bell. “If I feel shy, I can ring this for help,” she said.

Papa handed her a small, cozy scarf. “This can be your comfort scarf.”

Tilly rubbed it on her cheek. It felt soft like clouds. “Thank you.”

Next, they practiced picnic rules in the dining room. Tasha said, “We sit on the blanket.” She patted a folded towel. Tilly sat and wiggled. “We use gentle voices,” Mama said. They all spoke quietly. “We clean up when we are done,” Papa added, clink, clink, placing spoons in a bowl.

Tilly closed her eyes and used her imagination. The dining room became the park. She could almost hear birds chirp. She could almost smell flowers. She whispered, “Hello, birds.” Her shy body felt a little less tight.

Then Tasha asked, “What if it is windy?”

Tilly looked around and smiled. She put a fork on each corner of the napkin. “We hold the blanket corners with forks,” she said. “That is preparation.”

“What if the grass is too tickly?” Papa asked.

Tilly put two napkins together. “Double blanket!” she said.

“What if you need a quiet shell-time?” Mama asked.

Tilly touched her scarf and her bell. “I wear my scarf and ring my little bell. We take a quiet break.”

Everyone cheered softly. Tilly felt warm in her tummy.

They were almost ready to go when there was a knock on the door. It was Benny Bunny from next door, bouncing a little. “Can I come?” he asked. His ears twitched. “I hop fast!”

Tilly smiled a shy smile. “I am slow,” she said. “I like quiet.”

Benny’s ears drooped. “I’m noisy.”

“That’s okay,” Tilly said. “We are different. We can celebrate that.” She pointed to the basket. “We prepared a plan. We can make a hop path and a slow path.”

In the dining room, they practiced again. Tilly put a line of spoons on one side. “Hop path.” She put a line of forks on the other side. “Slow path.” Benny hopped, boing, boing, along the spoons. Tilly walked slowly along the forks. They giggled. “We did it!” Benny laughed.

At last, they went to the park. The grass smelled fresh. The sky was big and blue. Tilly held her scarf and stayed close to Mama. She put down the blanket and used the fork corners, just like she had practiced.

A friendly squirrel chattered nearby. Benny hopped on the hop path. Tilly stepped on the slow path. A breeze came. The blanket tried to wiggle, but the forks kept it still. “Our plan works,” Tilly said, smiling.

A group of kids played tag. They ran fast. Tilly watched and felt her shy tummy wobble. She took a breath. “I will have shell-time,” she said. She rang her small bell, ding-ding. Mama nodded and sat with her. They ate a strawberry. It tasted sweet and juicy.

After a little rest, Tilly felt brave again. “I want to share our paths,” she told the kids. She pointed and said, “Hop path and slow path! We can all play.”

The kids tried both. “I like the slow path,” said a girl with a big grin. “I can look at flowers.” A boy laughed, “I like the hop path! Boing!” Everyone found a way to enjoy the game.

Tilly’s shy heart felt proud. Her plan helped everyone. Her imagination and preparation made the picnic kind and fun.

On the walk home, Papa said, “What did we learn today?”

Tilly thought. “We learned to prepare. Preparation makes big things feel smaller. We can use our imagination. We can celebrate our differences. Slow or fast, quiet or loud, we can plan and play together.”

Mama squeezed her hand. “That is a wonderful lesson, Tilly.”

Tilly smiled, warm and brave. “I think I like picnics.”

The End.
