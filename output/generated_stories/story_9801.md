# Rosie Rabbit's Clean Workshop

Rosie Rabbit loved to build things in her cozy garage workshop. She had shiny tools hanging on the wall, colorful paint cans lined up on shelves, and wood pieces stacked in neat piles. But today, something was very wrong.

"Oh my!" said Rosie, twitching her pink nose. "What's that yucky smell?"

She looked around her workshop and gasped. There were dirty handprints on her workbench, sticky glue smeared on her tools, and sawdust covering everything like a dusty blanket.

"I can't build anything in this messy place," Rosie said sadly. "But I really want to make a birdhouse for my friend Chirpy!"

Rosie sat down and thought carefully. She remembered what her mama always said: "When you have a big problem, break it into small steps and follow them one by one."

"That's it!" Rosie exclaimed, her ears perking up. "I need to clean everything step by step!"

First, she went to the sink and washed her paws with warm, soapy water. The bubbles tickled her fur and smelled like fresh lemons. "Clean paws first," she said to herself. "That's what Mama taught me."

Next, Rosie looked at her dirty tools. She filled a bucket with warm water and added a squirt of soap. One by one, she carefully washed each tool, scrubbing away the sticky glue and dirt.

"Scrub, scrub, scrub," she sang happily. "Clean tools work the best!"

Then she wiped down her workbench with a damp cloth, watching the dirty handprints disappear. The wood began to shine again.

"Now for the sawdust," Rosie said, looking at the dusty mess on the floor. She got her little broom and swept carefully, pushing all the sawdust into a neat pile. Then she scooped it up with her dustpan.

As she cleaned, Rosie noticed something wonderful happening. The yucky smell was going away! Instead, her workshop smelled fresh and clean, like the soap and the sweet wood shavings.

"Wow!" said Rosie, looking around. "My workshop looks amazing!"

But she wasn't finished yet. Rosie remembered another important rule: always put things back where they belong. She hung each tool in its proper place on the wall. She organized her paint cans by color. She stacked her wood pieces neatly.

Finally, Rosie washed her paws again at the sink. "Clean paws at the end too," she said, drying them with a soft towel.

Now her workshop sparkled! The tools gleamed, the workbench shone, and everything smelled wonderful.

"Perfect!" said Rosie. "Now I can build Chirpy's birdhouse!"

She worked happily all afternoon, using her clean tools on her clean workbench. When she finished, she had made the most beautiful little birdhouse, painted bright blue with a yellow roof.

"Thank you, Rosie!" chirped her friend when she saw the gift. "It's perfect! Your workshop always smells so nice and clean."

Rosie smiled proudly. "I learned that keeping things clean and following the right steps makes everything work better," she said. "Clean paws, clean tools, clean workspace – that's the secret to building wonderful things!"

From that day on, Rosie always cleaned as she worked and washed her paws before and after every project. Her workshop stayed beautiful, and she built amazing things for all her friends.

The End.
