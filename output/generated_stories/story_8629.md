# Nutmeg Solves the Lab List

Nutmeg was a small, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a busy tail. She lived near a house with a basement lab. The lab was cool and dry. It smelled like soft soap and warm bread from the kitchen above. Little lights blinked. Gentle hums filled the air. It sounded like a friendly bee.

Today, Nutmeg had a job. She had a list! The list was taped to a jar with a blue lid. Three checkboxes were drawn with green crayon.

The list said:
1. Water the seed.
2. Sort the buttons by color.
3. Test the wind fan.

Nutmeg whispered, “I can do it. I can finish each job, one by one.”

She put the list in her tiny backpack. She took a deep breath. “Sniff,” she said. The basement smelled safe and busy. “First job!” She tapped the first box.

At a sunny lamp, a little seed sat in a small cup of dark soil. The soil looked like chocolate cake. A tiny tag said, “Bean from Nana’s garden.” Nutmeg smiled. “This bean is special from Nana. That is cultural appreciation,” she said gently. “We care for gifts from family.”

She found a little watering can. It was yellow with a smiley face. “Glug, glug,” went the water. The soil drank it up. Nutmeg felt the dirt with one paw. “Damp, not soggy,” she said. “Just right.” She wiped a drip and smiled. “Task one done.” She drew a neat check. It felt good.

“Second job,” Nutmeg said. She hopped to a tray of buttons. The buttons were inside a round tin that smelled like cinnamon. The buttons were red, blue, green, and yellow. Some were shiny like stars. Some were smooth like pebbles.

Nutmeg thought, “How do I sort fast? I will use bowls.” She looked around. She saw four bowls on a shelf. “Perfect.” She labeled them with sticky notes: RED, BLUE, GREEN, YELLOW.

She began. “Plink, plink,” went the buttons. She sang while she worked. “Red in red. Blue in blue. Green in green. Yellow too!” Her tail swished to the beat.

Suddenly, she found a big button with tiny flowers. It was many colors. Nutmeg paused. “Hmm,” she said. “Critical thinking time.” She did not rush. She looked at the bowls. She looked at the button. “This button has many colors. Maybe it needs a special place.”

She made a new note: RAINBOW. She put the button in a small glass jar. “All buttons belong,” she said. “We can make a place when we need one.” She felt proud. “Task two done!” She checked the second box.

“Last job,” Nutmeg said. “Test the wind fan.” The fan looked like a small pinwheel with soft, safe blades. It was on a little stand with stickers from many places. One sticker had a panda. One had a drum. Another had a kite. Nutmeg touched the drum sticker. “People around the world make music,” she said. “We can listen and learn. That is cultural appreciation too.” She smiled.

Nutmeg flipped the switch. Click! The fan hummed. “Whirrr,” it said. A gentle breeze moved her whiskers. “Ooo, that feels cool,” Nutmeg giggled.

Then she heard a soft cry from the corner. “Peep… peep.”

Nutmeg turned off the fan. “Hello?” she called. She followed the sound to a small, open box. Inside was a tiny moth with a bent wing. Its wing shimmered like silver paper.

“Oh dear,” said Nutmeg. “You are scared.” She knelt down. “I will help you.” She remembered to be gentle. She cupped her paws and spoke softly. “It’s okay. I see you. I care.”

She thought, “The fan breeze might be too strong.” Critical thinking again. She moved the fan away. She made a little tent from a clean tea towel. She set a cap of water nearby. She dimmed the lamp so the light was not too bright.

“Here is shade. Here is a sip,” she said. The moth rested. “You are safe.”

Nutmeg looked at the list. The last box was not checked yet. “I still need to test the fan,” she said. “But I can do it kindly.”

She placed a paper pinwheel by the fan, not the moth. She taped the pinwheel to a stick in a lump of clay so it would not fall. She turned the fan to low. “Whirr,” it hummed softly. The pinwheel spun. “Whoosh, whoosh.”

“It works!” Nutmeg cheered. She checked the last box. All three boxes were done. Nutmeg felt warm and proud inside, like a cozy blanket.

She wrote a note for her friend who owned the lab. It said, “I watered the bean. I sorted the buttons. I tested the fan with a pinwheel. I helped a moth feel safe. Love, Nutmeg.”

She placed the note by the blue-lid jar. She peeked at the moth. “Peep,” it whispered. Its wing moved a little better.

Nutmeg waved. “I finished my tasks. I used my brain. I was kind.”

She zipped her backpack. She looked around the lab. It was neat and calm. The lights blinked like tiny stars. The hum was soft and proud, like a happy song.

Nutmeg climbed the steps and said, “Today I learned: We finish our jobs, we think before we act, and we care for others. That helps our home and our town. That is civic responsibility.”

She smiled. “One by one, we can do it.”

The End.
