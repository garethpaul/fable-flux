# Tilly Turtle Finds Her Song

Tilly was a shy turtle with a soft green shell. She moved slowly and liked quiet. One sunny day, Tilly peeked into the musical practice room. It was warm and bright. The wooden floor felt smooth under her little feet. The room smelled like lemon polish and fresh paper. Instruments rested in neat rows: shiny bells, tiny drums, a big piano with black and white keys, and a tall stand holding colorful song sheets.

Tilly whispered, “Hello, music.” Her voice was small, like a breeze.

Ms. Melody, the music teacher, smiled kindly. “Hello, Tilly Turtle,” she said. “Would you like to make music today?”

Tilly’s heart went thump-thump. She was shy. “Maybe,” she said, hiding a little behind her shell.

Ms. Melody walked softly, so her steps sounded like gentle taps. “Music is a friend,” she said. “It helps us speak with sounds. Music can be loud or soft. Music can be happy, sleepy, or brave.”

Tilly listened. She heard the piano breathe. Plink-plonk, plink-plonk. She heard a drum go tum-tum. She heard bells chime like tiny stars. Ting-ting-ting!

Three children came in, smiling. “Hi, Tilly!” said Ben, waving. “Want to play with us?”

Tilly took a slow breath. “I can try,” she said.

Ms. Melody clapped a steady beat. Clap, clap, clap. “Let’s start with hello,” she said. “In music, we can say hello in many ways.”

She played one note on the piano. “Hello,” sang the note, soft and low.

Tilly felt brave enough to hum. “Hmm-hello,” she hummed, very quiet.

“That was lovely,” said Ms. Melody. “Music is a language. We can use sounds to show feelings. Listen.”

She played a happy pattern: do-re-mi! The notes rose like sunshine.

Tilly smiled. “That sounds like a smile,” she said.

“Yes,” said Ms. Melody. “Now listen.” She played slow, low notes: do… do… do… They felt heavy, like big steps.

“That sounds like a big elephant,” Ben giggled.

Tilly giggled too, a soft turtle giggle. “I want to try,” she said.

Ms. Melody showed Tilly a small, kind drum. It was warm to the touch. “Tap with two fingers,” she said.

Tilly tapped. Tum. Tum-tum. The sound was gentle, like a heartbeat. She liked it.

“Beautiful,” said Ms. Melody. “Let’s count. One, two, three, four.” The children counted together. Their voices blended, like colors mixing.

Tilly tapped with the counting. Tum, tum, tum, tum. She felt the beat in her chest. She felt the room listening. It felt safe.

“Now we will practice soft and loud,” said Ms. Melody. “Soft is called piano. Loud is called forte.”

“Pee-ah-no,” Tilly repeated. “For-tay.” The words felt bouncy in her mouth. She liked the new sound words. Language can be fun, she thought. Music words are like little songs!

“Show me piano,” said Ms. Melody.

Tilly tapped the drum very softly. tum… tum…

“Show me forte,” said Ms. Melody.

Tilly tapped a little bigger. TUM! TUM!

Everyone laughed with joy, and Tilly laughed too. She was still shy, but her laughter felt brave.

Next, Ms. Melody gave her a small bell. It was smooth and cool. “This bell sings high,” said Ms. Melody. “High notes can sound like bird songs.”

Tilly shook the bell lightly. Ting! The note flew up like a tiny bird. She closed her eyes and listened. Ting… ting… ting…

“It sounds like morning,” Tilly said.

“That’s language, Tilly,” said Ms. Melody. “You are telling a story with sound.”

Tilly stood a little taller in her shell. “Can we make a song together?” she asked.

“We can,” said Ms. Melody. “Let’s make a hello song. We will use beats, high notes, and words.”

The children made a circle. Ms. Melody whispered, “We start simple. We practice. Practice helps us grow.”

Ben tapped a steady beat on a big drum: boom, boom, boom, boom.

Mia played the piano: do-re-mi, do-re-mi. The keys felt smooth under her fingers, and the notes danced.

Tilly rang her bell: ting… ting… ting… Her bell joined the beat like a sparkle on water.

Ms. Melody sang, “Hello, hello, we say hello,” in a gentle voice.

Tilly took a breath. She was shy, but she remembered: music is a friend. She sang quietly, “Hello, hello, we talk with sound.” Her voice was soft, but true.

“Beautiful!” said Ms. Melody. “Let’s try again. We can improve by practicing.”

They tried again and again. Each time, Tilly felt braver. Her bell was bright. Her drum was steady. Her voice grew a little stronger.

Finally, they sang one more time. “Hello, hello, we say hello. Hello, hello, we talk with sound. Soft and loud, high and low. Practice, practice, we learn and grow.”

The room glowed with music. Tilly’s shell felt warm. Her shy heart felt proud. She had found her song.

Ms. Melody clapped softly, like rain. “Tilly, you learned the music language today. You listened, you tried, and you improved.”

Tilly smiled. “Music helps me speak,” she said. “I can be shy and still be strong.”

Ben grinned. “Tomorrow we can make a goodbye song!”

“Yes,” said Tilly. “We can say many things with music.”

And from that day, Tilly Turtle visited the musical practice room every day. She listened, she played, and she sang. She practiced a little each time. Little by little, her music grew, like a tiny seed growing into a tall, green plant.

Tilly learned that music is a language, practice makes us better, and shy hearts can shine.

The End.
