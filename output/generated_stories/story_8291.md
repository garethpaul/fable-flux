# Nutmeg Says, "My Turn, Please!"

Nutmeg was a small, resourceful squirrel with a fluffy tail and bright, curious eyes. Nutmeg lived in a cozy bedroom with soft pillows, a little lamp that glowed warm and yellow, and a window that let in sweet morning air. The room smelled like clean blankets and pine cones. Nutmeg kept a tiny basket of treasures: a shiny button, a smooth pebble, and a red ribbon tied in a bow.

Today was a busy, happy day. Nutmeg wanted to build a book tunnel on the bed, try a new tooth-brushing song, and call Aunt Hazel on the toy phone. Nutmeg wiggled toes under the blanket and whispered, “I can do it. One thing at a time.”

Tap-tap. Someone knocked on the bedroom door.

It was Tilly, the neighbor mouse, with pink ears and quick feet. Behind her was Barney, a big, gentle beaver who lived down the hall. They liked visiting Nutmeg’s cozy room.

“Can we play?” asked Tilly, bouncing.

“Yes!” said Nutmeg. “I have ideas.”

Barney spotted the toy phone on the nightstand. “I want the phone first,” he rumbled. He reached for it with his wide paw.

Nutmeg’s tummy felt tight. Nutmeg also wanted to call Aunt Hazel. The phone was Nutmeg’s special toy. Nutmeg took a deep breath and pressed paws to the chest, like hugging courage.

In a clear, calm voice, Nutmeg said, “I hear you want the phone, Barney. I want a turn, too. Let’s take turns.”

Barney paused. “But I saw it first.”

Nutmeg nodded. “I understand. It is important to you. We can make a plan.”

Tilly climbed on the bed and peeked at the books. “I want to build the tunnel right now!” she squeaked.

All at once, the room felt busy. Voices were loud. The lamp glowed bright. Nutmeg’s heart went thump-thump.

Nutmeg remembered the little rule taped near the lamp: Breathe, Speak, Plan. Nutmeg made shoulders soft with a slow breath in… and out. The room felt calm again.

Nutmeg stood tall on the bed and said in a kind, strong voice, “Friends, I have words. I want to use the phone first to call Aunt Hazel. Then Barney can have the next turn. While I call, Tilly can help me stack books for the tunnel. After the call, we can all crawl through. Is that fair?”

Tilly’s whiskers twitched. “Can I choose the first book for the tunnel?”

“Yes,” said Nutmeg, smiling. “You may choose a big one.”

Barney scratched his chin. “How long is your phone turn?”

Nutmeg looked at the little sand timer on the shelf. “I will use the timer. One turn is this much sand.”

Barney nodded. “Okay. I like the timer plan.”

Nutmeg lifted the toy phone and pressed the big red button. Beep! The sound was cheerful. Nutmeg spoke clearly, “Hello, Aunt Hazel! I am in my cozy bedroom. I feel brave and calm. Today we are building a tunnel.” The sand flowed down, soft and whispery. Tilly chose a big blue book and a small yellow book. She stacked them carefully. “This one is the door,” she said.

Barney watched the sand. “I am waiting,” he said. “Waiting is hard, but I can do it.”

Nutmeg said, “You are doing great, Barney. Thank you for waiting.”

The sand finished. Swish. Nutmeg clicked off the phone and handed it to Barney. “Your turn now.”

Barney smiled with his big teeth. “Thank you.” He pressed the green button. Beep! “Hello, Grandpa Beaver! I am at Nutmeg’s. We will crawl through a book tunnel.”

While Barney talked, Nutmeg and Tilly made the tunnel strong. The books smelled like paper and dust. The blankets were soft like clouds. Tilly hummed a little tune. Nutmeg slid the smooth pebble under the tunnel, like a treasure to find.

Barney finished his call and set down the phone. “All done,” he said. “Now tunnel time?”

“Yes!” said Nutmeg. “But first, water sip and hand wipe.” Nutmeg brought a small cup of water and a damp cloth. “Healthy habits help us feel good.” Everyone took a sip and wiped their paws. The water was cool and fresh.

They took turns crawling. Tilly went first. “My turn,” she said. “I feel excited!” She wriggled through and found the pebble. “Treasure!”

Barney went next. “My turn.” He shuffled slowly so the books stayed steady. “I feel proud.”

Nutmeg’s turn was last. “My turn now,” Nutmeg said. The tunnel smelled like pages and warm blanket. The red ribbon tickled Nutmeg’s ear. Nutmeg popped out the other side with a giggle. “We did it!”

When the game ended, Nutmeg looked at the room. The books were still out. The phone was on the bed. Nutmeg wanted to rest, but also to tidy. “Friends,” Nutmeg said, “I need help putting things away. We used these together. Let’s clean together.”

Tilly nodded. “Okay! I’ll stack the books.”

Barney lifted the heaviest book. “I can carry this one.”

They cleaned in small, easy steps. Books went back to the shelf. The phone went to the nightstand. The ribbon went into the basket with the shiny button. The room looked neat and cozy again.

Nutmeg felt warm and brave. “Today I said what I needed,” Nutmeg said softly. “I used a plan. We took turns. We were kind.”

Barney patted the bed. “You spoke up. That helped me wait.”

Tilly squeezed Nutmeg’s paw. “Your words were clear. That felt good.”

Nutmeg smiled. “When we use strong, kind words, everyone gets a turn. That is fair.”

The lamp hummed softly. The blankets smelled sweet. Nutmeg yawned and snuggled a pillow. “I can be brave and gentle,” Nutmeg whispered. “I can say, ‘My turn, please,’ and make a plan.”

Outside the window, a breeze rustled like a tiny cheer.

The End.
