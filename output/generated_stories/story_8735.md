# Kiki the Calm Koala Finds Her Spark

Every afternoon, Kiki the calm koala climbed down from her eucalyptus tree and padded softly to her cozy study corner. Her study corner was warm and bright, with a soft rug like a cloud, a little desk smooth as a pebble, and a tiny lamp that glowed like honey. Crayons slept in a cup. Paper rustled softly like leaves. A clock tick-tocked in a friendly, steady beat.

Tick-tock. It was Kiki’s routine. Snack, stretch, study time.

Today, Kiki felt a flutter in her tummy. Her class had a new project: “Draw and tell a story about you.” Kiki’s ears wiggled. She liked quiet. She liked soft light and slow days. But telling a story about herself felt big.

“I don’t know if my story is special,” Kiki whispered, smoothing her fuzzy gray fur. “What if it’s not good?”

Her friend Benny the bright blue bird chirped at the window. “Hi, Kiki! Ready to draw?”

Kiki looked down. “I’m not sure, Benny.”

Benny hopped inside, his tiny feet tapping on the desk. Tap-tap. “What do you feel?”

Kiki listened to her body. Her heart went thump-thump, like a drum. Her paws felt warm. “I feel wiggly,” she said. “And a little scared.”

Benny tilted his head. “Sometimes brave feels wiggly,” he chirped. “We can start small.”

Kiki nodded. “Start small,” she repeated, breathing in eucalyptus smell from a leaf in a jar. The air tasted minty and sweet.

She picked a crayon. It was green like her tree. She began with a tiny dot. “This is me,” she said softly. She made a small circle, then two gentle ears. She added soft, sleepy eyes. Scratch-scratch went the crayon. The sound was calm and friendly.

Kiki smiled a little. “I like soft.”

Benny fluffed his feathers. “What else is you?”

Kiki looked around her study corner. She drew her cloud rug. She drew her cup of crayons. She drew the honey lamp. She drew a big eucalyptus leaf with tiny lines that smelled like home in her mind.

Then she stopped. A shadow slid across her face. “But I don’t climb fast like Roo the kangaroo,” she said. “I’m not loud like Tinka the tiger. What if my story is too quiet?”

Before Benny could answer, there was a tap at the door. Tinka and Roo peeked in.

“Hi, Kiki!” Roo bounced once and then stood still when he saw her shy smile. “We’re sharing stories tomorrow. Mine is about racing the wind!”

Tinka grinned. “Mine roars! Rrrr-rrr!” She made a tiny roar, just a whisper, because she saw Kiki’s calm corner.

Kiki’s ears drooped. “My story is… gentle. Is gentle enough?”

The room felt still. The clock said tick-tock, tick-tock. Benny looked at Kiki. “Let’s find out,” he chirped.

Kiki put her paw on her chest. “I am calm,” she said softly. The words felt like warm tea. “I like slow. I like quiet.” She drew a slow, winding line like a river. She drew herself sharing leaves with friends. She drew a soft hug. She drew the moon and stars watching her read in the cozy glow.

Tinka leaned closer. “Your lines look like cuddles,” she said. “I can almost feel them.”

Roo sniffed. “It smells nice in here,” he said. “Like cool leaves.” He touched the paper. “Your picture feels cozy.”

Kiki took a brave breath. “My story is about being me. I am calm. I help friends breathe slow. I listen. I share my corner. Being calm can be brave too.”

Benny nodded. “That’s your sparkle, Kiki.”

Just then, a little argument buzzed at the window. Two beetles, Dot and Spot, were pushing a shiny button. “Mine!” “No, mine!”

Kiki blinked. Her tummy fluttered again, but she remembered her slow breath. “Let’s help,” she said.

She opened the window. “Hello, Dot. Hello, Spot,” she said in a kind voice. “This is a calm corner. We can solve problems here.”

Dot and Spot froze, tiny legs still. “We both like the button,” Dot squeaked.

Kiki set the button on the desk. “Let’s take turns,” she said. “Dot can hold it while Spot watches. Then Spot can hold it while Dot counts to five. I’ll help you breathe slow.” She breathed in with them, one-two-three, and out, one-two-three. Her breath sounded like a soft wind through leaves.

They tried it. Dot held the button. Spot counted, “One… two… three… four… five.” Then they switched. No pushing. No fuss.

“Thank you, Kiki,” the beetles chirped together. “Your calm helped us.”

Kiki’s heart felt bigger. Warmth spread to her paws. She looked at her drawing and added two tiny beetles, smiling.

Roo grinned. “Kiki, your calm is strong.”

Tinka nodded. “It’s brave to be you.”

The next day, in class, everyone shared. Roo zoomed his picture with whooshing sounds. Tinka whispered her tiny roar. Kiki held up her drawing. Her voice was soft but steady. “This is my story,” she said. “I am calm. I help with slow breaths and kind words. My quiet helps.”

The room was quiet. Then paws clapped, wings flapped, feet tapped. It sounded like rain on leaves. Kiki’s cheeks felt warm in a happy way.

Her teacher smiled. “Kiki, you showed us that being yourself is wonderful. Calm is a gift.”

Kiki smiled back. “I feel proud of me,” she said.

On the way home, the air smelled like sunshine and trees. Kiki hugged her paper. In her study corner, the lamp glowed like honey. The clock tick-tocked. Kiki whispered, “I am me, and that is enough.”

And her heart answered, “Yes.”

The End.
