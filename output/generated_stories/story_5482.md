# Fawn’s Balcony Band

Morning sun warmed the little balcony garden. Mint leaves smelled fresh. Tiny tomatoes hung like red buttons. A gentle deer fawn named Fawn peeked over the railing. Fawn’s big eyes blinked. Her soft ears twitched. She heard a distant hum from the street below—people talking, birds singing, and a faint drum beat.

“Tap, tap,” went a neighbor’s spoon on a pot. “La, la,” sang a child. A paper sign on the balcony door fluttered in the breeze. It said, “Community Music Day. Volunteers needed.”

Fawn’s heart felt fizzy and kind. “I want to help,” she whispered. “I can volunteer!”

Fawn looked around the balcony garden. Tiny watering cans sat in a row. Flower pots were full of soil and hope. A small drum, a tin pan, and a wooden spoon rested on a chair.

Fawn took a deep breath. “Helping is a big job,” she said. “I need a plan.” Her nose wiggled. The mint smelled sweet and bright. “First, I will get ready.”

She made a list in her head:
- Water the plants so the garden looks happy.
- Make music to invite friends.
- Prepare a basket of snacks from the balcony—mint leaves and cherry tomatoes.
- Decide where to put things so everyone can help.

Fawn poured a tiny cup of water. “Slow and steady,” she said. “Drip, drip.” Leaves shivered with delight. “Thank you,” the leaves seemed to say.

Fawn tapped the drum. “Thump-thump.” The sound felt gentle, like a heartbeat. She moved a chair beside the drum. She placed the tin pan on a soft towel so it would not clatter too loudly. “This is safe,” she said. “This is ready.”

A breeze tickled Fawn’s fur. She picked mint leaves. “Snip, snip.” She picked cherry tomatoes. “Pop!” They dropped into a basket. The basket smelled cool and green.

Then Fawn paused. “Hmm.” Her ears turned toward the door. She felt a flutter of worry. “What if I do not know what to do when people come? What if the music is too loud? What if the snacks are not enough?”

Fawn sat on the balcony rug and put a hoof to her chin. “I will make a good choice,” she told herself. “I will decide to ask for help, and I will practice.”

She peeked over the railing. Mrs. Sparrow chirped on the antenna. “Cheep-cheep! Need help?”

“Yes, please,” Fawn said. “I am volunteering to make a music time on the balcony. I want to be ready.”

Mrs. Sparrow fluttered closer. “Start soft,” she chirped. “Then grow. Soft to loud. Slow to fast.”

“Thank you,” Fawn said. “That is a good plan.”

Fawn practiced a pattern. “Tap… tap… tap-tap!” The drum answered kindly. She tried the pan. “Ting… ting… ting-ting!” She smiled. “I can do this.”

A neighbor child named Miko peeked through the railing. “Hi, Fawn! Is this for Music Day?”

“Yes,” said Fawn. “I am volunteering. Would you like to help?”

Miko nodded. “I can shake a jar of beans.” He held up a little jar. “Shake-shake!”

“Great idea,” Fawn said. “Let’s get ready together.”

They set up a little music corner: drum, pan, spoon, jar shaker. They made a quiet space with a blanket, in case anyone needed a rest. Fawn placed the basket of mint and tomatoes by a small sign that said, “Snack Time—Please share!”

Fawn looked around. “We are prepared,” she said. “We made decisions and a plan.”

Soon, two more neighbors came—Luna with a small bell and Ben with clapping hands. “Ding-ding!” “Clap, clap!”

Fawn raised a hoof. “Welcome,” she said softly. “We will start soft, then grow. If you need quiet, the blanket is there. You can help by playing or by handing out snacks. Thank you for volunteering with me.”

Fawn counted, “One… two… ready… play!” The music began like a whisper. “Tap… ting… shake… ding…” It felt like the garden was singing. The mint leaves waved in time. The tomatoes shone like little lights.

The breeze grew. The music grew too. “Thump-THUMP! Ting-TING! Shake-SHAKE! Ding-DING!” Fawn watched faces. She saw happy smiles. She saw a little baby cover ears. Fawn made a quick, kind decision. She lowered her drum. “Soft now,” she said. “Soft and sweet.”

Everyone followed. “Tap… ting…” The baby smiled again. Fawn beamed. “Good choice,” she whispered to herself.

After the song, Fawn asked, “Would someone like to share snacks?”

“I will,” said Miko. He offered tomatoes. “Here you go!” Luna passed mint leaves. “Smell this!” Everyone breathed in the cool, green scent. “Mmm,” they said.

They played three songs. Sometimes the shaker got stuck. “Shake-shake… oh!” Miko frowned.

“Try again,” Fawn said gently. “We can keep going.” Miko tried once more. “Shake-shake-shake!” He grinned. “I did it!”

When the music time ended, Fawn said, “Thank you for helping. We volunteered together. We prepared. We made smart choices. We kept trying.”

Mrs. Sparrow chirped, “Cheep! The garden says thank you too!”

Fawn’s heart felt warm. The balcony looked tidy. The neighbors waved good-bye. Fawn put away the drum, the pan, and the spoon. She watered the plants one last time. “Drip, drip.”

Fawn whispered, “When we volunteer, we help our friends. When we prepare, we feel ready. When we make good decisions and keep trying, everyone can enjoy the music.”

She nuzzled the basket, now empty but happy. The sun dipped a little lower. The mint leaves glowed. Fawn hummed, “La, la,” and smiled.

The End.
