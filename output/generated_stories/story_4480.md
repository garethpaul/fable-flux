# Shelly's Pantry Art Studio

Shelly the tortoise slowly walked into the big pantry. The shelves were full of colorful jars and boxes. Sunlight streamed through the small window, making everything sparkle.

"What a perfect day to make art!" Shelly said to herself. Her green shell gleamed as she looked around.

But there was one problem. Shelly wanted to paint a beautiful picture, but she didn't have any paint brushes or fancy art supplies.

"Hmm," Shelly said, tapping her wise old head. "I need to think like an artist."

She looked up at the tall shelves. There were so many interesting things! Shiny spoons hung from hooks. Colorful pasta sat in clear jars. Fresh herbs smelled wonderful.

"I can make my own art supplies!" Shelly said with excitement.

First, she carefully climbed up to reach a small bowl. Her strong legs helped her move slowly but surely. She filled the bowl with water from a pitcher.

Next, Shelly found some red beets, orange carrots, and purple cabbage. "These will make perfect natural paint!" she said happily.

She mashed the vegetables gently with a wooden spoon. The red beets made beautiful red juice. The carrots created bright orange color. The purple cabbage turned the water a lovely purple.

"Now I need brushes," Shelly thought out loud.

She looked around and spotted some fresh herbs. The rosemary had soft, fuzzy leaves. The thyme had tiny delicate branches.

"These will work perfectly!" Shelly smiled.

But then Shelly realized something. The paper towels were too high up on the shelf. She was too short to reach them, even when she stretched her long neck.

"Oh dear," Shelly sighed. "Maybe I can't make art today after all."

Just then, a friendly mouse peeked out from behind a box of rice.

"Hello, Shelly!" squeaked the little mouse. "What are you trying to do?"

"I want to paint, but I can't reach the paper," Shelly explained sadly.

The mouse's whiskers twitched with an idea. "I can help! I'm small, but I'm a very good climber."

"That would be wonderful!" Shelly said. "But what can I give you in return?"

The mouse thought for a moment. "Could you teach me how to make those beautiful colors?"

"Of course!" Shelly agreed happily. "We can work together!"

The mouse scurried up the shelf and pushed down some brown paper bags. They floated down like leaves.

Shelly showed the mouse how to mash the vegetables carefully. "Press gently," she said. "The colors come out slowly, but they're worth waiting for."

Soon they had red, orange, and purple paint ready. Shelly dipped her herb brush into the red beet paint. She made gentle strokes on the brown paper.

"Look!" the mouse squeaked with joy. "You're painting a beautiful sunset!"

Shelly painted orange and purple clouds. The mouse helped by holding the paper steady with his tiny paws.

"This is the most beautiful painting I've ever seen," said the mouse.

"And it's even more special because we made it together," Shelly replied warmly. "Sometimes the best art comes from using what we have around us."

They hung their colorful painting on the pantry wall where the sunlight could shine on it.

"Thank you for helping me reach high places," Shelly said.

"Thank you for teaching me about art," the mouse replied.

From that day on, Shelly learned that being an artist wasn't about having fancy supplies. It was about being creative, working hard to prepare, and sometimes asking friends for help.

And whenever she looked at their special painting, Shelly remembered that the most beautiful art comes from the heart.

The End.
