# Sunny Steps on the Porch

On a peaceful front porch, a resourceful squirrel named Sprig liked to visit every morning. The porch was warm and soft with sunshine. A rocking chair creaked gently. A flower pot smelled sweet, like honey. The wooden floor felt smooth under Sprig’s tiny paws.

Sprig loved the porch. He loved the basket of acorns set near the door. He loved the bright wind chimes that sang, ting-ting, ting-ting. He loved to hop, hop, hop. Today, Sprig had a big plan. He wanted to build a tall acorn tower. He wiggled his whiskers. “I can do it!” he said.

Sprig carried one acorn. He set it down. He carried another. He stacked them up. One, two, three. “Steady,” said Sprig. “Slow and steady.” The tower wobbled a little. Sprig took a small breath. “I can be calm,” he whispered.

A breeze came. The wind chimes sang louder. Ting-ting! Ting-ting! Sprig’s ears twitched. He turned to listen. His tail swished with excitement. “I want to chase the chimes!” he squeaked. He hopped once. He hopped twice. When he looked back, the tower leaned. Oh no!

Sprig’s tummy felt tight. His paws felt fast. He wanted to rush. He wanted to grab the tower and fix it quick. The acorns wobbled more. Clack, clack.

Sprig remembered something he had practiced. “Pause, Sprig,” he told himself. “Use your calm tools.” Sprig put his paws on his chest. He took a slow breath in. Smell the flower. He breathed out. Blow the wind. In. Out. In. Out. His tummy softened. His paws slowed.

“Okay,” Sprig said softly. “I can choose.” He looked at the chimes, still singing. He looked at his tower, almost falling. He thought, “What is my goal?” He tapped his head gently. “Build the tower first. Then play.”

Sprig decided what to do. He stood with his feet wide, like a sturdy little tree. He placed one paw on the side of the tower and held it still. He used his other paw to set a new acorn on top. He felt the smooth shell. He heard the quiet click as it settled. “Nice and slow,” he said.

Just then, a bluebird landed on the porch rail. “Cheep-cheep! Hello, Sprig,” the bird sang. “Want to fly with me?” The bird flapped. Whoosh!

Sprig’s eyes grew big. He wanted to jump and chase! His tail twitched fast. But he remembered his plan. He took one calm breath. In. Out. He smiled and waved. “Hi, Bluebird. I am building now. I will play after,” he said.

Bluebird tilted his head. “Good choice,” he cheeped. “I will watch.”

Sprig kept stacking. Four. Five. Six. The tower stood taller. The breeze brushed Sprig’s fur. The porch smelled like pine from the wooden rail. The sun felt warm on his back. Sprig hummed a tiny tune, “Slow and steady, I am ready.”

Then a fly buzzed by Sprig’s ear. Bzzzz! It tickled. Sprig giggled and almost bumped the tower. “Oops!” He felt his body get jumpy again. He paused. He did his calm steps: Stop. Breathe. Think. Choose.

“Stop,” he whispered. He stood still like a rock. “Breathe.” He smelled the flower. He blew the wind. “Think.” He looked at his tower and his paws. “Choose.” He chose to keep his paws gentle and slow.

He placed the last acorn on top. Tap. The tower did not wobble. It stood straight and tall. Sprig’s eyes shone. “I did it!” he cheered.

Bluebird clapped his wings. “Cheep! You used your calm tools. You made a good plan.”

Sprig felt proud. “When I felt fast inside, I paused,” he said. “Breathing helped my body. Thinking helped my brain. Then I chose what to do.”

He sat on the porch step and took one more soft breath. The wind chimes sang quietly now. Ting… ting. The flower smelled sweet and happy. Sprig touched the tower gently. “Thank you, paws,” he said. He patted his chest. “Thank you, breath.” He tapped his head. “Thank you, thinking.”

Bluebird fluttered to the basket. “Now will you play?” he asked.

Sprig smiled. “Yes. I finished my goal. Now I can play.” He looked at the chimes. “Let’s listen,” he said. Sprig and Bluebird closed their eyes and listened to the ting-ting song. They felt the warm sun on their faces. They took one breath in and one breath out together. It felt peaceful.

After listening, Sprig hopped and chased the dancing shadows on the porch. He laughed. Bluebird swooped and cheeped. They took turns ringing the wind chime softly. Sprig said, “Gentle hands.” The chime sang a sweet, small note.

When it was time to go, Sprig looked at his tower again. He felt calm and strong. “I can use my calm tools anytime,” he said. “Stop, breathe, think, choose.” He waved to Bluebird. He waved to the porch. He felt the smooth wood under his paws one more time. Then he scampered away with a light, happy heart.

That evening, Sprig returned to the peaceful front porch. The tower still stood. The air was cool now, and the porch smelled like evening rain. Sprig curled his tail around his feet and whispered, “I am proud of my choices. I can be calm. I can be kind. I can decide what to do.”

He took one last slow breath and smiled.

The lesson Sprig learned: When feelings feel fast, pause. Take a breath. Think of your goal. Make a kind choice. You can do it, one calm step at a time.

The End.
