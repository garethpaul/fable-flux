# Benny Beaver Says, "I Can Speak Up!"

Benny was a busy beaver with bright teeth and a warm, wiggly tail. One sunny morning, Benny tip-tapped into the pantry. The pantry smelled like oats, honey, and crunchy crackers. Jars clinked softly. Paper bags rustled. The cool floor felt smooth under Benny’s brown paws.

“My tummy rumbles,” Benny said. “I want a healthy snack.” He looked at the tall shelves. The berries were up high. The carrot sticks were behind a big box. The water jug was heavy.

Benny tried to reach. He stretched on tippy-toes. Stretch, stretch. His paws wobbled. His tail swished. Thump! He sat down gently and shook his head.

“It’s hard,” Benny whispered. “But I can try again.”

Benny remembered what Mama Beaver always said, “Use your words. Your words are strong.”

He took a slow breath. He wiggled his shoulders. He counted, “One, two, three,” to help his body feel calm. Then he stood up tall.

“Hello, pantry,” Benny said. “I need help. I can speak up.”

Just then, his friends scampered in. Pippa the chipmunk bounced, “Let’s make a snack pile!” Tiko the turtle smiled slow, “I can carry the water.” Lila the ladybug fluttered, “I can look for the berries.”

Benny’s chest felt warm and brave. He said clearly, “Friends, I have a plan. I want a healthy snack. I need the carrot sticks and the blue berries. I also want cool water. Can you help me safely?”

Pippa paused. “What’s the plan?”

Benny pointed with a gentle paw. “I will hold the door open. Tiko can nudge the water jug with his strong shell. Pippa, please pull the carrot bag. Lila, please fly and spot the berry box. We will move slowly. We will use gentle hands. If something feels unsafe, we stop and tell.”

“Good plan!” said Lila.

They started. Benny opened the pantry door wide. The hinges went creak, creak. He kept his tail steady so it didn’t bump anything. Tiko pushed the water jug. Glug, glug, it rolled a little. Pippa tugged the carrot bag. Rustle, rustle. Lila hovered by the berries, her tiny wings buzzing.

Pippa tugged too fast. The bag slipped and slid. “Oh!” she squeaked.

Benny put up his paw. “Pause,” he said. “Let’s move slower. My body needs slow.” He took a breath and showed slow pulling. “Like this.”

Pippa copied. “Slow,” she said. The bag slid safely to the edge.

Then Lila called, “The berries are high. I can see them, but I can’t lift them.”

Benny felt a little worry wiggle in his belly. He rubbed his paws together to feel calm. “I can ask for help,” he told himself. He looked around and saw a small step stool.

“I need the stool,” Benny said. “I will use it with care. Tiko, please spot me. Pippa, please hold the box steady. Lila, tell me if I am too close to the edge.”

“You got it,” said Tiko, planting his sturdy feet.

Benny placed the stool. Tap, tap. He tested it with one paw. Then the other. He climbed up, one step at a time. Step. Step. He kept his knees soft and his tail low for balance. He reached with gentle fingers. The berry box was cool and smooth. He held it with both paws. “I did it!” Benny said. His friends clapped softly so he wouldn’t wobble.

He climbed down slowly. Step. Step. Everyone smiled.

Benny set the berries on the table. He said, “Thank you, friends. Your help made it safe.” He poured water into little cups. Glug, glug. He washed the berries in a bowl. Splash, splash. He peeled the crunchy carrots. Snap!

They sat together on the cool floor. The water was cold and fresh. The berries tasted sweet and juicy. The carrots were crisp and bright. Benny’s tummy felt happy. His heart felt proud.

Pippa said, “Benny, you made a plan!”

Tiko said, “You used strong words.”

Lila said, “You told us what you needed.”

Benny nodded. “Yes. I asked for help. I used my words. I moved my body safely. I listened to my feelings. When I felt wiggly, I breathed. When I needed something, I said it clearly.”

Pippa munched and giggled. “That is self-advocacy,” she said carefully.

“What does that mean?” asked Tiko.

Benny smiled. “Self-advocacy means I speak up for myself. I say what I need. I ask for help. I make a safe plan. That way, my body and my heart feel good.”

They finished their snack and cleaned up together. Benny wiped crumbs with little circles. Swish, swish. Tiko put the cups in a neat line. Pippa and Lila folded the carrot bag and closed the berry box.

Benny looked around the pantry. It felt calm and tidy. “Thank you, team,” he said. “Today, I learned that my voice helps me. I can be brave and kind. I can move with care. I can say, ‘Please,’ and ‘I need help,’ and ‘Let’s go slow.’”

Everyone smiled big.

Benny patted his chest. “I will remember: My words are strong, my body is smart, and asking for help is a healthy choice.”

The End.
