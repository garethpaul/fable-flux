# Leo's Treasure Hunt Timer

The quiet attic was filled with dusty sunbeams streaming through a small round window. Little Leo the lion cub poked his fuzzy golden head through the attic door.

"Wow!" Leo gasped. The attic was full of old boxes, forgotten toys, and mysterious treasures. His amber eyes grew wide with excitement.

Leo's mama had told him he could explore the attic for exactly thirty minutes before lunch. She had given him a special timer shaped like a butterfly.

"Remember, Leo," Mama had said gently. "When the butterfly's wings stop fluttering, it's time to come down."

Leo set the timer on a dusty box. The butterfly's colorful wings began to flutter softly. Tick-tick-tick went the timer.

"I'm going to find the biggest treasure ever!" Leo roared quietly, so he wouldn't disturb anyone downstairs.

Leo bounded toward a pile of old books. The wooden floor creaked under his little paws. He sniffed the musty smell of old paper and dust. His whiskers twitched with curiosity.

In the first box, Leo found colorful marbles that clinked together like tiny bells. "Ooh, shiny!" he purred.

But then Leo heard something. Tick-tick-tick. The butterfly timer was still going.

Leo moved to another corner. There, he discovered a box of dress-up clothes. A feathery hat made him sneeze. "Ah-choo!" The dust tickled his little pink nose.

Time kept passing. Tick-tick-tick.

Leo found more treasures - a wooden train, some puzzle pieces, and a soft teddy bear. He wanted to play with everything at once!

"I need MORE time!" Leo growled, feeling frustrated. His tail swished back and forth. "This isn't fair!"

Leo looked at the butterfly timer. The wings were fluttering slower now. Soon they would stop completely.

"No, no, no!" Leo stamped his little paws. He felt angry and disappointed. His fur stood up a little bit.

But then Leo took a deep breath, just like Mama had taught him. He counted to three: "One... two... three..."

Leo's anger started to melt away. He looked around the attic with new eyes.

"Wait," Leo said to himself. "I found SO many wonderful things already!"

Leo picked up the soft teddy bear and gave it a gentle hug. It smelled like lavender and old memories. Then he carefully stacked the colorful marbles in a neat pile. The wooden train fit perfectly next to them.

Tick-tick-tick. The butterfly's wings were moving very slowly now.

"I know!" Leo had an idea. He began to move around the attic, organizing all the treasures he had found. Moving his body felt good. His muscles stretched and his heart pumped happily.

Leo made neat piles: books here, toys there, clothes in another spot. As he moved and organized, he felt proud and accomplished.

The butterfly timer gave one final tick, and the wings stopped fluttering.

"Time's up!" Leo announced cheerfully. He had used his thirty minutes perfectly.

Leo picked up the butterfly timer and looked around the tidy attic. Sunbeams still danced through the window, making everything look magical.

"Thank you, attic," Leo whispered. "I'll come back another day!"

Leo bounded down the attic stairs, his paws making happy thumping sounds.

"Mama! Mama!" Leo called excitedly. "I found amazing treasures, AND I came down right on time!"

Mama Lion smiled warmly. "I'm so proud of you, Leo! You managed your time well and stayed calm when you felt frustrated."

"And I moved my body lots too!" Leo added, doing a little dance. "Moving made me feel happy inside!"

"That's wonderful," Mama purred. "Good time management helps us enjoy our adventures even more."

Leo hugged his mama tight. He had learned that thirty minutes could hold many treasures when you use the time wisely and stay calm.

The End.
