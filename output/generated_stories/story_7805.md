# Bubbles in the Laundry Room

Splash the playful dolphin loved to giggle and wiggle. One rainy day, Splash visited his friend Mia. Mia’s house was warm and smelled like soap and fresh towels. The laundry room hummed and buzzed. “Whirr, whirr,” sang the washing machine. “Hummm,” hummed the dryer. Warm steam puffed like tiny clouds.

“Hi, Mia!” Splash chirped, flicking his tail. Water droplets from the umbrella by the door tickled his nose. He made a happy squeak.

“Hi, Splash,” said Mia. She held a basket of little socks. “I have a big goal today,” she said. “I want to wash, dry, sort, and fold all the laundry before snack time.”

Splash clapped his fins. “A goal! I like goals. Goals help us try, try, try!” he said.

Mia smiled. “You can help. We will work with honesty and care.”

Splash nodded. “Integrity means I do the right thing, even when no one is watching,” he said proudly.

Mia showed Splash the buttons. “This big button starts the wash. But we only push it when the basket is ready.”

Splash looked at the shiny buttons. They looked like bubbles. He loved bubbles. He wiggled closer. “They look so fun,” he whispered.

Mia set the basket down. “I will get more soap,” she said. “Please wait, Splash.”

Splash watched the door. He looked at the buttons. He felt a tingle of wanting. “Just one little push?” he thought. The laundry room smelled so clean and sounded so cozy. The shiny button winked at him.

Splash took a breath. “Integrity,” he whispered. “Do the right thing.” He sat on his tail and waited. The waiting felt long. “I can count,” he said. “One, two, three…” He tapped his fins and listened to the hum. He smelled warm cotton and a lemony soap puff.

Mia came back with a small cup of soap. “Thank you for waiting,” she said.

“I had a big want to push,” Splash admitted. “But I did not push. I waited. That is integrity.”

Mia nodded. “I am proud of you,” she said. “You told the truth. That is honest.”

They poured the soap. Plunk, plunk went the clothes. Click went the door. Splash got to press the big button—now the right time! The machine started. “Swish-swish, swish-swish,” it sang. Splash laughed. “It sounds like the sea!”

While they waited, they made a chart with stickers. “This chart is our goal helper,” Mia said. “Wash. Dry. Sort. Fold.”

“I want to give some time to help you,” Splash said. “I can be generous with my time.”

The washer finished. They moved wet clothes to the dryer. “Fluff, fluff,” went the dryer. Warm air hugged their cheeks. “Mmm,” said Splash. “Warm and toasty.”

Mia’s little brother, Max, toddled in with a tiny sock in his hand. “Sock!” he chirped.

“Thank you, Max,” Mia said. Max put the sock on his head and giggled. Splash giggled too.

The dryer beeped. Beep, beep! Mia opened the door. Whoosh! A wave of warm air rolled out. “Like sun on towels,” Splash said, nose wiggling.

They sorted socks. “Pairs!” Splash cheered, matching stripes to stripes and dots to dots. Sometimes he made a mistake. He held up a polka-dot sock and a striped sock. “Are you twins?” he asked. They were not. He fixed it and smiled. “It is okay to fix mistakes,” he said. “That is honest, too.”

Mia found a shiny coin under the dryer. Her eyes got big. “Oh! A coin!”

Splash looked around. “Whose coin is it?” he asked.

Mia thought. “It could be Mom’s. It could be Dad’s. We did not find it in our basket.”

Splash felt that tingle again. He liked shiny things. But he also felt a warm glow in his chest. “Integrity,” he whispered. “We tell the truth and keep things safe.”

He picked up the coin and held it carefully. “Let’s put it on the shelf and tell your mom,” he said. “We will not keep what is not ours.”

Mia smiled. “Yes. That is the right thing.”

They put the coin on the shelf and made a little note: “Found in laundry room.”

Mia’s mom came in. “What hard workers!” she said, smelling the fresh clothes. “And you found my coin! Thank you for keeping it safe.”

Splash beamed. His heart felt big. Mia’s mom gave them each a sticker star for the chart. “You met your goal,” she said. “Wash, dry, sort, fold—done! And you showed honesty and care.”

Splash looked at Mia. “We had a goal. We made a plan. We told the truth. We helped each other.”

Mia nodded. “You showed integrity when you waited, and when you told the truth about the button, and when you gave back the coin. You were generous with your time, too.”

Max clapped and put two matching socks on his hands like puppets. “Sock friends!” he laughed.

Splash giggled and did a little tail flip. “Doing the right thing feels good inside,” he said. “It makes the room feel warm, like the dryer.”

Mia hugged Splash. “Integrity keeps trust warm,” she said.

Splash looked at the chart. “What is our next goal?” he asked.

Mia grinned. “Snack time! We will share apples.”

Splash nodded. “I will share, too. Sharing is generous.”

They carried the folded clothes out in neat stacks. The laundry room hummed softly behind them, clean and bright. Splash took one last look at the shiny buttons. He smiled and waved. “See you next time. I will wait my turn,” he promised.

And he meant it.

The End.
