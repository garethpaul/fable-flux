# Pico the Pantry Parrot’s Clean Plan

Pico was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a sunny yellow beak. He loved to explore. One morning, Pico fluttered into the pantry. The pantry smelled like warm cinnamon and fresh apples. He heard little crinkles from paper bags and soft thumps from closed jars.

“Squawk! What is this?” Pico asked, tilting his head. He saw a jar of peanut butter, a bag of rice, and a shiny bowl. He also saw a sticky spoon lying on the shelf. The spoon had a gooey blob. It looked sweet, but it was dusty.

Pico’s tummy said, “Yum.” But his smart mind said, “Wait.”

Pico remembered what Nana Bird always said, “Clean hands, clean beak, happy tummy.” He puffed his feathers. “I will make a clean plan!” he chirped.

First, Pico looked around carefully.
- He saw crumbs on the shelf.
- He saw a tiny splash of juice on the floor.
- He saw the sticky spoon.

Pico tapped his beak. “Step one, find the right tools,” he said. He peeped into a little basket. Inside were paper towels, a small sponge, and a bottle that said, “Soap.” There was also a little trash bin.

“Perfect!” Pico flapped with joy.

He used his claws to pull a paper towel. “Step two, clean the dirty spots.” He gently wiped the crumbs into the bin. Scritch, scritch. The towel made a soft sound. He dabbed the juice splash with another towel. Squish. The floor felt smooth again.

Now the spoon. Pico looked closer. He felt brave, but careful. “This spoon needs a wash,” he said. He carried it to the sink by the pantry door. He turned the water on with a nudge. The water hummed and felt cool on his claws. Pico held the spoon and sang, “Wash, wash, wash, bye-bye germs!” He added a tiny drop of soap. Bubbles grew like tiny clouds. He counted slowly, “One… two… three… four… five… six… seven… eight… nine… ten.” He rinsed the spoon until it was shiny.

Pico dried the spoon with a clean towel. “Sparkle!” he chirped. He set the spoon in a clean cup.

Then he washed his own claws and beak tip. He rubbed, rubbed, rubbed, and rinsed well. “Ahh,” he sighed. The soap smelled like lemons. His feathers felt fresh.

Pico’s curiosity was not done. He wanted a snack, but he wanted to be safe. He made a plan.
- Plan A: Open the peanut butter jar with clean claws.
- Plan B: Use the clean spoon.
- Plan C: Put everything back and close the lids.

Pico tried Plan A. “Grunt… twist…” His claws slipped. The jar would not open. He took a breath. “I can ask for help. That is brave,” he said.

“Hello?” Pico called. “Is anyone near?”

Mina, the friendly mouse, peeked in. “Squeak! Hi, Pico. What’s up?”

“I want peanut butter, but the jar is tight,” Pico said. “I cleaned the shelf. I washed the spoon. I washed my claws. Can you help me twist?”

Mina smiled. “I can try. One, two, twist!” The lid popped with a soft “plop.”

“Thank you, Mina,” Pico said. “Let’s keep it clean.”

They both washed their paws and claws again, just to be sure. They sang together, “Wash, wash, wash, bye-bye germs!” They counted to ten. The water felt cool and nice.

Pico scooped a little peanut butter with the clean spoon and placed it on a clean cracker. He made a funny face on the cracker with apple slices for a smile. “Snack art!” he giggled.

Mina giggled, too. “It smells sweet and nutty,” she said, wiggling her whiskers.

Before they ate, Pico checked once more. “Clean place? Check. Clean tools? Check. Clean claws? Check. Lids closed? Not yet.” He put the lid back on the jar and twisted it snug. “Now, check!”

They both ate slowly. Crunch. Mmm. The cracker was crisp. The peanut butter was creamy. The apple was juicy. Their tummies felt happy.

Suddenly, Pico heard a buzz near a tiny spill he had missed. “Bzzz,” went a little fly. Pico frowned. “Flies like messes. We do not.”

He got another paper towel and wiped the missed spot. He tossed the towel into the trash and washed his claws again. The fly zipped away, bored. “Clean wins!” Pico cheered.

Mina looked around the tidy pantry. “You planned. You checked. You cleaned. That was smart,” she said.

Pico nodded. “Curious minds can be clean minds. When we explore, we also wash. When we eat, we also clean up. That keeps us safe and strong.”

Mina clapped her tiny paws. “You were brave to ask for help, too.”

Pico smiled. “Asking for help is part of the plan.”

They put the sponge back, the soap bottle upright, and the trash bin lid closed. Pico switched off the light. The pantry smelled fresh, like lemons and apples.

“Good day, clean day,” Pico said.

“Good day, smart day,” Mina added.

They both felt proud, peaceful, and ready for more adventures.

The clear lesson: Keep hands, tools, and places clean. Plan, check, and wash. Clean habits make happy tummies and happy hearts.

The End.
