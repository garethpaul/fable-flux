# Rollo Raccoon Says Thank You

Rollo was a musical raccoon with soft gray fur and bright curious eyes. He wore a tiny red scarf and carried a shiny tin drum. Tap-tap-tap! went his drum when he walked.

Today, Rollo visited the creative art room. The room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight made the glitter sparkle. Paints sat in rainbow jars—red, blue, yellow, and green. Brushes rested like sleepy cats. There was a big table with clay, scissors, glue, and soft pom-poms. It was a happy, busy place.

“Hello, art room!” Rollo sang. “I’m ready to make and play!”

Miss Dot, the art helper, smiled. “Welcome, Rollo. In this room we share, we help, and we say thank you.”

Rollo nodded and tapped his drum. “I can do that!”

Rollo wanted to make a music crown. He saw golden paper, shiny stickers, and tiny bells. He also saw friends: Benny Bear with paint on his nose, Mimi Mouse with a sparkly bow, and Lila Lamb with soft wool.

“May I use the golden paper?” Rollo asked.

“Yes,” said Mimi Mouse, handing him a sheet. “Here you go.”

Rollo took the paper and hurried away. He was excited. He forgot to say thank you.

He reached for the tiny bells. Lila Lamb held the jar. “Would you like some?” she asked.

“Yes!” Rollo said. He grabbed a handful. He forgot to say thank you again.

Then he needed glue. Benny Bear pushed the glue bottle toward him. “Here.”

Rollo squeezed the glue. Squish, squish. He used a lot. He forgot to say thank you again.

Soon, Rollo’s music crown was almost ready. He stuck on stickers—star, heart, star, heart. He tied on bells that jingled like tiny chimes. Jingle-jingle! He loved the sound.

Rollo lifted his crown. “Ta-da!” he cheered. He put it on his head and beat his drum. Tap-tap-tap! Jingle-jingle!

But the crown slipped and fell into a puddle of yellow paint. Plop! The bells got sticky. The paper tore at the edge. Rollo’s eyes felt hot with tears.

“Oh no,” he whispered. “My crown is ruined.”

He looked around for help. Mimi Mouse was painting a sky. Lila Lamb was rolling clay. Benny Bear was cleaning brushes. Miss Dot was stacking paper.

Rollo called, “Can someone help me?”

No one looked up. The room hummed with busy sounds—swish-swish brushes, snip-snip scissors, and soft chatter.

Rollo felt a thump in his chest. He stomped his foot, and the bells squeaked. He wanted to shout. He wanted to cry. Then he took a slow breath. He remembered Miss Dot’s words: “In this room we share, we help, and we say thank you.”

Rollo’s ears drooped. He thought about the golden paper, the bells, and the glue. He had taken them. His friends had helped him. He had not said thank you. He had not shown respect.

Rollo stood tall. “I can try again,” he told himself. “I can be brave. I can be kind.”

He walked to Mimi Mouse. “Mimi,” he said softly, “I forgot to say thank you for the golden paper. Thank you. It was very nice of you.”

Mimi smiled. “You’re welcome, Rollo.”

He went to Lila Lamb. “Lila, thank you for the bells. I like their sound. I’m sorry I grabbed them.”

Lila nodded. “Thank you for saying thank you. Would you like help cleaning the bells?”

“Yes, please,” Rollo said.

He turned to Benny Bear. “Benny, thank you for the glue. I used too much. I’m sorry. May I borrow a cloth?”

Benny handed him a soft cloth. “Here you go. I can help wipe.”

Rollo felt warm inside, like sunshine on fur. Saying thank you felt good. It felt like a song.

Miss Dot came by. “I heard kind words,” she said. “That shows respect. That shows gratitude.”

Rollo smiled. “I want to fix my crown. I won’t give up.”

“That’s resilience,” said Miss Dot. “You can do it.”

Together, they washed the bells in a little bowl of warm water. Swish, swish. They patted them dry. Tap-tap, went Rollo’s drum as he waited. Miss Dot gave him a fresh strip of golden paper. Mimi shared a sticker star. Lila tied a tiny ribbon. Benny showed him how to use just a dot of glue. “Dot, dot, not a lot,” Benny sang.

Rollo copied, “Dot, dot, not a lot.” He glued the bells one by one. Jingle, jingle. He pressed the stickers gently. He breathed slow and steady.

At last, Rollo put on his new music crown. It fit just right. He tapped his drum softly. Tap-tap. The bells answered, jingle-jingle. The sounds made a happy song.

Rollo looked at his friends. “Thank you, Mimi. Thank you, Lila. Thank you, Benny. Thank you, Miss Dot. I am grateful for your help.”

“You’re welcome!” they cheered.

Rollo bowed with his crown shining. “When I say thank you, I show respect. When I keep trying, I am strong inside. Gratitude and trying again help me and my friends.”

He led a little parade around the table. Tap-tap. Jingle-jingle. Swish-swish. Everyone smiled.

The End.
