# Joey's Big Brave Night

Little Joey the kangaroo sat in his soft, cozy nursery. The walls were painted sunny yellow with fluffy white clouds. His favorite stuffed elephant sat beside him on the rocking chair. But tonight, something was different.

"Mama," Joey called softly. "I hear scary sounds outside."

Whoooosh went the wind through the trees. Tap, tap, tap went the rain on the window. Joey's ears twitched with each new sound.

"Those are just nighttime sounds, sweetie," Mama Kangaroo said gently. "They can't hurt you." She tucked Joey into his warm, blue blanket. "I need to help Daddy in the kitchen. Can you be brave and stay here?"

Joey's bottom lip trembled. "But what if the sounds get louder?"

"Then you can be the bravest little joey ever," Mama said, kissing his forehead. "Remember, brave doesn't mean not scared. Brave means doing what you need to do, even when you feel scared."

After Mama left, Joey pulled his blanket up to his nose. The nursery felt big and quiet. His night light made gentle star shapes on the ceiling, but the sounds outside seemed louder now.

CRASH! Thunder rumbled far away.

Joey jumped. His heart went thump, thump, thump. "I don't like this," he whispered to his elephant. "But Mama said I could be brave."

Joey took a deep breath through his little black nose. The air smelled like his mama's lavender soap and the fresh rain outside. He sat up straighter in his chair.

"Elephant needs me to be brave too," Joey said, hugging his stuffed friend tight. "I can do this."

Another whoooosh of wind made the curtains dance. But this time, Joey watched them move like gentle ocean waves. "That's kind of pretty," he said.

Tap, tap, tap went the rain again. Joey listened carefully. "It sounds like tiny dancers on the roof," he giggled.

When thunder rumbled again, Joey didn't hide. Instead, he counted like Mama taught him. "One Mississippi, two Mississippi, three Mississippi..." The thunder was far, far away.

"I'm being brave!" Joey said proudly. He felt warm and strong inside, like sunshine in his tummy.

Just then, he heard a tiny sound from the hallway. "Mew, mew, mew."

Joey's ears perked up. That wasn't thunder or wind. Someone needed help!

Even though his legs felt wobbly, Joey hopped down from his chair. His soft feet padded across the smooth wooden floor. He opened his door just a crack.

There in the hallway sat Whiskers, the neighbor's little kitten. She was wet from the rain and shivering.

"Oh, Whiskers!" Joey said. "You're scared too!" He opened the door wider. "Come in where it's warm and safe."

Whiskers ran into the nursery. Joey got his softest blanket and wrapped it around the tiny kitten. Soon, Whiskers was purring like a little motor.

When Mama and Daddy came to check on Joey, they found him reading a picture book to Whiskers.

"Look, Mama!" Joey said proudly. "I was brave during the storm, and I helped Whiskers too!"

"Oh, Joey," Mama said, her eyes sparkling. "You were so brave and helpful. I'm very proud of you."

Joey's chest puffed out with pride. He had been scared, but he didn't let the scary feelings stop him from being helpful and brave.

"Being brave feels good," Joey said, snuggling with Whiskers and his elephant. "Even when I feel scared, I can still do good things."

That night, Joey slept peacefully, knowing he was braver than he ever imagined.

The End.
