# Otter Ollie’s Big Table World

Otter Ollie woke up with a wiggle and a giggle. “Good morning!” he squeaked, patting his soft whiskers. The sun peeked through the window, and warm light made little sparkles on his spoon. His tail swished on the floor. “Today, I will build a big world on the dining room table!”

Ollie slid into the dining room. The table felt smooth under his paws. He smelled toast and sweet apples. The clock on the wall went tick-tock, tick-tock. Mama Otter smiled. “Breakfast first, Ollie.”

Ollie nodded. “I have a plan, Mama. After breakfast, I will build a table world. I will invite friends from everywhere!” He pressed his paws together. “I will be kind to every friend.”

“That is a kind plan,” Mama said. “Eat now, build later. That’s good time use.”

Ollie made a choice. “Yes. First breakfast, then building.” He took a bite of warm toast. Crunch! He sipped cool milk. “Thank you for breakfast, Mama,” he said, feeling grateful in his tummy.

After breakfast, Ollie cleared his plate. He looked at the clock again. Tick-tock. “Now it is building time,” he whispered. He brought out blocks, napkins, and little cups. He put a blue napkin in the middle. “This is the big blue ocean,” he said.

He stacked red and yellow blocks for houses. He rolled a little marble. “This is a bus to share.” He lined up tiny spoons like bridges. Clink, clink. The table world grew and grew.

“Hello, Table World!” Ollie chirped. He clapped his paws. “Friends from many places can live here.”

He made a little island of apples. “This is Apple Island,” he said. He made a rice mountain from a small bowl of dry rice, with Mama’s help. “This is Rice Mountain.” Mama nodded. “We share just a little, and we clean up after. Good choice,” she said.

“Now I will invite friends,” said Ollie. He took out his toy phone. “Ring-ring! Hello, Mia the Mouse, from the cold place. Bring your cozy scarf! Hello, Tiko the Tortoise, from the warm place. Bring your sunny hat! Hello, Bibi the Bird, from the windy place. Bring your song!”

Soon the dining room filled with sounds. Tap-tap went tiny feet on the floor. Swish-swish went a little scarf. Flap-flap went soft wings. The air smelled like cinnamon toast and crayons.

Mia squeaked, “It is cold where I live. I like hot soup.”

Tiko said slowly, “It is hot where I live. I like cool water.”

Bibi chirped, “It is windy where I fly. I like to sing loud!”

Ollie smiled. “In our table world, everyone belongs. We can share. We can listen.” He placed three cups. “Hot soup here. Cool water here. And a singing spot here!”

Mia saw Rice Mountain. “Can I climb?” she asked.

“Let’s ask,” Ollie said. He thought for a moment. He looked at the clock. “We have time to play before lunch,” he decided. “Yes, we can climb, but we step softly.” They tiptoed, tiny paws and tiny claws. Crunch went one little rice grain. Ollie paused. “Oops. We must be gentle. Rice is a gift. Some friends eat rice every day. We say, ‘Thank you, Rice.’”

“Thank you, Rice,” the friends said together.

Bibi flapped over the blue napkin. “Ocean is big,” she sang. “Some friends cross the ocean to visit.”

Ollie put a spoon bridge across the napkin ocean. “Bridges help us meet,” he said. “We can build with care.” He balanced the spoons. Clink, clink. He held his breath. “Steady.” The bridge stayed!

Tiko smiled. “Slow and steady is smart.”

Mia peered at Apple Island. “Can we share these apples?” she asked.

Ollie nodded. “Yes, but we take only what we need.” He looked at Mama. “May we have apple slices, please?”

Mama brought a small plate. The apple smelled sweet. “One slice for Mia, one for Tiko, one for Bibi, one for Ollie,” Mama said.

“Thank you, Mama,” they all said.

Ollie held up his slice. “In our big world, we share. We take turns. We say thank you. We listen to friends from many places.” He crunched into the apple. Crunch! Juice dripped on his paw. It felt cool and sticky. He giggled and wiped gently with a napkin.

The clock went tick-tock, tick-tock. Mama called, “Ten minutes to clean up before lunch.”

Ollie made a choice. “Friends, it is clean-up time. Then we can eat.” He pointed to jobs. “Mia, you can gather the spoons. Tiko, you can stack the blocks. Bibi, you can fold the napkin ocean with me.”

They worked together. The spoons sang a soft clink song. The blocks clicked in a neat pile. The napkin felt smooth and smelled a little like apple. Ollie’s heart felt warm. “Thank you, team,” he said.

Bibi chirped, “Your table world was kind.”

Tiko nodded. “You made room for everyone.”

Mia squeaked, “You listened and shared.”

Ollie’s whiskers twitched with joy. “Today I learned: We are all part of one big world. We care for each other. We share food and time. We make good choices. We say thank you.”

Mama set bowls on the table. Steam rose like tiny clouds. “Lunch is ready,” she said.

Ollie looked at his friends. He felt grateful. “Let’s eat together,” he said. “In our big world, we help, we listen, we share, and we are thankful.”

They all smiled and sat. The dining room felt cozy and bright, and the clock ticked softly, like a gentle song.

The End.
