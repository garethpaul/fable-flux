# Daisy Fawn's Fair Share Plan

In a sunny guest room lived a gentle deer fawn named Daisy. Her soft brown spots sparkled in the morning light. She loved helping others and making everyone happy.

Daisy shared the guest room with three toy friends: Benny Bear, Lily Lamb, and Max Mouse. They all slept on the big blue bed together. The room smelled like fresh flowers from the window garden.

One morning, Daisy heard her friends arguing loudly. "I want the soft pillow!" squeaked Max Mouse. His tiny voice sounded upset.

"But I had it yesterday!" bleated Lily Lamb. She stamped her little hooves on the quilted bedspread.

"That's not fair!" growled Benny Bear. "I never get the good pillow!" His furry arms crossed tightly.

Daisy's heart felt heavy. Her friends were so unhappy. The room felt cold and sad now. "There must be a better way," she thought carefully.

"Friends, please stop arguing," Daisy said gently. Her voice was soft like a whisper. "Fighting won't help anyone feel better."

She walked to the middle of the room. The wooden floor felt smooth under her small hooves. "Let's think together. What would be fair for everyone?"

Max Mouse sniffled. "I just want to feel cozy sometimes."

Lily Lamb nodded. "Me too. The pillow is so fluffy and warm."

Benny Bear looked down. "I'm sorry I got angry. I want us all to be happy."

Daisy smiled warmly. "I have an idea! What if we make a sharing plan together?"

She found a piece of paper and crayons in the desk drawer. The crayons smelled waxy and bright. "Let's draw a calendar!"

"We could take turns!" squeaked Max excitedly. His whiskers twitched with joy.

"Each friend gets the pillow for one day," suggested Lily. Her wool felt soft as she bounced happily.

"That sounds fair to me," agreed Benny. His brown eyes sparkled with hope.

Daisy drew a beautiful calendar with colorful squares. "Monday for Max, Tuesday for Lily, Wednesday for Benny, and Thursday for me!"

"What about the other days?" asked Max, counting on his tiny paws.

"We could volunteer to share!" said Daisy proudly. "On weekends, we can snuggle together!"

The friends worked together to decorate their calendar. They drew hearts and stars in every corner. The paper felt smooth and special in their hands.

"This is much better than fighting," said Lily happily. She gave Daisy a gentle hug.

"You helped us think of a fair solution," thanked Benny. "You're a wonderful friend!"

From that day on, the guest room felt peaceful and warm again. The friends followed their sharing calendar every single day. When problems came up, they talked together instead of arguing.

Daisy learned that fairness means everyone gets a turn. She felt proud that she helped her friends solve their problem. The morning sunshine felt extra bright and cheerful.

"Being fair makes everyone happy," Daisy said to herself. She curled up on her turn with the fluffy pillow. The room smelled like friendship and kindness.

The four friends lived happily together, always remembering to share and be fair. Their guest room became the coziest place in the whole house.

The End.
