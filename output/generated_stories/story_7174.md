# Milo Monkey Says “I’m Sorry”

Milo was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and quick hands. He loved to jump, wiggle, and spin. His favorite place was the cozy study corner. It had a soft rug, a tiny desk, a basket of blocks, and a shelf with crayons and paper. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor, and the air smelled like paper and fresh bananas.

One rainy morning, Mama set a timer and smiled. “Study time,” she said. “Then we play.”

Milo clapped. “I want to build! I want to practice sports too!”

Mama nodded. “You can build a block gym right here in the study corner. After we build, we can practice gentle sports moves.”

Milo’s friend, Tia the turtle, came to visit. Tia moved slowly but smiled brightly. “Hi, Milo,” she said in a soft voice. “I brought my soft ball.”

Milo squeaked, “Yay! Let’s build and play!”

Milo stacked tall blue blocks. Tia placed red blocks carefully. Tap, tap, tap went the blocks as they clicked together. Milo sniffed the wood—warm and clean. The tower grew like a tiny gym. They made a little bridge, a tunnel, and a slide.

“I am fast!” Milo said. He hopped from the rug to the bridge. His tail swished. His feet went pat-pat. He wanted to show a big sports jump. “Watch this!”

Tia looked up. “Be careful, Milo.”

Milo bent his knees. “Super jump!” he shouted. He jumped too high. His foot bumped the tower. Crash! The tall blocks tumbled. Thunk, thunk, clatter. One block rolled and bumped Tia’s soft ball and then bumped Tia’s foot. Tia pulled back, surprised.

Tia’s eyes grew wide. She whispered, “Oh.” Her smile faded. She looked at the broken gym. The study corner felt quiet and heavy. Milo’s tummy felt twisty and hot.

Mama peeked in. “What happened?”

Milo looked at the floor. He felt a tickle of tears. He heard Tia’s small breath. He remembered her saying, “Be careful.”

Milo took a deep breath. He put a gentle hand on his chest and said softly, “I made a mistake.”

He turned to Tia. “Tia,” he said, looking into her eyes, “I am sorry. I jumped too big. I knocked down our gym. I scared you. I will fix it.”

Tia blinked. She listened. Her shoulders relaxed. “Thank you for saying sorry,” she said. “That helps my heart.”

Milo nodded. “I want to make it better.”

Mama smiled a little. “That is a true apology,” she said. “You said what happened, how Tia felt, and how you will fix it.”

Milo sniffled and wiped his eyes. “Can I give you time, Tia?”

Tia took a slow breath. “I am okay. Let’s build again. But slower.”

Milo grinned a small, brave grin. “Slower and safer.”

They began to rebuild. Tap, tap, tap. Milo handed Tia blocks. “Here is a red one.” Tia placed it steady. “Here is a blue one,” Milo said. His voice was calm now. The study corner felt warm again. The soft rug brushed their toes. The sun made the blocks shine.

Milo whispered, “I will try a new way. First build, then small moves. No big jumps.”

Tia smiled. “We can make a safe sports path.”

They built a low bridge, a wide tunnel, and a tiny slide. They used a ruler to make space for the soft ball. Milo drew a little sign with a crayon: “Slow Feet.”

After they built, Milo asked, “Ready for sports?”

Tia nodded. “Gentle practice.”

Milo showed a tiny hop. Hop. He kept his knees bent and feet soft. Tia rolled her soft ball slowly. Roll… stop. They took turns. “My turn,” Milo said. “Your turn,” Tia said. They clapped softly when each one finished.

Mama watched and cheered. “Great teamwork! Safer, stronger, smarter.”

Milo felt proud and calm. He could feel his heart going thump-thump in a happy way. He looked at Tia. “Thank you for building with me.”

Tia said, “Thank you for saying sorry and fixing it.”

Milo thought about his big jump. He touched the “Slow Feet” sign. “When I make a mistake, I can say sorry. I can make a plan. I can improve.”

Tia nodded. “That is brave.”

Milo laughed. “I am a courageous monkey! I can build, I can practice sports, and I can be kind.”

They played a little more. Hop. Roll. Clap. The study corner felt full of gentle sounds and happy hearts. When the timer dinged, they cleaned up together. Blocks went click into the basket. Crayons went swish into the cup. The soft ball went plop onto the shelf.

Mama gave them a hug. “What did we learn today?”

Milo stood tall. “I learned to say, ‘I am sorry,’ and to fix my mistake. I learned to build carefully. I learned small sports moves can be fun and safe.”

Tia added, “We learned to be a team.”

Mama nodded. “That is the best win.”

Milo waved his tail and giggled. “Apology helps us grow!”

The End.
