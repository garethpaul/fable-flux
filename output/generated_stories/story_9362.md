# Pebble Plans on the Porch

Pebble the cheerful penguin sat on a peaceful front porch. The porch was cool and smooth. The wooden boards felt warm from the sun. A soft breeze tickled Pebble’s feathers. Chirp, chirp, sang tiny birds. The porch smelled like lemon tea and fresh soap.

Pebble smiled. “Good morning, porch,” Pebble said. “Today, I want to help.”

A neighbor bunny named Bella hopped by. Her ears flopped and her nose wiggled. “Hi, Pebble,” Bella said. “The Little Free Library is messy. Books are everywhere. Some are upside down. Some are outside the box!”

Pebble tapped a flipper to the beak. “We can help,” Pebble said. “We can volunteer!”

Bella clapped. “Yes! But how?” she asked.

Pebble took a deep breath. “Let’s make a plan,” Pebble said. “A plan is a map in our minds. It helps us know what to do first, next, and last.”

Bella’s eyes grew big. “A plan sounds smart,” she said.

Pebble found a small chalkboard by the porch chair. Pebble drew three simple shapes.

“Step One,” Pebble said, pointing to a circle. “We look. We see what books are here.”

“Step Two,” Pebble said, pointing to a square. “We sort. We put big books with big books, and small books with small books.”

“Step Three,” Pebble said, pointing to a triangle. “We share. We tell a story to say thank you to the helpers.”

Bella wiggled happily. “That plan is clear!” she said.

They waddled and hopped to the little library by the porch railing. The tiny door squeaked. Eeeek! Books poked out like silly tongues. A fox cub named Finn padded over, tail swish-swish. “Can I help?” Finn asked.

Pebble smiled. “Yes, Finn. We are volunteering. We are using strategy. Strategy means thinking before doing.”

Finn nodded. “Thinking first,” he said.

“Step One: Look,” Pebble said. They looked with calm eyes. Pebble felt proud to be careful. The sun made the book covers shiny and warm. Pebble smelled old paper and sweet glue.

“I see big picture books here,” Bella said.

“I see tiny board books there,” Finn said.

“I see a book outside on the step,” Pebble said. Pebble gently picked it up and brushed off a leaf. Swish.

“Step Two: Sort,” Pebble said. “Let’s make three piles: Big, Small, and Just Right.”

They worked together. Pebble slid big books into a neat stack. Bella tapped small books into a tidy tower. Finn held the door open so it would not squeak shut. Eeeek—click. He smiled when it stayed open.

“Kind hands, kind hearts,” Pebble said. “We are careful with every book.”

A little squirrel named Suri peeked from the porch rail. “I like stories,” Suri whispered. “Can I help too?”

“Yes,” Pebble said. “You can be our checker. You can count. When each shelf holds five books, you say, ‘Just right!’”

Suri’s tail twitched. “One, two, three, four, five. Just right!” she chirped.

They sorted and smiled. Sometimes a book did not fit. Pebble paused. “Hmm,” Pebble said softly. “What is our strategy now?”

Finn tilted his head. “We could trade spaces,” he said.

Bella nodded. “We can turn the book sideways.”

Pebble tried both ideas, one at a time. Slide, turn, wiggle. “The book fits!” Pebble cheered. “Good thinking.”

At last the shelves were neat. Big books on the bottom. Small books on top. Just right in the middle. Suri counted again. “Just right!”

“Step Three: Share,” Pebble said. “Let’s tell a story to thank our helpers and to help new friends learn our plan.”

They all sat on the peaceful front porch. The afternoon breeze smelled like clover and warm wood. Pebble opened a book with a bright blue cover. Pebble’s voice was soft and happy.

“This story is called Pebble Plans on the Porch,” Pebble began. “Once, friends wanted to help. They used a plan. First, they looked. Next, they sorted. Then, they shared a story. The end.”

Bella giggled. “That’s our story!”

Finn grinned. “I like the part where we think first.”

Suri clapped her tiny paws. “I like counting,” she said.

Pebble closed the book with a gentle thump. “When we use strategy, we feel calm. We know what to do. We are kind to each other. We can help our neighborhood.”

A new friend, a duckling named Dot, waddled up. “How do I help next time?” Dot asked.

Pebble pointed to the chalkboard plan. “We use our three steps,” Pebble said. “Look, Sort, Share.”

Dot smiled. “I can look with careful eyes,” she said.

“And I can sort with gentle hands,” said Bella.

“I can hold the door,” said Finn.

“I can count,” said Suri.

Pebble felt the porch breeze again, soft as a feather. Pebble’s heart felt warm and light. “Together, we make a good team,” Pebble said. “Strategy helps us help.”

The sun dipped lower. The porch glowed golden. Pebble tucked the chalkboard by the library. “Our plan will be here for everyone,” Pebble said.

They all waved and said, “Thank you!” The birds sang a sweet, sleepy song. The porch was peaceful once more.

Pebble smiled and whispered, “Think first, be kind, and share stories. That is our way.”

The End.
