# Kiki the Calm Koala Shares the Rainbow Blocks

In a comfortable nursery filled with warm light and gentle music, Kiki the calm koala woke from a cozy nap. The room smelled like clean wood and soft soap. The rug felt fluffy under her paws. Toys rested in neat baskets. On a low shelf, bright rainbow blocks glowed like candy colors—red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple.

“Good morning, Kiki,” hummed Ms. Bea, the kind teacher. “Today we build. We share. We play fair.”

Kiki stretched her little arms and smiled. “I can be calm. I can be fair,” she whispered. Her ears perked up. She loved building!

Friends padded in. Timo the turtle clicked softly on the floor. Lila the lamb’s wool smelled like fresh hay. Poppy the panda’s paws made quiet thumps. Everyone saw the blocks and gasped, “Ooooh!”

Kiki placed her paw on the shelf. “Let’s build a tall tower,” she said in a soft voice. “We can take turns.”

Timo nodded. “Turn by turn.” Lila bleated, “Fair is share.” Poppy clapped. “Blocks for all!”

They sat in a circle. The rug tickled their toes. Ms. Bea tapped a tiny bell. Ting! “We use calm bodies and kind words,” she said. “We listen. We wait. We are fair.”

Kiki chose the red block. “My turn,” she said calmly. She set it down. Thunk. The sound was nice and firm.

Timo slid the orange block on top. “My turn,” he said. Click. The tower grew.

Lila placed the yellow block. “My turn.” Quiet and careful.

Poppy reached for the green block. His paw hurried. The block bumped and wobbled. Uh-oh! The tower leaned.

Kiki breathed in. She felt her soft tummy rise. She breathed out. “We can fix it,” she said. “We can be calm.”

Poppy’s eyes grew wide. “I want the blue block now,” he said. “Now, now!”

Kiki held up her paw like a little stop sign. “Let’s be fair,” she said gently. “We go in order. After Lila, it is Poppy. After Poppy, it is Kiki again.”

Ms. Bea smiled. “Kiki used clear words,” she said. “Fair means everyone gets a turn.”

Poppy hugged the green block tight. He listened to the bell in his mind. Ting. He took a slow breath, like Kiki. “Okay,” he said. “I can wait.”

Kiki and Timo used their paws together. They nudged the blocks straight. Tap, tap. The tower stood tall again, smooth and steady. Their hands moved in a little rhythm. That was coordination. It felt good to work together.

“Green goes now,” Poppy said. He placed the green block carefully. Soft thunk. He smiled. “I did it!”

“Blue next,” said Kiki, passing the block to Lila. The blue block was cool and shiny. Lila set it on top with a tiny wiggle. The tower didn’t fall. Everyone cheered, “Hooray!”

“Purple,” Timo said, sliding the last block over to Kiki. She placed it on top. Thunk. The tower was as tall as Kiki’s nose.

They stepped back. The colors shone like a rainbow snack. The tower smelled like fresh wood. It felt smooth under gentle paws. It looked strong and fair, built with turns and kind words.

Poppy tilted his head. “Can I make a bridge?” he asked.

Kiki nodded. “Yes. After we count, we can build more.” She loved words, and she loved numbers too. “Let’s use language to be clear,” she said. “We say what we plan.”

Kiki pointed to each color. “Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple.” The friends repeated the words, their voices soft and proud. “Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple.”

“Now we take turns again,” Kiki said. “First Poppy for the bridge, then Lila for the road, then Timo for the tunnel, then Kiki for the gate.”

Ms. Bea set a small sand timer down. The sand whispered, shhhh. “One turn is one timer,” she said. “Fairness is time and turns.”

Poppy’s turn came. He put two blocks down. Tap, tap. The bridge stood like a rainbow smile.

Lila’s turn came. She lined up little blocks for a road. Click, click. “My road is soft and safe,” she said.

Timo’s turn came. He made a tunnel. Thunk, slide. His shell fit right through. He giggled, “Tunnel tickles!”

Kiki’s turn came. She built a gate that swung open and shut. Swish. “This gate lets everyone in,” she said. “Fair means we all can play.”

A tiny quiver shook the tower when Poppy bumped the rug. Wobble, wobble. Kiki felt her heart thump once. She placed her paw on her chest and breathed. “In… out… calm,” she whispered. “Let’s hold it together.” Four little friends reached out with steady paws. Tap, tap, tap. The wobble stopped.

They all clapped. “We did it together!”

Ms. Bea rang the bell. Ting! “Clean-up time,” she sang.

Kiki smiled. “We can be fair for clean-up too.” She made a plan with words. “Timo takes tall blocks. Lila picks little blocks. Poppy carries the bridge. I stack the gate.”

They moved with smooth, careful steps. The blocks made friendly sounds as they returned to the shelf. Thunk, click, slide. The room felt tidy and calm again.

Kiki curled on the rug. “Today we were fair,” she said. “We waited. We used kind words. We took turns. We worked together.”

Poppy nodded. “Fair feels good.”

Lila nuzzled close. “Calm feels safe.”

Timo patted his shell. “Together feels strong.”

Ms. Bea tucked a soft blanket around them. The nursery hummed with happy quiet. Kiki’s eyes fluttered. She whispered, “Fairness helps everyone have fun.”

Everyone smiled and echoed, “Fair is share.”

The End.
