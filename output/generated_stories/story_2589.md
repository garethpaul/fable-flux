# Melody Bird and the Safe Song

Melody was a small singing bird with bright blue feathers and a golden beak. She lived near a big house with a basement lab. The lab belonged to kind Dr. Dot, who fixed toys and made helpful machines. One sunny morning, Melody fluttered down the steps to the basement lab, humming a cheerful tune.

The air smelled a little like lemon cleaner. Lights hummed softly. Machines blinked tiny green dots, like sleepy fireflies. Melody perched on a tall stool and peeped, “Hello?”

Dr. Dot waved. She wore big round glasses and a white coat. “Good morning, Melody! I’m working on a toy sorter to help kids tidy up. Would you like to sing while I test it?”

Melody’s wings shivered with joy. “I love to sing! Chirp, chirp, tweet-tweet!”

Dr. Dot smiled but held up a finger. “Before we start, we must remember safety.”

“Safety?” Melody tilted her head.

“Yes,” said Dr. Dot. “We have a few rules in the lab. We protect our bodies and help each other.”

Melody hopped closer. “Teach me, please!”

Dr. Dot pointed to a yellow box. “Rule one: Wear gear. Safety glasses keep eyes safe.” She gently placed tiny bird-sized goggles on Melody’s face. The world looked shiny. Melody giggled. “I can see my reflection!”

“Rule two,” Dr. Dot said, tapping a big red button on the wall, “Know the stop button. If something seems wrong, press it and call for help.”

Melody chirped, “Stop button. Call for help. Got it!”

“Rule three: Keep wings and tails away from moving parts,” Dr. Dot said. She showed a spinning wheel inside a clear cover. It whirred softly. “We stay behind the yellow line.”

Melody saw the bright yellow line on the floor. “Behind the line. Safe and fine!”

They both laughed. Melody took a deep breath. The lab smelled clean and a little warm, like sunshine on metal. Her heart felt bright. “I’m ready to sing!”

The toy sorter began to hum. Whirr, click, whirr. Little blocks rolled down a tiny ramp. Melody sang, sweet and light, “Tweet, tweet, tidy neat!”

Everything worked—until a small rubber ball bounced off the ramp. It rolled toward the spinning wheel and got stuck. The wheel started to wobble. It made a grumpy sound. Rattle-rattle-rattle!

Melody’s eyes grew wide. “Oh no!”

Her feathers prickled. She wanted to fly over and poke the ball free. But she remembered the rules. She looked at the yellow line. She looked at the stop button.

“Safety first,” Melody whispered.

She flew to the red button and pressed it with her golden beak. Click! The machine stopped with a soft sigh. “Dr. Dot!” Melody called. “I need help!”

Dr. Dot hurried over. “Good job calling, Melody.” She unplugged the machine and checked the wheel. “Thank you for not poking the ball. Your wings are safe.”

Melody’s chest felt warm with pride. “I stayed behind the line.”

Dr. Dot smiled. “You followed the rules. That is helping others and helping yourself too.” She gently removed the stuck ball and tightened a loose screw. “We’ll add a guard so balls can’t roll there again.”

Melody fluffed her feathers. “Can I help more?”

“You can help by singing,” said Dr. Dot, “and by reminding me of our rules!”

Melody hopped back to her stool. She sang a new song, soft and strong:

“Goggles on, wings behind,
Stop if something’s on my mind.
Call for help, take good care,
We stay safe because we care.”

The toy sorter hummed again. Whirr, click, whirr. Blocks slid into bins with happy clinks. Dr. Dot clapped. “Wonderful!”

Soon, they heard tiny footsteps on the stairs. Pip and Poppy, two small children with messy hair and big smiles, peeped into the lab.

“Hi, Dr. Dot!” said Pip. “Can we see?”

Dr. Dot held up a hand. “We can, if we follow safety rules.”

Melody puffed up proudly. “I can help!” She gave Pip and Poppy small goggles and pointed to the yellow line. “Please stand here. We sing the safe song!”

Pip and Poppy giggled. “Sing it, Melody!”

Melody sang again, and the children joined: “Goggles on, wings behind, stop if something’s on my mind!” Their voices blended like warm cocoa and marshmallows.

Suddenly, a soft beep-beep-beep sounded. A light blinked orange. Melody remembered: if something seems wrong, call for help. She looked at Dr. Dot and said, “Beep means we pause.”

Dr. Dot checked a screen. “The bin is full. Thank you, Melody.” She emptied it and gave a thumbs-up. “Safe to start!”

The rest of the day went smooth and calm. Melody sang. Pip and Poppy watched. Dr. Dot fixed and smiled. The lab felt cozy and bright. It smelled like lemon and a little bit like crayons, because Poppy had crayons in her pocket.

When it was time to go, Dr. Dot said, “Melody, you were brave and smart today. You helped keep everyone safe.”

Melody’s heart fluttered happily. “Safety helps me. Safety helps friends. We can all take care.”

As she flew home, she sang her safe song to the blue sky. The words felt like a soft blanket.

Here is the clear lesson she learned: When something seems wrong, stop, call for help, and protect yourself and others. Safety first is kind and wise. Helping others and taking care of yourself go together, like song and sunshine.

The End.
