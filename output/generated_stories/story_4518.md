# Shelly's Musical Discovery

In a cozy music room filled with shiny instruments, there lived a very shy turtle named Shelly. The room sparkled with golden trumpets, silver flutes, and a big brown piano that gleamed in the sunlight streaming through the windows.

Every day, Shelly would peek through the doorway and watch the other animals practice their music. The rabbits played violin with quick, bouncy movements. The birds sang beautiful songs that filled the air like floating bubbles. The frogs played drums with loud, happy beats that made everyone want to dance.

"I wish I could make music too," Shelly whispered to herself, pulling her head deeper into her shell. "But I'm too scared. What if I sound silly?"

One quiet afternoon, when all the other animals had gone home, Shelly slowly crept into the empty practice room. Her little feet made soft *tap-tap-tap* sounds on the wooden floor. The room smelled like old books and the sweet scent of rosin from the violin bows.

"Maybe I could try just one instrument," she said quietly, looking around at all the choices.

First, Shelly tried the big trumpet. She stretched her neck out and blew as hard as she could. *HONK!* The sound was so loud and surprising that she quickly pulled her head back into her shell.

"Oh my! That's too loud for me," she giggled nervously.

Next, she waddled over to the tiny triangle. She picked up the little metal stick and gave it a gentle *ting!* The sound was pretty, but so quiet she could barely hear it.

"Hmm, that's too soft," Shelly thought.

Then something wonderful caught her eye. In the corner sat a set of wooden blocks in different sizes. They looked just right for her small flippers! Shelly picked up two soft mallets and gently tapped the first block. *Tock!* It made a warm, gentle sound that felt just right.

She tried the next block. *Tick!* And the next. *Tunk!* Each block made a different musical note, like raindrops falling on different sized puddles.

"This feels perfect!" Shelly exclaimed, her eyes sparkling with joy.

Soon, Shelly was playing a simple, beautiful melody. *Tick-tock-tunk, tick-tock-tunk!* The sounds flowed together like a gentle stream. She felt warm and happy inside, like sunshine filling her shell.

Just then, Mrs. Robin, the music teacher, peeked into the room.

"What lovely music!" she chirped. "Shelly, I didn't know you could play!"

Shelly felt her cheeks turn pink, but this time she didn't hide in her shell. "I was scared to try before," she admitted. "But I found an instrument that feels just right for me!"

"That's wonderful!" said Mrs. Robin. "Every musician needs to find their own special sound. Would you like to play with the other animals tomorrow?"

Shelly's heart fluttered with excitement. "Yes, please! I think I'm ready now."

The next day, when all the animals gathered for music time, Shelly proudly played her wooden blocks along with everyone else. The rabbits' violins, the birds' singing, the frogs' drums, and Shelly's gentle blocks all blended together into the most beautiful music the practice room had ever heard.

"I learned something important," Shelly told her new friends. "Being shy is okay, but trying new things can lead to wonderful discoveries!"

From that day on, Shelly never missed music practice, and her gentle *tick-tock-tunk* became everyone's favorite part of their songs.

The End.
