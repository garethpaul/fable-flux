# Milo Mouse and the Sharing Circle

In a cozy living room filled with soft cushions and colorful toys, lived a kind little mouse named Milo. His whiskers twitched with excitement every morning as sunlight streamed through the big window, making the room sparkle like magic.

Milo loved playing in the living room, but today something felt different. He heard sniffling sounds coming from behind the big blue sofa.

"Hello?" Milo called softly, his tiny paws padding across the warm carpet. "Is someone there?"

A small voice answered, "It's just me, Bella the bunny. I'm new here, and I don't have anyone to play with."

Milo's heart felt warm and fuzzy. He remembered how lonely he felt when he first moved to this wonderful living room. "Hi Bella! I'm Milo. Would you like to be friends?"

Bella peeked out from behind the sofa. Her ears drooped sadly. "But I don't know how to play the games here. Everyone seems so good at everything."

Milo's whiskers wiggled thoughtfully. He noticed how Bella's paws trembled and her eyes looked worried. "That's okay! I felt scared too when I was new. Want me to show you around?"

"Really?" Bella's ears perked up a little. "You won't laugh if I'm not good at things?"

"Never!" Milo squeaked cheerfully. "Everyone learns at their own speed. That's what makes playing together fun!"

Milo took Bella's paw and showed her the toy box overflowing with blocks, balls, and puzzles. "What looks interesting to you?"

Bella pointed to some colorful building blocks. "Those look pretty, but I've never built anything before."

"Perfect!" Milo exclaimed. "I love building! But here's the best part - we can take turns. You pick a block, then I pick one. That way we both help make something amazing!"

As they began building together, Milo noticed Bella was very good at picking colors that looked beautiful together. "Wow, Bella! You have such a good eye for pretty colors. I never would have thought to put purple next to yellow like that!"

Bella's whiskers twitched with happiness. "Really? You think I'm good at something?"

"Absolutely!" Milo said, placing a red block where Bella suggested. "And you know what? When we help each other, we both learn new things!"

Soon, other friends in the living room noticed their beautiful tower. A friendly hedgehog named Henry rolled over. "Can I help too? I promise to wait my turn!"

Bella looked nervous again, but Milo gently squeezed her paw. "What do you think, Bella? Should we let Henry join our building team?"

Bella took a deep breath and smiled. "Yes! The more friends we have, the more beautiful our tower can be!"

As the day went on, their tower grew taller and more colorful. Each friend took turns adding blocks, and everyone listened to each other's ideas. Bella discovered she was wonderful at solving problems when blocks wouldn't fit quite right.

When the sun began to set, painting the living room in golden light, Bella hugged Milo tight. "Thank you for being so kind to me. You helped me feel brave enough to make new friends!"

Milo's heart felt as warm as the sunshine. "That's what friends do, Bella. When we understand how others feel and help each other, everyone has more fun!"

From that day on, whenever a new friend arrived in the living room, Milo and Bella worked together to make them feel welcome, showing them that kindness and taking turns makes everything better.

The End.
