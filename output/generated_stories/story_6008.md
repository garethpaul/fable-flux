# Della the Dolphin’s Guest Room Surprise

Della was a playful dolphin with bright, curious eyes and a cheerful click-click laugh. One rainy morning, Della visited her friend Mia’s house. The guest room was warm and cozy. A soft lamp glowed like a tiny sun. Pillows felt fluffy like clouds, and the carpet tickled Della’s fins. It smelled like clean soap and fresh lemons.

“Welcome, Della!” said Mia, clapping her hands. “Let’s play in the guest room.”

Della twirled her tail. “Click-click! Let’s make something special.”

On the table were paper, crayons, glue, buttons, yarn, and stickers. The craft box made a soft rattle when Mia opened it. Della’s eyes sparkled. “Ooo, crafts!”

Mia smiled. “We can make a welcome banner for guests.”

Della nodded. “Yes! A big, bright banner!”

They spread the paper. It rustled like dry leaves. Della sniffed the crayons. “They smell sweet and waxy,” she said with a giggle.

“I want to use all the red crayons,” said Della, reaching for the box.

“I want red too,” said Mia, holding one crayon tight.

Della blinked. “But I need red for big letters.”

Mia frowned. “I need red for hearts.”

They both tugged the red crayon. It made a tiny squeak. The room felt quiet. The lamp hummed softly.

Della took a deep breath. “Click-click,” she whispered. “I feel upset.”

Mia looked down. “I feel upset too.”

They sat very still. The clock on the wall tick-tock, tick-tock. Della wiggled her fins and listened to the tick-tock. It sounded like slow claps. Della remembered what her teacher once said: “When you feel upset, breathe and use kind words.”

Della breathed in. She breathed out. “Mia,” she said softly, “let’s learn a way to share.”

Mia nodded. “Yes. Let’s learn.”

They thought together. The carpet felt warm under their knees. The paper felt smooth under their hands.

Della said, “We can take turns with the red crayon. You draw a heart. Then I draw a letter.”

Mia said, “We can also mix colors. Red and yellow make orange!”

Della’s eyes grew wide. “That is smart. We can learn new colors.”

Mia smiled. “We can also use stickers for some hearts. Then we won’t need the red crayon all the time.”

Della clapped her fins. “Compromise! That means we both give a little to make it work.”

Mia giggled. “Compromise is kind.”

They made a plan:
- Mia would draw one red heart.
- Della would write one red letter.
- Then they would switch.
- They would mix colors when they could.
- They would use stickers, buttons, and yarn to add fun.

The plan felt good, like a gentle hug.

“Ready?” said Della.

“Ready!” said Mia.

Mia drew a red heart. It looked bright and happy. Della wrote a big red W. The crayon moved smoothly across the paper. Scritch-scratch, scritch-scratch.

“Your W is wonderful,” said Mia.

“Your heart is happy,” said Della.

They mixed red and yellow. They watched the colors swirl into orange. “Wow,” they both whispered. It looked like a little sunset on paper.

They glued buttons that clicked softly when they touched the table. They tied yarn into little bows. The yarn felt fuzzy. They added stickers that went pfft when pressed down.

Della said, “Let’s spell WELCOME!”

Mia nodded and sang, “W-E-L-C-O-M-E!”

They took turns with each letter. Della did W, Mia did E, and so on. Sometimes they used purple or blue. Sometimes they used orange they had mixed. They learned which colors mixed well. They learned that too much glue felt sticky on fingers, and just a little kept things neat.

At last, the banner was done. It shone with hearts, letters, stickers, and sweet color mixes. The guest room looked even warmer. The lamp light made the colors glow. The lemon smell mixed with the soft crayon smell. Della wiggled with joy.

Mia sighed, happy. “We did it together.”

Della nodded. “We learned about colors. We learned about taking turns. We learned how to compromise.”

Just then, Mia’s grandma peeked in. “Oh my! What a lovely welcome banner,” she said. “I can see teamwork. I can see careful hands. I can see big hearts.”

Della beamed. “Click-click! Thank you.”

Grandma said, “What did you learn today?”

Della and Mia said together, “We learned to share, to mix colors, to use kind words, and to make a plan.”

Grandma smiled. “That is smart learning. Learning helps our minds grow.”

They hung the banner across the guest room window. The letters danced in the light. Della felt calm and proud. She remembered the quiet tick-tock, her deep breaths, and her kind words.

Mia held Della’s fin. “Next time, let’s make paper flowers.”

Della laughed. “Yes! And we will plan and share again.”

In the warm, bright guest room, with soft carpets and happy colors, Della and Mia finished their rainy-day craft. Their hearts felt light. Their minds felt full.

The lesson was clear: when we listen, share, and make a plan, we can learn new things and make something beautiful—together.

The End.
