# Dippy the Dolphin Listens

In a cozy, comfortable nursery, soft lights twinkled like tiny stars. The room smelled like clean soap and warm blankets. Toys rested in baskets, and a gentle song hummed from a sleepy music box. In a big, bubbly fish tank sat Dippy, a playful dolphin toy who could wiggle and make little splashy sounds when pressed.

“Wheee! Splash-splash!” Dippy giggled as the children gathered on the soft rug. The rug felt fluffy like clouds. Little Mia hugged her teddy. Ben stacked blocks that clicked and clacked. Nia colored a rainbow with smooth, waxy crayons that made a soft scritch-scratch sound.

“Good morning, friends,” said Ms. Lila, the teacher, with a warm smile. “Today we’re sharing toys and sharing feelings.”

Dippy wiggled proudly. “I love to play! Who wants to splash with me?” His button nose lit up with a tiny blue glow.

Mia looked up. “Me, please,” she said softly.

Ben looked up too. “Me first!” he shouted, grabbing for Dippy. The blocks tumbled with a clatter. Nia’s crayon rolled away.

Dippy giggled and bounced. “Let’s all play faster! Splash-splash!” He zoomed toward Ben’s hands, eager for fun.

But Mia’s eyes grew shiny. Her lip trembled. She whispered, “I wanted a turn.”

Nia sighed and hugged her crayon. “I don’t like loud grabs,” she said quietly.

Ms. Lila knelt down. “Hmm,” she said calmly. “We have a problem. Our friends feel upset.”

Dippy stopped wiggling. He looked at Mia’s teary eyes and Nia’s little frown. He heard Ben’s quick, loud breaths. Dippy’s tummy felt twisty. The room didn’t sound fun anymore.

“What can I do?” Dippy asked in a small voice. “I only wanted to play.”

Ms. Lila nodded. “Sometimes playing is more than zooming fast. Playing also means listening,” she said. “Let’s use kind words. Let’s look with our eyes and listen with our ears.”

Dippy took a slow, bubbly breath. He listened. He watched. He noticed Mia’s hands held tight to Teddy. He noticed Nia’s shoulders droop a little. He noticed Ben’s eyebrows pinch.

Dippy made a gentle click. “Mia, I see your eyes are wet,” he said softly. “Are you feeling sad?”

Mia nodded. “Yes. I wanted a turn, and I felt small.”

Dippy turned to Ben. “Ben, I hear your words were quick and big. Are you feeling excited?”

Ben nodded. “Yes. I wanted the dolphin toy right now.”

Dippy swished his little fin. “Nia, you are very quiet. Are you feeling worried?”

Nia whispered, “Yes. I don’t like when toys get grabbed.”

Dippy’s eyes glowed warm. “Thank you for telling me,” he said. “I care about your feelings.” He looked at Ms. Lila. “I want to help.”

“That’s using initiative,” Ms. Lila said kindly. “You can make a plan.”

Dippy thought and thought. He listened again to the soft music box. He felt the cozy air. He wanted everyone to feel safe and happy.

“I have an idea!” Dippy said. “We can make turns with a song. When the song plays, we each get a turn. I can say, ‘Your turn!’ and then ‘My turn!’ and then ‘Your turn!’ again.”

Mia sniffled and smiled a tiny smile. “I like songs.”

Ben bounced on the rug. “I can wait if I know my turn is next!”

Nia nodded. “I can color while I wait.”

Ms. Lila clapped softly. “Great plan, Dippy. That is good communication.”

Dippy swam slow circles in the tank and tapped the music box. The sweet tune began: tink-a-tink, tink-a-tink. Dippy looked at Mia. “Your turn, Mia,” he said in a gentle voice.

Mia giggled and pressed Dippy’s button. “Splash!” The blue light twinkled. She handed Dippy back. “Thank you,” she said.

Dippy turned to Ben. “Your turn, Ben,” he said clearly.

Ben waited, then took Dippy carefully. “Splash-splash!” he laughed. He looked at Mia. “Thanks for waiting with me.”

Nia colored a bright sun. “I like our turn song,” she said. “It feels calm.”

After a few rounds, Dippy noticed Mia’s smile was big now. Ben’s eyebrows were soft. Nia’s shoulders were relaxed. The room felt warm and friendly again. The blocks went click-clack in a steady rhythm. The crayons went scritch-scratch in happy lines. The music box hummed softly like a lullaby.

Dippy clicked. “Friends, how do you feel now?”

Mia said, “I feel seen.”

Ben said, “I feel patient.”

Nia said, “I feel safe.”

Ms. Lila beamed. “Dippy, you listened with your ears and your heart. You noticed feelings. You spoke kindly. You made a plan. That is empathy, initiative, and communication.”

Dippy’s blue light sparkled. “I learned that playing is not just splash-splash and fast fun,” he said. “Playing is also caring. When we listen, we help friends feel better.”

Mia patted Dippy gently. “Thank you for asking how I feel.”

Ben stacked a tall tower and added a tiny block on top. “I can wait my turn.”

Nia handed Mia a purple crayon. “Here, you can use this color.”

Dippy wiggled with joy. “We are a team,” he said. “We can share, we can talk, and we can care.”

The music box ended with a soft tink. The nursery glowed cozy and calm. Everyone smiled. The day felt bright and kind.

And Dippy remembered: Empathy means listening, noticing, and helping.

The End.
