# Milo's Rooftop Lesson

High up on the red brick rooftop, Milo the monkey swung his long tail happily. The warm sun tickled his brown fur, and the cool breeze made him feel brave and strong. Milo loved playing on the rooftop where he could see the whole neighborhood below.

"What a perfect day for an adventure!" Milo said, jumping from one chimney to another. His feet made soft *thump* sounds on the tiles.

Soon, Milo spotted his friend Ruby the squirrel gathering acorns near the edge of the roof. She had made neat little piles of shiny brown nuts.

"Those look tasty!" Milo thought. Without asking, he grabbed a big handful of Ruby's acorns and started munching. *Crunch, crunch, crunch!*

"Hey!" Ruby squeaked, her fluffy tail twitching. "Those are MY acorns! I worked all morning collecting them!"

Milo felt his chest get hot and tight. "There are lots of acorns everywhere!" he said loudly, waving his arms. "Don't be so mean!"

Ruby's eyes filled with tears. "But I need these for winter! You took half my pile!"

Milo's face turned red. He felt angry and embarrassed. "Fine! Keep your silly acorns!" he shouted, throwing the rest of the nuts across the rooftop. They scattered everywhere with little *ping* and *plink* sounds.

Ruby gasped and started to cry. "Now they're all mixed up and dirty!"

Milo stomped away to the other side of the roof, his heart beating fast. He sat down hard on a warm tile and crossed his arms. "She's just being silly," he grumbled.

But as Milo sat there, watching Ruby sadly try to gather her scattered acorns, his angry feelings started to change. His tummy felt twisty and uncomfortable. The sunshine didn't feel as warm anymore.

"Maybe... maybe I wasn't being very nice," Milo whispered to himself. He remembered how happy Ruby had looked with her neat piles. He remembered how hard she said she had worked.

Milo took three deep breaths, just like his mama had taught him. *In... out... in... out... in... out.* His heart slowed down, and his red face cooled off.

Slowly, Milo walked back to Ruby. She was still sniffling, trying to clean dirt off the acorns.

"Ruby?" Milo said softly. "I'm really sorry. I shouldn't have taken your acorns without asking. And I definitely shouldn't have thrown them when I got mad."

Ruby looked up with watery eyes. "You hurt my feelings, Milo."

"I know," Milo said, sitting down beside her. "I was wrong. Can I help you clean them up? We can work together to make your piles neat again."

Ruby wiped her nose with her tiny paw. "You really want to help?"

"Yes! And next time, I'll ask before taking anything. That's what good friends do."

Ruby's face brightened. "Okay! That would be nice."

For the next hour, Milo and Ruby worked side by side. Milo carefully picked up each acorn and Ruby sorted them into piles. They talked and laughed as they worked, and soon the rooftop was filled with their happy chatter.

"Thank you for saying sorry," Ruby said when they finished. "And thank you for helping."

"Thank you for forgiving me," Milo replied. "I feel much better now that I made things right."

As the sun began to set, painting the sky orange and pink, both friends felt warm and happy again. Milo had learned that saying sorry and making things right felt much better than staying angry.

The End.
