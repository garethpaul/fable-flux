# Ollie Otter's Breakfast Adventure

Ollie Otter woke up with a big stretch and an even bigger yawn. His tummy made a loud "GRUMBLE!" sound that made him giggle.

"Time for breakfast!" called Mama Otter from the dining room. The smell of something yummy floated through the air.

Ollie slid down the hallway on his belly - his favorite way to travel! He loved how smooth and fast it felt. When he reached the dining room, he saw a beautiful table set with colorful plates and cups.

"Good morning, my playful pup!" said Mama Otter, giving him a warm hug. "I made your favorite - fish cakes with fresh berries!"

Ollie's eyes grew wide. The golden fish cakes smelled amazing, and the red and blue berries looked like tiny jewels on his plate. But then he spotted something else on the counter - a big jar of honey cookies that Grandpa Otter had brought yesterday.

"Mama," said Ollie, his whiskers twitching, "can I have cookies for breakfast instead?"

Mama Otter sat down next to him. "What do you think, Ollie? Are cookies the best choice for breakfast?"

Ollie thought hard. The cookies looked so sweet and crunchy. His mouth watered just thinking about them. "Well... they taste really, really good!"

"That's true," said Mama Otter gently. "But let's think about what our bodies need in the morning. What helps us swim fast and play all day?"

Ollie touched his tummy. "Um... good food that makes me strong?"

"Exactly!" Mama Otter smiled. "Fish gives us protein to build strong muscles. Berries give us vitamins to keep us healthy. And what do you think cookies give us?"

"Sugar that makes me feel funny?" Ollie remembered how his tummy felt upset last time he ate too many sweets.

"You're so smart, Ollie! Now, I have an idea. What if we eat our healthy breakfast first, and then we can share one cookie together as a special treat?"

Ollie's tail wiggled with excitement. "That sounds fair! And Mama... I want to tell you something. I was thinking about taking a cookie when you weren't looking. But that wouldn't be honest, would it?"

Mama Otter hugged him tight. "Thank you for telling me the truth, sweet pup. Being honest, even when it's hard, shows what a good otter you are."

Ollie took a big bite of his fish cake. It was warm and flaky and tasted like the ocean. The berries burst with sweet juice on his tongue. "Mmm! This is actually really yummy!"

"How does your tummy feel?" asked Mama Otter.

"Happy and strong!" Ollie said, doing a little wiggle dance in his chair.

After finishing his healthy breakfast, Ollie and Mama Otter shared one honey cookie. It tasted extra special because he had made a good choice first.

"I feel ready to swim and play now!" Ollie announced, flexing his little arms.

"That's because you gave your body the good fuel it needed," said Mama Otter. "And you were honest with me, which makes me very proud."

Ollie learned that making healthy choices and telling the truth made him feel wonderful inside and out. From that day on, he always remembered to feed his body good foods first, and his adventures were more fun than ever!

The End.
