# Ellie's Big Green Day

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved playing in her sunny backyard. The warm sunshine tickled her gray skin, and the soft grass felt squishy under her big round feet. Today was special because Ellie wanted to help make the world a better place.

"Mama," Ellie said, swinging her little trunk back and forth, "I want to help our Earth today!"

Mama Elephant smiled. "That's wonderful, Ellie! How would you like to help?"

Ellie looked around the backyard with her bright eyes. She saw empty water bottles sparkling in the sun, colorful paper scraps scattered on the grass, and shiny cans that made tinkling sounds when the breeze moved them.

"Look at all this stuff everywhere!" Ellie trumpeted softly. "It doesn't belong on the ground."

"You're absolutely right," said Mama. "What do you think we should do?"

Ellie thought hard, touching her trunk to her chin. "We could throw it all in the big trash can!"

"That's one idea," Mama said gently. "But there's something even better we can do. We can recycle!"

"Re-cy-cle?" Ellie repeated slowly, testing the new word with her trunk curled up. "What's that?"

Mama pointed to three different colored bins in the corner of the yard. "Recycling means we sort our trash so it can be made into new things instead of just throwing it away. The blue bin is for paper, the green bin is for bottles and cans, and the brown bin is for food scraps."

Ellie's eyes grew wide. "Wow! So the bottles can become new bottles?"

"Exactly!" Mama clapped her big ears. "And paper can become new paper, and cans can become new cans. It helps keep our Earth clean and beautiful."

Ellie felt excited butterflies in her tummy. "I want to try! But there's so much stuff. Can you help me?"

"Of course," Mama said. "We can work together. That's called compromise - when we help each other to get things done."

Ellie picked up a plastic bottle with her trunk. It felt smooth and light. "This goes in the green bin!" she announced proudly.

"Perfect!" Mama cheered.

Together, they walked around the sunny backyard. Ellie carefully picked up paper with her trunk - it crinkled and rustled as she carried it. "Paper goes in blue!" she said, dropping it in with a satisfying plop.

When they found banana peels, Ellie wrinkled her nose. "These smell funny, but they go in brown for compost!"

After working together for a while, Ellie felt tired but happy. The backyard looked clean and beautiful again. The green grass sparkled, and the flowers seemed to dance in the warm breeze.

"Mama, I feel proud!" Ellie said, spinning in a little circle. "We helped our whole world today!"

"Yes, we did," Mama agreed, giving Ellie a gentle trunk hug. "When we recycle, we're being good global citizens. That means we care about everyone and everything on Earth, even people and animals we've never met."

Ellie smiled big. "Tomorrow, can we teach my friends how to recycle too?"

"That's a wonderful idea," Mama said. "When we share what we know, we help even more!"

As the sun began to set, painting the sky orange and pink, Ellie felt warm and happy inside. She had learned that even little elephant calves can do big things to help the world.

The End.
