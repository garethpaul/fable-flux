# Tessa Tortoise’s Cozy-Clean Lesson

In a cozy bedroom with soft blankets and a little night lamp, Tessa the wise tortoise blinked her gentle eyes. Her shell was smooth and green, and it felt cool and safe. She loved her room. It smelled like fresh soap and warm laundry.

Tonight, Tessa was excited. Her friends were coming for a bedtime story. She had a small basket by her bed filled with books, a tiny bin for trash, and a special box for recycling paper. She also had a bright toothbrush and minty toothpaste by a little water cup.

“Tap, tap,” went the window as the wind made the curtains swish. “Knock, knock,” went the door.

“Come in!” Tessa called in her calm, kind voice.

In came Benny Bunny, with long soft ears. In came Dotty Duck, with yellow fluffy feathers. And in came Max Mouse, small and quick.

“Hi, Tessa!” they all said.

“Welcome, friends,” said Tessa. “Let’s have a cozy story time. But first, clean time!”

“Clean time?” asked Max Mouse, wrinkling his nose.

“Yes,” Tessa said with a smile. “We wash our hands, brush our teeth, and keep our room neat. Clean helps us stay healthy.”

Benny Bunny hopped and sniffed. “I smell cookies!”

“After we clean,” Tessa said. “First, listen to the plan.”

Tessa spoke slowly. “Step one: wash hands with warm water and soap. Step two: brush teeth—tiny circles, top and bottom. Step three: put trash in the trash bin and paper in the recycling box. Ready?”

Dotty Duck waddled to the sink. “Quack, I like bubbles!” She pumped the soap. Squish, squish. The soap smelled like lemons. She rubbed her hands. Bubbles popped like tiny stars. “This is fun!”

Benny Bunny listened and did the same. “Warm water feels nice,” he said. Swish, swish. He used a towel to dry. “Soft!”

Max Mouse crossed his arms. “I don’t need to,” he said. “I want cookies now.”

Tessa took a gentle breath. “Max, I hear you. Cookies are yummy. But we must wash first to keep our tummies happy. Sometimes little germs hide on hands. Washing sends the germs away.”

Max wiggled his whiskers. “Germs? Are they scary?”

“They are too tiny to see,” Tessa said. “But we can wash them away. Will you try? I will help.”

Max looked at the sink. He looked at Tessa’s kind eyes. He listened. “Okay,” he said softly.

Tessa showed him. “Wet hands. Soap. Rub, rub—front, back, and between fingers. Sing a short song.” She sang, “Wash, wash, wash your hands, make them clean and bright.” Max giggled and sang too. Splash, splash, rinse. Dry with the towel. “There,” said Tessa. “You did it!”

Next, Tessa passed out tiny toothbrushes. “Mint or strawberry?” she asked.

“Strawberry!” chirped Dotty.

“Mint!” said Benny.

Max sniffed both. “Strawberry, please.”

Tessa squeezed a little dot of toothpaste on each brush. “Just a pea,” she said. “Now little circles.” The room filled with the fresh smell of mint and sweet strawberry. Brush, brush. Swish, swish. Dotty made silly bubbles at the corner of her beak. Benny hummed. Max watched Tessa’s slow circles and copied. He spat into the sink. “Tingly!”

“Great listening,” Tessa said. “Now our teeth are happy.”

They all smiled at the mirror. Shiny!

On the floor, a crinkly paper wrapper and a fluffy dust bunny sat by the bed. Benny reached for the trash bin.

“Wait,” said Tessa. “Let’s sort. The wrapper goes in the trash. The paper from our book list goes in the recycling. We give paper a new life.”

Dotty tilted her head. “New life?”

“Yes,” said Tessa. “Recycling helps make new paper. It is kind to our world.”

Max picked up the paper and paused. “I wanted cookies first,” he said. “I didn’t listen before.”

Tessa nodded. “Thank you for telling me. It is okay to feel that way. Next time, we listen first, then we have fun. We can be patient with each other.”

Benny Bunny patted Max’s shoulder. “It’s okay, Max.”

Dotty Duck quacked softly, “We can wait together.”

Tessa smiled. “That is tolerance. We are kind when friends need time to learn.”

They put the wrapper in the trash and the paper in the recycling box. Rustle, clink. The room looked neat. The air felt fresh.

“Now,” said Tessa, “cookies and story!” She passed a small plate. The cookies smelled like cinnamon. Crunch, crunch. They were sweet and cozy.

Tessa opened her bedside book. “Tonight’s story is called The Clean Team.” She read in her warm voice. Her friends cuddled on soft pillows. The night lamp glowed like a little moon. Outside, crickets sang a sleepy song.

When the story was done, Max whispered, “Thank you for helping me wash, Tessa.”

“You’re welcome,” Tessa said. “You listened, and you tried. That is brave.”

Benny yawned. Dotty snuggled under a blanket. Max tucked his tail. Tessa pulled the covers up and turned the lamp low.

In the quiet, she said, “What did we learn?”

Max said, “Wash hands and brush teeth to stay healthy.”

Benny said, “Sort trash and recycling to help the Earth.”

Dotty said, “Listen to the plan and be patient with friends.”

Tessa nodded. “Yes. Clean bodies, clean room, kind hearts.”

The cozy bedroom felt safe and calm. Everyone smiled, warm and sleepy.

The End.
