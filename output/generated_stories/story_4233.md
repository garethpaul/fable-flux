# Ruby Rabbit's Clean-Up Adventure

Ruby Rabbit hopped into the bathroom one sunny morning, her fluffy white tail bouncing behind her. She looked in the shiny mirror and wrinkled her pink nose. "Oh my!" she said. "My fur is all messy, and my teeth need brushing!"

Ruby's mama had taught her that taking care of herself was very important. "When we feel clean and fresh, we feel happy and ready to help others," Mama Rabbit always said.

But today, Ruby felt overwhelmed. There were so many things to do! Brush her teeth, wash her face, comb her fur, and clean her ears. "This is too much work," she sighed, sitting down on the soft bathroom rug.

Just then, Ruby remembered the story her grandmother told her about a little rabbit from far away who didn't have a bathroom like hers. "That little rabbit had to walk to a well for water," Ruby whispered to herself. "And she still took care of herself every day because it made her feel strong and proud."

Ruby stood up with determination. "If she can do it, so can I!" she declared, her brown eyes sparkling.

First, Ruby turned on the warm water. It felt nice and cozy on her paws. She squeezed a tiny bit of minty toothpaste onto her small toothbrush. "Brush, brush, brush," she sang, making little circles on each tooth. The mint tasted fresh and tingly.

Next, she splashed the warm water on her face. "Splash, splash!" The water felt cool and refreshing. She patted her face dry with a fluffy yellow towel that smelled like sunshine.

Then Ruby picked up her special brush with soft bristles. She gently combed her fur, starting from her ears and working down to her tail. "Smooth and silky," she hummed happily, watching her reflection in the mirror.

Finally, she cleaned her long ears with a soft cloth, being very gentle and careful.

"There!" Ruby said, looking at herself in the mirror. Her fur was neat and shiny, her teeth sparkled white, and her ears were perfectly clean. "I feel wonderful!"

Ruby hopped out of the bathroom feeling proud and energetic. She found her little brother Ben sitting sadly in the living room.

"What's wrong, Ben?" Ruby asked kindly.

"I don't want to brush my teeth," Ben mumbled. "It's too hard."

Ruby's eyes lit up. "Let me tell you a story about a brave little rabbit who learned that taking care of herself made her feel like a superhero!" she said excitedly.

Ruby sat next to Ben and told him the same story her grandmother had shared with her. She explained how children all around the world take care of themselves in different ways, but they all do it because it helps them feel strong and ready to be kind to others.

"Really?" Ben asked, his ears perking up.

"Really!" Ruby nodded. "And when we take good care of ourselves, we have more energy to help our friends and family. Want me to help you brush your teeth?"

Ben smiled and nodded. Together, they hopped to the bathroom, where Ruby helped her little brother learn that self-care could be fun and easy.

That night, as Ruby snuggled into her cozy bed, she felt happy knowing that taking care of herself helped her take care of others too. "Tomorrow, I'll teach my friends at school," she whispered sleepily.

The End.
