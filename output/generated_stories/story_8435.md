# Joey Jumps on the Balcony

Joey was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a bouncy tail. He lived with Mama Roo in a cozy home with a sunny balcony garden. The balcony smelled like mint and sweet strawberries. Little bells tinkled in the breeze, and small pots lined the rail with green leaves and tiny flowers.

One warm morning, Joey stretched. “Good morning, garden!” he sang. His ears wiggled with happy wiggles.

Joey loved sports. He loved to move and try new things. Today was Balcony Sports Day! Mama Roo set out soft mats, little cones, and a tiny hoop made from a round vine. “Let’s play safely,” Mama said with a smile. “We take turns. We cheer for everyone.”

“Yay! Sports are fun!” Joey clapped his paws.

Neighbors peeked over the railing from their own balconies. Pip the pigeon fluttered down and cooed, “Coo-coo! Can I watch?”

“You can play, too!” Joey said. “We can all play.”

From the next balcony, Tessa the turtle waved slowly. “I’m a slow mover,” Tessa said. “Is that okay?”

“It’s more than okay,” Joey said. “We all move our own way!”

The sun felt warm on Joey’s fur. He sniffed the mint leaves—cool and minty. He heard the leaves whisper shhh, shhh, as the wind passed. He felt brave and kind.

“First game!” Joey called. “Hoop toss!” He placed the vine hoop on a pot. “We toss the soft bean bag into the hoop.”

Joey tossed. Plop! It landed inside. “I did it!” He hopped once, twice.

Pip flapped and tossed with a wing. The bean bag rolled to the side and missed. Pip’s feathers drooped.

Joey clapped. “Great try, Pip! Try again.”

Pip tried again. Plink! It bounced off the rim. Tessa smiled. “I can try a gentle slide.” She nudged the bean bag with her nose. Plop! It dropped in.

“We did it together!” Joey cheered. “Different ways can work.”

“Next game,” said Mama Roo. “A balance walk.” She laid a tape line along the balcony floor, between the pots of basil and tomatoes. The basil smelled peppery. The tomatoes were shiny and red.

Joey bounced to the line. Bounce-bounce—oops! He wobbled. His tail swished. He steadied himself and took tiny steps. Step. Step. Step. “I can do slow steps,” he whispered.

Pip tiptoed with tiny claws, wings out wide. “Coo! I feel the wind,” Pip giggled.

Tessa moved carefully. “I go slow and steady.” She made it to the end and smiled, big and proud.

Joey clapped again. “We all did it! Sports help our bodies be strong. Sports help our hearts be kind.”

Then came the Jump and Tap game. The rule was simple: jump and tap the hanging bell with a soft paw, wing, or nose. Ting-ting!

Joey was excited. Jumping was his favorite. He bent his back legs and bounced. Ting! The bell sang a shiny sound. Ting-ting!

Pip flapped and leaped. Ting! Tessa stretched her neck. She could not reach.

Tessa’s eyes looked wet. “I can’t tap the bell,” she said quietly. “I am too small and too slow.”

Joey’s ears drooped. He felt a squeeze in his tummy. He wanted everyone to feel happy. He took a slow breath. He looked around at the garden, the rope bell, the plants, the mats, and his friends.

“I have an idea,” Joey said. “Let’s solve this peacefully. Let’s change the game so everyone can play.” He moved the bell lower. Then he set up two bells—one high and one low.

Mama Roo nodded. “That is a kind and fair choice.”

“Ready?” Joey asked.

Tessa smiled. She tapped the lower bell with her nose. Ting! Her eyes sparkled. “I did it!”

Pip tried the high bell. Ting! “Coo! We can pick our bell!”

Joey felt warm sunshine inside. “See? Sports are for everyone. We can make fair rules.”

They took turns. They counted together. One, two, three—ting! The bells made a friendly song. The mint leaves waved like tiny hands.

Last, they played Team Garden Relay. They passed a little watering can from friend to friend without spilling. Joey held the handle with his paw. Pip balanced it with a wing. Tessa steadied it with her shell side as she moved slowly along the tape line. Splash-splash—just a few drops! The soil drank the water, and the plants glowed.

“Teamwork!” Joey said. “Go, team!”

When the games were done, Mama Roo brought cool apple slices. Crunch! Sweet juice ran down Joey’s chin. Pip pecked a piece. Tessa nibbled happily.

“I learned something,” Joey said, licking his lips. “Sports help us move. Sports help us be brave. Sports help us be kind. We cheer for each other. We use peaceful ideas to solve problems. And we celebrate our different ways.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “Coo! I flap. You hop. Tessa goes slow. All good!”

Tessa nodded. “Different is special. We can still play together.”

Joey hugged his friends gently. He listened to the soft city hum, the breeze in the leaves, and the tiny bell going ting in the wind. He felt proud, calm, and glad.

“Balcony Sports Day is the best day,” Joey said.

“It is,” Mama Roo agreed. “Because you made it fair and kind.”

Joey smiled. “Tomorrow, we can play again. We can make new games for everyone.”

“Yes,” said Pip. “Yes,” said Tessa.

And the balcony garden seemed to smile too, with minty air and happy bells.

The End.
