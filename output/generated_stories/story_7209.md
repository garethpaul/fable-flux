# Oliver Owl's Colorful Guest Room

Oliver the wise old owl lived in a cozy tree house with many rooms. His favorite was the guest room, where friends from all around the world would come to visit. The room had soft yellow walls, a fluffy blue bed, and a big window that let in warm sunshine.

One morning, Oliver fluffed his brown and white feathers and looked around the guest room. "Oh my!" he hooted softly. The walls looked so plain and empty. His friend Kiko the panda was coming all the way from China tomorrow, and the room felt boring.

Oliver's big round eyes blinked slowly as he thought. "How can I make this room special for Kiko? I want her to feel happy and welcome."

He waddled to his art box and pulled out colorful crayons, paintbrushes, and paper. "I know! I'll make beautiful decorations!" Oliver said, his feathers ruffling with excitement.

First, Oliver drew a picture of bamboo trees with green crayons. He made the leaves big and leafy, just like the ones Kiko loved to eat back home. The crayon felt waxy and smooth in his wing-tip.

Next, he painted a bright yellow sun with orange rays stretching across the paper. The wet paint smelled fresh and made pretty swirls as his brush danced across the page.

"This is fun!" Oliver hooted happily. "But I need more colors."

Oliver found red paper and carefully tore it into heart shapes. His sharp claws made tiny ripping sounds as he worked. Then he glued the hearts onto a big piece of white paper to spell "WELCOME KIKO" in cheerful letters.

But when Oliver stepped back to look at his work, his shoulders drooped. The bamboo picture had a big smudge where his wing had slipped. The sun painting had drips running down like yellow tears. The welcome sign had crooked letters that looked wobbly.

"Oh no," Oliver sighed. "My decorations don't look perfect. What if Kiko doesn't like them? What if she thinks I'm not good at making art?"

Oliver sat on the soft bed, feeling sad. He almost threw his artwork in the trash.

Just then, he remembered what his grandmother always told him: "Oliver, the most beautiful art comes from your heart, not from being perfect."

Oliver's eyes brightened. "Grandma Owl is right! I made these with love for my friend. That's what matters most."

He carefully hung his bamboo picture on the wall. Even with the smudge, it looked cheerful and green. The drippy sun painting went next to the window, where real sunshine made the yellow paint sparkle. The wobbly welcome sign went right above the bed where Kiko would see it first thing.

The next day, Kiko arrived with her black and white fur shining in the sunlight. When she saw the guest room, her eyes grew wide and happy.

"Oliver!" she squeaked with joy. "These decorations are wonderful! I can see you made them just for me. The bamboo reminds me of home, and your welcome sign makes my heart feel warm. Thank you for being so creative and thoughtful!"

Oliver's chest puffed up with pride. "I was worried they weren't perfect," he admitted honestly.

Kiko hugged her friend. "Perfect isn't about having no mistakes. Perfect is about making something with love and sharing it with friends."

From that day on, Oliver learned that creative expression comes from the heart, and being honest about his feelings made his friendships even stronger.

The End.
