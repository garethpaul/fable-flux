# Koko the Koala's Rooftop Garden

High up on a sunny rooftop, Koko the koala sat peacefully among his colorful garden. The warm sun tickled his fuzzy gray ears, and a gentle breeze made the green leaves dance. Koko loved his special rooftop home where he grew the most beautiful flowers and vegetables in the whole city.

"Good morning, little tomatoes," Koko said softly, sprinkling cool water on the red plants. "Good morning, pretty sunflowers." He always talked to his plants because it made them grow big and strong.

Suddenly, Koko heard angry voices coming from the rooftop next door. He looked over and saw two young raccoons arguing loudly.

"That's MY watering can!" shouted Rascal, the bigger raccoon.

"No, it's MINE! I found it first!" cried Ruby, the smaller raccoon, holding the blue watering can tight.

The raccoons pulled and tugged at the watering can. Their garden looked very sad and thirsty. The flowers drooped their heads, and the soil looked dry and dusty.

Koko felt sorry for the raccoons and their poor plants. He took a deep breath and climbed carefully over to their rooftop.

"Hello there," Koko said in his calm, gentle voice. "I'm Koko. I live next door. Is everything okay?"

Both raccoons stopped pulling and looked at the friendly koala.

"We both need this watering can," Ruby explained, her eyes filling with tears. "Our plants are so thirsty, but there's only one can."

"And I was here first!" Rascal added, crossing his arms.

Koko nodded thoughtfully. He could see that both raccoons loved their plants very much, just like he did. The sun was getting hotter, and the plants really did need water soon.

"You know," Koko said kindly, "I think I can help solve this problem. Would you like to hear my idea?"

The raccoons nodded eagerly.

"What if you take turns with the watering can?" Koko suggested. "Rascal, you could water your plants first for five minutes. Then Ruby, you could water yours for five minutes. That way, everyone gets what they need."

"But that's not fair!" Rascal protested. "I was here first!"

Koko smiled warmly. "Sometimes being fair means sharing, even when we don't want to. Your plants and Ruby's plants are all living things that need care. Isn't helping all the plants grow the most important thing?"

Ruby thought about this. "You know what? That sounds fair to me. I just want my flowers to be happy."

Slowly, Rascal's frown turned into a small smile. "I guess... I want all the plants to be healthy too. Okay, let's try sharing."

Koko clapped his paws together happily. "Wonderful! And if you ever need extra water, you can always ask me. I have plenty."

The raccoons took turns watering their gardens. Soon, all the plants stood tall and green again. The flowers seemed to smile in the sunshine.

"Thank you, Koko," said Ruby. "You helped us see that sharing is better than fighting."

"And our plants are much happier now," added Rascal, admiring his bright orange marigolds.

As the day ended, Koko returned to his own garden feeling proud and peaceful. He had learned something important too: when we help others solve their problems with kindness and fairness, everyone wins.

That night, all three friends' gardens sparkled with dewdrops under the twinkling stars, and everyone slept soundly knowing they had done the right thing.

The End.
