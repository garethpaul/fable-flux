# The Placemat Parade

Sunlight poured into the dining room and made little squares on the floor. A gentle deer fawn named Fern tiptoed over the cool tiles. The room smelled like warm toast and sweet apples. The wooden table stood tall like a friendly giant, with six chairs waiting in a neat row.

“Today is special,” said Mama Deer with a soft smile. “Grandma is coming for lunch.”

Fern’s ears wiggled. “I want to help,” she said. Her voice was small but bright. Helping made her chest feel warm, like a soft blanket.

Mama Deer nodded. “You can set the table,” she said. “Plates, cups, napkins, and placemats.”

Fern looked at the big stacked plates. They clinked like tiny bells. She looked at the napkins, folded like sleepy butterflies. On the counter lay a pile of plain brown placemats. They looked quiet and a little shy.

Fern’s hooves tapped. “They look… plain,” she whispered. She wanted the table to be beautiful for Grandma. But she wasn’t sure how. A teeny worry tickled her tummy.

Her older cousin Bramble trotted in. “I can make them fancy,” Bramble said proudly. He puffed his chest. “I’m very good at fancy.”

Fern’s ears drooped. “I’m not good at fancy,” she said. Her words came out like a tiny sigh.

Mama Deer bent down and touched noses with Fern. “You are good at being Fern,” Mama said. “Fern ideas are special. Your ideas count.”

Fern blinked. “My ideas count?” The thought felt shiny and new.

She took a deep breath. She sniffed the apple smell. She felt the smooth plate with her hoof. She looked at the brown placemats again. They were blank like quiet stories.

“I have an idea,” Fern said. Her heart gave a little hop. “Can I make a Placemat Parade?”

“What’s that?” asked Bramble, tilting his head.

Fern smiled. “Each placemat can be a picture. A parade of pictures!”

Mama Deer clapped softly. “We can use safe crayons and stickers.”

Soon, a little basket of chunky crayons and friendly animal stickers sat on the table. The crayons smelled like sweet wax. They felt warm in Fern’s hoof.

Fern picked a placemat. “This one will be the Sun Mat,” she said. She drew a big round sun with sleepy rays, like yawns of light. She added a smiling face. “Hello, Sun,” she giggled.

Bramble watched. “I want to try,” he said. He drew zigzags. “This is my Lightning Mat.”

Fern picked the next mat. “This one will be the Apple Mat,” she decided, thinking of Grandma’s apple pie. She drew a red apple with a tiny green leaf. The crayon squeaked a happy squeak.

She made a River Mat with wavy blue lines. She made a Leaf Mat with gentle green shapes. She stuck a bunny sticker on the corner. “Peek-a-boo, Bunny,” she whispered.

But then Fern reached a big mat and stopped. Her hooves felt still. “I don’t know what to draw,” she said. Her ears folded. “Maybe I’m not good at this after all.”

Mama Deer came close. “Let’s use our senses to find an idea,” she said. “What do you hear? What do you smell? What do you see?”

Fern listened. She heard the clock say tick, tick, tick. She heard a bird sing outside. She smelled apples and warm toast. She saw sunlight squares on the floor.

“I hear the clock,” Fern said. She smiled slowly. “I will make a Clock Mat!” She drew a round circle with numbers like little seeds. She drew hands pointing up. “Tick-tock,” she said. “My idea counts.”

Bramble grinned. “It’s great,” he said. “Your ideas are… Fern-fancy!”

They lined up the placemats around the table. Sun Mat, Apple Mat, River Mat, Leaf Mat, Clock Mat, and Lightning Mat. Fern stepped back. The dining room looked bright and brave. The table looked ready to sing.

Just then, Grandma Deer trotted in, soft and warm. “Oh, my,” she said, eyes twinkling. “What a Placemat Parade!”

Fern’s cheeks felt rosy. “I made them,” she said. “With my own ideas.”

Grandma touched the Leaf Mat. “I love the gentle leaves,” she said. “They feel like a hug.” She touched the Clock Mat. “Tick-tock, lunchtime o’clock!”

They all sat down. Fern gave Grandma the Sun Mat. “For you,” she said. “You shine.”

Grandma smiled. “And you glow, little Fern.”

When it was time to place the cups, Fern noticed something. “Oh!” she said. “We don’t have coasters. The cups might make wet circles.”

Bramble blinked. “What do we do?”

Fern looked at the leftover napkins. She looked at the sticker sheet. Her mind made a quick hop. “We can fold napkins into tiny squares,” she said. “And put a sticker in the middle so they stay folded. Ta-da! Cup Cozies!”

They folded. They pressed. They stuck. The napkins made soft little coasters. The cups sat happily without splashy rings.

“You solved the problem,” Mama Deer said. “You used your ideas.”

Fern’s chest felt big and bright. “My ideas count,” she said. The words felt strong now, like a tree trunk.

They ate sweet apple slices. They sipped cool water. The room was full of sunny chatter and the gentle tick-tock of the clock.

At the end, Grandma hugged Fern. “Your art made our meal a parade,” she said. “And your clever coasters saved the table.”

Fern nuzzled Grandma’s shoulder. “I learned something,” she whispered. “I can try. I can make. I can solve. I can be me.”

Grandma nodded. “And being you is just right.”

The End.
