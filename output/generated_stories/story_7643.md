# Poppy the Penguin Builds a Bright Boat

Poppy was a cheerful penguin with a bright orange beak and tiny squeaky feet. Today, Poppy waddled into the creative art room. The room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight danced on jars of buttons, ribbons, and sparkly glue. Paintbrushes stood like little trees in a cup.

“Good morning, colors!” Poppy chirped. Her voice echoed softly, like a happy song. “I’m ready to make something!”

She looked at the art table. There were soft papers, short scissors, glue sticks, cotton balls, smooth craft sticks, and bright stickers. Poppy flapped her wings. “I will craft a boat. A bright boat!”

Poppy picked up a blue paper and folded it. Crunch, crunch went the folds. She tried to make a pointy front. “Fold, fold, little boat,” she sang.

But the pointy front flopped. The paper slid and bent the wrong way. The boat looked squishy and lumpy.

Poppy frowned a tiny frown. “Hmm,” she said. “This is tricky.”

Her friend, a cozy turtle named Tilly, rolled by in her painty shell. “Hi, Poppy! What are you making?”

“A bright boat,” Poppy said. “But it keeps going floppy.” She poked the paper. It went fwip and fell flat.

Tilly smiled. “Sometimes crafts take time,” she said. “When I feel stuck, I take a slow breath.”

Poppy nodded. She put her flippers on her tummy and took a slow breath in. Then she let it out. “Whooo.” The air smelled like lemony glue and sweet crayons.

“Better,” Poppy said. She stood up taller. “I can try again.”

Poppy got a new blue paper. She watched her flippers. She pressed the folds slowly. “I can do hard things, little by little,” she whispered.

This time, the front stayed pointy. “Yes!” Poppy clapped. “Pointy front!”

She added craft sticks for the sides. Tap, tap. The sticks made a little click as they touched.

She dotted glue along the edges. Smoosh, smoosh. The glue was cool and sticky. She stuck on a paper sail. She pressed it down with gentle pats. “Pat-pat. Stay, sail.”

But then the sail tilted to the side. The boat slid and bumped the glue bottle. Splat! A blob of glue landed on Poppy’s beak.

“Eep!” Poppy squeaked. Her beak felt cold and gooey. She felt her chest get tight. “This is not going how I want.”

Poppy’s eyes filled with teeny tears. She wanted to quit. Her wings drooped.

From the corner, a soft hum floated over. Miss Dot, the art helper, smiled with kind eyes. “I see big feelings,” she said in a warm voice. “Crafting can be messy. Let’s pause.”

Poppy blinked. “I feel frustrated,” she said. The word was big but true. “I want my boat to be bright and strong.”

Miss Dot nodded. “Feelings are okay. Boats take practice. Would you like a towel for your beak? You can do the next steps all by yourself.”

Poppy nodded. “Yes, please.” She dabbed her beak with the towel. Dab, dab. The glue smell faded. Her belly felt softer.

“Try one small step,” Miss Dot said. “One step is enough.”

Poppy looked at her boat. “One step,” she repeated.

She chose a tiny triangle sticker to fix the tilty sail. “Triangle, help me.” She lined it up. Peel, stick. The sail stood straighter.

She added a button window. Click. She glued a ribbon flag on top. Swish! The ribbon tickled her flipper. She giggled.

“Poppy, your boat is getting bright!” Tilly cheered.

Poppy smiled. “I kept going,” she said. “I did one step, then another.”

She noticed a shy otter watching. The otter held a plain paper and twirled it, unsure. “Hi,” Poppy said gently. “Do you want to craft too?”

“I’m not good,” whispered the otter. “My paper always rips.”

Poppy waddled closer. “It’s okay to be new,” she said. “I felt frustrated. I took a breath. I tried one small step.”

Poppy showed the otter how to fold the edge softly. “Soft fold,” she said. “Like a cloud hug.” They both breathed in and out together. “Whooo.”

The otter tried. Rip—just a little. His whiskers drooped.

Poppy nodded. “Rips happen,” she said. “We can fix.” She handed him a sticker star. “Stars can cover tears.” Peel, stick. The rip hid under a shiny star.

The otter’s eyes sparkled. “I did it!”

“Yes!” Poppy chirped. “You did one step.”

At last, Poppy placed her bright boat on a tray of blue tissue paper waves. The boat bobbed gently. Rustle, rustle went the paper waves. The ribbon flag fluttered in the sunny breeze from the window.

Miss Dot clapped. “I see brave trying, careful fixing, and kind helping,” she said. “That is beautiful crafting.”

Poppy felt warm and big inside. She looked at her boat—the pointy front, the sticker sail, the button window, the ribbon flag. It was not perfect. The glue made one side a little bumpy. But it was hers. It was bright and strong enough.

Poppy held her boat and said, “I kept trying when I felt frustrated. I took breaths. I fixed mistakes. I can craft my own way.”

Tilly and the otter cheered. “Hooray for Poppy! Hooray for trying!”

Poppy grinned. “Hooray for us,” she said. “We can make, we can fix, and we can help.”

The bright boat bobbed again, and Poppy’s cheerful heart bobbed with it.

The End.
