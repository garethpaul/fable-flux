# Polly's Turn to Share

Polly the parrot loved breakfast time in the cozy dining room. Her bright green feathers sparkled in the morning sunlight that streamed through the big window. The wooden table smelled like fresh pancakes, and Polly could hear her family getting ready to eat.

"Good morning, Polly!" said Mom as she set down a plate of colorful fruit pieces. "Look what I have for you!"

Polly's eyes grew wide. She saw juicy red strawberries, sweet orange slices, and her very favorite - purple grapes! Her tummy rumbled loudly.

"GRAPES! GRAPES! I WANT GRAPES!" squawked Polly, flapping her wings excitedly. She reached her beak toward the plate right away.

"Wait, Polly," said Dad gently. "Let's all sit down together first. We need to take turns."

But Polly didn't want to wait. She felt so hungry, and those grapes looked so yummy! She stretched her neck as far as she could, trying to grab a grape.

"MINE! MINE!" she called out, her voice getting louder.

Little Emma, Polly's human sister, sat down at the table. "I'm hungry too, Polly," she said softly. "Can we share?"

Polly stopped stretching. She looked at Emma's kind face and felt a warm feeling in her chest. Emma was always nice to her.

"Taking turns means everyone gets to eat," explained Mom. "When we wait for each other, everyone feels happy and included."

Polly tilted her colorful head to one side. She thought about how good it felt when Emma shared her toys. Maybe sharing food would feel good too.

"I... I can wait," said Polly quietly, tucking her wings close to her body. It was hard to wait, but she wanted to be kind like Emma.

"Thank you for waiting, Polly," said Dad with a big smile. "That shows respect for our family."

They all held hands around the table - even Polly put her wing in the circle. It felt warm and special.

"Emma, would you like to go first?" asked Mom.

Emma picked up one small strawberry. "Thank you," she said politely, then looked at Polly. "Your turn, Polly!"

Polly felt so proud! Her chest puffed out with happiness. "Thank you, Emma!" she chirped sweetly. She carefully picked one purple grape with her beak. It tasted even more delicious because she had waited her turn.

"My turn!" said Dad, taking an orange slice.

"Now me!" said Mom, choosing a strawberry.

They went around the table again and again. Each time, Polly waited patiently for her turn. The fruit tasted wonderful, but the best part was seeing everyone smile and say "thank you."

"Polly, you did such a good job taking turns," said Emma, giving her feathered friend a gentle pet. "You made breakfast special for everyone."

Polly's heart felt as bright as her beautiful feathers. "Taking turns is fun!" she chirped happily. "Everyone gets to eat, and everyone feels good!"

From that day on, Polly remembered that taking turns made everyone feel respected and loved. She learned that waiting a little bit made everything taste better and feel more special.

The End.
