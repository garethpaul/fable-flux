# Gigi Builds Her Brave Voice

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe. She lived in a cozy house with a sunny home office. The office smelled like fresh paper and lemons. A soft fan hummed. Gigi loved to build things at her big desk. She used bright blocks, sticky notes, and a tiny toy hammer that went tap-tap.

One morning, Gigi’s long eyelashes blinked as she looked at her plan. “I will build a tall block tower,” she whispered. “I will make time for water breaks and stretches. That is a healthy plan.”

Gigi put on her blue glasses. She set a timer. Beep! She smiled. “I can do this.”

Just then, her neighbor, Benny Beaver, popped his head in the door. “Hi, Gigi!” he chirped. “Can you help me make a paper boat? Right now?”

Gigi’s heart made a little flutter. She wanted to help. She liked being kind. But she also had her plan. She looked at her tower pieces lined up in a rainbow row. She could smell the wooden blocks, warm and sweet. She took a slow breath in. The fan felt cool on her cheeks.

“I feel worried,” Gigi thought. “I want to finish my tower. I want to be kind to Benny. What should I say?”

She remembered a little rhyme she kept on a sticky note: “Kind and clear. Strong and near.”

Gigi stood tall. “Benny,” she said in a gentle voice, “I want to help you. Right now I am building my tower. I will help you after my timer rings.”

Benny blinked. “After the timer?”

“Yes,” Gigi said. “When it goes beep, I will take a break. Then we can make a paper boat together.”

Benny looked at the bright blocks. “Okay,” he said. “I will draw a boat plan.” He padded away, his tail thumping softly.

Gigi felt warm inside. Her words were kind and clear. She had used her brave voice.

She got to work. Tap-tap went her toy hammer. Clack-clack went the blocks. The tower grew. She hummed a little tune. “Build and breathe, build and breathe.” She took sips of cool water. She stood up and stretched her long neck to the ceiling. “Ahhh,” she sighed. It felt good to move.

Then her phone buzzed. Buzz-buzz! It was a message from Cousin Zara. “Gigi! Can you look at my picture? I want to show you now!”

Gigi felt a tug in her tummy. She put a hoof on her heart. “I feel busy,” she said softly to herself. “I need to finish my work.” She pressed the voice button. “Hi, Zara,” she said kindly. “I care about your picture. I am working for ten more minutes. I will call you after.”

She hit send. The room felt calm again. The fan hummed. The blocks smelled woody and warm.

Beep! The timer rang. Gigi smiled and clapped softly. “Break time!” she said. She rolled her shoulders. She took five deep breaths. In and out. She crunched a sweet apple slice. Crunch, crunch.

Benny popped back in. “Is it boat time?”

“Yes,” said Gigi. She cleared a corner of her desk. “I have five minutes for boats. Let’s build!”

They folded and smoothed. Swish-snap went the paper. Benny grinned. “You are good at this.”

“Thank you,” Gigi said. “You waited so nicely.”

After five minutes, Gigi checked the clock. “Benny, I am going back to my tower now,” she said. “We can make more boats after lunch.”

Benny nodded. “Got it.” He waved and left with his boat.

Gigi felt proud. Her chest felt light. Her cheeks felt warm and happy. She had said what she needed. She had still been kind.

She returned to the tower. Stack. Press. Balance. A wobbly block leaned to the side. “Oh no,” she whispered. “This part is tricky.” She placed a wide block at the base. “A strong base helps.” The tower stood taller. The sunlight made the colors glow like candy.

Cousin Zara called at last. “Ta-da!” she said, showing a bright picture of a river and trees.

“It is beautiful,” Gigi said. “Thank you for waiting. I like how you used green and blue.”

Zara giggled. “You sound calm.”

“I am,” said Gigi. “I used my brave voice today. I said, ‘I can help after my timer.’”

After the call, Gigi took a last stretch. She added a tiny flag on top of the tower. The flag fluttered in the fan’s breeze. She tapped her toy hammer one last time. Tap!

Benny and Zara peeked in. “Wow!” they said together.

Gigi smiled. “I built my tower. I took care of my body. I used clear words. That helped me and it helped you.”

Benny nodded. “When you say what you need, I understand.”

Zara grinned. “Me too!”

Gigi looked at the tower and her friends. She felt steady and bright. “Being kind and clear makes room for everyone,” she said.

Lesson: It is good to use a brave, kind voice. You can say what you need and still care for others. Take breaks, drink water, and build strong plans—just like a strong tower.

The End.
