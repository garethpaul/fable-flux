# Milly Mouse and the Messy Shed

Milly was a small gray mouse with bright pink ears. She lived in a cozy garden shed that smelled like fresh wood and flowers. The shed had tall shelves filled with gardening tools and clay pots.

Every day, Milly loved to help organize the shed. She would stack the small pots and arrange the tiny seed packets. But today, something was very wrong.

"Oh my!" squeaked Milly, looking around. The shed was a terrible mess! Flower pots were scattered everywhere. Seeds spilled across the wooden floor. Garden tools lay in jumbled piles.

Milly's whiskers twitched with worry. "How did this happen?" she wondered aloud. The shed looked like a tornado had spun through it.

Old Mr. Rabbit peeked through the doorway. His fur was gray and his eyes were kind. "The big storm last night knocked everything around," he explained gently. "It's quite a mess, isn't it?"

Milly's tiny heart sank. The job looked so big! "I'm too small to fix all this," she whispered. Her pink ears drooped sadly.

"Every big job starts with one small step," said Mr. Rabbit with a warm smile. "You're braver than you think, little Milly."

Milly took a deep breath. The air smelled like dirt and sunshine. "I'll try," she said softly. "But where do I start?"

She decided to begin with the smallest things first. Milly picked up one tiny seed. Then another. Her little paws worked carefully. Each seed went into its proper packet.

"This is taking forever!" Milly huffed after a few minutes. Sweat beaded on her furry forehead. Only one corner looked better.

But then she remembered Mr. Rabbit's words. "One small step," she repeated to herself. "I can do this!"

Milly kept working. She sorted seeds by color - brown ones here, black ones there. Her paws moved faster as she got better at the job. Soon, all the seeds were organized in neat rows.

Next came the flower pots. Some were heavy for a little mouse. Milly pushed and pulled with all her might. "Heave!" she grunted, moving a medium pot. "Ho!" she puffed, sliding another.

Mrs. Sparrow landed on the windowsill. "You're doing wonderful work, Milly!" she chirped cheerfully. "Look how much you've improved already!"

Milly looked around. Half the shed was tidy now! The flower pots stood in perfect lines. The seed packets lay in colorful rows. She felt proud and strong.

"I'm getting better at this!" Milly squeaked happily. Her confidence grew with each organized shelf.

For the big tools, Milly had to think creatively. She couldn't lift the heavy rake, but she could push it. She rolled it across the floor like a log. The shovel was tricky too, but Milly figured out how to drag it by the handle.

Hours passed. Milly's paws were tired, but her heart felt light. Finally, she placed the last tool in its spot. The shed looked perfect!

Mr. Rabbit returned as the sun set. "Magnificent!" he exclaimed. "You've transformed this place, Milly!"

Milly beamed with pride. Her pink ears perked up high. "It was hard work, but I kept trying. I got better and better at organizing!"

"You've learned something very important," said Mr. Rabbit. "When we practice and don't give up, we improve. You started small and accomplished something big!"

Milly nodded wisely. "Tomorrow, if the shed gets messy again, I'll know exactly what to do. I'll just take it one step at a time!"

The garden shed sparkled in the golden sunset light, and Milly felt like the proudest little mouse in the whole world.

The End.
