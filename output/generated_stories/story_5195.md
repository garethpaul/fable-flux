# Panda Poppy’s Quiet Closet

Poppy was a patient panda with soft black-and-white fur. She loved her cozy closet hideaway. It smelled like clean cotton and lavender. The closet was small and snug, with a fuzzy blue rug, a stack of pillows, and a tiny lamp that glowed warm and yellow. Poppy called it her Calm Corner.

One morning, Poppy wanted to build a block tower all by herself. “I can do it,” she whispered. She liked to try things on her own. She stacked red, then yellow, then blue. Tap, tap, tap. The blocks made a gentle click-click.

Suddenly, her friend Miko the monkey rushed in. “Let’s make it super tall!” he cheered, hopping and clapping.

Poppy felt her tummy tighten. The blocks wobbled. “Please move slow,” Poppy said softly.

But Miko was excited. He jumped, and the tower tumbled—clatter, clatter, crash! The blocks slid across the floor like little rainbows.

Poppy’s face felt hot. Her ears buzzed. Her paws curled into fists. “I am mad!” she shouted. “My tower is broken!”

Miko’s eyes went wide. “I’m sorry,” he said in a tiny voice. He stood very still.

Poppy’s heart thumped. Thump, thump. She wanted to yell more. She wanted to stomp. But she remembered her Calm Corner—the closet hideaway.

“I need a quiet minute,” Poppy said. She walked to the closet. The soft rug hugged her feet. The lamp glowed like a sunset. She closed her eyes and took a long, slow breath. In and out. In and out. She hugged a pillow. It was squishy and smelled like soap.

She whispered to herself, “When I am mad, I can breathe. When I am mad, I can calm down.” She counted slowly. “One… two… three.” Her tummy felt softer. Her paws relaxed.

Miko peeked into the closet. “Poppy? Are you okay?” he asked in a whisper.

“I am calming down,” Poppy said. “My feelings are big. I can wait. I can breathe.” Her voice sounded steadier.

Miko nodded. “I can wait too,” he said. He sat outside the closet and folded his hands. He took a deep breath, just like Poppy. Whoosh.

After a few minutes, Poppy felt ready. She opened her eyes. “I can talk now,” she said. She stepped out and gave Miko a small smile. “I felt mad when my tower fell. I needed space.”

“I understand,” said Miko. “I got too excited.”

Poppy nodded. “Thank you for listening.”

Miko looked at the scattered blocks. “Can I help fix it?” he asked.

Poppy thought. She liked to do things on her own. But she also liked helping and being helped. She took another small breath. “Yes,” she said. “We can build it together. We can move slow.”

“Slow,” Miko agreed. “And soft feet.” He tiptoed—tip, tip—to the blocks.

They began to build. Poppy stacked one block. Tap. Miko held the next one still. Hold. Poppy added another. Tap. Miko whispered, “Steady.” The blocks made gentle click-click sounds. The room felt peaceful.

“Let’s make a plan,” Poppy said. “I put two, you put one.”

“Good plan,” Miko said. “Cooperation!”

They worked like a team. Poppy used her own idea. Miko helped with careful hands. When the tower wobbled, Poppy paused and took a breath. In and out. She touched the tower gently to make it straight. “I can be patient,” she reminded herself.

Miko smiled. “You are very patient, Poppy.”

“Thank you,” said Poppy. “You are helpful.”

Soon the tower was tall again—red, yellow, blue, green—like a bright rainbow tree. The sun warmed their fur. The tower stood steady and proud.

Poppy clapped softly. “We did it!”

Miko giggled. “We did it together.”

Poppy looked at the closet with the soft lamp. “My Calm Corner helped me,” she said. “When I was mad, I went to my quiet place. I breathed. I waited. I used my words.”

Miko nodded. “I learned to move slow. I learned to ask, ‘Can I help?’”

Poppy patted the fuzzy rug with her paw. “When you feel mad, you can breathe too,” she said. “You can count. You can take time.”

“And we can make a plan,” Miko added.

Poppy smiled. “Plans help. Patience helps. Friends help.”

They built one more small tower for practice. This time, Miko asked, “Is this okay?”

Poppy said, “Yes, thank you for asking.”

At the end of the day, they sat in the closet hideaway. The lamp glowed. The rug felt warm. They sipped cool water and listened to quiet. Poppy’s heart felt calm. Her face felt soft.

Poppy whispered, “When big feelings come, I can be patient. I can breathe. I can talk. I can choose kind actions.”

Miko whispered back, “And I can help and listen.”

They held paws and smiled at the tall, happy towers they made together.

The End.
