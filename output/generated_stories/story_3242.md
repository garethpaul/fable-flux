# Nutmeg’s Music Plan

In a cozy garden shed behind a blue house, there lived a resourceful squirrel named Nutmeg. The shed smelled like pine wood and fresh straw. Sunbeams made warm, golden squares on the floor. Tools hung on hooks, and jars clicked softly when the wind wiggled the walls.

Nutmeg loved two things: collecting nuts and playing music. She had a tiny tin can drum, a string stretched on a wooden box that hummed like a guitar, and acorn caps that chimed like little bells.

One bright morning, Nutmeg tapped her drum. “Tap-tap, ta-ta-tap!” She squeaked, “Today I will play a concert for my garden friends!”

She peeked at her to-do list. She had drawn it with a stubby pencil on a leaf.
1. Sweep the shed.
2. Sort nuts for winter.
3. Practice songs.

Nutmeg grinned. “Easy! I can do it all.” She started to drum again.

Just then, Cricket chirped from the window. “Chirp-chirp! Nutmeg, will you help me find my lost leaf hat?”

Nutmeg’s whiskers twitched. She cared about Cricket. But she also had her list. Her tail flicked as she thought. “I want to help,” she said kindly, “but I need to do my jobs first. Can I help after I sweep and sort? I will be quicker if I do one thing at a time.”

Cricket nodded. “Chirp! I can wait. Thank you for telling me.”

Nutmeg felt proud. She took a deep breath. The shed smelled dusty. “First, sweep,” she said softly.

She picked up a tiny broom. Swish-swish. The floor made little squeaks. Dust danced in the sunbeams like glitter. Nutmeg sang as she worked, “Swish and sweep, swish and sweep, tidy floor for tiny feet.”

Soon the shed was neat. The golden squares on the floor shone brighter. Nutmeg smiled. “Done! Next, sort nuts.”

She opened a jar. Clink! The nuts clicked and clacked. She sorted big nuts in a red bowl and small nuts in a blue bowl. “Big here, small there,” she hummed. “One, two, three, for me!”

A small mouse peeked in. “Squeak! Nutmeg, can I borrow a soft leaf bed? My nest is chilly.”

Nutmeg paused. She remembered her plan. “I am sorting now,” she said gently. “I will finish this bowl. Then I can help you with a warm leaf bed. Can you wait a little?”

The mouse nodded. “Squeak, yes. Thank you for asking me kindly.” The mouse curled up by the door to wait.

Nutmeg kept sorting. She used patience and put one nut in at a time. Her paws moved steady and slow. She did not rush. She did not skip. Click, clack, click.

At last, the bowls were full. “Sorted!” Nutmeg cheered. She placed a happy checkmark on her leaf list. “Now I can help.”

She gathered soft leaves into a tiny basket. The leaves smelled sweet, like rain. “Here you go,” Nutmeg said, handing them to the mouse.

The mouse’s eyes sparkled. “Squeak! Thank you, Nutmeg. You are very kind.”

Nutmeg’s heart felt warm. “You’re welcome.”

She hopped to the window. “Cricket, now I can help you too!”

They looked in the grass by the shed. They looked under a flower pot that smelled like dirt. They listened. “Chirp?” Cricket tilted his head. Nutmeg listened hard and patient. The breeze whispered. The leaves rustled. Then Nutmeg spotted a tiny green hat stuck on a twig.

“There!” she said, pointing with her fluffy tail. “I see it!”

Cricket chirped with joy. “Chirp-chirp! Thank you!”

Nutmeg looked at her list. “Last, practice songs.”

She set the tin can drum on a wooden box. She plucked the string. Twang! She gently rang the acorn bells. Ting-ting. The shed filled with music. It felt like warm tea for the ears.

But then Bee buzzed in, worried. “Bzz! My wing is sleepy. Can you wait to play? Loud sounds make me dizzy.”

Nutmeg thought carefully. She felt a little sad. She wanted to play now. She also felt compassion. Bee did not feel well.

“I can play softly,” Nutmeg said. “I can practice quiet songs first. Then, when you rest, I will play louder.”

Bee smiled. “Bzz, thank you.” Bee settled on a sunflower petal and closed her eyes.

Nutmeg counted to three and tapped gently. “Tap… tap… tap.” She plucked the string softly. “Twing.” She waited between notes. She used time like a friend—one beat, then the next. She practiced being patient with the music and with herself.

Soon Bee felt better. “Bzz! Now I can buzz outside. Thank you for waiting.”

Nutmeg beamed. “You’re welcome!” She took a big breath and finished her practice with a happy tune. Tap-a-tap! Ting-ting-ting! Twang-twang!

When evening came, all the garden friends gathered by the shed door. The air smelled like mint and earth. Fireflies blinked like tiny stars.

Nutmeg bowed. “Thank you for waiting for my show,” she said. “I used my plan. I did one thing at a time. I helped friends. I practiced with patience. Now I am ready!”

She played her concert. It was sweet and steady. The mouse swayed. Cricket chirped along. Bee hummed a soft buzz.

After the song, everyone clapped and clicked. “Hooray for Nutmeg!”

Nutmeg’s cheeks felt warm with joy. “Thank you,” she said with gratitude. “Today I learned that a plan helps me use my time. When I do one job, then the next, I feel calm. Being patient and kind makes the day better for everyone.”

The friends nodded. The shed glowed in the moonlight. Nutmeg put a final check on her leaf list and tucked it into a jar.

“Tomorrow,” she whispered, “I will make a new plan.”

The End.
