# Roo’s Bouncy Guest Room Dance

Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey. He had bright, curious eyes and a soft, warm pouch on his tummy. One sunny morning, Roo visited Nana Dot’s house. Nana Dot opened the door with a smile and said, “Welcome, little Roo! Your cozy bed is in the guest room.”

The guest room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. The curtains were light and swishy, and a soft rug warmed Roo’s toes. Roo set down his tiny backpack and wiggled with happy energy. “I want to dance!” he said, bouncing on his heels. He loved to dance with his friends. He loved jumpy, bouncy, joyful hops.

Roo’s best friend, Pip the platypus, waddled in. “Quack-hello, Roo!” Pip said, flapping his smooth tail. “Let’s dance!”

They turned on gentle music. The tune was soft like a lullaby but with a peek of pep. Roo twirled. Pip clapped. Tap-tap went Pip’s flat feet on the rug. Boing-boing went Roo’s tail as he hopped. The lamp shade shook a little. The bed squeaked. The closet door clicked.

Nana Dot peeked in. “Careful, darlings,” she said kindly. “In the guest room, we use soft feet and quiet hops.” She showed them how to bend their knees and land gently. “Practice makes strong, safe dancers.”

Roo nodded. “Soft feet,” he repeated. He tried again, landing light as a feather. Pip tried, too. “Quack-soft!” Pip giggled.

They danced a little more. The music made Roo feel like a shining star. He spun too fast and—boing!—bumped the nightstand. The nightstand wobbled. The nightstand toppled. A small picture frame slid down, and the glass clinked on the rug with a tiny, scary sound.

Roo froze. His ears drooped. “Oh no,” he whispered.

Pip’s eyes went wide. “We made a mistake.”

Nana Dot hurried in, her slippers whisking over the rug. She looked at the frame. The glass was safe and not broken, but the frame was tilted. Nana Dot took a calm breath. “Accidents happen,” she said. “We learn and try again. What should we do now?”

Roo’s tail curled close. “I’m sorry,” he said. “We were too bouncy. We will clean up.”

Pip nodded. “We can help!”

Together, they lifted the frame and wiped the dust. Roo set it back on the nightstand, nice and straight. Nana Dot smiled. “Thank you for fixing it. You have kind hearts.” She looked around the small guest room. “This room is cozy, but it is not a great place for big bouncing. Where can big dancing go?”

Roo thought. He peeked out the window. The backyard was bright and green. A soft breeze made the leaves whisper. Birds chirped sweet notes. The sky was blue like a friendly hug. “Outside!” Roo said. “We can do big jumps outside.”

Pip clapped. “And we can do soft practice here.”

Nana Dot nodded. “That is a smart plan. It is a good compromise. Inside: gentle practice. Outside: big moves.”

Roo grinned. “Let’s practice our steps inside first. Then we can go out and try the big jumps.”

They made a plan. First, they stood tall and still. They felt the music with their hands on their hearts. Thump-thump went their little heart drums. They learned to bend their knees. They landed softly on the rug. Swoosh went the curtains when they spun slow. They whispered their steps.

Roo said, “Tiptoe, tiptoe, breathe.”

Pip said, “Soft feet, soft clap.”

They practiced pointing toes. They practiced tiny hops. They took turns. They waited for each other. “My turn,” said Roo. “Your turn,” said Pip. They smiled and cheered, “You did it!”

When they felt ready, Nana Dot opened the back door. Warm sunshine kissed their cheeks. The grass felt cool and tickly on their feet. A butterfly fluttered by like a tiny dancer in a yellow skirt. Roo’s heart felt glad.

“Now big dance,” Roo said.

The music flowed from the open window. Roo leaped high, higher, highest, with a happy boing! Pip spun with his tail swish-swish, and his feet tap-tapped on the soft ground. They laughed. They breathed. They tried the steps they had practiced.

Roo called, “Ready, Pip? Big jump!”

Pip called back, “Ready, Roo! I will clap the beat!”

Roo leaped and landed steady. He bent his knees like Nana Dot showed him. Pip clapped a friendly rhythm and spun slow, then fast, then slow again. They made up a friendship dance. It had a kind bow, a silly wiggle, a brave jump, and a happy hug.

When the song ended, Roo and Pip were warm and a little sweaty. Their cheeks were pink like peaches. They took sips of cool water. The breeze smelled like grass and flowers. Nana Dot clapped from the porch. “Bravo, dancers! You practiced with care, you worked together, and you found the right place to dance. That is smart teamwork.”

Roo beamed. “We solved the problem. Inside is for quiet practice. Outside is for big moves. We can dance anywhere if we choose the right way.”

Pip hugged Roo. “Friends help friends.”

Roo nodded. “And practice helps dancers.”

Nana Dot added, “Compromise helps everyone.”

Roo held Pip’s hand. “Let’s dance again tomorrow,” he said. “We can invite more friends to play outside.”

Pip smiled. “Yes! We will teach them soft feet and big hearts.”

Roo took a tiny bow. “Dance is for sharing. Dance is for joy.”

The End.
