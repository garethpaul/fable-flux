# Pippa Parrot’s Reuse Rainbow

Pippa the parrot fluffed her bright green feathers and looked around the creative art room. The room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight made the glitter sparkle. Paints sat in little cups. Brushes rested like sleepy caterpillars. Pippa chirped, “Squawk! Art time!”

Her friends gathered close. Ben the bunny thumped his soft feet. Mia the mouse wiggled her whiskers. Tiko the turtle blinked slowly. “What will we make today?” asked Mia.

Pippa tapped a stack of white paper. “We can make big, big posters!” she said. She picked up a sheet. Then another. And another. Paper flew like snow. “Squawk! So many pictures!”

Ben’s ears drooped. “That is a lot of paper,” he whispered.

Pippa laughed. “Paper is fun! We can use it all!”

Tiko looked at a little bin. It was a special bin with a green leaf on it. “This is the reuse bin,” Tiko said. “It has pieces of paper with blank backs.”

Pippa tilted her head. “Reuse? What is that?”

Mia smiled. “My grandma mouse taught me a tradition,” she said softly. “We use things again before we throw them away. It helps the trees. It helps our world.”

Pippa blinked. “A tradition? Like a special way our families do good?” Her feathers rustled as she thought. She looked at the big stack of new paper. She looked at the reuse bin. She felt a tiny twist in her tummy. “I do not want to hurt trees,” she said. “But I wanted big, big posters.”

Ben patted her wing. “We can still make art,” he said. “We can solve this together.”

Pippa nodded. “Problem: I used too much new paper,” she said. “Solution: We can try the reuse bin!” She peeked inside. There were colorful scraps, cardboard from a cereal box, ribbons, and bottle caps that went click-clack when she tapped them. “Oh!” Pippa’s eyes shone. “These look fun!”

Mia giggled. “Listen,” she said, shaking a jar of buttons. It went rattle-rattle. “Feel,” Tiko said, rubbing a piece of bumpy cardboard. “Smell,” Ben added, holding up a lemon-yellow crayon. “It smells sunny!”

Pippa flapped with joy. “We can make a Reuse Rainbow!”

They set to work. Pippa glued blue bottle caps in a curved line. Click, press, click. Ben cut green scraps into little leaves. Snip, snip. Mia tied tiny ribbon bows. Tie, pull, tie. Tiko stamped circles with the open end of a cardboard tube. Thump, thump, thump. The room hummed with happy sounds.

As they worked, they talked about traditions. Mia said, “My grandma mouse saves wrapping paper to use again. We smooth it with our paws.” She smoothed a shiny red piece now. “This is tradition-sharing,” she said proudly. “I share with you.”

Ben smiled. “My family has a cleanup song,” he said. He sang softly, “Use a little, share a lot, keep it tidy, waste we do not!” Pippa chirped along and added a silly “Squawk!” at the end. Everyone laughed.

Tiko nodded. “My grandpa turtle plants a tree on his birthday every year,” he said. “He says, ‘Give back to the earth, and the earth gives back to you.’”

Pippa’s eyes grew wide. “I want to share a tradition too,” she said. “Parrots in my flock keep a ‘Treasure Box.’ We put clean, safe bits inside—buttons, ribbons, boxes. We make art from treasures, not from new things.”

Ben clapped his soft paws. “Let’s start a Treasure Box here!”

They found a big shoebox and drew a green leaf on the lid. Pippa wrote in big letters, “TREASURE BOX.” She used the blank back of an old paper to make the sign. She felt warm and proud.

Soon, the Reuse Rainbow began to glow. Yellow napkin squares made sunshine. Purple yarn made wavy clouds. The rainbow felt fluffy in some spots and smooth in others. Pippa pressed her cheek to the yarn. “Soft,” she whispered. She sniffed the crayons again. “Sweet.”

Then Pippa looked at the floor. There were still a few new papers she had tossed around. Her tummy twist came back. She took a deep breath and said, “Friends, I am sorry I grabbed so many new sheets. I did not think about the trees. Next time, I will look in the reuse bin first.”

Mia squeezed her wing. “Thank you for saying sorry,” she said. “That is kind.”

Ben stacked the new papers neatly. “We can put these back,” he said.

Tiko carried the stack to the shelf. “We saved some trees today,” he said.

Pippa stood tall. “Our problem is solved,” she chirped. “We made beautiful art. We shared traditions. We helped the earth. Squawk! That feels good.”

They hung the Reuse Rainbow on the wall. It shone in the sun. Under it, they placed the Treasure Box so everyone could see. Pippa added a tiny note to the box: “Use again. Share with friends. Love the earth.”

As they cleaned up, they sang Ben’s family song. “Use a little, share a lot, keep it tidy, waste we do not!” Pippa added her happy “Squawk!” at the end. The room felt peaceful and bright.

Pippa looked at her friends. “Conservation means taking care of our world,” she said gently. “We can do it every day. We can reuse, we can share, and we can say sorry when we forget.”

Her friends nodded. They bumped paws, wings, and shells. Together, they smiled at their rainbow.

The End.
