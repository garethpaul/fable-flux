# Pippin's Honest Breakfast

Pippin the penguin waddled into the bright dining room, his orange beak gleaming in the morning sunlight. The wooden table was set with colorful bowls and spoons that clinked softly as Mama Penguin arranged them.

"Good morning, Pippin!" chirped Mama Penguin. "I made your favorite fish porridge for breakfast."

Pippin's tummy rumbled happily. But as he climbed onto his special blue chair, he accidentally knocked over his sister Penny's juice cup with his flipper. Orange juice splashed across the table and dripped onto the floor with tiny *plip-plop* sounds.

"Oh no!" Pippin gasped, watching the sticky mess spread.

Penny waddled in just then. "What happened to my juice?" she asked, looking confused.

Pippin felt his heart beating fast. He looked at the puddle of juice, then at Penny's sad face. For a moment, he thought about saying he didn't know what happened. Maybe no one would find out it was him.

But then Pippin remembered what Mama always said: "Honest penguins are happy penguins."

"I'm sorry, Penny," Pippin said, his voice small but clear. "I knocked over your juice with my flipper by accident. I didn't mean to, but I did it."

Penny's eyes grew wide, but instead of getting angry, she smiled. "Thank you for telling me the truth, Pippin. Accidents happen!"

Mama Penguin heard everything from the kitchen. She waddled over with paper towels that felt soft and crinkly in her flippers. "I'm proud of you for being honest, Pippin. Now, let's clean this up together."

"But I made such a big mess," Pippin sighed, feeling frustrated as he looked at all the orange juice. "And now we'll be late for breakfast!"

"Don't worry," said Mama gently. "When we work as a team, everything gets done faster. Penny, could you help too?"

"Of course!" Penny chirped.

The three penguins worked together. Pippin carefully wiped the table with slow, careful movements. Penny picked up the wet paper towels and put them in the trash. Mama mopped the floor until it sparkled clean again.

"Look how shiny everything is now!" said Pippin, admiring their work.

"And we still have time for breakfast," added Mama, checking the big round clock on the wall.

As they sat down to eat, Mama poured fresh apple juice into a new cup for Penny. The sweet smell of fish porridge filled the air, making everyone's tummies rumble with excitement.

"You know what, Pippin?" said Penny, taking a sip of her juice. "I'm glad you told the truth. It made me feel good that you trusted me with what really happened."

Pippin felt warm and happy inside, like sunshine was filling his belly. "Being honest feels much better than hiding the truth," he said with a big smile.

"That's right," said Mama, giving Pippin a gentle hug with her soft, warm flipper. "When we tell the truth, even when we make mistakes, people trust us more. And when we help each other clean up, problems become much smaller."

Pippin took a big spoonful of his delicious porridge and grinned. He had learned that being honest wasn't scary at all—it actually made everything better!

From that day on, whenever Pippin made a mistake, he always remembered how good it felt to tell the truth right away.

The End.
