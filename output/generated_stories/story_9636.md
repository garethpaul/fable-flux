# Hoppy's Big Jump Day

In a sunny backyard filled with soft green grass, a little kangaroo joey named Hoppy bounced excitedly. The warm sunshine tickled his fuzzy brown fur, and butterflies danced around the colorful flowers.

"Today I'm going to jump over that big rock!" Hoppy announced proudly. He pointed his tiny paw at a smooth, gray rock sitting in the middle of the yard.

Hoppy took three big hops backward. *Bounce, bounce, bounce!* Then he ran forward as fast as his little legs could carry him. But when he jumped... *thud!* Hoppy landed right on top of the rock instead of going over it.

"Ow!" Hoppy rubbed his fuzzy bottom. His ears drooped down sadly.

He tried again. *Run, run, jump!* This time, Hoppy didn't even reach the rock. He fell into the soft grass with a gentle *plop!*

"This is too hard!" Hoppy cried out, feeling his heart beat fast with frustration. His little fists were clenched tight, and his face felt hot and red.

Hoppy's mama poked her head out from their cozy burrow. "What's wrong, little one?" she asked gently.

"I can't jump over the rock! I've tried and tried, but I keep falling!" Hoppy's voice was shaky with upset feelings.

Mama hopped over slowly and sat down next to Hoppy in the cool shade of an apple tree. "Sometimes when we feel frustrated, it helps to take slow, deep breaths," she said softly.

"Like this?" Hoppy asked, taking a big breath that made his little chest puff out like a balloon.

"Perfect! Now let the air out slowly... whoooosh," Mama demonstrated. "Feel how that makes your body calm and relaxed?"

Hoppy tried it again. *Breathe in... breathe out.* His shoulders felt less tight, and his heart stopped racing so fast.

"Much better!" Hoppy smiled. "But I still want to jump over that rock."

"Maybe," Mama suggested with twinkling eyes, "you could try a different way? What if you practice on something smaller first?"

Hoppy looked around the backyard with fresh eyes. He noticed a small stick lying near the flower bed. "I could start with that little stick!"

*Hop, hop, jump!* Hoppy sailed right over the stick easily. "I did it!"

Next, he found a slightly bigger stone. *Bounce, bounce, leap!* Over he went again!

"I'm getting better!" Hoppy giggled with joy. His confidence grew with each successful jump.

After practicing on five different objects, each one a little bigger than the last, Hoppy felt ready. He stood in front of the big rock again, but this time he felt calm and peaceful inside.

He took three slow, deep breaths just like Mama taught him. *Breathe in... breathe out.* His body felt strong and relaxed.

*Bounce, bounce, bounce!* Hoppy ran forward with steady steps. When he reached the rock, he pushed off with his powerful legs and... *whoosh!* He sailed right over it, landing softly on the other side!

"I did it! I did it!" Hoppy cheered, doing a happy dance in the warm sunshine.

Mama clapped her paws together proudly. "You learned something very important today, Hoppy. When things feel hard, staying calm and trying new ways can help us succeed!"

Hoppy nodded wisely. "And taking deep breaths makes everything feel better!"

From that day on, whenever Hoppy felt frustrated, he remembered to breathe deeply and stay calm. He discovered that with patience and practice, he could overcome any challenge that came his way.

The End.
