# Bubbles the Dolphin Saves the Backyard

In a sunny backyard with warm grass and bright yellow flowers, a small blue pool sparkled in the light. The air smelled like oranges and fresh-cut grass. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” and a soft breeze said, “Whoosh.”

Splash! A playful dolphin named Bubbles popped up in the little pool. Bubbles wasn’t a big ocean dolphin. Bubbles was small, curious, and very friendly. He wiggled his tail and squeaked, “Eee-eee! Hello, sunshine!”

Mia and Leo, two kids with giggly smiles, clapped their hands. “Hi, Bubbles!” Mia said. “Do you want to play?”

“Yes!” squeaked Bubbles. “Let’s play ‘Find the Shiny Leaf!’”

Bubbles dove and spun. Water sprinkled in tiny rainbows. “Whee!” he cheered. But then his nose bumped something. “Ow!” He lifted a soggy plastic bag from the water. “Oh no.”

“What is it?” asked Leo.

“This bag is in the water,” Bubbles said. He wrinkled his nose. “It smells yucky. It does not belong here.”

Mia looked around. The grass had snack wrappers, a bent straw, and a crumpled cup. The flowers looked droopy. A bee buzzed sadly over a sticky soda spot.

Mia frowned. “Our backyard is messy.”

Bubbles took a deep breath. “We have a problem,” he said in a calm, brave voice. “The yard is not clean. The pool is not safe. This can hurt plants and animals.”

Leo’s eyes grew wide. “What do we do?”

Bubbles floated tall and spoke clearly. “We can fix it. We can care for our world. We can be helpers.”

Mia nodded. “Let’s make a plan!”

Step 1: Look and sort.

Bubbles pointed his fin like a teacher. “We will look for trash and sort it. Plastic in the blue bin. Paper in the green bin. Food bits in the brown bin.”

Leo smiled. “I can wear gloves.”

Mia tied her hair and said, “I will hold the bags.”

They started together. “Pick, sort, and smile!” sang Bubbles. He made a happy tune. “Eee-eee, pick and sort, make our yard a clean report!”

Mia picked up the bent straw. “Plastic goes in the blue bin.” Clink!

Leo found a banana peel. “Food goes in the brown bin.” Plop!

Bubbles nudged the soggy bag into the blue bin. “Good job, team!” he squeaked.

Step 2: Ask with kindness.

A neighbor boy named Max leaned over the fence with a juice box. He crunched his chips and shook his hands clean over the grass. Crumbs fell. A chip bag blew toward the flowers.

Bubbles lifted his head and spoke with gentle strength. “Max,” he said kindly, “please don’t drop trash. It can hurt bees and flowers. We are keeping our backyard clean. Will you help us?”

Max blinked. “Oh. I didn’t know.” He looked at the bees and the droopy flowers. “I’m sorry.”

Mia smiled. “Thank you for listening.”

Max nodded. He put his empty juice box in the blue bin and picked up the chip bag. “I can help!”

Step 3: Wash and water.

The yard looked better. The pool still had sticky splashes on the edge. “Let’s wash,” said Leo.

Bubbles showed how. “Use a little soap, lots of water, and gentle scrubs.” Swish-swish! The edges turned shiny. The air smelled soapy and clean.

Mia brought a small watering can. “These flowers are thirsty.” Drip-drip. The flowers lifted their faces to the sun. A bee buzzed happily, “Bzzzz!”

Step 4: Healthy habits.

Bubbles tapped his fin to his chin. “We can keep it clean every day. We can put trash in the right bins. We can drink water from reusable cups. We can wash our hands after cleaning.”

Mia washed her hands. “Rub-rub, rinse-rinse.” She giggled at the cool water.

Leo filled a green bottle. “I like my reusable cup!”

Max held up a tiny sign he drew. It said, “Please keep our yard clean.” He taped it to the fence with help from Mia. “This reminds us,” he said.

Step 5: Celebrate and share.

Bubbles spun in the bright pool. Splash! The water glittered. The grass smelled fresh. The flowers stood tall. The birds sang louder, “Cheep-cheep-cheeeep!”

“We did it,” said Mia.

“We solved the problem,” said Leo.

“We cared for our world,” squeaked Bubbles. “That is environmental care. When we clean up, sort our trash, use less plastic, and help living things, we keep our home safe.”

Max smiled. “I understand now. I will be careful. If I see litter, I will ask kindly, ‘Please use the bin.’”

Bubbles beamed. “That is assertive and kind. We can speak up with gentle words.”

They all took a deep breath. The breeze was sweet. The sun felt warm. The pool made happy ripples. Bubbles whispered, “Thank you, backyard.”

Mia patted the grass. “We will keep you clean.”

Leo pointed to the bins. “Blue for plastic. Green for paper. Brown for food. We know what to do!”

Max gave a thumbs-up. “Team Clean!”

Bubbles leaped one more time, and rainbow drops danced in the sun. “Friends,” he said, “when we care for our backyard, we care for the Earth.”

They smiled together, proud and bright.

The End.
