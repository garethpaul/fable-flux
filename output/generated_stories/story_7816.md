# Benny Beaver's Fair Share Day

Benny Beaver loved helping his family with chores. His big, flat tail went *thump-thump* on the floor as he waddled into the warm, steamy laundry room. The washing machine made gentle *whoosh-whoosh* sounds, and clean clothes smelled like sunshine and soap bubbles.

"Good morning, Benny!" called Mama Beaver from behind a pile of fluffy towels. "Today we have lots of laundry to fold."

Benny's whiskers twitched with excitement. "I want to help! I want to help!" he chirped, clapping his little paws together.

But when Papa Beaver and Sister Beaver arrived, Benny noticed something that made his tummy feel funny. Papa Beaver got the big basket of easy-to-fold towels. Sister Beaver got the medium basket of smooth sheets. And Benny? He got the tiny basket with just three little washcloths.

"That's not fair!" Benny squeaked, his tail slapping the floor. "I can fold more than three washcloths! I'm a big helper too!"

Mama Beaver paused, holding a warm pillowcase. "Oh my, Benny. You're absolutely right. Everyone should get a fair share of the work."

Benny's brown eyes sparkled as he thought hard. His busy beaver brain began planning. "I have an idea!" he announced, standing tall on his hind legs. "What if we make it fair for everyone?"

He waddled around the laundry room, his nose sniffing the fresh, clean smell of each pile. *Sniff, sniff!* Then he started moving clothes around with his strong teeth and clever paws.

"Look!" Benny said proudly. "I'm making four equal piles! One, two, three, four!" He counted carefully, moving his paw over each stack. "Now everyone gets the same amount of work!"

Papa Beaver's eyes widened. "Benny, that's wonderful thinking!"

Sister Beaver giggled as she watched her little brother organize everything. "You made it fair for all of us!"

But Benny wasn't finished. His imagination was buzzing like busy bees! "And I have another idea for fairness!" he announced. "We can take turns with the fun jobs too!"

He pointed his tiny paw at the sock-matching station. "Papa can match socks first, then Sister, then me, then Mama! That way everyone gets to do the fun part!"

"*Brilliant!*" cheered Mama Beaver, her tail creating happy *pat-pat* sounds on the floor.

The family got to work with Benny's fair plan. They folded and sorted, taking turns and sharing equally. The laundry room filled with happy chatter and the soft *rustle* of clean clothes being folded.

"This is so much better!" Sister Beaver said as she carefully folded a shirt. "Everyone gets to help the same amount!"

"And everyone gets to have fun too!" added Papa Beaver, holding up two matching striped socks.

When they finished, Benny felt warm and proud inside, just like the cozy clothes they'd folded. His family gathered around him for a big, squeaky group hug.

"Thank you, Benny," Mama Beaver said softly. "You taught us something very important today. Being fair means making sure everyone gets treated equally and has the same chances to help and have fun."

Benny's whiskers twitched with happiness. "Being fair makes everyone feel good inside!" he declared.

From that day on, the Beaver family always remembered Benny's lesson about fairness. And whenever they had work to do, they made sure everyone got their fair share – because that's what loving families do.

The End.
