# Koko the Koala Learns to Paint

Koko the koala sat quietly in the bright, colorful art room. Sunlight streamed through the big windows, making the paint jars sparkle like jewels. Red, blue, yellow, and green paints lined the shelves. Brushes of all sizes hung neatly on hooks. The room smelled like fresh paper and creativity.

"Today we're going to paint pictures!" announced Mrs. Robin, the art teacher. "Everyone choose your colors and start creating!"

All the other animals rushed to grab their supplies. Benny the bear took the biggest brush. Lily the lamb picked bright pink paint. Sam the squirrel grabbed three colors at once.

But Koko just sat there, feeling worried. His fuzzy gray ears drooped down.

"I don't know how to paint," Koko whispered to himself. "What if I mess up? What if my picture looks silly?"

He watched as his friends dipped their brushes and made colorful strokes on their papers. Splash! Swoosh! Their paintings came to life with trees, flowers, and rainbows.

"Mrs. Robin," Koko called softly, raising his small paw. "Can you help me? I don't know what to paint."

Mrs. Robin smiled kindly. "Koko, the best paintings come from your own heart. What makes you happy?"

Koko thought hard. "I like sitting in eucalyptus trees and watching clouds."

"That sounds wonderful! Why don't you try painting that?" Mrs. Robin suggested.

"But what if I can't do it right?" Koko asked, his voice small and uncertain.

"There's no wrong way to paint your feelings," Mrs. Robin said gently. "Trust yourself, little one."

Koko took a deep breath. The air tasted fresh and full of possibilities. Slowly, he walked to the paint station. He picked up a medium-sized brush – not too big, not too small, just right for his paws.

First, he dipped his brush in blue paint. It felt cool and smooth. Carefully, he painted the sky across his paper. The blue spread like a gentle wave.

"I did that by myself!" Koko thought proudly.

Next, he mixed green and brown paint. The colors swirled together like chocolate and mint. He painted a tall tree with a thick trunk and leafy branches.

"This is fun!" Koko giggled, his tail wiggling with excitement.

For the clouds, he dabbed white paint softly across the blue sky. Each little touch looked like fluffy cotton balls floating overhead.

Finally, Koko painted himself – a small gray koala sitting peacefully in the tree, just like he loved to do in real life.

When he stepped back to look at his painting, Koko felt warm and proud inside. His picture wasn't perfect, but it was his very own creation.

"Koko, that's beautiful!" exclaimed Lily the lamb. "You painted it all by yourself!"

"I love how peaceful your koala looks," added Benny the bear.

Mrs. Robin nodded approvingly. "You should be proud, Koko. You tried something new and trusted yourself to do it."

Koko beamed with happiness. "I was scared at first, but I learned I can do things by myself when I try!"

He looked around the art room with grateful eyes – at his friends' encouraging smiles, at Mrs. Robin's kind face, and at his very own masterpiece.

From that day on, whenever Koko felt unsure about trying something new, he remembered his painting and thought, "I can do this myself if I just believe in me!"

The End.
