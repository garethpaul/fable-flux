# Fawn’s Warm Kitchen Team

In a warm kitchen, golden light danced on the walls. The air smelled like sweet cinnamon and soft, buttery bread. A gentle deer fawn named Fawn stood on a little step stool by the big wooden table. Her ears were perky, and her nose twitched happily.

“I want to make apple muffins for my friends,” Fawn said. “I can share. Sharing is kind.”

Fawn opened the cupboard. Clink! The bowls made a tiny music sound. She touched the smooth spoon and felt the cool metal. Her little hooves clicked on the stool. She hummed a small song, “Mix, mix, mix!”

Fawn looked at the recipe card. “Flour, eggs, milk, apples, cinnamon,” she read slowly. “I will try. I can do it.”

Just then, there was a soft knock on the kitchen door. Tap-tap.

“Come in!” called Fawn.

Her friends entered, shaking off little snowflakes from their fur and feathers. Bunny hopped in with bright eyes. Squirrel scampered, tail all fluffy. Robin fluttered to the window ledge, chirping a cheery note.

“Oh! It smells warm and nice!” said Bunny. “What are you making?”

“I want to make apple muffins,” said Fawn. “But I never made muffins by myself.”

Squirrel sniffed the cinnamon. “I love muffins,” he said. “But I don’t know how either.”

Robin tilted her head. “We can try together,” she chirped. “We can be a team.”

Fawn felt a flutter in her chest. She wanted to lead, but she also felt a little shy. Her cheeks warmed like fresh toast. “I feel nervous,” she said in a small voice. “What if I make a mistake?”

Bunny’s ears wiggled kindly. “It’s okay to feel nervous,” Bunny said. “We can help.”

Fawn took a deep breath. She smelled apples and spice. She felt the smooth table under her hoof. “Thank you,” she said. “I will take the first step. That is called initiative.”

She put the big bowl in the middle. “Friends,” Fawn said, standing tall on her stool, “let’s make muffins as a team. I will read the recipe out loud. Bunny, can you measure the flour? Squirrel, can you chop the apples with the safe little cutter? Robin, can you count the muffin cups?”

Bunny nodded. “I can do flour!”

Squirrel cheered. “Chop-chop apples!”

Robin chirped, “I can count—one, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten, eleven, twelve!”

Fawn smiled. “Good team,” she said. She felt her tummy relax. Leading felt good when it was kind.

They began. The flour puffed like a tiny cloud. Pffft! Bunny giggled. “Snow flour!” he laughed.

Squirrel pressed the little cutter on the apples. Chop, chop, chop. “These smell sweet,” he said, licking a paw and then remembering. “Oops! Paws off. We wash first.” He went to the sink, and the water went splish, splash, warm on his paws.

Robin counted eggs. “One egg… two eggs…” Crack! A little bit of shell fell into the bowl.

Fawn looked. Her heart did a hop. “Oh dear,” she said. “There is shell.”

Robin’s feathers drooped. “I feel sad. I made a mistake.”

Fawn touched Robin’s wing gently. “It’s okay,” she said. “Mistakes happen. We can fix it together.” She used a big spoon like a little boat. The shell piece stuck to the spoon, and she lifted it out.

Robin brightened. “Thank you, Fawn. I feel better now.”

They mixed the batter. Swirl, swirl, swirl. The spoon went round and round, and the batter made soft plop sounds. Cinnamon floated up in the air like a cozy hug. Fawn gave each friend a turn to stir. “We share the jobs,” she said. “That is friendship.”

Then, they faced a new problem. The muffin tin was missing!

“Where is it?” asked Bunny, looking under a napkin.

Squirrel opened a drawer. Rattle-rattle. “Not here.”

Fawn felt worried again. “We need the tin,” she said. Her ears lowered. She took another deep breath. In and out. Warm air in. Calm air out.

“I have an idea,” Fawn said. “Let’s look carefully and listen for clues.” She paused and heard a tiny metal ting-ting sound from the lower cupboard.

“Do you hear that?” Fawn asked.

They all listened. Ting-ting.

Fawn opened the lower cupboard. There it was, shiny and waiting. “Found it!” she said. “Good listening, team.”

They lined the cups with paper. Rustle, rustle. Robin counted again, and this time she smiled. Squirrel spooned the batter. Plop. Plop. Bunny sprinkled a little cinnamon on top. The oven door opened with a warm whoosh, like a gentle sigh, and the muffins went in to bake.

They waited. The kitchen filled with a sweet apple smell. It felt safe and snug. Fawn felt proud and calm. She looked at her friends. “Thank you for helping,” she said. “We worked together.”

Ding! The timer rang. Fawn used mitts and carefully took out the muffins. They were golden and puffy. Steam rose in soft curls.

“Wow,” whispered Bunny.

“They look happy,” said Squirrel.

“They smell like hugs,” chirped Robin.

Fawn placed the muffins on a rack. They all sat at the table. Each friend took a warm, soft muffin. They blew gently. Hoo-hoo, cool air. Then they took small bites. Mmm. Sweet apple, cozy cinnamon, and friendship.

Fawn felt a glow in her chest. “Today, I learned something,” she said. “When I feel nervous, I can say how I feel. I can take the first step. I can be a kind leader. And with friends, problems get solved.”

Bunny nodded. “Friends listen and help.”

Squirrel added, “Friends share jobs.”

Robin chirped, “Friends fix mistakes with care.”

Fawn smiled. “Friendship makes the kitchen warm—and our hearts warm too.”

They clinked their muffin papers together like tiny cups. “To friendship!” they said.

The End.
