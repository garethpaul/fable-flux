# Bunny's Fair Share Kitchen

In a cozy, warm kitchen filled with the sweet smell of fresh carrots and herbs, lived a determined little rabbit named Rosie. The kitchen had sunshine streaming through yellow curtains, and pots and pans that gleamed like silver stars.

Every morning, Rosie's family gathered around their big wooden table for breakfast. But there was a problem that made Rosie's whiskers twitch with worry.

"More carrots for me!" shouted Rosie's big brother Max, grabbing the biggest orange carrots from the basket.

"I want all the lettuce!" cried her sister Lily, pulling the green leaves toward herself.

Poor little Rosie was left with only tiny carrot pieces and wilted lettuce scraps. Her tummy rumbled sadly. "This isn't fair," she whispered, her brown eyes filling with tears.

Mama Rabbit was busy cooking and didn't notice. Papa Rabbit was reading his newspaper. Rosie knew she had to find a way to make things fair for everyone.

"I have an idea!" Rosie said, her ears perking up with determination.

That afternoon, while her siblings napped, Rosie got to work in the warm kitchen. She found colorful paper, safety scissors, and crayons in the craft drawer. The kitchen smelled like cinnamon and felt as cozy as a hug.

Snip, snip, snip! Rosie carefully cut out circles of red, orange, and green paper. She drew happy vegetables on each one - smiling carrots, cheerful lettuce, and giggling celery.

"What are you making, little one?" asked Mama Rabbit, wiping flour from her paws.

"Something to help us share fairly," Rosie explained, her small paws working carefully with the crayons.

Next, Rosie crafted a special spinning wheel from cardboard. She painted it bright colors and attached it to a pencil so it could spin round and round. On the wheel, she drew pictures of all the healthy foods they ate.

The next morning, when Max reached for all the carrots again, Rosie hopped onto her chair.

"Wait!" she called out bravely. "I made something special for our family."

She showed everyone her beautiful spinning wheel. "This will help us share our food fairly. We each take turns spinning, and whatever it lands on, that's what we get first. Then we make sure everyone gets some of everything!"

Max looked curious. "How does it work?"

Rosie demonstrated, giving the wheel a gentle spin. It went round and round with a soft whirring sound before stopping on a picture of lettuce.

"See? Now whoever spun gets to take some lettuce first, but then we pass it around so everyone gets some. No one goes hungry, and no one takes everything!"

Lily clapped her paws together. "That's so smart, Rosie!"

Papa Rabbit put down his newspaper and smiled proudly. "What a wonderful way to be fair to everyone."

From that day forward, the family used Rosie's spinning wheel every morning. Everyone got plenty of healthy vegetables, and mealtimes became fun instead of fights. The warm kitchen filled with laughter and the happy sounds of a family sharing together.

"Thank you for teaching us about fairness, Rosie," said Mama Rabbit, giving her a warm hug that smelled like fresh bread.

Rosie's heart felt as warm as their cozy kitchen. She learned that when something isn't fair, she could use her creativity and courage to make it better for everyone.

The End.
