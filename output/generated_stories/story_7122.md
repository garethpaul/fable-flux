# Roo’s Big Little Morning

In a cozy bedroom with soft yellow light, a joyful kangaroo joey named Roo woke up. The blanket felt warm and fluffy. The pillow smelled like clean soap. Birds chirped outside the window, “Cheep, cheep!”

Roo stretched tiny paws. “Good morning, me!” Roo giggled. “Today, I try to do little things by myself.”

Mama’s voice floated from the hallway, gentle like a hug. “Good morning, Roo! Are you waking up?”

“Yes, Mama,” Roo called back. “I want to try my morning jobs all by myself.”

Mama peeked in with a smile. “You can do it. I’m right here if you need help.”

Roo felt brave. Roo’s bedroom was cozy and calm. A small rug felt tickly under Roo’s toes. A basket of blocks sat by the bed. A picture on the wall showed children from around the world holding hands. Roo waved to the picture. “Hello, friends! I can be a helper too.”

Roo’s first job was to make the bed. The blanket was big and a little wiggly. Roo took a deep breath. “One side, then the other,” Roo said softly. Swish went the blanket. Pat, pat went Roo’s paws. The bed looked neat. “I did it!” Roo laughed, tail thumping with happy thumps.

Mama clapped softly. “Nice coordination, Roo.”

“Co-or-di-na-tion,” Roo tried the big word. “That means my body and my brain are working together.”

“Exactly,” Mama said.

Next, Roo opened the drawer. “Time to choose clothes.” Roo touched a soft blue shirt. It felt smooth like the sky. Roo picked stretchy pants that felt warm. “These help me hop,” Roo said. Roo tried the shirt. One paw found a sleeve. The other paw got stuck. “Hmm,” Roo said. Roo paused and took another breath. “I will try again.”

“Would you like a hint?” Mama asked.

“Yes, please,” Roo said calmly.

“Peek inside the sleeve to find the hole,” Mama said.

Roo peeked. There it was! Slip, pop! The paw went through. “Thank you, Mama. I asked with peaceful words.”

“You did,” Mama said. “That was peaceful communication.”

Roo smiled. “I can ask nicely. I can try again.”

Now for socks! The socks were small like two little clouds. Roo sat on the rug. “Heel to heel,” Roo sang in a tiny song. Wiggle, wiggle. The socks snuggled onto Roo’s feet. “Ta-da!”

Roo’s tummy made a happy rumble. “Breakfast time!” But first, one more job: toy pick-up. Blocks were spread like colorful rainbows. Roo hummed a steady tune. “Red, blue, green, yellow,” Roo said, putting each block in the basket. Clack, clack, clack. The basket grew full. The floor grew clear. “I’m completing my tasks,” Roo said proudly.

Mama looked proud too. “You are taking good care of our home.”

Roo thought about the picture on the wall again. “When I take care of my room,” Roo said, “I help the whole world a little bit.”

“That’s right,” Mama said. “We are all part of one big world. When we help at home, we practice helping everywhere.”

Roo hopped to the door and paused. “Mama, may I carry my own cup at breakfast?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Use two paws and go slow.”

In the kitchen, sunlight made the table shine. The cereal smelled sweet. The milk felt cold. Roo held a small cup with two steady paws. Step, step, step. “I’m going slow like a turtle,” Roo whispered. Roo set the cup down. Plink! It landed safely. “I did it by myself!”

Mama beamed. “You used careful coordination and patience.”

Roo munched cereal. Crunch, crunch. “It tastes extra yummy when I help,” Roo said.

After breakfast, Roo brushed teeth in the cozy bathroom. The mint felt tingly. Swish, swish. “Up and down, all around,” Roo sang. Foam made a silly mustache. Roo giggled, then rinsed. “Clean!”

Back in the bedroom, Roo looked around. Bed made. Clothes on. Toys away. “I finished my morning jobs!” Roo cheered.

Mama knelt to Roo’s eye level. “Roo, I saw how you tried first, asked kindly for help, and kept going. How do you feel?”

Roo put a paw on a thumping little chest. “I feel proud and calm. My body and my brain worked together. I can be independent.”

“That’s a strong word,” Mama said. “Independent means you are learning to do things on your own, and you know when to ask for help.”

Roo nodded. “I am little, but I can help my room, my home, and our big world with kind actions.”

From the window, the birds chirped again, like tiny cheers. Roo waved and whispered, “Hello, world! I can help.”

Mama opened her arms. Roo hopped in for a warm cuddle. The cuddle felt soft and safe.

Roo pulled back and smiled. “Tomorrow, I will try again. One side, then the other. Slow and steady. Ask kindly. Finish my tasks.”

Mama kissed Roo’s forehead. “That sounds like a great plan.”

Roo took a big happy breath. The room smelled like sunshine and soap. The rug was cozy under Roo’s toes. The basket of blocks was tidy. Roo’s heart felt bright and brave.

“I can do little things by myself,” Roo said. “And little things grow into big things.”

The End.
