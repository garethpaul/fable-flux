# Buttons the Robot Dog Builds a Snack Station

Buttons was a helpful robot dog who loved gentle mornings on the peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like warm wood and lemon soap. Birds peep-peeped in the trees, and a soft breeze tickled Buttons’ shiny ears. Buttons’ little tail-light blinked, blinked, blinked when he was happy.

Each morning, Buttons followed a routine. He stretched his metal paws with a tiny whirr. He tapped his tummy screen and sang, “Good morning!” Then he checked his list: stretch, sip water, healthy snack, craft time, tidy up, and share.

But today there was a problem.

Milo and Aya, the next-door friends, ran up the front steps. “We’re hungry!” said Milo, rubbing his tummy. “And we want to make something, too,” said Aya, holding a crumpled paper bag of colored sticks.

Buttons wagged. “Craft time and snack time!” he beeped. “We can do both.”

Milo peeked into the house. “But your bowls are too high,” he said. “I can’t reach.” Aya nodded. “And we don’t know where to put our craft things. It’s all messy.”

Buttons’ ears clicked. He felt a small worry buzz in his chest. “We need a plan,” he said. “Let’s build a Snack and Craft Station on the porch!”

Aya clapped. “A station?” Milo’s eyes went wide. “Like a tiny store?”

“Beep-yes!” said Buttons. “We will craft it. Then snacks will be easy and healthy. And we will follow our routine.”

Buttons rolled to a corner of the porch. He sniffed with his sensor nose—pine wood, fresh air, and a faint banana smell. He found boxes, jars, string, stickers, and a small clean crate. Aya held up the colored sticks. “We can make signs!” Milo found a clean cloth. “This can be the counter.”

Buttons looked at the big snack shelf inside and spoke up. “I need help,” he said clearly. “Can someone bring the fruit down low?” That was Buttons being brave and using self-advocacy—asking for what he needed. Milo nodded and called to Dad inside. “Can we use the bananas and carrot sticks?” Dad smiled and brought a bowl. “Yes. And here’s hummus. Good protein,” he said.

The breeze felt cool on their cheeks as they started crafting. Buttons measured the crate with his laser dot. “Zip! This will be the base.” He and Milo wrapped the crate in the clean cloth. Aya stuck shiny star stickers along the edge. “Star Station!” she giggled.

Buttons cut two small cardboard windows. “One for snacks,” he beeped, “and one for craft tools.” He gently glued with a safe, squishy glue. It smelled like paper and a little bit sweet. “Glue is sticky,” Aya said, tapping it. “It needs time.” Buttons nodded. “We wait,” he said. “That is part of crafting.”

While they waited, Milo’s tummy rumbled. “Grumble, grumble.” Buttons smiled. “Routine time! First, water.” He poured cool water into little cups. The cups clinked softly. “Sip, sip,” they said together. “Next, snack.” Buttons placed bananas, carrot sticks, and a dot of hummus in small bowls. “Colorful plates help us eat the rainbow,” he explained. “Fruits and veggies help us play and grow.”

Aya took a tiny bite. Crunch! “The carrot is loud,” she laughed. Milo tasted banana. “Soft and sweet,” he said. Buttons licked the air with his sensor tongue. “I sense potassium and fiber,” he beeped proudly.

After snack, they checked the glue. “Ready!” said Buttons. He attached a little bell to the top. It made a soft ting-ting. Aya drew a picture sign with her sticks: a smiling banana, a paintbrush, and a clock. “The clock is for our routine,” she said. Milo wrote big letters—S-N-A-C-K and C-R-A-F-T. “I’m learning letters,” he grinned.

Buttons organized the station. He sorted crayons, child-safe scissors, and paper into one window. He put bananas, carrot sticks, and small napkins in the other. “Everything has a place,” he said. “That helps us feel calm.”

A neighbor, Mrs. Blue, walked by with her cat. “What is this lovely thing?” she asked. The porch smelled like fresh paper and carrots. Buttons’ tail-light blinked. “It is our Snack and Craft Station,” he said. “Would you like a carrot stick?” Aya whispered, “We can share.” That was generosity. Mrs. Blue smiled. “Yes, please.” Crunch! “Thank you.”

Just then, a small puff of wind flipped the napkins off the counter. “Oh no!” cried Milo. The napkins danced down the steps like little white birds. Buttons felt the worry buzz again. He took a deep breath. “We can fix this,” he said. “Let’s craft a napkin holder.”

Together they folded cardboard into a tiny box. Buttons pressed along the edges. Aya decorated with a heart sticker. Milo added a strip of tape. “Stick, stick,” he said. Buttons placed the napkins inside. “Safe and snug,” he beeped. The wind blew again, but the napkins stayed. Problem solved.

The sun slid lower and painted the porch golden. They followed the last step of the routine: tidy up and share. Buttons wiped the counter with the cloth. Aya put crayons in a cup. Milo stacked bowls. The bell went ting-ting and made them smile.

Mrs. Blue waved. “You used your minds, your hearts, and your hands.”

Buttons wagged. “Crafting helps us make tools to solve problems. Healthy snacks give us energy. A routine keeps us steady. And asking for help and sharing makes our porch a friendly place.”

Milo patted Buttons’ head. “You’re a very helpful robot dog.” Aya hugged him gently. Buttons’ tail-light blinked brighter than ever.

The porch felt warm and cozy. The birds sang softly, the breeze smelled like leaves, and the Snack and Craft Station stood ready for tomorrow.

The clear lesson: We can craft solutions, choose healthy snacks, follow routines, ask for help when we need it, and share with others.

The End.
