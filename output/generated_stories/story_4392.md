# Leo the Lion Cub's Big Voice

Leo was a brave little lion cub with golden fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a cozy house where Mama Lion worked in her home office. The office had a big wooden desk, a comfy rolling chair, and shelves full of colorful books.

Every morning, Leo watched Mama Lion tap-tap-tap on her computer. The clicking sounds were like a gentle rain on the roof. Leo loved being near Mama, but there was one big problem.

"Mama," Leo said in his small voice, "I want to help you work."

"That's sweet, Leo," Mama said, not looking up from her screen. "But I need to focus right now."

Leo's ears drooped. He felt invisible, like a tiny mouse instead of a brave lion. Day after day, he asked to help, but Mama was always too busy. Leo felt his little heart grow heavy.

One sunny Tuesday, Leo decided he needed to be brave like the big lions in his storybooks. He sat up tall and thought hard. "I need a plan," he said to himself, puffing out his fluffy chest.

First, Leo watched carefully. He noticed Mama always needed her coffee mug filled, her papers organized, and her pencils sharpened. The office smelled like coffee and vanilla candles, and Leo could hear Mama's fingers dancing across the keyboard.

Leo padded to the kitchen on his soft paws. He couldn't reach the coffee pot, but he could do something else! He found Mama's favorite mug and carefully carried it in his mouth to the office.

"Mama," Leo said clearly, standing tall like a mountain. "I see you need help. I can't make coffee yet, but I can bring you your mug when it's empty. I can also sort your colorful pens and keep you company quietly."

Mama Lion stopped typing and looked down at her brave little cub. Her whiskers twitched with surprise. "Leo, you've been thinking about this, haven't you?"

"Yes!" Leo's tail swished with excitement. "I made a plan! I watched what you do, and I found ways I can help that won't disturb your important work."

Mama's eyes sparkled like stars. "That's very thoughtful, my brave little lion. Let's try it!"

Leo beamed with pride. He organized the colored pens into a rainbow pattern, making soft clicking sounds as he worked. When Mama's mug was empty, he gently took it to remind her to get more coffee. He even found a small cushion to sit on quietly nearby.

"This is wonderful!" Mama said, giving Leo a warm hug that smelled like flowers. "You spoke up for what you needed, made a smart plan, and found a way we could both be happy."

From that day on, Leo became Mama's special office helper. He learned that using his voice clearly and thinking of solutions made him feel strong and important.

"Speaking up for yourself is brave," Mama told him as they worked together. "And when you think of ways to solve problems, you show how smart and caring you are."

Leo purred happily, his golden fur glowing in the warm office light. He had discovered that his voice was powerful, and his ideas mattered. Being a brave little lion meant more than just roaring – it meant speaking up, making plans, and working together.

The End.
