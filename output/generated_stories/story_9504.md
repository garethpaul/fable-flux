# Benny Bear's Colorful Discovery

Benny Bear was a small, fluffy cub with soft brown fur and bright curious eyes. One sunny morning, he wandered into Grandpa's old garden shed behind the big oak tree. The wooden door creaked as he pushed it open.

"Wow!" Benny gasped. Sunlight streamed through the dusty window, showing shelves full of interesting things. There were old flower pots, gardening tools, and in the corner... something magical.

Benny's nose twitched as he smelled something sweet and colorful. He followed his nose to a wooden table covered with paint jars, brushes, and blank paper. The paints sparkled like jewels - red like strawberries, blue like the sky, and yellow like sunshine.

"I want to paint!" Benny said, clapping his paws together. He grabbed a big brush and dipped it in the red paint. Splash! He made a wobbly circle on the paper. Then he tried blue - swoosh! A wavy line appeared.

Benny was having so much fun that he didn't hear tiny footsteps behind him.

"Excuse me," squeaked a small voice.

Benny turned around and saw Rosie Rabbit, her white fur clean and her pink nose twitching nervously.

"This is my family's art shed," Rosie said quietly. "My grandma and I paint flowers here every day."

Benny's ears drooped. He looked at the messy table covered with his paint splatters. "Oh no! I'm sorry, Rosie. I didn't know this belonged to someone else."

Rosie's eyes were sad as she looked at her special painting space. "You used up lots of paint, and now the brushes are all mixed up."

Benny felt his tummy do a little flip. He had been so excited about painting that he forgot to ask permission. "I made a big mistake," he said, hanging his head.

"I really love to paint too," Benny added softly. "The colors are so pretty, and it makes me happy inside."

Rosie's ears perked up a little. "You like art?"

"Yes! But I should have asked first. That wasn't nice of me." Benny looked around the shed. "Can I help clean up? And maybe... could you teach me the right way to paint?"

Rosie's face brightened like sunshine after rain. "You want to learn together?"

"Yes, please! I promise to be more careful and respectful."

Rosie hopped over to the sink. "Let's wash the brushes first. Grandma taught me that clean brushes make the prettiest colors."

Working together, they cleaned each brush carefully. Rosie showed Benny how to dip just the tip in paint and how to clean between colors.

"Now we can share the paints," Rosie said, arranging the jars neatly. "You use this side of the table, and I'll use this side."

Benny painted a big, happy sun while Rosie painted delicate purple flowers. They shared the yellow paint for Benny's sun rays and Rosie's flower centers.

"This is much more fun together!" Benny laughed, his tail wiggling with joy.

"Sharing makes everything better," Rosie agreed, adding green leaves to her flowers.

When Grandma Rabbit arrived, she found two friends painting happily side by side, their artwork bright and beautiful.

"I see you've learned that the best art comes from kind hearts and sharing hands," Grandma Rabbit smiled.

Benny nodded, his painting paws now clean and careful. He had discovered that asking permission, being respectful, and sharing with friends made art even more wonderful than painting alone.

The End.
