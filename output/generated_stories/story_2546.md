# Momo’s Big Breath and Little List

Sunlight danced on the rug in the playful living room. Pillows made a soft mountain. Crayons rolled like tiny logs. A gentle smell of banana bread floated from the kitchen. Momo, a courageous monkey with bright eyes and quick hands, bounced on the couch and giggled. “Today is a big day!” he said. “I will climb the pillow mountain and draw a treasure map!”

Momo leaped to the floor. His tail swished. His feet went pat-pat on the rug. He grabbed a big sheet of paper. “I am brave. I can do big things,” he whispered.

Then Momo’s tummy made a tiny grumble. His shoulders felt tight. The room seemed too busy and loud. A toy drum went tap-tap. The fridge hummed. The window made a sunny stripe that felt too bright.

Momo paused. “Hmm,” he said softly. “My body feels wiggly. My head feels buzzy.” He put his hand on his chest. It was going fast. Thump-thump-thump.

He remembered what Nana had taught him. “Wellness is taking good care of me,” Nana had said with a warm smile. “I can breathe, move, drink water, and rest. I can also care for my home and friends. That is being well.”

Momo stood tall and spoke to himself like a friend. “I can try a calm breath,” he said. He put one hand on his tummy and one hand on his chest. “Smell the flower,” he whispered, taking a slow breath in through his nose. The air smelled like bananas and soap. “Blow the candle,” he said, breathing out slowly. He watched his tummy go in and out. “Smell the flower. Blow the candle.” He did it three times.

His heart slowed. Thump… thump. His shoulders softened. “That feels nice,” Momo said with a smile. “Taking care of me is brave.”

He took a sip of cool water. The cup was blue. The water felt cold and sweet on his tongue. “Ahh,” he said. He did three monkey stretches—up, down, side to side. The rug tickled his toes. The curtains swished when a breeze came in. He giggled. “Hello, fresh air!”

Momo felt ready to play again. He looked around. The crayons were scattered. A book lay open on its face. A sticker sheet stuck to the coffee table. “Our living room is our place,” he said. “I can help care for it. That is being a good helper to my home.” He thought of his neighbors who shared the building. “I can keep things safe and tidy. That is civic responsibility,” he said slowly, proud to use a big idea.

He found his pencil. It felt smooth and warm in his hand. “I will write a tiny plan,” he told himself. He drew a big square on his paper and wrote in bouncy letters:

- Breathe
- Water
- Stretch
- Tidy
- Play
- Share

Momo read his list aloud. “This is my wellness list.” He smiled. “I can help myself. That is self-reliance.”

He started with tidy. “Crayons in the box,” he sang. Click, clack, click. “Books on the shelf.” Thump, thump. He peeled the sticker off the table and put it on his paper as a shiny star. He fluffed the pillows into a gentle hill. The room looked friendly again. He felt proud and calm.

“Now I can discover!” Momo cheered. He opened the window a little more. The breeze smelled like grass and sunshine. Far away, a bird chirped. “Tweet-tweet!” Momo answered.

He drew his treasure map. “Here is the pillow hill,” he said, making wavy lines. “Here is the sunny stripe,” he said, drawing a yellow path. “Here is the water stop,” he added, sketching a tiny cup. He put a big banana sticker at the end. “Treasure!”

Momo began his adventure. “Step, step,” he said, climbing the pillow hill. He felt strong and steady. At the top, he paused. “Smell the flower.” Inhale. “Blow the candle.” Exhale. He grinned. He slid down the pillows wheee! and landed with a soft thump.

At the water stop, he sipped again. “Yum,” he said. He wiped a small drip and cleaned it with a cloth. “I keep our home clean and safe,” he said. “That helps everyone.”

He tiptoed along the sunny stripe. “Warm,” he whispered, feeling light on his fur. He whispered to himself, “I am brave. I am kind. I am calm.”

Just then, he saw a tiny note under the couch. It had fallen from the mail. “Oh!” Momo picked it up. “This belongs to Mrs. Owl upstairs.” The envelope felt papery and crinkly. He looked at his list. “Share,” he read.

Momo wrote a quick message on his map’s corner: “Dear Mrs. Owl, I found your mail. From, Momo.” He drew a smiley face. He placed the note in a clean basket by the front door for Mama to take upstairs. “I helped my neighbor,” he said. “That keeps our home community happy.”

Back on his map, Momo reached the big banana sticker. He peeled it and put it on his chest. “Treasure found!” he laughed.

Mama peeked in. “I see a calm explorer,” she said. “What did you learn today, brave Momo?”

Momo held up his list. “I learned wellness,” he said proudly. “Breathe, water, stretch, tidy, play, and share. When I care for me and my home, I feel good inside. I can discover new things and help others, too.”

Mama hugged him. He felt warm and safe. The living room felt bright and cozy. Momo tucked his map on the fridge with a banana magnet.

He took one more slow breath. “Smell the flower. Blow the candle.” He smiled. “I can do big things, one little step at a time.”

The End.
