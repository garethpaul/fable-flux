# Benny Beaver's Big Mix-Up

Benny Beaver was the busiest beaver in the whole forest. He loved to build things with his strong teeth and flat tail. Today, he was visiting his friend Rabbit's house for lunch in the cozy dining room.

The dining room smelled like fresh carrots and warm bread. Sunlight streamed through the window, making the wooden table shine. Benny's tummy rumbled as he looked at all the yummy food.

"Thank you for inviting me, Rabbit!" said Benny, his big front teeth showing as he smiled.

"You're welcome, Benny!" said Rabbit, hopping around the table. "I made your favorite - tree bark soup!"

But when Benny sat down in the wooden chair, something felt wrong. The chair was too small! His big beaver tail hung over the edge, and his paws barely fit on the table.

"This chair is broken!" Benny said loudly, his voice echoing in the dining room. "Someone should fix it right now!"

Rabbit's ears drooped. "Oh my," she whispered. "I'm sorry, Benny."

Benny felt frustrated. His brown fur bristled as he tried to squeeze into the tiny chair again. "This isn't fair!" he huffed. "I can't eat my soup like this!"

Just then, wise old Owl flew through the open window and perched on the back of a bigger chair.

"Hoo-hoo, what's all this fuss about?" asked Owl, tilting her feathered head.

"The chair is too small for me!" Benny complained. "It's not working right!"

Owl blinked her big yellow eyes slowly. "Benny, may I ask you something? What size is Rabbit?"

Benny looked at his friend. Rabbit was much smaller than him, with tiny paws and a fluffy cotton-ball tail.

"She's... little," Benny said quietly.

"And what size are you?" Owl asked gently.

Benny looked down at his big, flat tail and strong paws. "I'm... big."

"Ah," said Owl. "So the chair isn't broken. It's just the right size for someone else. Do you understand now?"

Benny's eyes grew wide as he discovered something important. The chair wasn't wrong - it was perfect for Rabbit! He had been thinking only about himself.

"Oh!" Benny gasped. "I understand now! The chair is Rabbit's size, not my size!"

Rabbit hopped closer. "It's okay, Benny. Let me get you a bigger chair from the other room."

"Wait," said Benny. He wanted to make things right. "First, I need to write something."

Benny found a piece of bark and used his sharp claw to scratch words on it. His writing was wobbly but clear: "SORRY RABBIT. I WAS WRONG. YOUR CHAIR IS PERFECT."

He handed the bark note to Rabbit, whose pink nose twitched with happiness.

"Thank you, Benny," said Rabbit, giving him a warm hug. "That means so much to me."

Rabbit brought a big, sturdy chair that fit Benny perfectly. Now he could sit comfortably and enjoy his tree bark soup. The warm, earthy taste filled his mouth as steam tickled his whiskers.

"This soup is delicious!" Benny said, smiling. "And this chair is just right for me!"

Owl nodded approvingly. "When we take time to understand others and think about their feelings, we can solve problems together."

Benny learned that day that understanding means looking at things from someone else's point of view. Sometimes what seems wrong is actually just different. And saying sorry when you make a mistake helps everyone feel better.

From that day on, Benny always tried to understand before getting upset. And he and Rabbit remained the very best of friends.

The End.
