# Lark’s Little Tool Song

In a sunny garage workshop, a small singing bird named Lark fluttered in through the open door. The air smelled like pine wood and warm metal. Sunbeams made tiny dust sparkles float and dance. Shelves held jars of screws that chimed softly when the door moved. Tap, tap, tap went a loose drip on a bucket.

“Hello, tools!” Lark chirped. “Tweet-a-dee, let’s learn today!”

Lark loved to sing, and Lark loved to make things. But sometimes the busy workshop felt big and tricky. On a low table sat a pile of wood scraps, markers, glue, tape, and a little hand sander that hummed when switched on. Lark had a plan.

“I will make a picture board,” Lark said. “I will draw, and I will build. I can do it step by step.”

Lark looked at the scraps. Some were long. Some were short. The short ones wobbled when Lark nudged them. “Hmm,” Lark hummed. “First, I pick a good piece.”

Lark tapped each wood piece with a tiny beak. Tok. Tok. Tok. One piece sounded solid and smooth. “This one!” Lark trilled. Lark dragged it to the table with gentle hops. Scritch, scritch went the wood across the table.

“Now I make it safe,” Lark said. “Safe is smart.” Lark remembered the rule: check, plan, do. Lark touched the wood. It felt a bit rough. “Sand, sand, smooth as sand!” Lark sang, and pressed the little hand sander button. Bzzzzzzz. The sound tickled Lark’s feathers.

Lark moved the sander slowly, back and forth. “Slow is safe, slow is neat,” Lark sang. The wood became smooth like a warm cookie. Lark smiled. “I did it!”

Next, Lark chose bright tape in blue and green. Rip! went the tape. “I will make a frame,” Lark said. Stick, press, pat. The tape made a neat border. Lark’s wings tingled with pride. “I can do hard things, one small step at a time.”

On a nearby peg, a small ruler hung. Lark looked at its tiny lines. “Lines help me plan,” Lark chirped. Lark placed the ruler on the wood and drew light dots with a pencil. Tap-tap went the pencil tip. “Dot, dot, where my art will go.”

Now came the art. Lark opened a box of markers. It smelled like fruit. “Mmm, grape,” Lark giggled. Lark drew a big sun in the corner. Swish, swish. Then Lark drew a tree with strong branches. “This is my practice tree,” Lark said. “I am learning.”

Lark tried a tiny bird on a branch. The first bird looked like a blob. Lark’s chest felt tight. “Oh,” Lark whispered. Lark’s wings drooped.

The drip in the bucket went tap… tap… tap…

Lark took a deep breath. In and out. “I will try again,” Lark said softly. “I can fix it.” Lark remembered the rule: try, check, try again. Lark added two little eyes and a round belly. Swish. Lark traced a smooth curve for the beak. “Tweet!” Lark laughed. “Better!”

But the picture still needed a way to stand. Lark looked around. There was a small triangle of wood, a bit of glue, and a clamp. Lark’s mind buzzed like the sander. “I can make a stand,” Lark sang.

Lark tested the triangle. It slid. “It needs to be even,” Lark said. Lark used the ruler to mark a straight line and sanded the bottom just a little. Bzzzz. Now it sat flat. “Check.”

Lark put a tiny line of glue on the triangle. A sweet, sharp smell puffed up. “Just a dot, not a lot,” Lark sang. Press. Hold. “I will use the clamp.” The clamp looked like a big red beak. Click. It held the pieces together.

Lark waited. Waiting was hard. The garage hummed softly. Sun dust danced. Tap… tap… tap. Lark counted to twenty. “One, two, three…” Lark hummed to pass the time.

Click. Lark opened the clamp. The stand held strong. Lark set the picture board on its stand. It stood tall and proud, like the tree Lark had drawn. Lark’s heart felt warm and big.

“I did it!” Lark sang. “I planned. I practiced. I fixed. I finished!”

Lark hopped back and looked. The blue and green frame shone. The sun smiled. The tree stood strong. The little bird on the branch looked ready to sing. Lark added one more touch: a tiny sticker star in the corner. “For patience,” Lark said.

The garage door creaked. A breeze smelled like grass and soap. Lark bowed to the tools on the wall. “Thank you, ruler. Thank you, sander. Thank you, clamp,” Lark chirped. “Thank you, Lark, for trying.”

Lark picked up the picture board, light and smooth in little claws. “I learned a new skill today,” Lark sang. “Step by step, I can make art. I can help myself.”

Lark placed the picture board on a shelf by the sunny window. The colors glowed. Lark sang a happy song that filled the workshop, bright as the sun.

“Plan, then do.
Try, then see.
Fix it kindly.
Proud of me!”

The End.
