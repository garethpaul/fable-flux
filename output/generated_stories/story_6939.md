# Gigi the Giraffe and the Pantry Parade

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with big, kind eyes and a long, gentle neck. One rainy day, she tiptoed into the pantry. The air smelled like warm bread and sweet cinnamon. Jars glittered like tiny stars. Boxes lined up like little houses. It felt cozy and quiet.

“My tummy is rumbly,” Gigi said softly. “But my heart is thinky.” She tapped her hoof. “I want to make a snack. I want it to be yummy and kind.”

Gigi looked at the shelves. She saw a box of crackers. Crunch! She saw a jar of applesauce. Smoosh! She saw a can of beans. Plop! She saw a bag of oats. Sprinkle! So many choices.

She took a deep breath. The pantry air smelled like peanut butter and oranges. “How can I choose?” she wondered. “I need to use my imagination.”

Gigi closed her eyes. “What if the crackers are little boats?” she whispered. “And the applesauce is a shiny pond?” She smiled. “What if the beans are pebbles on the shore, and the oats are soft sand?”

Just then, she heard tiny footsteps. Tap, tap, tap.

Two little friends peeked in. Tilly the turtle wore a blue bow. Sami the squirrel had a fluffy tail.

“Hi, Gigi!” said Tilly. “We heard your tummy. Boop-boop,” she giggled. “Can we help?”

“Yes, please,” Gigi said. “I want to make a snack. But I want everyone to feel welcome. I want to make good choices.”

Sami sniffed the air. “Mmm! Crackers!” he chirped. “I love crunchy.”

Tilly smiled shyly. “I like soft,” she said. “Crunch is hard for my little mouth today.”

Gigi nodded. “We can include both,” she said. “We can use our imagination and care.”

She lined up crackers on a tray. “These can be boats,” she said.

Sami clapped. “Boat snack! Yay!”

Gigi opened the applesauce. Plop! A golden dollop fell onto a plate. It smelled like sweet sunshine. “This can be the pond,” she said.

Tilly’s eyes sparkled. “I like the soft pond,” she whispered.

Gigi shook the bag of oats. Sprinkle, sprinkle. The oats fell like snow. “This is our sandy beach.”

Sami rolled a bean with his paw. “And these are pebbles! Hello, pebble.” He giggled.

Gigi looked around the pantry again. She saw little carrot sticks in a cup. Crunch-crunch orange! She saw banana slices in a bowl. Soft and squishy yellow. She imagined a bright lighthouse made of carrot sticks and banana rounds so everyone could see the way.

“We have a problem,” Gigi said in a calm voice. “Tilly needs soft food. Sami needs crunchy food. We need a plan to help both.”

She made a chart in her mind. Try. Think. Choose.

“First, we try,” Gigi said. She dipped a cracker boat into applesauce. “Crunchy and soft together. How does that feel?”

Sami took a bite. “Yum! My teeth are happy.”

Tilly tasted applesauce by itself. “Mmm,” she said. “My mouth is comfy.”

“Next, we think,” Gigi said. “What else can we do?” She placed banana slices on top of oats to make tiny sandy islands. She put carrot sticks around the plate like tall trees. She set beans as little stepping stones from island to island.

“It looks like a snack park!” Tilly laughed.

“Finally, we choose,” Gigi said. “We can make two paths. A soft path and a crunchy path. We can share the pond in the middle.”

Gigi made the soft path with banana slices and applesauce. She added a gentle sprinkle of oats for a tiny crunch. She made the crunchy path with crackers and carrot sticks. She added beans for a little bump and fun. In the center, the shiny applesauce pond waited for everyone.

“Everyone is included,” Gigi said. “Our snack has choices.”

Sami danced in place. “I’ll sail on the crunchy path!”

Tilly smiled wide. “I’ll swim in the soft pond!”

Gigi took a small taste from both paths. “I will try a little of everything,” she said. “That helps me learn.”

They ate together. Crunch. Smoosh. Plop. Sprinkle. The sounds made a happy pantry song. The rain pattered on the window like claps.

When they were done, Gigi cleaned the tray. She stacked the crackers. She closed the jars. She wiped the tiny oats like sparkles. The pantry smelled fresh and sweet again.

Tilly patted Gigi’s hoof. “Thank you for choosing with care,” she said.

Sami nodded. “Thank you for using your imagination. You made a playground snack!”

Gigi’s eyes shone. “Imagination helps us make new ideas. Decision-making helps us choose kind ways. Compassion helps everyone feel welcome.”

They hugged. Their snack time felt warm like a blanket.

Gigi looked at the empty plates. “Today I learned to imagine first, think next, and choose with care,” she said. “When we include friends, our snacks—and our hearts—feel full.”

The End.
