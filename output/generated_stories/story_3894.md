# Miko Mouse and the Calm-Down Clock

Miko was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, shiny eyes. Miko lived in a cozy closet hideaway behind a row of shoes and a stack of soft scarves. It smelled like vanilla soap and dusty cardboard. The hideaway had a tiny lamp, a thimble cup, and a button bed that squeaked just a little.

One sunny morning, Miko woke up excited. “Today I will build a toy wheel!” Miko squeaked. “Spin, spin, spin!” Miko had a paperclip, a ribbon, two buttons, and a little spool from sewing thread. Miko’s whiskers twitched with joy.

Miko pushed the paperclip through the spool. The spool wobbled and fell. Clink, clack, plop!

“Oh no,” Miko said. Miko tried again. Clink, clack, plop!

Miko’s tummy felt tight. Miko’s ears got hot. Miko stomped a tiny foot. “I want it to work! Now!”

From the top shelf, the scarves whispered, “Woosh, woosh.” The hangers chimed softly, “Ting, ting.” The sounds made Miko pause. Miko took a breath. In. Out. In. Out.

“I feel mad,” Miko said out loud. “I need a calm plan.”

Just then, Miko’s friend, a gentle sock puppet named Dot, peeked from a shoe. “Hi, Miko,” Dot said in a hushed voice. “I heard clinks and clacks. Are you okay?”

Miko nodded slowly. “I’m trying to make a toy wheel. But it keeps falling. I feel hot and grumpy.”

Dot bobbed. “Feelings are okay. Let’s try a calm-down step. Can you name your feeling?”

“I feel angry,” Miko said. “Angry like a kettle.”

Dot smiled. “Great naming! Now let’s cool the kettle. Try three star breaths with me.”

Miko and Dot opened their paws wide. They breathed in, slow and deep. They breathed out, soft and long. One. Two. Three. The closet air smelled like clean cotton and a hint of cedar. Miko’s shoulders relaxed. The tight tummy loosened.

Miko blinked. “I feel better. The angry bubbles are smaller.”

“Now you can think,” Dot said. “Thinking is easier when we’re calm.”

Miko looked at the toys. “I can be persistent. I can try again in a new way.”

“That’s innovation,” Dot said happily. “New ideas!”

Miko looked around the closet hideaway. A ribbon hung from a knob. A cardboard tube lay by the lamp. A safety pin sparkled. Miko’s whiskers wiggled. “What if the ribbon holds the spool in place?” Miko whispered.

Miko wrapped the ribbon around the spool and tied a knot. The knot slipped. “Oops.” Miko felt a little hot again. Miko placed a paw on the chest and took one slow breath. “I can keep trying.”

Miko tied the ribbon again, this time looping it through the safety pin. The pin clicked. The spool stayed. “Yes!” Miko squeaked.

“Test spin!” Dot cheered. Miko pushed the spool. It rolled, rolled… and tipped over on a scarf. Flop.

Miko’s cheeks puffed. “Grr—” Miko stopped. “I feel frustrated.” Miko tapped the button bed three times. Tap, tap, tap. “That is my pause. Now breathe.” In and out. Calm returned like a soft blanket.

Miko looked at the scarf. “It’s bumpy. I need a smooth path.” Miko spread a shiny magazine page on the floor. It felt cool and slippery. “Try again.”

Roll. Roll. Roll. The spool spun and hummed, “Vrrr.” Miko clapped tiny paws. “It works!”

Dot wiggled. “You did it! You used anger-management. You paused, you breathed, you named your feeling, and you tried a new idea.”

Miko nodded proudly. “I kept going. That is persistence. I made something new. That is innovation!”

They decorated the toy wheel with stickers from an old label sheet. Stars, hearts, and a tiny moon. The wheel sparkled. The lamp glowed warm and golden. The closet felt snug and happy.

Suddenly, the ribbon slipped a little. The wheel slowed. Miko felt a flicker of mad again. Miko whispered, “Hello, feeling.” Miko placed a paw on the heart. “I can be calm.” Three star breaths. The flicker faded.

Miko tightened the knot and added a small piece of tape from a sticky note. “New idea!” The wheel spun even better. Vrrr, vrrr, vrrr!

Dot laughed. “What will you call it?”

Miko grinned. “The Calm-Down Clock! It spins while I breathe. When it stops, I feel ready.”

Miko and Dot took turns. Spin, breathe in. Spin, breathe out. The sounds—vrrr, woosh, ting—made a gentle song. The closet hideaway felt like a tiny workshop of peace.

At bedtime, Miko snuggled into the button bed. “Today I learned something big,” Miko said.

Dot whispered, “What did you learn?”

Miko smiled. “When I feel angry, I can pause. I can name my feeling. I can breathe. Then I can think of new ideas and keep trying. Calm helps me solve problems.”

Dot nodded. “That is a smart mouse lesson.”

Miko yawned. “Tomorrow, we will build a tiny bridge.”

“With calm, persistence, and innovation,” Dot said.

“And kindness,” Miko added, tucking in the soft scarf. The closet smelled sweet and safe. The lamp clicked off.

The Calm-Down Clock made one last soft spin. Vrrr… stop. Miko felt proud, peaceful, and ready for dreams.

The End.
