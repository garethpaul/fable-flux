# Mimi’s Plan in the Home Office

Mimi was a kind little mouse who lived in a cozy hole in a home office. The home office smelled like paper and pencil shavings. The carpet felt soft under Mimi’s tiny paws. The computer hummed like a sleepy bee. The window let in warm, golden light. Mimi loved this place.

“Good morning, desk. Good morning, chair,” Mimi said, stretching her whiskers.

Mimi had a big day. She wanted to help the home office shine. She also wanted time to draw a picture and nibble a tiny cheese snack. Mimi smiled. “I will use a plan,” she said. “A plan is a smart way to do things. A plan is a strategy!”

Mimi took a deep breath. She sniffed the air. Dust swirled in sunbeams. Paper clips were tangled like a little metal snake. Crayons were rolling under the printer. The trash can was full and wobbly.

“It looks busy,” said Mimi. “But I can do it, step by step.”

She drew a simple map on a sticky note with a blue crayon:
1. Pick up crayons.
2. Sort paper clips.
3. Empty trash.
4. Wipe desk.
5. Snack and draw!

Mimi tapped the sticky note. “This is my strategy,” she said proudly. “I will start small and then go big.”

She began with the crayons. “Roll, roll, roll,” she sang as she gathered red, yellow, and sky blue. The crayons felt smooth and smelled a little sweet. “In the cup you go.” She put them in a jar. Click! The crayons clinked together. “Step one, done!” Mimi cheered.

Next were the paper clips. They were stuck in a knot. Mimi frowned. “Hmm, this is tricky,” she said. She wiggled one silver loop. It didn’t move. She tried to pull hard, but the knot got tighter.

Mimi paused. “I will try a new strategy,” she said softly. “I will go slow. I will find the ends.”

She took a belly breath. In… and out… She found one end of the knot and gently shook it. Wiggle, wiggle, wiggle. A clip slipped free. Plink! “Yes!” Mimi squeaked. She kept going, slow and gentle. Soon the clips were free and shiny in a little pile.

“Step two, done!” Mimi clapped her tiny hands. She felt warm and proud inside. “I can do hard things when I use a plan.”

Mimi looked at the trash can. It was tall for a tiny mouse. “This is big,” she said. “Maybe I need help.”

Just then, her friend Pip the ant walked by, carrying a crumb. “Hi, Mimi! What are you doing?” Pip asked.

“I am doing chores,” Mimi said. “I have a strategy. But the trash can is too big for me. Can you help?”

Pip smiled. “I can get friends,” he said. He waved his antennae. Soon, three little ants came marching. Tap, tap, tap went their tiny feet.

“Thank you,” Mimi said. “You are kind. I will share my snack with you later.”

Together, they tipped the trash can carefully. “One, two, three… tip!” said Mimi. Paper rustled. A crumpled paper fell out like a soft mountain. Annie Ant and Leo Ant pushed from one side, while Pip and Mimi pulled from the other. Scritch, scritch, scritch. The trash slid into a bag.

“We did it!” said Pip.

“You used teamwork,” said Annie.

“And a smart plan,” said Mimi. “Step three, done!”

Mimi looked at the desk. It was dusty. She got a soft cloth. “Circle, circle,” she said, wiping gently. The cloth tickled her paws. The desk smelled like lemon. The computer screen shone. The keyboard went click, click when she tapped it lightly.

“Step four, done,” Mimi whispered. She felt calm and strong. She looked at her sticky note. Only one step left.

Mimi set out tiny cheese cubes on a leaf plate. “Friends, please share,” she said. The ants gathered happily. “Thank you for helping me,” Mimi said. “Sharing is kind.”

“You made a good plan,” said Leo Ant. “You are a great helper.”

Mimi blushed under her fur. “I used a strategy,” she said. “I started small and went step by step. When it was hard, I took a breath and tried a new way. When it was big, I asked for help and shared.”

After the snack, Mimi drew a picture. She drew her sticky note plan with numbers and stars. She drew her friends smiling. She drew the clean, shiny desk. She put the picture on the wall with a tiny piece of tape.

The home office felt peaceful. The sun made a warm square on the floor. The air smelled fresh and lemony. Mimi’s whiskers twitched with joy.

Pip said, “What did you learn today, Mimi?”

Mimi smiled. “I learned that a good strategy helps me do my chores. I learned that being generous makes friends. I learned that I can do hard things when I go step by step. That gives me self-confidence.”

Pip nodded. “And now the office is tidy!”

Mimi giggled. “Yes! Tidy and happy.”

She looked at her sticky note again and gave it a gold star. “Plan complete,” she said.

The day felt bright. Mimi curled up in her cozy hole, safe and proud. “Tomorrow,” she whispered, “I will make another plan.”

The End.
