# Pip the Penguin and the Busy Basement Puzzle

Pip was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and tiny waddly feet. One rainy day, Pip slid down the stairs to the busy basement. The basement was full of sounds and smells. Clink clink went the jars of buttons. Snip snip went the scissors on paper. Glue smelled sweet, like warm cookies. The lights hummed softly. It was a perfect place to make a craft.

“I will make a rainbow kite!” Pip said. “I will use red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple.”

Pip opened a big box of colored paper. “Oh!” Pip said. “Some papers are missing.” The red and blue papers were gone. Pip’s beak wiggled. “How can I make a rainbow kite without red and blue?”

Pip took a deep breath. “I will think with my penguin brain. Step by step,” Pip said. “First, I will look. Look carefully.”

Pip looked on the shelf. Pip looked under the table. “I see footprints! Little dusty footprints,” said Pip. They looked like tiny ovals in the dust. Pip followed them.

“Waddle, waddle, waddle,” went Pip. The footprints led to a basket. Inside the basket was a soft scarf and some ribbon. No paper.

“Second, I will ask,” Pip said. Pip waddled to a big box where Bear was stacking blocks. “Bear, have you seen red and blue paper?”

Bear shook his fuzzy head. “No, Pip. But I saw Bunny bounce by with a crinkly pile.”

“Thank you,” said Pip. “Third, I will listen.”

Pip heard a crinkle. Pip heard a tiny hum. “Hmm-hmm, hum-hum,” it went from behind the curtain. Pip pulled the curtain softly. Behind it was Bunny, wearing a paper hat with stars.

“Bunny!” Pip smiled. “That hat is shiny.”

“Thank you,” said Bunny. “I am making a boat. I used red paper. I used blue too. I like the colors.”

Pip’s heart felt tight and loose at the same time. Pip wanted the red and blue. But Pip also saw Bunny’s happy eyes. Pip took another deep breath. “Fourth, I will share and solve,” Pip said.

“Bunny,” Pip asked, “can we share the red and blue? I want to make a rainbow kite. I need those colors. Maybe we can both have enough.”

Bunny looked down at the crinkly pile. “I am sorry, Pip,” Bunny said softly. “I took the paper without asking. I forgot to tell you. I can share. I can say sorry.”

“Thank you for your apology,” Pip said. “Let’s think together. How much do we need?”

They spread the papers on a flat table. The table felt cool and smooth. Pip tapped the paper with a gentle flipper. “I need one small red triangle, and one small blue triangle.”

Bunny tapped the boat plan with a soft paw. “I need one big blue rectangle, and a little red flag.”

Pip smiled. “We can cut from the corners,” Pip said. “See? If I cut small shapes from the edges, you still have big pieces for your boat.”

They tried it. Snip snip went the scissors. Tiny triangles fell like colorful snow. Bunny’s big blue rectangle stayed big. Bunny’s red flag fit on the corner. Pip’s triangle pieces were just right.

“Fifth, we test,” Pip said. Pip glued the triangles to the kite. “Stick, stick,” went the glue. Bunny glued the boat flag. “Tap, tap,” went Bunny’s paw.

Pip tied ribbons to the kite tail. The ribbons were orange and green and gold. They tickled Pip’s flippers. Bunny tied string to the boat. The string felt bumpy and strong.

“Let’s see if they work,” Bunny said.

They made a little breeze with a fan. Whoosh! Pip held the kite up. The kite rustled and danced. Pip laughed. “It works!”

Bunny set the paper boat in a shallow pan of water. The water was cool. The boat bobbed. Plip plop, it went. “It floats!” Bunny cheered.

Bear clapped. “Good thinking, friends!”

Pip nodded. “We used steps,” Pip said. “Look. Ask. Listen. Share and solve. Test.”

Bunny’s ears perked up. “And say sorry,” Bunny added. “I am sorry I took the paper without asking.”

Pip patted Bunny’s paw with a warm flipper. “Thank you, Bunny. I forgive you. Next time, we will ask first. We can make a list, too.”

Pip found a chalk and a small board. Scratch, scratch went the chalk. Pip wrote a simple plan with drawings:

- Eyes look
- Mouth ask
- Ears listen
- Hands share
- Brain think
- Try and test

They drew little pictures next to the words. Pip drew eyes, a mouth, and ears. Bunny drew a heart and a lightbulb. Bear drew a check mark. Everyone giggled.

The basement felt bright and friendly. The glue smell was sweet. The fan was soft. The water in the pan made tiny waves. Pip and Bunny held their crafts proudly.

“We solved the problem,” Pip said. “We shared. We said sorry. We discovered a plan.”

Bunny smiled. “And we made beautiful things!”

Pip lifted the kite again. “When we think step by step, we can fix a tricky day.”

Bear nodded. “Logical thinking helps us all.”

Pip wiggled with joy. “Yes! We can look, ask, listen, share, and test. We can be kind and brave.”

They put the little plan board on the shelf where everyone could see it. Then they cleaned up together. Sweep, sweep. Tap, tap. All neat!

Pip looked around the busy basement. “I am ready for our next craft,” Pip said. “And our next puzzle!”

The End.
