# Bella Bird Finds Her Voice

In a bright, playful living room, sunlight danced on a soft rug. Crayons rolled like tiny logs. Blocks made colorful towers. A bowl of crunchy apple slices sat on the table, smelling sweet and fresh.

Bella Bird perched on the window ledge. She was a small, singing bird with a rainbow of feathers—soft blue, sunny yellow, and a little red tail that wiggled when she felt brave. “Tweet-a-lee! Tweet-a-loo!” she sang, warm and bright like morning.

“Bella, want to make art with me?” asked Max, a cheerful child with curly hair and paint on their cheeks.

“Yes, please!” chirped Bella. Her chest felt fizzy with happy bubbles. She flapped down to the table and looked at the colors. “I want to paint a song.”

Max smiled. “A song painting? Ooh! Let’s do it.”

They dipped brushes into paint. Swoosh went the blue. Dap-dap went the yellow. Bella hummed as she painted loops and lines that danced like music.

“Tweet, tweet,” Bella said softly. “Max, could you pass the small brush? My wings are tiny.”

Max reached for a big brush instead. “This one?”

Bella’s tummy fluttered. The big brush was heavy. It slurped too much paint. She tried it anyway. Splot! A giant blob covered her careful swirls.

Bella blinked. Her feathers felt tight. “I don’t like this brush,” she said, but it came out whisper-small.

Max didn’t hear. The room was busy with sounds: the clock tick-tocked, the fridge hummed, and blocks clacked as they toppled. Bella took a breath in and out, slow and steady, like a calm wave. She remembered what Grandma Bird once told her: “Your voice is your helper. Use it to say what you need.”

Bella stood tall on tiny feet. “Max,” she said clearly, “I need the small brush.”

Max looked up, eyes wide. “Oh! Sorry, Bella. Here you go.” They handed Bella the thin, soft brush.

“Thank you,” Bella chirped. Her feathers smoothed, and her heart felt light. She dipped the small brush into a little puddle of paint and traced gentle lines that made her painting sing.

“Tweet-a-lee,” she practiced, “I can say what I need.”

After art time, Max clapped. “Your painting looks like a song, Bella. I can almost hear it!”

Bella’s tummy rumbled—grrrrumble! She giggled. “My tummy is singing, too.”

Max pointed to the apple slices and a cup of cool water beaded with tiny drops. “Snack break!”

Bella looked at the bowl. She wanted a small piece she could hold. There were big slices and tiny ones. She felt shy again, like a quiet cloud. But she remembered her helper voice.

“Max,” Bella said, “may I have a small apple slice and a little sip of water? My beak is small.”

“Of course,” said Max. They picked the tiniest slice and poured a small sip into a tiny lid-cup. “Healthy snacks help us make great art and sing strong songs.”

Bella crunched the apple. Crunch, crunch! It tasted sweet and cool. The water felt fresh on her tongue. Her whole body hummed with happy energy. “Thank you for listening.”

“You told me what you needed,” Max said. “That helps me help you.”

They built a block stage and put their paintings on it. Bella hopped on top and sang a gentle song. “When I speak up, I feel brave. When I use kind words, I feel safe.”

Just then, Max’s little sibling, Mina, toddled in, giggling and waving a paper airplane. Zoom! It swooped over the block stage. Crash! The blocks tumbled.

Mina gasped. “Oops!”

Bella’s wings fluffed with surprise. Max’s eyes got big. The room felt still, then wobbly. Bella’s heart skipped, but she took that calm wave breath again. In and out.

“It’s okay,” Bella chirped softly. She looked at Mina and used her helper voice. “Mina, I worked hard on that stage. I feel sad when it falls. Can we build it together in a safe spot?”

Mina’s lip wobbled. “Help build?”

“Yes,” Bella said kindly. “Let’s make a stage away from the zooming airplanes.”

Max nodded. “Peaceful plan! We can put the stage by the couch.”

They worked side by side. Mina stacked two blocks. Max added three more. Bella tapped the top with her tiny feet—tap, tap—to check it was steady. Together, they moved their art to the new stage. It looked bright and proud.

“Teamwork,” said Max.

“Peaceful solution,” chirped Bella.

Mina grinned. “I like when you tell me what you need.”

Later, the sun stretched long golden lines across the rug. Bella felt a little tired. Her eyes fluttered. She yawned a small, squeaky yawn. “I need a rest,” she said.

Max patted a pillow near the window. “Rest time is a healthy habit. Your voice told us it’s time.”

Bella nestled into the soft pillow. It smelled like clean cotton and sunshine. She closed her eyes for a minute, then opened them, feeling calm and bright again.

“Today I learned something,” Bella chirped. “When I use my kind, brave voice:
- I can ask for the small brush.
- I can choose a good snack and drink water.
- I can share feelings and make peaceful plans.
- I can rest when my body asks.”

Max smiled. “That’s self-advocacy, Bella. You speak up for yourself in a kind way.”

Bella fluffed her rainbow feathers and sang a gentle, proud song that floated around the playful living room. “Tweet-a-lee, tweet-a-loo, my voice helps me, and helps you too.”

The End.
