# Bubbles the Dolphin Finds a Quiet Bubble

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash, giggle, and make little click-click sounds. One rainy afternoon, Bubbles visited a busy basement. The basement was full of sounds and colors. There were boxes of beads, soft yarn, paper with shiny stars, glue sticks, and crayons. The washing machine hummed. The dryer thumped. A fan whirred. Children laughed and talked. It smelled like fresh soap and warm towels.

“Wow! So many things!” Bubbles squeaked, wiggling her fin. “Let’s make crafts!”

Bubbles scooted across the floor in a little rolling chair. She wanted to make a gift for her friends. She wanted to make a big ocean mobile with blue paper waves, seashell shapes, and a smiling sun.

But the basement was very busy. Little Timmy shook a jar of buttons—rattle-rattle! Maya cut paper—snip-snip! The dryer went thump-thump-thump! Bubbles’ head felt fizzy, like soda bubbles.

“I can do it!” Bubbles said. She grabbed glue. She grabbed beads. She grabbed blue paper. “I will make it fast!”

She tried to stick waves to a string. The waves fell. Plop. She tried to thread beads. The beads rolled. Tink-tink-tink. The rolling chair squeaked. Eeeek!

Bubbles’ heart beat fast. Her fin fluttered. “Oh no,” she whispered. “I can’t think. It’s too noisy. It’s too busy.”

Bubbles took a big breath, but it was a quick, wiggly breath. She felt wobbly and a little sad.

Maya looked up. “Bubbles, are you okay?”

“I want to make my ocean,” Bubbles said, voice small. “But my brain feels splashy. I can’t focus.”

Maya nodded. “It’s busy down here.”

Just then, Grandma Jo came down the stairs with a warm basket of towels. “Hello, makers,” she said with a smile. “I hear a lot of energy.”

Bubbles blinked. “Grandma Jo, how do I feel calm? My thoughts go zoom-zoom.”

Grandma Jo set down the basket. “Let’s try a mindful moment,” she said kindly. “Mindful means we pay soft attention to right now. We listen. We feel. We breathe.”

Bubbles tilted her head. “Mind-ful? Like a full bowl?”

Grandma Jo chuckled. “A gentle, calm bowl. Want to try?”

Bubbles nodded.

“First,” said Grandma Jo, “let’s make a Quiet Bubble. Sit comfy. Feet or fins on the floor. Hands in your lap.”

Bubbles parked the squeaky chair. She put her fin in her lap. Her tail curled close.

“Now,” Grandma Jo said softly, “breathe in like you smell a flower.” Bubbles sniffed. It smelled like warm towels and crayons. “Breathe out like you blow a bubble.” Bubbles blew. Puh—lop. She pictured a shiny bubble around her, soft and safe.

“In,” Grandma Jo whispered. “Flower.” Bubbles breathed in. “Out. Bubble.” Bubbles breathed out.

The room still hummed, but the hum felt softer. Bubbles’ shoulders dropped. Her heart slowed. She noticed the cool paper on her fin. She heard the tiny click of beads. She saw the pretty swirl on Maya’s pink scissors.

“How do you feel?” asked Grandma Jo.

“Calmer,” Bubbles said. “Like floating.”

“Good,” said Grandma Jo. “Mindfulness helps us notice and choose. What do you need now?”

Bubbles looked around. “I need a small space. I need simple steps.”

“That’s good thinking,” Grandma Jo said. “You took initiative. You asked, and now you’re choosing.”

Bubbles smiled. “I can make a ‘Calm Craft Spot’!”

She took a soft towel from the basket and spread it on a little table in a quiet corner by a shelf. She placed only what she needed: three blue papers, one glue stick, five beads, and one string. She put a little sign that said, “Quiet Bubble.”

Timmy walked over. “Can I see?”

“Shh,” Bubbles whispered kindly. “This is a Quiet Bubble. We can share it. We take turns.”

Timmy nodded. “Okay.”

Bubbles sat at the towel table. She took one calm breath in—flower. One breath out—bubble. She touched the smooth paper. She listened to the gentle dryer thump. Thump… pause… thump. She glued one wave. Stick. She waited. She pressed. She smiled.

Maya came with extra shells she had cut. “Would you like to share these?” she asked.

Bubbles’ eyes sparkled. “Yes! Thank you. I will use two, and we can put the rest in the Quiet Bubble basket for friends.”

Together, they made the mobile slowly. Bubbles counted beads softly. “One… two… three… four… five.” She tied the string with careful fins. She laughed a small, happy laugh. “Click-click.”

Timmy waited his turn, then added a yellow sun. “I am sharing,” he said proudly.

Soon, the ocean mobile was done. Blue waves wiggled. Shells shone. Beads twinkled in the basement light. Everyone clapped.

“How did you do it?” asked Timmy.

Bubbles puffed a tiny bubble. “I used mindfulness,” she said. “I made a Quiet Bubble. I breathed in and out. I noticed what I felt. I chose small steps. I asked for help. I shared my space and supplies.”

Grandma Jo nodded. “You solved your problem with calm and care.”

Bubbles hung the mobile where the fan could blow it softly. Whoosh. The waves danced.

Bubbles turned to her friends. “When things feel too busy,” she said, “we can all make a Quiet Bubble. We can breathe. We can choose one thing. We can share and help.”

Maya smiled. “Mindful friends make mindful art.”

Bubbles giggled and tapped her fin. “And mindful hearts feel good.”

The End.
