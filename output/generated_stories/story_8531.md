# Polly's Colorful Gift

High up on a sunny rooftop lived a bright green parrot named Polly. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds in the morning light, and she loved to paint beautiful pictures with her beak and colorful paints.

Every day, Polly would flap her wings and fly around the rooftop, looking for new things to paint. She painted the fluffy white clouds, the golden sun, and the pretty flowers growing in pots around the roof.

"What a beautiful day for painting!" Polly chirped happily, dipping her beak into bright blue paint.

But one morning, Polly noticed something different. Down below in the yard, she saw a little girl sitting alone on a bench. The girl looked very sad, with tears rolling down her cheeks.

"Oh my," said Polly, tilting her colorful head. "That little girl looks so unhappy. I wonder what's wrong?"

Polly flew down closer to listen. She heard the girl sniffling and saying, "I wish I had something pretty to look at. Everything seems so gray and boring today."

Polly's heart felt heavy seeing the sad little girl. She flapped back up to her rooftop and looked at all her beautiful paintings. They were bright and cheerful, full of rainbow colors that always made Polly feel happy.

"I know!" Polly squawked with excitement. "I can share my paintings to make her smile!"

But then Polly felt worried. "These are my very best paintings," she thought. "What if I give them away and don't have any left for myself?"

Polly paced back and forth on the rooftop, her claws making tiny clicking sounds. She looked at her favorite painting of a rainbow with purple butterflies. Then she looked down at the sad little girl again.

"Sharing feels scary," Polly said to herself. "But making someone happy feels even better!"

With a brave flutter of her wings, Polly picked up her most beautiful painting in her beak. She flew down and gently placed it next to the little girl.

"Oh!" gasped the girl, her eyes growing wide and bright. "What a gorgeous painting! Look at all those amazing colors!"

The girl's face lit up with the biggest smile. "Thank you, pretty bird! This makes me feel so much better!"

Polly felt warm and tingly inside, like sunshine was filling her whole body. The happy feeling was so wonderful that she flew back up and brought down another painting, then another!

Soon, the little girl was surrounded by Polly's colorful artwork. Other children came to see the beautiful pictures too, and everyone was laughing and pointing at the bright colors.

"These paintings are magical!" said one boy. "They make everything look happy!"

Polly danced on her little feet, flapping her wings with joy. Even though she had given away her paintings, she felt happier than ever before.

"You know what?" Polly chirped to herself. "When I share my art, it makes everyone happy - including me! And I can always paint more beautiful pictures tomorrow!"

From that day on, Polly painted extra pictures every morning. She kept some for herself and shared others with anyone who needed a little color in their day. The rooftop became a place where happiness grew and spread, just like Polly's generous heart.

And whenever Polly felt the warm sunshine on her feathers, she remembered that sharing her gifts made the whole world brighter.

The End.
