# Benny Bear's Special Gift

Benny Bear was a small, fluffy cub with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved spending time in Grandpa Bear's garage workshop, where the air smelled like wood shavings and the sound of gentle hammering filled the room.

"Grandpa, I want to make something special!" Benny announced, bouncing on his little paws. "My friend Kenji from preschool told me about a beautiful Japanese festival called Cherry Blossom Festival. I want to make him a gift!"

Grandpa Bear smiled warmly. "That's wonderful, Benny! What would you like to make?"

Benny's eyes sparkled. "A wooden cherry blossom tree! But... I don't know how."

"Well," said Grandpa, "the first step is to set a goal. What exactly do you want your tree to look like?"

Benny thought hard, scrunching up his little nose. "Pink flowers, brown trunk, and it should be small enough for Kenji's desk!"

"Perfect!" Grandpa clapped his big paws together. "Now we have a clear goal. Let's make a plan."

They drew a picture together on paper. Benny carefully held the pencil in his small paws, making wobbly lines for the trunk and circles for the blossoms.

The next day, Benny started working. He tried to cut the wood, but it was too hard. *Thunk, thunk, thunk* went his little saw, but nothing happened.

"This is too difficult!" Benny huffed, sitting down with a plop.

"Remember," Grandpa said gently, "big goals take time and practice. Let's try a different tool."

They used a special small saw together. Benny's paws got tired, and sawdust tickled his nose, making him sneeze. "Achoo!"

On the second day, Benny tried to sand the wood smooth. The sandpaper felt rough and scratchy. He rubbed and rubbed until his little arms ached.

"I can't do it right!" Benny cried, throwing down the sandpaper. "It's still bumpy!"

"That's okay," Grandpa said kindly. "Making mistakes helps us learn. Look, you've already made it much smoother than yesterday!"

Benny looked at his work. Grandpa was right! It was getting better.

On the third day, painting time arrived. Benny dipped his brush in pink paint, but *splash!* He accidentally dripped paint on Grandpa's clean workbench.

"Oh no! I'm sorry, Grandpa!" Benny's eyes filled with tears. "I made a mess!"

"It's alright, little one," Grandpa hugged him. "Accidents happen when we're learning. Thank you for saying sorry. That shows you care."

Together, they cleaned up the paint. Then Grandpa showed Benny how to make tiny dots for cherry blossoms using a cotton swab.

"Dab, dab, dab," went Benny, making perfect little pink flowers. The sweet smell of paint filled the air.

Finally, after three days of trying, learning, and not giving up, Benny's wooden cherry blossom tree was finished! It had a smooth brown trunk and delicate pink blossoms that looked like tiny clouds.

"I did it!" Benny danced around the workshop. "I reached my goal!"

At preschool, Benny proudly gave Kenji the special tree. "I learned about your Cherry Blossom Festival and made this for you!"

Kenji's face lit up with joy. "This is beautiful, Benny! Thank you for learning about my culture. This makes me so happy!"

Benny felt warm and proud inside. He had set a goal, worked hard, asked for help when needed, said sorry when he made mistakes, and never gave up.

"Setting goals and working toward them feels wonderful," Benny thought, "especially when it makes friends happy!"

The End.
