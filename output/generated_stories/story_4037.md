# Ruby's Special Song

Ruby was a little red bird who lived in the big oak tree by Mrs. Chen's front porch. Every morning, she watched the other birds sing their beautiful songs. Their voices sounded like tiny silver bells ringing in the breeze.

But Ruby had a problem. When she opened her bright yellow beak to sing, only quiet squeaks came out. Her voice sounded different from all the others.

"Tweet-tweet-tweet!" sang the blue jays loudly.

"Chirp-chirp-chirp!" called the sparrows sweetly.

Ruby tried to join them. "Squeak-squeak-squeak!" she said softly.

The other birds stopped singing. They looked at Ruby with surprised eyes.

"Your voice sounds funny," said a blue jay. "Birds should tweet, not squeak."

Ruby felt her little heart sink down to her tiny toes. Her red feathers drooped sadly. She flew down to the wooden porch railing and tucked her head under her wing.

Mrs. Chen came outside with her morning tea. The warm cup smelled like cinnamon and honey. She sat in her favorite rocking chair and noticed Ruby looking sad.

"What's wrong, little bird?" Mrs. Chen asked gently.

Ruby lifted her head. "I can't sing like the other birds. My voice just squeaks. I don't belong with them."

Mrs. Chen's kind eyes crinkled at the corners. "Let me hear your voice, sweet Ruby."

Ruby felt nervous, but she trusted Mrs. Chen. She took a deep breath and sang her squeaky little song. "Squeak-squeak-squeak-squeak!"

Something magical happened. Mrs. Chen's face lit up with the biggest smile.

"Ruby, that's the most beautiful sound I've ever heard! Your voice reminds me of my grandmother's old rocking chair. It sounds like love and comfort and home."

Ruby blinked her bright black eyes. "Really?"

"Really! Every voice is special and different. That's what makes music so wonderful."

Just then, a little girl came running up the porch steps. She had been crying, and her cheeks were wet with tears.

"Grandma, I fell down and scraped my knee," the little girl sniffled.

Mrs. Chen hugged her granddaughter. "Let Ruby sing you a special song to help you feel better."

Ruby felt scared, but she wanted to help. She opened her beak and sang her gentle squeaky song. "Squeak-squeak-squeak-squeak!"

The little girl stopped crying. Her face brightened like sunshine after rain.

"That's the prettiest, softest song ever! It makes my knee feel better already."

Ruby's heart filled with warm, happy feelings. Her red feathers fluffed up with pride.

From that day on, Ruby sang her squeaky songs every morning on the porch. The other birds learned to love her unique voice too. They discovered that when Ruby's squeaks mixed with their tweets and chirps, they made the most beautiful music together.

"We all sound different," Ruby told the blue jay one sunny morning. "But when we sing together, we make something even more special."

The blue jay nodded. "You're right, Ruby. Your squeaky voice makes our songs complete."

Ruby learned that being different wasn't something to hide. It was something to celebrate. Her special squeaky voice brought comfort to sad hearts and joy to quiet mornings.

Every day, the porch filled with their mixed-up, wonderful music. And Ruby sang her squeaky songs with confidence and pride, knowing that every voice matters and every song has its own special magic.

The End.
