# Pipa the Parrot’s Postcard Party

Morning sunshine slipped through the guest room curtains and made little squares on the rug. The room smelled like clean laundry and lemon. Pipa the Parrot fluffed her rainbow feathers and clicked her beak. “Good morning, guest room!” she chirped. “Today, we make art!”

The guest bed had a cozy blue quilt. A small round table held paper, crayons, glue sticks, and shiny stickers shaped like little globes. A soft fan hummed, “Whirr-rr,” and the window showed a slice of blue sky.

Pipa hopped to the table. “I will make postcards for friends around the world,” she said. “I am a global friend!” She puffed with pride. Her eyes sparkled. “We all share one big world.”

She drew a sunny beach for her friend Niko, who lived near the sea. She drew a tall mountain for her cousin Lila, who lived near snow. She made a postcard with a big orange mango for Rafi, who loved fruit. She stuck tiny globe stickers on each one. “We may be far,” she sang softly, “but we are close in heart.”

Just then, the door creaked. “Creeeak.” In came Mina, a visiting cousin. Mina was five and wore bright socks with yellow stars. She wrinkled her nose. “This is the guest room,” she said. “I want to build my block city on the table.”

Pipa tilted her head. “Blocks are fun,” she said kindly. “I am making postcards to share kindness with friends. Maybe we can share the table?”

Mina crossed her arms. “No. I want the whole table. My city needs space.”

Pipa’s feathers drooped a little. Her tummy felt twisty. She wanted to be brave and kind at the same time. She took a slow breath. In and out. The fan went, “Whirr-rr,” like a gentle helper.

“I feel upset,” Pipa said in a clear, soft voice. “I need some space to finish my postcards. They help me be a good friend to people far away.”

Mina tapped her foot. “But my city will be big.”

Pipa looked at the shiny globe stickers. She had an idea. “We can make room for both,” she said. “We can draw a map on the table with a scarf.” She pulled a long, soft scarf from the chair. “This side is your city. This side is my art. We share the world, and we share the table.”

Mina’s face softened. “Share the world?”

“Yes,” said Pipa. “We live in one world with many homes. You like cities. I like postcards. We both belong.”

Mina peeked at the postcards. “Who are these for?”

“For my friends in different places,” Pipa said. “We send peace and hello. We learn about each other.”

Mina sat on the chair. “Can I make one too?”

Pipa smiled and clicked her beak happily. “Yes! You can make a postcard for someone new.”

They spread the scarf across the table. “Swoosh.” Pipa kept her crayons on her side. Mina stacked her blocks on her side. Click, clack, click. Pipa colored a bright green jungle. She could almost hear little birds sing. “Chirp-chirp!” Mina built a square, then a tower. “Look, a library,” she said.

“What is a library like in your city?” Pipa asked.

Mina thought. “Big and quiet. It smells like old books and soap.”

Pipa nodded. “My friend Niko says his library is near the ocean. It sounds wavy. Whoosh.”

Mina smiled. “Libraries everywhere! That is like sharing.”

They worked peacefully. Sometimes their elbows bumped. When that happened, they said, “Oops!” and scooted back with a giggle. Pipa practiced being assertive. “Please don’t move my crayons,” she said one time, gentle but firm. “I need them here.”

“Okay,” Mina said. “Please don’t bump my blue block.”

“Okay,” Pipa echoed. Their voices were calm and kind.

Soon, the guest room felt full of busy joy. The fan sang. Crayons swished. Blocks clicked. Pipa held up a new postcard. It showed children from many places holding hands around a big blue circle. She added a sticker shaped like the Earth. “This one is for our neighbor Mrs. Park,” Pipa said. “She speaks another language. I can draw hello in pictures.”

“I want to make one for my new friend Amit,” Mina said. “He taught me a song.” Mina drew a drum and tiny music notes. “Ting-ting-ting!”

Pipa clapped her wings. “We are global friends. We listen. We share. We care.”

Just then, a block rolled onto Pipa’s side. “Plonk.” Mina looked worried. “Sorry.”

Pipa smiled. “Thank you for saying sorry.” She rolled the block back. “Here you go.”

At last, the table was full of color and care. Pipa’s postcards made a little rainbow pile. Mina’s city stretched tall and proud. They both breathed out, “Ahhh,” happy and calm.

“Let’s read our messages,” Pipa said. She tapped each card. “Hello, neighbor. Hello, mountain. Hello, sea. We are different. We are friends. We share one Earth.”

Mina added, “And we use kind words when we share a room.”

Pipa nodded. “That is peaceful communication,” she said slowly so the words felt warm. “And saying our needs is being brave.”

They cleaned up together. Crayons clicked into a cup. Blocks slid into a box. The scarf folded soft as a cloud. The guest room smelled like lemon and crayons and a little like dreams.

Pipa tucked the postcards into envelopes. “I feel proud,” she said.

“I feel proud too,” Mina answered. “We made space for each other.”

Pipa fluffed her feathers and gave a happy squawk. “Squawk! Today’s adventure was right here in the guest room. We learned to share our world—with our table, our words, and our hearts.”

The End.
