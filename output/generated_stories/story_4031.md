# Polly's Colorful Mess

In a bright, sunny art room filled with rainbow colors, lived a creative parrot named Polly. Her feathers sparkled green and blue, and she loved to paint more than anything in the whole world.

Every morning, Polly would fly around the art room, her wings flapping excitedly. "Time to paint! Time to paint!" she would squawk happily. The art room had tall windows that let in golden sunlight, shelves full of paint jars, and tables covered with paper and brushes.

But Polly had a big problem. She was so excited to create art that she never put anything back where it belonged. Paint brushes were scattered everywhere like fallen twigs. Red paint dripped on the blue paint. Yellow crayons rolled under the tables. The art room looked like a colorful tornado had spun through it!

One Tuesday morning, Polly wanted to paint a beautiful sunset. "Where are my orange paints?" she chirped, looking around frantically. She couldn't find them anywhere in the messy room.

"Maybe they're under this pile of papers," Polly said to herself, pushing papers around with her bright orange beak. But no orange paint appeared.

Then she heard a gentle voice. "Hello, Polly. You look worried."

Polly turned to see Ms. Robin, the art teacher, walking into the room. Ms. Robin's eyes were kind, and she spoke in a calm, soft voice that made Polly feel better.

"I can't find my orange paint anywhere!" Polly squawked sadly. "I want to paint a sunset, but everything is so mixed up!"

Ms. Robin looked around the messy room and smiled gently. "I can see why you're having trouble. Would you like me to help you learn how to organize your art supplies?"

Polly nodded her colorful head eagerly. "Yes, please! But it looks so hard."

"Let's take it slow and work together," Ms. Robin said calmly. "First, let's take three deep breaths. In... and out. In... and out. In... and out."

Polly breathed deeply, feeling her feathers settle down. The room felt more peaceful already.

"Now," Ms. Robin continued, "let's put all the paintbrushes in this special jar. Can you help me?"

Polly carefully picked up each brush with her beak and placed them in the tall glass jar. Click, click, click went the brushes as they touched the glass.

"Great listening, Polly! Now let's put all the red paints together on this shelf," Ms. Robin suggested.

Working together, they organized all the colors. Red paints went on the red shelf, blue paints on the blue shelf, and yellow paints on the yellow shelf. The room began to smell fresh and clean instead of like mixed-up paint.

"Look!" Ms. Robin pointed to the orange shelf. "There are your orange paints!"

Polly's eyes sparkled with joy. "They were there all along! I just couldn't see them in the mess!"

From that day on, Polly learned to clean up after each painting session. She put each color back in its special place and washed her brushes in the sink. The art room stayed neat and tidy.

"Now I can find everything I need," Polly sang happily as she painted her beautiful sunset. "Orange for the sun, purple for the clouds, and pink for the sky!"

Ms. Robin smiled proudly. "When we organize our things and work together, everything becomes easier and more fun."

Polly learned that being organized helped her create even more beautiful art. And the best part? She never lost her paints again!

The End.
