# Polly's Problem-Solving Day

In a bright, colorful living room filled with soft cushions and cheerful toys, lived a beautiful green parrot named Polly. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds in the morning sunlight that streamed through the big windows.

"Good morning, sunshine!" Polly chirped happily, stretching her bright wings. She loved starting each day with her special routine.

First, Polly always ate her healthy breakfast of fresh fruits and seeds. But today, something was wrong. Her food bowl was empty!

"Oh no!" Polly squawked. "Where is my breakfast? I'm so hungry, and I always eat first thing in the morning!"

Polly felt upset and confused. She flapped around the living room, her colorful tail feathers swishing through the air. The soft carpet felt warm under her tiny feet as she paced back and forth.

"Wait," Polly said to herself, tilting her bright orange head. "Getting upset won't solve my problem. I need to think about this like a detective!"

Polly took a deep breath and began to think step by step. "Let me remember what happened yesterday," she said aloud. "I ate my dinner, then I played with my favorite bell toy, and then..."

Her round, black eyes grew wide. "Oh! I remember now! Yesterday I was so excited to play that I forgot to ask Mom to fill my bowl for this morning!"

Polly felt a little embarrassed. She had been so busy having fun that she forgot about her important morning routine.

"I know what to do," Polly said, puffing up her chest proudly. "I'll fly to the kitchen and politely ask Mom for help!"

Polly spread her beautiful wings and soared across the living room. The air felt cool and fresh as she glided over the soft sofa and past the colorful picture books on the shelf.

"Mom!" Polly called sweetly when she found her human friend in the kitchen. "I made a mistake yesterday. I forgot to remind you about my breakfast bowl. Could you please help me?"

Mom smiled warmly. "Of course, Polly! Thank you for thinking through the problem and asking so nicely. I'm proud of you for figuring that out!"

As Mom filled Polly's bowl with delicious seeds and juicy apple pieces, Polly felt happy and proud. The sweet smell of fresh fruit made her tummy rumble with excitement.

"You know what, Polly?" Mom said, gently stroking her soft feathers. "Everyone forgets things sometimes. What matters is that you stayed calm, thought about the problem, and found a good solution."

Polly munched happily on her breakfast, savoring each crunchy seed and sweet apple bite. "From now on," she chirped between bites, "I'll remember to check my bowl every evening as part of my bedtime routine!"

After breakfast, Polly felt energized and ready for a wonderful day of playing in her cozy living room. She had learned that when problems happen, the best thing to do is stop, think carefully, and work through them step by step.

"Thinking problems through is like solving a fun puzzle!" Polly sang happily, doing a little dance on her favorite perch. "And it feels so good to take care of myself and keep my healthy habits!"

The End.
