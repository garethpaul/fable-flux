# Rocco's Bubbly Bath Song

Rocco the raccoon loved making music. He had tiny drums, little bells, and a special whistle that sounded like birds singing. Every morning, Rocco would play his instruments and dance around his cozy tree house.

But today was different. Today, Mama Raccoon said it was bath time.

"Rocco, sweetie, you need a bath before we visit Grandma," Mama called from the bathroom. "The warm water is ready!"

Rocco's fuzzy ears drooped down. "But Mama, I want to play my drums! Bath time takes too long!"

He stomped his little paws on the wooden floor. *Thump, thump, thump!* The sound echoed through the house, but it didn't make him feel better. In fact, Rocco felt even more frustrated.

"I don't WANT a bath!" Rocco shouted, crossing his arms. His fluffy tail twitched with anger.

Mama Raccoon came over and knelt down beside him. "I can see you're feeling upset, little one. When we feel frustrated, we can take three deep breaths together. Want to try?"

Rocco sniffled. "Okay, Mama."

Together, they breathed in slowly through their noses. *Whoooosh.* Then they breathed out through their mouths. *Ahhhhhh.* They did this three times, and Rocco's shoulders began to relax.

"Better?" Mama asked gently.

Rocco nodded. "A little bit. But I still don't want to take a long bath."

"What if we make bath time musical?" Mama suggested with a twinkle in her eye.

Rocco's ears perked up. "Musical? How?"

"Well," Mama said, "you could bring your waterproof shaker and make music with the bubbles!"

Rocco's eyes grew wide with excitement. This sounded like an adventure! He grabbed his favorite shaker – the one that sounded like gentle rain – and followed Mama to the bathroom.

The bathroom was warm and steamy. Fluffy white bubbles floated on top of the water like tiny clouds. The sweet smell of lavender soap filled the air.

"Look, Rocco!" Mama pointed to the bubbles. "You can make bubble drums!"

Rocco carefully stepped into the warm water. It felt nice on his paws. He began to pat the bubbles gently. *Pop, pop, pop!* Each bubble made a tiny sound.

"And listen to this," Mama said, pouring water from a cup. *Splash, tinkle, splash!* "Water music!"

Soon, Rocco was having so much fun! He shook his rain shaker: *shake-a-shake-a-shake!* He popped bubbles with his paws: *pop-pop-pop!* He even discovered that splashing made different sounds – gentle splashes sounded like whispers, and bigger splashes sounded like drums.

"Mama, I'm sorry I got upset earlier," Rocco said as he played his bubble symphony. "Thank you for helping me feel better and making bath time fun."

"That's okay, sweetie," Mama smiled. "Everyone feels frustrated sometimes. The important thing is learning how to handle those big feelings."

As Rocco washed behind his ears, he created a special bath-time song. The bubbles popped in rhythm, the water splashed like cymbals, and his shaker kept the beat.

When bath time was over, Rocco felt clean, happy, and proud. He had learned that taking deep breaths helped when he felt frustrated, and that using his imagination could make even ordinary things like baths into musical adventures.

"Can we have musical bath time again tomorrow?" Rocco asked as Mama wrapped him in a fluffy towel.

"Of course!" Mama laughed. "But now, let's go visit Grandma. I bet she'd love to hear about your bubble symphony!"

The End.
