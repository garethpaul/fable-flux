# Rocco Raccoon’s Snack-Time Song

Rocco the musical raccoon loved two things: making music and munching crunchy snacks. He lived in a playful living room with soft pillows, a fluffy rug, and a bright window that let in warm sunshine. Toys were neatly stacked in baskets, and a little drum, a tiny xylophone, and a shiny harmonica rested on a low shelf. The air smelled like fresh apples and a little like peanut butter. It was snack time, and Rocco’s tummy made a friendly rumble. “Rumble, rumble,” he giggled. “That sounds like a drum!”

Rocco clapped his fuzzy paws and sang, “Snack-time, snack-time, share and care in snack-time!” His striped tail swished in happy swoops. On the coffee table sat a colorful plate with apple slices, carrot sticks, and cheese cubes. Next to it was a small bowl of crunchy crackers.

“Hello, healthy snacks,” Rocco said. “You look crunchy, you look fresh, you look yum-yum!” He picked up his harmonica and blew a cheerful toot-toot. The sound was bright and shiny, like a smile.

Just then, Rocco’s friends hopped in. Benny Bear, soft and round, waddled to the rug. Mimi Mouse, tiny and quick, wiggled her whiskers. Tilly Tortoise, slow and steady, smiled from under her smooth shell. They all sniffed the yummy air.

“Hi, Rocco!” said Benny. “I smell apples!”

“I hear music,” said Mimi, her ears twitching.

“I feel a comfy rug,” said Tilly, settling in. “Is it snack time?”

Rocco felt proud of his snack plate. “Yes! Look at all the crunchy, colorful bites. Apples help us grow strong. Carrots help our eyes see. Cheese helps our bones. Crackers are crunchy fun.” He puffed his chest and hugged the harmonica. “I made a snack-time song, too!”

Benny’s round belly made a soft growl. “I’m very hungry,” he said. He reached a big paw toward the crackers.

Mimi squeaked, “I want a cheese cube!”

Tilly blinked slowly. “I would like an apple slice, please.”

Rocco looked at the snacks. He wanted the crackers, the apples, the carrots, and the cheese all for himself. He thought, I brought the plate. Maybe I should eat first. His paws closed around the bowl of crackers, and he scooped up the apple plate, too.

Benny’s smile faded. Mimi’s whiskers drooped. Tilly tucked her chin inside her shell. The room felt less sunny.

Rocco’s ears perked. The quiet felt heavy. His harmonica felt cold in his paw. He remembered a gentle song his mama raccoon had taught him: “Sharing is caring, sharing is fair. When we share, we show we care.”

Rocco took a deep breath. He listened to the rumbly bellies and the soft, hopeful sighs. He noticed his own tummy was still rumbling, but he also felt a little pinch in his heart. It didn’t feel good to hold everything. It felt lonely.

He set down the plates and spoke softly. “Friends, I forgot something. I forgot to share. That wasn’t fair.” He looked at Benny, Mimi, and Tilly. “I’m sorry.”

Benny’s eyes brightened. Mimi’s whiskers lifted. Tilly peeked out more.

Rocco smiled and tapped a gentle beat on his little drum: tap-tap, tap-tap. “Let’s make a sharing plan,” he said. “We can take turns. We can make it fair.”

“How?” asked Mimi, tipping her head.

Rocco pointed to the snacks. “We can make little snack boats!” He found small napkins and folded them like tiny pockets. “We will each get a mix: one apple slice, one carrot stick, one cheese cube, and two crackers.” He counted carefully, tapping his drum with each count. “One for you, one for you, one for you, and one for me.”

Benny clapped. “That sounds fair!”

Tilly nodded. “Fair and friendly.”

Mimi squeaked happily. “I love snack boats!”

They made four little snack boats. The apples were cool and juicy. The carrots were crisp and snappy. The cheese was soft and creamy. The crackers were crunchy and salty. The friends could hear the crunch-crunch, smell the apple-sweet, and feel the cozy rug under their toes.

Before they ate, Rocco lifted his harmonica. “Let’s sing our sharing song.” He played a bright tune: toot-toot, tralala. The friends sang:

“Share a snack, share a smile,
Sharing makes it all worthwhile.
One for you and one for me,
Sharing tastes like harmony!”

They laughed and munched. Benny said, “Thank you for the carrot. It helps my eyes!”

Mimi said, “I like the cheese. It helps my bones!”

Tilly said, “Apple is sweet and juicy. It makes me feel fresh!”

Rocco said, “Crackers go crunch like my drum!” He tapped tap-tap and smiled. His tummy felt happy, and his heart felt even happier.

When the snack boats were empty, there were two apple slices left. Rocco looked around. “What should we do with the extra?”

“Let’s split them,” said Tilly. “We can be fair again.”

Rocco used a safe, small plastic knife to cut each slice in half. Everyone got a tiny extra bite. “Fair is when everyone gets a turn,” Rocco said. “Sharing shows we care.”

After snack time, they cleaned up together. Benny carried the plates. Mimi folded the napkins. Tilly pushed the basket of toys closer with her steady shell. Rocco played a gentle cleanup tune on his harmonica. The living room felt bright again—full of music, full of friends.

Rocco curled his tail and sighed a happy sigh. “Today I learned that sharing makes snack time better. It makes music sweeter and friends closer.”

Benny, Mimi, and Tilly nodded. “Sharing is caring,” they said together.

Rocco tapped his drum one last time. “Fair snacks for everyone!”

The End.
