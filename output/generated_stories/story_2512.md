# Daisy's Thank You Day

Little Daisy the deer fawn loved visiting Grandma Rabbit's warm kitchen. The cozy room always smelled like fresh cookies and warm milk. Sunlight streamed through the yellow curtains, making everything glow like gold.

"Good morning, Daisy!" called Grandma Rabbit, stirring something yummy in a big pot. "Would you like some breakfast?"

Daisy's tummy rumbled. "Yes, please!" she said, hopping onto her special chair.

Grandma Rabbit served Daisy a bowl of sweet oatmeal with berries and a tall glass of cold milk. Daisy gobbled it up quickly. "More, please!" she said, pushing her empty bowl forward.

"Of course, dear," smiled Grandma Rabbit, refilling the bowl.

When Daisy finished her second helping, she jumped down and started to run outside to play.

"Wait, Daisy," said Grandma Rabbit gently. "What do we say when someone gives us something nice?"

Daisy stopped. She had forgotten something important! "Oh! Thank you, Grandma Rabbit!"

"You're very welcome, sweetheart. But there's something else we should do too."

Daisy looked puzzled. "What else?"

"Well," said Grandma Rabbit, "when someone cooks for us and serves us food, it's fair to help clean up. And we should always wash our hands after eating to stay healthy and clean."

Daisy felt a warm feeling in her heart. Grandma Rabbit had been so kind to her, making breakfast and serving it with love. It wasn't fair for Grandma to do all the work alone.

"You're right!" said Daisy. "I want to help!"

First, Daisy carefully carried her bowl and cup to the sink. The dishes clinked softly as she set them down. Then she washed her hooves with warm, soapy water that smelled like lavender.

"What else can I do?" asked Daisy.

"You could help me wipe the table," suggested Grandma Rabbit.

Daisy took a soft cloth and wiped the wooden table until it sparkled. She swept up the tiny crumbs that had fallen on the floor. The kitchen looked neat and tidy.

"Thank you so much for helping, Daisy," said Grandma Rabbit, giving her a gentle hug. "You've been very thoughtful and fair."

Daisy felt proud and happy. "It feels good to help! You cooked for me, so I wanted to help you too."

"That's what gratitude looks like," explained Grandma Rabbit. "When we're thankful, we show it with our words AND our actions."

Just then, Daisy's friend Benny Bunny knocked on the door. "Can Daisy come play?" he asked.

"Of course!" said Grandma Rabbit. "Daisy has been such a wonderful helper this morning."

As they walked outside, Daisy told Benny about her morning. "I learned that saying thank you is just the beginning. Helping and being fair shows we're really grateful!"

"That's smart!" said Benny. "My mom always says actions speak louder than words."

Daisy smiled, feeling the warm sunshine on her soft fur. She had learned that being grateful meant more than just saying "thank you." It meant being fair, helpful, and kind. It meant thinking about others and showing appreciation through actions.

From that day on, whenever someone was kind to Daisy, she always remembered to say thank you AND find a way to help. Her heart felt full of joy, and everyone loved spending time with such a thoughtful little fawn.

The End.
