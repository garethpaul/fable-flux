# Jemma Giraffe’s Tidy Task

Jemma was a thoughtful giraffe with gentle eyes and a long, long neck. She lived in a sunny house with a cozy home office. The office smelled like fresh paper and warm tea. The computer hummed softly, “hmmm,” and the little fan went, “whirr, whirr.” Jemma liked the soft sounds. They made her feel calm.

But today, Jemma’s home office felt too messy. Papers were in piles like tiny mountains. Crayons rolled on the floor. Sticky notes stuck to her chair, her desk, and even her knee.

“Oh dear,” said Jemma. “I can’t think when it’s this messy. I need to do my chores.”

Jemma made a plan. “First, I will sort the papers. Then, I will put away the crayons. Last, I will dust and sweep.” She smiled. “I can do it, one small step at a time.”

Just then, her friend Pip the mouse popped his head in the doorway. “Squeak! Hi, Jemma! Can I help?”

Jemma nodded. “Yes, please. Thank you, Pip. Let’s work together.”

Pip sniffed the air. “Mmm, your tea smells like honey.”

“It does,” said Jemma. “After our chores, we can have a cup.”

Jemma picked up a stack of papers with her careful mouth. She read the first page. It was a drawing from her friend, Dottie the Zebra. It showed stripes and stars and a funny hat. Jemma smiled. “This one is special. It goes in the ‘Keep’ pile.”

Pip tugged on a sticky note. “This says: ‘Call Grandma.’”

Jemma’s cheeks felt warm. “Oh! I forgot to call. Thank you, Pip. I will call Grandma after we tidy.”

They made three piles on the desk: Keep, Recycle, and Share. Jemma liked this simple plan. “Organization helps me think,” she said softly. “One pile at a time.”

As they worked, the computer hummed, “hmmm,” and the window let in warm sun. Dust sparkles floated like tiny stars. Jemma felt proud. She was doing her chores. It felt good to be helpful and neat.

Then they heard a soft knock. “Tap, tap.” It was Benny the Beetle, wearing a bright green backpack.

“Hi,” buzzed Benny. “I brought your pencils back. Thank you for lending them.”

Jemma smiled. “You’re welcome, Benny. Would you like to help us put them away?”

Benny looked down. “I’m not very neat. I am slow. I make little messes.”

Jemma bent her long neck and spoke gently. “That’s okay, Benny. We all do things in our own way. We accept each other. We can learn together.”

Pip squeaked, “We have a place for everything! See the cups for pens? See the box for crayons?”

Benny’s eyes lit up. “I can do that.” He rolled the pencils into the cup, one… two… three. “Click, click, click.” He smiled. “I like the clicks!”

They all laughed. Jemma said, “Thank you, Benny. Your clicks are perfect.”

Next, Jemma dusted the desk. “Swipe, swipe.” Pip swept the floor. “Swish, swish.” Benny wiped the window ledge with a tiny cloth. “Wipe, wipe.” The room began to shine. The air felt light. The sun felt warmer. Jemma’s heart felt calm.

Then Benny tripped on a crayon and bumped the Keep pile. “Oh no!” Papers fluttered like butterflies. “I’m sorry,” whispered Benny. His feelers drooped.

Jemma took a deep breath. She looked at Benny with kind eyes. “It’s okay, Benny. Accidents happen. We accept mistakes. We can fix this together.”

Pip nodded. “We’re a team!”

Jemma showed Benny how to pick up papers by the corners. Pip read the labels. “Keep. Recycle. Share.” Benny put each page in the right pile. “Click, click.” Soon, the desk was neat again.

Benny’s feelers lifted. “Thank you for being kind.”

Jemma smiled. “Thank you for helping. You are part of our tidy team.”

They finished the chores. Jemma lined up her notebooks like tall, thin boxes. Pip stacked the sticky notes in a tiny tower. Benny put the crayons in a rainbow row: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple. The colors looked bright and happy.

Jemma poured warm tea. The sweet honey smell filled the room. She gave Pip a tiny cup and Benny a tiny drop on a leaf. “To teamwork,” she said. “To chores done well. To acceptance.”

“To acceptance!” Pip squeaked.

“To acceptance,” buzzed Benny.

They sat together and listened to the quiet “hmmm” of the computer and the soft “whirr” of the fan. Jemma felt peaceful in her clean, tidy office. She could think clearly now. She remembered the sticky note. “Oh! I must call Grandma.”

Jemma picked up the phone. “Hi, Grandma! I did my chores today. I organized my office with my friends. We made piles and put things away. We helped each other. And Grandma, we accepted mistakes. We accepted each other.”

Grandma’s voice was warm. “I am proud of you, dear Jemma. A clean space helps a clear mind. Kindness makes strong hearts.”

Jemma’s eyes shone. “Yes, Grandma.”

After the call, Jemma looked at her friends. “Today I learned that acceptance means being kind to myself and to others, even when things are slow or messy. Organization helps us finish our chores, step by step. When we work together and accept each other, we can do big things.”

Pip clapped. Benny buzzed happily. The office glowed in the soft afternoon sun.

Jemma took a deep breath. The room smelled like honey and clean paper. Her heart felt light.

“We did it,” she said.

“We did it,” her friends echoed.

The End.
