# Melody Finds Her Voice

In a quiet attic, where the air smelled like old wood and warm dust, a small singing bird named Melody woke up. Sunlight slipped through a tiny window and made soft golden shapes on the floor. The attic was peaceful. It held boxes, books, a big trunk, and a cozy nest made of yarn and straw.

Melody stretched her wings. “Good morning, me,” she chirped. She took a deep breath. The air felt cool on her feathers. “Today I will practice my new song, and I will clean my nest,” she said. “I can do hard things, one small step at a time.”

She hopped to her water dish and took a sip. “Sip, sip,” she said. Then she nibbled a few seeds. “Crunch, crunch.” Healthy habits help me grow, she thought. Now I am ready.

Melody began to sing. “La-la-la, tweet-tweet!” Her song was soft at first, like a whisper. “I will try again,” she told herself. “I can be brave. I can practice.” Her voice grew brighter. “La-la, tweet-tee-tweet!”

Just then, the attic door creaked. “Creeeeak.” A little mouse named Pip peeked in. “Hi, Melody!” Pip squeaked. “I found a shiny bell. Want to see?” He ran across the floor. The bell jingled. “Jingle-jingle!”

Melody smiled. “Hi, Pip! That sounds fun, but I am practicing my song right now. I want to finish one thing before I start another.” She fluffed her feathers and stood tall. “I also plan to clean my nest after I sing. Can we play later?”

Pip blinked. “Oh! I did not know you had a plan. That’s okay,” he said. “You can tell me what you need.”

Melody nodded. “Thank you for listening. I need quiet for a little while.” She took another deep breath. “Let’s make a plan. I will sing three times, then I will clean for five minutes. After that, we can play with your bell. Does that work?”

Pip’s whiskers twitched. “Yes! I can wait and collect tiny buttons while you work.” He scampered away, picking up a button, then another. “Clink, clink.”

Melody felt proud. I used my voice to say what I need, she thought. That is self-advocacy. She sang her song again. “La-la-la, tweet-tweet!” She tried a tricky part. It was wobbly. “Hmm,” she said. “This part is hard. But I can learn. I will try a slow way.”

She hummed slowly. “La… la… la.” She clapped gently with her wings. “Clap, clap.” Then she tried again. “La-la-la, tweet!” The note was clearer this time. Melody smiled. “I can improve with practice.”

When she finished three songs, she started to tidy her nest. “One feather, two feathers,” she counted. She shook out her soft yarn. She found a tiny leaf. “Oh! Hello, leaf,” she giggled, and placed it by the window. She wiped a little dust from her seed bowl with a cloth. “Wipe, wipe.”

Suddenly, the attic squeaked. A big, kind spider named Nettie lowered herself on a silky thread. “Good day,” Nettie said in a gentle voice. “I am making a new web in the corner. It will catch the flies so they don’t bother you while you sing.”

Melody chirped, “Thank you, Nettie. That is very helpful.” Then she tilted her head. The web shimmered near her nest. “Nettie, may I share a need?” she asked. “Your web is lovely, but it tickles my wings when I fly. Could you move it a little?”

Nettie paused. “Thank you for telling me kindly,” she said. “I can move it to the window corner.” She scooted along her thread, and the web glowed in the sunlight like a tiny rainbow. “How is that?”

Melody fluttered. “Perfect. I feel safe and comfy.” She felt warm inside. I used peaceful words, and we found a good solution.

At last, Melody finished her cleaning. Her nest looked neat and fresh. She drank some water. “Sip.” She washed her beak. “Wipe.” She took a few deep breaths in and out. “In… out…” Calm and ready.

Pip bounced back with his bell. “Jingle!” he said. “Did you finish?”

“I did,” Melody chirped. “I practiced my song and cleaned my nest. Thank you for waiting. Now I am ready to play.”

They rolled the bell gently across the floor. “Jingle-jingle, roll!” Pip laughed. Melody laughed too. They made a new game: roll, stop, sing! Melody sang a happy tune. Pip clapped his tiny paws. Nettie swayed in her web and hummed along.

When the sun moved lower and the attic grew rosy and soft, Melody tucked into her nest. She felt proud and peaceful. “Today I used my voice. I asked for quiet. I shared my needs. I finished my jobs. I kept trying when it was hard,” she said.

Pip waved good night. “You showed me how to ask kindly,” he squeaked. “Next time, I will say what I need too.”

Nettie whispered, “Your gentle words make our attic a calm place.”

Melody closed her eyes. “I can speak up. I can listen. I can learn, little by little.” The attic sighed with evening hush. The bell gave one last tiny sound. “Jing.”

And Melody drifted to sleep, proud of her brave, kind voice.

The End.
