# Gigi Speaks Up

The sun was warm and bright in the sunny backyard. The grass felt tickly under Gigi the giraffe’s hooves. She was a thoughtful giraffe with kind eyes and a long, gentle neck. She could smell sweet flowers and hear bees buzzing like tiny songs. Today, Gigi wanted to play with her friends.

In one corner, there was a little sandbox with shiny, clean sand. A bright red bucket and a blue shovel sat on top. Gigi’s friends were already there—Max the monkey, Bella the bunny, and Tiko the turtle.

“Let’s build a sand cake!” Max chattered, wiggling his tail.

“I want to make a tall tower,” said Bella, her ears twitching happily.

Tiko blinked slowly. “Maybe a smooth road,” he said in his quiet voice.

Gigi took a breath. She had a thoughtful plan in her mind. “I want to make a sand garden,” she whispered, almost too soft to hear. She pictured tiny sand paths and little leaf flags.

Max grabbed the red bucket. “My turn!” he shouted.

Bella snatched the blue shovel. “Me first!”

Tiko nudged a small shell into the sand. “I can make lines,” he murmured.

Gigi’s heart felt small. She wanted to speak, but her words felt stuck like a bubble that wouldn’t pop. She watched Max and Bella scoop, scoop, scoop. Sand swished. The bucket thumped. The shovel scraped. Gigi’s tail swished. She waited. She thought.

Gigi tried a tiny “Um…” but the buzzing bees were louder than her voice.

Max made a mound. Bella patted it flat. Tiko smoothed the sides. “This will be the tallest tower!” Max cheered.

Gigi saw that the tower kept tilting. It wobbled, wiggled, and almost fell. She knew why. The sand was too dry at the bottom.

Her thoughtful mind made a plan. But she still needed to use her voice.

Gigi closed her eyes. She felt the warm sun on her face. She felt the soft breeze on her ears. She put her hooves down strong and steady. Then she lifted her head high.

“I have something to say,” Gigi said in a clear, calm voice.

Everyone stopped. Max froze. Bella held her breath. Tiko looked up.

Gigi took another breath. “I want to play too. I would like a turn with the bucket and the shovel. And I have an idea. If we make the sand a little wet, the tower won’t fall. We can share the tools and take turns. We can make a tower, a road around it, and a garden on the sides.”

Max blinked. “You do?” His ears perked up.

Bella hugged the shovel to her chest. “A garden and a tower?”

Tiko smiled slowly. “And a road,” he said.

Gigi nodded. “Yes. We can do it together. I would like the bucket after Max. Bella can have the shovel after me. Tiko can use the shell to make the road while we build.”

Max scratched his head. “That sounds fair,” he said.

Bella’s nose wiggled. “I like turns,” she said softly. “And I like gardens.”

Tiko tapped the shell. “I agree,” he whispered.

Gigi felt warm and brave inside. “Thank you,” she said. “Max, may I please have the bucket when you fill it once?”

Max filled the bucket and handed it over. “Your turn, Gigi.”

“Thank you,” Gigi said. She walked to the small water dish by the hose. Splash, splash. She poured a little water on the sand. The sand felt cool and squishy under her hoof. She mixed it with the bucket. Swish, swish. “Now try,” she said.

Bella pressed the shovel into the damp sand. “It sticks!” she giggled.

Max patted the mound. “It’s standing!”

Tiko drew a curvy road around the base. “The cars can go here,” he said, making tiny tire marks with his shell.

Gigi used leaves to make flags and placed them gently. “Garden time,” she said, smiling. She traced a quiet path, and Bella sprinkled small pebbles like flowers.

The tower stood tall. The road was smooth. The garden looked pretty. Bees buzzed close, then zipped away. A butterfly floated by, landing on a leaf flag. Everyone clapped. Clap, clap, clap.

“I like your idea, Gigi,” Max said.

“And I like how you asked,” Bella added. “You were clear and kind.”

Tiko nodded. “You helped us work together.”

Gigi felt proud. “I used my strong voice,” she said. “I asked for a turn. I shared my idea. We shared the tools. We did it as a team.”

They kept playing, taking turns with the red bucket and blue shovel. When someone wanted a turn, they used their clear words.

“May I have a turn next?” Bella would ask.

“Yes, after me,” Max would say.

“Thank you,” Bella would answer.

The sun began to slide lower, making long, soft shadows. The sand felt cool. The air smelled like grass and sweet flowers. Gigi looked at the tall tower, the road, and the garden they made together.

“I learned something today,” Gigi said.

Max tilted his head. “What did you learn?”

Gigi smiled. “When I speak up with kind, strong words, I can share my ideas. We can take turns. We can work as a team. That is being brave and fair.”

Bella nodded. “Assertive,” she said carefully. “That means kind and strong.”

Tiko tapped the road. “Teamwork,” he added. “Sharing, too.”

Gigi’s heart felt big and bright, just like the sunny backyard. “Yes,” she said. “We did it together.”

The End.
