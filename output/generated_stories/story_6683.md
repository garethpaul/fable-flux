# Ellie's Secret Listening Place

Little Ellie the elephant calf had big, floppy ears and an even bigger problem. She loved to play with her friends, but sometimes she felt too shy to speak up when they played games.

One sunny morning, Ellie discovered something special behind the coats in her family's closet. It was a cozy little space with soft pillows and warm blankets. The closet smelled like lavender and felt safe and quiet.

"This is perfect!" whispered Ellie to herself. "My very own hideaway!"

Every day after playing with her friends, Ellie would curl up in her secret closet space. She would think about all the times she wanted to say something but didn't. Like when her friend Benny the bunny chose a game she didn't understand. Or when Rosie the raccoon spoke so fast that Ellie couldn't follow along.

One afternoon, Ellie heard her friends calling outside. "Ellie! Come play with us!"

But instead of going outside, Ellie tiptoed to her closet hideaway. She pulled the soft blue blanket over her head and sighed.

"I wish I was brave enough to tell them how I feel," she whispered.

Just then, Ellie heard gentle footsteps. Her mama's trunk appeared through the closet door.

"Hello, sweet Ellie," Mama said softly. "I noticed you've been spending lots of time in here. Would you like to talk about it?"

Ellie peeked out from under her blanket. "Mama, sometimes when I play with my friends, I don't understand the games. But I'm too scared to ask questions."

Mama elephant sat down beside Ellie in the cozy space. "Oh, my dear. Do you know what the most important thing is when playing with friends?"

"Having fun?" Ellie guessed.

"That's part of it," Mama smiled. "But the most important thing is listening to each other. And that means your friends need to listen to YOU too."

"But what if they think my questions are silly?" Ellie asked, twisting her trunk nervously.

"Good friends never think questions are silly," Mama explained. "Questions help everyone understand better. Would you like to practice with me?"

Ellie nodded eagerly. Mama pretended to be Benny the bunny.

"Let's play hopscotch!" Mama said in a squeaky voice.

Ellie took a deep breath. "Benny, I don't know how to play hopscotch. Could you please show me?"

"Of course!" Mama-as-Benny replied. "I'd love to teach you!"

They practiced different ways Ellie could speak up: "Could you explain that again?" and "I have a different idea!" and "That sounds fun, but could we try this too?"

The next day, when Benny suggested a new game, Ellie remembered her practice. Her heart beat fast, but she spoke up clearly.

"Benny, that sounds exciting! Could you teach me the rules first?"

Benny's face lit up. "Sure, Ellie! I love explaining games!"

When Rosie spoke too quickly, Ellie gently said, "Rosie, could you slow down a little? I want to understand everything you're saying."

"Oh, of course!" Rosie smiled. "Thanks for telling me!"

That evening, Ellie visited her closet hideaway one more time. But now it felt different. Instead of hiding from her problems, she used it as a quiet place to think about all the wonderful things that happened when she listened to her friends—and when they listened to her too.

"Listening works both ways," Ellie said happily. "When we all listen to each other, everyone has more fun!"

From that day on, Ellie's closet became her thinking space, where she planned new games to suggest and practiced being a good listener and a brave speaker.

The End.
