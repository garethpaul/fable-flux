# Nutkin's Clean Kitchen Adventure

Nutkin the squirrel lived in a cozy tree house, but today he was visiting his friend Mrs. Robin's warm kitchen. The kitchen smelled like fresh bread and cinnamon, and sunshine streamed through the yellow curtains.

"Oh my!" gasped Nutkin as he looked around. The kitchen was very messy! Dirty dishes were piled high in the sink, crumbs covered the counter, and sticky spots dotted the floor. Mrs. Robin sat at the table looking very sad.

"What's wrong, Mrs. Robin?" asked Nutkin, his fluffy tail twitching with concern.

"I hurt my wing yesterday," Mrs. Robin chirped softly. "I can't clean my kitchen, and now it's so dirty that I'm afraid to cook. What if germs make me sick?"

Nutkin's bright black eyes sparkled with an idea. "Don't worry! I'll help you clean everything up. But first, we need to wash our paws!"

"Wash our paws?" asked Mrs. Robin, tilting her head.

"Yes!" said Nutkin bravely. "My mama always says that clean paws keep germs away. When we wash with soap and warm water, we get rid of the invisible germs that could make us sick."

Nutkin scampered to the sink and turned on the warm water. "See? First we wet our paws, then we use soap and scrub for as long as it takes to sing the ABC song!"

Mrs. Robin watched as Nutkin demonstrated. "A-B-C-D-E-F-G..." he sang while rubbing his tiny paws together with bubbly soap. The soap felt slippery and smelled like lavender.

"Now you try!" encouraged Nutkin.

Mrs. Robin carefully washed her good wing and feet. "The warm water feels so nice!" she chirped happily.

"Now we're ready to clean safely!" announced Nutkin. He thought carefully about what to do first. "Let's start with the dishes, then wipe the counters, and sweep the floor last."

Working together, they made the kitchen sparkle. Nutkin washed dishes in soapy water while Mrs. Robin dried them with her good wing. They wiped every surface clean and swept up all the crumbs.

"Remember," said Nutkin as they worked, "we wash our paws again after touching dirty things, before we touch clean things, and especially before we eat!"

When they finished, the kitchen gleamed. The counters shined, the dishes sparkled, and everything smelled fresh and clean.

"Nutkin, you're so brave and smart!" said Mrs. Robin gratefully. "You knew just what to do, and you taught me about keeping germs away."

"And you helped too, even with your hurt wing!" replied Nutkin. "Teamwork makes everything easier."

Mrs. Robin smiled widely. "I feel so much better now! My kitchen is clean and safe for cooking again. Thank you for teaching me about washing paws and staying healthy."

"That's what friends do," said Nutkin warmly. "Now let's wash our paws one more time and then we can bake some acorn cookies!"

They washed their paws together, singing the ABC song and laughing. The warm, clean kitchen filled with the delicious smell of baking cookies.

"I'll always remember to wash my paws now," promised Mrs. Robin.

"And I'll always remember how good it feels to help a friend," said Nutkin with a happy smile.

From that day on, both friends kept their paws clean and their kitchen sparkling, staying healthy and happy together.

The End.
