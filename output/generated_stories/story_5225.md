# The Mouse Who Listened

In a cozy pantry, where jars clicked and spices smelled warm, lived a kind mouse named Mella. Mella had soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She loved the pantry’s sounds—the gentle hum of the fridge, the tiny crinkle of paper, and the soft clink of lids. She loved the smells too—sweet cinnamon, crunchy crackers, and shiny apples.

One sunny morning, Mella heard a little clatter. Clink, clink! She peeked around a flour bag. Two friends were frowning at each other: Pip the ant and Bibi the beetle. Between them sat a big, round crumb of cookie. It smelled like vanilla and sunshine.

“That crumb is mine!” Pip squeaked, tapping the crumb with his tiny antennae.

“No, it’s mine!” Bibi buzzed, bumping the crumb with her shiny shell.

Mella took a calm breath, in and out. She put her tiny paws together. “Hello, friends,” she said in a warm, gentle voice. “Let’s use our words. Let’s talk and listen.”

Pip stomped his tiny foot. “I saw it first!”

Bibi shook her head. “I carried it first!”

The crumb wobbled. Crumbs from the crumb fell like snowflakes. Mella held up her paw. “Pause,” she said softly. “Let’s take turns. We can follow directions. First, Pip talks. Then, we listen. Then Bibi talks. Then, we listen. I will help.”

Pip nodded. “Okay.”

Bibi sighed. “Okay.”

Mella smiled. “Pip, you go first. Use calm words.”

Pip took a breath. “I saw the crumb. It smelled sweet. My tummy said, ‘Yum!’ I walked to it. I wanted to bring it to my ant family.”

“Thank you, Pip,” Mella said. “Bibi, your turn. Use calm words.”

Bibi’s wings hummed softly. “I was carrying a seed. I bumped the crumb by accident. It rolled to me. I pushed it so it wouldn’t fall into a crack. I wanted a tiny bite for my beetle sister.”

Mella nodded. “I hear both of you. Pip saw it. Bibi saved it. Both of you helped the crumb be safe.”

Pip blinked. “We both helped?”

Bibi tilted her head. “We both did something?”

“Yes,” Mella said. “You both did good things.” She pointed to the crumb. “This crumb is big. Your families are small. Maybe we can share.”

Pip wiggled his antennae. “How?”

Bibi tapped her tiny feet. “What do we do?”

Mella smiled and used her story voice. “Let me tell a little pantry story. Once, a jar and a lid argued. The jar said, ‘I am important!’ The lid said, ‘I am important!’ They clinked and clanked. Then they listened. They learned they needed each other. Together, they kept the cookies fresh and safe. When they talked and took turns, the cookies stayed crunchy.”

Pip giggled. “Crunchy!”

Bibi laughed. “Fresh cookies!”

“Just like the jar and lid,” Mella said, “you can work together. You can follow directions. Listen, talk, and be kind.”

Pip and Bibi looked at each other. They both took a slow breath. In and out. The pantry smelled like cinnamon and a little like lemon soap. It felt calm.

“Okay,” Pip said. “Let’s share.”

“Okay,” Bibi said. “Let’s share.”

Mella clapped her tiny paws softly. “Great choice! Here are the directions. First, we break the crumb into three parts: one for Pip’s family, one for Bibi’s family, and one tiny thank-you bite for the helper who saved it.”

“Who saved it?” Pip asked.

“Bibi did,” Mella said. “She kept it from the crack.”

Bibi’s eyes shone. “Thank you.”

Mella used a clean leaf from the herb basket. She pressed the crumb gently. Crackle, crackle. “Second, we carry the pieces safely. Pip, you lead with slow steps. Bibi, you follow and guide from the side so nothing falls.”

Pip saluted with his antennae. “I will go slow.”

Bibi nodded. “I will guide.”

“Third,” Mella said, “we say thank you when we are done. We use kind words.”

Pip and Bibi both said, “We can do that.”

Together, they followed the directions. Pip walked with tiny, careful steps. Tap, tap, tap. Bibi hummed and nudged the leaf so the crumbs did not slide. Mella walked beside them, watching, smiling, and sniffing the warm cookie smell.

They reached a safe corner by the tea tins. Pip bowed and took his family’s piece. “Thank you, Bibi. Thank you, Mella.”

Bibi took her piece for her sister. “Thank you, Pip. Thank you, Mella.”

Mella pushed the tiny thank-you bite to Bibi. “For saving the crumb.”

Bibi beamed. “We did it together.”

Pip’s tummy gave a happy rumble. Brrrrr!

They all giggled. The pantry seemed extra bright. The lids clinked a friendly clink. The spices smelled cozy, like hugs.

Mella sat with her friends and said, “Today we used calm voices. We took turns. We listened. We followed directions. We solved a problem together.”

Pip nodded. “Talking helped.”

Bibi nodded too. “Listening helped.”

Mella’s whiskers twitched. “When we talk and listen, our hearts feel light, and our tummies feel happy. That is the power of good communication.”

The friends waved goodbye and carried their crumbs home. The pantry hummed its soft, happy song.

The lesson: Use calm words, take turns, listen carefully, and follow directions. Talking and listening help us solve problems and be kind.

The End.
