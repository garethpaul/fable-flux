# Benny Bear's Story Practice

In a cozy study corner filled with colorful books and soft cushions, a little bear cub named Benny sat with his favorite picture book. The morning sun streamed through the window, making golden patterns on the fluffy rug where he liked to read.

"Today I want to tell stories like Mama Bear," Benny said to himself, his small paws turning the pages carefully. He loved the way his mama's voice rose and fell like music when she read to him at bedtime.

Benny opened his mouth and tried to tell the story of the three little pigs. "Once upon a time... um... there were three... little... um..." He stopped and frowned. The words felt jumbled in his mouth like puzzle pieces that didn't fit together.

"This is hard," Benny sighed, his ears drooping a little.

Just then, his friend Rosie Rabbit hopped into the study corner. "Hi Benny! What are you doing?"

"I'm trying to practice telling stories, but it's too difficult," Benny said, closing the book with a soft thump.

Rosie's whiskers twitched thoughtfully. "My grandpa always says that practice makes everything easier. Like when I learned to hop on one foot!"

"Really?" Benny's brown eyes brightened.

"Really! Let's practice together," Rosie suggested, settling down on the soft cushions. "Tell me just one sentence first."

Benny took a deep breath and said slowly, "Once upon a time, there were three little pigs." The words came out clearly this time.

"That was perfect!" Rosie clapped her small paws. "Now try another sentence."

Day after day, Benny practiced in his cozy study corner. He would sit quietly for a moment first, taking deep breaths to calm his mind. Then he would practice one sentence, then two, then three. Rosie often joined him, listening carefully and cheering him on.

"The first little pig built his house with straw," Benny said, his voice growing stronger each day. He loved how the words felt rolling off his tongue, like smooth pebbles in a stream.

After a week of practice, Benny felt ready for something special. He had heard that the little bunnies at the forest daycare needed someone to read them stories.

"Would you like to volunteer to tell stories to the baby bunnies?" asked Mrs. Owl, who ran the daycare.

Benny's heart fluttered with excitement and nervousness. "Yes, please!"

On story day, Benny sat in the daycare's reading corner surrounded by six fluffy baby bunnies with bright, curious eyes. He took a mindful breath, just like he practiced, and began.

"Once upon a time, there were three little pigs..." His voice was clear and confident. The baby bunnies listened with wide eyes, giggling when Benny made funny voices for each character.

When he finished, the baby bunnies clapped their tiny paws. "More! More!" they squeaked.

Benny's chest filled with warm, happy feelings. All his practice had helped him share the joy of stories with others.

"You did wonderfully," said Mrs. Owl. "Practice really does make perfect!"

That evening, Benny curled up in his study corner with a new book, already excited to practice tomorrow's story. He had learned that with patience, practice, and mindful breathing, he could do anything he set his mind to.

The End.
