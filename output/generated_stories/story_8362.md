# Koala Kora and the Attic Helpers

Kora was a calm koala with soft gray fur and gentle eyes. She liked quiet places. One sunny day, Kora climbed the creaky wooden stairs to a quiet attic. The attic smelled like old books and clean dust. Light floated in through a tiny window, and tiny dust sparkles danced in the air.

Kora wiggled her round ears. “Today I will help,” she said in a small, calm voice. “I will volunteer to sort the attic for the community toy swap.” She looked around. Boxes were stacked high. Ribbons, buttons, and toy pieces peeked out. It looked like a big, big job.

“Tap, tap,” went the rain on the roof. “Creeeak,” went a floorboard. Kora took a slow breath. “I can do this, one step at a time,” she whispered.

Soon her friends came to help. Pip the puppy trotted in with clicky nails. Bibi the bird fluttered down with a flit-flit of blue wings. Timo the turtle came last, slow and steady, thump… pause… thump. They all wanted to volunteer too.

“Hi, Kora!” Pip wagged. “Let’s start fast!”

Bibi chirped, “Let’s start high!”

Timo nodded. “Let’s start slow.”

Kora smiled. “We can do this together. We can use calm bodies and calm minds.”

They picked one box. Kora read the label. “Stuffed animals.” The box was too heavy for Kora alone. She felt her shoulders get tight. “Oh,” she said. “This is hard.”

Pip grabbed the box and pulled. Bibi pushed from the top. Timo tried to lift from the side. The box wobbled. “Uh-oh!” Pip yelped.

Thump! The box tipped. Soft bears and bunnies spilled onto the floor. A teddy rolled under a table. A bunny ear got stuck on a nail. Everyone froze.

Kora’s chest felt tight. Her face got warm. She wanted to say, “Oh no! Oh no!” Instead, she sat down on the dusty floor. She placed her paws on her belly. “I feel frustrated,” she said quietly. “I want this to work. It is not working yet.”

Pip’s ears drooped. “I made a mess,” he said.

Bibi tucked her wings. “I pushed too hard.”

Timo pulled in his head. “I went the wrong way.”

Kora nodded. “It is okay to feel frustrated. Let’s do our calm steps.”

She showed them her calm steps:
- Stop.
- Breathe.
- Name the feeling.
- Ask for help.
- Try again slowly.

“Let’s breathe together,” Kora said. “Smell a flower.” They all sniffed. “Blow a bubble.” They all blew out into the air. The dust sparkles swirled and looked like tiny stars.

Kora smiled again. “I feel ready now. How about you?”

“Ready,” said Pip, tail swish-swish.

“Ready,” chirped Bibi.

“Ready,” said Timo with a tiny grin.

Kora looked at the box. “We need a plan. That is called coordination. We move together, like a team.” She pointed with a gentle paw. “Pip, can you hold the bottom steady? Bibi, can you watch from above and tell us where to go? Timo, can you push with me on three?”

Pip planted his paws. “I can hold.”

Bibi fluttered up to a beam. “I can guide.”

Timo pressed his shell to the side. “I can push.”

Kora counted in a slow, calm voice. “One… two… three.” They lifted just a little. Pip held tight. Bibi called, “A tiny step left! Now a tiny step right!” Timo pushed steady. Kora kept breathing in and out. The box moved to the shelf without a wobble.

Click. It fit! Everyone smiled.

“Teamwork!” Pip barked.

“Good coordination,” Bibi chirped.

“Slow is strong,” Timo said.

They kept going, one box at a time. Kora set labels. Pip lined up toys. Bibi sorted buttons and ribbons by color—red, blue, yellow, green. Timo rolled books to a neat stack with a soft thump. When a puzzle spilled pieces, Kora did her calm steps again. “Stop. Breathe. Name it. Ask. Try again.” She asked, “Friends, can we find the corner pieces first?” They did, and soon the puzzle picture of a sunny park smiled back at them.

After a while, Pip sighed. “I’m tired,” he said.

Bibi’s wings drooped. “Me too,” she chirped.

Timo blinked slowly. “Break time?”

Kora listened to her body. Her arms felt heavy. “Yes,” she said. “Let’s rest and sip water.” They sat by the tiny window. The rain went patter-patter. The air smelled like warm wood and orange peels from Kora’s snack. Kora closed her eyes and breathed in for four, out for four. The calm came back, like a soft blanket.

When they were ready, they finished the last shelf. Kora tied a ribbon on the final box. “All done,” she said, gentle and proud.

They looked around. The quiet attic was neat and bright. Toys waited in tidy rows for the community toy swap. “We helped,” Pip said. “We volunteered!”

“We did it together,” Bibi chirped.

“We moved as one,” Timo added.

Kora hugged her friends. Her fur felt warm. “Today we learned to handle frustration. We stopped, we breathed, we named our feelings, we asked for help, and we tried again. We used coordination and kind teamwork. Helping others feels good.”

They walked down the creaky stairs, paws and feet and shell in a slow, happy rhythm. The rain had stopped. A soft sunbeam made the hallway glow.

Kora turned back to the attic door and smiled. “When things feel hard,” she said, “we can stay calm, work together, and try again.”

Pip wagged. “We can do it!”

Bibi sang a tiny tune.

Timo nodded. “Slow is strong, and together is stronger.”

The End.
