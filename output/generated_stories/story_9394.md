# Rosie Rabbit's Big Dinner Party

Rosie Rabbit bounced excitedly around her cozy dining room. Today was special - she was hosting dinner for all her forest friends! The wooden table gleamed in the afternoon sunlight streaming through the windows.

"This will be the best dinner party ever!" Rosie declared, her fluffy tail wiggling with excitement.

But as Rosie looked around, her ears drooped. The dining room was empty except for the table and chairs. She had forgotten something very important - she needed to prepare!

"Oh no!" Rosie gasped. "My friends will be here in two hours, and I have no food, no decorations, and no plan!"

Rosie's whiskers twitched nervously. She could smell the fresh pine scent from the forest outside, reminding her that her friends would arrive soon. What would she do?

Taking a deep breath, Rosie straightened her ears. "I'm a determined rabbit," she said firmly. "I can solve this problem if I prepare properly!"

First, Rosie made a list on a piece of bark. "Food, decorations, table setting," she wrote with a twig dipped in berry juice. "I need to be organized!"

Rosie hopped quickly to her garden. She picked bright orange carrots, crisp lettuce, and sweet berries. The vegetables felt cool and bumpy in her paws, and the berries smelled like sunshine.

"Preparation means thinking ahead," Rosie reminded herself as she washed the vegetables in her kitchen sink.

Next, she gathered colorful autumn leaves and arranged them on the dining room table. The leaves crackled softly as she placed them, creating a beautiful golden and red pattern.

"Almost ready!" Rosie said, setting out her best acorn cups and wooden plates.

Just then, she heard a knock at the door. "Rosie! We're here!" called Benny Bear's deep voice.

Rosie's heart thumped. She opened the door to find all her friends: Benny Bear, Sally Squirrel, and Oliver Owl, all holding covered dishes.

"We brought food to share!" chirped Sally, her bushy tail swishing.

Rosie's ears turned pink with embarrassment. "Oh my," she whispered. "I... I forgot that when we have dinner parties, everyone brings something. I should have prepared better and asked what you were bringing."

Oliver Owl hooted gently. "It's okay, Rosie. We all make mistakes."

"But I need to apologize," Rosie said, looking at each friend. "I'm sorry I didn't plan properly. As the host, it was my responsibility to organize everything well."

Benny Bear smiled warmly. "Your apology means a lot, Rosie. And look - you prepared a beautiful table!"

The friends worked together, arranging all their dishes on Rosie's lovely table. The dining room filled with delicious smells: honey cakes, nut bread, and fresh fruit salad.

As they sat down to eat, Sally squeaked happily, "This is wonderful! You prepared such a pretty dining room, Rosie."

"Thank you," Rosie said, feeling proud. "I learned something important today. Good preparation means making lists, thinking ahead, and asking friends what they need too."

"And when we make mistakes, we apologize and learn," added Oliver wisely.

Rosie nodded, taking a bite of sweet carrot cake. "Next time, I'll prepare even better. I'll ask everyone what they're bringing and make sure we have everything we need."

The friends laughed and shared stories as the dining room glowed with warm candlelight. Rosie felt happy knowing she had solved her problem through good preparation and honest apologies.

The End.
