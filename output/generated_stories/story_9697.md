# Miko's Morning Routine Adventure

High up in the leafy green treehouse, a courageous little monkey named Miko stretched his fuzzy brown arms. The morning sun peeked through the wooden windows, making golden spots dance on the floor.

"Good morning, treehouse!" Miko called out cheerfully. But then he looked down at himself. His fur was messy and tangled. His teeth felt fuzzy. His tummy rumbled loudly.

"Oh my," Miko said, scratching his head. "I don't feel very good."

Miko's wise grandmother had taught him something important before she moved to the forest across the ocean. "Taking care of yourself is like watering a plant," she had said. "You must do it every day to grow strong and happy."

But Miko had forgotten! He had been so busy playing and swinging from branch to branch that he forgot to take care of himself.

"I know what to do," Miko said with determination. "Time for my morning routine!"

First, Miko climbed to his bathroom corner. He picked up his soft bamboo toothbrush. "Brush, brush, brush," he sang, moving the brush in gentle circles. His teeth felt clean and smooth. "Ahh, much better!"

Next, Miko filled his coconut shell with fresh rainwater. He splashed the cool water on his face and hands. The water felt refreshing and made him feel awake. He carefully combed his fur with a special brush made from palm leaves.

"Now I look neat and tidy," Miko smiled, looking in his tree-bark mirror.

But his tummy was still rumbling! Miko climbed down to his kitchen branch. He had learned from his friend Koko in Australia that breakfast was very important. He peeled a bright yellow banana and crunched on some nuts he had gathered yesterday.

"Mmm, delicious!" Miko said, feeling his energy return.

After eating, Miko did his morning exercises. He stretched his arms high like the tall trees. He touched his toes ten times. He did jumping jacks on his sturdy platform, counting, "One, two, three!" His coordination was getting better every day!

"Exercise makes my body strong," Miko puffed happily.

Finally, Miko sat quietly on his favorite cushion made of soft moss. He closed his eyes and took three deep breaths. "In... and out. In... and out." This helped his mind feel calm and ready for the day.

Just then, his friend Pip the parrot flew to his window. "Wow, Miko! You look wonderful today. Your fur is shiny, and you seem so happy!"

"Thank you, Pip!" Miko beamed. "I remembered to take care of myself this morning. Grandma taught me that self-care helps me be the best monkey I can be."

"That's so smart," Pip chirped. "My cousin in Brazil does morning exercises too. Animals all around the world take care of themselves!"

Miko felt proud and energetic. His body felt strong, his mind felt clear, and his heart felt happy. He was ready for any adventure the day might bring!

"Taking care of myself isn't hard," Miko realized. "It just takes a little time each day. And it makes everything else more fun!"

From that day on, Miko never forgot his morning routine. He brushed his teeth, washed his face, ate healthy food, exercised his body, and calmed his mind. Soon, other young animals in the forest started copying Miko's good habits.

"Self-care is the best care," Miko would tell them with a big smile.

And every morning, as the sun rose over his cozy treehouse, Miko felt grateful for his healthy, happy body and the wisdom his grandmother had shared with him.

The End.
