# Sunny Rooftop Paint Party

On a warm morning, a resourceful squirrel named Suri scampered up a brick wall and reached a big, flat rooftop. The roof felt warm under her tiny paws. The breeze smelled like fresh bread from the bakery next door. Pigeons cooed, “Coo-coo,” and far away, a bell rang, “Ding-ding.”

Suri loved to plan and to think. Today, she carried a little leaf pouch filled with berry paints—red from raspberries, blue from blueberries, yellow from dandelions, and green from spinach. “I will make a big rooftop mural,” Suri said, twitching her whiskers. “Art can bring friends together.”

On the rooftop, three friends waited. Pip the pigeon flapped his gray wings. Lina the lizard warmed her scaly back in the sun. Mei the moth fluttered softly with powdery, silver-gray wings.

Pip puffed out his feathers. “I want big blue sky everywhere!”

Lina flicked her tail. “I want bright yellow sun shapes!”

Mei floated in a circle. “I want a gentle moon garden with silver dots.”

Suri nodded and smiled. “We all have ideas,” she said. “Let’s listen. Let’s talk. Let’s make a plan.” Suri was good at using her brain and her heart. She knew how to negotiate—how to share words and find a way that works for everyone.

Pip stomped a pink foot. “Only sky! Only blue! Big, big blue!”

Lina shook her head. “Only sun! Only yellow!”

Mei whispered, “Only moon, please.”

Suri felt a flutter in her chest. She took a calm breath. The air smelled like rain even though the sky was clear. “Friends,” she said gently, “we share this roof. We share this world, too. We can make a picture that shows all of us.”

Pip ruffled. “But how?”

Suri opened her leaf pouch and showed the bright colors. “We can take turns. We can make space for each idea. We can mix, match, and blend.”

Pip tilted his head. “Blend?”

“Mix together,” Suri explained. She dipped her paw in blue and yellow. “Look—green! Like park trees from many places.” She pointed across the city. “People from many countries walk those streets. They speak many languages. We can share our art like good neighbors.”

Lina’s eyes sparkled. “Sharing space feels kind.”

Mei’s wings shivered in the light. “Maybe the moon can visit the day.”

Suri set three pebbles on the roof. “We will make three parts. Part one: blue sky for Pip. Part two: yellow sun for Lina. Part three: silver moon garden for Mei. And in the middle, we will join them together.” She smiled. “We will use our words to agree. That is negotiation.”

Pip looked at the pebbles. “But I like big! I want more.”

Suri nodded. “I hear you, Pip. You want a lot of blue. What if your sky goes across the top, very wide?” She pointed to the long edge. “Then the sun can shine from the side, and the moon garden can glow below. Each part will be big.”

Pip fluffed happily. “Wide sky sounds nice.”

Lina tapped a paw. “Can my sun be strong?”

“Yes,” said Suri. “Your sun can have long yellow rays that touch the blue sky and the moon garden. The rays will be like friendly hands.”

Mei whispered, “And my moon?”

Suri smiled. “Your moon can be a gentle circle with silver dots like tiny stars. It can float near the green place where sky and sun meet. It will feel calm.”

They all agreed. “Yes!”

Suri dipped her paw into the berry paints. The colors smelled sweet and earthy. Pip used his wing tip to spread blue in big, soft swirls. “Coo! The blue feels cool,” he chirped.

Lina dabbed yellow circles that warmed her toes. “So bright,” she said. “It tickles my tail!”

Mei tapped silver dots with a tiny leaf brush. “Ting, ting,” she sang softly. “The moon garden is quiet and kind.”

Suri worked in the middle. She mixed blue and yellow for green hills. She added red dots like flowers from many lands. “These flowers are for friends everywhere,” she said. “We are global citizens. We care for each other, near and far.”

A gust of wind whooshed. The paints wobbled. “Oh!” cried Pip, as a drip slid toward the moon garden.

Suri acted fast. She placed a smooth shell to guide the drip. “We can fix it together,” she said. “Pip, blow gently. Lina, make a warm spot. Mei, shade the paint with your wings.”

They worked as a team. Pip blew, “Puff, puff.” Lina warmed, “Mmm.” Mei shaded, “Whirr.” The drip settled into a soft green curve. It looked like a path.

Mei smiled. “A path to share.”

Suri nodded. “Yes. A path where the sun, the moon, and the sky can meet.”

When they finished, they stepped back. The rooftop mural glowed: wide blue sky, sunny yellow rays, a gentle silver moon garden, and green hills with red flowers. The air smelled like berries and sunshine. Far below, people looked up and smiled.

Pip cooed, “Our art is together.”

Lina beamed. “We listened and used kind words.”

Mei whispered, “We made space for each other.”

Suri’s whiskers twitched with pride. “We used negotiation. We said what we wanted. We listened to others. We made a plan that felt fair. That is how global neighbors solve problems. That is how friends make art.”

They all touched paws, wing, and tail. “Together,” they said.

The sun began to set, and the moon peeked out. The mural shimmered softly. Suri tucked her leaf pouch away. “Today we learned,” she said. “When we talk, listen, and share, we can make something beautiful.”

The friends curled up on the warm roof and watched the colors glow. The city hummed a friendly song.

The End.
