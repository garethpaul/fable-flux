# Rascal's Big Performance

In a cozy closet hideaway behind the winter coats, lived a little raccoon named Rascal. His fluffy tail had black and gray stripes, and his tiny paws were perfect for making music. Rascal loved to play his small wooden drums, but he was very, very shy.

"Tap-tap-tap," went his drums softly. Rascal played so quietly that even the dust bunnies could barely hear him.

One sunny morning, Mama Raccoon poked her head into the closet. "Rascal, sweetie, the Forest Friends are having a talent show today! Would you like to play your drums?"

Rascal's tummy felt all fluttery. "Oh no, Mama! I can't play in front of everyone. What if I make mistakes? What if they don't like my music?"

Mama Raccoon sat down beside him on the soft pile of scarves. "Tell me, what happens when you practice your drums here in your hideaway?"

"Well," Rascal thought, "I feel happy. The music sounds nice, and my paws know exactly what to do."

"That's wonderful!" Mama smiled. "You know how to play beautifully. But maybe we can help you feel more ready?"

Rascal's ears perked up. "How?"

"Let's practice together," Mama suggested. "First, let's play for just me."

Rascal took a deep breath. The familiar smell of cedar and lavender from the closet made him feel safe. He picked up his drumsticks and began to play. "Tap-tap-TAP! Boom-boom-BOOM!"

"That's beautiful music!" Mama clapped. "How did that feel?"

"Good!" Rascal's tail wiggled happily. "But what if I forget the rhythm when everyone is watching?"

Mama had an idea. "Let's practice the song so many times that your paws remember it all by themselves. Then, even if you feel nervous, your paws will know what to do!"

For the next hour, Rascal practiced his favorite song over and over. "Tap-tap-TAP! Boom-boom-BOOM! Tap-tap-TAP! Boom-boom-BOOM!" Soon, his paws were moving without him even thinking about it.

"Now," said Mama, "let's practice something else. When you feel scared, take three deep breaths and remember: you are a wonderful musician!"

Rascal practiced breathing slowly. "In... and out... in... and out... in... and out." His shoulders relaxed, and he felt calmer.

At the talent show, all the forest animals gathered in the sunny meadow. Rascal's heart went "thump-thump-thump" as he walked to the little stage with his drums.

He looked out at all the friendly faces - Bella Bunny, Oliver Owl, and Penny Squirrel. They were all smiling and waiting to hear his music.

Rascal remembered what Mama taught him. He took three deep breaths. "In... and out... in... and out... in... and out."

Then he closed his eyes and let his paws do what they knew how to do. "Tap-tap-TAP! Boom-boom-BOOM!" The rhythm was perfect! His music filled the meadow with happy sounds.

When he finished, all the animals cheered! "Hooray for Rascal! Beautiful music!"

Rascal's chest felt warm and proud. He had done it! By practicing lots and lots, and by remembering to breathe when he felt scared, he had shared his wonderful music with everyone.

"I did it, Mama!" he whispered as she hugged him tight.

"You certainly did, my brave little musician. You prepared well, and you believed in yourself!"

From that day on, Rascal knew that with practice and deep breaths, he could do anything he set his mind to.

The End.
