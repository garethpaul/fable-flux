# Ellie’s Kitchen Colors

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and bright curious eyes. One sunny morning, she padded into the warm kitchen. The air smelled like cinnamon and oranges. The sun made the yellow tiles glow like honey. Pots and pans gleamed, and a little radio hummed a happy tune.

“Mama, can I make a snack?” Ellie asked, her trunk wiggling with excitement.

Mama Elephant smiled. “Yes, Ellie. We can make fruit toast. It’s healthy and fun. But remember our rules. We wash, we plan, and we clean.”

Ellie nodded. “Wash, plan, clean!” She loved the rhythm of the words.

Ellie turned on the water. Splash, splash. The warm water tickled her trunk and paws. She rubbed the soap until bubbles popped like tiny stars. “I’m clean and ready,” she said proudly.

Mama set out a loaf of bread, a bowl of yogurt, and colorful fruit—red strawberries, green kiwi, orange slices, and purple blueberries. The colors made Ellie’s eyes sparkle. “It looks like a rainbow,” she whispered.

“Today,” Mama said, “you can make art with your snack.”

Ellie’s ears perked up. “Art? With food?”

“Yes,” Mama laughed softly. “Creativity can live anywhere. Even on toast.”

Ellie took a deep breath. The bread felt soft in her paws. The yogurt was cool and creamy. The strawberries smelled sweet and sunny. She spread yogurt with gentle swirls. Swish, swish. Then she placed berries one by one. Plink. Plink. Plink.

“I’m making a smiling face,” Ellie said. “Two blueberry eyes, one strawberry nose, and a curved orange smile!”

Mama nodded. “You are using your ideas. That’s creativity.”

Ellie grinned. She looked at her bright toast art. “I made a happy face! It makes me feel happy inside.”

Mama said, “When we make things, we care for our feelings and our bodies. That is wellness.”

Ellie thought for a moment. “Wellness means feeling good and strong,” she said slowly.

“That’s right. And being responsible keeps our kitchen safe,” Mama added.

Ellie looked over the counter. There were tiny yogurt drips and a few fruit seeds. She said, “I will wipe spills so no one slips.” She grabbed a small cloth. Wipe, wipe. She put the knife far from the edge. Tap. She put peels in the bin. Plop.

“Good responsibility,” Mama said. “You planned your snack, made your art, and cleaned your space.”

Ellie made another toast. This time, she wanted to try something new. “I will make a tiny garden,” she said. She sliced kiwi for leaves and lined blueberries as stones. “It’s a quiet garden.”

Just then, Ellie’s friend, Tavi the little tortoise, peeked in the doorway. “Hello,” Tavi said shyly. “Something smells nice.”

“Hi, Tavi!” Ellie waved her trunk. “We are making snack art. Do you want to try?”

Tavi looked down. “I’m slow. I might make a mess.”

Ellie’s heart felt warm. “It’s okay to be slow,” she said softly. “We can make art in different ways. We can help each other.”

Mama placed a small cutting board by Tavi. “We are patient here,” Mama said. “Every artist has a rhythm.”

Tavi smiled a tiny smile. “I like circles,” he whispered.

“Circles are great,” Ellie said. “Let’s make a sun.” She showed Tavi how to pat the yogurt softly. Pat, pat. “You make the round sun, and I’ll make the rays.”

Tavi moved slowly and carefully. He placed an orange slice in the center. Ellie added skinny apple sticks around it. “Look!” Ellie cheered. “Your sun is bright.”

Tavi’s eyes shone. “I did it!”

Ellie nodded. “We all do things our own way. That’s tolerance. We respect each other’s ways.”

They sat at the table. The chairs felt warm from the sun. They took small bites. Crunch, sweet, cool, and juicy. Ellie felt her belly say thank you. Tavi sighed happily. “This snack is like a picture you can eat.”

Mama poured water with lemon slices. The cups tinkled like tiny bells. “Drink water to help your body,” she reminded them.

Ellie took a sip. “I feel strong and calm.”

When they were done, Ellie looked at the counter again. “Time to clean,” she said. She and Tavi carried plates to the sink. Clink. Ellie wiped the table. Wipe, wipe. Tavi put the fruit bowls back in a neat row. Click.

Mama smiled. “What did we learn today?”

Ellie thought. Her ears wiggled. “I learned that creativity is using my ideas to make something new. I learned that being responsible keeps us safe and our kitchen tidy. And wellness means caring for our bodies and feelings.”

Tavi raised his little head. “And tolerance means being patient and kind with different speeds and styles.”

Ellie gave Tavi a gentle trunk hug. “We are a good team.”

The warm kitchen glowed. The radio hummed. The air smelled like clean lemon and sweet fruit. Ellie felt proud and peaceful.

“Tomorrow,” Ellie said, “let’s make sky toast with cloud yogurt.”

Tavi giggled. “And star blueberries!”

Mama nodded. “With planning, care, and big imaginations, you can make anything.”

Ellie whispered, “Creativity lives in me.”

“Yes,” Mama said. “And you use it with kindness.”

The End.
