# Nutmeg Keeps Her Promise

Nutmeg was a small, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail. She lived near a big house with a cozy guest room. The guest room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. Sunshine made warm squares on the soft rug.

One morning, Nutmeg peeked in the open window. “Hello, guest room!” she chirped. She saw a little table, a tidy bed with a blue blanket, and a basket of buttons and ribbons. On the wall was a paper that said, “Welcome!”

Nutmeg wiggled her paws. “I will make a welcome banner!” she said. “I promise I will finish it before lunchtime.” She loved to make things. She loved to help, too.

Nutmeg hopped inside and felt the smooth wood floor under her feet. “Tap, tap, tap,” went her tiny toes. She sniffed the air. “Mmm, lemon and fresh cotton.” She touched the blue blanket. “So soft,” she giggled.

On the table, there were crayons, sticky stars, and a roll of tape. There were also paper triangles in bright colors. “Perfect,” said Nutmeg. “I can do it.” Her heart felt big and happy. She wanted to do a good job because she had made a promise.

Just then, she heard a little rustle. Her friend, Pip the moth, fluttered by the lamp. “Hello, Nutmeg!” Pip hummed, his wings whispering like tiny paper fans.

“Hi, Pip,” said Nutmeg. “I’m making a banner for the guest room. I promised to finish it before lunch. Do you want to help?”

“Yes!” Pip said. “I can hold the paper with my gentle wings.”

Nutmeg smiled. “Thank you.” She picked up a purple triangle. “We will spell WELCOME,” she said. “W… E… L… C… O… M… E.” She lined up the triangles and put a letter on each one.

Pip watched. “It looks so nice!”

Nutmeg tied the triangles to a ribbon. But then—oh dear!—the ribbon slipped and fell to the floor. “Plop.”

Pip blinked. “Oh no.”

Nutmeg took a breath. “It’s okay,” she said. “I can fix it.” Her paws were steady. Her tail swished side to side. She picked up the ribbon. “Tape can help.”

She tried the tape. “Stick, stick, stick,” went the tape. But the tape was too sticky for her little paws. It folded on itself. “Oh dear,” Nutmeg said. She felt a tiny twist in her tummy.

Pip fluttered closer. “Do you want to play with the ribbons instead?” he asked. “They look fun!”

Nutmeg looked at the shiny ribbons. They did look fun. She wanted to twirl and dance with them. But she remembered her promise. She remembered the paper on the wall and the warm sunlight on the rug. She remembered why she came.

Nutmeg stood tall. “I promised to finish the banner,” she said softly. “I will be honest with myself. I will do what I said.”

Pip nodded. “That is integrity,” he said, humming. “Doing the right thing, even when no one is watching.”

Nutmeg smiled. “Yes. I can be truthful and strong.” She had an idea. “Pip, can you hold the ribbon up high? I will tie careful knots instead of tape.”

Pip held the ribbon gently, his wings making a soft “shh-shh” sound. Nutmeg used her clever paws. She tied one knot. “Pull.” She tied another knot. “Tug.” The ribbon stayed. The triangles lined up like bright flags.

“W-E-L-C-O-M-E,” they spelled. Nutmeg clapped. “We did it!”

But then, Nutmeg noticed one more thing. The last triangle was upside down. The “E” looked like a funny comb. Nutmeg felt a little tug in her heart. “Should I leave it?” she whispered. “No one may see.”

Pip tilted his head. The room was quiet. The lemon smell was sweet and calm.

Nutmeg shook her head. “No. I will fix it. I want the banner to be just right. I want to be proud because I was honest and careful.”

She untied the last knot. She turned the triangle the right way. She retied the knot with slow, sure paws. “There,” she said. “Now it is true and neat.”

They hung the banner over the bed. Sunlight touched the letters and made them glow. The guest room looked cheerful and ready. Nutmeg’s tummy felt light and warm.

Just then, Mia, the child who lived in the house, peeked into the door. “Oh!” Mia gasped. “What a lovely banner!”

Nutmeg waved her paw. “Welcome!” she chirped.

Mia smiled. “Thank you, little helper. You did your task so well.”

Nutmeg’s tail swished. “I promised I would finish,” she said. “I kept my promise.”

Pip buzzed happily. “Nutmeg used creativity and care. She finished what she started.”

Mia placed a tiny acorn on the table. “A thank-you snack,” she said.

Nutmeg nibbled the acorn. It was crunchy and sweet. The room felt safe, bright, and good. She looked at the banner once more. She felt proud, not just because it looked nice, but because she told the truth with her actions.

Nutmeg whispered to Pip, “Integrity means doing the right thing, even when it’s hard. Creativity helps us find a way. Finishing our tasks makes hearts happy.”

Pip hummed. “Yes,” he said. “We did it together.”

Nutmeg nodded. “We kept our promise.”

The End.
