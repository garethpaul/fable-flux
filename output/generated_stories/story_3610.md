# Joey Roo Plans a Pantry Art Day

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a bouncy tail. One morning, Joey hopped into the pantry. The pantry smelled like oats, apples, and a little bit like cinnamon. The shelves were tall. Boxes and jars whispered when Joey’s tail brushed by them. Crinkle, clink, crinkle!

Joey’s ears wiggled. “I want to make art,” Joey said. “But I don’t want to waste things.” Joey took a deep breath. “I can plan, plan, plan!”

Mama Roo peeked in. She smiled. “The pantry is full of sounds and shapes,” she said. “Let’s make a plan before we start. Planning helps us make smart choices.”

Joey clapped. “Step one,” Joey said, holding up one little paw, “we look for safe, clean things to recycle.”

“Good idea,” said Mama. “We do not use anything with food on it. We do not use anything sharp. We only use clean, dry, safe items.”

Joey nodded. “Step two,” Joey said, holding up two paws, “we sort the things.”

Mama nodded back. “Yes. Sorting helps us see what we have.”

Joey giggled. “Step three,” Joey said, “we make our art!”

They began. Joey’s feet went tap-tap on the cool floor. The pantry light hummed softly. Joey opened a small bin. “Crinkle!” went a box. “I found an empty cereal box,” Joey said. “It is clean!”

Mama checked. “Safe,” she said.

Joey found round paper rings from a paper towel roll. “Boop!” Joey put one on his nose and giggled. “These are clean too.”

Mama pointed to a jar. “Listen,” she said, tapping it. “Tink, tink.” The glass jar was empty and washed. “We can use the lid, not the glass.”

Joey put the metal lid with the boxes. “Clink!” It sounded like a tiny bell.

They sorted. Joey made three little piles on a cloth: boxes and cardboard in one pile, paper and labels in another, and clean lids and caps in the third. Joey whispered to the piles, “Hello, boxes. Hello, paper. Hello, lids. Thank you for being safe.”

Mama smiled. “You are planning and being kind,” she said.

Joey took a deep breath. It smelled like apples. “Now we plan our art,” Joey said. “What can we make?”

Mama asked, “What do you love?”

Joey’s eyes grew wide. “I love hopping and stars and the moon.”

“Then let’s make a moon and star picture,” Mama said. “We can also make a little hop trail for your toy.”

Joey’s tail thumped with joy. “Yes!”

They drew a simple plan on a scrap of clean paper. Joey made a circle for the moon, and little pointy stars. They planned where each piece would go. “Plan, plan, plan,” Joey sang in a soft voice. “We plan before we glue.”

Mama gave Joey a safe, non-toxic glue stick. It smelled a little sweet. “Mmm,” Joey said, “it smells nice.”

They worked. Joey smoothed a cereal box and pressed it flat. “Swoosh,” went his paw. He cut the box with safe, child scissors under Mama’s watch. Snip, snip, snip. Joey made a big circle moon. He glued shiny silver foil from a clean wrapper onto the moon. The foil felt cool and crinkly. “Crinkle-crinkle,” said the moon.

Next, Joey made stars from the paper rings. He pressed them into star shapes with Mama’s help. “Press, press,” Joey said. The metal lids became tiny planets. “Clink!” Joey placed them carefully.

Joey paused. “I want to glue everything now,” he said. His paws wiggled fast.

Mama touched Joey’s shoulder. “Let’s check our plan.”

Joey looked at the paper. He took a slow breath. “Yes,” he said softly. “We follow the plan.”

They counted. “One moon,” Joey said. “Three stars. Two planets.”

Joey glued the moon first. He smoothed it with gentle pats. “Pat, pat.” Then he glued the stars in the corners. “Stick!” The planets went in the middle. Joey hummed, happy.

When they finished, the picture sparkled. It looked like a night sky made from treasures. Joey’s eyes shone. “We made art from old things!” he said. “We recycled!”

“Yes,” Mama said. “We turned clean, safe items into something new. That is recycling. And we planned our steps. That helped a lot.”

Joey looked at the leftover pieces. “We still have some,” he said.

“Let’s put the extras in the recycling bin,” Mama said. “We can save some for the next art day too.”

Joey carried the bin. It felt light but important. “I am helping the Earth,” he said. His voice was proud.

They cleaned the pantry together. Joey wiped a tiny spill. Swish, swish. The floor felt smooth again. The shelves looked neat. Joey smiled at the tidy piles. “Planning and cleaning make me feel calm,” he said.

Mama lifted the art picture to the light. It twinkled. Joey hugged her. “Thank you,” he whispered.

“Thank you, planner and recycler,” Mama whispered back.

That evening, Joey showed the picture to his toy friends. “Look,” he told them. “We reused. We sorted. We planned before we glued. Recycling turns old into new. Planning helps us do our best.”

Joey placed the picture on the pantry door. Every time he bounced by, it went, “Shimmer!”

Joey yawned a big, happy yawn. “Today felt good,” he said. “Tomorrow, we can plan, plan, plan again.”

The End.
