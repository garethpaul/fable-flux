# Squeaky's Big Thank You

Squeaky the mouse lived in a cozy corner of the guest room. His tiny home was behind the big wooden dresser. Every morning, Squeaky would peek out with his bright black eyes and twitch his pink nose.

"What a beautiful day!" Squeaky squeaked softly. The guest room was quiet and peaceful. Sunlight streamed through the lace curtains, making pretty patterns on the carpet.

But today, Squeaky had a big problem. His tummy was rumbling loudly. "Grumble, grumble, grumble!" it went. He had eaten all his food yesterday and forgotten to save some for today.

"Oh no," Squeaky whispered. "I'm so hungry, but I don't have any food left." His little whiskers drooped sadly.

Squeaky scurried across the soft carpet to look for crumbs. He checked under the bed with the fluffy white pillows. Nothing there. He looked near the window where the warm sun made everything smell fresh and clean. Still nothing.

Then Squeaky heard gentle footsteps. Mrs. Chen, the kind lady who owned the house, came into the guest room. She was carrying a small plate.

"I wonder if my little mouse friend is hungry," Mrs. Chen said sweetly. She placed the plate on the floor near Squeaky's corner. On it were tiny pieces of cheese and some breadcrumbs that smelled like heaven.

Squeaky's eyes grew as big as buttons. "Food! Real food!" he squeaked with joy. But then he stopped. His mama had taught him something very important.

"When someone is kind to you, you should always say thank you," Squeaky remembered. But how could a tiny mouse thank a big person?

Squeaky thought and thought. His little brain worked hard to find a solution. Then he had a wonderful idea!

The next morning, Squeaky woke up extra early. He scurried around the guest room collecting things. He found a pretty button that had fallen under the dresser. He discovered a shiny penny behind the curtain. He even found a small, colorful feather from outside the window.

Carefully, Squeaky arranged all these treasures in a neat little pile right where Mrs. Chen had left his food. He made the display look as beautiful as he could.

When Mrs. Chen came in to check on the guest room, she gasped with delight. "Oh my! What a lovely surprise!" she exclaimed. Her voice was warm and happy. "Thank you, little mouse friend. This is the most thoughtful gift I've ever received."

Squeaky peeked out from his hiding spot. His heart felt as warm as the sunshine. Mrs. Chen understood! She knew the gifts were his way of saying thank you.

From that day on, Mrs. Chen and Squeaky became the best of friends. Every morning, she would leave him delicious treats. And every morning, Squeaky would leave her a small treasure he had found.

"Being grateful feels wonderful," Squeaky squeaked happily to himself. "When someone is kind to me, I want to be kind back. That's what makes friendship so special."

The guest room filled with the sound of Mrs. Chen's gentle laughter and Squeaky's happy squeaking. They had learned that gratitude shared between friends makes everyone's heart feel full and warm.

The End.
