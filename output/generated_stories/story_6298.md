# Fawn's Slow and Steady Song

Little Fawn had soft spots on her back and big, bright eyes. She was a gentle deer fawn who loved music. One afternoon, she stepped into the musical practice room for the first time. The room was warm and quiet. The floor was smooth. Sunlight made golden squares on the walls. Shiny instruments sat in a row. There was a small drum, a tiny bell, and a wooden xylophone that looked like a rainbow.

Ping. Ping. Ping. The xylophone made little songs when Fawn tapped it. She giggled and wiggled her tail.

“I want to play a whole song,” Fawn said. “Today!”

Her friend, Finch the small bird, hopped onto a music stand. “Chirp! Let’s try,” said Finch. “We can start with three notes. Slow and steady.”

Fawn’s ears perked up. “Three notes? That seems easy.”

Finch sang, “Ding, dong, ding,” nice and slow.

Fawn lifted the mallet in her hoof. She tapped too fast. Clack! Clack-clack! The notes crashed together and sounded funny.

Fawn’s cheeks felt warm. “Oh,” she sighed. “I want it to sound pretty right now.”

From the corner, Mr. Tortoise, the music teacher, smiled kindly. He moved as gently as a drifting leaf. “Hello, Fawn,” he said in a soft, deep voice. “I heard your taps. Learning takes time. Patience is our friend.”

Fawn looked at her hooves. “But I want to be good today.”

Mr. Tortoise nodded. “I know that feeling. Let’s breathe together.” He took a slow breath in and out. “We will go little by little. Patience helps your body feel calm, and calm helps your music.”

Fawn breathed in slowly. She breathed out slowly. Her shoulders softened. The room smelled like pine and polish. The tiny bell on the table made a faint tingle in the quiet.

“Let’s start with one note,” said Mr. Tortoise. “Just one beautiful note.”

Fawn lifted the mallet. Tap. The note rang, bright and clear. Ting!

Finch chirped, “I heard it! That was lovely.”

Fawn’s eyes shone. “I did it.”

“Now two notes,” said Mr. Tortoise, “with space between. Tap… wait… tap.”

Tap… pause… tap. Ting… ting.

Fawn waited in the middle. She felt the sound float in the air like a bubble.

“That space is patience,” said Mr. Tortoise. “You are doing it.”

Fawn smiled. “Patience is waiting on purpose.”

“Yes,” said Mr. Tortoise. “Patience lets your heart feel steady.”

They tried three notes next. Finch sang with her. “Ding… dong… ding.” Fawn followed. Tap… wait… tap… wait… tap. The song sounded like tiny stars.

But then Fawn saw the drum. It looked so fun. “I want to try the drum!” she said. She jumped over and gave a big thump. BOOM! The sound echoed and surprised her. She bumped the mallet. It rolled under the chair.

“Oh no,” Fawn whispered. “I messed up. Maybe I can’t do it.”

Mr. Tortoise bent down slowly and nudged the mallet out with his shell. “Here you go,” he said, smiling. “Making a mistake is part of learning. We can pause and try again.”

Fawn took a breath. In… out… She stood tall. “I can try again,” she said softly. Her chest felt warm and proud.

They went back to the xylophone. Fawn placed her hooves quietly. She looked at Mr. Tortoise. He nodded. Finch hummed a gentle “mmm.”

Tap… wait… tap… wait… tap. Ting… tong… ting. The sound filled the room like sunshine.

“You did it!” Finch cheered. “Chirp, chirp!”

Fawn laughed. “The waiting helped. The notes had room to sing.”

“Now,” said Mr. Tortoise, “let’s make a little song. Four notes, slow and steady.”

Fawn traced the wooden bars with her eyes. She felt safe. She felt brave. She tapped: tap… wait… tap… wait… tap… wait… tap. The song was smooth and sweet.

She tried again. And again. Each time, she waited and listened. The room’s quiet hum, the soft squeak of the stool, the warm smell, the sunlight—everything helped her feel calm. Her heart beat a gentle beat: thump… wait… thump… wait.

After a while, Fawn’s legs felt a little tired. She sat down. “I learned a song,” she said, surprised and happy.

“Yes,” said Mr. Tortoise. “You were patient. You believed in yourself. That is real music.”

Finch fluffed his feathers. “Your song makes me want to dance!” He twirled in a tiny circle.

Fawn stood up and bowed. “Thank you, patience,” she whispered. “Thank you, me.”

Mr. Tortoise winked. “When things feel hard, remember: slow, steady, and kind. Take a breath. Make space. Try again.”

Fawn nodded. “I can do hard things, one little step at a time.”

She played her song once more. The notes floated up like balloons. Fawn’s smile grew. She felt strong inside.

The End.
