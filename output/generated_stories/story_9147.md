# Ellie's Special Bedtime Story

Little Ellie the elephant calf sat on her soft blue blanket in the cozy nursery. The walls were painted with gentle clouds, and warm lamplight made everything glow. But Ellie looked worried. Her trunk drooped down, almost touching the floor.

"What's wrong, sweet Ellie?" asked Grandma Elephant, settling into the big rocking chair. Her gray skin was wrinkled with age and wisdom. The chair creaked softly as she rocked back and forth.

"Tomorrow is Show and Share at nursery school," Ellie said with a small sigh. "Everyone will bring something special from their family. But I don't know what to bring."

Grandma's eyes twinkled like stars. "Oh my! I have just the thing." She reached into her memory trunk - a beautiful wooden chest painted with colorful flowers. "Let me tell you about our family's special bedtime song."

"A song?" Ellie's ears perked up. She had never heard about a family song before.

"Yes, indeed," Grandma said warmly. "Your great-great-grandmother taught it to me when I was just your size. She learned it from her grandmother too. It's been passed down through our elephant family for many, many years."

Ellie's trunk wiggled with curiosity. "What does it sound like?"

Grandma began to hum a gentle melody. It sounded like wind blowing through tall grass and water trickling in a peaceful stream. "The words are about being brave and kind, just like elephants should be."

"But Grandma," Ellie said, thinking carefully, "what if the other children don't like elephant songs? What if they think it's too different?"

Grandma smiled and touched Ellie's trunk gently. "That's a very thoughtful question, little one. But sharing our traditions is like planting seeds. When we share what makes our family special, other children learn something new. And maybe they'll share their special traditions with you too."

Ellie's eyes grew wide. "Really? They might teach me their family songs?"

"Of course!" Grandma said. "Tommy Tiger might know a roaring song. Sally Sparrow might know a flying song. Each family has their own special ways."

Ellie stood up and did a little dance on her blanket. "That sounds wonderful! I want to learn about everyone's traditions."

"That's the spirit," Grandma said proudly. "Now, shall we practice our elephant lullaby together?"

Ellie nodded eagerly. Grandma began to sing in her deep, rumbling voice:

"Little elephants, strong and true,
With hearts so big and spirits new,
Remember always, wherever you roam,
Love and kindness will guide you home."

Ellie joined in, her small voice mixing with Grandma's like honey and milk. The nursery filled with their beautiful song. Even the stuffed animals seemed to listen from their spots on the shelves.

"I can't wait to share this tomorrow!" Ellie said, clapping her feet together. "And I can't wait to hear what songs my friends know too."

Grandma tucked Ellie into her cozy bed. "Sleep well, my brave little elephant. Tomorrow you'll give your friends a very special gift - a piece of our family's heart."

As Ellie drifted off to sleep, she hummed the lullaby softly. She dreamed of all the wonderful traditions she would learn from her friends, and how sharing her own special song would help everyone feel connected and happy.

The End.
