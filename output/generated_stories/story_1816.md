# Milo Makes a Big Bright Mural

Milo was a courageous monkey with quick hands and a warm smile. He loved to make art. One sunny morning, Milo tugged open the squeaky door of Auntie Bea’s garage workshop. The air smelled like lemon soap and sawdust. Sunlight slid through the small window and made golden stripes on the floor.

Inside the workshop were brushes, paints, tape, and big empty boards. Hammers and wrenches hung on the wall. Auntie Bea wiped her hands on a blue cloth and waved. “Good morning, Milo,” she said. “What is your goal today?”

Milo’s tail curled with excitement. “I want to paint a big bright mural,” he said. “I want it to help everyone feel happy.”

“That is a wonderful goal,” said Auntie Bea. “Let’s plan.”

Milo nodded. “Step one: Make a sketch. Step two: Gather tools. Step three: Paint with care. Step four: Share and help others.”

Auntie Bea clapped softly. “You are brave and smart. I will work on my toolbox while you paint. If you need help, I am here.”

Milo opened a sketchbook and drew with a soft pencil. Swish, swish. He drew a sun with long warm rays. He drew a tree with round, friendly leaves. He drew a path where friends could walk together. He smiled. “This looks kind,” he whispered.

He taped a big board to the wall. The tape made a sticky sound. Milo picked a brush. It felt smooth in his hand. He dipped it into yellow paint. Drip, drip. “Hello, sunshine,” he giggled as he painted the round sun. The paint smelled a little sweet and a little like eggs. The brush went whoosh across the board.

“Looking bright,” said Auntie Bea.

“Thank you,” said Milo. He cleaned his brush in a jar of water. Swirl, swirl. The water turned light yellow. Then he chose green. He painted the big tree, leaf by leaf. Pat, pat. He hummed a little song.

The door creaked again. Milo’s friend, Tiko the tiny turtle, peeked in. Tiko wore a red scarf. “Hi, Milo,” said Tiko. “What are you making?”

“A mural,” said Milo. “It is for everyone. Do you want to help?”

Tiko blinked. “I am slow,” he said. “But I can try.”

Milo smiled. “We all can help. I will show you.” He gave Tiko a small brush. “You can paint the little flowers. Tap, tap, tap.”

Tiko tapped purple dots. Tap. Tap. Tap. He smiled. “I like the tapping sound.”

Just then, Zip the squirrel rushed in. Zip moved very fast. “Wow! Colors!” Zip cried. He bounced near the paint table. His tail flicked. His foot slipped on a drop of water. “Oh no!” Zip wobbled and bumped the red paint. The can tipped. Glug! Red paint splashed across the board and onto the floor.

Milo froze. His heart thumped. The sun had a big red stripe across it. The tree had red spots. The floor had red puddles. Tiko gasped. “Oh dear,” he whispered.

Zip’s eyes grew wide. “I’m sorry,” he squeaked. “I didn’t mean to. I was too fast.”

Milo took a deep breath. In... and out. His tail uncurled. He looked at Zip’s worried face. He felt the warm sun from the window on his fur. He listened to the soft ticking of the clock on the wall. “It was an accident,” Milo said gently. “I forgive you.”

Zip’s shoulders dropped. “Really?”

“Yes,” said Milo. “But we need to fix it. Let’s use art skills.” He looked at the red splash. “We can turn the red into something new.”

Auntie Bea stepped over with rags and a smile. “You are brave, Milo,” she said. “How can we help?”

Milo pointed to the splash. “We can make red flowers and a friendly bird. We can add a red kite in the sky. Tiko, will you keep tapping flowers? Zip, can you hold the paint tray and move slow like Tiko?”

Zip nodded. “I can try. Slow and steady.”

They cleaned the floor together. Swish, wipe, pat. Then they painted. Milo mixed colors. Swirl, swirl. He showed Zip how to dab gently. Dab, dab. He showed Tiko how to add tiny yellow centers. Dot, dot.

The red stripe across the sun became a bright kite with a long tail. The red spots on the tree became sweet apples. The splash near the path became a red bird with warm wings. The garage was full of quiet sounds: whoosh of brushes, clink of jars, and soft happy humming.

When they were done, everyone stepped back. The mural glowed. The sun felt warm. The tree looked strong. The path looked friendly. The apples looked tasty. The kite flew high. The red bird looked ready to sing.

Milo put his brush down. “We met my goal,” he said. “We made a big bright mural. We built our skills. We helped each other. We stayed brave. And we forgave mistakes.”

Zip smiled. “I learned to move slow and careful.”

Tiko nodded. “I learned that small taps make lovely flowers.”

Auntie Bea patted Milo’s shoulder. “And I learned that art can fix a problem and make it even better.”

Milo looked at his friends. His chest felt warm and proud. “Art is for everyone,” he said. “Art helps us share feelings. Art helps us try, learn, and help others.”

They cleaned the brushes. Swish, swish. They washed the floor. Splash, splash. They opened the door wide. Fresh air came in, cool and clean. The mural shone in the sunlight.

Milo grinned. “Tomorrow, we can add a rainbow.”

“Yes,” said Tiko.

“Yes,” said Zip, slow and steady.

Auntie Bea smiled. “Yes.”

The End.
