# Nutkin's Rooftop Dance Party

High up on the red brick rooftop, Nutkin the squirrel was having the most wonderful morning. The sun felt warm on his fluffy gray fur, and a gentle breeze tickled his whiskers. Nutkin loved dancing on the rooftop because he had so much space to twirl and jump!

"La-la-la!" sang Nutkin as he spun around in circles. His bushy tail swished back and forth like a feather duster. He leaped from one chimney to another, feeling as light as a cloud.

But then Nutkin heard something that made him stop mid-dance. It was a soft sniffling sound coming from behind the big water tank.

"Hello?" called Nutkin gently. "Is someone there?"

A tiny gray mouse peeked out, her little pink nose twitching. Tears sparkled in her dark eyes like dewdrops.

"I'm Pip," whispered the mouse. "I'm sorry I'm on your rooftop. I was trying to find food for my babies, but I got lost."

Nutkin's heart felt warm and fuzzy, like when he found the perfect acorn. He could see that Pip was scared and sad. Instead of feeling upset that someone was on his dancing space, Nutkin felt something different – he wanted to help.

"Don't cry, Pip," said Nutkin softly. "You're not bothering me at all. Are you hungry?"

Pip nodded, wiping her tiny paws across her eyes.

Nutkin took three deep breaths – in through his nose, out through his mouth – just like his mama had taught him when he felt big feelings. This helped him think clearly about how to help his new friend.

"I know where the best bird feeder is!" Nutkin said with a bright smile. "The kind humans fill it with seeds every morning. Would you like me to show you?"

Pip's eyes grew wide with hope. "Really? You would help me?"

"Of course!" said Nutkin. "But first, would you like to dance with me? Dancing always makes me feel better when I'm worried."

Pip looked nervous. "I don't know how to dance like a squirrel."

"That's okay!" laughed Nutkin. "You can dance like a mouse! There's no wrong way to dance when you're having fun."

Nutkin started with gentle swaying, moving his arms like tree branches in the wind. "See? Just move however feels good!"

Pip began to wiggle her little body, and soon she was doing tiny mouse hops. The morning sun made their shadows dance together on the rooftop.

"This is fun!" giggled Pip, feeling much better.

After their dance, Nutkin led Pip to the bird feeder on the other side of the rooftop. It was overflowing with sunflower seeds and millet.

"Take as much as you need," said Nutkin kindly.

Pip filled her cheek pouches until they looked like tiny balloons. "Thank you, Nutkin! You're the kindest squirrel I've ever met."

"We all need help sometimes," said Nutkin. "And helping friends makes my heart feel as warm as sunshine."

From that day on, Pip visited Nutkin's rooftop every morning. They would dance together and share breakfast, and Nutkin never felt lonely again.

As Nutkin watched Pip scamper home to her babies, he realized something wonderful: sharing his special place and caring about others' feelings made everything even more special.

The End.
