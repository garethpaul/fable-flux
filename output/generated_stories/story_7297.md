# Splash the Dolphin Learns to Share

Splash was a playful young dolphin who loved to swim and play in the big blue ocean. But today was very special - Splash had been invited to have dinner at the underwater dining room of the Sea Palace with all his ocean friends!

The dining room was magical, with shimmering coral walls and glowing sea plants that lit up like fairy lights. Long tables made of smooth driftwood stretched across the sandy floor, and colorful fish swam overhead like living decorations.

"Welcome, everyone!" called Marina the seahorse, who was hosting the dinner. "Tonight we're having a feast of delicious sea vegetables and plankton soup!"

Splash swam excitedly to his seat, his gray skin gleaming in the soft underwater light. But when he looked around the table, he saw friends who were very different from him. There was Shelly the slow-moving sea turtle, Ollie the eight-armed octopus, and Finn the tiny fish who could barely reach the table.

When the food arrived, Splash gobbled up his portion quickly - *gulp, gulp, gulp!* He was still hungry and noticed that Shelly hadn't even started eating yet.

"Why are you so slow?" Splash asked impatiently, his tail swishing. "I'm already done!"

Shelly looked sad. "I can't eat as fast as you, Splash. My jaw moves slowly."

Then Splash noticed Ollie struggling to hold his spoon with his tentacles, dropping food everywhere. *Plop, plop, plop!* went the sea vegetables as they fell.

"You're making such a mess!" Splash said, swimming in circles. "Why can't you eat neatly like me?"

Poor Finn was having trouble too. He was so small that he could barely see over his bowl.

"This isn't fair!" Splash huffed. "Everyone eats so differently! This dinner is taking forever!"

Marina the seahorse swam over gently. "Splash," she said kindly, "look around you. Do you see how the coral comes in different shapes? How the fish are different colors? Our ocean is beautiful because we're all different."

Splash stopped swimming and really looked at his friends. Shelly's slow, careful movements were actually very graceful, like a peaceful dance. Ollie's tentacles, though messy, could reach many places at once to help clean up. And little Finn's small size meant he could swim through the tiniest spaces.

"I'm sorry," Splash said, his voice soft like gentle waves. "I didn't think about how you feel. Can I help?"

"That would be wonderful!" Shelly smiled.

Splash practiced being patient, waiting for Shelly to finish each bite. He helped Ollie by showing him how to hold the spoon with just two tentacles instead of eight. And he shared his food with little Finn, breaking it into tiny pieces.

"You know what?" said Splash, feeling warm and happy inside. "This dinner is much more fun when we help each other! And Shelly, your stories are so interesting when you take your time to tell them!"

As they finished their meal, Marina brought out a special dessert - sweet kelp cake for everyone.

"Splash," she said, "you've learned something very important tonight. When we're tolerant and patient with others who are different from us, we make our ocean home a happier place for everyone."

Splash nodded, his eyes sparkling like starfish. "Being different makes us special! And being kind makes us friends!"

From that day on, Splash always remembered to be patient and helpful with friends who were different from him. He learned that tolerance and practice made him a better friend, and taking care of each other made their ocean home the most wonderful place to live.

The End.
