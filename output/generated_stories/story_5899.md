# Tilly Turtle’s Table Tennis Try

Tilly was a shy turtle. She liked quiet corners and soft sounds. Her shell was smooth and green. She lived with her family, and her favorite place was the dining room. The dining room smelled like warm toast in the morning and sweet apples in the afternoon. The wooden table felt cool and smooth under her little turtle feet.

One sunny day, Tilly’s big brother, Toby, bounced a small, orange ball on the dining room table. Tap, tap, tap! The sound was light and bouncy. “I’m practicing table tennis,” Toby said, smiling. “Do you want to try, Tilly?”

Tilly peeked from behind her shell. “I’m shy,” she whispered. “What if I’m too slow?”

Mama Turtle poured milk and said, “Being slow is okay. Going slow can be strong. We can prepare.”

“Prepare?” Tilly asked, blinking her big eyes.

“Prepare means practice and plan,” Mama said. “We can make a plan in the dining room.”

The dining room felt safe. The curtains were yellow and soft. The chairs stood like friendly giants. Tilly took a deep breath. She smelled banana bread. Her tummy felt warm.

“Okay,” Tilly said softly. “Let’s prepare.”

Step one, Toby showed Tilly how to hold the paddle. “Gentle hands,” he said. “Like you’re holding a butterfly.”

Tilly held the paddle in both hands. “Like this?”

“Perfect!” Toby cheered.

Step two, Mama put a placemat on the table like a tiny net. “This is our practice net,” Mama said. “We can try small bounces.”

Tilly nodded. Her heart went thump-thump, but she stood tall. Tap. Tap. The ball bounced once, then twice. It rolled to the edge. “Oh no,” Tilly said.

“It’s okay,” Mama said. “We pick it up and try again. Preparation means we try, and try, and try.”

Tilly tried again. Tap… tap… tap! The little ball danced. Tilly smiled. “I did three!”

Step three, they made a practice song. “Bounce and breathe, bounce and breathe,” they sang together. Tilly breathed in and out. The air smelled like cinnamon. She felt calmer.

The next day, Tilly hung a small paper chart on the dining room wall. It had four boxes. Box one said, “Hold paddle.” Box two said, “Soft bounces.” Box three said, “Aim to the placemat.” Box four said, “Smile and breathe.”

“Each day, we check a box,” Mama said.

Tilly checked box one. “Hold paddle.” She held it gently. She checked box two. “Soft bounces.” Tap, tap, tap. Tilly laughed. “It sounds like raindrops!”

On day two, Tilly worked on aiming. “Aim to the placemat,” Toby said. “Eyes on the middle.”

Tilly’s eyes were wide and bright. Tap—plop! The ball landed right on the placemat. “I did it!” she cheered.

On day three, Tilly practiced moving her feet. The dining room rug felt fuzzy. She slid a little left and a little right. “Slide and tap, slide and tap,” she sang.

On day four, Tilly felt nervous. Her tummy fluttered like a butterfly. There was a tiny table tennis game at the community center. Tilly looked at her shell. “What if I’m too shy?”

Mama knelt beside her. “Being shy is part of you, and that is okay. You are accepted and loved, just as you are.”

Toby held up the orange ball. “And you prepared! Preparation makes you ready.”

Tilly touched her chart. All the boxes had happy checkmarks. She took a slow breath. “Bounce and breathe,” she whispered.

At the community center, there were other kids. The room buzzed like friendly bees. “Hi!” said a rabbit named Ruby. “I like your green shell.”

“Thank you,” Tilly said softly. Her cheeks felt warm, but her heart felt strong.

Coach Bear set up a small table with a real net. “Who wants to try?” he asked.

Tilly raised her hand. It rose slow, but it rose high. “I do.”

She stepped up. The paddle felt just right in her hands. She breathed in. She smelled orange slices. She breathed out. “Bounce and breathe,” she whispered.

Coach Bear served gently. Tap! The ball came to Tilly. She watched the middle, like she practiced. Tap! She returned the ball. It sailed over the net and landed with a soft plop. Ruby clapped. Toby cheered. Mama smiled big.

Tilly played a short game with Ruby. Sometimes Tilly missed. Sometimes Ruby missed. Each time, they smiled and tried again.

After the game, Coach Bear said, “Great job, players! I saw focus. I saw practice.”

Tilly’s shy smile grew. “I prepared,” she said. “I made a plan. I practiced at home in the dining room.”

Ruby nodded. “You did great. Want to play again next week?”

“Yes,” Tilly said. “I like going slow and steady. I like being me.”

They all shared orange slices. The slices were juicy and sweet. The taste was sunny. Tilly looked at her paddle. She felt proud inside her shell and out.

At home, Tilly added a new box to her chart: “Share what I learned.” She showed her little sister how to hold the paddle. “Gentle hands,” Tilly said. “Like a butterfly.”

Her sister giggled. “Bounce and breathe!”

Tilly laughed too. She was still shy, and that was okay. She was prepared. She was accepted. She was confident.

The lesson was clear: when we prepare with small steps, we feel ready. When we accept who we are, we feel strong. When we try, we grow.

The End.
