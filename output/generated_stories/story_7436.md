# Rumi Rabbit’s Rainbow Lab Day

Rumi was a determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. Rumi loved to learn. Every day, Rumi hopped down wooden steps to a cozy basement lab. The lab smelled like warm paper and orange soap. It felt cool and safe, like a quiet hug. Little lights blinked and twinkled. Bottles clinked softly on shelves. A fan hummed like a sleepy bee.

“Good morning, lab!” Rumi said, twitching whiskers.

Today, Rumi had a big idea. “I want to learn how friends around the world say hello,” Rumi said. “I want to make a Hello Machine!”

Rumi opened a colorful notebook. The pages were smooth and crinkly. Rumi drew a big circle and wrote HELLO in the center in big, bouncy letters. Around it, Rumi drew little flags and smiling faces.

Rumi picked up a shiny spoon and a tiny bell. “Ting!” went the bell. Rumi pressed a red button. “Beep!” sang the machine. The basement lab felt busy and bright.

Just then, friends came to visit. The lab door creaked open. “Hi, Rumi!” said Mia Mouse, wearing a blue scarf. “We brought snacks.” The air filled with yummy smells—crunchy apples, warm flatbread, sweet rice cakes, and salty crackers.

Kai Kitten peeked in, whiskers wiggling. “What are we making today?”

“A Hello Machine!” said Rumi. “We will learn to say hello in many ways. We will listen, watch, and be kind. We will celebrate each friend.”

Rumi put a soft round microphone on the table. It felt like a fuzzy peach. “Who wants to go first?”

Mia Mouse raised a tiny paw. “In my family, we say hola,” Mia said. “It means hello.” Mia spoke clearly: “Hola!” The sound was gentle and warm.

The machine lit up with a golden glow. It printed a sticker with the word hola. “It works!” said Rumi, clapping paws. “Thank you, Mia. We will remember hola.”

Kai Kitten purred. “In my family, we say ni hao.” Kai spoke carefully: “Ni hao.” The machine buzzed and printed another sticker. It smelled like new crayons. Rumi stuck the sticker on the big circle.

Tariq Tortoise slowly nodded. “In my family, we say salam.” He smiled like sunshine. “Sa-lam.” The machine made a happy beep and a blue light.

Anaya Ant waved. “We say namaste.” She placed her hands together and bowed. “Na-ma-ste.” The machine swirled rainbow colors and printed another sticker. Rumi bowed back, gently and kind.

Rumi’s eyes shone. “Each hello is special,” Rumi said. “Each hello is a gift.”

The friends kept sharing.

“Bonjour,” said Leo Lamb, with a soft voice.

“Konnichiwa,” said Hana Hedgehog, spines smooth.

“Jambo,” said Babu Bear, with a big grin.

“Marhaba,” said Zain Zebra, tail swishing.

“Sawubona,” said Nia Newt, smiling wide.

Every time, the Hello Machine glowed, beeped, and printed a new sticker. The paper felt warm in Rumi’s paws. The room filled with happy sounds and bright lights. The air was full of words like tiny songs. Rumi’s heart felt cozy and big.

But then, a small problem came. Pip Penguin shuffled his feet. His cheeks turned pink. “I’m shy,” Pip whispered. “What if I say it wrong?”

Rumi’s ears stood tall. “It’s okay, Pip,” Rumi said softly. “Being shy is okay. We can learn together. We can try again. We can be kind.”

Rumi put a small blanket on the chair. It was soft like clouds. “Do you want to hold my paw?”

Pip nodded. He took a deep breath. “In my family, we say ola,” he said. He paused. “Is that right?”

Mia Mouse tilted her head. “In my grandma’s home, we say hola with an h sound,” she said gently. “Your way is also okay. Words can change in different places.”

Rumi smiled. “Yes! Languages are like colors. Many shades. Many sounds. All beautiful.”

Pip tried again. “Hola,” he said, a little louder. The machine dinged with a cheerful “Doo-doo!” A new sticker slid out, and Pip laughed. “I did it!”

The friends clapped. The claps were soft, like raindrops on leaves.

Rumi taped all the stickers around the big circle. The paper looked like a sun with many rays. The lab seemed brighter. The friends shared snacks. The flatbread was warm and fluffy. The rice cakes were crisp and sweet. The apples were juicy. Everyone took turns and said, “Thank you,” in many ways.

“Gracias,” said Mia.

“Shukran,” said Zain.

“Arigato,” said Hana.

“Dhanyavaad,” said Anaya.

“Merci,” said Leo.

Rumi wrote in the notebook: We learned to say hello in many languages. We listened. We were brave. We were kind.

Rumi looked at each friend. “Today, we learned about cultural diversity,” Rumi said slowly. “That means many ways to live, speak, and share. We are different. We are the same. We all like friendship. We all like hello.”

The friends nodded. The fan hummed. The lights twinkled. The basement lab felt like a safe, happy world.

Rumi put the big HELLO circle on the wall. “This is our Hello Sun,” Rumi said. “When we see it, we remember to be curious, to listen, and to be kind to every person.”

Pip held Rumi’s paw. “I feel brave now,” he said.

Rumi’s smile was bright. “Brave and kind,” Rumi said. “That is the best way.”

They all said hello to each other, in many voices, like a soft, sweet song. Then they said goodbye in many ways too, with waves, bows, and hugs.

“Tomorrow,” Rumi said, “we can learn more words and more ways to care.”

The friends agreed. Their hearts felt warm, like toast.

The End.
