# Poppy the Parrot’s Paint-and-Play Plan

Poppy the Parrot loved the creative art room. It smelled like fresh paper and lemony clean tables. Sunlight made the glitter twinkle. Paint pots clicked open like tiny drums. Brushes whispered, swish-swish, in cups of water.

“Squawk! Time to make art!” Poppy chirped, hopping onto a stool. He tilted his rainbow head. “What shall I paint today?”

Poppy dipped a brush into bright blue. He painted a big circle. Then he painted little dots. But his wings felt sleepy. His tail drooped. His feet felt wiggly and stuck at the same time.

“My body is tired,” Poppy said softly. “But I want to make art. How can I feel strong and ready?”

Ms. Lina, the art teacher, smiled. She wore a smock with purple polka dots. “Hello, Poppy. Art is fun for our minds. Exercise is fun for our bodies. When our bodies move, our minds wake up too.”

“Exercise?” Poppy tilted his head. “Like flying?”

“Yes,” said Ms. Lina. “We can make a Paint-and-Play Plan. First we move. Then we make. We can use steps, like a little routine.”

Poppy loved plans. He fluffed his feathers. “Let’s solve this problem with a plan!”

They wrote the plan on a big paper:

1. Warm up
2. Move and count
3. Paint and rest
4. Stretch and smile

Poppy read each step out loud. “Step one: Warm up!”

He puffed his chest and took deep, belly breaths. In and out. He rolled his shoulders. He shook his tail feathers. Shake-shake! The tiny bells on the shelf jingled. “I feel tingly and awake!”

“Step two,” said Ms. Lina. “Move and count.”

Poppy hopped to one side. “One!” He hopped to the other side. “Two!” He hopped again. “Three!” His claws made soft tap-tap sounds on the floor. He flapped his wings slowly. “Four, five, six.” He touched his wing to his knee. “Seven, eight.” He spun in a gentle circle. “Nine… ten!”

Poppy giggled. “My heart goes thump-thump! I feel strong!”

“Step three,” Poppy read. “Paint and rest.”

He dipped his brush into sunny yellow. Swish-swish. He painted a smiling sun. Next, he dabbed grassy green. Dot-dot-dot. He made tiny leaves that smelled like the crayons in the basket. “The colors look happy,” Poppy said.

But then Poppy pressed too hard. His brush made a big splat. “Oh no!” he cried. “My sun has a blob!”

Ms. Lina pointed to the plan. “Use logical thinking. Let’s solve it. What can a blob become?”

Poppy tapped his beak. “Hmm… The blob can be a fluffy cloud!”

He added white around the blob. He dabbed little gray shadows. “Now the cloud is friendly,” he said. “It looks soft like cotton.”

Poppy painted more, but his feet got fidgety again. He looked at the plan. “Time for a tiny rest.” He set down his brush, took a sip of cool water, and shook his wings. He listened. The room hummed. Outside, a bird sang, tweet-tweet.

Then Poppy tried a new picture. He wanted to make a rainbow path. He took a ruler. He lined up the paper. “Straight lines, step by step,” he said. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple. He counted colors as he painted. “One, two, three, four, five, six.”

His body felt wiggly once more. Poppy looked at the plan and smiled. “Time to move and count again!”

He made a game. “Paint pose, exercise pose!” He froze like a tree. Then he stretched up tall. “One!” He crouched like a frog. “Two!” He balanced on one foot. “Three!” He wiggled like jelly. “Four!” He marched in place. “Five, six, seven, eight!” He clapped soft. “Nine, ten!”

Poppy felt calm and happy. His mind was clear like the clean water cup. “Now I can finish with care.”

He dabbed tiny stars around the rainbow path. He added footprints shaped like little hearts. He brushed silver sparkles. The paper shined. Poppy’s eyes sparkled too.

“Step four,” he said. “Stretch and smile.” He stretched his wings wide. He smiled a big parrot smile. “Squawk! I did it!”

Ms. Lina clapped. “You solved your problem with a routine. You used exercise to wake your body, and art to express your ideas.”

Poppy nodded. “I also used logical thinking. I looked at the blob and found a way. I made a cloud!”

“What is your discovery today?” Ms. Lina asked.

Poppy tapped his beak and thought. “I discovered that when I move my body, my brain feels bright. Exercise helps me focus. A little plan helps me make good choices. And art is even more fun!”

Ms. Lina hung Poppy’s pictures on the wall. The sun and cloud smiled. The rainbow path glowed. The room smelled like orange soap and glitter glue. Poppy’s heart felt light.

Poppy put the plan on the wall too. “I will use this every day,” he said. “First we move, then we make!”

He looked at the reader and chirped, “You can try it. Make your own Paint-and-Play Plan! Warm up. Move and count. Paint and rest. Stretch and smile. Your body will feel strong, and your art will shine.”

Poppy flapped once, softly. “Exercise helps me do my best. I can do it. You can too!”

The End.
