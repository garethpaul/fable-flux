# Koala Kori’s Colorful Break

Kori the koala was calm and kind. Kori loved the creative art room at the community center. The room smelled like fresh paper and orange soap. Bright paints shined in little cups. Soft brushes tickled Kori’s paws.

“Today I will paint a big, happy sun,” Kori whispered, feeling cozy.

Kori’s friends came in, giggling and bouncing. Miko the monkey did cartwheels. Tia the turtle hummed a tune. Suri the sparrow flapped her wings. Coach Lina, a friendly llama with a whistle, peeked in the door.

“Hi, artists!” Coach Lina smiled. “After art, we have a short sports playtime in the gym. We’ll stretch, sip water, and try gentle ball rolls. Healthy habits help our bodies and brains.”

Kori nodded. “Healthy habits help me think and paint.”

Kori dipped a brush and swished yellow paint in soft circles. “Swish, swish.” The brush made a whispery sound. Kori’s tummy made a tiny growl.

“Oh,” Kori said. “I forgot my snack and water.” Kori felt a little wobbly.

Miko whispered, “You can have my cookie.”

Kori smiled but shook their head. “Thank you, Miko, but my body needs water and a crunchy fruit. Coach says sugar is a sometimes treat. I will be honest with myself. I need a healthy snack.”

Suri chirped, “There’s a water cooler in the hallway.”

Kori looked at the door. The art show sign-up sheet hung on the wall. The art show was tomorrow. Kori wanted to finish the sunny painting. But Kori’s mind felt fuzzy.

Coach Lina trotted in. “Artists, time for a sip-and-stretch break! Healthy habit time,” she sang.

Kori raised a paw. “Coach, I forgot my snack and water. I feel wobbly. I want to finish my art, but I need a break.”

“That is honest and smart,” said Coach Lina. “Let’s solve this together.”

“Problem,” Kori said softly. “I’m hungry and thirsty.”

“Solution,” Coach Lina said. “Step one, drink water. Step two, stretch. Step three, eat a healthy snack. I have extra apple slices to share.”

Kori’s eyes shined. “Thank you!”

They went to the hallway. The water cooler bubbled, “glug-glug.” Kori held a paper cup. “Sip, sip.” Cool water slid down Kori’s throat. It felt like a tiny river. Kori sighed, “Ahh.”

Back in the art room, Coach Lina opened a container. “Crunch,” went the apple slices. Kori chewed slowly. “Crunch, munch.” The sweet apple tasted like sunshine. Tia the turtle stretched her neck and smiled. “Healthy habits help us move and make art.”

Kori felt steadier. “I can think again!”

Coach Lina nodded. “Now we do gentle sports play. Who wants to volunteer to help set up the soft cones?”

Kori raised a paw. “I will volunteer.” Kori placed bright cones on the rug. “Red here, yellow there.” Kori liked helping. It made the room feel friendly.

“Great volunteering,” said Coach Lina. “We’ll roll balls around the cones. Slow and safe.”

Miko rolled a blue ball. “Whoosh!” Suri tapped a tiny ball with a wing. “Tap, tap.” Tia rolled carefully. Kori rolled a green ball in a smooth line. “Roll, roll.” Everyone giggled.

Suddenly, Miko’s ball bumped the paint table. “Bump!” A cup wobbled. A drop of blue paint landed on Kori’s sunny picture. “Plop.”

Kori blinked. Their tummy tightened. “Oh no,” Miko whispered, eyes wide.

Kori took a calm breath. “It’s okay. We can fix it.” Kori looked at Miko. “Thank you for being honest.”

Miko nodded. “I am sorry.”

Kori dabbed a dry sponge. “Pat, pat.” The blue drop softened. Kori mixed a new color. “Blue and yellow make green,” Kori said. “I will paint a little leaf under the sun.” Kori smiled. “Now my sun has a garden friend!”

Coach Lina clapped softly. “That was a kind and clever solution. You used skills and stayed calm.”

After rolling balls, Coach Lina said, “Time for a stretch and a water sip.”

They reached high. “Stretch!” They touched toes. “Bend!” They breathed in and out. “In… out…” They sipped water. “Sip, sip.”

Kori felt warm and proud. “Healthy habits helped me fix a problem and have fun.”

When sports time ended, Kori cleaned the brushes. “Swish in water. Wipe on towel.” Kori volunteered to tidy the cones too. “Helping is a good habit,” Kori hummed.

At the end of the day, the art room shone. The sun painting glowed with a little green leaf. Friends gathered around.

Suri chirped, “It looks bright and brave!”

Tia said, “You took care of your body and your art.”

Miko smiled. “Thanks for forgiving me. You showed honesty helps.”

Coach Lina nodded. “Today we learned: tell the truth, take care of your body with water, healthy snacks, and stretches, move your body with safe sports, and help others. These habits build skills in art, in play, and in life.”

Kori wrapped their paws around the cup. “I feel calm, strong, and kind,” Kori said.

The friends waved goodbye. The art room smelled like soap and apples. The sky outside turned orange and soft.

Kori patted the painting. “Tomorrow, I will share my sun and my healthy habits,” Kori whispered.

The End.
