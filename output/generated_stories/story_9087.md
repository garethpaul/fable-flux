# Ruby the Helper Bird

Ruby was a little yellow bird who lived in a cozy nest outside Mrs. Chen's home office window. Every morning, Ruby loved to sing her beautiful songs while Mrs. Chen worked at her computer.

"Tweet-tweet-tweetle-dee!" Ruby sang happily, fluffing her bright feathers in the warm sunshine.

Mrs. Chen would smile and wave at Ruby through the glass window. "Good morning, little songbird!" she would say.

But one day, Ruby noticed something different. Mrs. Chen looked very tired. Her shoulders were slumped, and she kept rubbing her eyes. She had been working at her computer for hours and hours without taking a break.

"Oh my," chirped Ruby. "Mrs. Chen doesn't look happy today. I wonder what's wrong?"

Ruby pressed her tiny orange beak against the cool window glass and watched carefully. Mrs. Chen yawned a big yawn and stretched her arms up high. Then she put her head down on her desk.

"She looks so sleepy and sore," Ruby said to herself. "I wish I could help her feel better."

Ruby thought and thought. Her little bird brain worked hard to find a solution. Then suddenly, she had a wonderful idea!

"I know what to do!" Ruby chirped excitedly. "I can help Mrs. Chen remember to take healthy breaks!"

Ruby flew to her nest and gathered some of her friends - Benny the blue jay, Pip the robin, and Coco the cardinal. She told them all about Mrs. Chen's problem.

"Will you help me help Mrs. Chen?" asked Ruby.

"Of course!" said her friends. "We love helping our neighbors!"

The next morning, Ruby and her bird friends put their plan into action. Every hour, they would fly to Mrs. Chen's window and sing a special song.

"Time to stretch! Time to move! Get up and groove!" they sang in beautiful harmony. Their voices sounded like tiny bells ringing.

Mrs. Chen looked up from her computer and laughed. "What lovely music!" she said. She stood up and stretched her arms high like a tree reaching for the sky.

Ruby sang another verse: "Walk around! Touch your toes! Take deep breaths through your nose!"

Mrs. Chen smiled and did exactly what the birds suggested. She walked around her office, bent down to touch her toes, and took three deep breaths. "Ahh," she sighed. "That feels so much better!"

Every hour, the helpful birds returned with their reminder song. Mrs. Chen began to look forward to their visits. Her eyes became brighter, her back felt stronger, and she smiled more often.

"You know what?" Mrs. Chen said to Ruby one afternoon. "You birds have taught me something very important. Taking breaks helps me feel healthy and happy!"

Ruby puffed up her chest proudly. She felt warm and fuzzy inside, like sunshine filling her heart.

From that day forward, Ruby and her friends continued their community service. They helped Mrs. Chen and many other people in the neighborhood remember to take healthy breaks from their work.

"Helping others makes me feel wonderful," Ruby sang to herself. "When we work together to help our community, everyone feels better!"

Mrs. Chen even put up a little bird feeder outside her window as a thank-you gift. Now Ruby and her friends had healthy snacks while they did their important helping work.

Ruby learned that even the smallest acts of kindness can make a big difference in someone's day.

The End.
