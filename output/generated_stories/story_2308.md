# Pebble the Penguin Shares the Paints

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with shiny black feathers and a bright orange beak. Pebble loved to paint. Today, Pebble waddled into the creative art room at school. The room smelled like lemons and crayons. Soft music played. Sunlight made little squares on the floor.

Tables were covered with paper, brushes, and colorful paints. There were fuzzy pom-poms, smooth buttons, and glitter that sparkled like tiny stars. Pebble clapped little flippers. “I can’t wait to make art!” Pebble said.

Pebble saw a big tray of paint with red, yellow, blue, and green. “Ooh!” Pebble gently slid the tray closer. Pebble also grabbed a handful of brushes. “I will make a rainbow penguin,” Pebble chirped.

Friends came in too. Tia the turtle rolled in slowly. Max the mouse scampered up a stool. Lila the lamb trotted to a seat. “Hi, Pebble!” they all said.

Pebble smiled. “Hi, friends!”

“May I have a brush?” asked Tia.

“Can I use some blue?” asked Max.

Lila sniffed the glue. “It smells sweet,” she said. “Can I try the glitter after you?”

Pebble looked at the tray and the brushes. Pebble felt excited and a little worried. “I need all of these to make my rainbow penguin,” Pebble thought. Pebble hugged the brushes close. “Um… I’m using these now,” Pebble said. Pebble pulled the paint tray closer and turned the paper sideways.

Tia’s smile got small. Max’s whiskers drooped. Lila kicked a hoof softly. They looked at their empty spots. There were papers but no brushes. There were water cups but no paints near them.

Pebble dipped a brush in red. Swish, swish. The red made a big curve. Then Pebble used yellow. Swoop, swoop. But when Pebble looked up, the room felt quiet. The soft music played, but the friends were not chatting. Pebble’s belly felt wiggly, like when sliding on ice too fast.

Pebble’s teacher, Ms. Dot, came by. She had a polka-dot scarf. “Hello, artists,” she said gently. She looked at the table. “Pebble, I see you have many brushes and the big paint tray.”

Pebble nodded. “I need them,” Pebble said, but the words felt heavy, like a big snowball.

Ms. Dot knelt. “Our art room is for everyone,” she said. “Sharing lets all friends make art. When we share, we all feel happy and included.”

Pebble looked at Tia’s empty flippers, Max’s quiet paws, and Lila’s droopy ears. Pebble’s heart thumped. “I did not share,” Pebble whispered. Pebble took a deep breath. The air smelled like paper and clean water. Pebble’s eyes stung a little, like they might cry.

Pebble stood up and waddled to the middle of the table. Pebble took initiative. “Friends,” Pebble said in a clear voice, “I am sorry. I kept all the brushes and paints. That was not kind. I want to share now.”

Tia’s eyes got bright. “Thank you, Pebble,” she said.

Max squeaked, “That makes me feel better.”

Lila nodded. “I like to make art together.”

Pebble put the big paint tray in the center. Pebble passed out brushes—one to Tia, one to Max, one to Lila, and one kept for Pebble. Pebble pushed the water cups so everyone could reach. “We can take turns with the glitter too,” Pebble added. “Who would like blue first?”

Max raised a tiny paw. “Me, please!”

“Here you go,” Pebble said, sliding the blue closer. Pebble’s chest felt warm, like sunshine on snow.

They began to paint together. Swish, dab, dot! Tia made a slow, smooth turtle shell with green circles. Max painted a tiny cheese moon with yellow stars. Lila made soft, fluffy clouds with white puffs. Pebble painted the rainbow penguin again, but this time, Pebble waited for colors and shared the brushes.

“Can I try the red?” Pebble asked.

“Yes,” said Lila, “after I finish this heart.” She smiled and passed the red. “Thank you for asking.”

“Glitter time!” Max squeaked, shaking the jar gently. “We should pour a little,” Tia reminded, “so it does not make a mess.”

Pebble nodded. “Let’s use a tiny spoon.” Pebble found a small spoon and showed how to sprinkle just a sparkle. The glitter caught the light. It looked like snowflakes in the sun.

Ms. Dot smiled. “You solved the problem,” she said. “You included everyone.”

Pebble felt proud. “I am happy we can all make art,” Pebble said. Pebble looked at the rainbow penguin now. It had a shiny blue belly from Max, a green hat from Tia, and a red heart from Lila. Pebble giggled. “My penguin has gifts from friends!”

When clean-up time came, they helped each other. Pebble washed brushes, swish-swish in the water. Tia wiped the table with a soft cloth. Max put the caps back on the glue. Lila carried papers to the drying rack.

“Thank you for sharing,” Tia said.

“Thank you for saying sorry,” Max added.

“Thank you for taking action,” Lila said. “You had good initiative.”

Pebble flapped with joy. “I learned something,” Pebble said. “Sharing helps everyone feel included. Saying sorry makes hearts feel better. Taking action fixes problems.”

They all tapped flippers, paws, and hooves. Then they looked at their art, sparkly and bright.

Pebble whispered, “Art is more fun when we share.”

The End.
