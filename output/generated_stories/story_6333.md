# Della the Dolphin’s Balcony Garden Rescue

Della was a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a happy squeak. She loved to splash, flip, and sing, “Eee-eee!” One sunny morning, a gentle breeze carried the smell of mint and wet soil. Della peeked over a little wall and found a balcony garden high above the street.

“Wow!” Della squeaked. “So many green leaves! So many colors!”

The balcony garden had tiny tomatoes like red buttons, soft basil that smelled sweet, and shiny peppers that looked like little lanterns. Bees buzzed lazily. A wind chime tinkled like tiny bells. Della wiggled her tail. “I want to play and help,” she said.

On the balcony lived Mia, a small child with curly hair, and Abu, her grandpa. Mia waved. “Hi, Della! Do you like our garden?”

“I love it!” Della said. “Can I help water the plants?”

Mia laughed. “Yes, please!” She handed Della a small watering can. The water was cool. It made a soft “plink-plink” on the soil. The leaves drank it up.

Suddenly, a crinkle-crackle sound came from the corner. Della turned and saw a messy pile of bottles, cans, and old paper. A little sparrow hopped near it and chirped, “Cheep! Cheep!” It looked worried.

Mia frowned. “Someone tossed trash onto our balcony.”

“That’s not fair,” Abu said gently. “It hurts the plants and the birds.”

Della’s heart felt heavy. “This is a problem,” she said. “The garden is a home for tiny friends. We must keep it safe. We must be kind.”

Mia nodded. “What can we do?”

Della took a big breath. “We can use a plan. We can use compassion. We can help each other and help the Earth.”

Abu smiled. “A wise plan is a good start.”

They made a simple strategy:
- First, check on everyone.
- Then, sort and recycle.
- Finally, share the message kindly.

Della swam in a circle of air, pretending the balcony was the sea. “Step one,” she said softly. “Is everyone okay?”

The sparrow chirped again. “Cheep!” Della scooped it gently with a towel. “You are safe, little bird.”

Mia patted the towel. “We care about you.” The sparrow’s tiny feet relaxed. It smelled like sunshine and feathers.

“Step two,” Della said. “Let’s sort.” She tapped the bins. “Blue for paper. Green for bottles. Yellow for cans.” The cans made a happy clink. The bottles went clonk. The paper rustled like dry leaves. Della hummed, “Re-use, re-cycle, eee-eee!”

Abu showed them a crushed can. “This can can be new again,” he said. “That is justice for the Earth—giving back what we take.”

Della nodded. “Justice means being fair and kind to everyone, even our planet.”

They worked together. Mia wore little gloves. Della used her snout to push a rolling bottle toward the green bin. “Teamwork!” Mia cheered.

Soon the balcony was tidy. The air smelled fresh again: basil, mint, and sweet soil. The sparrow fluttered to the railing and sang a happy song.

“But who tossed the trash?” Mia asked. “Maybe they didn’t know.”

Della’s eyes sparkled. “Let’s be good neighbors. Let’s teach with kindness.”

“Global citizens,” Abu said, nodding. “We care for our big world, and our little balcony, too.”

They made signs with bright markers. Della painted a big blue wave. Mia drew a smiling Earth with little hands. Abu wrote kind words: “Please recycle. Plants and birds live here. Thank you.”

Della practiced what to say. Her voice was gentle, like soft rain. “Hello, friends! We share the air. We share the sky. Let’s keep it clean.”

A neighbor named Lin came by with a shy smile. “I’m sorry,” Lin said. “The wind blew my bag. I did not see.”

Della swished her tail. “Thank you for telling us. Would you like to help?”

“Yes,” Lin said. Together they folded a new recycling bag and clipped it so it would not fly away. They laughed when the clip made a tiny snap.

Mia looked proud. “We fixed the problem with a kind plan!”

Della grinned. “Compassion feels warm, like sunshine on water.”

They ended with a little garden party. Abu poured cool lemon water. The cups were reusable and smooth. Della liked the tangy taste. Mia passed crunchy carrot sticks. The wind chime sang. The sparrow danced on the railing. Even the tomatoes seemed to smile.

Della taught a song, and everyone sang:
“Be kind, be fair,
We all share air.
Sort and clean,
Keep our world green!”

As the sun set, the sky turned peach and gold. The balcony garden glowed. Della looked around at her friends. “We cared for the bird. We cleaned the garden. We shared our message,” she said. “That is what global citizens do.”

Mia hugged Della. “I learned that compassion means helping with a gentle heart.”

Abu nodded. “And strategy means making a smart plan.”

Lin lifted the recycling bag. “We can do this every day.”

Della splashed her tail with joy. “Eee-eee! Together, we make a fair and happy world.”

The garden was safe. The bird was free. The neighbors were friends. And the Earth felt just a little more loved.

The End.
