# Della Dolphin’s Laundry Room Splash

Della was a playful dolphin with a bright smile and twinkly eyes. One rainy afternoon, she visited her friend Tessa Turtle’s house. They went to the laundry room to help fold clothes. The laundry room hummed and whirred. Warm air puffed from the dryer. Soap smelled like lemons. The washing machine swished, swish-swish, like gentle waves.

“I love this room,” Della sang. “It sounds like the ocean!” She tapped her tail—tap, tap—on the cool tile floor. The tiles felt smooth, like shells under her fins.

Tessa pointed with a tiny flipper. “We have a big job. Towels, socks, shirts, and my favorite red scarf.”

Della nodded. “We can help! We will be calm leaders.” She made a big, calm breath. “In… and out.” Her breath sounded like soft bubbles: bloop… bloop…

Just then, the washing machine beeped, beep-beep! A basket tipped. Socks tumbled like little clouds. One sock landed on Della’s nose. “Oh!” she giggled. “Hello, sock.”

Tessa frowned. “We must pick them up.” She picked one sock, then another. Della scooped two with her flippers.

“Let’s make it fun,” Della said. “We will sing a calm song.” She sang quietly, “Fold, fold, nice and slow. Calm and steady, here we go.”

The dryer rumbled louder, RRRRRR. The room felt busy. A shirt got stuck on the dryer door. A bottle of soap wobbled—wibble, wobble—near the edge of the shelf.

Tessa’s eyes grew wide. “Oh no! It will fall!”

Della felt her heart go fast. Her tail twitched. The room seemed noisy and wiggly. She wanted to splash and rush. But she remembered her calm breath. “In… and out.” Bloop… bloop…

“Let’s be calm,” Della said softly. “When we are calm, we can help better.”

She spoke in a gentle voice. “Tessa, please hold the basket. I will steady the soap.”

“Okay,” said Tessa. She held the basket with both flippers. Her shell felt warm from the dryer air.

Della slid under the shelf and pressed the bottle back, nice and safe. “There,” she whispered. “All good.”

They both smiled. Then they heard a tiny sniffle. From behind the laundry basket peeked a small, shy mouse. He had a dusty gray nose and soft whiskers. He held a very tiny sock.

“Hi,” squeaked the mouse. “I’m Milo. I lost my baby sister’s sock. The room is loud. I feel… worried.”

Della looked at Milo with kind eyes. “Hi, Milo. I am Della. This is Tessa. It can be loud, but we can be calm together.”

She showed Milo. “In… and out.” Bloop… bloop…

Milo tried. “In… and out.” His whiskers wiggled. “That helps,” he said.

“Let’s use peaceful words,” Della said. “We will listen. We will take turns.”

Tessa nodded. “I will listen first.” She leaned close. “Tell us about the sock.”

“It is tiny and blue,” Milo said. “It has one white star.”

Della clapped softly, flap-flap. “Good telling! We will look slowly. Calm steps.”

They made a plan.

- Della would check near the warm dryer.
- Tessa would check under the towels.
- Milo would check behind the baskets.

They moved carefully. Della led with a calm voice. “Step, pause. Look. Breathe.” The dryer hummed like a purr. The soap smelled lemony-sweet. Warm air hugged their cheeks.

“I see something!” Tessa called softly. “A tiny blue corner under a towel.”

Della swam—slow and steady—across the tile. She lifted the towel. Out came the tiniest blue sock with one white star. It felt soft as a cloud.

Milo’s eyes sparkled. “You found it! Thank you!”

Della smiled. “We did it together. Calm breaths and kind words helped.”

Milo hugged the sock. “My baby sister will be so happy.”

Just then, the dryer beeped again. The door stuck a little. Della kept her voice steady. “We can fix this, too. Tessa, please press the button. Milo, count to three.”

“One… two… three,” Milo counted.

Click! The door opened. Warm, fluffy towels tumbled out. They smelled like sunshine. Della and Tessa folded them slow and neat. Fold, pat, smooth. Milo patted, too, with tiny paws.

When the last towel was stacked, the laundry room felt peaceful. The hum was soft. The floor was tidy. Everyone’s shoulders felt relaxed.

Tessa smiled. “You were a great leader, Della.”

Della touched her fin to her chest. “Thank you. I led with calmness. Calm helps us think. Calm helps us help.”

Milo nodded. “And calm helps me feel safe.”

Della giggled. “It helps me, too.”

They shared a quiet moment. They listened: hum… swish… warm breeze… lemon smell. It felt like a little ocean, right in the laundry room.

Della turned to her friends. “Today we learned: When things feel noisy or busy, we can stop. We can breathe. We can use gentle words. We can help each other. That is calm leadership.”

Tessa raised a folded towel like a flag. “Hip, hip, hooray for calm!”

Milo waved the tiny sock. “Hip, hip, hooray for helping!”

Della swished her tail. “Hip, hip, hooray for peaceful words!”

They laughed softly together. The room glowed cozy and bright. And the laundry was done, nice and neat.

The End.
