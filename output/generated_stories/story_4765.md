# Koby's Big Mistake

Koby the koala loved his morning routine. Every sunny day, he would wake up in his favorite eucalyptus tree and climb down to help Mama Bear tend to their beautiful backyard garden.

"Good morning, Koby!" called Mama Bear as the warm sunshine tickled his fuzzy gray ears. "Are you ready to water the flowers?"

"Yes, Mama!" Koby said with a big smile. He loved the way the cool water sparkled in the morning light and how the colorful flowers smelled so sweet.

Koby carefully carried the small watering can, feeling proud to be such a good helper. But as he walked past the recycling bins, something shiny caught his eye. It was a beautiful blue bottle that gleamed like a jewel in the sunshine.

"Ooh!" Koby gasped, forgetting all about his watering duties. He reached for the pretty bottle, but his paw knocked over the recycling bin with a loud CRASH!

Plastic bottles, aluminum cans, and paper scattered everywhere across the clean backyard. The noise was so loud that birds flew away from their nests, and Mama Bear came running.

"Oh my!" Mama Bear said, looking at the mess. Her voice sounded sad and tired.

Koby's tummy felt all twisty and uncomfortable. His calm, happy feeling was gone. "I'm sorry, Mama," he whispered, his voice very small. "I just wanted to see the pretty bottle."

Mama Bear sat down next to Koby on the soft grass. "I can see you're sorry, little one. But look at our beautiful backyard now."

Koby looked around. The mess made their special place look ugly and untidy. The flowers he was supposed to water were getting thirsty in the hot sun, and recyclables were scattered like confetti everywhere.

"I made a big mistake," Koby said, feeling tears in his eyes. "How can I fix it?"

Mama Bear's expression softened. "Well, what do you think you could do to make things better?"

Koby thought hard, his little koala brain working. "I could clean up the mess! And then do my watering job like I'm supposed to!"

"That sounds like a wonderful plan," Mama Bear said gently.

Koby worked very hard in the warm sunshine. He picked up every single bottle, can, and piece of paper. His paws got a little dirty, but he didn't mind. He sorted everything carefully back into the right recycling bins, just like Mama Bear had taught him.

"Red bin for cans, blue bin for bottles, green bin for paper," he sang quietly to himself.

When everything was clean and tidy again, Koby hurried to water the flowers. The plants seemed to dance happily as the cool water touched their leaves.

"Koby," Mama Bear said, giving him a warm, fuzzy hug, "I forgive you for making the mess. Thank you for working so hard to fix your mistake."

"Really?" Koby asked, snuggling into her soft fur.

"Really. Everyone makes mistakes sometimes. What matters is that we say sorry and try to make things right."

Koby felt that calm, happy feeling return to his tummy. The backyard looked beautiful again, the flowers were watered, and best of all, Mama Bear wasn't upset anymore.

From that day on, Koby always remembered to focus on his routine first, but he also learned that when mistakes happen, cleaning them up and saying sorry can make everything better again.

The End.
