# Benny Beaver’s Beat and Bounce

Benny was a busy beaver with bright eyes and a wide, toothy smile. Today, he waddled into the musical practice room at the community center. The room smelled like fresh wood and lemon polish. Shiny drums, tall stands, and big instruments stood in neat lines. Sunlight made the floor look like a golden river.

“Hello, room!” Benny said softly. “I’m ready to move and make music.”

He set his little tool belt on a chair. It made a gentle clink. Benny loved to build dams, but today he wanted to build a song with his body. He wiggled his whiskers. He stretched his arms wide. He stomped his tail softly on the floor. Thump. Thump. Thump.

“Ooh, that sounds like a drum,” Benny giggled. He felt the gentle bump-bump in his feet and legs. His heart thumped with him.

Coach Clara, the music helper, stepped in with a kind smile. “Good morning, Benny! Are you ready to move your body to the beat?”

“Yes!” Benny chirped. “But I am curious. How do I move safely around all these big instruments?”

“Great question,” said Coach Clara. She pointed to yellow tape on the floor. “These are safe paths. We keep hands gentle, tails soft, and feet inside the lines. Look with your eyes. Listen with your ears. Move with your body—slow at first, then faster when it’s safe.”

Benny nodded. “Eyes, ears, body. Slow to fast. I can do that.”

Clap, clap, clap! Coach Clara clapped a steady beat. Benny clapped back. His paws made a warm, snappy sound. Clap—clap—clap. He swayed side to side, feeling his shoulders loosen like leaves in the breeze.

“Now let’s tiptoe,” Coach Clara said. “Quiet toes, tall backs.”

Benny tiptoed along the safe path. His paws made soft whisper steps. Tap… tap… tap… He passed a shiny tuba and a sleepy drum. He kept his tail tucked close so it wouldn’t bump anything. He felt proud. “I am safe and moving!”

Then the door opened, and three friends peeked in.

“Hi, Benny!” said Dot the Duck, flapping tiny wings.

“Hi!” squeaked Pip the Mouse, ears twitching.

“Hi!” hummed Gus the Grasshopper, rubbing his legs with a zzzip sound.

“Come move with me!” Benny said. “We will follow the lines and the beats.”

Coach Clara smiled. “Teamwork time. Let’s listen for sounds and move our bodies to match. Remember: Curiosity is great, and safety is smart.”

She tapped a triangle. Ting! The sound was bright like a star. Dot the Duck waddled in a gentle zigzag. “Waddle-waddle-ting!” she sang.

Coach Clara brushed the drum with soft sticks. Shhhh-rum. It sounded like a soft rain. Pip the Mouse tiptoed even quieter. “Tip… tip… tip…” he whispered. His whiskers tickled.

Gus the Grasshopper listened to the maracas. Chika-chika, shake-shake. He hopped in tiny hops, not too high, just safe and small. “Boing-boing,” he grinned. His feet landed on the tape lines.

Benny watched and felt his body wake up. “I want to try a beaver move!” he said. He made a circle with his arms like he was gathering twigs. Scoop, scoop. He swayed his hips. Swoosh. He tapped his tail in a gentle pattern. Thump, thump—pause—thump.

“That’s a great pattern,” Coach Clara said. “Everyone, can you copy Benny’s beaver beat?”

The friends tried. Scoop, scoop. Swoosh. Thump, thump—pause—thump.

Dot’s wings rustled like paper. Pip’s paws made tiny pats. Gus’s legs buzzed softly. Together, they made a cozy forest sound inside the music room. Benny’s chest felt warm and brave.

Suddenly, Benny saw a tall stack of cymbals on a wobbly stand. His curiosity tingled. “What sound do those make?” he wondered. He took one step… then stopped. He remembered: Eyes, ears, body. Slow to fast. He looked at Coach Clara.

“May I try the cymbals?” he asked.

“Yes,” Coach Clara said, “with two hands, gentle touch, and I will hold the stand.”

Benny smiled. He walked the tape path. He kept his tail in. He took a slow breath. He lifted one cymbal just a little and brushed it. Sssshhhiiing. The sound was bright and smooth, like sliding across ice. His ears tingled. He set it back with care.

“Wow,” whispered Pip.

“So shiny,” said Dot.

“Nice teamwork,” said Gus.

Coach Clara clapped a soft beat. “Now let’s make a movement song together. We will stack safe moves like blocks.”

Benny started. “Scoop, scoop.”

Dot added, “Waddle-waddle.”

Pip added, “Tip-tip.”

Gus added, “Boing-boing.”

Coach Clara added, “Ting—shhhh—chika!”

They moved in a circle, bodies flowing. They stopped at corners, checked the lines, and smiled at each other. Benny felt strong, smart, and kind. He could feel his muscles working and his breath moving in and out like gentle waves.

After the song, everyone sat on the soft rug. Their cheeks were pink and happy. Benny sipped cool water. It tasted like the first drop of rain.

Coach Clara asked, “What did we learn today?”

Benny raised his paw. “Moving makes my body happy and strong. Curiosity helps me try new things. Safety keeps friends and instruments safe. And teamwork makes our music better.”

“Perfect,” said Coach Clara.

Benny stood up and did one last gentle tail tap. Thump, thump—pause—thump. “I can move with care, I can move with joy, and I can move with friends,” he said.

Everyone cheered. The golden floor glowed. The room hummed like a friendly song.

The End.
