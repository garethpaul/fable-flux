# Oliver the Owl Makes Things Right

High up in a cozy attic, where moonbeams danced through dusty windows, lived Oliver the Owl. His big yellow eyes sparkled like golden coins, and his soft brown feathers rustled gently when he moved.

Every night, Oliver had a special routine. First, he would arrange his favorite books in neat rows. Then, he would organize his collection of shiny buttons by color. Finally, he would practice his flying loops around the wooden beams.

But one evening, Oliver felt grumpy. His tummy rumbled, and he was tired from a long day. When his friend Squeaky the Mouse came to visit, Oliver didn't feel like sharing his space.

"Hello, Oliver!" squeaked the little mouse cheerfully. "May I read one of your books?"

"No!" hooted Oliver loudly. "These are MY books! Go away!"

Squeaky's whiskers drooped sadly. "But Oliver, we always read together..."

"I don't care!" Oliver flapped his wings rudely. "I want to be alone!"

Poor Squeaky scurried away with tears in his tiny black eyes. The attic felt suddenly very quiet and empty.

Oliver tried to continue his routine, but something felt wrong. The books didn't look as colorful. The buttons didn't shine as brightly. Even his flying loops felt wobbly and sad.

"Hoo-hoo," Oliver sighed deeply. The sound echoed in the lonely attic.

As the night grew darker, Oliver realized what was missing. His friend's happy squeaks usually made everything more fun. Squeaky's little paws helped sort the buttons faster. And when they read together, the stories seemed more magical.

"Oh my," Oliver whispered to himself. "I was very mean to my friend. I hurt his feelings when he just wanted to spend time with me."

Oliver's wise old heart knew exactly what he needed to do. He had to make amends – which means saying sorry and making things better.

The next evening, Oliver flew down to Squeaky's tiny hole in the wall. He knocked gently with his wing.

"Squeaky? It's Oliver. May I please come in?"

Squeaky peeked out cautiously. "Are you still grumpy?"

"No, my dear friend," Oliver said softly. "I came to say I'm very sorry. I was mean to you when you were being kind. That wasn't right."

Oliver's feathers fluffed up with nervousness. "I brought you something special."

From under his wing, Oliver pulled out a beautiful blue button – his very favorite one from his collection.

"This is for you," Oliver said. "To show you how sorry I am, and how much your friendship means to me."

Squeaky's eyes grew wide with wonder. "Really? Your special blue button?"

"Yes," Oliver nodded. "And I was hoping... would you like to come read stories with me again? I promise to share and be kind."

Squeaky's whiskers twitched with joy. "Of course! I missed reading with you!"

Together, they flew up to the attic. Oliver had used his creativity to make the space even more welcoming. He had arranged soft cushions for Squeaky and placed the books where they could both reach them easily.

From that night on, Oliver remembered that saying sorry and making amends when we hurt someone is one of the most important things we can do. And their friendship became stronger and more wonderful than ever before.

The End.
