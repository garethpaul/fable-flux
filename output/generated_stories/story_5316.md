# The Quiet Hop

Kiki was a joyful kangaroo joey. She loved to hop-hop-hop. Her tail swished, and her soft paws thumped the floor. Today, Kiki’s mama worked in the home office. The room smelled like fresh paper and warm tea. The computer made a gentle hum, like a sleepy bee.

Kiki peeked in. “Hi, Mama!” she whispered. “Can we play?”

Mama smiled and pressed a finger to her lips. “Hi, Kiki. I’m happy to see you. I have a work call soon. It is quiet time. After the call, it will be play time.”

Kiki bounced once. “But I want to hop now,” she said. Her ears wiggled.

Mama scooted her chair close. “Let’s make a plan,” she said kindly. “We can use peaceful words and a routine. Our routine helps us. First work, then play.”

Kiki took a big breath. She heard the clock tick-tick. She smelled the sweet tea steam. It made her feel warm and calm. “First work, then play,” she repeated.

Mama drew a simple chart on a sticky note. She drew a little clock, then a ball. “When the little hand is here,” she said, pointing, “work is done. Then we hop together.”

Kiki nodded. “Okay.” She liked the picture plan.

Mama put on her headphones. “Thank you for helping, Kiki,” she said. “You can play with soft toys in the cozy corner.”

Kiki tiptoed to the soft rug by the bookshelf. She found her plush koala and a stack of big blocks. The rug felt fuzzy under her paws. She built a tall tower, block by block. Tap. Tap. Tap. She made her taps soft.

Then Kiki’s happy feet forgot. Her tail swished faster. She wanted to do a big hop. Her legs wiggled. Her heart thumped. She bounced—thump!

Mama’s eyes looked up. The call was still on. Kiki saw Mama’s gentle hand signal: palm down, slow-slow. Kiki froze. Her cheeks felt warm. “Oh,” she whispered to herself. “Quiet feet.”

Kiki pressed her paws to her chest. She took a slow breath. In… and out. She counted softly, “One, two, three.” She watched the sticky note routine. First work, then play. She wanted to help.

But a problem came. The tower wobbled. It leaned like a sleepy tree. “Oh no,” Kiki whispered. She wanted Mama. She wanted help now.

Kiki thought, I can stomp and cry. Or I can use peaceful words.

She stood up tall and walked to the desk with slow feet. “Mama,” she said in a small, calm voice. “I have a problem. My tower is wobbly. I feel sad. Can you help when your call is done?”

Mama smiled with her eyes and whispered, “Thank you for using kind words, Kiki. I hear you. I will help right after the call.”

Kiki felt a warm glow in her tummy. She went back to the rug. She tried a new idea. She put big blocks on the bottom and small blocks on top. Tap. Tap. Tap. The tower stood straight. “I did it!” she whispered.

The clock went tick-tick-tick. The room smelled like crayons now, because Kiki opened her crayon box. She made a picture of a big sun and a smiling kangaroo. She wrote, in wiggly letters, “First work, then play.” The crayons felt smooth in her paws. Scratch-scratch went the orange crayon.

At last, Mama took off her headphones. She stretched. “Work is done,” she said softly.

Kiki’s ears popped up. “Is it play time now?” she asked.

“It is!” Mama said. “Thank you for waiting and helping. You used peaceful words. You followed our routine. That is cooperation.”

Kiki beamed. “I tried. I took a breath. I asked for help with calm words.”

Mama clapped quietly. “That was brave and kind.”

They made a new plan together. “Let’s hop five big hops,” said Mama, “then build a tower, then read a book.” She drew three simple pictures: a hopping joey, a stack of blocks, and a book with a star.

Kiki giggled. “Hop time!” She and Mama hopped on the soft rug. Hop! Hop! Hop! The floor felt bouncy like a cloud. They counted together. “One, two, three, four, five!” They laughed and fell onto the rug with a gentle whoosh.

Next, they built an even taller tower. “Big blocks on the bottom,” Kiki said. “Small blocks on top!” The tower stood strong. “Teamwork,” Mama said.

Last, they snuggled in the reading chair. Mama smelled like tea and soap. Kiki’s fur was warm and cozy. Mama read a short story in a soft voice. The room felt calm and happy.

Kiki whispered, “Peaceful talk helps. Routines help. We can work together.”

Mama kissed Kiki’s forehead. “Yes,” she said. “When we speak kindly, take turns, and follow our plan, everyone feels good.”

Kiki smiled and traced the picture plan with her paw. “First work,” she said, “then play.” She hugged Mama. “We did it.”

The End.
