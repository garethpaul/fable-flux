# Miko the Mouse and the Garden Shed Cleanup

Miko was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright whiskers. He lived near a sunny garden. One warm morning, he squeaked, “I will explore the garden shed today!”

The garden shed smelled like pine wood and dirt. Sunlight slid through dusty windows, making gold stripes on the floor. Miko’s tiny paws felt cool on the wood. He heard drip, drip from a leaky sink and the soft buzz of a sleepy fly.

“Hello?” Miko called. “Is anyone here?”

A gentle voice answered, “I am here,” from a corner. It was Buzzy the bumblebee. Buzzy’s stripes glowed like little sunshine bands. “I am stuck,” Buzzy said sadly. His wing brushed a sticky plastic ring on the floor. It was a ring from a bottle. It pinched his wing.

“Oh no!” Miko gasped. “I will help.” He took a deep breath and tugged. Tug, tug, tug. The plastic stretched and snapped. “You are free!”

“Thank you,” Buzzy hummed. “That ring should be in the right bin.”

Miko looked around. He saw crumpled paper, old seed packets, a cracked flower pot, and more plastic rings. The shed felt messy and loud now. Clink, rustle, clatter. Miko’s heart beat fast.

“This is not safe,” Miko said. “Someone could get hurt.” His whiskers drooped.

Just then, a wooden door creaked. Ellie the young gardener peeked in. She had dirt on her cheeks and a bright green hat. “Oh my,” Ellie said softly. “This shed is a mess.”

Miko felt a spark of anger. “Ellie!” he squeaked. “Did you leave this trash? Buzzy got stuck!”

Ellie’s eyes grew wide. “I… I think I did,” she said. “I was rushing. I forgot to clean. I am sorry.” Her shoulders sank. “I did not mean to hurt anyone.”

Miko’s tiny chest felt tight. He wanted to stomp. He wanted to shout. But he remembered a trick for big feelings. “Stop,” he told himself. “Breathe.” He put a paw on his tummy. In, out. In, out. His body felt softer, like warm bread.

“I feel upset,” Miko said in a calm voice. “The trash made danger.”

Ellie nodded. “You are right. Thank you for telling me. I want to fix it. Can we clean together?”

Miko blinked. “Yes,” he said. “We can make a plan.”

They made three buckets. Ellie drew pictures on each one. She drew a bottle on the recycle bucket. She drew a banana peel on the compost bucket. She drew a broken shovel on the trash bucket.

“Sort, sort, sort,” sang Miko. He liked to sing while he worked. “Paper here. Plastic there. Seeds and leaves in compost care!”

Buzzy buzzed along. “Bzzz-zz-zz!” He carried tiny bits of paper in his fuzzy feet. Miko picked up the plastic rings. “We will cut them,” he said, and Ellie carefully snipped each ring so no creature could get stuck again.

They found a wet cardboard box. Squish! It made a funny sound. They laughed. They found an old glove that smelled like grass. Sniff, sniff. “Pee-yew,” Miko giggled. “Trash!”

Ellie opened the window a little wider. Fresh air blew in, cool and sweet. The shed felt brighter. The drip, drip stopped as Ellie turned the tap tight. “Thank you, water,” Ellie said. “We will not waste you.”

Miko discovered a tiny seed packet under a broom. “Sunflower seeds!” he squeaked. “Can we plant them?”

“Yes,” Ellie said. “Let’s plant them outside. Sunflowers love sun.” They stepped out for a moment. The garden felt warm, and the soil was soft like cake. Pat, pat, pat. Miko’s paws pressed the seeds in. Ellie watered gently. Splash, splash. “We care for our world,” Ellie said. “We plant, not toss.”

Back in the shed, everything looked neat. The buckets were lined up. The broom stood tall. The floor gleamed like a smooth river stone.

Buzzy did a happy loop-de-loop. “Safe and tidy!” he buzzed.

Miko’s heart felt big and light. “I am proud,” he said. “We used calm words. We used honest words. We worked as a team.”

Ellie smiled at Miko. “Thank you for telling me the truth. Thank you for helping. I will make new shed rules.”

She hung a little sign by the door. It showed pictures so even small friends could understand:
- Cut rings
- Sort trash, recycling, and compost
- Save water
- Put tools away
- Plant and care

Miko read the pictures and clapped his tiny paws. “These are good rules,” he said.

Ellie knelt down. “I was careless,” she said. “But today I learned. I will be careful now.”

Miko nodded. “We all make mistakes. We can fix them with kind hearts.”

They stood in the doorway. The sun felt warm, and the air smelled like mint and soil. A robin sang, “Cheer-up! Cheer-up!” Miko sang back, “Clean-up! Clean-up!”

That night, Miko curled up in his nest with a happy sigh. He thought of Buzzy flying free, the clean shed, and the seeds tucked in the earth.

He whispered, “When we take care of our things, we take care of our friends and our world.”

The End.
