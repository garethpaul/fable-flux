# Mango the Brave and the Table Treasure

Mango was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and a big smile. One sunny morning, Mango padded into the dining room. The room smelled like warm toast and sweet bananas. Light sparkled on the spoons, and the wooden table felt smooth under Mango’s hand.

On the table sat a shiny, silver bowl. Inside the bowl was a tiny golden button that glowed like a little sun. Mango gasped. “Ooo! A treasure!” he whispered, tail swishing. “I will get it!”

But there was a problem. The bowl was in the middle of the big table. It was too far for Mango’s short arms.

Mango took a deep breath. “I am brave,” he said. “I can solve this.” He tapped his chin. “I will use my imagination!”

He looked around. Chairs stood like tall helpers. A napkin hung over the edge, soft and white. The fork and spoon lay like shiny sticks. Mango smiled. “We can all work together.”

Just then, Mama came in with a warm pot of oatmeal. “Good morning, Mango,” she said. “I see you looking at the bowl.”

“Yes, Mama,” Mango said. “There is a treasure inside! I have a goal. I want to reach it. But I cannot get to the middle.”

Mama nodded. “That is a good goal,” she said. “Let’s use peaceful words and make a safe plan. Remember to respect the table and the things on it.”

Mango placed his hand on his heart. “I will be gentle and careful,” he said. “I will ask for help.”

He turned to the chairs. “Hello, chairs,” Mango said softly. “May I borrow you to make a bridge? I will be kind. I will not jump hard.”

The chairs, in Mango’s imagination, creaked happily. “You may borrow us,” they seemed to say.

Mango waved to the napkin. “Hello, napkin. May I use you as a soft road?”

The napkin fluttered in the breeze from the window. In Mango’s mind, it whispered, “Yes.”

Mama smiled. “Good asking, Mango. That shows respect.”

Mango set his goal out loud. “Step one: move the chairs closer. Step two: lay the napkin like a road. Step three: reach for the bowl. Step four: say thank you.”

He looked at Mama. “Is this a good plan?”

“That is a lovely plan,” Mama said. “I will be your safety helper.”

Together, they gently scooted two chairs side by side. The floor made a soft shhh sound. Mango laid the napkin across the chair tops. It felt smooth and cool under his little fingers.

Mango stood on the first chair. His toes curled on the seat. He took a brave breath. “I can do this,” he whispered. He reached out, but the bowl was still far away.

Mango felt a tiny worry in his tummy. “I feel a little scared,” he said.

Mama nodded. “Thank you for sharing your feeling. Let’s use peaceful talk with ourselves. Say: ‘I am brave. I can try again.’ Also, we can change the plan.”

Mango’s eyes brightened. “Change the plan!” He looked around and saw a wooden spoon. “Hello, spoon,” Mango said. “May I use you as a long hand? I will be gentle.”

In Mango’s imagination, the spoon gleamed and said, “Yes, I like gentle.”

Mango added a new step. “Step three: use the spoon to slide the bowl closer.” He smiled. “We are good at coordination, Mama!”

“Yes,” Mama said. “We work together. That is coordination.”

Mango lay on his tummy across the napkin road. His fur tickled his arms. He could hear the soft hum of the fridge and smell the sweet oatmeal. He stretched the spoon toward the bowl. Tap, tap. The spoon touched the shiny bowl. Clink!

“Slow and gentle,” Mango whispered. He nudged the bowl. It slid a little. Mango nudged again. Clink, clink. The bowl came closer and closer until it sat near the edge.

Mango sat up carefully. He reached out with his little hands and lifted the tiny golden button. It felt warm and smooth. The sunlight made it sparkle. Mango’s heart felt big and happy. “I did it!” he cheered.

He turned to the chairs, the napkin, and the spoon. “Thank you, helpers,” he said. “You were very kind.” In his imagination, they all bowed and giggled.

Mama clapped softly. “Mango, you used your imagination to solve a problem. You spoke with respect. You used peaceful words. You made a plan and changed it when you needed to. That is being courageous.”

Mango nodded. “I had a goal, and I reached it with help.”

He placed the golden button back in the bowl. “This treasure will stay here for everyone to see,” he said. “We can share the sparkle.”

Mama hugged Mango. The hug felt warm like a soft blanket. “What did you learn today?” she asked.

Mango smiled. “I learned that imagination helps my brain make good ideas. I learned to use peaceful words and to ask for help with respect. I learned to make a plan and work together.”

Mama touched his nose. “Wonderful.”

Mango looked at the shining bowl and the bright dining room. “I am Mango the Brave,” he said. “I can use my mind, my words, and my friends to solve problems.”

The End.
