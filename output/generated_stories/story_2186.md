# Shelly's Rainbow Garden

Shelly the turtle peeked out from her cozy shell. The morning sun warmed the balcony garden where she lived. All around her grew the most wonderful plants she had ever seen.

Mrs. Chen, who took care of the garden, was watering the bright red tomatoes. "Good morning, little Shelly," she said softly. Mrs. Chen always spoke in two languages - English and Chinese. "These tomatoes will make delicious soup!"

Shelly pulled her head back into her shell. She was too shy to say hello back.

Next door, Mr. Rodriguez was picking purple eggplants. "¡Buenos días, tortuga!" he called out cheerfully. The Spanish words sounded like music to Shelly's ears. "These berenjenas will be perfect for dinner!"

Shelly wanted to learn what those beautiful words meant, but she was too scared to ask.

Later, Mrs. Patel came to tend her curry plants. The spicy, sweet smell made Shelly's nose tickle. "Namaste, little one," Mrs. Patel said gently in Hindi. "Would you like to smell these fresh herbs?"

Shelly's tummy rumbled. All the different foods smelled so good! But she stayed hidden in her shell.

As the days passed, Shelly watched her neighbors share their special foods with each other. Mrs. Chen brought steaming dumplings to Mrs. Patel. Mr. Rodriguez shared his colorful salsa with Mrs. Chen. Mrs. Patel gave everyone fragrant rice with golden spices.

"They all eat different foods," Shelly thought. "They speak different words. But they're all so kind to each other."

One sunny afternoon, Shelly noticed something terrible. A big storm had knocked over Mrs. Chen's tomato plants! The red fruits were scattered all over the balcony.

Shelly's heart felt heavy. Mrs. Chen looked so sad. But what could a small, shy turtle do to help?

Slowly, Shelly stuck out one little leg. Then another. She began picking up the tomatoes with her mouth, one by one. They tasted sweet and juicy.

"Oh my!" said Mrs. Chen, clapping her hands. "Xièxie, Shelly! Thank you!"

Mr. Rodriguez saw Shelly helping. "¡Qué buena tortuga!" he said with a big smile. "What a good turtle!"

Mrs. Patel joined them too. "You are very helpful, beta," she said warmly.

Shelly felt proud and brave. "What do those words mean?" she asked in her tiny voice.

Mrs. Chen knelt down. "Xièxie means thank you in Chinese."

"¡Qué buena means 'how good' in Spanish!" added Mr. Rodriguez.

"And beta means 'dear child' in Hindi," explained Mrs. Patel.

Shelly's eyes sparkled. "Can you teach me more words? And can I try your special foods?"

All three neighbors smiled. They spent the afternoon teaching Shelly new words and sharing tiny tastes of their delicious foods. The Chinese dumplings were soft and warm. The Spanish rice was colorful and zesty. The Indian curry was creamy and full of wonderful spices.

"Every family has special foods and words," said Mrs. Chen.

"But we all share the same garden," added Mr. Rodriguez.

"And the same big hearts," finished Mrs. Patel.

From that day on, Shelly wasn't shy anymore. She learned that being different made everyone special. She helped her neighbors every day and learned new words in three languages. Most importantly, she discovered that kindness tastes good in every language.

The End.
