# Robo's Big Decision

In a cozy nursery filled with soft blankets and colorful toys, lived a special robot dog named Robo. His silver fur sparkled in the warm sunlight streaming through the window, and his bright blue eyes glowed with kindness. Robo loved helping everyone in the nursery.

One sunny morning, Robo heard crying coming from different corners of the room. "Woof! Someone needs help," he said, his tail wagging with a gentle whirring sound.

Baby Emma was in her crib, reaching for her favorite teddy bear that had fallen on the floor. "Wah! Teddy!" she cried, her little hands stretching toward the fuzzy brown bear.

At the same time, toddler Max was sitting by the toy box, sniffling loudly. His puzzle pieces were scattered everywhere. "I can't do it!" Max said sadly. "It's too hard!"

And near the reading corner, little Sophie was trying to reach a book on the high shelf. "I want the bunny story," she said with tears in her eyes. "But it's too high up!"

Robo's circuits buzzed as he thought carefully. "Three friends need help, but I can only help one at a time," he said to himself. "What should I do first?"

Robo sat down and made his thinking beep-beep-beep sound. "I need to make a good decision," he said. "Let me think about this step by step."

First, he looked around the room carefully. Emma was crying the loudest and seemed the most upset. Max looked frustrated but was trying to work on his puzzle. Sophie was patient, looking hopefully at the book shelf.

"I'll help Emma first because she's the most upset," Robo decided. "Then I'll make a plan for the others."

Robo rolled over to Emma's crib with his wheels humming softly. "Don't worry, little one," he said gently. His mechanical arm stretched down and picked up the soft teddy bear. "Here's your friend!"

Emma giggled and hugged her teddy tight. "Thank you, Robo!" she gurgled happily.

Next, Robo rolled to Max. "Let me help you with your puzzle," Robo said kindly. "We can work together!" His blue eyes lit up as he helped Max sort the colorful pieces by shape and color.

"Look, Max! All the edge pieces go here, and the middle pieces go there," Robo explained patiently. Soon, Max was smiling as they built the puzzle together.

"I can do it!" Max cheered. "Thank you for showing me how to plan it out!"

Finally, Robo rolled to Sophie. His arm extended up high and gently took down the bunny book. "Here's your story," he said warmly.

"Robo, you're the best helper ever!" Sophie said, hugging the book.

As Robo looked around the peaceful nursery, he felt happy inside his circuits. All three friends were content and playing quietly.

"I'm glad I stopped to think before acting," Robo said to himself. "Making good decisions means thinking about who needs help most, then planning how to help everyone."

The nursery teacher, Ms. Kelly, smiled as she watched. "Robo, you showed wonderful decision-making today. You thought carefully, made a plan, and shared your kindness with everyone."

Robo's tail wagged proudly. "When we think before we act and plan our steps, we can help more friends and make everyone happy!"

From that day on, whenever someone needed help, Robo always remembered to stop, think, and make the best decision he could.

The End.
