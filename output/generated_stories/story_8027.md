# Poppy Parrot’s Tidy Tune

Poppy was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a tail like a paintbrush. She loved the musical practice room. It smelled like fresh wood and lemon polish. Drums, shakers, and tambourines rested on soft shelves. Sunlight made shiny dots on the floor.

“Good morning, room!” Poppy chirped. “Are you ready for music?” Her voice rang like a small bell.

Poppy was curious. She liked to ask questions. She tilted her head and looked at a tall stack of cymbals. “What sound do you make today?” she asked, tapping one with her beak. CLINK!

She hopped to the piano and pressed a key. Plink! She wiggled her toes. “Ooh, that note feels like a tickle!”

On the table nearby lay a list from Miss Lark, the music teacher. The list said: Sweep floor, wipe drums, sort shakers, stack books, and water the plant.

Poppy read it out loud, slowly. “Sweep… wipe… sort… stack… water.” She peeped at the drum. “But music first?” Her wings fluttered. She wanted to dance and sing.

Then she saw maracas rolling toward the door. Tap-tap—roll. Tap-tap—roll. “Oh no!” Poppy cried. “The room is messy. Someone could trip.” She ruffled her feathers. “Chores can help. Chores keep us safe.”

Poppy took a deep breath. “I can do it. I am small, but I am strong.” She puffed up with self-confidence. “And I can make chores fun!”

She began with the broom. Swish, swish. The straw made a soft whisper on the floor. Poppy made a beat with her feet. Tap—swish, tap—swish. “Dance with me, broom!” she sang. The dust bunnies scooted into a neat little pile.

Next, she got a cloth and a gentle spray. Spritz, spritz! The drums smelled fresh, like lemons and rain. Poppy wiped in circles. “Round and round, shiny and sound,” she hummed. Her wings moved in time. Wipe-wipe, pat-pat.

Then came the shakers. There were red ones, blue ones, and tiny ones like ladybugs. Poppy was curious. She shook each one to hear its voice. “Chi-chi!” “Cha-cha!” “Chick!” She giggled. “You go in the soft basket, little chick shakers. You go in the big basket, loud cha-chas.” She sorted by size and sound. “This is coordination,” she told herself proudly. “My eyes see, my wings move, and my feet stay steady.”

A book slid off a chair—thump. Poppy jumped. “Oops!” She picked it up and stacked it with the other music books. Big to small. Straight and tall. She tapped the top. “Good job, books.”

Last was the plant by the window. Its leaves were floppy. “You look thirsty,” Poppy said softly. She poured just a little. Plip, plip. The soil drank it up. “There. A happy sip.”

Poppy looked around. The maracas were in baskets. The drums were shiny. The floor was clear and smooth. The room seemed to smile.

She felt warm and proud inside. “I did my chores,” she said. “Now the room is safe for music.” Her heart beat like a drum, but in a calm way.

“Time to dance!” Poppy chirped. She put on a gentle beat with a small drum. Dum, dum—dum, dum. She stepped side to side. Step, step—tap. She waved her wings. Whoosh! The clean floor felt silky under her toes. She twirled once. Round she went, light as a leaf.

Miss Lark opened the door and peeked in. Her eyes widened. “Oh, Poppy! The room looks wonderful. It smells so nice.”

Poppy blushed under her feathers. “I did the chores,” she said. “I made a tidy tune.”

Miss Lark listened. “I can hear it! Swish, spritz, wipe, shake, stack, plip.” She laughed softly. “You turned chores into music.”

Poppy’s eyes sparkled. “Chores help us. When we clean and sort, we can move and dance without tripping. And we feel good inside.”

Miss Lark nodded. “That is a great lesson. Curiosity helped you start. Coordination helped you sort and stack. And your self-confidence kept you going.”

Poppy puffed up a little. “I was curious about every sound. I used my wings and feet together. And I told myself, ‘I can do it!’”

Miss Lark clapped. “Let’s celebrate with a dance.”

They both tapped their toes. Tap—tap—tap! Poppy shook a maraca—chi-chi! Miss Lark played the drum—dum, dum! The clean room echoed with happy sound. The sun made golden dots on their faces.

After the dance, Poppy put the maraca back in its basket. She smiled. “Chores first, fun next. That’s my tidy tune.”

Miss Lark smiled too. “That’s a smart plan, Poppy.”

Poppy fluffed her feathers and gave a little bow. “I like helping. When I help, I feel brave and kind.”

The room was calm and ready for the next song. Poppy perched on the piano, humming a soft, sweet note. She felt peaceful and proud.

Lesson: Doing chores can be fun and helpful. Chores keep us safe, make room for play, and build our confidence.

The End.
