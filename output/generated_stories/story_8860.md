# Shelly's Rooftop Dance Lesson

Shelly the turtle peeked her head out of her shell. She was on the big rooftop of the city building. The sun felt warm on her green shell. Cars honked far below, but up here it was peaceful.

Shelly loved this quiet rooftop. She came here every day to eat lettuce leaves. But today was different. Today she heard something new.

*Tap tap tap* went little feet nearby.

Shelly pulled her head back into her shell. She was very shy. "Who could that be?" she whispered to herself.

The tapping sounds got closer. Shelly listened carefully with her ears hidden in her shell.

"One, two, three, spin!" said a cheerful voice.

Slowly, Shelly poked just her eyes out. She saw a small gray pigeon dancing on the rooftop. The pigeon's feet made soft tapping sounds on the concrete.

"Oh!" gasped Shelly, pulling back into her shell.

The pigeon stopped dancing. "Hello there! I heard someone. Are you hiding?"

Shelly's shell felt safe and cozy. But the pigeon's voice sounded kind. Very slowly, Shelly stuck her head out again.

"I'm Shelly," she said in a tiny voice. "I didn't mean to watch you."

"That's okay!" chirped the pigeon. "I'm Pete. I love to dance up here. The rooftop has the best echo for my tap dancing."

Shelly listened as Pete explained. "I practice every day. Dancing makes me happy. Would you like to learn?"

"Me?" Shelly's eyes grew wide. "But I'm too slow. And too shy."

Pete fluffed his gray feathers. "Dancing isn't about being fast. It's about listening to the rhythm. Listen with me."

Pete began to tap again. *Tap tap tap, tap tap tap.*

Shelly listened carefully. The sounds made a pattern. It was like music!

"Do you hear it?" Pete asked gently. "The beat goes *tap tap tap*, then pause."

Shelly nodded. She was listening so hard, she forgot to be shy.

"Now try moving just one foot," Pete said. "Listen to my tapping and move when you feel ready."

Shelly put one green foot out of her shell. She listened to Pete's rhythm. When she felt the beat, she tapped her foot once.

*Tap!*

"Perfect!" Pete cheered. "You're a natural listener!"

Shelly felt proud. She had never danced before. But listening to Pete made it easy.

"Can you teach me more?" Shelly asked, coming all the way out of her shell now.

Pete smiled with his bright orange beak. "Of course! Good dancers are good listeners first."

For the next hour, Pete taught Shelly simple dance steps. Shelly listened to every instruction. She learned that listening helped her understand the rhythm. Listening helped her know when to move and when to pause.

"Thank you, Pete," Shelly said as the sun began to set. "I learned that listening is the most important part of dancing."

"You're welcome, Shelly! And thank you for being such a good listener. That made teaching you so much fun!"

Shelly felt warm and happy. She had made a new friend by listening carefully. She had learned something new by paying attention.

From that day on, Shelly came to the rooftop every afternoon. She and Pete danced together. Shelly was still shy, but she had learned that good listening could help her learn anything.

"Listening is like a superpower," Shelly told herself happily as she tapped her feet to Pete's rhythm.

The End.
