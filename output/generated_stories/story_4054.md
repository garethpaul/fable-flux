# Sunny’s Porch Song

Sunny was a small singing bird with a bright yellow chest and a tiny, tap-tap beak. Every morning, Sunny perched on the peaceful front porch of a blue house. The porch smelled like fresh wood and warm toast. The wind chimes sang tink-tink, and the rocking chair made a soft creak, creak. Sunny loved to sing, “La-la-la! Tweet, tweet, tweet!”

One day, Sunny fluttered to the porch and took a deep breath. “Today, I will sing my best song,” Sunny said. Sunny puffed up, ready to sing.

But just then, the wind blew stronger. The welcome mat flapped. The chimes clanged, CLANG-CLANG. A broom fell with a thunk. The sounds were big and busy.

Sunny tried anyway. “La—” Sunny stopped. The song sounded wobbly. “Oh no,” Sunny whispered. “I feel a little worried.”

Sunny looked around. A gray squirrel named Squeak peeked from the porch rail. “Hi, Sunny,” said Squeak. “Why the droopy wings?”

“My song won’t come out,” Sunny said. “There are so many sounds. I feel all mixed up.”

Squeak nodded. “I hear it too. Clang, thunk, flap!” He wiggled his nose. “Maybe we can help each other.”

From the porch steps, a slow turtle named Tilda lifted her head. “Hello,” she said in a soft voice. “I like your songs, Sunny. Can I help?”

Sunny felt a warm flutter inside. Friends were here. “Yes, please,” said Sunny. “But I don’t know what to do.”

Tilda blinked slowly. “First, let’s listen,” she suggested. “When we listen, we learn.”

They all got quiet. They listened with ears and with feelings. Sunny noticed the wind chimes were too loud. The mat flapped because it wasn’t tucked. The broom was loose and wobbly.

“I hear three big sounds,” Sunny said. “Clang. Flap. Thunk.”

Squeak tapped his little paws. “Let’s make a plan. We can fix one sound at a time.”

Sunny took a breath. “I like that plan.”

Tilda smiled. “That is being prepared.”

They started with the chimes. Squeak climbed up the post. “I will tie the chimes a little shorter,” he said. “They will sing soft, not shout.”

“Thank you,” said Sunny. “I feel hopeful.”

Squeak tied a small knot. The chimes changed from CLANG-CLANG to tink-tink-tink, like tiny raindrops. Sunny’s chest felt lighter.

Next, they looked at the welcome mat. It was flapping like a butterfly. “I can help with that,” said Tilda. She pushed the mat gently with her strong shell. “We will tuck it under the door edge.”

“Working together feels good,” Sunny said. “I feel proud.”

The mat stopped flapping. It lay flat and still.

Finally, the broom. It was leaning and wobbly. Sunny tilted her head. “Maybe the broom needs a home,” she said.

Squeak pointed to a hook on the wall. “There! But it is a little high.”

Sunny flapped her wings. “I can fly!” she chirped. “I will help lift the broom.”

Sunny held the broom handle with her beak. Squeak pushed from below. Tilda steadied the bottom with her strong shell. “One, two, three,” they counted together. Up, up, up! The broom clicked onto the hook.

They all paused. They listened again. The porch sounded calm. Tink-tink. Creak-creak. A soft breeze touched Sunny’s feathers like a gentle hand.

Sunny smiled. “I feel ready now.”

“Wait,” Tilda said kindly. “How do you feel inside your body?”

Sunny closed her eyes and listened to the feelings. “My tummy is calm. My wings are not tight. My heart is happy.” She opened her eyes. “Thank you for asking.”

Squeak clapped his tiny paws. “Sing, Sunny, sing!”

Sunny took a slow, deep breath. “In and out,” she whispered. Then Sunny sang a clear, sweet song that floated over the porch and down the path. “La-la-la! Tweet, tweet, tweet!” The song was like sunshine, warm and bright.

A little child opened the blue door and smiled. “I hear you, Sunny!” the child said. “It sounds so nice.”

Sunny felt joy sparkle like morning dew. But then, a cloud of worry drifted back. “What if the wind gets wild again?” Sunny asked. “What if new noises come?”

Tilda nodded. “That can happen,” she said. “Let’s make a plan for later.”

Squeak held up a tiny stick like a pointer. “We learned three things,” he said. “One: We listen first. Two: We work together. Three: We put things in their places.”

Sunny added, “Four: We talk about our feelings.”

Tilda added, “Five: We take deep breaths.”

They all smiled. “That is good preparation,” Tilda said.

Sunny sang a softer song just for her friends. “Thank you, thank you, thank you,” the notes said.

The wind danced across the porch, but the chimes stayed gentle. The mat stayed cozy. The broom stayed safe on its hook. The rocking chair creaked, not too loud, not too quiet.

Sunny lifted her wings. “When we cooperate, we can solve problems,” she said. “I feel brave now.”

Squeak nodded. “We listened. We helped. We used our thinking.”

Tilda beamed. “We are a team.”

Sunny sang one last happy chorus, and the porch seemed to glow. The child clapped, Squeak swished his tail, and Tilda tapped her shell like a drum. They all laughed together.

Lesson: When we listen, share our feelings, and work as a team, problems feel smaller, and songs feel stronger.

The End.
