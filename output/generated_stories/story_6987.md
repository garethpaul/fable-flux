# Whiskers Solves the Splash

Whiskers was a clever cat with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, Whiskers padded into the bathroom on quiet paws. The floor felt cool and smooth. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. The mirror shone like a lake. The air smelled like soap and mint.

Whiskers liked the bathroom. It was a place of bubbles and warm towels. But today, there was a problem.

“Hey!” squeaked a tiny voice. It was Squeak the toy mouse, sitting by the sink. “The toothbrushes are fighting!”

Whiskers blinked. “Fighting? Toothbrushes?”

On the counter, Blue Brush and Red Brush were arguing. They wiggled in their cup and clacked their handles.

“I want to go first!” said Red Brush. “I am the fastest!”

“No, I go first!” said Blue Brush. “I am the cleanest!”

Whiskers sat and wrapped his tail around his paws. “This is a problem,” he said calmly. “We need to use our thinking.”

From the shelf, Bottle Bubble, the soap, chimed in, “We all want clean smiles. But shouting is not helping.”

Whiskers nodded. He placed a gentle paw between the brushes. “Let’s take deep breaths,” he said. “In... out.” He breathed slowly. Blue Brush and Red Brush tried it too. In... out. The bathroom felt quieter.

Whiskers spoke kindly. “Tell me your feelings.”

Red Brush clacked softly. “I feel mad. I want to help first. I like to work fast.”

Blue Brush hummed. “I feel sad. I want to help too. I like to be careful.”

Whiskers listened. His ears turned toward each brush. “Thank you for telling me,” he said. “You both want to help. You both care.”

Squeak the toy mouse nodded. “Both are important!”

Whiskers flicked his whiskers. “Let’s make a plan,” he said. “We can solve problems with steps. Step one: stop. Step two: share feelings. Step three: think of ideas. Step four: choose a fair idea.”

Blue Brush and Red Brush were quiet and curious now. “What ideas?” they asked.

Whiskers looked around. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. The towel felt fluffy under his paw. He thought and thought.

“I have an idea,” he said. “We can take turns. On even days, Red Brush goes first. On odd days, Blue Brush goes first.”

Red Brush tilted. “What is even?”

Whiskers tapped the wall calendar with his paw. “See the numbers? Two is even. Four is even. Six is even. Even days for Red. Odd days for Blue.”

Blue Brush swished. “But what about today?”

Whiskers smiled. “We can flip a coin... but we don’t have a coin. Hmm.” He looked at the small cup of cotton balls. “We can draw! One ball is short. One ball is long. Short goes first today.”

He made a short cotton ball and a long cotton ball. He hid them in his paws. “Pick a paw,” he purred.

Red Brush tapped the left paw. Short! “I go first today!” Red Brush cheered.

Blue Brush wiggled. “And I go second. I can wait. I can breathe.”

Bottle Bubble clapped tiny soap bubbles. Pop, pop!

Whiskers felt proud. “There. We solved it with calm voices and a fair plan.”

Just then, a small child, Mia, came into the bathroom in fuzzy slippers. She rubbed her eyes and smiled. “Good morning, Whiskers.” She patted his head. “Time to brush.”

Red Brush went first, swish, swish, swish. Minty smells filled the air. Blue Brush went second, swish, swish, swish. The mirror fogged a little with warm breath, and Mia giggled.

After brushing, Mia washed her hands. Warm water ran. Bottle Bubble sang a little song. “Scrub, scrub, scrub! Front and back! Between the fingers! Rinse!” The water felt like a soft stream. The towel felt cozy and dry.

Then Mia looked at Whiskers. “You helped,” she said. “You solved the fight.”

Whiskers purred. “We listened. We took turns. We made a plan.”

Blue Brush turned to Red Brush. “Thank you for letting me go second today.”

Red Brush replied, “Thank you for waiting. Tomorrow, you go first.”

Whiskers blinked slowly. “That is generosity,” he said. “When we share and take turns, we are kind.”

Squeak squeaked, “And clever! You used your thinking!”

Whiskers smiled. “We also took care of ourselves. Clean teeth. Clean hands. Calm hearts.”

Mia hugged Whiskers gently. He felt warm and happy. The bathroom felt peaceful. No more shouting. Only soft sounds: drip, drip, drip; swish, swish; pop, pop.

Later, a tiny rubber duck peeped from the tub. “What if we argue about bath time?” the duck asked.

Whiskers flicked his tail. “We can use the same steps. Stop. Share feelings. Think of ideas. Choose a fair plan. And be generous.”

Everyone in the bathroom nodded. The mirror shone. The faucet gave one last drip.

Whiskers curled up on the bath mat and closed his eyes. He felt proud, and the room felt safe.

Today, Whiskers discovered that problems can be solved with calm words, careful thinking, and kind hearts.

The End.
