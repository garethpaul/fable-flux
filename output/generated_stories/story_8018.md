# Poppy the Parrot Plans a Picnic

Poppy was a bright, creative parrot with rainbow feathers. She lived with a kind family. One sunny morning, Poppy sat on a chair in the dining room. The table smelled like warm toast and sweet jam. Sunlight sprinkled across the shiny spoons. Poppy tapped her beak on the wooden table. Tap-tap-tap!

“I have an idea!” Poppy chirped. “Let’s help our neighbors. Let’s make a picnic at the park!”

Her friend Max, a small boy with curly hair, clapped his hands. “A picnic! Yay!”

Luna, Max’s big sister, peeked around the doorway. “A community picnic? We can share food and games with everyone.”

Poppy puffed up with pride. “Yes! I can plan it. I am creative!” Her feathers felt warm and glowy.

Mama came in with a basket of fresh bread. The bread smelled soft and toasty. “That sounds kind,” Mama said. “A picnic is a good way to help the community. Some neighbors feel lonely.”

Poppy nodded. “We can make cards. We can sing. We can clean the park too!” She hopped from chair to chair, excited.

Max grinned. “Let’s start now!”

They spread paper and crayons on the dining room table. Crayons rolled and made soft rattles. Poppy drew big smiling suns and tiny birds. Luna wrote “Community Picnic Today!” in big, friendly letters.

Poppy flapped. “I will plan everything,” she said. “I will pick the songs. I will pick the food. I will pick the games.” She felt busy and important.

But Max frowned. “I want to pick a game too.”

“And I want to choose a song,” Luna said.

Poppy’s tummy fluttered. “But I need to do it all. What if my ideas are not good? What if I am not enough?” Her feathers drooped. She looked at her drawings. The colors looked dull now.

Mama set a glass of cool water on the table. It chimed softly. “Poppy,” Mama said, “you have wonderful ideas. You are enough. You are creative and kind. You can lead and still share. We can work together.”

Poppy whispered, “What if I share and my plan gets messy?”

Mama smiled. “Working together can be a little messy, and that is okay. We can compromise.”

Poppy repeated the new word. “Com-pro-mise.”

“It means we all give a little and take a little,” Luna said. “We can mix our ideas.”

Max added, “Like fruit salad! Apples, bananas, and berries all together. Yummy!”

Poppy felt her chest lighten, like a balloon. “Okay,” she said. “Let’s try.”

They made a plan on the dining room table. The paper felt crisp under Poppy’s claws.

- Poppy would pick two songs. Luna would pick one.
- Max would choose a game. Poppy would choose another game.
- Mama would make sandwiches. Poppy would make fruit cups.
- Everyone would bring a trash bag to help clean the park after.

Poppy took a deep breath. “I can do this,” she told herself. “I believe in me.”

They packed the basket. Crunchy carrots, juicy berries, and soft bread went inside. Poppy tucked in the bright cards. The dining room smelled like strawberries and soap from clean plates.

At the park, the grass felt cool under their toes. The air was fresh and leafy. Neighbors came, smiling and waving. “Hello!” “What a nice idea!” “Thank you!”

Poppy stood tall on a picnic bench. Her feathers shimmered. “Welcome to our community picnic!” she chirped. “We will eat, play, sing, and clean the park together.”

Max started a game of gentle tag. Giggles bubbled like water. Poppy chose a scarf-dance game next. The scarves whooshed in the breeze. “Good choice, Max,” Poppy said. Max smiled big.

Luna began her song. Poppy sang her two songs too. Her voice was bright and clear. A little girl clapped and said, “I love your songs!”

Poppy’s heart felt warm and brave. “Thank you,” she said. “I believed I could do it, and I did.”

After eating, everyone helped pick up trash. Papa from next door held a bag. “Teamwork!” he called. Little hands and big hands worked together. The park looked clean and happy.

A neighbor named Mrs. Green patted Poppy’s wing. “You are a wonderful leader,” she said. “You planned this picnic, and you shared the work.”

Poppy smiled. “I learned to compromise,” she said proudly. “Sharing ideas makes them better. And I can lead with confidence.”

As the sun turned soft and golden, they headed home. Back in the dining room, the table felt smooth under Poppy’s claws again. The room smelled like lemon tea. Poppy perched on the chair and sighed a happy sigh.

Mama hugged Poppy gently. “How do you feel?”

Poppy fluffed her feathers. “I feel proud. I believed in myself. I listened to my friends. We helped our community. We played outside and took care of the park.”

Max added, “And the fruit cups were yummy!”

They all laughed. Poppy looked at her crayons. “Tomorrow,” she said, “we can make thank-you cards.”

Luna nodded. “Another great idea, Poppy.”

Poppy chirped, “I am creative. I am kind. I can lead, and I can share.”

She tapped the table. Tap-tap-tap. Her heart felt steady and strong.

The End.
