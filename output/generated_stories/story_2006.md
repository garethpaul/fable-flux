# Tilly Turtle Helps at Home

Tilly was a shy turtle who lived in a cozy home office. The room smelled like warm paper and lemony clean spray. The desk was tall like a mountain, and the chair went squeak-squeak when it turned. Soft light from the window made stripes on the floor.

Tilly liked quiet. She liked to sit by the little plant and listen to the tiny fan hum. “Hummm,” it sang. Tilly hummed back. “Hmmmm.”

One morning, Tilly looked around. Papers were scattered like leaves. Crayons rolled on the floor. A sticky note was stuck to her shell! She blinked. “Oh,” she whispered. “This is messy.” She felt a wiggle of worry in her tummy.

Mama’s voice came from the doorway. “Good morning, Tilly. I need to finish some letters today. The home office is our shared space. We all use it. We all care for it.”

Tilly tucked her head a little. “I am shy,” she said softly. “I don’t like to talk much.”

“You don’t have to talk a lot to help,” said Mama, smiling. “Civic responsibility means helping our community. Our family is a small community. Our home office is part of it. We take care of the places we share. Little turtle, can you be a helper?”

Tilly peeked out. Her heart thumped—thump, thump. She wanted to help, but she saw crayons under the chair. She reached out her small foot. Scratch. The chair squeaked and scared her. “Eep!” Tilly pulled into her shell.

She felt frustration like a tight knot. “It’s hard,” she whispered inside her shell. “I can’t.”

Mama knelt down. “When we feel stuck, we can take a slow breath. Want to try?”

Tilly poked her nose out. She took a deep breath in. Sssslow in. She let it out. Sssslow out. Her knot felt a little softer.

“We can make a plan,” Mama said. “First, pick up crayons. Next, stack papers. Then, wipe the desk. We go step by step.”

Tilly liked steps. Steps felt safe. “Okay,” she said. Her voice was quiet, but steady.

Step one: Tilly slid to the crayons. She listened. Roll, roll, roll. She caught one with her foot. Tap. She put it in a cup. Plop. The sound was happy. Tap, plop. Tap, plop. She found a blue crayon under the fan. The fan tickled her face. “Hee,” she giggled. “Hello, blue.”

Step two: Tilly stacked papers. Swish, swish. She lined up the corners. She discovered a paper with a big star sticker. “Ooo,” she said. “A star!” She stuck it on the crayon cup. “Now the cup is the Star Cup.”

Step three: Tilly wiped the desk with a soft cloth. The cloth smelled like lemon. Wipe, wipe, wipe. The wood shone like a little sun. Tilly smiled. She was doing it!

Just then, she saw a charging cord tangled like noodles. She tugged. The cord pulled back. Tug, tug. Tilly’s face scrunched. “It won’t!” she huffed. Frustration puffed in her chest again. Her eyes felt hot.

Mama touched Tilly’s shell. “Pause,” she said. “Breathe.”

Tilly took a slow breath. In. Out. She looked closely. “Ah,” she said. “It is under the chair wheel.” She discovered the problem. “I need help.”

Asking felt big. Tilly’s voice was a whisper. “Mama, can you help me, please?”

“Of course,” said Mama. She lifted the chair a tiny bit. Tilly guided the cord out, smooth and gentle. Slip! Free!

“You did it,” Mama said. “You looked. You found the problem. You asked for help. That is smart.”

Tilly’s shell felt warm with pride. The room looked neat now. The floor looked like a calm lake. The desk shone. The fan hummed, “Hummm.”

There was one more thing. The window was smudgy. Little hand prints made cloudy shapes. Tilly pressed a cloth to the glass. Wipe, wipe. Outside, a bird chirped. “Cheep-cheep!” Tilly chirped back, “Cheep!”

Knock-knock! Tilly’s friend, Benny the bunny, peeked in. “Hi,” he said. “Can I play? Hop?”

Tilly took a small breath. She remembered inclusion. “Yes,” she said. “We can play, but we take care of the office first. It is our shared space.” She pointed to the Star Cup. “Crayons go here. Papers go there. We all help.”

Benny’s ears wiggled. “I can help!” he said. He picked up a sticky note and stuck it on the board. “Stick!” He giggled.

Together they made a “Help Board.” Tilly drew little pictures: a crayon, a paper stack, a cloth, and a cord with a happy face. Benny clapped. “Now we know what to do!”

They played “Office Helpers.” Benny pushed the chair just a little. Squeak! Tilly guided the cord into a loop. Loop-de-loop. They counted: “One loop, two loops.” Tilly liked the counting. It made her feel calm.

Mama watched. “You two are great citizens of this home,” she said. “You care for your space. You include each other. You ask for help. You help back. That is civic responsibility.”

Tilly stood a little taller. She was still shy, but her heart felt brave. She could help. She could breathe when she felt stuck. She could discover new ways, like making a Help Board. She could welcome friends to help too.

When they were done, Mama put a small sign on the wall. It said: “We Care for Our Shared Space.” Tilly traced the letters with her finger. The paper felt smooth. She whispered the words. “We care.”

Benny hopped. “Now can we draw?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Tilly. “And when we’re done, we will clean up.” She smiled. “That is our plan.”

They drew a bright sun and a tiny turtle with a star. Crayons went tap, plop into the Star Cup when they finished. The room stayed neat. The fan hummed. The chair squeaked, but just a little.

Tilly sighed a happy sigh. She felt calm and proud.

Lesson: Helping take care of the places we share is part of being a good citizen. When we feel frustrated, we can breathe, make a plan, ask for help, and include others. Little helps make a big difference, every day.

The End.
