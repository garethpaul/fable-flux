# Melody Mouse's Helpful Routine

In a bright and colorful art room filled with paintbrushes, crayons, and paper, lived a small gray mouse named Melody. She had the softest whiskers and the kindest heart in the whole school.

Every morning, Melody would scurry around the art room, but she never knew what to do first. Sometimes she would start painting, then remember the brushes needed cleaning. Other times she would begin organizing the crayons, only to notice the paper was scattered everywhere.

"Oh my!" squeaked Melody one Tuesday morning, looking at the messy art room. Paint tubes were rolling on the floor, making soft *thump-thump* sounds. Colorful paper scraps fluttered like tiny butterflies when the window breeze touched them. "I want to help make this room beautiful, but I don't know where to start!"

Mrs. Robin, the art teacher, noticed Melody's worried expression. "What's troubling you, little one?" she chirped sweetly.

"I want to help every day," said Melody, twirling her tail nervously. "But I get so mixed up! I start one thing, then another, and nothing gets finished properly."

Mrs. Robin smiled warmly. "Would you like to learn about having a helpful routine, Melody? A routine means doing the same helpful things in the same order every day."

Melody's dark eyes sparkled with curiosity. "Yes, please! I love helping!"

Mrs. Robin gently picked up Melody and placed her on the art table. "Let's create your very own helping routine. First, every morning, you could organize the crayons by color. Can you do that?"

Melody nodded eagerly and began sorting the crayons. *Click-click-click* went the crayons as she arranged them. "Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple!" she counted happily.

"Wonderful!" said Mrs. Robin. "Next, you could check that all the paintbrushes are clean and ready. After that, you could make sure the paper is neatly stacked."

Melody hopped from task to task, her tiny paws moving quickly. She sniffed each paintbrush to make sure it smelled fresh and clean, not like old paint. She carefully straightened the paper stacks, making them perfectly neat.

"This feels so good!" squeaked Melody, doing a little happy dance. "When I follow the same steps every day, I don't forget anything!"

The next morning, Melody woke up excited. She knew exactly what to do! First, she organized the crayons, humming a little tune. Then she checked the paintbrushes, making sure they were sparkly clean. Finally, she arranged the paper in perfect stacks.

When the children arrived for art class, everything was ready and beautiful.

"Look how organized everything is!" said little Emma, clapping her hands.

"The crayons are like a rainbow!" added Tommy, reaching for the blue crayon.

Melody felt warm and proud inside. Her daily routine helped everyone have a better day.

From that day forward, Melody followed her helpful routine every single morning. She discovered that when she did the same helpful things in the same order, she had more time to play and even help other classroom animals learn their own routines.

"Thank you for teaching me about routines," Melody told Mrs. Robin one afternoon. "Now I know that doing good things the same way every day makes everything work better!"

Mrs. Robin smiled. "And your kindness makes our whole art room a happier place, Melody."

The End.
