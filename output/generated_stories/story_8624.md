# Toby the Tortoise Learns to Ask for Help

In a cozy nursery filled with soft toys and colorful books, lived a wise old tortoise named Toby. His green shell sparkled in the warm sunlight that streamed through the window, and his kind eyes twinkled behind tiny round glasses.

Every morning, Toby helped take care of the nursery. He watered the plants with his little watering can, organized the toy blocks by color, and made sure all the picture books were neatly arranged on the low shelves.

"Good morning, nursery!" Toby would say cheerfully. "Time to make everything nice and tidy!"

But one Tuesday morning, something was different. The teacher had left a special note asking Toby to hang up colorful paper stars on the bulletin board for the children's art display. The problem was, the bulletin board was very high up on the wall.

Toby looked up at the tall board and scratched his head. "Hmm," he said to himself. "I can reach that easily!"

He stretched his short legs as high as they could go, but his little arms couldn't even touch the bottom of the board. The paper stars fluttered in his hands like tiny butterflies, but they stayed far from where they needed to be.

"I'll try standing on my tip-toes," Toby mumbled, wobbling back and forth. Still too short.

Next, he tried jumping. "One, two, three... jump!" But tortoises aren't very good at jumping, and Toby just made soft *thump* sounds on the carpet.

Feeling frustrated, Toby's cheeks turned pink. He pulled his head into his shell and sat quietly. "I can't do this," he whispered sadly. "I'm too small."

Just then, Ms. Sarah, the nursery teacher, walked in carrying a basket of fresh crayons. She noticed Toby sitting by himself, looking upset.

"Good morning, Toby!" she said with a warm smile. "How are you doing with the star project?"

Toby peeked his head out slowly. His voice was very quiet. "I... I can't reach the bulletin board. I tried and tried, but I'm not tall enough."

Ms. Sarah knelt down beside him. "Oh, Toby! Thank you for telling me. You know what? I should have thought about that. Would you like some help?"

Toby's eyes lit up. "Really? You're not upset that I couldn't do it by myself?"

"Of course not!" Ms. Sarah laughed gently. "Even wise tortoises need help sometimes. That's what friends are for!"

Together, they found a sturdy step stool. Ms. Sarah held it steady while Toby climbed up carefully. Now he could reach the bulletin board perfectly!

"Red star goes here," Toby said happily, pressing it onto the board. "Blue star goes there! This is fun!"

"You're doing a wonderful job, Toby," Ms. Sarah said. "And I'm proud of you for telling me you needed help."

When they finished, the bulletin board looked beautiful with its rainbow of paper stars. Toby felt proud and happy.

"Ms. Sarah," Toby said thoughtfully, "I'm sorry I tried to do everything by myself first. Next time, I'll ask for help right away when I need it."

"That's very wise, Toby," she replied. "Asking for help isn't giving up – it's being smart! And saying you're sorry shows you care."

From that day on, whenever Toby had a task that seemed too difficult, he remembered to use his words and ask for help. The nursery children loved visiting him, and he taught them his special lesson: "It's always okay to ask for help, and it's always good to say sorry when we make mistakes."

The End.
