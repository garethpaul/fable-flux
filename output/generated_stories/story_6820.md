# Penny Panda's New Friend

Penny Panda loved her cozy bedroom. The walls were painted soft green like bamboo leaves, and her fluffy bed had the warmest, fuzziest blankets. Every night, she would snuggle up with her favorite books and look out her window at the stars.

One evening, as Penny was getting ready for bed, she heard a tiny voice.

"Hello? Is anyone there?" squeaked the little voice.

Penny looked around her room. "Who's that?" she called softly.

"Down here!" said the voice.

Penny looked down and gasped. There, by her bedroom door, stood the tiniest elephant she had ever seen! The elephant was bright purple with sparkly silver ears.

"Oh my!" said Penny, backing away. "You're... you're very different from any elephant I've ever seen!"

The little elephant's trunk drooped sadly. "I know I look strange. My name is Ellie. I'm from the Rainbow Forest where all the animals have special colors. I got lost trying to find my way home."

Penny felt a little scared at first. Ellie looked so different from the gray elephants in her picture books. But then she noticed that Ellie's eyes were filled with tears, and her patient heart began to feel sorry for the lost little elephant.

"Are you crying?" Penny asked gently, taking a small step closer.

"Yes," sniffled Ellie. "Everyone always stares at me because I'm purple. They think I'm too different to be a real elephant."

Penny's soft panda ears perked up. She remembered how sad she felt when other animals said pandas were too black and white to be interesting. Slowly, she sat down on her fluffy carpet.

"I'm sorry I stepped back," said Penny. "I was just surprised. I've never met a purple elephant before, but that doesn't mean you're not wonderful."

Ellie's sparkly ears wiggled with hope. "Really?"

"Really!" said Penny with a warm smile. "Would you like to tell me about the Rainbow Forest? It sounds magical!"

Ellie's whole face lit up. She told Penny about trees with golden leaves, blue rivers that sang lullabies, and animals of every color imaginable. As she spoke, Penny realized how amazing it would be to see such a colorful, diverse place.

"Your forest sounds beautiful," said Penny. "And you know what? Your purple color is the prettiest purple I've ever seen. It reminds me of my favorite bedtime sky."

Ellie beamed. "Thank you for being so kind, Penny. You have the biggest heart."

Penny felt proud that she had been brave enough to listen and learn about someone new. "I'm sorry I was scared at first. I should have been more open to meeting you right away."

"That's okay," said Ellie. "Sometimes new things can feel scary, but you were brave enough to give me a chance."

Together, they looked out Penny's bedroom window at the stars. Penny helped Ellie find the North Star, which would guide her home to the Rainbow Forest.

"Thank you for teaching me about your world," said Penny. "I learned that being different makes everyone special."

As Ellie prepared to leave, she hugged Penny with her tiny trunk. "And I learned that patient, kind friends can be found anywhere!"

From that night on, Penny always remembered that the most wonderful friendships come from being open to meeting new and different friends.

The End.
