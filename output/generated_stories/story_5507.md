# Pebble the Penguin Says, “I’m Sorry”

Pebble the penguin was a cheerful little friend with a round belly and shiny black-and-white feathers. One sunny morning, Pebble waddled into the bathroom. The bathroom was bright and warm. The light made the clean tiles sparkle. The air smelled like sweet bubble soap. Plink, plink, went the dripping faucet. Pebble loved the sound.

“Time for splashy-splash!” Pebble chirped. He flapped his tiny wings and peeked at the big blue towel hanging on the hook. “Hello, Towel,” he giggled. “You are soft like a cloud.” He rubbed his cheek on it. Mmm, cozy.

Pebble climbed up onto his little step stool. He turned the silver handle. Whoosh! The water rushed out. Pebble laughed as cool water tickled his flippers. He squeezed the soap bottle. Squeak! A bubble popped on his nose. “Boop!” he said, crossing his eyes to look at the wiggly bubble.

Just then, his friend Daisy Duck waddled in. “Hi, Pebble,” Daisy quacked kindly. “Can I wash my feathers too?”

“Yes!” Pebble chirped. “Let’s share the sink.” He scooted to make room. The two friends hummed a happy song.

Pebble liked to be funny. He flicked a tiny splash at Daisy. Splish! A drop landed on her beak.

“Hee-hee!” Pebble giggled.

Daisy blinked. “Please don’t splash my eyes,” she said softly. “It makes me blink too much.”

Pebble heard her words, but he wanted to be silly. He flicked again. Splish-splash! This time, water went on Daisy’s face and her fluffy head.

Daisy stepped back. “Pebble, please listen,” she said, a little louder. “No more splashing.”

Pebble’s smile stayed big. He didn’t slow down. He clapped his flippers and made a big wave. Sploosh!

“Oh!” cried Daisy. Water ran down her cheeks. Her feathers drooped. Her voice sounded small. “That hurt my eyes. I don’t like it.”

Pebble froze. The bathroom felt quiet except for the plink, plink of the faucet. The soapy smell didn’t seem sweet anymore. Pebble saw Daisy’s sad face. His tummy felt tight and wiggly. He remembered her words: “Please don’t splash my eyes.”

He had not listened.

Pebble took a little breath. He didn’t want his friend to feel bad. He wanted to make it better.

“Daisy,” Pebble whispered, “I’m sorry I splashed you. I didn’t listen when you asked me to stop.” His voice was soft and honest.

Daisy dabbed her eyes with the corner of the towel. She sniffed. “Thank you for saying sorry,” she said. “My eyes still sting a little.”

Pebble nodded. “I want to make amends,” he said. “That means I want to fix what I did.” He looked around. “Can I help?”

Daisy blinked, and her eyes looked calmer. “Yes,” she said. “Please hand me the towel gently. And can you turn the water down? It’s too splashy.”

Pebble moved slowly. He picked up the soft towel. It felt fluffy and warm. He held it out carefully. “Here you go,” he said.

He turned the handle and made the water a little trickle. Tck-tck, went the handle. The sink was quiet now. The bathroom felt peaceful again.

“Thank you,” Daisy said. She dabbed her face. “That helps.”

Pebble felt a small glow in his heart. He was fixing it. “Would you like me to wipe the counter?” he asked. “There are splashes here too.”

“Yes, please,” Daisy said.

Pebble used a little cloth. Swipe, swipe. He wiped the shiny counter. He wiped the mirror where little dots of water sparkled like tiny stars. He hummed a gentle tune. The soapy smell felt nice again.

Daisy watched. She smiled a tiny smile. “Pebble,” she said, “I’m glad you are listening now.”

Pebble nodded. “Listening is kind,” he said. “I want to be kind.” He looked at Daisy. “Next time, if you say stop, I will stop right away.”

Daisy’s smile grew warm. “That makes me feel safe,” she said.

They finished washing, taking turns. Pebble counted, “One, two, my turn. One, two, your turn.” Daisy giggled. The water made soft splashes, but no big waves. They both felt calm.

When they were done, Pebble turned off the faucet. Click. He put the soap back. He hung the towel neatly on its hook. He looked at Daisy and asked, “Do you want to do a gentle bubble pop with me? Just one, very gentle.”

Daisy nodded. “Yes, gentle is good.”

Pebble squeezed a tiny dot of soap. Bloooop, a bubble grew and floated up. It shimmered with rainbow colors—pink, green, blue. They watched the bubble drift, light as a feather. Pebble whispered, “Three, two, one.” They both touched it—pop! The bubble vanished with a soft sound, like a kiss.

Daisy laughed. “That was fun and kind.”

Pebble’s heart felt light. He had made amends. He had listened. He had been gentle.

“I’m proud of you,” Daisy said. “When you said sorry and helped me, it made my feelings better.”

Pebble smiled. “I learned that saying sorry is good, but fixing it is even better. Listening the first time is best of all.”

They waddled out of the bathroom together. The tiles no longer felt cold. The air smelled sweet again. Pebble took Daisy’s wing for a friendly squeeze. “Thank you for telling me how to help,” he said.

“Thank you for listening,” Daisy answered.

The End.
