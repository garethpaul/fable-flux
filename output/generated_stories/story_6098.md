# Gertie's Fair Share Day

Gertie the giraffe loved her family's home office. The tall windows let in warm sunshine that felt good on her spotted coat. She could stretch her long neck to see the green trees outside.

Today, Gertie's little brother Max was visiting. He was much smaller than Gertie, but he wanted to use the computer too.

"I want to play games!" Max said, reaching up toward the desk. His tiny hooves couldn't reach the keyboard.

Gertie was already using the computer to draw pictures. The colorful screen made her eyes sparkle with joy. "But I was here first," Gertie said. "That's not fair if I have to stop."

Max's ears drooped down. "Please, Gertie? I really want to try."

Gertie felt the soft carpet under her hooves as she thought. She looked at her little brother's sad face. Then she looked at the computer screen.

"Hmm," Gertie said slowly. "Maybe we can find a fair way to share."

Gertie's mind was full of ideas. She remembered what Mama always said: "Being fair means everyone gets a turn."

"I know!" Gertie said, her tail swishing with excitement. "Let's make a sharing plan!"

Gertie found some recycled paper in the office. The paper felt smooth and cool in her hooves. She drew a big circle and divided it like a pie.

"Look, Max," Gertie said, pointing with her long neck. "We can take turns. I'll use the computer for ten minutes, then you get ten minutes. We can use this timer!"

Max's eyes grew wide and bright. "Really? That sounds fair!"

Gertie set the small green timer on the desk. It made a gentle tick-tock sound. "When it rings, we switch. And we can help each other too!"

During Gertie's turn, she showed Max how to move the mouse. The little clicking sounds made them both giggle. During Max's turn, Gertie helped him reach the keyboard by letting him stand on her back.

"This is fun!" Max said, his voice full of happiness. "Thank you for sharing, Gertie!"

Gertie felt warm and proud inside. Her heart felt as big as her long neck. "You're welcome! Sharing makes everything better."

When they finished their computer time, Gertie had another idea. She noticed the office trash can was full of paper.

"Max, want to help me sort this paper for recycling?" Gertie asked. "We can make sure it gets used again instead of thrown away."

Max nodded eagerly. They sorted the white paper from the colored paper. The papers made soft rustling sounds as they worked together. Soon they had two neat piles.

"Look what we did!" Max said, clapping his small hooves. "We helped the Earth too!"

Gertie smiled, her long eyelashes fluttering. "When we're fair to each other and fair to our planet, everyone wins."

Mama giraffe peeked into the office. She saw the tidy recycling piles and her two happy children.

"What wonderful teamwork!" Mama said. "You both found a way to be fair and helpful."

Gertie hugged Max with her long neck. "Being fair feels really good," she said. "And it's even better when we work together!"

As the sun set through the office windows, Gertie felt proud. She had learned that fairness wasn't just about taking turns. It was about finding ways for everyone to be happy and helping take care of their world too.

The End.
