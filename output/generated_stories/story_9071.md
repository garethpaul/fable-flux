# Finn's Big Decision

Little Finn the fox kit loved his cozy study corner. It had soft cushions, colorful books, and a big window that let in warm sunshine. Every day, Mama Fox would bring him snacks and help him with everything.

"Mama, I'm thirsty," Finn would say.

"I'll get you water, sweetie," Mama Fox would reply, rushing to help.

"Mama, I can't reach my favorite book," Finn would call.

"Here you go, my little one," Mama Fox would say, getting the book for him.

One bright morning, Finn sat in his study corner looking at a picture book about baby animals. The pictures showed little cubs, chicks, and fawns doing things all by themselves. They were finding food, making nests, and helping their families.

"I wonder if I could do things by myself too," Finn thought, touching his fluffy orange tail.

Just then, he heard a tiny "peep, peep, peep" coming from outside his window. Finn pressed his black nose against the cool glass. Down below, he saw a baby bird on the ground. The little bird looked scared and alone.

"Mama!" Finn called. "There's a baby bird outside!"

But Mama Fox was busy in the kitchen. She couldn't hear him over the sound of running water.

Finn looked at the baby bird again. It was hopping around, trying to fly but falling down. "That little bird needs help right now," Finn said to himself. "I can't wait for Mama."

His heart beat fast like a drum. He had never done something important all by himself before. But the baby bird needed him!

Finn took a deep breath. He climbed down from his cushions. Step by step, he walked to the door. His paws felt shaky, but he kept going.

Outside, the grass felt soft and tickly under his paws. The baby bird saw him coming and chirped loudly.

"Don't be scared, little bird," Finn said in his gentlest voice. "I want to help you."

Finn looked up and saw a nest high in the oak tree. "That must be your home," he said. "But how can I get you up there?"

He thought hard, just like Mama Fox taught him. Then he had an idea! Finn found a long, sturdy stick. Very carefully, he used it to make a little ramp against the tree trunk.

"Try walking up this," Finn said to the baby bird. "Take your time."

Step by tiny step, the baby bird walked up Finn's ramp. When it got close to the nest, its mama bird flew down and helped it the rest of the way.

"Tweet, tweet!" sang both birds happily.

Finn felt warm and proud inside his chest. He had helped someone all by himself!

When Mama Fox found him outside, Finn told her the whole story.

"Oh, Finn!" Mama Fox said, giving him a big hug. "You were so brave and kind. You made a good decision to help that baby bird. And you did it all by yourself!"

That night in his study corner, Finn felt different. He felt bigger and stronger inside. He had learned that he could do important things on his own. And helping others felt even better than getting help himself.

"Tomorrow," Finn said, snuggling into his cushions, "I'm going to try doing more things by myself."

And he did.

The End.
