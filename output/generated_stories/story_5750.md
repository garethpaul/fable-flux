# Koko the Koala's Car Ride Solution

Koko the koala sat quietly in the middle seat of the family car, her soft gray fur warm against the smooth seatbelt. She loved car rides because she could watch the trees whoosh by through the window and feel the gentle rumble of the engine.

"Are we there yet? Are we there yet?" squeaked her little brother Pip from the seat beside her. He bounced up and down, making the whole car shake.

"Pip, please sit still," said Mama Koala from the front seat. "We have a long drive ahead."

But Pip kept bouncing. Bounce, bounce, bounce! His elbow bumped into Koko's arm. His foot kicked her leg. His backpack slid over onto her seat.

"Ow!" said Koko as Pip's elbow poked her again. "That hurts!"

"Sorry!" Pip said, but he kept wiggling and bouncing. He was just too excited about their trip to Grandma's house.

Koko felt her tummy getting tight and her face getting warm. She wanted to push Pip away or tell him to stop being annoying. But Koko was known for being calm and thinking before acting.

She took a deep breath through her black nose and thought carefully. "How can I solve this problem peacefully?"

"Pip," Koko said in her gentle voice, "I can see you're really excited about seeing Grandma. I'm excited too!"

Pip stopped bouncing for a moment. "You are?"

"Yes! But when you bounce, your elbow bumps me and it hurts my arm. Can we talk about this?"

Pip's ears drooped a little. "I didn't mean to hurt you, Koko. I just can't sit still!"

Koko nodded. "I understand. Let me think... What if we make a deal?"

"A deal?" Pip's eyes lit up.

"Yes! You can have the window seat so you can look outside while you wiggle. That might help you feel less bouncy. And I'll take the middle seat, but you have to keep your arms and legs in your own space. Does that sound fair?"

Pip thought about this. "But what if I still feel too wiggly?"

"Then we can ask Mama if we can play the quiet counting game. We count red cars, then blue cars, then green cars. It gives your brain something to do!"

"That sounds fun!" said Pip.

"And," added Koko, "if you need a break from sitting, we can ask Mama if we can stop at a rest area to stretch our legs."

Mama Koala smiled in the rearview mirror. "Those are wonderful ideas, Koko. I'm proud of how you solved this problem by talking it through."

Pip and Koko carefully switched seats. Pip pressed his nose against the cool window glass and watched the world go by. His body felt calmer now that he had something to look at.

"I see a red car!" Pip whispered excitedly.

"I see it too!" Koko whispered back. "That's one red car!"

The rest of the car ride was peaceful and fun. When Pip started to feel wiggly again, they played their counting game. When they both needed to stretch, Mama stopped at a rest area with a playground.

"Thank you for listening to me and helping me solve our problem," Koko told Pip as they climbed back in the car.

"Thank you for not getting mad at me," Pip replied. "Talking about it was much better than fighting!"

By the time they reached Grandma's house, both little koalas had learned something important: when there's a problem, the best solution comes from listening to each other and working together peacefully.

The End.
