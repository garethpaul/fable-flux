# Mango’s Pantry Parade

Mango the monkey loved to help. One sunny morning, Mango peeked into the pantry. The pantry smelled like warm oats and sweet cinnamon. Shelves stood tall with jars, boxes, and shiny cans. But oh no! A big jar of peanut butter sat way up high. It wobbled. It tipped. It rolled to the very edge.

“Eek!” squeaked Mango, his tail flicking. “If that jar falls, it will go splat!”

Mango took a deep breath. He was a courageous monkey. He could be brave and calm. He could use his body and his brain. He could use coordination.

“I will save the jar,” he said. “But I will not climb alone. I will plan. I will move slow and steady.”

Mango looked around. He saw a paper towel roll. He saw wooden spoons. He saw a soft dish towel. He touched each thing. The roll felt light. The spoons felt smooth. The towel felt fluffy and safe.

“I can craft a helper,” Mango whispered. “A gentle catcher!”

He spread the towel on the floor under the tall shelf. He taped the corners to the floor so it would not slide. “Stick, stick,” went the tape. Next, he placed two wooden spoons across a low stool to make a little bridge. “Tap, tap,” went the spoon handles. Then he rolled the paper towels and tucked them under the towel to make a soft bump, like a pillow. “Roll, roll,” went the paper.

Mango nodded. “Soft, steady, safe,” he said. “This will catch the jar if it drops.”

He looked up. The jar still wobbled. Mango stood with his feet apart. He bent his knees a little. He wiggled his fingers. “I will use my eyes, my hands, and my feet together,” he said. “That is coordination!”

He tried a reach. His fingers brushed the shelf. “Too far,” he said. He tried a step. The stool wiggled. “Not yet,” he said, and he stepped back. Mango placed one hand on the shelf, one hand on the stool, and kept his tail stretched for balance.

“Slow and steady, Mango,” he told himself. “Look, then move.”

He looked at the jar. He took a small breath. He moved one foot. He looked again. He moved one hand. He whispered, “Look, move. Look, move.” His body felt calm and strong.

Just then—thunk!—a cereal box on the shelf tipped too. “Oh dear,” Mango said. The sound made his heart thump. He felt a little scared. He placed his feet down and hugged himself. “It’s okay to feel scared,” he whispered. “Brave means trying, even when you are scared.”

He shook his hands, like shaking raindrops. He smiled a tiny smile. “I can do it.”

Mango reached again. This time, he kept his eyes on the jar. He counted softly. “One… two… three.” His fingers held the jar lid. The jar slid. Mango tightened his fingers and bent his knees. The jar slipped from his grip—oh no!—and fell.

But plop! The towel and the soft roll caught it. It did not break. It just bounced, gently. Mango clapped. “My crafting worked! My plan worked!”

He picked up the jar, hugged it, and placed it on a lower shelf, safe and snug. “There,” he said. “No more wobble.”

Mango looked at the cereal box. He fixed it too. He lined up the cans so they would not roll. He put lighter things on top and heavy things on the bottom. He made little labels with sticky notes and a red crayon. He drew a peanut, an apple, and a bowl. “Pictures help me know where things go,” he said, proud.

Just then Mama Panda peeked in. “Mango, what are you doing in the pantry?” she asked with a soft laugh. The air smelled like apples now; Mama had been baking pie.

“I was brave,” Mango said. “I saw a problem. The jar was going to fall. I crafted a catcher. I used coordination—eyes, hands, feet, and breath together. I moved slow and steady. I fixed the shelf!”

Mama Panda looked around. “You were careful and clever,” she said. “You planned first. You made it safe. You used your body in a smart way. That is wonderful coordination.”

Mango grinned. “Can we have a Pantry Parade?” he asked.

“A parade?” Mama smiled.

“Yes! We can practice moving together,” said Mango. He tapped the spoons: tap-tap! He rolled the paper towels like drums: rum-rum! He waved the soft towel like a flag: whoosh-whoosh!

Mama laughed. “Pantry Parade!”

They marched slowly around the pantry. “Step, step, sway,” said Mango. “Look, move, breathe.” They used their feet to step, their hands to wave, and their eyes to watch. They kept the path clear. They stayed safe. They giggled and sang, “Slow and steady, we can be. Brave and kind, you and me!”

When the parade ended, Mango felt warm and proud. He touched his chest. His heart felt calm. The pantry looked neat and safe. The peanut butter jar sat low and happy.

Mango said, “Today I learned: When there is a problem, I can stop, plan, craft, and use coordination. Being brave means moving carefully, not quickly. Hands, feet, eyes, and breath can work together.”

Mama Panda hugged him. “What a good helper,” she said. “And what a courageous monkey.”

Mango nodded. “I am Mango the Brave,” he said, with a smile. “I am Mango the Careful, too.”

The End.
