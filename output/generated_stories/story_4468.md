# Peanut, Please? A Pantry Promise

In a quiet house, there was a cool, cozy pantry. It smelled like cinnamon and warm bread. Jars clicked softly when the door moved. Paper bags rustled like tiny leaves. On a low shelf sat a gentle elephant calf named Nuru.

Nuru was small for an elephant, with big floppy ears and kind eyes. His gray skin felt smooth and cool against the wooden floor. His tummy made a little “grr-rrr” sound.

“I am hungry,” Nuru said, patting his belly. “What can I eat?”

He looked around. There were rows of colorful jars, shiny cans, and baskets full of food from many places. He saw rice in a clear bag and beans in a big jar. He saw a jar of honey, golden like the sun. He saw shiny red apples and a basket of round flat bread.

On the very top shelf, Nuru saw a small bag of peanuts. The bag was bright and crinkly. The label had tiny pictures of peanuts dancing. Nuru’s eyes sparkled.

“I love peanuts,” Nuru said softly. “But that shelf is high.”

He reached up with his trunk. He sniffed the air. The peanuts smelled toasty and warm, like a friendly campfire. Nuru wiggled his toes. He could not reach.

Just then, a tiny voice squeaked, “Hello! Please be careful!”

Nuru turned. A small mouse peeked from behind a tea tin. The mouse wore a little blue ribbon. Her whiskers twitched.

“I am Mimi,” said the mouse. “I am saving those peanuts for my family’s festival. We make peanut stew with sweet bananas. It is our special food.”

Nuru blinked. “A festival? That sounds happy.”

“It is,” Mimi said, smiling. “We sing. We dance. We share food from our families. We celebrate our different ways.”

Nuru’s tummy rumbled again. “I am very hungry,” he said in a small voice. “But I do not want to take your festival peanuts.”

Mimi nodded. “Thank you,” she said. “That is kind.”

A soft rustle came from a basket. A long green bottle whispered, “Bonjour.” A label with a bright hat smiled up at Nuru. “I am olive oil from a sunny place far away,” said the bottle.

From a spice jar came a warm laugh. “Namaste,” said the jar of curry powder. “I bring color and scent.”

Another jar chimed in, “Hola,” said the jar of salsa. “I am full of tomatoes and peppers.”

Nuru’s ears flapped. “Wow,” he said. “So many foods from many places live in this pantry.”

Mimi nodded. “Yes. We can learn about each one.”

Nuru sat on his bottom. He put his trunk on his knees and took a deep breath. “I have a problem,” he said. “I want peanuts. But I want to be fair. How can we solve it?”

Mimi tapped her tiny paw on her chin. “Let us talk. Let us make a plan.”

They both smiled. They were ready to negotiate.

Mimi said, “What do you need?”

“I need a snack,” Nuru said. “Something crunchy. Something that does not take your festival food.”

“And what do I need?” Mimi asked.

“You need the whole bag for your stew,” Nuru guessed.

“Yes,” said Mimi. “I also need help reaching the spice jar. It is high and I am small.”

Nuru’s eyes lit up. “I can help with that!”

He stretched his trunk and gently lowered the curry jar. He set it in front of Mimi. The jar smelled warm, like sunshine and stories.

“Thank you,” Mimi said. Her whiskers danced.

Nuru looked at the pantry again. “What if I choose something else?” he asked. “Maybe I can find another crunchy snack.”

From a low shelf, a box of rice cakes crackled. “Pick me,” it seemed to say. Next to it, a jar of chickpeas winked. “We can be roasted,” whispered the chickpeas.

Nuru smiled. “I can roast chickpeas! They are crunchy too.”

Mimi clapped her tiny paws. “That is a great idea,” she said. “We keep our peanuts. You get a snack.”

Nuru nodded. “We made a plan. We both feel good.”

They worked together. Nuru opened the jar with a gentle twist. Mimi sprinkled a pinch of curry and a tiny bit of salt. Olive oil sang, “Glug, glug,” as Nuru poured a little on the chickpeas. The air filled with warm, spicy smells. The small toaster oven hummed softly. Nuru and Mimi watched through the window.

“Tick, tick, tick,” went the timer.

“Crunch, crunch,” went the chickpeas when they were ready.

Nuru took a bite. “Mmm,” he said, cheeks round. “So crunchy! So warm!”

Mimi took a tiny piece. “Delicious!” she squeaked.

They shared the snack. They laughed. They listened to the pantry’s gentle sounds—the jars clinking, the paper bags whispering, the cozy hum of the house.

Mimi held the peanut bag with both paws. “Thank you for keeping your promise,” she said. “That is integrity.”

Nuru’s ears lifted. “Integrity means doing the right thing,” he said proudly. “Even when I am hungry.”

Mimi nodded. “And negotiation means we talk and make a plan that helps us both.”

Nuru smiled. “I learned about your festival and your stew. I like learning about foods from other places.”

“And I learned a new snack,” Mimi giggled. “Curry chickpeas!”

They bumped noses and paws—boop!

“We did it,” Nuru said. “We solved our problem with words, kindness, and care.”

The pantry felt extra warm and friendly now. The cinnamon and curry smells mixed like a happy hug.

Nuru looked up at the shelves and whispered, “We are all different. That makes this pantry special.”

“Yes,” Mimi said softly. “Different is beautiful.”

They cleaned up together. They put the jar and oil back carefully. Nuru helped Mimi carry the peanuts to a safe spot.

“See you at the festival,” Mimi said.

“I will bring rice cakes,” Nuru said.

They both smiled big, gentle smiles.

The End.
