# Leo the Rooftop Helper

Leo was a brave little lion cub with a soft golden mane and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, Leo climbed the stairs to the rooftop garden of his home. The rooftop felt warm under his paws. The breeze smelled like mint and strawberries. Birds chirped from the railings, and little flower pots lined the edges like a colorful parade.

Today, Leo had a big job. He would help water the rooftop garden. He puffed out his chest. “I can do it,” he said. His tail swished with excitement.

Mama called up from below, “You’ve got this, Leo! Take your time.”

Leo found a small watering can shaped like a rainbow. It felt cool and smooth. He lifted it with both paws. “I am strong,” Leo whispered. “I am careful.”

He started with the tiny carrot sprouts. Drip, drip, drip. The soil smelled fresh and earthy. “Good drink,” Leo said to the plants. He hummed a happy tune. The sun felt like a warm hug on his back.

Then Leo turned to a big pot of tomatoes. The pot was heavy and the can was full. “I can do big jobs,” Leo told himself. He tipped the can slowly. But his paws slipped on a tiny pebble. “Oh!” Water splashed. A tomato rolled off the pot, thump, thump, thump, and bumped into a little clay cup.

Crash!

The clay cup broke into two pieces.

Leo’s ears drooped. His heart felt wobbly, like jelly. “Oh no,” he whispered. “I made a mess. Maybe I’m not good at this.”

Just then, his friend Miri, a small gray pigeon, flapped down and tilted her head. “Coo-coo, what happened, Leo?”

Leo looked at the broken cup. “I spilled. I broke it. I think I’m not careful enough.”

Miri hopped closer. “Everyone slips sometimes. Even birds miss a perch. Watch.” She hopped, flapped, and pretended to wobble. “See? Oops!” She giggled. “You are still Leo. You are still brave.”

Leo took a deep breath. The air smelled like mint leaves and sunshine. He put a paw on his chest. “I am Leo. I am brave. I can fix mistakes.”

He picked up the broken cup pieces gently. “I’m sorry, cup,” he said softly. “I didn’t mean to break you.”

Mama came up the stairs. Her footsteps were slow and calm. “I heard a crash. Are you okay, Leo?”

Leo’s eyes felt shiny with little tears. “I slipped, Mama. I broke the cup. I’m sorry.”

Mama knelt beside him and stroked his soft mane. “Thank you for telling me the truth,” she said. “Accidents happen. I forgive you.”

Leo blinked. His chest felt lighter. “You forgive me?”

“Yes,” Mama said, smiling. “You are learning. Let’s clean up together.”

Leo nodded. “I want to help.”

They picked up the pieces and placed them in a safe box. Leo carefully swept the tiny bits with a small brush. Swish, swish. The rooftop looked neat again. He held the rainbow watering can and looked at the plants. “I can finish my job,” he said. “I will go slow.”

Miri flapped to the rail and sang, “Go, Leo, go!”

Leo watered the tomatoes, this time with slow, steady paws. He watered the basil next. The leaves smelled spicy and sweet. Drip, drip. He watered the orange marigolds. The petals looked like tiny suns.

When the can was empty, Leo carried it to the faucet. The water splashed cool and bubbly. He laughed. “It tickles!”

He walked carefully over the rooftop, paws steady and sure. He thought, I am brave. I can learn. I can finish. His tail made a happy swish-swish.

At the last pot, Leo saw something special. A tiny seedling peeked up from the soil, green and shy. “Hello, little one,” he whispered. “You can grow.”

He felt a warm glow inside. “I can grow, too.”

Mama clapped softly. “You did it, Leo. You finished the watering.”

Leo stood tall. “I made a mistake, but I fixed it. I kept going.”

Mama nodded. “That’s brave. That’s kind to yourself.”

Miri cooed, “And you made the rooftop sparkle!”

Leo thought about the broken cup. “Mama, can we still use it?”

Mama smiled. “We can make something new. Maybe a home for a tiny plant.”

Leo’s eyes lit up. “A cozy nest for the seedling!”

They tucked the seedling into one half of the cup with soft soil. Leo patted the dirt with gentle paw-taps. The little cup looked happy in its new job.

Leo took a big breath. The rooftop smelled like wet soil and hope. He said, “I can try. I can learn. I can finish. And when I make a mistake, I can say sorry and try again.”

Mama hugged him. “That is true, my brave cub.”

Miri flapped her wings. “Coo! Rooftop hero!”

Leo giggled. “I am Leo. I am brave.”

He looked at the tiny plant, the bright flowers, and the blue sky. His heart felt strong and calm.

The End.
