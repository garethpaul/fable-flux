# Bramble Bunny Tells the Truth

In a cozy guest room with soft blue walls, a small window let in warm morning light. The curtains swished like gentle waves. A clean bed waited with a puffy blanket. A plant in a clay pot sat on the sill, its leaves shiny and green.

A determined rabbit named Bramble peeked in. His ears stood tall. His whiskers twitched. “I can do it,” Bramble whispered. “I can help get the room ready for Grandma Goose.”

Bramble hopped onto the soft rug. It felt like clouds under his paws. He sniffed the air. It smelled like fresh soap and lemon. He wanted the room to be perfect. He wanted to be helpful and kind.

He started to tidy. “Pillow, fluff-fluff!” he said, patting the pillows. “Blanket, smooth-smooth!” He smoothed the blanket with little circles. He hummed a happy tune.

On the table was a shiny glass water cup. Next to it was a big red button that said “Lamp.” Bramble liked buttons. He wanted to be extra helpful. He pressed the button. Click! The lamp glowed. The room felt warm and bright.

Then Bramble saw the plant on the sill. Its leaves looked a little droopy. “Plants need water,” he said. “I care for the Earth. I care for the plants.” He lifted the glass cup and poured water into the plant’s soil.

But Bramble poured a little too fast. “Oh!” Splash! Water spilled. The soil burped up mud. A muddy drop splashed onto the white pillowcase. A brown dot spread like a tiny puddle.

Bramble’s heart thumped. His ears drooped. “I made a mess,” he whispered. He wished the dot would hide. He wished the dot would go away. He thought, Maybe I can say I didn’t do it. Maybe the dot came from the rain.

He picked up the pillow. It felt cool and a little damp. He listened to the clock tick-tock on the wall. Tick. Tock. Tick. Tock. The ticking sounded like a reminder. Bramble’s tummy felt twisty.

Bramble took a deep breath. He put his paw on his chest. “First, take care of me,” he said softly. “Deep breath in. Deep breath out.” His tummy felt a bit better. He remembered the lesson his friend Tilly Turtle told him: “Honesty is the best way to help your heart feel strong.”

Bramble nodded. “I will tell the truth,” he decided. “I will fix what I can. I will be gentle with me.”

He hopped to the hallway and called, “Mama!” His voice was small but steady. Mama Rabbit poked her head in. Her eyes were kind. “Yes, Bramble?”

Bramble’s paws shook a little. “I tried to help the plant,” he said. “I poured water. I spilled. I made a muddy dot on the pillow. I am sorry.” He looked at the floor. His ears drooped low.

Mama Rabbit knelt down. She touched his paw. “Thank you for telling me the truth,” she said. “That was very brave. We can clean it together.”

Bramble’s ears lifted. “Together?”

“Together,” Mama said with a smile.

They carried the pillowcase to the sink. Swish-swish went the warm water. Plip-plop went the drops. Mama squeezed a little soap that smelled like oranges. Bubbles popped like tiny stars. Bramble dabbed with a soft cloth. He moved slow and careful. “Gentle,” Mama said. “You are taking good care.”

As they worked, Bramble told the plant a story to keep calm. “Little plant,” he whispered, “once there was a river cloud who forgot to go slow. The cloud learned to pour just right. The end.” He giggled. The plant’s leaves seemed to sit up, shiny and glad.

The dot got lighter and lighter. “Look!” Bramble said. “It’s almost gone!”

“Your honesty made this easier,” Mama said. “And your careful work helped the pillow, helped the plant, and helped your heart.”

They hung the pillowcase to dry by the sunny window. The warm light hugged it. Bramble washed his paws with soap. Rub-rub. Rinse. Shake-shake. “Self-care,” he said. His paws smelled like oranges. He felt fresh and calm.

Grandma Goose waddled in with a gentle honk. “Oh my, what a lovely guest room!” she said. “It smells bright and clean.”

Bramble stood tall. “Grandma,” he said, “I spilled water and made a dot. I told Mama, and we cleaned it. I also gave your plant a drink.”

Grandma Goose smiled and patted Bramble’s head. “Thank you for telling me the truth,” she said. “That is generous and brave. You shared your mistake, and you shared your help. That’s a gift.”

Bramble’s chest felt warm. “Next time I will pour slowly,” he said. “And I will ask for help when I need it.”

Grandma nod-honked. “That is wise. And look at the plant! Caring for the Earth can be gentle and neat.”

Bramble brought Grandma a small glass of water. “For you,” he said. “And I will read you a story.” He opened a picture book and read in his best voice. The room was quiet and cozy. The lamp glowed. The clean pillow smelled sweet. The plant rustled softly, like it was listening too.

Bramble felt proud. He was honest. He took care of himself. He helped the plant and Grandma. He told a kind story. The guest room felt happy, like a hug.

The lesson was clear in Bramble’s heart: When you make a mistake, tell the truth, care for yourself, and then make it right. That is how trust grows.

The End.
