# Songbird Steps in the Home Office

Sunshine peeked through the blinds of the home office. The computer hummed softly. Papers sat in neat piles. Keys clicked, click-click, on the keyboard. It smelled like fresh apples and warm paper.

On the window sill, a small, bright bird wiggled her toes. She was a singing bird named Bella. Her feathers were blue like the sky. Her beak was tiny and yellow like a corn kernel.

“Good morning!” Bella sang. “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo!”

Inside the office, Mia, a kind big sister, rubbed her eyes. “I have a dance video to make for school,” she said. “But I have a problem. My tummy feels empty. My feet feel slow. And I don’t know what dance moves to do.” She made a wobbly wiggle. “Oh no.”

Bella tilted her head. “We can fix it together,” she sang. “Teamwork! Tweet!”

Mia smiled a little. “Teamwork sounds good. But how?”

Just then, a soft thump came from the hallway. Max, Mia’s little brother, toddled in holding a banana. “Dance?” he asked. “Snack?” He held up the banana with both hands. It smelled sweet and sunny.

Mia giggled. “Yes, Max. But I still don’t know where to start.”

Bella puffed up with a brave chirp. “Step one: Fuel up! Healthy food helps bodies dance. Step two: Make a plan together. Step three: Practice with music!”

Mia looked at the banana. “Good idea. Let’s have a snack break first.”

She peeled the banana. The peel made a soft “zip.” Max took a bite. “Mmm!” Mia sliced the banana and shared. They also crunched on apple slices and munched carrots from a little cup. Crunch, crunch, crunch! The sweet apple juice tickled Mia’s tongue. The carrots were orange and snappy. Her tummy felt happy and warm.

Bella tapped the window with her beak. “Tweet! I can sing us a beat.”

Mia wiped her hands and stood up tall. “Let’s make a team plan,” she said. “What if we have three moves?”

Max nodded hard. “Three!”

Bella sang a bubbly tune. “Chirp-choo, chirp-choo, tap-tap-tap!”

Mia clapped softly. “Move one: Banana Bend! We reach up high like tall banana trees, then bend, bend, bend.”

Max reached up, up, up and giggled. “Bend, bend!”

“Move two,” Mia said, “Apple Sway. We sway side to side like round apples rolling.” She swayed left, then right. Her ponytail brushed her shoulders.

Max swayed too and laughed. “Swooosh!”

Bella fluffed her feathers. “Move three: Birdie Hops! Little hops in a line. Hop, hop, hop!”

Max hopped. “Hop-hop!” His socks made tiny whispers on the rug.

Mia nodded. “Great team plan. But I still need a start and an end.”

Bella thought and sang a soft note. “We can start with a Hello pose. Hands to heart. We can end with a Together pose. Hands stacked, then big smiles.”

Mia grinned. “Yes. I like that.” She put her hands to her heart. Max copied her with serious eyes, then broke into a smile.

“Let’s practice,” Bella trilled. “I’ll sing the beat. You move your feet!”

Bella sang a gentle rhythm. “Tweet, tweet, tap. Tweet, tweet, tap.” Her song filled the home office with bright sounds. The humming computer seemed to dance along.

Mia began. “Hello pose.” She breathed in. She bent like a banana tree—stretch, bend, stretch. She swayed like an apple—soft and round. She hopped like a bird—light and bouncy. Max followed with happy wiggles. He bent big, swayed big, and hopped tiny hops.

Mia giggled. “This is fun!”

Max clapped. “Again!”

They practiced two more times. Each time, the moves felt smoother. Mia’s cheeks felt warm and pink. Her feet felt fast and sure. The home office felt bright and busy, like a tiny dance studio.

Then they tried the whole dance. Bella sang louder. “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo, hop-hop, sway!” The song sparkled like sunshine. Mia flowed from move to move. Max added a spin and a big giggle. At the end, they stacked their hands for the Together pose.

Mia pressed the record button on the computer. “Okay, team. One more time. This is it!”

They danced the Banana Bend. They swayed the Apple Sway. They hopped the Birdie Hops. Their feet whispered and thumped softly on the rug. Their smiles were bright. The dance ended with the Together pose and a cheer.

Mia stopped the video and watched. Her eyes shone. “We did it! We worked as a team. We ate healthy snacks. We made a dance.”

Max hugged Mia’s leg. “Team!”

Bella fluttered her wings and sang a happy trill. “Teamwork makes us strong. Healthy food helps us move. Music and dancing bring joy!”

Mia nodded. “Thank you, Bella. Thank you, Max. I felt stuck. Now I feel brave and proud.”

The sunshine warmed the room. The computer hummed kindly. The home office smelled like apples and paper and a little bit of victory.

And Bella sang a soft good-bye. “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo. Together, we can do it too.”

The End.
