# Benny Beaver's Garden Helper Day

Benny Beaver lived in a cozy apartment with his family. Their balcony had the most wonderful garden! Colorful flowers grew in pots. Green herbs smelled like pizza and cookies. Tomato plants reached up toward the sunny sky.

Every morning, Benny's mom watered the plants. "The garden needs care every single day," she would say. "Plants are living things, just like us!"

Benny loved watching the water sparkle on the leaves. He loved touching the soft soil with his little paws. But he always stayed inside while Mom worked.

One sunny Tuesday morning, Mom felt sick. "Oh dear," she said with a sneeze. "I don't feel well enough to water the garden today."

Benny looked out at the balcony. The flowers looked droopy. The herbs seemed thirsty. The tomato plants' leaves were curling up.

"Mom, I can help!" Benny said, standing up tall. "I know how to water plants!"

Mom smiled weakly. "That's very sweet, Benny. But you've never done it before. What if you forget some plants?"

Benny thought hard. He really wanted to help. The plants needed water, and Mom needed rest. "I'll be very careful," he promised. "I'll check every single pot!"

Mom gave Benny the small blue watering can. "Remember, just a little water for each plant. Too much can hurt them."

Benny stepped onto the sunny balcony. The warm air felt good on his fur. Birds were singing in the distance. A gentle breeze made the leaves dance.

He started with the red flowers by the door. Slowly, carefully, he poured cool water around their roots. "There you go, pretty flowers," he whispered.

Next came the herb pots. The basil smelled so fresh! The mint tickled his nose. Benny gave each one a drink.

But then Benny saw his favorite toy truck inside. "Just a quick play," he thought. He set down the watering can and ran inside.

After playing for ten minutes, Benny remembered something important. "Oh no! The tomato plants!"

He rushed back outside. The tomato plants looked even more thirsty now. Their leaves were very droopy.

"I'm sorry, tomato plants," Benny said. "I promised to take care of ALL the plants. That means you too!"

He filled the watering can again. Carefully, he gave the tomato plants extra water. He checked every single pot twice. The marigolds, the petunias, the lettuce - everyone got a drink.

As he worked, Benny felt the warm sun on his back. He heard bees buzzing around the flowers. The soil smelled rich and earthy. Being outside felt wonderful!

"All done!" Benny announced, going back inside.

Mom was feeling a little better. She looked out at the garden. "Benny, you did such a good job! Every plant looks happy and healthy."

Benny beamed with pride. "I almost forgot the tomatoes when I got distracted. But I remembered that I promised to help ALL the plants."

"That shows real responsibility," Mom said, giving him a big hug. "You kept your promise even when it was hard."

The next morning, Mom felt much better. "Would you like to help me water the garden again?" she asked.

"Yes!" Benny jumped up and down. "I love being outside with the plants!"

From that day on, Benny helped in the garden every morning. He learned that spending time outdoors made him feel happy and strong. He also learned that when you promise to help, you should always keep that promise - even when something fun tries to distract you.

And the plants? They grew bigger and more beautiful than ever before!

The End.
