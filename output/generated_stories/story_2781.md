# Rusty’s Sparkly Shed Helper

Rusty was a helpful robot dog who lived by the garden shed. His metal ears went beep-beep when he wagged his tail, and his nose light blinked blue when he was happy. The shed smelled like fresh soil and lemon soap. Sunlight made dust sparkle in the air. Little tools hung on hooks, and a tiny sink sat by the door.

“Good morning!” Rusty chirped in a friendly, tinny voice. “Beep! Time to help.”

Lulu and Max, two neighbors, ran to the shed in their rubber boots. “Hi, Rusty!” they cheered. They carried a basket of carrots and muddy toy shovels.

Rusty’s nose light blinked. “I can wash the carrots! I can wash the shovels! I can help with healthy habits.”

“Me first!” Max said, grabbing the sink handle.

“No, me first!” Lulu said, holding a carrot high.

Rusty sat down with a soft clink and wiggled his bolt-tail. “We can take turns,” he said warmly. “Beep. Taking turns is kind.”

Lulu crossed her arms. Max frowned. They both reached again. SPLASH! Water trickled over their fingers and dripped on the floor. The shed smelled extra wet now.

Rusty tilted his head. “Problem detected,” he said. “Beep. We need a plan.”

He took a little timer from the shelf. It tick-tick-ticked like a tiny bug. “Let’s make turn-time. Max washes one carrot. Lulu washes one carrot. Beep! Then I scrub the shovels. We say, ‘My turn, your turn.’”

Lulu looked at Max. Max looked at Lulu.

“My turn, your turn,” they said together, giggling.

Max held the carrot under the cool water. “Cold!” he laughed, feeling the splash on his hands.

Rusty handed him a soft brush. “Scrub-scrub,” Rusty said in a sing-song. “Scrub the dirt. Dirt goes away.”

Lulu waited, bouncing on her toes. She watched the timer’s tiny hand go tick-tick. When it beeped, she smiled. “My turn!” She washed her carrot. “Scrub-scrub,” she sang.

Rusty’s nose glowed. “Great taking turns,” he said. “Beep. Healthy food is clean food.”

Max sniffed the carrot. “It smells like the garden!”

Lulu sniffed too. “And a little like lemon soap!” she giggled.

Rusty nodded. “We wash food. We also wash hands.” He pointed to the sink. “Before we eat, we wash. After we play, we wash. That keeps us strong.”

Max held up his muddy palms. “Uh-oh,” he said.

Lulu held up hers. “Muddy, muddy!”

Rusty turned on the water. “Wet your hands,” he said gently. “Beep. Now soap. Rub rub rub—palms, backs, between fingers, and thumbs. Sing with me!”

Rusty sang a short, happy song:
“Bubbles here, bubbles there,
Bubbles dancing everywhere!
Rub and scrub, scrub and rub,
Clean hands for every hug!”

Lulu and Max sang and rubbed. The bubbles smelled bright and lemony. The water felt warm like sunshine. Their giggles echoed in the shed.

“Rinse!” Rusty said. “Shake-shake! Dry with a towel.”

Lulu and Max patted their hands dry on a soft, striped towel. It felt fluffy and cozy.

“Now can we eat a carrot?” Max asked.

“Yes,” Rusty said. “Beep. Healthy snack time.”

They each crunched a carrot. Crunch! Crunch! The sound was loud and happy. Their eyes went big at the sweet taste.

Rusty wagged. “Beep. Our problem is not done. The shovels are still muddy.”

Max peered at the shovels. “They are very, very muddy.”

Lulu nodded. “We need a plan again.”

Rusty’s nose blinked. “Solution mode,” he said. “Beep. We take turns scrubbing. We wear little gloves to protect our hands. After, we wash our hands again.”

He handed Max blue gloves and Lulu yellow gloves. They slipped them on. Snap! Rusty filled a bucket with warm water. Swish! He added a drip of soap. The shed smelled clean.

“My turn,” Max said, scrubbing a shovel. “Scrub-scrub!”

“Your turn,” Lulu said, taking the brush. “Scrub-scrub!”

Rusty hummed and steadied the bucket with his paw. “Gentle circles,” he coached. “Beep. Kind hands make clean tools.”

Soon the shovels shined. The bucket water turned brown like cocoa, but it did not smell yummy—it smelled like wet dirt! They poured it safely on the flower bed.

“All done,” Rusty said. “Beep. Now we wash hands again.”

They followed the song once more. Bubbles, rinse, and dry. Their hands were clean and smelled lemon-fresh.

Max smiled. “We solved the problem!”

Lulu hugged Rusty. His metal fur felt cool and smooth. “We took turns and stayed clean.”

Rusty wagged his tail so hard it went click-click-click. “Beep! Healthy habits help us feel good. We wash hands before we eat and after we play. We clean our tools and our food. We take turns and use kind words.”

Max and Lulu nodded. “My turn, your turn,” they said softly, like a promise.

Rusty looked around the tidy shed. The hooks were neat. The sink was shiny. The air smelled like lemon and fresh earth. The sun beam made tiny stars in the dust.

“Teamwork,” Rusty said. “Beep. Today was a healthy day.”

They all crunched one more carrot, then waved goodbye. Rusty curled by the door, happy and humming. He closed his bright blue eyes and whispered, “Beep-beep. See you next time.”

The End.
