# Daisy's Big Day Discovery

Little Daisy the deer fawn loved her cozy bedroom with its soft blue walls and fluffy white clouds painted on the ceiling. Every morning, sunlight streamed through her window, making her spotted coat shimmer like tiny stars.

"Ring, ring!" went Daisy's special alarm clock. It was shaped like a friendly sun with a big smile.

"Good morning, sunshine!" Daisy stretched her long legs and yawned. Today was very special - it was her first day helping Mama organize the forest playgroup.

Daisy bounced out of bed, her hooves tapping softly on the wooden floor. "I have so much time!" she said happily. "I'll play with my toys first, then get ready."

She pulled out her favorite puzzle with big, colorful pieces. "Just one puzzle," she whispered to herself. The pieces felt smooth under her hooves as she put together a picture of a rainbow.

"Tick, tock, tick, tock," went the sun clock on her dresser.

Next, Daisy found her collection of shiny pebbles. "Ooh, these are so pretty!" She arranged them by color - red ones here, blue ones there. The pebbles felt cool and smooth, like little treasures.

"Tick, tock, tick, tock."

Then Daisy discovered her crayons scattered under the bed. "I should draw a picture!" She drew swirls of green and yellow, making the paper smell waxy and new.

"RING, RING, RING!" The alarm clock's second bell chimed loudly.

Daisy's brown eyes grew wide. "Oh no! That's my 'time to get ready' bell!" She looked around her room. Toys everywhere! Crayons on the floor! Her breakfast bowl still empty!

"Mama!" Daisy called, her voice shaky. "I'm not ready!"

Mama Deer appeared in the doorway, her gentle eyes kind and understanding. "What happened, little one?"

"I played too long," Daisy said, her ears drooping. "I forgot about time. Now I'll be late for playgroup, and everyone will be waiting!" A tiny tear rolled down her fuzzy cheek.

Mama sat beside Daisy on the soft carpet. "Oh, sweetheart. We all forget about time sometimes. But you know what? We can fix this together."

"Really?" Daisy sniffled.

"Really! Let's make a plan. First, let's put toys in the toy box - that takes two minutes. Then brush your teeth - one minute. Then eat breakfast - five minutes. Can you help me count?"

Daisy nodded eagerly. "One, two!" she counted as they tossed blocks into the box. "This is like a game!"

Working together, they cleaned up quickly. Daisy brushed her teeth until they sparkled, and munched her sweet berries and oats.

"Look!" Mama pointed to the clock. "We finished with time to spare!"

At playgroup, Daisy told her friends about her morning. "I learned that time is like a special friend. When we pay attention to our time friend, everything works out better!"

Little Rabbit nodded. "I forget about time too sometimes!"

"That's okay," Daisy said kindly. "Mama says everyone learns. The important thing is trying again."

That night, tucked in her cozy bed, Daisy smiled at her sun clock. "Tomorrow I'll remember to watch you, Mr. Clock. Playing is fun, but planning my time makes everything even better!"

She closed her eyes, feeling proud and ready for another wonderful day.

The End.
