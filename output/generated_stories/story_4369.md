# Koko the Calm Koala’s Cozy Closet

Koko was a calm koala with soft gray fur and big round ears. Koko loved a special space called the closet hideaway. It was not a scary closet. It was cozy and kind. Inside were soft blankets, a tiny lamp that glowed warm gold, and a basket of little treasures—shells, buttons, and colored ribbons from friends around the world.

One bright morning, Koko padded over to the closet hideaway. The carpet felt fuzzy on Koko’s paws. The air smelled like clean laundry and sweet eucalyptus tea. Koko took a deep breath. In… and out. The breath felt like a gentle cloud floating in Koko’s chest.

“Hello, cozy place,” Koko whispered. “I’m ready for a calm day.”

Just then, friends came to play. Mei the panda peeked in, her eyes shining. Samir the camel clopped softly, his hooves going clip-clop on the floor. Asha the tiger cub bounced in with a playful purr. And Lina the llama hummed a friendly tune. Each friend brought a small gift for the treasure basket: Mei had a red ribbon from China, Samir had a shiny bead from the desert, Asha had a tiny bell from India, and Lina had a warm wool tassel from the Andes.

“Let’s play parade!” Asha said, tail twitching.

“Let’s do a quiet puzzle,” Mei said, holding little wooden shapes.

Samir lifted his bead. “Let’s make music.”

Lina swayed. “Let’s take turns and try all the things.”

Everyone talked at once. The closet hideaway felt a little too busy. The tiny lamp flickered. The basket rattled. Koko felt a flutter in the belly. The room seemed tighter, louder, faster.

Koko put a paw on the soft blanket. “Friends,” Koko said gently, “I feel swirly inside. Let’s get calm first.”

“How?” asked Asha, ears perked.

Koko smiled. “We can do Koala Calm. Sit soft. Hands on tummy. We breathe together.”

They all sat in a circle on the blanket. They felt the weave of the cloth on their paws and hands. Koko’s voice was steady and kind.

“In… smell the flower,” Koko said. “Out… blow the dandelion.”

They breathed.

“In… smell the flower.” The air tasted a little sweet, like tea.

“Out… blow the dandelion.” The air whooshed warm and gentle.

Koko counted softly. “One… two… three.”

The closet hideaway got quiet and cozy again. The lamp glowed steady. The basket stopped rattling. Koko’s belly felt calm like still water.

“Thank you,” said Mei, relaxing her shoulders. “I feel soft now.”

“Me too,” said Samir. “My heart is walking slow, not running.”

Asha giggled. “I feel like purring.”

Lina nodded. “Calm feels good.”

Koko looked at the treasures. “We come from many places,” Koko said. “We can listen to each other. We can be fair and take turns.”

“Fair means everyone gets a turn,” Mei said.

“And acceptance means we like each other as we are,” added Lina, pressing her tassel into the basket.

Koko clapped softly. “Yes. Let’s make a Sharing Plan.”

They made picture cards to help: a drum for music, a puzzle piece, a bell for parade, and a quiet heart for calm time. Koko placed the cards in a line.

“First, calm time,” Koko said, pointing to the heart. “Then puzzle. Then music. Then parade. If someone needs calm, we can pause.”

“Good plan,” said Samir. “Fair for all.”

They began. Mei slid puzzle shapes. “This piece is like a roof,” she said. Everyone watched and waited. Koko felt patience bloom like a little flower.

Next was music. Samir shook the shiny bead inside a gourd. It made a soft, sandy shhhhhh. Lina hummed low, like the wind on a mountain. Koko tapped the tiny bell. Ding! It sounded like a star. Asha patted the floor—tap tap—like tiger paws. They listened to each sound, each friend. The room filled with gentle music from many lands, and Koko’s heart felt wide and warm.

Then came the parade. They tied the ribbons to their wrists and tails. Red from China, blue from the sea, gold from the desert, and green like forests. They stepped in a line, slow and steady, so the closet hideaway stayed safe and cozy.

“Left… right,” said Koko. “We move together.”

“Wait!” Asha suddenly wanted to run fast. Her tail swished. The bell jingled wild. The closet felt squishy again. Mei’s puzzle almost slid off the blanket.

Koko placed a paw on the blanket and breathed. “In… smell the flower. Out… blow the dandelion.”

Asha looked at Koko and did the same. In… and out. Her whiskers relaxed.

“Thank you,” Asha said. “I can walk slow now.”

Koko smiled. “We can be calm and still have fun.”

They finished the parade with soft steps. After, everyone placed their gifts in the basket together.

Mei said, “We shared and waited. That was fair.”

Samir said, “We listened to all the sounds. That was kind.”

Lina said, “We liked each other’s ideas. That was acceptance.”

Asha said, “And when I felt fast, breathing helped me.”

Koko hugged each friend. The closet hideaway smelled like wool and tea and hope. The lamp made a tiny golden circle that felt like a hug.

Koko said, “Today we learned that calm helps us choose. Calm helps us share. Calm helps us hear each other, from places near and far.”

Everyone nodded. They sat for one last Koala Calm, hands on tummies, eyes soft.

“In… smell the flower. Out… blow the dandelion.”

The room was peaceful. Hearts were happy. Friends felt safe.

Koko whispered, “When we stay calm, we make fair choices, we accept each other, and we can enjoy the whole world together.”

“Together,” the friends echoed.

They waved goodbye and padded out, leaving the cozy closet ready for another calm day.

The End.
