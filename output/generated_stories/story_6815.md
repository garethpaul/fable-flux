# Panda Pauses and Says Sorry

Ping the panda hummed softly as he stepped into the musical practice room. The room smelled like fresh wood and lemon polish. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. There were drums, bells, stretchy bands, and a big mirror. Ping’s black-and-white fur looked extra fluffy in the light.

“I love music time,” Ping said, patting his round belly. “Tap, tap, tap! Clap, clap, clap!”

His friends were there too—Mimi the mouse, Tiko the turtle, and Bop the bunny. Their teacher, Miss Melody, rang a gentle bell. “Hello, friends,” she sang. “Today we make music and move our bodies. We exercise kindly and keep a steady beat.”

Ping smiled. “I can be patient. I can wait my turn.” He took a slow breath in and out. He liked being patient.

Miss Melody clapped a soft rhythm. “First, we share the drum. One by one.”

Mimi went first. Tap-tap, tap-tap. Then Tiko. Boom… boom. Then Bop. Rat-a-tat!

Ping’s paws tingled with excitement. He loved the big drum. When it was almost his turn, Ping felt wiggly. His paws bounced. His tail twitched. He hopped closer and closer. His heart went thump-thump like a drum. He forgot to wait.

Ping reached out too fast. “My turn!” he said, and he grabbed the drumstick from Bop.

The drumstick slipped. It bonked the drum with a loud BONG! Bop’s long ears drooped. Startled, Tiko pulled into his shell. Mimi squeaked and covered her tiny ears.

Miss Melody raised a hand. The room went quiet except for the echoing bong fading away.

Ping’s tummy felt tight. His paws felt hot. He saw Bop’s sad face and Tiko’s worried eyes. He saw Mimi’s tiny shake-shake shake. Ping blinked. “Oh,” he whispered. “I didn’t wait. I wasn’t gentle.”

Miss Melody knelt beside Ping. “What happened, Ping?”

Ping looked at his friends. His voice was small. “I got too excited. I grabbed. I didn’t wait my turn. I scared my friends.”

Miss Melody nodded. “Everyone makes mistakes. What can we do to make it better?”

Ping knew he could try to fix it. He remembered a gentle rule: We pause. We breathe. We make amends. He put the drumstick down. He placed his paws on his belly. He took a slow panda breath—inhale… exhale.

He turned to Bop. “I’m sorry, Bop. I grabbed from you. Are you okay?” His voice was warm and soft.

Bop wiggled his whiskers. “I was scared. But thank you for saying sorry.”

Ping nodded. He looked at Tiko. “I’m sorry I made a loud sound.”

Tiko peeked out. “It was loud. I feel better now that you spoke kindly.”

Ping looked at Mimi. “I’m sorry I forgot to wait.”

Mimi squeaked, “Thank you for saying sorry.”

Ping thought, “Saying sorry is good. But what else can I do?” He asked, “May I make it right? I can give the drumstick back. I can count to three and wait.”

Miss Melody smiled. “That is making amends, Ping. You are fixing the mistake.”

Ping put the drumstick into Bop’s paws. “You go first, Bop. I will be patient. I will move my body while I wait.”

Miss Melody clapped a gentle pattern. “While we wait, we can exercise! Let’s do the Panda Pause.”

Everyone stood tall. Ping led with a calm voice. “Stretch to the sky… one, two, three.” They reached up and felt their shoulders grow long. “Touch your toes… one, two, three.” The floor felt cool on Ping’s paws. “Roll your shoulders… roll, roll.” Their bodies loosened. “Big breath in… and out.” The room felt peaceful again.

Bop tapped the drum happily. Rat-a-tat, rat-a-tat. “Your turn, Tiko,” Bop said. Tiko tapped, then Mimi, then finally Ping.

Ping waited. He swayed side to side. He counted in his head. One… two… three. When it was his turn, he took one more Panda Pause breath. He lifted the drumstick gently. Tap… tap… tap. The sound was round and soft. It felt like a hug.

“Lovely,” Miss Melody said. “You kept a steady beat.”

Ping smiled wide. “I like the Panda Pause. It helps me wait. It helps me be kind.”

After the drum, they played bells. Ting-ting! They shook maracas. Shaka-shaka! They hopped to the rhythm—hop, hop, hop! Exercise made their muscles warm and their hearts happy.

Ping remembered his mistake, but he also remembered his fix. At clean-up time, he walked to Bop. “Thank you for forgiving me.”

Bop nodded. “We all learn. Friends forgive.”

Ping turned to Tiko and Mimi. “Thank you for being patient with me.”

Tiko smiled slowly. “Patience is a good shell,” he said.

Mimi giggled. “And forgiveness is a tiny bell that rings in your heart.”

Miss Melody rang the gentle bell again. “What did we learn today?”

Ping stood tall. “When I make a mistake, I can pause, breathe, say sorry, and make amends. I can fix it with kind actions. Exercise helps me wait. Forgiveness helps us feel better.”

Miss Melody beamed. “Beautiful, Ping.”

Ping felt light, like a song. He patted his drum gently—tap, tap—and whispered, “I can be patient. I can make it right.”

The End.
