# Robo's Big Game Day

In the cozy study corner of Emma's house, a shiny blue robot dog named Robo sat quietly by the bookshelf. His LED eyes glowed softly as he watched Emma slumped over her desk, looking very tired.

"Beep beep! Emma, you look sleepy," said Robo in his cheerful robot voice. "What's wrong?"

Emma rubbed her eyes. "I have a big soccer game tomorrow, but I feel so tired and weak. I don't think I can run fast or kick the ball well."

Robo's tail wagged with excitement. "Beep! I can help you get ready! But first, tell me what you ate today."

Emma thought for a moment. "I had candy for breakfast, chips for lunch, and more candy for snack time."

"Oh my!" beeped Robo. "No wonder you feel tired! Your body needs good fuel, just like I need the right energy to charge my batteries."

Robo rolled over to Emma's desk and projected a colorful picture on the wall with his built-in projector. "Look! These are power foods for athletes like you!"

The picture showed bright red apples, orange carrots, green broccoli, and brown whole grain bread. "These foods give you energy that lasts all day long!" Robo explained.

"But I don't like vegetables," Emma said, making a face.

"Beep beep! Let me show you something fun!" Robo's eyes sparkled. He rolled to the corner and started doing jumping jacks with his metal legs making soft clicking sounds. "Exercise makes healthy food taste better! When your body works hard, it wants good fuel!"

Emma giggled at the sight of Robo exercising. "You look funny doing jumping jacks!"

"Want to try with me?" asked Robo. "We can pretend we're training for your soccer game!"

Emma stood up and started jumping with Robo. Soon they were both laughing and moving around the study corner. They did stretches, pretended to kick soccer balls, and even did silly robot dances.

"Wow, I feel more awake already!" Emma said, breathing a little faster but smiling big.

"Beep! That's because exercise wakes up your body!" said Robo. "Now, let's go ask your mom for some power foods. I bet she has apples and peanut butter!"

In the kitchen, Emma's mom was so happy to see Emma asking for healthy snacks. "Of course, sweetie! How about apple slices with peanut butter and a glass of milk?"

As Emma crunched the sweet, juicy apple, she said, "Mmm, this actually tastes really good! And the peanut butter is creamy and yummy!"

"See?" beeped Robo proudly. "Good food tastes great when your body needs it!"

That evening, Emma went to bed early after eating a healthy dinner of chicken, rice, and colorful vegetables. She felt strong and excited for her game.

The next day, Emma ran faster than ever before on the soccer field. She kicked the ball with power and played the whole game without getting tired. After scoring a goal, she looked over at Robo cheering from the sidelines.

"Thank you, Robo!" she called out. "You taught me that my body is like a car - it needs the right fuel and exercise to run its best!"

"Beep beep! You're welcome, champion!" Robo replied, his LED eyes glowing with pride.

From that day on, Emma always remembered to eat power foods and exercise regularly. And Robo was always there in the study corner, ready to help her stay healthy and strong.

The End.
