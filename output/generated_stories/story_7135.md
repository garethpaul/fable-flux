# Miri Mouse Makes It Right

In a quiet house, behind a small wooden door, there was a cozy closet hideaway. Coats hung like sleepy trees. Scarves felt soft like clouds. The air smelled like cedar and soap.

A kind mouse named Miri lived there. Miri had bright eyes and careful paws. She loved to help. She loved to make art, too.

One rainy morning, drip-drop, drip-drop, Miri peeked out. She found a big cardboard box on the closet floor. Inside were buttons, ribbons, crayons, and a glue stick. They were shiny and colorful. Miri’s whiskers wiggled with joy.

“Oh! Art time!” Miri squeaked.

She spread the buttons on a scarf like tiny moons. She drew swirls with a purple crayon. She glued ribbon in a zigzag line. Snip-snip with a little paper scrap. Stick-stick with the glue. Swish-swish with the crayon. The closet felt warm, and Miri hummed a happy tune.

Then, oops!

Miri bumped a coat. THUMP. The coat fell. A shoe tumbled—clonk! A box of socks tipped over and spilled—plop, plop, plop—soft socks everywhere.

Miri’s eyes grew wide. Her ears drooped. She looked at the mess: buttons rolling, ribbons trailing, socks scattered, a crayon line on the coat sleeve.

“Oh no,” whispered Miri. “I made a mess. This is not kind.”

She heard footsteps. Tap, tap, tap. The door opened a crack. Light slipped in. A gentle voice said, “Oh dear, what happened in here?”

It was Lily, the child who shared the house with Miri. Lily didn’t see Miri yet. She looked at the tumbled coat and the spilled socks.

Miri’s heart thumped. She wanted to hide. But Miri was a kind mouse. Kind mice make amends.

“I will make it right,” Miri said to herself. “I can fix this.”

Miri took a deep breath. In, out. She felt brave. She stepped out where Lily could see her.

“Squeak,” said Miri softly. “I’m sorry.”

Lily blinked. “Oh! Hello, little mouse,” she whispered, smiling. “Did you knock things down?”

Miri nodded. “I was making art. I made a mess. I want to help.”

Lily knelt down. “Thank you for telling me. We can clean together.”

Miri’s ears perked up. “Yes. We can.”

They got to work. It became a clean-up game.

- Miri rolled the buttons into a tiny cup. Tink, tink, tink.
- Lily folded the socks. Flip, fold, pat.
- Miri tugged the ribbon into a neat loop. Swish, pull, tie.
- Lily hung the coat back up. Swoosh. The cedar smell came back.

Miri saw the crayon line on the coat sleeve. She felt a squeeze in her tummy. “I am sorry for the crayon,” she said.

Lily nodded. “Crayon on fabric is tricky. But we can try.” She brought a little damp cloth. Dab, dab, dab. The purple faded. Miri held the sleeve steady with gentle paws.

“There,” said Lily. “Better.”

Miri still wanted to make amends. “I will do more. I can help with chores.”

“What chores can a mouse do?” Lily asked with a giggle.

Miri’s eyes sparkled. “Mouse chores! I can sort the paper scraps. I can line up the shoes. I can make a sign.”

“A sign?” Lily tilted her head.

Miri nodded. “A sign for the art box, so we put things away.”

They worked again:

- Miri nudged the small shoes into a row. Tap, tap, toe-to-toe.
- Lily shook out the scarves, then hung them up. Floof.
- Miri gathered tiny paper pieces into a clean jar. Rustle, pat.
- Together, they wiped the floor. Swish, swish, swish.

Now the closet hideaway looked neat and calm. It smelled like cedar and lemon. The light from the door made soft stripes on the floor.

“Let’s make the sign,” said Lily.

Miri found a sturdy card. She drew a simple picture with big shapes. A box with crayons, a ribbon, and a button. She added arrows to show “in the box.” She used only a little glue this time. Stick. Press. Done.

Lily wrote the words “ART GOES HERE” next to Miri’s drawing. The letters were round and friendly.

They taped the sign to the box. Snap.

Miri felt her heart grow light and warm. Her whiskers lifted again.

“Thank you for helping,” said Lily.

“Thank you for letting me fix it,” said Miri. “I learned to say sorry. I learned to clean up. I learned to make a plan.”

Lily smiled. “You made amends. That means you did something to make it right.”

Miri nodded. “Making amends feels good.”

Lily looked at Miri’s first art—buttons, ribbon, swirls. “It’s beautiful,” she said. “Maybe we can make art together next time, and clean up together too.”

Miri clapped her tiny paws. “Yes! Art and chores. We can be tidy artists.”

They both laughed. The closet was peaceful again. The coats hung like tall, quiet trees. The ribbons were rolled like little snails. The socks were cozy in their box, soft as marshmallows.

Miri curled in a corner on a patch of felt. She felt proud and calm. The rain outside slowed. Drip... drip... stop.

Miri whispered, “When I make a mess, I make it right. I can say sorry. I can help. I can be resourceful.”

She looked at the sign one more time and smiled.

The End.
