# Rollo’s Quiet Song on the Porch

Rollo was a musical raccoon with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved making music. He wore a little red scarf and carried a small banjo that went twang, twang, twang. Every evening, he sat on the peaceful front porch of his little blue house. The porch smelled like warm wood and sweet flowers. A soft breeze touched Rollo’s whiskers, and the sky turned pink and gold.

Rollo plucked his banjo. “Twang, twang,” he sang. “Hello, porch. Hello, day.” He tapped his striped tail to the beat. Birds chirped. Crickets hummed. The porch steps creaked a happy creak.

But one evening, Rollo felt a jumble inside. His tummy felt wiggly. His paws felt busy. His thoughts were zingy and zappy, like popcorn popping. “Oh no,” Rollo said softly. “I have a busy feeling. My song sounds bumpy!”

He strummed again. The notes came out too fast. Twang-twang-twang-twang! He frowned. “My song is not calm,” he said. “How can I make a quiet song?”

Just then, Mrs. Bluebird landed on the railing. “Chirp?” she asked. “Why the fast song, Rollo?”

“I have a problem,” Rollo said. “My heart feels fast, and my thoughts run around. I want a peaceful song, but I can’t find one.”

Mrs. Bluebird tilted her head. “Try a small breath,” she chirped. “In and out.”

Rollo nodded. He sat tall. He put one paw on his belly and one paw on his heart. He smelled the flowers in. He blew a tiny cloud out. In. Out. In. Out.

He listened. The porch was quiet and kind. The breeze whispered, “Shhh.” The wood was warm under his paws. His belly moved in and out under his paw. His heart slowed. Thump. Thump.

Rollo smiled a little. “Thank you, Mrs. Bluebird,” he said. “That helps.” But when he tried to play again, his friend Tilly Squirrel came racing up the steps.

“Rollo! Rollo!” Tilly squeaked. “I lost my shiny acorn! It was here this morning. Now it’s gone!” Her tail shook. Her eyes were wide.

Rollo put down his banjo. “Let’s solve this together,” he said. “But first, let’s make our bodies calm so our minds can think. Can we sit for a moment?”

Tilly blinked. “Sit? Now?”

“Yes,” Rollo said gently. He patted the porch. “We will do Mindful Ears, Mindful Nose, and Mindful Toes.”

Tilly sat next to him. “What are those?” she asked.

“Mindful Ears,” Rollo said, touching his ear, “means we listen for three sounds. Ready?” They closed their eyes.

“I hear crickets,” said Tilly.

“I hear the wind in the leaves,” Rollo said.

“I hear your banjo string humming,” Tilly whispered.

“Good,” said Rollo. “Now, Mindful Nose. We smell three smells.”

“I smell flowers,” Tilly said.

“I smell warm wood,” said Rollo.

“I smell... peanut butter from your snack,” Tilly giggled.

Rollo laughed. “Yes! Now, Mindful Toes. We feel three feelings.”

“My toes feel the smooth porch,” said Tilly.

“My back feels the cool railing,” said Rollo.

“My whiskers feel the breeze,” said Tilly.

Rollo opened his eyes. “How do you feel now?”

“Slower,” Tilly said. “My tail is not shaking so much.”

“Me too,” said Rollo. “Now our minds are ready. Let’s tell the story of your shiny acorn. Stories help us remember.”

Tilly took a breath. “Once upon a time—this morning—I had a very shiny acorn. I put it right here on the step.” She touched the second step, which felt smooth and dusty. “Then I danced. I did twirls and hops. Then I chased a butterfly. Then I came back, and the shiny acorn was gone.”

Rollo nodded. “That is a good story. Let me add a part.” He plucked a slow, gentle note. “Twang.” He spoke in a soft storyteller voice. “While Tilly twirled, the wind came by and said, ‘Whoosh.’ The wind likes to move light things. Where might the wind carry an acorn?”

They both looked around. The porch had a little mat by the door. It had a picture of a sun and the word “Hello.” Rollo lifted the corner. Dust tickled his nose. “Achoo!”

“Bless you,” Tilly said.

“Thank you,” said Rollo. “No acorn here.”

They peered under the rocking chair that went creak, creak. Rollo’s whiskers brushed the floor. “Not here,” he said.

Mrs. Bluebird chirped from the railing. “Look by the flower pot!”

Rollo and Tilly crawled to the big red pot with bright yellow flowers. The dirt smelled rich. The pot felt cool. Tilly gasped. “I see a sparkle!” She reached behind the pot and pulled out her shiny acorn. “I found it!”

Rollo clapped his paws. “Hooray! The wind must have rolled it here.”

Tilly hugged the acorn. “Thank you, Rollo. Your mindful listening and smelling and feeling helped me calm down. And your story helped me think.”

Rollo picked up his banjo. He strummed slowly. Twang… twang… twang… The notes were round and soft, like marshmallows. “When we are calm, our songs are calm,” he said.

Tilly swayed. “Can we sing a porch song?”

“Yes,” said Rollo. He sang, “Breathe in slow, breathe out soft. Listen low, listen aloft. Feel your toes, feel your nose. Peace will come, and worry goes.”

Tilly sang too. Mrs. Bluebird chirped along. The breeze hummed. The porch glowed with golden evening light. Rollo’s tummy felt settled. His thoughts were smooth, like a quiet pond.

Rollo smiled at his friend. “Today we learned a lesson,” he said. “When feelings get big, we can stop, breathe, and use our senses. We can tell the story, and we can find peaceful solutions.”

Tilly nodded. “Mindful ears, mindful nose, mindful toes,” she said. “And a calm song.”

They sat together on the peaceful front porch, singing softly as the stars came out, one by one.

The End.
