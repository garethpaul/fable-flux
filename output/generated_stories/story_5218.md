# Milo Mouse and the Big Day

Milo Mouse lived in a cozy corner of Mama's home office. His tiny house was tucked behind the big computer, where he could hear the gentle *tap-tap-tap* of fingers on the keyboard every day.

This morning was different, though. Mama was rushing around, papers flying everywhere. "Oh no! I have such an important meeting today, and I can't find my notes anywhere!" she said, her voice shaky and worried.

Milo peeked out from his little door. He saw Mama's hands trembling as she moved stacks of papers around. Her breathing was fast and shallow. Milo's whiskers twitched with concern.

"I want to help," Milo whispered to himself. "But first, I need to stay calm so I can think clearly."

Milo sat down on his tiny cushion and took three deep breaths, just like his grandmother had taught him. *In... and out... in... and out... in... and out.* He felt his little heart slow down and his mind become clearer.

"Now I can think better," he said softly. "What would be the best way to help Mama?"

Milo remembered seeing Mama's important blue folder yesterday. She had placed it on the shelf behind her favorite coffee mug - the one with yellow sunflowers that smelled like vanilla.

But how could a tiny mouse help a big human? Milo thought carefully. He couldn't carry the heavy folder, but maybe he could show Mama where it was.

"I'll need to communicate somehow," Milo decided. "But I must be gentle and calm, so I don't startle her."

Milo scurried quietly to the shelf. He found a small, shiny paperclip and pushed it with his nose until it fell - *ping!* - right next to the blue folder.

Mama looked up at the sound. "That's odd," she said.

Milo gently knocked over a pencil so it rolled toward the shelf. *Roll, roll, tap.*

"Hmm," Mama said, walking over to investigate. Her eyes landed on the blue folder. "My notes! They're right here! How did I miss them?"

Milo smiled his tiny mouse smile. He had helped without causing any fuss or fear.

But Mama still looked stressed. Her shoulders were tight, and she was breathing quickly again. Milo decided to help her feel calm too.

He found a small piece of lavender that had fallen from Mama's tea bag. Carefully, he pushed it where she would notice the gentle, soothing smell.

When Mama caught the sweet scent, she paused. "Lavender," she said softly. She took a deep breath, then another. "I need to calm down. Getting upset won't help my meeting go well."

Mama sat in her chair and breathed deeply, just like Milo had done. Her shoulders relaxed, and her hands stopped shaking.

"Much better," she said with a smile. "Now I can prepare properly for my meeting."

From his hiding spot, Milo felt warm and happy. By staying calm himself, he had been able to think clearly and find a way to help. His small act of community service had made a big difference.

As Mama began preparing for her meeting with steady hands and a peaceful mind, Milo curled up in his cozy corner. He had learned that when we stay calm, we can communicate better, make good decisions, and help others in our community - even in small but important ways.

The End.
