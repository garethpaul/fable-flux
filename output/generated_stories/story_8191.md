# Benny Beaver Takes a Break

Benny Beaver was the busiest beaver in the whole sunny backyard. Every morning when the golden sun peeked over the fence, Benny would start working right away.

"Chop, chop, chop!" went Benny's strong teeth as he gathered sticks. "Build, build, build!" he hummed as he stacked them high. The warm sunshine made his brown fur feel toasty, and the sweet smell of flowers filled the air around him.

But today, something felt different. Benny's paws were tired and achy. His tummy rumbled loudly like thunder. His eyes felt heavy like they had tiny weights on them.

"I can't stop now," Benny said to himself, wiping sweat from his furry forehead. "I have so much work to do!"

Just then, Ruby Rabbit hopped over with her fluffy white tail bouncing. "Good morning, Benny! You look very tired today."

"I'm fine, I'm fine," Benny said quickly, but his voice sounded scratchy and weak.

Ruby's pink nose twitched with worry. "When did you last eat breakfast? Or take a drink of cool water?"

Benny stopped chopping and thought hard. "Um... yesterday? Maybe the day before?" His tummy rumbled even louder.

"Oh my!" squeaked Sally Squirrel as she scampered down from her tree. "Benny, you need to take care of yourself!"

"But I have to finish my dam," Benny protested. "Everyone is counting on me!"

Ruby and Sally looked at each other with understanding smiles. They knew how hard it was to ask for help sometimes.

"We have an idea," said Ruby gently. "What if we help you with your work? Then you can rest and eat!"

"Really?" Benny's tired eyes grew wide with surprise. "You would do that for me?"

"Of course!" chirped Sally. "Friends help friends. But first, you need to take care of yourself."

Sally brought Benny fresh, crunchy carrots and sweet berries. Ruby filled a big leaf with cool, sparkling water from the pond. The water felt so refreshing as it trickled down Benny's throat.

"Mmm, this tastes like sunshine!" Benny said, munching happily on a juicy berry.

After eating, Benny felt much better. His paws stopped aching, his tummy stopped rumbling, and his eyes felt bright and alert again.

"Now I can help too!" Benny said with renewed energy.

Working together, the three friends finished the dam in half the time. Ruby used her strong legs to carry heavy logs. Sally used her quick paws to weave small twigs together. And Benny used his special teeth to shape everything perfectly.

"This is so much more fun than working alone," Benny laughed as they splashed in the cool water.

As the sun began to set, painting the sky in pretty pink and orange colors, the friends sat together admiring their beautiful work.

"Thank you for teaching me something important today," Benny said thoughtfully. "I learned that taking care of myself isn't selfish – it helps me be a better friend and worker."

"And asking for help doesn't make you weak," added Ruby. "It makes you smart!"

"Tomorrow, I'll remember to eat breakfast, drink water, and rest when I need to," promised Benny.

The three friends watched fireflies begin to twinkle in the gathering dusk, feeling happy and proud of their teamwork.

The End.
