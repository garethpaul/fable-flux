# Mimi’s Colorful Plan

Mimi was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She lived in a playful living room full of cozy pillows, bouncy rugs, and warm sunbeams that danced on the floor. One sunny morning, Mimi stretched her tiny paws and smiled.

“Today, I have a goal,” Mimi said to herself. “I will make a picture for the wall. I will use colors, shapes, and my big mouse heart.”

She looked around the living room. The soft rug felt squishy under her feet. The air smelled like fresh bread from the kitchen. On the low table, she found crayons, paper, stickers, and glue. She clapped her paws. “This is perfect!”

Mimi made a simple plan. She even said it out loud, because saying a plan makes it real. “First, I will choose my colors. Next, I will draw my ideas. Then, I will add stickers and glue. Last, I will clean up and hang my art.”

Her best friend, a tiny toy bird named Pip, wobbled on the arm of the couch. Pip had a squeaky voice. “Cheep-cheep! What will you draw, Mimi?”

Mimi’s whiskers twitched. “I will draw a happy day in our living room. With the big yellow sun on the rug, the bright pillows, and you, Pip!”

Pip bounced. “Cheep! That is a good goal!”

Mimi picked up a yellow crayon. It felt warm in her paw from the sun. “This color is for sunshine,” she said. She made big gentle circles. Swish, swish. The paper made a soft scratchy sound.

Next, she used blue for the sky in the window. Then red and green for the pillows. She pressed lightly, then harder, feeling the crayon glide. “I like how this feels,” Mimi giggled. “It’s smooth and waxy.”

But then Mimi paused. Her picture looked messy in one corner. The green scribble bumped the red pillow. Mimi’s ears drooped. “Oh no,” she whispered. “It’s not perfect.”

Pip hopped closer. “Cheep! Remember your plan. You can fix it step by step.”

Mimi took a deep breath. In, out. The living room smelled like apples now. She smiled a little. “You are right. I can try again.”

She set a small goal: “I will draw one pillow at a time.” She used short, slow lines. She hummed a happy tune. Hum-hum, hum-hum. The pillows looked neat and bright. “I did it!” she cheered.

Next came the stickers. Mimi touched a shiny star. It felt smooth and cool. “Stars for sparkle,” she said. She pressed a gold star near the sun. Stick, press. Then she added a tiny sticker of a bird near the couch. “That one is you, Pip.”

Pip flapped his little wings. “Cheep! I love it.”

Mimi added glue swirls for a silly rug pattern. The glue was sticky and smelled a little sweet. She sprinkled soft paper confetti on the glue. Pitter-patter. It made a fun, bumpy texture. She ran her paw over it. “Bumpy and beautiful!”

Soon, Mimi felt a bit tired. Her paws were sticky. There were crayon bits and paper scraps on the table. She still had the last step: clean up and hang the art. Mimi frowned. “I want to play now.”

Pip tilted his head. “Cheep! What is your goal?”

Mimi looked at her picture. It was bright and full of love. “My goal is to finish my art and hang it on the wall.”

She made a tiny clean-up song to help her finish the task.
“Pick up, pick up, one-two-three,
Crayon bits and scraps with me.
Wipe and fold, count to four,
Now our room is neat once more!”

She sang and moved. Pick, place. Pick, place. The sound of the trash bin went swish. The table felt smooth again under her paw. Mimi washed her paws in a small bowl; the water was cool and splashy. Splish-splash! She giggled.

“Last step,” Mimi said. She held up the picture. It smelled like paper and a tiny bit like glue. She hung it on the wall with a soft press. Tap, tap.

Mimi and Pip stepped back. The living room looked extra bright. The sun on the rug in the picture seemed to glow. The pillows looked soft and happy. The tiny bird sticker near the couch made Pip squeak with joy.

“It is wonderful,” Pip said. “Cheep-cheep!”

Mimi’s heart felt warm. “I made a plan. I set a goal. I used my colors and my ideas. I finished my tasks. I did my best.”

Pip nodded. “And you fixed little problems, step by step.”

Mimi smiled. “Art can be many things. It can be neat, or scribbly, or shiny, or bumpy. Today, my art shows our happy room and my happy feelings.”

They cuddled on a bright pillow. The room was cozy and calm. Mimi whispered, “When I make art, I share my inside feelings with the outside world.”

Pip chirped softly. “Cheep. That is creative.”

Mimi nodded. “Yes. My idea became a picture because I had a plan, I kept going, and I finished.”

She looked at the wall and said, “I can set a new goal tomorrow!”

The End.
