# Riku Raccoon’s Bedtime Story Song

Riku was a musical raccoon who loved gentle drum taps, soft strums on a tiny toy guitar, and little hums that sounded like warm cocoa. His cozy bedroom smelled like clean blankets and sweet pine. Moonlight painted silver stripes on the rug, and fireflies blinked hello at the window.

“Plink, plunk, hum,” Riku sang as he tucked his tail under a quilt. “I can make a bedtime song!”

Riku’s best friend, Miri the mouse, peeked in. “Hi, Riku,” she squeaked. “Can I hear a story?”

Riku’s whiskers twitched. He loved stories. He loved songs. He loved mixing them together. “Yes!” he said. “I will sing a story about my day.”

Miri climbed onto a pillow. The pillow went floof! It felt soft like a cloud. Riku strummed his guitar. “Plink,” went the tiny string. He tapped a tin can drum. “Tap-tap,” like raindrops on a leaf.

Riku began, “Today, I went to the big berry bush. A bird tried to peck my hat. I said—”

Miri raised her paw. “Riku, I like stories where I feel safe. Can you make it gentle?”

Riku blinked. “Oh,” he said. He thought. His heart felt like a soft drum. “Yes. Thank you for telling me. That is your boundary. I can make it gentle.”

He tried again. “Today, I went to the big berry bush. A bird sang near my hat. I said, ‘Hello, bird!’ And we both listened to the wind.” He strummed slow. “Plink-plink,” like tiny stars.

Miri smiled. “I like that.”

Riku felt warm and proud. He took a breath. His nose smelled lavender from the little pillow spray. “Now the chorus!” he said. He sang, “Be kind, be true, tell stories new. Ask first, listen too.”

Miri swayed. “That’s a good song.”

Riku told more. “Once, I felt mad when my block tower fell.” He looked at Miri. “Is it okay if I tell about big feelings?”

Miri nodded. “Yes. Thank you for asking.”

Riku nodded back. He tapped, “tap-tap,” like tiny footsteps. “I felt mad. My chest went tight. My paws felt hot. I took three slow breaths.” He showed one—“In.” He showed two—“Out.” He showed three—“In and out.” His voice got soft. “Then I asked for help. And we built again.”

Miri clapped her tiny hands. “That helps me too.”

Riku’s door creaked. Mama Raccoon peeked in with a smile. “I hear music,” she whispered. “Is it time for a bedtime story song?”

“Yes!” Riku chirped. He patted a spot on the rug. Mama sat. The quilt rustled. The room felt extra cozy, like a hug.

Riku strummed and told a new part. “One time, my friend wanted to use my drum without asking. My tummy felt twisty.”

Miri’s ears wiggled. “That happened to me with my crayons.”

Riku nodded. “I said, ‘Please ask first. My drum is special.’ My friend said, ‘Oh! I will ask next time.’ And when I was ready, I said, ‘Now we can take turns.’”

Mama Raccoon smiled. “You told your boundary with kind words. That is brave.”

Miri asked, “What is a boundary?”

Riku thought. He tapped his chin. “A boundary is a kind line that keeps our bodies, our hearts, and our things safe. We can say yes. We can say no. We can say later.”

Miri whispered, “I like that.”

Riku made a little rhythm to help remember. “Yes, no, later,” he sang, with each word a soft tap on the drum. “Yes.” Tap. “No.” Tap. “Later.” Tap.

Miri tried it. “Yes.” Tap. “No.” Tap. “Later.” Tap. She giggled. “It feels good to say what I need.”

Riku added more story. “When my friend looked sad after I said no, I asked, ‘Do you need a hug or space?’ My friend said, ‘Space, please.’ So I gave space. Later, we played again.” He looked at Miri. “That is empathy. It means we care how others feel.”

Miri’s eyes shone. “Empathy is like a gentle blanket.”

Riku nodded. “Yes. It keeps feelings warm.”

He strummed the chorus again, and Mama hummed along. “Be kind, be true, tell stories new. Ask first, listen too.”

Miri snuggled down. The clock made soft tick-tock sounds. The window breeze smelled like night rain. Riku felt calm. He had one more part to tell.

“I have a storytelling plan,” he said. “First, I ask if my story feels safe for friends. Next, I use kind words. I listen to their words too. I share my feelings. I breathe when I need to. Then we rest.”

Mama whispered, “That is a wise plan.”

Riku closed his eyes and sang in a whisper, “We can tell gentle stories. We can set kind boundaries. We can care for each other.”

Miri yawned. “Thank you, Riku. Your story song helps my heart.”

Riku tucked the guitar away. He turned the lantern low. The room glowed like warm honey. He pulled the quilt to his chin and smiled.

“I like telling stories that help,” he said. “Tomorrow, I will ask, I will listen, and I will tell a new story.”

“Me too,” said Miri. “Yes. No. Later.” Tap, tap, tap.

Mama kissed Riku’s head. “Good night, my storytellers.”

“Good night,” Riku and Miri whispered.

The cozy bedroom was quiet, and the night felt safe and kind.

The End.
