# Fern's Big Feelings at Dinner Time

Little Fern the fawn had soft brown spots and the biggest, kindest eyes in the whole forest. Today was special because her best friend Bunny was coming for dinner at her family's cozy dining room.

Fern bounced on her tiny hooves as she looked at the wooden table. "Mama, when will Bunny get here? I'm so excited!" she said, her fluffy white tail wiggling.

"Soon, little one," Mama Deer smiled, placing pretty blue plates on the table. "But first, we need to set everything up nicely."

Fern tried to help, but she felt wiggly and jumpy inside. Her heart was beating fast like a drum - thump, thump, thump! She accidentally knocked over the salt shaker with her hoof.

"Oops!" Fern said, feeling her cheeks get warm. Now she felt excited AND worried at the same time. "Mama, my tummy feels funny. Like there are butterflies dancing in there!"

Mama Deer knelt down and gently touched Fern's nose. "Those are your feelings, sweetheart. When we're excited and a little nervous, our bodies tell us. Can you tell me what you're feeling right now?"

Fern thought carefully. "I'm happy because Bunny is coming. But I'm also worried because I want everything to be perfect. And I feel... bouncy!"

"Those are wonderful feelings to notice," Mama said. "When we have big feelings, it helps to take deep breaths. Want to try?"

Together, they breathed in slowly through their noses - sniff - and out through their mouths - whoosh. Fern felt the butterflies in her tummy start to calm down.

"Now," Mama said, "let's use our time wisely. We have fifteen minutes before Bunny arrives. What would help you feel ready?"

Fern looked around the dining room. The afternoon sunlight made everything glow golden through the windows. "Maybe I could put the napkins by each plate? And pick some flowers for the middle?"

"Perfect ideas!" Mama said.

Fern carefully placed soft yellow napkins beside each blue plate. Then she trotted outside and picked three pretty daisies that smelled like sunshine. When she put them in a small vase on the table, everything looked beautiful.

Knock, knock! Bunny had arrived!

"Fern!" Bunny hopped into the dining room, her whiskers twitching with excitement. "This looks so pretty! I brought you something!"

Bunny gave Fern a smooth, heart-shaped rock she had found. "Thank you!" Fern said, hugging her friend. "I was feeling really excited and nervous before you came, but Mama taught me to notice my feelings and breathe."

"I was nervous too!" Bunny admitted. "My ears kept twitching!"

During dinner, they ate sweet carrots and fresh lettuce. The friends talked and laughed, and Fern felt warm and happy inside. She had learned something important today.

"Mama," Fern said as they cleaned up together, "I'm glad you taught me about feelings. When I noticed them and took deep breaths, I felt better. And planning our time helped too!"

Mama Deer nuzzled Fern's head. "You're learning to understand your heart, little one. That's one of the most important things you can learn."

That night, Fern fell asleep holding Bunny's special rock, feeling proud that she had learned to recognize her feelings and use her time well to make everything perfect for her friend.

The End.
