# Rocco’s Rainbow Rhythm

Rocco was a musical raccoon with bright eyes and soft, stripey paws. He lived in a playful living room full of cozy pillows, colorful rugs, and twinkly lights. His toy drum went boom-boom, his tiny bell went ding-ding, and his little ukulele went plink-plunk. The air smelled like warm popcorn, and sunbeams danced on the floor.

Rocco loved to make music. He bobbed his head and hummed, “La-la-la!” Today he wanted to play with his friends. He set out cups of warm cocoa and a bowl of crunchy apple slices.

Knock, knock!

In came three friends. Lina the lion cub carried a small drum from her grandma. Milo the monkey brought a wooden flute. Aya the little bird had shiny ankle bells that chimed when she walked.

“Hi!” Rocco said, tail swishing. “Let’s play a song!”

“Yes!” said Milo. “Tweet-tweet!” sang Aya. Lina smiled and patted her drum.

Rocco started first. “Boom-boom, ding-ding, plink-plunk!” He played fast and loud. His heart thumped with joy.

Milo put his flute to his lips and played a soft tune, “Peeeep-peep,” like a gentle breeze. Aya stepped lightly. Her bells went “chingle-chingle” like tiny stars. Lina tapped her drum, “dum, dum,” slow and steady.

Rocco’s ears twitched. “Wait,” he said. “We don’t sound the same. My song goes fast. Your songs go slow. It is not working!”

He tried again, faster. “Boom-boom-boom!”

Milo’s flute whispered, “peep,” and got quiet.

Aya stopped moving. Her feathers trembled. Lina’s ears folded. “It is okay,” Lina said softly. “In my family, we play our drum slow and low.”

“In my home,” said Milo, “we play songs like wind in the trees.”

“In my family,” chirped Aya, “we dance and bells tell the beat.”

Rocco felt hot in his cheeks. His tail drooped. “But I want one song,” he said. “I want it to sound just right.”

His chest felt tight. He stomped his paw and crossed his arms. A little growl bubbled, “Grr.” Tears tickled his eyes.

Lina took a slow breath. “Rocco, I see you feel upset.”

Aya nodded. “We can calm our bodies.”

Milo said, “Let’s do three belly breaths together.”

“Okay,” Rocco whispered.

They all put hands or wings on their bellies.

“In,” said Lina. They breathed in. The room smelled like cocoa and apples.

“Out,” said Milo. They breathed out. Rocco felt softer inside.

“Again,” said Aya. In and out.

Rocco’s shoulders dropped. “Thank you,” he said. “I was frustrated. I want us to play together. But I don’t understand your songs.”

Lina smiled. “We can teach each other.”

Milo’s eyes sparkled. “We bring music from our families.”

Aya jingled her bells. “Different is not wrong. Different is new.”

Rocco’s ears perked up. “I am curious. Can you show me?”

Lina patted her drum. “In my family, we say, ‘dum… dum… dum-dum.’” She tapped a slow, strong beat. The rug buzzed under their paws.

Milo lifted his flute. “In my home, Nana plays this melody.” He blew a soft, wavy line, “peeeep, peep, peep,” like leaves swishing.

Aya stepped across the rug. “In my family, bells mark the steps.” Her ankle bells chimed, “ching-ching,” with each gentle hop.

Rocco listened. He closed his eyes. He felt the slow dum in his belly. He heard the soft peep in his ears. He followed the ching with his toes. He opened his eyes and smiled.

“I have an idea,” Rocco said. “We can make a rainbow rhythm! Each color is a sound.”

“Show us,” said Milo.

Rocco placed colored pillows in a line. “Red for Lina’s drum. Blue for Milo’s flute. Yellow for Aya’s bells. Green for my ukulele.”

They stood on the pillows, one by one.

“Red,” said Rocco.

Lina played “dum.”

“Blue,” said Rocco.

Milo played “peeeep.”

“Yellow,” said Rocco.

Aya jingled “ching.”

“Green,” said Rocco.

Rocco strummed “plink.”

They went slowly at first. Dum… peeeep… ching… plink.

They smiled. It fit! The room felt warm and bright. The twinkly lights seemed to wink.

“Again!” giggled Aya.

Faster now: dum, peeeep, ching, plink! The sounds danced together like friends holding hands. Rocco added a boom-boom to end. Milo trilled, Aya twirled, Lina rolled her drum.

When they finished, they laughed and sipped cocoa. Rocco’s tail swished happily.

“I learned your songs,” Rocco said. “Your music is special.”

“And we learned yours,” said Lina.

“We listened and talked,” said Milo. “We used words and breaths.”

“We turned our feelings into a plan,” chirped Aya.

Rocco nodded. “Different sounds can make one beautiful song. Different families can make one happy team.”

They played their rainbow rhythm again. The living room glowed with color and sound—dum, peeeep, ching, plink—like a kind, cozy hug.

That night, Rocco put his instruments away. He patted each one. “Thank you,” he whispered. He felt brave and calm. He knew that listening showed love. He knew that sharing made him strong. He knew that when he felt frustrated, he could breathe, talk, and try a new way.

Rocco looked at his friends. “We made music from many places,” he said. “Together is better.”

“Together is better,” everyone echoed, smiling.

The End.
