# Polly's Paintbrush Problem

In a bright, colorful art room filled with paints, brushes, and paper, lived a beautiful green parrot named Polly. She had bright red feathers on her head and loved to create amazing pictures with her talented beak.

Every morning, Polly would flutter her wings excitedly as she flew around the art room. "Good morning, paintbrushes! Good morning, crayons!" she would chirp happily. The art room smelled like fresh paint and new paper, and sunshine streamed through the big windows.

One busy Tuesday, Polly was working on a special painting of a rainbow. She dipped her beak in red paint, then blue, then yellow. "This is going to be my most beautiful painting ever!" she squawked with joy.

But Polly was so excited about her artwork that she forgot to clean her beak between colors. Soon, her beautiful rainbow turned into a muddy brown mess. Even worse, she had gotten paint all over her bright green feathers and hadn't washed her wings all day.

When her friend Benny the bunny hopped into the art room, he wrinkled his nose. "Polly, you smell like old paint, and your feathers look very messy," he said, stepping back.

Polly felt embarrassed and angry. "My painting is ruined, and now you're being mean!" she squawked loudly. In her frustration, she accidentally knocked over a jar of purple paint with her wing. SPLASH! The paint spilled all over Benny's clean white fur.

"Oh no!" cried Benny, looking down at his purple-spotted fur. "Now I'm all messy too!"

Polly felt terrible. She had made everything worse by not keeping clean and by getting angry. "I'm sorry, Benny," she whispered, hanging her head low. "I didn't mean to spill paint on you."

Benny looked at his purple spots and then at Polly's sad face. He could see that she was truly sorry. "It's okay, Polly," he said gently. "Everyone makes mistakes. I forgive you."

"Really?" asked Polly, her bright eyes hopeful.

"Really," smiled Benny. "But maybe we should both get cleaned up now!"

Polly nodded eagerly. "You're right! Let's go to the sink and wash up properly."

Together, they walked to the big sink in the corner of the art room. Polly carefully washed her beak and feathers with warm, soapy water while Benny cleaned the purple paint from his fur. The water felt refreshing and cool.

"You know what?" said Polly as she dried her now-sparkling feathers. "I feel so much better when I'm clean! And I have an idea - let's do some flying exercises to dry off!"

Polly spread her wings wide and flew in happy circles around the art room. "Flying is great exercise!" she called to Benny, who was hopping and jumping to dry his fur.

After their cleaning and exercise, Polly started a new painting - this time being very careful to clean her beak between each color. Benny watched and cheered her on.

"Thank you for forgiving me, Benny," said Polly as she painted a beautiful, clean rainbow. "And thank you for helping me remember to stay clean and healthy."

"That's what friends do," said Benny with a warm smile. "We forgive each other and help each other be our best!"

From that day on, Polly always remembered to keep her feathers clean, exercise her wings every day, and most importantly, to forgive her friends when they made mistakes, just like Benny had forgiven her.

The End.
