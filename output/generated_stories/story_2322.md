# Dotty the Dolphin Solves the Color Mix-Up

In a bright, splashy creative art room by the sea, a playful dolphin named Dotty wiggled her silky tail. The room smelled like fresh paper and soapy clean brushes. Sunlight made rainbows on the walls, and gentle waves hummed outside the window.

“Today is art day!” Dotty chirped. Her voice clicked and squeaked with joy. She loved colors, glitter, and glue. She loved friends, too. In the room were Star the sea star, Bubbles the little fish, and Coral the crab. They all wore tiny paint aprons. The aprons were soft and a little crinkly.

“Let’s paint a big ocean mural,” Dotty said. “I will splash the blues!” She nuzzled a soft sponge with her nose.

Coral clacked her claws. “I’ll make red shells.”

“I want yellow sunbeams!” Bubbles bubbled, swishing his fins.

Star smiled shyly. “I can paint green sea plants.”

Dotty blinked. “Green? But we only have blue, red, and yellow.” She tilted her head and felt curious.

Bubbles tapped the table. “Green comes from the green bottle.”

Coral frowned. “I don’t see a green bottle.”

Star sighed. “Oh no. No green?”

Dotty took a slow, belly-deep breath, like a good healthy habit she learned. In through her blowhole, out with a soft whoosh. The air smelled like lemon soap. “We can solve this,” she said. “Let’s think step by step.”

Dotty loved puzzles. She looked at the colors. She looked at the color wheel poster on the wall. It had big circles and arrows. “Colors can mix,” she said. “Maybe we can make green.”

Bubbles blinked. “How?”

Dotty pointed with her fin. “The poster shows yellow and blue next to green.” She spoke slowly and clearly. “If we mix yellow and blue, we might get green. Let’s test it.”

Star’s eyes grew wide with curiosity. “A test? Like a little experiment?”

“Yes!” Dotty smiled. “First, we get two clean brushes, so colors don’t get messy.” She passed out brushes and a small cup of water. The water was cool and made a tiny plink sound when a brush tapped it.

Coral dipped a brush in yellow and swirled it on a white plate. The yellow was bright and sunny. Star dipped another brush in blue. The blue was deep like the ocean. Together, they touched the colors.

“Mix, mix,” sang Dotty. The soft bristles whispered across the plate.

Slowly, the yellow and blue turned into green. Fresh, leafy green.

Star clapped her little arms. “It worked! We made green!”

Dotty giggled. “Logical thinking helps. We looked, we thought, we tested.” She felt warm pride.

They began to paint. Star made tall green plants. Bubbles painted long yellow sunbeams. Coral dotted red shells. Dotty sponged big waves of blue.

Then something went wrong.

Dotty swished too fast. Splash! Blue drops went onto the yellow sunbeams. The sunbeams turned green in the middle. Bubbles’ eyes grew round. He looked sad. “My sunbeams,” he whispered. “I wanted yellow.”

Dotty’s heart thumped. Her cheeks felt hot. She took another deep, calm breath. “I’m sorry, Bubbles,” she said gently. “I splashed by accident.” She looked him in the eyes. “Can I fix it?”

Bubbles flicked his tail. He sniffed. “Maybe.”

Dotty thought. “Let’s use steps again,” she said. “First, we clean the spot a little.” She dipped a clean cloth in the water. She dabbed, dabbed. The cloth felt cool and squishy. The green got lighter.

“Next, we wait for it to dry,” said Coral. “We can count to ten.” They all counted together. “One, two, three...” Their voices sounded like tiny bells. Counting made the waiting feel short.

When the paint was dry, Dotty said, “Last step, add more yellow on top.” Bubbles smiled and did careful strokes. The sunbeams turned bright again. He beamed. “Thank you for saying sorry and helping.”

Dotty’s tail swished with relief. “Thank you for letting me fix it.”

Star looked at the green plants. “I want light green too,” she said. “How can we make it lighter?”

Dotty thought and looked at the poster again. “We can mix in white, just a little.” She showed a tiny scoop. Star stirred. The green became soft and minty. “Ooo,” Star said. “It’s gentle and pretty.”

Coral raised a claw. “What about orange? For coral reefs?”

“Red and yellow make orange,” Dotty said. They tested. Orange bloomed like sunrise.

They all giggled and kept painting. The room filled with the soft scratch of brushes, the sweet smell of paint, and the happy sounds of friends. Dotty paused to drink some water. “Staying hydrated helps me think,” she said. “Healthy bodies help healthy minds.”

When the mural was done, they stepped back. The ocean scene sparkled: blue waves, yellow sunbeams, green plants, red shells, and orange coral. It looked like it was singing.

Bubbles swam a tiny loop. “We solved problems with steps.”

Star nodded. “We tried, we tested, we learned.”

Coral clicked. “And we were kind. Mistakes can be fixed.”

Dotty smiled wide. “Today we used logical thinking. We looked, we thought, we made a plan, and we tried it. We were curious. We included everyone’s ideas. And when I made a mistake, I said sorry and helped to fix it.” She felt calm and proud.

They placed the mural on the wall. The sunlight warmed the paper. Dotty whispered, “Our art shows our hearts.”

The friends touched fins, arms, and claws. Together, they said, “Think it through, step by step!”

The End.
