# The Brave Build in the Busy Garage

In a bright, busy garage workshop, a clever cat named Cinder wore a tiny red tool belt. Cinder’s whiskers twitched, and her green eyes sparkled. The garage smelled like wood shavings and warm rubber. Sunlight made little dust sparkles dance in the air. Clink, clink went the tools. Tap, tap went the hammer.

“Today,” Cinder said, tail high, “we will build a tall toy shelf for our block boxes!”

Her friends, Pip the puppy and Dot the duck, clapped and quacked.

“That shelf will be big,” Pip said. “Big and tall.”

“Tall is a little scary,” Dot whispered. “What if it wobbles?”

Cinder took a deep, brave breath. “We will build it strong. We will build it together.”

They gathered wood boards, smooth and sweet-smelling. They found screws, shiny and cool. They found a small screwdriver, a soft cloth, and bright blue paint.

Cinder drew a simple picture on a cardboard sheet. “First, we measure,” she said. “Then we mark. Then we screw the pieces together.”

“Step by step,” Pip wagged. “We can do it.”

Dot dipped her bill in a cup of water and dabbed the wood. “I help by cleaning the dust,” she said. The wood felt a little damp and very clean.

Cinder held the measuring tape. “One… two… three.” She marked a line with a blue pencil. Her paws were steady, but her heart thumped. The shelf would be tall. Would she be brave enough to climb the small ladder and fix the top?

A sudden wind whooshed under the garage door. The boards rattled. Pip’s ears perked. Dot tucked her head. Cinder’s tail puffed up like a duster.

Cinder swallowed. “Brave is not ‘not scared,’” she said softly. “Brave is doing the right thing, even when we feel scared.”

Pip nodded. “We can be brave together.”

They started. Pip held a board. Dot pressed with her soft webbed feet to keep it still. Cinder turned the screwdriver. Scritch, scritch, scritch. The screw went in with a gentle squeak.

“Good job,” Dot said. “It is snug.”

They built the bottom. They built the middle. The shelf grew taller. Shadows stretched across the floor. The garage hummed with the little fan, whirr, whirr.

Now came the top piece. The tall part. The part that made Cinder’s paws feel shaky.

Cinder looked at the small ladder. It looked very tall today. Her whiskers trembled.

“I can hold the ladder,” Pip said. He set his paws wide. “I will not let it slip.”

“I can spot you,” Dot said. She stood close. “You are not alone.”

Cinder put one paw on the first step. Thump. She put another paw on the next step. Thump. “I am brave,” she whispered. “I am careful. I am not alone.”

Up she went. Tap, tap went her heart. The top board was smooth and cool under her paws. The garage smelled like paint and fresh wood. She could hear Pip’s steady breathing and Dot’s gentle quack.

“You are doing it,” Pip said.

“You are brave,” Dot said.

Cinder lined up the top board with the sides. “Now the screws,” she said. Her voice was small, but steady. She turned the screwdriver. Scritch, scritch. One screw went in. Scritch, scritch. Another. The shelf stopped wobbling. It felt strong.

Cinder took a slow breath and climbed down. Step, step, step. When her paws touched the floor, she felt warm and proud.

“We did it,” Cinder said. Her tail swished like a happy ribbon.

Pip bounced. “Teamwork!”

Dot flapped. “Bravery!”

They checked the shelf. It stood tall and straight. It did not wiggle. It did not wobble. Cinder tapped the side. Knock, knock. “Solid,” she said. “Safe.”

“Paint time!” Pip wagged.

They spread a big sheet on the floor. They dipped soft brushes into the bright blue paint. Swish, swish went the brushes. The paint smelled a little sweet and a little sharp. Cinder painted slow and careful. Pip painted zigzags. Dot made tiny dots that looked like raindrops.

The shelf dried in the warm sun. The blue looked like the sky. They put the block boxes on the shelves. Click went the boxes. Everything fit.

Pip sighed happily. “We solved the problem. We needed a shelf. We made a plan. We worked together.”

Dot nodded. “And we were brave when it was hard.”

Cinder smiled. “Yes. I felt scared of the tall part. But I took a breath. I asked for help. I climbed carefully. That is brave.”

They cleaned the brushes. They wiped the floor. The garage was neat and bright again. The fan hummed. The dust sparkles danced.

Cinder looked at her friends. “Today we learned three big things,” she said. “One: Brave means doing the right thing, even when we feel scared. Two: Building is easier with a plan. Three: Teamwork makes hard jobs safe and fun.”

Pip bumped his nose to Cinder’s paw. “I will remember,” he said.

Dot quacked. “Me too.”

Cinder patted the shelf. “This shelf holds our blocks. Our hearts hold our lessons.”

They laughed together. Then they played with the blocks, stacking blue, red, and yellow towers on the new shelf. Tap, tap, tap. Giggle, giggle.

The garage felt safe and bright. The shelf stood tall and strong. Cinder felt brave, and so did her friends.

The End.
