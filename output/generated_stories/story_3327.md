# Benny the Beaver's Big Breath Day

Benny the Beaver loved being busy! His basement workshop was full of wonderful things - wood chips that smelled like fresh trees, shiny tools that clinked and clanked, and piles of smooth sticks waiting to become something special.

"Tap, tap, tap!" went Benny's hammer early one morning. He was making toy boats for the children at the nearby pond. But today felt different. Everything seemed to go wrong!

First, his favorite hammer slipped and bonked his paw. "Ouch!" Benny squeaked, feeling hot and bothered.

Then, a pile of carefully sorted sticks tumbled over with a loud "CRASH!" Wood scattered everywhere across his basement floor.

"Oh no, no, NO!" Benny's voice got louder. His fluffy tail started to twitch, and his whiskers trembled. He felt angry bubbles growing in his tummy, making him want to stomp his big flat feet.

Just then, Grandma Birch peeked down the basement stairs. Her wise old eyes sparkled kindly. "My dear Benny, you sound upset. What's happening down there?"

"Everything is going WRONG!" Benny huffed, his cheeks puffing out like little balloons. "I can't finish the boats, and the children are counting on me!"

Grandma Birch slowly walked down the creaky wooden steps. The basement smelled like sawdust and Benny's worry. "Would you like to try something magical with me?" she asked gently.

"Magic?" Benny's ears perked up, curious despite his frustration.

"Yes! It's called the Big Breath Magic. First, let's sit down together." Grandma Birch patted a soft pile of wood shavings beside her.

Benny plopped down, still feeling grumbly inside.

"Now," said Grandma Birch, "close your eyes and smell the lovely wood chips. Take a big, slow breath in through your nose... hold it... and let it out slowly through your mouth like you're blowing bubbles."

Benny tried it. "Sniff... whoooosh." The angry bubbles in his tummy felt a little smaller.

"Good! Now listen carefully. Can you hear the gentle drip-drop of water from the pipes? The soft whisper of wind through the basement window?" Grandma Birch's voice was calm and soothing.

Benny listened. "Drip... drop... whoosh..." His shoulders relaxed, and his tail stopped twitching.

"Now feel your paws resting on the soft wood shavings. Feel how your body sits peacefully here with me."

Benny wiggled his toes in the fluffy shavings. They felt cool and nice. The angry feeling was melting away like ice cream in sunshine.

"How do you feel now, dear one?" asked Grandma Birch.

Benny opened his eyes and smiled. "Better! The mad feeling went away!"

"When we take time to breathe and notice what's around us, we can handle any problem," Grandma Birch explained. "This is called mindfulness - paying attention to right now."

With his calm mind, Benny looked at his scattered workshop differently. "You know what? I can ask my friends to help! Ollie Otter loves organizing, and Rosie Rabbit is great at painting!"

Soon, the basement buzzed with happy helpers. Together, they finished all the toy boats. The children at the pond cheered with joy when they received their gifts.

That night, Benny practiced his Big Breath Magic before bed. He learned that taking time to breathe, listen, and feel helped him think clearly and solve problems with a happy heart.

The End.
