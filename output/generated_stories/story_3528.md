# Leo's Big Bedroom Adventure

Leo was a brave little lion cub with golden fur as soft as sunshine and eyes that sparkled like stars. He lived in the coziest bedroom you could imagine, with fluffy blue pillows, warm blankets, and toys scattered everywhere like colorful treasures.

One sunny morning, Leo woke up feeling extra adventurous. "Today I'm going to explore EVERYTHING!" he roared in his tiny cub voice. His bedroom suddenly looked like a magical jungle waiting to be discovered.

Leo spotted something shiny on top of his tall dresser. "Ooh, what's that sparkly thing?" he wondered aloud. Without thinking, Leo began to climb. Up, up, up he went, using the drawer handles like ladder rungs.

"I'm so brave and strong!" Leo puffed as he climbed higher. The dresser wobbled slightly, making a creaky sound. But Leo was too excited to notice.

Just as he reached for the shiny object—a pretty glass snow globe—Leo heard his mama's gentle voice from downstairs. "Leo, sweetie, are you being safe up there?"

Leo paused. The dresser felt wobbly under his paws, and suddenly he felt very high up. His tummy did a little flip-flop. "Um... maybe?" he called back, his voice smaller now.

"Let me come help you, little one," Mama said, her pawsteps soft on the stairs.

When Mama Lion entered the room, she gasped softly. "Oh my! Leo, you're very high up. That dresser could tip over and hurt you badly."

Leo's ears drooped. He looked down and realized how far he had climbed. The floor seemed so far away! "I... I wanted to see the sparkly thing," he whispered, feeling scared now.

"I understand, sweetheart," Mama said gently. "But climbing on furniture can be very dangerous. Let me help you down safely."

Mama carefully helped Leo climb down, holding him steady with her strong paws. When his feet touched the soft carpet, Leo felt much better.

"I'm sorry, Mama," Leo said, nuzzling against her warm fur. "I didn't think about being safe. I just wanted to explore."

Mama hugged him close. "Thank you for saying sorry, brave boy. It's wonderful that you want to explore and learn! But let's talk about how to do it safely."

She sat down on Leo's bed, patting the spot next to her. "When you want something up high, what should you do?"

Leo thought hard, his little whiskers twitching. "Ask for help?"

"Exactly!" Mama smiled proudly. "And what else should we remember about climbing?"

"Only climb on things made for climbing... like my jungle gym outside?"

"Very smart! You're learning so much." Mama reached up and carefully took down the snow globe. "Would you like to look at this together?"

Leo's eyes grew wide with wonder as Mama shook the globe. Tiny snowflakes danced around a little house inside. "It's beautiful! And much better when someone helps me see it safely."

"That's right," Mama said. "Being brave means making smart, safe choices. You can still be adventurous and explore—just remember to think about safety first and ask for help when you need it."

Leo nodded seriously. "I promise, Mama. Next time I'll ask for help instead of climbing by myself."

They spent the rest of the morning exploring Leo's room together safely, finding wonderful treasures and having the best adventure ever.

From that day on, Leo remembered that the bravest thing he could do was keep himself safe and ask for help when he needed it.

The End.
