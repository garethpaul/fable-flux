# Rusty's Rainbow Garden Party

Rusty the fox kit loved exploring Grandma Chen's balcony garden. The little orange fox had bright, curious eyes and a fluffy tail that wiggled when he was excited. Today, the warm sun made the colorful flowers smell sweet and fresh.

"Good morning, little friends!" Rusty called to the plants as he padded across the wooden deck on his soft paws.

In one corner, he discovered something wonderful. Three small pots sat in a row, each planted by a different child who visited Grandma Chen.

The first pot had bright red tomatoes planted by Maria. "¡Hola, Rusty!" the tomatoes seemed to whisper in the breeze. They smelled rich and earthy.

The second pot held purple eggplants from Amir's family garden. The smooth, shiny vegetables caught the morning light like jewels.

The third pot had green herbs that Kenji had planted - basil that smelled fresh and minty when Rusty brushed against it with his nose.

But Rusty noticed something that made his ears droop. Maria's tomato plant was much bigger than the others. It had been getting more water from the sprinkler.

"That's not fair," Rusty said to himself, his whiskers twitching. "All the plants should have the same chance to grow big and strong."

Just then, Grandma Chen stepped onto the balcony. Her silver hair sparkled in the sunlight, and she wore a colorful apron with flowers on it.

"Hello, Rusty," she said with a warm smile. "What are you thinking about so seriously?"

Rusty pointed his little paw at the three pots. "Grandma Chen, Maria's plant is getting more water. The other plants look thirsty. Their leaves are drooping."

Grandma Chen knelt down and looked carefully. "Oh my! You're absolutely right, little fox. The sprinkler isn't reaching Amir's and Kenji's plants properly."

Together, they moved the pots so each one could get the same amount of water and sunshine. Rusty helped by gently pushing the lighter pots with his nose.

"There," said Grandma Chen. "Now that's fair. Each plant will have what it needs to grow."

Rusty's tail wagged happily. "Can we make it even more special?"

"What do you have in mind?" Grandma Chen asked.

Rusty had a wonderful idea. He gathered smooth pebbles from around the garden and arranged them in pretty patterns around each pot. He made a sun design with yellow pebbles around Maria's tomatoes, a star pattern with white pebbles around Amir's eggplants, and a flower shape with gray pebbles around Kenji's herbs.

"Beautiful!" said Grandma Chen, clapping her hands. "You've made each plant special in its own way."

When Maria, Amir, and Kenji came to visit later that day, they were amazed.

"My tomatoes look so happy!" said Maria, giggling.

"The star pattern makes my eggplants look magical!" said Amir, his eyes wide with wonder.

"The flower design is perfect for my herbs!" said Kenji, smiling brightly.

All three children thanked Rusty for making sure their plants were treated fairly and for decorating them so beautifully.

As the sun set, painting the sky orange and pink, Rusty curled up between the three pots. He could smell the different scents mixing together - tomatoes, eggplant, and basil - creating something wonderful and new.

"Being fair means everyone gets what they need," Rusty whispered to the evening stars. "And when we're fair and creative together, we make something even more beautiful."

The plants rustled gently in the evening breeze, as if they were saying thank you to the kind little fox who made sure everyone was treated fairly.

The End.
