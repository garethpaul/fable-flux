# Fawn’s Helpful List in the Dining Room

In a sunny house, there was a cozy dining room with a big round table. The table was smooth and cool, like a shiny pond. A gentle deer fawn named Dottie carefully stepped inside on soft, quiet hooves. She wore a tiny blue scarf. It was warm and silky against her neck.

“Today is chore day,” Dottie whispered. Her brown eyes sparkled. She sniffed the air. It smelled like fresh bread and sweet strawberries. She felt happy and ready.

On the wall, Dottie saw a paper list with little boxes to check. It said:
1. Wipe the table.
2. Sort the napkins.
3. Place the cups.
4. Sweep the floor.

Dottie smiled. “I can do it,” she said. “One, two, three, four!”

Mama Deer peeked in and smiled. “Remember, little one,” Mama said gently, “one task at a time. Slow and safe.”

“Slow and safe,” Dottie repeated. She loved those words. They felt soft and calm.

Dottie picked up a cloth. It was damp and cool. “Hello, table,” she giggled. “Let’s make you clean.” She moved the cloth in little circles. Swish, swish, swish. Crumbs stuck to the cloth. The cloth made a tiny scritch sound. “Check!” Dottie said, and drew a neat check in the first box.

She reached for the napkins. They were fluffy and colorful. Red, blue, and yellow! “I will sort you by color,” Dottie said. “Red with red, blue with blue, yellow with yellow.” Her small hooves tapped the table as she worked. Tap, tap, tap. It felt good to put each napkin in the right pile. “Check!” She drew a check in the second box.

Next were the cups. The cups were clear and smooth. Dottie’s nose twitched as she smelled a tiny lemon scent from the soap. “I will place you safely,” she said. “Both hands, slow and safe.” She used two hooves to lift each cup, one at a time. Clink, clink. She set them gently around the table. “A cup goes with each plate,” she said. “Check!” She drew a check in the third box.

Dottie looked at the last box. “Sweep the floor.” She felt proud. “I am almost done,” she said. She picked up the little broom. The broom bristles felt tickly in her hoof. “Swish, swish,” she sang. Tiny crumbs slid into a neat pile. The floor smelled like lemon too. It made Dottie smile.

Then, Dottie saw a shiny spoon on the edge of the table. “Oh!” she said. “I forgot the spoon.” She reached for it quickly. The spoon slipped and fell. Clatter! The sound was loud and sharp. Dottie jumped. Her ears went down. “Oh no,” she whispered. “I made a mess.”

A small splash of water trickled from a cup. It spread like a little river on the table. The shiny spoon spun on the floor. Spin, spin, stop.

Mama Deer came in fast but calm. “Are you safe?” Mama asked.

Dottie took a deep breath. In, out. “Yes, Mama. I am safe,” she said. Her voice shook a little. “I rushed. I forgot slow and safe. I made a spill.”

Mama nodded. “Thank you for telling me,” she said. “We fix spills right away. We stay safe first. Let’s clean together.”

“I’m sorry, Mama,” Dottie said softly. Her eyes were wet.

Mama touched Dottie’s shoulder with a warm nuzzle. “I forgive you, little one. Accidents happen. You remembered safety. You asked for help. That is brave.”

Dottie felt her chest warm up. Forgiveness felt like a soft blanket. “Thank you,” she said.

They worked as a team. Mama held the cup steady. Dottie patted the spill with a towel. Pat, pat, pat. The water made a quiet squish sound. Dottie picked up the spoon with both hooves. “Both hands, slow and safe,” she said. She washed the spoon and dried it. Then she set it away from the edge.

“All clean,” Mama said, smiling.

Dottie looked at the list again. Only one box was left. She lifted the broom and swept the last crumbs into the dustpan. Swish, swoop. She emptied the crumbs gently. Then she made one last neat check mark in the final box.

“I did it,” Dottie whispered. Her heart felt bubbly, like a little stream. She looked around the dining room. The table shone. The napkins were bright and tidy. The cups stood safe and still. The floor was clean.

Mama clapped softly. “You finished each task,” she said. “You worked carefully. You fixed a mistake. You stayed safe. That is wonderful.”

Dottie’s tail flicked with joy. “I learned something,” she said. “I learned to do one thing at a time. I learned to go slow and safe. And I learned that when I make a mistake, I can say sorry, fix it, and try again.”

Mama nodded. “That is a big discovery,” she said. “Now we can enjoy a snack.”

They sat together at the round table. Dottie took a bite of a sweet strawberry. It was juicy and cool. She giggled when a drop tickled her chin. “Task time is done,” she said. “Snack time is fun!”

Mama smiled. “Yes. And next time, we will make a new list. You are a great helper, little one.”

Dottie looked at the wall. She saw the checks. She felt proud and calm. “One, two, three, four,” she said softly. “I can do it, slow and safe.”

The End.
