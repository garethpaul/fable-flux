# Dolly the Dolphin's Big Room Adventure

Dolly the dolphin loved to splash and play in her special water tank in Grandma's guest room. The tank was big and blue, just like the ocean she dreamed about visiting someday.

"Good morning, Dolly!" said Grandma, bringing her breakfast of yummy fish. "Today we have special guests coming to stay in this room."

Dolly's fins wiggled with excitement. "New friends? Will they want to play with me?"

"Maybe," smiled Grandma. "But remember, some people need time to feel comfortable around dolphins. You'll need to be very patient."

Soon, a family arrived with a little boy named Tommy. He peeked into the guest room and saw Dolly swimming in circles.

"Wow! A real dolphin!" Tommy whispered, but he stayed far away from the tank.

Dolly wanted to show off her best tricks right away. She jumped high out of the water with a big SPLASH! Water droplets sparkled in the sunlight streaming through the window.

But Tommy stepped back, looking scared. "She's too loud and splashy," he said to his mom.

Dolly felt sad. Her fins drooped down. "Why doesn't he want to play?" she asked Grandma later.

"Tommy needs time," Grandma explained gently. "Being patient means waiting calmly and being kind, even when things don't happen as fast as we want."

The next day, Dolly tried a different approach. Instead of big splashes, she swam slowly and gracefully. She made soft, gentle sounds - "click, click, whistle" - like a quiet song.

Tommy came a little closer. "She sounds like she's singing," he told his mom.

"That's her way of saying hello," his mom replied.

On the third day, Dolly practiced being even more patient. She floated calmly near the glass, watching Tommy color his pictures. When he looked at her, she would nod her head slowly and smile with her dolphin grin.

"She seems friendly," Tommy said, moving his chair closer to the tank.

By the fourth day, Tommy was sitting right next to the tank. "Hi, Dolly," he said softly.

Dolly's heart jumped with joy, but she remembered to stay calm. She swam over gently and touched her nose to the glass where Tommy's hand was pressed.

"She likes me!" Tommy giggled.

"Would you like to feed her a fish?" asked Grandma.

Tommy nodded eagerly. As he dropped the fish into the water, Dolly caught it gracefully and did a small, gentle spin.

"She said thank you!" Tommy laughed, clapping his hands.

From that day on, Tommy and Dolly became the best of friends. They would spend hours together - Tommy reading books while Dolly listened, and Dolly showing off her swimming skills while Tommy cheered quietly.

"You see, Dolly," said Grandma that evening, "patience helped you make a wonderful friend. Sometimes the best things take time."

Dolly nodded wisely. "I learned that being patient isn't just waiting - it's being kind and understanding while you wait."

"That's right," smiled Grandma. "And now you're both happy because you took the time to understand each other."

As the stars twinkled outside the guest room window, Dolly swam peaceful circles in her tank, knowing that patience had brought her the greatest gift of all - a true friend.

The End.
