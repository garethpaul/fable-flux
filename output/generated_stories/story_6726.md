# Fern Learns to Pause

Fern was a gentle deer fawn with soft brown fur and big bright eyes. One rainy afternoon, Fern stepped into Grandpa Gus’s garage workshop. The air smelled like pine wood and warm oil. The floor was cool and smooth. Tools hung neatly on the wall. A little radio hummed a happy tune.

“Welcome, Fern,” said Grandpa Gus, wiping his hands. “Would you like to help me make a sign?”

Fern’s ears perked up. “A sign? What will it say?”

“It will say, ‘Slow and Safe,’ for the path by the garden,” Grandpa Gus said. “We will build it, paint it, and write the words.”

Fern’s heart beat fast with excitement. “I want to do it now! I want to do it fast!”

Grandpa Gus smiled. “Fast can be fun, little one, but slow can be safe. Let’s use calm bodies and calm minds.”

Fern nodded, but her hooves clicked on the floor. Tap-tap-tap. She bounced on her toes. She wanted to grab the paint and make big swishy lines.

First, they found a smooth wooden board. Grandpa Gus showed Fern how to sand it. “Rub gently,” he said. “Listen to the sound.”

Shhh, shhh, shhh, went the sandpaper.

Fern rubbed fast. Skritch, skritch, skritch!

“Oops,” she said. Her hoof slipped. She made a scratch.

Fern’s tummy felt tight. “I messed up,” she whispered, eyes wet.

Grandpa Gus took a slow breath. He breathed in through his nose and out through his mouth. “Let’s try a Pause Plan,” he said softly. “When we feel wiggly or worried, we pause, breathe, and think. Would you like to try?”

Fern sniffled. “Okay.”

“Step one,” Grandpa Gus said, holding up a finger. “Pause. Hands still. Hooves quiet.”

Fern stood still. Her tail stopped twitching.

“Step two,” he said. “Breathe. In… one, two. Out… one, two.”

Fern breathed in. The air felt cool. She breathed out. Her shoulders relaxed.

“Step three,” said Grandpa Gus. “Think. What do we need? Soft rubs, small circles.”

Fern nodded. “Soft rubs, small circles.”

She tried again. Shhh, shhh, shhh. The wood felt smoother under her hoof. She smiled. “It’s working!”

Next, they tapped tiny nails to make a border. Ting, ting, ting went the little hammer. Fern wanted to hit hard and finish quickly. Her hoof wiggled. The nail bent.

“Oh no,” she said, ears drooping.

“Pause Plan?” Grandpa Gus asked kindly.

Fern breathed in… one, two. Out… one, two. She looked at the bent nail. “I can take it out, then tap gently. Tap-tap, not bang-bang.”

“Logical thinking,” Grandpa Gus said. “Good plan.”

Fern took her time. Tap. Pause. Tap. Straight and steady. She felt proud. “I can do gentle.”

Now it was time to paint. The blue paint looked shiny like a puddle. The brush felt soft. Fern dipped the brush too deep. Drip! A big drop fell on the floor.

Fern frowned. “I want to stop. I can’t do it.”

Grandpa Gus handed her a cloth. “We can clean. Then we can calm.”

Fern wiped the drop. Wipe, wipe. She closed her eyes and did the Pause Plan. Pause. Breathe. Think. “I can dip a little. I can brush slowly.” She tried small strokes. Swish, swish. The board turned a smooth, bright blue.

“It’s pretty,” Fern whispered, ears up.

Last, they needed to write the words: Slow and Safe. Grandpa Gus drew light lines with chalk. “You can write the letters,” he said. “I will guide your hoof.”

Fern felt her tummy flutter again. Letters looked tricky. The brush tickled her hoof. She wanted to rush and finish.

“Pause Plan,” she told herself. “I can do this step by step.”

She breathed in. She breathed out. “S starts at the top and curves,” she said slowly. She painted a soft S. “L is a line and a little foot.” She made an L. She shaped O and W, and then A, N, D. “Safe has S again,” she said, giggling. “My S is learning!”

Grandpa Gus nodded. “You are using calm, understanding, and thinking. You are writing with your heart and your head.”

Fern smiled wide. The sign said, Slow and Safe, in neat blue letters. The paint shone. The garage smelled like fresh paint and wood. The radio hummed. Fern felt warm and proud inside.

They set the sign to dry. Fern wiggled happily and did a little quiet hop. “Grandpa Gus, today I learned a big thing.”

“What did you learn, dear Fern?” he asked.

“I learned to pause,” she said. “When I feel wiggly, I can stop, breathe, and think. Then I can try again.”

Grandpa Gus patted her head. “That is self-regulation,” he said. “You are the boss of your body and your choices.”

Fern nuzzled his sleeve. “I like being calm. It helps me do my best.”

Later, they carried the sign to the garden path. Fern looked at the slow snails and the busy bees. She whispered, “Slow and safe,” and felt the words in her chest like a gentle drum.

She took one more calm breath. In… one, two. Out… one, two. She knew she could use her Pause Plan anytime—when painting, writing, or waiting for rain to stop. She knew that thinking steps help problems get small.

Fern’s eyes sparkled. “I did it,” she said softly. “I can pause. I can plan. I can do hard things.”

The End.
