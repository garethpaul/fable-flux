# Ruby's Rooftop Garden

Ruby the rabbit loved her special rooftop garden. Every morning, she hopped up the tall building stairs to tend her carrots, lettuce, and sunflowers. The sun felt warm on her soft brown fur, and the fresh air smelled like growing plants.

But today was different. Ruby felt very tired. Her paws ached from all the digging yesterday. Her tummy rumbled loudly because she forgot to eat breakfast. She yawned three big yawns in a row.

"I must water my plants," Ruby said to herself. "They need me!"

She picked up her heavy watering can. Splash! Water spilled all over her feet. The can felt too heavy for her tired paws.

Just then, her neighbor Benny the squirrel scampered over. "Ruby, you look sleepy! Are you okay?"

"I'm fine," Ruby said, but she stumbled and nearly dropped the watering can again. "My plants need water right now!"

Benny's bushy tail twitched with worry. "When did you last eat something? Or take a nap?"

Ruby thought hard. "Um... yesterday morning, I think?" Her ears drooped down. She felt dizzy and wobbly.

"Oh my!" said Benny. "You can't take care of your garden if you don't take care of yourself first!"

Ruby frowned. "But my plants will be thirsty!"

"I have an idea," Benny said gently. "How about I help water your plants while you eat some lunch and rest? Then you'll feel strong again!"

Ruby's whiskers twitched. She didn't want to seem lazy. "I should do it myself..."

Benny pointed to Ruby's favorite sunflower. It stood tall and bright yellow in the sunshine. "Look how healthy your sunflower is! Do you know why?"

"Because I water it every day?" Ruby guessed.

"Yes! And because you give it sunshine, good soil, and rest at night. You take good care of it!" Benny smiled. "But Ruby, you need the same things. Food, water, rest, and friends to help sometimes."

Ruby looked at her shaky paws. Her tummy growled again, louder this time. "You're right, Benny. I do feel pretty wobbly."

"It's okay to ask for help," Benny said kindly. "And it's important to eat and rest. That's how you stay healthy and strong!"

Ruby nodded slowly. "Would you really help me water the plants?"

"Of course! That's what friends do!"

Ruby hopped to her cozy corner of the rooftop. She munched on some crunchy carrots from her garden and drank cool water. The food tasted so good! She felt energy returning to her tired body.

While Ruby ate, Benny carefully watered each plant. He hummed a happy tune as gentle drops fell on the green leaves.

After lunch, Ruby curled up in a sunny spot for a short nap. The warm rooftop felt perfect against her fur. She dreamed of dancing vegetables and singing flowers.

When Ruby woke up, she felt wonderful! Her paws were strong again. Her tummy was happy and full. Her eyes sparkled with energy.

"Thank you, Benny!" Ruby hopped over to admire her garden. Every plant looked fresh and green. "You're such a good friend!"

"You're welcome!" Benny grinned. "How do you feel now?"

"Amazing! I can take good care of my garden because I took good care of myself first!" Ruby did a little bunny hop dance.

From that day on, Ruby remembered to eat healthy meals, drink water, and rest when she felt tired. And whenever Benny needed help with his acorn collection, Ruby was happy to share her energy with her kind friend.

The End.
