# Benji Beaver’s Basement Lab Routine

Benji the busy beaver loved his cozy basement lab. It smelled like fresh wood shavings and warm cocoa. Soft yellow lights twinkled above his workbench. Tools hung neatly on hooks. Jars of buttons and bolts sat in rows like tiny soldiers. Benji tapped his tail happily. “Good morning, lab!” he said.

Every day, Benji followed his routine. Routine means doing things in the same order. It helps our brains feel calm and ready. Benji smiled. “Step one: Put on my goggles.” The goggles felt smooth and snug. “Step two: Check my list.” He read in a slow, clear voice, “Plan, gather, test, tidy.”

His friend Dot the ladybug flew down, her wings buzzing like a tiny drum. “Hi, Benji! What’s today’s plan?”

Benji pointed to a big paper. “I am building a drip-drop drinker to water the fern. The fern gets thirsty. My drip-drop drinker will help.”

Dot landed on the paper. “Ooh! A drip, not a splash.”

Benji nodded. “Yes. We will use logic. Logic means thinking step by step.”

Benji took a deep breath. He liked the smell of the wood table and the cool air. “Plan,” he said, tapping the first word on his list. “We make a bottle tip. We make a slow tube. We make a tray to catch extra drops.”

“Good plan,” Dot said, clapping her tiny hands.

“Gather,” Benji said next. He walked to the shelf. “Bottle? Check.” He picked a clean bottle and felt the smooth plastic. “Tube? Check.” The tube was bendy and cool. “Tray? Check.” The tray was shiny and made a soft clink. “Tape? Check. Scissors? Check. Timer? Check.”

Dot hummed. “Everything in order!”

Benji smiled. “Order helps me think.” He set the parts in three neat piles. Then he looked at his list again. “Test comes after gather.”

He fitted the tube to the bottle. “Twist, twist, gentle,” he whispered. The tube slid on with a pop. “I like that sound,” Benji said.

Dot peered close. “Will it drip slowly?”

“We will test,” said Benji. He filled the bottle with water. The water was cold on his paws. He flipped the bottle and watched. Drip. Drip. Drip. The drops sparkled like tiny gems in the light.

Dot giggled. “It works!”

Then the tube slipped. Splish! Water splashed on the table. Benji blinked. For a moment, his heart felt fast. He took a calm breath, in and out. “It’s okay,” he said softly. “I can learn. I can try again.”

Dot nodded. “Growth mindset!”

Benji dabbed the splash with a towel. “Tidy,” he said, checking his list. “Tidy keeps things safe.” He wiped, wrung, and folded. The table felt dry again.

He went back to “Plan.” He drew a tiny picture. “Add tape here,” he said. “Logical thinking helps me fix the problem.”

“Gather,” he said again. He took a small strip of tape. “Test,” he whispered. He pressed the tape, firm but gentle. Flip went the bottle. Drip… drip… drip.

Dot leaned close. “Still too fast!”

Benji smiled. “We can adjust.” He turned the little valve with two careful clicks. The sound was soft, like a mouse tapping. “Test again.” Drip…… drip…… drip. “Better!” he cheered.

They set the tray under the tube. “Catch the extra,” Benji said. He put the fern’s pot nearby. The fern’s leaves made a soft rustle, like a whisper, as if it was saying, “Thank you.”

Dot sniffed. “It smells like wet earth.”

Benji checked his list. “Plan? Done. Gather? Done. Test? Almost done. Tidy? Later.”

They watched for one full minute. The timer ticked, tick-tick-tick. The drops fell slow and steady. The soil grew dark and damp, not soggy. Benji’s tail thumped happily. “It works!”

Dot did a tiny loop in the air. “Hooray for routine!”

Benji nodded. “Routine helps me remember. Routine helps me stay calm. When things go wrong, I use my steps. Plan, gather, test, tidy.”

He drew a star on his paper. “Now, tidy.” He put the tape back on its hook. He coiled the spare tube like a sleepy snake. He wiped the table, squeak, squeak. He washed the cup and put it upside down to dry. The lab looked neat and shiny again.

Dot buzzed to the little fern. “How do you feel, plant friend?”

The fern seemed to perk up, leaves lifting. Benji laughed. “The fern is happy. I am happy, too.”

He took off his goggles and set them in their spot. He checked his list one last time. “All steps done.”

Dot hovered by the lamp. “What did we learn today?”

Benji tapped his chin and said in a proud, gentle voice, “We learned that a routine helps us do our best. We learned to use logical thinking, step by step. We learned that when something slips, we don’t give up. We try again. That is a growth mindset.”

Dot smiled. “And we watered a fern!”

Benji patted the tray. “Tomorrow, we will check again. Routine means we come back, we care, and we learn more each day.”

He waved good-bye to the lab lights. “Thank you, lab,” he said. The lights seemed to glow warmer, like a hug.

Benji and Dot climbed the stairs, feeling proud and calm. The basement lab rested, neat and ready for tomorrow’s steps.

The End.
