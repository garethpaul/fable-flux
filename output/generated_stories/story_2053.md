# Daisy's Special Gift

Little Daisy the deer fawn stood in Grandma Bear's warm kitchen, watching the golden sunlight dance through the window. The kitchen smelled like fresh cookies and cinnamon. Tomorrow was Mother's Day, and Daisy felt worried butterflies fluttering in her tummy.

"Oh dear," Daisy whispered to herself, her big brown eyes looking sad. "I don't have anything special for Mama. All the stores are closed, and I don't have any money."

Grandma Bear was rolling out cookie dough on the wooden counter. She noticed Daisy's droopy ears and gentle frown. "What's troubling you, little one?" she asked kindly.

Daisy's voice was small and wobbly. "I want to give Mama something wonderful for Mother's Day, but I don't know what to do. I feel so sad because I can't buy her anything pretty."

Grandma Bear wiped her floury hands on her apron and knelt down beside Daisy. "You know what, sweet fawn? The most special gifts come from the heart, not from stores. What makes your mama happiest?"

Daisy thought hard, tapping her tiny hoof on the warm tile floor. "Mama loves when I help her... and she always smiles when I make her laugh... and she keeps all my drawings on the refrigerator!"

"Aha!" said Grandma Bear with a twinkle in her eye. "It sounds like you already know what to do. You just need to take the first step!"

Daisy's ears perked up a little. "But how do I start?"

"Well," said Grandma Bear, "I have some colorful paper, crayons, and ribbons in my craft box. What could you create with those?"

Suddenly, Daisy felt excited tingles in her hooves. "I could make Mama a special card! And... and maybe I could write down all the ways I want to help her!"

"Now you're thinking like a problem-solver!" cheered Grandma Bear.

Daisy took a deep breath and made her plan. "First, I'll make the most beautiful card ever. Then, I'll write down ten helpful things I can do. And maybe I'll pick some flowers from the garden too!"

With determination sparkling in her eyes, Daisy got to work. She carefully folded bright yellow paper and drew hearts with red crayons. The crayons felt smooth and waxy in her hooves. She wrote in her very best letters: "Dear Mama, I love you more than all the stars!"

Inside the card, she made a list of helpful things: "I will make my bed, I will put away my toys, I will help set the table..." Each word was written with love.

Finally, Daisy tied everything together with a silky purple ribbon that felt soft between her hooves.

When she finished, Daisy felt proud and happy. Her sad butterflies had turned into excited ones! "Grandma Bear, I did it! I solved my problem all by myself!"

"Yes, you did!" said Grandma Bear, giving her a warm hug. "You felt worried, but instead of giving up, you took action. You used your creativity and your loving heart to make something special."

The next morning, when Daisy gave her mama the handmade gift, Mama Deer's eyes filled with happy tears. "This is the most wonderful present ever, my sweet Daisy! You thought of this all by yourself?"

Daisy nodded proudly. "When I felt sad about not having a gift, I decided to make one instead!"

From that day on, whenever Daisy faced a problem, she remembered that she had the power to find solutions. All she needed to do was take the first step.

The End.
