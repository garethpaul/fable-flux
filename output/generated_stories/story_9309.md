# Tilly Turtle and the Fair Turn

Tilly was a shy turtle with a soft green shell. One rainy afternoon, she climbed the creaky wooden stairs to the quiet attic. Drip, drip went the rain outside. The attic smelled like old books and warm dust. Sunlight made golden lines through a small round window. Tilly liked the gentle hush. It felt safe.

In the attic was a big cardboard box full of toys. There were puzzles, a drum, soft blocks, and a shiny red wind-up mouse that whirred and zipped. Zzzzip! went the mouse when Tilly gently turned the key. Tilly smiled. The sound made her feel brave.

Soon two friends joined her. Pip the puppy wagged his tail, thump-thump-thump. Mimi the mouse twitched her whiskers and squeaked hello.

“Let’s play fair,” said Tilly in a small voice. “We can take turns.”

“Okay!” barked Pip. “I want the red mouse!” His nose wiggled. His paws danced.

Mimi peeked at the shiny toy and squeaked, “Me too! Me too!”

Tilly took a slow breath. “We can line up. We can wait. Fair means each friend gets a turn.”

Pip nodded fast. Mimi nodded too. They made a tiny line on a soft rug. Tilly put the wind-up mouse in the middle.

“Who goes first?” Pip asked.

Tilly looked at the box lid. On the lid was a small sand timer. The sand made a soft shhh sound when it moved. Tilly picked it up. “We can use this. When the sand is gone, it is the next turn.”

“That is fair,” Mimi said. “Who starts?”

Tilly hid a smile. “Let’s try rock-paper-shell.” She tucked her head a little. “Ready? One, two, three!”

Pip did a paw for rock. Mimi did two whiskers up for paper. Tilly lifted her shell. They laughed. “Paper covers rock. Mimi wins,” Tilly said. “Mimi goes first.”

Mimi wound the toy—click, click—and set it down. Zzzzip! The mouse zoomed in little circles. Mimi squeaked with joy. Pip barked, but he waited. His tail wagged. Tilly watched the timer. Shhh went the sand. When the last grain fell, Mimi stopped the toy.

“Your turn, Pip,” Tilly said.

Pip wound the mouse—click, click, click. Zzzzip! It raced under an old chair. “Oops!” Pip giggled. He crawled under and got it. He put it back on the rug. He waited for the timer, even though his paws were bouncy. Shhh went the sand. When it ended, Pip pushed the toy to Tilly.

“Your turn, Tilly,” Pip said.

Tilly’s cheeks felt warm. She was shy. She looked at her friends and the tiny timer. “Thank you,” she whispered. She turned the key—click, click—and let the mouse go. Zzzzip! The toy made a funny wiggle. Tilly laughed out loud. She felt brave again. Fair turns felt good.

Then they saw a puzzle with big wooden shapes. A round sun, a blue square, a green triangle. Tilly said, “Let’s do the puzzle next. We can share the pieces.”

Pip grabbed two at once. “I’m fast!” he said. Mimi reached for the triangle. Tilly held up her hand. “Let’s go slow,” she said. “Let’s be fair. One piece each.”

Pip paused. His ears tipped. “But I can do it fast!”

Tilly nodded kindly. “Fast is fine. But fair is better. Fair means we think of each friend. We can practice waiting. We can practice helping.”

Pip looked at Mimi. Mimi looked at Pip. “Okay,” they said together.

They took turns. Tilly picked the blue square and pressed it in. Thunk. Mimi picked the triangle and set it carefully. Tap. Pip chose the sun and slid it into place. Snug. Shhh went the rain, thunk went the puzzle, and the attic felt happy.

Soon they found a small drum. Pip wanted to bang loud. Mimi wanted to tap soft. Tilly put the timer between them. “We can each try. Loud time, then soft time. That is fair.”

Pip thumped—BOOM, BOOM—and then stopped when the sand ran out. Mimi tapped—tip, tip, tip—and smiled. “Your turn, Tilly,” they both said.

Tilly touched the drum—bom, bom—and felt the sound in her shell. It made a warm buzz in her belly. She liked it.

But then a little problem came. Mimi’s tail brushed the box. The shiny mouse fell and rolled close to Pip. Pip scooped it up and started to wind it.

“Wait,” Mimi squeaked. “Is it my turn?”

Pip froze. His paws held the toy. He looked at the timer. It was standing still, full of sand. Pip took a breath. His ears drooped a little. “I grabbed too fast,” he said. “That’s not fair.”

Tilly smiled gently. “It’s okay. We can fix it. Fair is when we make sure it is the right turn. Let’s check our order.”

Mimi whispered, “I think it’s mine.”

Pip nodded. “Yes. You go, Mimi.” He handed her the toy. “I will wait.”

Mimi’s eyes shone. “Thank you, Pip. That was patient.”

They set the timer. Shhh. Mimi wound the mouse. Click, click. Zzzzip! The toy zoomed in happy loops. Pip watched. Tilly watched. They clapped softly when the sand ran out. Then it was Pip’s turn again, and then Tilly’s.

They played until the rain slowed. Drip… drip… stopped. The attic felt calm and cozy. Tilly looked at her friends. “We practiced fairness,” she said. “We waited. We helped. We shared.”

Pip wagged. “It felt good to be fair.”

Mimi nodded. “Fairness makes play fun for everyone.”

Tilly’s shy smile bloomed. “Yes. Fair turns, patient hearts, and a little practice. That is how we play.”

They put the toys back in the box. The cardboard made a soft scritch sound. The sun peeked through the window. The friends hugged.

The lesson was clear in Tilly’s heart: Being fair means each friend matters, waiting is kind, and practice makes fairness easier every time.

The End.
