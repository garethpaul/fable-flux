# Rilo the Robot Dog Helps the Band

Rilo was a helpful robot dog. He had shiny silver ears that could hear many sounds. He had soft blue lights that blinked when he was happy. He wore a little red bandana with tiny music notes on it.

Rilo lived in a musical practice room. The room smelled like lemon cleaner and warm wood. There were small drums, a tall piano, and many bright shakers in a basket. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. The band of kids came here to practice music for a big show.

One day, the band arrived. “Hi, Rilo!” said Ami, waving. She held a tiny drum. “Hi!” said Yusuf, who had a shiny triangle. “Hi!” said Mei, with a flute that looked like a silver stick. “Hi!” said Luis, carrying maracas that clicked, click-click!

Rilo wagged his metal tail. “Welcome, friends,” he beeped in a gentle voice. “Let’s make music together.”

They started to play. Ding! Tap-tap! Whoosh! The sounds filled the room like bubbles. Rilo kept the beat with a soft paw tap on the floor. His blue lights blinked in time. The children smiled.

After a while, Mei looked at the flute. “My flute needs a wipe,” she said. “I will use this cloth.” She cleaned it gently. It made a tiny squeak sound. “All clean,” she said.

Rilo noticed Ami reached for Yusuf’s triangle. “I want to try!” Ami said. She hit it hard. CLANG! Yusuf’s eyes grew wide. He pulled the triangle back. “That is mine,” he said. His voice shook. He looked sad.

Rilo’s blue lights dimmed. He heard a problem. “Let’s pause,” Rilo beeped softly. He pressed a button, and a small chime rang. Ping!

Ami looked at her feet. “I only wanted to try,” she said. “I did not ask.”

Rilo nodded. “Asking shows respect,” he said. “Respect means we care about others’ feelings and things. We listen. We wait. We ask.”

Ami took a breath. She smelled the lemon cleaner. She felt the smooth drum in her hands. “Yusuf,” she said, “may I please try your triangle?”

Yusuf hugged the triangle close. “You can try after me,” he said. “I want to play first.” His voice was soft but firm.

Rilo wagged. “Good words, Yusuf,” he said. “You said what you need.”

Ami nodded. “Okay. I will wait,” she said. She tapped her tiny drum, tap-tap, and waited.

The music started again. It sounded warm and gentle now. When Yusuf finished his part, he smiled. “Ami, your turn,” he said. He handed the triangle to her carefully. Ami held it with two hands. “Thank you,” she said. She rang it softly. Ting! Her eyes shone.

Rilo’s lights glowed bright blue. “That is respect,” he beeped. “Ask. Wait. Share.”

Then Luis sneezed. Ah-choo! The sound bounced off the piano. Tiny dots flew in the sun squares. “Oops,” Luis said. He sniffled.

Rilo rolled to a little station. A pump of soap, a small sink, and soft towels waited there. He pressed a paw, and water went whoosh. “When we sneeze, we cover,” Rilo said kindly. “Then we wash our hands. Clean hands keep everyone healthy.”

Luis nodded. He covered his next sneeze with his elbow. Ah-choo. He washed his hands with warm water. The soap smelled like oranges. He dried them with a fluffy towel. “All clean,” he said. His maracas clicked, click-click, in a happy way.

Mei looked at a colorful poster on the wall. It showed children from many places. Some had bright saris. Some wore cozy sweaters. Some had curly hair. Some had straight hair. The poster said, “Music Around the World.”

Mei asked, “Rilo, do people far away play music too?”

Rilo blinked. “Yes,” he said. “Around the world, people make music to share feelings. In Ghana, people play drums that talk. In Japan, people play flutes that sound like wind. In Mexico, maracas dance with the beat. We can learn from everyone.”

Yusuf shook his triangle softly. Ting-ting. “We are like a world band,” he said.

Ami smiled. “We can listen to each other,” she said. “We can ask before we touch. We can keep clean hands. We can learn from friends far away.”

Rilo’s tail whirred. “Let’s try a song that shows respect,” he said. He set a gentle beat with his paw. Tap… tap… tap…

The band sang:
- “Ask and wait,
- Share and care,
- Clean hands show
- We’re kind and fair!”

Their voices blended like warm cocoa. The piano hummed. The maracas whispered. The triangle sparkled. The flute sighed like a soft breeze. The room felt cozy and bright.

When the song ended, the band was quiet. They smiled at each other. Yusuf gave Ami a high-five. Mei passed a clean cloth to Luis. Ami put the triangle back in its place. All the instruments looked neat and happy.

Rilo looked around. “Today we learned,” he said. “Respect means asking, listening, and waiting. Hygiene means covering sneezes and washing hands. Global awareness means we know people everywhere make music, and we can learn from them.”

Ami said, “When we show respect, music sounds better.”

“It feels better too,” said Yusuf.

Rilo’s blue lights shone like stars. “You did great,” he beeped. “You are a caring band.”

They packed up together. The lemon-clean smell floated in the air. The sun squares moved across the floor. Rilo gave a soft chime. Ping!

“See you next time, Rilo!” the kids called.

“See you, friends,” Rilo said. He wagged his tail and watched the door close.

The End.
