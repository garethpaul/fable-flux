# Spark, the Helpful Robot Dog

In a cozy garden shed at the end of a sunny yard, lived Spark, a helpful robot dog. Spark had shiny silver paws that went click-clack on the wooden floor. His little tail light blinked when he felt happy—blink blink!

Every morning, Spark checked his list. “Help. Learn. Share,” he beeped in a friendly voice. He loved helping his friends and learning about the big, big world.

One bright day, a soft breeze pushed through the open shed door. It smelled like flowers and fresh dirt. Spark wiggled his lighted tail. “Good morning, garden!” he said. He heard a small sniffle. “Who is there?”

“Me,” said Pip, a tiny bird with blue feathers. “I want to make art for my nest, but my paper is messy. And my wings got sticky.”

Spark rolled over to his art box with a whirr. “We can make art together,” he said. “Art comes from many places in the world. Colors and patterns are like little stories.”

Pip tilted her head. “What is the world?”

“The world is our big home,” Spark beeped. “People and animals live in many places. Some dance, some sing, some paint. We can learn from all of them. That is global awareness—knowing and caring about others far away and close by.”

Pip peeped, “Oh!”

Spark opened a drawer. Swish! He took out brushes, safe child paints, and paper. He also took out a small, clean towel and a gentle soap bottle. “First, we wash sticky wings and paws,” Spark said kindly. “Clean hands help us stay healthy. Clean tools help our art shine.”

Pip dipped her wings in a tiny bowl of warm water. “It’s warm,” she chirped. Spark hummed a washing song. “Rub-a-dub, bubbles pop! Rinse and dry; don’t you stop!” Pip giggled, “Pop! Pop!” They patted dry with the towel. “All clean,” Spark said. “Good hygiene helps everyone.”

Spark laid out the paper. “Let’s make a world art poster,” he said. “We will paint stories from around the globe.”

He put out colors: sunny yellow, ocean blue, forest green, and warm brown. He added a tiny cup of spice-scented paint made from safe cinnamon water. “This smells cozy,” Pip said, breathing in. “Mmm!”

“Let’s start with circles,” Spark suggested. “Circles can be the Earth.” He painted a big blue circle. “This is water,” he said. Pip dotted green for land. “This is where birds fly,” she chirped.

Spark wagged. “In some places, people make handprint art.” He pressed one shiny paw gently in paint and tapped the paper. “Tap!” Pip tried with a little wing print. “Tap!” The shapes made a pattern.

“In another place,” Spark said, “people weave bright cloth. It has zigzags and stripes.” He used a tiny comb to pull paint into zigzag lines. Pip added stripes—up, down, up, down. “Stripey!” she sang.

Suddenly, a gust of wind whooshed through the shed. Whoosh! The paper flapped. The paint splashed. Splot! A blue blob landed on their Earth circle. Pip’s feathers ruffled. “Oh no!”

Spark blinked his tail light, steady and calm. “It’s okay,” he said. “We can try again. That is resilience—keep going, even when it’s hard.”

Pip took a deep breath. “We can fix it,” she said.

“First, we clean up,” Spark replied. He brought out the towel again. “Wipe, rinse, dry.” They cleaned the table and washed their sticky spots. Spark sang the washing song once more. Pip sang along this time. “Rub-a-dub, bubbles pop!”

When everything was clean, Spark taped the corners of the paper so it wouldn’t flap. “Now we try again,” he said. Pip nodded. “Yes!”

They painted new circles. They made wavy blue water and soft green land. They added tiny houses with little doors. “People live in many kinds of homes,” Spark said gently. “Some are tall. Some are round. All are special.”

They painted foods from around the world: a round bread, a warm bowl of noodles, a bright mango. Pip sniffed. “Mmm, sweet mango!”

They painted music: a drum, a flute, a bell. “People sing and play in many ways,” Spark said. He tapped the table like a drum—tap-tap-tap. Pip chirped a happy note.

They added friends: a smiling child with curly hair, another with straight hair, one wearing a bright scarf, one holding a little book. “We are different and we are the same,” Spark said. “We all need care, clean water, and kind words.”

Pip’s eyes sparkled. “We can share our art,” she said. “We can say hello to friends near and far.”

“Yes,” said Spark. “Hello in many ways.” He beeped, “Hello!” Then he tried, “Hola!” Pip chirped, “Bonjour!” They both giggled.

At last, the poster was done. The shed smelled like cinnamon and clean soap. Sunlight made the paint glow. Spark and Pip washed their tools one more time. “Clean brushes last longer,” Spark said. “That is good care.”

They hung the poster on the shed wall. It showed the big blue Earth, smiling faces, and colorful lines. It looked like a hug.

Pip fluffed her feathers. “I learned about the world,” she said. “I learned to wash, and I learned to try again.”

Spark’s tail light blinked bright. “We helped, we learned, we shared,” he said. “That’s our adventure.”

They stood side by side, quiet and proud. The breeze was soft now. The garden made sleepy sounds—buzz, rustle, chirp.

“Good job, Pip,” said Spark.

“Good job, Spark,” said Pip.

The End.
