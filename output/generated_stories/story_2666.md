# Bear Cub Breathes and Tries Again

In a sunny home office, a friendly bear cub named Benny sat at a small wooden desk. The desk smelled like pine. The room was cozy and quiet. Mama Bear worked at a big desk nearby, tapping softly on her keyboard. Tap-tap, tap-tap. The sound was gentle like rain.

Benny had a little project. He wanted to make a picture chart for the day. He had paper, crayons, and stickers. The chart would show morning stretches, snack time, and story time. Benny wanted it to be neat and ready before lunchtime.

Benny picked up a blue crayon. “I can do it!” he said.

He drew a sun, but the sun looked like a blueberry. “Oh no,” said Benny. “This is not right.” His ears got warm. His paws felt wiggly. His cheeks puffed like little pillows.

Mama Bear looked over with a smile. “What’s up, little cub?”

“My sun is a blueberry,” Benny said with a frown. “I want it perfect. I want it now.”

Mama Bear nodded. “I hear you. That feels hard. Let’s try our calm breath. Mindful breath helps our bodies and our brains.”

Benny looked at his picture. He looked at Mama. He took a small breath. “Like this?”

“Let’s do it together,” Mama said. “Smell the flower.” They both breathed in slowly through their noses. “Blow the candle.” They both breathed out slowly through their mouths.

In—smell the flower. Out—blow the candle.
In—smell the flower. Out—blow the candle.

Benny’s shoulders softened. His paws stopped wiggling. He noticed the soft hum of the computer. He noticed the warm sun on his fur. He noticed the paper felt smooth under his paw.

“I feel softer,” Benny whispered.

“That’s mindfulness,” Mama said. “We notice our breath. We notice our body. We notice right now.”

Benny nodded. “Okay. I can try again.”

He flipped the paper and drew a big circle. This time it looked like a sun. “Yay!” Benny giggled.

Next, Benny wanted to draw his morning stretches. He tried to draw himself touching his toes. The crayon slipped and made a scribble. “Grr,” Benny growled. “Scribble!”

His tummy felt tight. His nose wrinkled. His eyes felt hot.

Mama Bear held up a little timer. “Let’s move our bodies. Exercise helps our feelings, too. We can take a movement break.”

Benny stood up. The carpet felt fuzzy under his feet. “What do we do?”

“Let’s do three bear stretches,” Mama said. “Reach up tall like a tree!”

Benny reached up. He felt his sides stretch. He felt his chest open.

“Now touch your toes like a sleepy bear,” Mama said.

Benny bent down. He felt his back go long. He wiggled his toes.

“Now shake your paws and hop, hop, hop!”

Benny hopped and giggled. His tight tummy felt loose. His nose un-wrinkled. His eyes felt cool.

They sat down again. “Smell the flower. Blow the candle,” Benny whispered to himself, breathing slowly. He drew himself touching his toes, with little smile lines. He added a tiny mat. He added a tiny water bottle.

“Nice details,” Mama said. “That’s preparation. You are planning your day.”

Benny smiled. He put a sticker for snack time and a sticker for story time. He drew a clock with big hands and small hands. He looked at his chart. It was almost done.

But the glue stick was missing. Benny looked in his cup. He looked in his drawer. No glue. His chest felt tight again. “Where is the glue?” he cried. “I can’t finish!”

Mama Bear pointed to a small basket labeled “Tools.” “Let’s prepare a place for tools. Then we can find them next time.”

Benny nodded. “First, breathe,” he said to himself. “Smell the flower. Blow the candle.” He breathed in and out, slow and soft. Then he checked the basket.

There was the glue stick! It clicked open with a little pop. It smelled sweet like paper. Benny glued the last sticker on the chart. He pressed it down with gentle paws.

“All done,” he said, his voice light.

Mama Bear clapped softly. “You did it! You used mindfulness. You took a movement break. You prepared your tools. You solved your problem.”

Benny hugged his chart. “When I feel big feelings,” he said, “I can breathe. I can move. I can get ready. Then I can try again.”

Mama Bear hugged Benny. The keyboard went tap-tap, tap-tap. The sun made warm squares on the floor. The home office felt calm and happy.

Benny hung the chart on the wall by his little desk. He looked at it and took one more slow breath. In—smell the flower. Out—blow the candle.

“I am ready for my day,” Benny said.

“Yes,” Mama said. “And your calm breath goes with you all day long.”

The End.
