# Fair Steps on the Front Porch

The sun was soft and warm. A gentle elephant calf named Elli sat on the peaceful front porch with her family. The porch smelled like sweet flowers. The boards felt smooth under Elli’s big, careful feet. Birds sang little songs. A breeze tickled Elli’s ears.

On the porch sat a basket of bright berries. Mama Elephant said, “These berries are for sharing. Let’s be fair.” Elli nodded. She loved to help. She loved to share.

Clop, clop. Elli’s friend, Tiko the turtle, came slowly up the porch steps. “Hello, Elli,” said Tiko. “May I have a berry?”

“Hello, Tiko,” said Elli with a smile. “Yes, you may.” She gave Tiko one red berry. Tiko’s eyes shone. “Thank you!”

Soon, Lila the little bird fluttered down and chirped, “Tweet! May I have a berry, too?” Elli gave Lila a berry. Lila sang a sweet thank-you song.

Then Pippa the puppy wiggled her tail and barked, “Woof! I’m hungry! Can I have two?” Pippa’s nose sniffed the air. The berries smelled fresh and bright.

Elli looked at the basket. “Hmm,” she said softly. “We should be fair.” She wanted to be generous. She wanted to be kind. She wanted to make good, just choices.

Mama Elephant stepped closer. “Justice means fair for everyone,” she said. “It means the rules are the same for all.”

Elli nodded. “One berry for each friend,” she said. “That is fair.”

But then a gust of wind whooshed across the porch. The basket tipped. A few berries rolled off the edge and plopped onto the grass. “Oh no!” cried Elli. Her heart thumped. Now there were fewer berries.

Tiko looked worried. “Do we still get one?” he asked.

Lila tilted her head. “Tweet? Is it still fair?” she asked.

Pippa pawed the floor. “I’m so hungry,” she said softly.

Elli took a deep breath. She put her big ear over the basket to keep the rest from rolling. The breeze felt cool on her cheeks. She paused. She listened. The birds sang again. Her mind felt clear.

“I will be responsible,” said Elli. “I tipped the basket by accident, but I can fix it.” She lumbered down the porch steps and gently picked up the berries with her trunk. She blew off the grass. “Puff, puff.” The berries looked shiny again.

When Elli returned, two new friends came to the porch. Nori the squirrel twitched her tail. “Chitter! I heard there are berries.” And Mako the mole popped up from a tiny hole near the steps. “Sniff, sniff. Is there a snack?”

Elli looked at all her friends. One, two, three, four, five. She looked at the basket. Not many berries left.

Mama Elephant asked, “Elli, what does justice look like now?”

Elli thought. She felt the smooth wood under her feet. She heard her friends breathing. She smelled the sweet berries. She wanted to be open-minded. “Maybe,” she said slowly, “we can make a fair plan.”

“What plan?” asked Tiko.

“A plan that is the same for everyone,” Elli said. “One berry each, and we will save some for later. If we run out, we can share something else, too.”

Pippa’s ears perked. “Like what?”

Elli smiled. “Like cool water! And crunchy leaves for Nori. And a shade spot for Mako. We can share more than berries.”

Lila chirped, “That is generous!”

Nori nodded. “Chitter! That sounds fair.”

Mako sniffed happily. “I like shade.”

Elli counted the berries. “One for Tiko. One for Lila. One for Pippa. One for Nori. One for Mako.” She gave the same to each friend, gently placing each berry in each small paw or wing or mouth. She kept her trunk steady and kind.

Pippa wagged her tail. “Thank you, Elli. You were fair.”

Tiko smiled. “This tastes even better because we all got one.”

Lila sang, “Tweet-tweet! Justice is sweet!”

Elli giggled. “Justice is fair for everyone.” She felt warm and proud.

Then Elli filled little cups with water from a pitcher. “Sip, sip,” went Tiko. Lila dipped her beak. Pippa lapped. Nori nibbled a crunchy leaf from a nearby plant with permission. Mako rested in a cool, shady spot under a big porch chair. Elli fanned everyone with her big ears. The breeze felt nice.

Suddenly, a tiny ant crawled onto the porch. The ant squeaked, “Is there anything for me?”

Everyone looked at the basket. No more berries. Elli blinked. She did not want the ant to feel left out. “We finished the berries,” she said. “But we still have water.” She dipped her trunk tip into a cup and let one bright drop fall onto a leaf for the ant.

The ant sipped the drop. “Thank you,” the ant squeaked. “That was fair and kind.”

Mama Elephant smiled. “Good work, Elli. You were responsible when the wind came. You were open-minded and found new ways to share. You were generous with more than food. And most of all, you practiced justice. Everyone was treated fairly.”

Elli’s cheeks felt warm. Her heart felt big. “Justice means fair for all,” she said. “We can always find a way to be fair.”

The friends sat together on the peaceful front porch. The sun dipped low and painted the sky soft pink. The porch smelled like flowers again. Elli hummed. Lila sang. Tiko tapped a tiny beat. Pippa sighed a happy “woof.” Nori swished her tail. Mako yawned. Even the little ant waved a feeler.

Elli looked at her friends. “Today was a fair day,” she said.

“Yes,” said everyone. “A fair day.”

The End.
