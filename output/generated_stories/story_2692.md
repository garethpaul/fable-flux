# Ollie the Otter’s Tidy Table Time

Ollie the playful otter slid across the smooth dining room floor with a happy squeak. The floor felt cool on his belly, and his whiskers wiggled with joy. The big wooden table smelled like warm toast, and the sun made little squares of light on the chairs.

“Time for snack and tidy-up,” called Mama Otter from the kitchen. Her voice was soft and kind. “We have fish crackers, apple slices, and warm oat tea.”

Ollie bounced. “Yum! Can I play one more game first?”

Mama Otter peeked around the door. “We can play after we tidy. We have ten minutes. Let’s use our time smartly.”

Ollie puffed his cheeks. Ten minutes sounded short. The dining room was busy with things. Crayons rolled near the chair legs. Napkins were folded like little tents. A small stack of picture cards was spread like a rainbow on the table.

A gentle voice came from the table edge. It was Bee, Ollie’s friend, a tiny bumblebee with fuzzy stripes and bright wings. “Bzz! I like tidy time. I can flutter and help,” Bee buzzed.

Ollie also saw Sloane the snail peeking from a placemat. Sloane had a beautiful swirly shell with tiny speckles. Sloane moved slowly, but carefully. “I can help, too,” Sloane whispered. “I am slow, but I am neat.”

Ollie clapped his paws. “Let’s go fast and finish now!” He scooped crayons and slid them in a cup. They clicked and clacked. He stacked napkins into a wobbly tower. He picked up too many picture cards at once. Whoosh! The cards slipped and fluttered like leaves.

Bee zipped close. “Bzz-bzz! I can carry two cards at a time.”

Sloane nudged a napkin with a soft slide. “Small steps,” Sloane said. “Small steps are okay.”

Ollie shuffled, hurried, and bumped the tea tray waiting at the edge of the table. The cups chimed. One cup tipped. A little splash of warm oat tea made a brown circle on the cloth.

Ollie’s eyes grew round. “Oh no!” His tummy felt twisty. He heard Mama Otter’s footsteps.

Mama Otter came in and saw the splash. She looked at Ollie’s worried face, at Bee’s hovering wings, and at Sloane’s careful crawl. Mama took a slow breath. “Thank you for trying to help,” she said. “Accidents happen. Let’s fix it together.”

Ollie’s whiskers drooped. He looked at Bee and Sloane. “I’m sorry,” he said softly. “I rushed. I didn’t use my time in a smart way.”

Bee landed on Ollie’s paw and buzzed kindly. “Thank you for saying sorry. We can still do it!”

Sloane nodded. “We can make a plan.”

Mama Otter smiled and set a small timer shaped like a lemon. It tick-tick-ticked. “We have eight minutes now. Let’s break it into little jobs.”

She held up three fingers. “Job one: Dab the tea. Job two: Sort the cards. Job three: Napkins and crayons. We can do each job for a few minutes. We can help each other.”

Ollie took a soft towel. Pat, pat, pat. He dabbed the tea circle. The towel felt squishy and warm. “Pat, not rub,” Mama said. “You’re doing great.”

Bee hummed a tiny tune and flew the picture cards into a neat stack, two at a time. “Bzz-bzz, stack-stack,” Bee sang. The cards made a gentle flap, flap.

Sloane glided to the napkins and lined them in a straight row. “One, two, three,” Sloane counted slowly. The napkins smelled like lemons. Sloane’s shell made a quiet tap when it touched the table edge.

Ollie watched his friends. They were helpful in different ways. Bee was quick and light. Sloane was slow and steady. Ollie smiled. “Our differences make us a good team,” he said.

Mama Otter nodded. “Yes. Everyone helps in their own way. That is something to celebrate.”

Tick-tick. The lemon timer showed five minutes left.

“Now crayons,” Mama said.

Ollie had an idea. “Let’s sort by color!” he cheered. “Bee can find the yellow and black ones. Sloane, can you line up the blues and greens? I will collect the reds and oranges.”

Bee zipped with a happy bzz. “Yellow and black! That’s me!”

Sloane smiled shyly. “Blues and greens are cool like shade.”

Ollie picked up red and orange crayons. They felt smooth and waxy. The colors made his paws smell a little sweet, like crayons always do. He placed them in the cup carefully—click, click.

Tick-tick. Two minutes left.

“Final job,” Mama said. “Napkins to the basket.”

Ollie and Sloane lifted together—Ollie with two paws, Sloane guiding the edge. Bee steadied a corner with a gentle wing. The napkins swished into the basket. Done!

The lemon timer chimed with a cheerful ding.

Ollie stood tall. The dining room looked neat. The tablecloth was clean and smooth. The picture cards were in a tidy stack. The crayons were sorted, bright and ready.

Mama Otter poured fresh oat tea. “You used your time wisely,” she said. “Plan, little jobs, and teamwork.”

Ollie took a crunchy fish cracker. “I learned to slow down and make a plan,” he said. “I can help others, and they can help me. We are different, and that makes us strong.”

Bee buzzed a happy circle. “Bzz! Smart time, kind hearts.”

Sloane tucked into their shell for a cozy peek. “Slow and steady can be on time, too.”

Ollie giggled. “Next time, I will think, plan, and then play.”

Mama Otter hugged him. Her fur was warm, and she smelled like cinnamon. “That’s good time-management,” she said.

Ollie munched, sipped, and felt proud. The dining room was calm, and his heart felt light.

The End.
