# Penny Penguin's Helpful Day

In a cozy nursery filled with soft toys and colorful books, lived a cheerful little penguin named Penny. Her black and white feathers were always neat and tidy, and she had the brightest orange beak that sparkled when she smiled.

Every morning, Penny loved to waddle around the nursery, looking at all the wonderful things to play with. There were wooden blocks scattered on the soft blue carpet, picture books left open on the reading corner, and stuffed animals sitting in different spots around the room.

One sunny morning, Penny noticed something that made her flippers droop a little. "Oh my!" she said, looking around with her round, curious eyes. "The nursery looks quite messy today. The toys are everywhere, and it doesn't look very comfortable for playtime."

Penny thought for a moment, tapping her orange beak with her flipper. "I know what I can do! I can help make this nursery neat and tidy again. But where should I start?"

She decided to make a plan, just like the grown-ups did. "First, I'll put all the blocks in their special box. Then, I'll arrange the books on the shelf. After that, I'll gather all the stuffed animals and put them in their cozy corner."

Penny started with the wooden blocks. She picked up each colorful cube - red ones, blue ones, yellow ones, and green ones - and carefully placed them in their storage box. "One, two, three," she counted happily as she worked. The blocks made a pleasant *clink-clink* sound as they touched each other.

Next, she waddled over to the picture books. Some were about farm animals, others about rainbow fish, and her favorite one about brave little penguins like herself. She gently closed each book and lined them up on the low shelf where they belonged. The books looked so pretty standing in a neat row, like colorful soldiers.

"Now for my stuffed animal friends," Penny said cheerfully. She gathered the soft teddy bear, the cuddly rabbit, and the sleepy elephant. Each one felt warm and fuzzy in her flippers. She arranged them carefully in the reading corner, making sure they all looked comfortable and ready for story time.

As Penny worked, she hummed a little tune: "Clean up, clean up, everybody everywhere! Clean up, clean up, everybody do your share!"

When she finished, Penny stepped back and looked around the nursery. The morning sunlight streaming through the window made everything look bright and welcoming. The room felt so much more comfortable now!

Just then, Mrs. Robin, who took care of the nursery, flew in through the open window. "Oh my goodness, Penny!" she chirped with delight. "Look how beautiful and organized everything is! Did you do all this by yourself?"

Penny's chest puffed up with pride, and her orange beak gleamed with happiness. "Yes, I did! I made a plan and worked step by step. It feels so good to help make our nursery comfortable for everyone."

"That's wonderful, Penny," said Mrs. Robin warmly. "When we help others and complete our tasks, we make the world a nicer place for everyone. You should feel very proud of yourself."

From that day on, Penny made helping and organizing part of her daily routine. She learned that when everyone helps a little bit, big jobs become much easier and more fun.

The End.
