# Ellie’s Space in the Treehouse

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and tiny, careful feet. She climbed the wooden steps to the treehouse, one step at a time. Tap, tap, tap. The sun made the leaves shine bright green. The tree smelled like sweet sap, and a warm breeze tickled Ellie’s trunk.

Inside the cozy treehouse, there was a round window, a basket of crayons, and a small blanket corner. Ellie loved the blanket corner. It was her calm space. She called it her “quiet nest.”

Today, friends were coming to play. Ellie felt happy, but she also felt a little flutter in her tummy. She whispered to herself, “I can be brave. I can use my words.”

Soon, her friends arrived. Pip the squirrel scampered in with a swish of his fluffy tail. Tika the turtle clinked her shell gently on the wooden floor. Benny the bluebird swooped through the window and chirped a cheerful tweet.

“Hi, Ellie!” they all said.

“Hi, friends,” Ellie said with a smile. “Welcome to the treehouse! We can draw, we can read, and we can build.”

Pip zoomed to the crayons. “Let’s all draw at once!” he squeaked, grabbing a big handful.

Benny hopped to Ellie’s quiet nest. “This blanket is so soft!” he chirped, fluffing it with his wings.

Tika moved slowly toward the window. “I like to sit by the sunshine,” she said.

Ellie watched. Her tummy flutter grew bigger. The blanket corner was special. It was Ellie’s calm space. She liked to sit there alone when she needed a rest.

“Um,” Ellie began, but her voice was small. Pip was already scribbling. Scratch-scratch-scratch. Benny was cuddling the blanket. Tika hummed softly, her shell warm in the sun.

Ellie took a deep breath. She smelled the sweet wood. She felt the floor under her feet. She put a gentle hand on her heart. “I can be brave,” she told herself again. “I can use my words.”

She walked to Benny. “Benny,” she said kindly, “that is my quiet nest. I need it for me. I feel calm there. Please don’t sit there right now.”

Benny blinked. “Oh! I didn’t know,” he chirped. “Thank you for telling me. I can sit by the window with Tika.” He fluttered over and settled beside Tika with a soft rustle.

Ellie smiled. Her tummy felt softer.

Then Pip scooted closer and started drawing on Ellie’s page. “Wheee! We share!” he squeaked, his tail flicking.

Ellie watched her picture blur. Her heart thumped. She liked to share crayons, but she didn’t like others drawing on her page without asking.

Ellie took another breath. In, out. She felt the cool air on her trunk. “Pip,” she said calmly, “I don’t like when you draw on my paper without asking. Please stop. You can have this paper, and I will use this other one.”

Pip froze. “Oh,” he said, ears drooping. “I was excited. I forgot to ask. I’m sorry, Ellie.”

“Thank you for saying sorry,” Ellie said. “Next time, please ask first.”

Pip nodded. “Can I draw on this paper, the one you gave me?”

“Yes,” Ellie said. “That’s okay.”

The room felt peaceful again. The crayons made soft scritch-scritch sounds. Tika and Benny whispered by the window. Ellie went to her quiet nest and sat for a moment. The blanket was warm on her legs. She closed her eyes and listened to the leaves rustle shhh, shhh. Her body felt calm.

After a minute, Ellie peeked out. “Friends,” she said, “let’s make a big picture together! We can make a treehouse map. We can each choose a space.”

“That sounds fun,” Benny chirped.

“I can draw the window,” said Tika.

“I will draw the ladder!” Pip squeaked.

“And I will draw my quiet nest,” Ellie said, “with a little sign that says, ‘Ellie’s Calm Space.’”

They worked as a team. Pip drew straight lines. Tika made gentle circles for the sun. Benny added tiny blue birds in the sky. Ellie drew a small heart on her quiet nest sign.

When they were done, the map looked bright and happy. Ellie showed them the sign. “This sign means this space is for me when I need a rest,” she explained in a soft voice. “If I need quiet, I will sit here. If you want to join me, please ask first, and I will tell you yes or no.”

Benny nodded. “We will ask.”

Tika smiled. “We can make signs too. I will make a ‘slow spot’ by the window.”

Pip raised his paw. “I will make a ‘zoom zone’ by the crayons!”

Everyone laughed. They made small signs together and taped them gently in the treehouse. The signs helped them remember each other’s spaces.

Later, Pip’s tail swished too close to Ellie, and he paused. “Ellie, can I sit next to you?”

Ellie checked her tummy. It felt calm. “Yes,” she said, “you can sit here.”

They sat side by side, happy and cozy. The tree sighed in the breeze. The crayons smelled waxy and sweet. The sunlight made golden shapes on the floor.

Ellie felt proud. She had used calm, brave words. She had set her boundary with kindness. Her friends listened. Together, they made a plan that worked for everyone.

Lesson: Your body and your space are important. You can say what you need with gentle words. Friends can listen, ask, and work together.

The End.
