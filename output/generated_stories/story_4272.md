# Miko the Kind Mouse Makes It Right

In a cozy garage workshop, tools hung like shiny silver fish. The air smelled like wood and warm rubber. A tiny fan hummed. A big orange cat poster watched from the wall. And in the corner, under a blue towel, lived Miko the kind mouse.

Miko liked the sounds of the garage. Tap-tap went the hammer. Whirr went the drill. Clink went the little screws in a jar. Miko liked to help. He pushed tiny nails into rows and wiped dust with a soft cloth. “I am small,” said Miko, “but I can be kind.”

One sunny morning, Miko heard a squeak that was not his. “Help! Help!” cried a voice. Miko’s ears stood up. He ran past the paint cans. He darted under the workbench. There, tangled in a loop of green string, was Pip the shy mouse.

“My foot!” squeaked Pip. “I can’t move.”

Miko felt his heart bounce. “I will help,” he said gently. “Stay still.” He breathed in slow. In and out. He remembered to be calm. He nibbled the knot with careful teeth. Snip! The string fell away.

“Thank you,” said Pip, rubbing his little foot. “I was so scared.”

“You’re safe now,” said Miko. He patted Pip’s paw.

Suddenly—CRASH! A small wooden car clattered off the workbench. A tall shadow moved. It was Rocco the rat from the alley. Rocco had a scar on his ear and a loud voice.

“I want that car,” Rocco growled. He stomped a heavy foot. “It is mine now.”

Pip hid behind a paint can. Miko stood still. His whiskers trembled. He felt a hot bubble in his belly. Anger tried to roar like the drill. “No,” he said, but his voice was little. He felt mad. He felt scared. He remembered a lesson: When mad, stop, breathe, and count.

Miko put his paw on his chest. “Stop,” he whispered. He took a deep breath. In for one, two, three. Out for one, two, three. He felt the hot bubble get smaller. He felt brave and kind again.

“That car is not yours, Rocco,” Miko said, his voice steady now. “The car belongs to Kim. Kim is the person who fixes things here. We must be fair. We must be just.”

Rocco snorted. “I don’t care,” he said. He reached for the car.

Miko stepped in front of it. His heart beep-beeped fast. “I am small,” he thought, “but I can be brave.” He looked at Rocco’s eyes, not his teeth. “Rocco,” he said softly, “are you hungry? Are you lonely? Do you need help?”

Rocco blinked. He did not expect kind words. His shoulders dropped a little. “I… I just want a toy,” he muttered. “No one shares with me.”

Miko listened. He nodded. “It feels bad when no one shares,” he said. “I hear you. But taking is not fair. Justice means we keep things safe. Courage means we speak up kindly. Compassion means we care about feelings.”

Pip peeked out. “You can play with us,” Pip squeaked. “We can share time.”

Rocco looked at the car. He looked at Miko. He looked at his own paws. He took a breath. “I was mad,” he said. “I made a bad choice.”

Miko smiled. “We all make mistakes,” he said. “We can fix them.” He pointed to a small wooden wheel that had rolled away. “Help us fix the car, and then we can all play.”

Rocco’s ears twitched. “Me? Help?”

“Yes,” said Miko. “Helping feels good.” He handed Rocco the soft cloth. “First, we clean.”

Cloth swish, swish. Tiny brush tickle, tickle. Pip found the little wheel by the hammer. “Got it!” Pip cheered.

Miko held the car steady. “Rocco, can you press?” he asked.

Rocco pressed. Click! The wheel fit snug. Together they turned it. Smooth and round.

Just then, Kim the fixer came in, humming a happy tune. She smelled like soap and sawdust. She saw the mice by the car. “Oh!” she said with a smile. “What great helpers!”

Miko waved a tiny paw. “We kept your car safe,” he said. “We fixed the wheel.”

Kim clapped softly. “Thank you,” she said. She set a little bowl of seeds and a tiny cap of water on the floor. “Kind friends get kind snacks.”

Rocco’s eyes went wide. “For us?”

“For helpers,” said Kim. “And for friends who make good choices.”

Rocco looked at Miko and Pip. “I was wrong,” he said. “I tried to take. I will not do that again. I will ask. I will help. I will share.”

Miko nodded. “That is fair,” he said. “That is just.”

They ate crunchy seeds. Crunch, crunch. The fan hummed. The tools shined. When the snack was done, they took turns. Pip rolled the car. Rocco rolled the car. Miko rolled the car. They laughed when it zipped over a soft rag ramp. “Zoom!” cried Pip. “Zoom!” cried Rocco. “Zoom!” cried Miko.

Before bed, Miko whispered to himself, “When I feel mad, I can stop, breathe, and count. I can choose kind words. I can be brave. I can be fair. Compassion helps everyone.”

In the cozy garage workshop, with the warm smell of wood and the gentle whirr of the fan, three small friends learned big things. They learned that courage is speaking up kindly. They learned that justice is keeping things fair. They learned that compassion turns angry moments into happy ones.

The End.
