# Brave Bunny's Kitchen Helpers

In a warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon and sunshine, a determined rabbit named Rumi hopped onto a little stool. The stool wobbled, but Rumi held steady. “I can do this,” Rumi said. “I want to make a healthy snack.”

The kettle hummed softly. The window let in golden light. The wooden spoon looked like a tiny paddle, and the mixing bowl was shiny and smooth. Rumi’s whisk made a whispery sound: swish, swish, swish.

Rumi looked at the recipe card. The card showed pictures: berries, oats, yogurt, and honey. “Berry Bunny Bites!” Rumi read slowly. “Mmm. Healthy and yummy.”

Rumi gathered the food. “Oats, check. Blueberries, check. Yogurt, check,” Rumi said, tapping each bowl. The blueberries were cool and plump. The yogurt was creamy and white like a cloud. The oats felt dry and tickly on Rumi’s paws.

Rumi opened the drawer. “Where is the small spoon?” Rumi asked. The drawer made a squeak. Only big spoons were inside. “Hmm.” Rumi’s whisk drooped.

Rumi took a deep breath in and out. “When I feel stuck, I can stop and breathe,” Rumi whispered. “In, and out.” The warm kitchen felt calm again.

Just then, Rumi’s friend Pip the mouse peeked out from behind a flour jar. “Hi, Rumi! What are you making?” Pip squeaked.

“Hi, Pip! I’m making Berry Bunny Bites. They are healthy,” Rumi said proudly. “But I can’t find the small spoon.”

Pip twitched whiskers. “Let’s be brave and check the high shelf,” Pip said. The high shelf was tall and a little scary. Rumi’s ears wiggled.

“I feel a little worried,” Rumi said. “But I can try. Brave means trying even when it’s hard.” Rumi moved the stool closer and climbed up carefully. The stool wobbled again. “Slow and steady,” Rumi said.

Pip held the stool with tiny paws. “I’m helping!” Pip said.

On the high shelf, behind a jar of cinnamon, a small spoon sparkled. “I see you!” Rumi said. Rumi reached, reached, reached—and got it! “Thank you for helping, Pip.”

“You did it!” Pip cheered.

Rumi washed the spoon with warm water and a bubble of soap. “Clean hands, clean tools,” Rumi said, rubbing paws under the tap. The water was warm and the bubbles smelled like lemons. “Healthy habits help me cook safely.”

Together, they mixed the oats with yogurt. Squish, squish. They added blueberries. Plop, plop. A little honey dripped like golden rain. Drip… drip. Rumi stirred gently. The bowl made a soft clink.

Rumi tried to scoop a round bite, but it fell with a splat. “Oh no!” Rumi said. The mixture looked messy. Rumi’s nose scrunched. “I feel frustrated.”

Pip nodded. “Frustration is okay. Let’s pause.”

Rumi put a paw on the table and took three slow breaths. “In… two… three. Out… two… three.” The warm kitchen hummed. Rumi’s shoulders relaxed.

“Maybe we chill the mix,” Pip said. “Cold helps it stick.”

“That’s a smart plan,” Rumi said. “Problem, solution!” Rumi put the bowl in the fridge. The fridge hummed like a sleepy bee. “While we wait, we can clean up.”

Rumi and Pip wiped the table. Wipe, wipe. They washed the whisk. Swish, swish. They put the oats back in the jar. Tap, tap. “Helping keeps the kitchen safe,” Rumi said. “It’s a healthy habit.”

Ding! The timer chimed. Rumi took out the bowl. The mixture felt cool and thicker. Rumi scooped a small ball. It stayed! “Yes!” Rumi smiled. “We solved it.”

They rolled more bites: roll, roll, roll. Blueberry dots peeked out like tiny moons. The plate looked bright and happy.

Rumi set two glasses of water on the table. “Water helps my body,” Rumi said. “Sip, sip.” The water tasted fresh.

They each tried a Berry Bunny Bite. “Mmm,” Pip squeaked. “Sweet, soft, and a little crunchy!”

Rumi chewed slowly. “I like how the blueberries pop,” Rumi said. “Eating fruit helps me grow strong.”

A little voice came from the doorway. It was Rumi’s cousin, Dot the kit, with big curious eyes. “Can I help?” Dot asked.

“Yes!” Rumi said. “Helping is kind.” Rumi gave Dot a tiny spoon. “We wash hands first.” They all scrubbed hands with warm, soapy water. Rub, rub, rinse.

Dot giggled. “Bubbles tickle!”

Dot rolled a small bite and placed it on the plate. “I did it!” Dot said proudly.

“You were brave to try,” Rumi said. “And you were helpful, too.”

They sat together in the warm kitchen, sharing snacks and smiles. The sun made a stripe of light across the table. It felt cozy and safe.

Rumi patted the recipe card. “Today I learned that healthy habits help me. Washing hands, cleaning up, drinking water, and eating fruit are good for my body. Taking breaths helps when I feel frustrated. And being brave and helpful makes hard things easier.”

Pip clapped tiny paws. “We are a good team!”

Rumi nodded. “Yes. When we have a problem, we can find a solution.”

They packed a small box of Berry Bunny Bites to share with a neighbor who wasn’t feeling well. “Sharing is kind,” Rumi said.

The warm kitchen hummed softly as the friends finished their snack, happy and proud.

The End.
