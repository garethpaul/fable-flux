# The Painting Hop

In a bright creative art room, a determined rabbit named Rumi stood on her tippy toes. She wore a tiny apron with blue stars. Paint bottles lined the table like a rainbow. The room smelled like fresh paper and lemon soap. Soft music hummed, and big windows let in warm sun.

“I am ready to make a big sky picture,” Rumi said. Her whiskers twitched with excitement. She dipped her brush in swirly blue paint and began to sweep the brush across the paper. Swish, swish. The sound was soft like a whisper.

Her friends worked nearby. Tiko the turtle rolled a sponge in green paint. Pop the puppy padded in, tail wagging, paws tapping on the floor. “I want to paint dots!” Pop barked happily. He bounced in place, his ears flopping.

Rumi took a deep breath. “I will make room for everyone,” she said. She moved her paper a little. She set out extra water cups and put three clean brushes in a jar. She was determined to help.

Pop jumped closer. His paws thumped the floor. Thump-thump. A little splash of blue landed on Rumi’s paper. A blob! “Oh!” Rumi blinked. Her ears stood tall.

“I’m sorry!” Pop yipped. “I just get so bouncy.”

“It’s okay,” Rumi said, but she felt a tiny pinch in her belly. She wanted her sky to be smooth and calm. She watched Pop hop again. Thump! Another splash. This time, blue drops dotted her paws. They felt cool and tickly.

Rumi paused. She put the brush down. Her heart made a quick beat—bum, bum. She remembered the words on the art room wall: Use your words. Be calm. Take turns.

She took a slow breath in, like smelling a flower. She blew out, like blowing a feather. Inhale. Exhale. “Pop,” Rumi said softly. “Can we talk?”

Pop stopped mid-bounce. His paws froze. His tail made a little swish. “Talk?”

“Yes,” Rumi said. “I feel worried when paint splashes on my picture. I worked hard on the sky. I like your dots. Can we make a plan so we both can paint?”

Pop’s ears perked. “A plan? I like plans.” His voice went from loud to gentle. “What do you need, Rumi?”

Rumi’s whiskers wiggled. “Thank you for listening. I need to keep this paper for smooth sky. Maybe you can make your dots on a new paper. You can jump over there.” She pointed to a big sheet on the floor with a taped border.

Tiko the turtle looked up. “I can help,” he said slowly. “I will tape the paper so it does not slide.” He pressed tape with his careful, steady hands. Rip. Press. Rip. Press.

Pop nodded. “I can do gentle jumps. I can be a dot-maker with soft feet.” He set his paws down and tried a slow, bendy bounce. Boing… soft. The floor felt cool under his pads. He giggled. “It feels funny!”

Rumi smiled. “I will try soft brush strokes too. Swish… soft.” She dipped her brush and moved it like a cloud. The blue across her paper looked smooth and calm.

For a moment, everyone was quiet. Then Pop whispered, “Rumi, can I show you my dot dance? It has three moves.” He stood tall. “One: tiptoe, tiptoe.” Tap, tap. “Two: tiny hop.” Boing. “Three: freeze!” Pop held still like a statue.

Rumi’s nose twitched. “I want to try!” She put down her brush and copied: tiptoe, tiptoe, tiny hop, freeze. Her feet felt happy on the floor. The movement made her legs warm. She giggled. “That is fun.”

Tiko clapped slowly. “Nice listening,” he said. “Nice moving.”

Rumi thought. “Let’s make a painting plan with movement. We can take turns. First, Pop does his dot dance for ten dots. Then I do my sky swish for ten swishes. Tiko can count with his sponge.”

Tiko smiled. “I love counting.” He dabbed green—dab one, dab two. “I will say when it is time.”

“Ready?” Rumi asked.

“Ready!” Pop barked softly.

“Go!” Tiko said.

Pop tiptoed: tap, tap. He made tiny dots: dot, dot, dot. The dots were neat and round. He froze and grinned. Tiko counted to ten in a warm, slow voice.

“My turn,” Rumi said. She made ten sky swishes: swish, swish, swish. The brush felt smooth in her paw. The paint glided like water. Tiko counted again.

They took turns. They listened. They moved with care. The room felt peaceful and busy at the same time. The sun made the paint sparkle. The sounds were soft—tap, swish, dab, breathe.

Soon, their big paper looked wonderful. The top was a calm, bright sky. The bottom was a field of happy green dots. In the middle, Pop made a dot rainbow. Rumi painted a white cloud. Tiko dabbed a tiny turtle hiding in the grass.

Pop wagged. “We did it!”

Rumi looked at her friends. She felt warm in her chest. “Thank you for listening to my words,” she said. “I listened to you, too. We made a plan. We moved in gentle ways. We took turns.”

Tiko nodded. “That is peaceful communication,” he said. “We used calm voices. We shared space. We listened.”

Rumi added a soft silver star at the corner of the sky. She wrote their names under it: Rumi, Pop, Tiko.

They all stepped back. They breathed in together. Paint smelled sweet like berries. The paper crinkled a tiny bit as it dried.

Rumi smiled. “When we listen and speak kindly, our art—and our hearts—feel good.”

Pop did one last tiny hop. “And dot dances can be soft!”

Tiko patted the tape. “And plans can be kind.”

The three friends cleaned their brushes. Swish in water. Tap, tap on the rim. They hung the picture on the wall. It shone in the sun.

The End.
