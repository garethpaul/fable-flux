# Fox Kit Follows the Steps

Pip was a curious fox kit with bright eyes and a swishy tail. Pip loved to sniff, peek, and poke. One sunny morning, Pip climbed the ladder to the big treehouse. The wood felt warm. The leaves made soft shhh sounds. Inside, it smelled like pine and fresh air.

“Today is Sports Day!” Pip cheered. Pip’s friends were there—Mina Mouse, Bop Bear, and Tully Turtle. On the wall was a big paper list with red words.

Bop pointed with a paw. “Coach Owl left directions,” he said.

Pip bounced. “Let’s just play! I can throw the soft ball so far!”

Mina squeaked, “We need to read the steps.”

Tully tapped the list. “Step one: Warm up. Step two: Team talk. Step three: Practice. Step four: Play the game. Step five: Clean up.”

Pip wrinkled a nose. Warm up sounded slow. Pip grabbed the soft ball. “Catch!” Pip tossed it high.

Thump! The ball hit a hanging bell. Ding-ding! Pip giggled. But the ball rolled to the corner, under a wooden bench.

“Oops,” Pip said. “I’ll get it.”

Pip wiggled under the bench. The wood was dusty. Dust tickled Pip’s nose. Aaah-choo!

Mina covered her ears. “Bless you!”

Bop rubbed the bell. “Pip, Coach Owl said to follow directions. We can get hurt if we don’t warm up.”

Pip sat up, ears drooping. “I just wanted to play.”

The door creaked. “Whoooo is ready?” Coach Owl swooped in. His feathers brushed the air like soft brushes. He landed by the list and blinked kindly.

“We are,” said Tully. “But Pip skipped steps.”

Coach Owl ruffled his wings. “Pip, being curious is good. But directions keep us safe and help us do our best.”

Pip looked at the floor. “I’m sorry. I want to learn.”

Coach Owl smiled. “Good. Let’s start from the top. Everyone, Step one: Warm up!”

They stretched arms up high. The wood floor felt smooth under paws and feet. They touched toes. They made circles with shoulders. Pip felt warm and bouncy, but in a steady way.

“How do you feel?” Coach Owl asked.

“Loose like a noodle!” Pip giggled.

“Step two: Team talk,” Mina read. They sat in a circle. Coach Owl placed a small basket in the middle. Inside were paper slips.

“Each slip has a job,” Coach Owl said. “Pick one, and we work together.”

Pip picked one and sounded it out slowly. “Score… keep… er?”

“Scorekeeper!” Bop grinned. “You count points.”

Bop picked “Thrower.” Mina picked “Catcher.” Tully picked “Ref.”

Coach Owl nodded. “Now Step three: Practice.”

They practiced passing the soft ball around the treehouse. It made a soft boop sound in paws. Pip counted, “One, two, three,” every time Mina caught it.

“Eyes on the ball,” Coach Owl reminded. “Soft paws. Small steps.” He spoke calmly. His voice was gentle, like a soft drum. Pip listened and felt proud when counting matched the catches.

Then Tully raised a flipper. “Coach, the floor is a little slippery near the window.”

Coach Owl peered. “Good discovery, Tully. We will place a mat there.” He spread a woven mat that felt bumpy under feet.

Pip whispered, “When we follow directions, we notice things.”

“Exactly,” Coach Owl said. “You also help each other.”

At last, Coach Owl tapped the list. “Step four: Play the game.”

They made teams. Pip and Mina on one side. Bop and Tully on the other. The rules were simple: pass three times, then try to tap the bell softly with the ball.

“Ready?” Tully called. The leaves whispered. Pip’s heart thumped with happy beats.

“Go!” Coach Owl hooted.

Pip remembered, “Soft paws. Small steps.” Pip passed to Mina. Mina passed back. Pip passed again. The ball went boop, boop, boop.

“Now tap the bell,” Mina said.

Pip aimed. Tap! Ding! Not too loud, just right.

“Point!” cheered Tully. Pip marked a 1 on a chalkboard. Chalk dust puffed. It smelled dry and squeaky.

Bop’s team tried. Bop passed to Tully. Tully passed back. Bop tapped. Ding! Pip smiled and drew another 1.

The game went on. They tied, 3 to 3. Then Pip saw Tully look tired.

“Time-out?” Pip asked.

Tully nodded. “Thank you.” He sipped water. The cool water glug-glugged. Pip took a sip, too. Coach Owl’s eyes twinkled. “Listening to your team is part of following directions and cooperating.”

They started again, but a little problem popped up. Mina wanted to throw faster. Bop wanted to throw slower.

“I like fast,” Mina squeaked.

“I like slow,” Bop rumbled.

Pip held up the list. “Let’s check the directions.” The paper had a small note at the bottom: “If players want different speeds, compromise—mix fast and slow.”

“Compromise means we take turns,” Tully said. “Two fast, two slow?”

Mina and Bop looked at each other. “Okay,” they said together.

They tried it. Two fast throws. Two slow throws. It felt fair and fun. They laughed when the bell made a happy ding-ding-ding rhythm.

At the end, the chalkboard showed 5 to 5. A tie!

“Step five: Clean up,” Coach Owl said.

They picked up balls. They folded the mat. They wiped the dust. Pip brushed under the bench and sneezed a tiny, happy sneeze. Aaah-choo!

Coach Owl fluttered up by the list. “What did we learn?”

Pip’s eyes shone. “Following directions keeps us safe. It helps us do our best. We can discover problems and fix them together. And we can compromise.”

Coach Owl bowed. “Whoooo wonderful.”

Pip hugged friends. The treehouse smelled clean and fresh. The sun made golden squares on the floor. Pip felt calm and proud inside.

“Next time,” Pip said, “we start with step one.”

“Together,” said Mina.

“As a team,” said Bop.

“With care,” said Tully.

They all waved goodbye and climbed down the ladder, one careful step at a time.

The End.
