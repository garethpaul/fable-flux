# Toby the Turtle Finds His Voice

Toby was a very shy little turtle who lived with his family in a cozy house. Every morning, his family gathered in the bright dining room for breakfast. The wooden table was big and round, with cheerful yellow placemats and pretty blue bowls.

But Toby had a problem. He was too shy to ask for what he wanted to eat.

"Good morning, everyone!" chirped Mama Turtle as she placed warm pancakes on the table. The sweet smell of maple syrup filled the air.

"Good morning!" called Papa Turtle and Big Sister Turtle together.

Toby opened his mouth to say good morning too, but only a tiny whisper came out. "Good... morning," he said so quietly that nobody heard him.

Papa Turtle reached for the orange juice. "Who wants juice?" he asked cheerfully.

Toby loved orange juice! It was sweet and tangy and made him feel happy. But instead of speaking up, he just pulled his head into his shell a little bit.

"I do!" said Big Sister Turtle, raising her flipper high.

Papa poured juice into her cup. The golden liquid sparkled in the morning sunlight streaming through the dining room window.

Next, Mama Turtle held up a plate of fluffy pancakes. "Who would like pancakes?" she asked with a warm smile.

Toby's tummy rumbled softly. The pancakes looked so fluffy and delicious, like little golden clouds. But again, he was too shy to speak up.

"Me, please!" said Big Sister Turtle politely.

Toby watched sadly as his sister got a tall stack of pancakes with butter melting on top. His shell felt heavy, and his tummy felt even hungrier.

Big Sister Turtle noticed that Toby looked sad. She had been watching him during breakfast time. "Toby," she said gently, "are you hungry too?"

Toby nodded his little green head slowly.

"You know," said Big Sister Turtle kindly, "it's okay to ask for what you need. Our family loves you, and we want to make sure you're happy and fed."

Mama Turtle heard this and smiled warmly. "Big Sister is right, Toby. We can't read your mind, but we can listen to your words."

Papa Turtle nodded. "Using your voice to ask for things isn't rude, little one. It's how we take care of each other."

Toby thought about this. Slowly, very slowly, he poked his head out of his shell a bit more. His heart was beating fast, but he felt a tiny bit braver.

"I... I would like some pancakes, please," Toby said in a small but clear voice.

"Of course!" said Mama Turtle happily. "How many would you like?"

"Two, please," said Toby, feeling more confident. "And... and may I have some orange juice too?"

"Absolutely!" said Papa Turtle, pouring juice into Toby's special green cup.

As Toby ate his delicious breakfast, he felt warm and happy inside. The pancakes tasted even better because he had asked for them with his own voice.

"Thank you for asking so nicely," said Mama Turtle, giving Toby a gentle pat on his shell.

From that day on, Toby practiced using his voice at the dining room table. He learned that speaking up for what he needed wasn't scary – it was a way to help his family take care of him and show respect for everyone's needs.

The End.
