# Kora the Calm Koala on the Rooftop Garden

Kora was a calm koala with soft gray fur and gentle eyes. She lived in a tall building with a rooftop garden. The garden was bright and green. There were little pots of plants, red tomatoes, yellow flowers, and a small blue watering can. The sun felt warm on Kora’s ears. A cool breeze tickled her nose. She liked to sit and breathe slowly. In and out. Calm and steady.

One morning, Kora climbed up to the rooftop to pick a snack. “I will make a tiny picnic,” she said softly. “I can do it myself.”

She brought a small basket, a napkin with green dots, and her favorite cup with a koala face. Kora smiled. She could hear birds chirping and the soft hum of the city below. The rooftop smelled like tomato leaves and fresh soil.

But when Kora reached for the door, she paused. The door had a big sign: “Please fix the wobbly handle.” The handle was loose and shaky. “Oh dear,” whispered Kora. She tried to turn it, but it wiggled and slipped. “Click… clack… clink.” The door stayed stuck. Kora was on the rooftop and could not go back down.

Kora felt a small flutter in her belly. “I am calm,” she said. “I can think. I can try. I can help myself.” She sat on the warm tiles and took three slow breaths. In and out. Calm and steady.

Just then, her friends popped up from the stairway window. Milo the mouse squeaked, “Do you need help?” Pippa the pigeon cooed, “Coo-coo, we can push!” Tomas the turtle blinked slowly. “We… can… take… turns,” he said.

Kora smiled. “Thank you,” she said. “Let me try first. I can be self-reliant.” She looked at the handle. It wobbled and rattled. She looked at the tools they kept in the garden box: a small screwdriver, twine, and a roll of bright blue tape.

Kora talked to herself in a soft voice. “What can I do? I can try to tighten. I can try to wrap.” She picked up the screwdriver. It felt cool and smooth in her paw. She tried to tighten the little screws. “Twist, twist,” she whispered. The handle stopped wobbling so much. “That is better.”

She pulled the handle again. “Click… clack.” Still stuck.

“May I try next?” asked Milo, bouncing on his toes.

“Yes,” said Kora. “We will take turns.” Milo tugged the handle with his tiny paws. “Squeak, squeak.” No luck.

“Now me,” cooed Pippa, flapping her wings. She pecked at the tape and wrapped it around the handle to give it grip. “Coo! Sticky and strong.” But the door still did not open.

Tomas nodded. “My… turn.” He pushed gently with his shell. “Thunk.” The door moved just a tiny bit, but stayed closed.

Kora watched and listened. She felt the warm sun on her fur and the cool breeze on her cheeks. She rubbed the handle and felt something rough. “There is dirt in the latch,” she said. She peered closely. Little grains of soil were stuck there, like tiny brown sugar crystals.

Kora took a deep breath. “I will try something new,” she said. “I will be open-minded.” She pulled a soft leaf from the basil plant. It smelled sweet and peppery. She used the leaf to brush the dirt from the latch. “Swipe, swipe,” she whispered. The grains fell away.

Then she took the twine and tied a small loop around the handle. She tied another loop to a sturdy post. “We will pull together,” she said. “We will take turns pulling gently so we do not break it.”

Milo pulled. “One, two, three.” Tug. The handle moved a little. Pippa pulled. “Coo-coo.” Tug. The latch loosened. Tomas pulled. “Slow… and… steady.” Tug. The door made a soft “click.” It was almost open.

Kora held the twine last. She stood with her soft paws firm on the tiles. “I can do it,” she said. “I can help myself.” She pulled with a calm, gentle tug. “Click… clack… click!” The door swung open with a breezy whoosh.

They all cheered. “Hooray!” The birds chirped with them, and the air smelled like basil and sunshine.

Kora smiled. “Thank you for waiting your turns,” she said. “Thank you for trying new ideas.” She patted the handle and taped a little note: “Handle fixed. Please be gentle.”

Then Kora set out her picnic. She placed the green-dotted napkin on a warm tile. She poured cool water into her koala cup. She sliced a red tomato and shared crunchy crackers with her friends. “Crunch, munch,” went Milo. “Coo-coo,” sang Pippa. “Yum,” said Tomas.

Kora took a bite and felt proud and peaceful. “I tried first,” she said softly. “I stayed calm. I used my own ideas. I asked for help in turns. I kept an open mind. I did it.”

Pippa nodded. “You were brave and smart.”

Milo squeaked, “And calm!”

Tomas smiled slowly. “Self… re… liant.”

Kora looked at the bright sky and the happy rooftop garden. “When something is hard,” she said, “I can breathe. I can think. I can try. I can wait my turn. I can listen to new ideas. I can help myself.”

And that felt just right.

The End.
