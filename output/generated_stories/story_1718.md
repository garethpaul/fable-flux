# Leo the Lion Cub and the Bouncing Ball Mystery

Leo was a brave little lion cub with golden fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved to explore every corner of his house, and today he discovered something very interesting in the laundry room.

"Wow!" Leo gasped as he pushed open the white door. The laundry room smelled like fresh soap bubbles and warm sunshine from the clothes hanging on the line. But what caught his attention was a bright red ball bouncing all by itself near the washing machine.

*Bounce, bounce, bounce!* The ball jumped up and down, making a cheerful rhythm on the smooth floor.

"That's my ball!" squeaked a tiny voice.

Leo looked around with his big eyes. "Who said that?" he asked gently.

A small gray mouse peeked out from behind a basket of towels. "I did," said the mouse, whose name was Pip. "That's my special bouncing ball, and I was playing with it first!"

Just then, a fluffy white rabbit hopped into the room. "No way!" said the rabbit, whose name was Rosie. "I brought that ball here to practice my basketball jumps. It's mine!"

Leo watched as both animals moved closer to the bouncing ball. Pip's whiskers twitched with worry, and Rosie's ears drooped sadly. The ball kept bouncing between them, and Leo could see that both his new friends really wanted to play with it.

"Wait, wait!" Leo said in his kindest voice, stepping between them with his soft paws. "Let's not argue. Can you both tell me what happened?"

Pip wiped his tiny eyes. "I was practicing my soccer kicks with the ball when it bounced away from me into this room."

Rosie nodded her fluffy head. "And I was practicing my basketball dribbling when my ball rolled in here too."

Leo's ears perked up as he had a wonderful idea. He looked at both balls more carefully - they were exactly the same size and color!

"I think I know what happened," Leo said with a gentle smile. "You both have red balls that look exactly the same! Maybe they got mixed up when they bounced in here together."

Leo walked over to the corner where he spotted another red ball hiding behind the laundry detergent. "Look!" he said, picking it up with his paws. "Here's the second ball!"

Pip and Rosie looked at each other with surprise and then began to giggle.

"You're right, Leo!" laughed Pip. "We were both so worried about losing our balls that we didn't think to look for a second one."

"That was very smart thinking," said Rosie, giving Leo a friendly hug.

Leo felt warm and happy inside. "You know what would be even more fun?" he suggested. "Instead of playing alone, why don't we all play together? We could make up a new game with both balls!"

And that's exactly what they did. They invented a wonderful game called "Bounce and Catch" where they took turns bouncing the balls to each other in the spacious laundry room. The sound of their laughter mixed with the gentle *whoosh* of the washing machine and the sweet smell of clean clothes.

From that day on, whenever there was a problem, Leo, Pip, and Rosie remembered to stop, listen to each other, and work together to find a peaceful solution.

The End.
