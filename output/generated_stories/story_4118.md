# Leo’s Learning Lantern

The brave little lion cub, Leo, curled his tail on a soft blanket in his cozy bedroom. The room smelled like warm cocoa and clean sheets. A tiny lamp made a gentle golden glow. Crayons, paper, and stickers rested on a small table. Outside, rain tapped the window, tap-tap-tap, like a tiny drum.

Leo held a big picture book. “I want to learn my shapes tonight,” he said, puffing his chest. “Triangles, circles, and squares! I can do it.”

Mama Lion peeked in with a smile. “That sounds brave and smart,” she purred. “Learning takes practice. I’m right here if you need help.”

Leo nodded. “I will try first!”

He opened his book. The pages rustled like leaves. “Circle,” he read slowly. He traced the round shape with his paw. “Round like my night lamp.” He looked at the lamp. “Yes, circle! I did it!”

He turned the page. “Square,” he whispered. He peeked at his toy box. “It looks like a square!” He grinned, showing tiny teeth. “Square, square!”

Then Leo flipped to a tricky page. “Triangle,” he read. The picture had three pointy sides. Leo looked around his room. He tried to find a triangle. He squinted. He sniffed. He listened to the rain. But no triangle.

Leo’s whiskers drooped. “I can’t find one,” he said. His tummy felt twisty.

Mama Lion stepped closer. “What do we do when something is hard?” she asked softly.

Leo took a deep breath. He remembered Mama’s words. “We try again. We ask for help. We keep going.”

“That’s resilience,” Mama said, rubbing his ear. “You are brave and patient.”

Leo thought. “Maybe I can make a triangle!” His eyes sparkled. “I can craft it!”

He scooted to his little table. The chair made a tiny squeak. He opened the crayon box. It smelled waxy and sweet. “Red, blue, yellow,” he sang.

“Do you want some paper?” Mama asked.

“Yes, please,” Leo said politely. “Thank you, Mama.”

Mama set down smooth paper and safe little scissors. “I will watch with kind eyes,” she said. “You can do it.”

Leo drew three lines. The crayon scratched softly. “One line, two lines, three lines,” he counted. He tried to join them. Oops! The lines didn’t meet. The shape looked wobbly.

Leo’s ears drooped. “It’s not a triangle.”

Mama smiled. “It’s a try-angle,” she teased kindly. “Every try helps you learn.”

Leo giggled. “A try-angle!” He tried again. “One, two, three,” he said slowly. He pressed his paw down firm. This time the lines met at the corners. “I did it! A triangle!” He clapped his paws. Clap clap!

“Wonderful,” Mama said. “What do triangles feel like?”

Leo touched the paper. “Pointy corners. Straight sides.” He rubbed the crayon lines. “Smooth and a little bumpy.”

“Can you make a learning lantern?” Mama asked. “A lantern with shapes?”

Leo’s eyes went wide. “Yes!” He carefully cut out his triangle. Snip, snip. He cut a circle. Snip. He cut a square. Snip. He glued the shapes to a paper sleeve. The glue smelled a little sweet. He added star stickers and a happy face. “This is my Learning Lantern,” he said proudly.

Mama slipped a small, safe, battery candle inside. The paper glowed warm and soft. Shapes danced on the walls. The triangle made a tiny mountain. The circle made a moon. The square made a window.

Leo hugged Mama. “Thank you for helping me learn.”

“You did the learning,” Mama said. “You were brave, patient, and kind to yourself.”

Leo nodded. “When I could not find a triangle, I made one. When my try-angle was wobbly, I tried again. Learning takes practice.”

A little noise came from the hallway. Leo’s stuffed zebra had fallen off the shelf. “Oh no,” he said. He picked it up and patted it gently. “Are you okay, Zee?” He tucked the zebra under his blanket. “There, all better.” He smiled. “Being kind feels warm, like my lantern.”

Mama tucked Leo in. The blanket felt cozy and soft. The lantern’s glow made the room look like a golden cave. The rain still went tap-tap-tap, but now it sounded like a happy song.

“Tell me, Leo,” Mama whispered, “what did you learn tonight?”

Leo thought carefully. “I learned shapes. Circle, square, triangle. I learned to ask for help. I learned to be kind. I learned to keep trying. That is how my brain gets strong.”

Mama purred. “Yes. Education is a treasure you carry everywhere.”

Leo yawned a big lion cub yawn. “I can carry it even in my cozy bed.”

He closed his eyes. The lantern glowed. The shapes danced quietly. Leo felt brave, smart, and kind.

“Tomorrow,” he murmured, “I will learn stars.”

“Tomorrow,” Mama agreed, kissing his forehead. “And you will shine.”

The End.
