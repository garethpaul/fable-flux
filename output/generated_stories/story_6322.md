# Tully the Shy Turtle’s Bedtime Band

Tully was a shy turtle who loved his cozy bedroom. It was soft and warm, like a gentle hug. His blanket was green and smooth. His pillow smelled like clean sunshine. In the corner, a little lamp glowed like a sleepy firefly.

Tully had special bedtime treasures. He had a small drum made from a tin, a shiny bell, and a wooden shaker with painted stars. He liked to make quiet music before sleep. Tap-tap on the drum. Ding-ding on the bell. Swish-swish on the shaker. The soft sounds made his heart feel calm.

One evening, Tully’s friends came to visit. There was Mia the mouse, Benny the bunny, and Pip the parakeet. They tiptoed into Tully’s cozy bedroom with fuzzy socks and sleepy smiles.

“Hi, Tully,” said Mia. “Can we play bedtime band?”

Tully felt his tummy flutter. He hugged his shell a little. He loved his music, but he felt shy. What if the sounds got too loud? What if his friends took his shaker and he didn’t get it back? He looked at his treasures. He wanted to share, but his cheeks felt warm and tight.

Benny’s ears wiggled. “I can drum!” he said.

Pip fluffed his feathers. “I can sing! Tweet-a-dee!”

Tully’s heart went boom-boom, fast like a drum. He whispered, “I feel… worried.”

Mia tilted her head. “What kind of worried?” she asked softly.

Tully took a slow breath. In, out. He touched his chest. “My tummy is fluttery. That means I’m shy. I’m scared it might be too loud. I’m scared I’ll lose my shaker.”

Mia nodded. “Thank you for telling us.”

Pip chirped quietly, “We can be soft.”

Benny patted the bed. “We can take turns.”

Tully blinked. He had named his feelings. He felt a little lighter, like a bubble.

“Let’s make a plan,” Tully said, voice small but brave. “Soft sounds only. We count to five, then we share.”

“Good plan,” said Mia. “We can use quiet paws and quiet claws.”

They sat in a circle on the soft rug. The lamp made a warm puddle of light. The room smelled like lavender from a tiny sachet tucked under Tully’s pillow.

Tully handed the drum to Benny. His hands shook a little. “Tap-tap, then pass,” he said.

Benny nodded. Tap… tap… tap. He counted, “One, two, three, four, five,” and passed the drum back. The sound was gentle, like raindrops.

Mia shook the shaker. Swish… swish… swish. “One, two, three, four, five,” she whispered, and handed it to Tully with a smile.

Pip rang the bell. Ding… ding… ding. “One, two, three, four, five,” he sang, then set it down carefully.

Tully felt safe. He tried the shaker. Swish-swish. His tummy felt warm now. He smiled. “I like this plan,” he said.

Then Pip forgot. He got excited and sang, “Tweet-a-dee-dee-dee!” very loud. The bell went DING! DING! DING!

Tully’s eyes went wide. His heart sped up. His shoulders crept into his shell. He wanted to hide.

Mia noticed. “Pause the band,” she said gently, holding up a tiny paw.

Everything got quiet. The room felt like a soft blanket again.

Tully took another slow breath. In… and out. “I feel scared again,” he said. “The loud sounds were too much for me.”

Pip’s feathers drooped. “I’m sorry, Tully,” he chirped softly. “I forgot our soft rule.”

Benny’s ears tucked down. “We can fix it,” he said. “Let’s make a hand signal. When someone needs quiet, we make a turtle sign.”

Mia made a turtle sign with her hands, like a little shell. “This means, ‘Please be soft.’”

Tully tried it. He made the turtle sign and smiled. “That helps me,” he said.

They began again. Tap… tap. Swish… swish. Ding… ding. Pip sang, “Tweet-a-dee,” as light as a whisper. When Tully felt his tummy flutter, he made the turtle sign. Everyone went extra soft, like a cloud.

Soon the music felt like a lullaby. Tully felt brave enough to share the shaker again. “Your turn, Benny,” he said. “Thank you for waiting.”

Benny grinned. “Thank you for sharing,” he said. He counted to five and passed it to Mia.

Mia said, “We are a kind band. We listen, we share, and we use our words.”

Tully nodded. “When I say my feelings, my friends can help.”

They finished with a quiet song. Pip hummed, “Hmm-hmm,” and the lamp glowed like a golden moon. The friends cuddled into the pillows. Tully’s blanket felt cool and smooth on his toes. The room smelled like lavender and cookies from the kitchen.

“I feel calm,” Tully said. “I feel proud.”

“I feel happy,” said Mia.

“I feel gentle,” said Benny.

“I feel helpful,” chirped Pip.

They did a soft cheer. “Hooray for feelings! Hooray for sharing! Hooray for our plan!”

Tully yawned. “Thank you for listening to my feelings,” he said. “Thank you for being soft.”

Mia patted his shell. “Thank you for telling us your feelings,” she said.

They turned off the lamp. The cozy bedroom was quiet and safe. Tully’s heart slowed to a calm, sleepy beat.

The friends drifted off, knowing that when feelings got big, they could use words, make a plan, and be kind.

The End.
