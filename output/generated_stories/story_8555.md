# Pao the Patient Panda's Wiggle Breaks

Pao the patient panda lived in a cozy house with a tiny home office. The office had a sunny window, a soft blue rug, and a desk that smelled like fresh wood. On the wall was a big clock that went tick-tock, tick-tock. Pao liked the sound. It felt calm, like slow raindrops on leaves.

One morning, Pao sat at the desk to help Mama Panda sort papers. The papers made a soft shhh sound when Pao stacked them. The computer hummed like a friendly bee. Outside, birds sang sweet chirps.

“I can help all morning,” Pao said with a proud smile. “I am patient.”

Mama Panda smiled back. “Thank you, Pao. We will work a little, then rest a little.”

Pao nodded. But after a while, his legs felt wiggly. His paws felt fizzy, like they were full of tiny bubbles. His shoulders felt tight, like a snug backpack. The clock went tick-tock, and Pao’s tummy went grumble-grumble.

“I want to move,” Pao whispered.

Pao looked at the neat papers. He looked at the quiet desk. He took a deep breath. In… and out… slow and soft, like blowing a feather.

“I can be patient,” Pao said to himself. He tried to sit still, but the wiggles grew bigger. His toes tapped the rug. Tap, tap, tappity tap.

Mama Panda peeked over the desk. “I see your toes tapping, Pao. How do you feel?”

Pao touched his tummy. “My body wants to wiggle. But we are working.”

Mama nodded. “It is okay to want to move. Your body is talking to you.”

Pao blinked. “My body talks?”

Mama smiled. “Yes. It says, ‘please stretch, please breathe, please play.’ We can listen.”

Pao felt warm and happy. He liked being heard. But he looked at the stacks of papers. He wanted to help Mama. He wanted to finish.

Pao took another breath. He tried again. He counted the tick-tocks. One, two, three, four… But his back felt stiff, like a stick. His ears drooped.

“Mama,” Pao said softly, “this is hard.”

Mama nodded again. “Let’s find a way. We can work and move. We can be calm and wiggly. Would you like to try a Wiggle Break?”

Pao’s eyes grew wide. “What is a Wiggle Break?”

“It is a tiny time for movement,” Mama said. “Then you can feel calm again.”

Pao smiled. “Yes, please!”

Mama pointed to the blue rug. “Our Wiggle Break starts there.”

Pao stood up. The rug felt warm and soft under his paws. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. Dust sparkles floated like tiny stars.

Mama said, “First, we wake up our toes.”

Pao wiggled his toes. Wiggle, wiggle, wiggle! It tickled. He giggled.

“Now our paws,” Mama said.

Pao shook his paws. Shake, shake, shake! His paws felt light.

“Now we stretch tall like bamboo,” Mama said.

Pao reached his paws up, up, up. He felt his sides grow long. He breathed in. The air smelled like lemon tea. He breathed out. His chest felt soft and wide.

“Now we twist,” Mama said.

Pao twisted side to side, slow and gentle. Swish, swish. His back felt happy.

“Now we roll our shoulders like tiny circles,” Mama said.

Pao rolled. Round and round. The tight backpack feeling melted away.

Pao stopped and noticed. His heart beat slow. His body felt calm, like a smooth pond.

“How do you feel now?” Mama asked.

Pao smiled. “I feel good. I feel quiet inside. And I still feel wiggly too… but it’s a friendly wiggle.”

Mama clapped softly. “That is body wisdom. You listened.”

They went back to the desk. The papers still said shhh. The clock still went tick-tock. Pao sorted a pile. He named the colors. “White, white, white.” He lined up pens. Click, click. He made a tidy row. He felt proud.

After a bit, Pao’s legs wiggled again. This time, Pao noticed early. His tummy fizzed like bubbly water.

“Mama,” Pao said, “my body is talking again.”

Mama nodded. “What does it say?”

“It says, ‘Wiggle Break, please!’” Pao laughed.

“Let’s do it,” said Mama.

They made a new dance. Pao called it Panda Steps. Step-step, pause. Step-step, sway. He tapped like rain on leaves. He stretched tall like bamboo. He curled small like a pebble and took three slow breaths. In… two… three… Out… two… three…

When they finished, Pao felt peaceful and strong. He went back to stacking papers and humming with the computer bee. He could work. He could move. He could be calm.

Soon the work was done. Mama Panda hugged Pao. Her fur smelled like soap and honey.

“You were patient with the work,” Mama said. “And you were patient with yourself.”

Pao hugged back. “I learned to listen to my body. When it wants to move, I can move. When I move, I feel calm. I can help and wiggle too!”

Mama nodded. “That is a wonderful discovery.”

Pao made a little sign for the wall with a crayon. He wrote: Wiggle Breaks Welcome. He drew a bamboo and a happy panda.

Every time the clock went tick-tock, Pao smiled. He knew he could take a tiny break. He could stretch, breathe, and dance Panda Steps. He could accept his wiggles and find his calm.

And the home office felt even cozier, with soft rugs, sunny squares, and a panda who listened to his wise, wiggly body.

The End.
