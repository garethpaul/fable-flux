# Brave Bear’s Big Nursery Day

In a warm, comfy nursery, a friendly bear cub named Bruno hugged his soft blue blanket. The room smelled like fresh crayons and warm oatmeal. The sun made golden squares on the floor. Toys sat in baskets, books lined the shelf, and a tiny bell chimed softly on the door.

“Good morning, Bruno,” said Miss Maple, the kind teacher with a smile like sunshine. “Today we have snack time, clean-up time, and story time.”

Bruno’s round ears perked up. He liked snacks. He liked stories. He was learning to like clean-up too. But most of all, Bruno wanted to use his big, brave voice when he needed it. He was practicing being assertive—kind and strong at the same time.

The bell chimed. In bounced Pip the squirrel, Lily the bunny, and Tiko the turtle. They took off their tiny coats and put them on hooks. Bruno took a deep breath and hung his coat, too.

“First, we wash our paws,” Miss Maple said. “Clean paws for healthy tummies.”

At the sink, the water felt warm and tickly. “Rub, rub, rub,” Bruno sang, making little soap bubbles. They smelled like lemons.

Then came snack time. Miss Maple set out bowls of crunchy carrot sticks, sweet apple slices, and small cups of creamy yogurt. “These foods help your body grow strong,” she said. “They give you energy to play and learn.”

Bruno’s tummy rumbled. He reached for a bowl, but Pip zoomed in front and grabbed the biggest apple pile. Bruno’s paw paused. His heart did a tiny hop. He remembered his practice: kind and strong.

“Pip,” Bruno said in a calm, clear voice, “I was reaching for that. Please wait for your turn.”

Pip froze. His fluffy tail twitched. “Oh! I didn’t see,” Pip said. “Sorry, Bruno.”

“Thank you for listening,” Bruno said, smiling. Miss Maple smiled too. “That was assertive and kind, Bruno,” she said. “Great words.”

Everyone ate together. Crunch, munch, yum! Bruno dipped a carrot stick in yogurt. “This is creamy and crunchy,” he said. The apple was cool and sweet. “Food fuels my body,” he told himself. His tummy felt happy.

After snack, it was clean-up time. There were blocks everywhere—red, blue, yellow—and a toy train under the table. Miss Maple rang the tidy bell. “Let’s complete our task together. We can do it step by step,” she said.

Bruno picked up two blocks. Lily found the missing train car. Tiko pushed a basket with his strong shell. Pip started stacking, but then he zoomed off to the book corner, leaving a wobbly tower behind.

The blocks began to lean. Wobble… wobble… crash! They tumbled down. Bruno’s eyes went wide. His paws felt warm. He wanted to say something, but not in a grumpy way. He took a slow breath, like smelling a flower. In. Out.

“Pip,” Bruno said, voice steady and clear, “when you zip away, the blocks fall. I feel worried about the mess. Please help me finish before reading.”

Pip blinked. He looked at the blocks and at Bruno’s calm face. “Okay,” Pip said. “I can help.” He picked up the blocks and put them in the basket. “Thank you for telling me,” Pip added.

Bruno felt a warm glow in his chest. His words worked! They were not loud. They were clear.

They kept going, step by step. “We are a team,” Miss Maple said. “When we complete our tasks, we feel proud.” Bruno nodded. He stacked the last block and put the train on the shelf. Click! The basket slid into place. The floor looked smooth and safe.

“Wonderful work,” said Miss Maple. “Now our space is ready for stories.”

But first, Miss Maple brought out a small plate. “Taste time!” she said. “Would anyone like to try green peas?” The peas were bright and round like tiny moons.

Bruno looked at the peas. He wasn’t sure. He remembered to use his brave, kind words. “I want to try three peas, please,” he said. “I am learning new foods.”

Miss Maple nodded. “That’s a smart choice.” Bruno tasted one. Pop! It was sweet and soft. “I like them,” he said with surprise. “They help my body, too.”

Everyone settled onto the reading rug. It was soft like a cloud. Miss Maple opened a colorful book. As she read, Bruno leaned back and thought about his day. He used his strong, kind voice with Pip. He helped his friends clean up. He tried a new, healthy food. His heart felt big and brave.

At the end, Miss Maple asked, “What did we learn today?”

Bruno raised his paw. “I learned to say what I need in a calm voice,” he said. “That helps solve problems. I can be kind and strong. I can finish my jobs. Healthy foods help me grow.”

Miss Maple clapped softly. “Exactly,” she said. “That is being assertive. That is teamwork. That is good nutrition.”

Bruno smiled. The nursery felt extra cozy now. The bell chimed once more, gentle and sweet. He hugged his soft blue blanket and whispered, “I did it.”

The End.
