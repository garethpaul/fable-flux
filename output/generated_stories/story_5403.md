# Leo the Lion Learns to Share

In a cozy living room filled with colorful toys and soft cushions, there lived a brave little lion cub named Leo. His golden fur sparkled in the sunlight that streamed through the big window, and his tiny mane was just starting to grow fluffy around his ears.

Leo loved playing in his special living room. There were building blocks scattered on the soft blue carpet, stuffed animals piled high on the sofa, and his favorite red toy car that made vrooming sounds when he pushed it around.

One sunny morning, Leo's friend Bella the bunny came to visit. Her white fur was clean and her pink nose twitched with excitement.

"Hi Leo!" Bella said, hopping through the door. "Can we play together?"

"Yes!" roared Leo happily, but not too loudly because he was still learning to control his big voice.

Bella's eyes grew wide when she saw Leo's wonderful red car. "Wow! That car looks so fun! Can I try it?"

Leo's smile disappeared. He hugged the car close to his chest. "No! This is MY car!" he growled, his little paws gripping it tightly.

Bella's ears drooped sadly. "But... but I just wanted to try it for a little bit," she whispered.

Leo turned away and started playing with his car all by himself. Vroom, vroom! But something felt different. The vrooming sounds didn't seem as happy anymore. When he looked over at Bella, she was sitting quietly in the corner, looking very sad.

Leo's tummy felt funny - not hungry funny, but worried funny. He watched Bella's sad face and remembered how his mama always said, "A brave lion takes care of his friends."

"Maybe," Leo thought to himself, "being brave means more than just being strong."

Leo took a deep breath and walked over to Bella. "Bella?" he said softly. "I'm sorry I said no so loudly. Would you like to play with my car?"

Bella's ears perked up. "Really? You would share with me?"

"Yes," Leo nodded, his chest feeling warm and proud. "We can take turns! You can play with it, and then I can play with it, and then you can play with it again!"

"That sounds wonderful!" Bella clapped her paws together.

Leo gently placed the red car in front of Bella. She pushed it carefully around the carpet, making soft vrooming sounds. "This is so much fun! Thank you, Leo!"

After a few minutes, Bella pushed the car back to Leo. "Your turn!"

They spent the whole morning taking turns with the car, building block towers together, and sharing all of Leo's toys. The living room filled with happy laughter and joyful playing sounds.

"Leo," Bella said as they snuggled on the soft cushions, "you're the nicest friend ever. Thank you for sharing with me."

Leo's little chest puffed up with pride. "You know what, Bella? Sharing made playing even MORE fun!"

From that day on, whenever friends came to visit Leo's playful living room, he always remembered that the bravest thing a little lion can do is share with others. And every time he shared, his heart felt as warm and golden as his beautiful fur.

The End.
