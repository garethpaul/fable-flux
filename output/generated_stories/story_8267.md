# Pebbles the Penguin Writes a Kind Note

Pebbles was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a happy waddle. Pebbles lived in a cozy treehouse with little windows, a round door, and a rope ladder that swished and creaked. The treehouse smelled like pine cones and paper. The breeze whispered, and the leaves made soft shhh sounds.

One sunny morning, Pebbles held a small pencil. “I want to write,” said Pebbles. “I want to write a helpful note.”

Pebbles liked to help. Pebbles liked to learn new things. Pebbles took a deep belly breath. In and out. “I can do this,” Pebbles said.

Pebbles looked out the window. Below, friends played and shared the park. A tiny squirrel tried to carry a big bag of seeds. Some seeds fell on the path. The path looked messy.

“Oh,” said Pebbles. “The path is for everyone. The path helps us walk and roll and play. I want to help the path.”

Pebbles waddled to the desk. The desk was smooth and warm from the sun. Pebbles set out paper that felt crisp and clean. The pencil felt a little slippery in Pebbles’ flippers.

“I will write a kind note,” Pebbles said. “A note to help keep the path clean.”

Pebbles pressed the pencil. Scratch, scratch. The pencil made a soft sound. Pebbles wrote, slowly, carefully.

K-E-E-P T-H-E P-A-T-H C-L-E-A-N.

Pebbles stopped. “Is that right?” Pebbles asked gently.

A ladybug landed on the windowsill. “It looks good,” said the ladybug in a tiny voice. “But some friends are small. Some friends are still learning to read.”

Pebbles nodded. “You are right. I want my note to help everyone.”

Pebbles took a mindful moment. Pebbles put a flipper on Pebbles’ belly and felt it rise and fall. In and out. Calm and slow. “Think, Pebbles, think.”

Pebbles added a drawing of a smiling broom. Pebbles drew a happy trash bin. Pebbles added hearts. Then Pebbles wrote simple words.

“Please help. Put seeds in the bin. Thank you!”

Pebbles read the note out loud, in a soft, kind voice. “Please help. Put seeds in the bin. Thank you!”

It sounded warm, like a hug.

Pebbles climbed down the rope ladder, step, step, step. The rope felt rough. The air smelled like sweet flowers. “Hello, friends,” Pebbles called.

The tiny squirrel looked up. “Hi, Pebbles! I dropped some seeds.”

“That is okay,” said Pebbles. “We can help. This path is for everyone. Let’s tidy together.”

Pebbles showed the note. The squirrel smiled. “I can read ‘Please’ and ‘Thank you’!” the squirrel said. “And I see the broom!”

A turtle rolled by on a little scooter. The turtle read the note too. “I like your picture,” said the turtle. “I can help.”

Pebbles passed a small basket. “We can pick up seeds and put them in here,” Pebbles said. Pebbles used gentle hands. Pebbles moved slowly and watched the ground. Pebbles listened to the tiny clinks as seeds dropped into the basket. Clink, clink, clink.

More friends came. A robin flapped down. A rabbit hopped close. Pebbles smiled and said, “Thank you for helping!” It felt good to say kind words.

Soon the path was clean. The sun felt warm on everyone’s backs. The breeze felt cool on cheeks and beaks and noses. Pebbles taped the note on a little board by the path. The tape made a soft rip sound.

The mayor of the park, a wise old owl, floated from a branch. “Whoo-hoo,” said the owl. “What a helpful note! You used simple words and friendly pictures. You thought about everyone. That is civic responsibility. That means we care for the places we share.”

Pebbles stood tall. “I wanted to write a note that helps,” Pebbles said. “I took a calm breath. I listened. I learned. I used words and pictures so everyone can understand.”

The owl nodded. “That is mindful writing,” the owl said. “You looked, you listened, you cared.”

The tiny squirrel hugged Pebbles. “Your note helped me help,” the squirrel said.

Pebbles felt warm and proud. “Writing can help people know what to do,” Pebbles said. “Writing can be kind.”

Pebbles returned to the treehouse and opened the window. The paper fluttered in the breeze. Pebbles wrote another note for the desk.

“I can write to help. I can write to share. I can write to care.”

Pebbles read it out loud. The words sounded like a song. Pebbles smiled and tapped the pencil. Tap, tap, tap.

Down below, friends kept the path tidy. They put seeds in the bin. They waved at Pebbles in the window. Pebbles waved back.

Pebbles took one more slow breath. “When I want to help,” Pebbles said, “I can write. I can use simple words. I can draw pictures. I can be kind. I can think of everyone.”

The treehouse felt safe and bright. The park looked clean and happy. Pebbles felt ready to write more kind notes another day.

The problem was solved, and Pebbles learned a big lesson: Writing with calm, kind words can help everyone learn, help everyone help, and keep our shared places clean and friendly.

The End.
