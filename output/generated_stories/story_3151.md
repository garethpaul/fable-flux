# Koko the Kind Koala's Rooftop Adventure

Koko the koala lived in a tall building with a special rooftop garden. Every morning, she climbed the safe stairs to the top, holding tight to the railing. The rooftop was her favorite place because she could see the whole neighborhood and feel the warm sunshine on her fluffy gray fur.

One sunny morning, Koko heard something new. "Chirp, chirp, chirp!" It was a very sad sound.

"What could that be?" Koko wondered aloud. She listened carefully with her big round ears. The sound was coming from near the rooftop garden's flower boxes.

Koko walked slowly and safely across the rooftop, staying away from the edges just like her mama had taught her. "Always stay in the middle of the rooftop, Koko," she remembered her mama saying. "That keeps you safe and sound."

Behind a big sunflower, Koko found three tiny baby birds in a nest. They were chirping loudly because they were very hungry.

"Oh my!" said Koko gently. "Where is your mama, little ones?"

The baby birds chirped even louder. Koko looked around and saw Mama Bird on the next building's rooftop. She was hurt and couldn't fly back to her babies.

"Don't worry," Koko called to Mama Bird. "I will help you!"

Koko thought carefully about what to do. She remembered her breakfast - a whole bag of delicious eucalyptus leaves and some sweet berries she had been saving for later.

"I know what I can do," Koko said with a smile. "I can share my food!"

But first, Koko needed to get to Mama Bird safely. She looked at the gap between the buildings. It was too far to jump, and that wouldn't be safe anyway.

Koko had an idea! She found a long, strong board that the rooftop gardeners had left behind. Very carefully, she placed it between the two buildings, making sure it was steady and secure.

"Safety first!" Koko said, just like her mama taught her.

Koko crawled across the board very slowly, staying low and holding on tight. When she reached Mama Bird, she saw that the bird had a hurt wing.

"Here," said Koko kindly, offering some of her sweet berries. "Eat these to feel better. I brought extra eucalyptus leaves too - maybe you'd like to try something new!"

Mama Bird ate gratefully. "Thank you, kind koala," she chirped softly. "But my babies are still hungry, and I can't fly to them."

"Don't worry," said Koko. "I have an idea!"

Koko carefully carried Mama Bird on her back as she crawled across the board again. Then she helped Mama Bird hop to her nest where the baby birds were waiting.

"Look what I brought for everyone!" Koko said cheerfully. She shared all her berries with the bird family and even gave them some of her eucalyptus leaves to try.

The baby birds chirped happily as they ate. Mama Bird's wing felt better after resting and eating the nutritious food.

"Thank you for listening to us and sharing your breakfast," said Mama Bird. "You are the kindest koala in the whole neighborhood!"

Koko felt warm and happy inside. Even though she had given away her breakfast, seeing the bird family together and fed made her feel wonderful.

"Sharing makes everything better," Koko said with a big smile. "And helping friends is the best feeling of all!"

From that day on, Koko always brought extra food to the rooftop, just in case someone needed help. She learned that being generous and listening to others made her neighborhood a happier place for everyone.

The End.
