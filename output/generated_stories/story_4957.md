# Benny Beaver’s Study Corner Chat

Benny the busy beaver had a cozy study corner. It was warm and bright. A small lamp made a soft, golden glow. The desk smelled like fresh wood. Benny loved the smooth feel of paper and the tick-tick of his little clock.

Today, Benny wanted to make a “Sharing Book.” He wanted to draw pictures and write simple words to share with his friends. He took a deep breath. He wiggled his whiskers. He picked up a fat, blue crayon.

“Busy, busy, Benny!” he hummed. “Draw, write, share.”

Benny started to draw a big, happy sun. But his crayon slipped. Scribble, scribble! The sun looked squiggly. Benny frowned. His tail thumped softly on the floor. Thump, thump.

“I want it to be nice,” he said. “How can I make it better?”

He looked around his study corner. He saw his jar of crayons—red, blue, yellow, green. He saw his stack of clean paper. He saw his little bell for help. Ding!

Benny took a pause. He remembered something his teacher said: “Use your words. Ask for help. Practice makes strong.”

Benny rang his bell. Ding! “I need help,” he said out loud.

His sister, Bella, peeked in. “Hi, Benny! I heard your bell. What’s up?”

Benny showed his squiggly sun. “I want to make a Sharing Book. I want to share with friends. But my sun is wiggly. I feel grumpy.”

Bella sat beside him. The chair made a squeak. “It’s okay to feel grumpy,” she said. “Let’s talk. Can you tell me what you want it to look like?”

Benny nodded. “Round and bright. With lines that go out. I want to write the word sun, too.”

Bella smiled. “Great communication, Benny! You told me your plan. Let’s practice together.”

She took a yellow crayon. “Watch,” she said gently. She drew a slow circle. “Round and steady.” Then she added straight lines. “Sunny rays.”

Benny watched closely. “I can try,” he said.

He held his crayon again. He drew slow. Round and steady. Then little lines. Ray, ray, ray. He whispered, “Practice, practice.” His tail made a happy swish.

“You did it!” said Bella. “Now, use your words to write s-u-n.”

Benny sounded it out. “Sss… uh… nnn.” He wrote the letters big and bouncy. SUN.

Benny felt warm with pride. He smiled so wide his cheeks felt cozy. “Thank you, Bella. I used my words, and I practiced.”

“You sure did,” Bella said. “What’s next in your Sharing Book?”

Benny thought. He sniffed the crayon box—sweet and waxy. “A snack page!” he said. “I want to share snacks with my friends.”

He drew a crunchy apple and a square of cheese. He wrote A P P L E and C H E E S E, slowly, with Bella cheering, “Sound it out! You can do it!”

Soon, their friend Dot the duck knocked on the door. Tap, tap. “Quack-hello!” Dot said. “What are you doing? It smells like crayons in here!”

Benny waved. “Hi, Dot! I’m making a Sharing Book. I’m practicing my drawings. Do you want to help?”

Dot waddled in, her feet going slap-slap on the floor. “Yes! I can share my ideas,” she said. “Let’s talk about what to put in the book.”

Benny pointed to a blank page. “We can have a feelings page. We can share how we feel and what we need.”

Dot clapped her wings. “Good idea! When I feel sad, I like a hug.”

Benny drew a little duck and a beaver hugging. He wrote H U G. “When I feel mad,” he said, “I like a quiet sip of water.”

Dot drew a tiny cup. “Sip, sip,” she said with a giggle.

Bella added, “When I feel shy, I like a friend to sit with me.”

They all talked and listened. They took turns. They shared the crayons. “My turn, your turn,” Benny said. The crayons made soft scratchy sounds. Scratch, scratch. The paper felt smooth under their paws and wings.

At last, the Sharing Book had many pages: sun and snacks, hugs and sips, quiet and rest, please and thank you. Benny rang the bell again. Ding! “Time to read!” he said.

Benny cleared his throat. “Friends, let’s communicate. We can use our words and our listening ears.” He pointed to his ears. Dot pointed to hers. Bella pointed to hers too.

Benny read in a strong, sweet voice. “When we share, we feel good. When we talk, we understand. When we practice, we grow.”

Dot said, “I like that. Quack-yes!”

Bella said, “Me too. Your book is wonderful, Benny.”

Benny felt calm and proud. He looked around his study corner. The lamp glowed. The clock ticked. His friends smiled.

“I learned to say what I need,” Benny said. “I learned to listen. I learned to practice. I learned to share.”

They put the Sharing Book on the shelf. Benny gave Dot a crayon to take home. “Here. You can practice, too.”

Dot hugged it gently. “Thank you for sharing.”

Benny nodded. “Talking helps. Practice helps. Sharing helps. We can do it together.”

The End.
