# Benny Bear's Big Voice

Benny Bear was a small, fluffy cub with soft brown fur and curious black eyes. He loved spending time in Grandpa Bear's garage workshop, where the air smelled like wood shavings and motor oil.

The garage was full of wonderful things. Shiny tools hung on the walls, and colorful paint cans sat on tall shelves. Benny's favorite spot was the wooden workbench where Grandpa fixed broken toys and built new treasures.

Today, Grandpa Bear was working on a special birdhouse. *Tap, tap, tap* went his hammer. Benny wanted to help so badly, but he felt too small and shy.

"Grandpa," Benny whispered very quietly, "can I help?"

Grandpa Bear kept hammering. The workshop was noisy with the sounds of his work. He didn't hear little Benny's tiny voice.

Benny tried again, just a little louder. "Grandpa, may I help please?"

But Grandpa was now using his buzzing saw. *Bzzzz, bzzzz!* The loud noise covered up Benny's words completely.

Benny's shoulders drooped. His ears felt droopy too. "I guess I'm too little," he said sadly to himself. "Maybe Grandpa doesn't want my help."

He sat on a small stool in the corner, watching Grandpa work. The birdhouse was getting prettier and prettier, but Benny felt left out and forgotten.

Then Grandpa Bear noticed something was wrong. He turned off his saw and looked around the quiet garage.

"Benny? Where's my little helper?" Grandpa called out. "This birdhouse needs your special touch!"

Benny looked up with surprise. "You... you want my help?"

"Of course I do!" Grandpa smiled warmly. "I was waiting for you to ask. But I think maybe I couldn't hear you with all this noise."

Benny realized something important. He had been speaking too quietly! In a noisy workshop, he needed to use his big voice.

"Grandpa!" Benny said clearly and loudly. "I would really like to help you build the birdhouse!"

"Now THAT'S what I call speaking up!" Grandpa laughed. "When we need someone to hear us, we must use our strong, clear voice. It's not rude - it's helpful!"

Benny felt his confidence growing like a warm feeling in his chest. "What can I do to help?"

"You can hold this piece steady while I nail it," Grandpa said. "And you can pick the perfect color paint for our bird friends."

Benny carefully held the wood piece. His small paws were actually perfect for the job! When it was time to choose paint, he picked a cheerful yellow that would make the birds happy.

"You know what, Benny?" Grandpa said as they painted together. "Speaking up isn't just about being loud. It's about being brave enough to share your thoughts and ask for what you need."

"Like asking for help?" Benny asked, dipping his brush in the sunny paint.

"Exactly! And asking questions, and telling people how you feel. Your voice matters, no matter how small you are."

As they finished their beautiful birdhouse, Benny felt proud and confident. He had learned that using his voice clearly helped him connect with others and get the help he needed.

"Thank you for teaching me about using my big voice, Grandpa," Benny said clearly and proudly.

"Thank you for being such a wonderful helper," Grandpa replied. "Tomorrow, what would you like to build together?"

"A doghouse!" Benny said with confidence. "And I'll make sure to speak up right away!"

They hung their yellow birdhouse outside the garage, where it would welcome feathered friends with its bright, cheerful color.

The End.
