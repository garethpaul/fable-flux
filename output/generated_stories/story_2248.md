# Leo’s Smart Study Plan

In a cozy study corner, a brave little lion cub named Leo sat on a soft rug. The rug felt warm and fuzzy under his paws. A small lamp glowed like a friendly moon. On the low table were crayons, a picture book, and a puzzle with animal shapes.

Leo wrinkled his nose. “This puzzle is hard,” he said. His tail made little flick-flick sounds on the rug.

Mama Lion peeked in. “Hello, brave cub. What’s the plan?” she asked kindly.

“The plan?” Leo blinked. He didn’t have a plan. He felt a tight twist in his tummy.

Mama smiled. “When something feels big, we can use a strategy. A strategy is a smart plan. Let’s breathe first.”

Leo took a slow breath in. He smelled paper, crayons, and a hint of vanilla from a candle. He breathed out. The twist in his tummy got smaller.

“Good,” Mama said. “What is the problem?”

Leo looked at the puzzle. “I want to finish it, but I keep getting stuck.”

“What could be your first small step?” asked Mama.

Leo’s ears perked. “Start with the corners!” he said.

“That’s a great strategy,” Mama said. “I’ll be right here if you need me.”

Leo looked carefully. The puzzle had four corner pieces with little star stickers. “Corner, corner, corner, corner,” he sang, tapping each one. Click! The first piece fit. Click! The second. Leo’s whiskers twitched with pride.

He tried another piece. It didn’t fit. “Hmm,” he said. “I will try again.” He turned the piece slowly. “Turn and check.” Click! It fit. Leo grinned.

Just then, his friend Tia the tiny turtle peeked around the doorway. “Hi, Leo,” she said softly. “Can I sit in your study corner too?”

“Yes!” Leo said. “We can share.” He moved over and patted the rug. “I’m using a strategy.”

“What is your strategy?” Tia asked, her eyes bright.

“Start small. Corners first. Then turn and check,” Leo said. “Do you want to help?”

Tia nodded. “I like to sort by color,” she said. She gently pushed the blue pieces into a little pile. Tap, tap. Her shell made a quiet shhh sound as she moved.

“That’s a good strategy too,” Leo said. He felt warm and big inside. “We can use both!”

They worked side by side. Leo used his paw to guide the corner pieces. Tia sorted colors and found edge pieces. “Blue sky pieces go on top,” Tia said.

“Green grass pieces go on the bottom,” Leo added.

Sometimes a piece did not fit. Leo felt a tiny frown. “It’s okay,” he whispered to himself. “Breathe, think, try.” He took a breath. He tried a different spot. “Turn and check.” Click!

“You did it!” Tia smiled.

“You helped,” Leo said. He looked at Tia with respect. “Your color idea was smart.”

Tia’s cheeks glowed. “Thank you. Your corner plan was brave.”

They kept going. The picture started to grow. A blue sky. A green field. A shiny sun with golden rays. The lamp hummed softly. The crayons smelled waxy and sweet. The rug felt safe.

Then they reached the last few pieces. One piece looked tricky. It had a little curve and a tiny dot.

Leo paused. “Let’s make a new plan,” he said. “We can look for the dot in the picture.”

Tia nodded. “Scan the picture,” she whispered, sliding her finger over the puzzle. “Dot, dot, dot…”

“There!” Leo pointed. “The dot is on the lion’s nose!”

They giggled. “Turn and check,” they said together. Click! The last piece fit. The puzzle showed a big lion and a little lion cub, both smiling in the sun.

“We did it!” Leo cheered. He felt tall and proud. His tail made happy swish-swish sounds.

Mama Lion came back with two cups of warm milk. “I see teamwork,” she said. “I see respect. I see a smart strategy.”

Leo sipped the milk. It tasted creamy and cozy. “I felt stuck,” he told Mama, “but I made a plan. Breathe, think, try. Corners first. Sort by color. Turn and check. Look for clues.”

Tia nodded. “We took turns and listened.”

Mama smiled. “That’s how brave learners grow. You used your minds and your manners.”

Leo looked at Tia. “Thank you for sharing your idea,” he said.

“Thank you for sharing your space,” Tia replied.

Leo touched the finished puzzle gently. He felt calm and bright. “When I have a big problem,” he said, “I will use a strategy, believe in myself, and be kind.”

Tia smiled. “Me too.”

They cleaned up together. Crayons in a neat cup. Book on the shelf. Puzzle in the box. The study corner felt tidy and happy.

Leo stretched and yawned. “I am a brave little lion cub with a smart plan,” he said.

“And a good friend,” Tia added.

Leo’s heart purred. He knew what to do next time a problem felt big: breathe, think, try, and use a plan—while respecting others and believing in himself.

The End.
