# Ollie Otter's Sharing Day

Ollie the otter loved playing in his cozy living room. The soft, fluffy carpet felt wonderful under his tiny paws, and colorful toys were scattered everywhere like a rainbow. His favorite red ball sat in the corner, shiny and bouncing, just waiting for fun.

"Bounce, bounce, bounce!" Ollie giggled as he rolled his special ball across the room. The ball made a happy *thump-thump* sound on the carpet.

Just then, the doorbell rang. *Ding-dong!* Ollie's whiskers twitched with excitement. He scampered to the window and saw his friend Benny the bunny standing outside, looking very sad.

"Come in, Benny!" Ollie called, opening the door wide.

Benny hopped inside slowly, his ears drooping down. "Hi, Ollie," he said quietly. "I was hoping we could play together. All my toys broke yesterday in the big storm."

Ollie looked at his wonderful living room full of toys - his building blocks, his puzzle pieces, his books, and especially his precious red ball. His tummy felt funny and tight. These were HIS toys. What if Benny broke them too?

"Um... maybe we could just sit and talk?" Ollie said, holding his red ball close to his chest.

Benny's eyes grew even sadder. "Oh, okay," he whispered. He sat down on the soft carpet and stared at his paws.

Ollie watched his friend and felt his heart growing heavy. Benny looked so lonely and sad. The living room felt too quiet without laughter and play.

"Wait," Ollie said, taking a deep breath. He remembered what Mama Otter always said: "Sharing makes happiness grow bigger, not smaller."

Ollie felt brave and walked over to Benny. "Would you like to play with my red ball?" he asked, holding it out with both paws.

Benny's eyes sparkled like stars. "Really? You'd share with me?"

"Yes!" Ollie said, feeling warm and happy inside. "And we can build with my blocks too! Sharing is more fun than playing alone."

"Thank you, Ollie! You're the best friend ever!" Benny exclaimed, his ears perking up tall.

The two friends spent the whole afternoon playing together. They bounced the red ball back and forth - *bounce, catch, bounce, catch!* They built a tall tower with colorful blocks that reached almost to the ceiling. They even shared Ollie's favorite fish-shaped crackers, which tasted extra delicious when eaten with a friend.

"This is the most fun I've ever had!" Benny laughed as they played hide-and-seek behind the soft couch cushions.

"Me too!" Ollie agreed. "My toys are even more fun when we share them!"

When it was time for Benny to go home, he gave Ollie the biggest, warmest hug. "Thank you for being so generous and kind," Benny said. "You made my sad day into the happiest day!"

Ollie felt proud and glowing inside. His living room looked exactly the same, but everything felt different - better and brighter.

That night, as Ollie curled up in his cozy bed, he smiled big. He had learned something wonderful: when you share what you have with others, you don't lose anything. Instead, you gain something even better - the joy of making a friend happy and the fun of playing together.

From that day on, Ollie's living room became the most popular playroom in the neighborhood, filled with laughter, friendship, and lots of generous sharing.

The End.
