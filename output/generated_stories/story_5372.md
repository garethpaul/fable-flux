# Songbird at the Study Corner Games

Chirpy was a small, singing bird with soft blue feathers and a bright yellow beak. He loved to sing sweet songs in his cozy study corner. The study corner had a tiny desk, neat pencils in a cup, and a soft green rug that felt warm on Chirpy’s feet. Sunlight made gentle squares on the floor, and the air smelled like paper and crayons.

“Tweet-a-dee! Tweet-a-doo!” Chirpy sang as he stacked little cards with letters and numbers. He liked to learn and he liked to play. Today, he wanted to do both.

Chirpy fluttered to the window and called, “Friends, come play Study Corner Games!”

Soon, his friends came hopping and giggling in. There was Bouncy the bunny with long ears, Rollie the round hedgehog, and Swoosh the fast little squirrel. They all wore tiny ribbon headbands that squeaked when they moved.

“Hi, Chirpy!” said Bouncy. “What games will we play?”

Chirpy smiled. “We will play gentle sports and learn too! We will have the Pencil Balance Walk, the Soft Ball Toss, and the Number Hop. We will have fun, and we will be kind.”

Rollie sniffed the air. “Mmm, I smell apples.”

Chirpy chirped, “Yes, apple slices for after the games! But first, we play. Remember, we use friendly words. We take turns. We tell the truth.”

“Okay!” said Swoosh, swishing his tail. “I like to go fast, but I can wait my turn.”

They started with the Pencil Balance Walk. Chirpy put a pencil on his wing and walked carefully on the green rug line. The rug felt tickly on his toes. “Step, step, breathe,” Chirpy sang softly.

Bouncy tried next, balancing the pencil on his head. “I can do it!” he said. The pencil wobbled. “Oops!” It fell with a tiny tap. Bouncy laughed. “I will try again.”

“Good try,” said Chirpy. “Friends help friends. You can do it!”

Rollie went last. He balanced the pencil on his back spikes and rolled slow. The pencil made a funny shh sound as it slid. Everyone giggled.

Next was the Soft Ball Toss. Chirpy had a small, squishy ball as light as a cloud. “We toss to the cup,” he said, pointing to the pencil cup. “Not too hard. Gentle toss. We count to three.”

Swoosh stood first. “One, two, three!” He tossed. The ball bounced off the rim and rolled under the desk.

“I will get it,” said Rollie, scooting under the desk. He came out with dust on his nose. “Achoo!” Everyone laughed kindly, and Rollie smiled.

Chirpy took a turn and made the ball land with a plop. “Tweet! Good feeling,” he said, clapping his wings.

Now it was Bouncy’s turn. He wiggled his ears and whispered, “I can do it.”

He tossed. The ball missed the cup, but then Bouncy looked around and nudged it with his foot. The ball rolled into the cup.

“I did it!” Bouncy said, eyes wide.

Chirpy blinked. He had seen the little nudge. Swoosh had seen it too. The room got very quiet. Chirpy felt a small squeeze in his chest. He liked winning, but he liked being true even more. Friendship needs truth, he thought.

Chirpy sang softly, “Tweet-a-dee... I saw a tiny nudge.”

Bouncy’s ears drooped. He looked at his feet. “I’m sorry,” he said in a small voice. “I wanted to win. I did not wait for luck. I gave it a push.”

Chirpy walked close and put a wing on Bouncy’s arm. The feathers were warm and soft. “Thank you for telling the truth,” he said gently. “That is called integrity. It means doing the right thing, even when no one is looking, and also when your friends are looking. Friends are honest.”

Swoosh nodded. “We can try again,” he said. “We are a team.”

Rollie smiled and offered the ball back. “Friends first. Games next.”

Bouncy took a deep breath. “Can I try again, the honest way?”

“Yes,” said Chirpy. “We will cheer for you, no matter what.”

Bouncy counted. “One, two, three.” He tossed. The ball bounced on the rim and fell out. He did not push. He stood still.

Chirpy sang a happy note. “Good try! You were honest. That is a win in our hearts.”

Everyone clapped. It sounded like soft rain on leaves.

Now they played Number Hop. Chirpy laid number cards on the rug: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5. “We hop on the numbers and say them out loud,” he said.

They took turns hopping: hop, hop, hop. The rug cushioned their feet. They counted together, voices bright and brave. “One! Two! Three! Four! Five!”

Swoosh tripped on the 4 card and almost fell. Bouncy reached out a paw and helped him up. “I’ve got you,” Bouncy said.

“Thank you,” said Swoosh. “You are a good friend.”

At last, Chirpy brought the apple slices. They were cool and crunchy. Juice dripped down Bouncy’s chin. “Crunch!” he said with a grin.

Chirpy sang a gentle song as they ate. “Friends tell the truth. Friends take turns. Friends cheer for each other. We play fair, we care, we share.”

Bouncy wiped his chin. “I learned something,” he said. “Winning is nice, but being honest and kind feels better.”

Rollie nodded. “Integrity is like a warm blanket,” he said. “It keeps our friendship safe.”

Swoosh raised his tiny cup of water. “To friends!”

Chirpy lifted a slice of apple. “To friends, to truth, and to fun games in the study corner.”

The sun made golden stripes across the rug. The study corner felt bright and safe. The friends smiled, full of apples and happy hearts. They knew they would play again, and they knew they would be fair.

The clear lesson of the day was simple: Be a good friend. Tell the truth. Play fair. That is how friendship grows strong.

The End.
