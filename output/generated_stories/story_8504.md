# Ella Tries Something New on the Porch

Ella was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and bright curious eyes. She loved sitting on the peaceful front porch of her little house. The porch had a wooden floor that felt warm in the sun. A soft breeze tickled Ella’s trunk. She could smell sweet flowers and hear birds singing, “Tweet, tweet!” in the trees.

“Good morning, porch,” Ella said. Her voice was calm and kind.

Mama Elephant brought two cups of cool water with lemon. “Good morning, Ella. Would you like to try porch stretches with me?” Mama asked. She rolled out a sunny yellow mat.

Ella looked at the mat. “I don’t know,” she said softly. Her tummy felt fluttery. “What if I look silly?”

Mama smiled. “It’s okay to feel unsure,” she said. “You can watch first. We keep open minds here on the porch.”

Ella nodded. She took a sip of her cool water. The lemon smelled bright and tasted tangy. “I’ll watch,” she said.

Just then, Ella’s friend Pip the Sparrow landed on the railing. “Chirp! I love stretches,” Pip sang. “Can I join?”

“Yes!” Ella said. She was glad to see a friend. The breeze ruffled Pip’s tiny feathers.

Mama showed a simple move. “Reach up, like you’re picking a cloud,” she said.

Pip reached up with his wings. “Chirp! I got a tiny cloud!” he laughed.

Ella watched. Her trunk wiggled. She wanted to try, but her legs felt wobbly.

Mama looked at Ella’s eyes. “Ella, how do you feel right now?” she asked.

“I feel nervous,” Ella said. She pressed a paw to her chest. “My heart is thump-thump. I don’t want to be laughed at.”

“That is very brave to say,” Mama said. “Thank you for sharing your feeling.”

Pip fluttered closer. “I won’t laugh,” he chirped. “I will cheer!”

Ella took a deep breath. She smelled lemon and flowers. “Okay,” she said. “I will try one reach.”

She stood on the mat. The wood was smooth under her big feet. She stretched her trunk up, up, up. A sunbeam touched her ear. It felt warm and nice.

“I’m picking a cloud,” Ella said. She smiled.

“Wonderful!” Mama clapped softly. Pip chirped a tiny cheer song.

Ella giggled. “That felt good!”

Mama showed another move. “Now we bend and say hello to our toes.”

Ella looked down at her toes. They were round and dusty from the porch. “Hello, toes,” she said. She bent a little. Her back felt a gentle stretch.

“I did it! I tried!” Ella said. Her nervous feeling got smaller. She noticed pride blooming like a flower in her chest.

Pip hopped in place. “Chirp! Next, trunk circles! Round and round!”

Ella tried trunk circles. Around and around she went, slow like a cloud. The breeze brushed her cheeks. The birds kept singing. The porch smelled like pine and sunshine.

A neighbor bunny named Blue peeked through the fence. “Hi,” Blue said, shy and quiet. “What are you doing?”

“We are doing porch stretches,” Ella said. She stood tall. “I was scared at first, but I tried. It felt nice.”

Blue twitched his nose. “I don’t think I can. I am too small.”

Ella remembered her fluttery tummy. She kept an open mind. “Maybe just watch,” she said kindly. “You can decide. It’s okay to say yes or no.”

Blue nodded and watched. Mama showed a gentle march. “We lift our knees and swing our arms. March, march, march.”

Ella marched with soft thumps. Thump, thump, thump. Pip tapped the railing like a drum. Tap-tap, tap-tap.

Blue smiled. “It looks fun,” he whispered.

Ella took a calm breath. She wanted to help, but she didn’t want to push. “Blue,” she said, “if you want, you can try one march. If you don’t want to, that is okay too.”

“That is assertive and kind,” Mama said softly.

Blue took one tiny march. Then another. “I can do little ones,” he said, his ears wiggling.

“You did it!” Ella cheered. She felt warm and proud, like sunshine inside. Her nervous feeling had turned into a brave feeling.

They all did one more stretch together. “Reach for the sky,” Mama said.

Ella reached high. She thought, I am trying new things. I can keep my mind open. I can say how I feel. I can say yes. I can say no.

They finished with a big, slow breath in and out. The porch was quiet and peaceful. A butterfly floated by, soft as a whisper.

Blue waved. “Thank you,” he said. “I liked trying a small bit.”

“Me too,” Ella said. “I’m glad I tried.”

Mama gave Ella a hug. “What did we learn?” she asked.

Ella touched her chest. “I learned to keep an open mind,” she said. “Trying new things can feel scary, but I can breathe, say my feelings, and try a little. I can also be kind and let others choose.”

Pip chirped, “And exercise can be fun on a sunny porch!”

Ella laughed. “Yes! Fun and kind and brave.”

The breeze hummed. The porch felt safe and bright. Ella sipped her cool lemon water and smiled.

The End.
