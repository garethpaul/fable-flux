# Leo’s Quiet Roar

In a cozy study corner, a brave little lion cub named Leo sat on a soft rug. The rug felt fluffy under his paws. A small lamp made warm, golden light. Shelves held books that smelled like paper and dust. A chalkboard stood nearby with a tiny piece of chalk. The chalk felt smooth and a little cold.

Grandma Lala, with silver whiskers and kind eyes, sat beside Leo. She wore a colorful scarf that smelled like sweet cinnamon tea. She smiled and said, “Leo, tonight is our Family Roar Night. It is our special tradition. We share stories and songs from our family.”

Leo’s ears perked up. “I want to share! But I don’t know how,” he said in a small voice. His tail twitched. His tummy felt fluttery.

Grandma Lala nodded. “We learn by practicing. We also prepare. That is our way.” She tapped the chalkboard. Tap, tap. “Let’s get ready together.”

Leo took the chalk. “I can do it!” he said, brave and bright. He tried to write a big R for “Roar.” The chalk squeaked. Eeeek! The line was wiggly. Leo frowned. “It’s too hard,” he huffed. His paws felt tight. His eyes felt hot.

Grandma Lala placed a gentle paw on his paw. “When I was a cub, I felt that too. Our tradition is to share, to teach, and to try again. We breathe, we break it into small steps, and we build our skill.”

Leo took a breath. In and out. His chest rose and fell like a slow wave.

“Step one,” Grandma Lala said, smiling. “Warm up.” She handed Leo a soft brush. “Brush the chalkboard like you brush your mane.”

Swish, swish went the brush. It felt calm and smooth. Leo smiled a little. “That feels nice.”

“Step two,” said Grandma Lala. “Trace.” She drew dotted lines for the letter R. “Follow the dots.”

Leo pressed lightly. Skritch, skratch. He followed the line. “I did it!” he giggled. His whiskers tingled with pride.

“Step three,” Grandma Lala said. “Practice your roar song.” She hummed a sweet, deep tune. “Our tradition is to sing the Roar Song to welcome everyone.”

Leo opened his mouth. “Rrr—” His roar came out tiny. “Rrr… meep.” He frowned again. “I can’t do it. My roar is small.”

Grandma Lala chuckled softly. “Small roars grow. Try the Lion Breath.” She showed him: paws on belly, slow breath in, slow breath out. “Feel the breath make your belly a balloon.”

Leo tried. In… out… His belly rose like bread baking. Warmth spread in his chest. He tried the roar again. “Rrr—ooo—ar!” It was still small but clearer. He smiled. “Better!”

“Very good,” said Grandma Lala. “Our family shares more than sounds. We share stories. Here is part of our old story.” She opened a little red book. The pages smelled like old leaves. “Once, a brave cub learned to roar by listening first, then practicing.”

“I can listen,” Leo said. He leaned close. He heard the lamp hum. Mmmmmm. He heard pages whisper. Shhh. He heard Grandma’s heart thump, calm and steady. Thump, thump.

Grandma Lala nodded. “You listened well. Now we prepare for tonight.” She pointed to a list on the board:
1. Warm up
2. Trace and write
3. Lion Breath
4. Practice song
5. Share the story

Leo traced the list with a paw. “I can do these steps.”

They practiced together. Leo wrote R, then O, then A, then R. The chalk tickled his paw. He sang the Roar Song softly, then louder. “Rrr-oo-ar, welcome, we share!” he sang. He told the short story back to Grandma, using simple words. “A cub tried. A cub breathed. A cub shared.”

When evening came, the study corner glowed. Family came in, paws quiet on the floor. Grandpa Tamu brought warm honey tea. Auntie Nia wore jingling beads. Baby cousin Poppy clapped, clap-clap!

Grandma Lala stood. “Welcome to Family Roar Night.” She nodded to Leo. “Our brave little lion cub will begin.”

Leo’s heart thumped fast. His paws felt wiggly. He remembered Step Three. Lion Breath. In… out… His belly ballooned and softened. He looked at the list on the chalkboard. He smiled.

He wrote R on the board. Skritch! He sang the Roar Song. “Rrr-oo-ar, welcome, we share!” His voice was clear. He told the short family story in his own words. “Long ago, a cub learned to roar by listening, by breathing, by trying again. We share our ways so we grow.”

The room was warm with happy sounds. “Bravo, Leo!” said Auntie Nia. “You prepared so well,” said Grandpa Tamu, his voice deep like a drum. Baby Poppy squealed, “Roar!”

Leo felt proud. His frustration had melted like ice in the sun. He nuzzled Grandma Lala. “Thank you for sharing our tradition,” he whispered.

Grandma Lala hugged him. “And thank you for keeping it alive. When we share, we remember. When we practice, we build skills. When we feel upset, we breathe and try again.”

Leo nodded. His tail swished. He looked at the little red book and the chalkboard. “I will teach Poppy when she is ready,” he said. “We will share our tradition together.”

The family laughed and sang the Roar Song once more. The study corner glowed with love and learning. Leo’s quiet roar felt big inside.

The End.
