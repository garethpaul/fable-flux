# Benny Bear's Treehouse Surprise

Benny Bear climbed up the wooden ladder to his favorite treehouse. His soft brown paws gripped each rung carefully. *Creak, creak* went the old wood as he reached the top.

"What a perfect day to play!" Benny said happily. The morning sun felt warm on his fuzzy ears, and he could smell the sweet flowers blooming below.

But when Benny peeked inside his treehouse, he gasped. "Oh my!" His toy blocks were scattered everywhere. His crayons were broken. His favorite teddy bear was lying upside down in the corner.

Benny's bottom lip started to wobble. "Who made this big mess in MY treehouse?"

Just then, he heard tiny voices coming from behind a pile of books. *Squeak, squeak!*

"Please don't be mad," whispered a small voice.

Two little mice poked their heads out. The mama mouse had tears in her bright black eyes. The baby mouse was shivering.

"We're so sorry," said Mama Mouse. "It rained very hard last night. Our home got all wet and cold. We were scared, so we came here to stay warm."

Benny felt his angry feelings start to melt away. He looked at the frightened mice family and remembered how scary storms could be.

"You were cold and scared?" Benny asked gently.

The baby mouse nodded. "We tried to be careful, but everything was so big for us. We knocked things over by accident."

Benny sat down cross-legged and thought hard. His grandpa always said, "When someone needs help, a good bear finds a way to help."

"I understand," Benny said with a kind smile. "Everyone needs a safe place when they're scared. But next time, could you please ask first? This is my special place."

"We promise!" squeaked both mice together.

"Now," said Benny, clapping his paws, "let's clean up together and then have a healthy snack!"

The mice's eyes lit up. "Really? You're not mad anymore?"

"Friends help each other," Benny explained. "And good friends share."

They worked as a team. Benny picked up the big blocks while the mice organized the small crayons. Soon the treehouse looked neat and tidy again.

"This is fun!" giggled the baby mouse as they stacked the last book.

Benny opened his lunch box and found crispy apples, crunchy carrots, and fresh berries. "Healthy foods give us energy to play," he said, sharing the snacks with his new friends.

As they munched together, Benny had a wonderful idea. "Would you like to build a tiny mouse house right next to my treehouse? Then you'll always have a safe, dry home!"

"That would be amazing!" cheered Mama Mouse.

They spent the afternoon gathering soft leaves and small twigs. Benny used his strong paws to help build while the mice used their tiny paws for the detailed work.

When the sun began to set, they admired their work. A perfect little mouse house sat right beside the treehouse, connected by a small bridge made of sticks.

"Thank you for being so kind and forgiving," said Mama Mouse, giving Benny a tiny hug.

"Thank you for teaching me that sharing makes everything more fun," Benny replied.

From that day on, Benny and the mice family were the very best of friends. They learned that respecting each other's homes, asking permission, and working together made their treehouse neighborhood the happiest place in the whole forest.

The End.
