# Brave Benny Beaver on the Rooftop Garden

Benny was a busy beaver with bright eyes and strong teeth. One sunny morning, he climbed up, up, up to the rooftop of the tall library. The rooftop had a small garden with green leaves, tiny yellow flowers, and boxes full of soil. The air smelled like sweet tomatoes and fresh mint. Bumblebees hummed softly, and the wind tickled Benny’s whiskers.

Benny wiggled his tail. “I will help the rooftop garden,” he said. “I love plants. Plants help animals and people. That is conservation. I can be brave and do good work.”

He wore a little tool belt. Inside were small sticks, a scoop, a water can, and a tiny notebook for planning. He patted the belt. Tap, tap. “I plan, then I do,” Benny said.

Just then, a little sparrow named Pip landed on the railing. “Cheep! Benny, there is a problem,” Pip chirped.

Benny’s ears perked up. “What is the problem, Pip?”

Pip pointed with a tiny wing. “Cheep! The water tank is stuck. The hose is twisted. The plants are thirsty. The sun is hot. I am worried.”

Benny looked at the hose. It was twisted like a knotty snake. The metal water tank was high on a shelf. It made a deep, echo-y clunk when the wind moved it. Clunk. Clunk. Benny felt a small flip in his tummy.

“This looks tall,” Benny whispered. He touched the warm rooftop tiles. He listened to the bees. He smelled the tomato vines. He remembered his lesson. “Bravery is trying, even when I feel a little scared,” he said softly.

Pip tilted his head. “Cheep? Can you fix it?”

“I can try,” Benny said. He took out his tiny notebook. “Step one: Think. Step two: Make a plan. Step three: Be brave and safe. Step four: Help the plants.”

He drew quick pictures. He tapped his chin. “I need to reach the tank. I need to untwist the hose. I need to give water. And I need a snack to be strong.” He opened his lunch. Crunch! He ate a carrot stick. Munch! He ate a slice of apple. “Healthy food gives me energy,” Benny said. “That is good nutrition.”

Pip cheeped happily. “I like seeds,” Pip added, pecking a sunflower seed. “Cheep!”

Benny looked around. He saw wooden crates, soft rope, and a short ladder. He tested the ladder with a paw. “Safe and steady,” he said. He wrapped the rope around the ladder and tied it to the railing. “Planning keeps me safe.”

He placed a crate at the bottom, so the ladder would not wobble. He took a deep breath. In and out. The wind brushed his fur and smelled like basil. “I can do this,” he told himself.

Benny climbed one step. Step. Step. Step. The tank gleamed in the sun. “Hello, tank,” Benny said kindly. “We will help the plants together.” He reached the top and felt the warm metal. His paws tingled. He tied the rope to a handle on the tank, just in case.

Pip watched with round eyes. “Cheep, cheep! You are brave!”

Benny smiled. “I feel a little scared,” he said. “But I am careful and I keep going.” He gently lifted the hose. Twist. Twist. It was tight. “Hmm,” he hummed. “I need a tool.” He took out a smooth stick from his belt and slid it under the knot. Wiggle, wiggle. The knot loosened. Swish! The hose relaxed like a sleepy snake.

“Great thinking!” Pip cheered.

Benny climbed down, slow and safe. Step. Step. Step. His paws touched the roof. “Now water,” he said. He turned the handle. The tank whooshed softly. Whoosh, whoosh. Cool water slid through the hose and sprinkled the soil. Spritz, spritz. The plants lifted their leaves, like they were smiling.

Benny walked to each box. “Here you go, little tomatoes. Here you go, mint. Here you go, beans.” The water made tiny rainbows in the sun. A ladybug crawled on Benny’s paw. It felt like tiny footprints. He giggled.

Pip hopped from box to box. “Cheep! The garden is happy. You were brave.”

Benny nodded. “I was brave because I felt scared and still tried. I used my brain to make a plan. I took care of nature. That is conservation. I ate healthy food to stay strong. That is good nutrition. And I asked, ‘What is the problem? What is the solution?’ That is critical thinking.”

They sat in the shade of a small lemon tree. The leaves smelled bright and clean. Benny sipped water. Pip sang a soft song. The wind was gentle now, and the tank was safe and quiet.

Benny wrote in his notebook: “Today I was brave. I planned. I helped.” He drew a picture of the garden with happy plants and a straight hose. He drew a small star next to it.

Pip pecked the page, making a tiny dot. “Cheep! A star for you.”

Benny smiled. “A star for us,” he said. “We did it together.”

That evening, the sun went pink and gold. The rooftop glowed. The garden looked calm and green. Benny packed his tools and waved. “See you tomorrow, plants,” he said. “I will keep helping.”

Pip fluttered up and did a loop in the air. “Cheep, cheep! Brave and kind, that’s you!”

Benny’s heart felt warm and big. He knew he could face new problems with plans, courage, and care.

The lesson: Being brave means trying even when you feel a little scared. Think, plan, eat well, and help nature. You can do it, too.

The End.
