# Ellie’s Cozy Nursery Parade

In a comfortable nursery with soft rugs and twinkly string lights, a gentle elephant calf named Ellie swayed her little tail. The room smelled like clean blankets and warm milk. A soft lullaby hummed from a small music box. Toys from many places sat on the shelves—bright blocks, a wooden drum, a fluffy llama, and a shiny spinning top.

Ellie loved the nursery. She loved the cushy beanbag, the big window, and the colorful books. She especially loved her friends—Tiko the tiger cub, Mimi the monkey, and Pippa the penguin. They all looked different. They all sounded different. They all liked different games. That made Ellie’s heart feel big and happy.

“Let’s have a parade!” Mimi clapped, her tiny hands making a quick pat-pat sound.

“I can drum,” Tiko purred, tapping the wooden drum. Thump-thump, thump-thump.

“I can waddle and sing,” Pippa said, her voice soft and cool like a snow breeze. “La-la-la.”

Ellie flapped her ears. “I can blow my trunk like a horn!” She tried a toot. It came out small and squeaky. Peeep!

Mimi giggled kindly. “Practice, Ellie! Practice makes strong.”

Ellie nodded. “I will practice.” She took a deep breath. The nursery smelled like fresh soap. She tried again. Peeeeeep! The sound bumped into the mobile above the crib and made the stars jingle.

“That was better,” Tiko said. “We can help each other.”

They lined up. Tiko first with his drum. Mimi next with ribbons to twirl. Pippa waddled with a tiny bell. Ellie was last with her trunk horn.

“Parade!” Mimi cheered.

Thump-thump. Swish-swish. Ting-ting. Peeep.

They marched past the rocking chair. Thump-thump. Swish-swish. Ting-ting. Peeep.

They marched past the bookshelf. Thump-thump. Swish-swish. Ting-ting. Peeep.

Then they reached a tricky spot. A block tower stood tall and wobbly near the doorway. A basket of crayons sat right beside it. The path was narrow. The friends stopped.

“My feet are slidey,” Pippa whispered. “I don’t want to knock the blocks.”

“My tail can bump things,” Tiko said, tucking it in.

“My ribbons might tickle your nose,” Mimi added, holding them close.

Ellie looked at the tower. The blocks were all different. Some were tiny. Some were long. They were red, blue, green, and yellow. They were all special. They were all part of the tower.

Ellie’s chest felt warm. “We can do this together,” she said. “We are different, and that helps us. I can use my trunk to hold the basket out of the way.”

She reached out carefully. Her trunk felt the smooth basket handle. She lifted it. The crayons smelled waxy and sweet. “I will go first and make a safe path,” Ellie said.

Tiko smiled. “I will step slow and keep the beat.”

Mimi nodded. “I will twirl tiny, not big.”

Pippa took a brave breath. “I will waddle steady.”

Ellie took one step. The rug brushed her feet. Another step. She moved the basket. Another step. Tiko followed with quiet thumps. Mimi tiptoed. Pippa waddled, bell tinkling like frost on glass.

Almost clear! Ellie tried a proud horn. Peeeee—oops! Her trunk bumped the very top block. The block slid. Tiko reached out with his paw and steadied it. The drum gave a soft boop.

“You saved it!” Ellie gasped.

“We all saved it,” Tiko said. “We helped together.”

Ellie’s ears warmed. “Thank you. I will practice my horn after we pass the tower.”

They reached the open space. Everyone cheered. “Parade!” Mimi twirled a happy rainbow. Pippa shook her bell. Ting-ting!

Now Ellie took her time. She breathed in. She breathed out. “I can practice. I can be gentle.” She felt the air in her trunk. She felt the floor under her feet. She tooted once, soft as a butterfly. Toot. She tooted again, strong and round. Toooooot!

“It sounds like sunshine!” Pippa said, eyes bright.

Ellie smiled. “We did it. We all moved in our own way. We kept each other safe.”

The friends finished their parade around the nursery. They passed the soft blanket corner that smelled like lavender. They passed the window where the afternoon light made golden squares on the floor. They passed the shelf where the llama watched with a stitched-on smile.

After the parade, they cleaned up. Ellie used her trunk to put crayons back. Tiko stacked blocks by size. Mimi rolled up ribbons. Pippa wiped a tiny drip of water near the sippy cups. Helping felt good, like a warm hug.

Mimi said, “We are all different. Look—stripy, fluffy, feathery, and wrinkly!”

Tiko added, “Different is great for teams.”

Pippa nodded. “We learn from each other.”

Ellie wrapped her trunk around her friends in a gentle hug. “Our differences make us strong. When we help and practice, we can do big things. And we take care of our things and our bodies.”

They drank cool water from cups. They munched crunchy apple slices. Chomp, chomp. Healthy snacks made their tummies happy and calm.

Ellie gave one last horn. Toooooot! It sounded just right.

The friends smiled. The nursery felt safe, bright, and full of love.

The End.
