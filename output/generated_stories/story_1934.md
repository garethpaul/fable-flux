# Benny Beaver’s Guest Room Games

Benny was a busy beaver with bright eyes and a warm smile. One sunny afternoon, Benny tip-tapped down the hall with his flat tail swish-swish behind him. He opened a door and peeked into the guest room. It smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. Sunlight made soft squares on the carpet. The room felt quiet and cozy.

“Today,” Benny said, “I will play sports inside, safe and gentle. I will share. I will help.” His whiskers twitched with a happy wiggle.

Benny set a soft foam ball on the rug. He lined up little paper cups like tiny goals. He put a jump rope in a neat circle. He wrote a big sign with crayons: Guest Room Games. The letters were wobbly, but bright.

Knock, knock!

His friends came to play. Tilly Turtle peeked around the door. “Hello,” Tilly said slowly. “I like games.”

Pip Pup bounced in, ears flopping. “Woof! I’m ready!”

Mina Mouse squeaked, “I brought snacks. Apple slices!” The apple smelled sweet and crunchy.

Benny clapped. “Welcome to the Guest Room Games! We will be kind. We will be fair. We will have fun!”

They started with Soft-Ball Kick. “The rules are gentle,” Benny said. “Kick softly. Aim for the cup goals. One kick each turn. We cheer for everyone.”

Pip Pup wagged his tail. “I love rules. They help us have fun.”

Benny nodded. “Rules are like a map. They help us go the right way.”

Tilly went first. Tap. The ball rolled, shhh, shhh, and kissed the cup. “Goal,” said Tilly, smiling.

Pip kicked next. His kick was big. Whoosh! The ball zoomed past the cups and bumped the bed. Thump!

Benny held up a paw. “Let’s try again more gently,” he said kindly. “We keep the room safe.”

Pip took a deep breath. “I can do a soft kick.” He tried again. Tap. The ball rolled and stopped at a cup. “Goal!” Everyone cheered, “Hooray!” Their voices filled the cozy room.

Mina’s turn was next. She aimed with her tiny paw. Tap. The ball slid to the middle. Not a goal, but close. Mina smiled anyway. “I did my best.”

Benny felt proud. “We try. We learn. We cheer.”

After three rounds, Benny said, “Let’s count the goals.” He pointed to each cup. “One, two, three, four.” He clapped. “We all did it!”

Now it was time for the Jump Rope Wiggle. Benny laid the rope in a long line. “We will hop over the rope. One at a time. We look, we think, we hop.”

Tilly looked at the rope. “It is long,” she said softly.

Benny patted her shell. “Let’s think. We can make the rope shorter.” He folded it in half. “Now it is just right.” Tilly smiled and hop-plopped over, thump-thump, safe and slow.

Pip hopped high, ears bouncing boing-boing. Mina hopped tiny hops, tip-tip. Benny hopped with a giggle. His tail thumped the rug, thap-thap.

Just then, a tiny voice squeaked from the hallway. “Wait for me!” It was Nibbles Squirrel, holding a bandage box. “I want to help. I can be the helper for safety.”

Benny nodded. “That is good community service. You help us stay safe.” Nibbles put the box on the dresser. He watched with kind eyes.

For the last game, Benny made Fair Share Relay. He lined up apples and water cups on the dresser. “We will pass one apple and one cup to each friend. No pushing. No rushing. We wait our turn. That is justice—fair for everyone.”

Pip blinked. “Justice means fair?”

“Yes,” said Benny. “Fair means everyone gets a turn and the same chance. That is just.”

They made a line. Benny passed an apple to Tilly. Tilly passed to Mina. Mina passed to Pip. Pip passed to Nibbles. Nibbles passed back to Benny. The apples smelled sweet, and the water tasted cool. The cups clinked softly. Everyone got a turn. Everyone got a snack.

Suddenly, the soft ball rolled under the guest bed. It was dark and dusty below. Pip frowned. “Oh no. The ball is stuck.”

Benny rubbed his chin. “Let’s use logical thinking. We can plan.” He looked around. “The jump rope is long. The paper cups can make a little scoop.”

Together, they tied a cup to the rope. Nibbles slid the rope gently under the bed. “I feel the ball,” he said. “Pull slow.”

“Pull, pull, pull,” they sang softly. The rope moved. The cup tapped the ball. Tink. The ball rolled out with a plop!

“Hooray!” cheered everyone. They had solved the problem together. Benny’s heart felt warm and full.

Benny looked around the guest room. It was still neat and safe. The sun made golden lines on the rug. The room smelled like apples and lemon soap. He smiled. “We played sports. We used our brains. We helped each other. We were fair.”

Tilly nodded. “I felt brave.”

Mina squeaked. “I felt kind.”

Pip wagged. “I felt strong.”

Nibbles grinned. “I felt helpful.”

Benny wrapped his tail around his friends in a gentle hug. “Teamwork makes big jobs small, and fair play makes games fun.”

They waved goodbye and tip-tapped out of the guest room. Benny turned off the light and whispered, “Thank you, Guest Room Games.”

The clear lesson was bright in Benny’s mind: Play fair, think first, and help others. That makes every day a win.

The End.
