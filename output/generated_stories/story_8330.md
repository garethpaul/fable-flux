# Leo's Colorful Discovery

Leo was a brave little lion cub with a fluffy golden mane and bright curious eyes. One sunny morning, he wandered into Grandpa's old garden shed. The wooden door creaked as it opened, and sunlight streamed through the dusty windows.

"Wow!" Leo gasped. The shed was full of wonderful things! Shiny paint cans sat on high shelves, and colorful brushes hung from hooks. In the corner, Leo spotted a big box overflowing with crayons, markers, and paper.

"These are mine now!" Leo said excitedly, hugging the box tight. "I can make the most beautiful pictures ever!"

Leo spread all the art supplies around him in a big circle. He had red crayons, blue markers, yellow paint, and purple pencils. The sweet smell of new crayons filled the air, and Leo's paws tingled with excitement.

Just then, he heard a tiny voice. "Excuse me," squeaked Rosie the rabbit, peeking through the door. "Could I please use a red crayon? I want to draw a strawberry for my mama."

"No!" Leo roared, though not very loudly since he was still small. "These are MY art supplies! Find your own!"

Rosie's ears drooped, and she hopped away sadly.

A few minutes later, Benny the bear cub knocked on the door. "Hi Leo! Could I borrow some blue paint? I want to paint the sky in my picture."

"Absolutely not!" Leo huffed, turning his back. "I need ALL the blue paint for myself!"

Benny's shoulders sagged as he shuffled away.

Leo tried to start drawing, but something felt wrong. His tummy felt heavy and twisted. The bright colors didn't seem as pretty anymore. The shed felt quiet and lonely, even with all his wonderful supplies.

He picked up a red crayon, but all he could think about was Rosie's sad face. He touched the blue paint, but remembered how disappointed Benny looked.

"Maybe," Leo whispered to himself, "having all these supplies isn't as fun when I'm all alone."

Leo took a deep breath and felt his heart grow calm. He realized something important: sharing might make him feel better than keeping everything to himself.

"Rosie! Benny!" Leo called out the door. "Come back! I want to share!"

Soon, the shed was filled with happy chatter. Rosie drew beautiful red strawberries while Leo helped her color green leaves. Benny painted a bright blue sky while Leo added fluffy white clouds.

"Thank you for sharing, Leo!" said Rosie, her nose twitching with joy.

"You're the best friend ever!" added Benny, giving Leo a gentle bear hug.

Leo felt warm and proud inside. "This is so much better!" he laughed. "When we share, we can make even more beautiful art together!"

The three friends spent the whole morning creating wonderful pictures. They painted rainbows, drew funny animals, and made handprint flowers. The shed smelled like crayons and friendship, and it echoed with happy giggles.

As the sun climbed higher, Leo looked around at all their colorful creations covering the shed walls. He felt proud and happy - not because he had kept everything for himself, but because he had learned to share.

"Sharing makes everything more fun," Leo said with a big smile. "And it makes my heart feel as bright as all these colors!"

From that day on, Leo always remembered that the best adventures happen when friends share together.

The End.
