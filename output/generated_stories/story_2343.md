# Ollie the Otter’s Rooftop Rescue

Every morning, a playful otter named Ollie climbed the stairs to the rooftop. He lived with his family in a cozy home by the sea. The rooftop was Ollie’s favorite place. The air was fresh and salty. The sun made the puddles warm on his paws. The wind made his whiskers wiggle.

“Rooftop time!” Ollie sang. He wore his little blue cap and a tiny backpack. Inside were soft cloths, a small brush, and a bright red ribbon. Rooftop time was part of Ollie’s routine. He watered the rooftop garden. He checked the wind chimes. He waved at the gulls.

On this day, the sky was very blue. The clouds looked like fluffy pillows. Ollie stepped onto the rooftop with a hop and a splash. “Splish-splash!” His paws patted a shiny puddle. He sniffed the air. It smelled like clean rain and salty waves.

“Good morning, plants!” Ollie said. He gave the green vines a gentle drink. “Sip, sip.” The leaves sparkled. A tiny ladybug crawled by. “Hello, little dot,” Ollie whispered. He smiled.

Then Ollie heard a small sound. “Cheep… cheep…” It came again, thin and shaky. “Cheep, cheep!”

Ollie froze. His ears perked. “Who is calling?” he asked softly.

He followed the sound to the corner of the rooftop. There, behind a flower pot, a little sparrow sat. Its wing was bent. Its tiny eyes were shiny and scared. The sparrow trembled.

“Oh,” Ollie breathed. His heart felt warm and tender. “You are hurt.”

“Cheep,” said the sparrow. It tried to hop, but it wobbled. Its feathers were ruffled. The wind puffed, and the sparrow tucked in tight.

Ollie knelt low. He made his voice gentle, like a soft blanket. “It’s okay. I will help.”

His paws tingled. He felt a little worried. What if he made it worse? He took a slow breath. He remembered what Mama Otter always said: “Compassion means caring for others with kind hands and a calm heart.”

Ollie nodded. “Kind hands. Calm heart.” He set his backpack down. “I will not leave you.”

He looked around. The rooftop had a small bench. A watering can. A coil of string. Wind chimes sang, “Ting-ting-ting.” He needed teamwork. He needed help.

Ollie cupped his paws and called to the sky, “Gull friends! I need a friend!”

Two gulls swooped down, wings white and wide. “Skree!” they cried, landing softly. One was Gus, who liked to laugh. The other was Gilly, who was calm and wise.

“Hello, Ollie,” said Gilly. “What do you need?”

Ollie pointed. “The sparrow is hurt. I want to help. Will you help me too?”

“Yes,” said Gus. “We can make a cozy nest spot.”

Teamwork began. Gilly blocked the wind with her wings. Whoosh turned to hush. Gus found soft leaves from the garden box. Ollie opened his backpack and took out the soft cloths. He laid them in a shallow box he found under the bench.

“Soft and snug,” Ollie said. He moved slowly. “Hello, little bird. I will lift you very gently.” He scooped the sparrow with two careful paws. “You are safe.”

The sparrow cheeped once, then grew quiet. Ollie’s touch was warm and steady. He placed the bird on the cloths. He tucked a leaf by its side. The rooftop felt peaceful, like a kind hug.

“Your wing needs rest,” Gilly murmured.

“I will bring water,” Gus said, flapping to a small cap of fresh rainwater. He set it by the bird.

Ollie brushed a feather from the sparrow’s eye with his tiny brush. “There,” he whispered. “You are not alone. We are your team.”

Time passed softly. The sun made a golden path. The wind chimes sang a lullaby. Ollie sat beside the box. He hummed. He told the sparrow stories about the shiny sea and the jumping fish. He told the sparrow about his routine: water the plants, check the chimes, and always be kind.

The sparrow blinked and relaxed. “Cheep,” it said, soft and hopeful.

Mama Otter peeked up the stairs. “Ollie? Rooftop helper, how is it going?”

Ollie looked up. “Mama, I found a hurt friend. We are helping.”

Mama smiled. “That is compassion. You saw the need. You used kind hands. You asked for help. That is good teamwork.”

Ollie’s whiskers twitched with pride. “We can take turns watching,” he said. “Gus can bring water. Gilly can block the wind. I can keep the nest clean.”

All afternoon, they worked together. The rooftop smelled like warm leaves and fresh air. The sparrow sipped tiny drops. Its breathing grew steady. Its eyes grew bright.

As the sun grew soft and pink, the sparrow stood, wobbly but brave. It lifted its wing and flapped—once, twice. “Cheep-cheep!” it sang, strong and sweet.

Gilly stepped back. Gus cheered, “Skree!”

Ollie opened his paws. “If you are ready, you can fly.”

The sparrow hopped to the edge, looked at the sky, and leaped. Whoosh! It flew in a small circle, then a big one, then it landed on the rooftop fence for one more look. “Cheep!” It sounded like “Thank you.” Then it soared away toward the golden light.

Ollie felt his heart glow. The rooftop felt even more special. It was not just a place to play. It was a place to care.

Mama Otter hugged him. “What did you learn today, Ollie?”

Ollie smiled. “Compassion means helping with kind hands and a calm heart. When we help together, small friends can do big things.”

He put the red ribbon on the box and tied a bow. “This will be our Rooftop Care Spot,” he said. “Every day, we can check. We can be ready to help.”

The wind chimes sang. The vines rustled. The sky turned purple and soft. Ollie waved at the place where the sparrow had flown.

“Good night, rooftop,” he whispered. “Good night, friends. Tomorrow, we will care again.”

The End.
