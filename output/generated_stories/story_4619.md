# Fawn Fixes a Little Wobble

In a sunny backyard, there was a cozy garage workshop. It smelled like pine wood and warm rubber. The floor felt cool and smooth. Tiny dust sparkles danced in the light. Tools hung on the wall like silver smiles.

A gentle deer fawn named Fawn peeked inside. Fawn’s coat was soft and speckled, and Fawn’s ears twitched at every tiny sound. “Hello?” said Fawn in a small voice. “I want to help. I can be careful. I can be kind.”

Clunk. Clink. Tap-tap. The tools answered with little songs.

Mr. Owl, with round glasses, turned from a workbench. “Welcome, Fawn,” he hooted softly. “Today we have a tiny task. We will fix a wobbly wagon.”

Fawn looked at the red wagon. It wiggled and wobbled. The squeaky wheel said, “Eee-ee!” Fawn’s heart thump-thump-thumped. “I want to try,” Fawn said. “But I feel small.”

Mr. Owl nodded. “Feeling small is okay. You are brave for trying.” He pointed to a simple picture chart on the wall. “Step one: Look. Step two: Ask. Step three: Try. Step four: Check. Step five: Tell the story.”

Fawn took a deep breath. It smelled like oranges and soap. “Step one: Look,” Fawn whispered. Fawn knelt close to the wheel. It felt loose and shaky. “Hmm.”

“Step two: Ask,” said Mr. Owl.

Fawn lifted a hoof. “Mr. Owl, may I use the small blue wrench? The big one is too heavy for me.”

“That is good asking,” Mr. Owl said. He gave Fawn the small blue wrench. It felt cool and just right in Fawn’s hoof. Fawn smiled. The smile felt warm inside, like sunshine in a pocket.

“Step three: Try,” Fawn said. Fawn turned the tiny nut. Twist, twist. The wrench made a soft squeak. “Eee-ee,” sang the wheel again, but a little less.

Fawn stopped. “It is still wobbly,” Fawn said, ears drooping.

“You are doing fine,” said Mr. Owl. “Try one more gentle turn.”

Twist. Twist. Fawn’s legs were steady now. The wagon wheel stood straighter. Fawn touched it. It felt firm.

“Step four: Check,” said Mr. Owl.

Fawn pulled the wagon a little. Roll, roll, roll. The wheel sang a new song. “Shhh-rush, shhh-rush.” It was smooth and soft.

“I did it!” Fawn beamed. Fawn’s chest felt big and proud, like a balloon that did not pop.

Just then, Squirrel zoomed in. “I can pull the wagon so fast!” Squirrel grabbed the handle and tugged. Zip! The wagon bumped a small pile of nuts. Clatter! Nuts rolled everywhere.

Fawn blinked. “Oh no,” said Fawn. “The nuts!”

Squirrel froze. “I’m sorry,” Squirrel squeaked. “I did not look. I moved too fast.”

Fawn felt a tiny pinch inside. But then Fawn took a slow breath. “I forgive you,” Fawn said. “We can fix it together.”

Mr. Owl smiled. “That is kind. And wise.”

Fawn looked at the chart again. “Step one: Look.” Fawn looked at the nuts on the floor. Some went under the table. “Step two: Ask.” Fawn said, “Mr. Owl, may we use the small broom and the short dustpan? The big broom is too tall for me.”

“That is good asking,” Mr. Owl said. He handed over the small broom. It felt light and friendly.

“Step three: Try,” Fawn said. Fawn swept. Swish. Swish. The broom made a soft whisking sound. Squirrel helped. Pat, pat, pat went little paws, lining up nuts.

“Step four: Check.” Fawn looked around. The floor was clean. The wagon was safe. The nuts were in a neat basket. It felt calm again in the workshop.

Squirrel held the wagon handle gently. “Can we test it slowly this time?” Squirrel asked.

“Yes,” said Fawn. Fawn’s voice was clear and strong now. “Slow and safe.”

Together they rolled the wagon. Roll, roll. The wheel hummed happily. Fawn’s hooves clicked softly on the smooth floor. Mr. Owl clapped his wings. “Well done, team!”

Fawn stood tall. “I did a task. I asked for what I needed. I fixed a wobble. I cleaned a spill. I can do hard things.” The words felt good to say. They were like a soft blanket for Fawn’s heart.

“Step five: Tell the story,” said Mr. Owl.

Fawn nodded. “Today I felt small. I asked for the small blue wrench. I tried gentle turns. I checked the wheel. I fixed the wagon. When the nuts fell, I took a breath. I forgave Squirrel. I asked for the small broom. We cleaned up. We went slow and safe. I am proud of me.”

Mr. Owl blinked kindly. “That is a very good story.”

Squirrel bounced and giggled. “Fawn, you are brave. You teach me to slow down.”

Fawn’s ears wiggled with joy. The workshop felt like a happy song. It smelled like pine wood and oranges and a little bit of nutty goodness. The tools on the wall looked like silver smiles again.

Fawn smiled at the wagon. “I can do it,” Fawn said. “I can ask. I can try. I can finish a task. I can be kind to friends and to me.”

And the wagon whispered, “Shhh-rush,” as if it agreed.

The End.
