# The Cozy Closet of Calm

In a small, sunny house, there was a cozy closet hideaway with soft blankets, twinkly stickers on the walls, and a tiny lamp that glowed like a firefly. This was Fawn’s favorite spot. Fawn was a gentle deer fawn with big ears, bright eyes, and wobbly legs that liked to hop and stretch.

One morning, Fawn practiced quiet jumping in the hallway. “Boing, boing!” Fawn giggled, landing on the rug. The rug felt fuzzy on tiny hooves. The air smelled like warm toast. Fawn’s heart felt light and bouncy.

Then a loud shout came from the kitchen. “Hey! That’s my apple!” cried Robin Raccoon.

“It rolled to me,” said Benny Bear. “I’m hungry.”

Fawn’s smile fell. The air felt heavy. The house did not feel sunny anymore. Fawn’s tummy made a knot. “I feel… funny,” Fawn whispered. “My chest is tight. My ears feel hot.” The feelings were big.

Fawn trotted to the closet hideaway. The blankets were soft and cool. The lamp glowed gentle yellow. The stickers sparkled like tiny stars. Fawn curled up and took a breath in… and out. In… and out.

“I feel worried,” Fawn said out loud. “My tummy is tight. That means worried.” Naming the feeling helped a little. Fawn put a hoof on the chest and counted. “One… two… three… four.” The breath was slow, like a snail. Fawn stretched legs long like a tree. “I can calm my body.”

A soft tap came at the door. “Fawn? Can we come in?” asked Robin.

“Only if it’s gentle,” Fawn said.

Robin and Benny peeked in. Robin’s masky eyes were shiny with tears. Benny’s ears drooped. “We are fighting,” Robin sniffled. “I had an apple. It rolled. Benny grabbed it.”

“I thought it was my turn,” Benny said. “I was so hungry. My tummy growled.”

Fawn listened. The closet felt safe and warm. The gentle light made their faces look kind. Fawn put a hoof on the blanket. “Let’s sit. We can use our feeling words.”

“My chest feels hot,” said Robin. “That means mad.”

“My belly rumbles,” said Benny. “That means hungry and grumpy.”

Fawn nodded. “I felt worried. I came here to calm. We can all calm.” Fawn showed how to breathe like blowing bubbles. They all puffed cheeks and blew slow. “Pfffff…”

Robin sniffed and smiled a little. Benny’s shoulders dropped. The air felt softer again.

“Now,” Fawn said, “we can talk about what is fair. Fair means everyone gets what they need. Justice is fair.”

Robin blinked. “I need a snack.”

“I need a snack too,” said Benny. “But I grabbed.”

Fawn thought. “We can share and take turns. That is fair. And we can say sorry. That is kind.”

Robin ducked head. “I’m sorry I yelled.”

Benny nodded. “I’m sorry I grabbed. I did not ask.”

Fawn’s ears perked. “Thank you for your brave words.”

They all stood and did a tiny exercise to shake out the mad and grumpy. “Shake, shake, shake,” sang Fawn, wiggling legs. They stretched to the stickers and touched their toes. They marched in place, tap-tap-tap. It felt silly and good. Their bodies felt calm and strong.

“Let’s make a plan,” Fawn said. “One apple, two friends. We can cut it. We can also add crunchy carrots. I know where the little knife is, and a grown-up can help.”

Robin and Benny looked at each other. “Yes,” they said together.

They went to the kitchen. The room smelled like apples and soap. A grown-up helped slice the apple into even pieces. The slices were shiny and sweet. Crunch! went the first bite. Crunch! went the second. Benny sighed, happy. Robin hummed.

“Thank you, Fawn,” said Robin. “You helped us use words.”

“You helped us be fair,” said Benny. “And the closet helped us calm.”

Fawn felt warm and proud. “When feelings are big, we can notice them. We can breathe. We can move our bodies. We can use kind words. We can make a fair plan.”

Later, they went back to the closet hideaway to play a gentle game. They made a tiny “Feelings Chart” with stickers.

- Star sticker for calm.
- Cloud sticker for sad.
- Flame sticker for mad.
- Pebble sticker for worried.
- Sun sticker for proud.

Each friend chose a sticker and said a word. “I feel sun,” said Fawn. “Proud and warm.” “I feel star,” said Benny. “Calm.” “I feel star-sun,” said Robin. Everyone laughed.

“Can we play jump-and-freeze?” asked Benny.

“Yes,” said Fawn, “but gentle jumps.” They hopped softly on the rug: boing, freeze! Boing, freeze! They listened to their bodies. If a feeling got big, they paused and breathed like blowing bubbles.

When it was time to rest, they snuggled into the blankets. The lamp glowed. The stickers sparkled. The closet hideaway felt like a tiny sky.

Fawn whispered, “Today we learned: Feelings are okay. We can name them. We can calm our bodies. We can be fair and take turns. We can be kind, even when we are different.”

Robin nodded. “That is justice,” they said softly.

Benny smiled. “And that is friendship.”

The cozy closet was quiet and safe. Their hearts felt light again.

The End.
