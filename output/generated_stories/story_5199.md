# Panda Poppy and the Cozy Closet Cave

Poppy was a patient panda with soft black-and-white fur and big curious eyes. She loved her closet hideaway. It was warm and quiet, with a small lamp that glowed like a firefly. The closet smelled like clean shirts and a hint of sweet lavender. Poppy called it her Cozy Closet Cave.

Inside the closet, Poppy kept a basket of treasures. There were wooden blocks, soft scarves, tiny bells, and a bright red ball. She liked to build, stack, and listen to the little bells ring. Poppy also liked to breathe slow and count when she felt big feelings.

One morning, Poppy wanted to build a tall block tower. “Up, up, up,” she hummed, stacking the blocks with careful paws. Tap, tap, tap. The blocks made a tiny clack as they touched. Her red ball sat nearby, shiny and round like a cherry.

Just then, her friend Tiko the tiny tiger cub peeked in. “Can I come in?” he asked. His whiskers twitched with curiosity.

“Yes,” said Poppy. “We can play in my Cozy Closet Cave. Please be gentle. The tower is wobbly.”

“I can be gentle,” said Tiko. He tiptoed in. Tip, tip, tip. He sniffed. “It smells nice in here.”

Poppy smiled. “Let’s build together. We can take turns. That’s fair.”

They took turns. Poppy placed a square block. Tiko placed a triangle block. The tower grew taller. It wiggled a little. Poppy steadied it with her paw. “Slow and steady,” she said. “We use good coordination.”

“What is coree… coor…?” Tiko asked.

“Coordination,” Poppy said softly. “It means our eyes and paws work together. We move carefully.”

Tiko nodded and moved slowly. They both breathed in and out. The little lamp glowed, and the tiny bells chimed when Poppy brushed them. Ding-a-ling.

Then Tiko saw the bright red ball. His eyes got wide. “Ball!” he squeaked. He batted it with his paw. Boing!

The ball bounced—boing, boing—right into the tower. Crash! The blocks tumbled down in a noisy clatter. The tower was gone.

Poppy’s chest felt hot. Her face felt tight. Her paws curled. She wanted to shout, “No!” Her tummy felt stormy, like thunder in a tiny sky.

Tiko’s ears drooped. “Uh-oh,” he whispered. “I am sorry.”

Poppy closed her eyes. She remembered her steps for big feelings. “Stop. Breathe. Choose,” she told herself in a small voice.

She put a paw on her belly. In. Out. In. Out. She counted softly, “One… two… three.” The storm grew smaller. Her chest felt calmer.

Poppy opened her eyes. “I feel mad,” she said slowly. “I worked hard. The tower fell.”

Tiko blinked. “I was curious about the ball,” he said. “I forgot to be gentle.”

Poppy nodded. “Thank you for telling me. Curiosity is good. But we must be fair. We take turns and we keep the tower safe.”

Tiko took another breath. “How can I help?”

Poppy looked at the blocks. “Let’s fix it together. We can use good coordination. And we can make a ball rule.”

“A ball rule?” Tiko asked.

“Yes,” said Poppy. “In the Cozy Closet Cave, balls rest while we build. Then we roll them outside. That is fair for both of us.”

Tiko smiled. “Okay. Balls rest while we build.”

Poppy smiled back. “Good plan.”

They began again. Poppy held a block with two paws. Tiko watched her paws and placed a block on top. Tap. They moved slow like turtles. They breathed in and out, in and out. The tower stood tall again, even taller than before.

“Wow,” said Tiko. “We did it!”

Poppy gently touched the tiny bells. Ding-a-ling. “We did it together,” she said. “Thank you for helping.”

Tiko picked up the red ball and whispered, “Rest now.” He set it in the basket. Then he whispered to Poppy, “After we build, can we roll the ball outside?”

“Yes,” said Poppy. “We can roll it down the hallway. We will take turns. You roll. I roll. That is fair.”

They placed the last block, a shiny blue one. The tower shone in the warm lamp light. It was steady and tall. Poppy’s heart felt light and soft.

Tiko clapped. “I like the Cozy Closet Cave,” he said. “It feels safe.”

“Me too,” said Poppy. “When I feel mad, I stop, breathe, and choose. That helps me use my kind voice.”

Tiko nodded. “I can do that too.”

They both took one more slow breath. In. Out. They smiled at their tower. Then they carefully crawled out of the closet and rolled the red ball down the hallway. It went smooth and straight. They took turns. They cheered for each other.

Back in the closet, Poppy rang the bells one more time. “Ding-a-ling,” she sang. “We used patience, coordination, curiosity, and fairness today.”

Tiko grinned. “And we had fun!”

Poppy giggled. “Yes. We had fun.”

The End.
