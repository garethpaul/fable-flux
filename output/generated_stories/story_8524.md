# Rooftop Flags and Brave Little No

High above the busy street, a courageous monkey named Momo sat on a sunny rooftop. The roof felt warm under Momo’s feet. The air smelled like fresh rain and warm bread from the bakery next door. A soft breeze tickled Momo’s ears. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” and a distant bus went “vroom.”

Today, Momo had a plan. “I will make rooftop flags,” Momo said. “Bright flags for everyone to see!” Momo’s eyes sparkled. Momo liked to make things and share.

Momo opened a little craft box. Inside were colorful cloth squares, string, safe child scissors, and a roll of friendly, sticky tape. “Snip, snip,” went the scissors. “Rip, rip,” went the tape. Momo hummed a happy tune. “La, la, flags today!”

Momo also loved to move. “First, I stretch,” Momo said. Momo reached arms up. “Stretch to the sun!” Momo touched toes. “Hello, toes!” Momo hopped side to side. “Hop-hop,” went Momo’s feet. Exercise made Momo feel strong and brave.

Just then, a neighbor pigeon landed with a flutter. “Coo! I am Pippa,” said the pigeon. “I like your red flag. Make only red ones. Red is best.”

Momo held the red flag. The cloth felt smooth and soft. Momo liked red, but also blue, yellow, and green. Momo took a slow breath. “Thank you, Pippa,” Momo said kindly. “I hear you. But I want many colors. I can choose. I say yes to colors.”

Pippa tilted her head. “Coo?” She blinked. “Okay,” she said, curious. “Show me.”

Momo smiled and snipped more flags. Red, blue, yellow, and green danced on the line. The breeze made them flap and whisper, “Flap-flap-flip!”

A friendly cat peeked over the roof wall. “Meow! I am Coco,” said the cat. “Let’s race to the other side, right now! Fast-fast! No more crafts.”

Momo liked to run, but Momo also wanted to finish the flags. Momo felt a little pull inside—like two ideas tugging. Momo put a hand on the heart and breathed in and out. “I can be brave and clear,” Momo whispered.

Momo looked at Coco with kind eyes. “Coco, running sounds fun. But not right now,” Momo said. “I want to finish my flags first. We can run after I am done.”

Coco’s whiskers twitched. “After?” Coco asked.

“Yes,” said Momo. “After flags, we run. That is my plan.”

Coco blinked, then nodded. “Okay, Momo,” Coco purred. “I can wait. Your voice is strong.”

Momo’s tail swished happily. Being clear felt good. It was not a shout. It was a brave little no, and a kind yes to the plan.

Soon a small turtle peeked from a sunny corner. “Hello,” the turtle said softly. “I am Tulla. I think only big monkeys can hang flags. You are small. Maybe you cannot.”

Momo’s tummy felt twisty for a moment. The wind brushed Momo’s cheeks like a soft hand. Momo stood tall. “Tulla,” Momo said gently, “I am small, but I am strong. I can try. I will ask for help if I need it.”

Tulla blinked slowly. “Oh,” she said. “I have short legs. But I can hold the string.”

“Thank you!” Momo said. “Help is welcome.”

Momo tied the string to a safe hook. Pippa held one end with her beak. Tulla pressed her wise little foot on the string. Coco counted, “Three, two, one!” Momo reached up high. “Stretch to the sun,” Momo said again, and clipped a flag. The clip went “click!” The flags rustled, “shhhh,” like tiny waves.

The line of colors grew. Red, blue, yellow, green. The breeze made them dance. Momo giggled. “They look like happy birds!” The friends laughed, too.

Coco bounced in place. “Now we run?”

“Almost,” Momo said. “One more flag.” This one was special. Momo crafted a rainbow heart with sparkly tape. The heart shone in the sunlight. “This is my brave heart flag,” Momo said. “It says, ‘My voice matters.’”

Momo climbed a little step stool. The roof felt safe and steady. “I will clip it now,” Momo said. “I can do it.” With calm hands and a strong breath, Momo clipped the heart. “Click!”

Cheers rose up. “Coo, beautiful!” said Pippa. “Meow, so bright!” said Coco. “Slow and strong,” said Tulla.

Momo beamed. “Thank you for watching and helping,” Momo said. “I was clear about my plan. I listened to you. I also listened to me.”

Coco stretched. “Now the run?”

“Yes!” Momo said. “Let’s do a rooftop race.” They set a soft scarf as the start line. Momo led a warm-up. “Arms up, swing-swing. Knees bend, bounce-bounce.” They ran from the chimney to the potted tree and back. Feet went “pat-pat-pat.” Laughter filled the bright air.

After the run, the friends lay on their backs, looking up. The sky was big and blue. The flags fluttered above them like colorful clouds. Momo felt calm and proud.

Pippa cooed, “You showed me many colors can be good.”

Tulla said, “You showed me small can be strong.”

Coco purred, “You said no and yes in a kind way. I liked that.”

Momo nodded. “Today I learned to be brave and clear. I can say what I need. I can listen to others, too. I can try new ideas and still be me.”

The friends smiled as the wind sang through the flags. The rooftop felt cozy and bright.

The End.
