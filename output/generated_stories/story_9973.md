# Kiri Koala’s Colorful Map

Kiri was a calm koala who loved her cozy study corner. It had a soft blue rug, a little lamp that glowed warm and yellow, and a low shelf with books that felt smooth under her paws. The window let in breezy air that smelled like fresh leaves. On her desk sat crayons, paper, and a big map with many bright paths.

Kiri took a deep breath. “Ahh,” she hummed. “Today is a map day.”

She opened a book with a shiny cover. It was about places far away. The pages made a soft swish-swish. Pictures showed children with different clothes, different foods, and different songs. Kiri’s ears twitched with joy.

Her friend Pip, a tiny parrot with green feathers like mint leaves, hopped onto the desk. “Good morning, Kiri!” chirped Pip. “What are we doing?”

“Drawing our world,” Kiri said in her calm, gentle voice. “I want to learn about friends from many places.”

Pip pecked the paper lightly. Tap-tap. “Ooh! Let’s draw outside too!”

Kiri smiled. “Yes. We can use our eyes and our hearts.”

They put on little sun hats. Kiri’s hat had a red ribbon. Pip’s hat had a tiny blue star. They walked through the door to the sunny yard. The grass felt cool and tickly on Kiri’s feet. Birds sang sweet tweet-tweets. A light breeze smelled like oranges from the tree.

Under the shade, three neighbors waved hello. Mina wore a bright scarf with purple swirls. Jun had a cap with tiny rockets. Tula wore jingling bracelets that went chime-chime.

“Hi, Kiri! Hi, Pip!” they called.

Kiri felt warm inside. “Hello, friends,” she said. “I am making a map of our big, colorful world. Can you help?”

Mina smiled. “I can show you how my family says hello. We say, ‘Salam.’” Her voice was soft and kind.

Kiri repeated, “Sa-lam.” The word felt smooth and gentle on her tongue.

Jun laughed. “My grandma says, ‘Annyeong!’ Like this.” His voice bounced like a happy ball.

“An-nyeong,” Kiri tried. She grinned. “It sounds bright and bouncy.”

Tula clapped. “We say, ‘Namaste,’ with hands like this.” She pressed her hands together and bowed a little. Her bracelets chimed.

Kiri copied her. “Na-ma-ste.” She felt peaceful, like the shade of a tree.

Pip fluttered. “So many hellos! My beak is busy!” He giggled.

Kiri drew three little speech bubbles on the paper. She wrote the words slowly. Salam. Annyeong. Namaste. The pencil made a soft scratch-scratch sound.

“Do you want to hear a song from my family?” Mina asked.

“Yes, please,” Kiri said.

Mina sang a sweet tune that rolled like a river. Jun tapped a gentle beat on his knees—tap tap tap. Tula shook a tiny bell—ring-a-ling. Pip whistled along—peep-peep!

Kiri closed her eyes for a moment. The sounds felt like warm sunlight on her fur. “Your music is beautiful,” she said.

Jun grinned. “We make dumplings at home. They smell like ginger.”

Tula added, “My mom makes flat bread. It is warm and soft.”

Mina said, “We drink mint tea. It is sweet and cool.”

Kiri’s tummy rumbled. “Mmm,” she murmured, smiling. “All different. All yummy.”

They went back to Kiri’s study corner. The cool shade hugged them. Kiri set out crayons. “Let’s draw a map of our friendship,” she said. “Each of us can add a path.”

Mina chose purple. “This path smells like mint tea,” she said, drawing curly lines.

Jun picked bright orange. “This path tastes like ginger dumplings,” he giggled, coloring zigzags.

Tula took gold. “This path jingles like my bracelets,” she chimed, making tiny dots that looked like stars.

Pip chose green. “This path sounds like my whistles,” he said, making swirls that loop-de-looped.

Kiri used calm blue. “This path feels like a hug,” she said, drawing a gentle river line around all the others.

When they finished, the map looked like a garden of colors. It had sweet smells, tasty memories, happy sounds, and soft hugs. Kiri’s heart felt big and light.

Just then, two more friends peeked in. Leo wore boots with mud spots. Aya had a scarf with little fish.

Leo shuffled. “Can we play?” he asked, shy.

Aya looked at the map. “We are new here,” she whispered.

Kiri patted the rug. “Come sit,” she said. “We are sharing our world.”

Leo smiled a tiny smile. “My family loves to stomp in puddles after rain. Splish-splash.”

Pip chirped, “Outdoor fun!”

Aya grinned. “We like to fly kites. They dance in the wind.”

Kiri’s eyes shone. “Let’s add puddles and kites.” She handed them crayons.

Leo drew blue puddles—plip-plop. Aya drew red kites with long tails—whoosh-whoosh.

They all looked at the map. It was now even more colorful and bright. It showed many hellos. It showed songs and foods. It showed games and the outside air. It showed friends sitting together in a cozy study corner.

Mina said softly, “We are different.”

Jun added, “We are the same too. We like to laugh.”

Tula chimed, “We like to share.”

Kiri nodded. “Different is special,” she said. “Different makes our world big.”

Pip flapped his wings. “And fun!” he squeaked.

Kiri drew a small heart in the corner of the map. “This is our promise,” she said. “We listen. We learn. We accept.”

Everyone placed a paw or hand or feather near the heart. They sat quietly for a moment. The lamp glowed warm. The room smelled like crayons and orange leaves. Outside, a soft breeze whispered through the trees.

Kiri took a calm breath. “Thank you for teaching me,” she said. “Today we used our eyes, ears, noses, and hearts. We made a map of friendship.”

All the friends smiled. Leo said, “Can we play outside after?”

Kiri giggled. “Yes. We can try kites and puddles. We can sing and dance. Then we can come back and add more to our map.”

They cheered together. The study corner felt like a tiny world where everyone belonged.

The End.
