# Miko's Special Guests

Miko the monkey swung happily through the trees outside his family's cozy home. His long brown tail helped him balance as he jumped from branch to branch. Today was special - three new animal friends were coming to stay in their guest room!

"Miko!" called his mama from below. "Our guests are here!"

Miko scrambled down the tree trunk, his little hands gripping the rough bark. He was so excited to meet new friends! But when he peeked into the guest room, he stopped and tilted his furry head.

There was Ellie the elephant, who was much bigger than anyone Miko had ever met. Her gray skin looked wrinkled and different. Next to her stood Spike the porcupine, covered in sharp quills that looked scary. In the corner sat Shelly the turtle, moving so slowly that Miko wondered if she was even awake.

"They're so... different," Miko whispered to himself, feeling a little nervous.

Mama Monkey noticed Miko hiding behind the doorway. "Come say hello, Miko. Remember, good hosts are kind to all their guests."

Miko took a deep breath and stepped forward. "Um... hi," he said quietly, staying far away from Spike's quills.

"Hello there!" trumpeted Ellie in a loud, happy voice. Her trunk swayed as she spoke. "Thank you for letting us stay!"

Miko jumped back. Ellie's voice was so loud! And her trunk looked so strange!

"Don't be scared," said Spike gently, noticing Miko's worried face. "I know my quills look sharp, but I'm very careful. I would never hurt a friend."

Shelly slowly turned her head toward Miko. "Hello, little monkey," she said in the softest, kindest voice. "You have such a beautiful home."

Miko felt bad for being scared. These animals seemed really nice, even though they looked different from him.

"I'm sorry," Miko said, his cheeks turning pink under his fur. "I was just... you all look so different from me. I didn't know what to think."

"That's okay," smiled Ellie. "We ARE different! But different can be wonderful. Watch this!" She used her long trunk to pick up Miko's favorite red ball from across the room. "My trunk helps me reach things that are far away!"

"Wow!" gasped Miko. "That's amazing!"

Spike carefully turned around to show his back. "My quills keep me safe from danger. But see how I can control them?" The quills lay flat against his back, looking much less scary.

Shelly slowly pulled her head into her shell, then popped back out. "My shell is my house! I carry it everywhere I go."

Miko's eyes grew wide with wonder. "You're all so cool! I wish I could do those things!"

"And I wish I could swing through trees like you!" laughed Ellie.

"Your tail is amazing!" added Spike.

"And you're so quick and nimble!" said Shelly admiringly.

Miko realized something important. Everyone was different, but that made them special, not scary.

"Would you like me to show you around?" Miko asked excitedly. "I can teach you about the trees, and maybe you can teach me about your special abilities!"

"We'd love that!" they all said together.

From that day on, Miko learned that being different makes everyone unique and wonderful. He discovered that when you treat others with kindness and respect, you can make the most amazing friends.

The End.
