# Squeaky the Squirrel Fixes the Mess

Squeaky the squirrel was small, quick, and very smart. He wore a tiny blue apron and a bright yellow hat. He loved helping in the garage workshop. The garage smelled like fresh wood and warm oil. Sunlight made dust sparkle in the air. Tools hung on the wall, shiny and neat.

One windy morning, Squeaky opened the big garage door. The door rolled up with a rumble, rumble. “Today I will build a birdhouse,” he said. His voice sounded brave and kind. “I can do it. I will do it the right way.”

He patted his pocket. “I have my plan. I have my list.” He felt proud.

But whoosh! A gust of wind came in. A box of nails tipped over. Tink, tink, tink! Nails rolled all over the floor. A stack of wood slid down, clack-clack. A tape measure sprang open—zip! The garage felt loud and messy. Squeaky’s heart beat fast.

“Oh no,” said Squeaky. His tail fluffed up. He wanted to race around and grab everything. His paws twitched.

Then he took a slow breath in. He let a slow breath out. “I can be calm,” he whispered. “I can be responsible.”

He put a paw on his chest. “Squeaky, we can fix this,” he told himself. “One small step.”

Squeaky looked at the nails. “First, I pick up the sharp things,” he said. He hummed a soft song. Hum, hum, hum. He picked up one nail. Then two. Then three. “One, two, three,” he counted. The cool metal felt smooth in his paws. He put the nails back in their box and closed the lid with a click.

Next, he looked at the wood. “I can stack the wood safely,” he said. He lifted the light boards and made a neat pile. He used both paws. The wood smelled sweet like trees. “Safe and tidy,” he said with a smile.

The tape measure lay across the floor. Squeaky gently pressed the button. Zip! It rolled back in. “You go home now,” he giggled. He hung it on its hook.

“Good job, Squeaky,” he told himself. “I am calm. I am careful. I am responsible.”

Just then, a tiny mouse peeped from behind a paint can. “Squeak?” said the mouse. Her whiskers trembled. “I am Mia. I got scared when things fell.”

Squeaky knelt. “Hi, Mia,” he said softly. “It got noisy. Are you okay?”

Mia nodded. “I hid. I am sorry I didn’t help.”

“It’s okay,” Squeaky said with a warm smile. “Sometimes we feel scared. We can be brave now. We can finish the job together.”

Mia’s eyes brightened. “How can I help?”

Squeaky pointed to the broom. “Can you sweep the sawdust into a little pile?” he asked. “We will make the floor safe.”

Mia held the tiny broom. Swish, swish, swish. “Like this?” she asked.

“Yes,” Squeaky said. “That is careful and kind.”

They worked as a team. Squeaky wiped up a small puddle of oil with a rag. The rag felt squishy and warm. The oil smelled strong, but Squeaky wrinkled his nose and kept working. He set out a bright orange cone. “Oil is slippery,” he said. “We take care to keep friends safe.”

Mia nodded. “Being responsible means thinking about others,” she said.

Squeaky grinned. “Yes! Responsible means we take care of our space, our tools, and our friends.”

Soon the garage was tidy again. The tools hung straight. The floor was clean. The sunbeam looked happy. Squeaky’s heart felt slow and steady.

“Now,” Squeaky said, “we can build the birdhouse.” He showed Mia the plan. “Step one: measure. Step two: mark. Step three: cut. Step four: sand. Step five: nail.”

Mia clapped. “Step by step,” she said.

They measured the wood. Click went the pencil as they marked a line. Squeaky held the small saw. “I will cut slowly,” he said. “Safety first.” Saw-dust snow fell soft and light. It smelled like pine. Squeaky wore tiny goggles. Mia held the board steady with gentle paws.

They sanded the edges. Scritch, scritch. The wood felt smooth as a shell. “Nice and safe for bird toes,” Mia giggled.

When it was time to hammer, Squeaky paused. He took a breath. “Tap, tap, tap,” he said. He kept his paws steady. “Being calm helps me aim.” The nails went in straight.

At last, the birdhouse was done. It had a little round door and a tiny porch. Squeaky painted it sky-blue. Mia added a small yellow heart. The paint smelled like lemons. The colors looked bright and friendly.

They hung the birdhouse by the window. A robin peeked in. Chirp! Chirp! “Welcome,” Squeaky said. He felt proud and peaceful.

Mia smiled up at Squeaky. “You fixed the mess. You stayed calm. You finished the task.”

Squeaky nodded. “I had big feelings,” he said. “I used slow breaths. I took small steps. I was responsible.”

He looked around the clean garage. He put the hammer back. He folded the rag. He checked the lid on the nail box. Click. “When I clean up, I show care. When I finish my job, I show care. When I help friends, I show care. That is responsibility.”

Mia hugged Squeaky. “I learned to be calm and to help,” she said. “Next time I will try right away.”

Squeaky’s tail curled happily. “We can do it together,” he said. “Step by step, calm and kind.”

Outside, the wind blew softer. Inside, the garage felt safe and bright. Squeaky smiled. “I did my job,” he whispered. “I am responsible.”

The End.
