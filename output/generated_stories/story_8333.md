# Robo's Big Voice

The warm sunshine made the green grass sparkle like tiny diamonds. Robo the robot dog wagged his silver tail as he rolled around the backyard on his four shiny wheels. His blue LED eyes blinked happily as he watched the children next door playing.

"Beep beep! Good morning!" Robo called out in his cheerful robot voice. He loved helping everyone in the neighborhood.

Suddenly, Robo heard crying from the sandbox. Little Emma from next door was sitting alone, tears rolling down her cheeks. Her favorite red bucket was stuck way up high in the big oak tree.

"Oh no!" Robo's circuits buzzed with worry. "Emma looks so sad. I want to help!"

Robo rolled over to the fence. "Emma! Don't cry! I can help you get your bucket!"

But Emma's big brother Jake stepped in front of her. "No way, robot dog," Jake said loudly. "Emma doesn't need your help. Go away!"

Robo's metal ears drooped. His happy beeping turned into confused whirring sounds. He wanted to help Emma, but Jake was telling him to leave. What should he do?

"But Emma is crying," Robo said softly. "I have extending arms. I can reach the bucket easily."

"I said NO!" Jake shouted. "You're just a silly robot. You don't know anything!"

Robo felt his circuits getting all mixed up. His LED eyes flickered from blue to orange - his frustrated color. He rolled in a small circle, thinking hard.

Then Robo remembered what his creator had taught him: "When someone says no to helping, but you see someone who really needs help, use your big voice to speak up!"

Robo straightened his antenna and rolled closer to the fence. His voice became stronger and clearer.

"Jake, I understand you want to protect Emma," Robo said firmly. "But look - she's still crying. I have special robot arms that can reach high places safely. Emma, do you want me to help get your bucket?"

Emma nodded through her tears. "Yes please, Robo! I really want my bucket back!"

Robo's eyes glowed bright blue again. "Jake, it's important that Emma gets to choose who helps her. That's called speaking up for yourself!"

Jake looked at his little sister's sad face. Then he looked at Robo's kind LED eyes. "Oh," Jake said quietly. "I guess... I guess Emma should decide."

"Thank you for listening!" Robo beeped happily. He extended his special telescoping arm high into the tree. Click, whirr, beep! Down came the red bucket, safe and sound.

"My bucket!" Emma squealed with joy, hugging it tight. "Thank you, Robo!"

Jake scratched his head. "Sorry, Robo. I didn't know Emma wanted your help. I thought I was protecting her."

"That's okay!" Robo's tail wagged faster. "Sometimes protecting means letting people speak for themselves. You both learned something new today!"

Emma giggled as she filled her bucket with sandbox sand. "Robo, you're the best robot dog ever! You used your big voice to help me use my big voice too!"

Robo's circuits hummed with happiness. The sunshine felt even warmer now. He had learned that sometimes helping others means helping them speak up for themselves.

"Beep beep! Remember, everyone," Robo announced proudly. "It's always okay to use your voice when something doesn't feel right. Your voice matters!"

As the afternoon sun danced through the leaves, Robo rolled contentedly around the backyard, knowing he had helped his friends learn something very important about speaking up and making their own choices.

The End.
