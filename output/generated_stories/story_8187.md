# Benny Beaver's Fair Share Day

In a cozy nursery filled with soft toys and colorful blocks, lived a busy little beaver named Benny. His brown fur was always perfectly groomed, and his flat tail made the perfect sound when he tapped it on the wooden floor. *Tap, tap, tap!*

Every morning, Benny loved to organize all the toys in the nursery. He would stack the red blocks here, line up the yellow cars there, and arrange the stuffed animals in neat rows. "Everything must be just right!" Benny would say, his whiskers twitching with excitement.

But today was different. When Benny woke up from his nap, he found three new friends in the nursery: Ruby the rabbit, Sam the squirrel, and Penny the porcupine.

"Hello!" squeaked Ruby, her pink nose wiggling. "We're new here. Can we play with the toys too?"

Benny looked at his perfectly organized toys. He had worked so hard to arrange everything! "Well," he said slowly, "I suppose you can play with the small blocks in the corner."

Sam's fluffy tail drooped. "Just the small blocks? But there are so many wonderful toys here!"

"And they all look so fun," added Penny, her quills rustling softly.

Benny felt his chest puff up. "I organized everything, so I should get to choose who plays with what!" He pointed his tiny paw toward the corner. "You three can share those blocks."

The three friends huddled together with just a few small blocks. Ruby tried to build a tower, but there weren't enough blocks. Sam wanted to make a bridge, but the blocks were all the same size. Penny just sat quietly, looking sad.

As Benny played with his big pile of colorful toys, he noticed something strange. Building with blocks wasn't as fun when he was all alone. The toy cars made less noise when only one was racing. Even his favorite stuffed bear felt lonely without friends to share tea parties.

"This doesn't feel right," Benny whispered to himself, his tail tapping nervously on the floor.

He watched Ruby, Sam, and Penny trying their best to have fun with so few toys. Ruby's ears had drooped down, and Sam's usually bright eyes looked dim. Penny's quills had flattened against her back.

Suddenly, Benny's tummy felt twisty and warm. He remembered when he was new at the nursery and how happy he felt when other animals shared their toys with him.

"Wait!" called Benny, scampering over to his new friends. "I made a mistake. It's not fair for me to keep all the toys when there are enough for everyone to share."

Ruby's ears perked up. "Really?"

"Really!" Benny smiled, his whiskers wiggling with excitement. "Let's divide everything fairly. We can each pick our favorite toys, and then we'll have plenty left to play with together!"

Soon, the nursery filled with happy sounds. Ruby's giggles mixed with Sam's chattering and Penny's gentle humming. They built the most amazing block castle together, with towers tall enough for Sam to climb and bridges strong enough for Penny to cross.

"This is so much better!" laughed Benny, as he raced toy cars with his new friends. "Playing together is way more fun than playing alone!"

"And it's fair for everyone," added Ruby, giving Benny a warm hug.

From that day on, Benny learned that being fair meant making sure everyone had a chance to play and have fun. The nursery became the happiest place, filled with friendship, sharing, and lots of joyful laughter.

The End.
