# The Bird Who Planned a Dance

In a cozy garage workshop, sunlight slipped through a small window and made tiny dust sparkles dance in the air. The place smelled like warm wood and orange soap. Tools hung on the wall—hammers, a blue ruler, and a shiny red tape measure that clicked when it rolled back in. On the floor were soft sawdust sprinkles that felt like crunchy sugar under tiny feet.

Pip was a small singing bird with bright yellow feathers and a cheerful whistle. He loved to sing and he loved to dance. “Tweet-a-dee! Chirp-a-doo!” Pip trilled as he hopped along the workbench. He tapped his little feet—tap, tap, tap!—on a smooth board.

Today, Pip wanted to hold a dance show for his friends. “I will dance on the big workbench stage,” Pip said. “And I will sing my best song.” He fluttered to the top of a tin can. “Tweet! Will you come to my dance?” he called to the soft broom, the sleepy toolbox, and the old radio with a silver dial.

“I will click-clack along,” said the tape measure, zipping a little. “Zzzip!”

“I will clap with my lid,” said a paint can, bumping gently. “Clink!”

Pip smiled. “Thank you! But I need to plan. I want my dance to be smooth and calm.”

He looked around. The workbench was a little messy. There were nails, a tiny pencil, and a roll of blue tape. A gentle fan hummed, whooo, whooo, making a sweet breeze that ruffled Pip’s feathers.

Pip took a deep breath. “First, I think. Then, I do,” he said softly. “That is my strategy.” He liked that word. Strategy meant a smart plan.

Step 1, Pip thought. Make space. Pip sang a tiny tune to stay calm. “Slow and steady, tweet and ready.” He nudged the nails into a small cup. He rolled the blue tape to the corner. He pushed the pencil to the side. When he felt wiggly, he paused. He listened to the fan. Whooo. He felt the breeze on his wings. Calm was his helper.

Step 2, Pip thought. Test the floor. He tapped the wooden board with his feet. Tap. Tap. Tap-tap. It was smooth. He hopped to the left. He hopped to the right. “Is it safe?” Pip asked.

The red tape measure zipped out, then zipped in. “Zzzip! It’s nice and flat,” it said.

“Thank you,” Pip chirped.

Step 3, Pip thought. Plan the dance. He drew shapes in a tiny sawdust patch with his toe. “I will start here,” he said, making a dot. “Then I will turn here,” he said, drawing a circle. “Then I will bow here,” he said, making a star.

He practiced slowly. “One, two, turn. One, two, bow.” He sang a calm beat. “Tweet… tweet… tweet.” When he felt excited and fast, he put his wing over his heart and breathed. In, out. In, out. The fan said whooo, whooo, and Pip smiled.

A little problem came. The radio clicked on. “Buzzz,” it hummed, but the sound was scratchy. Pip stopped dancing. His feathers lifted. “Oh no,” he said. “The sound is too fuzzy for my show.”

Pip closed his eyes for a tiny moment. “First, I think. Then, I do.” He looked at the radio. He could not fix it himself. But he could make another plan. Strategy time!

Pip looked at the tools. The paint can lid could make a gentle beat. Clink… clink. The tape measure could make a snappy sound. Zzzip… click. The broom could swish softly. Swish… swish. “We can be a band!” Pip chirped.

He hopped close to each friend. “Paint can, can you clink on two?” he asked.

“Clink!” said the paint can.

“Tape measure, can you zzzip on beat four?” Pip asked.

“Zzzip!” said the tape measure.

“Broom, can you swish like the wind?” Pip asked.

“Swish,” said the broom, brushing the floor gently.

Pip tapped his foot. “Let’s practice slow,” he said. “Slow is how we learn.” He counted. “One… two… three… four.” Clink. Zzzip. Swish. Pip felt calm and strong. He tried a small dip. He tried a tiny spin. He followed his sawdust map. Dot. Circle. Star.

Soon, the garage sounded happy. Clink. Zzzip. Swish. Tweet! Pip’s dance was ready.

Friends peeked in from the tiny window—a curious kitten, a ladybug on the glass, and a shy snail by the door. Pip took his starting place. His heart fluttered, but he remembered his plan. “First, I think. Then, I do,” he whispered. He breathed in the warm, woodsy smell. He felt the cool fan breeze. He heard his band waiting.

“Begin,” Pip sang.

Clink. Zzzip. Swish.

Pip danced. He stepped left and right. He made a careful turn. He bowed at the star. His song was clear and bright. “Tweet-a-dee, chirp-a-doo, I dance with a plan, and so can you!”

The friends clapped their paws and little feet. The kitten purred. The ladybug tapped her tiny toes. The snail smiled a slow smile.

Pip took another bow. “Thank you. Our plan worked. We stayed calm. We used strategy,” he said. “When we think first and move slow, we can do big things.”

The tape measure clicked proudly. The paint can chimed. The broom swished a happy sweep.

Pip felt warm and glad. He had made a dance show in the garage workshop. He had used a smart plan. He had stayed calm. And he had helped his friends make music too.

The lesson was clear in his bright little heart: Think first, make a plan, stay calm, and take each step—one, two, three, four. That is good strategy for any big dance, and for any big day.

The End.
