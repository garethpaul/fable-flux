# Sunny Bird and the Helpful Hammer

Sunny was a small singing bird with a bright yellow chest and a happy, trilling voice. “Tweet-tee-tee!” sang Sunny every morning. Sunny lived near a garage workshop that smelled like fresh wood, warm oil, and orange soap. The garage was full of sounds—tap-tap of a hammer, whirr-whirr of a drill, and the soft shhh of a broom.

One sunny day, Sunny hopped inside the garage workshop. Sunbeams danced on the floor. On a big table lay shiny screws, a tiny hammer, and a radio that played music from around the world. The music sang in different languages. Sunny listened and swayed. “Tweet-tee-tee! I hear happy songs from far places!” Sunny chirped. “People everywhere like music, just like me.”

Mr. Kim, the kind neighbor, worked in the garage. He fixed bikes and built shelves. He smiled at Sunny. “Hello, little singer,” he said. “Please watch your wings. The tools can be sharp.”

“I will,” said Sunny. “Tweet-tee-tee!”

Sunny wanted to help. Sunny wanted to be self-reliant and do a good job by themself. Sunny saw a small box of colorful bolts. “I can sort these by color!” Sunny chirped. “Red with red. Blue with blue.”

Sunny picked up a bolt with a gentle beak. But then the radio played a bouncy drum. “Boom-boom, tap!” Sunny felt excited and spun in a circle. Flap! Sunny’s wing bumped the tiny hammer. The hammer rolled and knocked the box. Clatter-clink-clink! Bolts spilled like rain across the floor.

Sunny froze. The sounds echoed in the garage. Mr. Kim turned and looked. His eyebrows lifted. The floor looked messy. Sunny’s chest felt tight. “Oh no,” Sunny whispered. “I made a mess.”

Sunny wanted to hide, but then Sunny took a deep breath. The music on the radio changed to a gentle song from far away. Sunny listened and felt brave. “All around the world, people make mistakes,” Sunny said softly. “And all around the world, people say sorry and make amends.”

Sunny fluttered down to Mr. Kim’s shoe. “I am sorry,” said Sunny. “I spilled the bolts. It was my wing. I will fix it.”

Mr. Kim’s face softened. “Thank you for telling the truth,” he said. “That is making amends. How will you help?”

Sunny thought. Sunny could not lift big things, but Sunny could do small chores. “I can sing while we work,” said Sunny. “I can push the bolts into a pile with my beak. I can sort by color again.”

“That sounds like a good plan,” said Mr. Kim.

So Sunny got to work. Tap, tap went Sunny’s beak as Sunny nudged the red, blue, and green bolts into small piles. Mr. Kim knelt and picked up the rest. The broom went shhh-shhh on the floor. The radio hummed a lullaby from another country. Sunny sang along. “Tweet-tee-tee, we help, you and me.”

After a while, the bolts were back in the box. The tiny hammer sat safely on the table. Mr. Kim wiped his hands on a soft cloth that smelled like orange soap. “Well done,” he said. “You used self-reliance and also asked for help. That is smart.”

Sunny’s feathers fluffed with pride. “It feels good to fix what I did,” Sunny said. “Next time, I will flap slower near tools.”

“Good idea,” said Mr. Kim. “Would you like a new job? You can be the Song Helper. When I sweep or fix, you can sing a steady beat. It keeps us calm and careful.”

Sunny nodded. “Yes! A steady song.” Sunny took a breath and sang, slow and gentle. “Tweet… tee… tee…” The sound was like small bells. It filled the garage with warm, happy air.

A little later, a girl named Amina came by with a squeaky tricycle. “Eee-eee,” went the wheel. “Can you help?” she asked.

Mr. Kim smiled. “Yes. Sunny, sing our careful song.”

Sunny sang the steady beat. Amina hummed along in her own language. “La-la-la.” They all worked together. Mr. Kim turned the wheel. Sunny watched and kept wings close. Amina brought a cloth and wiped the seat. Squeak turned into smooth. The tricycle rolled without a sound.

“Thank you,” Amina said. “I like your song. In my home far away, we sing while we work too.”

Sunny chirped. “People around the world help each other!” Sunny said. “And when we make a mess, we make amends.”

Amina waved and rode away, bell ringing, “ding-ding!”

The garage felt bright and calm. Sunny looked at the clean floor and the neat box of bolts. “I am glad I said sorry,” Sunny whispered. “I am glad I helped fix it.”

Mr. Kim patted the table. “Everyone makes mistakes,” he said. “What matters is what we do next. You did the right thing.”

Sunny lifted a wing and placed a tiny feather by the bolt box like a promise. “This is my reminder,” Sunny said. “Careful wings. Careful songs.”

Outside, the sky turned golden. The radio played a soft tune from a faraway place. Sunny sang along, feeling the warm air and smelling the sweet orange soap. “Tweet-tee-tee,” sang Sunny. “Make amends, be kind, help yourself, and help others.”

The End.
