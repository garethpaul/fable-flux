# Leo’s Plan in the Garage

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright curious eyes. One rainy afternoon, he padded into Grandpa Sam’s garage workshop. The air smelled like warm wood and a little bit like oil. The floor felt cool under his paws. Tools hung on the wall—clink, clank—and a big red toolbox sat by the door.

“Grandpa Sam,” Leo called, his voice small but strong. “Can I help today?”

Grandpa Sam smiled. “Of course, Leo. We are building a rolling cart! But first, we must clean the floor so we can move safely.”

Leo looked around. There were toy wheels, wooden blocks, and a long piece of string all over the floor. It looked like a tiny maze. He wanted to run and jump, but his paws could slip.

Leo took a deep breath. He remembered his mama’s words: “Use a plan, little lion. A plan is a way to do things step by step.”

Leo nodded to himself. “I need a plan.”

He spoke out loud, even though his voice wobbled. “Grandpa Sam, I have an idea. I want to make a safe path first.”

“That sounds smart,” said Grandpa Sam. “What’s your plan, Leo?”

Leo sat and thought. He listened to the soft drip-drop of rain on the roof. He watched the tools shine in the light. He felt the string brush his paw.

“Step one,” Leo said. “I will move the string into a neat circle.” He used his paws to gently loop the string. Swish, swish. The string made a soft whisper as it slid on the floor.

“Step two,” Leo said. “I will put the wheels in a bucket.” He picked them up one by one. Clink. Clunk. Roll, roll, stop.

“Step three,” Leo said. “I will put the blocks on the shelf.” He lifted each block with care. Thunk. Thunk. Thunk.

Grandpa Sam watched with kind eyes. “You are using a good strategy, Leo.”

Leo stood taller. His tail swished with pride. “Step four,” he said. “We sweep!” He pushed a small broom. Brush, brush, brush. Dust danced in the sunshine.

Soon there was a clear path. Leo smiled. “Now we can move!”

Grandpa Sam clapped softly. “Well done. Now, let’s build the cart.”

They brought a flat wooden board to the middle of the workshop. Leo touched the wood. It felt smooth and warm. Grandpa Sam handed him a tiny, safe rubber mallet.

“May I try?” Leo asked. He knew it was important to speak up for himself. “I want to help with the wheels.”

“I like how you asked,” said Grandpa Sam. “Yes, you may try. I will guide you.”

Leo’s ears perked up. He placed a wheel on a peg. Tap, tap. The mallet made a gentle sound. The wheel wobbled.

“It wiggles,” Leo said. “I need another plan.”

“What can you do?” Grandpa Sam asked.

Leo thought again. He rubbed his chin. He looked at the little nuts and bolts. He listened to the rain. He took a deep breath.

“My plan,” Leo said, “is to hold the wheel with one paw and tap slow with the other. I will count, too.”

He tried it. “One… tap. Two… tap. Three… tap.” The wheel sat snug and still.

“It worked!” Leo cheered. He felt brave and bright.

“Good strategy,” Grandpa Sam said with a grin.

They moved around the cart, attaching all four wheels. Leo walked, reached, squatted, and stretched. His paws pat-patted on the floor. His shoulders wiggled as he pushed. He loved the feeling of his body moving and helping.

When they finished, Grandpa Sam said, “Time to test the cart.”

Leo pushed with both paws. The cart rolled smooth. Whoosh! He giggled. “It goes! It goes!”

Then—oh no!—the cart bumped into a tiny ridge in the floor and stopped. Leo’s whiskers twitched.

“We have a problem,” he said. “The cart gets stuck.”

“What can we try?” asked Grandpa Sam.

Leo looked at the ridge. He crouched low and peered close. He pressed the cart gently and felt where it caught. He sat back and made a new plan.

“My plan has three steps,” Leo said. “Step one: I will move the cart back a little. Step two: I will put a thin board over the bump to make a bridge. Step three: I will push slow and steady.”

He found a thin board and set it down. Tap. He lined it up carefully. Then he pushed the cart again. “Slow and steady,” he whispered. “One, two, three.”

The cart rolled up, over, and down the board. Whoosh! No bump. No stop.

“We did it!” Leo shouted. He bounced on his paws. His heart felt warm and proud.

Grandpa Sam gave him a gentle high-five. “You used strategy. You spoke up for what you needed. You moved your body safely. That is brave and smart.”

Leo beamed. “When I have a problem, I can make a plan. I can ask for help. I can move my body to try different ways.”

“Yes,” said Grandpa Sam. “That is the lion way.”

Leo snuggled against Grandpa Sam’s arm and listened to the rain turn soft and quiet. The garage smelled like wood and clean air. The cart sat ready for new adventures.

Leo whispered, “Plan, try, and try again.”

He smiled. He felt ready for anything.

The End.
