# Benny's Beautiful Feelings Table

In a cozy dining room with warm wooden floors, there lived a busy beaver named Benny. His brown fur was soft and fluffy. His big front teeth gleamed white in the morning sunlight. Benny loved to build things with his strong paws.

Every morning, Benny's family gathered around their big oak dining table. The table was smooth and shiny. It smelled like fresh wood polish. But today, something was different.

"Oh no!" said Mama Beaver, looking sad. "Our beautiful table has scratches and marks all over it!"

Benny looked at the table carefully. There were deep grooves where his little sister had dragged her toys. There were crayon marks from his art projects. There were even some bite marks from when he was teething as a baby beaver.

"I'm sorry, Mama," Benny said, feeling his tummy get all twisty. "Some of those marks are from me. I feel really bad inside."

Mama Beaver gave Benny a warm hug. "It's okay to feel sorry, Benny. That feeling is called guilt. It means you care about doing the right thing."

Papa Beaver nodded. "And you know what? When we feel guilty about something, we can do something to help fix it!"

Benny's ears perked up. "Really? How can I help?"

"Well," said Papa Beaver, "instead of being sad about the scratches, we could turn them into something beautiful!"

Benny felt excited bubbles in his chest. "You mean like art?"

"Exactly!" said Mama Beaver. "We can paint the table together and make it even more special than before."

The whole beaver family went to the art store. They picked out bright blue paint, sunny yellow paint, and forest green paint. The paint cans felt heavy in Benny's paws. The brushes were soft and tickly.

Back in the dining room, they spread newspapers on the floor. The papers crinkled under their feet. Benny carefully dipped his brush in the blue paint. It felt cool and smooth.

"I'm painting a river!" Benny announced, making wavy blue lines.

His little sister painted yellow flowers. They looked like tiny suns dancing on the table. Papa Beaver painted green trees that reached toward the ceiling.

As they painted, Benny noticed his sad feelings were changing. "Mama, I don't feel guilty anymore. Now I feel... happy and proud!"

"That's wonderful, Benny!" Mama Beaver smiled. "You turned a mistake into something beautiful. That's what responsible beavers do."

When they finished painting, the dining table looked magical. It had rivers and flowers and trees all swirled together. The scratches were completely hidden under the colorful paint.

"Now our table tells the story of our family," said Papa Beaver. "And it's more beautiful than ever!"

That evening, as they ate dinner at their newly painted table, Benny felt warm and glowy inside. "I learned something today," he said. "When I feel bad about something I did, I can talk about it and find a way to make it better."

"That's right," said Mama Beaver. "Feelings help us learn and grow. And when we work together to fix problems, we make our home and our community even better."

Benny smiled big, showing his shiny white teeth. From that day on, whenever he felt any big feelings - happy, sad, excited, or worried - he remembered to talk about them and find good ways to help.

The End.
