# Benny Beaver's Colorful Pantry Adventure

Benny Beaver wiggled his fluffy brown tail as he opened the big pantry door. "Wow!" he said, looking at all the shelves filled with jars, boxes, and baskets. The morning sunlight made everything sparkle like tiny jewels.

Benny's tummy rumbled loudly. "Grumble, grumble!" He giggled and patted his round belly. "I'm so hungry, but I want to eat something really good for me today!"

He climbed up to the first shelf with his strong paws. There were shiny red apples that smelled sweet and fresh. "These apples are crunchy and give me energy to build my dam!" Benny said, taking a big bite. "Crunch, crunch!" The juice was so tasty and refreshing.

On the next shelf, Benny found bright orange carrots. He picked one up and felt how smooth and firm it was. "Carrots help my eyes see better when I'm swimming underwater," he said proudly. "And they make my teeth strong for chewing wood!"

Benny's nose twitched as he smelled something nutty and delicious. He found a jar of mixed nuts and seeds. "These tiny treasures give me lots of protein to make my muscles strong," he said, scooping some into his paw. The almonds were crunchy, and the sunflower seeds were small and tasty.

But then Benny noticed something sad. Some of the food was getting old, and there were empty containers scattered around. "Oh no," he said, his ears drooping. "This pantry needs some help! I can't let good food go to waste."

Benny got a wonderful idea. His eyes lit up like stars! "I'll organize everything and make sure nothing gets wasted. That's good for our Earth!"

He started by gathering all the fruits that were getting soft. "I can make a yummy smoothie with these," he said, putting overripe bananas and berries in a basket. The berries felt squishy and smelled super sweet.

Next, Benny found some vegetable scraps. "These can go in my compost pile to help new plants grow!" he said excitedly. He loved how the Earth could use old food to make new food grow.

For his craft project, Benny used empty jars to organize different foods. He put colorful dried beans in one jar - red kidney beans, white navy beans, and speckled pinto beans. They made a pretty rainbow! In another jar, he organized different grains like brown rice and oats.

"Now I'll make labels!" Benny said. He drew pictures on small pieces of bark - an apple for the fruit section, a carrot for vegetables, and a fish for proteins. His drawings were wobbly but full of love.

As Benny worked, he made up a happy song: "Healthy foods make me strong, eating well all day long! Red and green, orange too, rainbow foods are good for you!"

When Mama Beaver came to check on him, she gasped with joy. "Benny! You've made our pantry beautiful and organized!"

"And I learned so much!" Benny said, his tail wiggling with pride. "I ate foods from every color group, I didn't waste anything, and I helped take care of our Earth!"

Mama Beaver gave him the biggest, warmest hug. "You're such a smart and caring beaver," she said.

Benny smiled widely, showing his strong white teeth. He felt happy in his heart and strong in his body, knowing he had eaten well and helped his family and the Earth.

The End.
