# Ruby Rabbit's Musical Garden Party

Ruby Rabbit hopped excitedly around the sunny backyard. Today was special! Her friend Maria was coming to teach her a traditional Mexican dance for the neighborhood garden party.

"I can't wait to learn!" Ruby said, wiggling her pink nose. The warm sunshine felt wonderful on her soft gray fur. Colorful flowers bloomed everywhere - red roses, yellow sunflowers, and purple lavender that smelled so sweet.

When Maria arrived, she carried a beautiful guitar and wore a flowing dress with bright patterns.

"Hola, Ruby!" Maria said with a big smile. "Are you ready to learn the Jarabe Tapatío?"

"Yes, yes!" Ruby bounced up and down. "Let's start dancing right now!"

Maria laughed gently. "First, we need to prepare properly. Dancing is like cooking - we must follow the recipe step by step."

Ruby's ears drooped a little. She wanted to dance immediately! But she listened carefully.

"Step one," Maria explained, "we wash our hands and face. Clean dancers are happy dancers!"

Ruby wanted to skip this part. "But I'm already clean enough," she said, looking at her dusty paws from hopping in the garden.

"Trust me, pequeña rabbit. Following directions helps us do our best," Maria said kindly.

Ruby went to the outdoor spigot. She splashed cool water on her paws and face. "Ooh, that feels fresh and tingly!" she giggled.

"Step two," Maria continued, "we stretch our muscles gently. Like this!" She showed Ruby how to reach up high like a growing flower, then bend down to touch her toes.

Ruby copied each movement. Her muscles felt warm and ready.

"Step three - we learn the music first. Listen carefully." Maria strummed her guitar. The melody was bouncy and joyful, like sunshine dancing on water.

Ruby's feet started moving before she could stop them! But she caught herself and listened to the whole song.

"Now step four - we practice the basic steps slowly," Maria said. "Heel, toe, heel, toe. Like this."

Ruby watched Maria's feet carefully. She tried to copy exactly. At first, her paws got tangled up!

"Oops!" Ruby tumbled onto the soft grass. She felt frustrated. "This is too hard!"

"Remember, Ruby," Maria said gently, "following directions takes patience. Each step builds on the last one. Let's try again, slowly."

Ruby took a deep breath. She smelled the sweet lavender and felt the warm sun on her back. She tried again, focusing on Maria's instructions.

"Heel, toe, heel, toe," Ruby said to herself. This time, her feet moved smoothly!

"¡Muy bien!" Maria clapped. "Very good! Now you're ready for the full dance."

Maria played the guitar while Ruby danced. Her movements were graceful and confident. She spun and stepped in perfect rhythm.

"I did it!" Ruby cheered. "I followed all the directions, and now I can dance beautifully!"

Soon, other garden party guests arrived. Ruby performed her new dance proudly. Everyone clapped and smiled.

"Your dancing is wonderful, Ruby!" said Mrs. Chen from next door.

"Thank you!" Ruby beamed. "I learned that following directions step by step helps me do my best. And I learned about beautiful Mexican culture too!"

Maria hugged Ruby gently. "You were a perfect student. When we follow directions carefully, we can learn amazing new things."

As the sun set over the colorful garden, Ruby felt proud and happy. She had learned that patience and following directions led to the most wonderful discoveries.

The End.
