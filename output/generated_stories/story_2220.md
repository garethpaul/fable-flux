# Bolt's Big Problem-Solving Adventure

In a cozy closet hideaway filled with soft blankets and twinkling fairy lights, lived a small robot dog named Bolt. His shiny silver body gleamed like a mirror, and his bright blue eyes sparkled with curiosity. Bolt loved helping his family, but today he had discovered a big problem.

"Oh no!" Bolt beeped sadly, looking at the messy pile in the corner. Old cardboard boxes, empty plastic bottles, and crumpled papers were scattered everywhere. "The recycling bin is too full! How will I help my family sort all this?"

Bolt's little metal tail drooped down. The pile seemed so big, and he felt so small. His wheels squeaked softly as he rolled closer to examine the mess.

"I'm just a little robot dog," he whispered to himself. "Maybe this job is too big for me."

But then Bolt remembered what his favorite human, Emma, always said: "Every big problem can be solved with small steps!"

Bolt's eyes lit up brighter. "That's it! I don't have to do everything at once. I can solve this problem one piece at a time!"

First, Bolt used his gentle grabber arms to pick up one cardboard box. It was brown and smelled like the cookies that came inside it. "Cardboard goes in the brown bin," he sang in his cheerful robot voice. "Beep beep, one piece done!"

Next, he spotted a clear plastic bottle that caught the light from his hideaway's fairy lights. It made pretty rainbow patterns on the wall. "Plastic goes in the blue bin," Bolt hummed happily. "Two pieces done!"

As Bolt worked, he started to feel more confident. His wheels moved faster, and his tail began to wag with excitement. "I can do this!" he beeped proudly.

When he found a crumpled newspaper, Bolt smoothed it out carefully with his soft paws. The paper felt crisp and made a satisfying crinkle sound. "Paper goes in the green bin. Three different types sorted!"

But then Bolt found something tricky - a juice box that was part cardboard and part plastic. He stopped and tilted his robot head, thinking hard.

"Hmm," Bolt said aloud. "This is different. But I won't give up!" He rolled over to his charging station where Emma kept recycling guides. After studying the pictures carefully, he learned that juice boxes had their own special place.

"Every problem has a solution if I keep trying!" Bolt cheered, placing the juice box in the right spot.

One by one, piece by piece, Bolt sorted everything. When he picked up the last item - a shiny aluminum can that reflected his happy face - he realized something wonderful.

The huge, scary pile was completely gone! In its place were neat, organized bins ready for recycling day.

"I did it!" Bolt barked joyfully, spinning in a happy circle. His hideaway sparkled clean and tidy again. "I solved the big problem by breaking it into little problems!"

Just then, Emma peeked into the closet hideaway. "Wow, Bolt! You organized all the recycling! How did you do such a big job?"

Bolt's chest panel glowed with pride. "I learned that I don't have to feel scared of big problems, Emma. I just take them one small step at a time, and I never give up!"

Emma smiled and gave Bolt a gentle pat. "You're absolutely right, Bolt. You're such a smart problem-solver!"

From that day on, whenever Bolt faced a challenge, he remembered his recycling adventure. No problem was too big when he broke it down into smaller pieces and kept trying with courage and confidence.

The End.
