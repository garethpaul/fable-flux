# Pebbles the Penguin and the Busy Basement

Pebbles was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a happy waddle. One rainy day, Pebbles slid down the stairs to the busy basement. The basement hummed and clinked. Drip, drop, went the rain on the windows. Click, clack, went a sewing machine. Snip, snip, went scissors on a table. The air smelled like glue and crayons.

“Oh! So many things to make,” Pebbles chirped. “I want to build a boat!”

Pebbles saw boxes full of crafty things—buttons, yarn, glitter, paper, tape, and wooden sticks. On one shelf sat a shiny red toolbox. On another sat a big cardboard box with a sign that read: Craft Box—Please Share.

Pebbles flapped his little wings. “I can make a boat with a flag,” he said. “I can sail it in a puddle!”

He waddled to the table and pulled the Craft Box close. “I will just take what I need,” he whispered. Pebbles’ eyes grew wide. So many fun things! He grabbed a big handful of glitter, three rolls of tape, a whole stack of paper, and all the stickers shaped like stars. He filled his little basket to the top.

Just then, Mia the mouse and Benny the bunny came into the basement. “Hi, Pebbles!” Mia squeaked. “We want to make kites!”

“And I want to make a drum,” said Benny, thumping his soft feet. Thump, thump.

Pebbles hugged his basket. “I am making a boat,” he said. “I need all these things.”

Mia peeked into the Craft Box. “Oh,” she said softly. “There is not much left.”

Benny looked sad. “I can’t find any tape,” he said. “How will I stick things together?”

Pebbles felt a tiny twist in his tummy. He looked at his basket. It was so full. He tried to make his boat. He used too much tape. The paper ripped. Glitter spilled everywhere. “Oh no!” Pebbles cried. “My boat is a mess.”

The basement felt louder now. The sewing machine whirred. The lights buzzed. Pebbles’ eyes felt wet. “I wanted to make something great,” he sniffed. “But this is not great at all.”

Mia touched Pebbles’ flipper. “We can help,” she said. “We can work together.”

Benny nodded. “Maybe we can share,” he said. “There is enough if we share.”

Pebbles took a deep breath. He listened to the gentle drip, drop on the window. He looked at Mia and Benny’s hopeful faces. He looked at the sign on the box: Please Share.

Pebbles stood up tall. “I took too much,” he said. “I am sorry. Sharing means we all make something.”

He pushed his basket to the middle of the table. “Let’s sort what we need,” he said. “I will take just a little. You can take some too.”

Mia smiled. “Thank you, Pebbles!”

Benny giggled. “Thump, thump! Team time!”

They worked together. Pebbles showed initiative. He opened the toolbox and found a small ruler and tiny paintbrushes. “We can measure and paint,” he said. “We don’t need so much tape.”

Mia discovered a box of colorful string hidden under some paper. “Look what I found!” she squeaked. “String for kites!”

Benny discovered empty oatmeal tubs on a low shelf. “Drums!” he cheered. “Boom, boom!”

Pebbles found a small blue sticker star stuck to his flipper. He giggled. “I will share the star stickers,” he said. “Let’s count them.” They counted: one, two, three, four, five, six. “Two for each friend,” Pebbles said. “That is fair.”

They cut carefully. Snip, snip. They dabbed glue lightly. Dab, dab. They sprinkled just a little glitter. Sparkle, sparkle. Pebbles used one roll of tape, not three. His boat sides stuck nicely. He used a wooden stick for a mast and a small triangle of paper for the sail. “My boat is neat now,” he said. “It is not a mess.”

Mia tied her kite with the string. “My kite is light,” she said. “It can fly!”

Benny stretched paper over his oatmeal tub. He taped it snug. “Boom, boom!” he sang, tapping gently. “Listen to my drum!”

Pebbles painted a tiny blue star on the boat’s sail. “This is our star,” he said. “We made things together.”

When they finished, the basement looked bright. The table was tidy. The air smelled sweet like crayons and a little like glue. The rain slowed to a soft patter. Pebbles set his boat in a shallow tray of water. “Sail, little boat,” he whispered. The boat floated. Mia held up her kite. Benny tapped his drum. Boom, boom, swish, swish, splash!

Pebbles felt warm inside. “Sharing helped us all,” he said. “When we share, we each have enough. When we help, we make better things.”

Mia nodded. “Sharing is kind,” she said.

Benny smiled. “Sharing is fun!”

Pebbles clapped his flippers. “Next time, I will ask first and take only what I need,” he said. “I can use my eyes to discover and my hands to help.”

They put the extra supplies back in the Craft Box. Pebbles taped a new sign under the first one. It read: Please Share—We Make More When We Share.

The friends waved as the clouds parted. Light shone through the basement window. The busy basement felt happy and calm.

Pebbles whispered to his boat, “Thank you for teaching me.” Then he turned to his friends. “Let’s share again tomorrow.”

The End.
