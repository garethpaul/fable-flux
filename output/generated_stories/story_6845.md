# Benny Bear's Garden Helper Day

Benny Bear was a small, fluffy bear cub with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a cozy apartment with his mama, and his favorite place was their sunny balcony garden.

One morning, Benny woke up to the sweet smell of flowers and the gentle sound of water trickling. He padded out to the balcony in his little blue pajamas and gasped. "Oh no!" he cried.

The garden looked very sad. The tomato plants were drooping, the sunflowers hung their heavy heads low, and the herb pots looked dry and thirsty. Mama Bear had been sick in bed for two days, and nobody had watered the plants.

"Mama!" called Benny, running to her bedroom. "The garden needs help!"

Mama Bear smiled weakly from her bed. "I know, little one, but I'm still too tired to take care of it today."

Benny's heart felt heavy. He loved their garden so much. The red tomatoes, the bright yellow sunflowers, and the green basil that smelled like summer pizza. "Can I help?" he asked.

"That's very sweet, Benny, but watering the whole garden is a big job for a little bear," Mama said gently.

But Benny didn't give up. He had an idea! He remembered Mrs. Robin, who lived in the nest by their window, and Mr. Squirrel, who visited their balcony every day for sunflower seeds.

Benny went outside and called, "Mrs. Robin! Mr. Squirrel! Our garden needs help!"

Mrs. Robin flew down with a flutter of her red breast. "What's wrong, little Benny?"

"Mama is sick, and the plants are thirsty. It's too big a job for just me. Will you help?" Benny asked hopefully.

"Of course!" chirped Mrs. Robin. "I can carry small drops of water in my beak!"

Mr. Squirrel scampered down from the tree. "I have strong paws! I can help dig around the plants so the water soaks in better!"

Benny clapped his small paws together. "Thank you! Let's work together!"

First, Mr. Squirrel used his tiny claws to loosen the soil around each plant, making little channels for the water. The dirt felt cool and crumbly under his paws.

Then, Mrs. Robin flew back and forth to the water dish, carrying droplets in her beak and sprinkling them on the smaller plants. The water sparkled like tiny diamonds in the sunshine.

Benny carried the small watering can with both paws. Even though it was heavy, he didn't give up. Step by step, he watered the bigger plants. The water made a happy splashing sound as it soaked into the thirsty soil.

"This is hard work," puffed Benny, "but it's fun when we do it together!"

"Everything is easier with friends!" agreed Mrs. Robin.

When they finished, the garden looked much happier. The tomato plants stood up straight, the sunflowers lifted their faces to the sun, and the herbs smelled fresh and green again.

Mama Bear appeared at the balcony door, feeling better. "Oh my! What beautiful work you've all done!"

"We worked as a team!" said Benny proudly. "When we help each other, big jobs become easier!"

Mrs. Robin and Mr. Squirrel agreed to help every day until Mama felt completely better. From that day on, they became the best garden helpers ever.

As the sun set, Benny learned that when friends work together and don't give up, they can accomplish wonderful things.

The End.
