# Splash the Dolphin's Big Problem

Splash the dolphin loved to play! He lived in a special water tank in Grandma's guest room, where visiting sea animals could stay when they came to visit land friends.

One sunny morning, Splash woke up feeling very excited. "Today I want to play with EVERYONE!" he squeaked happily, making little splashes with his fins.

Soon, his friend Penny the pufferfish swam by. "Good morning, Splash!" she said in her tiny, squeaky voice.

"Penny! Let's play chase!" Splash zoomed toward her very fast, making big waves. WHOOSH!

"Oh my!" gasped Penny, puffing up like a spiky ball. "That's too rough for me, Splash. I need gentle play."

But Splash was so excited he didn't listen. He swam in big circles around Penny. SPLASH! SPLASH! The water got very choppy.

"Stop, please!" said Penny, hiding behind a coral decoration. "I don't like big waves!"

Next, Sally the seahorse floated over. She moved very slowly and gracefully.

"Sally! Want to race?" Splash zoomed around her super fast. ZOOM! ZOOM!

"Splash, you're making me dizzy!" said Sally, holding onto a piece of seaweed. "I like to move slowly and carefully."

But Splash kept racing around. "Come on! It's fun!" he called out.

Sally swam away to the quiet corner. "I need my space," she said sadly.

Then Ollie the octopus came to visit. He had eight arms and loved to build things.

"Ollie! Let's knock down your sand castle!" Splash swam straight toward Ollie's beautiful creation.

"Wait!" cried Ollie, wrapping his arms around his castle. "I worked hard on this! Please don't break it!"

But Splash was already swimming fast toward it. CRASH! Down went the sand castle.

Ollie's face turned red. "That wasn't nice, Splash! I said no!"

Now all three friends were upset. Penny was still puffed up and hiding. Sally was in the far corner. Ollie was trying to rebuild his broken castle.

Splash stopped swimming. He looked around the guest room tank. It was very quiet. No one wanted to play with him anymore.

"Why is everyone sad?" Splash asked himself. He felt confused and lonely.

He swam over to his friends. "I'm sorry," he said in a small voice. "I just wanted to have fun together."

"We want to have fun too," said Penny gently. "But we need different kinds of play."

"You didn't listen when we said 'no' or 'stop,'" explained Sally.

"And you broke my castle after I asked you not to," added Ollie.

Splash thought hard. His brain felt like it was working out a puzzle. "So... I should ask what kind of play you like?"

"Yes!" said all three friends together.

Splash's eyes lit up. "Penny, what gentle game do you like?"

"I love floating quietly and looking at pretty things," said Penny.

"Sally, what do you enjoy?"

"I like slow dancing with the water plants," said Sally.

"Ollie, what makes you happy?"

"Building things together," said Ollie.

Splash smiled. "I have an idea! What if we take turns? First, we can float quietly with Penny. Then dance slowly with Sally. Then help Ollie build a new castle!"

"That sounds perfect!" said Penny, unpuffing herself.

"What a wonderful solution!" said Sally, swimming closer.

"You're a good problem-solver, Splash!" said Ollie, sharing his building shells.

They spent the whole day playing different games. Splash learned that listening to his friends and respecting their wishes made playtime even more fun!

From that day on, Splash always asked, "What kind of play would you like?" And everyone in the guest room had the best adventures together.

The End.
