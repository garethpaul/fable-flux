# Little Hooves on the Balcony

Morning sun warmed the little balcony garden. Pots of mint, tomatoes, and tiny daisies sat in neat rows. A gentle deer fawn named Dapple stepped softly onto the balcony. Her hooves made a quiet tap-tap on the wooden floor.

“I am small,” Dapple whispered, looking at the tall tomato plant. “Can I really help?”

A soft flutter answered. “Good morning, Dapple!” chirped Pip the sparrow, landing on the railing. “The watering can is heavy today. Our plants are thirsty.”

Dapple sniffed the air. It smelled like wet soil and sweet mint. She heard the buzz of a friendly bee and the distant hum of city sounds below. The fawn’s ears twitched. She wanted to help. She wanted to feel brave.

“I can try,” Dapple said, taking a deep breath. “I can do small jobs with my small hooves.”

She nudged the little green watering can with her nose. It wobbled and sloshed. “Oh!” she giggled as a splash kissed her nose. Cool water dripped down, and she felt happy and a little tickly.

“You can do it,” Pip cheered. “One step at a time.”

Tap. Tap. Tap. Dapple moved slowly. She poured just a little water on the mint. The leaves shivered and released a bright, fresh smell.

“Good job,” Pip said. “Small and steady.”

Dapple smiled. “Small and steady,” she repeated. The words felt warm in her heart.

Next was the tomato plant. It looked tall and proud, with round green tomatoes hiding under leaves. Dapple worried. “What if I spill too much?”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “It’s okay to learn. Try a little, then look.”

Dapple tipped the spout. A thin stream flowed like silver ribbon. “Just a little,” she whispered. The soil drank. No spill. “I did it!” she beamed.

A tiny ladybug crawled onto the railing. “Hello,” said the ladybug in a small, tiny voice. “The daisies are thirsty too.”

Dapple nodded. “I can help.” She carried the can with care. She watered each daisy pot, one by one. “One, two, three,” she counted softly. Counting made her feel calm and strong.

When the watering was done, Dapple heard voices below. “Our steps are dusty,” called a neighbor. “Leaves have fallen. Can anyone help?”

Dapple peered over the railing. She could see the stairway that led up to the building. Dry leaves rustled like paper. A broom leaned against the balcony wall.

“I want to help the neighbors,” Dapple said. “This is our home too.”

Pip nodded. “That’s kind. That’s community service.”

Dapple took the broom in her teeth. Swish, swish. Her small steps made soft brushing sounds. Leaves danced into a tidy pile. A gentle wind tried to scatter them, but Dapple moved in closer circles. “Small and steady,” she said again. “I can do it.”

A turtle from the second floor poked his head out. “Thank you, little helper,” he said slowly. “You make the stairs safe for everyone.”

Dapple’s chest felt light and bright. “I’m helping,” she said. “I am small, and I can help.”

Back on the balcony, Dapple looked at the neat pots and the clean steps below. The sun made the tomatoes shine. The mint smelled extra fresh. Pip chirped a little song. The ladybug opened tiny red wings and clicked them closed again.

“Now it’s time to check the list,” Pip said. He pointed with his wing to a chalkboard on the wall. The list said: Water plants. Sweep leaves. Share a smile.

“We did the first two,” Dapple said. “But how do we share a smile?”

“Like this,” Pip sang, tilting his head and smiling with his eyes.

Dapple trotted to the railing and called, “Good day, neighbors!” She showed her gentle deer smile. The turtle smiled back. A cat on the third-floor window meowed hello. A child across the way waved a tiny hand. Dapple felt her smile grow bigger and warmer.

She tapped the chalkboard with her hoof and drew a little heart next to “Share a smile.”

“All done,” she said softly. “I completed my tasks.”

Pip fluttered beside her. “How do you feel, Dapple?”

Dapple closed her eyes and listened to the calm buzz of the bee, the soft rustle of leaves, and the happy sounds below. “I feel proud,” she said. “My body is small, but my heart is big. My small steps make big help.”

Pip chirped, “That is strong self-esteem.”

Dapple nodded. “I can try. I can learn. I can help.”

The sun moved higher, and a cool breeze brushed Dapple’s ears. The balcony garden glowed. Dapple took one more deep, happy breath. She knew she could do good things, one small, steady step at a time.

The End.
