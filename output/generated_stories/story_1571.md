# Pippa the Parrot and the Safe Backyard Show

Pippa the parrot loved colors, songs, and making things. Her feathers were bright green with tiny blue tips. In the sunny backyard, flowers waved and bees hummed. The grass felt cool, and the lemon tree smelled fresh and zesty. “Today,” Pippa chirped, “I will make a show!”

She flapped to the picnic table. She laid out art things—paper, crayons, ribbons—and a little bell. She also brought three small boxes to make a stage. “Ta-da!” she sang. “Welcome to Pippa’s Backyard Show!”

Her friends came to watch. Benny the bunny hopped softly. Tilly the tiny turtle inched along. Sunny the kitten padded near, her whiskers twitching. The breeze was warm. Butterflies floated by.

Pippa felt excited. Her heart beat fast like a drum. “I will perform a brave trick,” she said. “I will fly from the lemon tree to the boxes and ring my bell!”

Sunny tilted her head. “Is it safe?” she meowed.

Pippa puffed her chest. “I am brave!” she said. “I can do it.”

Benny’s ears wiggled. “Brave is good,” he said gently. “Safe is smart.”

Tilly blinked slowly. “We can practice,” she said. “Slow and safe.”

Pippa looked at her stage. She saw the boxes wobble a little. She saw the bell near the edge of the table. She felt the sun warm on her feathers. Pippa paused. “Hmm,” she said. “I want to be brave and safe.”

Pippa fluttered to the ground and took a deep breath. The grass smelled sweet. The ground felt steady. “Friends,” she said, “let’s make a safety plan.”

They all smiled. “Yes!” said Benny.

“Plan, please,” said Tilly.

“Good idea,” purred Sunny.

Pippa pointed with a wing. “First, we check the stage.” They pressed the boxes gently. Wobble-wobble. “Too wobbly,” Pippa said. “We need something strong.”

Benny brought a flat wooden board from the shed. It was smooth and wide. “This is better,” he said. They placed the board on the ground. No wobble.

“Next,” said Pippa, “we make a soft landing.” They spread a thick blanket beside the board. It was fluffy and blue. Pippa stepped on it. “Soft!” she chirped.

“Third,” said Tilly, “we move the bell to the middle.” Tilly nudged the bell so it could not fall off the edge.

“Fourth,” said Sunny, “we clear the space.” She gently pushed pebbles away with her paw. Swish, swish. The sun made the clean space shine.

Pippa felt calm. “Last,” she said, “we practice.” She climbed to a low branch on the lemon tree. The tree smelled bright like lemonade. The leaves whispered shhh in the breeze. “I will flap slowly and land on the board,” she told herself.

“Go, Pippa!” her friends cheered.

Pippa took a small hop. Flap, flap. She landed on the board. Thump. “I did it!” she chirped. Her heart still beat fast, but now it felt happy-fast. She tried again from a tiny bit higher. Flap, flap. Thump. Safe.

“Courage with practice,” said Tilly, smiling.

Pippa nodded. “Safe steps!” she said.

Now Pippa felt ready for the show. “Welcome, friends!” she called in a big voice. She put on a ribbon like a sash. She drew a star on the paper and held it up. “Today’s show is called The Brave, Safe Flight.”

Music drifted from a little wind chime in the tree. Ting-a-ling. Pippa climbed to a medium branch. She looked at the soft blanket. She looked at the strong board. The sun warmed her back. “I can do this,” she whispered. “I am brave. I am careful. I have a plan.”

She hopped. Flap, flap, flap! Pippa glided gently. Her feathers brushed the warm air. She landed on the board with a neat little thump and rang the bell. Ding!

Her friends clapped and cheered. “Hooray, Pippa!”

Pippa bowed. Her ribbon swished. “Thank you,” she said. “I will try one more time.” She went just a little higher. Not too high. She took another breath that smelled like lemons and cut grass. Flap, flap, flap! Thump. Ding! She did it again.

Sunny purred. “That was brave and smart.”

Benny smiled wide. “You practiced and stayed safe.”

Tilly nodded. “Safe steps make strong skills.”

Pippa’s eyes shone. “I learned something today,” she said. “Being brave is not rushing. Being brave is making a plan. Safety first, then practice, then try.”

They all shared crunchy carrot sticks and cool water on the blanket. The sky was bright and blue. A butterfly landed on Pippa’s wing. It felt like a tiny tickle. Pippa laughed.

“Tomorrow,” Pippa said, “I will make a new show. We will have a safe dance. We will mark a clear space, wear comfy shoes, and move slow at first.”

“Count me in,” said Benny.

“I will bring the music,” meowed Sunny.

“I will make signs that say Safe Zone,” said Tilly.

Pippa looked at her friends and her sunny backyard. She felt proud and peaceful. “Today was a good day,” she said. “We were brave. We were careful. We practiced. We had fun.”

The End.
