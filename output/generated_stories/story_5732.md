# Nutmeg Saves the Day

In a quiet attic, where the air smelled like dry wood and warm dust, a little squirrel named Nutmeg made a soft scritch-scritch with her paws. She was a resourceful squirrel, quick and clever. Sunlight made golden stripes on the floor, and tiny motes floated like glitter in the light.

“Good morning, attic,” Nutmeg chirped. “Let’s tidy, let’s plan, and let’s care!” Her fluffy tail swished with excitement.

Nutmeg had a cozy nest of soft yarn and feathers tucked near a cardboard box. Around her were jars with twisty lids, a tin that jingled, and old buttons that felt cool and smooth. But in the corner sat a big sack of nuts—walnuts, acorns, and hazelnuts. The sack had a small rip. One by one, nuts were rolling away.

“Uh-oh!” said Nutmeg, ears perking up. “The nuts will be lost. We must not waste.” She picked up a nut and listened. Clack clack. It sounded sad and lonely.

She called to her attic friends. “Pip! Dot! Flit! I need help!”

Pip the mouse peeked from a shoe. “Squeak? Help with what?” His whiskers twitched.

Dot the spider lowered herself on a silky thread. “I can spin and mend,” she said, her voice light like a whisper.

Flit the moth fluttered from behind a book, wings brushing like soft paper. “I see with gentle eyes,” she hummed. “I can spot the little nuts.”

Nutmeg held up her paws. “We have a problem. The sack is torn. Nuts are rolling away. We must save them. We must use, not waste. Let’s work together.”

Pip nodded. “We can do it if we plan.”

Nutmeg took a deep breath. She smelled the sweet, nutty scent. She felt brave. “Here is the plan,” she said. “Dot, please mend the sack. Flit, please find nuts in the cracks. Pip, please bring a small jar. I will sort and count. We will share and store. We will save what we have.”

“Ready!” said Dot, bouncing on her thread.

“Ready!” said Flit, wings whispering.

“Ready!” squeaked Pip, rolling out a little jar that went clink.

They began. Dot spun a shiny web patch, soft but strong. “Swish, swish,” went her silk. “I will sew the rip, nice and neat,” she said.

Flit floated low and high, peeking under a quilt and behind a box. “Here is one nut,” she murmured. “And here is two.” Her wings made a gentle “ff-ff-ff.”

Pip scooped nuts with tiny paws. “Tumble in, little nuts,” he said, dropping them into the jar. “Plink, plonk.”

Nutmeg sorted with care. “Big nuts in the tin,” she said. “Small nuts in the jar. Cracked ones in this bowl. We will eat the cracked ones first so nothing is wasted.”

Suddenly a floorboard creaked. A nut rolled toward a dark gap. “Oh no!” cried Nutmeg.

“I’ve got it!” Pip dashed, but the nut slipped. Flit swooped, but missed.

Dot zipped her silk like a tiny rope. “Nut net!” she called, and the nut landed safe in her web. “Caught!”

Nutmeg’s heart thumped. “Thank you,” she said. “Great coordination!” She smiled. “We did it together.”

Soon the sack was mended with a neat shiny patch. The nuts were safe in jars and tins. The attic looked tidy. It felt calm. The golden light made everything glow.

Pip patted his round jar. “We saved so many.”

Flit brushed her wings. “Nothing lost. Nothing wasted.”

Dot wiggled with pride. “My web is useful. Mending feels good.”

Nutmeg nodded. “We used what we had. We shared our skills. We cared for our home. That is conservation. It means we save and use wisely.”

Pip tilted his head. “Con-ser-vay-shun,” he said slowly.

Nutmeg smiled. “Yes. We keep things safe. We do not waste. We fix, we share, we plan.”

They all sat in a circle. Nutmeg opened the bowl of cracked nuts. “Snack time,” she said. “We will share.”

Pip took a tiny piece. “Crunch.” Flit sipped a crumb. Dot tapped a nut with a soft, happy click. Nutmeg nibbled and felt warm all over. The nuts tasted rich and toasty.

Then Nutmeg made labels from an old paper tag. She drew simple pictures. “This jar is for winter,” she said. “This tin is for rainy days. This bowl is for today.”

Flit smiled. “We are ready.”

Dot tucked a strand of silk around the sack like a ribbon. “Safe and snug.”

Pip yawned a happy yawn. “Teamwork is fun.”

Nutmeg looked around the quiet attic. She heard the soft wind outside, the tiny tick of a clock, and the friendly breaths of her friends. “Thank you,” she said. “We solved the problem. We saved our nuts. We shared. We used good coordination. We practiced conservation.”

She placed a small sign by the door: “Care, Share, Repair.”

“Let’s remember,” said Nutmeg. “When we care for our things and our home, we care for the world too.”

All three friends nodded. The attic felt safe, tidy, and bright. Nutmeg curled in her nest, tail over nose, and whispered, “We did it together.”

The End.
