# Milo the Monkey's Green Office Day

Milo the monkey loved helping his mama work in her home office. Every morning, he would swing from his little chair to her big desk, ready to be her special helper.

"Good morning, Milo!" Mama said, giving him a warm hug. "Are you ready to help me take care of our office today?"

"Yes, yes!" Milo chattered excitedly, his tail wiggling with joy.

But as Milo looked around the office, he noticed something that made his little heart feel sad. There were papers scattered on the floor, empty water bottles on the windowsill, and the lights were all turned on even though bright sunshine was streaming through the windows.

"Mama," Milo said softly, "our office doesn't look very happy today."

Mama looked around and gasped. "Oh my! You're absolutely right, Milo. We haven't been taking very good care of our special workspace, have we?"

Milo's brown eyes sparkled with an idea. "Can we make it better? Can we help our office feel happy again?"

"What a wonderful idea!" Mama smiled. "But it's a big job. Do you think you're brave enough to help?"

Milo puffed out his little chest. "I'm the most courageous monkey in the whole world! Let's do it!"

First, they tackled the scattered papers. Milo carefully picked up each piece, feeling the smooth texture between his tiny fingers. "These papers want to be in the recycling bin," he announced, carrying them one by one to the blue bin in the corner.

"That's right!" Mama cheered. "When we recycle paper, we help save trees!"

Next, Milo spotted the empty water bottles. He wrinkled his nose. "These bottles look lonely," he said. With determination, he gathered them up, listening to the gentle clink-clink sound they made as he placed them in the recycling bin too.

"Now the bottles can become new bottles!" Mama explained. "You're helping the Earth stay clean and healthy."

Then Milo noticed something else. The room felt very bright, but not from the warm sunshine outside. He looked up at the ceiling lights, all glowing brightly.

"Mama, why are the lights on when the sun is shining?" Milo asked, pointing up with his little finger.

"Oh my goodness, you're so smart!" Mama said. "We don't need those lights right now, do we?"

Together, they turned off all the lights. The office now glowed with beautiful, natural sunlight that felt warm and cozy on their fur.

"Look how pretty our office looks now!" Milo exclaimed, spinning around in a happy circle. The room sparkled clean, with everything in its proper place.

"Milo, you've taught me something very important today," Mama said, scooping him up for a big hug. "Taking care of our environment isn't just about big things – it's about the little things we do every single day."

"Like putting papers where they belong?" Milo asked.

"Exactly! And turning off lights when we don't need them, and recycling our bottles," Mama nodded. "When we take care of our little corner of the world, we're helping take care of the whole Earth."

From that day forward, Milo and Mama made it their special routine. Every morning, they would look around their office together, making sure everything was just right. Milo learned that being courageous meant taking care of the world around him, one small action at a time.

"I love our clean, happy office," Milo said, settling into his chair with a proud smile.

"And I love my courageous little helper," Mama replied, giving him one more hug.

The End.
