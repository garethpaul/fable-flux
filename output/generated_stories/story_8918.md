# Nutkin's Cozy Helper

The morning sun peeked through the soft curtains. Little Nutkin the squirrel stretched his fluffy tail. He lived in a warm, cozy bedroom with his friend Emma.

Emma was still sleeping in her bed. The blankets were pulled up to her chin. But something was wrong. Emma was making sad little sniffles. "Achoo! Achoo!" she sneezed.

Nutkin's brown ears perked up. His friend sounded sick! He scampered across the soft carpet. His tiny paws made no sound. "Emma," he whispered. "Are you okay?"

Emma opened her tired eyes. They were red and watery. "I don't feel good, Nutkin," she said in a scratchy voice. "My throat hurts, and I'm so cold."

Nutkin felt worried in his little heart. When friends hurt, we hurt too. That's what empathy means. "I want to help you feel better," Nutkin said. His bushy tail twitched with determination.

First, Nutkin tried to fluff Emma's pillow. But he was too small! He pushed and pushed with his tiny paws. The pillow barely moved. Nutkin felt frustrated. "This is hard," he puffed.

But Nutkin didn't give up. That's called resilience! He thought and thought. Then he had an idea. He scampered to the toy box. Inside were Emma's stuffed animals - a soft bear, a cuddly bunny, and a warm puppy.

"Friends," Nutkin squeaked to the toys. "Emma needs our help! She's sick and cold."

One by one, Nutkin pushed the stuffed animals toward Emma's bed. The bear was heavy. The bunny was big. The puppy was fluffy. Nutkin's little muscles worked hard. He was patient and didn't rush.

"What are you doing, Nutkin?" Emma asked weakly.

"Making you a comfort pile!" Nutkin said proudly. He arranged all the soft toys around Emma. Now she had extra warmth and snuggles.

But Emma still looked sad. Nutkin thought more. What else could help? He remembered how Emma always sang to him when he felt scared during thunderstorms.

Nutkin climbed onto Emma's nightstand. He took a deep breath and sang in his tiny, squeaky voice: "Twinkle, twinkle, little star, you are special as you are!"

Emma smiled for the first time that morning. "That's beautiful, Nutkin," she whispered.

Next, Nutkin brought Emma her favorite book. He couldn't read the words, but he pointed at the colorful pictures. "Look," he said. "Happy flowers! Sunny sky! Pretty butterflies!"

Emma giggled softly. Even though she felt sick, Nutkin's caring made her heart feel warm.

Then Nutkin had his best idea yet. He scampered to Emma's art table. With great effort, he pushed a crayon with his paws. On a piece of paper, he drew a wobbly heart. It wasn't perfect, but it was made with love.

"For you," Nutkin said, presenting his drawing. "A get-well heart!"

Emma's eyes filled with happy tears. "Thank you, Nutkin. You're the best friend ever. You showed me so much kindness when I needed it most."

Soon, Emma's mom came in with warm soup and medicine. "My goodness," she said. "Someone has been taking very good care of you!"

Emma hugged Nutkin gently. "Nutkin taught me that when we care about others and help them feel better, we feel good inside too."

Nutkin's heart felt full and happy. By being patient, trying different ways to help, and working together with the stuffed animals, he had made his sick friend smile.

The End.
