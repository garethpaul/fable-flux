# Kori the Calm Koala and the Pantry Plan

Kori the koala was calm and cozy. He loved soft leaves, quiet songs, and gentle hugs. One rainy day, Kori tiptoed into the big, bright pantry. The door creaked, “Eeeek.” The light hummed, “Hmmm.” It smelled like warm bread and sweet fruit.

“Hello, pantry,” Kori whispered. “I will listen. I will look. I will be safe.”

On the low shelf, Kori saw jars and boxes. The jars were shiny and smooth. The boxes were crinkly and loud. “Crunch, crinkle,” Kori giggled as he touched a box. He heard the rain patter on the roof. “Pitter-patter, pitter-patter.”

Kori wanted a snack. He saw a tall jar of honey high on a top shelf. It sparkled golden like sunshine. Kori’s tummy rumbled, “Ruuumble.” He reached up. The shelf looked wobbly. The jar looked heavy.

Kori stopped. He took a slow breath. In and out. “Safety first,” Kori said. “I will not climb. I will not pull. I will ask for help. I will listen.”

Just then, his friend, Mimi the mouse, zipped in. “Squeak! Hi, Kori,” Mimi said. “What do you see?”

“I see honey,” Kori said. “But it is high. The shelf is wobbly. I want to be safe.”

Mimi nodded. “Yes, safety first. We can make a plan.”

Kori smiled. “A calm plan,” he said.

They listened to the pantry sounds. Drip from the sink. Tick-tock from the clock. The light still hummed. The shelf gave a tiny “creeeak.”

“We should not climb,” Kori said.

“We should not pull,” Mimi said.

“We should ask,” Kori said.

“We should wait,” Mimi said.

Just then, Nana Roo, the wise kangaroo, peeked in. “Hello, little helpers,” Nana Roo said with a warm smile. She smelled like peppermint tea. Her tail made a gentle thump. “What is your plan?”

Kori spoke softly. “We want honey. The jar is high. The shelf is wobbly. We want to be safe.”

Nana Roo nodded. “Good listening. Good noticing. Safety is smart.” She looked up at the honey. “We will not climb. We will not tug. We will use a steady stool with me right here.”

Kori held his paws together. Mimi twitched her whiskers. “We will listen,” they both said.

Nana Roo brought a sturdy step stool, wide and strong. She checked it. Tap-tap. “It does not wobble,” she said. “I will hold it. You will stay on the floor. I will get the jar.”

Kori and Mimi stepped back. They made space. They watched with big eyes. Nana Roo climbed one step. She reached slowly—so slow and safe. “Almost there,” she said. The jar made a soft glass sound. “Ting.” Nana Roo held it with two hands and brought it down.

“Yay!” said Mimi. “Thank you!”

Kori’s heart felt warm. “Thank you for helping,” he said. “Thank you for listening with us.”

Nana Roo smiled. “You were calm. You were careful. That is safety.”

Kori put the jar on a low shelf. He patted the lid to make sure it was tight. “Click.” He looked around the pantry. The light was still on. Kori remembered another lesson.

“Conservation means we save and not waste,” he said. “We can save power. We can save food. We can save water.”

Mimi nodded fast. “We can take only what we need. We can turn off the light when we leave.”

Nana Roo poured a tiny bit of honey onto two small crackers. “A little is enough,” she said. “We do not waste.”

They tasted the honey. It was sweet and sticky. “Mmm,” said Kori. The cracker was crunchy. “Crunch.” The rain outside slowed to a soft “shhh.”

Suddenly, they heard a clatter. A loose can rolled in the corner. “Clink-clank, clink!” It made Kori jump. Mimi squeaked and took one step back. Kori felt a little flutter in his tummy.

Kori took a calm breath. “In… and out…” He looked with careful eyes. “The can is on the edge. It might fall. That is not safe.”

Mimi nodded. “What is our peaceful plan?”

Kori said, “We will not grab fast. We will not push. We will speak and make a safe space.”

Kori called, “Nana Roo, can you please help us fix the can?”

“Yes,” Nana Roo said gently. She moved slowly, held the can with both hands, and set it flat and far from the edge. “There. Safe and tidy.”

Kori smiled. “Thank you. We listened. We did not rush. We used calm words.”

Mimi clapped. “We kept things safe. We saved the can. We did not waste food.”

Kori looked at the shelves. He made sure lids were on tight. He made sure heavy things were low. He made sure the path was clear. The pantry smelled like clean wood and sweet fruit. It felt calm.

Kori said, “Today I learned:
- Ask for help.
- Do not climb.
- Make space and wait.
- Use only what you need.
- Turn off the light when you go.
- Speak gently and listen.”

Nana Roo turned off the light. Click. The pantry rested in the gray afternoon. They stepped out together.

Kori held Mimi’s tiny paw. “Safety first,” he said.

“Conservation too,” Mimi said.

“And listening,” Nana Roo added.

They all smiled. “Peaceful solutions,” they said together. Their voices were warm and soft like a hug.

The End.
