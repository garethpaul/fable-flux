# Fawn’s First Little Helper Song

A gentle deer fawn named Fern peeked into the musical practice room. The door was open just a little bit. Fern’s soft ears twitched. She heard tinkly notes from a piano, a hum from a drum, and the warm squeak of a violin string. The room smelled like fresh wood and lemon polish. Light danced on shiny music stands.

Fern felt a tickle of curiosity in her belly. “What is in there?” she whispered. “What makes those lovely sounds?”

She took a tiny step. Tap. Another tiny step. Tap. Her hooves clicked softly on the floor. Inside, she saw neat rows of chairs, a big black piano, and a basket of colorful scarves. On the wall, little bells hung like stars.

“Hello?” Fern called in a gentle voice.

A friendly rabbit teacher, Ms. Ruby, turned with a smile. “Hello, dear Fern! Are you curious about music today?”

Fern nodded. “Yes, I am very curious! I want to see. I want to learn. I want to help if I can.”

Ms. Ruby’s eyes sparkled. “That is wonderful. Curiosity helps us learn new things. Would you like to volunteer to be my little helper?”

Fern’s tail flicked with joy. “Oh yes! I want to help.”

Ms. Ruby showed Fern a basket of egg shakers. They were smooth and bright—red, blue, green, and yellow. Fern sniffed. They smelled faint and plasticky, like crayons. She gave one a gentle shake. Shaka-shaka! The sound was soft and happy.

Ms. Ruby said, “We can set the chairs and place one shaker on each seat. Would you like to try?”

Fern held her head high. She took a blue shaker in her mouth and walked carefully. Her hooves made a tiny clop-clop. She placed the shaker on the first chair. “I did it!”

“You did,” Ms. Ruby said. “That is good initiative. You started all by yourself.”

Fern smiled. “I like to start. I like to try.”

They worked together. Fern used her nose and chin to nudge shakers onto the chairs. Shaka for one, shaka for two, shaka for three. Sometimes a shaker rolled. Rattle-roll! Fern giggled and chased it, her ears bouncing. She brought it back and set it steady.

Soon, other little animals came in—a mouse, a squirrel, a duckling, and a tiny turtle. “Hello!” Fern said. “I helped put shakers on your chairs.”

“Thank you!” said the duckling. “You are a good helper.”

Fern felt warm and proud. She was curious, and she was helpful, too.

Ms. Ruby clapped gently. “Today we will learn the Helper Song. Fern will help us start. Fern, can you show us how to listen and how to begin?”

Fern took a small breath. She smelled the lemon polish again. She heard the room hush. She could even hear her own heart—thump, thump—in her chest.

“I will try,” she said.

Ms. Ruby gave Fern a tiny bell. It was cool and round in Fern’s mouth. “When you ring the bell once,” Ms. Ruby said, “we will all shake our shakers softly. When you ring it twice, we will stop and freeze, like statues.”

Fern nodded. She stood tall. She thought, I am curious. I can learn. I can lead.

She rang the bell. Ding!

The room filled with soft shaka-shaka sounds. The mouse swayed. The turtle smiled. The squirrel’s tail fluffed in time. The sounds were like gentle rain. Fern’s ears tingled with joy.

Fern rang the bell twice. Ding! Ding!

Everyone stopped. So still! So quiet! Fern felt a giggle bubble up. “You did it!”

“Again, please!” the duckling quacked.

Fern rang once. Ding! Shaka-shaka. Then twice. Ding! Ding! Freeze. They played again and again. Each time, Fern took initiative and led with care.

After the game, Ms. Ruby said, “Fern, your curiosity brought you in. Your volunteering helped us all. Your initiative showed us when to start and stop. How do you feel?”

Fern thought. She felt brave, like a small light inside her chest. “I feel proud. I asked a question. I tried a job. I helped my friends.”

Ms. Ruby nodded. “That is the lesson today. Curiosity helps us learn. Volunteering helps others. Initiative helps us begin.”

Fern looked at the piano. “What is this sound?” she asked. She pressed a key with her nose. Plink! The note hung in the air, shiny and sweet.

“Ooo,” said the tiny turtle. “Pretty!”

Fern smiled. “I am curious about more sounds.”

Ms. Ruby said, “Would you like to lead one more time? Let’s sing the Helper Song.”

They sang together, soft and clear:
“I look, I ask, I try,
With kind and ready heart.
I help, I lead, I learn,
That’s how good things start.”

The words felt like warm sunshine. Fern rang the bell one last time. Ding! The friends shook their shakers happily. The room buzzed with music and smiles.

When the class ended, Fern helped put the shakers back. She hopped just a little, light as a leaf. “Today, my curiosity opened the door,” she said. “And helping made the music grow.”

Ms. Ruby patted Fern’s shoulder. “Yes, little fawn. Keep asking. Keep helping. Keep starting. Your small steps make big songs.”

Fern’s heart felt full. She took one more tiny step, then another, ready for new sounds, new questions, and new ways to help.

The End.
