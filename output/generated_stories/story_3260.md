# Tova the Tortoise Plans a Park Day

In a small house on a sunny street, there was a cozy closet hideaway. A soft blanket hung like a curtain. Pillows made a puffy floor. It smelled like clean laundry and crayons. In this snug place lived Tova, a wise tortoise with gentle eyes and a smooth, green shell.

Tova loved to think and plan in her hideaway. She whispered, “Today I will make a plan. Today I will help my friends get outside to play.” She could hear birds chirp outside the window. She could feel a warm breeze slip under the closet door. It made her toes wiggle.

Tova spread paper and markers on a little box table. She took a deep breath. “Sniff. Smells like lemon soap and adventure,” she said with a smile. She wrote her goal in big letters: Park Day! Then she said, “A goal is a plan we work toward, step by step.”

Her friends popped their heads through the blanket curtain. Benny the bunny bounced in and said, “Boing! I want to play, but I am shy.” Lila the ladybug flew in with a soft buzz and said, “I am small. Will I be left out?” Mo the mole blinked and said, “It is bright outside. I like the dark.” Pip the puppy wagged his tail. “Outside! Outside!” he yipped, and then he stopped. “But I do not know the rules.”

Tova nodded kindly. “Thank you for telling me how you feel,” she said. “We can make a plan together. We will craft things we need. We will include everyone. We will go outside and have fun.”

Tova clapped her little turtle feet. “First, let us craft park passes!” She handed out blue paper. “A park pass reminds us we belong.” The friends drew pictures and wrote their names. “I belong,” Benny whispered, tracing a heart.

“Next,” Tova said, “we will craft a quiet cap for Mo.” She held up soft felt and string. “This will make shade.” Snip, snip went the scissors. Mo tried on the cap. “Ahh. Cozy. Not too bright,” he sighed.

“For Lila, we will make a glide guide,” Tova said. She folded a tiny map with simple shapes. “This shows where we can play safely.” Lila held the map close. “I feel seen and safe,” she buzzed.

“For Benny, we will craft courage buttons.” Tova glued small buttons on a ribbon. “When you touch a button, take a big breath.” Benny touched one. “In… and out,” he breathed. His ears relaxed.

“For Pip,” Tova said, “we will craft play cards with pictures of rules.” The cards showed a sun for water breaks, a leaf for gentle touch, a ball for taking turns, and a clock for coming back. Pip sniffed them. “I can follow pictures!” he barked.

Tova set all the crafts in a neat row. “We have our tools. Now our steps.” She drew four boxes on a chart:
1. Pack water.
2. Walk together.
3. Play and rest.
4. Come back and share.

The friends nodded. Their eyes shone. But then a tiny voice came from under a pillow. “Can I come?” It was Sora the snail. Her shell was chipped, and she moved very slowly. “I am slow,” she said. “I do not want to be a bother.”

Tova’s heart felt warm. “You belong with us,” she said. “We will set a gentle pace.” She crafted a shiny flag from a straw and a ribbon. “This is the Slow and Steady flag. We will walk with the flag so we stay together.”

They packed water bottles that clinked softly. They tucked in the crafts. Tova led the way out of the closet, past the laundry smell, into the bright hallway. “Step, step,” went Tova. “Boing,” went Benny, gently. “Buzz,” went Lila. “Tap-tap,” went Mo in his quiet cap. “Pat-pat,” went Pip’s paws. “Slide,” went Sora, proudly under the flag.

Outside, the air felt warm and fresh. The grass smelled sweet. The park was full of shining leaves and tiny shadows that danced. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” The friends found a picnic spot under a tree.

“Let’s play Leaf Loops,” Tova said. She showed how to toss a ring of grass onto a stick. “We take turns,” Pip read from his card. “We cheer for each other,” Benny said, touching his courage button. Lila flew through her glide guide path and cheered, “Go, Mo!” Mo popped up and steadied the stick with his soft paws. Sora slid forward and tried. The ring missed. She frowned.

Tova placed a gentle hand on Sora’s shell. “We try, we learn,” she said. “Would a bigger ring help?” The friends crafted a bigger ring from vines and flowers. It smelled like petals and fresh earth. Sora tried again. Plop! The ring landed on the stick. Everyone clapped and wagged and buzzed. Sora smiled wide. “I did it, slow and steady!”

They played Ball Balance next, rolling a soft ball along a log. Lila coached from her map. “Short path here!” Benny breathed in and out when he felt shy. Pip watched the picture cards and waited his turn. Mo rested in the shade when the sun felt strong. Tova reminded them to sip water. “Sip, sip,” they said together.

When it was time to go, Tova held the Slow and Steady flag. “Step by step back home,” she said. The friends followed, calm and happy.

In the closet hideaway, they sat on the pillows. “How do you feel?” Tova asked.

“Brave,” said Benny.
“Safe,” said Lila.
“Calm,” said Mo.
“Proud,” said Pip.
“Included,” said Sora.

Tova smiled. “We set a goal. We crafted tools. We cared for each other. We played outside. That is the lesson: Outdoor play is for everyone. We make space, we make plans, and we move our bodies in the fresh air. When we include and help each other, we all have fun.”

They hung their park passes on the blanket wall and put the Slow and Steady flag in a cup. The closet smelled like sunshine now, and the friends felt strong and kind.

“Tomorrow,” Tova said softly, “we can plan a garden game.” The friends cheered.

The End.
