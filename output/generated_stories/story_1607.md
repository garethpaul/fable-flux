# Ollie Otter’s Nursery Day

Ollie was a playful otter with bright, curious eyes. He loved to splash, giggle, and learn new things. Today, Ollie was in a comfortable nursery. The room was warm and cozy. Soft rugs tickled his paws. Painted stars shone on the ceiling. He could smell fresh soap and lemony wipes. A basket of books sat by the window, and a small sink hummed gently.

“Good morning, Ollie,” said Miss Bea, the kind teacher. She had a calm voice like a lullaby. “Today is our learning day.”

“I love learning!” Ollie squeaked, his whiskers twitching. “Can we learn about everything?”

“We can learn about many things,” Miss Bea smiled. “We will learn how to use gentle hands, clean hands, and clever minds.”

Ollie clapped his little paws. “Gentle, clean, and clever! That sounds fun.”

First, Miss Bea showed a big chart with pictures. “This is our number chart,” she said, pointing. “One sun, two shoes, three apples.”

Ollie counted with her. “One… two… three!” His voice was bright and bouncy.

“Great job,” Miss Bea said. “Now, who would like to help a friend?”

Ollie looked around. Teddy the bear toy had fallen off a chair. His fuzzy ear was bent. Ollie scooped him up carefully. “I will help,” he said softly. He patted Teddy’s ear and set him gently on the chair. “There you go, friend.”

Miss Bea nodded. “That is kindness. We use gentle hands to help.”

Ollie felt warm and proud. Kindness made his heart feel like a cozy blanket.

Next, it was snack time. The smell of sweet bananas filled the room. Crackers crunched in a bowl. Miss Bea said, “Before we eat, we wash our hands.”

Ollie bounced to the little sink. The water was warm like a hug. “Splash, splash,” he sang. He pumped the soap. It smelled like oranges. The bubbles felt slippery and soft, like tiny pillows.

“Front and back, in between,” Miss Bea sang. “Wash your thumbs, keep them clean.”

Ollie copied her song. He rubbed his paws and wiggled his thumbs. “Front and back, in between,” he chimed. He rinsed and shook his paws. Drip, drip went the water. Then he used a fluffy towel. “All clean!” he cheered.

“Clean hands keep us healthy,” Miss Bea said. “That is good hygiene.”

Ollie nodded. “Clean hands help me learn and play.”

After snack, Miss Bea brought out picture cards. Each card had a letter and a picture. “This is A for apple,” she said.

Ollie traced the big A with his paw. The card was smooth and cool. “A is for apple,” he repeated. “And B is for ball!”

“You are using your clever mind,” Miss Bea said. “Learning is like building a strong, bright bridge.”

Ollie smiled. He liked the thought of a bright bridge in his mind.

Suddenly, baby Mia in the crib began to whimper. “Mmm… mm…” Her eyes were shiny with tears. Ollie’s ears perked up. He tiptoed over the soft rug, which felt like a cloud under his paws.

“Hi, Mia,” Ollie whispered. “Do you want a book?” He picked up a soft cloth book. The cover was blue and hugged his paw like a gentle wave. “Look! A fish that goes swish!”

Mia blinked and reached out. Ollie held the book so she could touch the crinkly page. “Swish, swish,” he said, making a tiny wave with his paw. Mia smiled and made a happy “Mm!” sound.

Miss Bea watched with a warm smile. “That is kindness again, Ollie. You are helping Mia feel safe.”

Ollie felt the cozy blanket feeling again. He liked helping.

Later, it was art time. Paints smelled a little like berries, and the paper was smooth. Ollie dipped a brush and made bright swirls. “I’m painting the sun learning to shine,” he said. He added a little sink with bubbles and a teddy with a happy ear.

“What a smart story painting,” Miss Bea said. “Can you tell us what you learned today?”

Ollie thought. He put his paw on his chest. “I learned that learning makes my brain bright. I learned to wash my hands to stay healthy. And I learned to be kind and use gentle hands.”

Miss Bea clapped softly. “Wonderful lessons. Learning, hygiene, and kindness help us grow strong and happy.”

The day grew quiet and warm. The clock ticked like a soft drum. The rugs felt extra fluffy. Ollie curled up with a book. The pages smelled like clean paper and a little bit of lemon from the wipes. He read softly, “A is for apple, B is for ball,” and hugged Teddy.

“Good night, nursery,” Ollie whispered. “Thank you for teaching me.”

Miss Bea turned down the lights. “Sleep well, Ollie. Tomorrow, we will learn more.”

Ollie’s eyes closed. He felt safe, clean, and kind. In his mind, the bright bridge shone, and bubbles danced like tiny stars.

The End.
