# Maya the Monkey's Kitchen Adventure

Maya was a brave little monkey with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. She lived with her loving family in a cozy house that always smelled like warm cookies and fresh fruit. Maya's favorite room was the sunny kitchen, where golden sunlight streamed through the windows and made everything feel happy and bright.

One morning, Maya's mama was feeling very tired. "Oh my," Mama sighed, rubbing her forehead. "I promised to make fruit salad for the neighborhood picnic, but I'm not feeling well today."

Maya's heart felt heavy seeing her mama so tired. She wanted to help, but she had never made fruit salad all by herself before. The kitchen suddenly seemed very big, and the task felt impossible.

"Don't worry, Mama," Maya said softly. "You rest. I'll figure something out."

Maya stood in the middle of the warm kitchen, listening to the gentle hum of the refrigerator and feeling the smooth, cool tiles under her feet. She took a deep breath that smelled like cinnamon and vanilla. "Think, Maya, think," she whispered to herself.

First, Maya looked around carefully. She saw bananas hanging in a bunch, red apples in a bowl, and oranges that looked like little suns. But how could she reach them? And how much of each fruit should she use?

Maya remembered something her grandpa always said: "When you have a problem, break it into small pieces, like a puzzle."

"Okay," Maya said out loud. "First piece of the puzzle - I need to reach the fruit safely."

She pushed a sturdy chair to the counter, making sure it didn't wobble. "Safety first!" she remembered Mama always saying.

"Second piece - I need to know how much fruit to use." Maya thought hard, tapping her finger on her chin. Then she had an idea! She found Mama's recipe book and carefully looked at the pictures.

"Third piece - I need to cut the fruit safely." Maya knew she was too little to use sharp knives, so she thought some more. "Aha!" She found Mama's special plastic fruit cutter that was safe for little hands.

As Maya worked, she talked to herself: "The bananas feel smooth and smell sweet. The apples are crunchy and red like fire trucks. The oranges are bumpy and smell like sunshine!"

Step by step, Maya carefully washed each piece of fruit. She peeled the bananas, watching the yellow skin curl away. She used the safe cutter to slice the apples, hearing the satisfying crunch with each cut. She peeled the oranges, and the citrus smell made her nose tingle happily.

When Maya finished, she had a beautiful bowl of colorful fruit salad. The kitchen smelled amazing - like a rainbow of fresh fruit all mixed together.

"Maya!" Mama called from the living room. "What's that wonderful smell?"

Maya carefully carried the bowl to her mama, feeling proud and happy. Mama's eyes grew wide with surprise and joy.

"You made this all by yourself?" Mama asked, giving Maya the biggest, warmest hug.

"I used my thinking brain," Maya explained. "I broke the big problem into little pieces, just like Grandpa taught me. And I stayed safe too!"

Mama smiled. "You showed such good thinking and never gave up. I'm so proud of you, my clever little monkey."

At the picnic, everyone loved Maya's fruit salad. Maya felt warm and happy inside, knowing that when problems seem big, thinking step by step can help solve them.

The End.
