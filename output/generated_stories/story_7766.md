# Rocco Raccoon's Brave Bedtime Song

Rocco was a small, musical raccoon with soft gray fur and a striped, swishy tail. He loved to hum and tap and sing. Tonight, Rocco sat in his cozy bedroom. The nightlight glowed like a tiny moon. The blanket felt warm and fuzzy. The air smelled like clean soap and pine from the wooden bed.

Rocco held his little drum. Tap-tap. Tap-tap. He smiled, then took a big breath. “I will sing my bedtime song,” he said. “It helps me feel brave.”

He began to sing in a sweet, soft voice. “La la la… good night, stars, good night, moon…”

Suddenly—clang! The drumstick slipped and hit the floor. It made a loud, scary sound. Rocco jumped. His ears twitched. His heart felt fast and bouncy.

“Oh no,” Rocco whispered. “I messed up.” His tail drooped. “Maybe I should stop.”

He crawled under his blanket like a little burrito. The blanket was warm, but his tummy felt twisty. He wanted to hide. He wanted to be quiet.

Mama Raccoon opened the door. The hallway light made a golden shape on the floor. “Sweet Rocco,” she said softly. “I heard a clang. Are you okay?”

“I am not okay,” Rocco said. His voice was small. “I made a big noise. I made a mistake. I can’t sing. I can’t drum. I want to stop.”

Mama sat on the edge of the bed. The mattress sighed. She brushed Rocco’s ear with a gentle paw. “I am listening,” she said. “Tell me more.”

Rocco took a breath. He peeked out of the blanket. “I want to sing. But I got scared. I want to try again, but what if I mess up again?”

Mama nodded. “It is brave to say how you feel. That is called using your voice. That is self-advocacy. Thank you for telling me.”

Rocco blinked. “Self… advo… ca-see?”

“Self-advocacy,” Mama said slowly. “It means you tell someone what you need. You ask for help. You listen to your body and your heart.”

Rocco put a paw on his chest. Thump-thump. “My heart says, ‘I am scared.’ My ears say, ‘That clang was loud.’ My mouth says, ‘I still want to sing.’”

Mama smiled. “That is good listening. Your feelings are real. Mistakes happen when we learn. When things are hard, we try again. That is resilience. Resilience means we keep going, even when it’s tough.”

Rocco wiggled out from the blanket. His whiskers tickled in the cool air. “I want to try again,” he said. “But can we make it less scary?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “What do you need?”

Rocco looked at his drum. He looked at his nightlight. He looked at Mama. “I need a quiet drum. I need slow hands. I need you to stay with me.”

“Okay,” Mama said. “I will stay. Let’s make the drum soft.” She put a small scarf over the drum. “Now try.”

Rocco held the drumstick. His paw shook a little. He took a big, slow breath in. He breathed out. Tap… tap… tap. The sound was soft, like raindrops on a leaf.

He sang again, gentle as a whisper. “La la la… good night, stars…”

The notes trembled at first, like tiny mice. Then they grew warm and even. The nightlight glowed on the drum. The scarf felt smooth and silky. The soft taps made Rocco’s chest feel steady.

Then—uh-oh! His voice squeaked. “La—ee!” Rocco froze. He held his breath.

Mama squeezed his paw. “I heard that squeak,” she said. “You kept going. That is courage.”

Rocco smiled a little. He breathed again. “La la la,” he sang, and the squeak floated away like a bubble.

Tap… tap… tap. Rocco finished his song. He sat very still. The room was quiet and calm. The fear had gotten smaller, like a shadow at sunrise.

“I did it,” Rocco said. His eyes felt shiny. “I tried again.”

“You were resilient,” Mama said. “You listened to your body. You told me what you needed. You were brave.”

Rocco nodded. “I used my voice. I asked for help. I kept trying. I can do hard things.” His tail swished with pride.

Mama tucked the blanket around him. The blanket was warm and heavy in a nice way. She kissed his forehead. “Would you like one more song?” she asked.

Rocco thought. Then he said, “Yes. But this time, I want to start. And if I squeak, I will smile and try again.”

Together they sang, soft and cozy. The nightlight made the room glow like honey. The drum made gentle raindrop sounds. Rocco’s voice floated smooth and brave.

When the song was done, Rocco yawned. “Thank you for listening, Mama,” he whispered. “Thank you for staying.”

“Thank you for speaking up,” Mama said. “That is how we grow strong.”

Rocco closed his eyes. “I am resilient,” he murmured. “I am brave. I can do hard things.”

He fell asleep with a small smile and a soft tap-tap in his dreams.

The End.
