# Ellie's Dancing Dream

Little Ellie the elephant calf bounced excitedly in the back seat of her family's big blue car. Her trunk wiggled with happiness as she watched the trees zoom by through the window. Today was special - they were driving to her cousin's birthday party!

"Mama, Papa!" Ellie called out, her voice full of excitement. "Can I show everyone my new dance at the party? I've been practicing all week!"

Mama Elephant looked in the rearview mirror with gentle eyes. "Oh sweetie, I'm not sure about that. There will be lots of grown-ups there, and dancing might be too loud."

Ellie's ears drooped a little. She had worked so hard on her special wiggle-waggle dance! She could feel her trunk getting warm with disappointment. But then she remembered what her teacher had taught her about using her words to solve problems.

"Mama," Ellie said thoughtfully, "what if we think of a way together? I really want to share my dance because it makes me feel happy and proud."

Papa Elephant's trunk curled into a smile. "That's good thinking, Ellie! What ideas do you have?"

Ellie closed her eyes and thought hard. She could hear the gentle hum of the car engine and smell the sweet flowers blooming outside. Thinking always helped her feel more confident.

"Well," Ellie began slowly, "what if I dance outside in the garden instead of inside? That way it won't be too loud for the grown-ups talking."

"That's a wonderful idea!" Mama said. "What else could we do to make sure everyone is comfortable?"

Ellie's trunk perked up as she got more excited. "Oh! And maybe I could ask the other little ones if they want to dance with me? We could make it a fun group dance!"

"You're being such a good problem-solver," Papa said proudly. "Is there anything else we should think about?"

Ellie tapped her trunk against her chin, just like she did when she was thinking really hard. "Maybe... maybe I could ask Aunt Rosa first? Since it's her party, she should help decide too!"

"Ellie, that's very thoughtful of you," Mama said warmly. "You're showing respect for others while also standing up for what's important to you."

As they pulled into the driveway, Ellie felt butterflies dancing in her tummy - but good butterflies! She had figured out a way to share her special dance and make everyone happy.

At the party, Ellie took a deep breath and walked up to Aunt Rosa. "Aunt Rosa, I have a question. I learned a new dance and I'd love to share it. Could I dance in your garden with the other children? I promise we'll be respectful and not too loud."

Aunt Rosa's eyes twinkled. "What a lovely way to ask, Ellie! I think that sounds perfect. The garden has lots of space for dancing!"

Soon, Ellie was leading five little cousins in her wiggle-waggle dance under the sunshine. Her trunk swayed happily as she moved, and all the children giggled with joy. Even some grown-ups came outside to watch and clap along!

"I'm so proud of you," Mama whispered to Ellie during snack time. "You used your words to find a solution that made everyone happy."

Ellie beamed with pride. She had learned that when you have a problem, you can think of creative solutions and talk about them nicely. Speaking up for herself while caring about others felt really good!

The End.
