# Polly’s Basement Workout Parade

Polly was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a rainbow tail. She loved to make up songs and games. One rainy morning, Polly fluttered down the stairs to the busy basement. The basement was full of sounds and shapes. Fans hummed softly. A washing machine swished and swirled. Boxes were stacked like tiny mountains. The floor felt cool on Polly’s toes.

“Squawk! What can I do today?” Polly chirped. “I know! I will make a Workout Parade!”

She found a big orange cone, a jump rope, and a soft blue mat. She set them in a line. Polly made signs with crayons: “Stretch,” “March,” “Jump,” and “Rest.” She taped the signs to boxes. The markers smelled like grapes and lemons. Polly’s eyes sparkled. “This will be fun! I can move my body and learn to follow directions.”

Polly took a deep breath. “First, we warm up,” she said, copying what she had seen on a kid’s exercise show. “Warm up keeps us safe.”

She looked at her sign. “Stretch.” Polly reached her wings high. “Reach to the sky!” she sang. Her feathers rustled. She bent to her toes. “Hello, toes!” The floor was smooth and a little chilly. She twisted left and right, slow like a sleepy cat. “Good job, me,” Polly giggled. “I like how stretching feels.”

Just then, her friend Max the mouse peeked out from behind a toolbox. “Hi, Polly! What’s all this?” he squeaked.

“It’s my Workout Parade,” said Polly. “Do you want to join?”

Max nodded fast. “Yes! But I need help to follow directions.”

“We can learn together,” Polly said kindly. “We listen, we look, and we do. Ready?”

Max took a tiny breath. “Ready.”

Polly pointed to the next sign. “March.” She beat a gentle rhythm with her beak on a box: tap-tap, tap-tap. “March to the beat!” Polly lifted her knees. Thump, thump went her feet. Max marched too, pat-pat, pat-pat with his tiny paws. They marched past the humming washer and waved at the broom.

“I like the beat,” Max said, smiling. “It helps me know when to move.”

“That’s following directions,” Polly said. “We listen to the sound and we move our bodies in time.”

They stopped at the orange cone. The sign said “Jump.” Polly flapped her wings with excitement. “We jump over the cone. One at a time,” she said. “I go first.”

Polly bent her legs. “Bend, then jump!” she said. She hopped over the cone. Boing! Her feathers puffed with joy. “Your turn, Max.”

Max bent his tiny knees. “Bend, then jump,” he whispered, following the steps. He hopped. Pop! He made it over. Max squealed. “I did it! I followed the directions!”

“You sure did,” Polly cheered. “Let’s do three more together.” They counted: “One! Two! Three!” Each jump felt springy and fun. Their hearts beat faster, thump-thump, pat-pat, like happy drums.

They reached the blue mat. The sign said “Rest.” Polly lay on her belly. Max lay on his back. They breathed in and out, slow and soft. The basement smelled like clean soap and crayons. The fan blew a gentle breeze over their faces.

“Rest helps our bodies,” Polly said calmly. “Exercise is move, then rest.”

Max nodded. “I like that. I feel strong and calm.”

Polly stood up and clapped her wings. “Round two! Now we add a new move: ‘Balance.’” She drew a quick sign with a picture of one foot. “We listen and look, then do.”

They stood near a stack of boxes. “Balance on one foot,” Polly said. She lifted her left foot. Wobble, wobble! “It’s okay to wobble,” she laughed. “Eyes up, tummy tight.”

Max tried too. He wobbled and giggled. “Eyes up, tummy tight,” he repeated, following the words. He held it for three counts. “One, two, three!”

“Great job!” Polly said. “You learned by listening and trying.”

Polly looked around. The basement felt like a tiny gym. She had a bright idea. “Let’s make a Workout Parade song. Music helps us follow directions.”

She sang a simple tune: “Stretch up high, touch your toes. March in place, here we go. Bend then jump, boing-boing-boing. Balance tall, sing-sang-song. Rest and breathe, nice and slow. Exercise helps us grow!”

Max clapped along. They moved with the song, step by step. Polly watched the signs. Max watched Polly. They were a team. They were learning and moving. Their cheeks felt warm. Their smiles felt big.

After the last rest, Polly sipped water from a little cup. “Water helps our bodies too,” she said. Max took tiny sips. “Sip, sip.”

Polly put her wing on her chest. “I feel proud. I followed directions. I moved my body. I learned new things.”

Max nodded. “Me too. Exercise can be fun. It makes me strong and happy.”

Polly fluffed her feathers. “We can do our Workout Parade every day. We can add new moves and new signs. We listen, we look, and we do. That’s how we learn.”

Max squeaked, “And we rest, and we drink water!”

Polly laughed. “Yes! We take care of our bodies and our brains.”

They cleaned up together. Polly stacked the signs. Max rolled the jump rope. The basement hummed softly, like it was proud too.

Polly waved her wing. “Great job today, team. We moved, we listened, and we learned.”

Max raised his tiny paw. “High-five!” Tap!

Polly sang the last line of her song, “Exercise helps us grow,” and her voice rang like a happy bell in the busy basement.

The End.
