# Tully Turtle’s Brave Pantry Day

Tully was a shy turtle who liked quiet corners. One rainy morning, Tully peeked into the pantry. It smelled like warm bread and sweet cinnamon. Little lights from the kitchen made soft golden spots on the shelves.

“I will find the cinnamon,” Tully whispered to himself. “I want to help make warm toast for everyone.” His voice was small, but his heart felt big.

The pantry felt huge. Tall shelves stood like sleepy giants. Bags rustled. A box bumped. Tully’s shell tingled. He pulled his head in. Then he took a deep breath. In. Out. In. Out.

“I can do brave things,” he said softly. “Even when I am shy.”

On the bottom shelf, he saw a tin with a red lid. It said, “Spice Mix.” Next to it was a jar with a brown sticker. “Cinnamon?” Tully wondered. He stepped closer. The floor felt cool. He could hear tiny crumbs crunch under his feet. Crunch, crunch.

“Hello?” Tully called. “Is anyone here?”

A small voice answered, “I am here!” A little ant waved from beside a sugar bag. “I’m Nia,” she said. “I came from far away with my family. We like sweet things, but we don’t take too much. We share.”

“Hi, Nia,” Tully said. He felt shy, but he smiled. “I’m looking for cinnamon.”

“Oh! Cinnamon smells like a cozy hug,” Nia said. “Let’s sniff.” She wiggled her antennae. Tully lifted his nose. He smelled sweet sugar, dusty flour, and something warm and spicy. His tummy felt fluttery, but he kept sniffing.

They climbed past a tower of cereal boxes. “Careful,” said Nia. “Slow and steady.”

“I am slow,” Tully said, “but sometimes I get stuck.” He looked at a row of jars. “There are so many. I feel a little frustrated.”

Nia nodded. “When I feel stuck, I count and breathe. Want to try?”

Tully nodded. They counted together, “One, two, three,” and breathed in and out. Tully’s muscles relaxed. His heart felt calm and brave.

They moved again. A friendly voice hummed. “Hola! Hello!” A round jar with a bright label smiled. “I am cumin,” said the jar, “from far across the sea. I make soups smell warm.”

“Hello, cumin,” Tully said. “We are looking for cinnamon.”

“Buenas suertes—good luck,” said cumin. “We all come from different places, and we all add flavor. That’s like people! Different places. Different stories. All important.”

Tully’s eyes grew wide. “That sounds like the world. We can all share. We can all help.”

They passed paprika, who wore a red cap. “Szia! Hello!” Paprika puffed a gentle smoky smell. “I come from far away, too. Keep going. You are close.”

Tully’s shell bumped a jar. The jars wobbled. Clink, clink, clink! He froze. His cheeks felt hot. “Oh no! I made a mistake,” he whispered. “I want to hide.”

Nia touched his foot. “It’s okay to make mistakes. We can fix it. We can try again.” She took a deep breath. Tully copied her. In. Out.

Together they steadied the jars. Clink… still. Tully’s chest felt lighter. “Thank you,” he said.

They climbed a little step stool. The wood felt smooth under Tully’s feet. He looked down and felt wobbly. “It’s high,” he squeaked.

“Look at me,” said Nia. “We can be brave together. Brave means trying, even when you feel small.” Tully nodded. He tucked his head in, then peeked out. He tried one step. Then another.

At the top, a cinnamon jar winked. “Hello,” it said in a soft, sweet voice. “I smell like warm toast and happy mornings.”

Tully smiled big. He hugged the jar with his short arms. It felt cool and smooth. “We found it!” he cheered quietly.

On the way down, they met a bag of rice. “Namaste,” said rice. “I feed many families. Some make rice cakes. Some make rice bowls. Food is for sharing.”

Tully thought about the world. So many foods. So many homes. So many friends. “I will share the cinnamon,” he said. “Everyone can have a sprinkle.”

They reached the floor. Tully carried the jar carefully. His legs felt strong. He wasn’t fast, but he was steady. Step, step, step.

Back in the kitchen, warm toast waited on a plate. Tully sprinkled cinnamon in gentle shakes. The air filled with a sweet, cozy smell. Nia took a tiny sugar crumb. “Thank you for sharing,” she said.

Tully’s heart felt bright. “I was scared,” he said, “but I used my brave breath. I asked for help. I tried again.”

Nia smiled. Cumin and paprika on the shelf seemed to smile too. Rice rustled happily. The kitchen felt like a friendly world.

Tully took a bite of toast. Crunchy, warm, and sweet. “Being brave can be small,” he said. “A step. A breath. A try. And sharing makes the world kinder.”

He waved to his new friends. “Tomorrow,” he said, “I will try something new again.”

The End.
