# Tiko the Tortoise Plans the Rooftop Garden

On a warm morning, Tiko the tortoise climbed slowly up the little ramp to the rooftop. The sun felt cozy on his shell. The breeze smelled like fresh rain and mint. Pots sat in neat rows. The clay felt warm. The soil was dark and soft. Birds chirped from the edge of the roof, “Cheep-cheep!”

Tiko’s eyes twinkled. “Today,” he said, “I will grow a rooftop garden.” He touched the soil with his gentle toes. “I can do this if I plan,” he told himself.

He opened a small basket. Inside were tiny seed packets, a short list, and a yellow pencil. The list said: 1) Sweep. 2) Water. 3) Plant. 4) Shade. 5) Rest. Tiko nodded. “Step by step. Slow and sure.”

He started to sweep. Swish, swish went the little broom. Dust danced in the sunlight. A leaf tickled his nose. “Ah-choo!” Tiko laughed. “That was a silly sneeze.”

Then he filled his blue watering can. Glug-glug went the water. He watered each pot a little. “Not too much,” he said softly. “Just enough.” The water made the soil smell rich and earthy. A cloud moved over the sun and the air felt cool on his cheeks.

Tiko opened the first seed packet. “Lettuce,” he read. “You like cool shade.” He drew a tiny map in the dirt with his claw. “Lettuce here, in the back, where the big chimney makes shade.” He tapped the list. “Plan first, plant second.”

A shiny bird hopped closer. “Caw! Need help, slow shell?” the bird teased.

Tiko smiled kindly. “Thank you, Bird. I will try by myself first. I am practicing self-reliance. If I truly need help, I will ask.”

The bird tilted its head. “Caw-caw. Brave plan,” it said, and flew to the fence.

Tiko planted the lettuce seeds in a row. Pat, pat went his toes, tucking them in. “Sleep well, little seeds,” he whispered.

He opened the second packet. “Tomatoes,” he read. “You love sunshine.” He looked around. The middle of the roof was bright and warm. “Tomatoes here,” he decided. He smiled at the sky. “I planned for light.”

He opened the third packet. “Carrots,” he read. He scratched his chin. “Carrots need deeper soil.” He checked the pots. “Oh,” he said. “These pots are shallow.”

Tiko felt a little twist in his tummy. He wanted to plant the carrots now. He looked at the bird. He looked at the shallow pots. He looked at his list. He took a slow breath. “I must be honest with myself,” he said. “This pot is not right for carrots.”

He stood very still and thought. “I can fix this with a plan.” He drew a new line on his list. 6) Make a deep box.

Tiko found some wooden boards near the shed. He read a note on them. “For repairs,” the note said. Tiko’s heart thumped. “I should not take these,” he said. “That would not be honest.” He put the boards back and patted them. “I will find another way.”

He walked to the corner where old crates were stacked. The crates were empty and dusty. “May I use you?” he asked softly. The crates said nothing, but a sticker read, “Free.” Tiko smiled. “Yes!”

He pushed a crate across the roof. Scrape, scrape. It was hard work. His legs felt wobbly. He rested under the shade and sipped cool water. “Rest is part of planning,” he told himself. “Slow and steady.”

After a short rest, he lined the crate with a burlap bag he found in the Free pile. He poured soil inside. Thump, thump went the scoops. He patted the corners. “Deep and ready.”

He planted the carrot seeds. “Down you go,” he whispered. “Grow long and sweet.” He made a tiny sign that said, “Carrots,” and stuck it in the soil.

The bird fluttered closer. “Caw! You did it, slow shell!”

Tiko grinned. “I made a plan. I worked with what I had. I was honest about the problem.” He looked around the rooftop. The lettuce sat in the shade. The tomatoes basked in the sun. The carrots rested in their deep box. Everything felt calm and right.

But then a big gust of wind whooshed over the roof. Whoosh! A pot wobbled. The tomato sign flew away. The watering can rolled. Clank, clang, clunk!

Tiko held his hat. “Oh my!” He looked at his list and added another line. 7) Tie and weigh. He found smooth stones and placed them in the bottoms of the pots. He tied soft string around the tomato stakes. Knot, knot, knot. He checked each pot with a gentle push. “Steady,” he said. He smiled. “Problems come. Plans can change.”

The sun began to set. The sky turned orange and pink. The roof smelled like wet dirt and green leaves. Tiko’s shell felt warm and proud. The bird tucked its head under its wing.

“Good work today,” Tiko told himself. “I did it step by step. I told the truth. I made a plan. I used my own effort. I asked for no help first. I learned I can try.”

He watered the garden one last time. Drip, drip. The pots looked happy. Tiko looked up at the first evening star. “Tomorrow, I will check my list again,” he said. “I can do hard things when I plan and try.”

He curled up beside the deep carrot box. The breeze was soft. The rooftop garden was quiet and safe.

Tiko whispered, “Be honest. Make a plan. Try your best. Ask for help when you truly need it. That is self-reliance.”

The End.
