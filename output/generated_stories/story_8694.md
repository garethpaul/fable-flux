# Daisy's Garden Helper

Daisy the deer fawn loved visiting the sunny backyard behind Mrs. Chen's house. Every morning, she would peek through the white fence to watch the colorful flowers dance in the breeze.

The backyard smelled like sweet roses and fresh grass. Daisy's favorite spot was near the vegetable garden, where big green leaves grew in neat rows.

One bright morning, Daisy noticed something wrong. Mrs. Chen was sitting on her garden bench, looking very sad.

"What's the matter?" Daisy asked softly, stepping closer to the fence.

Mrs. Chen looked up with tired eyes. "Oh, hello little fawn. I'm too sore to tend my garden today. My back hurts, and my hands feel stiff. I'm worried my vegetables won't grow properly without care."

Daisy's heart felt heavy seeing Mrs. Chen so upset. She wanted to help, but she didn't know how. Her hooves weren't good for holding garden tools.

"I wish I could help you," Daisy said, her brown eyes full of concern.

Mrs. Chen smiled gently. "That's very kind of you, dear."

Daisy thought and thought. Then she had an idea! Maybe she couldn't use tools, but she could use her natural abilities.

"May I try something?" Daisy asked.

Mrs. Chen nodded curiously.

Daisy carefully stepped into the garden. She used her soft nose to gently nudge away fallen leaves from the small plants. Her delicate hooves helped her step lightly between the rows without crushing anything.

"Look!" Mrs. Chen exclaimed. "You're being so careful and gentle!"

Daisy felt proud, but she wasn't finished. She noticed the bird bath was empty. Using her mouth, she carefully carried a small watering can to the outdoor faucet. It took many trips, but she filled the bird bath with fresh water.

"The birds will be so happy," Mrs. Chen said, clapping her hands together.

Next, Daisy spotted some weeds growing near the tomatoes. She nibbled them carefully, making sure to only eat the unwanted plants and leave the vegetables alone.

"You're such a thoughtful helper!" Mrs. Chen praised. "But please don't work too hard. It's important to rest too."

Daisy realized Mrs. Chen was right. She had been working so eagerly that she forgot to take breaks. She sat down in the shade and drank some cool water from the bird bath.

"Taking care of ourselves is just as important as taking care of others," Mrs. Chen explained. "When we rest and drink water, we feel better and can help more."

As the morning went on, Daisy learned to work slowly and patiently. She took breaks when she felt tired. She listened carefully to Mrs. Chen's gentle instructions about which plants needed help.

By afternoon, the garden looked beautiful. The vegetables stood tall and clean. The pathways were clear of leaves. Fresh water sparkled in the bird bath.

"Thank you so much, Daisy," Mrs. Chen said warmly. "You've taught me something important today. Even when our bodies don't feel strong, we can find new ways to care for what we love. And working together makes everything better."

Daisy's heart felt warm and full. She had learned that helping others felt wonderful, but taking care of herself was important too. Rest, water, and patience had made her the best helper she could be.

From that day on, Daisy visited the garden regularly, always remembering to work gently, rest often, and care for both the plants and herself.

The End.
