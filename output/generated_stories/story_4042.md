# Penny's Rooftop Concert

Penny was a cheerful little penguin with bright orange flippers. She lived on top of a tall building in the city. The rooftop was her special home, with colorful flower pots and a cozy igloo.

Penny loved music more than anything. She could play the tambourine with her flippers. She could sing beautiful songs. The wind carried her melodies across the whole city.

One sunny morning, Penny had a wonderful idea. "I want to have a concert!" she said, clapping her flippers together. "All my friends can come and we'll make music together!"

Penny was so excited. She started right away. She put her tambourine here. She placed her bells there. She scattered her music sheets everywhere. Soon, her neat rooftop looked very messy.

"This will be the best concert ever!" Penny sang happily.

But when her friends arrived, something went wrong. Ollie the owl couldn't find the drum sticks. Sara the squirrel looked everywhere for the triangle. Max the mouse searched and searched for his tiny violin.

"Where is everything?" asked Ollie, flapping his wings.

"I put things somewhere," said Penny, looking around. "But I can't remember where!"

The rooftop was a big mess. Music instruments were scattered everywhere. Sheet music was blowing in the wind. Everyone felt confused and sad.

Penny's flippers drooped down. "I'm sorry, friends. I wanted to make beautiful music, but I made a big mess instead."

Sara the squirrel had an idea. "Let's work together to organize everything!"

"Yes!" said Max the mouse. "We can put everything in special places."

Ollie nodded his wise head. "Organization makes everything easier!"

So all the friends worked together. They made special spots for each instrument. Penny put her tambourine in a red box. Ollie placed the drum sticks in a blue basket. Sara organized the bells by size. Max lined up all the sheet music neatly.

"Let's label everything!" suggested Penny. They made colorful signs for each spot. The tambourine box got a picture of a tambourine. The bell basket got a picture of bells.

"Now we'll always know where everything belongs!" said Penny happily.

Finally, they were ready for their concert. Each friend could find their instrument quickly. The music sheets were easy to read. Everything was organized and beautiful.

Penny picked up her tambourine. "Ready, everyone?"

"Ready!" sang all her friends.

They played the most wonderful music together. Penny's tambourine went "shake, shake, shake!" Ollie's drums went "boom, boom, boom!" Sara's triangle went "ting, ting, ting!" Max's tiny violin went "la, la, la!"

The music floated across the city. People in the streets below smiled and danced. Birds flew in circles, singing along. Even the clouds seemed to dance in the sky.

"This is amazing!" laughed Penny. "When everything has its place, we can make beautiful music together!"

After the concert, they cleaned up quickly. Each instrument went back to its special spot. The rooftop looked neat and tidy again.

"Thank you for teaching me about organization," said Penny to her friends. "Now I know that being prepared makes everything more fun!"

From that day on, Penny always organized her things first. She prepared carefully for every concert. And her rooftop became the most wonderful place for making music with friends.

The End.
