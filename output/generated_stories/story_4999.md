# Milla the Mouse on the Rooftop

Milla was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. One breezy morning, she climbed a tall ladder and popped out onto a flat rooftop. The sun warmed her whiskers. The wind smelled like fresh bread from the bakery below. Pigeons cooed, “Coo, coo,” and tiny ants marched in a neat line near a shiny puddle.

“Hello, rooftop,” Milla whispered. “I want to learn something new today.”

She tiptoed past a small garden box. Green leaves rustled. A big blue water bowl sat by the garden. Milla saw a pigeon and a squirrel standing on either side of the bowl. The pigeon puffed his chest and flapped his wings. The squirrel flicked her tail and stamped her little feet.

“This is my water!” the pigeon squawked.

“No, it’s my water!” squeaked the squirrel.

Milla’s ears twitched. She felt a little nervous, but she took a deep breath. “I can be brave and kind,” she told herself.

She walked closer and said, “Hello. I am Milla. Can I help?”

The pigeon blinked. “I am Pip,” he said. “I found the bowl first.”

The squirrel nodded quickly. “I am Suri. I carried water here in a leaf. I worked very hard.”

Milla listened carefully. She put her tiny paws together and thought. The wind whooshed. A cloud floated by like a fluffy sheep. Somewhere far below, a bell went ding-dong. Milla heard Pip and Suri’s voices getting louder.

“My beak is dry!” Pip cried.

“My tongue is dry!” Suri squeaked.

Milla held up one paw. “I hear you,” she said softly. “You both feel thirsty. You both feel upset.”

Pip and Suri looked at Milla. Their noses wiggled. The rooftop got quiet except for the flapping of a laundry line. Shirts swished, swish, swish.

Milla pointed to the bowl. “I have an idea,” she said. “Let’s use kind words and make a plan.”

“What plan?” Suri asked.

“A sharing plan,” said Milla. “Sharing means we all get some.”

Pip frowned. “But how do we share water? If I drink first, there might be none left.”

Suri hugged her tail. “And if I drink first, you might feel sad.”

Milla’s whiskers twitched. “I learned something from the ants,” she said. She crouched to watch the ant line. “See how they take turns?” She tapped gently. “One ant goes. Then another. No pushing. No shouting.”

Pip looked down. “Coo,” he murmured. “They do take turns.”

Suri nodded slowly. “We can try.”

Milla smiled. “Let’s make a turn plan. Pip can drink while I count to five. Then Suri can drink while I count to five. We can do it again if we are still thirsty.”

Pip shuffled his feet. “That sounds fair.”

Suri’s eyes brightened. “Fair means kind for both.”

Milla added, “And we will use soft voices. If we feel mad, we can take a big breath.”

They all took a big breath together. In… and out…

“Ready?” Milla asked.

“Ready,” said Pip.

“Ready,” said Suri.

Pip dipped his beak. “Coo-coo,” he sighed. Milla counted, “One, two, three, four, five.” Pip stepped back.

Suri leaned in and lapped the water. “Sip, sip.” Milla counted again, “One, two, three, four, five.” Suri stepped back.

They did it one more time. The rooftop felt calm and bright. A warm breeze brushed Milla’s ears. The water bowl made tiny ripples that sparkled like stars in the daytime.

“Thank you,” Pip said. “I feel good. I did not shout.”

“Thank you,” Suri said. “I feel proud. I did not grab.”

Milla beamed. “You both used kind words. You both took turns. You both solved the problem.”

Pip tilted his head. “Milla, you are small, but you are very smart.”

Milla giggled. “I am small, but my heart is big. And I like to learn.”

Suri clapped her little paws. “What else can we learn?”

Milla looked around. “We can learn new ways to solve new problems. We can use steps.”

“What steps?” Pip asked.

Milla held up one paw for each step. “Step one: Stop and breathe. Step two: Use kind words. Step three: Listen. Step four: Make a fair plan. Step five: Try the plan.”

Pip repeated, “Stop, breathe, kind words, listen, plan, try.”

“Good memory!” Milla cheered. “That is knowledge. Knowledge helps us every day.”

Just then, a cloud covered the sun. A cool shade fell across the rooftop. Pip shivered. Suri rubbed her arms.

Milla looked at the laundry line. “Another plan,” she said. “We can move closer to the warm wall. We can share the sunny spot when the sun comes back.”

They all walked together to the wall. Pip cooed softly. Suri hummed a tiny tune. Milla felt the wall’s gentle heat on her back. It felt cozy, like a soft blanket.

Pip whispered, “I am glad we did not fight.”

Suri smiled. “Me too.”

Milla nodded. “When we listen, we learn. When we share, we care. When we make a plan, we solve the problem.”

They watched the sky turn bright again. The wind smelled like flowers. The city sounds below were gentle and far away. Milla’s heart felt light.

“Today we learned about turns,” Milla said. “Tomorrow we can discover something new.”

“Yes!” Pip and Suri chimed together.

Milla tucked her tail and sighed a happy sigh. She felt proud of the rooftop friends and the smart steps they used.

The End.
