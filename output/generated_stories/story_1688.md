# Robo-Pup’s Snack-Art Picnic

Robo-Pup was a helpful robot dog with little soft lights that blinked like stars. Beep! Boop! His paws made gentle clinks on the floor as he trotted into the dining room. The sun made warm squares on the table. The chairs were cozy and round. The air smelled like apples and fresh bread. Today felt special.

“Good morning!” beeped Robo-Pup. His tail light blinked blue for happy. “Craft time!” He set a basket on the dining room table. In the basket were paper shapes, safe craft glue, crayons, napkins, and little plates.

Mia waddled in, still a bit sleepy. She rubbed her eyes and smiled. “Hi, Robo-Pup. What are we making?”

Robo-Pup wiggled. “We will craft a Snack-Art Picnic! We will make a picture with real, healthy snacks. Then we will eat it.”

Mia’s eyes went wide. “We can make art and eat it? Yum!”

Robo-Pup placed a big, clean placemat on the table. He beeped, “Step one: preparation.” He washed his metal paws with warm water. Swish, swish. He hummed a clean-up song. “Wash, wash, wash your paws, keep the germs away!”

Mia washed her hands too. The water felt warm and the soap smelled like lemons. “All clean!” she giggled.

Robo-Pup set out bowls of sliced bananas, crunchy cucumber rounds, juicy cherry tomatoes, string cheese, and whole-grain crackers. He also brought a little cup of hummus that smelled nutty and cozy. “These are our craft pieces,” he said. “They help our bodies grow strong.”

Mia licked her lips. “I want all the tomatoes now.”

Robo-Pup’s tail blinked yellow for think-time. “I hear you want tomatoes,” he said kindly. “Your tummy is excited. How does your body feel?”

Mia touched her belly. “Rumbly.”

“Thank you for telling me,” said Robo-Pup. “We will eat soon. First, let’s craft. Then we can enjoy our art together. Is that okay?”

Mia nodded. “Okay. I can wait a little.”

Robo-Pup smiled with a soft whirr. “That is good patience.”

He put out big paper plates as art canvases. “Today we will make a Garden Face,” he beeped. “We will use cucumber rounds for eyes, a tomato for a nose, string cheese for a smile, banana slices for hair, and crackers for the hat.”

Mia clapped. “A silly hat!”

Robo-Pup showed Mia how to place the cucumbers. “Plop, plop,” they went. He let Mia choose her own shapes. “You are the artist.”

Mia gently set a cucumber eye. “Hello, Eye,” she whispered. She smelled the cucumber. “It smells fresh and green.”

Robo-Pup nodded. “That is a good noticing. Crafting uses our eyes and our noses and our hands. We see, smell, and touch.”

Mia put a cherry tomato in the center. “Boing!” she said. “Red circle nose!” Her giggle echoed in the warm room.

Robo-Pup peeled a piece of string cheese. “Let’s make a big smile,” he said. “Smiles help our hearts feel calm.” He curved the cheese into a happy grin.

Mia reached for the bananas. “Hair!” She laid the slices on top. “Soft and sweet,” she said, pressing them gently so they didn’t slide.

Robo-Pup added a row of crackers for a hat. Crunch, crunch. “We made a face! Great teamwork.”

Just then, Ben came into the dining room with a little frown. “I wanted to make a rocket,” he said. His shoulders drooped.

Robo-Pup’s tail blinked purple for feelings-check. He sat close and spoke softly. “You feel sad. You had a rocket idea. Thank you for telling me.” He looked at Mia. “Can we find a compromise? A way for both ideas to fit?”

Mia thought. She tapped her chin. “The hat can be a rocket,” she said. “We can add a cracker rocket on top!”

Ben’s eyes brightened. “Really?”

Robo-Pup beamed. “Great idea. We can craft a little cracker rocket, with cheese flames and tomato lights.”

Together, they placed two crackers into a tall rocket. Robo-Pup pulled thin cheese strips for fiery flames. Ben added tiny tomato “buttons.” Mia clapped. “Now it is a Garden Face with a Rocket Hat!”

Robo-Pup hummed. “Step two: appreciation.” He pointed to the food art. “We made art. We showed patience. We used our senses. We shared feelings. We found a compromise.”

Mia’s tummy rumbled again. “Snack time?”

“Step three: nutrition,” Robo-Pup beeped. “Let’s try a rainbow bite. Cucumber helps our bodies feel fresh. Tomatoes have juicy goodness. Cheese helps our bones. Crackers give us energy to play. Bananas help our muscles.”

They counted to three. “One, two, three—munch!” The crackers were crunchy. The cucumbers were cool. The bananas were sweet and soft. Ben smiled. “My body feels happy.”

Robo-Pup wagged. “Let’s sip water too.” They all took little sips. The water felt cool like rain.

After eating, they cleaned up together. Mia wiped the table with a soft cloth. Ben stacked bowls. Robo-Pup sang, “Clean up is part of crafting! We take care of our space.”

Mia hugged Robo-Pup. “Thank you for helping us make Snack-Art.”

Ben nodded. “And for hearing my feelings.”

Robo-Pup’s tail glowed rainbow. “Today we learned: Prepare before we craft. Use our senses. Share our feelings. Find a compromise. Choose healthy foods. Crafting can feed our hearts and our tummies.”

They all smiled in the sunny dining room. It felt warm and calm and proud.

The End.
