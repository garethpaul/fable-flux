# Pebble the Penguin’s Art Room Rescue

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with shiny black feathers and a bright orange beak. One sunny morning, Pebble waddled into the creative art room. The room smelled like crayons and clean paper. Colorful paints sparkled in little cups. Soft brushes waited in jars. The floor felt cool and smooth under Pebble’s little feet.

“Good morning, art room!” Pebble chirped. “Let’s make something kind and clever today.”

Pebble loved to make art from old things. Pebble called it “make-again art.” There were bins with labels. One bin said, “Paper.” Another said, “Plastic.” Another said, “Metal.” Pebble patted the bins. “You help us take care of our Earth,” Pebble said.

Pebble put on a bright yellow safety smock and gentle blue safety gloves. “Safety first,” Pebble sang. “Smock on, gloves on, eyes looking, wings ready!”

Pebble peeked into the paper bin. “Crumpled drawings! You can be a new book.” Pebble peeked into the plastic bin. “Clean bottles! You can be shakers.” Pebble peeked into the metal bin. “Shiny lids! You can be moons.”

Just then, a loud clatter echoed. “Clink, clank, clunk!”

Pebble turned. A raccoon named Riff hurried in with a big messy bag. Riff’s masky face looked worried. “I need a box!” Riff huffed. “I’m in a hurry!”

Riff dumped the bag onto the floor. Paper, plastic, and metal rolled and slid together. A glass jar wobbled near the edge of the table. Pebble’s eyes went wide.

“Freeze, please!” Pebble called. “Safety first. That jar could break.”

Riff stopped. The room grew quiet. Pebble could hear the soft hum of the air vent and the gentle drip of a paintbrush in water. Pebble took a slow breath. “We can be fast and safe,” Pebble said kindly. “And we can be fair.”

“Fair?” Riff asked, tilting his head.

“Fair means justice,” Pebble explained in a soft voice. “We treat people and things in the right way. We follow rules that help everyone. We put things where they belong so no one gets hurt.”

Riff’s ears drooped. “I just wanted to finish quick,” he said. “I didn’t think.”

“It’s okay,” Pebble said, smiling. “We can fix it together. Let’s make it right.”

Pebble pointed to the bins. “Paper goes here. Plastic goes here. Metal goes here. Glass goes in the safe tub, with a lid.”

Riff nodded. “I can do that.”

They started sorting. Pebble picked up paper with a soft shhh sound. Riff put plastic bottles in the bin with a gentle plunk. The metal lids went clink, clink, clink. Pebble held the glass jar with both flippers and slid it into the glass tub. “Safe and sound,” Pebble said.

“Thank you,” Riff whispered. “I feel better.”

“Me too,” said Pebble. “Now we can make-again art!”

Pebble and Riff washed their hands. They donned snug goggles. Pebble said, “Safety check! Smock, gloves, goggles.” Riff echoed, “Smock, gloves, goggles!”

They made shakers from clean plastic bottles. Pebble poured dry beans inside. Shake, shake! The sound was happy and bouncy. They made a moon mobile from metal lids and string. Ting, ting! It chimed like tiny bells.

Pebble stapled old paper into a little book. The pages were smooth and creamy. “This book can share our rules,” Pebble said. Pebble wrote in big, simple letters: “Recycle. Be Safe. Be Fair.”

Riff read the words slowly. “Re…cy…cle. Be Safe. Be Fair,” he said with a proud grin.

Pebble noticed a small sign had fallen from the wall. It said, “Please Sort Here.” The tape was weak. Pebble placed it back up with fresh tape. “Rules should be easy to see,” Pebble said. “That helps everyone follow them.”

Riff looked at the floor. “I should say sorry for the mess,” he said, shuffling his paws.

Pebble nodded. “Saying sorry is part of justice, too. It makes things right.”

Riff turned to the room, to the brushes and the bins. “I’m sorry, art room,” he said. “Next time, I will sort. I will move slowly with glass. I will ask for help.”

The room felt calm again. The air smelled like orange soap and crayons. Pebble’s heart felt warm. “Thank you, Riff,” Pebble said. “You are learning. That is brave.”

They held a tiny parade around the bins. Pebble shook the shaker. Riff carried the moon mobile, which went ting, ting, ting. They sang, “Sort, sort, sort! Keep us safe! Fair and kind in every place!”

Soon, friends came by: a bunny with long ears, a turtle with a shy smile, and a fox with bright eyes. They clapped their paws and flippers.

“What are the rules?” the bunny asked.

Pebble showed the little book. “We recycle to help the Earth. We use safety gear and gentle hands. We are fair and follow rules so everyone is safe and happy.”

Riff chimed in, “And if we make a mistake, we fix it. We say sorry. We make it right.”

The friends nodded. They sorted their scraps. The bins filled with the right things. The room stayed neat and safe.

Pebble looked around the creative art room. The colors were bright. The sounds were soft and happy. Pebble felt proud. “Today, we cared for the Earth,” Pebble said. “We kept each other safe. We were fair.”

Riff smiled. “That feels good in my heart.”

Pebble hugged Riff. “Mine too.”

And from that day on, the sign stayed up, the bins stayed sorted, and the art room sang with kind, safe, fair fun.

The lesson: Recycle to help our Earth, use safety rules to protect everyone, and be fair to make things right.

The End.
