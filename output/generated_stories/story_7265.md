# Momo’s Music Room Chores

Momo was a courageous monkey with bright eyes and quick feet. He loved to dance. He loved to jump. He loved to swing his tail to the beat. Every morning, Momo went to the musical practice room. The room was full of shiny drums, tiny bells, soft scarves, and a big piano that went plink-plonk when you pressed the keys.

One sunny day, Momo ran inside and shouted, “I’m ready to dance!” He spun in a circle. He tapped his toes on the wooden floor. Tap, tap, tap! The room smelled like lemon polish and warm wood. Sunbeams made little squares of light, and dust sparkled like tiny stars.

Then Momo slipped. “Whoops!” he said, landing with a soft thump. He looked down. Drumsticks were scattered. Scarves were crumpled. Shaker eggs rolled under the chair. The floor was a busy mess!

Momo’s teacher, Miss Lark, a gentle bird with a blue ribbon on her neck, fluttered in. “Good morning, Momo,” she chirped. “I see a job to do.”

Momo made a face. “But I want to dance now,” he said. “Chores are boring.”

Miss Lark tilted her head. “Chores help music happen,” she said. “When we tidy, the room can sing. When we help, our hearts can dance.”

Momo blinked. “Chores make music?”

“Listen,” Miss Lark said. She tapped a broom on the floor. Tap-tap. She shook a duster. Swish-swish. She clinked the shaker eggs into a basket. Clink, clink. “Hear the tidy beat?”

Momo’s ears perked up. “I hear it! Tap, swish, clink!” His tail twitched with excitement. “Can chores be a dance?”

Miss Lark smiled. “Let’s try. We will be open-minded. We will try something new.”

Momo stood tall and brave. “I am courageous. I can try.”

They made a plan. Miss Lark said, “First, we pick up. Then, we put away. Then, we wipe and sweep. We do it with kindness, to help the room and help each other.”

Momo nodded. “I will help. I will be gentle and kind.”

He picked up drumsticks. Tap-tap into the drumstick jar. He hummed, “Tap, tap, tap.” He folded scarves, soft and silky. They smelled like clean soap. “Fold, fold, float,” he sang as the scarves drifted down like colorful clouds. He rolled the shaker eggs out from under the chair. “Roll, roll, roll,” he giggled, as the little eggs made quiet rattles. Rat-a-tat, rat-a-tat.

Miss Lark wiped the piano keys. Wipe, wipe. “Thank you, keys,” she said. “You make sweet sounds.” She dusted the drums. Swish, swish. “Thank you, drums, for your big boom-boom.”

Momo watched. “You talk to the instruments?”

Miss Lark nodded. “We show compassion. We care for the things that help us. We care for each other, too.”

Momo’s heart felt warm. He patted the small bell. “Thank you, bell,” he whispered. “Ding, ding.”

A little mouse named Pip peeked out from behind a music stand. Pip looked shy and worried. “I made a mess,” Pip squeaked. “I dropped stickers. I am sorry.”

Momo crouched down. “It’s okay, Pip. We can help. We can clean together.” He smiled a big, friendly smile.

Pip’s whiskers twitched. “Really?”

“Really,” said Momo. “We are a team.”

Momo, Miss Lark, and Pip made a tidy rhythm. Tap-tap, swish-swish, clink-clink, sweep-sweep. The broom whispered over the floor. The lemon smell grew bright and fresh. The room looked shiny again. The sunbeams danced.

Momo felt proud. “Chores can be a dance! Chores can be a song!”

Miss Lark clapped her wings. “Yes! You were open-minded. You tried, and you learned.”

Pip smiled. “You were kind to me,” he said. “Thank you.”

Momo grinned. “Helping is brave. Helping feels good.”

When the room was clean, Miss Lark nodded. “Now the music can play.”

Momo hopped to the piano. “Plink-plonk-plink!” He stomped his feet. Stomp, stomp! He twirled a scarf in the clean, wide space. Whoosh! He helped Pip shake a tiny bell. Ding-ding! Their dance was light and happy. Momo’s tail drew circles in the air.

As they danced, Momo sang a little chore song:
“Pick up, put away,
Help a friend, help today.
Tap, swish, sweep the floor,
Then we dance and smile some more!”

They laughed together. Their feet and tiny paws made soft pats on the wood. The drums went boom, the bell went ding, and the piano went plink. The room really did sing.

After the dance, Momo hung a small sign on the wall. It said, “Chore Time = Music Time.” He traced the letters with his finger. “This reminds me,” he said. “When I help, the fun grows.”

Miss Lark nodded. “Chores show care. Open minds learn. Kind hearts dance.”

Momo took a deep breath. The room smelled bright and clean. He felt calm and strong. “Today I learned,” he said, “that chores help everyone. Chores make space for joy. I can be brave, I can be kind, and I can try new things.”

Pip gave Momo a tiny high-five. “Team tidy!”

Momo giggled. “Team tidy!”

They put the last basket on the shelf. Tap. The musical practice room glowed with quiet happiness.

Momo waved to the instruments. “Thank you, friends. See you tomorrow.” He felt proud, helpful, and ready to dance again.

The lesson was clear in Momo’s heart: Do your chores with kindness. Be open to new ideas. Dance through the work, and joy will follow.

The End.
