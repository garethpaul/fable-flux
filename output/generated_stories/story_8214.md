# Poppy Panda’s Helpful Car Ride

Poppy the panda woke up to a soft morning. The sun peeked in the window. Birds chirped a tiny song. Poppy stretched her arms and took a deep breath that smelled like warm toast.

“Good morning, Poppy,” said Mama Panda, tying a bright blue ribbon on Poppy’s ear. “It’s our routine day.”

Poppy smiled. Routine day was her favorite. It was the day they followed their family plan: make the bed, eat breakfast, brush teeth, help someone, and say thank you.

Poppy patted her pillow, smoothed the blanket, and made the bed neat. “Step one, done,” she said proudly.

In the kitchen, the toast made a happy crunch. The jam was sweet and a little sticky. “Step two, done!” Poppy giggled.

She brushed her teeth in tiny circles. The mint bubbles tickled her tongue. “Step three, done,” she said, making a minty smile in the mirror.

Mama Panda jingled the car keys. “Time for our family car ride,” she said. “Today we do our community service. We will bring warm muffins to Mr. Lee at the library.”

Poppy clapped. “We can help! I will be patient and kind.”

They climbed into the family car. The seat was soft, and the seat belt went click. Poppy loved the steady hum of the engine. It sounded like “m-m-m,” a calm song. Poppy looked at her routine chart stuck to the back of Mama’s seat. It had simple pictures: a bed, a toothbrush, a heart, a muffin, and a smile.

As they drove, the sky was bright and blue. Trees waved hello. Poppy counted the streetlights one by one. “One, two, three,” she said softly. Counting helped her feel calm.

Suddenly, the car slowed. Red brake lights blinked ahead. The road was busy and stopped. Poppy’s ears perked up. “Why are we stopping?” she asked.

“There is traffic,” Mama said gently. “We will wait. Remember our routine: we use patience.”

Poppy took a deep breath. She held her little panda paws on her lap. “I can be patient,” she whispered. She looked out the window and saw a dog with floppy ears. The dog wagged at Poppy. Poppy waved back and smiled.

A small voice came from the back seat in the next car. A baby was crying. The baby’s cry sounded like “wah-wah,” soft and sad. Poppy’s heart felt warm and a little tight. “The baby is upset,” she said. “I feel sad for the baby.”

“You are noticing feelings,” Mama said. “That is empathy. We care about how others feel.”

Poppy thought. “Maybe the baby needs a song,” she said. “Songs help me wait.”

Poppy sang in a gentle voice, “Wait, wait, we can wait. Take a breath and count to eight.” She counted slowly, tapping her paws. “One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight.” Her voice was soft like a blanket. The baby looked over, big eyes shining. The baby stopped crying for a moment and listened.

Poppy smiled. “We can help even when we wait,” she said.

The cars started moving, then stopped again. Poppy felt wiggly. She wiggled her toes and scrunched her nose. “Waiting is hard,” she said.

Mama nodded. “Waiting is hard. What does our routine say?”

Poppy looked at the chart. “Use patience, use kind words, and help someone,” she read.

She opened her small bag and took out a picture book. “I can read to you, Mama,” Poppy said. “Then I will not feel so wiggly.”

“That is a smart plan,” Mama said.

Poppy read the pictures out loud. “The bunny hops. The bunny rests.” Her voice was bright and bouncy. The car felt cozy. The air smelled like warm muffins from the basket on the seat. Poppy’s tummy rumbled.

Soon, they reached a corner where a crossing guard stood with a bright yellow vest and a big stop sign. He waved cars through, one at a time. His face looked hot and tired.

Poppy rolled down her window a tiny crack. Warm air brushed her fur. “Hello!” she called. “Thank you for helping us cross!”

The crossing guard smiled. “Thank you for waiting so nicely,” he said.

Poppy’s chest felt light and happy. “We are doing community service today,” she told Mama. “We can help him too.”

At the library, Poppy carried the basket of muffins carefully with both paws. The door smelled like old wood and paper. Inside, it was cool and quiet. Mr. Lee, the librarian, waved hello. “Oh my, warm muffins!” he said. “You remembered our reading day.”

Poppy nodded. “It is part of our routine,” she said. “Make bed, eat, brush, help, and thank.”

Mr. Lee laughed softly. “You are a very patient panda.”

Poppy gave him the muffins. “These are for you and the helpers,” she said. “And we have a song for waiting.”

She sang her waiting song. Mr. Lee’s eyes crinkled with joy. “That song will help many children,” he said. “Thank you.”

On the ride home, the car hummed again. Poppy’s body felt calm. She looked at the chart and traced each picture with a paw. “We did all the steps,” she said. “We were patient in traffic. We showed empathy to the baby. We did community service for Mr. Lee and the crossing guard.”

Mama squeezed Poppy’s paw. “And we did it together.”

Poppy leaned back. The seat felt snug. The day felt full and kind. “Routine helps me know what to do,” she said. “When I follow the steps, I can be calm, helpful, and kind.”

Mama smiled. “That is the lesson.”

Poppy nodded. “I am a patient panda,” she whispered, and watched the trees wave goodbye.

The End.
