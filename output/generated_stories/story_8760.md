# Gilly's Morning Routine

Gilly the giraffe was very tall, even for a giraffe. Her long neck reached way up to the clouds, and her spots were the prettiest shade of brown. But Gilly had a big problem every morning in the bathroom.

"Oh no!" Gilly said, looking in the mirror. "My mane is all messy, my teeth need brushing, and I forgot to wash my face again!" She felt frustrated because she always seemed to forget something important.

Her friend Benny the bunny hopped into the bathroom. "Good morning, Gilly! You look worried. What's wrong?"

"I can never remember everything I need to do in the morning," Gilly sighed. "Sometimes I brush my teeth but forget to wash my face. Sometimes I wash my face but forget to comb my mane. I feel so scattered!"

Benny's nose twitched thoughtfully. "Maybe you need a morning routine! My mom taught me to do the same things in the same order every day. Want me to help you?"

"Yes, please!" Gilly said, her brown eyes lighting up with hope.

Benny pulled out a colorful chart from his pocket. "Let's make a routine together! What should you do first?"

Gilly thought carefully. "Well, I should wash my face with warm, soapy water. It feels so nice and fresh!"

"Perfect!" Benny drew a picture of a face with soap bubbles. "What's next?"

"I should brush my teeth!" Gilly said, picking up her extra-long toothbrush. "They need to be clean and sparkly white!"

Benny drew a big smile with shiny teeth. "And then?"

"I should comb my beautiful mane so it's not all tangled," Gilly said, running her hooves through her hair.

"Excellent!" Benny added a picture of a comb. "Now let's practice!"

They hung the chart on the bathroom wall where Gilly could see it easily. The pictures were bright and colorful - blue soap bubbles, white teeth, and a golden comb.

"First, wash face," Gilly said, turning on the warm water. She splashed gently and felt the soap make her skin clean and smooth. "This feels wonderful!"

"Second, brush teeth," she continued, squeezing mint toothpaste onto her brush. She brushed up and down, making her teeth feel fresh and clean. "Minty fresh!"

"Third, comb mane," Gilly finished, carefully combing through her hair until it was neat and pretty. "All done!"

"You did it!" Benny cheered, clapping his paws.

The next morning, Gilly looked at her chart. "Wash face first," she said, following each step carefully. She felt proud and organized.

Day after day, Gilly followed her routine. Soon, she didn't even need to look at the chart anymore! Her body remembered what to do.

"Benny, thank you so much!" Gilly said one morning. "Now I feel confident and ready for each day. Having a routine makes everything easier!"

"That's what friends are for," Benny smiled. "And look how responsible you've become! You take such good care of yourself now."

Gilly looked in the mirror at her clean face, bright smile, and neat mane. She felt proud of herself. "A good routine helps me be my best self every day!"

From that day on, Gilly's morning routine became as natural as breathing. She learned that doing the same helpful things in the same order made her feel calm, confident, and ready for wonderful adventures.

The End.
