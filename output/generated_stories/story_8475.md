# The Closet of Little Truths

BunBun was a small, determined rabbit with bright ears and a twitchy nose. BunBun loved to hop, to help, and to learn. One rainy morning, BunBun’s mama said, “We will clean the closet hideaway today. It is our family tradition. We sort, we share, and we tell the truth about what we find.”

The closet hideaway was warm and cozy. It smelled like pine soap and soft blankets. Shelves made little shadows. Boxes whispered when BunBun touched them. Tap, tap. Rustle, rustle.

BunBun nodded. “I can do it! I am brave. I am honest.” BunBun’s nose wiggled with pride.

Mama Rabbit smiled. “Good. We work together. First, we make three baskets. One is Keep. One is Share. One is Recycle. This is our organization plan. It keeps things tidy and happy.”

BunBun placed three baskets on the floor. “Keep, Share, Recycle,” BunBun sang. “Keep, Share, Recycle!”

Mama opened a small box. Inside was a red scarf. It was soft like a cloud. “This was Grandma’s scarf,” Mama said. “She wore it on rainy days. Our family loves to wear it when we read stories. That is a tradition.”

BunBun hugged the scarf. It felt warm and smelled like cinnamon tea. “I like this tradition,” BunBun whispered. “We will keep it.”

They worked and hummed. BunBun sorted buttons, tiny bells, and little pictures. “Buttons go with buttons,” BunBun said. “Bells go with bells.” The closet began to look neat. The floor looked bigger. The air felt lighter.

Then BunBun found a shiny marble. It was blue like the sky and smooth like water. BunBun’s eyes went wide. “Ooooh,” BunBun breathed. “I want this.”

BunBun looked around. Mama was folding a blanket. BunBun felt a little thump in the chest. The thump said, “Tell Mama.” But another feeling whispered, “Hide it in your pocket.”

BunBun put the marble in a pocket. It felt cold and secret. BunBun’s ears drooped. The closet did not feel warm now. It felt tight. The pine smell seemed sharp.

Mama turned and smiled. “How are you doing, BunBun?”

BunBun’s paws felt wiggly. “I am okay,” BunBun said in a small voice.

They kept sorting. BunBun did not sing. The little thump in BunBun’s chest grew louder. Thump. Thump. Thump.

Mama picked up an old game tin. She shook it. “Hmm. I think a marble is missing,” she said. She opened the tin. “Yes, the blue sky marble. It is part of our family game night. It is our tradition to keep the set together.”

BunBun’s cheeks got hot. The pocket felt heavy. A tiny tear stood in one eye. BunBun took a deep breath and put a paw on the pocket.

“Mama,” BunBun whispered. “I have the marble. I put it in my pocket. I did not tell you. I am sorry.”

Mama knelt down and looked gently into BunBun’s eyes. “Thank you for telling the truth,” she said softly. “Honesty can be hard. But honesty helps our hearts feel light. Can you feel the difference?”

BunBun pulled out the marble and placed it in Mama’s paw. BunBun listened to the heart. Thump. Thump. The thumps felt calm now, like soft drum beats.

“It feels better,” BunBun said. “My tummy is not twisty now.”

Mama hugged BunBun. “When we tell the truth, we fix mistakes. We can make a plan, too. What should we do with the marble?”

BunBun thought. “It belongs in the game tin,” BunBun said. “We keep the set together. We can make a little home for marbles, so they do not get lost. That is good organization.”

Mama smiled. “Wonderful idea.”

They found a tiny jar with a blue lid. BunBun washed it with warm water. Swish, swish. The jar clinked. They dried it with a towel. Pat, pat. BunBun put a label on it. The letters were big and clear: MARBLES.

“Now each marble has a home,” BunBun said proudly. “Keep, Share, Recycle, and Marble Home!”

Mama laughed. “You are a determined helper. You told the truth. You honored our traditions. And you made our closet neat.”

BunBun’s ears perked up again. The closet felt cozy and safe. The pine smell was soft and sweet now. The boxes did not whisper anymore. They sat nice and still.

“Can we share a tradition now?” BunBun asked. “Can we wear Grandma’s scarf and read a story?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “We will wear the scarf together. Then we will play the marble game. You may roll the blue sky marble.”

BunBun grinned. “Thank you for trusting me.”

As the rain tapped the window, tap-tap-tap, BunBun and Mama cuddled in the closet hideaway. The scarf was warm. The pages of the book rustled, shhh-shhh. When they played, the blue marble rolled in a happy circle. Click, click, click.

BunBun felt proud and light. “Honesty helps my heart,” BunBun said. “Traditions keep us close. Organization keeps things safe.”

Mama nodded. “Yes, little rabbit. When we are honest, we can fix mistakes. When we share our traditions, we feel love. When we organize, we find what we need.”

BunBun hugged Mama. “I like our closet hideaway,” BunBun said. “It is tidy. It is true. It is home.”

The End.
