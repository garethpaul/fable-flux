# Bubbles and Brave Feelings

In a cozy den, a friendly bear cub named Benny padded toward the shiny bathroom. The floor tiles were cool on his paws. Drip, drip, went the faucet. Benny liked the soft hum of the fan and the bright light from the little window. It smelled like soap and mint.

Today was bath day. Benny loved bubbles. He loved scooping them like fluffy clouds. He loved making tall, silly hats on his head. But today, Benny was not alone. His best friend, Dot the little ladybug, buzzed along beside him, landing on the mirror with a tiny tap.

“Hi, Dot!” Benny smiled, nose twitching. “Want to play bubble boats?”

Dot’s wings shimmered. “I do! But I am a little worried,” she said softly. “The bathtub is big. The water is big. I am small.”

Benny looked at the tub. It did look very big. He blinked. “I hear you,” he said in a gentle voice. “Big things can feel big in our tummies.”

Dot nodded and tucked her wings. “My tummy feels fluttery.”

Benny put his soft paw on his heart. “My tummy feels fluttery sometimes, too,” he said. “Let’s take a slow breath together.”

They breathed in… one, two, three. They breathed out… one, two, three. The bathroom was quiet and calm. Drip, drip.

“Thank you,” said Dot. “That helped.”

Benny turned the water knob just a little. The tub filled with warm water. Swish, swish. He squeezed bubble soap. Glop! White puffs grew and grew. The smell was sweet and clean. Benny giggled. “Look! Bubble mountain!”

Dot peered closer. “It is pretty. But I still feel nervous.”

Benny thought. He remembered when his brush felt scratchy, and he had told Mama. She listened, and they found a soft towel to hold. That helped.

“I have an idea,” Benny said. “Friends listen. Friends help. We can make the bath feel safe for you.”

“How?” Dot asked.

Benny pointed. “Let’s make a tiny pool for you in this cup. You can float on a leaf. We will take turns talking about our feelings. We can use calm voices. We can stop anytime. We can make a plan.”

Dot’s antennae perked up. “A plan sounds nice.”

Benny found a wide cup and placed a shiny green leaf inside. He dipped the cup and scooped warm, bubbly water. The leaf floated softly. Dot landed on the leaf. It wobbled, then steadied. The bubbles tickled her tiny feet. “Ooh! It feels like a soft pillow,” she said.

Benny smiled. “I will be right here,” he said. “If you feel too fluttery, say ‘pause,’ and I will pause.”

Dot breathed in and out. “I feel a little brave now.”

“Me too,” said Benny. “Sometimes I feel shy about water in my ears. When I feel shy, I tell Mama. She helps me tilt my head and pour slowly.” He showed Dot, tilting his head and pouring a small cup. Plip, plip. No splash.

Dot watched. “You showed me your feeling and your plan,” she said. “Thank you.”

Benny nodded. “Friends share. Friends care.”

They made bubble hats. Benny put one on his ears like soft earmuffs. Dot made a tiny bubble crown. They laughed. Their laughs echoed off the shiny tiles. The mirror fogged a little.

Suddenly, a bubble popped near Dot. Pop! Dot jumped. Her leaf bobbed.

“Pause,” Dot said.

Benny froze like a statue, then looked at Dot’s face. “Thank you for telling me,” he said. “What do you need?”

Dot touched her chest. “I need a slower splash and your calm voice.”

“You got it,” said Benny. He dipped his paw slowly, very slowly. Swish… swish… He spoke in a warm voice. “You are safe. I am here. We can stop whenever you want.”

Dot’s tummy felt softer. “I want to keep going,” she said. “I want to try a tiny wave.”

Benny gently moved the leaf. The leaf slid like a little boat. Dot spread her wings and felt the warm mist. “This is fun!” she chirped.

Soon, Benny washed behind his ears. He scrubbed his paws. Scrub, scrub, scrub. He hummed a happy tune. Dot buzzed a harmony. They counted bubbles together. “One, two, three, four!” They made bubble boats and watched them sail from the faucet to the drain, like a tiny parade.

When it was time to rinse, Benny felt his tummy flutter a little. He remembered Dot’s plan. “Dot,” he said, “I feel a little nervous about water on my face. Can you help me?”

Dot smiled. “Yes! I will say calm words. I will help you tilt. I will say ‘pause’ if you need it.”

Benny took a breath. Dot buzzed near his cheek and spoke gently. “You are safe. I am here.” Benny tipped his head. Plip, plip. The water ran down his chin. He giggled. “That tickles!”

They finished the bath, warm and clean. Benny wrapped in a soft towel. Fluff, fluff. Dot dried her wings on a tiny washcloth. The bathroom smelled like fresh rain and soap. The mirror shone again.

Benny looked at Dot. “We did it together,” he said.

Dot beamed. “We listened. We used calm words. We made a plan. That is friendship.”

Benny nodded. “Friendship helps our feelings feel brave,” he said. “When we talk and listen, we can solve problems.”

They bumped a gentle high-five—paw to wing. Then they sang a little song:

“Feelings are okay,
Friends help every day.
We breathe and share,
We show we care.”

Benny and Dot smiled. Their hearts felt warm, like the bath water. They knew that next time, they could do it again—together.

The End.
