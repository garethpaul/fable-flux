# Leo's Thank You Garden

Leo was a brave little lion cub with golden fur that sparkled in the morning sun. He lived with his mama in a cozy apartment with the most beautiful balcony garden. Every morning, Leo loved to step outside and feel the warm sunshine on his fuzzy face.

"Good morning, little garden!" Leo would roar softly, though it sounded more like a tiny purr.

The balcony garden was full of wonderful things. Red tomatoes hung like little lanterns, green lettuce leaves danced in the breeze, and purple flowers smiled up at the sky. Leo's mama had taught him that plants needed water, sunshine, and care to grow big and strong.

One sunny Tuesday morning, Leo noticed something special. "Mama, look!" he called excitedly. "The tomatoes are getting so red and round!"

Mama Lion smiled. "Yes, Leo. Our garden is giving us a gift. What do you think we should say?"

Leo thought for a moment, his little tail swishing. "Thank you, garden!"

"That's right, my brave cub," Mama said. "When someone gives us something nice, we say thank you."

Leo decided he wanted to take care of the garden all by himself that day. "Can I water the plants, Mama? Please?"

"Of course," Mama said, handing him a small yellow watering can. "But remember, not too much water. Just enough to make the soil happy."

Leo carefully lifted the watering can. It was a little heavy, but he was strong! He walked slowly to the tomato plants. The water made a gentle splashing sound as it poured onto the dark soil. 

"Thank you for growing food for us," Leo whispered to the tomatoes.

Next, he watered the lettuce. The green leaves seemed to sparkle with tiny water drops. "Thank you for being so crunchy and yummy," he said.

When Leo reached the purple flowers, he noticed a tiny bee buzzing around them. "Hello, little bee!" Leo said. "Thank you for helping our flowers grow!"

The bee buzzed happily and flew to another flower.

As Leo finished watering, he felt proud and happy. His chest puffed out like a big, brave lion. "I did it all by myself, Mama!"

"You certainly did," Mama said, giving him a warm hug. "You learned how to take care of our garden, and you remembered to say thank you. That makes my heart feel warm and fuzzy."

Leo looked around at all the plants glistening with water drops in the sunshine. The soil smelled rich and earthy. A gentle breeze made the leaves rustle like they were whispering "you're welcome."

"Mama," Leo said thoughtfully, "I want to say thank you to more things."

"What would you like to thank?" Mama asked.

Leo looked up at the bright blue sky. "Thank you, sunshine, for making our plants grow!" Then he looked at the soft white clouds. "Thank you, clouds, for bringing rain!" He even thanked the wind for being gentle with the flowers.

That evening, as they picked fresh lettuce for dinner, Leo felt grateful for everything around him. "Thank you, garden, for our yummy salad. Thank you, Mama, for teaching me. And thank you, me, for working so hard!"

Mama Lion laughed. "You learned something very important today, Leo. When we say thank you and take care of the things around us, it makes everything more beautiful."

Leo curled up that night feeling proud, grateful, and excited to care for his garden again tomorrow.

The End.
