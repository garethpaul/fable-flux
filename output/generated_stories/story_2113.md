# Ellie's Helpful Heart

The bathroom tiles felt cool under Ellie's small gray feet. The little elephant calf looked around at the mess. Toothpaste squeezed everywhere. Towels on the floor. Water puddles by the sink.

"Oh my!" said Mama Elephant, peeking through the doorway. "What happened here?"

Ellie's trunk drooped sadly. "My big brother Eddie was rushing to school. He made such a big mess!" She pointed with her tiny trunk. "Look at all this!"

Mama's kind eyes sparkled. "This does look like a job for someone special."

"Who?" asked Ellie, swishing her little tail.

"Someone who cares about helping others," Mama said softly. "Someone with a helpful heart."

Ellie thought hard. Her forehead wrinkled like a raisin. "But I'm too little! I can't reach the high shelves. My trunk is too short!"

"Hmm," said Mama. "Let's think about this together. What CAN you do?"

Ellie looked around carefully. She used her eyes like a detective. "I can pick up the towels!" she said, her voice getting excited. "My trunk is perfect for that!"

"Wonderful thinking!" cheered Mama.

Ellie wrapped her trunk around a fluffy blue towel. It felt soft and warm. She folded it carefully and placed it on the towel rack. "This is fun!" she giggled.

"What else do you notice?" asked Mama.

Ellie studied the bathroom like she was reading a picture book. "The soap is on the floor. I can put it back in the soap dish!"

"You're such a good helper," said Mama proudly.

Ellie picked up the slippery soap. It smelled like lavender flowers. She placed it gently in its special home. "Helping feels good in my tummy," she said, patting her round belly.

"That's your helpful heart growing bigger," explained Mama.

Next, Ellie spotted the toothpaste tube. "I can squeeze out the extra toothpaste and wipe it clean!" She used a washcloth to clean up the minty mess. The bathroom started to smell fresh and clean.

"Look how much you've accomplished!" said Mama. "You solved each problem by thinking carefully."

Ellie beamed with pride. Her ears flapped happily. "I used my thinking brain AND my helping hands!"

Just then, Eddie rushed back through the door. His backpack bounced on his shoulders. "Oh no! I forgot my lunch and— wow! The bathroom looks amazing!"

"Ellie cleaned it all up," said Mama. "She volunteered to help."

Eddie's eyes grew wide like dinner plates. "Really? Thank you, Ellie! You're the best little sister ever!" He gave her a gentle trunk hug.

Ellie's heart felt warm and fuzzy. "I liked helping! It made me feel important and happy."

"That's what volunteering means," explained Mama. "Choosing to help others because you care."

"Can I volunteer to help more things?" asked Ellie, bouncing on her toes.

"Anytime you want," said Mama. "Helpers are always needed."

Ellie looked at her reflection in the now-clean mirror. She saw a little elephant with bright, proud eyes. "I'm a volunteer!" she announced. "I use my thinking brain to solve problems and my helpful heart to care for others!"

From that day on, whenever Ellie saw someone who needed help, she remembered her bathroom adventure. She learned that being small didn't mean she couldn't make a big difference.

The End.
