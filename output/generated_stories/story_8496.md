# Ruby Rabbit's Art Problem

Ruby Rabbit sat on her soft blue bed in her cozy bedroom. Sunlight streamed through the window, making warm golden patches on the fluffy carpet. Ruby held her favorite paintbrush in her paw, but her whiskers drooped sadly.

"Oh no," Ruby whispered to herself. She looked at the messy painting on her easel. Red paint had dripped everywhere. The yellow sun looked like a wobbly egg. The green grass was all smudged and bumpy.

Ruby's bedroom was her special art space. Colorful crayons lined her wooden desk. Pretty pictures hung on the pale pink walls. But today, nothing was going right.

*Knock, knock, knock!* Someone tapped on her bedroom door.

"Come in," Ruby called softly.

Her best friend Benny Bear peeked his fuzzy brown head inside. "Hi Ruby! Want to play outside? The flowers smell so sweet today!"

Ruby quickly turned her easel away from Benny. She didn't want him to see her terrible painting. "Um, I'm busy," she said, looking down at her paws.

Benny's kind eyes noticed Ruby's sad face. He stepped into the cozy room. The wooden floor creaked gently under his feet. "Ruby, you look upset. What's wrong?"

Ruby's ears twitched nervously. She wanted to tell Benny the truth, but she felt embarrassed. "Nothing's wrong," she said quietly.

But Benny was a good friend. He sat on the soft carpet next to Ruby's bed. "I can see paint on your whiskers," he said gently. "And you have that worried look you get when something's bothering you."

Ruby touched her whiskers. Sure enough, they had tiny blue paint spots! She sighed a big, long sigh. "I was trying to paint a beautiful picture for my mama's birthday," she admitted. "But it looks awful! The colors are all mixed up and messy."

Benny's eyes lit up with understanding. "Can I see it?" he asked kindly.

Ruby hesitated, then slowly turned the easel around. The painting really was very messy. Paint had dripped down like colorful tears.

But Benny smiled his biggest, warmest smile. "Ruby, this is wonderful!" he exclaimed. "Look at all these amazing colors! And I can see a happy sun and pretty grass."

"Really?" Ruby asked, her ears perking up hopefully.

"Really!" Benny nodded. "But you know what? Art is even more fun when friends help each other. Can I paint with you?"

Ruby's heart felt warm and happy. "You want to help me?"

"Of course!" Benny said. "That's what friends do. We help each other, especially when things get tricky."

Ruby felt much better. Being honest with Benny had been the right choice. "Yes, please help me!" she said, bouncing a little on her bed.

Benny picked up a clean brush. "What if we add some purple flowers here?" he suggested, pointing to an empty spot.

"And maybe some orange butterflies!" Ruby added excitedly.

Together, they dipped their brushes in the bright paints. Ruby painted careful purple petals while Benny added green stems. The room filled with the fresh smell of paint and the sound of happy giggles.

"This is so much better than painting alone," Ruby said, her whiskers twitching with joy.

"Everything is better with friends," Benny agreed, adding tiny yellow dots to the butterfly wings.

When they finished, the painting looked beautiful. It was still a little messy, but now it was full of love and friendship too.

Ruby hugged Benny tight. "Thank you for helping me, and thank you for being such a good friend. I'm sorry I wasn't honest at first."

"That's okay," Benny said warmly. "Sometimes it's hard to ask for help. But I'm always here for you."

Ruby hung the painting on her bedroom wall, right next to her bed where she could see it every morning. It reminded her that being honest with friends and working together makes everything more wonderful.

The End.
