# Waddle, Wiggle, Zoom!

In a quiet house on a rainy day, Pip the cheerful penguin waddled down the creaky stairs to the basement lab. The lab smelled like lemon soap and warm cardboard. Pip loved the cool floor under his webbed feet and the soft hum of little machines. Strings of twinkly lights glowed like stars.

“Hello, lights!” Pip chirped. “Hello, tools!” He tapped a tiny hammer. Ting! It sang a small song.

Today was Move Day in the lab. Pip had a big poster that said: “Try All The Moves!” The poster showed hopping feet, stretching arms, and wiggly bodies. Pip clapped his flippers. “Movement helps my body and my brain,” he said. “Movement makes me ready!”

Pip’s friends arrived. Timo the snail slid in, leaving a shiny trail. “I move slow,” Timo whispered, smiling. “But I never stop.”

Bella the bunny bounced in, ears flipping. “Boing! I move fast!” she giggled.

Zara the zebra trotted carefully, hooves going clip-clop. She wore a small helmet. “I like safe steps,” she said.

In the corner, Pip’s kind grown-up helper, Dr. Dot, waved. “Welcome, movers! Today we will make a Move Machine. It will go from this table to that basket. We must plan and prepare.”

Pip puffed with pride. “I can help! I am good at waddles and wiggles.”

Dr. Dot nodded. “First, let’s get ready. Goggles on. Floor clear. Water bottles filled. Bodies warmed up.”

They did a warm-up. Pip stretched his flippers wide. “Reach for the stars!” he sang.

Bella hopped in place, boing, boing. Zara lifted her knees slowly, steady-steady. Timo made tiny slides, shoop, shoop. Everyone’s bodies felt warm and awake.

Dr. Dot rolled out a big blue mat. “Here is the plan,” she said. “We need to move a red ball across the lab. It must go up the ramp, around the bend, and into the basket. How can we do it?”

Pip looked at the tools: tubes, blocks, scarves, and a fan that went whirr. He tapped his beak, thinking. “We can push, pull, and roll,” he said.

“Let’s try!” Bella cheered. “I can bounce and push it fast!”

Bella pushed the red ball. Zoom! It rolled, but it zoomed past the ramp and bumped the wall. Thump.

“Oops,” Bella said. Her nose twitched. “Too fast.”

Pip waddled over and patted the ball. “It’s okay. We can try again.”

Timo looked at the ramp. “I can guide it s-s-slowly,” he said. “I am careful.”

“Great idea,” Pip said. “Slow can be smart.”

They prepared the ramp with blocks on the sides. Zara tested the bend. Clip-clop, she checked each step. “The bend is wobbly,” she said. “We need more support.” She slid a box under it. “There. Safe and steady.”

Dr. Dot smiled. “You are cooperating. You are using your moves and your minds.”

Pip felt warm and brave. He waddled to the fan and turned it on low. Whirr. The air felt cool on his feathers. “The fan can help the ball go up,” he said.

“Test time,” Dr. Dot said. “Bella, push gently. Timo, guide. Zara, steady the bend. Pip, wiggle the fan when the ball slows.”

Bella crouched. “Ready?” she asked.

“Ready!” everyone said.

Bella pushed with a soft boing. The ball rolled. Timo slid next to it. “This way, little ball,” he whispered. Shoop. Shoop. Zara held the bend with her strong legs. Clip-clop. Pip watched the ball slow, then wiggled the fan. Whirr-wiggle! The breeze kissed the ball. The ball rolled up the ramp. It turned the bend. It wobbled toward the basket.

“Almost!” Pip cried. He waddled fast, flippers out. “I can do a penguin push!” He gave the ball a tiny, gentle nudge with his tummy. Bump—plop! The ball fell into the basket.

They all cheered. “We did it!” Bella danced. Zara smiled big. Timo sparkled in the light.

Dr. Dot clapped. “Team, your moves were different and perfect. Fast hops. Slow slides. Steady steps. Wiggly winds. And a penguin push!”

Pip’s chest felt big and bright. “We moved our bodies. We prepared. We cooperated,” he said, counting on his flippers. “And we did it together.”

Dr. Dot nodded. “Let’s try a bigger challenge. Can we make the ball ring the bell before it lands?”

Pip took a deep breath. He loved the sweet metal ding of the small silver bell. “We can,” he said. “Let’s get ready again.”

They checked the floor. Clear. They checked the tools. Ready. Pip added a scarf tunnel that felt soft like clouds. Bella brought a tiny ramp. Zara balanced the bell on a safe stand. Timo placed a guide strip, smooth and shiny.

“Warm-up moves again!” Pip sang. “Wiggle, jiggle, stretch, and giggle!”

They moved and laughed. Then they tried the path. Bella’s gentle boing sent the ball rolling. Timo guided. Zara steadied. Pip wiggled the fan. The ball slid through the soft scarf tunnel—whoosh! It touched the bell—ding!—and plopped into the basket.

They all hugged. Pip’s feathers rubbed soft on his friends’ fur and shell. The lab smelled like happy lemon and a little like fresh air from the fan.

Dr. Dot said, “What did we learn?”

Pip smiled. “Moving makes us ready and strong. Preparing keeps us safe. Cooperating helps us do big things. And our different moves make our team great.”

“Yes,” said Dr. Dot. “Your movements matter. Your differences shine.”

Pip waddled in a little circle, proud and cheerful. “I am a penguin. I waddle and wiggle. That is my way. And that is okay!”

Everyone cheered. “That is more than okay. That is wonderful!”

They cleaned up together, humming a happy tune. The twinkly lights blinked like stars saying, “Well done.”

The End.
