# Rosie Rabbit's Rainbow Veggie Art

Rosie Rabbit hopped into the bright, colorful art room at Sunny Meadow School. Her fluffy white ears twitched with excitement as she looked around at all the paintbrushes, crayons, and paper scattered on the tables.

"Good morning, little artists!" called Mrs. Bear, the art teacher. "Today we're going to paint pictures of our favorite healthy foods!"

Rosie's nose wiggled nervously. She loved to paint, but she had a big problem. "I don't like vegetables," she whispered to herself. "They taste yucky and look boring. How can I paint something I don't like?"

All around her, the other animals were already getting started. Freddy Fox was painting bright orange carrots. Sally Squirrel drew leafy green lettuce. But Rosie just sat there, staring at her blank white paper.

"What's wrong, Rosie?" asked Mrs. Bear gently, sitting down beside her.

"I don't want to paint vegetables," Rosie said softly. "I only like cookies and candy. Vegetables are gross."

Mrs. Bear smiled warmly. "You know what, Rosie? Let me show you something special." She walked over to a big basket covered with a colorful cloth. "Close your eyes and take three deep breaths with me."

Rosie breathed in slowly... and out slowly... three times. She felt calmer already.

"Now open your eyes!" Mrs. Bear pulled away the cloth, revealing the most beautiful vegetables Rosie had ever seen. There were purple eggplants that shimmered like jewels, bright red tomatoes that looked like little suns, and yellow corn that sparkled like golden treasure.

"Wow!" gasped Rosie. "They're so pretty! They look like rainbow colors!"

"That's right," said Mrs. Bear. "And here's the best part - these beautiful colors mean the vegetables are full of special powers that help you grow strong and healthy. Red tomatoes help your heart stay strong. Orange carrots help your eyes see better. Green broccoli helps your muscles grow big!"

Rosie's ears perked up with interest. "Really? Like superhero powers?"

"Exactly like superhero powers!" laughed Mrs. Bear. "And the more colors you eat, the more powers you get!"

Rosie picked up her paintbrush with determination. She had a plan now! First, she painted a big red tomato and imagined her heart getting stronger. Then she added orange carrots for super-sight. She painted green broccoli trees for strong muscles, and purple grapes for a smart brain.

"Look at my rainbow power plate!" Rosie announced proudly, holding up her colorful painting.

"That's wonderful, Rosie!" said Mrs. Bear. "Would you like to try tasting some of these rainbow vegetables for snack time?"

Rosie thought about her painting and all the superhero powers waiting inside those colorful foods. "Yes, please! I want to be strong like my painting!"

She tried a cherry tomato first - it was sweet and juicy! Then a baby carrot - it was crunchy and fun to eat! Each bite made her feel like she was getting special powers.

"You know what?" Rosie said to her friends as they munched their healthy snacks. "Eating vegetables is like eating a rainbow! And rainbows taste much better than I thought!"

From that day on, Rosie always remembered to eat her rainbow colors every day, and she grew up to be the strongest, healthiest rabbit in all of Sunny Meadow.

The End.
