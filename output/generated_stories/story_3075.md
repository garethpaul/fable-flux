# Nutkin's Helpful Heart

Nutkin the squirrel lived in a cozy oak tree next to Mrs. Chen's house. One sunny morning, he heard voices coming from the guest room window. His fluffy tail twitched with curiosity as he peeked inside.

"Oh my!" said Mrs. Chen, looking worried. "My sister arrives tonight, and this room is such a mess! I'll never get it ready in time."

Nutkin's whiskers wiggled as he thought. He loved helping others - it made his heart feel warm and fuzzy, like when he curled up in his nest on cold days. "I want to help!" he chittered softly to himself.

But first, Nutkin took three deep breaths, just like his mama taught him. "When you want to help," she always said, "think first, then act." He looked around the messy room with his bright, shiny eyes.

There were pillows scattered on the floor, books piled everywhere, and dust bunnies hiding under the bed. Nutkin's nose twitched at the dusty smell.

"Mrs. Chen!" Nutkin called out politely from the windowsill. "May I please help you clean?"

Mrs. Chen looked surprised but smiled. "Why, hello there, little squirrel! That's very kind of you to offer. Can you follow directions carefully?"

"Yes, ma'am!" Nutkin nodded his furry head eagerly. "I'm good at listening!"

"Wonderful! First, could you gather all the pillows and put them on the bed?"

Nutkin scampered down and began collecting the soft, squishy pillows. Some were big and some were small. He used his strong paws to carry them one by one. When he felt like rushing, he reminded himself to slow down and be careful.

"Excellent work!" Mrs. Chen praised. "Now, can you help me sort these books? Put the big ones here and the little ones there."

Nutkin loved this job! He picked up each book with his tiny paws, feeling their smooth covers and rough pages. Some books smelled like flowers, others like adventure stories. He sorted them carefully, making neat piles.

Just then, Nutkin's friend Ruby the robin flew to the window. "Nutkin, come play! Why are you working?"

Nutkin paused. Playing sounded fun, but he looked at Mrs. Chen, who was working so hard. "I promised to help," he said kindly but firmly. "Helping others feels really good! Want to join us?"

Ruby tilted her red head. "I've never tried volunteering before."

"It's when you help someone without getting anything back, except good feelings in your heart," Nutkin explained.

Ruby decided to try! She used her beak to pick up small pieces of paper while Nutkin swept dust bunnies with his bushy tail. The room filled with the fresh scent of cleaning spray and the sound of their cheerful chatter.

When they finished, the guest room sparkled! The bed had fluffy pillows, the books stood in neat rows, and everything smelled clean and fresh.

"Thank you both so much!" Mrs. Chen beamed. "You followed directions perfectly and worked as a wonderful team!"

Nutkin's chest puffed with pride. Ruby chirped happily, "My heart feels all warm and fluttery! I love volunteering!"

That evening, when Mrs. Chen's sister arrived, she gasped with delight. "What a beautiful, welcoming room!"

From his tree, Nutkin smiled. Helping others wasn't just about cleaning - it was about caring, sharing, and making the world a little brighter. He couldn't wait to volunteer again tomorrow!

The End.
