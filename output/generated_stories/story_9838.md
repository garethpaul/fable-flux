# Poppy Parrot’s Practice Plan

Poppy was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of blue on her wings. She loved to paint swishy, swirly pictures in the creative art room. The room smelled like lemon soap and crayons. Sunlight came in the big window and made the glitter jars sparkle. Paintbrushes stood in cups like little trees, and soft mats covered the floor.

One morning, Poppy hopped onto a stool and fluffed her feathers. “Good morning, colors!” she chirped. “Today I will paint and then play ball!” She wiggled with excitement and tapped a tiny drum with her beak. Tap-tap! Tap-tap!

Her friend, Coach Turtle, peeked in. He wore a small whistle and a friendly smile. “Hello, Poppy,” he said slowly. “Are you ready for Sports Time later?”

“Yes! I will paint first,” Poppy said. She grabbed a big brush. Swoosh! She painted fast, splashing red, yellow, and blue. Drops dotted the mat. Her wings flapped with happy flutters.

Coach Turtle watched. “Remember our routine,” he said kindly. “We paint, we tidy, we snack, we warm up, and then we play. Routine keeps us safe.”

Poppy nodded, but she wanted to paint more. “Just one more swoosh!” she chirped. Swoosh! Swoosh! The colors looked like a rainbow swing.

Soon it was time for Sports Time. Poppy looked at the messy table. Brushes lay sticky. Water cups were wobbly. Glitter lids were open, and sparkles twinkled on the floor like tiny stars.

“I will tidy later,” Poppy said. “I want to play now!”

She zipped to the soft mats with a blue ball. “Ready, set, bounce!” she sang. She bounced the ball high. Boing! Boing! But the ball rolled over a glitter spill. It slipped and slid. Poppy hopped after it, but her foot touched a wet paint dot.

“Eep!” she squawked. She wobbled, flapped, and sat down with a soft plop. The ball bumped the water cup. Slosh! Water splashed onto the floor.

Coach Turtle hurried over. “Are you okay, Poppy?”

Poppy rubbed her wing. “I’m okay,” she said, a little shaky. “But the floor is slippery. I forgot our routine.”

Coach Turtle nodded. His voice was kind. “Routine is our friend. It helps us have fun and stay safe. We paint, we tidy, we snack, we warm up, and then we play.”

Poppy took a deep breath. She smelled the sweet fruit snack on the counter. Her tummy gave a tiny rumble. “Let’s fix it,” she said. “I want to be safe.”

Together they made a plan. Poppy called it her Practice Plan.

“Step one: Tidy Time!” Poppy chirped.

She put lids on the glitter. Twist, click! She washed the brushes. Swish, swish! The water turned purple, then clear. She wiped the table with a soft cloth. Wipe, wipe! The floor went from sticky to dry. Coach Turtle put out a little sign: “Drying—Walk, Don’t Run.”

“Step two: Snack Time,” Poppy said. She perched on her stool and ate apple slices. Crunch! Crunch! She drank water. Sip, sip! She felt calm and strong.

“Step three: Warm-Up Time,” said Coach Turtle. He showed her gentle moves. “Wiggle your toes. Stretch your wings. Breathe in… and out.” Poppy followed. Her feathers smoothed. Her heart felt steady.

“Step four: Play Time!” Poppy cheered.

She picked up the blue ball again. This time, the floor was clean and dry. Poppy looked left and right. “Safe space,” she said. She stood behind the line Coach Turtle taped to the mat.

“Ready?” he asked.

“Ready!” Poppy smiled. She bounced the ball. Boing! She caught it. Thump! She rolled it to the goal. Roll, roll, roll. She kicked with a gentle tap. Tap! The ball slid into the soft net. Swoosh!

“I did it!” Poppy laughed. She hopped in a small happy circle. Coach Turtle clapped. “Great job! That’s what routine does. It helps our bodies and our brains.”

Poppy felt proud and warm inside. “Routine helps me be safe and have fun,” she said. “I will make a chart!”

She took a big sheet of paper and bright markers. She drew five boxes.

Box one showed a brush and a table: Paint.
Box two showed a broom and a cloth: Tidy.
Box three showed an apple and a cup: Snack.
Box four showed a stretching parrot: Warm Up.
Box five showed a ball and a smile: Play.

Poppy hung the chart by the window. The sunlight made the colors glow.

The next day, Poppy followed her Practice Plan. She painted a sunny sky. She tidied with little hums. She crunched her snack. She stretched her wings. Then she played with the blue ball, safe and happy. Boing! Swoosh! Giggle!

Other friends came to see. “Can we use the plan too?” asked Bunny and Bear.

“Yes!” Poppy said. “Routine is for everyone. It is our safety friend.”

They all took turns. They all followed the steps. No slips. No spills. Only smiles and cheers.

That night, Poppy curled her wings and whispered, “Thank you, Routine.” The art room was neat. The air smelled fresh. The glitter jars were closed, and the mats were dry. Poppy’s heart felt calm.

The clear lesson shone in her mind: When we follow our routine—paint, tidy, snack, warm up, and play—we stay safe, we grow strong, and we have more fun.

The End.
