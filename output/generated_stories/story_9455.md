# Polly Plans Her Snack

Polly was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of blue on her tail. She loved to sit on the back of a comfy chair in the home office. The home office smelled like fresh paper and warm tea. The soft hum of the computer filled the room. Sunlight made little squares on the floor.

“Squawk! I have a big idea!” Polly chirped, tapping the desk with her beak. “I want a tasty, healthy snack.”

On the desk sat a small bowl of crunchy carrots, a banana, and a jar of cookies. The jar had a shiny lid that went clink when it moved. A notebook lay open with a pencil. Polly liked to draw plans in the notebook. She was not just a parrot. She was a planner parrot!

Polly fluffed her feathers. “My tummy feels empty. I want a snack that helps me think and play,” she said. “How do I choose?”

She looked at the bowl of carrots. “Crunch, crunch,” Polly whispered. “Carrots are orange. They help my body.” She sniffed the banana. It smelled sweet like sunshine. She peered at the cookie jar. The cookies smelled sugary and cozy.

Polly smiled. “I will use a plan. I will use my brain. I will use logical thinking!” She picked up the pencil with her claw and gently pushed it with her beak. “Step one: What do I need?” Polly said aloud.

From the hallway, Max, a kind kid, peeked in. “Hi, Polly,” Max said. “Do you need help?”

Polly nodded. “Yes, please, Max. I feel hungry. I want a snack that helps me grow. What should I pick?”

Max came in and sat on the floor. The carpet felt soft and fuzzy. He said, “Let’s think. Your tummy needs good food. Good food helps your brain and body.”

“Squawk,” Polly said, “I like cookies. But if I eat cookies first, will I feel good?”

Max smiled. “Maybe for a moment. But carrots and banana give strong energy. They are good fuel.”

Polly tapped the notebook. “Step two: What are my choices?” She drew three little pictures: a carrot, a banana, and a cookie. “Step three: What happens if I pick each one?”

She pointed to the carrot picture. “If I pick carrots, I will crunch and chomp. My body will say thank you.”

She pointed to the banana picture. “If I pick banana, I will peel and munch. My brain will feel bright.”

She pointed to the cookie picture. “If I pick cookies now, I might feel sleepy later.”

Polly looked at Max. “I want to do what is best for me. I can speak up for me. I can say what I need.” She puffed her chest. “I choose banana and carrots first. Then, later, maybe one cookie.”

Max clapped softly. “That is self-advocacy, Polly. You used your voice. You made a smart plan.”

Polly hopped to the bowl. Crunch! The carrot snapped. It tasted cool and crisp. Peel, peel went the banana. It felt smooth and soft. Munch, munch. The sweet banana made Polly’s mouth smile.

“Squawk, this is yummy,” Polly said. “My tummy feels happy.”

They listened to the gentle tick of the clock. The computer hummed like a quiet bee. The sunlight felt warm on their feathers and skin.

After the snack, Polly looked at the notebook again. “Step four: Check my plan. Did it work?”

“How do you feel?” Max asked.

Polly spread her wings. “I feel strong. I feel bright. I can think and play.”

Max nodded. “The plan worked.”

Polly blinked at the cookie jar. The shiny lid winked back. She tilted her head. “I still want a cookie. Can I have one later?”

Max said, “Yes. After a little play and some water, you can have one cookie.”

Polly took a sip from her water cup. “Sip, sip,” she said. “Water helps too.”

She hopped to the window and watched a fluffy cloud drift by. “Max, can we draw a chart? I like charts. They help me see my plan.”

Max grabbed colored crayons. Together they drew a simple chart in the notebook:
- Picture of carrots: “Good for body.”
- Picture of banana: “Good for brain.”
- Picture of cookie: “Good for a small treat.”

Polly tapped the chart. “Now I know. This is knowledge. I can use it again.”

They played a little game. Max hid a small toy under a cup. “Where is the toy?” he asked.

Polly watched carefully. She looked left, then right. “Hmm,” she hummed. “I will use logic. I saw your hand move to the middle cup.” She pecked the middle cup. “Ta-da!”

Max laughed. “You used your thinking steps!”

Polly felt proud. “When I stop and think, I can choose well. I can speak up for me. I can use new facts.”

Later, Max opened the cookie jar. Clink! He gave Polly one small cookie. It tasted warm and sweet. “Thank you,” Polly said.

Max smiled. “Thank you for making a smart plan.”

Polly cuddled into the soft chair. The room was calm and cozy. She whispered, “Step five: Share the lesson.” She looked at Max and said, “Think first. Choose what helps. Use your voice. Then enjoy your treat.”

Polly closed her eyes, happy and proud. She knew she could use her logical steps any time she needed to solve a problem.

The End.
