# Miko Listens and Scores

Miko was a courageous monkey with bright, curious eyes. He loved swinging, painting, and trying new things. Today, he held a little paintbrush in his tail and a rolling pin in his hands. He stepped into the creative art room at Banana Grove School.

The art room smelled like fresh paper and orange peel glue. Colorful paint dots danced on the big tables. Soft music hummed from a tiny speaker. Brushes clinked in jars. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor.

Coach Tia, a friendly turtle with a whistle, waved. “Welcome, team! Today we make art, and then we play a gentle ball game right here. It’s an art-sports day!”

Miko bounced. “I’m ready! I can do it all!”

Coach Tia tapped the table with her flipper. “First, listen to directions. Listening helps us work together. Step one: paint a big circle. Step two: cut it out. Step three: tape it to a stick. We will use these as goal signs for our ball game.”

Miko’s tail swished. He heard “paint” and “goal” and felt too excited to hear the rest. “Got it!” he said quickly. He dipped his brush and splashed red everywhere. “Look, Coach! Big circle!”

Plip! Plop! Red dots landed on the floor and on Miko’s ear. His circle was wobbly, and he forgot to wait for it to dry. He tried to cut it, and the wet paper tore.

“Oh no,” Miko whispered. His ears warmed. “I wanted to do it fast.”

Next to him, Lila the ladybug listened very carefully. She painted a smooth blue circle. She waited. She hummed softly. Then she cut neatly and taped it to a stick.

Coach Tia smiled at both of them. “Remember, brave Miko, courage also means slowing down to listen.”

Miko looked at his torn paper. His tail drooped. “I didn’t listen all the way. What do I do now?”

Coach Tia pointed to a drying rack. “Strategy time. Step back. Breathe. Listen again. What were the three steps?”

Miko took a deep breath. In. Out. He repeated, “Paint. Dry. Cut. Then tape.” He grinned a tiny grin. “I will try again.”

This time Miko dipped his brush gently. Swish, swish. He made a round, bright yellow circle. He set it on the rack. “I will wait,” he told himself. He counted the drum beats in the soft song. “One, two, three, four.”

While waiting, he used his resourcefulness. He didn’t want to waste his torn red paper. He cut the torn parts into small stars. He glued the stars onto the table sign that said Team Sun. “These can cheer for us later,” he said.

“Smart thinking,” said Lila. “You turned a mistake into decorations!”

When Miko’s yellow circle was dry, he cut it carefully. Snip, snip. He taped it to a stick. It looked bright like a sunrise. He smiled widely.

“Now the game,” Coach Tia announced. She rolled out soft foam balls. She placed Miko’s yellow circle and Lila’s blue circle on stands across the room. “We roll the ball to the goal sign. Gentle rolls, good aim. First, listen for your turn.”

Miko loved sports. He also loved to go fast. But he remembered: listen first. He stood still. He watched Coach Tia show the stance. “Feet steady. Eyes on the sign. Gentle roll.”

When Lila went first, Miko clapped softly. “Go, Lila, go!” Lila rolled. The ball kissed the floor with a soft bump-bump and tapped the blue circle. “Yay!” everyone cheered.

Now it was Miko’s turn. He felt brave and a little wiggly. He whispered, “Feet steady. Eyes on the sign. Gentle roll.” He listened to his own helpful words.

He rolled the ball. It made a gentle whirr across the smooth floor and tapped his yellow circle with a happy thup. Miko’s heart felt like sunshine. “I did it!”

They played again, but then a problem popped up. The tape on Team Sun’s sign peeled. The sign drooped and fell over.

“Oh no!” said the class.

Miko crouched down and listened to the small sounds. The sign whispered shhh against the floor. The tape made a sticky rip. Coach Tia began, “We need a quick fix.”

Miko raised his hand. “I have a plan. I listened to how the tape sounds. It’s not sticky now. But the glue smells fresh. We can glue the stick to a paint cup and make a stand.”

Coach Tia nodded. “That is smart strategy and resourcefulness. Let’s try.”

Miko shared his glue and gave Lila some of his red star scraps to decorate the base. “You can have these,” he said. “They will make the stand special.”

Together they pressed, held, and counted. “One, two, three, four.” The glue set. The sign stood tall again.

“Teamwork!” shouted Lila.

They finished the game with gentle rolls and big smiles. Coach Tia blew her whistle softly. “Listen up, team. What helped us today?”

Miko raised his hand again. “Listening helped us. When I listened to directions, I made a better sign. When I listened to the sounds, I found a way to fix the problem. And when I listened to my friends, I felt brave and kind.”

Coach Tia winked. “That is the winning score.”

Miko packed his brush and patted the warm table. He felt proud. He had been courageous, not just in his body, but also in his ears and heart.

He whispered, “I can be brave by listening.”

The End.
