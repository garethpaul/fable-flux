# Polly's Big Voice

In a busy basement filled with boxes, tools, and treasures, lived a bright green parrot named Polly. The basement was always buzzing with activity - mice scurrying between cardboard boxes, spiders spinning webs in corners, and crickets chirping their tiny songs.

Polly loved to tell stories. She would perch on her favorite wooden crate and create magical tales about flying dragons, dancing butterflies, and singing flowers. Her colorful feathers would ruffle with excitement as she acted out each character with different voices.

"Once upon a time," Polly would begin, spreading her beautiful wings wide, "there was a brave little mouse who could fly!"

But whenever Polly started her stories, the other basement creatures were too busy to listen. The mice would squeak, "Not now, Polly! We're looking for cheese!" The spiders would call out, "Too busy spinning webs!" And the crickets would just chirp louder, drowning out her voice.

Poor Polly felt sad and small. Her stories felt stuck inside her chest like colorful bubbles that couldn't pop. She tucked her head under her wing and whispered, "Maybe my stories aren't important."

One rainy morning, Polly heard tiny crying sounds coming from behind a big cardboard box. She hopped down from her perch, her claws clicking on the concrete floor, and discovered three baby mice who were lost and scared.

"We can't find our mama!" squeaked the smallest mouse, tears rolling down his fuzzy cheeks.

"The basement is so big and confusing!" cried another.

Polly's heart felt warm and full. She knew exactly how to help! She puffed up her chest, stood tall on her orange feet, and said in a clear, strong voice, "I have an idea! Let me tell you a story that will help you find your way home."

"But we don't have time for stories!" said the first baby mouse.

"This story is special," Polly said firmly but kindly. "It's about three brave mice who used their noses to follow the smell of their mama's cooking all the way home."

As Polly told her story, she used her wings to point in different directions and made sniffing sounds. "Sniff, sniff! Can you smell something yummy?" she asked the baby mice.

The little mice began to sniff the air. Their tiny noses twitched as they caught the scent of their mama's fresh bread baking upstairs.

"I smell it!" squeaked one mouse excitedly.

"Me too!" said another.

Polly's story had given them exactly what they needed. She walked with them, continuing her tale until they found their mama mouse, who hugged them tight with her tiny paws.

"Thank you, Polly!" called the mama mouse. "Your story saved the day!"

From that day on, Polly spoke up whenever she had something important to share. She learned that her voice mattered and her stories could help others. The basement creatures began to gather around her wooden crate every evening, eager to hear her wonderful tales.

"My stories are important," Polly said proudly, her green feathers shining in the basement light. "And when I use my voice, I can help my friends!"

And whenever Polly felt unsure about speaking up, she remembered the three little mice and how her brave voice had helped them find their way home.

The End.
