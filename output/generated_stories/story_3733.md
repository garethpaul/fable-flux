# The Song in the Basement Lab

In a quiet house on Maple Street, there was a cozy basement lab. It smelled like warm toast and oranges because Mrs. Lark, the scientist, liked to snack while she worked. The basement lab had soft humming machines, bright buttons, and a green mat on the floor that felt bouncy like moss.

A tiny singing bird named Pico lived there. Pico was blue and gold, with bright eyes and a sweet, sweet voice. He loved to practice songs while Mrs. Lark measured things and wrote notes. Pico liked to pretend the green mat was a sports field. He would roll a soft foam ball with his feet and sing, “Kick and stop, listen and hop!”

One morning, Mrs. Lark said, “Pico, I’m testing a gentle wind machine. Please listen carefully. When I say ‘stop,’ you stop. When I say ‘go,’ you can play.”

Pico puffed his chest. “I love listening! I can listen and sing!” he chirped.

The wind machine hummed. A soft breeze brushed Pico’s feathers. It felt tickly, like a whisper. Pico giggled and sang louder. “Whoooosh, whoooosh, breezy breeze!”

Mrs. Lark smiled and held up a red card. “Red means stop, Pico.”

But Pico was busy rolling his foam ball. He scored a pretend goal on the green mat and shouted, “Goal! Goal!” He liked sports so much that he didn’t look. He didn’t listen. He didn’t see the red card. He only heard his own song.

“Pico,” Mrs. Lark said gently, “pause, please.” Her voice was calm and soft.

The wind machine beeped. Pico didn’t hear it. He swung his wing and bumped a tray of shiny bolts. Clink! Clank! Clatter! The tray slid and bumped a little tower of blocks Mrs. Lark had stacked to test the wind. The blocks toppled—thunk, thunk, thunk—onto the mat.

Pico froze. His feathers trembled. He peeped, “Oh.”

Mrs. Lark walked over and knelt down. She put a hand near Pico, not touching, just close, like a warm hug made of air. “Pico,” she said, “we had a rule. We use our eyes and ears. We listen for stop and go. The lab needs careful listening to be safe. That is fair.”

Pico’s eyes filled with tiny tears. He whispered, “I didn’t listen. I only sang. I only shouted ‘Goal!’ I’m sorry.”

Mrs. Lark nodded. “Thank you for your apology. Saying sorry is the first step. Next, we make it right. That is justice—fixing the mix-up in a fair way.”

Pico blinked. “I can help?”

“You can,” Mrs. Lark said. She handed Pico a tiny soft brush. “We will put the bolts back together. Then we will try again. We will practice listening like a team.”

Pico took a deep breath. He listened to the hum. He listened to Mrs. Lark’s gentle breathing. He listened to the tiny clinks as they sorted bolts. He listened to his own heartbeat, thump-thump, slow and steady.

When the lab was tidy again, Mrs. Lark smiled. “Let’s play a listening game,” she said. “It’s like sports. Red is stop. Green is go. When I say ‘go,’ you roll the foam ball to the blue tape line. When I say ‘stop,’ you freeze.”

Pico nodded. “I’m ready.”

Mrs. Lark held up the green card. “Go.”

Pico rolled the ball. His feet were light. His ears were open. The ball made a soft sound, shh, shh, over the mat.

“Stop,” Mrs. Lark said.

Pico froze. He stood still like a statue. The wind machine hummed. He felt proud and warm inside.

“Go,” she said again.

Pico rolled the ball to the blue tape line and tapped it gently. Tap!

“Goal,” he chirped, but softly this time. He kept his eyes on Mrs. Lark’s cards. He kept his ears open.

They played again and again. Sometimes Pico wanted to sing loud, but he reminded himself, “Listen first.” He sang a new song in a quiet voice: “Listen, look, stop and go. That’s how little players grow.”

Mrs. Lark clapped. “Excellent listening, Pico! You used your ears and your eyes. That keeps the lab safe. That is fair to everyone. That is what justice looks like here.”

Pico fluffed his feathers. “I like fair,” he said. “I like making things right. I like this listening sport!”

They took a water break. Pico sipped a tiny cap of water. It tasted cool and sweet. He could smell oranges again. His tummy felt calm.

“Pico,” Mrs. Lark said, “what will you do next time?”

Pico thought. He put a wing to his cheek. “First, I will listen. If I forget and make a mistake, I will say sorry. Then I will help fix it. That is fair. That is just.”

Mrs. Lark nodded. “That’s a good plan.”

Pico looked at the green mat. He looked at the foam ball. He looked at the red and green cards. “Can we play one more round?”

Mrs. Lark laughed softly. “Yes. Ready?”

“Ready,” Pico chirped.

They played. Pico listened. He stopped and started like a tiny pro. The wind machine whooshed gently. The buttons blinked. The basement lab felt safe and happy again.

At the end, Pico sang a bright, clear song: “Listen with your ears, listen with your heart. Say sorry, make it right, that’s how teams start.”

Mrs. Lark patted the mat. “Well done, teammate.”

Pico hopped onto her shoulder and nuzzled her ear. He whispered, “Thank you for teaching me.”

“Thank you for listening,” she said.

The lesson was simple and strong: When we listen first, we play better, we stay safe, and we treat each other fairly. And if we make a mistake, we can say sorry and help fix it.

The End.
