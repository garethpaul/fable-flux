# Kiko Koala’s Kind Morning

Kiko the calm koala woke up to warm sunlight. The sunny backyard smelled like fresh leaves and soft earth. Birds chirped, “cheep-cheep,” and the breeze felt cool on Kiko’s fuzzy ears.

Kiko had a gentle smile. “Good morning,” Kiko said to the sun. “Time for my morning routine.”

Kiko’s routine was simple and kind:
- Stretch and breathe.
- Tidy and sort.
- Craft and share.

Kiko stretched arms up, breathed in slowly, and made a happy “mmm.” The grass tickled Kiko’s toes. It was time for tidy and sort.

Under the big shade tree stood three little baskets. One basket said, “Reuse.” One said, “Recycle.” One said, “Compost.” Kiko liked the baskets. They helped the backyard stay clean and green.

Kiko picked up a crinkly paper cup. “Recycle,” Kiko said, and dropped it in. Plop! Kiko found a soft leaf. “Compost,” Kiko said. Whisper. Kiko found a shiny ribbon from yesterday’s picnic. “Reuse,” Kiko said, smiling. “This will be perfect for crafting.”

Just then, friends came through the gate. Tavi the tiny turtle rolled in with a slow “tap-tap.” Miri the magpie fluttered down with a “flap-flap.” Zuzu the zebra moth drifted like a little cloud.

“Hello, friends,” Kiko said in a calm voice.

“Hello!” chirped Miri. “What’s the plan?”

“Our routine,” said Kiko. “Stretch and breathe, tidy and sort, craft and share.”

Everyone stretched. Everyone breathed. The backyard felt peaceful.

Then they began tidy and sort. Tavi nudged a bottle cap. “Recycle,” he said softly. Miri picked up a long stick. “Reuse,” she sang. Zuzu brushed crumbs into the compost bin. “For the worms,” she hummed.

Suddenly, they heard a sniffle. Luna, a little lamb, stood near the gate. Her wool was fluffy, and her eyes were shiny with tears.

“What’s wrong, Luna?” asked Kiko, kneeling to be eye level.

“I want to help,” Luna said, “but I don’t know your routine. I am new here.” Her voice shook. “Is there a job for me?”

Kiko’s heart felt warm. “Yes,” Kiko said calmly. “Our routine is for everyone. We include all friends.”

Miri nodded. “We can show you.”

Zuzu fluttered close. “We will go slow.”

Kiko smiled. “Let’s do it together. First, we stretch and breathe.” They all reached to the sky. Luna reached, too. “Now we tidy and sort. I will say the words, and you can try.”

Kiko held up a banana peel. “Compost.” Plop.

Tavi pushed a tin can. “Recycle.” Clink.

Miri showed a clean jar. “Reuse,” she sang. “We can make a craft!”

Luna sniffled less. “I think I can do it,” she said. She picked up a paper bag. “Reuse?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Kiko. “Great thinking, Luna.”

They finished sorting. The yard looked bright and neat. The breeze whispered in the leaves. It was time for craft and share.

Kiko spread a soft mat. “We will make Kindness Kites,” Kiko said. “We use reusable things so we do not waste.”

Miri brought the clean jar for beads. Tavi rolled over ribbon and string. Zuzu laid out the paper bag Luna found. Kiko cut gentle shapes—diamonds and stars—snip-snip. The scissors made a happy sound.

“We can draw pictures of fair rules,” Kiko said. “Justice means everyone gets a turn and gets treated fairly.”

They drew together. Tavi drew a line of friends waiting. “One by one,” he said. Miri drew two nests at the same height. “Same chance,” she chirped. Zuzu drew big and small hands holding a heart. Luna drew herself helping sort bins.

Kiko tied the stars to the ribbon and made little tails from yarn ends. The kites were soft and colorful. They looked like floating smiles.

“Ready to fly?” asked Kiko.

They took turns. First Miri, then Tavi, then Zuzu, then Luna. Kiko counted, “One, two, three—whoosh!” The kites fluttered up and danced in the sunny backyard. The paper rustled, and the ribbon sang in the wind, swish, swish.

Luna giggled. “This is fun! I like the routine,” she said.

Kiko nodded. “Routine helps us feel safe. It helps us care for our home and for each other.”

But suddenly, the wind pushed hard. Luna’s kite swooped and got stuck on a low branch. “Oh no!” Luna said. “Did I break it?”

Kiko stayed calm. “We have a problem. We will solve it together.” Kiko’s voice was steady. “First, breathe.” They breathed in and out.

“Next,” said Kiko, “we check our tools.” Kiko looked at the baskets. “We can reuse the stick Miri found.”

Miri brought the long stick. Tavi braced the mat so no one slipped. Zuzu guided, “A little to the left.” Kiko lifted the stick and gently nudged the ribbon. Tap-tap. The kite slid free and floated down into Luna’s hooves.

“You did it!” Luna beamed. “Thank you.”

Kiko smiled. “We did it together. That is fairness and care.”

They packed up the craft tools, sorted scraps again—paper bits to recycle, leaf bits to compost, ribbons to reuse. The backyard was tidy and bright.

Kiko gathered the friends. “Let’s finish our routine,” Kiko said. “Craft and share.” They hung one kite on the fence for everyone to see. They gave the rest to neighbors who peeked over the gate, waving and smiling.

Luna trotted in a happy circle. “I learned the steps. Stretch and breathe, tidy and sort, craft and share.”

Kiko nodded. “Yes. When we follow a kind routine, we help the Earth, we include friends, and we make fair choices.”

Everyone hugged. The sun felt warm. The kites swayed softly in the breeze.

Kiko whispered, “See you tomorrow for our routine.”

The friends called, “See you tomorrow!”

The End.
