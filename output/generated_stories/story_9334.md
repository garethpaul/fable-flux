# Fern's Pantry Mystery

Little Fern the fawn had the softest brown spots and the biggest, most curious eyes in the whole forest. Today, she discovered something very interesting – a door she had never noticed before!

"What could be behind this door?" Fern wondered aloud, her pink nose twitching with excitement. The door was painted a cheerful yellow color and had a sweet, warm smell coming from underneath it.

Fern gently pushed the door open with her tiny hoof. *Creak!* Inside was a cozy room filled with shelves from floor to ceiling. There were jars, boxes, and containers of all shapes and sizes.

"Wow!" gasped Fern. "What is this magical place?"

Just then, a friendly voice called out, "Hello there, little one! Welcome to my pantry!"

Fern looked around and saw Mrs. Rabbit wearing a flowery apron. "A pantry?" asked Fern. "What's a pantry for?"

"Well," said Mrs. Rabbit with a warm smile, "a pantry is where we keep our food safe and organized. Would you like to help me solve a little mystery?"

Fern's ears perked up. "Oh yes, please! I love mysteries!"

Mrs. Rabbit pointed to three different jars on the shelf. "I need to find my sugar for baking cookies, but I forgot which jar it's in. Can you help me figure it out?"

Fern looked at the three jars carefully. They all looked similar, but she noticed something different about each one.

"Let's think about this together," said Mrs. Rabbit. "What do you notice about each jar?"

Fern stepped closer and used all her senses. The first jar had white powder that looked very fine, like snow. She sniffed carefully – it smelled clean but had no sweet smell.

"This one doesn't smell sweet," Fern observed.

The second jar also had white powder, but when Fern looked closely, she could see tiny sparkles in the sunlight streaming through the window.

"This one sparkles!" Fern said excitedly.

The third jar had white powder too, but it looked a bit different – not as fine as the others.

Mrs. Rabbit smiled proudly. "You're thinking very carefully, Fern! Now, what do you know about sugar?"

Fern thought hard. "Well, sugar is sweet and... and it sparkles sometimes when the light hits it!"

"Excellent thinking!" cheered Mrs. Rabbit. "So which jar do you think has the sugar?"

Fern pointed her small hoof at the second jar. "That one! It sparkles in the sunlight!"

Mrs. Rabbit opened the jar and let Fern smell it. The sweet, wonderful scent filled the air. "You're absolutely right! You used your eyes, your nose, and your thinking to solve the mystery!"

"I did it!" Fern danced a little happy dance. "But what are in the other jars?"

"The first jar has flour for baking bread," explained Mrs. Rabbit, "and the third has salt for cooking. They all look similar, but by looking carefully and thinking about what we know, we can tell them apart!"

Fern felt so proud. "I learned that I can figure things out by looking carefully and thinking about what I already know!"

"That's called critical thinking," said Mrs. Rabbit, "and you're very good at it! Would you like to help me bake some cookies now?"

"Yes, please!" said Fern happily.

As they measured the sugar together, Fern realized that asking questions, looking carefully, and thinking about what she knew helped her solve problems. She couldn't wait to use her thinking skills on her next adventure!

The End.
