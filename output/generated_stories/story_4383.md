# Milo the Monkey Learns to Wait

Milo the monkey loved spending time in Grandma's warm kitchen. The smell of fresh cookies always made his tummy rumble with excitement. The kitchen was cozy and bright, with sunshine streaming through yellow curtains and the oven humming a gentle tune.

One morning, Grandma was making Milo's favorite banana bread. The sweet smell filled the air, making Milo bounce up and down on his little stool.

"Is it ready yet, Grandma? Is it ready?" Milo asked, swinging his tail back and forth.

Grandma smiled as she mixed the batter in a big blue bowl. "Not yet, little one. Good things take time to bake properly."

Milo watched the golden batter swirl around and around. His mouth watered as Grandma poured it into the pan and slid it into the warm oven. The kitchen grew even cozier as the oven worked its magic.

"Now we wait thirty minutes," Grandma said, setting the timer with a cheerful ding.

But waiting was hard for Milo! He felt wiggly and impatient. His hands wanted to touch everything, and his feet wanted to jump. After just five minutes, he started to feel frustrated.

"I want banana bread NOW!" Milo said, his voice getting louder. He began to climb toward the oven.

"Whoa there, brave monkey," Grandma said gently. "I can see you're feeling impatient. That's okay, but the oven is hot and dangerous."

Milo felt his heart beating fast. His face felt warm, and his hands were making fists. He recognized these feelings from when Grandma taught him about emotions.

"I'm feeling really impatient, Grandma," Milo said, taking a deep breath like she had shown him. "My body feels all wiggly inside."

"Thank you for telling me how you feel," Grandma said proudly. "That's called self-advocacy - speaking up about your needs. Now, what can we do to help your body feel calmer while we wait?"

Milo thought hard. "Maybe I can do my deep breathing?" he suggested.

"Wonderful idea!" Grandma clapped her hands.

Milo sat on his stool and took three slow, deep breaths. He imagined he was smelling beautiful flowers and then blowing out birthday candles. His shoulders relaxed, and his heart slowed down.

"I feel a little better," Milo said. "But I still want to move around."

"How about we find a helpful activity?" Grandma suggested. "We could set the table or wash the mixing bowl."

Milo's eyes lit up. "Can I wash the bowl? I love the warm, soapy bubbles!"

As Milo carefully washed the bowl, he felt the warm water on his hands and watched the colorful bubbles dance. The activity helped his wiggly feelings settle down. Before he knew it, the timer was ringing!

"The banana bread is ready!" Grandma announced.

Milo felt so proud. "I waited the whole time! I used my breathing and found something helpful to do!"

"You certainly did," Grandma said, giving him a warm hug. "You noticed your feelings, told me about them, and found good ways to manage them. That's called self-regulation, and it's a very important skill."

As they enjoyed warm, delicious banana bread together, Milo felt happy and proud. He had learned that waiting was easier when he paid attention to his feelings and found positive ways to handle them.

"Next time I feel impatient, I know what to do," Milo said with a big smile.

The End.
