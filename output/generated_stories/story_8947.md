# Ollie Otter’s Porch Plan

Ollie was a playful otter who loved to wiggle, giggle, and splash. Today, Ollie sat on a peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like sweet lemonade. A soft breeze tickled Ollie’s whiskers. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” in the nearby tree.

Ollie clapped his paws. “I want to play a game!” he said.

Grandma Olive, a wise otter with silver whiskers, rocked in a squeaky porch chair. “On this porch,” she said with a smile, “we have a family tradition. We share stories and games. We take turns. We use kind words.”

Ollie wiggled. “I can do that!”

Just then, his friends came up the steps. Tina Turtle wore a bright blue scarf. Benny Bunny twitched his nose. Puff the Puppy wagged his tail so fast it thumped the floor—thump, thump, thump!

“Let’s play Shell Stack!” Tina said. She put a pile of smooth shells on the porch table. They clicked softly like tiny bells.

“I want the shiny shell!” Benny said. His ears stood tall.

“No, me!” Puff barked. His tail swished, and one shell rolled and plinked against a cup.

Ollie felt a little twist in his tummy. He wanted the shiny shell too. His paws felt tingly. This felt like a problem.

Grandma Olive nodded. “I see big feelings,” she said kindly. “Let’s use a porch plan. Strategy helps us solve problems.”

“Stra-te-gy?” Ollie asked, tasting the big word slowly.

“Strategy means ‘a smart plan,’” Grandma Olive said. “Here is our porch plan: Stop. Breathe. Share. Choose.”

Ollie took a slow breath. In… out… The breeze smelled like fresh cookies. He felt calmer.

“Stop,” he said softly. “Breathe.” He puffed his cheeks and blew a tiny whoosh. “Share,” he added, looking at his friends. “Can we share the shiny shell?”

Tina nodded. “We can take turns.”

Benny tapped his foot. “But who goes first?”

Puff whined, “I want NOW.”

Ollie’s whiskers twitched. He remembered Grandma’s words: Strategy helps. “Let’s choose with a tradition,” he said. “Grandma, what do we do on the porch when we can’t decide?”

Grandma Olive smiled. “We use the Clap-and-Count. It is our porch tradition. We clap the number of letters in our names. The fewest claps goes first.”

Ollie’s eyes sparkled. “Let’s try!” He pointed to himself. “Ol-lie.” Clap, clap. Two claps.

“Tina.” Clap, clap. Two claps.

“Benny.” Clap, clap, clap, clap, clap. Five claps.

“Puff.” Clap, clap, clap, clap. Four claps.

Tina giggled. “Ollie and I tie!”

Benny’s nose twitched. “I guess I won’t go first,” he sighed, but his eyes were kind.

Puff wagged. “Good plan.”

Ollie thought again. “When we tie, we do Rock-Leaf-Rain,” Grandma said. “It is our porch game. Rock beats Leaf, Leaf sips Rain, Rain rolls over Rock.”

“Rock-Leaf-Rain!” Ollie and Tina cheered. They counted, “One, two, three!” and showed their paws.

Ollie made Rain, wiggling fingers like drops. Tina made Rock, a little fist.

“Rain rolls over Rock!” Grandma laughed softly.

“That means Ollie goes first,” Tina said with a smile. “I go second.”

Benny raised a paw. “I can go third.”

Puff barked, “I’ll go last!” His tail thumped happily.

Ollie placed the shiny shell on top of the stack. The shells were cool and smooth. Click… click… The stack wobbled, then stood tall. Everyone gasped.

“My turn,” Tina said. She added a tiny shell—tick. The breeze hummed. The porch smelled like fresh wood and flowers. The stack stood!

Benny leaned in. His whiskers—well, his nose—twitched. He set a shell—clack. The stack shook and steadied.

Puff’s turn. He stuck out his tongue in concentration and very, very gently set a shell—plink. The stack quivered and held.

Around and around they went. Ollie noticed his tummy was calm now. The plan worked. He felt proud.

Then Puff sneezed. “A-choo!” The shells tumbled—clatter, rattle, roll! One shell bumped Ollie’s paw. His heart jumped.

Puff’s ears drooped. “I ruined it,” he whispered.

Ollie took a breath like the breeze. In… out… “It’s okay,” he said softly. “We can fix it. Let’s use the porch plan again: Stop. Breathe. Share. Choose.”

They all stopped. They all breathed. They shared smiles.

“Let’s choose to start over,” Tina said.

“And this time,” Benny added, “we put a leaf on top to make it softer.”

Grandma Olive clapped. “You used strategy! And you used our porch traditions.”

They stacked again, slow and steady. Click. Tick. Clack. Plink. The leaf on top made a soft hush sound. The stack grew tall, taller, tallest!

When the last shell was set, the porch was very quiet. Even the birds paused.

Then—cheers! “We did it!” Ollie laughed. His whiskers wiggled with joy. Puff wagged so hard his tag jingled. Benny hopped in a happy circle. Tina hugged her blue scarf.

Grandma Olive poured cool lemonade. The cups felt cold and smooth. The taste was sweet and sunny.

Ollie lifted his cup. “Today we used a smart plan. We solved a problem. We shared our porch traditions.”

Grandma Olive nodded. “And you showed kind hearts. That is the best tradition of all.”

Ollie looked at his friends. “When we have a problem, we can: stop, breathe, share, and choose,” he said. “That is our strategy.”

Everyone smiled. The breeze hummed. The porch felt warm and calm.

They played one more round. When they finished, they stacked the shells in a little heart shape, a porch tradition to say “thank you.”

Ollie pressed his paw on his chest. “I feel proud,” he whispered.

“We feel proud too,” said Tina, Benny, and Puff together.

The sun slid low, painting the porch gold. Ollie rocked in the chair, squeak, squeak, and smiled. Problems can be big. But with a plan, kind words, and shared traditions, we can solve them—together.

The End.
