# Birdie Sings in the Study Corner

Birdie was a small, bright singing bird with soft blue feathers and a warm, sweet voice. She loved to sing her ABC song. “A-B-C,” she chirped, “1-2-3!” Her favorite spot was the cozy study corner by the window. The study corner had a little table, a tiny lamp, soft crayons, picture cards, and a basket of books. Sunlight made gentle squares on the floor. The air smelled like crayons and paper, and the lamp made a soft, warm glow.

One morning, Birdie flapped to the study corner and gasped. “Oh no,” she peeped. “What a mess!” Crayons rolled on the floor. Picture cards were mixed up. Books were in a wobbly pile. Birdie stepped on a crinkly paper. “Crunch!” It made her jump.

“I want to sing and learn,” Birdie said. “But I can’t find anything.” She felt a little droopy. Her wings sagged. Her song got quiet. “How can I fix this problem?”

Birdie took a deep breath. “I will use my thinking skills,” she said. “Step one, look. Step two, sort. Step three, put away.” She nodded. “Yes! I can do it.”

She hopped to the crayons. “Hello, little colors,” Birdie sang softly. “Red, blue, green, and yellow.” She rolled each crayon into a small tin cup. “Tippy-tap,” went the crayons. “This cup is for warm colors. This cup is for cool colors,” she decided. The red and yellow crayons went into the warm cup. The blue and green crayons went into the cool cup. Birdie smiled. “You look cozy.”

Next, Birdie picked up the picture cards. “These show animals. These show shapes,” she said. She made two neat stacks. “Stack, stack.” The cards felt smooth in her wings. “Circles here. Squares here. Meow cat here. Woof dog here.” She put a small ribbon around each stack so they would not slip.

Then Birdie pecked at the books. “Heavy,” she chirped. The big books went on the bottom. The little books went on the top. “Safe and steady.” The wobbly pile became a straight, happy tower. Birdie puffed with pride.

But the floor still had little paper bits. “I need help,” Birdie said. She looked around. “Who can help me?”

Just then, Squeaky the mouse peeked from a tiny hole. “Hi, Birdie!” squeaked Squeaky. “I heard your sorting song.”

“I am cleaning the study corner,” Birdie said. “I have a problem. There are too many paper bits. Will you help me?”

“Yes!” said Squeaky. “We can work together.” His whiskers twitched. He held a small brush.

Birdie thought again. “We need a plan,” she said. “Plan: You brush. I sweep. We hum a steady beat.” Birdie began a gentle tune. “Sweep, sweep, brush, brush,” she sang, tapping her little foot. The sound went swish-swish, brush-brush. It felt good to work together. The paper bits went into a tiny bin with a soft rustle.

“Teamwork!” cheered Squeaky.

“Thank you,” Birdie chirped. “It is almost done.”

Just then, Poppy the bunny hopped in, ears wiggling. “I heard music,” she said. “Can I join?”

“Yes, please,” Birdie said. “We need labels.”

“Labels?” asked Poppy.

“Little tags that tell where things go,” Birdie explained. “Crayons here. Cards there. Books on the shelf.”

Poppy smiled and wrote simple labels with a thick blue marker. “CRAYONS,” she wrote on a card. The marker smelled a little sweet. “CARDS.” “BOOKS.” The letters were big and friendly. Birdie stuck the labels on the cups, the ribboned stacks, and the shelf.

Birdie flapped her wings. “We did it!” The study corner looked tidy. The lamp glowed. The air felt calm. The colors looked bright and happy. Birdie’s chest felt warm and light.

“Now we can learn and sing,” Birdie said. She hopped to the table and opened a book. Squeaky and Poppy sat close. Birdie sang, “A-B-C, easy as can be. One, two, three, tidy helps me!” Her voice was clear and sweet. Squeaky clapped his tiny paws. Poppy thumped a happy beat with her foot.

After the song, Birdie smiled. “What did we learn today?”

Squeaky squeaked, “We solved a problem by making a plan.”

Poppy nodded. “We stayed organized with labels.”

“And we worked together,” Birdie added. “That made the job faster and fun.”

Birdie looked at the labels. “Now,” she said, “when we are done, we put things back.” She held up a red crayon. “Where does this go?”

“In the warm colors cup!” laughed Poppy.

Birdie placed it there. “Click.” She felt proud. “Problem solved!”

Birdie took another deep breath. The study corner was neat, and her heart was happy. She sang one last soft line, “Clean and bright, we learn just right.”

Together, they sat in the warm light, reading, drawing, and humming. The study corner was ready for more songs and more learning, one tidy step at a time.

The End.
