# Hop, Wash, and Play!

Thump-thump-thump! A joyful kangaroo joey named Kiki hopped down the hallway. Her soft paws pat-patted on the floor. Her nose twitched. Her ears perked up.

“I can hear the drums!” Kiki giggled. “Boom, boom, boom! Practice time!”

Kiki loved music. The musical practice room smelled like fresh wood and lemon polish. Bright lights made the cymbals sparkle. A tiny triangle jingled when the air moved. Tap, ting! Tap, ting!

Coach Lila, a kind koala, waved. “Hello, Kiki! Are you ready to play the big drum?”

“Yes!” Kiki bounced. “I am ready!”

Coach Lila smiled. “We will start in ten minutes. That is not a lot of time. First, we wash hands. Clean hands keep us healthy.”

Kiki looked at the big drum. Her eyes went big. “Can I touch it now?”

Coach Lila shook her head gently. “Not yet, little hopper. We practice good hygiene. That means we keep our bodies clean. Clean hands, clean music.”

Kiki nodded. “Okay! Wash hands first. Then drum.”

She hopped to the sink in the corner. The water rushed, whooosh, cool on her paws. “Brrr!” Kiki giggled. She squirted a dollop of soap. It smelled like oranges. Squish, squish. She rubbed her paws together. Bubbles danced on her fur.

Coach Lila hummed a washing song. “Rub the palms, rub the backs. Rub between, keep on track. Round and round each little thumb. Rinse the bubbles—one by one!”

Kiki sang along. “Rub, rub, rub! Rinse, rinse, rinse!” She watched the bubbles slip away. The water tickled. Splash!

She dried her paws with a soft towel. “All clean!” she cheered.

Coach Lila checked a little clock. “Great time, Kiki! We have seven minutes left.”

Kiki’s tummy made a tiny rumble. “Coach, I ate a sticky mango. My drum sticks feel icky.”

“Good noticing,” said Coach Lila. “That is problem-solving. When we see a problem, we fix it. We have wipes for sticky things.”

Kiki opened a box of wipes. The wipes felt cool and smelled like fresh rain. Swish, swish—she cleaned her drum sticks. “All better!”

Coach Lila clapped softly. “Five minutes left. Let’s set up your seat.”

Kiki looked around. The chair was tall. The drum was big. She tried to climb. “Ooof!” Her tail tangled. She slipped and giggled. “I need a plan.”

“Another problem to solve,” said Coach Lila. “What can we do?”

Kiki looked. She spotted a small step stool. “I can use that!” She put the stool next to the drum. Step, step. “I did it!”

Coach Lila pointed to a little poster on the wall. It had pictures: Wash Hands, Set Timer, Clean Tools, Make a Plan, Share Music.

Kiki traced the pictures with a paw. “First, wash. Next, timer. Then, clean. Then, plan. Then, play!”

A soft chime sounded. Ding! The timer showed two minutes. Kiki’s eyes grew wide. “Two minutes left! I must be quick, but careful.”

She looked at her fur. “I have drum dust on my tummy.” She picked a small brush from a bin and brushed herself. Swish, swish. The dust floated away like tiny clouds.

She took a deep breath. In through the nose—fresh lemon polish and wood. Out through the mouth—slow and calm. “I am ready.”

Coach Lila smiled. “You are taking great care of yourself. That’s hygiene. You are using your time wisely. That’s time-management. You solved problems with a plan. That’s problem-solving!”

Kiki beamed. “I can do it!”

The timer chimed again. Ding! “Practice time,” said Coach Lila.

Kiki held her clean sticks in clean paws. Tap-tap on the drum rim—light like raindrops. Boom-boom on the big drum—deep like thunder far away. She listened to the triangle: ting! She listened to the soft shaker: shhh. She kept a steady beat. Her tail swayed, left and right.

“Listen, Kiki,” Coach Lila said. “Music is like washing hands. We follow steps. We go steady. We pay attention.”

Kiki nodded and tapped a pattern. “Rub, rub, rub. Rinse, rinse, rinse,” she whispered to herself. Tap-tap-tap. Boom-boom-boom. She smiled so big her cheeks felt warm.

Soon, other joeys came in. “Can we play too?” they asked.

“Yes,” said Kiki. “But first—wash hands!” She pointed to the sink and sang the washing song. The joeys laughed and sang with her. Bubbles popped like tiny kisses. Pop! Pop! Pop!

They lined up their clean sticks. They checked the little clock. They took turns. They shared the instruments with gentle paws. The room filled with happy sounds—boom, ting, shhh, tap! The music felt bright and clean, like sunshine after rain.

When the practice ended, Coach Lila gathered everyone. “What did we learn today?”

Kiki raised her paw. “I learned to keep my hands clean. Clean hands keep me healthy. I used the clock and did things on time. And when I had a problem, I looked, thought, and found a tool.”

Coach Lila nodded. “Wonderful. Clean body, calm plan, happy music.”

Kiki hugged her drum sticks. “Wash, plan, play! I can do that every day.”

As Kiki hopped home, her paws felt fresh, her heart felt proud, and her beat stayed steady. Tap-tap, boom-boom. She whispered, “Clean hands, bright day. I did my best.”

The End.
