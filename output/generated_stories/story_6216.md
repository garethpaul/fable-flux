# Miko’s Rooftop Routine

Every morning, when the sun peeked over the tall buildings, a courageous monkey named Miko climbed up to the rooftop. The roof was warm and bumpy. A gentle breeze smelled like fresh leaves. Pigeons cooed, “Coo-coo,” and the city hummed below.

Miko loved his morning exercise. “Stretch, bend, jump!” he said with a smile. He did ten tiny toe touches and five big monkey jumps. His tail swished with joy. Exercise made his body feel strong and his heart feel brave.

One bright morning, Miko had an idea. “I will make a rooftop obstacle course!” he said. He took soft cushions, a long ribbon, and a shiny bell. “This will be fun for everyone.” He was excited. He wanted to show his friends he could take initiative.

Soon, his friends arrived. There was Tiko the tiny turtle, Lali the laughing lark, and Pip the curious puppy. They all loved to visit the rooftop and play.

“What is that?” asked Pip, wagging his tail.

“It’s an obstacle course!” Miko said proudly. “We can stretch, balance, and jump. We can cheer for each other. We can do it together!”

Lali fluttered her wings. “Cheep-cheep! Let’s try!”

Miko put the ribbon on the ground like a balance beam. He set the cushions in a soft line. He hung the shiny bell from a small pole. “First, we balance. Next, we hop. At the end, we ring the bell!”

Miko jumped on the ribbon right away. “Watch me! I can do it fast!” He zoomed ahead. His feet made tiny tap-tap sounds on the rooftop. He hopped over the cushions with a boing-boing. He reached the bell and rang it, “Ding!”

Tiko moved slowly to the ribbon. “I will try,” he said softly.

“Come on, Tiko! Hurry!” Miko called. He didn’t mean to sound bossy, but his voice came out loud. Pip tried to hop on the ribbon too, and Lali fluttered down to watch.

Tiko wobbled. The ribbon shook. “Oh dear,” Tiko whispered. He slipped off and sat on a cushion. “I need more time.”

Pip put a paw on the cushion. “Can I go next?” he asked.

“I’m next!” Miko said quickly. He jumped again. He bumped the bell. It fell with a clatter. “Clang-clang!”

Lali flapped in surprise. “Cheep! That was too fast.”

Tiko’s eyes grew watery. Pip’s ears drooped. The rooftop didn’t feel fun anymore.

Miko stopped. His heart felt heavy. He looked at his friends’ faces. He listened to the quiet breeze and the faraway car sounds. He took a deep breath. The air smelled like rain clouds.

“I made a mistake,” Miko said softly. He sat down on a cushion. “I hurried you. I didn’t take turns. I didn’t cooperate.” He put a hand on Tiko’s shell. “I’m sorry, Tiko.” He looked at Pip and Lali. “I’m sorry to you too.”

Tiko blinked. “Thank you for the apology,” he said gently.

Pip wagged a little. “We can try again.”

Lali chirped, “We can help each other. That is cooperation!”

Miko nodded. “Yes! Let’s make a plan. We can go one at a time. We can cheer. We can help if someone wobbles.” He smiled. “We will still exercise, but we will do it together.”

They lined up. Miko stood to the side and clapped softly. “Tiko first!” he said.

Tiko inched onto the ribbon. The rooftop felt warm under his tiny feet. “Slow and steady,” Tiko said.

“You can do it, Tiko!” Lali sang.

Pip stood ready with a paw near the ribbon. “I’ll help if you wobble.”

Tiko wiggled and balanced. He made it across! Everyone cheered. “Yay, Tiko!” Miko rang the bell for him, “Ding!” Tiko smiled wide.

Next it was Lali’s turn. She hopped and fluttered. The ribbon tickled her toes. “Cheep-cheep!” She touched the bell with her beak. “Ding!”

Then Pip went. He placed one paw, then another. He wobbled. Miko held the ribbon still. “I’ve got you,” Miko said kindly.

Pip balanced, hopped the cushions, and booped the bell with his nose. “Ding!” His tail swished like a happy broom.

At last, Miko stepped onto the ribbon. He moved slowly. He felt the breeze on his fur and heard his friends cheer. “You can do it, Miko!” they called. Miko smiled. He hopped the cushions and paused at the bell.

“Team ring?” he asked.

They all reached up together—paw, wing, hand, and tiny turtle foot—and rang the bell as one. “Ding-ding-ding!” The sound sparkled in the morning air.

They finished with stretches. “Arms up, big breath in,” Miko said. They breathed in. They breathed out. The city smelled like clean air after a small rain. Their bodies felt strong. Their hearts felt warm.

Miko looked at his friends. “Today I learned something,” he said. “Cooperation means doing it together. It means waiting, helping, and cheering. And when we make a mistake, we say sorry.”

Tiko nodded. “And exercise is more fun with friends.”

Pip barked happily. Lali chirped a bright song. Miko hugged them all.

Every morning after that, they met on the rooftop. They followed their routine: stretch, balance, hop, ring, and cheer. They took turns. They helped. They cooperated. And the bell always rang with a happy, “Ding!”

The End.
