# Buzzy Beaver’s Calm and Sudsy Day

Buzzy the busy beaver hurried into the laundry room with a big basket. His small paws held socks, shirts, and a tiny red hat. The room smelled like warm soap and fresh cotton. Machines hummed, “Hummm-hummm,” and a dryer went, “Whirr-whirr.” The floor felt cool under Buzzy’s little feet.

“I have so much to do!” Buzzy said. “Wash, dry, fold, and put away.” His tail tapped, tap-tap-tap, on the tiles.

He tossed the clothes up, and they rained down like soft snow. “Whee!” he giggled, then frowned. “Oh no. This is a big job.”

A sock landed on his nose. “Boop!” Buzzy giggled again. But then he looked at the tall washer and felt a flutter in his tummy.

“I want to be fast,” he said. “Fast like a splashy river.”

A gentle voice answered, “Hello, little helper.” Buzzy looked around. It was the washing machine! Its shiny door looked like a big round eye.

“I am Swishy,” the washer hummed. “I like clean clothes. I like calm swirls.”

Buzzy blinked. “You can talk?”

Swishy bubbled. “Only to good listeners. Are you listening, Buzzy?”

Buzzy’s ears perked up. He stood very still. He felt his paws on the floor. He heard the soft hum. He smelled the soapy air. He whispered, “I am listening.”

“Good,” said Swishy. “When we feel busy, we can slow down. We can take a breath.”

Buzzy nodded. “Okay.” He put his paws on his belly and took a slow breath in. “In.” He let it out like a tiny wind. “Out.”

“In.” He felt his belly rise. “Out.” He felt it fall.

The flutter in his tummy felt smaller. His tail stopped tapping so fast. “I feel calmer,” Buzzy said with a smile.

Just then, the dryer chirped, “Ping!” A warm, fluffy voice sang, “I’m Dori the Dryer. I’m warm and patient. I like to wait and tumble.”

Buzzy waved. “Hi, Dori! I want to be patient like you.”

“You can,” Dori hummed. “Slow and steady, tumble and ready.”

Buzzy looked at his big pile of clothes. He also saw three friends peeking from the door: Pip the Porcupine, Dot the Duck, and Tilly the Turtle. They all liked to help in different ways.

Pip held a shiny clothespin. “I like pokey lines,” Pip said proudly.

Dot waddled in with a quack. “I like water splashes,” she said, flapping happily.

Tilly smiled slowly. “I like to stack things,” she said in a soft, low voice.

Buzzy felt a tug. He wanted to do it all himself, and fast. But he remembered Swishy’s calm swirls.

He took another belly breath. “Friends,” he said kindly, “let’s work together. We can each help in our own way.”

Dot quacked. “Can I pour the soap? I like careful pours.”

Buzzy thought. “That’s a big job,” he said. He put a paw on his heart. “Let’s make a plan.”

They all sat on the floor. The tiles were cool. The soap smelled like flowers. Buzzy spoke slowly.

“First, we sort. Colors here. Whites there. Socks in a buddy line.”

Dot raised a wing. “And then?”

“Then,” Buzzy said, “we pour the soap. One small cup. Swishy likes gentle soap.”

Dot nodded. “I can pour slowly.”

Pip asked, “What about pins?”

“After we dry,” Buzzy said. “You can pin the pairs together so they don’t run away.”

Pip grinned. “Pokey pairs!”

Tilly asked, “And me?”

Buzzy smiled. “You make the neat stacks. You are careful and steady.”

Tilly beamed. “I love neat stacks.”

They all clapped. Clap, clap, clap.

They began to sort. Buzzy touched each cloth and said, “Blue shirt here. White socks there.” He slowed down so he could feel the cotton and see the colors. He listened to his breath. In. Out. Calm.

Dot poured the soap. “Drip… drop… perfect.”

Swishy sang, “Swish-swish, calm dish. Thank you, friends.”

They watched the clothes swirl like a rainbow soup. Buzzy imagined the washer as a gentle river. The bubbles were tiny clouds. The round door was a moon.

“Let’s play a quiet game,” Buzzy whispered. “We watch three bubbles pop.” They all watched. Pop. Pop. Pop. It felt peaceful.

When Dori chirped, “Ping!” the warm air smelled like sunshine on towels. Pip gently pinned socks into happy pairs. “Two friends together,” Pip said, smiling.

Tilly stacked shirts into a tower. “One, two, three,” she counted softly. The tower stood tall and tidy.

Buzzy folded the tiny red hat last. He pressed it smooth and put it on his head. “Hat hug!” he laughed.

They all stood back. The laundry room felt bright and calm. No more flutter in Buzzy’s tummy. Just quiet joy.

Swishy hummed, “You were mindful, Buzzy. You listened, breathed, and noticed.”

Dori added, “You celebrated differences. Each friend helped in a special way.”

Buzzy nodded. “We made a plan. We took turns. We used our imaginations, too.”

Dot quacked a happy quack. “We worked together!”

Pip clapped softly. “Pokey high-fives!”

Tilly gave a slow thumbs-up. “Steady smiles.”

Buzzy took one more belly breath. In. Out. He felt proud and peaceful.

He looked at his friends. “Thank you,” he said. “When I feel busy, I can slow down. I can breathe. I can notice. I can ask for help. We are different, and that makes us strong.”

Everyone cheered. The laundry room hummed like a gentle song.

The End.
