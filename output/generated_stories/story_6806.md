# Moon Steps on the Rooftop

On a soft, warm evening, a kind mouse named Miki climbed up to the rooftop. The roof felt smooth under his tiny paws. A light breeze tickled his whiskers. The sky was pink and purple, and the first little stars began to glow.

Miki liked to help. He was little, but his heart was big. He wanted to lead a fun exercise time for his friends. He squeaked, “Friends, friends! Let’s move our bodies and feel strong!”

Soon, tiny feet pattered up the stairs. Pip the chipmunk, Coco the kitten, and Lolo the ladybug came to the rooftop. They smiled and sniffed the cool air. Coco purred, “It smells like flowers.” Pip giggled, “I hear crickets singing.” Lolo buzzed, “The moon is round and bright!”

Miki pointed to a chalkboard he had set on the roof. He had drawn big, simple pictures and times. He said, “We will use our time. We will follow our plan. Then we can play and rest, too!”

The chalkboard showed:
- 5 minutes: Warm-up wiggles
- 5 minutes: Big steps
- 5 minutes: Jumps and stretches
- 5 minutes: Water and a moon song

Miki tapped the top of a tiny bell. “Ding!” He smiled. “When the bell sings, we switch.” He felt proud to be a leader, and his friends were ready.

They began with warm-up wiggles. Miki wiggled his nose. Pip wiggled his tail. Coco wiggled her paws. Lolo wiggled her wings. They laughed and felt happy. The breeze smelled like fresh bread from the bakery below.

“Ding!” sang the bell. Miki said, “Time to switch!” But Pip wanted to keep wiggling. “Just a little more,” Pip begged.

Miki’s ears drooped. He wanted everyone to have fun, but he also wanted to keep time. He took a slow breath. He used his kind voice. “We can wiggle again later,” he said. “If we switch now, we can do all the moves and still have our song.”

Pip nodded. “Okay,” he said. “You are a good leader, Miki.”

They took big steps next. “Step like the moon!” Miki called. “Slow and round.” Step, step, step. Their feet made soft pat, pat sounds on the roof. Coco said, “I feel strong!” Lolo buzzed, “I feel brave!”

“Ding!” sang the bell again. Coco made a little face. “I want more big steps,” she said.

Miki looked at the chalkboard. He felt a tiny worry in his tummy. He did not want to rush. He did not want to forget the water. He remembered his plan. He smiled and said, “We will come back to big steps after jumps. We can do three more at the end if we finish on time.” He held up three tiny fingers.

Coco’s eyes shone. “Three more later? Deal!”

They jumped and stretched. “Jump like popcorn!” Miki squeaked. Pop! Pop! Pop! Their hearts thumped in a happy way. The night air felt cool on their warm faces. The moonlight made silver shapes on the roof. Lolo stretched her tiny legs. “Ahhh,” she sighed. “This feels good.”

Then came another “Ding!” Miki clapped. “Water time and moon song!”

Pip frowned. “But I want to jump more.”

Miki nodded. He understood. “We will do a bonus time,” he said. “If we drink water now, we can have two minutes more of jumping at the end. Our bodies need sips to stay strong.”

They each took a small sip from little cups. The water was cold and sweet. Coco licked a drop from her whisker and giggled. Their breathing slowed. Their shoulders relaxed.

Miki checked the chalkboard. He felt calm. “We have five minutes left,” he said. “Let’s sing our moon song, then we can do our bonus moves.”

They sang softly:
“Hello, moon so high and bright,
Help us use our time just right.
Move our bodies, kind and strong,
Lead with care and sing our song.”

Their voices floated into the night. The crickets hummed along. Miki felt proud. He was leading with love. He was using time wisely.

“Bonus time!” Miki cheered. He tapped the bell softly. “Two minutes of jumps, then three big steps.”

They jumped—Pop! Pop!—and counted together, “One, two, three, four!” Then they took three big steps, step, step, step, just like Miki had promised.

When they were done, Miki looked at his friends. “How do you feel?” he asked.

Pip said, “I feel strong.” Coco purred, “I feel calm.” Lolo buzzed, “I feel happy.”

Miki smiled. “We used our time. We made a plan. We listened to the bell. We had fun, and we did it all.”

Coco rubbed her head on Miki’s shoulder. “You are a kind leader,” she said.

Miki’s heart felt warm. “Thank you,” he said. “A plan helps us do our best. We can move, drink water, sing, and still have time to rest.”

They watched the moon for a quiet moment. The rooftop felt cool now, and the stars twinkled like tiny lights. Miki rolled up the chalkboard and tucked the bell away.

“Tomorrow,” Miki said, “we will make a new plan together.”

Pip, Coco, and Lolo cheered, “Yes!”

Lesson: Using a simple plan, listening to gentle reminders, and taking turns helps everyone use time well, be kind leaders, and keep our bodies healthy and strong.

The End.
