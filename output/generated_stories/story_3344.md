# Gigi’s Gentle Game Plan

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with long, gentle lashes and warm brown eyes. She loved the creative art room at school. The room smelled like fresh paper and sweet glue. Sunlight painted stripes on the floor. Crayons clacked in a tin. Paintbrushes rested like tiny trees in a jar.

“Good morning, art room,” Gigi whispered. Her soft hooves made a clip-clop sound on the tiles.

Coach Kiki the koala peeked in with a whistle. “Art first, then our mini hoop game,” she said, tapping a small basketball with a paw. “We will plan, play fair, and use kind words.”

Gigi smiled. “I love to plan,” she said. “I can make a game chart!”

She spread a big sheet of paper. It was smooth and bright. “Plan,” she wrote in big gentle letters. She drew boxes with a purple crayon: warm-up, turn-taking, cheering, snack. She added stars. She hummed a happy hum.

Her friends came in. Timo the turtle rolled a glue stick. Bella the bunny bounced with a soft thump-thump. Zuzu the zebra set out tiny orange cones. Everyone liked the plan. “This looks great!” said Bella.

Gigi felt tall with pride. She wanted the game to be perfect.

After art time, Coach Kiki set up the little hoop in the corner. The hoop was low and friendly. The ball felt bouncy and smooth in Gigi’s hooves.

“Remember,” said Coach Kiki. “We use our plan. We wait our turn. We tell the truth. That’s called integrity.”

“Integrity,” Gigi repeated. The word felt big and brave in her mouth.

Warm-up time! The music was soft and boppy. They stretched. They wiggled. They giggled. The cones made a tiny rubbery squeak on the floor.

Then the game began. Timo went first. He tossed the ball. Boing! It bounced off the rim.

“You can do it!” cheered Gigi. She clapped. Clap-clap!

Bella went next. Whoosh! She made a basket. Everyone cheered.

Now it was Gigi’s turn. She took a deep breath. She bounced the ball once, twice. Boing-boing. She looked at the hoop. Her heart beat a happy tap-tap. She tossed. The ball hit the rim and rolled out.

“Oh,” said Gigi. She felt a little pinch in her tummy. She wanted to win. She wanted a star by her name on the chart. Her eyes looked at the paper plan on the wall. She had drawn a big gold star for “score.”

When it was Gigi’s next turn, the room felt very quiet. She tossed again. The ball tapped the rim, then dropped in. Swish!

“I did it!” Gigi called. She felt a hot bubble of excitement. Without thinking, she skipped ahead in line and grabbed the ball again. “I’ll take another turn!”

“Um, Gigi,” said Zuzu. “It’s my turn.”

Gigi’s cheeks felt warm. “Just one more,” she said. She tossed—and missed. The ball rolled under a shelf. Markers rattled. A cup tipped and fell with a plop. Blue paint splashed on the floor.

Everyone froze. The room smelled like paint. A blue puddle spread like a little lake.

Coach Kiki raised her paw. “Pause,” she said kindly. “Let’s breathe.”

Gigi’s heart sank. She had taken an extra turn. She had not followed the plan. She looked at her friends’ faces. Zuzu’s ears drooped. Timo stared at the blue puddle. Bella hugged her tail.

Gigi felt a tight knot in her chest. She remembered the word. Integrity. Tell the truth. Be fair.

She took a slow breath in. She let it out like a soft sigh. “Friends,” she said, her voice small. “I made a mistake. I took an extra turn. I did not wait. I did not follow our plan. I am sorry.”

The room was quiet. Gigi’s eyes felt shiny.

Coach Kiki nodded gently. “Thank you for your honest words, Gigi. A true apology has three parts: say what you did, say you are sorry, and ask how to make it right.”

Gigi nodded. “Zuzu,” she said, looking into Zuzu’s eyes, “I took your turn. I am sorry. How can I make it right?”

Zuzu’s ears lifted a little. “Please put the ball back in line,” Zuzu said. “And help clean the blue.”

“I can do that,” said Gigi. She set the ball back on the cone. She fetched paper towels. The towels felt soft and squishy. She dabbed the paint with careful pats. Pat, pat, pat. Timo and Bella helped. Soon the floor was clean. The room smelled fresh again.

Gigi turned to her chart. She added a new box with her purple crayon. “Apology,” she wrote. She drew a heart and three little steps. Say it. Sorry. Make it right.

Coach Kiki smiled. “That is good planning,” she said. “Games and art both need plans, and hearts do too.”

“Thank you for waiting,” Gigi said to Zuzu. “Would you like to go now?”

Zuzu grinned. “Yes!” She bounced the ball. Boing. She tossed. Swish! Everyone cheered. Gigi cheered the loudest.

They finished the game with fair turns. They clapped for each other. They told the truth about scores. They felt proud and calm.

At snack time, Gigi shared orange slices. The juicy smell filled the room. “Today I learned that saying sorry helps hearts feel better,” she said. “Integrity means I tell the truth and follow the plan, even when I really want to go first.”

Coach Kiki tapped her whistle and nodded. “And that is a winning play, Gigi.”

Gigi’s tummy felt light again. She pressed a star on the “Apology” box. It shone like a tiny sun.

The End.
