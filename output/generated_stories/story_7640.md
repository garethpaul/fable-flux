# Pebble the Penguin’s Paint Plan

Pebble the penguin waddled into the creative art room with a happy hop. The room smelled like fresh paper and fruity markers. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. Paints shimmered in rainbow rows, and soft brushes waited in tidy cups.

“Good morning, colors!” Pebble chirped. “Today, I have a big goal.” She patted her small chest. “I will paint a picture all by myself.”

She looked at the “Goal Board” on the wall. It had bright stars and simple words. Pebble read them out loud, slowly and proudly. “Set a goal. Make a plan. Try your best. Ask kindly if needed. Say sorry if you make a mess. Keep going.”

Pebble clapped her flippers. “My goal: paint a picture of my home—snow, ocean, and sky.”

She took a deep breath. The air felt cool and smelled like glue sticks. She made a plan in her head. “Step one: gather tools. Step two: paint background. Step three: add details. Step four: sign my name.”

Pebble chose a big sheet of thick paper. It felt smooth and strong. She picked a wide brush for the sky, a medium brush for the sea, and a tiny brush for snowy hills. She tapped each brush on the table. Tap, tap, tap.

Her friend, Milla the mouse, peeked in. “Do you want help?”

Pebble smiled and shook her head. “Thank you, Milla. My goal is to try it by myself today. I want to learn what I can do.”

Milla nodded. “You can do it!” She scampered to a corner to make her own craft.

Pebble dipped the wide brush into blue paint. Swish! The brush felt soft and wet. She spread the blue across the top. “Swish-swish,” she sang. The sky looked big and calm. Then she dipped into a lighter blue for the ocean. “Swoosh!” The paint glided like gentle waves.

A little drip slid toward the edge. “Oh!” Pebble caught it with the corner of her brush. “I can fix this,” she said to herself. She dabbed gently. Dab, dab. The drip turned into a tiny boat shape. Pebble giggled. “Discovery! A drip can be a boat.”

She cleaned the brush carefully in a jar of water. Swirl, swirl, plink. The water turned cloudy, like fog. Pebble wiped the brush on a napkin. “Step three: details,” she said. With the tiny brush, she added snowy hills, soft and round. “Puff, puff,” she whispered, making gentle strokes.

She wanted to add a shiny sun. She reached for the yellow paint. Her flipper bumped a cup of glitter. Uh-oh! The cup tipped. Sprinkle—glitter fell like tiny stars onto the table and floor.

Pebble’s eyes grew wide. Her tummy felt wiggly. She looked around. Milla blinked. Mr. Pablo, the art room helper, turned from the sink.

Pebble took a slow breath. She remembered the Goal Board: “Say sorry if you make a mess.” She stood tall. “I’m sorry,” she said. “I spilled glitter. I will clean it up.”

Mr. Pablo smiled kindly. “Thank you for your honest words, Pebble. What is your plan?”

Pebble pointed to the dustpan and the little broom. “First, sweep the floor. Then wipe the table with a damp cloth. I can do it.”

She picked up the little broom. Swish, swish. The glitter made a soft, sandy sound. She swept carefully, one small area at a time. Milla came closer.

“Do you need help?” Milla asked again.

Pebble felt her heart. It beat steady and brave. “Thank you. I want to try myself first. It’s my mess, and I can fix it. If I can’t, I will ask.”

Milla smiled and watched, ready.

Pebble kept going. Swish, swish. Scoop, tap. She dampened a cloth at the sink. Drip, drip. She wiped the table in slow circles. The glitter lifted and stuck to the cloth. The room smelled fresh again. Pebble checked the floor. Sparkle-free!

Mr. Pablo gave a thumbs-up. “Well done. You made a problem, and you solved it. That is self-reliance.”

Pebble felt warm and proud, like a sunbeam on her feathers. “Now, back to my plan!” She added the yellow sun with a little glow. She painted a small penguin on the snow, with bright eyes and a cheerful grin. She signed her name at the bottom in neat, careful letters: Pebble.

She stepped back. The sky was smooth. The ocean waved. The snow looked soft. The tiny boat drifted happily. Pebble’s heart danced. “I did it,” she whispered. “I set a goal. I made a plan. I tried my best. I solved a problem. I said sorry. I finished my art.”

Milla clapped her tiny paws. “Your picture tells a story.”

Pebble nodded. “It tells my story. I can try by myself. I can discover new things. I can say sorry and fix mistakes. I can reach my goal.”

Mr. Pablo placed a shining star sticker on the Goal Board next to Pebble’s name. “Your self-reliance shines like your sun.”

Pebble packed her brushes, wiped the table one more time, and put the lids back on the paints. Click, click. The room looked neat and happy.

Pebble hugged her picture gently. “Thank you, colors,” she said. “Tomorrow, I will set a new goal.”

She waddled out of the creative art room, humming a swish-swoosh song, feeling brave, kind, and ready to discover more.

The End.
