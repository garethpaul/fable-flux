# Penny the Parrot's Big Voice

Penny was a bright green parrot with beautiful red feathers on her head. She lived with the Martinez family and loved to explore their cozy house. Today, she fluttered into the pantry, where tall shelves held jars of colorful spices, boxes of cereal, and cans of soup.

"What a wonderful place to practice my words!" Penny chirped happily. She had been learning new words every day, but sometimes she felt shy about speaking up.

As Penny perched on a shelf, she heard little Sofia Martinez crying in the kitchen. "Mama! Mama!" Sofia called out, but her voice was very quiet.

Penny tilted her head and listened carefully. Sofia sounded upset, but her mama was upstairs and couldn't hear her soft voice.

"I should help," Penny thought. "But what if I say the words wrong? What if my voice sounds silly?"

Penny took a deep breath. The pantry smelled like cinnamon and vanilla, which made her feel brave. She remembered what Papa Martinez always told her: "Practice makes perfect, Penny!"

"MAMA! SOFIA NEEDS MAMA!" Penny called out in her loudest, clearest voice.

From upstairs, Mama Martinez heard Penny's call. "Thank you, Penny!" she called back as her footsteps hurried down the stairs.

Sofia looked surprised when she saw Penny in the pantry doorway. "Penny helped me!" she said with wonder.

"Yes, she did," said Mama, giving Sofia a big hug. "And Sofia, remember to use your big voice when you need help, just like Penny did."

Penny felt proud and warm inside. Her feathers ruffled with happiness. "I used my voice to help!" she squawked joyfully.

Later that day, Penny decided to practice more words in the pantry. She looked at all the labels on the jars and cans. "SALT!" she said clearly. "PEPPER! RICE! BEANS!"

Each word felt easier than the last. The more she practiced, the more confident she became.

When Papa Martinez found her, Penny was reading labels out loud. "Listen to you, Penny!" he said with a big smile. "Your words are so clear and strong!"

"I practiced and practiced," Penny said proudly. "At first I felt scared, but then I remembered that my voice can help people!"

Papa nodded. "That's right, Penny. When we practice communicating, we get better and better. And when we use our voices to help others, we feel good about ourselves."

Sofia came into the pantry too. "Penny, will you teach me to use my big voice like you do?"

"Of course!" Penny chirped excitedly. "Let's practice together! Say 'HELLO' really loud!"

"HELLO!" Sofia shouted, giggling.

"HELLO!" Penny replied, flapping her wings with joy.

From that day on, Penny and Sofia practiced using their voices together in the pantry. They learned that speaking clearly and loudly when needed was important. They also learned that helping others with their words made them both feel special and proud.

Penny discovered that the more she practiced communicating, the better she became. She wasn't shy anymore because she knew her voice was important and helpful.

"Every voice matters," Penny would tell herself each morning. "Even mine!"

The End.
