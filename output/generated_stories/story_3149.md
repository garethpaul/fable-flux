# Rooftop Songs Around the World

Rolo the raccoon loved music. He wore a tiny blue cap, and he carried a shiny tin drum. Every evening, he climbed a warm, flat rooftop to play. The sun turned the sky orange and pink. The air smelled like fresh bread from the bakery down the street. Pigeons cooed. A soft breeze tickled Rolo’s whiskers.

“Tap-tap-ta-TA!” Rolo drummed and hummed. “Tonight I sing a song for friends.”

A little girl named Mina waved from a nearby balcony. “Hi, Rolo! What are you playing?”

Rolo smiled. “A hello song! Everyone belongs in my song.”

From another window, Baba the barber called, “Play something gentle, my friend.” From the alley, Chef Lina shouted, “Play something spicy!” A bus driver parked her bus and listened. A cat curled up by the chimney and purred.

Rolo’s ears twitched. “We all like different music,” he said. “I can listen. I can learn.”

He took a deep breath. He put a paw on his chest to feel his heartbeat. Thump-thump. “First, I listen,” he whispered.

Mina climbed the stairs and joined him on the rooftop. She brought a bright red shaker. “Can I play too?”

“Yes!” said Rolo. “We will make a rooftop band. We will listen and take turns.”

Rolo tapped slow and soft. Mina shook her shaker like rain. “Shhh—shhhh,” it whispered. Then they stopped and listened.

From a window, a man named Mr. Aziz hummed a tune from his home far away. It rose and fell like waves. “La-la-LAAA, la-la-la.”

“That is beautiful,” Rolo said. “May we learn your song?”

Mr. Aziz smiled. “Of course. It is a greeting song. It says, ‘Welcome, welcome, friend.’”

Rolo repeated it softly. Mina tried too. “La-la-LAAA, la-la-la,” they sang together. The tune felt cozy and warm, like a hug. The cat’s tail flicked in time.

Then the bus driver, Ms. Joy, tapped the bus horn lightly. “Beep-beep-bop!” She laughed. “On my route, I hear many sounds. Some kids show me claps from their home countries.” She clapped a pattern: clap, clap, pause, clap.

Rolo listened. Mina listened. They tried the claps. “Clap, clap, … clap!” Rolo grinned. “Our band is growing.”

Chef Lina waved a wooden spoon. “I stir soup in a circle,” she said. “Swish-swish-swish. In my family, we sing while we cook.” She sang a quick, spicy tune. It made Rolo’s toes wiggle.

Baba the barber brushed hair with a soft swish. “In my shop, we like calm music,” he said. He hummed low and smooth, like a slow river.

Rolo felt his drum under his paws. The rooftop was a little world. Many people. Many sounds. Many feelings. He wanted to include everyone.

Mina whispered, “How can we play all the songs?”

Rolo took another deep breath. He listened to the pigeons coo. He heard the wind. He heard the city. He placed a paw on his drum. “We can take turns. We can make space. That is being good neighbors. That is being good global friends.”

He looked at the group. “First, we play Mr. Aziz’s welcome song. Then we add Ms. Joy’s claps. Then Chef Lina’s swish. Then Baba’s calm hum. And I will keep a gentle beat so all can join.”

Everyone nodded. “We will listen,” they said.

Rolo counted softly, “One, two, ready, breathe.” His drum went tap… tap… tap… like a steady heart. Mina shook like rain. Mr. Aziz sang, warm and bright. Ms. Joy clapped her pattern. Chef Lina circled her spoon, swish-swish-swish. Baba hummed low. The bus lights blinked. The sun slipped down, and tiny stars peeked out.

A small boy from the next roof watched with wide eyes. “I am shy,” he said. “Can I just listen?”

Rolo smiled kindly. “Yes. Listening is playing too. Your ears are part of our band.”

The little boy hugged his knees and listened hard. His shoulders relaxed. The music wrapped around him like a soft blanket.

Soon, a gust of wind made Rolo’s hat fly. “Oh no!” he squeaked. The hat danced toward the edge.

Mina gasped. “Careful!”

Rolo felt a flutter in his tummy. He wanted to run, but he remembered to breathe. “Courage can be small and careful,” he said. Step by slow step, he moved. Ms. Joy held the door open. The cat pressed a paw on the hat, pinning it. “Mrrp!” The hat stopped. Rolo picked it up. “Thank you, team,” he said. Everyone cheered.

Back in the music circle, Rolo said, “We took care of each other. We listened. We were brave and gentle.”

He turned to the small boy. “Would you like to try a tiny triangle?” He held out a shiny triangle with a soft ring. The boy nodded. Ting! A bright star sound filled the air.

Rolo’s eyes sparkled. “That sound is perfect.”

They played one more time, all together. Tap. Shhhh. La-la-LAAA. Clap, clap, … clap. Swish-swish. Hummmm. Ting!

The music felt like sunlight and soup and soft blankets and happy buses and calm rivers. It felt like people from many places saying, “Welcome. You belong.”

Rolo bowed. “Our rooftop band shows global citizenship. We listen. We include. We care for our wellness with calm breaths and kind hearts. We show tolerance by making space for all songs.”

Mina added, “And we take turns!”

Everyone laughed and clapped. The stars twinkled. The cat purred a sleepy purr.

Rolo put his paw on his drum. “Where we live, and where we come from, can be different,” he said. “But on this roof, under one sky, we can play together.”

“Together,” they all said.

The End.
