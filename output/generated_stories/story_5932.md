# Melody's Messy Rooftop

High up on a tall building's rooftop lived a little yellow bird named Melody. Every morning, she would stretch her tiny wings, fluff her bright feathers, and sing the most beautiful songs. "Tweet-tweet-trill!" she would chirp, watching the sun paint the sky orange and pink.

Melody loved her rooftop home. She had built her cozy nest in the corner where two walls met, using soft twigs and colorful string she found. The rooftop was her whole world – her kitchen, her bedroom, and her stage all in one!

But one windy Tuesday morning, something was very wrong.

"Oh no!" gasped Melody, looking around with her round black eyes. "What happened to my beautiful rooftop?"

Plastic bags danced in the wind like ghosts. Empty soda cans rolled back and forth with loud clanking sounds. Candy wrappers stuck to the walls, and there were so many things scattered everywhere that Melody could barely see the gray rooftop underneath.

The wind had blown trash from the street below all the way up to her home. Now her rooftop looked like a messy playground, but not the fun kind.

Melody tried to sing her morning song, but she felt too sad. "Tweet... tweet..." she chirped quietly. Her voice sounded as gloomy as she felt.

"I can't live like this," she said to herself, hopping carefully between a milk carton and a plastic bottle. "But this mess is too big for one little bird."

Just then, she heard voices from below. Looking down, she saw children walking to school with their teacher.

"Help!" called Melody as loudly as she could. "Tweet-tweet-HELP!"

A little girl with curly hair looked up. "Listen! Do you hear that bird?"

"She sounds sad," said a boy with a red backpack.

Their teacher, Mrs. Chen, shielded her eyes from the sun and looked up too. "Oh my! Look at all that trash on the rooftop. That poor bird's home is covered in litter."

"Can we help her?" asked the curly-haired girl.

Mrs. Chen smiled. "What a wonderful idea! Let's come back after school with gloves and bags."

Melody's heart fluttered with hope. Could these kind people really help her?

That afternoon, Mrs. Chen returned with ten children and the building's caretaker, Mr. Rodriguez. They climbed the stairs to the rooftop, carrying big trash bags and wearing bright yellow gloves.

"Look at this mess," said Mr. Rodriguez, shaking his head. "The wind brings trash up here all the time."

The children worked together like busy bees. They picked up every piece of trash – the noisy cans, the dancing plastic bags, the sticky wrappers. They sorted everything into different bags for recycling and garbage.

"We're helping the bird!" giggled one child, holding up an empty chip bag.

"And we're helping our whole neighborhood stay clean!" added another.

As they worked, Melody sang her happiest song. "Tweet-trill-tweet!" Her voice sparkled with joy and gratitude.

When they finished, the rooftop sparkled in the afternoon sun. Melody could see every inch of her gray home again.

"Thank you!" she chirped, though she knew they couldn't understand her words. But somehow, she felt they understood her grateful heart.

Mr. Rodriguez set up a small trash can on the rooftop. "I'll empty this every week," he promised. "And maybe we can put up a sign asking people not to litter."

From that day on, Melody sang the most beautiful morning songs anyone had ever heard. Her rooftop stayed clean, and she learned that when we all work together to take care of our world, everyone benefits – birds, people, and the whole Earth.

The End.
