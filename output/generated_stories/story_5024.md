# Kitty Conductor’s Tidy Tunes

In a sunny musical practice room, a clever cat named Coco padded in on soft paws. The room smelled like polished wood and fresh lemon cleaner. Sunbeams made warm squares on the floor. Drums, shakers, ribbons, and tiny bells were everywhere. They were pretty, but they were messy.

Coco’s whiskers twitched. “Meow-wow,” she said, “I want to dance today! But where is my blue ribbon?”

She looked under a drum. Thump! She peeked behind a chair. Squeak! She sniffed near the piano. Plink-plonk! No ribbon.

Coco sat and wrapped her tail around her paws. “I feel a little frustrated,” she told herself softly. “I want to dance with my ribbon. I need to be patient.”

Just then, her friend, Mr. Bongo, a round, kind drum with a smiling face painted on, spoke in a gentle voice. “Boom-boom, Coco. When things are everywhere, it’s hard to find what you need.”

Coco blinked. “You can talk?”

Mr. Bongo chuckled. “In this musical room, instruments talk when you listen. Try organization. When we put things where they belong, we can find them again.”

Coco tilted her head. “Organization? Is that a big word for putting things in the right place?”

“Boom-boom! Yes,” said Mr. Bongo. “Let’s make a plan.”

Coco stood tall. “A plan helps me be calm. A plan helps me be patient.” She took a deep breath in. The room smelled like pine sticks and soft drum skins. She breathed out slowly. “Okay. Step one: make spots for things.”

She found three baskets. One was red, one was yellow, and one was blue.

Coco tapped the red basket. “Red is for ribbons.” She tapped the yellow one. “Yellow is for shakers.” She tapped the blue one. “Blue is for bells.”

From the shelf, a tiny bell tinkled kindly. “Ting! Thank you for a home.”

Coco smiled. “You’re welcome.” She began to clean. She hummed a steady song, “Pick-up, put-away, find-it-another-day.” Her paws were quick and careful.

She swished a green ribbon into the red basket. She dropped a maraca into the yellow basket. Shake-shake! She set two tiny bells into the blue basket. Ting-ting!

As she worked, Coco heard sounds from far away. She imagined children dancing in different places. “In Brazil, friends shake shakers to samba,” she said, tapping the maraca. “In India, feet ring little bells in kathak,” she whispered, placing the bells gently. “In Ireland, shoes tap-tap a jig,” she added, tapping the floor with her paw. “Around the world, people dance. They keep their things ready.”

Mr. Bongo boomed softly. “That is global thinking, Coco. We can learn from friends everywhere. They care for their music tools.”

Coco nodded. “I can care for mine.” She picked up scarves and folded each one. The blue scarf felt cool and smooth. The red one felt warm like a hug. She stacked them in a neat pile. She lined up drumsticks in a row: straight, straight, straight.

Sometimes she found a toy and wanted to play right away. Her tail swished with excitement. But she remembered, “Patience. First put away. Then we dance.” She giggled and kept going.

At last, the room looked different. The floor was clear. The baskets were full. The sunbeams seemed to clap.

Mr. Bongo smiled with his painted mouth. “Boom-boom! Step two: make labels.”

Coco drew simple pictures on sticky notes. She drew a ribbon on the red basket, a shaker on the yellow, and a bell on the blue. “Now I can see where things go,” she purred.

“Step three?” asked Mr. Bongo.

Coco’s eyes shone. “Dance time!”

She looked into the red basket. There it was—her blue ribbon! It was soft and silky. She wrapped it around her paw. “I found it because I was organized,” she said happily.

Music floated in from the hallway, a gentle beat, thump-thump, swish-swish. Coco began to dance. She twirled and leaped. The ribbon made loops like clouds. She felt the cool floor under her paws, and the air brushed her whiskers.

“Spin, step, swish!” she sang. “I can play because I put away.”

Mr. Bongo kept a quiet beat. Boom, boom, boom. The bells in the blue basket jingled softly, like tiny stars. The shakers in the yellow basket hummed like bees.

Coco paused and looked around. Everything had a place. Everything looked calm. Her heart felt calm too.

She picked up a small shaker and spoke to it kindly. “When dance time is done, I will put you back. That is organization. That is care.”

After one more big twirl, Coco bowed. “Thank you, room. Thank you, plan.”

She took a sip of cool water and wiped a little sweat from her nose with a soft cloth. She put the shaker back in the yellow basket. She placed the ribbon back in the red basket. She smiled and patted each label.

Mr. Bongo boomed a happy goodbye. “You did it, Coco. You showed patience. You learned organization. Now you can find your things, and you can share. Music and dance are more fun when the room is ready.”

Coco purred. “When we make a place for things, we make a place for joy.”

She turned off the light. Click. The room rested, neat and cozy, ready for tomorrow’s song.

The End.
