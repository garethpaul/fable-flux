# Leo the Lion Cub Stands Up for What's Right

In a cozy nursery filled with soft blankets and colorful toys, lived a brave little lion cub named Leo. His golden fur was fluffy like a dandelion, and his tiny roar sounded more like a squeaky toy than a mighty lion. Leo loved playing with all his nursery friends - the gentle elephant Ellie, the bouncy rabbit Ruby, and the quiet mouse Max.

Every morning, the friends would gather on the big rainbow rug to play together. They shared toys, told stories, and laughed until their bellies hurt. The nursery smelled like warm milk and lavender, making everyone feel safe and happy.

But one sunny Tuesday, something wasn't right. Leo noticed that Benny the bear was being mean to little Max the mouse.

"You can't play with us, Max!" Benny growled loudly. "You're too small and quiet. Go play by yourself!"

Max's whiskers drooped, and his tiny eyes filled with tears. He scurried to the corner with his small cheese toy, looking very sad.

Leo's heart felt heavy like a rock. He knew this wasn't fair. Max was always kind and shared his toys with everyone. Leo's mama had taught him that being brave meant standing up for what's right, even when it felt scary.

"That's not nice, Benny," Leo said, his voice shaking a little like autumn leaves. "Max is our friend. He should be able to play with us too."

Benny puffed out his chest. "Says who? I'm bigger than both of you!"

Leo took a deep breath that smelled like the sweet flowers by the nursery window. He remembered what his mama said about using words instead of claws when there was a problem.

"Benny," Leo said more clearly, "how would you feel if someone said you couldn't play because you were different?"

Benny paused, his brown eyes looking confused.

Leo continued gently, "Max makes our games more fun! Remember yesterday when he helped us find the lost puzzle piece with his tiny paws? And he always shares his crackers at snack time."

Ellie the elephant trumpeted softly in agreement. "Leo's right! Max is really good at hide-and-seek because he's small."

Ruby the rabbit hopped closer. "And Max tells the best bedtime stories!"

Leo had a creative idea that made his eyes sparkle like stars. "Benny, what if we made up a new game where everyone's different sizes help the team? You're strong, Max is small and quick, Ellie is tall, and Ruby is fast!"

Benny's frown slowly turned upside down. "That... that actually sounds fun."

Leo walked over to Max, who was still sniffling in the corner. "Max, would you like to help us create this new game?"

Max's ears perked up like tiny pink flowers. "Really? You want me to help?"

"Of course!" Leo said warmly. "We're all friends here, and friends include everyone."

Soon, all the nursery friends were working together, creating the most wonderful game where everyone's special talents mattered. Benny apologized to Max with a gentle hug that smelled like honey.

As they played their new game, Leo felt proud and warm inside, like sunshine filling his chest. He had learned that being brave and speaking up for others made everyone happier.

"Thank you, Leo," Max squeaked gratefully. "You're a true friend."

Leo smiled his biggest smile. Sometimes being brave meant using kind words to make things fair for everyone.

The End.
