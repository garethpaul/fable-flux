# Roo’s Gentle Clicks

Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and bouncy feet. One sunny morning, Roo hopped into the home office. The room smelled like warm paper and lemony cleaner. The computer hummed softly. A keyboard clicked, click-click, like tiny raindrops. A tall chair spun a little, squeak-squeak.

Mama worked in the home office. She smiled and waved. “Good morning, Roo,” she said. “I’m working. It’s quiet time.”

Roo looked at the keyboard. “Can I help, Mama?” Roo asked, ears wiggling.

“You can learn,” Mama said kindly. “We can build a new skill today. We will learn gentle clicks. Not big thumps.”

Roo’s tail thumped. Thump! “Oops,” Roo whispered.

Mama pointed to a small timer. “We will try a skill-building game,” she said. “We will practice wait, watch, and gentle.”

Roo liked games. “I can do it!” Roo chirped.

Mama showed Roo a big, colorful notepad and two pencils. One pencil was blue, and one was green. The notepad smelled sweet, like new paper. “These are for sharing,” Mama said. “We share so we can both do our work.”

Roo took the green pencil. “I will share the blue one,” Roo said. Roo felt proud.

Mama set the timer. Tick-tick-tick. “First, we watch,” Mama said. She placed her fingers on the keys. Click-click. “Soft fingers. Gentle clicks.”

Roo watched. Roo listened. The clicks were calm, like tiny feet on sand. Roo’s paws wiggled in the air. “Can I try?” Roo asked, voice small and excited.

“In a moment,” Mama said. “We are practicing wait.” Mama pointed to a small picture chart on the desk. The chart had three stars: Watch, Try, Share. “When the timer rings, it’s Try time.”

Roo took a big breath. In, out. The lemony smell tickled Roo’s nose. Roo drew a circle on the notepad with the green pencil. Swish-swish. “I am waiting,” Roo whispered.

The timer rang. Ding! Roo hopped closer, heart beat going pat-pat-pat. Mama smiled. “Now you can try. Gentle clicks.”

Roo put two small paws on the edge of the keyboard. “Soft,” Roo said to the paws. Click…click…click. The sound was quiet and friendly.

“You did it!” Mama cheered softly. “That is gentle.”

Roo’s chest felt warm and bright. “I can do gentle clicks!” Roo sang.

Then Roo saw something new. A pile of papers sat on the desk. They were slippery and shiny. Roo reached for them. Swoosh! The pile slid to the floor. Flump!

Roo’s eyes got big. “Oh no,” Roo said. “I made a mess.”

Mama looked surprised, then took a slow breath. “It’s okay, Roo. Accidents happen. We can fix it together.”

Roo felt shy. “I am sorry, Mama.”

“Thank you for saying sorry,” Mama said. “That shows kindness. Let’s solve the problem. We can use our skill-building steps. Watch, Try, Share.”

Roo nodded. “Watch first,” Roo whispered. Roo watched Mama pick up one paper at a time. Tap-tap to make the edges neat. Roo watched carefully.

“Now you try,” Mama said.

Roo bent down. The carpet felt soft under Roo’s paws. The papers felt cool and smooth, like leaves in shade. Roo picked up one paper. Tap-tap. Then another. Tap-tap. Roo smiled. “I can do it!”

“You are learning,” Mama said.

“Now we share,” Roo said. Roo passed a paper to Mama. “We can do it together.”

They worked side by side. Tap-tap. Tap-tap. The papers were neat again. The home office was calm again.

“Problem solved,” Mama said. “How do you feel?”

Roo touched Roo’s chest. “I feel proud. My body is warm and happy.”

Mama nodded. “You used your skills. Wait, watch, gentle, and share.”

Roo’s ears perked. “Can we practice more?” Roo asked.

“Yes,” Mama said. She brought out stickers. Stars, hearts, and tiny moons. “Let’s build a Job Chart. When we practice a skill, we add a sticker.”

Roo clapped. Clap-clap. “I love stickers!”

They made small jobs: Gentle Clicks, Paper Pick-Up, Pencil Share, Quiet Feet. Mama read each job. Roo traced the words with a paw. The letters felt bumpy and fun.

“Let’s try Quiet Feet,” Mama said. “Soft steps in the office.”

Roo stood tall. Roo lifted one foot. Step. Lifted the other. Step. The floor felt cool and smooth. No thumps. Only whisper steps. “I did Quiet Feet!” Roo giggled.

“Sticker time,” Mama said. She placed a golden star on the chart.

Roo’s eyes sparkled. “My turn to share!” Roo held out the green pencil. “Mama, would you like the green? I can use the blue.”

“Thank you for sharing,” Mama said. “That is very thoughtful.”

Roo felt a new feeling. It was soft and big, like a warm blanket. “Mama,” Roo said, “when I dropped the papers, were you worried?”

“I was,” Mama said gently. “But I also felt love. I knew we could fix it. When you are gentle and you share, the office feels safe. That helps me work.”

Roo nodded slowly. “I want you to feel safe. I can help.”

The timer ticked again. Tick-tick-tick. Roo looked at the keyboard. “Can I do gentle clicks while you work?” Roo asked.

“Yes,” Mama said. “Three gentle clicks. Then we switch and share.”

Roo counted softly. “One…two…three.” Click…click…click. Then Roo slid the chair to Mama. Squeak-squeak. “Your turn.”

Mama smiled. “That is great sharing.”

Roo grinned. “I learned new skills today. Gentle clicks, quiet feet, and careful hands. I can wait, watch, try, and share.”

Mama hugged Roo. The hug smelled like soap and sunshine. “You did, Roo. And when we use our skills, we help each other. That is empathy.”

Roo hugged back. “I like helping.”

In the calm home office, the computer hummed. The keyboard clicked. The timer ticked. Roo’s heart felt steady and proud. The Job Chart shone with stickers, bright as tiny stars.

“We did it,” Roo whispered. “We solved our problems with skills, empathy, and sharing.”

The End.
