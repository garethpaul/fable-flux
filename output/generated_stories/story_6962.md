# Tessa Tortoise and the Tumble of Towels

In a bright laundry room, Tessa the wise tortoise moved slowly across the cool tile floor. She liked the hum of the washer and the soft whoosh of the dryer. The warm air smelled like clean soap and fresh cotton. Swish-swish went the washer. Hummm went the dryer. Tessa smiled. “This is a good place to think and learn,” she said.

On a small step stool sat Benny Bunny, kicking his feet. “I want my red towel now!” Benny said, scrunching his nose. He tugged on the dryer door. Clunk-clunk. It was still spinning.

Across the room, Mia Mouse carefully folded socks on a basket lid. “We wait our turn, Benny,” Mia squeaked. “The towels will be ready soon.”

“I don’t want soon,” Benny said loudly. His ears drooped. “I want now!”

Tessa blinked slowly and took a deep breath. She listened to the machines. Swish-swish. Hummm. Then she spoke in a calm, low voice. “Hello, Benny. Hello, Mia. I hear big feelings.”

Benny huffed. “I need my towel for my bath. If I don’t get it, I will hop and stomp!”

Tessa nodded. “Big feelings can make our bodies want to hop and stomp.” She placed a gentle foot on the tile. “Let’s learn what to do with big feelings. We can cool down, then solve the problem.”

Mia smiled at Tessa. “How do we cool down?”

Tessa pointed to her shell. “I breathe in like I’m smelling warm cookies.” She sniffed. “And I breathe out like I’m blowing a bubble.” She puffed. “Let’s try together.”

Benny wiggled his nose. He breathed in, smelling the soap. He breathed out, slow and steady. “Smell the cookie. Blow the bubble,” he whispered.

“Again,” Tessa said kindly.

They did it three times. In, out. In, out. In, out.

Benny’s ears lifted. “I feel softer inside,” he said.

“Good,” said Tessa. “When we feel softer, we can listen and learn.” She tapped the dryer door. “This door is hot and locked while it spins. We must keep our bodies safe. We also need fairness. Others are waiting, too.”

Mia nodded. “I am waiting for my tiny socks,” she said, holding up a striped pair. “They are small, but they matter.”

Benny looked at Mia’s socks. “They do matter,” he said. “I want my towel, and you want your socks. We both want things.”

Tessa smiled. “That’s right. When we all want things, we take turns. We show respect. We can help each other. Let’s make a plan.”

Benny’s eyes grew curious. “A plan?”

“A plan helps us learn what to do,” Tessa said. “First, we wait in a line by the basket. We use gentle hands. We use kind words.”

Mia clapped softly. “I like lines.”

“The dryer will stop soon,” Tessa said. “When it stops, we listen for the ding. Then we take turns together. We can sort quickly if we help each other.”

Benny bounced on his toes but kept his hands to himself. “What if my turn feels too slow?”

“Use your calm breath,” said Tessa. “And you can count the hums. One hum, two hums, three hums…”

Benny started counting. “Four hums, five hums,” he giggled.

The dryer clicked. Then it sang a happy ding! The warm door opened with a soft sigh. A puff of clean, cozy air floated out. “Mmm,” said Mia. “It smells like sunshine.”

Tessa pointed to the basket. “Mia is first for socks. Benny is second for the red towel. I will watch and help.”

Mia reached in and pulled out a pair of tiny socks. “Found them!” she cheered. She held the warm socks to her cheeks. “Thank you for waiting, Benny.”

Benny smiled. “You’re welcome.”

“Now Benny’s turn,” said Tessa.

Benny reached in with gentle hands. He moved the towels slowly. “Blue…yellow…green…” He found the red towel at the bottom. “Here it is!” He hugged it. “It’s so warm!”

Tessa nodded. “You waited. You breathed. You used kind words. That is strong learning.”

Benny’s cheeks glowed. “I did it!”

“There are more towels,” Mia said. “Let’s fold them and help.”

Tessa showed them how to fold. “Match the corners,” she said. “Press the edges. Smooth like a calm pond.” Swish went their hands. Pat, pat went the folds.

They worked together. They made a neat stack. Benny handed Mia a little towel. Mia passed Tessa the big ones. They moved like a team.

When they finished, the laundry room looked tidy. The air felt peaceful, like a quiet hug.

Benny bounced once more. “I learned a lot today,” he said. “When I feel mad, I can breathe. I can wait my turn. I can help.”

Mia added, “And we show respect. We use fairness. We use gentle hands.”

Tessa’s eyes twinkled. “You both learned and helped. That is wise.” She lifted her head and spoke softly, so the words felt warm like the towels. “When we learn, we grow. When we help, we glow.”

Benny wrapped his red towel around his shoulders like a cape. “I am a helper,” he said proudly.

Mia wiggled her tiny socks. “I am a helper, too.”

Tessa smiled. “And I am a helper with you.”

They listened once more to the washer’s swish and the dryer’s hum. The room felt friendly and fair. Everyone had what they needed, and everyone had learned what to do with big feelings.

The End.
