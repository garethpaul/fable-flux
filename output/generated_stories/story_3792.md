# Pippa Penguin’s Rooftop Dance

Pippa was a cheerful penguin with shiny black feathers and a bright yellow scarf. She loved to dance. One sunny morning, Pippa waddled up the stairs to a tall building. Step, step, step. She opened the door to the rooftop. Whoosh! A gentle wind kissed her face. She could smell warm tar and fresh air. The roof felt bouncy under her feet.

“Today, I will dance!” Pippa chirped, clapping her little flippers. “I will dance for the birds and the sky!”

She set down a small music player. Click. Music floated into the air, soft and happy. Pippa tapped one foot. Tap tap. She swished her scarf. Swish swish. She twirled like a little top. Whee!

A pair of pigeons landed nearby. “Coo-coo,” they sang.

“Hello, friends!” Pippa said. “Would you like to watch my dance?”

“We’d love to,” said the gray pigeon. “But be careful. The wind is strong.”

Pippa took a deep breath. She felt the wind push at her tummy. “I will be brave and patient,” she said. “I will listen to the wind and make my steps slow.”

She started again. Tap, tap. Pause. Tap, tap. Pause. The wind blew her scarf across her face. “Peekaboo!” Pippa giggled. She gently moved the scarf and kept dancing. The pigeons clapped their wings. Flap flap!

Suddenly, Pippa noticed a shiny can rolling across the roof. Clank, clank, clank. It bumped into a little plant growing in a corner. The tiny green leaves shook.

“Oh no!” Pippa said. She pressed pause on the music. “We must take care of the rooftop garden.”

She waddled toward the plant. A pair of sparrows hopped up. “We found more bits of trash,” they chirped. “A plastic ring and a bag that goes crinkle.”

Pippa nodded. “Thank you for telling me. Clean roof, safe roof! Clean world, happy world!” She put on her small gloves and picked up the can. Clink. She freed a leaf that was stuck in the ring. Snap. She folded the bag. Crinkle.

The wind gusted. Whoosh! The bag tried to fly. Pippa held on tight. “I will be patient,” she said softly. “I will use slow, careful hands.” She tucked the trash into a bin. The sparrows peeped, “Thank you!”

Pippa patted the little plant. “You are safe now,” she whispered.

She turned back to her music, but a big seagull landed with a thump. “This is my sun spot,” the seagull squawked. “Your dance takes up space.”

Pippa felt a flutter in her belly. She took a slow breath. In… and out. She remembered: brave heart, kind words.

In a calm voice, Pippa said, “Hello, Seagull. My name is Pippa. I love to dance. I want to share the roof. Can we make a plan?”

The seagull tilted his head. “A plan?”

“Yes,” Pippa said. “I can dance in this square. You can have your sun spot in that square. We can both be happy.” Pippa pointed to chalk lines on the roof. The chalk felt dusty on her flippers as she drew a simple box. Scratch, scratch.

The seagull looked at the sunny patch. He ruffled his feathers. “Hmm.” He liked the warm sun. He liked Pippa’s kind voice too. “All right,” he said. “We can share.”

“Thank you,” Pippa smiled. “Would you like to watch my dance from your spot?”

The seagull nodded. The pigeons and sparrows gathered, too. Even a tiny ladybug crawled onto a leaf. The rooftop felt friendly and full.

Pippa pressed play. The music bubbled up again. La-la-la! She stood still for a moment. She felt the wind on her face. She listened to the beat. She was patient. When the wind paused, she began:

“Tap, tap,” went her feet.
“Swish, swish,” went her scarf.
“Spin!” went Pippa, turning with care.
“Slide,” went her little step.

She danced like the wind. She danced like the birds. She danced like a wave on the sea. She made a move she called the Trash Pick-Up Twist. Down, twist, up! The pigeons cooed. The sparrows chirped. The seagull gave a happy squawk.

“Look,” Pippa said between songs. “We can dance and care for our world.” She showed them the Sweep-Step: step, sweep, toss in the bin! The birds tried it too. Flap, step, sweep! It was silly and fun. They laughed together. Hee-hee!

A small cloud drifted over the sun. The air turned cool. Pippa wanted to spin fast, but the roof was a little slippery now. She stopped. She waited. She felt with her feet. “Patience,” she whispered. “Slow and safe.”

When the sun peeked out again, she finished with a gentle bow. The wind clapped in soft puffs. The birds cheered!

The seagull waddled over. “Thank you, Pippa,” he said. “You danced with care. You were brave and kind. You shared the roof. I learned the Sweep-Step!”

Pippa giggled. “I learned to listen, to be patient, and to clean as I go. Dancing can help the Earth.”

She looked at the tiny plant. It stood tall and green. The rooftop was tidy. The sky was bright. Her heart felt light and warm.

Pippa waved to her friends. “Let’s dance again tomorrow,” she said. “We’ll dance, we’ll care, and we’ll share.”

“Yes!” everyone chirped and cooed.

And that day, on a sunny rooftop, Pippa the cheerful penguin showed that dance can teach us many things: be brave, be patient, share and care. Step by step, we can make the world better—together.

The End.
