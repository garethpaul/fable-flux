# Bubbles on the Front Porch

Bubbles the playful dolphin loved to visit her friend Mia. Mia’s house had a peaceful front porch with a soft blue rug, a rocking chair that creaked gently, and flower pots that smelled sweet like oranges. Wind chimes tinkled in the breeze. The sun made warm spots on the wooden steps.

“Hi, Bubbles!” Mia laughed, opening the screen door with a squeak. “Want to help with porch chores?”

Bubbles clapped her fins and splashed a tiny sprinkle of porch water from her little bucket. “Yes! Let’s make it shiny and clean!”

On the porch sat three bins: a green one for leaves, a blue one for recycling, and a gray one for trash. The blue bin had a big label with a circle made of arrows.

Mia pointed. “We will sweep the porch, and we will sort the trash. We will take turns.”

Bubbles wiggled. “I can sweep! I can sort! I can do everything!”

Mia smiled and handed her a small broom. “We can take turns. First me, then you.”

The broom made a soft swish-swish on the porch. Mia swept a little pile of paper, a plastic bottle, and some shiny foil. Bubbles watched, tail flicking happily. Then Mia passed the broom to Bubbles.

“Your turn,” Mia said.

Bubbles took the broom and swish-swish-swept under the rocking chair. Dust danced in the sunlight like tiny glitter. A leaf tickled Bubbles’ nose. “Achoo!” She giggled.

From the yard, their neighbor boy, Leo, waved. “Can I help?”

“Yes!” Mia called. “We are taking turns.”

Leo climbed the steps. The porch smelled like soap and fresh flowers. The wind chimes sang again. Bubbles looked at the shiny foil. “Is foil trash?”

Mia nodded slowly. “Foil goes in the recycling if it is clean. Is it clean?”

Bubbles sniffed. “Smells like sandwich.” She wrinkled her nose. “Not clean.”

“Then it goes in the gray bin,” Mia said. “We can try to rinse next time.”

Bubbles picked up the foil and dropped it into the gray bin. Clink.

Leo found a cardboard tube. “Blue bin?” he asked.

Mia smiled. “Yes, that is recycling.”

Leo placed it gently in the blue bin. Thunk.

Bubbles saw the plastic bottle. “Blue bin,” she said proudly, and she put it in. Plonk!

A little ant crawled near the step, carrying a crumb. Bubbles watched. “Look, the ant is working too.”

Mia whispered, “We will be gentle. The ant is busy. Taking turns means letting others move and work too.”

Bubbles nodded. “We can wait,” she said softly. They waited a moment and let the ant walk by. Then they swept that spot.

Soon, the porch looked tidy. The rug felt soft under their toes and fins. The air felt warm and happy. Bubbles bounced. “Now the rocking chair!”

Mia touched the chair. “Let’s take turns rocking. One minute each.”

Bubbles’ eyes sparkled. “I want to go first!” She reached for the chair.

Leo raised his hand. “I haven’t had a turn yet.”

Bubbles paused. Her fin trembled. She really wanted to rock. The chair looked so comfy. Creak, creak, it seemed to sing her name.

Mia placed a gentle hand on Bubbles’ fin. “We can use kind words. We can also take turns.”

Bubbles took a deep breath and listened to the wind chimes. Ting-ting-ting. She looked at Leo’s hopeful face. She looked at the blue bin full of good choices. She remembered how the ant got a turn to walk.

Bubbles smiled. “Leo can go first. I will wait.”

Leo grinned and rocked, creak, creak, creak. He counted, “One… two… three…” He felt the breeze on his cheeks. When a minute was done, he stood up. “Your turn, Bubbles.”

Bubbles sat and rocked, creak, creak, creak. The chair felt smooth and warm. “Thank you for waiting,” she told Mia when she finished.

Mia sat next and rocked slow like a lullaby. “Thank you both for taking turns.”

A little bluebird landed on the porch rail and chirped. Cheep-cheep! It pecked at a tiny thread of straw and flew to its nest.

Bubbles watched with soft eyes. “The bird is building a home. That is like recycling too. It is using what it finds.”

Mia nodded. “Yes, we can be gentle with the earth. We can sort, we can clean, and we can share.”

Bubbles picked up a crumpled paper cup on the step. She checked it. “Clean and dry,” she said, smiling. She put it in the blue bin. Plop.

Then Bubbles looked at Leo. “Do you want the last rocking turn?” she asked.

Leo blinked. “You are giving me another turn?”

Bubbles nodded. “Taking turns is like a game. When we wait, everyone feels happy.”

Leo rocked once more and then hopped up. “Your kindness feels warm,” he said, touching his heart.

Bubbles touched her fin to her chest. She felt warm too, like sunshine on the porch.

Mia clapped softly. “We worked together. We showed compassion. We took turns and we cared for our porch and our planet.”

Bubbles flicked her tail and laughed. “Taking turns makes the day smooth, like calm water.”

They sat together on the peaceful front porch, listening to the wind chimes and the gentle creak of the chair. The blue bin was full, the gray bin was small, and the green bin smelled like fresh leaves. The porch felt clean, kind, and calm.

Today’s lesson, Bubbles thought, is simple and sweet: take turns, be gentle, and care for our home.

The End.
