# Milo the Brave Monkey Speaks Up

In a cozy nursery filled with soft toys and colorful books, lived a little monkey named Milo. The nursery had fluffy blue carpets, warm yellow walls, and a big window that let in golden sunshine. Milo loved swinging from the mobile above his crib and playing with his favorite red ball.

One morning, Milo woke up feeling different. His tummy felt rumbly and his head felt heavy. "I don't feel good," he said quietly to himself, touching his warm forehead with his tiny paw.

Miss Sarah, his caregiver, came into the nursery with a big smile. "Good morning, Milo! Time for breakfast and then we'll play outside!"

Milo wanted to tell Miss Sarah how he felt, but he felt shy. He thought maybe the feeling would go away. So he climbed down from his cozy bed and followed her to the breakfast table.

At breakfast, Milo looked at his bowl of bananas and oatmeal. Usually, he loved this yummy meal, but today his tummy felt too upset to eat. He pushed the bowl away with his paw.

"Come on, Milo," said Miss Sarah kindly. "You need to eat your breakfast to grow big and strong!"

Milo felt confused. He wanted to be good, but his body was telling him something important. He remembered what his mama had taught him: "When your body talks to you, you should listen and tell a grown-up."

Taking a deep breath, Milo stood up tall on his chair. "Miss Sarah," he said in his clearest voice, "my tummy hurts and my head feels hot. I don't think I can eat right now."

Miss Sarah stopped what she was doing and looked at Milo carefully. She knelt down beside him and gently felt his forehead. "Oh my! You do feel warm, little one. Thank you for telling me how you feel. That was very brave and smart of you."

"Really?" asked Milo, his brown eyes wide.

"Yes, really!" Miss Sarah smiled. "When we tell grown-ups how our bodies feel, we help them take better care of us. That's called speaking up for yourself."

Miss Sarah got a soft, cool washcloth and placed it on Milo's forehead. It felt wonderful and soothing. Then she brought him a special cup with cool water and arranged his favorite stuffed elephant next to him on the couch.

"Now you can rest here where it's comfortable," she said, fluffing the pillows. "I'll call the doctor to make sure you're okay."

As Milo snuggled into the soft cushions, he felt proud. Speaking up had been a little scary, but it helped him feel better. Miss Sarah knew just what to do because he had been brave enough to use his words.

"Miss Sarah?" Milo said softly.

"Yes, sweetie?"

"Thank you for listening to me and helping me feel better."

"Thank you for being brave enough to tell me," Miss Sarah replied, gently patting his paw. "Remember, your feelings and your body are important. You should always tell a grown-up when something doesn't feel right."

By afternoon, after resting and drinking lots of water, Milo felt much better. He learned that speaking up for himself wasn't just okay – it was the right thing to do. From that day on, whenever Milo needed something or felt uncomfortable, he remembered to use his voice and ask for help.

The End.
