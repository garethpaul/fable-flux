# Panda Pippa’s Rooftop Craft Day

Every morning, Panda Pippa followed her gentle routine. She stretched her soft arms—reach, reach!—and wiggled her round toes—wiggle, wiggle! She brushed her teeth with a minty swish, ate crunchy bamboo that went snap-snap, and took slow, happy breaths. Then she climbed the sunny steps to the rooftop.

The rooftop garden smelled like sweet flowers and fresh leaves. The breeze felt cool on Pippa’s fur. Birds went chirp-chirp. Wind chimes sang ting-ting. Pippa smiled. “Today is craft day,” she said. “I will make a kind kite!”

She laid out her craft mat: a big blue blanket. She set her supplies in a neat line, just like her routine taught her. “Paper, string, sticks, tape, and paint,” Pippa said softly. “I am ready.”

Her friend, Squeaky the squirrel, scampered up the ladder. “Hi, Pippa! Can I help?” Squeaky asked, his tail flick-flicking.

“Yes,” Pippa said. “We will craft together. First, we plan.” She drew a simple kite shape with a chalk line. “We measure, we cut, we tape, and we test.”

Squeaky bounced. “Let’s paint first! Red! Red! Red!”

Pippa took a calm breath. “We will paint after we build. That is our routine. Plan, build, decorate.” She smiled. “We can add red and gold stars later.”

Squeaky nodded and tried to sit still. “Okay. Plan, build, decorate.”

They measured the paper. Snip-snip went the scissors. Squeaky slid a stick too fast, and the paper crinkled. “Oh no!” Squeaky said, ears drooping.

Pippa patted the paper gently. “It’s okay. We will be patient. Smooth and slow.” Together they pressed the paper flat. Pippa held the corners while Squeaky taped carefully. Stick, tape, press. The kite shape grew strong.

Thump, thump. Heavy feet came up the stairs. It was Bruno the big bear. He carried a box of shiny ribbons that jingled. “I love ribbons!” Bruno boomed. “I will use all the ribbons for my tail.”

Squeaky blinked. “All of them? We need some for our kite tail.”

Bruno frowned. “But I brought them.”

Pippa took another calm breath. “Let’s use fair turns,” she said. “That is justice. We can share and make a plan.”

Bruno grumbled. “I want a long tail.”

Pippa nodded. “We can negotiate. We can talk and choose. What if you pick four ribbons, we pick four ribbons, and we tie the rest together for all three kites to share?”

Bruno scratched his chin. “Hmm.”

Pippa added, “We can also trade colors. If you like blue, we can give you two blues. We like yellow stars.”

Squeaky chimed in, “And we will help tie the knots. They are tricky!”

Bruno’s frown turned into a small smile. “Okay. Four for me, four for you, and a big shared tail.” He handed Pippa a bright yellow ribbon. “For your stars.”

“Thank you,” Pippa said. “That is fair and kind.”

They kept crafting. Pippa guided the steps. “Now we add the string.” She tied a steady knot—pull, pull, snug. Squeaky repeated the knot—pull, pull, snug. Bruno held the kite steady with his big paws. The string hummed in the breeze, a soft mmm sound.

“Time to decorate!” Squeaky squeaked.

Pippa smiled. “Yes. Now is the decorate step.” They dabbed paint with small brushes. Dot, dot, dab. The paint smelled a little like lemons. Pippa painted gold stars. Squeaky made tiny red zigzags. Bruno stamped big blue circles with a sponge.

When the paint dried, Pippa checked everything. “Tape is tight. Knots are strong. Ribbons are fair.” She nodded. “We are ready to move!”

They stood in a row. The sky was bright and wide. The rooftop felt warm under their paws. “We run together,” Pippa said. “Slow to start. Then fast. Count with me. One, two, three—go!”

Their feet went pat-pat-pat across the rooftop. The kite tails fluttered—flit-flit-flutter! The kites lifted and swished. Up, up they went, dancing in the wind. Squeaky laughed. “It tickles my ears!” Bruno rumbled a happy “Ha!”

But then a gust pushed Squeaky’s kite toward a wobbly edge. “Oh!” Squeaky squealed.

Pippa kept her voice calm. “Plant your feet. Bend your knees. Pull steady, not fast.” She showed him how. Squeaky copied—bend, pull, breathe. The kite floated back to the middle. “I did it!” he cheered.

Bruno’s ribbon knot slipped and dangled. “My tail!” he cried.

Pippa said, “Pause and fix.” She set her kite down gently and knelt by Bruno. “I will help. You hold. I tie.” She tied the knot again—around, through, pull snug. “There. Teamwork.”

Soon the three friends stood together, kites shining like little stars in the blue sky. The wind sang. The chimes chimed. Their hearts felt light.

Bruno said, “I am glad we shared. It was fair.”

Squeaky said, “I am glad we followed the steps: plan, build, decorate.”

Pippa smiled, warm and proud. “And we were patient. We used kind words. We moved our bodies safely. We solved problems together.”

They rolled up the strings and tidied the rooftop. Pippa put the tools away in the same neat line. “Routine helps us,” she said. “It keeps things safe and smooth.”

As the sun began to yawn, they sipped cool water and munched crunchy apple slices. The day tasted sweet.

Pippa gave a gentle hug. “Today we learned: Craft with a plan. Share with fairness. Use calm words to make a deal. Move safely. Be patient.”

Squeaky and Bruno nodded, eyes bright. “Let’s craft again tomorrow!”

“Yes,” Pippa said softly. “Crafting makes kind hearts.”

The End.
