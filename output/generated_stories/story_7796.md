# Ollie's Dancing Day

Ollie the otter loved to wiggle and jiggle on Grandma Willow's peaceful front porch. The wooden boards felt smooth under his tiny paws, and the morning sun made everything warm and cozy.

"Good morning, Ollie!" called Grandma Willow from her rocking chair. "What are you doing today?"

"I'm practicing my new dance!" Ollie squeaked excitedly. He spun around in circles, his fluffy tail swishing back and forth. "Look at me go!"

Just then, his friend Bella the bunny hopped up the porch steps. Her ears drooped down sadly.

"Hi, Ollie," Bella said quietly. "Can I sit here? I don't feel like playing today."

Ollie stopped spinning. "What's wrong, Bella? You look sad."

Bella's whiskers twitched. "My brother and I had a big fight this morning. He said my dancing was silly and made fun of my hop-step move. Now I don't want to dance anymore."

Ollie's heart felt heavy for his friend. He thought about how happy dancing made him feel. The music in his head always made his paws want to move!

"Dancing isn't silly," Ollie said gently. "Dancing is wonderful! It makes me feel like I'm floating on water."

"But what if he's right?" Bella sniffled. "What if I really do look funny?"

Grandma Willow rocked slowly in her chair. "You know, children, when I was young, my grandmother taught me that dancing comes from the heart. Every family has their own special way of moving."

Ollie's eyes grew bright. "Really? Tell us more, Grandma Willow!"

"Well," she smiled, "otters have always danced like flowing water. Bunnies dance with happy hops. Birds dance with fluttering wings. Everyone's dance is beautiful because it's theirs."

Ollie felt an idea bubbling up inside him like a happy stream. "Bella, what if we talked to your brother together? Maybe he doesn't understand that dancing is special."

Bella's ears perked up a little. "You would do that with me?"

"Of course!" Ollie took a deep breath to feel calm and brave. "Sometimes when people say mean things, they're feeling bad inside too. Maybe your brother needs to learn about dancing like we did."

Bella thought about this. The porch felt so peaceful with the gentle breeze and Grandma Willow's kind smile.

"Okay," Bella said softly. "Let's go talk to him. But first, will you show me your otter dance? I want to learn something new."

Ollie grinned and began to move like water flowing down a stream. His paws glided smoothly, and his tail swayed like river grass. "This is how my family has always danced," he explained.

Bella watched carefully, then showed Ollie her special bunny hop-step. "This is my family's dance," she said proudly.

"That's beautiful!" Ollie clapped his paws. "Your hop-step is so bouncy and fun!"

Together, they practiced combining their dances. Ollie flowed while Bella hopped, and they both giggled at how wonderful it looked.

"Now I feel ready to talk to my brother," Bella said, standing tall. "I'll tell him about family dances and ask him to show me his moves too."

"And I'll help explain that all dancing is special," Ollie added.

Grandma Willow nodded approvingly. "When we talk calmly and share what we know, problems become much smaller."

Hand in paw, Ollie and Bella headed off to find Bella's brother, ready to turn their conflict into a dance party.

The End.
