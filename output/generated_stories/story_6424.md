# Sparky Leads the Way

In a cozy basement lab, lights blinked and tools clicked. The floor felt cool and smooth. The air smelled like warm metal and fresh soap. A helpful robot dog named Sparky wagged a shiny tail that went “whirr-whirr.” His ears were little speakers that beeped softly. His eyes glowed a gentle blue.

“Good morning!” Sparky said. His voice was friendly and clear.

Dr. Lina, who wore purple socks and a big smile, waved. “Good morning, Sparky. We have work to do. The new letter posters fell down. The jump mats need testing. And Tim the toy turtle is sad.”

Sparky tilted his head. “I can help,” he said. “I can lead.”

Dr. Lina nodded. “Yes, Sparky. Be a leader who listens. Help everyone work together.”

Sparky took a deep breath. It smelled like lemon cleaner. He felt ready. “Team, gather!” he called.

Little helper bots rolled in. A tiny vacuum bot named Vee hummed. A paint bot named Dot blinked rainbow lights. Tim the toy turtle scooted slowly, eyes droopy. A shy speaker named Echo whispered, “Hello.”

Sparky smiled. “We can fix the posters, test the mats, and help Tim feel better. I will lead. We will go step by step.”

“Step by step,” Echo repeated softly.

Sparky held up a paw. “First, we care for ourselves. Good leaders take care of their bodies.”

He showed them how to stretch. “Arms up!” he said. He lifted his paws high. “Reach, reach.” The bots copied. Vee hummed a happy tune. Dot wiggled. Sparky bent gently. “Touch toes,” he said. “Bend slow.” Tim reached his little flippers and smiled a tiny smile.

Sparky sipped cool water from a tiny bowl. “Sip, sip,” he said. “Water helps our bodies.” The bots blinked and beeped, and tiny tubes gave them energy juice. “Now we move,” Sparky said. “Let’s do jumps.”

He hopped on the soft blue mat. “Boing! Boing!” His feet made little squeaks. Vee did tiny hops. Dot bounced a bit and giggled, “Sproing!” Tim tried a small jump. “Plip,” he went. Everyone clapped.

Sparky looked around. “I see smiles,” he said. “I hear laughs. I feel strong. We are ready.”

He led them to the letter posters scattered on the floor. “Oh no,” Echo whispered. “The letters look sad.”

Sparky nodded. “We can cheer them up. We will sing the sounds. Language is fun!” He pointed to A. “A says ‘aah’ like apple.” He sang, “A-a-apple!” The room echoed softly. “B says ‘buh’ like ball,” he added. “B-b-ball!”

Dot made a round red dot like an apple. Vee rolled in a circle like a ball. Tim tapped his shell. “T says ‘tuh’ like turtle,” Sparky said. Tim smiled bigger. “T-t-turtle!”

As they sang, Sparky gave jobs. “Dot, you paint sticky stars to hold the posters. Vee, you clean the wall so the stars stick. Echo, you lead the song so we go together. Tim, you check if the posters are straight.”

“Jobs, jobs,” Echo sang, a little stronger now. The wall smelled like lemon again as Vee whirred. Dot dabbed stars. Echo sang, “A-a, B-b, C-c,” and the sounds bounced gently in the lab.

Tim looked up carefully. “A little left,” he said kindly. Sparky nodded. “Thank you, Tim. Good eyes.” He moved A just a tiny bit. The poster lined up. Tim’s smile grew.

But then, Dot’s brush slipped. A drip splashed on the floor. Dot’s lights dimmed. “Oh no,” she said. “I messed up.”

Sparky walked over slowly. He put a soft metal paw on Dot’s side. “It is okay,” he said. “Leaders help. We all make little slips. Take a breath with me.”

They breathed in and out. The lab felt calm. “Wipe, wipe,” Vee hummed, cleaning the drip. “Thank you, Vee,” Sparky said. “Dot, you are doing great. You can try again.”

Dot’s lights glowed bright. “I can try again,” she said.

They finished the posters. The wall looked happy and colorful. “We did it!” Echo cheered, louder than before.

Sparky wagged. “Team, exercise time again! Strong bodies, strong hearts, strong helpers.” He led gentle marches. “Step, step.” He led arm circles. “Round and round.” He led calm breaths. “In, out.”

Tim lifted his head. “I still feel a little sad,” he said softly.

Sparky sat beside him. The floor felt cool on his paws. “Tell me more,” he said. “I am listening.” His voice was warm like a blanket.

“My shell is heavy,” Tim said. “I move slow.”

Sparky nodded. “That can feel hard. Moving slow is okay. Slow can be strong. You help us check the posters. You help us be careful.”

Tim blinked. “I help?”

“Yes,” Sparky said. “You help. You lead in your way. Can you lead our calm-down time?”

Tim took a breath. “I can try.”

Sparky smiled. “Team, Tim will lead.”

Tim spoke softly. “Hands on bellies. In… out.” The bots followed. The room grew quiet and cozy. Tim looked proud. “Thank you for listening,” he said.

Sparky beamed. “Great leading, Tim. Leaders listen. Leaders care. Leaders move their bodies. Leaders love words and sounds.”

Dr. Lina came down the steps. “Wow,” she said. “The posters are up. The mats are tested. And Tim is smiling. Sparky, you led with kindness.”

Sparky wagged. “We led together,” he said. “We stretched, we sipped water, we jumped, we sang, we listened, and we helped.”

Dr. Lina nodded. “That is true leadership.”

Sparky looked at his team. “Friends,” he said, “today we learned: A good leader takes care of self, helps others, uses kind words, and lets every voice be heard. Step by step, we can do big things.”

Everyone cheered. The lab felt bright and warm. The wall of letters shone. The mats were neat. Tim stood tall and calm.

“We did it, together,” Sparky said.

The End.
