# The Clever Treehouse Plan

Mina the kind mouse lived in a cozy treehouse high in a leafy oak. The treehouse smelled like fresh wood and sweet berries. Tiny windows let in warm, golden light. The floor felt smooth under her soft paws. Birds sang, “cheep-cheep,” and the breeze made the leaves whisper, “shhhh.”

One sunny morning, Mina squeaked, “Today I will build a play shelf. I can put my pinecones and story stones here.” She patted her bag of smooth stones. They were cool and heavy. She also had a bundle of sticks she found on the ground. “I use only fallen wood,” she said proudly. “That keeps the tree happy.”

Soon, her friends arrived. Tiko the tiny turtle peeked from his shell. Lila the little ladybug buzzed in on shiny wings. Pip the playful squirrel leaped onto the balcony with a thump. “Let’s play!” Pip chattered.

Mina smiled. “First, we build the shelf. Then we can play. We need a good plan.”

Pip grabbed a long stick. “I want to hammer! I want to do it now!” His tail flicked fast. His voice got loud.

Tiko blinked slowly. “Maybe we take turns,” he said in his calm, low voice.

Lila landed on Mina’s shoulder. “Yes, we can share jobs,” she chimed. “We can make a list.”

Mina took a deep breath. She felt her whiskers twitch. Pip was frowning. His paws were tight. Mina remembered her calm-down steps. “Let’s all stop and breathe,” she said gently. “Smell a flower.” She sniffed in. “Blow a candle.” She blew out. The air felt cool on her nose.

Pip copied her. “Sniff… blow.” His shoulders dropped. “I was mad,” he said softly. “I wanted my turn.”

“It’s okay to feel mad,” Mina said. “We can use a strategy. Strategy means a smart plan to solve a problem.”

“What is our plan?” asked Lila.

Mina spread her stones in a neat row. They felt smooth like water. “Step one: We think before we act. Step two: We take turns. Step three: We use what we have and do not waste.”

Tiko nodded. “Good strategy,” he said.

Mina drew a simple turn chart on a leaf with a tiny piece of charcoal. “First Tiko measures, then Lila sorts, then Pip hammers, then I tie the vines.”

“Why do I measure?” asked Tiko.

“You have steady paws,” Mina said. “You can make lines straight.”

Tiko smiled. “Okay.” He measured the sticks, tapping gently. Tap, tap. The sound was soft like rain.

Lila buzzed over the wood pile. “I sort short sticks here and long sticks there,” she said. “I also pick strong sticks and leave the cracked ones.” She felt the sticks. Some were rough. Some were smooth.

Pip watched, bouncing on his toes. “I want to hammer soon,” he whispered.

“You are third,” Mina reminded him, pointing to the leaf chart. “We will say, ‘Your turn, Pip!’ when it is time.”

Tiko finished measuring. “Your turn, Lila,” he said.

Lila sorted quickly. “Your turn, Pip!” she sang.

Pip grinned wide. He took the tiny wooden mallet. “Tap-tap, tap-tap!” The sound was happy. He tapped the pegs into the holes. He looked at the chart and said, “Next is Mina!”

Mina wrapped soft vines around the joints. The vines smelled green and fresh. “These vines fell this morning,” she said. “We use what nature gives. We do not cut new ones.” She pulled the knots snug. “Safe and strong.”

They lifted the shelf together. It fit perfectly in a sunny corner. Golden light made the stones sparkle. The wood smelled warm.

“Strategy worked!” Lila buzzed.

“It did,” said Tiko. “We thought, we took turns, we saved things.”

Pip stroked the smooth shelf. “I’m proud,” he said. His tail swished slow and happy.

Mina clapped her paws. “Now we play a game. We will practice our plan again.” She drew three simple pictures on another leaf: a brain for “think,” two paws passing a ball for “take turns,” and a leaf for “save and reuse.”

They played “Leaf Shop.” Pip was the shopper. Lila was the helper. Tiko was the counter. Mina was the bagger. They used old leaves as bags and fallen seeds as coins. When Pip got excited and spoke over Lila, he stopped and looked at the picture. He breathed in and out. “Your turn,” he said. Lila smiled and helped him choose a shiny seed.

At the end of the day, the sun turned orange. The birds sang soft. The shelf stood strong, filled with pinecones, story stones, and a tiny jar of acorns. The treehouse felt cozy and calm.

Mina gathered her friends. “What did we learn today?” she asked.

Pip raised his paw. “When I feel mad, I can stop, breathe, and wait for my turn.”

Lila buzzed, “A good plan—our strategy—helps us work and play.”

Tiko added, “We can use fallen wood and old leaves. That keeps our world healthy.”

Mina nodded. “Yes. Think first, take turns, and care for our home.”

They all touched the shelf gently. It felt strong under their paws and feet. They smiled at each other, warm and proud.

The End.
