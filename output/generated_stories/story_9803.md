# Ellie’s Sunny Kitchen Helpers

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and bright curious eyes. One sunny morning, she tip-tapped into the warm kitchen. The air smelled like sweet oatmeal and cinnamon. Sunbeams danced on the floor. The kettle made a happy hiss. The window was open, and a soft breeze kissed Ellie’s cheeks.

“Mama, I want to help today,” Ellie said, lifting her trunk with a little trumpet.

Mama Elephant smiled and stirred the oatmeal. “That is wonderful initiative, Ellie. Helping is a healthy habit. It keeps our bodies and hearts strong.”

Ellie’s feet felt tingly with excitement. “What can I do?”

“First,” Mama said gently, “we wash our hands.”

Ellie waddled to the sink. The water was warm like a bath. Squish! The soap made tiny bubbles. They smelled like lemons. Ellie rubbed the tops and bottoms of her hands. She scrubbed between her fingers. She sang, “Wash, wash, wash,” in a soft song. She rinsed and dried with a fluffy towel.

“My hands are clean!” Ellie giggled.

“Healthy habit number one,” Mama said. “Clean hands before we cook.”

Ellie puffed with pride. “I can do more!”

Mama nodded. “Please bring the fruit basket.”

Ellie stepped carefully. Her big feet made soft thumps on the floor. She balanced the basket on her trunk, one step, two steps, three. She set it down with care. “Ta-da!” she said.

“Thank you,” Mama said. “We will make a rainbow fruit bowl.”

“A rainbow?” Ellie’s eyes grew wide. “Like art?”

“Yes,” Mama smiled. “Food can be art. We can make it pretty and healthy.”

Ellie clapped her feet. “I love art!”

They washed the fruit together. Splash, splash! Cool water trickled over shiny apples, bumpy oranges, and tiny blueberries. Ellie used a small brush on the carrots. “This is tickly,” she giggled as the bristles tickled her trunk.

“Now we slice,” Mama said, “and you can place the colors.”

Mama sliced safely with a knife while Ellie watched and waited. Ellie’s job was to lay the fruits in a big round bowl. She made a ring of red apples, then a ring of orange slices, then a yellow line of banana coins. “They look like sunbeams,” Ellie said softly.

“Beautiful,” Mama whispered. “You are careful and responsible.”

Ellie’s heart felt warm. She made a green circle with kiwi, then a blue sprinkle of blueberries, then a purple swirl of grapes. She leaned close and sniffed. Mmm. Sweet and fresh. Her tummy made a tiny rumble.

“Breakfast is ready,” Mama said. “But first, we set the table.”

Ellie nodded. “I can do it.”

She placed plates. Clink. She set spoons. Clink. She folded napkins like little triangles. She put a small flower in the middle. The kitchen smelled like flowers and oats and fruit. Ellie stepped back. “It looks pretty,” she said.

Mama patted Ellie’s head. “You took initiative. You helped the family. That is responsibility.”

They sat. Ellie took a deep breath. “I will taste the rainbow,” she said. She tried a blueberry. Pop! She tried a slice of orange. Juicy! She ate some oatmeal with cinnamon. Warm and cozy.

“This is healthy and yummy,” Ellie said.

“Healthy habits help us grow strong,” Mama said. “We wash hands, we eat fruits and grains, we help, and we clean up.”

“Clean up?” Ellie echoed. She looked at the counter. There were little blueberry dots and a few peel ends. “I can do it!”

Ellie took a small cloth. She wiped the table in gentle circles. Swish, swish. She picked up peels and put them in the compost bin. Plop, plop. She put bowls in the sink. Clink. The warm water ran. She watched bubbles wiggle and pop.

Mama hummed a tune. Ellie hummed too. The kitchen felt calm and bright. The breeze tickled the curtains. The sun painted golden spots on the floor.

“Now for art,” Mama said, pointing to a big paper on the fridge. “Would you like to draw our rainbow bowl to remember our healthy breakfast?”

“Yes!” Ellie said. Her trunk curled with joy.

Mama taped the paper to the table. Ellie chose safe, chunky crayons. She drew a big round bowl. She made rings of red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple. She added a little spoon. She drew herself with a big smile.

“It looks like a happy sun,” Ellie said.

“It looks like a healthy habit picture,” Mama said. “We can look at it every morning.”

Ellie tapped her chest. “I feel proud. I washed. I helped. I ate good food. I cleaned. I made art.”

Mama hugged Ellie gently. “You showed initiative. You were responsible. You cared for your body and our home.”

Ellie listened to her tummy. It felt full and comfy. Her hands felt clean. Her heart felt bright. She looked at the rainbow drawing and the tidy kitchen. Everything sparkled.

“Tomorrow,” Ellie whispered, “I will help again.”

Mama smiled. “Healthy habits happen every day, little one.”

Ellie smiled back and gave a tiny trumpet, soft and happy. In the warm kitchen, with sunshine and the smell of cinnamon, she felt ready for a good day.

The End.
