# Ellie's Colorful Voice

Little Ellie the elephant calf sat quietly in the corner of the bright, cozy living room. Colorful cushions were scattered everywhere, and toys filled every basket. The carpet felt soft and squishy under her small gray feet.

Her big sister Maya was painting a beautiful rainbow on a large piece of paper. "Look at my pretty colors!" Maya trumpeted happily, swishing her brush through red, then blue, then yellow paint.

Ellie watched with wide, curious eyes. The paint smelled fresh and exciting. She wanted to paint too, but Maya had all the brushes and paints on her side of the low wooden table.

"Maya," Ellie whispered very quietly, "can I paint too?"

Maya didn't hear her tiny voice over the sound of her brush swishing and the happy music playing from the radio.

Ellie tried again, a little louder. "Maya, may I please have some paint?"

But Maya was humming along to the music and didn't notice.

Ellie's trunk drooped sadly. She looked at all the beautiful colors - bright red like strawberries, sunny yellow like bananas, and ocean blue like the sky. Her heart felt heavy because she really, really wanted to create something beautiful too.

Then Ellie remembered what Mama had taught her. "When you need something important, use your voice clearly and kindly," Mama had said. "It's okay to speak up for yourself."

Ellie took a deep breath that filled her little elephant lungs. She stood up tall on her four sturdy legs and walked closer to Maya.

"Maya!" Ellie called out in her clearest, kindest voice. "I would really like to paint with you. May I please share the colors?"

Maya looked up, surprised. "Oh! Ellie! I didn't know you wanted to paint. Of course you can!" Maya's eyes sparkled with excitement. "I was so busy with my rainbow, I didn't hear you."

Maya quickly made space at the table and gave Ellie her very own brush. "What would you like to paint?" she asked warmly.

Ellie's trunk wiggled with joy. "I want to paint purple flowers!" she announced proudly.

"Purple is my favorite color too," Maya giggled. "Let's mix red and blue together to make the most beautiful purple ever!"

The two elephant sisters dipped their brushes into the paint. Ellie felt the smooth, cool paint as she carefully made purple petals on her paper. The brush felt perfect in her trunk, and she discovered she could make big swooshes and tiny dots.

"Look, Maya! I'm making a whole garden!" Ellie trumpeted happily.

"It's gorgeous, Ellie! I'm so glad you asked to paint with me," Maya said, admiring her sister's colorful flowers.

As they painted together, Ellie felt warm and proud inside. She had used her voice to ask for what she wanted, and now she was creating something wonderful.

When Mama came into the living room, she saw both her daughters covered in happy paint splatters, surrounded by beautiful artwork.

"What lovely paintings!" Mama smiled. "Ellie, I'm proud of you for speaking up and asking to join the fun."

Ellie beamed. "I used my clear, kind voice, just like you taught me, Mama!"

From that day on, Ellie always remembered that her voice was important and that it was perfectly okay to ask for what she needed.

The End.
