# Fawn Finishes Before Fun

In a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls, a small lamp made warm, golden light. The room smelled like clean blankets and pine. A gentle deer fawn named Fawn curled on a fluffy rug. Her ears were silky and her spots looked like tiny snowflakes. Outside the window, the evening sky was purple and quiet.

Fawn’s bed had a quilt that felt like a hug. On the shelf sat puzzle pieces, storybooks, and a basket of blocks. Fawn loved to play. Tonight, she wanted to stack blocks high. But her room had toys on the floor, crayons without caps, and books open like sleepy flowers. Her list for the night sat on the dresser. It was a little card with pictures and words:

1. Pick up toys.
2. Put away books.
3. Brush teeth.
4. Bedtime story.

Fawn looked at the list. “Hmm,” she whispered. “I want to play now.” Her hooves tapped the rug. Tap-tap. “Maybe I can skip the cleaning. Just this once.”

She reached for the blocks. Then she stopped. Her cheeks felt warm. Her tummy made a tight, twisty feeling. She thought about the list again. Fawn knew the list was there to help. It kept the room tidy and her heart calm. Her mama always said, “We finish our tasks. We tell the truth. We take care of our things.”

“Fawn?” Mama Deer peeked in. Her voice was gentle like a lullaby. “Do you need help?”

Fawn looked at the floor. “I want to play first,” she said. Her voice was small and shy. “But the list says clean first.”

Mama Deer stepped in, hooves soft on the rug. “You have a choice,” Mama said. “You can do a little and rest, then do a little more. Or you can rush and miss things. Or you can skip, but that would not be honest with yourself or the list.”

Fawn’s ears tilted. “Honest with the list?”

Mama Deer smiled. “Yes, little one. The list is a promise you made to yourself. When you finish your tasks, you feel proud and safe. That is integrity. That means doing the right thing, even when no one is watching.”

Fawn breathed in. The room smelled like pine and soap. She made her body still. “I can try,” she said.

“Try slow,” Mama said. “One small step at a time.”

Fawn looked at the toys. “One small step,” she repeated. She picked up a red ball. Plink! It dropped in the toy bin. That sound felt good. She picked up a yellow truck. Vroom… plunk! Into the bin it went. She heard the soft thud of blocks sliding into their basket. Thud-thud. Her hooves made a quiet clip on the floor. Clip, clip.

“I am doing it,” Fawn said. Her chest felt light. She smiled.

When she felt a wiggle of hurry in her legs, she paused. She put a hoof on her heart. “Slow and steady,” she whispered. “Breathe in. Breathe out.” The room felt calm again.

Mama watched from the door. “You are using self-control,” she said. “That is self-regulation. You are guiding your body and your choices.”

Fawn stood tall. “I am guiding me.”

Soon the toys were in place. The floor looked like a smooth, sandy path. Next came the books. Fawn picked up the open ones. She smoothed the pages with her soft nose. Shh, shh. She stacked them gently, then slid them onto the shelf. “Sleep tight, books,” she said.

She checked the list. “Pick up toys.” Done. “Put away books.” Done. Fawn touched the next picture. “Brush teeth.”

She trotted to the bathroom. The light hummed softly. Fawn squeezed berry toothpaste onto her brush. Squish! The toothpaste smelled sweet, like summer jam. She brushed in tiny circles. Scritch-scritch-scritch. She watched the bubbles in the mirror and giggled. She spat, rinsed, and wiped her mouth. “Minty,” she said, even though it was berry. It felt cool and clean.

Back in the cozy bedroom, Fawn looked at the last line: “Bedtime story.” She peeked at Mama. “Now can we read?”

Mama’s eyes shone. “Yes. You finished your tasks. You kept your promise.”

Fawn climbed into bed. The quilt was warm and soft on her back. Mama sat beside her and opened a storybook. The pages smelled like paper and rain. Mama read in a gentle voice, and Fawn listened. Her eyes felt heavy like pebbles in a pond.

After the story, Mama kissed Fawn’s forehead. “How do you feel?” she asked.

Fawn thought. “I feel proud,” she said. “I wanted to play first. But I waited. I did my list. I told the truth to myself. Then I had fun. The fun felt better, because I finished first.”

Mama nodded. “That is the secret,” she said. “Finish before fun. Small steps. Honest heart. Calm body.”

Fawn hugged her pillow. “Finish before fun,” she whispered. She closed her eyes. The room was neat and quiet. The lamp made a golden glow. A soft breeze came through the window, carrying the scent of pine and night.

Fawn took one more deep breath. “I can do hard things,” she murmured. “I can finish my tasks.”

“Yes,” Mama whispered. “You can.”

Fawn drifted to sleep, cozy and proud, ready for tomorrow’s little steps and big heart.

The End.
