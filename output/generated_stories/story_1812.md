# Ellie's Colorful Creation

Ellie the elephant calf loved sitting on Grandma's peaceful front porch. The wooden boards felt warm under her little gray feet. Sweet honeysuckle flowers smelled so nice in the morning air.

Today was extra special. Grandma had given Ellie a big box of art supplies. Inside were fat crayons in every color - red like strawberries, blue like the sky, and yellow like sunshine.

"I'm going to make the most beautiful picture ever!" Ellie trumpeted softly.

She spread out a large white paper on the porch floor. The smooth paper felt cool under her trunk. Ellie picked up a red crayon with her trunk tip.

"What should I draw first?" she wondered aloud.

She decided to draw her family. Ellie carefully made a big circle for Mama's head. Then she added two triangle ears. The red crayon felt waxy and smooth as she colored.

"This is so much fun!" Ellie giggled.

Next, she reached for the blue crayon. She wanted to draw Papa elephant standing next to Mama. But as she moved her trunk, something terrible happened.

CRASH!

Her trunk bumped the crayon box. All the beautiful crayons scattered across the porch. Some rolled between the wooden boards. Others bounced down the front steps.

"Oh no!" Ellie gasped. Her big brown eyes filled with tears.

She heard Grandma's gentle footsteps coming to the porch door.

"What happened, little one?" Grandma asked kindly.

Ellie's ears drooped low. She wanted to say the wind knocked over the box. She wanted to blame a squirrel. But deep in her heart, she knew what was right.

"I... I knocked over the crayons with my trunk," Ellie whispered honestly. "I'm sorry, Grandma. I wasn't being careful enough."

Grandma's wrinkled face smiled warmly. "Thank you for telling me the truth, Ellie. That shows you're growing up to be a good elephant."

Together, they picked up all the crayons. Grandma showed Ellie how to hold her trunk steady when drawing.

"Move slowly," Grandma said. "Take your time. Art is about patience and practice."

Ellie nodded and tried again. This time, she moved her trunk very slowly. She drew Papa elephant with careful blue lines. His ears came out perfectly!

"Look, Grandma! I'm getting better!" Ellie trumpeted proudly.

"Yes, you are! What else would you like to add?"

Ellie thought hard. She picked up a green crayon and drew soft grass under her elephant family. With yellow, she made a bright sun with happy rays. Purple flowers dotted the grass like tiny jewels.

"It's beautiful!" Grandma clapped her hands.

Ellie stepped back to look at her artwork. The colors were bright and cheerful. Her elephant family looked happy together under the sunny sky.

"I love making art," Ellie said. "Even when I make mistakes, I can try again."

"That's the best part about creating," Grandma agreed. "Every mistake helps us learn something new."

As the morning sun climbed higher, Ellie carefully put each crayon back in the box. She had learned to be honest when things went wrong. She had practiced being patient with her trunk. Most importantly, she had discovered the joy of creating something beautiful with her own imagination.

"Can we make more art tomorrow?" Ellie asked hopefully.

"Of course, dear one," Grandma smiled. "There are so many wonderful things we can create together."

Ellie curled up next to Grandma on the peaceful porch, her colorful picture drying in the warm sunshine.

The End.
