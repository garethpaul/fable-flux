# Joey's Big Dinner Adventure

Joey was a happy little kangaroo with soft brown fur and bright curious eyes. He lived with his mama in a cozy house with a big dining room. The dining room had a long wooden table, four blue chairs, and pretty yellow flowers by the window.

Every day, Mama Kangaroo made Joey's meals. She would hop to the kitchen, prepare his food, and bring it to the dining room table. Joey would sit in his special chair and wait. "Thank you, Mama!" he would say with a big smile.

But one Tuesday morning, something different happened. Mama Kangaroo was sniffling and sneezing. "Achoo! Achoo!" she went. Her nose was red and stuffy.

"Oh dear," said Mama Kangaroo softly. "I don't feel well today, Joey. I need to rest in bed."

Joey's tummy started to rumble. "Grumble, grumble," it went. He looked at the empty dining room table. No breakfast was there. He felt worried and a little scared.

"But Mama," said Joey, "who will make my breakfast?"

Mama Kangaroo gave him a gentle hug. "You're a big joey now," she said. "I think you can do it yourself. Just be calm and think carefully."

Joey hopped to the kitchen. It looked so big! The counter was tall. The cupboards were high up. Everything seemed different when Mama wasn't there to help.

First, Joey felt upset. "I can't do this!" he said out loud. But then he remembered Mama's words about being calm. He took three deep breaths. "Whoosh, whoosh, whoosh."

Joey looked around carefully. He spotted a low shelf with his favorite cereal box. It had colorful pictures of fruit on it. "I can reach that!" he said happily.

Next, he needed a bowl. Joey opened the bottom cupboard. There were plastic bowls just his size! He picked a bright red one that felt smooth in his paws.

Then Joey needed milk. The refrigerator was tall, but the handle was low enough for him to pull. Inside, he saw the white milk jug. It was heavy, but Joey used both paws to carry it slowly and carefully.

"Step by step," Joey said to himself, just like Mama taught him when learning to hop.

Joey carried everything to the dining room table. He poured the cereal into his bowl. The little pieces made a soft "plink, plink" sound. Then he poured the cold milk. It smelled fresh and sweet.

"I did it!" Joey said proudly. He hopped up onto his chair and took a big bite. The cereal was crunchy and delicious. It tasted even better because he made it himself!

When Mama Kangaroo came downstairs later, she found Joey finishing his breakfast with a huge smile.

"Look, Mama!" said Joey excitedly. "I made my own breakfast! I was calm, and I planned each step, and I did it all by myself!"

Mama Kangaroo's eyes sparkled with pride. "You are such a clever and independent joey," she said. "You didn't need me to help you. You figured it out on your own!"

From that day on, Joey felt more confident. He knew that when he stayed calm and thought things through step by step, he could solve problems by himself. And that made him feel very proud and grown-up indeed.

The End.
