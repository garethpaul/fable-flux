# Ollie Otter’s Bubbly Laundry Plan

Swish, swash, hum! The laundry room sounded busy and bright. Ollie the playful otter peeked in with big, shiny eyes. His whiskers twitched, and his paws felt warm from the sun. He loved bubbles and splashes and soft, clean smells.

“Good morning, Ollie,” said Mama Otter, folding a fluffy towel. “Today, we have chores. We wash, we sort, we fold.”

Ollie wrinkled his nose. “Chores? They sound slow,” he said. He bounced on his webby feet. “Can I play instead?”

Mama Otter smiled kindly. “Chores can be fun. Let’s make a plan. We can set a goal.”

“A goal?” Ollie tilted his head.

“A goal is a promise to do a job, step by step,” said Mama. “Our goal is to make the laundry clean and neat before snack time.”

Ollie’s eyes sparkled. “A goal! I like it. I can do it. But… can we add a little splash of fun?”

“Let’s see your ideas,” Mama said, tapping her chin.

Ollie sniffed the air. It smelled like warm soap and lemon. He listened to the washer: hum-hum-hummm. He touched a soft sock and giggled. “This sock feels like a cloud!”

He set his paws on his hips. “Step one: sort! Whites here, colors there,” he said. He placed a white T-shirt in one basket and a blue sock in another. Plop, plop. “Step two: wash! Water whoosh!”

Mama Otter nodded. “Good plan.”

Ollie clapped. “I will make sorting into a game. I will call it Color Catch!” He drew a little circle on the floor with tape. “When I toss a color in the circle, I say, ‘Catch the blue! Catch the red!’”

He picked up a red shirt. “Catch the red!” Plop! Right into the circle. He giggled, tail wiggling. He picked up a white towel. “Catch the—oh! Whites go in the white basket.” He fixed the mix-up, proud and careful.

“You are checking and fixing,” Mama said. “That is smart.”

Ollie’s chest puffed. “I am an inventor,” he said. “I make chores fun.”

When the sorting was done, Mama showed Ollie the buttons on the washer. “Cold for colors. Warm for towels. We pour a little soap. Not too much.”

Ollie sniffed the soap. “Mmm, lemon bubbles.” He watched the washer fill. Swish, swash. “It looks like a tiny river!”

He pressed his paw on the glass. “Hello, socks! Swim, swim!”

While the washer hummed, Ollie counted slowly. “One, two, three…” He wiggled. Waiting was hard. “What do we do now?”

“We can set a mini-goal,” said Mama. “We tidy the shelf before the washer sings.”

“A mini-goal!” Ollie grinned. He lined up clothespins. Click, click, click. He made a tiny pin parade. He stacked the soap neatly. He wiped a little spill with a small cloth. “Wipe, wipe, wipe,” he sang.

Ding! The washer beeped. “Time to switch,” said Mama.

Ollie opened the door and felt the damp clothes. “They are cool and heavy,” he said. He lifted a shirt. Drip, drip. “I will invent a Shake-Shake,” he said. He gave the shirt a gentle shake. “Shake-Shake, smooth and flat!”

“Careful and clever,” Mama said.

They put the clothes in the dryer. Thump, thump went the drum. Ollie laid his ear on the warm side. “It sounds like a sleepy drum,” he whispered.

As they waited, Mama brought a small basket of socks. “These are dry. Want to match them?”

Ollie nodded. “Match-Match game!” He sniffed them. “They smell like sunshine.” He touched them. “So soft.” He found two blue socks. “Blue buddies!” He found two striped socks. “Zig-zag friends!”

One sock had no twin. Ollie frowned. “Where is your buddy?” he asked the lone sock.

Mama smiled. “Let’s set a search goal. We will look with our eyes and our hands.”

Ollie looked under the table. He checked behind the basket. He listened to the dryer hum. Then he spotted a peek of stripes by the door. “Aha!” He pulled it out. “Found you!” He felt brave and bright.

Ding-ding! The dryer finished. Warm, sweet air puffed out. Ollie sighed happily. “It smells like cozy lemon clouds.”

“Now we fold,” Mama said. “One fold, two folds. Smooth and neat.”

Ollie made one fold, two folds, then patted the towel. “Soft and square!” He folded a tiny shirt. “Small and snug!” He even folded his own little scarf. “Perfect,” he whispered.

All the clothes made a happy stack. The baskets were tidy. The floor was clean. Ollie wiggled with pride. “We met our goal! We did it before snack time!”

Mama Otter hugged him. “You worked hard. You made smart ideas. You turned chores into games. That is innovation.”

“In-no-va-tion,” Ollie said slowly. “It means new ideas!”

Mama nodded. “Yes. New ideas to help the job.”

Ollie smiled big. “I like chores now. They help our home. They make me strong and helpful. When I set a goal, I can do big things, one small step at a time.”

Mama placed a bowl of crunchy apple slices on the counter. “Snack time for my helper.”

Ollie took a bite. Crunch! “Next time,” he said, “I will invent a Sock Slide to help the socks go to the basket.”

Mama laughed. “I can’t wait to see.”

Ollie looked around the clean, sweet-smelling room. He felt calm and proud. “Chores, check. Goal, check. New ideas, check,” he said softly.

He lifted his paw for a high-five. Smack! “Teamwork,” he whispered.

The End.
