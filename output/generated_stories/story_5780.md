# Leo's Big Voice

Little Leo the lion cub was staying at Grandma's house for the weekend. He loved the cozy guest room with its soft blue blankets and fluffy pillows. But today, something was bothering him.

Leo sat on the edge of the bed, swinging his small paws. Through the window, he could hear his cousins playing loudly in the backyard. They were having a dance party!

"I want to dance too," Leo whispered to himself. His tail twitched with excitement as he heard the happy music - *boom, boom, boom* - coming from outside.

Leo tiptoed to the guest room door and peeked out. His three older cousins were spinning and jumping on the patio. They looked like they were having so much fun!

"Can I dance with you?" Leo called out softly from the doorway.

But his cousins didn't hear him. The music was too loud, and Leo's voice was too quiet.

Leo tried again, a little louder this time. "Um... can I play too?"

Still nothing. His cousins kept dancing without him.

Leo's whiskers drooped. He walked back to the guest room and flopped onto the soft carpet. "They don't want me to dance," he said sadly.

Just then, Grandma poked her head into the room. Her kind eyes noticed Leo's sad face right away.

"What's wrong, little lion?" she asked, sitting down beside him.

"I want to dance with my cousins, but they won't let me," Leo explained.

Grandma smiled gently. "Did you tell them clearly that you want to join?"

Leo shook his head. "I tried, but my voice was too small."

"Sometimes we need to use our big, brave voice," Grandma said. "Lions are known for being strong and confident. You have that strength inside you too!"

Grandma stood up and showed Leo how to stand tall. "Put your shoulders back, take a deep breath, and speak from your heart."

Leo practiced in the guest room. "Can I dance with you?" he said, making his voice bigger and stronger.

"Perfect!" Grandma clapped. "Now go show them your brave lion spirit!"

Leo marched to the patio with confident steps. The music was still playing - *thump, thump, thump* - and his cousins were still dancing.

This time, Leo stood tall and used his big voice. "Hey everyone! I want to dance too!"

All three cousins stopped and turned around.

"Oh, Leo!" said his cousin Maya. "We didn't know you wanted to join us!"

"We're sorry we didn't hear you before," added cousin Jake.

Leo felt proud and happy. "That's okay! I should have spoken up louder."

"Come on!" said cousin Sara, reaching out her paw. "We're doing the jungle boogie!"

Leo jumped into the circle. He wiggled his tail, stomped his paws, and roared with joy. The warm sunshine felt wonderful on his golden fur as he danced.

"This is the best dance party ever!" Leo laughed, spinning around with his cousins.

Later, as they all caught their breath, Maya said, "Leo, we're glad you used your brave voice to tell us what you wanted."

Leo beamed with pride. He had learned something very important today.

That evening in the guest room, Leo told Grandma all about his dancing adventure.

"I'm proud of you for being assertive," Grandma said, tucking him into the cozy bed. "Speaking up for yourself takes courage."

Leo snuggled into his soft pillows, feeling happy and confident. He had found his brave voice, and it felt wonderful.

The End.
