# Polly's Perfect Story

Polly the parrot loved her cozy living room. Soft cushions covered the big sofa. Colorful books filled the tall shelves. Crayons and paper scattered across the coffee table like a rainbow.

Every day, Polly wrote stories. She held her special red crayon in her bright orange beak. She drew pictures of flying adventures and singing contests. Her green and blue feathers sparkled as she worked.

Today, Polly was writing her best story ever. It was about a brave parrot who saved the day. She drew beautiful pictures with swirly clouds and shining suns.

"This story is perfect!" Polly squawked happily. Her tail feathers wiggled with joy.

Just then, her little brother Pip hopped into the room. His tiny feet went pitter-patter on the wooden floor.

"What are you doing, Polly?" Pip chirped. He climbed onto the coffee table to see better.

"I'm writing the most amazing story!" Polly said proudly. "Look at my pictures!"

Pip leaned closer. Too close. His little wing bumped the crayon box. CRASH! Red and blue crayons rolled everywhere. Yellow and purple ones scattered across the floor.

But that wasn't the worst part. Pip's foot stepped right on Polly's story! The beautiful paper crumpled up. The pretty pictures got all wrinkled and torn.

"MY STORY!" Polly screeched loudly. Her feathers puffed up big and angry. "You ruined everything, Pip! It was perfect and now it's broken!"

Pip's eyes filled with tears. "I'm sorry, Polly! I didn't mean to!" He tried to smooth out the paper, but it just made more wrinkles.

Polly was so mad. She wanted to yell more. But then she saw Pip's sad little face. His bottom lip was trembling. One tiny tear rolled down his yellow beak.

Polly's anger started to melt away. She remembered how excited Pip looked when he wanted to see her story. He wasn't trying to be mean. He just wanted to share her special moment.

"Oh, Pip," Polly said softly. She hopped down from her perch. "I'm sorry I yelled at you. You were just curious about my story."

"I really am sorry, Polly," Pip sniffled. "Can I help fix it somehow?"

Polly looked at her crumpled story. Then she looked at all the scattered crayons. Suddenly, she had a wonderful idea!

"You know what, Pip? Maybe we can make it even better together!" Polly's eyes sparkled with excitement. "Want to help me write a new story?"

Pip's face lit up like sunshine. "Really? We can write together?"

"Of course!" Polly gathered up the crayons. "You can draw the trees, and I'll draw the birds. We can make it about two parrot friends who go on adventures!"

They spread fresh paper on the coffee table. Pip carefully drew green trees with his tiny orange crayon. Polly drew colorful birds flying through fluffy white clouds. They worked side by side, sharing crayons and giggling.

"This story is even more special," Polly said, admiring their teamwork. "Because we made it together!"

"I love writing stories with you," Pip chirped happily.

When they finished, their new story was beautiful. It had twice as many colors and twice as much love. They hung it on the refrigerator for everyone to see.

That night, as they snuggled on the soft sofa, Polly learned something important. Sometimes accidents happen, but saying sorry and working together can make everything better. The best stories aren't perfect – they're the ones made with love.

The End.
