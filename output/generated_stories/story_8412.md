# Squeaky Follows the Plan

In a warm, comfortable nursery, the curtains were soft and sunny yellow. The rug felt fluffy under little feet. Blocks were stacked on a low shelf, and picture books smelled like paper and crayons. The room was cozy and quiet, with a gentle tick-tock from a clock.

A resourceful squirrel named Squeaky lived in a small woven basket by the window. Squeaky had bright eyes, a bushy tail that swished like a feather broom, and little paws that could open latches and tie tiny bows. Squeaky loved helping friends and figuring out puzzles.

One morning, Miss Dot, the kind teacher, clapped her hands softly. “Friends,” she said with a smile, “today we will build a Fair Share Tower. We will take turns and follow directions. That way, everyone gets to help.”

Squeaky’s ears perked up. “I can help!” he chirped.

Miss Dot nodded. “Yes, Squeaky. Here are the directions. Listen carefully.”

She pointed to a big poster with simple pictures:
1. First, wash hands.
2. Next, line up blocks by color.
3. Then, take turns—one block each.
4. Finally, clap and cheer for our tower.

Miss Dot asked, “Can you repeat the directions, Squeaky?”

Squeaky took a deep breath. “First, wash hands. Next, line up blocks by color. Then, one block each. Finally, clap and cheer!”

“Perfect,” said Miss Dot. “Following directions keeps things fair and safe.”

Squeaky scampered to the little sink. The water was warm and splishy-splashy. “Swish, swish,” he hummed. He rubbed his paws with foamy soap that smelled like oranges. He dried them on a soft, clean towel.

Then Squeaky lined up the blocks. Red. Blue. Yellow. Green. The blocks felt smooth and cool. “Click, clack,” they sounded as he set them in tidy rows.

“Now we take turns,” Squeaky told his friends, Bunny Bella and Bear Cub Ben. “One block each. Fair is fair.”

Bella twitched her nose. “Can I go first?”

Ben hugged a blue block. “I want to go first!”

Squeaky thought. He remembered the rules. “We can follow directions and use fairness,” he said. “Let’s sing the Turn-Taking Song. The order is: left to right.”

They stood side by side. Squeaky looked from left to right. “Bella is on the left. Ben is next. I am last. That is our order today.”

Bella nodded. “Okay. I go first.”

Ben sighed but said, “Okay. I go second.”

Squeaky smiled. “Thank you for respecting the plan.”

Bella gently placed a red block. “Tap.” She grinned.

Ben set a blue block on top. “Tap.” He smiled, too.

Squeaky added a yellow block. “Tap.” The tower reached higher. It wobbled a little, like jelly. Squeaky steadied it with his paw. “Let’s go slow and follow the steps,” he said. “Slow is safe.”

Around and around they went. Red, blue, yellow, green. Left to right. One block each. Squeaky watched the tower carefully. “We are following directions,” he said. “This is helping all of us.”

But then Squeaky noticed a problem. A little kid named Max, with paint on his shirt and a giggle in his voice, tiptoed over. “I want to put five blocks at once!” Max whispered. He grabbed a handful.

The tower leaned. “Whoa,” said Bella. “It’s wiggly!”

Ben hugged his block tight. “What do we do?”

Squeaky felt his heart thump. He remembered the poster. First, he took a calm breath. In. Out. He used his strategy paws. “Max,” Squeaky said kindly, “we have directions. They help us be fair and safe. One block each. Will you follow the steps with us?”

Max frowned. “But I want to go fast.”

Squeaky nodded. “I like fast, too. But when we follow directions, everyone gets a turn. That is fair. And the tower stands tall. That is safe.” He pointed to the poster and spoke slowly, “First, we ask. Next, we wait. Then, we place one block. Finally, we cheer together.”

Miss Dot came closer. She knelt and smiled. “Squeaky is right. Thank you for using kind words, Squeaky.”

Max looked at the shiny blocks. He listened to the tick-tock clock. He felt the smooth wood in his hand. “Okay,” he said softly. “I can follow the steps.”

Squeaky clapped once. “Great! You can stand on the left next round, Max. Then Bella moves to the middle. Ben is next. I’ll be last.”

“That’s fair,” said Bella.

“That shows respect,” said Ben.

Max nodded. “That helps me.”

They continued together. Tap. Tap. Tap. The tower grew, straight and proud. When the last green block clicked into place, the room felt still and happy.

Miss Dot said, “Friends, what did we learn?”

Squeaky raised his paw. “We learned to follow directions so that things are fair and safe. We helped each other. We used kind words and a plan.”

Max added, “Waiting was hard, but I did it!”

Bella said, “Taking turns felt good.”

Ben said, “The tower stayed tall.”

Miss Dot smiled. “You all showed respect and great strategy. Now, the final step—clap and cheer!”

They clapped. Clap, clap, clap! They cheered. “Hooray for the Fair Share Tower!”

Squeaky’s tail swished happily. He looked at his friends. “When we listen and follow directions, we can build big things together,” he said. “That is fair. That is kind. That is smart.”

Everyone nodded. The nursery felt warm and bright, and the tower stood tall, safe, and shared by all.

The End.
