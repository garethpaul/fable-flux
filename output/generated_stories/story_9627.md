# Koko Koala’s Bedroom Camp-In

In a cozy bedroom with soft moonlight on the walls, a calm koala named Koko snuggled into a fluffy quilt. The quilt felt warm like a hug. The room smelled like clean cotton and a little bit like lavender from a tiny pillow. A gentle night-light glowed like a sleepy star, and the curtains swished quietly when the fan went whoosh.

Koko wiggled his round ears. “Tonight is our camp-in!” he said. He set a little tent made of blankets at the end of the bed. He put a tiny lantern on the nightstand and a cup of cool water on a coaster. “I can do this,” Koko whispered. “I can be calm and brave.”

His friend Nia, a small stuffed hedgehog, peeked from the pillow. “What if the room gets too dark?” Nia asked in a tiny voice.

Koko took a slow breath in and out. “We can turn the lantern to low,” he said. “And we can listen to the fan. Whoosh. Whoosh. That sound is friendly.” He pressed the lantern so it glowed like a little firefly. The soft light made the blanket tent look golden.

Knock-knock. Koko’s brother, Bobo, tapped on the door and rolled in a toy drum. “Can I join the camp-in?” Bobo asked, bouncing with energy.

“Yes,” Koko said with a small smile. “But we take turns tonight. Quiet turns, loud turns, rest turns.” He patted the bed. “Right now, it’s a quiet turn.”

Bobo nodded, but his paws wiggled. “Quiet turn,” he whispered, and the drum went shhh on the blanket.

Koko set out bedtime books that smelled like paper and a little like vanilla. He touched the pages. “We will try new things,” he said. “Let’s be open to new games.” He looked around the room. “We can make star shadows!”

Koko held the lantern behind his paw. A round koala shadow wobbled on the ceiling. “Hello, shadow me,” Koko said. Bobo giggled and made a spiky hedgehog shape with Nia.

The curtains rustled. A soft creak came from the hallway. Bobo’s eyes got big. “What was that?”

Koko felt a tiny flutter in his tummy. He put his paw on his chest and breathed. In… one, two. Out… one, two, three. “It was the house stretching,” he said kindly. “Houses make little night sounds. We can be calm.”

They listened. The fan went whoosh. The lantern hummed very quietly. Somewhere, a clock went tick, tock. The sounds lined up like a gentle song.

Bobo poked the drum. “My turn now?”

Koko nodded. “Yes. It is a loud turn for one minute. Then we rest.” He set a tiny sand timer. Bobo tapped the drum softly at first, then a little louder. Dum, dum, dum. The sound felt bouncy, like kangaroo hops.

“Time,” Koko said when the sand ran out. “Now a rest turn.” He handed Bobo the water. “Sip, sip.” Bobo took a drink and sighed. “Ahhh.”

Koko opened a book. “Tonight’s new thing is reading by lantern,” he said. “I have never tried it.” He placed the lantern just right. The pages shone. “Oh! It works,” he said, surprised and pleased.

Nia whispered, “What if we cannot sleep?”

Koko stroked Nia’s soft felt. “We can try again,” he said. “Trying again is called being strong inside. That is resilience.” He tucked Nia under the quilt. “We can count five soft things. My quilt is soft. My pillow is soft. My ears are soft. Your prickles are soft on the inside. The curtains are soft. See? Counting helps us feel calm.”

Bobo yawned. “I want another drum turn.”

Koko nodded. “I want one too. We can take turns.” He held up the sand timer. “Which turn is first?”

Bobo frowned a little. Then he touched the timer and said, “You first.”

Koko smiled. “Thank you. That is kind.” He played a soft dum, pa, dum, pa. The sound was like footsteps in snow. Then he passed the drum. Bobo copied the pattern. Dum, pa, dum, pa. “We did it together,” Koko said.

A sudden puff of air made the lantern flicker. The tent wobbled. “Oh!” Bobo squeaked.

Koko felt the flutter again, but he squeezed his paws and breathed. “Let’s fix it ourselves,” he said. “We can be self-reliant.” He smoothed the blanket edge. He tucked the corner under a pillow. He asked, “Bobo, can you hold this side?” They worked slowly, whispering, “Tuck and smooth, tuck and smooth.” The tent stood taller than before.

Bobo grinned. “We fixed it!”

Koko nodded. “We stayed calm. We tried again. We took turns. We did it together and by ourselves.”

He turned the lantern to the tiniest glow. The room felt like a soft cloud. The fan went whoosh, the clock went tick, tock, and the quilt hugged them close. Koko traced a star on the blanket with his paw. “I learned something tonight,” he said.

“What?” Bobo asked, eyes drooping.

“When I feel a flutter, I can breathe slow. I can use my calm to try new things. I can wait for my turn. I can fix little problems. Calm helps me and helps you.”

Bobo smiled sleepily. “Calm is cozy.”

Koko kissed Nia’s felt nose and patted Bobo’s paw. “Yes,” he whispered. “Calm is cozy, and calm is strong.”

The night-light glowed like a tiny moon. Koko closed his eyes. He felt brave, open, and steady. The cozy bedroom sighed with quiet sounds, and everyone drifted to sleep with calm hearts.

The End.
