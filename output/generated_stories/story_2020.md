# Koala Kori’s Story Circle

Kori was a calm koala who lived in a cozy home office. The room smelled like warm paper and sweet eucalyptus tea. The desk hummed softly from a tiny fan. Sunlight made golden squares on the rug. Kori liked the quiet tap-tap of keys and the soft scratch of pencils.

One bright morning, Kori said, “Today, I will host a story circle.” Kori’s voice was gentle like a whispering leaf. “Everyone’s story matters.”

Kori set out cushions in a round shape on the rug. One cushion was striped, one was fluffy, one was shiny, and one had stars. Kori put a small bell in the center. “Ding,” went the bell. It sounded like a tiny raindrop.

Friends arrived. “Hi, Kori!” chirped Pip the parrot, flapping bright wings.

“Hello,” squeaked Mia the mouse, carrying a tiny notebook.

“Howdy,” rumbled Gus the old tortoise, moving slowly, smiling slowly.

“Welcome, friends,” Kori said. “We will take turns. We will listen with our eyes, our ears, and our hearts.”

Pip puffed up. “I want to go first! I have a big, loud story!” Pip hopped to the shiny cushion.

Mia looked at the star cushion. She held her notebook close. “I have a small, quiet story,” she whispered.

Gus blinked kindly. “I’ve got a slow, long story,” he said.

Kori nodded. “All stories belong here,” Kori said. “Loud or quiet. Big or small. Fast or slow. We all fit.”

The fan went whirr. The tea smelled sweet. The bell sat in the center like a little moon. Kori smiled. “Pip, you may begin.”

Pip spread wings. “Once, I flew over rainbow trees!” Pip’s voice was bright and bold. “The wind whooshed. The colors swooshed. I sang, squawk-squawk! The end!”

Kori clapped softly. “Thank you, Pip. I heard your brave voice and felt your happy wind.”

Mia peeked over her notebook. “Um,” she said, “my story is quiet.”

Pip flapped. “Make it louder,” Pip chirped. “Loud is better!”

Kori placed a calm paw on the soft rug. “Different is not worse,” Kori said gently. “Different is special. Let’s make space for quiet.”

Pip tilted his head. “Space for quiet?”

Kori nodded. “We can show respect. We can listen softly.”

Kori rang the bell. “Ding.” Everyone took a breath in and out. The room felt like a warm hug.

Mia smiled a tiny smile. She sat on the star cushion. “Once,” she began, voice like a soft feather, “I found a shiny button under my bed. It was blue like the sky. I made it my moon. At night, I told the moon button my hopes. I whispered, ‘Hello, moon button. Please keep me company.’ The end.”

Gus’s eyes shone. “Lovely,” he said. “I felt that quiet moon.”

Pip tucked his wings close. “I liked your moon,” he said. “Quiet can be nice.”

Kori beamed. “Thank you for listening with your hearts.”

Gus stretched his neck. “My turn,” he rumbled. “Once, I walked and walked. The path was sandy. Crunch, crunch. I met an ant. Ant was fast. I was slow. We walked together. Step… step… sip… of water. We reached a shady leaf. We both fit under it. The end.”

Kori patted the floor. “I could hear the crunch. I could feel the shade. Thank you, Gus.”

Pip hopped. “We all fit,” he echoed. “Like under the leaf!”

Mia nodded. “Like our story circle.”

Kori poured warm tea. “In our home office,” Kori said, “we include every voice. Big or small. Loud or quiet. Fast or slow. We all belong.”

Just then, a soft tap-tap sounded at the door. It was Nia the snail, peeking in shyly. “Am I too late?” Nia asked. “Do I go too slow?”

Kori’s eyes were kind. “You are right on time,” Kori said. “Your time. Please join us.”

Nia slid onto the fluffy cushion. “I have a tiny story,” Nia whispered. “It is about a raindrop that rode on my shell. Plip-plop, plip-plop. We sang together. The end.”

Pip clapped his wings quietly. “Plip-plop! I love it.”

Mia held out her notebook. “Would you like to draw your raindrop, Nia?”

Nia smiled. “Yes, please.”

Kori passed around crayons and paper. Red, blue, green, and gold rolled across the desk. Everyone drew. Pip drew rainbow trees. Mia drew her moon button. Gus drew the shady leaf. Nia drew a happy raindrop with tiny music notes.

Kori looked at the pictures. “These stories are different,” Kori said, “and they are all important.”

Pip raised a wing. “How do we remember to include?” he asked.

Kori tapped the bell softly. “Ding,” it went. “We can remember three things:
- We take turns.
- We listen kindly.
- We make space for each other.”

Gus nodded. “Slow, quiet, loud, fast. All welcome.”

Mia whispered, “We all fit.”

Nia added, “We all belong.”

The fan hummed. The tea was warm. The room felt safe and bright.

Kori wrapped the day with a gentle smile. “Our story circle is like a big hug. When we include everyone, the hug grows.”

Pip flapped happily. “Big hugs!” he cheered.

They placed their drawings in a circle on the rug, like petals of a story flower. Kori looked around at all the friends. “Thank you for being you,” Kori said.

And all the friends, in voices loud and soft, said together, “We belong. We include. We listen.”

The End.
