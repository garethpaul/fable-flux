# Benny Bunny’s Blanket Fix

Benny Bunny lived in a cozy bedroom with soft, blue walls and a night-light that glowed like a tiny moon. His bed was fluffy, and his favorite blanket was sunshine yellow with little stars. Benny was a determined rabbit. When he said, “I can try,” he really meant it.

One rainy afternoon, raindrops tapped the window. Tip-tap, tip-tap. The whole room smelled like clean sheets and warm tea. Benny hopped onto his bed and pulled his blanket up to his chin. “Story time!” he cheered.

But when he tugged the blanket, it got stuck. Rip! A corner tore with a little sound like paper. “Oh no,” Benny whispered. He looked at the tiny tear. The star on the corner drooped.

Benny’s ears drooped too. Then he took a deep breath. In and out, slow and calm. “I can try,” he said. “I can fix it.” He wanted to keep his room tidy and his blanket safe. He wanted to do the right thing.

“Hello?” he called softly. “Any helpers in here?” He did not shout. He stayed calm.

From the pillow stack, a sleepy stuffed bear lifted his head. “Mmm?” said Mr. Bear in a deep, rumble voice.

“We have a little problem,” Benny said. “My blanket has a tear. I want to fix it.”

Mr. Bear blinked. “I believe in you,” he said. “Let’s look for tools.”

Benny nodded. “Yes. Let’s be resourceful.” He liked that big word. It felt like a toolbox in his mouth.

Benny hopped to his bedside drawer. It smelled like pencils and crayons. He found a small box of buttons, a safety pin, and a roll of soft, sticky fabric tape. “Ooh,” Benny said. “These might help.”

He listened to the gentle rain. Tip-tap. The sound helped him stay calm. Benny sat on the fluffy rug. It felt warm under his paws. He laid the blanket flat and patted it smooth.

Just then, his neighbor, a tiny toy mouse named Mimi, peeked from the bookshelf fort. “Hi, Benny,” Mimi squeaked. “I heard ‘helpers.’ What’s wrong?”

“My blanket tore,” Benny said. “I’m going to fix it. Do you want to help?”

Mimi’s whiskers twitched. “I can hold things,” she said.

“Thank you,” Benny replied with a smile. “Everyone can help in their own way.” He was patient and kind. He did not say, “Go away.” He said, “Join in.” That was tolerance.

Benny gently touched the torn corner. “We need to close this tear.” He tried the safety pin first. “Click.” The pin held the fabric together, but it felt pokey. “Hmm,” Benny said. “This is not comfy for sleeping.”

Mr. Bear nodded. “We need a soft fix.”

Benny tried the sticky fabric tape next. He pressed it on the inside of the blanket. Press, press, smooth. “It’s holding!” he said. He rubbed the tape to make it warm and snug. “But the star still droops,” Mimi whispered.

Benny tapped his chin. “We can be more resourceful,” he said. He looked around the cozy bedroom. He sniffed the air. It smells like crayons... Oh! He opened his art box and found a square of yellow felt, a little piece from craft time. “This can be a patch,” he said. “A star on a star.”

Benny cut the felt with little scissors, snip, snip, into a shiny star shape. He asked softly, “Is it okay to use this, Mr. Bear?” Benny wanted to be honest and careful with his things.

Mr. Bear smiled. “It’s your felt, Benny. Good asking. Good integrity.”

Benny spread a thin line of fabric glue around the tear, all the way to the edges. It smelled a little sweet. He set the new felt star on top. “Mimi, can you hold it while I count?”

“I can!” Mimi said. She pressed with her tiny paws.

Benny counted calm and slow. “One... two... three... all the way to twenty.” The rain helped him keep a steady beat. Tip-tap, tip-tap.

When they finished counting, Benny touched the patch. It felt smooth and strong. He tugged the blanket gently. No rip! The star shone bright again.

“We did it!” Benny cheered. He hugged Mr. Bear and high-fived Mimi. Clap!

Benny made his bed neatly. He set the buttons back in the drawer. He wiped a drop of glue from the rug. “We used what we had,” he said proudly. “We stayed calm. We were kind. We fixed it!”

Mimi smiled. “You didn’t yell. You didn’t give up.”

Mr. Bear rumbled, “You tried ideas. You chose the best one. That’s being resourceful.”

Benny snuggled under his blanket. It smelled like sunshine felt and clean laundry. His night-light glowed like a tiny moon. The cozy bedroom felt safe and bright.

Benny whispered, “When something breaks, I can breathe, I can think, and I can try. I can ask for help, and I can use what I have.” He patted the new star. “This star is our helper star.”

Outside, the rain slowed to a soft hush. Benny closed his eyes and smiled. He felt proud, calm, and kind.

Lesson: When a small problem comes, stay calm, be honest, and use what you have. Try different ideas, ask for help, and keep going. That’s resourcefulness.

The End.
