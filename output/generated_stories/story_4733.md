# Ruby Rabbit's Big Sharing Day

Ruby Rabbit loved playing in the busy basement. The basement was full of wonderful things! There were colorful blocks that clicked together. There were soft pillows that smelled like flowers. There were shiny toy cars that went "zoom zoom" across the smooth floor.

Today was special. Ruby's friends were coming to play! She bounced up and down on her fluffy white paws. Her pink nose twitched with excitement.

"I can't wait!" said Ruby. She hugged her favorite red ball tight. The ball felt smooth and bouncy in her arms.

Soon, Ruby heard footsteps on the wooden stairs. *Thump, thump, thump!* Down came Max Mouse and Bella Bird.

"Hi, Ruby!" squeaked Max. His tiny whiskers wiggled as he smiled.

"We're here to play!" chirped Bella. Her yellow feathers sparkled in the basement light.

Ruby looked around at all her toys. She felt a funny feeling in her tummy. What if her friends wanted to play with her special red ball? What if they made it dirty?

"Let's play blocks!" said Ruby quickly. She hid the red ball behind her back.

Max and Bella started building with the colorful blocks. *Click, click, stack!* But Max kept looking at the basement corner where Ruby was hiding.

"What's behind your back, Ruby?" asked Max kindly.

Ruby's ears drooped down. "It's... it's my special ball," she whispered.

"Can we see it?" asked Bella sweetly.

Ruby shook her head. "No! It's mine. I don't want to share it."

Max and Bella looked sad. They played quietly with the blocks, but it wasn't as fun anymore. The basement felt quiet and lonely.

Ruby watched her friends. Their ears were droopy. Their smiles were gone. The funny feeling in Ruby's tummy got bigger.

"Why do I feel so yucky?" Ruby thought. She looked at her beautiful red ball. It wasn't as shiny when she played alone.

Then Ruby remembered something. Last week, Bella had shared her sparkly stickers. Max had shared his cheese crackers at snack time. They always shared with her!

Ruby took a deep breath. Her heart beat fast like a drum. *Thump-thump, thump-thump.*

"Wait!" called Ruby. She hopped over to her friends. "Would you like to play with my red ball?"

Max's whiskers perked up. "Really? You want to share?"

"Yes!" said Ruby. Her voice was strong and happy. "Sharing makes everything more fun!"

Ruby rolled the red ball to Max. He caught it with his tiny paws and giggled. Then Max rolled it to Bella. Bella used her wing to push it back to Ruby.

"This is the best game ever!" laughed Bella.

The three friends played catch with the red ball. They took turns. They said "please" and "thank you." The basement filled with happy sounds - giggles, squeaks, and chirps!

When it was time to go home, Ruby felt warm and glowy inside.

"Thank you for sharing your special ball," said Max, giving Ruby a tiny hug.

"You're the best friend ever!" said Bella, fluffing her feathers happily.

After her friends left, Ruby held her red ball. It looked even more beautiful now! It had been touched by friendship and kindness.

"Sharing makes my toys better," Ruby said to herself. "And sharing makes my friends happy too!"

From that day on, Ruby always shared her toys. The busy basement became the happiest place to play, full of laughter and friendship.

The End.
