# Pip the Penguin and the Fair Fix-It Shed

Pip was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a curious waddle. One sunny morning, Pip slid across the grass to the garden shed. The shed smelled like fresh wood and peppermint soap. Sunlight made little dust sparkles dance in the air. Inside, there were jars of buttons, rolls of colorful tape, soft yarn, and a big box of clean, safe craft tools.

“Good morning, tools!” Pip chirped. “Let’s make something helpful today.”

Pip clapped flippers together. “Idea time!” Pip loved to use careful thinking to solve problems. Pip called it penguin thinking: look, listen, think, and then do.

First, Pip looked. On the work table sat a broken snack basket. One side was torn. Carrots and apple slices were falling out. Pip listened. “Plop, plop,” went the apples on the floor.

“Oh no,” said Pip. “Healthy snacks need a safe basket. This is a job for crafting!”

Just then, two neighbors peeked in: Tilly the turtle and Buzz the busy bee. Tilly wore a little green hat. Buzz hummed like a tiny drum.

“Hi, Pip,” said Tilly. “We’re hungry for snack time.”

“Zzznack!” buzzed Buzz, wiggling. “But the basket is broken.”

Pip nodded. “We can fix it. But we must be fair. Everyone will get a snack, and no one will take more than their share. That is justice.”

“What is justice?” asked Tilly, tilting her head.

“Justice means fair and kind,” said Pip. “We share, we take turns, and we listen.”

Pip put on soft cotton gloves. The gloves felt warm and snug. Pip used safe, round-tip scissors, and a little wooden needle with thick yarn. “Look, listen, think, and then do,” Pip whispered.

Pip looked at the torn side. Pip listened to the little rip when the basket moved. Pip thought. “Hmm. The hole is big. We need strong patches.”

Pip found fabric squares in blue and yellow. The blue felt felt soft like a cloud. The yellow felt felt warm like sunshine. Pip lined up the edges. “Tilly, please hold the basket steady. Buzz, please count with me.”

Buzz buzzed happily. “One, two, three!”

Pip sewed slow, big stitches: in, out, pull. In, out, pull. The yarn slid like a gentle snake. The patch grew steady and snug. Pip tied a careful knot. “Ta-da!”

Tilly smiled. “That looks strong.”

“Ztrong!” said Buzz.

Pip tested the basket. Pip placed one apple inside. No plop. Pip added three carrot sticks. No plop. “It works!” Pip cheered.

Tilly reached for two apples. Buzz zoomed toward the carrots. Pip held up a flipper. “Wait, friends. Let’s be fair. We will make a plan.”

Pip drew a simple chart on a big card. Pip drew three smiley faces: Pip, Tilly, Buzz. Under each face, Pip drew one apple and two carrot sticks. “Everyone gets the same. That is justice,” Pip said. “And we choose healthy food. That is good nutrition.”

Tilly nodded. “I like this plan.”

Buzz buzzed, “Fair zhare!”

They began to share, but a gust of wind whooshed under the door. Whoosh! The basket tipped, and a shiny red apple rolled away under a shelf.

“Our apple!” cried Tilly.

Pip took a breath. “Look, listen, think, and then do.” Pip got down low. The floor smelled like pine. Pip listened. “Roll…stop.” Pip peeked under the shelf and saw the apple glinting.

“The apple is stuck,” said Pip. “We need a helper tool.”

Pip crafted a reacher from a clean paint stick and a loop of yarn. Pip tied the yarn into a soft lasso. “This is a gentle grabber,” Pip said. Pip slid the loop under the shelf and scooped the apple back. “Got it!”

“Wow,” said Tilly. “Crafting helps!”

Buzz clapped tiny legs. “Crafting zaves the day!”

They placed the apple back in the basket. Pip smiled. “Now we can share again. But first, hand wash!” Pip set out a small bowl of warm, soapy water and a soft towel. The bubbles smelled like lemons. They each washed hands—front, back, between fingers. Splash, swish, dry.

Pip said, “Now, one apple slice and two carrot sticks each.” Pip handed food with care. Tilly chewed slowly. Crunch, crunch. Buzz sipped apple juice from a tiny cup. Sip, sip.

There was one extra carrot stick left in the basket. Buzz zoomed toward it. Tilly looked at the chart. Pip paused and touched the chart gently. “Let’s think. Who has had two carrot sticks?”

Buzz looked at the chart. “I had two.”

“I had two,” said Tilly.

“I had one,” Pip said. Pip smiled. “The fair choice is for me to take the last one. But I can also save it for later.”

Buzz nodded. “Fair is fair.”

Tilly clapped. “Justice wins!”

Pip put the extra carrot stick in a little paper wrap and wrote, “For later.” Pip added a heart sticker. “Crafting helps us fix things. Justice helps us share. Thinking helps us choose.”

They cleaned the table. Pip put tools back in their jars. The shed felt neat and calm. The sun made a warm square on the floor. Pip stretched and sighed happily.

“Today we learned three things,” Pip said. “One: Crafting can fix and create. Two: Justice is being fair and kind. Three: Critical thinking—look, listen, think, and then do—helps us solve problems. And healthy snacks help our bodies grow strong.”

Tilly smiled. “Thank you, Pip.”

Buzz buzzed a happy tune. “Best zhed day!”

Pip waddled to the door and waved. “See you tomorrow for another craft!”

The End.
