# Panda's Sharing Spoon

In a sunny house with red doors, a gentle, patient panda named Pao padded into the dining room. The room smelled warm and cozy, like sweet rice and leafy tea. Sunlight danced on the wooden table. Pao’s round ears perked up. Today was a special day.

“It is Family Sharing Day,” Pao whispered, patting his soft tummy. “I will share our noodle tradition.”

Pao set the table with care. Tap, tap, tap went the chopsticks. Clink went the bowls. He laid a shiny, long wooden spoon in the center. He smiled. “This is our Sharing Spoon. We take turns. We tell stories. We taste first, then others do.”

Mama Panda walked in, her paws clean and warm. “Good morning, Pao,” she said softly. “What a tidy table.”

“I used my time chart,” Pao said proudly, pointing to a picture clock he had colored. “First, set the table. Next, boil the noodles. Then, invite friends. Last, share stories.”

Mama nodded. “Good plan. Good time-management.” She sang a tiny tune. “Little steps, steady and slow. That’s how traditions grow.”

Pao checked the pot. Bubble, bubble. Steam hugged his face like a warm cloud. He smelled garlic, ginger, and a little soy. “Mmm,” he hummed. “Almost ready.”

Knock, knock! Pao opened the door. There stood Bunny, Fox, and Tori the Tortoise. They sniffed the tasty air. Bunny wiggled her nose. Fox swished his tail. Tori blinked kindly.

“Welcome to Family Sharing Day!” Pao said. “Come to the dining room.”

They sat at the table. The chairs squeaked a little. The table felt smooth under their paws and hands. Pao lifted the heavy pot. “Careful,” Mama said. “Use both paws.”

“I can do it,” Pao said. He moved slow and steady. Steam curled up like soft ribbons. He poured noodles into the big bowl. Swish! He added crunchy carrots and tiny green onions. Sprinkle, sprinkle!

Bunny clapped. “Can we eat now?”

Pao held up the long spoon. “First, I tell how we do it,” he said. “This is our tradition. We use the Sharing Spoon. We take turns. We say one kind thing, or a story, then we serve a little to a friend. We all get a try.”

Fox tilted his head. “Why take turns?”

“So everyone feels seen and safe,” Pao said. “Traditions help us remember to be kind every time.”

Tori gave a slow smile. “I like that.”

Pao took a breath. “I will start. I say thank you to Mama for teaching me. I serve Bunny first.” He dipped the spoon, scoop, scoop, and placed noodles in Bunny’s bowl.

Bunny’s eyes shone. “My turn!” She held the spoon and paused. “I will say: I like Fox’s brave heart.” She served Fox a little. Plop.

Fox wagged his tail. He took the spoon. “I say: Tori is patient.” He served Tori. Tori’s eyes twinkled. Tori took the spoon next. “I say: Pao is kind and careful.” She served Pao, slow and steady.

Pao’s heart felt warm, like a soft blanket. He wanted to take a big bite. But he waited. “We will eat together,” he said. “When we all have some.”

Bunny wiggled again. “Waiting is hard.”

Pao nodded. “Waiting is part of the tradition. We can breathe together.” They all took a deep breath in. The noodles’ smell tickled their noses. They giggled, then breathed out.

Mama poured tea. The cups made tiny clinks. “You are cooperating so well,” she said.

Pao checked his picture clock. “We did the first step. Next is a story round.”

Fox bounced in his chair. “But I am hungry now.” His ears drooped.

Pao thought. He looked at his friends’ faces. He made a decision. “We can tell short stories,” he said. “Small stories, then we eat. That is our plan.” He showed his thumb and finger close together. “Tiny tales!”

Bunny nodded fast. “I can do a tiny tale!”

Pao smiled. “I go first. When I was little, I learned to stir with two paws. My grandma sang, ‘Round and round, slow and sound.’ I still sing it.” Pao hummed the song. The sound felt cozy and calm.

Bunny told a tiny tale about finding a carrot as big as her foot. Fox told a tiny tale about a windy day and a flying leaf. Tori told a tiny tale about a nap in a sun patch.

They all laughed softly. Their eyes sparkled. Pao checked his chart. “Now we eat!”

They lifted their chopsticks and spoons. Slurp, slurp! The noodles were warm and silky. The carrots were crunchy. The green onions were zippy. Everyone sighed, happy and full.

When the bowls were empty, Pao placed the Sharing Spoon back in the center. “Traditions help us remember good ways,” he said. “We shared. We decided together. We waited. We were kind.”

Bunny patted her belly. “I like your family tradition.”

Fox licked his lips. “Can we do it again next week?”

Tori nodded. “I will bring a song.”

Pao felt proud. “Yes! We will keep our Sharing Day. We can invite more friends. We can learn their traditions too.”

Mama smiled. “Traditions grow when we share them.”

Pao washed the spoon, shh, shh, shh. He dried it with a soft towel. He placed it carefully in a special drawer. “See you next time, Sharing Spoon,” he whispered.

The dining room was calm and bright. The table smelled like tea and ginger. Pao hugged his friends goodbye. His heart felt big and light.

Today, Pao learned that sharing a tradition takes patience and planning. Working together and taking turns makes everyone feel loved. And making good choices helps the day go smoothly.

The End.
