# Bubbles Learns to Use Her Words

In a bright, comfortable nursery with soft rugs and sunny windows, toys made quiet clinks and clacks. Colorful books rested on low shelves. A small fish tank hummed softly, and a gentle mobile spun overhead with tiny seashells that tinkled like little bells.

Bubbles, a playful dolphin, visited each day in a big, warm water tub made just for her. The tub had soft foam waves and floating rings that squeaked when touched. Bubbles loved to splash and giggle. She made happy clicks and whistles. “Eee-eee!” she sang, flapping her tail.

Ms. Sunny, the teacher, smiled. “Good morning, Bubbles. Good morning, friends.”

“Good morning!” the children said.

Today was Cleaning and Story Day. On the wall, a picture chart showed the steps:
1. Put blocks in the blue bin.
2. Put books on the shelf.
3. Wipe the table with a cloth.
4. Sit on the rug for a story.

“Teamwork time,” Ms. Sunny said. “We can help together.”

Bubbles wanted to play first. She bounced a ring. Squeak! Squeak! Water splashed—splish, splash! A little wave reached the table. A crayon rolled away. Milo gasped. “My picture!”

The corner of Milo’s paper got a little wet. Bubbles looked at the ripples. She giggled, then saw Milo’s face. His eyebrows dipped. He held his picture to his chest.

“Uh-oh,” Bubbles whispered. Her tummy felt wiggly. She didn’t mean to make Milo sad.

“Bubbles,” Ms. Sunny said softly, crouching by the tub. “It looks like Milo is upset. What can we do?”

Bubbles flicked her tail. She didn’t have hands to pick up blocks. She didn’t know what to say. She wanted to hide under a foam wave.

Ariana walked over with a kind smile. “You can use your words,” she said. “You can say, ‘I’m sorry.’”

Bubbles blinked her big dolphin eyes. Use my words? She took a small breath. “I… I am… s-sorry,” she said in a quiet clicky voice.

Milo looked up. “Thank you, Bubbles,” he said. He touched the wet spot. “It’s okay. It’s only a little wet.”

Bubbles felt warmer in her chest. Ms. Sunny nodded. “That was wonderful, Bubbles. You used your words. Now, let’s finish our steps together.”

Bubbles looked at the chart again. Step 1: blocks in the blue bin. The children started to put blocks away. Clunk, clink. Bubbles could not reach the blocks. She felt worried. “How can I help?” she asked, her voice like tiny bubbles.

“Good asking,” Ms. Sunny said. “You can be the helper who tells the steps. You can use your words to guide us.”

Bubbles smiled. “Okay! Step one: blocks in blue bin,” she called.

The children giggled and moved faster. “Step two?” Ariana asked.

“Books on shelf!” Bubbles said, flipping her tail. The foam rings bobbed. The fish in the tank made little swish sounds.

Milo put the last book away. “What’s next?”

“Wipe the table,” Bubbles said. “Careful near the tank.” She remembered the splash. “I will splash softly.”

Milo and Ariana wiped the table with lemony cloths. The room smelled fresh. Swish, swish.

Bubbles breathed in the lemon smell. “Yum,” she said. “It smells like sunshine.”

“Last step?” Ms. Sunny asked.

“Sit on the rug for story,” Bubbles said proudly.

Everyone sat on the soft rug. Bubbles floated close, quiet in her tub. The mobile made a gentle tinkle. Ms. Sunny held up a big picture book with bright colors. “Today’s story is called ‘Using Our Words.’”

As Ms. Sunny read, the room felt cozy. The children leaned in. Bubbles listened closely. The book showed a bear who got too excited and knocked down blocks. The bear said, “I’m sorry,” and asked, “How can I help?” The friends talked and fixed the tower together.

Bubbles’ eyes sparkled. She whispered, “Like me.”

After the story, Ms. Sunny asked, “What did we learn?”

Ariana raised her hand. “We learned to use our words.”

Milo added, “We learned to say sorry and to ask how to help.”

Bubbles lifted her head. “We learned to finish our steps. We work as a team.”

Ms. Sunny clapped softly. “Yes. Communication helps us. When we talk kindly, we understand each other. We can fix mistakes. We can finish our tasks.”

Bubbles looked at Milo. “Milo,” she said, “I am sorry for the splash. Can I help with your picture now?”

Milo smiled. “Yes. You can tell me which color to use.” He held up two crayons. “Blue or yellow?”

Bubbles thought of sunny lemons and calm water. “Both,” she said. “Blue for water, yellow for sunshine.”

Milo colored. Scratch, scratch. “Thank you, Bubbles.”

Bubbles made a happy whistle. “Thank you for listening.”

Ms. Sunny rolled a little towel near the tub. “Bubbles, you practiced using words. You did it with care.”

Bubbles nodded. “I will keep practicing,” she said. “When I feel wiggly, I can take a breath. I can use my words. I can say sorry. I can ask how to help.”

The nursery felt peaceful. The toys were put away. The table was clean. The rug was soft under little toes. The fish swam in quiet circles. Bubbles floated, calm and bright.

“Teamwork time is happy time,” Bubbles said.

“Happy time,” the children echoed.

And together, with kind words and listening ears, they were ready for another sunny day.

The End.
