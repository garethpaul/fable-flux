# Bella Bird’s Bedroom Helpers

Bella was a small singing bird with soft blue feathers. She lived in a cozy bedroom with a round window, a tiny nest by the pillow, and a night-light that glowed like a warm moon. In the morning, the sun made stripes on the rug. The room smelled like clean blankets and sweet soap.

“Good morning!” Bella chirped. Her voice sounded like little silver bells. “Tweet-a-dee! Time to sing!”

Bella sang and sang. She fluttered from the windowsill to the bookshelf and back again. Music floated through the room. “La-la-laaa! Tweet-tweet!”

But when Bella looked around, her song got quiet. “Oh,” she whispered.

The cozy bedroom was messy. Socks peeked out from under the bed. Crayons rolled near the door. A teddy bear lay face-down on the rug. A pile of books leaned like a wiggly tower.

Bella’s chest felt tight. “I like a tidy nest,” she said softly. “But this is a big job.”

Just then, a gentle voice spoke from the hallway. It was Grandma June. “Bella, sweetheart,” she called, “our neighbor, Mr. Pine, is feeling tired today. He needs a restful nap. Could you help make the bedroom calm and tidy, so we can share this space with him? He’ll rest here while I brew tea.”

Bella blinked. “Help Mr. Pine rest?” Her heart warmed. She liked helping. She liked being part of the home team. She liked being part of the neighborhood, too.

“I will help!” Bella said. “But I feel a little worried. The mess is big.”

Grandma June smiled. She handed Bella a simple list written in big letters with pictures. “We can do it step by step. Follow the directions and sing your helpful song.”

The list said:
1. Pick up books.
2. Tuck in toys.
3. Roll the socks.
4. Sweep with soft brush.
5. Make the bed smooth.

Bella took a deep breath. She smelled the lemony wood polish. “I can do this,” she chirped. “I can help our neighbor.”

Step one: Pick up books.

Bella flapped down to the rug. “Hello, stories,” she said kindly. She scooped the books one by one, pat-pat with her soft wings. Thump. A big book closed with a friendly sound. She lined them on the shelf. Straight, straight, straight. “Tweet, tuck, tidy!” she sang.

Step two: Tuck in toys.

Bella found the teddy bear. Its fur felt fuzzy and warm. “You need a hug,” she said. She gave a tiny bird hug and set Teddy on the bed with a gentle plop. She gathered a toy car—vroom—and a puzzle piece—click—and put them in the toy basket. “All toys home,” she chirped.

Step three: Roll the socks.

Bella nudged the socks into pairs. “Stripe with stripe. Dot with dot.” She rolled them like little cinnamon buns. They smelled like clean cotton. “Round and neat,” she sang. “Tweet-tweet, feet treat!”

Step four: Sweep with soft brush.

Grandma June brought a small brush just right for a bird. Swish, swish. Dust danced in the sunlight like tiny sparkles. Bella giggled. “You look like stars,” she told the dust. She swept them into the pan, tap-tap, and emptied it with care.

Step five: Make the bed smooth.

Bella fluttered up. “Stretch the sheet, smooth the blanket.” She used her wings to pat-pat. Puff went the pillow. She tugged the corners until the bed looked cozy and calm. The night-light winked softly.

Bella looked around. The room felt peaceful. It sounded quiet and safe. It glowed with gentle colors. Her chest felt light and proud.

Grandma June peeked in. “Oh, Bella,” she whispered. “You followed directions and finished each task. You used your helping skills. This room is restful now.”

Just then, Mr. Pine shuffled in with a smile. He wore cozy slippers. “What a lovely room,” he said. “It feels like a soft song.”

Bella’s feathers fluffed with joy. “I sang and cleaned,” she chirped. “I wanted to help our neighbor.”

Mr. Pine sat on the smooth bed. “Thank you, Bella. Because of your help, I can nap and feel better.”

Bella listened to her own feelings. She felt brave for starting. She felt steady for finishing. She felt kind for helping the community. “My worry got smaller,” she told Grandma June, “when I did one step at a time.”

Grandma June nodded. “That is a smart skill, Bella. We serve our community by doing small, caring tasks. We follow directions so our help is strong.”

Bella hopped to the window and sang a soft song for Mr. Pine. “Rest, rest, peace-nest,” she trilled. The note was smooth like warm milk.

Mr. Pine closed his eyes. “Mmm,” he murmured. “Thank you.”

Bella settled into her tiny nest by the pillow. The cozy bedroom now smelled like fresh blankets. The quiet felt like a hug. Bella smiled.

“I can help,” she whispered. “I can follow steps. I can finish what I start. My song can be service.”

And the room, and the neighbors, felt better together.

The End.
