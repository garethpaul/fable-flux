# Fawn’s Honest Fix-It Day

In a cozy house on a quiet street, there was a bright, safe basement lab. It smelled like clean soap and fresh wood. Lights hummed softly. Little fans whirred. Labels on shelves read, “Goggles,” “Glue,” and “Gears.” On the floor lay soft mats that felt squishy under hooves.

A gentle deer fawn named Fawn tip-tapped down the steps. Tip, tap, tip, tap. Fawn wore tiny safety goggles and a soft yellow vest. “Good morning, lab,” Fawn whispered. “I am ready to help.”

Today was Community Fix-It Day. Friends would bring broken things to fix and share. Fawn’s heart felt warm. “I will be careful. I will be honest. I will help my friends,” Fawn said.

Knock, knock!

Mr. Mole peeked in. “Hello, Fawn,” he said. He blinked under big glasses. “My little fan won’t spin.”

Fawn smiled. “We can try to fix it. Safety first.” Fawn pointed to a sign: “Ask for help. Tell the truth. Be safe.” Then Fawn unplugged the fan. Click. “We never touch plugs when they are on,” Fawn said.

Mr. Mole nodded. “Good rule.”

Fawn listened. Whirr… nothing. Fawn sniffed the air. No burn smell. Good. Fawn looked inside. “A leaf is stuck,” Fawn said. “It needs a gentle clean.”

Fawn got a soft brush. Swish, swish. The leaf came out. Fawn plugged the fan back in and pressed the button. Whirr! The fan spun and made a cool breeze. “Brrrr!” Mr. Mole giggled as his whiskers wiggled.

“Thank you, Fawn,” he said.

“You’re welcome,” said Fawn. “You can keep this small brush to clean the fan. And always unplug first.”

Knock, knock!

Squirrel Twins rushed in with a wooden block tower in a bucket. “Our blocks won’t stick!” they chattered. “We want to build a tall house.”

Fawn patted the bucket. “Let’s build together,” Fawn said. “We will be safe and tidy.” Fawn spread a mat. The twins washed sticky paws at the sink. Splash, splash. They put on little vests. “We are ready!” they said.

Fawn found gentle glue and a tiny ruler. “We use a little glue. We wait. We do not rush,” Fawn said. The twins hummed as they worked. Tap, tap. Press, press.

As the tower grew, Fawn saw a wobbly block. Uh-oh. “This block is crooked,” Fawn said. “If we keep going, it may fall.” The twins looked worried.

Fawn took a breath. “Let’s tell the truth. We made a mistake. That is okay. We can fix it.” Fawn softly wiggled the block off and cleaned the glue with a damp cloth. Wipe, wipe. “Try again,” Fawn said.

The twins smiled. “Thank you for being honest,” they said. They pressed the block straight. The tower stood tall and strong. “Hooray!”

Knock, knock!

Mrs. Rabbit came in, holding a small night light shaped like a moon. “It doesn’t glow,” she said. “My bunny kids feel brave with it.”

Fawn nodded. “We can look.” Fawn unplugged the night light. Click. Fawn opened the back. The battery was old and dull. Fawn had one new battery left on the shelf. Fawn reached out… and stopped.

Fawn’s ears wiggled. Fawn looked at a list: “One battery for the weather alarm today.” The weather alarm kept the whole street safe. If a big storm came, the alarm would beep to warn everyone.

Fawn felt a twist in the tummy. Mrs. Rabbit needed light. The street needed the alarm. What should Fawn do?

Fawn stood tall. “I will tell the truth,” Fawn said softly. “Mrs. Rabbit, I have only one battery. It is for the weather alarm. The alarm helps everyone. I can’t use it for the night light.”

Mrs. Rabbit’s ears drooped. The bunny kids peered from the stairs. “Oh,” they whispered.

Fawn’s heart thumped. “But I will not stop helping,” Fawn said. “We can use a safe cord for tonight, far from little paws. And I will call the store for more batteries. We can also make a paper moon that glows with a tiny, safe glow stick until the new battery comes.”

Mrs. Rabbit smiled a little. “Thank you for being honest, Fawn,” she said. “You care for all of us.”

Fawn plugged in the night light with a safe cord and taped the cord down. Smooth and flat. “No trips,” Fawn said. The bunny kids made a happy paper moon with Fawn. Snip, snip. Tape, tape. The moon shined soft.

Then Fawn climbed a small, steady step stool with non-slip feet. “Holding the rail,” Fawn said. Fawn put the one battery into the weather alarm. Beep-beep! It was ready.

Neighbors clapped. Clap, clap, clap. The lab felt warm and bright. Mr. Mole fanned the air. The Squirrel Twins hugged their sturdy tower. Mrs. Rabbit and the bunny kids hopped in a circle.

Fawn’s cheeks felt sunny. “Today we learned to tell the truth,” Fawn said. “We fixed things the safe way. We helped our whole street.”

Mrs. Rabbit nodded. “Integrity means doing what is right, even when it is hard,” she said.

Fawn smiled. “Yes. Integrity keeps us safe. Helping others makes our community strong.”

Everyone said, “Hooray for honest helping!”

That night, the paper moon glowed. The weather alarm was ready. The street was calm and safe. Fawn curled up on the soft mat and listened to the gentle hum of the lab. Hum, hum. Fawn’s heart felt light, because doing the right thing felt good.

The End.
