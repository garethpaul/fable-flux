# Ruby Rabbit's Rainbow Garden

Ruby was a small, fluffy rabbit with soft brown fur and bright pink ears. She lived in a cozy apartment with her family, and her favorite place was the sunny balcony garden. Every morning, Ruby would hop outside to check on the colorful flowers and green plants.

"Good morning, garden!" Ruby would say cheerfully, wiggling her little pink nose at the sweet smells of roses and mint.

One sunny day, Ruby noticed something troubling. All the flowers in her garden looked exactly the same - they were all red roses! Ruby scratched her head with her tiny paw.

"This doesn't look right," she said to herself. "I remember there being different colors before."

Ruby's friend Carlos the caterpillar crawled over on a leaf. "Hola, Ruby! Why do you look worried?"

"Carlos, all my flowers are the same now. They're all red roses. But I think gardens should have many different flowers, don't you?" Ruby asked.

Carlos nodded his green head. "Sí! In my home country, we had gardens with yellow marigolds, purple violets, and white daisies. Different flowers make gardens more beautiful!"

Just then, Aisha the butterfly fluttered down. Her wings sparkled with orange and black patterns. "What's wrong, friends?"

Ruby explained the problem. Aisha listened carefully, then said, "In my family's garden, we always planted many types of flowers. My grandmother taught me that different flowers help each other grow better!"

Ruby's ears perked up. "Really? How?"

"Well," said Aisha, "some flowers keep bugs away from other flowers. Some flowers attract helpful bees. And some flowers grow tall to give shade to smaller ones!"

Ruby thought hard. She tapped her foot on the wooden balcony floor. "So having different kinds of flowers isn't just prettier - it's actually better for the whole garden?"

"Exactly!" said Carlos. "Just like how we're all different friends, but we help each other!"

Ruby's eyes lit up. "I have an idea! But I need to think about this carefully." She sat quietly for a moment, watching a bee buzz from flower to flower.

"I know what to do!" Ruby announced. "I'll ask my mom if we can plant different types of flowers. But first, I should learn which flowers grow well together."

Ruby hopped inside and brought back her picture books about gardens. She spread them on the balcony floor. "Look! This book shows that sunflowers grow tall and give shade. These little pansies like shade! And these herbs smell good and keep bad bugs away!"

Carlos and Aisha looked at the colorful pictures. "You're thinking like a real gardener!" said Aisha.

Ruby's mom came outside. "What are you three planning?"

"Mom, I learned that gardens are better when they have many different kinds of flowers! Can we plant some new ones? I found out which ones will help each other grow!" Ruby showed her mom the books.

Mom smiled. "Ruby, I'm proud of you for thinking so carefully about this. Yes, let's go to the garden store and pick out some different flowers together!"

Soon, Ruby's balcony garden bloomed with yellow sunflowers, purple pansies, white daisies, and fragrant herbs. The garden buzzed with happy bees and colorful butterflies.

"Now this looks perfect!" said Ruby, sniffing the wonderful mix of flower smells. "Different flowers make everything more beautiful and healthy!"

Carlos and Aisha agreed. They had learned that thinking carefully about problems and respecting differences makes everything better - whether in gardens or friendships!

The End.
