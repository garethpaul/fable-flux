# Milo Mouse and the Great Cleanup Game

On a sunny morning, Milo Mouse sat on his favorite peaceful front porch, watching the world wake up. The wooden boards felt warm under his tiny paws, and he could smell the sweet flowers blooming in the garden. Milo loved his porch because he could see everything happening in the neighborhood.

"What a beautiful day!" Milo squeaked happily, stretching his whiskers toward the golden sunshine.

But as Milo looked around, his smile turned into a worried frown. Colorful pieces of trash were scattered everywhere! Empty juice boxes rolled in the gentle breeze, candy wrappers stuck to the fence, and plastic bottles lay on the grass like sleeping giants.

"Oh no!" Milo said, his little heart feeling sad. "Our beautiful neighborhood looks messy. The birds won't want to sing here, and the flowers might get covered up!"

Just then, Milo heard bouncing sounds coming down the street. *Bounce, bounce, bounce!* A tall giraffe named Grace was dribbling a bright orange basketball. Behind her came Benny the bear, spinning a soccer ball on his nose, and Ruby the rabbit, carrying colorful tennis balls.

"Hi, Milo!" called Grace, her long neck swaying gracefully. "We're going to play sports in the park. Want to join us?"

Milo's ears perked up, but then drooped again. "I'd love to play, but look at all this trash! Our neighborhood needs help first."

Benny stopped spinning his soccer ball and looked around with his big brown eyes. "Wow, you're right, Milo. This doesn't look good at all."

Ruby's nose twitched as she sniffed the air. "And it doesn't smell very fresh either. What can we do?"

Milo's whiskers twitched with excitement as an idea popped into his head. "I know! Let's make cleaning up into a fun sports game!"

"A sports game?" asked Grace, tilting her spotted head curiously.

"Yes!" Milo squeaked, jumping up and down on his tiny paws. "We can have a trash basketball game! Grace, you can shoot the bottles into that big recycling bin. Benny, you can kick the cans like soccer balls into the metal bin. And Ruby, you can bounce the small pieces into the regular trash bin!"

The friends' eyes lit up like sparklers. "That sounds amazing!" they all said together.

Grace picked up plastic bottles with her long neck and shot them perfectly into the blue recycling bin. *Swoosh!* "Score!" she cheered.

Benny gently kicked aluminum cans across the grass, making them roll right into the metal bin. *Clang!* "Goal!" he roared happily.

Ruby hopped around, collecting small pieces of paper and bouncing them off her tennis balls into the trash bin. *Plop!* "Perfect shot!" she giggled.

Milo scurried everywhere, cheering for his friends and collecting tiny pieces that only his small paws could reach. "Great teamwork, everyone!" he squeaked proudly.

As they worked together, something magical happened. Other neighbors came outside to watch. Soon, Mrs. Cat was helping, and even the busy ant family joined in, carrying crumbs away in perfect lines.

When they finished, the neighborhood sparkled like a jewel. The flowers stood tall and proud, birds began singing sweet songs, and the air smelled fresh and clean.

"Look what we did together!" Milo said, his heart full of joy. "When we take care of our Earth and work as a team, amazing things happen!"

Grace, Benny, and Ruby gathered around their small friend on the peaceful porch. "Thank you for teaching us that cleaning can be fun," Grace said. "And that taking care of our world is the most important game of all."

From that day on, the friends made cleanup games a weekly tradition, keeping their neighborhood beautiful for everyone to enjoy.

The End.
