# Benny Beaver and the Nursery Build

In a comfortable nursery with soft rugs, gentle lamps, and shelves full of blocks, a busy beaver named Benny tapped his tail. Tap-tap-tap. His fur was warm and brown, and his little paws moved fast. He had a big plan.

“Today I will build the Best Block Bridge,” Benny said. He sniffed the wood blocks. They smelled sweet, like fresh pencils. He stacked red, then blue, then yellow. Click, clack, clunk.

The big clock on the wall went tick-tock, tick-tock. Morning was for building. After snack would be story time. Benny knew the plan. He liked plans. Plans made his tail feel calm.

Benny worked and worked. “I am busy,” he hummed. “So very, very busy.”

Just then, soft feet padded over the rug. It was Lila Lamb. Her wool was fluffy, and she held a green block. “Hi, Benny,” Lila said in a tiny voice. “Can I help?”

Benny’s paws froze. He looked at his neat stacks. He looked at the clock. Tick-tock. “I need to go fast,” he said. “I am busy.”

Lila’s ears drooped. “Oh,” she whispered. She set her green block down and turned away.

Benny felt a pinch in his chest. He shook it off and kept building. Click, clack. The bridge grew tall and wobbly.

Next came Tico Turtle, slow and steady. “Hello, friend,” Tico said. “I can carry blocks on my shell. May I help?”

Benny looked at the tower. He looked at the clock. Tick-tock. “I have no time,” he said. “I am busy.”

Tico blinked and nodded. He pulled his head into his shell, just a little. He moved back toward the book corner.

Benny’s tail drooped for a moment. He gave it a quick shake and stacked another block. Clunk. The tower wobbled again.

Sunlight swam across the rug. The air smelled like orange slices. Snack time was near. Miss Nori, the kind teacher with jingling bracelets, walked by. “Builders,” she sang, “please check your plans and your hearts.”

Benny’s whiskers twitched. “Check my heart?” he asked.

Miss Nori smiled. “A strong bridge lets many friends cross,” she said. “Inclusion means everyone can join, in their own way, in their own time.”

Benny looked at the green block Lila left. He looked at Tico’s gentle eyes. His busy brain slowed. Tick… tock… He took a deep breath. The air felt cool in his nose.

“You can help,” he called softly. “Lila? Tico? I want you with me.”

Lila turned, surprise shining like little stars in her eyes. Tico peeked out of his shell and smiled.

Benny made a new plan in his head. “We will share jobs,” he said. “I can stack. Lila can choose colors. Tico can carry.”

Lila clapped her hooves. “I love colors!” she said. She pressed her nose to the blocks. “Mmm, they smell like sunshine.” She picked red, then blue, then green. “Rainbow bridge!”

Tico scooted over with a hum. Tap-tap went his feet on the rug. He balanced six blocks on his shell and brought them close. “Slow and steady delivery,” he said.

Benny stacked the blocks that Lila chose, on the spot where Tico placed them. Click, clack, clunk—firm and even now. The tower did not wobble. It stood strong.

Other friends came to look. Mina Mouse squeaked, “Can I draw signs?” Benny nodded. “Yes! Draw arrows so we know the way.”

Sammy Squirrel hopped. “Can I hold the base?” Benny smiled. “Yes! Hold and count to three.”

They worked together. Their voices made a happy song.

“One, two, three!” said Sammy.

“Red, blue, green!” sang Lila.

“Delivery,” hummed Tico.

“Stack, stack, stack,” said Benny, tap-tapping his tail in time.

The clock went tick-tock, but Benny did not feel rushed now. He felt calm. He had a team. He looked at Miss Nori. She winked.

Soon the bridge arched like a smile from one soft rug to another. Mina taped her signs. “Come on through!” she cheered.

Miss Nori clinked her bracelets. “Snack time in five minutes,” she said.

Benny nodded. “We have five minutes,” he told the team. “Let’s tidy. Tico, can you return extra blocks? Lila, can you sort by color? Mina, you can stack the signs. Sammy, check the base. I will sweep the scraps.”

Everyone knew what to do. The nursery smelled like oranges and clean wood. The rug felt warm under their feet. The room hummed with happy work.

When the timer chimed—ding-ding!—the bridge was done, the floor was clear, and everyone smiled.

Lila nudged the green block she had first brought. “Thank you for letting me help,” she said.

“Thank you for asking,” Benny said. “I needed you.”

Tico touched the bridge with his gentle foot. “Together is stronger,” he said.

Benny’s heart felt big and light. He thought about his plan. He changed it and made it better with friends. He learned that being busy is not just going fast. Being busy can be sharing jobs, making time, and making space.

Miss Nori clapped. “Builders, what did we learn?” she asked.

Benny stood tall. “Inclusion means everyone is welcome,” he said. “When we help others, they help us too. When we plan our time and work together, we make big, strong things. And we feel happy.”

They took turns crossing the bridge, one by one, paws and hooves and tiny feet. The bridge did not wobble. It held them all.

Benny’s tail tapped a soft, glad beat.

Tap. Tap. Tap.

The End.
