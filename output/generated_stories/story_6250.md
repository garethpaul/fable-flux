# Nutkin's Sorry Letter

In a cozy closet behind the winter coats, a little squirrel named Nutkin had made the most wonderful hideaway. Soft blankets covered the floor, and tiny acorns lined the walls like decorations. This was Nutkin's special place where he felt safe and happy.

But today, Nutkin didn't feel happy at all. He sat curled up in his fluffy tail, feeling very sorry about something he had done.

Earlier that morning, Nutkin had been playing with his best friend Chippy the chipmunk. They were having so much fun collecting colorful leaves when Nutkin accidentally knocked over Chippy's carefully organized pile of seeds.

"Oh no!" Chippy had cried, watching his seeds scatter everywhere.

Instead of helping, Nutkin had gotten scared and run away to his closet hideaway. He left poor Chippy all alone to clean up the mess.

"I was such a bad friend," Nutkin whispered to himself, his little heart feeling heavy. "Chippy must be so sad and angry with me."

Nutkin's tummy started to rumble, but he didn't feel like eating. His eyes felt tired, but he couldn't sleep. When we feel bad about our actions, our bodies feel bad too.

"I need to do something," Nutkin said, wiping his eyes with his tiny paws. "But what if Chippy doesn't want to be my friend anymore?"

That's when Nutkin remembered something his mama had taught him. "When you make a mistake, the bravest thing you can do is say you're sorry and try to make it better."

Nutkin took a deep breath. The sweet smell of the cedar closet usually made him feel calm, but today he needed to be brave. He found a piece of bark and a berry to use as a crayon.

With his little paws shaking just a bit, Nutkin began to write. The letters were wobbly, but they came from his heart:

"Dear Chippy,
I am very sorry I knocked over your seeds.
I am sorry I ran away.
You are my best friend.
Can I help you clean up?
Love, Nutkin"

Writing the letter made Nutkin feel a little better already. Sometimes putting our feelings into words helps our hearts feel lighter.

Nutkin carefully folded the bark letter and crept out of his closet hideaway. His little legs felt wobbly with nervousness, but he kept walking toward Chippy's tree.

He found Chippy sitting sadly by his scattered seeds, still trying to sort them out.

"Chippy?" Nutkin called softly. "I... I brought you something."

Chippy looked up with surprise as Nutkin handed him the letter. Chippy's eyes grew wide as he read the wobbly words.

"Oh, Nutkin!" Chippy smiled. "I was hoping you would come back. I wasn't angry, just sad that you left."

"I was scared you wouldn't forgive me," Nutkin admitted.

"Of course I forgive you!" Chippy said, giving Nutkin a warm hug. "Everyone makes mistakes. What matters is that you came back to make it right."

Together, the two friends gathered all the scattered seeds. Nutkin even found some extra pretty ones to add to Chippy's collection. They laughed and chatted as they worked, and soon the pile was bigger and better than before.

That evening, back in his closet hideaway, Nutkin felt peaceful again. His tummy was happy after sharing dinner with Chippy, and his heart felt light and warm.

"Making amends takes courage," Nutkin said to himself as he snuggled into his soft blankets, "but it always makes everything better."

The End.
