# Benny Beaver's Big Bathroom Problem

Benny Beaver was always busy, busy, busy! He loved to build dams, chop wood, and swim in the pond. But there was one place where Benny had a big problem - the bathroom.

Every morning, Benny would rush into the bathroom. "Brush teeth, wash face, comb fur - GO, GO, GO!" he would chatter to himself. But rushing made everything harder.

SPLASH! Toothpaste would fly everywhere. CRASH! His toothbrush would fall in the sink. "Oh no!" Benny would cry, feeling frustrated and messy.

One Tuesday morning, Benny's tummy rumbled loudly. "GRUMBLE, GRUMBLE!" He had been so busy rushing that he forgot to eat his healthy breakfast of fresh bark and berries.

"I'm too hungry to think straight," Benny said, looking at his reflection in the mirror. His fur was all messy, and toothpaste was stuck on his whiskers. "There has to be a better way!"

Just then, Benny's wise grandmother, Nana Beaver, knocked on the bathroom door. "Benny, dear, may I come in?"

"Yes, Nana," Benny sighed. "But I'm making a mess again."

Nana Beaver waddled in and saw the splattered toothpaste and Benny's worried face. "Oh my," she said gently. "It looks like you need to slow down and take deep breaths."

"But I have so much to do!" Benny protested.

"Let me share a secret," Nana said, sitting on the edge of the bathtub. "When I was young like you, I learned that going slow actually helps you go faster in the end."

"Really?" Benny's eyes grew wide.

"Really! Let's try together. First, take three deep breaths with me." Nana demonstrated, breathing in slowly through her nose and out through her mouth. "In... and out. In... and out. In... and out."

Benny copied her, feeling his shoulders relax. The bathroom suddenly felt calmer.

"Now," said Nana, "let's practice doing one thing at a time. What do you need to do first?"

"Brush my teeth," Benny said thoughtfully.

"Perfect! Put just a tiny bit of toothpaste on your brush - about the size of a pea." Nana showed him. "Now brush gently, counting to ten for each part of your mouth."

Benny tried it. "One, two, three..." he counted slowly. The toothpaste stayed on his teeth instead of flying around the bathroom!

"Wonderful!" Nana clapped. "See how much better that worked?"

Next, they practiced washing his face with gentle, slow movements. Benny splashed the warm water carefully and patted his face dry with his fluffy towel.

"This feels so much better," Benny said, looking at his clean reflection. "And I don't feel stressed anymore!"

"That's because you're using self-regulation," Nana explained. "That means taking control of your feelings and actions, even when you want to rush."

After they finished in the bathroom, Nana shared her nutritious breakfast with Benny - crunchy apple slices and sweet maple bark. "A good breakfast helps your brain work better too," she said.

From that day on, Benny remembered Nana's lesson. When he felt the urge to rush, he would take three deep breaths and remind himself: "Slow and steady, one thing at a time."

Soon, Benny's bathroom time became peaceful and fun. He even had time to help his little sister learn the same calm routine.

"Thank you for teaching me, Nana," Benny said, giving her a big hug. "Now I know that being patient with myself makes everything easier!"

The End.
