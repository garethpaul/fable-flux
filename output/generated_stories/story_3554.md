# Nutkin's Big Office Adventure

Nutkin the squirrel lived in a cozy oak tree right outside Mrs. Chen's home office window. Every morning, he watched her type on her computer and talk on the phone. The office smelled like fresh coffee and had pretty plants on the windowsill.

One sunny Tuesday, Nutkin noticed something terrible. "Oh no!" he squeaked, pressing his tiny paws against the glass. The beautiful plants were drooping! Their leaves looked sad and brown around the edges.

Mrs. Chen was so busy working that she hadn't noticed her plants needed help. Nutkin's fluffy tail twitched with worry. "I need to think of a plan," he said to himself, tapping his little claws on the tree branch.

First, Nutkin tried chittering loudly at the window. "Chit-chit-chit! Look at your plants!" But Mrs. Chen had her headphones on and couldn't hear him.

Next, he tried waving his bushy tail like a flag. He jumped up and down, but Mrs. Chen was looking at her computer screen, not the window.

Nutkin sat on his branch and thought hard. "What would get her attention?" he wondered, scratching behind his ear. Then his brown eyes lit up with an idea!

He scampered down to gather supplies. First, he collected colorful fall leaves - red ones, yellow ones, and orange ones that crinkled when he touched them. Then he found some smooth pebbles and a few twigs.

Back at the window, Nutkin began his creative plan. He carefully arranged the red leaves in the shape of an arrow pointing down at the thirsty plants. The leaves rustled softly in the breeze.

"That's good, but I need more," Nutkin said. He used the yellow leaves to spell out "H-E-L-P" on the windowsill. The bright colors looked beautiful against the white window frame.

Finally, he placed the pebbles in a line leading from his leaf message to the droopy plants. The smooth stones caught the sunlight and sparkled.

Mrs. Chen looked up from her computer to stretch. "What's that?" she said, noticing the colorful display outside her window. She walked closer and saw Nutkin sitting proudly next to his leaf message.

"Oh my goodness!" Mrs. Chen laughed, reading the word "HELP" spelled in leaves. She followed the arrow and stone path with her eyes. "My poor plants! Thank you, little squirrel friend!"

She immediately got her watering can - a blue one that made gentle splashing sounds as she filled it. The water sparkled like tiny diamonds as it fell on the thirsty plants.

"Glub, glub, glub," went the water as the plants drank deeply. Soon their leaves perked up and turned green again. The office filled with the fresh, clean smell of happy plants.

Nutkin clapped his tiny paws together with joy. Mrs. Chen smiled and waved at him through the window. "You're very smart," she said. "Thank you for helping me take care of my plants!"

From that day on, whenever Mrs. Chen got too busy to notice her plants, Nutkin would create helpful messages with leaves and stones. He became the office's special plant helper, and Mrs. Chen always made sure to look out the window to check on both her plants and her clever squirrel friend.

Nutkin learned that when you have a problem, thinking carefully and being creative can help you find the perfect solution. And the best solutions help everyone!

The End.
