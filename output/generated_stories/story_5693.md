# Ellie's Big Heart

Little Ellie the elephant calf sat in the back seat of the family car, her trunk swaying gently as they drove down the sunny road. The car smelled like her mama's favorite flowers, and the soft seats felt cozy against her gray skin.

"Are we there yet, Mama?" Ellie asked, pressing her small trunk against the cool window.

"Almost, sweet pea," Mama Elephant said with a warm smile. "We're going to visit Grandpa Elephant, remember?"

Ellie bounced excitedly in her seat. She loved Grandpa's house with its big garden and the tire swing that hung from the old oak tree. But as she looked in her little backpack, her happy feeling turned into worry.

"Oh no!" Ellie gasped, her ears flapping. "Mama, I forgot something very important!"

"What is it, little one?" Papa Elephant asked, looking at her in the rearview mirror with kind eyes.

Ellie's trunk drooped sadly. "Last week, I was playing with Grandpa's special writing journal. I wanted to draw him a picture, but I accidentally tore one of the pages. I was so scared that I hid it under his couch cushion."

Her eyes filled with tears. "Grandpa will be so upset with me. That journal has all his important stories in it!"

Mama Elephant pulled the car over to a shady spot under some tall trees. She turned around and gently touched Ellie's trunk with her own.

"Oh, sweetheart," Mama said softly. "Grandpa loves you very much. Do you think he would stay angry at you for an accident?"

Ellie sniffled. "But it was his special book. What if he doesn't want to play with me anymore?"

Papa Elephant's voice was gentle and warm. "Ellie, when we make mistakes, the best thing we can do is tell the truth. Grandpa's heart is even bigger than ours, and it's full of love for you."

"Really?" Ellie asked, wiping her eyes with her trunk.

"Really," Mama said. "And you know what? When we ask for forgiveness, it helps our hearts feel better too. It's like sunshine after a rainy day."

Ellie thought about this as they continued driving. She watched the trees dance in the breeze and listened to the happy chirping of birds outside. Slowly, she began to feel a little braver.

When they arrived at Grandpa's house, Ellie took a deep breath. The familiar smell of his garden roses filled her nose, and she could hear him humming his favorite song inside.

"Grandpa?" Ellie called out as she walked into his cozy living room.

"My dear Ellie!" Grandpa Elephant beamed, opening his arms wide for a hug.

With her heart beating fast, Ellie told Grandpa everything about the torn page. She spoke slowly and clearly, just like Mama had taught her.

Grandpa listened carefully, then gave her the biggest, warmest hug. "Oh, my precious granddaughter," he said, his voice full of love. "Thank you for being so brave and honest with me."

"You're not angry?" Ellie asked, surprised.

"Not at all," Grandpa chuckled. "Pages can be fixed, but honesty and love are much more important. I'm proud of you for telling me the truth."

Together, they carefully taped the page back into the journal. Then Grandpa gave Ellie her very own writing book.

"Now we can write stories together," he said with a twinkle in his eye.

Ellie's heart felt light and happy again. She had learned that forgiveness makes everything better, and that telling the truth, even when it's scary, is always the right thing to do.

The End.
