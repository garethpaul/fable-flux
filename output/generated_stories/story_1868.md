# Milly Mouse and the Busy Basement

In the corner of a busy basement lived a kind little mouse named Milly. The basement was always full of sounds - *thump, thump* went the washing machine, *whoosh* went the dryer, and *click, click* went the heater turning on and off.

Milly had soft gray fur and bright black eyes that sparkled when she smiled. She loved her cozy home behind the old wooden boxes, but sometimes the basement felt very crowded and noisy.

Every day, many other creatures visited the basement. There was Benny the big brown rat who liked to stomp around loudly. *Stomp, stomp, stomp!* There was Susie the spider who spun webs everywhere. And there was Freddy the frog who had hopped in through a crack and liked to sing very loudly. "RIBBIT! RIBBIT!"

At first, Milly tried to make everyone be quiet. "Please, Benny," she squeaked politely, "could you walk more softly? The noise hurts my little ears."

But Benny just laughed. "This is how I walk, little mouse! I can't help being big!"

Milly tried talking to Susie. "Susie, your webs are blocking my path to the water drip. Could you move them?"

Susie shook her head. "Sorry, Milly, but I need my webs right there to catch flies for dinner."

Then Milly asked Freddy, "Could you please sing more quietly? I'm trying to sleep."

Freddy puffed up his green throat. "But singing loud makes me happy! RIBBIT!"

Milly felt sad and frustrated. She wanted everyone to change so she could be comfortable. She tried plugging her ears with cotton fluff, but she could still hear everything. She tried moving to different corners, but the basement wasn't very big.

One day, Milly sat down and thought really hard. She looked around at her basement friends. Benny was being helpful, moving heavy things with his strong paws. Susie was keeping the basement clean by catching bugs. Freddy was just being cheerful and happy.

"Maybe," Milly whispered to herself, "I don't need them to change. Maybe I can find a way to be okay with how they are."

Milly decided to try something new. Instead of fighting the noise, she listened carefully. Benny's stomping had a rhythm - *stomp, stomp, rest, stomp, stomp, rest.* Susie's web-spinning made a gentle *swish, swish* sound. And Freddy's singing was actually quite musical!

"You know what?" Milly said out loud. "You all make beautiful sounds together!"

She started to tap her tiny paws to Benny's stomping rhythm. She began to dance around Susie's webs instead of being annoyed by them. And she started humming along with Freddy's ribbiting songs.

"Milly!" called Benny. "You're dancing! Do you like our sounds now?"

"I do!" squeaked Milly happily. "I learned that I can't change you, and I don't want to! You're all perfect just the way you are. Instead, I changed how I think about things."

From that day on, the busy basement became Milly's favorite place. She learned that accepting others for who they are made everyone happier. She also learned that it was okay to have quiet time in her own corner when she needed it - that was her special boundary.

And whenever new creatures came to visit the basement, Milly would say with a warm smile, "Welcome! We're all different here, and that's what makes us special!"

The End.
