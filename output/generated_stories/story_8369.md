# Bear Cub Steps in Time

The old house was quiet. The attic was even quieter. Dust sparkled in the sun like tiny stars. Boxes sat like little mountains, and a big round window let in warm, golden light. It smelled like pine wood and old books.

A friendly bear cub named Benny climbed the creaky stairs. Creak, creak, creak. His soft paws tickled the steps. “Hello?” Benny whispered. “Is anyone up here?”

From behind a trunk came tiny squeaks. Three field mice peeked out. “Hi, Benny!” squeaked Mia. “We’re trying to move these books to the donation box. We are volunteering to help the library!”

Benny’s round ears perked up. “Volunteering is helping! I want to help, too,” he said with a smile.

Milo, the smallest mouse, pointed to a wobbly pile. “We tried, but the books slide. We bump into each other. We keep tripping.”

Benny looked around. He noticed yarn on the floor, a jump rope, and a line of boxes. He felt a flutter in his tummy. He wanted to be a helper. He wanted to be a leader who listened. But how?

He took a deep breath. The attic air tasted dusty but sweet. “We can move together,” Benny said gently. “We can use coordination. That means moving our bodies in the same way, at the same time.”

“Co-or-di-na-tion,” the mice repeated.

Benny nodded. “Let’s warm up our bodies first. Exercise helps us move better.”

They made a little circle. Benny showed them. “Arms up,” he said. “Stretch to the sky.” Their fingers wiggled. “Arms down,” he said. Their paws touched their toes. “Wiggle, wiggle.” They giggled.

“Now march in place,” said Benny. “Step, step, step.” The attic floor thumped softly. Thump, thump, thump. “Clap, clap,” he added. Their claps sounded like tiny raindrops. Clap, clap.

“Step, step, clap. Step, step, clap,” sang Benny. The mice followed. Step, step, clap. Step, step, clap. Their bodies began to move together. They felt steady. They felt ready.

Mia smiled. “This feels good!”

Benny’s eyes shone. “Great job! Now we will move the books in a line. We will make a book train. We pass the books with the same rhythm. Step, step, pass. Step, step, pass.”

He set them in places: Mia by the tall box, Milo in the middle, and Max near the donation box by the window. Benny stood at the first stack. “Listen to my count,” he said softly. “One, two, pass. One, two, pass.”

They began. One, two, pass. The books slid from paw to paw. The attic was quiet except for gentle breaths and the soft swish of pages. Swish, swish. The sun made warm stripes on the floor.

“Oops!” cried Max as a book wobbled.

“That’s okay,” said Benny. “We slow down and try again. We keep the same beat. Breathe in… and out. One, two, pass.”

They tried again. One, two, pass. The book train rolled. Soon the stack grew in the donation box.

A sudden gust of wind whooshed through the window. The jump rope on the floor slithered like a snake. Milo squeaked and stepped on the rope. “Ah!”

Benny moved carefully. He held out his paw. “Freeze,” he said. “We pause together. We look. We think.” He smiled calmly. “We can use coordination to be safe.”

He showed them a tip. “Eyes look where you step. Knees soft. Slow feet.” He lifted the rope and coiled it neatly. “Let’s make a safe path.”

They shifted boxes, still using the rhythm. They counted as they moved: One, two, slide. One, two, slide. The path cleared. The attic felt friendly again.

Mia’s whiskers twitched. “Thank you, Benny. You are a good leader.”

“A leader listens and helps,” said Benny. “You are leaders, too. We are a team.”

They finished the book train, and the donation box was full. It looked proud and tall. The mice cheered. “We did it!”

Benny’s tummy rumbled. “That was good exercise,” he said with a giggle. His fur was warm. His paws felt strong.

Max pointed to a dusty drum in the corner. “Let’s make a coordination song!”

Benny tapped the drum, boom-boom. The mice clapped, clap-clap. Boom-boom, clap-clap. They marched in place. Step, step, clap. Their feet made soft thumps. Their hearts felt happy.

“Now we cool down,” Benny said. “Breathe in the cookie smell.” The attic did smell a little like cookies. “Breathe out slow.” They stretched and smiled.

Mia hugged Benny’s leg. “We learned to move together. We learned to help others.”

Benny nodded. “When we use coordination, we can do hard jobs. When we exercise, our bodies feel ready. When we volunteer, we make the world kinder.”

They carried the donation box downstairs, one careful step at a time. Creak, creak, creak. The sun warmed their backs. The box felt lighter because they worked together.

At the door, Benny waved. “Great team today!”

“See you soon!” squeaked the mice.

Benny looked back at the quiet attic. He felt proud and calm. He had listened, led with kindness, and kept a steady beat. He whispered, “One, two, thank you,” and padded down the hall.

Lesson: Move together. Help together. Be kind together.

The End.
