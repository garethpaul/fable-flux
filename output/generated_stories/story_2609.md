# Tessa Turtle’s Teamwork Day

In a warm, comfortable nursery, sunlight made soft squares on the floor. Blocks were stacked like tiny towers, crayons smelled sweet and waxy, and a clock tick-tocked like a gentle drum. Tessa the turtle peeped from her shell. She was shy, and she liked quiet corners. She loved crafting, but she worried about talking to others.

“Good morning, friends,” said Miss Alma, the teacher, with a bright smile. “Today we will make a Giant Friendship Garden! We will use paper, glue, and colors. We will need to work together.”

Tessa’s heart made a little thump. Work together? She tucked her head in, then peeked out again. She liked gardens. She liked making things. She just felt small when there were many voices.

“Can I help?” whispered Tessa, almost too quiet to hear.

Miss Alma knelt beside her. “Yes, Tessa. Your careful hands will be perfect. We will need leaves, stems, petals, and a big sunny sky. We have a short time before snack, so we will plan first.” She pointed to the clock. “Tick-tock means we use time wisely.”

Tessa nodded. Planning felt safe. “Okay,” she whispered.

Miss Alma clapped softly. “Team, let’s talk and listen. Cooperation means we work together, share, and help. Communication means we use kind words and ears that listen.”

Ben the bunny hopped up. “I can cut big circles for flowers!”

Mia the mouse squeaked, “I can color the sky!”

Leo the lamb said, “I can glue!”

Tessa took a breath. “I can make leaves,” she said, a bit louder. “I like tiny shapes.”

“Great idea,” said Miss Alma. “Let’s make a plan board. Ben: circles by the window. Mia: sky at the art table. Leo: glue station by the rug. Tessa: leaf table near the sunny spot. We will meet in ten minutes and put it all together.”

The room filled with soft sounds. Snip-snip went the scissors. Scratch-scratch went the crayons. Squish-squish went the glue. Tessa pressed green paper under her gentle turtle thumbs. She traced smooth leaf shapes and cut slowly. She felt proud. Her leaves looked neat and friendly.

Ben called from the window, “I made so many circles! But some are too wiggly.”

Tessa looked up. “It’s okay,” she said softly. “Flowers can be wiggly.”

Mia waved a blue crayon. “I colored a big sky, but I missed a corner.”

Tessa smiled. “We can add a cloud there,” she suggested. “A fluffy white cloud.”

Leo the lamb held the glue bottle. “Oops,” he said, “a little too much squish.”

Miss Alma walked over. “Let’s remember respect-for-others. We take turns with the glue. Leo, use tiny dots. Dot, dot, not a lot.”

Tessa nodded. “I can help you count the dots,” she offered. “One, two, three.”

Leo grinned. “Thanks, Tessa.”

Tick-tock went the clock. Miss Alma said, “Friends, time check! Two minutes to meet.”

Tessa gathered her leaves on a tray. The paper felt smooth and cool. She smelled the glue, sweet and sticky. She took a deep breath and carried the tray carefully.

At the big table, everyone brought their parts. Ben’s circles were bright and cheerful. Mia’s sky was wide and calm. Leo had the glue ready. Tessa set down her leaves like a little green breeze.

“Let’s talk it through,” said Miss Alma. “Where do the stems go?”

Tessa pointed to the bottom. “Down here, like tiny ladders to the flowers.”

“Good idea,” said Ben. “I’ll cut long strips for stems.”

Mia placed the sky on top of the paper wall. “Here is the blue. Tessa, where should your leaves go?”

“On the stems,” Tessa said softly. “Two on each, like hugs.”

Leo dabbed small dots of glue. “Dot, dot, not a lot,” he chanted. Tessa pressed each leaf gently. Press, hold, release. She felt the paper warm under her hands.

“Cloud in the corner,” Mia remembered. She tore a soft white shape. “Fluffy!”

Ben giggled. “My wiggly circles can be petals.”

“Wiggles make them dance,” Tessa said, and her shy voice sounded a little bright.

They worked and talked. They asked, “Can I try?” and said, “Your turn.” They listened to ideas. They waited and watched the clock. When a petal stuck sideways, Tessa whispered, “Let’s fix it together.” When Leo reached for the glue, he asked, “May I, please?” and Tessa passed it with a smile.

At last, the Giant Friendship Garden was done. Flowers in oranges, reds, and yellows smiled up from the wall. Leaves hugged stems. The blue sky had a soft white cloud. It looked like a happy day.

Miss Alma clapped. “Look at what cooperation can do! You planned, you talked, you shared, and you used your time well.”

Ben hopped. “We did it!”

Mia squeaked, “Together!”

Leo bleated, “Teamwork!”

Tessa felt warm and brave. “I liked helping,” she said. “When we cooperate, the work feels lighter, and the garden grows bigger.”

Miss Alma nodded. “Yes, Tessa. Cooperation means we do better together than alone. Communication, crafting, time care, and respect made this garden bloom.”

Tessa touched a leaf. It was smooth and green. She smiled at her friends. “Tomorrow,” she said, “maybe we can make a pond.”

Ben grinned. “With turtles!”

Tessa giggled. “Maybe just one shy turtle who likes friends.”

They all laughed, and the clock ticked a happy tick-tock. The nursery felt cozy and bright, and the Giant Friendship Garden glowed like a big, kind hug on the wall.

The End.
