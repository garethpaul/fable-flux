# Miko’s Porch Picnic Plan

Miko was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. Every morning, Miko sat on the peaceful front porch of his little blue house. He listened to birds sing, felt the warm sun on his whiskers, and smelled fresh leaves in the breeze.

One sunny day, Miko had a big idea. “I will make a porch picnic!” he squeaked. “I will make a rainbow snack board with healthy foods. It will be colorful and yummy.”

He brought out a tiny wooden table and a clean, shiny board. He spread a sunny-yellow cloth that felt smooth under his paws. He hummed a happy tune. But when he opened his food box, he frowned. Inside were only a few crackers and one tiny grape.

“Oh no,” said Miko softly. “This is not a rainbow. This is not a healthy picnic for my friends.”

Miko felt a little worried. His tail drooped. He wanted to share good, strong foods that helped bodies grow. He wanted crunchy, juicy, bright foods. He wanted to teach his friends about colors and vitamins. But he didn’t have enough.

Then Miko took a deep breath. He put his paws on his heart. “I can ask for help,” he said. “We can work as a team. We can craft a beautiful snack board together.”

Miko stepped to the edge of the porch and called, “Friends! Porch picnic today! We will make a rainbow of healthy snacks! Teamwork time!”

First came Bella the bunny, hopping with soft thumps. “I heard picnic!” she giggled. Her ears twitched in the breeze. “I have orange carrot sticks. Crunch, crunch!”

“Thank you, Bella!” said Miko. “Orange foods help our eyes see well.”

Next came Tito the turtle, slow and steady, with a tiny basket tied by a green ribbon. “I brought green cucumbers,” he said, voice gentle like a whispering stream. “They are cool and juicy.”

“Wonderful, Tito!” said Miko. “Green foods help our bodies stay strong.”

Flit the finch fluttered down, wings humming. “Tweet! I found red strawberries,” she chirped. The strawberries smelled sweet, like sunshine and rain. “They are soft and sweet.”

“Perfect, Flit,” Miko smiled. “Red foods can help our hearts.”

Luna the lamb trotted up, hooves clicking softly on the porch. “I have yellow corn bites,” she baa-ed. “They go pop in your mouth.”

“Thank you, Luna,” said Miko. “Yellow foods can give us energy.”

Pip the puppy wagged and wiggled. His nose was wet and cool. “I brought purple grapes,” he yipped. “Plump and round!”

“Great job, Pip,” said Miko. “Purple foods help our brains think.”

Miko clapped his tiny paws. “We have colors! Now let’s craft our rainbow board.”

Everyone gathered on the porch. The wood felt warm under their feet. The air smelled like herbs and fresh water. Birds sang a happy song. Miko showed them how to make a rainbow path on the board.

“Red first,” Miko said, placing strawberries in a curvy smile. “Then orange carrots in a neat, crunchy line.” Bella carefully stacked carrot sticks like tiny logs.

“Yellow corn next,” Luna said, sprinkling golden kernels like little stars.

“Green cucumbers,” Tito added, laying cool slices in a wavy river.

“And purple grapes!” Pip placed them gently, one by one, like round marbles.

Miko added the crackers and a small bowl of hummus that he whisked with a spoon. “Hummus is made from chickpeas,” he explained. “It gives us protein. Protein helps our muscles.”

Flit sniffed the hummus. “It smells nutty and smooth,” she chirped.

Miko nodded. “Let’s also add water cups,” he said, pouring clear water that splashed and tinkled. “Water keeps us hydrated. Hydrated means our bodies have enough water to feel good.”

They stood back. The rainbow snack board looked bright and beautiful. The porch felt cozy and calm. But then a little problem popped up. The wind began to blow. Whooooosh! The napkins fluttered and the light cups tipped.

“Oh no!” Pip barked. “Our picnic!”

Miko stayed calm. “Teamwork again,” he said. “Let’s craft a wind-safe porch.”

He showed them how to fold napkins into little fans and tuck them under smooth stones he found by the steps. “Crafting can help,” he said.

Tito used his steady shell to hold the cloth. Bella gathered small pinecones as tiny weights. Luna tied the corners of the cloth to the porch posts with soft ribbon. Flit flew to bring a small basket to hold the cups.

The wind slowed. The cloth stayed put. The rainbow board stayed lovely and bright.

Miko smiled. “We solved the problem together,” he said. “Now, let’s taste.”

They crunched and munched. Crunch went the carrots. Snap went the cucumbers. Pop went the corn. The strawberries were juicy and sweet. The grapes were cool and smooth. The hummus was creamy and kind to their tummies. The water was fresh and splashy.

“This rainbow snack makes my body happy,” said Bella.

“My mind feels clear,” said Tito.

“My heart feels full,” chirped Flit.

“My wag is strong!” laughed Pip.

Miko patted his tummy. “Good food helps us play, learn, and grow,” he said. “Colors in our snacks give us many vitamins.”

They finished their picnic as the sun made the porch glow golden. Miko looked at his friends with joy. “Thank you for helping,” he said. “We used teamwork. We crafted a beautiful board. We learned about nutrition.”

Everyone hugged. The porch felt warm, safe, and proud.

Miko whispered, “When we work together and choose healthy foods, our bodies and our hearts feel good.”

The End.
