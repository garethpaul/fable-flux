# Otto the Otter’s Backyard Boogie

Otto was a playful otter with soft whiskers and a shiny, splashy tail. One sunny morning, he peeked out the back door and wiggled with joy. “Today is the Backyard Boogie!” he squeaked. The grass sparkled with dew. Flowers smelled sweet, like honey and sunshine. Birds chirped, “Tweet-tweet!” and the warm breeze tickled Otto’s fur.

Otto loved to dance. He twirled. He hopped. He flopped! But today he wanted to do his best dance. He wanted to learn a new move called the Star Spin. It looked big and bright and spinny!

Otto rushed outside. “I can do it now!” he said. He tried to spin fast. Whoosh—whoa—plop! Otto tumbled onto the soft grass. His paws were all mixed up, and his tail thumped the ground.

From the fence, his friend Mia the mouse squeaked, “Are you okay, Otto?”

Otto sat up and brushed off a leaf. “I’m okay. I want to do the Star Spin right now!”

Mia tilted her head. “Maybe we can get ready first.”

“Ready?” Otto blinked. “Why? I just want to dance!”

Mia smiled. “Preparation helps. We can warm up and practice small steps. Getting ready helps us feel safe and strong.”

Otto’s whiskers twitched. He looked around the sunny backyard. The soft blanket was spread under the shade. A big blue water bowl sat by the porch. The music speaker waited, quiet and still.

“Okay,” Otto said. “Let’s prepare!”

First, they made space. Otto moved a garden pail and a bouncy ball to the side. “Clear and safe,” Mia said. “Good job.”

Second, they warmed up. Mia shook her tiny paws. “Let’s stretch. Wiggle fingers and toes!” Otto wiggled his paws and tail. He felt warm and ready. The sun kissed his fur. He took a deep breath. The air smelled like fresh cut grass.

Third, they practiced the steps. “The Star Spin is three parts,” Mia said kindly. “One: step wide. Two: arms like a star. Three: slow spin.”

Otto tried part one. “Step wide.” His paws spread, steady on the ground.

He tried part two. “Arms like a star.” His little otter arms reached out big and bright.

He tried part three. “Slow spin.” He turned slowly. The world moved gentle, not too fast.

“Nice!” Mia clapped. “You prepared!”

Otto smiled. His tummy felt calm. “Let’s add music,” he said. He pressed play. Soft, happy beats floated in the air. Tap-tap, thump-thump.

Just then, more friends came to the backyard. Gus the turtle carried a tiny drum on his shell. “I’m slow, but I can keep the beat,” he said proudly.

Zara the zebra trotted in with stripes shining. “I like hops and zigzags,” she said.

Pip the parrot flew down and squawked, “Squawk! I flap and clap!”

Otto waved. “We’re getting ready for the Backyard Boogie. We are preparing so we can do our best.”

Gus nodded. “I need extra time. Slow is my style.”

“Different styles are great,” Mia said. “Let’s celebrate them.”

They all prepared together. Gus practiced slow steps to the drum. Zara counted, “One-two-hop, one-two-hop.” Pip flapped and clapped, “Flap-clap, flap-clap!” Otto kept trying the Star Spin, one, two, three.

Otto felt happy. “We are all different,” he said, “and that makes our dance colorful!”

The music got a little faster. Otto took a sip of water. “Preparing means taking care of our bodies too,” Mia reminded. Otto smiled. The cool water felt good.

“Ready for the show?” Pip asked, eyes bright.

Otto’s tummy fluttered. He remembered his first tumble. He remembered to prepare. “I can do this,” he whispered.

They lined up on the sunny grass. The audience was small: some ladybugs on a leaf, a sleepy cat on the porch, and a butterfly that liked to float. The breeze whispered, “Shhhhh,” and then, the beat began.

Gus started with slow, steady taps: dum… dum… dum. Zara added hops and zigzags that made her stripes shimmer. Pip flapped and clapped with a happy squawk. Otto stepped wide—one. He made his arms like a star—two. He spun slowly—three.

He did not tumble.

He felt strong. He felt ready. He felt proud!

“Go, Otto!” Mia cheered.

Otto grinned and added a tiny sparkle hop at the end. The friends finished together. The ladybugs waved their tiny wings. The butterfly dipped and danced. The sleepy cat raised one paw, like a quiet cheer.

Otto bowed. “We did it because we prepared,” he said.

Gus nodded. “Preparing helped me keep the beat.”

Zara swished her tail. “Preparing helped me hop safer.”

Pip ruffled his feathers. “Preparing helped me clap brighter!”

Mia smiled at them all. “Preparation helps us do our best. It keeps us safe. It helps us improve. And our differences make our dance special.”

Otto’s heart felt warm, like sunshine on fur. He looked at his friends. “Next time, we can learn a new move together. We’ll prepare in steps, and we’ll cheer for each other.”

Everyone shouted, “Yes!”

They turned down the music and stretched again. The grass felt cool on their toes. The sky was blue and wide. The Backyard Boogie was bright and friendly, just like their hearts.

That night, Otto placed a little list by the door: Make space. Warm up. Practice steps. Drink water. Go slow. Have fun.

He smiled. “I am ready for tomorrow.”

The End.
