# Ellie’s Pantry Parade

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a curious trunk. One rainy morning, Ellie tiptoed into the pantry. The pantry smelled like cinnamon and warm oats. The shelves were full of jars that clicked and clinked, and paper bags rustled like little whispers.

“I want to make a snack for my friends,” Ellie said, her trunk wiggling. “A kind snack to share!”

Her friends were small and shy: Pip the mouse, Nib the squirrel, and Dot the ladybug. They peeked in through the open door.

“Is it safe?” Pip squeaked.

“It looks big,” Nib chattered.

“It smells sweet,” Dot buzzed.

Ellie smiled. “We can work together. Let’s make crunchy oat bites!”

But there was a problem. The oats were in a tall jar on the top shelf. The honey was sticky and heavy. The dried fruit was in a tight bag. Ellie felt her tummy flutter.

“I’m big,” Ellie said. “I can reach!”

Ellie stretched and stretched. Her feet slid on a crinkly paper bag. Thump! She sat down, surprised. The jars rattled. Pip squeaked. Nib covered his eyes with his tail. Dot held her tiny wings still.

Ellie took a deep breath. “I need to be smart, not just strong,” she said softly. “Kindness means keeping everyone safe.”

Pip peeked out. “We can help,” he said. “I’m small. I can climb a little.”

“I have nimble paws,” Nib added. “I can twist the bag.”

“I can spot things from up high,” Dot buzzed. “I can guide!”

Ellie nodded. “We will use knowledge. We will make a plan.”

They looked around. The pantry had a stool with rubber feet. It did not slide. There was a wooden spoon, a big bowl, and a clean cloth.

Step one, Ellie said: “Safety first.” She placed the stool on a flat spot. She pressed it gently with her foot. “Stable,” she said, smiling.

Step two: “Team roles.” Dot flew up and pointed with a tiny buzz. “The oat jar is near the corner.”

Step three: “Kind choices.” Ellie asked, “Pip, would you like to hold the cloth to keep the jar from slipping?”

“Yes!” Pip said, wrapping the soft cloth around the jar’s side.

Step four: “Compromise.” Nib wanted to open the tight fruit bag all by himself. “I’m fast!” he chattered.

Ellie shook her head kindly. “Let’s share the job,” she said. “You twist. I will hold. That way we are safe.”

Nib paused. He wanted to be the only helper. He looked at Ellie’s warm eyes. “Okay,” he said, nodding. “We can do it together.”

Ellie climbed onto the stool slowly. The wood felt smooth under her feet. She reached with her careful trunk. Dot buzzed, “A little to the left!” Ellie wrapped her trunk around the jar. Pip held the cloth steady. Together, they lifted the jar down. Plop! It landed safe on the counter.

“Teamwork!” Dot cheered.

Next, the honey. The golden honey shone like sunshine in a jar. It smelled like flowers. Ellie tried to twist the lid. Tight! She frowned, then smiled. “Use knowledge,” she whispered. “Warm cloth helps sticky lids.” She dipped the cloth in warm water, wrung it out, and wrapped it around the lid. Twist, twist. Pop! It opened.

“Smart idea!” Pip squeaked.

Now the fruit bag. Nib gripped the corner and twisted. Ellie held the bag steady with her trunk. Crackle, crackle. Open! Sweet smells of apricot and raisins filled the air.

They poured oats into the big bowl. Sprinkle, sprinkle. Pip added tiny fruit pieces. Pat, pat. Dot flew in circles, giggling. Ellie drizzled honey, slow and shiny.

“What about shapes?” Dot asked.

“Let’s craft,” Ellie said. “We can make kind hearts.”

They washed their hands. They rolled the oat mix into little balls. Squish, squish. Then Ellie gently pressed each one into a heart shape with the back of the spoon. Tap, tap. Pip made a tiny star. Nib made a wiggly circle. Dot drew a heart in the air with her flight.

Soon, the tray was full. The pantry smelled cozy and sweet. “We did it,” Ellie said, her voice warm. “We were kind, we used our brains, and we shared jobs.”

But there was one more problem. Pip looked at the tray and frowned. “I want the biggest heart,” he said.

Nib puffed his cheeks. “Me too!”

Ellie placed her trunk softly around them like a hug. “Let’s use kindness and compromise,” she said. “We can each take a turn choosing. We can also cut the biggest heart in half and share.”

Pip and Nib looked at each other. Pip’s whiskers twitched. Nib’s tail flicked. They both nodded.

“Half for you,” Pip said.

“Half for you,” Nib answered.

They giggled and clapped. Dot buzzed a tiny song. Ellie felt warm and proud. They tasted the oat hearts. Crunchy! Chewy! Sweet!

Ellie said, “Today we learned:
- Kindness keeps friends safe and happy.
- Knowledge helps us solve problems.
- Compromise means sharing and taking turns.”

Pip added, “Kind hands, safe steps.”

Nib said, “Smart plans, happy teams.”

Dot sang, “Share, care, be fair!”

Ellie looked around the tidy pantry and smiled. “We made a snack with love, and we made our hearts even bigger.”

The End.
