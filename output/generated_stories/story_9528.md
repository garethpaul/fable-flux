# Splash's Splendid Schedule

Splash the dolphin loved playing in the big white bathtub. Every morning, she would swim in circles and blow bubbles. The warm water felt so good on her smooth, gray skin.

"Time for your morning routine, Splash!" called Mama Dolphin from the doorway. "Remember, we have swimming lessons at the ocean today."

But Splash was having too much fun! She splashed and played with her rubber duck. The yellow duck squeaked when she pushed it under the water. Splash giggled as tiny bubbles tickled her nose.

"Just five more minutes, Mama!" Splash called out. She loved the way her voice echoed in the bathroom.

Mama Dolphin swam closer. "Splash, sweetie, what happens when we don't follow our schedule?"

Splash stopped playing. She remembered last week when she took too long. They were late for swimming lessons, and her friend Finn was sad because they couldn't play together.

"We miss fun things," Splash said quietly. The water around her felt a little cooler now.

"That's right," said Mama. "But I know you're different from other dolphins. You love to play longer than most. That's wonderful! We just need to make a special schedule that works for you."

Splash's eyes lit up like sparkly bubbles. "Really? A special schedule just for me?"

Mama nodded. "Let's think together. What if we wake up ten minutes earlier? Then you can have extra play time AND still be ready on time."

Splash clapped her flippers. The water splashed against the white tile walls. "That sounds perfect! But Mama, what if I forget?"

"Good thinking, Splash! Let's put a special timer shaped like a starfish right here on the edge of the tub. When it makes its gentle chime sound, you'll know it's time to get ready."

Splash swam in a happy circle. "And I can still play with my duck?"

"Of course! Playing is important too. We're just making sure we have time for everything we need to do."

The next morning, Splash heard the starfish timer's soft chime. "Ding-a-ling!" At first, she wanted to keep playing. The bubbles were so pretty, and the water was so warm.

But then she remembered her friend Finn waiting at swimming lessons. She remembered how proud she felt when she was on time.

"Okay, little duck," Splash said to her toy. "Time to get ready! We can play again tonight."

Splash quickly washed behind her fins and brushed her teeth. She even helped Mama gather their swimming gear.

When they arrived at the ocean, Finn was so excited. "Splash! You're here right on time! Want to practice diving together?"

Splash felt warm and happy inside, warmer than even the bath water. "Yes! And guess what? I learned that being different is okay. I just needed to find the right way to manage my time."

As they dove into the deep blue ocean, Splash realized something important. She could be herself - playful and fun-loving - while still being responsible. The best part was that now she had time for everything she loved: playing, learning, and being with friends.

"Thank you, Mama," Splash whispered as they swam together. "My special schedule makes everything better."

And from that day on, Splash always remembered that everyone is different, and that's perfectly wonderful. She just needed to find her own special way to make time for all the important things in her life.

The End.
