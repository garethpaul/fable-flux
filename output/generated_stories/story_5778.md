# Robo's Big Sorry

The nursery glowed with warm yellow light from the sunshine lamp. Baby Emma sat in her high chair, reaching for colorful fruit pieces on her tray. Her robot dog, Robo, whirred quietly beside her with his silver tail wagging.

"Beep beep! Time for healthy snacks!" Robo chirped in his friendly robot voice. His blue LED eyes sparkled as he watched Emma pick up a soft banana slice.

But then - CRASH! Robo's excitement made him spin too fast. His metal paw knocked over Emma's special sippy cup. Apple juice splashed everywhere - on the floor, on Emma's bib, and all over her carefully arranged fruit pieces.

Emma's bottom lip started to wobble. "Oh no!" she said sadly, looking at her soggy snacks.

Robo's LED eyes dimmed. His circuits felt heavy with sadness. "Oh dear, oh dear," he beeped quietly. His tail stopped wagging and drooped down low.

"I made a big mess," Robo said, his voice sounding tinny and sad. "Emma's healthy snacks are all wet now."

Emma looked at her robot friend. Even though she was only little, she could see that Robo felt very sorry. His usually bright lights were dim and his shoulders slumped.

Robo rolled closer to Emma's high chair. He looked up at her with his gentle blue eyes. "Emma, I am very sorry," he said clearly. "I was too excited about your nutritious snacks. I should have been more careful with my spinning."

Emma reached out her small hand and patted Robo's smooth metal head. "It's okay, Robo," she said sweetly.

But Robo wanted to make things right. "I have an idea!" he beeped, his lights getting a little brighter. "Let me help fix this mess I made."

Robo's chest compartment opened with a soft whoosh. Out popped a small cleaning cloth and a tiny spray bottle. "I always carry cleaning supplies to help," he explained proudly.

Carefully, Robo cleaned up every drop of spilled juice. His movements were slow and gentle this time - no more spinning! Then he rolled to the kitchen area of the nursery.

"Look what I found!" Robo announced. From his compartment, he pulled out fresh apple slices, orange wedges, and crackers shaped like animals. "New healthy snacks from around the world! These crackers are made like the ones children eat in faraway places."

Emma clapped her hands with joy. "Pretty!" she giggled, pointing at the colorful food.

Robo arranged everything carefully on Emma's tray. "I learned something important today," he said thoughtfully. "When we make mistakes, we should say sorry right away. Then we should try to make things better."

"Sorry makes it better," Emma repeated, practicing the new words.

"That's right!" Robo's tail wagged again, but gently this time. "And I'm setting a goal - I will always be careful around your food. No more fast spinning near snack time!"

Emma picked up an orange slice and took a tiny bite. The sweet juice made her smile big. "Yummy! Thank you, Robo!"

Robo's LED eyes glowed bright blue again. His circuits hummed happily. "You're welcome, Emma. I feel much better after saying sorry and helping fix my mistake."

The nursery felt cozy and warm again. Emma enjoyed her healthy snacks while Robo sat nearby, his tail wagging slowly and safely. They had both learned that saying sorry and making things right feels much better than staying sad.

"Best friends help each other," Emma said, offering Robo a pretend bite of her apple.

"Beep beep! The very best," Robo agreed happily.

The End.
