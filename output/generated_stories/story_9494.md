# Little Lark’s Gentle Song

In a comfortable nursery, sunlight made golden squares on a soft rug. A small window was open, and a sweet breeze smelled like clean soap and fresh leaves. On the windowsill sat Little Lark, a singing bird with bright eyes and a warm, feathery chest. Little Lark loved to visit every morning and sing hello.

“Good morning!” chirped Little Lark. “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo!”

A child named Mia clapped softly. “Good morning, Little Lark!” she said. Her snug blanket felt cozy. Her teddy bear, Button, seemed to smile.

On a low shelf were baskets for toys, crayons, and paper. A tiny watering can sat by a potted plant with shiny green leaves. The plant’s soil looked a little dry.

Little Lark fluffed her feathers. “Today is a special day,” she sang. “Today, we learn to care. Caring is sharing and saving. It is called sustainability. It means we take care of our world so it stays happy and healthy.”

Mia tilted her head. “How do we do that?” she asked.

Little Lark hopped to the plant and touched a leaf with her little beak. “We help plants. We save water. We reuse and share. My grandma bird taught me this in the morning sun. It is our family tradition,” she chirped proudly.

Mia listened. The room smelled like crayons and warm toast. Outside, a car hummed softly. Inside, the clock made a gentle tick-tock.

“Let’s start with your plant,” said Little Lark. “Touch the soil. Is it dry?”

Mia pressed a finger to the soil. “It feels dry and dusty.”

“We will water it,” said Little Lark, “but only a little. We use just what we need.” She sang a tiny tune, “Sip-sip, not splash-splash.”

Mia poured a small sip from the watering can. The soil turned dark and cool. “The plant looks happy!” Mia smiled.

“Now let’s save light,” chirped Little Lark. “The sun is bright. We can turn off the lamp.” Mia clicked the lamp off. Sunlight danced on the wall, making shapes like boats and bunnies.

“Next,” said Little Lark, “we sort.” She pointed her wing at two baskets. “One for toys to keep. One for toys to share.”

Mia picked up a red truck, a soft ball, and a puzzle piece. She rolled the truck in her hands. Vroom-vroom. She thought.

Little Lark sang gently, “Share what you do not need. Share what others can use.”

Mia nodded. “My friend Leo loves trucks. I can share this truck.” She placed it in the share basket. The soft ball went into keep. The puzzle piece went back to its box so nothing was lost.

“Very wise,” said Little Lark. “Sharing is caring. It helps people and the planet. We do not need to buy more and more. We use what we have and pass it on. That is a good tradition.”

Mia smiled. “A tradition is something we do again and again,” she said, remembering a story from Grandma.

“Yes,” chirped Little Lark. “My family has a song we share each spring. We teach it to younger birds. The song says, ‘Take only what you need. Give back what you can. Leave a nest for another day.’ Would you like to learn it?”

“Yes, please!” Mia whispered.

Little Lark began. Her voice was soft like a pillow and bright like the sun.

“Take what you need,
Give back with care.
Leave a little kindness
So all can share.”

Mia sang along. Their voices mixed like warm cocoa and honey. Button the teddy sat close, listening.

After the song, Little Lark peered into the trash bin. “Oh dear,” she chirped. “Paper and crayons are mixed with peels from your snack.”

Mia frowned. “Is that not okay?”

“It is better to sort,” said Little Lark. “Paper goes here to reuse. Peels go there to make new soil.” She showed Mia two small bins: one for paper to draw on the back, one for food scraps to take to the garden compost later.

Mia pulled out a sheet with a scribble on one side. “I can draw on the other side!” She got a crayon and drew a big green leaf. She sniffed the crayon. It smelled waxy and sweet. Her drawing made her feel calm and proud.

Little Lark nodded. “Reusing keeps trees strong. Compost helps plants grow. This is how we help the earth every day.”

Tap-tap. A gentle sound came from the door. It was Leo! He waved shyly.

Mia ran to the share basket. “Leo, would you like my red truck?” she asked. “I’m sharing!”

Leo’s eyes sparkled. “Thank you!” he said.

Little Lark fluttered. “This is the sharing part of our tradition,” she sang. “We share things, songs, and care.”

Mia and Leo pushed the truck across the rug. Vroom! Vroom! They took turns. They laughed. The room felt full of soft joy.

Before flying away, Little Lark sang the song one more time. Mia and Leo joined in.

“Take what you need,
Give back with care.
Leave a little kindness
So all can share.”

The plant’s leaves shivered happily in the sun. The lamp was off. The papers were sorted. The share basket was lighter, and hearts were lighter, too.

Little Lark winked. “Today we learned to care for our world. We saved water, used sunlight, sorted our things, and shared. This is sustainability. We can do it every day.”

Mia hugged Button and waved. “We will keep the tradition, Little Lark!”

Little Lark lifted into the warm breeze. “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo! See you tomorrow!”

The nursery felt snug and bright. Mia and Leo smiled, proud and peaceful. They knew a small song and small actions could help in a big way.

The End.
