# Ellie's Big Problem

Little Ellie the elephant calf sat on Grandma Elephant's peaceful front porch, swinging her tiny trunk back and forth. The warm sun felt nice on her gray skin, and she could smell the sweet flowers blooming in the garden.

"Grandma," Ellie said with a big sigh, "I have a really big problem."

Grandma Elephant looked up from her rocking chair. "Oh my! What's troubling you, little one?"

"My friends Monkey and Rabbit want to play different games," Ellie explained. "Monkey wants to play tree-swinging, but Rabbit wants to play hop-around. They're both getting upset, and I don't know what to do!"

Ellie's ears flapped sadly as she remembered how her friends had started to argue. "They both looked so mad, Grandma. Their faces got all red, and they crossed their arms like this." Ellie demonstrated by crossing her little arms.

"I see," said Grandma, nodding wisely. "And what did you do when you saw them getting upset?"

"Well," Ellie said, thinking carefully, "first I took three deep breaths like you taught me. In... and out... and in... and out. That helped me feel calmer."

"Very good!" Grandma smiled. "What happened next?"

"Then I used my thinking brain," Ellie said, tapping her head with her trunk. "I asked myself, 'What would make everyone happy?' But I couldn't think of an answer right away."

Grandma's eyes twinkled. "Sometimes the best solutions take time to find. Did you think of any ideas?"

Ellie's trunk wiggled with excitement. "Yes! I thought maybe we could take turns. First play Monkey's game, then Rabbit's game. But Monkey said tree-swinging was too hard for Rabbit, and Rabbit said hopping was too bouncy for Monkey."

"Hmm," said Grandma. "What did your thinking brain tell you to do next?"

"I asked them questions!" Ellie said proudly. "I said, 'What do you both like about your games?' Monkey said he liked moving high up, and Rabbit said she liked moving fast."

"Excellent thinking, Ellie! And then?"

Ellie bounced a little on the porch steps. "Then I had an idea! I remembered when you told me about the old elephant tradition of the 'Happy Dance.' It has high steps like tree-swinging AND fast hops like Rabbit's game!"

"What a wonderful solution!" Grandma clapped her big hands together.

"So I taught them the Happy Dance," Ellie continued. "We held trunks and paws in a circle. We did high steps for Monkey - up, up, up! And quick little hops for Rabbit - hop, hop, hop! Everyone got to do what they liked!"

"How did your friends feel about that?" Grandma asked.

"They loved it!" Ellie giggled. "Monkey said the high steps made him feel like he was in the trees. Rabbit said the quick hops made her feel speedy. And I felt happy because everyone was smiling again!"

Grandma reached over and patted Ellie's head gently. "You solved that problem beautifully, little one. You stayed calm, you listened carefully, and you thought of a creative solution that made everyone happy."

"It felt good to use my thinking brain," Ellie said. "And sharing our family tradition made it even more special!"

As the sun began to set, painting the sky orange and pink, Ellie felt proud and peaceful. She had learned that taking time to think, asking good questions, and being kind could solve even big problems.

The End.
