# Ellie's Exercise Adventure

Little Ellie the elephant calf lived in a sunny backyard with her mama. The warm sunshine felt wonderful on her gray skin, and the soft grass tickled her big feet. But today, Ellie felt sad and tired.

"Mama," Ellie said with a small trumpet sound, "I don't feel happy today. My body feels heavy and slow."

Mama Elephant wrapped her trunk around Ellie gently. "What have you been doing all week, little one?"

Ellie looked down at her feet. "I've been sitting under the big oak tree. I like watching the clouds and eating peanuts. But now I feel... yucky."

"Ah," said Mama with a knowing smile. "Your body is telling you something important, Ellie. It wants to move and play!"

"But moving is hard work," Ellie sighed. "I'd rather just sit."

Mama's eyes twinkled. "What if I told you that moving your body is like giving yourself a special gift? When we exercise, we show respect for our bodies and take good care of ourselves."

Ellie's ears perked up. "A gift? Really?"

"Yes! Let me show you," Mama said. "First, let's start with something fun. Can you swing your trunk in a big circle?"

Ellie giggled and swung her trunk around and around. "Whee! This feels silly!"

"Now try marching in place," Mama encouraged. "Lift those big feet up high!"

Stomp, stomp, stomp! Ellie marched like she was in a parade. The ground shook a little, and she laughed out loud.

"I can feel my heart beating faster, Mama!" Ellie said, touching her chest with her trunk tip.

"That's wonderful! Your heart is getting stronger. Now, let's try some gentle stretches. Reach your trunk up to the sky like you're picking stars!"

Ellie stretched her trunk up, up, up toward the fluffy white clouds. The warm sunshine felt even better on her skin now.

"This feels good!" she said. "Can we do more?"

Mama clapped her big feet together. "Of course! Let's play follow-the-leader around the yard!"

They walked quickly around the flower beds, stepped carefully over the garden hose, and even did a little dance by the bird bath. The birds chirped happily, as if they were cheering for Ellie.

After ten minutes of moving, Ellie stopped and took a deep breath. The fresh air smelled like flowers and grass. Her body felt warm and tingly in the best way.

"Mama, I feel so much better!" Ellie trumpeted joyfully. "My body feels light and happy now!"

"That's because you showed your body respect by giving it exercise," Mama explained. "When we move and play, we take care of ourselves. It's like brushing our teeth or eating healthy food."

Ellie nodded eagerly. "So exercise is self-care?"

"Exactly! And the wonderful feeling you have right now is your body saying 'thank you,'" Mama said with a proud smile.

From that day on, Ellie made sure to move her body every single day. Sometimes she marched, sometimes she stretched, and sometimes she danced. She learned that taking care of herself through exercise wasn't just good for her body—it made her feel happy and proud too.

"Taking care of myself feels amazing!" Ellie would say as she played in the sunny backyard, her trunk swinging with joy.

The End.
