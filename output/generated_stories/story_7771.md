# Brave Bubbles and the Wobbly Toothbrush

In a sunny little house, a determined rabbit named Rumi hopped into the bathroom. The floor tiles were cool and smooth under Rumi’s paws. The mirror was shiny. The light made tiny sparkles on the faucet. Rumi sniffed the sweet smell of bubble soap and heard the plip-plop of a slow, friendly drip.

“Today, I will brush my teeth all by myself,” Rumi said. Rumi’s ears stood tall. “I can do it.”

Rumi picked up a small, soft toothbrush. It felt light and a little slippery. The blue cup clinked softly. Rumi squeezed the toothpaste. SQUISH! A big blob plopped onto the brush and oozed down the side.

“Oh no,” Rumi whispered. The toothpaste smelled minty and cool. It made Rumi’s nose wiggle.

Rumi took a deep breath. “Try again,” Rumi said. “I can be patient.”

Rumi wiped the extra paste with a gentle paw and squeezed a tiny dot—just a pea-sized dot—onto the toothbrush. “Good job, Rumi,” Rumi said, smiling.

Rumi opened wide. “Ahhh!” But when Rumi started brushing, the toothbrush slipped—whoops!—and fell into the sink. CLINK!

Rumi’s heart hopped fast, and the little rabbit felt a lump in the tummy. “I feel wobbly,” Rumi said in a small voice.

Just then, Mama Rabbit peeked in. “I heard a clink. Need a hand, Rumi?” Mama’s voice was warm like a soft towel.

Rumi shook their head. “I want to try. I am brave and patient.”

Mama smiled. “I know you are. I’ll stand right here if you need me.”

Rumi washed the brush with a gentle whoosh. The water felt cool. The sound was like a tiny rain. Rumi tried again. “One… two… slow circles,” Rumi said. “Top teeth, bottom teeth.”

The mint taste tingled. Foam made tiny bubbles. Pop! Pop! They tickled Rumi’s tongue. Rumi giggled. But then—oops!—a bubble popped right into Rumi’s nose. Sniff! Rumi sneezed. “Ah-choo!” The toothbrush wobbled again.

Rumi’s ears drooped a little. “This is hard,” Rumi said.

Mama nodded. “Hard things can take time. Let’s use the three P’s: patience, practice, and a pause.”

Rumi blinked. “What is a pause?”

“A pause is a tiny rest,” Mama said. “Put your paw on your chest, breathe in like you smell a flower, breathe out like you blow a bubble.”

Rumi put a paw on their chest. In… the soap smelled sweet. Out… a long, slow breath. Rumi’s shoulders relaxed.

“I am calm. I can try again,” Rumi said. “I am resilient. I keep going.”

Rumi held the brush with two paws this time. The handle felt firm. “Small circles,” Rumi whispered. “Front teeth, back teeth.” The bristles made a soft swish-swish sound. Rumi counted to five. “One, two, three, four, five.” Then Rumi moved to the next part. “Chew-chew teeth!” Rumi giggled at the silly name and kept brushing.

Suddenly, the bathroom light flickered. The shadows danced. Rumi’s eyes grew wide. “I feel a little scared,” Rumi said.

Mama touched Rumi’s shoulder. “You are brave. Courage means doing the right thing even when you feel a little scared.”

Rumi nodded. “I am brave,” Rumi said softly. “I can finish brushing.”

Rumi kept going—slow, careful circles, top and bottom, front and back. Swish-swish, swish-swish. The bubbles made a fizzy sound, like tiny footsteps on snow. Rumi felt proud. The mirror showed a rabbit with bright eyes and a steady smile.

When Rumi was done, Rumi leaned over the sink and spit. “Ptoo!” The water ran. Whoosh! Rumi rinsed the brush and set it back in the blue cup. “I did it!” Rumi’s ears lifted high again.

Mama clapped softly. “You showed resilience, Rumi. You kept trying, you were patient, and you were brave.”

Rumi patted the sink. “Thank you, sink. Thank you, brush.” Then Rumi looked at Mama. “Thank you for being kind.”

Mama smiled. “Kindness helps us grow.”

Rumi hopped down from the little step stool. The bath mat felt fluffy and warm. The bathroom smelled like mint and soap. The faucet stopped dripping, like it was listening.

Rumi thought about the three P’s. “Patience, practice, and a pause,” Rumi said. “That helps me be resilient.”

Mama nodded. “And when something is hard, we try again, and again, and again, until it gets easier.”

Rumi grinned. “Tomorrow, I will brush again. I will keep going.”

Just then, Baby Squeak, a tiny field mouse friend, peeked in the doorway. “Hi, Rumi! Can I learn too?”

Rumi’s heart felt big and warm. “Yes! I can show you gentle circles,” Rumi said kindly. “We can go slow together.”

Rumi picked up the brush to model small circles in the air. “Swish-swish,” Rumi sang. Baby Squeak copied, giggling. Mama hummed a happy tune.

Rumi whispered, “I am strong inside. When I stumble, I pause. I try again. I keep going.”

The bathroom felt bright and brave. The mirror sparkled. The little drip finally stopped, as if it smiled.

The lesson was clear in Rumi’s heart: Be patient. Be brave. Be kind to yourself. Try again.

And that is how a determined rabbit learned resilience in the bathroom—with bubbles, courage, and lots of love.

The End.
