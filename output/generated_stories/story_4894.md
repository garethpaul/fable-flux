# Miko's Messy Day

Miko the monkey swung into the bright living room. His long tail curled with excitement. Colorful toys covered the soft carpet everywhere. Building blocks, puzzle pieces, and stuffed animals made a big mess.

"Time to play!" Miko cheered. He jumped from the cozy couch to the fluffy rug.

But when Miko tried to walk, he stepped on a hard toy car. "Ouch!" he cried. His foot hurt. He hopped on one leg.

Next, Miko wanted his favorite red ball. He looked under the coffee table. He looked behind the big chair. But he couldn't find it anywhere in the messy room.

"Where is my ball?" Miko scratched his fuzzy head. He felt frustrated.

Then Miko heard a gentle voice. "Good morning, Miko," said Mama Monkey from the doorway. Her voice was warm and kind.

"Mama! I can't find my red ball," Miko said sadly. "And I hurt my foot on this toy car."

Mama Monkey walked carefully through the messy room. Her feet stepped around the scattered toys. "I see the problem, little one. This room needs some organization."

"What's or-gan-i-za-tion?" Miko asked. The big word felt funny on his tongue.

"Organization means putting things in their special places," Mama explained. "Each toy has a home where it belongs."

Miko looked around the messy room. Toys were everywhere! "But there are so many toys, Mama. It looks too hard."

"Let's start small," Mama said gently. "Can you listen to my plan?"

Miko sat on the soft carpet. His ears perked up. He was ready to listen carefully.

"First, let's put all the blocks in the blue box," Mama said. "Then we'll put the puzzle pieces in the yellow box."

Miko nodded. That sounded easy! He picked up three wooden blocks. They felt smooth in his small hands. He dropped them into the blue box with a satisfying *clink, clink, clink*.

"Good job!" Mama cheered. "You're being very helpful."

Together, they worked as a team. Miko put blocks in the blue box. Mama gathered puzzle pieces for the yellow box. The room started looking better already.

"Look, Miko!" Mama pointed under the couch. "I found your red ball!"

"My ball!" Miko jumped with joy. The ball felt bouncy and perfect in his hands. "Thank you for helping me find it!"

They kept organizing together. Stuffed animals went on the toy shelf. Books stood up straight in the bookcase. Soon, the living room looked clean and tidy.

Miko could walk safely across the room now. No toys hurt his feet. He could see his favorite things easily.

"Mama, I feel so happy!" Miko said. He gave her a big, warm hug. "Thank you for teaching me about organization."

"You're very welcome, sweetie," Mama said. "How do you feel about our clean room?"

Miko looked around. Everything had its own special place. "I feel grateful! And proud too! I listened to your plan, and we did it together."

"That's right," Mama smiled. "When we organize our things and listen to good advice, life becomes much easier and more fun."

Miko bounced his red ball happily. The clean room felt peaceful and bright. He had learned something very important today.

From now on, Miko would remember to put his toys away. He would listen when others tried to help him. And he would always feel grateful for the people who cared about him.

The End.
