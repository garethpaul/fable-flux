# Benny Beaver's Big Day

In a cozy nursery filled with soft toys and colorful books, lived a little beaver named Benny. His fur was brown and fluffy, and his flat tail made a gentle *thump-thump* sound when he walked across the wooden floor.

Every morning, Benny's mama would help him get dressed, brush his teeth, and make his breakfast. But today felt different. Today, Benny wanted to try something new.

"Mama," said Benny, tugging gently on her apron, "I want to do things by myself today!"

Mama Beaver smiled warmly. "That sounds wonderful, Benny! What would you like to try first?"

Benny looked around the sunny nursery. The morning light streamed through the curtains, making everything glow golden yellow. "I want to make my own breakfast!" he announced proudly.

"Alright," said Mama, "but remember, if you need help, just ask."

Benny waddled to the little kitchen corner of the nursery. He could smell the fresh wood chips that lined his food bowl. First, he tried to reach the honey jar on the high shelf. *Stretch, stretch* went his little arms, but it was too high.

"Hmm," thought Benny, tapping his chin with his tiny paw. Then he had an idea! He pushed his favorite blue chair over to the counter. *Scrape, scrape* went the chair legs across the floor. Carefully, he climbed up and reached the honey jar.

"I did it!" Benny cheered, his voice echoing softly in the warm nursery.

Next, he wanted to spread honey on his wooden crackers. But the jar was heavy, and honey dripped everywhere - on his paws, on the counter, even on his nose! The sweet smell filled the air.

Benny felt frustrated. His ears drooped down. "This is harder than I thought," he whispered.

Just then, he heard a tiny *squeak, squeak*. His friend Ruby the mouse was stuck under a fallen block tower in the play corner.

"Help!" called Ruby in her small voice.

Even though Benny was busy with his breakfast, he knew Ruby needed him. He wiped his sticky paws on a soft towel and hurried over.

"Don't worry, Ruby!" said Benny. He used his strong beaver teeth to carefully move the blocks. *Click, click* went the wooden blocks as he stacked them neatly. Soon, Ruby was free!

"Thank you, Benny!" squeaked Ruby gratefully. "You're so kind and clever!"

Benny's chest puffed up with pride. He felt warm and happy inside, like sunshine filling his heart.

When he returned to his breakfast, Mama Beaver was there with a gentle smile. "I saw how you helped Ruby," she said. "That was very caring of you. And look how well you solved the problem with the chair!"

Together, they cleaned up the sticky honey. Mama showed Benny how to hold the jar with both paws and pour slowly. The honey made a golden ribbon as it flowed onto his crackers.

"You know what, Benny?" said Mama as they sat down to eat. "Doing things independently doesn't mean doing everything alone. It means trying your best, being creative when you face problems, and knowing when to ask for help or help others."

Benny nodded, crunching his honey crackers. They tasted extra delicious because he had made them himself - with just a little help from Mama.

"Tomorrow, I want to try making lunch!" said Benny, his tail thumping happily against his chair.

"I believe you can do anything you set your mind to," said Mama, giving him a warm hug.

The End.
