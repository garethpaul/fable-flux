# Polly's Big Brave Day

Polly the parrot loved to paint beautiful pictures with her bright feathers. She had red feathers, blue feathers, and yellow feathers that sparkled in the sunlight streaming through the dining room window.

"Good morning, Polly!" called Mama from the kitchen. "Breakfast is ready!"

Polly flapped her wings excitedly and flew to her special perch by the big wooden dining table. But when she looked down at her breakfast bowl, she felt scared. There was something new and green floating in her water.

"What's that?" squawked Polly, her voice shaking a little.

"It's a special cleaning tablet to keep your water fresh and clean," Mama explained gently. "It will help keep you healthy and strong."

Polly's colorful feathers ruffled up. She had never seen anything like this before! The green tablet fizzed and bubbled, making tiny "pop-pop-pop" sounds. It looked scary and different.

"I don't want it!" Polly cried, backing away from her bowl. "It's making funny noises!"

Polly felt frustrated. Her tummy was rumbling because she was hungry, but she was too scared to drink from her bowl. She started to feel hot and upset.

"Take a deep breath with me, Polly," Mama said softly. "In through your beak... and out slowly. When we feel scared, breathing helps us feel calmer."

Polly breathed in deeply. The air smelled like the fresh flowers on the dining room table. She breathed out slowly, feeling her feathers settle down.

"That's better," she whispered to herself.

"Being brave doesn't mean you're not scared," Mama explained. "Being brave means doing something even when you feel a little scared. Would you like to look at the tablet more closely?"

Polly nodded slowly. She hopped closer to her bowl, her tiny claws making soft "click-click" sounds on the table. The green tablet was getting smaller and smaller as it dissolved.

"It's just helping to clean the water, like when you take a nice warm bath," Mama said. "Clean water helps keep your beautiful feathers shiny and your body healthy."

Polly watched the last bit of the tablet disappear. The water looked crystal clear and smelled fresh, like a spring morning after rain.

"I think... I think I can try it now," Polly said quietly.

She dipped her bright orange beak into the water. It tasted just like regular water - cool and refreshing! Her tummy felt much better after drinking.

"I did it!" Polly chirped happily, doing a little dance on her perch. "I was brave!"

"You were very brave indeed," Mama smiled. "You felt scared, you took deep breaths to feel calm, and then you tried something new. That's what courage looks like."

Polly preened her beautiful feathers proudly. She had learned that being brave wasn't about not feeling scared - it was about trying new things even when they seemed different or scary.

From that day on, whenever Polly felt frustrated or scared about something new, she remembered to take deep breaths and be brave. She knew that staying clean and healthy was important, and that trying new things could lead to wonderful discoveries.

"Tomorrow I might even try that new green apple Mama mentioned!" Polly thought to herself, already feeling excited about her next brave adventure.

The End.
