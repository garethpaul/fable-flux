# Tiko Tortoise’s Pantry Parade

Tiko the wise tortoise lived in a cozy house with a bright blue door. He loved the quiet pantry best. It smelled like warm bread and sweet cinnamon. The shelves were full of jars that glowed like little suns and moons when the light peeked in.

Every morning, Tiko visited the pantry. “Good morning, jars,” he would say in his gentle voice. “Good morning, baskets.” He liked to listen to the soft crinkle of paper bags and the tiny clink of spoons.

One sunny day, Tiko heard a rustle, a shuffle, and a squeak. Three little neighbors had slipped under the blue door to share breakfast in the pantry.

Mina the mouse wore a crumb-crumb smile. Pip the pigeon had a shiny feather stuck to his tail. Zig the tiny lizard flicked his tongue like a tick-tock clock.

“Hello, friends,” said Tiko. “Welcome to the pantry. We share here.”

Mina sniffed the air. “I smell rice!” Pip fluffed. “I see seeds!” Zig blinked. “I want a warm spot on that soft flour bag.”

Tiko smiled. “We can all enjoy, but we need to be kind. We wait our turn. We use quiet bodies. We use gentle words.”

They all nodded. But when Tiko opened a little jar of seeds to pass around, the pantry turned busy in a hurry.

“I want the sunflower seeds!” said Pip, grabbing with his beak. The jar tilted. Tap-tap-tap.

“I want the rice first!” squeaked Mina, tugging with both paws. Scritch-scritch.

“I want the warm spot now!” said Zig, wagging his tail as he hopped onto the flour bag. Poof! White dust floated like snow.

Tiko blinked as a cloud of flour puffed over his shell. It felt cool and soft. It smelled like pancakes. But Pip’s wings flapped, Mina’s whiskers trembled, and Zig stamped his tiny feet. Everyone was talking at once.

“I was here first!” Pip cried.

“That’s not fair!” squeaked Mina.

“Move! Move!” chirped Zig.

Tiko felt a little bubble of mad in his tummy. His head felt hot. His heart went thump-thump. He wanted to shout, “Stop it!” But Tiko was wise. He knew big feelings can be loud, and loud feelings can make ears close.

He took a slow breath in. Sniff. He let it out slow. Whoooo. He tucked his head and counted in his mind, “One… two… three.” The bubble of mad got smaller. His heart slowed. He lifted his head.

“Friends,” Tiko said softly. “Let’s pause.”

Everyone froze. Flour snow drifted down. Pip’s shiny feather bobbed. Mina’s nose went twitch-twitch. Zig’s tail made a tiny swirl on the flour bag.

Tiko moved a little sugar bowl with his gentle foot. It made a smooth scrape on the shelf. “We are different,” he said. “Pip likes seeds. Mina likes rice. Zig likes warm. We do not need the same thing at the same time. We can still be kind.”

Pip peered at the jar. “But I really, really want sunflower seeds.”

Mina hugged the rice bag. “I really, really want rice.”

Zig nuzzled the flour bag. “I really, really want warm.”

Tiko nodded. “I hear you. Your wants matter. My wants matter, too. I want quiet and fair turns. Let’s make a plan.”

He used his claw to draw a tiny circle in the flour dust. “We take turns,” he said. “Pip, take three pecks. Then pass. Mina, take three scoops. Then pass. Zig, warm up for three slow breaths. Then share the warm spot. We will all get a turn. That is fair.”

Pip looked at Mina. Mina looked at Zig. Zig looked at Tiko’s kind eyes. They all breathed together. Sniff. Whoooo.

“Okay,” said Pip. “Three pecks.”

“Okay,” said Mina. “Three scoops.”

“Okay,” said Zig. “Three breaths.”

They tried it. Pip counted out loud, “Peck, peck, peck,” and then stepped back with a proud ruffle. Mina scooped, “One, two, three,” and smiled with rice on her whiskers. Zig took “One… two… three” warm breaths and slid a little to make space.

The pantry felt calm again. The flour snow settled. The cinnamon smelled sweet. The tiny clink of spoons sounded like music.

But then, a little crunch sounded from the corner. A shy ant peeked out. “Is there room for me?” the ant whispered. “I am small and slow.”

Tiko’s heart felt big and warm. “Yes,” he said. “There is room when we make room.” He broke a tiny crumb and placed it on the edge of the flour circle. “Welcome.”

Mina scooted to the side. Pip lowered his head so he would not bump wings. Zig tucked his tail so he would not swish flour on the ant. Everyone smiled.

“Thank you for waiting,” Tiko told his friends. “Thank you for listening. Thank you for sharing.”

Pip nodded. “I learned to wait and pass.”

Mina giggled. “I learned to count and share.”

Zig wiggled. “I learned to make space.”

The ant lifted a crumb. “I learned I belong.”

Tiko touched the shelf with his foot. “When we are different, we can still be kind. We can be calm when we feel mad. We can be fair. That is tolerance. That is how friends fit together.”

They finished breakfast with gentle bodies and soft voices. The pantry felt like a warm hug. When they were done, they cleaned up the flour with little sweeps and little pats. The shelves shone again.

As the friends left, Tiko looked around his quiet pantry. It smelled like cinnamon and smiles.

He whispered, “We all fit here.”

The End.
