# Sunny’s Song of Care

In a warm, comfortable nursery, soft sunlight peeked through the curtains. A tiny clock ticked gently. Plush toys rested on a shelf, and a green watering can sat by a sleepy plant. In a cozy nest by the window lived Sunny, a small singing bird with bright yellow feathers and curious eyes.

“Good morning, nursery,” chirped Sunny. “Tweet-a-dee! Time to start the day.”

Sunny loved to sing. His voice sounded like a silver bell. When he sang, the air felt sparkly and sweet, like warm toast with honey. But today, Sunny had a big plan. He wanted to be responsible. He wanted to help.

“I can take care of things,” Sunny told himself. “I can be gentle. I can be kind. I can keep my promise.”

He fluttered to the shelf and saw the plant. Its leaves whispered, “Thirsty.” The soil looked dry and crumbly like cookie crumbs.

Sunny nodded. “I will water you,” he said. “Just enough. Not too much.” He picked up the little green watering can with his beak. Drip, drip, drip. The water made soft music as it touched the soil. The plant sighed, “Ahh,” and lifted its leaves toward the light.

Sunny smiled. “Caring for you is my job.”

He hopped to the toy basket. Blocks were on the floor, and crayons rolled under a chair. “Oh dear,” Sunny chirped. “We must keep our home tidy. We can share the space.”

He pushed a red block back into the basket with his wing. Thunk. He rolled a blue crayon to its box. Roll, roll, click. It felt good to put things away. “I can be responsible,” he sang softly. “I can clean up as I play.”

From the window came a soft rustle. A tiny spider tried to cross the sill. Sunny watched. The spider was small and shy. Sunny remembered a gentle rule: be kind to small friends.

“Hello, little spider,” Sunny said. “This is your home too. Let’s keep each other safe.”

He opened the small vent a little to guide the spider toward a cozy corner plant. “There you go,” Sunny chirped. “No squishing, no fear. Peaceful solutions live here.”

Sunny’s tummy rumbled. “Time for a seed snack,” he giggled. He pecked a few seeds from his bowl. Crunch, crunch. He drank a sip of water. Sip. He fluffed his feathers and breathed in. He breathed out. “Taking care of me helps me care for others,” he said. “That’s self-care.”

Just then, Sunny heard a faint drip. Plip. Plop. He looked around. A cup by the sink was dripping water into the basin. Plip. Plop.

“Water is precious,” Sunny whispered. He flew to the faucet, nudged the handle with his beak, and turned it off. The dripping stopped. “We save water to help our planet,” he sang. “Con-ser-va-tion!” The nursery felt proud and quiet.

Sunny saw a soft blanket on the floor. It was his night blanket. “Oh!” he chirped. “I forgot to fold you.” He shook it gently, folded it small, and placed it by his nest. Pat, pat. It looked neat and calm.

Knock, knock. A plush bear leaned on the pillow and tipped over. “Oh no!” Sunny laughed. He stood the bear back up. “You like to sit tall, friend.” He patted the bear’s fuzzy head. “I accept you just as you are. Fuzzy, quiet, and kind.”

As the day went on, Sunny sang little songs while he worked.

“Put it away,
right where it stays,
so we can find it
on happy days!”

He listened to the room. The clock said tick-tock. The curtains whispered shhh. The plant hummed green and glad.

But then, Sunny felt a little tired. His wings drooped. His song grew soft. “I did many jobs,” he yawned. “I took care. I cleaned up. I saved water. I helped a small friend. I ate and I rested. Now I will have quiet time.”

He snuggled into his nest and closed his eyes for a minute. The nest smelled like straw and sunshine. After his rest, he felt bright again.

A breeze touched the chimes by the window. Tinkle, tinkle. Sunny hopped out and looked around the nursery. It was tidy, safe, and calm. The plant’s leaves were shiny. The blocks were in the basket. The blanket was folded. The faucet was off. The spider was safe.

Sunny puffed his chest and sang a happy song:

“When I take care of my space,
I make a gentle, happy place.
When I save water and I share,
I show that I know how to care.”

He looked at the plush bear and the sleepy plant. “I learned something today,” Sunny said. “Being responsible means I do my part. I clean up my things. I help small friends. I save water. I take care of me. I choose peaceful ways. That makes our nursery feel good for everyone.”

The plant rustled. The bear smiled in his quiet, soft way. The tiny spider waved a leg.

Sunny’s heart felt warm. He chirped, “I can do it again tomorrow. Little jobs, little songs, big care.”

The nursery glowed in the late light. Sunny tucked one feather into place and hummed a sweet good-night tune for later. For now, it was still day, and the room was bright and full of love.

The End.
