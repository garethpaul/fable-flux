# Ollie Otter's Messy Art Day

Ollie the otter loved to play! He splashed in the water, rolled on the soft grass, and especially loved making art. Today, Ollie was so excited because his teacher, Miss Rabbit, had set up a special art room with bright paints, fluffy brushes, and sparkly glitter.

"Good morning, Ollie!" said Miss Rabbit with a warm smile. "Today we're making pictures for the school sports day celebration. Remember to clean up after yourself, okay?"

"Yes, Miss Rabbit!" Ollie said, but he was already eyeing the rainbow of paint colors. Red like strawberries, blue like the ocean, yellow like sunshine!

Ollie dipped his brush into the red paint. *Swoosh, swoosh!* He painted a big circle. Then he grabbed the blue paint without washing his brush. "Oops!" The colors mixed into a muddy brown.

"That's okay," Ollie giggled. "I'll just use more colors!"

Soon, Ollie was having so much fun that he forgot all about being careful. Paint dripped on the floor - *drip, drop, drip!* His paws were covered in purple and green. Glitter stuck to his whiskers, making them sparkle and tickle.

"Look at my masterpiece!" Ollie called out, holding up his painting. But when he turned around, he saw something that made his tummy feel funny. The art table was covered in paint splatters. Brushes lay dirty in the wrong cups. Glitter was sprinkled everywhere like tiny stars.

Little Bunny was trying to paint at the next table, but there were no clean brushes left. "I can't make my picture," she said sadly, her bottom lip wobbling.

Ollie's ears drooped. He felt bad seeing his friend upset. "Oh no," he whispered. "I made such a big mess."

Miss Rabbit walked over with gentle eyes. "Ollie, what do you think happened here?"

"I was having so much fun that I forgot to be responsible," Ollie said, his voice small. "I didn't clean up as I went, and now Bunny can't paint."

"That's right," Miss Rabbit nodded. "But you know what? We can fix this together. What could you do to help?"

Ollie thought hard, his little otter brain working. Then his eyes lit up! "I can clean everything and share my supplies with Bunny!"

"That's a wonderful idea!" Miss Rabbit smiled.

Ollie got to work right away. He wiped the table with a soft cloth - *wipe, wipe, wipe!* He washed all the brushes until they were squeaky clean. He carefully swept up the glitter with a tiny broom. The art room started to smell fresh and clean again.

"Here, Bunny," Ollie said, offering his friend a clean brush and his favorite blue paint. "Would you like to paint together? We could make pictures of our favorite sports!"

Bunny's face lit up like sunshine. "Yes! I want to paint myself playing soccer!"

As they painted side by side, Ollie felt warm and proud inside. Being responsible felt even better than making a mess! They painted soccer balls, tennis rackets, and swimming pools. Their pictures were beautiful.

When art time was over, Ollie made sure to clean up properly. He put every brush away, wiped his table, and even helped other friends clean theirs.

"Thank you for being so responsible, Ollie," Miss Rabbit said. "You turned a messy situation into something wonderful by caring about others and fixing your mistake."

Ollie's chest puffed up with pride. He learned that being responsible meant thinking about others and cleaning up after himself. And the best part? He still had just as much fun!

The End.
