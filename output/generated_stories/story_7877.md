# Tilly Tortoise and the Music of Everyone

In a bright musical practice room, soft lights twinkled like tiny stars. Colorful drums, shiny bells, and a big piano lined the walls. The room smelled like fresh wood and lemon polish. Tap, tap, tap went little feet on the floor. Ding, ding, ding sang the bells.

In the middle of the room sat Tilly, a wise tortoise with a smooth, green shell. Tilly wore a small blue scarf and a warm, gentle smile. She moved slowly, but she listened fast. “Hello, friends,” Tilly said in a calm voice. “Today, we make music together.”

Around Tilly, children gathered in a circle. Mia clapped softly. Ben hummed a tune. Zara tapped a tiny drum. Kai jingled bells. A new friend, Leo, stood near the door. He squeezed his hands and looked at the floor.

Tilly noticed. She slowly scooted closer. “Hi, Leo,” she said. “I am Tilly. Would you like to make music with us?”

Leo shook his head. “I don’t know all the words,” he whispered. “My home words are different.”

Tilly nodded kindly. “Different is okay,” she said. “Different is special. We can use many ways to make music. Words, claps, taps, signs, hums—every voice counts.”

Mia smiled. “We want Leo to play!”

Ben nodded. “But we always sing the same song,” he said. “What if Leo can’t sing it?”

Tilly looked around the warm room. The piano sat quiet, waiting. The drum skins felt smooth under small hands. The air held a soft, hopeful hush. “Hmm,” said Tilly. “We have a little problem. Our song uses only one way—the words we know. That is not fair.”

Zara tilted her head. “What can we do?”

“Let’s make an Everyone Song,” Tilly said. “A song that welcomes all voices and all ways.”

“How?” asked Kai, shaking his bells. Jing-a-ling!

Tilly closed her eyes and listened. “First, we listen to each other. Leo, what sound feels good to you?”

Leo thought. He hummed a low, cozy sound. “Mmm-mmm,” he went, a gentle buzz like a sleepy bee.

“Beautiful,” said Tilly. “Let’s all try it.” The room filled with soft humming. Mmm-mmm. The floor hummed too. The drums hummed when Zara rubbed them with her palms. The windows hummed a tiny echo.

Ben grinned. “My turn! I like clap-clap.” Clap clap!

“Great,” said Tilly. “That’s our beat.” Everyone clapped gently—clap clap, clap clap. The sound felt like happy footsteps.

Mia waved. “I can sing La-la,” she said, “and I can show it with my hands too.” Mia moved her hands in the air, like waves.

Tilly nodded. “Singing and signing—two ways together.” She copied Mia’s gentle hands with her small tortoise feet.

Zara tapped her drum—tap tap poom. Kai added bells—jing-a-ling! The room became a friendly rainbow of sounds. It felt warm and bright, like sunshine you could hear.

But Ben still looked unsure. “What about the words of our old song?” he asked. “I like them.”

Tilly smiled. “We can keep some words, and we can make space for new ones. That is inclusion. We make room for everyone.”

Leo lifted his head. His eyes sparkled. “I can say hola,” he said softly. “That is hello in my home words.”

“Hola!” sang Mia, and she signed hello with her hand. Everyone tried: “Hola!” The word felt smooth and kind on their tongues.

Tilly tapped her shell gently. “Listen to our plan,” she said. “We start with humming. Then we clap. Then we add hola. Then we sing la-la. Then we tap and ring. Everyone can join in any part. You can sing. You can hum. You can sign. You can play. You can rest and listen. All are welcome.”

They began. Mmm-mmm. Clap clap. “Hola!” La-la-la. Tap tap poom. Jing-a-ling!

Tilly led slowly so no one felt rushed. She watched faces. She listened for shy sounds. When a child paused, she smiled and waited. When a child tried a new sound, she nodded. Everyone had space. Everyone belonged.

Soon, the song grew big and happy. Leo’s humming rose strong and proud. He said “hola” again and again, and each time he felt lighter. Ben kept the steady clap. Mia sang and signed. Zara’s drum made the floor feel like a friendly heartbeat. Kai’s bells twinkled like stars.

Then the door opened. Two more children peeked in. “Can we play?” they asked.

Tilly’s eyes shone. “Yes,” she said. “There is room for you. Listen first. Then join in the part you like.”

One child stomped softly—step step—making a new sound. Another whispered “bonjour,” a new hello. Tilly smiled wider. “We are learning language appreciation,” she said gently. “Different words, same welcome.”

They played until the sounds faded like a warm hug. The room felt calm and fair. No one was left out. Each child had a choice—what to play, when to rest, how to join. That was independence. That was inclusion.

Tilly gathered everyone close. “What did we learn?” she asked.

Leo raised his hand. “We can make space,” he said. “We can say hello in many ways.”

Ben said, “We can keep old parts and add new parts.”

Mia added, “We can sing and sign so more friends can join.”

Tilly nodded. “Yes. Inclusion means we make room for everyone. Fairness means we share and listen. Independence means you choose your way to join. When we do this, our music is strong and kind.”

They ended with one soft hum together. Mmmmmm. The sound felt like a smile.

“Thank you,” said Tilly. “Our Everyone Song is for all of us.”

The End.
