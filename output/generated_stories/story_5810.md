# Ollie Otter's Tidy Time

Ollie the otter loved to play in Grandpa's garden shed. The wooden shed smelled like fresh dirt and old tools. Sunlight peeked through the dusty windows, making everything sparkle.

"Wow!" squeaked Ollie, looking around with his bright brown eyes. "So many fun things to explore!"

The shed was full of wonderful treasures. There were shiny garden tools, colorful flower pots, bags of soft soil, and boxes of seeds. Ollie's whiskers twitched with excitement.

"I want to play with everything!" he chirped, diving into a pile of gardening gloves.

Ollie grabbed a big watering can. *Clang!* It fell over, spilling water everywhere. Then he knocked over a stack of flower pots. *Crash! Bang!* They rolled across the floor, making a terrible noise.

"Oops!" giggled Ollie, but he kept playing.

He opened bags of soil and dug his paws deep inside. The dirt felt cool and crumbly between his toes. Then he scattered seeds everywhere, watching them bounce and roll like tiny marbles.

Soon, the neat and tidy shed looked like a tornado had visited. Tools were scattered, soil covered the floor, and seeds were stuck in spider webs.

Just then, Grandpa opened the shed door. His kind eyes grew very wide.

"Oh my!" said Grandpa gently. "What happened here, little Ollie?"

Ollie's tail drooped. "I wanted to play with everything at once," he whispered. "But now it's all messy, and I can't find anything!"

Grandpa sat down on an overturned bucket. "Would you like to learn a special secret about having fun AND keeping things tidy?"

Ollie nodded eagerly, his whiskers perking up.

"It's called 'one thing at a time,'" explained Grandpa. "When we play with one thing, put it back, then choose the next thing, we can have more fun! Plus, we can always find what we need."

"Really?" asked Ollie, tilting his furry head.

"Let's try it together," smiled Grandpa. "First, let's put all the tools back on their hooks. Can you help me?"

Ollie picked up a small trowel. It felt smooth and cool in his paws. He carefully hung it on its special hook. "Like this?"

"Perfect!" cheered Grandpa. "Now the flower pots."

One by one, they stacked the pots neatly. Ollie loved hearing the gentle *clink* as each pot found its place.

Next, they swept up the soil with a soft brush. The bristles tickled Ollie's paws as he helped push the dirt into a dustpan.

"Now for the seeds," said Grandpa, handing Ollie a small container.

They carefully collected each tiny seed. Ollie discovered that going slowly helped him see better and feel prouder of his work.

When they finished, the shed looked beautiful again. Everything had its own special place, and the afternoon sun made the clean tools shine like jewels.

"Wow!" gasped Ollie. "It looks even better than before! And it was fun to clean up together!"

Grandpa patted Ollie's head gently. "When we take our time and do one thing at a time, everything works better. Plus, now you know where everything belongs for next time!"

From that day on, Ollie always remembered Grandpa's special secret. He would choose one toy, play with it carefully, put it back, then choose another. His games became even more fun because he could always find exactly what he needed.

"One thing at a time makes everything shine!" Ollie would sing happily.

The End.
