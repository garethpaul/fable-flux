# Ellie's Clean and Happy Day

In a sunny backyard filled with colorful flowers and buzzing bees, lived a gentle elephant calf named Ellie. She had soft gray skin, big brown eyes, and a trunk that wiggled when she was excited.

One bright morning, Ellie woke up feeling sleepy and grumpy. The warm sun tickled her back, and she could smell the sweet flowers blooming nearby. But instead of getting up to wash her face and brush her teeth like Mama Elephant had taught her, Ellie rolled over and decided to play right away.

"Good morning, Ellie!" chirped her friend Ruby the robin from a nearby tree. "Want to play together?"

Ellie trumpeted softly, "Yes! Let's play hide and seek!"

But as Ellie ran around the backyard, she noticed something strange. Ruby flew away quickly. Then Benny the bunny hopped past without stopping to say hello. Even the friendly butterflies seemed to flutter away from her.

Ellie felt sad and confused. Her trunk drooped down, and her big eyes filled with tears. "Why doesn't anyone want to play with me today?" she wondered aloud.

Just then, Mama Elephant walked into the backyard. She had been watching from the kitchen window and saw how upset Ellie looked.

"Oh my sweet little one," Mama said gently, wrapping her trunk around Ellie in a warm hug. "I think I know what might be wrong."

"What is it, Mama?" Ellie sniffled.

Mama Elephant smiled kindly. "Well, darling, when we don't wash our faces and brush our teeth in the morning, we might not smell very fresh. Our friends might not want to come close because it's not pleasant for them."

Ellie touched her trunk to her mouth and realized Mama was right. She could taste that her mouth felt yucky, and she hadn't washed the sleep from her eyes.

"I forgot to clean up this morning," Ellie said quietly. "I was too excited to play."

"That's okay, sweetheart," Mama said with compassion. "We all forget sometimes. The important thing is that we learn and try to remember next time. Taking care of ourselves shows respect for our friends too."

Together, they walked to the outdoor washing station that Papa Elephant had built. Ellie splashed cool, refreshing water on her face with her trunk. The water felt so good and made her skin tingle. Then she brushed her teeth with her special elephant toothbrush until they sparkled white and clean.

"How do I smell now, Mama?" Ellie asked, feeling much better.

Mama sniffed gently. "You smell fresh as a daisy! Now you're ready to play with your friends."

Ellie trumpeted happily and ran back into the sunny backyard. This time, Ruby the robin flew down immediately.

"Oh Ellie, you smell so nice and clean!" Ruby chirped. "Now I'd love to play with you!"

Soon, Benny the bunny hopped over, and the butterflies danced around Ellie's clean, sweet-smelling trunk. They all played together until the sun began to set, laughing and having the most wonderful time.

That evening, Ellie helped Mama write in her special journal: "Today I learned that keeping clean helps me feel good and shows my friends that I care about them too."

From that day forward, Ellie always remembered to wash her face and brush her teeth first thing every morning. She discovered that taking care of herself made her feel confident, happy, and ready for fun adventures with all her friends.

The End.
