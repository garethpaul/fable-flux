# Benny Beaver’s Quiet Guest Room Art

Benny was a busy beaver with soft brown fur and a wide, flat tail. One sunny afternoon, Benny visited his aunt’s house. She smiled and said, “Benny, you can play in the guest room. Please keep it neat and quiet. Grandma is napping.”

Benny waddled into the guest room. It smelled like clean soap and warm blankets. The curtains were silky and blue. The carpet felt squishy under his webbed feet. On the bedside table sat crayons, paper, and a little sign that said, “Art Corner.”

Benny’s eyes sparkled. “I love art!” he whispered. He opened the crayon box. Snap! The lid made a tiny sound. Benny smiled. “I will draw a river and a dam!” He started to scribble gently. Swish, swish went the crayons on paper. He hummed softly, “Do-do-do,” trying to stay quiet.

Soon Benny had an idea—a big idea. “I want to make a huge dam picture on the wall!” he gasped. His tail thumped. Thump! He looked at the wall. It was clean and white like a cloud.

Benny picked up a crayon. He reached toward the wall.

Just then, Aunt Maple peeked in. “Oh! Benny,” she said in a calm voice, “walls are not for drawing.”

Benny froze. His ears drooped. “I’m sorry,” he whispered. “I just wanted to make a big picture.”

Aunt Maple smiled gently. “I see your big idea. Art is wonderful. But drawing on walls can be messy and hard to clean. It is not safe for our house. Let’s find a peaceful solution.”

Benny blinked. “A peaceful solution?”

“Yes,” said Aunt Maple, sitting beside him. “We talk. We listen. We make a plan that is kind and safe.”

Benny nodded. “Okay. I want to make a big picture, but I don’t want to make a mess.”

Aunt Maple looked around. She rubbed the silky curtain between her fingers. “Hmm. The wall is not for drawing. But we can use big paper.”

She opened the closet. Rustle, rustle. She pulled out a giant roll of paper. It smelled like new books. She also brought out blue painter’s tape and a soft plastic mat.

Aunt Maple spread the mat on the floor. “This keeps the carpet safe.” She taped the big paper to the mat. Rip, tap, tap went the tape. “Now you can draw big, and the room stays neat. And we stay quiet for Grandma.”

Benny’s tail swished happily. “That is a great plan!” he said. “Thank you.”

Aunt Maple handed him some crayons and two fat markers that made gentle, quiet lines. “Try these. Soft strokes, please.”

Benny drew rivers and trees. He drew a dam with little sticks and smooth stones. He added fish with tiny smiles. He whispered, “Hello, fish,” as he colored them orange and yellow. The paper felt cool and smooth under his paws. The crayons smelled a little like sweet wax.

Knock, knock. Benny looked up. His cousin, Dot, peeked in. “Can I draw too?” she asked.

Benny said, “Yes! We can share. We can make a plan.”

Dot’s eyes shone. “What plan?”

Benny smiled proudly. “We draw on the big paper. We sit on the mat. We use quiet voices. We take turns. That keeps the guest room safe and calm.”

Dot nodded. “Peaceful plan!” She picked a purple crayon and drew a bright butterfly. “Flutter-flutter,” she whispered.

They drew together. When Dot needed the green, Benny said, “Here you go,” and handed it over. When Benny wanted the purple, Dot said, “Your turn now.” They giggled softly, like tiny bells. Tinkle, tinkle.

Soon their picture grew and grew. It showed a happy forest, a cool blue river, a strong dam, and a sky full of butterflies and puffy clouds. Aunt Maple brought a little timer. “Let’s set the timer for quiet art time,” she said. “When it dings, we clean up together.”

The timer ticked softly. Tick, tick, tick. Benny felt calm. His body was busy, and his heart was peaceful. He liked the plan. He liked the rules. He liked the kind sharing.

Ding! The timer rang like a tiny bell. Benny and Dot stopped. “Clean-up time,” Aunt Maple said.

They put crayons in the box. Click. They rolled up the big paper. Swish. Aunt Maple wrote, “Benny and Dot’s Guest Room Art” on the bottom. She pinned it to a portable board, not the wall. “We can show Grandma when she wakes,” she said with a wink.

Benny’s chest felt warm and proud. “I learned something,” he said. “When I have a big idea, I can talk, listen, and make a plan. That is a peaceful solution.”

Aunt Maple hugged him. “Yes. Peaceful choices keep our home safe. Art can be big and bright, and we can still be careful and kind.”

Grandma slowly opened the door with a soft smile. “What a lovely quiet I heard,” she said. “And what a lovely picture I see!”

Benny and Dot beamed. They showed Grandma their art. Grandma clapped gently. “Beautiful work, little artists.”

Benny whispered, “Thank you. We used a peaceful plan.”

The guest room felt cozy and calm. The air smelled like crayons and clean blankets. Benny’s tail gave one happy swish.

The End.
