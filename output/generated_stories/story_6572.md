# Pebble the Penguin’s Crafty Helper Day

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a wiggly waddle. One sunny morning, Pebble slid into the dining room, where a big wooden table waited. The table felt smooth and cool under Pebble’s flippers. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. The room smelled like warm toast and sweet fruit.

“Good morning, table!” Pebble chirped. “Today is craft day!”

Pebble set down a small box. It rattled and jingled. Inside were paper shapes, safe glue sticks, soft yarn, buttons big as grapes, and shiny stickers. Pebble’s plan was to make a Kindness Crown to wear at snack time.

Pebble spread out a sheet of blue paper. “I will make a crown that reminds me to help,” Pebble said. “It will sparkle with good choices!”

Just then, Pebble’s friend, a stuffed seal named Squeaky, slid from a chair and plopped onto the floor. “Oh dear!” said Pebble, picking up Squeaky and giving a gentle hug. “I will help you sit tall.”

Pebble looked back at the table. The paper had a tiny tear. Pebble blinked. “Did I pull too hard?” Pebble asked. Pebble took a deep breath. “It’s okay. I can fix it. I will think first.”

Pebble tapped a flipper on the table. Tap, tap, tap. “A small tear needs gentle tape,” Pebble decided. Pebble got a short piece of tape and smoothed the paper. “There,” Pebble said with a smile. “When things rip, we fix them with care.”

Pebble measured the paper around the head. “Too big,” Pebble giggled as the paper slid over the eyes. “I can trim it just a little.” Pebble used safe, round-tip scissors and snipped, snip, snip. The sound was soft, like tiny bird steps.

“Now for decorations!” Pebble opened the box. Pebble reached for the shiny star stickers but paused. Pebble saw a small, neat note inside the box. It said, “These special stars are for sharing. Use only what you need.”

Pebble’s eyes grew wide. Pebble wanted many, many stars. But Pebble read the note again. “Use only what you need,” Pebble said softly. “I will be honest. I will take just enough.”

Pebble counted out three stars. “One for helping. One for telling the truth. One for thinking carefully.” Pebble pressed them onto the crown. The stickers felt smooth and cool. They shimmered in the sunlight.

Pebble found a little pot of safe glue next. “I will add buttons!” Pebble cheered. Pebble dabbed glue carefully. “Dot, dot, not a lot,” Pebble sang. The glue smelled a tiny bit sweet and made a soft squish when Pebble pressed down the buttons. Pebble added a strand of soft yarn like a rainbow line.

As Pebble worked, a small voice seemed to whisper from the doorway. It was Pebble’s cousin, Pip, who waddled in with curious eyes. “Wow, Pebble! Can I see?” Pip asked.

“Yes, come sit with me,” said Pebble. “We can craft together.”

Pip picked up a sticker sheet that was not theirs. Pip looked at Pebble. “Can I use this? It was on the chair.”

Pebble shook their head gently. “Let’s ask first. These might belong to Mama.”

They both called, “Mama?” Mama Penguin waddled in with a warm smile. “Are you two crafters?” she asked.

“Yes!” Pebble said. “But we found these stickers. Are they yours?”

Mama nodded. “They are for the family craft box. Thank you for asking. I am proud of your honesty. You may use a few.”

Pebble and Pip grinned. They picked two stickers each. “We are using just what we need,” Pip said.

As they crafted, a button rolled off the table. Clink! It fell under a chair. Pip reached fast. “I can get it!” Pip said. But the button slid farther away. Pebble stopped and thought. “Let’s try a new way. We can use this long spoon to gently slide it back.”

Pebble held the spoon, Pip watched carefully, and together they nudged the button out. “Teamwork!” Pip cheered. “You thought of a safe tool.”

Pebble smiled. “Thinking before doing helps us solve problems.”

Soon, the crowns were almost done. Pebble tried on the Kindness Crown. It fit just right. It felt light and cozy. Pip clapped. “You look so kind and shiny!”

Pebble looked at Pip’s crown. “Yours is lovely and bright. I see happy shapes.” Pebble gave a proud nod.

“Snack time!” called Mama. The dining room smelled like fresh apples and cinnamon. Pebble and Pip wore their crowns and munched crunchy slices. “Thank you for the snack, Mama,” they said together.

Mama beamed. “Thank you for crafting with care. You used gentle hands, asked before taking, and helped each other. That is integrity and kindness.”

Pebble wiggled with joy. “Today I learned to slow down, use just what I need, tell the truth, and help my friend. Crafting teaches my heart.”

Pip tapped the crown. “And thinking helps us fix little problems.”

Pebble hugged Pip. The crowns twinkled. The dining room felt warm and safe, like a big hug too.

Pebble whispered, “When we craft with care, our choices shine.”

The End.
