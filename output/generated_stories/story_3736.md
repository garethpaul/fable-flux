# Pip’s Tidy Tune

In a bright little study corner by the window, a singing bird named Pip loved to practice songs. The corner smelled like fresh crayons and clean paper. The desk felt smooth, and the sun made warm, golden squares on the floor. Pip’s feathers were blue and soft, and his voice was sweet like honey tea.

Pip chirped, “La-la-la! Today I sing a new song!” He hopped to the desk. But—oh dear! The desk was messy. Crayons rolled everywhere. Papers were crinkled. A tiny whistle and a small soft ball were buried under a pile of books.

Pip puffed his cheeks. “Where is my music page?” he asked. He flipped a paper. Crunch. He stepped on a crayon. Snap.

From the hallway, Coach Cat called, “Team time soon! We practice gentle throws!”

Pip liked sports time. He loved to toss the small soft ball into a little basket. It made a happy thump. But first he wanted to sing. “I just need my music page,” Pip said.

Mouse Mina peeked in. “Pip, I heard a snap. Are you okay?”

Pip nodded. “I can’t find my music page.”

Mina sniffed. “I smell crayons. I smell dust. I hear rolling, rolling.” A crayon rolled off the desk. Plink. “Maybe we need to tidy.”

Pip shook his head. “No time! I will look faster.” He flapped and fluttered. Papers flew. The whistle fell. Tweet! The soft ball bounced. Boing, boing, boing!

Rabbit Rio hopped in with long ears high. “I heard boing! What’s up?”

“I lost my music page,” Pip said. “I want to sing before sports.”

Rio looked around. “Hmm. It is hard to find things in a mess.”

Pip felt a pinch in his tummy. He wanted to sing. He wanted to play. He did not want to clean. He chirped louder. “La-la—”

“Pip,” Mina said softly, “can you listen for a moment?”

Pip stopped and listened. He heard the window breeze shhh. He heard his own heart thump-thump. He heard his friends breathe. He nodded. “I am listening.”

Mina smiled. “Let’s make a plan. Then it will be fair and fast.”

“Fair?” Rio asked.

“Yes,” Mina said. “When the study corner is messy, it is not fair. Pip loses time. We all wait. It feels mixed-up. If we clean together, we all get time to sing and play. That is justice.”

Pip blinked. “Justice means fair,” he said.

“Right,” Rio said. “We can understand each other and help.”

Pip took a deep breath. He smelled the dusty crayons. He said, “Okay. Plan?”

Mina held up three fingers. “One: Put papers in a folder. Two: Put crayons in a cup. Three: Put sports things in a bin.”

Pip sang a tiny note. “I can do that!”

They started. Pip flapped gently to lift papers. Swish. He smoothed them. Smoooooth. He put them in a red folder. “Red means ready,” he said.

Rio picked up crayons. Clink, clink, clink into a blue cup. “Blue for bright ideas.”

Mina rolled the soft ball and placed it in a green bin with the little whistle. Thump. Tweet. “Green for go to sports!”

Pip’s wings felt lighter. He smiled. The desk looked clean. The sun made the papers glow. He could smell the wood of the desk now, warm and nice.

“Music page, where are you?” Pip whispered.

The red folder sat flat and neat. Pip opened it. There, right on top, lay the music page with a big star. “Found you!” he sang.

Coach Cat peeked in again. “Team time in five minutes!”

Pip chirped, “I can sing now! Then I can play!” He stood tall on the clean desk. He took a deep, deep breath. He listened to the quiet. Then he sang a clear, sweet tune.

“La-la-la, tidy and true,
Folders red and cups so blue.
Green bin waits for balls and play,
Clean and ready makes our day!”

Mina swayed. Rio tapped a gentle beat on the desk—tap, tap, tap. The song felt smooth and happy.

Coach Cat clapped. “Great listening, team! Great organizing too. After sports, we put things back, yes?”

“Yes!” the friends called.

In the gym corner, Pip tossed the soft ball. Thump—into the little basket! Rio cheered. Mina blew the tiny whistle—tweet! They took turns, they waited, they listened. It felt fair. It felt fun.

After play, they went back to the study corner. Pip said, “Organize time!” He put the ball in the green bin. Thump. Rio put the crayons in the blue cup. Clink. Mina put the music page in the red folder. Smooth.

Pip’s heart felt full. “I understand now,” he said. “When I am organized, I can find things. I can listen better. I have time for songs and sports. It is fair for me and fair for friends.”

Mina nodded. “Being tidy helps everyone.”

Rio grinned. “And it makes play time even more fun.”

Pip sang a soft, cozy ending note. The study corner looked bright and calm. The air smelled clean. The sun warmed their backs.

Pip whispered, “Tidy today, happy play.”

The End.
