# Hoot's Little Wellness Plan

In a quiet attic, where dust danced in sunbeams and old boxes sighed, a wise old owl named Hoot lived in a cozy nest on a wooden beam. The attic smelled like pine and cinnamon. The floor creaked softly, like a whisper. Hoot liked the hush and the glow. He liked to think.

One morning, Hoot blinked his big golden eyes and stretched his wings. “Hoo-hoo,” he yawned. His tummy rumbled, and his feathers felt a little droopy.

“I feel slow today,” Hoot said to himself. “My wings feel heavy. My head feels foggy. Why?” He tilted his head and listened to the quiet. The attic clock ticked. The wind tapped the window. Tick-tock. Tap-tap.

Hoot remembered last night. He had stayed up too late learning a new whistle. “Hoo, too long,” he sighed. He had also forgotten to drink water. He had also not moved much. He had sat and sat and sat.

“This is a problem,” Hoot said. “But I am a wise owl. I can make a plan.”

He hopped to the edge of a soft hat box and looked around his attic home. There was a green glass bottle for water. There was a tiny bell for reminders. There was a shiny button that felt smooth and cool. Hoot liked to touch it when he thought.

Hoot took a deep breath. The air felt dry and dusty in his beak. He blinked. “I will make a little wellness plan,” he said. “A plan can help me feel bright and strong.”

He tapped the tiny bell. Ting! “Step one,” Hoot said. “Drink water.” He poured water into a small lid and took slow sips. Sip, sip, sip. The water felt cool and kind. His throat went, “Ahhh.” His tummy stopped grumbling.

“Step two,” Hoot said. “Move my body a little.” He flapped his wings gently. Whoosh. He hopped from the hat box to an old trunk. Thump. He hopped back. Thump. “This is fun,” he chuckled. He made a little hopping song. “Hop, hop, hoot! Hop, hop, hoot!”

Just then, a friendly mouse named Pip peeped out from behind a book. Pip had soft gray fur and bright black eyes. “Good morning, Hoot,” Pip squeaked. “You look busy. What are you doing?”

“I am making a wellness plan,” Hoot said. “I felt slow and heavy. I want to feel light and happy.”

Pip sniffed the air. “Wellness?” he asked. “What is that?”

“Wellness means taking good care of your body and your feelings,” Hoot said kindly. “It is drinking water, moving a bit, resting enough, eating foods that help, and being gentle with yourself.”

Pip’s whiskers wiggled. “Can I try too?”

“Yes,” Hoot said. “We can do it together.”

Hoot tapped the bell again. Ting! “Step three,” he said. “Eat a rainbow snack.” He opened a tiny basket. Inside were red apple slices, orange carrots, and green peas. “Crunch, crunch,” Pip said happily. “Munch, munch,” Hoot said. The apple was sweet and cool. The carrot was crisp. The peas were soft like little pillows. “My beak feels busy,” Hoot giggled. “My tummy feels glad.”

Pip wiped his whiskers. “What next?”

“Step four,” Hoot said softly. “Rest and breathe.” He puffed his feathers and sat tall. “Let’s try belly breaths. In… one, two. Out… one, two.” The attic felt extra quiet. The sunbeam felt warm on their faces. Hoot’s heart went slow and steady, like the clock. Tick… tock. “I feel calm,” Pip whispered.

Just then, the wind knocked a box. It tipped, slid, and spilled a pile of buttons. Clatter-clack! The noise made Pip jump. “Oh no!” he cried. “It’s a mess!”

Hoot’s eyes were gentle. “It is a small problem,” he said. “We can solve it. Let’s use our plan.”

Pip took a breath. In… one, two. Out… one, two. His whiskers stopped shaking.

“Step five,” Hoot said. “Make a tiny goal.” He tapped the bell. Ting! “Our goal: pick up ten buttons and put them in the jar.”

“Ten is not too big,” Pip said, nodding. “We can do it.”

They counted together. “One, two, three,” Pip said, plinking shiny buttons into a glass jar. Plink, plink, plink. “Four, five, six,” Hoot said, the buttons cool and smooth under his feathers. “Seven, eight, nine, ten!” they finished.

They looked at the floor. It was tidy again. The attic felt peaceful. The sunbeam looked proud.

Pip squeaked, “We did it! The little goal helped.”

Hoot smiled. “Little goals make big jobs feel small,” he said. “Step by step. Breath by breath. Sip by sip.”

Pip’s eyes shone. “What is our next goal, Hoot?”

Hoot thought. He touched the shiny button for luck. “Our next goal is rest. Tonight, I will sleep earlier,” he said. “I will make a cozy nest. I will say goodnight to the moon.”

Pip yawned a tiny yawn. “I will rest too,” he said. “I will stretch, then curl up.”

Hoot nodded. “We learned today: take care of your body, take care of your feelings, and make tiny goals. That is wellness.”

The attic hummed with quiet joy. The clock ticked kindly. The wind was gentle on the window. Hoot and Pip snuggled into their cozy spots, feeling calm, strong, and proud.

“We can do this every day,” Pip whispered.

“Yes,” Hoot said. “Every day, a little wellness.”

The End.
