# Miko Makes a Plan

In a sunny dining room, a courageous monkey named Miko sat at the big wooden table. The table smelled like warm toast and sweet bananas. The chairs made tiny squeaks on the floor. Light poured in and made soft squares on the rug.

Miko wiggled his tail and smiled. “Good morning, table! Good morning, chairs!” he said. He loved this room. It was where his family ate, talked, and laughed.

Mama set a bowl on the table. “Banana oatmeal for everyone,” she said. Steam rose in curly lines. “We’ll eat, then we’ll tidy up, please.”

Miko nodded. “I can help,” he said. He liked helping. It made his chest feel warm and strong.

Just then, a soft clatter came from the hallway. The mailbox by the door was full. Letters poked out like little tongues. A flyer slid onto the floor and fluttered into the dining room.

Miko picked it up. The paper felt smooth and cool. The picture showed a big, happy broom. The words said: “Neighborhood Clean Table Day! Please keep shared spaces neat. Say kind words. Work together.”

Miko’s eyes grew wide. “Mama, our dining room is a shared space for our family,” he said. “We can have our own Clean Table Day!”

Mama smiled. “That is civic responsibility,” she said. “We take care of places we all use. We speak kindly. We listen. We help.”

Miko stood tall on his chair. “I will make a plan,” he said bravely. “We will eat. We will talk. We will clean.”

Little sister Lila clapped. “A plan! A plan!” she sang.

They ate their warm oatmeal. It tasted creamy and sweet. Spoons made soft taps. Miko chewed and thought. He looked around. Crumbs on the placemats. A sticky spot near the juice. A napkin on the floor. He felt a little worried. It was a lot of mess.

He took a slow breath. “We can do it,” he whispered to himself. “I am brave, and I can ask for help the peaceful way.”

When they finished eating, Miko spoke in a gentle voice. “Family, can we clean the table together?” he asked. “We all use it. We can take turns. We can say thank you.”

Papa nodded. “Good plan, Miko. Thank you for asking kindly.”

Lila looked at the sticky juice spot and frowned. “It’s yucky,” she said.

Miko smiled at her. “We can do it together. I will get the cloth. You can hold the spray. We can count to three.”

He padded to the sink. The cloth was cool and damp. He wrung it out with a squish-squish. He handed Lila the small spray bottle. “Ready?” he asked.

Lila nodded. “One… two… three!” they said together.

Spritz! Spritz! The juice spot made a tiny sss sound. Miko wiped in little circles. The sticky went away. “Teamwork!” he cheered.

Mama gathered plates. Papa folded napkins. Lila picked up crumbs with a little brush. Brush, brush, brush. Miko lined up the chairs so they sat straight and tall.

Soon, only one thing was left: a tall stack of clean bowls. They looked wobbly.

“Uh-oh,” said Lila. “I don’t like the wobbles.”

Miko felt his heart beat fast. He wanted to rush. But he remembered peaceful communication. He put his hand on the table and spoke slowly. “I feel worried about the bowls,” he said. “Can someone please help me carry them? I want to be safe.”

Papa smiled. “Thank you for saying how you feel,” he said. “I will carry the bowls.”

They moved the bowls together. No wobbles. No crash. Miko felt proud and calm.

When the dining room was clean, the wood shone. The air smelled like lemon. The rug felt soft under Miko’s feet. Sunlight made the room glow. It was their family’s shared space, clean and ready.

Miko bounced in a happy circle. “We did it!” he said. “Thank you, Mama. Thank you, Papa. Thank you, Lila.”

Lila hugged his arm. “Thank you, Miko,” she said. “You were brave.”

Mama touched Miko’s shoulder. “You showed civic responsibility,” she said. “You noticed a need. You made a plan. You asked for help with kind words. You helped our family.”

Papa nodded. “And you used peaceful communication. You spoke calmly. You listened. You said thank you.”

Miko felt that warm, strong feeling again. “It feels good to take care of our place,” he said. “I like being part of our family team.”

He took the flyer and taped it on the fridge. “Clean Table Day can be every day,” Miko said. “A little bit at a time.”

Lila giggled. “Every day!” she sang.

They sat back at the table for a small treat: slices of cool banana. The fruit was soft and sweet. The clean table felt smooth under their fingers.

Miko looked around the bright dining room and whispered, “Thank you, room.”

Then he turned to his family. “Let’s remember: We take care of our shared spaces. We use kind words. We say thank you.”

Mama, Papa, and Lila nodded. “Yes,” they said together.

Miko grinned. “That is our family promise.”

The End.
