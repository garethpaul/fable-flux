# Toby the Tortoise and the Great Pantry Clean-Up

Toby the Tortoise woke up one sunny morning and stretched his little green legs. He walked slowly to the kitchen pantry to get his breakfast cereal. But when he opened the pantry door, he gasped!

"Oh my!" said Toby, looking at the messy shelves. Boxes were tumbled over, cans were rolling around, and everything was mixed up. "This pantry needs help!"

The pantry was dark and smelled like old crackers. Toby could hear the soft *thunk* of a can rolling behind the flour. He saw cereal scattered on the floor like tiny colorful pebbles.

"This looks like a very big job," Toby said to himself. "But I know what Grandpa Tortoise always says: 'Slow and steady wins the race!'"

Toby took a deep breath and made a plan. "First, I'll pick up all the things on the floor. Then I'll put the cans together. After that, I'll organize the boxes."

He started with the scattered cereal, picking up each piece one by one. *Plip, plop, plip* went the cereal as it dropped into the dustpan. Even though it was taking a long time, Toby kept going.

"One piece at a time," he said cheerfully. "I can do this!"

After cleaning the floor, Toby felt tired. His little arms were sore, and he wanted to take a nap. The pantry still looked messy, and there was so much more work to do.

"Maybe I should quit," Toby thought. "This is too hard for a little tortoise like me."

But then he remembered something important. "Wait," he said out loud. "When I feel overwhelmed, I should take a break and breathe."

Toby sat down on his shell and took three big, slow breaths. *Inhale... exhale... inhale... exhale... inhale... exhale.* He felt much better!

"Now I can keep going," he said with a smile.

Next, Toby gathered all the cans. They were heavy, so he rolled them one by one to the right shelf. *Roll, roll, roll* went the cans across the floor. Some cans tried to roll away, but Toby patiently chased after them.

"Come back here, silly soup can!" he giggled.

Then came the boxes. Toby stacked them carefully, making sure the heavy ones were on the bottom. The boxes felt rough and smooth under his little hands. Some boxes smelled like sweet cookies, and others smelled like pasta.

When Toby put the last box in place, he stepped back and looked at his work. The pantry was clean and organized! Everything had its special place, and it smelled fresh and tidy.

"I did it!" Toby cheered, doing a little happy dance. "I finished the whole job!"

Just then, Mama Tortoise came into the kitchen. "Toby! What a wonderful surprise! You cleaned the entire pantry all by yourself!"

"It was hard work, Mama," said Toby proudly. "I wanted to give up, but I remembered to take breaks and keep trying. I did it one step at a time!"

Mama Tortoise gave Toby a big, warm hug. "You showed such persistence, my dear. When we stick with our tasks and don't give up, we can accomplish amazing things!"

That night, Toby felt very proud as he got his cereal from the neat, clean pantry. He had learned that big jobs become easier when you break them into small steps and never give up.

The End.
