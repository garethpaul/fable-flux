# Tully the Tortoise Talks It Out

In a cozy garden shed, where the air smelled like fresh soil and warm wood, lived Tully, a wise tortoise. The shed was snug and tidy. Sunlight slipped through the little window and made golden squares on the floor. Tully loved the quiet hum of the day, the soft tick of the clock, and the gentle creak of the door in the breeze.

One bright morning, Tully stretched his legs and took a big, slow breath. “Today,” he said, “I have a goal. I will tidy the tool shelf so everyone can find what they need.” He smiled. “A neat shelf helps us feel calm and well.”

Just then, the door creaked wide. In came Pip the mouse, with quick tiny feet, and Bella the bluebird, with fluttery wings. Pip squeaked, “I want to build a tiny house with twigs!” Bella chirped, “I want to make a soft nest with string!”

Tully nodded. “We can share the space. We can use the shelf and the floor. Let’s talk about our plan.”

Pip darted to a basket of nails. “These are mine!” Pip grabbed too many and they clinked like rain. Bella swooped to a spool of string. “This is mine!” She tugged and the string tumbled like a fluffy cloud.

Tully watched. Tools rattled. A hammer rolled off the shelf with a thud. Pip jumped. Bella flapped. The shed felt noisy and jumbled. Tully felt a little tight in his chest.

He pulled into his shell for a short, calm pause. Inside, it was quiet and cool. He took three slow breaths. In… out. In… out. In… out.

Then Tully peeked out and said in a warm, low voice, “Let’s use peaceful words. We can say how we feel. We can listen. We can make a plan together.”

Pip’s whiskers twitched. “I feel excited,” Pip said. “I want to build now!”

Bella hopped on the window ledge. “I feel excited too! I want to make my nest now!”

Tully nodded. “I feel a little worried when things are messy. I want us all to be safe and calm.”

The shed grew quiet. The sunbeam felt warm on their faces. The smell of fresh straw was sweet.

“Here is an idea,” Tully said. “We can take turns. We can share. We can sort.”

“How?” Pip asked, eyes wide.

Tully smiled gently. “Step one: We name our goal. We all want a tidy, safe shed and time for our projects.”

Pip and Bella nodded.

“Step two: We listen. Pip wants twigs and nails. Bella wants string and soft bits. Tully wants tools in order.”

“Step three,” Tully went on, “We make a plan. Pip uses the small mat on the left. Bella uses the table by the window. I will tidy the shelf. We will put back what we take. We will ask before we move something.”

Pip lifted a paw. “Can we have a signal if we need help?”

“Yes,” said Tully. He tapped the bench gently. “Three soft taps means ‘Please help.’ One tap means ‘All is okay.’”

Bella chirped, “I like that.”

They began. Tap, tap, tap. Tully set the hammer on the shelf. Clink. Pip counted nails, one, two, three, placing them in a tiny bowl. Bella snipped a short piece of string with a snip-snip and lined it with soft moss. The air smelled like twigs and warm rope. A little breeze made the chimes sing a quiet ting-ting.

Soon, Pip frowned. “I can’t reach the glue,” he said. Tap, tap, tap.

Tully turned. “I hear your signal,” he said. “Thank you for asking.” He walked slowly, steady and kind. “May I pass you the glue?”

“Yes, please,” Pip squeaked.

Tully handed it over. “Here you go.”

Bella looked at the broom leaning in the corner. “May I move the broom to the door?” she asked. “I need a little more space.”

“Thank you for asking,” Tully said. “Yes, you may.”

They worked and talked. They asked. They listened. They said please and thank you. The shed felt calm and bright again. Tully hummed a happy tune. Pip’s tiny house took shape. Bella’s nest grew round and soft.

At last Tully stepped back. The shelf was neat. “Goal met,” he said, beaming. Pip wiggled with pride. “House done!” Bella fluffed her feathers. “Nest done!”

They all clapped softly so the tools would not rattle—clap, clap, clap.

Pip squeaked, “We did it together!”

Bella chirped, “Peaceful words help us work well.”

Tully nodded. “When we feel big feelings, we can pause. We can breathe. We can use calm voices. We can listen and take turns. That keeps our bodies and hearts well.”

Pip smiled. “Next time, I will ask before I grab.”

Bella smiled too. “Next time, I will share the string.”

Tully looked around the tidy shed. The golden light made everything glow. “Friends,” he said, “our teamwork and peaceful talk turned a messy moment into a good day.”

Pip, Bella, and Tully sat together, sipping warm peppermint water from tiny cups. It smelled fresh and cozy. They rested, happy and calm.

Tully whispered, “Peaceful-communication helps us reach our goals. It makes our space safe. It makes our hearts feel good.”

Pip and Bella nodded. “We agree.”

Outside, the garden buzzed softly. Inside, the shed felt like a hug.

The End.
