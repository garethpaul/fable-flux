# Ollie Otter’s Study Corner Surprise

In a quiet study corner, by a sunny window and a shelf of bright books, lived a playful otter named Ollie. Ollie loved to wiggle his whiskers and tap his tail. He liked soft cushions, shiny pencils, and the gentle smell of paper and crayons. The study corner felt safe and cozy, like a warm hug.

“Good morning, corner!” Ollie chirped. He stretched, wiggled, and took a sip of cool water. “Sip, sip! Water helps me think,” he said proudly. Healthy habits made Ollie feel strong.

Today, a new box sat on the table. It was covered in star stickers and tied with a blue ribbon. Ollie’s eyes grew wide. “Ooo! A mystery box,” he whispered. His tummy fluttered. He was curious, but also a little unsure.

His friend, a tiny ladybug named Dot, buzzed onto the ribbon and giggled. “Open it, Ollie! Let’s see!”

Ollie tapped the box. Tap, tap. He put his ear close. “I hear… nothing.” He sniffed. “It smells like paper and lemon,” he said, scrunching his nose. “Should we open it now? Or after snack?”

Dot twirled in the air. “Now! Now!”

Ollie took a slow breath, just like he learned. In… and out… “Breathing helps my brain,” he said. “Let’s be smart. Let’s have a plan.”

Dot tilted her head. “A plan?”

Ollie nodded. “Yes! A strategy. Step one: drink water.” He drank a little more. “Step two: tidy the table.” He stacked the crayons, lined up the pencils, and brushed off crumbs with his paw. “Step three: open the box slowly.”

He tugged the ribbon. Shoop! He lifted the lid. Inside was a big puzzle book with smooth pages, a small timer shaped like a sunflower, and a card that said, “Try something new today!”

Ollie’s whiskers twitched. “A puzzle book? Hmm. I like splashing and drawing. Puzzles look… tricky.”

Dot landed on the book and patted it. “We can try. If we don’t like it, we can stop.”

Ollie looked at the window. Tiny dust sparkles danced in the sun. He looked back at the book. He felt a swirl of I-don’t-know feelings. “What if I can’t do it?” he asked softly.

Dot’s voice was kind. “What if you can? What if it’s fun?”

Ollie blinked. He remembered a time he tried celery sticks with peanut butter. “Crunch!” He thought it would taste funny, but it was crunchy and yummy. He smiled. “I can be open. I can try.”

He set the sunflower timer. “Ding in five minutes,” he said. “We will try for five. That is our plan.” His tail thumped happily on the rug.

The first puzzle showed four shapes and a picture of a little otter holding a circle. “Find the circle,” Dot read.

Ollie traced each shape with his paw. “This one is pointy. Not a circle. This one is bumpy. Not a circle. This one is… round and smooth!” He held up the circle. “Found it!”

Dot clapped her tiny hands. “You did it!”

Ollie felt warm in his chest. The next page showed a pattern: red sticker, blue sticker, red sticker… “What comes next?” Dot asked.

Ollie thought. He hummed a soft tune. He lined the crayons in the same pattern: red, blue, red… “Blue!” he said. He stuck a blue dot on the page. “Patterns are like songs,” he giggled.

They turned the page again. This one had a maze. “Start at the apple. Find the water,” the page said. The maze looked twisty. Ollie’s nose twitched. “This is big,” he whispered.

Dot buzzed closer. “Let’s break it into little paths. We can try one path, and if it’s wrong, we try another. That’s a strategy too.”

Ollie smiled. “Little steps.” He gently slid a blue crayon along a path. “Oops, a wall,” he said. He tried another path. “Oops, another wall.” He took a slow breath. In… and out… “I can try a new way.” Finally, the crayon swooped to the water. “Splash! I found it!”

Just then, the timer sang a soft ding. Ollie sat back, surprised. “Five minutes already?”

Dot grinned. “How do you feel?”

Ollie listened to his body. His paws felt warm. His head felt bright. His heart felt big. He wasn’t scared anymore. He was proud. “I feel good,” he said. “Trying new things can be fun. My plan helped. My breaths helped. And you helped, Dot.”

Dot wiggled. “You helped you, too!”

Ollie closed the book and stretched. “Snack time,” he said. He washed his paws with a cool, wet cloth. “Clean paws, healthy paws.” He munched apple slices. “Crunch, crunch!” He sipped more water. “Sip, sip!”

He looked at the puzzle book again. “Tomorrow, I will try more,” he said. “I will keep an open mind. I can listen, learn, and try. If I need help, I will ask. If it’s hard, I will breathe and make a plan.”

Dot buzzed a happy buzz. “That is the Ollie way!”

Ollie patted the sunny window. “Thank you, study corner,” he whispered. The room smelled like apples and paper. The light felt warm and friendly. Ollie felt brave and calm.

“New things can be good things,” he said. “When we are open, we grow.”

The End.
