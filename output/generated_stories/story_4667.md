# Benny Beaver's Big Game Day

Benny Beaver loved to play water polo with his friends at the pond. He was the best swimmer on his team! But today was the biggest game of the year, and Benny had a problem.

"Oh no!" Benny said, looking at his reflection in the bathroom mirror. His usually shiny brown fur was muddy and messy from building dams all week. His teeth were yellow, and his paws were covered in sticky tree sap.

"I can't play like this," Benny worried. "My teammates won't want to swim with me if I'm all dirty!"

Benny's mom peeked into the bathroom. "What's wrong, sweetie?"

"Mom, the big game is in one hour, and I'm too messy to play!" Benny's voice was shaky. "I should have cleaned up sooner."

Mom smiled warmly. "Don't worry, Benny. We have time to get you squeaky clean! But we need to make a plan and work together."

First, Mom helped Benny fill the bathtub with warm, bubbly water. "Splash!" went Benny as he jumped in. The water felt so good on his fur.

"Now, let's start with your teeth," Mom said, handing him his special beaver toothbrush. "Remember to brush in circles, just like we practiced."

Benny brushed carefully. "This minty toothpaste tastes good!" he said. Soon his teeth were white and sparkly again.

Next came the tricky part - washing his fur. "The soap smells like fresh pine trees," Benny noticed as he scrubbed behind his ears. Mom helped him wash his back where he couldn't reach.

"Don't forget between your toes," Mom reminded him. "That's where mud likes to hide!"

Benny giggled as he wiggled his clean toes. The sticky sap was finally gone!

"Now for your tail," Mom said. Benny's big, flat tail was his most important tool for swimming fast. They scrubbed it until it was smooth and clean.

"I feel so much better!" Benny said, standing up in the tub. "But Mom, what if I get dirty again before the game?"

"That's why we plan ahead," Mom explained. "After you dry off, we'll pack your swim bag with a towel and extra soap. If you get messy before games, you'll always be ready to clean up!"

Benny dried himself with a fluffy towel that smelled like sunshine. He looked in the mirror again - his fur was shiny, his teeth sparkled, and his paws were perfectly clean.

"I look like a champion!" Benny said proudly.

At the pond, Benny's teammates cheered when they saw him. "Wow, Benny! You look great!" said his friend Bella.

"Thanks!" Benny said. "I learned that staying clean helps me feel confident and ready to play my best!"

During the game, Benny swam faster than ever. His clean fur glided smoothly through the water, and his sparkling smile made his whole team feel happy and proud.

After they won the game, Benny's coach said, "Good job planning ahead, Benny! A clean player is a confident player!"

That night, Benny put his toothbrush and soap in his sports bag. "Tomorrow I'll brush my teeth right after breakfast and wash my paws before practice," he planned. "Being clean makes me feel strong and ready for anything!"

From that day on, Benny always remembered: when you take care of your body by staying clean, you feel better and can do your best at everything you love.

The End.
