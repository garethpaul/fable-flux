# Ollie Otter’s Balcony Garden Day

Ollie was a playful otter who loved to splash in his water tub and paint colorful pictures. He lived with Auntie May in a cozy home with a sunny balcony garden. The balcony smelled like mint and tomatoes and felt warm on Ollie’s whiskers. Bees hummed. Leaves swished. A little blue watering can sat by a pot of strawberries.

“Good morning, garden!” Ollie chirped. He wiggled his paws and peeked at the red strawberries. “Mmm, juicy!”

Auntie May came outside with a timer shaped like a lemon. It tick-tick-ticked softly. “Good morning, Ollie. Today we water, then paint, then snack,” she said. “We’ll share the balcony with our neighbors, too. They’ll come see the art.”

Ollie clapped. “I love painting!”

Auntie May set the lemon timer. “Ten minutes to water. Then we paint. A plan helps us use time well.”

Ollie grabbed the blue watering can. “Splish, splish!” The cool water sparkled in the sun. He watered the mint and the tomatoes, and he was careful with the tiny basil leaves. He giggled when a drop landed on his nose. “Drip!”

“Nice job being gentle,” Auntie May said. “That’s caring for our place. That’s civic responsibility. We share this balcony garden with the whole building.”

Ollie stood taller. “I can help!”

Just then, two little friends peeked through the rail. Nia the snail slid along, leaving a shiny trail. Pip the finch chirped from the railing. “Hello, Ollie!”

“Hi!” Ollie waved. He saw the strawberries again. His tummy made a small grumble. “I feel hungry,” he said, patting his belly. “I want all the strawberries now.”

Nia blinked her tiny eyes. “They do look tasty.”

Pip tilted his head. “Cheep! I like berries too.”

Ollie grabbed the bowl. He held it close. “Mine,” he said. His whiskers drooped. He felt tight in his chest, like a little knot. He wanted the berries so much.

Auntie May knelt beside him. “Ollie, take a slow breath. Smell the mint.” Ollie sniffed. Fresh, cool mint tickled his nose. The knot felt softer.

“Can you name your feeling?” Auntie May asked.

Ollie thought. “I feel… excited and hungry. And a little grabby.”

“It’s okay to feel that way,” Auntie May said. “Now think: What will help everyone? We share the garden, and we share the fruit. Sharing helps friends and helps our home.”

Ollie looked at Nia’s hopeful eyes and at Pip’s bright feathers. He looked at the strawberry plant. Some berries were red and ready. Some were still white. “We can share now and save some for later,” he said. “And leave a few for the bees and birds who visit.”

“That is kind and smart,” Auntie May said. “You are thinking about others and about our balcony community.”

Ollie smiled. “Let’s make a plan.” He set the bowl down. “One berry for me, one for Nia, one for Pip, and one for the visitor birds tomorrow.”

Pip chirped, “Cheep-cheep! Thank you!”

Nia smiled slowly. “Thank you, Ollie.”

The lemon timer dinged. “Water time is done,” Auntie May said. “Painting time!”

Ollie pulled out paper, big brushes, and bright paints. The balcony smelled like paint and mint and sunshine. “Who wants to paint with me?”

Nia tapped a tiny brush. “I will make swirls.”

Pip dipped a feather tip. “I will make dots!”

Ollie dabbed red paint and made a strawberry shape. He added green for leaves. He painted a little blue watering can. “This is our sharing picture,” he said. “It will show our plan.”

Auntie May nodded. “Art can tell a story.”

Ollie wrote, with Auntie May’s help, tiny words at the bottom: We share. We care. We take turns.

When the picture was done, they set it to dry. The sun warmed the paper. Ollie felt proud. His chest felt light, like a bubble.

Neighbors came to look from their balconies. Mr. Lee waved. “What a nice painting!”

Ms. Rosa called, “Thank you for watering the shared plants.”

Ollie waved back. “We made a sharing plan!” He pointed to the bowl. “We kept some berries for tomorrow and for the birds.”

Pip fluttered. “Cheep! That’s caring for everyone.”

Nia added, “And it feels good inside.”

Ollie nodded. “It does. My tummy is happy, and my heart is happy.”

Auntie May set the lemon timer again. “Five minutes to clean up, then snack time,” she said.

Ollie put brushes in a cup of water. Swish, swish. He wiped the table with a soft cloth. Wipe, wipe. He stacked the papers and set the painting on the railing to show. The timer dinged. “All done!”

They sat together on the warm balcony floor. Ollie handed out strawberries. “One for Nia, one for Pip, one for me.” The juice was sweet on his tongue. Birds sang. Leaves rustled. The blue sky smiled.

Auntie May hugged Ollie. “I’m proud of you. You used your time well, you cared for the balcony, and you shared.”

Ollie hugged back. “Sharing makes more happy.”

He looked at his painting and at his friends. He took a deep breath of minty air and said, “Today we learned: When we share, we care for others, our home, and our hearts.”

The End.
