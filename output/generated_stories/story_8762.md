# Bucky the Busy Beaver Takes a Breath

Bucky the busy beaver pattered into the bathroom with his flat tail swish-swish-swish on the tiles. The room was bright and clean. The mirror was shiny like a pond on a sunny day. The soap smelled like lemons. Warm steam puffed like tiny clouds.

“I’m ready to brush! I’m ready to scrub! I’m busy, busy, busy!” Bucky sang. He wiggled his whiskers and grabbed the blue toothbrush. He wanted to brush his teeth, wash his paws, and comb his fur—everything all at once.

Bucky turned on the water. Whoosh! The sink bubbled with cool, splishy water. He pumped the soap. Squish! A big blob fell and slid across the counter. “Oops!” Bucky giggled. He liked moving fast.

Just then, his little sister, Bella Beaver, poked her nose around the door. “I need the sink,” she said softly. She held a tiny cup and her little toothbrush. Her eyes were shiny and hopeful.

“I’m using it,” Bucky said quickly. He brushed one tooth, two teeth, three teeth. “I have a lot to do!”

Bella shuffled her feet. “Can I have a turn soon?”

Bucky felt the tight, hot feeling of hurry in his tummy. He looked at the mirror. His fur stuck up like a fuzzy dandelion. He wanted to be done first. He wanted to be fastest. His tail thumped-thump-thumped on the floor.

“No! I need more time!” he blurted. His voice echoed in the bathroom. The sound made Bella’s ears droop. The soap blob slid onto the floor with a plop.

Bucky’s cheeks grew warm. The water sounded louder—whoooooosh! The lights felt bright. Everything felt too much. He clutched the brush tight. His body was busy, but his heart felt wobbly.

Mama Beaver’s gentle face peeked in. She smelled like warm towels. “I hear big feelings,” Mama said softly. “It’s hard to wait. It’s hard to share when you feel rushed.”

“I want to be done now,” Bucky said. “I’m busy! I’m fast! I’m… I’m frustrated.” The word felt big in his mouth.

Mama nodded. “Your body is telling you, ‘Slow, please.’ Let’s help it. Try Stop, Breathe, Decide.”

Bucky blinked. “Stop, Breathe, Decide?”

Mama smiled. “Yes. First, stop. Put the brush down.”

Bucky set his brush on the sink. His paws felt sticky with soap. “Stop,” he whispered.

“Now breathe,” Mama said. “Smell the lemon soap.” Bucky sniffed in. “Mmm.” “Blow out like you’re cooling hot cocoa.” Bucky blew slowly. “Hoooo.”

In, out. In, out. The whoosh of the water got quieter. His tail stopped thumping. His tummy felt less tight.

“Now decide,” Mama said. “What can we do to help each other?”

Bucky looked at Bella. Her cup was small. Her turn would be quick. He felt a tiny kindness bubble up like a little spring.

“I can share,” Bucky said. “Bella can go first to rinse, and I can count my calm.” He smiled a little. “I can help.”

Bella’s ears perked up. “Really?”

“Really,” Bucky said. “I will volunteer to hold your cup.” He held out his paws.

“Thank you,” Bella said. Her voice was like a tiny bell. Bucky turned off the water for a moment. The bathroom felt peaceful. He filled Bella’s cup to the line. “Here you go.” The cup felt cool in his hand. The water sparkled.

Bella rinsed and brushed with quick tiny circles. “All done!” she chirped. She hopped aside. “Your turn.”

Bucky felt proud and calm, like a rock in a quiet stream. He turned the water back on just a little. Whoosh-shh. He pumped one small dot of soap. Squish. He brushed his teeth in slow, happy strokes. “Top teeth, bottom teeth,” he sang. “Front teeth, back.” He made little circles like bubbles.

Mama hummed. “I like your slow song.”

Bucky grinned with a foamy smile. He combed his fur gently. The comb made a soft tick-tick sound. He wiped the counter with a towel. “I will volunteer to clean the soap drip,” he told Bella. “I made the plop. I can fix the plop.”

Bella giggled. “Plop!”

Bucky wiped the floor where the soap had fallen. The towel was warm and fuzzy. The lemon smell floated up again. He rinsed the sink, turned off the tap, and hung the towel straight.

“All tidy,” Bucky said. He felt light. The bathroom looked cozy, and the mirror showed a calm beaver with smooth fur and bright eyes.

Mama touched his shoulder. “How did Stop, Breathe, Decide feel?”

“It felt like my busy turned into gentle,” Bucky said. “When I stop, I can hear my good ideas. When I breathe, I can choose. I shared. I helped. I feel proud.”

Bella nodded. “You helped me and the bathroom.”

Bucky puffed his chest, then laughed at himself. “I still like to be fast,” he said. “But I can be kind-fast. I can be sharing-fast.”

Mama smiled. “You practiced self-regulation. That means you guided your feelings, like steering a little boat.”

Bucky held an imaginary paddle. “Paddle left, paddle right,” he said. “Calm crabapple, cool cocoa.” He took a gentle breath in, and a slow, long breath out.

He looked at Bella. “Next time, we can take turns. You first on even days. Me first on odd days. We can help each other and clean together.”

Bella clapped. “Sharing plan!”

Bucky hung two tiny cups on a hook. “We are bathroom helpers,” he said. “I volunteer to check the towels.”

Mama gave them both a hug that smelled like lemons and love.

Bucky swished his tail softly. “Stop, Breathe, Decide,” he whispered. “I can do it.”

The End.
