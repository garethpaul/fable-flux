# Momo Breathes and Listens

Momo was a courageous little monkey with bright, curious eyes and soft, fuzzy fur. One sunny afternoon, Momo tiptoed into the laundry room. The room smelled like soapy bubbles and warm cotton. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer sang, “Thump-a-thump.”

“Hello, laundry room,” Momo whispered. “I am here to help.”

Momo wanted to fold the tiny socks and towels for Mama and to write a kind note for the family. On a small shelf, there was paper, a blue crayon, and a banana sticker. “Perfect,” Momo said. “I will write: Thank you, family. I love you.”

But when Momo reached for the paper, the washing machine suddenly gurgled and burped. “Glub! Glub!” A wet sock slid down to the floor. Then another sock. Plop! Plop!

“Oh no,” said Momo. “The socks are escaping!”

Momo hurried to grab them, but the socks were very slippery. One stuck to Momo’s tail. Another stuck to Momo’s foot. The floor felt cold and squishy. The dryer rattled, “Thump-a-thump,” like a sleepy drum. Momo’s heart beat fast.

“I must be brave,” said Momo. “I can fix this.”

Momo tried to do everything at once. Momo reached for the paper, the socks, the crayon, and even the banana sticker. The crayon rolled away. The paper bent. Momo’s shoulders felt tight, and Momo’s tummy felt twisty.

“This is too much,” Momo said, with a little frown. “I can’t do it all.”

Just then, a gentle drip sound came from the sink. Drip… drip… drip. Momo listened. Momo remembered what Mama taught: “When you feel big feelings, breathe, listen, and be kind.”

Momo put a hand on Momo’s chest and took a slow breath in. In… and out. Momo felt the air go in like a cool breeze, and out like a warm sigh. Momo watched the breath like a little cloud floating by.

“In… and out,” Momo said softly. “I can be calm.”

Momo looked around. The laundry room was not scary. It was full of sounds and smells. Whirr, thump, drip. Soap and cotton. Warm and safe. Momo smiled a tiny smile and listened again.

“I will do one thing at a time,” said Momo. “First, I will help the socks.”

Momo bent down and picked up one sock. “Hello, little sock,” Momo said kindly. “You look cold.” Momo patted the sock dry with a towel. “Back to your partner you go.” Momo found the match and folded them together, soft as clouds.

Momo picked up the next sock. “Hello, other sock,” Momo said. “I will help you too.” Momo dried it and matched it. Soon, the floor was clean and dry. Momo’s shoulders felt soft again, and Momo’s tummy felt calm.

“Now I will write my note,” said Momo.

Momo sat on a small stepstool. The paper felt crisp. The blue crayon felt smooth in Momo’s hand. Momo thought about the words and took another slow breath. In… and out. “I will write with care,” Momo whispered. “I will write with love.”

Momo wrote slowly: Thank you, family. I love you. Your brave helper, Momo. The letters were big and round. The dots were tiny and neat. Momo stuck the banana sticker at the top. It looked cheerful and bright.

Just then, Mama walked in, carrying a basket. Mama saw the folded socks, the dry floor, and the blue note with the banana sticker.

“Oh, Momo,” Mama said, smiling. “You were very brave and very kind! What happened?”

Momo told the story. “The socks slipped. I felt busy and twisty. But I remembered to breathe. In… and out. I listened to the drip and the hum. I did one thing at a time. I helped the socks. I wrote a note. I used mindful monkey breaths.”

Mama nodded. “That is mindfulness. You noticed your feelings. You calmed your body. You listened. You were gentle with the socks and gentle with yourself. That shows compassion.”

Momo’s eyes sparkled. “I showed respect, too! I took care of the laundry room. I took care of our things.”

Mama hugged Momo. The hug felt warm and snug, like a cozy towel from the dryer. “I am proud of you,” Mama said. “Your kind writing will make everyone smile.”

Momo held the note and read it out loud, slowly: “Thank you, family. I love you. Your brave helper, Momo.” Momo felt happy and peaceful.

The dryer finished with a soft ding. Momo and Mama folded the warm towels together. They counted the folds. One, two, three, four. The towels smelled like sunshine and soap. Momo took one more mindful breath. In… and out.

“I like breathing and listening,” Momo said. “It helps my heart and my brain.”

Mama nodded again. “When we are mindful, we can see clearly. We can choose kind words. We can fix problems, one small step at a time.”

Momo placed the blue note on the laundry shelf, where everyone could see it. The room felt quiet, bright, and friendly. Momo patted the washing machine. “Thank you for washing our clothes,” Momo said with respect.

As they left the laundry room, Momo waved and whispered, “I am brave. I am kind. I can breathe, listen, and help.”

The End.
