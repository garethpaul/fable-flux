# Rosie Rabbit and the Garden Shed Mystery

Rosie Rabbit was small, soft, and very determined. Her ears stood tall, like two little flags. One morning, the sun made the grass sparkle with dew. The air smelled like wet leaves and sweet flowers. Rosie twitched her pink nose and listened.

Clink, clank.

The sound came from the garden shed. The shed sat behind the carrot patch. Its wooden door was old and green. A little spider web shimmered on the handle.

Rosie’s eyes grew wide. “What is that sound?” she whispered. Her whiskers trembled with curiosity. She loved to learn new things. She loved new words too.

Clink, clank.

Rosie took a careful hop. “I want to know,” she said. “I will get ready. I will be safe.” She tied a bright yellow scarf around her neck. She put a small acorn in her pocket for bravery. She packed a tiny leaf-notebook and a stubby pencil. On the first page she wrote: “New words. New ideas. Be kind.”

Rosie went to see her friend, Tilly Tortoise. Tilly was slow and very wise. She was sipping warm dandelion tea in a little leaf cup.

“Tilly,” said Rosie, “I hear clink, clank in the shed. I feel curious. I want to look. But I want to be careful.”

Tilly smiled and nodded. Her shell made a soft, tap-tap on the ground. “Curiosity is good,” she said. “Curiosity means you want to learn. Let’s prepare. Let’s be kind. And let’s use our words.”

Rosie bounced. “Yes! Prepare. Be kind. Use words.”

They packed a tiny lantern, a clean cloth, and a carrot snack. “Snacks help when you feel worried,” said Tilly. “And a cloth helps if something is dusty.”

Together they walked to the garden shed. The wooden door felt rough. The metal handle felt cool. The air smelled like soil and old wood. A breeze made the leaves whisper.

Clink, clank.

Rosie lifted a paw and knocked. “Hello?” she called in a clear, gentle voice. “We are friends. We are kind. We are curious. May we come in?”

No answer. Only the sound again.

Clink, clank.

Rosie looked at Tilly. Tilly nodded slowly. “Let’s open the door together,” she said. “We will be brave and careful.”

They pushed the door. It creaked like a sleepy song. Inside, the shed was dim. Dust sparkled in the sunbeams. Shelves held pots, seeds, and shiny tools. It smelled like rosemary and earth.

Clink, clank.

Rosie and Tilly peered under a long wooden table. There, trapped between a tin watering can and a coil of hose, was a little blue bird. The bird’s tiny foot had a string around it. The string tugged a metal bucket. Clink, clank.

“Oh!” Rosie gasped. “A friend needs help.”

The bird chirped in a shaky voice. “Cheep-cheep. I am Bree. I got stuck. I am scared.”

Rosie knelt and spoke softly. “Hello, Bree. We are here. We are kind. We will help you. We will go slow.”

Tilly set down the lantern. The warm light made the shadows softer. “Let’s think,” said Tilly. “We will solve this problem step by step.”

Rosie took her leaf-notebook and wrote: “Problem: String on foot. Goal: Free Bree. Plan: Cut, lift, comfort.”

She looked around. On a hook, she saw small garden scissors. They were shiny and silver. “Scissors,” she said, tasting the new word. “Scissors start with ssss.” She smiled. “I like learning words.”

Rosie showed the scissors to Bree. “These are scissors. We use them to cut the string. May I snip the string?”

Bree breathed in. “Cheep,” she said. “Yes. Please.”

Rosie’s paws were steady. Tilly held the bucket so it would not clank. “Ready,” Tilly whispered.

Snip.

The string fell away. Bree fluttered her wings. “Cheep! I feel free. Thank you. Thank you.”

Rosie took the clean cloth and gently wiped Bree’s little foot. “Soft and safe,” she said. “How does it feel?”

“Better,” said Bree. She hopped to the window and sang a bright note. The note sounded like sunshine.

Rosie felt warm in her chest. “Curiosity helped us find you,” she said. “Preparation helped us help you. And kindness made it gentle.”

Bree nodded. “You listened. You used careful words. You were brave.”

Rosie wrote in her notebook: “New words: scissors, careful, prepare. New idea: Ask, plan, help.”

Bree tilted her head. “I want to learn words too. Can you teach me a word?”

Rosie giggled. “Yes! Today’s word is curious. Curious means ‘I want to know.’”

Bree chirped, “Curious! I am curious about clouds.”

Tilly poured a drop of dandelion tea into a bottle cap and shared it. They had the carrot snack together. The carrot was crunchy. The tea was sweet and warm. The shed felt friendly now.

Rosie looked around and tidied the tools. She coiled the hose in a neat circle. She put the scissors back on the hook. “Preparing also means cleaning up,” she said. “That helps the next time.”

Bree fluttered to the doorway. “I will visit you,” she said. “We can learn new words together. We can be curious every day.”

Rosie waved her paw. “Yes. We will ask questions. We will listen. We will be kind.”

As the sun set, the garden turned golden. The air smelled like lavender. Rosie tucked her notebook into her pocket. Her heart felt light.

“We had a problem,” she said to Tilly. “We made a plan. We solved it. Curiosity, kindness, and preparation helped us.”

Tilly smiled. “That is a good lesson,” she said. “Curious minds grow. Kind hearts help. Ready friends are brave.”

Rosie’s ears wiggled happily. “I will remember,” she said. “Tomorrow, I will learn a new word.”

The End.
