# Joey's Fair Share Workshop

Joey the kangaroo was a happy little joey who loved to hop and play. Today, he bounced into Grandpa Roo's garage workshop, where the smell of wood shavings and paint filled the air.

"Good morning, Grandpa!" Joey said, his nose twitching at the sweet smell of sawdust.

"Hello there, little hopper!" Grandpa Roo smiled, his whiskers wiggling. "I'm building toy cars for the forest children. Would you like to help?"

Joey's eyes grew wide with excitement. On the workbench sat three beautiful wooden cars - one red, one blue, and one yellow. They were shiny and smooth, and Joey could hear the gentle tap-tap-tap of Grandpa's hammer.

"Oh yes!" Joey bounced up and down. "Can I paint them?"

Just then, Joey's friends Bella Bunny and Freddy Fox peeked into the garage.

"Wow!" said Bella, her ears perking up. "Those cars are amazing!"

"I want the red one!" Freddy called out, rushing forward.

"No, I saw it first!" Joey said quickly, grabbing the red car.

"That's not fair!" Bella's voice became sad. "I wanted to help too."

The garage became very quiet except for the tick-tock of the old clock on the wall. Joey looked at his friends' sad faces and felt his tummy feel funny - not the good kind of funny.

Grandpa Roo set down his hammer gently. "Joey, what do you think would be fair for everyone?"

Joey took a deep breath, feeling the cool garage air fill his lungs. He thought very carefully, just like Grandpa had taught him. "Well..." he said slowly, "there are three cars and three of us."

"That's right," Grandpa nodded. "What could you do?"

Joey's ears drooped as he realized he had been selfish. "I think... I think we should each pick a car. But how do we choose fairly?"

Bella hopped closer. "Maybe we could take turns picking?"

"But who goes first?" Freddy asked, his tail swishing.

Joey's face lit up with an idea. "I know! Let's put our names in Grandpa's hat and pick without looking!"

"That sounds very fair," Grandpa Roo said proudly, handing over his soft brown hat.

The three friends wrote their names on small pieces of paper. Joey's paws felt a little shaky as he folded his paper and dropped it in the hat.

Grandpa shook the hat gently. "The first name is... Bella!"

Bella chose the blue car, running her paw over its smooth surface. "It's perfect!"

"Next is... Freddy!"

Freddy picked the yellow car and made little "vroom-vroom" sounds.

"And Joey gets the red car!" Grandpa announced.

Joey smiled big. Even though he got the car he wanted, he felt even happier because everyone was happy. The garage filled with the sound of their laughter and pretend car noises.

"Now," said Grandpa Roo, "let's paint racing stripes on all of them together!"

As they painted side by side, Joey learned that sharing and being fair felt much better than keeping everything for himself. The paint smelled fresh and colorful, and working together made everything more fun.

"Thank you for helping us be fair, Joey," Bella said, giving him a gentle hug.

"Being fair makes everyone happy," Joey said wisely, his heart feeling warm and light.

From that day on, whenever Joey and his friends played together, they always remembered to be fair and kind to each other.

The End.
