# Ruby's Rooftop Writing Club

Ruby was a small brown rabbit with soft, floppy ears and bright curious eyes. She lived in a cozy apartment building and loved to hop up to the rooftop garden every morning.

The rooftop smelled like fresh flowers and warm sunshine. Colorful plants grew in big pots. A wooden bench sat near the edge where Ruby could see the whole neighborhood below.

Ruby had a special secret. She loved to write stories! Every day, she brought her little notebook and purple crayon to write about her adventures.

"Today I'll write about flying like a bird," Ruby said, wiggling her pink nose. She hopped onto the bench and began to write.

Just then, she heard quiet sniffling sounds. Behind a tall sunflower plant, Ruby saw Maya, a little mouse with gray fur and tiny paws.

"Hello," said Ruby gently. "Are you okay?"

Maya peeked out shyly. "I'm fine," she whispered. "I just like to watch you write. Your stories look so pretty."

"Would you like to write with me?" asked Ruby, patting the bench with her paw.

Maya's whiskers twitched nervously. "Oh no, I can't write. I'm too small. My paws make messy marks."

Ruby's ears perked up. "Everyone can write stories! Come sit with me."

Maya climbed up slowly. The bench felt smooth and warm from the sunshine. Ruby opened her notebook to a fresh page.

"What do you like to do?" Ruby asked.

"I love to climb and explore tiny spaces," Maya said, her voice getting stronger.

"That sounds like a wonderful story!" Ruby handed Maya a yellow crayon. "Tell me about your favorite climbing adventure."

Maya held the crayon carefully. "Well, yesterday I found a secret tunnel behind the mailboxes..."

As Maya talked, Ruby helped her make marks on the paper. Some lines were wobbly. Some circles were lopsided. But together, they created Maya's very first story.

"Look what we made!" Ruby said, pointing to their colorful page.

Soon, Oliver the owl landed on the rooftop railing. His feathers were speckled brown and white.

"What are you two doing?" Oliver hooted curiously.

"We're writing stories!" said Maya proudly. "Want to join us?"

Oliver shuffled his feet. "I don't know how to hold a crayon with my wings."

Ruby thought for a moment. "You could tell us a story, and we'll help you write it down!"

Oliver's round eyes grew bright. "I know lots of stories about flying at night!"

The three friends spent the morning sharing stories. Ruby wrote with her purple crayon. Maya drew pictures with yellow. Oliver told amazing tales about moonlight and stars.

When the sun was high and warm, they looked at their notebook filled with colorful stories and drawings.

"This is the best writing club ever!" said Maya, clapping her tiny paws.

"Everyone has special stories to share," said Ruby. "We just need to help each other find ways to tell them."

Oliver spread his wings wide. "Tomorrow I'll bring my friend Sam the squirrel. He loves to tell jokes!"

"And I'll bring my sister Lucy," said Maya. "She knows songs about cheese!"

Ruby smiled, her heart feeling as warm as the sunshine. "Our rooftop writing club is for everyone. No matter how big or small, we all have important stories inside us."

The friends agreed to meet every morning to write and share together. The rooftop garden became their special place where every voice mattered and every story was welcome.

From that day on, the rooftop was filled with laughter, friendship, and the most wonderful stories ever told.

The End.
