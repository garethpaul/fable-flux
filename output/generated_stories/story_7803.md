# Bubbles the Dolphin Sets a Boundary

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash in the sink and sing little “click-click” songs. One rainy day, Bubbles visited a bright laundry room. The room smelled like clean soap and warm cotton. The washing machine hummed, “whirr-whirr,” and the dryer made a soft “thump-thump.” Warm air puffed like a gentle hug.

Bubbles swam in a big blue laundry basket. “Wheee!” he squeaked. “This is my splash spot!” He liked the soft towels and the tickly socks. A striped sock felt like a tiny blanket, and a pink towel smelled like flowers.

Just then, his friends peeked in. Tilly the turtle rolled in slowly. Pepe the parrot flapped in with a colorful whoosh. Ziggy the zigzag snail slid along with a shiny trail. They all liked to play, too.

“Let’s make a towel fort!” Pepe squawked.

“Let’s spin, spin, spin!” Ziggy giggled, looking at the round washer window.

Tilly blinked. “Let’s play… but gently,” she said in a calm voice.

Bubbles bounced in the basket. He loved his friends. He loved games. But he also loved his cozy splash spot. It felt safe and calm. Bubbles wanted to keep his splash spot just for him.

Pepe flapped to the basket. “I’ll jump in!” he said.

Bubbles felt a pinch in his tummy. His tail twitched. His heart went thump-thump like the dryer. He wanted to say yes, because he liked to share. But he also needed space. He needed a boundary.

Bubbles took a slow bubble-breath. In… pop-pop-pop. Out… swish-swish. He remembered a plan he made with Tilly: When you need space, use your kind words and your Dolphin Plan.

“Friends,” Bubbles said, using a gentle voice, “this blue basket is my splash spot. I need it to be just for me. Please do not jump in.”

Pepe paused. “But I want to play with you,” he said, wings drooping.

“We can play,” Bubbles said. “Let’s think and make a plan.” He wiggled his fin and smiled. “Goal: We will play a fun game. Boundary: My splash spot stays just for me.”

Tilly nodded. “That’s a good plan. I can help write it.” She found a little chalkboard by the dryer. It felt dusty and cool. She wrote:

1. Bubbles keeps his splash spot.
2. We play a laundry room game.
3. We take turns and ask first.

Ziggy’s eyes sparkled. “What game fits the plan?”

Bubbles listened to the washer’s “swish-swish.” He sniffed the clean soap and rubbed a soft towel under his chin. He got an idea. “Soap Bubble Ring Toss!” he chirped. “I blow bubble rings. You toss soft socks through. We cheer for each other!”

“That sounds silly and safe,” Tilly said. “And we will ask before touching things.”

Pepe perked up. “Can I stand on the rug, not in your basket?”

“Yes,” said Bubbles. “Thank you for asking.”

They set their goal: Make five sock tosses through bubble rings. They made a simple plan:
- Count to three before each toss.
- Ask, “Ready?” and wait for “Ready!” back.
- Keep Bubbles’ splash spot just for Bubbles.

“Ready?” Pepe asked, holding a tiny striped sock. His feathers felt soft against the sock.

“Ready!” Bubbles clicked. He blew a shiny bubble ring. It smelled faintly like soap and popped with a gentle “pup!”

Pepe tossed. The sock whooshed through the ring and landed with a plop on a warm towel. Everyone cheered, “Hooray!”

Ziggy took a turn. “Ready?” he whispered.

“Ready!” Bubbles said. Bubble ring! Toss! Plop! Ziggy giggled. “That was squishy!”

Tilly went next. “Ready?”

“Ready!” Bubble ring! Toss! The sock made a soft “fwump” and smelled like lavender. Tilly smiled. “We are meeting our goal.”

Pepe looked at the basket again. “Bubbles, can I touch your splash spot?”

Bubbles shook his head kindly. “No, thank you. I need my splash spot just for me.” He put a small sign on the basket that said, “Bubbles’ Spot.” The sign felt smooth in his fin. He placed it gently so everyone could see.

Pepe nodded. “Thanks for telling me. I will not touch it.”

They reached their goal with five happy tosses. Then they set a new goal: Clean up together. They planned who would do what:
- Bubbles stacked towels.
- Tilly folded washcloths.
- Ziggy gathered socks like tiny noodles.
- Pepe hung the sign nicely on a hook.

“Ready?” Tilly asked.

“Ready!” they all said.

Swish, fold, pick, hang. The laundry room looked neat again. The warm air felt proud.

Pepe flapped close to Bubbles. “I like your boundary. It helps me know what to do.”

Bubbles smiled and clicked. “Thank you for listening. I feel safe when I share my needs.”

Tilly patted the rug. “Good plan. Good goal. Good friends.”

Ziggy wiggled. “New goal next time: Towel fort in the corner, with signs!”

Bubbles clapped his fins. “Yes! And we will ask first. We will use kind words. We will keep our spots safe.”

They all took bubble-breaths together. In… pop-pop-pop. Out… swish-swish. The washer hummed a soft song. The dryer sighed warm air. The friends felt calm and happy.

Bubbles looked at his splash spot and at his friends. He felt brave and kind. He learned that making a plan and setting a boundary can help everyone play and feel safe.

The End.
