# Rosie Rabbit’s Porch Routine

Rosie Rabbit lived in a cozy burrow with a peaceful front porch. The porch had a squeaky step, a sleepy rocking chair, and a flower box full of mint and marigolds. In the morning, the porch smelled like wet leaves. In the evening, it smelled like warm wood and sweet mint.

Rosie was a determined rabbit. She liked to say, “I can learn! I can try!” But sometimes, she forgot her routine. One windy morning, she hopped outside and saw wrappers rolling like tiny tumbleweeds across the porch. A watering can lay on its side. The flower box was thirsty. Rosie’s broom was hiding under the chair.

“Oh no,” whispered Rosie. Her whiskers drooped. “My porch is messy. My plants are thirsty. I forgot my plan.”

Rosie took a big breath. The air felt cool on her nose. The wind tickled her ears. She put a paw on her chest and said, “I can fix this. I will make a routine.”

Her neighbor, Mr. Toad, peeked over the fence. “Good morning, Rosie,” he croaked. “That wind made a fuss!”

Rosie nodded. “I am making a porch routine, Mr. Toad. I will practice every day.”

Mr. Toad smiled. “A routine is like a steady beat. Hop, hop, sweep. Hop, hop, water.”

Rosie smiled back. “Yes! A steady beat.”

Rosie tapped her paw and made a plan out loud, so she could hear it and feel it.

“Step one: Pick up litter.
Step two: Sweep the porch.
Step three: Water the plants.
Step four: Check the tools.
Step five: Say thank you.”

She said each step slowly. She even made hand motions to match. Her paws made picking shapes for step one. They made sweeping shapes for step two. They made pouring shapes for step three. Rosie liked how her body and words worked together. It felt like a dance.

“Let’s try,” she said.

Step one: Pick up litter. Rosie hopped to a shiny candy wrapper. It crackled in her paw. She picked up a straw that smelled like soda and a napkin that fluttered like a moth. She put them in her little bin. “Good for the Earth,” she whispered. “I care for my porch and my home.”

Step two: Sweep the porch. Rosie pulled out her broom. Swish-swish. Swish-swish. The broom whispered over the wood. Dust danced in the sunlight. Rosie moved her paws left and right, left and right, like a gentle song. She had to practice her coordination. Sometimes she bumped the chair. “Oops,” she giggled. “Try again.” Swish-swish. The squeaky step even squeaked a happy squeak.

Step three: Water the plants. Rosie stood tall, then lifted the watering can. It was a little heavy. Her arms wobbled. “I can practice,” she said. She used both paws and poured slowly. Glug-glug. The soil drank the water. The mint smelled like candy. The marigolds smiled with orange sun faces.

Step four: Check the tools. Rosie wiped the broom and tucked it in its corner. She set the watering can upright and patted it. “See you tomorrow,” she said.

Step five: Say thank you. Rosie closed her eyes and listened. Crickets chirped. Leaves rustled. A soft breeze hugged her cheeks. “Thank you, porch,” she said. “Thank you, plants. Thank you, Earth.” She waved to Mr. Toad. “Thank you for cheering me on.”

“Bravo!” croaked Mr. Toad. He tapped a little beat on the fence. “Routine makes strong habits.”

Rosie felt proud. Her porch looked clean. The air smelled fresh. Her heart felt full.

The next day, the wind blew again. A new wrapper rolled in. Rosie almost hopped past it. But then she remembered her routine. She sang her steps in a tiny song:

“Pick up, sweep-sweep,
Water slow and deep.
Tools away, tools away,
Thank you for today.”

She picked up the wrapper. She swept. She watered. She checked. She said thank you. It took a little time, but each step made her smile. It felt easier than the day before.

On the third day, rain pattered on the porch roof. Pit-pat, pit-pat. Rosie thought, “Maybe I can skip today.” But she was a determined rabbit. She put on a little leaf hat and stepped outside. The porch smelled like rain. She did each step, carefully and slowly. “Practice makes it smooth,” she said.

Mr. Toad clapped. “Your routine is strong, Rosie!”

Rosie blushed under her fur. “It helps me remember. It helps me care for the Earth. It helps my body learn the moves.”

That evening, Mama Rabbit came to the porch. She rocked in the chair and looked around. “Rosie,” she said, “the porch is peaceful and tidy. The flowers look happy. I can smell the mint!”

Rosie’s ears stood tall. “I have a porch routine, Mama. I do it every day. I pick up litter, sweep, water, check tools, and say thank you.”

Mama smiled. “That is a wonderful habit. You are helping our home and our world.”

Rosie curled beside Mama. The sky turned peach and purple. Crickets sang a sleepy song. Rosie whispered, “I feel calm when I follow my routine. I keep practicing, and I get better each day.”

Mama kissed Rosie’s head. “That is the lesson, my dear. Small steps, every day.”

Rosie hugged her knees and grinned. “I can learn! I can try! I can help!”

And the porch, clean and cozy, seemed to whisper back, “Yes, you can.”

The End.
