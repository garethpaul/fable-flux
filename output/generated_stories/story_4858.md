# Mango's Green Mission

Mango the monkey lived in the tallest treehouse in the whole forest. His home was made of strong branches and had windows that looked out at all the green trees below.

One sunny morning, Mango swung down from his treehouse to play. But something was very wrong. The forest floor was covered with shiny wrappers, empty bottles, and crumpled papers.

"Oh no!" Mango gasped. His brown eyes grew wide. "Who made this mess in our beautiful forest?"

The colorful trash sparkled in the sunlight, but it didn't belong here. Mango's furry hands touched a plastic bottle. It felt cold and hard, not warm and soft like the tree bark he loved.

Mango's tummy felt heavy with worry. "The animals won't have clean places to drink water," he said to himself. "And the pretty flowers might get sick from all this trash."

He scratched his head with his long fingers. This was a big problem for such a little monkey. But Mango was brave and wanted to help his forest friends.

"I know what to do!" Mango declared. He climbed back up to his treehouse and found his favorite red wagon. It creaked as he pulled it down the rope ladder.

First, Mango picked up all the bottles. *Clink, clink, clink* they went as he dropped them in his wagon. The sound echoed through the quiet trees.

Next came the paper wrappers. They crinkled in his hands like autumn leaves. Some were stuck under rocks, so Mango had to be very careful and gentle.

"Whew!" Mango wiped his forehead. His arms were getting tired, but he didn't give up. He took three deep breaths to calm himself down, just like his mama taught him.

A little rabbit hopped over. "What are you doing, Mango?"

"I'm cleaning our forest home," Mango explained proudly. "Want to help?"

Soon, a wise old owl flew down. Then a family of squirrels joined in. Even a slow turtle came to help. Working together felt much easier and more fun.

"Look how shiny the bottles are!" chirped the owl.

"These papers could be recycled into new things," squeaked the smallest squirrel.

Mango organized everything into neat piles. Bottles here, papers there, and food scraps for the compost pile. His coordination was getting better with each piece of trash he sorted.

When they finished, the forest looked beautiful again. The green grass sparkled with morning dew. Colorful flowers stood tall and proud. A clear stream bubbled happily nearby.

"Thank you, Mango!" said all the animals together.

Mango's chest puffed up with pride. "Taking care of our home is everyone's job," he said wisely. "When we work together and don't give up, we can solve big problems."

Back in his treehouse that evening, Mango looked out his window at the clean, happy forest. The sunset painted everything golden and peaceful.

"Tomorrow I'll teach the baby animals how to put trash in the right places," Mango decided. "And I'll make signs to remind everyone to keep our forest clean."

Mango yawned and stretched his tired arms. Helping others and caring for the environment made him feel warm and happy inside. He had learned that even small monkeys can make big differences when they care about their world.

The End.
