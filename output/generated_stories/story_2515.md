# Daisy's Kitchen Adventure

Little Daisy the deer fawn peeked through the window of Grandma Rabbit's warm kitchen. The sweet smell of fresh-baked cookies filled the air, making her tummy rumble loudly.

"Grumble, grumble!" went her belly.

Daisy knocked gently on the door with her small hoof. "Tap, tap, tap!"

"Come in, dear!" called Grandma Rabbit in her kind voice.

Daisy stepped inside, her hooves clicking softly on the clean wooden floor. The kitchen was so cozy and warm! Steam rose from a pot of vegetable soup, and golden cookies cooled on a rack near the window.

"Hello, Grandma Rabbit," said Daisy politely. "Those cookies smell wonderful!"

Grandma Rabbit smiled, but then she noticed something. "Oh my, Daisy! Your hooves are very muddy, and your fur has leaves and dirt all over it."

Daisy looked down at herself. Brown mud covered her legs, and twigs stuck out from her spotted coat. She had been playing in the forest all morning and forgotten to clean up.

"I'm sorry," Daisy said sadly. "I was so excited about the cookies that I forgot to wash."

"I understand, sweetie," said Grandma Rabbit gently. "But I can't let you eat in my clean kitchen when you're all dirty. The germs might make us sick."

Daisy's ears drooped. She really wanted to share the warm cookies with her friend.

"What if I help you get clean?" suggested Grandma Rabbit. "Then we can enjoy the cookies together!"

Daisy's eyes lit up. "Really? You would help me?"

"Of course! That's what friends do," Grandma Rabbit said warmly.

Grandma Rabbit filled a basin with warm, soapy water that smelled like lavender. "This will feel nice and cozy," she said.

Daisy stepped carefully into the basin. The warm water felt wonderful on her muddy hooves! Grandma Rabbit used a soft brush to gently clean the dirt from Daisy's legs.

"Splash, splash!" went the water as they worked together.

Next, Grandma Rabbit helped pick the leaves and twigs from Daisy's fur. "There we go! You look beautiful and clean now."

Daisy looked at herself in the shiny pot. Her spots sparkled, and her hooves gleamed! "Thank you so much, Grandma Rabbit! You're so kind to help me."

"Now," said Grandma Rabbit, "would you like to help me finish making lunch? We could share it together."

"Yes, please!" Daisy said happily.

Daisy carefully stirred the soup with a long wooden spoon while Grandma Rabbit sliced fresh bread. They worked side by side, chatting and laughing.

"You know," said Daisy thoughtfully, "you were so generous to help me get clean instead of sending me away."

"And you're being generous too," replied Grandma Rabbit, "by helping me cook even though you were hungry."

Soon they sat down at the little table together. They shared the warm soup, crusty bread, and sweet cookies. Everything tasted extra delicious because they had made it together.

"This is the best lunch ever!" said Daisy, her tummy full and happy.

"It tastes better when we share it with friends," agreed Grandma Rabbit.

From that day on, Daisy always remembered to clean up before visiting friends. And whenever someone needed help, she thought of Grandma Rabbit's kindness and offered to help too.

Being generous and clean made every day brighter!

The End.
