# Melody's Messy Day

Melody the little blue bird loved to sing in the cozy nursery. She perched on the white wooden crib and chirped happy songs all day long.

"Tweet-tweet-tweet!" she sang sweetly. The soft yellow walls made her voice sound even prettier.

But today, something was wrong. Baby Emma's toys were scattered everywhere! Colorful blocks covered the fluffy carpet. Stuffed animals lay in messy piles. Books were open all around the room.

"Oh my!" Melody chirped. "This nursery is so messy. How can I sing my best songs in here?"

Melody tried to sing anyway. "Tweet-tweet-OH!" She bumped into a dangling mobile. The little stars and moons jingled loudly.

"This won't do," Melody said firmly. She puffed up her tiny chest. "I need to speak up about this mess!"

When Mama came in to check on Baby Emma, Melody sang her loudest song. "TWEET-TWEET-TWEET!"

"What's wrong, little bird?" Mama asked gently. She noticed Melody flying around the messy room.

Melody chirped and pointed her wing at the scattered toys. She flew to a block, then to a book, then to a stuffed bear.

"Oh!" Mama smiled. "You want to help organize the nursery!"

Together, they began to clean. Melody picked up small things in her beak. The wooden blocks felt smooth and cool. She placed them carefully in their red toy box.

"Good job, Melody!" Mama said. "You're very helpful."

Melody felt proud. Her feathers sparkled in the warm sunlight streaming through the window. She discovered that organizing was actually fun!

Next, they put the soft stuffed animals on the toy shelf. Melody loved how fluffy the teddy bear felt against her feathers. The books went back on their special rack, standing tall and neat.

"Tweet-tweet!" Melody sang happily. She found a lost rattle under the changing table. It made a gentle shaking sound when she carried it.

Soon, the nursery looked beautiful again. The carpet was clear and soft. The toys were in their proper places. Everything smelled fresh and clean.

"Now I can sing my very best songs!" Melody chirped. She perched on the crib rail and began to sing.

Her voice sounded clearer than ever in the organized room. The melody echoed perfectly off the tidy walls. Baby Emma clapped her tiny hands and giggled.

"Thank you for speaking up about the mess," Mama said. "And thank you for helping organize everything."

Melody felt warm and happy inside. She had been brave enough to show the problem. She had worked hard to fix it. And she had discovered that a clean, organized space made everything better.

"Tweet-tweet-tweet!" she sang a special celebration song. The nursery felt peaceful and cozy again.

From that day on, Melody made sure to speak up whenever the nursery got messy. She learned that being organized helped her sing better, feel happier, and take better care of Baby Emma.

And every evening, as the golden sunset filled the tidy nursery, Melody sang the most beautiful lullabies for Baby Emma to sleep.

The End.
