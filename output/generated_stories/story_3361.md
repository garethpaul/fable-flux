# Otter’s Morning on the Balcony Garden

Plip-plop! A playful otter named Ollie padded onto the sunny balcony garden. His little paws were damp from a splash in his water bowl. He giggled and sniffed the air. It smelled like mint and warm soil. Tiny tomato plants stood in a row, and pots of basil swayed in a soft breeze. Bees buzzed, “Bzz-bzz,” and a bluebird sang, “Tweet-tweet!”

“Good morning, garden!” Ollie chirped. He stretched his body long like a noodle. “Stretch, breathe, and drink water,” he said, tapping his chest. “That is my healthy start!”

“Who taught you that?” asked a gentle voice.

Grandma Otter popped out from behind a pot of lavender. She wore a green sunhat and a smile that felt like a hug.

“You did!” Ollie beamed. “Our morning steps: sip, stretch, and breathe.”

Grandma nodded. “That is our family tradition. My grandma taught me too.” She handed him a tiny watering can shaped like a fish. “Today, you can be the garden helper.”

Ollie’s whiskers wiggled. “I will take initiative! I can do it.”

They made a plan together:
- First, water the thirsty plants.
- Then, pick herbs for lunch.
- Last, sing a garden song while they work.

Ollie lifted the watering can. “Glug-glug,” went the water. “Thank you, water,” Ollie whispered. “You help us grow strong.” He sprinkled soil with a gentle hand. The air felt cool and damp on his fur.

Suddenly, the bluebird fluttered and squawked, “Tweet!” Ollie looked up. A big gray cloud rolled in. A chilly wind brushed his whiskers. “Uh-oh,” Ollie said. “It might rain hard.”

Grandma peered at the sky. “We need to protect the baby sprouts.”

Ollie’s eyes shone. “I can problem-solve! I have an idea.” He ran to the shelf and brought back a small, clear cover and some cloth clips. “We can make a cozy plant tent.”

“Wonderful initiative,” Grandma said.

Together they clipped the cover over the sprout box. “Click, click,” went the clips. The rain began, patter-patter, soft at first, then stronger. The little tent kept the sprouts safe and warm.

The wind made a low “whooo,” and the mint leaves danced. Ollie shivered. “I feel chilly.”

“What does your body need?” Grandma asked softly.

Ollie paused and took a slow breath. He put a paw on his belly. “I need to move and warm up.” He started their family stretch: reach to the sky, touch toes, big circle arms. “My body feels better when I move,” he said.

Grandma clapped. “Healthy habits help us feel good.”

The rain slowed to a sprinkle. The balcony smelled fresh, like clean water and mint tea. Ollie took a small sip from his cup. “Sip, stretch, breathe,” he hummed.

“Now, for herbs,” Grandma said. “In our family, we make a crunchy green snack.” She showed him a woven basket. “My grandma called it ‘Garden Crunch.’ We pick basil and mint, wash them well, and toss them with lemon.”

Ollie’s eyes sparkled. He loved family traditions. “I want to help.”

He picked leaves carefully. “Pick, pick,” he whispered, using gentle paws. He rinsed the herbs in a bowl. Swish, swish. Cool water tickled his fingers. He patted the leaves dry with a soft cloth. “I’m taking care,” he said proudly.

They added tiny tomato halves and a squeeze of lemon. “Squeeeze!” The scent was bright and sunny. Ollie took a bite. Crunch! His face lit up. “Mmm! Fresh and zippy!” His tummy felt happy.

Grandma laughed. “Food that helps us grow also tastes good.”

Ollie picked up a wooden spoon. “Let’s sing the garden song!” he said. He tapped the spoon on a pot—tink, tink, tink. The bluebird added “tweet-tweet,” and the rain kept a soft beat. Grandma hummed, and Ollie sang:

“Water, stretch, and tasty greens,
Clean paws, kind hearts, happy scenes.
Help and share, we do our part,
Healthy habits, start with heart!”

They grinned at each other. Music made the work feel fun and easy.

Then Ollie noticed something: one basil pot was droopy. “Oh no,” he said. “It looks sad.” He knelt and checked the soil. “Too dry.” He felt with his finger. “It needs a slow drink.”

Ollie filled the fish can and counted, “One… two… three.” He watered just enough. He turned the pot so it got soft sunlight. “There. I listened and helped,” he said.

Grandma touched his shoulder. “You showed initiative and problem-solving. The plant will perk up soon.”

As the clouds drifted away, a warm sunbeam landed on the balcony. The basil leaves lifted, just a little. Ollie smiled. He felt calm and strong.

“What did we learn today?” Grandma asked.

Ollie lifted one paw, then another, counting:
- “Drink water, stretch, and breathe to start the day.”
- “Wash food and paws to stay clean.”
- “Listen to our bodies and our plants.”
- “Share family traditions.”
- “Take initiative and help right away.”

Grandma hugged him. “Yes. Healthy habits make happy days.”

Ollie wiggled with joy. “Tomorrow, can we make Garden Crunch again?”

“Tomorrow,” Grandma said, “and we can teach our song to a friend.”

Ollie waved to the bluebird. “Tweet you later!” The balcony smelled like lemon and mint. The plants stood tall. His heart felt light.

The End.
