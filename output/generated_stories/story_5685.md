# Penny the Parrot Learns to Forgive

In a bright, colorful art room filled with paintbrushes, crayons, and paper, lived a beautiful green parrot named Penny. Every morning, Penny loved to dip her feathers in paint and create wonderful pictures on big white papers.

"Good morning, art room!" Penny would squawk happily each day. She had a special routine: first she would organize her paints, then practice writing her letters, and finally paint a beautiful picture.

One sunny Tuesday, Penny was carefully writing the letter "A" with her favorite red paint when her friend Benny the bunny hopped into the art room.

"Hi Penny!" Benny called out excitedly. He was so happy to see his friend that he accidentally bumped into Penny's easel.

SPLASH! The red paint spilled all over Penny's perfect letter "A" and dripped onto her beautiful green feathers.

"Oh no!" cried Penny, her feathers now sticky and red. "My letter! My feathers! Everything is ruined!"

Benny's ears drooped down sadly. "I'm so sorry, Penny. I didn't mean to bump into your easel. I was just excited to see you."

Penny felt very upset. Her daily routine was broken, and she looked quite messy. "You should be more careful!" she squawked angrily. "Now I can't finish my writing practice!"

Benny's eyes filled with tears. "I really am sorry, Penny. Can I help you clean up?"

But Penny was too upset to listen. She flew to the corner of the art room and wouldn't talk to Benny anymore.

For the rest of the morning, Penny sat alone while Benny quietly cleaned up the spilled paint. The art room felt sad and quiet without their usual laughter and chatting.

As Penny watched Benny carefully wiping up every drop of red paint, she began to feel something warm in her heart. She remembered all the fun times they had shared in the art room - painting rainbows together, making handprint art, and laughing at silly pictures.

"Benny really is trying to help," Penny thought to herself. "And he said he was sorry. Maybe accidents happen sometimes."

Penny took a deep breath and flew down to where Benny was cleaning. "Benny," she said softly, "I forgive you. I know you didn't mean to spill my paint."

Benny looked up with hopeful eyes. "Really? You forgive me?"

"Yes," Penny nodded. "Friends make mistakes sometimes. The important thing is that you said sorry and helped clean up."

Benny smiled the biggest bunny smile. "Thank you for forgiving me, Penny. You're the best friend ever!"

Together, they cleaned Penny's feathers with warm, soapy water until they were bright green again. Then Benny helped Penny start a new letter "A" - this time even more beautiful than before.

"You know what?" said Penny, dipping her feather in blue paint. "Maybe we can write the whole alphabet together!"

"That sounds wonderful!" said Benny, picking up a yellow crayon.

From that day on, Penny learned that forgiving friends made her heart feel light and happy. Even when accidents happened, understanding and forgiveness could make everything better. And their art room was filled with even more joy and laughter than before.

The End.
