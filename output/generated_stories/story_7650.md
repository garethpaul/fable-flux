# Pebble the Penguin’s Paint Day

The big clock on the wall went tick-tock, tick-tock. Pebble the cheerful penguin waddled into the creative art room. The room smelled like fresh paper and sweet glue. Sunlight sparkled on jars of paint—red like strawberries, blue like the sky, yellow like warm sand. Brushes stood tall like tiny trees.

Pebble clapped his flippers. “I will make a rainbow poster today!” he chirped. “I will finish before snack time!”

Ms. Dot, the kind art helper, smiled. She wore a bright apron with paint dots. “Hello, Pebble. Safety first,” she said gently. “Apron on, goggles on when using glitter, and soft feet, no running.”

Pebble nodded. “Apron on!” He wiggled into a small blue apron. “Soft feet!” He waddled softly. His feet made little tap-tap sounds.

He spread a big paper on the table. It felt smooth like ice. Pebble picked up a brush. “Red first,” he sang. He dipped the brush into the red paint. Swish, swish. A bold red stripe appeared.

“Now orange! Now yellow! I will be so fast,” Pebble said. He looked at the clock. Tick-tock. “I can finish before snack.”

He reached for orange. Oops! The brush slipped. Plop! A red dot fell on Pebble’s flipper. “Oh,” said Pebble. He wiped it on a rag. “It’s okay. I can do it.”

He painted orange. He painted yellow. He hummed a happy tune. But when he tried green, the brush bristles stuck together. The paint made a squelchy sound. “Squelch. Ew.” The green stripe looked bumpy and lumpy.

Pebble’s smile wobbled. “It’s not smooth. I want it smooth!” He pressed harder. The brush bent. The stripe smeared. “Oh no!”

His tummy felt tight. His face felt hot. “I can’t do it!” Pebble blurted. He stomped one little foot. The water cup wobbled and wiggled—then—splash! Water fell on the corner of the paper.

Pebble’s eyes grew shiny. “My rainbow!” he whispered. His flippers drooped.

Ms. Dot came close on soft feet. “I see big feelings,” she said calmly. “Let’s keep safe. Brushes down. Hands on heart.”

Pebble placed his flippers on his chest. “Thump-thump,” he felt. Ms. Dot breathed in slow. “Smell the flower.” She breathed out slow. “Blow the candle.”

Pebble copied. In: sniff. Out: blow. In: sniff. Out: blow. His tummy loosened. His shoulders relaxed. “I feel a little better,” he said.

“Good coping,” said Ms. Dot. “Frustration is okay. It means you care. We can try again. We can also make a plan.”

“A plan?” Pebble blinked.

Ms. Dot pointed to a small sign that said Plan, Pace, Pause. “Plan what to do. Pace your steps. Pause for breaks.”

Pebble nodded. “Plan: First red to yellow. Then green to purple. Pace: Slow and gentle. Pause: Drink water, stretch, breathe.”

“Great plan,” said Ms. Dot. “And safety: wipe spills, keep cups back from the edge.”

Pebble got a towel. Wipe, wipe. He cleaned the water. “Safe table,” he said. He moved the cup back. He washed his brush until the water swirled light. Swirl, swirl.

He started again on a new paper. Swish red. Smooth orange. Soft yellow. He paused. “Sip of water.” He smiled. “Pace.”

For green, he dipped just the tip. He dragged the brush lightly. “Soft like a feather,” he whispered. The stripe looked smooth. Pebble giggled. “Green like grass!”

For blue, the paint felt cool and silky. “Blue like the ocean,” he sang. For purple, he mixed a tiny bit of blue and red on a palette. “Tap, tap, mix.” The purple looked like grape jelly. “Yum!” he said, but he did not eat it. “Paint is for paper,” he reminded himself.

The clock went tick-tock. Pebble glanced up. Snack time was coming soon. He felt a little flutter. “Plan, pace, pause,” he told himself. He took one slow breath. He painted the last stripe with care.

He stepped back. “My rainbow!” The colors shone bright and smooth. Little dots of sparkle glitter sat on the shelf. Pebble reached for them.

Ms. Dot held up goggles. “Glitter safety,” she said. Pebble slid them on. “Ready.” He sprinkled a tiny shake. Tinkle-tinkle, the glitter fell like tiny stars. He put the lid back on. “Safety,” he said proudly.

“Pebble,” Ms. Dot said, “you worked hard. You felt frustrated. You made a plan. You paced your work. You paused to breathe. That is a growth mindset!”

Pebble’s chest puffed with happy warmth. “I didn’t give up,” he chirped. “I can try, even when it’s hard.”

Friends waddled over. “Ooo!” said Tilly. “It’s so shiny!” said Finn. Pebble smiled. “I had a big feeling. I breathed. I made a plan. You can do it too!”

The bell chimed for snack. Pebble washed his hands, put brushes in the rinse cup, and hung up his apron. “All clean,” he said. He looked at his rainbow one more time. It smelled like paper and a tiny bit like lemons from the soap. It made his heart feel light.

At the snack table, Pebble munched crunchy apple slices. “Next time,” he said, “I will make a sunset. I will plan, pace, and pause.”

Ms. Dot nodded. “And stay safe,” she added.

Pebble giggled. “And stay safe.”

He wiggled with joy. He had learned something big: When frustration comes, breathe, make a plan, go slow, take breaks, and keep trying.

The End.
