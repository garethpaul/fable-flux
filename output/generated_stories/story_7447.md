# Splash the Dolphin's Clean Discovery

Splash the dolphin loved to explore new places. Today, she found herself in Dr. Marina's special basement lab, where the kind scientist studied ocean creatures from around the world.

"Oh my!" squeaked Splash, looking around with her big, curious eyes. The lab had colorful tanks filled with water plants from different countries. There were soft blue lights that made everything glow like the deep sea.

Dr. Marina smiled when she saw Splash. "Hello there, little friend! Welcome to my lab where I help sea creatures stay healthy and clean."

Splash noticed something interesting. In one corner, a small octopus from Japan was washing his tentacles very carefully. In another tank, a colorful fish from Brazil was swimming through a stream of bubbles that cleaned her scales.

"Why is everyone cleaning themselves?" asked Splash, tilting her head.

Dr. Marina knelt down to Splash's level. "Just like you brush your teeth and wash your hands, sea creatures need to stay clean too! It keeps them healthy and happy."

Splash watched as a wise old turtle from Australia showed a young seahorse how to use the cleaning station. The turtle's shell sparkled in the light.

"I want to try!" said Splash bravely, even though she felt a little nervous.

Dr. Marina clapped her hands. "That's wonderful! Being curious and trying new things takes courage."

She led Splash to a special cleaning area with gentle brushes and warm, flowing water. "First, we'll clean your beautiful gray skin," Dr. Marina explained. "Then we'll make sure your blowhole is clear so you can breathe perfectly."

Splash giggled as the soft brushes tickled her sides. "This feels nice!" The warm water made her skin tingle in the best way.

A friendly sea turtle named Shelly swam over. "In my home in the Great Barrier Reef, little fish help clean my shell every day," she said with a kind smile. "It's like having friends help you stay healthy!"

Next, Dr. Marina showed Splash how to clean her teeth with a special sea-safe brush. "Dolphins have many teeth, just like sharks do," she explained. "Keeping them clean helps you catch fish and smile brightly!"

Splash practiced opening her mouth wide and gently brushing. "Look at me!" she chirped proudly.

A colorful parrotfish from the Caribbean cheered, "¡Muy bien! Very good! In my coral reef home, we clean the coral while we eat. Everyone helps everyone stay clean!"

As Splash finished her cleaning routine, she felt amazing. Her skin was smooth and shiny, her teeth sparkled white, and she felt fresh and energetic.

"Now I understand!" Splash said excitedly. "Staying clean isn't just about looking good – it helps me feel strong and healthy!"

Dr. Marina nodded proudly. "Exactly! And look how brave you were to try something new. Your curiosity led you to learn something very important."

All the sea creatures from different parts of the world gathered around Splash. They shared stories about how they stayed clean in their ocean homes, and Splash learned that keeping clean was important everywhere in the world.

"Thank you for teaching me," Splash said, giving Dr. Marina a gentle hug with her fin. "I'll remember to stay clean every day!"

As Splash swam away, she felt proud and confident. She had been curious, shown courage, and learned that staying clean was a wonderful way to take care of herself – just like sea creatures do all around the world.

The End.
