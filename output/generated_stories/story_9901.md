# The Pantry Party Letter

Bunny Roo was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She loved to hop, learn, and help. One sunny morning, Bunny Roo opened the pantry door. The pantry was cool and quiet. It smelled like warm oats and sweet strawberries. Jars clinked softly. Paper bags rustled. It felt like a little, cozy room full of crunchy, munchy secrets.

Bunny Roo whispered, “Hello, pantry.” Her whiskers twitched. “Today, I am brave. Today, I will write a letter.”

Bunny Roo wanted to invite everyone to a Snack Share Party. She wanted all friends to come, big and small, fast and slow, furry and feathery. “Everyone can snack. Everyone can smile,” she said. “No one gets left out.”

She found a big box of cereal and a small box of raisins. She found a roll of paper towels. “This will be my paper!” she giggled. She tugged and tugged. Rrrrrip! A long, soft sheet rolled out like a blanket of clouds.

Bunny Roo dipped a carrot into a tiny bowl of purple berry juice. “Carrot can be my pen,” she said. She pressed the carrot on the paper towel. Splot! The juice made a bright, sweet smell. Bunny Roo smiled. She began to write.

“Come to the Snack Share Party. Everyone is welcome.”

Clink. Clink. A jar lid wobbled. Out popped Pickle, the plump pickle. “Pickle is here!” he sang in a squeaky voice. “What are you writing?”

Bunny Roo took a brave breath. “I’m writing an invite. I want everyone to come.”

Pickle blinked. “Even me? I am juicy and sour.”

Bunny Roo nodded. “Yes. Sour is welcome. Sweet is welcome. Crunchy and soft are welcome.”

Rustle. Rustle. A bag of chips shimmied. Chip the chip slid out. “I am loud,” Chip crackled. “I crunch and crumble.”

“Loud is okay,” said Bunny Roo kindly. “We can listen to each other. We can take turns.”

Thump. A big potato rolled near. “I am heavy,” said Potato in a low voice. “I move slow.”

“We will move at your pace,” said Bunny Roo. “Slow is welcome.”

The raisins peeped, “We are tiny.” The rice whispered, “We are quiet.” The apple sauce hummed, “I am wiggly.” The oat box murmured, “I make dust.”

Bunny Roo’s ears stood tall. “Tiny is welcome. Quiet is welcome. Wiggly is welcome. Dusty is welcome. Everyone is welcome.”

She dipped her carrot pen again and wrote more. “We share. We care. We use kind words. We listen.”

Pickle leaned in too close. The paper towel got a wet spot. The purple letters blurred. “Oh no,” said Pickle, “I made a mess.”

Bunny Roo felt a wobble in her tummy. She wanted to shout, but she was brave and calm. She used peaceful words. “It’s okay, Pickle. Accidents happen. Please step back a little. We can fix it together.” She blotted the wet spot with a napkin. Dab, dab. The letters came back. “See?” she said, smiling.

Chip bounced and sprinkled crumbs. Crick, crackle, crumbles rained down. “I can’t help it!” Chip cried.

Bunny Roo nodded. “Thank you for telling me. Let’s make a crumb corner.” She put a small plate nearby. “Chip, can you crunch over the plate?” Chip tried. Crackle, crunch—plop! The crumbs fell on the plate. “I did it!” Chip cheered.

Potato sighed. “I want to help, but I am slow.”

Bunny Roo patted Potato gently. “We have time. Maybe you can hold the paper steady.” Potato pressed his round body on the edge. The paper stayed flat. “You are a big helper,” said Bunny Roo.

Raisin twins tugged her ear. “We cannot see the letters,” they peeped.

Bunny Roo made the words bigger. She said them out loud while she wrote. “Come. Snack. Share. Please.” Her voice was warm and clear. The raisins beamed. “Now we can read with our ears!”

Bunny Roo added pictures: a smiling pickle, a kind potato, loud chips, tiny raisins, soft applesauce, and a brave bunny. She drew big hearts all around. “Hearts mean care,” she said.

A little jar of honey rolled near and said softly, “I am sticky. People might not want me.”

Bunny Roo looked honey in the eyes. “We want you. We can use a spoon and a napkin. Sticky is welcome, too.” Honey felt warm and golden inside.

When the letter was done, Bunny Roo read it aloud. Her voice was bright and gentle.

“Snack Share Party.
Everyone is welcome.
We share. We care.
We take turns.
We listen.
We help each other.”

The pantry friends clapped and clinked. Clap, clink, rustle, rumble! Bunny Roo taped the letter to the pantry wall. The tape made a soft sssst.

“Now,” she said, “let’s practice.” She passed a small bowl. “Chip goes first.” Chip crunched one chip and passed the bowl. “Thank you,” Chip said. Pickle took a tiny bite and passed it on. “Thank you,” Pickle said. Potato took a slow nibble. “Thank you,” Potato said. Honey shared a sweet drop with a clean spoon. “Thank you,” everyone said.

They took turns. They used kind words. When a crumb fell, someone helped. When someone was slow, everyone waited. When someone was loud, others listened. Every friend felt seen. Every friend felt safe. Every friend felt included.

Bunny Roo’s heart felt big and light. She was a determined rabbit, and she had used courage and writing to make a kind, cozy party. She looked around the pantry, full of smells and sounds and smiles.

“We did it together,” she said. “Everyone belongs.”

The End.
