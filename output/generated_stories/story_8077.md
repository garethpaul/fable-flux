# Ellie Paints and Plays

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and bright, curious eyes. She loved the creative art room at school. The room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Colorful brushes stood in jars like tiny trees. Sunlight danced on the floor, making warm, golden squares.

“Good morning, Ellie,” said Ms. Star, the smiling teacher. “Today we paint and play sports!”

Ellie’s ears flapped happily. “Paint and sports?” she asked, her trunk wiggling. “That sounds fun!”

On a long table, there were jars of red, blue, yellow, and green paint. There were soft balls in a basket too—striped, bouncy, and light. The balls felt squishy when Ellie squeezed one with her trunk. Squeak! It made a funny sound that made Ellie giggle.

“First, we paint our team flags,” said Ms. Star. “Then, we try gentle games. Remember, we all try our best. We all cheer for each other.”

Ellie dipped her brush in blue paint. Swish, swish. She painted a big heart and a tiny ball. Her friends painted too. Sam the giraffe painted tall stripes. Nia the kitten painted tiny stars. Ben the bunny made dots, dot, dots.

Ellie looked at the balls. “But I am big,” she said softly. “I might be too strong for these games.”

Ms. Star knelt beside her. “You are gentle, Ellie,” she said. “Your body is big and strong, and that can help. We each have different bodies. Different is good.”

Ellie took a slow breath. She listened to the quiet hum of the classroom and felt the cool brush in her trunk. She liked being gentle.

“Time for snack before we play,” said Ms. Star. “Healthy food helps our bodies move.”

They washed hands at the sink first. “Scrub, scrub,” said Ms. Star. “Front and back, in between.” The warm water felt nice. The soap smelled like lemons. Ellie scrubbed her trunk and her big hands until they were clean and shiny.

At the snack table, there were orange slices, crunchy carrots, banana pieces, and cups of cool water. “Crunch,” went the carrot in Ellie’s mouth. Juice dripped sweetly from the orange. “Mmm,” she hummed. Her tummy felt happy.

“Good fuel,” said Ms. Star. “Now we’re ready!”

They carried their painted flags to the play area in the art room. Tape marked lines on the floor. There were cones made from rolled paper. The room looked like a tiny, colorful stadium.

“We will try three games,” said Ms. Star. “Ball roll, gentle toss, and balance walk.”

Ellie’s ears twitched. She wanted to try, but her feet felt heavy. What if she knocked things over? What if she was too slow?

“Ellie, would you like to go first?” asked Ms. Star.

Ellie shook her head. “I feel shy,” she whispered.

“That’s okay,” said Ms. Star. “You can watch and then try.”

Sam rolled a ball with his long legs. Thump-roll, thump-roll. It bumped a cone. “Oops!” he said, and laughed.

Nia tossed a tiny ball into a hoop. Swish! “I did it!” she cheered.

Ben walked on a line of tape, arms out. “Balance… balance…” He wobbled and then steadied.

Ellie watched and clapped for each friend. Clap, clap, clap. Her heart felt warm. “I want to try now,” she said.

She started with ball roll. Ellie bent her knees and used her trunk to push the ball softly. “Gentle,” she reminded herself. The ball rolled smooth and straight. It kissed the cone and stopped. “I did it!” Ellie giggled. Her friends cheered, “Go, Ellie!”

Next was gentle toss. Ellie held a soft ball with her trunk. She aimed at the hoop. “Slow and soft,” she whispered. Whoosh. The ball sailed in. Swish! Ellie’s eyes went wide. “I can be gentle and strong,” she said proudly.

Last was balance walk. The tape felt a little sticky under her big feet. Tap, tap. Ellie spread her ears like wings. She took one step, then another. Wobble… steady. She reached the end and raised her flag high. “Team Ellie!” she laughed.

“Team All of Us,” said Ms. Star. “We all did great in our own ways.”

Ellie looked at her friends. Sam was tall and fast. Nia was tiny and quick. Ben was bouncy and brave. Ellie was gentle and strong. They were different, and that made the games more fun.

After playing, they cleaned up. They wiped tables with soft cloths that smelled like clean rain. They washed hands again. “Scrub, scrub,” said Ellie, smiling. She liked the warm water and the bubbles.

Ms. Star gathered everyone in a circle. “What did we learn today?” she asked.

Ellie lifted her trunk. “Sports are fun when we try our best,” she said. “Different bodies can play together. Being gentle can help. Healthy snacks and water give us energy. And clean hands keep us healthy.”

“Wonderful,” said Ms. Star. “You found the solution to your worry. You used gentle moves, and you shined.”

Ellie hugged her flag. Her heart felt big and light. The art room still smelled like crayons and lemons. She could hear her friends giggling and the soft squeak of the balls being put away.

Ellie whispered, “I can paint. I can play. I can be me.”

Her friends cheered one more time. “Go, Team All of Us!”

The End.
