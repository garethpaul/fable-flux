# Benny's Big Decision

Benny the beaver lived in a special basement lab under the old oak tree. He had soft brown fur and a big flat tail. Benny loved to build things with his tiny paws. His lab was full of colorful blocks, shiny buttons, and sparkly wires.

Every morning, Benny woke up excited. "What should I build today?" he would ask himself. But today felt different. Today, Benny had a BIG problem.

Three of his forest friends had asked for his help. Rosie Rabbit wanted a new burrow door. Ollie Owl needed a reading lamp. And Freddy Frog asked for a lily pad boat. All three friends needed help TODAY!

Benny's whiskers twitched with worry. His lab smelled like sawdust and metal. The bright lights buzzed softly overhead. "Oh no!" Benny said, scratching his furry head. "I want to help everyone, but I can only work on one thing at a time."

Benny started running around his lab. He grabbed some wood for Rosie. Then he picked up wires for Ollie. Then he found boat parts for Freddy. His paws were full, and nothing was getting done!

"This isn't working," Benny sighed. He sat down on his favorite thinking stump. The lab felt messy and confusing now.

Just then, wise old Grandpa Beaver climbed down the lab stairs. His gray whiskers sparkled in the light. "What's troubling you, little builder?" he asked kindly.

"I want to help all my friends," Benny explained. "But I can't do everything at once. My paws get mixed up, and nothing gets finished!"

Grandpa Beaver smiled. "Ah, you need to make a good decision. Let me teach you something special." He sat down next to Benny. "Good decision-making means stopping to think first."

"How do I do that?" Benny asked, his big brown eyes wide with curiosity.

"First, take three deep breaths," Grandpa said. Benny breathed in slowly. The lab smelled like pine and metal polish. He felt calmer already.

"Now," Grandpa continued, "think about each friend. Who needs help the most right now?"

Benny thought carefully. "Well, Rosie's burrow door is broken. Rain might get in tonight. Ollie can read during the day. And Freddy can swim without a boat."

"That's excellent thinking!" Grandpa praised. "You used your brain to make a smart choice."

Benny felt proud and excited. "I'll help Rosie first! Then Ollie tomorrow, and Freddy the day after!"

Benny worked carefully on Rosie's door. He measured twice and cut once. His paws felt steady and sure. The hammering sounded like a happy song. When he finished, the door was perfect!

Rosie hopped over to see her new door. "Oh, Benny! It's beautiful and strong!" she squeaked with joy.

The next day, Benny made Ollie's reading lamp. It glowed warm and bright. Ollie hooted with happiness. "Now I can read my favorite stories!"

On the third day, Benny built Freddy's lily pad boat. It floated perfectly on the pond. Freddy croaked with delight. "This is the best boat ever!"

All three friends came to thank Benny together. "You're the most helpful beaver in the forest!" they said.

That evening, Benny sat in his tidy lab. Everything smelled fresh and clean. The lights twinkled like stars. "I learned something important," he told Grandpa Beaver. "When I stop and think, I make better decisions. And when I do one thing at a time, I do my best work!"

Grandpa Beaver patted Benny's head. "That's right, little one. Good decision-making helps us help others better. And everyone gets the care they deserve."

From that day on, whenever Benny felt overwhelmed, he would stop, breathe, and think. He became known as the wisest builder in the whole forest.

The End.
