# Ollie Otter’s Shed Surprise

Ollie was a playful otter with bright eyes and whiskers that tickled when he smiled. One sunny morning, Ollie found a tiny green door on the side of the garden shed. It smelled like fresh wood and sweet soil. He heard a soft hum inside, like bees singing.

Ollie tapped the door with his paw. Knock, knock.

“Hello?” he called. “May I come in?”

The door creaked open. Inside the shed, the air was cool. Sunlight painted golden stripes on the floor. There were flower pots, shiny tools, and a small watering can that smelled like rain.

“Welcome,” chirped a little robin from the windowsill. “I’m Ruby. I like shiny things.”

“Hi, Ruby! I’m Ollie,” said the otter. “What are you doing?”

Ruby pointed with her wing. A jumble of seeds, twine, and tiny labels lay in a heap. “We’re getting ready to plant,” she said. “But we disagree.”

From the shadows, a shy hedgehog rolled forward. “I’m Hazel,” she mumbled. “I like quiet and order.”

A bright green cricket hopped onto a clay pot. “Cricket here! I love music and big jumps!” He chirped a happy note that bounced off the walls.

Ollie’s whiskers wiggled. “What’s the problem?”

Ruby fluffed her feathers. “I want the shiny watering can.”

Hazel hugged a small bag of seeds. “I want the same watering can. It pours softly. It won’t splash me.”

Cricket raised a leg. “I want the watering can, too. It makes a ping sound when I tap it.”

All three looked at the one little can. Ollie could feel the shed grow tense, like a drum stretched tight. He took a slow breath. He smelled the dusty straw hat on the hook and the lemony soap in the corner. He felt calm.

“Let’s try using our words,” said Ollie gently. “We can make a plan. Everyone can be heard.”

Ruby tilted her head. “A plan?”

Ollie nodded. “We can negotiate. That means we talk and find a way that works for all of us.”

Hazel peeked up. “Will it be kind?”

“Yes,” said Ollie. “Kind and fair.”

Ollie took initiative. He picked up a chalk stub and drew a big circle on the floor. “First, tell me what you need.”

Ruby hopped inside the circle. “I need the can to water the blue flowers by the door. They are thirsty now.”

Hazel stepped closer. “I need the can to plant tiny seeds. I don’t like big splashes.”

Cricket leaped high. “I need the can for the ping sound in my song! Ping ping!”

Ollie smiled. “Thank you for sharing. Now, what can each of you give?”

Ruby thought. Her feathers rustled. “I can bring more water from the barrel with the little scoop. It is not shiny, but it works.”

Hazel whispered, “I can share my seeds if we are gentle.”

Cricket chirped, “I can make music to keep us happy while we work.”

Ollie clapped his paws softly. “Great ideas! Let’s make a schedule. We will take turns.”

He set three small pots on the floor like clocks. “Ruby first for quick flower sips. Hazel second for soft seed showers. Cricket third for music time with the can. Then repeat.”

Ruby blinked. “I can be first?”

“Yes,” said Ollie. “But only for a little while. Count to twenty drops.”

Ruby tried. She poured carefully. Drip, drip, drip. It sounded like rain kisses. “One… two… three…” The blue flowers perked up. “Thank you,” Ruby told Hazel and Cricket. “Your turn soon.”

Hazel held out her paws. “May I have it now?”

Ruby handed her the can. “Here you go.”

Hazel tipped the can very, very slow. “Plip… plip…” The seeds snuggled into the soil. Hazel smiled. “I feel safe when it’s gentle.”

Cricket tapped the can lightly when it was his turn. “Ping! Ping!” He sang a merry tune, and his legs clicked like little castanets.

Ollie listened. He heard drip, plip, ping. It was a shed symphony! He felt warm and proud.

Then Ollie noticed a dry corner where tiny sprouts drooped. He took initiative again. “Friends, those sprouts need help. We can also use the big red bucket. It is not shiny, but it holds lots of water.”

Ruby tilted her head. “But I like shiny.”

Hazel sniffed. “Big buckets splash.”

Cricket bounced. “Can it go ping?”

Ollie patted the bucket. “It goes thump, not ping. But we can be generous and share our tools. We can use the bucket outside for the big beds. We can keep the can for quiet jobs and music time.”

Ruby fluffed. “I can carry the scoop and fill the bucket.”

Hazel said, “I can hold the leaves so they don’t get splashed.”

Cricket grinned. “I’ll make a new song. Thump, plip, ping!”

They all worked together. Sunlight warmed their backs. The air smelled like mint and damp earth. Water sparkled. Seeds tucked in. Music danced around them.

When they finished, the plants stood tall and happy. Ruby gave the can a little bow. “We did it.”

Hazel smiled shyly. “Thank you for waiting and sharing.”

Cricket chirped, “Best band ever!”

Ollie’s whiskers tickled as he laughed. “We listened. We took turns. We made a plan. That is negotiation. It helps everyone.”

Ruby peeped, “We are different, and that is good.”

Hazel nodded. “Different needs. One garden.”

Cricket sang, “Ping for you, plip for me, thump for all!”

Ollie looked around the cozy shed. He felt thankful. “When we talk kindly and share, we all grow.”

The End.
