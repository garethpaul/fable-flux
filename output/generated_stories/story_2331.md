# Pippa Parrot’s Peaceful Morning

Pippa was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of blue on her tail. She lived with Mia on a sunny balcony garden. The garden was full of soft basil leaves, tiny cherry tomatoes, and sweet-smelling mint. Bees hummed. Leaves whispered. The sky felt big and blue.

Every morning, Pippa followed a gentle routine. She liked to wake up, stretch her wings, and breathe. In and out. Slow and soft. Mia would smile and say, “Good morning, Pippa.”

“Good-morn-ing,” Pippa sang, tapping the railing with her beak. Tap, tap. “Good. Morning.” She loved words. She loved sounds. She loved to make new songs.

Today, the sun was warm, and a soft breeze smelled like lemon and mint. Pippa perched by a pot of basil. She closed her eyes. She listened. The wind went whooosh. The bees went bzzz. The city far below went beep-beep. Pippa took a slow breath. In. Out.

Mia came out with a tiny watering can. “Time to water,” she said. “Let’s be mindful. We go slow. We notice what we see, hear, smell, and feel.”

Pippa nodded. “Mind-ful,” she echoed. She liked how the word felt in her beak. “Mind. Full.”

They worked together. Mia poured water. Pippa counted drops. “One, two, three,” she said softly as the water made little rain sounds on the soil. Plip, plip, plip.

“Can you smell the mint?” Mia asked.

Pippa leaned close. “Mmm. Minty!” She giggled. “Cool and sweet.” She brushed her feathers against the leaves. They felt smooth and tickly. “Tickle, tickle,” she said, and they both laughed.

Next, Pippa practiced her words. She had a word song. It was part of her routine.

“Hello, basil. Bonjour, basil,” she chirped.

“Bonjour means hello in French,” Mia said.

“Bon-jour,” Pippa repeated, slow and careful. “Bon. Jour.” She liked to say words like tiny treasures. She tried another. “Hola, mint.” She swayed her tail. “Hola!”

“Hola means hello in Spanish,” Mia said.

Pippa tilted her head, listening to the breeze. “Bees say bzzz in all languages,” she said, and she and Mia giggled again.

A small sparrow landed on the railing. He looked shy and a little hungry. “Peep,” he said.

Pippa puffed her chest kindly. “Hello, friend,” she said in a gentle voice. “Would you like a seed?”

Mia nodded. “We can share. Sharing is generous.”

Pippa picked a few seeds and placed them near the sparrow. “Here you go,” she said. “Eat, eat.”

The sparrow peeped happily and nibbled. Crick-crick. Pippa watched with soft eyes. She took another slow breath. In. Out. She felt warm inside.

Suddenly, a loud truck honked below. HONK! Pippa’s feathers ruffled. Her heart went thump-thump.

Mia knelt beside her. “Let’s be mindful,” she whispered. “We can calm our bodies. Let’s breathe together.”

Pippa nodded. “Breathe,” she said. She put one claw on the railing. She looked at a tiny drop of water on a tomato leaf. It shone like a little star. She breathed in. She breathed out. The honk faded. The breeze came back. Whooosh.

“I feel better,” Pippa said. “Thank you, breath.”

Mia smiled. “Your breath is always with you.”

Pippa tried a new word. “Tranquilo,” she said softly. “It means calm in Spanish.”

“Tranquilo,” Mia echoed.

Pippa whispered the word to the little water drop. “Tran-qui-lo,” she sang, turning it into a gentle song. The sparrow tilted his head and peeped along.

They moved on to the tomato plant. A tiny red tomato was ready to pick. Pippa looked close. “Red like a ladybug,” she said. She touched it with her beak. Smooth. Warm from the sun. She listened to her tummy. It said, “Mmm.” But she also looked at the sparrow, who still seemed a bit hungry.

Pippa picked the tomato and set it between them. “We share,” she said. “Half for you. Half for me.” She pecked softly to split it. The tomato made a small pop and a sweet smell filled the air.

“Mmm,” said Pippa. “Delicious.” The sparrow peeped happily with tomato juice on his beak.

Mia clapped softly. “That was generous, Pippa.”

Pippa felt proud, but quiet proud. She took another mindful breath. The garden smelled like mint and sunshine. The leaves made little shadows that danced on the floor. The wind brushed her feathers like a soft hand.

“Let’s finish our routine,” Mia said. “Water, word song, share, breathe, rest.”

Pippa nodded. She sang her word song once more, slow and happy. “Hello. Bonjour. Hola. Tranquilo.” Then she added a new word Mia taught her: “Namaste.” She bowed her head. “Namaste, garden.”

The bees buzzed like tiny drums. The sparrow peeped a thank you. Pippa closed her eyes for one more breath. She felt her feet on the railing. She felt the warm sun. She felt calm and kind.

Pippa opened her eyes and smiled. “I like mindful mornings,” she said. “I can listen. I can share. I can learn new words. I can be peaceful.”

Mia hugged her gently. “Yes,” she said. “When we are mindful, our hearts feel big and our words feel bright.”

Pippa fluffed her feathers and tapped the railing. Tap, tap. “Big heart. Bright words,” she sang. “Good morning, world.”

The End.
