# Benny's Big Office Day

Benny the beaver sat at his tiny desk in his home office. His fluffy brown tail twitched nervously. Today he had three very important jobs to do, but he felt scared.

"I'm too little," Benny whispered to himself. "I can't do big beaver work like Mama and Papa."

His office was cozy and warm. Sunlight streamed through the window, making everything glow golden. On his desk sat three colorful boxes: a red one for organizing, a blue one for scheduling, and a green one for recycling.

Benny's first job was to sort through a pile of old papers. The stack looked enormous to his small paws.

"This pile is taller than me!" he squeaked. "How can I ever finish this?"

He picked up the first paper. It was an old grocery list. "Hmm," thought Benny. "This has writing on only one side. I can use the other side for drawing!"

Into the green recycling box it went.

The next paper was a birthday invitation from last month. "This is too old to keep," said Benny, feeling a little braver. "But the pretty colors can be recycled too!"

Paper by paper, Benny sorted carefully. Some went in the trash, others in the green box for recycling. His little paws worked steadily.

"Look at me go!" Benny giggled as the pile got smaller. "I AM doing big beaver work!"

Next came his scheduling job. Benny had to plan his afternoon activities. He looked at his colorful clock on the wall. The big hand pointed to twelve, and the little hand pointed to two.

"Two o'clock means snack time," Benny said proudly. He drew a picture of an apple on his schedule. "Then at three o'clock, I'll play outside. At four o'clock, I'll help Mama with dinner."

Benny felt his chest puff up with pride. "I'm good at planning time!" he said to his reflection in the window.

His final job was organizing his desk drawers. The first drawer was full of mixed-up crayons, paper clips, and rubber bands.

"What a mess," Benny sighed. But then he remembered how well he did with the papers and the schedule. "I can do this too!"

He sorted the crayons by color - red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple. The paper clips went in one small box, and the rubber bands in another.

"Everything has its special place," Benny said happily. "Just like me!"

As he worked, Benny noticed something wonderful. His paws felt more sure. His breathing was calm and steady. The worried feeling in his tummy had turned into warm, happy bubbles.

Just then, Mama Beaver peeked into the office. "Benny! Look at all your hard work!"

"I did it all by myself, Mama!" Benny beamed. "I sorted papers for recycling, made a schedule, AND organized my drawers!"

Mama's eyes sparkled with pride. "You used your time so well, little one. And look how much paper you saved for recycling! You're helping our whole forest."

Benny's tail wagged with joy. "I thought I was too little for big beaver work. But I'm not! I can do important jobs when I try my best."

"That's right," said Mama, giving him a warm hug. "Being confident means believing in yourself, even when jobs seem scary at first."

Benny looked around his neat, organized office. The recycling box was full of papers ready for their new life. His schedule showed a perfectly planned afternoon. His desk drawers sparkled with organization.

"Tomorrow I want to do even MORE big beaver work!" Benny announced proudly.

He had learned that being small didn't mean he couldn't do important things. With confidence, good planning, and care for the environment, even little beavers could have very big, successful days.

The End.
