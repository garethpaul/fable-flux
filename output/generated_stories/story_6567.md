# Dippy the Dolphin and the Garden Shed Helpers

Dippy the dolphin was playful and kind. He loved to splash, giggle, and sing, “Flip-flap, splish-splash!” One sunny day, a new smell danced on the breeze—fresh wood and lemon soap. Dippy lifted his nose. “Sniff, sniff! What is that?”

He wriggled up the little stream that ran near the house. With a happy whoosh, he slid into a big puddle just outside a small, cozy garden shed. The door was open. Inside, it was cool and shady. Tools hung on hooks. Ribbons rested in a basket. A small fan hummed, “Whirr, whirr.” Sunlight made bright squares on the floor.

“Hello?” Dippy called softly. His voice echoed, “Lo… lo…”

“Hi, Dippy!” said Bee the bunny, hopping out from under a table. “We are making a gift for Nana Robin. She fixed the fence for everyone.”

Tito the turtle peeked from behind a paint jar. “We want to help! But we need a plan.”

Dippy twirled his fin. “I can help! I love helping.” He swished his tail and splashed a tiny splash. A few droplets landed on the floor.

Bee put up her paw. “Dippy, can I tell you a boundary? Please no splashing inside. The floor gets slippery.”

Dippy blinked, then smiled. “Thank you for telling me. I will keep the water in the puddle.” He scooted back a little, staying near the door where the puddle was. “Is this okay?”

“Yes,” said Bee. “Thank you for listening.”

The shed smelled like wood and sweet glue. Dippy heard the soft tap-tap of Tito’s shell as he moved carefully. On the table were smooth sticks, bright buttons, and string that felt like soft grass.

“What are we making?” asked Dippy.

“A wind chime,” said Bee. “We want it to sing in the wind.”

“Ooo!” Dippy clapped his fins. “I know about songs.” He took a deep breath and sang, “Do-do-do!” The sound bounced around the shed, warm and happy.

Tito nodded. “We need to think. What should go on top? How do we hang it up?”

Dippy wiggled. “Let’s use a strong stick on top,” he said. “Then we can tie strings.”

Bee tapped her whiskers. “Good idea. But how many strings?”

Tito counted slowly, “One… two… three… four.” He smiled. “Four is nice. Not too many.”

Bee passed the string. “Dippy, can you cut this?”

Dippy paused. He looked at his fins. “I can hold it. But cutting is hard for me. My fins are smooth.”

“That’s okay,” said Bee. “Thank you for telling us. That is your boundary. I will cut.” Snip, snip went the scissors.

Dippy beamed. “I can tie simple knots!” He dipped a fin in the puddle to make it cool and sticky with water, then carefully pressed the string to the stick while Bee tied the knot. Together, they made four neat ties.

Tito placed the buttons and shiny shells in a row. They clicked together like tiny claps. “Which ones should go where?”

Dippy listened to the little sounds. He tapped a shell with his fin. “Tink!” He tapped a button. “Tok!” He giggled. “Let’s make a tink-tok pattern.”

Bee clapped. “Pattern! That is smart thinking.”

They worked slowly. The fan went “Whirr.” The glue smelled sweet. The sticks were smooth and warm. Dippy hummed and counted, “Tink, tok, tink, tok.” Tito tied each piece snug. Bee checked, “Is it safe? Is it even?” Everyone nodded.

Dippy looked at the door. “Can we test it in the wind?”

“Let’s ask Nana Robin,” said Bee. “It is her yard.”

They walked carefully. Dippy stayed in his puddle path and slid along the grass outside the shed. “I will not go in the flower bed,” he said. “Those are Nana’s flowers.”

Nana Robin fluttered down. “Hello, little makers! What do you have there?”

Bee held up the wind chime. “We made this to say thank you for the fence.”

Nana’s eyes twinkled. “How thoughtful. May I touch it?”

“Yes,” said Tito. “We would like to hang it by the gate. Is that okay?”

“Thank you for asking,” said Nana. “That is kind. Yes, please hang it there.”

They hung the chime. A soft breeze came. The chime sang, “Tink, tok, tink, tok,” like tiny bells. Dippy closed his eyes and smiled. “It sounds like the sea and the sky.”

Nana Robin patted Dippy’s fin. “You helped, and you listened. You kept the shed dry. You asked before touching. That is helpful and respectful.”

Dippy’s cheeks felt warm with pride. “Helping feels good,” he said. “And asking is kind.”

Bee nodded. “We made a plan. We used our minds and our hearts.”

Tito added, “We worked together. We used everyone’s best way.”

They all stood very still and listened. Tink. Tok. Tink. Tok. The wind chime sang a gentle thank you song.

Dippy took a small bow. “Today I learned: Helping means listening, asking, and using the right job for me.”

Bee smiled. “And we learned to tell our boundaries and to hear yours.”

Nana Robin spread her wings. “My heart is full. Thank you, helpers.”

The sun felt warm. The shed smelled sweet. The wind sang softly. Dippy splashed once—in the puddle—and giggled. “Flip-flap, splish-splash! Helpful hearts make happy days.”

The End.
