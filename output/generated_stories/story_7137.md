# Mila Mouse's Colorful Solution

In a busy basement beneath Mrs. Chen's house, there lived a kind little mouse named Mila. The basement was always bustling with activity - the washing machine hummed "whoosh-whoosh," the dryer tumbled "thump-thump," and water pipes gurgled "glub-glub" all day long.

Mila loved her cozy home behind the water heater, but she had one big dream. She wanted to paint beautiful pictures! Every day, she watched the colorful soap bubbles float through the air and wished she could capture their pretty colors on paper.

"I want to be an artist," Mila squeaked to herself. "But I don't have any paint or brushes!"

One sunny morning, Mila scurried around the basement looking for art supplies. She found some old newspapers, but no paint anywhere. She sat down and twitched her whiskers sadly.

"How can I make art without any colors?" she wondered.

Just then, Mila heard a "drip-drop, drip-drop" sound. She looked up and saw Mrs. Chen's grandson Tommy coming down the stairs, carrying a leaky juice box.

"Oh no!" Tommy said. "My grape juice is making purple drops everywhere!"

Mila's bright black eyes sparkled with an idea. She watched the purple drops fall - drip, drop, drip, drop - onto the concrete floor.

"Excuse me," squeaked Mila politely, poking her head out from behind a box.

Tommy jumped! "A talking mouse!"

"Don't worry," said Mila gently. "I'm friendly. I was wondering... could I use those purple drops for painting?"

Tommy looked curious. "You want to paint with juice?"

"Yes!" Mila's tail wiggled with excitement. "I've been wanting to make art, but I don't have any paint. Your juice drops could be perfect!"

Tommy smiled. "That's really clever! But I need to clean up this mess before Grandma sees it."

Mila thought for a moment, her whiskers twitching. "What if we help each other? I'll help you clean up the sticky spots, and you could help me make some art with the juice?"

"That sounds fair!" Tommy agreed.

Together, they worked as a team. Tommy carefully squeezed tiny drops of grape juice onto Mila's newspaper. The purple liquid smelled sweet and fruity. Mila dipped her tiny paws into the juice and began making paw prints and swirls.

"This is beautiful!" Tommy exclaimed, watching Mila create purple flowers and butterflies.

"Thank you for sharing," Mila said happily. "And thank you for helping me discover that art supplies can be found in unexpected places!"

Tommy brought down some orange juice and apple juice too. Soon, Mila had created a whole rainbow of fruit-juice paintings - purple grapes, orange suns, and yellow-green trees.

"You're so resourceful, Mila," Tommy said admiringly. "You turned a problem into something wonderful!"

Mila beamed with pride. "When we work together and use what we have, we can solve any problem!"

From that day on, Tommy visited Mila every week. They became the best of friends, sharing juice-painting sessions and cleaning up together afterward. Mila learned that being resourceful meant looking at things in new ways and that the best solutions often come from working together.

The basement was still busy with its whooshing and thumping sounds, but now it was also filled with colorful art and the happy squeaks of a creative little mouse who had learned that with imagination and teamwork, any dream could come true.

The End.
