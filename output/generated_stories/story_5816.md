# Ollie Otter's Busy Day

Ollie the otter loved to play in the big garden behind his house. His favorite place was the old wooden shed where Grandpa kept all his gardening tools. The shed smelled like fresh dirt and flowers, and sunlight streamed through the dusty windows.

One sunny morning, Grandpa asked Ollie to help him get ready for the big garden party. "We need to clean the shed and organize everything before our friends arrive at three o'clock," Grandpa said, checking his watch. "Can you help me, Ollie?"

"Yes, yes, yes!" squeaked Ollie, clapping his little paws together. "This will be so much fun!"

Grandpa smiled and handed Ollie a list. "Here are the jobs we need to do. First, we'll sweep the floor. Then we'll put the tools in order. After that, we'll set up the chairs. Can you remember that?"

Ollie nodded eagerly, but he wasn't really listening. He was too excited about playing with the colorful watering cans he spotted in the corner.

"Ooh, look at these pretty cans!" Ollie giggled, grabbing a bright red one. He started pretending it was a rocket ship, making whooshing sounds as he zoomed it around the shed. Water droplets sparkled in the sunlight as they splashed everywhere.

"Ollie," Grandpa said gently, "remember our plan? We need to sweep first."

But Ollie had already moved on to a pile of flower pots. "These look like little hats!" he laughed, stacking them on his head. The pots clinked and clanked as they wobbled.

Time ticked by. Grandpa swept while Ollie played with seeds, making little piles and drawing pictures in the dirt with his paw. The shed floor was still messy, and the tools were scattered everywhere.

"Ding dong!" The doorbell rang.

"Oh no!" said Grandpa, looking worried. "Our friends are here early, and we're not ready!"

Ollie looked around the messy shed. His friends Bella Bunny and Sam Squirrel were walking toward them, but they had to step carefully around all the scattered tools and puddles.

"I'm sorry, Grandpa," Ollie said, his voice small and sad. "I wanted to help, but I got distracted by all the fun things."

Grandpa knelt down and hugged Ollie. "It's okay, little one. Sometimes we all forget to follow our plan. But look – your friends are here. Maybe we can all work together!"

"Can we help?" asked Bella, twitching her pink nose.

"We love organizing!" added Sam, swishing his fluffy tail.

Ollie's eyes lit up. "Yes! But this time, I'll follow the plan. First sweeping, then tools, then chairs!"

The friends worked as a team. Ollie swept carefully, watching where he put the broom. Bella sorted the tools by size – big shovels here, small trowels there. Sam arranged the chairs in a perfect circle.

In just thirty minutes, the shed looked beautiful and ready for the party.

"We did it!" cheered Ollie, doing a little happy dance. "Following directions and working together made everything faster and more fun!"

Grandpa beamed with pride. "And now we have time to enjoy our party with everyone!"

As more friends arrived, Ollie felt proud and happy. He had learned that taking time to follow directions and work with others made everything better for everyone.

"Next time," Ollie whispered to himself, "I'll listen first, then play!"

The End.
