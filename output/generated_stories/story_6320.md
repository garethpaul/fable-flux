# Tilly Turtle’s Treehouse Tune

Tilly was a shy turtle. She wore a tiny red scarf and moved slowly, step by careful step. One sunny morning, Tilly climbed the wooden ladder to a cozy treehouse. The treehouse smelled like pine and sweet apples. Sunlight made warm patches on the floor. Little bells tinkled in the breeze.

Inside the treehouse, friends gathered for Music Day. There were maracas in a basket, a small drum, a triangle, a soft ukulele, and a row of shiny shells that clicked. The air hummed with quiet excitement.

“Hi, Tilly!” chirped Pip the sparrow, flapping his small wings. “Do you want to play music with us?”

Tilly tucked her head a little into her shell. “I think so,” she whispered. Her voice was soft, like a leaf falling.

Luna the bunny bounced in place. Her ears wiggled. “Let’s make a song! I like fast music!”

“I like loud music!” boomed Benny the bear cub, patting the drum with a thump-thump.

Tilly felt her tummy flutter. She liked soft music. She liked slow music. She liked listening first. Her shy feelings made her chest feel tight and warm. She held the triangle in her hand. It felt cool and smooth.

Pip chirped, “One, two, three—go!”

Benny banged the drum. Boom! Boom! Boom! Luna shook the maracas. Chika-chika! Pip whistled high and bright. Tilly lifted the triangle and waited, but the sounds were big and busy. Her ears buzzed. She pulled back into her shell a little more.

“Tilly, play!” Benny said. He didn’t see Tilly’s worried eyes.

Tilly tried to ring the triangle. Ting! But the drum drowned it out. Boom! Her cheeks warmed. Her eyes felt watery. She stopped moving. Her triangle slipped and clinked on the floor.

Luna paused. “Tilly, are you okay?” she asked gently.

Tilly took a slow breath. She noticed her heart thump-thump. She placed her hand on her chest. “I feel shy,” she said quietly. “And a little squished inside by the loud and fast sounds.”

Pip tilted his head. “Thank you for telling us, Tilly,” he chirped. “Your feelings matter.”

Benny blinked. “I didn’t know you felt squished,” he said. “I was excited.”

Tilly nodded. “I want to play too,” she said. “I like soft sounds. Maybe we can take turns?”

The treehouse grew calm. A gentle breeze tickled the wind chimes. Ting-a-ling.

“That is a good plan,” Luna said. “We can share the music space.”

Pip clapped his wings softly. “Let’s make a feelings plan! First, slow and soft for Tilly. Then fast and loud for Luna and Benny. We will listen to each other.”

Tilly’s shoulders relaxed. Her shell felt lighter. “Thank you,” she whispered.

They made a small circle on a fuzzy rug. The rug smelled like lavender from a little pillow. Pip drew a picture chart on a leaf with a chalk stone: a tiny triangle, then a drum, then maracas, then ukulele. They all nodded.

“Soft time,” Pip said.

Tilly lifted her triangle. She took a deep breath. In. Out. “I can do this,” she told herself. She rang the triangle gently. Ting… ting… The sound was clear and sweet, like little stars. Luna swayed slowly. Benny smiled and tapped the drum with just one finger. Tap… tap… Pip hummed along, low and steady.

Tilly’s smile peeked out. She felt warm and calm. “I feel brave now,” she said.

“Loud time,” Pip chirped.

Benny grinned. Boom-boom-boom! Luna shook the maracas fast. Chika-chika-chika! Pip whistled bright notes. Tilly covered her ears a little, then remembered the plan. She took two steps back to the window where the air felt cool on her face. She watched and nodded to the beat.

“I can take a quiet step back,” Tilly said to herself. “I am still part of the song.”

They took turns again. Soft time. Loud time. Slow time. Fast time. Tilly tried the ukulele. The strings felt smooth as she strummed. Plink, plonk. Benny tried whisper-drumming. Luna tiptoed while shaking the maracas very softly. Pip kept the plan going with smiles and gentle words.

When they finished, the treehouse was peaceful. Everyone took a deep breath together. In. Out.

“How do you feel now?” Pip asked.

Tilly placed her hand on her chest. “I feel calm. I feel proud. I used my words,” she said.

Benny nodded. “I learned to look and listen,” he said. “If someone feels shy, I can play softer.”

Luna twirled. “Sharing the music and taking turns helps everyone,” she said.

Pip chirped, “We solved our problem by talking about our feelings and making a plan.”

Tilly picked up her triangle one more time. Ting! She said, “When I feel shy, I can breathe, use my words, and ask for turns. My friends can help.”

They all smiled. The sun made golden stripes on the floor. The bells on the window sang a tiny song. Ting-a-ling-ling.

“Friends,” Tilly said, “thank you for listening.”

“Thank you for telling us,” the friends answered.

Together, they sang a soft goodbye song and shared the instruments neatly back into the basket. The treehouse felt happy and safe.

The End.
