# Benny Beaver's Big Day

Benny Beaver lived in the coziest nursery you could imagine. His little bed had soft blue blankets, and colorful toys filled every corner. The morning sun streamed through the window, making everything glow like golden honey.

"Tick-tock, tick-tock," went the big clock on the wall. Benny's eyes popped open wide. "Oh my!" he squeaked. "I have so many things to do today!"

Benny jumped out of bed and looked at his special picture schedule on the wall. It showed all his daily activities with bright, happy pictures. "Let's see," he said, tapping his tiny paw on each picture. "Breakfast, exercise time, play with friends, story time, and help Mama with chores."

But instead of following his schedule, Benny got distracted by his favorite wooden blocks. "I'll just build one quick tower," he said. Before he knew it, he had built three tall towers and a bridge!

"Benny!" called Mama Beaver from downstairs. "Breakfast is ready!"

"Coming, Mama!" Benny called back. He looked at the clock again. "Oh no! I'm already behind!"

Benny rushed downstairs, his little feet pattering on the wooden steps. The kitchen smelled like warm pancakes and sweet maple syrup. His tummy rumbled loudly.

"Good morning, sleepyhead," Mama smiled, giving him a gentle hug. "You seem worried about something."

"I have so much to do today, Mama," Benny said, gobbling his pancakes quickly. "I don't know how I'll fit everything in!"

Mama sat down beside him. "Slow down, little one. When we rush, we sometimes miss the best parts of our day. Tell me about your schedule."

Benny showed Mama his picture schedule. She nodded thoughtfully. "This looks wonderful! But remember, we can make time for everything if we plan carefully and stay focused."

After breakfast, Mama helped Benny set a timer for each activity. "When the timer rings, we'll move to the next thing," she explained. "This way, you'll have time for everything you want to do."

First was exercise time. Benny did jumping jacks in the nursery, counting "One, two, three!" His heart pumped fast, and he felt strong and happy. When the timer rang, he was ready for the next activity.

During play time, his friend Ruby Rabbit came to visit. "I brought my new puzzle," Ruby said shyly. "But it might be too hard."

"Let's try it together!" Benny said kindly. "We can figure it out!" They worked on the colorful puzzle piece by piece, helping each other find the right spots.

When story time came, Benny listened carefully to Mama read about brave adventures. Then, when it was time to help with chores, he folded his little socks and put away his toys without rushing.

As the sun began to set, painting the nursery walls orange and pink, Benny looked at his completed schedule with pride.

"How do you feel?" Mama asked, tucking him into his cozy bed.

"Happy and proud!" Benny said, snuggling into his soft blankets. "When I used my timer and stayed focused, I had time for everything. And I was kind to Ruby, and we both learned something new!"

"That's right," Mama said, kissing his forehead. "Good time management means we can enjoy each moment without worrying about the next one."

Benny closed his eyes, listening to the gentle tick-tock of the clock. Tomorrow would bring another wonderful day, and now he knew just how to make the most of every minute.

The End.
