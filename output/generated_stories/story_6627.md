# Benny Beaver’s Safe and Busy Day

In a bright, playful living room, Benny the busy beaver woke up with a wiggle and a grin. Sunlight made soft squares on the cozy rug. Pillows looked like fluffy clouds. Blocks, crayons, and a toy drum waited nearby. Benny’s big flat tail thumped-thump-thumped with excitement.

“Today I build!” Benny said, his whiskers twitching.

Mama Beaver peeked in with a warm smile. “Good morning, Benny. Busy is fun, but safe is number one.”

Benny nodded fast. “Safe is number one!” he repeated. He hugged Mama’s soft fur and sniffed the sweet smell of oatmeal. His tummy rumbled.

After breakfast, Benny scooped up blocks. “I’ll make a tall tower! Taller than the couch!” he cheered.

Mama pointed to a friendly poster on the wall. It had pictures and little stars. “Let’s make a safety plan,” she said. “Step one: Look with your eyes. Step two: Clear the floor. Step three: Ask for help with big jobs.”

Benny tapped the poster with his paw. “Look, clear, ask!” he sang. He looked around. “Oops.” A ball rolled under the coffee table. Crayons lay open on the rug. A blanket trailed like a snake.

Benny set a goal. “I will make a safe space before I build,” he said proudly.

He picked up crayons and clicked the lids. Click, click. He folded the blanket, whoosh, and put it on the couch. He rolled the ball into a basket—plop!

Mama clapped softly. “Good start, builder.”

Benny lined up blocks and began to build. Click, clack, stack. The tower grew. His tail swished. The living room smelled like clean soap and pine from the wooden blocks. Benny felt warm and happy.

He wanted to make it bigger. He reached higher and higher. “Just a little more,” he whispered, stretching his little paws.

“Remember step three,” Mama called from the doorway. “Ask for help with big jobs.”

“I can do it,” Benny said, bouncing on his toes. His tail went thump-thump—oops! The tip of his tail bumped the tower.

The tower wobbled. Wibble-wobble. Wibble-wobble. Then—CRASH! Blocks tumbled. One block bumped the lamp. The lampshade tilted and slid. The room went dim, and Benny’s heart sank.

Benny’s eyes grew big and wet. “Oh no,” he said. “I forgot to ask for help.”

Mama hurried over, calm and kind. She unplugged the lamp with a click. “Thank you for telling the truth, Benny,” she said, kneeling down. “This is a problem, but we can fix it. First, we make the room safe.”

Benny sniffled and nodded. “Safe is number one.”

Together, they checked the lamp. Mama felt the cord and made a thoughtful face. “The lamp is okay, but it wobbles. We have a rule: no climbing near lamps, and tails stay low near towers.”

Benny gently set the lampshade upright. “I want to make amends,” he said softly. “I want to fix what I did.”

“That’s a good plan,” Mama said. “Let’s make a new goal.”

Mama took out a small basket and some soft stickers. “Goal one: Tape the cord so it stays safe and flat.” She showed Benny how to place the cord under the little clips that stuck to the wall. Snap. “Goal two: Build in a safe zone.”

They placed a blue blanket on the floor as a build circle. “This is the safe zone,” Mama said. “Nothing tall outside the circle. And tails stay tucked.”

Benny tucked his tail, pat-pat, and nodded. “Look, clear, ask,” he repeated. He took a deep breath. In…out. He felt calm, like a quiet pond.

“May I have help with the top blocks?” Benny asked.

Mama smiled. “Yes. Thank you for asking.”

They built together. Click, clack. Benny stacked, Mama steadied. The tower rose, but this time it leaned against a safe wall. Soft bumpers kept it from tipping. The room felt bright and safe again.

Benny added a red block and whispered, “Almost there.” Mama lifted a final blue block. “Ready?”

“Ready!” Benny said.

They placed it gently. The tower stood tall and strong. No wobble. Benny’s tail thumped once, then he remembered and tucked it snug.

“You did it,” Mama said. “You solved the problem.”

Benny beamed. “I made amends by fixing the lamp and using the safe zone. I learned to ask for help. I met my goal!”

“Busy and safe,” Mama said, giving a warm hug. Benny felt cozy and proud. The living room smelled like warm cookies now—Mama had a surprise snack.

They sat by the tower and munched. Crunch, munch. Benny licked a crumb from his whisker. “Safety helps fun,” Benny said.

Mama nodded. “Yes. Safety helps fun, and asking for help is smart. When we make a mistake, we make it right.”

Benny looked at the poster again and added a star sticker next to the steps. “Look, clear, ask,” he read. “I will use my safety steps every day.”

The tower shimmered in the sunlight. Benny whispered to it, “Thank you for being safe.”

He tucked his tail, smiled at Mama, and felt ready for more busy, safe fun.

The End.
