# Melody's Big Decision

In a cozy, quiet attic filled with dusty boxes and golden sunbeams, lived a little yellow bird named Melody. She had the sweetest voice in the whole neighborhood and loved to sing happy songs that drifted through the small round window.

Every morning, Melody's mama would bring her seeds and berries for breakfast. "Here you go, my little songbird," Mama would chirp softly. "Eat up so you can grow big and strong!"

But one sunny Tuesday morning, something was different. Melody waited and waited, but Mama didn't come with breakfast. She peeped out the window and saw Mama Bird helping an injured sparrow who had hurt his wing.

"Oh my," thought Melody, her little tummy rumbling like a tiny drum. "Mama is busy helping someone who really needs her. What should I do?"

Melody had never found her own food before. She always waited for Mama to bring it to her. But now she had to make a big decision all by herself.

"I could wait here and feel hungry," she said to herself, fluffing her bright yellow feathers. "Or I could be brave and try to find my own breakfast!"

Melody took a deep breath that smelled like old wood and sunshine. She spread her small wings and flew out the attic window for the very first time by herself.

"Wow!" she chirped with excitement. "I can fly wherever I want to go!"

First, she spotted some shiny red berries on a bush below. They looked delicious! But as she got closer, she remembered what Mama had taught her: "Red berries can be dangerous, little one. Always choose the blue and purple ones - they're much better for you."

Melody made her first smart decision. "No thank you, red berries. I need to find the healthy ones!"

She flapped her wings and searched until she found a bush full of plump blueberries. "Perfect!" she sang happily. The berries tasted sweet and juicy, making her feel strong and energetic.

Next, she discovered some nutritious sunflower seeds scattered on the ground near a bird feeder. An elderly robin was struggling to reach them.

"Would you like me to help you?" Melody asked kindly.

"Oh, that would be wonderful, dear," said the robin. "These old wings aren't what they used to be."

Melody carefully picked up the seeds and shared them with the grateful robin. It felt so good to help someone else, just like Mama was doing!

When Melody finally flew back to the attic, her tummy was full and her heart was happy. She had made good choices about healthy food and had even helped a friend.

Soon, Mama Bird returned. "Melody! I'm so sorry I was late. The injured sparrow needed—"

"It's okay, Mama!" Melody sang proudly. "I found my own breakfast today! I chose healthy berries, found good seeds, and even helped Mr. Robin."

Mama Bird's eyes sparkled with pride. "My little songbird, you've learned to take care of yourself! You made wonderful decisions and showed such kindness to others."

From that day on, Melody knew she could be independent and make good choices. She still loved spending time with Mama, but now she was confident and capable too.

And her songs sounded even sweeter than before.

The End.
