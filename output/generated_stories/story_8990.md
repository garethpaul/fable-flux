# Sunny Backyard, Brave Little Bear

In a sunny backyard, a friendly bear cub named Bibi woke up to the warm smell of grass and sweet flowers. Bees buzzed softly. A breeze tickled Bibi’s round ears. The sunlight made her brown fur look golden.

“Today is my day,” Bibi said, patting her chest. “I will try something new.”

Across the yard, birds practiced chirps. “Tweet-tweet, cheep-cheep!” They sounded different, but happy. Bibi smiled. “I like how we all have our own sounds.”

Bibi heard voices by the sandbox. Her pals were there—Milo the mouse, Lila the ladybug, and Gus the goose. They were building a tiny castle with towers made of cups.

“Hi, friends!” Bibi called. “Can I help?”

“Yes!” squeaked Milo. “We need a strong paw to pat the sand.”

Bibi puffed with pride and patted the sand gently. Thump, thump. The tower stood tall.

Gus flapped. “Great job, Bibi!”

Lila zipped close and said in a tiny voice, “I speak a little bit of a different word for thank you. In my family we say gracias.”

Bibi’s eyes grew wide. “Gra…see…as?”

“Gra-see-as,” Lila said, giggling. “That means thank you.”

Bibi tried again. “Gra-see-as.” The word felt like a song in her mouth. “I like learning new words.”

Gus honked happily. “In my pond, we say honk-honk for hello!”

Bibi laughed. “Honk-honk, Gus!”

They all giggled, and the castle got bigger. The warm sun hugged them as they worked. Bibi’s paws felt sandy, and she liked the crunchy sound of little shells in the sand.

Soon, a group of squirrel cousins raced to the yard. They were fast and chatty. “We’re playing Leaf Jump!” shouted Suri Squirrel. She tossed a soft leaf pile in the grass. “Jump high and shout your name!”

Milo jumped first. “Milo!” he squeaked. Plop!

Lila zipped up and landed like a tiny dot. “Lila!” she chimed.

Gus waddled, took a hop, and flapped. “Gus!” Splashy leaves flew.

Bibi’s heart thumped. She wanted to try, but she liked slow and careful. She took a little step back.

Suri smiled. “Your turn, Bibi!”

Bibi took a breath. The leaf pile smelled like fall. The sun felt warm on her nose. She listened to her body. It felt a bit tight and wiggly inside.

“I want to jump,” Bibi said softly, “but I want to do it my way. Small jump first, please.”

Suri nodded. “Good idea!”

Bibi bent her knees and made a tiny hop. “Bibi!” she said in a clear voice. She landed with a gentle crunch. The leaves tickled her toes.

“You did it!” Lila cheered. “Bravo! That means great job!”

Bibi felt warm pride in her chest. It was like a little light turning on. “Thank you. Gracias. Bravo to me,” she said with a giggle.

Suri bounced. “Bigger jump next?”

Bibi smiled. “I will, but when I am ready.” She held up a paw. “I like to listen to my body. That is my boundary.”

Gus nodded. “Boundaries are smart. They keep us safe and comfy.”

Milo squeaked, “We can cheer from here!”

The friends clapped and honked and buzzed. Bibi wiggled her shoulders, breathed in the sunny smell, and tried a bigger hop. “Bibi!” she called again, stronger now.

Leaves flew up like tiny green birds. Bibi landed and laughed. Her laugh was deep and bouncy. It was her own special sound.

Just then, a shy bunny peeped from the flower bed. “H-hello,” he whispered. “My name is Beno. I talk softly. Is that okay?”

Bibi knelt to Beno’s eye level. The petals brushed her nose. “Hi, Beno,” she said gently. “Soft is okay. Loud is okay. We are different, and that is good.”

Beno smiled. “Thank you.”

Bibi added, “In my family, we say asante for thank you.” She tried the word in a cozy voice. “Asante.”

Beno repeated, “Asante.” His ears twitched happily.

Suri pointed to the sky. “Race time!”

Bibi shook her head. “I don’t want to race right now.” She put her paw on her chest. “I need a rest. That is my boundary.”

Suri gave a thumbs up with her tiny paw. “We’ll play near you.”

The friends made a leaf parade around Bibi. Drums were made from buckets. The beat went boom-boom, soft and friendly. Bibi swayed with the rhythm.

She spoke to herself, kind and calm. “I am brave when I listen to me. I can try new things step by step. I am enough just as I am.”

When the parade ended, Bibi took one more hop, just for fun. “Bibi!” she sang. Everyone cheered in all their ways—honk, buzz, squeak, and clap.

Bibi’s heart glowed. The sunny backyard felt like a big hug. She looked at her friends. “We are all different,” she said. “We use different words. We move in different ways. We listen to our bodies. And we cheer for each other.”

“Si!” Lila said. “Yes!”

“Yup!” said Milo.

“Asante,” said Beno.

Bibi grinned. “I believe in me.”

The End.
