# Oliver Owl's Big Decision

Oliver the owl sat on his favorite perch by the bathroom window. His big yellow eyes watched the morning sun peek through the curtains. The bathroom was quiet and peaceful, just how Oliver liked it.

"Hoot, hoot!" Oliver stretched his fluffy brown wings. "Time to get ready for the day!"

Oliver flew down to the sink. He turned on the water with his wing. The water sparkled and bubbled as it flowed from the faucet. It looked so pretty and fun!

"I love watching the water dance!" Oliver said to himself. He let the water run and run while he preened his feathers in the mirror.

The water made a gentle splashing sound. Oliver was having so much fun that he forgot all about turning it off. He brushed his beak and fluffed his chest feathers.

Suddenly, Oliver heard a tiny voice. "Help! Help!"

Oliver looked around with his big round eyes. "Who's there?"

A little drop of water peeked out from the drain. "It's me, Droplet! Please help us!"

"What's wrong?" Oliver asked, tilting his head.

"Too much water is going down the drain!" Droplet explained. "My friends and I are getting wasted. We could help the flowers in the garden instead!"

Oliver looked at the running water. He had been letting it flow for a very long time. "Oh my! I didn't think about that."

"Every drop counts," said Droplet. "We want to help plants grow and give animals drinks!"

Oliver felt his feathers droop a little. He had to make a decision. Should he keep playing with the pretty water, or should he help save it?

Oliver thought hard. His wise owl brain remembered what his mama had taught him. "Use only what you need, and save the rest for others."

"You're right, Droplet!" Oliver said. He quickly turned off the faucet with his wing. "I should only use the water I need."

Droplet smiled. "Thank you, Oliver! Now we can help the thirsty roses outside!"

Oliver felt proud of his good decision. But then he realized something else. He still needed to wash his face and brush his beak properly. How could he do that without wasting water?

Oliver put his wing under his chin and thought. "I know! I'll turn the water on just when I need it, then turn it off again!"

He turned on the water for just a few seconds to wet his face cloth. Then he turned it off while he washed. When he needed to rinse, he turned it on again for just a moment.

"This is fun!" Oliver hooted. "It's like a game!"

Oliver finished getting ready much faster than before. He had saved lots of water and still got nice and clean.

"Great job!" cheered Droplet from the drain. "You made a very wise decision!"

Oliver puffed out his chest feathers proudly. "It feels good to help!"

Just then, Oliver heard chirping outside the window. He looked out and saw the garden sprinkler watering the flowers. The roses were drinking up the saved water and growing tall and beautiful.

"Look!" Oliver pointed with his wing. "The flowers are happy because we saved water for them!"

A little bluebird landed on the windowsill. "Thank you, Oliver! The birdbath is full of fresh water for us to drink!"

Oliver smiled his biggest owl smile. Making good decisions felt wonderful. He had learned that thinking about others made his choices even better.

From that day on, Oliver always remembered to turn off the water when he didn't need it. He became known as the wisest owl in the whole neighborhood, not just because he was smart, but because he cared about everyone around him.

"Hoot, hoot!" Oliver sang happily. "Good decisions make everyone smile!"

The End.
