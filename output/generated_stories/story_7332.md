# Polly's Special Space

In a cozy house lived a bright green parrot named Polly. She had beautiful red and blue feathers that sparkled in the sunlight. Polly loved to make things with her clever claws - colorful pictures, pretty necklaces from beads, and tiny boats from paper.

Polly had discovered the most wonderful place in the whole house - a big closet under the stairs. She had made it into her very own craft hideaway! Soft blankets covered the floor like fluffy clouds. Rainbow ribbons hung from the ceiling like gentle rain. Small boxes held her treasures - shiny buttons, smooth pebbles, and bits of colorful paper.

"This is MY special place," Polly would say happily as she arranged her craft supplies. The closet smelled like lavender from the sachets she had made, and the soft light from her little lamp made everything feel magical.

One sunny morning, Polly was in her hideaway making a friendship bracelet. She was carefully threading tiny blue beads when she heard loud footsteps. THUMP, THUMP, THUMP!

"Polly! Where are you?" called her friend Benny the bear. "I want to play!"

Benny pushed open the closet door without asking. His big paws knocked over Polly's bead box, sending tiny treasures rolling everywhere.

"Oh no!" squawked Polly, her feathers ruffling with worry. "My beads!"

"Sorry!" said Benny, but he kept walking around, accidentally stepping on Polly's ribbons. "Wow, this place is cool! Can I make something too?"

Polly felt her tummy get tight and fluttery. She loved Benny, but this was her special space. She needed it to feel calm and creative.

"Benny," said Polly gently, "I feel upset when you come in without asking. This is my special place where I need quiet time."

Benny's ears drooped. "But I just wanted to craft with you!"

Polly took a deep breath, the way her mama had taught her. "I understand you want to play, and that makes me happy. But everyone needs their own special space sometimes. When you come in without asking, it makes me feel worried."

"Oh," said Benny quietly. "I didn't know. What should I do?"

Polly's feathers smoothed down as she felt calmer. "You can knock on my door and ask, 'May I come in?' If I say yes, then we can craft together! If I say no, it means I need alone time, but we can play later."

Benny nodded slowly. "Like how I don't like when others touch my teddy bear without asking?"

"Exactly!" chirped Polly. "Everyone has things that are special to them."

Benny carefully stepped out of the closet. Then he knocked gently on the door. "Polly, may I please come in and help you pick up the beads?"

"Yes, thank you!" said Polly, feeling much better. "That would be very helpful."

Together, they collected all the scattered beads. The closet soon looked perfect again, with everything in its place.

"Thank you for teaching me about asking first," said Benny. "And thank you for using kind words to tell me how you felt."

"Thank you for listening and respecting my special space," replied Polly.

From that day on, Benny always knocked and asked before entering Polly's craft hideaway. Sometimes Polly said yes, and they made wonderful projects together. Sometimes Polly said she needed quiet time, and Benny would wait patiently until she was ready to play.

Polly learned that it was okay to protect her special space, and Benny learned that asking permission shows respect for others.

The End.
