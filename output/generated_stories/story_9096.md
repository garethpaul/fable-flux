# Benny Beaver Takes Turns

Benny was a busy beaver with bright, curious eyes. He worked in a cozy home office by the window. His desk was smooth and shiny, and his chair squeaked a little when he wiggled. The computer made a soft hum, and the pencils smelled like fresh wood. Benny loved to click, tap, and type.

“Tap-tap-tap,” went Benny’s little paws on the keyboard. “Click-click,” went the mouse.

Benny was making a colorful chart about healthy foods. “Apples, carrots, beans, and nuts,” he said proudly. “Crunchy and good for beaver tummies!”

Just then, his little sister, Bella, peeked in. Bella had a fluffy tail and a brave smile. “Benny, can I try the big clicker?” she asked, pointing to the mouse.

Benny loved his work. He wanted to say, “No, it’s my turn forever!” But he remembered that sharing can feel kind. He also remembered his own feelings. Sometimes he needed space to finish his job.

Benny took a slow, brave breath. He felt the cool air in his nose and the warm air out. “Bella,” he said gently, “I am working right now. I need to finish one more page. Then we can take turns. I will set the timer.”

Bella nodded. “Okay! I can wait.” She plopped onto a soft rug. The rug felt fuzzy under her paws.

Benny set a kitchen timer. It tick-tick-ticked on the desk. “Five minutes for me,” he said, “then five minutes for you.” He smiled. “That’s taking turns.”

Tick-tick-tick. Benny typed about crunchy carrots. He added a picture of a carrot with a happy face. He giggled.

Ding! The timer sang. Bella clapped. “My turn!” she cheered.

Benny stood up and rolled his chair back. “Your turn,” he agreed. “I will watch.” He was brave to pause his work. He felt proud and a little wiggly.

Bella sat in the squeaky chair. “Squeak!” She held the mouse with careful paws. “What do I do?”

Benny was helpful. “Click on the apple picture,” he said. “Then drag it to the green box.”

Bella clicked. “Click!” She dragged… and dropped it in the blue box by mistake. “Oops,” she whispered. Her whiskers drooped.

“It’s okay,” Benny said kindly. “Try again. Taking turns means we help each other, too.”

Bella tried again. “Click… drag… drop!” The apple landed in the green box. “I did it!” she squealed, bouncing in the chair.

“You did it!” Benny cheered. He clapped his paws. The office felt warm with happy sounds.

Ding! The timer sang again.

Benny peeked at Bella. He really wanted another turn. His paws twitched. But Bella was still smiling and learning. Benny felt a little tug in his tummy. He remembered personal boundaries. He remembered fairness, too.

“Bella,” he said softly, “the timer says it’s my turn now. Are you ready to switch?”

Bella looked at the screen. She took a tiny breath. “Yes,” she said. “We can switch. Taking turns makes it fair.”

They switched. The chair squeaked again. Benny typed about beans that are soft and warm. He could almost taste bean soup in his mind—savory and cozy.

Suddenly, a soft knock sounded at the door. It was Mama Beaver with a tray. Steam curled up from a little pot. “Snack break,” Mama sang. “Green apple slices, carrot sticks, and warm bean soup.”

The room filled with yummy smells—sweet apple, crunchy carrot, and gentle, salty soup. “Mmm,” Benny said. His tummy rumbled politely.

Bella’s eyes sparkled. “We can take turns with snacks, too,” she said. “First, Benny picks one, then me.”

Benny picked a carrot stick. Crunch! It snapped like a tiny twig. “Crunchy power,” he laughed.

Bella picked an apple slice. “Sweet and juicy,” she said, with a happy wiggle.

Mama smiled. “I love how you two take turns and use kind words. That is brave and helpful.”

After snack time, Bella pointed to the screen. “Benny, may I have another turn after you finish this page?”

Benny listened to his feelings. He still had one more picture to place. He needed a little more time. He spoke clearly and kindly. “I need two more minutes. Then it’s your turn.”

Bella nodded. “Thank you for telling me.”

Benny set the timer again. Tick-tick-tick. He finished the page with a smiling bowl of bean soup. He saved the chart. Click!

Ding! The timer sang. Benny stood up. “Your turn, Bella.”

Bella hopped in and added a golden star sticker. “For taking turns,” she said proudly.

They looked at the chart together. Apples, carrots, beans, and nuts. Colors glowed on the screen—red, orange, brown, and green. The office felt bright and calm.

Benny smiled at Bella. “We did it together,” he said.

Bella smiled back. “We were brave to ask. We were helpful to share. And we took turns!”

They high-fived with soft, happy pats. The squeaky chair squeaked one more time, like a little cheer.

The End.
