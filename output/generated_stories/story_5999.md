# Penny Panda's Big Brave Dinner

Penny Panda sat at the big wooden dining table, her fluffy black and white paws folded neatly in her lap. The dining room smelled like warm soup and fresh bread. Her tummy rumbled loudly.

"Grrrrowl!" went her belly.

"Oh my!" giggled Penny, patting her round tummy. "You're very hungry today!"

Mama Panda brought a steaming bowl to the table. "Here's your favorite bamboo soup, sweetie," she said with a warm smile.

Penny looked at the green soup and wrinkled her nose. Something was different. There were tiny orange pieces floating on top.

"What are those orange bits?" Penny asked, pointing with her paw.

"Those are carrots, dear. They'll help you grow big and strong," Mama explained.

Penny's bottom lip started to wobble. "But I don't like carrots! They look scary and crunchy!" She crossed her arms and pushed the bowl away. "I won't eat them!"

Her frustration grew bigger and bigger, like a balloon filling up with air. Penny felt her cheeks get hot and her paws get shaky.

"I'm MAD!" she huffed, and accidentally knocked over her cup of water. SPLASH! The water spilled all over the pretty tablecloth.

"Oh no!" Penny gasped, watching the water drip onto the floor. Her eyes filled with tears. "I made a big mess! I'm sorry, Mama!"

Mama Panda quickly grabbed a soft towel and smiled gently. "It's okay, sweetie. Accidents happen when we're feeling frustrated."

"But I ruined dinner!" Penny sniffled.

"You didn't ruin anything," Mama said, wiping up the water. "Sometimes we feel scared of new things, and that's normal. But being brave means trying something even when we feel a little scared."

Penny looked at the orange carrots floating in her soup. They did look different from her usual green bamboo. Her tummy rumbled again, reminding her how hungry she was.

"What if I don't like them?" she whispered.

"Then we'll try something else tomorrow," Mama said. "But what if you DO like them? You'll never know unless you're brave enough to try."

Penny took a deep breath, just like Mama taught her when she felt worried. She picked up her spoon with her small paw.

"I can be brave," she said to herself. "Just one tiny bite."

She scooped up a small spoonful with just one little carrot piece. She closed her eyes and took a bite.

"Mmm!" Her eyes popped open in surprise. "It's sweet! And crunchy in a good way!"

"You were so brave, Penny!" Mama clapped her paws together. "How do you feel?"

"I feel proud!" Penny beamed, taking another bigger bite. "And the orange makes the soup look like a sunset!"

As she ate her soup, Penny felt warm and happy inside. Not just from the yummy food, but from being brave enough to try something new.

"Mama, I'm sorry I spilled the water when I got frustrated," Penny said.

"I forgive you, sweetheart. And I'm proud of how you took deep breaths and found your courage," Mama replied, giving Penny a big, fuzzy hug.

Penny finished her entire bowl of soup, carrots and all. "Being brave made me feel strong inside," she said, patting her full, happy tummy.

"That's because courage helps us grow, just like good food does," Mama smiled.

From that day on, whenever Penny felt scared or frustrated, she remembered her brave carrot moment and took deep breaths to find her courage.

The End.
