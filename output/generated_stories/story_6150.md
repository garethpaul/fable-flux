# Ollie Otter Learns to Share

Ollie the otter loved playing in his cozy living room. The soft carpet felt wonderful under his furry paws, and colorful toys were scattered everywhere like a rainbow on the floor.

"What should I play with today?" Ollie wondered aloud, his whiskers twitching with excitement.

He spotted his favorite red ball near the sunny window. "Perfect!" he squeaked, diving toward it with a happy splash sound, even though there was no water around.

Just as Ollie grabbed the ball, his little sister Luna waddled into the room. Her brown eyes sparkled when she saw the bright red toy.

"Ollie, can I play with the ball too?" Luna asked sweetly, her tiny paws reaching out hopefully.

"No way!" Ollie hugged the ball tight against his fluffy chest. "I saw it first! It's mine to play with!"

Luna's whiskers drooped sadly. She sat down on the soft rug with a small sigh that sounded like air leaving a balloon.

Ollie bounced the ball high in the air - bounce, bounce, bounce! The sound echoed through the warm living room. But something didn't feel quite right. Playing alone wasn't as much fun as he remembered.

He peeked over at Luna, who was now playing quietly with some blocks in the corner. She was building a small tower, but her movements seemed slow and sad.

"Hmm," Ollie thought, rolling the ball between his paws. "Luna looks really sad. Maybe I should ask Mama what to do."

Mama Otter was reading nearby in her favorite blue chair. "Mama," Ollie called softly, "Luna wanted to play with my ball, but I said no. Now she looks sad, and I don't feel happy either."

Mama's kind eyes crinkled with understanding. "Oh, Ollie. How do you think Luna felt when you wouldn't share?"

Ollie's ears drooped as he thought carefully. "Probably sad... and maybe left out?"

"That's right, sweetheart. When we don't share, it can hurt others' feelings. But when we take turns, everyone gets to have fun!" Mama explained gently.

"Taking turns means we both get to play?" Ollie asked, his tail starting to wiggle with a new idea.

"Exactly! You could play with the ball for a few minutes, then let Luna have a turn. That way, you're both happy and showing respect for each other."

Ollie's eyes lit up like tiny stars. He scampered over to Luna with the red ball bouncing beside him.

"Luna!" he called cheerfully. "Would you like to take turns playing with the ball? You can have it first!"

Luna's whole face brightened like sunshine after rain. "Really? Thank you, Ollie! That's so nice!"

She gently took the ball and rolled it across the soft carpet. "This is fun! Want to roll it back and forth together?"

"Yes!" Ollie clapped his paws excitedly.

For the rest of the afternoon, Ollie and Luna took turns with all their toys. They shared the building blocks, the puzzle pieces, and even the cozy reading corner. The living room filled with happy giggles and the warm sounds of play.

"Taking turns is actually more fun than playing alone," Ollie realized as they built a tower together. "When we respect each other and share, we both feel good inside!"

Luna nodded, her whiskers twitching with joy. "And we get to play together, which makes everything better!"

From that day on, Ollie always remembered that sharing and taking turns wasn't just about being nice - it was about showing respect and making sure everyone could join in the fun.

The End.
