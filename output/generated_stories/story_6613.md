# Ollie Owl’s Little Steps Plan

Sunlight danced across the playful living room. Soft pillows made a cozy nest on the rug. Blocks clicked and clacked. A basket of fruit smelled sweet and bright. In the window, a breeze whispered, shhh.

Perched on a tall bookcase sat a wise old owl named Ollie. His feathers were soft and speckled. His eyes were warm and golden. He hooted gently, “Hoo-hoo, good morning!”

Mia and Max, two small siblings, looked up. Mia held a teddy bear. Max wore bright socks that made squish-squish sounds on the floor.

“Ollie,” said Mia, “I feel wiggly and tired at the same time.”

“I feel grumpy-hungry,” said Max, patting his tummy. “And I don’t want to clean up.”

Ollie tilted his head. “Hoo-hoo, it sounds like you have a problem. Let’s find a solution with little steps.”

“What are little steps?” Mia asked.

“Little steps are tiny goals,” Ollie said softly. “We make a simple plan. We do one small thing, then another. Little steps make big changes.”

Max sniffed the air. “I smell bananas.”

“Yes,” said Ollie. “Healthy habits help our bodies and feelings. Today, we will try three little steps: move, munch, and tidy. Are you ready?”

Mia nodded. Max shrugged. “Maybe,” he said.

Ollie fluffed his feathers. “Step one is move. When our bodies move, our energy feels happy.”

Mia looked around. “Can we dance?”

“Let’s do the Wiggle-Warm,” Ollie hooted. “Goal one: dance for one song.”

Mia pressed a big blue button. Music tinkled like tiny bells. The living room came alive. Mia swished her arms like ribbons. Max stomped like a dinosaur. Ollie bobbed on the bookcase and flapped, flap, flapped.

“Wiggle fingers!” called Ollie.

They wiggled.

“Stretch tall!” he said.

They stretched.

“Slow breaths,” he whispered. “In like smelling a flower. Out like blowing bubbles.”

They breathed in and out. The room smelled like orange peels and clean soap. The music ended with a ding.

Max giggled. “I feel less grumpy.”

Mia smiled. “My legs feel warm.”

Ollie nodded. “Goal one, done. Little step success.” He blinked kindly. “Step two is munch. Healthy food helps our bodies grow.”

Max pointed. “Fruit! I want a snack.”

Ollie glided down to the coffee table with a soft whoosh. He set out small plates. “Goal two: rainbow snack. Choose two colors.”

Mia looked in the basket. “Red strawberry,” she said, touching the bumpy seeds. “And green apple. Crunchy!”

Max chose banana and blueberry. “Yellow and blue!” He peeled the banana slowly. It smelled sweet and sunny. The blueberries were cool and round.

Ollie said, “Chew, chew, chew. Sip water too.”

They drank from little cups. The water felt cold and smooth. Max licked his lips. “Yum.”

Mia patted her belly. “I feel calm.”

Ollie smiled. “Goal two, done. Little step success.”

Max stared at the floor. Blocks were everywhere—click, clack, clatter. “Uh-oh,” he said. “Mess.”

Mia frowned. “I don’t like cleaning.”

Ollie tucked his wings. “Step three is tidy. When our space is tidy, our minds feel tidy. But we will make it fun. Goal three: three-minute tidy time.”

“Three minutes?” Max asked. “We can try.”

Ollie hooted a gentle tune. “Tidy, tidy, one, two, three! Pick a color just for me.”

Mia picked red blocks. Max chose blue. Ollie watched and cheered. “Plop the red in the red bin. Slide the blue in the blue bin. Listen—click, clack, nice and neat.”

Mia laughed as her red blocks went plop, plop. Max grinned at the smooth clack of the blue ones. They found a lost puzzle piece under the couch. “Hello!” Mia said to the piece. “You can go home now.”

Ollie checked a tiny sand timer. The golden sand whispered down. “Almost there,” he said.

Max hummed the tidy song. Mia put the teddy on a pillow. The last grain of sand fell—shhh.

“We did it!” Mia cheered.

Max threw his hands up. “Goal three, done!”

Ollie flapped once. “Little step success.”

Mia sat on the fluffy rug. “Ollie, I feel good. Not so wiggly-tired.”

Max nodded. “I feel proud. My tummy and my room are happy.”

Ollie’s eyes twinkled. “You moved your body. You ate rainbow foods. You tidied your space. That’s healthy habits, little ones. When we set simple goals and take little steps, we feel strong and calm.”

Mia hugged her teddy. “Can we make a plan for bedtime too?”

Ollie hooted softly. “Yes. A calm bath, a cozy story, and lights out. Little steps help at bedtime, morning time, and playtime.”

Max took a deep breath in, like smelling a flower, and blew out like a bubble. “I can do little steps,” he said.

Ollie stretched his wings. The room felt soft and bright and peaceful. “Remember,” he said, “little steps, big smiles. Move, munch, and tidy. Then rest.”

Mia and Max bumped their fists together. “Little steps!” they said.

Ollie blinked slowly, proud and kind. The living room was still playful, but now it was neat and cozy too. The fruit basket glowed with colors. The pillows made a comfy nest. The air smelled fresh and sweet. Inside their hearts, Mia and Max felt brave, healthy, and happy.

The lesson was clear: set a small goal, take a little step, and try your best. Healthy habits help our bodies and feelings every day.

The End.
