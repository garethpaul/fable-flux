# Fawn’s First Song

In a bright musical practice room, a gentle deer fawn named Fawn stepped softly on the shiny wood floor. The room smelled like lemon polish and fresh paper. Sunlight made soft squares on the floor. Music stands lined the wall. A small drum, a xylophone, and a tiny bell sat on a table, waiting.

Fawn’s ears twitched with excitement. “Today I learn a song,” Fawn whispered. “I want to play with my friends.”

Coach Melody, a kind robin with a blue scarf, fluttered in and sang, “Good morning, friends! Today, we make music together. We will follow directions, step by step.”

Fawn’s friends waved. There was Pip the bunny, who held a drumstick. There was Tilly the turtle, who wore big, round glasses. And Buzz the bumblebee hovered happily over the xylophone.

Coach Melody chirped, “First, we sit. Then we listen. Then we play. We follow directions so our music is safe and sweet.”

Fawn nodded. The words felt simple, like soft steps on moss. But the shiny drum and the twinkly bell looked so fun. Fawn’s hooves tingled.

Coach Melody clapped a gentle beat. “One, two. One, two. When I say play, we play. When I say stop, we stop.” She smiled. “Let’s try.”

They practiced stopping and starting. Pip tapped, tap-tap. Buzz tinged the xylophone, ting-ting. Tilly shook the tiny bell, ding-ding.

Coach Melody said, “Stop.” Everyone stopped. Even the room felt still and happy.

Fawn took a breath. “I can do this,” Fawn said.

Coach Melody brought out a small triangle on a string. It sparkled like morning dew. “Fawn, this is for you,” she said. “Hold it gently. When I point, tap it once. Just once.”

Fawn’s heart made a quick drum. “Just once,” Fawn repeated.

The lesson began. Coach Melody hummed a sweet tune. “Listen for the point,” she said. She lifted a wing and pointed.

Fawn tapped the triangle. Ting!

The sound was light, like a silver raindrop. Fawn smiled. “I did it!”

They played again. Coach Melody pointed. Ting! Fawn tapped once, soft and clear. The music felt like a friendly walk, step by step.

Then Fawn heard Buzz buzz-buzz a happy extra note. It sounded fun. Fawn’s hooves itched to play more. When Coach Melody pointed again, Fawn tapped three times. Ting ting ting!

Coach Melody lowered her wing. The room went quiet. “Friends,” she said softly, “what did I ask for?”

“Just once,” said Tilly, peeking from behind her glasses.

Fawn’s cheeks felt warm. “I wanted to show I can play more,” Fawn said. “I wanted it to sound fancy.”

Coach Melody nodded kindly. “I know it is exciting. But we follow directions to make music together. That is how we help our friends and keep our beat.”

Fawn looked at the triangle. “I did not follow the directions,” Fawn said. “I am sorry.”

Pip scooted closer. “It’s okay,” Pip said. “You told the truth. That is honest. We can try again.”

Buzz buzzed a gentle tune. “We are a team,” Buzz said. “We listen together.”

Coach Melody smiled. “Thank you, friends. Fawn, thank you for your honesty. That is integrity. Let’s practice. We will use our song steps.”

She tapped the board with a small stick. On the board were simple pictures: an ear, a finger pointing, and a smiley face.

“Step one: Ears on.” Coach Melody cupped her ear.

Fawn lifted both ears high. “Ears on.”

“Step two: Watch the point.” She wiggled her wing.

Fawn’s eyes followed carefully. “Watch the point.”

“Step three: One tap, then smile at a friend.”

Fawn took a calm breath. “One tap, then smile.”

They tried again. Buzz played ting-ting on the xylophone. Pip tapped the drum, tap-tap. Tilly rang the bell, ding. Coach Melody pointed to Fawn.

Ting!

Fawn smiled at Tilly. Tilly smiled back, her eyes warm and shiny.

Coach Melody pointed again. Ting!

Fawn waited, patient as a tree. The music danced around the room like soft wind through leaves.

Soon, the song came together. Tap-tap, ting, ding, ting! It sounded like a happy morning walk. Fawn felt light and proud, but calm too.

Coach Melody fluffed her feathers. “Beautiful,” she sang. “You followed directions. You helped your friends. You told the truth when it was hard. That is integrity. That is friendship.”

Fawn’s chest felt warm. “I liked the steps,” Fawn said. “Ears on, watch the point, one tap, then smile.”

“Me too,” Pip said. “The smile part is my favorite.”

“Mine too,” said Tilly. “Smiles make music sweeter.”

Buzz buzzed a little heart shape in the air. “Smiles are like honey,” Buzz said.

Coach Melody gave each friend a sticker shaped like a tiny music note. Fawn pressed the sticker on the triangle string. It felt smooth under Fawn’s hoof.

“I learned that following directions helps everyone,” Fawn said. “It makes our music safe, sweet, and together.”

Coach Melody nodded. “Yes,” she said. “When we listen, we learn. When we follow directions, we care for friends. When we tell the truth, we grow strong inside.”

Fawn looked around the sunny room. The lemon smell, the soft wood floor, the happy faces—everything felt bright. “I am ready for more songs,” Fawn said. “Step by step.”

“Step by step,” the friends echoed.

They played the song one more time, with ears on, eyes watching, and hearts smiling.

The End.
