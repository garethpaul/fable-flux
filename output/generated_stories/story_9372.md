# Koala Kimi’s Peaceful Porch Plan

Koala Kimi lived in a cozy treehouse with a peaceful front porch. The porch had a soft blue mat, a rocking chair that creaked, and flower pots that smelled sweet like honey. Every morning, Kimi sat on the porch, paws folded, ears relaxed, and took slow, quiet breaths.

“In… and out,” Kimi whispered. “I listen. I feel. I am calm.”

One sunny day, Kimi heard little feet on the path. Tap, tap, tap! It was Lila the lizard, Zig the zebra finch, and Benny the bunny. They looked worried.

“Kimi,” Benny said, ears drooping. “Our playground is messy. Leaves everywhere! The slide is sticky. We can’t play.”

Kimi placed a soft paw on the porch mat. “Let’s take a breath together first,” Kimi said kindly. “In… and out.” Kimi’s voice was gentle like a soft song.

Lila, Zig, and Benny breathed with Kimi. They felt their tummies rise and fall. The air smelled like lemon from the porch flowers.

“I feel better,” Zig chirped.

Kimi nodded. “When we are calm, we can help. We can be leaders with kind hearts. Let’s make a plan.”

Kimi stood up slowly. The rocking chair went creak, creak. Kimi rolled out a little basket from under the porch. Inside were gloves, tiny brooms, and a shiny dustpan. “We can volunteer to clean the playground,” Kimi said. “We can help our friends.”

“We can!” Benny said, hopping high.

“But,” Lila whispered, “Sam the squirrel spilled sticky juice on the slide yesterday. I’m mad.”

Kimi’s ears twitched. “Feel your feelings,” Kimi said. “Put a paw on your heart. Take a breath. In… and out. When we are ready, we can forgive. Forgiving helps our hearts feel light.”

Lila took a breath. “In… and out.” Her scales shimmered in the sun. “Okay. I want my heart to feel light.”

They all picked up a broom or a rag. Kimi led the way down the little path. Crunch, crunch went the leaves under their feet. The playground was messy, just like Benny said. Leaves danced in the wind. The slide was sticky and smelled like sweet berries.

Kimi clapped softly. “Team, we will do this together. I will show, and we will follow. First, we breathe.” They all smiled and took one more slow breath.

“Now,” Kimi said, “I’ll sweep the leaves into a pile. Lila, please hold the dustpan. Benny, can you wipe the slide with warm water? Zig, you can sing a steady song to help us work.”

Zig puffed up his tiny chest and began a happy tune. “Sweep, sweep, swish, swish. Clean and kind, that’s our wish!”

They worked and worked. Kimi’s broom made soft whooshing sounds. Lila held the pan steady. Benny wiped, wiped, wiped. The sticky smell faded. The air felt new and clear.

Just then, Sam the squirrel peeked from behind a tree. His tail drooped. “I’m sorry,” Sam said. “I spilled my berry juice. I ran away. I felt scared.”

Kimi put the broom down. “Thank you for telling us, Sam,” Kimi said. “Let’s all take a breath together. In… and out.” The breeze tickled their noses.

Lila looked at Sam. She put a small claw on her chest. “I was mad,” she said softly. “But I want my heart to be light. I forgive you.”

Sam’s eyes sparkled. “Thank you,” he squeaked. “Can I help?”

Kimi smiled. “Yes. You can help rake the last leaves. When we make a mess, we fix it. That is kind leadership too.”

They finished the playground together. The slide shone in the sun. The swings sang squeak, squeak. The air smelled like clean water and flowers. The ground felt smooth under their feet.

Back on the peaceful front porch, Kimi brought out a small pitcher of lemon water and tiny cups. “We did it,” Kimi said. “Let’s have a calm sit.”

They all sat on the soft blue mat. “Close your eyes,” Kimi whispered. “Listen. What do you hear?”

“I hear a bee,” Zig chirped.

“I hear the wind,” Benny said.

“I hear the rocking chair,” Lila giggled. “Creak, creak!”

Kimi nodded. “Now, feel your breath. In… and out.”

Everyone breathed together. Their bodies felt relaxed and warm. Their hearts felt light.

Sam set his cup down carefully. “Kimi,” he said, “you helped us slow down and be kind. You are a good leader.”

Kimi shook their head and smiled. “We are all leaders when we care and help. We volunteered. We breathed. We forgave. We worked as a team.”

Benny bounced a little. “I like being calm,” he said.

“Me too,” Lila and Zig said.

Kimi looked at the bright playground and the gentle sky. “When we are mindful,” Kimi said softly, “we notice our breath, our friends, and our choices. Mindful hearts make kind actions.”

They all took one last slow breath together. In… and out. Their smiles grew big and warm.

The End.
