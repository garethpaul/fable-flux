# Squeaky Saves the Guest Room

Squeaky was a small, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail. One sunny morning, Squeaky scampered into the guest room of the big, friendly house. The room smelled like clean sheets and a little like lavender. Sunlight danced on the floor, and dust sparkles floated in the air.

But oh dear! The guest room was messy. There were paper scraps, empty cardboard boxes, plastic bottles, and a tin can jingling on the carpet. A basket of clean towels waited on the bed. The window was open, and the curtains swished with a soft whoosh.

Squeaky put a tiny paw to his cheek. “Tsk, tsk. This is a problem,” he said. “Someone is coming to visit, and the room is not ready. What can I do?”

Squeaky took a deep breath. He smelled the lavender and felt calm. “I can help,” he decided. “I am a helpful squirrel. I can use recycling!”

He ran to the hallway and called, “Friends! I need help! Teamwork time!”

Soft footsteps padded closer. Momo the mouse peeked in with her whiskers twitching. “I can help,” squeaked Momo.

Bibi the bluebird fluttered to the windowsill. “Tweet! I can help too,” she chirped.

Tuck the turtle slowly rolled in, his shell shiny. “I am not fast,” he said in a low, warm voice, “but I am steady. I can help.”

Squeaky smiled. “Thank you! We will recycle. We will clean. We will make the guest room cozy.”

He pointed with his paw. “We need three bins. One for paper, one for plastic, and one for metal.” He used clear, simple words and tapped each basket. “Paper. Plastic. Metal.”

Bibi nodded. “Paper,” she said softly, tasting the word like a song. “Plastic.” She chirped again. “Metal.” The words felt nice in her beak.

Momo clapped her tiny paws. “I like new words! Paper, plastic, metal!”

Tuck chuckled. “I will remember. Slow and sure.”

Squeaky started sorting. He picked up a cardboard box. It felt rough and bendy under his paws. “Paper,” he said, and dropped it in the paper bin with a soft thump.

Momo found a shiny foil ball. It crinkled in her paws. “Metal,” she said, and plink! it landed in the metal bin.

Bibi lifted a light plastic bottle with her beak. It made a little squeak. “Plastic,” she chirped, and pop! it dropped into the plastic bin.

They worked together. Swish went the broom as Tuck pushed it slowly. Rustle went the papers. Clink, clink went the cans. The room began to look neat. Squeaky’s heart felt warm and proud.

Suddenly, Bibi fluttered down with a worried chirp. “Oh no! What about this?” She showed a sticky yogurt cup. “Is it clean?”

Squeaky sniffed. “Phew! It smells like old yogurt.” He wrinkled his nose. “We must rinse it. Recycling works best when items are clean.”

They all went to the little sink in the corner. Squeaky turned the faucet. Splash, splash! Cool water washed the cup. Momo held a tiny cloth and wiped it dry. “Clean now!” she said, smiling.

Back in the guest room, Tuck pointed with his steady paw. “What about this soft paper? It is a tissue.”

Squeaky shook his head. “Tissues are not for recycling. We will put them in the trash.” He spoke gently so it was easy to understand. “Some things recycle, some do not. That is okay. We try our best.”

Momo found a box with words on it. “Look! Letters!” she squeaked. “Can we read them?”

Squeaky traced the letters with his paw. “Re-cy-cle,” he sounded out slowly. “That word means we use things again to make new things.” He smiled. “Words help us learn. Reading helps us help the Earth.”

Bibi fluffed her feathers. “I like that word. Recycle!” she sang, turning the word into a happy tune.

They kept sorting. Paper here. Plastic there. Metal in the shiny bin. They worked in a gentle rhythm: pick, name, place. Pick, name, place. The room smelled fresh now, like lavender and sunshine.

At last, the floor was clear. The towels were folded in neat, soft stacks. Squeaky pulled the curtains open wider. Light poured in, warm and golden. The guest room felt peaceful and ready.

Squeaky looked at his friends. “We did it! Problem solved,” he cheered.

Momo giggled. “Teamwork makes big jobs small.”

Tuck nodded slowly. “We helped each other. We helped the Earth.”

Bibi hopped in a circle. “And we learned new words. Paper, plastic, metal, recycle!”

Just then, the human family peeked in. “Wow!” they said. “The guest room looks wonderful. Thank you, helpers!”

Squeaky’s tail swished with joy. “We are happy to help,” he said. “We sorted, we cleaned, and we recycled.”

The family placed a small sign by the bins. It read: “Recycle Here.” Squeaky traced the letters again. “Re-cy-cle,” he whispered, proud and calm.

The friends bumped paws, wings, and shell. “Teamwork!” they shouted together, and their voices echoed softly in the bright, clean room.

Lesson time, Squeaky thought. He looked at the bins and smiled. “When we recycle, we care for our home. When we help, we show love. When we work as a team and use kind words, we can solve problems.”

Everyone nodded. The guest room was ready for the visitor, and their hearts felt full.

The End.
