# Pippin's Fair Share Feast

Pippin the penguin waddled into the sunny dining room, his black and white feathers gleaming. The wooden table was set with colorful plates and cups, and delicious smells filled the air. Today was the big family feast!

"Good morning, everyone!" Pippin chirped happily, flapping his little wings.

His sister Penny was already sitting at the table, and their cousin Pete had just arrived. Mama Penguin was bringing out a big platter of fresh fish - Pippin's favorite!

But when Mama Penguin started serving, something didn't seem right. She gave Pete a huge pile of fish, gave Penny a medium pile, and gave Pippin just three tiny fish.

"Wait, Mama," said Pippin, his voice gentle but confused. "That doesn't look fair. We're all the same size, but we got very different amounts."

Pete looked down at his overflowing plate. "Yeah, this seems like way too much for me," he said honestly.

Penny nodded. "And I'm not even that hungry today. Maybe we should share more evenly?"

Mama Penguin paused and looked at the plates. Her eyes grew wide. "Oh my! You're absolutely right, Pippin. I wasn't paying attention, and that's not fair at all."

Pippin's tummy was rumbling, but he had an idea. "What if we put all the fish back on the big platter and divide it fairly? That way everyone gets what they need."

"That's a wonderful idea!" said Mama Penguin, smiling proudly. "You're being very fair and thinking about everyone."

Together, they gathered all the fish back onto the big platter. The fish smelled salty and fresh, making Pippin's mouth water. But he felt good inside knowing they were doing the right thing.

"Let's count together," suggested Pippin. "One fish for Pete, one for Penny, one for me. Two fish for Pete, two for Penny, two for me."

They continued counting until everyone had exactly five fish on their plate. The dining room filled with happy chatter as they worked together.

"This feels much better," said Pete, patting his now-comfortable portion. "I was worried I wouldn't be able to eat all that food, and it might go to waste."

"And now I won't be hungry," added Pippin, his orange beak clicking with excitement. "Thank you for helping me speak up about fairness."

Mama Penguin hugged Pippin tight. "You showed great courage by speaking up when something wasn't fair. And you found a kind way to fix the problem that made everyone happy."

As they began to eat, the fish tasted even more delicious because everyone felt good about sharing fairly. The dining room filled with laughter and the gentle sounds of a happy family meal.

"You know what?" said Penny, crunching on her fish. "When we're fair to each other, everything feels better. Even the food tastes better!"

Pippin nodded, his belly getting full and his heart feeling warm. "Being fair isn't just about getting what we want. It's about making sure everyone is taken care of."

After their meal, they all helped clean up together, each penguin carrying their own plate to the kitchen. The dining room sparkled in the afternoon sunlight, and everyone felt happy and satisfied.

"Tomorrow, let's remember to always check that we're being fair," said Pippin. "It makes everything more fun when everyone feels included and cared for."

The End.
