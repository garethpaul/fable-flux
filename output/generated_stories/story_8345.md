# Penny Parrot's Big Dream

In a cozy garden shed filled with old flower pots and rusty tools, lived a bright green parrot named Penny. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds in the dusty sunlight that peeked through the wooden slats.

Every morning, Penny would perch on an old watering can and watch the gardener tend to the beautiful flowers outside. "Oh, how I wish I could make something beautiful too!" she would chirp to herself.

One sunny day, Penny noticed some colorful paint cans sitting on a high shelf. "That's it!" she squawked excitedly. "I want to paint the most wonderful picture ever! But how can I reach those paints?"

Penny flapped her wings and tried to fly up to the shelf, but it was much too high. She felt sad and sat on her favorite watering can with drooping feathers.

"I need a plan," Penny said thoughtfully, tilting her bright orange head. "What can I use to help me reach up there?"

She looked around the shed with her sharp eyes. There was a tall ladder leaning against the wall! But it was heavy and wouldn't budge when she pushed it with her small body.

"Maybe I can find another way," Penny chirped, not giving up. She spotted some wooden boxes stacked in the corner. "I can move these one by one!"

Using her strong beak and claws, Penny carefully pushed the first small box across the floor. *Scrape, scrape, scrape* went the box on the wooden floor. It was hard work, but she kept going.

"One box down, two more to go!" she panted, wiping her beak with her wing.

After much effort, Penny had stacked all three boxes into a wobbly tower. She climbed up carefully, step by step, until she could just reach the paint cans with her beak.

"Hooray!" she celebrated, carefully carrying down a can of bright blue paint, then yellow, then red.

Penny found an old piece of wood and began to paint. She dipped her tail feathers in the blue paint and made swirling sky patterns. With her beak, she painted a bright yellow sun. Her little claws helped her add red flowers dancing in the breeze.

*Splash, dab, swish* went the paint as Penny created her masterpiece.

Just then, she heard tiny squeaking sounds. Three little mice peeked out from behind a flower pot, looking cold and scared.

"Oh my!" said Penny. "You look chilly, little friends."

The smallest mouse whispered, "We don't have a warm place to sleep."

Penny looked at her beautiful painting, then at the shivering mice. Her heart felt warm with kindness. "I have an idea! You can use my painting as a cozy blanket!"

She carefully placed her artwork over the mice like a colorful tent. The paint was dry and made a perfect little shelter.

"Thank you, Penny!" squeaked the mice happily. "Your painting is not only beautiful, but it's keeping us warm!"

Penny felt prouder than ever. She had set a goal to create something beautiful, worked hard to solve her problem all by herself, and then shared her creation with friends who needed help.

"Tomorrow, I'll paint another picture," Penny chirped joyfully. "And maybe the mice can help me reach the paints!"

From that day on, Penny learned that when you set a goal, work hard, and believe in yourself, wonderful things can happen. And sharing your success with others makes it even more special.

The End.
