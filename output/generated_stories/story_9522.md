# Benny Bear's Special Art Box

Benny Bear was a small, fluffy cub with soft brown fur and a pink nose that twitched when he was excited. Today, he felt very happy because he had found the perfect place to make art - Grandpa's old garden shed!

The wooden shed smelled like fresh dirt and flowers. Sunlight streamed through the dusty window, making golden spots dance on the floor. Benny had brought his special art box filled with colorful crayons, sparkly stickers, and smooth paper.

"This is perfect!" Benny said, spreading his art supplies on the wooden table. "I can draw pictures of all my favorite things!"

Just as Benny picked up a red crayon, he heard a soft knock on the shed door.

"Can I come in?" asked a tiny voice.

Benny opened the door and saw Rosie Rabbit, her white ears drooping sadly. "Hi, Benny. I saw you go into the shed. It looks so cozy in there."

Benny felt his tummy get tight. He really wanted to keep his special art space all to himself. "Well, I'm making art right now," he said, holding his crayon close to his chest.

Rosie's eyes filled with tears. "Oh, okay. I just thought... maybe we could draw together. I don't have any art supplies at home."

Benny watched Rosie's sad face and felt something funny in his heart. It was like a little squeeze that made him feel uncomfortable. He recognized this feeling - Mama Bear had taught him about emotions.

"I feel worried about sharing," Benny said out loud, practicing what Mama had taught him. "But I also feel sad when I see you crying, Rosie."

Rosie wiped her eyes. "Thank you for telling me how you feel, Benny. I feel lonely when I can't play with friends."

Benny thought hard. The shed felt extra quiet now, and making art by himself didn't seem as fun anymore. He looked at his big box of supplies, then at Rosie's hopeful face.

"You know what?" Benny said, his voice getting stronger. "I have lots of crayons! Would you like to use some? We could draw pictures together!"

Rosie's ears perked up. "Really? Are you sure?"

"Yes!" Benny opened his art box wide. "You can use the blue crayons - they're my second favorites! And look, I have enough paper for both of us!"

They sat at the wooden table together. The shed filled with happy chatter as they drew. Benny made a picture of a sunny garden, while Rosie drew a beautiful rainbow.

"This is so much better than drawing alone!" Benny said, watching Rosie carefully color her rainbow. "I feel warm and happy inside when we share."

"Me too!" Rosie giggled. "And look how pretty our pictures are together!"

When they finished, they hung both pictures on the shed wall. The colorful drawings made the whole space feel brighter and more cheerful.

"Thank you for sharing with me, Benny," Rosie said, giving him a gentle hug. "You're such a good friend."

Benny hugged her back, feeling proud and happy. "I learned something today, Rosie. When I feel worried or scared, I can talk about it. And sharing makes everything more fun!"

From that day on, Benny and Rosie met in the garden shed every week to make art together. Benny learned that understanding his feelings and sharing with friends made his heart feel as bright and colorful as their beautiful pictures.

The End.
