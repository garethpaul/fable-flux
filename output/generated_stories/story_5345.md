# Koala Kori’s Porch Parade

Kori was a calm koala who loved to sit on the peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like lemon tea and warm wood. A soft breeze tickled Kori’s fur. Birds chirped in the gum tree. Tap-tap-tap went tiny raindrops on the roof.

“I like this quiet,” Kori whispered, hugging a small, round drum. “Quiet helps me hear music.”

Kori tapped the drum. Tum-tum. Tum-tum. The sound was gentle, like a heartbeat. A butterfly fluttered by. “Hello,” said Kori. “Would you like a song?”

The butterfly danced in the air. Kori smiled and tapped again. Tum-tum-tum. Tap-tap. Soon, a neighbor possum named Pip climbed onto the porch rail.

“Hi, Kori!” Pip said. “What are you playing?”

“It’s a drum,” said Kori. “Music can say hello. Listen. Tum-tum. Hello, Pip!”

Pip clapped. “Can I try?”

Kori’s calm eyes sparkled. “Yes. We can share. Everyone gets a turn. That is fair.”

Pip tapped softly. Tip-tip-tip. Kori nodded. “Good listening,” Kori said. “Music is about listening and taking turns.”

A little wombat named Willa waddled up the path. She had a tiny toy whistle. “I want to play! I want to play now!” Willa puffed her cheeks and blew. Toot! Toot! The whistle made a bright, squeaky sound.

Kori took a deep breath. The breeze smelled like eucalyptus leaves. “We will all play,” Kori said kindly. “We will make a porch parade. But we must be fair. One, two, three. Kori, then Pip, then Willa. Then we repeat.”

Willa stomped. “I want more turns!”

Kori shook their head gently. “Justice means fair for everyone,” Kori said. “Fair means we all get the same chance. When it is your turn, we will listen.”

Willa looked down and kicked a leaf. “Okay,” she said in a small voice.

Kori tapped the drum. Tum-tum. Pip clacked two smooth porch pebbles together. Click-click. Willa blew the whistle. Toot! The sounds mixed like colors in a picture. Tum-click-toot. Tum-click-toot.

Kori felt happy and warm. “Let’s use our imagination,” Kori said. “Close your eyes. What do you see?”

“I see a bright parade!” Pip squeaked. “With purple hats!”

“I see a tall cake with sprinkles that sing,” Willa giggled. “It goes tralala!”

Kori smiled. “I see sunshine stepping on little puddles. Splish-splash music.”

They played again. Tum-tum. Click-click. Toot! The porch became a street in their minds. The wind was the crowd, whispering, “Hooray!” The lemon tea smelled like a sweet bakery. A cloud was a fluffy drum major hat.

Then a magpie swooped down to the porch railing. “Squawk! That whistle is mine!” the magpie cried, pointing its beak at Willa’s toy.

Willa hugged her whistle. “It’s mine!” she said. Her eyes filled with tears.

Kori stayed calm. “We can solve this,” Kori said. “Justice helps us find what is true and fair. We will listen to everyone. One at a time.”

The magpie ruffled its feathers. “I saw it in the yard,” it said. “I wanted shiny music!”

Kori nodded. “Thank you for telling us. Willa, where did you get the whistle?”

“My nana gave it to me,” Willa said. She patted the little string with her name on it. “See? It even has my name, Willa.”

Kori looked kindly at the magpie. “The whistle belongs to Willa. It has her name. That is fair and true. But we can still share music. You can join our porch parade.”

The magpie blinked. “Share?”

“Yes,” Kori said. “We take turns. Everyone gets a chance.”

The magpie’s voice softened. “I can sing.”

Kori clapped softly. “Wonderful. Let’s set our pattern again. Kori drum. Pip pebbles. Willa whistle. Magpie song. Then repeat.”

They tried it. Tum-tum. Click-click. Toot! La-la-la! The magpie sang a sweet, clear note that floated like a ribbon. Willa smiled. “I like your song.”

“Thank you,” said the magpie. “I like your whistle.”

The porch parade grew bigger in their minds. They imagined bright flags and twinkly lights. The raindrops kept a tiny beat on the roof. Tip-tip-tip. The gum leaves swished like soft shakers. Shhh-shhh.

Kori raised a paw. “Now, let’s make a new song,” Kori said. “We will listen. We will take turns. We will be fair.”

They made a pattern: loud, soft, soft. Tum! click, click. Toot! la, la. Kori counted gently. “One, two, three. Great listening!”

Pip grinned. “Music feels good when it’s fair.”

Willa nodded. “And when we imagine.”

The magpie fluffed its feathers. “And when we sing together.”

Kori felt proud and calm. “Today we learned three things,” Kori said. “Music is for everyone. Imagination makes music fun. Justice means we share and take turns so it is fair.”

The sun popped from behind a cloud and warmed the porch. The lemon tea steamed. The friends sat together, happy and quiet, hearing the last soft beat of their song.

Tum. Click. Toot. La.

The End.
