# Pippin the Penguin Learns to Try Again

Pippin the penguin waddled into the bright, cozy bathroom with his orange flippers flapping excitedly. The white tiles sparkled under the warm light, and fluffy towels hung neatly on their hooks.

"Today I'm going to brush my teeth ALL BY MYSELF!" Pippin announced proudly to his reflection in the shiny mirror.

He climbed onto his special step stool with a happy "hop, hop, hop!" His black and white feathers ruffled with excitement as he reached for his small blue toothbrush.

Squeeze, squeeze went the toothpaste tube. But oh no! A big glob of minty toothpaste plopped right into the sink with a splat.

"Oops!" giggled Pippin. "That's okay. I'll try again!"

He squeezed more carefully this time. The toothpaste made a perfect little stripe on his brush. Pippin felt so proud as he began brushing his beak.

Brush, brush, brush went his flipper. But the toothbrush slipped! Foam splashed on the mirror and dripped down like white snow.

"Oh my!" Pippin said, looking at the messy mirror. His tummy felt a little sad. "I made such a mess. Mama won't be happy."

Pippin's bottom lip trembled. He wanted to give up and call for help. But then he remembered what his mama always said: "When things get tough, little penguins don't give up!"

"I can fix this," Pippin said bravely. "I'll keep trying!"

He grabbed a soft washcloth and carefully wiped the mirror clean. The cloth felt warm and squishy in his flipper. Soon the mirror sparkled again like ice in the sunshine.

"There!" Pippin smiled. "Now let me try brushing again."

This time, Pippin held his toothbrush with both flippers. Slowly and carefully, he brushed each tooth. The minty foam tasted fresh and cool in his mouth.

"I'm doing it! I'm really doing it!" he whispered excitedly.

When he finished brushing, Pippin rinsed his mouth and wiped his beak with a fluffy towel. He looked in the clean mirror and grinned, showing his sparkling teeth.

Just then, Mama Penguin peeked into the bathroom. "How did tooth brushing go, my little one?"

Pippin's cheeks turned pink. "Well, Mama, I made a big mess at first. Toothpaste went everywhere! But I didn't give up. I cleaned it all up and tried again until I got it right."

Mama Penguin's eyes twinkled with pride. "Oh, Pippin! I'm so proud of you for not giving up. And thank you for cleaning up your mess. That shows you're growing up to be a responsible little penguin."

She gave him the biggest, warmest hug. Pippin felt so happy and proud in his mama's flippers.

"It felt good to keep trying," Pippin said. "And it felt even better to make things right when I made a mistake."

"That's what persistence means," Mama explained. "It means trying again and again, even when things are hard. And making amends means fixing our mistakes. You did both today!"

Pippin beamed with joy. From that day on, whenever something seemed too hard, he remembered his bathroom adventure. He learned that making mistakes was okay, as long as he kept trying and made things right.

The End.
