# Melody's Morning Promise

In a cozy nursery with soft yellow walls, a little blue bird named Melody lived in a beautiful wooden birdhouse. The nursery smelled like lavender and felt warm from the morning sunshine streaming through lace curtains.

Every day, Melody had an important job. She sang gentle wake-up songs for baby Emma, who slept in the white crib nearby.

"Tweet-tweet-trill! Good morning, sunshine!" Melody sang sweetly.

Baby Emma giggled and clapped her tiny hands. Her parents, Mama and Papa, smiled at Melody.

"Thank you, Melody," Mama said softly. "Your songs help Emma wake up happy."

Melody felt proud. She puffed out her little chest. Being responsible felt wonderful!

But this morning was different. Melody felt tired. Her throat felt scratchy. Her beautiful blue feathers looked messy.

"Maybe I don't need to sing today," Melody thought. "Emma will wake up anyway."

Melody stayed quiet in her birdhouse. She watched Emma sleep peacefully.

Soon, Emma began to stir. Without Melody's gentle song, Emma woke up confused and scared. She started crying loudly.

"Waaah! Waaah!" Emma sobbed.

Mama rushed in, looking worried. "Oh, sweet baby, what's wrong?"

Papa picked up Emma, but she kept crying. The nursery felt sad without Melody's cheerful music.

Melody watched from her birdhouse. Her heart felt heavy. "Emma needs me," she realized. "Even when I'm tired, I have a job to do."

But first, Melody knew she needed to take care of herself. She flew to her water dish and took small sips. She ate some healthy seeds. She preened her feathers carefully.

"If I don't take care of myself, I can't take care of others," Melody said wisely.

After feeling better, Melody flew to her favorite perch near Emma's crib.

"Tweet-tweet-trill! Don't cry, little one!" she sang gently.

Emma stopped crying immediately. Her big brown eyes found Melody. She smiled and reached her chubby fingers toward the bird.

"There's our special friend," Papa said, relieved.

Melody sang a soft, soothing song. Emma giggled and cooed along. The nursery filled with joy again.

"I'm sorry I didn't sing earlier," Melody chirped to Emma. "I was tired, but I learned something important. Taking care of myself helps me take care of you better."

Emma babbled happily, as if she understood.

From that day on, Melody made a promise. Every evening, she would eat well, drink fresh water, and get plenty of sleep. Every morning, she would sing her best songs for Emma.

"Being responsible means keeping my promises," Melody sang. "And taking care of myself so I can help others too!"

Mama heard Melody's song and smiled. "You're such a good friend, Melody. Thank you for being so responsible."

Melody's heart soared. She learned that responsibility wasn't just about doing her job. It was about preparing herself to do her best work. When she took care of herself, she could take better care of Emma.

The nursery felt perfect again - filled with sunshine, love, and Melody's beautiful songs.

"Tweet-tweet-trill! Every day is better when we keep our promises!" Melody sang joyfully.

Emma clapped and laughed, and the whole nursery sparkled with happiness.

The End.
