# Binky Bunny Writes in the Bathroom

Binky Bunny was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. One bright morning, Binky hopped into the bathroom. The tiles felt cool on his paws. Drip, drip went the faucet. Swish, swish went the toothbrush in its cup. The bathroom smelled like minty soap and warm towels.

“I will write my story today,” Binky said. He wiggled his whiskers. “I am brave. I can do it.”

Binky placed a small notebook on the closed toilet seat, like a tiny desk. He held a big pencil in his paws. He took a deep breath. The mirror shone back at him, and he smiled at his reflection.

“Hello, writer Binky,” he giggled.

Binky loved the bathroom. It was quiet. The light was soft. He could hear only the gentle drip, drip. It helped him think. He tapped the pencil. Tap, tap. “I will write about breakfast,” he said.

Just then, Mama Rabbit peeked in. “Good morning, Binky. What are you doing?”

“I am writing my story,” said Binky. “I am a determined rabbit!”

Mama Rabbit smiled. “That sounds wonderful. Do you need a snack first?”

“Yes, please,” Binky said. “A snack can help my brain.”

Mama brought carrot sticks, apple slices, and a small cup of water. The carrots were crunchy. Crunch, crunch. The apples were cool and sweet. Munch, munch. The water was fresh. Sip, sip.

“These are healthy snacks,” Mama said. “Good nutrition helps you think and write.”

Binky nodded. “Thank you, Mama. The carrots help my words grow.”

He turned to his notebook. He wrote big, round letters. He sounded them out. “B-r-e-a-k-f-a-s-t,” he whispered. “Breakfast.” He drew a little carrot with a smile.

But writing is not always easy. Binky felt his ears droop. “I have an idea,” he said, “but it is jumbled.” He looked at the mirror. He looked at the drip, drip. He smelled the minty soap. He listened to the soft fan hum. Hmmm.

“I can use my senses,” Binky said. “I can write what I see, hear, smell, and feel.”

He wrote, “I hear drip. I smell mint. I feel cool tile. I see my smile.” His paws trembled a little, but he kept going. He was determined.

Mama Rabbit came back with a gentle pat. “How is the writing, Binky?”

Binky looked at his page. It was messy. Some letters were wiggly. A crumb had fallen on the corner. He felt a little shy. “It is not perfect,” he said softly. “Maybe I should say I wrote a lot, even if I did not.”

Mama knelt beside him. “Honesty matters,” she said kindly. “Telling the truth helps your heart feel light. You can be proud of your real work, even if it is small.”

Binky took a deep breath. “Okay. I will be honest. I wrote a little, and I can write more.”

“That is brave,” Mama said. “Small steps make big stories.”

Binky smiled. He ate one more apple slice. The sweet taste woke up his brain. He stretched his paws. “I can try again.”

He read his words out loud. “I hear drip. I smell mint. I feel cool tile. I see my smile.” He looked around and added, “I taste apple. Crunch!”

Binky giggled. “This is fun!”

He drew a shiny water droplet and a green toothbrush with tiny bristles. He wrote slowly, “I like to write.” He added, “I tell the truth.”

Binky paused. He had an idea. “I will write about me,” he said. “I am a determined rabbit. I try, and I eat smart snacks.”

He wrote, “I am Binky. I am brave. I write and tell the truth. Carrots help my brain. Water helps my body. I can do hard things.”

The mirror seemed to glow. Binky stood taller on the cool tile. He could feel warm pride in his chest, like sunlight on his fur.

Mama Rabbit read his page and clapped softly. “Binky, this is beautiful writing. You used your senses. You told the truth. You chose healthy snacks. You kept going.”

Binky’s ears perked up. “Thank you, Mama. Writing makes me happy.”

Mama nodded. “Writing is a way to share your ideas. It helps your words grow strong, just like good food helps your body grow strong.”

Binky looked at his notebook. The page was not perfect, but it was full of real, brave words. He carefully put his pencil back in the cup. Swish, swish went the toothbrush beside it. Drip, drip went the faucet. Everything felt calm.

“I finished my bathroom story,” Binky said. “Can I read it to you at lunch?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “We will have crunchy carrots, juicy apples, and cool water. You can read your true, creative words.”

Binky hopped happily. “I am a writer,” he said. “I am honest. I eat healthy snacks. I try and try.”

He hugged Mama. His fur felt soft. The bathroom smelled like mint and fresh towels. Binky smiled at his reflection one more time.

“Hello, writer Binky,” he whispered. “Let’s write again tomorrow.”

The End.
