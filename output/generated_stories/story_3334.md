# Gigi's Colorful Surprise

Gigi the giraffe loved the bright, cheerful art room at her school. The walls were covered with rainbow paintings, and shelves held jars full of colorful crayons, brushes, and sparkly stickers. The sweet smell of paint and the soft feel of clay always made her happy.

One sunny morning, Gigi walked into the art room with her long neck held high. But something looked different today. Mrs. Robin, their art teacher, was sitting at her desk looking worried.

"Oh my," Mrs. Robin sighed. "The little bunnies from the nursery class are coming for art time, but I haven't had time to set up their supplies. I have so much to do!"

Gigi's big brown eyes looked around the messy room. Paint brushes were scattered everywhere, and the tables needed to be wiped clean. The art smocks were in a jumbled pile, and the crayons were all mixed up.

"Mrs. Robin," said Gigi in her gentle voice, "I can help you! I have long legs and a long neck. I can reach high places and move quickly!"

Mrs. Robin's face brightened. "Oh, Gigi! That would be wonderful, but are you sure? It's a lot of work."

"I'm sure!" Gigi said with a big smile. "I want to help make art time special for the little bunnies."

Gigi didn't wait to be asked twice. She stretched her long neck up high to get the clean paint brushes from the top shelf. Her hooves clicked happily on the floor as she walked from table to table, placing brushes in neat rows.

Next, she used her gentle mouth to pick up the scattered crayons. Red ones went in the red cup, blue ones in the blue cup, and yellow ones in the yellow cup. The crayons made soft clinking sounds as they dropped into their homes.

"Gigi, you're amazing!" said Mrs. Robin as she watched the giraffe organize the art smocks by size. "The little bunnies will love these!"

Gigi's heart felt warm and happy. She wiped the tables with a soft cloth until they sparkled. Then she had an idea that made her eyes twinkle.

"Mrs. Robin, can I make welcome cards for each little bunny?" Gigi asked.

"What a thoughtful idea!" Mrs. Robin clapped her hands.

Gigi carefully drew a picture on each card with bright crayons. She made one with a smiling sun, another with a pretty flower, and one with a happy rainbow. Each card said "Welcome to Art Time!" in her best writing.

Just as Gigi finished the last card, she heard tiny footsteps in the hallway. The little bunnies were coming!

"Wow!" squeaked the first little bunny as he hopped into the room. "Everything looks so pretty and neat!"

"Look! We each have our own special card!" said another bunny, holding up Gigi's drawing.

Mrs. Robin smiled at Gigi. "Thank you for taking the initiative to help, Gigi. Because you saw what needed to be done and did it without being asked, the little bunnies are having the best art day ever!"

Gigi felt proud and happy inside. She learned that when you see someone who needs help, you don't have to wait to be asked. Taking the first step to help others feels wonderful and makes everyone's day brighter.

The art room filled with giggles and happy chatter as the little bunnies began to paint, and Gigi knew she had made the right choice.

The End.
