# Rocco's Fair Music Corner

Rocco the raccoon loved making music in his cozy study corner. He had a tiny drum made from an old tin can. He had a guitar with colorful strings. He had bells that jingled like raindrops. Every day, he sat on his soft blue cushion and played happy songs.

But Rocco had a big problem. Three mean squirrels kept coming to his study corner. They would grab his instruments without asking. They would play them very loudly and roughly. Then they would laugh and run away.

"That's not fair!" said Rocco one morning. The squirrels had just taken his favorite drum. "Those are my instruments. They should ask first!"

Rocco felt sad and angry. His fluffy gray tail drooped down. His little black paws shook. He wanted to hide under his cushion and never come out.

"Maybe I should just give up," whispered Rocco. "Maybe I don't deserve to have nice things."

But then Rocco remembered something important. His mama had taught him that everyone deserves to be treated kindly. Everyone deserves to feel safe and happy.

"I need to stand up for what's right," said Rocco bravely. "But I need to do it in a good way."

The next morning, the three squirrels came back. They reached for Rocco's bells with their grabby paws.

"Stop!" said Rocco in a clear, strong voice. "Those bells are mine. Taking things without asking is not okay."

The biggest squirrel laughed. "We're bigger than you, little raccoon! We can take whatever we want!"

Rocco's heart beat fast like his tiny drum. But he stood tall on his study corner cushion.

"Being bigger doesn't make it right," said Rocco. "How would you feel if someone took your things?"

The smallest squirrel stopped laughing. She looked down at her fuzzy feet. "I... I wouldn't like that," she said quietly.

"Everyone deserves to feel safe and happy," said Rocco. "That includes me, and that includes you too."

Rocco had an idea. His brown eyes sparkled like stars.

"Would you like to make music together?" asked Rocco. "We could take turns. We could share. That would be fair for everyone."

The squirrels looked surprised. No one had ever invited them to play before.

"Really?" asked the middle squirrel. "You would share with us?"

"Yes," said Rocco with a warm smile. "But we need rules. We ask before we touch. We take turns. We play gently. And we use kind words."

The smallest squirrel nodded. "That sounds fair," she said.

Soon, all four friends were making beautiful music together. Rocco played his drum - tap, tap, tap! The biggest squirrel shook the bells - jingle, jangle, jing! The other squirrels took turns with the guitar - strum, strum, strum!

"This is the best music ever!" laughed the smallest squirrel.

Rocco felt warm and happy inside. His study corner was filled with friendship and fair play. He had stood up for what was right, but he had also been kind and inclusive.

"When we treat each other fairly," said Rocco, "everyone wins!"

From that day on, Rocco's study corner became the happiest place in the whole forest. All the animals knew they would be treated with kindness and respect there.

And Rocco learned that standing up for justice doesn't mean being mean. It means speaking up for what's right and finding ways for everyone to be happy together.

The End.
