# Tiko’s Tiny Map in the Closet

Tiko the wise tortoise liked quiet places. His shell was smooth and warm like a round stone in the sun. His favorite spot was a cozy closet hideaway. It smelled like clean cotton and cedar blocks. There was a soft rug, a tiny lamp, and a low shelf with paper and crayons.

One rainy morning, drip, drip, drip sang on the window. Tiko blinked his gentle eyes and crawled into the closet. He turned on the tiny lamp. The light felt golden and soft. He sighed, calm and happy.

“I will write today,” Tiko said in his slow, kind voice. “I will make a map of our world.”

He took a big sheet of paper. He picked up a blue crayon. Scritch, scratch, the crayon whispered as he drew a big circle. He smiled. “A circle for the world,” he said. “So many places. So many friends.”

Tap tap. Someone knocked on the closet door.

“Come in,” Tiko called.

The door opened, and three friends peeked in. There was Mina the mouse with tiny bright eyes, Pako the parrot with green feathers like leaves, and Suri the snail with a shiny shell.

“Hi, Tiko!” said Mina. “It is rainy. Can we sit in your closet hideaway?”

“Of course,” said Tiko. “Come in. We can write together.”

The friends curled up on the soft rug. They listened to the rain. The closet felt snug and safe.

“What are you drawing?” asked Pako.

“A map,” said Tiko. “A map of our big world. We will learn about faraway places.”

Mina’s whiskers twitched. “But I have never been far away. I only know the kitchen and the garden.”

“That is okay,” Tiko said gently. “We can learn from each other. We can be calm and curious.”

Suri slid closer. “What can I do?”

“You can help with colors,” Tiko said. “We will write names and draw pictures. We will be kind and take turns.”

They began. Tiko drew a wiggly line and said, “Here is a river. Rivers sing in many lands.” He hummed a soft tune. The friends hummed with him. Hmmm, hmmm. Calm music filled the closet.

Pako picked up a yellow crayon. “Where is the warm place with big sun?” he asked. “My cousin lives where mangoes grow.”

Tiko pointed. “Here. Let’s draw a bright sun and a mango tree.”

Scritch, scratch. The mango tree looked sweet and golden. “Mmm,” said Mina. “I can almost taste it.”

Mina drew a snowy mountain with a white crayon. “I saw snow in a picture book,” she said. “It looked cold.”

Suri added tiny dots like snowflakes. “Cold can be nice,” said Suri. “It makes you slow and calm. Like me!”

They all giggled.

Tiko wrote simple words under each picture. He wrote Sun. He wrote Snow. He wrote River. He said each word slowly, and the friends said them too. The letters looked like little steps around the world.

Then Pako squawked, “Let’s draw my favorite food! Crackers!”

Mina squeaked, “But I like cheese!”

Suri said, “I like lettuce!”

They all spoke at once. The closet buzzed. The map began to look messy.

Tiko took a deep breath. He closed his eyes. He felt the soft rug under his toes. He listened to the rain. Drip, drip, drip.

“Friends,” he said kindly. “Let us be calm. Our world is big. It has crackers and cheese and lettuce. It has many foods, many songs, many clothes, many ways. We can make room on the map for all.”

Pako ruffled his feathers and nodded. Mina tucked her nose and smiled. Suri made a tiny cheer, “Yay!”

They made three little baskets on the map and labeled them: Crackers, Cheese, Lettuce. They drew a drum for music, a scarf for clothes, and a book for stories.

“People far away may do things in new ways,” Tiko said. “That is okay. We can be gentle and listen. We can learn. That is called global awareness.”

“Glo-bal a-ware-ness,” Mina said slowly. “Knowing about many places and people.”

“Yes,” said Tiko. “Knowing, caring, and being fair.”

The friends took turns. They asked, “May I draw now?” and they waited. They shared crayons. They spoke softly. The closet felt peaceful again.

Soon, the map was bright and beautiful. It had rivers and mountains, suns and snow, foods and drums, scarves and books. It looked like a hug around the world.

“Let’s add friends,” Pako said.

They drew tiny hearts in many parts of the map. Mina wrote, “Hello!” next to one heart. Suri wrote, “Hola!” next to another. Pako wrote, “Jambo!” Tiko wrote, “Namaste!” The words looked different, but each meant “Hello, friend.”

Tiko smiled. “Different words. Same kindness.”

The rain slowed to a soft pat. The lamp glowed warm. The closet felt like a tiny world full of love.

Mina yawned a happy yawn. “I like learning about faraway places,” she said.

“I do too,” said Pako. “Next time, we can learn a song from a new place.”

“And a snack,” said Suri. “We can share.”

Tiko rolled up the map and tied it with a ribbon. “Today we were calm. We were kind. We worked together. We learned about our big world.”

He tapped the map with his gentle foot. “Our lesson: The world is full of different people and ways. When we listen and share, we understand and care.”

The friends hugged. Their hug felt soft and safe.

“Thank you, Tiko,” they said.

“Thank you, friends,” Tiko said. “Our closet hideaway is small, but our hearts are big.”

The End.
