# Rosie Rabbit's Garden Helper Day

Rosie Rabbit hopped excitedly toward the big wooden garden shed. Today was special - Farmer Joe had asked her to help organize all the garden tools before spring planting time!

"I can do this!" Rosie said to herself, her fluffy tail wiggling with excitement. She pushed open the creaky shed door and gasped. "Oh my carrots!"

Inside the dusty shed, everything was mixed up. Shovels were tangled with hoses, flower pots were stacked on rakes, and seeds were scattered everywhere. The sweet smell of old wood mixed with the earthy scent of soil filled her pink nose.

Farmer Joe appeared in the doorway, his kind eyes twinkling. "Hello, Rosie! I know it looks like a big job, but I wrote down some simple steps to help you." He handed her a piece of paper with pictures and words.

Rosie looked at the list:
1. Put all shovels in the tall corner
2. Coil hoses on the wall hooks
3. Stack flower pots by size
4. Put seeds in the labeled boxes
5. Sweep the floor clean

"These directions will help you finish before lunch time," Farmer Joe said gently. "Take your time and follow each step. You're very capable, little rabbit!"

At first, Rosie felt overwhelmed. The shed seemed so messy! But she took a deep breath and looked at step one. "Put all shovels in the tall corner," she read aloud.

Hop, hop, hop! Rosie gathered the big shovels, medium shovels, and tiny hand shovels. Her strong paws carried them one by one to the corner. "Step one - done!" she cheered.

Next, she found the long green hoses. They were heavy and wiggly like giant snakes! Rosie remembered step two and carefully coiled each hose, hanging them on the metal hooks. The hoses made a satisfying *thunk* as they settled into place.

"This is fun!" Rosie giggled, checking her list again.

For step three, she sorted the flower pots. Big clay pots went on the bottom, medium plastic pots in the middle, and tiny seed pots on top. They made a perfect tower that looked like a pot pyramid!

The seed packets were tricky - there were so many! Rosie found the special boxes labeled "Flowers," "Vegetables," and "Herbs." She carefully read each packet and sorted them. The colorful pictures of tomatoes, sunflowers, and basil made her mouth water.

Finally, step five - sweeping! Rosie found the small broom and swept all the dirt and leaves into a neat pile. The wooden floor now gleamed in the sunlight streaming through the window.

"Wow!" said Farmer Joe, returning just as Rosie finished. "You followed every direction perfectly! Look how organized and clean everything is now."

Rosie beamed with pride. Her whiskers twitched happily as she looked around the tidy shed. "Following the directions made it so much easier! And I finished right on time for lunch!"

"You showed great courage tackling such a big job," Farmer Joe said, patting her soft head. "When we follow directions step by step, even big tasks become manageable."

Rosie hopped home feeling proud and accomplished. She had learned that following directions, taking her time, and staying organized made everything work out perfectly. From that day on, whenever she had a big job to do, she always asked for clear directions and followed them carefully.

The End.
