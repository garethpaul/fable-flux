# Poppy the Penguin’s Shed Routine

Poppy was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a happy wobble. She lived near a little garden, and at the edge of the garden stood a neat, green shed. The shed smelled like fresh wood and clean earth. Inside, there were shiny watering cans, soft gloves, and rows of little pots.

Every morning, Poppy waddled to the shed and tapped the door gently. Tap, tap. “Good morning, shed,” she chirped. “It’s time for our routine.”

Routine means doing the same helpful things each day. It made Poppy feel calm inside, like a soft, warm blanket.

Poppy opened the door. Creak. She smiled at the sunbeams dancing on the floor. “Hello, tools,” she said. “Let’s get ready.”

She had a list on a tiny chalkboard:
- Sweep the floor
- Water the seedlings
- Put tools back
- Check the door

Poppy took a deep breath. The shed smelled like mint leaves and a hint of soil. “Mmm,” she hummed. “I can do this.”

First, Poppy picked up a small broom. Swish, swish. She swept leaves and little dirt crumbs into a tidy pile. “There you go,” she whispered to the floor. “All clean.” A little spider waved from a corner web. Poppy waved back. “Hi, tiny helper.”

Next, Poppy reached for the shiny watering can. It felt cool in her flippers. “Water time,” she sang softly. She filled the can and listened to the gentle gush. Gurgle, gurgle. Then she waddled to the row of seedlings in the window. The tiny leaves were bright and green.

“Good morning, sprouts,” Poppy said. “Sip, sip.” She poured carefully. Drip, drip, patter. The soil turned dark and cozy. The seedlings looked happy, standing straight like little dancers.

Poppy’s friend, Tilly the snail, peeked from behind a pot. “Hello, Poppy,” Tilly said slowly. “You are always so calm.”

Poppy smiled. “I like our routine. It helps me remember what to do. It helps me be responsible.”

“What is responsible?” Tilly asked.

“It means taking care of things,” said Poppy. “Even small things. Even when no one is watching.”

Tilly nodded. “That is kind.”

Poppy put the watering can back in its place. Clink. Then she checked the gloves. She patted them gently and matched each glove with its friend. “Together,” she said. “Everything has a home.”

Just then, Poppy heard a flutter at the door. It was Freddie the finch. He zipped in and landed on a rake. “Tweet! Poppy, the big puddle is perfect for splashing! Come now!”

Poppy looked at her list. She looked at the happy rake. She wanted to splash. Her feet wiggled. “I love splashing,” she said. “But first, I finish my jobs.”

Freddie tilted his head. “But the puddle is here right now.”

Poppy took a slow breath. In and out. She put a finger on her list. “Routine first. Then splash.” Her voice was calm and kind.

Freddie hopped to a shelf. “Need help?” he asked.

“Yes, please,” Poppy said. She handed him a soft cloth. “Let’s wipe the trowels.”

They worked together. Wipe, wipe. The metal shone in the sun. Poppy hummed her gentle song. “Wipe and put back, wipe and put back.”

Freddie chirped along. “Tweet and wipe, tweet and wipe!”

When they finished, Poppy checked the last item. “Check the door.”

She went to the door and pushed it. Click. The latch felt snug. “Safe and shut,” she said. “All done.”

Poppy stood still for a moment. Her tummy felt warm. Her mind felt quiet. She had done her jobs. She felt proud.

Tilly smiled. “You were responsible,” she said.

Freddie flapped his wings. “And calm!”

Poppy giggled. “Routine helps me be both!”

They went outside. The air was cool and sweet. Birds sang softly. The puddle sparkled with tiny rainbows. Poppy waddled in. Splish! Freddie fluttered across the water. Tilly slipped along the edge. Splash, giggle, splash!

After splashing, Poppy shook off the drops. She looked back at the shed. It stood neat and bright. She felt a gentle joy in her chest. “Thank you, shed,” she called. “See you tomorrow for our routine.”

Freddie landed on her shoulder. “Poppy, what if the puddle is gone tomorrow?” he asked.

Poppy patted his wing. “Puddles come and go. But taking care is every day. When I do my jobs first, I can play with a happy heart.”

Tilly nodded. “Slow and steady, job by job.”

Poppy laughed. “Yes. Slow, steady, and cheerful.”

They sat in the soft grass. The sun felt warm on their faces. A light breeze smelled like lavender and leaves. Poppy closed her eyes for a second and breathed in. Calm. Then she opened them and smiled at her friends.

“I can be responsible,” Poppy said. “I can be calm. I can follow my routine.”

Freddie chirped, “And then we can splash!”

Poppy clapped her flippers. “Exactly.”

The friends shared a quiet hug. The garden hummed kindly. The shed waited for tomorrow, and Poppy felt ready.

The lesson was clear in Poppy’s happy heart: When we do our jobs first, with calm and care, our playtime feels even better.

The End.
