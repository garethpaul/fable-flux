# Ollie Otter Says “I’m Sorry”

Ollie the playful otter loved to splash, swish, and slide. Today, the sun was warm and bright in the sunny backyard. The grass smelled sweet, like fresh hay. Bees buzzed softly by the yellow flowers. A tiny fountain bubbled, and Ollie clapped his paws. “Splish-splash time!” he cheered.

In the backyard lived friends: Bella the bluebird, Gus the gentle tortoise, and Moppy the fluffy mop dog. They liked quiet games and tidy spaces. “Good morning!” chirped Bella from the birdbath. “Hi, Ollie!” said Gus with a slow smile. Moppy wagged his tail, “Woof-woof!”

Ollie wiggled his whiskers. “Let’s play water slide!” He ran to the fountain and pushed a big bucket. Water whooshed over the grass. It sparkled like diamonds. “Wheee!” Ollie slid on a leaf, swish-swoosh, and landed with a plop. He laughed and clapped. Splash! Some water splattered onto Bella’s feathers.

Bella fluttered her wings. “Oh! My feathers feel soggy,” she said softly.

Ollie giggled, still bouncing. “It’s just water! Fun, fun!”

Gus blinked and looked at the garden. “Hmm,” he said, “the flowers are drooping.” The water had rushed over the flower bed. Soil slipped, and petals sagged. The air smelled muddy now.

Moppy sniffed. “Woof?” He stepped in a puddle. Squish! Mud splashed on his clean fur. He whined and tucked his tail.

Bella fluttered to a branch. “I don’t like the splash on me,” she said in a quiet voice. “It makes me cold.”

Gus nodded. “And the flowers need gentle drinks, not big floods. The worms and bugs live there.”

Ollie’s happy bounce slowed. He looked around. The grass was messy. The flowers looked tired. Bella shivered. Moppy’s paws were muddy. Ollie’s tummy felt tight, like a knot. His whiskers drooped.

“I… I didn’t mean to make a mess,” Ollie whispered. He felt a warm tear on his cheek. “I wanted to play.”

Bella fluttered down and tilted her head. “We know you wanted to play,” she said. “Can we talk in kind voices?”

Gus nodded. “Yes. Peaceful words help.”

Ollie took a deep breath. He placed his paws on his chest. “I can listen,” he said.

Bella spoke softly, “When the water splashed on me, I felt cold and sad. I want to stay fluffy and dry.”

Gus said, “When the water rushed, the flowers got hurt. I want the garden safe for bees and bugs.”

Moppy wagged once and gave a tiny “woof,” as if to say, “I want clean paws, please.”

Ollie nodded. “Thank you for telling me.” He looked at each friend. “I am sorry, Bella, for splashing you. I am sorry, Gus, for washing the flowers too hard. I am sorry, Moppy, for muddy paws.” He stood tall and said clearly, “I will fix it.”

Bella smiled. “Thank you for saying sorry.”

“What can we do?” asked Ollie. His voice was small but brave.

Gus looked thoughtful. “We can work together,” he said. “We can care for the yard.”

Ollie’s eyes brightened. “Yes! Team clean!”

First, Ollie brought a small towel. “Bella, may I help?” he asked.

“Yes, please,” Bella chirped. Ollie dabbed her feathers very gently. “There,” he said. “Warm and dry.”

Next, Gus showed Ollie how to tuck the soil back. “Use slow hands,” said Gus. Ollie patted the dirt around the flowers, one, two, three pats. He stood a little fence of sticks to guide water away. “This will protect the petals,” Gus said. They added a small, clean cup to give tiny sips to each plant. Sip, sip, sip.

Moppy wagged. Ollie filled a shallow pan with warm water and a drop of soap. “Foot bath!” he said. Moppy dipped his paws. Swish-swish. Ollie dried them with a soft cloth. Moppy licked Ollie’s paw and gave a happy “woof!”

They all looked around. The backyard smelled fresh again. The sun felt cozy on their fur and feathers. Bees hummed. The fountain bubbled softly.

Ollie smiled, but he also remembered. “I will play in safe ways,” he said. “I will keep water in the splash zone.” He pointed to a small splash mat far from the flowers. “Here is our water play place.”

Bella chirped, “That sounds kind.”

Gus nodded. “That sounds safe.”

Moppy wagged so hard his ears flopped.

They played a new game. Ollie splashed only on the mat. Bella sang a sunny song. Gus counted clouds, “One, two, three fluffy puffs.” Moppy chased a squeaky toy, squeak-squeak!

When the game ended, Ollie checked the garden. “Flowers okay?” he asked.

Gus smiled. “Flowers happy,” he said.

Ollie felt the knot in his tummy untie. He felt light and bright again. “Thank you for listening,” he told his friends. “And thank you for telling me how you feel.”

Bella chirped, “Thank you for saying sorry and fixing it.”

Ollie wiggled his whiskers. “Saying ‘I’m sorry’ helps hearts feel better,” he said. “And caring for the earth keeps our home happy.”

They all sat in the warm sun, peaceful and pleased, as the fountain whispered and the bees buzzed.

The End.
