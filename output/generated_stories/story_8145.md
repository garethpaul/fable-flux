# Benny's Big Bathroom Problem

Benny the rabbit loved to play outside. He loved rolling in the dirt, splashing in puddles, and digging holes in the garden. But there was one thing Benny did not love - taking baths.

"Bath time, Benny!" called Mom from the bathroom. The water was running. Steam floated up like fluffy clouds.

Benny's ears drooped down. His whiskers twitched nervously. "Do I have to?" he asked, looking at his muddy paws.

"Yes, little bunny," Mom said gently. "You're covered in dirt from head to toe!"

Benny hopped slowly to the bathroom. The white tiles felt cold under his paws. The bathtub looked big and scary. The water made splashing sounds that echoed off the walls.

"I don't like baths," Benny said, sitting down with a thump. "The water is too wet. The soap makes bubbles that pop. And I can't play outside when I'm in here."

Mom sat beside him on the bathroom floor. "What if we could make bath time more fun?" she asked. "What do you love most about playing outside?"

Benny's ears perked up a little. "I love digging tunnels and finding treasures," he said. "And I love watching the clouds float by."

"Hmm," said Mom, tapping her chin. "Maybe we can bring some of that magic inside."

Benny thought hard. His nose wiggled as he got an idea. "What if... what if we pretend the bathtub is a big pond? Like the one in our garden!"

"That's a wonderful idea!" Mom said, clapping her hands.

Benny hopped up, excited now. "And we could use my toy boats! They could be like lily pads floating on the water!"

Mom smiled. "What else could we add?"

Benny looked around the bathroom with new eyes. "The bubbles could be like clouds! And my rubber duck could be a friendly fish swimming in our pond!"

Together, they filled the tub with warm water. Mom added bubble bath that smelled like lavender flowers. Benny carefully placed his toy boats in the water. They bobbed and floated like real lily pads.

"Look!" Benny said, pointing at the mirror. "The steam makes the mirror look like a foggy morning by the pond!"

As Benny climbed into the warm water, he giggled. "This feels just like the pond, but warmer!" He splashed gently, making ripples that moved his boat lily pads.

"And look," Mom said, scooping up some bubbles. "You can blow the bubble clouds around, just like the wind does outside."

Benny blew softly at the bubbles. They danced through the air and popped with tiny, magical sounds. "This is the best pond ever!" he laughed.

He washed his muddy paws, pretending they were little fish swimming in the water. He shampooed his ears, imagining he was a water lily getting ready to bloom.

When bath time was over, Benny felt clean and happy. His fur was soft and fluffy. He smelled like flowers instead of dirt.

"Mom," Benny said as he dried off with his favorite towel, "I had the best idea ever! Tomorrow, can we pretend the bathtub is an ocean? I could be a brave sea captain!"

Mom hugged him tight. "Of course, my creative little bunny. You turned something you didn't like into something wonderful."

Benny learned that when something seems scary or boring, thinking of new ways to make it fun can change everything. Sometimes the best solutions come from using your imagination and being honest about what you need.

The End.
