# Kody the Koala's Fair Play Lab

In a cozy basement lab filled with colorful bottles and fun gadgets, lived a calm koala named Kody. Every morning, Kody would put on his special white coat and check all his equipment. "Good morning, lab!" he would say cheerfully, patting each beaker and tube.

Kody loved making things in his lab, but his favorite time was when the forest animals came to play sports in the big open space. The basement had smooth concrete floors perfect for rolling balls and soft mats for tumbling.

One sunny Tuesday, Kody heard arguing voices coming down the stairs. Benny the bear and Ruby the rabbit were not happy.

"That's not fair!" squeaked Ruby, her little nose twitching. "Benny always wins because he's bigger!"

"I can't help being big," grumbled Benny, his furry shoulders drooping. "It's not my fault I'm stronger."

Kody adjusted his round glasses and listened carefully. The two friends had been playing catch, but Benny could throw the ball so hard that Ruby couldn't catch it. When they played tag, Benny's long legs made it impossible for Ruby to keep up.

"Hmm," said Kody thoughtfully, stroking his gray chin. "This sounds like a problem I can solve! But first, let me ask you both something. Do you want to have fun together?"

"Yes!" both animals said at the same time.

"And do you think sports should be fair for everyone?" asked Kody.

"Of course!" replied Ruby and Benny together.

Kody smiled and walked to his crafting corner, where colorful papers, safe scissors, and non-toxic glue waited on neat shelves. "Then let's make this fair! Ruby, Benny, would you like to help me create something special?"

For the next hour, Kody showed them how to make different types of sports equipment. They cut soft foam balls that were easier for small paws to catch. They created colorful bean bags that didn't bounce too high. Using cardboard and tape, they built adjustable goals that could be made shorter or taller.

"The secret to fair sports," explained Kody as they worked, "is making sure everyone can play their best. It's not about being the same size – it's about everyone having the same chance to have fun!"

Ruby's eyes sparkled as she decorated a soft yellow ball with star stickers. "This is so much better! Now I can catch it!"

Benny carefully painted stripes on a bean bag. "And I can practice being gentle instead of just using my strength!"

When they finished crafting, Kody set up a daily routine. "Every Tuesday at three o'clock, we'll have Fair Play Sports Time," he announced. "We'll use our special equipment and take turns choosing games that work for everyone."

They played their first fair game right away. Ruby could catch the soft balls, and Benny learned to throw gently. When they played tag, they used the bean bags – whoever was "it" had to carry one, which slowed them down just enough to make it fair.

"This is the best sports day ever!" laughed Ruby, successfully catching a rainbow-colored ball.

"I like this better too," admitted Benny. "It's more fun when we both have a chance to win!"

Kody beamed with pride as he watched his friends play happily together. His calm thinking and creative crafting had solved their problem. From that day on, every Tuesday became the most popular day in the basement lab, where fairness and friendship always won the game.

The End.
