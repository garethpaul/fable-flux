# Joey's Big Bathroom Adventure

Joey the kangaroo was a happy little joey who loved to hop and play. His soft brown fur was fluffy, and his big dark eyes sparkled with curiosity. Today was a very special day because Mama Kangaroo was going to teach Joey how to use the big bathroom all by himself!

"Come here, my little hopper," called Mama Kangaroo in her gentle voice. "It's time to learn something very important."

Joey bounced excitedly into the bright, clean bathroom. The white tiles felt cool under his little paws, and he could smell the fresh, soapy scent in the air. Everything looked so big and shiny!

"Wow, Mama! Everything is so tall!" Joey said, looking up at the sink and mirror.

Mama Kangaroo smiled warmly. "That's why we need to follow directions carefully, Joey. Following directions keeps us safe and helps us do things the right way. Are you ready to listen?"

Joey nodded eagerly, his little ears perking up. "Yes, Mama! I'm ready!"

But when Mama started explaining the steps, Joey got distracted by his reflection in the shiny faucet. He began making funny faces instead of listening.

"First, we always wash our hands," Mama said. "Joey, are you listening?"

"Uh-huh!" Joey said, but he was still looking at himself in the mirror, not paying attention.

When it was time to try, Joey hopped up to the sink without following any of Mama's directions. He tried to reach the soap, but it was too high. He stretched and stretched, standing on his tippy-toes.

"Help! I can't reach!" Joey called out, feeling frustrated. His little heart was beating fast.

Mama Kangaroo hopped over quickly. "Oh my, Joey! You forgot to use the special step stool I told you about. When we don't follow directions, things can be harder and less safe."

Joey's ears drooped down. "I'm sorry, Mama. I wasn't listening very well."

"That's okay, little one," Mama said, giving Joey a warm hug. "Let's try again. This time, let's follow the directions step by step. Can you be brave and try to listen carefully?"

Joey took a deep breath. "Yes! I want to learn the right way!"

Mama Kangaroo spoke slowly and clearly. "First, get your step stool. Second, turn on the warm water. Third, use soap. Fourth, wash for as long as it takes to sing 'Twinkle, Twinkle, Little Star.' Fifth, rinse and dry your hands."

This time, Joey listened with his whole body. He pointed his ears toward Mama and looked right at her face. 

"Step stool first!" Joey said, pulling over the colorful step stool. He climbed up carefully.

"Perfect! Now turn the water on gently," Mama guided.

Joey followed each direction exactly. The warm water felt nice on his paws. The soap made bubbly, slippery bubbles that smelled like lavender. He sang his song while scrubbing, and the soft towel felt cozy when he dried his hands.

"I did it, Mama! I followed all the directions!" Joey hopped with joy.

"You certainly did, my brave little joey! When we follow directions, we learn new things safely and show respect for the people teaching us."

From that day on, Joey always remembered to listen carefully to directions. He learned that following directions wasn't just about rules – it was about staying safe, learning properly, and showing love and respect for others.

"Following directions helps me be the best joey I can be!" Joey would say proudly.

The End.
