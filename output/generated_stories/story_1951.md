# Bear Cub Benny Finishes His Breakfast Job

Benny was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and bright eyes. He woke up to the warm smell of pancakes and berries. His tummy gave a happy rumble. Mama Bear called from the dining room, “Benny, breakfast is ready!”

Benny padded in on little paws. The dining room felt cozy. Sunshine slid across the wooden table. The chairs looked like tall trees. There was a little stack of pancakes, a bowl of blueberries, and a tiny honey pot that smelled sweet and golden.

Mama Bear smiled. “Good morning, helper. Today, you have a special job.”

Benny’s ears perked up. “A job? For me?”

“Yes,” Mama Bear said. “Your job is to finish your breakfast and then help clear the table. You can do it one small step at a time.”

Benny nodded. He liked jobs. Jobs made him feel big inside.

He climbed onto his chair. “Step one,” he whispered to himself. “Taste a bite.” He cut a small piece of pancake with his safe, child-sized fork. It felt warm and soft. He put it in his mouth. Mmm. Sweet and fluffy. A blueberry popped with a tiny “squish.”

“I can do this,” Benny said. He took another bite. But then he saw a crumb roll away and hide under his plate. The plate wobbled a little. Benny frowned. “This is hard,” he said softly.

Mama Bear sat nearby and sipped warm tea. “Remember, Benny,” she said, “small steps, big finish.”

Benny took a breath. He sat up tall. “I am doing my job,” he told himself. That was Benny being assertive—using his brave voice to cheer himself on. He took three more bites. One, two, three. He counted the blueberries as they burst with a “pop-pop-pop.”

Then his little paw got sticky with honey. “Ew, sticky,” Benny giggled, holding up his paw. It felt like warm glue.

“You can wipe and keep going,” Mama Bear said. She handed him a soft napkin. It felt cool and clean. Benny wiped his paw. “Thank you, Mama,” he said. “I will finish.”

Just then, a tiny wobble in Benny’s chair made him slide a little. His fork clinked on the plate. “Clink!” Benny blinked. “Uh-oh.” He felt a bubble of worry. “Maybe I should stop.”

Mama Bear shook her head kindly. “You can keep trying. That is called resilience. When something is tricky, we try again.”

Benny looked at his plate. He took a deep breath, in and out. “I can try again,” he told himself. He pressed his feet to the chair gently, held his fork steady, and took two more bites. “Chew, chew, chew.” The pancake was warm and sweet. The blueberries tasted like little stars.

Soon the pancakes were gone. The bowl was empty. Benny felt proud. His chest felt warm, like a small sun. “I did it! Step one is done.”

Mama Bear clapped softly. “Well done, Benny. Now step two: clear the table.”

Benny hopped down and picked up his plate. It felt smooth and a little heavy. He moved slowly. “I am strong and careful,” he said. He set the plate on the counter with a gentle “tap.” Then he went back for the cup and the fork.

On the way, his cup wobbled. A drop of juice slipped out and splashed on the table. “Plip.” Benny’s eyes got big. “Oh no! A spill.”

“It’s okay,” Mama Bear said. “Spills happen. What can we do?”

Benny stood tall. “I can fix it,” he said boldly. He took a small towel and dabbed the juice. “Pat, pat, pat.” He wiped in tiny circles. The table felt smooth again. “All clean!”

“That’s assertive and resilient,” Mama Bear said. “You solved the problem.”

Benny smiled. “Step three,” he said. “Work together.” He looked at Mama. “Mama, will you help me carry the honey pot?”

Mama Bear nodded. “That’s cooperation.” They walked side by side. The honey smelled sweet and sunny. Together, they set it on the counter. “Tap.”

Benny looked around the dining room. The table was tidy. The chairs were pushed in straight. The room felt calm and bright. He took a big happy breath. It smelled like berries and soap and a little honey.

Mama Bear crouched to his level. “How do you feel, Benny?”

Benny touched his chest. “Proud. I finished my breakfast job!”

Mama Bear hugged him. Her fur felt soft and warm. “You used small steps. You spoke up. You kept trying. And you asked for help. That’s how we finish tasks.”

Benny nodded. “First I ate. Then I cleared. I cleaned the spill. I worked with you. Step by step.”

Mama Bear smiled. “Yes. When we complete our tasks, we feel ready for the day.”

Benny’s tummy gave a happy little growl again. He giggled. “What is my next job?”

Mama Bear winked. “Playtime in the living room. But first, high five for finishing!”

“High five!” Benny said. Their paws made a soft “slap.” Benny stood taller. He felt brave, strong, and kind.

As they left the dining room, Benny looked back at the clean table. He whispered, “I can do hard things. I can finish what I start.” His words felt like a warm blanket.

He waved to the sunny room. “Thank you, pancakes,” he giggled. “See you next time, blueberries!”

Mama Bear laughed. “Let’s go, helper.”

Benny trotted off, ready for new fun, knowing he could finish his tasks, one small step at a time.

The End.
