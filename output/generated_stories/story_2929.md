# Ellie's Quiet Leaves

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf. She lived with her family in a tall building. Every afternoon, Ellie stepped onto the balcony garden. The garden had soft soil in big pots. Little mint leaves grew in a row. Cherry tomatoes hung like red beads. The air smelled sweet and green.

Ellie loved the soft sun on her ears. She liked the tickle of mint on her trunk. She moved carefully. Her big feet went tap, tap, tap on the wooden floor.

“Good afternoon, leaves,” Ellie whispered. “I will water you.”

She lifted a blue watering can. The water made a soft hush sound. The leaves shivered and sparkled. Ellie smiled. Her heart felt calm and warm.

Next door, a small bird named Pip hopped onto the railing. Pip had bright eyes and quick feet. “Cheep! Cheep! I like to sing!” he chirped. Pip opened his beak and sang a big, happy song.

Pip’s song was very loud. It bounced on the glass door. It bounced on the railing. It bounced right into Ellie’s ears.

Ellie blinked. The sound felt like a big drum inside her head. Her ears wiggled. She held her watering can very still.

“Cheep-cheep-CHIRP!” sang Pip.

Ellie’s trunk curled. The mint smell was strong. The sun felt hot. Her chest felt tight. She wanted to say, “Stop!” but the word stayed stuck. She did not want to be unkind. She did not want to make Pip sad. But her ears hurt.

Ellie took a slow breath in. In… and out. She remembered what Mama Elephant had taught her. “When you feel big feelings, be calm. Put your hand on your heart. Breathe. Use kind words. Tell what you need.”

Ellie put her small gray foot on her chest. Thump, thump. Her heart slowed down.

Pip hopped closer. “Did you like my song? I made it extra big!” His feathers puffed with pride.

Ellie wanted to hide behind the tomato vine. Her trunk trembled. She looked at Pip’s bright eyes. She looked at the sun and the mint and the red tomatoes. She thought about her ears and about Pip’s happy face. She wondered how he felt.

Ellie spoke in a soft voice. “Pip, your song is full of joy. It sounds like sunshine.”

Pip smiled. “Cheep! Thank you!”

Ellie took another breath. “But my ears are very soft,” she said. “When the song is very loud, my ears feel ouch. I feel shaky. I need quiet to water my plants.”

Pip blinked. He looked at Ellie’s big, gentle ears. He watched her trunk shake. He saw her careful feet. He listened to the hush-hush water. Pip had not thought about Ellie’s ears before.

Pip tilted his head. “Oh,” he said softly. “I didn’t know your ears feel ouch. I was excited. I wanted to share my song.”

Ellie nodded. “I like your song. Could you sing it softer? Or could we pick a singing time? Maybe after I water?”

Pip fluffed his wings. He hummed a tiny note. “Cheep… cheep,” he tried, very soft.

Ellie’s ears relaxed. The sound was like a gentle breeze. She smiled. “That is nice,” she said. “Thank you for trying.”

Pip smiled back. “I can sing soft during watering time. After watering, we can have singing time. You can tell me when you are ready. You can say, ‘ready.’”

Ellie’s eyes shone. “I can say, ‘ready.’ Thank you for listening.”

Pip nodded. “Thank you for telling me. I want your ears to feel good.”

They made a little plan. Every afternoon, Ellie would water first. The water would go hush-hush. The mint would smell cool and sweet. The tomatoes would shine red. Pip would sit and hum soft, like this: “cheep… cheep…” When Ellie was done, she would say, “Ready!” Then Pip would sing his happy song. Ellie would wiggle her ears in time, and tap her feet, tap, tap, tap.

The next day, the sun felt warm on Ellie’s back. The wood felt smooth under her feet. The watering can felt cool in her trunk. Pip perched on the railing. He held his song inside like a bubble.

Ellie whispered, “Thank you for being soft.”

Pip whispered back, “Thank you for telling me your need.”

When the last pot was wet and shiny, Ellie took a breath and said, “Ready!”

Pip opened his beak and sang. The song was big, but now it felt gentle in Ellie’s heart. She smiled and swayed. The mint leaves danced. The tomatoes blushed brighter. A tiny breeze brushed Ellie’s cheeks. Everything felt just right.

Ellie learned something new. She learned she could be kind and ask for what she needed. She learned that listening is love. Pip learned to look and listen, too. He learned that soft can be strong.

That night, Ellie told Mama. “I used calm words. I told Pip my feelings. He listened. We made a plan.”

Mama hugged her. “That is empathy,” Mama said. “You cared about how Pip felt, and he cared about you. That is peaceful communication.”

Ellie closed her eyes. She could still smell mint. She could still hear Pip’s gentle cheep.

“I feel safe,” Ellie said.

The End.
