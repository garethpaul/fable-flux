# Jemma the Giraffe Finds a Place for Everything

Jemma was a thoughtful giraffe with long, gentle eyelashes and a soft, warm smile. She lived in a sunny, playful living room with colorful rugs, squishy pillows, and a basket of shiny dance scarves. The morning light made little dust sparkles in the air. Jemma took a big, healthy stretch. Her tall neck reached up, up, up.

“Good morning,” she hummed. “Time to dance, time to snack, time to help.”

Jemma loved to help and loved to dance. She also loved her friends, Benny Bear and Lila Lamb. Today, they were coming over to play. Jemma felt excited butterflies in her tummy. She put some crunchy apple slices and tiny carrot sticks on a plate. She poured cool water into three cups. “Healthy habits help me feel good,” she sang softly.

Knock, knock!

Benny Bear waddled in with a boom-boom step. Lila Lamb bounced in with a hop-hop. “Hi, Jemma!” they cheered.

“Hi!” said Jemma. “Let’s dance!”

First, Jemma reached for the music box. But the music box was hiding under blocks. Crayons rolled on the rug. Scarves were tangled on the couch. Puzzle pieces peeked from under the table.

“Oh,” Jemma said, blinking. “I can’t find anything.”

Benny Bear tripped on a toy truck. “Oof!” he said, rubbing his knee. Lila Lamb’s hoof landed in a sticky spot. “Yuck,” she said, wrinkling her nose.

Jemma’s chest felt tight. “I wanted today to be fun,” she whispered. “But this feels messy.”

Benny crossed his arms. “I want the red scarf. Where is it?”

Lila stamped her hoof. “I want the music now!”

Jemma felt a big feeling. Her heart went thump-thump. She took a slow belly breath in and out. “Friends,” she said gently, “we have a problem. Our living room is messy. We can’t dance or snack safely. Let’s fix it together.”

Benny frowned. “But I want to play now.”

Lila’s ears drooped. “Cleaning is boring.”

Jemma nodded. “I hear you. Playing now sounds fun. Cleaning can feel not-fun. But cleaning is caring. It keeps us safe. It is our job in our home. We can help each other. That is civic responsibility. That means: we take care of our place and our people. We can make it fun!”

“How?” asked Benny, peeking up.

“Dance-clean!” said Jemma, smiling. She tapped the music box. “When we clean, we move and sing. We put things in their home. Everything gets a place.”

Jemma made a plan with her friends:
- “Benny, you put blocks in the blue bin.”
- “Lila, you gather crayons in the green cup.”
- “I will untangle scarves and wipe the sticky spot.”

She placed the bins where everyone could reach. She held up a picture chart with simple drawings: blocks-bin, crayons-cup, scarves-basket, puzzles-box, books-shelf. “Pictures help us remember,” Jemma said.

Jemma pressed play. Soft, bouncy music floated through the room. The beat went tap-tap, swish-swish. “One, two, tidy-shoo!” she sang. “Three, four, clear the floor!”

Benny swayed his big bear hips and scooped blocks with a boom-boom rhythm. Clunk! Clunk! into the blue bin. Lila twirled, her wooly ears bouncing, and slid crayons into the green cup with a click-click. Jemma swished the scarves, feeling the silky fabric on her hooves. She wiped the sticky spot. It smelled like sweet apples. “All clean,” she said, wringing out the cloth.

“Wait,” Benny said, holding a puzzle piece. “Where does this go?”

Jemma showed the picture chart. “Puzzle pieces live in the puzzle box.”

“Oh!” Benny smiled. “A place for everything.”

Lila found the red scarf. Her eyes sparkled. “I was mad,” she said softly, “but now I feel proud.”

Jemma nodded. “You told me your feeling. That helped. We listened. We made a plan. That is conflict resolution. We solved the problem together.”

They kept dance-cleaning. Swish, swish went the scarves into the basket. Slide went the books onto the shelf. Tap went the trucks into the car box. The room began to look wide and bright again.

Jemma saw a toy that wasn’t theirs. It was a little wooden fish with a blue tail. “This came from next door,” she said. “We should return it.”

“That’s helping our neighbors,” Lila said.

“That is civic responsibility too,” Jemma agreed. “We take care of our community.”

They put the fish by the door to return later. Then Jemma turned off the music and looked around. The rug showed its bright colors again. The couch was cozy. The music box sat on the low table, easy to reach. The picture chart hung where they could see it.

“Wow,” Benny said. “It feels calm.”

“It smells fresh,” Lila said.

“It looks happy,” Jemma said. Her heart felt light.

Now it was time to dance for fun. Jemma started the music again. They twirled with scarves, tiptoed, and hopped. The floor felt safe and smooth. After dancing, they drank cool water and crunched apple slices and carrot sticks. “Yum,” said Benny. “Healthy snacks give me energy.”

Jemma smiled. “Organizing helps us play, helps us share, and keeps us safe. When we put things away, we are kind to ourselves, to our friends, and to our home.”

Benny and Lila nodded. “A place for everything,” they said together.

Jemma made a little rhyme to remember: “Play, pause, put away. Then we’re ready for the next fun day!”

They all laughed. Benny hugged Jemma. Lila gave a wooly nuzzle. Later, they carried the little wooden fish next door and rang the bell. “We found your toy,” Jemma said. The neighbor smiled and thanked them.

On the walk back, the sun felt warm, and Jemma’s thoughtful heart felt proud. She had listened, made a plan, and helped her friends. The living room was tidy and bright, ready for more songs, more stories, and more kind choices.

The End.
