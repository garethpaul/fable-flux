# Benny's Big Garden Plan

Benny the beaver stood on the sunny balcony garden, his fluffy brown tail tapping nervously on the wooden deck. The morning sun felt warm on his whiskers. Sweet flower smells filled the air. But Benny felt worried.

"I want to help Mama plant new flowers today," Benny said to himself. "But what if I mess up? What if I'm too little?"

Mama Beaver came outside carrying a watering can. Water sloshed gently inside. "Good morning, Benny! Ready to help me in the garden?"

Benny's ears drooped. "I don't know, Mama. Gardening looks really hard. What if I plant the seeds wrong? What if I water too much?"

Mama set down the watering can with a soft clink. She sat beside Benny on the warm deck. "Tell me, little one. When you build your stick dams, do you just start building?"

"No," Benny said, thinking hard. "First I look at where the water goes. Then I pick the best sticks. Then I make a plan!"

"Exactly!" Mama smiled. "Gardening is just like building. We can make a plan together."

Benny's tail stopped tapping. "Really? We can plan it?"

Mama nodded. "Let's start. What do we need to think about first?"

Benny looked around the balcony garden. Colorful pots sat in neat rows. Some spots got lots of sunshine. Others stayed shady and cool.

"The flowers need the right spots!" Benny said excitedly. "Sunny flowers go in sunny places!"

"Perfect thinking!" Mama cheered. "What else?"

Benny touched the soil in a nearby pot. It felt soft and crumbly. "The dirt needs to be just right. Not too wet, not too dry!"

"You're such a smart planner!" Mama said proudly.

Benny stood up straighter. His chest puffed out with pride. "I am good at planning! Just like with my dams!"

Together, they walked around the balcony. Benny pointed to sunny spots for the sunflowers. He found shady corners for the ferns. He felt the soil in each pot, checking if it needed water.

"Now for our plan," Mama said. "First, we'll prepare the soil. Then we'll plant the seeds. Last, we'll water them gently."

"I can do the first part!" Benny said confidently. He picked up a small shovel. It felt just right in his paws.

Benny dug carefully in the first pot. The soil smelled earthy and rich. He made perfect little holes, just the right size for seeds.

"Wonderful job!" Mama said. "You're being so careful and thoughtful."

Next, Benny placed tiny seeds in each hole. His paws were gentle and steady. "One seed per hole," he said to himself. "Not too deep, not too shallow."

Finally, Benny sprinkled water from the watering can. The drops sparkled like diamonds in the sunshine. The soil drank up the water happily.

"Look what we did!" Benny exclaimed, stepping back to admire their work. Six pots sat in perfect spots around the balcony. Each one ready to grow beautiful flowers.

Mama hugged Benny tight. "You were amazing today. You made such a good plan and followed it perfectly."

Benny's tail wagged proudly. "I was scared at first. But planning made it easy! Just like building my dams!"

"That's right," Mama said. "When you plan carefully and believe in yourself, you can do anything!"

Benny looked at their planted pots. Soon they would have colorful flowers dancing in the breeze. And he had helped make it happen!

"Tomorrow, can we plan what to plant next?" Benny asked eagerly.

"Of course!" Mama laughed. "Good planners always think ahead!"

Benny smiled wide, feeling proud and confident. He had learned that with good planning and self-confidence, even big jobs could be fun and successful.

The End.
