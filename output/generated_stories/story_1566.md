# Benny's Big Art Dream

Benny the rabbit hopped into the bright art room. Colorful paintings covered the walls. Paintbrushes stood tall in jars like tiny soldiers. The smell of fresh crayons filled the air.

"Today I'm going to make the most beautiful painting ever!" Benny announced. His fluffy tail wiggled with excitement.

Mrs. Fox, the art teacher, smiled. "That's a wonderful goal, Benny. What will you paint?"

Benny's pink nose twitched as he thought. "A rainbow! With every single color!"

He grabbed a big brush and dipped it in red paint. Swish, swish went the brush across the paper. But the red looked wobbly and drippy.

"Hmm," Benny frowned. "This doesn't look right."

Next to him, Lily the mouse was painting tiny, perfect flowers. Her small paws made delicate strokes. Across the table, Max the bear painted big, bold trees with his large paws.

Benny's ears drooped. "Everyone else is so good at this."

Mrs. Fox hopped over. "Benny, what makes a rainbow special?"

"All the different colors?" Benny guessed.

"Exactly! And what makes our art room special?"

Benny looked around. Lily's tiny flowers were different from Max's big trees. Sarah the bird painted with her wing-tip, making feathery strokes.

"We're all different!" Benny said, his ears perking up.

"That's right. Your rabbit paws are perfect for your own special way of painting."

Mrs. Fox showed Benny how to hold his brush differently. "Try making small circles for your rainbow."

Benny tried again. This time, he made little circular motions. The red paint formed a gentle curve. It felt good in his paws.

"I can do this!" Benny said. "But it might take time."

"The best goals often do," Mrs. Fox nodded. "Let's make a plan. What color comes after red?"

"Orange!" Benny dipped his brush carefully. Circle by circle, he added orange below the red.

When yellow came next, Benny's paw was getting tired. But he remembered his goal. "I want to finish my rainbow," he told himself.

Green was tricky. The brush felt heavy. But Lily called out, "You're doing great, Benny!"

"Thanks!" Benny smiled. Friends helping friends made everything better.

Blue went on smoother. Benny was learning how his paws worked best. Purple was the hardest color, but he took his time.

"Look!" Benny stepped back. His rainbow curved across the paper like a colorful bridge. It wasn't perfect, but it was his.

"Wow, Benny!" Max said. "Your rainbow has such happy curves!"

"I love how you made each stripe different," added Sarah.

Mrs. Fox clapped her paws. "Benny, you set a goal, made a plan, and kept trying. That's what makes dreams come true."

Benny's chest puffed with pride. His rainbow looked different from anyone else's art, and that was perfect.

"Tomorrow I want to paint a garden," Benny announced. "With flowers like Lily's and trees like Max's!"

"Another wonderful goal," Mrs. Fox smiled. "Remember what you learned today?"

Benny nodded. "Take my time, use my special rabbit way, and keep trying!"

As Benny cleaned his brush, he looked at his rainbow one more time. Every wobbly line told the story of not giving up. Every color showed how patience helped him reach his goal.

"I can't wait for tomorrow's art time," Benny whispered to himself, already dreaming of his next colorful adventure.

The End.
