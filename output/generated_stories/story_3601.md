# Toby Turtle's Big World Dance Party

Toby the turtle lived in a cozy corner of a very busy basement. The basement was full of wonderful sounds - *tap tap tap* went the washing machine, *whoosh whoosh* went the dryer, and *beep beep* went all sorts of interesting machines.

But today, something different was happening. Toby heard music coming from upstairs - music from all around the world! There was the *dum dum dum* of African drums, the gentle *ting ting* of Chinese bells, and the happy *strum strum* of Spanish guitars.

"Oh my," whispered Toby, pulling his head into his shell. "That sounds so loud and scary!"

Just then, a little mouse named Maria scurried down the stairs. She was dancing and spinning to the music. "Hola, Toby!" she called cheerfully. "Come dance with us! We're having a world music party upstairs!"

Toby felt his tummy get all twisty. "I... I don't know how to dance to music from other places," he said quietly. "What if I do it wrong?"

Maria's whiskers twitched with understanding. "Oh, Toby! Dancing is just about moving your body to feel happy. Every country has different dances, and they're all beautiful!"

"But I get so nervous," Toby admitted, feeling a little angry at himself for being scared. His shell felt tight and uncomfortable.

"When I feel angry or scared," Maria said gently, "I take three deep breaths. Want to try?" She showed Toby how to breathe in slowly through his nose - *sniff* - and out through his mouth - *whoosh*.

Toby tried it. *Sniff... whoosh.* *Sniff... whoosh.* *Sniff... whoosh.* His shell didn't feel so tight anymore.

"Now," said Maria, "let me tell you about the music upstairs. The drums are from my friend Kofi in Ghana - they help people celebrate and feel strong. The bells are from Lin, who came from China - they make peaceful, gentle sounds. And the guitar is from Carlos in Mexico - it makes people want to smile and move!"

"Really?" asked Toby, peeking his head out a little more. "People all over the world make music?"

"Yes!" Maria danced a little circle. "And you know what? Your slow, gentle movements would be perfect for any kind of music. Every culture loves different ways of dancing!"

Toby thought about this. The music didn't sound scary anymore - it sounded... interesting! "Could you show me some easy moves?"

Maria clapped her tiny paws. "Of course! For the African drums, we can step side to side - like this!" She showed Toby a simple step. "For the Chinese bells, we can move our arms slowly, like flowing water. And for the Mexican guitar, we can sway back and forth like happy trees!"

Toby tried each movement. His feet went *tap tap* on the basement floor. His arms moved like gentle waves. His whole body swayed like a tree in a warm breeze.

"I'm doing it!" Toby laughed. "I'm dancing to music from around the world!"

"You're wonderful!" cheered Maria. "Every country would be happy to have you dance to their music!"

Together, they danced their way upstairs to join the big world music party. Toby learned that being different wasn't scary - it was special. And when he felt nervous, he remembered to breathe deeply and think about all the friends around the world who loved to dance, just like him.

From that day on, Toby knew that no matter where music came from, dancing was a way to celebrate with friends everywhere.

The End.
