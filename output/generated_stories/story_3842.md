# Joey's Big Day

Joey the kangaroo hopped around the sunny backyard. His soft gray fur felt warm in the golden sunlight. The grass smelled fresh and green under his big feet.

"Hop, hop, hop!" Joey sang happily. He loved bouncing everywhere he went.

Today was special. Joey's friend Bella Bunny was coming to play. He wanted everything to be perfect for their playdate.

"I need to clean up my toys," Joey said, looking around. His red ball sat by the fence. His blue truck was under the apple tree. His yellow blocks were scattered near the flower bed.

Joey hopped to get his ball first. But then he saw a pretty butterfly. "Ooh, look at those orange wings!" He chased the butterfly around the yard.

When the butterfly flew away, Joey remembered his truck. He bounced over to get it. But then he smelled something sweet. "Mmm, those flowers smell like honey!" He stopped to sniff each pink flower.

Time passed quickly. The sun moved across the blue sky. Joey kept getting distracted by all the wonderful things in the backyard.

Suddenly, he heard a gentle knock at the gate. "Joey! I'm here!" called Bella Bunny in her soft, cheerful voice.

Joey's tummy felt funny. He looked around the yard. His toys were still everywhere! The backyard was still messy!

"Oh no," Joey whispered. His ears drooped down. "I didn't finish cleaning up."

Bella hopped into the yard. Her white fur sparkled in the sunshine. She had kind brown eyes and a twitchy pink nose.

"Hi Joey!" Bella said with a big smile. Then she saw Joey's sad face. "What's wrong, friend?"

Joey's voice was quiet. "I wanted to clean up before you came. But I got distracted by butterflies and flowers. Now our play area is messy."

Bella looked around at the scattered toys. Then she looked at her friend's worried face.

"That's okay, Joey," Bella said gently. "We can solve this problem together! Two friends can clean up faster than one."

Joey's ears perked up. "Really? You'd help me?"

"Of course!" Bella giggled. "That's what friends do. Let's make a plan."

Bella pointed with her paw. "You get the toys on the left side. I'll get the toys on the right side. We'll meet in the middle!"

"That's a great idea!" Joey felt happy again. His tail started to wiggle.

They worked together, hopping and gathering. Joey collected his ball and blocks. Bella picked up the truck and some other toys.

"One, two, three!" they counted as they put each toy in the toy box.

Soon the backyard was clean and tidy. The green grass looked perfect for playing.

"We did it!" Joey cheered. He gave Bella a gentle hug.

"Teamwork makes everything easier," Bella said with a warm smile.

They spent the rest of the afternoon playing together. They rolled the red ball back and forth. They built a tall tower with the yellow blocks. They drove the blue truck around pretend roads in the dirt.

As the sun began to set, painting the sky soft orange and pink, Bella had to go home.

"Thank you for helping me today," Joey said. "And thank you for being such a good friend."

"Thank you for a wonderful playdate," Bella replied. "Next time, we can clean up together right from the start!"

Joey nodded happily. He had learned something important. When you have a good friend, problems become easier to solve. And asking for help makes you a better friend too.

The End.
