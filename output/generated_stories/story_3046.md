# Buzzy Beaver’s Splashy Chore Day

Buzzy the beaver was very busy. His little paws liked to build, stack, and swish. Today, Buzzy hurried into the bright, bubbly bathroom. The floor was cool and smooth. The mirror was shiny. The air smelled like minty soap.

“I’m fast! I can do all my chores fast,” Buzzy said with a proud smile.

Mama Beaver called from the door, “Chore time, Buzzy! Brush, wipe, tidy, and dry. Then playtime.”

Buzzy nodded. “Brush, wipe, tidy, and dry. I can do it!” He climbed onto a sturdy step stool. The sink water sang, trickle-trickle. Buzzy squeezed too much toothpaste—plop! A big minty lump landed on the counter.

“That’s okay,” Buzzy whispered. He brushed quickly. Scrub-scrub-scrub! Bubbles foamed like tiny clouds. He made silly faces at the mirror. “Grr! Smile! Cheese!” Mint tickled his tongue. He giggled and spit—splish!—a little too hard. Water sprayed the counter and the mirror with spots.

Buzzy rushed to wipe. He grabbed the soft hand towel. “Swipe, swipe!” He swiped once and tossed the towel onto the floor. The towel landed with a soft flop.

Next, Buzzy tidied. He scooped up rubber duckies from the tub. Squeak, squeak! “All done,” he said quickly. He put the ducks in the basket, but one duck’s tail stuck out and fell—plunk!—back into the tub.

Last, Buzzy had to dry the splashes. He waved his paws in the air. “Dry, dry, dry!” He blew at the mirror. “Hoo!” It fogged up. He drew a smiley face and laughed. “Playtime now?”

Just then, Mama Beaver stepped back into the bathroom. She looked around with gentle eyes. The mirror was dotted with water and smiley fog. Toothpaste sat in a sleepy blob. The towel was on the floor. A duck was floating in the tub, saying, “Squeak?”

Mama knelt beside Buzzy. “I see you tried, Buzzy. Thank you for starting your chores.” Her voice was warm like a hug. “But chores are not just fast. Chores mean caring.”

Buzzy’s ears drooped. “I wanted to be fast. I wanted playtime.”

Mama nodded. “I understand. It’s hard to slow down when play is waiting. Let’s try again together. We can practice. Practice helps.”

Buzzy took a small breath. He listened to the quiet drip-drip of the faucet. He felt the cool tile under his feet. “Okay,” he said. “I can make it right.”

Mama smiled. “That is making amends. When we make a mess, we help fix it.”

First, they looked at the toothpaste plop. Mama showed Buzzy how to use just a pea-sized dot. “Little pea, clean teeth,” she said. Buzzy giggled. “Pea for me!” He wiped the extra paste with a tissue and put it in the trash. “There,” he said softly. “That feels better.”

Next, they checked the mirror and counter. Mama handed Buzzy a dry cloth. “Small circles,” she said. “Round and round.” Buzzy moved his little paws in gentle circles. Swish, swish. The spots disappeared. The mirror shined so bright that Buzzy could see his happy eyes.

Then, they picked up the towel. Buzzy shook it out. “This towel lives on the hook,” Mama said, tapping the hook. Buzzy reached up and hung it neatly. The towel looked cozy and proud.

They heard the duck say, “Squeak?” Buzzy smiled. “Duck goes in the basket.” He lifted the duck and tucked it in with the others. Pat-pat. “All friends together.”

Mama nodded. “Brush, wipe, tidy, and dry. You did each step with care.”

Buzzy’s tail thumped the floor, thump-thump, like a small drum. “I did it! I made amends.”

Mama gave him a gentle forehead kiss. “You understood what was needed. You practiced. You finished your chores.”

Buzzy looked around. The bathroom smelled fresh and clean. The counter was smooth. The mirror was clear. The towel hung straight. The ducks were snug in their basket. He felt warm and proud inside, like sunshine in his chest.

“I can be busy and careful,” Buzzy said. “Busy doesn’t mean messy.”

Mama chuckled. “That’s right. We can be fast after we are careful. Care first, then speed.”

Buzzy tried the steps again by himself to practice. He pretended to brush with just a pea of paste. He pretended to wipe the mirror in circles. He pretended to tidy the ducks and hang the towel. He whispered the steps like a song:

“Brush, wipe,
tidy, dry.
Do my best,
then I try!”

Mama clapped softly. “Now it’s playtime.”

Buzzy beamed. As he left, he looked back at the shiny bathroom. “Thank you, bathroom,” he said. “I will take care of you.”

From then on, when Buzzy did his bathroom chores, he took a slow breath and remembered his song. He knew that chores were not just jobs. Chores were ways to show care, make amends when he made a splash, and practice being helpful every day.

The clear lesson for Buzzy—and for you—is this: When we make a mess, we make it right. We slow down, we care, and we try again. Chores help us help our home and help our hearts feel proud.

The End.
