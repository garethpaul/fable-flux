# Pip's Perfect Guest Room

In a cozy guest room with soft blue walls and fluffy white pillows, lived a kind little mouse named Pip. Every morning, Pip had a special routine that made him feel happy and ready for the day.

"Good morning, sunshine!" Pip squeaked as golden light peeked through the lace curtains. He stretched his tiny paws up high, wiggled his pink nose, and took three deep breaths of the fresh morning air.

First, Pip would make his tiny bed in the corner behind the big dresser. He fluffed his cotton ball pillow and smoothed his soft leaf blanket. "A tidy bed makes a happy head!" he sang in his squeaky voice.

Next, Pip would wash his whiskers at the little water droplet that formed on the window sill each morning. The cool water felt refreshing on his furry face. "Splash, splash, clean at last!" he giggled.

Then came breakfast time. Pip would search for crumbs that the guests sometimes dropped. He always found just enough to fill his tiny tummy.

But one Tuesday morning, something was different. When Pip went to make his bed, he discovered that his precious cotton ball pillow was missing!

"Oh no!" Pip squeaked, his little heart beating fast. "Where could my pillow be?"

Pip looked under the dresser. No pillow. He looked behind the curtains. No pillow. He even looked inside the guest's suitcase, but still no pillow.

Pip felt sad and worried. Without his routine, the day didn't feel right. His whiskers drooped, and his tail dragged on the wooden floor.

Just then, he heard a tiny voice crying. "Help! Help!"

Pip followed the sound and found a baby spider stuck in a spider web in the corner of the ceiling. But this wasn't just any web – it was tangled around Pip's cotton ball pillow!

"Please help me!" cried the baby spider. "I was trying to fix my web, but I got all twisted up!"

Even though Pip was upset about his pillow, his kind heart wanted to help. "Don't worry, little friend. I'll help you!"

Pip climbed carefully up the curtain, using his sharp little claws. Step by step, he made his way to the ceiling. His paws were shaking, but he didn't give up.

"I'm scared of heights," Pip whispered to himself, "but my new friend needs help."

Gently, Pip untangled the baby spider from the sticky web. "There you go! You're free!"

"Thank you so much!" said the baby spider, whose name was Stella. "I'm sorry I got your pillow stuck in my web. Here, let me help you get it down."

Together, Pip and Stella carefully freed the cotton ball pillow. It was a little dusty, but still perfectly soft and fluffy.

"You know what?" said Stella. "You helped me even when you were upset about your pillow. That was very kind!"

Pip smiled, his whiskers twitching happily. "Helping friends is more important than my routine."

From that day on, Pip added something new to his morning routine. After making his bed and washing his whiskers, he would visit Stella to make sure she was okay. Sometimes routines can grow and change, and that's perfectly wonderful!

"Good morning, Stella!" Pip would call each day.

"Good morning, Pip!" she would answer back.

And they both learned that the best routines include kindness, helping others, and making new friends.

The End.
