# Ellie Elephant's Work-Play Plan

In a sunny home office, a gentle elephant calf named Ellie stood on a soft rug. The rug felt fuzzy under her big, round feet. The air smelled like lemon cleaner. On the desk, Mama’s keyboard clicked, click, click. A fan hummed softly. Papers were neat in tidy stacks.

“I want to help you, Mama,” Ellie said in a small voice. Her long ears wiggled. “But my head feels buzzy, and my tummy feels grumbly.”

Mama Elephant looked up and smiled. “Hello, helper,” she said warmly. “What does your body say?”

Ellie touched her tummy. “It says, ‘grum-grum,’” she replied. She tapped her forehead. “And my head says, ‘too much screen.’”

Mama nodded. “Let’s think it through together. We can use our smart steps.”

“What are smart steps?” Ellie asked.

“Smart steps help us make a plan,” Mama said. She held up her trunk. “Step one: Notice the problem. Step two: Think of choices. Step three: Try a good choice. Step four: Check how it went.”

Ellie took a deep breath. She smelled Mama’s tea—warm and minty. “Okay,” she said. “Step one: My tummy is hungry, and my head is buzzy.”

“Good noticing,” said Mama. “Step two?”

Ellie looked around the home office. There was a small water bottle with a blue lid. There was a bowl with shiny apples on a shelf. A little basket held crayons and sticky notes that smelled like paper. A yoga mat sat rolled by the window.

Ellie wrinkled her trunk. “Choices!” she said. “I can drink water. I can eat a snack. I can stretch or take a break. I can blink and look out the window.”

Mama smiled. “Great thinking.”

Ellie thought some more. She used her logical mind. “If I drink water, my body gets happy. If I eat a snack, my tummy stops grumbling. If I stretch, the buzzy feeling might calm down. If I look far away, my eyes can rest.”

“Which choice will you try first?” asked Mama.

Ellie patted her tummy. “Snack first,” she decided. “Then water. Then a stretch!”

Mama clapped softly, her bracelets jingling. “Let’s do it.”

Ellie gently picked a red apple. It was cool and smooth. Crunch! The bite was crisp and sweet. Her ears wiggled with joy. “Yum,” she said, chewing carefully. She took slow bites and sat tall in the chair, just like Mama taught her.

Next, Ellie lifted the water bottle. The water felt cold on her tongue. “Sip, sip,” she said, smiling. She set the bottle down. “Now I stretch!”

Mama rolled out the yoga mat. Ellie stretched her trunk to the ceiling. She wiggled her toes. She reached her trunk to her toes. “Hello, toes,” she giggled. She turned her head to look out the window. The sky was bright blue. A tiny bird chirped, peep-peep-peep.

“How do you feel now?” Mama asked.

Ellie paused and listened to her body. She put a paw on her tummy. “My tummy is quiet,” she said. She touched her head. “My head is calm. The buzzy feeling is smaller.”

“Step four: Check how it went,” Mama reminded her.

Ellie nodded. “The plan worked,” she said proudly. “Snack, water, stretch. It helped!”

Mama gave her a gentle nuzzle. “You used healthy habits. You listened to your body. You used smart steps.”

Ellie beamed. “Can I help you now?” she asked.

“You can be my break buddy,” Mama said. She set a small timer on the desk. It ticked softly. “We will work for a little while. Then we will take a tiny break. We can blink, stretch, and sip water.”

Ellie clapped her big feet on the rug. “I like that plan!” she said.

Tick, tick, tick, went the timer. Ellie drew a picture of a shiny red apple on a sticky note. She stuck it on the corner of the desk. “This is a reminder,” she explained. “A healthy-habits note.”

When the timer dinged, Ellie whispered, “Break time!” She and Mama stood up. They rolled their shoulders. They looked out the window and counted three clouds. One. Two. Three. Ellie took a sip of water. Mama sipped her tea.

“How do you feel?” asked Mama.

“Happy and strong,” Ellie said. “My body and my brain are friends.”

Mama nodded. “That’s the lesson,” she said softly. “When we listen to our bodies, we can make a smart plan. We can choose healthy habits to feel good and do our best.”

Ellie hugged Mama’s leg. The fan hummed. The room felt peaceful. The apple scent lingered in the air. Ellie smiled and whispered, “Smart steps, healthy habits, happy Ellie.”

The End.
