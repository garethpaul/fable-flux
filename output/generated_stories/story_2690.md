# Melody's Big Day Song

Melody was a bright yellow bird who lived in the Johnson family's dining room. She had a beautiful cage by the big window where warm sunlight streamed in every morning. Melody loved to sing happy songs, especially when the family ate their meals together.

"Tweet-tweet-trill!" Melody sang loudly as Mrs. Johnson set the breakfast table. Her voice echoed off the wooden walls and made the crystal glasses ring softly.

"Good morning, Melody!" said little Katie, sitting down for breakfast. "You're singing so loud today!"

Melody felt excited. She wanted to sing the biggest, loudest song ever! She puffed up her yellow feathers and opened her tiny beak wide.

"TWEET-TWEET-TRIIIIILL!" she sang as loud as she could.

Baby Sam started crying. The loud noise scared him. Mr. Johnson covered his ears. "Melody, that's too loud for breakfast time," he said gently.

Melody felt confused. She loved singing! Why didn't everyone want to hear her beautiful voice?

At lunchtime, Melody tried again. She saw the family gathering around the big wooden table. The smell of warm soup filled the air. This time, she would sing even louder!

"TWEET-TWEET-TWEET-TRIIIIIIILL!" Melody sang with all her might.

Katie dropped her spoon with a clang. "Melody, please!" she said. "We can't talk when you sing so loud!"

Melody's little heart felt sad. She tucked her head under her wing. Maybe no one liked her singing at all.

That afternoon, Melody watched Katie playing with her building blocks on the dining room floor. Katie stacked the colorful blocks carefully, one by one. When the tower got too tall, it wobbled and fell down.

"Oh no!" Katie said. But instead of getting upset, she took a deep breath. "I'll try again, but slower this time."

Katie built her tower again, but this time she went slowly. She placed each block gently and waited to make sure it was steady before adding the next one.

Melody watched carefully. Katie was controlling herself! She wasn't rushing or getting frustrated.

"That's beautiful, Katie," said Mrs. Johnson. "You're being so patient and careful."

Melody thought about this. Maybe she needed to control her singing too, just like Katie controlled her building.

At dinnertime, Melody had an idea. She waited until the family finished eating and started telling stories about their day.

"Today I helped Daddy in the garden," Katie said.

"And I learned a new recipe," added Mrs. Johnson.

Then Melody began to sing, but this time very softly. "Tweet... tweet... trill..." Her gentle voice was like a quiet whisper, floating through the warm air like a musical story.

The family smiled. Baby Sam giggled and clapped his tiny hands. Katie looked up at Melody with sparkling eyes.

"That's perfect, Melody!" Katie said. "Your soft singing sounds like you're telling us a story too!"

Melody felt warm and happy inside. She had learned something important. Just like Katie with her blocks, she could control her voice. Loud singing was good sometimes, but gentle singing was perfect for family time.

From that day on, Melody sang loud, joyful songs in the morning when everyone was waking up. But during meals and quiet times, she sang soft, gentle songs that sounded like musical stories.

The whole family loved both kinds of singing, and Melody felt proud that she had learned to control her beautiful voice.

The End.
