# Fawn’s Sunshine Routine

Every morning, a gentle deer fawn named Fern peeked over the tall grass into the sunny backyard. The backyard smelled like warm earth and fresh apples. Bees hummed. A sprinkler whispered, “shhh,” and the sun made puddles of gold on the soft green lawn.

Fern had a routine. Routine means doing helpful things in the same calm way each day. Fern liked how routines made her feel safe, ready, and happy. She said, “Good morning, sun,” and stretched her legs. She wiggled her ears. She took three slow breaths. “In… and out… in… and out… in… and out,” she whispered.

“Hi, Fern!” chirped Pip the robin.

“Hello!” squeaked Dot the squirrel.

“Hello, friends,” Fern said. “It’s time for my morning routine. Do you want to join?”

Pip fluffed his red chest. Dot flicked her tail. “Yes!” they both said.

Fern smiled. “First, we wake up our bodies.” She did five gentle steps. “Step, step, step, step, step.” The grass felt cool under her hooves. Pip hopped beside her. Dot did tiny squirrel steps. They giggled together.

“Next, we check our space,” Fern said. “We make it tidy and safe.” She nudged a fallen stick off the path. Pip picked up a blue bottle cap with his beak and dropped it in a little bin. Dot gathered loose leaves into a neat pile. The backyard looked clear and cozy.

Fern sniffed the air. “Now, drink water,” she said, and she sipped from a shallow stone bowl. “Glug, glug.” Pip took tiny sips from a leaf cup. Dot nibbled a juicy berry and licked her lips. “Mmm!”

Fern took out a tiny leaf-list she kept under a smooth rock. “My routine helps me remember,” she said. “Stretch. Step. Tidy. Sip. Plan.” She tapped the last word. “Plan means we think about our day.”

“Can we make a game?” asked Dot.

“Yes,” said Fern. “But we also have Sunny Help Time.” Sunny Help Time was part of Fern’s routine. “We help someone each day,” she explained. “Helping makes our hearts warm.”

A soft voice called from the fence. “Hello?” A shy hedgehog peeked through a crack. His name was Hugo. He had bright eyes and tiny toes. “I moved near here,” Hugo said. “I want to play, but the ground feels prickly and the path is too bumpy for my little feet.”

Fern’s heart made a warm thump. “You can join us,” she said softly. “We have Sunny Help Time. We can make the path smooth.”

Pip fluttered close. “We can include every friend!”

Dot nodded. “We can try a new idea!”

“Innovation means trying a smart new way,” Fern told them. “We can use our brains and work together. That is cooperation.”

Fern looked around the sunny yard. “We need a smooth path. We need a cozy corner. We need shade and a sip spot.” She smiled. “Let’s plan.”

Pip sang, “Plan, plan, plan,” like a happy bell.

Dot found flat leaves and small sticks. “These can be bridge pieces,” she said.

Fern pushed soft moss with her nose. “This can be a gentle rug,” she said. “No pokey bits.”

Hugo watched with wide eyes. “I can help,” he said. He used his little paws to pat the moss smooth. “Pat, pat.”

Fern made a line of round stepping stones out of sturdy bark. “Step here,” she said, “then here.” Pip tested them with tiny hops. “Boop, boop, boop!” Dot tested with quick skitters. “Skitter, skitter!”

They set a leaf shade on a stick tent. Sunlight sprinkled spots on the ground. The shade swayed and whispered, “Hush.” Fern placed the stone water bowl near the shade and added a berry bowl too. “Sip spot,” she said.

Hugo smiled. “The path looks friendly,” he whispered.

“Let’s try it,” Fern said. Hugo stepped onto the first bark stone. “Step.” He touched the moss. “Soft,” he sighed. He walked all the way to the shade. Pip cheered. Dot clapped her paws. Fern’s tail gave a happy wiggle.

“You did it,” Fern said. “We did it together.”

Hugo’s eyes shone. “Thank you for including me.”

Fern felt proud. “Including friends is part of my routine now,” she said. “We help each day.”

After Sunny Help Time, Fern checked her leaf-list again. “Play time,” she read. They made a gentle game where everyone could join. Pip hid a shiny pebble. Dot counted, “One… two… three…” Fern listened with her big ears. Hugo sniffed with his tiny nose. “Sniff, sniff… found it!” he said, and they all laughed.

They rested under the leaf shade. The air smelled like sweet clover. A soft breeze tickled their cheeks. Fern took three slow breaths again. “In… and out… in… and out… in… and out.” Her body felt calm. Her heart felt full.

Pip said, “Your routine helps me feel ready.”

Dot said, “Your new idea helped Hugo.”

Hugo said, “Your kindness made a place for me.”

Fern nuzzled her leaf-list. “Routine helps us remember to care,” she said. “Routine helps us make time to help. When we include friends and try smart new ways, we can make the day bright for everyone.”

The sun slid a little lower. The sprinkler whispered. The backyard glowed gold. Fern looked at her friends and smiled. “Let’s keep this routine,” she said. “Stretch, step, tidy, sip, plan, help, play, rest.”

All four friends said together, “We can do it!”

And they did, every sunny day.

The End.
