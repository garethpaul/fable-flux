# Rocco Raccoon’s Song of Yes

Rocco was a musical raccoon with a soft gray tail and bright, curious eyes. He loved to hum, tap, and make little songs. His favorite place was the cozy study corner, with a small desk, a basket of crayons, and a sunny window that smelled like warm wood and clean paper.

“Good morning, study corner!” Rocco sang, tapping a pencil—tap tap tap. The pencil made a happy sound. The paper felt smooth under his paws. The window’s sunbeam felt warm on his whiskers.

Today, Rocco had chores. He also had a plan. “I will sweep, snack, and sing!” he said. “In that order.” He made a little beat: “Sweep-sweep, snack-snack, sing-sing!”

Mama Raccoon peeked in. “Good plan, Rocco,” she said. “Strategy helps. First chores, then snack, then music time.”

Rocco nodded and swished his tail. He picked up a tiny broom. “Sweep-sweep,” he sang. He brushed crumbs into a pan. The crumbs went scratch-scratch. He stacked books with a thump. He put crayons in a row—red, blue, green, yellow. “All tidy,” he smiled.

Then he washed his paws with warm water—splash, swish! The soap smelled like lemons. “Healthy paws,” he said. “Clean for snack.”

Just then, Rocco heard a soft knock. “Knock-knock.”

It was Miss Owl from next door, wearing tiny glasses. She looked worried. “Hello, dear Rocco,” she hooted kindly. “The Little Library needs help. The books are jumbled. Would you come volunteer? Only a little while.”

Rocco looked at his plan on a sticky note. It said: Sweep. Snack. Sing. He hugged his belly. It grumbled, “rumble-rumble.” He looked at his drum, shiny and round. He wanted to sing right now.

“I don’t know,” Rocco said. “I have a plan. My plan is very neat.”

Miss Owl nodded. “Plans are helpful,” she agreed. “But sometimes being open-minded helps too. If we say yes to new ideas, we learn new songs.”

Rocco blinked. “New songs?” His ears perked up. He loved new sounds. But his belly rumbled again. He thought and tapped his chin. Tap, tap.

Mama Raccoon came back with a small basket. “Here is a quick apple slice,” she said, smiling. “A healthy snack to help you think.”

Rocco crunched—crunch, crunch. The apple was cool and sweet. “Mmm. My body feels ready,” he said. He looked at Miss Owl. “Maybe I can change my plan. I can be open-minded,” he said slowly. “I can use a new strategy. I can help first, then come back to sing.”

Miss Owl clapped softly. “What a thoughtful choice.”

Rocco put on his little scarf. “Let’s go!”

At the Little Library, the room smelled like books—papery and cozy. Stacks were tall, short, and crooked. “Oh my,” Rocco said. “This looks like a big drum solo!”

Miss Owl chuckled. “We will make it a gentle duet.”

Rocco made up a sorting song to help him focus: “Big books here, small books there, picture books everywhere!” He tapped a beat on the table—tap-tap-tap. Other friends joined—Bea Bunny, Timmy Turtle, and Pip the mouse. “Can we help?” they asked.

“Yes,” Rocco said, smiling. “Let’s volunteer together. We can use a strategy: lines and labels.”

They made a line and took turns. Rocco placed labels with big letters. “A is for apples, B is for bees.” Pip squeaked, “C is for carrots!” Bea giggled. Timmy moved slowly but steady. “Steady is good,” Rocco said kindly.

A little fox frowned at a wobbly stack. “I can’t,” he whispered.

Rocco knelt beside him. “Let’s try together. Open mind, open heart,” he sang softly. “We can try a new way.” He showed the fox how to slide one book at a time. “One, two, three.” The fox’s face lit up. “I can!”

Soon the library looked neat and bright. The shelves stood straight. The labels smiled in a row. Miss Owl’s eyes twinkled. “Thank you, helpers.”

Rocco felt warm and proud. His belly made a happy little “mm-hmm” sound. He noticed Bea yawning. “Water break!” he sang. Everyone sipped cool water. “Healthy habits help us help,” Rocco said.

Back at the study corner, Rocco sat at his desk. He breathed in slowly. In through the nose, out through the mouth. “I feel calm,” he said. The sunbeam tickled his whiskers again. He picked up his drum.

“I will sing about saying yes to new ideas,” Rocco said. He tapped a gentle beat. “Open mind, open door, try a little, learn some more,” he sang. The notes were round and bright. He added soft shakers—shish-shish. He added claps—clap-clap. Mama Raccoon swayed. Miss Owl hummed. Even the little fox tapped a tiny foot.

Rocco smiled wide. “Today, I changed my plan. I used a strategy. I ate a healthy snack. I volunteered to help. And I learned a new song,” he said.

Mama Raccoon hugged him. “What did you learn, my musical helper?”

Rocco’s eyes sparkled. “When I keep an open mind, I can be kind, I can be strong, and I can find new ways to help and to sing.”

“Beautiful,” said Miss Owl.

Rocco placed his broom, his drum, and his crayons neatly away. Chores done, heart happy, mind open.

The End.
