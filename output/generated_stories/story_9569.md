# Squeaky’s Helpful Guest Room Surprise

Squeaky the resourceful squirrel lived in a big maple tree outside a cozy house. One sunny morning, Squeaky peeked in the window and saw a bright, quiet room. It had soft curtains, a comfy bed, a tiny bookshelf, and a small desk with crayons. It was the guest room.

“Hello, guest room,” Squeaky whispered. “You look calm and kind.”

Squeaky wiggled his bushy tail. He heard the family talking in the hallway.

“Grandma is visiting today,” said Mia, the little girl. “I want the guest room to feel happy!”

Squeaky’s ears perked up. He loved to help. Helping made his heart feel warm, like sunlight on his fur. He wanted to make the guest room happy for Grandma, too.

Squeaky squeezed through the window screen (just a tiny gap!) and landed on the soft rug. The rug felt like a cloud under his paws. He sniffed the air. It smelled like clean sheets and a little like lavender. “Mmm,” Squeaky hummed.

On the bed sat a small note card. It said, “Welcome, Grandma!” in bright purple. Squeaky smiled. He loved words. He liked how letters made sounds. He liked how words could be kind.

Squeaky whispered, “Words can hug.” He tapped the card. “We can add more sweet words, in many languages!”

He had an idea. Squeaky was very good at ideas. He was resourceful. He could find, fix, and make things work.

Squeaky looked around the guest room. He saw:
- A basket with clean towels, soft and fluffy.
- A little lamp that made warm, golden light.
- Crayons on the desk.
- A small flower in a vase that smelled like spring.

He also saw that the welcome card was lonely on the bed. “Let’s make a welcome corner,” he said. “A corner full of helpful things!”

Squeaky dragged the basket to the desk with tiny, careful pushes. “Push, push, push,” he grunted. The basket bumped and made a soft thump on the rug.

Next, Squeaky clicked on the lamp. Click! The light glowed like honey. “Cozy,” Squeaky said, patting the lamp gently.

He opened the crayon box. The waxy crayons smelled sweet and colorful. “Let’s write welcome words,” Squeaky said. “Many words for Grandma!”

Squeaky thought of the words he had heard Mia say when they read books near the tree.

“Hello,” he said, and wrote HELLO with a blue crayon.

“Hola,” he said softly, and wrote HOLA with a sunny yellow crayon.

“Bonjour,” he sang, and wrote BONJOUR in pink.

“Nǐ hǎo,” he chirped, and wrote 你好 in green, copying from a little sticker he had once seen on Mia’s notebook.

“Jambo,” he added, and wrote JAMBO in orange.

“Namaste,” he smiled, writing NAMASTE in purple to match the card.

Squeaky lined up the notes like a rainbow. He felt proud, but he kept thinking. “What else is helpful?” he asked the quiet room.

His whiskers twitched. He remembered Grandma’s cane from last visit. She liked to keep it near the door. Squeaky looked at the door. There was no hook. “Hmm,” Squeaky hummed. “We can make a gentle holder.”

He found a soft scarf in the closet. It felt smooth and cozy. Squeaky tied the scarf into a loop on the doorknob. He pulled it snug but kind. “There,” he said. “A safe loop for the cane.” It was a small, smart fix—an innovation from a resourceful squirrel.

Squeaky climbed onto the bed and patted the pillow. “Pillows need friends,” he said. He looked at the little flower. Carefully, he moved the vase to the bedside table. The flower’s smell danced in the air. “Grandma can smell spring right away,” he said.

He listened. Footsteps! Mia and Mom were coming. Squeaky felt a tiny flutter in his tummy. “Will they like it?” he wondered.

The door opened. “Oh!” said Mia. Her eyes grew wide. “Look, Mom! Welcome words!”

Mom smiled. “What a helpful surprise. The room feels so friendly.”

Mia read the words out loud, one by one, with a happy voice. “Hello. Hola. Bonjour. Nǐ hǎo. Jambo. Namaste.” The sounds were like music. Squeaky’s ears wiggled with joy.

“What’s this loop?” Mom asked.

“It’s for Grandma’s cane!” Mia said, trying it. The scarf loop held gently and safely.

Mom nodded. “Very thoughtful.”

Squeaky peeked from behind the curtain. Mia spotted him and whispered, “Thank you, Squeaky. You are so helpful.”

Squeaky stepped out, tail swishing. “I wanted Grandma to feel welcome,” he said. “Words can hug in many languages. Little fixes can help a lot.”

Grandma arrived soon. She walked into the guest room and smiled. “Oh my,” she said, breathing in the flower. “It smells like a garden. And look at these kind words!” She rested her cane in the soft loop. “How clever.”

Squeaky’s chest felt warm and bright. “Welcome, Grandma,” he said, and pointed to the notes.

Grandma chuckled softly. “Thank you, little helper. You used your head and your heart.”

They all sat on the bed together. The lamp glowed. The room felt calm, safe, and full of love.

Mia hugged Squeaky. “Today we learned something,” she said. “Helping makes a home happy. Words can show love in many ways. And small, smart ideas can make big comfort.”

Squeaky nodded. “Yes,” he said. “We can all be helpful, every day.”

The End.
