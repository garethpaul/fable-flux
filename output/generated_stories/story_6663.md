# Ellie's Bubble Tower Adventure

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved bath time! Her trunk wiggled with excitement as Mama filled the big bathtub with warm, bubbly water. The bubbles sparkled like tiny rainbows, and they smelled like sweet strawberries.

"Splash, splash!" Ellie giggled as she stepped into the tub. The warm water felt so good on her gray skin. She used her trunk like a snorkel, blowing bubbles that danced in the air.

Just then, Ellie heard tiny voices coming from the corner of the bathroom.

"Help! Help!" squeaked a small voice.

Ellie looked around with her big, kind eyes. There, by the soap dish, she saw three little rubber duckies. They were yellow, red, and blue, and they looked very sad.

"What's wrong, little friends?" Ellie asked gently, reaching out with her trunk.

"We want to play in the bubbles too," said Yellow Duck. "But we're too small to climb into the big tub."

"And we can't reach the bubble mountain you're building," added Red Duck, pointing at Ellie's tall tower of bubbles.

Blue Duck nodded sadly. "We always have to watch from down here. No one ever includes us in bath time fun."

Ellie's heart felt heavy. She didn't like seeing her new friends left out. "That's not fair," she said. "Everyone should get to play with bubbles!"

Ellie thought for a moment, tapping her trunk against her chin. Then her eyes lit up with a wonderful idea.

"I know! Let me help you!" Ellie said excitedly.

Very carefully, Ellie used her gentle trunk to lift each duck, one by one, into the warm bubble bath. Yellow Duck went first, then Red Duck, and finally Blue Duck.

"Wheee!" they all cheered as they splashed into the bubbly water.

"Now we can all build together!" Ellie announced. "But we need to take turns so everyone gets a chance."

Ellie showed her new friends how to make the best bubble towers. "First, Yellow Duck, you scoop up some bubbles. Then Red Duck adds more on top. Blue Duck, you can make it even taller!"

The three ducks worked together, taking turns just like Ellie taught them. Yellow Duck gathered fluffy white bubbles. Red Duck carefully placed them higher. Blue Duck added the finishing touches with her tiny orange beak.

"Look how tall our tower is when we work together!" gasped Blue Duck.

Their bubble tower reached almost to the ceiling! It was much bigger and more beautiful than anything Ellie could have built alone.

"This is the most fun I've ever had!" said Red Duck, doing a little spin in the water.

"Me too!" agreed Yellow Duck. "Thank you for including us, Ellie!"

Ellie felt warm and happy inside, even warmer than the bath water. "Friends are always better when we're all together," she said with a big smile.

They spent the rest of bath time building bubble castles, taking turns being the builder and the helper. They made bubble hats, bubble boats, and even tiny bubble animals.

When Mama called that bath time was over, all four friends were giggling and splashing happily.

"Same time tomorrow?" asked the three ducks hopefully.

"Of course!" trumpeted Ellie. "We're a team now!"

As Ellie dried off with her fluffy towel, she felt proud. She had learned that including everyone makes everything more fun, and taking turns helps everyone feel special.

The End.
