# Wiggle Breaks in the Home Office

Pip was a curious fox kit with bright eyes and soft, rust-red fur. One rainy morning, Pip padded into the home office on quiet paws. The room smelled like warm tea and fresh paper. Daddy Fox sat at a big desk, typing with a clack-clack-clack. A small fan hummed. The window showed raindrops racing down the glass.

Pip whispered, “Daddy, what are you doing?”

“I’m working,” Daddy Fox said kindly. “Lots of words today. Click, click, click!”

Pip looked at the tall chair and the shiny keyboard. Pip’s tail swished. His legs felt bouncy. “Can I help?”

Daddy Fox smiled. “You can help by playing gently. I need quiet paws, little kit.”

Pip tried to sit still on the soft rug. He listened to the clock go tick-tock. He watched the cursor blink on the screen. He smelled the tea—sweet and lemony. But his body wiggled. His toes wanted to tap. His tail wanted to swish. His knees wanted to bend.

“My body says move,” Pip whispered to himself. “But Daddy needs quiet.”

Pip spotted a stack of sticky notes and a jar of paper clips. They sparkled like tiny fish. “I’ll just look,” he said. He leaned closer. The chair rolled. The papers fluttered. Pip’s paw brushed the jar.

Clink!

Paper clips spilled like shiny rain. They slid under the desk. Sticky notes floated down like yellow leaves.

“Oh no,” gasped Pip. His ears drooped. “I made a mess!”

Daddy Fox turned his chair. “Pip?”

“I’m sorry,” Pip said softly. “I wanted to help. My body wanted to move. I didn’t mean to spill.”

Daddy Fox took a deep breath. He looked at Pip’s worried face, then at the floor. “Thank you for telling me the truth,” he said. “I forgive you.”

Pip’s eyes brightened. “You do?”

“Yes,” Daddy Fox said. “Everyone makes mistakes. Let’s solve the problem together.”

Pip’s tail gave a small wag. “Teamwork?”

“Teamwork,” Daddy Fox agreed. “First, we will move our bodies in a good way. Then we will clean. Moving can help our brains and our hearts.”

Pip bounced. “A move break?”

Daddy Fox stood and stretched his arms up, up, up. “Reach for the rain clouds,” he said.

Pip reached too. He felt his sides stretch. His paws tingled. “I can reach!”

“Now bend down and touch the floor,” said Daddy Fox. “Say hello to your toes.”

“Hello, toes!” Pip giggled. The rug felt fuzzy and warm.

“Wiggle fingers! Wiggle paws!” Daddy Fox wiggled. Pip wiggled. Their whiskers twitched. The fan hummed. The rain tapped the window like tiny drums.

“Can we hop?” Pip asked.

“Let’s do quiet hops,” Daddy Fox said. “Soft like snowflakes.”

They hopped softly in place. Boing, boing, boing. Pip felt happy. His legs felt strong. His worries felt lighter.

“My body likes to move,” Pip said.

“Bodies need movement,” Daddy Fox said. “Moving helps us think and feel calm. After we move, we can do our jobs.”

Pip nodded. “Job: clean up!”

They knelt on the rug. “I will pick up the clips,” Daddy Fox said. “Can you gather the sticky notes, Pip?”

“Yes!” Pip scooped the yellow squares. They felt smooth and sticky on his paws. He sniffed the paper—clean, like fresh rain. He lined the notes in a neat stack. He peered under the desk and saw a paper clip. “I see a shiny fish!” he said.

“I’ve got it,” Daddy Fox said, reaching. “Thank you for spotting.”

Pip’s tail swished with pride. “Teamwork is fast-work!”

Soon the floor was tidy. The jar was full again, with a happy clink. Daddy Fox set the jar far from the edge. He put the sticky notes in a little box.

“Problem solved,” Daddy Fox said. “How do you feel, Pip?”

“I feel calm,” Pip said. “I feel strong. I feel helpful.”

Daddy Fox smiled. “I feel proud of you. You noticed your body needed to move. You told the truth. You worked as a team.”

Pip looked at the chair. “Can we make a plan for moving?”

“A great idea,” Daddy Fox said. He took a marker and drew simple pictures on a big note: a sun, a fox hopping, a fox stretching, a fox drinking water. “Every time the clock says tick four times, we take a wiggle break. Stretch, hop, sip water, then back to work.”

Pip clapped his small paws. “Wiggle plan!”

Tick, tick, tick, tick went the clock.

“Wiggle break!” Pip cheered.

They stretched up tall like trees. They bent low like bridges. They tiptoed like mice. They did five soft hops. Pip sipped cool water. It tasted fresh and sweet. The rain outside slowed. Light slid in through the window, warm on Pip’s fur.

When the break ended, Daddy Fox sat and typed again. Pip drew on his own paper with a fat, blue crayon. Scritch, scritch. He drew a fox and a fan and a jar full of clips. He drew a big heart. He smiled.

“I discovered something,” Pip said.

“What did you discover?” asked Daddy Fox.

“I discovered that moving helps me do my best,” Pip said. “And when I make a mess, I can say sorry and fix it. And we can be kind.”

Daddy Fox nodded. “That is a wise discovery, little kit. Movement, forgiveness, kindness, and teamwork make a happy home.”

Pip wiggled one more time. “Wiggle happy!”

They laughed together. The home office felt cozy and bright. The room still smelled like tea and paper, and now, like a fresh new start.

The End.
