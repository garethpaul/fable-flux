# Benny Beaver's New Friend

Benny Beaver loved to build things. Every morning, he would waddle into the old garden shed behind Mrs. Chen's house, where he kept all his special building tools. The shed smelled like wood chips and sunshine, and it was filled with sticks, logs, and shiny nails.

"Time to build my best dam ever!" Benny said happily, picking up his favorite hammer. *Tap, tap, tap!* He loved the sound it made on the wood.

But today was different. As Benny worked, he heard a strange new sound. *Squeak, squeak, squeak!*

"What's that noise?" Benny wondered, looking around the cozy shed. Behind a pile of old flower pots, he saw a small gray mouse with big round eyes.

"¡Hola!" said the mouse in a cheerful voice. "Me llamo Rosa!"

Benny's eyes grew wide. He had never heard words like that before. "I... I don't understand," he said, feeling confused and a little scared.

Rosa tried again. "Hello! My name is Rosa!" she said in English, but with a beautiful accent that made the words sound like music.

"Oh!" said Benny, feeling relieved. "You speak differently than me. I'm Benny. What were those other words you said?"

"Those were Spanish words," Rosa explained, her whiskers twitching with excitement. "I speak two languages! Spanish and English. My family is from Mexico."

Benny had never met anyone who spoke two languages before. At first, he felt worried. "Will we be able to be friends if you speak differently?" he asked.

Rosa's eyes sparkled. "Of course! Speaking different languages makes friendship even more special. I can teach you Spanish words, and you can teach me about building!"

Benny's tail slapped the wooden floor with excitement. "Really? That sounds wonderful!"

Rosa showed Benny how to say "amigo," which means friend in Spanish. Benny practiced saying it over and over: "Ah-MEE-go! Ah-MEE-go!"

Then Benny showed Rosa how to use his tiny hammer. "First, you hold it like this," he explained patiently. "Then you tap gently on the nail."

*Tap, tap!* Rosa tried it, and her little paws worked perfectly.

"You're a great teacher, amigo!" Rosa said, and they both giggled.

Together, they built the most amazing tiny house for Rosa's family. Benny used his strong teeth to cut the wood just right, while Rosa used her small paws to fit pieces into tight spaces.

As they worked, Rosa taught Benny more Spanish words. "Madera" means wood, and "casa" means house. Benny loved how the words rolled off his tongue like a happy song.

"You know what?" Benny said, stepping back to admire their beautiful work. "Having a friend who speaks different languages is the best thing ever! You've taught me so many wonderful new words."

"And you've taught me how to build!" Rosa replied. "Different languages and different skills make us both stronger together."

From that day on, Benny and Rosa worked together in the garden shed every morning. The shed was filled with the sounds of hammering, Spanish songs, and lots of laughter. Benny learned that differences don't divide friends – they make friendships more colorful and fun.

Mrs. Chen would often peek into the shed and smile, hearing the happy chatter in two languages and seeing the beautiful things that friendship could build.

The End.
