# Tiko the Tortoise and the Bedroom Bins

Tiko was a wise tortoise with a soft, green shell and gentle eyes. He lived in a cozy bedroom with warm yellow walls, a fluffy blue rug, and a window that smelled like fresh rain when it was open. The room was quiet except for the tick-tock of a tiny clock. Tiko liked it tidy. He liked it calm. He liked it clean.

One morning, Tiko woke up and stretched his legs. “Good morning, room,” he said. He rubbed his sleepy eyes and sniffed. “Sniff-sniff. Hmm. What is that smell?”

He looked around. The rug had crumbs. The bed had wrinkled sheets. Toy blocks rested in a pile like little mountains. On the floor he saw paper, plastic bottles, and a banana peel near his laundry basket.

“Oh my,” said Tiko softly. “This is not cozy. This is messy.”

His friend, Bella the Bunny, hopped in. Her fur was snowy white, and her nose twitched-twitch-twitched. “Hi, Tiko!” she sang. “I brought juice!” She held up a crunchy plastic bottle with a bright label.

“Thank you, Bella,” said Tiko. “But look at my room. It needs love. It needs chores.”

Bella looked down. Her ears drooped. “I’m sorry. I dropped some wrappers yesterday. I forgot to help.”

Tiko smiled kindly. “It’s okay. We can be kind and fix it together. We can learn.”

They sat on the rug. Tiko tapped his chin. “We have a problem,” he said. “There is too much trash on the floor.”

Bella nodded. “Yes. Problem.”

Tiko took a deep breath in. He felt the cool air. He could smell the banana peel. “Phew. We need a plan!”

“A plan!” Bella clapped. “What is the plan?”

Tiko went to his closet and pulled out three little bins. One was blue, one was green, and one was yellow. He set them in a row. Tap, tap, tap.

“Listen,” said Tiko. “Blue bin is for paper. Green bin is for plastic. Yellow bin is for food scraps. We will recycle. We will compost. We will keep the room clean.”

Bella’s eyes got wide. “Re-cy-cle,” she said slowly. “What does that mean?”

“It means we use things again,” Tiko explained. “Paper can become new paper. Plastic can become new toys. Food scraps can help plants grow. When we recycle, we help our home and our Earth.”

Bella’s ears perked up again. “That sounds nice. Let’s try!”

They started sorting. Tiko picked up a soft paper drawing. “Blue bin,” he said. It made a gentle whisper as it slid in. Bella found a crinkly plastic wrapper. “Green bin!” she chirped. It made a crinkle-crackle sound.

They found the banana peel. “Yellow bin,” said Tiko, wrinkling his nose. “Pee-yew!” Bella giggled.

Tiko showed patience. When Bella dropped a paper cup into the wrong bin, he did not fuss. He said, “It’s okay. Let’s try again.” Bella smiled. “Thank you for being kind. I am learning.”

They worked and hummed a little song:
“Sort, sort, sort with care,
Blue and green and yellow there.
Clean our room, we all can try,
Help the Earth, oh me, oh my!”

The room began to smell fresh again. The rug felt soft under their toes and claws. The window breeze felt cool on their faces. The tick-tock clock sounded happy and calm: tick, tock, tick.

Next, Tiko said, “Chores time. I will make the bed.” He smoothed the sheets. Swish. He puffed the pillows. Poof!

Bella said, “I will dust.” She dusted the shelf with a gentle wipe-wipe. Tiko said, “Thank you, Bella. You are helpful.”

Tiko remembered hygiene too. He went to the small sink and washed his hands. Swish-swash, swish-swash, bubble-bubble-bubble. He hummed while he scrubbed. “We wash to stay healthy,” he said. Bella washed her paws. “So clean!” she cheered.

Then they faced a new problem. The bottle Bella brought was empty now. “Where does this go?” she asked, holding the plastic bottle.

“Green bin,” said Tiko. “But first, we rinse.” He added a little water, shook it, and poured it out. They listened. Drip, drip. “Now it is ready.”

Bella nodded. “I can do it!” She put the bottle in the green bin. Thunk.

Soon, the floor was clear. The bins were sorted. The room looked bright. Tiko put the toy blocks back in their box. Click, clack. Bella lined up the books on the shelf. Tap, tap.

They both sat on the fluffy rug. Tiko breathed in. “Ahh. Cozy again.”

Bella touched Tiko’s shell. “Thank you for teaching me. You were patient.”

Tiko smiled. “Thank you for trying. We showed tolerance. We were kind when we made mistakes. We did our chores. We kept good hygiene. And most of all, we recycled.”

Bella’s nose twitched. “I like helping the Earth.”

Tiko nodded. “We can do this every day. We can sort our trash. We can keep our room clean. When we recycle, we give new life to old things. That is a wise choice.”

They bumped noses and laughed. Sunlight peeked in the window and made warm stripes on the floor. The room felt safe and happy. The tiny clock went tick-tock, like a little cheer.

Tiko whispered, “Today we had a problem. We made a plan. We found a solution.”

Bella whispered back, “And now our cozy bedroom is cozy again.”

They looked at the blue, green, and yellow bins all in a neat row. They looked at their clean paws and the tidy bed. Tiko said, “The Earth says thank you.”

Bella giggled. “You’re welcome, Earth!”

The End.
