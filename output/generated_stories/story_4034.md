# Polly Parrot’s Music Cleanup

Polly was a creative parrot who loved colors and songs. She lived in a bright, creative art room. The room had soft paintbrushes, shiny buttons, big paper, and tiny bells that jingled. Sunlight made little rainbows on the floor. It smelled like crayons and orange soap.

Polly fluffed her green and blue feathers. “Squawk! Today, I will make music art!” she chirped. Her eyes sparkled. She tapped her little claws on the table. Tap-tap. Her beak clicked, tick-tick. “Listen, a beat!”

Polly pulled out pots and pans from the pretend kitchen corner. She set them in a line. “You are drums,” she told them. “Boom, boom!” She shook a jar of beads. “You are maracas! Shake-shake!” She found rubber bands and stretched them over a box. “You are a guitar. Twang!”

Soon the room was full of sounds. Boom, shake, twang, ting! Polly danced and flapped. “Music is fun!” she sang. “Music makes my heart go happy!”

But as Polly played and played, she made a big, big mess. Crayons rolled under chairs. Brushes hid in cups. The pots were all over the floor. The tiny bells spilled and scattered. Ting-ting-ting! The sounds were loud and wild.

Mrs. Dot, the gentle teacher, came in. She wore a polka-dot apron and had kind eyes. She smiled at Polly. “I hear your music,” said Mrs. Dot. “It sounds creative. But I see a problem.”

Polly stopped and tilted her head. “A problem?” she asked.

Mrs. Dot nodded. “Our art room is very messy. When things are messy, it is hard to listen. It is hard to find the sounds we want. Music needs listening. Music needs order.”

Polly felt her cheeks get warm. “I did not mean to make trouble,” she said softly.

“It’s okay,” Mrs. Dot said in a calm voice. “Problems can be solved. Would you like to solve it together?”

Polly took a deep breath. She listened. She could hear the tiny bells still rolling. Tink. She heard a crayon bump, bump, bump. She heard her heart go thump-thump. “Yes,” she said. “Let’s solve it.”

Mrs. Dot pointed to the shelves. “We can make a music station. We can organize. Let’s put like sounds together. Drums here. Shakers here. Strings here. Bells in a safe bowl.”

Polly’s eyes lit up. “Like a band!” she squawked.

“Exactly,” Mrs. Dot said. “And we will use our listening ears.”

Polly smoothed her feathers and sang, “Listen, sort, and play!” She picked up the pots. “Boom-boom group, come with me.” She stacked them neatly on a low shelf. She found the jar of beads and poured them back with a soft shhh. “Shakers here,” she whispered. She plucked the rubber band box. Twang! She smiled and put it in a bin with a picture of strings. She scooped the tiny bells and placed them in a bowl that made a soft clink.

As they worked, Polly and Mrs. Dot listened for quiet. “Do you hear the room saying thank you?” Mrs. Dot asked.

Polly paused. The room sounded gentle now. She could hear the hum of the lights, the whisper of paper, and the tiny breeze from the window. “I hear it,” she said. “It is calm.”

Mrs. Dot clapped softly. “Now we can make music that tells a story. Would you like to try a listening game?”

“Yes!” Polly chirped.

“First, we listen,” Mrs. Dot said. “I will clap a rhythm. You listen. Then you play it back on the drums.”

Mrs. Dot clapped: clap, clap, pause, clap.

Polly put one pot on the table. She tapped with a wooden spoon: tap, tap, pause, tap. She listened before each tap. The sound was clear. “I did it!”

“You did,” said Mrs. Dot. “Listening helps our music.”

Polly grinned. “My turn!” She shook the maracas: shake, shake, shake.

Mrs. Dot listened with a smile, then shook a second shaker: shake, shake, shake. “We match,” she said.

Polly felt proud. Her feathers felt light and fluffy. She looked at the organized shelves. The labels showed little pictures: a drum, a bell, a string, a shaker. “Organization helps me find my sound,” she said. “When I know where things live, I can listen better.”

“Exactly,” Mrs. Dot said. “Organization and listening make music stronger.”

Polly spread her wings and made a little song:

“Listen first, then play along,
Put things back, and make them belong.
Music is sweet when we care and share,
We make neat sounds when the room is fair.”

Mrs. Dot swayed to the tune. “Beautiful,” she said.

Polly and Mrs. Dot played one more song. Boom, shake, twang, ting! The music was joyful but gentle. The room stayed neat. When they finished, Polly carefully put each instrument back in its home. She listened to the last tiny ting fade away.

Polly smiled. “I learned a big lesson,” she said. “Music is not just noise. Music is listening. Music is feeling. Music is taking care. When I organize and listen, my music shines.”

Mrs. Dot nodded. “That is a wonderful lesson.”

Polly fluffed her feathers again. She was ready to make more songs, one careful, happy note at a time.

The End.
