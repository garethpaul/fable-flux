# Splash the Dolphin's Special Hideaway

In a cozy little house by the sea, there lived a playful dolphin named Splash. But Splash wasn't swimming in the ocean today. Instead, he had made himself a wonderful hideaway in the bedroom closet!

Splash had hung soft blue blankets from the closet rod, making it feel like being underwater. He placed his favorite shell collection on the floor and brought in a squishy pillow that smelled like ocean breeze. "This is my special place," Splash said happily, clicking his dolphin sounds. "Click-click-whistle!"

Soon, Splash heard little footsteps outside his hideaway. "Splash? Are you in there?" called his friend Bella the bunny.

"Yes! But this is my special hideaway," Splash replied, poking his smooth gray head out from behind the blue blankets.

"Can I come in? It looks so cozy!" Bella asked, her pink nose twitching with excitement.

Splash felt his tummy get tight. He loved his friend, but he also loved having his own special space. "Well... okay, but just for a little bit," he said.

Bella hopped inside and gasped. "Wow! It's like being underwater! Can we play ocean adventures?"

Before Splash could answer, Tommy the turtle came crawling over. "What's all the fun? Can I join too?"

Now Splash felt even more squeezed. His special hideaway was getting very crowded! "I... I guess," he said, but his voice sounded wobbly.

Soon, three more friends had squeezed into the closet hideaway. Splash felt frustrated and overwhelmed. The space that had felt so peaceful now felt too full and noisy.

"Let's all tell stories!" shouted Ruby the raccoon.

"I want to go first!" said Tommy.

"No, me first!" said Bella.

Everyone started talking at once. Splash's head began to spin. "STOP!" he called out, making a loud dolphin whistle. "This is too much!"

All his friends looked surprised. Splash took a deep breath, just like his mama had taught him when he felt overwhelmed.

"Friends," Splash said gently, "I love playing with you, but I need to be honest. This is my special hideaway where I come when I need quiet time. When everyone comes in at once, it doesn't feel special anymore."

Bella's ears drooped. "Oh! I'm sorry, Splash. I didn't know."

Splash smiled. "It's okay! But maybe we can take turns? One friend at a time can visit my hideaway, and we can tell stories together. That way, everyone gets a turn, and my space stays peaceful."

"That's a great idea!" said Tommy. "Taking turns means everyone gets to enjoy it!"

So they made a plan. Bella would visit first and share a story about dancing carrots. Then Tommy would have his turn and tell a tale about racing clouds. Each friend would get fifteen minutes in the cozy hideaway with Splash.

"When it's not your turn, you can play in the bedroom," Splash explained kindly. "And if someone says they need alone time, we should listen and respect that."

As each friend took their turn, Splash felt much happier. He could enjoy his special space AND spend time with his friends. The storytelling was wonderful when it was just two friends sharing quietly together.

"Thank you for teaching us about taking turns and respecting your special space," Bella said when everyone had their turn.

Splash beamed. "And thank you for understanding! Taking turns makes everything more fun for everyone."

From that day on, Splash's hideaway became even more special - a place where friends could visit one at a time, share stories, and learn that taking turns and respecting boundaries makes friendships even stronger.

The End.
