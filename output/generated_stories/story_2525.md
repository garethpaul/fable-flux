# Daisy's Cookie Sharing Day

The warm kitchen smelled like vanilla and cinnamon. Golden sunlight streamed through the window, making everything glow. Little Daisy Deer stood on her tippy-hooves at the counter. Her mama had just pulled fresh cookies from the oven.

"Mmm! They smell so good, Mama!" Daisy said. Her brown eyes sparkled as she watched steam rise from the chocolate chip cookies.

"They're for your playdate today," Mama Deer smiled. "Ruby Rabbit and Tommy Turtle are coming over soon."

Daisy's stomach rumbled. The cookies looked perfect - golden brown with melty chocolate chips. She counted them carefully. "One, two, three, four, five, six!" 

Just then, the doorbell rang. *Ding-dong!*

"That must be your friends!" Mama said, wiping her hooves on her apron.

Ruby Rabbit hopped into the kitchen first. Her pink nose twitched. "Wow! Something smells amazing!"

Tommy Turtle slowly walked behind her. His shell bumped softly against the doorframe. "Hi, Daisy! What's that yummy smell?"

"Mama made cookies!" Daisy announced proudly. She pointed to the cooling rack.

Ruby's eyes grew wide. "Can we have some? Please?"

Daisy looked at the six cookies. Then she looked at her two friends. Her heart felt heavy. "Well... there are only six cookies," she said quietly. "If I give you each two, I'll only have two left for me."

Tommy's face fell. "Oh. That's okay, Daisy. We don't need any."

Ruby nodded sadly. "Yeah, we understand."

Daisy watched her friends' disappointed faces. Her tummy felt funny - not hungry funny, but worried funny. She remembered when she had been sad last week. Ruby had shared her crayons without being asked. And Tommy always let everyone play with his favorite toy truck.

Suddenly, Daisy had an idea. Her spotted ears perked up.

"Wait!" she called out. "I know what we can do!"

She carefully picked up all six cookies. "Let's break each cookie in half! Then we'll have twelve pieces. We can each have four pieces!"

"Really?" Ruby's ears stood up straight.

"That's so smart!" Tommy smiled big.

Together, they gently broke each warm cookie. The kitchen filled with giggles as they counted the pieces. "Four for Ruby, four for Tommy, and four for me!"

Mama Deer watched from the doorway. Her heart felt warm and proud.

The three friends sat at the little table. They munched their cookie pieces slowly, making happy "mmm" sounds.

"These are the best cookies ever!" Ruby said with chocolate on her whiskers.

"They taste even better when we share them," Tommy agreed.

Daisy felt a warm, fuzzy feeling in her chest. It was different from being full of cookies. It felt like sunshine inside her heart.

"You know what?" Daisy said, licking chocolate from her hoof. "Sharing made the cookies taste magical!"

Ruby and Tommy nodded. Their faces were covered in happy crumbs.

After their snack, Mama Deer gave each friend a hug. "Daisy, I'm so proud of how you solved that problem. You found a way to be fair and kind to everyone."

Daisy's cheeks turned pink with happiness. "It felt good to share, Mama. Really, really good."

As her friends played in the garden, Daisy helped clean the kitchen. She learned something important that day: when you share with others, everyone feels happy - especially you.

The End.
