# Cubby Fixes and Reuses

Cubby was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, Cubby padded into Grandpa Bear’s garage workshop. The air smelled like pine wood and orange soap. Sunlight made little dust dances in the warm air. Tools hung on hooks. Jars of buttons and screws clinked softly when the door closed.

“Good morning, Cubby,” said Grandpa Bear, wiping his paws on a clean cloth. “What brings you to the workshop?”

Cubby held up a small red wagon with a wobbly wheel. “My wagon goes clunk-clunk,” Cubby said. “I think it’s broken.”

Grandpa Bear knelt down. “Hmm. The wheel is loose,” he said gently. “We can fix it.”

Cubby’s ears perked up. “Fix it? Not throw it away?”

Grandpa Bear smiled. “We fix, we reuse, we take care. That is called sustainability. It means we help the Earth by using things wisely.”

Cubby nodded slowly. “Soo-sus… sustain-a-bil-ity,” he said, tasting the big word. “I want to help the Earth.”

“You can,” said Grandpa Bear. “Let’s learn some skills.”

He set the wagon on a wooden bench. The bench felt smooth, and the wood was warm from the sun. “First, we look,” Grandpa said. “We use our eyes.” He pointed. “See that nut? It is loose.”

Cubby leaned close. “I see!” he said. “It wiggles.”

“Next, we plan,” said Grandpa. “We use our brains. We do not rush.”

Cubby took a deep breath. He liked the orange soap smell. It made him feel calm and ready. “I can plan,” he whispered.

Grandpa Bear handed Cubby a small wrench. It felt cool and a little heavy. “This tool can turn the nut,” Grandpa explained. “Hold it tight. Lefty-loosey, righty-tighty.”

Cubby giggled. “Lefty-loosey, righty-tighty!”

Together they turned the nut. The metal made a tiny squeak. “Squeee,” Cubby said, giggling again. The wheel stopped wobbling.

“We did it!” Cubby cheered.

Grandpa Bear nodded. “You fixed, you reused. That is responsibility. We care for our things.”

Cubby hugged his wagon. “I feel proud.”

Grandpa Bear pointed to a bin. “We also sort. We recycle. Look.” Inside were tidy boxes: one for metal, one for paper, one for plastic, and one for “to repair.” Little labels showed pictures. “Sorting helps the Earth too.”

Cubby peered in. He saw shiny screws in the metal box, soft paper in another, and a cracked toy car in the “to repair” box. “Can we fix the car?” he asked.

“We can try,” said Grandpa. “But first, we clean the workshop. We put tools back.” He winked. “That is responsible too.”

Cubby liked putting things back. He held the wrench and asked, “Where do you live, little wrench?”

Grandpa Bear chuckled. “On the hook with the picture of a wrench.”

Cubby hung it up. Click. He wiped the bench with a soft cloth. Swish, swish. The wood smelled even nicer.

“Now the car,” Grandpa said. The toy car had a split roof. “We can glue it,” he explained. “We use a tiny bit. We hold. We wait. Patience is a skill.”

Cubby watched a small drop of glue sparkle. He held the car with steady paws. “I can wait,” he said. He counted softly, “One, two, three…” The glue set. The car looked happy again.

Cubby grinned. “We fixed it! We did not throw it away. That helps the Earth.”

Grandpa Bear nodded. “Yes. When we fix and reuse, we make less trash. Less trash means cleaner woods, cleaner water, and happier animals.”

Cubby thought about the river where he splashed. He thought about birds singing in trees. He thought about the sweet berries he liked to eat. “I want happy woods,” he said.

Grandpa Bear opened another drawer and held up a little bell. “Your wagon could use a bell. We can upcycle this. That means we make something new from something old.”

Cubby’s eyes grew wide. “Upcycle,” he said. “New from old.”

They attached the bell with a small screw. Ding-a-ling! The sound was bright and cheerful. Cubby rang it again. Ding-a-ling!

“Now it’s time to sweep,” Grandpa said, handing Cubby a small broom. The broom bristles whispered on the floor. Whoosh, whoosh. Cubby swept up tiny wood curls and a bit of sawdust into a pan.

“We keep our space tidy,” Grandpa said. “That keeps us safe and helps us find things. That is responsibility.”

Cubby nodded. “I can be careful. I can be kind to the Earth.”

When they were done, the workshop was neat. The jars were still, the tools shiny, the air fresh. Cubby pulled his wagon to the door. The wheel rolled smooth and quiet. The bell went ding-a-ling as the sun warmed his fur.

“Thank you, Grandpa,” Cubby said. “Today I learned three things. Fix and reuse. Sort and recycle. Clean and be careful. That is sustain-a-bil-ity!”

Grandpa Bear gave Cubby a gentle hug. “Yes, little one. You learned skills. You took responsibility. You helped the Earth.”

Cubby smiled big. “I will teach my friends,” he said. “We will have happy woods.”

He waved and rolled away, the wagon steady, the bell bright, his heart full. As Cubby went down the path, he whispered, “Fix, reuse, recycle, and care.” The words felt good and strong, like a promise.

The End.
