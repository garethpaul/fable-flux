# Binky Bunny and the Tumble Trouble

Binky Bunny was a determined rabbit with soft gray fur and long, listening ears. He loved to help. Today, Binky hopped into the laundry room to help his friend, Mia. The room hummed and buzzed. The washing machine swished like the ocean—swish, swish, swish. The dryer tumbled warm clothes with a gentle thump-thump-thump. The air smelled like soap bubbles and clean towels.

“Hi, Binky!” Mia smiled. “Can you help me fold the clothes?”

“Yes!” said Binky. “I can do it. I am a big helper.” His whiskers wiggled with pride. He was determined to fold every sock and shirt.

Binky reached for a tiny red sock. It was warm and soft. He pressed it to his nose. “Mmm, it smells like sunshine,” he said. He looked for the other red sock. He looked on the basket, under a towel, and inside a sleeve. No sock.

“Where is your sock friend?” Binky asked the red sock. He liked things to match. He wanted it to be just right.

Mia laughed. “Socks hide sometimes. Keep looking.”

Binky looked more. He checked the dryer. He checked the floor. He checked behind a chair. He found a blue sock, a striped sock, a fuzzy sock… but not the little red sock.

His ears drooped. His cheeks felt hot. His paws gripped the basket. The thump-thump of the dryer felt too loud now. The swish-swish of the washer made his belly feel twisty.

“I can’t find it!” Binky shouted. He stomped one paw. Sock pairs tumbled out of the basket. A shirt fell on his foot. “I want it now!”

Mia crouched low, eye to eye. “Oh, Binky. Are you feeling mad?”

“Yes,” said Binky. His voice was shaky. “I tried and tried. I can’t make it right.”

Mia nodded. “It is okay to feel mad. Let’s help your body calm down.” She spoke in a soft, slow voice, like a warm blanket. “Would you like to try a calm-down plan?”

Binky’s ears twitched. “What is a calm-down plan?”

“It’s a way to help big feelings get smaller,” said Mia. “We can Stop, Breathe, and Think.”

Binky sniffed. He liked plans. He was a determined rabbit. “Okay. Stop, Breathe, and Think.”

Mia held up her hand. “Step one: Stop. Put your paws still.”

Binky put his paws on his belly. He felt it move up and down.

“Step two: Breathe,” said Mia. “Smell the flower.” She breathed in, slow. “Blow the bubbles.” She breathed out, slow.

Binky copied her. In through his nose—sniff, sniff. Out through his mouth—whooo. He imagined a purple flower. He imagined shiny bubbles floating up. Pop, pop.

The room felt quieter. The thump-thump sounded gentle again. The swish-swish sounded like a lullaby.

“Step three: Think,” Mia said. “What can we do next?”

Binky blinked. “We can look one more time. We can ask for help.”

“That’s a smart idea,” said Mia.

Binky picked up the socks he had dropped. He smoothed the shirt that fell. His paws felt calm now. “Mia, can you help me check the dryer lint screen? Socks like to hide there.”

“Great thinking,” said Mia. She pulled out the lint screen. It smelled warm and dusty. They looked. No sock.

Binky’s nose twitched. He peered inside the dryer drum. It was shiny and round. He reached in and felt the smooth, cool metal. “Hello?” he whispered to the sock. “Are you in there?”

They heard a tiny rustle. “What’s that?” Mia asked.

Binky’s eyes widened. “Listen!” He put his ear to the dryer. Rustle, rustle. Then he saw it—just a bit of red peeking from the dryer door seal.

“I found you!” Binky giggled. He gently pulled the little red sock free. It was warm and a little damp. He hugged it to his cheek. “You were hiding!”

Mia cheered. “You did it, Binky! You used your calm-down plan. You stopped. You breathed. You thought. And then you found a smart way to look.”

Binky’s chest felt light. He matched the red socks together and patted them smooth. “I feel proud,” he said. “My mad got smaller, and my brain got bigger.”

Mia laughed kindly. “Your brain is always big, Binky. But calm helps it work.”

They folded towels. They folded shirts. Binky made a rainbow stack—red, orange, yellow, green, blue. The towels were fluffy and smelled sweet. The basket looked neat and cozy.

Soon, the laundry was done. Binky did a happy hop. “Today I learned a new trick,” he said. “Stop, Breathe, and Think.”

Mia gave him a high-five. “That is a friendship trick, too. When friends get mad, we can help. We can use calm words and take breaths together.”

Binky nodded. “I can teach my friends,” he said. “If they feel mad, I will say, ‘Let’s smell the flower and blow the bubbles.’”

They both practiced again. In—sniff. Out—whooo. The bubbles in their minds floated up and popped, pop, pop. They giggled.

Binky picked up the matched red socks. “Thank you for waiting,” he told them. “Thank you for being patient,” Mia told Binky.

Binky’s ears stood tall. “I am a determined rabbit,” he said. “I can work hard. I can also be calm.”

They put the clean clothes away. The laundry room hummed softly, like a happy song. The day felt warm and bright.

Binky waved goodbye. “Next time, if I feel mad, I know what to do.”

Mia waved back. “Me too. Stop, Breathe, and Think.”

Binky hopped off, light as a bubble.

The End.
