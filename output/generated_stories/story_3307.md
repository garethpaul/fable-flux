# Benny Beaver Breathes and Builds

In a busy basement full of boxes and tools, a busy beaver named Benny was building a block bridge. The basement smelled like warm wood and fresh laundry. A soft hum came from the washing machine. Little dust sparkles danced in the light.

“I will make the best bridge,” Benny said. His tail thumped gently on the smooth floor. Tap, tap. He lined up red blocks, then blue, then yellow. He listened to the click-clack of blocks touching. It sounded happy.

Benny’s friend, a tiny clock on the shelf, ticked softly. Tick-tock. Tick-tock.

“Nice and steady,” Benny whispered. “I can do this.”

Then, the dryer buzzed. Bzzz! The sound startled Benny. His paw bumped the tower. The top wobbled. The blocks tumbled down. Clatter! Clack! Crash!

Benny’s cheeks got hot. His tail thumped hard. Thump! Thump! His chest felt tight like a knot. His eyebrows scrunched. His paws curled into fists.

“I worked so hard!” Benny shouted. “It’s not fair!”

He wanted to kick the blocks. He wanted to roar like a big bear. His body felt like a storm.

Just then, Mama Beaver peeked down the stairs. “I heard a crash,” she said softly. “Are you okay, Benny?”

“No,” Benny huffed. “My bridge fell. I feel mad. I feel hot, hot, hot.”

Mama nodded. “Your feelings are big,” she said. “Let’s help your body feel safe. Try Belly Breathing.”

Benny frowned. “What is Belly Breathing?”

“It is a calm-down tool,” Mama said. “Put your paws on your belly. Breathe in slow through your nose. Feel your belly fill up like a balloon. One… two… three. Then blow out slowly like you are blowing bubbles. One… two… three.”

Benny put his paws on his belly. He sniffed in. He felt his belly rise. He blew out and made a soft “whoooo” sound. He did it again. In… out. In… out. The basement felt a little quieter. The knot in his chest loosened.

Mama smiled. “Good job. Your breath is your friend.”

Benny looked at the messy blocks. He still felt sad, but not so hot. “I’m calmer,” he said. “What can I do now?”

“You can use your calm-down plan,” Mama said. “First, Pause. Then, Name your feeling. Then, Pick a tool.”

Benny nodded. “Pause.” He touched his heart.

“Name it,” Mama said.

“I feel angry and sad,” Benny said. “I feel disappointed.”

“Pick a tool,” Mama said.

Benny looked around the busy basement. He heard the soft hum. He smelled the clean laundry. He felt the cool floor on his feet. “I will do Five Senses,” he said. “I see yellow paint. I hear tick-tock. I smell soap. I feel smooth blocks. I taste mint from my toothpaste.” He giggled. “Now I feel steady.”

Mama clapped softly. “Mindful Benny,” she said. “You are ready to try again.”

Benny wiped his eyes. He took three more belly breaths. In… out. In… out. In… out.

“Can I fix my bridge?” he asked.

“Yes,” Mama said. “And you can make a plan. Plans help when things go wrong.”

Benny thought. “Plan: I will build wide and low first. I will use a strong base. I will keep my tail still. I can count to three before I place each block.”

He began. He pressed two big blocks side by side. “One… two… three.” Click. He added another row. “One… two… three.” Click. He listened to the happy sound. His tail stayed quiet. Tap, tap? No. Stillness.

The dryer buzzed again. Bzzz!

Benny felt a pinch in his tummy. He paused. He put a paw on his belly. “Breathe,” he whispered. In… out. He looked at the blocks. They didn’t fall.

“I did it!” Benny cheered. He placed a long flat block on top. The bridge looked strong and shiny. The colors made a rainbow. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue. The light in the basement made the blocks glow.

Mama clapped louder. “How does your body feel now?” she asked.

Benny smiled. “My cheeks feel cool. My heart feels soft. My tail wants to do a happy thump.” He thumped once, gently. Thump.

“What did you learn?” Mama asked.

Benny stood tall. “When I feel mad, I can Pause. I can Name my feeling. I can Belly Breathe. I can use my Five Senses. Then I can try again with a plan.”

Mama nodded. “That is anger-management,” she said. “You are practicing self-regulation and mindfulness.”

Benny grinned. “I like those big words,” he said. “They help my big feelings.”

He added tiny toy cars to the bridge. “Beep-beep,” he said in a little voice. The cars drove across, smooth and safe. The clock went tick-tock. The dryer went quiet. The basement felt warm and calm.

Benny tucked one last block into place. He whispered, “Thank you, breath.” He patted his belly. He looked at Mama. “Next time I get mad,” he said, “I know what to do.”

Mama hugged him. “That’s the best build of all,” she said. “You built calm inside you.”

Benny beamed. He took one more slow breath. In… out. He picked up a fallen block and put it away. He liked the neat sound it made. Click.

The busy basement was still busy, but Benny felt peaceful. He could hear the quiet between the sounds. He could feel the strong bridge under his paws and the strong calm inside his heart.

The End.
