# Rilo’s Quiet Song

Rilo was a musical raccoon with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved music. He loved the gentle thump of the drum, the jingle of the tiny bells, and the sweet hum of the strings. Today, Rilo walked into the musical practice room. The room was warm and cozy. Soft lights glowed. Shiny instruments rested on velvet shelves. The air smelled like clean wood and a little bit like lemon polish.

Rilo took a deep breath. “Ahhh,” he sighed. “Today, I will play a calm song.” His whiskers twitched with a smile.

His friends came in to practice too. Tully the turtle wore a tiny blue scarf. Pip the sparrow fluttered to a perch and shook her feathers. Mira the mouse carried a small drum that went tap-tap when she touched it.

“Hi, Rilo!” said Pip. “What are we playing?”

Rilo tapped his chest. “My grandma taught me a special raccoon song,” he said. “It is a tradition in my family. We sing it when we want to be calm and kind.”

Tully blinked slowly. “I like calm,” he said.

Rilo nodded. “First, we sit. We breathe in and out. We listen.”

They all sat in a circle on soft pillows. Rilo closed his eyes. He breathed in through his nose. In. Out. In. Out. The room felt quiet and safe. Pip listened to the warm hum of the lights. Tully felt the soft pillow under his shell. Mira smelled the lemon polish and smiled.

Rilo opened his eyes. “Now I will tell the story of the song,” he said. “In my family, when the moon is big and bright, we share stories. We share music too. We sing a calm song to help our hearts slow down. We remember that friends and family make us strong.”

Pip tilted her head. “Can we learn it?”

“Yes,” said Rilo. He picked up a small string instrument. It sounded like honey when he plucked it. Pling, pling, pling.

“First, it is very soft,” Rilo whispered. “Like a bedtime whisper.”

He began to play. Pling… pling… pling. The notes floated like feathers. Mira tapped her drum, very gentle. Tap… tap… tap. Tully shook a tiny bell once. Jingle. Pip hummed a low sound. Mmmmmm.

It felt calm. It felt slow. Rilo smiled. “Good,” he said.

Just then, the door opened. A group of older raccoons peeked in. One wore a bright scarf. She waved. “Hello, little players,” she said. “I am Auntie Roo. I knew Rilo’s grandma. I know that song.”

Rilo’s eyes shone. “Auntie Roo! Please share the story you know.”

Auntie Roo came in and sat with them. “Long ago, our family sang this song under tall trees,” she said. “We sang to share peace. We sang to tell our stories. We sang to be calm together.”

Pip leaned closer. “How do we sing it right?”

Auntie Roo smiled. “There is no wrong,” she said. “But there is a way to feel it. Start with your breath. Put your hand on your heart.”

Rilo put his hand on his heart. His friends did too. They could feel a soft thump, thump. Auntie Roo hummed a gentle tune. “When we sing, we make room for quiet,” she said. “When we listen, we make room for friends.”

Rilo played the strings again. Pling… pling… pling. The sound curled around them like a blanket. Mira’s drum was a soft heartbeat. Tap… tap… tap. Tully’s bell made a tiny star sound. Jingle. Pip hummed like a warm cup of tea. Mmmmmm.

Outside, a bird chirped loudly. Cheep! Cheep! Pip jumped. “Oh!” she said. “I lost the calm.”

Rilo nodded kindly. “That is okay,” he said. “When noise surprises us, we can come back to calm. Let’s try. Breathe in. Breathe out. Listen.”

They did. In. Out. Their shoulders relaxed. Their smiles returned.

Auntie Roo nodded. “Calm is a friend you can invite back,” she said. “You can share it, like a story, like a song.”

Rilo looked at his friends. “Let’s play the calm song for each other,” he said. “We will keep the tradition. We will share it with our friends.”

They began again, even softer this time. Pling… pling. Tap. Jingle. Mmm. The practice room felt like a peaceful forest. They could almost see moonlight on leaves. They could almost hear their own smiles.

When the song ended, the room was quiet. It was a happy quiet. A full quiet. Pip whispered, “I feel safe.”

Tully said, “I feel slow and steady.”

Mira said, “I feel warm.”

Rilo said, “I feel calm.”

Auntie Roo clapped softly. “You learned the song,” she said. “You learned the story too. Now you can share it with new friends, and they can share it again. That is how traditions grow.”

Rilo packed his instrument. He looked at his friends. “Thank you for playing with me,” he said. “Thank you for listening.”

Pip chirped softly. “Thank you for teaching us.”

Tully smiled. “We can use the song when we need calm.”

Mira nodded. “At home. At school. Anytime.”

Rilo took one more deep breath. He felt proud and peaceful. He felt the love of his friends and his family tradition. He knew the calm song would help them all.

Today’s adventure taught them a simple lesson: when we breathe, listen, and share gentle traditions with friends, calm comes back, and our hearts feel good.

The End.
