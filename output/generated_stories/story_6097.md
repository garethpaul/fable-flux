# Breezy Rides and Recycled Art

The gentle deer fawn sat buckled in the family car, hooves tucked in snug. The seat felt soft and warm. Sunshine made tiny sparkles on the window. The car smelled like pine trees and a little like crayons.

“Ready, Little Fawn?” asked Mama Deer from the front. “We’re going to the park. We’ll pick up recycling on the way.”

“Yes!” said the fawn. “I want to help the Earth.”

The car hummed, vrrrrr, vrrrrr. The fawn wiggled happy hooves. “My body wants to move!” they giggled. “Can we do car wiggles?”

“Great idea,” said Mama Deer. “Let’s do seat-stretches. Reach your hooves up, then down.” The fawn reached up, up, up, and then down, pat-pat on the knees. They tapped their hooves to the rhythm of the road. Tap-tap, tap-tap. The air felt cool on their nose.

They stopped by Mr. Bear’s porch. A small blue bin waited there. “Hello, Mr. Bear!” called the fawn through the open window.

“Hello, Little Fawn,” rumbled Mr. Bear. “I have clean jars and paper for recycling.” He lifted the bin. The glass clinked: tinkle-tink! The paper rustled: shhhh.

“Thank you,” said the fawn, eyes wide. “We will take them to the big recycling place.”

“Good helpers move their bodies and their hearts,” said Mr. Bear with a smile.

Back in the car, the fawn looked at the jars. “They shine like tiny moons,” they whispered. “What happens to them, Mama?”

“Recycling gives old things new jobs,” said Mama Deer. “Glass can be melted and made into new jars. Paper can be turned into new paper.”

The fawn nodded. “Old things, new jobs. That is kind.”

They drove a little more. The car made a low, steady song. “Let’s do shoulder wiggles,” said Mama. The fawn wiggled shoulders and giggled. “Now take a deep breath.” They breathed in slow. The car smelled like lemon cleaner. They breathed out slow. “Ahhh.”

At the next stop, Ms. Rabbit waved with long ears. “I saved cardboard tubes,” she said. “We used the paper, not the tubes.”

“Thank you!” said the fawn. The cardboard made a soft thunk when it went into the bin. The fawn held one tube to their eye and peeked. “I see a circle world!” They laughed.

“That tube could be art,” said Mama Deer. “We can make something.”

“Art from recycling!” the fawn sang. “Yes, yes!”

At the park, the trees whistled in the breeze. The family car found a shady spot. The fawn unbuckled and stretched tall. “Move, move, move!” they chirped. They hopped out and felt the warm sun on their back and the cool grass under their hooves. The birds sang, tweet-tweet-tee!

Near the picnic table, the fawn saw a plastic bottle on the ground. “Oh no,” they said softly. “This is not where it belongs.”

“What can we do?” asked Mama Deer.

The fawn took a breath. “We can sort it. Plastic goes in the blue bin.” They picked up the bottle. It crinkled in their hooves. Crinkle-crinkle. They dropped it into the bin. Plonk.

“Good choice,” said Mama. “When we move and help, the Earth feels better.”

The fawn saw a paper bag, too. They picked it up and smoothed it. “Paper can be recycled,” they said. They put it in the bin and clapped. Clap, clap!

“Let’s make art now,” Mama Deer said. She opened a small box. Inside were crayons, tape, and glue. The fawn’s eyes sparkled. “We can make a park friend,” they said.

They used a jar for a body, a cardboard tube for a neck, and paper for wings. The fawn colored the wings with bright blue and sunny yellow. Scritch-scratch went the crayon. The waxy smell floated up. They taped on a smile. Stick, stick.

“What will we call our friend?” asked Mama.

“Recy-bird!” said the fawn, bouncing on their hooves. “Recy-bird likes clean parks.”

They held Recy-bird up to feel the breeze. The jar felt cool and smooth. The paper wings fluttered, flup-flup. The fawn danced in a circle, stepping lightly. Step, step, twirl! “Move with me, Recy-bird!”

Other animals at the park smiled. “What a kind helper,” said Squirrel. “And what lovely art.”

“Thank you,” the fawn said, cheeks warm with pride. “We used old things and gave them new jobs.”

When it was time to go, the fawn gently placed Recy-bird on the picnic table with a little sign: “Please recycle and make art!” Mama Deer took the full blue bin to the big recycling container. It made a friendly echo when the bottles went in: tonk, tink, tonk.

Back in the family car, the fawn buckled up and yawned. The car was cozy and safe. The road hummed again. Vrrrrr. The fawn watched the trees slide by like slow waves.

“Mama,” they said sleepily, “today we moved our bodies, made art, and helped the Earth.”

“Yes,” said Mama Deer. “You learned that recycling is easy, kind, and fun. We sort things, we keep parks clean, and we can make new art from old items.”

The fawn smiled and closed their eyes. “Old things, new jobs,” they whispered. “I can do that every day.”

“Yes,” Mama said softly. “Every little helper makes a big difference.”

The car hummed. The breeze whispered. The fawn dreamed of blue bins, fluttering wings, and happy, clean parks.

The End.
