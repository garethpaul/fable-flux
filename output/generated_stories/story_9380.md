# Pebble Talks on the Porch

Pip was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a shiny black-and-white belly. He liked to waddle, wiggle, and whistle little tunes. Pip lived at a cozy house with a peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like fresh wood and warm tea. The wind made the chimes sing, ting-ting, ting-a-ling. Pip loved to sit there in the morning sun and feel it warm his feathers.

One sunny day, Pip had a big plan. He wanted to build a tiny stage on the porch. He would stand on it and lead a happy song club. He had even packed a little backpack with his plans: a paper drawing, a string to measure, and a red pencil. He was prepared.

Pip waddled onto the porch with a smile. “Good morning, porch!” he chirped. “I am ready!”

Just then, two friends arrived. Buzz the bee buzzed by with a gentle bzz-bzz. Nib the bunny hopped up the steps, thump, thump. They liked the porch too.

Buzz said, “Bzz! I need the porch to dry my honey jars in the sun.”

Nib said, “I need the porch to practice quiet hops. My baby sister is sleeping inside.”

Pip blinked. His plan stopped in his head. Three friends, one porch, three different needs. The porch felt smaller now. The chimes went ting-ting, like a tiny question.

Pip felt a little twist in his tummy. He wanted to say, “But my stage!” He wanted to stomp his feet. Instead, he took a brave breath. He remembered: Be calm. Be kind. Use words.

Pip set his backpack down. “Friends,” he said softly, “we each have a plan. Maybe we can talk and find a way. We can share.” That was courage. Talking kindly can be brave.

Buzz buzzed in a small circle. “Bzz. I need warm sun for one hour.”

Nib twitched her nose. “I need quiet space to hop and count.”

Pip nodded. “I need a little place for a stage to lead songs.” He opened his paper. “I made a plan.” He showed them the drawing and used the string to measure a small corner of the porch. “This space is tiny,” he said. “Maybe the stage can be here.”

They all looked and listened. The porch boards felt smooth under Pip’s feet. He could hear the rustle of leaves and a far-away dog bark.

Nib touched the porch with a soft paw. “I can hop on the other side,” she said. “It is quieter there.”

Buzz landed on the porch railing. “Bzz. My jars can sit near the sunniest edge.”

Pip smiled. “What if we make a schedule?” He drew three boxes on his paper. “First, Buzz can dry honey for one hour. Then, Nib can hop for one hour. After that, I will sing on my tiny stage.”

Buzz buzzed. “Bzz, I like the order. But I must watch my jars. Could the stage wait where I can still see them?”

Pip thought. He tapped his red pencil. “I can place the stage near the steps, so you can see your jars. Is that okay?”

Nib raised a paw. “What about quiet? The baby is sleeping. Can the songs be soft?”

Pip nodded fast. “Yes! Soft songs first. Loud songs later.”

Buzz smiled. “Bzz! That sounds fair.”

Nib smiled too. “I feel good about this plan.”

They all looked at the porch again. Pip measured the stage space with his string. “One, two, three steps,” he counted. He drew a tiny X for the stage feet. He checked his backpack. He had tape to mark the space, a small cloth to make the stage soft, and a tiny bell to start the club. Pip had prepared well.

“Thank you for talking with me,” Pip said. “We made a plan together.”

Buzz set out golden jars on the sunny edge. The warm sun made the honey smell sweet, like flowers. Nib practiced quiet hops on the far side—soft, soft, soft—like feathers landing. Pip gently taped a little square for his stage and placed the cloth on top. He rang his tiny bell—ding!

After an hour, Buzz buzzed, “My jars are dry! Thank you.” He carried them away, humming a happy bzz.

Nib took her turn. “Hop, hop, hop,” she whispered. Pip watched and counted with her. “One, two, three, four!” Nib finished with a grin. “I did it!”

Then it was Pip’s turn. He stepped onto his tiny stage. The cloth felt cozy under his feet. He looked at his friends. He felt proud, not just of the stage, but of the plan they made together.

“Welcome to the song club,” Pip said. “Today we sing soft songs first.” Pip sang a gentle tune about the porch and the sun and being kind. Buzz hummed along. Nib tapped her paw to the beat. The chimes went ting-ting, like they were singing too.

When the song ended, Pip said, “We did it! We shared, we waited, and we listened. We used our words. That is called negotiation. It means we talk, we take turns, and we find a way that is fair.”

Buzz buzzed, “Bzz! Negotiation is sweet, like honey.”

Nib nodded. “It feels calm, like quiet hops.”

Pip puffed his chest with happy pride. “And it takes courage to speak up kindly, and preparation to bring a plan.”

They all cheered. The porch felt big again, full of sun, songs, and friends. Pip knew this: when we talk and listen, we can solve problems. We can be leaders by being fair and brave.

The End.
