# Rocco’s Bubble Beat

Rocco was a musical raccoon with soft gray stripes and bright, curious eyes. He loved making music everywhere he went. Today, he padded into the bathroom with his little drum, a wooden spoon, and a shiny toothbrush.

“Time to brush and time to play!” Rocco sang. The bathroom smelled like mint and soap. Warm light shone on the tiles. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet, keeping a tiny beat.

Rocco stood on a stool. “Brush, brush, brush,” he hummed, tapping the spoon on the tub. Tink, tink, tink! The tub sang back. The mirror was foggy from a warm bath, and Rocco drew a smiley face with his paw. He giggled.

But then—splonk!—his drum slid off the sink and bumped his paw. The drum rolled, bumped into the trash can, and the lid clattered. “Oh no!” Rocco cried. “Stop rolling, drum!” He reached, but his toothbrush slipped and fell into the sink with a plop.

Rocco’s cheeks felt hot. His tail puffed up. “This is not going the way I want!” he said, voice tight. He thumped the spoon hard on the tub. CLANG!

From the hallway, Mama Raccoon called, “I hear a big clang. Are you okay?”

Rocco huffed. “I’m mad! Everything is messy. My drum fell. My toothbrush fell. I can’t make my song!” His ears drooped. His tummy felt twisty.

Mama stepped into the bathroom. She smelled like vanilla and wore a soft blue sweater. She knelt beside Rocco. “Thank you for telling me,” she said gently. “Your feelings are big. Let’s listen to them.”

Rocco sniffled. “I wanted my Bubble Beat. Now it’s all wrong.”

Mama nodded. “It’s okay to feel frustrated. We can help your body calm down. Want to try the Bubble Breath?”

Rocco blinked. “Bubble Breath?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Breathe in slow like you smell a flower. Then blow out like you’re making a big bubble.”

Rocco lifted his nose. He sniffed in: sniiiiiff. He blew out: whooooo. Mama did it with him. They did it again. In… and out. The drip of the faucet sounded softer now. Drip. Drip. Drip.

“My tummy feels better,” Rocco said quietly.

“Good listening to your body,” Mama smiled. “Now, what can we do first?”

Rocco looked around. The drum was under the towel. The toothbrush was in the sink. The floor had tiny water splashes that felt cool on his toes. He took another small breath. “I can pick up my drum,” he said. He set it on the floor this time, where it was steady. “And I can rinse my toothbrush.”

“Great plan,” said Mama. “Would you like help?”

“Yes, please.” They turned on the faucet. Rocco liked the sound: shhhhh like a gentle rain. He rinsed the toothbrush. Minty bubbles tickled his nose. He giggled. “It smells like a snowflake,” he said.

Mama chuckled. “Let’s finish brushing, and then you can make your Bubble Beat.”

Rocco brushed his teeth. “Brush, brush, brush,” he sang softly. The bristles felt bumpy but nice. He swished and spit. “All clean!”

He looked at the tub, the faucet, and the mirror. “I can make music with listening ears,” he said. He tapped the spoon lightly on the tub. Tink. He flicked the towel ring. Ding. He patted the drum, soft then loud. Dum… DUM!

Rocco closed his eyes and used his imagination. The bathroom became a bubble stage. Clear bubbles floated like tiny moons. The faucet was a friendly drummer. The mirror held a crowd of bubbly faces smiling back.

“Bubble Band, are you ready?” Rocco said.

“Ready!” he answered in a funny deep voice for the faucet. He laughed at himself.

He began a new song. “When I feel mad, I stop and breathe. I listen to my body. I find what I need.” Tap, ding, dum. “I slow it down. I make a plan. I try again, and now I can!”

Mama clapped softly. “Lovely, Rocco. I like your calm song.”

Rocco’s ears perked. “Mama, I’m sorry I banged the tub so loud. That was not kind to our ears.”

“Thank you for making amends,” Mama said. “That was thoughtful. What could you do next time?”

Rocco held his spoon like a conductor’s stick. “Next time, I can use Bubble Breath. I can ask for help. And I can tap gentle.”

“Those are smart choices,” Mama said. “Your listening helped you.”

Rocco played a quiet ending: tink, ding, dum… shhhh. He bowed to the mirror crowd. The bubbles popped with tiny pffts that sounded like kisses.

He cleaned up with Mama. They wiped the little splashes with a fluffy towel. The towel felt warm and soft. The bathroom looked neat again.

Rocco smiled. His tail unpuffed. “I did it,” he said. “I turned mad into music.”

Mama hugged him. “You did. Feelings come and go. You can handle them.”

Rocco took one more Bubble Breath. He looked at his drum, his spoon, and his shiny clean teeth in the mirror. “Bubble Band,” he whispered, “thank you for listening.”

From the faucet came a friendly drip… drip… drip. Rocco tapped once more, very softly. “Good night, bathroom.”

The End.
