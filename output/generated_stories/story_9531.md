# Benny Beaver's Musical Day

Benny Beaver loved music more than anything! His big orange teeth gleamed as he smiled, thinking about all the wonderful sounds he could make. Today was special - the forest animals were having a concert tomorrow, and Benny wanted to play his wooden drums for everyone.

"Tap, tap, TAP!" went Benny's tail as he hurried into the cozy musical practice room. The room smelled like fresh pine wood and had soft, squishy cushions everywhere. Sunlight streamed through the windows, making the musical instruments sparkle.

"Oh my!" said Benny, looking around with wide eyes. "There are so many things I want to try!" 

He saw shiny bells that went "jingle-jangle," a big piano with black and white keys, colorful maracas that went "shake-shake-shake," and his favorite wooden drums. Benny's heart felt excited and happy, but also a little worried.

"I want to practice everything!" he said to himself. "But the concert is tomorrow. How will I have enough time?"

Benny started by grabbing the maracas. "Shake-shake-shake!" They sounded like rain on leaves. Then he ran to the piano. "Plink-plonk-plink!" The keys felt smooth under his little paws. Next, he jingled the bells. "Ring-a-ling-ling!" They sounded like tiny wind chimes.

But when Benny sat down at his drums, he felt confused. His paws didn't know what to do! He had spent so much time with the other instruments that he forgot to practice what he really needed.

"Oh no!" Benny's ears drooped down. "I'm all mixed up! What if I can't play well for my forest friends tomorrow?"

Just then, Mrs. Owl flew into the practice room. Her feathers were soft gray and white, and her big eyes looked kind and wise.

"Hello, Benny," she hooted gently. "You look worried. What's troubling you?"

"Mrs. Owl," said Benny, his voice small and sad, "I want to play drums for the concert, but I kept trying all the other instruments instead. Now I don't know if I'll be ready to help make beautiful music for everyone!"

Mrs. Owl nodded her head thoughtfully. "Ah, I see. Benny, it's wonderful that you love all kinds of music! But sometimes, we need to make a plan and focus on one important thing at a time."

"A plan?" asked Benny, tilting his furry head.

"Yes!" said Mrs. Owl. "Tomorrow, your forest friends are counting on you to play drums. That's your special job - your responsibility to the community. How about we make a practice schedule?"

Mrs. Owl helped Benny think. "First, let's practice drums for the concert. Then, if there's extra time, you can explore other instruments for fun!"

Benny's tail started tapping again - but this time with purpose! "Boom-boom-TAP! Boom-boom-TAP!" His paws found the perfect rhythm on the drums. The sound was deep and warm, like a heartbeat.

"I did it!" Benny cheered. "I made a plan, and now I feel ready to help my friends have the best concert ever!"

Mrs. Owl smiled. "You learned something very important today, Benny. When we manage our time well and focus on our responsibilities first, we can still have fun AND help others too!"

The next day, Benny played his drums beautifully at the concert. All the forest animals danced and sang along. After the show, Benny had time to try other instruments with his friends, and it felt even more special because he had done his important job first.

The End.
