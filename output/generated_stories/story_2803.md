# Shelly's Fair Share Day

Shelly the turtle peeked her small green head into the big pantry. The shelves smelled like sweet cookies and salty crackers. Her shell felt heavy as she slowly walked across the cool tile floor.

Every morning, Shelly had the same job. She helped sort the snacks for all the forest friends. But today felt different. Today felt unfair.

"Oh no," Shelly whispered, her voice quiet and worried. 

On the bottom shelf sat three boxes. One tiny box had only two crackers. One medium box had five crackers. But the biggest box was overflowing with twenty crackers!

Shelly knew the three boxes were for Rabbit, Squirrel, and Bear. But which friend got which box? That didn't seem right at all.

"This isn't fair," Shelly said softly. Her little claws tapped nervously on the floor. "Everyone should get the same amount."

Shelly felt scared to speak up. She was always too shy to say when things seemed wrong. But her tummy felt twisty thinking about one friend getting so much more than the others.

She took a deep breath. The pantry smelled like cinnamon and made her feel a little braver.

"I need to think," Shelly said to herself. "What would make this fair?"

Shelly looked at all the crackers carefully. She counted slowly with her tiny claws. "Two plus five plus twenty equals... twenty-seven crackers total!"

Her eyes lit up. "If I divide twenty-seven by three friends, that's nine crackers each!"

Shelly felt proud of her math. But now came the hard part. She had to move all those crackers by herself. Her legs were short and her shell was heavy.

"I can do this," Shelly whispered. "I'll take my time."

First, she carefully took crackers from the big box. One by one, she carried them across the pantry. Her shell bumped gently against the shelves, making soft clicking sounds.

After many trips, Shelly had moved eleven crackers from the big box to the small box. Now it had thirteen crackers - too many!

"Oops," she giggled. "I need to take four crackers out."

Shelly moved four crackers to the medium box. Now it had nine crackers - perfect!

Then she took four more crackers from the big box to make nine there too.

"Nine, nine, and nine!" Shelly said happily. "Now everyone gets the same fair share!"

Just then, Mama Owl flew into the pantry. "Shelly! What wonderful work you've done!"

Shelly's cheeks turned pink under her green skin. "I... I noticed the boxes weren't fair. So I fixed them."

"You showed great courage speaking up for what's right," Mama Owl said warmly. "And you used your brain to solve the problem all by yourself!"

When Rabbit, Squirrel, and Bear came for their snacks, they were all so happy.

"Thank you, Shelly!" said Rabbit. "Nine crackers is perfect!"

"You made sure we all got treated fairly," said Squirrel.

"You're very thoughtful," said Bear with a big smile.

Shelly's shell felt lighter now. Her heart felt warm and proud. She learned that even shy turtles can stand up for what's right. And when she used her mind and worked step by step, she could solve big problems.

From that day on, Shelly always made sure everyone got their fair share. And she wasn't quite so shy about speaking up for justice.

The End.
