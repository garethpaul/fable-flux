# Nutkin's Big Apology

In a cozy, quiet attic filled with dusty boxes and golden sunbeams, lived a little squirrel named Nutkin. The attic smelled like old books and warm wood, and soft light danced through the small round window.

Nutkin loved his peaceful home, but he had made a big mistake. Earlier that morning, he had taken some shiny acorns from his friend Chippy's special collection without asking. The acorns clinked together in his tiny paws as he hid them behind an old trunk.

"These are so pretty," Nutkin had whispered to himself. "Chippy has so many, he won't miss just a few."

But now, sitting alone in the quiet attic, Nutkin's tummy felt all twisty and uncomfortable. His fluffy tail drooped, and his bright eyes looked sad. He knew in his heart that taking Chippy's acorns was wrong.

"I don't feel good," Nutkin said out loud, his small voice echoing in the dusty space. "My tummy hurts, and I feel yucky inside."

Nutkin's grandmother had always told him that when you do something wrong, your body tells you by making you feel unwell. She called it "feeling guilty," and she said the only way to feel better was to make things right.

The little squirrel sat very still, thinking hard about what to do. He could hear birds chirping outside and the gentle creaking of the old attic beams. The warm sunlight felt nice on his fur, but inside, he still felt terrible.

"I need to give the acorns back," Nutkin decided, his voice getting stronger. "And I need to say sorry to Chippy."

Making this decision felt scary, but also a little bit better. Nutkin scampered over to the trunk and carefully gathered all of Chippy's acorns. They felt heavy in his small paws, not because they weighed a lot, but because he knew how important they were to his friend.

Nutkin climbed down from the attic and ran through the soft grass to Chippy's tree house. His heart was beating fast, like a tiny drum in his chest.

"Chippy?" Nutkin called softly. "Are you home?"

Chippy poked his furry head out of his tree house. His whiskers twitched with surprise when he saw Nutkin holding the missing acorns.

"Nutkin! You found my special acorns!" Chippy exclaimed.

"No," Nutkin said, his voice quiet but brave. "I took them this morning without asking. I'm very, very sorry, Chippy. I know it was wrong, and I feel really bad about it."

Nutkin held out the acorns with trembling paws. "Here they are, all of them. I promise I'll never take anything without asking again."

Chippy's eyes grew wide, then soft and kind. "Thank you for being honest, Nutkin. It takes courage to say sorry."

"I felt sick in my tummy when I had them," Nutkin explained. "Grandma says that happens when we do wrong things."

"Your grandma is very wise," Chippy smiled. "And you made a very good choice to return them and apologize."

As soon as Nutkin said sorry and gave back the acorns, the yucky feeling in his tummy disappeared. He felt light and happy again, like a warm, cozy blanket was wrapped around his heart.

"Would you like to play together?" Chippy asked, his tail swishing happily.

"Yes, please!" Nutkin chirped, and both friends scampered off to play in the sunshine.

From that day on, Nutkin always remembered that saying sorry and making things right was the best way to feel good inside and keep his friendships strong.

The End.
