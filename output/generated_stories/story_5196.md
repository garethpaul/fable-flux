# Owl Hoots and Cool-Down Boots

In a cozy home office at the top of a tall tree, a wise old owl named Olive sat at a wooden desk. The desk felt smooth under her feathers, and a small lamp glowed warm and golden. Click-clack went the keyboard. Hummm went the little fan. On the walls were colorful drawings from friends: a fox, a squirrel, a duck, a lizard, and a rabbit. Olive loved her work helping friends solve problems.

One windy morning, Olive heard fast thumps on the stair-branch. Thump, thump, thump! The door squeaked open. In tumbled five friends, all talking at once.

“I am mad!” squeaked Suri the squirrel, her tail puffed big like a dandelion.

“I am mad too!” quacked Dax the duck, flapping wet wings that smelled like fresh rain.

“My feet are hot with mad,” said Riko the rabbit, stomping quietly but fast.

“My scales feel prickly,” hissed Lina the lizard, blinking bright eyes.

“I am a fox, and I feel like a fire,” said Finn the fox, tail swishing.

Olive blinked her round eyes and hooted softly. “Hoo-hoo. Sounds like stormy feelings. Come in. Come in.”

They crowded into the home office. The floor rug felt bouncy. The air smelled like paper and cinnamon tea. Each friend looked different and sounded different, but all had the same tight, scrunchy face.

“What happened?” asked Olive.

“We were making a dance for the park party,” said Suri. “But no one listened! I wanted to spin.”

“I wanted to splash-step!” said Dax, dripping tiny drops on the floor.

“I like hop-hops,” said Riko.

“I have slow slides,” said Lina.

“I like quick zips,” said Finn.

“And then,” said Suri, “we all shouted at the same time. I felt hot and loud inside.”

Olive nodded. “Hoo-hoo. Big feelings are okay. But big shouting can hurt. Let’s do a cool-down first.”

“How?” asked Finn. His ears twitched.

Olive stood and picked up a soft, blue mat. It felt cool like shade. “We use my Cool-Down Boots,” she said, pointing to two blue footprints on the mat. “Stand here. Breathe here. Count here.”

Suri stepped on the footprints. “They are squishy,” she said.

“Good,” said Olive. “Now: Smell the flower.” Olive lifted a paper flower from her desk. It smelled sweet like honey. “In through your nose.” They all breathed in. “Now blow the candle.” Olive pointed to a tiny pretend candle. “Out slow.” They all breathed out. Whooooo.

“Again,” said Olive. “Smell the flower. Blow the candle.” In. Out. In. Out. The room got quiet, like snow.

Riko’s feet stopped stomping. Lina’s scales settled. Dax smoothed his feathers. Finn’s tail slowed its swish. Suri’s tail un-fluffed.

“How do you feel?” asked Olive.

“Cooler,” said Lina.

“Like a soft puddle,” said Dax.

“Like a calm drum,” said Riko, patting his chest. Thump-thump, slow.

“Now we can talk,” said Olive. “One at a time. We listen with our ears, eyes, and hearts.”

Suri spoke first. “I wanted to spin, but no one said yes. I felt left out.”

“I wanted to splash-step,” said Dax. “But I splashed on Riko. He got wet.”

“I got mad,” said Riko. “I did not like it.”

“I like slow slides,” said Lina. “But the music was fast.”

“I like quick zips,” said Finn. “But slow felt boring.”

Olive hooted. “Thank you for sharing. You are all different. That is wonderful. Different moves make a beautiful dance. But first we need a plan.”

“What plan?” asked Finn.

“A cool-down plan for when you feel hot inside,” said Olive. “And a dance plan so everyone is heard.” She held up five colorful sticky notes. “Step 1: Pause. Step 2: Breathe. Step 3: Say your feeling. Step 4: Listen. Step 5: Try a fair turn.”

She stuck the notes on the wall. The colors looked like little flags.

“Let’s try,” said Olive. She turned on soft music. It sounded like wind and bells. “Suri, you start with two spins. Then point to a friend. That friend does two moves. We take turns. We cheer for each other.”

Suri felt brave. She spun once, twice. The lamp light sparkled on her fur.

“I pick Dax,” she said.

Dax did two splash-steps with gentle feet so water drops did not hit anyone. Drip, drip, drip. He smiled. “I pick Riko.”

Riko hopped, hop-hop, like a soft drum. “I pick Lina.”

Lina slid slowly, smooth as a leaf. “I pick Finn.”

Finn zipped quick-zip, quick-zip, with careful paws. “I pick Suri.”

They clapped and laughed. The room felt bright and warm.

Then the music got faster. Suri’s tail twitched. “I feel my mad coming back,” she said.

Olive nodded. “What is Step 1?”

“Pause,” said Suri. She froze like a statue.

“Step 2?” asked Olive.

“Breathe,” said everyone.

They smelled the flower. They blew the candle. In. Out. In. Out.

“Step 3?” asked Olive.

“Say your feeling,” said Suri. “I feel mad and excited.”

“Step 4?” asked Olive.

“Listen,” said Dax. “I can slow my splash.”

“I can hop to the side,” said Riko.

“I can slide between,” said Lina.

“I can zip around,” said Finn.

“Step 5?” asked Olive.

“Try a fair turn,” said Suri. “I will do one spin now, and one spin later.”

They danced again. The moves fit together like puzzle pieces. Spin. Splash. Hop. Slide. Zip. The lamp light danced too, and the little fan hummed a happy tune.

When the song ended, they cheered and hugged. They smelled like rain, leaves, and warm fur.

“Thank you, Olive,” said Finn. “You helped us cool our fire.”

“You helped us listen,” said Lina.

“You helped us be leaders together,” said Riko.

Olive smiled. “You helped each other. That is good leadership. Remember: Feelings are okay. Anger can visit. We can stay safe and kind.”

Suri nodded. “When I feel hot inside, I will pause, breathe, say my feeling, listen, and take turns.”

Dax flapped softly. “I will splash with care.”

“Different is good,” said Lina.

“Helping is strong,” said Riko.

“And we can always dance,” said Finn.

Olive poured cinnamon tea for everyone. They sipped. Mmm. Warm and sweet.

Outside, the wind blew soft. Inside, the friends felt calm. The sticky notes fluttered like tiny flags of peace. The home office glowed golden with quiet joy.

The End.
