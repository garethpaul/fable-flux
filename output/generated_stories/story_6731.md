# Rosie Rabbit Learns to Stay Calm

Rosie Rabbit loved helping in the dining room. She had soft brown fur and long ears that wiggled when she was excited. Today, Mama Rabbit asked her to help set the table for dinner.

"I can do it all by myself!" said Rosie, hopping up and down. Her little cotton tail bounced as she jumped.

Mama Rabbit smiled. "Remember, Rosie, take your time and stay calm. That's how we do our best work."

But Rosie was too excited to listen carefully. She grabbed the shiny plates and started rushing around the big wooden dining table. The plates felt heavy in her small paws.

"One, two, three!" she counted quickly, placing plates down with loud CLANKS. The sound echoed in the cozy dining room.

Then disaster struck! Rosie tried to carry too many forks at once. They slipped from her paws and scattered across the floor with a loud CLATTER!

"Oh no!" cried Rosie. Her ears drooped down, and her whiskers trembled. She felt her heart beating fast like a drum. "I messed up everything!"

Mama Rabbit came over and sat down beside Rosie on the soft carpet. "It's okay, little one. Let's take a deep breath together."

"But I wanted to help!" Rosie sniffled.

"You ARE helping," said Mama gently. "But when we feel rushed or worried, our bodies get all wiggly. Let me show you something special."

Mama Rabbit placed her paw on her chest. "Feel how my breathing is slow and steady? When we breathe slowly, our minds become calm and clear."

Rosie put her small paw on her own chest. "I can feel my heart!"

"That's right! Now let's breathe together. In slowly through your nose... and out slowly through your mouth." Mama demonstrated with a soft "whoosh" sound.

Rosie tried it. "Whoosh... whoosh..." The air felt cool going in and warm going out. Her shoulders relaxed, and her ears stopped trembling.

"Good! Now, let's pick up these forks together, one at a time," said Mama.

This time, Rosie moved slowly and carefully. She picked up each fork and looked at it closely. They were shiny silver and felt smooth in her paws. She placed each one gently beside a plate.

"Look, Mama! When I go slow, I can see how pretty the forks are!"

"Yes! When we're calm, we notice wonderful things we might miss when we rush," said Mama Rabbit.

Together, they finished setting the table. Rosie carefully placed the napkins, folding each one just so. She arranged the cups, making sure each handle pointed the same way.

"The table looks beautiful!" said Papa Rabbit when he came into the dining room. "What lovely, careful work!"

Rosie's ears perked up with pride. "I learned to stay calm, Papa! When I breathe slowly and move slowly, I can do things really well!"

At dinner, Rosie sat quietly and enjoyed her carrot soup. She could smell the sweet carrots and taste the warm, creamy broth. Everything seemed more delicious when she wasn't rushing.

"Thank you for teaching me about staying calm, Mama," said Rosie. "Tomorrow, can I help set the table again?"

"Of course, dear one. And remember, whenever you feel rushed or worried, just take those slow, deep breaths."

Rosie nodded, her whiskers twitching happily. She had learned that being calm helped her do her best work and notice all the wonderful things around her.

The End.
