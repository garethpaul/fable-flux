# Joey Roo and the Cozy Closet Club

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a soft, bouncy tail. He loved to hop, hug, and giggle. His favorite place was the closet hideaway—a small, warm closet with twinkly string lights, soft blankets, and a tiny tea set. It smelled like clean shirts and a little bit like lavender soap. The floor felt fuzzy under his toes. It was the perfect cozy spot.

“Today,” Joey Roo whispered, peeking inside, “I will make a Cozy Closet Club!” He took a big, brave breath. “I will invite my friends.”

He hopped to find his friends: Pip the possum, who liked to dangle; Kiki the kookaburra, who liked to laugh; and Benji the bandicoot, who liked to build. Joey Roo’s heart pitter-pattered with excitement.

“Come to my closet hideaway!” Joey said. “We can play shop, and read, and sip pretend tea.”

“Yay!” said Pip, swinging her tail.

“Ha-ha-HA!” laughed Kiki. “I’ll bring my tiny bell.”

“I’ll bring blocks,” said Benji. “We can build a tower.”

They all hurried to the closet. Joey Roo switched on the twinkly lights. They made tiny stars on the walls. He spread a blanket that felt like a cloud. The friends sat crisscross and smiled.

“I have an idea,” Joey said. “We can take turns. First, tea shop. Then, blocks. Then, story time.”

“I want blocks first!” Benji blurted, holding a handful of colorful pieces.

“I want to ring my bell now!” Kiki squawked, flapping her wings.

“I want to dangle from the hanger,” Pip whispered, eyes shiny.

Joey Roo’s tummy tightened. The closet felt smaller. The air got hot on his cheeks. He held the tiny tea cups close. He wanted to shout, “My closet! My plan!” His tail twitched. His heart thumped.

Joey Roo remembered something Mama Roo said: “When you feel big mad, pause and take a slow breath. Use words that help.”

Joey closed his eyes. He took one slow breath in. He let one slow breath out. He patted his chest. “I feel frustrated,” he said softly. “I wanted to play tea shop first.”

His friends got quiet. The twinkly lights hummed. The closet smelled like lavender and a little like crayons now.

Kiki tilted her head. “I feel wiggly,” she said. “I want to ring the bell.”

Benji nodded. “I feel excited. My hands want to build.”

Pip dangled, swinging gently. “I feel shy, but I want a turn too.”

Joey Roo looked at his friends. He had an idea. He showed courage and took initiative. “What if we make a plan together?” he asked. “We can use a Turn Token!”

“What’s that?” asked Benji.

Joey picked up a soft, round button from the blanket. It was shiny and blue. “This is the Turn Token. The one who holds it gets a turn. We can line up turns and count to five.”

Kiki’s eyes sparkled. “I like that game!”

Pip clapped her tiny paws. “Me too.”

Joey smiled. “Let’s negotiate. We can make a plan that is fair. We can listen and choose together.”

He set the Turn Token in the middle. “Who wants to speak first?” he asked.

Kiki raised a wing. “I would like one bell ring first. Then I can wait.”

Benji nodded. “I can build after I hear the bell one time.”

Pip whispered, “I would like to dangle while we listen.”

Joey Roo thought. He tapped his chin. “How about this: Kiki rings the bell one time. Then we have five sips at the tea shop. Then Benji builds the tower while we all add one block. Then Pip dangles, and we count her swings—one, two, three, four, five. Then we read a story.”

Kiki tilted her head. “Can I ring the bell at the start of each new turn? Just once?”

Joey Roo smiled. “That sounds fair.”

Benji grinned. “And can I be the block helper who hands out blocks?”

“Yes,” said Joey. “And I can pour the tea. Pip can be the swing counter!”

Pip’s eyes brightened. “I can count. One, two, three, four, five.”

They all agreed. Joey Roo gave the Turn Token to Kiki. Kiki rang the bell—ding! The sound was bright and tiny. Joey poured pretend tea. The cups clinked softly. “Sip, sip,” they said, giggling. The warm blanket tickled their knees.

Then Kiki rang the bell—ding! Benji held the Turn Token and started the tower. “One block for you,” he said to Joey. “One block for Kiki. One block for Pip.” The blocks clicked and made a little tap-tap sound. The tower grew tall.

Kiki rang the bell—ding! Pip dangled from the hanger. “Count with me,” she said. “One, two, three, four, five!” Everyone cheered. Pip’s cheeks turned pink with happy.

Kiki rang the bell—ding! Joey Roo picked a story book. The pages were smooth. The pictures were bright. He read in a cozy whisper. “Once upon a time…” Everyone listened and snuggled close.

Soon, the closet felt big again. The air felt cool and gentle. The twinkly lights made them all look like stars.

“I liked our plan,” Joey said. “We used the Turn Token. We took turns. We used words. No yelling.”

“I liked that we listened,” said Benji.

“I liked the bell,” Kiki laughed softly.

“I liked counting,” Pip whispered, proud.

Joey Roo’s tail thumped happily. “When we feel big feelings, we can breathe. We can say how we feel. We can negotiate and make a plan. Taking turns helps everyone have fun.”

They all nodded. The Cozy Closet Club was a success.

“Friends,” Joey said, holding up the shiny button, “who wants the Turn Token for cleanup?”

“I do!” said Benji. “I’ll start, and we can all help.”

Ding! went the bell, just once, as they worked together, smiling.

The End.
