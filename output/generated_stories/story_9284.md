# Panda Poppy’s Honest Choice

Poppy was a patient panda who loved her cozy home office. The room was bright and calm. Sunlight warmed the bamboo desk. A tiny fan hummed. Plants with shiny leaves stood by the window, and a small recycling bin waited by the door. Poppy’s big chair was soft, and her computer made gentle beeps.

One morning, Poppy clapped her paws and smiled. “Today I will finish our team report,” she said. “We are making a plan to save paper!” She took a deep breath. She loved the smell of her peppermint tea. She listened to the fan, whirr, whirr, whirr, and felt ready.

Her friends pinged on the screen. “Good morning!” chirped Tiko the tiny turtle. “Slow and steady,” he said.

“Hey there!” buzzed Bea the busy bee. “I brought ideas,” she hummed.

“Hi!” sang Lark the little bird. “I can double-check words!”

Poppy nodded. “Thank you, team. Our plan is to print less, share more, and plant trees. We will use both sides of paper and make a paper swap box. Let’s work together!”

They all got to work. Tiko counted pages. Bea gathered good tips. Lark read the words out loud. Poppy typed, click-clack, click-clack.

Suddenly, Poppy’s computer pinged again. A new message popped up. It was from a big company, Quick Print Co. The message said, “Use more paper! We will pay you to say it is best.” There was a shiny gold sticker on the screen and a happy face. It said, “Easy win!”

Poppy’s ears felt hot. Her tummy did a slow flip. She looked at the plants by the window. Their green leaves rustled softly. She looked at her recycling bin and at her careful notes about saving trees.

Tiko blinked. “What is it?” he asked.

Bea buzzed closer. “Trouble?” she hummed.

Lark tilted her head. “Tell us,” she chirped.

Poppy took another deep breath. “Friends,” she said, “we got a message. They want us to say ‘Use more paper,’ but that is not true. Our plan is to save paper. This message offers money.”

The room felt very quiet. The fan hummed. Whirr, whirr, whirr.

Tiko shook his head slowly. “Integrity means doing what is right, even when it is hard,” he said softly.

Bea frowned. “Honey is sweet, but lies leave a sticky mess,” she buzzed. “We need to be honest.”

Lark fluffed her feathers. “Our words can help the world. Let us keep them true,” she sang.

Poppy nodded. Her heart slowed down. “Yes,” she said. “Integrity is telling the truth and standing by it. We must make a good choice.”

Poppy looked at the message again. “We will say no,” she decided. “But we will be polite. We will explain our plan.” She typed with calm paws: “Thank you for your offer. We care about trees, truth, and teamwork. Our plan is to use less paper. We cannot say the opposite. We wish you well.” Click. Send.

Poppy felt light, like a leaf on the wind. Tiko smiled. “Good choice,” he said.

Bea buzzed in a happy circle. “Honest and kind,” she hummed.

Lark sang, “Tweet, tweet! Integrity!”

They went back to work. Poppy read the plan out loud. “Step one: Use both sides of paper.” Tiko nodded. “Step two: Share digital notes instead of printing.” Bea buzzed, “Zing! Green and clean!” “Step three: Plant a tree for each big project,” Lark sang.

Poppy added one more step. “Step four: Speak up with honesty. We tell the truth, even when a shiny sticker says ‘Easy win.’”

They checked each step. They smiled at each other. The home office felt warm and brave. The plants looked extra green. The fan hummed a steady song.

At lunchtime, Poppy poured cool water. She handed cups to her friends. The water splashed and tinkled. “To teamwork,” she said. “To integrity,” said Tiko. “To sustainability,” buzzed Bea. “To good choices,” sang Lark.

Ding! Another message came in. It was from the school down the street. “We loved your plan,” it said. “Can you share it with our students?” Poppy’s eyes shone. “Yes!” she said. “We will teach and learn together.”

In the afternoon, they made a simple poster. Poppy drew a big tree with strong roots that spelled I-N-T-E-G-R-I-T-Y. Tiko colored the trunk brown. Bea dotted green leaves. Lark added a tiny nest. They laughed when Bea got a green dot on her nose. “It tickles!” she buzzed. They giggled and wiggled and kept coloring.

Before sunset, Poppy sent the report. She felt calm and proud. The room smelled like crayons and peppermint tea. The window glowed orange and pink. The day had been long, but good.

Poppy turned to her friends. “What did we learn today?” she asked.

Tiko raised his tiny foot. “Integrity is doing what is right, even when no one is watching,” he said.

Bea nodded. “Teamwork helps us be brave and kind,” she buzzed.

Lark sang softly, “Sustainability means caring for our Earth, a little bit each day.”

Poppy smiled. “Yes,” she said. “We think, we share, we tell the truth. That is a good choice.”

They packed up. Poppy watered the plants. Drip, drip. She turned off the fan. The room grew quiet. Her heart felt peaceful.

“See you tomorrow,” said Tiko.

“Buzz you later,” said Bea.

“Good night,” sang Lark.

Poppy waved her paw. “Thank you, team,” she said. “I am proud of us.”

And the little home office felt safe and bright, full of honest work and gentle joy.

The End.
