# Lila Leads the Study Song

In a cozy study corner, sunlight made warm squares on the carpet. Crayons sat in a cup. Books lined a little shelf. A soft clock went tick-tock, tick-tock. In the window, a tiny bird perched on the sill. Her name was Lila, the singing bird.

Lila liked to sing bright songs while children colored and read. Her feathers were blue like the morning sky, and her voice sounded like tea cups clinking gently. “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo,” sang Lila. “Let’s learn something new!”

One day, the study corner felt a little mixed-up. The crayons rolled under the table. The books made a tall, wobbly tower. The children frowned. “I can’t find my green crayon,” said Ana.

“My book keeps closing,” said Ben, puffing his cheeks.

The clock went tick-tock, tick-tock, and Lila listened. She tilted her head. She could hear the crayon cup rattling. She could see the books leaning. She could feel a flutter in her chest. “This study corner needs a helper,” she whispered. “This study corner needs a leader.”

Lila took a deep breath. She remembered that a leader listens first. So she hopped closer and asked in her gentle voice, “What do we need?”

Ana said, “I need my green crayon.”

Ben said, “I need a way to keep my book open.”

Maya pointed to the wobbly tower. “That looks scary.”

Lila nodded. “Thank you for telling me.” She fluffed her feathers and listened to the quiet room. She heard the window breeze. She smelled the lemony clean table. She felt the soft carpet under her tiny feet. “We can fix this together,” she said. “We will use calm voices. We will try new ideas. We will help one another.”

She sang a small, steady song, “Listen first, then we try. Step by step, we reach the sky.” The children smiled and hummed along.

“First,” Lila said, “let’s find the crayons.” She hopped under the table and chirped, “I see a green peek!” Ana got on her knees and reached. “Got it!” Ana laughed, holding up the green crayon. It smelled like fresh grass.

“Next,” said Lila, “let’s help the books.” She fluttered to the shelf and tapped with her beak. “Heavy books on the bottom. Light books on top.” Ben clapped. “That makes sense!”

“But my book still closes,” Ben said.

Lila blinked and thought. Leaders look for new ideas, she remembered. “I have an idea,” she said. “An innovation!” She picked up a clean, smooth stone from the windowsill. It was warm from the sun. “We can make a book buddy,” she chirped. “This stone can hold your page.”

Ben placed the stone on his book. “It works!” he cheered. “It stays open!”

Maya looked at the crayons on the table. “They still roll,” she said.

Lila listened again. She looked carefully. She saw a shallow box that held note cards. The box was empty. “Another new idea,” Lila sang. “Let’s make a crayon bed.”

The children lined the crayons in the little box. The crayons lay side by side like rainbow sleepers. “They don’t roll away!” Maya said. She giggled and touched the smooth red one. It smelled like cherries.

Soon, the study corner looked neat and calm. The books were steady. The crayons had a bed. Ben’s book had a sun-warm stone buddy. The air felt peaceful, like a quiet hug.

Then a small problem came. Two children reached for the same blue crayon.

“It’s mine,” said Ana.

“I want blue,” said Ben.

Lila fluttered between them and took a patient breath. Leaders choose peaceful solutions, she thought. “Let’s listen first,” she said softly. “Ana, tell us how you feel.”

“I feel sad,” said Ana. “I need blue for the sky.”

“Ben?” asked Lila.

“I feel excited,” Ben said. “I want blue for the river.”

Lila nodded. “Thank you for sharing.” She tapped the crayon bed. “We can take turns. We can also share the color by mixing.” She pointed to a purple crayon. “Blue plus red can make a sunset sky. Blue plus green can make a deep river.”

Ana thought. Ben thought. They looked at each other.

“We can take turns,” Ana said.

“I can start with green,” Ben said. “Then I’ll use blue.”

Lila smiled and sang, “When we listen, we find the way. We help each other every day.” The room filled with soft humming. The clock went tick-tock, tick-tock. The crayons made whispery scritches on paper. Pages turned with a gentle swoosh. The sunny stone warmed Ben’s book. The study corner felt happy and bright.

At the end, the children showed their pictures. Ana’s sky was blue with a tiny purple sunset. Ben’s river was green and blue with a shiny stone bridge. Maya drew the crayon bed. She wrote, in big letters, “Leader Lila.”

Lila’s heart puffed with warm pride. “We all were leaders today,” she said. “We listened. We tried new ideas. We used calm words. We helped our study corner get better.”

The children nodded. “When we lead,” said Ana, “we make things work.”

“When we listen,” said Ben, “we find answers.”

“When we share,” said Maya, “everyone smiles.”

Lila sang a soft good-bye song. “Little steps, light and true. Lead with kindness in all you do.”

The study corner glowed in the afternoon light. The books stood tall. The crayons rested in their bed. The children felt brave and calm, ready to learn more tomorrow.

Lesson: A good leader listens, uses peaceful solutions, and tries new ideas to help everyone improve.

The End.
