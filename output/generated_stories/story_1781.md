# Nutkin's Healthy Helper Adventure

Nutkin the squirrel peeked into the guest room where his friend Emma was staying. Emma was visiting for the whole week! But something didn't look right. Emma sat on the bed looking very tired.

"Good morning, Emma!" Nutkin chirped cheerfully. "Want to play outside?"

Emma rubbed her sleepy eyes. "I don't feel very good, Nutkin. My tummy hurts and I'm so tired."

Nutkin's bushy tail twitched with worry. He noticed empty candy wrappers on the nightstand. Emma's water cup was still full from yesterday. The healthy snacks Nutkin's mama had left were untouched.

"Oh no!" thought Nutkin. "Emma needs help feeling better!"

Nutkin scampered to his mama in the kitchen. "Mama, Emma doesn't feel well. Can you help me help her?"

Mama Squirrel smiled warmly. "What a caring friend you are, Nutkin! Let's think about what makes our bodies feel strong and happy."

"Water!" Nutkin squeaked excitedly. "And healthy food! And moving our bodies!"

"Exactly right," Mama said. "Why don't you show Emma how good it feels to take care of ourselves?"

Nutkin gathered fresh water in a pretty cup. He picked the juiciest berries and crunchiest nuts. Then he hurried back to the guest room.

"Emma, I brought you something special!" Nutkin said gently. "This water will help your tummy feel better. And these berries taste like sunshine!"

Emma took a small sip of water. "Mmm, that does feel nice," she said softly.

Nutkin munched on a berry too. "See how sweet and juicy they are? My mama says good food gives us energy to play!"

Emma tried a berry. Her face brightened. "It's delicious! Much better than all that candy I ate yesterday."

"Want to do some stretches with me?" Nutkin asked. "Sometimes when I feel yucky, moving helps!"

Nutkin showed Emma how to reach their arms up high like tree branches. They twisted their bodies left and right like leaves in the wind. They marched in place like happy woodland creatures.

"I'm starting to feel better!" Emma giggled as they marched around the guest room.

"That's because you're taking good care of your body!" Nutkin explained. "Water helps us stay strong. Healthy food gives us energy. Moving makes us feel happy!"

Emma drank more water and ate another berry. "Nutkin, thank you for being so kind. You didn't have to help me, but you did anyway."

Nutkin's heart felt warm and fuzzy. "That's what friends do! We take care of each other."

For the rest of the week, Emma and Nutkin started each morning with fresh water and healthy snacks. They did stretches together and played active games outside. Emma felt stronger and happier every day.

On Emma's last day, she hugged Nutkin tight. "You taught me something very important. Taking care of my body makes me feel so much better! I'm going to keep drinking water and eating healthy food when I go home."

Nutkin beamed with pride. "And I learned that helping friends feels wonderful too!"

From that day on, whenever Nutkin had guests in the guest room, he always made sure they had everything they needed to feel healthy and happy. Because taking care of ourselves and others is one of the best gifts we can give.

The End.
