# Poppy the Patient Panda Helps

Poppy was a patient panda. Poppy lived in a sunny backyard with soft green grass and bright yellow flowers. The sun felt warm like a hug. The breeze smelled like oranges from the little tree by the fence. Birds sang, “Tweet-tweet!” and a tiny bee hummed, “Bzzz.”

“Good morning,” Poppy said to the sun. “Today I will be kind.”

Poppy liked to move and stretch. “Wellness means taking care of me,” Poppy said. She took a deep breath. In…and out. Her tummy went up and down. She stretched her arms high. She bent her knees and touched her toes. “I can do it by myself,” she smiled. “That is self-reliance.”

Then Poppy heard a rustle. “Who is there?” she asked.

A small snail peeked out from under a leaf. “Hello,” said the snail in a tiny voice. “I am Swoosh. I want to cross the yard to see the flowers, but the ground is dry. My shell gets hot.”

Poppy knelt down. The grass tickled her knees. “I can help,” Poppy said kindly. She looked around. “I have my water cup.”

Poppy picked up her little blue cup. She filled it at the spout by the fence. Drip, drop, drip. The water was cool on her paws.

“Would you like a little water road?” Poppy asked.

“Yes, please,” said Swoosh.

Poppy poured a thin, shiny line of water on the ground. It sparkled in the sun. “Take your time,” Poppy said. “I will wait.”

Swoosh slid along the water road. “Thank you for being kind,” Swoosh said. “You are patient.”

Poppy nodded. “Kindness means helping gently,” she said. “I feel happy when I help.”

Next, Poppy heard a squeak. A little wagon with a wobbly wheel bumped along the path. A kitten pulled the wagon with both paws. The wagon was filled with small books. The books had bright covers with pictures of kids, animals, and places around the world.

“Hi,” said Poppy. “I am Poppy.”

“I am Kiki,” said the kitten. “I want to share my books. I am taking them to the sunny spot. But my wheel is wiggly.”

Poppy sat beside the wagon. She touched the wheel. It squeaked. “We can fix it,” she said. “Let’s try together.”

Kiki looked worried. “I am little,” she said.

“You can try,” said Poppy. “We will learn.”

Poppy found a smooth stick by the orange tree. She used it to nudge the wheel back on the little round peg. Kiki held the wagon steady. Poppy turned the stick. Click! The wheel sat snug and still.

“You did it!” Kiki said.

“We did it together,” Poppy smiled. “Helping is kindness. Fixing things is self-reliance. Now the books can go.”

Kiki opened a book and showed Poppy a page with children in many places. “Look,” Kiki said. “Kids wave hello all around the world.”

Poppy waved. “Hello!” she said to the pictures. “We are global citizens. We share. We care.”

They heard a soft sob. Under the orange tree, a little bird sat with a bent feather. “Oh,” said the bird. “I am Pip. I fell. My wing feels funny.”

Poppy moved slowly. “May I sit near you?” she asked.

“Yes,” Pip sniffled.

Poppy took a deep breath with Pip. “In…and out,” she said. “Breathing helps our feelings.”

Kiki brought a leaf and set it like a tiny mat. Poppy dipped her paw in cool water and tapped the bent feather very softly. “Gentle touch,” Poppy said. “Kind and calm.”

Pip shook his wing. The sun warmed his feathers. “It feels better,” Pip chirped. “Thank you.”

“You are brave,” Poppy said. “Let’s rest in the shade.”

They all sat together. The grass felt cool. The orange blossoms smelled sweet. The bee hummed a quiet song. Poppy shared orange slices. “Wellness is eating good food,” she said. “Would you like some?”

“Yes, please,” said Pip and Kiki and Swoosh.

They chewed slowly. The juice was bright and tangy. “Mmm,” said Kiki.

Swoosh blinked. “I learned that a little help and patience go a long way,” he said.

Pip chirped, “I learned to breathe when I feel scared.”

Kiki purred, “I learned I can try to fix things, and friends can help.”

Poppy smiled. “I learned that kindness can be big or small. Kindness is a smile. Kindness is sharing. Kindness is waiting. Kindness is gentle hands.”

The sun began to slide across the sky. The backyard glowed golden. Poppy stood up and stretched. “Today was a good day,” she said. “We cared for ourselves, and we cared for each other. We learned and helped. That makes the world better.”

Kiki waved her paw. “I will share my books with more friends,” she said.

Pip flapped his wings. “I will sing a thank-you song,” he chirped.

Swoosh inched along. “I will visit the flowers and say hello to the bees.”

Poppy patted the grass. “Wherever we are, we can be kind,” she said. “We are all neighbors in one big world.”

They laughed softly. The breeze whispered, “Whoosh.” The orange tree rustled. The birds sang a happy tune. Poppy felt calm and proud.

“Good night, sunny backyard,” Poppy said. “Thank you for a day of kindness.”

The End.
