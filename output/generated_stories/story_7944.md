# Melody on the Porch

A kind mouse named Miri lived in a cozy house with a bright blue door. Every morning, Miri liked to sit on the peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like warm wood and fresh flowers. The sun felt soft on her fur, and the breeze tickled her whiskers.

One sunny day, Miri held her tiny tin whistle. She loved to make music. “Toot, toot!” she played a happy tune. The notes floated like bubbles. Birds chirped back. “Tweet-tweet!” A ladybug landed on her toe and seemed to dance.

Miri smiled. Playing music made her heart feel light. But today she had a little problem. Her whiskers drooped. She whispered, “I want to practice my new song. But I also need to rest my paws. They feel tired. What should I do?”

Miri looked at her list on a small leaf paper:
- Practice music
- Drink water
- Stretch paws
- Help a friend

“I can’t do everything at once,” she said. Her tummy made a tiny rumble. “And I might need a snack, too.”

Just then, her neighbor, Tully the turtle, peeked around the porch step. “Hello, Miri,” said Tully in a slow, warm voice. “Your music sounds lovely.”

“Thanks, Tully,” said Miri. “I want to play my big song now, but my paws are tired. I can’t decide.”

Tully blinked gently. “When I have a choice,” he said, “I stop. I breathe. I listen to my body. Then I pick one thing. One small step.”

Miri wiggled her nose. “Stop. Breathe. Listen. Pick one,” she repeated.

She set down her whistle and closed her eyes. She took a slow breath in. She let it out like a soft hiss. “Sssss.” The breeze felt cool on her face. She listened. She heard a bee buzz by. She heard leaves clap. She felt her paws. They felt a bit sore.

“I choose to take care of me first,” Miri said. “That’s my one small step.” She smiled. “Self-care helps my music.”

Miri sipped cool water. It tasted like sweet rain. She ate a crunchy nut and a juicy grape. “Crunch! Pop!” She giggled. Then she stretched her paws. “Reach up, reach out, wiggle-wiggle, shake-shake,” she sang softly, making a tiny stretch song.

Her shoulders loosened. Her paws felt warm and comfy. “Ahh,” she sighed. “That feels better.”

Tully nodded. “Good choice,” he said. “Now what is your next small step?”

Miri looked at her whistle. “I still want to practice,” she said. “But I don’t want to get tired again.”

She had an idea. “I will play in little parts. I will rest between parts. I will listen to my body.”

Miri set a tiny pebble timer. “When the pebble is warm from the sun, I rest,” she said.

She lifted her whistle and played the first part. “Toot-toot, tooooot!” The sound was bright like sunshine. Her ears tingled with joy. Her paws stayed soft and loose. When the pebble felt warm, she stopped. She shook her paws and took a breath. She sipped water. She smiled.

“Part two,” she said. “Toot, toot, teeeet!” A butterfly fluttered by in pink and gold. Its wings whispered, “Whirr.” Miri giggled when the butterfly paused as if clapping.

Again, she rested. She stretched her neck. She hummed. “Hmmmm.” Tully tapped his shell like a drum. “Tum, tum.”

“Now the last part,” said Miri. “Toot-tee-toot! Toot!” The notes danced across the porch. Even the flowers seemed to sway.

She finished and put the whistle in her lap. “I did it,” she said. Her eyes shone. “I practiced with care. I made a plan. I made music!”

Tully smiled. “Your song is kind and bright,” he said. “And you were kind to yourself, too.”

Just then, Pip the sparrow landed on the porch rail. “Cheep! I liked your music,” Pip said. “How did you make it so smooth?”

Miri patted the seat. “I had a problem, Pip,” she said. “I wanted to play, but I was tired. I stopped. I breathed. I listened to my body. I picked one small step. First I rested. Then I practiced in parts. Rest, play, rest, play.”

Pip chirped, “Cheep-cheep, that is a smart plan.”

Miri nodded. “Music is joyful. Music is sharing. Music grows when we take care of ourselves. When we make good choices, our notes feel happy.”

The sun slid lower. Shadows grew long and soft on the porch. Miri played her full song one more time, gentle and sweet. Tully tapped his drum-shell. Pip sang along. The porch glowed with sound and smiles.

Miri put her whistle away and wrapped her paws around a warm cup of tea. The steam smelled like mint. She felt calm and proud.

She said, “Today I learned: I can solve a problem by making a plan. I can choose self-care. I can make music with joy. Stop. Breathe. Listen. Pick one. Then play.”

The breeze whispered, “Yes,” and the porch felt even more peaceful.

The End.
