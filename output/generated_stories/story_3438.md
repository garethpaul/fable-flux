# Rocco’s Rest-and-Run Rhythm

Rocco was a musical raccoon with a shiny silver drum. He lived near a sunny backyard that smelled like warm grass and sweet flowers. The birds chirped, the breeze whispered, and Rocco’s drum went tap-tap-tap.

“Good morning, sunshine!” Rocco said, squinting his bright eyes. “Let’s make happy music!”

Rocco loved to play and dance. He played fast beats with his drumsticks. Tap-a-tap! Boom-boom! His striped tail swished with the rhythm.

In the yard, friends arrived to listen and move. Mia, a small girl with curly hair, rolled her wheelchair over the soft path. “Hi, Rocco,” she said with a smile. “I’m ready to dance with my hands.”

“I’m ready to hop,” said Kiko the frog, stretching his long legs.

“I’m ready to flap,” said Bella the bluebird, fluffing her feathers.

Rocco grinned. “Let’s warm up!” he said. “Wiggle fingers. Wiggle toes. Big breaths in… and out.” They all breathed in. They breathed out. The air felt clean and cool in their noses.

Rocco began a gentle beat. “We move our bodies with care,” he said. “We listen to our bodies, too.” Tap… tap… tap…

They started their exercise. Kiko hopped in a soft circle. Bella fluttered from fence to fence. Mia rolled her chair in smooth figure eights, pushing the wheels with steady hands and lifting her arms to the drum’s rhythm. Rocco marched in place, knees up, shoulders loose. The sun warmed their faces. The grass tickled their feet. Laughter bubbled like a little stream.

Rocco got excited. He played faster. Tap-a-tap-a-tap! Boom-boom-BOOM!

“Wait,” Mia said, slowing her chair. “My arms feel tired.”

Kiko rubbed his leg. “My hops are getting wobbly.”

Bella panted, tiny chest rising and falling. “Too fast for me!”

Rocco’s heart thumped. He wanted everyone to have fun. He hit the drum even faster to cheer them up. Tap-RA-TA-TA! BOOM!

Suddenly, Rocco’s paws felt heavy. His shoulders drooped. His tummy made a rumbly sound. His head felt fuzzy, like a dandelion puff.

“I feel funny,” Rocco whispered. The beat wobbled. The drumstick slipped from his paw and landed with a soft plop in the grass.

Mia rolled closer. “Rocco, are you okay?”

Rocco blinked. “I think I forgot to take care of me. I didn’t drink water. I didn’t rest. I was too busy playing loud and fast.”

Bella tucked a wing around his arm. “It’s okay to pause.”

Kiko nodded. “Good helpers listen to their bodies.”

Rocco took a slow breath. In… and out. He felt the cool air again. He looked at his friends’ kind faces. “I think I need a plan,” he said. “A self-care plan!”

“What is self-care?” Mia asked.

“Self-care means being kind to yourself,” Rocco said, tapping his chest gently. “It means water, rest, healthy snacks, and moving in ways that feel good. It means listening to our bodies and our feelings.”

“That sounds smart,” Bella chirped.

Rocco smiled. “Let’s do it together. Different friends, different needs. That’s okay! We can all take care in our own ways.”

They made a little circle in the sunny backyard. The grass felt cool under Rocco’s paws. The leaves made soft shhhh sounds. A sweet strawberry smell floated from the garden.

Step one: water. Mia passed around bottles. Rocco sipped. The water felt cold and happy in his mouth. “Ahhh,” he said, tail relaxing.

Step two: snack. Kiko shared crunchy carrot sticks. Crunch, crunch! Rocco munched slowly. His tummy stopped rumbling and felt calm.

Step three: stretch. Bella led gentle moves. “Wings wide,” she sang. “Arms wide,” Mia echoed, lifting her arms. Kiko reached up high. Rocco stretched his back and shoulders. The stretch felt like sunshine in his muscles.

Step four: rest break. They sat under a shady tree. Rocco closed his eyes for a moment. He listened to the breeze, the birds, and a faraway lawnmower hum. His breath went in and out like tiny waves.

After a little while, Rocco opened his eyes. “I feel better,” he said. “Thank you for waiting.”

Mia smiled. “Self-care helps us play more.”

Rocco picked up his drumstick. “Let’s try a new rhythm,” he said. “A rest-and-run rhythm! We play. We pause. We sip. We smile.”

He tapped a comfy beat: tap-tap… pause. Boom… pause. Tap-tap… boom… pause.

Everyone moved in their own way. Mia rolled and clapped to the slow beat, strong and steady. Kiko hopped, then stopped to shake out his legs. Bella flapped twice and perched to breathe. Rocco marched, then paused to drink a sip of water. They all called out, “Play and pause! Care and cause!” and giggled at their silly rhyme.

The sun felt warm, not too hot. The grass smelled green. Their bodies felt good and safe.

After the song, Rocco said, “Let’s check in. How do we feel?”

“Happy,” said Mia. “My arms feel strong.”

“Springy,” said Kiko. “My legs feel ready.”

“Light,” said Bella. “My chest feels calm.”

Rocco touched his drum. “I feel proud and peaceful,” he said. “Today I learned that self-care is part of the music. Rest and water help the rhythm of our bodies.”

He looked at his friends. They were all different, and they all belonged. They moved in different ways, and they cared in different ways. But they made one big, kind team.

Rocco played a soft ending beat. Tap… tap… tap. “We listen. We care. We move with love.”

Everyone cheered.

And from that sunny day on, Rocco always kept a little self-care plan in his drum: a bottle of water, a healthy snack, and a tiny note that said, “Pause, breathe, and be kind to you.”

The End.
