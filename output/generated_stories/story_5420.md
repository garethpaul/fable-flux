# Benny Beaver Says Sorry

In a sunny backyard filled with green grass and colorful flowers, lived a busy beaver named Benny. Benny loved to build things with his strong teeth and tiny paws. The warm sunshine made his brown fur feel cozy as he worked all day long.

"Chop, chop, chop!" went Benny's teeth as he cut down small sticks near the garden. He was building the biggest dam he had ever made! But Benny was so excited about his project that he didn't notice what was happening around him.

"Oh no!" cried Luna the ladybug from her favorite daisy. "Benny, you cut down the stick that held up my flower!"

Benny looked up and saw Luna's beautiful white daisy lying flat on the ground. The pretty yellow center looked sad, and the white petals were getting dirty in the soil.

"I need those sticks for my dam," Benny said quickly, and went back to his chopping. "Chop, chop, chop!"

Next, Carlos the caterpillar called out, "Benny! You took the branch where I keep my cozy leaf blanket!" His soft green blanket was now on the ground, covered in dirt.

But Benny was too busy to listen. "My dam is almost finished!" he said, dragging more sticks to his pile.

Then Ruby the robin flew down, flapping her red wings. "Benny, you cut down the twig where I built my nest for my babies! Now they have nowhere safe to sleep!"

Benny stopped chopping. He looked around the sunny backyard and saw what he had done. Luna's daisy was wilted. Carlos had no blanket. Ruby's baby birds were chirping sadly with no nest.

Benny's tummy felt heavy and sad. His heart felt like it was sinking down, down, down.

"Oh no," Benny whispered. "I was so busy thinking about MY dam that I forgot about my friends." He set down his stick and walked slowly to each friend.

First, he went to Luna. "Luna, I'm very sorry I knocked down your daisy. I should have asked before taking the stick. Can you forgive me?"

Luna's tiny voice was sweet. "Thank you for saying sorry, Benny. That makes me feel better."

Then Benny walked to Carlos. "Carlos, I'm sorry I took your blanket branch. I should have been more careful. I'm sorry."

Carlos smiled with his many little legs. "I forgive you, Benny. Saying sorry shows you care."

Finally, Benny looked up at Ruby. "Ruby, I'm really, really sorry about your nest. Your babies need a safe home. I was wrong."

Ruby's red chest puffed up with happiness. "Thank you, Benny. Your apology means so much to me."

Benny thought hard. "I want to fix this. Will you help me make our backyard beautiful for everyone?"

"Yes!" said all his friends together.

Benny used his strong teeth to carefully prop up Luna's daisy with a new stick. Carlos helped by wrapping his soft body around the stem to hold it steady. Ruby flew around finding the perfect twigs, and together they built an even better nest in a safe tree.

As the warm sun set, painting the sky orange and pink, all the friends sat together in their beautiful backyard.

"When we work together and say sorry when we make mistakes, everything turns out better," said Benny, his brown fur glowing in the sunset.

"And when we think about others, not just ourselves, we make the whole world happier," added Luna.

From that day on, Benny always remembered to think about his friends before starting any project. And whenever he made a mistake, he always said those two important words: "I'm sorry."

The End.
