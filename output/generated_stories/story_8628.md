# Sammy Squirrel’s Talking Tail

Sammy was a small, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail. He lived in a cozy treehouse high in a friendly oak tree. The treehouse smelled like pine and warm nuts. Sunlight made little dust sparkles dance in the air. Outside, leaves whispered, “shhh, shhh,” in the soft breeze.

One sunny morning, Sammy stretched and said, “Today I will finish three tasks!” He held up his tiny paws. “One, sweep the floor. Two, fix the loose step. Three, make a snack for friends.”

Sammy put on his little tool belt. It jingled softly. “Clink, clink.” He felt happy and ready.

Just then, his friend Bella Bluebird fluttered to the window. “Cheep! Hi, Sammy,” she sang. “Can I help?”

“Yes, please,” said Sammy. “I have three tasks. We can do them together. But first, let’s talk about the plan.”

They sat at the small table. It felt smooth under their paws and tiny bird feet. Sammy drew simple pictures with a chunky crayon. “First, sweep,” he said. “Second, fix,” he said. “Third, snack,” he said. Bella nodded. “Cheep-cheep! I understand.”

They started to sweep. Swish, swish went the little broom. Dust made Bella sneeze. “Ah-choo!” She giggled. Sammy smiled. “Let’s open the window,” he said. Fresh air came in—cool and leafy.

Then Tap the chipmunk popped his head in the door. “Hey! What are you doing?” he asked.

“We are finishing tasks,” said Sammy. “We made a plan. Would you like to help?”

Tap grinned. “Yes! I like to sweep fast!”

Sammy held up a paw. “Let’s talk first,” he said kindly. “We want to be fair. We take turns and use kind words. Bella and I started sweeping. You can take the next turn.”

Tap’s whiskers twitched. He wanted the broom now. His paws wiggled. Sammy noticed and said, “Tap, it’s hard to wait. I see that. Let’s count to five together, then it’s your turn.”

They all counted: “One, two, three, four, five!” Tap took the broom and swept. Swish, swish! He smiled. “This is fair,” he said.

The floor looked clean. It felt smooth under Sammy’s paws. “First task done!” Sammy cheered.

Next, they went to the wobbly step. It went “creak, creak.” Sammy got his small hammer. “Tink, tink,” went the nails.

Bella leaned close. “Cheep—how can I help?”

Sammy said, “Let’s talk about safety. We sit back and hold the bucket. Tap, you can pass me nails when I ask.”

Tap nodded. “Okay.” He held the nail box. He really wanted to hammer, too.

“Tap,” said Sammy, “would you like one hammer turn at the end?” Tap’s eyes lit up. “Yes!”

Sammy said the plan again so everyone felt sure. “I hammer first. Then Tap has one gentle tap. Bella holds the bucket.”

They followed the plan. Tink, tink. Then Tap had his turn. He made one soft “tink.” The step felt strong. No more wobble. “Second task done!” Sammy said.

Just then, a gust of wind whooshed through the tree. The door swung open. A pinecone rolled in and bonked the snack basket. Oh no! The basket tipped and berries spilled. Plop, plop, plop. Red and purple dots went on the clean floor.

Bella gasped. “Oh dear!”

Tap frowned. “Now we have to clean again.”

Sammy took a deep breath. He wrapped his tail around himself. It felt soft and warm. “Let’s talk,” he said calmly. “We can fix this together. We can share the jobs.”

They made a quick plan with simple words:
- Bella picks up berries with her beak.
- Tap wipes the floor with a damp cloth.
- Sammy washes the berries and checks for leaves.

They worked and talked. “I found a leaf!” said Bella. “Thank you,” said Sammy. “I wiped a purple spot,” said Tap. “Great job,” said Sammy.

Soon the floor was clean again. The basket was full of shiny berries. They smelled sweet, like jam in the sun. “We can make the snack now,” said Sammy. “Third task, here we come!”

They made tiny nut-butter sandwiches with berries on top. Squish, squish went the spread. The bread felt soft. The berries felt cool and popped a little. “Yum,” said Bella. “Yum,” said Tap. “Yum,” said Sammy.

Knock, knock! Two more friends peeked in—Mina Mouse and Ollie Owl (a small owl who liked daytime naps). “May we share?” asked Mina.

Sammy smiled. “Yes, we have plenty. Let’s be fair.” He spoke clearly. “Everyone gets one sandwich now. If we are still hungry, we talk and we share the rest.”

They all agreed. They counted the sandwiches together. “One, two, three, four, five.” They passed them around. Everyone said, “Thank you.” The treehouse felt warm and friendly. The sun painted golden stripes on the floor.

After the snack, Sammy checked the list. “Sweep—done. Fix—done. Snack—done.” He felt proud. He looked at his friends. “We finished our tasks because we talked, we listened, and we were fair.”

Bella flapped. “Cheep! Talking helps!”

Tap nodded. “Waiting and taking turns felt good.”

Mina squeaked. “Plans make work easy.”

Ollie hooted softly. “Kind words keep peace.”

Sammy wrapped his tail around his friends in a big, fluffy hug. “When we use our words, we can solve problems. When we share and take turns, everyone gets a fair chance. When we make a plan, we finish our tasks.”

They all smiled. The leaves whispered, “shhh, shhh,” like a happy song. The treehouse smelled like berries and clean wood. Sammy felt calm and bright inside.

“Tomorrow,” said Sammy, “we can have another little adventure. We will talk first, and we will do it together.”

The End.
