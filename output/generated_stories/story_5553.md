# Benny Bear Finds the Beat of Chores

Benny was a friendly little bear cub with soft, fuzzy ears and a round, shiny nose. He loved music. He loved tapping spoons, humming tunes, and making his drum go boom, boom, boom. Today, Benny padded into the musical practice room. The room smelled like polished wood and lemon soap. Light danced across shiny cymbals. The piano keys looked like little smiles.

But the room was messy. Drumsticks were on the floor. Music sheets lay in a crinkly pile. Bells and shakers were spread out like colorful leaves. A soft dust floated in the sunlight.

Mama Bear peeked in. “Good morning, Benny,” she said gently. “Time to practice.”

Benny thumped his drum with a happy boom. “I want to play, Mama!”

Mama Bear smiled. “First, a small chore. Let’s tidy up the room.”

Benny’s smile drooped a little. “A chore?” He wrinkled his nose. “Chores are not fun.”

Mama Bear nodded with understanding. “I hear you,” she said. “Sometimes chores feel big and boring. But chores help our space feel safe and happy. And do you know a secret?”

Benny blinked. “A secret?”

“Chores can have a beat,” she whispered. “We can make tidy time into music time.”

Benny’s ears perked up. “Music time?”

Mama Bear clapped a soft rhythm. Clap, clap, pause. Clap, clap, pause. “We sort to the beat. We clean to the beats. We finish, and then we can play our songs.”

Benny wiggled his paws. He liked the clap, clap, pause. “Okay,” he said. “Let’s try.”

First, they picked up drumsticks. Tap-tap in the stick bin. Tap-tap in the stick bin. Benny sang, “Tap, tap, in!” His voice was warm and bouncy.

Next, they smoothed the crinkly music sheets. Swish, swish, stack. Swish, swish, stack. The paper whispered like soft rain. Benny giggled. “The papers are shhh-ing!”

Mama Bear laughed. “They like being in order.”

Benny scooped up the bells. They chimed, ding-a-ling, as he dropped them into a blue box. “Ding in! Ding in!” he sang, and the bells agreed. The maracas went into a red basket with a soft shaka-shaka. The triangle went into a green bin with a tiny ting!

Benny paused and looked around. “Mama, the room looks nicer.”

“It does,” said Mama Bear. “How does your body feel?”

Benny closed his eyes. He took a big breath in. The room smelled clean and warm. The floor felt smooth under his paws. “I feel calm,” he said. “My tummy feels happy.”

Mama Bear nodded. “Chores can help our feelings. A tidy room helps our minds feel clear.”

Benny thought about that. Then he spotted a dusty drum. A little cloud puffed when he touched it. “Oops,” he coughed. “Dust!”

Mama Bear handed him a soft cloth. “Wipe-wipe with gentle paws,” she said.

Benny swiped the cloth in slow circles. Swish, swish, swish. He hummed a low note that matched the wiping. The drum skin shone. “Hello, shiny drum,” he whispered.

They moved to the piano. The keys felt cool and smooth. Benny used a tiny brush to sweep between them. Tick, tick, tick. “It tickles!” he giggled. He played a little scale. Plink, plonk, plink-plonk-plink. The sound was bright like sunshine in a jar.

Then Benny saw the biggest job. The floor needed a sweep. He held the broom, but it felt tall and heavy. He frowned. “This chore is too hard.”

Mama Bear knelt beside him. “I understand,” she said softly. “Some chores feel big. We can break a big chore into little steps. We can also share.”

Benny felt better. “Little steps,” he said. “And sharing.”

Mama Bear held the broom with him. Together they swished in short, gentle strokes. Swish, pause. Swish, pause. Benny found the rhythm again. “Swish, pause. Swish, pause.” Soon the floor looked clear and neat. Benny’s chest puffed with pride.

“You did it,” Mama Bear said. “How do you feel now?”

Benny smiled wide. “I feel strong. And I feel… ready to play!”

Mama Bear clapped. “Yes! A tidy room is ready for music.”

Benny sat at his drum set. He tapped a soft beat. Tap-tap, boom. Tap-tap, boom. He sang, “Chores have a beat! Chores help us feel neat!” He added the bells. Ding-a-ling. He added the maracas. Shaka-shaka. He played the piano keys. Plink-plonk-plink. The sounds danced together like friendly birds.

Mama Bear swayed and hummed. “Your song is lovely, Benny.”

Benny nodded. “I made a chore song. It helped me do the work. It made my heart happy.”

Mama Bear hugged him. “You used understanding and creativity. You listened to your feelings. You found a way that worked for you. That is very smart.”

Benny looked around the clean, bright room. He could see the shiny cymbals. He could find every stick and shaker. “Now the music is clear,” he said. “The room is peaceful.”

He picked up his drumsticks and played one last gentle rhythm. Boom… tap… boom… tap. “When I do my chores,” he whispered, “I help my home. I help myself. I can make it fun.”

Mama Bear smiled. “Yes, Benny. Chores help us care for our things and each other. And with a little creativity, chores can have a happy beat.”

Benny grinned. “Tomorrow, I will make a sweeping song!”

“And I will dance,” Mama Bear said.

They laughed together. The musical practice room glowed with warm light and soft, tidy sounds. Benny’s heart felt light, and his paws felt ready for new tunes.

He took a deep breath and began to play.

The End.
