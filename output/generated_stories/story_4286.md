# Bubbles on the Balcony

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash and giggle. Today, Bubbles visited a sunny balcony garden. The garden had little tomato plants, mint leaves, and bright marigold flowers. A gentle breeze carried the smell of wet soil and sweet basil. Birds chirped, and wind chimes sang, “Ting-ting, ting-ting!”

“Hello, balcony!” Bubbles squeaked, flipping her tail. She wore a small blue headband and a big smile.

On the balcony, there was a kid-sized soccer ball. It was green and white, and it felt smooth and bouncy. There was also a tiny net made from yarn. Bubbles clapped her fins. “I will play balcony soccer! Goal, goal, goal!”

A sparrow on the railing chirped, “Tweet! You can do it!”

Bubbles lined up the ball. She took a deep breath. “Kick!” she said. The ball rolled… and bonked the flower pot. No goal.

“Hmph,” Bubbles said. Her cheeks puffed. Her tail swished fast. “I wanted a goal!”

She tried again. “Kick!” The ball bounced, hit the net, and rolled away. No goal.

Bubbles felt hot in her face. Her tummy felt tight. A little growl came out. “Grrr.”

The wind chimes sang softly, “Ting-ting.”

A wise snail peeked from a leaf. “Hola,” said the snail in a kind voice. “That means hello in Spanish.”

Bubbles blinked. “Hola,” she said. The word felt calm and round in her mouth.

The snail smiled. “When I feel mad, I pause. I say ‘hola’ to my feelings. Then I breathe in… and out. Like this: in, out, slow, slow.”

Bubbles listened. She put a fin on her tummy. “In… out… in… out.” The tight feeling began to soften. The hot feeling began to cool.

A neighbor waved from another balcony. “Namaste!” she called. “That means a warm hello in Hindi.”

Bubbles waved back. “Namaste.” The word felt gentle, like a hug.

The sparrow chirped, “Tweet! Try again. Slow and steady.”

Bubbles nodded. “I will be calm. I will keep trying.” She made a little plan.

Step one: Breathe. “In… out.” She smelled the mint leaves. They smelled fresh, like a cool morning.

Step two: Aim. She looked at the net and picked a spot.

Step three: Encourage. “I can do this,” she whispered. “I can try and try.”

Bubbles tried another kick. Tap! The ball rolled closer to the net but stopped. No goal yet.

Her face started to scrunch. She felt the grrr coming back. But then she remembered. “Hola, feelings,” she said softly. She took three breaths. “In… out. In… out. In… out.”

A little bell rang from the chimes. “Ting-ting.”

“Merci,” said a butterfly that fluttered by. “Merci means thank you in French. Say merci to your breath.”

Bubbles grinned. “Merci, breath.” The word felt light and smooth.

She tried again. Tap! The ball kissed the edge of the net. Almost! Bubbles clapped her fins. “Getting closer!” she cheered.

She wiped a tiny bead of sweat and sipped some cool water. The water tasted clean and crisp.

A second try. A third try. A fourth try. Each time, Bubbles talked kindly to herself.

“I am strong.”
“I can learn.”
“I will be patient.”

She also moved a small stone to make the ground flatter. She asked the sparrow, “Will you cheer?”

“Si, si!” chirped the sparrow. “That means yes in Spanish.”

With the breeze on her skin and the smell of basil in the air, Bubbles lined up again. She felt calm and ready. “Namaste, net,” she giggled. “Hola, ball.”

Tap! The ball rolled smooth and straight. Swish! It slipped into the yarn net.

“Goal!” shouted Bubbles, the sparrow, the snail, and the neighbor. The wind chimes sang a happy song.

Bubbles did a little flip. “I did it! I stayed calm. I kept trying.”

She set a new game: ten gentle kicks. She did not get a goal every time. Sometimes the ball wobbled. Sometimes it stopped. Each time she felt a little mad, she paused and breathed.

“Hola, feelings,” she would say.
“Merci, breath,” she would add.
“Namaste, try again,” she would smile.

The sun began to set. The sky turned peach and purple. The marigolds smelled warm and spicy. Bubbles sat by the plants and patted the soccer ball.

The snail said, “When anger comes, be a friend to it. Breathe. Use kind words. Try again.”

The neighbor waved. “Good game!”

The sparrow chirped, “Tweet-tweet! You learned a strong skill.”

Bubbles nodded. “I learned anger has a door. I can open the door with my breath. I can choose calm. I can keep trying.”

She looked at the tiny net and the little garden and said, “Thank you.” She even whispered, “Gracias,” and “Shukriya,” and “Merci,” to the wind, the plants, and her breath, enjoying words from many places.

Bubbles smiled a big, bright dolphin smile. “I will play again tomorrow,” she said. “I will be calm, kind, and brave.”

The wind chimes answered, “Ting-ting,” like a soft bedtime song.

The End.
