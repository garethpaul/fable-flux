# Ollie Otter's Special Letter

High up in a cozy, quiet attic, a playful otter named Ollie loved to explore. The dusty wooden floor creaked softly under his little paws as he sniffed around old boxes and forgotten treasures.

"What's this?" Ollie whispered to himself, finding a small desk with paper and colorful crayons scattered on top. Sunlight streamed through a tiny window, making the dust sparkle like fairy glitter in the air.

Ollie climbed onto the squeaky chair and picked up a bright red crayon. He loved to draw and write, even though his letters were still wobbly. As he was about to start drawing, he heard a tiny sound.

"Sniffle... sniffle..."

Ollie's round ears perked up. He looked around the quiet attic and saw a little mouse sitting in the corner, tears rolling down her fuzzy cheeks.

"Hello there," Ollie said gently, sliding down from his chair. "I'm Ollie. What's wrong?"

The mouse looked up with sad, shiny eyes. "I'm Mia," she squeaked softly. "The big cats downstairs took my favorite acorn. They said I was too small to have nice things. It's not fair!"

Ollie's heart felt heavy hearing Mia's story. He knew how it felt when bigger animals weren't kind. "That sounds really sad and unfair," he said, sitting down next to her. "I'm sorry that happened to you."

Mia wiped her eyes with her tiny paws. "No one listens to little mice like me."

Ollie thought for a moment, then his eyes lit up with an idea. "I know! Let's write a letter together. Sometimes writing down our feelings helps, and maybe we can make things right."

"But I don't know how to write very well," Mia said quietly.

"That's okay! I'm still learning too," Ollie smiled warmly. "We can help each other."

Together, they climbed up to the desk. Ollie picked up the red crayon and handed Mia a blue one. "What should we say?" he asked.

Mia thought carefully. "Maybe we could ask them nicely to give my acorn back?"

"That's a great idea!" Ollie said. "Being kind is always better than being mean."

They worked together, taking turns writing wobbly letters. Ollie helped Mia spell the big words, and Mia helped Ollie think of kind words to use. The letter said:

"Dear Cats,
Please give back Mia's acorn. It makes her happy. Everyone deserves to have special things, no matter how big or small they are. Thank you for listening.
From, Ollie and Mia"

When they finished, Ollie and Mia carefully carried the letter downstairs. The cats read it and looked surprised. They hadn't realized how much they had hurt Mia's feelings.

"We're sorry, Mia," said the biggest cat, gently placing the shiny acorn in her tiny paws. "We didn't think about how you felt."

Mia's face lit up with joy. "Thank you!" she squeaked happily.

Back in the attic, Ollie and Mia celebrated with a little dance on the creaky floor.

"Thank you for helping me, Ollie," Mia said. "You showed me that my feelings matter."

"And you showed me that working together makes us stronger," Ollie replied with a big smile.

From that day on, whenever someone in the house felt sad or treated unfairly, they knew they could come to the quiet attic where Ollie and Mia would listen with kind hearts and help write letters that made things better.

The End.
