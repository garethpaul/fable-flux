# Toby's World of Colors

In a cozy study corner filled with soft cushions and warm sunlight, a shy little turtle named Toby sat quietly by himself. His green shell felt heavy as he looked at the big colorful world map hanging on the wall. All those faraway places seemed so exciting, but Toby felt too scared to even imagine visiting them.

Toby pulled his head into his shell a little. "I wish I could be brave like the animals in those places," he whispered to himself.

On the small wooden table, bright crayons lay scattered like tiny rainbows. Toby reached out one small flipper and touched a red crayon. It felt smooth and waxy under his touch.

"Maybe I could draw something," Toby said softly. But what should he draw?

Just then, he heard a tiny *sniff sniff* sound. Toby peeked out from his shell and saw a little tear rolling down from behind the bookshelf.

"Hello?" Toby called gently. "Are you okay?"

A small gray mouse appeared, rubbing her eyes with tiny paws. "I'm sorry," she squeaked. "I'm Mia. I was looking at the map too. My grandma lives far away in a place with tall mountains, and I miss her so much."

Toby's heart felt warm and fluttery. Someone else felt sad too! He slowly crawled closer to Mia.

"I'm Toby," he said quietly. "I was looking at the map too. All those places seem so far away."

Mia nodded, her whiskers twitching. "Grandma always tells me stories about the beautiful purple flowers that grow near her house. But I can't remember what they look like anymore."

Toby looked at the purple crayon on the table. An idea began to grow in his mind like a tiny seed.

"Would you like to draw the flowers together?" Toby asked, his voice getting a little stronger. "Maybe drawing them will help you remember."

Mia's eyes sparkled. "Really? You would help me?"

Toby picked up the purple crayon with his flipper. The waxy smell reminded him of art time. "What did your grandma say they looked like?"

"She said they had soft petals like velvet and they danced in the mountain breeze," Mia said, her voice getting happier.

Together, they began to draw. Toby made big purple circles while Mia added tiny details with her small paws. The paper filled with beautiful flowers that seemed to sway and dance.

"Oh!" Mia clapped her paws together. "They look just like grandma described!"

Toby felt proud and warm inside. Helping Mia made his shell feel lighter somehow. "Should we draw the mountains too?" he suggested.

They picked up brown and gray crayons. Soon, tall mountains rose up around the purple flowers. Mia drew a tiny house where her grandma lived.

"This makes me feel closer to her," Mia said, smiling big. "Thank you for helping me, Toby. You're so kind."

Toby's cheeks felt warm. "You helped me too, Mia. I felt scared of all those faraway places before. But now I know that everywhere in the world, there are friends who have feelings just like us."

They looked at their beautiful drawing together. The purple flowers seemed to dance right off the paper.

"Maybe tomorrow we can draw more places from the map," Mia suggested. "And imagine all the friends who live there."

Toby nodded, feeling braver than ever before. "Yes! And we can think about how to make them feel happy too."

As the afternoon sun streamed through the study corner window, Toby realized something wonderful. When you help others feel better, you feel better too. And the big wide world doesn't seem scary anymore—it seems full of friends waiting to be understood.

The End.
