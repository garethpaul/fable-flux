# Pip's Special Space

Pip the penguin loved his cozy corner in the old garden shed. The wooden walls smelled like fresh pine, and sunlight streamed through the dusty window, making rainbow patterns on the floor. Pip had arranged his favorite things just right: his soft blue blanket, his collection of smooth pebbles, and his special red ball.

Every morning, Pip would waddle into his corner, flap his flippers happily, and say, "Good morning, special space! Time to play!"

One sunny day, while Pip was quietly stacking his pebbles, he heard loud footsteps outside. THUMP, THUMP, THUMP!

"HONK! HONK!" called Gertie the goose as she burst through the shed door. "What a perfect place for my nest!"

Before Pip could say anything, Gertie started pushing his blanket aside with her orange beak. "Move over, little penguin! I need this whole corner for my eggs!"

Pip's heart felt fluttery and worried. He didn't like when someone moved his things without asking. "Um, Gertie," he said softly, "this is my special corner. I play here every day."

"Nonsense!" honked Gertie loudly. "Geese need more space than penguins! You can find somewhere else!"

Gertie began spreading her feathers wide, taking up even more room. She knocked over Pip's carefully stacked pebbles. CLATTER, CLATTER, CLATTER!

Pip felt his tummy get tight and uncomfortable. He took a deep breath, just like his mama had taught him. When someone makes you feel uncomfortable, it's okay to speak up for yourself.

"Stop, please!" said Pip, standing a little taller. "I don't like when you move my things. This corner is important to me."

Gertie paused, her feathers ruffling. "But I really need a good spot for my nest!"

Pip looked around the shed. The afternoon sun made everything glow golden and warm. He could see other cozy spots too.

"I understand you need a good place," said Pip kindly but firmly. "But this corner is mine. I was here first, and I need my space too."

Gertie's eyes grew wide. She had never thought about how the little penguin might feel.

"Maybe we can find you another good spot?" suggested Pip. He pointed with his flipper to a sunny corner near the window. "Look! That corner gets morning sunshine, and it's bigger too!"

Gertie waddled over to investigate. The spot was indeed lovely, with soft hay on the ground and plenty of room for a nest.

"You know what, Pip?" said Gertie, her voice much gentler now. "You're right. I should have asked before taking your space. And this corner is actually perfect for me!"

Pip smiled, his tummy feeling much better. "Thank you for listening, Gertie. And thank you for understanding that everyone needs their own special space."

As Gertie began building her nest in her new corner, she called over, "I'm sorry I moved your things, Pip. Next time I'll ask first!"

Pip carefully restacked his pebbles, smoothed his blue blanket, and picked up his red ball. His corner felt safe and cozy again.

"It's okay to say no when something doesn't feel right," Pip said to himself proudly. "And it's okay to help find solutions that work for everyone!"

From that day on, Pip and Gertie shared the garden shed happily. They both had their own special spaces, and they always asked before borrowing anything from each other.

The End.
