# Pebble the Penguin Finds the Right Words

Pebble was a cheerful penguin who loved to wiggle and write. Pebble lived in a playful living room with bright pillows, a soft rug, and crayons in a rainbow tin. The room smelled like warm cocoa and fresh paper. Sunlight danced on the walls like tiny golden fish.

One cozy morning, Pebble clapped their flippers. “I will write a happy note!” Pebble said. “I want to tell my friend Pippa how much I like playing.”

Pebble waddled to the little table. Tap tap went the crayons on the wood. Scritch scritch went the purple crayon on smooth paper. Pebble drew a big heart and wrote, “I like you!”

Just then, Pebble’s brother, Pip, bounced in. Pip loved to drum with spoons. Clink clink! Tap tap! The spoons made shiny music.

Pebble’s tummy felt fluttery. The paper wiggled. The purple crayon made a squiggle. “Oh no,” Pebble whispered. “My words are messy.”

Pebble took a deep breath. In, out. Pebble tried again. Scritch scritch. Clink clink! Tap tap! The spoons got louder. The crayon line slipped again.

Pebble’s cheeks felt hot. “I feel upset,” Pebble said softly. “My body feels tight, and my tummy is twisty.”

Pip smiled and drummed faster. “Listen to my beat!” Pip laughed.

Pebble wanted to shout. Pebble’s flippers squeezed the crayon. But Pebble remembered a little poster on the wall. It said, “Pause. Breathe. Use kind words.”

Pebble put the crayon down. “Pause,” Pebble said to themself. Pebble closed their eyes and took three slow breaths. In, out. In, out. In, out. The tight feeling melted a little.

Pebble touched their chest. “What am I feeling?” Pebble asked. “I feel frustrated. I want quiet to write my note.”

Pebble waddled to Pip and spoke in a calm voice. “Pip, I have big feelings. I feel frustrated. I want to write a kind note. The spoons are too loud for me.”

Pip stopped. The room became soft again. “Oh,” Pip said. “I didn’t know. Thank you for telling me.” Pip looked at the spoons. “I can make a soft beat,” he said. He tapped gently. Tap… tap… tap…

Pebble listened. The sound was like raindrops on the window. Pebble smiled. “Thank you, Pip. That helps.”

Pebble went back to the table. Scritch scritch went the purple crayon. Pebble wrote slowly. “Dear Pippa, I like how you share blocks. I like your giggles. Do you want to play penguin slides later? Love, Pebble.”

Pebble read the note out loud. The words felt warm, like cocoa. Pebble’s chest felt light. Pebble smiled big. “I did it!”

Pip peeked over Pebble’s shoulder. “That is a kind note,” Pip said. “You used your words and your heart.”

Pebble nodded. “I named my feelings,” Pebble said proudly. “I asked for help with kind words. That helped me feel calm.”

The doorbell rang. Ding-dong! Pippa slid in with a whoosh, her scarf swishing like a ribbon. “Hi!” Pippa chirped. “Your living room smells like cocoa. Mmm!”

Pebble waddled over and handed the note to Pippa. “This is for you,” Pebble said.

Pippa opened the note. Her eyes sparkled. “I feel happy and warm,” Pippa said. “Thank you, Pebble. These words are special.” She gave Pebble a gentle flipper hug.

Pip smiled. “Can I drum a happy beat?” he asked.

Pebble laughed. “Yes! A soft happy beat, please.”

Tap… tap… tap… The beat was cozy. Pebble, Pippa, and Pip slid on the rug like little snow gliders. The soft rug rubbed under their feet. They giggled and wiggled. The pillows bounced like colorful clouds.

After sliding, Pippa said, “Pebble, how did you write such a sweet note?”

Pebble sat tall. “I listened to my feelings,” Pebble said. “I felt frustrated. I took breaths. I used peaceful words to ask for what I needed. Then I felt calm and strong, and I wrote from my heart.”

Pippa nodded. “That is a brave skill,” she said. “You helped your feelings. You used peaceful communication.”

Pebble beamed. “I can do hard things with calm words,” Pebble said. “That makes me feel proud.”

The sun made a warm square on the floor. Pebble, Pippa, and Pip sat in the light. They passed the rainbow tin. The crayons clicked and clacked like tiny shells. Together, they drew hearts, stars, and smiley penguins. They wrote small notes: “Thank you.” “I like your laugh.” “You are kind.”

Pebble held the notes and felt a gentle glow inside. “These words are hugs,” Pebble said.

Pip nodded. “And soft beats help, too!”

Pippa giggled. “We are a great team,” she said. “We listen. We speak kindly. We help each other.”

Pebble looked at the happy drawings and the neat purple note. Pebble knew the lesson: Feelings tell us what we need. Breathing and kind words help us share our needs. That keeps our hearts calm and strong.

The End.
