# Mango, the Courageous Monkey on the Balcony Garden

Mango was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and soft, fuzzy fur. He lived with his family in a cozy home. Outside the living room was a sunny balcony garden full of pots and plants. There were green basil leaves, tiny tomatoes, a tall sunflower, and a sweet-smelling mint that tickled Mango’s nose.

One morning, Mama said, “Mango, today we have three jobs in the balcony garden. We’ll water the plants, pick ripe tomatoes for our neighbors, and tidy the floor. We will do one job at a time.”

Mango nodded. “I can do it! I am brave, and I can finish my tasks.”

The sun was warm on Mango’s fur. The breeze was soft and smelled like mint and wet dirt. He picked up the little watering can. It was shiny and blue. When he tipped it, the water went splish-splash, like tiny rain on the leaves.

“Good listening, Mango,” said Mama. “Let’s give each plant a drink.”

Mango hummed a happy song. “Splish-splash, sip-sip.” He watered the basil. He watered the mint. He watered the sunflower. He wanted to run and play, but he remembered Mama’s words: one job at a time.

He looked at the tomato plant. Red tomatoes hid under bright green leaves. Mango reached out carefully. “I will be gentle,” he said.

Just then, a sparrow named Pip landed on the railing. “Chirp! Hello, Mango,” Pip sang. “Can I help?”

“Yes!” Mango smiled. “We are picking tomatoes for our neighbors. It’s community service. We share!”

Pip fluffed her feathers. “Chirp-chirp! Sharing is caring.”

Mango picked the tomatoes with a soft twist. Plop, plop, plop. They fell into a little basket. The tomatoes were warm from the sun and smelled sweet. Mango licked his lips. “They look yummy.”

“After we finish our tasks, we can taste one,” Mama said. “Now we tidy.”

Mango looked at the balcony floor. There were dry leaves, a small shovel, and a coil of green hose. He felt a tiny frown. “This looks like a big job,” he whispered.

Mama knelt beside him. “We can make it small. First, sweep the leaves. Second, roll the hose. Third, put the shovel away. We do steps. We finish one, then the next.”

Mango took a deep breath. He felt brave again. “I can do steps!”

Swish-swish went the broom. The leaves made soft crackly sounds. Mango liked the sound. It made him smile. He made a neat pile. Then he rolled the hose. It felt cool and smooth. He put the shovel in its place.

“Chirp!” Pip pecked at a stray leaf and hopped around the pile. Mango giggled. “Thank you, Pip!”

Mango looked around. The balcony garden looked clean and bright. “We did it!” he cheered, tail flicking with joy.

Mama gave him a warm hug. “You did each job, one by one. That is how we complete tasks.”

Just then, Mango saw something. A small pot was tipped over near the door. Dark soil spilled out. A tiny green sprout lay on its side. Mango’s tummy felt twisty. “Oh no,” he said. “I knocked it down when I rolled the hose.” He felt sad and a little scared.

Pip tilted her head. “Chirp?”

Mama touched Mango’s shoulder. “It’s okay to make mistakes. What can we do to fix it?”

Mango took another deep breath. “We can be gentle. We can forgive me. I can forgive me.” He picked up the pot carefully. He scooped the soil back with his small hands. The dirt felt cool and crumbly. He tucked the sprout into the soil and patted it softly. “There, little plant. I am sorry.”

Mama smiled. “That is forgiveness and care. You fixed your mistake. I am proud of you.”

Mango’s chest felt warm and strong. “I did it. I kept going.”

“Chirp-chirp!” Pip hopped in a circle. “You are courageous!”

Now the basket of tomatoes was ready. Mango and Mama walked down the hall to Mrs. Lee’s door. Mrs. Lee was an elderly neighbor who loved soup and stories.

“Hello, Mrs. Lee,” Mango said. “We picked these for you. It’s our community service.”

Mrs. Lee clapped her hands. “How kind! Thank you, Mango.” She leaned down and whispered, “You are a big helper.”

Mango’s cheeks felt sunny. His heart felt big.

Back on the balcony, Mama sliced one small tomato. They each took a bite. It was juicy and sweet. “Mmm,” said Mango. “Tastes like sunshine.”

Mama nodded. “Today you listened, you worked in steps, you shared, and you fixed a mistake. How do you feel?”

Mango stood tall. “I feel proud. I can complete my tasks, even when it is hard. I can help my neighbors. I can forgive myself and try again.”

Pip sang a happy song. The sunflower nodded in the breeze. The mint leaves waved like tiny green hands.

Mango looked at the tidy balcony garden. “Tomorrow,” he said, “I will do my jobs one by one again.”

Mama smiled. “One by one. That’s how we grow.”

The End.
