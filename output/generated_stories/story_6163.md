# Tall Necks, Tiny Pots

Sunny the giraffe lived on a high balcony with a tiny garden. The garden was full of little pots, soft soil, and bright leaves. The air smelled like mint and tomatoes. Bees hummed, and a small wind tickled Sunny’s long neck.

Sunny was a thoughtful giraffe. He liked to look, listen, and think before he spoke. One morning, he peeked over the railing and whispered, “Good morning, world.” He could see so many roofs, birds, and clouds. Far away, a plane drew a thin white line in the blue sky.

On the balcony, Sunny’s friends came to visit. There was Pip the pigeon, who cooed gently. There was Nia the snail, who moved slow and shiny. There was Tiko the tortoise, who loved warm stones. And there was Mei the ladybug, tiny and bright red with dots like little moons.

“Let’s plant new seeds today,” said Sunny, swishing his tail. “We can grow food from different places.”

“Yes, yes!” cooed Pip. “I found basil seeds.”

“I brought cilantro,” said Mei, landing softly on Sunny’s ear.

“I have tiny millet,” Pip added, pecking a little pouch.

“I like tomatoes,” said Tiko, patting a pot.

Nia lifted her head. “I have hibiscus seeds. My grandma said they make a pretty drink in far-away places.”

Sunny smiled. “Seeds from many places. That is wonderful. Our garden can be a world garden.”

They set the pots in a row. The sun felt warm on their backs. The soil smelled rich and cool. But when they started to plant, a problem grew like a weed.

“I want the sunny spot,” said Tiko, moving toward the brightest corner.

“I need the sunny spot too,” said Basil, if it could talk—because Pip wanted to plant basil there.

Mei fluttered. “Cilantro likes a little shade.”

Nia sighed. “Hibiscus loves warmth.”

Pip ruffled his feathers. “My seeds first. I found them!”

Tiko shook his head. “My tomatoes need room.”

Mei’s wings buzzed. “I am tiny. I always get the tiny place. It is not fair.”

Sunny listened. He felt a flutter in his chest. His neck felt tall and a little wobbly. He wanted everyone to be happy. He took a deep breath and smelled the mint. He thought, and thought some more.

“Friends,” Sunny said softly, “we are all different, like our seeds. Different is special. Different needs different things. Let’s talk and make a plan together.”

“How?” asked Tiko.

“We can take turns talking,” said Sunny. “One at a time. I will listen to each of you.”

Pip bobbed. “Basil likes sun. And I can fly, so I can water the top pots.”

Mei landed on a leaf. “Cilantro likes a little shade. I can help with tiny drops of water.”

Tiko tapped his shell. “Tomatoes need sun and a big pot.”

Nia traced a slow swirl. “Hibiscus likes warmth and a cozy corner.”

Sunny nodded after each friend. “Thank you for telling me. Now, what can we share? What can we swap?”

Pip looked around. “I can give basil the top shelf. I can fly up there.”

Tiko said, “I can take the wide pot by the railing.”

Mei smiled. “I can move under the mint to make shade for cilantro.”

Nia added, “I can live near the warm brick wall with hibiscus.”

Sunny’s eyes shone. “Let’s make a map.” He used his hoof to draw lines in a little spill of soil. He made boxes for pots and drew a sun in one corner and a little umbrella in another.

“Top shelf: basil,” said Pip.

“Wide pot: tomatoes,” said Tiko.

“Under mint: cilantro,” said Mei.

“Warm wall: hibiscus,” said Nia.

Sunny asked, “Does this feel fair to everyone?”

Pip fluffed his wings. “Yes!”

Tiko nodded slowly. “Yes.”

Mei twirled. “Yes!”

Nia smiled with her eyes. “Yes.”

They planted together. Sunny’s long tongue carefully placed seeds. Pip sprinkled water like tiny rain. Mei brushed soil smooth. Tiko pushed the big pot into place. Nia sang a soft snail song.

Days passed. The garden changed. Basil popped up like little green hands. Cilantro waved feathery leaves. Tomatoes showed yellow flowers like tiny stars. Hibiscus grew big, shiny leaves. The balcony smelled like a picnic. The bees hummed happy songs.

One evening, the sky turned pink and gold. The plane line in the sky grew long again. Sunny looked at the plants from many places. He whispered, “Hello, world,” and the world seemed to whisper back.

Pip said, “Look! Our garden is like a little map.”

Mei added, “We are different, and we made it work.”

Tiko smiled. “We shared. We talked. We listened.”

Nia lifted her head. “We grew a world garden.”

Sunny felt warm inside. He had been thoughtful. He had helped his friends speak and find a plan. He looked at his tall reflection in the window and said, “I am proud of my long neck and my careful heart.”

Mei landed on Sunny’s nose. “Your neck helps you see the big picture,” she giggled.

“And your tiny wings help you see small things,” Sunny replied.

Pip cooed, “Different is not a problem. Different is a gift.”

They all cheered. They ate a leaf of basil and a leaf of cilantro. The flavors were bright and new. The wind was soft, and the stars peeked out like wishes.

Sunny looked at his friends. “Today we learned: We are all different, and that is something to celebrate. When we talk and listen, we can make a plan that works for everyone.”

Everyone nodded. The garden glowed with love and leaves. The city hummed below, and the sky held them all.

The End.
