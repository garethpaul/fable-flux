# Benny's Big Helper Day

Benny the rabbit hopped into the warm, steamy laundry room. The washing machine was making a loud "whoosh-whoosh" sound, and colorful clothes were spinning round and round inside.

"Oh my!" said Benny, twitching his pink nose. The room smelled like fresh soap bubbles and clean cotton. But something was wrong. Mrs. Mouse was sitting on a pile of towels, looking very sad.

"What's the matter, Mrs. Mouse?" asked Benny, his long ears perking up with concern.

Mrs. Mouse sniffled. "I dropped my favorite red scarf behind the big washing machine. It's the scarf my grandmother gave me, and now I can't reach it!" A tiny tear rolled down her furry cheek.

Benny's heart felt heavy seeing his friend so upset. "Don't worry," he said gently. "I want to help you!"

But when Benny looked behind the washing machine, his ears drooped. The space was too narrow for him too. "Hmm," he said, scratching his head with his paw. "This is tricky."

Just then, Sally Snake slithered into the laundry room. "Ssssomething wrong?" she asked, noticing their sad faces.

"Mrs. Mouse's special scarf is stuck back there," explained Benny, pointing with his paw.

Sally's eyes lit up. "I'm long and thin! I can help!" She wiggled her green body behind the washing machine. "I can sssee it, but I can't pick it up with my mouth."

Then Oliver Octopus squeezed through the door, carrying a basket of wet clothes. His eight arms were full! "What's happening here?" he gurgled.

"We're trying to help Mrs. Mouse get her scarf," said Benny. "Sally can reach it, but she needs hands to grab it."

Oliver smiled. "I have lots of arms! But I'm too big to fit back there."

Benny's eyes sparkled with an idea. "Wait! What if we work together?" He hopped excitedly. "Oliver, can you give Sally one of your stretchy arms?"

"Of course!" said Oliver. He carefully handed one of his purple arms to Sally.

Sally slithered back behind the washing machine, holding Oliver's arm. "Perfect! I can reach it now!" She wrapped Oliver's arm around the soft red scarf and pulled it out.

"My scarf!" cried Mrs. Mouse, jumping up and down with joy. "Oh, thank you, thank you all!" She hugged the scarf close to her heart.

"We did it together!" said Benny, his cotton tail wiggling happily.

"Yesss! Teamwork!" hissed Sally proudly.

"Many arms make light work!" laughed Oliver, waving all eight arms.

Mrs. Mouse's eyes filled with happy tears. "You are all such kind friends. You saw I was sad and chose to help me. That makes my heart feel so warm!"

"Helping friends feels good," said Benny, giving Mrs. Mouse a gentle pat on the back.

From that day on, whenever someone in the laundry room needed help, all the friends remembered Benny's lesson. They looked out for each other with caring hearts, worked together as a team, and always said "thank you" for acts of kindness.

And Mrs. Mouse? She wore her grandmother's red scarf every day, remembering not just her grandmother's love, but also the love of her wonderful, compassionate friends.

The End.
