# Ping's Rooftop Art Class

Ping the panda loved to learn new things. Today was special - his first art class on the tall building's rooftop! The morning sun felt warm on his black and white fur as he climbed the stairs, one step at a time.

"Welcome, everyone!" called Ms. Rosa, their art teacher. She had curly hair and a bright smile. "Today we'll paint the beautiful city below us."

Ping looked around. There were children from many places - Maria with her long braids, Ahmed wearing his colorful hat, and tiny Emma with freckles on her nose. They all sat on colorful mats with paintbrushes and paper.

"First," said Ms. Rosa, "we need to stretch our bodies like the tall buildings we see. Can everyone stand up?"

Ping watched carefully as Ms. Rosa raised her arms high above her head. "Reach up to the sky!" she said. Ping stretched his paws up, up, up. The cool breeze tickled his nose.

"Now touch your toes!" Ms. Rosa bent down low. Ping tried to touch his toes, but his round belly made it tricky. He kept trying, breathing slowly like his mama taught him.

"Good job following directions, Ping!" Ms. Rosa smiled. "Now let's sit and pick up our brushes."

Ping held his brush carefully. It felt smooth and light in his paw.

"Step one," said Ms. Rosa, "dip your brush in the blue paint for the sky."

Ping dipped his brush in the bright blue paint. It looked like the color of his favorite berries!

"Step two, paint gentle strokes across the top of your paper."

Ping moved his brush slowly across the paper. Swoosh, swoosh! The blue paint made soft sounds as it spread.

Maria was painting fast, making big splashes. Ahmed was mixing colors together. But Ping listened carefully to each instruction and painted one careful stroke at a time.

"Step three," continued Ms. Rosa, "add white clouds with small dots."

Ping dipped his brush in white paint. Dot, dot, dot! Little fluffy clouds appeared on his paper. They looked like cotton balls floating in the sky.

"Now for step four - paint the tall buildings with gray and brown."

Ping painted rectangles for buildings, just like Ms. Rosa showed them. His buildings looked different from everyone else's, and that was perfectly fine!

"Last step," said Ms. Rosa, "add something special that makes you happy."

Ping thought carefully. What made him happy? He painted a small green bamboo plant on top of one building, just like the ones his grandpa grew back home in China.

"Ping, your painting is wonderful!" said Ms. Rosa. "You followed every direction so patiently."

Emma looked at Ping's painting. "I love your bamboo plant! What is it?"

"It's bamboo," said Ping proudly. "My grandpa taught me that bamboo grows strong because it bends but never breaks, just like when we follow directions and keep trying."

All the children gathered around to see each other's paintings. Every picture was different and beautiful! Maria's had bright purple buildings, Ahmed's showed golden domes, and Emma's had rainbow flowers everywhere.

"Look what happens when we all follow directions together," said Ms. Rosa. "We create something amazing!"

As they cleaned their brushes in the warm soapy water, Ping felt proud. By listening carefully and following each step, he had created something beautiful. The city sparkled below them, and Ping's heart felt just as bright.

"Can we paint again tomorrow?" asked Ping.

"Of course!" laughed Ms. Rosa. "Good things happen when we follow directions and never give up."

The End.
