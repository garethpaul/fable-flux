# Milo the Monkey's Washing Day Wonder

In the warm, steamy laundry room, a little brown monkey named Milo sat on top of the washing machine. His soft fur was messy, and his favorite red bandana was covered in mud from playing outside.

"Oh no!" Milo said, looking at his dirty bandana. "I can't wear this to my friend Bella's birthday party today. What should I do?"

Milo's tummy rumbled loudly. *Grumble, grumble!* He was hungry and tired from all his morning adventures. The laundry room smelled like fresh soap and clean towels, making him feel cozy.

First, Milo thought about his problem. "Let me think step by step," he said to himself, scratching his fuzzy chin. "My bandana is dirty. The washing machine cleans clothes. So I should wash my bandana!"

Milo jumped down and opened the washing machine lid. *Click!* But then he stopped and thought some more. "Wait! I'm hungry and sleepy. Mama always says to take care of myself first."

He climbed onto the counter and found a banana in the fruit bowl. *Munch, munch!* The sweet banana made his tummy feel much better. Then he curled up on a pile of warm, fluffy towels for a quick nap.

"Ahh," Milo yawned. "Now I can think clearly!"

After his rest, Milo felt much better. He carefully put his bandana in the washing machine with some soap. *Swish, swish!* The water danced around, cleaning his special bandana.

While waiting, Milo noticed his little sister Maya's favorite yellow dress in the dirty clothes basket. "Maya was sad yesterday because her dress got muddy too," he remembered. "I should help her!"

Milo thought carefully. "The washing machine is already running. I can wash Maya's dress with my bandana. That's being generous and helpful!"

He gently added Maya's dress to the washing machine. The soapy water bubbled and swirled, cleaning both items together. *Bubble, bubble, splash!*

When the washing was done, Milo moved everything to the dryer. The warm air whooshed around, making everything soft and dry. *Whoosh, whoosh!*

"Perfect!" Milo cheered, clapping his little hands.

Just then, Maya peeked into the laundry room. Her eyes were still sad about her dirty dress.

"Maya!" Milo called happily. "Look what I have for you!"

He pulled out her bright yellow dress, now clean and fresh-smelling. Maya's face lit up like sunshine.

"Oh, Milo! Thank you so much!" Maya hugged her big brother tight. "How did you know I was sad about my dress?"

"I used my thinking brain," Milo said proudly, tapping his head. "First, I took care of myself by eating and resting. Then I could think clearly about helping you too!"

Maya twirled in her clean dress. "You're the best brother ever!"

Milo put on his fresh red bandana and looked in the mirror. He felt proud, happy, and ready for the birthday party.

"I learned something important today," Milo said to himself. "When I have a problem, I should think step by step. I should take care of myself first, then I can help others too!"

The laundry room filled with the sweet smell of clean clothes and the happy sounds of two little monkeys getting ready for a wonderful day.

From that day on, whenever Milo faced a problem, he remembered to think carefully, take care of himself, and look for ways to help others too.

The End.
