# Pippa Parrot’s Colorful Cleanup

Pippa was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of blue on her wings. She loved to hum little tunes and say, “Squawk! I can make it fun!” The dining room was Pippa’s favorite place. It smelled like warm bread and sweet oranges. Sunlight made sparkles on the shiny table. But today, the dining room looked messy. Crumbs dotted the floor like tiny stars. Napkins were crumpled like little mountains. Spoons clinked in a sleepy pile.

Mama’s voice was kind. “Pippa, it’s chore time. We need to clean the dining room.”

Pippa’s tail feathers twitched. She puffed up with worry. “Squawk… I don’t know if I can,” she said softly. “What if I do it wrong? What if it takes too long?”

Mama knelt to look at Pippa’s bright eyes. “You can do it. We can do it together. Let’s try. We can make a plan.”

Pippa hopped onto a chair and looked at the table. The wood felt smooth under her toes. She took a deep breath. In… out. The room smelled like cinnamon. “Maybe I can make it fun,” she whispered. “Squawk!”

Mama smiled. “I respect your ideas, Pippa. What is your plan?”

Pippa thought. Her feathers fluffed. “Music! We can clean to a song. And colors! We can sort by colors.”

“That sounds creative,” said Mama. “We can compromise. We’ll use your music and color game, and we’ll also make sure we finish each chore.”

Pippa nodded. “Deal! I will try. I can do it.”

They started with the napkins. Pippa picked one up with her beak. It felt soft and a little damp. “Red napkins here, blue napkins there,” she chirped. “Squawk! Red, blue, red, blue!” She made a little beat with her feet: tap-tap, tap-tap. Mama folded each napkin neatly. Together, they made a stack like a rainbow sandwich.

Next were the spoons. They clinked like tiny bells. Pippa liked the sound. “Clink-clink, sparkle-shine,” she sang. “I can shine. I can try.” She rubbed each spoon with a clean cloth. The spoons looked at her like little moons. Pippa felt a warm glow in her chest.

“Look at you,” Mama said. “You are being careful and kind to our things.”

Pippa’s wings fluttered. “I am doing it!” she chirped. “I can do chores!”

On the floor, crumb stars still twinkled. Pippa blinked. “There are so many,” she whispered. A big worry bubble popped in her tummy. “What if I can’t pick them all up?”

Mama put a gentle hand on Pippa’s back. “We can make a tiny plan for tiny crumbs. I will sweep. You can sing and point. Then you can use the little brush.”

Pippa nodded. “Compromise!” she said proudly. “We share the job.”

Mama swept—swish, swish. Pippa sang a soft crumb song. “Here and here, little crumbs, go home now.” She used the little brush. Brush, brush. The crumbs made a soft rustle like leaves. Soon the floor was smooth and clean.

The last chore was the table. Pippa took a cloth. It smelled like lemon. She wiped in little circles. Circle, circle, shine. “I like this smell,” she said. “It smells happy.”

“You are doing a respectful job,” Mama said. “You take care of our home.”

Pippa’s chest felt big with pride. “I am strong. I am smart. I can try new things,” she said. “Squawk!”

They stepped back to look. The dining room glowed. The chairs were straight like soldiers. The table shone like a lake in the sun. The air smelled like lemon and bread and a tiny bit of orange.

Pippa hopped in a circle. “We did it! I did it!”

Mama hugged her. “You believed in yourself. You used your ideas. You worked with me. That is self-confidence and compromise.”

Just then, Pippa’s little brother, Pip, peeked in. His eyes were wide. “Wow,” Pip said. “Can I help next time?”

Pippa nodded. “Yes! We can make a new song. We can share the chores. We can respect each other’s ideas.”

Pip smiled. “I like that.”

Pippa fluffed her feathers and sang a happy tune. “I was worried, but I tried. I can learn. I can help. I can be me.” Her voice sounded sweet and bright in the clean room.

Mama set a small bowl of orange slices on the shiny table. “A thank-you snack for good helpers,” she said.

Pippa tasted a slice. It was juicy and sunny. She closed her eyes. “Mmm,” she said. “This tastes like happy.”

Mama raised a finger. “What did we learn today?”

Pippa thought. “I learned that I can do it, even when I feel unsure. When we share ideas and chores, we finish faster. When we listen and respect each other, it feels good.”

Mama nodded. “Yes.”

Pippa looked at Pip. “And next time, we can all clean together,” she said. “We can sing. We can sort. We can shine.”

Pip giggled. “Squawk!”

Pippa laughed. “Squawk!”

The dining room felt bright and warm. Pippa stood tall on her chair. She felt brave, kind, and proud. “I believe in me,” she whispered.

The End.
