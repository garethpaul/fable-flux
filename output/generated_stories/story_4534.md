# Miko Mouse and the Crumb Map

Miko was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived behind a tiny crack in the wall by the dining room. The dining room smelled like warm toast in the morning and sweet apples in the afternoon. The table was big and wooden. The chairs made gentle squeaks. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor.

Every day, Miko peeked out to see what was new. He liked to help in small ways, too. If a crumb fell, he would sweep it into a neat pile with his whiskery nose and tiny paws. He felt happy when the room looked clean and shiny.

One morning, Miko sniffed the air. Sniff, sniff. Something smelled like cinnamon and honey. Mmm!

“What is that smell?” Miko asked. His whiskers wiggled. “I am curious!”

He stepped onto the smooth floor. It felt cool on his feet. He followed the yummy smell under the table. Tap-tap went his paws. But then he stopped. Little crumbs were lined up like dots.

“Hmm,” Miko said softly. “These crumbs make a path. I wonder where they go.”

He wanted to run and nibble right away, but Miko remembered his smart thinking. “Curiosity is good,” he told himself. “I can look, think, and learn.”

Miko tilted his head. “If I follow the dots, I might find the sweet smell. If I count the dots, I can know how far it is.”

He pulled out his tiny notebook. It was made from a folded leaf. He had a tiny pencil, too—a short twig with a bit of soot. Miko loved writing down what he saw. It helped him solve little puzzles.

“First, I will make a plan,” he said. “Step one: count the crumb dots. Step two: look for shapes. Step three: clean as I go.”

Miko began to count. “One, two, three,” he whispered, tapping each crumb with his paw. He drew little circles in his notebook. He saw the crumbs made a curve around the chair leg, then a straight line to the window, then a small zigzag near the rug.

“This looks like a map!” Miko squeaked with joy. He drew the curve, the line, and the zigzag. “A crumb map!”

Just then, his friend Tilly, a tiny ant, marched by. “Good morning, Miko!” Tilly waved a shiny antenna. “What are you doing?”

“I am using curiosity to learn,” said Miko. “I am making a crumb map. Do you want to help?”

“Yes, please!” said Tilly. “I like maps. I like lines. I like dots!”

Together, they followed the dots. Miko brushed crumbs into a little pile as they went. “I will clean. It is our dining room, too,” he said kindly.

“You are very helpful,” said Tilly. “That is good mouse work.”

They turned the curve. They walked the straight line. The sun on the floor felt warm on their backs. They smelled cinnamon again. Stronger now! Sniff, sniff!

At the zigzag, Tilly paused. “The crumbs go two ways,” she said. “Left and right!”

Miko tapped his chin. “Let’s use logical thinking. Which way smells sweeter?”

They both sniffed. Left smelled a little sweet. Right smelled very sweet.

“Right!” they said together, and giggled.

They went right and counted more crumbs. “Four, five, six,” whispered Miko, writing neat numbers in his notebook. He drew a little heart next to six. “I heart counting,” he said with a smile.

Soon, they reached the end of the crumb map. There, under the table edge, sat a small, sticky bun. It was rolled with cinnamon. A bite was missing. The bun looked lonely.

“Oh,” said Miko softly. “Someone dropped it. Maybe a child. Maybe they were in a hurry.”

Tilly nodded. “We found it because we were curious and careful.”

Miko looked at the bun. He wanted to nibble it all. But he also wanted to be kind. “Let’s be fair,” he said. “We can share. And we can tidy up so the dining room stays nice.”

They broke the bun into tiny pieces. Tilly took a tiny piece. Miko took a tiny piece. He put the rest on a clean leaf plate near the wall, just in case any other small friend was hungry.

Then Miko did his chores. He swept the crumbs into a neat pile and pushed the pile onto a little napkin he kept for cleaning. He wiped a tiny sticky spot with a damp corner of the napkin.

When the floor looked shiny again, Miko wrote in his notebook: “We made a plan. We counted. We looked. We sniffed. We shared. We cleaned.”

Tilly clapped her tiny feet. “We did it!”

Miko’s heart felt full and warm. “Curiosity helped us learn,” he said. “Thinking helped us choose. Chores helped us care.”

He peeked around the room one more time. The chairs were still. The sunlight was bright. The air smelled fresh and nice.

Miko smiled and closed his notebook. “I am glad I asked, ‘What is that smell?’ Asking a question can start a good adventure.”

He waved to Tilly. “Thank you for helping.”

“Anytime!” said Tilly. “Let’s make more maps tomorrow.”

“Yes,” said Miko. “Tomorrow, we can explore the sound of the clink-clink spoons!”

They both giggled. Then Miko went back to his tiny home, happy and proud.

The End.
