# Tortoise Tim’s Colorful Day

Tortoise Tim was a wise tortoise with gentle eyes and a soft, scratchy shell. One sunny morning, he slid slowly into the creative art room. The room smelled like fresh paper and fruity paint. Sunlight danced on jars of colors—red like strawberries, blue like the sky, yellow like warm butter. Brushes stood like tiny trees in a cup. Snip-snip scissors whispered on the table. Crayons lay in a rainbow row.

“Good morning,” Tim said in his calm voice. “Today we make art with our hearts.”

Around the big table sat many friends. There was Momo the monkey with curly fur, Bee the bunny with long ears, Ravi the raccoon with bright eyes, and Zuzu the zebra with bold stripes. Everyone looked different, and everyone smiled.

Teacher Lina clapped softly. “Let’s make pictures of feelings,” she said. “Use colors and shapes to show what you feel.”

Tim nodded. “Art can help our feelings talk.”

Bee picked a pink crayon. “Pink feels happy,” she whispered. Momo dipped a brush into green. “Green is bouncy like me!” he giggled. Zuzu chose black and white stripes. “These are my favorites,” she said proudly.

Tim opened a jar of blue paint. It looked cool and calm. He took a slow breath in and out. “Today, I feel quiet like water,” he said. He painted soft blue waves. Swoosh, swoosh went the brush. The sound was gentle, like rain on leaves.

Ravi, the raccoon, frowned. He wanted the big red paint jar. “It’s mine,” he said, grabbing it fast.

Momo reached for it too. “I need red for my rocket!” His voice got loud. Their paws bumped. The jar wobbled.

Plop! Red paint splashed. It splattered on the table. It splashed on Momo’s fur. A tiny drop landed on Tim’s shell—dot!

Momo’s face scrunched. “Hey! I don’t like this!” he yelled. He stomped his foot. Stomp, stomp. His tail twitched.

Tim felt a bubble of worry. “Big feelings are okay,” he said softly. “Let’s help them find a safe way out.”

Teacher Lina set the jar upright. “Deep breaths, everyone.” She showed a slow breath. In through the nose, out through the mouth. “Smell the flower,” she said, “blow the candle.”

Tim did it with her. “Smell the flower,” he whispered. He breathed in. “Blow the candle.” He breathed out. Momo copied. Smell... blow. His shoulders relaxed.

Ravi looked down. “I grabbed too fast,” he said. His ears drooped. “I was excited and forgot to ask.”

Tim nodded. “When we want something, we can use words. We can say, ‘May I have a turn?’”

Momo wiped a red dot from his fur and looked at Ravi. “Please may I use red after you?”

Ravi’s eyes brightened. “Yes. Thank you for asking.” He held the jar gently and kept it steady. “I will use it for two minutes,” he said. “Then it’s your turn.”

Tim smiled. “That is fair and kind.”

At the next table, Zuzu drew bold stripes. Bee hopped closer and reached for Zuzu’s marker. Zuzu pulled back. “That’s mine right now,” she said. Her voice was small but brave.

Tim heard and nodded. “Thank you for using your boundary words,” he said. “Bee, we ask before we take.”

Bee hugged her ears. “Oh! I forgot.” She asked, “May I try that marker when you’re done?”

Zuzu smiled. “Yes, after I finish my stripe.”

The room filled with friendly sounds—swish-swish brushes, click-clack markers, and soft hums. Everyone made different art. Bee painted pink circles that looked like bubbles. Zuzu drew stripes and stars. Ravi made a red volcano that looked strong. Momo painted a red rocket zooming across a green sky. Tim made blue waves, then added a yellow sun that warmed his shell.

Suddenly, Momo saw a drop of blue on his paper. “Who did that?” he cried. His face grew tight again. Tim put down his brush.

“I did,” Tim said calmly. “I didn’t see your paper. I’m sorry.”

Momo looked at the blue drop. He took a deep breath—smell the flower, blow the candle. “It’s okay,” he said. “I can make it a cloud.” He added a fluffy shape. “Look! My rocket flies past a blue cloud.”

Ravi grinned. “That’s brave and creative!”

Tim’s eyes twinkled. “When we have a problem, we can use art to help. We can change a mistake into something new.” He tapped his shell. Tap-tap. “That is creative-expression.”

Teacher Lina rang a tiny bell. Ting! “Artists, it is sharing time,” she said. Everyone held up their art.

Bee said, “My pink circles are happy bubbles. They pop-pop with joy.” She giggled.

Zuzu said, “My stripes and stars show who I am. I am different, and that is special.”

Ravi said, “My red volcano is big energy. When I feel mad, I paint it out instead of shouting.”

Momo said, “My rocket flies past a blue cloud. I used my words and waited my turn.”

Tim held up his blue waves and yellow sun. “This is calm and warm. My art shows my inside feelings.”

Teacher Lina clapped. “Our room is full of colors and kindness,” she said.

Tim turned to the friends. “Today we learned to:
- Use words to ask and wait our turn.
- Use boundary words like ‘That’s mine right now.’
- Breathe when we feel mad.
- Turn mistakes into art.
- Share our different styles with pride.”

Everyone nodded. The art room smelled like fruit paint and felt safe and bright. The sun slipped lower, making gold on the floor.

Momo waved. “Thank you, Tim,” he said. “You are wise.”

Tim chuckled. “You are wise too,” he said. “We all are, when we listen to our hearts and let our art speak.”

They cleaned their brushes, washed their hands, and left their pictures to dry. The colors shone. The feelings smiled.

The End.
