# Pippa Parrot’s Busy Basement Day

Pippa was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of sunny yellow on her wings. She lived with a family who loved to sing and build things. One morning, Pippa fluttered down the stairs to the busy basement. The basement hummed and buzzed. The washing machine rumbled. The dryer whooshed warm air. Tools clicked and clacked in a drawer. It smelled like clean soap and wood.

“Today, I do things by myself,” Pippa chirped, puffing up her chest. “I can try. I can learn.”

Pippa hopped onto the soft rug and stretched her wings. “Flap, flap!” she said. She marched her little parrot feet in a circle. “Step, step!” The floor felt cool and smooth under her toes. She liked the way her feet made tiny taps.

On a shelf sat a small red box of buttons and ribbons. Pippa loved to make art. She tilted her head. “Can I reach it?” she wondered. Up above her, the box looked high and shiny.

Pippa took a deep breath. “I can try,” she said to herself. She moved a small stool with a gentle push. “Scoot, scoot,” went the stool on the floor. Pippa stepped up carefully. Her heart beat fast. Thump, thump. She spread her wings for balance.

“Hello, box,” Pippa whispered. “I can do this.”

She reached out. Her feathers brushed the shelf. The stool wobbled a little. Pippa’s tummy felt fluttery like tiny butterflies. “Stop, wobble,” she said firmly. “I am steady.” She bent her knees and stood tall. Tap! Her beak nudged the box closer. She used her wing to slide it down into her claws.

“I did it!” Pippa chirped, beaming. She hopped down. The box felt smooth and cool. Pippa’s chest felt warm and proud. “I am brave. I am careful. I am me.”

She opened the box and smiled at the colors. Red buttons. Blue ribbons. Gold string. “Let’s make!” she said. She laid out a square of cardboard from the recycling bin. She dabbed glue with her beak and patted it with her wing. It smelled like sweet paste. She stuck ribbons in loops and buttons in lines. Click, click went the buttons. Swish, swish went the ribbons.

“Beautiful,” Pippa said softly. “This art is my own.”

Across the basement, a small ball rolled away under a table. “Oh!” Pippa cried. She loved that bouncy blue ball. It made a happy boing sound. Pippa wanted to get it by herself.

She crouched down. The table was low and dusty. She could smell the wood and a little laundry soap in the air. “I can ask for space,” Pippa told herself. She cleared her throat. “Excuse me, boxes,” she said to the stack by the table. “I need room.” She used her wing to slide a light box a tiny bit. “Push, push,” she said. She moved slowly and safely.

Then Pippa lay on her tummy and stretched her wing. “Almost,” she whispered. She tried again. “I can try one more time.” Her feathers brushed the cool floor. Her claws tickled. She reached and tapped the ball. Boing! It bounced closer. Pippa giggled. “Come here, little ball!” She tapped again and pulled it out with a gentle scoop.

“I did it by myself,” Pippa said. She held the ball and felt strong. She tossed it up a little. “Boing!” It bounced back into her wing. She laughed and did a happy march. “Step, step, step!”

Just then, the dryer buzzed. Bzzzz! Pippa jumped. “That is loud,” she said, covering her ears with her wings. She took a deep breath in and out. “I am okay. I can say what I need.”

Mama came down the stairs with a basket. “Hi, Pippa,” Mama said with a smile. “You look busy.”

“I am busy and brave,” Pippa replied. “The dryer is loud. I need it softer, please.”

“Thank you for telling me,” Mama said. She closed the laundry room door partway. The sound got softer. “Is that better?”

“Yes,” said Pippa. “Thank you. I used my words.”

Mama looked at the art on the floor. “You made this by yourself?”

Pippa nodded proudly. “I planned it. I moved the stool carefully. I asked the boxes for space. I reached my ball. I did my art. I said what I needed.” She puffed up, feeling tall inside.

“That is wonderful,” Mama said. “You were careful, you were strong, and you were kind with your words.”

Pippa felt happy from her beak to her tail. She flapped her wings in a little dance. “Flap, flap! Step, step!” The basement felt bright and friendly now.

She picked up her artwork and held it to the light. The ribbons shone. The buttons gleamed. “This is my independent art,” she said. “I can try new things. I can do things by myself. I can ask for help when I need it.”

Mama clapped softly. “That is a great lesson.”

Pippa nodded. “I am independent. I am assertive. I am proud of me.”

She placed her art on the shelf where she could see it. Then she cuddled her ball and took one more deep breath. The room smelled like soap and warm air. The tools were quiet. The dryer hummed gently.

Pippa smiled. “Today, I learned to try, to move safely, to speak up, and to believe in me.”

The End.
