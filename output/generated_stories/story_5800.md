# Gigi’s Garage Games

In a sunny little town, a thoughtful giraffe named Gigi loved to tinker in her garage workshop. The workshop smelled like fresh wood and orange soap. Sunlight made dusty sparkles in the air. Tools hung on hooks. A soft radio played a gentle drum rhythm from faraway lands.

Gigi hummed and stretched her long neck to a high shelf. “Today,” she said, tapping her hoof, “I will make sports for everyone.”

She pulled out bright cones, soft balls, and a roll of colorful tape. She laid out a big rubber mat that felt squishy under her hooves. She drew lines with chalk. Swish, swish! The chalk dust tickled her nose. “Hee-choo!” she sneezed, and giggled.

Knock-knock!

Gigi opened the garage door. Warm air whooshed in. Friends peeked inside: a tiny turtle named Tiko, a bouncy kangaroo named Kiri, and a careful panda named Poe. Behind them came a friendly kid named Lina, rolling a soccer ball with her foot.

“Hi, Gigi!” they all said.

“Welcome to Gigi’s Garage Games!” Gigi announced. “We will learn about sports today. Sports help our bodies move and our hearts be kind.”

Kiri hopped high. “I love jumping!”

Tiko tucked in his head. “I am slow,” he said softly.

Poe rubbed his paws together. “I get nervous.”

Lina smiled. “I want to try new games.”

Gigi nodded. “We will try different sports, from places all around our big world. We will go at our own speed. We will cheer for each other.”

She set four stations.

Station One had a soft soccer goal made from pool noodles. The net smelled like clean rope. “Soccer is played in many countries,” Gigi explained. “In Spain, Kenya, Brazil, and so many more.”

Lina placed the soccer ball on the mat. “Pass, please!” she called.

Gigi tapped the ball gently to Tiko. The ball rolled slowly. Tiko nudged it with his shell, tiny push, tiny smile. “I can do it,” he whispered.

Kiri kicked next, but whoosh! Her strong foot sent the ball zooming past the goal. It bonked a paint can. Clonk!

“Oh no,” Kiri gasped. Her ears drooped.

“It’s okay,” Gigi said. She patted Kiri’s shoulder. “We learn by trying. Let’s aim soft.” They all tried again, slower. This time, the ball slid into the noodle goal. Thunk! Everyone clapped. Clap-clap-clap!

Station Two was a jump rope line with ribbons that smelled like fresh laundry. “Jump rope is loved in many places,” Gigi said. “In China, in Ghana, in the USA.”

Kiri’s eyes shined. “I can jump!” She bounced in a steady rhythm. Thump-thump-thump.

Poe watched, breathing deep. “I can try small jumps.” He lifted his paws and stepped over the rope. Plop. He smiled. “That felt nice.”

Tiko looked at the rope and shook his head. “Too fast.”

Gigi flipped the rope into a gentle wave on the floor. “Sea turtle jumps,” she said. “Slow and smooth.” Tiko stepped over the wavy rope, one careful foot at a time. Step. Pause. Step. “I did it!” he cheered.

Station Three had a basket of soft beanbags and a hoop taped low to the wall. The beanbags smelled like warm corn. “Basketball is played in Canada, Japan, Nigeria, and more,” Gigi said.

Lina tossed a beanbag. Swish! “That sound is happy,” she laughed.

Poe missed his first toss and hugged his beanbag tight. “I feel worried.”

Gigi knelt so her eyes met his. “We can try again. Sports are for practice, not perfect.” Poe tried once more. The beanbag went plop into the hoop. His eyes sparkled.

Station Four had a tiny cricket bat, a soft foam ball, and a small wicket made from clothespins. “Cricket is loved in India, England, Jamaica, and many other places,” Gigi shared.

Kiri swung too hard and spun in a circle. “Whoops!” She giggled, dizzy.

“Let’s try a gentle tap,” Gigi coached. Lina rolled the ball slowly. Kiri tapped. Tick! The ball rolled through the wicket. Everyone cheered, and Tiko did a little shell-wiggle dance.

After all the stations, Gigi rang a little bell. Ding-ding! “Time for our big team game,” she said. “We will mix our ideas to make a new sport. We will call it Garage Ball!”

They set rules together:
- Kick the ball to start like soccer.
- Jump over a ribbon like jump rope.
- Toss a beanbag through the hoop for bonus points.
- Tap the foam ball through the wicket to finish.

“Ready?” Gigi asked.

“Ready!” they all shouted.

The music on the radio changed to a cheerful beat. Lina kicked the ball. Tap! Tiko moved it along slowly. Kiri leapt the ribbon. Thump! Poe tossed a beanbag. Swish! Gigi tapped the foam ball. Tick! It rolled through the wicket, and the garage filled with happy shouts.

“We did it as a team,” Poe said, proud.

“I went slow, and that was okay,” Tiko said.

“I tried again when I missed,” Kiri added.

Lina wiped a little sweat and laughed. “Sports make us strong and kind.”

Gigi smiled big. “Sports from around the world teach us to move our bodies, to listen, to take turns, and to accept each other. Fast, slow, tall, small—everyone can play.”

They cleaned up together. The garage smelled like orange soap again. The sun made the tools sparkle. Gigi hung the jump ropes and folded the mat. “Thank you for playing,” she said. “Tomorrow we can learn a game from another country.”

“Count me in!” Lina said.

“Me too!” Tiko, Kiri, and Poe chimed.

Gigi looked at her friends and felt warm inside. “Today’s lesson,” she said gently, “is that sports are for everyone, and everyone belongs.”

The End.
