# Bunny Beats in the Basement Lab

Thump-thump. Thump-thump. In a cozy basement lab, a determined rabbit named Rilla tightened her little goggles and wiggled her whiskers. The lab smelled like warm wood and orange soap. Soft blue lights blinked on the walls. A tiny radio sat on a shelf, quiet and waiting.

“I want to learn to dance,” Rilla said, standing on a square mat she had taped with bright yellow lines. “I can do it. I can try. I can try again.” Her voice was small but brave.

Rilla had a dance plan. She had drawn it in crayon and taped it to the table:
- Warm up
- Wiggle
- Step-step
- Share a smile
- Try again

She pressed the radio. Click. Music bubbled out—plink, plunk, boom. It felt like lemon fizz in her ears.

“Okay, feet,” Rilla whispered. “Step-step.” She hopped to the beat. Her ears bounced. Her tail twitched. She spun—too fast!

Bump!

Rilla’s foot tapped a stack of soft foam blocks. They tumbled—thud, thud, thud. “Oh!” Her nose wiggled. Her cheeks felt hot. For a tiny moment, she wanted to stop. Her tummy made a fluttery feeling.

Rilla put a paw on her heart. “I can try again,” she said. “I am brave. I am kind. I can dance.”

She cleaned up the blocks—stack, stack, stack. She looked at her plan. “Warm up.” She stretched her legs and rolled her shoulders. She took a deep breath. In. Out. The air smelled like vanilla glue. Her body felt ready.

“Wiggle!” Rilla sang. She wiggled her whiskers, her ears, and her toes. The music went bump-bump-bump. Rilla smiled. “Step-step.” She hopped to the yellow lines. Left foot. Right foot. Tap. Tap. “Share a smile,” she giggled, smiling at the mirror. Her smile made her feel strong.

The door creaked. Eeeeee. Two friends peeked in—Tess the turtle and Pip the mouse. “Hi, Rilla,” said Tess in a soft, slow voice. “We heard the music.”

“It smells nice down here,” Pip squeaked. “Like oranges!”

Rilla’s ears perked up. “Hi! I am practicing my dance. I want to be brave. I want to do my routine.” She pointed to the crayon plan. “Do you want to watch? Or help?”

Tess blinked. “We can cheer,” she said. Pip nodded fast. “We can clap!”

Rilla felt a warm glow in her belly. “Thank you. I will try my best,” she said. She pressed the radio again. The beat rolled like tiny drums. Rilla began:

“Warm up.” Stretch. Reach. Wiggle.

“Step-step.” Tap, tap. Hop, hop.

“Spin.” She turned slowly this time. Swish. Her ears whooshed like soft brush. She kept her feet on the yellow lines. She did not bump the blocks. She landed with a gentle thump.

“Ta-da!” She bowed. Tess clapped. Pip squeaked, “Yay!”

Rilla felt proud and calm. “I did it,” she whispered. Then she looked at her friends. “Would you like to try? We can share the mat.”

Tess looked shy. “I am slow,” she said. “Dancing is fast.”

Rilla shook her head kindly. “Dancing can be slow. Dancing can be you.” She drew a big square with chalk for Tess. “This is your dance space.”

Pip tugged his tail. “I am small. Will I get lost?”

Rilla smiled. “We can make a tiny square for you.” She drew a little box and put a soft scarf on it. “This is your stage.”

They all took a breath. Rilla taught the steps, one by one. “Warm up,” she said. “Roll your shoulders. Wiggle your toes.” Tess rolled, roll, roll. Pip wiggled, wiggle, wiggle. “Step-step,” Rilla said. “Left, right. Tap, tap.”

Tess moved slow and smooth. Pip moved quick and light. Rilla moved steady and brave. The music made their hearts feel like happy bubbles. The floor felt cool under their feet. The lights blinked like friendly stars.

Suddenly, the radio made a crackle and stopped. Silence. Pip’s whiskers drooped. Tess pulled into her shell a little.

Rilla took a good breath. “It is okay,” she said. “We can make our own music.” She patted her chest. Thump-thump. “Heart beat.” She clapped her paws. Clap, clap. She stomped gently. Stomp, stomp. “Listen,” she said. “We can dance to us.”

Tess peeked out. “Clap,” she tried. Pip squeaked, “Stomp!” Soon the basement lab was full of soft beats—clap, stomp, thump. Rilla led: “Warm up. Wiggle. Step-step.” They danced together, using their own sounds. They laughed. They felt brave. They felt strong.

When the dance was done, Rilla shared her water bottle lid by lid. “Sip for you, Tess. Sip for you, Pip,” she said. She passed orange slices from a small bowl. “Share, share,” they sang. The orange smell was bright and happy.

Tess smiled. “You kept trying,” she said. “You were brave.”

Pip nodded. “You helped us dance our way,” he said. “And you shared!”

Rilla hugged her friends. “Dancing is practice,” she said. “Dancing is courage. Dancing is sharing. I can learn. You can learn. We can help each other.”

They wrote a new plan together on the table:
- Warm up
- Wiggle
- Step-step
- Share a smile
- Help a friend
- Try again

Rilla taped it to the wall. The blue lights blinked. The lab felt safe and bright.

“Tomorrow, we dance again,” Rilla said.

“Tomorrow!” Tess and Pip cheered.

That night, as the radio sat quiet, Rilla whispered, “I am a dancer.” She felt the steady thump of her heart. She knew the beat lived in her.

The End.
