# Melody's Musical Gift

On a sunny morning, a little yellow bird named Melody perched on Mrs. Chen's peaceful front porch. The wooden railing felt warm under her tiny feet, and the sweet smell of morning flowers filled the air.

"Tweet, tweet, la-la-la!" sang Melody, practicing her beautiful songs. She loved to sing more than anything in the whole world.

Just then, she heard a soft sniffling sound. Looking down, Melody saw a small boy sitting on the porch steps with tears in his eyes.

"Hello there," chirped Melody gently. "My name is Melody. What's wrong?"

The little boy looked up, surprised to hear the bird talking. "I'm Tommy," he said, wiping his nose. "I'm sad because I can't do anything special. My sister can paint pretty pictures, and my brother can build tall towers with blocks. But I can't do anything good."

Melody's heart felt heavy hearing Tommy's words. She fluttered down to sit beside him on the warm wooden step.

"Oh, Tommy," she said kindly, "everyone has special talents! Sometimes we just need to practice and try new things to find them."

"But I already tried painting," Tommy sighed. "My pictures look like messy scribbles."

Melody thought for a moment, then had a wonderful idea. "Would you like me to teach you something new? I could show you how to make music!"

Tommy's eyes brightened a little. "Really? You would do that?"

"Of course!" Melody chirped excitedly. "Music is all around us. Listen carefully."

Melody began to tap her tiny feet on the wooden porch. *Tap-tap-tap, tap-tap-tap.* "Hear that rhythm? Now you try!"

Tommy used his hands to copy the pattern on the step. *Pat-pat-pat, pat-pat-pat.* The sound echoed softly in the morning air.

"That's wonderful!" Melody sang. "Now let's add some humming. Like this: *Hmm-hmm-hmm.*"

Tommy tried humming along. At first, his voice was shaky, but Melody encouraged him to keep going.

"You're doing great!" she chirped. "Music isn't about being perfect. It's about having fun and expressing your feelings."

They practiced together for a while. Melody showed Tommy how to clap different rhythms and hum simple melodies. She taught him that he could make music with anything - tapping spoons on pots, shaking a jar with beans inside, or even just humming while he walked.

"The best part about music," Melody explained, "is that you can practice a little bit every day, and you'll get better and better!"

Soon, Tommy was smiling and laughing as he created his own musical patterns. The sad feelings had flown away like clouds on a windy day.

"Thank you, Melody!" Tommy said, giving the little bird a gentle pat. "I never knew I could make music. Will you teach me more tomorrow?"

"I would love to!" Melody replied, her yellow feathers shining in the sunlight. "Remember, Tommy, everyone has special gifts. Sometimes we just need a friend to help us discover them."

As Tommy ran inside to show his family his new musical skills, Melody felt warm and happy. She had used her own special talent - singing - to help someone else find theirs.

From that day on, Tommy visited the front porch every morning to practice music with his new friend. And Melody learned that sharing her gifts with others made her songs even more beautiful.

The End.
