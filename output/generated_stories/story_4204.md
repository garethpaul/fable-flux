# Finn's Big Brush Practice

Finn the fox kit wiggled his fluffy orange tail as he stood on his tippy-toes at the bathroom sink. The white porcelain felt cool under his tiny paws, and the morning sunlight made everything sparkle clean.

"Today I'm going to brush my teeth ALL BY MYSELF!" Finn announced proudly to his reflection in the mirror.

Mama Fox smiled from the doorway. "That's wonderful, Finn! But remember, your sister Faye needs to brush her teeth too. What should we do?"

Finn's ears perked up as he thought hard. His little sister Faye was still sleeping, but she would wake up soon and need the bathroom too.

"We can take turns!" Finn said, his bushy tail swishing with excitement. "I'll practice first, then Faye can have her turn. That's fair!"

"Very good thinking," Mama Fox said, handing him his small blue toothbrush. "Let's make a plan. First, you'll wet your brush. Then add toothpaste. What comes next?"

Finn concentrated, holding his toothbrush carefully. "Then I brush! Up and down, round and round!" He demonstrated in the air, making swooshing sounds.

The cool water felt refreshing as it splashed gently over his toothbrush. Finn squeezed a tiny dot of minty toothpaste onto the bristles - just like Mama had taught him. The fresh mint smell tickled his little black nose.

"Here I go!" Finn began brushing his small white teeth. At first, his paw felt wobbly. The toothbrush seemed big in his grip.

"This is harder than I thought," Finn mumbled through the foam.

"That's okay," Mama encouraged. "Remember, practice makes everything easier. Try moving more slowly."

Finn took a deep breath and tried again. Slowly, carefully, he brushed each tooth. The bristles felt funny and tickly, but he kept going. Up and down on the front teeth, round and round on the back ones.

Just then, little Faye padded into the bathroom, rubbing her sleepy eyes.

"Finn's brushing!" she said, watching with big curious eyes.

"Yes, and when he's finished, it will be your turn," Mama explained. "Finn is practicing very hard."

Finn felt proud hearing that. Even though his paw was getting tired, he kept practicing his brushing technique. The minty foam made his mouth feel tingly and fresh.

"Almost done!" Finn announced. He rinsed his mouth with cool water, making little spitting sounds that made Faye giggle.

"My turn! My turn!" Faye bounced excitedly.

Finn wiped his mouth with his fluffy orange paw and stepped down from the sink. "Here you go, Faye! I practiced really hard, and now it's fair for you to have your turn."

As Faye climbed up to the sink with her own tiny toothbrush, Finn watched proudly. "Practicing made me better at brushing," he told Mama. "And taking turns made sure Faye could practice too!"

"You're absolutely right," Mama Fox said, nuzzling both her kits. "When we practice, we get better at things. And when we take turns and make fair plans, everyone gets to learn and grow."

Finn's teeth felt squeaky clean, and his heart felt warm and happy. He had practiced something new, been fair to his sister, and learned that planning ahead made everything work better.

"Tomorrow I want to practice even more!" Finn declared, his tail wagging with joy.

The End.
