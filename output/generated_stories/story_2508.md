# Daisy's Sharing Kitchen

Little Daisy the deer fawn stood on her tippy-hooves in Mama's warm kitchen. The morning sun streamed through the yellow curtains, making everything glow like gold. The kitchen smelled wonderful - like fresh bread and sweet honey.

"Oh my!" said Daisy, looking at the big table covered with yummy treats. There were blueberry muffins, strawberry jam, and a pitcher of creamy milk. Her tummy rumbled loudly. "Grumble, grumble!"

Mama Deer had made all this food for the forest picnic today. But first, Daisy needed to wash her hooves at the sink. The warm, soapy water felt bubbly and tickly between her tiny toes.

"Clean hooves make for happy eating," Mama always said.

Just then, Daisy heard a tiny "tap, tap, tap" at the kitchen window. She looked up and saw her friend Benny the bunny. His nose was pressed against the glass, and his eyes looked very sad.

"Hi, Benny!" called Daisy, opening the window. "What's wrong?"

"Oh, Daisy," sniffled Benny. "I'm so hungry, but I forgot my lunch for the picnic. My tummy feels so empty and rumbly."

Daisy looked at all the delicious food on the table, then back at her hungry friend. Her heart felt warm and fluttery, like butterflies dancing inside.

"Wait right here!" said Daisy with a big smile.

She carefully climbed onto a chair and reached for the basket of muffins. They were still warm and smelled like summer berries. Daisy picked the biggest, most beautiful muffin - the one she had been saving for herself.

"Here, Benny!" she said, passing the muffin through the window. "This one is for you!"

Benny's eyes grew wide and sparkly. "Really? For me? But Daisy, this is YOUR special muffin!"

"That's okay," said Daisy, feeling happy and proud. "Friends share with friends. That's what makes the food taste even better!"

Soon, more forest friends appeared at the window. There was Rosie the raccoon, who had dropped her sandwich in the creek. And little Pip the mouse, whose lunch got blown away by the wind.

Daisy's imagination began to sparkle like stars. "I know!" she said excitedly. "Let's make this the most wonderful sharing kitchen ever!"

She washed her hooves again - splash, splash - and got to work. Daisy shared her strawberry jam with Rosie, spreading it on crackers with a wooden spoon. She poured creamy milk into acorn caps for Pip and his mouse family.

"This is the best kitchen in the whole forest!" giggled Rosie.

"And the most generous!" added Pip, his whiskers twitching with joy.

When Mama Deer returned, she found Daisy surrounded by happy friends, all sharing the picnic food together. The kitchen was filled with laughter and the sound of contented munching.

"Oh, Daisy," said Mama, her eyes twinkling with pride. "You've learned something very special today."

"What's that, Mama?" asked Daisy, licking jam from her lips.

"When we share what we have, we don't have less - we have more! More friends, more joy, and more love in our hearts."

Daisy felt warm and glowing inside, warmer than the sunny kitchen, warmer than fresh bread. She had discovered that giving to others made her feel happier than keeping everything for herself.

From that day on, Daisy's kitchen became known throughout the forest as the most generous, cleanest, and most magical place to share a meal with friends.

The End.
