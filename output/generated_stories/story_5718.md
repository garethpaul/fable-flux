# Joey Jumps Into Helping

Joey the kangaroo joey woke up to warm sunshine tickling his nose. He stretched his springy legs and peeked outside. The sunny backyard sparkled. Flowers smiled. A soft breeze carried the sweet smell of oranges and wet grass.

“Good morning, backyard!” Joey chirped. He bounced out the door, his little feet thumping. Thump-thump! Thump-thump!

Joey loved to explore by himself. He liked to try new things and make good choices. Today, he wanted to have a daily adventure. He packed his tiny pouch with a water bottle, a small snack, and a little cloth bag. “I will be careful and kind,” he told himself. “I can do it!”

In the backyard, Joey heard, “Chirp, chirp!” A bright blue bird hopped on the fence. “Hello, Joey!” the bird sang. “I am Asha. I flew here from far away. Where I live, our trees are tall and old. We keep them safe.”

Joey’s eyes grew wide. “Hi, Asha! I want to keep our backyard safe too.”

Asha tilted her head. “Let’s look around.”

They bounded and fluttered past the orange tree. Suddenly, Joey wrinkled his nose. “What’s that smell?” he asked.

Asha pointed with her wing. “Oh no. I see a shiny can and a crinkly bag on the ground.”

Joey felt a little frown. “Trash makes animals sad,” he said softly. “It can hurt the earth.”

Just then, a gentle tortoise named Tío Tomás shuffled by. His shell had tiny painted flowers from a festival his family loved. “Buenos días, amigos,” he greeted. “In my family, we take our time and take care of our home.”

Joey nodded. “We will take care of our backyard too!”

Joey opened his little cloth bag. “I brought this to help,” he said proudly. He picked up the shiny can. “Clink!” Asha picked up the crinkly bag with her beak and dropped it in. “Crrrinkle!” Tío Tomás nudged a paper cup toward them. “Scoot, scoot.”

Joey looked around more. He saw a dry plant drooping by the fence. Its leaves were limp. “Oh no,” Joey whispered. “This plant is thirsty.”

“We can help,” Asha chirped.

“I can carry water,” Joey said. He hopped to the spigot and turned it slowly. “Drip… drop… drip.” He filled his little watering can. The metal felt cool in his paws. He carried it carefully, one hop at a time. Thump… thump… thump.

“You are doing it by yourself,” Tío Tomás said. “That is independence.”

Joey smiled. “I am careful. I am kind.” He poured gently. “Pour… pour.” The soil drank. The plant lifted one leaf, like a tiny wave.

Asha sang a soft tune. “In my home, we share water when the days are hot.”

“In my family,” Tío Tomás added, “we reuse things. Mira, look!” He showed a small tin with holes. “A friend taught me to make a seed shaker.”

Joey’s ears perked up. “Can you teach me too?”

“Claro,” Tío Tomás said with a smile. He sprinkled seeds with the tin. “Shake-shake!” Little seeds dotted the soil.

Joey tried. “Shake-shake!” He planted a line of wildflower seeds by the fence. He patted the dirt with his paws. “Soft, soft.” The earth smelled rich and warm.

Asha flapped her wings. “Where I come from, we thank the earth in many languages.” She sang, “Gracias, earth. Dhanyavaad, earth. Thank you, earth.”

Joey hummed along. “Thank you, earth,” he whispered. He felt his chest glow with pride.

They kept exploring. They sorted the trash they found. Joey made two piles: “Recycling” and “Rubbish.” He read the tiny pictures on the bin lids. “The can goes here. The bag goes there.” He worked slowly and carefully. He liked figuring it out. He liked being a helper.

A small breeze brushed Joey’s cheeks. He closed his eyes and listened. He heard leaves rustle. He heard bees buzz. “Buzz-buzz.” He smelled orange blossoms and wet dirt. The backyard felt happy again.

Then Joey heard a soft cry. “Peep… peep…” A ladybug sat on a leaf, turned the wrong way and stuck in a cobweb.

Joey crouched low. “It’s okay, little friend,” he said gently. “I will help.” He used a dry leaf like a tiny slide. “Swoosh.” The ladybug climbed on and rode down, landing safely on his paw. It tickled. “Hee-hee!” Joey giggled.

“Thank you,” said the ladybug in a teeny voice. “You are very kind.”

Joey’s heart felt big. “We care for our earth and for each other,” he said.

Asha chirped with joy. “Today, you learned to notice, to sort, to water, and to plant.”

Tío Tomás nodded. “And you did it with your own paws. You were patient, and you listened.”

Joey looked at the tidy yard, the happy plant, and the seeds tucked in the soil. He felt calm and strong. “I can care for the earth every day,” he said. “I can pick up litter. I can save water. I can plant and share.”

Asha puffed her feathers. “And we can celebrate our different ways. We all bring special ideas.”

Joey bounced high, his ears flapping. “I will remind my friends too,” he cheered. “We can help together!”

The sun yawned and slid lower in the sky. The backyard glowed golden. Joey packed his empty cloth bag. He waved to Asha and Tío Tomás. “Thank you for teaching me,” he said.

“Gracias,” Tío Tomás replied.

“Shukriya,” Asha sang.

Joey smiled. “Thank you,” he said, feeling the warm earth under his feet and a happy beat in his heart. “Today I learned: When I care for the earth, the earth cares for me.”

The End.
