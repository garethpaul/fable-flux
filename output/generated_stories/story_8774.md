# Benny Beaver's Bathroom Busy Day

Benny Beaver was the busiest beaver in the whole forest! He loved to build things with his strong teeth and tiny paws. But today, Benny had a very important job to do - he needed to get ready for his friend's birthday party.

"Oh my!" said Benny, looking at himself in the bathroom mirror. His brown fur was messy, his teeth needed brushing, and his paws were muddy from building his dam all morning. "I need to hurry, hurry, hurry!"

Benny tried to do everything at once. He turned on the water faucet with one paw while reaching for his toothbrush with the other. SPLASH! Water went everywhere! Then he tried to brush his fur and wash his face at the same time. 

"Ouch!" Benny bumped his head on the medicine cabinet. His toothbrush fell into the sink with a CLATTER, and soap bubbles got in his eyes.

"This isn't working!" Benny said, sitting down on the fluffy bathroom rug. He felt frustrated and a little sad. "I'm making such a mess, and I'm still not clean!"

Just then, Mama Beaver knocked gently on the door. "Benny, dear, is everything alright? I heard some splashing and bumping."

Benny took a deep breath. He decided to be honest about his problem. "Mama, I'm trying to get ready fast, but I keep making mistakes. I can't do everything at the same time!"

Mama Beaver opened the door and smiled warmly. "Oh, my busy little beaver! May I help you learn something special?"

"Yes, please!" said Benny, wiping soap bubbles from his whiskers.

"The secret," said Mama Beaver, "is to do one thing at a time. That's called coordination - when we make our body parts work together nicely, step by step."

Mama Beaver showed Benny how to prepare by gathering everything he needed first. They laid out his toothbrush, soap, a soft towel, and his special fur brush on the counter.

"Now," said Mama Beaver, "let's start with washing your paws. Use both paws together, but just focus on this one job."

Benny carefully washed his paws, using both of them to rub the soap gently. The warm water felt nice, and he didn't spill a drop!

"Great coordination!" cheered Mama Beaver. "Now, what's next?"

"Brushing my teeth!" said Benny proudly. He picked up his toothbrush with one paw and squeezed a tiny bit of toothpaste with the other. He brushed slowly and carefully, making sure his teeth were sparkly clean.

Next, Benny brushed his fur with smooth, gentle strokes. His brown fur became soft and fluffy. Finally, he washed his face with a warm, soapy washcloth.

"Look at you!" said Mama Beaver. "You're all clean and handsome!"

Benny looked in the mirror and smiled his biggest smile. He felt proud and happy. "I did it! And I didn't make a mess!"

"You learned that doing one thing at a time helps your body work together better," said Mama Beaver. "That's much more peaceful than rushing and bumping around."

Benny hugged his mama tight. "Thank you for teaching me about coordination! Now I'm ready for the party, and I feel calm and happy too!"

From that day on, whenever Benny had many things to do, he remembered to slow down, prepare what he needed, and do one thing at a time. His coordination got better and better, and he felt proud of his peaceful solutions to busy problems.

The End.
