# The Brave Little Breakfast Song

The dining room was bright and warm. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. On the table, there were bowls, cups, and a plate of colorful fruit. A singing bird named Pip fluttered to the back of a chair and chirped a happy tune.

“Good morning!” sang Pip. “Tweet, tweet, tweet! Is it time to eat?”

At the table sat Mia, her brother Leo, and Grandma. Their tummies rumbled like little drums. “It is time,” said Grandma, smiling. “But we will take turns. Everyone gets a turn to choose a healthy food.”

Pip loved to sing during breakfast. But today, Pip felt a tiny flutter in his chest. His heart beat fast. He looked at the fruit. He looked at the spoon. He looked at Mia and Leo. Pip was nervous to speak up. He wanted to help, but he felt small.

Mia reached for a banana. “I choose this!” she said.

Leo bounced in his chair. “I want juice! I want it now!”

Grandma held up her hand. “We take turns, Leo. First Mia, then you, then Pip will sing us a choice.”

Pip’s feathers tingled. “Me?” he peeped.

“Yes,” Grandma said gently. “You are brave, little bird. You can help us make good choices.”

Mia peeled her banana. The peel made a soft, squeaky sound. The banana was sweet and creamy. “Mmm,” said Mia. “This is yummy and gives me energy.”

Leo’s turn came. He grabbed the juice. The glass was cool and smooth in his hand. “My turn!” he shouted.

Grandma nodded. “Slow and steady. Pour a little. Feel the weight. Good job.” The juice splashed like tiny raindrops into Leo’s cup.

Pip took a deep breath. He puffed his chest and tried to be brave. He looked at the plate of fruit: red strawberries, green kiwi slices, and orange carrots. He sang, soft at first, then strong and bright.

“Red, green, orange too,
Crunch and munch for me and you.
Sweet berries, crunchy sticks,
Healthy bites are clever picks!”

Mia clapped. “I love that song!”

Leo giggled. “Crunchy sticks!”

Grandma smiled. “Carrots are crunchy sticks. They help our eyes see well. And strawberries give us vitamins.”

Just then, Leo reached across the table for the strawberries. His hand bumped Mia’s bowl. The spoon fell with a clink. Juice wobbled in his cup. “Oops!”

Pip fluttered up, startled. His wings made a tiny whoosh. Leo frowned. “I want the strawberries now.”

Grandma’s voice was calm. “We are learning to take turns. Watch, listen, and wait. Waiting is brave too.”

Leo grumbled. Pip could feel the heat of the sun on his feathers. He remembered the lesson. He hummed a gentle tune.

“Wait, wait, patience grows,
Healthy bodies, calm hearts, those!
We take turns and share the plate,
Brave friends can kindly wait.”

Leo took a breath. He put his hands in his lap. “Okay. I will wait,” he said in a small voice.

Mia finished her banana and passed the plate. “Your turn now, Leo.”

Leo took three strawberries. They were cool and juicy. “Yum!” Red juice tickled his tongue. “They taste like tiny sunshine.”

Pip felt proud. He had used his brave voice to help. But then a new problem came. On the table sat a big plate of cookies. They smelled sweet, like vanilla. Leo reached for a cookie. Mia reached too. Their hands bumped again.

Pip’s tummy fluttered. He liked cookies. But he remembered the fruit and carrots. He remembered energy for flying and playing. He was brave again.

Pip sang a new verse:

“Cookies later, lunch or treat,
First we choose our growing food to eat.
Colors bright on every plate,
Fuel our fun—now that is great!”

Grandma nodded. “Yes. We can have a cookie after our healthy foods. Our bodies need good fuel first.”

Mia and Leo looked at the colorful plate. “I will try a carrot stick,” said Mia. She crunched. “It’s loud!” Crunch, crunch.

Leo tried kiwi. “It’s green and tickly!” The tiny seeds felt bumpy on his tongue.

Pip pecked at a blueberry. Pop! It burst sweet and purple. He sang, cheerful and strong:

“Blue, red, green, and orange too,
Healthy foods help me and you.
We wait our turns, we bravely try,
With yummy bites, we soar and fly!”

Soon their bellies felt happy and full. The room smelled like fruit and warm toast. The sunlight made the plates shine. Grandma poured a little milk. “You all were brave and patient,” she said. “And you chose healthy foods.”

Leo smiled. “I waited my turn!”

Mia grinned. “I tried a new food!”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “And I sang my brave song.”

Grandma passed a small cookie to each of them. “A sweet treat after good nutrition.”

They nibbled, one careful bite at a time. It tasted extra special because they had waited and chosen well.

Pip finished with a soft lullaby:

“Brave hearts, kind turns, healthy bites,
Make our days feel bright and light.”

They all hugged. The dining room felt cozy and calm.

Lesson: Healthy foods help our bodies grow strong. Waiting our turn is kind. Trying new foods is brave.

The End.
