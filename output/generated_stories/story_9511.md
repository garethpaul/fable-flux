# Bubbles in the Garden Shed

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with bright, shiny eyes and a smooth, silver-blue back. One sunny morning, Bubbles slid up a little stream that tickled his fins and wiggled right into a backyard. He spotted a small garden shed near some flowers. The shed smelled like fresh wood and leaves, and it had a creaky door that said, “Eee-aaa,” when he nudged it open with his nose.

Inside the shed, there were treasures! Colorful buckets, soft brushes, a watering can, seeds in tiny packets, and a big box of chalk. Sunlight danced through a dusty window, making glittery specks in the air. Bubbles flapped his tail in excitement, and little splashes sprinkled the floor.

“I want to play with everything!” Bubbles squeaked. His voice echoed, “Everything, everything!”

Just then, Mia, a kind little gardener with curly hair, came to the shed. She wore yellow boots and carried a small shovel. “Oh! Hello,” Mia said softly. “You are a dolphin!”

Bubbles grinned. “I am Bubbles! Can I play? Can I use your things? Can I draw a sea picture?” His tail swished, and more tiny droplets pattered like rain.

Mia smiled, but her eyebrows wrinkled a bit. “I like to share,” she said, “but this is my special shed. We need to ask, and we need to be gentle.”

Bubbles felt a flutter in his tummy. He wanted to touch everything right now. His heart beat fast—thump, thump, thump. He looked at the chalk, the watering can, the brushes. His eyes felt big and hot. “I feel wiggly,” he whispered. “I feel excited and impatient.”

Mia nodded. “Thank you for telling me your feelings,” she said. “Let’s take deep breaths. In… and out.” They breathed together. In… and out. The air smelled like mint leaves from a pot on the shelf. Bubbles’ tummy felt calmer, like soft waves instead of stormy ones.

“Now,” Mia said kindly, “we can make a plan. We can play outside with the chalk and water. We will use gentle fins and hands. And we will ask each time before we use something.”

Bubbles blinked. “Ask first. Be gentle. Make a plan,” he repeated. He liked the plan. It felt safe and cozy, like a warm towel.

They carried the chalk and the watering can outside. The garden was full of sounds—bees buzzing, a bird singing, and the gentle drip-drip of the watering can. The grass felt cool under Bubbles’ tummy. The sun warmed his back.

“May I use the blue chalk?” Bubbles asked.

“Yes,” Mia said. “Thank you for asking.”

Bubbles drew a big, happy wave on the patio. Swish, swish, swish. “This looks like home,” he said. He added a smiling fish. Then he paused. His tail twitched. He wanted to use the yellow chalk too. He felt the wiggles again. “I feel excited. I want more colors,” he said.

Mia handed him the yellow. “It’s okay to feel excited,” she said. “We can take turns and keep the chalk on the ground, not in our mouths.” She giggled.

They drew a garden ocean—seaweed shaped like carrot tops, shell-friends that looked like tiny pumpkins, and a giant sun that made the chalk warm and soft. Mia dipped the brush in water and painted swirly waves over the chalk. The colors got brighter and slid together like rainbows. “Oooh,” Bubbles cooed. The brush felt smooth and cool on the patio.

Soon, Tim, the neighbor boy, peeked over the fence. “Can I play?” he asked.

Bubbles remembered the plan. “Yes! We share,” he said. “But we ask, and we are gentle. This is Mia’s shed.”

Tim nodded. “May I use the green chalk?” he asked Mia.

“Yes,” Mia said, smiling.

They all drew together. When Tim reached for the watering can without asking, Bubbles felt a tight pinch in his chest. He remembered the fluttery feeling. He took a breath and spoke kindly. “Tim, I feel worried when things are taken without asking. Please ask Mia first.”

Tim blinked. “Oh. Sorry. Mia, may I use the watering can?”

“Thank you for asking,” Mia said. “Yes, you may.”

Splash, drizzle, swoosh! The water made soft puddles. The chalk pictures looked like they were dancing. Bubbles laughed, a tinkly dolphin laugh that sounded like bubbles popping. He felt proud. He had used his words. He had named his feelings. He had kept the plan.

When they were done, they went back to the shed. Bubbles felt calm and happy. The shed was quiet and smelled like soil and lemon soap. He looked at the shelves. He wanted to put things away to say thank you.

“May I help clean up?” Bubbles asked.

“Yes,” Mia said. “That is generous.”

Bubbles placed the chalk back in the box. Tap, tap, tap. Tim hung the brush. Mia wiped the floor with a cloth—swish, swish. The shed felt neat and safe.

Bubbles turned to Mia and Tim. “Today, I learned something,” he said. “When I feel big feelings, I can breathe. I can use my words. I can ask before I touch. That keeps everyone comfy.”

Mia nodded. “That is emotional awareness,” she said gently. “It means you notice your feelings and other people’s feelings too.”

Bubbles’ heart felt like a warm, steady wave. “Thank you for sharing your shed,” he said.

“Thank you for being careful and kind,” Mia replied.

They went outside one more time to look at their art. The sun made the colors glow. Bees hummed a happy song. The air felt sweet and soft.

Bubbles wiggled his fins. “I will remember our plan,” he said. “Ask first. Be gentle. Make a plan. Share and care.”

Everyone smiled. Their garden ocean shimmered. It was beautiful because they made it together.

The End.
