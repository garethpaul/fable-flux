# Squeaky the Squirrel's Kitchen Clock Plan

Squeaky the squirrel lived by a sunny window in a warm kitchen. The kitchen smelled like cinnamon toast and fresh apples. The stove hummed softly. A little clock tick-tick-ticked on the wall.

One bright morning, Squeaky woke up on a soft oven mitt. “Today I will make a nut cracker picture!” he cheered. He loved art. He also wanted to help Mama Human make lunch. And he needed to sweep up the snack crumbs he had dropped yesterday. There was a lot to do.

Squeaky’s tail flicked with happy energy. He ran in circles on the counter. “Art! Snacks! Helping! Play!” he chirped.

The clock went tick-tick-tick.

Mama Human stirred a pot. “Be safe, little friend,” she said kindly. “Hot things stay hot. Sharp things stay out of tiny paws.”

Squeaky nodded. “Safe paws, yes!”

He grabbed a spoon and tried to stir the soup. “I can help now!” he said.

But the spoon was heavy. The pot was hot. Steam puffed his whiskers. “Yikes!” Squeaky squeaked and jumped back. His heart thumped. “Too hot.”

He tried to start his nut cracker picture next. He spread crunchy shells and shiny leaves on the table. He wanted to make a tree shape. “I will glue them fast!” he said.

But the glue cap was stuck. Squeaky tugged and tugged. The clock kept ticking. He felt worried. “So much to do,” he whispered. “What first?”

He remembered the crumbs. Ants liked crumbs. Squeaky did not want ants in the warm kitchen. He scampered to the crumbs by the toaster. He tried to sweep with a big broom. Whoosh! The broom was taller than he was. It bonked into a chair. A measuring cup fell with a clatter.

Squeaky’s ears drooped. His tail sagged. “I can’t do everything,” he sighed. “I started three things, and none are done.”

Mama Human knelt and smiled. “You are helpful and creative,” she said. “You just need a plan.”

“A plan?” Squeaky blinked. “What is a plan?”

“A plan is a list in time,” Mama said. She touched the clock. Tick-tick. “First this. Then that. We do one safe step at a time.”

Squeaky’s eyes grew bright. “One safe step at a time.” He liked how that sounded. “How do we make a plan?”

Mama put a clean tea towel on the table like a tiny map. She drew three simple pictures with a marker: a crumb, a glue bottle, and a pot. “What should be first?” she asked.

Squeaky sniffed the air. The soup smelled like carrots and peas. The stove hissed. “Safety first,” he said. “No hot pot for me. I can help with things that are not hot.” He pointed to the crumb picture. “I can sweep. That keeps the kitchen clean for the earth and for us. No ants. No waste.”

Mama nodded. “Good critical thinking.”

Squeaky stood tall. “Step one: sweep crumbs.” He looked at the big broom and then at a small hand brush. “The brush is squirrel-sized.” He chose the small brush. It felt smooth in his paws. Sweep, sweep. The crumbs made soft sandy sounds as they went into the dustpan. The floor looked neat.

Squeaky smiled. “Done!” He looked at the clock. Tick-tick. “What is next?”

Mama tapped the picture of the glue. “Art time.”

Squeaky wiggled with joy. “Creative time! But… the cap is stuck.” He thought. “Warm water can loosen lids.” He had seen Mama do that with jam. “But water and glue can be messy,” he said. “Let’s use a towel.”

Mama ran warm water over the cap, wrapped it in the towel, and handed it back. Squeaky twisted gently. Pop! The cap opened. The glue smelled sweet.

He squeezed a tiny dot, then another. “Little dots, not lots,” he sang. He pressed leaves and shells into a tree shape. The table felt cool under his paws. The leaves felt crisp. He added a seed as a bird. “Tweet!” he chirped. His picture looked cheerful and green. “I used things we already had. That is good for the earth,” he said proudly.

The clock ticked kindly on. Squeaky breathed in and out. “Step two: done.”

He looked at the last picture—the pot. “I cannot stir the hot soup,” he said. “But I can help in a safe way.”

“What do you think would be safe?” Mama asked.

Squeaky’s whiskers twitched. “I can set the table!” He picked cloth napkins with little leaf prints. He counted slowly. “One, two.” He placed spoons. Clink, clink. He put a cup by each bowl. He even added a small apple slice on each napkin for a surprise.

Mama tasted the soup and smiled. “Lunch is ready. And the kitchen is tidy. And your art is beautiful.”

Squeaky stood by his picture. His heart felt light. “My plan worked,” he said softly. “First sweep. Then art. Then set the table. One safe step at a time.”

They sat together. The soup was warm and sweet. The apples were crisp. The kitchen felt cozy and bright. Squeaky’s tail curled with joy.

Mama said, “You used time like a friend today.”

Squeaky nodded. “Time helps me do things in order. A plan makes hard things feel small. Safety first. Clean and kind to the earth. And space for art!”

He looked up at the clock. Tick-tick. It sounded like a song now.

Squeaky whispered to himself, “First this, then that, and I can do it.”

The End.
