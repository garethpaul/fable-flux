# Leo's Safety Adventure

In a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls, lived a brave little lion cub named Leo. His golden fur was fluffy and warm. Leo's bedroom had a big comfy bed with rainbow blankets and a wooden toy chest full of treasures.

Every night, Leo loved to write in his special journal before bedtime. He would draw pictures and write about his daily adventures with his favorite purple crayon.

Tonight felt different. Leo heard a strange *tap, tap, tap* sound coming from somewhere in his room. His little ears perked up, and his whiskers twitched.

"What could that be?" Leo whispered to himself. He looked around his cozy room with curious amber eyes.

The tapping sound came again. *Tap, tap, tap!* It seemed to be coming from near the window where his night light glowed softly.

Leo wanted to investigate right away. He started to jump down from his bed, but then he remembered something very important. Mama Lion had taught him about being safe at nighttime.

"When something seems different or scary, always ask for help first," Leo said aloud, remembering Mama's gentle voice.

Instead of exploring alone, Leo called out, "Mama! Papa! Can you help me? I hear a strange sound!"

Soon, Mama Lion's warm paws padded into the room. Her golden mane smelled like lavender from her evening bath. "What's wrong, my brave little cub?"

"There's a tapping sound by the window," Leo explained, pointing with his small paw. "I wanted to check, but I remembered to ask for help first!"

Mama Lion smiled proudly. "That was very smart thinking, Leo! Let's investigate together."

They walked hand-in-paw toward the window. The tapping grew louder. *Tap, tap, tap!*

Papa Lion appeared in the doorway. "Is everything okay?" he asked with concern.

"We're working as a team to solve the mystery," Mama explained. "Leo heard a strange sound and did exactly the right thing by asking for help."

Together, the lion family looked carefully around the window area. Papa Lion gently moved the curtain aside. There, on the windowsill, was a small tree branch that had grown too long. The wind was making it tap against the glass!

"Mystery solved!" Papa Lion chuckled, his deep voice rumbling like a happy purr.

"The tree just wanted to say goodnight," Leo giggled, feeling much better now that his family was with him.

Papa Lion carefully opened the window and gently moved the branch away from the glass. "There! No more tapping tonight."

Leo felt proud and safe. He had been brave by not ignoring the sound, but he had also been smart by asking for help instead of investigating something unknown by himself.

"Now I have something wonderful to write about in my journal!" Leo said excitedly. He climbed onto his bed and picked up his purple crayon.

In his journal, Leo drew a picture of his family working together. He wrote in his best letters: "Safety means asking for help. Teamwork makes everything better."

Mama and Papa Lion tucked Leo in with his rainbow blankets. The room felt extra cozy and safe now.

"Sweet dreams, our brave and smart little cub," Mama whispered, giving him a gentle kiss on his furry forehead.

Leo smiled sleepily. He had learned that being truly brave meant making safe choices and working together with people who love you.

The End.
