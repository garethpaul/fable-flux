# Ollie Otter’s Quiet Clicks

Ollie the playful otter loved to wiggle and giggle. He lived with his family by a shining river, but today he was inside the home office. The home office smelled like fresh paper and lemon-clean desk. The computer hummed like a sleepy bee. The soft rug felt squishy under Ollie’s webbed feet. Tap, tap, tap went the keyboard. Click went the mouse. Sunlight made warm stripes across the floor.

“Today is help day!” said Ollie, swishing his tail. “I will help Mama work.”

Mama smiled and put on big headphones. “Thank you, Ollie,” she said. “I have a quiet call. Quiet means soft sounds.” She tapped a sign that said, “Quiet Time, Please.”

Ollie nodded. “Quiet time,” he whispered. “I can be quiet.” He puffed his cheeks and made a tiny fish face to show he understood. Then he spotted something shiny. It was a red, bouncy ball under the chair.

“Ooo!” said Ollie. He reached for the ball. It squeaked. Squeak! Ollie froze. He peeked at Mama. Mama made a small smile and held up one finger. One more minute for quiet.

Ollie took a deep breath. “I can wait,” he told himself. Waiting felt hard. His tummy fluttered and his paws wiggled. He wanted to bounce. He wanted to splash. But there was no water here, only papers and pens and a big green plant that smelled like pepper.

He tried to sit. He tried to be still. His tail went flick, flick, flick. His paws went drum, drum, drum on his knees. The ball peeked at him. Squeak, it seemed to say. Come play, Ollie.

Ollie reached again. The ball rolled away. It rolled toward a tower of sticky notes, yellow like baby ducklings. Thump! The notes tumbled. They floated down like tiny feathers.

“Oh no,” whispered Ollie. His whiskers drooped. His heart felt hot and jumpy. He did not mean to make a mess.

Mama turned in her chair. She lifted one headphone ear. “Are you okay, Ollie?”

“I am frustrated,” Ollie said, with a wobbly voice. “I tried to be quiet. I tried to wait. But I wanted to play, and I made a mess.”

Mama nodded. “Thank you for telling me. Let’s try a peaceful plan.” She looked at the big clock. “My call is short. You can choose a quiet job. Or we can make a quiet game. Which feels good to you?”

Ollie sniffed. The lemon-clean smell made him feel calm. He rubbed his paws on the soft rug. “A quiet game,” he said. “But I don’t know how.”

Mama smiled. “We can learn. First, take three tummy breaths with me.”

They put paws on bellies. In through the nose, slow like a turtle. Out through the mouth, soft like bubbles. One… two… three. Ollie’s shoulders relaxed. His tail went still.

“Now,” said Mama softly, “we use our S.O.F.T. plan. S is for Stop. O is for Observe. F is for Friendly choice. T is for Try again.”

Ollie repeated, “Stop. Observe. Friendly choice. Try again.”

They looked around. They observed the room. Tap, tap, went the keyboard. Hummm, went the computer. The plant leaves were shiny and green. The sticky notes were scattered like sunny snow.

Friendly choice time. “I can pick up notes,” Ollie said. “And I can play a quiet game with the ball.”

“What quiet game?” Mama asked.

Ollie thought. “Roll and catch,” he said. “No bounce. No squeak.”

Mama gave a thumbs-up. “Peaceful plan!”

Ollie gathered the sticky notes. Squish, went the rug under his knees. Whisper, went the papers as he stacked them. He drew a tiny smile face on the top note. He felt proud.

He placed a tape line on the floor, with Mama’s help. “This is our gentle river,” he said. “The ball is a little boat.” He rolled the ball slowly along the tape river. Shhh, went the ball. No squeak, no thump. Ollie caught it with open paws. He rolled it back to himself. Roll… catch. Roll… catch.

Mama put her headphone back on and smiled with her eyes. Ollie played the quiet game. He counted rolls in a whisper. “One, two, three, four,” he breathed. He paused to rest when his paws got jumpy. He took one tummy breath. He felt in control.

Soon Mama took off her headphones. “All done,” she said. “Thank you for your peaceful plan.”

Ollie grinned. “I did S.O.F.T. I stopped. I looked. I picked a friendly choice. I tried again.”

Mama hugged him. “You solved a problem with calm. That helps everyone.”

Just then, Ollie heard a tiny chirp. A bird sat at the window, peeking in. Its feathers were blue like the sky. “Hello, Bird,” Ollie said. “We are playing a quiet game. You can watch if you like.”

The bird tilted its head and chirped softly. Ollie rolled the ball river-slow. He felt happy to include the bird. He felt proud to keep the room calm.

He looked at Mama. “When I feel frustrated, I can breathe. I can use S.O.F.T. I can choose peaceful ways.”

Mama nodded. “Yes. Peaceful choices help our hearts and our home.”

Ollie picked up the sign that said, “Quiet Time, Please.” He flipped it over. The back said, “Play Time, Please.” He laughed. “Now we can splash outside,” he said.

They went to the river, and Ollie made gentle splashes that sparkled like stars. The bird fluttered above, and the sun felt warm like a hug.

Ollie whispered to the water, “Peaceful plans help me.” His tail swished, but his heart felt calm and strong.

The End.
