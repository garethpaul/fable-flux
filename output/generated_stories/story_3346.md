# Tall Steps, Colorful Hopes

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with soft, sandy fur and bright, curious eyes. Today, Gigi stood in the creative art room. Paints glowed like rainbows. Paper crinkled like leaves. Clay smelled warm and earthy. Sunlight made little gold squares on the floor.

Gigi whispered, “Today is sports day. I want to try something new.”

Her friends were there too. Benny the bunny bounced. “Boing, boing! We can make sports posters!” Lulu the ladybug fluttered. “Let’s make team flags!” Tiko the turtle smiled slowly. “We can practice moves too.”

Gigi looked at the brushes. “I can paint sports,” she said. “Sports help bodies grow strong. Sports help hearts feel brave.”

Miss Maple, their teacher, clapped gently. “Friends, today we will learn about sports. We will make art. We will move our bodies. We will share our family traditions. We will cheer for each other.”

Gigi felt a flutter in her tummy. “I am tall,” she said softly. “I feel wobbly when I run fast.”

Benny’s ears wiggled. “That’s okay,” he said. “We will go slow. Slow can still be strong.”

They began to paint. Gigi dipped her brush in blue. Swish, swish. She painted a big ball and a tall hoop. She painted a giraffe gently bouncing the ball. “This sport is called basketball,” she said. “It uses tall steps.”

Lulu dotted red on a banner. Tap, tap. “My family cheers for soccer,” she said. “We sing a tiny song.” She hummed sweetly. “La-la-kick, la-la-pass.”

Tiko pressed green clay. Squish. “My grandpa taught me gentle stretches,” he said. “We breathe in. We breathe out.” He showed a slow arm stretch. “It helps before sports.”

Miss Maple smiled. “What good traditions you are sharing.”

Gigi listened. She held the brush and thought. “I can learn from my friends,” she said. She took a deep breath in. She let a slow breath out, just like Tiko.

Benny bounced a soft ball. Thump, thump. “Want to try a pass?” he asked.

Gigi’s legs felt shaky. “I am a little scared,” she said. “But I will be brave.” She opened her long hooves. Benny passed the ball gently. Thump. Gigi caught it! Her heart felt warm and proud.

“Great catch!” Lulu cheered. Her tiny wings buzzed happily.

They made more art. They glued paper stars on flags. They drew big smiles. The room smelled like oranges from their snack. Miss Maple passed slices around. “Healthy fuel for play,” she said. “Water and fruit help our bodies.”

Gigi ate a juicy orange slice. Drip, drip. “Sweet and bright,” she said. “My body feels ready.”

Miss Maple set up a little sports path in the art room. There was a soft mat, a tape line, and a cardboard hoop. “We will do a friendly mini-game,” she said. “We will take turns. We will cheer.”

Benny hopped the tape line. Boing-boing! Lulu tapped a tiny ball into a tiny goal. Tap! Tiko rolled the ball slow and steady. Roll, roll.

Gigi’s turn came. She held the ball. Her knees wobbled. “I am tall,” she reminded herself. “I can take tall steps.” She looked at Tiko and breathed in. She looked at Lulu and hummed a little tune. She looked at Benny and felt brave.

“Go, Gigi!” her friends sang. “Tall steps, tall heart!”

Gigi took one tall step. Then another. The ball bounced, thump, thump. She gently tossed it up to the hoop. The ball kissed the cardboard rim and dropped in. Swish!

Gigi laughed. Her eyes sparkled. “I did it,” she said. “I tried a new sport.”

Her friends clapped. Miss Maple nodded. “You showed courage, Gigi. You listened to your body. You practiced. You used your friends’ traditions. That is the spirit of sports.”

They sat in a circle on the soft mat. The room buzzed with happy sounds. Crayons clicked. Paper rustled. Outside, a little breeze tickled the window.

Gigi shared. “I learned three things,” she said. “One: Move your body every day. Two: Eat and drink healthy to help your body. Three: Be a kind teammate. Cheer for friends.”

Benny added, “And four: It is okay to be slow.”

Lulu added, “And five: Songs make courage grow.”

Tiko added, “And six: Breathe in, breathe out.”

Miss Maple smiled. “Wonderful lessons. Sports can be fun. Sports help our bodies and our hearts. We try. We rest. We try again.”

They hung their posters on the wall. The flags fluttered like tiny birds. Gigi looked up at her tall giraffe painting making a basket. She felt calm and proud.

“Tomorrow,” Gigi said, “we can learn another sport. We can share more family traditions.”

“Yes!” laughed her friends. “We can try together.”

Gigi took a last sip of water. She felt strong, kind, and brave. “I like sports,” she said. “Sports help me grow tall inside, not just outside.”

The End.
