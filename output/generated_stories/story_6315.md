# Tilly Turtle Tries Again

In a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls, a tiny lamp glowed like a warm moon. The bed was fluffy and green, like a garden leaf. On a shelf sat a little toy drum, a small red car, and a shiny shell. The room smelled like clean blankets and cocoa. It was bedtime for Tilly, a shy turtle with a smooth, speckled shell.

Tilly pulled her quilt up to her chin. “Goodnight,” she whispered to her favorite stuffed star, Twinkle. But her tummy felt twisty. Her cheeks felt warm. Her heart did a little tap-tap-tap.

Mama Turtle peeked in. “Lights low?” she asked, turning the lamp to a soft glow. “You look thoughtful, Tilly.”

Tilly nodded slowly. “I had a hard day, Mama.”

“Tell me,” Mama said, sitting on the edge of the bed. The quilt rustled. The room was quiet and safe.

Tilly took a deep breath. “At playtime, I wanted the red car. But my cousin Toby had it. I said in a tiny voice, ‘Can I please have a turn?’ He didn’t hear me. He kept zooming. My tummy got tight. I felt mad. I said, ‘Mine!’ very loud, and I grabbed it. Toby cried.” Tilly’s eyes filled with tears. “I didn’t like that feeling.”

Mama nodded gently. “You felt unheard and mad. Your body told you with tight tummy and hot cheeks. That was big, big feelings.”

Tilly sniffled. “I wish I used my kind words. I wish I waited. But my feelings felt too big.”

Mama touched Tilly’s shell. “Feelings can be big. We can still choose kind actions. Do you want to practice?”

Tilly blinked. “Practice? In my bedroom?”

Mama smiled. “Practice helps our brave voice grow. We can practice calm, and we can practice words.”

Tilly sat up. The quilt made a soft swish. “Okay.”

“First, let’s notice the feeling,” Mama said. “Where is it now?”

Tilly put a hand on her tummy. “It’s a little wiggly.”

“Let’s try turtle breathing,” Mama said. “In, like you smell a flower.” They breathed in slowly through their noses. “Out, like you blow a bubble.” They blew out gently. The lamp light looked soft and fuzzy. The room felt quieter.

“Flower, bubble,” Tilly whispered. They did it three times.

“How is your tummy?” Mama asked.

“Less wiggly,” Tilly said. “More cozy.”

“Good,” Mama said. “Now we practice words. I’ll be Toby. You be you.” She reached for the small red car on the shelf and held it. “Vroom, vroom!”

Tilly smiled a tiny smile. “Excuse me, Toby,” she said in a clear, small voice. “I would like a turn when you are done.”

Mama pretended to zoom. “I didn’t hear you,” she said, keeping her voice silly and loud.

Tilly felt her cheeks warm. She remembered the flower and bubble. “Flower,” she breathed in. “Bubble,” she blew out. Then she tried again. “Excuse me, Toby. I would like a turn when you are done.”

Mama looked at Tilly and nodded. “I hear you now. I will give you a turn in two minutes.”

Tilly grinned. “Okay! I can wait.”

Mama put the car down. “That was brave. What could you do while you wait?”

“I can count snails on my shell,” Tilly giggled, patting the bumps. “Or I can play with my drum.” She tapped the toy drum with her finger. Tap, tap. The sound was soft like little raindrops.

“Let’s try a tricky one,” Mama said. “What if Toby says, ‘No!’?”

Tilly’s eyes widened. She felt the wiggly feeling try to come back. “Flower.” She breathed in the cozy blanket smell. “Bubble.” She blew out slowly. “Then I say, ‘That makes me sad. I will ask a grown-up for help.’”

Mama clapped softly. “Yes. You named your feeling. You asked for help. That is strong, not mean.”

Tilly tucked Twinkle under her chin. “I want to try again tomorrow.”

“You can,” Mama said. “When we practice, our kind words get stronger. Our calm grows, too.”

Just then, there was a soft knock. Knock, knock. “Tilly?” It was Toby, peeking in with shy eyes. He held the red car to his chest. “I’m sorry for taking a long time,” he said. “Do you want a turn now?”

Tilly’s heart did a gentle thump. She remembered flower and bubble. “Thank you, Toby,” she said, her voice clear and kind. “I feel happy when we share.” She took the car and rolled it on the quilt. Rrrr—roll, roll. The wheels tickled the blanket.

Toby sat on the floor and tapped the drum. Tap, tap-tap. “I felt sad when you grabbed,” he said softly. “But I feel better now.”

“I’m sorry I grabbed,” Tilly said. “Next time, I will ask and wait. If I feel too mad, I will breathe and ask for help.”

Toby smiled. “We can practice together.”

They played side by side for a few minutes. The cozy bedroom was full of gentle sounds: roll, tap, giggle, whisper. The lamp glowed warm. The quilt felt snug.

When it was time to sleep, Tilly gave Toby the car back. “Thank you for sharing.”

“Thank you for asking,” Toby said.

After Toby left, Mama kissed Tilly’s head. “What did you learn tonight?”

Tilly yawned a little yawn. “I learned that feelings can be big,” she said. “I can breathe. I can use kind words. I can wait or ask for help. I can practice.”

Mama tucked the quilt around Tilly. “That is a wise turtle.”

Tilly hugged Twinkle. Her tummy felt calm and warm. The lamp clicked off. In the quiet, she whispered, “Flower. Bubble. Kind words.” She smiled in the dark.

The End.
