# Ollie the Otter's Garden Helper Day

Ollie the otter lived in a cozy apartment with a beautiful balcony garden. Every morning, he loved to splash in his little water bowl and watch the colorful flowers dance in the breeze.

One sunny Tuesday morning, Ollie woke up feeling very tired. His whiskers drooped, and his usually shiny fur looked dull.

"Oh my," said Ollie, looking at himself in the mirror. "I don't feel like my happy self today."

His neighbor, Mrs. Robin, was watering her plants on the next balcony. She noticed Ollie's sad face.

"Good morning, Ollie! You look a little sleepy today," chirped Mrs. Robin kindly.

Ollie's ears perked up. "I am tired, Mrs. Robin. I stayed up too late playing with my toys last night."

"That happens to everyone sometimes," said Mrs. Robin with a warm smile. "What do you think might help you feel better?"

Ollie thought carefully, tapping his little paw on his chin. "Maybe I should take better care of myself?"

"That's a wonderful idea!" said Mrs. Robin. "Self-care means doing things that help your body and mind feel good."

First, Ollie took a long, refreshing bath in his water bowl. The cool water felt amazing on his fur, and he splashed playfully, making tiny rainbows in the sunlight. "Ahh, that feels so much better!" he giggled.

Next, he brushed his fur until it was soft and shiny again. Then he ate a healthy breakfast of fresh fish and crunchy vegetables from Mrs. Robin's garden.

"I'm starting to feel more like myself!" Ollie announced happily.

Mrs. Robin was struggling to water all her plants. Her watering can was heavy, and she looked tired.

"Mrs. Robin, can I help you?" asked Ollie. "I love water, and helping others makes me feel good inside!"

"Oh, that would be wonderful, Ollie! Thank you for offering to volunteer your time."

Ollie carefully carried the watering can with his strong paws. Together, they watered the bright red tomatoes, the purple petunias, and the fragrant herbs. The plants seemed to sparkle with happiness as the water droplets caught the morning sun.

"The basil smells so good!" said Ollie, taking a deep breath. "And look how the flowers are opening up!"

"You're such a good helper, Ollie," said Mrs. Robin. "Volunteering to help others is another wonderful way to take care of yourself. It makes your heart feel warm and happy."

As they worked together, Ollie felt his energy returning. His tail swished with joy, and his eyes sparkled with excitement.

"I learned something important today," said Ollie thoughtfully. "Taking care of myself means getting enough sleep, eating good food, staying clean, AND helping others!"

"Exactly right!" said Mrs. Robin. "And don't forget about talking to friends when you need help. Communication is very important too."

That evening, Ollie made sure to put away his toys early and go to bed on time. He felt proud of himself for learning about self-care and for helping Mrs. Robin in her garden.

"Tomorrow I'll wake up feeling great because I took good care of myself today," Ollie said with a big yawn. "And maybe I can help someone else too!"

As he drifted off to sleep, Ollie smiled, knowing that taking care of himself and others made every day brighter.

The End.
