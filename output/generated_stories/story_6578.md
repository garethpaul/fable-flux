# Dippy the Dolphin in the Garden Shed

Dippy was a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a happy splashy laugh. One sunny morning, Dippy swam up the little stream that ran behind Mr. Willow’s house. The stream tickled Dippy’s fins and smelled like fresh leaves. “Hee-hee! What a fun day!” Dippy chirped.

Near the stream stood a small garden shed. It was brown and warm in the sun. Birds sang, “Cheep, cheep!” Bees hummed, “Bzzzz!” The air smelled like soil and sweet flowers. Dippy peeked in the open door. “Hello? Anybody here?”

Inside the shed, tools hung neatly on hooks. A blue watering can sat on a shelf. A red wheelbarrow waited by a stack of flower pots. Everything looked tidy and cozy, like a little garden house.

Mr. Willow stepped in, wiping his hands. He wore a green hat and had kind, crinkly eyes. “Oh! A dolphin! In my garden stream!” he laughed. “Hello, friend! I’m Mr. Willow.”

“I’m Dippy,” said the dolphin. “I love to play and help. Your shed smells like mint and dirt. Mmm!”

Mr. Willow smiled. “Today I’m planting seeds. I could use a helper with good splashes.”

Dippy’s tail wiggled. “I can splash! I can help!”

They started to work together. Mr. Willow held a tiny brown seed. “This seed will grow with love, water, and care.” Dippy watched closely. Plop, plop, the seeds went into the soft soil. Dippy brought water in the blue watering can. “I can do it! Splash-splash!” he sang.

Soon they found a tangled pile of string and small sticks on the floor. “Oh dear,” said Mr. Willow. “I thought these were on the shelf.”

Dippy looked at the shelf. A shiny shell sat there. It glittered like a little moon. Dippy’s eyes grew wide. “Oooh,” he whispered. He reached up with his nose and knocked the shell down. Clink! The shell cracked a tiny bit.

Dippy gasped. “Oh no! I broke it.” His tummy felt twisty. He wanted to hide. He looked at the door. He looked at the floor. His heart went thump-thump. “Maybe Mr. Willow won’t notice,” Dippy thought. “Maybe I can just be quiet.”

Mr. Willow turned around. “Dippy, can you pass me the string?” he asked. He did not see the cracked shell yet.

Dippy held the string, but his fins felt wobbly. He took a deep breath. The shed felt warm and safe. He remembered: Helping means telling the truth. That is integrity.

“Mr. Willow,” Dippy said softly. “I made a mistake. I touched the shiny shell. It fell and got a little crack. I am sorry.”

Mr. Willow blinked, then smiled gently. “Thank you for telling me the truth, Dippy. That is very brave. That is integrity.”

Dippy felt tears prick his eyes, but he also felt lighter, like a bubble. “I want to fix it,” he said. “Can we fix it together?”

“We can try,” said Mr. Willow. He brought a tiny brush, some safe glue, and a soft cloth. “Let’s be careful and work as a team.”

They sat side by side. Mr. Willow held the shell steady. Dippy dabbed the little crack with the brush. Tap, tap, tap. The glue smelled a bit sweet. The shell felt smooth and cool. Together they pressed it gently. They counted, “One, two, three, four, five!” Then they let it rest on a soft towel.

“Thank you for helping me fix it,” said Dippy. “And thank you for being kind.”

“Thank you for your honesty,” said Mr. Willow. “And for your splashes!”

They went back to planting. Dippy practiced slow, careful motions. He said, “Practice helps me be gentle.” Mr. Willow nodded. “We practice so our hands and fins learn good moves. That is coordination.”

Dippy carried the watering can again. He moved slowly. Drip, drip, drip. The soil looked dark and happy. A ladybug crawled by. “Hello, little friend,” Dippy giggled. The ladybug tickled his nose.

They sang a simple planting song:
“Plant and pat, water and wait,
Tell the truth, and we feel great!
Thank you, friend, for helping me,
We can grow a garden, you will see!”

When they finished, the shed looked neat again. Tools were back on hooks. The wheelbarrow was empty. The air smelled like tomato leaves and sunshine.

Mr. Willow lifted the mended shell. It shone softly. “It looks good,” he said. “You did careful work.”

Dippy’s eyes sparkled. “We did it together. I am grateful for your help.”

“And I am grateful for you,” said Mr. Willow. “You showed integrity. You told the truth. You were brave. You helped fix the mistake. That is how we grow.”

Dippy felt proud and calm. His tummy was no longer twisty. He splashed his tail in the stream. “I learned something today,” he said. “When I make a mistake, I tell the truth, say sorry, and help make it right.”

“Exactly,” said Mr. Willow. “That is integrity. That is cooperation. That is kindness.”

The sun yawned, and the birds sang a soft evening song. Dippy waved his fin. “Goodbye, garden shed! Goodbye, Mr. Willow! See you tomorrow for more practice.”

“See you tomorrow, Dippy!” Mr. Willow waved back.

Dippy swam down the stream, feeling warm and happy inside, like a little light. He knew he could play, help, and be honest every day.

The End.
