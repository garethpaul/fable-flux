# Ollie's Musical Clean-Up Adventure

Ollie the otter loved music more than anything! His whiskers twitched with excitement as he splashed into the school's musical practice room. The room smelled like wood polish and had shiny instruments everywhere.

"Time to practice my drum solo!" Ollie chirped happily. He grabbed the drumsticks with his wet paws and started playing. BOOM! CRASH! BANG!

But something felt wrong. Ollie's paws were still dripping from the pond outside. Water drops fell onto the beautiful drums. The drumsticks slipped from his wet grip.

"Oh no!" Ollie gasped. He looked around the room. His wet paw prints were everywhere - on the piano keys, the guitar strings, and even the music sheets from different countries.

Mrs. Robin, the music teacher, flew into the room. "Ollie, what happened here?" she asked gently.

Ollie's ears drooped. "I was so excited to play music, I forgot to dry off first. Now everything is wet and messy!"

Mrs. Robin smiled kindly. "Let's fix this together, Ollie. But first, what should we do?"

Ollie thought hard. His tail tapped on the floor. "I should clean up and get dry!"

"That's right!" Mrs. Robin chirped. "Clean hands and paws help us take better care of our instruments."

Ollie hurried to the sink. He washed his paws with warm, soapy water. The bubbles tickled and smelled like lavender. Then he dried them carefully with a soft, fluffy towel.

"Now I'm clean and dry!" Ollie announced proudly.

Together, they organized the practice room. Ollie gently wiped the drums with a special cloth. Mrs. Robin showed him how to carefully clean the piano keys.

"These music sheets are from Japan, Mexico, and Ireland," Mrs. Robin explained as they dried each paper. "Musicians from all around the world share their beautiful songs with us."

"Wow!" Ollie's eyes sparkled. "I want to learn songs from everywhere!"

As they worked, Ollie noticed something special. The clean instruments sounded much better! The drums had a crisp, clear beat. The piano keys played sweet, pure notes.

"Listen, Ollie," Mrs. Robin said. "When we keep our instruments clean and organized, the music sounds more beautiful."

Ollie nodded eagerly. He picked up his pencil with his clean, dry paws and wrote in his music notebook: "Always wash and dry hands before playing music."

"That's wonderful writing, Ollie!" Mrs. Robin praised.

Now the practice room was spotless and organized. All the instruments gleamed. The music sheets were neatly sorted by country. Everything had its special place.

Ollie sat at the clean drums. This time, his dry paws gripped the sticks perfectly. He played a gentle rhythm that sounded like raindrops dancing on water.

"Beautiful music, Ollie!" Mrs. Robin clapped her wings.

Ollie beamed with pride. "I learned something important today. Clean paws make better music! And taking care of instruments from other countries shows respect for all musicians."

From that day on, Ollie always washed and dried his paws before entering the practice room. He became the cleanest, most organized little musician in the whole school. His music sounded more wonderful than ever before.

"Being clean helps me share music with the whole world!" Ollie sang happily as he played his drums.

The End.
