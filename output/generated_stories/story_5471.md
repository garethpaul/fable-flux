# Panda Poppy’s Balcony Garden

Panda Poppy woke up with a gentle stretch. “Good morning, balcony garden,” Poppy said softly. She stepped onto the small sunny balcony and breathed in. The air smelled like mint and wet soil. Little pots lined the railing—basil, spinach, sweet strawberries, and tiny tomato plants. Birds sang peep-peep. A breeze tickled Poppy’s cheeks.

“I will water you slowly,” Poppy told the plants. “I am patient. I respect your needs.”

Poppy filled a small can with cool water. Drip, drip, drip. She poured a little at each pot. “Not too much, not too fast,” she whispered. “I’m listening.” The soil turned dark and soft. The leaves looked shiny and happy.

“Hi, Poppy!” called Mia Mouse from next door’s balcony. Mia’s whiskers twitched. “Can I pick a strawberry now? I want a big one!”

Poppy smiled kindly. “Hi, Mia. The strawberries are still white. They need time to turn red and sweet. Let’s wait.”

Mia scrunched her nose. “But I want it now!”

Poppy took a calm breath. “I hear that you want it now. Waiting is hard. Let’s make a plan together.” She sat on a little stool. “How about we check the strawberries every day after snack time? When they are red and soft, we can share.”

Mia’s ears perked up. “Share?”

“Yes,” said Poppy. “We will be fair. We will be respectful to each other and to the plants.”

Mia wiggled her tail. “Okay. After snack time.”

A loud “Caw!” sounded from above. It was Cody Crow, flapping onto the railing. His black feathers glowed in the sun. “Shiny pot! Mine!” Cody tapped a pot with his beak. Tap-tap.

Poppy held up a paw. “Cody, please stop. That pot belongs to the basil. The basil needs a safe home.” She spoke gently but clearly.

Cody blinked. “But I like tapping.”

“I hear you,” Poppy said. “You like the sound. Let’s make a tapping post.” She pointed to a small wooden block near the wall. “You can tap here. Tap-tap-tap.”

Cody tilted his head. “Tap there?”

“Yes,” Poppy nodded. “That way we respect the basil’s pot. You get your sound, and the plant stays safe.”

Cody hopped to the block. Tap-tap-tap! “Nice!” he cawed happily.

Poppy smiled. “Thank you for respecting our garden.”

A tiny breeze carried the smell of basil and mint. The mint leaves looked crowded. Poppy touched them gently. “You need more space,” she said. “We can build you a bigger box.”

Mia clapped her paws. “Let’s build!”

Poppy got a small wooden box, a bag of soil, and a little scoop. “First, we plan,” she said. “Planning shows respect for safety.” She placed a mat on the floor. “Soil stays on the mat.”

Mia nodded. “Soil on the mat.”

Poppy scooped slowly. Scoop, pat, scoop, pat. “We are patient builders,” she hummed.

“Can I scoop fast?” Mia asked.

“We can try slow and steady,” Poppy said. “Slow keeps the garden clean.”

Cody watched with bright eyes. “Can I carry a stick?”

“Yes,” said Poppy. “Carry one stick at a time. That is persistent work.”

Cody brought a stick. Mia brought a little cup. Poppy gently moved the mint. “Easy, roots,” she whispered. The mint smelled sweet and cool. Poppy set it into the new soil. “Snug and comfy.”

Mia leaned close. “It looks happy!”

Poppy pointed to a watering plan on a small board. “We will water in the morning and check the soil with one finger. If it feels dry, we add water. If it feels wet, we wait. This is a healthy habit.”

Mia pressed a tiny finger into the soil. “It feels cool and damp.”

“So we wait,” Poppy said. “Waiting shows respect.”

Just then, a butterfly fluttered in, orange and gold. It landed on a marigold flower and sipped. Mia whispered, “Wow.” Cody fluffed his feathers. “Pretty.”

Poppy smiled. “Our quiet, careful work makes a home for friends.”

Soon the sun moved higher and the balcony felt warm. Poppy passed around cucumber slices and cups of cool water. “Snack time!” she sang. Crunch, crunch. “Healthy snacks help us grow, just like our plants.”

After snack time, Mia gasped. “Poppy! Look!” She pointed at the strawberry plants. One berry had turned bright red while they worked.

Poppy’s eyes shone. “You noticed. Great watching.” She asked, “Mia, what was our plan?”

“Share,” Mia said. “And ask the plant with respect.”

Poppy nodded. “Let’s pick it gently together.” They held the berry and tugged softly. Pop! The berry came free. It smelled sweet and sunny.

They split it into small bites. Mia tasted. “Mmm! Waiting was good.”

Cody pecked his tapping post happily. “Tap for the sweet berry!”

Poppy looked at her friends and her garden. She felt proud and calm. “Today we respected each other. We respected the plants. We were patient. We were persistent builders. And we made healthy choices.”

Mia hugged Poppy. “Thank you for listening.”

Cody bowed his head. “Thank you for the tapping place.”

Poppy patted the soil around the mint. “Thank you, garden, for growing.”

The breeze whispered through the leaves. Peep-peep, sang the birds. The balcony glowed with green life. Poppy closed her eyes for a moment and smiled.

“We can care, we can share, we can wait, and we can build,” she said. “That is respect.”

The End.
