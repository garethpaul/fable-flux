# Pippa Parrot and the Closet Choices

In a small, quiet house, there was a cozy closet hideaway. It was soft and snug. A warm lamp glowed like a tiny sun. There were baskets of buttons, ribbons, and old hats. It smelled like fresh rain boots and cotton.

This was Pippa Parrot’s favorite place. Pippa was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of blue on her tail. She loved to make things. She loved to sing little songs like, “Squawk-a-doodle-doo, I can make something new!”

One rainy morning, Pippa hopped into the closet hideaway. Tap, tap, tap went her little claws on the wooden floor. She wanted to make a special surprise for her friends. “But what should I make?” Pippa asked out loud. “A hat? A puppet? A sign?”

Pippa felt a flutter in her tummy. Too many choices! Her head felt busy. She fluffed her feathers. “I need to decide,” she said softly. “I can do it. I can make a plan.”

She took a deep breath. In, out. In, out. The warm lamp hummed. The ribbons rustled when she touched them—swish, swish.

Pippa said, “Step one: Stop and breathe. Step two: Think of choices.” She made a little list in her mind.
- Choice one: Make a Kindness Hat for a friend.
- Choice two: Make a Volunteer Bag to help carry books at the library.
- Choice three: Make a Brave Sign to use my big, strong voice.

She nodded. “Good list,” she chirped. “Now I will pick.”

Just then, Pippa heard a soft knock. Tap, tap. The closet door creaked. “Pippa?” whispered a small voice. It was Teddy, the shy teddy bear. His fur was the color of honey. He held an old book with a loose cover.

“Hello, Teddy,” said Pippa, smiling. “Come in. What do you need?”

Teddy peeked inside. “I… I need help,” he said. His voice was squeaky and small. “The library needs helpers today. The books need to go on the shelf. I want to help. But I feel shy.”

Pippa looked at her list. She felt her tummy flutter again. “I have choices,” she said. “I can make a Kindness Hat for Teddy. I can make a Volunteer Bag for the library. Or I can make a Brave Sign to help Teddy speak up.”

Teddy shuffled his paws. “I wish I could be brave,” he whispered. “I wish I could say, ‘I can help!’ But my voice hides.”

Pippa put a soft wing on Teddy’s arm. “We can choose together,” she said. “Let’s think. What is the problem?”

“The problem is,” Teddy said, “I want to help, but I am shy. And the books are heavy.”

Pippa nodded. “We need two things. We need a way to carry. And we need brave words.”

Pippa stood tall. “I will decide! I will make the Volunteer Bag. And I will make the Brave Sign. Then we can help the library. We can be brave and kind!”

Pippa flapped her wings. Swish! She gathered a sturdy scarf, bright buttons, and a big ribbon. Snip, snip went the scissors. Clip, clip went the clothespins. She tied the scarf and the ribbon into a strong bag. She sewed on buttons—red, blue, yellow. The bag smelled like clean cotton and lemon soap.

Next, she made a sign from a smooth piece of cardboard. She painted big letters with a safe brush. The letters were bright green. The sign said, “I CAN HELP!” The paint looked shiny and wet. It felt cool under her feather tips.

Pippa showed Teddy. “Try it!”

Teddy slipped on the bag. It fit snug and cozy. He held the sign. His eyes grew wide. “It feels good,” he said. He took a big breath. “I can help.”

Pippa smiled. “Let’s go to the library.”

They walked down the hall. Step, step, step. The floor was warm. They could hear raindrops tick-tick on the window. At the library room, books were stacked like little towers. The librarian, a gentle rabbit named Miss Lila, waved. “Hello, friends,” she said. “We need helpers.”

Teddy looked at Pippa. Pippa nodded. She whispered, “Use your brave sign. Use your brave voice.”

Teddy held up the sign. “I can help!” he said. His voice was still soft, but it came out. Pippa stood beside him and said clearly, “We volunteer to help.”

Miss Lila clapped her paws. “Thank you! That is kind and brave.”

Pippa and Teddy worked together. They used the Volunteer Bag to carry books. Thump, thump went the books into the bag. Slide, slide went the books onto the shelf. Pippa hummed a happy tune. Teddy smiled and stood a little taller.

When they finished, Miss Lila gave them warm cocoa in little cups. It smelled like chocolate and hugs. “You made a good decision,” she said. “You chose to help. You spoke up. You were kind.”

Back in the closet hideaway, Pippa and Teddy sat on a soft blanket. The lamp glowed. The ribbons swished. Pippa said, “Today I learned to stop, think of choices, and pick a good plan. I used my voice. I helped a friend. I felt brave.”

Teddy nodded. “Me too. I can decide. I can help. I can say what I need.”

Pippa fluffed her feathers and sang, “Squawk-a-doodle-doo, I can make something new—and choose what to do!”

The End.
