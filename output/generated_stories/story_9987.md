# Paint-Feather Polly and the Sunny Backyard

Polly was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of red on her cheek. She loved to make things. She loved colors. She loved song. In the sunny backyard, the air smelled like sweet grass and warm soil. Bees hummed near yellow flowers. A soft breeze tickled the leaves. Polly sat on the fence by a big cardboard box filled with safe, washable paints and old brushes.

“Chirp-chirp! Time to make backyard art!” Polly sang. Her voice sounded like tiny bells.

Ben and Maya, two kind kids, clapped. “We can paint the cardboard box,” Ben said, bouncing on his toes.

“We can make a rainbow,” Maya giggled. She dipped a brush into blue paint. Drip, drip! The blue dot fell onto the grass.

Polly bobbed her head. “Squawk! Let’s use all the colors. Red for warm, blue for calm, yellow for bright, green for good, purple for brave!” She started to tap a little rhythm on the fence with her claws, tick-tick-tick. It made Ben and Maya smile.

Then, from under the orange marigold flowers, a small gray squirrel named Nutty popped out. His tail twitched and his nose wiggled. “What is this?” he asked. “Your paint smells strange. I don’t like strange.” He looked at the bright splashes on the box. “Too many colors. It hurts my eyes.”

Ben frowned. “We like the colors.”

Maya bit her lip. “We can share the space.”

Nutty crossed his little arms. “You can paint, but not near my favorite nut spot.” He pointed to a sunny patch near the tree trunk. “And not with so many colors. Just brown. Brown is safe.”

Polly puffed her feathers. Her heart felt hot and tight. “Squawk… but colors are part of me,” she said softly. “I like being colorful.”

Nutty stamped his tiny foot. “I don’t like different. Different makes me worry.”

Ben whispered to Polly, “This feels like a problem.”

Maya nodded. “We can find a way. We can talk.”

Polly took a deep breath. She listened to the bees. Bzzzzz. She felt the sun warm her wings. She looked at Nutty’s tense tail. She wanted him to feel safe too. “Nutty,” she said gently, “thank you for telling us your feelings.”

Nutty blinked. “You… thank me?”

“Yes,” Polly said. “Feelings are important.” She touched the cardboard with her beak and made a soft tap. “Here is our problem: I love many colors. You love just brown. We all love this sunny backyard. How can we make it good for all of us?”

Maya raised her hand like at circle time. “What if we make a brown nut path next to your favorite spot so you feel safe?”

Ben added, “And we keep the rainbow on the other side of the yard.”

Polly bobbed. “And maybe I can paint a small brown acorn in the rainbow to show we accept Nutty too.”

Nutty’s nose twitched. “You would paint my acorn?”

“Squawk, yes,” Polly said. “Because you belong here. Different is okay. We can accept different and still be friends.”

Nutty looked at the bright box. He looked at the sunny patch by the tree. He took a slow breath. “I still feel a little worried,” he said, voice tiny.

“That’s okay,” Maya said softly. “We can go slow.”

Ben dipped a brush in brown. Swish, swish. He painted a gentle, curvy path near the tree. “This will be your nut path,” he said. “Safe and calm.”

Polly dipped her brush first in yellow, then in red, then in blue. Tap, tap, tap. She made a rainbow on the far side of the box. Next, with careful strokes, she painted a small brown acorn right in the middle. “For Nutty,” she said.

Nutty stepped closer. He sniffed. The paint smelled less strange now. The acorn was neat and round. “It’s… nice,” he whispered. He traced the nut path with his paw. “And this is calm.”

The breeze rustled the leaves, whispering shhhh. A bird chirped high in the tree. The sun made the paint shine.

“I still don’t love so many colors,” Nutty said. “But I like that you listened. I like that you made a place for me.”

Polly smiled with her eyes. “We listened to you, and you listened to us. That is how friends act.”

Ben held out a tiny paintbrush. “Do you want to add one brown leaf to the rainbow side? Just one, to say hello?”

Nutty’s tail slowly relaxed. “Okay,” he said. Dip, stroke. He painted a small brown leaf next to the acorn.

Everyone stepped back. The box had bright colors on one side, a calm nut path on the other, and a little acorn and leaf where the two worlds met.

Polly fluffed her feathers. “Squawk! Different can fit together,” she sang. “Colors and calm. We are all welcome.”

Nutty smiled. “I still like brown best. But I can accept your rainbow.”

“And we accept your brown,” Maya said.

Ben grinned. “Friends?”

“Friends,” Nutty said.

Polly chirped a happy tune. The bees hummed along. The sun felt warm and kind. In the sunny backyard, they all painted, and they all belonged.

The End.
