# Melody's Recycling Dance

In a cozy nursery with soft yellow walls and fluffy white clouds painted on the ceiling, lived a cheerful little bird named Melody. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds in the morning sunlight that streamed through the big window. Melody loved two things more than anything else: singing beautiful songs and dancing with her tiny feet.

"Tweet-tweet-trill!" Melody sang as she hopped along the windowsill. "What a wonderful morning!"

But as she looked down at the nursery floor, Melody's happy song turned into a worried chirp. Scattered everywhere were crumpled papers, empty juice boxes, and plastic bottle caps. The beautiful nursery looked messy and sad.

"Oh my!" Melody gasped. "This won't do at all. The nursery needs to be clean and happy for all the children who play here."

Melody fluttered down to the floor and picked up a piece of paper with her tiny beak. As she carried it, she began to dance, spinning and twirling with the paper held high.

"Dancing makes everything more fun!" she chirped to herself.

Just then, she noticed three special bins in the corner of the room. One was blue and had pictures of paper on it. Another was green with pictures of bottles and cans. The third was brown with pictures of food scraps.

"These must be recycling bins!" Melody sang excitedly. "I know what to do!"

She began her special recycling dance. First, she picked up all the papers and danced over to the blue bin, doing little spins as she dropped each piece in.

"Papers go in blue, papers go in blue!" she sang in her sweetest voice. "Twirl and spin, drop them in!"

Next, she gathered the plastic bottles and juice boxes. With graceful wing flutters, she danced to the green bin.

"Bottles and cans, in green they go!" Melody trilled, doing a happy hop with each item. "Leap and bound, recycling found!"

For the apple cores and banana peels, Melody performed her most elegant dance yet, gliding smoothly to the brown bin.

"Food scraps help plants grow tall!" she sang, doing a beautiful curtsy. "Dance and sway, help Earth today!"

As Melody finished her recycling dance, the nursery looked bright and clean again. The soft carpet was visible, and the colorful toys sparkled in their proper places.

"Magnificent!" came a gentle voice from the doorway.

Melody turned to see Mrs. Rose, the nursery teacher, clapping her hands with delight.

"Melody, you've made our nursery beautiful again," Mrs. Rose said with a warm smile. "And you've shown us how recycling can be fun when we dance and sing!"

"When we take care of our Earth," Melody chirped proudly, "everything feels happier! Recycling helps make new things from old things, just like how my dance makes cleaning feel like playing!"

Mrs. Rose nodded. "You're absolutely right, little one. When we put things in the right bins, we help our planet stay healthy and beautiful."

From that day forward, whenever the children in the nursery needed to recycle, they would remember Melody's special dance. They would sing her recycling song and dance to each bin, making taking care of the Earth the most fun part of their day.

And Melody? She became the official Recycling Dance Teacher, helping everyone learn that caring for our planet is as joyful as the most beautiful song.

The End.
