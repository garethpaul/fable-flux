# Owl Hoots and Happy Hearts

In a bright musical practice room, a wise old owl named Oona blinked her big golden eyes. The room smelled like polished wood and lemon oil. Sunlight made warm stripes on the floor. Instruments rested in neat rows: shiny drums, a small piano with black and white teeth, and a soft blue flute that looked like a sky ribbon.

Oona perched on a music stand. “Hoo-hoo,” she said kindly. “Today, we learn to play with our bodies and our hearts. Wellness first, music next.”

Little friends gathered around. A tiny rabbit named Rumi twitch-twitched his nose. A squirrel named Suri swished her fluffy tail. A turtle named Timo blinked slowly, smiling shy. They loved to visit Oona each day for a small adventure.

Rumi bounced. “Can we play loud-loud songs?”

Suri clapped. “Can we drum like rain?”

Timo tilted his head. “Can we learn a calm song?”

Oona nodded. “Yes, little ones. First, we wake up our bodies. Healthy bodies help happy music.”

She fluffed her soft feathers. “Let’s start with owl breaths,” she hooted. “In through your nose… fill your belly like a round drum… and out like a gentle wind.”

The room grew quiet and cozy. They breathed in, slow and deep. The air smelled like clean wood. They breathed out, soft and smooth. Rumi’s shoulders dropped. Suri’s tail settled. Timo’s eyes shone bright.

“My tummy feels warm,” said Rumi.

“My chest feels light,” said Suri.

“My shell feels brave,” said Timo.

“Good,” said Oona. “Now we stretch. Up like tall trees.” They reached their paws and wings high. “Down like sleepy stars.” They bent and giggled. “Shake-shake your hands and feet,” Oona sang. “Wiggle your ears, if you have them!”

Rumi wiggled his long ears. Suri tried and giggled. Timo wiggled his toes. The practice room filled with small happy sounds.

Oona tapped the piano keys. Plink, plonk, plink. “Before we play, we drink water.” She poured tiny cups. “Water keeps our voices clear and our bodies kind.”

They sipped. Rumi licked a droplet from his whisker. Suri sighed, “Ahh.” Timo patted his belly. “Thank you.”

Then Oona pointed to a little drum. “This drum belonged to my grandma. She taught me a tradition. We share a wellness song before every practice. Would you like to learn it?”

“Yes!” the friends cheered.

Oona hummed a gentle tune, warm like sunlight. “This is the ‘Good-Body Song,’” she said. “It helps us remember to listen to our breath, our heart, and to each other.”

She tapped the drum softly. Tum… tum… tum-tum. “Repeat after me.”

Oona sang:
“Hello breath, you help me grow.
Hello heart, you help me know.”

The friends sang back, little and sweet. Their voices sounded like a soft blanket. Oona smiled. “Beautiful.”

Just then, a new sound squeaked from the corner. A small mouse named Mimi peeked in. Her ears trembled. Her tiny hands held the blue flute. “I want to play,” she whispered, “but my tummy feels twisty.”

Oona’s feathers rustled with care. “Thank you for telling us, Mimi,” she said. “Everyone, how can we help?”

Rumi raised a paw. “We can do owl breaths with Mimi.”

Suri nodded. “We can sing the Good-Body Song.”

Timo moved slowly to stand by Mimi. “We can sit close.”

“Wonderful,” said Oona. “Helping others helps our hearts.”

They sat on the warm floor. The sunlight made soft squares on their fur and feathers. Together they breathed in and out. Mimi’s shoulders relaxed. Then they sang:

“Hello breath, you help me grow.
Hello heart, you help me know.”

Mimi’s eyes shined. “My tummy feels calmer.”

Oona nodded. “Now, a small tradition from my grandma: we share one kind wish before we play.”

Rumi said, “I wish Mimi feels brave.”

Suri said, “I wish our music helps someone smile.”

Timo said, “I wish my beats are gentle and strong.”

Mimi squeaked, “I wish I can play one tiny note.”

Oona blinked warmly. “My wish is that we remember to pause, breathe, sip water, and listen.”

They got ready. Oona gave two soft taps. Tum, tum. Rumi picked a tiny shaker that sounded like raindrops. Suri chose a hand drum that went boom-boom like a happy heart. Timo found a chime that rang like a little star. Mimi lifted the blue flute to her lips.

“Breath in,” Oona whispered. “Breath out.” Mimi blew gently. The flute sang a sweet, sky-blue note. It floated around the room like a feather.

“I did it!” Mimi smiled wide.

Cheers filled the air. The instruments sparkled and hummed. Their song was soft, then bright, then soft again. It sounded like a hug. Oona kept the steady drum, just like her grandma taught her.

When the song ended, they were quiet. They could hear their breaths, slow and happy. The room felt warm and safe.

Oona spoke softly. “Today we learned that wellness helps our music and our hearts. We breathed. We stretched. We drank water. We helped a friend. We shared a tradition.”

Mimi hugged her flute. “I feel brave and kind,” she said.

Rumi bounced, but gently. “I can play and stay calm.”

Suri patted her drum. “I can help others feel okay.”

Timo nodded. “I can listen to my body.”

Oona blinked her golden eyes. “Wellness is a daily adventure. We can do it every day.”

They sang the Good-Body Song one last time. Then they cleaned the room together, humming and smiling. The lemons and wood smelled sweet. The sunlight felt like a warm blanket on their backs.

“See you tomorrow,” Oona hooted. “Bring your breath. Bring your heart. Bring your helping hands.”

The End.
