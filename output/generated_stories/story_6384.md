# Benny Beaver's Helpful Workshop

Benny Beaver loved his cozy garage workshop. The smell of fresh wood shavings filled the air, and his tools hung neatly on the wall. *Tap, tap, tap!* went his little hammer as he worked on a new birdhouse.

"Perfect!" said Benny, admiring his work. The birdhouse was painted bright blue with a tiny red door. "The robins will love this!"

Just then, Benny heard a soft *sniff, sniff* from outside his workshop. He peeked through the window and saw Rosie Rabbit sitting by the fence, looking very sad.

"Hello, Rosie!" called Benny. "What's wrong?"

Rosie wiped her eyes. "Oh, Benny! My baby bunnies are cold at night. Their burrow isn't warm enough, and I don't know how to fix it."

Benny's kind heart felt heavy seeing his friend so worried. He looked at his beautiful new birdhouse, then back at Rosie. An idea began to grow in his mind.

"Don't worry, Rosie!" said Benny cheerfully. "I have an idea! Would you like to help me in my workshop?"

Rosie's ears perked up. "Really? But I don't know how to build things."

"That's okay!" said Benny. "I can teach you! Come on in!"

Rosie hopped into the warm workshop. The sweet smell of wood made her nose twitch happily. Benny showed her his tools - the smooth hammer, the rough sandpaper, and the colorful paints.

"First," said Benny, "let's make a cozy shelter for your little ones."

Together, they measured pieces of wood. Benny showed Rosie how to hold the boards steady while he hammered. *Bang, bang, bang!* The sounds echoed in the workshop.

"You're such a good helper!" said Benny as Rosie carefully painted the walls yellow like sunshine.

But then Rosie looked confused. "Benny, what about your pretty birdhouse? Don't you want to finish it?"

Benny smiled his biggest smile. "You know what, Rosie? Helping you makes me happier than keeping the birdhouse for myself. Let's turn it into a warm house for your babies!"

Rosie gasped with joy. "Really? You would do that for me?"

"Of course!" said Benny. "That's what friends do!"

They worked together all afternoon. Benny taught Rosie how to sand the wood until it felt as smooth as silk. They added soft hay inside and painted little flowers on the outside. The workshop filled with their happy laughter and the gentle sounds of their work.

When they finished, the little house was perfect - cozy, warm, and beautiful.

"Oh, Benny!" said Rosie, her eyes sparkling. "This is the most wonderful gift ever! Thank you for teaching me and for sharing with me."

Benny felt warm and fuzzy inside. "You know what I learned today, Rosie? When we share what we have and help others, we feel even happier than when we keep things just for ourselves."

That evening, Rosie's baby bunnies snuggled safely in their new warm home. And Benny? He started working on another birdhouse, humming happily. He had learned that the best feeling in the world comes from being generous and helping friends.

From that day on, Benny's workshop became a place where all the forest animals came to learn and help each other. And Benny was always ready to share his tools, his knowledge, and his kind heart with anyone who needed help.

The End.
