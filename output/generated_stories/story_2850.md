# Ollie Otter's Fair Building Day

In a cozy study corner filled with colorful books and soft cushions, a playful otter named Ollie loved to build towers with his wooden blocks. The blocks were smooth and warm in his little paws, and they made a satisfying *click* sound when they fit together perfectly.

One sunny morning, Ollie's friends came to visit his study corner. There was Bella Bunny, who loved to hop around, and Sam Squirrel, who was very good at stacking things high.

"Let's build the tallest tower ever!" squeaked Sam, grabbing a big pile of blocks.

"Wait!" said Bella, reaching for the same blocks. "I want to use those pretty red ones!"

Soon, all three friends were pulling at the blocks. Ollie watched sadly as his friends argued over who got which blocks. The study corner didn't feel peaceful anymore – it felt loud and unfair.

"Stop!" called Ollie in his gentle voice. "This doesn't feel right. We're not being fair to each other."

Sam held tight to his blocks. "But I'm the best builder! I should get the most blocks!"

Bella's ears drooped. "That's not fair, Sam. I want to build something beautiful too."

Ollie's whiskers twitched as he thought. He looked around his study corner at all the books about sharing and kindness. Then he had an idea that made his heart feel warm and fuzzy.

"I know!" said Ollie, clapping his paws together. "Let's count all the blocks and share them equally. Then we can each build our own special tower!"

"But what if someone gets better blocks?" worried Bella.

Ollie smiled. "We can take turns picking! Sam can pick first, then Bella, then me. We'll keep going until all the blocks are shared."

The friends sat in a circle on the soft carpet. Ollie poured out all the blocks – red ones, blue ones, yellow ones, and green ones. They smelled like cedar wood and felt smooth and cool.

"One for Sam," counted Ollie, "one for Bella, one for me." 

*Click, click, click* went the blocks as they were sorted into three equal piles.

"This is much better!" said Bella, her ears perking up. "Now we all have the same amount to build with."

"And look," said Sam, "we each got some of every color!"

The three friends spread out in the study corner and began building. Ollie built a tower that looked like a lighthouse, with windows he could peek through. Bella made a castle with smooth walls and a garden. Sam created a bridge that curved like a rainbow.

"Wow!" said Ollie, looking at all three creations. "Our buildings are all different and all wonderful!"

"Being fair made everything more fun," said Bella, doing a little hop.

"And I feel proud of what I built with my fair share," added Sam, his tail twitching happily.

Ollie felt warm and confident inside. He had helped solve the problem by making sure everyone was treated fairly. The study corner felt peaceful again, filled with the happy sounds of friends playing together.

As the sun streamed through the window, the three friends admired their buildings. They had learned that when everyone gets a fair chance, everyone can feel proud and happy.

"Tomorrow, let's build a whole city together!" suggested Ollie.

"With fair shares for everyone!" laughed his friends.

The End.
