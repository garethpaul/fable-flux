# Ellie’s Cozy Closet Club

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a warm, wiggly trunk. She loved the quiet corner of the house called the closet hideaway. Inside, there were blankets like clouds, a tiny lantern that glowed like a firefly, and a soft rug that felt like moss. It smelled like clean sweaters and cinnamon tea.

One rainy day, tiny drops tapped the window like drum beats. Ellie wiggled into the closet hideaway with her friends: Pip the parrot, Lina the lamb, and Tobi the turtle. They carried a little basket of snacks—bright orange carrot sticks, yellow banana slices, crunchy cucumber rounds, and a bottle of cool water.

“Welcome to the Cozy Closet Club,” Ellie said, her voice gentle and brave. “We can play, we can learn, and we can share.”

Pip flapped his green wings. “Let’s have a snack first! I’m hungry,” he chirped.

Lina nibbled her lip. “I’m hungry too,” she bleated softly.

Tobi peeked from his shell. “I like to take turns,” he said slowly.

Ellie smiled. “We will take turns,” she agreed. “First, let’s wash our hands.” She passed around a small bottle of soap and a damp cloth. “Clean hands keep us healthy.”

They scrubbed and giggled. The cloth felt cool and squishy. The soap smelled like lemons.

Ellie set the basket down. “We are friends from different places,” she said. “Pip’s family is from a warm island. Lina’s family is from a snowy hill. Tobi’s family likes quiet ponds. My family loves wide plains. We are all part of one big world.”

Pip blinked. “One big world?”

“Yes,” Ellie said. “Being a good neighbor to our world is called global citizenship. It means we care for each other and for our home, Earth.”

Just then, the lantern flickered. The rain became louder, like the sky was whispering, “Shhhh, listen.”

“Oh no,” said Lina. “What if we run out of snacks? I am scared.”

Ellie felt her tummy flutter. She was a little scared too. But she took a brave breath. “We have a problem,” she said. “We want to share snacks and care for everyone. But we must make sure we all get enough.”

Pip looked at the basket. “Maybe I should eat all the banana. It is my favorite!”

Tobi shook his head slowly. “If one friend takes all, others get none.”

Ellie nodded. “We can fix this. We can make a plan.”

She folded a blanket like a tablecloth. “First, we take turns. Second, we choose foods that help our bodies. That is good nutrition.” She pointed to the carrot sticks. “Crunchy carrots help our eyes.” She pointed to the cucumbers. “Cool cucumbers make us feel fresh.” She pointed to the bananas. “Bananas give us energy.”

Lina raised a hoof. “Third, we save some for later,” she suggested.

“Yes!” Ellie said. “That is civic responsibility. We take care of our club and our world by not wasting food.”

They made a little line. Ellie used her trunk to give each friend two carrot sticks, two cucumber rounds, and two banana slices. The basket still had some left.

Pip took a bite. Crunch! “I feel strong,” he chirped. “But what about friends outside our closet? They might be hungry.”

Ellie’s ears warmed. “You are right. Global citizens think beyond our own little space. We can share with others too.” She reached into a tiny drawer and found a reusable bag with blue stripes.

“We will save this extra snack,” Ellie said. “When the rain stops, we can bring it to the community snack shelf in the hallway. Everyone in the building can take what they need.”

Tobi smiled. “That is kind.”

Lina’s eyes sparkled. “That is brave.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “That is fair!”

They finished their small plates and sipped cool water. The lantern’s glow felt like a hug. The rain became soft, like a cat purring.

A sudden knock made them jump. Knock, knock.

It was Niko the neighbor mouse, peeking in. His whiskers twitched. “Hello,” he squeaked. “I heard giggles. My tummy is rumbling.”

Ellie’s heart thumped. She took another brave breath. “We have a plan,” she said. “We are saving snacks to share. Would you like a banana slice now?”

Niko’s eyes lit up. “Yes, please.”

Ellie gave him one slice and a smile. “We are taking turns, so everyone gets some.”

Niko nibbled. “Thank you. I feel seen and welcome.”

Pip, Lina, and Tobi clapped softly. The closet felt big and warm, like the whole world had squeezed in to say hello.

Soon, the rain slowed to tiny taps. Ellie tucked the reusable bag with the extra snacks under her arm. “Let’s visit the hallway shelf,” she said.

They stepped out together. Their feet made soft sounds on the rug—pat, pat, pat. They placed the bag on the shelf with a little note: “For everyone. From the Cozy Closet Club.”

Back in the hideaway, Ellie curled up. “Today we learned to be global citizens,” she said. “We cared for our bodies with good food. We took turns. We were brave. We helped our neighbors.”

Pip nodded. “We shared.”

Lina bleated. “We planned.”

Tobi added, “We did what is right.”

Ellie felt calm and proud. “Our world is big,” she whispered, “and our hearts can be big too.”

The End.
