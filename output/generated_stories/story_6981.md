# Whiskers Solves the Sock Puzzle

Whiskers was a clever cat with bright green eyes and soft, stripey fur. One sunny morning, he padded into the laundry room. The floor felt cool on his paws. The air smelled warm and soapy. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” like a sleepy bee.

“Hello, laundry room,” Whiskers purred. “What adventure do you have today?”

On the floor sat a basket of clean socks. They were all different colors—blue, red, yellow, and green. Some socks were long and some were small. Some had dots, and some had stripes like Whiskers. Next to the basket stood Mia, a little girl with gentle hands and a big smile.

“Oh, Whiskers,” Mia sighed. “I can’t find the sock pairs. I want to help Mama, but the socks are all mixed up.”

Whiskers twitched his whiskers. He loved to help. He loved to think, too. Thinking made his whiskers wiggle.

“Let’s use our clever cat brains,” Whiskers said. “We can solve this like a puzzle.”

Mia nodded. “A sock puzzle!”

Whiskers sat and looked carefully. He listened to the soft hum, “Whirr, whirr.” He felt the basket edge with his paw. It was smooth and warm from the sun.

“First,” said Whiskers, “we need a plan. Good thinkers make a plan.”

“What’s the plan?” asked Mia.

“Sort,” said Whiskers. “We sort by something we can see. Let’s sort by color.”

Mia clapped. “Color!”

They pulled socks out slowly. “Blue socks here,” said Mia, making a blue pile. “Red socks here,” said Whiskers, patting a red pile with his paw. The socks made a soft “flop” sound as they landed.

Soon there were piles of blue, red, yellow, and green socks. There was even a tiny pile with rainbow stripes.

“Now what?” Mia asked.

“Next,” said Whiskers, “we look for matches in each pile. A match is two socks that are the same. Same color, same dots, same size.”

Mia picked up two blue socks. One had little stars. One had tiny whales. “Are these a match?”

Whiskers sniffed. “Blue, yes. But the pictures are different. Stars and whales are not the same. Let’s find two with stars or two with whales.”

Mia smiled. “I get it!”

They looked closely. Whiskers narrowed his eyes and thought. Mia looked with her careful eyes too. “Here!” Mia said. “Two blue socks with stars!”

“Match!” Whiskers cheered. His tail swished happily.

They worked slowly, thinking step by step. Whiskers said, “We used a plan. We sorted. We checked for same. We can do this.”

The washing machine sang, “Beep, beep,” and the dryer made warm air that smelled like clean towels. Whiskers felt cozy and proud.

They found red matches and yellow matches. They found green socks with tiny frogs. But then Mia held up one lonely rainbow sock. It was bright and silly.

“I can’t find the friend for this one,” Mia said quietly.

Whiskers tilted his head. “Hmm.” He looked under the basket. He looked behind the dryer. He listened. He even patted the lint trap. “No sock here,” he said.

Mia frowned. “Maybe it is lost.”

“Maybe,” said Whiskers. “Let’s think. Where do socks hide? They can slip under, or they can stick to something.”

Whiskers pressed his ear to the dryer door. “Thump, thump,” went the tumbling towels. He remembered something. “Socks can stick to towels. They hold on like little kittens!”

Mia’s eyes grew wide. “Let’s check the big towel!”

They opened the dryer. Warm air puffed out, like a cozy hug. “Ahh,” said Whiskers, stretching. Mia shook the big towel. “Flap!” Out fell a rainbow sock, soft and warm.

“You found it!” Mia laughed. “You used your brain.”

Whiskers purred, “We used our brains. We asked a good question. We looked for clues. We tried a smart idea.”

They matched the two rainbow socks and placed them gently together. The basket now had neat pairs, all side by side like good friends.

Mama walked in and smiled. “Oh my! Look at these lovely pairs. Thank you, Mia. Thank you, Whiskers.”

Mia said, “Whiskers taught me a plan. Sort by color. Check for same. Ask questions. Try ideas.”

Mama hugged Mia and scratched Whiskers under his chin. “That is very clever.”

Whiskers noticed one tiny sock left on the counter. It was little and pink with a heart. He looked at the basket. There was a tiny pink sock there too. But Mia had already closed the basket.

Whiskers gently picked up the tiny sock with his teeth and placed it on top of the basket. “Here is a match for the little sock,” he meowed.

Mia clapped. “You are generous, Whiskers. You share your help.”

Whiskers blinked kindly. “Helping is sharing. Sharing makes hearts warm.”

They tied the socks in pairs with soft ribbons. Whiskers listened to the quiet room. The hum was gone. The air smelled sweet and clean. He felt calm and happy.

Mia looked at the neat basket. “We did it,” she said. “We used thinking, and we were kind.”

Whiskers nodded. “When things are mixed up, make a plan. Look closely. Ask good questions. Try one idea, then another. And share your help.”

Mia smiled. “That’s our sock plan!”

They both laughed. Whiskers curled up on the warm dryer, purring like a tiny engine. Mia carried the basket to Mama, feeling proud.

The laundry room seemed to wink at them, as if to say, “Good thinking, good hearts.”

The End.
