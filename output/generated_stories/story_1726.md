# Milly Mouse and the Great Art Room Mix-Up

In the bright, colorful art room, Milly Mouse scurried between the tall tables. Her tiny pink nose twitched as she smelled the sweet scent of paint and glue. Sunshine streamed through the big windows, making the crayons sparkle like rainbows.

"Oh my!" squeaked Milly, looking around with her bright black eyes. "What a mess!"

The art room was in complete chaos. Red paint was spilled on the floor. Blue crayons were scattered everywhere. The glue bottles had no caps, and paper scraps covered every surface like confetti.

Mrs. Robin, the art teacher, fluttered her wings worriedly. "Oh dear, oh dear! The children will be here soon for art class, and everything is mixed up!"

Milly's whiskers twitched thoughtfully. She loved solving problems! "Don't worry, Mrs. Robin. Let me think about this."

She sat on her tiny haunches and put her paw to her chin. "Hmm, what's the biggest problem first?"

Looking around carefully, Milly noticed something important. "The paint is still wet! If we don't clean it up first, everyone will slip and slide."

"You're absolutely right!" chirped Mrs. Robin. "But how can we clean it quickly?"

Milly's eyes sparkled with an idea. She scampered to the supply closet and found some soft paper towels. "If I roll in the towels, I can soak up the paint with my whole body!"

Rolling and tumbling, Milly soaked up all the red paint. Her gray fur was now spotted with red, but she giggled happily.

"Now what?" asked Mrs. Robin.

Milly looked at the scattered crayons. There were so many colors! "I could pick them up one by one, but that would take forever." She thought harder. "Wait! I have an idea!"

She found a big cardboard box and pushed it to the center of the room. "If I sweep all the crayons toward the box with my tail, they'll all gather in one place!"

Swish, swish, swish went Milly's tail. Soon, all the crayons rolled into a colorful pile by the box.

"Brilliant thinking!" praised Mrs. Robin.

But there was still one big problem. The glue bottles had no caps, and the glue was getting hard and sticky.

Milly sat quietly, thinking very hard. She noticed something shiny in the corner - bottle caps that had rolled away!

"The caps aren't lost," she realized. "They just rolled away! If I follow the trail of sticky spots, I can find where each cap belongs."

Like a tiny detective, Milly followed each sticky trail. She matched each cap to its bottle by checking which size fit perfectly.

"You used your thinking skills so well!" said Mrs. Robin admiringly.

Just as Milly put the last cap on, the children's voices echoed in the hallway.

"We did it!" squeaked Milly proudly. The art room was clean, organized, and ready for creativity.

When the children arrived, they found everything perfectly arranged. The crayons were sorted by color, the paint was clean and fresh, and the glue bottles were all capped tight.

"Wow!" said little Emma. "Everything looks so nice and neat!"

Mrs. Robin winked at Milly. "We had a very special helper who used her thinking skills to solve our problems."

Milly beamed with pride. She had learned that when faced with a big problem, the best thing to do was stop, think carefully, and solve one piece at a time.

From that day on, whenever there was a problem in the art room, everyone knew they could count on Milly Mouse and her wonderful thinking skills.

The End.
