# Leo’s Clever Color Plan

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright, curious eyes. Today, he padded into the creative art room at cub school. The room smelled like fresh paper and fruit-scented markers. Sunlight made the glitter sparkle. Paint brushes stood in jars like flowers.

“Hello, room!” Leo said with a happy grin. “I feel ready to make something new.”

Ms. Zebra, the art teacher, smiled. “Welcome, artists. Today we paint big pictures! Remember: keep your paws safe, use your tools carefully, and ask before touching someone’s art.”

Leo nodded. He liked being careful. He also liked trying new ideas. That made his whiskers tingle.

On the long tables were big sheets of paper, smooth and white. Next to them were paints: red like apples, blue like the sky, yellow like sunshine, and green like leaves after rain. There were smocks, too. Leo slipped on a smock. It was soft and a little squeaky, and it kept his fur clean.

“Roar!” said Leo softly, excited. “I will paint a rainbow jungle.”

His friend Tia the turtle scooted over. “I want to help,” she said.

“I like help!” Leo said. “But let’s make a plan.” He tapped his paw to think. “I want to try a new way. I call it the ‘Color Path.’ We make a path of colors with tape, then roll paint with toy wheels!”

Tia’s eyes got wide. “That sounds new and fun!”

Ms. Zebra came closer. “What a smart, safe idea. Wheels can roll paint if we keep it on the paper.”

Leo set down gentle rules. “We keep paint on the paper. We roll slowly. We ask before touching another’s art or body. And we say ‘Stop’ if we don’t like something.”

Tia nodded. “Yes. Stop means stop.”

They placed strips of tape in zigzags and circles. Leo pressed the tape gently. It felt sticky and cool. They dipped toy wheels into paint. The wheels hummed a tiny sound on the paper: whirr, whirr. Red rolled into blue. Yellow met green. The room was full of soft brush swishes and happy giggles.

Just then, Max the monkey bounced over. “Ooo! I want to roll fast!” He grabbed for Leo’s roller.

Leo stepped back and held his roller close. He took a brave breath. “Max, I like your energy. But this is my roller. Please ask first.”

Max blinked. “Can I use it?”

“Not yet,” said Leo kindly. “We can share later. For now, you can use that roller.” He pointed to an extra one. “We are going slow to keep it safe.”

Max wiggled, then nodded. “Okay.” He took the extra roller and dipped it in blue.

They rolled together. The colors made soft patterns. Tia clapped. “The Color Path is working!”

Soon, drip… drip… some paint slid toward the edge. “Uh-oh,” said Tia.

Leo’s ears perked. He looked around and had an idea. “Innovation time!” he said. “Let’s make a paint fence.”

“What’s that?” asked Max.

Leo found dry sponges and small blocks. “We put sponges at the edge to drink the extra paint. We line blocks like a wall to keep wheels from rolling off.” He placed them carefully. The sponges felt squishy, like little clouds. The blocks clicked softly as they touched.

Ms. Zebra watched with a proud smile. “That is clever thinking, Leo. You saw a problem and made a new plan.”

Leo beamed. He loved trying new ways. The paint fence worked. No more drips!

When they were almost done, Max reached to pat Leo’s mane. “Good job, Leo!”

Leo took a small step back. He used his brave voice. “Please don’t touch my mane. I like my space.”

Max pulled his hand back right away. “Sorry. I didn’t know.”

“Thank you for stopping,” said Leo. “High paw?” He held up a paint-free paw.

Max bumped it gently. “High paw!”

At the next table, a small puff of glitter floated up. “Achoo!” sneezed Tia. Leo remembered safety. He helped close the glitter lid. “We keep lids on when we’re not using them,” he said. “That keeps noses happy.”

They peeled off the tape together. Ooooh! Under the tape were bright, clean lines. The Color Path was a rainbow road through a jungle of shapes. It made everyone smile.

Ms. Zebra clapped her hooves. “Artists, gather round. What did we learn today?”

Leo lifted his chin. “I learned to try new ideas. When we had drips, we made a paint fence. That was innovation!”

Tia raised her flipper. “I learned that ‘Stop’ means stop. And asking is kind.”

Max waved. “I learned to go slow and keep paint on the paper. Safety helps us have fun.”

Ms. Zebra nodded. “Wonderful. New ideas plus safe choices and kind boundaries make art time great.”

Leo looked at his friends. His heart felt warm, like sunshine on his fur. “We did it together,” he said.

They washed paws with warm, soapy water. It smelled like oranges. Squeak, squeak went the sink. Their paws came out clean and soft.

At the drying wall, Leo wrote a tiny note on the corner of the painting: “Color Path by Leo, Tia, and Max.”

He whispered to the art room, “Thank you for helping us think.”

On the way out, Max asked, “Tomorrow, can we try a sparkle bridge?”

Leo grinned. “Yes! We can plan it safe, ask before touching, and make a new idea.”

They all nodded, tails and flippers and hands swishing happily.

The painting shone in the sun, bright and brave, just like Leo.

The End.
