# Gemma the Giraffe Makes a Plan

Gemma was a thoughtful giraffe who lived in a sunny house with a small home office. The office had a big wooden desk, a soft rolling chair, and a tall window that let warm light pour in like honey. On the desk sat a neat stack of crayons, a tick-tock clock, and a computer that hummed like a quiet bee.

“Good morning,” Gemma whispered to the room. She stretched her long neck and felt the gentle pop-pop of her joints. The air smelled like paper and lemon cleaner. Outside the window, birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!”

Gemma had many things to do. She had to finish a picture for her art class. She wanted to practice gentle neck stretches for exercise. She also needed to read a little book and tidy the desk. Her heart felt wiggly. Her tummy fluttered like a butterfly.

“That is a lot,” she said softly. “Can I do it all?” Her spots felt warm with worry.

Tick-tock went the clock.

Gemma took a deep breath. In…and out. “I need a plan,” she decided. “A plan is like a friendly map.”

She reached for a blue crayon. On a small notepad, she drew four boxes. “Box 1: Art. Box 2: Stretch. Box 3: Read. Box 4: Tidy.” She added little pictures—paintbrush, bendy giraffe, tiny book, and a happy broom. She smiled. “This feels good.”

Gemma made a brave choice. She set a tiny timer on the desk. It was round and red and went “beep!” when time was done. “I will try 10 minutes for art,” she said. “Just 10.”

Knock-knock went the door. It was Milo, a friendly mouse who liked to visit. He wore a tiny green scarf. “Hi, Gemma!” he squeaked. “Want to play a game?”

Gemma’s ears twitched. She wanted to say yes. She loved games. But she looked at her plan. She felt the wiggles again. “I can be brave,” she told herself.

“I want to play,” Gemma said kindly. “I also have my plan. May I finish Box 1 first? Then we can play a quick exercise game for Box 2. Will you help me?”

Milo’s whiskers wiggled. “Yes! I can cheer!”

Gemma sat at the desk. Crayons smelled sweet and waxy. She colored smooth lines—swish, swish. She drew the tall window, the tick-tock clock, and a smiling sun. The timer went beep!

“Box 1 is done!” Gemma said. Her chest felt warm and proud.

“Great job!” squeaked Milo. “What’s next?”

“Box 2: Stretch.” Gemma stood tall, hooves firm on the floor. “Let’s play ‘Reach the Cloud.’”

They stretched their necks up, up, up. Gemma breathed in and out. She felt the gentle pull in her shoulders. “I can feel my body getting strong,” she said. Milo stretched too, little paws in the air.

After a few breaths, the timer went beep! again. Gemma laughed. “Box 2 is done! That was fun.”

“Box 3: Read,” she said. Gemma chose a small book with big, bright pictures. She read in a soft, steady voice. “Once there was a seed…” Her voice sounded calm, like a warm blanket. Milo listened, eyes shiny. The book felt smooth in her hooves. The paper made a soft whisper when she turned a page.

Beep! went the timer. “Box 3 is done!” Gemma clapped. Her heart felt steady now.

“Only Box 4 left,” Gemma said. “Tidy time!”

She put the crayons back in their little cup. Click, click. She stacked the papers. She wiped the desk with a lemony cloth. “Ah,” she sighed, “so fresh.” The office looked neat. The window light made the desk shine.

Tick-tock went the clock. Gemma checked her plan. All four boxes had happy check marks. She had done it! She felt taller, even for a giraffe.

Milo cheered. “You did everything!”

Gemma nodded. “I made a plan. I used my timer. I took turns with my jobs. I was brave when I wanted to rush or stop.” She touched her chest. “I can do hard things. I can try, little by little.”

Just then, the door opened again. Breeze swirled in, cool and sweet. Gemma looked at Milo. “Now we can play for a little time.”

“Let’s do a victory wiggle!” Milo giggled.

They danced a tiny dance in the office. Gemma’s hooves tapped softly—tip, tap, tip. Milo spun like a leaf. The room felt bright and happy.

When they finished, Gemma sat in her rolling chair. She looked at her plan and smiled. The worry butterfly in her tummy was gone. In its place was a calm, cozy feeling, like warm tea.

Milo tugged her scarf. “Gemma, how did you do it?”

Gemma spoke slowly, so it was easy to hear. “I made a plan with simple steps. I used a friendly timer. I did one thing at a time. I took breaks to stretch. I asked for help. That is good time management.”

Milo nodded. “Time management helps us feel brave.”

“Yes,” said Gemma. “When we make a plan and try our best, we grow strong inside. We can be kind to ourselves. We can finish our jobs and still have time to play.”

Gemma drew a small star at the bottom of her notepad. “Star for courage,” she said. She drew a heart. “Heart for self-esteem.” She drew a clock. “Clock for time.”

She looked at Milo. “Tomorrow, we can make another plan.”

Milo squeaked, “I can’t wait!”

The End.
