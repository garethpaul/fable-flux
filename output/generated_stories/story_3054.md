# Benny Beaver's Sorry Letter

Benny Beaver sat on his peaceful front porch, feeling very sad. His big brown tail dragged on the wooden floor, and his whiskers drooped down. The morning sun felt warm on his fur, but Benny didn't feel warm inside.

"I made a big mistake yesterday," Benny said to himself, looking at his small paws. "I was so busy building my dam that I forgot to help my friend Rabbit when she asked me to."

Benny remembered how Rabbit had come to him with tears in her eyes. She needed help carrying heavy carrots to her burrow before the rain came. But Benny was so focused on stacking sticks that he said, "Not now, Rabbit! Can't you see I'm busy?"

Now Benny felt his tummy twist with worry. "Rabbit looked so hurt when I said that," he whispered. "I think I made her feel bad."

Benny's mama came out onto the porch carrying a steaming cup of tea. The sweet smell of honey filled the air. "Good morning, Benny. You look troubled. What's making you feel sad?"

"Mama, I think I hurt Rabbit's feelings yesterday," Benny said, his voice small and shaky. "I was too busy to help her, and now I feel awful inside."

Mama Beaver sat down next to Benny, her warm paw gently patting his back. "It sounds like you're feeling sorry for what happened. That feeling has a name - it's called regret."

"Regret?" Benny asked, tilting his head.

"Yes, sweet beaver. Regret is when we wish we had done something different. It's okay to feel this way - it means you care about your friend," Mama explained kindly.

Benny nodded slowly. "I do care about Rabbit. But how can I make her feel better?"

Mama smiled. "Well, when we hurt someone's feelings, we can say sorry. And sometimes, writing a letter can help us share our feelings clearly."

"A letter?" Benny's ears perked up. "But Mama, I'm not very good at writing yet."

"That's okay, little one. We can practice together. The most important thing is that your sorry comes from your heart."

Benny and his mama went inside and got paper and a big, chunky crayon. Benny worked very hard, his tongue sticking out as he concentrated. The letters were wobbly and some were backwards, but each one was made with love.

"Dear Rabbit," Benny wrote slowly. "I am sorry I did not help you. I was wrong. You are my good friend. Love, Benny."

When Benny finished, he felt lighter inside, like a heavy rock had been lifted from his chest. "Mama, my tummy doesn't feel twisty anymore!"

"That's because you're doing the right thing," Mama said, giving him a big hug.

Benny carefully folded his letter and walked to Rabbit's burrow. When Rabbit read the wobbly words, her eyes filled with happy tears.

"Oh Benny, thank you for saying sorry. I forgive you!" Rabbit said, giving him the biggest hug.

From that day on, Benny learned to notice his feelings and the feelings of others. And whenever he made a mistake, he remembered that saying sorry - especially in a letter written with love - could help heal hurt feelings.

The End.
