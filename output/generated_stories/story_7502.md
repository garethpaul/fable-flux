# Kody's Garden Shed Adventure

Kody the koala loved quiet places. With his soft gray fur and gentle black eyes, he always spoke in a calm, sweet voice. Today, he discovered something wonderful behind the big oak tree - an old wooden garden shed!

"Oh my," Kody whispered softly as he pushed open the creaky door. Sunlight streamed through dusty windows, making golden patches on the floor. The shed smelled like earth and old wood. Garden tools hung on the walls, and colorful flower pots sat on wooden shelves.

Suddenly, Kody heard a loud CRASH!

"ARGH! This stupid watering can won't fit!" shouted a gruff voice.

Kody peeked around a corner and saw a prickly hedgehog throwing garden tools around. The little hedgehog's spikes were all puffed up with anger.

"Hello there," Kody said gently. "I'm Kody. What's your name?"

The hedgehog stopped throwing things and looked surprised. "I'm... I'm Spike," he mumbled, his spikes slowly settling down.

"You seem upset, Spike," Kody said in his calm voice. "Would you like to talk about it?"

Spike's bottom lip trembled. "I'm trying to water the flowers outside, but everything in here is too big for me! The watering can is heavy, the hose is tangled, and I can't reach the high shelves!"

Instead of telling Spike what to do, Kody sat down on a small stool and patted the ground beside him. "That sounds really frustrating. I can see why you're feeling angry."

Spike sat down too, feeling safer with Kody's peaceful energy. "All the other animals think I'm just a prickly troublemaker. But I really want to help the garden grow!"

"I believe you," Kody said warmly. "You know what? I think we're different in wonderful ways. You're small and can fit in tiny spaces. I'm tall and can reach high places. Maybe we could work together?"

Spike's eyes lit up. "Really? You'd help me?"

"Of course!" Kody smiled. "Let's think peacefully about this problem."

They looked around the shed together. Kody's sharp eyes spotted a small watering cup on a low shelf. "Look, Spike! This little cup is perfect for you."

"And look!" Spike pointed to a narrow space behind the big tools. "I can squeeze back there and untangle the hose!"

Working as a team, they organized the shed. Kody reached the high shelves and handed small tools down to Spike. Spike crawled into tight spaces and sorted the seed packets. When they found the heavy watering can, Kody filled it while Spike guided him to the right flowers.

"This is so much better than being angry," Spike said, his spikes now soft and relaxed. "Thank you for listening to me, Kody."

"Thank you for sharing your feelings with me," Kody replied. "When we use peaceful words instead of throwing things, we can solve problems together."

As the sun began to set, they sat in their newly organized shed, watching the watered flowers sparkle with dewdrops outside the window.

"You know what, Kody?" Spike said. "Being different isn't bad. You're calm and I'm energetic. You're tall and I'm small. Together, we make a great team!"

Kody nodded peacefully. "And when we talk kindly to each other, even big problems become small."

From that day on, Kody and Spike met every morning in their special garden shed, taking care of the plants and practicing peaceful communication. They learned that differences make friendships stronger, and gentle words can solve any problem.

The End.
