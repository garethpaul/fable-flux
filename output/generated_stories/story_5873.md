# Benny Beaver's Garden Friends

Benny Beaver loved his cozy balcony garden high up in the tall apartment building. Every morning, he would waddle out with his tiny watering can, his flat tail swishing happily behind him. The morning sun felt warm on his brown fur as he tended to his special plants.

"Good morning, little tomatoes!" Benny chirped, sprinkling cool water on the red fruits. "Hello there, sweet carrots!" The orange vegetables peeked up from the rich, dark soil.

Benny had worked very hard to grow his garden all by himself. He had learned to plant seeds, water them every day, and pull out the weedy plants that tried to steal the sunshine. His little paws were always busy, busy, busy!

One sunny afternoon, Benny heard soft sniffling sounds coming from the balcony next door. He peeked through the green leaves of his bean plants and saw Luna, a little rabbit, sitting sadly by her empty flower pots.

"Hello there," called Benny gently. "I'm Benny! What's wrong?"

Luna wiped her nose with her soft white paw. "Hi, I'm Luna. I just moved here from far away, and I don't know how to grow anything. My grandmother back home had the most beautiful garden with flowers from our country, but I don't know how to make them grow."

Benny's kind heart felt heavy seeing Luna so sad. He looked at his flourishing garden, then back at Luna's empty pots. An idea began to grow in his mind, just like his vegetables!

"Would you like me to help you?" Benny asked with a warm smile.

Luna's ears perked up. "Really? But I don't have anything to give you back."

"That's okay!" Benny said. "Sharing makes gardens grow better! Wait right here."

Benny hurried to his garden shed and gathered some of his precious seeds. He had been saving them for next season, but seeing Luna's sad face made him want to share. He also grabbed his extra small shovel and a packet of special plant food.

"These are tomato seeds," Benny explained, holding up tiny brown seeds that felt rough in Luna's paws. "And these are marigold seeds - they make bright orange and yellow flowers!"

Luna's eyes sparkled like dewdrops. "In my country, we always planted marigolds for good luck!"

Together, they filled Luna's pots with fresh soil. The earth smelled rich and earthy, like rain on a summer day. Benny showed Luna how to make small holes with her finger, drop in the seeds, and cover them gently.

"Now we water them softly," Benny demonstrated, letting the water trickle like gentle rain. "And we wait patiently for them to grow."

Every day after that, Benny and Luna watered their plants together. They shared stories about their families and different ways people grew gardens around the world. Luna taught Benny a special song her grandmother used to sing to help plants grow strong.

Weeks passed, and green shoots began poking through the soil. Soon, Luna's balcony bloomed with cheerful orange marigolds and plump red tomatoes.

"Thank you for sharing with me, Benny," Luna said, picking a ripe tomato. "Now I can share with you too!" She handed him the juicy red fruit.

Benny bit into the sweet tomato, and it tasted even better because it came from friendship and sharing. His tail slapped happily against the balcony floor.

"Sharing makes everything grow better," Benny said with a big smile. "Gardens, friendships, and happiness too!"

From that day on, their balconies bloomed side by side, just like their wonderful friendship.

The End.
