# Poppy the Penguin's Porch Talk

Poppy the penguin loved mornings on the peaceful front porch. The porch was wide and sunny. A little breeze tickled Poppy’s cheeks. The wooden floor felt smooth and warm under her feet. She could smell mint from the garden and hear birds singing, “Tweet-tweet!”

Poppy set two tiny cups on a low table. “Mint tea time!” she chirped. The steam looked like little clouds. Poppy took a careful sip. “Mmm! Warm and gentle.”

Just then, her friend Tilly the turtle came up the steps. Her shell clicked softly on the wood. “Hi, Poppy,” Tilly said, smiling slow and sweet.

“Hi, Tilly!” Poppy flapped her little wings. “Let’s sit and sip.”

They rocked together on a squeaky porch swing. Creak, creak, creak. The sun felt cozy. A hummingbird buzzed by. The world felt calm.

Poppy lifted a small paintbrush. “I’m painting a sunny picture,” she said. “Would you like to help?”

“Yes, please,” Tilly said. Her eyes shone.

Poppy set out colorful paints. Yellow was bright like a lemon. Blue was soft like the sky. Green smelled a little like grass. “Let’s share,” Poppy said. “You can use the blue.”

Tilly nodded. “Thank you.”

They painted side by side. Poppy hummed. Tilly swayed. But then, oops! Tilly’s elbow nudged the paint cup. The blue paint wobbled… and splashed.

“Oh!” Poppy gasped. Blue dots landed on her picture and on her cup of tea. A drop landed on Poppy’s beak. Cold and sticky.

Tilly’s face drooped. “I’m sorry,” she whispered. “I didn’t mean to.”

Poppy’s tummy felt tight. Her cheeks felt hot. She looked at her blue-speckled picture. She wanted to shout, “Why did you do that?” But she remembered the porch rules. On the peaceful porch, they used calm words. They took deep breaths.

Poppy put her wing on her belly. In… and out. In… and out. The minty air helped. The birds kept singing, “Tweet-tweet!”

She looked at Tilly’s worried eyes. “I feel upset,” Poppy said softly. “My picture has blue dots now. I worked hard on it.”

Tilly blinked. “I hear you,” she said. “You feel upset because your picture changed. I would feel upset too.” She took a breath. “I’m sorry, Poppy. My elbow was clumsy.”

Poppy listened. Her tummy felt softer. “Thank you for saying sorry,” she said. “Can we fix it together?”

Tilly nodded quickly. “Yes.”

They got a cloth and dabbed the tea cup. Poppy giggled when the blue dot on her beak smeared into a silly mustache. “Look at me!” she laughed.

Tilly smiled. “Fancy penguin!”

They looked at the picture. Blue dots were still there. Poppy tilted her head. “Maybe the dots can be bubbles,” she said. “Or little blueberries in the sky. Or happy raindrops!”

Tilly’s eyes lit up. “We can add yellow suns and green leaves. The blue can be tiny ponds.”

Poppy tapped her beak. “Good idea. Let’s plan.” They pointed with the brush. “Blue dots become ponds here, and here. Yellow sun here. A big green tree there.”

“Together?” Tilly asked.

“Together,” Poppy said.

They painted in gentle strokes. Swish, swish. The brush tickled the paper. The porch swing creaked softly. The picture grew bright and friendly. The blue dots turned into shiny ponds with little fish. The yellow sun smiled. The green tree swayed.

When they finished, they sat back. Poppy’s eyes sparkled. “I like it. I like it a lot.”

Tilly let out a breath. “Me too.”

Poppy poured fresh mint tea. The steam rose again. “Tilly,” she said kindly, “next time, let’s keep the paint cup in the middle. That way, our elbows are safe.”

“That’s a smart plan,” Tilly said. “I will be careful. If I forget, please remind me.”

“I will,” Poppy said. She patted Tilly’s shell. “Thank you for listening.”

“Thank you for speaking kindly,” Tilly said. “It helped my heart feel calm.”

They clinked cups. Clink! The hummingbird zipped by again. The porch felt peaceful, like a big soft hug.

Poppy smiled. “Today I learned to use calm words,” she said. “I said how I felt. We fixed the problem together.”

Tilly nodded. “And I learned to listen, say sorry, and try again.”

They rocked on the swing, creak, creak. The mint leaves shivered in the breeze. The air smelled fresh. Their picture dried in the sun, full of ponds and sunshine and a big green tree.

Poppy looked at Tilly. “We are a good team,” she said.

“A peaceful team,” Tilly agreed. “With kind hearts.”

They sipped their tea and watched the bright day. Their porch felt safe and happy, a place for talking, forgiving, and taking care of each other.

The End.
