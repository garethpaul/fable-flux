# Buzzy Beaver’s Dinner Dance

Buzzy the busy beaver liked to make things, fix things, and help. Today, Buzzy had a big plan in the dining room. The table was long and shiny. The chairs were lined up like little soldiers. Sunlight made the spoons sparkle. It smelled like warm bread and sweet apples.

“I will make a happy dinner,” Buzzy said, patting his flat tail. “I can do it myself.” He rolled up his sleeves. He hummed a little tune. Tap-tap went his tail on the floor. Swish-swish went a napkin as he set it just right.

Buzzy placed plates, one by one. “Plate, fork, cup,” he said. “I can do it. I am strong. I can try.” He stood on his tippy toes to reach the napkins. He wobbled. He giggled. “I did it!” he cheered.

Knock, knock! Someone was at the door. It was his neighbor, Squeak the mouse. Squeak held a tiny basket of berries. “Hi, Buzzy! May I help?” Squeak asked with a smile.

Buzzy looked at the big table. He felt proud. “I can do it,” he said. Then he saw Squeak’s hopeful eyes. He remembered a warm thought: kindness makes hearts big. “Yes,” Buzzy said, “you can help. We can be kind and share the work.”

Squeak clapped. “I can put the berries in a bowl,” he squeaked.

They walked to the dining room. The floor felt cool and smooth on their feet. The air smelled like cinnamon. Buzzy placed cups. Squeak washed berries. Drip, drop went the water. Plip, plop went the berries.

Knock, knock! Another friend came. It was Twirl the squirrel with a bundle of fresh herbs. “I brought mint and thyme,” Twirl said, tail swishing. “Can I help too?”

Buzzy thought, I want to do it myself. But he also thought, Kindness means letting friends share. He smiled. “Yes, please. Your herbs smell so nice!”

Twirl sniffed the air. “Mmm, minty!” She scattered green leaves in a bowl. The leaves made a soft rustle sound.

Soon, the dining room looked lovely. Buzzy paused. He felt a warm glow in his chest. He looked at his friends. “Thank you,” he said. “I worked hard, and you were kind. We did it together.”

Squeak pointed at the corner. “Buzzy, you forgot something.”

Buzzy blinked. “What did I forget?”

“The music!” Twirl laughed. “Dinner needs a dance!”

Buzzy rubbed his chin. “I can fix that!” He tapped his tail on the wooden floor. Tap-tap-tap. Squeak squeaked a happy beat. Eee-eek! Twirl swished her tail like a ribbon. Swish-swish-swish.

Buzzy said, “Let’s make a Dinner Dance.” He showed them a simple step. “Step, step, wiggle-wiggle, clap!” They tried together.

“Step, step,” said Squeak, tip-toeing.

“Wiggle-wiggle,” sang Twirl, swishing her tail.

“Clap!” went Buzzy, smiling wide.

They laughed. Their feet made soft thumps. The cups tinkled. The sweet apple smell floated in the air. The room felt warm and happy. The lights made little sparkles on the spoons.

Knock, knock! It was Mrs. Duck with a loaf of bread. “I smelled dinner and dance,” she quacked. “May I join?”

“Yes!” the friends said in chorus.

Mrs. Duck fluffed her feathers. “Quack-cha-cha!” she joked. Everyone giggled.

“Let’s set one more plate,” Buzzy said. He reached for a plate, but it was high. He stretched. He almost dropped it. His paws shook. He took a little breath. “I can try,” he whispered. “I can ask for help too.”

“I can help,” said Squeak, climbing onto a chair.

“I can help,” said Twirl, steadying the plate with her paws.

“Thank you,” Buzzy said. “We can be strong and kind at the same time.”

They placed the plate together. Buzzy felt proud, not just for doing, but for sharing. “Kindness makes our work light,” he said. “And it makes our hearts bright.”

They all sat. Crunch went the bread. Pop went the berries. The mint smelled fresh. The apples were sweet and soft. Everyone said, “Mmm!”

After dinner, Buzzy stood up. “Friends,” he said, “I wanted to do it all myself. I worked hard. But you came with kind hands and big smiles. You brought berries, herbs, and bread. You brought dance and joy. Thank you for your generosity.”

Squeak nodded. “You let us help. That is kind.”

Twirl added, “You asked for help when you needed it. That is smart.”

Mrs. Duck quacked, “We made a happy table. That is love.”

Buzzy’s eyes felt shiny with happy tears. He wrapped his tail around his friends in a gentle beaver hug. “Let’s do the Dinner Dance one more time!”

They stood and danced around the dining room. Step, step, wiggle-wiggle, clap! Tap-tap went Buzzy’s tail. Eee-eek sang Squeak. Swish-swish went Twirl’s tail. Fluff-fluff went Mrs. Duck’s feathers. The room was full of music, full of smiles, full of kindness.

Buzzy said softly, “Today I learned: I can try by myself. I can also share. Kindness makes us strong. Generosity makes joy grow. Dancing makes it fun!”

Everyone cheered, “Yes!”

They sat together, warm and full, and the dining room felt like a big, bright hug.

The End.
