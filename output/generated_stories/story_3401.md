# Rosie Rabbit's Big Sharing Day

Rosie Rabbit loved her mama's home office. It had a big wooden desk, a soft spinning chair, and colorful books on tall shelves. The morning sun made everything sparkle through the window.

"Hop, hop, hop!" Rosie bounced around the office while Mama worked on her computer. The clicking sounds made a happy rhythm.

"I'm hungry, Mama," Rosie said, rubbing her fluffy white tummy.

"There are fresh carrots in the kitchen, sweetie," Mama said with a smile. "But remember to wash your paws first for safety."

Rosie hopped to the kitchen and found the most beautiful orange carrots she had ever seen. They were crunchy, sweet, and perfect! She counted them carefully. "One, two, three, four, five carrots!" 

Just then, the doorbell rang. "Ding-dong!"

"That must be your friend Benny Bear," Mama called from the office.

Rosie looked at her five perfect carrots, then at the door. Her tummy rumbled loudly. "Grumble, grumble!" She really wanted to eat all the carrots by herself.

Benny Bear knocked gently. "Knock, knock! Rosie, are you there?"

Rosie opened the door and saw Benny's sad brown eyes. "Hi, Rosie. I haven't eaten anything today. My tummy feels so empty and rumbly."

Rosie's ears drooped. She looked at her carrots, then at hungry Benny. What should she do?

"Come in, Benny," Rosie said slowly. "Let's go to Mama's office."

In the cozy office, Rosie sat in the big chair with her carrots. Benny sat on the soft carpet, his tummy making quiet grumbling sounds.

"These carrots look so yummy," Benny said quietly, not wanting to be rude.

Rosie's heart felt heavy. She really wanted all five carrots, but Benny was her friend. She remembered what Mama always said: "Sharing makes our hearts grow bigger and our friendships stronger."

"Benny," Rosie said, taking a deep breath. "Would you like some carrots? They're very crunchy and sweet!"

Benny's eyes lit up like sunshine. "Really? You would share with me?"

"Yes!" Rosie said, feeling warm and happy inside. "But first, let's wash our paws to stay safe and healthy."

They hopped to the bathroom together. The soap smelled like flowers, and the warm water felt nice on their paws.

Back in the office, Rosie carefully divided the carrots. "Two for you, two for me, and we can share the last one!"

"Crunch, crunch, crunch!" They munched their carrots together. The orange vegetables were crispy and delicious. The sweet juice made their mouths happy.

"These are the best carrots ever!" Benny said. "Thank you for sharing, Rosie. You're the kindest friend!"

Rosie felt her heart grow warm and full. Sharing the carrots made them taste even better! "Sharing is caring," she said proudly.

Mama smiled from her desk. "I'm so proud of you, Rosie. You showed great kindness today."

As they finished their healthy snack, Rosie and Benny played quietly in the office. They built block towers, read picture books, and giggled together.

"I'm so glad I shared," Rosie whispered to herself. "Having a happy friend is better than having extra carrots!"

From that day on, Rosie always remembered that sharing good food with friends makes everything taste better and makes friendships stronger.

The End.
