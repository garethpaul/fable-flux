# Rocco's Bathroom Band

Rocco the raccoon loved making music more than anything in the whole wide world. He had fuzzy gray fur with black stripes around his eyes and tail, and he could make beautiful sounds with anything he found.

One sunny morning, Rocco waddled into the big white bathroom. "What a perfect place for a concert!" he chirped, his little paws clapping together with excitement.

*Tap, tap, tap!* went his paws on the shiny bathtub. *Clink, clink, clink!* went his claws on the faucet. Soon, Rocco was making such loud, wonderful music that he didn't hear anything else.

"This is the BEST music ever!" Rocco sang loudly, spinning around and around.

But down the hall, Baby Bird was trying to take her very first nap in her new nest. The loud sounds made her tiny heart flutter with worry.

"Peep, peep," she called softly. "Please, could you play more quietly?"

Rocco was making so much noise, he couldn't hear her small voice at all.

Next door, Old Turtle was reading his favorite book about the ocean. The *BANG, CRASH, CLANG* made it hard for him to think about peaceful waves.

"Excuse me, young raccoon," he said in his slow, gentle voice. "Could you perhaps make softer music?"

But Rocco was having too much fun to listen. He banged even louder on the soap dispenser!

Then Mama Mouse came running with her three little babies. "Please, Rocco!" she squeaked. "My babies are scared of the loud sounds!"

The three tiny mice were hiding behind their mama, their whiskers trembling.

Finally, Rocco stopped his music and looked around. He saw Baby Bird's sad little face, Old Turtle's tired eyes, and the frightened mouse family.

"Oh no," Rocco whispered, his ears drooping down. "I wasn't listening to my friends."

His heart felt heavy and sad. "I'm so sorry," he said, sitting down on the soft bathroom rug. "I was having so much fun, I forgot to use my ears to listen."

Baby Bird flew down and perched on the sink. "It's okay, Rocco. We know you love music."

"But music is better when everyone can enjoy it together," said wise Old Turtle.

Rocco's eyes lit up with a wonderful idea! "What if we all make gentle music together?"

He showed Baby Bird how to make soft *tink, tink* sounds by gently tapping a toothbrush holder. Old Turtle discovered he could make peaceful *whoosh* sounds by slowly moving his flippers near the running water. Mama Mouse and her babies learned to make tiny *pitter-patter* rhythms with their little feet.

"Listen," whispered Rocco, "when we all play softly together, we can hear each other's special sounds!"

The bathroom filled with the most beautiful, gentle music. Each friend added their own special sound, and together they created something magical.

"This is much better than loud music all by myself," Rocco smiled, his tail swishing happily.

From that day on, Rocco always remembered to listen with his ears before making music. He learned that the best concerts happen when everyone feels happy and safe.

Baby Bird got her peaceful nap, Old Turtle could read his ocean stories, and the mouse family felt brave and joyful. And Rocco? He discovered that listening to his friends made his music even more wonderful.

"Good listening makes good friends," Rocco sang softly, and all his friends hummed along.

The End.
