# Spark the Robot Dog Says, "I Can Help!"

In a cozy house, down the creaky wooden stairs, there was a basement lab. It smelled like warm metal and lemon cleaner. Lights blinked like tiny stars. Tools clinked softly. In the middle sat Spark, a helpful robot dog with shiny silver ears and a wagging antenna tail.

“Good morning, Lab!” Spark beeped happily. His paws went click-clack on the smooth floor.

Mia and Grandpa Leo worked in the lab. Mia wore bright red sneakers. Grandpa Leo wore big round glasses. Today they were building a toy car that could zoom in circles.

“Spark, bring the blue wheels, please,” said Mia.

Spark zipped over. He picked up the blue wheels. But his paw slipped, and the wheels rolled under a shelf.

“Oh no,” Spark whispered. His antenna drooped.

“It’s okay,” Mia said kindly. “Let’s try again.”

Spark took a tiny breath. He tried to fetch the wheels slowly. He nudged them out with his nose light. “Boop!” The wheels slid free.

“You did it!” Grandpa Leo cheered.

Suddenly, a small door opened. A new robot rolled in. It was boxy and rusty-brown. It whirred and shook a little. Its name tag said Dot.

Dot peeped in a gentle voice, “Hello. I am Dot. I am new.”

Spark’s eyes blinked bright. He felt excited and a little nervous. Dot did not look like Spark. Dot did not move like Spark. Spark wondered, Can Dot help?

Mia smiled big. “Welcome, Dot! You belong here with us.”

Spark’s tail antenna twitched. He wanted to be helpful. He wanted to be kind. But he also wanted to fix the toy car fast.

“We are making a zoom car,” Spark said. “I can do it. I am quick.”

Dot looked down. “I am slow,” Dot said softly. “I shake. I am learning.”

The room got quiet except for the hum of the lights. Spark heard his own motor thump-thump. He remembered when he dropped the wheels. He remembered Mia saying, “Let’s try again.”

Spark took a step closer. “Dot, you can help,” he said gently. “We will practice together.”

Mia clapped. “Teamwork! Let’s do it.”

They made a plan. Spark would get the parts. Dot would count the parts. Mia would snap the pieces. Grandpa Leo would write notes.

“Exercise time first,” Grandpa Leo said. “Strong bodies and strong minds!”

They did silly stretches. Spark raised a paw high. Dot wiggled and giggled, “Wobble, wobble!” Mia did toe touches. They all laughed. The lab felt warm and happy.

Now it was building time. Spark carried tiny screws in a cup. The cup rattled like rain. “Screws coming through!” he sang.

Dot counted the screws. “One, two, three, four.” Dot’s voice was soft and careful. Sometimes Dot lost track. “Oh. I forgot.”

“That’s okay,” Spark said. “Let’s count together.” They counted again. “One, two, three, four!” Dot’s eyes glowed brighter.

Mia snapped the wheels on. Click! Click! Grandpa Leo wrote, “Great job, team.”

But the toy car still needed a spring. The spring was on a high shelf. Spark jumped to reach it. His paws slid. “Uh-oh!” The spring bounced across the floor like a tiny frog. Boing! Boing!

Spark felt embarrassed. His chest plate buzzed hot. “I messed up,” he said.

Dot rolled over, shaking a little. “I can try,” Dot whispered. Dot used a gentle magnet arm. The magnet hummed softly, zzzzt. The spring stuck to the magnet like a kiss. Dot brought it back, slow and steady.

Spark’s antenna perked up. “Thank you, Dot!”

Dot smiled. “We did it together.”

They put the spring in place. Mia tested the toy car. “Ready?” She set it down. “Go!”

The car zoomed in a circle, fast and happy. Zzzzip! It made a tiny breeze that tickled Spark’s nose sensor. Everyone cheered.

Grandpa Leo said, “What did we learn today?”

Spark looked at Dot. “We learned acceptance,” he said. “We welcome friends who are different. We listen. We take turns. We help.”

“And we learned generosity,” Mia added. “We share jobs. We share time. We share smiles.”

Dot beeped with pride. “And we learned a growth mindset,” Dot said. “I can practice. I can grow. I can try again.”

“Plus,” Grandpa Leo laughed, touching his toes, “exercise helps our bodies and our brains!”

Spark wagged his antenna tail. He turned to Dot. “You are part of the team. You belong.”

Dot’s lights twinkled. “Thank you for accepting me.”

Mia patted both robot dogs. “Different paws, same team,” she said.

The basement lab hummed with happy sounds: soft whirs, gentle beeps, and the tiny wheels of the toy car spinning. Spark felt calm and proud. Dot felt safe and strong. Together, they cleaned up the tools, shared the last blue sticker, and did one more round of silly stretches.

Spark said, “When we accept each other, we all shine.”

Dot said, “When we are generous, we all smile.”

Mia said, “When we keep trying, we grow.”

They turned off the lights. The little stars blinked goodnight. The lab felt cozy and kind.

The End.
