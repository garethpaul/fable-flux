# Leo Finds a Gentle Way

Every morning, the brave little lion cub named Leo padded to the garden shed. It was part of his routine. He liked the soft crunch of dry leaves under his paws. He liked the warm sun on his golden fur. He liked the smell of fresh soil and the sweet scent of tomatoes sleeping on their vines.

The garden shed was small and cozy. It had wooden walls that felt smooth. It had a tiny window that made a square of light on the floor. Inside were tools that hummed softly when the breeze pushed through the door. There were clay pots, a watering can, a broom, and a big soft rag that smelled like lemon.

Leo loved to stack the pots every day. He sorted them by size—small, medium, large. “This is my calm job,” he said. “Tap, tap,” the pots whispered as he stacked them high.

One day, as Leo reached for a pot, a quick shadow zipped by. “Eek!” squeaked a tiny field mouse with bright eyes. She popped out from behind the watering can. “This is my snack spot!”

Leo blinked. “I’m Leo,” he said. “I’m brave and gentle.” He practiced his brave face, then his gentle smile.

“I’m Pip,” said the mouse. Her whiskers wiggled. “I like crumbs and quiet. Your paws go stomp-stomp.”

Leo looked at his big paws. Stomp-stomp. He felt a little hot inside. “I don’t mean to stomp,” he said. “I’m just excited to stack.”

“Your excited is loud,” Pip whispered, covering her tiny ears.

Leo’s ears drooped. He felt a knot in his tummy. He liked his routine. He liked his calm job. Now it felt messy. “I’m feeling frustrated,” Leo said softly. “My feelings are big.”

Pip tilted her head. “My feelings are small but fast.”

Leo sat down on the cool floor. He took a deep breath in, and a slow breath out, like Mama Lion had taught him. “In… out… I can calm down.” He rubbed the soft lemon rag on his cheek. It felt smooth and smelled happy.

“I like quiet,” Pip said. “And I like crumbs.”

Leo nodded. “I like gentle and kind.” He looked at the tall stack of pots. “Maybe we can make a plan.”

Pip blinked. “A plan?”

“Yes,” said Leo. “A peaceful plan.” He smiled. “We can share the shed.”

Just then, a bumblebee buzzed by the window. “Bzzzz,” it sang. The square of light on the floor looked like a tiny stage. Dust danced like glitter in the sun.

Leo pointed with a paw. “This side can be my stacking space,” he said. “Tap, tap, quietly.” He tapped a pot very softly. “Tap.” The sound was like a tiny kiss.

Pip’s whiskers twitched. “And this side can be my snack space,” she said. She pattered to a corner and found a crumb of crust from the gardener’s lunch. “Nibble, nibble,” she said. “I will be quiet too.”

They both listened. Outside, a bird sang sweet notes. The shed door made a gentle creak. The lemon smell floated in the warm air.

Pip peeped at the broom. “That broom looks scary.”

Leo stood tall and brave. “I can put it in the corner where it sleeps,” he said. He lifted it slowly and set it down. “There. The broom is resting.”

Pip relaxed. “Thank you.”

Leo and Pip worked together. Leo stacked the pots: small, medium, large. He counted in a soft voice. “One, two, three.” Pip gathered crumbs and tiny seeds. She made a neat snack pile. “Nibble, nibble, tidy-tidy,” she sang.

Soon, the shed felt calm again. The square of light moved a little, like the sun was nodding.

“Leo,” Pip said, “you are big and brave. I am small and quick. We are different.”

Leo smiled. “Different is good. Different helps us too.” He looked at his paws. “I can step soft.” He looked at Pip. “You can speak up.”

Pip stood on her back legs. “If I feel worried, I can say, ‘I need quiet, please.’”

“And if I feel frustrated,” Leo added, “I can breathe in and out. I can ask for help. I can use gentle paws.”

They shook on it—paw to paw, paw to tiny paw.

Every day after that, Leo and Pip had a new routine. Leo came in the morning. He tapped the door twice, very softly. Pip squeaked twice back. “Tap-tap.” “Squeak-squeak.” It was their hello song.

Leo stacked. Pip snacked. Sometimes they told stories.

“I like the smell of tomatoes,” Leo said.

“I like the tickle of crumbs,” Pip said.

They laughed a little. “Hee-hee.”

One day, the wind whooshed and knocked a pot down. It rolled across the floor. “Clonk!” Leo felt the hot knot again. His tail twitched.

Pip saw his eyes get big. “Breathe,” she whispered. “In… out.”

Leo breathed. He picked up the pot with gentle paws. “Thank you, Pip,” he said. “I felt frustrated, but I calmed down.”

“You did great,” Pip smiled. “We used our plan.”

Outside, the bird sang a happy song. The lemon rag smelled sunny. The bumblebee buzzed a low hum, like a lullaby.

Leo looked at the neat shelves and the cozy snack corner. “We did this together,” he said. “We found a peaceful way.”

Pip nodded. “We can be different and still be friends.”

Leo’s heart felt warm and light. He loved his routine. He loved his new friend. He loved that his brave could be gentle.

“Peaceful plans help everyone,” he said.

“Every day,” Pip agreed.

They smiled in the soft, golden light.

The End.
