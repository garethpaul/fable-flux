# Spark the Robot Dog and the Song Inside

Beep-beep! Tap-tap-tap! Spark the robot dog wagged his shiny metal tail in the musical practice room. The room smelled like fresh wood and clean strings. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Drums rested in a row. A piano sat big and kind in the corner. A shelf held shakers, bells, and colorful scarves that felt soft like clouds.

“Good morning, Spark,” said Mia, a little girl with bright eyes. She hugged Spark’s smooth head. “I want to play a song. But I don’t know how to start.”

Spark’s ears blinked blue. “Woof-beep! We can try,” he said in a friendly robot voice. “Let’s listen first.”

Mia stood very still. She could hear the soft hum of the room. The piano keys whispered, “Plink.” A drum skin said, “Pum,” when Spark’s paw touched it gently. The scarves rustled like tiny winds.

“I hear sounds,” Mia said. “But I don’t hear my song.”

Spark tilted his head. “Your song is inside you,” he said. “Let’s use our imagination to find it.”

Mia’s eyes grew wide. “How do we do that?”

“Step one,” said Spark, lifting one shiny paw, “we breathe. In…and out.” They took slow breaths. “Step two,” Spark said, “we picture a place.”

Mia closed her eyes. “I see a rainbow garden,” she whispered. “There are giggling flowers and a small river that goes shh-shh.” She smiled.

Spark wagged. “Good! Step three: we match sounds to the picture. What does a giggling flower sound like?”

Mia picked up a little bell. It felt cool in her hand. She shook it softly. “Ting-ting-ting!” She laughed. “That is my giggling flower!”

“What about the river?” Spark asked.

Mia slipped her fingers over the piano keys, very light. “Shh…shh…shh,” the notes went, like water in the sun. Her face glowed. “I did it!”

Spark nodded. “You used your imagination. You trusted yourself. That is smart and brave.”

Mia touched a drum next. “This drum can be my feet walking on the garden path.” She tapped gently. “Pum…pum…pum.”

Spark listened, tail swishing. “We have a garden song. But we have a problem,” he said kindly. “The sounds do not stay together. They run away like little frogs.”

Mia looked puzzled. “How do we keep them?”

“We make a plan,” Spark said. “Education helps us. Let’s count. One, two, three, four. We can keep the beat.”

Mia nodded. “I can try.” She tapped the drum on each number. “One, two, three, four.” She played the piano on two and four. “Shh…shh…” Then she rang the bell on one. “Ting!”

Spark wagged. “You solved it! Your plan holds the song.”

Mia giggled. “It feels good to do it by myself.” She stood taller.

“Let’s add our voices,” Spark said. “Use words from your picture.”

Mia sang softly, “Hello, flowers, ting-ting-ting. River, river, shh-shh-shh. Feet go walking, pum-pum-pum. Garden song, we sing and hum.”

Her voice was sweet and small, like a cozy blanket. Spark hummed a soft beep-beep harmony. The room filled with warm notes. The air felt like honey and sunshine.

They played again. Mia counted. “One, two, three, four.” The bell giggled on one. The piano flowed on two and four. The drum walked on each beat. Mia smiled so big her cheeks felt like round apples.

Then the scarf slipped from the shelf and fell on the keys. “Clonk,” went the piano. Mia jumped. “Oh no! I made a mistake.”

Spark shook his head gently. “Not a mistake,” he said. “A surprise. We can use it.”

“How?” Mia asked.

“We imagine again,” Spark said. “What could make a clonk in your garden?”

Mia looked at the scarf. She touched its soft edge. “A friendly turtle,” she said. “It wears a shell that goes clonk when it bumps a rock.” She laughed. “Hello, Turtle!”

She added a low note on the piano. “Clonk.” Then she made a slow beat on the drum. “Pum…pum.” She sang, “Turtle walks slow, clonk and pum. That’s okay, we still have fun.”

Spark barked a happy beep. “You turned a surprise into music. You used your own ideas.”

Mia’s eyes shone. “I did it. I can solve problems. I can learn new things.”

They played the full garden song from start to end. The room felt bright and proud. When the last note faded, the quiet was soft and sweet.

Mia hugged Spark. “Thank you for helping me find the song inside.”

Spark nuzzled her hand. “You found it with your imagination. You counted and planned. You kept trying. That is self-reliance. That is learning.”

Mia nodded. “When I imagine, I can make my own music. When I learn, I can make it strong.”

Spark wagged. “Yes. Your ideas matter. Your mind is a musical room.”

Mia put the bell back, patted the drum, and closed the piano. “I can’t wait to make another song tomorrow,” she said.

“Woof-beep,” Spark said. “Your song is always with you.”

The End.
