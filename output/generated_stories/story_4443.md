# Polly's Helper Hideaway

In a cozy bedroom closet lived a bright green parrot named Polly. Her closet hideaway was filled with colorful art supplies, soft blankets, and a tiny mirror where she could practice her beautiful songs. Polly loved her special space, but she noticed something troubling happening in the house.

Every morning, Polly heard little Emma crying. "I don't want to brush my teeth!" Emma would wail. "I don't want to take a bath!"

Polly's heart felt heavy. She wanted to help Emma, but how could a parrot in a closet make a difference?

"Squawk! I have an idea!" Polly chirped excitedly, fluffing her bright feathers.

Polly began working on a special project. With her colorful crayons, she drew pictures of happy children brushing their teeth with sparkly toothpaste. She painted pictures of kids splashing in bubbly baths with rubber duckies. The sweet smell of her art supplies filled the closet as she worked day after day.

But when Polly tried to show Emma her pictures, Emma was too upset to notice.

"This isn't working," Polly sighed, her green feathers drooping. "Maybe I should give up."

Then Polly remembered what her grandmother always said: "When you want to help others, never give up! Try, try again!"

Polly thought harder. "What if I make helping fun?" she wondered aloud.

The next morning, Polly flew out of her closet hideaway with a new plan. She perched near Emma's bathroom and began to sing:

"♪ Brush, brush, brush your teeth,
Make them sparkly white!
Up and down and all around,
Morning, noon, and night! ♪"

Emma stopped crying and giggled. "That's a funny song!"

"Squawk! Want to hear my bath song?" Polly asked, dancing on her perch.

"♪ Splash, splash in the tub,
Bubbles everywhere!
Wash your hands and wash your face,
And don't forget your hair! ♪"

Emma clapped her hands with delight. The bathroom filled with the sound of happy laughter instead of tears.

Every day, Polly volunteered to be Emma's hygiene helper. She sang tooth-brushing songs that made Emma want to brush for the full two minutes. She created splashing games that made bath time feel like playing in a magical bubble kingdom.

"Polly, your songs make everything more fun!" Emma said, giving the parrot a gentle pet.

Soon, Emma looked forward to her daily routines. She would call out, "Polly! It's tooth-brushing time!" and run to the bathroom with excitement.

Polly felt warm and happy inside. Her persistence had paid off! By using her creativity and never giving up, she had found a way to help Emma develop healthy habits.

Other children in the neighborhood heard about Polly's wonderful songs. Soon, Polly was volunteering to help lots of families make hygiene routines fun and enjoyable.

From her closet hideaway, Polly had discovered something amazing: when you use your special talents to help others, you make the whole world a little bit brighter.

"Helping others feels even better than singing," Polly chirped happily, preening her beautiful green feathers in her tiny mirror.

And from that day forward, Polly continued to volunteer her time and talents, proving that even the smallest helper can make the biggest difference when they care about others and never give up.

The End.
