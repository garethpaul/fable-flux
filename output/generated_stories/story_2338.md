# Benny Beaver Builds on the Rooftop

Benny was a busy beaver with bright brown fur and a big flat tail. One sunny morning, Benny climbed the steps to the rooftop garden of his apartment. The rooftop felt warm. The breeze was gentle and smelled like flowers and fresh leaves. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” over the city.

Benny clapped his paws. “Today, I will build a tiny splash pond right here on the roof,” he said. “It will be for everyone to cool their toes. We can move, jump, and play outside!”

His friend Mia, a little mouse, peeked from behind a pot of mint. “Hi, Benny! Can I help?”

“Yes!” said Benny. “Helping hands make work feel light.”

Benny and Mia brought up small buckets of water, smooth stones, and soft sand. They wore sun hats. Benny’s hat was red with a blue stripe. Mia’s hat had little stars. The sun felt warm on their faces. The rooftop garden hummed with bees and the rustle of leaves.

Benny set the stones in a circle. “Plunk, plunk,” went the rocks. He patted the sand. “Pat, pat, pat.” He hummed a building song. Mia carried tiny plants and put them near the stones. “This will be so fun,” she said.

Just then, a strong gust of wind whooshed past. “Whoooosh!” The wind pushed the light sand. Some stones rolled away. The water bucket tipped and splashed Benny’s tail.

Benny’s eyes got wide. “Oh no!” he cried. His cheeks felt hot. His paws squeezed tight. “I worked so hard,” he said. “Now it’s all messy.”

Mia touched his paw. “It’s okay to feel mad,” she said softly. “Let’s take a calm breath together.”

Benny nodded. He put one paw on his belly. “In,” he said, taking a slow breath. “Out,” he said, blowing like the wind. He did it again. In. Out. His shoulders relaxed. His cheeks felt cool again.

“Thank you, Mia,” said Benny. “I feel better. I can try again.”

They looked around the rooftop. There were planters, a little bench, and a low wall. Benny had an idea. “We can build a wind shield,” he said. “We will use the bench and my extra boards. We will keep the sand safe.”

Mia smiled. “Smart plan! Let’s do some outdoor moves to feel strong first.”

They did big arm circles. Swish, swish. They touched their toes. They hopped like bunnies. Hop, hop, hop! The city sounds seemed to clap for them—cars far below going vroom, vroom, and a dog barking, “Woof!”

Now they were ready. Benny stacked boards behind the stones. Mia held them steady. “Thank you for helping,” said Benny.

“You’re welcome,” said Mia. “Together is better.”

They rebuilt the stone circle. Plunk, plunk. Benny packed the sand with his wide tail. “Swoosh, swoosh.” He carried water up in careful steps. “Step, step.” Mia added mint leaves nearby. The leaves smelled fresh and cool. A butterfly landed for a second and fluttered away.

Soon, a small splash pond sat on the rooftop. The water sparkled in the sun. Benny dipped a paw. “Ahh, cool!” he said. Mia dipped a toe. “Tee-hee! It tickles!”

A pigeon waddled close. “Coo-coo?” it asked.

“Hello, Pigeon,” said Benny. “Would you like a sip?” He set a tiny dish of water by the edge.

“Coo!” said the pigeon happily. It took a drink and flapped its wings.

Benny saw two children from the building—Sam and Lila—peek through the rooftop door with their grandma. “Can we see?” asked Lila.

“Yes,” said Benny. “This pond is for sharing.”

Sam smiled. “We brought a small watering can,” he said. “Can we help water the plants?”

“Please do,” said Benny. “Outdoor work is good for our bodies. We move, we breathe, and we care for living things.”

Sam poured water on the mint. Lila gently patted the sand. Grandma sat on the bench and clapped. “What a kind group,” she said. “Look how you helped each other. You stayed calm when it was hard.”

Benny felt proud. He remembered the windy moment. He took a little breath and smiled. “When I felt mad, I breathed in and out,” he said. “Then I tried again. Thank you all for helping.”

“Teamwork!” shouted Mia.

They took turns splashing their toes. Splash, splash! They played a game: “Find the round stone!” Lila found a shiny one. “Here it is!” she laughed. Benny gave her the shiny stone as a gift. “This is for you,” he said. “You were gentle and kind.”

Lila hugged the stone. “Thank you, Benny,” she said. “You are generous.”

The sun began to set. The sky turned pink and gold. The rooftop smelled like mint and warm wood. The breeze was soft now. Everyone stretched their arms up to the sky and took one more calm breath.

Benny packed away the buckets. He looked at the pond and the happy faces. “We moved our bodies outside,” he said. “We felt the sun and the wind. We helped our friends. We kept trying even when it was hard.”

Mia nodded. “That is the lesson,” she said. “Outdoor time helps us feel strong and calm. Kindness and patience help us build good things. Sharing makes joy grow.”

Benny’s tail gave a gentle thump. “Tomorrow,” he said, “we can play again on the rooftop. We can water plants, watch clouds, and splash our toes.”

Everyone waved. “See you tomorrow!”

The End.
