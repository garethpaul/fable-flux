# Ollie Otter and the Fair Seat

Ollie was a playful otter with soft whiskers and bright, curious eyes. He loved to wiggle, giggle, and sing little songs. Today, Ollie climbed into the family car with Mama, Papa, and his big cousin, Mia. The car smelled like warm vanilla and oranges from a snack. The seat was cool and smooth under Ollie’s paws, and his car seat straps were cozy like a hug.

“Click, click,” said Papa, buckling Ollie in. “Safety first.”

“Safety keeps us safe,” Ollie sang. “Click, click!”

Mia slid into the back seat too. She held a shiny red toy car. It was small and fast. It made a tiny rattle sound when it rolled. Ollie’s eyes got big.

“I want a turn,” Ollie said, wiggling his whiskers.

“I had it first,” Mia said, hugging the toy to her shirt. “I’m still playing.”

Ollie felt a big feeling in his tummy. He reached out his paw. The car bumped, and the toy fell onto the floor with a soft “thup.”

“Hey!” Mia frowned, and her tail (she was an otter too!) twitched.

Mama turned her head a little. “I hear upset voices. What happened?”

Ollie’s cheeks warmed. He looked at his paws. The car hummed and the windows showed trees whooshing by. “I wanted a turn,” he said. “I reached, and the toy fell.”

Mia held her arms tight. “He grabbed. That’s not fair.”

Papa’s voice was calm like a slow river. “Let’s talk about fair. Justice means being fair and kind. Justice means everyone gets a turn and we keep each other safe.”

Ollie sniffled. “Justice?” He tried the new word. It felt big and round in his mouth. “What does justice do?”

Mama smiled softly. “Justice helps us make things right. When there’s a problem, we listen, tell the truth, and make a fair plan.”

Ollie took a deep breath. In, out. He patted his own chest. “Self-care,” he whispered. “Take a breath when I’m mad.” His tummy felt softer.

He looked at Mia. “I’m sorry I grabbed,” he said. “I didn’t use safe paws.”

Mia’s face relaxed a little. “Thank you for saying sorry,” she said. “I was scared when you reached. It didn’t feel safe.”

Papa nodded. “Good words. We also need a fair plan.”

Ollie wiggled his whiskers and thought. The car seat smelled like clean soap. The engine made a steady “mmmmm” sound. He thought about the shiny red toy and about justice.

“I have an idea,” he said. “We can make turns with a song. When the song ends, it’s the other person’s turn.”

Mama clapped her paws softly. “A turn-taking song! That can be fair.”

Mia touched the toy car, then looked at Ollie. “I can finish this lap,” she said kindly, “then you can have a lap too.”

“Thank you,” Ollie said. “I will keep my paws to myself and wait.”

While he waited, Ollie practiced self-care. He held his little water bottle and took a sip. The cool water tickled his tongue. He looked out the window and counted trees. “One, two, three,” he whispered. He hummed a soft tune to stay calm. His body felt safe and quiet.

“Ready?” Mia asked. She rolled the toy once more. “Vroom!” It sounded like a tiny bee. Then she placed the toy in Ollie’s lap. “Your turn.”

Ollie’s chest felt warm. “Thank you,” he said. He rolled the red car on his knees. “Vroom, vroom!” He wanted to roll it fast, but he remembered safety. He kept the toy close and used gentle paws.

Papa smiled in the mirror. “You both are using justice. You made a fair plan. You’re being safe.”

Ollie nodded. “Justice is fair and kind. Justice keeps people safe.”

They took turns while the family car moved down the road. When it was time to switch, Mama sang their new turn-taking song:

“Time to share, time to care,
When the song is done,
We change with flair!”

When the song ended, Ollie handed the toy to Mia with a little bow. “Your turn,” he said.

Mia giggled. “Thank you, Ollie. That was fair.”

Soon the car slowed. The smell of warm bread blew in through the window. They were almost home. Ollie felt proud. He had a big feeling, he took care of his body, he used his words, and he helped make a plan. That felt like justice in action.

Mama looked back. “What did we learn?”

Ollie held up his paw. “Justice means fair turns,” he said.

Mia held up her paw. “Justice means safe paws and safe bodies.”

Papa held up his paw. “Justice means listening and telling the truth.”

They stacked their paws together in the air. “Justice, safety, and care!” they cheered, and then they laughed.

Ollie wiggled and sang, “Click, click! Safety first. Justice too! I can be fair, and so can you.”

The family car hummed, the sun felt warm, and everyone’s hearts felt light. They had a problem. They found a solution. They chose justice, they used conflict-resolution, and they took care of themselves and each other.

The End.
