# Ellie Speaks Up

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved playing in her family's cozy living room. The soft, colorful carpet felt wonderful under her tiny feet, and sunlight streamed through the big windows, making everything warm and bright.

Today, Ellie's older cousins came to visit. They were much bigger than her, with long trunks and loud voices that echoed through the room.

"Let's play soccer!" trumpeted Cousin Max, bouncing a red ball with his trunk.

"Great idea!" said Cousin Lily, flapping her big ears excitedly.

Ellie's little trunk wiggled with excitement. She loved sports and wanted to join in! But when she stepped forward, Max shook his head.

"Sorry, Ellie. You're too small to play with us. Why don't you go color instead?" He pointed to some crayons scattered on the coffee table.

Ellie's ears drooped. She felt sad and left out. The living room suddenly seemed too quiet, except for the *thump, thump* of the soccer ball bouncing around.

For a few minutes, Ellie sat by herself, watching her cousins play. The ball made a satisfying *whoosh* sound as it flew through the air. She really, really wanted to join them.

Then Ellie remembered something important her mama had taught her: "If something doesn't feel fair, you can speak up for yourself in a kind way."

Taking a deep breath, Ellie walked over to her cousins. Her heart beat fast like a little drum in her chest.

"Excuse me," she said in her sweetest voice. "I know I'm smaller, but I'm really good at sports too! Can I please try to play?"

Max and Lily stopped playing and looked at her.

"But you might get hurt," said Lily, sounding worried.

"I'm careful," Ellie replied. "And I've been practicing! Watch this!" 

Ellie gently picked up the ball with her little trunk and balanced it perfectly. Then she tossed it in a high arc that landed right where she aimed.

"Wow!" gasped Max. "That was amazing!"

"Can you teach us that trick?" asked Lily, her eyes wide with wonder.

Ellie beamed with pride. "Of course! I learned it by watching sports on TV and practicing every day."

The three cousins spent the rest of the afternoon playing together. They discovered that Ellie was not only good at soccer, but she also knew lots of interesting facts about different sports from around the world.

"Did you know that in some places, people play soccer with their feet AND hands?" Ellie shared excitedly.

"Really?" said Max. "That sounds fun!"

When it was time for art, Ellie showed them how to draw soccer balls and sports equipment. Her cousins were impressed by how much she knew about both sports and art.

As the sun began to set, painting the living room in golden light, Max and Lily apologized to Ellie.

"We're sorry we didn't include you at first," said Max. "We learned that being small doesn't mean you can't do big things!"

"And we learned so much from you today!" added Lily. "Thank you for speaking up and showing us what you could do."

Ellie felt warm and happy inside. "Thank you for listening to me and giving me a chance!"

That evening, as they all curled up together on the soft living room rug, Ellie knew she had learned something very important: when you speak up for yourself kindly and show others what you can do, wonderful things can happen.

The End.
