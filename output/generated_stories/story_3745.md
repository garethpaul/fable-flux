# Chirpy's Fair Race

In a cozy study corner filled with colorful books and soft cushions, lived a little yellow bird named Chirpy. Chirpy loved to sing sweet songs while perched on the wooden bookshelf. Her favorite spot was right next to the window where warm sunlight made her feathers sparkle like gold.

"Tweet-tweet-tra-la-la!" Chirpy sang happily every morning. She loved making music, but today something was bothering her little heart.

Outside the window, Chirpy could see the playground where all the forest animals were getting ready for the Big Spring Race. There were rabbits hopping in place, squirrels stretching their tiny paws, and even a slow turtle practicing his steps.

"I want to join the race too!" Chirpy chirped excitedly. She fluttered down from her bookshelf and flew outside to the starting line.

But when she arrived, Bruno the big brown bear shook his head. "Birds can't run in our race," he said with a gruff voice. "You have wings! That's not fair to us land animals."

Chirpy's little heart felt heavy. "But I want to compete too," she said sadly. "I promise I won't fly. I'll only use my tiny feet to run, just like everyone else."

Bruno crossed his arms. "No way! Birds don't belong in running races."

The other animals looked uncomfortable. They could see that Chirpy really wanted to participate, and she was being very honest about following the rules.

Wise old Owl, who was sitting on a nearby branch, hooted softly. "Now wait a minute, Bruno. Let's think about what's really fair here."

"What do you mean?" asked Rosie the rabbit, twitching her pink nose.

"Well," said Owl, adjusting his round glasses, "Chirpy promised to only use her feet, just like the rest of you. If she keeps that promise, then she's following the same rules as everyone else. That sounds fair to me."

Chirpy nodded eagerly. "Yes! I'll keep my wings folded tight against my body. I'll only run with my little orange feet. Please let me try!"

Bruno thought for a moment. The warm sunshine made him feel sleepy and less grumpy. "I guess... if you really promise to only use your feet..."

"I promise!" Chirpy sang out. "Cross my heart and hope to fly!"

All the animals giggled at her funny saying.

The race began with a loud "READY, SET, GO!" from Owl. Chirpy kept her promise, running as fast as her tiny orange feet could carry her. Her wings stayed folded tight against her bright yellow body. 

She huffed and puffed, working hard to keep up with the bigger animals. Even though she was small, she didn't give up. She completed every step of the race course, from the big oak tree to the babbling brook and back again.

When the race ended, Bruno the bear came over to Chirpy. "I'm sorry I was mean to you," he said, his voice much gentler now. "You kept your promise and ran fairly. You should be proud!"

Chirpy beamed with joy. "Thank you for giving me a chance!"

From that day on, all the animals learned an important lesson: being fair means giving everyone a chance to participate when they follow the same rules. And whenever they had races, they made sure everyone who wanted to compete could join in, as long as they played fairly.

Chirpy sang her happiest song that evening, perched in her study corner. She had learned that standing up for what's right and keeping promises makes everyone feel good.

The End.
