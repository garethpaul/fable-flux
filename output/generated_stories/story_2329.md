# Benny Beaver's Mindful Garden Morning

The sun peeked over the city buildings as Benny Beaver woke up on his cozy balcony home. His whiskers twitched with excitement.

"Today I'll make the perfect garden!" Benny announced, jumping out of his tiny bed.

Benny loved his balcony garden. Colorful pots lined the railings, filled with tomatoes, carrots, and bright flowers. But today felt different. Today felt rushed.

"Water the plants! Check the soil! Pick the vegetables!" Benny chattered to himself, scurrying from pot to pot.

He grabbed his little watering can and splashed water everywhere. SPLASH! Water dripped on his fur. SPLASH! Water missed the plants completely.

"Oh no!" Benny groaned. His tail slapped the ground in frustration.

Mrs. Robin, who lived in the nearby tree, fluttered down to the balcony railing.

"Good morning, Benny," she chirped sweetly. "You seem very busy today."

"I have SO much to do!" Benny panted, wiping water from his whiskers. "My garden needs everything RIGHT NOW!"

Mrs. Robin tilted her head. "But Benny, gardens grow slowly. They need gentle care."

Benny stopped running around. He looked at his messy balcony. Water puddles sparkled in the morning light. His plants looked confused and droopy.

"I wanted everything perfect," Benny sighed.

"Perfect gardens start with taking one slow breath," said Mrs. Robin wisely.

Benny sat down on his favorite stepping stone. He closed his eyes and took a deep, slow breath. The morning air smelled like fresh dirt and blooming flowers.

"Now," said Mrs. Robin, "what does your garden really need?"

Benny opened his eyes and looked carefully. Really carefully this time.

"The tomatoes are thirsty," he noticed. "But just a little bit."

Benny filled his watering can with the perfect amount of water. He poured it slowly, watching each drop soak into the dark soil.

"The carrots need their leaves trimmed," he observed next.

With his tiny paws, Benny gently removed the old leaves. He moved slowly, feeling each leaf between his fingers.

"And the flowers want their dead petals picked," he discovered.

One by one, Benny carefully removed the brown petals. Underneath, he found bright new buds ready to bloom.

Mrs. Robin watched approvingly. "How does your garden look now?"

Benny stepped back and smiled. His plants stood tall and happy. The morning sun made everything glow golden. His balcony felt peaceful and calm.

"It looks... just right," Benny said softly.

"And how do YOU feel?" asked Mrs. Robin.

Benny touched his chest. His heart felt warm and quiet instead of racing and worried.

"I feel just right too," he realized.

Mrs. Robin spread her wings. "When we slow down and pay attention, everything becomes easier."

Benny nodded, understanding. "Taking care of my garden means taking care of myself too."

From that day on, Benny started each morning the same special way. He would sit on his stepping stone, take three slow breaths, and look carefully at his garden. He learned that rushing made everything harder, but being mindful made everything better.

"Good morning, garden," Benny would whisper each day. "Let's grow together, slowly and gently."

And his balcony garden became the most beautiful, peaceful place in the whole city.

The End.
