# Chirpy's Big Game Dream

Deep in Professor Oak's basement lab, a little yellow bird named Chirpy lived in a cozy cage near the window. Chirpy loved to sing beautiful songs that echoed through the quiet lab filled with bubbling beakers and colorful experiments.

"Tweet, tweet, la-la-la!" Chirpy sang every morning, watching the sunlight dance across the shiny lab equipment.

One day, Chirpy heard exciting sounds coming from upstairs. *Bounce, bounce, bounce!* The professor's children were playing basketball in the yard.

"I want to play sports too!" Chirpy chirped loudly. "Tweet, tweet! I want to play!"

But Professor Oak was busy mixing purple and green liquids. He didn't hear Chirpy's song. The little bird felt sad. His bright yellow feathers drooped, and his cheerful song became quiet.

"Nobody listens to me," Chirpy whispered. "I'm just a small bird in a big lab."

Chirpy looked around the basement lab with his bright black eyes. He saw cardboard boxes, colorful tape, scissors, and empty bottles. Suddenly, he had a wonderful idea!

"I can make my own sports equipment!" Chirpy sang excitedly. "Tweet, tweet! I'll craft what I need!"

First, Chirpy used his tiny beak to tear small pieces of cardboard. He rolled them into little balls. *Crinkle, crinkle!* The cardboard made fun sounds.

Next, he found a small plastic bottle cap. "This will be my basketball hoop!" he chirped happily.

Chirpy worked very hard. He used his claws to arrange tape in a circle on the lab table. The sticky tape felt smooth under his tiny feet. Soon, he had made a perfect mini basketball court!

"Tweet, tweet! Look what I made!" Chirpy sang proudly, bouncing his cardboard ball with his beak.

Professor Oak heard the excited singing and came downstairs. His eyes grew wide when he saw Chirpy's amazing creation.

"Chirpy! Did you make this sports court all by yourself?" Professor Oak asked in amazement.

"Tweet, tweet! Yes!" Chirpy sang loudly. "I wanted to play sports like the children upstairs. I used my voice to ask, and I used my creativity to make it happen!"

Professor Oak smiled warmly. "You showed great self-advocacy, Chirpy. You spoke up for what you wanted, and when I didn't hear you, you found another way to solve your problem."

"What's self-advocacy?" Chirpy asked, tilting his little yellow head.

"It means speaking up for yourself and finding ways to get what you need," Professor Oak explained gently. "You did both today!"

From that day on, Professor Oak made sure to listen carefully to Chirpy's songs. He even built Chirpy a bigger sports area with tiny hoops, ramps, and obstacle courses.

Every morning, Chirpy would play sports, sing happy songs, and practice speaking up when he needed something. The basement lab filled with joyful tweets and the sounds of bouncing balls.

"Tweet, tweet! I learned that my voice matters!" Chirpy sang proudly. "And when I craft solutions and speak up for myself, wonderful things happen!"

The other lab animals heard Chirpy's story and learned to use their voices too. Soon, the basement lab became the happiest, most active place in the whole house.

The End.
