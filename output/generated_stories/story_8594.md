# Pippa Penguin Shares the Shed

Pippa was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a wiggly walk. One sunny morning, she waddled to the little garden shed at the edge of the yard. The shed smelled like fresh wood and sweet soil. Sunlight made dust sparkle in the air. Pippa loved this cozy place.

“Good morning, shed!” Pippa chirped. She opened the squeaky door. “Eeeek!” sang the hinges. Inside, she saw boxes, brushes, soft rags, and a shiny little radio.

Pippa tapped the radio. Music floated out—soft and bouncy. “Oh! Let’s dance!” she said. She flapped her wings. Tap-tap went her feet on the wooden floor. Swish-swish went her scarf. Pippa giggled. Dancing made her heart feel warm and light.

Just then, Pippa heard a tiny voice. “Hello?” squeaked Mouse Mia. Mia peeked in with her whiskers twitching. “May I borrow a brush? We are painting a sign for the park.”

Pippa smiled. “Yes! Sharing is caring,” she said. She picked up a soft brush and held it out. Mia frowned. “That one is too big,” she said softly. “Do you have a small, smooth brush?”

Pippa looked around. She found a small brush tucked in a cup. It felt gentle in her flipper. “Here you go,” she said. Mia’s eyes shone. “Thank you! You are very kind.”

Pippa felt proud. “I like to help,” she said. “I like to share.” Mia waved and hurried out.

The music played. Pippa twirled again. The wooden floor felt cool and the air smelled like lemon oil. “Dance, dance,” she sang.

The door creaked once more. “Eeeek!” Duck Dan poked his head in. “Hi, Pippa. I need a rake to clean leaves at the playground. We’re volunteering today.”

Pippa clapped her flippers. “Volunteering is great! We can help together.” She waddled to the corner. “Here is a rake, Dan.” Dan tilted his head. “Hmm, I see two rakes. One big, one small. Which one can I have?”

Pippa thought. She liked the small rake for her own tidy piles. She hugged it tight. “Maybe you can take the big one,” she said slowly.

Dan looked at his wings. “The big one is heavy,” he said. “The small one fits me.” Pippa felt a squeeze in her tummy. She wanted to keep the small rake. But she remembered Mia’s happy eyes. She remembered the word she said. Sharing.

Pippa took a deep breath. The shed smelled like warm straw and paint. She listened to the gentle music. “I can share,” she whispered to herself.

“Dan,” Pippa said out loud, “you can use the small rake. I will use the big one. We can rake together.”

Dan’s beak spread in a wide smile. “Thank you, Pippa! You are a good friend.” Pippa’s tummy squeeze melted away. She felt light again, like a feather in a breeze.

They started out, but just then, a little bunny named Bella hopped in. “Oh no,” Bella said, ears drooping. “We need a sign, but we have no paint. And the leaves are messy too. Our playtime will be bumpy.”

Pippa patted Bella gently. “We can help. We can share. We can volunteer,” she said. “First, we make a plan. A peaceful plan.”

Pippa turned down the music and clapped a rhythm. Tap-tap. “Let’s talk and take turns,” she said. “Mia, you paint the sign with the small brush. I will find some paint.” Pippa opened a box and found small jars—red, blue, and yellow. “We can share these colors.”

“Dan, you and I will rake,” Pippa said. “We can switch rakes if we get tired.” Dan nodded. “Good plan!”

Bella sniffed. “What can I do?” she asked.

Pippa beamed. “You can dance and cheer! Dancing makes work feel fun.”

They all smiled. The music rose again, soft and happy. Pippa hummed. Hmmm-hmmm. Mia dipped the brush in blue paint. Swish, swish. The brush made soft sounds on the wood. “The sign will say, ‘Play Kind,’” Mia said.

Pippa and Dan went outside the shed. Leaves were gold and brown. Crunch, crunch, crunch went the rakes. The air smelled like crisp apples. Bella twirled in the grass. “Go, team! Share and care!” she sang. Her feet thump-thumped in a cheerful beat.

After a while, Dan puffed. “I’m getting tired,” he said. Pippa smiled. “Let’s switch rakes now.” They traded. The small rake felt good in Dan’s wings again. Sharing felt good in Pippa’s heart again.

They worked and danced. They talked kindly. They took turns. When they were done, the yard was neat. The sign was bright with blue letters and a yellow border.

Mia held up the sign. “Play Kind,” it read. Everyone cheered. “We did it!” Bella hopped high. “We shared. We helped. We danced!”

Pippa wiggled with joy. “When we share, we all shine,” she said. “Sharing turns little jobs into fun times. Volunteering helps our friends. Dancing keeps our smiles big.”

They took the sign to the park and placed it by the swings. The sun felt warm on their faces. The breeze felt soft and sweet. Pippa turned to her friends. “Thank you for talking and taking turns. We solved our problems in a peaceful way.”

Dan quacked happily. “You shared the small rake. That helped me help others.”

Mia squeaked, “You shared your paint and your brush. That helped me make a kind sign.”

Bella giggled, “You shared your music and your dance. That helped us all feel good!”

Pippa’s heart felt full. “Sharing makes friends. Sharing makes peace,” she said.

They all did one last happy dance by the garden shed. Tap-tap, swish-swish, quack-quack, squeak-squeak, hop-hop! The music sparkled in the air.

The End.
