# The Bunny Plan in the Closet

Thump-thump! Little Rumi Rabbit’s feet tapped on the soft rug. Rumi was a determined rabbit with bright eyes and a twitchy pink nose. Today’s adventure was inside the closet hideaway. The closet was cozy and dark, with a warm smell like clean blankets and a tiny squeaky floorboard. Rumi liked the quiet, safe space. It felt like a little cave.

“I have a big job,” Rumi said, puffing up with courage. “I need to line up the boots and find my red scarf.”

Rumi wiggled into the closet hideaway. The air was cool. Coats brushed Rumi’s ears—swish, swish. A flashlight made a soft circle of light. “Click!” Rumi turned it on. The light felt friendly.

But the boots were jumbled. One boot lay on its side. Another boot hid under a hat. Socks were mixed in with scarves. It looked like a tiny storm had lived here.

Rumi took a deep breath. “I need a plan. A smart plan!” Rumi’s whiskers quivered with a happy idea.

Step one, Rumi thought, we look and listen. Rumi stood very still. The house hummed. The closet creaked. Rumi looked at all the things. “Boots here, hats there, scarves in a basket,” Rumi whispered. “That is my map.”

Just then, a gentle voice came from behind a coat sleeve. “Hi,” said a shy mouse named Momo. Momo’s fur was gray and soft. Momo held a tiny crumb. “I’m resting here. Is that okay?”

Rumi felt a little surprise. The surprise felt fizzy, like bubbles. But Rumi remembered: be kind, be calm. “Hi, Momo,” Rumi said. “We can share the space. I have a plan to tidy. Do you want to help?”

Momo’s nose twitched. “I’m small. I move slow. I can hand you things.”

“That is great!” said Rumi. “We can be a team. We will go step by step.”

Step two, Rumi thought, we sort. “Boots together!” Rumi called. Rumi hopped—hop, hop, hop—light as a feather. Rumi lifted a tall black boot. “Heavy!” Rumi giggled. Momo pushed a tiny red boot toward Rumi. Scritch, scritch. “Thank you,” said Rumi. “We move with care.”

The closet smelled like leather and a hint of cinnamon from an old sachet. Rumi sneezed. “Ah-choo!” Momo giggled. “Bless you.”

Step three, Rumi said, we make rows. Rumi lined up the boots on the left. Big boots at the back, small boots at the front. Rumi wiggled them until they stood straight. “Stand tall, boots,” Rumi said. “Like brave trees.”

Step four, Rumi smiled, we find the scarf. Rumi checked the hat shelf. Rustle, rustle. No scarf. Rumi peered behind a coat. Swish. No scarf. Rumi looked at Momo. “We need a new idea.”

“Strategy,” Rumi said slowly. “Strategy means a smart way to do a job. When one way does not work, we try another way.”

Momo’s eyes shined. “We can use colors,” Momo said. “What color are scarves?” 

“Red, blue, green,” Rumi said. “My scarf is red, like a strawberry.”

“Then we look only for red,” Momo said.

“That is a good plan,” said Rumi. They both looked for red shapes. Rumi saw a red mitten. “Not a scarf.” Momo saw a red tag. “Not a scarf.” Rumi bent down and listened. The floorboard squeaked again—eeek! Rumi tapped it. “Momo, what is under this?”

Momo nodded. “Sometimes things slide there.”

Rumi took another breath. “Step five, we move together.” Rumi pressed one side. Momo pressed the other. The board lifted just a little. Rumi slid a paw inside. “Soft,” Rumi said. “I feel something soft.”

Rumi pulled. Out came a red scarf! It felt warm and fuzzy, like a hug. “We did it!” Rumi cheered. Momo clapped tiny paws. “Strategy worked!”

Rumi wrapped the scarf around their neck. It smelled like sunshine and soap. Rumi’s heart felt calm and proud. “Thank you for helping, Momo. You moved slow, and that was okay. We took turns. We used our minds and our bodies.”

Momo smiled. “You were patient with me. You made a plan. I felt safe.”

Rumi looked at the tidy closet hideaway. Boots in rows. Hats in a basket. Scarves folded on a shelf. It looked peaceful. It sounded quiet. It felt good.

“Let’s make a chart,” Rumi said. Rumi drew pictures on a small paper with a stubby crayon:
- Eye for Look and Listen
- Hands for Sort
- Lines for Make Rows
- Heart for Try a New Idea
- Paws for Move Together

“This is our strategy list,” Rumi said. “We can use it every day.”

Momo nodded. “And we can be kind to others who share this space.”

Rumi peeked out of the closet. “Anyone who comes can help and learn,” Rumi said. “We will be patient. We will move with care. We will use our strategy.”

The sun made a warm stripe on the floor. Rumi and Momo sat in the stripe and wiggled their toes. Their bodies felt strong from moving. Their minds felt bright from planning. Their hearts felt big from being kind.

Rumi whispered, “When we make a plan, take turns, and keep trying, we can do big jobs, even in small places.”

Momo whispered back, “And we can be friends while we do it.”

The End.
