# Pippa the Patient Parrot

Pippa was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of blue on her wings. She lived in a sunny backyard where flowers smelled sweet, like honey, and the grass felt soft under her toes. Bees buzzed, a sprinkler tick-tick-ticked, and warm sunshine made everything glow.

Pippa loved words. She loved how words could feel bouncy and smooth in her beak. “Hello!” she chirped. “Hola! Bonjour!” She liked new sounds. She liked to make songs out of words. “Hello-hello, tap your toe!”

One day, Pippa wanted to build a word sign. She found smooth pebbles and tiny sticks near the sandbox. “I will spell ‘SUN,’” she said. “S-U-N! Easy-peasy!” She flapped with joy and started.

But the wind whispered whoosh. The pebbles rolled. Her letter S turned into a snake squiggle. The U tipped over. The N split in two.

Pippa’s tummy felt tight. Her wings drooped. “Oh no, oh no,” she squawked. “I can’t do it!”

A butterfly fluttered by. “You can try again,” it seemed to say with soft wings. The sprinkler ticked, and a bird on the fence chirped, “Cheer-up, cheer-up!”

Pippa stomped a little parrot stomp. “I feel mad,” she said aloud. Her voice was squeaky. Her face felt hot. “I want it now.”

She remembered something she had heard from a kind child who played in the yard. “When I feel mad, I take a breath.” Pippa puffed her chest. She breathed in slowly. One… two… three. She breathed out. One… two… three. The air smelled like cut grass and oranges from the tree.

“I can try a calm plan,” Pippa told herself. “First, breathe. Then, try small steps.”

She found a flat spot near the big yellow flower pot. “Less windy here,” she said. “Good choice, Pippa.” She wiped her eyes with her wing tip.

“Let’s start with S,” she whispered. She picked a long curved stick and two small pebbles. “Ssss,” she said, letting the sound slide like a snake. “Ssss is a smooth sound.” She shaped the S and tapped it gently. Tap, tap.

She smiled a tiny smile. “Next, U!” She picked two sticks and a pebble for the bottom curve. “Uuu,” she hummed, making her beak round. “U is a cozy cup sound.” She curved the sticks like a cup.

Then, N. “Nnn,” she buzzed, feeling the warm sound in her nose. “N is a neat line and a bridge.” She placed two straight sticks and one slanty stick.

Whoosh went the wind again. The S wiggled. Pippa felt her tummy twist. She paused. “Breathe, Pippa,” she said softly. In… one, two, three. Out… one, two, three.

“I can make a helper wall,” she decided. She found two leaves and a flat shell. She set them as a tiny fence to block the wind. “I did that myself,” she said, proud.

She checked the letters. S. U. N. “SUN!” she cheered. Her voice sounded like a bell. The letters looked steady and bright in the sunshine. A ladybug crawled by and seemed to nod.

Pippa wanted to add more. “I love words. I love sounds.” She picked another stick. “Can I spell ‘FUN’?” She giggled. “Fun in the sun!”

She tried the F. The first line slid. Her chest got tight again. “I’m stuck,” she whispered. She sat on the warm step and swung her little claws. The stone felt toasty. The air tasted like sweet lemonade.

“I can ask myself for help,” she said. “What do I know?” She closed her eyes and listened to the yard. Tick-tick-tick. Chirp-chirp. Hummm. She tapped her beak. “Small steps. Gentle hands. Sound it out.”

“Fff,” she said, blowing air softly. “F is a front-teeth puff.” She used a thicker stick for the long line and tiny twigs for the small lines, pressing them in firm. “Uuu,” she added, and then “Nnn.” She took her time. She breathed slow.

“FUN!” she chirped. “SUN and FUN!” The two words sat side by side. Pippa danced a happy hop. Tap-tap, hop-hop!

The child came out with a watering can and smiled. “Wow, Pippa,” the child said. “You made words!”

Pippa puffed up. “I felt mad,” she told the child. “I breathed slow. I tried small steps. I used my own ideas.” She pointed with her wing. “Ssss, Uuu, Nnn. Fff, Uuu, Nnn. Words are sounds and shapes!”

The child clapped. “That’s smart. When I feel mad, I breathe too.” They watered the flowers. The water made a soft splish-splash. The soil smelled rich and earthy.

Pippa looked at her sign in the sunny backyard. She felt calm and strong. She had done it herself. She had enjoyed the sounds. She had found a way through her big feelings.

She sang a little song, light and clear: “When I feel stuck, I breathe and wait. I try again. I make it great. Sounds and shapes, I love each one. I can do it. I’m not done!”

Then she tucked a blue feather next to the words like a ribbon. “For me,” she said, “from me.”

Pippa flapped her wings, warm air brushing her cheeks. “I can be patient. I can be kind to me.” She looked at the bright letters one more time and smiled.

The lesson was clear in her heart: When you feel frustrated, pause and breathe, try small steps, and trust yourself. Words are friends, and you are strong.

The End.
