# Dolly the Dolphin's Porch Problem

Dolly the dolphin loved visiting her friend Emma's house by the ocean. Emma's peaceful front porch had soft blue cushions and wind chimes that tinkled like gentle music in the breeze. Today, Dolly had splashed up from the water to play, but something was wrong.

"Oh no!" squeaked Dolly, looking at the porch with her big, curious eyes. "Emma's favorite toy boat is stuck!"

The little red boat was wedged tight between two wooden porch boards. Emma sat on the steps, her chin in her hands, looking very sad.

"I was playing with my boat, and it rolled right into that crack," Emma sniffled. "Now I can't get it out. It's stuck forever!"

Dolly's heart felt heavy seeing her friend so upset. She wanted to help, but how could a dolphin fix this problem? She took a deep breath of the salty air and thought hard.

"Don't worry, Emma!" Dolly said with a cheerful click. "Let's think of ways to get your boat out. Problems always have solutions!"

First, Dolly tried pushing the boat with her nose. She stretched and stretched, but she couldn't reach far enough onto the porch. 

"Hmph!" Dolly huffed, feeling frustrated. Her smooth gray skin felt warm from trying so hard.

"It's no use," Emma sighed. "I should have been more careful. Now my boat is ruined."

But Dolly didn't give up. She remembered what her mama always said: "When you feel frustrated, take a breath and try a different way."

Dolly closed her eyes and listened to the gentle waves. She heard the wind chimes singing and felt the cool ocean breeze. This helped her feel calmer.

"Emma, do you have a long stick or a broom?" Dolly asked, her voice bright with hope.

Emma's eyes lit up. "Yes! Mama's broom is right inside!"

Emma ran to get the broom while Dolly positioned herself carefully by the porch edge. When Emma returned, Dolly had an idea.

"Let's work together!" Dolly said excitedly. "You use the broom handle to gently push one end of the boat, and I'll be ready to catch it when it pops out!"

Emma held the broom with both hands. The wooden handle felt smooth and sturdy. She carefully poked it down between the boards.

"I can feel the boat!" Emma giggled. "It's moving!"

With a gentle push, the little red boat popped free! It sailed through the air, and Dolly caught it perfectly in her fins.

"We did it!" they both cheered.

Emma hugged her boat tight. "Thank you, Dolly! I'm sorry I got so upset and said it was hopeless."

"That's okay," Dolly smiled, her eyes twinkling like stars on water. "Sometimes we all feel frustrated. The important thing is that we helped each other and didn't give up!"

Emma placed her boat safely in a basket on the porch. "Next time I have a problem, I'll remember to stay calm and think of different solutions, just like you showed me!"

Dolly splashed happily in the shallow water by the porch. "And I'll remember that working together makes everything better!"

As the sun painted pretty colors across the sky, both friends felt proud and happy. They had learned that problems might seem big and scary at first, but with patience, creativity, and friendship, there's always a way to solve them.

The End.
