# Bubbles Finds Feelings in the Closet Hideaway

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to make tiny splashes and happy clicks. Every day, Bubbles visited a cozy closet hideaway. It was a quiet nook with soft scarves, a basket of fuzzy socks, and a small lamp that glowed like a warm seashell.

Bubbles had a gentle routine. First, deep breath in, deep breath out. Then, a wiggle and a giggle. After that, “Hello, feelings,” Bubbles would say, patting a little feelings chart on the door with pictures of faces: happy, sad, mad, scared, and calm.

One morning, Bubbles swam—swish, swish—right into the closet hideaway. “Today I will practice calm,” Bubbles said. The lamp made a soft hum. The scarves smelled like clean cotton. Everything felt safe.

Suddenly, there was a tap-tap on the door. “Can I come in?” asked Pip, a tiny penguin friend with bright eyes.

Bubbles felt a flutter inside. “I’m not sure,” Bubbles said softly. “I want quiet time.”

Pip waddled in a circle. “I want to play hide-and-seek! Please?”

Bubbles put a fin on Bubbles’ chest. “My heart feels fast. That means I feel worried.” Bubbles pointed to the chart and touched the worried face. “I need calm.”

Pip nodded, but took a small step in. “Just a peek?”

Bubbles remembered personal boundaries. “Pip, I need space in my hideaway right now,” Bubbles said, kind but firm. “You can wait by the door. Then we can play.”

Pip blinked. “Okay. I can wait.” Pip sat on the soft mat outside. “I will count to ten,” Pip said. “One… two…”

Bubbles breathed in and out. The scarves brushed Bubbles’ fin—soft as clouds. The lamp glowed warm. Bubbles felt the fast heartbeat slow down. “I feel calmer,” Bubbles whispered. Tap-tap went the feelings chart as Bubbles pointed to calm.

“Ten!” Pip chirped. “How do you feel now?”

Bubbles smiled. “Thank you for waiting. I feel calm. Now you can come in.”

Pip waddled inside, careful and small. “This closet is cozy,” Pip said, rubbing a fuzzy sock against a flipper. “It feels like a hug.”

Bubbles giggled. “It does. Let’s make a plan.” Bubbles liked plans. Plans were part of the routine.

“Plan?” Pip asked.

“Yes,” said Bubbles. “First, we breathe. Then, we whisper. Then, we pick a game that is quiet.”

Pip nodded. “I can whisper,” Pip said, practicing, “Peep, peep.”

Together they breathed in for three counts and out for three counts. Bubbles felt proud. “My body feels calm,” Bubbles said. “How does your body feel?”

Pip wiggled toes. “My toes feel tingly. I feel excited!”

“Excited can be loud,” Bubbles said gently. “Can excited be quiet, too?”

Pip thought. “I can try,” Pip said. “I can tuck excitement into my belly like a warm egg.”

“That sounds cozy,” Bubbles said. “Let’s play Feelings Hide-and-Seek. We hide a feeling card and then talk about the feeling when we find it.”

They hid the happy face under a striped scarf. “Found it!” Pip whispered, peeking under. “When do you feel happy?”

“I feel happy when friends wait for me,” Bubbles said. “What about you?”

“I feel happy when we play together,” Pip said, eyes bright.

They hid the mad face in the sock basket. Pip found it with a soft rustle. “When do you feel mad?”

Bubbles looked down. “I feel mad when someone comes in without asking.” Bubbles took a deep breath. “I felt mad a little when you stepped inside before I was ready.”

Pip’s eyes grew round. “Oh. I am sorry. I forgot to ask and wait.”

“Thank you for saying sorry,” Bubbles replied. “Saying sorry helps my heart feel better.”

They hid the scared face by the lamp. Bubbles found it. “I feel scared when the light goes off,” Bubbles said.

Pip gently tapped the lamp. “We can keep the light on,” Pip said. “We can hold fins. We can breathe.”

“That helps,” Bubbles said, smiling.

Then they hid the sad face near the door. Pip found it slowly. “I feel sad when I think I can’t play,” Pip said, voice small.

Bubbles nudged a fuzzy sock toward Pip. “I hear your sad,” Bubbles said. “We can make a plan so we both feel okay.”

Bubbles and Pip made a new routine sign for the closet door:
- Knock.
- Ask, “Can I come in?”
- Wait for “Yes.”
- Use whisper voices.
- Breathe together.

They practiced. Pip knocked. “Can I come in?”

Bubbles checked the feelings chart. “I feel calm. Yes.”

Pip smiled and stepped in. Gentle, gentle. Whisper, whisper.

At the end of play, Bubbles touched the calm face again. “We did it,” Bubbles said. “We kept our boundaries. We included each other. We listened to our feelings.”

Pip wrapped a scarf around both of them like a shared hug. “We kept trying,” Pip said. “That’s persistence!”

Bubbles’ clicks sounded like tiny bells. “Today I learned that feelings are clues,” Bubbles said. “When I pause and breathe, I can choose what to do. When I tell a friend my need, that is a boundary. When we wait and listen, that is inclusion.”

The lamp hummed softly. The closet hideaway felt safe and warm.

Bubbles and Pip put away the scarves and socks. They took one last deep breath together.

“We can come back tomorrow,” Bubbles said. “We will follow our routine again.”

“Yes,” said Pip. “We can feel our feelings and be kind.”

They bumped fins and flippers, gentle and proud.

The End.
