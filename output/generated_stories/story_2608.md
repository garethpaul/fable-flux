# Shelly's Special Snack Time

In a cozy nursery with soft blue walls and fluffy clouds painted on the ceiling, lived a little green turtle named Shelly. The nursery smelled like warm milk and had the softest carpet that felt like grass under Shelly's tiny feet.

Shelly was very shy. When the other animal friends played together, she would hide inside her shell and watch quietly. She wanted to join them, but she didn't know how to ask.

One sunny morning, Teacher Rabbit clapped her paws together. "Today we're making healthy fruit kabobs for snack time!" she announced cheerfully. "Everyone needs a partner to help!"

All the little animals quickly found friends to work with. Bunny paired with Squirrel, and Kitten chose Puppy. Soon, only Shelly sat alone at her little table, tucked halfway into her shell.

"Oh no," whispered Shelly to herself. "I want to make fruit kabobs too, but I'm too scared to ask anyone."

Teacher Rabbit noticed Shelly sitting by herself. She walked over with gentle steps that made no scary sounds on the soft carpet.

"Shelly, would you like to make fruit kabobs with me?" Teacher Rabbit asked in her kindest voice.

Shelly peeked out of her shell just a little bit. "I... I would like that," she said so quietly it was almost a whisper.

"Wonderful!" smiled Teacher Rabbit. "But first, let's practice using our big voice. Can you say 'I would like to help' a little louder?"

Shelly took a deep breath. "I would like to help," she said, her voice a tiny bit stronger.

"Perfect! Now, what fruits do you see on our table?"

Shelly looked at the colorful fruits. They smelled sweet and fresh. "I see red strawberries, orange pieces, and purple grapes," she said, feeling braver.

"Excellent! Now, let's ask our friends if they need help too. Sometimes helping others is the best way to make friends."

Shelly watched as Bunny struggled to hold his wooden stick steady while Squirrel tried to put a grape on top. The grape kept rolling off!

"Maybe... maybe I could help them?" Shelly asked Teacher Rabbit.

"That's a wonderful idea! What could you say to them?"

Shelly thought carefully. Then she walked slowly to Bunny and Squirrel's table. "Excuse me," she said in her clearest voice yet. "Would you like me to hold your stick while you put the fruit on?"

"Yes, please!" said Bunny with a big smile. "Thank you for asking, Shelly!"

Squirrel clapped his tiny paws. "You're so helpful! Would you like to work with us?"

Shelly's heart felt warm and happy. "I would love that!"

The three friends worked together beautifully. Shelly held the sticks steady with her strong little hands. Bunny carefully placed the strawberries, and Squirrel added the grapes and orange pieces. They made the most colorful, delicious-looking kabobs in the whole nursery!

"Look what we made together!" said Shelly proudly, showing Teacher Rabbit their creation.

"When we use our words and ask for help, wonderful things happen," said Teacher Rabbit. "And when we offer to help others, we make new friends!"

At snack time, all the friends sat together in a circle, enjoying their healthy fruit kabobs. The sweet strawberries and juicy grapes tasted even better because they had made them together.

Shelly smiled as she crunched on a piece of orange. She had learned that using her voice to ask for help and offer help to others wasn't scary at all – it was the best way to make friends and have fun!

From that day on, Shelly always remembered to use her words and speak up when she needed help or wanted to help others.

The End.
