# Joey’s Plan on the Balcony Garden

Joey was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a bouncy tail. He lived with Mama Roo in a cozy home with a sunny balcony garden. The balcony smelled like fresh mint and wet soil. Little wind chimes tinkled, ting-ting-ting, in the gentle breeze.

One morning, Joey peeked at the pots. “Mama, look! Leaves so green!” he giggled, touching a soft basil leaf. The soil felt cool and crumbly under his tiny paws.

Mama Roo smiled. “Yes, Joey. Our balcony garden is happy today. What would you like to do?”

Joey clapped. “I want to make art! A big garden picture!”

Mama Roo nodded. “Art is wonderful. Let’s make a plan first.”

Joey blinked. “A plan? Can we bounce and make it now?”

“We could,” Mama Roo said kindly, “but planning helps us know what to do, when to do it, and what we need. Planning makes things easier.”

Joey tilted his head. “Okay. Let’s plan!”

They sat on a soft mat. Mama brought a small notepad and a green crayon. The crayon smelled waxy and sweet.

“What do we want to make?” Mama asked.

“A garden poster!” Joey chirped. “With leaves, flowers, and happy bugs.”

Mama wrote: Goal—Garden Poster. “What do we need?”

Joey tapped his chin. “Paper. Glue. Colors. Safe scissors. And maybe… old boxes?”

“Good idea,” Mama said. “We can recycle. We’ll use paper from an old cereal box and bottle caps for flowers.”

Mama wrote: Materials—cereal box, glue, caps, crayons, safe scissors, clean jar for water, old newspaper for the floor.

Joey’s ears wiggled. “What do we do first?”

Mama drew three simple steps with little pictures:
1. Spread newspaper on the floor.
2. Gather materials.
3. Make the poster.

Joey bounced. “I can do step one!” He carefully spread the newspaper, rustle-rustle. “It sounds crunchy,” he giggled.

“Good planning, Joey,” Mama said. “Now step two.”

Joey carried the cereal box. It felt smooth and a little bendy. He found colorful bottle caps in a jar—click, clack, clink! He placed safe scissors and glue on the mat. He even brought a small bowl of clean water and a cloth for sticky paws.

“Ready?” Mama asked.

“Ready!” Joey chirped.

They traced a big rectangle from the cereal box and cut it. Snip, snip. Joey colored a blue sky and a yellow sun. “The sun is warm,” he whispered, rubbing the crayon slowly.

He glued a long green strip for a stem. He added mint leaves that had fallen on the soil. “Recycling!” Joey said proudly. “We use things again.”

“That’s right,” Mama smiled.

Joey pressed a red bottle cap in the middle. Pop! “A flower!” The glue smelled a little like apples. He wrinkled his nose and giggled.

Soon, Joey wanted to hurry. He grabbed too many bottle caps at once. “I can do it all fast!”

The caps slipped and rolled. Clink-clank-clatter! One cap knocked over the small bowl. Water splashed on the mat. Joey’s tummy felt squirmy.

Mama Roo looked at him gently. “What happened, Joey?”

Joey’s ears drooped. He wanted to say, “I don’t know.” But he took a deep breath. He remembered honesty. “I rushed,” he said softly. “I did not follow the plan. I made a spill. I am sorry.”

Mama nodded and hugged him. “Thank you for being honest, Joey. That is brave. Let’s fix it. What does our plan say?”

Joey looked at the steps. “Go slowly. One step at a time.” He dabbed the water with the cloth. The cloth felt cool and squishy. “First, we clean. Then, we try again.”

“Good planning,” Mama said.

They cleaned together. Then Joey picked one cap at a time. He whispered, “Step by step.” He glued carefully. Press… one, two, three. He added a smiley face bug with a tiny paper circle. He drew wings with soft, sh-sh crayons. He glued a shiny leaf he found on the balcony floor. The poster began to sparkle with colors—red, green, yellow, and blue.

When they finished, they held up the art. The wind chimes sang, ting-ting. The sun made the bottle cap shine like a jewel.

“Mama, we did it!” Joey beamed.

“Yes,” Mama said. “We planned, we recycled, we made art, and we told the truth. How do you feel?”

Joey’s heart felt warm. “I feel proud. Planning helped me. Planning made it fun.”

Mama smiled. “What did we learn today?”

Joey bounced softly. “Make a plan. Gather what we need. Go step by step. Be honest if we make a mistake. We can fix it.”

Mama kissed his head. “That’s a wonderful lesson.”

They put the poster on the balcony wall. It smelled like fresh glue and mint. Joey waved at the tiny bug he drew. “Hello, little friend!”

The wind blew gently. The leaves whispered. The chimes sang. Joey and Mama held hands and looked at their garden art.

Joey whispered, “Tomorrow, we can plan again.”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Tomorrow, a new plan and a new adventure.”

The End.
