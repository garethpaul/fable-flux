# Momo's Morning Bathroom Adventure

Momo the monkey loved mornings! Every day, he would swing from his cozy bed and hop, skip, and jump to the bathroom. The white tiles felt cool under his furry feet, and the mirror sparkled in the morning sunlight.

"Good morning, bathroom!" Momo chirped happily. He liked to greet everything around him because Mama Monkey taught him that being kind made everyone feel special.

But today was different. When Momo pushed open the bathroom door, he heard a tiny voice crying.

"Sniff, sniff... I'm so scared!" came a squeaky sound from behind the toilet.

Momo's ears perked up. He was brave, but he was also gentle. "Hello? Who's there?" he called softly, not wanting to frighten anyone.

A small gray mouse peeked out, his whiskers trembling. "I'm Mickey Mouse, and I'm lost! I was looking for water, but this place is so big and scary!"

Momo's heart felt warm and fuzzy. He remembered when he was little and got lost in the kitchen. It felt very frightening! Instead of being upset that someone was in his bathroom, Momo smiled kindly.

"Don't worry, Mickey! I'm Momo, and this is my family's bathroom. You're safe here," Momo said in his gentlest voice. "Would you like me to help you?"

Mickey's tiny black eyes grew wide with hope. "Really? You're not angry that I'm here?"

"Of course not!" Momo laughed, doing a little happy dance that made his tail wiggle. "Everyone needs help sometimes. That's what friends are for!"

Momo thought carefully about how to help Mickey feel better. "First, let's get you some water," he said. He climbed up to the sink and turned the faucet just a tiny bit. Crystal clear water dripped down slowly.

"Oh, thank you!" Mickey squeaked, taking small sips. The water sparkled like diamonds in the morning light.

"Now," said Momo, "let's do some exercises to make you feel brave and strong!" Momo started doing jumping jacks, his arms and legs moving like a happy star. "Exercise always makes me feel better!"

Mickey giggled and tried to copy Momo's movements. Soon, both friends were laughing and exercising together. The bathroom echoed with their happy sounds.

"I feel much braver now!" Mickey said, his whiskers no longer trembling.

Momo clapped his hands. "That's wonderful! Now, let me help you find your way home safely." He thought about the best path for a small mouse. "I know! The kitchen has a little door that leads outside. Would you like me to show you?"

"Yes, please!" Mickey said gratefully.

Before they left, Momo made sure to prepare Mickey for the journey. "Here's a tiny piece of banana for energy," he said, breaking off a small bit from his breakfast. "And remember, if you ever need help again, you can always come back. We're friends now!"

Mickey's eyes sparkled with joy. "Thank you, Momo! You showed me that talking about problems and asking for help is much better than being scared alone."

As they walked together toward the kitchen, Momo felt proud and happy. He had used peaceful words instead of being frightened or angry. He had shown respect for Mickey, even though Mickey was different from him.

"Helping others feels even better than swinging from trees!" Momo thought to himself.

From that day on, whenever Momo went to the bathroom, he always remembered to be gentle and kind, just in case someone else needed a friend.

The End.
