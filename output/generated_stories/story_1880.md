# Fawn's Big Mistake

Little Fawn loved exploring the quiet attic above Grandma Rabbit's house. The dusty wooden floor creaked softly under her tiny hooves, and golden sunbeams danced through the small window.

"What's this?" Fawn whispered, spotting a beautiful music box on an old table. The box was painted with pretty flowers and had a tiny ballerina inside.

Fawn's brown eyes sparkled with curiosity. She stretched her neck to get a closer look. "I'll just peek," she said softly.

But when Fawn bumped the table with her nose, the music box tumbled down! CRASH! It broke into pieces on the floor.

"Oh no!" Fawn gasped. Her heart felt heavy like a rock in her chest. The beautiful ballerina lay still, and the pretty painted flowers were chipped.

Fawn heard footsteps on the creaky stairs. "Someone's coming!" she thought. Her first idea was to hide behind the old trunk. But then she remembered what Mama Deer always said: "Being honest is always the right choice, even when it's scary."

Grandma Rabbit appeared at the top of the stairs, followed by Little Mouse and Baby Squirrel.

"Oh my!" said Grandma Rabbit, seeing the broken pieces. "What happened to my special music box?"

Fawn's legs felt wobbly, but she took a brave step forward. "I'm sorry, Grandma Rabbit," she said in a small voice. "I broke your music box. I was curious and bumped the table. I didn't mean to, but I did it."

Grandma Rabbit's whiskers twitched sadly. "This was very special to me. My grandmother gave it to me when I was just a little bunny."

Fawn felt tears in her eyes. "I'm so, so sorry. Can I help fix it? Please?"

Little Mouse scurried closer. "I have tiny paws that are good for holding small pieces!"

"And I can gather all the broken bits with my quick hands," chirped Baby Squirrel.

Grandma Rabbit smiled gently. "Fawn, I'm proud of you for telling the truth. That took courage. And friends, thank you for wanting to help."

Together, they carefully picked up every piece. Fawn used her soft nose to nudge the tiniest fragments toward Mouse. Squirrel organized the pieces by size. They worked as a team, being very gentle and patient.

"We need special glue," said Grandma Rabbit. "Let's go to Mr. Fox's workshop."

Mr. Fox was happy to help when he heard about their teamwork. "Working together and being honest are wonderful things," he said, carefully gluing each piece.

The next day, the music box was almost like new! There were tiny lines where it had been broken, but when Grandma Rabbit wound it up, the little ballerina danced again, and sweet music filled the attic.

"Thank you all," said Grandma Rabbit, hugging each friend. "Fawn, your honesty helped us fix this together. The music box is even more special now because it reminds me of good friends who care."

Fawn felt warm and happy inside. "I learned that telling the truth and asking for help makes everything better," she said.

From that day on, whenever Fawn visited the quiet attic, she was extra careful. And the music box, with its tiny scars, played the most beautiful songs about friendship, honesty, and making things right.

The End.
