# Pip the Parrot’s Sparkly Feather Dance

Pip the parrot lived in a cozy bedroom with soft pillows, warm blankets, and a night-light that glowed like a tiny moon. The room smelled like clean sheets and a little like lemon soap. Pip was a creative parrot who loved to make things. He made paper crowns, ribbon tails, and sparkly feather hats. Pip also loved to dance.

One morning, sunlight peeked through the curtains. “Good morning!” chirped Pip. He ruffled his green and blue feathers. They were bright, but some feathers felt sticky. “Hmm,” Pip said, tapping his beak. “My feathers feel icky.”

Pip fluttered to his little mirror. “Oh dear,” he squawked. “Glue from crafts! Crumbs from snack! A tiny paint smudge on my cheek!” He sniffed. “Phew! Not fresh.” He wanted to dance, but his wings felt heavy and messy.

Just then, Pip’s friend, Nib the toy rabbit, wiggled from the pillow tower. “Dance time?” Nib squeaked.

“I want to,” Pip said, “but I feel sticky, stinky, and slow. Maybe…I should clean up first.”

Pip felt a little nervous. He remembered soap bubbles popping in his eyes once. He took a brave breath. “I can do this,” he said. “I can be brave. I can be clean.”

Pip hopped to the small wash basin by the dresser. The water was warm like a hug. He dipped his toes. “Ooh!” he chirped. “Nice!” He added a tiny drop of gentle soap. Bubbles rose up, shiny and rainbowy. They smelled sweet, like lemons and flowers.

“Splish, splash!” sang Pip. He dipped his wings, one at a time. “Front, back, under, between,” he said, washing carefully. He swished his tail feathers and cleaned his beak with a soft cloth. “Wipe, wipe, wipe.” He rinsed until the water ran clear. He patted dry with a fluffy towel. “Pat, pat, pat.” The towel was warm from the sun. It felt cozy on his feathers.

Nib clapped his soft paws. “You did it! You were brave!”

Pip preened a little, smoothing each feather. He felt light and fresh. “I smell like lemons!” he chirped. “I feel ready to dance!”

He set up music on a tiny toy drum. Tap-tap-tap! Pip twirled. He glided. He shook his tail feathers. “Sparkly Feather Dance!” he sang. “Clean wings swing!”

But then—oops! Pip’s foot tapped the jar of glitter. The jar wobbled. “Careful!” he squawked. Too late. The glitter spilled, sprinkling all over the clean floor and a little on Nib’s ears.

Nib’s button eyes went wide. “Oh!”

Pip stopped. His heart thumped. He felt sorry and a bit scared. He had worked so hard to clean. Now the floor was messy again, and Nib was sparkly from ear to ear.

Pip took another brave breath. “I’m sorry, Nib,” he said softly. “I knocked the glitter. I made a mess. Are you okay?”

Nib touched his sparkly ear. He looked at Pip’s worried eyes. Nib gave a tiny smile. “I am okay. I forgive you,” he said. “It was an accident.”

Pip’s shoulders relaxed. “Thank you for forgiving me.” He nodded. “Now I will fix it.”

He got the small broom and dustpan. Swish, sweep, swish. He hummed a clean-up song. “Sweep, sweep, slow and steady. Clean and calm. Ready, ready.” He wiped the glitter with a damp cloth so the tiny bits didn’t fly away. He washed Nib’s ears with a soft corner of the towel. “Warm water, gentle soap, rinse, and pat,” Pip said. “There. All clean!”

Nib wiggled his ears. “Thank you. They smell nice.”

Pip smiled. “Keeping clean keeps us healthy,” he said. “Clean feathers help me fly and dance. Clean hands, clean face, clean space!” He fluffed his now-sparkly-free feathers. “And saying ‘I’m sorry’ helps hearts feel clean too.”

“Let’s dance again,” Nib said. “But first, close the glitter jar tight.”

Pip nodded with a giggle. “Good plan!” He twisted the lid. Click! “Safe and tidy.”

The music began again. Tap-tap-tap! Pip danced a brave, bright dance. He leaped, he swished, he spun in smooth circles. The cozy bedroom smelled fresh and felt happy. Sunlight made friendly shapes on the floor. Nib hopped along, ears bouncing.

Pip sang, “Wash, rinse, dry, hooray! Clean and kind to start the day. If there’s oops, we make it right. We try again with gentle light.”

After the dance, Pip put away the soap, the towel, and the broom. He set out a small basket: a tiny brush, a washcloth, and a little bottle of soap. He made a sign with crayons: “Pip’s Clean Kit.” He placed it by the basin.

Nib read the sign. “Courage, clean, and kind,” he said.

Pip nodded. “Today I learned three big things,” he said. “Be brave and wash. Say sorry and forgive. Keep our room neat and our bodies clean.”

Nib smiled. “And dance!”

Pip laughed. “Yes! Dance with clean, happy feathers!”

They snuggled on the soft pillow for a rest. The night-light blinked like a sleepy star even though it was day. The room felt safe and cozy. Pip felt proud, fresh, and loved.

“Tomorrow,” Pip whispered, “we will wash, we will dance, and we will shine.”

“Tomorrow,” Nib agreed.

The End.
