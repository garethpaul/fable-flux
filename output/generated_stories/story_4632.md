# Penny's Healthy Workshop Snacks

In a cozy garage workshop filled with colorful tools, lived a gentle black and white panda named Penny. Her round ears perked up every morning when she heard the *clink-clank* of her papa working on his projects.

The workshop smelled like wood shavings and had bright sunlight streaming through the windows. Penny loved watching Papa build birdhouses and fix broken toys. But today, she had a big problem.

*Rumble, grumble!* Penny's tummy made loud, hungry sounds.

"Papa, I'm so hungry!" Penny said, rubbing her soft, round belly. "Can I have a snack?"

Papa looked up from his workbench, sawdust in his whiskers. "Of course, little one! What would you like?"

Penny's eyes sparkled. She remembered the sweet cookies in the kitchen cabinet. "Cookies! Lots and lots of cookies!"

Papa smiled gently. "Hmm, let me think about this with you, Penny. What does your body need to feel strong and happy?"

Penny tilted her fuzzy head. She touched her tummy and listened carefully. *Rumble!* "My tummy feels empty and tired," she said slowly.

"That's very good listening, Penny!" Papa said proudly. "Your body is telling you something important. Let's think together - what foods help pandas grow big and strong?"

Penny closed her eyes and thought hard. She remembered feeling energetic after eating crunchy carrots. She remembered feeling sleepy after too many sweets.

"Carrots make me feel bouncy!" Penny said, hopping a little. "And apples make my teeth feel clean and strong!"

"Excellent thinking!" Papa cheered. "What about bamboo shoots? How do those make you feel?"

Penny's black nose twitched as she remembered. "They make me feel full and happy for a long, long time!"

Papa nodded wisely. "Your body is very smart, Penny. It tells you what it needs. Cookies taste yummy, but they don't give you energy to play and learn."

Penny looked at the bright workshop around her. She wanted to help Papa with his projects. She wanted to feel strong and alert.

"I choose the healthy snacks!" Penny announced. "My body needs good food to help me think and play!"

Together, they walked to the kitchen. Papa helped Penny wash crisp apple slices. The water felt cool on her paws. The apples smelled fresh and sweet.

Penny crunched happily. *Crunch, crunch!* The apple was juicy and made her mouth feel clean. She felt energy flowing through her body.

"How do you feel now?" Papa asked.

Penny bounced on her toes. "I feel strong! I feel happy! My tummy feels good!" She paused and listened to her body again. "And I think I can help you in the workshop now!"

Papa hugged his thoughtful little panda. "You made a wonderful choice, Penny. You listened to your body and chose what it really needed."

Back in the workshop, Penny helped organize Papa's tools. She felt alert and energetic. Her body felt strong and ready for anything.

"Thank you for teaching me to listen to my body," Penny said. "Good food helps me feel good inside!"

Papa smiled as they worked together, the afternoon sun warming their happy workshop.

The End.
