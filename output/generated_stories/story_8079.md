# Leo's Messy Art Adventure

Leo the lion cub loved to paint. His golden fur sparkled in the bright lights of the art room as he bounced excitedly toward the easel. Today was finger painting day!

"I'm going to make the most colorful painting ever!" Leo roared softly, his little paws reaching for the red paint.

Squish! Squash! Leo dipped his paws into the thick, gooey paint. It felt cool and squishy between his toes. He made big red circles on his paper, then reached for the blue paint.

"This is so much fun!" he giggled, spreading blue paint with his whole paw.

Soon, Leo's painting was beautiful, but Leo himself was very messy. Red and blue paint covered his paws, his arms, and even his whiskers! Paint dripped from his fur onto the floor.

"Time to clean up!" called Ms. Owl, the art teacher. "Remember to wash your hands and paws before snack time."

But Leo looked at his messy paws and felt worried. "There's so much paint!" he whispered. "It will take forever to clean off. Maybe I can just hide my paws."

Leo tried to sit on his paws during snack time, but it was very uncomfortable. When his friend Bella the bunny offered to share her crackers, Leo couldn't take them without showing his dirty paws.

"What's wrong, Leo?" asked Bella, her pink nose twitching with concern.

"My paws are too messy," Leo mumbled. "I don't want anyone to see them."

Bella looked at Leo's colorful paws and smiled. "Those colors are beautiful! But you know what? Let's ask Ms. Owl to help us clean them together."

Leo felt nervous, but Bella's kind eyes made him feel braver. They walked to Ms. Owl together.

"Ms. Owl," said Bella, "Leo needs help cleaning his paws. Can you show us the best way?"

Ms. Owl's eyes twinkled warmly. "Of course! Leo, cleaning up after art is just as important as making art. Let me show you my special cleaning strategy."

At the sink, Ms. Owl turned on the warm water. "First, we rinse with warm water," she explained. "Listen to how it sounds - splash, splash!"

The water felt nice and warm on Leo's paws. Some paint swirled down the drain in pretty spirals.

"Now we add soap," said Ms. Owl, squirting sweet-smelling soap onto Leo's paws. "Rub your paws together like this - scrub, scrub, scrub!"

Leo rubbed his paws together, making bubbly white foam. The soap smelled like flowers and felt slippery and fun.

"Now rinse again," guided Ms. Owl. "See how clean your paws are getting?"

Leo watched amazed as the colorful paint washed away, leaving his golden paws clean and shiny.

"Last step - dry with a soft towel," said Ms. Owl, handing Leo a fluffy blue towel.

Leo's paws felt clean, soft, and fresh. "Wow! That wasn't hard at all!" he said, examining his sparkling clean paws.

"Being clean helps keep us healthy," explained Ms. Owl. "And clean paws make snacks taste better too!"

Leo hugged Bella. "Thank you for helping me be brave enough to ask for help," he said.

"That's what friends do," Bella smiled. "And your painting was absolutely beautiful!"

From that day on, Leo always remembered to clean his paws after art time. He even taught other cubs Ms. Owl's special cleaning strategy. Leo learned that being clean wasn't just about looking good - it was about feeling good and staying healthy too.

The End.
