# Panda Patches Cleans the Workshop

Panda Patches lived near a small town. Patches was a patient panda with soft black-and-white fur. Patches liked calm days and kind words. One sunny morning, Patches opened the big squeaky door to a garage workshop.

Creak! The door made a long sound. The workshop smelled like wood shavings and orange oil. Dust sparkled in the light. Tools hung on the wall. A blue bike leaned by the workbench. A basket sat in the corner, full of crumpled paper and empty bottles.

Patches took a deep breath. “Mmm,” Patches said. “I smell wood and oranges. Today, I will fix, sort, and move.”

Patches liked to plan. Planning helped Patches feel brave and calm. Patches whispered, “First, make a plan. Second, use kind words. Third, take turns and take breaks.”

Patches clapped soft paws. “Let’s start with a clean space,” Patches said. “Clean space, clear mind.”

A tiny mouse named Dot poked out from behind a paint can. “Squeak! Are you sure we can clean all this?” Dot asked. Her whiskers wiggled.

“Yes,” Patches said, smiling. “We will take small steps. We will be patient.”

Patches picked up a bottle and read the label. “This goes to recycling,” Patches said. The bottle clinked into a blue bin. “Clink!”

Dot picked up a paper cup. “Trash?” she asked.

Patches shook their head. “Let’s look. Is it dirty? Yes. Is it paper? Yes. But it is too dirty for recycling. It goes to trash.” Patches tossed it into a small black bin. “Plop!”

They worked together. The broom went swish-swish. The dustpan went tap-tap. The air smelled less dusty and more fresh. Patches stretched and moved, bending and lifting safely. “Exercise time!” Patches sang. “Bend and sweep. Bend and sweep.” Dot giggled and tried the moves. “Squeak and sweep!”

When Patches reached the blue bike, the back tire felt soft. “This tire needs air,” Patches said, pressing it with a paw. “I can help.” Patches found the silver pump. It was under a cardboard box. Patches said, “Excuse me, box.” Patches lifted it slowly. “I use slow muscles,” Patches said. “Slow and safe.”

Dot peered at a pile of paint cans. “Let’s pour the old paint in the drain,” she said.

Patches shook their head gently. “No, Dot. That can hurt water and fish,” Patches said. “We must care for our Earth. We can take old paint to a special place. That is environmental care.”

Dot’s ears drooped. “I didn’t know,” she said softly.

“It’s okay,” Patches said. “You asked. That is honest and brave. Now we know better.”

They made three signs with big letters:
- Recycle
- Trash
- Take to Special Place

Patches drew a blue arrow for the recycling sign. Dot colored a green leaf on the special place sign. The markers smelled like grapes and lemons. “Sniff,” Dot said, smiling.

A raccoon named Rocco peeked in. His paws were smudgy. “Hey, friends! I will help,” he said. He picked up a pile of bottles and tossed them all into the trash bin. “Done!”

Patches took a slow breath. The plan, the feelings, the goal—Patches remembered. Self-advocacy is using your voice. “Rocco,” Patches said kindly and clearly, “I need your help to care for our Earth. Bottles go in the blue bin to be made new again. Please put them there.”

Rocco blinked. “Oh! I didn’t know. Thanks for telling me.” He moved the bottles to the blue bin. “Clink, clink, clink!”

Dot whispered, “That was brave.”

Patches smiled. “We all learn. We help each other.”

They took a stretch break. Patches led them. “Arms up like tall trees. Wiggle fingers like leaves. Bend down slow like rain.” The floor smelled like pine soap now. Rocco laughed. “My legs feel strong!”

After the break, they made a strategy to finish:
- Sort first.
- Sweep next.
- Fix last.

They said it together: “Sort, sweep, fix!”

Soon the workshop looked bright. The windows were clear. The floor was clean. The blue bike waited by the door. Patches pumped air into the tire. “Hiss, hiss,” went the pump. Patches pressed the tire again. “Nice and springy!”

Rocco pointed at an old fan. “Should we throw it away?”

Patches looked closely. “The cord is loose. We can fix it. Fixing is caring. Fixing keeps trash out of the land.” Patches wrapped the cord with safe tape and checked the plug. “Now it is safe.”

Dot found a tiny green plant growing in a crack. “Hello, little plant,” she said. Patches brought a small pot and some soil from the shelf. The soil felt cool and grainy. Together, they scooped the plant into the pot. “We saved you,” Dot said. “We will put you by the window.”

At the door, Patches hung the three signs. Patches also wrote a small promise on a card: “We will sort, we will fix, we will be kind to Earth.”

The sun lowered, and the workshop glowed warm and golden. Patches, Dot, and Rocco stood side by side. They took a deep breath. The air smelled clean and sweet.

Rocco said, “I learned to put bottles in the blue bin.”

Dot said, “I learned not to pour paint in the drain.”

Patches said, “I learned to use my voice with care and to move my body to help. We made a plan, and we kept it.”

They rang a tiny bell on the wall. “Ding!” The sound was bright and happy.

Patches smiled. “Today we cared for our place. When we care for our place, we care for our Earth.”

The friends waved and stepped outside. The sky turned pink. The plant in the window looked like a little green star.

The End.
