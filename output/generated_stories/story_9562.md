# Sunny the Squirrel and the Busy Pantry

Sunny the squirrel lived in a cozy pantry at the back of a cheerful house. The pantry smelled like cinnamon, apples, and warm bread. Jars clinked softly when the door opened. Bags of rice rustled like tiny waves. Sunny loved this place. It felt safe. It felt full.

One bright morning, Sunny heard a tiny tap-tap-tap. She peeked from behind a flour bag. “Hello?” she chittered.

“Hello, Sunny!” called Miso the mouse. Miso wore a tiny blue scarf. “We need help. The pantry is messy. Everyone is hungry. We cannot find our foods.”

Sunny’s ears perked up. “I can help!” she said. “I am resourceful.”

Just then, other friends arrived. A cricket named Chili sang a chirp-chirp song. A small bird named Naan fluttered in with soft wings. An ant named Coco marched with a little crumb. Each friend liked different foods. Each friend came from a different home in the big house and garden.

“I like rice,” said Miso, patting a bag that smelled like warm steam.
“I like spicy chili flakes,” chirped Chili, tickling a red jar.
“I like seeds and nuts,” tweeted Naan, hopping near a basket.
“I like cocoa powder,” said Coco, pointing to a brown tin with a sweet smell.

Sunny took a deep breath. She smelled cinnamon and cocoa and pepper and peanuts and garlic. The scents danced like colors in the air. “We can make places for everyone,” Sunny said. “We can make it easy to find foods. That is helpful to all.”

Naan tilted her head. “How will we do that?”

Sunny rubbed her paws. “First, we tidy. Then, we sort. Then, we label.”

“Label?” asked Chili.

“Little signs,” Sunny said. “Pictures and words. We all can use them.”

Miso smiled. “We can help too!”

They began. Sunny pushed a rolling can with her shoulder. Clink, clink! Coco dusted crumbs into a tiny pile. Sweep, sweep! Naan flapped to lift a light bag to a safe shelf. Whoosh! Chili sang a little tidy song. Chirp-chirp, clean-clean!

Sunny made groups. Crunchy foods here: nuts, seeds, crackers. Soft foods there: bread, tortillas, naan. Rice and grains in this spot. Spices and flavors in that spot. Jars lined up like soldiers. Bags stood tall like trees. The pantry started to look calm and bright.

“Let’s make labels!” Sunny squeaked. She dipped a little twig in cocoa powder and made a brown line on a scrap of paper. “Picture of rice.” She drew a simple rice bowl. Miso clapped. Sunny drew a tiny chili pepper. Chili beamed. Sunny drew a round flat bread. Naan puffed her feathers with joy. Sunny drew a nut for the crunchy shelf. She also added small words: rice, chili, bread, nuts.

Miso pointed. “I read a little. The pictures help me too.”

Naan chirped, “We all can find our foods. It is fair.”

Coco looked shy. “Can I help more?” she asked.

Sunny smiled kindly. “Yes, please. Put the labels on the shelves.” Coco carried the tiny signs, one by one. Stick, stick. Her small steps made soft tap sounds on the wooden shelf.

Soon the pantry looked new. It smelled fresh. The floor felt smooth. The shelves looked neat. Everyone stood back and took a breath. Ahhh.

Suddenly, the door creaked. The baker from the house peeked in. She wore a bright scarf with many colors. She hummed a tune from her home far away. “Oh my,” she said softly. “This pantry is so tidy! Thank you, little friends.” She placed a basket on the floor. Inside were treats from many places: sweet dates, crunchy plantain chips, sesame sticks, and tiny spice cookies. “For my helpers,” she said, smiling.

“Thank you!” the friends cheered.

Miso wrinkled his nose. “What is this?” He held up a date.

“It is sweet,” said Naan. “Try a little bite.”

Miso tasted. “Mmm! Sticky and yummy.”

Chili nibbled a sesame stick. “Crunchy and nutty!”

Coco tried a tiny crumb of spice cookie. “Warm and cinnamon-y.”

Sunny took a plantain chip. “Salty and crisp!” Her tail twitched with delight.

They shared and learned. Some foods were new. Some were old favorites. All were good to try. They listened to the baker’s soft song and clapped to the beat.

After the snack, Sunny saw one more thing. There was a small spill of rice under the shelf. She felt a little tired, but she remembered the plan. “I can improve this,” she said. She scooped the rice into a jar and added a label: rice. She made a little broom from straw and tied it with string. Sweep, sweep! The floor was clean again.

Miso hugged Sunny. “You helped us so much.”

Naan chirped, “You showed us a way.”

Chili sang, “Helpful hearts make bright places!”

Coco nodded. “We all helped. We all learned.”

Sunny’s whiskers twitched with pride. “When we help, we make things better. We can listen, share, and improve, little by little.”

They all looked at the neat shelves. The pantry glowed in the warm light. It smelled like cinnamon and kindness.

The End.
