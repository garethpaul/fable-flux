# Sunny's Treehouse Treasure

Sunny the squirrel lived in a cozy treehouse at the top of a tall oak. The walls smelled like warm wood. The floor felt smooth under tiny paws. A little round window let in golden morning light. Birds sang, twit-twit, outside.

Sunny was resourceful. That means Sunny could solve problems. Sunny liked to collect acorns, little buttons, and shiny leaves. “I can use these,” Sunny would say with a bright smile. Every day was a small adventure.

One sunny morning, Sunny peered out the window. “Oh no,” Sunny whispered. Down below, the forest floor looked messy. Wrappers flapped like little noisy flags. A plastic bag was stuck on a bush. A bottle rolled and clinked, clink, clink.

Sunny’s tail twitched. “The forest is our home,” Sunny said. “We need to take care of it.” Sunny took a deep breath. The air smelled a bit sour, not fresh and leafy like before. “I will help,” Sunny decided. “I will use my ideas.”

Sunny packed a small basket with helpful things: soft gloves, a long stick with a loop, and a tiny rope. Sunny also made a sign from an old cardboard square: “Please keep our forest clean.” Sunny drew a big green leaf on it.

Down the ladder Sunny went, step, step, step. At the bottom, Sunny met Robin the bird and Tilly the turtle.

“What are you doing, Sunny?” chirped Robin.

“I’m cleaning our home,” Sunny said. “I want the forest to be safe and pretty.”

Tilly blinked slowly. “That is kind,” Tilly said. “May we help?”

“Yes, please!” Sunny smiled. “Let’s work together.”

They started with the crinkly wrappers. They made a fun game. “One, two, three—crinkle!” Robin sang, picking up light bits with a beak. Sunny used the loop stick to catch the bag on the bush—swish, got it! Tilly nudged the bottle into the basket with a gentle push—clink.

Sunny said, “We will sort this. Things that can be used again go here. Trash goes there.” Sunny drew a line in the dirt and made two piles. The friends nodded.

Soon, a raccoon named Rocco peeked from behind a stump. “Are you cleaning?” Rocco asked, ears perked.

“Yes,” Sunny said. “We love our home. Will you join us?”

Rocco looked shy. “I… I left a snack bag here yesterday,” Rocco mumbled. “I forgot to take it.”

Sunny spoke kindly. “Thank you for telling us. We all make mistakes. You can help us fix it.”

Rocco’s eyes brightened. “Okay!” Rocco picked up the snack bag and placed it in the right pile.

Together they cleaned: pick, drop, sort. The forest began to smell fresh again, like leaves and sweet sap. Sunlight danced on the clean ground. A tiny breeze whispered, shhh, thank you.

When they were done, the friends sat under the oak. Sunny passed around cool cups of water and crunchy apple slices. “We did it,” Sunny said. “I feel proud.”

“I feel proud too,” Tilly said slowly.

“Me three!” chirped Robin.

Rocco looked at the clean path. “I want to do better,” Rocco said. “But what if I forget again?”

Sunny’s whiskers twitched with an idea. “Let’s make a plan,” Sunny said. “Plans help us remember.”

Sunny ran up the ladder to the treehouse and brought down the cardboard sign. Together they painted big friendly letters. The paint smelled a little like berries. They added pictures: a bin, a leaf, and happy faces.

Robin read aloud, “Please keep our forest clean. Put trash in the bin. Reuse what you can.”

Sunny said, “We will hang the sign by the path.” Sunny tied the sign with the tiny rope so it would not fall.

Then Sunny said, “Let’s talk to our neighbors. We can use calm words.”

They visited Beaver by the stream. “Hello, Beaver,” Sunny said softly. “We love our forest. Will you help us keep it clean?”

Beaver nodded. “Yes, I will. I can build a little bin by the water,” Beaver said, pat-pat with busy paws.

They asked Deer by the meadow. “Hello, Deer,” said Tilly. “Please put wrappers in the bin.”

Deer smiled. “Of course,” Deer said. “I like a tidy meadow.”

Sunny felt warm and happy. “When we speak kindly,” Sunny said, “friends listen.”

That evening, the forest was quiet and peaceful. Fireflies blinked like tiny stars. Sunny climbed back into the treehouse. The room smelled sweet after a good day. Sunny curled on a soft leaf bed and thought about the day.

Sunny whispered, “I saw a problem. I took action. I asked for help. We spoke kindly. We cared for our home.”

The next morning, Sunny looked out the window. The sign stood bright. The path was tidy. A little chipmunk put a wrapper in the bin and waved. Sunny waved back.

Sunny smiled and said, “Small paws can do big things. Caring for our home is something we can all do, every day.”

The End.
