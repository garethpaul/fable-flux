# Benny's Big Bounce Back

Benny the kangaroo joey loved bouncing around the cozy living room. The soft, fluffy carpet felt wonderful under his tiny paws, and the colorful cushions made perfect landing spots for his happy hops.

"Boing, boing, boing!" Benny giggled as he bounced from the blue sofa to the yellow chair. The morning sunlight streamed through the big window, making everything sparkle and shine.

Mama Kangaroo was in the kitchen making breakfast. "Remember, Benny," she called out, "be careful with Grandma's special vase on the coffee table!"

"Okay, Mama!" Benny replied, but he was so excited about his bouncing game that he didn't really listen carefully.

Benny decided to try his biggest bounce ever. He crouched down low, wiggled his bottom, and sprang high into the air. "Look at me! I'm flying!" he shouted with joy.

But oh no! Benny bounced too close to the coffee table. His fluffy tail bumped into Grandma's beautiful blue vase with tiny white flowers painted on it.

CRASH!

The vase tumbled to the floor and broke into several pieces. The sound was loud and scary. Benny's tummy felt all twisty and worried.

"What was that noise?" Mama called from the kitchen.

Benny looked at the broken pieces scattered on the carpet. His eyes filled with tears. He felt very sad and scared. Maybe if he hid behind the big green chair, Mama wouldn't find out what happened.

Benny hopped quietly behind the chair and curled up in a little ball. But sitting there didn't feel good at all. His heart felt heavy, like a rock in his pouch.

"Benny? Where are you, little one?" Mama's voice sounded closer now.

Benny heard Mama gasp when she saw the broken vase. "Oh my! What happened here?"

From behind the chair, Benny could hear Mama's footsteps walking around. He knew she was looking for him. His tummy felt even more twisty.

Then Benny remembered something important. Last week, when his friend Rosie the rabbit had accidentally torn his favorite book, she had said "I'm sorry" and helped tape it back together. That had made everything better.

Benny took a deep breath. Being honest was the right thing to do, even when it felt scary.

Slowly, the little joey hopped out from behind the chair. His ears drooped down, and his voice was very quiet. "Mama, I broke Grandma's vase. I was bouncing too big, and I didn't listen carefully. I'm very, very sorry."

Mama's face looked sad about the vase, but she smiled gently at Benny. "Thank you for telling me the truth, sweetheart. That was very brave and honest."

"Can we fix it?" Benny asked hopefully, his little paws touching the broken pieces carefully.

"Let's try together," Mama said, giving him a warm hug. "And maybe we can make it even more special."

They spent the morning gluing the pieces back together. Where the cracks showed, they painted pretty gold lines that sparkled in the sunlight. The vase looked different now, but it was beautiful in a new way.

"I like our fixed vase," Benny said, admiring their work. "It tells a story about making mistakes and fixing them together."

Mama kissed the top of his fuzzy head. "That's right, Benny. When we make mistakes, the best thing to do is tell the truth and try to make things better."

From that day on, Benny always listened more carefully and remembered that being honest, even when it's hard, always leads to the best endings.

The End.
