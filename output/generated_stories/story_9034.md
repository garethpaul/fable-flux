# Shelly's Special Guest Room Adventure

Shelly the turtle was very shy. She lived in a cozy guest room with soft blue walls and a big window that let in warm sunshine. Today was special because children from around the world were coming to visit!

"Oh my," whispered Shelly, pulling her head into her shell. "I don't know how to talk to children from other places."

The first visitor was Maria from Mexico. She had long black hair and wore a colorful dress with bright flowers. Maria knocked gently on the guest room door.

"Hola! Hello!" said Maria with a big smile. "May I come in?"

Shelly peeked out just a tiny bit. "H-hello," she said quietly.

Maria sat down on the soft carpet. "I brought something to share!" She pulled out beautiful crayons that smelled like vanilla and showed Shelly how to draw zigzag patterns. "In Mexico, we make art like this!"

Soon, Ahmed arrived from Egypt. He had kind brown eyes and carried a small bag. "As-salamu alaykum! Peace be with you!" he said cheerfully.

"What's in your bag?" Shelly asked, feeling a little braver.

"Special soap that smells like jasmine flowers!" Ahmed said. "In my country, we always wash our hands before we eat or play. Would you like to try?"

The jasmine soap felt smooth and bubbly. Shelly giggled as the sweet smell tickled her nose. "It smells like a garden!"

Next came Lin from China. She had short black hair and wore red shoes that made soft tapping sounds on the floor. "Ni hao! Hello, friends!"

Lin opened her backpack and pulled out shiny paper in gold and red. "Let's make paper cranes together! In China, we fold paper to make beautiful birds."

Shelly watched Lin's gentle fingers fold and crease. "Can you teach me?" Shelly asked, stretching her neck out further.

"Of course!" Lin said. "We can all learn from each other."

As the afternoon went on, more children arrived. There was Kofi from Ghana who shared a wooden drum, Emma from Australia who brought eucalyptus leaves that smelled fresh and minty, and Raj from India who taught them a happy clapping song.

The guest room filled with laughter, music, and the wonderful smells of jasmine soap and eucalyptus leaves. Shelly's shell felt warm and cozy, not scary anymore.

"I have something to share too!" Shelly said suddenly. She had never felt so brave! She showed everyone her collection of smooth, round stones from the garden. "Each stone is different, just like all of us!"

The children sat in a circle, each holding one of Shelly's special stones. Maria drew pictures on hers with her vanilla crayons. Ahmed washed his stone with the jasmine soap until it sparkled. Lin folded tiny paper flowers to decorate hers.

"We're all different, but we can be friends!" said Shelly, no longer hiding in her shell.

"Yes!" said all the children together. "Friends from everywhere!"

As the sun set through the big window, painting the guest room walls orange and pink, Shelly realized something wonderful. Being different made each friend special. Sharing what they loved made everyone happy. And being clean and kind made playing together even more fun.

From that day on, Shelly was never too shy to make new friends from anywhere in the world.

The End.
