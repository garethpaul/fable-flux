# Rooftop Raccoon Rhythm

On a soft, blue evening, a musical raccoon named Rolo climbed up to the rooftop. The air smelled like fresh rain. Tiny lights twinkled in the city below. Rolo wore a small red scarf and carried a tin drum. Tap-tap! Tum-tum! The sound bounced off the chimneys and made Rolo smile.

“I love to play and dance,” Rolo said. He wiggled his striped tail and did a little spin. His paws went pat-pat on the roof tiles. A cool breeze brushed his whiskers. It felt nice.

Rolo had a little rooftop club with friends. There was Bibi the bluebird, Nib the mouse, and Hana the hedgehog. They met to play music, dance, and do their rooftop chores. Everyone had a job.

Rolo read the list painted on a small wooden sign:
- Rolo: sweep the roof
- Bibi: pick up leaves
- Nib: tidy the snack box
- Hana: check the water bucket

Rolo looked at his drum. He looked at the broom. He wanted to dance, but he also had a chore. He took a slow breath in and out. “Mindful breath,” he whispered. “I can do both. First we work, then we dance.”

Bibi zipped down with a flutter. “Tweet! Let’s start!” she chirped.

Rolo nodded. “Yes! We all have a job. That is fair.”

They began. Sweep-sweep went Rolo’s broom. Whoosh-whoosh went Bibi, carrying leaves to the bin. Rustle-rustle went Hana, checking the bucket. Nib hummed as he stacked crackers, crunch-crunch.

But soon the tin drum winked at Rolo. It was so shiny. “Just a little tap,” he said. Tap-tap-tap! Tum-tum-tum!

Bibi paused. “Rolo, your job is sweeping,” she said gently.

“I will sweep after one song,” Rolo said. He did a twirl. His feet went pat-pat-pat. “Listen to the beat!”

Nib watched the dance and forgot the snack box. Crackers slid out. Crinkle! Pop! “Oops,” Nib squeaked.

Hana peeked into the water bucket. “Oh dear,” she said. “It is still dirty.”

Leaves started to blow back on the roof because the bin lid was open. The roof was messy again. The snacks were everywhere. The water was not clean. Rolo stopped drumming. His ears drooped.

“This does not feel fair,” Bibi said. “I am doing my part, but the roof is still messy.”

Rolo’s heart thumped. He felt warm in his cheeks. He looked at his friends’ tired faces. “You are right,” he said. “I wanted to dance first. That made more work for you.”

Rolo put the drum down. He placed a paw on his chest and took three slow breaths. In and out. In and out. In and out. The breeze cooled his fur. He felt calm.

“I can fix this,” Rolo said. His voice was steady and kind. “Friends, I will sweep now. Nib, can you please close the snack box? Bibi, may you hold the bin lid? Hana, can we wash the bucket together? Let’s make the jobs fair.”

Bibi nodded. “Thank you for saying it clearly, Rolo.”

“That was very brave,” Hana added. “You were assertive and kind.”

Rolo smiled. “We can be fair and have fun.”

They tried again. This time, Rolo swept all the tiles. Sweep-sweep-sweep. He moved the dust into neat piles. Bibi held the bin lid tight. Whoosh! In went the leaves. Nib stacked the crackers and clicked the lid shut. Click! Hana and Rolo rinsed the bucket together. Splish-splash! The water swirled clean and clear.

When they were done, the rooftop looked fresh and bright. The moon shone like a silver coin. The city lights blinked like tiny stars. Rolo felt proud. His tail swished happily.

“Now it is time to dance,” Rolo said. “And here is my fair idea: We take turns picking the song.”

Bibi chirped, “I pick a flutter song!”

Nib squeaked, “I pick a tiny tiptoe song!”

Hana said, “I pick a slow roll song.”

Rolo said, “I pick a happy drum song.”

They made a circle. The tin drum went tum-tum. Bibi fluttered, Nib tiptoed, Hana rolled, and Rolo spun. Their feet made soft sounds on the tiles: pat, tap, tip, roll. The air smelled like night flowers. Everyone laughed.

After the dance, Rolo spoke up. “Friends, today I learned about fairness. Fair means we share the work and the fun. Fair means we listen and take turns. Fair means we speak up in a kind way.”

Bibi said, “When we were fair, the chores were easy.”

Nib added, “When we were fair, the dance was more fun!”

Hana smiled. “When we were fair, everyone felt good.”

Rolo hugged his drum and the broom. “Music and chores can be friends,” he said. “We can be mindful, do our jobs, and still dance.”

They watched the moon a little longer. The night felt calm. The rooftop felt safe and clean. Rolo’s scarf fluttered in the breeze. He did one last gentle tap on the drum. Tap.

“All done,” Rolo whispered. “Fair and fun.”

The End.
