# Squeaky’s Smart Choices

Squeaky the resourceful squirrel lived in a warm kitchen inside a little blue house. The kitchen smelled like cinnamon toast and sounded like a gentle hum from the fridge. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. Squeaky loved this cozy place.

One morning, Squeaky wiggled his whiskers. “Today I will make nut muffins,” he said. “Yum!” His tiny paws clapped. But the kitchen looked messy. The wooden spoon was under a towel. The mixing bowl was upside down on a chair. Nuts and raisins were in two different jars, far apart.

Squeaky took a deep breath. He felt the warm air in his nose and the soft floor under his feet. “I can make a smart plan,” he whispered. “I can make good choices, one at a time.”

He looked at three big steps on the fridge on a sticky note. He had written them yesterday.

Step 1: Stop and Breathe.
Step 2: Think of Choices.
Step 3: Pick One and Try.

Squeaky touched the note. “I remember,” he said.

He stood still and breathed in and out, slowly. In… out… in… out. He felt calm. The kitchen seemed friendlier.

“First problem,” said Squeaky. “I can’t find the spoon.” He thought of choices.

Choice A: I can look under the towel.
Choice B: I can climb the cupboard.
Choice C: I can use my paw.

Squeaky smiled. “I will try Choice A.” He lifted the towel. “Aha!” There was the spoon, cool and smooth. “Good choice,” he said, tail twitching happily.

Next, he needed the mixing bowl. It sat upside down on the chair. “How can I get it safely?” he asked himself.

Choice A: Jump from the counter.
Choice B: Move the chair closer.
Choice C: Ask for help.

He looked at the chair. It was close, but not close enough. “I will try Choice B.” He braced his paws, pushed gently, and slid the chair beside the counter. “Squeak-squeak, push-push.” The chair made a soft scrape on the tile. He climbed down carefully and lifted the bowl. It felt cool and light. “That was safe,” he said. “I like safe choices.”

Now he needed to measure flour. The bag was big. “I can be mindful,” he told himself. “Slow and careful.” He poured the flour scoop by scoop. White puffs floated in the warm air. “It looks like snow,” he giggled.

He added sugar. He cracked an egg, listening to the tiny tap-tap. He smelled the sweet vanilla. The kitchen began to smell like a hug. Squeaky wiggled with joy.

Then he saw the nuts and raisins—two jars far apart. “I want both,” he said. “But I have only two paws, and I need to stir. What should I do?”

He took a breath. In… out… in… out.

Choice A: Carry both jars at once.
Choice B: Bring one jar and then the other.
Choice C: Ask the whisk to help. (He laughed. Whisks can’t walk!)

He looked at his small paws. “I will try Choice B.” He brought the nut jar first. He set it by the bowl. Then he brought the raisins. He felt proud. “That worked,” he said. “One step at a time.”

He stirred the batter with slow circles. Swish, swish. The wooden spoon made a friendly sound. “I can keep the kitchen tidy,” he said. “That is good organization.” He put the lid back on each jar. He wiped a little spill with a cloth. The counter felt smooth and clean.

When the muffins were in the pan, Squeaky had one more problem. “Which rack should I use?” He looked at the oven, warm and glowing like a soft campfire.

Choice A: The high rack.
Choice B: The middle rack.
Choice C: The very bottom.

He remembered what the cookbook said in big simple letters: Use the middle rack. “I choose B,” he said. “I follow the recipe.” He slid the pan in. The oven whooshed gently. He set a timer. Tick, tick, tick.

Waiting was hard. Squeaky’s tail wiggled and waggled. He wanted to open the door. But he stopped and breathed. In… out… in… out. “I can be patient,” he told himself. “I can practice mindfulness.”

He looked around. “While I wait, I can build skills.” He sorted the spoons big to small. He stacked bowls from largest to tiniest. He lined up the spices in a neat rainbow: red paprika, yellow turmeric, green basil. Everything looked tidy. He felt calm and strong.

Ding! The timer sang. Warm muffin smells danced in the air. Squeaky wore his oven mitts. He took the muffins out. The tops were golden and soft. Steam curled like tiny clouds. “We did it,” he whispered to the kitchen. He took a little nibble. “Mmm! Sweet, nutty, and just right.”

He set a muffin on a plate for later. He wrote a new sticky note and put it by his first one.

Today’s Smart Choices:
- I looked before I leaped.
- I moved the chair, not me.
- I carried one thing at a time.
- I followed the recipe.
- I waited and stayed calm.
- I cleaned as I cooked.

Squeaky smiled. “Good decisions make the day easier,” he said. “When I stop, think, and choose, I feel safe and proud.”

He washed the spoon. He folded the towel. He put the bowl back where it belongs. The warm kitchen shone. The golden squares of sun slid across the tidy floor. Squeaky munched his muffin and hugged himself with his fluffy tail.

“Next time,” he said, “I can make good choices again. One smart step at a time.”

The End.
