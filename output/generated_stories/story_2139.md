# Nutkin's Rainbow Discovery

Nutkin was a fluffy gray squirrel with bright black eyes and a bushy tail. Every morning, she scampered across the red brick rooftop of her apartment building, collecting acorns for breakfast. The rough tiles felt warm under her tiny paws as the sun painted everything golden.

"Same old routine," Nutkin sighed, stuffing another acorn into her cheek. "Find acorns, eat acorns, sleep. Every single day." Her tail drooped as she looked around the quiet rooftop.

Just then, she heard cheerful chirping from below. Nutkin's ears perked up. She scurried to the roof's edge and peeked down at the colorful garden.

"¡Buenos días!" called Maya, a bright yellow canary perched on a sunflower. "Good morning!"

"Bonjour!" sang Pierre, a blue jay with shimmering feathers. "Beautiful day, non?"

"Guten Morgen!" chirped Klaus, a tiny red robin. "The flowers smell wonderful!"

Nutkin's whiskers twitched with curiosity. "They all sound so different!" she thought. "But they're all saying the same thing - good morning!"

She scrambled down the fire escape, her claws clicking on the metal steps. "Hello!" she called shyly. "I'm Nutkin. I live on the rooftop."

Maya fluttered her sunny wings. "¡Hola, Nutkin! I'm from Mexico. Would you like to learn some Spanish words?"

"Oui!" said Pierre, puffing out his blue chest. "And I can teach you French! My grandmother flew here from Paris."

Klaus hopped excitedly on his thin legs. "My family came from Germany! German words are fun to say!"

Nutkin's tail began to twitch with excitement. "Really? You all speak different languages?"

"Si! Yes! Ja!" they all said together, then burst into musical laughter.

For the rest of the morning, Nutkin learned amazing new words. "Bellota" meant acorn in Spanish. "Gland" was acorn in French. "Eichel" was acorn in German. Each word felt like a tiny treasure in her mouth.

"Your languages make everything sound so beautiful!" Nutkin exclaimed, her eyes sparkling. "Even my ordinary acorns sound special now!"

Maya taught her a Spanish song about dancing in the sunshine. Pierre showed her how to say "I love you" in French: "Je t'aime." Klaus shared a German counting song that made numbers sound like music.

As the sun climbed higher, Nutkin realized something wonderful. "You know what? We're all different, but we're all friends. We all love the morning sun, and we all care about each other."

"Exacto!" said Maya. "Exactly!"

"C'est vrai!" agreed Pierre. "That's true!"

"Genau!" nodded Klaus. "Precisely!"

From that day forward, Nutkin's morning routine became magical. She still collected acorns, but now she counted them in three languages. She still watched the sunrise, but now she greeted it with songs from around the world.

The rooftop didn't feel lonely anymore. It felt like the top of the whole wide world, where different languages mixed like colors in a rainbow, and friendship spoke every language of all.

When Nutkin tucked herself into her cozy nest that night, she whispered, "Gracias, merci, danke" to her new friends. Her heart felt as full and warm as her cheeks full of acorns.

"Tomorrow," she thought happily, "I'll learn even more beautiful words."

The End.
