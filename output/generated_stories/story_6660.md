# Leo's Little List

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright curious eyes. Today, he padded into the home office, where Mama worked. The room smelled like warm paper and lemony cleaner. The sun shone through the window, making a cozy square of light on the carpet.

Tap-tap-tap, went Mama’s keyboard. Whirr, hummed the printer. “Good morning, Leo,” Mama said, smiling. “What is your plan today?”

Leo stood tall on his paws. “I want to do everything!” he roared in a tiny voice. “I want to build blocks, draw pictures, water the plants, read a book, and help you, too!”

Mama nodded. “That is a lot. Let’s be brave and smart with our time. We can make a little list.”

“A list?” Leo blinked. He liked the word. It felt soft and neat.

Mama slid over a small notepad that smelled like fresh paper. “A list helps us know what to do first, next, and last. We will do one thing at a time.”

Leo took a deep breath in—slow and calm. He listened to the tap-tap-tap and the soft hum. His whiskers twitched. “One thing at a time,” he said.

They wrote:
1. Water the plants.
2. Build blocks.
3. Draw a picture.
4. Read a book.
5. Help Mama.

Mama set a gentle timer. It ticked like a tiny cricket. “We will give each job a little time,” she said. “When the timer sings, we can pause and check our list.”

Leo nodded. “I can do that!”

First: water the plants. Leo held a small watering can. The water plink-plink-plinked into the soil. The leaves looked shiny and happy. “Good morning, plant friends,” Leo whispered. “Here is your drink.” He noticed the cool water on his paw and the earthy smell of the dirt. “I am going slow,” he told himself. “Slow helps me not spill.”

The timer chimed—ding-ding! Leo smiled. “I did the first thing!” He padded to the list and put a bright star sticker next to number one.

“Great focus,” Mama said. “How do you feel?”

“Proud,” said Leo. His chest felt warm like the sunny square on the floor.

Next: build blocks. Leo stacked red, blue, and yellow blocks. Click, clack, click. He made a tall tower. It wobbled a little. “Hmm,” Leo said. “I can try a strong base.” He placed the bigger blocks on the bottom. The tower stood firm. “I was open to a new way,” he said. “That helped!”

Ding-ding! The timer sang again. “Time to pause,” said Mama.

Leo gave the tower a gentle pat. He put a star by number two. “I like my list,” he said. “It helps me know what to do.”

Next: draw a picture. Leo sat at the desk. The paper felt smooth under his paw. Scritch-scratch went the crayons. He drew his tower and his plant friends, and a small sun peeking in. He chose colors slowly. “Yellow for the sun. Green for the leaves. Blue for the sky,” he whispered. He took a slow breath to feel calm and happy. The room smelled like waxy crayons now.

Ding-ding!

“I’m not done,” Leo said, frowning a little.

Mama knelt beside him. “You can finish after the next thing. Our list helps us make time for everything. Would you like to put a tiny dot next to number three and add a star later when it is all done?”

Leo thought. He was open-minded and brave. “Yes,” he said. “A dot now, a star later.”

Next: read a book. Leo curled in the sunny square with Mama. The pages went flip, flip. The pictures were bright. They read about animals who took turns. Leo listened to Mama’s soft voice and the rustle of pages. He felt calm and cozy.

Ding-ding!

Leo grinned and put a star by number four. “Almost there!”

Last: help Mama. Mama pointed to a basket of paper clips that had spilled. “Can you help me sort these back into the cup?”

Leo’s paws were gentle. Click, click, the clips went into the cup. He sorted them by small and big. “Small clips here, big clips there,” he said. “This is tidy!”

Ding-ding!

Leo placed a star by number five. He looked at the list and gasped. “I need to finish my drawing!” He ran back to the paper and added a little lion cub with bright eyes. Scritch-scratch. He drew a tiny heart next to Mama.

“All done,” Leo said. He placed a big shiny star next to number three. Every number had a star now.

Mama clapped softly. “You made a plan, took mindful breaths, and did one thing at a time. How does your body feel?”

Leo closed his eyes and took a slow breath in and out. He felt light and peaceful. “I feel proud and calm,” he said. “My list helped me use my time.”

Mama hugged him, and Leo heard her heartbeat—thump, thump—steady and kind. “That’s time-management,” she said. “You were open to trying new ways, and you were mindful with each step.”

Leo looked around the home office. The plants were watered. The tower stood tall. The drawing was bright. The book was read. The clips were in the cup. Leo’s whiskers twitched with joy. “I did everything,” he said, “by doing one thing at a time.”

He placed the list on the desk. “Tomorrow,” Leo said, “I can make a new list.”

“Yes,” Mama smiled. “And we will listen, breathe, and take turns with our time.”

Leo purred a tiny roar. “I am brave,” he said, “and I can manage my time.”

The End.
