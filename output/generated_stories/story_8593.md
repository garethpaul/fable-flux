# Leo Listens in the Shed

One sunny morning, a brave little lion cub named Leo padded across the yard. His paws were soft on the warm grass. He sniffed the air. It smelled like flowers and fresh dirt. He heard a gentle clink-clank from the garden shed.

“I want to help,” Leo said, his whiskers twitching. “I want to do something brave today!”

Mama Lion smiled from the porch. “Brave cubs listen first and decide carefully,” she called. “Remember to think, listen, and get ready.”

“I will!” Leo said. His heart felt big and bouncy.

Leo nudged open the garden shed door. The shed was cool and shadowy. Sunbeams made little dust sparkles dance. He heard a soft drip-drip from a faucet and the tiny buzz of a fly. The shed smelled like wood, grass, and a little bit of paint.

Inside, there were shelves with neat boxes. There were gloves, a watering can, a rake, and a shiny red bucket. There was also a small wagon with squeaky wheels.

Leo’s eyes grew wide. “So many things!” he whispered. “What should I do first?”

He wanted to rush. His paws wiggled. But then he remembered Mama’s words: brave cubs listen first and decide carefully.

Leo closed his eyes for a moment and took a slow breath. “Listen,” he told himself. He heard the drip-drip again. He heard the squeak of the wagon. He heard a tiny rustle—was it a mouse? It was just a dry leaf sliding on the floor.

“Okay,” Leo said. “I will make a plan. I will decide in three steps: listen, think, and get ready.”

He looked at the shiny red bucket. “The tomatoes are thirsty,” he guessed. He walked to the faucet. The metal felt cool under his paw. “Should I fill the bucket full?” he asked himself. He thought about the heavy bucket.

He remembered Mama’s words again. “Think, Leo,” he said. “Is it safe? Is it smart? Am I ready?”

Leo decided to fill the bucket only halfway. “I can carry this,” he said. He put on the small garden gloves from the shelf. They felt snug and soft. “Gloves keep my paws safe,” he said proudly.

As he lifted the bucket, the wagon squeaked. “Maybe the wagon can help,” Leo said. He set the bucket in the wagon and pulled the handle. It rattled but rolled. “Good choice,” he smiled.

Just then, Papa Lion peeked in. “Hello, helper! What’s your plan?”

Leo lifted his chin. “I listened. I thought. I got ready. I am taking water to the tomatoes. I used the wagon so it won’t spill.”

Papa Lion nodded. “That is a good decision. You listened to the sounds. You thought about safety. You prepared with gloves and a wagon.”

Leo’s chest felt warm and proud. He pulled the wagon outside. The sun touched his fur, and it felt like a hug. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” The tomato plants stood tall and green.

“Here you go, tomatoes,” Leo said. He poured slowly. The water made a soft trickle sound. The dirt smelled sweet and wet.

Back in the shed, Leo heard another sound. Tap-tap-tap. A small pot on the shelf had a tiny crack. “Hmm,” Leo said. “What should I do?”

He did his three steps again. “Listen. Think. Get ready.” He touched the pot gently. It felt rough on the crack. “It can break,” he said. “I need to fix it or put it away safely.”

He looked around. He found a roll of tape and a small box labeled “Fix-It.” He put on his gloves again. “I will tape the crack for now and tell Mama,” he decided. He carefully taped the pot and set it in the box so it would not fall.

Mama Lion came to the door and sniffed the air. “It smells like wet dirt,” she said. “What have you decided today, my brave cub?”

Leo told her, “I listened to the sounds. I thought about what was safe and smart. I got ready with gloves and the wagon. I watered the tomatoes. I fixed the cracked pot a little, and I will tell you when something needs more help.”

Mama Lion’s eyes shone. “That is wise. Brave is not just big and loud. Brave is using your ears, your mind, and your heart.”

Leo’s ears perked up. “Using my ears, my mind, and my heart,” he repeated. The words felt like a song.

A breeze blew in the shed door. It felt cool on Leo’s nose. He could smell mint leaves nearby. “Can I help with one more thing?” he asked.

Mama Lion pointed to the seeds on the shelf. “We can plant these together. What should we do first?”

Leo smiled. “First, we listen. Then we think. Then we get ready. We need a small shovel, soft soil, water, and a sunny spot.”

They gathered the tools. The shovel felt smooth. The soil felt crumbly and cool. The seeds were tiny and tickly in Leo’s paw. They dug small holes, dropped in the seeds, and patted the soil. Leo gave a gentle drink of water. Plink-plink.

Mama Lion hugged Leo. “You made great choices today.”

Leo purred. “It feels good to decide carefully. Listening, thinking, and getting ready makes me brave.”

He looked at the shed and the sunny garden. He felt calm and strong. “Tomorrow, I will listen, think, and get ready again,” he said.

The End.
