# Ollie's Big Cleanup Adventure

In a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls lived Ollie, a playful brown otter. His whiskers twitched with curiosity. His fluffy tail swished when he was happy.

Ollie loved his special corner by the window. Warm sunlight made his fur feel toasty. He could see the garden pond where real otters played.

One morning, Ollie woke up excited. "Today I'll organize my toy river!" he squeaked. His toy river was made of blocks, shells, and smooth stones.

But oh no! His river looked messy. Blocks were scattered everywhere. Shells hid under the bed. Stones rolled in different corners.

"This is too hard!" Ollie huffed. His little paws felt tired already. "There are too many pieces!"

Ollie tried picking up one block. It was heavy. He tried gathering shells, but they slipped through his paws. The stones kept rolling away.

"Grrrr!" Ollie growled, feeling frustrated. His whiskers drooped down. "I can't do this! It's impossible!"

Just then, Teddy Bear waddled over. "What's wrong, Ollie?" Teddy asked in his gentle voice.

"My river is too messy," Ollie sniffled. "I wanted to make it beautiful, but everything is too hard to move."

Teddy smiled warmly. "When I feel frustrated, I take three deep breaths. Want to try?"

Ollie breathed in slowly. The air smelled like lavender from Mom's garden. He breathed out. In again. Out again. His shoulders felt less tight.

"Better?" asked Teddy.

"A little," Ollie admitted.

"Now, what if we work together?" suggested Bunny, hopping over. "Many paws make light work!"

"And let's start small," added Teddy. "Just one corner first."

Ollie's whiskers perked up. "Really? You'll help me?"

"Of course!" said his friends.

They made a plan. Teddy would stack blocks with his strong arms. Bunny would hop and gather shells in her soft ears. Ollie would carefully arrange the smooth stones.

"One, two, three!" they chanted together.

Working as a team felt wonderful. Teddy's blocks made perfect walls. Bunny's shells sparkled like real riverbanks. Ollie's stones created a beautiful flowing path.

"Look!" Ollie squeaked with joy. "We're making something amazing!"

As they worked, Ollie felt proud. His careful paw movements made the prettiest stone patterns. His ideas helped make the river look real.

"You know what?" said Ollie, stepping back to admire their work. "This river will help us remember to keep real rivers clean too!"

"How?" asked Bunny.

"We can pretend to pick up trash," Ollie explained. "Just like we organized these toys! Real otters need clean water to be happy."

When they finished, their toy river looked magnificent. Clear blue fabric flowed between the blocks. Shells dotted the banks like tiny treasures. Stones created gentle rapids.

"We did it!" Ollie cheered, doing a happy spin. "When I felt frustrated, I almost gave up. But taking deep breaths helped me feel calmer."

"And asking for help made it fun!" added Teddy.

"Now I know I can do hard things," Ollie said proudly. "Especially with good friends and a good plan."

That evening, as golden sunset light filled the bedroom, Ollie curled up by his beautiful river. He felt calm and proud. Tomorrow he would play otter games and maybe help his friends with their projects too.

"Taking deep breaths, asking for help, and starting small," Ollie whispered sleepily. "That's how to handle big feelings and big jobs."

The End.
