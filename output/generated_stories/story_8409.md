# Sunny’s Treehouse Trophy

Sunny the squirrel lived in a cozy treehouse at the top of a tall oak. The treehouse smelled like pine wood and fresh leaves. In the mornings, golden light danced on the wooden floor, and a gentle breeze made the wind chimes sing. Sunny was small, quick, and very resourceful. Sunny loved to build things, and Sunny loved to play sports too.

One bright day, a flyer fluttered into the treehouse window. Sunny caught it with quick paws. “Tree Treetop Games!” Sunny read aloud. “Build a hoop. Then play fair and follow every rule.” Sunny’s eyes sparkled. “I can do that!”

Sunny pulled out a little toolbox—a tiny hammer, a measuring string, smooth nails, and wood pieces. There were also clear directions on a big folded paper with pictures and numbers.

“Step 1: Measure the board,” Sunny read. “Step 2: Hammer slowly. Step 3: Attach the hoop. Step 4: Test safely.”

Sunny nodded. “I will follow every direction.” The wind smelled sweet, like warm leaves. Sunny placed the board flat and used the measuring string. “One, two, three paw-lengths,” Sunny counted softly.

A blue jay named Pip landed on the windowsill. “Hi, Sunny! Need help?” chirped Pip.

“Yes, please!” said Sunny. “I need you to hold the board still. The directions say to keep it steady.”

Pip pressed the board with a wing. “Steady!” Pip chirped.

Sunny tapped the nails. Tap, tap, tap. The hammer made a gentle beat. “I’m going slow, just like it says,” Sunny said, feeling proud. The wood smelled warm and clean. Dust tickled Sunny’s nose. “Achoo!” Sunny giggled.

By the time the sun climbed higher, the hoop frame was ready. “Now Step 3: Attach the hoop,” Sunny read. Sunny lined up the shiny ring with the marks on the board. “Right on the dot,” Sunny said. Pip handed over tiny screws. Twist, twist, twist. The hoop stuck on snug and straight.

Just then, a chipmunk named Tiki scurried in, bouncing with a soft ball. “Let’s play now!” Tiki cheered. “Skip the last step. We can test later.”

Sunny looked at the paper. It said, “Step 4: Test safely.” Sunny felt a little tug in the tummy. “I want to play,” Sunny said, “but the directions say to test safely.”

Tiki rolled the ball in paws. “We’ll be fine! I’ll toss gently.”

Sunny shook a fluffy tail. “The directions help us play safe and fair. We should check the hoop first.”

Pip nodded. “Rules keep wings and tails safe.”

Sunny tugged the board, checked each screw, and wiggled the hoop. “Hmm,” Sunny said. “This screw is loose.” Sunny tightened it. Twist, twist. “Now it’s safe.”

They carried the hoop to the treehouse window where a soft leaf mat waited below. Sunny read the game rules on the flyer. “One: Take turns. Two: Toss from the leaf line. Three: Count together. Four: Clap for everyone.”

Sunny drew a leaf line on the floor with a chalky stick. Pip chirped, “I see it!” Tiki bounced. “I go first!”

Sunny smiled. “We will follow the rules. Let’s pick the order.” Sunny placed three acorn caps in a cup and shook it. “This is fair.” Tiki picked first. “I got a 1!” Pip picked next. “I got a 2!” Sunny picked last. “I got a 3!”

Tiki stood at the leaf line and tossed. The ball swished through the hoop. “Swish!” It sounded like a whispering breeze. Pip clapped wings. “Good job!” Sunny cheered.

Pip went next. The ball tapped the rim—tink!—and bounced out. Pip drooped a little. Sunny smiled kindly. “Great try. We clap for everyone.” They clapped together. Pip tried again on the next turn and scored. “Swish!” Pip beamed.

Sunny stepped to the leaf line. The ball felt smooth and cool. Sunny breathed in the pine smell and counted softly, “One, two, toss!” The ball went up, up, and swish! Sunny laughed with joy. “Hooray!”

After a few rounds, Tiki scooted a little past the leaf line. Sunny noticed. “Tiki,” Sunny said gently, “the rules say to toss from the line. Let’s keep it fair.”

Tiki’s cheeks turned pink. “Oh! I forgot. I’m sorry.”

Sunny nodded. “Thanks for fixing it. Following directions keeps the game fair.” Tiki stepped back to the line and tossed again. Swish!

They played until the sky turned peach and the wind chimes tinkled softly. The hoop stayed strong. The board didn’t wobble. Sunny’s careful building and testing worked. They counted scores together. “Let’s see,” Sunny said. “We each tried hard. We each followed the rules. Today, we all win fun!”

Pip fluffed feathers. “Following directions kept us safe.”

Tiki grinned. “And it made the game fair!”

Sunny looked at the neat paper of directions and smiled. “Directions tell us what to do, and we listen. When we follow them, we build better and play better.”

They hung a ribbon on the hoop that read, “Safe, Fair, and Fun.” Sunny felt warm and proud inside. The treehouse glowed in the soft sunset.

Sunny said, “Today I learned that following directions is a way to be kind, safe, and fair. It helps us build strong things and enjoy our games.”

Pip and Tiki nodded. They all took a deep breath of the leafy evening air. The wind chimes sang a happy little song.

“Tomorrow,” Sunny said, “let’s read the directions for a new game.”

“Let’s!” chirped Pip.

“Yes!” squeaked Tiki.

Together, they cleaned up the tools, folded the paper neatly, and set the ball by the window. The treehouse felt calm and cozy, ready for more fun to come.

The End.
