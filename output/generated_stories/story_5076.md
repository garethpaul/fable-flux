# Melody's Gift of Song

In a cozy guest room with soft yellow walls and fluffy white curtains, lived a little singing bird named Melody. She had bright blue feathers that sparkled in the morning sunlight and the sweetest voice in the whole house.

Every day, Melody would perch on the windowsill and sing beautiful songs. "Tweet-tweet-trill!" she would chirp happily, watching the world outside through the big glass window.

One morning, Melody noticed something different. Mrs. Chen, who lived in the house, was lying in the guest room bed looking very tired and sad. Her face was pale, and she had tissues scattered around her.

"Oh my," thought Melody, ruffling her blue feathers. "Mrs. Chen doesn't look well at all. She usually smiles and waves at me when she passes by."

Melody heard Mrs. Chen cough softly. "Achoo!" Mrs. Chen sneezed, then sighed deeply. "I feel so sick and lonely," she whispered to herself.

The little bird's heart felt heavy. She wanted to help, but what could a small bird do? Melody looked around the guest room. She saw the soft pillows, the warm blanket, and the pretty flowers on the nightstand, but none of those things seemed to make Mrs. Chen feel better.

Then Melody had a wonderful idea! She puffed up her chest and cleared her tiny throat.

"Tweet-tweet-trill! Good morning, sunshine!" Melody sang in her most cheerful voice. The melody was soft and gentle, like a musical hug.

Mrs. Chen's eyes opened wider. She turned toward the window and smiled just a little bit. "Oh, hello there, little bird," she said weakly.

Melody felt excited! Her song had helped! She decided to sing even more. She sang a happy song about dancing butterflies: "Flutter-flutter, up so high, pretty colors in the sky!"

Mrs. Chen giggled softly. The sound made Melody's heart feel warm and fuzzy.

All day long, Melody sang different songs for Mrs. Chen. She sang about bouncing bunnies, splashing rain, and twinkling stars. With each song, Mrs. Chen smiled a little more and looked a little brighter.

"Your songs are like medicine for my heart," Mrs. Chen told Melody. "They make me feel so much better!"

Melody realized something important. Even though she was just a small bird, she had something very special to give – her beautiful voice and cheerful songs. By sharing her gift, she was helping Mrs. Chen feel less lonely and more hopeful.

The next day, Mrs. Chen brought a small dish of fresh water and tiny seeds to the windowsill. "Thank you for being so kind to me when I was sick," she said. "Your generous heart and beautiful songs helped me heal faster."

Melody chirped happily and ate the delicious seeds. She felt proud and warm inside, knowing that sharing her talents had made someone else feel better.

From that day on, whenever someone in the house felt sad or sick, Melody would sing her special healing songs. She learned that being generous with her gifts – even something as simple as a song – could bring joy and wellness to others.

"Tweet-tweet-trill! Sharing is caring!" Melody sang, and everyone in the house felt a little bit happier.

The End.
