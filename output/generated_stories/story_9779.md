# Gigi’s Good-Morning Plan

In a warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon toast and sweet bananas, a thoughtful giraffe named Gigi peeked in through the wide, sunny window. Her long neck bent low, and her soft nose touched the curtains. “Good morning,” Gigi whispered to the little friends inside—Bun the bunny, Dot the mouse, and Pip the puppy. The kettle hummed, the toaster popped, and the sun made golden squares on the floor.

Every morning, Gigi helped. She liked the warm kitchen and the safe, cozy sounds. But lately, mornings felt messy. Cups clinked, crumbs scattered, and no one knew what to do first.

“I want our mornings to feel calm,” Gigi said, tapping her hoof softly. “I have a goal. I want a good-morning plan.”

“A plan?” asked Bun, wiggling her whiskers.

“A plan,” Gigi nodded. “A simple routine. We do the same helpful steps each day.”

Dot squeaked, “Steps are tiny! I can do tiny steps!”

Pip’s tail thumped on the floor. “Can we still have toast?”

“Of course,” Gigi smiled. “Toast fits in the plan.”

They all giggled. The kitchen clock tick-tick-ticked, like it was cheering them on.

Gigi used her gentle voice. “Let’s set a goal: We will be ready for the day by the last kettle whistle.”

“What are the steps?” Bun asked, ears up.

Gigi looked around and sniffed the warm, toasty air. “Step one: Good-morning stretch and drink of water. Step two: Tidy the table. Step three: Set out cups and plates. Step four: Toast time and fruit. Step five: Quick clean. Then, ready!”

“Teamwork!” barked Pip, hopping.

“Leadership!” squeaked Dot, pointing at Gigi.

Gigi’s cheeks warmed. “Leadership means listening, too. What do you think?”

Bun said, “I can put napkins in a neat square.”

Dot said, “I can line up spoons with tiny clinks.”

Pip said, “I can carry the bread—carefully!”

Gigi nodded. “Great ideas. We can all help.”

They tried it right away. Gigi poured cool water into little cups. The cups made a tink-tink sound. “Sip,” she said. Everyone took a happy sip. Stretch, stretch—Pip’s paws reached forward, Bun’s ears reached high, and Dot’s tail swished like a tiny ribbon.

They tidied the table. Crumbs brushed into a sunny little pile. Plates and cups lined up like a friendly parade. The toaster hummed. Pop! Pop! Golden toast jumped up, warm and crisp. The smell was buttery and sweet. Pip’s nose wiggled. Dot clapped. Bun smiled and smoothed the napkins.

But then—uh-oh! Pip forgot to carry the bread carefully. A bag slipped to the floor with a soft thump. Slices flopped out like wiggly cards.

Pip’s ears drooped. “I’m sorry,” he whispered, tail still.

Gigi took a slow breath. “Thank you for saying sorry,” she said kindly. “We can make amends. Making amends means we fix the mistake.”

“I can pick up the slices,” said Pip, voice small. “I can check for crumbs.”

“I can help,” said Bun, hopping over with a dustpan.

“I can hold the bag open,” said Dot, paws steady.

Gigi smiled. “Teamwork fixes spills.”

Together, they picked up the bread. Pip checked each slice. Bun brushed crumbs into the pan—swish, swish. Dot held the bag wide. Gigi tied the top with a neat little knot. The kitchen felt calm again. Pip’s tail wagged slowly, then faster.

They tried the steps once more. Tick-tick-tick went the clock. Sip, stretch, tidy, set, toast, fruit, clean. Gigi placed banana slices in a smiling circle on each plate. The yellow moons smelled sweet. “We did it,” she said.

Dot squeaked, “We finished before the kettle’s last whistle!”

The kettle sang a soft whoooo. They cheered.

Gigi’s heart felt warm, like the toast. “Our routine helped,” she said. “It keeps us steady. It helps us reach our goal.”

Pip licked a crumb from his nose. “And when I made a mistake, I made amends,” he said proudly.

“You did,” said Gigi. “That is brave and kind.”

“Can we make a chart?” Bun asked. “A picture plan?”

“Yes,” said Gigi. She drew with a blue crayon on a big paper:
- A cup for drink
- A stretch stick figure
- A tidy broom
- Plates and spoons
- Toast and fruit
- A clean sparkle

She taped the chart near the window where the sun made it glow. “Leadership means helping us follow the plan,” Gigi said. “And listening when we need changes.”

Dot pointed. “Tiny steps lead to big goals!”

“Tomorrow,” Gigi said, “we will follow our plan again. Routine makes mornings gentle.”

They ate their toast. Crunch. They shared banana smiles. Sweet. The warm kitchen felt safe and happy. The clock ticked like a lullaby, steady and slow.

When they finished, Pip carried the plates to the sink with careful paws. Bun stacked napkins for tomorrow. Dot set the spoons in a little nest. Gigi looked around and felt proud.

“Good job, team,” Gigi said. “We used routine. We set a goal. We made amends. We showed leadership and teamwork.”

Bun, Dot, and Pip chimed, “We did it together!”

Gigi bent her long neck low and nuzzled each friend. “Tomorrow, we will do it again,” she whispered. “Step by step.”

And the warm kitchen, full of toast smells and sunshine, smiled with them.

The End.
