# Leo Listens in the Home Office

The home office was quiet and bright. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. The computer hummed softly, and papers smelled like new books. A brave little lion cub named Leo padded in on soft paws. Tap, tap, tap went his claws on the wooden floor.

Leo wrinkled his nose. “Mmm, I smell oranges,” he said. On the desk sat a bowl of fruit—orange slices, green pears, and crunchy carrots for a snack.

Mama sat in the tall chair, typing. “Click, clack,” went the keys. She smiled. “Hi, Leo. I’m working, but I can listen.”

Leo puffed his tiny chest. “I want to help!” he said. His tail swished with courage. “I can be the office helper.”

Just then, there came a happy chirp. “Peep!” Leo’s friend Pip the sparrow hopped onto the windowsill. Pip wore a tiny leaf hat. “I found a shiny paper!” Pip chirped, flapping. “It flew from the trash and danced in the wind.”

Leo looked at the recycling bin. A crinkly paper had fallen out and slid under the bookcase. He crouched low. “I will save it,” he said bravely. He reached a paw and pulled it out. “Got it!” The paper made a “shhh” sound. Leo dropped it into the blue bin. “Paper goes here,” he said. “We care for the Earth.”

Pip chirped, “Tweet! That is good for trees.”

From the doorway came soft footsteps. Tali the turtle, Leo’s new neighbor, peeked in, carrying a little lunch box. Tali’s shell was green and smooth like a leaf. “Hello,” Tali said in a quiet voice. “May I sit with you? My home is noisy today.”

Leo blinked. He had not seen a turtle in the office before. Tali moved slowly, slower than Leo liked. Leo’s whiskers twitched. He felt a funny squeeze in his tummy. He wanted to run and roar and go fast.

Mama whispered, “Remember to listen.”

Leo took a breath. He listened. He heard Tali’s soft steps. He heard Pip’s gentle peeps. He heard his own heart, thump, thump, slow down.

“Okay,” Leo said kindly. “You can sit with us.”

Tali smiled. “Thank you,” Tali said. He opened his lunch box. Inside were shiny cucumber slices, bright cherry tomatoes, and a small bread roll. “Would you like a tomato?” Tali asked.

Leo looked at the red tomato. He usually liked meat. He wasn’t sure. Pip hopped closer. “I like seeds!” the bird chirped.

Leo thought. He wanted to be brave and fair. “I will try one,” he said. He took a tiny bite. “Pop!” went the tomato in his mouth. It tasted sweet and juicy, and it made his whiskers wet. He grinned. “That is yummy!”

Pip pecked a seed. “Peep! Tasty!”

Tali said, “We all like different foods. That is okay.”

On the desk, two pencils lay side by side. One was long. One was short. “I want the long pencil,” Leo said, reaching out.

“I need a pencil, too,” Tali said softly, reaching for the same long one.

Both paws touched the same pencil. Leo felt a spark of grumpy. His tail swished fast. Pip tilted his head. “Tweet? What is fair?”

Leo remembered Mama’s words: “We listen. We share. We use gentle paws.” He looked at Tali’s eyes—round and hopeful. He looked at the short pencil. It still could write.

Leo asked, “How can we be fair?” His voice was calm now.

Tali said, “Maybe we can take turns.”

Pip chirped, “Yes! First, second!”

Leo nodded. “Okay. You use the long one first. I will try the short one.” He took the short pencil and pressed it to paper. Scratch, scratch. The sound was soft and nice. He drew a big sun with smiling rays. “This works!” he laughed.

Tali wrote his name, slow and neat. Pip flapped. “Justice means fair turns!” Pip sang.

Mama turned in her chair. “I heard kind voices,” she said. “I saw good listening.”

Leo’s cheeks felt warm and proud. He passed the bowl of fruit to his friends. “Orange slice?” he asked. “They are juicy and make us strong.” Pip took a tiny piece. Tali took a slice and smiled.

A breeze came through the window. It smelled like grass after rain. The curtains fluttered like butterfly wings. A tiny scrap of paper on the floor began to glide toward the door.

“Wait!” Leo said. He leaped and caught it gently with his paw. He placed it in the blue bin. “We keep our home office clean and our Earth happy.”

Tali clapped slowly. “You care for the Earth,” he said.

Pip chirped, “And you share food and pencils!”

Leo looked at his friends. He saw that Tali moved slowly, and Pip flapped quickly, and he himself liked to leap. All were different. All were good. “We are the same in one way,” Leo said. “We all want to be safe, to be heard, and to belong.”

Mama nodded. “That is acceptance,” she said. “We listen to others. We make fair choices. We try new healthy foods. We take care of our world.”

Leo felt warm and brave and calm. He curled his tail around his paws. “Today I learned to listen, to share, and to try new bites,” he said. “I learned to welcome friends who are not like me.”

Pip sang a happy note. Tali smiled big and slow. The home office felt bright and kind.

The brave little lion cub purred. “This is a good office,” he said. “This is a good pride.”

The End.
