# Benny Beaver’s Rooftop Routine

Benny was a busy beaver with a bright orange hard hat. He lived in a cozy house near a tall building. On top of the building was a flat, sunny rooftop. Benny loved the rooftop. It was his special place to work, play, and learn.

Every morning, Benny followed his routine. He sang, “Rise and shine, brush and dine!” He brushed his teeth—swish, swish—ate oatmeal with crunchy apples—munch, munch—and packed his tiny tool belt. Click-clack went his tools. Tap-tap went his paws as he climbed the stairs.

When he opened the rooftop door, warm sunshine kissed his fur, and a soft breeze smelled like fresh rain. “Good morning, rooftop!” Benny said. “Time for our routine!”

Benny’s routine had four friendly steps:
1. Sweep the leaves.
2. Water the garden.
3. Check the rooftop pond.
4. Build something small.

“Step one!” Benny sang. He held a little broom and swept crunchy leaves into neat piles. Scratch, scratch, swoosh! “Leaves go here,” he said, smiling.

“Step two!” He watered the garden. Sprinklers tick-ticked and tsss-ed. Benny smelled mint and sweet tomatoes. “Drink up, plants,” he giggled. “Glug, glug!”

“Step three!” Benny checked the rooftop pond. The water went plip-plop as he dipped a paw. “Clear and clean!” he said. “Fish friends, are you happy?” Tiny goldfish swirled with soft swishes.

“Step four!” Benny built a small bird perch. Tap-tap-tap went his wooden pegs. “Just right,” he said, eyes shining. “Routine done!”

One breezy day, Benny pushed open the rooftop door and found… a mess! The wind had whooshed big boxes across the roof. Tools were scattered. The garden hose was tangled like spaghetti.

“Oh no,” Benny whispered. “This is not our routine.” His paws felt wobbly. His heart went thump-thump.

Just then, three friends popped up the stairs. Miri the mouse squeaked, “We came to play!” Gus the gull flapped, “Let’s fly kites!” Tilly the turtle smiled slowly, “And I brought snacks.”

Benny took a deep breath. Sniff. He could smell strawberries from Tilly’s basket. He heard the wind whistle. He felt the rough broom handle in his paw.

“I want to play,” Benny said, “but my routine keeps the rooftop safe and happy. I sweep, water, check, and build. Today the wind made a big mess. I need help.”

Miri frowned. “But I want kite time now.” Gus squawked, “Me too!” Tilly blinked. “Maybe… we can do both?”

Benny thought. His whiskers twitched. “Maybe we can compromise,” he said. “We can do part of my routine together, fast, and then we can play. Teamwork makes it quicker!”

“What’s compromise?” Miri asked.

“It means we all give a little to get a little,” Benny said gently. “I’ll make my routine shorter today, and you’ll help me do it. Then we’ll have time for kites and snacks.”

Gus tilted his head. “Okay. What do we do?”

Benny’s voice grew steady. “Step one: Sweep the leaves. Miri, can you sweep the small corners? I’ll sweep the big parts. Gus, please pick up the light boxes with your wings. Tilly, can you hold the bag?”

“Teamwork!” they all said.

Scratch, swoosh, flap, plop! Leaves slid into the bag. Boxes stacked neatly. The wind hummed, but the friends worked on. Benny smiled. “Great job!”

“Step two: Water the garden,” Benny said. He lifted the hose—and it was still tangled. He tugged. Tug, tug. It knotted tighter. Benny’s eyes felt prickly. “Oh no.”

Tilly touched his paw. “We can be resilient,” she said softly. “That means we keep trying.”

Benny sniffed. “Okay. We can do it.” Miri held one end. Gus lifted a loop with his beak. Tilly nudged the knot with her shell. Benny turned the hose slowly. Twist, twist, lift… slip! The knot loosened. Water sprayed in a sparkly arc. Rainbow drops fell on their noses. They giggled.

“Glug, glug!” Benny said, watering the plants. “You did it,” Miri cheered.

“Step three: Check the pond.” Benny peered in. The water looked a bit cloudy. “Hmm.” He scooped leaves with a net—swish, swish—and Gus skimmed the top. Tilly hummed a slow song, and the goldfish wiggled close. “Clear now,” Benny said.

Benny looked at his friends. “We did three steps. That’s enough for today. For step four, I’ll build the bird perch tomorrow. That’s our compromise.”

“Play time!” Gus cried.

They flew kites that fluttered like butterfly wings—flap, flutter, fwoosh! The strings tickled their fingers. Tilly shared juicy strawberries. Miri’s giggle squeaked in the wind. Benny’s heart felt light and warm.

When the sun drifted lower, Benny put away the kites. He stacked tools. He hung the hose neatly. He patted the roof. “Thank you, rooftop. Thank you, routine.”

Miri smiled. “I liked helping. Routines make things smooth.”

Gus nodded. “Compromise was fair.”

Tilly added, “And we were resilient.”

Benny hugged his friends. “Teamwork made it fun. Tomorrow, we’ll do all four steps again. Today, we learned how to change a little and still care for our place.”

They climbed down the stairs, paws and feet going tap-tap, flap-flap, clip-clop. Benny whispered, “Rise and shine, brush and dine,” thinking of tomorrow. He felt proud, calm, and ready.

The lesson? Routines help us take care of our world. When things get messy, we can compromise, be resilient, and work as a team. Then we can play, too.

The End.
