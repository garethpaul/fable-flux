# Gigi’s Gentle Game Night

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with soft, spotted fur and long, sleepy eyelashes. One rainy evening, pitter-patter drops tapped the window of her cozy bedroom. The room smelled like clean blankets and warm cocoa. A lamp glowed like a little sun beside her bed, and a green plant swayed on the shelf.

Tonight was special. It was Family Game Night, a tradition Gigi’s grandma taught her. Every rainy night, they played games and shared stories about their family. Gigi loved this tradition. It made her heart feel warm.

Gigi laid a soft rug on the floor. She set two wooden hoops and a small beanbag in the center. “This is Hoop Toss,” she said to herself. “Grandma taught me. We play, we share a story, and we cheer for each throw.”

Tap-tap! Her friends arrived. Timo the tiny turtle peeked in, and Bella the bunny hopped behind him. “Hi, Gigi!” they chimed.

“Hi, friends!” Gigi smiled. “Come in. Cozy up. Tonight, I want to share my family’s game tradition.”

Timo sniffed the warm cocoa. “Mmm, it smells like chocolate clouds,” he said.

Bella wiggled her nose. “And your rug feels so soft! Like a mossy meadow!”

Gigi’s heart fluttered. “First, I share the tradition,” she said. “We take turns tossing the beanbag. We cheer for each other. After each turn, we share a little story.”

“A story?” asked Timo, blinking.

“Yes,” Gigi said. “It can be about family, or friends, or caring for our world. We also ask before we touch someone or their things. That’s a boundary. It means we respect each other.”

Bella nodded. “I like that.”

Gigi set clear rules, her voice gentle. “Rule one: We take turns. Rule two: We say ‘May I?’ before touching. Rule three: We cheer kind words. Rule four: We pick up after ourselves, so we care for our room and our planet.”

Timo raised his flipper. “May I toss first?”

“Yes, Timo,” Gigi said. She handed him the beanbag. “Thank you for asking.”

Timo tossed. The beanbag went plop between the hoops. “Hooray!” cheered Bella. “Nice try!” said Gigi.

Timo shared a story. “My grandma taught me to plant a tree each spring,” he said. “We water it gently. The birds sing there. It’s our tradition.”

Gigi clapped softly. “That is beautiful. Planting trees helps the Earth.”

Bella’s turn came next. She wiggled with joy. “May I toss?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Gigi. Bella threw the beanbag. It landed in a hoop with a soft thump. “Yay, Bella!” shouted Timo.

Bella smiled. “My family has a dance night. We hop and spin. We stop if someone needs space.”

Gigi nodded. “Good boundary setting. Thank you for sharing.”

Now it was Gigi’s turn. She kissed the beanbag and whispered, “For Grandma.” She tossed. The beanbag landed near a hoop. “Almost!” she laughed.

Gigi told her story. “On rainy nights, my grandma said, ‘We play, we share, we care.’ We cheer for tries, not just wins. We listen to each other. We clean up after, to keep our home nice and our Earth happy.”

“Your grandma sounds kind,” Timo said.

“She is,” Gigi replied, her eyes soft.

Just then, Bella reached for the plant on the shelf. “Ooh, pretty leaves!”

Gigi took a deep breath. Her neck felt warm. She wanted to keep her plant safe. “Bella,” she said gently, “please ask before touching my plant. I want to keep it healthy. It’s special to me.”

Bella’s ears drooped. “Oh. I’m sorry. May I touch one leaf?”

Gigi smiled. “Thank you for asking. Today, let’s just look. We can water it together later. That helps the plant and the Earth.”

Bella brightened. “Okay!”

They made teams for a new game: Gentle Goal. It was a sport with soft moves. They used pillows as goals. “We move slow and safe,” said Gigi. “We pass the beanbag, say ‘May I?’ and cheer.”

The room filled with giggles and soft thumps. “May I pass to you, Timo?” Bella asked.

“Yes!” he replied.

“Great pass!” cheered Gigi. “Nice teamwork!” The lamp glowed, the rain tap-danced on the glass, and the room felt happy and calm.

When they finished, Gigi held up a basket. “Clean-up time,” she sang. “This is part of our tradition too.” They picked up the beanbag, the hoops, and the pillows. Timo wiped a small cocoa drop with a cloth. Bella folded the rug’s corner straight.

“Everything looks tidy,” Timo said. “Earth and room both pleased!”

Gigi opened her Memory Book. It smelled like paper and sunshine. “Let’s write our stories,” she said. She drew a little tree for Timo and a dancing bunny for Bella. Next, she drew a giraffe tossing a beanbag. “Now our tradition is shared,” she whispered.

Bella hugged the air near Gigi, then paused. “May I hug you?”

Gigi’s smile was warm. “Yes,” she said, and they hugged.

“Gigi,” Timo asked, “can we share this tradition with our families too?”

“Yes,” Gigi said. “Traditions grow when we share them. We can keep the rules: we ask, we cheer, we clean, and we care for the Earth.”

The rain grew quiet. The cozy bedroom felt still and safe. Gigi looked at her friends. “Thank you for playing, for asking, and for caring,” she said.

“Thank you for sharing your tradition,” Bella replied.

Gigi’s heart felt full and light. “We learned to share a tradition, to play gentle sports, to set boundaries, and to care for our world,” she said. “That is our win.”

They smiled together. The night was soft and sweet.

The End.
