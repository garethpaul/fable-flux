# Gigi Listens on the Porch

Gigi the thoughtful giraffe had a long, gentle neck and kind brown eyes. She loved her peaceful front porch. In the morning, the porch smelled like warm toast and orange blossoms. The wood floor felt smooth under her hooves. Little wind chimes sang, ting-ting, in the soft breeze.

Gigi had a big basket by the door. Inside were porch things: a broom, a soft cloth, a tiny bell, story cards, and a little notebook with a pencil. Gigi liked to keep the basket neat. “When things have a place, I can find them,” she said.

One sunny day, her friends came to visit. Pip the puppy trotted up with happy paws. “Woof! Play time?” he barked.

Mina the mouse peeked from the porch rail. “Squeak! I brought tiny cookies.”

Tiko the turtle slowly climbed the step. “Hello,” he said softly. “I brought a shell song.” He tapped his shell, tik-tok, tik-tok.

Gigi smiled. “Welcome to my porch. Let’s listen and talk, one at a time. We will have a fun day.” She rang her tiny bell, ding! “The bell means we take turns.”

Pip wagged so fast his ears flopped. “I want to play ball!” He dropped a red ball that rolled across the porch.

Mina squeaked, “I want story cards!”

Tiko whispered, “I want the shell song.”

Three voices, all at once. The wind chimes tried to keep up, ting-ting-ting! Gigi’s ears twitched. Her heart felt full and a little busy.

Gigi took a deep breath. She could smell Mina’s cinnamon cookies. She could see Pip’s bright eyes. She could hear Tiko’s soft tapping. She touched the tiny bell. Ding.

“Friends,” Gigi said, “let’s listen. We will each share, and we will take turns. Listening shows respect. Listening helps us know what to do.”

Pip sat, tail thumping softly on the wood. Mina folded her little paws. Tiko tucked in, still as a smooth stone.

Gigi opened her notebook. “I will write our plan. First, we listen. Then, we do. This is organization.” She smiled and sounded out the word slowly so everyone could hear: “Or-ga-ni-za-tion.”

She looked at Pip. “Pip, what do you want?”

Pip said, “I want to play ball, please.” His voice was quieter now.

“Thank you for asking kindly,” said Gigi. She wrote: 1. Ball game.

She turned to Mina. “Mina?”

Mina squeaked, “I want story cards, please.”

Gigi wrote: 2. Story cards.

She looked at Tiko. “Tiko?”

Tiko smiled. “I want to share my shell song, please.”

Gigi wrote: 3. Shell song.

Gigi held up the notebook. “Here is our plan. We listened. We took turns. We used kind words. Now our day will be smooth, like this porch.”

The friends nodded. Gigi put the ball in a basket spot. She had a spot for balls, a spot for cards, and a spot for music. “Everything in its place,” she said. “This is organization.”

They started the ball game. Pip rolled the ball to Gigi. “Your turn!” he said. Gigi tapped it to Mina. “Your turn,” Gigi said. Mina giggled. Her tiny feet pitter-pattered as she nudged the ball to Tiko. Tiko pushed it with his careful foot. The ball went slow, roll, roll, roll. Everyone clapped.

After a while, Gigi rang the bell. Ding. “Ball game is done. Thank you for listening.” Pip wagged. He put the ball back in its spot. “I like the basket,” he said. “The ball knows where to sleep!”

Next, Gigi brought out the story cards. The cards smelled like paper and crayons. The pictures were bright: a sun, a tree, a kite, a nest. “We will tell a story by listening,” said Gigi. “One picture, one voice.”

Mina picked the sun. “Once there was a warm sun,” she squeaked.

Pip chose the kite. “The kite flew whoosh!” he barked.

Tiko chose the nest. “And the nest was safe,” he said.

Gigi chose the tree. “The tree gave shade on the porch,” she added. They looked at each other and smiled. They had made a sweet story by taking turns and listening.

Ding. “Story cards are done,” said Gigi. She stacked the cards and put them in their spot. The porch felt calm and tidy.

Finally, Tiko tapped his shell. Tik-tok, tik-tok. The sound was soft like rain on leaves. Pip closed his eyes. Mina curled her tail. Gigi’s long neck swayed slowly. They listened. They did not talk. They let the music fill the porch. The wind chimes joined, ting-ting, and the breeze carried the notes down the steps and into the garden.

When the song ended, Gigi whispered, “Thank you, Tiko.” She rang the bell very softly, ding. “Listening lets us hear small, special things.”

Pip opened his eyes. “I heard the tiny taps,” he said.

Mina sighed happily. “I felt calm.”

Tiko smiled. “I felt heard.”

Gigi looked at her friends. “Today we learned three things. One, listening shows respect. Two, taking turns is kind. Three, being organized helps us have fun.”

They all nodded. Pip put the bell back. Mina brushed crumbs into the bin. Tiko stacked the chairs. The porch looked neat again. It smelled like cookies and fresh air. The chimes sang a slow goodbye.

As the sun began to set, the porch was golden and warm. Gigi hugged her friends with her gentle neck. “Thank you for listening,” she said.

“Thank you for leading,” said Tiko.

“Thank you for the plan,” squeaked Mina.

“Thank you for play,” barked Pip.

Gigi smiled. “When we listen, we care. When we care, we share. When we’re organized, we all enjoy the day.”

They waved and went home. Gigi sat on her peaceful front porch and wrote in her notebook: We listened. We respected. We stayed organized. It was a very good day.

The End.
