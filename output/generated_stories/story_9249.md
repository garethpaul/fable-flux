# Milo the Brave in the Art Room

Milo was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright whiskers. He lived in a cozy corner of a big, sunny creative art room. The room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Paintbrushes stood like tiny trees in jars. Colorful paper sparkled, and glue sticks sat in a neat row.

Every morning, Milo peeked out from his tiny basket bed and whispered, “Good morning, art room.” He loved listening to the soft hum of the air, the click of scissors, and the happy squeak of markers on paper.

One day, a new project was set on the big table. It was the tallest paper tower Milo had ever seen! It was made of boxes, tubes, and shiny foil. A sign said, “Build Your Brave Castle.”

Milo’s heart did a little jump. “A brave castle?” he said softly. “I want to help! But the tower is so tall. What if I fall? What if the tape sticks to my whiskers?”

Milo took a tiny breath and put his paw on his chest. “I can try,” he said. “Courage is trying, even when I feel a little scared.”

He took a step onto a soft pile of felt. It felt fuzzy under his paws. “Hello, felt,” he giggled. He climbed onto a sponge brush. It was squishy and bouncy. Boing! Boing!

“Be brave, Milo,” he whispered. “One small step.”

As he climbed, he met a friendly helper. It was a blue pom-pom with googly eyes glued on. “Hi!” said the pom-pom in a wiggly voice. “I’m Poppy. Want to make the castle shine?”

“Yes, please,” said Milo. “But I’m a little nervous.”

“That’s okay,” said Poppy. “We can be brave together. Brave doesn’t mean big. Brave means try.”

Milo smiled. His whiskers wiggled. “Let’s do it!”

They found a roll of shiny foil. It crinkled and made a happy, crunchy sound. Crinkle, crinkle! “This can be the castle roof,” Milo said.

He pulled the foil. It ripped with a gentle rrrrip! “Oops,” said Milo. “I made a mistake.”

Poppy nodded. “Mistakes help us learn. Try again with small pieces.”

Milo tried again. Small piece. Press, press. Smooth, smooth. The foil roof glowed like a tiny moon. “We did it!” said Milo.

Next, they needed a brave flag. Milo found a red triangle of paper. He touched the glue stick. It felt cool and sticky. “It feels a little yucky,” he giggled. He pressed the triangle onto a straw. “Ta-da! A flag!”

But the castle still looked plain. “We need color,” said Milo. He dipped a brush into sunny yellow paint. Swish, swish! The brush tickled the paper. He made dots and stars. He tried sky-blue and grassy green. He smelled the sweet paint and heard the soft swish of bristles.

Then Milo saw the highest box. It was way up high, on top of a stack of paper tubes. His tummy felt fluttery, like tiny butterflies. “I want to put the flag up there,” he whispered. “But I feel scared.”

Poppy looked up. “Let’s think,” Poppy said. “What helps when we feel scared?”

Milo stood very still. He listened to his breath. In… and out… In… and out… The air felt cool in his nose and warm when he breathed out. “I can take slow breaths,” Milo said. “I can ask for help.”

“Yes!” said Poppy. “I can steady the tubes. You can climb slowly. We can talk the whole time.”

Milo nodded. “Okay. I will be brave.”

Poppy pressed the tubes so they didn’t wiggle. Milo took one step. Then another. He wrapped his tail around a straw for balance. “I am safe. I am trying,” he said. His paws felt the smooth paper. His ears heard the soft tap of his steps.

He reached the top. The room looked huge! He could see the paint jars, the crayon boxes, and his tiny basket bed in the corner. Milo held the flag. His paws shook a little. He breathed in, and out.

“I can do it,” he said. With a gentle push, he tucked the flag into a little hole in the box. The red triangle fluttered. Flap, flap!

“You did it!” cheered Poppy.

Milo’s face felt warm and happy. “I did it! I was brave!”

He climbed down carefully. When his paws touched the felt again, he gave Poppy a big hug. “Thank you for helping,” Milo said.

Poppy giggled. “We learned together. You showed courage. You used your imagination. You made a discovery.”

“What did I discover?” asked Milo.

“You discovered that brave can be small steps,” said Poppy. “You discovered that asking for help is smart. You discovered that feelings change when we breathe and try.”

Milo looked at the bright castle. The foil roof shone. The paint sparkled. The flag waved. He felt proud and calm.

He turned to the art room and said, “Today I learned: Courage means trying, even when I feel a little scared. I can use my imagination to find a way. I can discover new things when I keep going.”

Milo curled up in his basket bed as the room grew quiet. He smiled and whispered, “Good night, brave castle. Good night, brave me.”

The End.
