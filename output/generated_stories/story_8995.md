# Splash the Dolphin's Special Words

In a sunny backyard with a big blue pool, lived a playful dolphin named Splash. The warm sunshine made the water sparkle like tiny diamonds, and Splash loved to swim in happy circles.

"Click-click-whistle!" said Splash, making her special dolphin sounds. She was trying to talk to the children who came to visit, but they couldn't understand her clicks and whistles.

Little Emma sat by the pool edge, dangling her feet in the cool water. "I wish I could talk to you, Splash," she said softly.

Splash swam closer and made gentle clicking sounds. She wanted to be friends, but she felt sad that Emma couldn't understand her beautiful dolphin language.

Mrs. Garcia, who took care of Splash, came outside carrying a colorful book. "Emma, would you like to learn about how dolphins talk?" she asked with a warm smile.

"Yes, please!" Emma clapped her hands excitedly.

Mrs. Garcia sat beside Emma and opened the book. The pages showed pictures of dolphins making different sounds. "Dolphins have their own special language, just like we do," she explained. "When Splash clicks fast, she's excited. When she whistles softly, she's saying hello."

Splash listened carefully, floating peacefully in the water. She made a gentle whistle sound.

"She's saying hello to us!" Emma giggled, her eyes bright with wonder.

Mrs. Garcia nodded. "That's right! And just like we use different words for different feelings, Splash uses different sounds. But remember, Emma, we should always be gentle when we try to communicate with animals."

Emma reached toward the water, but Mrs. Garcia gently touched her hand. "It's important to respect Splash's space," she said kindly. "We can talk to her from here, but we shouldn't grab or splash unless she invites us to play."

Splash made a happy clicking sound and swam in a small circle near Emma. Then she gently splashed a tiny bit of water with her tail.

"I think she's inviting you to play!" Mrs. Garcia laughed.

Emma carefully cupped some water in her hands and let it trickle back into the pool. Splash clicked excitedly and did a little jump, her gray skin glistening in the sunshine.

"Look! She understands that I want to be her friend!" Emma said, feeling proud that she was learning Splash's language.

For the rest of the afternoon, Emma and Splash played together. Emma learned that loud sounds meant Splash was excited, soft whistles meant she was content, and when Splash stayed still, it meant she needed some quiet time.

"Every language is special and beautiful," Mrs. Garcia told Emma as they watched Splash swim gracefully through the sparkling water. "Whether it's the words we speak, the clicks dolphins make, or even the songs birds sing. When we take time to listen and learn, we can make wonderful friends."

Emma smiled and made a soft whistling sound, trying to copy Splash. The dolphin responded with a gentle click, and Emma felt her heart fill with joy.

As the sun began to set, painting the sky in pretty pink and orange colors, Emma waved goodbye to Splash. "Thank you for teaching me your language," she whispered.

Splash made one last happy whistle, as if to say, "Thank you for listening."

The End.
