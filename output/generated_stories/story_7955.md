# The Porch Craft and the Patient Bunny

Bree the rabbit sat on her peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like fresh wood and sun-warmed straw. The wind made the chimes go ting-ting-ting. Bree’s paws felt the smooth boards. The afternoon was soft and quiet, and the leaves whispered shhh.

“I want to make a kite,” Bree said, her whiskers twitching with a smile. “I want to make it today!”

She had string, paper, sticks, glue, and bright crayons. She lined everything up in a neat row. She took a slow breath in. She took a slow breath out. Bree liked to be calm when she crafted.

“Hello, little breeze,” Bree whispered to the wind. “Please be gentle.”

Bree began. Tap, tap went the sticks as she set them in a cross. “Stick, meet stick,” she said. “We will be friends.” She put glue on the ends. Squish, squish. The glue was cool and sticky. Bree waited. Her feet wanted to hop, hop, hop, but she stayed still. She hummed a soft song. “Wait for the glue. Wait for the glue,” she sang.

Her friend Wren, a tiny bird, landed on the porch rail. “What are you making?” chirped Wren.

“A kite,” said Bree. “I am a determined rabbit. I will do it!”

Wren tilted her head. “I like your song.”

“It helps me be patient,” Bree said. “When I wait, I feel calm inside.”

The glue dried. Bree put paper over the frame. She smoothed it with gentle paws. Rustle, rustle. She tied the string. She drew bright stars with her crayons. Yellow stars and blue swirls. “You look beautiful,” Bree told the kite.

Bree stood up and held the kite high. The breeze brushed her fur. She ran two small hops and let go. The kite wobbled. The kite bobbed. Then the kite fell—flop—right onto the porch.

“Oh,” said Bree. Her ears drooped. Her nose wriggled. “It did not fly.”

Wren chirped, “Maybe the tail is too short?”

Bree looked. The tail was small and silly. “I can fix it,” she said. She took a breath in and a breath out. “I can try again.”

She cut long strips of paper. Snip, snip, snip. She knotted them into a longer tail. Knot, knot. She felt proud. She felt strong. “I am still calm,” Bree told herself. “I can keep going.”

She tried again. She held the kite up. The breeze was a little stronger now. Bree ran three small hops and let go. The kite lifted. It rose a bit. Then it twisted, turned, and flopped down with a soft thud.

Bree sat on the porch step. Her paws felt warm from the sun. Her eyes felt wet. “I worked so hard,” she whispered. “Why didn’t it work?”

Wren hopped close. “You worked carefully. You waited. You tried again,” Wren said. “That is brave. That is patient.”

Bree sniffled. “Maybe I need help.” She looked at her tools. She looked at her kite. She looked at the clouds drifting slow and white.

Bree closed her eyes. She listened to the chimes: ting-ting-ting. She felt her breath go in and out. In, out. “Hello, feelings,” she said softly. “I see you. It is okay to be sad. I can be kind to me.”

When she opened her eyes, she saw the knot on the frame was loose. The cross had slid. “Oh!” said Bree. “The sticks need a tighter hug.”

She wrapped the center with extra string. Around and around, tug-tug, neat and snug. She put a tiny bit more glue. She waited, humming her waiting song. “Wait for the glue. Wait for the glue.”

Her neighbor, Tortoise, peeked around the fence. “Good afternoon, Bree,” he said slow and warm. “What are you making?”

“A kite,” Bree said, smiling a little. “It fell two times. I felt sad. I breathed. I looked again. Now I am fixing the middle.”

Tortoise nodded. “Sometimes slow is fast,” he said. “Care makes strong.”

Bree’s ears perked. “Care makes strong,” she repeated. She touched the glue to see if it was dry. It was. She patted the paper. Smooth. She checked the tail. Long. She checked the string. Tied well.

“Ready?” asked Wren.

“Ready,” said Bree.

Bree stood on her peaceful porch. The breeze kissed her cheeks. She took a long, calm breath. “I can try again.” She ran four small hops and let go.

The kite lifted. It fluttered. It climbed. Up, up, up it went! The tail danced. The string hummed. Bree’s eyes shone. Her heart felt light.

“It’s flying!” she laughed. “We did it!”

Wren circled in the air. “It’s beautiful!”

Tortoise clapped slowly with a smile. “Care makes strong,” he said again.

Bree held the string and watched the kite dip and sway. The sky was bright and blue. The porch smelled like sunshine. Bree felt peaceful. She felt proud.

She said to herself, “When I make, I go slow. I breathe. I look. I try again.” She looked at Wren and Tortoise. “Thank you for being with me.”

Wren chirped, “You were patient and brave.”

Bree nodded. “I accept that sometimes it is hard. I accept that I can learn. Crafting is practice. Crafting is fun.”

The kite danced. Bree hummed her calm song. She felt the string softly buzz in her paws. She smiled at the world. “I am a determined rabbit,” she said. “I can make things with care.”

And the porch chimes went ting-ting-ting, like a happy thank-you.

The End.
