# Gigi’s Tick-Tock Plan

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe. She lived in a playful living room with bright pillows, a soft blue rug, and a window that let in warm, golden light. The living room smelled like fresh oranges from a bowl on the table. Toys made tiny clack and click sounds when she moved them with her hoof.

“Good morning, living room,” Gigi whispered, stretching her long neck. “Today I will do my chores. Then I will play.”

She looked around. Blocks were in a colorful pile. Books lay open like little tents. A small basket waited by the shelf.

Gigi’s heart felt busy. “So many things,” she said softly. Her ears twitched. In the quiet, she heard a gentle tick-tock. It was the little clock on the wall. Tick. Tock. Tick. Tock.

“Hello, clock,” Gigi said. “You sound calm.”

Tick-tock, tick-tock, the clock went, steady as a soft drum.

Gigi took a deep breath. “I can be calm too,” she told herself. “First chores, then play. I can make a plan.”

She picked up the basket and smiled. “A plan will help me. A plan is like a gentle path.”

Just then, Mama’s voice floated from the kitchen. “Gigi, sweet giraffe, it’s tidy-up time. After chores, we can have orange slices.”

Gigi’s tummy rumbled. She loved juicy orange slices. “Yes, Mama!” she called. “I’m making a plan.”

She tapped the basket with her hoof. “Step one,” she said. “Blocks in the basket.” She scooped the blocks. Clack, clack, clack. The sound was cheerful.

“Step two,” she said. “Books on the shelf.” She closed the books with a soft flap. She stroked the smooth covers. “Thank you, books,” she whispered.

Tick-tock, tick-tock.

Gigi looked at her art crayons. Bright reds, sunny yellows, sky blues. “I want to color now,” she said. Her neck bent in a curious curve. “But wait. My plan says chores first.”

She felt a tiny tug inside, like a string pulling her toward the crayons. She pressed her hoof to her heart. “I can wait,” she told herself. “I am strong and smart. Waiting helps me feel proud later.”

She made her voice gentle. “Crayons, I will play with you soon.”

Gigi looked at the rug. Crumbs sparkled like tiny stars. “Step three,” she said. “Sweep the crumbs.” She used a little hand broom. Swish, swish. The crumbs made a soft sandy sound. Gigi giggled. “It’s like the ocean,” she said, swish, swish, swish.

Mama peeked in. The room smelled even fresher now. “I see your plan, Gigi,” Mama said. “I hear your broom. Swish, swish!”

Gigi smiled. “Tick-tock helps me,” she said, pointing at the clock.

Mama nodded. “The clock is a helper. We can use it together. How about a tiny timer game?”

Gigi’s eyes grew bright. “A game?”

Mama set a little timer beside the oranges. It made a soft beep when she pressed it. “Let’s give chores five minutes,” Mama said. “We will listen for the beep. Then we will cheer.”

Gigi stood tall. “I love this game,” she said. She moved with gentle speed. Clack went the blocks. Flap went the books. Swish went the broom. Tick-tock, tick-tock, sang the clock.

Beep!

Gigi froze, then laughed. “We did it!”

Mama clapped. “Teamwork and time make chores go fast.”

Gigi felt warm and proud. Her heart was light like a balloon. “My plan worked,” she said. “I can do hard things, step by step.”

Mama brought the orange bowl. The scent was sweet and bright. She peeled the skin with a peel-peel sound. The juice sparkled. “Here you go,” Mama said. “Crunchy, juicy slices for my thoughtful giraffe.”

Gigi took a bite. “Mmm!” The orange was cool and sweet. “Now it’s play time,” she said. “Can I color?”

Mama nodded. “Yes. You used your time well. Chores first, then fun.”

Gigi put her crayons on the tidy table. She chose sunny yellow. “Today I will draw a clock,” she said. “It helped me.”

As she colored, her friend Pip, a small stuffed panda, smiled from the couch. Gigi gave Pip a gentle pat. “We can play because we planned,” she told him.

She drew a happy clock with friendly eyes and little tick-tock lines. She added a basket of blocks, neat and proud. She drew a tiny broom making soft swishes.

When she was done, she held up the picture. “Mama, look!”

Mama’s eyes shone. “I see your plan in your art,” she said. “I see your patience and your care.”

Gigi’s spotty cheeks glowed. “Next time I feel busy,” she said, “I will listen to tick-tock. I will make a little plan. One, two, three.”

Tick-tock, tick-tock, the clock hummed kindly.

Gigi put the picture on the shelf. She hugged Mama’s leg. “Thank you for the timer game,” she said.

“Thank you for trying,” Mama replied. “You understood your feelings. You did your chores. You took care of your time.”

Gigi nodded. “I am a time helper,” she said. “I can do it again tomorrow.”

She picked up her crayons and began a new drawing, humming with the clock. Tick-tock, swish-swish, clack-clack, and happy little giggles filled the playful living room.

The End.
