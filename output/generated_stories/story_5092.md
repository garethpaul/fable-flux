# Ruby Robin's Big Mistake

Ruby Robin loved to sing on Mrs. Chen's peaceful front porch every morning. The wooden porch had pretty white railings and colorful flower pots that smelled like sunshine. Ruby would perch on the railing and sing her sweetest songs while Mrs. Chen swept the porch clean.

"Tweet-tweet-trill!" Ruby sang happily. "Good morning, Mrs. Chen!"

"Good morning, Ruby!" Mrs. Chen smiled, holding her broom. "Your songs make my morning chores so much nicer."

Ruby felt proud. She puffed out her little red chest and sang even louder. But then she noticed something interesting. Mrs. Chen had left a bowl of fresh blueberries on the porch table.

"Ooh, those look delicious!" Ruby thought. She flew down and started pecking at the juicy berries. Pop! Pop! Pop! The sweet juice burst in her beak.

Ruby was having so much fun eating that she didn't notice the mess she was making. Purple juice dripped everywhere! It splashed on the clean white table. It dropped onto the freshly swept porch floor. Some even splattered on Mrs. Chen's favorite yellow chair.

When Mrs. Chen came back outside, she gasped. "Oh my! What happened to my clean porch?"

Ruby looked around and saw the purple mess everywhere. Her tiny heart felt heavy. She had been thinking only about the yummy berries, not about what would happen if she made a mess.

"I need to think about this," Ruby said to herself. "What should I do?"

Ruby thought carefully. She remembered how hard Mrs. Chen worked to keep the porch clean. She thought about how kind Mrs. Chen was to let her sing there every day. Then Ruby realized what she needed to do.

"Mrs. Chen," Ruby chirped softly, flying down to perch near her. "I'm very sorry. I ate your berries and made a big mess. I wasn't thinking about what would happen."

Mrs. Chen's face softened. "Thank you for saying sorry, Ruby. But now we need to clean this up together."

"How can I help?" Ruby asked. "I want to fix my mistake."

Mrs. Chen thought for a moment. "You could help me think of the best way to clean this up. What do you think we need?"

Ruby looked at the purple stains and thought hard. "Water to wash away the juice? And maybe soap to make it extra clean?"

"That's very good thinking!" Mrs. Chen said. "And what about something to wipe with?"

"A cloth!" Ruby chirped excitedly. "I can help by bringing you small pieces of cloth in my beak!"

Together, they worked as a team. Mrs. Chen filled a bucket with warm, soapy water that smelled like lemons. Ruby flew back and forth, bringing small rags in her beak. Mrs. Chen scrubbed while Ruby pointed out spots they missed.

"There's a little purple drop by the flower pot!" Ruby called out helpfully.

Soon the porch was sparkling clean again. Mrs. Chen sat down in her yellow chair and smiled.

"Ruby, I'm proud of you for thinking about what to do and helping to fix your mistake," she said.

"I learned something important," Ruby said. "Before I do something, I should think about what might happen. And if I make a mistake, I should say sorry and help fix it."

"That's wonderful thinking, Ruby. And you know what? You can still have some berries – but next time, let's put them in a special bird feeder so you can eat without making a mess!"

Ruby sang her happiest song. She had learned that thinking before acting and taking responsibility made everything better.

The End.
