# Tilly Turtle on the Rooftop

Tilly was a shy turtle with a smooth, green shell. One warm morning, she found herself on a rooftop garden. The rooftop was bright and breezy. Pink flowers danced in pots. Little flags flapped-flapped in the wind. The sun felt cozy on Tilly’s shell.

Tilly blinked. “Oh,” she whispered. “How did I get up here?” She peered over the edge. The world below looked far away. Cars hummed like bees. A bird chirped, “Tweet-tweet!”

“I am small,” Tilly said softly. “And I am shy.” Her tummy felt wobbly, like jelly. She tucked her head into her shell for a moment. It felt safe in there. But when she peeped out, the rooftop was still the rooftop.

A kind pigeon landed near a pot of basil. “Coo-coo! Hello, little turtle,” said the pigeon. “Do you need help?”

Tilly took a deep breath. She remembered Mama Turtle’s words: “Try first. Trust yourself. You are stronger than you think.”

“Thank you,” Tilly said to the pigeon. “I am a little scared. But I will try to find my way.”

The pigeon nodded. “Coo! I will cheer for you.” He fluffed his feathers and stepped back.

Tilly looked around. She saw a wooden ramp by the door. It was not very steep, but it seemed long. The ramp had small bumps. “Bump, bump, bump,” she said, touching them with her feet. The wood felt warm. The breeze smelled like mint and soil. Tilly’s heart beat, thump-thump, but she stood tall.

“I can do this,” Tilly told herself. “Slow and steady. One step. Then another.”

She began to move. Tap. Slide. Tap. Slide. Her little claws clicked on the wood. The wind whooshed softly. The flags whispered shhh-shhh. Tilly slipped a tiny bit. “Oh!” she gasped. Her tummy wobbled again.

The pigeon called, “Coo! You can do it. Slow and steady!”

Tilly stopped. She took a breath in. And a breath out. “I will try again,” she said. “I can help myself. I can keep going.”

She pressed her feet down and moved one step. Then another. Tap. Slide. Tap. Slide. Her shell rubbed gently against the ramp. Scritch-scratch. She felt the bumps under her feet. They held her up. “Thank you, bumps,” she whispered with a tiny smile.

A little gray cloud drifted by. A cool shadow touched her shell. Tilly shivered. “I am okay,” she told herself. “I am brave in a quiet way.”

Halfway down the ramp, a gust of wind puffed her hat—oh yes, Tilly had a small yellow hat—right off her head! “Oh no!” she cried. The hat rolled, flip, flop, flip, toward the edge.

Tilly wanted to hide, but she did not. She watched the hat. She watched where it rolled. “I can solve this,” she said.

She paused. She thought. She saw a rake by the flower pots. The rake had a long handle. Tilly scooted to it, still on the ramp, careful and slow. Tap. Slide. Tap. Slide. She nudged the rake with her shell. Clink! The handle tipped toward the hat.

“Almost,” Tilly said, stretching her neck. She pushed again. Clink! The rake touched the hat and stopped it. The hat did not roll off the roof. Tilly giggled with relief. “I did it!”

The pigeon clapped his wings. “Coo-coo! Clever turtle!”

Tilly’s cheeks felt warm with shy pride. She inched along the ramp again. Tap. Slide. Tap. Slide. When she reached the bottom, she smiled so big her eyes crinkled. She picked up her hat and placed it on her head. It felt snug and right.

Near the door, Tilly saw a small sign. It had a picture of steps. “Hmm,” she said. “Steps can be tricky. But I can try.”

The steps were short and firm. Tilly tried one step. Thump. She pushed with her strong back legs. “I can do one more,” she told herself. Thump. Thump. Thump. Down she went, steady and calm, like a little drumbeat. The stairwell smelled like warm dust and lemons. The light was soft.

At the bottom of the stairs, a child held the door open. “Hi, Turtle,” the child said kindly. “Do you need help?”

Tilly smiled. “Thank you,” she said. “I worked it out. I found the ramp. I used the rake. I can get home now.” She paused and added, “Your kindness makes me brave.”

The child grinned. “You are strong,” they said.

Tilly walked toward the garden downstairs. The grass felt cool, like a soft hug. The sun kissed her shell once more. The pigeon swooped by. “Coo! Well done, Tilly!”

Tilly lifted her head high. “I was shy,” she said. “But I tried. I helped myself. I can do hard things, one small step at a time.”

She looked at the flowers. She listened to the bees. She smelled the sweet air. She felt calm and proud and brave.

That night, Tilly told Mama Turtle all about the rooftop. Mama smiled. “You showed self-reliance,” she said. “You trusted yourself. You kept going.”

Tilly snuggled into her cozy spot. “Slow and steady. Think and try,” she whispered. “I can do it.”

The End.
