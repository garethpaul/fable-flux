# Poco’s Colorful Traditions

Poco the parrot loved colors, sounds, and new ideas. He lived in a playful living room with soft pillows, a bouncy rug, and twinkly fairy lights that made tiny stars on the walls. The room smelled like warm cookies and orange peels. Sunlight slid across the floor like a golden ribbon.

“Good morning, room!” chirped Poco. “Today, I will make something special.”

Poco flapped to the art corner. There was a box of crayons, paper, and shiny stickers. He drew a big circle and colored it with bright red, cool blue, sunny yellow, and leaf green. He added dots like tiny raindrops. He added lines like dancing noodles.

“Ta-da!” said Poco. “This is my color circle.”

Just then, neighbors came to visit. Mia brought a soft drum. Abba brought a scarf with silver bells. Tita brought a sweet rice cake wrapped in a green leaf. The living room filled with happy hello hugs. The drum went “dum-dum,” the bells went “ting-ting,” and the rice cake smelled sweet and cozy.

“What are you making, Poco?” asked Mia.

“A new art,” said Poco. “I want to share it with everyone!”

Abba smiled. “Sharing is good. We can share our traditions too.”

“Tra-di-tions?” Poco tasted the long word. It felt like a friendly train rolling over his tongue. “What is that?”

Tita sat on a pillow. “Traditions are the special things families do again and again. They are stories we keep. We share them to remember and to care.”

Poco’s eyes sparkled. “Can we share now?”

“Yes,” said Mia. “We can each show something from our families. Then we can make something new together.”

Poco’s feathers shivered with happy energy. “I will listen and learn. I can grow my ideas!”

Mia tapped her drum softly, “dum-dum, dum-dum.” She whispered, “My grandma sings this beat when we make bread. We knead and sing. The beat helps our hands.” The drum felt warm and steady, like a heartbeat. Poco closed his eyes and felt the bread song in his chest.

Abba shook the bell scarf. “Ting-ting-ting!” He said, “We dance with this scarf at parties. We twirl and clap. It reminds us to celebrate together.” The bells sounded like tiny stars laughing.

Tita opened the rice cake. “This is from my family. We say thank you before we eat.” She held the leaf close. “It smells like home.” She taught Poco to say, “Thank you,” in her language. Poco tried, careful and slow. Everyone smiled.

Poco puffed his chest. “Now I share! I make art with many colors. I mix and I try. I do not give up. That is my tradition—keep trying!”

Mia nodded. “That is a growth mindset. When we try and learn, we grow.”

Poco flapped. “Let’s make a Sharing Circle! One circle with all our traditions.”

They spread paper on the floor. The rug felt bouncy under their knees. The fairy lights blinked like happy eyes. Poco drew a big circle again. “This part is for Mia’s bread beat,” he said, and he drew soft, round bumps, “dum-dum,” around the edge.

Mia hummed and tapped the drum while Poco colored. “Dum-dum, dum-dum.”

“This part is for Abba’s scarf dance,” said Poco. He drew twirly lines and stuck tiny silver star stickers. “Ting-ting!” laughed Abba, swirling the scarf so it whispered, “swish-swish.”

“This part is for Tita’s thank-you rice cake,” said Poco. He drew a little leaf and a cozy house. Tita taught him to write one kind word. Poco wrote it slowly with his beak. “Thank you,” he said again, proud and warm.

“And this bright corner,” Poco chirped, “is for my trying tradition.” He drew a small ladder with five steps. On each step he wrote: Try, Listen, Learn, Try Again, Share. He colored the steps rainbow bright.

Poco looked at the circle. “Oh!” He felt a tiny worry flutter. “What if I did it wrong?”

Mia touched his wing. “Traditions are about caring. There is not one ‘right’ look. You listened and learned.”

Abba added, “You asked. You tried. That shows respect.”

Tita smiled. “And you shared your heart. That makes it beautiful.”

Poco took a deep breath. He smelled oranges and sweet rice. He heard drum beats and bell chimes. He felt the soft rug under his toes. His worry flutter flew away.

“Let’s celebrate our Sharing Circle,” said Poco. “We can say thank you in many ways.”

They took turns. Mia tapped the bread beat. Abba danced with the bell scarf. Tita taught a gentle bow. Poco led a rainbow cheer, “Try, listen, learn, try again, share!”

Together they hung the Sharing Circle on the wall. The fairy lights made it glow. It looked like a warm sun made of many stories.

Poco puffed his feathers. “Today I learned that traditions are special ways to remember and care. When we share them, we understand each other. When we try and learn, we grow.”

Everyone held hands. “Thank you,” they said in many voices, soft and bright.

Poco smiled. “Tomorrow, we can learn more. We can keep our traditions shining, and we can make new ones together.”

The room felt extra cozy. The stars on the wall seemed to wink. The drum hummed, the bells chimed, and the sweet rice taste lingered like a hug.

The End.
