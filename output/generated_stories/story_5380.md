# Quiet Paw, Calm Mind

Sunny the squirrel loved her cozy study corner. It was under a bright window with warm sunlight. A soft blue rug hugged the floor. Crayons rested in a cup. Paper lay in a neat stack. A small clock tick-tick-ticked. It smelled like fresh paper and pine cones.

Sunny swished her fluffy tail. “Today I will make a picture chart,” she said. “It will help me remember my acorn facts!”

She opened her crayon cup. “Brown for acorns, green for leaves, yellow for sunshine,” she hummed. She drew a circle. She drew a leaf. She drew a tiny sun. Her paws moved quick-quick. Her heart felt excited.

Tap-tap. A small beak pecked the window. It was Pip the sparrow. “Hi, Sunny!” chirped Pip. “Want to play hop-leaf?”

Sunny looked at her chart. “I want to, Pip, but I am working. I will play later.”

Pip nodded. “Okay!” He flapped away.

Sunny tried to draw more, but her mind felt jumpy. The clock went tick-tick-tick. Outside, a mower went vroom. Her tummy made a little grr. Her paws felt wiggly.

“I can’t think,” she said, frowning. She pressed her crayon too hard. Snap! The crayon tip broke. “Oh no!”

She picked another crayon. Her tail bumped the cup. Clatter! Crayons rolled across the rug. One rolled under the chair. One slid under the shelf.

Sunny’s eyes got wet. “Now my chart is a mess,” she sniffed. Her chest felt tight. Her ears felt hot.

Just then, Nettle the hedgehog peeked in. “Sunny? I brought you a pine-nut muffin,” he said softly. “It smells warm!”

Sunny stomped a tiny paw. “I don’t want muffins! I want quiet. I want my chart to be perfect!”

Nettle blinked. “Oh. I’m sorry, Sunny. I didn’t mean to make it harder.”

Sunny saw Nettle’s little face droop. Her own tummy wiggled again. She took a breath in, quick. It felt tight, like a knot.

Sunny remembered a rhyme her grandma taught her. “Quiet paw, calm mind.”

She looked at her paws. “I can try,” she whispered.

Sunny sat on the blue rug. She placed one paw on her heart and one paw on her belly. “In and out,” she said.

She breathed in through her nose. Sniff… She breathed out through her mouth. Whooo…

“Quiet paw,” she whispered as she breathed in.
“Calm mind,” she whispered as she breathed out.

Tick-tick-tick went the clock.
Whooo went Sunny’s breath.
Warm muffin smell floated up, sweet and nutty.

Her chest felt softer. Her ears cooled down. Her thoughts slowed like leaves floating in a pond.

Nettle sat beside her. He breathed too. “Quiet paw,” he whispered with her.
“Calm mind,” they breathed together.

After three slow breaths, Sunny opened her eyes. “I felt angry,” she said. “I felt tight and shouty.”

Nettle nodded. “That happens. I forgive you,” he said gently. “Do you forgive yourself?”

Sunny blinked. “Yes,” she said. “I forgive me. Big feelings come and go.”

She looked at the crayons on the floor. “I can fix this,” she said. “I can be careful.”

Nettle smiled. “I can help.”

“Thank you,” said Sunny. “I accept help.” She smiled back. “And I accept that my chart does not need to be perfect. It can be my chart.”

Together they picked up the crayons. Click, click, click into the cup. Sunny wiped tiny crumbs of paper from the rug. She reached under the chair and found the brown crayon. “Hello, friend,” she giggled.

Sunny took a small bite of warm muffin. Sweet, toasty, crumbly. “Mmm,” she said. Her tummy said, “Happy!”

She set the muffin on a napkin. “Now I will draw slowly,” she said. “I will notice my breath, my paws, and my picture.”

She drew a smooth acorn. She drew a gentle leaf. She drew a bright sun with soft rays. When her mind started to jump, she whispered, “Quiet paw, calm mind,” and breathed.

Tap-tap. Pip peeked in again. “How is your chart, Sunny?”

Sunny looked at her picture. It wasn’t perfect. The sun was a bit wiggly. The leaf had a bumpy line. But it was hers. It was made with calm breaths and kind thoughts.

“It is done,” she said. “I like it.”

Pip chirped. “It looks lovely!”

Sunny turned to Nettle and Pip. “I am sorry I shouted,” she said. “I was overwhelmed.”

“We accept your sorry,” said Nettle.
“We forgive you,” chirped Pip.

Sunny nodded. “Thank you. I forgive me too.”

She rolled up the chart. She took one more slow breath. Sniff… Whooo…

“Now,” Sunny giggled, “let’s play hop-leaf. And later, we can have more muffin.”

Pip flapped. Nettle clapped his tiny paws. Sunny felt light and cozy in her study corner. The clock went tick-tick-tick, but her heart went calm-calm-calm.

Lesson: When big feelings come, we can stop, breathe, forgive ourselves and others, and accept help. Quiet paw, calm mind.

The End.
