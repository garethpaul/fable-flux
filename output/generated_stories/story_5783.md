# Beep’s Brave Nursery Day

In a comfortable nursery with sunny windows and soft rugs, a helpful robot dog named Beep wagged his shiny tail. His tail made a happy whirr-whirr sound. The air smelled like clean blankets and warm oatmeal. Soft music hummed like a friendly bee.

“Good morning, friends!” Beep beeped kindly. His eyes glowed a gentle blue. “I am here to help.”

The children clapped. Mia hugged her plush bear. Sam stacked colorful blocks. Jo rolled a red ball back and forth. The teacher, Ms. Lani, smiled and set a bowl of fruit on the table. The bright bananas and juicy blueberries looked yummy.

“We will have a snack soon,” said Ms. Lani. “Then we will tidy, share, and play.”

Beep’s ears clicked open. “I can help,” he said. “Helping is my favorite!”

A tiny voice came from the reading corner. “Um… I feel wobbly inside,” whispered Eli. He sat on a beanbag shaped like a cloud. His hands were tucked under his chin.

Beep padded over with quiet tinks on the floor. “What do you need, Eli?” he asked softly. His warm fan made a gentle hush-hush sound.

“I want to try the tall slide,” Eli said. His eyes were shiny with worry. “But it looks so big. My tummy feels twisty.”

Beep sat beside him. The beanbag made a soft poof. “Being brave means trying, even when you feel twisty,” said Beep. “We can go slow. We can use words. We can breathe. I will be with you.”

Eli nodded a little. “Okay.”

Just then, a squeaky squabble popped up by the fruit bowl. Mia held the biggest banana. Sam held the biggest orange. Their eyebrows were wrinkly.

“I had it first,” Mia said, hugging the banana close.

“No, I had it first!” said Sam, frowning at the orange.

Beep stood between them and sat down. His paws made a click-click on the floor. “Let’s use peaceful words,” he said. “Let’s be fair.”

He looked at both children with calm blue eyes. “Please tell me what you want.”

Mia took a breath. “I want the big banana because I am very, very hungry.”

Sam nodded. “I want the big orange because I am very, very thirsty.”

Beep thought for a moment. “I hear you both,” he said. “We can be fair. We can share and take turns.”

Ms. Lani came over. “Great idea, Beep. Let’s make equal pieces.” She peeled the banana and carefully cut it into two halves. She peeled the orange and made two little mountains of slices. The sweet citrus smell filled the room.

Mia and Sam looked at the plates. Mia said, “We both get banana and orange.”

Sam smiled. “We both win.”

Beep wagged. “That is fairness,” he said. “That is peaceful talking.”

The friends ate slowly. “Mmm,” said Mia. “The banana is soft.”

“Zing!” said Sam. “The orange is juicy!”

Beep crunched one blueberry with a little buzz. “Healthy food gives brave energy,” he said. “Fruits help our bodies and our brains.”

After snack, Ms. Lani said, “Play time!”

The tall slide waited in the sunny play area. The steps were smooth and neat. Eli looked at the steps. His tummy made a little flutter. “Beep,” he whispered, “I still feel twisty.”

Beep nodded. “Let’s be brave together. We will go step by step.”

They stood at the bottom. “Step one,” said Beep in a sing-song voice. “Breathe in… and out.” They breathed together. In, out. In, out. Eli’s shoulders softened.

“Step two,” said Beep. “Use brave words.”

Eli whispered to himself, “I can try. I can go slow.”

“Step three,” said Beep. “Ask for help.”

Eli reached for Beep’s paw. It was smooth and warm. “Please help me,” Eli said.

“I am here,” said Beep.

They took one step. Then another. The steps felt cool under Eli’s shoes. The sunshine tickled his cheeks. He could smell crayons from the art shelf and clean soap from the sink. Children laughed below like little bells.

Halfway up, Mia and Sam came over. “You can do it, Eli!” Mia called. “We can wait our turn,” Sam added. “We will cheer for you.”

Eli’s face brightened. “Thank you,” he said.

They reached the top. Eli’s knees wiggled. Beep sat close, steady as a chair. “You are safe,” Beep said. “You are brave.”

Eli took a deep breath. He scooted forward. Whoosh! Down he went. The slide felt smooth and fast, like a friendly wind. His heart said thump-thump in a happy way. He bumped softly onto the carpet at the bottom and giggled. “I did it!”

Mia clapped. Sam cheered. Beep slid down next, making a silly tinkle-tinkle sound. “Beep!” he said proudly.

Eli stood tall. “I was brave,” he said. “I felt twisty, but I tried.”

Beep nodded. “Bravery is trying even when you feel scared. Fairness is sharing and taking turns. Peaceful words help everyone feel safe. Healthy food gives us strength. Compassion is caring for each other.”

Ms. Lani smiled. “Wonderful teamwork,” she said. “I am proud of all of you.”

As the day ended, the nursery smelled like warm tea and fresh paper. The sky outside turned soft pink. Beep tucked blankets, collected blocks, and hummed a gentle tune. Eli handed Beep a sticker shaped like a star.

“For you,” Eli said. “Thank you for helping me be brave.”

Beep’s eyes glowed bright. “The bravest star is inside you,” he said.

Eli grinned. “Tomorrow I will try the slide again. Maybe even the twisty tunnel!”

“We will be brave together,” said Beep.

The End.
