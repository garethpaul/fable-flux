# Milla Mouse Finds Her Voice

Milla was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She lived in a busy basement under a big, bustling house. The basement hummed and rattled all day. The washing machine swished. The dryer thumped. Pipes tick-tick-ticked. Boxes stacked high made little walls and tunnels for Milla to explore.

Milla loved music. She had a tiny tin lid that sounded like a bell. She had a wooden spoon that made a gentle tap-tap on an old bucket. She had a ribbon she would wave as she hummed. Her favorite time was when she made little songs.

One afternoon, the basement got even busier. The family upstairs had guests. Feet pitter-pattered. Laughter drifted down. The dryer rumbled warm air that smelled like clean cotton.

Milla smiled. “Today is a good music day,” she said. She set her tin lid on a crate, picked up her spoon, and tapped a sweet rhythm. Tap… tap-tap… tap.

“Hey!” squeaked Benny Beetle, crawling out from under a shoe box. “I like that beat!” He clicked his tiny legs. Click-click! “Can I join?”

“Yes!” said Milla. “Let’s make music kindly.”

Soon, Suri Spider dangled down on a silk thread. “Is this a band?” she asked in a whisper. “I can shake my web. It makes a soft shh-shh.”

“Perfect,” said Milla. “We all make different sounds.”

Then Big Gus the Cricket hopped in, green and grand. “I can chirp loud!” he said. “Listen!” Chirp-CHIRP! Chirp-CHIRP!

The sound filled the basement. The dryer thumped. The pipes ticked. The lid rang. The web shushed. And Gus chirped so loudly that Milla could feel it in her whiskers.

Milla tried to hum her little melody, but no one could hear it. Her voice felt small. Her heart felt small too.

“Um,” she said softly. “Can we play a little softer? I can’t hear the melody.”

Gus chirped louder. “What? Louder is fun!”

Benny clicked even faster. Suri shushed and shook. The music turned into noise. Milla’s ears drooped. Her tummy made a tiny twisty knot.

Milla sat on her spool-stool. She held her spoon but didn’t tap. She wanted to be kind. She didn’t want to hurt feelings. But she also wanted to share her song. The basement smelled like warm soap and dusty wood. The air felt thick with sound.

She took a slow breath in and let it out, just like Mama Mouse had taught her. In… and out.

Milla put a paw on her chest. Her heart went thump-thump, steady like a drum.

“I can be kind and strong,” she whispered to herself. “I can use my clear voice.”

She stood up, tail curled for balance. She tapped the bucket three times, louder than before. TAP! TAP! TAP!

Everyone paused.

Milla looked at her friends. “Friends,” she said in a clear, calm voice, “I like playing with you. I like when we all make music. But I feel sad because I can’t hear my melody. I need us to take turns and listen. I want to be heard too.”

Suri blinked her bright eyes. “Oh,” she said, “I didn’t know you felt sad.”

Benny lowered his clicks. “I want to hear your song, Milla.”

Big Gus rubbed his wings. “I like loud,” he said. “Loud is my way.”

Milla nodded kindly. “Loud is great sometimes. Your chirp is special. But we can make room for my soft sounds too. Can we try a plan?”

“What plan?” asked Gus.

“We can make music in parts,” Milla said. “First soft, then loud, then soft again. We can take turns. We can make a pattern: soft-soft-loud!” She smiled. “We can also use signals. Three taps means ‘quiet time.’ One ring means ‘Gus time!’ Two shakes means ‘Suri time.’”

Benny’s antennae wiggled. “I love patterns!”

Suri spun a tiny circle. “Signals help me feel safe.”

Gus thought. He looked at Milla’s hopeful face. “Okay,” he said. “I can be loud and also take turns.”

Milla’s whiskers lifted. “Thank you.”

They tried the plan.

Milla tapped three times. TAP! TAP! TAP! Everyone played softly. The tin lid rang like a little bell. The web went shh-shh like wind. Benny’s clicks were like rain. Milla hummed a sweet, gentle tune. The basement felt calm. The smell of warm cotton made her think of soft blankets.

Then Milla rang the lid once. Ding! “Gus time!”

Gus chirped bright and proud. Chirp-CHIRP! Chirp-CHIRP! It filled the room like sunshine. Milla swayed her ribbon. Benny clicked in a strong line. Suri made sparkly shh-shh sounds around the edges.

Two web shakes. Swish-swish. “Suri time!” The music softened again. Everyone listened. The pipes ticked like little metronomes. The song grew and changed, together.

They played soft-soft-loud, again and again. They smiled. They laughed. Their music felt fair and friendly.

When they finished, the basement seemed to glow. Even the dryer gave a happy thump-thump, like applause.

Gus hopped to Milla. “Thanks for telling us how you felt,” he said. “Your melody is beautiful.”

“Thanks for listening,” Milla said. “Your loud part makes the song exciting.”

Benny clicked a tiny rhythm. “We made room for everyone!”

Suri made a little heart shape with her silk. “We celebrated our differences.”

Milla’s chest felt warm. The twisty knot was gone. She had been kind and brave. She had used her voice.

Milla turned to her friends. “What did we learn today?” she asked.

Gus chirped softly. “We can be loud sometimes and quiet sometimes.”

Benny said, “We can take turns.”

Suri added, “We can speak up with kind words.”

Milla smiled. “Yes. Being assertive means saying what you need in a clear, calm way. It helps everyone play and feel good.”

They packed up their instruments. The basement hummed. Their hearts hummed too.

The End.
