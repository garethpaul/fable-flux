# Bluebird's Brave Writing Day

The sun was warm, and the grass was tickly. In a sunny backyard, a tiny bluebird sat on the fence. The bluebird loved to sing. He sang, “Tweet-a-tee, tweet-a-toot!” The sound was bright and happy.

But today, Bluebird had a new idea. “I want to write my own song,” said Bluebird. “Not just sing it. I want to write it down!”

Bluebird’s tummy felt wiggly. Writing felt big. Writing felt new.

“Can I do it?” he whispered to the breeze.

A soft wind touched his feathers. The leaves went rustle-rustle. The flowers smelled sweet. The backyard felt safe. Bluebird took a brave breath. “I can try,” he said.

Bluebird hopped to a small table by the garden. On the table was a tiny notebook and a twig for a pencil. The notebook smelled like paper and sunshine.

“Hello, notebook,” said Bluebird. “I am Bluebird, and I want to write my song.”

He opened the notebook. The pages were white and clean. Bluebird looked and looked. The blank page felt like a big, wide sky.

“My song starts with a feeling,” he said. “It feels sunny!”

Bluebird tapped his twig. Tap, tap. “First, I will plan,” he said bravely. “I will organize.” He drew three little boxes on the page.

“Box one is for beginning. Box two is for middle. Box three is for end.” Bluebird smiled. “I can put my ideas in the boxes. That will help me.”

A ladybug crawled by. “What are you doing?” asked Ladybug.

“I am writing my song,” said Bluebird. “I am making a plan.”

“That is smart,” said Ladybug. “Plans help small steps feel easy.”

Bluebird nodded. He closed his eyes. He listened. He heard bees buzz-buzz by the flowers. He heard the sunshine in the leaves—soft, warm, hush. He heard his own heart, thump-thump, brave and steady.

He whispered, “Beginning: I wake up in the sun.”

He wrote in box one: “Wake up. Warm sun. Happy heart.”

“Middle,” said Bluebird. “What comes next?” He listened again. The sprinkler went tick-tick-swish, tick-tick-swish. A puppy barked, “Woof!” The air smelled like wet grass and sweet berries.

He wrote in box two: “Sprinkler song. Bee buzz. Woof! I feel a little scared to sing loud.”

Bluebird’s wings trembled. He remembered why. “Sometimes, I feel shy,” he said softly. “What if no one likes my song?”

Ladybug patted his wing. “It is okay to feel shy. Courage is doing the new thing even when you feel shy.”

Bluebird took another brave breath. “I will keep writing.”

He looked at box three. “End: I sing anyway,” he said. “I sing my own words. I feel proud.”

He wrote in box three: “I sing. Friends listen. I smile.”

Bluebird looked at his plan. “It is organized!” he chirped. “Now I can write my song words.”

He turned the page and began.

He wrote, slowly and neatly:

“Good morning, sun, you warm my wings.
Tick-tick-swish, the sprinkler sings.
Buzz, little bee, I hear your tune.
I find my voice this afternoon.”

He paused. “Hmm.” He listened to his tummy. It felt fluttery. He added:

“My heart says yes, my knees say shake.
I sing out loud for courage’s sake.”

He hummed the words. “Tweet-a-tee… tweet-a-true…” The song felt like gold in his chest.

A squirrel popped up on the fence. “What is that sound?”

“My new song,” said Bluebird. “I wrote it down.”

The squirrel tilted his head. “Will you sing it for us?”

Bluebird looked around. Ladybug watched with tiny eyes. A butterfly fluttered near the marigolds. The puppy sat with a wag-wag tail. Bluebird felt shy again. His twig trembled.

“I am scared,” he whispered. “But I made a plan. I wrote my words. I can be brave.”

He stood tall. He touched his notebook with his wing. “My plan will guide me,” he said. He took a deep breath that smelled like grass and sunshine.

Bluebird sang his new song. His voice was soft at first. Then it grew warm and bright.

“Good morning, sun, you warm my wings.
Tick-tick-swish, the sprinkler sings.
Buzz, little bee, I hear your tune.
I find my voice this afternoon.
My heart says yes, my knees say shake.
I sing out loud for courage’s sake.”

When he finished, the backyard was quiet for one small moment. Then, “Clap-clap!” The puppy pat-patted the ground with his paws. Ladybug cheered, “Hooray!” The butterfly twirled in the air. The squirrel chittered happily.

Bluebird’s cheeks felt hot with pride. “I did it,” he said. “Writing helped me sing my own song.”

Ladybug smiled. “Your plan made strong steps. Your words were clear. Your courage was big.”

Bluebird put his notebook back on the table. He drew a little heart at the end of the page. He whispered, “Thank you, plan. Thank you, brave breath.”

Then he turned to his friends. “Do you want to write too?” he asked. “We can make boxes for ideas. We can use our senses. We can be brave together.”

“Yes!” said Squirrel. “I will write about nuts!”

“I will write about flowers,” said Butterfly.

“Woof!” said Puppy. “I will write about bones.”

Bluebird giggled. “Let’s organize our ideas. Beginning, middle, end.”

The sun shone brighter. The breeze went hush. The backyard felt full of new stories.

Bluebird sang softly as they wrote, “Write it down, line by line. Organize and you will shine. Be brave, be kind, use your mind.”

And that day, in the sunny backyard, Bluebird learned an important lesson: when you make a plan, use your senses, and take a brave breath, you can write your own true song.

The End.
