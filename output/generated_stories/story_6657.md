# Pip's Puzzle Party Plan

Pip was a tiny gray mouse with bright black eyes and whiskers that twitched when she was excited. She lived in the cozy guest room of a big house, where soft sunlight streamed through lace curtains.

"What a wonderful day!" Pip squeaked, stretching her little paws.

Today was special. Three of her friends were coming over to play with her new puzzle. It had colorful pieces that showed a beautiful garden with flowers and butterflies.

*Tap, tap, tap.* First came Rosie the red ladybug, her shiny shell gleaming.

"Hi Pip! I can't wait to see your puzzle!" Rosie buzzed.

*Thump, thump.* Next came Oliver the green frog, hopping across the soft carpet.

"This is going to be so much fun!" Oliver croaked happily.

*Flutter, flutter.* Last came Luna the yellow butterfly, landing gently on the windowsill.

"I love puzzles!" Luna sang in her sweet voice.

But when Pip opened the puzzle box, she noticed a problem. There were many, many pieces scattered on the fluffy rug.

"Oh dear," Pip said, her whiskers drooping. "There are so many pieces! How will we all work together?"

Rosie immediately grabbed a handful of pieces. "I'll do the flowers!"

Oliver hopped over and scooped up more pieces. "I want the pond parts!"

Luna fluttered down and tried to take pieces too. "Save some for me!"

Soon everyone was grabbing pieces, and the puzzle pieces were getting mixed up and scattered everywhere.

"Stop!" squeaked Pip. "This isn't working. We're making a mess!"

Everyone paused and looked around. Puzzle pieces were everywhere - under the bed, by the dresser, even stuck to Oliver's sticky frog feet.

Pip's whiskers twitched as she thought carefully. "I have an idea! What if we use strategy?"

"What's strategy?" asked Rosie, tilting her tiny head.

"Strategy means making a smart plan," Pip explained. "First, let's gather ALL the pieces together in one pile."

Working together, they collected every single piece and made a neat pile on the soft carpet.

"Now," said Pip, "let's take turns choosing pieces. Rosie, you pick first. Then Oliver, then Luna, then me."

"That sounds fair!" said Oliver, clapping his webbed hands.

They sat in a circle around the puzzle pieces. The afternoon sun made everything warm and golden.

Rosie picked a red flower piece. Oliver chose a blue water piece. Luna selected a piece with butterfly wings. Pip found a green leaf piece.

"This is much better!" laughed Luna. "We each get a turn!"

As they took turns, something magical happened. They started helping each other too.

"Rosie, I found another flower piece that matches yours!" called Oliver.

"Luna, this piece has butterfly colors - it might fit with yours!" said Pip generously.

Soon the beautiful garden puzzle was coming together. They could see flowers blooming, butterflies dancing, and a sparkling pond.

"Look what we made together!" squeaked Pip proudly.

The puzzle was complete, and it was even more beautiful because they had all worked on it together.

"Thank you for teaching us about strategy, Pip," said her friends. "Taking turns and sharing made everything more fun!"

Pip's whiskers twitched happily. "When we use strategy and take turns, everyone gets to be included. That's what makes the best adventures!"

They spent the rest of the afternoon admiring their beautiful puzzle, each friend proud of the part they had contributed.

The End.
