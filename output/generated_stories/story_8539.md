# Melody's Creative Song

In a cozy home office with tall windows and soft sunlight, lived a little yellow bird named Melody. She had bright orange feet and the most beautiful voice in the whole neighborhood. Every morning, Melody would perch on the windowsill and sing her heart out.

"Tweet-tweet-tweet!" Melody sang happily. "Good morning, world!"

But today was different. Today, Melody felt frustrated and angry. She had been trying all week to create a special new song for the spring concert, but nothing sounded right.

"This is too hard!" Melody chirped loudly, fluffing up her feathers. "I can't think of anything good!"

She tried singing high notes: "La-la-la!" Too squeaky.

She tried singing low notes: "Do-do-do!" Too grumbly.

She tried singing fast: "Tweet-tweet-tweet-tweet!" Too rushed.

Melody felt her little heart beating fast with anger. Her wings trembled, and she wanted to give up completely.

Just then, she heard a gentle voice from below. "Hello up there, little bird. You sound upset."

Melody looked down and saw Mrs. Chen, who worked at the big wooden desk covered with colorful papers and pencils. Mrs. Chen had kind eyes behind her glasses and always spoke softly.

"I am upset!" Melody chirped. "I need to make a new song, but I can't think of anything creative. It's making me so mad!"

Mrs. Chen smiled warmly. "You know, when I feel frustrated with my work, I take three deep breaths and try something different. Would you like to try that with me?"

Melody tilted her little head. "Okay," she peeped quietly.

"Breathe in slowly," Mrs. Chen said, taking a big breath. "One... two... three... and breathe out. One... two... three."

Melody breathed with her, feeling her tiny chest rise and fall. The angry feeling started to melt away like ice cream in the sun.

"Now," Mrs. Chen said kindly, "instead of trying to force a song, why don't you listen to the sounds around you? Sometimes creativity comes from the world around us."

Melody listened carefully. She heard the gentle hum of the computer, the soft whisper of papers rustling, the tick-tock of the clock, and the whoosh of wind through the trees outside.

"I hear music everywhere!" Melody chirped excitedly.

"That's right!" Mrs. Chen clapped her hands. "Now, what if you made those sounds part of your song?"

Melody's eyes sparkled with new ideas. She began to sing, copying the rhythm of the clock: "Tick-tock, tick-tock, spring is here!"

Then she added the whoosh of the wind: "Whoosh-whoosh, flowers bloom!"

Finally, she included the gentle hum: "Hum-hum-hum, happy day!"

"Tweet-tick-whoosh-hum, spring is here to stay!" Melody sang her complete new song, dancing on her tiny feet.

Mrs. Chen smiled widely. "That's the most creative and beautiful song I've ever heard!"

Melody felt proud and happy. "Thank you for helping me stay calm and think differently. Working together made everything better!"

From that day on, whenever Melody felt frustrated, she remembered to breathe deeply, stay calm, and listen to the world around her. She learned that creativity comes not from forcing ideas, but from being kind to herself and open to new possibilities.

And her spring concert song? It was the most popular song of all, because it was filled with the real sounds of life and love.

The End.
