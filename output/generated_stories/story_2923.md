# Miska the Brave Little Mouse

In a quiet garden shed, where the air smelled like fresh soil and sweet straw, lived a kind mouse named Miska. The shed was cozy. Sunlight slipped through tiny cracks and made golden stripes on the floor. Miska loved to listen to the soft buzz of bees outside and the gentle drip-drip of water from a hose.

One sunny morning, Miska hummed a little song as she swept crumbs into a neat pile with her tiny whiskers twitching. “I love my cozy corner,” she said. Her nest was made of leaves, bits of string, and a red button that sparkled like a tiny apple.

Suddenly, the door creaked open. In walked Bruno, a big brown beetle with shiny wings. He clacked his little feet and said, “Hello! I am Bruno. I am traveling from the big flower bed. I need a place to rest.”

Miska smiled kindly. “Hello, Bruno. Welcome to the garden shed. You can rest on that wooden shelf,” she said, pointing with her paw. “It is a safe spot.”

Bruno looked around. He saw Miska’s red button. “Ooo! Shiny!” he said. “May I hold it?”

Miska felt a little squeeze in her tummy. The red button was special. Her grandma had found it and given it to her. “It is special to me,” Miska said softly. “Please do not touch it.”

Bruno clicked his wings. “I will be careful! I will only hold it for a moment.”

Miska took a deep breath. She felt brave inside. “Bruno,” she said, “I said no. No means no. That is my boundary.” She stood tall, even though she was small.

Bruno blinked. He was surprised. “Boundary?” he asked.

“Yes,” Miska said gently. “A boundary is a line we use with our words. It keeps us safe and comfortable. I share some things, but I do not share my red button.”

Bruno nodded slowly. “I am from far away,” he said. “In my flower bed, friends share shiny things.”

Miska smiled again. “We are different, and that is okay. We can still be kind. We can still be friends. But friends listen to each other’s words.”

Bruno looked at the button and then at Miska’s careful eyes. He lowered his head. “I am sorry,” he said. “I did not listen.”

“Thank you for saying sorry,” Miska said. “You may rest on the shelf. I can make you a tiny leaf pillow.”

Bruno brightened. “That would feel nice,” he said.

Miska padded to the corner and gathered soft leaves. She made a tiny pillow and a small blanket from a dry petal that smelled like roses. “Here you go,” she said.

Bruno settled on the shelf with a happy sigh. “It is comfy,” he buzzed.

Just then, a gust of wind whooshed through the crack. The door banged. A stack of little pots wobbled—clink, clink, clink—and toppled toward Miska’s nest!

“Look out!” Bruno cried.

Miska’s heart raced. She dashed forward, whiskers brushing the dusty floor. “Courage,” she whispered to herself. She pushed one pot with her shoulder. “Oof!” It was heavy. Another pot rolled toward the red button.

Bruno fluttered down. “I will help!” he said. Together they pushed. Scrape, slide, roll! The pots settled safely against the wall.

Miska panted. “Thank you, Bruno.”

“You are brave, Miska,” Bruno said. “And you are kind.”

Miska touched her red button and smiled. “You were brave too. We solved the problem together.”

Bruno looked thoughtful. “I want to learn your boundaries,” he said. “Will you teach me?”

“Yes,” Miska said. “And I want to learn about your travels. We can share stories.”

They sat together, feeling the warm sun stripes. Miska poured a drop of water into a button-cap cup. Bruno sipped. “Delicious,” he said.

Miska said, “Here are my words: Please ask before you touch my things. If I say no, please listen. If I say yes, please be gentle.”

Bruno repeated, “Ask first. If no, listen. If yes, be gentle.” He smiled. “I can do that.”

Bruno shared, “In my flower bed, we greet with a wing tap.” He tapped his wing lightly on the shelf. “May I greet you with a tiny wing tap on the floor near you?”

Miska giggled. “Yes, thank you for asking.” She tapped her paw on the floor near his wing. Tap-tap. It felt friendly and safe.

The wind became soft again. The shed smelled warm and earthy. Outside, a bird sang, peep-peep-peep.

Bruno stretched. “I will rest now,” he said. “After I rest, may I help you tidy the shed?”

Miska nodded. “Yes, please. I like helpers.”

They worked together later, humming. Miska showed Bruno where things belonged. Bruno asked before moving anything special. “May I put this string here?” he asked. “Yes,” Miska replied. “Thank you for asking.”

When the sun began to set and the golden stripes turned pink, Bruno packed his tiny leaf pillow. “I will visit again,” he said. “May I knock on the door next time?”

“Yes,” Miska said. “Knock-knock, and I will answer.”

Bruno waved a shiny wing. “Goodbye, friend.”

“Goodbye,” Miska said. Her heart felt peaceful and strong.

That night, Miska curled up in her nest. She whispered, “Boundaries keep me safe. Listening keeps friends close. Courage helps me speak up.” She closed her eyes and smiled.

The End.
