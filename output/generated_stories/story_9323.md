# Rusty Helps Everyone Take Turns

Whirr-click. Beep-boop. Rusty was a helpful robot dog with shiny silver ears and a wagging metal tail. He lived with twins, Mia and Max. One bright morning, steam puffed gently from the bathroom like a warm cloud. The mirror was foggy and smelled like minty toothpaste. Drip, drop, drip went the sink.

“It’s my turn to brush first!” said Max. He held the red toothbrush tight.

“No, it’s my turn,” said Mia. She crossed her arms and stamped a little foot. “Yesterday you went first.”

Rusty tilted his head. His little screen eyes blinked. “Beep? Problem detected,” he chirped kindly. He rolled into the bathroom on soft rubber paws. His tail made a friendly swish-swish.

Max huffed. “I want to go first because I’m fastest!”

Mia shook her head. “I want to go first because I practiced the song!”

Rusty sat between them. He could feel the warm steam on his nose panel. He could hear the tiny fizz of the toothpaste cap. “Fair is fair,” Rusty said in a gentle beep-voice. “When we share, we care. When we take turns, we all feel good.”

Max’s cheeks were pink. Mia’s eyes were shiny. The mirror showed two worried faces and one smiling robot dog.

“How do we make it fair?” Mia asked softly.

Rusty’s ear lights flickered. “Solution mode!” he chimed. “We can make a turn chart. Today, Mia first. Tomorrow, Max first. We switch and we wait with patience.” Rusty tapped his paw. “Ping! And we practice good brushing.”

Max looked at Mia. “But I want to race,” he muttered.

Rusty shook his head. “Brushing is not a race. Healthy habits are careful and slow. Two minutes is just right.” He wagged. “I have a song to help us practice and wait.”

Mia nodded. “I can try waiting.”

Max sighed. “I can try, too.”

Rusty smiled. “Beep-beep. Thank you for being fair.”

He pulled a little timer from his side pocket. It looked like a shiny blue bubble. “Mia first,” he said. He stuck the timer to the mirror. It glowed soft and blue. “Max, you can hold the cup and watch the timer dots. That is your job while you wait.”

Max held the cup. It was cool and smooth. Water swished inside, shhh-shhh. He liked that sound. “Okay,” he said. “I can do this.”

Mia put toothpaste on her brush. Squeeze, ploop! “It smells minty,” she giggled. “Tingly!”

Rusty sang in a cheerful beep-tune. “Top teeth, bottom teeth, brush, brush, brush. Little circles, gentle touch. Front teeth, back teeth, side to side. Two minutes long, nice and wide.”

Mia brushed with small circles. Swish, swish, swish. Bubbles tickled her tongue. Rusty wagged and counted the glowing dots. Max watched. He took a deep breath. He tapped the cup gently, tip-tip-tip, and listened to the timer. He felt proud to wait and help.

The last dot blinked. Ding!

“All done,” Rusty said. “Great job, Mia. You were careful. That is healthy.”

Mia spit into the sink. “Puh-too!” She rinsed. “Max, your turn!”

Max smiled. “Thanks, Mia. That feels fair.” He put toothpaste on his brush. Squeeze, plop. “It smells minty to me, too!”

Rusty set the timer again. Mia held the cup now. She hummed the song while Max brushed.

“Top teeth, bottom teeth, brush, brush, brush,” she sang softly.

Rusty nodded. “You are both practicing. Practice makes strong skills.”

Max brushed slow and steady. He watched himself in the mirror. His shoulders relaxed. He felt the soft bubbles and heard the gentle swish. He did not rush. Ding! The timer finished.

“All done,” Rusty said. “Great job, Max.”

Max rinsed and grinned. “I did it!”

Mia clapped. “We both did it!”

Rusty wagged fast. “Fair turns feel good,” he said. “Now it is time to wash faces.”

The twins reached for the towel at the same time. “My turn!” they both said, then stopped and giggled.

Mia smiled. “You first, Max. I was first with brushing.”

Max nodded. “Thanks, Mia. That’s fair.” He patted his face. The towel felt soft and warm. Then he handed it to Mia. “Your turn.”

Mia patted her cheeks. “Fluffy!” she said, laughing.

Rusty’s screen eyes sparkled. “Beep-boop. You solved the problem with fairness, practice, and patience. You took turns. You waited kindly. You built strong, healthy habits.”

Max gave Rusty a hug. His metal fur felt cool and smooth. “You helped us, Rusty.”

Mia hugged, too. “I feel proud.”

Rusty wagged. “When we are fair, everyone can feel proud,” he said. “Tomorrow we switch again. We can do it!”

The bathroom felt calm and bright. The mirror was clear now. The sink went drip… stop. The air smelled fresh and minty. Mia and Max held hands and skipped out with Rusty, tails and ponytails bouncing.

Fair is fair. That was the lesson they took with them, all day long.

The End.
