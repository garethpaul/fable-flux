# Tessa Tortoise Fixes the Fussy Noise

In a sunny little town, there was a quiet garage workshop that smelled like warm wood, orange soap, and a tiny bit of motor oil. Tools hung on the walls like shiny silver smiles. In the corner, under a soft lamp, lived Tessa the wise tortoise.

Tessa liked to help friends fix squeaks, rattles, and clunks. She moved slowly and spoke gently. “Slow and steady,” she would say, “helps hands and hearts.”

One breezy morning, the garage door rolled up with a rattly rumble. In rolled Benny Bunny on a red scooter. “VROOM-VROOM!” shouted Benny, twisting the handle. The scooter made a loud BZZZZZ and wobbled. Nuts rattled. A loose bell went ting-a-ting-a-ting!

Tessa blinked her kind eyes. “Good morning, Benny,” she said. “How can I help?”

“My scooter is noisy!” Benny cried, ears flopping. “I need it fixed now, now, now! VROOM!” He revved again. The sound filled the workshop like a buzzing bee.

Tessa took a deep breath. She smelled the lemon oil she used to polish the bench. “Let’s use calm,” she said. “Calm helps us see and hear.”

Benny bounced. “I don’t want calm. I want fast!”

Just then, Miko Mouse hurried in, carrying a blue toolbox almost bigger than he was. “I’m ready!” squeaked Miko. “I’ll tighten all the things!” He hopped up on a little stool and grabbed a big wrench.

“Wait,” Tessa said kindly. “First, we listen.”

Benny groaned. “Listening is slow!”

Tessa tapped the scooter bell gently. “Ding.” She touched the wobbly handle. “Wiggle.” She rolled the wheel. “Whirr.” She nodded. “I hear three sounds: ding, wiggle, whirr.”

Benny crossed his paws. “I hear one sound: VROOM!”

Miko spun the wrench. “I can fix it super fast!” He reached for the bell, but the wrench slipped and clanged on the floor. CLANG! The loud noise made Benny jump and Miko squeak.

Tessa placed her smooth, cool shell between them like a small, strong wall. “Let’s pause,” she said softly. “Let’s breathe.”

She taught them a calm breath. “In like smelling a flower.” They sniffed. “Out like blowing bubbles.” They blew softly. In. Out. In. Out. The garage felt quieter. The lamp hummed. A tiny dust speck floated in the sun like a golden snowflake.

Benny’s ears relaxed. “Okay,” he said. “I can be calm.”

Miko put down the big wrench and picked a small screwdriver. “I can be careful.”

Tessa smiled. “Now, we make a plan. We will fix the bell first, then the handle, then the wheel. Step by step.”

Benny nodded. “Can I help?”

“Yes,” said Tessa. “You can hold. You can count. You can use gentle hands.”

They began. Tessa held the bell. “Benny, can you count three turns?”

Benny whispered, “One… two… three.” The bell made a happy “ding!” Not a wild ting-a-ting, just a sweet, small sound.

“Now the handle,” Tessa said. “Miko, light turns.”

Miko turned the screw. Tick. Tick. Tick. The handle hugged the scooter tight. No more wiggle.

Tessa checked the wheel. She turned it slowly. Whirr… whirr… “Benny, do you hear that soft whirr?” she asked.

“It sounds like wind in grass,” Benny said with a smile.

Tessa nodded. “Soft and steady. We used calm, and calm helped us see the steps.”

Just then, the garage door thumped. Benny had bumped it the first time he rushed in. He looked at the small scuff mark. His cheeks turned pink. “Oh,” he said in a small voice. “I was too loud and too fast. I made a mess. I’m sorry.”

Tessa’s eyes were warm. “Thank you for your apology, Benny. Saying sorry helps hearts feel better.”

Miko shuffled his paws. “I’m sorry too,” he said. “I grabbed the big wrench without thinking. I scared you. I can be small and careful.”

Tessa nodded. “Apology heard. We all learn.”

Benny looked at his scooter, then at Tessa and Miko. “Can we make a deal? I won’t rev inside the garage. I can make quiet vrooms with my voice.” He whispered, “vroom-vroom.”

Miko giggled. “And I will ask before I use tools,” he said. “I can hand you what you need.”

Tessa smiled. “That is a good compromise. We will keep the garage calm and safe. We can work together.”

They shook on it—Benny’s soft paw, Miko’s tiny paw, Tessa’s gentle foot. It felt warm and friendly.

Benny rolled the scooter outside. The sun was bright and the air smelled like cut grass. He put on his helmet with a click. “Vroom,” he whispered, and the scooter purred softly down the sidewalk.

Miko swept the floor where the wrench had fallen. Swish, swish. The marks faded away. He hummed a little song.

Tessa cleaned her tools and set them in their spots. Click. Clack. She looked around the neat garage. She could hear the birds chirp, and the lamp’s quiet hum.

Benny came back and gave Tessa a hug. “Thank you,” he said. “Calm helped.”

Tessa patted his shoulder. “Calm helps our hands. Calm helps our ears. Calm helps our hearts.”

Miko nodded. “And saying sorry helps too.”

Tessa added, “And making a fair plan together is called a compromise. It helps everyone feel okay.”

They all smiled. The garage felt peaceful, like a soft blanket.

The End.
