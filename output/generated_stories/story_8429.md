# Bounce's Big Bathroom Adventure

Bounce the kangaroo joey loved to hop and jump everywhere he went. His soft gray fur bounced as he moved, and his little pouch wiggled with each leap.

One morning, Bounce woke up and stretched his tiny arms. "Time to get ready for the day!" he said happily.

He hopped into the bathroom, where the white tiles felt cool under his feet. The morning sunlight streamed through the window, making everything bright and cheerful.

"I need to brush my teeth," Bounce said. But there was one big problem - the sink was very high up!

Bounce looked at the tall counter. The toothbrush and toothpaste sat way up there, like treasures on a mountain top. The faucet gleamed in the sunlight, but it seemed so far away.

"How will I reach it?" Bounce wondered aloud.

First, he tried standing on his tippy-toes. He stretched his arms as high as they could go. But his little fingers could only wiggle in the air.

"That didn't work," Bounce said with a small sigh.

Then he had an idea! "I know! I'll use my special kangaroo power - hopping!"

Bounce took a deep breath. He bent his strong legs and jumped as high as he could. Up, up, up he went! But he came back down without reaching the counter.

"I need to be brave and try again," Bounce told himself. "Mama always says practice makes perfect!"

He tried jumping again. This time, he used his tail for balance, just like he had seen the big kangaroos do. His tail swished back and forth, helping him stay steady.

Jump! Up he went again. Still not quite high enough.

"Maybe I need to warm up first," Bounce said smartly. "Athletes always warm up before they exercise!"

Bounce started doing little hops in place. Hop, hop, hop! His legs felt stronger with each bounce. He did stretches, reaching his arms to the ceiling and touching his toes.

"Now I feel ready!" he said with confidence.

Bounce took several steps back from the sink. He looked up at his goal - the toothbrush waiting for him. He took three deep breaths.

"One... two... THREE!" 

Bounce ran forward and made the biggest, strongest jump of his life! His legs pushed off the floor like springs. His arms reached up high. His tail helped him balance perfectly.

And... SUCCESS! His little paws grabbed the edge of the counter!

"I did it! I did it!" Bounce cheered as he pulled himself up safely.

He carefully reached for his toothbrush and toothpaste. The mint smell made his nose twitch happily. He brushed his teeth thoroughly, making sure they sparkled clean.

When he finished, Bounce looked at himself in the mirror. His eyes sparkled with pride.

"I was brave, and I kept trying," he said to his reflection. "Moving my body and exercising made me strong enough to reach my goal!"

Bounce carefully climbed down from the counter, using his strong legs to land softly on the bathroom floor.

Just then, Mama Kangaroo hopped into the bathroom. "Good morning, little one! I see you brushed your teeth all by yourself!"

"Yes, Mama! I used my jumping skills and didn't give up," Bounce said proudly. "Exercise and practice helped me be brave!"

Mama Kangaroo gave him a warm hug. "That's wonderful, Bounce! Moving our bodies makes us strong and helps us solve problems. You're becoming such a big, brave joey!"

Bounce smiled widely, showing his clean, sparkling teeth. He had learned that with movement, practice, and bravery, he could reach any goal - even the tallest bathroom counter!

The End.
