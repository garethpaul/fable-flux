# Poppy Panda’s Tummy Tune-Up

Poppy Panda padded into the bathroom, her soft paws making quiet tap-tap sounds on the tiles. The room smelled clean like soap and warm like steam. The mirror was shiny. The fluffy towels were stacked like little clouds.

Poppy rubbed her round belly. “My tummy feels grumpy,” she said in a small voice.

Mama Panda peeked in with kind eyes. “Good morning, Poppy. Did you eat breakfast?”

Poppy nodded. “I ate only sweet swirls,” she whispered. She meant the sugary cereal with the rainbow box. “It was yummy. But now my tummy says, ‘Ouch.’”

Mama Panda knelt and gave Poppy a gentle hug. “I hear your tummy,” she said. “Let’s listen with love. We can help it feel better.”

Poppy looked at the sink. She listened to the water go drip, drip. She felt her breath go in and out. She tried to be patient, like a calm mountain.

Mama turned on the tap. Warm water whooshed. “First, a small drink,” she said. “Sip, sip.”

Poppy took little sips from her tiny cup. The water felt cool and smooth. It made her belly feel like a soft wave. “That helps,” she said with a hopeful smile.

Mama smiled back. “Our bodies like water. They also like colorful foods. Sweet swirls can be a treat. But for strong bodies, we need balance.”

Poppy tilted her head. “What is balance?”

“Balance means a little of this and a little of that,” Mama said. “Crunchy greens, bright fruit, cozy grains, and protein that helps us grow.”

Poppy nodded slowly. “I can try,” she said. Her voice was brave and small.

Mama pointed to the toothbrushes in a cup. “Let’s practice patience while we care for our teeth,” she said. “We can make a plan for breakfast together.”

Poppy liked plans. Plans felt safe. “Okay,” she said.

They squeezed a tiny dot of minty paste on Poppy’s brush. “Swish, swish,” sang Mama.

Poppy brushed in little circles. “Minty!” she giggled. “My mouth feels tingly and fresh.” She spit and rinsed. The water swirled like a tiny whirlpool.

Poppy looked at her reflection. “Can we help my tummy now?” she asked.

Mama nodded. “Yes. We will make a compromise.”

“What is a comp… com-pro-mise?” Poppy tried the new word with care.

“It means we meet in the middle,” Mama said. “You like sweet swirls. I want you to have strong food. Let’s mix them.”

Poppy clapped her paws. “Mix!”

Mama brought a small bowl into the bathroom so they could talk and plan while Poppy rested on a fluffy bath mat. The bowl had soft oats, some banana slices like yellow moons, tiny green spinach ribbons, and a sprinkle of sweet swirls on top.

Poppy sniffed. “It smells warm. I smell banana. I smell sweet.” Her tummy made a hopeful gurgle.

“First a slow bite,” Mama said.

Poppy took a gentle spoonful. The oats were cozy. The banana was sweet and soft. The spinach was tiny and mild. The sweet swirls added a little crunch. “Mmm,” Poppy said. “My tummy says, ‘Thank you.’”

Mama touched Poppy’s paw. “You are patient and brave.”

Poppy took another bite. “Can I help make it next time?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Mama. “We can practice together every morning. You can choose colors.”

Poppy’s eyes sparkled. “Green spinach, yellow banana, red strawberry!” she said. “Maybe crunchy nuts when I am bigger.”

Mama nodded. “You are learning to listen to your body. That is kindness.”

They finished the small bowl. Poppy sat still on the bath mat. She listened to her belly. It felt calmer, like a purring kitten. She patted it gently. “Good job, tummy,” she whispered.

Mama gave her a warm washcloth to wipe her paws. It felt like a hug for her hands. “How do you feel now?” Mama asked.

“Better,” Poppy said. “I can be patient with my tummy. I can practice balance. I can make a compromise.”

Mama smiled. “Yes. And we can show compassion too.”

Poppy blinked. “Com-pash-on?”

“Compassion means being gentle and caring,” Mama explained. “We can be gentle with our tummies and gentle with others.”

Just then, Poppy heard a tiny buzz. Her little toothbrush robot, Beep, rolled out from the corner. “Beep-beep!” it cheered in a happy squeak.

Poppy patted Beep. “My tummy hurt,” she told it. “But I used water, and a warm bowl with good foods, and I waited. Now I feel okay.”

Beep beeped softly, like a friend saying, “Good job.”

Poppy stood and washed her paws. The water felt like rain on a leaf. She dried them on a fluffy towel. “I am ready for today,” she said.

Mama and Poppy looked in the mirror together. “What did we learn?” Mama asked.

Poppy lifted a paw and counted on her soft fingers. “We drink water. We eat colors. We make a compromise. We practice every day. We are kind.”

Mama gave her a hug that smelled like clean soap and bamboo tea. “That is a wonderful lesson,” she said.

Poppy smiled, tummy calm and heart warm. “I am Poppy Panda,” she said proudly. “I am patient. I listen to my body. I grow strong.”

The End.
