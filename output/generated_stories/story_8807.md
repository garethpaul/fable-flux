# Ellie's Big Voice

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved playing in her family's cozy living room. The soft carpet felt wonderful under her tiny feet, and colorful toys were scattered everywhere like a rainbow on the floor.

Today, Ellie's big sister Maya and cousin Ben were making art at the low wooden table. They had bright crayons, sparkly stickers, and big sheets of white paper spread out before them.

"Look at my castle!" Maya trumpeted happily, holding up her drawing of purple towers and pink flags.

"Mine's a rocket ship!" Ben added, showing off his red and blue creation with yellow flames shooting out the bottom.

Ellie watched from across the room, her little trunk swaying with excitement. She wanted to make art too! The crayons looked so pretty, and she could smell the waxy scent that made her trunk tingle with curiosity.

But Ellie felt too shy to ask. She was the littlest elephant, after all. Maybe they wouldn't want her to join.

So Ellie sat quietly on her favorite cushion, watching her trunk droop sadly toward the floor. The room felt big and lonely, even with all the cheerful chatter nearby.

Maya noticed Ellie sitting alone. "Ellie, why do you look sad?" she asked gently.

Ellie shuffled her feet. "I... I want to make art too," she whispered so quietly that her voice was barely louder than a mouse squeak.

"What did you say?" Ben asked, looking up from his rocket ship. "I couldn't hear you."

Ellie's cheeks felt warm. She took a deep breath, remembering what Mama had taught her about using her voice. "I want to make art with you!" she said louder, her trunk lifting up with courage.

"Of course you can!" Maya smiled widely. "We'd love to have you join us!"

"But I don't know how to draw castles or rockets," Ellie worried, her ears flapping nervously.

Ben scooted over to make room. "That's okay! You can draw anything you want. What do you like?"

Ellie thought hard. "I love flowers! And butterflies! And... and peanuts!" she giggled.

"Those sound perfect!" Maya handed Ellie a green crayon. "Here, start with this one."

Ellie wrapped her little trunk around the crayon. It felt smooth and cool. She pressed it to the paper and made her first mark – a wobbly green line that made her heart sing with joy.

"I'm making a flower stem!" she announced proudly.

As Ellie drew, she discovered she had lots of ideas. She added yellow petals that looked like sunshine, orange butterflies with wiggly antennae, and brown peanuts that made everyone laugh.

"Ellie, your picture is beautiful!" Maya said. "I love how the butterflies are dancing around your flowers."

"And those peanuts look delicious!" Ben added with a grin.

Ellie felt warm and happy inside. Her picture was different from theirs, but it was special because it came from her own imagination.

"I'm glad I asked to join you," Ellie said, admiring her colorful creation. "Speaking up helped me have fun!"

Maya hugged her little sister. "Your voice matters, Ellie. When you tell us what you want, we can help make it happen."

From that day on, Ellie remembered to use her voice when she wanted something. She learned that asking for what she needed wasn't scary – it was brave! And the living room became an even more wonderful place where everyone's ideas could shine.

The End.
