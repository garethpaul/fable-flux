# Nutkin's Great Discovery

Nutkin the squirrel sat on the warm windowsill of Mrs. Baker's kitchen, watching the golden sunlight dance across the cozy room. The kitchen smelled like fresh bread and cinnamon, making his little nose twitch with delight.

"Oh my!" Nutkin sighed, pressing his tiny paws against the glass. "I'm so hungry, but I don't know where to find good food anymore."

All winter long, Nutkin had been coming to this warm kitchen window, hoping to find scraps. But spring had arrived, and he felt restless just sitting inside all day.

Mrs. Baker noticed the little squirrel and opened the window. "Hello there, little friend," she said gently. "You look worried. What's troubling you?"

"I'm hungry," Nutkin squeaked. "But I don't know how to find fresh, healthy food. I've been eating leftover crumbs for so long, I forgot what real squirrel food tastes like!"

Mrs. Baker smiled kindly. "Oh, Nutkin! Spring is the perfect time to go outside and discover all the wonderful foods nature provides. Would you like me to show you?"

Nutkin's ears perked up. "Really? You'd help me?"

"Of course! But first, let's go outside together. The fresh air and sunshine will give you energy for your food adventure."

Hand in paw, they stepped out into the bright garden. Nutkin felt the warm sun on his fur and breathed in the sweet spring air. "Wow!" he exclaimed. "I feel better already!"

Mrs. Baker pointed to a tall oak tree. "See those branches, Nutkin? Climb up and look for acorns that fell last autumn. They're buried under the leaves."

Nutkin scampered up the rough bark, his claws clicking against the wood. He pushed aside crunchy brown leaves and found three perfect acorns. "I found some!" he called down, his tail twitching with excitement.

"Wonderful! Now look for the bird feeder," Mrs. Baker suggested. "Sometimes seeds fall to the ground."

Nutkin leaped from branch to branch, feeling stronger with each jump. Near the red bird feeder, he discovered scattered sunflower seeds in the soft dirt. He gathered them in his cheek pouches, their nutty smell making his mouth water.

"This is amazing!" Nutkin chattered. "What else can I find?"

"Try the vegetable garden," Mrs. Baker called. "Look for nuts that squirrels buried and forgot about."

Nutkin raced across the green grass, feeling the cool earth under his paws. He dug near the carrot patch and found two buried walnuts. "I can't believe how much food is out here!"

As the afternoon sun warmed his back, Nutkin realized something important. "Mrs. Baker, I feel so much happier and stronger when I'm outside moving around and finding my own food!"

"That's right, Nutkin," she smiled. "When you go outside and stay active, your body feels good. And when you find fresh, natural foods, you get all the nutrition you need to be healthy and strong."

Nutkin cracked open a walnut and took a bite. It tasted so much better than stale crumbs! "From now on, I'll spend every day outside exploring and finding fresh food. Thank you for teaching me!"

From that day forward, Nutkin became the most active squirrel in the neighborhood. He climbed trees, jumped between branches, and discovered new foods every day. He learned that the best way to stay healthy was to play outside and eat the nutritious foods that nature provided.

The End.
