# Tilly Turtle Takes Her Time

Tilly was a shy turtle with a smooth green shell. She liked quiet. She liked soft music. One day, Tilly went to the musical practice room at the community center. The room smelled like clean wood and lemon polish. Sunlight made little golden squares on the floor. Tilly’s heart went thump-thump, but she took a slow breath.

“I will take my time,” she whispered. “Slow and steady.”

Inside the room were all kinds of sounds. Plink, plonk went a piano. Toot, toot went a small horn. Tap, tap went little drumsticks. Tilly carried her small triangle. It was shiny and silver. When she hit it with a stick, it went ting! The sound was light and bright, like a star.

“Welcome, friends,” said Ms. Melody, the music teacher. She had kind eyes and a scarf with music notes. “Today we will practice our song. We will listen. We will wait. We will take turns.”

Tilly liked the word wait. Waiting felt safe. But it was also hard. Sometimes waiting made her belly feel twisty.

A red fox named Finn flicked his tail. “I want to go first!” he said, hopping up and down.

A blue bird named Bella fluffed her feathers. “Me too! Me too!” she chirped.

Ms. Melody smiled. “We will all have a turn. The song needs each voice, each sound. Patience helps the music fit together.”

Tilly looked at her triangle. She wanted to play it right away. But she remembered: take your time. She tucked her head a little and listened.

The song began. Plink, plonk, plink sang the piano. Toot, toot, toooot sang the horn. Tap, tap-tap-tap went the little drum. Tilly waited. Her triangle felt smooth and cool in her hand. She felt the stick, light like a feather. Her shell felt warm in the sun square on the floor.

“Triangle next,” said Ms. Melody.

Tilly lifted her stick. Her paw shook a little. “You can do it,” she whispered to herself. She waited for the right beat. One, two, three… ting! The sound sparkled in the air. Tilly smiled. She could hear her tiny star-sound in the big song.

But Finn the fox didn’t wait. He tooted his horn over her ting. It was too loud. It covered Tilly’s sound. Toot! Toot! Toot!

Tilly blinked. Her smile faded. Her shy heart went quiet.

Ms. Melody held up her hand. “Freeze, please.”

The room got still. The lemon smell felt strong. The sun squares didn’t move.

“Finn,” Ms. Melody said softly, “we take turns here. That is fair. Fair means each friend gets a turn. That is justice.”

Finn’s ears drooped. “I got excited,” he said. “I forgot to wait. I didn’t mean to make it unfair.”

Bella hopped closer to Tilly. “I like your ting,” she said. “It sounds like a tiny bell.”

Tilly nodded. “Thank you,” she whispered. Her voice was small, but it was there.

Ms. Melody looked at Finn. “When we make a mistake, we can make amends. That means we fix it. Would you like to try?”

Finn took a breath. “Yes.” He turned to Tilly. “I am sorry I played over your ting. I will wait this time. Can I help make it fair?”

Tilly felt warm inside. “Thank you,” she said. “Maybe you can count with me?”

Finn smiled a little. “Okay. One, two, three…”

Ms. Melody lifted her hands. “Let’s try again. We will use patience. We will use fair turns.”

The song began again. Plink, plonk, plink. Toot… waited. Tap, tap-tap-tap. Finn whispered, “Almost.” Tilly felt brave. She listened carefully. The room was full of gentle sounds and gentle hearts.

“One, two, three,” Finn counted softly.

Tilly raised her stick. Ting!

The note floated up like a bright bubble. This time, everyone stopped to hear it. Bella sighed, “Pretty.” Finn grinned. “We did it!”

Tilly’s shy heart felt bigger. She had waited. Her friends had waited, too. The song sounded better when everyone used patience.

Ms. Melody clapped. “Beautiful. That is what patience sounds like. That is what justice looks like. And that is how we make amends.”

They practiced again. They took turns. They listened. Sometimes someone went too fast. When that happened, they stopped. They said, “Oops,” and tried again. Each time, they listened more. Each time, Tilly felt braver.

After practice, Ms. Melody brought out a small basket. “One treat for each helper,” she said. The basket smelled sweet, like apples and cinnamon. Tilly chose a round, soft cookie. Finn chose one, too.

Finn walked to Tilly. “Thank you for counting with me,” he said.

Tilly smiled. “Thank you for waiting with me.”

Bella fluttered. “Can we play our ting together?” she asked.

Tilly giggled. “Triangles are for one at a time,” she said, “but we can share turns.”

They sat in a sun square and took small, slow bites. The cookies were warm. The room felt peaceful. The instruments were quiet now, resting after the good work.

Ms. Melody waved. “Before we go, what did we learn?”

Tilly lifted her chin. “Patience helps us do things well,” she said. Her voice was still soft, but it was clear.

Finn added, “Justice means fair turns.”

Bella chirped, “Making amends fixes mistakes.”

Ms. Melody nodded. “Yes. We listen. We wait. We fix. Then our music—and our hearts—sound just right.”

Tilly tucked her triangle into its soft case. She took a slow breath. “I will keep taking my time,” she whispered. She felt calm. She felt proud. She felt ready for next time.

The End.
