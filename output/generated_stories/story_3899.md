# Bubbles Helps in the Guest Room

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to giggle and splash. One sunny morning, Bubbles visited a cozy house by the sea. Inside was a quiet guest room with soft blue walls, a gentle lamp, and a big soft bed that smelled like clean soap. The curtains swished like waves when the window was open. “Ooo, it feels like the ocean in here,” Bubbles said, wiggling his tail.

“Good morning, Bubbles,” said Maya, the child who lived there. She held a basket of folded towels. “Grandma is coming to visit. We need to get the guest room ready.”

Bubbles clapped his fins. “I can help! I like to help.” His nose nuzzled the basket. The towels felt fluffy and warm.

Maya smiled. “We have a routine. First, we tidy toys. Next, we make the bed. Then, we dust and put out fresh towels.”

Bubbles nodded. “First tidy, next bed, then dust and towels! I can remember.” He sang a little song, “Tidy, bed, dust, towel,” and clicked happily.

They started to tidy. There were blocks on the floor and a puzzle with big pieces under the chair. Bubbles scooped up the blocks with his fins. “Plip plop, into the box!” he chirped. Maya picked up the puzzle and hummed.

Suddenly, there was a tiny squeak. From under the bed rolled a small red ball. “That’s mine!” said a little crab named Crinkle, peeking from the corner. His shell was shiny like a button.

Bubbles blinked. “Hi, Crinkle! We are helping. We have a routine.”

Crinkle hugged his ball. “I play here. This is my favorite spot. It smells like salt and dust. I don’t want you to move my things.”

Maya crouched down. “We want the guest room to be clean for Grandma. We can make a special space for your toys too.”

Crinkle frowned. “But you are moving my ball.”

Bubbles felt a twist in his tummy. He wanted to help, and he wanted Crinkle to be happy. He took a deep breath and listened to the soft swish of the curtains. “Let’s talk,” Bubbles said gently. “I feel sad when we don’t agree. What do you feel?”

Crinkle clicked his claws. “I feel worried. I don’t want my fun to go away.”

Maya nodded. “Thank you for telling us. We can fix this together.”

Bubbles thought. “We have a plan. We can make a play basket for your toys. It can stay in a cozy corner. We won’t touch your special things without asking.”

Crinkle’s eyes brightened. “A cozy corner?”

“Yes,” Maya said. “And we can put a little sign that says ‘Crinkle’s Corner.’ We will keep the room clean and still keep your play safe.”

Crinkle wiggled with joy. “Okay! That feels fair.”

They kept going. Bubbles and Maya sang their routine song. “Tidy, bed, dust, towel!” Bubbles put toys in a basket and placed it in the corner by the window. Maya wrote a tiny sign on a soft card and drew a smiling crab. “Crinkle’s Corner,” she read aloud.

Crinkle placed his red ball in the basket and patted it. “Thank you,” he said. “I like helping too. I can dust low places.”

Bubbles giggled. “That is volunteering! You are helping because you want to.”

Crinkle puffed up with pride. “I volunteer!”

They made the bed next. The sheets were crisp and cool. “Whoosh,” said Bubbles as he lifted a corner with his fin. The blanket flopped like a gentle wave. Maya smoothed it with flat hands. “Smooth and neat,” she said. The pillow smelled like lavender. Bubbles took another deep breath. “Mmm, calm.”

Dusting came next. Maya dusted the lamp, and Bubbles carefully swished a small duster across the bookshelf. “Tickle tickle,” he laughed as the feathers brushed his fin. Crinkle scurried along the baseboard, dusting with a tiny cloth. “Squeak sweep,” he sang.

When it was time for towels, Bubbles carried them carefully. “Fluffy clouds,” he whispered. He folded and refolded, trying to make the corners match. One towel went crooked. He frowned.

“Try again,” Maya encouraged. “You can do it.”

Bubbles took a breath and tried again. “Fold, fold, pat.” This time, the towel made a neat square. “I did it!” he cheered.

Crinkle clapped his claws. “You kept trying. That is persistence!”

At last, the guest room was ready. The bed was neat, the floor was clear, the air smelled fresh. Sunlight made soft shapes on the carpet. The little sign in the corner made Crinkle smile.

Grandma arrived with a warm hug. “Oh my, what a lovely room,” she said. “Who helped?”

“We all did,” Maya said. “We followed our routine. We used kind words. We made a plan when we had a problem.”

Bubbles nodded. “We listened and shared. We found a way that felt fair.”

Crinkle stood tall. “And I volunteered to dust!”

Grandma smiled. “That is wonderful. When we talk, listen, and keep trying, we can solve problems together.”

Bubbles felt peaceful. The guest room felt like calm ocean waves. “Today we learned,” he said softly, “that we can fix conflicts by listening, using gentle words, and making a plan. Routines help us. Persistence helps us. Helping others feels good.”

Everyone smiled. The guest room was ready for sweet dreams and happy visits.

The End.
