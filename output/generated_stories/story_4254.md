# Ollie's Big Mess

Ollie the otter loved to play! His soft, brown fur sparkled when he wiggled, and his tiny paws were always busy touching everything around him.

Today, Mama Otter had a special job for Ollie. "Grandma Otter is coming to visit tomorrow," she said with a warm smile. "She will sleep in our guest room. Can you help me make it nice and clean?"

"Yes, yes!" squeaked Ollie, clapping his little paws together. "I want to help!"

Mama Otter showed Ollie the cozy guest room. It had a soft blue bed, a wooden chair, and pretty flowers on the windowsill. "This needs to be perfect for Grandma," Mama said. "Can you dust the chair while I change the sheets?"

"I can do it!" Ollie said proudly. He picked up the fluffy yellow duster and started cleaning.

But Ollie got so excited! He spun around and around with the duster, pretending to be a dancing cloud. "Whee!" he giggled, twirling faster and faster.

CRASH! 

Ollie bumped into the little table by the window. The pretty flower vase wobbled, tipped, and fell to the floor with a loud SMASH! Water splashed everywhere, and the beautiful flowers scattered across the wet floor.

Ollie's whiskers drooped. His tummy felt funny and tight. "Oh no," he whispered, looking at the broken pieces.

When Mama Otter heard the crash, she hurried into the room. She saw the mess and Ollie's sad face.

"I'm sorry, Mama," Ollie said in a tiny voice. "I was supposed to help, but I made everything worse. I was being silly instead of careful."

Mama Otter knelt down and gave Ollie a gentle hug. "Thank you for telling me the truth, little one. Everyone makes mistakes when they're learning."

"But now Grandma's room is all messy!" Ollie's eyes filled with tears.

"You know what?" Mama said softly. "We can fix this together. Being responsible means cleaning up when we make mistakes. Would you like to help me make it right?"

Ollie nodded eagerly. "Yes! I want to be responsible!"

First, they carefully picked up all the broken pieces. Ollie was very gentle with his paws. Then they mopped up the water with a soft towel. The floor sparkled when they finished!

"What about the flowers?" Ollie asked, looking at the wilted petals.

"Let's go pick fresh ones from our garden," Mama suggested.

Outside, Ollie carefully chose the prettiest yellow and purple flowers. He smelled their sweet scent and felt their soft petals. Back inside, they put the new flowers in a different vase - one that was harder to tip over.

"Look, Mama!" Ollie said, stepping back to admire their work. "The room looks even more beautiful now!"

The next morning, Grandma Otter arrived. She hugged Ollie tight and said, "What a lovely room! Someone worked very hard to make it special."

Ollie beamed with pride. "I helped! I made a mistake, but Mama taught me how to fix it. Being responsible means cleaning up and trying again!"

"That's exactly right, my dear Ollie," Grandma said, patting his head. "You're learning to be very responsible indeed."

From that day on, whenever Ollie helped with chores, he remembered to be careful and thoughtful. And when he did make mistakes, he always helped clean them up. That's what being responsible means!

The End.
