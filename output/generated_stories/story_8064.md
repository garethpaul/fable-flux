# Bruno Bear Cub and the Rooftop Garden

Bruno was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and bright curious eyes. He lived in a cozy house with a flat rooftop. On the rooftop, there was a small garden in big pots, a little bench, and a wind chime that sang, ting-ting-ting, when the breeze came by.

One sunny morning, Mama Bear said, “Bruno, today you are in charge of our rooftop garden. That means you are responsible. Can you care for the plants?”

Bruno’s ears perked up. “I can do it, Mama! I will be careful and kind.” He felt proud and tall inside.

Mama Bear smiled and handed him a small watering can and a tiny list. “Here are your jobs: water the plants, check the wind chime hooks, and read the labels aloud to keep practice with words. If you need help, ask.”

Bruno climbed the steps to the rooftop. The door opened with a soft creak. The air smelled like mint and warm soil. The sun felt cozy on his fur. Bruno looked around. “Hello, garden,” he said. “I am here to care for you!”

He walked to the first pot. A little wooden label stuck in the dirt said, “Mint.” Bruno touched the letters with his paw and sounded them out slowly, “Mmm… i… nnn… t. Mint!” He smiled. “I am learning the word.” He felt proud of using his words.

He tipped the watering can and poured a gentle drink. “Not too much,” he whispered. “Just right.” He watched the water sink in like tiny sparkles. A butterfly fluttered by. “Hi, butterfly,” Bruno said softly. “I will not splash you.”

Bruno moved to the next pot. The label said, “Tomato.” He sounded it out. “To… ma… to. Tomato!” The little plant had bright green leaves. Bruno touched the soil. It felt dry and crumbly. “Dry needs water,” he said. “That is logical thinking.” He poured until the dirt turned dark and damp. “There,” he said. “You were thirsty.”

At the third pot, the label said, “Basil.” Bruno read it slowly and proudly. “Ba… sil. Basil!” The leaves smelled peppery and sweet. He sniffed. “Mmm. You smell happy.”

Then Bruno heard a tiny clink-clink. The wind chime was wobbling. One hook looked loose. Bruno remembered his list. “Check the wind chime hooks.” He stood on his toes and gently pushed the hook back in place. “Safe and snug,” he said. He tugged it a little. It held tight. “Being responsible means keeping things safe.”

Suddenly, Bruno heard a faint peep. “Peep… peep…” He looked around. Under the bench, a small sparrow sat with a fluffed wing. The bird’s eyes were shiny and worried.

“Oh no,” Bruno whispered. He knelt. “Hello, little bird. Are you okay?” The sparrow peeped again and hopped, but the wing drooped.

Bruno’s heart felt soft. He wanted to help. “I will think,” he said aloud. “First, the bird needs calm. Second, the bird needs shade. Third, I should tell Mama.” He took a deep breath. “I can do this.”

Bruno slowly moved a small umbrella to make shade. He placed a tiny capful of water nearby. “Here is a little drink,” he said gently. “I will be right back.”

He walked quickly, not running on the stairs. “Mama,” he called softly. “There is a little bird on the rooftop. The wing looks hurt. I gave shade and water. Can you help me help the bird?”

Mama Bear nodded. “That was very responsible, Bruno. You were calm and kind. Let’s go.”

Back on the rooftop, Mama Bear looked at the sparrow. “We will call the animal helper,” she said. Bruno dialed the bird rescue number on Mama’s phone with careful paws. He said, “Hello. We have a small sparrow with a hurt wing. We are on our rooftop garden.” He listened and said, “Thank you. We will wait.”

While they waited, Bruno read the plant labels again to the bird in a soft voice. “Mint. Tomato. Basil.” He smiled at the sparrow. “Words can be gentle. You are not alone.”

The wind chime sang, ting-ting-ting, like a tiny bell. The animal helper arrived with a soft towel. “Thank you for caring,” she said. “You did the right things.” She gently picked up the sparrow. “I’ll help the wing heal.”

Bruno felt warm and proud. “Goodbye, little bird,” he said. “Get better soon.”

When the rooftop was quiet again, Bruno checked his list. “Water plants,” he said. “Done. Check wind chime. Done. Read labels. Done. Help a friend. Extra done!”

Mama Bear hugged him. “You were responsible,” she said. “You used logical thinking to solve problems. You showed empathy by caring for the bird. You practiced your words and listened.”

Bruno’s smile grew big. “I can be careful and kind,” he said. “I can take care of things.”

The sun was softer now, and the mint leaves shimmered. The rooftop felt peaceful. The wind chime sang one last gentle song.

Bruno picked up his watering can and placed it neatly by the door. He cleaned a tiny spill with a cloth. He waved to the garden. “See you tomorrow,” he said. “I will be ready.”

Mama Bear and Bruno went downstairs for lunch. “Today,” Bruno said, “I learned that being responsible means thinking, caring, and finishing my jobs. It makes me feel good inside.”

Mama Bear smiled. “That is a wonderful lesson.”

Bruno nodded. “I can do it again tomorrow.”

The End.
