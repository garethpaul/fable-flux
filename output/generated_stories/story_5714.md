# Joey's Big Feelings Day

In a sunny backyard filled with colorful flowers and buzzing bees, lived a little kangaroo joey named Pip. Pip loved to hop around the soft green grass and feel the warm sunshine on his fuzzy brown fur.

One bright morning, Pip woke up feeling extra bouncy. "Today feels special!" he said to himself, stretching his little arms up high. The sun was shining, the birds were singing sweet songs, and everything smelled like fresh flowers.

Pip hopped outside and saw his friend Benny the bunny sitting quietly by the garden. But something was different. Benny's ears were drooping down, and he wasn't eating the yummy carrots like he usually did.

"Hi Benny!" Pip called out cheerfully. "Want to play hop-scotch?"

Benny looked up with sad eyes. "I don't feel like playing today, Pip. I'm feeling really sad."

Pip stopped hopping and tilted his head. He had never seen his friend look so unhappy before. "Oh," Pip said softly. "What does sad feel like?"

"It feels heavy in my tummy," Benny explained, touching his fluffy white belly. "And it makes my eyes want to leak water."

Pip sat down next to his friend. He remembered when he felt sad last week when he lost his favorite red ball. His tummy had felt heavy too, just like Benny described.

"I felt that way when I lost my ball," Pip said gently. "Mama told me that feeling sad is okay. She said all feelings are important."

Benny's ears perked up a little. "Really? Even sad feelings?"

"Yes!" Pip nodded. "Mama says feelings help us understand what we need. When I felt sad about my ball, it helped me ask for help to find it."

Pip thought for a moment, then had an idea. "What made you feel sad, Benny?"

"I saw Mrs. Robin's baby bird fall from the nest this morning," Benny whispered. "I wanted to help, but I didn't know how."

Pip's heart felt warm and fuzzy. "That shows you care about others! That's a good feeling inside the sad feeling."

"But I still feel sad," Benny said.

"That's okay," Pip replied. "Let's think together. What could we do to help?"

The two friends put their heads together. Pip suggested they could ask Mama Kangaroo for help. She always knew what to do when someone needed caring.

They hopped together to find Mama Kangaroo, who was watering her vegetable garden. The water sparkled like tiny diamonds in the sunshine.

"Mama," Pip called, "Benny and I want to help Mrs. Robin's baby bird. Can you show us how?"

Mama Kangaroo smiled proudly. "Of course! It's wonderful that you want to help your neighbors. Let's go see what we can do together."

With Mama's help, they gently placed the baby bird back in its cozy nest. Mrs. Robin chirped a happy "thank you" song.

"I feel much better now," Benny said, his ears standing up tall again. "The heavy feeling in my tummy is gone!"

"Helping others can help our sad feelings feel lighter," Mama Kangaroo explained. "And Pip, you did something very special today."

"I did?" Pip asked, his eyes bright with curiosity.

"You listened to your friend's feelings and helped him feel better. That's called being caring and responsible."

Pip felt proud and happy. His chest felt warm and tingly, like sunshine was glowing inside him.

"I learned that all feelings are okay," Pip announced. "And when we help others, it helps us too!"

Benny hopped with joy. "Thank you for helping me understand my feelings, Pip!"

The two friends spent the rest of the sunny day playing together, knowing that sharing feelings and helping others made their friendship even stronger.

The End.
