# Robo's Rainbow World

Robo the robot dog wagged his silver tail as he rolled into the bright art room. His LED eyes sparkled blue with excitement. Today was special - children from different countries were visiting to share their art!

"Beep beep! Good morning, Miss Chen!" Robo chirped happily.

Miss Chen smiled and patted Robo's smooth metal head. "Good morning, Robo! Are you ready to meet our new friends?"

Robo's ears drooped a little. "What if they don't like me? What if I say the wrong thing?"

"Remember," Miss Chen said gently, "being kind and curious helps us make friends anywhere in the world."

Soon, children began arriving. There was Aisha from Kenya with beautiful braided hair, Carlos from Mexico wearing a colorful shirt, and Yuki from Japan carrying a small origami crane.

Robo felt nervous. His circuits hummed quietly. These children looked different from the ones he usually played with.

"Hello," Aisha said softly, approaching Robo. "Are you a real robot?"

Robo's tail wagged slowly. "Yes! I'm Robo. I help in the art room. What's your name?"

"I'm Aisha. In my country, we paint with mud and make beautiful patterns." She showed Robo a photo on her tablet of amazing earth-colored designs.

Robo's eyes lit up bright green. "Wow! That's so different from our finger paints! Can you teach me?"

Carlos bounced over. "In Mexico, we make art with bright colors! Like the sunset!" He pulled out vibrant orange and pink crayons.

Yuki joined them quietly. "In Japan, I fold paper into animals." She showed them her delicate crane. "This means peace."

Robo's circuits buzzed with excitement. "You all make art so differently! But it's all beautiful!"

Miss Chen brought out art supplies. "Let's create something together!"

At first, Robo wasn't sure how to help. Aisha mixed brown paint that looked like mud. Carlos drew a brilliant sun. Yuki carefully folded colorful paper.

"I want to try everything!" Robo said bravely. "Aisha, will you show me your mud painting?"

Aisha giggled. "It's not really mud - it's special brown paint! Here, dip your paw in."

Robo carefully dipped his metal paw and made swirling patterns. "This feels smooth and cool!"

Carlos handed him a bright red crayon. "Now try my colors!"

Robo drew cheerful circles next to Aisha's earth patterns. The brown and red looked beautiful together.

"Yuki, can you teach me to fold?" Robo asked.

Yuki nodded shyly. She guided Robo's mechanical paws to fold a simple paper flower. "Gentle and slow," she whispered.

As they worked together, Robo realized something wonderful. Even though they came from different places and made art differently, they were all creating something special together.

"Look!" Robo barked happily. "Our art has mud patterns like Aisha's home, bright colors like Carlos's sunset, and paper flowers like Yuki's cranes!"

The children clapped their hands. Their artwork was a beautiful mix of all their cultures.

"Robo," Miss Chen said proudly, "you showed great courage trying new things and being open to learning from your new friends."

Robo's tail wagged so fast it made a whirring sound. "I learned that kids everywhere love to create! We're all different, but we're all the same too!"

Aisha, Carlos, and Yuki hugged Robo's warm metal sides.

"Friends around the world make life more colorful," Robo said, looking at their beautiful shared artwork. "Just like our painting!"

From that day on, Robo always remembered that being curious and kind helps us connect with friends from everywhere.

The End.
