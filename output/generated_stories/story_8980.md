# Benny Bear's Big Garden Goal

Benny Bear was a fluffy brown cub who loved playing in his sunny backyard. The warm sunshine tickled his nose, and the soft grass felt good under his paws. Today felt like a special day for something new!

"I want to grow the biggest, most beautiful sunflower ever!" Benny announced to his mama. His eyes sparkled with excitement as he looked at the empty patch of dirt by the fence.

"That's a wonderful goal, Benny," said Mama Bear with a smile. "But growing a sunflower takes time and care. Are you ready to work hard?"

Benny nodded eagerly. "Yes! I'll be the best sunflower grower ever!"

Mama Bear helped Benny plant the tiny black seed in the rich, dark soil. "Remember," she said gently, "you'll need to water it every day and make sure it gets plenty of sunshine."

"I will! I promise!" Benny said, already imagining his giant sunflower reaching up to the clouds.

For the first three days, Benny was perfect. He watered his little patch every morning, singing happy songs to his seed. "Grow, little seed, grow big and tall! You'll be the prettiest flower of all!"

But on the fourth day, Benny saw his friend Rosie Rabbit playing with a new bouncy ball. It looked so fun and shiny in the bright sunlight.

"Come play with me, Benny!" called Rosie, bouncing the red ball high into the blue sky.

Benny forgot all about his sunflower and played with the ball all day. The sun felt warm on his fur as he laughed and played.

The next morning, Benny remembered his goal. He rushed to his garden patch, but the soil looked dry and cracked. "Oh no!" he cried. "I forgot to water you yesterday!"

Benny felt sad and worried. His tummy felt heavy with disappointment. "Maybe I'm not good at growing flowers," he sniffled.

Mama Bear found him sitting by his patch, looking very sorry. "What's wrong, little one?"

"I forgot to water my seed yesterday. I was playing and having fun, but now my sunflower might not grow. I'm a bad gardener!" Benny's voice was small and sad.

Mama Bear hugged him tight. "Oh, Benny, everyone makes mistakes. The important thing is what we do next. Do you still want to reach your goal?"

Benny wiped his eyes with his paw. "Yes, but what if it's too late?"

"Let's water it now and see what happens," Mama Bear suggested. "Sometimes plants are stronger than we think."

Benny carefully watered the dry soil, whispering, "I'm sorry, little seed. I'll take better care of you."

Every day after that, Benny remembered his goal. Even when friends wanted to play, he watered his patch first. "Goals come first, then play time!" he would say.

After two weeks, a tiny green sprout poked through the soil! Benny danced with joy. "It forgave me! My sunflower is growing!"

Day by day, the little plant grew taller. Benny measured it with his paw, watching it reach higher and higher. The green leaves felt smooth and the stem grew strong and thick.

Finally, after many weeks of careful watering and patient waiting, a huge yellow sunflower bloomed! It was as big as Benny's head, with bright golden petals that shone in the sunshine.

"I did it!" Benny cheered. "I reached my goal!"

"You sure did," said Mama Bear proudly. "You learned that reaching goals takes daily work, and that it's okay to make mistakes if we keep trying."

Benny smiled big, feeling proud and happy. He had learned that setting goals, working hard every day, and never giving up could make wonderful things happen.

The End.
