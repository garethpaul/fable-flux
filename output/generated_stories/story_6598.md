# Ruby Rabbit's Porch Paint Plan

Ruby was a determined rabbit. Every morning, she hopped to her peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like fresh wood and mint leaves. The air felt soft and cool on her whiskers. Birds chirped, “Tweet-tweet,” and the wind whispered, “Whoosh.”

Ruby had a routine. She swept the porch with her tiny broom. Swish, swish! She watered the potted flowers. Drip, drip! She sang a little song, “Good morning, porch. Good morning, day. I’ll care for you in every way.”

One sunny day, Ruby sat on her cozy porch chair. She held a paintbrush. “I will paint a picture,” she said. “I will paint the flowers, the sky, and the birds.”

She opened her paint box and sniffed. “Pee-yew!” Ruby wrinkled her nose. “These paints smell funny.” She looked at the bottles. The labels said, “Harsh” and “Strong.” Ruby did not know what that meant, but it did not feel right.

A breeze blew a paper cup across the porch. It rattled and rolled. Rattle, roll, bonk! Ruby’s ears stood tall. She looked around. She saw more cups and wrappers by the steps. She saw a little spill under the water can. The porch did not look happy.

“Oh no,” said Ruby. “My porch is not clean. The air smells strong. The birds flew away. I have a problem.” Her heart felt tight. Her nose twitched fast.

Ruby took a deep breath. In, out. “I can fix this,” she said. “I am determined. I care for my porch. I will help the Earth too.”

First, Ruby started a clean-up. She put on her green gloves. Snap, snap! She picked up the cups and wrappers. Plop, plop, plop, into a bin. She hummed, “Pick up, tidy up, thank you, Earth.” She wiped the little spill with a cloth. Squeeze, wipe, dry. The porch looked better already.

Next, Ruby sorted the trash. She made three baskets. “This one is for paper,” she said. “This one is for plastic. This one is for compost.” She dropped a dry leaf into the compost basket. Crunch! “Your old leaf will become new dirt,” she smiled. “That helps plants grow.”

Her neighbor, a bluebird, fluttered back to the rail. “Tweet?” it asked.

“It’s okay,” said Ruby. “I am cleaning with care.”

Ruby still wanted to paint. But she did not want the funny-smelling paints. She thought. Tap-tap went her paw. “I can make Earth-friendly paint,” she said. “I can use things from the kitchen.”

She hopped inside and came back with a bowl of blueberries, a little spinach, and a small jar of turmeric. The colors were bright. Blue, green, and sunny yellow!

Ruby put the berries in a cup and mashed them with a spoon. Squish, squish! Purple juice splashed. It smelled sweet. She tore the spinach and mixed it with a bit of warm water. Swirl, swirl! It looked like a tiny forest. She added a pinch of turmeric and a drop of water. Stir, stir! It became golden like a warm sunrise.

She dipped her brush into the purple. “Hello, purple,” she giggled. “You are berry nice.” She painted soft petals on her paper. Brush, brush. She used the green for leaves. Swish, swish. She dotted the center with sunny yellow. Tap, tap, tap.

As Ruby painted, the breeze smelled like fruit and plants, not like strong chemicals. The bluebird chirped a happy song. Tweet-a-dee! Ruby smiled. “This feels kind,” she said. “Kind to my porch. Kind to the air. Kind to the Earth.”

Soon, the picture was done. It showed a flower, a tree, and the big blue sky. Ruby added a little sign at the bottom. She wrote, “Made with love and plants.”

A grasshopper hopped by. Boing, boing. “Chirp?” it asked.

Ruby nodded. “Yes, friend. I used safe paint. I cleaned my porch. I sorted my trash. This is my care routine.”

She looked at her bins. Paper, plastic, compost. She looked at her cloth and broom, ready for next time. She made a plan chart with simple pictures: a broom for sweep, a bin for sort, a leaf for compost, and a paintbrush with a leaf for Earth-friendly art. She taped the chart by the door.

Every day after that, Ruby followed her routine. Sweep the porch. Sort the trash. Water the plants. Make art with safe colors. She hummed her song, “Good morning, porch. Good morning, day. I’ll care for you in every way.”

The porch smelled fresh. The flowers swayed. The bluebird sang, and the grasshopper danced. Ruby’s heart felt light and proud. She knew she had solved her problem with care and creativity.

Ruby held up her painting. “We can help the Earth,” she said. “We can clean up, reuse, and make gentle art. Little steps, every day.”

The wind whispered, “Whoosh.” The porch creaked softly, “Thank you.” And Ruby whispered back, “You’re welcome.”

The End.
