# Sunny's Song on the Porch

On a peaceful front porch, a small blue bird named Sunny sat on the wooden rail. The morning sun felt warm on Sunny’s feathers. The porch smelled like sweet flowers and fresh tea. The wind was soft, and a rocking chair made gentle creak-creak sounds.

Sunny loved to sing. Her song was bright and sweet. Trill, tweet, la-la-la! The notes danced in the air like little bubbles. Sunny’s round eyes sparkled. She sang about the sun, the breeze, and the smiling day.

“Good morning, world!” Sunny chirped. “I’m happy! I’m here!”

“Meow,” said a sleepy cat named Whiskers, curling up on the porch rug. “Your song is nice, Sunny.”

The door opened, and Grandma May stepped out with a cup of tea. Steam swirled up like a tiny cloud. “What a lovely song,” she said. “It makes the morning feel even kinder.”

Sunny fluttered. Her chest felt warm. She sang a little louder. Trill, tweet, la-la-la!

Then, from the fence, a crow named Cora cawed. “Too loud! Too early!” she called. “My head hurts. Please stop.”

Sunny’s song stopped like a door gently closing. Sunny’s feathers trembled. Her heart went thump-thump. “Oh,” she said softly. “I didn’t mean to bother you.”

Whiskers opened one eye. “I like the song,” he purred.

Cora flapped closer. Her black feathers shone like shiny buttons. “I like music,” she said, “just not big, booming music in the morning. It makes me grumpy.”

Sunny felt a swirl of feelings—sad, shy, and a little brave. She loved her song. Singing was how she said, “This is me.” But she also wanted to be kind.

Grandma May took a slow sip. “Sometimes,” she said, “we use strong voices to say what we need. That is being assertive. Strong and kind at the same time.”

Sunny turned her head. “Assertive?” she asked.

“It means you can say your feelings in a clear, calm way,” Grandma May said. “You can also listen to others.”

Sunny took a deep breath. The porch smelled like cinnamon toast now. She stood taller on the rail. “Cora,” she said, “I want to sing. Singing is important to me. It helps my heart feel light. I hear that your head hurts. I want to be kind to you, too.”

Cora tilted her head. “Thank you for saying it like that,” she said. “I can listen to a gentle song. Just not too loud.”

Sunny thought. “What about this?” she said. “I will sing softly in the early morning. Whisper-songs. Later, when the sun is higher, I will sing my big song. We can choose a time together.”

Whiskers stretched and yawned. “That sounds fair,” he mumbled.

Cora nodded. “Yes. Morning whisper-songs. Afternoon big songs. I can rest now, and I can enjoy later.” Her eyes softened. “I like the plan.”

Sunny smiled. Her feathers smoothed. She felt a calm glow inside. She began a soft song—hummm, trill, tweet—like a tiny bell far away. It was gentle and light, like a feather falling. Cora closed her eyes and breathed easy.

Grandma May listened. “Sunny,” she said, “your voice has many colors. Soft. Round. Bright. Did you know?” She pointed to the porch letters on a little wooden sign that said WELCOME. “Words and songs are both language. We can pick the right size of words and the right size of song for the moment.”

Sunny liked that. Language was like a toolbox. Big words for big feelings. Quiet words for quiet times. Songs that fit the moment. She felt proud. “I used my voice,” she said. “I asked for what I need.”

Later, the sun climbed higher. The porch got warm and golden. Bees buzzed by the flowers. A small girl named Lila came outside with a picture book. “Hi, Sunny,” Lila said. “Is it big-song time now?”

Sunny glanced at Cora. Cora gave a happy caw. “Yes,” Cora said. “My head is better.”

Sunny stood up tall. She looked at her friends. “I will sing my big song now,” she said, clear and calm. “Please tell me if it’s too much.”

Then Sunny sang. Trill, tweet, la-la-la! Her voice rang like sunshine. It was bright and full, but not too loud. It told a story about the porch, the tea, the cat’s purr, and the crow’s shiny feathers. Lila clapped. Whiskers purred louder. Cora swayed on the fence and smiled.

When the song was done, the porch felt even more peaceful. The rocking chair went creak-creak, like a happy heartbeat.

Lila hugged herself. “Sunny, I love how you asked and listened,” she said. “You were brave and kind.”

Sunny nodded. “I can be strong and gentle,” she said. “I can use my words. I can use my songs.”

Grandma May set down her empty cup. “That is assertiveness,” she said. “You said what you needed, you listened to others, and together you found a good plan. That is peaceful communication. And that is the magic of language.”

Sunny’s eyes shone. “Words and songs help hearts understand,” she chirped.

Everyone smiled in the warm light. The porch felt safe and friendly. Sunny looked at the sky and felt proud, brave, and calm.

The End.
