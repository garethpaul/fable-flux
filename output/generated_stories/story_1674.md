# Tessa Tortoise’s Colorful Kitchen Plan

Tessa the wise tortoise lived in a warm kitchen with sunny windows and a soft, striped rug. The kitchen smelled like cinnamon toast and oranges. The kettle sang a tiny song, “Tee-tee-tee.” Pots and pans shined like little moons.

One morning, Tessa rubbed her smooth shell and said, “Good morning, tummy.” Her tummy made a small rumble. “Oh my,” she said softly. “I feel a little slow and sleepy.”

Tessa opened the cupboard. She saw a big jar of sugar cookies. They were round and sparkly. “Yum,” she whispered. “I want cookies for breakfast.” She reached out her gentle tortoise paw.

Just then, she stopped. Tessa was a very wise tortoise, and she liked to be honest with herself. She put her paw on her heart. “Tessa,” she said, “let’s tell the truth. Cookies are a sometimes food. My body needs good fuel first.”

Her tummy rumbled again. “But cookies smell so sweet,” the tummy seemed to say.

Tessa nodded. “I hear you, tummy. Sweet is nice, but we need rainbow power!” She smiled a slow, cozy smile. “We will make a Colorful Crunchy Plate. Then we can have one small cookie after, as a treat. That is kind self-care.”

To make it fun, Tessa decided to craft her meal. She took out paper, crayons, child-safe scissors, and glue. “I will make a Rainbow Plate Plan,” she said. The kettle puffed warm steam, and the toaster gave a friendly pop.

Tessa drew a big circle plate on the paper. She colored it sunny yellow. Then she drew little shapes for foods: red circles for tomato slices, orange sticks for carrots, green leaves for spinach, blue dots for blueberries, and a white cloud for yogurt. She added a brown oval for whole-grain toast. Snip, snip went the scissors. Dab, dab went the glue. She had made a bright menu map.

“Beautiful,” Tessa said. “Now my hands and my mind worked together. Crafting helps me plan.”

She placed the paper on the counter. The counter felt cool and smooth. She washed her hands with warm water and bubbly soap. “Scrub, scrub,” sang Tessa. “Caring for me keeps me safe.”

Tessa opened the fridge. A soft chill tickled her nose. She took out tomatoes, carrots, spinach, blueberries, and a little cup of yogurt. She put bread in the toaster. Pop! The toast smelled nutty and warm.

“Hello, crunchy carrot,” Tessa said as she peeled it. “Hello, juicy tomato.” She tore the spinach into tiny leaves and hummed. She spooned creamy yogurt into a bowl and sprinkled a few blueberries on top. “Blue dots for brain power,” she said.

Her friend, Pip the sparrow, fluttered to the window. “Cheep-cheep! Tessa, are you having cookies for breakfast?” Pip asked.

Tessa looked at the cookie jar. For a tiny moment, she felt shy. She took a deep breath and chose honesty. “I wanted to,” she said, “but my body needs rainbow foods first. I am making a Colorful Crunchy Plate. Then maybe one small cookie.”

Pip hopped closer. “Cheep! That is smart,” he said. “Can I watch?”

“Of course,” said Tessa. She placed the food like a picture. Red tomatoes made a smile. Orange carrot sticks made sunbeams. Green spinach leaves made a grassy hill. The toast became a little bridge, and the yogurt with blueberries was a puffy cloud. “A food rainbow,” Tessa said proudly.

They sat at the cozy table. The sun warmed Tessa’s shell. She took a slow bite. Crunch! The carrot was sweet and crisp. The tomato was juicy and cool. The toast was toasty and soft. The yogurt was creamy, and the blueberries popped like tiny bubbles. Her tummy made a happy sound. “Glug-glug,” it said.

Pip chirped, “How do you feel?”

Tessa closed her eyes and listened to her body. “I feel steady,” she said. “I feel bright and calm. Good food is good fuel.”

After the plate was clean, Tessa poured a small cup of water. “Sip, sip,” she said. “Water helps me, too.” She brushed a crumb off her chin and patted her shell. “Nice self-care, Tessa.”

Pip looked at the cookie jar again. “Cheep… now?”

Tessa smiled. “Yes. I kept my promise. I was honest with myself. I fed my body first. Now I will enjoy one small cookie, slowly.” She took one cookie, broke it in half, and shared with Pip. They nibbled. The cookie was sweet like a tiny party.

Pip fluffed his feathers. “Cheep! Your rainbow plan was yummy and fun.”

Tessa held up her craft. “This plan helps me choose. We can craft our meals. We can care for our bodies. We can be honest about treats.” She taped the Rainbow Plate Plan to the fridge. It made the kitchen look even warmer and happier.

Tessa washed the dishes, humming. The warm water felt silky. She dried her hands on a soft towel. She stretched her legs and did a slow shell wiggle. “I feel ready for my day,” she said.

Then Tessa winked at Pip. “Remember: colors on your plate help you feel great.”

Pip chirped, “Cheep-cheep! Rainbow power!”

They both laughed. The kettle sighed. The kitchen glowed.

The End.
