# Penny Penguin’s Rooftop Parade

Penny was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a bouncy waddle. One sunny morning, she climbed the stairs to the rooftop garden. The air smelled like fresh mint and warm bread from the bakery below. Flags from many places fluttered on strings—red, blue, green, and gold. The rooftop felt smooth and warm under Penny’s little feet.

“Today, we practice our parade,” Penny chirped. “We will play music and march!”

Her friends gathered on the roof. There was Tiko the tiny turtle with a drum, Lila the lamb wearing a flower crown, Mei the monkey with shiny cymbals, and Ayo the parrot with a bright scarf. Each friend came from a different place, and each friend had a different sound.

“Boom-boom,” said Tiko, tapping the drum.

“Cling-clang,” sang Mei’s cymbals.

“Baa-baa,” hummed Lila softly.

“Squawk-squawk!” called Ayo, flapping happily.

Penny held a little whistle. “Tweet!” she tooted. “Let’s start!”

They began to march. The sun felt warm like a cozy blanket. A soft breeze tickled Penny’s feathers. But after a few steps, everything got noisy and mixed up.

“Boom! Boom! BOOM!” Tiko drummed very loud.

“Cling! Clang! CLANG!” Mei crashed the cymbals.

“Baa… um… where do I go?” Lila asked, unsure.

“Squawk! I want to lead!” Ayo cried, flying in circles.

Everyone wanted to be first. Everyone wanted to be loud. The sounds bumped into each other like clunky blocks.

Penny stopped and took a deep breath. “Friends,” she said kindly, “this is not working. Let’s use our words. Let’s negotiate.”

“Ne-go-shi-ate?” Lila repeated.

“It means we talk, we listen, and we make a plan,” Penny said. “We can be patient. We can take turns. We can be brave and share ideas.”

They all nodded. Penny smiled. “First, let’s sit on the soft rug.” The rug was fluffy and felt like clouds. They sat in a circle.

Penny held up one flipper. “Who would like to go first?”

Tiko raised a small foot. “I want to drum the starting beat,” he said. “Boom-boom, boom-boom.”

Mei tilted her head. “I want to crash the big finish,” she said, “Cling-CLANG!”

Lila’s ears wiggled. “I want to sing a gentle part,” she whispered, “Baa-baa-baa.”

Ayo hopped. “I want to lead the march and wave the scarf,” he squawked. “I can fly up high so everyone can see.”

Penny listened to each friend. She nodded and repeated their ideas. “Tiko starts the beat. Mei makes the big finish. Lila sings the gentle part. Ayo leads the march.” She placed a little pebble by each friend as they spoke, to show they had a turn.

“But when?” asked Lila. “And how do we keep it fair?”

Penny tapped her chin. “Let’s make a plan with turns. We can count to five for each part.”

“Count to five,” Tiko said, tapping his drum softly, “One, two, three, four, five.”

“Good practice,” Penny said. “We will go in this order: Ayo leads, Tiko starts the beat, Lila sings, Mei finishes. Then we do it again, and we switch! Next time, Lila leads, Tiko finishes, Ayo sings a call, and Mei starts the beat. Everyone will try new parts. That is fair.”

Ayo blinked. “I can try singing?” he asked.

“Yes,” Penny said. “We can practice. We can be brave.”

Mei smiled. “I can start soft,” she said, tapping the cymbals gently. “Cling… cling…”

They all agreed. The wind rustled the flags from many places. They heard soft music from a radio below—la-la-la in a language Penny did not know. She swayed to the new sound and felt happy to share the roof with friends from many homes.

“Tweet!” Penny tooted the whistle. “Positions!”

Ayo stood tall with the scarf. Tiko tapped his steady boom-boom. Lila took a deep breath and sang a sweet baa-baa-baa. Mei waited, patient and calm, holding her cymbals close.

They marched across the rooftop. Their feet went tap-tap on the tiles. The sun shone. The breeze smelled like oranges from a nearby tree. People on other rooftops waved. A child clapped and called, “Bravo!”

At the count of five, Ayo dipped the scarf, and Tiko nodded to Lila. Lila sang her part, soft and warm like a blanket. Mei watched carefully. When Penny counted to five again, Mei lifted her cymbals and—Cling-CLANG!—a bright, happy finish rang across the sky.

“Yay!” cheered Penny. Everyone smiled. The sounds fit together like puzzle pieces.

“Now we switch,” Penny said. They took turns in new roles. Ayo tried singing a squawky la-la-la. Lila led the march with brave steps. Tiko did a tiny finish, tap-tap. Mei started with a shimmer. They practiced and practiced. Each time, they talked. Each time, they listened. Each time, they waited their turn.

When they rested, they shared crunchy crackers and sweet mango slices. “This tastes like sunshine,” Penny giggled. They told stories from their homes—snowy days, sandy beaches, busy markets, quiet forests. They learned new words and new songs. The rooftop felt like a friendly world.

Penny looked around. “We did it,” she said. “We used our words. We made a plan. We were patient. We were brave. We practiced together.”

Ayo nodded. “Negotiation helped us share.”

Mei added, “Taking turns made our music beautiful.”

Tiko tapped, “Boom-boom for teamwork.”

Lila smiled. “I feel proud.”

Penny hugged her friends. The flags fluttered. The sun dipped a little lower, and the sky turned peach and pink.

“Tomorrow,” Penny said, “we can make a new plan.”

“Yes!” they all cheered.

Lesson: When we talk, listen, and take turns, we can solve problems together. That is negotiation, and it makes space for every voice.

The End.
