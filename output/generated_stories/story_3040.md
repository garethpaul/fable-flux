# Gigi's Special Sorry Gift

Gigi the giraffe had a very long neck and kind brown eyes. She lived with her family in a cozy house with tall ceilings. Every morning, Gigi helped with bathroom chores because she could reach the high shelves.

Today felt different. Gigi's tummy felt twisty and her heart felt heavy. Yesterday, she had been grumpy and said mean words to her little brother Gary. She had called his toy cars "silly" and knocked over his block tower.

"I was not nice," Gigi whispered to herself as she looked in the bathroom mirror. Her reflection looked sad too.

Mama Giraffe poked her head around the door. "Good morning, sweetie. Are you ready to help clean the bathroom?"

"Yes, Mama," Gigi said quietly. But inside, she was thinking about something else. She wanted to say sorry to Gary, but sorry felt like a big, hard word.

As Gigi dusted the high bathroom shelves with her long neck, she had an idea. Her eyes grew bright and sparkly.

"Mama, can I use some of the empty toilet paper tubes?" Gigi asked.

"Of course, dear. What are you planning?" Mama smiled.

"I want to make something special," Gigi said, her voice getting excited.

Gigi gathered three empty tubes. She found colorful paper, safe scissors, and glue. She sat on the bathroom floor and began crafting.

"Snip, snip," went the scissors as she cut the paper. The colorful pieces felt smooth in her hooves. She smelled the fresh, clean scent of the bathroom soap while she worked.

First, she wrapped one tube in red paper. "This will be a fire truck," she said.

Then she covered another tube in blue paper. "This will be a police car."

The last tube got yellow paper. "And this will be a school bus!"

Gigi worked very carefully. When the glue felt sticky on her hooves, she washed them in the sink. The warm water felt good. She dried her hooves on the soft towel.

She drew windows and wheels on each car with her crayons. Her tongue poked out a little as she concentrated hard.

"These are much better than Gary's old cars," she thought. Then she stopped. Her heart felt heavy again. "No, that's not true. Gary's cars are special because they're his."

When Gigi finished, she had three beautiful tube cars. They looked wonderful, but something was still missing.

Gigi took a deep breath. She picked up her handmade cars and walked to Gary's room. Her long legs felt wobbly, like jelly.

"Gary?" she called softly.

Her little brother looked up from playing. His eyes were still a little sad from yesterday.

"I made these for you," Gigi said, holding out the cars. "And I want to say I'm sorry. I was mean yesterday. Your toys aren't silly. They're special because you love them."

Gary's face lit up like sunshine. "Really? These are for me?"

"Yes," Gigi said. "I'm sorry I knocked down your blocks too. Can we build together today?"

Gary hugged Gigi's long leg. "I forgive you, Gigi! These cars are amazing! Will you help me make a city for them?"

Gigi's heart felt light and happy again. "Yes! And next time I feel grumpy, I'll use my words better."

They spent the whole morning building block roads and playing together. The new cars rolled smoothly across the floor, making soft whooshing sounds.

"Thank you for saying sorry," Gary said. "And thank you for making these beautiful cars."

Gigi smiled. Making amends felt much better than staying sorry inside her heart.

The End.
