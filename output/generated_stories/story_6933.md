# Gertie's Great Pantry Problem

Gertie the giraffe stood in the big pantry, her long neck reaching up, up, up to the highest shelf. She could smell the sweet cookies way up there. The chocolate chip cookies smelled so yummy!

"Oh no," Gertie said softly. "The cookies are too high, even for me!"

Gertie stretched her neck as far as she could. She stood on her tippy-toes. But the cookie jar was still too far away on the very top shelf.

Just then, she heard tiny squeaking sounds. "Squeak, squeak, squeak!"

"Who's there?" Gertie asked, looking around the pantry.

Out from behind a bag of flour came three little mice. There was Pip, Pop, and Peanut. They had brown fur and twitchy whiskers.

"We heard you talking," said Pip, the smallest mouse. "We want cookies too!"

"But we're too little to reach anything," squeaked Pop.

"Even the bottom shelf is too high for us," added Peanut sadly.

Gertie thought and thought. She tapped her hoof on the floor. "Hmm," she said. "I have an idea! What if we work together?"

The mice looked excited. "How?" they all asked at once.

"Well," Gertie explained, "I can reach high places, but I can't fit into small spaces. You mice are small and can go anywhere!"

Gertie pointed to a narrow gap between two shelves. "See that space? You could crawl through there and push things down to me!"

"That's a great idea!" squeaked Pip.

"But wait," said Pop. "The cookie jar is still too heavy for us to push."

Gertie smiled. "What if we make a plan together? I could lift you up, and you could open the jar. Then you could pass the cookies down to me!"

The mice wiggled with excitement. "Yes! Yes! Let's try it!"

First, Gertie bent her long neck down low. The three mice climbed up, up, up her neck like it was a bumpy slide. They giggled as they held on tight.

"This is fun!" laughed Peanut.

Gertie slowly lifted her head up to the top shelf. The mice could now reach the big cookie jar.

"I'll hold the jar steady," said Pip.

"I'll open the lid," said Pop.

"And I'll pass the cookies down," said Peanut.

Working together, they opened the heavy lid. The sweet smell of chocolate chips filled the air. One by one, Peanut carefully passed the cookies down to Gertie.

"Catch!" squeaked Peanut as he dropped a cookie.

"Got it!" said Gertie, catching it gently in her mouth.

When they had enough cookies for everyone, Gertie lowered her neck back down. The mice slid down like going down a fun slide.

"Wheee!" they all squeaked happily.

They sat together on the pantry floor, sharing the delicious cookies. The cookies were crunchy and sweet.

"Mmm, these taste even better when we share them," said Gertie.

"Working together was so much fun!" said Pip.

"We each used our special skills," added Pop.

"Your long neck and our small size made the perfect team!" said Peanut.

Gertie smiled big. "You know what? I think we should be pantry partners from now on. When we work together, we can solve any problem!"

The three mice cheered. "Hooray for teamwork!"

From that day on, whenever there was a tricky problem in the pantry, Gertie and her mouse friends worked together. They learned that everyone has special talents, and when you combine them with others, you can do amazing things!

The End.
