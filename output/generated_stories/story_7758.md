# Ellie’s Soft Song

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft, floppy ears. She lived in a comfortable nursery filled with warm light, cozy pillows, and baskets of toys. The rug felt fluffy under her feet, and the air smelled like clean cotton and sweet lavender.

Every afternoon, the music teacher came to the nursery. Ellie loved music. She loved the jingling bells, the humming drum, and the pretty shaker eggs that sounded like raindrops. Today, the teacher smiled and said, “Friends, we will play quiet music first. We will take turns. We will listen and follow directions.”

Ellie’s heart danced. She wanted to shake all the eggs right now. Her trunk wiggled with excitement.

“First we sit,” said the teacher. “Hands in our laps. We breathe in… and out.” The teacher showed how to breathe slowly, like blowing a feather.

Ellie tried, but her trunk reached for a shiny blue shaker egg. “I want to play now,” she whispered. Her toes tapped-tapped on the rug.

“Ellie,” the teacher said gently, “we will all get turns. Let’s practice self-control. Can you hug your trunk and count to five?”

Ellie wrapped her trunk around herself. It felt warm and safe. “One… two… three…” But then she heard the drum go thump-thump, and her trunk uncurled. She grabbed the shaker and shook it hard. Shake-a-shake! Shake-a-shake!

The sound filled the room. It was loud. Too loud. A tiny bunny plush fell off a shelf. Baby Mira covered her ears. “Too big,” she said softly.

Ellie froze. Her tummy felt tight. “Oh,” she said, “I didn’t follow directions.” Her cheeks felt warm. She set the shaker down, and it made a quiet tap against the rug.

The teacher knelt beside Ellie. “Thank you for stopping,” she said kindly. “Let’s solve this together. When we feel excited, we can use a calm plan.”

“What is a calm plan?” Ellie asked, blinking.

“It has three steps,” the teacher said. “Step one: Stop. Step two: Breathe. Step three: Choose a gentle action.”

Ellie nodded slowly. “I can try.”

They practiced. Ellie placed her hands on her soft belly and took a slow breath in through her trunk. In…and out. In…and out. The air smelled like lavender. She felt her tummy rise and fall. Her heart slowed down.

“Now choose a gentle action,” the teacher whispered.

Ellie looked around. She saw Baby Mira peeking out from behind a pillow. “I can help,” Ellie said. She picked up the bunny plush and gave it back. “Here you go, Mira. I am sorry for the loud noise.”

Mira smiled and hugged the bunny. “Thank you,” she said.

Ellie’s tummy felt warm again, but in a good way. Kindness felt like a soft blanket.

“Wonderful,” said the teacher. “You followed directions and used your calm plan.”

The music time started again. “We will play a quiet rain song,” the teacher announced. “We will share instruments. Ellie, you may go second.”

Ellie’s ears perked up. Second! She breathed in and out. She held her hands in her lap. She whispered to herself, “Stop. Breathe. Gentle.” Her toes were still, like little rocks in a garden.

First, Leo the lion cub tapped the drum softly: thump… thump… like a sleepy heartbeat. Ellie listened. The sound was cozy. She felt proud that she was waiting.

Then it was Ellie’s turn. The teacher handed her the blue shaker. “Remember our plan,” she said.

Ellie wrapped her trunk around the shaker. She took a breath. The shaker felt cool and smooth. She moved it very gently. Shhhhh… like soft rain on leaves. The sound made Mira giggle and sway.

“Beautiful,” the teacher said. “Ellie is playing with control.”

Ellie smiled. “I can play softly,” she said. “I can wait, too.”

They finished the rain song with tiny chimes that sang like stars. Everyone clapped. Ellie felt a happy flutter in her chest. She looked at the teacher. “Thank you for helping me,” she said.

“You helped yourself,” the teacher replied. “You stopped, you breathed, and you chose a gentle action. That is self-regulation.”

After music, it was clean-up time. “Please put instruments in the basket,” said the teacher.

Ellie remembered to follow directions. She picked up two shakers and a little bell. She gave one shaker to Leo. “Here you go,” she said. “We can do it together.”

“Thanks, Ellie,” Leo said.

Ellie placed the bell in the basket and felt grateful. “I am thankful for music,” she told the teacher. “I am thankful for my friends. I am thankful for my calm plan.”

They all sat on the rug. The nursery felt peaceful. The light was soft and golden, and the air smelled sweet. The teacher hummed a tiny tune. Ellie hummed along, slow and steady.

“I learned something,” Ellie said. “When I feel too excited, I can stop, breathe, and choose gentle. That helps me, and it helps my friends.”

Everyone nodded. Baby Mira tapped her bunny’s nose. “Gentle,” she said.

Ellie’s ears wiggled happily. She hugged her trunk. She felt calm, kind, and proud.

The End.
