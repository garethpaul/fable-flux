# Whiskers Shares the Rainbow

In the bright creative art room, Whiskers the clever cat sat surrounded by colorful supplies. Paint tubes squeezed out rainbow drops. Brushes stood tall like tiny trees. The smell of fresh paper filled the air.

Whiskers had been working all morning. Her orange paws were covered in purple paint. She was making the most beautiful painting ever - a big rainbow with fluffy white clouds.

"This is perfect!" Whiskers purred, stepping back to admire her work. The colors were so bright they seemed to glow. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple stripes danced across the paper.

Just then, she heard soft sniffling sounds. Behind the easel, a little mouse sat crying. His tiny whiskers trembled. His small paws were empty.

"What's wrong?" asked Whiskers gently, her ears perking up.

"I wanted to paint too," squeaked the mouse. "But there's no more purple paint left. Purple is my favorite color." A big tear rolled down his furry cheek.

Whiskers looked at her painting. She had used lots of purple paint for her beautiful rainbow. The paint tube was completely empty now.

"Oh no," she thought. "I used all the purple paint!"

Then Whiskers noticed something else. A little rabbit hopped over, looking sad too. "I really wanted to paint some carrots," said the rabbit. "But there's no orange paint anywhere."

Whiskers looked down. Her paws were covered in orange paint. She had used that color too.

More animals gathered around. A small bird wanted yellow for sunshine. A tiny frog needed green for lily pads. They all looked so disappointed.

Whiskers felt her heart grow heavy. She had taken all the best colors for herself. Her beautiful rainbow was finished, but her new friends couldn't paint anything.

She looked at her gorgeous painting again. The colors sparkled and shone. It was the most wonderful thing she had ever made.

Then Whiskers had a clever idea. Her whiskers twitched with excitement.

"Wait here!" she called to her friends.

Whiskers carefully picked up her wet painting. The colors were still fresh and gooey. She carried it to the center of the room.

"Everyone, come here!" she announced.

The mouse, rabbit, bird, and frog gathered around curiously.

"My painting has all the colors you need," said Whiskers with a big smile. "Let's share! You can dip your brushes right into my rainbow!"

The mouse's eyes grew wide. "Really? You would let us use your beautiful painting?"

"Of course!" purred Whiskers. "Art is more fun when we share it together!"

Soon, all the animals were carefully dipping their brushes into Whiskers' rainbow painting. The mouse painted purple flowers. The rabbit made orange carrots. The bird created a yellow sun. The frog added green grass.

The art room filled with happy chatter and laughter. Brushes swished and colors mixed. The sweet smell of paint grew stronger.

"Thank you, Whiskers!" said all the animals together.

Whiskers felt warm and fuzzy inside. Her rainbow painting was now covered with everyone's artwork, but it looked even more beautiful than before. It showed flowers, carrots, sunshine, and grass all mixed with her rainbow.

"This is much better than painting alone," Whiskers realized. "When we share, everyone gets to create something wonderful!"

From that day on, Whiskers always made sure to save plenty of paint for her friends. The art room became a place where everyone shared and helped each other make beautiful things together.

The End.
