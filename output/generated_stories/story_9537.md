# Rizzo’s Thank-You Song

Rizzo was a musical raccoon with soft gray fur and bright curious eyes. He loved to make music. He tapped spoons, hummed little tunes, and played rhythms on pots and pans. Today, Rizzo stood in the dining room. The room smelled like warm bread. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. The table was big and round, and the chairs made soft scrapes on the wood.

“I feel a song in my paws,” Rizzo said. He patted his tummy. “Thum-thum, tap-tap!”

On the table sat a bowl of rice, a plate of carrots, and a glass of cool water. Rizzo climbed onto a chair and wiggled with joy. He held two spoons like drum sticks.

“Today,” he said, “I will sing my Thank-You Song.”

Rizzo tapped the edge of a bowl. Ting! He tapped a cup. Tock! He made a gentle beat. Ting-tock, ting-tock.

He began to sing in a warm, clear voice. “Thank you, bread, soft and brown. Thank you, carrots, crunchy sound. Thank you, water, cool and bright. You help me feel just right!”

He paused and sniffed. The carrots smelled sweet. The bread smelled cozy. The water looked like a tiny lake in a glass. Rizzo smiled.

From under the table, he heard a little squeak. It was Pip, a tiny mouse friend. “What are you doing, Rizzo?” Pip asked, peeking out with shiny eyes.

“I’m singing thanks,” said Rizzo. “It makes my heart feel big.”

Pip tilted her head. “Who do you thank?”

Rizzo tapped a spoon on the table, gently. “I thank the food,” he said. “And I thank the helpers.”

“Helpers?” Pip squeaked.

“Yes,” Rizzo said. “Look with your heart.”

Rizzo closed his eyes and sang again, his voice floaty and kind. “Thank you, farmer who grew the grain. Thank you, sun and gentle rain. Thank you, hands that washed and cut. Thank you, drivers, doors they shut.”

Pip sat on a napkin and listened. The napkin was soft, like a cloud. Rizzo’s song filled the dining room like warm light.

Pip smiled. “I like that. The world is big, and many help us.”

“Yes,” Rizzo said. “That is global awareness.” He said the words slowly. “It means we see the big world and the many helpers in it.”

Pip nodded. “Can I sing too?”

“Of course!” said Rizzo. He tapped the glass. Ting! “Let’s try a new verse.”

Pip sang in a tiny voice, “Thank you, bees, who visit flowers. Thank you, cooks, who use their powers. Thank you, friends who share a seat. Thank you, neighbors on our street.”

“Beautiful!” said Rizzo. “You have a brave voice.”

Just then, Rizzo noticed crumbs on the table and a little spill of water. He felt a small frown, then took a deep breath. “I can help,” he said. “I can be responsible.”

“What does that mean?” asked Pip.

“It means I help take care of our space,” Rizzo said. “It is civic responsibility. We share this dining room. We keep it clean for everyone.”

Rizzo grabbed a cloth and wiped the water. Swish, swish. He picked up the crumbs. Crinkle, crinkle. He hummed as he worked. “When I help, I feel proud,” he said.

Pip hopped to help. She moved tiny crumbs into a little pile. “Teamwork!” she squeaked.

Rizzo smiled. “We can grow and learn every day,” he said. “That is a growth mindset. If I make a mess, I can fix it. If I forget to say thank you, I can try again.”

He climbed down from the chair and looked around. The table was tidy. The room felt calm and bright. The spoons were clean and made a happy clink when he set them down.

Rizzo took a sip of water. The glass felt cool in his paws. “Ahh,” he said. “I am grateful.”

Pip swished her tail. “What is grateful?”

“Grateful is a warm feeling,” Rizzo said. “It is when we notice good things and say thank you. It is like sunshine inside.”

Pip touched her chest. “I feel it,” she said softly.

Rizzo smiled and began the last part of his song. “Thank you, world, both near and far. Thank you, helpers, where you are. Thank you, friends, who sing with me. Thank you, home, for safety.”

He added a beat on the table with his paws. Tap, tap. He made a soft whoosh with his breath like wind. Whoooosh. The sounds made Pip giggle.

“Let’s share our song,” Pip said. “We can teach others to say thank you.”

“Yes,” Rizzo said. “We can share at dinner time. We can share at clean-up time. We can share with neighbors. We can share with family.”

They both held their heads high. They felt big inside, like balloons full of kind air.

Rizzo looked at the table one more time. “Ready to eat?” he asked.

“Yes,” Pip said. “But first—thank you.”

Rizzo grinned. “Thank you,” he echoed. Together, they bowed to the food, to the helpers they could not see, and to each other.

They ate their meal slowly, tasting each bite. Crunch. Sip. Mmm. The dining room was full of peace, music, and smiles.

The lesson was clear in Rizzo’s heart: be grateful, help your home, and remember the big world that helps you grow. Every day is a chance to learn and say, “Thank you.”

The End.
