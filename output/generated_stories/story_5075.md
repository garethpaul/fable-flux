# Koko's Big Helper Day

Koko the koala lived in a cozy house with his family. Today was very special because Grandma Rosa was coming to visit from far away! She spoke Spanish and made the most delicious empanadas. Koko loved when Grandma Rosa came to stay in their guest room.

"Koko," called Mama from the hallway. "We need to clean the guest room for Grandma Rosa. Will you help me?"

Koko was playing with his favorite toy car. The little red car made fun "vroom vroom" sounds as he pushed it across the soft carpet. "Just five more minutes, Mama!" he called back.

Mama walked into the living room, her arms full of fresh, white sheets that smelled like sunshine. "Koko, I really need your help now. Grandma Rosa will be here soon!"

But Koko kept playing. The toy car was so much fun! He didn't want to stop.

An hour later, Papa came home from the store with flowers for Grandma Rosa. The bright yellow sunflowers smelled sweet and looked cheerful. "How's the guest room coming along?" Papa asked.

Mama looked tired. Her hair was a little messy, and she had been working all by herself. "I'm still cleaning. Koko didn't want to help."

Koko looked up from his toy. He saw Mama's sad face and felt a funny feeling in his tummy. It wasn't a good feeling.

Just then, the doorbell rang! "Ding dong!" 

"¡Hola, mi familia!" called Grandma Rosa from outside. Her voice was warm and happy.

Koko ran to the window and saw Grandma Rosa with her big, colorful suitcase. She was wearing her favorite purple dress with tiny flowers on it.

But when they opened the guest room door, it wasn't ready! The bed had no sheets, toys were scattered on the floor, and dust made Papa sneeze. "Achoo!"

Grandma Rosa smiled kindly, but Koko could see she was tired from her long trip. "It's okay, mijos," she said gently. "We can fix this together."

Koko felt terrible. His tummy felt heavy, like he had eaten too many cookies. "I'm sorry, Mama," he said in a small voice. "I should have listened. I should have helped with the chores."

Mama gave him a gentle hug. "Thank you for saying sorry, Koko. Would you like to help now?"

"Yes!" Koko said, jumping up. "What can I do?"

"You can put the pillows on the bed," Mama said. "And maybe pick up those toys?"

Koko worked very hard. He fluffed the soft, fluffy pillows until they looked perfect. He picked up every single toy and put them in the toy box. The toys made little "clink" sounds as they went in.

Papa swept the floor while Grandma Rosa arranged her beautiful flowers in a vase. The room began to smell fresh and clean.

"Now we need sheets," said Mama.

Koko helped stretch the clean sheets across the bed. They worked together, pulling and tucking until the bed looked cozy and inviting.

"¡Perfecto!" said Grandma Rosa, clapping her hands. "What wonderful helpers you all are!"

Koko felt proud and happy. His tummy felt light and good again. "Chores are more fun when we do them together," he said.

That night, Grandma Rosa told them stories in Spanish and English while they all snuggled in the perfectly clean guest room. Koko learned that helping with chores shows love for family, and saying sorry makes everything better.

The End.
