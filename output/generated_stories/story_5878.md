# Milo the Brave and the Pantry Parade

Milo was a courageous monkey with bright eyes and soft, brown fur. One rainy afternoon, he tiptoed into the pantry. The pantry smelled like warm bread and sweet bananas. It was quiet except for the tiny hum of the fridge and the soft tap-tap of rain.

“Hello, pantry,” Milo whispered. “Let’s use our imagination!”

Milo loved to pretend. He tied a red napkin around his neck like a cape. “I am Captain Milo!” he cheered. He climbed onto a sturdy stool and looked around. Jars, boxes, and baskets lined the shelves.

But there was a problem. The snack basket sat on the highest shelf. “I want to share snacks with my friends,” Milo said. “But it’s too high. I can’t reach.”

Milo took a deep breath. “I will be brave. I will use my mind.” He touched his chest. “I have courage inside me.” He touched his head. “I have ideas inside me.”

He looked at the items around him. A box of spaghetti poked out like a wand. A whisk shone like a silver flower. “What can help?” he wondered.

Just then, two friends peeked in. Tia the tiny turtle wore a bright blue bow. Zuzu the zebra had zigzag stripes and kind eyes.

“Hi, Milo!” said Tia. “What are you doing?”

“I want to share snacks,” Milo said. “But the basket is too high.”

“We can help,” said Zuzu. “We are all different. We have different strengths.”

Milo’s eyes sparkled. “Let’s make a Pantry Parade! We will use our imagination to build a helper tool.”

“What is a helper tool?” asked Tia.

“It is something we build to solve a problem,” Milo explained. “It helps us do a job safely.”

They looked and listened. The pantry was full of sounds and colors. Crinkly bags rustled like leaves. Smooth jars clicked softly. The floor felt cool under their feet and paws.

“Let’s practice our skills,” Milo said. “We can try, test, and try again.”

Milo spotted a long wooden spoon. “This could be our brave stick,” he said.

Tia found a sturdy cardboard tube. “This can be our tunnel piece,” she said in her tiny voice.

Zuzu nudged a roll of tape. “Tape can hold things together,” he said proudly.

They worked as a team. Milo held the spoon. Tia slid the tube over the spoon. Zuzu wrapped the tape around and around. “Wrap, wrap, wrap,” Zuzu sang. The tape felt sticky and made a funny rip sound.

Milo gave the tool a name. “We call it the Reacher.” He grinned. “Let’s test it.”

He lifted the Reacher toward the snack basket. The basket wobbled. “Whoops!” Milo said. “Not safe yet.”

“Let’s try again,” said Tia. “Slow and steady.”

Zuzu had an idea. “I can hold the stool so it does not wiggle. My legs are strong.”

“I will guide the Reacher,” said Tia. “My eyes are close to the shelf.”

“And I will lift and listen,” said Milo. “I will be brave and gentle.”

They tried again. The stool stood steady under Zuzu’s hooves. Tia pointed with her small, careful flipper. “A little to the left,” she said softly.

Milo moved the Reacher. The wooden spoon tip touched the basket handle. “I feel it,” he whispered. His hands felt warm and his heart thumped, thump, thump.

“Now pull,” said Zuzu.

Milo pulled slowly. The basket slid forward with a soft shhh sound. “I’ve got it,” Milo said. “Almost there.”

The basket came down into Milo’s arms. “We did it!” he cheered. The pantry filled with happy sounds—claps, giggles, and the crinkle of paper.

Milo opened the basket. There were bananas, crackers, and little cups of yogurt. “Let’s share,” he said. “Sharing makes the snack taste better.”

They set a small picnic on the pantry floor. Milo peeled a banana. The sweet smell made their noses wiggle. Tia crunched a cracker. “Crunch, crunch!” Zuzu dipped a spoon into yogurt. “Mmm,” he hummed.

“You were so brave,” Tia told Milo.

“You were so steady,” Milo told Zuzu.

“And you were so careful,” Zuzu told Tia.

Milo smiled. “We are different, and that helps us. Your careful eyes, your strong legs, and my brave ideas made a great team.”

Tia nodded. “We used our imagination to solve a problem.”

Zuzu swished his tail. “And we practiced skills—looking, thinking, building, and trying again.”

Milo wrapped the red napkin around all three of them like a cozy cape. “We are the Pantry Parade Team,” he said. “When we have a problem, we can imagine, plan, and try together.”

They finished their snack. Milo put the basket back on a low shelf where everyone could reach. He patted the Reacher and set it safely in a corner.

“Today I learned that imagination is a power inside me,” Milo said. “It helps me be brave, kind, and smart.”

“And sharing makes our hearts feel full,” said Tia.

“And our differences make us strong,” said Zuzu.

Milo nodded. “We solved the problem by using our minds and our hearts.”

The rain stopped. A soft light peeked into the pantry. The friends hugged. Their bellies were full, and their smiles were warm.

The End.
