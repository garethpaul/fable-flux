# Ping the Panda's Rooftop Game

Ping the panda lived on top of a tall apartment building. His black and white fur was soft and fluffy. His round belly jiggled when he walked. Ping loved to play on the rooftop every day.

The rooftop had colorful chalk lines drawn everywhere. There were circles, squares, and long straight paths. This was Ping's special sports playground high up in the sky.

Today, Ping bounced his red rubber ball. *Bounce, bounce, bounce!* The ball made happy sounds on the concrete. The warm sunshine felt good on his fur.

"I love playing ball!" Ping said to himself. He threw the ball high into the blue sky and caught it with his paws.

Just then, three little raccoons climbed up the fire escape. They had gray masks around their eyes and striped tails that swished back and forth.

"Can we play too?" asked the first raccoon named Ruby.

Ping stopped bouncing his ball. His tummy felt tight. He had never shared his rooftop games before.

"This is MY playground," Ping said. "You can't play here."

The raccoons' ears drooped down. Their whiskers twitched sadly.

"But we love sports too," said the second raccoon named Rex. "We brought our own ball."

"No!" Ping hugged his red ball tight. "Go find your own place to play!"

The raccoons sat down in the corner. They looked very sad. The wind blew gently across the rooftop, making small whistling sounds.

Ping tried to keep playing alone. But something felt wrong. The ball didn't bounce as high. The sunshine didn't feel as warm. Even the pretty blue sky looked gray to him.

He watched the raccoons sitting quietly. They weren't causing trouble. They just wanted to have fun like him.

Ping's patient heart began to think. "Maybe sharing could be good," he whispered to himself.

He walked slowly over to the raccoons. His paws made soft padding sounds on the rooftop.

"I'm sorry I was mean," Ping said. "Would you like to play ball with me?"

Ruby's eyes lit up like stars. "Really? You want to share?"

"Yes," Ping nodded. "We can all play together."

The third raccoon named Rosie clapped her tiny paws. "Thank you, Ping!"

They started a fun game of catch. Ping threw the red ball to Ruby. Ruby tossed it to Rex. Rex passed it to Rosie. Rosie rolled it back to Ping.

*Bounce, catch, throw, giggle!* The sounds of friendship filled the rooftop.

"This is more fun than playing alone!" Ping laughed. His belly jiggled with joy.

They played hopscotch on the chalk lines. They had races around the rooftop. They even invented a new game called "Rooftop Tag."

When the sun began to set, painting the sky orange and pink, all four friends sat together.

"Thank you for sharing your playground," said Ruby.

"Thank you for teaching me that sharing makes everything better," Ping replied. "Sports are more fun with friends!"

From that day on, Ping and the raccoons played together every afternoon. The rooftop became filled with laughter, games, and friendship.

Ping learned that being patient and kind made him the happiest panda in the whole city.

The End.
