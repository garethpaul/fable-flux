# Penny Penguin and the Attic Treasure

Penny was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a warm scarf. One rainy afternoon, she waddled up the creaky stairs to the quiet attic. The attic smelled like old books and soft dust. Sunlight slipped through a tiny window and made sparkly dust dance in the air.

“Oh! What a cozy place,” Penny said. Her voice sounded gentle and small in the quiet room.

She opened a wooden trunk. Squeak! Inside were colorful buttons, shiny ribbons, and tiny bells. There were also fuzzy mittens, a small drum, and a box of crayons.

“This is like a treasure chest,” Penny whispered, smiling. She loved the soft feel of the ribbons and the jingling sound of the bells. She picked up a blue button. It felt cool and smooth in her flipper.

Just then, her friends peeked in. Tilly the tiny mouse sniffed the air with her whiskers. “It smells like cookies,” Tilly giggled.

“It smells like old blankets,” said Gus the gentle goose, stretching his long neck.

“It sounds so quiet,” hummed Bea the bunny, twitching her ears.

Penny waved. “Come in! I found treasures.”

Everyone stepped onto the soft, dusty rug. The floorboards went creak, creak. The rain went patter-patter on the roof. Penny felt happy. She liked sharing the cozy feeling with her friends.

Tilly pointed at the shiny ribbons. “Can I have the red one? It would make my tail fancy!”

Bea looked at the crayons. “I want the purple crayon,” she said.

Gus eyed the tiny bells. “I want all the bells,” he honked. “All for me!”

Penny paused. She loved the bells too. She loved the way they went jingle-jingle. She held a bell and shook it softly. Ting-a-ling! Her heart felt warm. But then she looked at her friends. Tilly’s eyes were bright. Bea’s ears were wiggly with hope. Gus’s wings twitched with excitement.

Penny took a deep breath. “I want to be generous,” she said to herself. “Sharing is kind.”

She smiled and said, “There are many treasures. Let’s share. We can make things together.”

Gus frowned a little. “But I wanted all the bells.”

Penny nodded kindly. “I hear you, Gus. Bells are fun. But when we share, we can make more music. We can make a band!”

Bea clapped. “A band!”

Tilly squeaked. “A craft band!”

Penny giggled. “A craft-and-music band!”

They made a plan. Plans help everyone feel calm and included.

“First,” Penny said, “we take turns. That is cooperation.”

“Second,” Bea added, “we use gentle hands. That is compassion.”

“Third,” Tilly whispered, “we try again if something is hard. That is a growth mindset. We can say, ‘I can learn!’”

Gus looked at the bells. He looked at his friends. He took a slow breath. “Okay,” he said. “I can share the bells. I can learn.”

Penny patted his wing. “Thank you, Gus. That is generous.”

They began. Tilly tied a red ribbon around her tail. Swish! Bea colored a big purple heart on a piece of paper. Gus shook one bell. Ting! Penny tied bells to a string. Jingle-jingle!

Soon the attic was full of happy sounds. Crayons went scratch-scratch on paper. Bells went ting-a-ling. The little drum went dum, dum, dum. The air smelled like crayons and old books. The sun made warm golden squares on the floor.

Penny passed out mittens. “These are so soft,” she said, rubbing one on her cheek. “Who needs warm flippers or paws?”

Tilly’s nose twitched. “My paws get cold,” she said.

Penny gave Tilly a mitten. “Here you go.”

Gus shivered. “My wings get chilly.”

Penny wrapped a scarf around Gus’s neck. “Try this.” Gus smiled and stood taller.

Bea peered into the trunk. “There are only two bells left,” she said.

Gus picked up one and gave it to Bea. “For you,” he said softly. “I am practicing generosity.”

Bea’s eyes shone. “Thank you, Gus.” She shook the bell and smiled at the bright sound.

They worked together to make a picture and a song. Penny drew a big house with an open door. Bea drew friends holding hands. Tilly glued buttons like rainbow stones along a path. Gus tied one last ribbon on the picture and hummed.

At last, they all stood back. The picture was beautiful and kind. It showed friends sharing, helping, and smiling. The attic felt warm and full of love.

Penny said, “Let’s sing our sharing song.”

They sang:
“We share, we care,
We help, we try.
With open hearts,
Our joys can fly.”

Their voices were soft and sweet in the quiet attic. The rain slowed. Drip, drop. The dust sparkles danced.

Gus said, “I wanted all the bells. But sharing feels better. My heart feels big.”

Tilly nodded. “When we share, everyone gets a turn.”

Bea added, “When we help, we all learn.”

Penny hugged her friends. “That is generosity. When we give, our hearts grow. We can be kind, we can cooperate, and we can try again.”

They placed the picture by the tiny window. Sunlight made it glow. The attic was still quiet, but now it was full of happy feelings.

Penny whispered, “Today we learned to share.”

Everyone smiled.

The End.
