# Whiskers Welcomes a Guest

Whiskers was a clever cat with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a cozy house with a sunny window and a comfy guest room. The guest room smelled like clean sheets and warm soap. The bed was fluffy, like a cloud. A little night-light made a gentle glow on the wall.

One day, Whiskers heard a new sound. Tap-tap-tap. Swish-swish. He lifted his head and sniffed the air. The guest room door was open. A small suitcase rolled across the floor. A scarf fluttered like a butterfly. And in walked… a tiny hedgehog!

“Hello,” said the hedgehog in a quiet voice. “My name is Hazel. I’m here to stay for a little visit.”

Whiskers blinked. He had never shared his favorite room. The guest room was where he napped, stretched, and lined up his toy mice. He curled his tail around his paws and thought, Think, think. Be kind first. Ask questions. Learn.

“Hi, Hazel,” Whiskers said. “I’m Whiskers. The guest room is soft and safe. It smells like lavender. Would you like some water?”

“Yes, please,” Hazel said with a shy smile.

Whiskers padded to the kitchen. The floor felt cool on his paws. He poured water into a little bowl. Drip, drip, drip. He also brought a tiny saucer with crunchy apple slices. Crunch! Hazel liked those.

Back in the guest room, Hazel set down her suitcase. She touched the quilt with her little paws. “It’s so puffy,” she whispered. “But I feel a little prickly. I don’t know where to put my things.”

Whiskers looked around. He saw his toy mice lined up on the pillow. He saw his favorite blanket, striped and warm, folded at the end of the bed. He felt a little tight feeling in his chest. This is my spot, he thought. Then he took a slow breath. In. Out.

Kindness is sharing. Tolerance is making space. Critical thinking is solving puzzles, he reminded himself.

“We can make a plan,” Whiskers said. “Let’s think together.”

Hazel’s eyes brightened. “A plan?”

Whiskers nodded. “First, let’s find a place for your things. Second, let’s make both of us cozy. Third, let’s set some gentle rules.”

Hazel giggled. “That sounds smart.”

Whiskers pointed with his paw. “The bottom drawer is empty. It smells like cedar. You can use it for your scarf and socks. My toy mice can move to this basket.” He nudged a woven basket that sat beside the bed. It made a soft rustle. “And my striped blanket can stay here, but we can share it. It’s big.”

Hazel carefully opened the drawer. It slid with a shhhh sound. She folded her scarf and placed it inside. “Thank you,” she said. “I was worried I would be in the way.”

“You are not in the way,” Whiskers said kindly. “You are our guest.”

Hazel smiled wider. Then she looked at the pillow. “Um,” she said. “Sometimes my quills poke out when I sleep. I don’t want to hurt your pillow.”

Whiskers sat and thought again. Think, think. Be kind. Solve the puzzle. He looked at the comfy chair near the window. Sunlight made a warm square on the cushion.

“I have an idea,” Whiskers said. “We can put a folded towel on the pillow to keep it safe. And you can sleep with the towel on your side. I’ll sleep on the chair in the sun. It’s very warm. I like the sun.”

Hazel nodded. “That’s a good plan. I brought a soft towel.” She unzipped her suitcase. Zip! She lifted out a bright yellow towel that smelled like lemon.

Together, they tucked the towel onto the pillow. Pat, pat. They both felt better.

“Would you like to play a game?” Hazel asked. “We can build a little fort with the quilt.”

Whiskers purred. Rrrr. “Yes! Let’s make a kindness fort.”

They draped the quilt over the chair and the bed. It became a cozy tent. The night-light made the inside glow like a tiny sunrise. Whiskers brought a book with big pictures. Hazel brought a small flashlight that clicked on with a soft tick.

Inside the quilt fort, they read and whispered.

“Once there was a seed,” Whiskers read. “It needed water and care.”

“Like me,” Hazel said. “I needed a kind welcome.”

Whiskers nodded. “Everyone needs kind care.”

Later, Whiskers heard footsteps in the hall. His human peeked in. “How’s my clever cat and our new guest?”

Whiskers lifted his head proudly. “We made a plan. We shared space. We used gentle rules.”

Hazel added, “And we made a fort!”

The human smiled. “I see kindness. I see teamwork.”

That night, the guest room was peaceful. The lemony towel felt safe. The quilt fort rustled softly. The night-light glow was warm. Whiskers curled on the sun-warmed chair, even though the sun had set. Hazel rested on the pillow, snug and careful.

“Good night, Hazel,” Whiskers purred.

“Good night, Whiskers,” Hazel whispered.

In the quiet, Whiskers felt happy. He had been kind. He had made space for someone different. He had used his thinking to solve a problem.

In the morning, the room smelled like pancakes. Whiskers and Hazel stretched and smiled. They had a new day and a new friend.

The End.
