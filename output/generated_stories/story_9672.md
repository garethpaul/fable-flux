# Gigi's Big Helper Day

Gigi the giraffe sat tall in the family car. Her long neck bent down so she could look out the window. The car went bump-bump over the road. Mom sat in the front seat, driving carefully.

"We're almost at Grandma's house," Mom said with a smile. "She really needs our help today."

Gigi's tummy felt funny. Not sick-funny, but worried-funny. Her spots seemed to wiggle with nervousness. "What if I can't help?" Gigi whispered. "What if I'm too tall? What if I break something?"

The car smelled like Mom's sweet perfume and the apple slices in Gigi's snack bag. Outside, trees whooshed past the windows. Birds flew high in the blue sky.

"Oh, sweetheart," Mom said gently. "You have special gifts that make you perfect for helping."

When they arrived, Grandma came outside. Her gray hair sparkled in the sunshine. "My dear Gigi!" she called out. "I'm so happy you're here!"

Gigi stepped out of the car slowly. Her long legs felt shaky. "Hi, Grandma," she said quietly.

Grandma pointed to her tall apple tree. "I need to pick apples for my pie, but they're too high up. The ladder is broken."

Gigi looked up, up, up at the tree. The red apples hung from the highest branches. They looked like little Christmas ornaments swaying in the breeze.

"I can't reach those," Grandma sighed. "They're just too high."

Gigi's ears perked up. Wait! She looked at her long, long neck. She looked at the tall tree. Suddenly, her tummy stopped feeling funny.

"Grandma," Gigi said, standing up straighter. "I think I can help!"

Gigi walked to the apple tree. She stretched her beautiful long neck up toward the branches. Her nose could almost touch the apples! They smelled sweet and crisp.

"Careful, dear," Mom called out.

Gigi gently used her soft lips to pick the biggest, reddest apple. Plop! It fell safely into Grandma's basket below.

"Wonderful!" Grandma clapped her hands. "You're the perfect helper!"

One by one, Gigi picked the high apples. Her neck didn't feel too long anymore. It felt just right! Each apple made a happy plop sound in the basket.

A little blue bird landed on Gigi's head. "Thank you for helping," the bird chirped. "Now I can eat the apple pieces that fall!"

Soon the basket overflowed with shiny red apples. Grandma hugged Gigi's long legs. "I couldn't have done this without you, my tall helper!"

Back in the car, Gigi felt warm and happy inside. Her spots seemed to shine brighter. The car smelled like fresh apples now.

"I'm proud of you," Mom said. "You used your special gift to help someone you love."

Gigi smiled big. "My long neck isn't too long at all. It's just perfect for helping!"

As they drove home, Gigi looked out at the tall trees. She wasn't worried about being different anymore. Being tall made her special. Being different made her helpful.

"Next time someone needs help reaching high places," Gigi said happily, "I'll be ready!"

The car rolled smoothly down the road, carrying one very confident little giraffe home.

The End.
