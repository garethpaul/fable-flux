# Benny Beaver’s Colorful Plan

Benny was a busy beaver with bright eyes and a big, warm smile. Today, Benny padded into the creative art room. The room smelled like fresh paper and lemony clean tables. Sunlight made soft squares on the floor. Paints gleamed in little cups—red, yellow, blue. Paper rustled like leaves. Brushes felt soft like tiny tails.

“Today I will make everything!” Benny said. “A painting, a clay bear, a paper crown, and a glitter river!” His tail thumped with happy taps.

Miss Dot, the kind art teacher, clapped softly. “Hello, artists! We have one hour to make art. One hour is from the big hand to the next big hand on the clock.” She pointed to the round clock on the wall. Tick-tock, tick-tock.

Benny bounced to the paint table. “I will paint a tall tree!” He dipped his brush in green, then red, then blue. “Oh! Maybe a rainbow tree!” He swirled and splashed. Splat! Some paint landed on his paw. It felt cool and creamy.

“Hi, Benny,” said Mia Mouse, rolling clay. “Want to make clay animals with me later?”

“Yes!” said Benny. “I also want to make a crown and a glitter river and—” He looked around. So many colors! So many things!

Tick-tock, tick-tock, went the clock.

Benny ran to the clay. “Clay bear time!” He squished and squashed. The clay was squishy and a little cold. “Maybe a clay boat too?” He started a bear, then poked the clay into a boat, then back to a bear.

“Are you okay?” asked Jay Jay, a small blue jay, folding paper.

“I am busy!” Benny said with a grin. But his eyes felt a little wiggly. His tree painting looked half done. His clay bear looked like a blob. His paper crown was still just flat paper. His glitter river was only a sprinkle.

Tick-tock, tick-tock.

Miss Dot walked by with a gentle smile. “Benny, you have many ideas. Ideas are wonderful. Let’s make a plan to help your ideas grow.”

“A plan?” Benny’s ears perked.

“Yes,” Miss Dot said. She placed three picture cards on the table. One card showed a paintbrush, one showed clay, and one showed a crown. She drew tiny boxes under each picture. “First, choose one thing. When you finish, put a check in a box. Then you can do the next thing.”

Benny took a deep breath. He smelled the sweet glue and the soapy water. “Okay. I choose the painting first.”

Mia Mouse nodded. “I can help wash brushes when you’re done.”

“Thank you,” Benny said. He felt calm. He picked the green brush. Swish, swish. The brush whispered on the paper. He added a brown trunk—drag, drag. He tapped on red apples—dot, dot, dot. He painted a blue sky—soft swoops. “My tree is done!” He drew a tiny check in the box under the brush picture. Check!

Tick-tock, tick-tock.

“Now clay,” Benny said. He rolled the clay into a ball—roll, roll. He pinched ears—pinch, pinch. He poked eyes—poke, poke. “Hello, little bear.” The clay felt firm now. Mia smiled. “I’ll carry it to the shelf.”

“Thanks, Mia!” Benny made a check in the clay box. Check!

Tick-tock, tick-tock.

Jay Jay fluttered over with shiny paper. “Would you like help cutting the crown points?”

“Yes, please,” said Benny. “Then I will add stickers.” Snip, snip went the scissors in Jay Jay’s careful wings. Benny pressed star stickers—press, press. He added two puffs—poof, poof. “My crown is ready!” He checked the crown box. Check!

The clock went tick-tock and felt friendly now, not rushy. Benny smiled. “I did three things! I have a little time left.”

Miss Dot pointed to a small hourglass with golden sand. “This shows five more minutes. Would you like to use them?”

Benny nodded. “I will make a tiny glitter river for the bottom of my tree.” He spread glue—squiggle, squiggle. He sprinkled glitter—shhh, like rain. The glitter sparkled like tiny stars.

“Beautiful,” said Miss Dot. “You used your time well.”

Benny puffed with pride. “Making a plan helped me finish.”

Mia put on the crown and twirled. “We made art together!”

Jay Jay chirped, “Your checks helped you see what to do next.”

Benny looked around. Everyone’s art was different and bright. Paper flowers, clay turtles, painted suns. “I like how we all did our own art,” he said. “We helped each other, too.”

Miss Dot rang a soft bell. “Artists, it is time to clean up.” Benny checked the table for paint drops. He wiped—wipe, wipe. He put brushes in the water—swish. He returned the cards to Miss Dot. “Thank you for the plan.”

“You’re welcome,” Miss Dot said. “You made a plan, worked step by step, and you finished. That is great time management.”

Benny nodded. “Time management means I choose, I finish, and I help my friends. It feels good.”

On the way out, Benny touched his crown and waved his little clay bear hello. His tail thumped a happy beat. “Tomorrow,” he said, “I will make a new plan.”

He looked at Mia and Jay Jay. “We can plan together. Everyone can join.”

“We will!” said Mia.

“Count me in!” chirped Jay Jay.

The sun still made soft squares on the floor. The clock went tick-tock, tick-tock, like a gentle song. Benny felt calm, proud, and included with his friends. He learned that making a simple plan, taking turns with tasks, and helping others makes busy time feel just right.

The End.
