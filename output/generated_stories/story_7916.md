# Birdie Sings and Writes

Birdie was a small, bright blue singing bird who lived in a playful living room. The room had a soft yellow rug, a squishy red couch, and a low table with big crayons in a cup. Sunlight made warm stripes on the floor. You could hear the clock go tick-tock, tick-tock. Birdie loved to sing and chirp. Today, Birdie had a plan.

“I will write my song!” Birdie chirped, fluffing soft feathers. “I will write it down so I can remember. I will practice, and I will finish.”

Birdie hopped to the table. The crayons smelled like wax and fruit. The paper was smooth and white. Birdie picked up a big blue crayon in tiny claws.

Scratch, scratch! The crayon made a happy sound. Birdie drew a big music note. “Laaa!” Birdie sang. “This is my first line.”

Just then, the squishy red couch made a bounce. Thump! Pillow the pillow toppled over. “Play time?” asked Pillow in a muffled voice.

Birdie took a deep breath. “I want to play later,” Birdie said kindly. “Right now, I am writing my song. I will finish my task.”

Pillow rolled to the side. “Okay! I can wait,” Pillow said. “You can do it!”

Birdie smiled and looked at the paper again. “La, la, la,” Birdie sang softly. “How do I write that?” The crayon wiggled. Birdie made some curvy lines. It was tricky.

Birdie felt a flutter in the tummy. “This is hard,” Birdie whispered.

From the shelf, a small clock said, “Tick-tock. Take your time. Practice helps.”

Birdie nodded. “I will practice.” Birdie sang just one sound, “La.” Then Birdie drew one small circle. Then another. Little steps felt good.

The window let in a warm breeze. Birdie smelled fresh air and dust and crayons. It felt cozy and calm. “I can do this,” Birdie said.

Just then, the toy drum rolled over. Boom-ba, boom-ba. “Let’s make noise!” the drum boomed.

Birdie stood tall on tiny legs. “No, thank you, Drum,” Birdie said clearly. “I am working. I need quiet. I will play with you when I am done.” Birdie was brave and used a strong, kind voice.

The drum wobbled and nodded. “Okay. I will be quiet now.” The room grew still. Tick-tock, tick-tock.

Birdie wrote a new line. The crayon went scratch, scratch. Birdie sang soft and sweet. “Tweet, tweet, tweet.” Birdie wrote three little notes. One, two, three.

The red couch smelled like strawberries. The yellow rug felt warm under Birdie’s feet. Birdie took a sip of cool water from a tiny cup. It tasted fresh. “I am doing it,” Birdie chirped. “I am practicing.”

Soon, Birdie felt a yawn. “I am a little tired,” Birdie said. “I need help.”

From under the table, Pencil the pencil peeked out. “Need a friend?” Pencil asked.

“Yes, please,” said Birdie. “Can you help me make neat lines?”

Pencil smiled. “I can help you practice.” Pencil showed Birdie how to make a straight line. Zip! Then a small dot. Tap! Birdie copied. Zip, tap! Zip, tap!

Birdie clapped wings. “I did it!” Birdie said. “Thank you for helping. I asked for what I needed.”

Pencil winked. “That is good self-advocacy,” Pencil said. “You used your voice.”

Birdie looked at the page. There were music notes and small words. Birdie read them slowly. “La la la. Tweet tweet.” Then Birdie sang. The song matched the page. It was sweet and clear like a bell. Ting!

Pillow wiggled. “Is it time to play now?” Pillow asked softly.

“Almost,” Birdie said. “I have one more line. I will finish my task.”

Birdie took a deep breath. In, out. The air smelled like sunshine. Birdie sang the last part, soft as a whisper. Then Birdie wrote the last notes. Scratch, scratch, scratch. Done!

“I did it!” Birdie chirped. “I practiced. I finished. I wrote my song!”

The room cheered. The clock went tick-tick-yay! The drum made a quiet tap-tap. Pillow gave a gentle flop-hug. The crayons shone in the light.

“Now I can play,” Birdie said. “I kept my promise to myself. Thank you for waiting.”

Pillow bounced. “Play time!” Everyone giggled.

Before playing, Birdie stood on the table and sang the new song. “La la la, tweet tweet tweet,” Birdie sang. The notes felt smooth and shiny. Birdie’s heart felt warm and proud.

Pencil said, “Practice made your song strong. You asked for help when you needed it. You stayed kind and kept going.”

Birdie nodded. “When things are hard, I can practice. I can say, ‘Please wait,’ or ‘I need help.’ I can finish my task. I can be proud of me.”

Then Birdie put the paper in a safe spot. Birdie patted the page with a small wing. “I will sing this again tomorrow,” Birdie said, happy and calm.

The playful living room glowed with soft light. Everyone felt cozy and bright. And then they all played a gentle game together, with smiles and quiet laughs.

The End.
