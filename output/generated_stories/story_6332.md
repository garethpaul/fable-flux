# Dolly the Dolphin's Helpful Day

Dolly the dolphin lived in a magical house where sea creatures could visit on land. Today, she was staying in the cozy guest room with soft blue walls and fluffy white pillows that looked just like ocean waves.

"Good morning, Dolly!" called Mrs. Seahorse from downstairs. "We have special guests coming today, but oh my! There's so much to do!"

Dolly poked her shiny gray head out of the guest room. She could hear Mrs. Seahorse's voice sounding worried and tired. Dolly's heart felt warm and fuzzy because she wanted to help her friend feel better.

Swimming down the stairs in her magical bubble of water, Dolly found Mrs. Seahorse looking at a very messy living room. Toys were scattered everywhere, dishes were piled high, and dust covered the tables.

"What's wrong, Mrs. Seahorse?" asked Dolly, her voice gentle and caring.

"My family is coming to visit in just two hours," sighed Mrs. Seahorse. "But I have so many chores to do, and I'm feeling very frustrated. I don't think I can clean everything in time!"

Dolly's big, kind eyes sparkled with an idea. "I can help you! Many fins make light work!"

Mrs. Seahorse looked surprised. "But Dolly, you're my guest! It wouldn't be fair to ask you to do chores."

"Helping friends isn't a chore when you do it with love," said Dolly with a happy click. "Besides, it's the right thing to do when someone needs help!"

Dolly started right away. She used her smooth nose to gently push toys into their proper boxes. The toys made soft *clink* and *plop* sounds as they landed safely inside.

"I'll wash the dishes!" offered Dolly. She filled the sink with warm, bubbly water that smelled like ocean breeze. The soap bubbles tickled her nose as she carefully cleaned each plate and cup until they sparkled.

Mrs. Seahorse watched in amazement. "Dolly, you're so kind! Let me help too!"

Together, they dusted the tables with soft cloths. The dust danced in the sunlight streaming through the windows. Dolly made it fun by pretending they were painting beautiful pictures in the air.

When Dolly felt a little tired, she took deep breaths and remembered how good it felt to help her friend. "When we help others, we help ourselves feel happy too," she said to herself.

Soon, the house looked beautiful and clean. Everything smelled fresh and felt cozy. Mrs. Seahorse's eyes filled with happy tears.

"Dolly, thank you so much!" said Mrs. Seahorse, giving her a warm hug. "You didn't have to help me, but you chose to anyway. That shows what a wonderful friend you are!"

Just then, the doorbell rang. Mrs. Seahorse's family arrived to find a perfectly clean house and two smiling friends.

"How did you get everything done so quickly?" asked Mrs. Seahorse's sister.

"Dolly helped me," said Mrs. Seahorse proudly. "She showed me that helping others makes everything easier and more fun!"

Dolly felt proud and happy inside. Her heart was as warm as sunshine. She had learned that when someone needs help, it's always right to lend a fin – or a hand!

That night, as Dolly settled into the comfortable guest room, she smiled. Helping others had made her day special and bright.

The End.
