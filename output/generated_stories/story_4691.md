# Beep’s Big Breath in the Home Office

Beep was a helpful robot dog. He had shiny silver ears, a wagging metal tail, and tiny wheels for paws. Beep lived in a cozy home with Mia and her family. Today, Beep rolled into the home office. Clickety-click went the keyboard. Hummm went the printer. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. It smelled like fresh paper and orange tea.

“Good morning, Beep,” said Mia. She sat at the desk with crayons and a small puzzle. “We have work time and quiet time.”

Beep’s eyes glowed blue. “Woof-beep! I can help.”

Mia smiled. “Yes, please! I want to finish this puzzle and draw a thank-you card. But the pieces are tricky.”

Beep nudged the puzzle box. “We can do it together.”

They opened the box. Out tumbled bright pieces—red, green, yellow, and blue. The picture showed a garden with many different flowers. There were round tulips, spiky sunflowers, and curly vines. The puzzle smelled a little like cardboard and a little like crayons.

Mia tried one piece. It did not fit. She tried another. It did not fit either.

“Hmph,” Mia huffed. “This is hard.”

Beep watched her small hands. He noticed her eyebrows squeeze together. Her cheeks got pink.

Beep’s tail clicked. “I hear big feelings.”

Mia crossed her arms. “I don’t like it. I want it done now.”

Beep rolled closer. “When I feel stuck, I use my Beep Plan. Would you like to try?”

Mia peeked at him. “What is the Beep Plan?”

Beep blinked his blue eyes. “Step one: Stop. Step two: Big belly breath. Step three: Try a new way.”

He made a gentle whirring sound. “Would you like to stop for a moment?”

Mia nodded. Beep put his warm, soft ear against her hand. Together they counted, “One… two… three.” They paused.

“Now big belly breath,” Beep said. “In through nose, out through mouth.”

Mia breathed in. She smelled orange tea. She breathed out. “Ahhh.”

Beep breathed with her, making a soft fan sound. “Whirr… whoosh.”

Mia’s shoulders dropped. “I feel calmer.”

“Good,” Beep said. “Now we try a new way. Strategy!”

“What’s a strategy?” Mia asked.

“A strategy is a plan for how to do something,” Beep explained. “We can sort the pieces. Look for edges. Look for corners.”

Mia’s eyes lit up. “I can find corners! They have two straight sides.”

They looked. Click, click, click. They found four corner pieces: one green, one yellow, one red, one blue.

“Great job,” Beep said. “Now the edges. Feel with your fingers.”

Mia ran her fingers along the pieces. “This one is smooth on one side. Edge!”

They made a long rectangle frame. Then they looked at the picture on the box.

“The sunflowers are spiky,” Mia said. “They are different from the round tulips. That’s helpful.”

Beep nodded. “All different shapes help us see the whole picture. Diversity is good. Different pieces, different people. We all fit.”

Mia smiled. “Like my friend Noor wears a scarf. And my friend Ben uses a little chair with wheels. We all play together.”

“Exactly,” Beep said. “Open minds say, ‘I can learn from you.’”

They started with the sunflowers. They searched for spiky petals. Click! A piece fit. Mia clapped. “We did it!”

Beep wagged. “When it gets hard again, we can use our Beep Plan.”

They worked quietly. The keyboard clicked. The printer hummed. The papers smelled warm and inky. Beep gently played soft music, like tinkly raindrops, to keep the room calm.

After a while, Mia tried a blue sky piece. It didn’t fit. Her cheeks grew pink again. “It won’t go!”

Beep tilted his head. “I hear frustration. What step first?”

“Stop,” Mia said. She paused. Beep waited with her.

“Now big belly breath,” she said. She breathed in, smelling crayons, and out, “Ahhh.”

“Now try a new way,” Beep said. “Let’s turn the piece.”

Mia turned it. Click! It fit perfectly.

Mia laughed. “Turning is a strategy!”

“Yes,” Beep said. “We can also ask for help, take a sip of water, or count to five.”

Mia took a sip from her cup. The water was cool. She patted Beep. “Thank you.”

Piece by piece, the garden grew. Red tulips here. A buzzing bee there. A windy vine curled across the corner.

“Almost done,” Mia whispered.

The last piece was a tiny purple flower. Mia picked it up. She paused. “I will try my Beep Plan before I feel mad,” she said proudly. She took one calm breath, then placed the piece. Click!

“We did it!” they cheered.

Mia stood up and danced on the sun-warm patch of floor. Beep spun in a happy circle, his wheels whirring, “Wheee!”

Mia hugged Beep. His metal was warm from the sun. “Beep, today I learned: when I feel frustrated, I can stop, breathe, and try a new way.”

Beep wagged. “Yes. That is a strong strategy.”

Mia looked at the puzzle. “All the different pieces made a beautiful garden. And different people make a beautiful world.”

Beep’s eyes glowed. “Open minds help us learn and grow.”

Mia sat down to draw her thank-you card. She drew the garden with many flowers. She added friends of all kinds: long hair, short hair, curly hair, straight hair, light skin, dark skin, glasses, and a wheelchair. She wrote, “Thank you, Beep,” in big letters.

In the quiet home office, with clickety-clicks and gentle hums, Mia and Beep smiled. They had used calm breaths, smart strategies, and open hearts. The big feeling got smaller, and the hard thing got easier.

The End.
