# The Little Mouse and the Stuck Jar

In a sunny garden shed, a kind mouse named Miri lived behind a soft pile of rags. The shed smelled like fresh soil and wood. Sunlight made little golden dots on the floor. Miri loved the shed. She loved the quiet drip-drop of a leaky tap and the tick-tick of a tiny clock.

One morning, Miri found a big glass jar on its side. Inside, she saw three shiny sunflower seeds. “Oh! Seeds,” Miri whispered. Her whiskers twitched. “I can share these with my friends.”

Miri tugged the lid. It did not move. She pushed. It still did not move.

“I can do this,” she said to herself. “I will keep trying.”

Miri wrapped her little paws around the lid. She pulled. The lid squeaked. “Eeek,” it said. Miri pulled again. The lid would not open. Her cheeks felt hot. Her tail swished.

“I feel mad,” Miri said, putting her paw on her chest. “My belly feels tight. I want to stomp.” She took a breath in—slow, like smelling a flower. She blew a breath out—slow, like blowing a bubble. “Smell the flower. Blow the bubble,” she said. She did it again. Her belly felt softer.

Miri looked around the shed. She saw a small wooden spoon on the floor. She saw a piece of string. She saw a tiny drop of oil near the leaky tap. “Maybe I can try a new way,” she said. “I will be brave and keep going.”

First, Miri tried the spoon. She wedged it under the lid and pushed, gentle and steady. “Push, push,” she whispered. The spoon slipped with a clack. The jar did not open.

“I feel frustrated again,” Miri said. She put her paw on her chest. “Smell the flower. Blow the bubble.” In. Out. In. Out. She felt calmer.

Next, Miri tied the string around the lid. She pulled the string with all her might. Her paws slid. “Slip! Slide!” The string popped off. “Oh,” she sighed. “That did not work. But I will not give up.”

Miri’s friend, a shy ladybug named Dot, crawled out from under a flower pot. “Hi, Miri,” Dot said in a tiny voice. “What are you doing?”

“I found seeds,” said Miri. “I want to share them, but the lid is stuck.” She looked at Dot and felt a little pinch in her tummy. “Dot, I need to tell the truth. I felt mad, and I wanted to kick the jar. But I did not. I took breaths instead. It was hard.”

Dot nodded. “That was honest,” said Dot. “And very brave.”

Miri smiled. “Thank you. I’m trying to be calm and keep trying.”

Dot touched the lid with her tiny feet. “Maybe a drop of oil will help,” she said. “Just a tiny, tiny bit.”

Miri dipped the tip of the string in the little oil drop by the tap. “Just a dot,” she said. She rubbed the dot around the lid rim, gentle as a whisper.

“Ready,” Miri said. “One, two, three.” She gripped the lid again. “Twist, twist,” she chanted softly. The lid squeaked louder. “Eeeeek!”

It still did not open. Miri felt tears tickle her eyes. She sat down. “I worked so hard,” she said. “I want to give up.”

Dot sat beside her. “Let’s rest,” Dot said. “We can count to five.”

Together they counted. “One, two, three, four, five.” Miri took one deep flower smell and one long bubble blow. Her shoulders dropped. “I feel better,” she said. “I can try again.”

Miri stood up. “I will be gentle. I will be steady. I will not rush.”

She wrapped a small cloth around the lid to help her grip. She set her tiny feet firm on the floor. “Twist slow,” she whispered. “Twist slow.”

Twist. Twist. Pop!

The lid jumped off with a happy pop! The smell of fresh seeds filled the air—nutty and warm. Miri laughed. Dot cheered. “You did it!” Dot sang.

Miri looked at the open jar. “We did it,” she said. “I kept trying, and you helped me. Thank you, Dot.” She picked up one seed. “I want to be honest with everyone. I found the seeds, but they should be shared.”

She called to the other shed friends: a small snail named Wink and a fuzzy moth named Luna. “Friends!” Miri called. “I have seeds. I tried many times to open the jar. I felt mad, but I used calm breaths. I kept going. Now we can share.”

Wink slid over, leaving a shiny trail. “Sharing is kind,” Wink said.

Luna fluttered down. “And you told the truth,” Luna hummed. “That makes my heart warm.”

Miri broke the seeds into tiny pieces so everyone could have a taste. Crunch, crunch—the seeds were crisp and yummy. The friends giggled together. The shed felt bright and cozy.

Dot smiled. “What did we learn today?” she asked.

Miri put her paw on her chest. “When something is hard, I can be calm. I can tell the truth about my feelings. I can try again and again. That is called persistence.”

The friends nodded. The little clock went tick-tick. The sun made golden dots dance.

Miri curled up on her soft rags with a happy sigh. “I am proud,” she said. “I did not give up.”

Outside, a bird sang a sweet song. Inside, the friends felt safe, honest, and brave.

The End.
