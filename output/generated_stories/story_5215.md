# Milo's Peaceful Porch Day

Milo the mouse stretched his tiny paws as the warm morning sun tickled his whiskers. He loved starting each day on the peaceful front porch of the cozy blue house where he lived. The wooden boards felt smooth under his feet, and he could smell the sweet flowers blooming in the garden nearby.

"What a beautiful day!" Milo squeaked softly to himself. He took three deep breaths, just like his mama had taught him. "In through my nose, out through my mouth. This helps me feel calm and happy."

Just then, Milo heard loud chirping from above. Two baby birds were arguing in their nest on the porch roof.

"That's MY worm!" chirped the first baby bird, whose name was Pip.

"No, it's MINE!" chirped the second baby bird, whose name was Peep. "I saw it first!"

Their voices got louder and louder. Milo's ears twitched as he listened to their upset sounds. He felt his little heart beating faster from all the noise.

"Oh my," thought Milo. "They sound very upset. Maybe I can help them feel better."

Milo took another deep breath to stay calm. Then he climbed up the porch post using his sharp little claws. Climb, climb, climb until he reached the birds' nest.

"Hello, Pip and Peep," Milo said in his gentlest voice. "I can hear that you're both feeling upset. Would you like to talk about what's wrong?"

Both baby birds stopped chirping and looked down at the kind little mouse.

"We found this juicy worm," said Pip, pointing with his wing. "But we both want to eat it, and there's only one!"

"I'm so hungry," added Peep sadly. "My tummy is making grumbly sounds."

Milo nodded thoughtfully. He could see the problem clearly now. "I understand," he said. "You're both hungry, and you both found the worm together. That must feel frustrating."

The baby birds nodded their fluffy heads.

"When I feel upset like this," Milo continued, "I like to take deep breaths and think of solutions. Would you like to try breathing with me first?"

"Okay," said Pip and Peep together.

"Breathe in slowly through your beaks," Milo demonstrated. "Now breathe out slowly. Feel how that makes your bodies more relaxed?"

The baby birds tried it. "That does feel better," said Peep, her feathers settling down.

"Now," said Milo, "let's think of ways to solve this problem. What if you shared the worm? You could each eat half."

Pip's eyes lit up. "That's a great idea! We could break it in the middle."

"And tomorrow we can look for worms together," added Peep excitedly. "Maybe we'll find two!"

The baby birds carefully split the worm and ate their portions happily. Their tummies stopped grumbling, and they felt much better.

"Thank you, Milo," they chirped together. "You helped us stay calm and find a good solution."

Milo smiled as he climbed back down to his peaceful porch. The sun felt even warmer now, and the flowers smelled even sweeter. He had learned that when problems arise, taking deep breaths and talking calmly can help everyone find answers.

As Milo settled into his favorite sunny spot on the porch, he felt proud and peaceful. "What a wonderful way to start the day," he whispered, closing his eyes and enjoying the gentle morning breeze.

The End.
