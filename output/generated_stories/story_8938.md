# Rocco’s Porch Parade

Rocco was a musical raccoon with soft silver fur and bright curious eyes. He loved to make music on his peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like warm wood and sweet flowers. The breeze felt gentle on his whiskers. Birds chirped softly, and the porch swing creaked in a slow, sleepy rhythm.

On the porch, Rocco kept a small drum, a tiny bell, a shiny harmonica, and a homemade shaker filled with acorns. “Ting, tap, toot, shake!” he sang, tapping each one. It was almost time for the Moonlight Porch Parade, a family tradition. Every year, Rocco’s family played music on the porch when the moon rose. They sang, shared stories, and made gentle sounds so the night animals could listen.

Rocco wanted to be ready. “I want to play my best,” he said. “But which instrument should I play?” He felt a little flutter in his tummy. There were so many choices!

Mama Raccoon brought out a basket of blueberry muffins. The warm, sweet smell floated in the air. “Mmm,” said Rocco, nibbling a crumb. “Mama, I need to decide. Drum, bell, harmonica, or shaker?”

Mama smiled and sat on the porch step. “This is a big decision,” she said kindly. “Let’s use our decision steps.”

“Decision steps?” Rocco blinked.

“Yes,” said Mama. “Step one: Stop and breathe.” Rocco put his paw on his tummy and took a slow breath. In… out… The breeze tickled his nose.

“Step two: Think about the plan. What is the goal?” Mama asked.

“My goal is to play music that fits our family tradition,” said Rocco. “Soft, happy porch music.”

“Good,” Mama said. “Step three: Choose by trying. Try each one and listen.”

Rocco nodded. He picked up the drum. Tap-tap-tap. The sound was round and bouncy. His paws tingled with the steady beat.

He tried the bell. Ting-ting! The sound was bright like stars. It sparkled in the air.

He blew the harmonica. Toot-weep-woo! The sound slid and hummed like a friendly wind. His cheeks puffed, and he giggled.

He shook the acorn shaker. Shake-a-shake-a-shake. It sounded like soft rain on leaves. His tail swished to the rhythm.

“Hmm,” said Rocco. “I like all of them.” He scrunched his nose. “I still don’t know.”

Grandpa Raccoon stepped onto the porch with a cozy blanket. “What’s the riddle, little drummer?”

“I need to decide my Parade sound,” said Rocco. “Drum, bell, harmonica, or shaker?”

Grandpa’s eyes twinkled. “Our tradition has three parts,” he said. “We start with a welcome, we tell a story, and we say goodnight.”

Rocco’s ears perked up. “A welcome, a story, and a goodnight.”

Grandpa nodded. “The welcome should be bright so friends know we are here. The story needs a steady beat to carry the words. The goodnight is soft, like a lullaby.”

Rocco’s eyes grew wide. “I can prepare! I can match each part.”

Mama clapped softly. “That is step four: Make a plan.”

Rocco made a little list in his head.
- Welcome: bell, ting-ting!
- Story: drum, tap-tap.
- Goodnight: shaker, shake-a-shake.

He looked at the harmonica and felt a tiny tug. “But I love the harmonica,” he whispered.

Papa Raccoon came with lanterns that smelled like lemon and pine. “You can also share,” Papa said. “Traditions grow when we share them. Maybe someone else can use one, and you can switch.”

Rocco smiled. “I can share the harmonica for the story song!”

The sun dipped low. Fireflies blinked. The porch felt cozy and safe. Rocco set the bell by the porch rail, the drum by the rocking chair, and the shaker by the swing. He placed the harmonica on a little cushion. “All ready,” he said. He felt calm and proud.

Neighbors padded over—Sofia Squirrel, Benny Bunny, and Tilly Toad. “We love the Moonlight Porch Parade,” said Sofia, carrying a tiny nut cookie. It smelled like cinnamon.

Rocco took a breath. “Welcome!” he said. He rang the bell. Ting-ting! The sound sparkled and made everyone smile.

Grandpa told the old porch story about how the moon once wore a cloud hat. Rocco kept a gentle drum beat. Tap… tap… tap… The steady sound helped everyone listen. Benny Bunny’s ears wiggled happily.

Tilly Toad admired the harmonica. “It is so shiny.”

“Would you like to try it for the story song?” Rocco asked, remembering to share. Tilly puffed a small note. Toot! The sound wiggled like a giggle. Everyone laughed softly.

At last, the moon floated high and round. Time for goodnight. Rocco held the shaker close. Shake-a-shake, shake-a-shake. The sound was soft like rain, and the porch felt sleepy-sweet. Mama hummed. Papa swayed. The fireflies blinked slower. Even the breeze whispered, “Shhh.”

When the music faded, Rocco felt warm and light inside. He had made a choice. He had prepared. He had shared the tradition.

Mama hugged him. “You used your decision steps.”

Rocco nodded. “I stopped and breathed. I thought about the goal. I tried my choices. I made a plan. I shared and got ready.”

Grandpa winked. “And you made beautiful porch music.”

Rocco curled his tail and smiled. “I can make good choices when I prepare and think.”

The moon glowed like a big, silver cookie. The porch was peaceful and happy. Rocco whispered, “Goodnight,” and the night whispered back.

The End.
