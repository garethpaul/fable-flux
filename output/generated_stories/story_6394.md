# Benny Beaver’s Quiet Corner

Benny was a busy beaver with bright teeth, warm brown fur, and a big, flat tail that went thump-thump when he was excited. He loved his study corner. It was a cozy spot by the window with a little desk, a soft chair, and a lamp that glowed like a tiny sun. On the desk were chunky crayons, smooth wooden blocks, and a book with shiny pages. It smelled like crayons and clean paper. It felt safe and calm.

“One thing at a time,” Benny hummed. “I can do it.”

Today, Benny wanted to build a tall block tower and draw a picture for his book. He tapped his tail, thump-thump, and lined up the blocks. Tap, stack, pat. The blocks made soft clack sounds. He smiled. “Steady paws,” he whispered. “I am strong and calm.”

Then—knock, knock! His friend Dotty Duck peeked in. “Hi, Benny! What are you doing? Can I touch? Can I help? What does this button do?” Dotty waddled in quickly, flappy feet going slap-slap on the floor. Her eyes were big with curiosity.

Benny liked Dotty. He liked her questions. But his paws felt busy and his tummy felt a little wiggly. He needed calm to stack the tall blocks. He took a deep breath. In and out. He remembered his study corner rule: This is my quiet space. I can say what I need.

“Hi, Dotty,” Benny said kindly. “I’m building and drawing right now. My study corner is for quiet work. You can watch, but please don’t touch the blocks yet.”

Dotty blinked. “But I’m so curious,” she quacked. “I want to see! I want to feel!”

“You can be curious,” said Benny, “and I can keep my space calm. You can look with your eyes. In a little while, we can build together.”

Dotty rocked on her webbed feet. “Okay,” she said, a little sadly, and sat on a cushion. “I will look with my eyes.”

Benny stacked three more blocks. Clack, clack, clack. The tower got taller. He felt proud. But then—wiggle, wobble—Dotty scooted closer without thinking. Her wing brushed the tower.

“Oh!” cried Dotty.

Crash! The blocks tumbled down like soft rain. Clatter-clatter all over the floor.

Benny’s heart dropped. His tail thumped once, hard. He felt hot in his cheeks. He wanted to shout. But he closed his eyes and took another slow breath. In and out. He remembered: I can be strong. I can try again.

Dotty’s eyes filled with tears. “I’m sorry, Benny,” she whispered. “I was too close. I forgot.”

Benny nodded. “Thank you for saying sorry,” he said softly. “I feel upset. I worked hard. I also know I can try again.”

He looked at his quiet lamp. He felt the soft chair. He listened to the tiny hum of the room. Calm came back like a gentle wave.

“I need my study corner to be quiet,” Benny said. His voice was kind and clear. “Please sit on the cushion. Please keep your wings to yourself until I say it’s time.”

Dotty dried her eyes. “I can do that,” she said. She folded her wings and sat still, her feathers rustling softly.

Benny picked up the blocks. Tap, stack, pat. He hummed, “One thing at a time.” He made the base wide this time, like a strong beaver dam. He felt steady. He drew a deep green river on his paper. He drew a yellow sun and a smile.

Dotty watched with bright, curious eyes. “Why is the bottom so big?” she asked in a whisper.

“It helps the tower stay strong,” Benny said, smiling. “Wide bottom. Steady top.”

“I like learning,” Dotty whispered back.

The tower grew taller than before. It looked grand and brave. Benny sat up straight. “I did it,” he said. His tail made a happy thump-thump.

Dotty clapped softly. “May I help now?” she asked.

Benny checked his tummy feelings. Calm and warm. He nodded. “Yes. You can add one block at the top. Use gentle wings.”

Dotty lifted one block. She moved slow like a cloud. She placed it on the top. Clack. The tower stayed tall.

“We did it,” Benny said.

“We did it together,” Dotty quacked.

Benny held up his drawing. “This is my book page,” he said. “It says, ‘I can try again.’”

Dotty smiled. “I learned, too,” she said. “I can be curious and also respect your space. I can wait. I can ask.”

Benny grinned. “And I can say what I need. That helps us both.”

They read the shiny book together. The lamp glowed warm. The room smelled like crayons and happiness. The tower stood tall and steady, like Benny’s brave heart.

Benny whispered, “I was upset, but I took breaths. I tried again. I kept my study corner rules. That is resilience.”

Dotty whispered, “I was curious, and I learned about personal space. That is respect.”

They bumped heads gently. “Teamwork,” they said.

The End.
