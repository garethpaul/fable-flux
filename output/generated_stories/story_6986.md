# Whiskers Shares the Bathroom

Whiskers the clever cat stretched his orange paws and padded into the bathroom. The morning sun made the white tiles sparkle like tiny stars.

"Time for my bath!" Whiskers purred happily.

He loved splashing in the warm, sudsy water. The bubbles tickled his whiskers and made him giggle.

Just as Whiskers turned on the faucet, he heard a tiny voice.

"Excuse me," squeaked someone very small.

Whiskers looked down. A little gray mouse sat by the bathtub, looking very dirty and sad.

"Oh my!" said Whiskers. "What's wrong, little friend?"

The mouse's whiskers drooped. "I fell in the muddy garden. I'm all yucky and smelly. But I'm too small to reach the faucet."

Whiskers thought carefully. His clever brain worked like a puzzle. The mouse needed help, but cats and mice were supposed to be enemies, weren't they?

Then Whiskers looked at the sad, dirty mouse again. His kind heart felt warm.

"I have an idea!" Whiskers announced. "We can share!"

"Share?" asked the mouse, surprised.

"Yes! The bathtub is big enough for both of us. I'll help you get clean."

Whiskers gently lifted the little mouse up to the edge of the tub. The warm water felt wonderful on their fur.

"This is amazing!" giggled the mouse as soap bubbles danced around him.

Whiskers used his clever paws to make a tiny washcloth from a corner of his towel. He helped scrub the mud from the mouse's tiny paws.

"Why are you being so nice to me?" asked the mouse.

Whiskers smiled. "Because sharing makes everything better. When we help each other, we both feel happy."

The mouse's eyes sparkled like the bubbles. "You're right! I feel so much better already."

After their bath, both animals felt fresh and clean. Their fur was fluffy and smelled like lavender soap.

"Thank you, Whiskers," said the mouse. "You solved my problem with your clever thinking and kind heart."

"And you made my bath time even more fun!" purred Whiskers.

The mouse had an idea. "I know where the sweetest berries grow in the garden. Would you like me to show you?"

"That sounds wonderful!" said Whiskers.

From that day on, Whiskers and the mouse became the best of friends. They shared many more baths, always making sure the bathroom stayed clean and tidy.

Whiskers learned that being generous - sharing what he had - made him feel even happier than keeping everything for himself. His clever thinking helped him see that friends come in all sizes.

The little mouse learned that asking for help was okay, and that kindness could come from the most unexpected places.

Every morning, they would meet in the sparkling bathroom, sharing bubbles, giggles, and the warm feeling that comes from caring for each other.

"Sharing is caring," Whiskers would say as they splashed together.

"And caring makes the best friends!" the mouse would add with a happy squeak.

The bathroom became their special place - clean, bright, and full of friendship.

The End.
