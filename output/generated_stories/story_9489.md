# Sunny Songs on the Rooftop

On a warm morning, a little singing bird named Lila hopped onto the rooftop. The roof tiles were warm under her tiny feet. The breeze smelled like sunshine and soap from laundry lines. Lila fluffed her soft blue feathers and sang, “Tweet-tweet, trilla-la! Good morning, sky!”

“Good morning, Lila!” called a gray pigeon named Pip. He wiggled his tail. “What are you singing today?”

“I’m singing a helping song,” Lila chirped. “Today, I want to volunteer. Vol-un-teer means we help for free, just to be kind.”

Pip blinked. “Help? On the roof?”

“Yes!” Lila looked around. She heard clinking pots and a gentle hum from the kitchen below. She smelled warm bread. She felt the wind brush her wings. “There is always a way to help,” she said softly. “Let’s listen and look.”

A gust of wind blew a small red scarf from a clothesline. The scarf fluttered like a little flag, then caught on a nail. “Oh no!” said a tiny mouse named Miri, peeking from a roof corner. “That scarf is for my friend. She gets cold.”

Lila took a calming breath. “We can help,” she said. “Slow and calm keeps us safe.” She sang a soft tune, “Breathe in, breathe out, we can help, no doubt.” Pip bobbed his head to the rhythm.

Lila fluttered up, light as a feather. The scarf tickled her beak. She held it carefully and hummed. Miri clapped her paws. “Thank you, Lila!”

“You’re welcome,” Lila chirped. “That was volunteering. We helped because we care.”

Down on the street, a drum started. Boom-ba! Boom-ba! Music floated up to the rooftop. Children giggled below. “Dance time!” Pip cooed.

Lila smiled. “Let’s share the fun! We can volunteer a song for everyone.” She hopped to the roof edge and called, “Hello down there!”

The children looked up. “Hi, bird!” they shouted. “Can you sing?”

“Yes,” said Lila. “And you can dance!” She sang a bright tune. “Step-step, clap-clap, twirl around the map!” The children stepped and clapped. Pip did a silly pigeon wiggle. Miri tapped her tiny toes. The rooftop felt happy and alive. The warm sun kissed Lila’s wings.

Suddenly, two cats crept onto the roof—Soot, a black cat, and Ginger, an orange cat. They both wanted the same sunny spot. Their tails flicked. Their ears twitched.

“That warm spot is mine!” hissed Soot.

“No, I found it first!” meowed Ginger.

Lila felt the air get tight and prickly. The music seemed to stop. Miri squeaked and hid. Pip tucked his head. Lila took a deep, calm breath. “We can use peaceful solutions,” she said softly. “We can help them share.”

She sang gently, like a lullaby. “Calm like clouds, soft and slow. We can talk, and let go.” The cats’ tails slowed. Their ears relaxed just a little.

Lila spoke in a kind voice. “We can solve this together. Let’s try turns. Soot, five calm breaths in the sun. Ginger, five calm breaths in the shade. Then switch. I will count.”

Soot and Ginger looked at each other. Their whiskers twitched. Lila smiled and counted, “One… two… three… four… five.” The sun warmed Soot. Then, “Switch!” Ginger stepped in. “One… two… three… four… five.” The sun warmed Ginger too.

“That felt nice,” purred Soot.

“I feel calm,” purred Ginger. They both curled at the edge and shared the space. Their purrs were like tiny motors, rrrrrr, soft and steady.

Miri peeked out. “Lila, you helped the cats!” she said.

Lila nodded. “That is volunteering too. We helped without being asked. We used calm and kind words.”

Pip flapped happily. “Let’s sing a sharing song!”

Lila began, “Share the sun, share the sky, helping hearts can fly so high.” The children below danced again. Their feet went tap-tap. The drum went boom-ba. The wind whispered shhh-shhh over the roof.

Soon, a big paper bag rolled across the rooftop. It was full of old leaves and tiny trash. “This is messy,” Pip said, wrinkling his beak.

“Let’s volunteer to clean,” Lila chirped. “We can make our roof nice for everyone.”

They worked together. Lila scooped light bits with her wings—rustle, rustle. Pip pushed leaves—swish, swish. Miri gathered crumbs—pick, pick, pick. Soot and Ginger used their paws to tap the bag—pat, pat—so the trash slid inside. Lila hummed a happy working tune, and everyone moved like a dance. It felt good to help.

When the roof was tidy, the sun felt brighter. The air smelled clean. Lila’s heart felt warm. The children below waved and shouted, “Thank you!”

“You’re welcome!” Lila sang back. “We are volunteers. We help because we care.”

Pip asked, “Lila, what did we learn today?”

Lila looked at her friends. She saw kind eyes, calm smiles, and clean tiles. “We learned that volunteering is helping for free. We learned to be calm and use peaceful words. We learned we can make helping into a dance—step, clap, swish!”

Miri nodded. “Helping makes my heart feel big.”

Soot and Ginger purred together. “Sharing feels warm.”

Lila lifted her beak to the blue sky and sang, “When we help, we shine.” The rooftop glowed with sun, songs, and smiles.

And every day after, when the drum went boom-ba, Lila and her friends met on the rooftop. They sang, they danced, and they volunteered to help anyone who needed a hand, a paw, a wing, or simply a calm, kind word.

The End.
