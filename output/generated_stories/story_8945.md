# Melody's Messy Art Room

In a bright, colorful art room filled with paintbrushes, crayons, and paper, lived a little yellow bird named Melody. She loved to sing beautiful songs while children created their artwork. Her sweet voice made the room feel magical and happy.

"Tweet-tweet-la-la!" Melody sang from her perch near the sunny window. "Good morning, art room! Time to make something wonderful!"

But when Melody looked around, she gasped. Paint bottles were scattered on the floor, brushes were left dirty in cups, and crumpled papers were everywhere. The art room was very messy!

"Oh my!" chirped Melody, ruffling her bright yellow feathers. "How can the children make beautiful art in such a messy place?"

Just then, three children walked into the art room: Emma, Sam, and little Zoe. They wanted to paint pictures of flowers.

"Where are the clean brushes?" asked Emma, looking confused.

"I can't find any blue paint!" said Sam, searching through the messy shelves.

Little Zoe started to cry. "It's too messy! I don't want to paint anymore!"

Melody's heart felt heavy seeing the children so upset. She knew she had to help, but how could a little bird clean such a big mess?

Then Melody had a wonderful idea. She began to sing a special song:

"When things are messy, don't feel sad,
Working together makes us glad!
One by one, we'll clean this place,
With gentle hands and smiling face!"

The children stopped and listened to Melody's beautiful song. Emma tilted her head. "Are you trying to tell us something, little bird?"

Melody nodded and flew to a paintbrush on the floor. She gently picked it up in her beak and placed it in the brush holder.

"I think she wants us to clean up together!" said Sam, his eyes lighting up.

"But it's not our mess," Emma said, crossing her arms.

Melody sang again, softer this time:

"It doesn't matter who made the mess,
Helping others brings happiness!
When we work as a team so bright,
Everything will turn out right!"

Little Zoe wiped her tears. "The birdie is right! We can help!"

The children began to clean. Emma picked up the paint bottles, Sam washed the dirty brushes in warm, soapy water, and Zoe gathered all the crumpled papers. Melody helped by carrying small items in her beak and singing encouraging songs.

"This is actually fun!" laughed Emma as she organized the crayons by color.

"Look how shiny these brushes are now!" said Sam, holding up a clean brush.

"The room smells like soap and sunshine!" giggled Zoe.

As they worked together, Melody sang happy songs that made the cleaning feel like a dance. Soon, the art room sparkled! Clean brushes stood ready in their holders, paint bottles lined the shelves in rainbow order, and the tables were clear and ready for creating.

"Now we can paint our flowers!" cheered Emma.

The children created beautiful artwork while Melody sang her sweetest songs. The room filled with laughter, creativity, and joy.

"Thank you, Melody," said Zoe, showing the bird her painting of a yellow flower. "You taught us that cleaning together is better than being upset about messes."

"And that helping others feels really good!" added Sam.

Emma nodded. "Even when it's not our mess, we can still help make things better."

Melody puffed up her feathers proudly and sang one final song:

"When we choose to help and care,
Joy and peace are everywhere!
Working together, hand in hand,
Makes our world a better land!"

From that day on, the children always remembered Melody's lesson. Whenever they found a mess, they worked together peacefully to clean it up, making their art room a happy place for everyone.

The End.
