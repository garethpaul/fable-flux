# Ellie Plans the Garden Day

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears that flapped like tiny blankets. One sunny morning, she stood at the door of the garden shed. The wooden door smelled like warm pine. The metal latch felt cool on her trunk. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” in the trees.

“Today I will help in the garden,” Ellie said. “I will be ready!”

Inside the shed, it was cozy and a little dusty. Sunlight made golden lines through the tiny window. Ellie saw a small shovel, packets of seeds, a watering can, and bright gloves. She took a deep breath. “First, I will make a list,” she said, tapping her trunk.

She found a chalk board on the wall. She wrote with a squeaky chalk:
- Hat
- Water bottle
- Gloves
- Shovel
- Seeds
- Sunscreen
- Snack

Ellie smiled. “Preparation helps me do my best.”

Just then, her friend Benny the bunny peeked in. His nose twitched. “Hi, Ellie! Can I help?”

“Yes,” said Ellie. “You are included. We will work together.” She handed Benny a tiny pair of gloves. “These are just your size.”

“Ooh! They feel soft,” Benny said, rubbing the gloves. “What’s next?”

Ellie pointed to the list. “We check our plan. We gather what we need.”

They put on hats. Ellie’s hat was wide and floppy. It smelled like straw and felt light. Benny’s hat had a blue ribbon. They filled a little bottle with cool water that went glug-glug. Ellie dabbed sunscreen on her cheeks and ears. “We protect our skin,” she said.

Maya the mouse scurried in with a squeak. “Can I help too?”

“Yes,” said Ellie. “You are included.” Ellie found a tiny thimble for Maya to use as a hat. “This will shade you.”

Maya giggled. “Thank you!”

Ellie lifted the shovel. It was not too heavy, but she held it carefully. “I will use my trunk to carry it. Please watch your toes,” she said. “I need my space to swing the shovel.”

Benny and Maya stepped back. “We will give you room,” Benny said. Ellie felt cozy and safe. “Thank you for respecting my personal space,” she said. “That is a boundary. When I say ‘please give me space,’ you listen.”

They walked to the garden plot just outside the shed. The soil was warm and smelled like earth after rain. Ellie knelt and pressed the ground. “Soft and ready.”

Benny hopped close. “Can I dig?”

Ellie nodded. “Yes, after we plan where the seeds go.” She opened a seed packet. Tiny round seeds rattled like little marbles. “We prepare rows. We plant carefully.”

Ellie used the shovel to make gentle lines. Scritch-scritch went the blade. “One, two, three rows,” she counted. “We will plant carrots, sunflowers, and beans.”

Maya’s eyes shone. “I love sunflowers!”

“We all do,” Ellie said. “Maya, please pour the seeds into my trunk one by one.” Maya poured three seeds. Plink, plink, plink. Ellie placed them in the first row. Benny patted soil on top with his soft paws. Pat, pat, pat.

A busy beetle crawled by. “Hello,” said Ellie. “You can watch from the leaves.” She smiled. “Everyone can be part of garden day.”

Benny reached toward the shovel. “Can I try?”

Ellie nodded. “Yes, but this is my body, and I need to hold the handle with my trunk. Please wait until I pass it to you.” She looked kindly at Benny. “That is another boundary.”

Benny put his paws behind his back. “I will wait.”

When Ellie finished a row, she placed the shovel down. “Now it is safe,” she said. Benny picked it up and dug a small groove. “Scritch!” He giggled. “I’m learning a new skill!”

“You are skill-building,” Ellie said. “Practice makes us strong.”

They planted the beans and carrots. The packets crinkled like soft paper snow. Maya hummed a happy tune. The tune sounded like tiny bells.

At last, it was time to water. Ellie held the watering can. The water splashed and sparkled in the sun. “Swish-swish,” it went, making dark, happy spots in the soil. “Not too much,” Ellie said. “We prepared a plan. We follow it.”

Maya tugged Ellie’s ear. “May I touch your ear? It looks so soft.”

Ellie smiled. “Thank you for asking. Yes, you may touch gently.” Maya petted with one finger. “So soft!” Maya whispered.

Ellie nodded. “We ask before we touch. We respect each other’s bodies.”

When they were done, Ellie looked at the chalk board again. She checked every item. “Hat, water, gloves, shovel, seeds, sunscreen, snack.” She opened a lunch box that smelled like sweet bananas. “Snack time!”

They sat in the shade by the shed. The wood felt warm at their backs. They crunched carrot sticks and shared banana slices. “We prepared,” Ellie said, “and everything went smoothly.”

Benny wiggled his ears. “I liked being included.”

Maya squeaked, “I learned to wait and to ask. I feel safe.”

Ellie’s heart felt big and calm. “Preparation helps us do our best. Inclusion makes work joyful. Boundaries keep everyone safe and comfy. And practicing gives us new skills.”

They packed their tools and put them back in the shed where they belonged. The gloves hung neatly. The shovel stood tall. The watering can dripped one last drop. Plip.

Ellie looked at the garden rows. “We are ready for growing,” she said. “Tomorrow, we will check again. We will be prepared.”

The friends held trunks and paws and tiny mouse hands and watched a butterfly float by. Their garden, and their hearts, felt ready.

The End.
