# Lila the Singing Bird Makes a Friend

In a sunny study corner, dust sparkles danced in the light. A little desk sat by a window. Crayons lived in a cup. Soft papers rustled like whispering leaves. On the windowsill perched Lila, a tiny singing bird with sky-blue feathers.

“Tweet-a-dee!” sang Lila. Her song was warm and sweet, like honey tea. The scent of sharpened pencils tickled her beak. The desk felt smooth under her tiny feet.

Across the desk sat Theo, a quiet child with big curious eyes. He was drawing a city with green parks and tiny people picking up litter. “I am making a clean city,” he said softly. “Everyone helps.”

Lila tilted her head. “I love your parks. May I sing while you draw?”

Theo smiled. “Yes, please. Your song helps me think.”

Lila breathed in slowly. In, out. Mindful and calm. Then she sang again, “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo,” like raindrops on a tin roof.

Suddenly, the door opened. Mia, Theo’s friend, hurried in. Her steps were quick. The rug went swish under her shoes. She looked at the window, then at Lila. Her eyebrows scrunched.

“That bird is too loud,” Mia said. “I can’t think!”

Lila’s song wobbled and stopped. Her chest felt tight. “Oh,” she whispered. She tucked her wings close.

Theo looked at Mia. “Lila sings to help me draw,” he said kindly. “Her song is gentle.”

“It is not gentle to me,” Mia huffed. “It pokes my ears.”

The room grew very quiet. Only the clock went tick-tick, tick-tick. The study corner did not feel cozy now. It felt prickly, like a bush with thorns.

Lila’s heart made a small thump. She wanted to sing. Singing was her way to share joy. But she did not want to bother Mia. Lila took a slow breath. In. Out. “I feel sad,” she said in a small voice. “I want to be a friend.”

Mia crossed her arms. “I feel upset,” she said. “I want it quiet.”

Theo tapped his pencil. Tap. Tap. He remembered his drawing of the clean city. In the city, people helped each other. They took turns. They listened. That was civic responsibility. That meant caring for the whole group.

Theo said, “Let’s use kind words. Let’s listen. We can solve this together.”

Mia looked down at the rug. “Okay,” she whispered.

Lila nodded. “Okay,” she chirped.

Theo pointed to the crayon cup. “We can make a plan. First, we each say one need.”

Lila took another mindful breath. “I need to sing sometimes. It makes me calm and happy.”

Mia took a breath too. In. Out. “I need some quiet sometimes. It helps me think.”

Theo smiled. “Both needs are important. Friends care about each other.”

He looked around the study corner. Sunlight warmed his cheek. The clock ticked kindly now. “What if we make signals?” he asked. He drew two little signs. One sign had a tiny music note. One sign had a tiny quiet finger by lips. He taped them near the desk with a soft sticky sound.

“When the music note is up, Lila can sing softly,” Theo said. “When the quiet sign is up, we all make quiet time. We can switch every few minutes.”

Mia touched the quiet sign. “Can we also have a humming time?” she asked. “Humming is softer.”

Lila’s eyes shone. “Yes! I can hum. Mmm-mmm, like a warm blanket.”

Theo set the timer on his watch. Beep! “Two minutes of quiet. Two minutes of soft humming. Two minutes of gentle song. We will take turns. We will watch and listen.”

They tried it. Quiet time came first. The room felt like a calm cloud. Pencils whispered over paper. Lila closed her eyes and breathed. In. Out. She listened to the tiny tick of the clock, the soft whoosh of the window breeze.

Beep! Humming time. Lila hummed, mmm-mmm, like a purring kitten. Mia’s shoulders relaxed. She smiled. “That feels nice,” she said.

Beep! Singing time. Lila sang very softly, like a silver ribbon in the air. “Tweet-a-dee,” she breathed. Theo drew children planting trees. Mia added a little sign in the park: Please share. Please listen.

They kept going. Switch, switch, switch. They watched the signs. They listened to each other’s needs. The study corner grew warm and friendly again.

After a while, Theo put down his pencil. “Look!” he said. “Our city is full of helpers.”

Lila fluffed her feathers. “Our study corner is full of helpers too.”

Mia nodded. “I felt upset before,” she said. “Now I feel peaceful. Thank you for listening.”

Lila chirped, “Thank you for telling me your feelings. Friends talk. Friends care. Friends share.”

Theo raised his hand like a little wave. “Teamwork!” he cheered.

The sun slid across the desk. The crayons clicked back into their cup. The paper smelled like fresh wood. The study corner glowed with quiet joy and soft song.

Lila sang one last gentle note. “Tweet,” she whispered, like a promise.

They all smiled. They had solved the problem together. They had been mindful. They had been kind. They had been good friends to each other and to their shared space.

Lesson: Friendship means listening, sharing, and caring. We can take turns, speak kindly, and make a plan so everyone feels safe and happy.

The End.
