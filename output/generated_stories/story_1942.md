# Benny Beaver’s Big Guest Room Surprise

Benny was a busy beaver with bright eyes and a tickly nose. He loved to make things. He loved to fix things. He loved to try, try, try. One sunny morning, Benny tip-tapped into the house to see the guest room.

The guest room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. The curtains were soft and blue. A small suitcase sat on the bed. A tiny world map hung on the wall with colorful dots. Each dot looked like a candy button.

Benny’s tail thumped with joy. “I want to make this room welcoming,” he said. “A friend is coming to visit from far away!”

He looked at the map. There was a green dot on a place called Peru. “Our guest is coming from there,” Benny whispered. “That is far across the world. I want them to feel at home.”

Benny clapped his paws. “I will make a welcome sign! I will make a cozy corner too!”

He gathered art supplies from a little basket. There were crayons, markers, glue, and paper that smelled like new books. There were ribbons that felt smooth and silky. There were stickers of stars, trees, and smiling faces.

Benny spread the paper on the floor. “Welcome!” he said softly, then he wrote the letters slowly with a blue marker. The marker squeaked. Squeak, squeak, squeak.

He drew a little beaver with a big tail. He drew a mountain like the one on the map. He drew a sun with warm yellow rays. He stuck on tree stickers and star stickers too.

“Oh no!” Benny gasped. His paw bumped the glue. Blob! Sticky glue dripped onto the sign. “Oops,” he said. “It’s not perfect now.”

Benny felt a small frown. His tail drooped. For a moment, he wanted to stop. He looked at the map again. The green dot smiled at him. He took a deep breath.

“I can fix it,” Benny said. “I can try another way.”

He pressed a soft blue ribbon over the glue spot. He made it into a bow. He added shiny star stickers around the bow. The spot looked pretty now. Benny smiled. “Mistakes can become ideas,” he said.

He put the welcome sign on the door. The tape made a tiny rip sound. Rip, rip. Benny liked that sound. It felt like a job well done.

Next, Benny padded to the corner by the window. He fluffed a pillow. Puff! He draped a blanket with colorful squares. He put a small basket of books on the floor. The books smelled like stories and adventures.

“What else can help our guest feel at home?” he wondered. He looked again at the map. Peru had tall mountains and big forests. “Maybe a little picture of their home.”

Benny flipped open a book about the world. He found a page with a picture of mountains and a llama. He drew a friendly llama with a long neck and kind eyes. He colored it brown and white. He added a tiny heart. He placed the picture by the books.

He listened to the room. The curtains whispered when the wind came. The blanket felt warm. The lemon soap smell was sweet and bright. It felt welcoming.

Just then, Benny heard a bump. Clunk! He turned. His paws had knocked over the basket of markers. Colors rolled across the floor. Red, green, blue, purple. They rolled under the bed.

“Oh dear,” said Benny. He looked under the bed. Dust made his nose wiggle. “Ah-choo!” He sneezed. He could not reach the last marker. He felt a little frustrated.

Benny sat down. He patted his tail. Thump, thump. “I can solve this,” he said calmly. “I will try a new tool.”

He found a wooden spoon in the hallway. He lay on his tummy. He stretched the spoon under the bed. Tap, tap, tap. He gently rolled the purple marker out. “Got you!” Benny giggled. “I kept trying.”

The front door creaked. “Hello?” called a warm voice. “I’m María. I came from far away to visit!”

Benny’s heart jumped. “Welcome!” he said, hurrying to the guest room door. His welcome sign shone with stars and the blue bow.

María stepped in with a small suitcase. She had curious eyes and a happy smile. She looked at the cozy corner. She touched the soft blanket. “Oh! This feels so nice,” she said.

She saw the picture of the llama. Her eyes got shiny. “This looks like my home,” María said softly. “Thank you. You made me feel cared for.”

Benny’s chest felt warm. “I wanted you to feel welcome,” he said. “I used my ideas. I tried again when I made mistakes.”

María nodded. “That is creative and brave,” she said. “Where I live, we share blankets and stories. We drink warm tea and listen to the wind.”

Benny’s ears perked. “Here, we have apple slices and story time,” he said. “Would you like to share?”

“Yes, please,” María said.

They sat in the cozy corner. Crunch went the apples. The pages of the book went flip, flip. The wind whispered at the window. Benny read a simple story. María pointed to the map on the wall. “The world is big,” she said. “We are neighbors even when we are far.”

Benny smiled. “We can learn from each other,” he said. “We can be kind and creative.”

That night, María tucked the blanket under her chin. “Good night, Benny,” she said. “Thank you for my welcoming room.”

“Good night,” said Benny. His tail thumped softly. “Today I learned that creativity helps me solve problems. When I keep trying, I can make something beautiful. And when I care about others, the world feels close.”

The End.
