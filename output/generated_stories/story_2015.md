# Koda's Big Discovery

In a cozy home office, a calm koala named Koda sat on a soft blue chair. The room smelled like fresh paper and pencils. Sunlight streamed through the big window, making everything warm and bright.

Koda's mama worked at her computer. Click, click, click went the keyboard. Koda loved the gentle tapping sounds. But today, something was different. On mama's desk sat a big, mysterious box.

"What's in there, Mama?" Koda asked, pointing with his fuzzy paw.

"It's a surprise for later," Mama said with a smile. "But you can help me work first."

Koda tried to focus on his coloring book. He drew purple circles and green squares. But his eyes kept looking at that box. It was brown and had tiny holes on the sides. What could be inside?

The box made a soft rustling sound. Scritch, scratch, scritch.

"Mama, the box is making noise!" Koda said, his ears perking up.

"Is it?" Mama asked, not looking up from her computer.

Koda felt curious and a little worried. Should he tell Mama again? Maybe she didn't hear him. He took a deep breath. Being honest was always the right thing to do.

"Mama, I really think something is moving in there," Koda said louder.

This time, Mama looked up. "Oh my! You're very observant, Koda. You discovered something important."

Mama walked over to the box. "This was supposed to be a surprise for after lunch, but since you were so good at noticing, let's look together."

Koda's heart beat fast with excitement. Thump, thump, thump! What would they find?

Mama carefully opened the box. Inside was the softest, tiniest hamster Koda had ever seen. It had golden fur and bright black eyes like little buttons.

"Meet Hazel," Mama said. "She's going to live with us. I thought you might feel lonely when I work."

Koda gasped. "She's so small! Will she like me?"

"Let's find out," Mama said. "But we need to be very gentle and quiet. Hazel is new here, just like when you started preschool."

Koda remembered his first day at school. He felt scared and shy. "I was nervous too," he whispered.

Mama helped Koda hold out his paw very slowly. Hazel sniffed with her tiny pink nose. Then she stepped onto Koda's paw! Her little feet felt like feathers tickling his fur.

"She likes me!" Koda said softly, trying not to scare his new friend.

"You were very brave to tell me about the sounds," Mama said. "Your honesty helped us take good care of Hazel. And your gentle voice makes her feel safe."

Koda felt proud and happy. His chest felt warm and fuzzy inside. "Can I help take care of her?"

"Of course! You discovered she was here, so you can be her special friend."

They set up Hazel's new home together. Koda helped pour the soft bedding and fill her tiny water bottle. Hazel explored her new space, running on her wheel and stuffing her cheeks with seeds.

"Look, Mama! She's smiling!" Koda giggled.

"Just like you are," Mama said, giving Koda a gentle hug.

From that day on, Koda learned that being curious and honest led to wonderful discoveries. And sometimes, the best surprises come from paying attention to the world around you.

Hazel became Koda's best friend, and the home office became their favorite place to play and learn together.

The End.
