# Rosie Rabbit's Special Art Day

Rosie Rabbit hopped into the warm, sunny kitchen where Mama was making breakfast. The smell of fresh pancakes filled the air, and steam danced up from Mama's coffee cup.

"Good morning, Mama!" Rosie said, her whiskers twitching with excitement. "Today I want to make the most beautiful painting ever!"

Mama smiled and gave Rosie a gentle hug. "That sounds wonderful, little one. What are you planning to paint?"

Rosie's ears perked up. "I want to paint a rainbow with flowers and butterflies! It will be so pretty!"

"That does sound lovely," Mama said, flipping a golden pancake. "But making a beautiful painting takes planning. What supplies will you need?"

Rosie thought for a moment, then started hopping around the kitchen. "Oh! I need paints and brushes and paper and... and..." She stopped hopping. "I don't know what else!"

Mama sat down at the wooden kitchen table with Rosie. The morning sunlight made everything feel warm and cozy. "Let's make a plan together. First, what colors do you need for a rainbow?"

"Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple!" Rosie counted on her tiny paws.

"Very good! And what about the flowers and butterflies?"

Rosie's eyes grew wide. "I need lots of colors! Pink for roses, yellow for sunflowers, orange for butterfly wings!"

Together, they wrote a list on a piece of paper. Rosie drew little pictures next to each word to help her remember. The pencil felt smooth in her paw as she carefully drew a paintbrush, a cup of water, and paper towels.

When they finished their list, Rosie gathered everything from the art closet. But when she opened her paint box, her heart sank. "Oh no, Mama! My blue paint is all dried up, and I don't have any purple!"

Rosie felt tears starting in her eyes. She really wanted to make her rainbow perfect.

"What should we do?" Mama asked gently.

Rosie sniffled. "Maybe I could just skip the blue and purple? Or maybe I could tell everyone I painted them, even though I didn't?"

Mama sat beside Rosie and rubbed her soft ears. "How would that make you feel inside?"

Rosie thought about it. The kitchen was quiet except for the gentle tick-tock of the clock. "It would make me feel yucky inside. That wouldn't be honest."

"You're right, sweet pea. Being honest is always the best choice, even when things don't go as planned."

Suddenly, Rosie's ears stood straight up. "I have an idea! What if I mix colors to make new ones? Red and blue make purple! And I can use my light blue for the sky blue in the rainbow!"

"That's brilliant planning!" Mama clapped her paws together.

Rosie spent the morning carefully mixing colors on her palette. She discovered that yellow and blue made a beautiful green, and red and white made the prettiest pink. The kitchen smelled like paint and creativity.

When she finished her painting, it was even more beautiful than she had imagined. The rainbow sparkled with mixed colors, flowers bloomed in every shade, and butterflies danced across the paper.

"I'm so proud of you, Rosie," Mama said, admiring the artwork. "You planned carefully, solved problems, and stayed honest about what you could do."

Rosie beamed with pride. "Planning made everything better! And being honest felt good in my heart."

That evening, they hung Rosie's rainbow painting on the refrigerator, where it brightened the whole kitchen with its cheerful colors.

The End.
