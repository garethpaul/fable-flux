# Ellie’s Porch Painting Helpers

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft ears and a curious trunk. She loved to sit on her peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like warm wood and lemon soap. A little breeze tickled Ellie’s ears. Birds sang, “Cheep-cheep,” and a butterfly danced by.

Mama Elephant came out with a big box. “Ellie,” Mama said kindly, “our porch needs a bright, happy welcome sign. Would you like to help paint it?”

Ellie’s eyes sparkled. “Yes, please! I want to learn. I can practice my painting skills.”

Mama smiled. “We will build your skills step by step. We will follow directions. That helps us do our best work.”

Mama set the box down. Inside were paintbrushes, big and small. There were cups of paint—red, blue, yellow, green, and white. There was a wooden board for the sign. There were rags and a small water bowl.

Ellie sniffed the wood. “It smells like a tree,” she said.

Mama nodded. “First, we set up. Step one: Put on aprons. Step two: Lay the board flat. Step three: Place the paints and water in a line. Step four: Wipe the brushes when we change colors. Following directions keeps our art neat.”

Ellie wiggled into her little apron. It felt smooth and safe. “Step one, done!” she chirped.

“Good,” said Mama. “Now we choose our plan. Plans help us feel ready.”

Ellie thought. She tapped her trunk gently. “I want big sunshine and flowers. And the words: ‘Welcome Friends!’”

“That is a kind plan,” Mama said. “It is also community service. The sign helps everyone who comes. It says, ‘You belong here.’”

Just then, neighbors came by. Mr. Duck waddled up with a smile. “Quack, may I watch?”

“Of course,” Ellie said. She felt proud and a little shy. “I am practicing my skills.”

Little Squirrel hurried over, tail swishing. “Can I help?”

Ellie took a breath. She remembered to be assertive and kind. “Yes, you can help after we set the rules,” she said. “We will take turns. We will follow directions. We will use gentle hands.”

Squirrel nodded. “I can do that!”

Mama guided them. “Ellie, you lead.”

Ellie dipped a big brush into yellow paint. She felt the brush’s soft bristles. Swish, swish. She made a bright round sun. The paint smelled a little like fruit. “Sunshine!” she giggled.

“Beautiful,” Mama said. “Skill-building step: Press gently and move slowly.”

Ellie tried again. Swish, swish. She made sun rays that reached like kind fingers. “I did it!”

“Now my turn?” asked Squirrel.

Ellie stood tall. “Yes,” she said clearly. “Please use the small brush for petals. Wipe your brush when you change colors.”

Squirrel dipped into red. Dab, dab. A flower grew under the sun.

Mr. Duck flapped happily. “Quack! May I paint a leaf?”

“Yes,” Ellie said. “Use the green cup and the small brush.” Ellie watched. She liked leading with calm words. It felt good to speak up and be kind.

Mama whispered, “You are using your voice in a strong, gentle way. That is assertiveness.”

Ellie smiled. “Thank you, Mama.”

A tiny drip of blue fell on the porch step. Plip!

Squirrel gasped. “Oh no!”

Ellie took a deep breath. “It’s okay. We can fix it. Step five: Wipe spills right away.” She handed Squirrel a rag. “Soft circles,” Ellie said.

Squirrel wiped. The drip faded. “All better!”

“Good problem-solving,” Mama said. “You followed directions.”

Birds hopped closer. “Cheep, cheep! We see colors!”

Ellie laughed. “Hello, birds! We are making a welcome sign for everyone.”

The sun on the board glowed bright. Flowers smiled up. Ellie picked up a thin brush. “Now the words,” she said. She spoke slowly, sounding out the letters. “W-e-l-c-o-m-e F-r-i-e-n-d-s!”

Mama guided her trunk. “Keep your hand steady. Breathe in, breathe out.”

Ellie breathed. The brush glided. The letters appeared, neat and friendly. She felt proud. “I am building my painting skills!”

Footsteps pattered. Two Rabbit twins bounced up. “Can we help?” they asked together.

Ellie looked at the sign. “Yes,” she said with a gentle voice. “You can add tiny dots around the flowers. Please use the white paint. Dip, dab, wipe.”

“Dip, dab, wipe!” the twins sang. They worked carefully. Dot, dot, dot.

Soon, the sign was done. The porch looked bright and loving. The air smelled like sunshine and fresh paint. Neighbors smiled. “We feel welcome!” Mr. Duck quacked.

Mama clapped her hands softly. “Team, you followed directions. You took turns. You used gentle hands. You spoke up with kind voices. This is art, and this is community service.”

Ellie stood back and looked. Her heart felt warm, like hot cocoa. “We worked hard,” she said. “We learned new skills.” She touched the wooden board lightly. “We can use these steps again tomorrow.”

Mama nodded. “Yes. Skill-building grows with practice.”

Ellie turned to her friends. “Thank you for helping. Next time, we can paint flower pots for the porch.”

“Cheep, cheep! Quack! Yes!” the birds and Mr. Duck agreed. The Rabbit twins wiggled. Squirrel clapped.

Ellie lifted her trunk and spoke clearly. “Let’s clean up now. Step six: Wash brushes in the water bowl. Step seven: Close the paint lids. Step eight: Hang the sign to dry.”

Everyone helped. Swish went the brushes. Snap went the lids. The sign hung from a hook, shining in the soft breeze.

Ellie sat on the peaceful front porch. She felt calm, proud, and happy. “Today I learned to plan, to follow directions, to take turns, to fix mistakes, and to speak up kindly,” she said. “Skills grow when we practice.”

Mama hugged her. “Yes, little artist. Your gentle heart and careful hands make our porch a welcoming place.”

The friends waved as they went home. The sign swayed and glowed. Welcome Friends! it said to everyone who came.

The End.
