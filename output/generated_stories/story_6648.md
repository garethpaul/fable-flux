# Benny's Building Surprise

Benny the beaver loved to build things. He had soft brown fur and big front teeth that helped him chew wood. Today, he was staying in Grandpa's cozy guest room while his dam was being fixed.

The guest room smelled like fresh flowers. Sunlight streamed through pretty curtains. But Benny felt restless. His paws tapped on the wooden floor.

"I need to build something!" Benny said, looking around the room.

He saw a pile of wooden blocks in the corner. His eyes lit up like stars. "Perfect!" he squeaked with excitement.

Benny started building right away. *Tap, tap, tap!* He stacked blocks high and wide. Soon, he had built a tall tower that reached almost to the ceiling.

Just then, Grandpa's cat, Whiskers, walked into the room. Her gray fur was fluffy, and her green eyes were curious.

"Wow, what a big tower!" Whiskers purred. "Can I help you build?"

Benny's tail slapped the floor. "NO!" he said loudly. "This is MY project! Go away!"

Whiskers' ears drooped down. She felt sad and hurt. "I just wanted to help," she whispered.

Benny kept building, but now the blocks kept falling down. *Crash!* Down came his tower. He tried again. *Crash!* It fell again.

"This is so frustrating!" Benny shouted. His face turned red under his brown fur. He wanted to throw the blocks across the room.

Whiskers watched from the doorway. "Maybe if we worked together, the tower would be stronger," she said gently.

Benny took a deep breath. He remembered what Mama Beaver always told him: "When you feel angry, count to three and think before you speak."

"One... two... three," Benny counted slowly. His breathing became calmer.

"I'm sorry I yelled at you, Whiskers," Benny said in a quiet voice. "I was frustrated, but that wasn't nice. Would you still like to help me?"

Whiskers' whiskers twitched happily. "Yes! I would love to help!"

"How about we take turns?" Benny suggested. "You place one block, then I place one block."

"That sounds fair!" Whiskers agreed.

They worked together peacefully. Whiskers placed a red block carefully. Then Benny added a blue one on top. Back and forth they went, taking turns.

"Your paws are good at making straight walls," Benny noticed.

"And your teeth can make the wooden blocks fit together perfectly!" Whiskers replied.

As they built, they talked about being careful and safe. "Let's not make it too tall," Whiskers said wisely. "We don't want it to fall and hurt someone."

"Good thinking," Benny agreed. "And we should clean up any blocks that fall, so no one trips."

Soon, they had built the most beautiful castle together. It had strong walls, safe towers, and even a little bridge. It was much better than anything Benny could have built alone.

"Look what we made together!" Benny said proudly.

"It's wonderful!" Whiskers purred. "And we used every single block, so nothing was wasted."

Grandpa peeked into the guest room. "What a magnificent castle!" he said with a big smile. "And what good teamwork!"

Benny and Whiskers looked at their creation with happy hearts.

"Thank you for being patient with me when I was angry," Benny said. "Using gentle words and taking turns made everything better."

"Friends help each other," Whiskers replied. "And talking kindly always works better than shouting."

That evening, they carefully took apart their castle and put all the blocks back in their box. Nothing was wasted, and the room was tidy and safe.

As Benny snuggled into his cozy bed, he felt grateful. He had learned that peaceful words, taking turns, and working together could build something much more beautiful than anger ever could.

The End.
