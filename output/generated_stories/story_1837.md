# Splash the Dolphin's Special Hideaway

Deep in the ocean, there lived a playful dolphin named Splash. Splash loved to swim fast, jump high, and play with his ocean friends. But sometimes, when the ocean got too noisy and busy, Splash needed a quiet place to think.

One day, Splash discovered something amazing behind a big coral reef. It was a cozy underwater cave that looked just like a closet! The walls were covered in soft, colorful seaweed that swayed gently like curtains. Smooth, round rocks sat on the sandy floor like little chairs.

"This is perfect!" clicked Splash happily. "My very own hideaway!"

Splash made the cave his special quiet place. He collected shiny shells and arranged them on the rocky shelves. He brought his favorite piece of driftwood to use as a reading spot. The cave smelled like fresh seaweed and felt cool and peaceful.

But soon, Splash had a big problem. His ocean friends Finn the fish and Shelly the seahorse were having a loud argument right outside his hideaway.

"That's MY favorite swimming spot!" squeaked Finn angrily.

"No, it's MINE! I was here first!" neighed Shelly, her tiny fins flapping fast.

Their voices echoed through the water, making Splash's peaceful hideaway feel not-so-peaceful anymore. The arguing sounds bounced off the cave walls - "MINE!" "NO, MINE!" - over and over.

Splash covered his fins over his ears, but the arguing continued. He felt sad that his friends were upset, and frustrated that his quiet place wasn't quiet anymore.

"I need to help them," thought Splash. "But how?"

Then Splash had a wonderful idea! He remembered how his grandmother always said, "When voices get loud, speak soft and kind."

Splash swam out of his hideaway and approached his friends gently. Instead of talking loudly like they were, he spoke in the softest, kindest voice he could make.

"Hello, Finn. Hello, Shelly," whispered Splash sweetly. "I can hear you're both upset. Can you tell me what's wrong?"

Finn and Shelly stopped arguing and looked at Splash. His gentle voice made them want to listen.

"We both want to swim in the sunny spot by the coral," said Finn, much more quietly now.

"And we can't decide who gets to use it," added Shelly, her voice soft too.

Splash smiled. "That sounds like a problem we can solve together. What if you take turns? Finn could swim there in the morning, and Shelly could swim there in the afternoon. Then you could both enjoy the sunny spot!"

Finn's eyes lit up. "That's a great idea!"

"Yes!" agreed Shelly. "We could even swim there together sometimes!"

The three friends hugged with their fins. The ocean felt peaceful again, and Splash's hideaway was quiet once more.

From that day on, whenever his friends had disagreements, they came to Splash's cozy closet hideaway. They would sit on the smooth rocks, surrounded by swaying seaweed, and talk through their problems with kind, gentle voices.

Splash learned that the best way to solve problems was not to hide away, but to use peaceful words and creative thinking. His hideaway became even more special - not just a place to be alone, but a place where friends could come together and solve problems with kindness.

"Peaceful words make everything better," Splash would say, and all his friends agreed.

The End.
