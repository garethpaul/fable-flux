# Daisy's Special Breakfast

In a cozy kitchen filled with morning sunshine, a gentle deer fawn named Daisy stood on her tiny hooves. The warm yellow walls made everything feel happy and bright. Daisy's soft brown spots sparkled like little stars on her fuzzy coat.

Today was special - Daisy had made her very first breakfast all by herself! On the wooden table sat a big bowl of fresh berries she had picked. Red strawberries, blue blueberries, and purple blackberries made a colorful rainbow in the bowl.

"I did it!" Daisy said proudly, her little tail wiggling with excitement. "I made breakfast for me!"

The sweet smell of berries filled the kitchen. Daisy's tummy rumbled loudly. *Grumble, grumble!* She was so hungry and so proud of her beautiful berry bowl.

Just then, *tap, tap, tap* came a gentle sound at the kitchen door.

"Hello?" called a small voice. "I smell something yummy!"

Daisy peeked outside and saw her friend Benny the bunny. His white fur looked messy, and his pink nose was twitching sadly.

"Hi, Benny," Daisy said. "What's wrong? You look upset."

Benny's ears drooped down. "I'm so hungry, Daisy. I looked everywhere for breakfast, but I couldn't find any food. The garden is empty, and I don't know what to do."

Daisy looked at her beautiful bowl of berries, then at her hungry friend. Her tummy rumbled again, reminding her how hungry she was too. For a moment, she felt worried. What if there weren't enough berries for both of them?

"But I worked so hard to pick these," Daisy whispered to herself.

Then Daisy remembered what Mama Deer had taught her: "When we share with friends, the happiness grows bigger, not smaller."

Daisy took a deep breath and smiled. "Benny, would you like to share my berries with me?"

Benny's ears perked up like two furry antennas. "Really? Are you sure?"

"Yes!" Daisy said, feeling warm and happy inside. "Come sit with me!"

They sat together at the sunny table. Daisy pushed the bowl between them. "Try the strawberries first - they're the sweetest!"

Benny took a small bite. *Crunch!* "Mmm! These are delicious, Daisy! You're such a good berry picker!"

As they ate together, something magical happened. The berries tasted even better when shared! They laughed and talked, and soon both their tummies were full and happy.

"Thank you for sharing," Benny said, giving Daisy a gentle hug. "You're the kindest friend ever!"

Daisy felt proud - not just because she had made breakfast, but because she had helped her friend feel better. Her heart felt as warm as the sunny kitchen.

"You know what?" Daisy said with a big smile. "Tomorrow, let's pick berries together! Then we can both help make breakfast."

"That sounds wonderful!" Benny said, his whiskers twitching with joy.

From that day on, Daisy and Benny picked berries together every morning. They learned that sharing made everything more fun - and more delicious too!

As the warm sun filled the kitchen with golden light, Daisy learned something very important: when we share what we have with others, we don't have less - we have more joy, more friendship, and more happiness in our hearts.

The End.
