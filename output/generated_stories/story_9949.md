# Hoppity Says Sorry

Hoppity was a joyful kangaroo joey with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, he sat on the peaceful front porch with his mama. The porch smelled like fresh eucalyptus tea, and a gentle breeze tickled Hoppity’s nose. Birds chirped a happy song, and the wooden boards felt warm under his big feet.

Mama poured tea. “Good morning, Hoppity,” she said. “Today is a big day. We will wave hello to the world.”

Hoppity tilted his head. “The whole world? From our porch?”

Mama smiled. “Yes. Friends from faraway places will send messages. We will learn about them, and we will be kind and careful.”

Hoppity clapped his little paws. “I can hop hello!”

On the small porch table sat a colorful globe with tiny stickers. There was a blue sticker for the ocean, a green sticker for forests, and a golden sticker for warm deserts. A bright red sticker marked their home. Beside the globe was a shiny tablet that chimed like tiny bells.

Bing! The tablet lit up. A girl appeared on the screen, waving. “Hello! I’m Amara,” she said. Behind her was a bright market with mangoes, drums, and kites dancing in the sky.

Hoppity gasped. “Wow! Your home looks so fun.”

Amara laughed. “I am in Ghana. Today we drum and dance.”

Hoppity wanted to copy the drums. He thumped his feet on the porch boards. Thump-thump-thump! The sound was loud. The tea cup wobbled. The globe shook. The bright red sticker peeled off and drifted to the floor.

“Oh!” Hoppity frowned. “I didn’t mean to.”

Mama steadied the cup. “Hoppity, your hops were too strong on the porch.”

Amara blinked from the screen. “It is okay,” she said gently.

Bing! Another friend waved. “Hi, I’m Sora,” said a boy with a bright scarf. Snowflakes floated behind him. “I am in Japan. It is winter here.”

Hoppity felt excited again. He bounced to show snow jumps. Boing! His tail brushed the tablet. The screen tilted. The wooden wind chimes banged together with a loud clatter. The sound made Hoppity’s ears twitch. He saw Mama’s eyes grow wide.

Hoppity’s tummy felt tight, like a knot. He looked at Amara and Sora on the screen. “I’m sorry,” he whispered. “I was too bouncy.”

Mama knelt and looked into his eyes. Her voice was soft and warm. “Saying sorry is brave. When we say sorry, we care for other people and their things. We are part of a big world.”

Hoppity took a deep breath. He felt the porch air, cool and clean. He heard the birds again. He spoke clearly. “Amara, I am sorry I shook the globe and scared your drums. Sora, I am sorry I bumped the tablet and made loud chimes.”

Amara smiled. “Thank you, Hoppity. Your words are kind.”

Sora nodded. “I accept your apology. Maybe we can do gentle jumps.”

Hoppity’s shoulders loosened. His tummy unknot. “Gentle jumps,” he said. He bent his knees and made a small hop. Tap. The porch boards made a soft sound like a quiet heartbeat.

Bing! A new message came from a girl named Lila in Brazil. Bright green leaves swayed behind her. Birds with rainbow feathers squawked. “Oi!” she said. “I am planting a tree with my grandma.”

Hoppity’s eyes grew shiny. He wanted to help, but he remembered. Gentle. He pressed his paw to his chest. “Hello, Lila. I will listen. I will be careful.”

They all took turns sharing. Amara drummed softly—dum, dum, dum. Sora showed a paper crane and folded it slowly. Lila held up a tiny seed and patted the soil. Hoppity listened. He smelled Mama’s tea. He felt the smooth wood of the porch rail. His heart felt calm.

Then Hoppity spoke. “I live here,” he said, pointing to the red sticker Mama had put back on the globe. “The sun feels warm. The porch is peaceful. I like to hop. I am learning gentle hops.”

Mama kissed his forehead. “We are neighbors with the whole world,” she said. “Being a kind neighbor means listening, taking care, and saying sorry when we make a mistake.”

Hoppity nodded. “Saying sorry helps,” he said. “It makes room for smiles.”

Amara drummed a happy rhythm. Sora made his paper crane flap. Lila waved with muddy hands. Everyone laughed.

Hoppity took one more tiny hop. Tap. He looked at the screen. “Friends, if I forget and hop too hard again, I will say sorry right away. I will try to fix it. I will learn.”

Amara said, “That is good global friendship.”

Sora said, “We care for each other, near and far.”

Lila said, “We share our big world.”

Hoppity breathed in the fresh porch air and felt the warm boards under his feet. “I am a good neighbor,” he said. “I can say sorry. I can be gentle.”

Mama raised her cup. “To kind hearts and careful feet.”

Hoppity raised his paws. “To friends around the world!”

The porch felt extra peaceful. The birds sang. The wind chimes tinkled softly. Hoppity’s heart glowed like a small, cozy lantern. He had made a mistake. He had said sorry. He had learned. And now, he felt ready to be a kind friend to the whole wide world.

The End.
