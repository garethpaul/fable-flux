# Bubbles on the Front Porch

Early one calm morning, a playful dolphin named Dippy peeked over the white rail of a peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like fresh pine wood and sweet flowers. A little wind made the chimes go tinkle-tinkle. Sunlight made a warm stripe across the floorboards.

“Good morning, porch!” Dippy chirped, splashing his tail in a big bucket of water he kept by the steps. “Today is Clean-and-Play Day!”

Every day, Dippy visited the porch. It was part of his happy routine. He would sweep, water the porch plants, and then play a game. Sweep, water, play. It felt cozy and safe.

Dippy put on his tiny blue apron. He wiggled with joy. “Time to sweep-sweep!” he sang. He picked up a little broom with his flipper and swished it across the boards. Swish, swish, swish. Dust floated in the sunshine like soft snowflakes.

Just then, a small snail, Sammy, peeked from a flower pot. “Good morning, Dippy,” Sammy whispered. “You look busy.”

“I am!” Dippy smiled. “It’s Clean-and-Play Day. Would you like to help?”

Sammy’s eyes twinkled. “I can help slow and steady. I can pick tiny bits.”

“Perfect,” said Dippy. “Slow and steady is good.”

They worked together. Dippy swept big leaves. Swish, swish. Sammy picked tiny petals. Plink, plink. The porch smelled even fresher, like lemon soap and wet wood.

A little squirrel named Suri scampered onto the rail. Her tail flick-flicked. “What are you doing?” she asked.

“Cleaning so we can play,” Dippy said. “Sweeping and watering, then games!”

“I like games!” Suri chirped. “Can I help clean first?”

“Yes!” said Dippy. “We help, then we play.”

Suri gathered acorn caps into a neat pile. Clack, clack, clack. She hummed a happy tune. Everyone smiled. Working together felt warm and kind.

When they finished sweeping, Dippy poured water into a bright green watering can. He sniffed the flowers. Mmm. They smelled sweet and soft. “Plants need a drink,” he said. “We take care of our porch. We take care of our world.”

Sammy nodded. “Little care makes a big difference.”

Dippy poured gently. Drip, drip, drip. The flowers seemed to sit up taller. Suri patted the soil. “Comfy now,” she said.

Suddenly, a gust of wind whooshed across the yard. Whoosh! A crinkly plastic bag flew onto the porch. It flapped like a silly bird and got stuck on a step.

“Oh no,” Dippy gasped. “That bag could hurt the flowers. It could blow to the pond.”

Sammy curled in his shell. “It’s big,” he peeped.

Suri’s eyes were wide. “What do we do?”

Dippy took a deep breath. He felt a little worried, but he remembered: We can help together.

“We will use teamwork,” Dippy said. “I will hold the bag. Suri, can you keep it from flying? Sammy, can you open the recycling bin?”

“Yes!” said Suri.

“I can,” said Sammy.

Dippy flapped water on the bag to make it heavy. Splash. Suri pressed the corner with her tiny paws. Sammy slid to the bin, inch by inch. “Almost there,” he said, steady and brave.

“Ready?” Dippy asked.

“Ready!” said Suri and Sammy.

“One, two, three—cooperate!” Dippy cheered.

Together they lifted the bag. Rustle, rustle. They dropped it into the blue bin with a soft thump. The wind blew again, but the bag stayed safe inside.

“We did it!” Suri giggled, hopping in a little circle.

Sammy smiled shyly. “Teamwork is strong.”

Dippy felt proud and calm. The porch looked tidy. The flowers were happy. The air felt clean.

“Now,” Dippy said, “it’s time to play!”

They decided on a bubble game. Dippy dipped a big bubble wand into the bucket. He blew. Whoooo. A giant rainbow bubble floated up, shining pink and blue. It smelled like soapy lemons.

“My turn!” Suri squeaked. She blew tiny bubbles that went pop-pop-pop. Sammy blew very gently. A small bubble sat on his shell and wiggled like a jellybean. Everyone laughed.

“Let’s make a bubble train,” Dippy said. “We will take turns. First me, then Suri, then Sammy. We wait and we cheer.”

They did. Dippy made a big bubble. Suri clapped. Sammy said, “Go, go, go!” Then Suri made three bubbles in a row. Pop, pop, pop. Dippy and Sammy cheered. Last, Sammy made one brave bubble. It touched a flower and didn’t pop. “Amazing!” said Dippy. “Careful and kind.”

The sun slid a little lower. The chimes went tinkle-tinkle again. It was almost time to rest.

Dippy put the broom away. He closed the bin. He checked the plants. “Thank you, friends,” he said. “We kept our porch clean. We took care of our world. We helped each other. That feels good.”

Suri nodded. “Helping makes play more fun.”

Sammy whispered, “Teamwork makes big jobs small.”

Dippy smiled. “Our routine works: we clean, we care, we play. We do it together.”

They sat on the peaceful porch, quiet and happy. A soft breeze touched their faces. The flowers swayed. The day felt safe and bright.

Lesson: When we cooperate, we can care for our home and each other. Helping together makes work easier and play happier.

The End.
