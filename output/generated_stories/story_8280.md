# Splash the Dolphin's Garden Adventure

Splash the dolphin lived in a magical water tank on Mrs. Chen's sunny balcony garden. The tank was special—it had windows all around so Splash could see the beautiful flowers, buzzing bees, and colorful butterflies.

One bright morning, Splash woke up feeling excited. "Today I want to help in the garden!" he chirped, wiggling his fins happily.

Mrs. Chen smiled and filled a special water sprinkler. "Would you like to help me water the plants, Splash?"

"Yes, yes!" Splash squeaked with joy. He loved helping his friend.

Mrs. Chen carefully lifted Splash in a small water bowl and placed him near the tomato plants. "Just splash gently, little friend. The plants need soft, gentle water."

Splash tried his best, but WHOOSH! His excited splash sent water flying everywhere—on the flowers, on Mrs. Chen, and all over the balcony floor.

"Oh no!" Mrs. Chen gasped, wiping water from her glasses.

Splash felt his tummy get hot and tight. His fins started to shake. "I... I messed up!" he cried. "I'm so bad at helping!" His voice got louder and angrier. "I hate being clumsy! I hate everything!"

Mrs. Chen sat down next to Splash's bowl. She spoke in a calm, gentle voice. "Splash, I can see you're feeling very angry. That's okay. Everyone feels angry sometimes."

"But I ruined everything!" Splash huffed, his tail slapping the water.

"Let's try something special," Mrs. Chen said softly. "Close your eyes and take three deep breaths with me. Breathe in the sweet smell of the jasmine flowers."

Splash closed his eyes. He breathed in slowly. The jasmine smelled like sunshine and honey. "That smells nice," he whispered.

"Now breathe out slowly," Mrs. Chen said. "Let all that angry feeling float away like a bubble."

Splash breathed out. His fins stopped shaking. His tummy felt cooler.

"Again," Mrs. Chen encouraged. "Breathe in the fresh morning air."

Splash took another deep breath. He could hear the gentle buzzing of bees and the soft rustle of leaves. His heart felt calmer.

"How do you feel now?" Mrs. Chen asked.

"Better," Splash said quietly. "But I still made a big mess."

Mrs. Chen smiled. "Would you like to try again? This time, we'll practice together. Small splashes, just like raindrops."

Splash nodded. "Yes, please. I want to learn."

This time, Mrs. Chen showed Splash how to make tiny, gentle splashes. "Like this—soft and slow," she demonstrated.

Splash tried again. Pitter-pat, pitter-pat. Perfect little drops landed on the thirsty plants.

"I did it!" Splash giggled. "Look at the plants drinking up the water!"

The tomato plants seemed to sparkle in the sunlight. A yellow butterfly landed on a nearby marigold and fluttered its wings as if saying "thank you."

"You see, Splash?" Mrs. Chen said, gently patting his fin. "When we feel angry, we can stop, breathe, and try again. Making mistakes is how we learn."

Splash beamed with pride. "Next time I feel angry, I'll remember to breathe like the flowers do—slow and peaceful."

From that day on, whenever Splash felt frustrated, he would close his eyes, smell the garden flowers, and take three deep breaths. He learned that anger was just a feeling that would pass, like clouds in the sky.

And Mrs. Chen's balcony garden grew more beautiful every day, with help from her mindful little dolphin friend.

The End.
