# Whiskers Says Thank You

Whiskers was a clever cat with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a cozy bedroom with a big, fluffy rug and a warm, sunny window. Every morning, the room smelled like clean sheets and a little like lavender. The pillow was squishy, the blanket was warm, and the floor was cool under tiny paws.

“Good morning, room!” Whiskers purred as he stretched long, his tail making a gentle S-shape. “Thank you, soft bed, for a good sleep.”

He hopped down and padded to his water bowl. Sip, sip, sip. “Thank you, water, for being fresh and cool,” he said. The water made his tongue tingle, and that felt nice.

Whiskers liked to start the day tidy. He looked around at his toys—balls, blocks, and a little mouse with a jingle bell. Some were under the bed. Some were on the rug. “Hmm,” he said, tapping his whiskers. “I can do this myself.”

He made a plan. “First, I put the balls in the basket. Second, I stack the blocks. Third, I place the mouse by my bed.” He spoke slowly, using his paw to count. One, two, three.

Plink, plink, plink went the colorful balls into the basket. Clack, clack went the wooden blocks. Jingle, jingle went the little mouse. Whiskers smiled. “Thank you, paws, for helping me. Thank you, brain, for good ideas.”

His friend, a small stuffed rabbit named Bun-Bun, sat on the pillow. Bun-Bun did not talk, but Whiskers liked to talk to him anyway. “Bun-Bun, this room is our cozy nest,” Whiskers said. “Let’s keep it clean.” He smoothed the blanket and fluffed the pillow. The blanket felt soft like clouds.

His tummy gave a tiny rumble. “Time for breakfast,” he said. He opened the drawer where his little dish lived. He scooped his crunchy food into the dish. Crunch, crunch, crunch. “Thank you, food, for giving me energy,” he purred. He licked his whiskers clean.

After breakfast, Whiskers wanted to explore. He noticed a small sparkle under the dresser. “What is that?” he whispered. He slid on his belly and reached with a careful paw. His fur brushed the smooth floor, cool and a little dusty. “Aha!” He pulled out a shiny button.

The button was round and blue. It felt cool and smooth. “What a neat discovery!” Whiskers said. “Thank you, eyes, for seeing. Thank you, paws, for finding.” He set the button in a little dish on his shelf, a special place for treasures.

Next, he listened. He heard the soft hum of the heater and a gentle tick-tock from the clock. He heard a bird chirp at the window. “Chirp-chirp-chirp!” Whiskers chirped back. “Thank you, ears, for listening. Thank you, bird, for your song.”

Whiskers brushed his fur with his little brush. Swish, swish. “Brushing keeps me healthy,” he told Bun-Bun. “I can do it all by myself.” He brushed his back, his cheeks, even the tip of his tail. It felt cozy and calm. “Thank you, brush, for helping me care for me.”

After tidying and brushing, Whiskers felt proud. His cozy bedroom looked neat. The sun made warm stripes on the rug. He curled up in the sun and closed his eyes for a moment. He breathed in and out. In, out. Slow and happy.

Then he remembered one more thing—his gratitude list. Whiskers kept it in his heart, but he liked to say it out loud. “Today, I am thankful for my bed, my water, my food, my toys, my brush, my room, and my brave, helpful self.” He paused and smiled. “And Bun-Bun, too.”

He stood up and checked his list in the room. Bed made? Yes. Toys away? Yes. Dish washed? He carried his dish to the little mat and washed it with a soft cloth. Swish, swish. “Thank you, soap, for bubbles,” he giggled.

Just then, a breeze came through the window. It smelled like fresh air and a hint of sunshine. A tiny paper star fluttered down from the bookshelf. “Oh!” Whiskers said. He picked it up. On the star were small letters: Be thankful.

Whiskers placed the star next to the blue button in the treasure dish. “A reminder,” he said. “When I say thank you, I feel warm and brave. When I care for my things, my room cares for me. When I help myself, I grow strong.”

He hugged Bun-Bun gently and looked around the cozy bedroom one more time. Everything had a place. Everything felt calm and bright. Whiskers took a deep breath. “Thank you, cozy room,” he whispered. “Thank you for today.”

He curled up on the warm rug, tail tucked, paws under chin. The clock ticked softly. The sun made him sleepy in a happy way. He felt safe, clean, and loved.

“Tomorrow,” he murmured, “I will say thank you again.”

The End.
