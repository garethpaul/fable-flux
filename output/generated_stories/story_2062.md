# Fawn’s Warm Kitchen Feelings

In a warm kitchen, sunlight made golden squares on the floor. A gentle deer fawn named Fawn stood on soft hooves by a little wooden stool. The room smelled like sweet apples and cozy cinnamon. A kettle hummed. Butter sizzled in a pan.

“I want to help,” Fawn whispered. Her ears twitched. Her heart felt jumpy. She wanted to be helpful all by herself. She also wanted a friend to smile with her.

Fawn lifted a small bowl. Clink! The bowl tapped the table. She placed red apple slices in a neat circle. But one slice slid off. Plop! It fell on the floor.

“Oh no,” Fawn said. Her tummy felt tight. Her cheeks felt warm. “I made a mistake.”

From the window, a sparrow chirped. “Tweep-tweep! Try again, friend!” But Fawn still felt wobbly inside.

Just then, the door creaked. Fawn’s friend, a fluffy gray bunny named Bop, hopped in. His nose twitched. He sniffed the cinnamon air. His ears perked like little spoons.

“Hi, Fawn!” Bop said. “What are you making? It smells like hugs.”

“I’m making apple pancakes,” Fawn said softly. “I want to do it by myself. But I feel… funny. I feel tight and squiggly.”

Bop nodded. “You feel worried,” he said. “Sometimes I feel worried too. Can I sit with you?”

Fawn looked at the apple slice on the floor. She looked at Bop’s kind eyes. Her heart slowed down a little. “Yes,” she said. “Please sit. But I still want to try on my own.”

Bop hopped onto the stool. “I won’t touch,” he said. “I can cheer.”

Fawn took a deep breath in. She smelled apples. She let a slow breath out. The breath made her shoulders loosen.

“I can talk to my feelings,” Fawn said. “Hello, worry. You can ride on my tail, but I will steer.”

Bop smiled. “Good plan!”

Fawn wiped the floor with a little rag. Swish-swish. She washed her hooves. Splash-splash. Then she tried again. She cracked an egg into the bowl. Tap-tap. The egg slid in. Plop! A tiny shell fell too.

Fawn frowned. “Oh dear.”

Bop said, “You can fix it. You are strong and gentle.”

Fawn used a spoon to scoop the shell. Scoot-scoop! Out it came. She felt a warm glow in her chest. “I did it.”

The pan hissed softly. The batter made a happy sound: pffff. Fawn poured a small circle. It puffed up like a cloud. She waited. She sniffed. When it smelled toasty, she slid the spatula under. Her hooves trembled.

“I’m scared to flip,” she said.

Bop wiggled his whiskers. “You can say a bravery word.”

Fawn nodded. “Brave-hooves,” she whispered. “I can do hard things.”

She flipped. Whoosh—plop! The pancake landed a little crooked, but it stayed in the pan. Fawn giggled. “It’s a silly pancake.”

“Silly is tasty,” Bop said. He clapped his soft paws. “Tappity-tap!”

They made more pancakes. Some were small like buttons. Some were big like the moon. The kitchen felt warm and safe. The windows hummed with afternoon bees. The butter smelled nutty. Fawn’s worry grew smaller and smaller, like a dot.

Then the batter bowl was almost empty. Fawn looked at Bop. “Thank you for cheering,” she said. “You didn’t do the cooking, but your friendship helped my heart.”

Bop’s eyes shone. “Friends can help by being near. Friends can listen. Friends can say kind words.”

Fawn set two plates on the table. Clink. She added apple slices and a little honey. Drip-drip. She poured warm milk into two small cups. Steam curled like tiny ribbons.

“Let’s eat,” Fawn said. “We worked together. I tried by myself, and you helped my feelings.”

They took bites. Mmm. The pancakes were soft and sweet. Cinnamon tickled their noses. Fawn felt proud. Her chest felt light and sunny.

“I learned something,” Fawn said. “I can be brave and try new things. When I feel worried, I can breathe. I can fix small mistakes. And I can ask a friend to sit with me.”

Bop nodded. “I learned too. When a friend wants to do it by themselves, I can cheer. I can listen. I don’t have to take over. That is friendship.”

Fawn smiled. “Friendship is sharing kind words. Friendship is feelings-safe. Friendship helps us grow.”

They finished the last pancake. Fawn licked a honey drop from her nose. Boop. Bop laughed, and his laugh sounded like little bells.

The warm kitchen felt extra cozy now. The golden sunlight moved across the floor like a soft blanket. The kettle sighed one last time.

Fawn washed the dishes. Swish-swish. She dried them. Pat-pat. Bop stacked them carefully. Click-click. They worked side by side, calm and happy.

Fawn whispered, “Thank you, friend.”

Bop whispered back, “Thank you, brave chef.”

Fawn’s heart felt steady and strong. She had cooked with courage. She had listened to her feelings. She had let friendship help without doing the work for her. She knew she could try new things again.

The lesson was warm and sweet, like the pancakes: Be kind to your feelings. Be brave and try. Let friends support you. And cheer for your friends, too.

The End.
