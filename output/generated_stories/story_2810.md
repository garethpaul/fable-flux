# Benny Bear's Big Office Mystery

Benny Bear was a small, fluffy cub with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. Today, he was visiting Mama Bear's home office while she worked on her computer. The office smelled like vanilla candles and had tall bookshelves filled with colorful books.

"Mama, what's that clicking sound?" Benny asked, tilting his fuzzy head.

"That's me typing on my keyboard, little one," Mama Bear said with a warm smile. "I'm writing important letters for work."

Benny loved exploring, so he wandered around the cozy office. He noticed something strange on the floor near Mama's desk. "Oh no!" he gasped. "Mama, your favorite coffee mug is broken!"

Mama Bear looked down and saw her special blue mug in pieces. It had tiny flowers painted on it and was a gift from Grandma Bear. "Oh dear," she sighed sadly. "I wonder how that happened."

Benny's little nose twitched as he sniffed around. He was very curious and wanted to help solve this mystery. "Don't worry, Mama! I'll figure out what happened," he said bravely.

First, Benny looked for clues like a detective. He crawled under the desk and found something interesting - tiny paw prints in the dust! They were much smaller than his own paws.

"Hmm," Benny thought out loud, scratching his round ear. "These prints are too small to be mine or Mama's."

Then he heard a tiny "meow" from behind the bookshelf. Benny's ears perked up. He was being very resourceful, using all his senses to solve the problem.

"Hello?" Benny called softly. "Is someone there?"

A small orange kitten peeked out nervously. Her whiskers trembled, and her green eyes looked scared.

"I'm sorry!" the kitten cried. "I was chasing a butterfly that flew in through the window. I jumped on the desk and accidentally knocked over the pretty mug. I was too scared to tell anyone!"

Benny's heart felt warm. He could see the kitten was really sorry. "What's your name?" he asked kindly.

"I'm Penny," the kitten whispered. "I live next door, but I got lost."

Benny thought carefully. He wanted to be fair and help everyone feel better. That's what justice meant - making things right for everyone.

"Penny, it's okay that you made a mistake," Benny said gently. "But we should tell Mama what happened and try to fix it together."

Benny was being very resourceful. He had an idea! "Mama," he called. "I found out what happened to your mug, and I think we can make it better!"

Benny explained everything to Mama Bear, who listened carefully. She wasn't angry at all.

"Thank you for being honest, Penny," Mama Bear said warmly. "And Benny, you did wonderful detective work!"

Together, they used special glue to fix the mug. It looked almost as good as new! Then Mama Bear called Penny's family, who were very worried and grateful to find her.

"You know what, Benny?" Mama Bear said, giving him a big, fuzzy hug. "You used your curiosity to ask good questions, your resourcefulness to solve the problem, and your sense of justice to help everyone feel better."

Benny's chest puffed up with pride. He had learned that being resourceful means using your brain, your senses, and your creativity to solve problems and help others.

From that day on, whenever there was a problem to solve, everyone knew they could count on Benny Bear!

The End.
