# Ollie Otter's Big Backyard Plan

Ollie the otter loved splashing in the sunny backyard. The warm sun tickled his whiskers, and the cool grass felt soft under his paws. He had the most wonderful idea ever!

"I want to build the biggest, most amazing water slide in the whole backyard!" Ollie squeaked with excitement. He could already imagine sliding down into his favorite kiddie pool.

Ollie started gathering sticks and leaves, making a big pile right in the middle of the yard. But soon, he heard a tiny voice.

"Excuse me," chirped Ruby the robin from her nest in the oak tree. "That's where my babies learn to fly. Could you please move your pile?"

Ollie's tail drooped. "But I need this spot! It's perfect for my water slide!"

Just then, Benny the bunny hopped over. "Oh no!" he gasped. "That's where I eat my breakfast carrots every morning. The sun shines just right there!"

Now Ollie felt confused. He really wanted his water slide, but his friends seemed upset. His mama had taught him to be kind to others.

"Wait," said Ollie, sitting down to think. "Maybe we can figure this out together. What if we make a plan that works for everyone?"

Ruby tilted her head. "What do you mean?"

"Well," Ollie said, his eyes brightening, "what if I move my water slide over there, by the fence? Then you can still teach your babies to fly here, Benny can eat his carrots in the sunshine, and I can have my slide!"

"But," squeaked Benny, "what if the slide is too loud when my little bunnies are napping?"

Ollie thought hard. "How about this - I'll only use my slide in the morning and afternoon, but not during nap time. Would that work?"

Ruby flapped her wings happily. "That sounds fair! And maybe my babies and I could even try your slide sometime?"

"Really?" Ollie's whiskers twitched with joy. "That would be so much fun!"

Benny's nose wiggled. "Could I help you build it? I'm really good at digging!"

"Yes, yes, yes!" Ollie clapped his paws. "We can all work together!"

The three friends spent the sunny morning planning. Ruby would help gather the smoothest sticks from up high. Benny would dig the perfect starting spot. And Ollie would arrange everything just right.

"This is even better than I imagined," Ollie said as they worked. "When we talked about what everyone needed, we made an even better plan!"

By lunchtime, they had built the most wonderful water slide. It was in the perfect spot where everyone was happy. The slide was smooth and safe, and there was still plenty of room for flying lessons and carrot breakfasts.

"Thank you for listening to us," said Ruby, giving Ollie a gentle wing pat.

"Thank you for helping me think of a better way," said Ollie. "Talking together and making a plan that works for everyone is much more fun than doing it all by myself!"

As Ollie splashed down his new slide, he felt the warm sun on his fur and heard his friends cheering. He had learned something very important: when friends talk together and respect each other's needs, they can find wonderful solutions that make everyone happy.

The End.
