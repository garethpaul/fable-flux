# Otter Oki and the Balcony Book

Oki was a playful otter who loved his cozy balcony garden. The garden had tiny pots of mint and basil, bright flowers, and a small birdbath that shimmered in the sun. The air smelled like lemon leaves. The wind felt soft on Oki’s whiskers.

“Good morning, garden!” Oki chirped, clapping his paws.

Today, Oki had a big plan. He wanted to write his very first garden book. He set out a little notebook and a fat blue crayon on the garden table. He also put a small bell beside the notebook.

“Ting-a-ling means it’s my turn,” Oki said to himself, practicing a gentle ring. “I will write, then I will rest. I can wait. I can take turns with my friends.”

Just then, friends arrived. Busy Bee buzzed by the marigolds. Tutu the Turtle waddled from the shade. Pip the Sparrow hopped on the railing, chirping.

“What are you doing, Oki?” Pip asked, head tilting.

“I’m writing our garden book,” Oki said. “We will all take turns. We’ll discover things and write them down. Taking turns helps us share. It helps us be patient.”

Bee sniffed the mint. “Bzzz! I want to go first!”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “I found a shiny seed! My turn!”

Tutu blinked slowly. “I can wait,” he said, smiling. “Waiting can feel calm.”

Oki held up the bell. “We will use the bell for turns. I will write one page, then I will ring. Then you, then me, then you. We can all have time.”

Oki sat and saw a tiny green sprout peeking from the soil. The sprout wore a cap of dirt like a little hat.

Oki whispered, “Hello, baby sprout.” He wrote: “I see a baby sprout. It is small and brave.” He drew a big smile next to the sprout.

Ting-a-ling!

Bee zoomed to the notebook. “My turn!” She sniffed a purple flower and wiggled with joy. “This flower smells sweet like candy clouds!” she said. She wrote, with Oki’s help, “Sweet flower. Bzzz happy.”

Pip hopped in place. “I want a turn now! Now!”

Oki felt his paws twitch. He wanted a turn too. But he took a deep breath. The mint smell helped him feel calm. “We wait kindly,” Oki said. “Waiting is a kind of care. It cares for our hearts.”

Tutu nodded. “Slow is okay.”

Ting-a-ling!

Pip rushed up and bobbed his head over a shiny drop of water on a leaf. “Look! A tiny moon!” He wrote, “Leaf has a tiny moon!” Oki traced a circle to help.

Oki’s tummy made a small gurgle. He remembered self-care. “Snack time!” he said. He took a sip of cool water and a nibble of apple slices. “When we take turns, we also take care. We rest. We sip. We breathe.”

Bee buzzed in a slow circle. “I can wait while I stretch my wings.”

They all stretched. Arms, wings, necks, and paws. The wind felt like a soft blanket. The basil leaves brushed Oki’s fur, and he giggled.

Oki’s turn came again. He listened. Far away, a train rumbled. Close by, the mint made a tiny rustle. He wrote, “I hear rumble far, rustle near.” He smiled. “I am learning to listen.”

Ting-a-ling!

Tutu moved to the book, one careful step at a time. He looked at the birdbath. A bubble popped. “Plip.” He wrote slowly, “Plip pop. Water talks.” He looked up. “I like taking turns,” he said. “It gives me time to see.”

Pip fluttered, but he stayed in line. “I’m waiting. Waiting is hard, but I can do it,” he said, puffing his chest.

Oki felt proud. “We are doing it together,” he said. “Taking turns helps us all share the fun.”

Suddenly, the blue crayon rolled off the table. Tap. Tap. Tap. It almost slid under the plant stand.

“Oh no!” Oki cried.

Tutu stopped it with his shell. “I’ve got it,” he said.

“Thank you,” Oki said. He placed the crayon back and tapped the bell lightly. “Teamwork is part of taking turns too.”

They kept going. Bee found a new blossom and wrote, “New bloom!” Pip found a feather and wrote, “Soft!” Oki felt the sun on his fur and wrote, “Warm.”

The notebook pages filled with colors and words. The garden book smelled like paper and mint. It looked bright and happy.

At last, Oki rang the bell one more time. Ting-a-ling! “Let’s read our book,” he said.

They gathered close. Oki read slowly, with a gentle voice.

“I see a baby sprout. It is small and brave.
Sweet flower. Bzzz happy.
Leaf has a tiny moon!
I hear rumble far, rustle near.
Plip pop. Water talks.
New bloom!
Soft!
Warm.”

When Oki finished, everyone clapped or flapped or tapped.

Pip chirped, “We did it together!”

Bee buzzed, “Taking turns made space for everyone.”

Tutu smiled. “Waiting helped me see more.”

Oki hugged the notebook to his chest. “Today, we learned to take turns. We discovered new things. We were patient. We took care of ourselves with rest, sips, and stretches. And we wrote our garden book!”

The friends sat in the soft light. The bell was quiet. The garden felt calm and bright.

Oki patted the bell. “Tomorrow, we can write more,” he said. “We will take turns again.”

Everyone agreed.

The End.
