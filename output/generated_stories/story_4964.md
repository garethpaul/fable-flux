# Toby's Colorful Sharing Day

In a cozy garden shed filled with flowerpots and gardening tools, lived a wise old tortoise named Toby. His green shell sparkled in the morning sunlight that streamed through the dusty windows. Toby loved his quiet home, but today felt different. Today, he heard excited voices outside.

"I wish I had some paint!" squeaked a small voice.

Toby peeked out of his shell and saw three little mice standing near the shed door. They looked sad as they stared at some plain wooden blocks they had found.

"We want to make pretty art, but we don't have any colors," sighed the smallest mouse named Pip.

Toby's wise old eyes twinkled. He remembered the colorful paint tubes tucked away on his favorite shelf. The gardener had left them there last spring, and Toby had been saving them for a special day.

"Hello there, little friends," Toby said in his gentle, slow voice as he stepped outside. "I couldn't help but hear you talking about art."

The three mice jumped in surprise, then smiled when they saw Toby's kind face.

"Oh! Hello, Mr. Tortoise," said Pip politely. "We found these blocks, but they're so plain and boring."

Toby nodded thoughtfully. "You know, I have something that might help. Would you like to come into my shed?"

The mice's eyes grew wide with excitement as they followed Toby inside. The shed smelled like fresh earth and dried flowers. Toby slowly climbed up to his shelf and carefully brought down a box of colorful paint tubes.

"Wow!" gasped the mice together. "Real paint!"

"But Mr. Tortoise," said Pip carefully, "these are yours. We can't take your special paints."

Toby chuckled, a warm rumbling sound. "My dear friends, sharing makes everything more wonderful. These paints have been waiting for the right moment to create something beautiful."

Toby squeezed out dots of red, blue, and yellow paint onto an old plate. "Let's paint together! But first, we need to exercise our bodies to get ready for art."

"Exercise?" asked the second mouse, Squeaky.

"Yes indeed!" Toby said. "Let's stretch our arms like paintbrushes!" He moved his legs slowly up and down. The mice giggled and copied him, waving their tiny arms.

"Now let's march in place like we're mixing colors!" Toby lifted his feet one at a time. The mice marched enthusiastically, their whiskers twitching with joy.

After their warm-up, they began to paint. Toby showed them how to mix colors. "Red and yellow make orange," he explained as they watched the colors swirl together. "Blue and yellow make green!"

The mice chattered excitedly as they painted their blocks. Pip painted hers bright orange like a sunset. Squeaky chose purple stripes. The third mouse, Whiskers, made a rainbow pattern.

"This is the most fun ever!" squeaked Pip. "Thank you for sharing with us, Mr. Toby!"

As they painted, they talked and laughed together. Toby told them stories about the garden, and the mice shared tales of their adventures in the walls.

When their masterpieces were dry, the mice hugged Toby's shell gently.

"You're the kindest tortoise in the whole world," said Whiskers.

Toby smiled warmly. "When we share what we have, we all become richer. And when we exercise our bodies and talk with friends, we make our hearts happy too."

From that day on, the mice visited Toby often. They painted together, exercised together, and shared many wonderful conversations in the cozy garden shed.

The End.
