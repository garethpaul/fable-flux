# Daisy's Fair Forest

Daisy was a gentle deer fawn with soft brown spots and big curious eyes. She lived near the forest but loved spending time in Grandma Owl's cozy study corner. The study had tall bookshelves, a warm reading lamp, and cushions that smelled like lavender.

Every day, forest animals came to the study corner when they had problems. Grandma Owl was very wise and helped everyone find fair solutions.

One sunny morning, Daisy heard loud arguing outside. "That's MY acorn!" squeaked Chippy the squirrel.

"No, I found it first!" chittered Nutkin, another squirrel.

Both squirrels tugged on the same big acorn. They pulled and pulled until they both fell backward with a thump.

Daisy's heart felt sad watching her friends fight. She walked over on her wobbly fawn legs. "What's happening?" she asked in her soft, gentle voice.

"Chippy took my acorn!" said Nutkin, his fluffy tail twitching angrily.

"I found it under the oak tree first!" said Chippy, holding the acorn tight.

Daisy thought carefully. She remembered what Grandma Owl always said: "When friends disagree, we must listen to everyone and find what's fair."

"Let's go to the study corner," Daisy suggested. "We can figure this out together."

In the cozy study corner, Daisy sat between her squirrel friends on the soft cushions. The warm lamp made everything feel peaceful.

"Tell me exactly what happened," Daisy said, just like Grandma Owl would.

Chippy spoke first. "I was looking for food for winter. I found this acorn under the big oak tree."

Nutkin nodded. "But I was already digging there! I saw the acorn first, but a leaf covered it."

Daisy listened carefully to both friends. She noticed they both looked tired and worried. "Are you both hungry?" she asked gently.

"Yes," they both said. "Winter is coming, and we need food."

Daisy's kind heart had an idea. "What if we help each other instead of fighting? There are many acorns in the forest."

She led her friends outside to the oak tree. Together, they looked around carefully. Daisy used her sharp eyes to spot acorns hidden under colorful fall leaves.

"Look!" Daisy pointed with her nose. "There's one by that rock!"

"And there's another by the stream!" Chippy called out.

Soon, they found six more acorns. Chippy and Nutkin each had plenty for winter.

"Thank you, Daisy," said Nutkin, his anger gone. "Sharing and working together was much better than fighting."

"You helped us find a fair solution," added Chippy. "And we found more food than we would have alone!"

Daisy felt warm and happy inside. She had helped her friends solve their problem fairly. No one was hurt, and everyone got what they needed.

Back in the study corner, Grandma Owl smiled proudly. "You did wonderfully, Daisy. You listened to both sides, stayed calm, and found a solution that helped everyone."

"It felt good to help," Daisy said, curling up on her favorite lavender cushion. "When we're fair and kind, everyone wins."

That evening, all three friends shared acorn soup together. They laughed and planned more adventures. Daisy learned that being fair and helping others solve problems peacefully made the whole forest a happier place.

From that day on, whenever forest animals had disagreements, they came to Daisy's study corner. She always listened carefully, stayed calm, and helped everyone find fair solutions together.

The End.
