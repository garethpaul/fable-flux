# Dilly Dolphin’s Study Corner Splash

Dilly was a playful dolphin who loved to learn. She didn’t live in the big blue sea today. She was visiting a special study corner at the Ocean Learning Room. The study corner smelled like fresh paper and salty air. Sunlight sparkled on the table, making tiny waves of light. Soft pillows shaped like shells sat in a cozy pile. Crayons, books, and shiny stickers were ready to use.

“Wow,” Dilly whispered, flipping her fin. “So many things to try!”

Her friends swam in too—Tiko the tiny turtle, Bella the bright fish, and Sandy the seahorse. Ms. Coral, their gentle teacher, smiled. “Today, we will make a learning poster. We’ll share our tools, take turns, and prepare our ideas,” she said.

Dilly’s eyes landed on the biggest, bluest crayon. It looked like the color of a happy wave. She scooped it up and hugged it close. “Mine first!” she giggled.

Tiko blinked. “Can I use it after you?”

Dilly held the crayon tighter. “I want to draw all the waves.”

Bella opened a book with shiny pages. Swish. “We can all draw,” she said kindly. “Let’s share.”

Ms. Coral nodded. “Sharing helps everyone learn. Respect means we care for our friends’ needs. We also prepare by planning who uses what and when.”

Dilly’s fin wiggled. She liked to go fast. Planning sounded slow. She pressed the blue crayon on the paper—scritch, scritch—making big loop-de-loops. The paper smelled like clean cotton. The blue swirls looked like giggles.

Sandy brought a cup of sea-glitter glue. “I’ll add sparkles after you draw,” Sandy said softly.

Dilly didn’t answer. She was busy. Her loops got bigger and bigger. The paper started to wrinkle. Tiko waited. Bella waited. Sandy waited. Their smiles got smaller.

Tiko tapped the table with a tiny flipper. Tap, tap. “My turn soon?” he asked.

Dilly frowned. “I’m not done.”

Ms. Coral swam closer. “Dilly, pause and look,” she said gently.

Dilly looked. She saw Tiko’s slow blink, Bella’s quiet tail, and Sandy’s soft sigh. Dilly’s tummy felt tight, like a knot in a net. She didn’t want her friends to feel sad.

“I… I really like the blue,” Dilly said. “I forgot to share.”

“That can happen,” Ms. Coral said. “We can grow by trying again. A growth mindset means we keep learning. Let’s prepare a sharing plan.”

Dilly took a deep breath. Swish-in, swish-out. The air smelled a little like lemon from the clean table. “Okay,” she said. “We can make turns.”

She set the blue crayon in the center. “I’ll count to ten when I draw,” she said. “Then I’ll pass it.”

Tiko smiled. “I’ll count to ten too!”

Bella clapped her fins. “I’ll get the green crayon ready.”

Sandy jingled the glue bottle. “I’ll wait with the sparkles.”

Dilly drew for ten slow counts. “One… two… three…” The blue crayon went shhh across the paper. At ten, she put it down. “Your turn, Tiko!”

Tiko drew little bubble circles. “Pop, pop,” he whispered happily. At ten, he passed the crayon to Bella.

Bella drew seaweed lines. “Swoosh,” she sang, then gave it to Sandy.

Sandy made tiny stars with the blue tip. “Twinkle,” she said. Then she set the crayon in the center again.

Dilly felt light and floaty inside. Sharing felt like swimming in warm water. “Thank you,” she told her friends. “Your ideas make the poster so pretty!”

They took turns with the stickers too. Stick. Stick. Stick. They respected each other’s choices. “Do you want the shell sticker here?” Bella asked.

Sandy nodded. “Yes, please.”

“Let’s prepare the letters,” Tiko said. He lined up stencils. “We can write: Our Ocean Learning Poster.”

Ms. Coral handed out small cloths. “Wipe glue from fins before passing tools,” she reminded. “Being prepared keeps our study corner neat.”

Dilly wiped her fin. The cloth felt soft and damp. “Clean fin, coming through!” she giggled, passing the scissors handle-first to Bella. “I respect your turn.”

They worked in a calm, happy rhythm—draw, pass, smile, thank you. The room sounded like gentle waves and little clicks of crayons. It smelled like sea-salt and lemon clean. The poster grew bright and big.

At last, they were done. Ms. Coral held up the poster. It showed blue waves, bubble circles, tall seaweed, and twinkling stars. In shiny letters it read: “We Learn Together.”

Dilly’s heart bubbled with pride. “We did it because we shared,” she said.

Tiko nodded. “And we made a plan.”

Bella swished her tail. “And we were respectful.”

Sandy hugged the glitter glue bottle. “And we kept trying.”

Ms. Coral beamed. “What did we learn today?”

Dilly raised her fin. “Sharing makes more smiles. When I share, we all grow. I can plan my turn, respect my friends, and try again if I forget.”

Ms. Coral tapped the poster. “That is a wonderful lesson.”

Dilly looked at the blue crayon in the center of the table. She pushed it gently toward her friends. “Who needs it next?” she asked.

“Me, please,” Tiko said.

“Here you go,” Dilly said, smiling.

The study corner felt warm and kind. The poster dried in the sun, sparkling like tiny waves. Dilly’s knot was gone. Her heart felt like a happy splash.

Sharing made the day shine.

The End.
