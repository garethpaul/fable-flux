# Fern's Tower Tumble

Little Fern the deer fawn loved playing in Daddy's home office. The big wooden desk smelled like pine trees. Sunlight streamed through the tall windows, making everything warm and bright.

Today, Fern had a wonderful idea. She gathered colorful blocks from her toy box. "I'll build the tallest tower ever!" she said, her brown eyes sparkling with excitement.

Click, clack, click! The blocks made happy sounds as Fern stacked them high. Red block, blue block, yellow block - up, up, up they went!

"Look, Daddy!" Fern called out. "My tower is taller than me!"

Daddy looked up from his computer and smiled. "Wow, Fern! That's amazing. You're such a good builder."

Fern felt proud and bouncy. She wanted to make her tower even bigger. She reached for more blocks on the shelf.

Just then, her little hoof bumped into Daddy's special soccer ball. The black and white ball rolled across the smooth floor.

CRASH! The ball knocked right into Fern's beautiful tower.

Blocks scattered everywhere! They tumbled and rolled under the desk, behind the chair, and into every corner. The room filled with the sound of clattering blocks.

"Oh no!" Fern gasped. Her ears drooped down sadly.

But that wasn't the worst part. The rolling ball had also knocked over Daddy's coffee mug. Brown coffee spilled all over his important papers, making them soggy and stained.

Daddy's eyes grew wide. "Oh my!" he said, quickly moving his laptop away from the spill.

Fern's bottom lip began to wobble. She felt scared and sad. Her tummy felt all twisty inside.

"I... I didn't mean to," she whispered, her voice very small.

Daddy looked at the mess. Coffee dripped from the desk. Papers were ruined. Blocks were everywhere.

Fern took a deep breath. She remembered what Mama always said about doing the right thing.

"Daddy," Fern said softly, walking closer. "I'm really, really sorry. I knocked your ball by accident, and it made a big mess. I feel bad that your papers got hurt."

She looked up at Daddy with her big, honest eyes. "Can you forgive me? I want to help clean up."

Daddy's face softened into a gentle smile. He knelt down and gave Fern a warm hug that smelled like his morning coffee.

"Thank you for saying sorry, little one," he said, stroking her soft fur. "Accidents happen. What matters is that you told the truth and want to make it better."

Together, they cleaned up the spill with paper towels. The towels felt rough and scratchy in Fern's small hooves, but she worked hard.

"Let's pick up these blocks together," Daddy suggested.

They crawled around the office, gathering blocks. Under the chair, behind the bookshelf, near the window - it was like a treasure hunt!

"This is actually fun!" Fern giggled as she found a green block hiding behind the wastebasket.

When they finished, Fern had another idea. "Daddy, want to build a new tower together? We could make it even more special!"

"I'd love that," Daddy said. "And maybe we can use my soccer ball as the base - safely this time!"

They built the most wonderful tower ever. It had a soccer ball bottom, colorful block walls, and even a little flag on top made from paper.

"You know what, Fern?" Daddy said, admiring their creation. "Saying sorry and helping fix problems shows how much you've grown. I'm proud of you."

Fern's heart felt warm and happy. She learned that saying sorry wasn't scary - it was brave and kind.

From that day on, whenever Fern made mistakes, she remembered to say sorry right away. And somehow, everything always turned out even better than before.

The End.
