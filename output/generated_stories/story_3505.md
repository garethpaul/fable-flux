# Benny Bear’s Bedtime List

Benny was a friendly bear cub with soft, fuzzy fur and bright, curious eyes. He was in his cozy bedroom, where the nightlight made tiny stars glow on the walls. His blanket smelled like warm pine, and his pillow was squishy like a marshmallow. Outside, the moon shone through the window, silver and calm.

“Benny,” called Mama Bear from the doorway, her voice gentle like a lullaby. “It’s almost bedtime. Let’s finish your bedtime tasks.”

Benny bounced on his toes and nodded. “Okay, Mama! But sometimes I forget what to do.”

Mama Bear smiled and handed him a small, colorful card. “I made you a bedtime list. It will help you remember and finish your tasks, one by one.”

Benny held the card carefully. There were four little pictures on it:
1) A toy box.
2) A toothbrush and bubbles.
3) Pajamas.
4) A cozy bed.

Benny felt proud. “I can do it! First toys, then brush, then pajamas, then bed.”

He took a deep breath. The room smelled like clean sheets and a little bit of honey. He looked around. Toys were everywhere—blocks near the rug, a red car by his slippers, and his favorite stuffed owl under the chair.

Benny tapped the first picture. “Step one: toys in the box!”

He made a game. “Zoom, zoom, little car, drive to your garage,” he said, rolling the red car into the toy box. Plunk! It made a happy sound. He stacked the blocks two by two. Click, clack. He found the stuffed owl and gave it a hug. “Good night, Owl,” he whispered, tucking it on the shelf.

Mama Bear peeked in. “Nice sorting, Benny. You are completing your first task.”

Benny’s chest felt warm and big. “Step one, done!” he cheered. He drew a tiny check mark in the air with his finger.

He looked at the next picture. “Step two: brush my teeth.” Benny padded to the bathroom. The floor felt cool under his paws. He squeezed a small sparkle of minty paste. “Just a pea,” he said, remembering. He hummed a little tune while brushing—scrub, scrub, scrub—front teeth and back teeth. The bubbles tasted minty-sweet. He watched the sand timer—swish, swish—until the last grain fell.

“All done!” Benny rinsed and smiled at his shiny teeth. “Step two, done!” He felt proud again and gave himself a soft paw-high-five.

Back in his room, Benny looked at picture three. “Pajamas!” His pajama drawer made a soft whoosh. He picked the blue pajamas with tiny yellow stars. They felt smooth and warm. He wiggled one paw, then the other. “Left paw, right paw,” he said, giggling. “I am doing this in order.” He zipped up—zzzip!—and spun in a tiny circle. The stars on his pajamas twinkled under the nightlight.

Mama Bear clapped quietly. “You are using time wisely, Benny. You are thinking and doing. That’s good time management.”

Benny nodded. “Step three, done! One more to go.”

He looked at the last picture: a cozy bed. He climbed in and pulled up his blanket. The blanket felt like a warm hug. He listened. The room was quiet, with only the soft hum of the nightlight and the whisper of the wind. But then Benny blinked. “Oh! Story time,” he said. “Is story time a task?”

Mama Bear smiled and sat beside him. “It can be part of getting ready to sleep. But you finished your list. Would you like a short story to celebrate?”

“Yes, please!” Benny said, snuggling close.

Mama Bear opened a picture book. Her voice was low and calm. Benny smelled her sweet honey tea as she read. The pages made a soft flip, flip. Benny’s eyes felt a little heavy, like pebbles sinking in a pond.

After the story, Benny whispered, “Mama, the list helped me. I knew what to do, and I finished every step.”

Mama Bear stroked his ear. “You planned, you followed the order, and you finished your tasks. That’s logical thinking. And you did it before bedtime. That’s good time management.”

Benny smiled a sleepy smile. “I like finishing. It feels good in my tummy.”

Mama Bear kissed his forehead. “When we do our tasks one by one, we have time to rest and play. Tomorrow, you can make a morning list.”

Benny’s eyes closed. He pictured the four little pictures on the card. He whispered, “Toys, brush, pajamas, bed.” Then he added, “I can do it. I can finish my tasks.” His breath grew slow and soft.

Mama Bear turned off the lamp. The starry nightlight glowed. The cozy bedroom felt safe and snug.

Before sleep took him, Benny said, “Mama?”

“Yes, sweet cub?”

“Lists make big jobs small.”

Mama Bear smiled in the dark. “Yes, Benny. One step at a time.”

Benny drifted to sleep, proud and peaceful.

The clear lesson glowed like the nightlight: When we make a plan, do steps in order, and finish our tasks, we feel calm, we have time for stories, and we rest well.

The End.
