# Milo Mouse and the Busy Garage

Milo was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright whiskers. He lived in a cozy corner of a garage workshop. The garage smelled like clean wood and warm oil. Sunlight peeked in through the window and made dusty sparkles in the air. Tools hung on the wall—hammer, wrench, and saw—each in its place. On the floor sat boxes of nuts and bolts that made a tiny clink when they touched.

Every morning, Milo stretched his small paws. “Good morning, garage,” he squeaked. “I can do it today.” His tail made a happy curl.

Milo loved to help. He loved to move things, tidy things, and make little fixes. He was still small, and some tasks felt big. But he wanted to try by himself before asking for help. He called it “Milo’s practice.”

One sunny day, Milo found a little red wagon with squeaky wheels. “Squeeeak!” went the wheel. “I can fix that,” Milo said. He pushed the wagon with his shoulder. It felt heavy. His paws slid a little on the smooth floor. “One push at a time,” he whispered. “Slow and steady.”

He took a deep breath. He found a bottle of oil. It felt cool and slippery when a tiny drop fell on his paw. “Oops!” he giggled. He wiped it on a rag. Then he held the oil up and said, “I’ll do it myself. I will be careful.”

“Milo, what are you doing?” asked Bella, a blue butterfly who lived near the window.

“I’m fixing the squeak,” Milo said. “I want to try on my own.”

Bella fluttered closer. “I can watch,” she said. “I’ll cheer.”

Milo smiled. “Thank you, Bella.”

He rolled the wheel with one paw. “Squeeeak!” He found a little spot to drip one tiny drop of oil. “Plip.” He rolled the wheel again. “Squeak?” It was softer now. He tried again. “Plip.” Roll. “Ssssh.” The squeak was gone!

“I did it!” Milo squeaked. His heart felt warm and proud. He had been patient. He had taken his time.

“Great job!” Bella cheered. Her wings made a soft ffffft sound.

Milo pulled the wagon in a circle. The wheels whispered on the floor. “Ssssh, ssssh.” He laughed. “Let’s make a path,” he said.

He set up a tiny course with small blocks and a line of chalk. He made a tunnel from a cardboard tube. “I will practice pulling,” he told Bella. “Practice makes me strong.”

He pulled the wagon through the tunnel. He bent his knees. He kept his tail low. “Pull, pull, pull,” he sang. It felt like exercise. His legs felt warm and a little wiggly. “I can do this,” he told himself. He rested when he needed to and took sips of water from a bottle cap.

Then Milo heard a soft clatter. “Tink-tink-tink.” Nuts and bolts spilled from a small box. Pip and Dot, two tiny field mice, stood nearby with wide eyes.

“We tried to pick up the box,” Pip said.

“It fell,” Dot whispered. Her whiskers drooped.

Milo took a breath. He remembered how it felt to try something big. He also remembered his promise to try by himself first. “Let’s see what we can do,” he said kindly. “We can do it together, but let’s each try our own part.”

He showed them how to pick up one bolt at a time. “Pinch, lift, drop,” he said. The bolts made a happy “plink!” in the box. Pip copied. “Pinch, lift, drop.” Dot followed. “Pinch, lift, drop.”

“This is slow,” Dot sighed.

“Slow is okay,” Milo said. “That is patience. We can take our time.”

They worked. Their paws made soft pat-pat sounds. The garage smelled like warm wood and clean air. The sun moved across the floor. The three friends hummed a little song. “Pinch, lift, drop. We can do it, plop!”

When they were done, Dot smiled. “We did it!”

Pip smiled, too. “We did it ourselves!”

Milo nodded. “You did your parts. That is self-reliance. You tried first. You asked for help when you needed it, and you kept trying with practice.”

Bella clapped her wings. “Hooray!”

Milo’s tummy rumbled. “Snack time.” He opened a tiny tin with sunflower seeds. He had packed it himself that morning. “I brought enough to share.” He set the tin in the middle. “One seed at a time,” he said.

They shared the snack. Crunch, crunch. It tasted nutty and fresh. “Thank you,” Pip said.

“Sharing makes my heart feel big,” Milo said.

After snacks, Milo looked at a loose handle on a little wooden box. He touched it gently. “Wiggly,” he said. He found a small screwdriver. He tried turning it. It was hard. He paused. He took a deep breath. “I can try again.” He used both paws. Turn, turn. The screw tightened. The handle felt snug. He grinned. “I did it!”

Bella smiled. “Milo, you are very helpful.”

Milo wiggled his whiskers. “I like to help. I like to practice. I like to try by myself. When I need help, I ask kind friends.”

The sun became soft and golden. The garage felt cozy and calm. Milo put the tools back in their places. He pushed the red wagon to the wall. The wheels went “sssht” on the floor.

Milo curled up in his cozy corner. His paws felt tired in a good way. His heart felt strong. “Today I learned,” he whispered, “that I can do many things by myself. When I practice and am patient, I get better. When I share, everyone smiles.”

Bella, Pip, and Dot whispered, “Good night, Milo.”

“Good night,” Milo said. “Tomorrow, I will try again.”

The End.
