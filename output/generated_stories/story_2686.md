# Busy Beaver’s Wiggle-Break Dinner

In a sunny house by a river, a busy beaver named Benny padded into the dining room. The room smelled like warm corn bread and sweet apples. The wooden table felt smooth under Benny’s paws. The clock on the wall went tick-tock, tick-tock, like a gentle drum.

Benny liked to help. He stacked napkins into neat squares. He lined up spoons that shined like little moons. “I am a busy beaver!” Benny said with a smile. “I can do hard things, one small step at a time.”

Mama Beaver peeked in and sniffed the air. “Dinner soon,” she said. “Corn bread, veggie stew, and apple slices.”

“Yum!” Benny’s tummy said rumble-rumble.

Mama nodded. “Before dinner, let’s do a wiggle-break. Exercise helps our bodies grow strong and our minds feel bright.”

Benny blinked. “In the dining room?”

Mama laughed softly. “Yes, right here. We move with care and keep the room safe.”

The chairs stood like patient friends. The table stood tall and proud. The window let in soft gold light. Benny felt excited. He also felt a little wiggly-worried. “What if I knock something over?” he asked.

“That’s honest and brave to say,” Mama said. “We will make a safe space. We will move slowly at first. We tell the truth, we try, and we grow.”

Benny took a deep breath. He smelled the stew—carrots, peas, and cozy herbs. He puffed out his breath slow, like a gentle breeze.

“Ready?” Mama asked.

“Ready!” Benny said.

“First, toe-taps,” Mama said. “Tap-tap the floor.”

Tap, tap, tap went Benny’s feet, soft as raindrops. He felt the floor cool under his toes. He smiled. “This is fun!”

“Next, arm circles,” Mama said. “Small circles like tiny pancakes.”

Benny moved his arms in small round circles. The air whooshed like a whisper. He kept his body tall and careful. He checked the room with his eyes. “I am moving safe,” he said. His chest felt proud.

“Now, beaver tail swish!” Mama giggled.

Benny swished his flat tail side to side, slow and smooth. Swish-swish. He looked at the napkins. He looked at the spoons. He kept a little space from the table. “I can do it,” he whispered. “I can learn.”

The clock went tick-tock, tick-tock.

“Last, stretch tall,” Mama said, reaching up. “Reach for the sun.”

Benny reached up high. He felt the stretch in his sides like a big yawn. He wiggled his fingers. He felt strong and calm. His tummy went rumble-rumble again, but softer now.

Just then, Papa Beaver came in with a tray. “Oops,” Papa said as a napkin slid. It fluttered down like a snowflake.

Benny dashed to help, but he stopped. “I will move with care,” he told himself. He bent his knees, picked up the napkin, and set it back on the pile.

“Thank you,” Papa said. “You helped and stayed safe.”

Benny’s cheeks felt warm and happy. “Exercise helps me focus,” he said.

From the doorway, little neighbor Otter, Olive, peeked in. “Hi,” Olive said. “I heard giggles. Can I learn the wiggle-break too?”

Benny looked at Mama. Mama nodded. “Yes, but we must be honest and careful.”

Benny turned to Olive. “We will make space,” he said. “We push chairs in. We keep our tails and paws away from the table.”

They pushed chairs in with tiny squeaks. The wood smelled sweet and clean. The room felt ready.

“Toe-taps!” Benny called. Tap, tap, tap. Olive smiled. “I can do it!”

“Arm circles, tiny pancake size,” Benny said. Olive’s arms went round and round. Whoosh-whoosh.

“Beaver tail swish,” Benny said. Olive swished her little otter tail, slow like a river.

“Reach for the sun,” Benny said. They reached up high. The light from the window made their paws look golden.

Olive giggled. “This makes my body feel awake!”

Benny nodded. “Exercise gives energy. It helps us do hard things, step by step.”

The clock sang a soft ding. Mama said, “Time to eat.” She looked at Benny. “You led kindly and safely. You told the truth when you felt worried. You helped Olive. You used your time well.”

Benny felt big on the inside. He looked at the table. “Wait,” he said. “I moved a napkin by accident.” He picked it up and put it back straight. “I want it tidy,” he said. “That is the honest thing to do.”

Papa smiled. “That’s integrity.”

They sat down. The chairs made friendly creaks. The stew was warm. The corn bread was crumbly and soft. The apples were cool and sweet.

Olive took a bite and sighed. “Mmm. My legs feel strong. My heart feels happy.”

Benny nodded. “Me too. I can grow. I can try. If I make a mistake, I can fix it. I can help others. And I can take little exercise breaks to feel ready.”

Mama raised her spoon. “To wiggle-breaks!”

“To wiggle-breaks!” they said together, clink.

Benny took a big spoonful of stew. He felt calm, proud, and cozy. He listened to the tick-tock and the soft munch-munch at the table. He thought, I am a busy beaver, and I can do hard things, one small step at a time.

The End.
