# Milo's Big Feelings in the Workshop

Milo the monkey loved spending time in Grandpa Joe's garage workshop. The smell of wood shavings tickled his nose, and the sound of tools made happy clicking noises. Today, Milo was building a special birdhouse for his friend Robin.

"Tap, tap, tap!" went Milo's little hammer. He was trying to nail two pieces of wood together, but the nail kept bending. "This is easy!" Milo said confidently.

But then... BONK! The hammer hit his thumb instead of the nail.

"OUCH!" Milo shouted. His face turned red, and his hands started to shake. "This stupid hammer! This dumb nail!" He threw the hammer down with a loud CLANG!

Grandpa Joe looked up from his workbench. He had kind, wrinkly eyes and a gentle smile. "Oh my, Milo. I can see you're feeling very angry right now."

"YES!" Milo jumped up and down. "Everything is broken! Nothing works! I hate this birdhouse!" His tail swished back and forth like a angry windshield wiper.

Grandpa Joe walked over slowly. "When I feel angry like that, my whole body feels hot and tight. Do you feel that way too?"

Milo stopped jumping. He touched his chest. "Yes... it feels all squeezy in here."

"That's your anger," Grandpa Joe said softly. "Anger is a normal feeling, but we need to help it calm down. Would you like to learn my special trick?"

Milo nodded, his breathing still fast and bumpy.

"First, let's take three deep breaths together," Grandpa Joe said. "Breathe in through your nose like you're smelling fresh cookies... and out through your mouth like you're blowing out birthday candles."

"In... and out... in... and out... in... and out," they breathed together. Milo felt the tight feeling in his chest start to loosen.

"Good job!" Grandpa Joe smiled. "Now, let's count to ten while we look around the workshop. I spy one red toolbox..."

"Two blue screwdrivers!" Milo added, starting to feel calmer.

"Three yellow measuring tapes..."

By the time they reached ten, Milo's face wasn't red anymore. His hands had stopped shaking.

"I'm sorry I threw your hammer, Grandpa Joe," Milo said quietly. "And I'm sorry I called it stupid. That wasn't nice."

"Thank you for apologizing, Milo. That shows great respect," Grandpa Joe said, giving him a warm hug. "The hammer isn't hurt, and neither are you. Sometimes when we're learning new things, we get frustrated. That's okay!"

"Can you help me with the nail?" Milo asked. "I want to make Robin a beautiful house, but I think I need to learn more."

"Of course!" Grandpa Joe picked up the hammer. "Let's work together. You hold the nail steady, and I'll show you how to tap gently."

Working as a team, they finished the birdhouse. It had a round door and a tiny perch. Milo painted it bright yellow with purple polka dots.

"Look how wonderful it turned out when we stayed calm and worked together!" Grandpa Joe said.

Milo hugged the birdhouse. "Next time I feel angry, I'll remember to breathe deep and count to ten. And I'll ask for help instead of throwing things."

"That's my brave, smart monkey," Grandpa Joe said proudly.

When Robin saw her new house, she chirped with joy. Milo felt proud and happy, knowing he had learned something very important about managing his big feelings.

The End.
