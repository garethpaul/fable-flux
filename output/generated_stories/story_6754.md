# Leo Waits for the Bubbles

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and a tiny tuft at the end of his tail. One sunny morning, Leo padded into the bathroom. His paws made soft tap-tap sounds on the tile. The room smelled like fresh soap and warm towels, and a little window let in a stripe of bright light.

“Today I will have a big bubble bath,” Leo said. “Bubbles, bubbles, bubbles!”

He turned the silver knobs. Water whispered and splashed into the tub, shhhhh and plip-plop. Leo reached for the bottle with the smiling whale on it. “Bubble bath time!” he cheered.

But Mama Lion’s voice came from the doorway. “Careful, Leo. We need to wait for the right water and the right bubbles. We use patience.”

“Patience?” Leo wrinkled his nose. “I want bubbles now!”

Mama smiled. “Patience means we wait and watch. We take our time, and good things happen. Let’s discover together.”

Leo took a deep breath. He listened to the water. Shhhhh. He watched the tub slowly fill. Little swirls danced on top.

“Can I pour the bubble soap?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Mama, “but first we check the water. Use your paw to test. Is it too hot or too cold?”

Leo dipped one paw in. “It is just right. Warm like sunshine.”

“Good discovery,” Mama said. “Now pour a little. Not too much. We will see what happens.”

Leo carefully tipped the bottle. Glug… glug. A small ribbon of soap slid into the water. He wanted to pour more. His paws wiggled.

“I want lots of bubbles,” he said.

“Let’s be patient,” Mama reminded. “Pour a little, then wait. Watch.”

Leo sat on the bath mat. It felt fluffy under his paws. He watched the water. Tiny bubbles popped up. Pop! Pop! More bubbles grew and floated. The bubbles smelled like sweet oranges.

“This is fun,” Leo said, eyes wide. “The bubbles are coming!”

He waited and counted softly. “One… two… three…” The bubbles grew taller. They hugged together like a little mountain of foam.

“Now you discovered the trick,” Mama said. “When we wait, the bubbles grow.”

Leo smiled. “I can be patient!”

Before he climbed in, Leo looked around. His bath toys were in a jumble. A red boat was upside down, a yellow duck was hiding under a washcloth, and his little brush was missing.

“Oh no,” Leo said. “Where is my brush?”

“Let’s organize,” Mama suggested. “When we keep things in their places, we find them faster.”

Leo nodded. He made a plan. “Boats go in this basket. Ducks go on the ledge. Washcloths go on the towel bar.” He used his paws to place each toy. Clink. Squeak. Rustle.

He peeked behind the shampoo. “Brush? Are you here?” He listened. He sniffed. The brush smelled like clean fur. He checked the basket. “Found you!” he shouted, lifting the little brush with pride.

“You used problem-solving,” Mama said. “You looked, smelled, and listened. You stayed calm and kept trying.”

Leo puffed his chest. “I am a brave, patient cub.”

Now it was time to get in. Leo stepped into the warm water. “Ahh,” he sighed. The bubbles hugged his legs and tickled his belly. He splashed a little. Swish! The duck bobbed and said, “Squeak!” The boat floated by with a gentle glug.

Leo made a bubble hat and a bubble beard. “Look at me!” he giggled. Mama laughed with him.

Then Leo saw the drain slow the water. “Why is it going down?” he asked.

“The water will go when bath time is done,” Mama said. “We will let it go slowly. Patience, Leo.”

Leo felt a tiny worry. “But I want to play more.”

“You can, for a little while,” Mama said. “Let’s sing a waiting song.”

They sang, “We wait and we watch, we sing and we smile. Patience helps us every while.” Leo swayed in the water, and the song made his heart feel calm.

When bath time was over, Leo stood up. Mama wrapped him in a warm towel. It felt like a soft hug. Leo helped put his toys away in their places. Boat in the basket. Duck on the ledge. Brush on the cup. Everything neat and easy to find.

He looked at the clean tub and the tidy shelf. “I feel proud,” he said. “I waited for the bubbles. I kept my toys in order. I found my brush by thinking.”

Mama kissed his forehead. “Today you learned the power of patience, discovery, organization, and problem-solving.”

Leo grinned. “When I wait, good things happen. When I tidy, I can find things. When I think, I can solve problems.”

He yawned a sleepy, happy yawn. “Tomorrow, I will be patient again.”

“Me too,” Mama said.

Leo’s tail flicked with joy as he padded out of the bathroom, warm, clean, and brave.

The End.
