# Pippa Parrot Builds a Bubble Bridge

Pippa the creative parrot hopped into the bright, sunny bathroom. The tiles were cool on her feet. Drip, drip, went the faucet. The air smelled like soap and mint. Pippa loved the bathroom. It was full of shiny things and soft towels and bubbly fun.

“Today, I will build!” Pippa chirped. “I will build a Bubble Bridge!”

She looked around. There were cups, a little step stool, a rubber duck, and colorful washcloths. Pippa’s feathers rustled with excitement. Her heart felt fizzy, like soda. She flapped her wings and said, “I feel excited!”

Pippa turned the water on a little bit. “Just a tiny stream,” she said. Swish, swish. She filled a blue cup and poured it into the sink. Plop! The rubber duck bobbed in the water and squeaked, “Squeak!”

“Hello, Ducky,” Pippa said. “We are building a Bubble Bridge. Would you like to help?”

“Squeak!” said the duck.

Pippa puffed up with pride. “I can do this,” she whispered. She stacked a small cup on a big cup. She folded a washcloth into a flat square. She made little towers along the edge of the sink. Her bridge began to grow. She hummed a tune. “Build, build, build. Slow and steady.”

Then—oops! One cup slipped. Clatter! Water splashed. A drip landed on Pippa’s beak. Another drip slid down her feather. Her bridge wobbled and fell. Plop-plop-plop.

Pippa’s tummy felt twisty. Her wings drooped. “Oh,” she said softly. “I feel sad. I feel frustrated.” She put a wing over her heart. “Hello, feelings,” she whispered. “I see you.”

The rubber duck bumped her gently. “Squeak?”

Pippa took a deep breath. In... and out. She looked at the mirror. Her eyes looked watery. “It’s okay to feel big feelings,” she told herself. “I can name them. I can tame them.”

She tried again, but the cups slid and tipped. The sink was messy now. Towels were everywhere. Water splashed onto the floor. Pippa’s chest felt hot. “I feel overwhelmed,” she said. “I need a plan.”

She turned off the water. Click. The drip stopped. Pippa looked around. “First, I will organize,” she decided. “Organize means to sort and put things in their spots.”

She lined up the cups by size: big, medium, small. She folded the washcloths into neat stacks: red, blue, yellow. She put the rubber duck in a safe corner of the sink. She wiped the counter with a towel. Swish, swish. The bathroom felt calm again.

Pippa’s body felt calm, too. Her shoulders relaxed. Her breath slowed. “Now I feel better,” she said. “I feel ready.”

She made a new plan. “Step one: dry the edge,” she said, patting the sink rim. “Step two: build a strong base with big cups. Step three: add medium cups. Step four: top with small cups and soft washcloth roads. Step five: bubbles!”

Pippa started to build. Tap, tap, tap went her beak as she set each cup just right. “This cup goes here,” she said. “This cloth goes there.” She checked her feelings as she worked. “I feel focused. I feel proud.”

A little splash startled her. The rubber duck wiggled, happy. Pippa giggled. “I hear your squeak, Ducky! We are almost done.”

She poured a tiny bit of soap into a bowl and added a little water. Swirl, swirl. The soap made creamy bubbles. They smelled like lemons. Pippa scooped the bubbles and laid them gently on top of the washcloth road. The bubbles looked like puffy clouds. The bridge sparkled.

“Ta-da!” Pippa cheered. “Bubble Bridge is open!”

She placed the rubber duck at one end. “Ducky, would you like to cross?” she asked.

“Squeak!” said the duck.

“Walk slowly,” Pippa said. “We are careful builders.” She moved the duck along the soft, bubbly road. The bubbles tickled her beak. The duck made it across. Pippa clapped her wings. “You did it!”

She listened to the quiet bathroom. Drip, drip. Squeak. Soft bubble pops. It sounded peaceful. Pippa felt warm and happy inside. “I feel calm. I feel proud,” she said. “I built my bridge. I organized my tools. I cared for my feelings.”

Then Pippa noticed a wet spot on the floor. “Oops,” she said kindly. “We made a splash.” She picked up a towel and patted the floor dry. Pat, pat, pat. She put the cups back in a stack. She folded the washcloths and set them in a tidy pile. She turned the soap bottle so the label faced front. Everything looked neat.

Pippa looked into the mirror. “Hello, Pippa,” she said to herself. “Today you learned to name your feelings. You learned to make a plan. You learned to organize your things. You built something new.”

She gave a happy chirp. “Next time I feel big feelings,” she said, “I will stop, breathe, and make a plan.”

The rubber duck bobbed and squeaked, as if to say, “Me too!”

Pippa fluffed her feathers and took one more deep breath. In... and out. She smiled. “I am a creative parrot,” she said. “I can build with care. I can keep things tidy. I can listen to my heart.”

The bathroom was shiny. The Bubble Bridge was bright. Pippa felt ready for a cozy rest and a cheerful day.

The End.
