# Kody Koala's Fair Share Day

Kody Koala sat quietly in Mama's home office, watching the morning sunlight dance through the big window. The room smelled like fresh coffee and paper. Kody loved spending time here while Mama worked at her computer.

"Good morning, Kody!" said Mama, giving him a gentle hug. "I have a special job for you today. Can you help me sort these colorful stickers for my work project?"

Kody's gray ears perked up with excitement. "Yes, Mama! I love helping!"

Mama placed three small bowls on the desk - one red, one blue, and one yellow. She poured a big pile of mixed stickers onto the soft desk mat. "We need to put the red stickers in the red bowl, blue stickers in the blue bowl, and yellow stickers in the yellow bowl. Can you do that?"

"That sounds easy!" Kody said, reaching for the pile with his fuzzy paws.

As Kody began sorting, he noticed something. There were many more red stickers than blue or yellow ones. The red bowl was getting very full, while the other bowls looked almost empty.

"Mama," Kody said thoughtfully, "there are so many red stickers and not very many blue or yellow ones. That doesn't seem fair."

Mama smiled proudly. "You're very observant, Kody. What do you think we should do to make it more fair?"

Kody tapped his little black nose with his paw, thinking hard. He could hear the gentle hum of Mama's computer and feel the warm sunshine on his soft fur. 

"Maybe we could count them first?" Kody suggested bravely. "Then we'll know exactly how many we have."

"That's a wonderful idea!" Mama said. "You're being a great leader by thinking of a fair solution."

Together, they counted carefully. "One, two, three..." Kody counted slowly, moving each sticker with his paw. There were twenty red stickers, but only five blue ones and seven yellow ones.

"Now I see the problem," said Kody. "We need more blue and yellow stickers to make it fair for everyone."

Mama opened her desk drawer. "You know what? I have more stickers right here! Should we add some to make the groups more equal?"

Kody clapped his paws together. "Yes! Then everyone gets a fair share!"

They added more blue and yellow stickers until each bowl had about the same amount. Kody felt proud and happy seeing the three bowls sitting evenly filled on the desk.

"Kody, you did something very important today," Mama said, stroking his soft gray fur. "You noticed when something wasn't fair, and you were brave enough to speak up about it. Then you helped find a solution."

"It feels good to make things fair," Kody said with a big smile. "Everyone should get the same chance."

"That's right, little one. Being fair means making sure everyone is treated equally and kindly. You set a goal to solve the problem, and you did it!"

Kody looked at the three balanced bowls of colorful stickers gleaming in the sunlight. The office felt cozy and warm, filled with the sweet smell of success.

"Can we do more fair projects tomorrow, Mama?" Kody asked hopefully.

"Of course we can," Mama laughed, giving him another gentle hug. "Fair helpers like you make the world a better place."

Kody curled up contentedly in his favorite corner of the office, feeling proud that he had learned something important about fairness, bravery, and helping others.

The End.
