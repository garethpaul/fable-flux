# Benny Beaver Builds a Game

Benny was a busy beaver with bright eyes and quick paws. He loved to help. He loved to build. He loved to try new things. Today, Benny padded into the creative art room. The room smelled like fresh paper and glue. Sunlight warmed the colorful tables. Paint pots glowed like rainbows. Scissors snipped. Crayons clicked. Brushes swished.

Benny’s friends were there too. Mia Mouse stacked cups. Ollie Owl sorted markers. Pip Puppy bounced a ball. Pip sighed, “I wish we could play a sports game inside.”

“Me too,” said Mia. “But the ball rolls under the shelves.”

Ollie blinked. “We need a plan,” he hooted softly.

Benny felt a fizz in his tummy. Initiative popped like a bubble. “I can start!” he said. “Let’s make a gentle game. We can build a beanbag toss with art room things.”

Pip tilted his head. “Can we really?”

Benny nodded. “Yes! We try. We plan. We build. Then we play!”

The friends gathered close. The table felt cool and smooth under their paws. “First,” Benny said, “we make a list.” He drew simple pictures: a big box, soft beanbags, colorful targets. “We will keep it safe. No running. No throwing hard things.”

Mia clapped. “I can find fabric scraps!”

“I will find a box,” said Pip, tail thumping softly on the floor.

“I can draw targets,” said Ollie, spreading his wings.

Benny smiled. “Great plan! Let’s begin.”

They took initiative. They did not wait. They got busy. Rustle, rustle went the paper. Tap, tap went the box. Snip, snip went the scissors. Benny guided gently. “Mia, soft fabric makes safe beanbags. Pip, check the box is not too big. Ollie, make targets with big, bright circles.”

Mia found blue, red, and yellow fabric. It felt soft like a cloud. “These will be cozy beanbags!” she squeaked.

Pip rolled a box. “This one fits under the table,” he barked happily.

Ollie drew three big circles. “One is near. One is middle. One is far,” he hooted. He colored them green, orange, and purple.

Benny filled small bags with rice from the craft bin. Swish, swish went the rice. He tied them with string. Knot, knot. “Let’s test,” he said.

Pip tossed a beanbag. Plop! It missed. “Oh no,” he sighed.

“That’s okay,” Benny said. “We try again. We use patience.” He took a calm breath. “In… and out.” The room smelled like oranges from the paint water. “We can move the near target closer.”

Ollie slid the green circle forward. “Good planning,” he said.

Mia smiled. “I will count. One… two… three!” Pip tossed again. Plop! It landed on green. “I did it!” Pip wagged and laughed.

Benny drew a simple chart. “We take turns,” he said. “We cheer for friends.” He pointed to their names. “Mia, Pip, Ollie, Benny.”

They lined up. Their feet made soft thumps on the floor. “Ready?” Benny asked.

“Ready!” said everyone.

Mia tossed. Plop on green. “Yay, Mia!” cheered Pip.

Ollie tossed. Plop on orange. “Hoo-hoo! Nice aim,” said Benny.

Pip tossed. Plop near purple. “So close!” said Mia. “Try again with a gentle swing.”

Benny tossed last. His beanbag flew too far. Thud against the box. He frowned. “Hmm. I can learn too.” He took a breath and tried again with a softer paw. Plop on orange. “Yes!”

Soon friends from the art room watched. “Can we play?” asked Tia Turtle.

“Of course!” said Benny. “We share. We take turns. We keep it safe.”

Benny added a rule card with pictures: a slow foot, a smile, a beanbag. “Walk. Cheer. Toss soft,” he read.

They kept score with stickers. A green star was one sticker. Orange was two. Purple was three. The chart shone with sparkly dots. Laughter bubbled like little bells. The beanbags felt warm and squishy in their paws.

But then a problem popped up. The box slid and tipped. The targets wobbled. The beanbags fell behind the table.

“Oh no!” said Mia.

“We need a fix,” said Ollie.

Benny put on his thinking face. “We can do it,” he said calmly. “Let’s plan a brace.” He found tape and cardboard. “Pip, hold the box. Mia, cut two triangles. Ollie, mark where to tape.” They worked together, steady and slow. Rip, stick, press. The box stood firm.

“Try again,” said Benny.

Pip tossed. Plop on purple! Everyone cheered. “Great teamwork!” cried Tia.

Benny’s heart felt warm. “We did this because we started. We made a plan. We kept trying. That is initiative.” He smiled at his friends. “And we had patience.”

They finished with a friendly match. Each friend took three turns. They cheered. They shared. They helped. In the last round, Pip aimed at purple. He bent his knees. He breathed in. He smiled. Toss. Plop! Purple!

“We did it together!” said Benny.

Mia nodded. “We built a gentle sports game.”

Ollie added, “We planned and used patience.”

Pip wagged. “And you took the first step, Benny. You showed initiative!”

Benny’s eyes shone. “Anyone can show initiative. See a problem? Start with a kind plan. Ask friends. Try, try, try.”

They packed up the art room, humming and happy. The tape felt sticky. The table felt smooth. The room smelled like crayons and clean hands. The game box sat neatly on a shelf, ready for tomorrow.

The End.
