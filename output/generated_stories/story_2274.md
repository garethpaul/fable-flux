# Ellie’s Big Gentle Words

In a cozy, comfortable nursery, a gentle elephant calf named Ellie curled her soft trunk around a fuzzy blue blanket. The room smelled like warm milk and clean cotton. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. Stuffed animals sat in a friendly circle, and a tiny clock went tick-tock, tick-tock.

Ellie loved to play pretend. She imagined the rug was a green jungle. She imagined the blocks were bright, shiny trees. Today, Ellie wanted to build a tall, tall tower that reached the cloud-painted ceiling.

“Blocks, please,” Ellie whispered to herself. She waddled to the shelf, her big feet making soft thump-thump sounds. The red block felt smooth. The yellow block felt warm from the sun. The blue block felt cool in her trunk.

Across the room, her friend Pip, a small plush panda, leaned on a pillow. Pip had button eyes and a stitched smile. Ellie liked to pretend Pip talked, too.

“Let’s make a tower, Pip!” Ellie said. She set the blocks one by one: thump, tap, balance. Her ears flapped with joy.

But Ellie noticed her favorite sparkly block was missing. It was the shiny one that made rainbow dots on the wall. Ellie looked under the blanket. She looked in the toy bin. No sparkly block.

Her tummy felt twisty. Her cheeks felt hot. She wanted her block back right now. Ellie stomped one foot. The tower wobbled.

“Where is it?!” she huffed. Her voice was loud and grumpy.

From the corner, she saw Baby Ben, a tiny mouse puppet with soft gray fur, sitting by the crayons. The sparkly block was next to him.

Ellie felt a rumble in her chest. She wanted to grab it. Her trunk reached out, but then she stopped. She remembered what her teacher, Miss Star, always said: “Use your gentle words. Tell people how you feel. Ask for what you need.”

Ellie took a deep breath. In, out. In, out. She held Pip close like a hug. Her tummy slowly untwisted.

“I can use my words,” Ellie whispered. “I can say how I feel.”

Ellie walked to Baby Ben with slow, soft steps. She bent her knees so her eyes were kind and level.

“Hi, Baby Ben,” Ellie said in a calm, gentle voice. “I feel sad and a little mad. I was using the sparkly block to make a tall tower. May I please have it back when you’re done?”

Baby Ben’s tiny whiskers twitched. He squeaked, “Squeak?” and patted the block with his paw. His puppet head tilted, like he was thinking.

Ellie waited. She counted quietly. “One, two, three,” she whispered. Waiting felt hard, but she kept breathing.

Baby Ben pushed the sparkly block toward Ellie. “Squeak!” he said, like a happy yes.

Ellie’s heart felt warm. Her cheeks cooled. “Thank you, Baby Ben,” she said, smiling. “I’m grateful for your help.” She gave a small bow with her trunk. Baby Ben squeaked and hugged the crayon.

Ellie carried the sparkly block back to her tower. She turned to Pip. “We asked with gentle words,” she told her plush friend. “And we waited. That was kind.” Pip, in her pretend voice, said, “Kind words are strong words!”

Ellie put the sparkly block on top. Rainbow dots danced on the wall like tiny butterflies. She imagined the tower was a lighthouse on a candy sea. She imagined a boat with jelly sails and a peanut-butter captain. Her tower grew—red, yellow, blue, and sparkly—tap, tap, tap.

Just then, she saw Mia the doll reach for the red block at the bottom. Mia’s yarn hair brushed the tower. The blocks wobbled—wiggle, wiggle, CRASH! The tower tumbled down like quiet rain. Blocks rolled under the crib. One bumped Ellie’s toe.

Ellie’s eyes filled with tears. She felt the rumble again. She wanted to shout, “No!”

She pressed her ears to her head and remembered Miss Star’s words. “Use gentle words. Tell people how you feel. Ask for what you need.”

Ellie took another deep breath. In, out. In, out. She wiped her eyes with her soft trunk.

“Mia,” Ellie said softly, “I feel upset because my tower fell. I worked hard, and I wanted to see it stand tall. May we please build together? Can you hand me the red block?”

Mia’s stitched smile seemed to brighten. Ellie pretended Mia spoke, “I’m sorry, Ellie. Yes, let’s build!”

Ellie’s heart felt light. “Thank you, Mia,” she said. “I’m grateful you want to help.”

They picked up blocks together. Tap, tap, tap went the tower. Ellie guided with words: “Red on the bottom, please. Blue next. Sparkly on top.” They giggled when the sparkly block made rainbow dots on Mia’s yarn hair. Ellie imagined the tower was now a rainbow castle with a friendly dragon who loved lullabies.

When the last block clicked into place, Ellie and Mia clapped. Baby Ben squeaked and did a tiny hop. Pip leaned in the pillow circle like a proud coach.

Miss Star peeked in the door. “I hear kind voices,” she said. “I see helpful hands. I am proud of you.”

Ellie felt calm, strong, and happy. She hugged Pip. “Today I learned something big,” she said. “When I use gentle words, people can understand me. When I say thank you, hearts feel warm. And when I imagine, play feels magical.”

She looked at her friends. “Let’s always talk, listen, and help,” Ellie said. “That’s how we shine.”

The clock went tick-tock, tick-tock. The nursery smelled like crayons and cookies. The rainbow dots danced on the walls. Ellie’s tower stood tall, and so did her gentle words.

The End.
