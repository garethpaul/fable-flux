# Ruby Rabbit's Rainbow Art Room

Ruby Rabbit hopped into the bright art room, her fluffy white tail wiggling with excitement. The room smelled like fresh paint and crayons. Colorful pictures covered the walls, and sunshine streamed through big windows.

"Good morning, everyone!" called Ms. Fox, their art teacher. "Today we're making friendship pictures!"

Ruby looked around at her classmates. There was Benny Bear with brown fur, Lily Lamb with curly wool, Sam Squirrel with a bushy gray tail, and Maya Mouse with tiny pink ears. Everyone looked so different from her!

"I want to sit with someone who looks like me," Ruby whispered to herself, looking at her white fur. But everyone else had different colored fur, different sizes, and different shapes.

Ruby's ears drooped down. She felt sad and alone. She sat by herself at a small table, picking up a white crayon that matched her fur.

Maya Mouse noticed Ruby sitting alone. Her little whiskers twitched with concern. "Ruby, why are you sitting by yourself?" Maya asked in her squeaky voice.

"Nobody looks like me," Ruby said quietly, her nose twitching nervously. "Everyone has different fur colors."

Benny Bear waddled over, his big paws making soft sounds on the floor. "But Ruby, that's what makes us special! We're all different!"

"Different can be scary," Ruby admitted, taking deep breaths to calm herself.

Sam Squirrel scampered over, his tail swishing. "Want to see something cool?" He pointed to the wall where all their pictures hung together. "Look how beautiful they are because they're all different colors!"

Ruby looked up. The wall looked like a beautiful rainbow! There were pictures with brown trees, yellow suns, blue skies, and green grass - all different colors working together.

"Can I help you with your picture?" asked Lily Lamb, her soft voice gentle and kind.

Ruby felt her heart warm up. "Yes, please," she said, her ears perking up a little.

Maya Mouse brought over red paint. "Red is my favorite!"

Benny Bear shared his blue paint. "Blue makes me happy!"

Sam Squirrel offered yellow paint. "Yellow is bright like sunshine!"

Lily Lamb gave Ruby green paint. "Green is peaceful like grass!"

Soon, Ruby was mixing all the colors together with her new friends. She dipped her brush in red paint and made big, sweeping movements across her paper. Then blue for the sky, yellow for the sun, and green for the grass.

"This is so much fun!" Ruby giggled, hopping a little as she painted. Her body felt loose and happy as she moved the brush around.

"See?" said Maya Mouse. "When we all share our different colors, we make something beautiful together!"

Ruby stepped back and looked at her painting. It was full of every color her friends had shared. It was the most beautiful picture she had ever made!

"You're right!" Ruby said, her tail wiggling with joy. "Our differences make everything more colorful and special!"

Ms. Fox clapped her paws together. "What a wonderful lesson, everyone! Just like your paintings, our classroom is beautiful because we're all different and unique!"

Ruby hugged each of her friends. She felt proud, happy, and grateful. Being different wasn't scary anymore - it was wonderful!

From that day on, Ruby always remembered that differences make the world more beautiful, just like colors make a painting special.

The End.
