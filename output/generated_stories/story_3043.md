# Koby's Big Sorry

On a peaceful front porch with white wooden chairs, a calm koala named Koby sat very still. His gray fur was soft like velvet. His round black nose wiggled as he smelled the sweet morning air.

Koby looked at the broken flower pot beside him. Yellow daisies lay scattered on the wooden boards. Dirt was everywhere. His tummy felt funny and sad.

"Oh no," Koby whispered. "I broke Mama's favorite flowers."

Yesterday, Koby had been practicing his jumping. Hop, hop, hop across the porch! But he jumped too close to the pretty yellow flowers. CRASH! Down went the pot.

Koby had run inside quickly. He didn't tell Mama what happened. Now the broken pieces sparkled in the sunshine, and Koby felt worse and worse.

"I should have been more careful," he said to himself. His little paws covered his eyes.

Just then, Mama Koala came outside. She carried a steaming cup of tea that smelled like honey. When she saw the broken pot, she stopped.

"Oh my," said Mama gently. "What happened to my daisies?"

Koby's heart beat fast like a little drum. He took a deep breath. "Mama, I did it. I broke your flowers when I was practicing jumping. I'm really, really sorry."

Mama sat down in the rocking chair. It creaked softly. "Thank you for telling me the truth, Koby. That was very brave."

"I feel terrible," Koby said. His voice was small. "I want to make it better. Can I help fix it?"

Mama smiled warmly. "Of course! Making amends means trying to fix what we've done wrong. What ideas do you have?"

Koby thought hard. He tapped his fuzzy chin with one paw. "Maybe we could plant new flowers together? I could practice being extra gentle."

"That's a wonderful idea," said Mama. "And maybe you could help me water all the plants every morning?"

Koby's eyes grew bright. "Yes! I want to help take care of everything. I'll be super careful."

They walked to the garden shed together. Mama showed Koby how to hold the small shovel properly. They chose bright red flowers this time.

"Gentle scoops," Mama said. "Just like this."

Koby practiced digging small holes. Scoop, scoop, scoop. The dirt felt cool and soft between his claws. "I'm getting better!" he said proudly.

Together, they planted six new flowers in a bigger, stronger pot. Koby watered them with a tiny watering can. Sprinkle, sprinkle went the water drops.

"These flowers are even prettier," said Koby. "And I helped make them grow!"

"You did wonderful work," said Mama. "I'm proud of you for telling the truth and helping to fix things."

Koby hugged Mama tight. She smelled like eucalyptus leaves and sunshine. "I learned that saying sorry and helping fix things feels much better than hiding."

From that day on, Koby watered the flowers every morning. He practiced jumping in the yard instead of on the porch. The red flowers grew tall and beautiful.

Whenever Koby made a mistake, he remembered to tell the truth right away. He always asked, "How can I help make this better?"

And Mama always smiled and said, "Making amends shows how much you care."

The End.
