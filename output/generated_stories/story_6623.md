# Oliver Owl's Big Goal

High up in a cozy attic, where moonbeams danced through dusty windows, lived Oliver the wise old owl. His feathers were soft and brown, and his big round eyes sparkled like golden coins. Oliver loved his quiet home among the old books and wooden boxes.

One morning, Oliver felt tired and sluggish. "Hoo, hoo," he sighed, ruffling his feathers. "I don't feel very strong anymore. I used to fly so fast and far!"

Oliver looked at himself in an old mirror leaning against a trunk. His tummy was a bit rounder than before, and his wings felt heavy. "I need to get healthy again," he said to himself. "But how?"

The wise owl thought and thought. Then his eyes lit up! "I know! I'll make a goal to get stronger and healthier. But I need a plan."

Oliver found a piece of paper and a pencil. With his wing, he carefully wrote down his big goal: "Get healthy and strong again!"

"Now," Oliver hooted softly, "what small steps can I take every day?"

He wrote down three simple things:
1. Fly around the attic ten times each morning
2. Eat healthy seeds and berries instead of too many cookies
3. Sleep well every night

"These are my daily goals!" Oliver said proudly. "Small steps will help me reach my big goal!"

The next morning, Oliver stretched his wings wide. "One, two, three!" he counted as he flew in circles around the attic. The dusty air tickled his feathers, and his heart began to beat faster. "Four, five, six!" 

By the time he reached ten, Oliver was breathing hard but felt wonderful. "That felt good!" he hooted happily.

For breakfast, instead of reaching for the jar of sweet cookies, Oliver chose some crunchy sunflower seeds and juicy blueberries. "Mmm," he said, "these taste fresh and make my tummy feel light and happy!"

Each day, Oliver followed his plan. Some days were harder than others. When it rained, he didn't want to exercise. "But I made a promise to myself," Oliver reminded himself. So he did stretches and wing flaps inside instead.

When he felt like eating cookies, Oliver would say, "I can have one small cookie, but first I'll eat my healthy foods." This made him feel proud and in control.

After two weeks, Oliver looked in the mirror again. His eyes were brighter, his feathers shinier, and he felt so much stronger! "Hoo, hoo!" he celebrated. "My small daily goals helped me reach my big goal!"

Oliver learned that the secret to reaching big goals was breaking them into small, daily steps. He felt proud every time he completed his daily goals, and soon the healthy habits became easy and fun.

From that day on, Oliver shared his wisdom with all the young animals who visited the attic. "Remember," he would tell them, "big goals are reached with small steps. Make a plan, do a little bit each day, and celebrate your progress!"

Oliver continued his healthy habits, feeling strong and wise. He had learned that taking care of himself was the best gift he could give to his body and mind.

The End.
