# Bucky Beaver and the Bubble Flood

Bucky Beaver was a very busy beaver. He loved to help. He loved to fix. He loved to make things shine. Today, Bucky’s job was in the bathroom.

Drip, drip, drip went the sink. “I can fix that,” Bucky said with a bright smile. The bathroom smelled like mint soap. The tiles felt cool under his little beaver feet. The mirror was foggy from a warm bath. Bucky wiggled his whiskers and got to work.

He turned the handle. “Squeak!” said the knob. He scrubbed the sink. “Swish, swish,” went his brush. He wiped the mirror. “Wipe, wipe,” went his cloth. He squeezed the soap. “Squirt!” went the bottle. A sweet, bubbly smell filled the air.

Bucky liked the bubbles. They were shiny and soft. He poked one. “Pop!” He giggled. “Just a little more soap,” he said. “Then it will be super clean.”

Squirt! Squirt! The bottle slipped. Plop! It fell into the sink. The water rushed in. The bubbles grew. They grew fast. They grew big. They grew everywhere.

“Uh-oh,” Bucky whispered. The bubbles tumbled over the sink. They rolled onto the floor. They slid to the door. The bathroom looked like a white, fluffy cloud. Bucky’s tail twitched. His heart thumped. He wanted to run around and push and splash.

“I have to hurry! I have to fix it!” he squeaked.

But then Bucky stopped. He took a slow breath in. He blew a slow breath out. He put his paws on his chest and said softly, “Pause, Bucky. Use your calm.” He had learned this from his forest friends. When things felt too big, he could breathe. He could think.

Bucky closed his eyes for three tiny breaths.
“In… and out,” he said.
“In… and out.”
“In… and out.”

He opened his eyes. His busy tail felt less wiggly. His heart felt steady. “I can do this,” he said. “First, turn off the water.”

He waddled through the bubbles. Swish, swish went the foam on his fur. “Excuse me, bubbles,” he said kindly. He turned the handle left. “Squeak!” The water stopped. The bubbles still spread, but slower now.

“Next, use tools,” Bucky said. He looked around. He saw a big towel. He saw a small cup. He saw the bath mat. He saw the drain.

“Tools can help,” he told himself. “Think, then do.”

Bucky put the cup in the sink and scooped out extra bubbles. “Scoop, scoop.” He poured them into the tub. “Plop, plop.” He placed the bath mat by the door so the bubbles would not slide out. “There you go,” he said to the mat. He laid the big towel on the floor where it was the most slippery. “You can drink up the water,” he told the towel.

He remembered the drain. “Drains like water, not bubbles,” Bucky thought. He grabbed the small strainer from the shower. He held it over the sink drain. “You catch the bubbles,” he said. With gentle paws, he scooped bubbles into the strainer and let the water slip through. “Drip, drip,” went the water. “Good job,” Bucky whispered.

Bucky’s friend, Dot the Duck, peeped from the tub toy basket. “Peep? Is it safe?”

“Yes, Dot,” said Bucky. “I’m staying calm. I’m fixing the bubble flood.”

Dot waddled to the edge and flapped her soft wings. She fanned bubbles toward the tub. “Peep-peep! Teamwork!”

“Thank you,” said Bucky. “Helping makes a big job small.”

They worked and hummed. Bucky hummed, “Hmm-hmm, we can do it.” Dot peeped a happy beat. The bathroom began to look like itself again. The mint smell was gentle now. The floor felt less slippery. The mirror shone.

Bucky checked the sink. No more bubble mountain. He picked up the soap bottle and set it back. “Only a little,” he said, tapping it lightly. “Tap, not squirt.”

Dot smiled. “Peep! You stayed calm.”

Bucky nodded. “When I paused, I could think. Then I could fix.”

He rinsed his paws and dried them on a clean towel. “Soft,” he said, rubbing his paws together. He looked around the bathroom. It was clean. It was safe. It was ready for everyone to use.

Bucky peeked into the hallway. His neighbors were there—Mama Moose, Little Squirrel, and Owl. They had heard the squeaks and pops.

“Is everything okay?” asked Mama Moose.

“Yes!” Bucky said with a proud smile. “I made a big bubble mess. I felt wiggly and worried. Then I took calm breaths. I used tools. My friend helped. Now it’s all clean.”

Little Squirrel clapped. “Can you teach me the calm breaths?”

Bucky nodded. “Let’s practice. Paws on chest. In… and out.” They all breathed together. Even Owl blinked slowly and hooted, “Hoo… hoo…”

Bucky felt warm inside. He loved to help. He loved to fix. He loved to share. “Next time,” he said, “I will use just a tiny drop of soap. I will turn the water slowly. And if something goes wrong, I will pause, breathe, and think.”

Mama Moose smiled. “That is self-control,” she said. “You took care of yourself. You took care of the bathroom. You helped us all.”

Bucky’s tail gave a happy thump. “That’s community service,” he said softly. “Helping makes our home feel good.”

Dot peeped, “And bubbles can be fun… in the right amount!”

Bucky laughed. “Yes. In the right amount.”

The bathroom sparkled. The floor was dry. The friends felt calm and proud.

Lesson: When things feel big, pause, breathe, and think. Use helpful tools. Ask for help. Helping yourself helps your whole community.

The End.
