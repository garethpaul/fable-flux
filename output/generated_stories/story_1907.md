# Benny Bunny and the Fair Art Day

In the bright, colorful art room at Sunny Day School, Benny Bunny hopped excitedly between the tables. His fluffy white tail wiggled with joy as he looked at all the wonderful art supplies. There were red crayons, blue paint, yellow paper, and sparkly stickers everywhere!

"Today we're making friendship pictures!" announced Mrs. Fox, their kind teacher. "Everyone will get five minutes with each art station."

Benny's ears perked up. He loved making art! He could smell the fresh crayons and hear the gentle swish of paintbrushes. This was going to be the best day ever!

But when Benny hopped to the painting station, he found Sammy Squirrel already there. Sammy had been painting for a very long time - much longer than five minutes.

"Excuse me, Sammy," said Benny politely. "I think it's my turn to paint now."

Sammy didn't look up from his picture. "I'm not done yet. I need more time," he said, dipping his brush in bright green paint.

Benny felt his whiskers twitch with worry. This didn't seem fair. He looked at the big clock on the wall. Sammy had been painting for ten whole minutes!

Other animals were waiting too. Lucy Lamb wanted to use the glitter, but Max Mouse had been there too long. Penny Pig was waiting for the stickers that Ollie Owl wouldn't share.

Benny's heart felt heavy. Everyone was getting upset, and no one was having fun anymore. The art room felt tense instead of happy.

"This isn't right," Benny said to himself. He took three deep breaths, just like Mrs. Fox had taught them. "When something isn't fair, I need to speak up kindly."

Benny hopped to the center of the room and cleared his throat. "Friends," he said in his clearest voice. "I think we have a problem. Some of us are taking too much time at each station. That's not fair to everyone else."

The room grew quiet. All the animals looked at Benny with their big, curious eyes.

"Mrs. Fox said five minutes at each station," Benny continued. "When we follow the rules, everyone gets a fair turn. That's what makes art time fun for everybody!"

Sammy Squirrel's cheeks turned pink. "Oh! I'm sorry, Benny. I got so excited about my painting that I forgot about the timer." He carefully put down his brush and stepped away from the easel.

"Thank you, Sammy!" said Benny, giving him a gentle pat on the shoulder. "Your painting looks beautiful!"

Mrs. Fox smiled proudly at Benny. "You're absolutely right, Benny. Let's set the timer for each station so everyone remembers."

*Ding!* went the little timer as Mrs. Fox set it on each table.

Soon, the art room was buzzing with happy sounds again. Animals moved from station to station when the timer rang. Everyone got to paint, color, glitter, and stick!

Benny felt warm and proud inside. When he saw something unfair, he had spoken up kindly instead of getting angry. He had helped make things better for everyone.

At the end of art time, all the friends gathered in a circle with their beautiful pictures.

"Thank you, Benny," said Lucy Lamb. "You helped us remember to be fair."

"And you did it nicely," added Sammy Squirrel. "That made me want to do the right thing too."

Benny's ears stood up tall with pride. He had learned that being fair and kind made everyone happier - including himself!

The End.
