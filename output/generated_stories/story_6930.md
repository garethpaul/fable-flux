# Grace the Giraffe Speaks Up

Grace was a thoughtful giraffe. She lived with her family near a sunny community garden. One rainy morning, Grace tiptoed into the pantry. The pantry smelled like warm oats and sweet apples. Jars clinked softly. Paper bags rustled. Grace loved the pantry. It felt cozy and safe.

Grace reached her long neck to a high shelf. She could see a rainbow row of paint jars way in the back. “Oh!” she said. “My art club needs paint for our big outdoor mural. The mural will show our town helping each other.” She blinked her long lashes and smiled. “We can ask before we take. We can share.”

Grace heard voices. “We will plan the garden clean-up today,” said Mr. Otter, the pantry helper. “We need signs, snacks, and smiles!”

Grace took a breath. “Excuse me,” she called gently. Her voice shook a little. “I have an idea.”

Mr. Otter poked his head around a sack of flour. “Hello, Grace! What do you need?”

Grace’s knees felt wobbly. She tapped her hoof. Tap-tap. “I feel nervous,” she whispered to herself. “But my voice is important.” She lifted her head. “I need help reaching the paint on the high shelf. Our art club wants to paint a big welcome mural outside. It will show everyone helping, and everyone welcome. Could we please use the paint?”

Mr. Otter smiled. “Thank you for asking with kind words,” he said. “Let’s check the label.” He read the jar. “These paints were donated for community projects. Your mural is a community project!”

Grace’s heart felt warm, like toast. “Thank you,” she said. “May I also borrow brushes? We will bring them back clean.”

“You may,” said Mr. Otter. “And I’ll carry the step stool so no one climbs on bags.”

Grace nodded. “That is safe. Thank you for keeping us safe.”

Just then, little Tilly Tortoise peeped from behind a box of tea. “I want to help,” said Tilly. “But I am slow. I am worried I will be in the way.”

Grace bent her long neck low so her eyes met Tilly’s. “You belong with us,” Grace said softly. “Everyone helps in their own way. Would you like to pass out paint lids and choose colors?”

Tilly’s eyes sparkled. “Yes, please!”

A raccoon named Riley peeked from beneath a stack of napkins. “I can carry snacks,” Riley said. “My paws are quick.”

Grace smiled. “Thank you, Riley. We can help together.”

Grace turned back to Mr. Otter. She felt brave now. “May we put a sign in the pantry that says, ‘Ask for help if you need it’? Sometimes I am tall and can reach high. Sometimes I need help too.”

Mr. Otter nodded. “That is a good idea. Asking for help is using your strong voice.”

Grace carefully balanced the paint in a basket. The brushes felt soft and tickly. The rain slowed. The pantry door opened to the fresh air. The smell of wet grass reached in, cool and bright.

Outside, the art club gathered near the garden fence. Birds chirped. The ground squished a little under hooves and paws. Grace placed the paints on a cloth. “Friends,” she said, “today we paint a mural for everyone.”

“Can I paint clouds?” asked Sam the sparrow.

“Yes,” said Grace.

“Can I paint a hand waving hello?” asked Mina the mouse.

“Yes,” said Grace.

Tilly lifted a blue lid with care. “Blue for sky,” she said. She passed a brush to Mina. Riley opened a box. “Crunchy crackers,” he said, grinning.

Grace dipped her brush. Swish-swish. The paint smelled faintly like berries. Her strokes made big, friendly shapes. A sun. A garden. A table where friends shared food. She added a tall giraffe and a tiny tortoise painting side by side.

A gust of wind puffed. “Oh!” said Sam. A paper sign skittered toward a puddle.

“I’ve got it,” said Grace. She stepped gently and pinned the sign with a smooth stone. She looked at everyone. “Is anyone too tired or needing a break?” she asked.

Tilly raised her little hand. “I need a rest,” she said.

Grace nodded. “Thank you for telling us what you need,” she said. “There is a dry bench by the pantry. Riley, will you bring Tilly some water?”

“On it,” said Riley. He padded off and came back with a cup and a napkin.

They painted until the clouds drifted away and the sun peeped out. The mural shone with bright colors. It showed many friends passing tools, sharing snacks, and smiling. Above it, Grace hung a small painted sign: “Please Ask. We Are Here.”

Mr. Otter clapped. “You did it!”

Grace felt a happy flutter in her chest. She looked at her friends. “Today I used my voice,” she said. “I asked for what we needed. You used your voices too. We listened. We helped. We made art for our whole town.”

Tilly smiled from the bench. “I feel included,” she said.

Riley waved his painty paw. “Me too!”

Grace took a deep breath of fresh, leafy air. “When we speak up kindly, we make room for everyone,” she said.

The End.
