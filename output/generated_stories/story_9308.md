# Rocco’s Fair Turn Band

Rocco was a musical raccoon with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved sounds. Tap, tap on a box. Ting, ting on a jar. Boom, boom on a drum. He kept his special instruments in a quiet attic where the air smelled like old wood and warm dust. Sunlight slid in through a little round window and made golden stripes on the floor.

One rainy afternoon, Rocco climbed the creaky stairs. Creak, creak. He pushed open the attic door. It made a soft squeeeak. Inside, the room felt cozy and calm. Rocco smiled. “Hello, music,” he said.

He lined up his instruments. A tiny drum with a red ribbon. A shiny triangle. A wooden xylophone with rainbow bars. A gentle shaker with smooth beans inside. When he shook it, it went swish-swish like soft rain.

Soon, friends came to play music too. Pip the pigeon fluttered in and landed by the window. “Coo! I want to play the drum,” Pip said.

Mimi the mouse scurried out from a hat. “Squeak! I want the drum too,” Mimi said.

Gertie the snail slid across the floor, her shell a little rainbow. “I can keep a slow beat,” she said. “I would like the drum.”

Rocco’s ears twitched. Three friends. One drum. The attic felt quiet again. The drum sat in the middle like a tiny red moon.

“I brought the instruments,” Rocco said. “I want the drum first.” He grabbed the sticks. Tap-tap-tap! He played fast and loud. Pip fluffed her feathers. Mimi’s whiskers drooped. Gertie sighed a tiny snail sigh.

Pip said, “Coo, Rocco, may I have a turn?”

Mimi squeaked, “Me too!”

Gertie whispered, “And me.”

Rocco held the sticks tight. “But the drum is my favorite,” he said. “Maybe later.”

He played and played. Tap-tap-tap! Boom-boom! Ting! The attic filled with sound. At first, it felt exciting. Then the music started to feel bumpy. Pip stopped fluffing her feathers. Mimi curled her tail around herself. Gertie pulled in her feelers.

Pip said softly, “It doesn’t feel fair.”

Mimi nodded. “No fair.”

Gertie whispered, “Fair means we share.”

Rocco paused. He looked at their faces. Pip’s eyes were sad. Mimi’s ears drooped. Gertie’s shell glowed dull. Rocco’s whiskers tingled. The attic felt too tight. He heard his own heart beat, boom… boom… like a slow drum.

Rocco took a deep breath in. The attic smelled like apples from a box in the corner. He let the breath out slowly. “I want to be fair,” he said. “I can try again.”

“How?” asked Pip.

Rocco tapped the floor with a stick. “We can make a turn plan. Everyone gets a turn. I will wait with patience. We can practice together so the music feels good for everyone. That is wellness for our hearts.”

Gertie smiled. “A turn plan is kind.”

Mimi clapped her tiny hands. “Yes, yes!”

Rocco set the drum in the center. He put a small sand timer next to it. “When the sand falls, we switch,” he said. “I will go last this time to be fair.”

The friends nodded. Pip went first. She tapped gently. Tap… tap… tap. Her wings rustled like paper. Mimi shook the shaker. Swish-swish. It sounded like rain on leaves. Gertie counted slow and steady. “One… two… one… two…”

Rocco felt a little wiggle inside. He wanted to grab the sticks. He took another slow breath in and out. “Patience,” he whispered. “I can wait.”

The sand fell. Pip passed the drum to Mimi. “Your turn,” said Pip. Mimi tapped with tiny paws. Tippy tap, tippy tap. She smiled. The attic felt warm. Rocco swayed and played the triangle. Ting! Ting! Gertie kept the beat. “One… two… one… two…”

When the sand fell again, Mimi looked at Gertie. “Your turn,” Mimi said. She passed the sticks with care.

Gertie took a long moment. She lifted a stick, then the other. Boom… pause… boom… pause. It was slow and steady, like footsteps in soft mud. Rocco listened. He noticed how the slow beat made his shoulders relax. He felt calm.

“You’re doing great,” Rocco said. “Your slow beat is strong.”

Gertie glowed. “Thank you. I like going slow.”

Soon, the sand fell one more time. Pip said, “Now it’s your turn, Rocco.”

Rocco smiled. He felt proud for waiting. He tapped the drum, but this time he listened to his friends. Pip shook the shaker. Swish-swish. Mimi tinged the triangle. Ting! Ting! Gertie counted. “One… two… one… two…”

Rocco kept the beat fair and even. Boom-boom. Boom-boom. The sounds fit together like puzzle pieces. The attic sang with gentle music, and the golden sun stripes seemed to dance.

“We did it together,” Pip said.

“We were fair,” Mimi added.

“We had patience,” Gertie whispered.

“We practiced taking turns,” Rocco said. “I felt wiggly inside, but slow breaths helped. Waiting made the music better. Sharing made our hearts feel good.”

They played one more song with the turn plan. Sometimes fast, sometimes slow, always fair. When they were done, they packed the instruments with care. The attic felt even cozier, like it was smiling too.

Rocco looked at his friends. “Fair means everyone gets a turn,” he said. “Patience helps us wait. Practice helps us play well. When we include everyone, the music is happy.”

Pip cooed. “That is a good lesson.”

Mimi squeaked, “I will remember!”

Gertie nodded. “Me too.”

Rocco’s tail swished. “Me three,” he said, and they all laughed.

The End.
