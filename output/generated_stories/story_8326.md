# Leo the Lion Cub Cleans the Home Office

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright, curious eyes. Today, he padded into the home office on quiet paws. The room smelled like warm paper and a little like fresh lemon from the clean desk. The keys on the keyboard made tiny click-click sounds. Sunlight shone in, laying a square of warm light on the floor. It felt good on Leo’s paws.

Mama Lion worked at the desk. She had a big mug of tea that smelled sweet and cozy. “Leo,” Mama said gently, “I have work to do. Please play quietly. And please keep the home office tidy.”

Leo nodded. “I can do that!” he said in a brave voice.

He looked around. There were crinkly papers, smooth pens, and a blue box for recycling. There was a soft trash bin for real trash. There was also a plant in a pot with shiny green leaves. It smelled like clean rain when Leo sniffed it.

“I will help the Earth,” Leo whispered. “I will be a helper cub.”

He picked up a crumpled paper. It felt light and scratchy. “This goes in the blue box!” Leo said. He dropped it in. Plink!

He found a banana peel on the desk from snack time. It was squishy and a little sticky. “Does the peel go in the blue box?” Leo wondered.

“Hmm,” he said, tapping his chin with a paw.

Mama looked over. “Leo, can you listen for a moment?”

Leo sat down. “I’m listening,” he said.

“Paper and cardboard go in the blue box,” Mama said kindly. “Food scraps do not. The peel can go in the trash, or even better, the compost, if we have one.”

Leo nodded. “Thank you, Mama. I will put the peel in the trash today.” He dropped it into the soft bin. Thump.

He found an empty water bottle. It felt cool and smooth. He squeezed it. Squeak! “Blue box or trash?” he asked.

Mama smiled. “Blue box.”

“Okay!” Leo said. Plonk! In it went.

Leo felt proud. He liked helping the Earth. He liked listening, too. Listening made his heart feel calm and warm.

Just then, he saw his friend, Tilly Turtle, peeking in from the hallway. Tilly wore tiny round glasses that made her eyes look big and kind.

“Hi, Leo,” Tilly said in a soft voice. “Can I play with you in the home office?”

Leo’s tail swished. He remembered Mama’s words. “We can play quietly,” he said. “We must keep it tidy. We are Earth helpers.”

Tilly smiled. “I can help. I like helping,” she said.

They found a box of old magazines by the shelf. The pages were smooth and shiny. They smelled like ink and a little bit like apples. “Let’s make pretend mountains,” Leo said. He stacked the magazines gently. Tilly slid one off with a soft shffft. They both giggled.

Soon, some pages fluttered to the floor. Flip, flop! Leo’s ears perked up. “We need to tidy,” he said.

Tilly picked up a page with a picture of a tree. “It’s so pretty,” she whispered. “Where does it go?”

Leo knew! “In the blue box,” he said proudly. “We are caring for the Earth.”

Tilly nodded, but then she reached for the plant. “Can the plant wear a paper hat?” she asked shyly.

Leo paused. He felt a tiny worry. He loved the plant. He wanted it to be happy. He took a breath and listened inside his heart. Then he listened to Tilly’s voice. She wanted to play. He also listened to the plant’s leaves, which rustled softly like, Please be gentle.

Leo spoke kindly. “The plant does not like hats,” he said. “But we can give it water. That will make it happy.”

Tilly’s face brightened. “Okay,” she said. “I like that idea.”

They asked Mama, “May we give the plant a little water?”

Mama smiled. “Thank you for asking and listening,” she said. “Yes, just a little.”

Leo poured carefully. The water made a quiet glug-glug and the soil smelled rich like the forest. The plant’s leaves looked shiny and glad.

Then Leo saw something under the desk. A small, shiny battery! It felt hard and cold in his paw. “Blue box?” he asked.

Mama shook her head gently. “Batteries are special. We save them in the small red jar for safe recycling.”

Leo placed the battery in the red jar. Clink. He felt even braver now. He was learning so much by listening.

Soon, the home office looked neat. The blue box had papers and the bottle. The trash bin had the peel. The red jar had the battery. The plant had a little drink. The floor felt smooth and clean under their paws and little turtle toes.

“You did wonderful work,” Mama said. “You showed care for our home and for the Earth. You listened. And you accepted each other’s ideas.”

Leo and Tilly smiled at each other. “We make a good team,” Leo said.

“We do,” Tilly agreed. “Different ideas can still be kind ideas.”

Leo puffed his chest, just a little. He felt brave and gentle at the same time.

Mama poured more tea. Its steam smelled like sweet lemons. The keyboard went click-click as she worked. The home office hummed softly, peaceful and tidy.

Leo whispered, “Earth helpers today, Earth helpers always.”

Tilly nodded. “We can do small things every day,” she said. “We can sort, we can reuse, and we can listen.”

Leo curled his tail around his paws. He felt happy. He learned that caring for the Earth starts at home, that listening helps everyone, and that accepting others makes room for good ideas.

The End.
