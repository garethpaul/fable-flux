# Panda Patch Fixes It Gently

In a cozy garage workshop, the air smelled like wood and warm glue. Sunlight slid through a small window and made bright squares on the floor. Tools hung on the wall, clink-clink, like tiny wind chimes when the door moved. A big blue bin for recycling sat by the workbench.

Panda Patch wore a soft apron with pockets. He was a patient panda who loved to fix old things. He liked to give them a new life. He patted his round tummy and smiled. “Good morning, little workshop,” he said. “Let’s see what we can do today.”

On the bench was a wobbly toy truck. One wheel was missing. Next to it was a torn kite with a rainbow tail. Patch’s ears perked up. “We can help you both,” he said kindly.

Patch picked up the toy truck. Clack-clack went the loose wheels. He reached for a screw, but the screw slipped from his paw and tinked on the floor. Tink! It rolled under a shelf.

Patch felt a hot bubble of grump rising in his chest. His paws wanted to clench. His face started to scrunch. “Oh no,” he whispered. “I feel mad.”

He put a paw on his tummy. “Pause, Patch,” he reminded himself. “Slow down and breathe.” He closed his eyes and took a soft breath in. One… two… three. He blew out gently like he was cooling hot soup. “Whoooo.”

He listened to the quiet garage. Drip-drop, went a tiny leak into a bucket. Far away, a bird chirped. Patch’s shoulders softened. “I can try again,” he said calmly. “I can be gentle with myself.”

He got down on his knees. Dust tickled his nose. “Achoo!” he sneezed. He giggled. “Excuse me.” He peered under the shelf and saw the little screw sparkle. “There you are.” He used a magnet stick to pull it out. Click! “Thank you, magnet.”

Patch looked in the blue bin. There were old bottle caps, a bent spoon, and a cardboard tube. “Recycling can help us,” he said. “Let’s see what we can use.” He tried a bottle cap on the truck. It fit just right! “A new wheel from an old cap,” Patch cheered softly. “You get a second life.”

He tightened the screw slowly. Twist, twist, twist. The wheel spun smooth and happy. “Vroom,” Patch whispered, rolling the truck across the bench. “Good work, little truck.”

Next came the torn kite. Its rainbow tail felt silky. The paper made a crinkle sound. Patch touched the rip. It felt jagged. His paw slipped in the glue, and a sticky string stretched between his fingers. “Ew, sticky!” He almost frowned again. The grump bubble bumped at his chest.

Patch paused. “Feelings are okay,” he told himself. “I can choose what to do.” He placed his paw on his heart. “Breathe in… one, two, three. Blow out like a breeze on a kite.” Whoooo. He smiled. “I can clean up. I can take my time.”

He wiped his paws with a soft rag, warm and damp. He cut a neat patch from an old cereal box in the recycle bin. Snip, snip, snip went the scissors. He brushed on a thin layer of glue. Swish, swish. He pressed the patch on the rip and held it while he hummed. “Hum-hum-hum.” The glue smelled sweet, like school paste. “We wait, and we breathe,” he said gently.

As the kite dried, Patch looked around. On a shelf sat broken crayons. Some were tiny. Some had no paper. Patch’s eyes lit up. “We can recycle these too.” He found a muffin tin. He dropped the little crayons into the cups. Plink, plunk, plunk. “New rainbow crayons,” he whispered. He asked a grown-up neighbor to help with the warm oven. Safety first! Soon, the crayons melted into swirls. When they cooled, Patch popped them out. Pop! Pop! “Beautiful,” he said, holding the chunky round crayons. “Easy to hold.”

The kite was ready. Patch carried it to the open garage door where a soft breeze waited. The rainbow tail fluttered like a fish in water. “Are you ready, kite?” Patch asked. The kite didn’t answer, but Patch felt brave and calm. He ran two small steps. The kite lifted. Whoooosh! Up it went, higher and higher, dancing in the sky above the driveway.

A little mouse from next door peeked in. “Wow, Patch!” squeaked the mouse. “You fixed it! And that truck looks brand new.”

Patch smiled wide. “Thank you. I felt mad twice,” he said. “But I paused. I breathed. I tried again. Old things can become new. And feelings can calm down.”

The mouse nodded. “Can I ride the truck?” Patch gently pushed it. Vroom! The wheels rolled smooth. They both laughed.

Patch put the extra bottle caps back in the blue bin. He patted the bin. “Recycling helps the earth,” he said. “We use what we have. We make less trash.”

The sun slid lower, and the garage smelled like wood and crayons. Patch cleaned his tools and hung them up. Clink, clink. He folded his apron. His heart felt light and warm.

“Today I learned,” Patch said softly, “I can accept my feelings, I can slow down and breathe, and I can use old things in new ways.”

He looked up at the dancing kite and waved. “Thank you, day.”

The End.
