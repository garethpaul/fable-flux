# Koa the Calm Koala’s Bedroom Cleanup

Koa the calm koala woke up in a cozy bedroom with soft pillows and a fuzzy green blanket. The morning sun made warm stripes on the walls. A gentle breeze brought the smell of eucalyptus leaves from the window. Koa stretched, yawned, and blinked slowly.

“Good morning, room,” Koa whispered. “I’m ready for a kind day.”

Koa loved quiet mornings. He listened to the tiny tick-tock of a clock. He listened to the soft hum of a fan. He rubbed his furry cheeks and smiled.

On the floor were toy blocks, picture books, and little paper scraps from yesterday’s craft. Koa picked up a bright blue block. “Hmm,” he said softly. “My room is a little messy.”

Koa’s friend, a small plush wombat named Womby, looked up from the bed. “Koa, can we play?” Womby asked in a squeaky pretend voice.

“We can play,” Koa said, “but first, I want to care for our space. Caring for our space helps the Earth.”

“How?” Womby squeaked.

Koa held up a paper scrap. “When we reuse and sort things, we make less trash. Less trash means cleaner air, happy plants, and safe animals. That is good for the Earth.”

Koa took a deep breath and smelled the fresh air again. He felt calm and steady. “I can do this,” he said.

He set three baskets by the window. One basket was green, one was blue, and one was brown.

“The green basket is for recycling,” Koa said. “Paper and clean boxes go here.”

“The blue basket is for reusing,” he said. “Things we can use again go here.”

“The brown basket is for trash,” he said. “Only the things we can’t recycle or reuse go here.”

Womby blinked. “What about the toys?” he asked.

“Toys go back to their home,” Koa smiled. “Everything has a home.”

Koa picked up the paper scraps. “Paper can be recycled,” he told Womby. He dropped them into the green basket. Swish! The paper made a soft sound.

He found a plastic bottle cap and a clean cardboard roll. “The cap can be re-used for crafts. The roll can be recycled.” Plink! Swirl!

Koa’s neighbor, a sleepy possum named Pippa, peeked in the door. “Hello,” Pippa said with a tiny yawn. “What smells so nice?”

“The window is open,” Koa said. “Fresh air is good. We save energy by using the breeze instead of the fan when we can.”

Pippa stepped inside and felt the warm sun on her whiskers. “Mmm,” she said. “Can I help?”

“Yes,” Koa said calmly. “Helping is kind. We can all care for the Earth.”

They picked up books and set them on the shelf. Thump, thump—soft and neat. They placed crayons in a small tin. Tap, tap. They folded the fuzzy green blanket. Swoosh.

Koa found a torn picture. He felt a little sad. “This is broken,” he said. “I made it yesterday.”

Pippa touched Koa’s paw. “It’s okay,” she said.

Koa took a slow breath. “I will be honest,” he said. “I tore it by accident.” He looked at the green basket. “I can recycle the paper. Next time, I will be gentle.”

“That is integrity,” Pippa said. “You told the truth.”

Koa smiled. His chest felt warm and proud. He put the torn paper in the green basket. Swish.

Soon, the floor was clear. The sun made gold shapes on the clean rug. Koa ran his paw over the smooth wood of the shelf. “I like how this feels,” he said. “My room is cozy again.”

Pippa pointed to the blue basket. “What will you do with the reuse things?”

Koa held up the bottle cap and a ribbon. “I will make a shaker toy,” he said. “We can make music.”

He placed the cap into a small jar with a few buttons and tied it with the ribbon. Rattle-rattle! The sound was bright and happy.

“Listen,” Koa said. They shook the jar together. Rattle-rattle! The breeze carried the sound like a little song.

Womby wiggled with joy. “Music time!” he squeaked.

Koa turned off the fan and let the sunlight warm his fur. He took another deep breath. “I feel calm,” he said. “Caring for my room helps me, and it helps the Earth.”

Pippa nodded. “Small steps make a big help,” she said.

Koa put a tiny sign on the wall with simple pictures:
- A leaf for care
- A circle of arrows for recycle
- A heart for honesty

“These are our room rules,” Koa said. “We care. We recycle. We tell the truth.”

Pippa smiled. “I can remember that.”

Koa hugged Womby and waved to Pippa. “Thank you for helping,” he said.

“You’re welcome,” Pippa replied. “I will make a green, blue, and brown basket at my home too.”

Koa felt proud. He listened to the quiet tick-tock again. He smelled the clean air. He looked around his cozy bedroom and felt safe.

“Today was a good daily adventure,” Koa said. “We learned. We helped. We were kind.”

And in his calm voice, he added, “When we care for our room, we care for our world.”

The End.
