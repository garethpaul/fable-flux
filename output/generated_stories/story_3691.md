# Leo Learns to Speak Up

In a comfortable nursery, soft and warm like a hug, a brave little lion cub named Leo sat on a round rug. The rug felt fluffy under his paws. Sunlight painted golden stripes on the walls. The room smelled like clean crayons and fresh soap. Tiny books rested on low shelves. Soft blocks sat in a basket. A gentle clock went tick-tick-tick.

“Good morning, friends,” said Ms. Aya, the teacher, with a smile. Her voice was calm like a lullaby. “Today we learn about sharing, kind words, and our big world.”

Leo’s ears perked up. He loved learning. Learning made his heart feel bright. He wanted to be brave and smart, like the lions in his picture book.

“Roar!” he whispered happily to himself.

His friends were busy. Pippa Panda stacked blocks. Sami Squirrel drew a sun. Noor Narwhal, a plush toy, rested by the window to watch. The air was full of quiet rustles and soft scribbles.

Ms. Aya showed a round, colorful globe. It spun slowly, making a gentle whir. “This is our Earth,” she said. “Many people live in many places. We all learn together. We all share. We all use kind voices.”

Leo reached out a paw to touch the smooth globe. “It feels cool,” he said. “Like a pebble by a stream.”

Ms. Aya nodded. “Yes, Leo. Today we will read and build. We will practice using our voices.”

Leo’s whiskers twitched. He liked that. But when he turned, he saw that Pippa Panda had the blue book Leo loved. The book had shiny letters and big pictures. It was called “Colors Around the World.” Leo had planned to read it and then build a colorful tower to match.

Leo’s tummy fluttered. He took a breath. “I can be brave,” he told himself. “I can use my words.”

He walked over softly. “Hi, Pippa,” he said. “I really want to read that book. May I have a turn soon?”

Pippa hugged the book close. “I want it now,” she said. Her voice sounded tight, like a knot.

Leo’s heart thumped. The clock went tick-tick. The room felt smaller for a moment. He wanted to roar, but he remembered Ms. Aya’s lesson. Kind words. Sharing. Using his voice.

Leo stood tall but gentle. “Pippa,” he said, “I feel sad when I cannot read. I planned to build a color tower. Can we make a plan?”

Pippa blinked. Her ears drooped a little. “A plan?” she asked.

Ms. Aya walked over slowly. “I hear two strong voices,” she said softly. “I hear feelings. Leo used ‘I feel’ words. That is brave. Pippa, can we listen to Leo?”

Pippa looked at the shiny letters. She took a small breath. “I like this book too,” she said, quieter now. “I wanted to see the green sea turtles.”

Leo nodded. “Let’s read together,” he said. “We can take turns pointing. Then I will build a green turtle tower.”

Pippa thought, then smiled. “Okay. We can read together.”

They curled up on the rug. The book pages made a soft crackle. The pictures were bright—red markets, yellow fields, blue oceans, and happy faces from many places. “Hello,” Leo said, pointing to a child waving. “Hola,” Pippa tried, and they both giggled.

“This is global citizenship,” Ms. Aya said. “We learn about others. We care. We share.”

Leo felt warm and proud. He could almost hear a tiny brave roar in his chest. “I like learning about our big world,” he said. “It makes my heart grow.”

After reading, they walked to the block basket. The blocks smelled like clean wood. Leo picked green blocks. Pippa picked blue ones. Sami joined with yellow blocks.

Sami asked, “Can I add a sun?”

Leo smiled. “Yes! Let’s make a turtle with a sun hat.” They laughed.

They began to build. But the tower wobbled. “Uh-oh!” said Pippa. The blocks tilted. The turtle tower trembled like jelly.

Leo felt worry again. His paws felt tingly. “We can fix it,” he said. “Let’s try a strong base. We can learn from the book.” He pressed a wide block at the bottom. “Wide base,” he said. “Then gentle hands.”

Pippa held the side. Sami kept still. They placed each block slowly. Tap. Tap. Tap. The tower stood tall and steady. The green turtle wore a yellow sun hat and a blue smile.

“We did it!” cheered Sami.

Leo’s tail swished. “We learned, we spoke up, and we worked together,” he said. He looked at Pippa. “Thank you for sharing.”

Pippa patted the book. “Thank you for asking with kind words,” she said. “I liked our plan.”

Ms. Aya clapped softly. “I saw brave voices, kind hearts, and smart problem solving. That is education. That is friendship. That is being part of our big world.”

The clock went tick-tick. The sun felt warm on Leo’s fur. The nursery smelled like snack time now—sweet apple and cinnamon. Leo’s tummy rumbled.

As they washed hands, Leo looked at his reflection in the shiny sink. His eyes were bright. “I am brave,” he told himself. “I can use my words. I can learn every day.”

He turned to his friends. “Next time, we can build a red market,” he said. “We can say hello in many ways.”

“Bonjour!” giggled Pippa.

“Jambo!” laughed Sami.

Leo grinned. “Roar—hello!” Everyone laughed.

The tower stood tall and happy. The book rested open to a page of smiling children from around the world. The nursery felt big and gentle again.

Leo’s heart was calm and strong. He had learned the power of kind words, smart planning, and caring for others near and far.

The End.
