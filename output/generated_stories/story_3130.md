# Tiko’s Turn to Try

In a bright musical practice room, a gentle elephant calf named Tiko wiggled his soft ears. The room smelled like clean wood and lemon polish. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Drums sat in a neat row. Tiny bells jingled when the door moved. A big piano waited in the corner like a friendly giant.

Tiko’s teacher, Ms. Lark, smiled. “Today,” she said softly, “you will practice by yourself for a little while. You can do it, Tiko.”

Tiko’s heart thumped like a drum. “By myself?” he asked. His voice was small but brave.

“Yes,” said Ms. Lark. “I will be right outside. Try three things: warm-up taps, a steady beat, and a quiet finish. You are strong and gentle. You can try on your own.”

Tiko nodded. He wanted to be independent. He wanted to show he could start, keep going, and finish. He took a deep breath. He smelled the warm wood. He felt the drum skin, smooth and tight under his trunk.

“Warm-up taps,” Tiko told himself. “Tap-tap-tap.” He tapped softly with the tip of his trunk. Tap, tap, tap. The sound was like rain on big leaves. He smiled. “I can do this.”

Then he tried a steady beat. “Boom… boom… boom…” Tiko kept the rhythm slow and even. His feet rocked a little. The sound filled the room like a calm heartbeat. He felt proud.

Suddenly, the door creaked. The bells went jingle-jingle. Two monkey twins, Zizi and Zuzu, peeked in. Their eyes were bright. Their tails curled like question marks.

“Hi, Tiko!” said Zizi.

“We want to play now!” said Zuzu. “Move, move! It’s our turn!”

Tiko’s ears drooped. “It is my practice time,” he said softly. “Ms. Lark said I can try by myself.”

Zizi grabbed a shaker. Rattle-rattle! Zuzu hopped to the drums. Boom-bam! The sound was loud and fast. Tiko’s steady beat broke apart. His trunk wobbled.

“I need quiet,” Tiko whispered. His tummy felt twisty. He wanted to call Ms. Lark. He wanted to hide. But he remembered the plan: warm-up taps, steady beat, and quiet finish. He wanted to finish by himself.

Tiko stood tall. He took a slow breath. “Please,” he said, gentle but firm. “It is my turn now. I am practicing being independent. I will share after.”

“Why should we wait?” Zuzu asked. “We are fast!”

Tiko blinked. “Fair is fair,” he said. “We take turns. I started. You can be next. That is justice. It is the fair way.”

Zizi and Zuzu looked at each other. Their tails made little question marks again.

Tiko pointed to the wall chart with big, simple pictures: a drum, a clock, and two smiling faces taking turns. “See? First me, then you. I will be quick.”

Zizi frowned. “But we want fun now.”

Tiko nodded. “I want fun too. I feel excited and a little scared. I am learning to do things on my own. You can help by waiting. That is kind.”

He offered a plan. “You can listen for my beats. When I finish the quiet part, I will hand you the shaker. That will be the signal. Deal?”

Zuzu’s ears perked up. “A signal?”

Zizi peeked at the drum. “A quiet part?”

“Yes,” Tiko said. He smiled a small, hopeful smile. “I will do warm-up taps, a steady beat, and then a soft, soft finish. Shhhh like a bedtime hush. Then I will share.”

The twins thought for a moment. They put down the shaker and stepped back. “Okay,” they said together. “We will listen.”

The room felt calm again. Tiko’s tummy untwisted. He began his warm-up taps. Tap, tap, tap. Like rain on leaves. He moved to the steady beat. Boom… boom… boom. Like a big, friendly heart. Zizi swayed. Zuzu counted on fingers. One… two… three… four…

Now Tiko tried the quiet finish. He lowered his trunk. Soft… softer… softest. The sound floated like a feather. Hushhhhh. He let the last tiny note rest in the warm sunlight.

He did it. He finished all three parts. All by himself.

Tiko looked up. “Signal!” he said with a smile. He picked up the shaker and handed it to Zizi. “Your turn now.”

Zizi and Zuzu grinned. “That was nice,” said Zuzu. “You were brave and fair.”

Ms. Lark opened the door. The bells went jingle-jingle. “I heard a lovely hush,” she said. “Tiko, you practiced by yourself. You solved a problem with gentle words. You finished your task.”

Tiko’s cheeks felt warm and happy. “I did it,” he said. “I asked for my turn. I kept the beat. I shared after.”

Ms. Lark nodded. “That is independence,” she said. “You tried, you stayed calm, and you finished. You also used justice—fair turns. And you found a peaceful way when there was a mix-up.”

Zizi shook the shaker, soft-slow-slow. Zuzu tapped a drum, boom… boom… boom. Tiko clapped along. The room sounded friendly and bright.

Tiko’s heart felt strong. He learned that being independent means trying by yourself, asking for what is fair, and finishing what you start. It also means using kind words to fix problems.

He flapped his ears and smiled. “I can do hard things,” he whispered. “One tap at a time.”

The End.
