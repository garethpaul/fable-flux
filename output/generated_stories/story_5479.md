# Poppy the Patient Panda

Poppy the panda climbed up the last step and stepped onto the rooftop garden. The sun felt warm on her fur. A soft breeze tickled her ears. She could hear city birds chirp, “Cheep, cheep!” and smell sweet mint growing in a pot. The rooftop felt big and bright and open, like a sky playground.

Poppy wore a small red helmet and little knee pads. She held a colorful ribbon streamer. “Today I will learn to jump-rope,” Poppy said. “I will be patient. I will practice.”

Her friend, Mia the meerkat, peeked out from behind a planter. “Hi, Poppy! Are you ready?” Mia’s eyes were shiny with excitement. She held a blue jump rope with green handles.

“I am ready,” said Poppy, taking a slow breath. “I practiced my deep breaths. I brought water. I tied my shoes. I am prepared.”

Mia nodded. “Good plan! First, we warm up. Wiggle paws. Wiggle toes. Big arm circles!” They moved together—swish, swish, swish. Poppy felt her body wake up. She bounced gently on her heels. The rooftop tiles felt warm and smooth under her feet.

“Now I try,” Poppy said. She held the rope. Her paws felt a little slippery. “Oops,” she giggled, wiping them on her knee pads.

“Go slow,” Mia said kindly. “Start with counting.”

Poppy swung the rope. It tapped her toes. “Tap.” She lifted one foot late and tripped. “Oh!” She fell to her knees with a soft thump.

Poppy blinked. Her cheeks felt hot. A pigeon fluttered by and cooed, “Coo, coo,” as if cheering. Poppy put her paw on her chest and breathed in and out. “I am patient,” she whispered. “I can try again.”

Mia gave her a thumbs-up. “Let’s break it into steps,” she said. “Step one: swing. Step two: jump. Step three: land soft.”

Poppy smiled. “Swing, jump, land soft,” she repeated. She practiced just the swing. “Swish.” She listened to the rope brush the ground. She felt the timing. “Swish… swish…”

She tried a tiny jump. “Swish—hop!” The rope tickled her toes, and she stumbled. “Oh no,” she said, a little frown forming.

“It’s okay,” said Mia. “You moved! That’s great. Movement helps us learn.”

Poppy nodded. “Movement helps me learn. I will try again.” She drank a sip of water. The water was cool and crisp. It made her feel fresh.

They tried again. Poppy swung. “Swish.” She jumped. “Hop!” Her feet cleared the rope once. “I did one!” she laughed, eyes wide. The sun made her helmet shine.

“Can you do two?” Mia asked.

Poppy took a deep breath. “I will be patient. I will prepare. I will count.” She whispered, “One… two…” She swung. “Swish.” Jump. “Hop.” Then “Swish—hop.” The second loop tapped her heel, and she wobbled. “Yikes!”

Poppy felt a lump in her tummy. She almost wanted to stop. She looked at the fluffy clouds. She listened to the birds. She smelled the mint and rubbed a leaf. It made her paws smell fresh and sweet. Poppy’s face softened. “I can try small,” she said. “Small steps.”

Mia smiled. “Let’s practice just jumping without the rope,” she said. “Ten gentle hops.”

Poppy hopped on the spot. “Hop, hop, hop…” She counted to ten with Mia. The hops made her giggle. Her body felt bouncy and strong. “Now I feel ready,” Poppy said.

She picked up the rope again. “Swing, jump, land soft,” she reminded herself. “I am patient. I am prepared.” She tried: “Swish—hop. Swish—hop.” One… two… three! She did three in a row.

“I did three!” Poppy squealed, clapping her paws. The ribbon streamer fluttered, and the breeze brushed her ears.

“You did it because you kept going,” Mia said. “You were patient. You practiced. You moved your body and your mind.”

Poppy nodded. “Can I try four?”

“You can,” said Mia. “Remember your plan.”

Poppy stood tall. She set her feet. She wiggled her toes inside her shoes. She felt the warm tiles. She saw the rope. She heard the birds. She breathed in minty air. “Ready,” she said.

“Swish—hop. Swish—hop. Swish—hop. Swish—hop!” Four! On the fourth, she landed soft and steady. She laughed so loud the pigeon flapped up in surprise. “Coo!” it called.

Poppy hugged Mia. “Thank you for helping me break the steps down.”

Mia hugged back. “Thank you for not giving up.”

They sat on a cozy rooftop bench. The sun began to slide across the sky, turning the edges of clouds pink. The breeze felt cooler. Poppy sipped more water and kicked her feet happily.

“I learned something today,” Poppy said. “When something is hard, I can be patient. I can prepare with a plan. I can move in small steps. If I keep trying, I can learn new things.”

Mia nodded. “That’s the power of persistence.”

Poppy smiled at the garden. “Tomorrow, I will try five.”

“And I will cheer,” said Mia.

They watched the sky turn the color of peach tea. Poppy’s heart felt calm and proud. She tucked her rope into her bag, patted her helmet, and whispered, “Patient panda power.”

The End.
