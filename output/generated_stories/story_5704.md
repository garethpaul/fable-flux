# Kiko Joey’s Sunny Backyard Feelings

Kiko was a joyful kangaroo joey. Kiko loved to hop. Hop, hop, hop! The sunny backyard felt warm and bright. The grass tickled Kiko’s toes. A gentle breeze smelled like sweet flowers and fresh earth. Bird songs filled the air—chirp, tweet, trill!

“Good morning, sun!” Kiko said, lifting tiny paws to the sky. “Today will be big and bright!”

Kiko’s friends came to play. Mei the turtle slid slowly through the clover. Asha the sparrow flapped her soft wings. Tariq the squirrel twitched his fluffy tail. They liked to share games and stories from their homes.

Asha said, “In my family, we sing a hello song.” She sang softly, “Salaam, bonjour, hello!” The notes sounded like sunshine.

Mei smiled. “My grandma shows love with warm tea and a hug,” she said. She patted Kiko’s paw.

Tariq grinned. “My cousins drum on little cans,” he said, tapping a steady beat on a bucket. Tap-tap, tap-tap!

Kiko’s heart felt big with joy. “Let’s make a leaf picnic!” Kiko said. They gathered leaves, tiny sticks, and fallen flower petals. The petals smelled like honey. The sticks felt smooth and dry. The leaves made a friendly rustle.

“Let’s keep the backyard clean,” Mei reminded. “We love our home.” They placed trash in a little bin by the fence. Kiko nodded. “Caring for the earth is kind,” Kiko said.

They played tag. Hop, hop—flap, flap—pat, pat—scurry! Kiko leaped fast. The sun grew warmer. Kiko’s cheeks felt hot. Suddenly, Kiko’s hop went thump. Kiko tripped on a small rock and bumped a knee.

“Ouch,” Kiko whispered, eyes getting wet. Kiko’s tummy felt twisty. The world seemed too bright, too loud. The bird songs felt sharp. The sun felt too hot. Kiko’s smile fell away.

Asha fluttered close. “Kiko, are you okay?” she asked softly.

“I feel…big mad and big sad,” Kiko said. “I want to cry, but I feel wobbly.” Kiko’s ears drooped.

Mei took a slow breath. “We can care for feelings,” she said. “Let’s do the calm-count.” Mei touched her shell and counted, “One… two… three…” The counting sounded like waves.

Tariq sat beside Kiko. “We can cool down,” he said. He held out a little water bottle. “Sip, sip.”

Kiko took a sip. The water felt cool and kind. “Thank you,” Kiko said, voice small. “My knee still hurts.”

“Let’s check your body,” Mei said. “Where does it hurt?”

“My knee,” Kiko said, pointing. “And my heart feels stormy.”

Asha chirped, “It’s okay to cry.” She sang a tiny comfort tune from her family, the notes soft as feathers. Kiko let two tears fall. Plip, plip. The tears felt like rain that helps flowers grow.

Kiko breathed in through the nose. Sniff. Out through the mouth. Hoo. Kiko did it again. Sniff. Hoo. The storm inside got smaller. The sun still shone, but now it felt warm again, not too hot.

“I feel a little better,” Kiko said. “Thank you for helping me name my feelings.”

“Feelings tell us things,” Mei said. “Mad and sad said, ‘Slow down. Take care.’”

“Self-care time,” Tariq said. He opened a tiny cloth. Inside was a cool leaf wrap. “My aunt taught me this,” he said. “In my family, we use clean leaves and cool water for small bumps.” He wrapped Kiko’s knee gently. The leaf smelled fresh, like rain on grass.

Kiko smiled. “This feels nice.”

They sat in the shade by the flower bed. A bee buzzed by—bzzzz—and Kiko watched with calm eyes. “I can be gentle with me,” Kiko said. “I can rest. I can breathe.”

After a short rest, Kiko noticed a candy wrapper near the fence. “Oops,” Kiko said. “Let’s help the earth.” They picked it up together and put it in the bin. “Clean yard, happy heart,” Asha sang.

When Kiko was ready, they changed the game to something soft and slow. Mei showed a turtle stretch: “Arms out, breathe in… arms in, breathe out.” They all stretched. Their bodies felt loose and light.

“Thank you for sharing your ways,” Kiko said. “I learned songs, leaf wraps, and slow breaths. Different families help in different kind ways.”

Kiko looked at the sunny backyard. The colors seemed bright and friendly again—the grass green and cool, the sky blue and wide. “I feel brave now,” Kiko said. “I felt mad and sad. I asked for help. I took care of my body and my heart. And I cared for the earth, too.”

Tariq drummed a happy beat. Tap-tap, tap! Asha sang, “Salaam, bonjour, hello!” Mei smiled and passed around tiny cups of cool water like tea.

Kiko hopped once, softly. “My feeling plan is: name it, breathe, sip, rest, and share. Then play in a gentle way.”

The friends bumped paws, wings, and tails. “We’re a caring team,” they said together.

The sun began to tilt, warm and golden. Kiko waved to the sky. “Thank you for the bright day,” Kiko said. “And thank you, friends.”

The backyard rustled like a happy whisper. Kiko’s heart felt calm, cozy, and proud.

The lesson: All feelings are okay. We can name them, care for our bodies, be kind to the earth, and ask friends for help. That is brave and smart.

The End.
