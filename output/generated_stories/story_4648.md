# Poppy Panda’s Pancake Plan

The warm kitchen smelled like cinnamon and sweet berries. Sunlight slipped across the floor, making golden squares. A patient panda named Poppy stood on a little step stool by the counter. Her soft paws held a wooden spoon. She listened to the gentle sizzle on the stove. Ssssss. It sounded like a tiny song.

“Good morning, team,” Poppy said with a calm smile. “Today, we make pancakes for our friends.”

Her helpers, Chip the chipmunk and Bree the bluebird, clapped their tiny hands and wings. “Yay, pancakes!” they cheered.

Poppy took a deep breath. “We have a plan,” she said. “First, mix. Then, pour. Then, flip. Then, top with fruit.”

Chip hopped up and down. “I want to pour now! All the batter! Fast!”

Bree zipped in the air. “I want to flip first! Flippity-flip!”

Poppy’s ears twitched. The kitchen felt busy and loud. The bowl wobbled in her paws. She felt a flutter in her belly, like little butterflies. She was patient, but she needed to be clear.

Poppy placed the spoon down. Tap. “Friends,” she said in a warm, steady voice, “I hear you. Pouring is fun. Flipping is fun. But we need to be safe. We need to work together.”

Chip pouted. “But I want to do it my way right now.”

Bree squeaked, “Me too!”

Poppy nodded. “I understand. I want to help too. Let’s make a good choice.” She took another deep breath. In. Out. The air smelled like vanilla.

She pointed to a simple chart she drew with a crayon. It was on a big paper by the fridge. “See? First, Chip stirs with me. Then, Bree pours with me. Then, I flip. After that, we all add fruit on top.”

Chip’s whiskers drooped. “But what if I never get to flip?”

“You will get a turn,” Poppy said kindly, looking into his eyes. “Today, I will flip because the pan is hot. That keeps us safe. Next time, we can practice with a toy pan.”

Bree fluttered and landed on the counter. “I can pour slowly,” she said, peeking at the measuring cup. “But what if I spill?”

“If we spill,” Poppy said, “we wipe and try again. That’s part of learning.”

Chip still looked unsure. The batter bowl smelled sweet and wheaty. Poppy felt the butterflies again, but she stood tall on her stool.

She spoke up, clear and calm. “I am the leader today. I choose safe steps. Please follow the plan.”

Chip blinked. Bree tilted her head. The kitchen got quiet. Ssssss went the pan.

“Okay,” Chip said at last. “I can stir first.”

“I can pour second,” Bree added.

Poppy smiled. “Thank you for listening to my words.”

They got to work. Poppy held the bowl steady while Chip stirred. Swirl, swirl, swirl. The batter turned smooth and thick. The spoon made soft plop sounds. Chip giggled. “It looks like a little lake!”

Bree held the measuring cup with Poppy’s help. They poured a circle on the pan. Pssss. A sweet steam floated up. “It smells like warm hugs,” Bree said, closing her eyes.

Poppy watched the tiny bubbles. “We wait for bubbles. Then I flip.”

Chip leaned close but kept his paws behind his back. “I see bubbles!”

“Flip time,” Poppy said. She slid the spatula under the pancake. Her paw was steady. Flip! The pancake landed with a gentle flap. Bree cheered. Chip clapped.

Soon, a stack of pancakes grew on a plate. Golden, soft, and round. Poppy set bowls of blueberries and sliced bananas on the table. The fruit shined like little moons.

“Now it’s topping time,” Poppy said. “We can each choose.”

Chip picked blueberries. “I choose a happy face,” he said, placing two eyes and a big smile.

Bree chose bananas. “I choose a sun,” she chirped, making bright rays.

Poppy added both. “I choose a heart,” she said, shaping it in the middle.

They sat at the table. The chairs made tiny squeaks on the floor. Poppy poured a little maple syrup. Drip, drip, drip. The pancakes were warm and fluffy. They took bites together. Mmm. Sweet, soft, and cozy.

Chip wiped his mouth. “Poppy, I liked your plan,” he said. “Your voice was strong and kind.”

Bree nodded. “You were a good leader. You helped us make safe choices.”

Poppy felt warm in her chest. “Thank you,” she said. “I used my assertive voice. That means I spoke clearly and kindly. I said what we needed, and I listened too.”

Chip smiled. “I learned to wait and follow steps.”

Bree chirped, “I learned to pour slowly and ask for help.”

Poppy patted their hands and wing. “We solved our problem. We wanted to go fast, but fast wasn’t safe. We made a plan. We made good choices together.”

They finished their pancakes. The kitchen smelled sweet and clean. Poppy hung up her apron. “Next time,” she said, “we can practice flipping with a toy pan. We will take turns.”

“Deal!” Chip and Bree said together.

Poppy looked at her friends. “When we speak up with kind words, we can lead, decide, and keep everyone safe.”

They all smiled. It felt like sunshine inside.

The End.
