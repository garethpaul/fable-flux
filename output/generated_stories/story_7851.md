# Koala Kumi’s Cozy Guest Room Adventure

Koala Kumi was calm and cuddly. Her fur felt soft like a warm blanket. One sunny morning, Kumi visited a friend’s house. “Today, you can nap in our guest room,” said Mia, Kumi’s human friend. “The guest room is for everyone who visits. It is a cozy, safe place.”

Kumi stepped into the guest room. The air smelled like clean sheets and a little bit like lavender. The light was gentle and golden. “Mmm,” Kumi hummed, “this feels calm.”

On the dresser sat a basket of surprise items. A note said, “Welcome, friends.” Inside the basket were different things from different places: a tiny rainbow flag, a soft scarf with bright patterns, a little drum, a picture book with many faces, and a small nightlight shaped like a moon.

Mia smiled. “Guests like different things. We share so everyone feels at home.” Kumi’s ears perked up. “Different is good,” she said softly.

Tap-tap! There was a knock at the door. Mia opened it. “This is Ana,” she said. Ana waved shyly. She wore a colorful scarf. “Hola,” said Ana. “That means hello.” Kumi waved back. “Hello, Ana.”

Another knock! “This is Ben,” said Mia. Ben rolled in with a bright blue wheelchair. His smile was wide. “Hi!” Ben said. Kumi gave a gentle nod. “Hi, Ben.”

Soon, a tiny voice said, “Namaste.” It was Priya. She carried a little drum. “This drum goes dum-dum,” Priya giggled. Kumi’s round nose twitched with joy. “Let’s share,” Kumi said. “Everyone can try.”

Mia set some safety rules. “We keep hands gentle. We take turns. We listen to each other. We ask before touching. If we’re not sure, we say, ‘Is this okay?’”

“Is this okay?” Kumi asked, pointing to the drum. “Yes,” said Priya. Kumi tapped softly. Dum-dum. The sound was low and friendly. Ben laughed. “My turn?” he asked. “Yes,” said Kumi, passing the drum carefully. “Hands gentle,” she reminded.

Kumi noticed the scarf. “May I touch?” she asked Ana. “Sí,” said Ana. “Yes.” Kumi rubbed the scarf on her cheek. It felt smooth and cool. “It feels like a river,” Kumi said. Ana grinned. “My abuela made it.”

Ben rolled to the nightlight. “I like the moon,” he said. “It helps me feel safe at night.” Kumi nodded. “I like calm lights too.” Mia turned the nightlight on. The guest room glowed soft and silver. Everyone sighed, “Ahh.”

They opened the picture book. Inside were faces—light skin, dark skin, freckles, glasses, curly hair, straight hair, long hair, short hair. Some people used canes. Some people wore hearing aids. Some people wore headscarves. Some people signed with hands. Kumi traced the pictures with her paw. “We are all different,” she said. “We can all be friends.”

Priya showed how to say hello in sign. She wiggled her fingers and tilted her hand. “Hello,” she signed. Kumi tried it too, slow and careful. “I am learning,” Kumi said. “We all can learn,” said Mia. “When we learn, we grow.”

Ben pointed to the rug. “Let’s make a circle,” he said. They made a circle with space for everyone. Kumi checked. “Ben, do you have room?” “Yes,” Ben smiled. “Thank you for making space.” Kumi felt warm inside. Making space is kind.

They each shared something special:
- Ana shared her scarf’s colors: red, blue, green, gold. “My scarf tells a story,” she said.
- Priya shared a song her family sings: “La la la,” soft and sweet.
- Ben shared a trick with his chair, spinning slowly and safely. “Look,” he said. “I can turn like the moon.”
- Kumi shared her calm breaths. “In… out…” Everyone tried breathing with Kumi. Their bodies felt relaxed.

Suddenly, there was a tiny problem. The drum rolled near the edge of the bed. It could fall. Kumi held up a paw. “Pause,” she said. “Safety first.” She moved slowly. “May I help?” she asked Priya. “Yes,” said Priya. Kumi bent down, kept her back straight, and picked up the drum with two paws. “Safe and sound,” Kumi said. Everyone clapped softly.

Mia nodded. “Great teamwork. You asked. You moved slowly. You kept everyone safe.”

Kumi looked around the guest room. The room smelled like tea now, because Mia brought small cups. The steam felt warm on Kumi’s nose. She listened to gentle music from the moon nightlight speaker. She saw friends with different smiles, different clothes, different ways to move and greet. Her heart felt big and cozy.

“I learned,” Kumi said, “that different is wonderful. We can share. We can learn from each other. We can make space. We can be safe. We can get better at listening and asking.”

Ana nodded. “We are like a rainbow.” Ben added, “All colors belong.” Priya said, “All songs belong.” Kumi hugged her pillow. “All friends belong.”

Mia tucked a blanket around everyone. “Guests are special,” she said. “This room is for all.” Kumi whispered, “Our hearts are guest rooms too. We can make space for everyone.”

They turned off the big light and kept the moon light on. The room glowed. The drum sat still. The scarf lay soft. The book was closed, but the learning stayed open in their minds.

Kumi took a calm breath. “In… out… Different is good. Different is safe. Different makes us better.”

Everyone smiled, cozy and close. The guest room felt like a big, warm hug.

The End.
