# Benny Beaver’s Big Backyard Plan

Benny was a busy beaver with a bright orange apron and a neat little notebook. He lived by a sunny backyard with soft grass, warm breezes, and birds that sang, “Tweet-tweeet!” Bees hummed, flowers smelled sweet, and the sun felt like a cozy hug.

One morning, Benny clapped his paws. “Today is Build-a-Bridge Day!” he said. “I will make a tiny bridge over the little puddle by the garden. Then everyone can cross without muddy toes.”

Benny opened his notebook. “Step one: plan. Step two: gather. Step three: build.” He smiled. “I am prepared!”

He packed sanded sticks, soft twine, and a small hammer. He wore his round yellow hat. “Time to begin!” Benny said. He looked at the clock on the porch. “I will finish before snack time.”

At the puddle, he tapped the ground. “Tap, tap.” He sniffed the air. It smelled like lemons and grass. The water in the puddle sparkled and went, “Plip-plop,” when a breeze made tiny waves.

Just then, Lily Ladybug fluttered down. “Hi, Benny!” she said in a tiny voice. “I love bridges. Can I help?”

Benny smiled kindly. “Thank you, Lily. I planned to work alone today. I have my steps right here.”

Lily tilted her head. “Maybe I can share an idea?”

Benny felt proud of his plan. He hugged his notebook close. “Hmm, I think my way is best,” he said. He put two sticks over the puddle and tied them with twine. He worked fast. “Tick-tock,” he whispered, thinking of the clock. “I must manage my time.”

Along came Max Mole with muddy paws. “Sniff, sniff! Hello, Benny. I can dig tiny holes for the bridge posts,” Max said. “It will be strong.”

Benny shook his head. “No, thank you, Max. I am prepared. I have everything I need.”

Max nodded and sat under a marigold, listening to the bees hum.

Next, Tilly Turtle slowly waddled over. “Good day, dear,” she said softly. “I have a wide shell. I can hold sticks steady.”

Benny took a deep breath. “I have it handled. I want to do it all by myself.”

Tilly smiled kindly. “All right, I’ll watch and cheer.”

Benny worked and worked. He tied knots. He tapped sticks. He wiped a drop of sweat from his nose. The sun felt warmer now. The birds sang louder. But when Benny tried to cross, the bridge wiggled. “Wobble, wobble… crack!” The sticks slipped into the puddle with a splash. “Oh no!”

Benny’s whiskers drooped. His tummy gave a tiny “grumble,” not from hunger, but from worry. He looked at the porch clock. Snack time was getting closer. “I used time well,” he said softly, “but my bridge is not strong.”

Lily fluttered near his ear. “May I share my idea now?”

Benny paused. He looked at his notebook. He looked at the puddle. He felt his heart go thump-thump. He remembered his own lessons: be prepared, be kind, manage time. And then he thought of something new: be open-minded.

Benny took a slow breath. He opened his paws. “Yes, please. I will listen.”

Lily smiled. “Try crossing the sticks, not just side by side. Like an X. It can help them balance.”

Max Mole peeked out. “I can dig tiny holes for two strong posts at the ends.”

Tilly Turtle lifted her chin. “And I can hold the sticks steady while you tie snug knots.”

Benny’s eyes lit up. “What good ideas! Thank you.” He drew a quick new plan in his notebook. “New step: listen and learn.”

They began together. Max dug two neat holes—“scritch-scratch!”—just the right size. Benny and Lily crossed the sticks in an X. Tilly held them steady with her gentle shell. Benny tied the twine in crisscross knots. He tugged each knot. “Snug, snug.”

The air smelled like wet wood and flowers. The bees buzzed a happy tune. Benny checked the porch clock. “We still have time before snack,” he said. “Good time management, team!”

Benny tapped the bridge. “Thump-thump.” It did not wobble. He stepped on it. “No wobble!” he laughed. Lily flew across with a soft “whirr.” Max scurried over with a smile. Tilly took careful steps—“tap… tap… tap”—and crossed with ease.

“We did it!” Benny cheered. He felt warm pride in his chest, like a sunshine glow. He looked at his friends. “Thank you for your help. My plan was good, but your ideas made it better.”

Lily giggled. “You were open-minded, Benny.”

Max nodded. “You were prepared, too.”

Tilly added, “And you watched the time.”

Benny closed his notebook and drew a big star. “Today I learned something important,” he said. “Being open-minded means listening to new ideas. When we listen, we learn. When we work together, we do our best.”

They all sat on the grass and shared crunchy apple slices. The apples went “crunch,” and the juice tasted sweet. The sun felt soft and happy on their faces. The bridge shone in the light, strong and neat.

Benny smiled. “Next time I make a plan, I will leave room for new ideas. I can be prepared, use my time well, and be open to help. That makes work fun and kind.”

Everyone cheered, “Hooray for open minds and helping hands!”

The End.
