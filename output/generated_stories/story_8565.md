# Toby the Turtle Learns to Forgive

In a cozy living room filled with soft pillows and colorful toys, lived a shy little turtle named Toby. His green shell sparkled in the warm sunlight that streamed through the big window. Toby loved to hide inside his shell whenever he felt nervous or scared.

Today was special because his friend Maria was coming to play. Maria's family had just moved from Mexico, and she spoke both Spanish and English. Toby was excited but also very nervous about meeting someone new.

"¡Hola, Toby!" called Maria as she walked into the living room with her mama. Her voice was musical and friendly. She carried a beautiful woven bag made from recycled materials that her abuela had given her.

Toby peeked out from behind a big cushion. "H-hello," he whispered, pulling his head partway into his shell.

Maria sat down on the soft carpet and opened her special bag. "I brought my favorite toys to share!" she said, pulling out wooden animals painted in bright colors. "My papa made these from old wood pieces."

Toby's eyes grew wide with wonder. The toys were beautiful! Slowly, he crawled closer to get a better look.

"This is my favorite," Maria said, holding up a tiny wooden turtle painted with rainbow stripes. "Just like you!"

Toby felt happy and reached out his little green foot to touch the toy. But as he did, his shell bumped into Maria's arm, and the wooden turtle flew across the room. CRACK! It hit the wall and broke into two pieces.

"Oh no!" Toby gasped, quickly pulling his whole body into his shell. His heart felt heavy and sad. "I'm sorry, I'm sorry!" came his muffled voice from inside his shell.

Maria looked at her broken toy, and tears filled her eyes. "My papa made this just for me," she said softly.

Toby felt terrible. He wanted to disappear forever. From inside his shell, he could hear Maria sniffling.

But then something surprising happened. Maria crawled over to where Toby was hiding and gently tapped on his shell.

"Toby," she said kindly, "accidents happen. I know you didn't mean to break it."

Slowly, Toby poked his head out. "But it was your special toy from your papa," he said with tears in his eyes.

Maria smiled and picked up the two pieces of the wooden turtle. "You know what? Now we have two turtle friends instead of one! And maybe your mama can help us glue them back together. In my family, we believe that when something breaks and gets fixed, it becomes even more special."

Toby's eyes lit up. "Really?"

"Sí! Yes!" Maria giggled. "And we can make it even more beautiful. Do you have any paint we could use?"

Soon, Toby and Maria were sitting together on the soft rug, carefully painting the wooden turtle with new colors. They used green like Toby's shell and added golden spots that sparkled in the sunlight.

"Thank you for forgiving me," Toby said, no longer feeling shy.

"That's what friends do," Maria replied, teaching Toby a new word: "Los amigos se perdonan - friends forgive each other."

As they played together with their newly decorated toy, Toby learned that forgiveness makes friendships stronger, just like the glue that made their turtle whole again.

The End.
