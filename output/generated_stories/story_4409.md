# Benny's Green Dream

Benny was a busy beaver with soft brown fur and a big flat tail. He lived in a cozy bedroom with wooden furniture and a warm quilt on the bed. Every morning, Benny loved to build things with his blocks and sticks.

But today, Benny felt worried. He looked out his bedroom window at the trees outside. "Oh no!" he gasped. "The leaves are turning brown and falling down. The trees look sad!"

Benny's whiskers twitched with concern. His little paws pressed against the cool glass window. "I need to help them," he said. "But how can a little beaver like me make the trees happy again?"

Just then, his friend Rosie the rabbit hopped into the bedroom. Her white fur was fluffy and her pink nose wiggled. "Hi Benny! Why do you look so worried?"

"The trees outside look sick, Rosie," Benny said sadly. "Their leaves are falling off. I want to help, but I don't know what to do."

Rosie's ears perked up. "Oh Benny! The leaves are supposed to fall in autumn. It's natural! But I know how we can still help the trees and all the animals."

"Really? How?" Benny's brown eyes sparkled with hope.

"We can make the earth healthier!" Rosie said excitedly. "My mom taught me that eating good foods and taking care of our environment helps everyone - even the trees!"

Benny clapped his paws together. "Tell me more!"

Rosie hopped to Benny's toy box. "First, let's pretend to plant seeds with your blocks. Trees need good soil to grow strong roots." They carefully arranged the wooden blocks like little gardens.

"What else?" Benny asked, his tail slapping the soft carpet with excitement.

"We can eat healthy foods like carrots and apples," Rosie explained. "When we're strong and healthy, we have more energy to help take care of nature!"

Benny nodded eagerly. "And we can save water when we brush our teeth!"

"Yes!" Rosie cheered. "And we can be kind to all the animals - big and small. Even tiny ants and buzzing bees help plants grow!"

Benny felt warm and happy inside. His fur felt shinier and his whiskers stopped twitching. "I understand now! We can help the environment by being healthy and kind."

The two friends spent the afternoon playing "Earth Helpers." They pretended to water plants with a toy watering can that made gentle splashing sounds. They shared pretend healthy snacks and practiced being gentle with Benny's stuffed animals.

"Look, Benny!" Rosie pointed out the window. The autumn trees swayed gently in the breeze, their colorful leaves dancing like confetti. "The trees are beautiful just as they are. And now we know how to help them stay healthy!"

Benny smiled big, showing his white teeth. "Tomorrow, I'll ask my mom if we can plant real seeds in our garden. And I'll eat all my vegetables to stay strong!"

"That's the spirit!" Rosie giggled. "When we take care of ourselves and our world, everything grows better together."

As the sun set through the bedroom window, painting everything in warm golden light, Benny felt proud and peaceful. He learned that even little beavers can make a big difference by eating well, being kind to all living things, and caring for the environment.

The End.
