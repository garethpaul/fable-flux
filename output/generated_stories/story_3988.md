# Ellie's Big Bathroom Problem

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved playing with her colorful blocks and splashing in puddles. But there was one thing she didn't love at all - cleaning up the bathroom after her bath time.

"Ellie, it's time to tidy up!" called Mama Elephant from the kitchen. The warm, steamy bathroom was a mess. Wet towels lay scattered on the tile floor, soap bubbles clung to the bathtub edges, and Ellie's rubber ducky bobbed forgotten in the soapy water.

Ellie's trunk drooped low. "But Mama, it's too much work! Can't you do it?" she called back, her voice echoing off the bathroom walls.

Mama Elephant's gentle footsteps approached. "Oh my," she said, looking at the soggy mess. "This is quite a big job for one little elephant."

Ellie's ears perked up. Maybe Mama would do it all herself! But instead, Mama sat down on the bathroom stool with a thoughtful expression.

"Ellie, what if we made a deal?" Mama asked kindly. "You know how negotiation works - when two people talk together to solve a problem?"

Ellie nodded slowly. She remembered when she and her friend Leo had negotiated about sharing the swing at the playground.

"I have an idea," Ellie said, her trunk swaying as she thought. "What if I clean up the toys and you help with the heavy towels?"

"That's good thinking!" Mama smiled. "But I wonder - what else could we do to make this easier?"

Ellie looked around the steamy bathroom. The mirror was all foggy, and she could smell the sweet lavender soap. Then her eyes lit up like sunshine.

"I know! What if we make it fun? We could sing the cleanup song and pretend we're bathroom superheroes!"

Mama clapped her hands together. "What a resourceful idea! And you know what? When we keep our bathroom clean, we're being responsible members of our family. Everyone feels happy in a tidy space."

"Like civic responsibility?" Ellie asked proudly. She had learned that big word at school - it meant helping make things better for everyone.

"Exactly right!" Mama beamed.

So they made their deal. Ellie would gather all her bath toys and put them in the basket, while Mama would hang up the wet towels. Together, they would wipe down the bathtub.

"Ready, Bathroom Superhero Ellie?" Mama giggled.

"Ready!" Ellie trumpeted softly.

They began to sing: "Clean up, clean up, everybody everywhere! Clean up, clean up, everybody do your share!"

Ellie's trunk worked like a helpful hand, picking up her rubber ducky, her floating boat, and her squeaky fish. The toys made little splashing sounds as she placed them carefully in their basket.

Mama lifted the heavy towels with ease, hanging them on the towel rack where they could dry properly. The soft towels smelled like sunshine and fresh air.

Together, they wiped the bathtub until it sparkled. Ellie felt proud as she watched the soap bubbles swirl down the drain.

"We did it!" Ellie cheered, looking at their clean, fresh-smelling bathroom. "Negotiating made the work easier and more fun!"

"You were so smart to think of sharing the work and making it enjoyable," Mama said, giving Ellie a warm hug. "When we work together and talk about solutions, we can solve any problem."

From that day on, Ellie always remembered that negotiating, being resourceful, and taking responsibility made everything better - especially when you add a little fun and teamwork!

The End.
