# Leo’s Gentle Paws

In a sunny creative art room filled with paper suns and painted rainbows, a brave little lion cub named Leo padded in on soft, golden paws. The room smelled like crayons, fresh glue, and orange slices. Music hummed from a small speaker, a gentle beat that made Leo’s tail swish-swish.

“Good morning, friends,” said Miss Maya, the art teacher, clapping softly. “Today we make art and we dance! We also practice caring for our Earth and for each other.”

Leo’s ears perked up. “I can be brave and gentle,” he said. “I can help the Earth!”

Miss Maya smiled and showed two big bins. One bin had a green leaf sign. The other bin had a blue circle sign. “Green is for paper and clean scraps we can use again,” she said. “Blue is for bottles and safe plastic we can recycle.”

Leo sniffed the bins. They smelled like cardboard and a little bit like paint. “I will remember,” he promised.

The music grew a tiny bit louder. Tap-tap-tap. The children danced while they made art. Leo wiggled his shoulders and did a brave little leap. “Roar—whoosh!” he sang, soft like a purr.

On the big table, there were paint pots, brushes, and sparkly paper. There were also cloth towels for wiping, and a small bucket of soapy water that smelled like lemons. Miss Maya said, “Please use only what you need. Share kindly. Ask before you touch someone else’s things or body. We keep our bodies safe with personal space.”

Leo nodded and chose a small brush. He dipped it gently and painted a big green tree with a happy bird. His friend Tia, a tiny turtle in a flower apron, painted blue waves. “I like your tree,” Tia said.

“Thank you,” said Leo. “I like your ocean.”

Then came the problem.

Max, a bouncy monkey, danced a big, swingy dance. His long tail swirled, and his feet slid. “Ta-da!” Max laughed. But he bumped the table—bump!—and a cup of sparkly paper fell to the floor. Little pieces scattered like stars. A plastic bottle rolled under a chair.

“Oh no,” said Tia, pulling in her shell just a little.

Leo felt his heart thump-thump. He wanted to help. He also wanted to keep dancing. He took a deep breath and remembered: be brave and gentle.

“It’s okay, Max,” Leo said. “Accidents happen. Let’s clean up to help our Earth.”

Max’s ears drooped. “I didn’t mean to,” he whispered.

Miss Maya came closer. “Thank you for telling the truth, Max. We can fix this. Let’s solve it together.”

Leo bent down and picked up the sparkly paper. He looked at the bins. “Hmm,” he said. “Where does this go?” He read the signs. “Paper scraps, green bin!” He shook the papers gently. “But the plastic bottle goes in the blue bin.”

“Yes,” Miss Maya said. “That’s careful sorting. Careful sorting helps our Earth.”

Leo and Max sorted together. Tia peeked out and joined them, pushing the bottle to Leo with a tiny tap. Leo felt proud. His chest felt warm, like sunshine in fur.

When they finished, the floor looked clean again. But another small problem peeked in. The music changed to a drummy beat. Max began to spin close to Leo. Spin-spin! Max’s elbows brushed Leo’s shoulder. It felt pokey.

“Stop,” Leo said, stepping back. His whiskers twitched. “I need space.”

Max froze. “Oh! I forgot,” he said, eyes wide.

Miss Maya nodded. “Thank you for using your words, Leo. That is a personal boundary. We can dance with space.”

Leo placed two paintbrushes on the floor between them. “Let’s make a dance circle,” he said. “We stay outside the brush circle.”

“That’s smart,” Tia said. She placed two more brushes, making a big gentle ring. Everyone could see the circle. It smelled like wood and a tiny bit like paint.

They tried again. Max danced inside the circle with small, careful steps. Leo danced outside the circle, swish-swish, paws light and quiet. Tia clapped a soft beat. Clap, clap.

Miss Maya smiled. “You solved two problems: you cleaned without wasting, and you used boundaries to keep bodies safe.”

Leo had one more idea. He held up an empty box and said, “Let’s make an Earth Helper Box. We put clean scraps here for new art.”

“Ooh,” said Tia. “Then we don’t throw away good pieces.”

“We reduce waste,” said Miss Maya. “That is big Earth care.”

Everyone cheered. Leo wrote “Earth Helper Box” on a bright label. The marker smelled like grapes, and his letters were big and wiggly. He placed the box next to the green bin.

They made Earth art. Leo glued a leaf shape from scrap paper to his tree. Tia made a fish with a shiny scrap tail. Max made a sun from yellow triangles. They used only a little glue and wiped extra with cloth towels. Squish-wipe, squish-wipe. The room stayed neat, and the air smelled fresh.

“Let’s finish with a dance,” said Miss Maya. “A gentle, green dance.”

Leo lifted his paws and moved like wind in the trees. Tia swayed like water. Max bounced like sunshine, but now he watched the circle. No bumps. No spills. Just smiles.

At the end, everyone placed their brushes neatly, lids on paint pots, and scraps in the right bins. Leo patted the Earth Helper Box. “We cared for our art room and our Earth,” he said.

Miss Maya gave a warm nod. “What did we learn today?”

Leo raised his paw. “We sort our trash and use scraps again to help the Earth. We ask for space and say ‘Stop’ to keep bodies safe. We can dance and be careful at the same time.”

Tia added, “Small steps can be big help.”

Max giggled. “And circles are cool.”

Leo felt brave and calm, like a quiet roar inside. He was proud of his gentle paws.

The End.
