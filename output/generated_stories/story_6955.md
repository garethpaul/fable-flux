# Tiko Tortoise and the Band That Learned to Listen

In a bright musical practice room, instruments waited quietly. Drums sat like round red cookies. A shiny triangle hung on a hook. A little piano blinked its tiny lights. The room smelled like clean wood and lemon polish. Sunlight made the floor warm.

Tiko the wise tortoise walked in, slow and steady. His shell was smooth and green like a leaf. He wore a soft blue scarf. Tiko smiled and said, “Hello, friends. Time to make music. We are a team.”

His friends hurried in. Pip the squirrel clutched two drumsticks. “I want to play fast!” Pip chattered.

Bella the bunny held a bell. “I want to ring now!” she said, hopping.

Milo the mouse hugged a small recorder. “I want to try a new song,” he squeaked.

Tiko looked at the big round clock on the wall. “We have time for all,” he said kindly. “We will take turns. We will work together. That is teamwork.”

Pip bounced. “But I feel so wiggly!” he cried. He banged the drum—BOOM BOOM BOOM! The sound was loud like thunder.

Bella covered her ears. “Too loud! I can’t hear myself,” she said, frowning.

Milo squeaked, “I can’t think!”

Tiko closed his eyes and took a slow breath. He felt the warm floor under his feet. He listened to the echo of the drum fade, shhh, like waves going away. He opened his eyes and smiled. “Friends, I hear big feelings. Let’s pause.”

Pip’s tail twitched. “I’m mad,” he said, stomping. “I had to wait all morning.”

Tiko nodded. “Thank you for telling us. Waiting is hard. When we feel mad, we can breathe. In… out… like the wind.” He breathed in, then out. The scarf fluttered. Pip copied. In… out… Pip’s shoulders dropped.

Bella held up her bell. “I want to be heard,” she said softly.

Milo whispered, “Me too.”

Tiko pointed to the clock again. “We can make a plan. A plan helps our time. Teamwork means we all fit in.”

He took three colored cards from a little box: red, yellow, blue. “Red is for Pip on drums. Yellow is for Bella on bell. Blue is for Milo on recorder.” He placed the cards in a neat row. “We will play in this order. Then we will play together.”

Pip squirmed but nodded. “Okay. I can wait for red.”

“Thank you,” said Tiko. “Bella, you will be second. Milo, you will be third. While we wait, we listen. We clap soft to cheer our friend.”

Pip went first. He tapped the drum softly—tap tap tap. Tiko whispered, “Soft like raindrops.” Pip smiled and tapped a gentle beat. Bella and Milo clapped quietly. Clap… clap… clap.

“Now yellow,” said Tiko. Bella rang her bell—ding… ding. The sound was bright like a star. “Lovely,” said Tiko. “Space between rings lets us hear.”

“Blue time,” said Tiko. Milo lifted his recorder and blew a sweet, small note—peep. He looked up. “Is that okay?”

“It is kind,” said Tiko. “Kind to ears.”

The room felt calm. The sun made a golden square on the floor. Dust sparkles danced in the light.

Tiko said, “Now we try a song as a team. Listen and look. We start together. Pip, give us four soft taps.”

Pip counted, “One, two, three, four.” Tap, tap, tap, tap.

Bella rang on two—ding. Milo peeped on three. Tiko kept the beat with a gentle foot tap, tap, tap. The music felt like a friendly walk.

“Wow,” said Bella. “I hear everyone!”

Milo grinned. “We made a pattern!”

Pip’s tail swished happily. “I didn’t have to be the loudest to be heard.”

They played again. This time Pip got excited and sped up—tap-tap-tap-tap! The music bumped into itself. Bella’s ears drooped. Milo squeaked, “Too fast!”

Tiko raised his hand. “Pause.” He breathed in and out. “Pip, your drum is strong. Can your drum share the road?”

Pip blushed. “I forgot. I was excited.” He put his paw on his chest. In… out. “I can slow down.”

Tiko smiled. “That is learning. Try again. Watch my foot. We stay together.”

They tried once more. Pip kept the beat steady like a heart. Bella waited for her turn with a smiling face. Milo watched the foot and played right on time. The sounds fit like puzzle pieces. The music filled the room, warm and bright. It felt like a hug you could hear.

When the song ended, the room was quiet and happy. The clock ticked softly. Pip said, “I am proud. I waited and I listened.”

Bella nodded. “I felt respected. I took my turn.”

Milo said, “I learned to watch and to wait.”

Tiko bowed his head. “We all learned. Teamwork means we listen. We take turns. We make a plan for our time. We help each other when big feelings come.”

Pip put down his sticks. “Thank you for the red card,” he said. “It helped my waiting.”

Bella jingled her bell gently. “Thank you for the plan.”

Milo squeaked, “Thank you for the breathing.”

Tiko wrapped his scarf a little tighter and looked at his friends. “Thank you for being a team. When we work together, our music is kind. Our hearts feel calm. Our friends feel heard.”

They stacked the instruments neatly. Pip counted to ten while they cleaned. Bella folded the cloths. Milo put the cards back in the box. Tiko checked the clock and nodded. “We finished on time.”

“Can we play again tomorrow?” asked Milo.

“Yes,” said Tiko. “And we will bring our listening, our plan, and our big, kind team.”

They all smiled. The practice room felt cozy and bright. The instruments seemed to sparkle with happy rest.

The End.
