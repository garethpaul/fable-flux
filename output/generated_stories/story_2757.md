# Shelly Speaks Up

In a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls and fluffy pillows, lived a wise little tortoise named Shelly. Her shell was green and brown, like the prettiest leaves in autumn. Every morning, warm sunlight streamed through the window, making her cozy corner by the bookshelf sparkle.

Today felt different though. Shelly's tummy made quiet rumbling sounds, and she felt very hungry. She looked at her food bowl – it was completely empty! The ceramic bowl sat there, white and clean, but with no yummy lettuce or carrots inside.

"Oh my," Shelly said softly, touching her empty bowl with her little foot. "I'm so hungry, but there's no food here."

Shelly's friend Max the hamster scurried over from his cage. "Good morning, Shelly! Want to play hide-and-seek?"

"I would love to play," Shelly said honestly, "but I'm really hungry. My food bowl is empty, and I don't know what to do."

Max tilted his tiny head. "Why don't you just wait? Maybe food will appear!"

Shelly thought carefully about this. She was very good at thinking through problems. "Hmm," she said, tapping her shell gently. "I don't think food appears by magic. Someone needs to know I'm hungry so they can help me."

"But what if you bother them?" Max squeaked.

Shelly felt a little scared, but she knew what was right. "It's important to speak up when I need something," she said bravely. "That's called self-advocacy – asking for what I need in a kind way."

Just then, Emma, the little girl who lived in the bedroom, came in wearing her favorite yellow pajamas. Her curly hair bounced as she walked.

Shelly took a deep breath. Speaking up felt scary, but she knew it was the right thing to do. "Emma," she called in her gentle voice. "Could you please help me? I'm very hungry, and my food bowl is empty."

Emma's eyes grew wide. "Oh no, Shelly! I'm so sorry!" She knelt down and gently petted Shelly's shell. "I forgot to feed you yesterday because I was playing with my new puzzle. That wasn't fair to you."

"Thank you for being honest," Shelly said warmly. "I understand that people sometimes forget things. I just needed to let you know so you could help me."

Emma nodded seriously. "You were very brave to tell me. Let me fix this right away!" She hurried to get fresh lettuce, bright orange carrots, and even a special strawberry treat.

As Shelly munched her delicious breakfast, she felt proud and happy. The lettuce was crisp and green, the carrots were sweet and crunchy, and the strawberry was perfectly juicy.

"I learned something important today," Emma said, sitting cross-legged on her soft carpet. "I need to remember to take better care of you. And you taught me that it's good to speak up when we need help."

"And I learned that speaking up for myself isn't scary – it's helpful!" Shelly smiled. "When we're honest about what we need, good things happen."

Max the hamster watched from his cage. "Wow, Shelly! You were so smart to ask for help instead of just waiting."

From that day on, Shelly always remembered to speak up kindly when she needed something. And Emma never forgot to feed her friend again. Their cozy bedroom became an even happier place because they both learned the importance of honest communication and caring for each other.

The End.
