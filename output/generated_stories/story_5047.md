# Benny's Beautiful Birdhouse

Benny the rabbit hopped into the warm kitchen. Sunlight streamed through the window. The smell of fresh bread filled the air. His nose twitched with excitement.

"Today I'm going to make something special!" Benny announced to himself.

Benny had found some wooden pieces in the garage. He wanted to build a birdhouse for his friend Robin. But there was a big problem. The pieces didn't fit together right.

"This is too hard!" Benny said, his ears drooping. He tried pushing the wood together. Nothing worked. His little paws felt tired.

Mama Rabbit hopped into the kitchen. "What's wrong, Benny?" she asked softly.

"I want to make Robin a house," Benny sniffled. "But these pieces won't stick together. It's not fair!"

Mama's whiskers twitched with a smile. "Building takes patience, little one. Let's take a break first."

"A break?" Benny asked, confused.

"Yes! When we feel frustrated, our bodies need to move," Mama explained. "Let's hop around the kitchen ten times!"

Benny felt silly at first. But hopping felt good! His heart pumped faster. His muscles felt warm and wiggly.

"One, two, three!" they counted together as they hopped. "Four, five, six!" Benny giggled as his fluffy tail bounced.

After ten hops, Benny felt much better. His mind felt clearer too.

"Now," said Mama, "let's look at your birdhouse again. But this time, we'll use the right tools."

Mama showed Benny some special wood glue. "This helps pieces stick together," she said. "And this tiny hammer helps tap things into place."

Benny's eyes grew wide. "Real tools? For me?"

"Crafting is important work," Mama nodded. "You deserve the right tools to do it well."

They worked together slowly. Mama held pieces while Benny squeezed drops of glue. The kitchen smelled like pine wood and possibilities.

"Take your time," Mama reminded him when Benny started rushing. "Good crafting can't be hurried."

Benny took deep breaths. He moved his paws carefully. Each piece clicked into place with a satisfying sound.

"It's working!" Benny cheered. His ears perked up with joy.

As they worked, Mama shared a secret. "When I feel frustrated, I like to stretch my arms up high like tree branches," she said, demonstrating. "It helps my feelings flow out."

Benny tried it too. He stretched his arms up, up, up toward the ceiling. Then he wiggled his fingers like leaves in the wind. It felt wonderful!

Soon, the birdhouse was finished. It had four walls, a pointed roof, and a perfect round door.

"We did it!" Benny exclaimed, doing a happy dance around the kitchen table.

Just then, Robin flew to the window. "Tweet, tweet! Is that for me?" she chirped.

"Yes!" Benny called back. "I made it with my own paws! Well, Mama helped with the tricky parts."

Robin's eyes sparkled. "It's the most beautiful house I've ever seen!"

Benny felt proud and warm inside. His chest puffed out with happiness.

"Building things takes practice," Mama said, giving Benny a gentle hug. "When we feel frustrated, we can move our bodies to feel better. Then we can try again with patience."

Benny nodded wisely. "And using the right tools helps too!"

"That's right," Mama smiled. "You showed great determination today."

Benny looked at his finished birdhouse. Tomorrow, he wanted to make something else. Maybe a little table for his toy cars. He knew exactly what to do if he felt frustrated again.

The End.
