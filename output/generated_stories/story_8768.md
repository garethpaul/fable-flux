# Rocco Raccoon’s Bubble Band Breakfast

Rocco the raccoon loved to make music. He had a shiny toothbrush he called Toot, a little cup named Clink, and a comb that went Zing! Rocco liked to practice in the bathroom because it echoed. “Toot, Clink, Zing!” he sang. His tail swished like a soft broom on the floor.

One sunny morning, Rocco padded into the bathroom, humming. He felt wobbly in his tummy. “I want to play a big song,” he said, “but I feel slow like mud.” He plopped on the bath mat and listened. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. His tummy answered, grrrowl, like a little bear.

Mama Raccoon peeked in. She smelled like warm toast and oranges. “Good morning, Rocco,” she said gently. “Your music sounds sleepy today.”

“I’m hungry,” Rocco whispered. “I forgot to get ready for breakfast. I just wanted to play music.”

Mama knelt beside him. “Music is fun,” she said, “but our bodies need fuel, like a drum needs sticks. Let’s prepare your morning, little musician.”

Rocco tilted his head. “Prepare? Like practice?”

“Yes,” Mama smiled. “We practice for music, and we prepare for eating. It helps us feel strong.”

Rocco looked at Toot, his toothbrush. He gave it a small toot-toot. “Can we make it a song?”

Mama laughed. “We can make it a breakfast band!”

Rocco tapped Clink on the sink. “Clink says he wants water,” he said. He turned the faucet on. Water splashed and sparkled. “Shiny!” Rocco giggled.

Mama touched the handle. “Let’s save water while we brush. We can turn it off so it doesn’t drip drip drip all day. That’s called conservation.”

Rocco nodded. “Con-ser-va-tion,” he said carefully. He turned the faucet off. The bathroom grew calm and cozy. He could hear a bird tweet outside.

“Toot time,” Mama said. Rocco brushed with tiny circles. The toothpaste tasted minty and cool, like snowflakes on his tongue. “Swish, swish,” he sang. He spat into the sink. “All clean!”

“Now hands,” Mama said. “We wash before we eat.” Rocco turned the water on, wet his paws, then turned it off while he rubbed, rub, rub. He smelled the soap—lemony and bright. He scrubbed between tiny fingers. “Bubbles!” he cheered. He turned the water on again to rinse. “We saved water,” he said. “Conservation!”

“Exactly,” Mama said. “You’re taking care of the Earth.”

Rocco’s tummy grrrowled again. He patted it gently. “Time to fuel my drum,” he said.

“In the kitchen, we will prepare a balanced breakfast,” Mama said as they walked. Rocco stepped softly on the mat; it felt squishy like clouds. He kept humming, “Toot, Clink, Zing.”

At the table, Mama showed him choices. “Colors help our bodies. We can mix foods. That’s called nutrition,” she explained.

She set out:
- Slices of banana, sunny and soft
- Blueberries, round and sweet
- Oats, warm and cozy
- Milk, cool and white
- Nuts, crunchy like pebbles (for Mama)
- A little honey, golden and sticky

Rocco’s eyes sparkled. “It’s a rainbow for my tummy!” he said.

“Let’s prepare it together,” Mama smiled. “You can wash the berries.”

Rocco filled Clink, his cup, just a little. He swished the blueberries in the water. “Splash, splash,” he whispered. He poured the water into a tiny plant on the windowsill. “No waste,” he said. “Conservation.”

“Great idea,” Mama said. “The plant says thank you.”

Rocco sprinkled berries and banana on his oats. He poured a little milk. “I made a music bowl!” he cheered.

“How does it sound?” Mama asked.

Rocco took a bite. The oats felt warm. The banana was sweet and soft. The milk was cool. His spoon went tap-tap on the bowl. “It sounds like mmmmmm,” he hummed with happy cheeks.

They ate together. Mama added, “Fruit and oats give you energy. Milk helps your bones. This is good nutrition.”

Rocco nodded. He felt the warm food curl through his tummy like a gentle drum beat. “Boom-boom, I feel stronger,” he said.

When he finished, Rocco looked at his little bit of oats left. “I will save this,” he decided. “We can put it in a container for later.” He helped Mama spoon it in. “Preparation,” he said proudly. “Now I am ready for my music.”

“Wait,” Mama said softly. “What comes after we eat?”

Rocco hopped up. “We clean up!” He wiped the table with a small cloth. He rinsed his bowl with a tiny splash and turned off the tap. Drip stopped. The kitchen felt quiet and sunny.

Back in the bathroom, Rocco picked up Toot, Clink, and Zing. He stood tall. He took a deep belly breath. “I prepared my morning. I made good nutrition choices. I conserved water,” he whispered to himself.

Then he played. Toot went toot-toot, Clink went clink-clink, Zing went zing-zaaang. Rocco’s song was bright and bouncy. His tail swished in time. He laughed, a happy glitter sound.

Mama peeked in and swayed. “Your music has energy now,” she said.

Rocco winked. “Breakfast helped my band. Nutrition makes me strong. Preparation gets me ready. Conservation takes care of the Earth.”

He finished with a big clink and a soft toot. He bowed to the mirror. The raccoon in the glass bowed back.

Rocco hugged Mama. “Tomorrow,” he said, “I will prepare again.”

“Tomorrow and every day,” Mama agreed.

The End.
