# Dori Dolphin’s Balcony Band

On a sunny morning, a playful dolphin named Dori visited her friend Mina’s balcony garden. Dori lived in the sea, but today she splashed up a gentle water bubble pathway that swirled right to the railing. The balcony smelled like mint and soil. Tiny wind chimes tinkled. Bees hummed softly over yellow flowers. Tomato leaves brushed against Dori’s smooth, blue-gray side as she peeked in.

“Hi, Mina!” Dori called, clicking and chirping. “Your garden sounds like music.”

Mina grinned. “Hi, Dori! Let’s make a balcony band!”

Dori’s tail swished with joy. “Yes! I can click, sing, and splash.” She flicked the bubble water, making plip-plop notes.

Soon neighbors joined. Rico the robin perched on the railing. “Tweet-twee! I love to sing,” he chirped.

Lulu the ladybug landed on a marigold. “I tap tiny beats with my feet!” tap-tap-tap.

Nani the cat padded over, tail high. “I can purr and shake this bell,” she purred, nudging a small bell with her nose. Jingle-jingle.

Mina clapped. “Let’s play a happy song!”

They began. Rico sang bright and high. Nani jingled and purred. Lulu tapped a tiny beat. Dori sang low, warm whale-like notes—oooo-eee-oooo—blending with the chimes and bees.

Suddenly, two crickets hopped in. “We play louder!” chirped Pip and Pop. They rubbed their legs—cree-cree-cree—very fast and very loud. The sound grew sharp and crowded. Dori’s ears buzzed. Her heart beat quick. The music felt messy. She tried to sing, but Pip and Pop chirped over her.

Rico fluffed his feathers. “I can’t hear my tweet.”

Lulu stopped tapping. “The beat is lost.”

Nani’s ears flattened. “Too noisy,” she murmured.

Dori took a deep breath. The balcony smelled like basil and warm sun. She felt the cool bubble water around her. She wanted to be kind. She wanted to play. But she also needed space to sing. Dori remembered: It’s okay to say what I need. I can be strong and gentle.

She lifted her head. “Pip, Pop,” she said clearly, voice calm and steady, “I like your chirps. They are quick and shiny. But I can’t hear myself. I feel squished.”

Pip blinked. Pop tilted his head. “Squished?” they chirped together.

Dori nodded. “Yes. I need us to take turns so everyone can shine. Please chirp softer, or chirp when it’s your turn.”

Mina smiled and showed a rhythm with her hands: clap, clap, pause. “We can make room in the music,” she said. “Soft, loud, soft. Everyone gets a turn.”

Pip rubbed one leg slowly. Cree. Pop tried a tiny chirp. Cree. It was still sharp, but softer, like a small spark instead of fireworks.

Dori smiled. “Thank you,” she said. “Let’s try a pattern. Rico sings first. Then you chirp. Then I sing. Then Lulu taps. Then Nani jingles.”

Everyone agreed.

They began again.

Rico sang sweet notes that floated like clouds. Tweet-twee-tweet.

Pip and Pop chirped softly, one, then the other. Cree. Cree.

Dori sang a low round note that rolled like a gentle wave. Ooooo.

Lulu tapped steady. Tap-tap.

Nani jingled like silver rain. Jingle-jingle.

The garden seemed to dance. The mint leaves swayed. The bees hummed along. The wind chimes answered, ting-ting.

Dori felt safe and happy. She could hear herself. She could hear her friends. Her chest felt light, not tight.

Then a new neighbor peeked in—Gogo the snail, with a rainbow shell. “I move slow,” Gogo said shyly. “Can I join?”

“Of course!” said Dori. “We celebrate different sounds here.”

“I can slide on a leaf,” Gogo said, making a whispery swoosh. Ssshhh.

Mina grinned. “That’s beautiful.”

They tried a new song. Dori counted softly, “One, two, ready, go.”

Rico sang. Pip chirped. Pop chirped. Dori sang. Lulu tapped. Nani jingled. Gogo added a soft swoosh. Then they all rested. Silence felt like a cozy blanket.

Pip beamed. “Taking turns is fun.”

Pop nodded. “We can hear everybody!”

Dori felt proud. She had spoken up. She had been kind and strong. She had made space for herself and her friends. That was being assertive.

After the song, Mina poured cups of cool cucumber water. Dori splashed a thank-you sprinkle that smelled like the sea. The sun warmed their faces. The balcony glowed green and gold.

Rico said, “Dori, how did you know what to say?”

Dori tapped her fin to her chest. “I listened to my feelings,” she said. “My tummy felt tight. My ears felt tired. That told me I needed something. Then I used a clear, kind voice. I said what I liked and what I needed.”

Nani purred. “That is respect—for you and for us.”

Lulu wiggled. “And we celebrated our different sounds!”

Mina nodded. “We made music with room for everyone.”

They played one more song. It rose like the sun and settled like a hug. When they finished, they all whispered together, “Thank you.”

Dori looked at the wide, bright sky. “I can be playful and brave,” she said softly. “I can say yes. I can say no. I can say, ‘Please make room for me.’”

Her friends smiled and nodded. The garden chimed. The bees hummed. The music stayed in their hearts.

The End.
