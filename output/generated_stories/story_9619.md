# Rabbit Rosie Cleans Her Cozy Room

Rosie was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She loved her cozy bedroom. It had a warm quilt, a tiny bookshelf, and a round window that let in sunshine. The room smelled like fresh hay and sweet soap.

One morning, Rosie woke up and wiggled her nose. “Good morning, room,” she said. She stretched her long ears and listened. Drip, drip. There was a little sound by the window.

Rosie hopped over. A small plant in a clay pot sat on the sill. Its leaves drooped. The soil was dry. “Oh no,” Rosie whispered. “My plant is thirsty. I can help!”

She looked around. Toys were on the floor. Books were open. A soft scarf lay under the bed. Rosie felt a tiny worry in her tummy. The mess made it hard to find things. But she took a deep breath. “I can start,” she said. “I can try.”

She took initiative. She did not wait. “First, I will get water,” said Rosie. She picked up her tiny cup. Drip, drip. She filled it at the sink. She walked carefully, ears straight, paws steady. “I can do this,” she told herself.

Rosie poured slowly. “Drink up, little plant,” she said. The soil turned dark. The plant looked a little happier. Rosie smiled. “I did it!”

She felt proud. She could do more. “Today, I will care for my room,” she said. “I will be kind to my things. I will be kind to the Earth.” She thought about how clean rooms use less energy and keep things safe. “I can help,” she said, nodding.

Rosie started with her toys. “Blocks, you go in your box,” she said. Clack, clack, clack! The sound made her giggle. “Doll, you go on the shelf.” She brushed the doll’s hair. “There you go.”

Rosie found a crinkly paper bag under her chair. “This is trash,” she said. She put it into her small recycle bin. “Bottles, paper, boxes—we can use you again,” she told the bin. The bin made a soft thump. Rosie liked that sound. It sounded like good choices.

Mama Rabbit peeked in. “Good morning, Rosie,” Mama said. “What are you doing, little one?”

“I am taking action,” Rosie said, standing tall. “I am starting by myself.” She pointed to the window. “I watered my plant. I am cleaning my room. I am sorting recycling. I am helping our home.”

Mama’s eyes shone. “That is called initiative,” Mama said. “You saw a need and started. I am proud.”

Rosie’s chest felt warm and happy. She kept going. She picked up the soft scarf and shook it. It smelled like sunshine. She folded it and put it in her drawer. Swish. The drawer slid shut.

Then Rosie saw a big problem: a tower of books, tall and wobbly. “Oh,” she said. “This is tricky.” She put her paws on her hips. “I will be careful.” One by one, she lifted each book and put it on the shelf. The last book was very big.

It slipped! Thump!

Rosie jumped. Her ears drooped for a moment. “That scared me,” she said. She took a deep breath. In, out. “I can try again.” She set her feet. She used both paws. She lifted slowly. Slide. The big book found its place.

Rosie grinned. “I did not give up,” she said. “I am resilient.”

Next, Rosie opened her window a little. Fresh air floated in. It smelled like grass and morning. She dusted her windowsill with a soft cloth. She hummed a tune. “Clean room, clean mind,” she sang. “Kind hands, kind Earth.”

She noticed a tiny puddle of water on the floor from the plant. “Uh-oh,” said Rosie. She could slip. She got a towel and patted the floor. Pat, pat, pat. The floor felt dry and safe.

Her room now looked bright and calm. Rosie felt brave. She had one more idea. She made a little “Care Corner” by the window. She put a small jar for dead batteries, a box for paper scraps, and a basket for cloth to reuse. She drew a sign with a green leaf and a happy face. “This is for the Earth,” she said. “I can help every day.”

Papa Rabbit came to the door. “Rosie!” Papa said. “Your room looks wonderful. Did you clean all by yourself?”

Rosie nodded. “I started, I kept going, and I asked for help when I needed it.” She pointed to the big book. “That part was hard. But I tried again.”

Papa smiled. “That is assertiveness and resilience. You spoke up for what needed to be done, and you didn’t stop when it was tough.”

Rosie’s plant seemed to wave in the breeze. Its leaves looked greener already.

Rosie cuddled on her bed with her quilt. The fabric felt soft and warm. She looked around at her neat shelves and her sunny window. Her heart felt light.

“I learned something,” Rosie said. “When I see a problem, I can start. I can speak up. I can keep trying. I can care for my room and for the Earth.”

Mama and Papa hugged her. “Yes, little rabbit,” Mama said. “That is initiative. That is love. That is you.”

Rosie wiggled her nose and smiled. “Tomorrow,” she said, “I will check my plant again. I will pick up my toys. I will sort my paper. I will help.”

The room was quiet and cozy. The air smelled like clean hay. The sun made a warm spot on the quilt. Rosie closed her eyes, proud and peaceful.

The End.
