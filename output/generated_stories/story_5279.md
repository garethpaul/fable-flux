# Grace the Giraffe's Green Art Day

Grace the giraffe loved making art. Her long neck helped her reach the highest shelves in the colorful art room. Today, she wanted to make something special for her friend Benny the bunny's birthday.

"What should I make?" Grace wondered aloud, tapping her chin with her hoof. She looked around the bright room filled with paints, papers, and sparkly things.

Grace noticed something that made her sad. The trash can was overflowing with crumpled papers, broken crayons, and empty paint tubes. "Oh my," she said softly. "That's a lot of waste."

Mrs. Robin, the art teacher, flew over. "Good morning, Grace! Ready to create something wonderful?"

"Yes, but..." Grace pointed her long neck toward the overflowing trash. "All this waste makes me feel worried about our Earth."

Mrs. Robin's eyes sparkled. "That's very thoughtful of you, Grace. What do you think we could do?"

Grace put on her thinking face. She looked at the broken crayons with their pretty colors still inside. She saw papers that were only drawn on one side. Her big brown eyes lit up with an idea.

"I know!" Grace exclaimed. "Instead of throwing things away, we can use them again!"

Grace carefully picked up the broken crayons with her gentle hooves. "These still have lots of color left," she said, feeling their waxy smoothness. She gathered papers that were clean on one side. They felt crisp and smooth between her hooves.

"That's called recycling and reusing," Mrs. Robin chirped happily. "You're helping take care of our planet!"

Grace began her special project. She peeled the paper off the broken crayons and saw beautiful colors - sunset orange, ocean blue, and grass green. She rubbed the crayons sideways on the clean side of the used papers, making colorful patterns that looked like rainbows dancing.

"This feels so good," Grace said, enjoying the gentle scratching sound the crayons made. "And look how pretty it is!"

Next, Grace found empty yogurt cups in the recycling bin. She washed them until they sparkled clean. The water felt cool and refreshing on her hooves. She turned them into little planters, decorating them with her crayon art.

"These will be perfect for growing flowers!" Grace said excitedly.

Grace filled the cups with soil that smelled rich and earthy. She planted tiny sunflower seeds, pressing them gently into the dark earth. "Grow big and strong," she whispered to the seeds with love.

When Benny arrived for his birthday surprise, his eyes grew wide. "Grace! These are the most beautiful planters I've ever seen!"

"I made them from things that were going to be thrown away," Grace explained proudly. "When the sunflowers grow, they'll clean our air and make our world more beautiful."

Benny hugged Grace's long neck. "You're so smart and kind! You helped our Earth and made me happy too."

Mrs. Robin smiled warmly. "Grace, you showed us that thinking carefully about what we throw away can lead to wonderful creations. You turned trash into treasure!"

Grace felt warm and happy inside. She had learned that being thoughtful about waste could help the Earth and make others smile too.

From that day on, the art room had a special "reuse box" where everyone could find treasures for their next creation.

The End.
