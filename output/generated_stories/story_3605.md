# Joey’s Balcony Art Plan

Joey was a joyful kangaroo joey who loved to bounce. He lived with Mama Roo in a small home with a sunny balcony garden. The balcony smelled like mint and wet soil. Tiny tomato plants stretched up, and a lemon-yellow watering can waited by the door.

One morning, Joey peeked outside and clapped his little paws. “Mama! The balcony looks sleepy,” he said. “Can we make art to wake it up?”

Mama Roo smiled. “Art sounds wonderful,” she said. “But let’s make a plan first.”

“Plan?” Joey tilted his head. He liked to bounce right into fun.

“Yes,” Mama said gently. “Planning helps us think before we start. We can make a list, gather things, and choose a place to work. That way, our art time will be smooth and happy.”

Joey’s ears perked up. “Okay! Let’s plan.”

They sat at the tiny balcony table. A soft breeze tickled Joey’s whiskers and rustled the mint leaves.

Mama said, “Step one: What will we make?”

Joey looked around. He saw the railing, the pots, and a big empty wall. “A garden rainbow!” he said. “And flower friends.”

“Lovely,” Mama said. “Step two: What do we need?”

Joey said, “Colors! Paper! Glue! And… and shiny things!”

“Good,” said Mama. “Step three: Let’s use what we have. We can recycle. That means we give old things a new job.”

Joey clapped. “I like new jobs!”

They gathered supplies. Joey found a cereal box in the recycling bin. He sniffed it. It smelled like crunchy flakes. “Cardboard!” he cheered.

Mama washed a yogurt cup for water. It felt cool in Joey’s paws. They found old magazines with bright pictures, a roll of paper from a paper bag, and a short piece of string. Mama brought out a little box of safe, washable paints and a big paintbrush that felt soft like a cloud.

“Step four,” said Mama. “Set up our art space.”

They spread newspaper on the balcony floor. The paper crinkled and made a happy rustle sound. Mama taped the paper bag to the wall to make a big, brown canvas.

Joey wiggled. “Now we can paint!”

“First,” Mama said kindly, “let’s plan our rainbow. Where will the red go? Where will the blue go?”

Joey pointed. “Red at the top. Blue down here. Green in the middle to match the mint!”

“Great thinking,” Mama said.

Joey dipped the brush into red paint. “Swish, swish,” went the brush. The paint smelled a little like soap. Joey made a big red curve. He giggled. “It looks like a smile!”

Next came orange, then yellow like sunshine on his nose. Green came after, and Joey leaned close to the mint leaves. “Green like you,” he whispered to the plant.

Blue and purple came last. The rainbow looked bright and cozy on the brown paper bag.

“Now the flower friends,” Joey said. He cut circles from the cereal box. The cardboard felt bumpy. He glued magazine petals around each circle. Poppy petals were red from a tomato picture. Daisy petals were white from a cloud photo. He even made a silly flower with dots from a polka-dot dress.

Joey held up the yogurt cup of water. “Splash!” He rinsed the brush and painted stems. “Tall, tall, tall,” he sang softly.

A little breeze blew, and the paper fluttered. Joey frowned. “Oh no, it wiggles.”

Mama nodded. “What can our plan do?”

“More tape!” Joey said. He pressed the corners with gentle paws. “Stick, stick.”

They stepped back. The balcony felt happy now. Colors danced with the leaves. Joey bounced once, twice. Then he stopped and looked at the floor. A small drip of paint was near the mint pot.

Joey’s ears drooped. “I made a mess,” he whispered.

Mama knelt. “That happens. We planned a cleanup, remember?”

“We did!” Joey brightened. He got a cloth and wiped the drip. “Wipe, wipe, all clean.” He put scraps of paper in the recycling bin and washed the brush. The water turned swirly purple. “It looks like grape juice,” Joey said, but he knew not to drink it. He wrinkled his nose and giggled.

“Last step,” Mama said. “Let’s name your art.”

Joey looked at the rainbow and the flowers. He smelled mint and damp paper. He heard a bee buzz by the tomatoes. He felt proud and warm. “Balcony Bloom,” he said.

Mama clapped. “Perfect.”

They hung “Balcony Bloom” on the wall. The sun peeked through and made the colors glow. Joey felt a little thump-thump of happy in his chest.

“Mama,” Joey said, “I like planning. Planning makes art easy and calm.”

Mama hugged him. “Yes. Planning helps us make, fix, and clean. And recycling helps the earth.”

Joey nodded. “I gave the cereal box a new job. It became a flower!”

He bounced once more and then took a deep breath. The balcony smelled like fresh air and paint. He listened to the soft city sounds below. He touched the cool railing and looked at his art again.

“I can plan my next art,” he said. “Maybe a butterfly! First, I will draw the wings, then cut, then glue, then hang, then clean!”

Mama smiled. “That’s a wonderful plan.”

Joey grinned. “Art, planning, planning, and recycling,” he said slowly. “I can do them all.”

“Yes, you can,” said Mama.

Joey waved to the mint plant. “Thank you for the green idea,” he laughed.

He and Mama Roo went inside for a snack, leaving the balcony garden bright and cheerful.

The End.
