# Miko’s Quiet Banana Breath

Miko the courageous monkey loved his closet hideaway. It was cozy and calm. Soft sweaters hugged the walls like sleepy clouds. A flashlight made tiny stars on the ceiling. There was a gentle smell of clean soap and banana peel, because Miko kept a little basket with wipes and a toothbrush inside.

“Hello, hideaway,” Miko whispered each morning. “I’m ready to be brave.”

One sunny afternoon, Miko was building a block tower in the living room. “Tall, tall, tall!” he sang. His little sister, Lulu, bounced nearby, clapping with her tiny hands. “Block! Block!”

Miko smiled. “Yes, Lulu. Don’t touch, please.”

But Lulu wanted to help. Her paw bumped the tower. The blocks trembled. Clack… clack… CRASH!

Miko’s face got hot. His chest felt like a drum, boom-boom, boom-boom. His hands squeezed. “My tower!” he shouted.

Lulu blinked. “Sowwy,” she peeped.

Miko felt a storm inside. His tail flicked. He wanted to roar. He wanted to push the blocks. He wanted to stamp his feet. The storm grew bigger and bigger.

Then Miko remembered his hideaway. He took a big breath that smelled like banana and soap. “I need a calm minute,” he said.

He walked to his closet hideaway. He did not slam the door. He closed it gently. Click.

Inside, it was soft and quiet. The flashlight stars twinkled. Miko curled on a fluffy scarf. He put a paw on his chest. “Boom-boom,” he said softly. “I hear you, heart.”

Miko used his special steps. He had learned them with Mama.

- Step one: Stop and sit.
- Step two: Breathe and count.
- Step three: Talk kind to yourself.

Miko sat. He breathed in through his nose. Sniff. He breathed out through his mouth. Hoooo. He counted, “One… two… three…” He did it again. Sniff. Hoooo. “Four… five… six.”

He looked at his basket. Inside were wipes, a tiny mirror, and a small toothbrush with a yellow banana on it. On the basket was a sticker that said: Calm Body, Calm Voice.

Miko picked up the mirror. His eyebrows were squished. His mouth was tight. “Hello, mad face,” he whispered. “You are big. But I am brave.”

He brushed his teeth to feel fresh. Scritch-scritch, scrub-scrub. Minty foam tickled his tongue. He wiped his hands with a clean wipe. Swish. The cool wipe made him shiver and smile.

“Clean hands help calm hands,” he said. He wiggled his fingers. They felt soft and ready.

“Use your words, not your roar,” Miko told himself. “Respect Lulu. She is little. She wanted to help. I can teach her.”

He took one more deep breath. Sniff. Hoooo. Then he stood up tall. His tail made a brave curl. He opened the closet door. Click.

The living room was quiet. Lulu sat by the blocks, holding one like a lonely boat. Her eyes were shiny. “Miko?” she whispered.

Miko walked over slowly. He knelt so his eyes were kind. “Lulu, I felt very mad,” he said in a calm voice. “My tower fell. I needed a calm minute in my closet.”

Lulu nodded. “Sowwy, Miko. I help?” She held up the block.

Miko smiled. “Thank you for saying sorry. I respect your helping heart. Let’s build together. But first, we use gentle hands, and we ask.”

“Ask!” Lulu chirped. “May I help?”

“Yes,” said Miko. “You may help.”

They began again. Tap, place, tap. The blocks felt smooth and cool. The tower whispered, “Click, click,” as it grew. Miko showed Lulu to place one block at a time. “Soft hands,” he said. “Slow hands.”

“Soft… slow,” Lulu repeated. Her tongue poked out with careful work.

When they finished, the tower was even taller. It had a doorway and a tiny bridge. Sunshine warmed their fur. Mama peeked in. “Oh my! What a fine tower,” she said. “I heard a loud crash before. What happened, Miko?”

Miko took a breath. “I felt angry. My belly was hot. I wanted to roar. But I went to my closet hideaway. I did my calm steps. I used my words. I respected Lulu’s feelings. We built again. We helped each other.”

Mama’s eyes softened. “That was very brave. That is anger-management,” she said. “You noticed your feelings. You chose a safe, kind plan.”

Miko grinned. “My calm steps help my heart. My clean hands help my calm.”

Mama hugged both monkeys. “I am proud of you for helping and respecting each other.”

Lulu sniffed Miko’s breath and giggled. “Banana mint!” she cheered.

Miko laughed. “Yes! Banana mint bravery!”

They played a game. Lulu asked, “May I place this block?” Miko answered, “Yes, please,” or “Wait, please,” in a gentle voice. When they finished, they cleaned up together. Clatter, clatter, into the bin. Miko sang, “Clean up shows respect,” and Lulu chimed, “Help, help, help!”

Before snack time, Miko and Lulu washed their hands with warm water and soapy bubbles. Splish, splash, squish. The bubbles smelled like oranges. Their hands were shiny and clean.

Miko looked at Lulu. “Thank you for helping,” he said.

“Thank you for calm,” Lulu said back.

Miko touched his chest. Boom-boom, now slow and steady. He felt proud. He felt kind. He felt strong.

He thought of his hideaway, with its soft sweaters and tiny stars. “When my feelings get big,” Miko said, “I can stop, breathe, and choose kind.”

Lulu nodded. “Me too.”

The End.
