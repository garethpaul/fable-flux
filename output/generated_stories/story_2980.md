# Bubble's Bedtime Beat

In a cozy bedroom by the sea, a playful dolphin named Bubble peeped in through the open window. The moonlight made the pillows look like soft clouds. The room smelled like clean sheets and lavender. A little night lamp glowed warm and yellow. It was quiet—almost.

“Click,” went a toy car.
“Tick-tock,” went the clock.
“Drip… drip,” went a leaky sink somewhere down the hall.

Bubble wiggled his tail and whispered, “Hello, room! I came to play a soft song and help with bedtime.”

Bubble loved music. He loved to make little tunes with his blowhole. He could hum, whistle, and make gentle waves of sound. Tonight, he wanted to help the room feel calm. But the little sounds kept jumping in.

“Hmm,” Bubble hummed. “The noises are not together. They bump and bump. That makes the room feel bouncy, not sleepy.”

He closed his eyes and took a slow breath in. He let the breath out like a tiny bubble. “Puh…puh…puh.” He remembered his calm steps: Pause, Notice, Think, Try.

“First, I pause,” he said softly.
He sat on the windowsill and felt the cool night air on his smooth skin. He put his flippers on his belly and felt it go up and down. In… out. In… out.

“Next, I notice,” Bubble whispered.
He listened with his dolphin ears.
“Click.” A toy car on the rug.
“Tick-tock.” The clock on the wall.
“Drip… drip.” The sink down the hall.
“And me,” he smiled. “Soft hum.”

“Now, I think,” he said. “How can I make these sounds into a bedtime song?”

Bubble wiggled his tail and spoke to the room. “Hello, clock. Can you be the beat?”

“Tick-tock, tick-tock,” the clock replied, steady and kind.

“Hello, toy car. Can you rest for a bit? Or can you roll slow, like a whisper?”

The toy car seemed to listen. It rolled one tiny inch and went “click…,” then stopped. It sat still and shiny in the moonlight.

“Hello, little drip,” Bubble called softly toward the hall. “Can you be a soft note? Drip on the beat, please.”

“Drip… (pause) drip… (pause),” came the gentle answer, almost like a raindrop bowing.

Bubble smiled. “I have my band! Now I try.”

He lifted one flipper like a conductor. “One, two,” he breathed. “Slow and low.”

The clock kept the beat. Tick… tock… tick… tock…
The sink added a tiny note. Drip… (wait) drip… (wait)
Bubble hummed a sleepy tune. “Loo-loo-loo,” he sang, warm and round, like a blanket.

He swayed a little. The curtains swished. The night lamp made a soft circle of light. The room felt cozy, like a hug. Bubble’s hum got softer. The car stayed quiet, listening.

But then—squeak! The bed springs made a loud sound. Bubble blinked. “Oh!” he said. “A surprise sound.”

He paused again. He put a flipper to his belly. In… out. “I notice,” he said. “A squeak wants to join.”

He thought, “The squeak is sharp. Where can it fit?” He tapped his flipper lightly on the windowsill. Tap… (wait) tap… (wait). “Maybe the squeak can come only once in a while,” Bubble decided. “Like a tiny star sound.”

“Hello, bed,” Bubble whispered. “Can the squeak be soft and rare? Just a little peep, every four ticks?”

The springs settled. Then, after four gentle ticks: peep. It was small and sweet, like a mouse kiss.

“Perfect,” Bubble said, grinning. “Now we try again.”

Tick… tock… drip… loo-loo-loo… (wait) peep.
Tick… tock… drip… loo-loo-loo… (wait) peep.

The room felt peaceful. The air smelled like sleepy tea. Even the moon seemed to listen.

From the hallway, a grown-up voice whispered, “What a calm song.” Footsteps slowed. A door clicked softly. The house took a deep breath with Bubble.

Bubble finished the song with a long, low hum. “Looooo,” he sang, letting it fade like a wave on the shore.

He looked around and smiled. “We had a problem,” he said gently. “Too many jumpy sounds. We used our minds and our breaths. We made a plan. We turned noise into music.”

The toy car twinkled in the lamp light. The clock kept time, steady and proud. The sink dripped politely. The bed gave one last tiny peep, then settled into stillness.

Bubble yawned a small dolphin yawn. “Time for sleep,” he whispered. “Remember: Pause. Notice. Think. Try. That is how we solve little puzzles. We can be calm and smart.”

He slid from the windowsill. He left one soft note behind, like a lullaby ribbon hanging in the air. “Good night, cozy bedroom. Good night, little sounds.”

Outside, the waves shushed the sand. Shhh… shhh… shhh…

Inside, everything was quiet, warm, and happy. The clock said, “Tick-tock,” like a heartbeat. The room rested.

Bubble swished his tail and dove back toward the moonlit sea, feeling proud and peaceful.

And the lesson floated like a feather: When things feel busy or loud, we can breathe, listen, think of a plan, and try. We can make calm choices. We can make music out of life.

The End.
