# Gigi the Thoughtful Giraffe Solves the Color Puzzle

Gigi the giraffe stepped into the creative art room. She ducked her long neck under a paper lantern. It was bright and warm inside. The room smelled like fresh paint and orange peels. Soft music hummed like a friendly bee. Paints and papers made a rainbow on the tables. Brushes waited in cups like tiny trees.

Gigi’s hooves clicked on the floor. “I am ready to make art,” she said in her gentle voice. Her big eyes blinked with ideas. Today was Art Share Day. Children and animals from many places would hang their art on the long wall. A sign said, “Welcome, friends!” in many languages. Gigi read the words slowly, then smiled.

But a problem sat in the middle of the room.

A giant poster lay across the table. It showed a world of circles and squares. There were shapes for flags, patterns, and kites. Each shape had a little number. There were bowls of paper cutouts too—red circles, blue triangles, yellow diamonds, green squares. A small note said, “Match the shapes to tell our art story.”

Gigi tilted her head. “We need to match the right shapes to the right spots,” she said. “Then we can hang the poster for everyone.”

Her friend Mei the mouse squeaked, “I tried! I put a red circle in spot number three. But it fell off. I think I chose wrong.”

Samir the squirrel flicked his tail. “I like fast,” he said. He grabbed a handful of shapes and stuck them everywhere. The glue smelled sweet and sticky. The shapes slid and did not fit.

Gigi took a slow breath. She watched. She listened. She looked at the note again. “Hmm,” she said. “Let’s think step by step.”

Gigi tapped the paper with the tip of her hoof. “First, we read the clues.” She lifted the note. It had simple pictures and numbers.

“Look,” Gigi said. “Number one shows a sun and a kite. The picture key says: For the sun, use yellow. For the kite, use a diamond shape. So spot number one needs a yellow diamond.”

Mei’s whiskers twitched. “Oh! The key is like a helper map.”

“Yes,” said Gigi. “We can follow the map.”

Gigi, Mei, and Samir gathered around. They spoke softly and took turns. The brushes clinked in their cups. The paper felt smooth and cool. Outside, a bird chirped, “Cheep, cheep,” like it was cheering.

Gigi made a plan. “We will do three steps,” she said.
- Step one: Look at the number.
- Step two: Check the picture key.
- Step three: Pick the shape and color that match.

Samir bounced. “I can be the finder!” He ran to the bowls. “Yellow diamond, coming right up!”

Mei held the glue. “Dot, dot, not a lot,” she sang, pressing gently. The glue smelled like mint sugar.

Gigi placed the yellow diamond in spot number one. It fit perfectly. “Click,” went the sound in Gigi’s head when things felt right. She smiled.

They went on. At spot number two, the key showed a drum from West Africa. It said: Brown circle. “We do not have brown,” Mei said, worried.

Gigi looked around. She sniffed the air. Paint jars lined the shelf—red, yellow, blue, and white. “We can make brown,” Gigi said. “We can mix.”

“How?” asked Samir, eyes wide.

“Let’s try,” said Gigi. She put a drop of red, a drop of yellow, and a drop of blue on a palette. She mixed with a brush. Swish, swish. The colors swirled like chocolate milk. “Brown!” she said with joy.

Mei cut a circle from plain paper. Gigi painted it brown. The brush tickled the paper. The paint looked shiny and smooth. They let it dry, then placed it on spot number two. Perfect.

At spot number three, the key showed a pattern from India, a rangoli shape: a purple flower with a green square in the center. “We can make that,” said Mei. She cut a flower. Samir found a green square. Gigi dabbed purple paint on the flower. It smelled like grapes.

As they worked, friends peeked in. A penguin with a scarf said, “In my home, we dance in the snow.” He pointed to a blue triangle for a mountain shape. A turtle with a bright hat said, “In my home, we sing near the sea.” She passed a shiny blue circle for a drum. Everyone smiled and shared.

When they reached spot number seven, they paused. The key showed a lantern from China, red with a yellow star. “Red oval, yellow star,” Gigi read. Mei cut, Samir found, Gigi placed. They did not rush. They checked each step. If it did not fit, they tried again.

At last, the poster was full. It showed a sunny kite, a brown drum, a purple flower, mountain shapes, a bright lantern, and more. The art told a story of many homes, many colors, many songs.

Gigi wiped a tiny spot of paint from her nose. “We did it,” she said softly.

“How did we do it?” asked Samir.

Gigi smiled. “We used our minds. We took small steps. We looked for clues. We practiced. We did not give up.”

Mei nodded. “And we listened to each other.”

They hung the poster on the long wall. The paper rustled like leaves. Everyone clapped. The room glowed with color. The music felt warm like a hug.

Gigi’s heart felt tall and calm. She said, “When we use logical thinking and practice, we can solve problems. When we work with friends from many places, we make something beautiful together.”

The End.
