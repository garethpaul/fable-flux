# Panda Poppy’s Pantry Plan

Poppy was a patient panda with soft, black-and-white fur and gentle eyes. One sunny morning, she padded into the pantry. The pantry smelled like oats and oranges. It was cool and quiet. Shelves stood tall like trees, full of boxes, cans, and baskets.

Poppy’s tummy rumbled. “Rrr-rrr,” it said.

Poppy smiled. “Hello, tummy. We will choose a healthy snack,” she whispered.

She looked at the shelves. There were crunchy carrots, a jar of honey, a box of cookies, a bowl of apples, and a bag of nuts. There was also a note on the door that said: “Choose foods that help you grow strong.”

Poppy blinked. “So many choices,” she said. “I need to think.”

Poppy liked to be patient. She took a deep breath. In… and out… She touched the smooth red apples. She smelled the sweet honey. She felt the rough carrot tops. Her paws were cool on the pantry floor.

Just then, her friend Benny the bluebird fluttered in. “Hi, Poppy! What are you doing?” chirped Benny.

“I am choosing a snack,” said Poppy. “I want a snack that helps me grow strong. I want to make a good choice.”

Benny hopped on a jar. “Let’s look! Apples are crunchy and juicy. Carrots are crunchy and sweet. Cookies taste yummy, but too many cookies can make us feel slow.”

Poppy nodded. “I like apples. I like carrots too. Hmm.”

Benny tilted his head. “Maybe we can make a snack mix!”

Poppy smiled. “Yes! A rainbow snack mix. Apples, carrots, and nuts.”

Benny flapped. “Tweet! Good idea.”

Poppy took out a small bowl. She washed her paws with warm, soapy water. Splash, splash. “Clean paws for clean food,” she said. Benny sang a little clean-paws song.

Poppy cut the apples into small squares. Chop, chop. She cut the carrots into little circles. Snip, snip. She added a few nuts for crunch. Pop, pop. She looked at the honey. “Just a tiny drizzle,” she said. Drip, drip.

“Smells good,” Benny chirped. “It looks like orange, red, and brown confetti.”

Poppy took a taste. Crunch! Crunch! “Mmm,” she said. “Sweet apple, crisp carrot, crunchy nuts. My tummy feels happy.”

Benny pecked a tiny carrot circle. “Tweet! Healthy and tasty.”

Just then, they heard shuffling in the hallway. Three little neighbors peered in: Mimi the mouse, Gus the goat, and Dot the duckling.

Mimi squeaked, “We are hungry. Can you help us choose a snack?”

Poppy’s heart felt warm. She loved to help. “Yes,” she said. “We are learning about healthy choices. Come in.”

Dot waddled in and sniffed. “It smells sweet.”

Gus licked his lips. “I see cookies!”

Poppy smiled gently. “Cookies are a sometimes treat. Today, we want foods that help our bodies grow strong and feel good.”

Mimi asked, “How do we choose?”

Poppy tapped her chin. “We can use a plan. Let’s write it down.”

Poppy loved to write. She took a big paper and a bright blue crayon from a pantry drawer. She printed big, round letters.

“Our Snack Plan,” she read slowly. “One—Pick something fresh, like fruit or veggies. Two—Add a little protein, like nuts or yogurt. Three—Add a tiny treat, like a small drizzle of honey. Four—Drink water.”

Benny cheered. “A plan! A plan!”

Poppy stuck the paper on the pantry door. Mimi traced the letters with her tiny paw. “I can see the words!”

“Let’s try the plan,” said Poppy. “Who wants apples?”

“I do!” quacked Dot.

“Who wants carrots?” asked Poppy.

“I do!” bleated Gus.

Mimi squeaked, “Can I have both?”

“Yes,” said Poppy. “Good decision.”

They washed their paws and beaks. Splash, splash. They scooped apples, carrots, and a few nuts into bowls. Poppy poured cups of cool water. Glug, glug.

Gus looked at the cookies again. “Can I have a cookie too?”

Poppy nodded kindly. “We can have one small cookie after our healthy snack. That is our plan.”

They ate together. Crunch, crunch. Sip, sip. Smiles grew on their faces. Poppy felt proud. She had waited, thought, and chosen. She had helped her friends too.

When they were done, Poppy picked up the blue crayon again. She drew stars next to the steps. “We did it!”

Benny added a happy note at the bottom. “Good choices make strong bodies.”

Mimi clapped. “I feel good!”

Gus patted his tummy. “I feel full and strong.”

Dot flapped her wings. “I learned to use a plan.”

Poppy hugged the crayon. “When we are patient and think, we can make good choices. Writing our plan helped us remember.”

They cleaned up the bowls. They put the nuts away, the apples back in the basket, and the carrots in the crisper bin. The pantry looked neat again. It smelled like apples and a tiny bit of honey.

Poppy looked at the door. The blue letters smiled back.

“Our Snack Plan,” she read. “We can use it every day.”

Everyone nodded.

Poppy took a small cookie. Crunch. “A sometimes treat,” she said. “And many healthy foods every day.”

Benny sang, “Strong, smart, and kind!”

Poppy’s gentle eyes shone. “Patience, good choices, and helping others make our hearts happy.”

The End.
