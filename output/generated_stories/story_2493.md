# Miri Mouse and the Big Brave Brush

Miri was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She lived near a colorful creative art room. The art room smelled like paint and glue. It sounded like gentle music and happy voices. Today, Miri peeked in and whispered, “Hello, colors.”

Inside, tables were covered with paper, crayons, and glitter that sparkled like stars. Paints sat in little cups—red, blue, yellow, and green. Brushes stood like tiny trees. Miri’s whiskers twitched. She loved to watch art. But today, Miri wanted to try art herself.

She took a brave breath. “I can do it,” she said softly. Courage felt warm in her chest, like a cozy blanket.

Miri climbed onto a little stool. She held a small brush. It felt tickly in her paw. A big, friendly rabbit named Rumi hopped over. “Hi, Miri! Want to paint with me?” he asked.

“Yes, please,” Miri squeaked. Her tail curled with joy.

They dipped their brushes. Plip, plop, went the paint. Miri made small circles. Rumi made big swirls. The paper looked like a rainbow cloud.

Just then, a squirrel named Sola zipped by. “I like fast painting!” Sola chirped. She splashed paint, swish-swish-swish! Drops landed near Miri’s paws.

Miri scooted back. “I need a little space, please,” she said, her voice small but brave. “I don’t like paint on my paws.”

Sola stopped and blinked. “Oh! Thanks for telling me,” she said. “I will try to slow down.” Sola smiled and moved to a new paper. Miri felt proud. She used her brave voice, and it was kind.

A new friend rolled in—Tiko the turtle. Tiko wore orange headphones. He liked quiet music. He painted slowly, one careful dot at a time. Dot. Dot. Dot. “I paint slow,” Tiko said. “Is slow okay?”

Miri nodded. “Slow is great. We all paint our own way.” She felt happy to celebrate the differences.

Rumi peeked at Miri’s paper. “Wow, tiny circles! They look like bubbles,” he said.

Miri smiled. “Thank you,” she said. Her heart felt bubbly, too.

Then the teacher, Ms. Sun, clapped softly. “Friends, let’s make one big art together! A giant mural!” She rolled out a long, long paper, as long as a train. It smelled like fresh paper and sounded crinkly.

“Let’s paint a garden,” Ms. Sun said. “Flowers, bugs, and a big bright sky.”

Miri’s ears tickled. A big mural looked big and new. She felt a flutter in her tummy. “I feel a little scared,” she whispered.

Ms. Sun knelt down. “Being scared is okay,” she said gently. “Courage is trying, even when you feel a little scared.”

Miri held her brush tight, but not too tight. She took one brave breath. In went the brush—dip. Across went the brush—swish. She painted one small flower. It was pink with a yellow dot. “I did it,” she said, surprised and proud.

Rumi painted a huge sunflower. Sola painted quick orange tulips, but she kept her splashes on her paper. Tiko dotted tiny blue berries near the grass. The room smelled like lemon cleaner and sweet paint. The music hummed softly, la-la-la.

A raccoon named Rio walked up. “Can I add a rainbow?” he asked.

“Yes!” Miri said. “Rainbows make gardens happy.” Rio painted a rainbow with gentle curves.

Soon the mural grew and grew. There were tall trees, small bugs, and a big friendly sun. Miri added little silver stars, even in the daytime. “Sometimes stars shine in our hearts,” she explained.

Then Miri saw a spot on the mural that was empty and high. “I want to paint up there,” she said. But the stool was tall, and tall felt tricky. Her paws felt wobbly.

“You can do it,” Rumi said kindly. “Do you want help?”

Miri nodded. “Yes, please. But please don’t touch my tail,” she said. “I don’t like that.” Her voice was calm and clear.

“Thank you for telling me,” Rumi said. He held the stool steady, gentle and careful. Miri climbed up. She took another brave breath. She painted a bright kite with a long wiggly ribbon. The ribbon danced across the sky. “Whee!” Miri giggled.

Everyone cheered. Clap, clap, clap! The room felt warm with happy feelings.

Ms. Sun smiled. “Look at our art,” she said. “All different. All beautiful.”

Miri looked at the mural and felt her courage grow. Her flower was small, her kite was high, and her voice was strong. She was proud of her kind heart, her brave breath, and her careful words.

Ms. Sun put a shiny sticker on Miri’s paw. “You showed courage today,” she said. “You tried something new. You used your voice to share your needs. You helped friends make art together.”

Miri nodded. “Courage is trying,” she said. “Courage is kind. And everyone can make art in their own way.”

They cleaned up with soapy water. Swish, swash, wipe. The room smelled fresh again.

The mural dried on the wall, bright and beautiful. Miri waved her tail. “Good job, team!” she called. Her friends laughed and waved back.

That night, Miri curled up cozy. She could still see the kite in her mind. It floated in her dreams, high and happy.

The lesson was clear in Miri’s heart: Be brave and try. Use kind words to share your space. Celebrate how we are all different. Together, we make something wonderful.

The End.
