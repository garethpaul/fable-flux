# Leo's Big Bedtime Problem

Leo was a brave little lion cub with golden fur as soft as sunshine. He lived in the coziest bedroom you could imagine, with fluffy blue pillows, a warm quilted blanket, and stuffed animals lined up on his windowsill. But tonight, Leo had a big problem.

"I can't sleep!" Leo whispered to his favorite teddy bear, Mr. Buttons. The room felt too dark, and strange shadows danced on the walls. Every little sound made Leo's ears twitch - the wind whooshing outside, the house creaking, and even his own heartbeat seemed too loud.

Leo tried closing his eyes tight. "One sheep, two sheep, three sheep," he counted, but his mind kept wandering to the scary shadows. He pulled his soft blanket over his head, but it felt too stuffy underneath.

"Maybe I'm not brave enough," Leo sighed, feeling his confidence shrink like a deflating balloon.

Just then, Mama Lion peeked her head through the doorway. Her warm amber eyes sparkled in the gentle lamplight. "What's wrong, my little cub?" she asked softly, padding across the fluffy carpet.

"I can't fall asleep, Mama," Leo admitted, his voice small. "The shadows look scary, and I keep hearing noises. Maybe I'm not as brave as I thought."

Mama Lion sat on the edge of Leo's bed, making the mattress dip comfortably. "Oh, sweetheart, being brave doesn't mean you're never scared. It means you find smart ways to handle your fears. Let's think of a strategy together."

"What's a strategy?" Leo asked, tilting his fuzzy head.

"A strategy is like a plan - a smart way to solve a problem," Mama explained, gently stroking Leo's mane. "Let's look at those shadows that seem scary. What do you think is really making them?"

Leo peeked over his blanket. "Um... maybe the lamp and my toys?"

"Exactly!" Mama smiled. "Let's investigate together." They walked around the room, and Leo discovered that his toy giraffe was casting a long shadow that looked like a monster, and his hanging mobile created dancing shapes on the wall.

"They're just my toys!" Leo giggled, feeling much better. "But what about all the sounds?"

"Let's listen carefully and figure out each one," Mama suggested. Together, they identified the wind in the trees outside, the old house settling, and the gentle hum of the heater keeping them warm.

"Now I understand!" Leo said proudly. "Those aren't scary sounds - they're just normal nighttime sounds!"

Mama Lion hugged him tight. "You used your brain to solve the problem, Leo. That's what makes you truly brave - not the absence of fear, but using smart thinking to understand and overcome it."

Leo felt his confidence growing warm in his chest like hot cocoa on a cold day. "Can we make a bedtime strategy for tomorrow night too?"

"Of course!" Mama said. "We can leave the hallway light on so there aren't as many shadows, and we can remember what each sound really is."

Leo snuggled into his cozy bed, pulling his soft blanket up to his chin. "I feel much braver now, Mama. Thank you for helping me understand."

"Sweet dreams, my clever little strategist," Mama whispered, giving him one last gentle kiss on his furry forehead.

As Leo drifted off to sleep, he felt proud and confident. He had learned that being brave meant using his mind to understand problems and find solutions. The shadows were just toys, the sounds were just the house saying goodnight, and he was indeed a very brave little lion cub.

The End.
