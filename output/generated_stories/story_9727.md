# Buzzy Beaver and the Whirly Wash Day

Buzzy the busy beaver loved to help. He lived with his family near a bright, cozy laundry room. It smelled like warm towels and lemon soap. The floor felt cool on his paws, and the washing machine hummed like a sleepy bumblebee.

“Today I will wash all the clothes!” Buzzy said, puffing his chest. “I will be fast. I will be neat. I will save water, too!”

Mama Beaver smiled. “Slow and steady, Buzzy. We take care of clothes, and we take care of Earth.”

Buzzy nodded. He opened the laundry basket. “Socks, shirts, and towels!” He giggled when a tiny sock landed on his nose. “Boop!”

“First, we sort,” Mama said. “Lights, darks, and towels. Organization helps everyone.”

Buzzy made three neat piles. He felt proud. The clothes were soft and cool, like clouds and little rivers. “I like making lines,” Buzzy said. “It makes my head feel calm.”

He checked the washing machine. “We wait until the load is full,” he said, remembering. “That saves water.” He peered inside. “Almost full!”

Just then, his friend, Raccoon Riley, tip-toed in with a big, dirty cape. “My superhero cape is muddy,” Riley said. “I need it now!”

Buzzy looked at the machine. It wasn’t full yet. “We should wait, Riley. A full load saves water,” he said gently.

Riley crossed his arms. “But I want it now. Now, now, now!” His tail twitched. His eyes got hot and shiny.

Buzzy felt his tummy twist. He wanted to help quickly. He also wanted to do the right thing. His cheeks felt hot. “I feel mad, too,” he whispered to himself. “I want to be fast.” He put a paw on his chest and took a slow breath in. He let a slow breath out. “In like soup. Out like soup,” he said softly.

Mama Beaver nodded. “Good breathing, Buzzy. Anger can sit with us, but it does not drive the washing machine.”

Buzzy turned to Riley. “Let’s be fair,” he said. “Justice means we think about everyone. We think about you. We think about Earth. We think about the water in the river where fish live.”

Riley sniffed. “But my cape…”

Buzzy had an idea. “We can find more dirty clothes to make a full load! Then your cape gets clean, and we save water.”

Riley blinked. “A teamwork plan?”

“A teamwork plan!” Buzzy said.

They looked around the room. “Check the hamper!” Buzzy called.

Riley opened the lid. “Two shirts and a towel!” he cheered.

Buzzy wiggled his tail. “I will find socks.” He checked under the bench. “Aha! Sneaky socks!”

They added the clothes. The machine was full and ready. “Good organization,” Mama said. “Now, just enough soap. Too much soap is wasteful.”

Buzzy held the soap cup. He poured slowly. “One… two… stop.” He smiled. “We used just enough.”

The machine started. Whirr, swish, whirr. The sound was like rain on leaves. Buzzy and Riley listened. They felt calmer.

Riley tugged Buzzy’s paw. “I felt angry,” Riley said. “My tail got all spiky. I didn’t like waiting.”

Buzzy nodded. “I felt squishy-angry, too. I breathed soup breaths. That helped. And we made a fair plan.”

Riley smiled a little. “Thank you for being fair.”

When the wash was done, warm steam puffed out. It smelled like sunshine. Buzzy carefully moved the clothes to the dryer. “Heavy towels on the bottom, shirts on top,” he said. “Organization helps them dry better.”

Mama held up a drying rack. “We can hang some shirts to air-dry. That saves energy. The dryer is for the heavy towels.”

Buzzy clapped. “Energy saving!”

They hung the shirts on the rack. Drip, drip, plink, plink. The drops made tiny tapping sounds.

Riley held his cape. “Can I hang my cape, too?”

“Yes,” Buzzy said. “It will flap like a flag.”

The cape swished as it hung. Riley watched it swing. “I like waiting now,” he said. “I can see the drips turn into nothing.”

They tidied the room while they waited. Buzzy folded a towel. Fold, pat, smooth. He placed it on a shelf. Riley lined up the soap and the basket. “I like lines,” he said. “It makes my head feel calm, like yours.”

The dryer dinged. The towels were fluffy and warm. “Like clouds!” Buzzy giggled, snuggling a towel against his cheek.

Riley’s cape was dry, too. He tied it around his shoulders and swirled. “I am Super Saver Riley!”

Buzzy grinned. “And I am Buzzy the Busy Helper!”

Mama Beaver gave them both a thumbs-up. “Today, you used kind hearts and smart minds,” she said. “You were fair to each other, you cared for the Earth, and you stayed calm.”

Buzzy nodded. “We saved water with a full load. We used just enough soap. We air-dried shirts to save energy. We organized. And we breathed when we felt mad.”

Riley struck a pose. “Justice for the river! Justice for the fish! Justice for clean capes!”

Everyone laughed. The laundry room felt warm and happy. The lemon soap smell floated like a friendly cloud.

Buzzy wrapped a small towel around his shoulders. “We are heroes,” he said. “Slow and steady, kind and ready.”

They placed the clean clothes in neat stacks. The room was tidy, the water was saved, and the friends were calm.

Buzzy patted the washing machine. “Thank you, whirly friend,” he whispered. “We take care of you, and you help us. We take care of Earth, and Earth takes care of us.”

He looked at Riley. “We did it together.”

“Together,” Riley agreed.

The End.
