# Bubbles on the Front Porch

SPLASH was a playful dolphin who loved to try new things. One sunny afternoon, Splash visited a peaceful front porch by the sea. The porch had a soft blue chair, squeaky wooden steps, and flower pots that smelled like sweet lavender. A gentle breeze tickled the wind chimes. Tinkle, tinkle, tinkle.

“Hello, porch!” Splash chirped, flipping his tail. “Today, I will learn to blow the biggest, roundest bubbles!”

On the little table sat a bubble wand and a bowl of soapy water. The soap smelled like lemons. The water looked shiny and rainbowy in the sun. Splash wiggled with excitement. “I can do this!” he said.

Splash dipped the wand. Drip, drip. He took a big puff and blew. Pfft. The bubble popped right away. Splash blinked. “Oh,” he said softly. “That did not work.”

He tried again. Dip. Blow. Pop! Tiny drops landed on his nose. They tasted a little salty. Splash’s smile drooped. “Why is this so hard?” he asked.

From the rocking chair, an old turtle named Nana Shell peeked over her glasses. Rock, creak. “Hello, Splash,” she said kindly. “New things can be tricky. Try slow and calm. Breathe in, breathe out.”

Splash listened to the wind chimes. Tinkle, tinkle. He felt the warm sun on his back. He put a fin on his chest. “Breathe in,” he whispered. He felt his belly rise. “Breathe out.” He felt his belly fall. His face felt softer.

“Thank you, Nana Shell,” Splash said. “I will try again.”

He dipped the wand. Drip, drip. He whispered, “I can learn. I just need time.” He blew gently. A tiny bubble wobbled. It floated like a little moon. Splash gasped. “I did it! A baby bubble!”

Pop. The bubble disappeared. Splash frowned. “It popped so fast.”

Nana Shell smiled. “Baby steps make big steps,” she said. “Each try teaches you.”

Splash’s friend, a bright green grasshopper named Zip, landed on the railing. Boing! “Hi, Splash!” Zip chirped. “I can jump high! Want to see?” Zip jumped to the top step. Boing! Then he slipped. Thump. He laughed. “Oof! I need practice too.”

Splash giggled. He felt better. “We can practice together,” he said.

The breeze grew stronger. The porch smelled like the sea. Waves shushed in the distance. Shhh, shhh. Splash held the wand again. “Mindful and slow,” he told himself. “Breathe in. Breathe out. Try, try, try.”

He blew. A bubble grew… bigger… bigger… then pop! Soap splashed his cheek. It felt cool and slippery.

Splash’s eyes filled with little tears. “I feel sad,” he said. “I wanted a big bubble.”

Nana Shell nodded. “It is okay to feel sad,” she said softly. “Feel it. Breathe with it. Then choose your next try.”

Splash sniffed. He closed his eyes for a moment. He felt the wooden porch under his belly, smooth and warm. He heard the wind chimes sing. He smelled the flowers. “I can try again,” he whispered. “My brain can grow.”

Zip clapped his tiny hands. “Yes! Your brain is a muscle!”

Splash smiled a tiny smile. He dipped the wand and counted. “One… two… three.” He blew a slow, steady breath. A bubble formed. It grew round and shiny. Colors swirled—pink, blue, gold. The bubble floated up, up, up. It bobbed near the wind chimes. Tinkle. It drifted past the flowers. Then it bumped a leaf and—pop!

Splash gasped with joy. “That was my biggest bubble yet!”

“You learned from each try,” said Nana Shell. “That is the power of yet.”

Splash tilted his head. “The power of yet?”

Nana Shell rocked and smiled. “When you cannot do something, you can say, ‘I can’t do it yet.’ Then you keep trying.”

Splash’s tail swished. “I like that. I can’t blow the biggest bubble… yet.”

They practiced together. Zip counted the breaths. “In… two… three. Out… two… three.” Splash tried different blows—soft, softer, softest. Some bubbles popped fast. Some floated long. Each time, Splash learned something new. “If I blow too hard, pop,” he said. “If I blow steady, the bubble grows.”

The sun slid lower. The porch glowed golden. A gull called, “Kee-yah!” Splash felt calm and happy. He dipped the wand one more time. He breathed in and out, slow and smooth. He blew. A giant bubble, as big as a melon, formed and floated. It glided over the steps, over Nana Shell’s chair, past Zip on the railing. It caught the sunset and shone like a rainbow shell.

“Wow,” whispered Zip.

“Beautiful,” said Nana Shell.

Splash watched the bubble drift into the soft sky. Finally, it popped. He did not feel sad. He felt proud. “I kept trying,” he said. “I learned from each try. I stayed calm. I did it!”

Nana Shell nodded. “That is growth,” she said. “Your heart and brain grew today.”

Splash hugged the bubble wand. “When things are hard, I will remember to breathe, try again, and say, ‘not yet.’”

Zip bounced. “And I will practice my jumps!”

The wind chimes sang one last tinkle. The porch felt warm and peaceful. Splash smiled at his friends. “Thank you,” he said. “Today was a good day for learning.”

The End.
