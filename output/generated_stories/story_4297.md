# Bubbles in the Laundry Room

Splash was a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a happy click-click laugh. One sunny day, Splash visited a big, warm house. He slid in on a soft towel that felt like a little ocean raft. He peeked into a room that hummed and smelled like soap and lemon. It was the laundry room!

“Click-click! What a bubbly place,” Splash said. The washing machine whirred, swish-swish, like tiny waves. The dryer hummed, mmm-mmm, like a friendly whale. Baskets of clothes were stacked like little islands. Socks in colors dotted the baskets like shells.

In came Mia and her grandma, Abuela Rosa. Mia clapped. “Splash! You came to help?”

Splash flicked his tail. “I love bubbles! I can help. I am a good helper.” He sniffed the air. It smelled warm, clean, and sweet.

Abuela Rosa smiled. “We will do laundry with care. In our family, we have a washing song. It is our tradition.”

Mia nodded. “We sing it every time.”

Splash felt excited. “Can I learn?” he asked.

“Of course,” said Abuela. “But first, a rule. We use the right soap, and we tell the truth about spills and drops. That is integrity.”

“Integrity,” Splash repeated slowly. The word felt big, but kind. “It means I tell the truth,” he said.

“Sí,” Abuela said. “It also means we do what is right, even when it is hard.”

They sorted clothes. Whites in one basket, colors in another. Splash used his nose like a pointer. “White here, red there,” he sang. He bumped a little red sock, and it tumbled onto the white shirts.

“Oh!” Splash scooped it up with his snout and placed it back with the reds. He felt proud. “Strategy,” he said. “We make a plan. Sort first!”

Mia giggled. “Yes! That is our plan.”

Abuela poured soap. The bubbles were fluffy and shiny like tiny moons. Splash leaned close. “Click-click,” he whispered. “So many bubbles!”

Abuela started to sing the washing song. “Lava, lava, canta el agua…” Her voice was soft and warm. Mia joined her. “Wash, wash, water sings…”

Splash listened and hummed along. The song sounded like waves on the sand. It was new and special. He felt cozy inside. “I love your song,” he said. “It is your tradition.”

“We share it with you,” Abuela said. “That is tradition-sharing.”

They loaded the machine. Swish-swish went the water. The room smelled soapier. Splash’s tummy did a happy flip. He bounced a little and—oops!—his tail tapped a big blue bottle. The bottle wobbled. “Oh no,” Splash whispered. The bottle tipped and a little splash of soap dripped onto the floor. Slippery. Shiny.

Splash froze. He felt worried. He did not want to make more work. He looked at Mia and Abuela. They were folding towels. They did not see the drip.

Splash’s heart thumped. He remembered Abuela’s words. “Integrity means we tell the truth, even when it is hard.”

He took a breath. “Abuela? Mia?” he called. “I made a drip. I am sorry. My tail bumped the bottle.”

Mia looked. “It’s okay, Splash,” she said gently.

Abuela nodded kindly. “Gracias for telling the truth. That is integrity. We can clean it together.”

Splash’s worry melted. “Together,” he echoed. Mia brought a cloth. Splash pushed the cloth with his nose. Swish-swish, wipe-wipe. Abuela added warm water. Soon the floor shone clean again.

“To keep safe, we make a strategy,” Abuela said. “We put the bottle far from tails.” She moved the bottle to a high shelf.

“Smart plan!” said Splash. He felt proud and calm. He had told the truth. He had helped fix the problem. His heart felt light.

The washer beeped. The clothes were done. Steam puffed, warm and soft. “It smells like sunshine!” Mia said.

Abuela showed Splash how to shake a shirt, smooth and gentle. “In my home, we fold like this,” she said, guiding his flippers. “It is our way.”

Splash tried. Flip, fold, pat. He made a neat little square. “I did it!” he cheered.

“You learned our tradition,” Abuela said. “And you shared your ocean song. I heard it in your clicks.”

Splash smiled. He clicked a soft sea song: click-click, whooo, shh. It sounded like tiny waves in the laundry room. Mia swayed. Abuela tapped the beat.

They made a new plan: fold, sing, stack. Fold, sing, stack. It felt like a dance. The laundry room glowed with happy work.

When they finished, Abuela clapped. “We worked with integrity. We told the truth. We used a good strategy. We shared traditions.”

Mia hugged Splash. “You are a great helper.”

Splash’s eyes sparkled. “I learned that telling the truth makes the work feel light. I learned to make a plan. And I love learning songs from your family.”

Abuela smiled. “And we love your ocean song.”

Splash took a deep, happy breath. The room smelled like clean clothes and lemon. The machines were quiet now, like a calm sea. He felt peaceful.

“Click-click,” he said. “I will always tell the truth. I will always help. I will always listen and learn.”

They all smiled together.

The End.
