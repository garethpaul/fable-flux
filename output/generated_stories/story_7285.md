# Leo the Lion Cub Learns to Share

In a cozy dining room with a big wooden table, lived a brave little lion cub named Leo. He had a fluffy golden mane and bright amber eyes. Every day, Leo's mama would set out colorful plates and yummy food on the long table.

"Time for lunch, Leo!" Mama Lion called. The dining room smelled like fresh carrots and warm bread. Leo's tummy rumbled loudly.

Leo ran to the table. He saw orange carrots, green broccoli, and red apples. But Leo only wanted the sweet cookies on the special plate.

"I want ALL the cookies!" Leo roared, grabbing the whole plate. He hugged it tight against his fluffy chest.

Just then, the doorbell rang. *Ding dong!* Three little animal friends stood at the door. There was Bella the bunny, Sam the squirrel, and Penny the puppy. They looked hungry and tired.

"We've been playing outside all morning," said Bella. Her pink nose twitched. "We smell something yummy!"

Leo's mama smiled. "Come in, friends! There's plenty of food for everyone."

But Leo held his cookie plate even tighter. "No! These are MY cookies!" he growled. His friends' faces looked sad. Their tummies grumbled too.

Leo sat at the big table. He took a big bite of his first cookie. It was sweet and crunchy. But when he looked at his friends, he didn't feel happy anymore. Bella's ears drooped down. Sam's fluffy tail wasn't swishing. Penny's tongue wasn't hanging out like usual.

"Why do I feel funny inside?" Leo wondered. The cookie didn't taste as good anymore.

Mama Lion sat down next to Leo. "How do you think your friends feel?" she asked gently.

Leo looked around the dining room. His friends sat quietly, eating only carrots and bread. No one was laughing or playing. The room felt cold, even though the sun was shining through the window.

"They look... sad," Leo said softly. His little lion heart felt heavy.

"When we share with others, everyone feels happy," Mama explained. "Good friends take care of each other."

Leo thought about this. He remembered when Bella shared her crayons with him. He remembered when Sam gave him half of his acorn. He remembered when Penny let him play with her favorite ball.

Suddenly, Leo stood up tall. His mane puffed out proudly. "I know what to do!"

Leo walked around the table. He gave Bella two cookies. He gave Sam two cookies. He gave Penny two cookies. "Here! These are for you!" he said with a big smile.

"Thank you, Leo!" his friends cheered. Their faces lit up like sunshine.

Now the dining room felt warm and happy again. Everyone munched their cookies together. They shared stories and giggled. Leo felt proud and warm inside.

"Sharing feels even better than keeping everything for myself!" Leo realized.

Mama Lion hugged her brave little cub. "You learned something very important today, Leo. When we share and help others, we make our whole community happy."

From that day on, Leo always made sure everyone had enough to eat. He learned that being brave meant caring for others, not just himself. And every meal in the dining room was filled with laughter, friendship, and lots of sharing.

The End.
