# Squeaky-Clean Keys for Squeak the Squirrel

Squeak was a resourceful squirrel who lived in a cozy home office. The room smelled like paper and warm tea. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. The desk was big and smooth, and the keyboard made tiny click-clack sounds when Squeak typed with his soft paws.

“I love words,” Squeak chirped. “Click, clack! Tap, tap! Hello, letters!” He liked to write letters to friends and label his seed jars. He stuck small, neat labels on boxes: acorns, pinecones, pencils.

One morning, Squeak poured crunchy sunflower seeds into a bowl. He wiggled into his chair and opened a new page. “Good morning, words,” he said. “Today, I will write about the wind and the trees.”

He popped a seed into his mouth and typed. Click, clack. Crunch, crunch.

Then Squeak stopped. His whiskers twitched. His paws felt sticky. The keys felt sticky too. “Oh no,” he squeaked. “My paws are icky! The keys are icky!” A little smear shined on the space bar. It smelled a bit salty and oily.

Squeak frowned. “My words need clean keys,” he said. He pressed the M key. It stuck. “Mmm—oh dear.”

At that moment, Pip the mouse peeked in with a tiny cloth bag. “Hi, Squeak! I borrowed your blue pen yesterday,” Pip said. “I forgot to ask.”

Squeak’s tail made a worried curl. “Pip, I don’t like that you didn’t ask,” he said gently. “It’s not fair to take things without asking.”

Pip’s ears drooped. “You’re right. I’m sorry. I will ask next time.”

Squeak took a deep breath. He felt the warm tea smell, and his fur settled. “I forgive you,” he said. “Thank you for telling me.” Forgiveness made his chest feel light.

Pip stepped closer and sniffed. “What’s that icky smell?”

“Seeds on my keys,” Squeak said. “My paws and the keyboard are sticky. I didn’t wash.”

Pip nodded. “Hygiene helps,” he said. The word sounded big and friendly. “Hygiene means keeping things clean so we stay healthy.”

Squeak’s eyes got bright. “Hygiene! I like that word. Hi-geen,” he tried. “Hygiene!” He smiled at the sound.

Pip reached into his cloth bag. “I brought a soft brush and a tiny spray bottle,” he said. “We can clean with just a little water and a drop of soap. That’s good for the planet. It saves water and doesn’t waste.”

Squeak clapped his paws. “That’s sustainable,” he said proudly. He loved big words. He loved the click-clack of letters. He loved the smell of fresh air through the window.

But he remembered. “I ate and typed. That wasn’t fair to my keyboard,” he said. “The keyboard works hard for my words.”

Pip giggled. “Let’s be fair. We will care for it.”

They made a plan.

First, Squeak scooped his seeds back into the bowl and set the bowl on a little napkin far from the keys.

Second, he went to the sink. The water felt cool and soft. “Wet, soap, rub, rinse,” Squeak sang. His paws made bubbles. The soap smelled like lemon. “Clean paws, happy paws.” He used a small towel. “Dry, dry, dry,” he sang. His paws felt warm and fluffy.

Third, Pip showed the tiny spray. “Only a little,” Pip said. “One, two.” They sprayed a soft cloth, not the keyboard. Squeak wiped gently. “Swipe, swipe,” he whispered. The keys began to shine. The oily smell faded. The air smelled like lemon and paper.

Fourth, Pip used the soft brush to dust between the keys. “Tickle, tickle,” Pip said. Squeak giggled. “It tickles my ears to hear it!”

When they finished, the keyboard looked new. Squeak tapped. Click, clack! The M key no longer stuck. His heart felt happy.

“Thank you, Pip,” Squeak said. “You helped me fix my sticky problem.”

Pip smiled. “You forgave me about the pen. That was kind. I wanted to help.”

Squeak nodded. “Next time, let’s make a fairness rule,” he said. He wrote a label in neat, big letters: ASK BEFORE BORROWING. He stuck it on a little jar with the blue pen inside. He wrote another label: CLEAN PAWS BEFORE TYPING. He stuck it on the desk near the keyboard.

Squeak loved the language of labels. He read them out loud. “Ask. Clean. Before. Borrowing. Typing.” The words felt smooth in his mouth. He liked how letters made meaning.

Pip pointed to the trash can. “We used a cloth, not paper towels. That’s fair to the trees.”

Squeak looked at the window and whispered, “Thank you, trees.” The breeze moved the curtain. The room smelled sweet and clean.

Later, Squeak wrote his story: “Today I learned hygiene helps my hands and my keys. I learned to forgive. I learned to be fair and to ask. I learned to clean in a way that helps the Earth.”

He tapped the last period. Click. “Hello, letters,” he said softly. “Thank you for waiting for clean paws.”

Pip waved. “Snack time?” he asked.

Squeak nodded. “Yes! But at the little table.” They set the bowl on the napkin away from the keyboard. Crunch, crunch. The keyboard stayed clean. The room stayed fresh. The words stayed easy.

Squeak smiled. “Clean paws, clear words,” he said. “That’s our rule.”

The End.
