# Leo's Big Goal Adventure

In a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls and fluffy white clouds painted on the ceiling, lived a brave little lion cub named Leo. His golden fur was as warm as sunshine, and his tiny mane was just starting to grow around his fuzzy ears.

One morning, Leo stretched his little paws and yawned a big lion yawn. "ROAAAAR!" he said, though it sounded more like a gentle purr. The sunlight streamed through his window, making everything sparkle.

"Today feels special," Leo said to his favorite teddy bear, Mr. Buttons. "I want to do something really important!"

Leo looked around his room. His toy blocks were scattered everywhere like colorful stepping stones. His books were in a wobbly pile by his bed. His crayons had rolled under the dresser.

"I know!" Leo's eyes lit up like little stars. "I want to make my room the tidiest, most helpful room ever! That's my big goal!"

But as Leo looked at all the mess, his whiskers drooped a little. "Oh my," he whispered. "That's a really big goal. How will I ever do it all?"

Leo sat down on his soft carpet and thought very carefully. His mama had taught him that big goals need good plans.

"First, I need to think about safety," Leo said wisely. He remembered not to climb on tall furniture or reach for things that were too high up. "I'll only clean things I can reach safely."

Leo decided to break his big goal into smaller pieces, just like his favorite puzzle. "First, I'll put all my blocks in their special box. Then I'll stack my books neatly. After that, I'll ask Mama to help me get the crayons from under the dresser."

"One step at a time," Leo purred to himself.

He started with the blocks, picking up each one carefully. Red block, blue block, yellow block - into the box they went! The blocks made happy clicking sounds as they found their home.

"This is working!" Leo said cheerfully. His tail swished with excitement.

Next came the books. Leo gently stacked them from biggest to smallest, making a perfect tower. The books smelled like adventures and felt smooth under his paws.

When Leo tried to reach the crayons under the dresser, he remembered his safety rule. "I need help with this part," he said. "That's the smart thing to do!"

"Mama!" Leo called. "Can you please help me reach my crayons? I'm trying to achieve my goal of cleaning my room!"

Mama Lion came in with a big smile. "I'm so proud of you for asking for help, Leo! That shows you're thinking logically and being safe."

Together, they retrieved all the colorful crayons. Leo put them carefully in their special cup, arranging them like a rainbow.

Leo stepped back and looked at his room. Everything was tidy and in its place. The afternoon sun made his clean room glow like a magical kingdom.

"I did it!" Leo roared happily. "I set a big goal, made a safe plan, asked for help when I needed it, and worked step by step. My room looks wonderful!"

Mama Lion gave Leo the biggest, warmest hug. "You learned something very important today, little one. Any goal can be reached if you break it into small steps, stay safe, and ask for help when you need it."

That night, as Leo snuggled into his cozy bed, he felt proud and happy. His clean room felt even more comfortable, and he had learned that with good planning and patience, he could achieve anything he set his mind to.

The End.
