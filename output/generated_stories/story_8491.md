# Whiskers and the Rainbow Room

In a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls and fluffy white curtains, lived a clever cat named Whiskers. Whiskers had beautiful orange and white fur that felt as soft as cotton balls. The bedroom belonged to a little girl named Emma, who loved to draw and paint colorful pictures.

One sunny morning, Whiskers stretched his paws and yawned. "What a lovely day!" he purred, looking around the cozy room. Emma's art supplies were scattered everywhere - crayons rolled under the bed, paintbrushes lay on the windowsill, and colorful papers covered the floor like a rainbow carpet.

Emma sat at her small wooden desk, carefully drawing with her favorite purple crayon. "I'm making a picture of our garden," she said to Whiskers, who jumped onto the desk to watch.

Just then, Emma's little brother Tommy peeked into the room. Tommy was only three years old and used a wheelchair to move around. "Can I draw too?" he asked hopefully, his eyes sparkling with excitement.

"No, Tommy!" Emma said quickly. "You'll mess up my art supplies. You're too little, and your wheelchair might bump into things."

Tommy's face fell, and his bottom lip began to tremble. Whiskers noticed how sad Tommy looked and felt his heart squeeze tight. The clever cat knew this wasn't right.

Whiskers had an idea. He gently placed his soft paw on Emma's hand and meowed thoughtfully. "Meow, meow," he said, which in cat language meant, "Everyone deserves to make art!"

Then Whiskers did something very clever. He carefully picked up a red crayon in his mouth and dropped it near Tommy's wheelchair. He picked up some paper and placed it on Tommy's lap. Finally, he pushed a small box with his nose to make a perfect table for Tommy.

"Look, Emma!" Tommy giggled with delight. "Whiskers made me an art station!"

Emma watched as Whiskers continued to help. The clever cat used his paws to organize the art supplies so both children could reach them easily. He even found a special cup that could hold crayons right next to Tommy's wheelchair.

"Whiskers is showing us how to share," Emma realized, feeling a warm feeling in her chest. "I'm sorry, Tommy. I wasn't being very nice. Would you like to draw the flowers while I draw the trees?"

"Yes, please!" Tommy said, his smile returning like sunshine after rain. His small hands gripped a yellow crayon, and he began making bright, happy circles on his paper.

As the children drew together, Whiskers purred loudly - a sound like a tiny motor running. The room filled with giggles and the scratchy sound of crayons on paper. Tommy drew wobbly but wonderful yellow flowers, while Emma added tall green trees.

"Your flowers are beautiful, Tommy!" Emma said, admiring her brother's artwork. "They look so sunny and happy!"

Tommy beamed with pride. "Your trees are really tall! Can we hang our pictures together?"

"That's a perfect idea!" Emma agreed.

Whiskers watched as the children worked side by side, sharing crayons and ideas. The clever cat had learned that when everyone is included, art becomes even more magical. The cozy bedroom felt warmer and happier than ever before.

From that day on, Emma always made sure Tommy could join in their art adventures. And Whiskers? He became the best art helper in the whole house, teaching everyone that respect and kindness make the most beautiful pictures of all.

The End.
