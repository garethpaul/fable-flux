# Joey Roo Helps the Backyard

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with big springy feet and a soft, swishy tail. He loved to hop in the sunny backyard. The grass felt cool on his toes. The sun felt warm on his ears. The air smelled like oranges and fresh soil. Birds sang, “Chirp, chirp!” and a little breeze whispered, “Whoosh.”

“Today is a big day,” said Mama Roo, smiling. “We are having a tiny neighborhood picnic. We will share snacks and stories. Everyone is welcome.”

Joey Roo clapped his little paws. “I can help! I am a big helper.”

Mama Roo nodded. “Helping is civic responsibility. That means we take care of our place and our friends.”

“Civic res-pon-si-bi-li-ty,” Joey Roo said slowly, giggling. “I can do it!”

He hopped across the sunny yard. He saw a colorful banner on the fence. He felt the rough wood as he taped it gently. “Stick, stick,” he said. He smelled sweet fruit in a bowl—mango, berries, and crunchy apples. The colors looked like a rainbow.

Friends came to the backyard. Kiki Koala wore a bright yellow scarf. Tino Tortoise had a shiny blue shell sticker. Padmini Possum brought spicy flatbread that smelled warm, like cinnamon and sunshine. Mei Magpie had rice crackers and seaweed sheets. Benji Bilby brought a drum that went “dum-dum-dum.” Everyone smiled.

“Welcome!” Joey Roo said. “Let’s be kind and help.”

Kiki Koala waved. “I can set cups.” She stacked them carefully. Clink, clink.

Tino Tortoise nodded slowly. “I can carry napkins.” He walked steady and proud. Step. Step.

Padmini Possum said, “I can share food from my family.” She opened a small tin. “Try a little,” she said. Joey tasted a tiny piece. “Mmm,” he hummed. “New flavors!”

Mei Magpie chirped, “I can show how we fold paper cranes.” She folded with soft, quick clicks. Joey watched, eyes wide. “So cool,” he whispered.

Joey Roo looked around. He saw a small piece of paper on the grass. He saw a bottle on its side near the flowers. He saw a gate open a crack. He took a big breath. “I can do this,” he told himself. “I can help.”

He picked up the paper and put it in a bin. Rustle. He set the bottle in the recycling. Clink. He gently closed the gate with a click.

“Thank you,” said Mama Roo. “You are keeping our yard safe and clean.”

“That is civic responsibility,” Joey Roo said proudly. He puffed his chest a little. He felt warm and happy inside.

Soon, a gust of wind blew. Whoosh! The banner flapped. A napkin flew away. The bowl of fruit wobbled.

“Oh no!” said Kiki Koala.

“I can help,” said Joey Roo. He held the banner with both paws. “Hold tight!” He called, “Friends, can you help me, please?”

“Yes!” said everyone.

Tino put pebbles on the napkins. Padmini steadied the fruit bowl. Mei tied the banner tighter with a neat knot. Benji beat his drum: “dum-dum, we can do it, dum-dum!” The rhythm made them all giggle and work together.

The wind calmed. The sun smiled again. The backyard felt cozy and bright.

“Teamwork!” said Mama Roo. “We took care of our place together.”

Joey Roo’s tummy rumbled. “Snack time?” he asked with a big grin.

They sat on a soft blanket. It felt bumpy from the grass below. Everyone shared. Joey tried a bit of each food. He tasted sweet mango, salty seaweed, and warm flatbread. “Our foods are all different,” Joey said, licking his lips. “Different is delicious!”

“And our families are from different places,” Mei said gently. “We bring our stories and our snacks.”

“That is cultural diversity,” Mama Roo explained. “We learn from each other. We share. We are kind.”

Joey Roo nodded. “I like learning,” he said. His ears wiggled with joy.

After snacks, Joey saw a small puddle by the tap. “It can be slippery,” he said. He picked up a towel and patted the puddle. Pat, pat, pat. He set out a little sign he made: “Wet—Be Careful.” The letters were wiggly, but clear. Joey smiled. “I can be careful and help others be safe.”

Kiki Koala clapped. “Joey, you are independent! You see a job, and you do it.”

Joey Roo felt proud. “I can look, think, and help.”

Mama Roo hugged him. “Yes, my joey. That is civic responsibility, too: noticing what needs care and doing it, even a small thing.”

As the sun began to rest, the sky turned peach and pink. The air smelled like grass and sweet tea. Everyone cleaned up together. Cups in a stack. Crumbs in the bin. Drums in a bag. The yard looked tidy and happy.

“Thank you for helping our place,” Mama Roo said.

Joey Roo stood tall on his springy feet. “Today I learned to care for our backyard, to share with friends from many places, and to help all by myself.”

He waved to his friends. “See you soon!”

“See you soon!” they cheered.

Joey Roo looked at the neat yard and the closed gate. He felt calm, like a soft blanket. He smiled a big, sunny smile.

“We all live here,” he said. “We all help here.”

And the backyard, quiet and clean, seemed to whisper back, “Thank you.”

The End.
