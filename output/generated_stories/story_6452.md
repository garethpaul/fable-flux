# The Cozy Nursery Build

In a comfortable nursery with soft yellow walls and a warm, sunlit rug, a gentle deer fawn named Della flicked her tiny tail. The room smelled like clean cotton and warm wood blocks. Della’s big brown eyes sparkled as she nudged a basket of blocks with her nose. Clack, clack, clack went the blocks on the rug.

“I want to build a tall tower,” Della whispered. Her voice was soft like a lullaby.

Her friends were there, too. Pip the bunny hopped on the squishy beanbag, his long ears flopping. “I want the red blocks,” Pip said, twitching his nose.

Mimi the mouse peeked from behind a pillow fort. “I want to make a bridge,” she squeaked, wrapping her tiny paws around a smooth, curved block.

Della felt a flutter in her tummy. So many ideas! The basket had only a few red blocks and only one curved bridge block. Della took a slow breath. In… and out. The nursery clock tick-tocked gently. Far away, a lullaby music box played a sweet, ding-ding song.

“Let’s talk,” Della said, bravely lifting her head. “We can share. We can make a plan.”

Pip’s ears perked up. “A plan?”

Mimi’s whiskers wiggled. “A plan!”

Della set three blocks in a small circle. “We can take turns. We can use kind words. We can listen.” She smiled. “That is called negotiation. It means we talk and make a plan that feels good for everyone.”

Pip scrunched his nose. “But I really, really want the red blocks now.”

Mimi hugged the curved block close. “And I really want my bridge!”

Della felt that tummy flutter again. She pressed her hoof gently on the soft rug. The rug felt fuzzy and warm. “Let’s try calm first,” she said softly. “Let’s breathe together.”

They all took a deep breath. In… and out. Pip’s ears slowly relaxed. Mimi’s tiny paws loosened.

Della pointed with her hoof. “I have an idea. Pip, you can use two red blocks first. Mimi, you can use the curved block first. I will start with the square blocks. Then we switch. We can count to ten, then trade.”

Pip looked at the red blocks, shiny and bright like apples. He wanted them very much. But he liked Della’s gentle voice. “Okay,” he said. “I can wait for ten.”

Mimi gave the curved block a little pat. “Okay,” she said. “I can share after ten.”

Della smiled. “We can make a big building together. A tower with a bridge!”

They began. Pip stacked two red blocks. Tap, tap. He added a blue block. Mimi set the curved block on two short blocks. Click. Della placed square blocks into a sturdy base. Thump, thump. The blocks felt cool and smooth on her hoof.

“One… two… three…” Della counted. Her friends counted with her. Their voices were hushed and happy. When they reached “Ten!” Pip put the red blocks down carefully. Mimi gently slid the curved block toward Della.

“Your turn,” Pip said.

“Your turn,” Mimi echoed.

Della felt proud. “Thank you,” she said. “Now it’s your turn to use the square blocks and the blue blocks.”

They kept building. They traded and shared. They used calm voices. When someone wanted a block, they asked, “May I use that next?” When someone felt wiggly or worried, they took a breath.

Soon the tower grew tall, tall, tall. The bridge curved like a rainbow. The sun through the window made golden stripes on the rug. The room smelled sweet, like warm crayons and fresh paper. Della’s ears heard soft giggles and the shhh of blocks sliding.

But then—oh no!—the tower wobbled. Wobble-wobble. Pip’s eyes went wide. Mimi squeaked, “Eek!”

Della stepped forward on her tiny hooves. “We can be brave,” she said. “We can fix it together.” Her voice stayed calm, like a quiet stream.

“How?” Pip asked.

“First, we talk,” Della said. “Then we do.” She looked closely at the shaky side. “This side needs a wide block. Who has one?”

“I do,” Pip said, holding a wide green block.

“May I use it to make the tower safe?” Della asked.

Pip nodded. “Yes. Then I can use the red one next.”

“Deal,” said Della with a smile. “That is negotiation.”

Mimi brought two small squares. “These can help the bridge,” she said.

Together they pressed the wide green block at the bottom. The tower became steady. Mimi slid the little squares under the bridge. The bridge hugged the tower, snug and safe.

They all stepped back. “Wow,” they whispered. Their building was bright and strong. It looked like a cozy castle with a happy rainbow road.

Pip did a tiny hop. “We did it!”

Mimi clapped her paws. “We shared!”

Della’s heart felt warm, like sunshine. “We talked. We listened. We made a plan. We were calm and brave. We used negotiation.”

They cleaned up together, too. Clack went the blocks into the basket. Swish went the rug as they smoothed it with gentle paws and hooves. The nursery felt neat and peaceful.

Pip smiled. “When we talk and share, we can build big things.”

Mimi nodded. “And we can feel safe and happy.”

Della nuzzled her friends. “Yes,” she said softly. “When we use calm words and kind plans, everyone wins.”

The End.
