# Rocco's Rooftop Rest

High on a tall city rooftop lived a musical raccoon named Rocco. Rocco loved making music. He played his tiny drums all day long. *Tap-tap-tap!* went his drums. *Bang-bang-bang!* 

Every morning, Rocco woke up early. He played music for the sunrise. He played music for the birds. He played music for the busy people walking below.

"Music, music, music!" Rocco sang happily. "I must play all day!"

But one gray morning, something felt different. Rocco's paws felt heavy. His eyes felt tired. His drums sounded quiet and sad.

*Tap... tap... tap...*

"That doesn't sound right," Rocco said. He tried again. *Bang... bang...*

"Still not right!" Rocco felt worried. "What's wrong with my music?"

Rocco played harder. He played faster. But the music still sounded tired and sad.

Rosie the robin landed on the rooftop. She was Rocco's friend. "Good morning, Rocco," she chirped. "You look very tired."

"I'm not tired!" Rocco said quickly. "I just need to play more music!"

But Rosie noticed something. Rocco's fur looked messy. His little house looked messy too. There were no clean water bowls. No fresh food.

"Rocco," Rosie said gently, "when did you last eat breakfast?"

Rocco stopped drumming. "Um... yesterday? Maybe?"

"When did you last drink water?"

"I... I don't remember."

"When did you last sleep?"

Rocco's ears drooped. "I've been playing music all night for three days."

Rosie fluffed her feathers. "Oh, Rocco! No wonder your music sounds sad. You forgot to take care of yourself!"

"But I love making music," Rocco said softly.

"I know you do," Rosie said. "But to make beautiful music, you need to take care of your body first. Your body is like your drums - it needs care to work well."

Rosie helped Rocco understand. "Let's try something new. First, let's get you some fresh water."

Rocco drank the cool, clean water. "Mmm, that tastes good!"

"Now some yummy berries for breakfast."

Rocco ate the sweet, juicy berries. "I feel a little better already!"

"Now let's clean your cozy house and make your bed soft and warm."

Together, they tidied up. Rocco's house looked bright and clean.

"Last step," Rosie said. "A nice, long nap."

"But my music..." Rocco started.

"Will sound even more beautiful when you're rested," Rosie promised.

Rocco curled up in his soft, clean bed. He closed his tired eyes. Soon he was fast asleep, dreaming of wonderful melodies.

When Rocco woke up, the sun was setting. He felt amazing! His paws felt strong. His eyes felt bright. His heart felt happy.

He picked up his drums. *TAP-TAP-TAP!* The sound was clear and joyful. *BANG-BANG-BANG!* The rhythm was perfect and strong.

"Listen, Rosie!" Rocco called excitedly. "My music sounds beautiful again!"

Rosie smiled. "That's because YOU feel beautiful again. When you take care of yourself, everything you do gets better."

From that day on, Rocco learned a new song. It went: "Eat good food, drink clean water, sleep when tired, keep things tidy. Take care of me, and I'll make music that's pretty!"

Every day, Rocco remembered to care for himself first. Then his music filled the rooftop with the most wonderful sounds the city had ever heard.

The End.
