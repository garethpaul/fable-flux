# Whiskers and the Snack Puzzle

In a quiet study corner, a clever cat named Whiskers curled his tail around a soft cushion. The lamp made a warm, golden glow. The room smelled like paper, crayons, and a hint of apples. On the little desk sat three small bowls. One bowl had crunchy carrot sticks. One bowl had bright apple slices. One bowl had crinkly, shiny wrappers.

Whiskers twitched his whiskers. “Hmm,” he purred. “My tummy says snack time! But my brain says, let’s think.”

A tiny voice peeped from the pencil cup. “Think first, snack second!” chirped Pip the paperclip. Pip liked to help.

Whiskers smiled. “Yes, Pip. Let’s use our thinking paws.”

He stood on the chair and looked carefully. He sniffed the carrot bowl. Crunchy. Fresh. He sniffed the apple bowl. Sweet. Juicy. He poked the wrapped snacks. Crinkle, crinkle. Loud and shiny.

Whiskers asked aloud, “Which snack helps my body and the Earth?”

Pip chimed, “Good question! Let’s ask more questions.”

Whiskers nodded. “Question one: What is good for my body?” He tapped his head gently. “My body likes vitamins.” He touched the carrots. “Carrots help eyes.” He touched the apples. “Apples help tummies.”

Pip bounced. “Question two: What makes less trash?”

Whiskers looked at the crinkly wrappers. He heard the loud crinkle, and his ears flicked. “These make trash,” he said. He looked at the carrots and apples. “These grow on trees and in the ground. No wrappers here.”

Whiskers took a deep breath. He listened to the quiet lamp hum. He felt the smooth desk under his paws. He wanted to choose fast. But he remembered, “Slow down. Think it through.”

He made a plan. “Step one: Look. Step two: Ask. Step three: Choose.”

He looked again. He saw the bright orange carrots. He saw the shiny red apples. He saw the wrapped snacks with pictures of sugar stars.

Pip whispered, “What does your body say?”

Whiskers touched his belly. “My belly says hungry. My mind says, pick a snack that helps me grow strong.”

He tasted a tiny carrot stick. Crunch! It was cool and crisp. He tasted a tiny apple slice. Mmm! It was sweet and juicy.

He paused. He thought. He even closed his eyes. He pictured himself running and jumping. He pictured the Earth, big and blue and green.

Whiskers opened his eyes. “I have an idea. I will make a rainbow plate! Carrots and apples together.”

Pip clapped. “Smart choice! That helps you and makes less trash.”

Whiskers said, “Yes, and I can save the seeds from the apple. Maybe we can plant them.”

He took a small leaf-shaped plate from the shelf. He put carrots on one side. He put apples on the other side. He added a little bowl of water to sip. He sat and ate. Crunch, crunch. Slurp, slurp. He felt happy and calm.

Just then, a soft breeze came from the window. It carried the smell of fresh leaves. The study corner felt bright and cozy. Whiskers heard birds outside. He smiled and swished his tail.

Pip asked, “What about the wrapped snacks?”

Whiskers thought again. “Sometimes, treats are okay,” he said. “But every day, I pick foods that help me grow.” He put the wrapped snacks in a box for later, for a special day.

After snack time, Whiskers wiped his whiskers with a tiny napkin. He cleaned the desk. He put the apple seeds in a small cup of soil on the windowsill. He patted the soil gently. Pat, pat.

Pip watched with big, shiny eyes. “You are caring for the Earth,” Pip said.

Whiskers nodded. “Caring is part of thinking,” he purred. “I asked good questions. I listened to my body. I thought about trash and trees. I made a plan.”

He drew a little picture with a green crayon: a carrot, an apple, and a small tree under a smiling sun. He wrote, very slowly, “Think. Ask. Choose.”

Pip read the words. “Think. Ask. Choose. That is a good rule!”

Whiskers curled up on the cushion. His belly felt full. His heart felt proud. The lamp hummed softly. The room smelled sweet, like apples and warm paper. He felt ready for the next little adventure.

Pip whispered, “What did we learn today?”

Whiskers purred, “We learned to use our smart minds. We ask questions. We pick foods that help our bodies. We make less trash. We care for our home, the Earth.”

Pip smiled. “And we did it together.”

Whiskers closed his eyes. “Yes,” he said, “together.”

The study corner was quiet and safe. The apple seeds rested in their tiny bed. The carrot bowl and apple bowl were clean. The wrapped snacks waited for a special time. Whiskers’ sign—Think. Ask. Choose.—shined green on the desk.

Whiskers whispered one last time, “I can think. I can choose. I can care.”

Pip whispered back, “Me too.”

The End.
