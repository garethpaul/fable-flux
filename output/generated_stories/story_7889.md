# Rooftop Hops Around the World

Sunny Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with big springy feet and a bright, bouncy smile. One breezy morning, Sunny hopped up the stairs to the rooftop garden of the apartment building. The sky felt close and wide. The wind smelled like fresh leaves and warm bread from the bakery below. Potted flowers nodded in the sunlight. The rooftop was Sunny’s favorite playground.

“I’m ready to hop!” Sunny cheered. “Boing, boing, boing!”

Sunny warmed up, stretching legs tall and arms wide. “Stretch to the sun, wiggle like jelly, shake-shake-shake!” The rooftop floor felt warm and smooth under Sunny’s paws. A flock of birds swooped by, chirping a friendly hello.

Just as Sunny began a big, super hop, a soft voice crackled from a small radio in the corner of the rooftop. It was the building’s sharing radio, used by neighbors. “Hello, rooftop friends,” said Mrs. Lin from downstairs. “I am having quiet time. Could you please make small hops?”

Sunny paused. “Oh! Someone needs quiet.” Sunny put a paw on the radio button and spoke gently. “Hi, Mrs. Lin. I hear you. I will try small hops.”

Sunny took little hops. Tip, tap, boing. The little hops felt good, like bouncing bubbles. Then another voice floated up, warm and sing-song. “Sunny, dear, I am dancing with my baby in Brazil!” said Auntie Lila, who lived far away. “Can we dance together across the world?”

Sunny’s eyes grew big. “Wow! Brazil is far. But we can dance together on the rooftop!” Sunny clapped to the beat Auntie Lila sent through the radio: clap, clap, clap-clap-clap. Toes wiggled. Feet shuffled. Sunny smiled so wide that cheeks felt sunny, too.

Just then, a sleepy yawn came from a window nearby. “I am in Spain,” said Mateo through the radio. “It is nap time here. Can you keep the music soft?”

Sunny’s ears perked. So many friends, in so many places! Some wanted to move, some needed rest. Sunny felt a little twist in the tummy. “How can I hop and dance and also be quiet? Hmm.”

Sunny took a deep breath. The breeze tickled the nose. The flowers smelled like honey. “I can listen. I can talk. We can find a way,” Sunny said.

Sunny pressed the radio. “Friends around the world, let’s share. I want to hop for exercise. Mrs. Lin needs quiet. Auntie Lila wants to dance. Mateo needs a nap. Let’s try a plan. We will use soft feet and quiet music. We can do slow, gentle hops. Then, later, we can have big hops when napping is done. Does that sound okay?”

There was a happy hum. “That sounds fair,” said Mrs. Lin. “Thank you.”

“Gentle dance is sweet,” said Auntie Lila. “I can sway with my baby.”

“Soft is good,” yawned Mateo. “I can nap with a smile.”

Sunny felt lighter. “We did it! We made a plan together.”

Sunny started the Quiet Hop Dance. “Soft paws,” whispered Sunny. “Breath in, breath out.” The rooftop felt peaceful. The wind brushed Sunny’s fur like a feather. Feet barely tapped—tup, tup, tup—like raindrops. Arms floated like kites. Sunny counted, “One, two, three,” in a hushed voice. The radio played a soft beat. Auntie Lila hummed a gentle tune, “La-la, la-la.” Mrs. Lin sighed happily. Mateo’s sleepy breathing puffed like tiny clouds.

Sunny’s muscles felt warm and strong, even with gentle moves. “Exercise can be big jumps,” Sunny said, “and also small moves.” Sunny did slow knee bends, heel raises, and quiet side steps. “Boing inside, quiet outside,” Sunny giggled.

A moment later, another ping came from the radio. It was Zuri from Kenya. “Hello from morning time!” she chimed. “I am stretching in the sun. Can we say hello in our own ways?”

Sunny’s eyes sparkled. “Yes! Let’s learn greetings from around the world.”

Sunny held the radio close and spoke softly. “I say, ‘G’day!’ in Australia.”

“Olá!” sang Auntie Lila from Brazil.

“Hola,” whispered Mateo from Spain.

“Jambo,” smiled Zuri from Kenya.

“Ni hao,” said Mrs. Lin.

Sunny repeated each word, slow and clear. “G’day. Olá. Hola. Jambo. Ni hao.” The sounds felt new in Sunny’s mouth—round, bright, and friendly. “We are far apart, but we are together,” Sunny said. “We listen, we move, we care.”

Time passed in gentle steps. The sun slid higher. Mateo’s nap ended. The radio crackled. “I am awake!” he said. “Now you can have big hops!”

Mrs. Lin added, “Thank you for the quiet. I feel calm.”

Auntie Lila laughed softly. “The baby is happy. Now we can dance louder!”

Sunny beamed. “It’s Big Hop Time!” But Sunny remembered the plan. “Let’s check first.” Sunny asked, “Is loud okay now?” Everyone replied, “Yes!”

Sunny turned the music up a little. The beat went thump-thump. Sunny hopped high—boing!—and landed with strong, safe knees. “Arms out, eyes forward,” Sunny coached. Zuri clapped. Auntie Lila spun. Mateo tapped his toes. Mrs. Lin hummed. The rooftop filled with happy sounds—the swish of wind, the pat of paws, the clap of hands, and cheerful voices from many places.

After the last big hop, Sunny pressed the radio. “Thank you, friends around the world. We listened. We talked. We made a plan. We took care of each other.”

Sunny sat on the warm rooftop floor, heart beating like a drum, breath slow and proud. “Today I learned that people in different places have different needs and times,” Sunny said. “We can exercise and still be kind. We can compromise. We can use our words. That is how the world feels close.”

The sky seemed even wider now, full of places to learn about. Sunny waved at the clouds. “G’day, Olá, Hola, Jambo, Ni hao,” Sunny whispered, smiling.

And then Sunny gave one last gentle hop.

The End.
