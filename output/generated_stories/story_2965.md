# Joey's Honest Art Day

Joey the kangaroo was a happy little joey who loved to bounce and play. Today, he hopped up the creaky wooden stairs to visit Grandma Roo's quiet attic. The attic smelled like old books and lavender, and dusty sunbeams danced through the small window.

"Wow!" Joey whispered, his big brown eyes growing wide. On a low table sat colorful paints, brushes, and big white papers. "Grandma Roo said I could paint up here!"

Joey bounced excitedly toward the art supplies. His fluffy tail wiggled with joy as he picked up a bright red brush. He dipped it in yellow paint and made a big, wobbly sun on his paper.

"This is fun!" he giggled, painting orange flowers and green grass. The soft bristles of the brush tickled his small paws.

Just then, Joey heard footsteps on the stairs. His cousin Emma, a little rabbit, poked her head into the attic.

"Hi Joey! Grandma Roo said I could paint too!" Emma said cheerfully.

Joey's happy feeling suddenly changed. His chest felt tight and his face got hot. "But I was here first!" he said loudly. "These are MY paints!"

Emma's ears drooped sadly. "Oh... I thought we could share and take turns."

Joey felt angry bubbles in his tummy. He wanted to keep all the paints for himself. Without thinking, he grabbed the red paint jar and held it close. "No! Go away!"

But as Joey pulled the jar toward him, his elbow bumped the table. SPLASH! Red paint spilled all over Emma's white dress and the wooden floor.

"Oh no!" Emma cried, looking at her messy dress.

Joey's anger melted away like ice cream in the sun. He felt terrible. His heart beat fast, and his eyes filled with tears.

"I... I'm sorry, Emma," Joey said in a small voice. "I was being mean because I didn't want to share. I made a big mess."

Emma wiped her eyes with a clean part of her dress. "It's okay, Joey. But why didn't you want to share?"

Joey sat down on his bouncy tail and took a deep breath, just like Grandma Roo taught him when he felt upset. "I was excited about painting, and when you came, I got scared there wouldn't be enough paint for me. But that was silly."

"We could paint together!" Emma said kindly. "Taking turns is more fun than playing alone."

Joey nodded. "You're right. And I should tell Grandma Roo the truth about the spill. Being honest is important, even when I'm scared."

Together, they hopped downstairs. Joey's voice shook a little as he told Grandma Roo exactly what happened.

"Thank you for being honest, Joey," Grandma Roo said, giving him a warm hug. "Everyone makes mistakes. What matters is telling the truth and making things better."

They cleaned up the red paint together. Then Grandma Roo found Emma a painting smock, and both friends went back to the quiet attic.

"You paint the sky, and I'll paint the ground!" Joey suggested happily.

"Great idea!" Emma agreed.

They took turns with each color, creating a beautiful picture together. The attic filled with their giggles and the sweet smell of paint.

As the afternoon sun made everything golden, Joey learned that being honest felt much better than hiding the truth, and sharing made everything twice as fun.

The End.
