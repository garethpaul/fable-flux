# Fox Kit's Busy Shed Workout

The morning sun peeked into the garden shed. It made little gold squares on the floor. A curious fox kit named Finn poked his nose inside. He sniffed the air. It smelled like clean wood, soft soil, and a little bit of lemon soap.

“Hello, shed,” Finn whispered. “What can we do today?”

A broom leaned against the wall. A jumble of flower pots made a clinky stack. A jump rope hung like a smile. Finn’s ears perked up. His paws felt bouncy.

“I want to move my body,” he said. “I want to play and help.”

Finn took a deep breath. In-breathe, out-breathe. His whiskers tickled. “I can start with exercise,” he decided. “Exercise helps me grow strong and calm.”

He saw the jump rope. “Hello, rope!” Finn giggled. He picked it up carefully. “Would you like to dance with me?” He put the rope on the floor and hopped over it. Hop! Hop! Hop! His paws made soft thumps. Dust sparkled in the sun.

“Good job, paws,” said Finn. “One, two, three!” He counted his hops. “Exercise can be fun.”

A tiny voice squeaked from a corner. “Please be careful,” said a mouseling. She was peeking from a shoe box. “I am Mindy. Loud bumps make me nervous.”

Finn sat down right away. “Oh! I am sorry, Mindy,” he said kindly. “I did not know you were there.”

“It’s okay,” Mindy said. “Thank you for stopping.”

Finn thought. “I can move and still be gentle. I can take turns and be kind.”

He looked around. “I will make a safe space,” he said with a nod. Finn used his initiative. He pushed the flower pots to one side. Clink, clink. He lined up the pots in a neat row. “Tall pot here, small pot there,” he hummed. He set the broom against a hook. He rolled the hose into a tidy circle. Swish, swish.

The shed felt bigger now. Mindy peeked out more. “You are organizing,” she said. “Thank you. It helps me feel calm.”

Finn smiled. “When we organize, we all feel better,” he said. “And we have room to move.”

He tried the jump rope again, softer this time. Whoosh. Tap. Whoosh. Tap. His paws went light and quiet. He counted in a whisper. “One, two, three, four.” His tail swished like a little flag. Finn felt warm and happy. His heart beat thump-thump in a good way.

“May I try?” asked Mindy, stepping closer.

Finn nodded. “Yes! We can take turns.” He made the rope into a tiny loop for her. Mindy did mini hops. Tip, tip, tip. Finn clapped softly. “Great job!”

A ladybug on a gardening glove waved a red wing. “I like slow moves,” she buzzed. “Can we do slow exercise too?”

“Yes,” said Finn. “We can be tolerant. We can like fast and slow.”

Finn led a stretch. “Hands—well, paws—up high,” he said. He reached to the sunbeam. Warmth touched his fur. “Now touch toes.” The floor felt cool. He rolled his shoulders. Circle, circle. He wiggled his nose. Wiggle-wiggle! Mindy and the ladybug copied him. They laughed.

Finn made a little plan. “We will do three things,” he said. “Hop, stretch, and carry.” He drew tiny shapes in the dust to show the steps. “We exercise, and we help the shed too!”

They started with hopping. Hop, hop. Then stretching. Reach, breathe, and smile. Then carrying: Finn moved a light box to a shelf. Mindy carried a paper clip. The ladybug nudged a tiny button. Plink.

Finn felt strong. He also felt kind. “We are a team,” he said. “We move our bodies. We help our space.”

Just then, a beetle rolled in, bumping a marble. “Excuse me,” he said. “I am slow. Do you have room for me?”

Finn nodded. “Yes. We made space together. You can join us.” He pointed to a soft spot on a mat. “You can roll the marble back and forth. That is good exercise too.” The beetle smiled and rolled, roll, roll, roll. The marble made a gentle click-click.

Soon, the shed was tidy. The air felt fresh, like mint leaves. The sunlight made a warm stripe on Finn’s back. He lay down for a rest. “Exercise makes my body happy,” he said. “It also helps me be calm and kind.”

Mindy brought a crumb of cracker. “For you,” she said. “Thank you for being careful.”

“Thank you,” said Finn. He broke the crumb and shared. “We share. We care.”

The ladybug tucked a tiny leaf under the rope so it wouldn’t slide. “Good idea,” said Finn. “You used initiative too!”

They all listened to the quiet. They heard birds outside. Tweet-tweet. They heard the hose drip. Plip. They heard their own breathing. In. Out.

Finn stood again. “Before we go, let’s put things back.” He coiled the rope on its hook. Mindy closed the shoe box door softly. The beetle parked the marble in a little cup. Everything looked neat.

Finn smiled big. “Today we learned to move our bodies, be kind, and help our space,” he said. “Exercise can be hopping, stretching, and carrying. It can be slow or fast. We can include everyone.”

Mindy nodded. “When we organize, the shed feels safe.”

The ladybug buzzed. “When we are kind, hearts feel light.”

“And when we start with a good idea,” said Finn, “we can make a great day.”

They bumped paws and tiny hands. Tap, tap, tap.

Outside, the sun was higher. Finn took one more deep breath. “I feel strong and happy,” he said.

The End.
