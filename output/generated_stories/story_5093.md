# Sunny Porch Chores

The sun was soft and warm on the peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like clean wood and sweet flowers. A kind mouse named Miri stood on the top step. Her whiskers wiggled. Her tiny broom was ready.

“Good morning, porch,” Miri said. “Today, I do my chores!”

She took a deep breath. She felt brave. She felt helpful. She wanted to make the porch tidy and safe.

Miri looked around. There were leaves on the floor and crumbs from yesterday’s snack. The watering can was on its side. The shoe basket was messy. On a small table sat a bowl of fruit from friends—bright red apples, yellow bananas, and tiny tart kumquats. A colorful woven mat lay by the door. It was a gift from her neighbor, Lala the lark, whose family came from far away. The mat had patterns like waves and little suns.

“First, sweep,” Miri said. She held the broom with both paws. Swish, swish, swish. The broom made a soft whoosh sound. Dust danced in the light like tiny sparkles.

Her friend Tim the turtle peeked from behind a flower pot. “Hi, Miri,” he said slowly. “Do you need help?”

“Yes, please,” said Miri. “Chores are better with friends.”

Tim nodded and put on his sun hat. “Safety first,” he said. “Hats and water.”

Miri smiled. “Safety first,” she echoed. She tied a bright scarf around her neck so the sun wouldn’t tickle her too much. She sipped water from a little cup. Slurp!

They started to work. Swish, swish. Sweep the leaves into a neat little pile. Crunch, crunch. The leaves sounded crispy. A gentle breeze said hello and carried one leaf onto Tim’s nose. He giggled.

Next, Miri picked up the watering can. “We will water the porch plants,” she said. The can felt heavy. She tried to lift it. “Oof!” It was too full.

“Try again,” said Tim. “You can pour some out first.”

Miri thought. She looked brave again. “I can do hard things,” she whispered. She poured a little water into a bowl for the birds. She tried again. Lift! This time, it worked.

They watered the pink flowers and the green herbs. Drip, drip, drip. The porch smelled like mint and sunshine. A small bee buzzed by and then buzzed away. “Hello, bee. We give you space,” said Miri kindly. Safety first.

A cheerful voice sang from the sidewalk. “Buenos días!” It was Ana the ant and her cousin Boon the beetle. Ana wore a tiny apron with bright orange stripes. Boon wore a little cap with moon shapes.

“Buenos días means good morning,” Ana said with a smile.

“Good morning!” Miri said back. “We are doing porch chores. Do you want to join?”

“Sí!” said Ana. “I love to sort.” Boon nodded. “I can stack.”

They all washed their paws at the porch sink. Scrub, scrub. “Clean paws for clean chores,” said Miri.

Ana sorted the shoe basket. Tap, tap, tap. Little shoes on the left, big shoes on the right. “In my home, we take off shoes to keep floors clean,” she said. “We do that too,” said Tim. “It keeps us safe from slips.”

Boon stacked the fruit with gentle claws. “In my family,” he said softly, “we share fruit after work.” He balanced a banana on an apple. Wobble. “Careful,” said Miri. “Two paws, steady.” Boon tried again. Steady, steady. He smiled. “I did it.”

Miri wiped the small table with a warm, soapy cloth. Wipe, wipe. The bubbles shined like tiny rainbows. “This is hard,” she said when a sticky spot didn’t go away.

“Try, try again,” said Tim. “Growth mind-set,” added Ana. She said the words slowly so everyone could say them too.

“Growth mindset,” Miri repeated. She pressed gently and moved the cloth in little circles. The sticky spot disappeared. “I did it!” she cheered.

They put the colorful woven mat back by the door. Miri patted it smooth. “Lala’s family made this. It reminds me of songs from far away,” she said.

Boon ran a tiny finger over the pattern. “In my culture,” he said, “we weave baskets with shapes like this.” Ana nodded. “We can all share and learn.”

Suddenly, a small clatter! A bucket rolled near the steps. Miri held out her paw. “Stop,” she said calmly. “We check for safety.” Everyone paused. Miri set the bucket upright and moved it to the side where it could not roll. “Safe now,” she said. She felt proud.

When the chores were almost done, Miri looked at the leaf pile. “Who wants to jump?” she asked.

“Me!” said everyone.

“One at a time, and not near the steps,” said Miri. “Safety first.”

They took turns. Rustle, rustle! Leaves tickled their toes. They laughed and clapped.

At last, the porch was clean. The air smelled fresh. The mat was straight. The shoes were sorted. The plants were happy. The friends sat together on the step. The sun felt like a warm hug.

Miri passed the fruit bowl. “Thank you,” she said to Ana and Boon. “Gracias,” said Ana. “Khop khun,” said Boon. “That means thank you,” he explained.

“Thank you,” said Tim. “We did our chores. We did them safely. We learned from each other.”

Miri nodded. “Chores make our home nice. Chores can be fun with friends. We can try, try again. And we can share ideas from many places.”

Everyone took a bite. Crunch went the apple. Sweet went the banana. Tart went the tiny kumquat. Their smiles were big and bright.

Miri looked at her shiny porch and her kind friends. Her heart felt light. “Today,” she said softly, “we cleaned, we cared, and we grew.”

The End.
