# Momo Listens to the Room

Momo was a small, courageous monkey with bright, curious eyes. He lived in a comfortable nursery with soft rugs, colorful pillows, and shelves filled with books and blocks. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. The air smelled like clean blankets and sweet banana snacks.

One morning, Momo wanted to build the tallest tower. The blocks were smooth and cool in his hands. Tap, tap, tap went the blocks as he stacked them. The tower wobbled like jelly.

“I will make it reach the stars!” Momo said.

His friend, Nurse Nia, smiled. She wore a cozy sweater the color of lavender. “Momo, before you build, let’s listen,” she said softly. “The room can tell us what it needs.”

Momo giggled. “Rooms don’t talk,” he said.

“Not with words,” Nia said. “With sounds and clues. Listening helps us learn.”

Momo shrugged and kept stacking. Tap, tap, tap. The tower grew high. It began to lean. Momo stepped back. The tower leaned more. Then—crash! The blocks scattered like popcorn on the rug.

Momo’s shoulders dropped. His ears drooped. “Oh no,” he whispered. “My tower is broken.”

Nurse Nia sat beside him on a soft pillow. “I heard a wobble sound,” she said kindly. “Did you hear it too?”

Momo thought. He remembered the tiny tap-tap-wiggle. He nodded slowly. “I heard a wiggly sound.”

“That sound was the room telling us something,” Nia said. “It was saying, ‘This tower isn’t ready yet.’ Listening is brave. Listening helps us do things better.”

Momo took a deep breath. He felt the soft rug under his toes. He felt the cool block in his hands. He looked around and tried to listen.

He heard the hum of the little nightlight. Hmmmm. He heard the gentle whoosh of the air vent. Whoooosh. He heard the clock go tick, tick, tick. He even heard a bird outside say, “Cheep-cheep!”

“I hear a lot,” Momo said.

“What do the sounds tell us?” Nia asked.

Momo listened again. Near the window, the whoosh felt stronger. “The air is pushy there,” he said. “It might push the tower.”

“Good listening,” Nia said.

Momo walked to the wide, quiet corner by the bookshelf. It felt still. “Here,” he said. “It is calm.”

He placed a flat, heavy book under the first block. “A strong base,” he said proudly.

Nia clapped softly. “That is smart and safe.”

Momo stacked slowly this time. He touched each block and waited. He listened for the wiggly sound. He pressed his ear close: quiet and steady. He smiled. “The room says yes,” he whispered.

A tiny mouse puppet peeked from the puppet basket. Momo made the puppet squeak, “Squeak-squeak, what about me?”

“The mouse wants a window,” Momo giggled. He added a small block shelf near the top.

He stacked higher. Tap, pause, listen. Tap, pause, listen. When he heard a small wobble, he stopped. He looked around, then had a new idea.

“I will make triangle feet!” he said. He built little supports on each side. The tower felt stronger. It stood tall and straight, like a proud tree.

Nia sniffed the air. “Do you smell something nice?” she asked.

Momo sniffed. Mmm. “Banana muffins,” he said. “Snack time soon!”

“The clock and the smell told us,” Nia said. “You listened with your nose and your ears.”

Momo laughed. “I like listening.”

Just then, a soft “thump-thump” came from the hallway. The door opened a crack. A breeze puffed in. The tower wiggled just a little.

Momo put his hand on the tower. “Shh, tower,” he said. He listened to the breeze. He had another idea. He opened a big picture book and placed it like a gentle wall beside the tower, blocking the wind.

“An invention!” Nia said. “A wind wall.”

Momo’s eyes shone. “I helped the room be quiet for my tower.”

They waited. The tower stood tall and calm. The mouse puppet had a long view to the window. The nursery felt cozy and proud.

“You did it,” Nia said. “How did you do it?”

Momo held up his hands and wiggled his ears. “I used my hands. I used my ears. I used my nose. I listened to the room. I learned new ways. I made a wind wall. I made triangle feet. I made a strong base.”

Nia nodded. “That is listening. That is learning. That is innovation.”

Momo’s heart felt warm, like a hug. “Listening is brave,” he said. “It helps me try smart ways.”

Nia gave him a small banana muffin. It was soft and sweet and warm. Momo took a bite. “Mmm,” he said. “My tower tastes like winning!”

They both laughed. The clock ticked gently. The light was golden and soft. Momo patted the tower and whispered, “Thank you, room.”

He turned to the puppets and blocks. “Friends,” he said, “we will always listen first.”

And the nursery felt even more comfortable, like it was smiling.

The End.
