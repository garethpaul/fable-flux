# Chirpy's Clean-Up Song

Chirpy was a bright yellow bird who loved to sing. She lived in Dr. Kim's basement lab, where bubbling sounds came from colorful bottles and bright lights twinkled like stars.

The lab was very messy. Papers were scattered everywhere like fallen leaves. Test tubes rolled around on tables. Books were piled up in wobbly towers.

"Tweet, tweet! This place is so messy!" sang Chirpy. She flapped her tiny wings and looked around with her shiny black eyes.

Dr. Kim worked at her desk, but she looked tired and worried. "Oh dear," she sighed. "I can't find my important notebook anywhere. And I need it for my experiment today!"

Chirpy watched Dr. Kim search through the messy piles. Papers rustled loudly as they fell to the floor. Glass bottles clinked together dangerously.

"Maybe I can help!" chirped Chirpy. She had never cleaned before, but she wanted to try something new.

Dr. Kim looked up in surprise. "You want to help organize, little bird? That's very kind of you!"

Chirpy started small. She picked up one pencil in her beak and placed it in a cup. "Tweet! One pencil in its home!" she sang happily.

The pencil fit perfectly in the cup. It felt good to put things where they belonged.

"What a wonderful idea!" said Dr. Kim. "Let's work together!"

They made it into a fun game. Chirpy sang a cleaning song: "Papers here, bottles there, everything has a special care!"

Dr. Kim sorted the papers into neat stacks. Chirpy helped by holding down the corners with her little feet. The papers smelled like vanilla and felt smooth.

Next, they organized the test tubes. Dr. Kim placed them carefully in a special rack. "We must be very gentle," she explained. "These could break and hurt us."

Chirpy nodded seriously. Safety was important too.

They found homes for everything. Books went on shelves from big to small. Colorful bottles lined up like a rainbow. Tools had their own special drawer.

As they worked, something magical happened. The lab began to sparkle! Sunlight from the small window made everything gleam.

"Look!" chirped Chirpy excitedly. "There's your notebook!"

The important notebook was sitting neatly on the now-clean desk, right where it belonged.

Dr. Kim clapped her hands with joy. "Chirpy, you're amazing! I feel so much better now. When everything is organized, I can think clearly and work safely."

Chirpy felt proud and happy. Her feathers puffed up with excitement. "I learned something new today! Organizing isn't just about cleaning - it helps us feel good too!"

Dr. Kim smiled warmly. "You're absolutely right. Taking care of our space is taking care of ourselves."

From that day on, Chirpy sang her organizing song every morning. The lab stayed neat and tidy. Dr. Kim could find everything she needed, and she had more energy for her important work.

"Tweet, tweet! Clean and bright, everything feels just right!" Chirpy sang as golden sunlight filled their beautiful, organized lab.

Both friends learned that being open to new ideas and working together made everything better. And keeping things organized helped them feel happy, safe, and ready for each new day.

The End.
