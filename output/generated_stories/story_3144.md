# Rooftop Tea with Kimi Koala

Kimi was a calm koala with soft, gray fur and big, gentle eyes. She liked quiet places. Her favorite spot was the rooftop garden, where the sun felt warm and the wind smelled like flowers and fresh rain. The rooftop had little pots of mint, tiny orange trees, and a blue bench that looked out over the town.

One bright morning, Kimi climbed the safe ladder, step by step. “Breathe in, breathe out,” she hummed, feeling the soft breeze tickle her nose. She carried a little basket with eucalyptus leaves, a teapot, and cups. “Today I will have tea and listen to the city,” she said.

On the rooftop, she heard music drifting up. Ting ting! Tap tap! It was coming from the next building’s rooftop. Kimi peeked over and saw two neighbors. One was a boy named Leo, wearing a red cap. He had a small drum. The other was a girl named Asha, wearing a yellow scarf. She had a stringed instrument that looked like a small guitar. “Hi!” Kimi waved. “The breeze brings your music. It sounds nice.”

“Hi, Kimi!” Leo called. “I’m playing a marching beat. Boom, boom, tap!”

“Hello,” Asha said with a smile. “I’m playing a gentle raga. Pling, pling, plaaang.”

They both began to play, but soon the sounds bumped into each other. Boom tap boom! Pling pling plaaang! The music got louder. Leo frowned. Asha frowned, too.

“You’re too loud!” Leo said, banging his drum harder.

“Your beat is chasing my melody,” Asha replied, plucking faster.

Kimi felt a little squeeze in her chest. The wind seemed to stop. The oranges on the tree were very still. This was a conflict. Kimi knew conflicts can happen when people want different things. She took a slow breath. “Breathe in. Breathe out,” she whispered.

Kimi set her basket down and spoke softly. “Friends, I hear two beautiful sounds. But they are not happy together yet.”

Leo crossed his arms. “I want to practice my parade beat.”

Asha hugged her instrument. “I want to practice my song for my family.”

Kimi nodded. “Both are important. Both are special.” She climbed over to their rooftop, carefully and calmly. “May I show you a way to work together?”

Leo and Asha looked at each other, then at Kimi. They both nodded a little.

Kimi smiled. “First, let’s use our calm voices.” She placed her paw on her chest. “We can say what we need.” She looked at Leo. “Leo, can you tell Asha what you need, using kind words?”

Leo took a breath. “I need to practice my drum for the school parade,” he said. “The beat helps me remember the steps.”

Kimi turned to Asha. “Asha, can you tell Leo what you need, using kind words?”

Asha nodded. “I need to practice a raga for a festival. It is from my family’s culture. It helps me feel close to my grandma. It is gentle.”

Kimi’s eyes were warm. “Thank you for sharing. Now we understand each other. You both have music with meaning.” The breeze began to flutter again. The mint leaves smelled cool and sweet.

“Maybe we can take turns,” Leo offered. “I play for a little time. Then Asha plays.”

“That is a good idea,” Kimi said. “Taking turns is one way to solve a conflict.”

Asha thought for a moment. “Maybe we can also try to play together,” she said softly. “I can make my notes match your beat.”

Kimi clapped her paws quietly. “Another good idea! Working together is a kind way to solve a conflict. Let’s choose what to try first.”

They decided to start with taking turns. Kimi set her teapot like a timer. “When the pot whistles softly, we switch,” she said. “Leo first, then Asha.”

Leo played: boom, boom, tap. The sound was bright and bouncy. Kimi swayed and smiled. When the teapot gave a tiny hiss, they switched.

Asha played: pling, pling, plaaang. The sound was smooth and shining, like sun on water. Kimi closed her eyes and breathed in the sweetness of the orange tree.

After a few turns, Leo said, “I like your melody. It feels like a story.”

Asha smiled. “I like your beat. It feels like footsteps.”

Kimi’s ears perked up. “Would you like to try together now? We can count. One, two, three, four.” She tapped her paw softly.

Leo kept a gentle beat. Boom... tap... boom... tap. Asha danced her notes around the beat. Pling-pling, plaaang. The music met in the middle, like two friends holding hands. The wind hummed along. The city sounded happy.

Neighbors peeped from windows. “What a lovely song!” someone said. The mint leaves waved. The oranges glowed warm and sweet.

When they finished, Leo and Asha laughed. “We did it!” they cheered.

Kimi poured tea. “Thank you for trying,” she said. “How do you feel?”

“I feel proud,” Leo said. “I learned to listen.”

“I feel calm,” Asha said. “I learned to share space.”

Kimi nodded. “Today we had a conflict. We solved it with kind words, listening, taking turns, and working together. We also learned about each other’s music and culture. That helped us understand.”

They clinked cups gently. The tea smelled like leaves and sunshine. “To music, to friendship, and to peace,” Kimi said.

The sun began to set. The sky turned pink and gold. Kimi packed her basket. “Breathe in, breathe out,” she hummed. She felt light and happy.

On the rooftop, the world felt big and friendly. Leo beat a soft goodbye. Tap tap. Asha played a tiny tune. Pling.

Kimi waved. “See you tomorrow,” she said. “We can make more music together.”

And the rooftop garden whispered back with a warm, sweet breeze.

The End.
