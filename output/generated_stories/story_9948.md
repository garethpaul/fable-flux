# Joey's Big World Discovery

Joey the kangaroo was bouncing on Mama's peaceful front porch. The morning sun felt warm on his soft brown fur. He loved the way the wooden boards creaked under his little feet when he hopped.

"Bounce, bounce, bounce!" Joey giggled. "I'm the best hopper in the whole world!"

Just then, a colorful bird landed on the porch railing. The bird had bright red feathers and a yellow beak.

"Hello there!" chirped the bird. "I'm Carlos from Mexico. I flew a very long way to visit your neighborhood."

Joey stopped bouncing. "Mexico? Where's that?"

"It's far across the big world," Carlos explained. "In my home, children hop and play just like you! But we speak Spanish. We say 'hola' instead of 'hello.'"

Joey's eyes grew wide. "There are other children who hop like me?"

Before Carlos could answer, a small gray elephant walked up the porch steps. She looked very tired.

"Excuse me," the elephant said softly. "I'm Asha from India. I've been walking for hours looking for water. Do you know where I can find some?"

Joey tilted his head. "India? Is that near Mexico?"

"Oh no," Asha smiled. "India is on the other side of the world from Mexico. In my country, children love to exercise and play, just like you!"

Joey felt confused. "But I thought I was the only one who loved to hop and play!"

Carlos and Asha looked at each other sadly. Joey realized he had been thinking only about himself.

"I'm sorry," Joey said, his ears drooping. "I didn't know there were children everywhere who liked the same things as me."

"That's okay," Asha said kindly. "Now you're learning about our big, wonderful world!"

Joey's mama appeared with a pitcher of cool water and some crackers. "Would you like to share these with our new friends, Joey?"

Joey's face lit up. "Yes! Carlos and Asha, please have some!"

As they shared the snacks, Carlos taught Joey to say "hola" and "gracias." Asha showed him how children in India wave hello with both hands pressed together.

"This is amazing!" Joey bounced excitedly. "Children all over the world like to play and learn!"

"That's right," said Mama. "We're all part of one big world family. When we're kind to others from different places, we make the whole world happier."

Joey practiced his new Spanish words. "Hola, Carlos! Gracias, Asha!"

Carlos clapped his wings. "¡Muy bien! Very good!"

Asha smiled. "In India, we say 'namaste' to show respect to friends."

"Namaste!" Joey tried, pressing his little paws together.

As the sun climbed higher, Joey realized something wonderful. The world was much bigger than his front porch, and it was full of friends he hadn't met yet.

"I want to learn about children everywhere!" Joey declared. "And I want to share my crackers with all of them!"

Mama hugged Joey tight. "That's what being a good world citizen means, sweetie. Caring about everyone, everywhere."

Joey bounced one more time, but now his hops felt different. Each bounce reminded him of all the children around the world who were probably bouncing too.

The End.
