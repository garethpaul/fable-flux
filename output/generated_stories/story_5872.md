# Benny Beaver Builds a Balcony Garden

Benny was a busy beaver with bright eyes and a big, warm smile. He lived with his family in a cozy home high above the city. Right outside their door was a small balcony garden. There were pots, little boxes of soil, and a tiny water can with a rainbow sticker.

Each morning, the balcony smelled like wet dirt and mint leaves. The sun felt soft and golden on Benny’s fur. The city hummed below, like a quiet drum.

“Today, I will build something helpful,” Benny said. His tail thumped gently. Thump, thump. “I want the plants to be happy.”

Benny looked around. The tomato plant leaned to one side. The basil wiggled in the breeze. The strawberry plant reached for the sun. “They need a trellis,” Benny said. “A trellis is a little frame. It helps plants climb.”

He had sticks, soft twine, and tiny clips. He also had a little plan in his head.

Mama Beaver opened the sliding door. “Good morning, Benny,” she said. “What are you building today?”

“A trellis!” Benny chirped. “I will help the plants stand tall.”

“That sounds wonderful,” Mama said. “Remember to slow down and breathe when you work.”

Benny nodded. “I can do that.”

He set the sticks on the floor. Tap, tap. He lined them up. Straight, straight. He wiggled one stick into the soil. Squish. The soil was cool on his paws. He smiled.

“Stick, stay tall,” he whispered.

Benny tied twine to the stick. He pulled too hard. Snap! The twine popped. Benny’s ears drooped. His tail went flop.

“I want it to be perfect now,” he grumbled. He felt his face get tight. His tummy felt hot.

Mama peeked out. “How is it going?”

“I broke the twine,” Benny said. “I want it, and I want it now.”

Mama nodded. “That feeling is big,” she said. “Let’s name it.”

“Frus… fra… frustrated,” Benny said slowly.

“Yes,” Mama said. “When we feel frustrated, we can pause. Let’s try a self-regulation trick. Ready?”

Benny nodded.

“Put your paw on your belly,” Mama said. “Breathe in like you smell a flower. Sniff. Breathe out like you blow a bubble. Whooo.”

Benny tried. Sniff… whooo. Again. Sniff… whooo. His tummy felt softer. His face felt calm.

“I can try again,” Benny said. His eyes were bright again. “I can build step by step.”

He picked up a new piece of twine. “Gentle pulls,” he told himself. “Left paw, right paw. Slow and steady.”

He looped the twine around the stick. Soft, soft. He tied a knot. Pull, pull. Not too tight. The knot held firm.

“Smart move,” Mama said with a smile. “You are building with care.”

Benny added a second stick. Squish. He tied more twine. He made a little ladder. The tomato plant touched the twine with a tiny green curl.

“Climb, little friend,” Benny whispered. “This is for you.”

A breeze danced through the balcony. Benny felt cool air on his whiskers. He heard a bee buzz past the marigolds. Bzzzz. The city sounds hummed below. Honk, rumble, ding.

Benny looked at the strawberry plant. “You need shade,” he said. “The sun is too strong at noon.”

He looked at the cloth bag the carrots came in. “I can use that,” he said. His tail thumped lightly. “Innovation!” He grinned at the big word.

He clipped the soft bag to two sticks and made a tiny shade screen. He tested it with his paw. “A little shade, not too much.” The strawberry leaves looked cool and happy.

Next, Benny saw the watering can. He remembered he once poured too fast and made a muddy mess. Today he wanted balance.

“I will count,” Benny said. “One, two, three little pours.”

He breathed in like a flower and out like a bubble. Then he poured: tip, tip, tip. The soil drank the water with a quiet gulp. Glug, glug.

“Good job, me,” Benny whispered. He felt proud and calm.

He checked his work. The trellis stood tall. The twine was snug and kind. The shade screen fluttered gently. The plants looked cozy, like they were hugging the garden.

Mama came back with two tiny cups of cool water. “For my builder,” she said.

Benny took a sip. The water tasted fresh. “I built with my brain and my breath,” he said. “I slowed down. I tried new ideas. I helped the plants.”

Mama nodded. “You used self-regulation,” she said. “You also used innovation when you made the shade.”

Benny’s heart felt warm. He patted the tomato plant softly. “Thank you for growing,” he said.

A small green tomato peeked from a leaf. Benny giggled. “Hello, little tomato!”

He gathered his sticks and twine neatly. He put the clips in a tiny box. He swept the floor with a tiny brush. Swish, swish.

“I like building,” Benny said. “Building takes time. Building takes care. When I slow down, I can do big things.”

Mama hugged him. “You built more than a trellis,” she said. “You built patience and ideas.”

Benny looked at the bright sky. He felt calm and strong. “Tomorrow,” he said, “I will build a bug hotel.”

Mama smiled. “Step by step,” she said.

“Step by step,” Benny agreed. His tail made a happy thump.

The plants swayed in the soft breeze. The balcony smelled like mint and sunshine. Benny sat with Mama and watched the leaves wiggle up the twine.

“We built a kind place,” Benny whispered.

“Yes,” Mama said. “And you built it with your heart.”

The End.
