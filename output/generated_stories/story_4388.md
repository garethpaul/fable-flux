# Pip's Big Voice

Pip was a small gray mouse who lived in a cozy home office. Her tiny paws were soft, and her whiskers twitched when she was thinking hard.

Every day, Pip watched the family work at the big wooden desk. She loved the clicking sounds of the keyboard and the scratching of pencils on paper. But Pip had a problem.

The office was very messy! Papers scattered everywhere. Pencils rolled off the desk. Books lay open in tall, wobbly stacks.

"This mess makes it hard to find anything," Pip squeaked quietly to herself. But her voice was so small, nobody heard her.

Pip wanted to help make the office neat and tidy. She had lots of good ideas! But every time she tried to speak up, her voice came out as the tiniest whisper.

One morning, Pip saw Dad searching everywhere for his reading glasses.

"Where could they be?" Dad muttered, moving papers around.

Pip knew exactly where they were! She had seen them under a pile of magazines. But when she tried to tell Dad, only a soft "squeak" came out.

Dad couldn't hear her at all.

Pip felt frustrated. Her little heart beat fast. "I need to be brave and speak louder," she told herself.

That afternoon, Pip made a plan. She found a small acorn and practiced speaking into it like a microphone.

"Hello! Hello!" she said, making her voice bigger and stronger each time. "I have something important to say!"

She practiced all day long. Her voice grew clearer and more confident with each try.

The next morning, Mom was looking for her favorite pen.

"I can't find my blue pen anywhere!" Mom sighed.

Pip took a deep breath. She remembered her practice. Standing tall on her tiny paws, she called out in her clearest, strongest voice:

"Excuse me! I can help you!"

Mom looked around, surprised. "Did someone say something?"

"Down here!" Pip called again, waving her little paws. "I'm Pip the mouse, and I know where your pen is!"

Mom looked down and gasped. "Oh my! A talking mouse!"

"Yes!" said Pip proudly. "Your blue pen rolled behind the computer yesterday. I saw it happen!"

Mom carefully moved the computer and found her pen. "You're absolutely right! Thank you so much, Pip!"

Pip's whiskers twitched with happiness. "I have more ideas to help organize the office too!"

"I'd love to hear them," said Mom, sitting down to listen.

Pip shared all her wonderful ideas. She suggested small boxes for paper clips, a special tray for pens, and neat stacks for important papers.

"These are fantastic ideas, Pip!" said Mom. "Would you like to be our official Office Helper?"

"Yes, please!" squeaked Pip joyfully.

From that day on, Pip helped keep the office organized. She learned that speaking up with confidence helped everyone. Her small voice could make a big difference when she was brave enough to use it.

Dad and Mom always listened to Pip's helpful suggestions. The office became the neatest, most organized room in the whole house.

Pip felt proud and happy. She had learned that being assertive meant speaking up for good ideas, even when you feel small. With practice and planning, anyone can find their strong voice.

"Sometimes the smallest voices have the biggest ideas," Pip would say with a smile.

The End.
