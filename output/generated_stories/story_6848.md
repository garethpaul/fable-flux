# Benji Bear Cub’s Shed Surprise

Benji was a friendly bear cub with soft, fuzzy fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, he padded across the grass to the garden shed. The shed smelled like fresh wood and warm earth. Sunbeams made tiny dust specks sparkle like stars.

Benji wiggled his nose. “Today, I will make something new!” he said. He opened the squeaky door—eeeek!—and peered inside. He saw boxes, jars, old paper rolls, and a big basket.

Mama Bear stood by the doorway with a smile. “Good morning, Benji,” she said. “In the shed, we keep things we can use again. That is called recycling.”

“Re-cy-cling,” Benji repeated slowly. The word felt big and bouncy in his mouth.

Mama Bear pointed to three color labels on the shelves. “Green is for glass, blue is for paper, and yellow is for plastic. We follow directions so everything goes in the right place.”

Benji nodded. “I can follow directions. I can practice!”

He stepped inside. The floor felt a little cool under his paws. A soft breeze smelled like flowers. Benji saw a clear jar in the wrong spot. It sat on the blue paper shelf.

“Oh!” Benji said. “This glass jar belongs on green.” He picked it up carefully. The jar felt smooth and chilly. He placed it on the green shelf. Clink! It made a gentle sound.

“Good sorting,” Mama Bear said. “Recycling helps us care for our world.”

Benji smiled. He found a cardboard tube, some paper, and a clean milk jug. He had an idea. “I want to make a music shaker!”

Mama Bear nodded. “Great idea. First, we practice safety. Ask first. Then use things the right way.”

“May I use the milk jug, the paper, and the beans?” Benji asked.

“Yes,” said Mama Bear. “Here are the directions: One, rinse the jug. Two, add beans. Three, close the lid tight. Four, decorate with paper. We will recycle the extra pieces.”

Benji washed the jug. Splash, splash! The water was cool and tickly. He scooped beans with a soft plop-plop-plop and poured them in. He twisted the lid. Twist, twist. Tight!

He tore bright paper into little shapes. Rip, rip. He glued the paper onto the jug and pressed with his paw. Press, press. The glue smelled sweet like flowers. His shaker looked colorful and happy.

“Shake, shake!” Benji said. The sound went chika-chika-chika! He bounced on his paws.

“That’s wonderful,” Mama Bear said. “You followed directions and practiced each step.”

Benji looked at the floor. Little paper bits were scattered like confetti. He frowned. “Oh no. It’s messy.”

Mama Bear’s voice was gentle. “We can clean up. Cleaning is part of being kind. And we recycle what we can.”

Benji took a deep breath. He picked up the paper bits. “Paper goes in blue,” he said. Drop, drop. He found a small plastic ring. “Plastic goes in yellow.” Plink. He found a tiny glass bead. “Glass goes in green.” Clink.

He wiped the table with a damp cloth. Swish, swish. The shed smelled clean and fresh again. Benji’s shoulders relaxed.

“Now I want to make a robot,” Benji said, eyes shining. He spotted a box, two bottle caps, and a paper roll.

Mama Bear smiled. “Let’s practice again. Ask first.”

“May I use the box, the caps, and the roll?” Benji asked.

“Yes,” Mama Bear said. “Directions: One, build the body. Two, add the head. Three, attach arms. Four, decorate.”

Benji set the box on the table. “Body.” He placed the roll on top. “Head.” He tapped the bottle caps for eyes. Tap, tap. He drew a big friendly smile with a marker. The scent was a little sharp. He added arm tubes. Rustle, rustle. He stood back and grinned.

“Hello, Recycle Robot!” Benji said. He gave a gentle hug. The cardboard felt dry and crinkly.

“Benji,” Mama Bear said, “you used old things in a new way. That is creativity. You followed directions. You practiced. And you recycled.”

Benji’s heart felt warm. He looked around the neat shed. The green, blue, and yellow shelves were tidy. The sun made his shaker shine. Chika-chika! The robot smiled back.

“I learned a big thing,” Benji said. “Recycling helps our world. Following directions keeps me safe. Practice makes me better. And being creative is fun!”

Mama Bear nodded. “Yes. Little steps make a big difference.”

Benji placed his shaker and robot on a low shelf. He closed the glue and put the marker away. Then he checked the floor one more time. All clean.

“Can we show Papa?” Benji asked.

“We can,” Mama Bear said. “And we can sing a recycling song.”

Benji sang softly as they walked: “Sort, sort, sort it right. Green, blue, yellow, clean and bright. Practice, follow, try again. Caring for our world, my friend.”

They stepped out of the shed. The air felt warm and kind. Birds chirped. Benji held Mama’s paw. He felt proud, calm, and happy.

The End.
