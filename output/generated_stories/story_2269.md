# Ellie's Big Voice

In a cozy nursery filled with soft blankets and colorful toys, lived a gentle elephant calf named Ellie. Her nursery had puffy clouds painted on the ceiling and warm sunshine streaming through the windows. Ellie loved her special room, but today she felt worried about something very important.

Ellie's tummy had been hurting for two whole days. The pain felt like little butterflies fluttering around inside her belly. She wanted to tell someone, but she felt too shy to speak up. Her mama and papa were always so busy taking care of the other baby animals in their care center.

"Maybe my tummy will feel better tomorrow," Ellie whispered to her favorite stuffed bunny. She curled up on her soft blue blanket, hoping the hurt would go away.

The next morning, Ellie's mama noticed her little elephant wasn't eating her breakfast of sweet bananas and crunchy apples.

"Good morning, my sweet Ellie," Mama said gently, her warm trunk touching Ellie's forehead. "You seem quiet today. Is everything okay?"

Ellie looked down at her food bowl. Her tummy rumbled, but not in a hungry way. She wanted to tell Mama about the fluttery feeling, but the words felt stuck in her throat like peanut butter.

"I'm... I'm okay, Mama," Ellie said softly, even though she wasn't really okay at all.

Papa Elephant walked over with his kind, crinkly eyes. "Ellie, you know you can always talk to us about anything, right? We love you very much."

Ellie's ears drooped. She realized that by not telling her parents about her tummy ache, she wasn't helping herself feel better. She also wasn't letting her parents do their job of taking care of her.

Taking a deep breath that filled her little lungs with courage, Ellie looked up at her parents.

"Mama, Papa," she said in her small but brave voice, "my tummy has been hurting for two days. It feels like butterflies are dancing inside, but not the happy kind."

Mama's eyes filled with love and concern. "Oh, my precious Ellie! Thank you so much for telling us. That was very brave and very important."

Papa gently rubbed Ellie's round belly with his trunk. "Let's call Dr. Giraffe right away. She'll help us figure out how to make your tummy feel all better."

Dr. Giraffe arrived with her long neck and gentle smile. She listened to Ellie's tummy with a special tool called a stethoscope. It felt cool and smooth against Ellie's gray skin.

"I think Ellie just ate too many sweet treats yesterday," Dr. Giraffe explained. "Some warm chamomile tea and rest will help her feel much better."

As Mama prepared the soothing tea that smelled like flowers, Ellie felt so grateful she had found her voice.

"Thank you for listening to me," Ellie said to her parents. "I was scared to tell you, but now I feel better already!"

"Using your words to tell us when something is wrong is one of the most important things you can do," Papa explained. "It helps us take care of you, and it helps you take care of yourself too."

That evening, as Ellie sipped her warm tea and snuggled in her cozy blanket, her tummy felt much better. She learned that speaking up when something is wrong isn't just good for her – it's good for everyone who loves her.

"I promise to always use my big voice when I need help," Ellie said sleepily.

And from that day forward, Ellie always remembered that communication is like a special gift we give to the people who care about us.

The End.
