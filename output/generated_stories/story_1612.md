# Nutty Professor Niko Rides to School

The morning sun peeked through the leaves as a little squirrel named Niko scampered down a tree. He wore a tiny blue backpack and a bright yellow cap. Today, Niko was riding in the family car with Mia and her dad. Niko was a resourceful squirrel who loved to learn, help, and make plans.

“Hop in, Niko!” said Mia, opening the car door. The seat felt soft under Niko’s paws, and the car smelled like lemons and clean cloth. The seatbelt clicked with a happy sound.

“Click! Safety first,” Niko said with a grin. “Seatbelts are part of responsibility!”

Mia giggled. “You sound like a teacher.”

Niko puffed his chest. “I am the Nutty Professor Niko! Today, we learn everywhere—even in the car!”

Dad started the engine. It hummed like a sleepy bee. The car rolled forward. Sunlight danced on the dashboard. Outside, trees swished and birds chirped.

Mia pulled out a sticky snack bar. “I’m hungry.”

Niko sniffed the air. “I smell peanut butter,” he said, twitching his nose. “Yum! But look, your hands are a little sticky from breakfast.”

Mia looked at her fingers. “Oh! They are sticky.”

“Hygiene time,” said Niko. “Clean hands help keep us healthy.”

Dad smiled in the rearview mirror. “Good idea. There are wipes in the pocket.”

Mia took a wipe. It felt cool and smelled like fresh apples. “Wipe, wipe, wipe,” she sang, cleaning her hands and the seatbelt clip she touched. Niko wiped his tiny paws with a corner of the wipe, too.

“Fresh and ready!” said Niko. “Now we can read our map.”

“Map?” Mia asked.

Niko pulled a crinkly paper from his backpack. It was a simple picture map he drew with big shapes. “This shows our plan: Step 1—Buckle up. Step 2—Clean hands. Step 3—Learning game!”

“What’s the game?” asked Mia, eyes bright.

“ABC Windows!” Niko declared. “We find letters on signs. Letters help us read. Reading helps us learn anything.”

Dad chuckled. “Let’s play.”

They drove past a bright red stop sign. Niko pressed his paw to the window glass, cool and smooth. “S is for Stop,” he said. “Ssss like snake.”

Mia copied the sound. “Ssss!”

“Great job!” said Niko. “That is education, right in the car.”

They passed a blue bus with big letters. Mia pointed. “B! B is for bus and book.”

“Brilliant,” Niko said. “And B is for brush—like brushing our teeth.”

Mia touched her mouth. “We brushed this morning. My toothpaste was minty.”

Niko nodded. “Minty clean makes smiles shine. Hygiene is a habit.”

The road bumped softly. Thump, thump. Niko held the strap of his seatbelt. “I am safe,” he whispered. “I am responsible.”

“Me too,” said Mia. “I am safe and responsible.”

Soon, the car grew a little quiet. Mia looked down. Her papers and crayons from yesterday were scattered on the floor and seat. A crayon rolled and tapped Niko’s paw. Tap, tap.

“Oh no,” Mia said. “It’s messy.”

Niko took a deep breath. He liked things tidy so he could think. “It’s okay,” he said calmly. “We can fix it. Responsibility means taking care of our things.”

“How?” Mia asked.

Niko’s eyes sparkled. “We make a cleanup plan! Step 1—Sort. Step 2—Store. Step 3—Shine.”

Dad pulled into a parking spot to wait for a light. “You have one minute, helpers,” he said.

“Ready!” said Niko. He and Mia picked up the papers. The paper felt smooth and a little crinkly. “Sort,” Niko said, placing drawings in a neat pile on Mia’s lap.

“Store,” Mia said, sliding crayons into a small zip bag. The bag made a soft zzzzip sound.

“Shine,” Niko said, brushing crumbs into a tiny trash bag. Crinkle, crinkle.

Dad peeked back. “Teamwork! The car looks great.”

Mia smiled. “I feel proud.”

Niko nodded. “A tidy space helps our brains learn. When things are clean, our thoughts are clear.”

They drove again. Wind whooshed softly. Niko pointed to a tall, yellow school sign. “S again! S is for school.”

Mia clapped. “School is where we learn together.”

“And the car is our moving classroom,” said Niko. “We can learn letters, numbers, and rules.”

“Rules?” Mia asked.

“Yes,” said Niko. “Rules help everyone. We keep our seatbelts on. We keep our hands and feet to ourselves. We talk kindly. We clean up after we snack. That is responsibility, hygiene, and safety.”

Mia took a deep breath. The air felt cool and clean. “I can do that.”

Niko leaned back, happy. He imagined he was the Nutty Professor again, teaching a class of acorn students. In his mind, the acorns wore tiny glasses and nodded, “Yes, Professor!” He giggled.

“What’s funny?” Dad asked.

“I’m practicing my teacher voice,” said Niko. “Learning is fun when we make it a game.”

The car stopped at school. The engine quieted. Click went the seatbelts as they unbuckled. Mia lifted her neat pile of papers. Niko zipped his backpack.

“Thank you, Niko,” Mia said. “You taught me a lot today.”

“What did we learn?” Niko asked, tilting his head.

Mia counted on her fingers. “We learned to buckle up, clean our hands, read letters, and clean our mess. That is education, hygiene, and responsibility.”

Niko smiled wide. “And we used our imagination, too!”

Dad opened the door. Cool morning air rushed in. It smelled like grass and new crayons.

“Class dismissed,” Niko said with a playful bow.

Mia laughed and took Niko’s paw. “Let’s go learn more.”

They hopped out of the family car, ready for the day, hearts bright and minds open.

The End.
