# Rosie Rabbit's Big Brave Day

Rosie Rabbit lived in a cozy burrow with her mama and papa. Every morning, Mama worked in her home office, typing on her computer with clickety-clack sounds. The office smelled like fresh flowers from the windowsill garden and had colorful pictures on the walls from friends around the world.

"Good morning, little bunny," said Mama, giving Rosie a warm hug. "Today I have an important video call with my friends from different countries. Would you like to say hello?"

Rosie's ears drooped down. She felt butterflies dancing in her tummy. "But Mama, what if they don't understand me? What if I say something wrong?"

Mama's whiskers twitched with a gentle smile. "Oh, sweet pea, being brave means trying new things even when we feel scared. My friends speak different languages, but kindness sounds the same everywhere."

Rosie nibbled her carrot breakfast slowly, thinking hard. She could hear Mama's computer making beeping sounds as friends joined the call. Through the office door, she heard cheerful voices saying "Hello!" in different ways.

"Hola!" came one voice.
"Bonjour!" said another.
"Konnichiwa!" called a third.

Rosie's ears perked up. The voices sounded so friendly and musical! She hopped closer to the office door, her pink nose twitching with curiosity.

"Rosie," called Mama softly, "would you like to meet my friends? They'd love to see you."

Rosie's heart went thump-thump-thump. She took three deep breaths, just like Mama taught her when she felt nervous. "Being brave means trying," she whispered to herself.

With a big hop, Rosie bounced into the office. On the computer screen, she saw smiling faces from around the world. A lady with beautiful dark skin waved from sunny Africa. A man with kind eyes smiled from snowy Canada. A grandmother with silver hair giggled from busy Japan.

"Everyone, this is my brave little Rosie!" said Mama proudly.

"Hello, Rosie!" they all said together, waving their hands.

Rosie felt her scared feelings melting away like ice cream in sunshine. "Hi!" she said in her small, clear voice. "I'm three years old, and I love carrots!"

The friends laughed with joy. The lady from Africa showed Rosie colorful beads. The man from Canada held up a maple leaf. The grandmother from Japan folded a paper crane and held it up to the camera.

"Wow!" said Rosie, clapping her paws. "You're all so different and so wonderful!"

"And you're very brave for saying hello," said the grandmother with a warm smile.

After the call ended, Mama hugged Rosie tight. "I'm so proud of you, little bunny. You were scared, but you tried anyway. That's what real bravery looks like."

Rosie's chest puffed up with pride. "Mama, can we call them again tomorrow? I want to learn how to say 'hello' in their languages!"

"Of course, my brave little rabbit. And maybe we can share some of our healthy carrot recipes too!"

That night, Rosie munched her dinner carrots extra proudly. She had learned that being brave doesn't mean not feeling scared – it means doing kind things even when your tummy has butterflies. And the best part? Making new friends from around the world was the most wonderful adventure of all.

The End.
