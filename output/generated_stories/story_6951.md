# Gigi the Giraffe Makes a Plan

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with kind eyes and a long, gentle neck. One rainy morning, she tiptoed into the pantry. The pantry smelled like warm oats and sweet bananas. Shelves stood tall with jars and boxes. Rustle, rustle went the paper bags. Tap, tap went the raindrops on the window.

Gigi blinked and smiled. “I want to make a snack for a rainy-day picnic,” she said softly. “But what should I choose?”

Her tall head reached the top shelf. Her hooves stood steady on the cool floor. She felt curious and a little excited. She also felt a tiny twist in her tummy. There were so many choices!

On one shelf, she saw crunchy crackers. On another, she saw smooth peanut butter. A bright basket held apples and bananas. A little jar of cinnamon winked at her with a warm, cozy smell.

Gigi took a deep breath. “Hmm,” she said. “I need to make a decision.”

She remembered what Mama Giraffe always said: “When you need to choose, use your head, your heart, and a plan.”

Gigi nodded. “I will use my head, my heart, and a plan.”

First, she used her head. She thought, “What do I have? What can I do?” She looked at the shelves carefully.

She whispered, “Crackers, peanut butter, apples, bananas, oats, honey, cinnamon, and cups.” She rubbed her chin. “I could make banana crackers. I could make apple slices with peanut butter. I could make warm oat cups with honey.”

Her tummy gave a happy growl. Grumble, grumble.

Next, she used her heart. “What do I want? What feels good today?” She closed her eyes and listened. Rain pattered. The pantry felt cozy. “I want something warm and something crunchy,” she decided. “I want to share, too.”

Then, she made a plan. She spoke out loud to stay calm and clear.

“Step one: choose two snacks. Step two: gather the things. Step three: clean and prepare. Step four: taste and share.”

Gigi smiled. “That is my plan.”

She reached for oats and honey for something warm. She chose crackers and banana for something crunchy and sweet. “Two snacks! Good choice,” she told herself.

She gathered a bowl, a spoon, and little cups. Clink, clink went the cups. Swish went the bowl. She put the oats, honey, and a pinch of cinnamon into the bowl. The cinnamon smelled like hugs. The honey shone like sunlight.

“Now I will prepare,” Gigi said. She poured warm water over the oats and stirred. Stir, stir, stir. The oats grew soft and steamy. She tasted a tiny spoonful. “Mmm, warm and cozy.”

Next, she spread peanut butter on the crackers. Spread, spread. She placed banana slices on top. Plop, plop. The crackers looked like little smiling moons.

Just then, her friend Dotty the ladybug buzzed by. “Bzz! What are you doing, Gigi?”

“I am making a rainy-day picnic,” Gigi said. “I had to make a big decision. I used my head, my heart, and a plan.”

Dotty landed on a spice jar. “Bzz, that sounds smart! Can I watch?”

“You can help,” Gigi said. “Would you please count the cups? We want to be ready.”

Dotty counted softly. “One, two, three, four.” She patted each cup with her tiny foot. “We have four cups.”

Gigi nodded. “Thank you. That is good preparation. Preparation helps us be ready.”

They heard little footsteps. Pitter, patter. Two tiny field mice peeked in. “Hello,” squeaked Pip and Pop. “It smells good in here. Is there a snack?”

Gigi smiled. “Yes. I planned two snacks to share.” She looked at her plan and checked each step.

“Step one, choose two snacks. Done!
Step two, gather the things. Done!
Step three, clean and prepare. Done!
Step four, taste and share. Now!”

She set warm oat cups on a tray. She put the banana crackers next to the cups. “Please wash paws,” she reminded. The mice washed in a little bowl of water. Splash, splash. Dotty brushed her tiny legs. Gigi wiped the counter with a soft cloth. Swipe, swipe.

They all sat in the cozy pantry, with the rain singing outside. Gigi tasted the oats again. “Mmm,” she hummed. “Sweet and warm.” Pip and Pop crunched the crackers. Crunch, crunch. Dotty dabbed a crumb. “Bzz, tasty!”

Pip looked up. “Gigi, how did you pick such good snacks?”

Gigi’s eyes twinkled. “I felt curious, so I explored. I wanted to be ready, so I prepared. I had many choices, so I made a plan. I used my head to think, my heart to feel, and my plan to do.”

Pop nodded. “That helps me when I choose toys.”

Dotty buzzed in a happy circle. “That helps me when I choose a flower!”

Gigi laughed softly. “It helps me when I choose, too.”

The rain slowed to a gentle drip. Drip, drip. The pantry felt warm and safe. Everyone’s tummies felt full and calm.

Gigi cleaned up with care. She put the jars back. She washed the bowl. She folded the cloth. “Preparation also means we finish well,” she said. “Now we are ready for next time.”

Pip and Pop waved. “Thank you, Gigi!”

Dotty twirled. “Bzz! Thank you!”

Gigi felt proud and peaceful. She had made a good decision and helped her friends. She lifted her chin and smiled.

“Today I learned,” she said, “that when I need to decide, I can be curious, make a plan, and prepare. I can use my head, my heart, and my plan.”

Outside, a soft rainbow peeked through the clouds. Inside, the pantry glowed with happy feelings.

The End.
