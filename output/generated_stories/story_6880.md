# Koko the Calm Koala Says Thank You

Koko the calm koala lived in a cozy home with a bright home office. The office had a soft blue rug, a big wooden desk, and a chair that squeaked a little when it turned. Sunlight came in the window and warmed Koko’s fuzzy gray ears. The room smelled like paper and lemon tea.

Koko liked to work and draw in the home office. He kept crayons in a tin that went clink-clink when he opened it. He kept sticky notes that felt smooth. He kept a small bell that went ding when he pressed it. Koko liked order, and Koko liked calm.

One morning, Koko had a big plan. “Today I will make a Thank-You Book,” he said, patting his soft belly. “I will draw pictures for my friends. I will say thank you to everyone who helps me.”

Koko sat in his chair. It squeaked, eeek. He picked up a red crayon. Scratch, scratch went the tip on the paper. Koko drew his friend Pip the panda, who always shared snacks. He drew Lila the lizard, who told funny jokes. He drew Sunny the snail, who moved slow but stayed by his side.

Then Koko reached for the glue to stick the pages together.

“Oh,” said Koko softly. “The glue is all gone.”

He shook the little bottle. Rattle-rattle. Nothing. Koko’s ears drooped.

“I want to finish my Thank-You Book,” he said. “But I don’t have glue.” His tummy felt fizzy, like little bubbles. He took a slow breath in. He breathed out, long and gentle. Koko was a calm koala. He knew how to stop, think, and try.

“I can solve this,” he told himself. “I can try by myself first.”

He looked around the home office. The desk was tidy. The drawers slid open with a hush. In one drawer, Koko found tape. The tape felt sticky on his paws.

“I can use tape,” Koko said. “I can be self-reliant.” He smiled and pressed the pages. Rip… stick… smooth. The pages held together, one by one.

But then the tape ran out too. Whirr, whirr, click. Empty.

Koko’s ears drooped again. “Oh dear.” He took another slow breath. In… out… calm.

He peeked out the window. The sun sat warm on the sill. Outside, Pip the panda was watering flowers. Splash, splash. Koko waved.

“Hi, Pip!” called Koko. “I am making a Thank-You Book. My glue and tape are gone. I tried to fix it by myself. I got this far.” He held up the pages. They fluttered like soft leaves.

Pip padded over. “You did a lot all by yourself,” said Pip. “That’s great! I have a glue stick in my backpack. Would you like to borrow it?”

Koko felt his heart glow like a little lamp. “Yes, please. And thank you,” he said.

Pip handed him the glue stick. Koko twisted it up. It smelled sweet, like school days. Smear, smear. The pages hugged tight. Koko finished the book.

Just then, Lila the lizard tapped on the door. Tap-tap. “Hi! What are you two doing?” Her tail swished.

Koko held up the book. The cover said Thank You in big crayon letters. “I made this for my friends,” Koko said. “I felt stuck when the glue and tape were gone. I took calm breaths. I tried to solve it by myself. Then I asked Pip for help. I am grateful.”

Lila’s eyes sparkled. “That is kind! Can I read it?”

Koko nodded. They sat on the soft blue rug. The rug felt warm and fluffy against their toes. Koko turned the pages. They showed Pip sharing snacks. They showed Lila telling jokes. They showed Sunny the snail moving slow and steady with a smile.

“This is for you, Pip,” Koko read. “Thank you for sharing and for being near.”

Pip hugged the book. “You’re welcome,” he said. “Thank you for making this!”

“This page is for you, Lila,” Koko read. “Thank you for making me laugh when my face feels frowny.”

Lila giggled. “I am happy to help!”

Koko made one more page for Sunny. “Thank you for staying with me when things take time,” he read. He put a small sticky note with a heart on it to save it for Sunny.

Koko felt calm and proud. “I am grateful for my friends,” he said. “I am also proud I tried by myself first. I can be self-reliant and also ask for help. Both can be true.”

Pip nodded. “Friends help friends.”

Lila smiled. “And friends say thank you.”

Koko closed the book. The chair squeaked as he turned to put it on the desk. Eeek. He poured lemon tea. The steam smelled bright. He shared little cups with Pip and Lila. Sip, sip.

Koko’s home office felt extra cozy now. The sun was warm. The rug was soft. The friends were close.

Koko whispered, “Thank you for you.” His friends whispered back, “Thank you for you, too.”

And Koko learned, and Pip learned, and Lila learned: being grateful makes hearts feel big. Trying first helps us grow. Asking kindly brings friends close. Together, calm and thankful, they could solve little problems and big ones too.

The End.
