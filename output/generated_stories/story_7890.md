# Joey Roo’s Big Backyard Map

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey. He loved to hop-hoppity-hop in his sunny backyard. The grass was warm and tickly on his toes. The sky felt big and blue, and the breeze smelled like sweet flowers. Birds chirped, “Cheep, cheep!” and a butterfly fluttered by Joey’s nose.

“Good morning, world!” Joey sang. He stretched his little paws wide. “I want to learn about every place!”

Mama Roo smiled from the porch. “The world is very big, little jumper,” she said gently. “But learning can start right here.”

Just then, Joey heard noise next door. He peeked through the fence and saw his friends. Mei was watering bright red flowers. Rafi was spinning a colorful pinwheel. Lila was drawing with chalk. Their families had moved from faraway places, but now they all played together in the sunny backyard spaces.

“Hi, friends!” Joey called. “I want to know about the big world.”

Mei waved. “I’m from a place where we celebrate with lanterns,” she said. “At night, the sky looks like it’s full of tiny moons.”

“Wow,” Joey breathed. He could almost see the glowing lights.

Rafi grinned. “I come from a place where the desert sand is golden and warm,” he said, wiggling his toes. “We drink cool mint tea.”

Joey sniffed the breeze. “Mmm, mint smells fresh!” he said.

Lila twirled a ribbon. “I come from a place with long, happy dances,” she said. “The drums go boom-boom-boom!”

Joey clapped his paws. Boom-boom-boom! His heart thumped with joy. But then he drooped a little. “The world is so big,” he whispered. “How can I learn it all? I am only a little joey.”

Mama Roo hopped over and knelt beside him. “What is the problem, Joey?” she asked kindly.

Joey sighed. “I want to know about the whole world, but I don’t know where to start.”

Mama Roo tapped her chin. “Hmm. What could you do, little thinker?”

Joey looked around the sunny backyard. He felt the sun warm his ears. He heard the birds sing. He smelled the flowers. His paws touched the soft grass. A bright idea popped like a bubble. “I can make a backyard map!” he said. “We can share pieces of the world right here.”

“That is good initiative,” Mama Roo said proudly. “Ask your friends for help.”

Joey took a deep breath. “I can do this,” he told himself. His chest felt strong. His tail swished with self-confidence. He hopped to Mei. “Will you help me?” he asked. “Let’s make a map of our big world in our sunny backyard.”

“Yes!” Mei cheered. “I’ll bring lanterns.” She drew a curvy river with blue chalk. “This is where my grandma lives,” she said. “We say nǐ hǎo to say hello.”

Joey carefully repeated, “Nee how.” He smiled. “Hello to your grandma.”

Rafi brought a small bowl of mint leaves. “Smell,” he said. Joey sniffed. “Mmm!” Rafi drew tiny dunes with yellow chalk. “This is my desert,” he said. “We say marhaba to say hello.”

Joey tried it, “Mar-ha-ba!” The word felt bouncy on his tongue.

Lila drummed softly on a toy drum. Boom-boom-boom! She drew a sun with long rays. “This is our dance circle,” she said. “We say hola to say hello.”

Joey hopped to the middle of the chalk drawings. He drew a big green heart. “This is our backyard,” he said. “We say hello, too!” He looked around. “Now our map shows parts of the world. Lanterns, dunes, dances, and a big green heart.”

They hung Mei’s paper lanterns on the fence. They set Rafi’s mint leaves in a little cup. They placed Lila’s ribbon by the drum. The backyard looked bright and friendly. The air smelled like mint and flowers. The lanterns swished softly in the breeze.

But a tiny worry tickled Joey’s tummy. “What if I do it wrong?” he asked. “What if my map is not real?”

Mama Roo gave him a warm hug. “Maps help us remember and learn,” she said. “Your map is a picture of your friends’ stories. That is real and kind.”

Joey stood tall. “We can share our words,” he said. He pointed to each friend. “Nee how. Marhaba. Hola.” He added, “And hello!”

They all practiced together. “Nee how! Marhaba! Hola! Hello!” Their voices sounded like a happy song.

A breeze rustled the leaves. A bird chirped along. Boom-boom-boom went the drum. Joey felt his heart boom, too, brave and bright.

“Today we learned that the world is big,” Joey said, “and we can learn about it right here. We listened. We shared. We cared.”

Mei nodded. “The world is full of friends.”

Rafi smiled. “The world is full of tastes and smells.”

Lila twirled. “The world is full of music and smiles.”

Joey beamed. “And I can take the first hop!”

They all held paws and hands in a circle and danced around the backyard map. The lanterns glowed, the mint smelled cool, and the chalk colors made the grass look like a rainbow.

Mama Roo clapped. “What a wonderful world,” she said.

Joey Roo’s tail swished with joy. “I did it,” he whispered to himself. “I can learn. I can start. I can share.”

He looked up at the big blue sky. “Hello, world!” he cheered. “We are neighbors.”

The End.
