# Ollie the Otter’s Gentle Paws

Sunlight sprinkled soft gold dots on the cozy rug in the comfortable nursery. Plush pillows made a mountain, and a basket of blocks sat like bright candy. Ollie the Otter wiggled his whiskers and patted his smooth, shiny tail. He was a playful otter who loved to build, splash in pretend puddles, and hug his friends.

“Good morning, room!” Ollie chirped, rolling onto the rug. He listened. He could hear the hum of the heater, the soft tick-tick of the clock, and the whisper of pages turning. Ahhh, it felt calm. It smelled like clean blankets and warm toast.

Across the room sat Mia the Mouse, small and shy, holding a worn teddy. She was rubbing her eyes. Her whiskers trembled. Near the window, Benny the Bunny stacked blocks as tall as his ears. “One, two, three!” Benny said with a happy wiggle. “I’m making a tower!”

Ollie scooted over. “Can I help, Benny?” he asked.

“Sure!” Benny smiled. “Let’s make it tall-tall-tall!”

Ollie’s paws were fast and excited. He loved to play. His paws went tap, tap, tap. Blocks clicked and clacked. The tower grew and grew. But then—oops!—Ollie’s tail swished too big. Whoosh. The tower wobbled. Clatter-crash!

Benny’s ears drooped. “My tower,” he whispered.

Ollie blinked. His tummy felt funny and tight. He didn’t mean to knock it down. He looked at Benny’s face and saw watery eyes. He listened to Benny’s little sigh. Ollie took a slow, soft breath in, like smelling a flower. He let it out, like blowing a tiny cloud. He remembered what Ms. Dove, the gentle teacher, always said: “Stop. Breathe. Notice. Choose kind.”

Ollie placed his paws on his belly. “I’m sorry, Benny,” he said, voice warm as a blanket. “My tail went too swishy. I want to help fix it.”

Benny sniffled. “Okay,” he said, wiping a tear. “Maybe we can make a stronger tower.”

Ollie’s heart felt lighter. “Let’s try together,” he said. “Slow and gentle.”

They stacked slowly. Ollie watched his tail. He moved with care. Tap. Click. Careful. The tower stood steady and proud. Benny giggled. “We did it!”

Across the room, Mia the Mouse squeezed her teddy. She looked small in the big chair. Her tummy growled. She didn’t say anything. Ollie saw her ears droop. He listened to his heart again. It whispered, Check on Mia.

Ollie waddled over and sat beside her. The chair was soft and squishy. “Hi, Mia,” he said in a quiet voice. “Your eyes look a little rainy. Do you want a friend?”

Mia nodded, tiny and slow. “My teddy’s ear is loose,” she said. “I’m worried it will fall off.”

Ollie looked closely. The ear hung by a little thread. He felt a warm wish to help. He remembered compassion means caring about feelings and helping gently. Ollie took another slow breath. “We can ask Ms. Dove,” he said. “She has a sewing kit.”

Mia’s whiskers twitched. “Will it hurt Teddy?”

“No,” Ollie said. “We will be soft and still.”

They walked to Ms. Dove, who had kind eyes and soft wings on her sweater. “Ms. Dove,” Ollie said, holding Teddy very carefully. “Mia is worried. Can you help Teddy’s ear?”

“Of course,” Ms. Dove said, smiling. “Thank you for noticing, Ollie. That was very compassionate.”

Ollie’s chest felt warm, like sunshine. Ms. Dove sewed with tiny stitches. Snip. Stitch. Snip. Stitch. “All done,” she said.

Mia hugged Teddy close. “Thank you,” she whispered to Ms. Dove, then looked at Ollie. “Thank you for caring.”

“You’re welcome,” Ollie said. He placed one paw on his belly and one on his heart. “I like to use my gentle paws.”

They went back to the rug. Benny was lining up soft animal toys for a pretend tea party. “Tea time!” Benny said. “Would you like some berry tea?”

“Yes, please,” Mia and Ollie chimed.

Ollie poured pretend tea into tiny cups. The cups were light and smooth. He made a soft pouring sound. “Glug, glug.” The tea smelled like strawberries in their imaginations. They nibbled invisible cookies. Crunch. Yum!

Then a loud squeak came from the corner. Pip the Puppy, a small plush toy with a button nose, had fallen off the shelf when someone bumped it earlier. It lay on the floor. Benny frowned. “Poor Pip,” he said. “He looks lonely.”

Ollie felt that warm wish again. “Let’s help,” he said. “We can be kind together.”

Ollie bent his knees and lifted Pip with both paws. “I’m using my careful body,” he said. “Slow and steady.” He carried Pip back to the shelf and tucked him between soft blankets. Mia stroked Pip’s ear. Benny patted the blanket smooth. “There,” Ollie said. “Safe and cozy.”

Ms. Dove rang a tiny bell. “Circle time, friends,” she sang. The bell made a clear, happy ding. Everyone sat on the rug. Ms. Dove asked, “What did we practice today?”

Ollie raised his paw. “Compassion,” he said. “I stopped. I breathed. I noticed feelings. I chose kind.”

Benny nodded. “Ollie said sorry and helped me rebuild.”

Mia hugged Teddy. “Ollie helped me ask for help. That felt good.”

Ms. Dove beamed. “When we are mindful—when we pause and pay attention—we can see what others need. Compassion means caring with our hearts and helping with our hands. Kindness makes our nursery feel safe and warm.”

Ollie felt proud, but not big-loud proud. He felt calm-proud, like a candle glow. He listened to the soft hum of the room and smelled the clean, sleepy air. He wrapped his tail around himself, so it didn’t swish too big. He smiled at his friends.

“Today,” Ollie said, “we used gentle paws and gentle words.”

Benny and Mia smiled back. “Let’s keep doing that,” Benny said.

“Yes,” Mia whispered. “Gentle and kind.”

Ollie nodded. “Compassion is our superpower.”

The End.
