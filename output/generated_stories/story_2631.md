# Joey Roo and the Bubble Plan

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with springy legs and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, Joey hopped into the bathroom. The bathroom smelled like minty toothpaste and warm soap. Water hummed in the pipes. The mirror was shiny. “Boing, boing!” Joey sang. “Time to brush, wash, and splash!”

Mama Roo called from the hallway, “Remember, Joey. Gentle hands, patient feet.”

“I can do it!” Joey chirped. He turned the faucet. Whoosh! The water was cool on his paws. He squeezed the soap. Smush! A big blob splatted on the sink. “Oops,” Joey giggled. He rubbed his paws. Bubbles grew and grew. Pop, pop, pop! The bubbles tickled his nose.

Joey brushed his teeth. Swish, swish. He made silly faces at the mirror. “Hello, Joey! Hello, bubbles!” he said, with a bubbly grin. He reached for his towel—oops!—his tail bumped the pump. Glug-glug! More soap spilled. It slid down like a slimy snake.

Joey’s eyes went wide. “Uh-oh.” The soap puddle spread on the floor. It looked like a sleepy lake. The rug got soggy. Squish. Joey’s happy hop slowed into a worried wiggle. “Mama will not like this,” he whispered. His chest felt tight. His cheeks felt hot.

He wanted to stomp. He wanted to shout. “It’s not fair!” he almost said. His paws clenched. He scrunched his nose. Then he remembered: Mama’s words, gentle hands, patient feet. Joey took a deep breath in: ssssh. He let it out: whoooo.

“I can calm down,” Joey told himself softly. “I can fix this.”

He looked around. He needed a plan. He needed help. He peeked out the bathroom door. “Mama?” he called. “I made a big soapy mess.”

Mama Roo hopped in and looked at the floor. She did not shout. She put a warm paw on Joey’s shoulder. “Thank you for telling me, Joey. What can we do together?”

Joey thought. “We can make a Bubble Plan!” he said. His ears perked up. “Step one: Stop the spill.” He turned off the soap pump carefully. Click.

“Step two: Clean the floor,” Mama added.

Joey nodded. “Step three: Help our friends, too.”

Mama smiled. “Community service starts at home. Then we can help others. Let’s begin.”

They got a bucket and some towels. The water was lukewarm. The towels felt heavy and squishy. Slurp, slurp, they soaked up the soap. Joey pressed with gentle paws. He hummed a little song. “Push, push, squeeze. Pat, pat, please.” When he slipped a tiny bit, he paused. He breathed again. In, out. “I am patient,” he said.

Tap-tap! Someone knocked at the window. It was Wally Wombat, their neighbor. “G’day!” Wally waved. “Our garden path is muddy. The little ones can’t play. We could use help.”

Joey’s eyes sparkled. “That’s step three!” he cheered. “Community service!” He looked at Mama. “Can we help, after we finish?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “First we solve this problem. Then we help the community.”

They dried the last bubble. The bathroom smelled like lemons now. The floor was safe and clean. Joey washed his paws again, this time with one small pump. “One squeeze is enough,” he said proudly.

Outside, the sun warmed Joey’s fur. The sky was bright and blue. Birds chirped, peep-peep. The fresh air felt like a hug. Joey and Mama carried a broom, a little rake, and a bucket of pebbles. Wally waved again. “Thanks for coming!”

Joey’s friends, Kiki Koala and Benny Bandicoot, were there too. “We can help!” Kiki clapped. Benny bounced. The path was squishy like pudding. Squelch, squelch. A little joey wombat frowned. “I can’t play,” she sniffed.

Joey remembered his Bubble Plan. “We can make a Mud Plan!” he said. “Step one: Stay calm. Step two: Work together. Step three: Fix the path.”

Benny started to jump in the mud. Splish! Mud sprayed on Kiki’s fur. Kiki pouted. “Hey!”

Joey felt heat rise in his cheeks again. “I want to shout,” he thought. He put his paws on his belly. “Breath time,” he whispered. He breathed in and out, like waves: in, out, in, out. “Let’s be patient,” he said kindly. “Gentle feet, Benny. We can do this.”

They spread the pebbles. Clink, clink. They brushed the water to the side with the broom. Swish, swish. They laid flat stepping stones. Thunk. Joey counted slowly, “One stone, two stones, three stones.” He didn’t rush. He smiled. “Teamwork feels good.”

Wally brought lemonade. The cups were cool and sweet. “You kids are amazing,” he said. “You fixed the path!”

The little joey wombat tried the steps. Tap, tap, tap. “I can play!” she squealed. Everyone cheered. The birds sang louder. The wind made the gum leaves whisper, shhh.

Mama Roo patted Joey’s head. “Today you cleaned up your own spill. Then you helped the whole neighborhood.”

Joey’s tail thumped happily. “When I feel mad, I can breathe. When I make a mess, I can help. When my friends need help, I can serve.”

Kiki hugged him. “You are a good helper, Joey.”

Joey grinned. “We are all good helpers. Community service is working together to make things better.”

They played hop-and-stop along the new path. Hop, stop. Hop, stop. Laughs bubbled like soft soda fizz. The sun dipped a little, and the air smelled like wet earth and lemon.

That night, back in the shiny bathroom, Joey washed up with one gentle pump. He looked at his bright eyes in the mirror. “I am patient. I am kind. I help my community,” he said. He felt calm and proud.

Mama tucked him in. “Good night, helper.”

“Good night,” Joey whispered. “Tomorrow, more helping.”

The End.
