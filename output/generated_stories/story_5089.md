# Penny's Problem-Solving Day

In a cozy closet hideaway, behind the hanging winter coats, lived a cheerful little bird named Penny. Her feathers were bright yellow like sunshine, and she loved to sing sweet songs that echoed softly in her special hiding place.

"Tweet-tweet-trill!" Penny sang happily as she woke up one morning. The closet smelled like lavender sachets and felt warm and safe. But as she looked around her nest, she noticed something was wrong.

"Oh my!" chirped Penny. "My beautiful home is so messy!" 

Tiny twigs were scattered everywhere. Soft cotton pieces lay in jumbled piles. Her favorite shiny button had rolled behind a shoebox. The water in her little bottle cap was almost empty, and seeds were spilled all over the closet floor.

Penny felt overwhelmed. "This is too much work," she sighed. "Maybe I'll just leave it messy."

But then she remembered what her mama bird had taught her: "When you have a big problem, Penny, stop and think. Break it into small pieces, like crumbs!"

Penny tilted her tiny head and thought carefully. "Let me see... what should I do first?"

She looked around with her bright black eyes. The water bottle cap was nearly empty, and she felt thirsty. "I know!" she chirped. "First, I need water to drink. Then I'll have energy to clean!"

Penny flew to the bathroom and carefully filled her bottle cap with fresh, cool water. "Glub, glub, glub," went the water as she drank. "Ahh, much better!"

Next, she thought about what was most important. "My nest needs to be cozy for sleeping," she decided. So she gathered all the soft cotton pieces and fluffy feathers, arranging them in a perfect circle.

"Tweet-trill-tweet!" she sang as she worked. The singing made the chores feel like a fun game!

Then Penny noticed the scattered seeds. "If I don't pick these up, they might attract mice," she thought. So she carefully picked up each seed with her small beak, saving them in a tiny acorn cap for later.

As she worked, Penny accidentally knocked over Mrs. Mouse's reading glasses that were stored on a shelf. CRASH! They fell to the floor.

"Oh no!" gasped Penny. She felt terrible. Mrs. Mouse was always kind to her, sharing stories and keeping her company.

Penny thought about what to do. "I should tell Mrs. Mouse what happened and say I'm sorry," she decided. "That's the right thing to do."

When Mrs. Mouse came home, Penny flew down nervously. "Mrs. Mouse, I have something to tell you. I accidentally broke your glasses while cleaning. I'm very sorry."

Mrs. Mouse smiled warmly. "Thank you for being honest, dear Penny. Accidents happen when we're working hard. I'm proud of you for telling the truth!"

Together, they carefully picked up the pieces. Mrs. Mouse showed Penny how the glasses could be fixed with a tiny bit of tape.

By evening, Penny's closet hideaway sparkled clean. Her nest was fluffy and comfortable. Her seeds were organized, and fresh water sparkled in her bottle cap.

"I learned something important today," Penny sang softly. "When I have a big job, I can think step by step. And when I make mistakes, saying sorry makes everything better!"

That night, Penny slept peacefully in her tidy home, dreaming of tomorrow's adventures.

The End.
