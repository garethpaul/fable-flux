# Benny's Big Bathroom Project

Benny the beaver loved to build things. He had soft brown fur and big front teeth that were perfect for chewing wood. One morning, Benny woke up with a wonderful idea.

"I want to build the best dam ever!" Benny said excitedly. He rushed to the bathroom to wash his face and brush his teeth. But when he looked in the mirror, he saw a big mess.

Toothpaste was everywhere! Towels were on the floor. Water dripped from the sink. Benny's tail drooped down sadly.

"Oh no," Benny whispered. "How can I build a dam if I can't even keep my bathroom clean?"

Benny tried to clean up quickly. He grabbed a towel and wiped the counter. *Splash!* Water spilled on his feet. He picked up his toothbrush. *Clatter!* It fell in the sink.

"This isn't working," Benny said, feeling frustrated. His whiskers twitched as he thought hard.

Just then, his friend Rosie the rabbit hopped into the bathroom. Her pink nose wiggled when she saw the mess.

"Hi Benny! What happened here?" Rosie asked kindly.

"I wanted to build a dam, but I can't do anything right," Benny said sadly. "Everything is so messy and mixed up."

Rosie's ears perked up. "You know what you need? A plan! My mom always says planning makes everything easier."

"A plan?" Benny asked, tilting his furry head.

"Yes!" Rosie said cheerfully. "Let's make a list of what to do first, second, and third."

Benny's eyes grew bright. "That sounds smart! Will you help me?"

"Of course!" Rosie smiled. "Friends always help each other."

Together, they sat on the bathroom floor. Rosie found a piece of paper and a crayon.

"First," Rosie said, "we should put away the towels."

"Good idea!" Benny said. They folded the soft, fluffy towels and hung them on the rack.

"Second," Benny added, "let's wipe the toothpaste off the counter."

They used a warm, damp cloth to clean the white, minty toothpaste. The counter sparkled when they finished.

"Third," Rosie said, "we should organize your toothbrush and soap."

They put everything in its special place. The bathroom smelled fresh and clean like lavender soap.

"Wow!" Benny said, looking around. "Planning really works! Everything looks perfect now."

"And you did most of the work," Rosie said proudly. "You're a great leader, Benny."

Benny's chest puffed out with pride. "I discovered something important today. Planning makes big jobs feel small and easy."

"Now you're ready to build that amazing dam!" Rosie said, clapping her paws.

Benny hugged his friend. "Thank you for teaching me about planning, Rosie. Want to help me plan my dam too?"

"I'd love to!" Rosie said happily.

From that day on, Benny always made plans before starting big projects. He learned that taking time to think and organize made everything work better. And he discovered that the best plans are made with good friends.

Benny built the most wonderful dam in the whole forest, and it all started with learning to plan in his little bathroom.

The End.
