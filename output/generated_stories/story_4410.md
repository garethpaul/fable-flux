# Nutkin's Big, Brave Day

The warm sunshine tickled Nutkin the squirrel's fluffy gray tail as he stretched and yawned in his cozy tree home. "What a beautiful day!" he chirped, looking down at the bright green backyard below.

Nutkin's tummy rumbled loudly. "Grumble, grumble!" He needed to find some tasty acorns for breakfast, but there was one big problem. The best acorn tree was all the way across the yard, and between his tree and the acorn tree lived Buster, a very large dog who loved to chase squirrels.

"Woof! Woof!" Buster's deep bark echoed through the yard. Nutkin's little heart went "thump, thump, thump." He felt scared, but his tummy rumbled again, even louder this time.

"I need to be brave," Nutkin whispered to himself. "But how can a little squirrel like me get past such a big dog?"

Nutkin sat on his branch and thought very hard. He watched Buster carefully. The big dog was lying in the shade, panting in the warm sun. His pink tongue hung out, and his eyes were getting sleepy.

"I have an idea!" Nutkin said excitedly. "I'll wait until Buster takes his afternoon nap, then I'll be very quiet and quick."

Soon, Buster's eyes closed, and he began to snore softly. "Zzz, zzz, zzz." This was Nutkin's chance!

Taking a deep breath, Nutkin scampered down his tree. His tiny paws made no sound on the soft grass. Step by step, he crept across the yard, his bushy tail twitching nervously.

Halfway to the acorn tree, Buster suddenly opened one eye! Nutkin froze like a statue, his heart beating fast. But instead of barking, Buster just looked at the little squirrel curiously.

"Hello there, little fellow," Buster said in a surprisingly gentle voice. "You look scared. Are you okay?"

Nutkin was so surprised that he forgot to be afraid. "Oh! I... I thought you didn't like squirrels. I was trying to get to the acorn tree for breakfast."

Buster's tail wagged slowly. "I don't chase squirrels because I'm mean. I just get excited and want to play! But I can see that scares you. I'm sorry."

"Really?" Nutkin asked, feeling much braver now. "You just want to be friends?"

"Of course!" Buster smiled, showing his big white teeth. "Would you like me to help you gather acorns? I know where the best ones fall."

Nutkin's whiskers twitched with happiness. "That would be wonderful! Thank you, Buster."

Together, the new friends walked to the acorn tree. Buster used his strong nose to sniff out the tastiest acorns hidden in the grass, while Nutkin used his nimble paws to gather them up.

"You know," said Nutkin as he filled his cheeks with delicious acorns, "I was so scared of you, but you're actually very kind."

"And you're much braver than you thought," Buster replied. "It takes courage to face your fears."

As the sun climbed higher in the bright blue sky, Nutkin realized he had learned something very important. Being brave didn't mean he wasn't scared – it meant doing what he needed to do even when he felt frightened. And sometimes, the things that seem scary turn out to be wonderful friends.

From that day on, Nutkin and Buster shared the sunny backyard together, proving that courage, kindness, and understanding can turn fear into friendship.

The End.
