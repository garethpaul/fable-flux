# Miko Makes It Right

Miko was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and gentle hands. He lived with Nana in a warm kitchen that always smelled like cinnamon toast. The sun made golden squares on the floor, and the kettle sang a soft song. Pots hung like shiny moons. A big wooden spoon sat by the stove, and a bowl of ripe bananas rested on the counter.

“Good morning, brave Miko,” Nana said, her voice smooth like honey. “Today we make banana oatmeal cookies. We will use kind hands, calm breaths, and careful steps. That keeps our bodies and hearts well.”

Miko bounced on his toes. “I can do careful steps,” he said. He put his paw on his chest and took a slow breath in. He let the breath out. His shoulders felt soft.

Nana handed him a tiny apron. “You are my helper,” she smiled.

They washed their hands. The water felt warm, and the soap smelled like oranges. “Rub-rub-rub,” Miko sang. “Rinse-rinse-rinse.”

Nana measured oats. Miko mashed bananas with the big spoon. Squish! Squash! The bananas were sweet and mushy. “Like sunny clouds,” Miko giggled.

“Now we add raisins,” Nana said. “And a little cinnamon.” She sprinkled the brown powder. The kitchen smelled cozy, like a hug.

Miko imagined the cookies. He could almost hear them say, “We will be tasty and kind to tummies!”

He wanted to help more. He wanted to be independent. He wanted to be brave. He reached for the big jar of raisins. It was heavy, round, and cool.

“Miko, wait,” Nana said gently. “Two hands, please.”

But Miko wanted to show he could do it himself. He used one hand.

Uh-oh.

The jar slipped. Clatter! Clink! Raisins rolled like tiny beetles. They clicked across the warm kitchen floor. Some dough fell with a plop. Miko’s tummy went tight. His cheeks felt hot. His brave heart thumped fast.

“I… I wanted to help,” Miko whispered. His eyes felt watery.

Nana set down the spoon and knelt. She put a soft hand on Miko’s shoulder. “Accidents happen,” she said. “We can fix this. Let’s take a wellness pause.”

“What is a wellness pause?” Miko sniffed.

“It is a calm moment to help our body and mind,” Nana said. “We breathe, notice our feelings, and make a plan.”

Miko nodded. He put his paw on his belly. Nana said, “Smell the warm cookies.” They breathed in slowly through their noses. Then Nana said, “Blow the cool tea.” They breathed out gently through their mouths. In, out. In, out. Miko’s shoulders dropped. His tummy untied its knot.

“I feel better,” Miko said.

“Good,” Nana smiled. “Now we notice. What do we see?”

“I see raisins on the floor,” Miko said. “I see dough on my toes. I see a sad mess.”

“What do we need?” Nana asked.

“A cloth, a broom, and kind words,” said Miko. “And maybe an I’m sorry.”

Nana nodded. “That is brave making-amends.”

Miko stood tall. He looked at the raisins. “I’m sorry, floor,” he said softly. “I will make you clean.” He looked at Nana. “I’m sorry, Nana. I used one hand.”

“Thank you, Miko,” Nana said. “Your apology helps my heart feel warm.”

They worked together. Sweep-sweep. Wipe-wipe. The cloth felt damp and cool. The broom made a soft whisper on the tiles. Miko hummed a tune like the kettle. The warm kitchen smiled again.

“Now we try again,” Nana said. “What is our plan?”

Miko tapped his chin, thinking. “Two hands for the jar. Slow steps. Ask for help if I need it. And we breathe when we feel tight.”

“That is good problem-solving,” Nana said.

Miko used two hands. He felt the jar’s weight. He moved slow like a sleepy turtle. “I can do it,” he whispered. Nana held the bowl steady. Plink-plink-plink went the raisins. No spills!

“Teamwork,” Nana cheered.

They stirred. Stir-stir. The dough was sticky and speckled. Miko’s fingers got gummy. It felt squishy, like cool mud. He giggled. “Hello, dough!” he said. “You are silly.”

Nana laughed. “Time to scoop.”

They put small scoops on the tray. Dot-dot-dot. The oven hummed. Warm air whooshed when Nana opened the door. Miko stepped back with careful feet. He felt proud. He had been brave in a new way—brave and calm.

While the cookies baked, Nana poured water into little cups. “Let’s also care for our bodies,” she said. “A drink of water, a stretch, and a wiggle.”

Miko sipped. The water was cool and crisp. He stretched his arms wide. He wiggled his toes. “I feel strong,” he said.

Ding! The cookies were ready. They were golden and soft. The kitchen smelled like bananas and hugs. Miko took a tiny bite. Warm, chewy, sweet.

“How do they taste?” Nana asked.

“They taste like brave and kind,” Miko said. “They taste like ‘I can fix it.’”

Nana hugged him. “You learned wellness today. You paused, breathed, noticed, made amends, and made a plan.”

Miko smiled, eyes bright. “Next time, I will remember two hands. I can be independent and careful.”

“Yes,” Nana said. “Being independent means asking for help when you need it, and being gentle with yourself.”

Miko nodded. He looked at the shining floor and the happy cookies. “I am a courageous monkey,” he said. “And I can make things right.”

The warm kitchen glowed. The kettle sang. Miko and Nana shared cookies and smiles. Their hearts felt calm and full.

Lesson: When a mistake happens, take a wellness pause—breathe, notice, say sorry, and make a plan. With kind hands, careful steps, and brave hearts, we can fix mistakes and feel good inside.

The End.
