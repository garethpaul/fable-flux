# Rilla Rabbit’s Backyard Band

Rilla was a determined rabbit with soft gray ears and bright, curious eyes. She lived beside a sunny backyard full of green grass, sweet daisies, and a neat wooden table. The air smelled like warm dirt and fresh leaves. Bees hummed, and a gentle breeze tickled Rilla’s whiskers.

“Today, I will make music,” Rilla said, patting her chest. “I will write a song, and I will play it calmly.”

She hopped to the wooden table. On top, she placed a small notebook, a stubby blue crayon, and a shiny metal tin. The tin went ting! when she tapped it. She smiled.

“Hello, notebook,” she whispered. “We will write together.”

Rilla took a deep breath. In and out. The breeze felt cool on her nose. “First, we plan,” she said. “Then, we play.”

She drew a happy sun in the corner of the page. Then she wrote big, simple words: Song Plan.

- Tap
- Clap
- Hum

Rilla’s paw tapped the tin. Ting, ting. She clapped softly. Clap, clap. She hummed a gentle tune, mmm-mmm-mmm, like a friendly bumblebee. It felt calm and sweet.

Just then, her friend Tilly Tortoise peeked from under a marigold. “Hi, Rilla,” Tilly said in a slow, warm voice. “What are you doing?”

“I am making music,” Rilla replied. “I am writing a song. I am staying calm and neat.”

Tilly blinked. “Can I listen?”

“Yes! But first, I must organize,” Rilla said. She lined up her things: notebook on the left, crayon in the middle, tin on the right. She smoothed the table with her paw. “I play better when my space is tidy.”

Tilly nodded. “That looks nice.”

Rilla tapped the tin again. Ting, ting, ting. She wrote the sound in her notebook: ting. Then she clapped and wrote clap. She hummed and drew wavy lines to show the soft notes.

A gentle whoosh of wind brushed the daisies. The petals smelled like honey. Rilla breathed in. “I feel calm,” she said. “Calm helps my music.”

A bluebird landed on the fence. “Chirp-chirp!” it sang.

Rilla’s ears perked up. “That is a pretty sound,” she said. She wrote chirp and drew a tiny bird. “Thank you, Bluebird!”

“Chirp!” said the bird, fluffing its feathers.

Rilla tried to play all the sounds together. Tap-ting, clap, hum, chirp! But the sounds bumped into each other. The tin was loud, the claps were fast, and her humming got squeaky. Rilla’s nose twitched. Her paws felt busy. Her heart thumped quickly.

“Oh no,” she said. “It sounds messy. I feel a little stormy inside.”

Tilly moved closer. “Maybe take a calm breath,” she said. “Slow and deep.”

Rilla nodded. She closed her eyes. In through her nose… out through her mouth. The sunshine warmed her ears. She felt her heart slow down. “Better,” she whispered.

She looked at her notebook. “I can try again. One step at a time. I will be patient.”

Rilla drew three boxes. In the first box, she wrote ting. In the second, clap. In the third, hum. She pointed with her crayon. “First ting, then clap, then hum.”

“Good plan,” said Tilly.

Rilla counted softly. “One… two… three…”

Ting. Clap. Mmm.

The sounds fit together like friends holding hands. The music felt smooth and kind.

Rilla smiled. “I hear it! It’s calm and pretty.”

She added chirp to the end, leaving a little space first. Ting. Clap. Mmm. Chirp! The bluebird sang right on time. Tilly swayed gently, her shell catching the sunlight.

Rilla wrote the whole pattern in her notebook with simple marks and pictures. “Now I can play it again, and again,” she said. “Writing helps me remember. Organizing helps me play. Staying calm helps me enjoy it.”

“Can I play too?” Tilly asked.

“Yes!” Rilla gave Tilly two smooth pebbles. “Tap the pebbles softly when I hum.”

They practiced. Rilla counted. One… two… three… Ting. Clap. Mmm. Tap. Chirp!

They played the song three times. Each time, the breeze carried their music across the yard. The daisies danced. The bluebird bobbed. Rilla’s whiskers twitched with joy.

Rilla giggled. “We made a backyard band!”

After the music, Rilla put the tin back on the right, the notebook on the left, and the crayon in the middle. She drew a big smiley face at the bottom of the page and wrote: Calm Song.

Tilly clapped slowly. “You did it, Rilla. You stayed calm. You got organized. You wrote your music.”

Rilla felt proud. Her tummy felt warm, like sunshine in a cup. “I learned something,” she said. “When I feel messy inside, I can breathe. I can make a plan. I can write my ideas. That helps my music sound clear.”

The bluebird chirped a happy goodbye and flew to a tall tree. The backyard glowed with soft light. Rilla hugged her notebook. “Tomorrow, we can write a new song,” she said. “Maybe a quiet bedtime tune.”

Tilly smiled. “I’ll bring more pebbles.”

Rilla nodded. “Music is fun. Writing is helpful. Calm hearts make beautiful sounds.”

The End.
