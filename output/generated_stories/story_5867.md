# Benny Beaver’s Balcony Workout

Benny was a busy beaver with a flat, paddle tail and bright, curious eyes. He didn’t live by a river. He lived with his family in a cozy apartment with a sunny balcony garden. The balcony had green pots, tiny trees, and a big blue watering can. It smelled like mint and wet soil. When the wind blew, the leaves made a soft shhh sound.

One morning, the sun peeped over the buildings. Birds chirped, “Tweet-tweet!” Benny stretched his arms up high. “Good morning, garden!” he said. “Today, I want to move my body. I want to do exercise!”

Mama Beaver smiled. “Exercise helps your body grow strong and your brain feel calm,” she said. “But remember to listen to your body. Little breaks help, too.”

Benny nodded. He bounced onto the balcony. His friend Dot the ladybug sat on a yellow flower. “Hi, Benny!” Dot buzzed. “What are you doing?”

“I’m making a balcony workout,” Benny said. “I’ll hop, I’ll bend, I’ll stretch!” He sniffed the mint. Mmm, fresh and tingly.

Benny started with a warm-up. “March, march, march,” he said, lifting his knees. Tap-tap went his feet on the balcony floor. His tail swish-swish-ed. He waved to the tomatoes. “Hi, red friends!”

Dot clapped tiny claps. “Go, Benny!”

Next, Benny did big arm circles. “Round and round,” he said. The air felt cool on his fur. He laughed. “This feels like flying!”

Neighbor Nico, a small boy with curly hair, peeked over the balcony divider. “Hi, Benny! Can I try?”

“Yes!” said Benny. “Let’s share the space.” He scooted his flower pots to make a wide path. “We can make a workout path. We can take turns.”

They set up simple stations:
- The Hop Spot by the mint
- The Bend Bench by the blue watering can
- The Balance Line, a chalk line near the tiny tree

“First, Hop Spot,” Benny said. “Ready? Hop like frogs!” He bent his knees and hopped. Boing! Boing! His tail thumped softly. Nico hopped too. Boing! Boing! They counted, “One, two, three, four, five!”

Benny felt warm and happy. “Water break,” he said, putting a paw on his chest. “I feel my heart going thump-thump.” He took a sip and breathed in and out. “I’m listening to my body.”

At the Bend Bench, Benny reached for his toes. “Slow and gentle,” he said. He smelled the soil. It smelled like rain. Nico copied him. “I feel a stretch,” Nico said with a grin.

Then they tried the Balance Line. Benny put one paw in front of the other. “Arms out like airplane wings.” He wobbled. “Whoa!” He giggled and tried again. Step, step, step. “I did it!”

Dot zipped around. “You two are strong! You two are brave!”

Soon, Lila the cat from next door peeked in. “May I join?” she purred.

Benny nodded. “Yes. We can all share. But we move one at a time, so no one bumps.” He placed smooth stones to show where to wait. “Waiting stones,” he said. “We can cheer while we wait.”

Nico hopped on the Hop Spot while Lila balanced on the line. Benny waited on a stone and clapped. “Go, friends!” He felt a tiny itch to jump in right away, but he took a deep breath. “I can wait,” he whispered. “I can be calm.”

When it was Benny’s turn, he jumped high. Boing! “I feel strong!” He heard the leaves whisper and the birds sing. The sun felt warm on his fur. He smiled so big his cheeks felt stretchy.

They made up new moves:
- Tomato Twists: “Twist side to side like turning to say hello to every tomato.”
- Watering Can Lifts: “Lift a pretend can. Up, down. Up, down.”
- Leaf Reaches: “Reach-tickle-reach the sky. Wiggle fingers!”

Benny noticed Dot looking a little small in the big space. “We can make a tiny workout, too,” Benny said. He placed a tiny leaf for Dot’s hop. “This is for you.” He was generous and kind.

Dot beamed. “Thank you! I can do Dot Hops!” She hopped from leaf to leaf. “Hop, hop, hop!”

After more moves, Benny felt his legs a little wiggly and his breath fast. He put a paw on his tummy. “Time for a calm break,” he said. He sat on the mat. “Smell the flower.” He breathed in. “Blow the candle.” He breathed out. Nico and Lila did it, too. In, out. In, out. Their bodies felt peaceful.

They ended with a stretch circle. “We say thank you to our bodies,” Benny said. “Thank you, legs. Thank you, arms. Thank you, tail!” His tail gave a happy thump.

Mama brought orange slices. “You shared the space and took turns,” she said. “You listened to your bodies. That is smart self-regulation.”

Benny passed the plate around. “Here you go, Nico. Here you go, Lila. Dot, here’s a tiny drop.” The orange smelled sweet and juicy. They all said, “Mmm!”

Nico waved. “I can make a workout at my balcony, too! I’ll share with my sister.”

Lila purred. “I will invite my grandma.”

Benny looked at the plants. The garden looked bright and proud. He felt proud, too. “Exercise makes me strong,” he said. “Sharing makes us happy. Taking turns keeps us safe. Listening to our bodies helps us feel calm.”

The sun dipped a little, and the wind said shhh again. Benny curled his tail. “Tomorrow,” he said, “we can do it again.”

The End.
