# Buttons the Determined Rabbit

Buttons the rabbit had soft gray fur and long, strong ears. Buttons liked to try and try until a job was done. One sunny afternoon, Buttons visited a friend named Mia. Mia had a cozy house. It smelled like warm vanilla and clean soap. The guest room was bright and tidy, with a soft blue rug and a big window that let in dancing light.

“Welcome, Buttons!” Mia said with a smile. “You can play in the guest room while I make a snack.”

Buttons’ nose twitched. The guest room felt special and quiet. On the bed lay a neat pile of colorful paper, shiny stickers, and fat markers. There was also a small sign on the table that said, “Guest Room. Please be gentle.”

Buttons’ heart hopped with excitement. “I will make a big picture! I will make it for Mia!” Buttons whispered. Buttons loved to make art. Buttons was determined to make the best picture ever.

Buttons began to draw. Scratch, scratch went the marker on the paper. Swish, swish went the soft curtains in the breeze. The paper smelled sweet and new. Buttons drew a bright sun and a happy house. Buttons added smiling flowers and a jumping rabbit with long ears.

“I need more space,” Buttons said. Buttons looked around. The bed looked wide and perfect. The wall looked big and blank, like a very large paper.

Buttons felt a little tug in the tummy, a tiny unsure feeling. The sign said, “Please be gentle.” Buttons looked at the wall. “It would look so pretty on the wall,” Buttons whispered. “But is it gentle to draw on a wall?”

Just then Mia peeked in. “How is it going?” she asked.

Buttons lifted the drawing. “I am making a picture for you! But I want it to be bigger. Can I draw on the wall?”

Mia’s eyes grew wide, then kind. “Thank you for asking, Buttons,” she said softly. “I love that you want to make art for me. The wall is part of the house. The guest room is a special place. We need to respect it and keep it clean.”

Buttons’ ears drooped a little. “Respect means… be gentle and careful?”

“Yes,” said Mia. “Respect means we think about other people’s things and feelings. We take care of shared spaces.”

Buttons took a deep breath. The room smelled like orange juice now—Mia must be making a snack. Buttons’ tummy rumbled. “I still want to make it big,” Buttons said. “I am determined.”

Mia nodded. “Let’s find a way together. We can use the big blue rug? No, we should not draw on rugs. What else?”

Buttons looked and looked. The sun shone on a roll of tape on the shelf. The light made it sparkle. Buttons’ eyes lit up. “We can put many papers together!” Buttons said. “We can tape them to make one big paper. Then we can hang it on the wall with gentle tape that comes off.”

Mia clapped softly. “That is a respectful idea! We can protect the wall and still make art.”

They worked together. The tape made little rip sounds. Rip, press. Rip, press. Buttons and Mia made a giant paper blanket. They used soft, safe tape that would not hurt paint. Buttons felt proud. Buttons’ ears stood tall.

“Now we can draw,” Buttons said. Buttons drew the big sun even bigger. Mia drew a bright kite. Buttons added a pond with little fish. Mia added clouds like cotton. They listened to the birds outside going tweet-tweet. The markers smelled like grapes and strawberries. Their fingers got colorful, but the wall stayed clean.

“Let’s add stickers,” Mia said.

“Stars!” Buttons cheered. They stuck shiny stars in the sky. The picture sparkled when the light moved. Buttons felt warm inside. Working together felt good.

When the picture was done, they stepped back. The guest room looked joyful and safe. The wall was fine. The big picture hung gently, like a friendly flag.

Mia smiled. “Thank you for respecting my home, Buttons,” she said. “You asked before drawing on the wall. You listened. You were careful and kind.”

Buttons’ nose twitched proudly. “I wanted to make you happy,” Buttons said. “I learned that respect means asking first, listening, and taking care of shared spaces.”

“And it also means working together,” Mia added. “Your idea made this possible.”

Buttons’ tummy rumbled again. Mia laughed. “Time for snacks!” She brought in orange slices and small crackers. The orange was juicy and sweet. The crackers were crunchy. They sat on the soft blue rug and looked at their art.

“I like how the fish sparkle,” said Buttons.

“I like how the rabbit is jumping,” said Mia.

Buttons touched the safe tape. “I am glad we used this,” Buttons said. “I am glad we were gentle.”

Mia nodded. “Me too.”

Buttons wiggled with joy. “Today, I learned to respect others’ things. I learned to ask and to find a kind way. I learned that friends can make big art together.”

Mia raised her cup of water. “To respect, friendship, and cooperation,” she said.

Buttons raised a cracker. “To creative art!” Buttons giggled.

They shared a happy hug. The guest room felt cozy and calm. The sun made soft squares of light on the floor. The big picture smiled down at them.

The End.
