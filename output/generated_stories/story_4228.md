# Pebble the Penguin Listens

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with shiny black feathers and a bright orange beak. One morning, Pebble waddled into the bathroom. The tiles felt cool on Pebble’s little webbed feet. Drip, drip, drip went the silver faucet. The mirror was foggy and soft like a cloud.

“Good morning, bathroom!” Pebble chirped. “I’m ready for bubbles and stories!”

Pebble loved bath time. Pebble also loved to talk. Pebble talked about snowflakes. Pebble talked about fish. Pebble talked about everything, all at once!

Mama Penguin came in with a fluffy blue towel. “Pebble,” Mama said gently, “first we listen, then we do. Listening helps us stay safe and learn.”

Pebble bounced. “I can listen and talk and splash!” Pebble giggled and turned the faucet. Water whooshed out. Bubbles bloomed like tiny white mountains.

“Wait, Pebble,” said Mama. “Please listen. Test the water first with your flipper. It might be too hot.”

Pebble heard the words, but Pebble’s mouth was busy telling a long story. “Once there was a brave penguin who swam across the biggest, splashiest sea! The sea went whoosh, whoosh—”

“Pebble,” Mama said again, “pause and listen.”

But Pebble did not pause. Pebble dipped both flippers in at once. “Eep!” Pebble squeaked. The water was too warm. Pebble pulled back fast and shook, speckling tiny drops everywhere. The drops felt prickly on Pebble’s skin.

Mama knelt beside Pebble. “Are you okay?”

Pebble nodded, eyes wide. “I forgot to listen.”

Mama smiled softly and turned the knob. The water cooled. “Let’s try again. Listen to my words, and listen to the sounds. The water says shhh when it’s gentle. Your tummy says calm when it feels safe.”

Pebble took a deep breath. Shhh went the water. Drip, drip went the faucet. Pebble’s tummy felt calm. Pebble touched the water with just one flipper. “Ahh,” Pebble sighed. “Just right.”

Pebble slid into the tub. The bubbles smelled like sweet soap. They tickled Pebble’s beak. “Pop, pop,” went the tiny bubbles. Pebble smiled.

Mama began to wash behind Pebble’s ears. “Pebble, I have a story for you,” she said. “Do you want to hear it?”

Pebble’s eyes sparkled. Pebble loved stories. But Pebble also loved to tell stories. Pebble opened their beak to start a big tale about a giant fish.

Then Pebble remembered the warm water surprise. Pebble put a flipper on the tub edge and said, “I will listen.”

Mama nodded. “Thank you for listening.”

Mama told the story in a soft, cozy voice. “Once there was a little penguin who found a shiny shell. The shell whispered, ‘Thank you,’ when the penguin washed it gently.”

Pebble listened. The bathroom was warm and steamy. The mirror made foggy shapes. The towel felt fluffy on Pebble’s neck. Pebble’s heart felt happy and slow.

Mama continued, “The little penguin learned that listening helped the shell feel safe. And saying thank you helped the penguin feel kind.”

Pebble whispered, “Thank you, Mama.”

“That’s gratitude,” Mama said. “Gratitude means saying thank you and feeling thankful.”

Pebble nodded. “I feel thankful for warm water. I feel thankful for bubbles. I feel thankful for you.”

Mama smiled. “I’m thankful for you too.”

Pebble wanted to tell a story now. Pebble wiggled with excitement. “May I tell a story?” Pebble asked.

“Yes,” said Mama, “and I will listen.”

Pebble felt proud. Pebble began, “Once there was a brave penguin named Pebble who listened with both ears and with a kind heart.” Pebble paused to think. “Pebble heard the water’s shhh. Pebble heard Mama’s gentle words. Because Pebble listened, Pebble stayed safe and felt brave.”

Mama nodded. “I love your story.”

Pebble added, “The penguin said thank you to the soap, to the towel, and to Mama.”

“Thank you, towel,” Pebble said out loud, giving it a little pat. The towel was soft like a cloud. “Thank you, soap,” Pebble giggled, making a foam hat. The foam made a funny mustache on Pebble’s beak. Pebble and Mama laughed together.

Soon bath time was done. Mama wrapped Pebble in the fluffy blue towel. It was warm and cozy. Pebble listened to the quiet drip, drip. Pebble listened to Mama’s heartbeat in a gentle hug. Thump-thump, thump-thump. Pebble felt safe.

Mama asked, “What did you learn today?”

Pebble took a breath and said slowly, “I learned to pause and listen. Listening keeps me safe. Listening helps me learn. Listening helps me share and care. And saying thank you makes my heart happy.”

Mama kissed Pebble’s head. “That is a wonderful lesson.”

Pebble smiled a big penguin smile. “Tomorrow, I will listen first, then splash.”

Mama chuckled. “That sounds like a plan.”

Pebble waddled out of the bathroom, clean and proud. The tiles were cool, the air smelled like soap, and the mirror slowly cleared. Pebble whispered, “Thank you, bathroom. Thank you, story. Thank you, ears, for listening.”

And the bathroom seemed to answer with a gentle shhh, like a soft, happy song.

The End.
