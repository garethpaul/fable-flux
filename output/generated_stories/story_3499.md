# Splash the Dolphin Gets Ready

Splash the dolphin loved living in the big bathtub in the Johnson family's bathroom. The warm, bubbly water felt so nice on his smooth gray skin, and he could swim in circles all day long. But today was different – today was bath time for little Emma!

"Splash, splash!" went the water as Splash dove and played. He was having so much fun that he didn't notice Emma's mom coming into the bathroom.

"Oh my!" said Mom, looking at the messy bathroom. "Splash, we need to get ready for Emma's bath time. Can you help me prepare?"

Splash popped his head up from the water. "But I'm playing!" he squeaked in his happy dolphin voice. "Can't I play just a little bit longer?"

Mom smiled kindly. "I understand you're having fun, Splash. But Emma needs her bath soon, and we need to get everything ready first. Being responsible means preparing ahead of time."

Splash thought about this. He loved Emma and wanted her to have a nice, clean bath. "Okay," he said slowly. "But can we make it fun?"

"That's great negotiation, Splash!" Mom said. "Let's work together. First, can you help me gather all the bath toys?"

Splash's eyes lit up. This sounded like a game! He swam around the tub, using his nose to push the rubber ducky, the colorful boats, and the foam letters toward the edge. "Look, Mom! I found them all!"

"Wonderful!" Mom said, scooping up the toys. "Now, can you help me check if the water temperature is just right for Emma?"

Splash dove down and swam around. The water felt warm and cozy, not too hot and not too cold. "It feels perfect!" he reported, doing a little flip to show how comfortable it was.

"Excellent work!" Mom praised. "Now we need to make sure we have Emma's special shampoo and her soft towel ready."

Splash watched as Mom placed the gentle, tear-free shampoo on the edge of the tub and hung Emma's fluffy yellow towel nearby. "Why do we need to get everything ready first?" Splash asked curiously.

Mom sat down next to the tub. "When we prepare ahead of time, everything goes smoothly. Emma won't have to wait, and bath time will be more fun for everyone. That's what being responsible means – thinking ahead and getting ready."

Just then, Emma toddled into the bathroom with her dad. "Splash!" she giggled, clapping her little hands when she saw her dolphin friend.

"Look, Emma," Mom said. "Splash helped prepare everything for your bath. He gathered the toys, checked the water, and made sure everything was ready."

Emma smiled big. "Thank you, Splash!"

Splash felt proud and happy. Getting ready hadn't been boring at all – it had been helpful and fun! As Emma climbed into the tub, Splash gently swam around her, making sure she felt safe and happy.

"You know what, Mom?" Splash said as Emma giggled and played with her toys. "Preparing really does make everything better. Emma is so happy, and I feel good about helping!"

Mom nodded. "That's right, Splash. When we prepare and take responsibility, we help others and ourselves. You're a very good friend."

From that day on, Splash always helped prepare for bath time. He learned that being responsible and getting ready ahead of time made everything more fun and helped everyone feel happy and safe.

The End.
