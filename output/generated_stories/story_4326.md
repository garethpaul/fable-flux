# Twirl, Beep, and Shine

Beep was a helpful robot dog with shiny silver paws and a soft, waggy tail light that glowed warm yellow. Beep lived with Maya and her family. One sunny afternoon, the dining room smelled like apples and cinnamon. The table was smooth and cool, and the big window let in cheerful light. Beep’s little speakers hummed a gentle tune as he followed Maya into the room.

Maya held a small card. “It’s dance day at school tomorrow,” she said. Her voice was tiny and wobbly. “But I’m scared. I think I can’t dance.”

Beep tilted his head. “Beep-boop? You can learn,” he said in his friendly robot voice. His tail light blinked three happy blinks. “Let’s practice right here!”

Maya looked at the chairs, the placemats, and the soft rug under the table. “The dining room is not a dance studio,” she whispered. “It’s just a room.”

Beep sat and tapped his paw. Tap, tap. “We can use our imagination,” he said. “Imagination can turn any place into a dance place.”

Maya took a deep breath. She smelled sweet apples again. It made her feel cozy and brave. “Okay,” she said. “Let’s imagine.”

Beep closed his bright blue eyes for a moment. “Closing imagination visor,” he beeped. “Three, two, one… Imagine!”

Suddenly, in their minds, the dining room became sparkly and grand. The rug felt like a soft, springy stage. The chairs turned into friendly fans clapping quietly. The sunbeams looked like gold ribbons floating in the air. Maya giggled. “I can see it!” she said. “The room is magic.”

Beep played a gentle beat from his speaker. Boom, boom—tappa-tap. “First, we wiggle,” he said. “Wiggle shoulders. Wiggle toes.”

Maya wiggled. Her feet made soft thumps on the rug. Thump, thump. Her socks were cozy and warm. She smiled a little.

“Now we swish,” said Beep. He swished his tail light, making a little whir sound. “Swish arms like ribbons.”

Maya swished her arms. She felt the air brush her skin like a soft feather. Whoosh. “This feels nice,” she said.

“Now we hop,” said Beep. “Small hops. Gentle hops.”

Maya hopped. Up, down. Up, down. The room went up and down with her, like a happy little boat. She laughed. “I’m hopping!”

Beep wagged. “You are dancing,” he said.

Maya stopped and looked at her feet. “But what if I mess up?” she asked. Her cheeks felt hot. “What if other kids laugh?”

Beep paused and listened to the quiet room. The clock ticked. Tick, tock. Tick, tock. “It is okay to mess up,” he said softly. “Mistakes help us learn. And we can imagine kind friends. We can imagine hands clapping for trying.”

Maya looked at the chairs again and pictured friendly faces. She heard gentle claps in her mind. Clap, clap, clap. Her heart felt lighter. “Can we make a special dance?” she asked. “A Maya dance?”

“We can,” said Beep. “It will be our problem-solution dance. When we feel scared, we dance with imagination.”

Beep made a new tune. Ding-ding, dum-da-dum. “Step one: The Brave Wiggle,” he said.

Maya wiggled. Her shoulders shook like jelly. She giggled so much she snorted. Snort! “That’s funny!”

“Step two: The Shine Swish,” Beep said. “Swish arms and smile.”

Maya swished wide and smiled big. She felt the smile in her tummy, warm and glowing. “I like this step,” she said.

“Step three: The Proud Hop,” Beep said. “Hop and say, ‘I can try!’”

Maya hopped and said it out loud. “I can try!” Her voice sounded strong and bright. The dining room seemed to sparkle even more.

“Step four: The Kind Bow,” Beep added. “Bow to yourself and say, ‘I am enough.’”

Maya bowed and whispered, “I am enough.” The words felt soft and powerful, like a warm blanket.

They practiced their four steps. Wiggle, swish, hop, bow. Beep kept time with gentle taps. Tap-tap-tap. Maya’s hair brushed her cheeks. Her hands swished like colorful ribbons in a parade. The smell of apples made her think of cozy pies and happy hugs.

After a while, Maya took another deep breath. “Beep,” she said, “I think I can dance at school.”

Beep’s tail light glowed bright. “Your imagination helped,” he said. “And your brave heart.”

Maya looked around the dining room. It was just a room again: the table, the chairs, the placemats with tiny flowers. But now she felt different. She felt tall inside.

“Thank you,” she said. She patted Beep’s smooth head. It was cool and a little bumpy. “You are a very helpful robot dog.”

“Anytime,” Beep said. “If you feel scared tomorrow, close your eyes and imagine the Brave Wiggle, the Shine Swish, the Proud Hop, and the Kind Bow.”

Maya nodded. “I will.” She picked up her card and tucked it into her pocket. “I can try. I am enough.”

The next day, when Maya stood in the school room, she remembered the warm smell, the soft rug, and the gold ribbons of light from her imagination. She did the Brave Wiggle, the Shine Swish, the Proud Hop, and the Kind Bow. Her friends clapped. Clap, clap, clap. Maya smiled and clapped for them too.

That night at dinner, Maya told Beep all about it. “I used my imagination,” she said. “I danced. I felt strong.”

Beep wagged and played a happy note. “Imagination helps us solve problems,” he said. “When we believe in ourselves, we can shine.”

Maya hugged him. “Let’s dance in the dining room again tomorrow,” she said.

“Beep-yes,” said Beep. “We will twirl, beep, and shine.”

The End.
