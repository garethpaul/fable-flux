# Joey Roo and the Porch Parade

Every afternoon, Joey Roo bounced out to the peaceful front porch. The porch was cool and shady. The wooden boards felt smooth under his big, soft feet. A gentle breeze smelled like lemon tea and gum leaves. Birds chirped sweet songs from the fence.

Joey Roo loved the porch. He loved the bright flower pots. He loved the comfy chair with the striped cushion. He loved the creaky step that went squeak when he hopped.

One sunny day, Joey Roo heard a clatter. “Clink, clank, clonk!” A little turtle, wearing a tiny red cap, slowly pushed a metal wagon up the path.

“Hello!” Joey Roo called. “I’m Joey Roo. Do you need help?”

“Yes, please,” said the turtle. “I’m Tully. I like to make music. I brought bells and spoons and a drum. I want to play on your porch. Is that okay?”

Joey Roo smiled. He loved to share. “We can make a porch parade!” he said. “We can invite friends.”

Tully’s eyes twinkled. “That sounds fun.”

Soon, friends came. Pip the parrot fluttered down with bright green feathers. “Squawk! I sing!” Pip said. Minka the mouse tiptoed up with a tiny broom. “I can sweep, so it stays tidy,” she squeaked. Benny the bilby arrived with a paintbrush. “I can paint a sign,” he said. “Music today!”

Everyone was excited. Joey Roo’s tail thumped happily. “Let’s start!” he cheered.

Tully rang the bells. “Ding, ding!” Pip sang, “La-la-la!” Joey Roo clapped. “Clap, clap!” Minka swept gently. “Swish, swish.” Benny painted a sunny yellow sign. The porch felt alive. The breeze danced with the sounds.

Then, a new friend shuffled close. It was Wally the wombat. He covered his ears. “Oh dear,” Wally said. “It is too loud for me. My ears feel big today. My belly feels rumbly. I like quiet.”

Joey Roo stopped clapping. He looked at Wally’s face. Wally’s eyes were watery. His shoulders were droopy.

Joey Roo took a slow breath. The porch was for everyone. He remembered what Mama Roo always said: “Listen first. Kind words help.”

Joey Roo hopped to Wally. “Hi, Wally. Thank you for telling us. We don’t want you to hurt. Can you tell us more?”

Wally nodded. “I want to sit on the porch and feel the breeze. But the drum is boomy. The bells are bright. My head feels full.”

Pip landed on the railing and tucked his feathers. “Squawk… I mean, okay,” he said softly. “I can sing quieter.”

Tully held the drum. His face looked worried. “I like the drum,” he whispered. “It makes me happy. But I don’t want to make Wally sad.”

Joey Roo felt a little tug in his chest. He liked the parade. He also wanted Wally to stay. What should they do?

He sat on the squeaky step and thought. “Squeak,” went the wood. Joey Roo smiled. He had an idea.

“We live together on this street,” he said. “This porch is where we gather. We can take care of each other. That is our porch promise—our civic job. Let’s make a plan so we all feel okay.”

Minka nodded. “A plan is good.”

Joey Roo held up one paw. “First, let’s ask, not assume. Let’s talk and listen.”

He held up another paw. “Second, let’s make quiet times and loud times.”

He swished his tail. “Third, let’s set a ‘soft corner’ with pillows for ears that need rest.”

Benny grinned. “I can paint a clock on the sign,” he said. “Loud time and quiet time.”

Tully tapped the drum softly. “I can use the drum gently during quiet time. Boom… soft.” He smiled. “And I can use the bells during loud time.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “I can sing like a whisper wind,” he said. “Laaa… soft.”

Wally’s eyes brightened. “I can bring ear muffs for anyone who needs them,” he offered. “And I can clap softly.”

Everyone cheered, but quietly. “Hooray,” whispered Joey Roo.

They got to work. Benny painted a big round clock with two colors: blue for quiet, yellow for loud. Minka made a soft corner with the striped cushion and two extra pillows. Pip practiced whisper-singing. Tully tried gentle drumming on his lap. Wally set a little basket of ear muffs by the door.

Joey Roo rang a tiny bell. “It’s quiet time,” he said.

The porch hummed gently. “Ding… soft.” Pip sang like a breeze. “Laaa…” Tully tapped. “Tip… tap.” Wally sighed happily and closed his eyes. The breeze smelled sweet. The porch felt calm.

Joey Roo rang the bell again. “Now it is loud time!”

Tully played the drum. “Boom, boom!” Pip sang bright and bold. “La-la-la!” Joey Roo clapped and stamped. The porch felt joyful and bouncy. Wally put on ear muffs and swayed. He smiled.

They took turns—quiet time, loud time, quiet time again. They watched the painted clock. They used kind words. They asked, “How do you feel?” They said, “Thank you for telling me.” Everyone felt safe and welcome.

At sunset, the sky blushed pink and gold. The porch smelled like warm wood. Joey Roo’s heart felt big.

“Today we learned something important,” he said. “Tolerance means we make room for each other. We listen. We share space. We take care of our porch and our friends. That is good for everyone.”

Wally nodded. “I stayed, and I felt okay.”

Tully tapped two soft beats. “I played, and I felt happy.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “I sang, and I felt heard.”

Minka swept the last speck. “We kept it tidy together.”

Benny pointed to the sign. “And we made a plan!”

Joey Roo hugged his friends with a gentle bounce. “Our porch is for all of us,” he said. “When we talk and care, we all belong.”

The End.
