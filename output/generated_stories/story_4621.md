# Leo's Garden Adventure

Leo the little lion cub stood on the sunny balcony garden. His golden fur sparkled in the morning light. Red tomatoes hung heavy on their vines. Green herbs smelled fresh and wonderful. But Leo felt worried and unsure.

"I have so many jobs to do today," Leo said with a small sigh. His mama had asked him to water the plants, pick the ripe tomatoes, and pull the weedy grass. The tasks seemed so big for such a little cub.

He looked at the watering can. It was almost as tall as he was! The tomato plants reached high above his fuzzy ears. Leo's whiskers drooped. "What if I can't do it all?" he whispered.

Just then, a friendly butterfly landed on a purple flower. Her wings were orange and black like a sunset. "Good morning, little lion," she said in a tinkling voice. "You look worried."

"Hello, butterfly," Leo said politely. "I have lots of work to do, but everything seems too big for me."

The butterfly smiled. "My name is Bella. What if we break your big jobs into tiny steps? That's what I do when I have far to fly."

Leo's ears perked up. "Really? How?"

"First, let's start with just one plant," Bella suggested. She fluttered to the smallest pot. "Can you water this little mint plant?"

Leo picked up the watering can with both paws. It was heavy, but he could manage it for just one plant. The water made a gentle splashing sound. The mint leaves smelled cool and fresh. "I did it!" Leo said proudly.

"Wonderful!" chirped Bella. "Now try the next one."

Step by step, Leo watered each plant. His confidence grew with every splash. The soil turned dark and rich. The plants seemed to dance with joy.

"Now for the tomatoes," said Bella. "Just pick the red ones that come off easily."

Leo stretched up on his tiptoes. He gently twisted a bright red tomato. Pop! It came right off. The tomato felt smooth and warm from the sun. "This is fun!" Leo laughed.

One by one, Leo filled his basket with ruby-red tomatoes. Some were big, some were small. All were perfect.

"Last job," said Bella. "Those weeds by the carrots."

Leo knelt down and pulled the unwanted grass. The earth felt cool under his paws. Soon, the carrot tops had room to grow.

When Mama Lion came to check, she gasped with delight. "Leo! You finished everything! The garden looks beautiful."

Leo's chest puffed out with pride. His golden mane seemed to shine brighter. "I did it all by myself! Well, Bella helped me learn to take small steps."

"Taking small steps is very wise," said Mama. "You should feel proud of completing all your tasks."

That evening, Leo sat in the garden telling Bella about his day. "I learned something important," he said. "Big jobs aren't scary when you break them into little pieces."

Bella nodded. "And now you have a wonderful story to tell about your brave day in the garden."

Leo smiled wide, showing his tiny teeth. He had completed every task, and his heart felt full of joy and confidence. Tomorrow, he would be ready for new adventures.

The End.
