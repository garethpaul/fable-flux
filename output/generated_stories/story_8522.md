# Momo’s Porch of Calm Words

Momo was a courageous monkey with bright eyes and gentle hands. Every morning, Momo sat on the peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like sweet oranges from the tree nearby. The wooden steps were warm from the sun. Birds chirped soft songs, “Tweet tweet,” and a breeze whispered, “Whooo,” over Momo’s fur.

“Good morning, porch,” Momo said. “I am ready to be well today.”

Momo liked taking care of body and heart. Momo had a little routine. First, a big stretch. “Up, up, reach to the sky!” Momo said. Fingers wiggled. Toes wiggled. Shoulders rolled. It felt good, like a gentle hug.

Next, Momo took deep breaths. “Smell the flower,” Momo whispered, breathing in. “Blow the feather,” Momo whispered, breathing out. In and out. In and out. The air tasted like orange and sunshine. Momo felt calm and strong.

Just then, a tiny ant marched across the porch rail. “Hello, Ant,” Momo said kindly. “I see you are working.”

The ant paused. “I carry a crumb,” the ant seemed to say with its little legs. Momo smiled. “You are responsible. I can be responsible too.”

Momo checked the porch. A watering can sat by a pot of mint leaves. The mint smelled cool, like a tiny breeze on the tongue. Momo lifted the can carefully. “I will water just enough,” Momo said. “Not too much. Not too little.” Momo tipped the can and watched the water sparkle. The mint leaves wiggled happily.

Footsteps came down the path. It was Nana Turtle, slow and sweet. Her shell was shiny like smooth pebbles. “Good morning, Momo,” Nana Turtle said. Her voice was soft and round.

“Good morning, Nana Turtle,” Momo said. “Would you like to sit on the porch and breathe with me?”

“Yes, please,” Nana Turtle said, smiling. She climbed one step, two steps, three steps. “Oof,” she puffed. “That was a lot.”

Momo patted the step. “Let’s smell the flower. Blow the feather.” Together they breathed. In and out. In and out. The porch felt even more peaceful.

Soon, Pip Parrot flew by and landed on the rail. “Squawk! Words are wonderful!” Pip said. His feathers were red like apples and green like leaves.

“I like words too,” Momo said. “We can use kind words. We can use calm words.”

“Show me,” Pip said, hopping.

Momo nodded. “When I need help, I say, ‘Please.’ When I’m thankful, I say, ‘Thank you.’ When I bump someone, I say, ‘I’m sorry.’”

Pip bobbed. “Please. Thank you. I’m sorry,” he repeated. “Words help!”

Nana Turtle smiled. “Words can be soft as the breeze and sweet as mint.”

Then a small sound came from the path. “Sniff… sniff…” It was Dottie Deer. Her big eyes looked shiny with tears.

Momo put a gentle hand on Dottie’s shoulder. “Hello, Dottie. I see you.” Momo spoke slow and calm. “Do you want to share?”

Dottie sniffed. “My ribbon fell in a muddy puddle,” she said. “I feel sad.”

Momo breathed in. “Smell the flower.” Momo breathed out. “Blow the feather.” Then Momo said, “We can help. We can use calm words and careful hands.”

Pip Parrot hopped closer. “Please, may I bring a cloth?” he asked, and flew off to fetch one.

Nana Turtle said, “I can hold the bowl.” She scooted to the side and took a small bowl from her bag.

Momo brought warm water. “We will take turns,” Momo said. “We will be patient.” The porch smelled like warm water and mint. The bowl felt smooth and quiet in Momo’s hands.

Dottie took a deep breath like Momo. “Smell the flower. Blow the feather,” she said softly. Her shoulders relaxed.

Pip returned with a soft cloth. “Here you go,” he said. “Please be gentle.”

Momo dipped the cloth and dabbed the ribbon. “There,” Momo said. “Slow and steady.” Dottie watched. The mud began to fade. “Thank you,” Dottie whispered.

Momo smiled. “You’re welcome. We are a team.”

When the ribbon was clean, Dottie’s eyes sparkled. “I feel better,” she said. “My heart feels light.”

Momo nodded. “That is wellness,” Momo said. “We care for our bodies with rest and breath. We care for our hearts with kind words. We care for our friends with gentle help.”

Nana Turtle clapped softly. “You were responsible, Momo. You noticed. You helped.”

Pip Parrot fluffed his feathers. “Words are wonderful! Please. Thank you. I’m sorry. And also, I can wait my turn!”

Momo laughed. The birds sang, “Tweet tweet.” The breeze whispered, “Whooo.” The porch smelled like fresh mint and clean ribbon. The sun warmed everyone like a soft blanket.

Dottie tied the ribbon in a neat bow. “Thank you, Momo. Thank you, friends.” She took a big, calm breath. “I can breathe when I’m upset. I can use kind words. I can ask for help.”

Momo stood tall. “I am a courageous monkey,” Momo said, “and I choose wellness every day.”

They all sat together on the peaceful front porch. They stretched. They breathed. They shared words that were soft and kind. The day felt bright and safe.

Momo looked at the friends and the flowers and the sky. “Today we learned,” Momo said. “Wellness is caring for our bodies, our hearts, and our words. And being responsible helps everyone feel safe.”

Everyone nodded. “Yes,” they said.

The End.
