# Robo-Pup’s Rooftop Relay

On a sunny afternoon, a friendly robot dog named Robo-Pup padded onto a flat rooftop. His paws went click-click on the warm tiles. A soft breeze whooshed past his shiny ears. From up high, he could see tiny cars below and hear birds chirp-cheep, chirp-cheep!

“Hello, rooftop!” Robo-Pup beeped. His tail light blinked bright blue. “I am ready to play sports!”

On the rooftop, some kids stood in a circle. They had a soft, squishy ball. Mia held the ball close. Jamal bounced on his toes. Lin waved and smiled.

“Hi, Robo-Pup!” Mia called. “We want to play a game. But we don’t know how to pass. We get mixed up.”

Robo-Pup tilted his head. Beep-woof! “I can help. We can play a relay game. We will use our words. We will use our bodies. We will practice. Sports are fun when we try and talk.”

He pressed his nose button. Whirr! A gentle beeping coach voice came out. “Warm-up time.”

Robo-Pup showed the kids how to warm up. “Arms up, big stretch,” he said. “Wiggle fingers. Wiggle toes.” The kids giggled and copied. The breeze felt cool on their cheeks. The sun felt warm on their shoulders. Their shoes made soft squeaks on the roof.

“Now we breathe in,” said Robo-Pup. They breathed in—sniff, sniff—and out—whooo.

“Okay,” said Jamal, “but I still feel mixed up when I pass.”

“That is our problem,” said Robo-Pup. “We feel mixed up. We bump into each other. We don’t know who gets the ball. We will find a solution.”

Robo-Pup tapped his ear. Click. His eyes glowed green. “Step one,” he beeped. “We use words. When you want the ball, say, ‘I’m ready!’ When you pass, say, ‘Your turn!’”

Mia nodded. “I can say, ‘I’m ready!’”

“Step two,” said Robo-Pup. “We use eyes. Look at your friend. Make eye contact. Smile. That shows, ‘I see you.’”

Lin smiled a big smile. “I see you, Mia!”

“Step three,” Robo-Pup said, tail light blinking. “We use hands and feet. Bend your knees. Hold your hands out like a basket. Keep soft knees. This keeps you safe and strong.”

The kids bent. Knees soft. Hands ready. Hearts thump-thumped with happy energy.

“Let’s try,” Robo-Pup said. “Mia, start. Say the words.”

Mia hugged the ball, took a breath, and looked at Lin. “Lin, are you ready?”

“I’m ready!” Lin said, eyes bright.

“Your turn!” Mia said, and gently pushed the ball. The ball glided—shoop! Lin caught it in her basket hands.

“I did it!” Lin laughed.

“Great pass,” Robo-Pup beeped. “Great catch. That is good communication.”

They kept going. Lin looked at Jamal. “Jamal, are you ready?”

Jamal planted his feet and bent his knees. “I’m ready!”

Lin said, “Your turn!” and passed. Jamal caught it with a soft thump. “I got it!”

But then the wind picked up. Whooosh! Jamal tried to pass to Mia, but the wind pushed. The ball wobbled and rolled toward a small bump near the edge. Mia’s eyes went wide. “Oh no!”

“This is a new problem,” Robo-Pup said calmly. “The wind is strong. We need a new solution.”

Robo-Pup clicked his paw. A little orange cone popped out. Plop! He placed cones to make a safe play zone away from the edge. “Safety first,” he said. He beeped again. “Adjust for wind. Use two hands. Pass low and steady. Say the words louder so your friend can hear in the wind.”

The kids nodded. The breeze brushed their hair. They felt it on their cheeks.

“Jamal, try again,” said Robo-Pup.

Jamal held the ball with two hands. He bent his knees. He looked at Mia. “Mia, are you ready?” he called, louder this time.

“I’m ready!” Mia called back, cupping her hands.

“Your turn!” Jamal said. He passed low and steady. The ball slid along the roof—shoop, shoop—and Mia caught it safe.

“Yes!” Mia cheered. “We did it!”

Robo-Pup wagged his tail light. “Practice helps. Talking helps. Adjusting helps. That is how teams play sports.”

They made a fun relay. Pass to the left. Pass to the right. Turn around. Touch a cone. Do five gentle hops—boing, boing, boing, boing, boing! Then pass again. Their feet tapped. Their hands clapped. Their voices rose and fell with happy shouts.

“I’m ready!”

“Your turn!”

“I see you!”

The rooftop felt like a playground in the sky. The kids’ faces glowed. Their bodies felt strong and warm. Their hearts felt proud.

After a while, Lin puffed a little. “I feel tired,” she said.

“Tired is okay,” Robo-Pup said kindly. “In sports, we move. We rest. We drink water.”

He opened a little door in his side. Click! Out came tiny cups. Sip, sip. The water was cool and sweet.

“Let’s end with our cheer,” Robo-Pup said.

They all stood in a circle. Hands in. Eyes bright.

“Teamwork!” said Mia.

“Talking!” said Jamal.

“Practice!” said Lin.

“Healthy bodies!” beeped Robo-Pup.

They lifted their hands and laughed.

Robo-Pup looked around. The wind was gentle now. The sun was soft and golden. “Today we had a problem,” he said. “We felt mixed up. We found a solution. We used words. We looked with our eyes. We bent our knees. We practiced. We stayed safe. Sports help our bodies and our hearts.”

Mia hugged Robo-Pup. His metal fur felt smooth and warm from the sun. “Thank you, Robo-Pup.”

“Woof-beep! You are welcome,” he said. “Remember: Talk to your team. Move your body. Practice, practice, practice. That is how we learn.”

The kids waved as the birds chirped one more time. Chirp-cheep!

They couldn’t wait to play again tomorrow.

The End.
