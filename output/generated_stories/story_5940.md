# Nutkin's Nursery Problem

In a cozy nursery with soft yellow walls and a rocking chair by the window, lived a clever little squirrel named Nutkin. His fluffy gray tail twitched when he was thinking hard. His bright black eyes sparkled when he had good ideas.

Nutkin loved living in the nursery. The room smelled like lavender and baby powder. Sunlight streamed through lace curtains, making dancing shadows on the carpet. But Nutkin had a big problem.

"Oh no!" Nutkin chittered one morning. His tummy rumbled loudly. "I'm so hungry, but where are my acorns?"

Nutkin had hidden his winter acorns somewhere safe in the nursery. But now he couldn't remember where! He searched under the crib. He looked behind the bookshelf. He even checked inside the toy box.

"Think, Nutkin, think!" he said to himself. His tail flicked back and forth as he sat on the windowsill. "Where would be the safest place for acorns?"

Nutkin took a deep breath. He closed his eyes and thought carefully. "I need to be brave and not give up," he whispered. "And I need to think like a smart squirrel."

First, Nutkin made a plan. "Acorns need to stay dry," he said out loud. "So not near the humidifier." He crossed that spot off his mental list.

Next, he thought about temperature. "Acorns stay fresh in cool places," Nutkin remembered. The sunny windowsill was too warm. The spot near the heating vent was too hot.

Then Nutkin had an idea! He scampered to the changing table and sniffed carefully. His little pink nose twitched. He could smell something nutty and familiar.

"Wait!" Nutkin's eyes grew wide. "I remember now! I put them somewhere babies can't reach, but where air flows nicely."

Nutkin climbed up the tall dresser. His tiny claws clicked on the smooth wood. At the very top, behind a picture frame of smiling babies, was a small gap.

"Found them!" Nutkin squeaked with joy. There were his acorns, perfectly safe and fresh. He had been so smart to hide them there!

But now Nutkin faced another problem. How could he get the heavy acorns down safely? The dresser was very tall, and the acorns were round and might roll away.

Nutkin sat quietly and thought again. "I need to be brave and try something new," he said. He looked around the nursery with fresh eyes.

"The baby blanket!" Nutkin exclaimed. He carefully pulled a soft blue blanket from the crib. The blanket smelled like gentle soap and sunshine from hanging on the clothesline.

Nutkin made a plan. He tied one corner of the blanket to the dresser handle. Then he lowered the other end down like a slide. One by one, he rolled his precious acorns into the blanket pouch and gently lowered them to the floor.

"Success!" Nutkin cheered, munching on a delicious acorn. "I stayed calm, I thought carefully, and I was brave enough to try new ideas."

From that day on, Nutkin always took time to think through his problems. When he felt worried or stuck, he would take deep breaths, make plans, and remember that being brave and thinking carefully always helped him find good solutions.

The nursery felt even cozier now that Nutkin knew he could solve any problem that came his way.

The End.
