# Otter Oli’s Rooftop Garden Day

Oli the playful otter peeked out from his little door and felt the warm morning sun. “Good morning, sky!” he chirped. He wiggled his whiskers and heard birds sing, “Chee-chee, tweet-tweet!” Oli smiled. Today was Rooftop Garden Day.

Oli lived in a tall building with a big, safe rooftop. Up there, there were pots, soil, tiny watering cans, and a soft green rug that felt like grass. The rooftop smelled like wet earth and fresh air. The wind went whoosh, and the clouds drifted by like fluffy boats.

Oli clapped his paws. “I want to plant, and play, and help!” he said. He grabbed his backpack. He checked inside, just like his friend Tia Turtle taught him. “Prepare first,” Tia always said. “Check, check, check!”

Oli sang, “Water bottle?” He took it out. Slosh! “Snack?” He saw a crunchy apple. “Sun hat?” He put it on. “Sunscreen?” He dabbed his nose. “Gloves?” He wiggled them on. “I am ready!”

He paddled his paws up the stairs, pat-pat-pat, and pushed open the rooftop door. Warm light kissed his face. He smelled tomatoes and mint. “Hello, plants!” said Oli.

On the rooftop, his friends were waiting. Tia Turtle waved slowly. “Good morning, Oli.” Benny Bird hopped and chirped, “Tweet! Time to plant.” Lila Lamb bleated softly, “Let’s make the garden pretty.”

Oli’s tail swished with joy. “Let’s do an outdoor activity!” he squeaked. “Let’s plant seeds and make a play path!”

Tia nodded. “We can do both if we prepare and cooperate.” She held a list. “We need water, soil, seeds, and tools. We also need to talk calmly and share.”

Benny flapped his wings. “I can get tiny pebbles for the path.” Lila said, “I can bring soft straw for a resting spot.” Oli thumped his paws. “I can carry soil and dig little holes!”

They all took deep breaths in, and out. The breeze brushed their cheeks. The rooftop felt warm on their feet. The city hummed below, but up here it was peaceful.

Oli grabbed a small shovel. Scrape, scoop, pat. He dug little holes in a row. “One, two, three,” he counted. Lila sprinkled seeds—tick, tick, tick. Benny brought pebbles that clicked and clacked in a bucket. Tia poured water that went glug-glug and splish-splash.

Soon, Oli saw a shiny red watering can by the mint. “I want that one,” he said, reaching. At the same time, Benny hopped over. “I need it for the pebbles,” Benny chirped. Their paws and wings touched the handle.

Oli’s whiskers drooped. Benny’s feathers puffed. For a moment, the wind felt still.

Tia took a slow breath. “Let’s use peaceful words,” she said. “We can solve this together.”

Oli put his paw to his chest. “I feel sad when I can’t water,” he said softly. “I want to help our outdoor work.”

Benny tilted his head. “I feel worried the pebbles will be dusty. I want them clean.”

Lila stepped closer. “We can take turns,” she suggested. “We can also find another way.”

Tia smiled. “Good ideas. Let’s make a plan.”

Oli nodded. “Benny, you can use the watering can first to rinse the pebbles. I will count to ten and get more soil while I wait.”

Benny chirped, “Thank you, Oli. After I rinse, you can water the seeds.”

They all agreed. Benny rinsed: drip-drip, plink-plink. Oli counted: “One, two, three...” He filled a pot with soil. Lila hummed a song. Tia checked the list and gave a thumbs-up.

“Ten!” Oli said. Benny passed the watering can with a smile. “Your turn, friend.”

Oli watered the seeds gently. The water made tiny dark circles in the soil. “Drink, little seeds,” Oli whispered. The smell of wet dirt rose up, earthy and sweet.

Next, they placed pebbles in a curvy line. Click, clack, click. Lila laid soft straw under the shade. Tia drew a simple map with chalk. “Start here,” she said. “Walk the pebble path, stretch by the mint, sip water, rest on the straw.”

Oli’s eyes shone. “This is our outdoor activity station!” He bounced, then paused. “We should stretch first,” he said, remembering preparation. He showed big arm circles. “Circle, circle.” Everyone followed. They bent knees. “Bend, bend.” They wiggled fingers. “Wiggle, wiggle.”

They tried the new path. Oli stepped on the pebbles—cool and bumpy under his paws. Benny balanced with tiny hops. Lila laughed, a soft “baa,” and Tia smiled slow and wide. The breeze kissed their cheeks. The sun warmed their backs. A butterfly fluttered by, whisper-light.

After walking, they watered the mint and tomatoes again, then sat on the straw to snack. Crunch went Oli’s apple. Sweet juice dripped on his chin. He wiped it with a giggle. “Today was fun,” he said. “We worked outside, we prepared, and we cooperated.”

Benny chirped, “And we used peaceful words.”

Tia nodded. “That is how small helpers make big things.”

Lila bleated happily. “Look at our garden. We did it together.”

Oli patted the soil. “Grow strong, little seeds. We will care for you.”

As the sun drifted lower, the rooftop glowed golden. The air felt gentle and calm. Oli packed up: hat, gloves, list, and empty bottles. He checked his backpack again. “Prepare, prepare, prepare,” he sang softly.

Before going down, they all took one last walk on the pebble path. Step, step, step. They breathed in and out. Their hearts felt full.

Oli waved at the sky. “Thank you for the outdoor day,” he said. “We learned to get ready, to share, and to speak kindly.”

And the garden seemed to whisper back, in leaf-shakes and minty smells, “Yes, yes, yes.”

The End.
