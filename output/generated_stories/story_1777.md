# Sunny’s Sharing Treehouse

High in a big green oak, a little wooden treehouse sat between rustling leaves. It smelled like sweet sap and dry pine. Inside lived Sunny, a small, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail. Sunny kept tiny tools in a box, a soft blanket in a corner, and a round basket full of shiny acorns.

One warm morning, the wind whooshed and whispered. Sunny peeked out the window. “Good morning, leaves! Good morning, sky!” Sunny chirped.

Sunny pulled out a small map and a pencil. “Today I will fix the squeaky door and sort my acorns by size,” Sunny said with a brave nod. Sunny loved fixing things and making the treehouse cozy.

Creak. Creak. The door made a little song as Sunny tested it. “Hmm,” said Sunny, ears twitching. “I can make this better!”

Then Sunny heard a tiny cough. “A-choo!”

Sunny hurried to the window and saw a little bluebird on a nearby branch. The bird’s feathers were ruffled, and its eyes looked tired.

“Hello,” Sunny called softly. “I am Sunny. Are you okay?”

“My name is Pip,” said the bluebird with a small voice. “I’m cold and hungry. The wind blew my nest away.”

Sunny felt a warm squeeze in the chest. Compassion bubbled up like warm tea. “Come inside, Pip,” Sunny said. “My treehouse is safe.”

Pip hopped in. The floor felt smooth. The air smelled like sunny wood. Sunny wrapped Pip in the soft blanket and brought a tiny cup of warm acorn tea.

“Sip, sip,” said Sunny. Pip smiled and took a drink. “Thank you,” Pip whispered.

Sunny opened the acorn basket. The acorns shone like brown marbles. Sunny paused. “These are my winter acorns,” Sunny thought. “I worked hard to gather them.” Sunny’s tail swished back and forth. Sunny looked at Pip’s small, shaking wings.

Sunny took a deep breath. “Sharing helps friends,” Sunny told the heart inside. “I can be generous.”

Sunny poured some acorns into a little leaf bowl. “Here you go, Pip,” Sunny said. “Eat and rest.”

Pip chirped happily. “You are very kind, Sunny.”

Just then, the sky grew gray. The wind pushed harder. Whoooosh. Branches swayed. Leaves danced. A tiny mouse, named Dot, peeped from the door. “Squeak! May I come in?” Dot’s whiskers trembled. “The wind took my hat, and I am scared.”

Sunny opened wide arms. “Yes, Dot. Come in, come in!”

Dot shivered. Pip fluffed closer to the blanket. Sunny tucked Dot in beside Pip. “We will be cozy together,” Sunny said.

Sunny looked at the acorn basket again. “I have enough to share,” Sunny told themself. Sunny poured more acorns into a second leaf bowl. “Here, Dot. Crunch, crunch.”

Dot nibbled and smiled. “Thank you, Sunny. I feel safe.”

Now the problem grew bigger. The wind squeaked the door extra loud. Creak. Creeeak. Pip covered ears. Dot wiggled.

Sunny stood tall. “Friends, I have an idea,” Sunny said with a clear voice. “We will make the treehouse quiet and warm. We will work together.” Sunny’s voice sounded brave and calm. That is leadership.

Sunny handed Pip a soft cloth. “Pip, please hold this cloth at the window. It will stop the draft.” Pip nodded and pressed the cloth.

Sunny gave Dot a small wooden peg. “Dot, please pass me the peg when I say ‘Now!’”

“Now!” said Sunny, holding the door hinge with steady paws. Dot passed the peg. Tap, tap. Sunny gently tapped it in with a tiny mallet. The door stopped squeaking. The wind still whooshed, but inside was snug and still.

“Wow,” Pip chirped. “You fixed it!”

Sunny smiled. “We did it together.”

The storm calmed to a soft breeze. The treehouse smelled like warm tea and toasty acorns. Pip sang a little song. Dot hummed along. Sunny’s heart felt big and light.

“Sunny,” Pip said, “You shared your food and your home. You helped us feel better. That is generous.”

Sunny’s cheeks felt warm. “I was a little worried about my acorns,” Sunny admitted. “But sharing made all of us happy. And look—we still have enough.”

Dot nodded. “You also showed us what to do. You were a good leader. You used gentle words. You had a plan. That helped me feel brave.”

Sunny wrapped the blanket around all three friends. “Thank you,” Sunny said. “Helping friends is important. When we have a little extra, we can give a little extra.”

The afternoon sun peeked through the leaves. The treehouse glowed golden. Pip fluffed new feathers. Dot found the lost hat under the chair and laughed, “Squeak!”

Sunny placed a small sign above the door. It said, “Sharing Treehouse.” Sunny looked at Pip and Dot. “This is a place for kindness. We listen. We help. We share.”

Pip chirped, “I will bring berries next time.”

Dot squeaked, “I will bring dry leaves for soft beds.”

Sunny nodded. “And I will make more warm tea.”

They sat together, warm and peaceful, crunching, sipping, and smiling.

And the clear lesson in Sunny’s heart sparkled bright: When we share with generosity, lead with calm, and show compassion, everyone feels safe, loved, and strong.

The End.
