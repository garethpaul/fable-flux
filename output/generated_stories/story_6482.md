# Leo's Windy Day Challenge

Leo the lion cub loved playing on the rooftop of his apartment building. The wind ruffled his fluffy golden mane. He could see the whole neighborhood below. Cars looked like tiny toys. People walked like busy ants.

Today, Leo wanted to help his community. He had made colorful paper flowers to brighten everyone's day. Red ones. Purple ones. Sunny yellow ones too.

"I'll put these beautiful flowers around the rooftop garden," Leo said proudly. "Everyone will smile when they see them!"

But the wind had other plans. *WHOOSH!* A strong gust blew through Leo's mane. His carefully made flowers scattered everywhere.

"Oh no!" Leo cried. His little paws reached for the flying flowers. But they danced away in the breeze like butterflies.

Leo's shoulders slumped. His tail drooped. "This is so frustrating!" he growled softly. "I worked so hard on those flowers!"

Leo felt his chest getting tight. His paws clenched into fists. The feeling was big and uncomfortable.

"Take a deep breath," Leo whispered to himself. He had learned this from Mama Lion. "In... and out... in... and out."

The tight feeling started to go away. Leo's golden eyes looked around the rooftop. Some flowers were stuck under flower pots. Others clung to the fence.

"Maybe I can still help," Leo thought. "I just need to think differently."

Leo's ears perked up. He noticed the wind patterns. The corner by the big planter was calm and peaceful. The wind couldn't reach there.

"That's it!" Leo exclaimed. "I'll put my flowers where the wind can't blow them away!"

Leo gathered his scattered flowers one by one. He walked carefully to the sheltered corner. The colorful petals stayed perfectly still there.

But Leo wanted to do more. He looked at the empty planters around the rooftop. An idea sparked in his clever mind.

"I can plant real flowers!" Leo said. "They'll have roots to hold them down!"

Leo found packets of seeds in the garden shed. Sunflower seeds. Daisy seeds. Marigold seeds too. He dug small holes with his paws. The cool soil felt good between his toes.

"One seed here," Leo counted. "Two seeds there. Three seeds everywhere!"

As Leo worked, neighbors came up to the rooftop. Mrs. Robin smiled at the neat rows of planted seeds. Mr. Bear gave Leo a thumbs up.

"What a wonderful idea, Leo!" said Mrs. Robin. "These flowers will make our rooftop beautiful for everyone."

"And they won't blow away like paper ones," added Mr. Bear. "You solved the problem perfectly!"

Leo's chest puffed up with pride. His frustration had melted away like ice cream in sunshine. He had turned a disappointing moment into something even better.

"When things don't go as planned," Leo said to himself, "I can take deep breaths and find a new way."

The sun began to set, painting the sky orange and pink. Leo looked at his newly planted garden. Soon, real flowers would bloom here. They would bring joy to everyone in the building.

Leo learned that frustration is just a feeling that passes. With patience and creativity, problems become opportunities. And helping his community felt better than anything else in the world.

The End.
