# Melody Mouse Learns to Speak Up

In a bright, colorful art room filled with paintbrushes, crayons, and sparkling glitter, lived a kind little mouse named Melody. She had soft gray fur and tiny pink paws that loved to create beautiful things. The art room smelled like fresh paper and sweet-scented markers, and sunshine streamed through the big windows.

Every day, Melody watched the children come to art class. She loved seeing them paint rainbows, sculpt with clay, and dance to music while they created. But Melody noticed something that made her little heart sad.

"Oh no," whispered Melody, peeking from behind a jar of paintbrushes. The children were throwing paper scraps on the floor, leaving paint caps open to dry out, and not cleaning their brushes. The beautiful art room was becoming messy, and art supplies were being wasted.

Melody wanted to help, but she felt too small and shy. "What if they don't listen to me?" she squeaked quietly. "I'm just a little mouse."

The next morning, Melody saw something even worse. Tommy dropped his paintbrush, and it rolled under the table with wet paint still on it. Emma left her glue stick open, and it was getting all dried up. The room felt sad and cluttered.

"I have to do something," Melody said, taking a deep breath. She remembered what her mama always told her: "Even the smallest voice can make the biggest difference when it speaks with kindness."

Melody scurried to the center of the room and cleared her tiny throat. "Excuse me, everyone!" she called out in her sweetest voice.

The children stopped and looked around. "Did you hear that?" asked Emma.

"Down here!" squeaked Melody, waving her little paws. "I'm Melody Mouse, and I live in this wonderful art room!"

The children gasped with delight. "A talking mouse!" they exclaimed.

"I love watching you create beautiful art," Melody said, doing a little twirl. "But I've noticed our special room needs some help. When we take care of our art supplies, they can help us make even more amazing things!"

Tommy looked at his paintbrush under the table. "Oh! I didn't mean to drop that."

"That's okay!" said Melody cheerfully. "Let me show you a fun way to remember!" She began to dance and sing:

"Clean your brush, swish, swish, swish!
Cap your markers, click, click, click!
Put things back where they belong,
Sing the cleanup helper song!"

The children giggled and started dancing along with Melody. They swished their brushes in the water, making little splashes. They clicked their marker caps on tight. They picked up paper scraps and put them in the recycling bin.

"This is fun!" laughed Emma, spinning around as she organized the crayons by color.

"When we take care of our art room," explained Melody, "we're taking care of our Earth too! We don't waste supplies, and we keep everything clean and beautiful."

From that day on, whenever the children came to art class, they would dance Melody's cleanup song. The room sparkled with cleanliness, and the art supplies lasted much longer. The children learned that speaking up for what's right, even when you feel small, can make a big difference.

"Thank you, Melody!" the children would say. "You taught us that taking care of our things is taking care of our world!"

Melody beamed with pride. She had learned that being assertive didn't mean being loud or mean – it meant speaking up with kindness to help others. And the best part? Everyone was happier when they worked together to keep their special art room beautiful.

The End.
