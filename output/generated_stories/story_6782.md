# Sunny Songs and Honest Wings

In a sunny backyard, a small singing bird named Bibi woke up on a warm morning. The grass smelled sweet and fresh. The sun felt soft on Bibi’s feathers. Bees buzzed like tiny drums. A little breeze brushed Bibi’s wings. “Good morning!” Bibi sang. Her song was bright like the sun. “Tweet-a-lee, tweet-a-loo!”

Bibi loved to sing from the top of the tall tomato stakes. She liked to feel the smooth wooden post under her tiny feet. She could see the whole yard—flowers, a blue birdbath, and a row of small, new plants. The plants had shiny leaves and little labels stuck in the dirt.

“Those are baby trees,” said a gentle voice. It was Mr. Mole, popping up from a small, round hole. He wore little dirt glasses made of mud. “The family planted them to help the earth. Trees give shade. Trees give air. We all must take care.”

Bibi hopped closer. “Baby trees,” she whispered. The leaves smelled crisp and green. She felt proud. “I can help too,” she sang. “Tweet! I can watch them every day.”

Later that morning, Bibi found a shiny blue ribbon under the tomato stake. It glowed like a piece of sky. “Ooh,” Bibi said. She loved shiny things. She wrapped the ribbon around her neck. It tickled. She felt fancy.

Just then, a brown squirrel named Tavi scampered along the fence. His tail flicked like a broom. “Hey!” Tavi chattered. “That ribbon looks like the ones the family uses to tie the baby trees. The wind is strong today. The ribbon helps them stand.”

Bibi’s heart fluttered. She looked at the baby trees. One small tree wobbled in the breeze. Its little trunk bent like a straw. Bibi gulped. She pecked the ribbon knot around her neck. “I… I found it,” she said softly. “It was on the ground. I wanted to look pretty.”

Tavi’s eyes were wide. “The ribbon is important,” he said. “It helps the tree grow straight.”

Bibi felt hot and squirmy. She looked down at her feet. “I’m sorry,” she whispered. “I should have asked. I should have put it back. I wanted to look special.” Her cheeks felt as warm as the sun.

Mr. Mole peeked from his hole again. He sniffed the air. “Hmm,” he hummed. “Honesty is good, little bird. Telling the truth is brave. Now, what can we do to help?”

Bibi stood tall on the tomato stake. The ribbon slid down to her toes. She took a deep breath. The air smelled like tomatoes and mint. “I will tell the family the truth,” she said. “I will return the ribbon. I will help the little tree.”

Bibi fluttered to the back porch railing. A small child came out with a watering can. Water splashed like tiny rain. The child wore a green hat with a ladybug button. “Hello, bird,” the child said, smiling. “Tweet?” Bibi chirped back. She dropped the ribbon into the child’s open hand.

“I found this,” said Bibi in her sweet bird way, tilting her head. She pressed her chest forward, as if to say, “I’m sorry.”

The child laughed kindly. “Oh! Our tree ribbon!” The child walked to the baby tree. “The wind is wiggly today.” The ribbon went back around the little trunk, nice and snug. The tree stood straighter and didn’t wobble as much.

Bibi felt lighter. Her chest didn’t squirm now. She hopped closer and sang, “Tweet-a-lee, I told the truth. Tweet-a-loo, I can help, too!”

Tavi clapped his tiny paws. Mr. Mole nodded. “Good choice,” he said. “Honesty helps hearts. It keeps gardens safe.”

The wind puffed again, playful and cool. The leaves rustled like quiet claps. The child looked at Bibi. “You like pretty things,” the child said. “We do, too. Let’s make a plan.” The child tied a small, extra ribbon to the tomato stake, far from the baby trees. “This ribbon is for decoration,” the child said. “It can be your singing ribbon.”

Bibi’s eyes shone. “A singing ribbon!” she chirped. She hopped up, felt the smooth post, and brushed her feathers against the breeze. The ribbon fluttered and made a soft hush-hush sound that matched her song.

“That is called a compromise,” Mr. Mole explained. “You can enjoy pretty things. And the trees can stay safe and strong.”

Bibi nodded. “I will not take what the garden needs,” she sang. “I will ask. I will tell the truth. I will care for our home.”

All day, Bibi sang cheerful songs. She watched the bees visit the flowers. She watched the child water the plants. She watched the baby trees stand a little taller with their ribbons snug.

When the sun began to yawn and the sky turned peach and gold, Bibi sang one last song. “Honesty feels good,” she trilled. “Caring for trees helps our world. Sharing and planning keep friends happy.”

Tavi tucked a leaf blanket into his nest. Mr. Mole slid back into his soft, cool tunnel. The child waved and went inside. The backyard grew quiet and cozy. The new trees whispered in the gentle wind.

Bibi closed her eyes. The singing ribbon fluttered like a sleepy lullaby. She felt proud and peaceful. She had told the truth. She had helped the trees. She had found a kind plan with her friends.

And the sunny backyard felt like a happy, honest home.

The End.
