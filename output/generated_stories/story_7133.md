# Miko Mouse and the Tidy Closet Art Club

Miko was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright whiskers. He lived in a cozy closet hideaway behind a blue door. The closet smelled like clean soap and old shoes. It felt warm and safe. Inside, there were boxes, buttons, ribbons, and crayons. Miko loved to make art in his special space.

One sunny morning, Miko squeaked, “I want to draw a rainbow!” He opened a box. Thump. A pile of paper slipped out. He opened a drawer. Clatter. Buttons rolled everywhere. He reached for crayons. Rustle. Ribbons fell on his head.

“Oh dear,” Miko said softly. He felt his tummy twist. His tail drooped. “I can’t find my red crayon.”

Just then, his friends came in. Pip the sparrow fluttered to a shelf. “Tweet! Let’s make a collage!” Lila the ladybug landed on a spool of thread. “I brought shiny stickers!”

Miko smiled. “Yes! Art time!” But when he looked around, his smile faded. There were socks on the paint. There were paints on the socks. The glue was stuck to a cup. Cup, glue, and sock made a wobbly stack.

Pip pecked gently at a crumpled paper. “It’s a little messy,” he chirped.

Lila tilted her head. “I feel squished,” she said. “I can’t spread my wings.”

Miko took a deep breath in. He smelled dust and lemons. He let the breath out slowly. “I feel frustrated,” he said. “I want to make art, but I can’t find things. My body feels tight.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “I feel worried. I don’t want to step on glue.”

Lila nodded. “I feel grumpy. I want space.”

Miko listened to his feelings and his friends’ feelings. He put a paw on his chest. “Feelings are okay,” he whispered. “We can fix this.”

He had an idea. “Let’s organize,” Miko said. “We can make the closet tidy. Then we can make art!”

Pip blinked. “Organize? How?”

Miko pointed to the boxes. “We give each box a job,” he said. “Paper here. Crayons here. Buttons here.” He held up a sock and giggled. “Socks go in the sock box.”

Lila clapped her tiny hands. “We can make labels!” she said. “Labels help us know what goes where.”

Miko smiled bigger. “Yes! Labels!” He found a stack of sticky notes. He found a small pencil. He wrote simple words and drew pictures.

He stuck a note on a box. “Paper,” he said, and drew a square sheet. He stuck a note on a tin. “Crayons,” he said, and drew a little crayon. He stuck a note on a jar. “Buttons,” he said, and drew a circle.

Pip helped too. He sang, “Clip, clip, clip!” as he clipped ribbon into a neat roll. “Ribbons,” he chirped, and Miko added a label with a ribbon drawing.

Lila sorted stickers by color. “Red here. Blue here. Rainbow here,” she said. Her wings felt less squished. “Stickers,” she whispered, placing a label with a star.

They made a special space on a low shelf. Miko put a soft mat there. “Art spot,” he said. “This is where we make.”

Pip fluttered to the top shelf and peered into a basket. “What about the socks?” he asked.

Miko thought. He felt calm now. “Compromise,” he said. “Half the basket for socks. Half for rags to clean paint.” He divided the basket and added two labels. “Socks” and “Rags.”

Lila buzzed happily. “We did it together!”

They all took another deep breath. The closet looked different. The floor looked clear. The boxes looked friendly with their little pictures. It was quiet except for the soft tick of the house clock and a tiny happy hum from Pip.

Miko’s tail lifted. “Now, art!”

He reached to the “Crayons” tin. The red crayon smiled up at him. He held it. It felt smooth and warm in his paw. “Found you,” he said.

They sat at the art spot. Miko drew a big rainbow: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple. Pip glued feathers in a sun shape. Lila made a sticker garden. They laughed and shared. “Blue, please,” said Lila. “Here you go,” said Miko. “Thank you,” said Lila.

When they were done, Miko said, “Clean-up time.” He sang a tidy song. “Paper with paper, crayons with crayons, buttons with buttons, ribbons in rolls!”

Pip and Lila sang too. They put each thing in its home. It was quick and easy. The closet stayed cozy and neat.

Miko felt proud. His heart felt soft and warm. “I learned something,” he said. “When we organize, we can find what we need. We feel calm. We have more time for fun.”

Pip bobbed. “I learned to say my feelings.”

Lila smiled. “I learned to share space and make a compromise.”

Miko looked at his friends. “We listened. We worked together. We made art. We cleaned up. Now our closet hideaway is happy.”

He taped their rainbow, sun, and garden on the inside of the blue door. The colors glowed when the light came in. “Our art belongs here,” he said, “and our supplies belong in their places.”

All three friends snuggled on the soft mat. The closet smelled like paper and a little like lemons. It felt safe, tidy, and bright.

“Tomorrow,” Miko whispered, “we can make a castle.”

“Organized first,” Pip chirped.

“Then art,” Lila giggled.

Miko nodded. “That’s our plan.”

The End.
