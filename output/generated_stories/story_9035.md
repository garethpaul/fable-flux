# Mango the Brave Builder

Mango the monkey was small, soft, and very brave. He had bright eyes and a curly tail. One sunny morning, Mango woke up in the guest room. It was a cozy room with a big window, a soft blue rug, and a wooden shelf. The room smelled like clean sheets and warm toast. On the floor, there were blocks, crayons, and a little globe that spun with a gentle click.

Mango rubbed his eyes and smiled. “Today, I will build,” he said. “I will build something big!”

He shook the blocks out of a basket. Clack, clack, clack! Red blocks, yellow blocks, blue blocks, and green blocks tumbled onto the rug. Mango set one block down. He set another on top. Click! He hummed a happy tune. “Build, build, build,” he sang.

There was a soft knock at the guest room door. Tap tap.

Mango looked up. “Come in!”

The door opened. Two friends stepped in. Lila the ladybug fluttered her tiny wings. Her red shell was shiny with black dots. “Hello, Mango,” she buzzed. “It smells so nice in here!”

“Hi!” squeaked Niko the mouse. He wore a tiny scarf and carried a pencil. “What are you building?”

Mango puffed his chest. “I am building a tall tower! It will be the tallest tower in the guest room. Maybe in the whole world!”

Lila’s eyes sparkled. “Can we help?”

Niko wiggled his whiskers. “I can draw a plan.”

Mango looked at the blocks. He liked to build on his own. He felt a little tight in his tummy. “Um,” he said. “Maybe I can build it myself.”

Lila landed on the shelf and touched the little globe. It spun with a soft whoosh. “The world is big,” she said. “There are many homes. Many friends.”

Niko nodded. “We can build together, like friends all over the world. We can share.”

Mango frowned. “But if I share, will my tower still be mine?”

Lila fluttered down and whispered, “If we share, the tower can be ours. We can make it stronger.”

Mango listened. He heard the gentle hum of the house, the chirp of birds outside, and his own heartbeat thump-thump. He took a deep breath. The room smelled like crayons and sunshine. He felt his tummy loosen.

“Okay,” Mango said softly. “We will share. We will build together.”

“Yay!” Lila clapped her tiny feet. Niko squeaked happily.

They made a plan. Niko spread paper on the rug and drew a rectangle. “This is the base,” he said. “Strong base first.”

Lila lifted small blocks, light as a feather. “I will find the colors,” she said. “Red for love, blue for sky, green for trees, yellow for sun.”

Mango placed the first big block at the bottom. “I will stack,” he said. “I am brave and careful.”

They worked together. Click. Tap. Click. Mango stacked the heavy blocks. Niko slid thin blocks to fill the gaps. Lila found bright colors and cheered, “Red, blue, green, yellow!”

The tower grew taller. Mango felt proud. He also felt warm and happy. He liked the way they moved together. It sounded like a gentle drum: tap, tap, click, click.

Then, the tower wobbled. “Oh!” Mango gasped.

Niko steadied it with his tiny paws. “We need more support here,” he said.

Lila brought two long blocks. “Use these on the sides.”

Mango nodded. “Good idea.” He pressed the blocks in place. The tower stood stronger.

As they worked, Lila spun the globe again. “Look,” she said. “People around the world build homes and schools. They share tools. They share ideas.”

Niko smiled. “We are like them. We are global friends.”

Mango thought about children far away. Maybe they were building with blocks too. Maybe they shared like this. Mango felt brave and kind. “We share. We build. We help,” he said.

Soon, the tower was very tall. It reached up, up, up toward the window. Sunlight made the colors glow. The room smelled like warm wood and sweet apples from the hallway. Mango’s heart felt big.

“Let’s add flags,” Lila said. She drew tiny flags with crayons: red, blue, green, yellow, and more. Niko taped them to the top. The flags fluttered in the sun. They looked like smiles.

Just then, a small bird peeped at the window. “Peep?”

Mango waved. “Hello, little friend! We built this together. Would you like to see?”

The bird peeped again, happy, and flew away.

Lila landed on Mango’s shoulder. “You are a courageous monkey,” she said. “You shared your blocks.”

Niko nodded. “You shared the building. And look how tall and strong it is!”

Mango’s cheeks felt warm. He grinned. “Sharing made it better. Sharing made it fun.”

They sat on the soft rug and ate apple slices. Crunch, crunch. The tower stood bright and tall. Mango patted the blocks gently. “Thank you, friends,” he said. “Tomorrow, we can build a bridge. We can build for everyone.”

Lila hummed. “We can make a little school for toy bears.”

Niko squeaked. “We can build a hospital for dolls.”

Mango smiled. “We can build a place for all. We will share our blocks and our ideas.”

He looked at the little globe and gave it a tiny spin. Whoosh. Mango felt close to friends everywhere. He felt brave, kind, and ready to help.

“Today I learned,” Mango said, “that sharing makes us strong. Sharing makes us a team. When we share, we can build big things for everyone, all around the world.”

They bumped fists—paw, wing, paw—and giggled. The guest room was full of light, color, and happy hearts.

The End.
