# Leo Learns to Roar Softly

On a sunny afternoon, a brave little lion cub named Leo sat on the peaceful front porch of his den. The porch was warm and smooth under his paws. Birds sang sweet songs. A soft breeze carried the smell of flowers. Leo’s tail flicked back and forth as he stacked smooth pebbles into a tall, wobbly tower.

“I am making a super-tall tower,” Leo said proudly. “It will touch the sky!”

Mama Lion sat nearby in a rocking chair, sipping cool lemonade. “That sounds wonderful, brave Leo,” she said with a smile. “Remember to breathe and take your time.”

Leo’s friend, Tessa the tortoise, slowly climbed onto the porch. “Hi, Leo,” she said. “Can I play too?”

“Yes!” Leo said, eyes bright. “But please don’t bump the porch. My tower is very wobbly.”

Tessa nodded. “I will be careful.”

Just then, a friendly monkey named Miko jingled onto the porch, his tail swishing. “Ooo-oo! Can I help?” he chirped, bouncing with excitement.

Leo looked at his tower. It shimmered in the sun. “Okay,” he said, “but gentle hands, please. This is my tower, and I like it calm.”

Miko clapped. “Gentle hands. Got it!”

They worked together. Leo placed a pebble. Tessa passed him another. Miko hummed a silly tune. The tower grew taller and taller. The porch felt peaceful and safe.

But then Miko giggled and did a little hop. His foot tapped the porch. The tower wobbled. “Oh no!” Leo cried. The pebbles rattled like tiny bells. The whole tower tumbled down with a clatter.

Leo’s ears grew hot. His chest felt tight. His face scrunched up like a wrinkly lemon. He wanted to roar. He wanted to stomp. He wanted to say, “Go away!”

He took a big breath in, but it felt stuck.

Mama Lion saw Leo’s whiskers shake. She spoke softly. “Leo, I see big feelings. Let’s try your calm tools.”

Leo remembered. He had calm tools! He put his paw on his belly. He breathed in like smelling a flower. He breathed out like blowing a feather. In… and out. In… and out. His belly rose and fell like a little boat on the sea.

Miko looked worried. “I’m sorry, Leo,” he whispered. “I forgot to be gentle.”

Tessa nodded slowly. “It’s okay to be mad, Leo. Your tower was special.”

Leo felt tears prick his eyes. “I feel angry,” he said. “I worked hard. I asked for gentle hands.”

Mama nodded. “Thank you for using your words. That is brave.”

Leo kept breathing. The hot feeling cooled, like water on warm sand. His paws relaxed. He blinked away the tears. “I need a little space,” he said softly. “Please give me porch space. No bouncing.”

Miko stepped back. “I hear you,” he said. “I will give you space. I will sit on the step.”

Tessa scooted to one side. “I will move slowly. I will use quiet feet.”

Leo sat still for a moment. He listened to the birds. He felt the gentle breeze. He smelled Mama’s lemon drink. He counted five clouds. One… two… three… four… five. His heart felt steady again.

Then Leo spoke. “I am ready to try again. But we need a porch plan.”

Miko’s ears perked up. “A plan!”

Leo held up a paw. “My boundary is no jumping on the porch near the tower,” he said. “Please ask before you touch the pebbles. And if I say stop, we stop.”

Miko nodded hard. “I agree. No jumps. I will ask.”

Tessa smiled. “I will pass pebbles very slowly. If you say stop, I stop.”

Mama Lion beamed. “That is good understanding. Thank you for listening.”

They started again. Tessa slid a smooth pebble into Leo’s paw. “Here you go,” she whispered.

“Thank you,” Leo said. He set it down gently. Tap. Tap. Tap. The pebbles felt cool and silky. The tower grew. Miko sat crisscross on the step. “May I hand you this shiny pebble?” he asked.

“Yes, please,” said Leo. “Hand it gently.”

Miko stretched out his arm like a careful branch. Leo placed the shiny pebble on top. The tower stood tall, taller than before. It shimmered in the sun.

“We did it!” Leo cheered. His tail swished in happy loops.

Mama clapped softly. “Leo, I am proud of you. You felt angry. You breathed. You used words. You set a boundary. You listened to your friends. And you made a plan.”

Leo stood tall, his mane fluffing. “I learned that it’s okay to feel mad,” he said. “I can take calm breaths. I can say what I need. My friends can listen and understand.”

Miko grinned. “And I learned to ask and to give space.”

Tessa nodded. “I learned to move slowly and to stop when a friend says stop.”

They sat together on the peaceful front porch, sharing a bowl of sweet mango slices. The birds sang. The breeze was soft. The tower sparkled. Leo smiled at his friends.

“We are a good team,” he said.

“Yes,” Mama Lion agreed. “With calm hearts and kind words, we can solve big problems.”

The End.
