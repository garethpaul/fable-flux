# Koala Kai’s Quiet Clicks

Koala Kai lived in a cozy home office with soft light, a leafy fern, and a big wooden desk. The desk smelled like warm pine. The computer made a gentle hum, like a purring kitten. Tap-tap went the keyboard keys when Kai worked on little projects.

Kai was a calm koala. He loved to breathe slow and count to three. He loved to try new things. One sunny morning, Kai wiggled his fuzzy ears and said, “I’m curious today!”

On the desk were many colorful buttons on the keyboard. Red, blue, and green sticky notes fluttered like tiny flags. A warm cup of tea steamed, smelling like sweet lemon.

Kai said, “I want to make a music postcard to send to friends.” He wanted to add sounds from places around the world. He wanted to learn and share.

He clicked open a music app. Bright pictures showed drums, flutes, and bells. “Ooh,” said Kai. “So many sounds!” He felt excitement fizz in his belly. His paws felt wiggly.

He tapped a tiny drum icon. BOOM. He tapped again. BOOM-BOOM. He giggled. “Fun!” He clicked a bell. Ding-ding, ding! He clicked a flute. Whoooo.

Soon there were many sounds all at once. BOOM, ding, whooo, clap-clap, shake-shake. The office was full of noise. The computer hummed louder. Kai’s ears twitched.

His heart started to beat fast. He wanted to click more. He wanted to click all the buttons. His paws moved quick, quick. “Click, click, click!”

Then the sounds got too big. The music app froze. The mouse stopped. The screen was stuck. Uh-oh.

Kai’s tummy felt tight. He wanted to shout, “No!” He wanted to stomp. His eyes grew wet and shiny. “I can’t make my postcard now,” he whispered.

He remembered his calm plan. Kai had a self-regulation plan for tricky times. He said it out loud: “Pause. Breathe. Choose.”

Kai put his paws in his lap. He closed his eyes. He breathed in slow through his nose. One… two… three. He breathed out slow through his mouth. One… two… three.

The warm tea smell of lemon hugged his nose. He wiggled his toes on the soft rug. He felt the chair under him, sturdy and safe. The tight feeling in his tummy loosened.

Kai opened his eyes. “I can try again,” he said. “I can solve this step by step.”

He looked at the screen. It was still stuck. He took one more slow breath. Then he chose a helpful action. He clicked the small X to close the app. It did not work.

He tried a different step. He pressed one key gently. Nothing. He counted: “One… two… three.” He whispered, “Stay calm, Kai.”

He remembered the power button. He said, “I can restart.” He pressed and held, just like the helpful poster on the wall showed. The computer went quiet. Then it blinked back on. The gentle hum returned.

Kai smiled. “I did it,” he said softly. “I used my plan.”

He opened the music app again. This time, he wanted to be careful and kind to himself. He made a new plan with his curious brain.

He said, “I will listen to one sound at a time. I will learn where it comes from. I will add slowly.”

First, he clicked a soft drum called a djembe. He read the tiny note: “This drum is used in West Africa.” Kai nodded. “I will say thank you,” he whispered. BOOM-boom. He tapped gently. The sound felt like warm footsteps.

Next, he clicked a bell called a ghungroo. Tiny bells danced. The note said, “These are used in Indian dance.” Kai swayed with the light jingles. “Thank you,” he said.

Then he chose a wooden flute called a quena. The note said, “This flute is from the Andes.” The sound was like a cool breeze. Whoooo. “Thank you,” Kai whispered again.

He listened with care. He added one sound, then paused. He breathed. He asked, “Does this feel calm and kind?” If it did, he kept it. If it felt too big, he took it out.

He watched the volume bar and kept it low. He made a gentle pattern: BOOM-boom… ding… whooo… pause. There was space between sounds. The music felt like a friendly walk.

Kai smiled bigger. “This is better,” he said. He tapped record and made his music postcard. At the end, he added his voice. “Hello, friends! I made calm music. I learned about instruments from different places. Thank you to the people who play them. I hope you like it!”

He clicked send. His paws felt steady and proud.

Just then, a message popped up from a friend. “Hi Kai! Your music is lovely. It sounds kind and calm. How did you do it?”

Kai typed, “I had a problem. I got too excited and clicked too much. I paused, breathed, and chose a slow plan. I learned, I listened, and I improved.”

He leaned back. The fern leaves brushed his shoulder. The lemon tea was warm and sweet. The computer hummed like a soft song.

Kai whispered, “When I feel big feelings, I can pause, breathe, and choose. Curiosity can be careful and kind. I can help myself.”

He felt calm, clever, and brave.

The End.
