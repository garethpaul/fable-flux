# Squeak Says, “Let’s Talk!”

Squeak was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright whiskers. He lived in a cozy hole by a big dining room. The dining room smelled like warm bread and sweet apples. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. The table stood tall like a mountain, and the chairs were like trees.

Every morning, Squeak peeked out. He stretched his tiny legs and wiggled his toes. “Time for my morning run,” he said. He loved to exercise. He ran in a circle, hopped over a crumb, and did three little twirls. “Hop, hop, twirl!”

One day, Squeak heard a clink and a clatter. He peeked out and saw friends in the dining room. There was Nibbles the hamster with round cheeks, Bop the rabbit with long ears, and Pip the little bird with shiny blue feathers. They were all different, and Squeak liked that.

“Hello!” Squeak squeaked. “Welcome to the dining room!”

“Hi, Squeak!” chirped Pip. “Can we play a game?”

“Yes!” said Bop. “Let’s play the Crumb Race!”

Nibbles nodded. “I love to run.”

Squeak smiled. “Great! We can run to the shiny spoon and back. But first, let’s talk about the rules.”

Bop wiggled his feet. “Do we have to talk? I want to go fast!”

Squeak took a deep breath. He smelled cinnamon and clean soap. “Talking helps us understand,” he said kindly. “Talking keeps us safe. We need to listen too.”

Pip tilted her head. “Okay. What are the rules?”

Squeak pointed. “Rule one: We all start at the chair leg. Rule two: We go around the shiny spoon. Rule three: We say ‘Coming through!’ if we get close. Rule four: We stop if someone says ‘Stop!’”

The friends nodded. “We can do that,” they said together.

“Ready?” asked Squeak.

“Ready!” they sang.

“Go!” cried Squeak.

The friends ran. Tap, tap went little feet. Thump, thump went big feet. Pip fluttered and hopped. Squeak’s heart beat happy. He felt the cool floor under his paws. He smelled the apples on the table. He heard the soft whoosh of Pip’s wings.

But halfway to the spoon, Bop’s big feet bumped Nibbles. Nibbles rolled and rolled like a fuzzy ball. “Whoops!” cried Bop. “I didn’t mean to!”

Nibbles stopped and looked upset. “I fell,” he said softly.

Squeak skidded to a stop. “Stop!” he called. Everyone stopped. The room went still. Even the clock seemed to listen.

Squeak walked to Nibbles. “Are you okay?”

Nibbles took a breath. “I am okay. But I got scared.”

Bop’s ears drooped. “I am sorry,” he said. “I did not say ‘Coming through.’ I just ran and ran.”

Squeak nodded. “Thank you for being honest,” he said. “Honesty helps us trust each other.”

Pip hopped closer. “Can we talk about what we need?”

Squeak smiled. “Yes. Let’s use our words.”

Nibbles said, “I need space when we run.”

Bop said, “I have big feet. I need to slow down near friends.”

Pip said, “I am small. I need you to say ‘Coming through!’ so I can hop aside.”

Squeak said, “I need everyone to listen.”

They all looked at one another. Their faces felt softer. Their shoulders relaxed.

Squeak clapped his tiny paws. “Let’s try again. We will talk as we move. We will listen too.”

They lined up at the chair leg. Squeak called, “Ready?”

“Ready!” they said.

“Go!”

They ran again. This time, the room was full of kind words.

“Coming through on your left!” called Bop.

“Thanks!” said Nibbles, rolling to the right.

“I’m stopping,” chirped Pip, “tie my feather!” Everyone stopped and waited. Pip shook her feather and laughed. “Just a tickle!”

They moved like a little parade. Squeak felt proud. His feet went tap-tap. His lungs filled with fresh, cool air. Exercise made him warm and strong. His friends were different, and that made the race fun. Big feet, small wings, round cheeks, tiny paws—everyone moved in their own way.

They reached the shiny spoon and turned. The spoon made a bright, happy sparkle. On the way back, Squeak called, “Coming through, Pip!” Pip hopped aside and waved.

They crossed the chair leg together. “We did it!” they cheered.

Bop bounced. “That was better! Talking helped.”

Nibbles smiled. “I felt safe.”

Pip sang a tiny song. “Tweet, tweet, we used our words!”

Squeak’s whiskers twitched. “We solved our problem by talking and listening. We were honest about our needs. We moved our bodies and had fun. Our differences make us a good team.”

Bop nodded. “Next time, we can make a new game.”

Nibbles giggled. “Maybe a slow dance race!”

Pip flapped. “Or a feather float!”

Squeak laughed. “We can plan together. We will talk first, and everyone can share ideas.”

The sun made warm light on their faces. The dining room felt cozy and safe. They munched a tiny apple crumb, one nibble each, taking turns.

Squeak looked at his friends. “Today we learned: Use your words. Listen with your ears and your heart. Be honest. Celebrate what makes us different. Moving together is fun.”

“Let’s say it,” said Pip.

Together they said, “Talk, listen, be honest, and move with care!”

Squeak did three little twirls. “Hop, hop, twirl!”

Everyone laughed. It sounded like bells.

The End.
