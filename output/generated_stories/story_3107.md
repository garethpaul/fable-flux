# Whiskers and the Wobbly Birdhouse

Whiskers was a clever orange cat who loved to explore Papa's garage workshop. The garage smelled like wood shavings and paint, and sunlight streamed through the dusty windows, making everything sparkle.

One morning, Whiskers discovered something wonderful on Papa's workbench - a half-built birdhouse! It had pretty blue walls and a tiny round door, but something was wrong. The roof sat crooked, and one wall wobbled when Whiskers touched it with her soft paw.

"Oh no!" meowed Whiskers. "The little birds won't be safe in a wobbly house!"

Whiskers wanted to help, but she had never built anything before. The tools looked so big and scary. The hammer seemed heavy, and the screwdriver was almost as long as her tail!

"I'm just a cat," she whispered to herself. "I don't know how to build things."

But then Whiskers remembered something Mama Cat always said: "Being brave means trying, even when you feel scared."

Whiskers took a deep breath. The workshop air smelled like fresh wood and made her feel stronger. She looked at the wobbly birdhouse again. The birds needed a safe, cozy home before winter came.

"I can do this!" she announced bravely.

First, Whiskers used her sharp eyes to study the problem. She walked around the birdhouse, her whiskers twitching as she thought. The screws were loose! That's why everything wobbled.

Whiskers carefully picked up the small screwdriver with her mouth. It tasted like metal but felt solid and helpful. She positioned it on the first loose screw and slowly turned it. Click, click, click! The screw got tighter.

"This isn't so scary after all," purred Whiskers, feeling proud.

She moved to the next screw, then the next. Her paws were steady, and her green eyes focused carefully on each turn. The workshop was quiet except for the gentle clicking sounds and Whiskers' happy purring.

When she finished tightening the screws, Whiskers gently pushed the wall. It didn't wobble anymore! But the roof still sat crooked.

Whiskers studied the roof carefully. She noticed that one corner wasn't lined up properly. Using her clever paws, she gently nudged it into the right position. Then she tightened those screws too, her pink tongue sticking out in concentration.

"Perfect!" she meowed with joy.

Just then, Papa walked into the garage. His eyes grew wide with surprise and delight.

"Whiskers! Did you fix the birdhouse?" Papa asked, kneeling down to pet her soft fur.

"Meow!" said Whiskers proudly, rubbing against Papa's leg.

Papa examined the birdhouse carefully. "This is wonderful work! The screws are tight, and the roof is perfectly straight. You're very brave and clever, Whiskers."

Whiskers purred loudly, her chest puffed out with pride. She had felt scared at first, but she tried anyway. And look what happened - she had saved the day!

That afternoon, Papa and Whiskers hung the beautiful birdhouse in the oak tree. Soon, a family of cheerful robins moved in, chirping happy songs every morning.

Whenever Whiskers saw the birds flying safely in and out of their cozy home, she remembered that being brave doesn't mean you're not scared - it means you try to help even when something feels difficult.

From that day on, Whiskers became Papa's official workshop helper, always ready to lend a paw when something needed fixing.

The End.
