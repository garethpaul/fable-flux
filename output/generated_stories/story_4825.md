# Oliver's Peaceful Rooftop

High up on a tall red building, Oliver the owl sat on his favorite rooftop. The morning sun felt warm on his soft brown feathers. But Oliver didn't feel good today.

"Hoot, hoot," Oliver sighed sadly. His big yellow eyes looked tired. He had been flying around all night, helping other animals. A lost kitten needed help finding home. A baby bird fell from its nest. Oliver helped everyone, but forgot to help himself.

Now Oliver felt grumpy and tired. His tummy rumbled loudly. "GRUMBLE, GRUMBLE!" When did he last eat? He couldn't remember.

Just then, a little sparrow landed nearby. "Good morning, Oliver!" chirped Stella the sparrow. Her tiny feet made soft tapping sounds on the roof tiles.

"Morning," Oliver mumbled. He turned his head away.

Stella tilted her small head. "You look sad, Oliver. What's wrong?"

Oliver's feathers ruffled up. "I'm fine!" he hooted loudly. But his voice cracked a little.

Stella hopped closer. She could see Oliver's droopy eyes and messy feathers. "When did you last eat something yummy?" she asked gently.

Oliver thought hard. "Um... yesterday morning? Maybe?"

"Oh my!" Stella gasped. "And when did you sleep?"

Oliver's big eyes blinked slowly. "I helped animals all night. Sleep is for... later."

Stella shook her tiny head. "Oliver, you take such good care of everyone else. But who takes care of you?"

Oliver looked down at his talons. He felt silly. "I... I forgot about me."

"That's not good," Stella said kindly. "Even wise owls need food and rest. How can you help others if you don't feel good?"

Oliver's tummy made another loud rumble. "GRUMBLE!" This time, both friends giggled.

"I have an idea!" Stella chirped excitedly. "Let's make this rooftop your special self-care place!"

"Self-care?" Oliver asked.

"Yes! Taking care of yourself so you can be healthy and happy," Stella explained. "First, let's find you some breakfast."

Stella flew away and came back with a plump mouse. "Eat slowly and enjoy every bite," she said.

Oliver ate carefully. The food tasted so good! He felt energy coming back to his tired body.

"Now," said Stella, "let's make a cozy sleeping spot." Together, they gathered soft leaves and twigs. They made a perfect nest in the corner of the rooftop.

"This feels wonderful," Oliver hooted happily. The nest smelled like fresh leaves and felt like a warm hug.

"One more thing," Stella said. "Every morning, come here first. Eat breakfast. Rest if you're tired. Then you can help others."

Oliver nodded his wise head. "You're right, Stella. I can't pour water from an empty cup."

"Exactly!" Stella chirped. "Taking care of yourself isn't selfish. It's smart!"

Oliver stretched his wings wide. The morning breeze felt cool and fresh. His tummy was full. His new nest looked cozy and inviting.

"Thank you for helping me remember," Oliver said. "From now on, I'll take care of myself first. Then I can help others even better!"

Stella smiled. "That's what good friends do. We remind each other to be kind to ourselves."

Oliver settled into his new nest. He felt calm and peaceful. Tomorrow, he would help other animals again. But first, he would always take care of himself.

The End.
