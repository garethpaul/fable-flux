# Piper the Parrot’s Smart Choice

Piper the parrot loved colors, crayons, and cozy places. Her favorite spot was the study corner by the window. The study corner had a small table, a soft beanbag, and a basket of crayons that smelled like sweet wax. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. The bookshelf whispered with pages when the breeze came in.

“Squawk! Time to make a poster!” Piper chirped, fluffing her green and blue feathers. “I want a big, bright poster about helping nature.”

Piper set out her crayons and a stack of paper. The paper felt smooth like a leaf. She tapped her beak, thinking. “Should I use lots of paper? Or just one? Hmm.” She wanted to draw trees, birds, and a clean blue pond. She also wanted to cut shapes and glue them.

Her friend, Miko the mouse, peeked in with tiny whiskers twitching. “Hi, Piper! What’s that nice crayon smell?”

“It’s my poster,” Piper said. “I want it to say, ‘We care for our world!’ But I can’t decide how to start.”

Miko climbed onto the beanbag. It made a soft whoosh. “Let’s think. What is the problem?”

Piper blinked. “I want to use all the paper to make big flowers. But I also want to save paper to help trees. I want to use lots of glue and stickers. But I also want to use just what I need. My head says ‘save,’ my wings say ‘make!’”

Miko nodded. “Let’s make a plan. A plan helps us decide.”

Piper spread her wings like a cape. “A plan! Yes!”

Just then, Tali the turtle slid across the floor with a gentle scrape. “I heard ‘plan,’” Tali said in a slow, kind voice. “Can I help?”

“Yes!” Piper said. “We are deciding how to make a nature poster without wasting things.”

Tali smiled. “We can use the three R’s: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle.”

Piper tilted her head. “Three R’s? That sounds like a song!”

Miko clapped. “Let’s sing while we think!”

Together they sang softly:
“Reduce what we use,
Reuse what we can,
Recycle the rest,
That’s our helpful plan!”

Piper looked at the paper stack. “Reduce. That means use less. I can use one big paper for the poster, not five.”

She checked the basket. There were short, stubby crayons and long, new ones. “Reuse. I can use the stubby crayons first!”

Tali pointed to a little box in the corner. “Look, scraps!” It was full of old paper bits, shiny sticker backs, and clean ribbon from a gift.

Piper’s eyes sparkled. “We can reuse these for shapes!”

Her decision felt good, like a warm hug. “Yes,” Piper said. “Here is my choice: One big paper. Use stubby crayons. Reuse scraps for shapes. Only a little glue.”

“Great choice,” said Miko. “We can all work together!”

They started. Piper drew a tall tree with a brown trunk that smelled like crayon chocolate. She shaded green leaves with tiny circles. Miko cut leaf shapes from green scraps. Snip, snip! The scissors made a happy sound. Tali carefully dabbed glue. “Just a dot, not a lot,” Tali hummed.

Piper added a bright blue pond. “Swish, swish,” she said, moving the crayon in smooth lines. She pressed lightly to save the crayon. She drew fish with smiling faces. Miko placed a small ribbon sun in the corner. It shone with golden threads.

Soon, the basket of scraps was smaller, and the poster was bigger and brighter. But there was one more decision. Piper held a big glitter bottle. “Should I use this? It will sparkle. But glitter can be messy and hard to clean. And it can hurt ponds.”

Piper paused. She took a slow breath. She listened to her heart and her plan. “Reduce. Reuse. Recycle.”

She set the glitter down. “I will not use glitter. I will draw tiny stars instead.” She dotted yellow stars all around. Tap, tap, tap. The stars looked friendly and bright.

Miko smiled. “Smart choice, Piper.”

Tali nodded. “You thought before you did. That is good decision-making.”

Piper puffed with pride. “We did it together!”

They added the words: “We care for our world!” Piper said the letters out loud as she wrote them: “W-e c-a-r-e f-o-r o-u-r w-o-r-l-d!”

They cleaned the study corner. They put away the crayons, even the tiny ones. They saved the extra scraps in the box. They put the empty glue bottle into the recycle bin with a gentle clink.

Piper looked at the sunny poster. She felt warm and bright inside. “We reduced, we reused, we recycled,” she said. “We made a plan. We made a choice. We worked together. We cared for nature.”

Miko squeaked, “And we had fun!”

Tali added, “And we left the study corner clean.”

Piper flapped her wings and laughed. “That is the best part.”

They hung the poster on the wall by the window. The sun touched the paper and made the colors glow.

Piper turned to her friends. “Next time, let’s make a book from old paper. We can choose wisely again.”

“Yes!” said Miko.

“Slow and smart,” said Tali.

Piper faced the poster and spoke to the room. “Friends, when you have a choice, stop, think, and plan. Then choose what helps you and helps our world.”

She felt calm, like a soft beanbag hug. She felt proud, like a bright ribbon. She felt ready to make more good choices.

The End.
