# Ellie Listens on the Porch

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a little pink scarf. She loved to sit on the peaceful front porch of her home. The porch was warm in the sunshine. The wooden floor felt smooth under her big, careful feet. A breeze brought the smell of sweet flowers. Bees hummed. A bluebird sang, “Chee-chee-chee!”

Ellie wiggled her trunk and smiled. “Today, I will learn something new,” she said. Her voice was calm, but her heart felt excited. She liked learning by herself. She also liked listening to others. Ellie knew that listening could help her grow.

On the porch sat a small basket. Inside were three things: a little watering can, a paintbrush, and a tiny bell. Ellie’s mama had put them there. “You can choose what to do,” Mama had said. “Be brave, be kind, and be open to new ideas.”

Ellie looked at the watering can. “I can water the porch flowers,” she said. The flowers were bright and red. Their leaves were shiny. “That would be helpful.”

She looked at the paintbrush. “I can paint a picture for Papa,” she said. The paint pots were colorful—red like strawberries, yellow like sunshine, and blue like the sky.

She looked at the tiny bell. It made a soft ding. “I can use the bell to ask for help if I need it,” she said. “But I will try first.”

Just then, her friend Pip the sparrow landed on the porch rail. “Hi, Ellie!” chirped Pip. “What are you doing?”

“I am choosing,” Ellie said. Her ears flapped gently. “I want to learn something new.”

Pip hopped closer. “I think you should paint a big picture first!” Pip said. “Make a huge splash of blue.”

Ellie tilted her head. “That sounds fun,” she said. “But I will think. I will listen. I will choose.” She took a deep breath and closed her eyes. The breeze touched her cheeks. The porch smelled like wood and soap. She felt calm.

Then Tiko the turtle slowly climbed the steps. Tap, tap, tap. “Hello,” Tiko said in a low, warm voice. “I like slow and steady. Maybe start with watering. One sip for each plant.”

Ellie smiled at both friends. “Thank you for your ideas,” she said. “I like to hear them. I also want to try my way.”

She picked up the watering can. It was small in her big trunk, but not too heavy. “I will water first,” she said. “I can be careful and gentle.”

She tilted the can. “Sip, sip,” she said softly, like Tiko had suggested. The water made the soil dark and cool. The flowers seemed to stand taller. A drop splashed on her toe. It felt cold and tickly. Ellie giggled.

Pip fluttered. “Now paint! Blue swirls!” he chirped.

Ellie looked at the paintbrush and thought again. “I will try painting next,” she said. “But I will try a new idea too.” She dipped the brush in yellow. “I will paint what I hear.” She listened. Chee-chee-chee. Hum, hum. Ding from the tiny bell. She moved the brush slowly. Swish. Swirl. Dot. The yellow looked like sunshine songs.

Pip blinked. “I didn’t think of that!” he said.

Ellie nodded. “It is new for me too,” she said. “I like trying new ways.”

Tiko smiled. “Open ears make open hearts,” he said.

Ellie painted blue for the sky song and red for the flower song. She mixed a little to make purple, like blueberries. She was careful and strong. When the brush slipped, she took a deep breath and tried again. “I can do it,” she told herself. “I will keep going.” Her voice was small but firm.

Soon, Mama came out to the porch. She smelled like cinnamon tea. “Oh, Ellie,” Mama said softly. “What a beautiful picture. Tell me about it.”

Ellie’s eyes were bright. “I listened to ideas,” she said. “I listened to my heart too. I watered the flowers gently. I painted the songs I heard. Pip wanted big blue. Tiko wanted slow sips. I tried both in my own way.”

Mama nodded. “That is being open-minded,” she said. “You heard others. You tried new ideas. You also made your own choices. That is self-reliance. And when you said, ‘I will try my way,’ that was assertiveness.”

Ellie felt warm and proud. “I can listen and choose,” she said. She rang the tiny bell once—ding!—just for joy.

Pip chirped, “Your painting sings, Ellie!”

Tiko tapped the floor. “Your watering helped the flowers.”

Ellie leaned against Mama’s side. The porch boards were cozy. The sky was soft and blue. She smelled cinnamon and flowers and paint. She heard bees, birds, and a far-away laugh.

Mama asked, “What did you learn today?”

Ellie lifted her trunk. “I learned to keep my ears open and my heart open,” she said. “I learned to try new ideas and to trust myself. I can be gentle and strong.”

Mama kissed Ellie’s forehead. “Yes,” she said. “That is a wonderful lesson.”

Ellie smiled at her friends. “Thank you for your ideas,” she told them. “I liked hearing them. I liked trying my way too.”

Pip and Tiko nodded. The bluebird sang again. The porch felt peaceful and bright.

Ellie looked at her painting and the happy flowers. “Today was a good day,” she said. “When I listen and try, I grow.”

The End.
