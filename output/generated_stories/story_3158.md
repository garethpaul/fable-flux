# Ollie Owl Speaks Up in the Pantry

In a cozy kitchen pantry, jars sat like shiny soldiers. Flour felt soft as clouds. Cinnamon smelled warm and sweet. Crackers made a quiet crunch when the door opened. In the highest shadow sat Ollie, a wise old owl with soft gray feathers and bright, kind eyes.

“Good morning, pantry,” hooted Ollie softly. “Today, I will help and be brave.”

Ollie watched the food shelves. The fruit basket held apples, bananas, and one shy pear. The snack shelf had crackers, nuts, and a little box of raisins that rattled when moved.

Down below, Mouse, Squirrel, and Bunny scampered in. “Snack time!” squeaked Mouse.

“Me first!” chattered Squirrel, grabbing a paw-full of nuts.

“I want crackers,” said Bunny, bouncing. “Crunchy ones!”

Ollie spread his wings and glided down. “Hello, friends,” he said. “Let’s be fair. We can share.”

Squirrel hugged the nuts close. “But I found them.”

Mouse looked at the raisins and whispered, “I like raisins, but I’m too small to reach.”

Bunny’s ears drooped. “I always forget my water cup.”

Ollie blinked slowly. He felt a flutter in his chest. He wanted to help everyone. He also wanted something, too. Today, Ollie wanted the pear. It looked juicy and sweet. Ollie took a deep breath. “I can speak up,” he told himself. “I can use my voice.”

He turned to his friends. “Friends, I have something to say,” Ollie hooted kindly. “I want the pear, please.”

Mouse looked up. “You do?”

“Yes,” said Ollie. “I feel thirsty, and the pear will help me feel good. I’m asking with my words. May I have it?”

Squirrel paused. His tail twitched. “You asked in a nice way.”

Bunny nodded. “That is self-advocacy,” she said proudly. “It means you use your words to say what you need.”

Ollie felt warm and brave. “Thank you for listening.”

Squirrel thought about fairness. “If we all say what we need, we can make a plan.” He set the nuts back down. “I want nuts because they give me energy.”

Mouse piped up, “I want raisins, but I need help to reach.”

Bunny said, “I want crackers, and I also need water.”

Ollie fluffed his feathers. “We can be creative. Let’s make a snack chart so we all get what we need.”

They found a small piece of paper and a stubby blue crayon in the pantry drawer. The crayon smelled waxy. Ollie drew simple shapes.

“Pear for Ollie,” he said, drawing a green teardrop shape. “Nuts for Squirrel,” he added little brown ovals. “Raisins for Mouse,” he drew tiny dots. “Crackers for Bunny,” he drew squares.

Bunny clapped softly. “What about water?”

“Good thinking,” said Ollie. “Wellness matters.” He drew a cup with a little wave. “We will all have water.”

Mouse tilted his head. “But how do I reach the raisins?”

Ollie looked around. He saw a sturdy wooden spoon. He saw a small step stool and a clean jar lid.

“Let’s prepare,” said Ollie. “Preparation keeps us safe.” He set the step stool by the shelf. He put the jar lid on top to make a tiny tray. He gave the spoon to Mouse.

Mouse carefully tapped the raisin box with the spoon. Tap, tap, tap. The box slid onto the tiny tray. Down, down, down it came. “I did it!” Mouse cheered. “Thank you for helping me help myself.”

Squirrel picked up a small handful of nuts and counted. “One, two, three.” He left some for later. “Fair to share,” he said. “Fair is not ‘me first.’ Fair is ‘we all get some.’”

Bunny took two crackers and poured water from the little pitcher into a cup. “Sip, sip.” She smiled. “Now I feel good.”

Ollie reached for the pear. He took a bite. Juicy drops ran down his beak. “Mmm,” he hummed. “Sweet and cool.”

They all sat together on a soft kitchen towel that felt like a cozy blanket. Crunch, sip, chew, munch. The pantry felt happy. The cinnamon smell curled around them like a hug.

After snacks, Ollie said, “Let’s make a plan for next time.” He wrote with the crayon again: “Ask with kind words. Say what you need. Share fairly. Prepare tools. Drink water.”

Mouse traced the words with a tiny paw. “I can say, ‘I need help reaching,’” he practiced.

Squirrel smiled. “I can say, ‘Let’s share so it’s fair.’”

Bunny wiggled her ears. “I can say, ‘I need water, please.’”

Ollie nodded. “I can say, ‘I would like the pear, please,’ and I can listen to you, too.”

They cleaned up together. Crumbs went into a small bin. The spoon went back. The step stool folded shut with a little click. The chart stayed on the shelf where all could see.

As the pantry light clicked off, Ollie felt calm and proud. He had used his voice. He had listened. He had helped his friends be strong and kind.

Ollie whispered into the soft dark, “When we speak up with kind words, we take care of ourselves. When we listen and share, we take care of each other.”

The pantry seemed to smile.

The End.
