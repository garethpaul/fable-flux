# Koko's Peaceful Garden Day

Koko the koala loved spending quiet time in Grandpa's old garden shed. The wooden walls smelled like fresh earth and sunshine. Colorful flower pots lined the shelves, and soft light streamed through the dusty windows.

One morning, Koko was arranging his favorite smooth stones when he heard a loud "BUZZ BUZZ BUZZ!"

"Oh my!" said Koko softly. A busy bee was flying around and around, bumping into the window. The bee looked very upset.

"Excuse me, little bee," Koko said in his gentle voice. "You seem worried. Can I help you?"

The bee stopped buzzing and landed on a flower pot. "I'm Bella! I need to get outside to visit the sunflowers, but this clear wall won't let me through!"

Koko took a deep breath, the way Grandpa taught him when solving problems. "I understand you're frustrated, Bella. Let's think together."

"But I need to hurry!" Bella buzzed loudly. "The other bees are waiting!"

"When we're upset, it helps to slow down first," Koko said peacefully. "May I show you something that helps me feel calm?"

Bella tilted her tiny head. "Okay..."

Koko sat cross-legged on his soft cushion. "I like to breathe slowly and count to three. Want to try? Breathe in... one, two, three. Breathe out... one, two, three."

Bella tried it too. Her buzzing became quieter. "That does feel better!"

"Now," said Koko, "let's look for a solution together. I notice you're trying to go through the window, but what about the door?"

"Door?" Bella flew up to look. "Oh! I was so focused on the bright light, I didn't see the open door!"

"Sometimes we all do that," Koko smiled. "We're different - you love flying fast to beautiful flowers, and I love sitting quietly with my stones. But we can still help each other."

Bella did a happy little loop in the air. "You're right! Being different makes us special. Thank you for helping me slow down and think."

"Would you like to make a deal?" asked Koko. "I can watch for the door when you visit the shed, and maybe you could tell me about the pretty flowers you see?"

"Yes!" buzzed Bella excitedly. "I'd love to share stories about the garden!"

Koko walked with Bella to the open door. The warm air smelled like roses and lavender. "There are the sunflowers, just past the tomato plants."

"Perfect!" Bella did a grateful dance in the air. "Remember - when things feel hard, breathe slowly first!"

"And remember," added Koko, "asking for help is always okay. We can solve problems better together."

Bella flew toward the bright yellow sunflowers, calling back, "Thank you, Koko! See you tomorrow!"

Koko returned to his cozy corner, feeling happy and peaceful. He arranged his stones in a calm circle and practiced his breathing. The shed felt even more special now that he had helped a new friend.

As the sun moved across the sky, Koko thought about his morning. Taking time to breathe and listen had helped both him and Bella feel better. Even though they were very different - one quiet and slow, one busy and fast - they had worked together perfectly.

That evening, Bella returned with stories of butterfly friends and sweet flower nectar. Koko shared his favorite smooth stone, and they planned more garden adventures together.

The End.
