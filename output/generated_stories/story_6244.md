# The Clean-Up Note in the Cozy Closet

Bunny Ben was a determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, brave eyes. He loved his cozy closet hideaway. It smelled like cedar and warm blankets. It was quiet and snuggly, with a tiny window that let in a stripe of morning sun. Ben kept his crayons, a small notebook, and a little cup for water in the corner.

One day, Ben wrinkled his nose. “Sniff, sniff,” he said. “Hmm. My hideaway smells funny.” He looked around. There were muddy paw prints on the floor. There was a sticky carrot cup with juice dried on the rim. A tiny pile of fluff sat by the door, and a crumpled napkin stuck to his foot.

Ben’s ears stood tall. “I can fix this,” he said. “I am determined.”

He took a big breath. The air felt dusty and tickly. “Ah-choo!” Ben sneezed. His whiskers wiggled. “This is a hygiene problem,” he said, trying the new word he learned. “Hygiene means keeping things clean and healthy.”

Ben looked at his notebook. He liked to write little notes. Writing helped him think. He opened to a clean page and drew a happy soap bubble. He sounded out words, speaking softly. “C-l-e-a-n. Clean.” Then he wrote:

“Plan:
1. Wash paws.
2. Wipe floor.
3. Rinse cup.
4. Ask for help kindly.”

He smiled. “A plan helps me be brave.”

Ben went to the sink. The water felt cool on his paws. “Splish, splash.” He rubbed with gentle soap. “Bubbles!” he giggled, watching the rainbow swirls. He rinsed and shook, shake-shake, and dried with a clean cloth. His paws smelled like lemon and sunshine.

Back in the closet, Ben whispered, “Okay, floor.” He found a soft cloth and a tiny bowl of warm water. He dipped the cloth. “Swish.” He wiped the muddy paw prints. The floor looked smooth and clean. He hummed a happy tune. It felt good.

The sticky carrot cup looked at him from the corner. Ben picked it up. “You need a bath,” he told the cup. He rinsed it until it shined. “Now you are ready for more water,” he said. “We can use this cup again and again. That is good for the Earth.” Ben felt proud. “That is called sustainability,” he added, practicing another big word.

Just then, his friend, Mouse Mina, peeked in. “Hi, Ben,” she squeaked. “Can I play in your closet hideaway?”

Ben smiled. He remembered his plan. He also remembered peaceful words. “Hi, Mina,” he said kindly. “I want to play. But first, I need help. My hideaway got messy, and it made my nose sneeze. Clean places help us stay healthy. Can we clean together?”

Mina’s whiskers twitched. “I stepped in a puddle,” she said. “Maybe I made a muddy print. I’m sorry.” Her ears drooped a little.

Ben shook his head gently. “It’s okay,” he said in a warm voice. “We can fix it. Let’s wash our paws and wipe the floor. We can do it as a team.”

Mina nodded. “Yes! Team clean!” She giggled.

They went to the sink. “Splish, splash,” they sang. The soap made soft clouds in their paws. “Rub, rub, rub,” said Ben. “Between fingers and thumbs,” added Mina. Their paws smelled fresh and sweet.

Back in the closet, Ben took his notebook. He wrote a little “Clean-Up Note” in big, friendly letters. He sounded out the words so Mina could hear. “Please wipe paws. Please use the cup for drinks. Please put trash in the bin. Thank you, friends! Love, Ben.”

Mina clapped. “That is peaceful communication,” she said slowly. “You are asking nicely.”

Ben taped the note on the wall with a tiny bit of reusable tape. “Click.” He smiled. “Now everyone can see our clean rules.”

They looked around. The floor was dry. The blankets were folded. The cup was rinsed. The air smelled like lemon and cedar. The tiny sun stripe felt warm on their fur.

“Let’s celebrate,” said Mina.

“After we tidy one more thing,” said Ben. He picked up the crumpled napkin and put it in the small recycling bin. “Paper can be recycled,” he explained. “That helps the Earth.” He felt brave and calm.

They cuddled into the blanket corner. It felt soft and cozy. Ben’s heart felt light. “Thank you for helping,” he said.

“Thank you for asking kindly,” Mina replied. “I like your note. It helps me remember.”

Ben wiggled with happy energy. “We made a clean, healthy space. We washed our paws. We talked kindly. We wrote a note. We cared for our Earth. We were very brave.”

Mina nodded. “And now we can play.”

They drew pictures in the notebook: bubbles, a bright sun, and a big cup with a smile. They sang their clean-up song again, soft as a whisper: “Splish, splash, rub and rinse. Clean paws, happy friends.”

The closet hideaway felt safe and fresh. Ben’s nose didn’t tickle anymore. He was proud of his plan, and proud of his kind words.

The clear lesson sparkled in Ben’s mind: When we keep our bodies and spaces clean, and when we use our words kindly, everyone feels good.

The End.
