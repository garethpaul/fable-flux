# Bubbles the Dolphin’s Study Corner Splash

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a happy squeak. One rainy morning, Bubbles swam into the study corner. The study corner was cozy and warm. A soft lamp glowed like a little sun. Crayons smelled sweet and waxy. Papers felt smooth under Bubbles’ flippers. A small fan hummed, “whirr, whirr,” and the rain tapped gently on the window, “tip-tap, tip-tap.”

“Today, I will practice my shapes and sounds,” Bubbles said. “I will be ready for Ocean School!”

Bubbles set out a plan. “First, shapes. Then, sounds. Then, share time.” Bubbles smiled. “I am prepared.”

Bubbles’ friends joined on the screen shell, a magic shell that showed friends from far-away seas. There was Lina the lanternfish from the deep sea, shining a soft blue light. There was Tiko the turtle from a warm island reef, with a bright patterned shell. There was Noor the narwhal from icy waters, with a long, twisty tusk.

“Hello!” said Bubbles. “Let’s learn together!”

“Hello!” sang Lina. “I brought my glow notes.”

“Hola!” said Tiko, waving a flipper. “I brought my lucky pencil.”

“Salam,” said Noor. “I brought a warm scarf. It smells like sea salt and mint tea.”

Bubbles felt happy to have friends from many places. Their voices sounded different but kind. Their smiles felt the same—warm and bright.

Bubbles started with shapes. “Circle, square, triangle,” Bubbles said, drawing carefully. The crayon slid and squeaked, “skrip, skrip.” The circle looked a little bumpy. The triangle was wobbly.

“Oh no,” Bubbles said softly. “It is not perfect.” Bubbles’ fins drooped.

Lina’s light twinkled. “It is okay. Try again. We can glow with you.”

Tiko tapped the screen shell. “We all learn step by step. I practice slow. See?” Tiko drew a slow, steady square. “Straight lines take time.”

Noor smiled gently. “When the ice is thick, I nudge, nudge, nudge. I don’t stop. That is resilience.”

“Re-sil-ience,” Bubbles repeated. The word felt big, like a bubble that needed a deep breath. Bubbles took one. “I can do it.”

Bubbles looked at the plan again. “First, shapes. I will try again.” Bubbles made a dot for the center and went around slowly. “Skrip, skrip.” The circle was smoother!

“You did it!” Lina cheered. Her light flickered like tiny stars.

Bubbles clapped flippers. “Thank you! Now, triangle.” Bubbles drew one line, then another. The last line shook when thunder rumbled, “Rrrr.” The triangle tilted.

“I messed up,” Bubbles whispered.

Tiko smiled. “It’s not a mess. It’s a try. We can improve.”

“How?” asked Bubbles.

“Preparation,” said Noor. “Let’s set our papers with shells so they don’t slip.” Noor showed a neat row of pebble weights.

Bubbles placed small shells at the corners. “Click, click.” The paper stayed still. Bubbles tried again. One line, two lines, three lines. Triangle!

“Yes!” Bubbles giggled. “Prepared and patient.”

“Now sounds,” said Bubbles. The friends practiced letter sounds together.

“B says b-b-bubble,” said Bubbles.

“L says l-l-light,” Lina glowed.

“T says t-t-turtle,” laughed Tiko.

“N says n-n-narwhal,” nodded Noor.

They sounded words in turn. When it was Bubbles’ turn to read a small word, the letters swam together in Bubbles’ eyes. The word felt slippery.

“I can’t,” Bubbles said, tummy tight.

Lina dimmed her light to calm the room. “Let’s break it into tiny bits,” she said softly.

Tiko hummed a gentle tune, “doo-doo-doo,” to slow their breaths.

Noor counted, “One, two, three.” He said, “We can try again. We can be resilient.”

Bubbles took another deep breath. The lamp glowed warm on Bubbles’ face. The fan whispered, “whirr.” The rain tapped, “tip-tap.”

“C… a… t,” Bubbles said slowly. “Cat!”

The friends cheered. “You did it!” Their voices sounded like waves clapping the shore.

Bubbles smiled big. “I can read small words when I go slow. I can try again when it’s hard. That is resilience.”

It was share time. Each friend showed something special from their home.

Lina held up a glowing pebble. “From the deep sea,” she said. It shone like moonlight.

Tiko showed a tiny drum. “From my island,” he said, tapping, “tum-tum.” The beat was warm and happy.

Noor shared a scarf. “From the cold sea,” he said, rubbing it on his cheek. “Soft and cozy.”

Bubbles held up a shell from the study corner. “From my shelf,” Bubbles said. “It smells like the ocean. It reminds me to listen.”

Bubbles felt grateful. “Thank you for helping me,” Bubbles said. “Your lights, sounds, and stories make me brave.”

“We are grateful for you,” said Lina. “You keep trying.”

“You make plans,” said Noor. “That is good preparation.”

“You improve every day,” said Tiko. “Little steps!”

Bubbles looked at the plan: shapes, sounds, share. All done! The rain slowed to a quiet drip. The lamp felt warm. The papers were full of smoother circles and stronger triangles. The letters looked friendlier.

Bubbles squeaked with joy. “Today I learned that when things are hard, I can breathe, try again, and ask friends for help. I can be resilient.”

The friends waved goodbye in many ways. “Bye!” “Adiós!” “Ma’a salama!”

Bubbles tucked the crayons away, stacked the papers, and placed the shell on top. “Thank you, study corner,” Bubbles whispered. “I am ready for tomorrow.”

The End.
