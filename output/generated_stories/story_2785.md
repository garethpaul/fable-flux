# Rusty Pup and the Attic School

The house was very still. Up a creaky ladder, under a slanted roof, there was a quiet attic. Dust danced in the soft light from a small round window. Boxes made gentle cardboard mountains. Old books waited on a shelf. In the quiet, a tiny blue light blinked.

“Beep-beep! Good morning,” said Rusty Pup, the helpful robot dog. His metal paws made a soft tap-tap on the wooden floor. His ears whirred and wiggled. His screen-face showed a smile.

“Today is clean-up day,” Rusty Pup said. He wagged his antenna tail. “We can learn and help at the same time.”

Two children climbed into the attic. Mia had curly hair and a bright red shirt. Theo wore a striped hat that slid over one ear.

“It smells like old paper,” said Mia, wrinkling her nose.

“And lemons,” said Theo, holding up a fuzzy yellow duster. “I like the lemon soap smell.”

Rusty Pup’s nose light blinked. “Sniff-scan: paper, lemon, and a little bit of… mystery.” He giggled. “Let’s learn while we clean. Chores can teach us new things.”

Mia clapped. “I want to dust!”

“I want to sweep!” said Theo, grabbing the broom.

They both reached for the same box of old books. Their fingers bumped. Mia frowned. Theo frowned too.

“I saw it first,” Mia said.

“But I want books,” Theo said. “Books are my favorite.”

Rusty Pup sat down with a soft clink. “Pause and think,” he said gently. “We use peaceful solutions. We can take turns.”

Mia crossed her arms. Theo hugged the broom. The attic felt extra quiet.

Rusty Pup’s belly screen lit up with a picture of a clock. “Let’s learn about taking turns. We will use a timer. Beep-beep! Mia looks at the books for two minutes. Then, Theo looks for two minutes. While one looks, the other does a different job.”

Mia and Theo looked at each other. Their faces softened.

“Okay,” Mia said.

“Okay,” Theo said.

“Teamwork,” Rusty Pup said. He set the timer. The gentle tick-tick sounded like raindrops.

Mia opened the first book. The pages felt soft and thin. “Oh! It’s a picture of the moon,” she whispered.

Theo swished the broom. Swish-swish. Dust bunnies danced away. “I am a sweeping hero,” he said in a deep voice, and he laughed.

Rusty Pup wagged. “New word: crater,” he said, pointing to the moon picture. “A crater is a hole on the moon. Can you say crater?”

“Crater!” Mia and Theo said together.

The timer went ping! “Switch!” said Rusty Pup.

Theo put down the broom and opened the next book. “This one has a big triangle roof,” he said.

“New word: attic,” Rusty Pup said, tapping the slanted ceiling with his paw. “We are in an attic now. An attic is a room under the roof.”

Mia picked up the duster. Swish-swish on the shelf. The lemon smell grew stronger. “I like the attic,” she said. “It is quiet. I can hear my own breath.”

Theo turned another page. “What is this shape?”

“Triangle,” Rusty Pup said. He drew a triangle in the dust with his paw and then wiped it clean. “We can learn shapes while we do chores.”

Ping! The timer sang again.

“Switch!” said Rusty Pup. “Mia, your turn to read. Theo, your turn to dust.”

They smiled and swapped. It felt fair. It felt calm. The attic seemed brighter already.

Rusty Pup opened a small box with his gentle metal teeth. Inside were shiny buttons, smooth marbles, and a little bell.

“Math time!” he said. “Let’s sort and count. Sorting is a great job. Counting is learning.”

Mia and Theo sat cross-legged on a soft old blanket. The wood felt cool under their legs. They made neat little piles—buttons here, marbles there.

“One, two, three,” Mia counted the marbles. She rolled one in her fingers. It felt cold and round.

“Four, five, six,” Theo counted the buttons. They clicked like tiny raindrops in his hand. “We have six buttons!”

Rusty Pup wagged. “Six is a good number. How many in all?”

Mia and Theo pushed the piles together. “One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine!” they said, giggling.

“Great job,” Rusty Pup said. “We learned numbers while we did our chore. We also cleaned, and we shared.”

When they reached the biggest box, they both paused. “It looks heavy,” said Theo.

“I feel a little worried,” Mia whispered.

Rusty Pup nodded. “When a job is big, we make a plan. Plans make our minds feel calm. Step one: slide the box. Step two: open together. Step three: sort and count.”

They each held one side and slid, shhhk. The box moved slowly. They lifted the lid. Inside were paper stars, a small drum, and a tiny chalkboard.

Mia tapped the drum, tum-tum. Theo wrote his name on the chalkboard with a squeaky line. Rusty Pup’s ears perked.

“School time!” he said. “I am Teacher Pup. Today’s lesson: taking turns and helping.”

They took turns tapping the drum. Tum-tum for Mia, tum for Theo, then tum-tum for Rusty Pup’s metal paw. They took turns writing shapes on the chalkboard. Triangle. Circle. Square. Rusty Pup drew a star and said, “Star for teamwork!”

Finally, the attic was tidy. The shelves were shiny. The floor was clear. The quiet felt happy and warm.

Mia hugged Rusty Pup. His metal was cool, but his screen-face glowed soft and kind. “We did it,” she said.

Theo nodded. “We learned and we cleaned.”

Rusty Pup wagged. “Today we learned peaceful solutions, taking turns, sorting, counting, and shapes. Chores can be like school. When we share and plan, big jobs feel small.”

Mia smiled. “Next time, I will ask to take turns.”

“And I will listen,” said Theo.

Rusty Pup blinked his blue light and lay down with a gentle whirr. The lemon scent floated in the quiet attic. The little bell went ting-ting, like a happy goodbye.

The End.
