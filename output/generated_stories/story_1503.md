# Leo the Lion Cub Saves the Kitchen Day

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and a tiny, twitchy tail. One sunny morning, he padded into a warm kitchen. The air smelled like cinnamon toast and sweet oranges. Pots hummed, a kettle sang a soft whistle, and the floor felt cozy on Leo’s paws.

“Good morning!” chirped Pip the sparrow from the window. “Tweet! Big day?”

Leo puffed up. “Yes! We have a picnic at noon,” he said, checking the round clock on the wall. “We have to be ready on time.”

He peered around. The fridge hummed. The tap dripped drip, drip, drip. A light glowed even though the sun was bright. The trash bin bulged. A plastic bottle rolled across the floor.

“Oh no,” Leo whispered. “This kitchen is using too much and wasting things. That’s not kind to our world.” His whiskers wiggled with worry, but he stood tall. “I can help.”

Just then, Mia the mouse popped out from under a towel. She wore a tiny green ribbon. “Squeak! I can help,” she said.

And Benny the bunny hopped in, ears flopping. “Hop-hop! Me too!”

Leo smiled. “Friends, we can fix the waste and still get ready for our picnic by noon. We will move, we will share, and we will be brave.”

Leo made a plan, tapping his paw to the tick-tock of the clock. “First, we stop the drip. Then we sort the trash. Then we save energy. Then we pack our food.”

Pip flitted to the tap. “Tweet! Twist-twist!” Leo climbed on a sturdy stool, paws steady. He turned the handle gently. Drip… stop. The kitchen grew quiet for a breath, like the room smiled.

“Great job,” said Mia, clapping her tiny paws. “Now the trash!”

They opened the bin. Inside were orange peels, paper, a banana peel, a plastic bottle, a glass jar, and a squished can.

Leo wrinkled his nose. “Pee-ew! It smells like old bananas.” He took a brave breath and nodded. “We can sort this.”

Mia rolled a small basket out. On it were labels: Food scraps, Paper, Plastic, Glass, Metal.

Leo pointed with a gentle paw. “Food scraps for the compost. Compost turns old food into new soil. Soil helps plants grow. That is sustainability. That means we keep our world healthy and not waste things.”

“Hop!” Benny scooped the peels into the Food scraps. “Hop-hop! For the garden!”

Pip pecked at the glass jar and nudged it into Glass. “Tweet!”

Leo put the plastic bottle in Plastic. “We can clean and recycle this, so it can be used again.”

Mia folded the paper nice and flat and placed it in Paper. “Squeak!”

Benny carefully plinked the can into Metal. “Plink!”

They moved fast, like a little dance. Step, sort, tap, hop! Their bodies warmed up. The warm kitchen smelled less stinky now, more like cinnamon and clean soap.

The clock ticked. Leo checked it. “We are on time!” he said. “Next, save energy.”

He turned off the extra light. Sunshine poured through the window, bright and golden. He closed the fridge quickly after taking out what they needed: apples, carrots, and cheese. “Open, pick, close,” he said. “This saves cool air.”

Pip fluttered by the kettle. “The water is singing! Do we need it now?”

Leo listened. “Not yet,” he said, and clicked it off. The singing stopped. The kitchen hummed softly, calm and kind.

Mia pointed to a reusable bag hanging on a hook. “Let’s pack in this! No plastic bags.”

“Smart!” said Leo.

Benny bounced. “Hop! But the floor is messy.” A splash of juice glowed in a sticky puddle.

Leo took a cloth and warm water. “We can clean with a little soap,” he said. Swish, wipe, swish! The cloth made circles. The floor shone. It smelled fresh, like oranges and sun.

The clock tick-tocked. Leo’s heart beat with it. “How much time?” he asked.

Mia peeped at the clock. “We still have time before noon,” she said. “Good planning!”

Leo grinned. “Now, picnic food.” He washed the apples with a trickle, not a gush. “We use only what we need,” he said. “That is another way to care.”

Pip dried the apples on a towel. “Tweet-tweet, shiny apples!”

Benny sliced carrots carefully with a safe, small cutter while Leo held the board steady. Crunch! Crunch! The sound was happy.

They filled a small jar with water for flowers. “Reuse!” Mia sang, placing daisies inside.

Leo looked around. The warm kitchen sparkled. The trash bin was no longer bulging. The compost pail was ready for the garden. The lights were off, but sunlight danced on the walls. The tap was quiet. The clock showed almost noon.

Pip twirled in the air. “We did it!”

Benny thumped his foot softly. “Hop-hooray!”

Mia squeaked, “We worked together!”

Leo stood proud. His brave heart felt big and bright. “We saved energy. We sorted waste. We cleaned up. We used only what we needed. We were on time. We did it with friendship and movement. That is brave.”

They packed their basket: apples, carrot sticks, cheese cubes, and a cloth napkin. No throwaway stuff. Leo carried the reusable bag, and they all stepped toward the door.

Before they left, Leo placed a tiny note on the fridge: “Use only what you need. Sort and save. Love our Earth.”

He smiled back at the warm kitchen and whispered, “Thank you.”

Outside, the sun kissed their faces. The grass felt springy under their feet and paws. They sat for their picnic and shared their food. It tasted extra good, like crunchy kindness.

Mia nibbled. “Today we learned that small steps can help our big Earth.”

Pip chirped. “And friends make it fun!”

Benny nodded. “And moving our bodies helps us get things done on time!”

Leo licked an apple juice drop from his whisker. “We were brave and gentle,” he said. “We took care of our kitchen and our world.”

They clinked their carrot sticks together like tiny green flags. “To caring!” they cheered.

The End.
