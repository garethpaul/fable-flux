# Busy Beaver's Attic Story Show

In a quiet attic, a busy beaver named Bix lived in a cozy corner. The attic was soft with dust and warm with sun beams. The floorboards smelled like dry wood, and little specks danced in the light. Bix loved the hush. He could hear his own tiny heartbeat. Thump-thump! He could hear the clock tick. Tick, tock, tick.

Bix wiggled his flat tail and clapped his paws. “Today, I will make a story show!” he said. His voice was bright and bouncy. “I will tell a story. I will move and play. I will get ready, step by step.”

He looked around the attic. There were boxes, ribbons, buttons, and blankets. There was a small lamp. There were old hats and soft scarves. Bix took a deep breath. The attic smelled like cinnamon from an old candle. It made him smile.

“First, I prepare,” Bix said to himself. “Good stories need plans.” He got a little notebook and a stubby pencil. Scritch-scratch! He drew a big sun. He drew a river. He drew a tiny boat. He drew a smiling beaver. “This is my hero,” he said. “Hello, hero!”

He stood up tall. “Next, I warm up my body. Stories can dance!” Bix stretched his paws to the sky. “Reach, reach!” He touched his toes. “Bend, bend!” He hopped side to side. Hop, hop, hop! His tail thumped the floor. Thump-thump! He giggled. “My body is ready!”

Then he warmed up his voice. “La-la-la,” he sang softly. He whispered, “Hello.” He boomed, “HELLO!” He laughed. “Not too loud, Bix,” he said kindly to himself. “This is a quiet attic.”

He set a small blanket stage. He tied a ribbon as a curtain. He placed a hat for a mountain. He put a blue scarf for a river. Swish. He set a tiny button as a boat. Click. “Now I am ready to tell.”

He lit the lamp. A gentle glow filled the corner. It felt like warm honey. Bix took a breath. “Friends,” he said to the hats and boxes, “come sit. I have a story!” He waved to his pretend friends: Mr. Teacup, Miss Ribbon, and Captain Button. It made him feel brave.

Bix began. “Once, there was a brave little beaver. He wanted to cross a river to share a song with his friends.”

He made the scarf ripple. “The river went swish-swish.” He made his paws paddle. “The beaver went paddle-paddle.” He moved the button boat. “The boat went bob-bob.” He changed his voice to a squeaky voice. “But the wind said, Whooo.” He made a deep voice. “And the rocks said, Bump.”

Bix paused. “Oh no,” he said softly, feeling the worry in his belly. “How will the beaver cross?” He looked at his notebook. He smiled. “Prepare!” he whispered.

He showed the beaver doing little moves. “Lift, lift!” He did ten tiny tail taps. Tap-tap-tap-tap-tap-tap-tap-tap-tap-tap! He breathed in and out. “In, out.” He tied a strong ribbon rope from the hat mountain to the boat. “This will help,” he said.

He spoke to the pretend beaver in a gentle voice. “You can do it. You planned. You practiced. Try again.”

Bix moved the boat. The boat went steady now. He moved his body like a swimmer. Splash without water, swoosh without mess. He felt his muscles warm and happy. His heart beat brave. Thump, thump, thump.

“The beaver reached the other side,” Bix said with joy. He made the hat bow. He made the button boat spin. “He shared his song.” Bix sang a simple tune. “La, la, la, we did it, yes we did!” He made the river hum softly. “Swish.” He made the wind whisper kindly. “Whooo, good job.”

Bix took a big bow. He smiled so wide his cheeks felt round and rosy. “Thank you, friends,” he told the hats and boxes.

He sat down and hugged his notebook. The attic felt proud and calm. The lamp hummed. The dust specks still danced.

Just then, a little creak sounded at the door. Creak. Bix looked up. His neighbor, a shy mouse named Mip, peeked in. “Hi, Bix,” Mip squeaked. “I heard your soft song. Can I watch?”

“Yes!” said Bix. “Do you want to tell a small part?”

Mip’s whiskers twitched. “I am shy,” he said.

Bix nodded. “We can prepare together,” he said. “We can move our bodies, warm our voices, and make a plan.”

They stood side by side. They stretched. Reach, reach. They hopped. Hop, hop. They whispered, “Hello.” They smiled. Bix gave Mip a tiny scarf. “This can be your river.”

Mip held the scarf. “Swish,” he said. He giggled. “That feels nice.”

Together, they told the story again. Bix moved the boat. Mip made the wind friendly. “Whooo-hoo!” They both sang the little song. “La, la, la, we did it!”

When they finished, Mip clapped his tiny paws. “I told a story!” he said. His eyes were bright. “It was fun. The plan helped.”

Bix nodded. “Stories can be strong with good plans. Stories can be fun with movement. Stories can be kind with gentle words.”

They cleaned up the attic. Fold, fold. Tie, tie. Breathe in, breathe out. Bix put the notebook away. He patted it. “Tomorrow, a new tale,” he said.

The lesson was clear in Bix’s heart: prepare your plan, move your body, and tell your story with care. Then even a quiet attic can shine.

The End.
