# Penny Panda's Brave Breakfast Adventure

In a cozy bedroom with soft yellow walls and fluffy cloud curtains, lived a gentle panda named Penny. Her room smelled like lavender and sunshine, with bamboo plants by the window that swayed in the morning breeze.

Every morning, Penny's tummy would rumble like a tiny thunder cloud. "Grumble, grumble, GRUMBLE!" But Penny had a big problem - she was too shy to ask for breakfast.

"I'm too little," Penny whispered to her stuffed elephant, Mr. Peanuts. "What if I can't reach the kitchen counter? What if I drop something?"

Mr. Peanuts seemed to smile back at her with his button eyes.

One sunny Tuesday morning, Penny's tummy rumbled extra loud. "GRUMBLE-RUMBLE-TUMBLE!" It sounded like a drum! Penny giggled at the funny noise, but she was still very hungry.

"Mama and Papa are still sleeping," Penny said softly, looking at her bedroom door. "But my tummy is so hungry it's singing songs!"

Penny took a deep breath that smelled like the fresh air coming through her window. "Maybe... maybe I can try to help myself today," she whispered.

She put on her fuzzy pink slippers - pat, pat, pat - and tiptoed to the kitchen. The morning light made everything look magical and golden.

In the kitchen, Penny looked up, up, up at the tall counter. "Oh my," she said. "That's very high."

But then Penny remembered something. "I'm resourceful!" she said, using a big word her mama had taught her. "That means I can figure things out!"

Penny found her special step stool hiding behind the kitchen table. She pushed it - scrape, scrape - to the counter. "Perfect!" she said, feeling proud.

On the counter, she found a bowl, a banana, and some round, crunchy cereal that looked like tiny donuts. "I can make my own breakfast!" Penny said, her heart beating with excitement.

Carefully, she peeled the banana. The sweet smell made her mouth water. She broke it into small pieces - plop, plop, plop - into her bowl. Then she sprinkled the cereal on top. It made a cheerful rattling sound like tiny bells.

"This looks beautiful AND healthy!" Penny said, admiring her colorful breakfast.

Just then, Mama Panda walked into the kitchen, yawning and stretching.

"Good morning, my sweet Penny!" Mama said, then stopped and stared. "Did you make breakfast all by yourself?"

Penny nodded, her black ears wiggling with pride. "My tummy was singing hungry songs, so I used my resourcefulness!"

"What a brave, capable little panda you are!" Mama said, giving Penny the warmest, softest hug. "You chose nutritious foods and solved your problem all by yourself!"

Penny's chest felt warm and glowy, like sunshine was living inside her heart. "I was scared at first, but I tried anyway," she said.

"That's what being confident means," Mama explained. "Believing in yourself even when things seem hard."

As Penny crunched her delicious breakfast, she felt bigger and stronger inside. The banana was creamy and sweet, and the cereal made happy crunching sounds.

"Tomorrow, I'm going to make breakfast again!" Penny announced proudly.

From that day on, whenever Penny felt unsure about trying something new, she remembered her breakfast adventure. She learned that being brave doesn't mean not feeling scared - it means trying anyway!

The End.
