# Kody's Fair Music Day

Kody the koala had soft gray fur and gentle black eyes. He loved music more than eucalyptus leaves! Today he walked into the bright practice room with his small violin case.

The practice room smelled like wood polish and had shiny floors. Colorful music notes decorated the walls. A big piano sat in one corner, and music stands lined up in neat rows.

"Good morning, everyone!" said Mrs. Robin, their music teacher. Her feathers sparkled in the sunlight streaming through the windows.

Kody looked around and saw his friends. There was Penny the penguin with her flute, Max the monkey with his drums, and Luna the lamb with her trumpet. But something didn't seem right.

"I want the best music stand!" squeaked Max, grabbing the tallest one. "I'm the loudest, so I should have it!"

"That's not fair!" said Penny. "I was here first!"

Luna started to cry. "Now I don't have anywhere to put my music!"

Kody felt his tummy get tight. This wasn't how music time should be. Everyone looked upset, and no one was being kind to each other.

"Wait," said Kody in his calm, gentle voice. "This doesn't feel right. We should all have a turn with the good music stands."

Mrs. Robin nodded. "What do you think we should do, Kody?"

Kody thought carefully. He touched his fuzzy chin with his paw. "I have an idea! What if we take turns? We could make a plan so everyone gets to use their favorite stand."

"But I want the tall one NOW!" Max stomped his feet, making the floor shake.

Kody stayed calm. "Max, how would you feel if someone took your favorite banana without asking?"

Max's eyes got wide. "I... I would be sad."

"That's how Penny and Luna feel," Kody explained gently. "Being fair means everyone gets a chance."

Kody walked to the whiteboard and picked up a colorful marker. "Let's make a schedule! Max can use the tall stand today. Tomorrow, Penny gets it. Then Luna gets her turn. After that, I'll try it too!"

The sweet smell of markers filled the air as Kody drew a chart with pictures. A drum for Max's day, a flute for Penny's day, a trumpet for Luna's day, and a violin for his own day.

"That looks fair!" chirped Mrs. Robin. "Everyone gets what they want, just at different times."

Max looked at his feet. "I'm sorry I was grabby. That wasn't nice."

"It's okay," said Penny, patting Max's shoulder with her flipper. "We all make mistakes."

Luna stopped crying and smiled. "I like Kody's plan! Now I know I'll get my turn tomorrow!"

Kody felt warm and happy inside. "When we're fair to each other, everyone feels good. Music sounds better when we're all happy!"

The friends spent the rest of practice time playing beautiful songs together. The room filled with lovely music - drums beating steady rhythms, the flute singing high notes, the trumpet calling out strong melodies, and Kody's violin adding sweet harmony.

When practice ended, Mrs. Robin clapped her wings. "You solved that problem wonderfully, Kody. You showed everyone how to be fair and kind."

Kody packed up his violin, feeling proud. "Being fair isn't always easy, but it's always right. When everyone gets a turn, everyone can be happy!"

As they left the practice room, all four friends were smiling. They had learned that being fair and thinking of others makes everything more fun.

The End.
