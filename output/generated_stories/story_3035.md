# Squeaky's Big Cleanup

Squeaky the mouse lived in a cozy hole behind the bathroom sink. He had soft brown fur and tiny pink paws. Every morning, he loved to watch the sunshine stream through the frosted window.

But today, Squeaky felt worried. The bathroom was very messy! Toothpaste tubes lay on the counter. Towels sat crumpled on the floor. Water drops covered the mirror like tiny raindrops.

"Oh no," squeaked Squeaky. "I made this mess yesterday when I was playing. The family will be so upset!"

Squeaky remembered how he had climbed on the toothpaste tube to reach the faucet. SQUIRT! Toothpaste went everywhere. Then he had swung from towel to towel like a tiny acrobat. The soft towels tumbled down with gentle THUMP sounds.

"I need to fix this," Squeaky said bravely. "But how can such a little mouse clean such a big mess?"

Squeaky took a deep breath. The bathroom smelled like lavender soap and fresh mint. He decided to start small.

First, he pushed the toothpaste cap with his nose. ROLL, ROLL, ROLL. It was heavy, but Squeaky kept trying. His little paws worked hard. After many pushes, the cap reached the tube.

"One thing done!" Squeaky cheered.

Next came the towels. Squeaky grabbed a corner with his tiny teeth. He pulled and tugged. The towel was much bigger than him! It felt soft and fluffy against his whiskers.

"This is hard work," puffed Squeaky. "But I won't give up!"

Slowly, slowly, Squeaky dragged the first towel toward the towel rack. His little heart beat fast. THUMP-THUMP, THUMP-THUMP. When he finally hung it up, Squeaky felt proud.

The second towel was even harder. Squeaky's paws felt tired. He wanted to quit. But then he remembered how kind the family was to him. They always left tiny crumbs for him to find.

"They deserve a clean bathroom," Squeaky whispered.

With patience, Squeaky worked on the second towel. Push, pull, push, pull. His whiskers twitched with effort. Finally, it hung neatly too.

Last was the mirror. Water drops sparkled like diamonds in the morning light. Squeaky found a small cloth. He climbed up the sink, his tiny claws clicking on the smooth surface.

Squeaky wiped and wiped. His reflection looked back at him - a determined little mouse with messy fur but a big heart.

Just then, the bathroom door opened. Little Sarah walked in with her mom.

"Wow!" said Sarah. "The bathroom is so clean and tidy!"

"You're right," said Mom, smiling. "Someone did a wonderful job in here."

Squeaky hid behind his hole, but his heart felt warm and happy. He had worked hard to make things better. The sweet smell of success filled his tiny nose.

From that day on, Squeaky was more careful when he played. And whenever he made a mess, he always cleaned it up right away. He learned that making mistakes was okay, but making things better was even more important.

"Small paws can do big things," Squeaky whispered happily, "when you try your best and never give up."

The End.
