# Milo's Basketball Dream

Milo the monkey loved the creative art room at school. The colorful walls were covered with finger paintings. Bright crayons filled big boxes on every table. The room smelled like fresh paper and paint.

Today was special. Milo wanted to make something for the school sports day. All the other animals were making posters about their favorite sports.

"I want to make a basketball hoop!" Milo said, clapping his furry hands together. "Then I can practice shooting baskets!"

Milo looked around the art room. He saw paper, glue, and scissors. But he needed something round for a hoop. Something strong too.

"Hmm," Milo scratched his head. "What can I use?"

He tried rolling paper into a circle. But it kept falling apart. The paper was too floppy.

"This won't work," Milo sighed. His shoulders drooped down. The soft fur on his arms felt heavy with sadness.

Just then, his friend Bella the bunny hopped over. "What's wrong, Milo?" she asked in her gentle voice.

"I want to make a basketball hoop, but I can't find anything round and strong enough," Milo explained.

Bella's pink nose twitched as she thought. "What about the paper plates from snack time?" she suggested.

Milo's eyes grew wide and bright. "That's a great idea! But how do I make a hole in the middle?"

"Let's ask Mrs. Robin for help," Bella said kindly.

Mrs. Robin, their teacher, had soft feathers and a warm smile. She listened to Milo's problem.

"I can help you cut the middle out," she said. "But first, let's think about how to make it strong."

Milo thought hard. He tapped his finger on his chin. "What if I use two paper plates? One on top of the other?"

"Smart thinking!" Mrs. Robin chirped. "That will make it much stronger."

Together, they glued two paper plates together. Mrs. Robin carefully cut out the center. Now Milo had a perfect hoop!

"But how will it stand up?" Milo wondered aloud.

Milo looked around the room again. He saw cardboard tubes from paper towels. His tail swished with excitement.

"I can tape the hoop to this tube!" he exclaimed. "Then I can hold it up!"

Bella helped him wrap colorful tape around the tube. The red, blue, and yellow stripes looked beautiful.

"Now I need a ball," Milo said.

He crumpled up some paper and wrapped it in tape. The ball felt just right in his small hands.

"Let's test it!" Bella said, bouncing on her feet.

Milo held up his homemade basketball hoop. He aimed carefully and threw the paper ball. Swish! It went right through!

"I did it!" Milo cheered, jumping up and down. His fur bounced with each happy hop.

Other animals gathered around to watch. Soon everyone was taking turns shooting baskets. They laughed and cheered for each other.

"Great job, Milo!" said Carlos the cat. "You figured out how to solve your problem!"

"And you shared your game with all of us," added Bella. "That was very kind."

Milo felt proud and happy. His chest puffed out with joy. "I learned that when something seems hard, I can think of different ways to solve it. And asking friends for help makes everything better!"

At sports day, Milo's basketball game was the most popular activity. All the animals lined up to play. They practiced aiming, taking turns, and cheering for each other.

Milo discovered that sports were even more fun when shared with friends. His resourcefulness had created something wonderful for everyone to enjoy.

The End.
