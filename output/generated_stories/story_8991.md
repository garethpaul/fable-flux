# Benny Bear's Big Sorry

Benny the bear cub loved his cozy treehouse. It had soft cushions, colorful books, and a special box for his favorite honey cookies. The morning sun streamed through the round window, making everything glow golden and warm.

"What a beautiful day!" Benny said, stretching his fuzzy arms. He looked around his treehouse and frowned. Toys were scattered everywhere. Books lay open on the floor. His honey cookies were spilled across the wooden table.

Just then, he heard gentle knocking on his tree trunk. "Knock, knock, knock!"

"Come up!" called Benny.

His friend Rosie Rabbit climbed up the rope ladder, her pink nose twitching. Behind her came Oliver Owl, flapping his soft brown wings.

"Oh my!" gasped Rosie, looking at the messy treehouse. "What happened here?"

Benny's furry cheeks turned red. Yesterday, he had promised to organize his treehouse before his friends visited. He was supposed to help them plan a healthy snack party for all the forest animals.

"I... I forgot to clean up," Benny mumbled, looking at his paws. "I was playing and forgot my promise."

Oliver's big yellow eyes looked sad. "We brought carrots and apples for our healthy snack party. But there's no clean space to prepare them."

Rosie's ears drooped. "We told all our friends to come at noon. That's in just two hours!"

Benny felt his tummy twist like a pretzel. He had let down his best friends. The sweet smell of honey cookies mixed with his worried feelings.

"I'm really, really sorry," Benny said, his voice small and quiet. "I made a promise and I broke it. That wasn't kind to you."

He looked at his friends' disappointed faces. "I know saying sorry isn't enough. Can we work together to fix this?"

Rosie's ears perked up a little. "What do you mean?"

"Well," Benny said, thinking hard, "maybe we can compromise. I'll clean up super fast, and you can help me organize. Then we'll all prepare the healthy snacks together!"

Oliver hooted softly. "That sounds fair. We all want the party to be special."

"Yes!" said Benny, already picking up his toys. "I'll put my books on the shelf. Rosie, could you help me wipe the table? Oliver, maybe you could arrange the cushions in a circle?"

The three friends worked together like busy bees. Benny carefully placed each toy in its special spot. The wooden blocks went in the blue box. The soft animals went on the low shelf. Soon, the treehouse smelled fresh and looked sparkly clean.

"Now for the healthy snacks!" said Rosie, pulling out bright orange carrots and shiny red apples.

They washed the fruits and vegetables in Benny's little sink. The water splashed and sparkled. They cut the apples into star shapes and arranged the carrots like tiny logs.

"This looks beautiful!" said Oliver, admiring their colorful display.

When their forest friends arrived – Squeaky Squirrel, Danny Deer, and Bella Butterfly – everyone gasped with delight.

"What a wonderful, organized party!" said Squeaky.

Benny smiled proudly, but then he stood up tall. "Before we eat, I want to say something. I made a mistake and didn't keep my promise at first. But my friends helped me fix it. Saying sorry and working together made everything better."

All the animals munched on the crunchy, healthy snacks. The treehouse filled with happy chatter and the sweet sound of friendship.

"Thank you for forgiving me," Benny whispered to Rosie and Oliver.

"That's what friends do," they said together, giving him the biggest, warmest hug.

The End.
