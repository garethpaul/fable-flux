# Maple Tea in the Treehouse

A kind mouse named Mila lived in a cozy treehouse high in a maple tree. Her little home smelled like warm bread and sweet leaves. Sunlight made tiny gold dots on the wooden floor. Mila loved to share. She loved to think. She loved to help.

One bright morning, Mila squeaked, “Today is Sharing Day! I will make maple leaf tea and nut bread. Friends will come. We will share stories and traditions.”

Mila whisked around the kitchen. The kettle sang, “Hiss-hiss.” The bread went, “Crunch, crunch,” when she tapped it. She set tiny cups on a leaf table. She hummed a happy tune.

Knock, knock!

Squirrel Sami popped in first, tail fluffy and eyes wide. “Happy Sharing Day!” she chattered. “In my family, we shake the tree for luck before we eat.”

Knock, knock!

Next came Owl Oli, soft and quiet. “Happy Sharing Day,” he hooted. “In my family, we sit very still and listen for the wind before we eat.”

Knock, knock!

Then Tortoise Tia came slow and smiling. “Happy Sharing Day,” she whispered. “In my family, we tap our shells three times to say thank you before we eat.”

Mila clapped her tiny paws. “Wonderful! Let us do all the traditions!”

The kettle hissed. The cups steamed. The nut bread smelled toasty and sweet.

Sami bounced. “Let’s shake the tree now!”

Oli lifted a wing. “But first, we must sit very still.”

Tia tapped her shell. “Tap-tap-tap,” she said softly. “Thank you.”

Sami frowned. “If we sit still, we can’t shake the tree.”

Oli blinked. “If we shake the tree, we can’t listen to the wind.”

Tia looked down. “If we rush, my taps will be lost.”

Mila felt a twist in her tummy. The room grew quiet. She wanted everyone to be happy. She wanted to be kind. She took a slow breath. She used her thinking brain.

Mila said, “We have a problem. We have different ways to start. Let’s use careful thinking. Let’s make a plan.”

She put three little buttons on the table: one fluffy like a tail, one soft like a feather, one smooth like a shell. “These buttons will help us think.”

“First,” said Mila, “let’s learn. Sami, why do you shake the tree?”

Sami’s whiskers twitched. “We shake to share our joy with the tree. It is our way to say, ‘We are here!’ It makes me feel brave.”

Mila nodded. “Thank you. Oli, why do you sit and listen?”

Oli ruffled his soft feathers. “When we listen, we hear the wind say, ‘Welcome.’ It makes me feel calm.”

Mila smiled. “Thank you. Tia, why do you tap your shell?”

Tia smiled back. “We tap to say, ‘Thank you for this food.’ It makes me feel grateful.”

Mila tapped her chin. “Brave. Calm. Grateful,” she said. “All are good. We can try a new way. We can make space for all three.”

Sami wiggled. “How?”

Mila pointed to the window. The leaves whispered, “Shhh.” The bread smelled, “Mmm.” The tea cups glowed with steam. “We can do them in a gentle order,” said Mila. “First, we sit very still. We listen for the wind. It will be quiet and calm. Next, we tap three times together: tap, tap, tap. Last, we shake the small branch, not the big trunk. A small shake is still a shake. We will be careful with the tea.”

Oli blinked. “Sitting first keeps the tea safe.”

Tia nodded. “Tapping next helps us remember thanks.”

Sami’s tail swished. “A small shake is fun and careful! I can do that!”

Mila said, “This plan is a compromise. We all give a little, and we all get a little. Does that feel fair?”

Sami, Oli, and Tia smiled. “Yes!”

They tried it. They sat very still. The room was quiet. The kettle ticked. The wind whispered through the leaves, “Welcome.” Oli smiled.

They tapped their cups gently, “Tap, tap, tap.” Tia beamed. “Thank you.”

Then they went to the little side branch. Sami gave a tiny shake-shake. The leaves giggled. No tea spilled. Sami grinned. “Brave!”

“Now,” said Mila, “time to eat!”

They sipped warm maple leaf tea. Sweet steam tickled their noses. They nibbled nut bread. Crunchy crumbs made happy sounds.

As they ate, they shared more traditions. Sami told of winter dances. Oli told of moon songs. Tia told of sunny naps. Mila told of ribbon lights in the treehouse windows.

Later, the sun painted orange stripes on the floor. “Today, we had a problem,” said Mila. “We used our thinking brains. We asked questions. We listened. We made a plan. We tried it. We kept trying until it worked.”

Oli hooted softly, “That is critical thinking.”

Sami clapped her paws. “And we were brave when it was hard.”

Tia nodded, “And we were kind and patient.”

Mila’s heart felt warm. “When we think carefully, we find new ways. When we keep trying, we are strong. When we share and compromise, everyone belongs.”

They all hugged. The treehouse felt safe and bright. The wind hummed a happy song.

“Happy Sharing Day,” they said together.

The End.
