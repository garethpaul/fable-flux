# Robo's Wellness Workshop

In a cozy basement lab filled with colorful buttons and blinking lights, lived a helpful robot dog named Robo. His shiny silver coat sparkled under the warm glow of the lab's lights, and his tail wagged with a gentle whirring sound whenever he was happy.

One morning, Robo noticed something troubling. His friend Dr. Maya, who worked in the lab, looked very tired. Her shoulders drooped, and she rubbed her eyes often.

"Good morning, Dr. Maya!" Robo barked cheerfully, his voice sounding like gentle chimes. "You look sleepy today."

Dr. Maya sighed. "Oh, Robo. I've been working so late every night. I forget to eat dinner, I don't get enough sleep, and I haven't taken any breaks. I feel yucky."

Robo's LED eyes blinked thoughtfully. His computer brain whirred as he processed this information. "That doesn't sound healthy, Dr. Maya. May I help you feel better?"

"I don't know how, Robo. I have so much work to do," Dr. Maya said, slumping in her chair.

Robo's tail wagged excitedly. "I have an idea! Let's make a wellness craft together. It will help you remember to take care of yourself!"

Dr. Maya looked curious. "What kind of craft?"

Robo rolled over to the craft corner of the lab, where colorful paper, markers, and stickers were stored. "We'll make a special wellness wheel! It will remind you to do healthy things every day."

"That sounds wonderful," Dr. Maya smiled for the first time that morning.

Together, they cut out a big circle from bright yellow paper. It felt smooth and crisp under their fingers. Robo helped Dr. Maya divide the circle into sections, like pieces of a pie.

"Now," Robo explained, "let's draw pictures of healthy activities in each section. What makes you feel good?"

Dr. Maya thought carefully. "Eating yummy food makes me feel strong."

"Perfect!" Robo barked. They drew a colorful apple in the first section.

"Sleeping makes me feel rested," Dr. Maya added. They drew a cozy bed with fluffy pillows.

"Exercise makes your body happy," Robo suggested, and they drew a person stretching with arms reaching high.

They continued adding sections: drinking water (a blue water bottle), taking breaks (a comfortable chair), playing (colorful toys), and spending time with friends (two smiling faces).

As they worked, Dr. Maya felt more energetic. The bright colors and fun activity made her smile. The scissors made satisfying snip-snip sounds, and the markers smelled like sweet fruit.

"Now for the special part," Robo said. He helped Dr. Maya attach a spinning arrow to the center with a brass fastener. "Every morning, you can spin the wheel and pick a wellness activity to focus on that day!"

Dr. Maya spun the wheel. It made a soft whirring sound as it turned. The arrow landed on the water bottle.

"I should drink more water today!" she laughed. "Robo, this is perfect. But what if I forget to use it?"

Robo's eyes lit up. "You can ask for help! Tell your friends about your wellness wheel. Ask them to remind you to take care of yourself. It's okay to speak up for what you need."

Dr. Maya hugged Robo gently. "You're right! I'll put this wheel right on my desk where I can see it every day. And I'll tell my lab partner about it too."

From that day forward, Dr. Maya used her wellness wheel every morning. She felt stronger, happier, and more energetic. And Robo felt proud knowing he had helped his friend learn to take better care of herself.

"Taking care of yourself is the most important job of all," Robo would say, his tail wagging with joy.

The End.
