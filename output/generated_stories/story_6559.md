# Dolly the Dolphin's Fair Music Day

Dolly the dolphin loved music more than anything! Every morning, she swam to the special underwater music room where all the sea animals came to practice their songs.

The music room sparkled with colorful coral walls and floating instruments. There were shell drums, seaweed harps, and bubble flutes that made the most beautiful sounds. "Good morning, music room!" Dolly chirped happily, clapping her flippers together.

Today, Dolly brought her favorite snack - crunchy kelp crackers and sweet sea berries. She placed them carefully in her special lunch box. "These healthy foods give me energy to sing my best!" she said to herself.

Soon, her friends arrived. There was Ollie the octopus with his eight arms ready to play drums, Sally the seahorse with her tiny triangle, and Benny the blowfish who loved to sing.

But when practice time began, something wasn't fair at all.

"I want to use the big shell drum first!" demanded Ollie, pushing Sally away with his tentacles.

"Hey!" squeaked Sally. "I was here first! That's not nice!"

Dolly watched as Ollie took up all the space near the instruments. He spread his eight arms wide, blocking everyone else. "I'm the biggest, so I get to choose first!" he announced.

Benny floated sadly in the corner. "I guess I can't practice today," he sighed.

Dolly's heart felt heavy. This wasn't right! Everyone deserved a turn to make music. She took a deep breath and swam over to Ollie.

"Ollie, stop please," Dolly said in a calm but firm voice. "What you're doing isn't fair to our friends."

"But I want to play NOW!" Ollie grumbled, his tentacles still spread wide.

"I understand you're excited," Dolly said kindly. "But when we take turns, everyone gets to have fun. That's what being fair means."

Dolly turned to address everyone. "Let's make a practice schedule! We can each have ten minutes with our favorite instrument. That way, everyone gets a fair turn."

Sally's eyes lit up. "That's a wonderful idea, Dolly!"

"And while we wait for our turns," Dolly continued, "we can eat our healthy snacks and cheer for each other!"

Ollie looked around at his friends' sad faces. His heart began to feel sorry. "I... I didn't mean to be unfair," he said quietly. "I just got so excited about the drums."

"It's okay to feel excited!" Dolly smiled. "But we need to make sure everyone feels included and safe in our music room."

Dolly pulled out her kelp crackers. "Would you like to share my snack while we wait? These give me energy, and sharing makes them taste even better!"

Soon, all the friends were munching on healthy sea snacks and taking fair turns with the instruments. Ollie discovered that listening to his friends play was just as fun as playing himself!

Sally played a beautiful melody on her triangle. Benny sang the most amazing song about friendship. And when it was Ollie's turn, he played the drums while everyone sang together.

"This is the best music practice ever!" laughed Benny.

Dolly felt warm and happy inside. "When we're fair to each other and respect everyone's space, we all have more fun!"

As the music filled the sparkling room, Dolly learned that standing up for what's right and making sure everyone is treated fairly makes the whole world a more beautiful place.

The End.
