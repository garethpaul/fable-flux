# Koko's Helper Day

The morning sun peeked through the guest room curtains. Koko the koala stretched his fluffy gray arms and yawned. "What a lovely day," he said softly.

Mama Koala knocked on the door. "Good morning, Koko! Today is cleaning day. Can you help me make the guest room nice and tidy?"

Koko's ears drooped a little. Cleaning wasn't his favorite thing. He liked to read books and take naps. "Do I have to, Mama?" he asked quietly.

"Yes, sweetie. When we all help with chores, our home stays beautiful. Plus, Grandma Koala is visiting tomorrow!"

Mama handed Koko a small dust cloth. It felt soft in his paws. "You can dust the nightstand while I change the sheets."

Koko looked at the dusty nightstand. Tiny specks danced in the sunlight. "I don't know how," he whispered.

"Watch me first," Mama said kindly. She showed Koko how to wipe gently in circles. "See? Just like drawing rainbows with the cloth."

Koko tried it. The cloth glided smoothly across the wood. The dust disappeared like magic! "Look, Mama! It's shiny now!"

"Wonderful job!" Mama smiled. "Now, let's organize these books on the shelf."

But Koko stopped listening. He saw his favorite book on the floor. He sat down and started reading instead of helping.

"Koko," Mama said gently. "The books need to go on the shelf, not in your lap."

"But this story is so good!" Koko said, turning a page.

Mama's voice grew a little sad. "When we don't listen and help each other, work takes much longer. I need your help to finish before lunch."

Koko looked up at Mama's tired face. His tummy felt funny - not hungry funny, but sorry funny. "I'm sorry, Mama. I should have listened."

He put down his book and picked up three more books from the floor. They smelled like vanilla and felt smooth. He placed them carefully on the shelf, organizing them by size just like Mama showed him.

"Red book, blue book, green book," he counted happily.

Together, they fluffed the pillows. Koko pressed his nose into one - it smelled fresh like flowers. They folded the blanket, and Koko helped hold one corner. The blanket felt warm and cozy.

"Now for the best part," Mama said. "We earned a healthy snack!"

In the kitchen, Mama sliced crisp apples and spread creamy peanut butter on them. The apples crunched between Koko's teeth. The peanut butter tasted sweet and nutty.

"Mmm! This tastes even better because we worked hard together," Koko said, licking his lips.

"That's right," Mama agreed. "When we do our chores first, fun things feel extra special."

After their snack, they went back to admire the guest room. Everything sparkled. The books stood in neat rows. The bed looked fluffy and inviting.

"Grandma Koala will love this!" Koko said proudly.

"She will, thanks to your help," Mama said, giving him a warm hug.

That evening, Koko made a plan. Tomorrow he would listen right away and help with chores before playing. He learned that helping made Mama happy, made their home beautiful, and made him feel proud inside.

"Good night, clean room," Koko whispered as he drifted off to sleep, dreaming of all the ways he could help tomorrow.

The End.
