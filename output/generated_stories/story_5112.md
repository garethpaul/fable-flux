# Ollie Otter’s Bedtime Book

Ollie was a playful otter who loved to wiggle and giggle. He lived in a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls, a fluffy sea-green rug, and a little desk by the window. His bed smelled like fresh soap and sunshine. His favorite thing was his shiny purple notebook and a big, friendly pencil with a pink eraser cap.

“Tonight,” said Ollie, patting his notebook, “I will write my very own bedtime book!”

He climbed onto his chair and opened the notebook. The paper felt smooth under his paws. He sniffed the pencil. It smelled like wood and a tiny bit like sweet bubblegum. Ollie grinned. “I’m ready!”

But Ollie’s paws were sticky from his bedtime snack—honeyed apples. He didn’t notice. He pressed his paw on the page. Squish! The page stuck to his paw and made a goopy sound.

“Oh no,” said Ollie. “My page is sticky!”

He tried to write a big O for “Ollie.” The pencil slid across the sticky page and made a messy zigzag. The eraser smudged the zigzag into a gray blur. Ollie’s whiskers drooped. His tummy felt twisty.

From the hallway, Mama Otter called softly, “Ollie, do you need help, sweet pea?”

Ollie sniffled. “Mama, my writing is all wiggly and sticky. I want to write my story. But I can’t!”

Mama padded in on quiet feet, her fur smelling like lavender soap. She looked at the page. She looked at Ollie’s paws. She smiled kindly. “I see a sticky problem. What can we do to fix sticky paws?”

Ollie blinked. He tapped his chin with the eraser. Tap, tap. “Wash them?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Clean paws help clean writing.”

They went to the bathroom. Ollie turned on the warm water. It whooshed and whispered. Mama squeezed a little soap into his paws. Squish. The bubbles smelled bright and happy, like lemons. Ollie rubbed—front, back, between his fingers. Swish, swish, swish. He sang, “Bubbles, bubbles, wash, wash, wash!” The bubbles tickled. He giggled.

They rinsed. Drip, drip. Mama handed him a soft towel. Pat, pat, dry. “Feel your paws now,” she said.

Ollie wiggled his clean paws. “They feel smooth! Like a pebble by the river.”

Back in the cozy bedroom, the lamplight glowed like a little moon. Ollie took a fresh page. He leaned close and took a deep breath. The paper smelled like paper, not honey. He smiled. “I can write now.”

He pressed his pencil to the page. “O,” he said, making a big round circle. “L,” he said, making a tall line and a little foot. “L—I—E.” His letters were not perfect, but they were strong and clear. Scratch, scratch, went the pencil. Ollie’s heart felt warm.

“Listen, Mama,” said Ollie. He read slowly, “Ollie Otter’s Bedtime Book.” He beamed.

“What comes next?” asked Mama, sitting on the rug.

Ollie tapped the pencil again. “First, I had a sticky problem,” he said. “Then I found a peaceful way to fix it.”

“That sounds like a story,” Mama said.

Ollie wrote, “Once, an otter wanted to write. His paws were sticky. He washed with soap and warm water. Swish, swish. Then he wrote again. He felt proud.”

He drew a little picture of bubbles with smiling faces. He added tiny stars. He whispered, “I discovered something.”

“What did you discover?” Mama asked.

“I discovered that clean paws help me write. And when I have a problem, I can stop, think, and choose a calm, kind way,” Ollie said.

He kept writing. He wrote about how the desk felt smooth, how the room felt cozy, and how the lamp was like a moon. He wrote a little line that said, “I can write my feelings.” He drew a happy heart.

Suddenly, Ollie’s pencil rolled off the desk. Clink! It landed under the bed. Ollie’s eyes widened. “Problem again!”

He peeked under the bed. It was dark and a tiny bit dusty. He felt a tickle in his nose. “A-choo!” he sneezed.

Mama smiled. “Another small problem. How can we solve this peacefully?”

Ollie thought. He got his little flashlight from the drawer. Click. A soft circle of light shone under the bed. “Hello, pencil,” he said. He reached with a clean paw and pulled it out. He wiped the eraser with a tissue. “All clean,” he said proudly.

Back at the desk, Ollie wrote the last line: “I am gentle. I am clean. I am a writer.”

Mama hugged him. Her fur was warm and soft. “You are a writer,” she said. “And you solved your problems with clean hands and calm ideas.”

Ollie smiled a sleepy smile. He read his bedtime book out loud, slowly and clearly. The words sounded like soft waves. His eyes felt heavy. He put his pencil and notebook neatly on the desk. He washed his paws one more time. Swish, swish. Pat, pat.

He snuggled into bed. The covers smelled fresh. The room was quiet. “Good night, Mama,” he whispered.

“Good night, my writer,” Mama whispered back.

Ollie closed his eyes. He felt proud, clean, and calm. He knew that tomorrow he could write more. When problems came, he could stop, think, wash, and find a peaceful way.

The End.
