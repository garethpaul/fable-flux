# Rolo Raccoon’s Thank-You Song

In a cozy house on Maple Street, there was a small closet hideaway under the stairs. It smelled like clean cotton and warm wood. Soft scarves hung down like curtains. Inside, a little lamp made a golden glow. This was Rolo Raccoon’s special place.

Rolo was a musical raccoon. He wore a tiny blue cap and loved to make music. He had a toy drum, a small bell, and a shiny harmonica. He kept them neat on a low shelf in the closet hideaway.

“Good morning, drums! Good morning, bell! Good morning, shiny harmonica!” Rolo chirped. He tapped a gentle beat. Tap-tap. Ding-ding. Hoo-woo, sang the harmonica.

Today was Sweep-the-Block Day. The neighbors would clean the sidewalk and pick up leaves. Rolo wanted to help. He also wanted to play music for everyone while they worked.

Rolo peeked out of the closet hideaway. He saw a small red broom leaning by the door. “I will sweep, and I will sing,” he said. “I can do both!”

His friend, Dot the ladybug, buzzed by the lamp and landed on Rolo’s cap. “Hi, Rolo! Let’s help the block!” she said, rubbing her tiny hands together.

Rolo smiled. “Yes! We can help. Helping is kind. Helping keeps our street safe and clean.”

They started to pack. Rolo put the bell in his pocket. He put the harmonica in a soft sock so it would not get scratched. He hugged his drum. It felt smooth and round. He felt happy and ready.

Then Mama’s voice drifted in, warm like cocoa. “Rolo, remember your jobs, please. Make your bed, put your toys away, and feed the flowers a cup of water.”

Rolo looked at his bed roll in the corner of the closet. It was messy. His scarf pile was tippy. The flowers in a little pot looked thirsty. He wiggled. “But I want to go now,” he said softly.

Dot tapped his ear. “We can do small jobs first. Then we go,” she said.

Rolo puffed his cheeks and thought. He remembered how the closet hideaway kept his instruments safe and dry. He remembered how the lamp gave him warm light. He remembered how Mama always helped him tie his cap.

Rolo took a deep breath. “I am thankful for my bed. I am thankful for my toys. I am thankful for my little flowers. I will take care of them,” he said.

He shook his broom tail and made his bed smooth. Swish-swish. He lined up his scarves. Plop-plop. He gave the flowers a tiny drink. Drip-drip. The flowers perked up and smelled sweet, like fresh rain. Rolo felt proud. His heart felt like a drum—thump, thump—with joy.

“Good job,” said Dot. “Task complete!”

Rolo smiled wide. “Thank you, Dot.”

They went outside. The air was bright and crisp. The sun warmed Rolo’s whiskers. Neighbors waved. Mr. Owl had a rake. Ms. Squirrel had a bin. Kids had small buckets.

“Hi, friends!” Rolo called. “We are here to help!”

Rolo held the red broom. He swept leaves into a friendly pile. Swish-swish, swish-swish. Dot zipped around and pointed to tiny bits of paper. “There’s one! And there! Thank you for picking it up,” she said to each helper.

Rolo started a song with his bell and harmonica.

“Thank you, street,
for our feet.
Thank you, sky,
way up high.
Thank you, friends,
side by side.
We work with love
and happy pride!”

The neighbors smiled and sang along. The children clapped. The sound was bright and bouncy. Rolo’s paws tapped the drum. Boom-boom. The broom made a soft sh-sh sound on the ground. The air smelled like leaves and soap bubbles from a bucket of suds.

A little child dropped a cup. “Oops!” the child said.

Rolo scooped it up and smiled. “It’s okay. We all help,” he said. “We take care of our block because we live here together.”

Mr. Owl nodded. “That is civic responsibility,” he hooted gently. “We care for our place.”

Rolo beamed. “We say thank you, and we do our jobs.”

After a while, the sidewalk was clean. The leaf pile looked like a puffy orange pillow. Ms. Squirrel brought water cups. “Thank you for helping,” she said.

Rolo took a sip. It was cool and crisp. “Thank you,” he said. “The water tastes so good.”

Dot buzzed. “Rolo, we did it! We finished the tasks.”

Rolo tapped his bell once more. Ding! “I am grateful,” he said. “Thank you, closet hideaway, for keeping my things safe. Thank you, Mama, for reminding me. Thank you, friends, for helping. Thank you, street, for a nice place to live.”

Mama hugged Rolo. “I am proud of you,” she said. “You did your jobs first. You helped your neighbors. You used your music to make work fun.”

Rolo felt warm and light. He climbed back into his closet hideaway. He put away his drum, his bell, and his harmonica, each in its spot. He folded his cap and smiled.

He sang softly, “Thank you, thank you, every day. I help, I care, I put away.”

Rolo curled up by the lamp. Dot tucked under his cap. The closet smelled like clean cotton and happy work. Rolo closed his eyes, proud and calm.

He had learned that saying thank you feels good. Doing your tasks helps everyone. Helping your home and your street is a kind, brave thing.

The End.
