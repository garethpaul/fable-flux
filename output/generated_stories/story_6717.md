# Daisy's Big Feelings Workshop

Little Daisy the deer fawn loved exploring. Her soft brown spots sparkled in the sunlight as she wandered into Grandpa Bear's garage workshop. The smell of wood shavings tickled her nose, and colorful paint jars lined the shelves like a rainbow.

"Oh wow!" Daisy gasped, her big brown eyes growing wide. "So many pretty things!"

She wanted to touch everything at once. Her hooves danced with excitement as she spotted brushes, crayons, and shiny tools. But then something went wrong.

*CRASH!* 

Daisy accidentally knocked over a jar of blue paint with her tail. The paint splashed everywhere - on the floor, on her spots, even on a half-finished birdhouse!

"Oh no, oh no!" Daisy cried. Her heart beat fast like a drum. She felt hot and shaky. "I ruined everything!"

Daisy wanted to run away, but her legs felt wobbly. She wanted to cry very loudly, but that might make things worse. She felt like a balloon ready to pop!

Just then, Grandpa Bear appeared in the doorway. His kind eyes twinkled behind his glasses.

"Hello there, little one," he said gently. "I see you're having some big feelings."

"I made a big mess!" Daisy sniffled. "I'm sorry, Grandpa Bear!"

Grandpa Bear sat down slowly beside her. "Big feelings are normal, Daisy. But let's take some deep breaths together. Can you smell the pine wood?"

Daisy breathed in deeply through her nose. "It smells like Christmas trees," she whispered.

"Good! Now let's count to five while we breathe out," Grandpa Bear said. "One... two... three... four... five."

Daisy felt her heart slow down. Her legs stopped shaking.

"Feeling better?" Grandpa Bear asked.

Daisy nodded. "A little bit. But I still made a mess."

"Let's think about this like a puzzle," Grandpa Bear said. "What could we do to fix it?"

Daisy looked around with her thinking face on. "We could... clean it up?"

"Great idea! What else?"

Daisy's eyes lit up. "The blue paint looks like water! Maybe we could make the birdhouse look like it's by a lake!"

"Now that's creative thinking!" Grandpa Bear clapped his paws together.

They worked together, wiping up the extra paint and turning the accident into art. Daisy carefully painted blue waves around the bottom of the birdhouse. She added white clouds with her hoofprint and yellow flowers with her spots dipped in paint.

"This is the most beautiful birdhouse ever!" Daisy said, stepping back to admire their work.

"You turned a problem into something wonderful," Grandpa Bear said proudly. "You used your breathing to calm down, your brain to think of solutions, and your imagination to create something special."

Daisy beamed. "When I felt those big feelings, I thought everything was ruined. But taking deep breaths helped me think better!"

"That's called self-regulation," Grandpa Bear explained. "It means taking care of your feelings so you can make good choices."

Daisy practiced her deep breathing one more time. "Next time I feel upset, I'll remember to breathe and think before I act."

As they hung the beautiful lake birdhouse outside, Daisy felt proud and peaceful. She had learned that big feelings were okay, but she could choose how to handle them.

"Thank you for teaching me, Grandpa Bear," she said, giving him a gentle nuzzle.

"Anytime, little artist," he replied with a warm smile.

The End.
