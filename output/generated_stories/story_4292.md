# Dippy the Dolphin Builds a Bubble Blower

Dippy the dolphin was playful and kind. He loved splashing in a big blue tub in Mia’s garage workshop. The workshop smelled like fresh wood and lemon soap. Sunlight made bright stripes on the floor. Tools hung on the wall like shiny fish. Clink, clink went the tiny screws. Buzz, buzz went the little fan.

“Good morning, Dippy!” said Mia, wearing green goggles. “Do you want to help me today?”

“Eee-eee!” squeaked Dippy, flipping his tail and spraying tiny sparkles of water. “I want to help! What are we making?”

“We are making a bubble blower,” Mia said. “Big bubbles! Rainbow bubbles!”

Dippy wiggled with joy. “Bubbles! I love bubbles!”

Mia showed Dippy a small box, a gentle fan, and a hoop. “We will put the hoop here,” she said. “The fan will puff. Then the bubbles will come!”

Dippy took a deep breath. He felt the cool air and heard the hum of the fan. He smelled the sweet soap in a blue bowl. He wanted to do a good job.

Mia handed Dippy a soft cloth. “First, we clean,” she said.

“Clean, clean,” sang Dippy. He wiped the table. Wipe, wipe. He wiped a little too fast. The cloth slipped. Plop! The hoop slid and tapped the floor.

“Oh!” Dippy’s eyes grew wide. “I made a bump.”

Mia smiled gently. “It’s okay. Let’s try again. Slow and steady.”

Dippy nodded. “I will try again.” He moved slowly. He breathed in and out. In. Out. His flippers felt calm. “Slow and steady,” he whispered.

They set the hoop back on the table. Mia showed Dippy how to turn the tiny screws. “Righty-tighty,” she said.

“Righty-tighty,” echoed Dippy. He turned a screw. Turn, turn. But then the screw rolled away. Tink, tink! It hid under a shelf.

“Oh no,” said Dippy. “I lost the screw.”

Mia knelt down. “We will look together.”

Dippy peeked under the shelf. It was dusty and dark. He listened. Drip, drip went the tub. He smelled the lemon soap and the metal tools. “I will not give up,” he said softly.

He kept looking. “Here!” he chirped. The screw sparkled in the shadow. Dippy used his flipper carefully and nudged it out. “Found it!”

“Great persistence,” Mia said. “You kept trying.”

Dippy felt warm and proud. “I kept trying,” he repeated, smiling.

They built the bubble blower. Dippy held the hoop. Mia clicked the fan. Whirr! The fan hummed. “Ready for bubbles?” asked Mia.

“Yes!” Dippy dipped the hoop in the soap. He lifted it up. The fan puffed. Puh-puh-puff. A tiny bubble wiggled… then popped.

“Pop,” said Dippy. His smile fell. “It did not work.”

Mia patted Dippy’s fin. “Sometimes we need a new plan. Let’s think.”

Dippy closed his eyes. He listened to his breath. In. Out. He felt the cool air on his nose. He thought of a strategy. “Maybe more soap,” he said. “And hold the hoop steady.”

“Good idea,” Mia said. “Let’s try your plan.”

Dippy dipped the hoop deeper. He lifted it slowly. His fins were still and strong. The fan puffed again. A big bubble grew. It shimmered with pink, green, and blue. “Ooooh,” whispered Dippy.

Then—pop! It burst again.

Dippy’s eyes watered. “I am trying. It keeps popping.”

From the corner, a small squeak sounded. It was Pip the mouse, wearing a tiny tool belt. “May I help?” Pip asked softly.

Dippy blinked. “Yes, Pip. Thank you.” He wanted to be fair and kind. “We can all help. We can take turns.”

Mia nodded. “Teamwork with tolerance. We listen to each other.”

Pip pointed to the fan. “Maybe the fan is too strong,” Pip squeaked. “Turn it down a little.”

Dippy nodded. “Good idea.” He turned the dial gently. “Click.” The fan hummed softer. “Let’s try again.”

Dippy dipped the hoop. Pip held the bowl. Mia counted, “One, two, three.”

The bubble grew. It grew bigger. It didn’t pop! It floated up, soft and shiny. It smelled faintly like clean soap. It made a quiet, happy sound: bloop!

“We did it!” cheered Dippy. He clapped his fins, splish-splash! More bubbles floated, slow and round. They drifted like jellyfish. Rainbow light danced on the garage walls.

Dippy laughed. “We kept trying. We were honest and kind. We listened. We used a plan. We breathed slow. We did not give up!”

Mia smiled. “That is persistence. That is integrity—being honest and careful. That is tolerance—taking turns and listening to others. And that is mindfulness—breathing and going slow.”

Pip squeaked, “And strategy—making a plan!”

They made a bubble parade. Big bubbles, small bubbles, heart-shaped bubbles! Dippy shared the hoop with Pip. Pip shared the dial with Dippy. Mia cheered and clapped.

At the end, Dippy cleaned the table again. He put the tools back where they belonged. “We did our best,” he said. He felt calm and happy.

Dippy looked at the last bubble floating up. “I learned to keep trying,” he said softly. “When I slow down, listen, and share, good things happen.”

Mia and Pip nodded. The bubble popped with a tiny, musical ping, like a bell saying, “Well done.”

The End.
