# Milo Mouse and the Honest Glow

Milo was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and tiny pink paws. He lived in a cozy corner of a basement lab. The lab was full of gentle hums, warm lights, and jars that glowed like stars. The air smelled a little like soap and a little like lemons.

Every morning, Milo squeaked, “Good day, lab!” and swept crumbs into a dustpan. He liked to help. He liked to listen, too. The lab had many friends. There was Dr. Kim, who wore bright socks. There was Dr. Amara, who laughed like bells. There was Mr. Luis, who hummed songs. And there was Taro the turtle and Zuzu the zebra finch, visiting for a school show about science and caring.

Today, the lab buzzed with excitement. “We are making the Honest Glow,” said Dr. Kim. “It is a safe, tiny light. It shines when we tell the truth.” Dr. Amara winked. “It can help us practice honesty, even when it is hard.”

Milo’s whiskers twitched. “I want to see it,” he whispered.

On a table sat a small round button inside a clear jar. It pulsed softly, pink and blue. A sign said: “Please Do Not Touch.”

Milo swept the floor. He hummed with Mr. Luis. He helped Taro find a sunny spot. He offered Zuzu a little seed. The lab was busy and happy.

Then Milo smelled something yummy—fresh banana bread! He squeaked and scurried toward the snack table. His tail brushed the table leg. Clink! The clear jar wobbled. The Honest Glow button slid—plop!—onto the floor.

“Oh no,” Milo whispered. He picked up the button with both paws. It felt warm. His heart beat fast. Thump-thump. He set it back on the table, but it was not in the jar now. He looked around. No one had seen.

Milo felt hot in his cheeks. He wanted to hide. “Maybe it is okay,” he said to himself. “Maybe no one will know.” He reached for the jar to put the button back, but his paw slipped. Tap! Tap! The button rolled under a shelf.

Milo’s tummy felt twisty. “I made a mistake,” he said. “I am scared.”

Zuzu fluttered down and chirped, “Are you alright, little friend?”

Milo squeezed his paws. “I… I bumped the table,” he said in a tiny voice. “The Honest Glow rolled away.”

Zuzu tilted her head. “You can tell the truth. That is brave.”

Milo took a deep breath. In, out. In, out. “I can do it,” he said. He walked to Dr. Kim and Dr. Amara, who were looking at colorful charts with Mr. Luis.

“I have to say something,” Milo squeaked. “I touched the table by accident. The Honest Glow rolled under a shelf. I am sorry.”

The room went quiet. Milo’s ears drooped. Dr. Kim knelt down, smiling gently. “Thank you for telling us the truth, Milo. That was honest.”

Dr. Amara nodded. “Everyone makes mistakes. Honesty helps us fix them.”

Mr. Luis looked under the shelf and said, “We can work together.” He handed Milo a small flashlight. “Want to volunteer and help find it?”

Milo felt a little braver. “Yes,” he said. He clicked the light. It made a soft whirr. He crawled with Taro and Zuzu by his side. “I see it!” Milo called. The button was next to a fluffy dust bunny.

Milo reached in. His paw got stuck. “Oh no,” he squeaked, feeling a spark of anger. “Why won’t it move?” His tail twitched. His face felt hot again.

Taro spoke slowly. “Breathe with me. In, out. In, out.”

Milo breathed. In, out. His body calmed. “Thank you,” he said. He wiggled his paw gently and freed it. He picked up the button and held it high. “Got it!”

Everyone cheered. Zuzu sang a happy trill. Dr. Amara clapped. “Wonderful volunteering, team!”

Dr. Kim put the button back in the jar. Then she pressed it. The Honest Glow shone a bright, warm gold. The light made the room look cozy and safe.

“It shines brighter when someone is brave and honest,” Dr. Kim said. “Milo told the truth. Milo helped fix the problem. Milo managed big feelings.”

Milo’s heart felt light. “I was scared,” he said. “I felt mad when my paw got stuck. I breathed. I told the truth. I helped.”

Mr. Luis smiled. “We all come from different places,” he said. “We use different words and songs. But honesty is the same in every language.” He hummed a soft tune. Dr. Kim said “gomawo.” Dr. Amara said “asante.” Mr. Luis said “gracias.” Milo squeaked, “thank you.”

They all shared banana bread. It was warm and sweet. Milo nibbled and licked a crumb from his whisker. “Mmm,” he said. “Helping tastes good!”

Before they left, Milo swept the floor again. He also made a tiny sign with a crayon: “Please Ask for Help.” He taped it near the table.

Dr. Amara read the sign. “That is kind, Milo,” she said. “You are a helper.”

Milo stood tall. “I will try to be honest every day,” he said. “I can be calm and ask for help. I can volunteer to fix my mistakes.”

The Honest Glow pulsed softly, gold and warm, like a little sun in a jar. The lab hummed, and everyone felt safe and proud.

The End.
