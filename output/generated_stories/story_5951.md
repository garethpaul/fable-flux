# Leo's Big Rooftop Adventure

Leo was a small lion cub with a fluffy golden mane. He lived in a tall building with his mama and papa. Every day, Leo looked out the window at the big city below and dreamed of being brave like the grown-up lions.

One windy morning, Leo climbed the stairs all the way to the rooftop. The sun felt warm on his fur, and he could see everything from up high! Cars looked like tiny toys, and people looked like ants walking around.

"Wow!" said Leo, his eyes sparkling. "I'm so high up! I feel like the king of the jungle!"

But then Leo heard a tiny voice crying. "Help! Help me please!"

Leo looked around with his sharp lion eyes. Near the edge of the rooftop, he saw a little blue bird sitting in a broken nest. The bird's wing was hurt, and she couldn't fly.

"Oh no!" said the bird. "The wind blew my nest apart! My babies fell out, but I can't reach them. They're on the fire escape below!"

Leo's tummy felt funny. The fire escape looked very far down, and the metal steps looked slippery. What if he fell? What if he wasn't brave enough?

"I'm just a little cub," Leo whispered to himself. "I'm not big and strong like Papa."

But then Leo heard tiny peeping sounds from below. The baby birds needed help right now! Leo took a deep breath and puffed out his small chest.

"I may be little, but I have a big, brave heart!" Leo said loudly. "I can do this!"

Carefully, Leo climbed down the fire escape. Step by step, he moved slowly and safely. His paws gripped the metal bars tightly. The wind made his mane flutter, but he didn't give up.

"Don't worry, little birds!" Leo called. "I'm coming to help you!"

When Leo reached the baby birds, they were cold and scared. Gently, he picked them up one by one in his soft mouth and carried them back up to the rooftop. It took three trips, but Leo didn't stop until all the babies were safe.

"Oh, thank you!" chirped the mama bird. "You saved my family! You're so brave and kind!"

Leo's chest felt warm and happy. "I did it! I really did it!" he roared softly.

Just then, Papa Lion came onto the rooftop. "Leo! There you are! What happened here?"

Leo told Papa the whole story. Papa's eyes grew wide with pride.

"My little cub," said Papa, nuzzling Leo's head, "you showed real courage today. Being brave doesn't mean you're not scared. It means you help others even when you feel scared."

Leo smiled the biggest smile. "I felt scared, but I did it anyway! I helped the bird family!"

"That's right," said Papa. "You have everything you need inside you to be brave. Your kind heart makes you stronger than you know."

From that day on, Leo knew he was special just the way he was. He didn't need to be big to be brave. He just needed to believe in himself and care about others.

Every morning, Leo visited his bird friends on the rooftop. And every day, he felt proud of his big, brave heart.

The End.
