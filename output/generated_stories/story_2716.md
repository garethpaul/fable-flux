# Rainbow Pots on the Balcony

Benny was a determined rabbit with soft gray fur and big, curious ears. He lived with Grandma Roo in a cozy home with a sunny balcony garden. The garden was full of pots—red, blue, yellow, and green. Each pot held different plants, and each plant had its own shape, smell, and color.

One bright morning, Benny bounced onto the balcony. He wiggled his nose. He smelled wet soil and fresh mint. He heard bees buzz-buzzing near the lavender and tiny birds chirp-chirp-chirping in the lemon tree.

“Today,” Benny said, puffing up with pride, “I will grow the biggest carrot.” His whiskers twitched with excitement.

Grandma Roo smiled. “Growing takes time and care,” she said softly. “And help from friends.”

Benny thumped his foot. “I can do it! I am determined.”

He had a small orange pot and one carrot seed. He poked the soil with his paw and planted the seed. “Grow, little seed,” he whispered.

He gave the pot one tiny cup of water. Drip, drop. The soil turned dark and smelled like rain. Then Benny sat. He watched. He waited. He wiggled. He waited more.

Nothing happened.

Benny’s ears drooped. “Why won’t it grow?”

From the blue pot nearby, Minty the mint plant rustled her leaves. “Hello, Benny,” she whispered. “I like a little shade and a sip of water. Not too much.”

From the tall yellow pot, Sunny the sunflower stretched and smiled. “I love bright light! I reach, reach, reach for the sun.”

From the red pot, Basil the basil plant waved. “I like to feel warm and cozy. I don’t like cold wind.”

Benny blinked. “You all like different things?”

Minty giggled. “We are all plants, but we are not the same.”

Sunny nodded. “Different needs. Different ways.”

Benny looked at his carrot pot. “What do carrots like?” he asked.

A tiny voice squeaked from the soil. “I like cool soil and room to grow,” said the carrot seed. “Please be patient.”

Benny sat up tall. “I will be patient,” he said. “And I will listen.”

He hopped to get more soil. Scoot-scoot went the little shovel. The soil felt crumbly and soft in his paws. He made sure the pot was deep enough. “Room to grow,” he repeated.

He shaded the pot during the hottest part of the day with a little umbrella. Tap-tap went the umbrella as he set it in place. “Cool soil,” Benny said.

He gave the seed water when the soil felt dry. He touched it gently. “Not too much,” he whispered, remembering Minty’s words. Drip, drop. Just enough.

Days passed. Benny checked every morning. He sniffed the air, sweet with flowers and soil. He listened to the bees. He felt the sun’s warm hug and the breeze’s soft kiss. He kept trying, even when he felt wiggly and wanted to play. “I am determined,” he reminded himself.

One afternoon, a tiny green tip peeked up. “Hello!” squeaked the carrot sprout.

Benny’s eyes shined. “You’re here!”

He clapped his paws and hopped in a circle. “Thank you for waiting with me,” he told the sprout.

Sunny leaned toward the light. “Good job, Benny.”

Minty swayed. “You listened and learned.”

Basil nodded. “You respected our ways.”

Just then, Benny heard a soft cry. From the corner, a shy purple flower drooped in a small pot. “I am Viola,” she said. “I like gentle water on my feet, but the hose is too strong.”

Benny looked at the hose. It made a loud whoosh. He thought and thought. Then he smiled. “Teamwork!” he said.

He asked Sunny to block the strong wind with her tall leaves. He asked Minty to share her cool shade. He asked Basil to tell him when the air felt too cold. Then Benny dipped a tiny cup into the watering can and made a soft rain with his paw. Pitter-patter. Just right for Viola.

Viola lifted her petals. “Thank you,” she whispered. “I feel safe.”

Benny felt warm inside. “We all like different things,” he said. “But we can help each other.”

Days turned into a week. The carrot leaves grew feathery and green. They brushed Benny’s nose when he leaned close. They smelled sweet and earthy. Finally, Grandma Roo came out with a smile. “It is time,” she said.

Benny pulled gently. Tug, tug, tug. Pop! Out came a bright orange carrot, long and smooth. Benny’s ears twirled with joy. “We did it!”

Sunny clapped her leaves. Minty jingled. Basil bowed. Viola sparkled with tiny dew drops.

Grandma Roo sliced the carrot into little coins. Crunch! It was crisp and sweet. Benny shared with everyone. “One for Sunny, one for Minty, one for Basil, and one for Viola,” he said. “Thank you for being different. Thank you for helping me.”

Grandma Roo hugged Benny. “You were patient. You used teamwork. You respected others,” she said. “And you learned that diversity makes our garden strong.”

Benny nibbled a carrot coin and smiled. “Different friends make everything better,” he said. “I will always listen, learn, and work together.”

The balcony glowed golden in the sunset. The plants swayed softly. The air smelled like mint and sweet carrots. Benny felt proud, calm, and kind.

The End.
