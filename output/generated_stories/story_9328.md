# Benny Beaver’s Attic Club

In a quiet attic under a sloping roof, a busy beaver named Benny tapped and stacked with gentle care. The attic smelled like warm wood and clean cotton. Sunlight slid through a small round window, making dust sparkle like tiny stars.

“Tap, tap, tap,” Benny hummed as he lined up soft pillows. “Today I make a club! A cozy Club of Friends.” He rubbed his paws together. “We will read, stretch, and play.”

Benny loved to plan. He drew a simple list on a big cardboard sheet. “Step one: make comfy spots. Step two: choose a game. Step three: invite friends.” He smiled. “Everyone is welcome.”

Soon, little footsteps creaked on the attic stairs. “Knock, knock,” called Tilly the turtle in a calm, slow voice. “Is the club open?”

“Yes!” said Benny. He waved. “Come in, Tilly! I planned a reading corner.” He pointed to a pile of books and a soft beanbag. “You can take your time.”

Tilly blinked happily. “Slow is my way,” she said, moving to the beanbag. “Thank you for planning a comfy spot.”

“Knock, knock,” came a bouncy voice. Flick the firefly zipped inside, glowing golden and bright. “Hi, hi, hi! I like to move and shine!” Flick buzzed around the window, leaving little trails of light.

“Welcome, Flick!” Benny laughed. “I planned a dancing space.” He showed a square rug with tape lines. “You can dance and glow.”

Flick spun. “I love the lines! They help me zoom without bumping.”

“Knock, knock,” said a gentle whisper. Momo the mouse peeped in, ears twitching. “I like quiet,” Momo said softly. “Is there a quiet place?”

Benny nodded. “Yes,” he said kindly. “I planned a quiet corner with blankets and a soft bell. If it gets too loud, ring the bell, and we will take a quiet moment.”

Momo smiled and curled into the blankets. “Thank you for thinking of me.”

“Knock, knock,” thumped a cheerful thud. Poppy the panda climbed up, carrying a small drum. “I like rhythms,” Poppy said, beating a soft dum-dum-dum. “Do you like music?”

“I do!” said Benny. “I planned music time after story time.” He pointed to the cardboard list. “We will take turns.”

Everyone looked at the list. It said:

1. Welcome
2. Story
3. Stretch
4. Dance
5. Music
6. Kind snack and quiet time

Benny clapped his paws. “Welcome to the Club of Friends! In our club, we include everyone. We listen. We plan. We celebrate what makes us different.”

Flick zipped closer. “I am fast and bright!”

Tilly nodded slowly. “I am slow and steady.”

Momo whispered, “I like soft and quiet.”

Poppy patted the drum. “I like beats and hugs.”

Benny puffed with pride. “I am a busy beaver who loves to plan. All our ways are good.”

They began with the story. Benny read in a gentle voice, “Once upon a time, a little raft floated on a calm lake...” The attic felt warm. The pages smelled like fresh paper. Flick dimmed to a tiny glow so Momo felt calm. Tilly turned pages carefully. Poppy kept the drum still.

After the story, Benny said, “Stretch time!” He led slow stretches. “Reach up like a tree.” Tilly stretched slowly. Flick stretched fast then paused. Poppy stretched wide and gave a giggle. Momo made a small stretch and a happy sigh.

Then came dance time. Flick zipped along the tape lines, sparkling. Poppy swayed with soft dum-dum steps. Tilly took tiny careful steps, smiling. Momo tapped one paw and watched, feeling safe. Benny clapped a steady rhythm. “We can dance in many ways,” he said. “All ways are welcome.”

Suddenly, the attic felt crowded. Flick bumped a broom. It toppled—clatter! Momo startled and rang the little bell—ting!

Benny took a deep breath. “Pause,” he said kindly. “Let’s make a new plan so everyone feels good.” He looked at his friends. “What do you need?”

Momo whispered, “I need a little more quiet.”

Flick said, “I need a path to zoom.”

Poppy nodded, “I need a steady beat.”

Tilly smiled, “I need time.”

Benny tapped his chin. “I hear you.” He moved the broom and used tape to make a glow path for Flick. He added a soft rug near Momo’s blankets. “This will muffle sounds,” he said. He gave Poppy two soft drumsticks. “These make gentle beats.” He drew a clock with arrows for Tilly. “This shows when we change.”

Everyone tried again. The attic felt peaceful and bright. Flick zoomed along the path, shining softly near Momo’s corner. Poppy played dum-dum in a quiet way. Tilly watched the clock and nodded when the arrow moved. Momo breathed in, and out, and smiled.

Snack time came. Benny passed apple slices and tiny cups of water. The apples were crisp and sweet. “Crunch,” said Poppy. “Mmm,” said Tilly. “Sip,” said Momo. Flick’s glow flickered happily.

Benny looked around. “Friends,” he said, “our club works because we include everyone. We listen to what each friend needs. We plan together. We celebrate our differences.”

Flick buzzed, “Different is bright!”

Tilly added, “Different is calm.”

Momo whispered, “Different is kind.”

Poppy hugged the drum. “Different is fun!”

Benny beamed. “Different is our strength.”

They made a new sign for the attic wall. It read, in big friendly letters: “All Friends Welcome. We Plan. We Listen. We Care.”

The attic felt safe, soft, and happy. The dust stars twinkled in the golden light. Benny’s tail gave a busy wiggle.

“See you tomorrow,” Benny said. “We will plan again. We will include again. We will celebrate again.”

Everyone nodded with shining eyes.

The End.
