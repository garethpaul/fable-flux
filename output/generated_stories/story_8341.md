# Kiko the Calm Koala and the Busy Basement Lab

Kiko the calm koala lived in a cozy house with a bright, clean basement lab. The lab was full of soft humming sounds. Lights blinked gently. Buttons glowed like tiny stars. There were jars of shiny screws, smooth wires, and a big table that smelled like fresh wood. Kiko loved the lab. It felt safe and quiet.

One morning, Kiko had a big job. “I will make the Helping Fan,” Kiko said softly. “The fan will send cool air to friends far away who are too hot.” Kiko looked at a map on the wall. There were pictures of friends from around the world—Mina in a sunny desert, Jo in a warm island town, and Bao in a busy city. “I want to help them all,” Kiko whispered.

Kiko put on soft ear muffs, a tiny tool belt, and a sky-blue apron. “Step one,” Kiko said, reading a list. “Build the fan.” Kiko turned a knob. The fan spun and hummed. “Good,” Kiko smiled.

“Step two: Connect the pipes.” The pipes were long and silver, like sleepy snakes. Kiko tried to fit them together. Clink. Clank. Clunk. One pipe rolled away and bumped a cardboard box. The box slid. The pipes tumbled. “Oh dear,” Kiko said, feeling a little squeeze in the tummy. Kiko’s ears got warm.

Kiko took a breath. “Calm helps,” Kiko reminded. Kiko watched a tiny dust spark dance in the sunlight. Breathe in, one… two… three. Breathe out, one… two… three.

“Step three: Share the plan with friends.” Kiko tapped a tablet. A soft ping! Mina’s smiling face popped up. “Hi, Kiko,” Mina said. “It is very hot here.”

“I am making a Helping Fan,” Kiko said. “It will blow cool air. But the pipes are tricky.”

Mina nodded. “Sometimes I get frustrated too,” she said. “When I do, I drink cool water and count to five.” She held up fingers. “One, two, three, four, five.”

Kiko took a drink of cool water. Gulp. Ahh. “Thank you,” Kiko said.

Kiko tried the pipes again. Clink. Clank. Snap! One pipe fit. “Yes!” Kiko said. But the next one stuck. Kiko pulled. It would not move. Kiko’s face felt hot again. “I want to finish,” Kiko said, voice a little tight.

Jo called from the island town. “Hi, Kiko! The sun is bright here.” Waves swished behind Jo. “What’s wrong?”

“The pipe is stuck,” Kiko said. “I feel frustrated.”

Jo smiled. “When I feel that way, I wiggle my toes in the sand. I take a wiggle break.”

Kiko looked down. No sand here, only the smooth lab floor. “I can wiggle anyway,” Kiko said. Kiko wiggled toes. Wiggle-wiggle. Kiko shook arms, then shoulders. Shake-shake. “I feel softer now,” Kiko said, giggling a little.

Kiko tried a new way. “Twist, then push.” Twist-twist. Push. The pipe slid in with a nice click. “It worked!” Kiko cheered. “Thank you, Jo!”

Bao called from the city. “Hi, Kiko! There are many people here. We share the bus, we share the park.”

“I need to share my tools,” Kiko said, looking at the messy table. There were too many things at once. Kiko’s whiskers twitched. “I feel busy in my head.”

Bao nodded. “When it’s busy, I share my task with a friend. Can I help?”

“Yes,” Kiko said. “I can share the plan.” Kiko sent the list. “Could you read me the steps one by one?”

Bao read slowly. “Step four: Tighten the knobs.”

Kiko picked up a small, friendly screwdriver. It felt cool in the paw. Turn-turn. Tight, but not too tight. “Done.”

“Step five,” Bao read. “Test the fan.”

Kiko pressed the green button. The fan whirred. Wind kissed Kiko’s nose. “It works!” Kiko laughed. The air smelled clean and cool.

But then, the fan slowed. Whirr… whirr… stop. “Oh,” Kiko said, feeling a little drop in the heart. “It stopped.”

Mina said, “Remember the cool-water count.”

Jo said, “Remember the wiggle break.”

Bao said, “We can do it together.”

Kiko sat on a soft stool. Breathe in… one… two… three. Breathe out… one… two… three. Kiko took a sip of water. Wiggle toes. Shake arms. Kiko felt calm again. “Thank you, friends,” Kiko said. “We can solve this.”

Kiko looked carefully. A tiny wire was loose. Kiko’s ears perked. “Aha.” Kiko gently pressed the wire in until it clicked. “Now we try again.”

Kiko pressed the button. The fan spun strong. Whoosh! Cool air flowed through the silver pipes. On the tablet, Mina smiled and held up a scarf that fluttered. “I feel the breeze!” Jo’s palm leaves swayed. Bao’s paper kite danced in the city room.

“We did it,” Kiko said. “We shared ideas. We stayed calm. We kept going.”

Kiko put a sticker on the map for each friend. A sun for Mina, a wave for Jo, a star for Bao. “We are far apart,” Kiko said, “but we can help each other.”

Kiko cleaned the lab, sharing space with the tools. The screws went tink-tink into jars. The wires curled up like little naps. The hum in the basement was happy.

Kiko hugged the plush koala on the shelf. “When I feel frustrated, I can stop, breathe, and try again. I can ask for help and share. I can finish my task.”

The tablet pinged. “Thank you, Kiko!” said the friends together. Their voices sounded warm like cocoa.

Kiko smiled, calm and proud. “We did it as a team,” Kiko said. “Little steps, big help, all around the world.”

The End.
