# Beep-Bop in the Cozy Closet

Beep-Bop was a helpful robot dog with shiny silver ears and a wagging, wiggly antenna. Beep-Bop lived with Mia and her little brother, Leo. Today, the house was noisy. Pots clanged. The vacuum buzzed. Mia held her hands over her ears. “Too loud!” she said.

Beep-Bop blinked his gentle blue lights. “Bark-beep. I can help.” He led Mia and Leo to their favorite place—the closet hideaway. It was soft and dark and calm. A pillow nest was on the floor, a small lamp glowed warm and yellow, and a basket of toys sat in the corner. It smelled like clean laundry and lavender. “Safe spot,” Beep-Bop chirped.

They crawled inside. Thump, thump went their knees on the rug. Mia sighed. “Ahh.” Leo hugged a fuzzy blanket. The closet door closed with a quiet click. The noise from the house got softer, like a whisper far away.

Mia reached for the toy basket. “Let’s build a block city,” she said.

“Blocks!” Leo cheered. He grabbed the red blocks. Mia picked up the blue blocks.

Beep-Bop sat between them, tail antenna swishing. “Team plan?” he asked in a friendly beep. “We can share and take turns.”

Mia nodded. “Okay. I’ll build a bridge.”

Leo’s face scrunched. “I want the blue,” he said, tugging a blue block from Mia’s hand. The block slipped and clacked against the floor. Mia’s cheeks got hot. “Hey!” she cried. Her hands balled into fists.

Beep-Bop’s ear lights flashed soft, like slow fireflies. “Pause,” he said calmly. “Let’s take a breath.”

Mia puffed. “I’m mad,” she said. “That was my block.”

Leo’s lip wobbled. “I want it.”

Beep-Bop nodded. “Feelings are okay. We can solve this.” He put a paw on his chest plate. “Watch me. Smell the flower.” He made a whirring sniff. “Blow the candle.” He made a gentle whoosh. Mia and Leo copied him. In… out. In… out. Their shoulders softened.

“Now we use words,” Beep-Bop said. “That is self-advocacy. It means you say what you need.”

Mia tried. “Leo, I feel mad when you grab. I want a turn with the blue blocks.”

Leo blinked. “I feel sad when I wait,” he said.

Beep-Bop wagged. “Good sharing of feelings. Let’s make a plan.”

Mia looked at the blocks. “We can take turns. I use blue for the bridge. Then Leo uses blue for the tower.”

Leo nodded slowly. “And I can use red now.”

“Deal?” Beep-Bop asked.

“Deal,” said Mia.

“Deal,” said Leo.

“Click,” said Beep-Bop, pretending to stamp the plan like a ticket. He set a tiny timer on his paw. “Two minutes for Mia. Then two minutes for Leo.”

They built. The blocks made soft tap-tap sounds. The lamp glowed like honey. The closet felt warm and safe. Mia placed blue blocks carefully. Leo stacked red blocks like a short tower. He hummed. Mia smiled at him. The timer chimed. “Beep!”

Leo reached for the blue. “My turn,” he said. His voice was calm. Mia let go and said, “Okay.” She felt proud. She had used her words.

Suddenly, the tower wobbled. “Uh-oh,” Leo said. The blocks tilting made a tiny scraping sound. The tower fell down with a gentle clatter. Leo’s eyes filled with tears. “It broke!”

Mia started to feel tight again. “That took a long time,” she said.

Beep-Bop held up a paw. “Problem-solving time. First, we notice. What happened?”

“It fell,” Leo sniffed. He touched a block and whispered, “I’m frustrated.”

“Second, we think of ideas,” Beep-Bop said. “Idea one: build again with a wider bottom. Idea two: ask for help. Idea three: take a small break.”

Leo wiped his eyes on the fuzzy blanket. “Help, please,” he said softly.

“That is strong self-advocacy,” Beep-Bop said. “You asked for what you need.”

Mia scooted closer. “I can help. Let’s make the bottom wide. Like a big pancake.”

Leo giggled. “Pancake tower!”

Together, they set four blocks flat. Tap. Tap. Tap. Tap. Mia pressed gently. Leo added blocks in the middle. Beep-Bop made a little building song. “Beep, bop, stack and stop.” The tower grew steady and tall.

From the hallway, the vacuum stopped. The house got quiet. They heard a bird tweet outside the window. The closet felt extra cozy.

Mia’s tummy grumbled. “Snack?” she asked.

Leo nodded. “Snack!”

Beep-Bop smiled. “Before we go, let’s check our plan. Did we share? Did we take turns? Did we solve the problem?”

Mia thought. “Yes. We used words. We took turns with the blue blocks. We took breaths when we felt mad.”

Leo nodded. “We asked for help. We made a pancake bottom.” He laughed.

Beep-Bop wagged. “Team success.” He opened the closet door. Light from the hallway made the room bright. The smell of warm toast drifted in. They carried their tall tower carefully to show Mom. It didn’t wobble at all.

Mom clapped. “What a strong tower! How did you do it?”

Mia stood tall. “We used our words and made a plan.”

Leo added, “We took turns. We breathed in and out. And we made a pancake bottom.”

Beep-Bop’s lights twinkled. “Problem solved,” he chimed.

Mom smiled. “I’m proud of you. When things feel hard, you can pause, use your words, and make a plan.”

Mia and Leo grinned. Their hearts felt warm and light. Beep-Bop gave a happy bark-beep.

Clear lesson: When you feel frustrated, stop and breathe, say what you need, and make a plan together. You can solve problems with gentle words and teamwork.

The End.
