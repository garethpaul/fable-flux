# Benny Beaver’s Big Table Talk

Benny was a busy beaver with bright teeth and a shiny, flat tail. He loved to build, fix, and help. One sunny afternoon, Benny waddled into the community dining room. The room smelled like warm cornbread and sweet apples. Sunlight danced on the wooden floor. The big table was set with colorful plates and cups. Today was Community Dinner Day!

Benny clapped his paws. “I will make the best place for everyone,” he said. “I can do it fast!”

Neighbors came in, smiling and chatting. Miss Robin fluffed napkins. Mr. Moose carried a basket that rustled with carrots. Little Squirrel held tiny flowers that smelled like honey.

Benny started right away. He stacked plates high. He lined up cups in a perfect row. Tap, tap, tap went his tail as he scooted chairs. He wanted everything just right.

Miss Robin chirped, “Benny, can we put napkins in the middle?”

Benny shook his head. “No time! Napkins go on the left. I know the fastest way.”

Little Squirrel tugged his paw. “Can my small chair be near the window? I like the light.”

Benny puffed his chest. “I am the helper. I will pick the best places.”

Mr. Moose said in a low voice, “I brought tall carrot sticks. They stand up well in the big jar. May I place them?”

Benny waved his paw. “I will do it. I am very good at this.”

He moved quickly. Chairs scraped. Silver forks clinked. The room buzzed. But the neighbors grew quiet. They looked at Benny, and their smiles faded a little.

Miss Robin tried again. “Benny, we want to help too.”

Benny’s tail thumped. “I can finish faster alone.” He reached for the big jar of carrot sticks. The jar was heavy. It slipped!

“Whoops!” Benny squeaked.

Thunk! The jar bumped the table, and a few carrots tumbled out. A cup wobbled. Water splashed like raindrops on the tablecloth. Little Squirrel jumped back. “Eep! My flowers!”

Benny froze. His ears drooped. “Oh no. I wanted it to be perfect.”

Miss Robin gently dabbed the water with a napkin. “It’s okay, Benny,” she chirped. “We can fix it.”

Mr. Moose nodded. “We’re a team. We can do more together.”

Benny took a slow breath. He felt his heart thump-thump. He looked at the worried faces. He didn’t want to be bossy or make spills. He wanted to help everyone feel happy.

He asked softly, “How can we do it… together?”

Miss Robin’s eyes twinkled. “Let’s talk and make a plan.”

They all gathered around the table. The room smelled like fresh water and carrots now. The sunlight felt warm on Benny’s fur.

Mr. Moose rumbled, “I’ll lift the heavy jar. My antlers are steady.”

Little Squirrel squeaked, “I’ll place the flowers on the low plates. I have small paws.”

Miss Robin chirped, “I’ll fold napkins into little fans.”

Benny swallowed and nodded. “I can line up the cups… but maybe not in one straight row. Would a zigzag be fun?”

Little Squirrel giggled. “Zigzag cups!”

Mr. Moose smiled. “Sounds good to me.”

Benny’s tail tapped slowly. “And how about chairs? Do you want the small chair by the window, Little Squirrel?”

“Yes, please!” she said, bouncing.

Benny smiled. “Okay. Let’s try it your way.”

They moved together like a dance. Scoot, scoot went the chairs. Swish went the napkins. Clink went the cups in a happy zigzag. Mr. Moose set the jar of carrot sticks without a wobble. Little Squirrel placed tiny flowers with care. Benny checked the paths so everyone could walk safely. He listened. He asked. He nodded.

“Does this feel good to everyone?” Benny asked.

“It does,” Miss Robin said, fluffing a fan napkin. “Thank you for listening.”

Benny stood taller. His chest felt warm. “I’m learning. When we share ideas, we make it better.”

Neighbors began to arrive. “Ooo,” said Turtle. “The cups make a fun line!” “Ahh,” hummed Rabbit, sniffing the sweet flowers. “Everything looks lovely.”

Benny beamed. He felt proud, but in a soft way, not a loud way. He felt strong because he worked with his friends. He felt helpful because he let others help too.

During dinner, everyone laughed and crunched and sipped. The room sounded like a happy song: mmm, chew, giggle, clink. Benny passed the bread to Little Squirrel. She handed him a carrot stick. Mr. Moose poured water carefully. Miss Robin told a silly joke, and even Turtle snorted.

After dessert, Benny stood and tapped his tail gently. “Thank you, friends. Today I learned a big thing. When I try to do everything alone, I make spills and frowns. When we talk and share, we make smiles. Compromise means I listen, you listen, and we choose a way that works for all of us.”

Mr. Moose nodded. “That’s respect.”

Miss Robin added, “That’s good teamwork.”

Little Squirrel squeaked, “That’s community!”

Benny grinned. “And that makes me feel brave and helpful.”

They all cheered. The dining room glowed with happy hearts and clean plates. Benny looked at the zigzag cups and the flower plates by the window, and he knew: working together made the dinner shine.

The End.
