# Ollie Otter’s Team-Time Tunnel

Ollie the playful otter lived in a sunny, playful living room. Soft rugs tickled his paws. Pillows made mountains. Bright blocks filled a big basket. The air smelled like warm toast and clean soap. Sunlight made little squares on the floor.

“Splish-splash!” Ollie giggled, even without water. He wriggled like a happy fish and clapped his paws. “Today, I will build a Team-Time Tunnel! We can play sports inside it!”

Ollie poured blocks on the rug. Clack, clack, clack. He lined them up like a rainbow road. He stacked them like tall trees. He hummed a bouncy tune. “Do-dee-do! Let’s build. Let’s play.”

His friends came to visit. Tia Turtle peeped from her shell. “Hello, Ollie,” she said softly. “I move slow, but I like to try.”

Max Monkey swung his tail and grinned. “I’m fast! Let’s race, jump, and cheer!”

Lulu Ladybug fluttered down with tiny wings. “I’m small, but I’m brave,” she buzzed.

Ollie puffed his chest. “I am the leader today,” he said. “We will build the tunnel and then play ball through it. I call it Tunnel Ball!”

Max tapped the blocks. “Let’s go, go, go!” he shouted.

Tia looked at the tall stack. “It might fall,” she whispered. “We should plan.”

Ollie blinked. He liked going fast. But he also wanted his friends to be happy. “Good idea, Tia,” he said. “We will prepare.”

They gathered on the rug. Ollie spread a big blanket on the floor so blocks would not slide. It felt soft and warm. “Step one,” Ollie said. “We make a wide door so everyone fits.”

Lulu flew close and measured with her tiny legs. “This door is too skinny for Tia,” she said.

Ollie nodded. “Let’s make it wider.” He moved blocks with gentle paws. The blocks clicked and felt smooth and cool.

“Step two,” said Tia, tapping slowly. “Make the roof strong.”

Max handed blocks quickly. “Here! Here! Here!” he chirped.

Ollie smiled. “Max, you are fast helper. Tia, you are wise helper. Lulu, you are careful helper.” He felt proud to be a kind leader.

Soon the tunnel stood tall and bright. Sunlight danced through the holes. It smelled a little like wood crayons. Ollie placed a soft ball near the door. “Now we play sports!” he cheered. “We roll the ball through the tunnel. Everyone gets a turn.”

Max hopped up. “Me first! I’m the best!”

Tia tucked into her shell. “I will go last,” she said.

Lulu landed on the ball. “I’m nervous,” she whispered. “What if I can’t push it?”

Ollie’s whiskers twitched. He looked at his friends. They were all different. They moved in different ways. His heart felt warm. “We can all play,” he said. “We will be kind and wait. We will cheer for each friend. That is being tolerant.”

Max tilted his head. “Tol-er-ant?”

“It means we accept each other,” said Ollie. “Fast, slow, small, or tall. We are a team.”

Max’s ears perked. “Okay! I can try waiting.”

Ollie clapped. “Max, you can go first. Then Lulu. Then me. Then Tia, if she wants.”

Max rolled the ball. “Zoom!” It swished through the tunnel and bumped a pillow with a soft thump. He jumped and cheered. “That was fun!”

Lulu took a deep breath. She put her tiny feet on the ball. Roll…roll… roll. The ball moved slowly. The tunnel glowed golden in the sun. “I did it!” she buzzed. Her wings hummed like a quiet song.

“Great job, Lulu!” said Ollie. “We cheer for all!”

Ollie rolled next. The ball made a gentle thud-thud on the blanket. It rolled smooth and straight. He felt proud and calm.

Tia peeked out. “I will try now,” she said. She pushed with steady feet. The ball moved very slowly. Max bounced in place, but he did not rush her. He counted softly, “One… two… three…” His voice sounded like a drum.

The ball reached the end. Tia smiled a little smile. “I did it,” she whispered.

Ollie clapped his paws. “Teamwork! Tolerance! Ta-da!”

They played again, and Ollie changed the rules so everyone could shine. “New round! Max, use tiny steps,” he said. Max giggled and tiptoed. “New round! Tia, you get extra time.” Tia nodded, calm and sure. “New round! Lulu, fly and cheer while you roll.” Lulu buzzed a happy cheer that tickled Ollie’s ears.

When they were done, they cleaned up together. The blocks clicked back into the basket. The room smelled like fresh air from an open window. Their hearts felt light.

Ollie hugged his friends. “Today, I was a leader who listened,” he said. “We prepared. We built. We played sports. We were tolerant.”

Max grinned. “Waiting can be hard, but I did it!”

Tia nodded. “Planning helped me feel safe.”

Lulu sparkled. “Cheering helped me feel brave.”

Ollie’s whiskers twitched again. “The clear lesson is this: Good leaders make space for everyone. When we accept our differences, we all have fun.”

They giggled together on the soft rug. The sun made warm stripes on their fur and shells and tiny wings. Their hearts said yes.

The End.
