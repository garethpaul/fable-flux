# Benny Beaver's Garden Helper Journal

Benny Beaver loved his cozy home on the balcony garden. Every morning, he would wiggle his fluffy tail and look out at all the beautiful plants growing in colorful pots. The sun felt warm on his brown fur, and the sweet smell of flowers tickled his little black nose.

"What a perfect day to help in the garden!" Benny said, clapping his tiny paws together.

Mrs. Robin lived in the apartment next door and took care of the balcony garden. She was getting older and sometimes found it hard to water all the plants and pull the weedy weeds.

Benny had a wonderful idea. "I want to help Mrs. Robin, but I should write down everything I do so I can remember how to be the best garden helper!"

He found a small notebook and a chunky crayon. With his paws, he carefully wrote "Benny's Garden Helper Journal" on the cover. The letters were wobbly, but they were his very own!

First, Benny stepped outside into the fresh morning air. The breeze ruffled his whiskers as he looked around. "I'll start by watering the thirsty plants," he said to himself, writing "Water plants" in his journal with big, careful letters.

He picked up the small watering can and filled it with cool water. Splash, splash, splash! The water sparkled like tiny diamonds as it fell on the green leaves. The plants seemed to dance and say "Thank you!" as they got their morning drink.

"The tomato plants look so happy now!" Benny giggled, making little drops on his journal page as he wrote "Tomatoes are happy!"

Next, Benny noticed some sneaky weeds growing between the flower pots. "Those don't belong here," he said, gently pulling them out with his small paws. The soil felt cool and crumbly between his fingers. He wrote "Pull weedy weeds" in his journal, drawing a little picture of a weed next to the words.

Just then, Mrs. Robin came outside. "Oh my!" she exclaimed, her eyes twinkling with surprise. "Benny, what are you doing?"

"I'm helping take care of your garden, Mrs. Robin!" Benny said proudly, holding up his journal. "And I'm writing everything down so I can remember how to help every day!"

Mrs. Robin's face lit up with the biggest smile. "What a thoughtful little beaver you are! Would you like to be my official garden helper?"

"Yes, yes, yes!" Benny jumped up and down, his tail slapping the ground with excitement.

Mrs. Robin showed Benny how to check if the soil was dry by poking his finger into the dirt. She taught him which plants needed more water and which ones liked to stay a little dry. Benny wrote everything down in his special journal, his tongue poking out as he concentrated.

"Tomorrow, we can plant some new seeds together," Mrs. Robin said. "And you can write about that too!"

As the sun began to set, painting the sky orange and pink, Benny looked at his journal filled with wobbly words and little drawings. He felt proud and happy inside his fuzzy chest.

"Being outside and helping others feels wonderful," Benny said, snuggling into his cozy spot. "And writing about it helps me remember all the good things I did!"

From that day on, Benny spent every morning in the balcony garden, helping Mrs. Robin and writing in his journal. The garden grew more beautiful each day, just like their friendship.

The End.
