# Toby's Big Attic Problem

In a cozy house, there lived a wise old tortoise named Toby. He had made his home in the quiet attic, where dusty sunbeams danced through the small window and old treasures sat covered in soft white sheets.

Toby loved his peaceful attic. Every morning, he would slowly stretch his wrinkled neck and blink his kind, dark eyes. "Ahh," he would sigh happily, "what a perfect place for thinking deep thoughts."

But one sunny Tuesday, Toby heard a loud THUMP! THUMP! THUMP! coming from the corner of the attic.

"Oh my!" said Toby, pulling his head into his shell. "What could that be?"

He slowly walked over to investigate. Behind an old rocking chair, he found three little field mice building a nest. They were dragging twigs and leaves across the wooden floor, making lots of noise.

"Excuse me," said Toby politely. "I'm Toby the tortoise. This is my quiet attic home. Could you please build your nest somewhere else?"

The smallest mouse, named Pip, looked up with big round eyes. "But Mr. Toby, it's getting cold outside! We need a warm place for winter. This corner is perfect!"

The middle mouse, Squeaky, nodded quickly. "Yes! And we've already worked so hard gathering all these soft materials."

The biggest mouse, Whiskers, stood tall. "We were here first today! We have the right to stay!"

Toby felt confused. He wanted his quiet space, but he could see the mice were scared and cold. Their tiny whiskers were trembling, and their little paws looked tired from all their hard work.

"Hmm," thought Toby, tapping his foot slowly. "This is quite a problem. We all need this attic, but we want different things."

For a whole hour, Toby sat very still and thought. The mice continued building, but they tried to be quieter because they could see Toby was thinking hard.

Finally, Toby's eyes brightened. "I have an idea! What if we share the attic?"

"Share?" squeaked Pip.

"Yes!" said Toby excitedly. "You can build your nest in that corner, but only during the day when I'm taking my afternoon nap. In the morning and evening, when I need quiet time for thinking, you can play in the garden below."

Squeaky tilted her head. "But what about when it's really cold?"

Toby smiled warmly. "On the coldest days, you can stay up here all day. But you must use your inside voices and walk softly. I'll teach you how to move quietly like I do."

Whiskers looked uncertain. "What do we get in return?"

"I'll share my collection of soft blankets," offered Toby. "And I know where the coziest spots are when the sun shines through the window."

The three mice looked at each other and whispered. Then Pip jumped up and down. "Yes! That sounds fair!"

From that day on, the attic became a happy shared home. The mice learned to walk on their tippy-toes during Toby's quiet times. Toby shared his warm blankets and taught them which corners stayed warmest.

When visitors came to the attic, they would see an amazing sight: a wise tortoise reading peacefully while three little mice played quietly nearby, all sharing the cozy space perfectly.

"You see," Toby would tell anyone who listened, "when we listen to each other and work together, everyone can be happy. That's what compromise means – finding a way for everyone to get what they need."

The End.
