# Penny Panda's Clean Paws Day

Penny Panda sat on her peaceful front porch, watching the morning sun paint golden stripes across the wooden boards. The sweet smell of jasmine flowers drifted through the warm air. She stretched her fluffy black and white arms and yawned.

"What a beautiful day to play outside!" Penny said to herself, her dark eyes sparkling with excitement.

Just then, her friend Benny Bunny hopped up the porch steps. His usually white fur was covered in brown mud from his ears to his tail.

"Hi Penny!" Benny called out cheerfully. "Want to come play in the mud puddles with me? It's so much fun!"

Penny wrinkled her nose. She could smell the earthy, wet dirt on Benny's fur. "Oh my, Benny! You're very muddy. Don't you think we should clean up first?"

Benny's ears drooped. "But I don't want to take a bath right now. I want to keep playing!" His voice got louder and more frustrated. "Cleaning up is boring!"

Penny took a deep breath and counted to three, just like her mama had taught her when she felt upset. She patted the porch swing beside her with her clean paw.

"Come sit with me, Benny," she said gently. "Let me tell you why I love being clean."

Benny hopped onto the swing, leaving muddy paw prints on the white cushions. Penny felt a little frustrated seeing her clean porch get dirty, but she remembered to breathe slowly.

"When I wash my paws," Penny explained, "they feel soft and smell like the lavender soap mama uses. Clean paws help me stay healthy and feel good about myself."

"But washing takes so long!" Benny sighed, crossing his muddy arms.

Penny smiled patiently. "I have an idea! What if we make washing fun? We could sing songs, blow bubbles, or play with rubber duckies in the water!"

Benny's ears perked up a little. "Bubbles? I do like bubbles..."

"And," Penny continued, "when we're all clean, we can have a tea party on my porch with fresh cookies mama baked this morning!"

The warm smell of chocolate chip cookies drifted from inside the house, making both friends' tummies rumble.

"That does sound nice," Benny admitted. "But Penny, I'm sorry I got your clean porch all muddy. Are you mad at me?"

Penny looked at the muddy paw prints and felt her patience being tested again. She closed her eyes for a moment and took another deep breath.

"I forgive you, Benny," she said warmly. "Friends make mistakes sometimes. What matters is that we learn and try to do better next time."

Benny's eyes filled with happy tears. "Thank you for being so patient with me, Penny. Will you help me get clean?"

"Of course!" Penny clapped her paws together. "Let's go wash up together. I'll show you my special soap that smells like strawberries!"

They walked inside, where Penny helped Benny wash his paws and face with warm, soapy water. They sang the "Scrub-a-Dub Song" and made silly faces in the mirror.

Soon, Benny was clean and fluffy again. His white fur sparkled in the sunlight as they returned to the porch with their tea cups and cookies.

"I feel so much better when I'm clean!" Benny said, taking a bite of his cookie. "Thank you for teaching me, Penny."

Penny smiled as they enjoyed their treat together on the peaceful porch. "Being patient and kind makes everything better," she said. "And clean paws make cookies taste even sweeter!"

The End.
