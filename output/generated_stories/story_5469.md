# Panda Poppy’s Car Ride Routine

Poppy was a patient panda. She had soft black-and-white fur and bright, curious eyes. Today, Poppy was going on a ride in the family car with Mama Panda and Baby Brother Bao. The morning sun felt warm on Poppy’s cheek. The car smelled like orange slices and clean seats. The seat belts made a tiny click, click, click.

“Ready to roll?” Mama Panda asked, buckling in.

“Ready!” said Poppy. She held her little backpack. It was blue with a tiny bamboo patch. Inside were her water bottle, her snack, and her favorite soft cloth.

“Wait,” Poppy whispered to herself. “Healthy habits first.”

She took a deep breath in… and out. “Water sip,” she said, and took a small drink. The water was cool and fresh. “Snack check,” she said, and peeked at her crunchy carrot sticks. “Seat check,” she added, tugging her seat belt. “Snug and safe!”

Bao clapped his paws. “Pop-pop good!” he chirped.

Mama smiled in the mirror. “I love how you prepare, Poppy. It shows respect for your body and for our trip.”

Poppy’s whiskers twitched with pride. “I am practicing,” she said. “Practice makes strong.”

The car hummed down the street. Trees waved hello. Birds sang, “Cheep-cheep!” The tires made a gentle whoosh on the road. Poppy saw a big yellow digger building a new playground. She pressed her nose to the window and felt it cool against her fur.

“I want to build strong habits like they build strong slides,” Poppy said.

“That’s a wonderful idea,” Mama said. “Healthy habits help us grow.”

Poppy nodded. “Today I will practice three. Drink water. Use kind words. Be patient.”

Bao bounced. “I help! I help!”

Soon, the car reached a red light. The car stopped with a soft shhh. Bao wriggled. “Go now! Go now!” he squeaked.

Poppy patted Bao’s paw. “We wait for green,” she said gently. “Red means rest. Green means go.”

Bao made a small huff. “Don’t like wait.”

Poppy took another deep breath and let it out slowly. “Waiting is hard,” she agreed. “Let’s count clouds. One… two… three puffy candies in the sky!”

Bao giggled. “Candy clouds!”

The light turned green. The car rolled on. “Thank you for being patient,” Mama said. “That is respectful to everyone on the road.”

Poppy felt warm and proud again. “Healthy habit number two,” she said softly. “Patience.”

A little later, Bao dropped his toy. It rolled under his seat with a clunk. Bao’s eyes filled with tears. “My bee! My bee!”

Poppy’s ears perked up. She looked at Mama. “Mama, may I help Bao?” she asked.

“Thank you for asking,” Mama said. “When we stop, I’ll pass it to you.”

Poppy turned to Bao. “It’s okay to feel sad,” she said in a calm voice. “We will get your bee soon. Let’s sing while we wait.”

“Song?” Bao sniffed.

Poppy sang softly. “Brush-brush teeth in the morning, wash-wash paws before we eat. Sip-sip water for our bodies, buckle up to keep us sweet.”

Bao smiled, then giggled. “Sweet paws!”

The car pulled into a quiet parking spot under a shady tree. Sunlight danced on the window like little sparkles. “Now we can try,” Mama said. She reached down, found the toy bee, and handed it to Poppy.

“Thank you, Mama,” Poppy said. “Bao, here is your bee.” She passed it with both paws. “I use gentle hands,” she added.

“Bee!” Bao hugged the toy. “Tank you, Pop-pop.”

“You’re welcome,” Poppy said. “That is habit number three: use kind words and gentle hands.”

Mama opened a small cooler. “Snack time,” she said. The oranges smelled bright and happy. The carrot sticks were crisp. Poppy wiped her paws with a wet wipe, rub-rub-rub, and passed one to Bao.

“Clean paws,” Poppy said. “Then snack.”

Bao copied her. “Clean, then snack,” he repeated.

They munched together. Crunch, crunch, crunch. The sound was fun. Poppy took a sip of water between bites. She felt her tummy say thank you.

“I like our car ride routine,” Poppy told Mama. “Prepare, be patient, be kind, and keep clean.”

“That’s a strong routine you’re building,” Mama said. “Habits are like blocks. You add one, then another, and soon you build something big.”

Poppy’s eyes shone. “Like a healthy-habits house!”

“Yes,” Mama laughed. “A safe, cozy house inside your day.”

On the drive back, the clouds grew fluffy and gray. A few drops of rain tapped on the roof. Tap, tap, tap. The air smelled like wet leaves. The wipers swooshed. Swish, swish.

Bao yawned. “Sleepy…”

Poppy tucked his blanket around him. “Rest helps us grow,” she whispered. “That’s another healthy habit.”

He snuggled close and closed his eyes. Poppy watched the raindrops race on the window. She felt calm. She had practiced her habits all day: she prepared for the ride, she was patient at the light and when Bao dropped his bee, and she used kind words. She felt strong and kind inside.

When they reached home, Mama turned off the car. Click. The rain made a soft hush on the driveway.

“Poppy,” Mama said, “I’m proud of the respect you showed—for yourself, for Bao, and for the road. Your persistence helped everyone have a good day.”

Poppy smiled a big panda smile. “Little habits, big help,” she said. “I will practice again tomorrow.”

The End.
