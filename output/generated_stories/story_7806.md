# Buzzy Beaver and the Dancing Laundry

Buzzy Beaver was a busy beaver with a bright, wiggly tail. He loved to help. He loved to move. He loved to imagine. Today, Buzzy stood in the warm, steamy laundry room. The washer hummed like a sleepy bee. The dryer whooshed like a soft wind. The soap smelled bubbly and clean, like lemon and clouds.

Mama Beaver called, “Buzzy, it’s laundry day. Can you help with the clothes?”

Buzzy puffed up with pride. “Yes, Mama! I can help. I am a Helper Beaver!”

He looked around. Socks waited in a basket like tiny puppets. Shirts hung on a line like flags. Towels were fluffy mountains. Buzzy’s whiskers twitched with ideas.

Buzzy whispered, “I will use my imagination.” He tapped his tail. “Let’s make this fun.”

He set the basket on the floor. “Okay, socks,” he said, “we’re going on a sock parade! One, two, march with you!” Buzzy marched in place. His little feet went tap-tap-tap on the tile. The socks flopped and flipped. Buzzy giggled. “Left foot, right foot, wiggle, wiggle, wiggle!”

Mama Beaver peeked in and smiled. “I hear marching. Are the socks behaving?”

Buzzy saluted. “Yes, Captain Mama! The socks are marching to their drawer.”

He picked up one blue sock. “Where is your partner?” Buzzy looked and listened. The dryer rattled. The washer swished. “Blue sock needs a buddy,” he said. “Let’s look.”

Buzzy crawled like a sneaky crab. He wiggled his body and looked under the table. He sniffed. “I smell warm towels.” He looked behind the door. “I see a dust bunny.” He peeked into the basket. “Aha!” He found the other blue sock hiding. “Found you! Partners stay together,” he said kindly. “It’s honest to put you back where you belong.”

He matched the socks and clapped. “Match, match, good catch!”

Next came the shirts. “Shirts, you are kites,” Buzzy decided. He lifted a red shirt and whooshed it through the air. “Swoosh!” He felt the soft cotton on his cheeks. It tickled! He laughed. “Up, up, little kite. Now fold, fold, fold.” Buzzy tried to fold, but the sleeves kept flopping out like little wings.

He frowned. “Hmm.” Buzzy wanted to be fast. He wanted to be perfect. His tail twitched. A little voice in his head said, “Just stuff it. No one will know.” Buzzy stopped. He took a deep breath. The room smelled like soap and warm water. He shook his head.

Buzzy said out loud, “I will do the right thing. I will tell the truth. If I need help, I will ask.”

He called, “Mama? I’m trying to fold the shirts. The sleeves go boing!”

Mama Beaver came in with a gentle smile. “Good asking, Buzzy. It’s brave to tell the truth.” She showed him slow hand motions. “Fold the bottom up. Tuck one sleeve, then the other. Pat, pat.”

Buzzy copied. “Fold, tuck, pat, pat.” The shirt became a neat, happy square. He grinned so big his whiskers wiggled. “I did it! My paws can fold!”

“Your paws can do many things,” Mama said. “Your imagination makes work feel like play.”

Buzzy nodded. “Now the towels are mountains.” He climbed onto his knees and pushed a towel with his nose. “I am a snow-plow beaver!” He scooted and slid. The floor felt cool on his knees. He pushed and stacked. “Big mountain. Little mountain. Baby mountain.”

He sang, “Scoot, scoot, push it neat. Busy beaver, quick and sweet!”

Then he heard a tiny plop. A sock fell behind the washer. Buzzy’s eyes got big. The space was narrow and shadowy. He felt a flutter in his tummy. “That sock is stuck,” he said. “Should I leave it?”

He looked at the clean piles. He looked at the gap. He put his paw on his heart. “I will do the right thing,” he whispered. “All the clothes need to go home.”

Buzzy lay on his belly. The tile was cool like a river rock. He stretched one paw, then the other. “Stretch, wiggle, scoot.” His tail swished for balance. He felt the sock with his fingertips. “Got you!” He pulled it out, dusty but safe.

He held it up. “Integrity means I do the right thing, even when it’s hard,” Buzzy said proudly. “Sock, you belong with your buddy.”

Buzzy tossed the sock into the pair pile. “Reunited!” He clapped once, twice.

Mama Beaver clapped too. “You moved your body. You used your big imagination. You told the truth and asked for help. That is great work.”

Buzzy beamed. He smelled the last warm load. He pressed his cheek to a towel. “Mmm, cozy.” He folded the final shirt—fold, tuck, pat, pat. He matched the final socks—match, match, good catch. He carried the basket, stepping heel-to-toe like a balance beam beaver. “Careful steps,” he whispered.

All the clothes were neat and happy. The room hummed softly. It felt calm, like a quiet pond.

Buzzy bounced and said, “Chores can be a game when I use my imagination. Moving my body helps me do my best. Telling the truth and asking for help keeps my heart strong.”

Mama hugged him. “Yes, my busy beaver.”

Buzzy hugged back. “I am a Helper Beaver, and I am an Honest Beaver too.”

They smiled at the clean, tidy room. Buzzy’s tail gave a happy thump. “Ready for the next adventure?” he asked.

“After a snack,” Mama laughed.

Buzzy giggled. “Snack parade!”

The End.
