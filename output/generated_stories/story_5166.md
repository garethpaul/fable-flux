# Ellie’s Treehouse Rules

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, Ellie walked through the forest, her feet going thump-thump on the ground. She sniffed the sweet smell of flowers and heard birds singing, tweet-tweet. A cool breeze tickled her trunk.

Today Ellie would visit the big treehouse. It was high in a tall, friendly tree. The steps were sturdy, and the wood smelled like warm sunshine. Inside, friends played together—Milo the monkey, Lila the ladybug, and Tico the turtle.

“Hi, Ellie!” Milo chattered, hanging by his tail. “Come play!”

Ellie smiled. “Hi, friends! I want to play too.”

As Ellie climbed the steps, she went slowly. Step. Pause. Step. “I’m being safe,” she said. “One step at a time.”

“Good idea,” Tico said in his calm, slow voice. “Safety first.”

Inside the treehouse, the light was soft and golden. A little window let in fresh air and the scent of pine. The floor felt smooth under Ellie’s big feet. A sign on the wall said: “Our Treehouse Rules: Respect, Include, Reuse, Ask First, Be Safe.”

Milo pointed. “Let’s build a nest with leaves!”

Lila buzzed in a circle. “Yes! And please be gentle. My wings are small.”

Ellie nodded. “I will be gentle.”

They gathered fallen leaves from a basket. The leaves were dry and crinkly—crunch, crunch. “We reuse leaves from the ground,” Tico said. “That is good for our forest. We don’t pick new ones.”

“That’s called sustainability,” Lila added. “We care for nature.”

Ellie smiled at the word. “Su-stain-a-bil-i-ty,” she said slowly. “We care for nature.”

Everyone began to build. Milo stacked leaves high. Tico placed them neat and flat. Lila flew around, making sure the nest felt soft.

Ellie wanted to help fast. She pushed a big pile with her foot. Swoosh! The leaves slid and bumped into Milo.

“Ouch!” Milo said, rubbing his arm. “Ellie, that was too hard.”

Ellie’s ears drooped. “I’m sorry. I didn’t mean to bump you.”

Tico looked kindly at Ellie. “Let’s remember our rule: Respect. We care about each other’s bodies and feelings.”

Lila landed on Ellie’s trunk. “And we ask first. ‘May I push the leaves?’”

Ellie took a deep breath. She liked her friends. She wanted them to feel safe. “You’re right,” she said softly. “Respect means I am gentle. Respect means I ask.”

She looked at Milo. “May I push the leaves, gently?”

Milo smiled. “Yes, Ellie. Thank you for asking.”

Ellie pushed slowly. Swish. The leaves moved like a quiet wave. Lila clapped her tiny hands. “That feels nice!”

They worked together until the nest looked cozy. It smelled like clean earth and sunshine. Ellie felt proud.

“Let’s play doctor!” Milo said. He put on a leaf hat. “I’ll check everyone. Beep-beep!”

Ellie giggled. When it was her turn, Milo touched her ear.

“Wait,” Ellie said. “Please ask first. My ears are tender.”

“Oh!” Milo said. “May I check your ear, Ellie?”

Ellie nodded. “Yes. Thank you for asking.”

Milo touched gently. “All good!” he said.

Lila buzzed near a tiny drawer. “Look, crayons!” There were bright reds, blues, and greens. “Let’s draw a picture of our tree.”

Tico opened a jar. “We use scrap paper,” he said. “We don’t waste. That is respectful to the earth.”

Ellie picked a green crayon. She drew big branches and little leaves. She held the crayon gently so it wouldn’t snap. “I am being careful,” she said.

When Ellie reached for a blue crayon, she noticed Tico’s hand was already there. They both paused.

“You first?” Ellie asked.

“You first?” Tico asked.

They laughed. “We can share,” Ellie said. “You color the sky, and I will color the river.”

They colored together. Swish-swish went the crayons. The picture looked bright and happy.

Suddenly, the floor made a tiny creak. The treehouse swayed just a little. The friends stopped. The wind whispered outside.

“Let’s check safety,” Tico said. He looked at the sign. “Be Safe.”

Ellie used her strong trunk to hold the rail. “Everyone sit for a moment,” she said in a calm voice. “We can listen.”

They sat quietly. The creak stopped. The treehouse felt steady again.

“Thank you, Ellie,” Lila said. “You are gentle and careful. That helps everyone feel safe.”

Milo nodded. “And you asked before pushing. That shows respect.”

Ellie’s eyes shone. “Respect means I care about you, and I care about our tree.”

They cleaned up together. They put the crayons back. They put extra leaves in the basket. They closed the little window to keep the treehouse dry.

“Can we invite more friends next time?” Ellie asked. “Maybe Zara the zebra and Pip the parrot?”

“Yes!” Lila buzzed happily. “We include everyone.”

“We will show them the rules,” Tico said. “Respect, Include, Reuse, Ask First, Be Safe.”

Ellie touched the sign with her trunk. “These rules make our treehouse happy.”

As they climbed down, Ellie went step by step. “One at a time,” she said. “Safe and gentle.”

At the bottom, the forest smelled fresh and green. Birds sang again, tweet-tweet! The friends hugged.

Milo grinned. “Today we learned a lot.”

Ellie smiled wide. “Respect is our big rule. We are gentle. We ask first. We share. We care for the earth. That makes everyone feel happy and safe.”

The friends walked home, hearts light and tails wagging or trunks swaying. The treehouse stood tall, cozy and clean, waiting for another day.

The End.
