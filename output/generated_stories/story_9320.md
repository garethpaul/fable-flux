# Rocco’s Bathtub Band

Rocco was a musical raccoon. He loved songs and beats. He loved claps and taps. One rainy afternoon, Rocco padded into the bathroom with his striped tail swishing and his whiskers twitching.

Drip, drip, drip went the faucet.
Plink, plink, plink sang the drops in the tub.

Rocco smiled. “Listen! The bathroom is a band,” he said. He picked up a soft washcloth and patted it on the sink. Thup-thup. He tapped the toothbrush on the soap dish. Tick-tick. He hummed a happy note, “La-la-la!”

Rocco’s little sister, Ruby, waddled in with her toy duck. “Quack!” she squeaked. “I want to play too!”

Rocco puffed out his chest. “I am the band leader,” he said. “I will play the big solo!”

Ruby looked at the shiny cup by the sink. She wanted to make music too. She tapped the cup, ding-ding, and smiled. “My turn?” she asked.

Rocco shook his head. “No, no. It is my solo. Only me.” He banged the cup fast. Ding-ding-ding-ding! He bopped the shampoo bottle. Bonk-bonk! The sounds got loud.

Ruby’s ears drooped. The toy duck slipped from her paws into the warm water with a soft plop. She whispered, “But I want a turn.”

Rocco kept playing, louder and faster. The bathroom echoed with clinks and clanks. The mirror fogged a little from the warm bath steam. The air smelled like bubbles and fresh soap. Water splashed gently. But Ruby sat on the bathmat, quiet and sad.

Mama Raccoon peeked in. “What lovely sounds,” she said. “But I see a big problem. Ruby looks upset.”

Rocco paused. The last ding hung in the air and faded away. He felt a tiny pinch in his tummy. “I was doing my solo,” he said.

Mama knelt down. “Music is more fun when we share,” she said. “Fair means everyone gets a turn. Fair means we wait with patience. Fair means we practice kindness.”

Ruby whispered, “I want to play the cup.”

Rocco hugged his tail. “But I was not done,” he said softly. He looked at Ruby’s droopy ears. He listened to the soft drip-drip of the faucet. He felt the warm steam on his nose. He thought, “I do not like it when I do not get a turn. Ruby does not like that either.”

Rocco took a deep breath. “Okay,” he said. “Let’s make a plan. We can take turns.”

Mama smiled. “That sounds fair. How will you know when it is time to switch?”

Rocco tapped his nose. “We can count to ten,” he said. “Then we switch. I will go first for ten. Then Ruby goes for ten.”

Ruby’s ears perked up. “Ten is good,” she said.

Rocco handed Ruby the shiny cup. “You can go first,” he added. “I can practice waiting.”

Ruby beamed. She tapped the cup gently. Ding…ding…ding. She listened to the sound. She tried soft taps and then a big tap. DING! She giggled.

Rocco sat on the bathmat and squeezed his paws together. He wanted to tap, tap, tap. But he waited. He counted out loud with a smile. “One, two, three…” He watched the bubbles in the tub. He listened to the sweet dings. He took slow breaths. “Four, five, six…” He could do it. “Seven, eight, nine, ten!”

Ruby stopped. “Your turn!” she said, placing the cup in Rocco’s paws.

Rocco felt proud. He had been patient. He tried a new beat. Ding-ding…ding. Tick-tick on the soap dish. Thup-thup with the washcloth. He looked at Ruby. “How do I sound?” he asked.

Ruby clapped. “Good! Try soft, then loud,” she said.

Rocco practiced. Soft. Loud. Fast. Slow. The bathroom filled with happy sounds. Drip, plink, ding, tick, thup. The warm steam swirled. The bubbly soap smell puffed up. The rubber ducky bobbed and squeaked.

“Switch!” said Ruby when he reached ten.

They traded. Ruby practiced too. She tried a slow beat. She tried a quick beat. She tapped and paused. She listened and learned. Rocco smiled and waited his turn again. He felt calm and strong. He liked being fair. He liked practicing. He noticed he got better each turn.

Mama tapped the tub gently. “May I join the band?” she asked.

Rocco and Ruby nodded. “Yes! But we must be fair,” Rocco said. “We can each have ten taps. We can cheer for each other. We can wait with patience.”

Together, they took turns. Rocco tapped. Ruby tapped. Mama tapped. They counted and switched. The bathroom band made a lovely song. It was not too loud. It was not too fast. It was just right for sharing.

When they finished, the last note floated like a bubble and popped with a tiny plink. Rocco looked at Ruby. “Thank you for waiting for me,” he said.

Ruby smiled. “Thank you for sharing,” she said.

Mama hugged them both. “Today we learned about fairness,” she said. “Fairness means everyone gets a turn. Patience helps us wait. Practice helps us grow. And music is happiest when we share.”

Rocco nodded. His heart felt light. “I like fairness,” he said. “It makes our band sound better.”

They put away the cup, the toothbrush, and the washcloth. The bathroom was quiet again, with only the soft drip-drip to say goodnight.

Rocco whispered, “Tomorrow, we can practice again. We will be fair again too.”

Ruby squeezed his paw. “Yes,” she said. “Ten taps each.”

They smiled, and the rubber duck gave one last friendly squeak.

The End.
