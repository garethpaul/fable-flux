# Bella Bird Sings at the Table

Bella was a small, bright-blue singing bird. Her feathers shimmered like tiny rain drops, and her eyes sparkled with curious light. She lived in a cozy house with a big family. The family’s dining room was her favorite place. It was warm, with a round wooden table, soft chairs, and a friendly clock that went tick-tock, tick-tock. Sunlight slid across the floor like honey. The air often smelled like toast, cinnamon, and warm soup.

Bella loved to sing. She sang in the morning: “Tweet-tweet, tralala!” She sang at noon: “Peep-peep, la-la!” Her songs were soft and sweet, like a gentle hug.

One busy evening, everyone gathered in the dining room for dinner. Plates clinked. Spoons tapped. The family smiled and talked. Bella hopped onto the back of a chair beside little Mia. Mia giggled and said, “Hi, Bella! I like your sparkly feathers.”

Bella felt a warm flutter in her chest. “Thank you,” she chirped. “I like your bright bow.”

Mia’s brother, Max, sniffed the air. “Mmm, tomato soup!” The steam smelled cozy and tomato-y. The bread was warm and crusty. Butter melted and made tiny shiny pools.

Bella loved the happy noises. She wanted to share a song. She puffed her chest and began, softly at first: “Twinkle, twinkle, chirp-chirp, la…”

But just then, Uncle Joe, who was very tired from work, rubbed his head. He frowned. “Oh dear,” he said, “my head hurts. I can’t listen to too much noise right now.”

Bella froze. Her song fluttered away like a leaf in the wind. She blinked her shiny eyes. “I didn’t mean to bother you,” she whispered.

Mia patted Bella’s wing gently. “It’s okay, Bella. Maybe later?”

Bella’s heart felt small. She wondered, “Is my singing too much? Am I too much?” Her feathers drooped a little. She hopped down to the end of the table near the window and stayed quiet. The clock went tick-tock, tick-tock. The soup smelled nice, but Bella felt a little sad.

Soon, Grandma noticed Bella’s quietness. Grandma had kind, soft hands and a calm voice. She set a small dish of water and a tiny dish of seeds on the window sill, in a safe spot away from the hot soup and sharp forks. “Here you go, Bella,” she said. “We want you safe and happy. The window is your safe perch.”

Bella sipped the cool water. It tasted like clear rain. She looked around the room. Everyone was different. Uncle Joe needed quiet. Max liked crunch. Mia liked bright colors. Grandma liked calm. The dining room had many sounds and smells, and many needs too.

Grandma smiled at Bella. “You have a gift,” she said. “Your singing is special. Sometimes people need quiet. Sometimes people need songs. Different needs, different times. We can make a plan.”

“A plan?” Bella chirped, tilting her head.

“A safety and kindness plan,” Grandma said. “We will keep you safe on your perch near the window, away from hot food and sharp tools. And we will choose a special time for your songs, so no one’s head hurts. Everyone can enjoy you.”

Mia clapped. “Yes! A Bella Song Time!”

Uncle Joe took a deep breath. He rubbed his temples a little less. “I’m sorry, Bella,” he said. “Your voice is lovely. I just had a hard day. Thank you for being patient.”

Bella chirped softly. “Thank you for telling me. I can be brave and try again later.”

After dinner, the family cleared the table. The clinks and clatters quieted down. The clock’s tick-tock grew cozy again. The evening sun painted the wall golden. Grandma said, “Now is Bella Song Time.”

Mia set a little paper sign by the window. It said, “Sing Time!” in big, round letters. Max brought a small bell. “I will ring this when it’s time to listen,” he said. He rang it gently: ding!

Bella took a deep, brave breath. She stood tall on her safe perch. Her feathers glowed in the soft light. She sang, “Tweet-tweet, tralala, peep-peep, la-la!” The tune floated like a butterfly, gentle and bright. It brushed the room with joy.

Mia swayed and hummed along. Max tapped softly on the table like a drum. Uncle Joe smiled with his eyes closed. “That’s perfect,” he whispered. “Thank you.”

Bella felt warm from beak to tail. She sang a song about colors. She sang a song about kindness. She sang a song about being different and being proud. “Blue feathers, green leaves, many ways to be,” she trilled. “All together, you and me!”

When the song ended, the room was quiet in a happy way. The family clapped softly. Grandma nodded. “See? We kept everyone safe. We listened to each other. We made a plan. And we celebrated what makes Bella special.”

Bella fluttered her wings. “I learned something,” she said. “Being different is good. I can be brave and share my song. I can also be kind and wait for the right time. We can all make space for each other.”

Mia hugged her gently. “We’re all different, and that’s wonderful!”

Bella looked at the window and the golden light. She felt proud, safe, and loved. She began a tiny, soft lullaby, just right for bedtime. The clock’s tick-tock joined in, and the dining room felt like a comfy nest.

Lesson: We all have special gifts. When we listen to each other and make safe, kind plans, we can celebrate our differences and shine together.

The End.
