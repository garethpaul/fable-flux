# Tessa Tortoise Makes a Plan

Tessa the Tortoise lived in a cozy house with a sunny rooftop. The rooftop had warm tiles, a little garden box, and a soft blanket in the shade. From up high, Tessa could see the tops of trees and hear birds sing, “Cheep, cheep!” The breeze smelled like fresh leaves and a tiny hint of flowers.

One bright morning, Tessa climbed onto the rooftop. Tap, tap, tap went her careful feet on the tiles. She squinted at the sky. “Hmm,” she said in her gentle, wise voice. “Today I will plant a tiny rooftop garden. I will grow crunchy lettuce.”

Tessa loved lettuce, but the rooftop had some problems. The garden box was empty. The sun felt hot. A small watering can sat far away by the stair door. A blue bird feather fluttered by and tickled Tessa’s nose. “Ah-choo!” she sneezed.

Tessa took a deep breath. “This is a big job,” she said. “I feel a little worried.” Her heart went thump-thump, but her shell felt strong on her back. “I am a wise tortoise. When I plan, I can do big things.”

She sat on the soft blanket and tapped her chin. Tap, tap. “Step one,” she said, “I will make a list.” She found a tiny chalkboard and a small piece of chalk. Scratch, scratch. She drew simple pictures: a seed, a cup of water, a sun with sunglasses, and a happy lettuce.

Tessa read her plan out loud in a kind voice.
“Step one: Gather seeds.
Step two: Bring water.
Step three: Find shade.
Step four: Plant and pat.
Step five: Check and cheer!”

She smiled. “That sounds good to me.”

Just then, a curious crow landed on the rail. “Caw?” he asked. His feathers were shiny black like wet stones.

“Hello, Crow,” said Tessa. “I am planting lettuce. I made a plan.”

“Caw!” Crow tilted his head. “Plan?”

“Yes,” said Tessa. “A plan is a map for your idea. It shows you what to do, step by step.”

“Caw!” Crow flapped once, as if to clap.

Tessa started with step one. She opened her pocket pouch—zip!—and took out a tiny paper packet. It made a soft crinkle sound. Inside were tiny brown seeds. They looked like sleepy dots. “Seeds,” she said. “Check!”

Step two: water. Tessa looked at the far-away watering can. It sat by the stair door like a lonely little moon. The sun made the tiles warm on her feet. “This will take time,” she said. “But I will go slow and steady.” Tap, tap, tap. Her feet moved in a steady beat. She reached the can and hugged the handle. “You are heavy,” she told it, “but I am strong.”

She pushed, she pulled. Scrape, scrape went the can. “Caw!” Crow hopped beside her, picking up a corner of the handle with his beak. Together they nudged it across the tiles. When they reached the garden box, Tessa smiled wide. “Water,” she said. “Check!”

Step three: shade. Tessa looked around and found a little umbrella folded near the chair. It smelled like dust and lemons. She opened it with a soft whoosh. Now there was a cool patch right over the garden box. “Shade,” she said. “Check!”

Step four: plant and pat. Tessa poured a little soil from a bag. It felt soft and crumbly, like chocolate cake crumbs. She made tiny holes with her careful claws. Plink, plink, plink went the seeds as they dropped in. She covered them with a gentle pat-pat. “Good night, little seeds,” she whispered. “Grow strong.”

She tipped the watering can. Drip, drip, trickle. The water smelled clean and cool. The soil turned dark and shiny. Crow leaned in and watched with bright eyes.

Step five: check and cheer. Tessa looked at her chalkboard. Every step had a little star now. Her heart felt warm. “I did it,” she said softly. “I followed my plan.”

But then a small gust of wind whooshed across the rooftop. Whoosh! The umbrella wobbled and almost fell. Tessa’s eyes grew wide. “Oh no!”

She touched her shell and took a slow breath in… and out. “I will not panic,” she said. “I will improve my plan.” She added another picture to the chalkboard: a rope tying the umbrella. “New step: Tie it tight.”

Tessa found a short rope near the chair. She tied a neat knot around the umbrella and the rail. Tug, tug. It stayed put. The wind blew again. Whoosh! The umbrella did not fall. “There,” she said. “Plans can change, and that is okay.”

Crow fluffed his feathers. “Caw!”

Days passed. Each morning, Tessa came to the rooftop. Tap, tap. She checked the soil with her toe. She gave a small drink of water. Drip, drip. She hummed a soft song. The sun warmed her shell. The breeze smelled sweet and green.

One morning, Tessa saw a tiny green sprout. It was as small as a little finger nail. “Oh!” she gasped. “Hello, baby lettuce!” Her eyes sparkled. Crow hopped and did a happy little dance. “Caw-caw!”

Soon, more sprouts popped up. Pop! Pop! The garden box looked like a tiny forest. Tessa clapped her front feet. “My plan worked. I was patient. I was brave. I can do big things, one step at a time.”

She picked one leaf, crisp and cool, and took a small bite. Crunch. “Delicious,” she said. “Thank you, plan.”

Tessa looked at Crow and smiled. “When we plan, we feel ready. When we try, we feel strong. When we fix our plan, we learn even more.”

Crow nodded. “Caw!”

Tessa tucked her chalkboard away and patted her shell. The rooftop was quiet and bright. The umbrella swayed softly, the leaves shivered in the breeze, and the sky was a calm blue blanket.

Tessa whispered, “I can do it. I make a plan, I take my time, and I believe in me.”

The End.
