# Leo’s Gentle Roar

Every sunny morning, Leo, a brave little lion cub, followed his happy routine. He woke up, stretched his paws, and whispered, “I am kind. I am brave.” He brushed his fluffy mane, ate crunchy cereal, and packed his art bag. It smelled like crayons and clean paper. Today was Art Day in the creative art room, and Leo loved Art Day.

The creative art room was bright and warm. Sunlight painted golden shapes on the floor. Jars of paint looked like rainbows—red, blue, yellow, and green. Soft brushes waited in a cup. Paper rustled like leaves. Leo’s friends were there too—Mina the monkey, Timo the turtle, and Zara the zebra. Ms. Daisy, their gentle teacher, smiled. “Good morning, artists,” she said. “Today we will make Kindness Kites. After art, we’ll go outside to fly them!”

Leo’s whiskers twitched with joy. “A Kindness Kite!” he said. “My kite will soar and share smiles.”

Ms. Daisy clapped softly. “First, we listen. Then we choose. Then we create. That is our art routine.”

Everyone nodded. Leo took a deep breath. The room smelled like glue and oranges from snack time. He picked a shiny blue paper for his kite. He drew a big heart in the middle and tiny stars around it.

Mina’s tail swished. “I want the sparkly stickers!” she said, reaching for the jar.

Zara reached too. “I need them for my stripes!” she said.

Their hands bumped. The sticker jar wobbled, then bumped the paint water. Splash! Blue drops dotted the table like raindrops.

Mina frowned. “Hey!”

Zara stomped. “I had them first!”

Leo felt his tummy tighten. Arguments made his ears droop. He looked at Ms. Daisy. She was helping Timo tie string and did not see yet. Leo remembered his morning whisper: I am kind. I am brave.

Leo put a paw on his chest and took a slow breath. In… and out. He felt calm. He stood up tall—well, as tall as a little lion cub can stand. “Friends,” he said softly, “let’s use a peaceful plan.”

Mina and Zara looked at him. Their faces were scrunchy and sad.

“We can share,” Leo said. “We can take turns. We can ask with gentle words. We can listen. We can make a plan.”

Mina’s tail stopped twitching. “A plan?”

Zara’s ears perked. “What plan?”

Leo pointed to the art timer on the shelf, a little clock with a red slice. “Let’s set the timer. Five minutes for you, Mina. Five minutes for you, Zara. While one uses the stickers, the other can draw or paint. Then we switch. We can also say what we need.”

Mina and Zara blinked. “Okay,” they said together.

Leo turned the timer. Tick, tick, tick. He handed the jar to Mina. “Mina, you can start. Zara, do you want to pick a color while you wait?”

Zara nodded. “Yellow, please.” Her voice was soft now.

Mina stuck two stars on her kite. “Thank you, Leo. I felt mad. Now I feel better.”

Zara drew gentle stripes and hummed. “Thank you, Mina, for letting me go next.”

Leo smiled. His tummy felt light again, like a kite in the wind. Ms. Daisy came over and saw the calm table. “What wonderful problem-solvers,” she said. “Leo, how did you help?”

Leo explained the peaceful plan. Ms. Daisy nodded. “That is a kind and brave roar, Leo. A gentle roar. You used your voice to bring peace.”

Soon, everyone finished their Kindness Kites. Leo’s kite had a big heart, tiny stars, and a tail made of red bows. It smelled like fresh paper and a little bit like glue. He wrote one word on the heart: “Thanks.”

Ms. Daisy clapped. “Outdoor time!”

Outside, the air felt cool and sweet. The grass tickled Leo’s paws. The sky was bright and wide, the perfect place for kites. The wind whispered, whoooosh, like a friendly dragon. Leo ran with his kite. The string hummed in his paw. Up, up, up went the Kindness Kite, fluttering and dancing.

Mina laughed. “Look at mine!”

Zara cheered. “Mine is flying too!”

Timo’s kite bobbed. “Slow and steady,” he said, smiling.

Leo felt grateful. He looked at his friends. “Thank you, wind. Thank you, sky. Thank you, friends,” he said. His heart felt warm.

Ms. Daisy gathered the group under a shady tree. Leaves made soft shadows on their faces. “What did we learn today?” she asked.

Mina raised her hand. “We can share.”

Zara nodded. “We can take turns.”

Timo added, “We can speak kindly.”

Leo touched his kite string and said, “We can use peaceful solutions. We can breathe, listen, and make a plan. That makes our day bright.”

Ms. Daisy smiled. “Yes. Peaceful solutions help everyone feel safe and happy. They help our routine go smoothly. And they make outdoor time fun.”

On the way back, Leo whispered his end-of-day words, “I am grateful. I am kind. I am brave.” He gently rolled his kite and helped tidy the creative art room. The brushes clinked softly. The tables felt clean and smooth. The room looked shiny and ready for tomorrow.

Leo’s gentle roar was quiet, but strong, like a cozy purr. He felt proud. He had used his brave heart and kind mind to help his friends.

The End.
