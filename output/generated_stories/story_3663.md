# Polly's Big Decision

Polly the Parrot perched on the windowsill in the cozy laundry room. Her bright green feathers sparkled in the warm sunlight streaming through the glass. The washing machine hummed a gentle tune, and clean clothes hung on the line like colorful flags.

But Polly had a big problem. She loved to talk and sing, but today she felt confused about which words to use.

"Good morning, Polly!" called Mrs. Garcia as she folded towels. "What would you like to say today?"

Polly tilted her bright orange head. She knew so many words - happy words, silly words, kind words, and even some not-so-nice words she had heard on TV. But which ones should she choose?

"I... I don't know," Polly chirped softly. "There are so many words in my head! How do I pick the right ones?"

Mrs. Garcia smiled warmly. "That's a wonderful question, Polly. What do you think makes words special?"

Polly fluffed her feathers and thought hard. She watched Mrs. Garcia's gentle hands folding the soft, lavender-scented sheets. She listened to the rhythmic swoosh-swoosh of the washing machine. Everything felt peaceful and kind.

"Maybe..." Polly said slowly, "maybe the best words are the ones that make people smile?"

"That's a great start!" Mrs. Garcia encouraged. "What else?"

Polly hopped down to a basket of warm, fluffy socks. She touched one with her tiny claw - it felt so cozy! "Words that make people feel good inside, like these warm socks?"

"Exactly!" Mrs. Garcia clapped her hands. "You're making a very smart decision, Polly."

Suddenly, Polly felt excited. She had an idea! She could choose words that helped people, just like how the washing machine helped make clothes clean and fresh.

"I want to say helpful words!" Polly announced proudly. She cleared her throat and began: "Thank you! Please! Good morning! I love you!"

Mrs. Garcia beamed. "Those are beautiful words, Polly!"

But then Polly heard the neighbor's dog barking angrily outside. She knew some angry words too. For a moment, she felt tempted to repeat them.

Polly paused and took a deep breath. She remembered her decision. "No," she said to herself. "I choose kind words."

Instead, she called out sweetly, "Hello, doggy! Have a nice day!"

The barking stopped, and soon she heard a gentle "woof" in return.

"Polly, you made such a good choice!" Mrs. Garcia said proudly. "You decided to use your voice to spread happiness."

Polly puffed up her chest with pride. "I did! I chose the words that make hearts feel warm, like these clean clothes!"

From that day on, whenever Polly felt unsure about what to say, she remembered her special decision. She would ask herself: "Will these words make someone smile? Will they help someone feel good?"

And because Polly learned to choose her words carefully, everyone loved talking with her. She became known as the kindest, most thoughtful parrot in the whole neighborhood.

"Thank you for teaching me about decisions," Polly chirped to Mrs. Garcia as the sun set over the laundry room. "Now I know that choosing good words is like choosing the softest, coziest clothes - they make everyone feel better!"

Mrs. Garcia gave Polly a gentle pat. "You're absolutely right, sweet Polly. Good decisions make the whole world a little brighter."

The End.
