# Whiskers Learns to Share

The afternoon sun streamed through the window into the cozy study corner. Whiskers the clever cat sat surrounded by colorful books, crayons, and his favorite orange cushion. His whiskers twitched as he counted his snacks. "One, two, three crackers. One apple slice. One cookie," he purred happily.

Just then, his little sister Mittens padded into the study corner. Her tiny paws made soft sounds on the carpet. "Hi, Whiskers! What are you doing?" she asked with big, curious eyes.

Whiskers quickly pulled all his snacks closer. "I'm having my afternoon snack time," he said. "These are MINE. Go find your own food."

Mittens' ears drooped down. Her tummy made a little rumbling sound. "But I'm really hungry," she whispered. "Mama is busy cooking dinner. Could I please have just one tiny cracker?"

"No!" Whiskers said firmly. "I worked hard to gather these snacks. I planned my goal to eat healthy foods. These are for ME only."

Mittens' eyes filled with tears. She walked away slowly, her tail dragging on the floor.

Whiskers munched his first cracker. *Crunch, crunch, crunch.* It tasted good, but something felt wrong in his tummy. Not sick-wrong, but sad-wrong.

He looked at his pile of snacks. Then he looked at the empty doorway where Mittens had left. His whiskers drooped.

"Wait," Whiskers thought. "Sharing my snacks doesn't mean I can't reach my goal of eating well. It means I can help Mittens eat well too!"

Whiskers picked up half his crackers and the apple slice. He padded quickly to find Mittens. She was curled up in the hallway, looking very small and sad.

"Mittens?" Whiskers said softly. "I'm sorry I wasn't kind. Would you like to share snack time with me?"

Mittens looked up with surprise. "Really? You want to share?"

"Yes!" Whiskers purred. "Sharing makes snacks taste even better. And it helps both of us eat healthy foods."

They walked back to the study corner together. Whiskers spread out a soft blanket. "Here," he said, giving Mittens the apple slice and two crackers. "The apple has vitamins to help you grow strong!"

Mittens' face lit up like sunshine. "Thank you, Whiskers! You're the kindest big brother ever!"

They sat together, munching their snacks. The crackers went *crunch* and the apple slice was sweet and juicy. But the best taste of all was the warm, happy feeling in Whiskers' heart.

"This IS better than eating alone," Whiskers realized. "When I'm kind and share, I feel good inside. And Mittens feels good too!"

From that day on, Whiskers always saved some of his snacks to share. He learned that taking care of himself AND being kind to others made him the happiest cat in the whole house.

"Being kind doesn't take away from my goals," Whiskers would tell himself. "It makes everything better!"

And whenever other animals visited their study corner, Whiskers always asked, "Would you like to share a healthy snack with us?" Because he discovered that kindness, just like good food, helps everyone grow strong and happy.

The End.
