# Kori Koala’s Brave Little Routine

Kori the calm koala had a cozy study corner. It was a small nook by a sunny window. Soft light made gentle squares on the floor. A tiny table held chunky crayons, a water cup, and a stack of picture books. A fuzzy blue rug tickled Kori’s toes. The corner smelled like fresh paper and sweet eucalyptus leaves.

Every afternoon, the clock went tick-tock in a friendly way. “Tick-tock, time to learn,” Kori whispered. Kori liked calm breaths. In and out. Slow and soft. But Kori had a little problem.

“I forget my healthy steps,” Kori said, ears drooping. “I want to drink water. I want to stretch. I want to clean my space. But I get busy, and I forget.”

Kori looked at the crayons. Colors winked like candy. The books whispered, “Read me, read me!” The rug felt so snuggly. It was hard to remember all the steps.

Kori placed a paw on the heart and said, “I can be brave. I can try again.”

Tap-tap-tap. The rain began outside. It smelled like wet leaves. The window went pitter-patter. Kori smiled. “This is a good day to make a new plan.”

First, Kori took three calm breaths. “In… out. In… out. In… out.” The tummy rose like a balloon and fell like a cloud. Kori felt strong and ready.

“I will make a Healthy Habit Song!” Kori said. “A song can help me remember.”

Kori grabbed a purple crayon. “Purple for power,” Kori giggled. On a big card, Kori drew simple pictures:
- A drop for drink water.
- A little broom for clean up.
- A sun for stretch and move.
- A heart for calm breaths.
- A book for study time.
- A moon for rest time.

Kori hummed and made words to match the pictures. “Sip-sip, sweep-sweep, stretch-stretch, breathe deep. Read-read, rest-rest. I do my best-best!”

Kori clapped a soft beat on the table. Clap, clap. “This is fun!” The song felt warm in the chest, like tea.

But then, a tricky thought tiptoed in. “What if I forget the song too?” Kori’s ears tilted again.

Kori took a brave breath. “I can try something new.” This was the problem, and Kori wanted a solution.

Kori used blue tape to put the card on the wall. The pictures were big and bright. “My eyes can help my ears,” Kori said. “I will sing and look.”

Next, Kori set the water cup to the left, the crayons in a little basket, and a soft cloth on the table. “Everything has a spot,” Kori said. “A clean space helps my mind.”

The rain made a steady drum beat. Kori swayed and sang, “Sip-sip,” and took a drink. Cold water felt fresh on the tongue. “Sweep-sweep,” Kori wiped the table in small circles. The cloth smelled lemony. “Stretch-stretch,” Kori reached up high, then folded low. The back felt long and happy. “Breathe deep,” Kori put paws on the belly. “In… out.” Calm and cozy.

Then came “Read-read.” Kori opened a picture book. Pages whispered flip, flip. The pictures showed tall trees and bright birds. Kori traced a bird with a gentle claw. “Hello, bird.”

When the eyes felt a little tired, Kori blinked slow. “Rest-rest,” Kori said, and placed the book down. A tiny timer hummed. “Ding!” Study time was done.

Kori looked around. The table was tidy. The water cup was half full. The card with pictures smiled from the wall.

“I did it,” Kori said. The chest felt proud and light. “My routine helped me.”

Just then, a friend peeped from the doorway. It was Mina Mouse, with bright whiskers. “Hi, Kori! What is that song?” Mina asked, squeaking softly.

Kori blushed under fuzzy fur. “It is my Healthy Habit Song. It helps me remember. I was afraid I would forget. But I was brave. I made a picture plan too.”

Mina’s eyes sparkled. “Can I try?”

Kori nodded. “Let’s sing together.”

They sang, “Sip-sip, sweep-sweep, stretch-stretch, breathe deep. Read-read, rest-rest. I do my best-best!” Their voices were gentle and happy. Clap, clap. Tap, tap. The rain kept the beat.

Mina smiled wide. “I feel calm. I like your study corner. It smells nice! The rug is so soft.”

Kori giggled. “Thank you. The routine helps me learn. It helps me feel safe. It helps me grow.”

Mina pointed to the heart on the card. “You were brave, Kori. You tried a new way.”

Kori nodded. “Being brave can be quiet. It can be a calm try. Little steps help.”

The clouds outside opened a bit. A stripe of sun slid onto the rug. The blue fibers glittered.

Kori rolled the shoulders and took one more breath. “Today, I learned that healthy habits are small, simple steps. A song and a picture plan help me remember. I can improve with practice. I can be brave and try again.”

Mina clapped. “Best-best!”

Kori laughed. “Yes. Best-best.”

They sipped water, put the crayons back, and waved goodbye. Kori looked at the corner once more. Tidy. Bright. Peaceful.

“See you tomorrow, routine,” Kori whispered, feeling proud and calm.

The End.
