# Pico Parrot’s Car Concert

Pico was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a sunny yellow tail. He loved to sing new songs. Today, Pico sat on a soft blanket in the back seat of the family car. The seat felt warm. The window was cool when he touched it with his beak. The car hummed, “Vrrr-rrr,” as it rolled down the street.

Mama drove. The turn signal tick-tocked, “click, click.” The air smelled like orange slices from a snack. Pico listened to the soft whoosh of the air and the gentle rumble under his feet. He fluffed his feathers and whispered, “What a perfect place for a car concert!”

In the car with Pico were twins, Mia and Max. Mia wore a blue hat with tiny stars. Max held a toy bus. They were going to visit Grandma. Pico loved family car rides. He could see trees and houses and people from all over the world right through the window.

Pico chirped, “Let’s sing! We are a family from many places. We can share and care in many ways!”

Mia clapped. “I want a fast song!”

Max shook his toy bus. “I want a slow song!”

Pico blinked. His eyes were round and kind. “We can do both,” he said. “We can take turns.”

But Mia shouted, “Fast!”

Max shouted, “Slow!”

Their voices bumped into each other. The car felt noisy. Pico’s chest felt tight, like a little drum. He did not like fighting. He took a slow breath. In, out. He listened to the steady hum of the tires. Hummm. He looked at Mama’s eyes in the mirror. Mama smiled. “Use your kind words,” she said. “We can solve this.”

Pico nodded. “Friends,” Pico said softly, “the world is big. People like different things. That is okay. We can listen and learn. That’s how we are good world neighbors.”

Mia held her hat. “What is a world neighbor?” she asked.

Pico puffed his feathers. “A world neighbor is someone who cares about people, near and far,” he said. “We share, we take turns, and we show respect. We celebrate all the little differences.”

Max tilted his head. “Like different songs?”

“Yes,” Pico said. “Different songs, foods, and games. We can mix them and make something new.”

Pico tapped the window lightly. Drops of rain began to patter, soft and sweet. Tap-tap-tap. The car smelled like rain and oranges. Pico felt a new idea blooming in his head. “Let’s make a Sharing Song,” he said. “First fast, then slow. We will use words from different hellos I have learned.”

Mia smiled. “I like hello words!”

Max grinned. “Me too.”

Pico cleared his throat. “Ahem. Ready?” He began with a fast beat, tapping the seat with his claws. “Hola! Hello! Jambo! Salaam! We ride together, vroom-vroom-vroom!” His voice was bright like sunshine. Mia clapped fast. Max giggled at the vroom-vroom part.

Then Pico slowed down and swayed. “Bonjour, namaste, ni hao, too,” he sang softly. “We take turns. We care for you.” His voice was gentle like a blanket. Max rocked his toy bus slowly. Mia let her hands float like leaves.

Mama hummed along. “What a beautiful mix,” she said. The wipers went swish-swish across the glass. The lights of the cars ahead glowed warm and red. Pico felt calm and happy. The car felt like a little world where everyone belonged.

Mia said, “I like the fast part.”

Max said, “I like the slow part.”

Pico flapped once. “I like that we listened,” he said. “We made space for both.”

They kept singing, taking turns. Fast like popcorn pops. Slow like a sleepy cuddle. Pico added more hello words. “Ciao! Konnichiwa! Habari! Shalom!” He spoke them with care. He imagined children everywhere waving and smiling. The words sounded like colors. They felt like warm hands held together.

At a red light, the car stopped with a soft sigh. Mama asked, “What did we learn?”

Mia said, “Different is good.”

Max said, “We can share turns.”

Pico said, “When we listen to each other, we are good world neighbors. We show respect to all people.”

The light turned green. The car rolled on. The rain slowed to tiny kisses on the window. Pico looked outside and saw a bakery. He smelled sweet bread as Mama’s window cracked open. He saw a child in a bright scarf and another child with shiny boots. They waved. Pico waved back with a wing.

“Hello!” Pico chirped. “Hola! Bonjour!” The children laughed and waved more. Pico felt his heart grow warm and big, like a balloon filled with kindness.

Soon they reached Grandma’s house. The car engine went quiet. Click. Everything felt still and cozy. Grandma opened the door and hugged them. “What music do I hear?” she asked.

“Our Sharing Song!” Mia said.

“It has fast and slow,” Max added.

“And hello words from around the world,” Pico said proudly. “Because we care about everyone, near and far.”

Grandma clapped. “That is global citizenship,” she said. “You are little helpers for a big world.”

Pico smiled. “We are world neighbors,” he said. “We listen, we share, and we celebrate our differences.”

They sang the Sharing Song for Grandma. Fast and slow, loud and soft, with kind hearts and happy faces. The house smelled like warm soup. The rain made a gentle drum on the roof. Pico felt safe, loved, and brave.

He whispered, “The world is big, and our hearts can be big, too.”

The End.
