# Robo's Turn to Help

In the busy basement of the community center, a shiny blue robot dog named Robo wagged his metal tail. His LED eyes blinked happily as he watched children playing with colorful toys scattered everywhere.

"Beep beep! So many friends!" Robo said in his cheerful electronic voice.

The basement smelled like crayons and play dough. Toys clicked and clattered as children moved them around. But soon, Robo noticed something troubling.

"I want the red truck!" shouted Tommy, grabbing it from Maya's hands.

"No! I had it first!" Maya cried, pulling the truck back.

"Mine now!" said little Sam, reaching for the same truck.

All three children tugged at the bright red fire truck. Their faces turned red and their voices got louder. Other children stopped playing to watch the argument.

Robo's sensors detected sadness and frustration. His heart-shaped screen displayed a worried face. "Oh my circuits! This is not good," he said softly.

The helpful robot dog rolled over on his smooth wheels. His tail wagged slower now. "Hello, friends! I see you all love this wonderful red truck."

"We all want it!" Tommy said, still holding tight.

"It's the best truck ever!" Maya added.

"Fire trucks go WOO-WOO!" Sam said excitedly.

Robo's screen lit up with a bright idea - a lightbulb appeared on his display. "I have a super solution! Would you like to hear it?"

The three children looked at the friendly robot dog. His warm LED lights made them feel calmer.

"What if we take turns?" Robo suggested gently. "Taking turns means everyone gets to play!"

"What's taking turns?" asked Sam, tilting his head.

Robo's screen showed a happy clock. "Taking turns means one friend plays first, then the next friend, then the next! Everyone gets a special time with the truck."

"But I want it NOW," Tommy said, stamping his foot.

"I understand wanting things right away," Robo said kindly. "But taking turns helps everyone feel happy and included. It's like sharing kindness!"

Maya thought for a moment. "How long does each person get?"

"Great question!" Robo's tail wagged faster. "How about we count to fifty together? That's enough time to drive the truck around and make fire engine sounds!"

The children looked at each other. The idea sounded fair.

"I'll go first since I grabbed it," Tommy said.

"Actually," Robo said gently, "Maya had it first. Fair taking turns means she goes first, then you, then Sam."

Tommy's shoulders dropped, but then he nodded. "Okay. That's fair."

Maya smiled and drove the truck around, making "WOO-WOO" sounds. Everyone counted together: "One, two, three..." When they reached fifty, Maya happily handed the truck to Tommy.

"This is fun!" Tommy said as he took his turn. The counting made the waiting feel like a game.

When Sam got his turn, he was so excited he made the loudest fire truck sounds ever. "WOO-WOO-WOO!"

"You see?" Robo said, his screen showing hearts. "Taking turns means everyone gets to play AND everyone feels happy!"

Soon, other children in the basement started taking turns with their toys too. The room filled with laughter instead of arguments.

"Thank you, Robo!" Maya said, giving the robot dog a gentle hug.

"Taking turns is the best!" Tommy agreed.

Robo's tail wagged proudly. "When we take turns, we take care of each other. That's what good friends do!"

The End.
