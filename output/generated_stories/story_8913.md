# Nutkin's Garden Helper Day

Nutkin the squirrel loved his cozy home in the big oak tree next to Mrs. Chen's balcony garden. Every morning, he would peek through the green leaves and watch her water her pretty flowers and vegetables.

"Good morning, little tomatoes!" Mrs. Chen would say cheerfully. "Time for your breakfast drink!"

Nutkin thought this was very funny. Plants drinking water for breakfast! He wished he could help Mrs. Chen take care of her garden, but he was just a small squirrel.

One sunny Tuesday morning, Nutkin noticed something different. Mrs. Chen wasn't on her balcony watering the plants. The flowers looked droopy, and their colorful petals were starting to curl.

"Oh no!" squeaked Nutkin. "The plants look thirsty!"

He waited and waited, but Mrs. Chen didn't come. The sun got hotter, and the poor plants drooped even more. A little butterfly landed on a wilted sunflower.

"These flowers used to give me sweet nectar," sighed the butterfly sadly. "Now they're too dry."

Nutkin's heart felt heavy. He wanted to help, but how could a little squirrel water a whole garden?

Then he had an idea! Nutkin scampered down to the balcony and found Mrs. Chen's small watering can. It was too heavy for him to lift, but he was very clever.

"I know!" he chattered excitedly.

Nutkin found a tiny acorn cap that had fallen from his tree. He dipped it into the watering can and carefully carried the water to the first plant. Drip, drop! The water fell onto the dry soil.

"This will take a long time," Nutkin said to himself. "But these plants need help!"

Drop by drop, acorn cap by acorn cap, Nutkin worked patiently. His little paws got tired, and his fluffy tail was damp with water, but he didn't stop. He thought about how happy the plants would be to have a cool drink.

"Thank you, little squirrel!" whispered the sunflower as water touched its roots.

The butterfly fluttered around Nutkin happily. "You're so kind!"

After many trips back and forth, Nutkin had given water to every single plant. His tiny arms were tired, but his heart felt warm and happy.

Just then, Mrs. Chen appeared on the balcony with her arm in a sling.

"Oh my!" she gasped, looking at her plants. "How are my flowers still so beautiful? I hurt my arm yesterday and couldn't water them!"

She noticed the wet acorn cap next to the watering can and smiled. "Could it be... did someone help my garden?"

Nutkin chittered softly from his tree branch. Mrs. Chen looked up and saw his kind, bright eyes.

"Thank you, little friend," she whispered. "You have such a caring heart."

From that day on, whenever Mrs. Chen couldn't tend her garden, Nutkin was ready to help. He learned that even small acts of kindness could make a big difference. The plants grew more beautiful than ever, and the balcony garden became a happy place where everyone helped each other.

"Helping others feels even better than finding the biggest acorn!" Nutkin would tell his squirrel friends.

And it was true.

The End.
