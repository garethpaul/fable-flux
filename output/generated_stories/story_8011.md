# Penny the Parrot's Big Idea

Penny was a bright green parrot with beautiful red feathers on her wings. She lived with the Johnson family and loved to sit on her perch in their cozy dining room. The dining room had a big wooden table, soft yellow walls, and windows that let in warm sunshine.

Every morning, Penny watched the family eat breakfast together. She could smell the sweet pancakes and hear the cheerful chatter. But Penny felt sad because she had a wonderful idea that she was too scared to share.

"I wish I could help make breakfast special," Penny whispered to herself, ruffling her colorful feathers nervously.

One Tuesday morning, little Emma spilled her orange juice all over the table. "Oh no!" Emma cried. "I made a big mess!"

Penny's heart fluttered with excitement. This was her chance! But what if her idea was silly? What if nobody liked it?

"Squawk! I have an idea!" Penny called out, her voice shaking a little.

The whole family looked up at her perch. Mom, Dad, Emma, and baby Sam all turned their eyes toward the nervous parrot.

"What's your idea, Penny?" asked Mom with a gentle smile.

Penny took a deep breath. The dining room felt very quiet. "I... I could sing a cleanup song! It might make cleaning up more fun!"

Dad's face lit up. "That sounds wonderful, Penny! We'd love to hear it!"

Penny felt her confidence growing like a warm feeling in her chest. She cleared her throat and began to sing in her sweet parrot voice:

"Clean up, clean up, wipe away the mess!
Clean up, clean up, we can do our best!
Scrub and wipe and make it shine,
Working together, everything's fine!"

Emma giggled and started wiping the table while Penny sang. Even baby Sam clapped his tiny hands!

"That was amazing!" said Emma. "Can you teach me the song?"

Penny's feathers puffed up with pride. "Of course! We can take turns singing different parts!"

From that day on, Penny became the family's official cleanup song leader. When someone spilled something, Penny would sing her special song. Sometimes Emma would sing the first part, and Penny would sing the second part. Sometimes Dad would whistle along while Mom and Emma cleaned.

But Penny's biggest surprise came the next week. Mrs. Henderson from next door visited during breakfast.

"I heard the most beautiful singing coming from your dining room," Mrs. Henderson said. "It made me smile so much!"

Mom explained about Penny's cleanup song. Mrs. Henderson's eyes sparkled.

"Would Penny like to sing for the children at the community center? We're having a fun day next Saturday, and the kids would love to learn a cleanup song!"

Penny's heart raced with excitement and nervousness. Singing for her family was one thing, but singing for lots of children?

"I... I think I can do it!" Penny said, surprising herself with how brave she sounded.

On Saturday, Penny sat proudly on a special perch at the community center. Twenty children sat in a circle, looking up at her with bright, curious eyes.

"Clean up, clean up, wipe away the mess!" Penny sang with confidence.

Soon, all the children were singing along, cleaning up their toys and laughing together.

After the song, a little boy raised his hand. "Miss Penny, you're the best singing teacher ever!"

Penny's heart felt as warm as sunshine. She had learned that sharing her ideas, even when she felt scared, could bring joy to others and help her community.

"Remember," Penny told the children, "everyone has special talents to share. Be brave and let your ideas shine!"

The End.
