# Nutmeg's Treehouse Picnic Plan

Nutmeg was a small, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail. She lived in a cozy treehouse at the top of a big oak tree. The treehouse smelled like pinecones and warm wood. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. A soft breeze whispered, “Shhh,” through the leaves.

One morning, Nutmeg said, “Today, I will host a picnic in my treehouse.” Her whiskers twitched with excitement. “I want to invite friends from many places in the forest. I want to learn about their foods and songs!”

Nutmeg took a deep breath. “I will make a plan.” She liked to plan. Planning helped her feel calm. She pulled out her picture list. It was a big leaf with drawings. She drew boxes for: Invitations, Food, Music, Seats, and Thanks.

“First, invitations,” she said. She wrote with a tiny acorn pencil, then she read gently, “Dear friends, please come to my picnic. Bring a snack you love from your home. We will share, listen, and learn.” She tied each note with a grass string and scampered off.

She gave a note to Poppy the rabbit, who wore a bright scarf. “I will bring carrot pancakes from my burrow,” Poppy said, ears bouncing. “They are soft and sweet!”

She gave a note to Lilo the chipmunk, who came from the pine grove. “I will bring pine nut rice,” Lilo chirped. “It smells nutty and warm.”

She gave a note to Koko the blue jay, who lived near the river. “I will bring river berry tea,” Koko squawked. “It is tart and cool!”

She gave a note to Tavi the turtle, who lived by the pond reeds. “I will bring reed crackers,” Tavi said slowly. “They are crunchy and salty.”

Nutmeg’s heart felt warm. “So many yummy foods,” she whispered. She tapped her plan. “Next, food and seats.” She stacked little leaf plates. She set bark trays. She made soft moss pillows for seats. She hummed a happy tune.

But as Nutmeg worked, clouds gathered. A chill breeze blew. A leaf fell on her nose. “Oh!” she squeaked. “What if it rains? Will my picnic be okay?”

Nutmeg felt her chest flutter. She placed a paw on her heart and said, “Calm and plan.” She took three slow breaths. In... and out. In... and out. In... and out. She looked around her treehouse and smiled. “I can hang the dandelion curtains inside and make space.” She moved the table to the corner. She rolled up a little rug. She set a basket by the door for wet leaves.

Tap-tap! Someone knocked. Poppy hopped in with a warm basket. “Carrot pancakes!” The sweet smell filled the room.

“Welcome,” Nutmeg said. “Please put them on the bark tray.”

Flap-flap! Koko flew in with a teapot that jingled. “River berry tea!” The air smelled like blueberries and mint.

“Welcome,” Nutmeg said. “Please set it by the cups.”

Scurry-scurry! Lilo zipped in with a tiny pot. “Pine nut rice!” It smelled toasted and cozy.

“Welcome,” Nutmeg said. “Right there, please.”

Step-step. Tavi came slowly, smiling. “Reed crackers.” Crunch, crunch, they sounded dry and good.

“Welcome,” Nutmeg said. “Come sit on a moss pillow.”

Soon the treehouse was full of friends and foods. Raindrops began to patter on the roof. Pit-pat, pit-pat. The sound was gentle, like a lullaby.

Nutmeg clapped her paws. “Thank you for bringing food from your homes. I want to learn about each snack. Let’s take turns. We will listen with kind ears.”

Poppy went first. “In my burrow, my grandma sings while we cook,” she said. “We grate carrots, we add warm spice, and we flip the pancakes.” She handed out tiny triangles. “We say, ‘Share the sweet!’”

Everyone tasted. “Mmm,” said Koko. “Soft and cinnamon!” Nutmeg wrote “Share the sweet” on her leaf list.

Lilo held her pot. “In the pine grove, we hum the wind song.” He spooned rice into leaf cups. “We roast pine nuts, and we stir, stir, stir.” The rice tasted nutty and warm. “We say, ‘Wind brings us together.’”

Tavi raised a cracker. “At the pond, we sit very still first,” he said. “We listen to frogs. Then we bake reed crackers in the sun. We say, ‘Crunch with care.’”

Koko poured berry tea. “By the river, we pick berries and thank the water,” he said. The tea was bright and cool. “We say, ‘Sip and sing.’” He sang a short river song. It sounded like splish and sparkle. Everyone smiled.

Nutmeg’s tail swished happily. She learned new words, new tastes, and new songs. Her treehouse felt full of love and different ways of doing things.

Suddenly, the rain grew louder. Drum-drum on the roof. A drop slipped in by the window. “Oh no,” Poppy said. “Will the food get wet?”

Nutmeg held up a paw. “Calm and plan,” she said softly. She took a slow breath. “We can move the trays to the middle. We can spread this big leaf as a cover.” With teamwork, they scooted the trays and tucked the leaf over them. The food stayed dry. Everyone cheered.

After they ate, Nutmeg said, “Let’s make a thank-you circle.” They held paws, wings, and flippers. “Today I learned that we all have special foods and special ways. When we listen, we understand. When we plan, we feel safe. When we stay calm, we solve problems.”

Poppy nodded. “We shared the sweet.”

Lilo smiled. “The wind brought us together.”

Tavi crunched. “We crunched with care.”

Koko chirped. “We sipped and sang.”

Nutmeg’s eyes shone. “Different is special. Your foods and songs make the forest bright. My plan helped, and our calm hearts helped too.”

The rain slowed to a soft hush. The treehouse felt warm and bright. Nutmeg wrote one more line on her list: “We learn when we share.”

“Next time,” Nutmeg said, “we can visit each of your homes and learn more.”

“Yes!” everyone said.

They hugged. The air smelled like berries, spice, and wood. The sky peeked blue again. Nutmeg waved from her cozy door. Her heart was full and calm.

The End.
