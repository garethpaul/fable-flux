# Clover the Cub and the Guest Room Galaxy

Clover was a friendly bear cub with soft, warm fur and bright, curious eyes. Today, Clover padded into the guest room. The room smelled like clean blankets and lemon soap. Sunlight made soft squares on the rug. A tidy bed waited with two fluffy pillows. A shiny lamp sat on the bedside table.

“Hello, guest room,” Clover whispered. “You look calm and cozy.”

Mama Bear peeked in. “Clover, we will have a visitor soon,” she said gently. “Please help by doing some chores in here. Remember to be safe and respect our guest’s things.”

Clover nodded. “I can do it! I will use my big imagination and my smart thinking.”

Mama smiled. “Wonderful. First, please pick up the little toys from the floor. Then dust the table. After that, put the extra blankets in the basket.”

When Mama left, Clover took a deep breath. The rug felt soft under Clover’s paws. “I can make this fun,” Clover said. “I can make this safe.” Clover looked around and saw a toy rocket, a tiny car, and a red scarf left behind.

“Guest Room, do you want to be a space station?” Clover asked.

The lamp seemed to wink. Clover giggled. “Three, two, one—blast off!”

In Clover’s mind, the bed became a gentle spaceship with fluffy pillow clouds. The lamp became a guiding star. The dust cloth was a friendly comet tail. But Clover remembered Mama’s words. “I will not jump on the bed,” Clover said. “Spaceships are for resting, and we keep guests safe.”

Clover crawled on the rug instead and picked up the tiny car. “Car, you are a moon rover,” Clover said. “You go in the toy garage.” Clover placed the car in a small basket. Next, Clover picked up the toy rocket. “Rocket, you need a safe launch pad.” Clover set it on a shelf where it would not fall.

Clover saw the red scarf. It looked like a shooting star. Clover held it carefully. The scarf felt smooth and cool. “This is not mine,” Clover said softly. “It might be our guest’s. I will respect it.” Clover folded the scarf and placed it neatly on the dresser.

Clover picked up the dust cloth. “Hello, Comet,” Clover said. “We will swish and whoosh!” Clover dusted slowly, watching tiny sparkles of dust swirl in the sun. “Whoosh, whoosh,” Clover sang. “Good job, Comet.” Clover’s nose twitched. The air smelled fresh.

Next, Clover looked at the extra blankets. They were soft as clouds. “Clouds belong in the basket,” Clover said. Clover folded each one. “Fold, pat, tuck,” Clover hummed. The basket filled with puffy, neat clouds.

Clover stopped and thought. “What else?” Clover used careful thinking. The bedside table had a glass of water. It was near the edge. “Hmm,” Clover said, tapping a paw. “If it falls, it could spill.” Clover moved the glass to the middle of the table. “Safe and steady,” Clover said.

Clover checked the window. It was closed and locked. “Good,” Clover nodded. “Windows stay closed when we are inside alone.”

Clover looked at the bed. The pillow looked like a sleepy moon. Clover smoothed the blanket and made tiny pats. “Sweet dreams, space traveler,” Clover whispered to the imaginary guest.

Just then, Mama Bear came back. She sniffed the lemony air. “It smells clean,” she said. She looked around. Toys were in their basket. The scarf was folded. The table was dusted. The blankets were in their basket.

“Clover,” Mama said, smiling, “you did your chores with care. You kept things safe. You respected our guest’s scarf.”

Clover’s ears warmed with pride. “I used my imagination,” Clover said. “The room became a space station! But I remembered the rules. I did not jump on the bed. I moved the water. I folded the clouds… I mean, blankets!”

Mama laughed softly. “Imagination makes work fun,” she said. “And thinking helps us do things safely and kindly.”

Clover bounced a little, paws tapping the rug. “Can I welcome our visitor?” Clover asked.

“Yes,” said Mama. “How will you greet them?”

Clover thought again. “I will smile and say, ‘Welcome to your cozy space!’ And I will ask, ‘Do you need water or a blanket?’”

“That is respectful,” Mama said. “Our guest will feel happy.”

Clover looked around one more time. The room felt bright and peaceful. Clover listened. The house was quiet except for the soft tick-tock of the hall clock. Clover felt calm inside, like a quiet night sky.

Mama gave Clover a hug. “Thank you, helper.”

Clover hugged back. “I learned a lesson today,” Clover said. “Imagination can turn chores into an adventure. Respect means taking care of other people’s things. Safety means thinking before we act. When we use our minds and our hearts, we help.”

Mama nodded. “Yes, little star.”

Clover smiled at the guest room galaxy. “Shine on,” Clover whispered.

The End.
