# Nutkin's Wonderful Day of Care

In a sunny backyard filled with tall oak trees and colorful flowers, lived a clever little squirrel named Nutkin. His fluffy gray tail twitched with excitement as the warm morning sun tickled his whiskers.

"What a beautiful day!" Nutkin chirped, stretching his tiny paws high above his head. "But oh my, I feel so tired and messy."

Nutkin looked down at his fur, which was covered in dirt from yesterday's acorn-hunting adventure. His little tummy rumbled loudly. "Grumble, grumble, GRUMBLE!"

"I know what I need to do," Nutkin said thoughtfully. "I need to take care of myself first!"

He scampered down to the birdbath, where the clear water sparkled like diamonds in the sunlight. Splash, splash! Nutkin washed his face and combed his tail with his tiny claws until it was fluffy and clean.

"Ah, that feels so much better!" he said, admiring his reflection in the water.

Next, Nutkin found some fresh berries and crunchy nuts for breakfast. He ate slowly, savoring each bite. "Mmm, delicious! My body needs good food to feel strong," he said, patting his happy tummy.

Just then, Nutkin heard beautiful music floating through the air. "La-la-la-la!" It was his neighbor, Maria the mockingbird, singing a cheerful Spanish song while she cleaned her nest.

"¡Buenos días, Nutkin!" Maria called out. "Good morning!"

"Good morning, Maria! Your song sounds so lovely," Nutkin replied. "But I notice your nest looks a bit messy. Would you like some help?"

Maria's eyes sparkled with gratitude. "¡Sí! Yes, please! That would be wonderful."

Nutkin felt energized and ready to help because he had taken care of himself first. Together, they picked up fallen twigs and arranged them neatly. Maria taught Nutkin some Spanish words while they worked.

"Rama means branch," Maria sang. "And hoja means leaf!"

"Rama! Hoja!" Nutkin repeated happily, his tail swishing with joy.

Soon, their friend Chen the chipmunk joined them. "Nǐ hǎo! Hello!" Chen said in Chinese. "What are you doing?"

"We're cleaning up the backyard together!" Nutkin explained. "Want to help make our home beautiful for everyone?"

"Yes!" Chen squeaked excitedly. He taught them that "tree" in Chinese is "shù."

The three friends worked together, singing songs in different languages. They picked up scattered acorn shells, arranged pretty rocks around the flower bed, and even built a small shelter for the garden beetles.

As they worked, Nutkin realized something important. "You know," he said, wiping his brow, "I feel so good because I took care of myself this morning. When I'm clean, fed, and rested, I have energy to help my friends and our community!"

Maria nodded wisely. "¡Exacto! Exactly! When we care for ourselves, we can care for others too."

Chen added, "And when we all work together, sharing our different languages and ideas, we make everything better!"

As the sun reached high in the sky, the backyard looked more beautiful than ever. The friends sat together under the oak tree, sharing stories and learning new words from each other.

"Taking care of myself helps me take care of my friends and our home," Nutkin said with a big smile. "That's what being a good neighbor means!"

The three friends hugged, their hearts full of joy and their backyard sparkling clean in the golden sunshine.

The End.
