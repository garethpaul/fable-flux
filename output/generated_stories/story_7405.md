# The Fair Share in the Study Corner

In a quiet forest cottage, there was a cozy study corner with a small table, soft pillows, and a basket of bright crayons. A gentle deer fawn named Fern loved to sit there. The air smelled like fresh paper and pine. Sunlight made warm stripes on the floor.

Fern’s big ears twitched as she set out her things. “Today I will draw,” she whispered. Her hooves tapped softly. She hummed a happy tune and opened her sketchbook.

Clip-clop. Two friends came in: Tippy the squirrel and Bramble the bear cub. Their paws rustled like leaves on the soft rug. Tippy’s eyes were shiny. Bramble’s nose wiggled at the crayon smells.

“I want the blue crayon!” said Tippy, swishing his tail.

“I want the blue crayon too,” said Bramble, thumping his paw.

Fern looked at the basket. Only one blue crayon. Her tummy fluttered. “We can share,” she said in a small voice.

“No, I want it first!” Tippy grabbed the crayon.

Bramble reached out. “That’s not fair,” he rumbled, frowning.

The study corner did not feel cozy now. The air felt tight. The happy hum in Fern’s heart went quiet. She took a slow breath, like Mama Deer had taught her. In, out. In, out.

Fern remembered what Mama Deer said: “Justice means fair for everyone. We use gentle words. We keep everyone safe. We listen.”

Fern lifted her head. “Friends,” she said softly, “our study corner is a safe place. We have a problem. We both want the blue crayon. Let’s be fair and kind.”

Tippy squeezed the crayon. “But I need the blue for the sky!”

Bramble’s ears drooped. “I need the blue for my river.”

Fern nodded. “I hear you. Sky and river are important. Let’s make a plan.” She pointed to a small sand timer on the shelf. The sand was pale and soft like sugar. “We can take turns with the timer. Two turns each.”

Tippy twitched. “What if my sky is not done?”

Fern smiled. “Then we can ask for help. We can blend colors. Blue with white makes a soft sky. Blue with green makes river shadows. We can use our ideas.”

Bramble sniffed. “Sharing feels fair.”

Fern set the timer. “Tippy, you go first for one turn. Bramble, you can choose another color while you wait. Maybe green leaves? I will draw a path in brown and count the sand. We will all be safe and calm.”

Tippy looked at the timer. The sand started to fall with a soft hush. He held the crayon gently. “Okay,” he said. “I will be careful.”

Bramble chose a green crayon. “I will draw trees.”

The study corner felt warm again. Fern listened to the soft scratch-scratch of crayons. “Tippy, your sky looks wide,” she said. “Bramble, your trees look strong.”

The sand ran out with a tiny whisper. “Time,” Fern said. “Let’s be fair. Now it is Bramble’s turn with blue.”

Tippy paused. He looked at Bramble. He looked at Fern. He put the blue crayon in the basket and took out a white one. “I can make clouds,” he said. “Clouds help the sky.”

Bramble’s face brightened. “Thank you.” He drew the river, swish, swish, swish. The blue felt cool and smooth in his paw.

Fern kept the timer going. She took another slow breath to keep the study corner calm. “We can also make a rule for safety,” she said. “Crayons stay on the table. Paws stay gentle. Words stay kind.”

Tippy nodded. “Kind words.”

Bramble nodded too. “Gentle paws.”

The sand fell. “Time,” Fern said again. “Now we switch back.”

They followed the plan. The study corner filled with color and soft sounds. Sunlight moved across the table like a golden blanket. Fern’s shoulders relaxed. Her heart hummed again.

Soon, three pictures were done. Tippy held up a sky with fluffy clouds. Bramble held up a river with tall trees. Fern held up a path with tiny pawprints.

Tippy giggled. “My sky has a bird!”

Bramble grinned. “My river has a fish!”

Fern smiled. “My path leads to us.”

Bramble looked at Tippy. “I am glad we shared.”

Tippy looked at Fern. “Your plan felt fair.”

Fern nodded. “That is justice. Justice means fair for everyone. We listen. We take turns. We keep each other safe. When we are fair, everyone can make something beautiful.”

Bramble placed his picture next to Fern’s. “Our pictures fit together,” he said.

Tippy placed his picture on the other side. “Sky, trees, river, and path. All together.”

Fern’s eyes sparkled. The study corner felt peaceful and bright. The air smelled like crayons and pine again. Her ears relaxed. “I am proud of us,” she said. “We used our words. We made a plan. We were kind and safe.”

They cleaned up, tap-tap, rustle-rustle. Crayons went back into the basket. The timer went back on the shelf. Fern stretched and took one last slow breath.

“Justice is fair,” Fern said. “Fairness makes friends.”

“Fairness feels good,” said Bramble.

“Fairness helps everyone,” said Tippy.

They bumped paws and tail and hoof, gentle and happy. Then they hung their pictures on the wall in a bright, even line.

The study corner glowed with color, and their hearts glowed too.

The End.
