# Buckle-Up Bubbles

Ollie was a playful otter with bright eyes and soft, shiny fur. Today, Ollie hopped into the family car with Mama Otter and Grandpa Otter. The car smelled like pine from a little tree freshener, and the seats felt smooth and cool. Sunlight made warm stripes on the floor mats.

“Click, clack, check!” Grandpa sang from the front seat. He wore a funny hat with a tiny fish pin on it. “That’s our family travel song.”

Ollie wiggled with excitement. “Click, clack, check!” he echoed, tail swishing.

Mama smiled. “Before we go, we follow directions. First, sit in your seat. Then, buckle up. That keeps our bodies safe and strong.”

Ollie climbed into his booster seat. The buckle was shiny and red. He reached with his little paws. “I can try,” he said. His paws felt the cool metal. Click! The buckle snapped. Clack! The strap slid snug. “Check?” he asked, looking up.

Mama gently tugged the belt. “Check! Great job using your own paws. That’s called taking initiative.”

Ollie’s whiskers twitched proudly. “I did it!”

Grandpa started the car. The engine hummed like a sleepy bee. “On our rides,” Grandpa said, “we have our healthy-habits game. We drink water, take calm breaths, and use kind bodies.”

Ollie clapped. “Game time!”

Mama passed a small water bottle back. It was cool and smooth, with a blue lid that squeaked a little. “Sip, sip, pause,” she said. “Little sips help our bodies feel good.”

Ollie took a tiny sip. Cold water kissed his tongue. “Mmm. Sip, sip, pause,” he repeated, closing his eyes to feel it.

They rolled down the street. Trees waved. The sky was bright and wide. From the radio, soft music tinkled like raindrops. Grandpa kept a steady pace. “Eyes forward, paws on the wheel,” he said. “We follow the road rules.”

Ollie pressed his paw pads to his chest. “I can follow rules too,” he said.

“Let’s practice our calm breaths,” Mama said. “In through the nose like you smell a flower, out through the mouth like you blow a bubble.”

Ollie sniffed. He smelled the pine and a hint of orange from Mama’s hand lotion. “Flower,” he whispered. Then he blew, slow and gentle. “Bubble.” He imagined a big, shiny bubble floating up, up, up.

“Perfect,” Mama said. “That helps our bodies stay calm.”

Suddenly, Ollie saw a snack bag peeking from under the seat. He remembered the crunchy treats inside. His tummy made a tiny grumble. He reached down.

The buckle tugged him back. The strap held him safe. Ollie frowned. “I want the snack,” he said. His voice wobbled a little.

Grandpa peeked in the mirror. “We have a tradition,” he said kindly. “In our family, we wait for the park to eat snacks so we don’t drop crumbs in the car. Then we all sing the clean-paws song.”

Ollie’s ears perked. “A song?”

Mama nodded. “Yes. We follow directions to keep the car tidy and our tummies happy. Can you take a breath and wait?”

Ollie thought. He wanted the snack now. But he remembered the shiny bubble. He sniffed the pine. “Flower,” he whispered. He blew out. “Bubble.” He hugged his belly. “I can wait,” he said.

The car turned. Tires hummed on the road. Houses drifted by like little boxes. Ollie’s tail thumped softly on the seat. He took another sip of water. “Sip, sip, pause,” he said, feeling proud.

They reached a red light. The car stopped with a gentle sigh. A big green sign glowed ahead. “Park next,” Grandpa said.

Ollie bounced a tiny bit, then caught himself. “Kind body,” he reminded. He rested his paws and looked out the window. A dog trotted by with a shiny collar. A baby laughed in a stroller. Ollie smiled and waved.

At last, the car turned into the park lot. Trees rustled. Birds chirped like tiny bells. Grandpa parked, and the engine went quiet.

“Now we follow our steps,” Mama said. “First, unbuckle. Then, open the door with help. Then, snack.”

Ollie nodded. He pressed the button. The buckle popped free. He felt warm air touch his fur as the door opened. He stretched his legs. The grass smelled fresh and tickly.

Grandpa brought out the snack bag and a small bottle of hand spray. “Clean paws first,” he said. “This is our family tradition.”

Ollie held out his paws. Spritz! The spray felt cool and smelled like lemons. He rubbed his paws together. “Clean, clean, clean,” he sang.

Then Grandpa began the song. “Click, clack, check, clean paws on deck!”

Ollie giggled and joined in. “Click, clack, check, clean paws on deck!”

Mama handed him crunchy carrot sticks and sweet apple slices. Ollie took a bite. Crunch! The carrot snapped. Juice from the apple tickled his chin. “Yum,” he said. “I waited, and now my tummy is happy.”

Grandpa nodded. “You followed directions. You took calm breaths. You used your own paws. That’s healthy habit power.”

Ollie puffed with pride. “Healthy habit power!”

They finished their snacks and tossed the peels in the bin. Ollie helped by holding the bag. “I like helping,” he said. “It feels good.”

On the ride home, the car felt cozy. The music tinkled softly. Ollie buckled himself again. Click, clack, check. He took a sip of water. Sip, sip, pause. He breathed in flower, out bubble. His eyes grew sleepy.

“Grandpa,” Ollie murmured, “can we sing our song again?”

All together they sang, “Click, clack, check, clean paws on deck!”

Ollie smiled. He learned that healthy habits keep him safe, calm, and strong, and that family traditions help him remember what to do.

The End.
