# Polly's Laundry Room Band

Polly was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a shiny red beak. She loved making music. One sunny afternoon, she flapped into the laundry room. It smelled like clean soap and warm cotton. The washing machine hummed, “Hmmm,” and the dryer went, “Whirr, whirr.”

“Listen!” Polly chirped. “The machines are singing!”

Polly bobbed her head. Tap, tap went her claws on the tile floor. She pecked gently on a metal laundry basket. Ting! She shook a box of clothespins. Clickety-click! She strummed a string from a hoodie that was hanging. Twang!

“Polly makes music!” she sang. “Hmmm, whirr, ting, click, twang!”

Just then, Mia walked in. Mia was small, with curly hair and a big smile. She held a basket filled with soft towels. “Hi, Polly,” she said. “I’m helping Daddy do the laundry today. We have to follow directions.”

Polly puffed up with pride. “Polly helps! Music helps!”

Mia giggled. “Music is fun. But we need to listen to the laundry rules. Daddy said, ‘Sort the clothes, wash on gentle, and don’t pull the strings.’”

Polly tilted her head. “No pull strings?” She looked at the hoodie string she had strummed. Twang? Her feathers drooped a little.

Mia patted Polly’s wing. “It’s okay. We can still make music. We just have to find a safe way.”

Polly perked up. “Safe music!” She tapped the basket again. Ting!

They started sorting. Mia held up a red sock. “Red goes here.” She made a tiny mountain of red clothes. Polly added a red washcloth, saying, “Red here!”

Mia held up a white shirt. “White goes here.” The shirt smelled like sunshine. Polly sniffed. “Mmm! Fresh!”

They worked together. The washing machine hummed loudly. Hmmm. The hum tickled the floor. Polly felt it in her feet. She bobbed again. “Song, song!”

Mia smiled. “After we sort, we can sing. But first, directions.” She pointed to a list on the wall with pictures. The list showed: a basket, a washing machine, a measuring cup, and a timer.

“First basket,” Mia said. “Then wash.” She poured a little soap. The soap smelled like lemons. “Gentle wash,” she said, pressing a button. Beep!

Polly loved the beep. “Beep, beep!” she repeated. She wanted to peck the buttons too.

But when she fluttered closer, Mia said, “Wait, Polly. The rule says, ‘Only a grown-up presses buttons.’”

Polly’s wings drooped again. She wanted to help. She wanted to play. She felt wiggly inside. “Polly sad,” she said softly.

Mia saw Polly’s sad eyes. She thought and thought. Then she smiled. “We can make music together, but we must follow directions. Let’s make a laundry band with safe instruments. We can compromise.”

“Com-pro-mise?” Polly repeated slowly.

“It means we find a way that works for both of us,” Mia said. “You want music. I want to follow the rules. We can do both!”

Polly’s eyes sparkled. “Both!”

Mia looked around. “We can use the empty plastic tub for a drum.” Thum-thum! “We can shake this closed box of clothespins like a shaker.” Shake-a-shake! “We can tap the folded towels gently.” Puff-puff! “And we can clap our hands.” Clap-clap!

Polly flapped happily. “Polly drum! Polly shake!”

But when Polly tried to peck the metal hose, Mia quickly said, “Wait. That is not safe. It’s not on the list. Let’s choose a safe sound.”

Polly paused. She took a deep breath. “Compromise,” she whispered. She looked at a wooden spoon on the shelf. “Spoon tap?” she asked.

Mia checked the list and nodded. “A wooden spoon is okay. Tap the spoon on the empty tub.”

Polly tapped. Tok-tok! It made a friendly sound.

Together, they made a plan. Mia pointed to the list again. “Sort, wash, then music.” They sang their steps:

“Sort-sorts, wash-wash,
Follow rules, no rush-rush.
Beep and hum, clap and strum,
Laundry band, here we come!”

When the washing machine started, it went “Hmmm.” Polly matched the hum with a low “Hmmm.” Mia shook the clothespin box. Shake-a-shake! Polly tapped the tub. Tok-tok! Mia clapped. Clap-clap! The room filled with happy sounds. The lemon smell and warm air wrapped around them like a hug.

They took turns leading. Sometimes Mia said, “Quiet now,” when Daddy walked by with a sleepy baby. They both hushed and listened to the soft dryer, whirr, whirr. Then Mia whispered, “Now soft music,” and they played very gently. Polly loved the whispery music. She felt proud. She was following directions and still making art.

At last, the timer went, Beep! The wash was done. Mia opened the washer. Steam puffed out, warm and damp. Polly sighed, “Ahhh.”

They moved the clean clothes to the dryer. Mia said, “Towels first.” Polly handed her one towel at a time with her beak. “Thank you, Polly,” Mia said. “You are a great helper.”

When the dryer started, Polly didn’t peck the buttons. She stood by the tub and tapped instead. Tok-tok-tok. She had learned to wait and to choose safe sounds.

Mia grinned. “We did it! We followed directions and made music.”

Polly fluffed her feathers proudly. “Com-pro-mise!” she said. “Polly music. Mia rules. Both happy.”

They sang a gentle goodbye song to the humming machines:

“Hum and whirr, we hear you play.
We worked safe in a careful way.
We listened, shared, and took our turns.
Compromise is what we learned.”

Polly nuzzled Mia’s shoulder. The laundry room felt cozy and calm. “Polly and Mia, laundry band,” she chirped.

Mia hugged her. “Best band ever.”

The End.
