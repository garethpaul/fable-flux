# Joey's Big Sorry

The warm morning sun shone on the peaceful front porch where Joey the kangaroo lived with his mama. Joey loved bouncing around the wooden boards, but today something felt different.

"Good morning, little one," Mama Kangaroo said softly. "Mrs. Possum from next door is coming over for tea."

Joey's ears perked up. He loved visitors! He bounced excitedly, his fluffy tail wiggling with joy.

Soon, Mrs. Possum arrived carrying a beautiful basket of fresh flowers. "These are for you, dear," she said to Mama Kangaroo. The flowers smelled like sweet honey and sunshine.

Joey wanted to see the pretty flowers up close. He bounced higher and higher, trying to peek into the basket.

"Careful, Joey," Mama warned gently.

But Joey was too excited. BOUNCE! BOUNCE! CRASH! His big hop knocked right into Mrs. Possum. The beautiful flowers scattered all over the porch floor.

"Oh no!" Mrs. Possum gasped. Her special flowers were everywhere - some with broken stems, others with crushed petals.

Joey's tummy felt funny. He saw Mrs. Possum's sad face and his mama's worried expression. The porch that had been so peaceful now looked messy.

"I... I..." Joey started to say, but then he hopped away to hide behind the rocking chair.

From his hiding spot, Joey watched Mrs. Possum sadly pick up her flowers. Some were too broken to save. His heart felt heavy like a rock.

"Joey," Mama called softly. "Can you come here, please?"

Joey's little paws trembled as he hopped out slowly. He looked at the mess he had made.

"Mrs. Possum worked very hard in her garden to grow these flowers," Mama explained. "She wanted to share something beautiful with us."

Joey looked at Mrs. Possum's kind eyes. Even though she was sad, she wasn't angry. That made Joey feel even worse.

"I'm sorry," Joey whispered, so quietly it sounded like the wind. "I'm really, really sorry, Mrs. Possum."

Mrs. Possum knelt down to Joey's level. "Thank you for saying sorry, little one. That takes courage."

"I wanted to see your pretty flowers, but I bounced too hard," Joey said, his voice getting stronger. "I didn't mean to hurt them."

"I know you didn't mean it," Mrs. Possum smiled gently. "Accidents happen when we get excited."

Joey's ears drooped. "Can I help fix them?"

"What a wonderful idea!" Mama Kangaroo said proudly.

Together, they carefully sorted the flowers. Joey used his gentle paws to arrange the good ones in a glass of water. Mrs. Possum showed him how to trim the broken stems to help them heal.

"You know," Mrs. Possum said as they worked, "helping each other is what makes our neighborhood special. When we make mistakes, we can always make things better."

Joey nodded seriously. "Like being good neighbors?"

"Exactly! Good neighbors help each other all around the world," Mrs. Possum explained. "Saying sorry and helping fix problems makes everyone feel better."

As they finished arranging the rescued flowers, Joey felt the heavy feeling in his tummy disappear. The porch looked beautiful again, and Mrs. Possum was smiling.

"Thank you for helping me, Joey," Mrs. Possum said. "And thank you for your honest apology. That means more to me than perfect flowers."

Joey bounced gently this time, careful not to knock anything over. "Next time I'll ask before I bounce near special things!"

Mama Kangaroo hugged them both. "Now, who's ready for that tea?"

As they sat together on the peaceful porch, Joey learned that saying sorry wasn't scary at all. It actually felt wonderful to help make things right again.

The End.
