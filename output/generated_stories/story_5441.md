# Polly's Big Voice

In a busy basement filled with colorful boxes and humming machines, lived Polly the parrot. Polly had bright green feathers that shimmered like emeralds. Her orange beak was perfect for cracking nuts, and her dark eyes sparkled with curiosity.

The basement was always bustling with activity. Big washing machines went "whoosh-whoosh." Dryers tumbled clothes with a gentle "thump-thump-thump." Storage boxes were stacked high like colorful towers.

Polly loved to help sort things in the basement. She could carry small items in her strong beak. She could organize colorful buttons by color. But there was one big problem - nobody ever listened to Polly's ideas.

One busy morning, Mrs. Chen came downstairs carrying a heavy basket of laundry from many different countries. There were bright saris from India, soft kilts from Scotland, and beautiful dashikis from Ghana.

"Oh my," said Mrs. Chen, looking confused. "I need to wash these special clothes, but I don't know the right way. Each one needs different care."

Polly flapped her wings excitedly. She had been watching Mrs. Chen wash clothes for months! Polly knew exactly what to do. But when she tried to speak, her voice came out as a tiny whisper.

"Squawk," Polly said softly. Mrs. Chen didn't hear her.

Polly watched as Mrs. Chen picked up the delicate Indian sari. "Wait!" Polly tried to say, but again her voice was too quiet. She knew that beautiful fabric needed gentle, cold water.

Mrs. Chen reached for the hot water button. This wasn't fair! Polly knew the right answer, but nobody was listening to her good ideas.

Polly took a deep breath. She puffed out her bright chest feathers. She remembered what her mama had taught her: "When you have something important to say, use your big voice!"

"STOP!" Polly squawked loudly and clearly. "Cold water for the sari!"

Mrs. Chen jumped in surprise. She turned around and saw Polly perched on a washing machine, her wings spread wide.

"Oh my goodness, Polly! Did you just tell me how to wash this sari?" Mrs. Chen asked with wide eyes.

"Yes!" Polly said proudly, using her strong, clear voice. "Cold water keeps the pretty colors bright. Hot water makes them fade away."

Mrs. Chen smiled. "You're absolutely right! How did you know that?"

"I watch and learn," Polly explained. "The Scottish kilt needs warm water, not hot. And the African dashiki needs gentle soap because the patterns are hand-painted."

"Polly, you're amazing!" Mrs. Chen exclaimed. "I should have been listening to you all along. Will you help me take care of all these special clothes?"

Polly's heart felt warm and happy. "Yes! I love helping, and I love learning about clothes from around the world."

From that day forward, Mrs. Chen always listened carefully to Polly's ideas. Polly learned that using her big voice wasn't about being loud or rude - it was about speaking up when she had something important to share.

The basement became even busier, but now it was filled with teamwork. Polly would call out washing instructions in her clear, confident voice. Mrs. Chen would follow Polly's directions carefully. Together, they took perfect care of beautiful clothes from many different countries.

Polly learned that her voice mattered. When she spoke up with confidence and kindness, good things happened. She felt proud knowing that being assertive helped everyone.

The End.
