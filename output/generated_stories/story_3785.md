# Luna's Lullaby Practice

Luna was a gentle deer fawn with the softest brown fur and the biggest, kindest eyes. She lived in a cozy bedroom filled with fluffy pillows, warm blankets, and twinkling star lights on the ceiling. Luna loved her special room, but tonight she felt a little worried.

"Tomorrow is the Forest Talent Show," Luna whispered to herself, looking at her reflection in the shiny window. "All my friends will be there, and I want to sing a beautiful lullaby for them."

Luna had been practicing her lullaby for weeks, but sometimes her voice would shake, and she would forget the words. She took a deep breath and decided to try again.

"Twinkle, twinkle, little star," Luna began softly. But halfway through, she stumbled on the words. "Oh no," she sighed, her ears drooping. "I'll never get it right."

Just then, her friend Benny the bunny hopped through the bedroom door. His fluffy white tail wiggled as he bounced over to Luna.

"Hi Luna! I heard you practicing. Your voice sounds so sweet!" Benny said with a big smile.

"But I keep making mistakes," Luna said sadly. "Maybe I should just give up."

Benny's nose twitched thoughtfully. "You know what my mama always tells me? She says that practicing is like planting seeds in a garden. You have to water them every day, even when you can't see them growing."

Luna tilted her head. "Really?"

"Really!" Benny nodded. "And I have an idea. What if I help you practice? I could be your audience!"

Luna's eyes sparkled. She had never thought about asking for help before. "Would you really do that for me?"

"Of course! That's what friends are for," Benny said, settling onto a soft purple cushion. "Now, take your time. Remember, even the most beautiful flowers need lots of patience to bloom."

Luna felt warm and grateful. She stood up straight, took a deep breath that filled her chest with cool, calm air, and began again.

"Twinkle, twinkle, little star, how I wonder what you are..."

This time, when she forgot a word, Benny gently whispered it to her. When her voice shook, he gave her an encouraging nod. They practiced together as the moon rose higher in the sky, casting silver light through the bedroom window.

"Up above the world so high, like a diamond in the sky," Luna sang. Her voice grew stronger and more confident with each try.

"That was beautiful!" Benny clapped his little paws together. "See? Practice really does make everything better."

Luna felt proud and happy. "Thank you for helping me, Benny. I learned something important tonight."

"What's that?" Benny asked.

"That practicing takes patience, and it's okay to ask friends for help. When we work together, everything feels easier," Luna said, nuzzling Benny's soft fur.

The next evening at the Forest Talent Show, Luna sang her lullaby perfectly. Her voice was clear and sweet, like honey flowing from a jar. All her forest friends listened with wonder, and when she finished, they cheered and clapped.

Luna smiled, remembering Benny's words about planting seeds. With practice, patience, and the help of good friends, her little seed had grown into something beautiful.

The End.
