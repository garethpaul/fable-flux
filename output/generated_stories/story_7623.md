# Mabel Mouse Shares on the Porch

Mabel was a kind little mouse. She lived in a cozy house with a peaceful front porch. The porch had a soft green rug, a small round table, and a row of tiny hooks along the wall. The warm sun made the wood glow. A gentle breeze brought the smell of fresh flowers. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” and the porch creaked in a friendly way.

Every morning, Mabel swept the porch with her tiny broom. “Sweep, sweep,” she sang. She lined up her colorful buttons in a neat tin, stacked her storybooks on the little shelf, and put her paintbrushes in a jar on a hook. “Everything has a home,” she said. “When we are tidy, we can find things and share things!”

One sunny day, Mabel planned a porch art time. She wanted to paint. She set out paper, brushes, and a tray of bright paints. Red like strawberries. Blue like the sky. Yellow like warm sunshine. Mabel smiled. “I will practice painting flowers,” she said. “Practice helps me learn.”

Her friends came down the path. There was Bibi the bunny with long floppy ears. There was Gus the goose with soft white feathers. There was Ravi the raccoon with a striped tail. And there was Lila the ladybug with tiny shiny wings. They all looked different, and Mabel loved that. “We are all special,” she said. “We are all welcome on my porch.”

“Hello, friends!” Mabel squeaked. “Let’s paint together!”

Bibi sniffed the air. “Mmm, I smell sweet lemonade.”

Gus flapped his wings. “I hear the wind chimes sing!”

Ravi tapped the table. “I like how smooth this wood feels.”

Lila buzzed gently. “The colors look so bright.”

Mabel clapped her paws. “I have paper and brushes for you. Please find a seat.” She pointed to the hooks. “We hang aprons here, so our clothes stay clean.”

But when they started, there was a problem.

“Uh-oh,” said Bibi. “I don’t have a brush.”

“Me neither,” honked Gus. “No brush for me.”

Ravi looked around. “I only see two brushes, not four.”

Lila hovered near the paint. “And I need a tiny brush for tiny dots.”

Mabel blinked. She opened the jar. There were only two paintbrushes. “Oh dear,” she said softly. “I forgot to bring the extra brushes from my art box. I did not check my list.” She felt a little twist in her tummy.

Bibi’s ears drooped. Gus tucked his wings. Ravi’s tail swished. Lila’s wings went still. Everybody felt a little sad.

Mabel took a deep breath. She put her paw on her heart. “I am sorry, friends. I want us all to paint. Sharing is caring. I want to be generous.”

“What can we do?” asked Bibi.

Mabel looked at her neat porch. She looked at the hooks, the shelf, and the table. She had an idea. “We can solve this together,” she said. “We can share, and we can make a plan.”

First, Mabel made a list on a small notepad. She wrote: Brushes, Cups, Paper, Towels. She drew little pictures, too. “A list helps me remember,” she told her friends.

Next, she set a timer shaped like a carrot. “We will take turns,” she said. “Two friends paint, and two friends draw with crayons until the bell rings. Then we switch.” She put out a basket of crayons she kept on the lowest hook. “See? Things have homes, so they are easy to find and share.”

Bibi and Gus started with the brushes. “Swish, swish,” went the bristles. Bibi painted a purple flower. Gus painted a blue pond. Ravi and Lila used crayons. Ravi drew a striped tree. Lila drew tiny red dots like little berries.

When the carrot timer went “ding!”, they switched. Ravi held a brush like a wand. “I will practice circles,” he said. “Round and round.” Lila dipped the tip. “I will make teensy dots,” she whispered. “Tap, tap, tap.”

Mabel smiled. “I will share my brush too,” she said, handing it to Lila when she needed it. “We can paint together.” Mabel used a crayon to draw sunshine while she waited. The porch hummed with happy sounds: swish, tap, ding, and giggles.

After a while, Mabel remembered something. “Friends, I have extra brushes in my art box inside. I forgot to bring them out. Will you help me get them and put them in the right place?”

“Yes!” said everyone.

They walked inside. Mabel opened the art box. There were four small brushes and one tiny brush. Mabel held them like treasure. “These belong in the jar on the hook,” she said. “Let’s put them in their home.”

They returned to the porch. Mabel placed the brushes in the jar. “Now we have enough for all.” She poured more lemonade and set out napkins from the drawer by the door. “Thank you for being patient,” she said. “Thank you for helping me organize.”

They all painted together. The porch smelled like lemons and paint. The wind chimes rang. Bright colors danced on the paper. Bibi’s ears perked up. Gus hummed. Ravi’s tail flicked happily. Lila’s wings glowed in the sun.

When they finished, Mabel said, “Let’s clean up.” They washed brushes in a bowl. Swish, swish. They dried them with towels and put them back in the jar. They stacked the papers, wiped the table, and hung the aprons neatly on the hooks.

Mabel looked at her friends. “Today I learned something,” she said. “Being generous means sharing what I have and making sure everyone can join. Practice helped us paint better. Organization helped us find what we needed. And our different styles made beautiful art.”

Bibi nodded. “Sharing felt good.”

Gus smiled. “Taking turns worked.”

Ravi grinned. “Lists help!”

Lila chimed, “And tiny brushes help tiny dots.”

Mabel’s heart felt warm. “Next time, I will check my list first. I will bring enough for all. We can practice more, share more, and keep our porch tidy together.”

They taped their paintings along the porch rail. The colors fluttered like flags in the breeze. The front porch looked even more peaceful and proud.

Mabel poured the last lemonade. “To generous hearts,” she said.

“To generous hearts!” the friends cheered.

The End.
