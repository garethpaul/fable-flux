# Chirpy's Fair Play Discovery

Chirpy the singing bird lived in a cozy nest inside Dr. Kim's basement lab. The lab had bright white lights and tables full of colorful bottles. Chirpy loved to sing sweet songs while Dr. Kim worked.

One day, Chirpy heard sad chirping from outside the small basement window. She flew over and saw three young birds on the grass.

"What's wrong?" Chirpy called through the glass.

"The big birds won't let us play ball," said a small blue bird named Pip. "They say we're too little."

Chirpy's heart felt heavy. Through the window, she could see the big birds playing with a shiny red ball. The little birds sat sadly on a branch, watching.

"That's not fair!" Chirpy sang loudly. Her voice echoed in the lab.

Dr. Kim looked up from her work. "What's the matter, Chirpy?"

Chirpy flew to Dr. Kim's shoulder. "The big birds won't share! The little ones want to play too."

Dr. Kim smiled kindly. "What do you think we should do?"

Chirpy thought hard. Her tiny brain worked like the computers in the lab. "I need to help them! But how?"

"Sometimes," said Dr. Kim, "being fair means speaking up for others."

Chirpy nodded. She knew what to do! She flew to the window and called out, "Hey, big birds! Can I talk to you?"

The big birds stopped playing. "What do you want, little lab bird?"

Chirpy took a deep breath. Her chest puffed out with courage. "Everyone should get to play. It's more fun when we all play together!"

"They're too small," said the biggest bird. "They might get hurt."

Chirpy's eyes sparkled with an idea. "What if we make teams? Big birds and little birds together! That way, everyone stays safe AND has fun!"

The big birds looked at each other. They hadn't thought of that before.

"We could teach them how to play," suggested one big bird.

"And they could teach us their quick moves," said another.

Soon, all the birds were playing together! The big birds gently tossed the ball to the little ones. The little birds showed the big birds how to zip and zoom around the yard.

Chirpy watched from her window, singing a happy tune. Her song sounded like tiny bells ringing in the lab.

"Tweet-tweet-hooray! Playing fair makes the best day!" she sang.

Dr. Kim patted Chirpy's head gently. "You did something very important today, Chirpy. You stood up for what's right."

"What's that called?" Chirpy asked, tilting her small head.

"That's called justice," Dr. Kim explained. "Justice means making sure everyone is treated fairly."

Chirpy felt warm and proud inside. Her feathers fluffed up with happiness. "Justice feels good!"

From that day on, whenever Chirpy saw someone being left out, she would speak up. She learned that her voice, no matter how small, could make big changes.

The little birds visited Chirpy's window every day. They would play fair games together and share stories. The basement lab filled with the sound of happy chirping and Chirpy's joyful songs.

"Being fair makes everyone happy," Chirpy would sing. "And happy friends make the best days!"

Dr. Kim smiled as she worked, listening to all the birds playing together outside. She was proud of her brave little bird who learned that standing up for others is always the right thing to do.

The End.
