# Benny Beaver's Moving Morning

Benny Beaver sat at the big wooden dining room table, his fluffy brown tail twitching with excitement. The morning sun streamed through the window, making the room warm and bright. His breakfast bowl was empty, and his tummy felt full and happy.

"What should I do now?" Benny wondered aloud, looking around the cozy dining room.

Mama Beaver walked in, carrying a basket of fresh apples. "Good morning, my busy little beaver! Did you finish your breakfast?"

"Yes, Mama!" Benny said honestly. "I ate every bit of my oatmeal and berries. They were yummy and sweet!"

Mama Beaver smiled. "I'm proud of you for telling the truth, Benny. Now, what are your plans for today?"

Benny's eyes sparkled. "I want to tell stories! But first..." He paused and felt his body. "I feel wiggly inside, like I need to move around!"

"That sounds like your body is asking for exercise!" Mama said with a laugh. "Exercise helps us feel strong and happy."

Benny hopped up from his chair. "Can I exercise right here in the dining room?"

"Of course! Let me show you some fun ways to move," Mama said.

First, they did jumping jacks together. "One, two, three!" counted Benny as his arms went up and down like tree branches swaying in the wind. His heart went thump-thump-thump in his chest.

Next, they marched in place around the dining table. "Left, right, left, right!" Benny called out, lifting his knees high. The wooden floor made soft tapping sounds under their feet.

"Now let's do beaver stretches!" Mama suggested. They reached their arms up high like tall trees, then bent down to touch their toes like they were picking up sticks for their dam.

After ten minutes of moving, Benny felt wonderful. His cheeks were rosy pink, and he was breathing a little faster. "Exercise makes me feel so good inside!" he said honestly.

"Now you're ready for storytelling," Mama said, sitting down in her favorite chair.

Benny climbed onto the soft cushioned chair across from her. "I want to tell you a story about a beaver who learned something special today!"

"I'd love to hear it," Mama said, her eyes twinkling.

Benny cleared his throat importantly. "Once upon a time, there was a young beaver who felt wiggly after breakfast. His wise mama taught him that when our bodies feel restless, they're asking us to move and exercise!"

As Benny told his story, he used his hands to show the jumping jacks and marching. "The little beaver learned that exercise isn't just good for his body – it made his mind feel clear and ready for other fun activities like storytelling!"

Mama clapped her paws together. "What a wonderful story, Benny! And you managed your time so well today – exercise first, then storytelling."

Benny felt proud and happy. "Mama, I learned that being different is okay too. Some friends like to sit quietly, but I like to move around. That's what makes me special!"

"Exactly right, my dear beaver," Mama said, giving him a warm hug. "Every beaver is different, and that's what makes our family so wonderful."

Benny snuggled into the hug, feeling grateful for his moving morning in the dining room and the important lessons he had learned.

The End.
