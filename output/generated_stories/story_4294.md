# Bubbles on the Porch

Bubbles the dolphin loved to play. He splashed, he flipped, and he giggled with little squeaks. One sunny morning, Bubbles visited his friend Nia’s peaceful front porch. The porch was wide and cozy. A wind chime sang tinkle-tinkle songs. A lemony smell came from a cool pitcher of lemonade. The wood floor felt warm on Bubbles’s smooth tummy as he wiggled up beside a big blue pot of flowers.

“Hi, Nia!” squeaked Bubbles, waving a fin.

“Hi, Bubbles!” Nia smiled. “We can sit and sip lemonade. It’s quiet and nice.”

Bubbles wiggled with joy. “Let’s play a splash game!” he said. He puffed a tiny bubble from his blowhole. Pop! It sparkled in the sun.

Nia giggled. “Let’s use gentle bubbles. We don’t want to make a mess.”

“I can be gentle,” Bubbles said. He meant it with his whole dolphin heart. He wanted to do the right thing.

They took little sips. The lemonade was sweet and sour. The breeze was soft. The chimes sang. Birds peeped in the trees. Bubbles felt happy and safe on the peaceful porch.

A small voice called from the path. “Um… excuse me?”

Bubbles and Nia looked up. A shy snail in a tiny blue shell peered from the porch step. His name was Slo. He wore a leaf as a hat.

“Hello, Slo,” said Nia kindly. “Do you need help?”

Slo nodded slowly. “My shell is dusty. My leaf-hat feels heavy. I am trying to reach my sunflower friend, but the path is bumpy. I’m tired.”

Bubbles’s fin twitched. He loved to help. “I can help!” he squeaked. “I can wash your shell with gentle bubbles.”

Slo blinked. “Will it be soft and safe?”

“Yes,” Bubbles said. He remembered Nia’s words about gentle bubbles. He wanted to keep his promise. That is integrity—doing what you say, even when it is hard.

Bubbles puffed a tiny bubble. Pop! He puffed another, even softer. Pop-pop. He used his fin like a small brush and swished a little porch water across Slo’s shell. Swish, swish. The dust floated away. Slo’s shell gleamed in the sunlight.

“Oh!” said Slo. “That feels nice.”

Nia clapped softly. “Good helping, Bubbles.”

Bubbles smiled. Helping felt warm and bright in his chest.

Just then, a fluttery sound came from the porch rail. A little bluebird flapped and chirped, “Cheep-cheep! My string is tangled! It is stuck on the railing.”

Bubbles peeked. A thin string was looped around the railing and the bluebird’s foot. The bluebird looked worried. Her wings shook.

Bubbles wanted to rush and pull the string hard. But he remembered peaceful communication. He took a calm breath. In and out. He said, “It’s okay. We can fix it together. May I touch your string?”

The bluebird relaxed a little. “Yes, please,” she cheeped.

Nia held the string edge. “I will keep it steady,” she said. “Bubbles, you can untie the knot.”

Bubbles looked closely. The knot was tiny. He could smell the wood of the rail and the lemon from the pitcher. He moved his fin slow, slow, slow. He spoke gently. “I am going to lift this loop. Are you comfy?”

“I am okay,” said the bluebird.

Lift. Slide. Wiggle. The knot came loose. The string fell free.

“You did it!” chirped the bluebird. She hopped and fluffed her feathers. “Thank you for asking before touching my foot. That felt safe.”

“You’re welcome,” said Bubbles. His heart felt warm again. Helping with kind words made the porch feel even more peaceful.

Slo smiled under his leaf hat. “You helped me too. I feel strong now. But the path is still bumpy. I might tip.”

Bubbles thought. He wanted to keep his promise to be gentle. He also wanted Slo to reach the sunflower. “We can make a smooth path,” he said. “We can work together.”

Nia nodded. “Let’s use the small porch mat. We can lay it over the bumps.”

They talked kindly as they worked.

“Is this okay, Slo?” asked Nia.

“Yes,” said Slo. “Thank you for asking.”

Bubbles and Nia lifted the mat and placed it on the path edge. Slo slid along the mat with a soft shhhh sound. His shell shone like a blue marble. The sunflower waved in the breeze. “Hello, Slo!” the tall flower seemed to say as its yellow petals glowed.

Slo reached the sunflower and smiled. “I did it with your help!”

Bubbles squeaked with joy. He felt bouncy inside, but he stayed gentle on the porch. He kept his promise. He was honest and kind. He used soft words and careful fins.

The bluebird sang a happy song. The wind chime answered, tinkle-tinkle. The lemonade smelled sweet. The porch felt even more peaceful now that everyone felt safe and seen.

Nia poured tiny sips for everyone. “To helping others,” she said. “To honest promises. To kind talking.”

Bubbles lifted his fin like a cup. “To helping!” he said. He looked at Slo and the bluebird. “When we help, we all feel better.”

Slo nodded. “I will help someone next time.”

The bluebird chirped, “I will ask for help with calm words.”

Bubbles smiled. “Me too. Helping is a happy splash in the heart.”

They sat together on the peaceful front porch, listening to the breeze and the birds. The day felt bright and calm. Bubbles wiggled just a little and puffed one more tiny bubble.

Pop!

The End.
