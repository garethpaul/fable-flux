# Whiskers and the Big Bath Rescue

Whiskers was a clever cat with soft gray fur and bright green eyes. One sunny morning, he padded into the bathroom. The tiles were cool under his paws, smooth like little icy squares. He sniffed the air. It smelled like bubbly soap and sweet orange shampoo.

“Today,” Whiskers said to himself, “I will sail my boat!”

He pointed his paw at the bathtub. To Whiskers, the tub was not a tub at all. It was a wide blue ocean. The bath mat was a sandy beach. The towel on the hook? That was a tall white sail.

Whiskers hopped onto the rim of the tub. “Captain Whiskers is ready!” he meowed. He pushed a little plastic cup into the water. It was his boat. He dropped in a rubber ducky as his helper. “Quack if you see trouble,” he told the ducky.

He turned the faucet slowly. Warm water fell with a cheerful hiss. The tub filled with swirls and bubbles. “Listen to the ocean,” Whiskers purred. “Shhhhh.” He closed his eyes and heard the splash-splash and drip-drip. It sounded like tiny rain on a big sea.

Then—oh no! The plastic cup tipped and spun. It filled with too much water. Glug-glug-glug! It began to sink. The rubber ducky bumped the drain and got stuck.

“Trouble!” Whiskers cried. “Captain, we have a problem.”

His whiskers twitched. His tail flicked. He wanted to jump back and hide behind the laundry basket. But he took a deep breath. “I can be brave,” he told himself. “I can make a plan.”

He sat down on the bath mat beach and looked around. “What do I have?” he asked out loud.

He saw a big blue cup, a small spoon, a washcloth, and a long white towel. He touched the towel. It was soft and warm from the sunny window. He patted the washcloth. It was squishy and smelled like clean rain.

“Step one,” Whiskers said, tapping his paw. “Save the ducky. Step two: make a boat that does not sink. Step three: sail!”

He smiled. “Let’s start.”

Whiskers turned the faucet off. The hiss stopped. The bathroom was quiet except for a tiny drip-drip. He took the big blue cup and scooped water out of the tub. Splash! He poured it into the sink. He did it again. Scoop. Pour. Scoop. Pour.

His paws got wet. The water was warm and tickly between his toes. “I can do it,” he sang softly. “Scoop and pour, scoop and pour.” His song made the work feel light.

The water went down just enough. The rubber ducky wiggled free. “Quack!” Whiskers cheered. “You’re safe!”

Now for the boat. He looked at the small spoon and the washcloth. “Hmm,” he hummed. He balanced the washcloth on the big blue cup. He put the spoon like a mast. He needed a sail.

Whiskers eyed the long white towel. It was too big. He remembered the hand towel hanging low. It was small, just right! He folded it into a triangle and clipped it to the spoon with a hair clip he found on the counter. Click! The sail stood up.

“Good plan,” Whiskers said. “But will it float?”

He set the new boat gently on the water. It bobbed. It did not sink. He placed the rubber ducky on top. The boat wiggled, but stayed strong.

“Captain Whiskers, ready to sail again!” he announced.

He pushed the boat with one careful paw. The sail caught the little breeze from the open window. The boat glided across the tub ocean. Swish-swish. The rubber ducky looked very proud.

Whiskers felt proud, too. He had a warm glow inside, like a tiny sun. He smiled so wide his whiskers curved.

Just then, a drip from the faucet made a tiny wave. The boat bumped the tub wall and turned around. “Storm!” Whiskers giggled. “We need a lighthouse.”

He looked around and found a bright yellow sponge. He stood it up on the bath mat beach. “Be a lighthouse,” he told it. “Shine, shine!” He pretended the sponge glowed. In his mind, the sponge made a golden light that showed the safe way.

“Follow the light,” he told the ducky. He gave the boat a soft push. It floated toward the sponge lighthouse. Swish. The boat slid to a stop. Safe!

Whiskers clapped his paws. “We did it. We used a plan. We did not give up.”

He cleaned up his ocean. He scooped the water down the drain and wrung out the washcloth with a squish-squish. He folded the little towel sail and hung it neatly. He put the ducky by the sink to dry. He patted the sponge lighthouse and whispered, “Thank you.”

Mama came to the door. “Whiskers, what are you doing?” she asked with a smile.

“I had a big bath rescue,” Whiskers said proudly. “I used my mind. I made a plan. I kept trying. And I used my imagination! The tub was an ocean. The sponge was a lighthouse. The towel was a sail.”

Mama nodded. “That is clever and kind. You solved a problem. You were brave and careful.”

Whiskers purred. “When something is hard,” he said, “I can imagine new ideas. I can plan my steps. I can try again.”

Mama gave him a gentle chin scratch. “That is a wonderful lesson.”

Whiskers stretched on the warm bath mat beach. He felt calm and happy. The bathroom smelled like oranges and clean rain. The tiles felt cool on his belly. The little boat rested, safe and dry.

Captain Whiskers closed his eyes for a moment and whispered, “I can do hard things with my mind and my heart.”

The End.
