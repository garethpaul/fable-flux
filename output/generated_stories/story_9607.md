# Penny Panda's Wonderful Water Day

The morning sun peeked through the soft yellow curtains in Penny Panda's cozy bedroom. She stretched her fluffy black and white arms and yawned a big panda yawn. "Ahhhh!"

Penny rolled out of her warm, green blanket and padded to her water bowl. But oh no! The bowl was completely empty. Not even one tiny drop sparkled at the bottom.

"Mama!" called Penny. "My water bowl is empty!"

Mama Panda's voice drifted from downstairs. "Oh dear, Penny! The water pipes are being fixed today. We won't have water from the sink until tonight."

Penny's tummy felt thirsty already. She looked around her cozy bedroom with its soft carpet and colorful toy chest. "What can I do?" she wondered aloud.

Then Penny remembered something important. Last night, it had rained! She pressed her black nose against the cool window glass. Outside, she could see sparkly raindrops sitting on the big green leaves of Mama's garden plants.

"I know!" Penny said excitedly. She clapped her paws together. "I can collect the rainwater!"

Penny found her favorite blue cup on her bedside table. She opened her bedroom window carefully, just like Mama had taught her. The fresh morning air smelled like flowers and clean rain.

"Hello, little raindrops!" Penny said to the glistening water on the leaves. She gently tilted a big leaf and watched the clear rainwater trickle into her cup. Drip, drop, drip, drop!

"This water is perfect for drinking," Penny said proudly. "And I'm helping the plants too!" She knew that collecting the rainwater would help the leaves dry off so they could breathe better.

After filling her cup, Penny took a refreshing sip. The rainwater tasted clean and cool. "Mmm!" she hummed happily.

But then Penny noticed her little cactus friend on the windowsill. His soil looked very dry and dusty.

"You must be thirsty too, little cactus," Penny said gently. She went back to the window and collected more rainwater in a small dish. Very carefully, she poured just a little bit around the cactus. "There you go!"

The cactus seemed to perk up immediately. His green skin looked brighter and happier.

Penny felt so proud of herself. She had solved her water problem all by herself! She had been resourceful by using the rainwater. She had taken care of the environment by helping both the garden plants and her cactus friend. And she had made sure to drink water to keep her body healthy and strong.

"Penny!" called Mama Panda from downstairs. "How are you doing up there?"

"I'm wonderful, Mama!" Penny called back confidently. "I found water for me and my cactus friend! I used the rainwater from the leaves!"

"What a smart and resourceful little panda you are!" Mama said proudly. "You took care of yourself and helped nature too!"

Penny beamed with happiness. She had learned that when problems come up, she could think of creative solutions. She could take care of herself, help the environment, and be proud of her clever ideas.

As she got dressed for the day, Penny smiled at her reflection in the mirror. "I am resourceful," she said to herself. "I can solve problems and help others too!"

The End.
