# Ollie's Rooftop Adventure

Ollie the otter loved to play! His sleek brown fur sparkled in the sunshine as he slid down the smooth water slides at the zoo. But today was different. Today, Ollie had climbed up, up, up to the tall rooftop of the otter house to see the whole world below.

"Wow!" Ollie squeaked with excitement. The rooftop felt warm under his webbed paws, and he could see children laughing and pointing at him from far below. The wind tickled his whiskers as he waddled around, exploring every corner.

Soon, other animals noticed Ollie on his special rooftop spot. First came Benny the beaver, who climbed up with a loud THUMP, THUMP, THUMP.

"This looks fun!" Benny said, slapping his big flat tail on the roof. "Can I play up here too?"

Ollie felt his tummy get a little tight. The rooftop was his quiet place to think and see the world. But Benny seemed so excited.

"Um, well..." Ollie started to say.

Before he could finish, Rosie the raccoon scampered up with her tiny paws clicking on the roof tiles. "Ooh, a rooftop party!" she chattered. "I love parties!"

Then came Max the monkey, swinging up with a loud "WHEEE!" He immediately started doing flips and making the whole roof shake.

Ollie's whiskers drooped. His peaceful rooftop was getting very crowded and very noisy. The warm sunshine didn't feel as nice anymore, and he couldn't hear the gentle sounds of the wind.

"This is too much," Ollie whispered to himself, feeling overwhelmed.

He remembered what his mama had taught him: "It's okay to say how you feel, Ollie. Use your words kindly, and others will listen."

Ollie took a deep breath that filled his little chest. He stood up tall on his hind legs and spoke in a clear, gentle voice.

"Friends," he said, "I really like playing with you all. But right now, I need some quiet time on my rooftop. This is my special thinking place."

Benny tilted his head. "Oh! I didn't know this was your special place."

"I'm sorry, Ollie," said Rosie, her black mask making her eyes look extra sorry. "We just wanted to have fun with you."

Max stopped flipping and sat down quietly. "We can find another place to play," he said kindly.

Ollie felt much better. His friends understood! "Thank you for listening," he said with a big smile. "Maybe later we can all play together by the pond?"

"That sounds perfect!" all three friends said together.

As his friends climbed down, Ollie felt the peaceful feeling return to his rooftop. The warm sun felt cozy again, and he could hear the soft whoosh of the wind through the trees. He had learned something very important.

Later that afternoon, Ollie slid down to the pond where his friends were waiting. They splashed and played together, and everyone had the most wonderful time.

"Thank you for teaching us about your special place," Benny said as they floated on their backs.

Ollie's heart felt as warm as the sunshine. "And thank you for listening when I used my words," he replied.

From that day on, all the animals knew that it was okay to have special places and to speak up kindly when they needed space. And they all became even better friends because they learned to listen to each other.

The End.
