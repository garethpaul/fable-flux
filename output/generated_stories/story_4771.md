# Tilly Tortoise’s Sunny Sharing Day

Tilly the wise tortoise lived in a sunny backyard full of warm grass, tiny clover flowers, and the sweet smell of oranges from a nearby tree. The sun felt like a soft blanket on Tilly’s shell. Birds sang, “Chee-chee!” A breeze whispered, “Whoosh,” through the leaves. It was a perfect day for a small, special adventure.

Tilly blinked her gentle eyes and smiled. “Today,” she said slowly, “we will make something together.” Her voice sounded like a soft drum: calm and steady.

Her friends gathered on the bright picnic blanket. There was Mia the mouse, quick and squeaky. There was Pio the parrot, bright green and brave. There was Hana the bunny, quiet and kind. And Niko the snail, shiny and shy.

“What will we make?” asked Mia, twitching her whiskers.

Tilly touched a basket full of colorful scraps. “We will make a welcome banner. We will use old things and give them new life. That is good for our Earth.” Tilly nodded. “We will use kind words from many languages. That is good for our hearts.”

Pio flapped his wings. “Polly says hello! Hola! Bonjour! Jambo!” he squawked.

Niko peeked out. “I only know hello,” he whispered.

“That is okay,” Tilly said. “We learn together. We include everyone.”

Tilly opened the basket. Inside were ribbon ends, soft fabric squares, cardboard stars, and a roll of string. The sun made the colors glow—red like a strawberry, blue like the sky, yellow like the sun. The ribbon smelled a little like soap. The cardboard felt bumpy.

Hana picked up a yellow ribbon. “This can be the sunshine,” she said.

Mia held a blue square. “This can be the sky!”

Pio tapped a red heart shape with his beak. “This can be love!”

Niko touched a small green leaf. “This can be… me,” he said with a tiny smile. His shell gleamed like a little moon.

Tilly clapped her short legs softly. “Wonderful. We will make the banner with all of you.”

They spread the pieces on the blanket. Tilly showed how to use the string. “We tie. We loop. We pull.” Her movements were slow and careful. “Slow and steady is safe and strong.”

Mia tied fast, fast, fast. “Like this?” The knot slipped apart.

Tilly chuckled. “Try slow.” Mia tried again, slower. This time, the knot held tight. “I did it!” Mia squeaked.

Hana glued a cardboard star. “Sticky,” she giggled, wiggling her nose. The glue smelled sweet and a little sour. The star stuck, shining.

Pio looked at the letters. “What words do we add?”

Tilly smiled. “Let’s say ‘Welcome’ in many ways.”

Pio puffed up proudly. “Hola!” He placed letters that said H-O-L-A.

Mia whispered, “Hello.” She put H-E-L-L-O on a blue square.

Hana said softly, “Konnichiwa,” and added neat letters with a black crayon.

Tilly said, “Jambo,” and added it on a sunny ribbon.

Niko looked worried. “I don’t know another hello.”

Tilly leaned close. “You can add a smile. Smiles are a hello in every language.”

Niko’s eyes brightened. He drew a big, round smile on a green heart. “Here,” he said. “Hello.”

The banner began to wobble in the breeze. “Whoosh,” went the leaves. The banner fluttered like a happy fish tail. But then, uh-oh! The middle started to sag. The string was too short. The words dipped down and almost touched the grass.

Mia squeaked, “It’s falling!”

Hana’s ears popped up. “We need help.”

Pio squawked, “I will hold it with my beak!” He tried, but the banner still drooped.

Tilly took a deep breath. “We can fix this together.”

She looked around the sunny backyard. There, near the orange tree, was a pile of fallen sticks. Tilly pointed with her nose. “We can use those sticks. We will not take from the tree. We will use what has already fallen. That is called re-use.”

Everyone nodded. They walked slowly to the sticks. The ground felt warm under their feet. The air smelled like oranges and grass. A little ladybug tickled Tilly’s foot.

Hana carried two light sticks. “I can do this,” she puffed.

Mia carried a thin twig like a tiny sword. “I can do this too!”

Pio lifted a long stick with his claws. “Strong!” he said happily.

Niko pushed a small stick, inch by inch. “I am slow,” he said.

Tilly smiled. “Slow and steady helps us all. We will wait for you.”

They tied the sticks to the ends of the banner. Tilly taught a new knot. “Around, around, under, and pull.” They all said it together. “Around, around, under, and pull.” The knot became firm and neat.

The banner lifted straight and tall. The words shone in the sun: Hello, Hola, Konnichiwa, Jambo. Niko’s green smiling heart beamed in the middle.

Pio flapped. “It works! It works!”

Mia danced. “It’s so pretty!”

Hana patted the sticks. “Thank you, fallen sticks.”

Niko looked shy again. “Is my smile okay?”

Tilly nodded, her eyes kind. “Your smile is perfect. Your smile included everyone.”

They hung the banner on the garden fence. The breeze made it wave hello to the world. “Whoosh, flutter, whoosh.”

Neighbors peeked over the fence. A cat purred. A child giggled. “What does it say?” the child asked.

Tilly called, “It says, ‘Welcome,’ in many ways, for every friend.”

The child smiled and said, “Welcome!” back.

Tilly felt warm and proud. “Today we learned to include every friend. We used many words. We used old things in new ways. We took our time. We helped each other. When we work together, everyone belongs.”

The friends sat on the blanket and shared orange slices. The juice dripped sweet and sticky on their paws and claws and shell. They laughed and watched the banner wave.

Tilly whispered, “In our sunny backyard, there is room for every hello.”

The End.
