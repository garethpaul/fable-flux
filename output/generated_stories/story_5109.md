# Olive Otter’s Sparkly Spoon

Olive was a playful otter with bright eyes and a swishy tail. She loved to giggle, wiggle, and scribble. One sunny morning, Olive slid into the dining room. The window was open, and warm air smelled like buttered toast. The table was set with a bowl, a cup, and a shiny spoon.

“Oooh, shiny!” Olive said, tapping the spoon. It chimed like a tiny bell. Ting!

Mama Otter walked in with a basket. “Good morning, Olive. Time for breakfast.”

Olive wriggled in her chair. “I want to write a menu!” she chirped. She loved writing pretend words with her stubby pencil. Scribble-scribble! She wrote squiggles on a notepad: “Toasty Toast. Berry Bubbles. Soggy Seaweed.” She giggled. “This menu is silly!”

Mama smiled. “That’s lovely writing. But first, clean paws.”

Olive looked at her paws. They were dusty from drawing outside. She wiggled her whiskers. “They look okay.”

Mama shook her head gently. “We wash, then we eat.” She pointed to the sink near the dining room. “Soap, water, scrub-scrub.”

Olive pouted. She wanted to write more and taste toast now. “But I’m hungry.”

Mama’s voice was calm. “Let’s do it together. Breathe in like a bubble… and out like a wave.” They took a big breath in, then out. Whoosh.

Olive felt her tummy slow down. “Okay,” she said, hopping off the chair.

At the sink, the water was warm on her paws. Ssshhhhh. The soap smelled like lemons. Plip-plop. Mama sang, and Olive sang too: “Top and bottom, in between, scrub them clean, scrub them clean!” They counted to twenty. Olive watched tiny bubbles slide down her fur. “The bubbles are sparkly,” she whispered, smiling.

Back at the table, Olive touched her spoon. “Time to eat!” She lifted it to her mouth. But then—she froze. The spoon didn’t chime. It felt sticky. Olive blinked. “Mama? My spoon is… gloopy.”

Mama looked closer. A gummy smear clung to the spoon. “Hmm,” Mama said. “This spoon isn’t clean. Good noticing, Olive.”

Olive frowned. “I washed my paws. But my spoon didn’t get a bath.”

Mama nodded. “You discovered a problem. What should we do?”

Olive thought. She tapped her pencil. Tap-tap. “We can give the spoon a bath too!”

Mama smiled. “Great solution. Tools of the table need washing, just like paws.”

Olive carried the spoon to the sink. She turned the water. Ssshh. The sticky bit slithered away. She rubbed with a soapy sponge. “Round and round,” she said. “Now it sparkles!” She dried it with a towel. Rub-rub.

Back at the table, Olive set the spoon down. Ting! It chimed again. Olive beamed. “The spoon sings when it’s clean.”

Mama brought a small jar of berry jam and a plate of toast. “Before we eat,” Mama said, “let’s make a Dining Room Rules poster. Your writing is wonderful.”

Olive’s eyes grew wide. “I can write the rules?”

“You can help,” said Mama. “I’ll say them. You write and draw.”

Olive took her pencil and notepad. Scribble-scribble. She drew little pictures too. Mama spoke slowly.

“One: Wash paws before food.” Olive drew bubbles and a tiny paw. “Two: Clean tools—spoons, forks, cups.” Olive drew a shiny spoon with a smile. “Three: Wipe the table if sticky.” Olive drew a cloth and a happy table. “Four: Take big breaths when waiting.” Olive drew a wavy line for a calm breath. “Five: Say please and thank you.”

Olive read her squiggles out loud. “Wash. Clean. Wipe. Breathe. Please!” She clapped. “I’m a writing otter!”

“You discovered the steps,” Mama said. “And you used great self-control.”

Olive looked at the toast and jam. Her tummy rumbled. “I can wait,” she whispered. She put her paws in her lap. In… and out… Her whiskers trembled, but she smiled. “Now I’m ready.”

They spread jam on toast. The jam smelled sweet and berry-bright. “Mmm,” said Olive. Crunch. “My spoon is clean, my paws are clean, my tummy is happy.”

After breakfast, Olive saw a little jam dot on the table. “Oops. Sticky.” She got a cloth. Swoosh-swipe. “Clean again!”

Mama nodded. “You’re taking care of our space.”

Olive taped her poster to the wall. The paper crinkled softly. “Now everyone can see.”

Just then, Olive’s friend Pip popped by the open window. Pip was a curious squirrel with a bushy tail. “Hi, Olive! Can we play?”

Olive waved. “Yes! But first, we wash paws. It’s the rule.” She pointed at the poster. “See? Wash, clean, wipe, breathe, please!”

Pip read the pictures. “I like the smiley spoon.”

Olive giggled. “Me too. It sings when it’s clean. Ting!”

Pip washed his paws at the sink. Ssshh. The lemon smell filled the room again. “Clean feels nice,” Pip said.

Olive felt proud. Her chest felt warm, like sunshine. She turned to Mama. “Today I learned: Clean paws and clean tools help our bodies. We can find problems and fix them. We can wait with calm breaths.”

Mama hugged her. “That’s a wonderful lesson.”

Olive picked up her pencil one more time. Scribble-scribble. She added one more line to the poster: “Six: Share smiles.” She drew a big, happy face.

Pip grinned. “Let’s play Menu Restaurant!”

Olive laughed. “Yes! But first—”

Pip and Olive chanted together, “Wash, clean, wipe, breathe, please!”

The spoon chimed. Ting! The dining room felt fresh and bright, like a tidy little sea.

The End.
