# Ollie Otter's Clean Hands Discovery

Ollie Otter bounced excitedly into the bright dining room. His whiskers twitched as he smelled something wonderful. "Mmm! Fish sticks for lunch!" he squeaked happily.

The dining room had a big wooden table with four chairs. Sunlight streamed through the window, making everything sparkle. Ollie's tummy rumbled loudly. *Grumble-grumble!*

"Time to eat!" Ollie said, reaching for a golden fish stick with his paws.

"Wait, Ollie!" called Mama Otter from the kitchen. "Did you wash your hands?"

Ollie looked down at his brown paws. They were covered in mud from playing outside. There were bits of grass and dirt stuck between his tiny fingers. His paws smelled like the pond where he'd been swimming.

"But I'm so hungry!" Ollie whined. "My fish sticks will get cold!"

Mama Otter walked into the dining room, carrying a warm washcloth. "Let me show you something special," she said with a smile. She placed a piece of white paper on the table.

"Press your paws here, Ollie."

Ollie pressed his muddy paws onto the paper. When he lifted them up, he gasped. "Wow! Look at all those brown marks!"

"Those are germs and dirt," Mama explained gently. "If you eat with dirty hands, those germs might make your tummy hurt. But clean hands make yummy food taste even better!"

Ollie's eyes grew wide. "Really? Clean hands make food taste better?"

"Let's find out!" Mama said. She led Ollie to the kitchen sink. The warm, soapy water felt bubbly and nice on his paws. He rubbed them together, watching the dirt swirl down the drain.

"Scrub between your fingers," Mama sang. "Make them sparkle and shine! Clean paws are happy paws, and happy paws are fine!"

Ollie giggled as the soap made funny squeaky sounds. *Squeak-squeak!* He counted to twenty while washing, just like Mama taught him.

After drying his paws with a fluffy towel, Ollie ran back to the dining room. His paws felt fresh and clean. They smelled like lavender soap instead of pond water.

"Now try your fish stick," Mama said.

Ollie picked up the golden fish stick with his clean paws. He took a big bite. "Mmm! It tastes amazing!" he exclaimed. "You're right, Mama! Clean hands DO make food taste better!"

After lunch, Ollie found his favorite crayon and drew a picture on the back of the muddy handprint paper. He drew himself washing his hands at the sink, with big soap bubbles everywhere.

"I want to help other little animals learn about clean hands too!" Ollie said excitedly. He wrote in his best letters: "WASH YOUR HANDS!" at the top of his drawing.

The next day, Ollie brought his picture to show his friends at the pond. "Look what I discovered!" he said proudly. "Clean hands make everything better - food tastes yummier, and we stay healthy too!"

His friend Bella Beaver looked at the drawing. "Wow, Ollie! Can you teach me how to wash my hands properly?"

Ollie beamed with pride. "Of course! It's easy and fun!" He showed Bella the counting song and the special way to scrub between fingers.

From that day on, Ollie always remembered to wash his hands before eating. He even helped his little sister learn the hand-washing song. Ollie felt proud knowing that clean hands kept his family healthy and happy.

"Clean paws, happy day!" became Ollie's favorite saying.

The End.
