# Toby Turtle Tidies the Shed

Toby was a shy turtle. He liked quiet places and soft sounds. One sunny morning, Toby peeked into the garden shed. The air smelled like fresh soil and wood. Sunlight made tiny dust sparkles dance. But oh dear—the shed was messy!

Bottles clinked in a pile. Cardboard boxes were squished. A little metal pail rolled and went clank, clank. Toby tucked his head a little. “This mess makes me feel small,” he whispered. “But I can help.”

He took a slow breath. In… and out. “I can speak up,” Toby told himself. “I can use my brave voice.”

Just then, his friends came by. Buzz the bee hummed, “Bzz-bzz, hello, Toby!” Nia the newt waved her tiny hand. Pip the mouse nibbled a seed and squeaked, “What’s going on?”

Toby lifted his head. “Friends,” he said softly, “the shed is messy. It makes me feel worried. I want to make it nice and neat. Can you help me?” His voice was quiet, but clear.

Buzz nodded. “We can!” Nia smiled. “We can use peaceful plans.” Pip squeaked, “We can sort things!”

Toby felt warm and brave inside. He drew a little chart in the dust with his toe. “Let’s make three bins,” he said. “One bin for paper and cardboard. One bin for plastic. One bin for metal.” He pointed to an old box. “We can count and sort.”

They found three baskets. The wicker basket smelled a little like hay. It would be for paper. The blue bucket would be for plastic. The shiny tin tub would be for metal. Clink!

Buzz zipped to the corner and said, “Bzz, I found a paper bag!” He dropped it into the wicker basket. Rustle, rustle. Nia slid a cardboard roll inside. Pip pushed a cereal box. “Paper pile!”

Toby moved slowly, but carefully. He picked up a crinkly bottle. “This one is plastic,” he said. He squeezed it and it made a little squeak. Into the blue bucket it went. Squeak, plop!

Nia tapped a can with her tail. “Tink, tink!” “Metal,” Toby said. He smiled and set it in the tin tub. Clink, clink. The sounds became a happy sorting song—rustle, squeak, clink!

Soon, a big spider named Silk peeked down from a beam. “Excuse me,” Silk said in a gentle voice. “All this moving is shaking my web.”

Toby paused. He looked up. “I’m sorry, Silk,” he said kindly. “We are cleaning and recycling. But we don’t want to bother you. How can we help?”

Silk smiled. “Thank you for asking.” She lifted a silvery thread. “If you move that rake and that broom slowly, my web will be safe.”

Toby nodded. “We can do that.” He turned to his friends. “Let’s go slowly near Silk.” Everyone agreed. They moved the rake with soft steps. Scritch, scritch. Silk’s web stayed still and shiny. Peaceful problem solved!

They kept sorting. Pip found a jar lid. “Metal!” Buzz found a clear cup. “Plastic!” Nia held up an old picture book. “Paper!” Toby lined up the bins. He made neat labels from scrap cardboard: PAPER, PLASTIC, METAL. He drew simple shapes, too—a book, a bottle, and a can—so everyone could tell what went where.

As they worked, Toby noticed a cracked flower pot. He touched the rough edge. “This can’t go in the recycling bins,” he said. “But we can reuse it.” He looked around the shed and took a breath. “I have an idea.”

“What is it?” asked Nia.

“We can make it a home for seeds,” Toby said. “Let’s make a seed station.” He found a small scoop, some leftover soil, and little paper envelopes. He wrote SEEDS on a tiny sign. The cracked pot became a cozy holder for the seed envelopes. It looked tidy and helpful. Resourceful!

Buzz buzzed with joy. “Bzz, it’s beautiful!” Pip clapped his tiny paws. “Neat and sweet!” Silk lowered down. “Your shed feels calm now.”

Toby’s heart felt big. He looked at the bins. They were sorted and ready. “We will take the bins to the recycling center,” Toby said. “Then the paper can become new paper. The plastic can become new bottles. The metal can become new cans. Less trash. More new things!”

Pip pointed to a toy truck in a corner. “It’s broken,” he said. Toby touched the loose wheel. He thought and thought. “We can fix it together,” he said. “If we can’t fix it, we can take the plastic parts to the plastic bin.” They tried a gentle twist. Click! The wheel popped back on. “We fixed it!” Pip squeaked. Everyone cheered.

At last, the shed smelled like clean wood and fresh air. The floor felt smooth under Toby’s feet. The bins stood side by side, like three proud soldiers. The seed station sat by the window, warm with light.

Toby took another slow breath. “I was shy,” he said, “but I used my voice. I asked for help. We made a plan. We were peaceful. We recycled. We reused. We got organized. We were resourceful.”

Buzz hummed a happy tune. Nia patted Toby’s shell. Silk made a sparkly ribbon across the door like a celebration.

Toby smiled. “When we recycle and work together, we care for our home. We can all help—even if we are shy.”

The friends waved goodbye. The garden shed felt safe, neat, and kind. Toby walked out into the sunshine, feeling brave and proud.

The lesson was clear: Speak up kindly, make a peaceful plan, sort and recycle what you can, and find new ways to use old things.

The End.
