# Ruby's Big Discovery

Ruby was a little red bird who loved to sing beautiful songs. Every morning, she would perch on the windowsill outside the Johnson family's house and chirp her cheerful melodies. "Tweet-tweet-trill!" she would sing, watching the family get ready for their day.

One sunny morning, Ruby noticed something strange. She could hear water splashing and see steam coming from a special room with white tiles - the bathroom! Ruby had never been inside a house before, and her curious little heart began to flutter with excitement.

"I wonder what happens in there," Ruby chirped to herself. "It sounds so interesting with all that splashing and bubbling!"

When little Emma Johnson opened the bathroom window to let in fresh air, Ruby saw her chance. With a brave flutter of her tiny wings, she flew right through the window and landed on the edge of the bathtub.

"Oh my!" squeaked Ruby, looking around with wide, bright eyes. The bathroom was full of amazing discoveries! There were bottles that smelled like flowers, a big white tub filled with warm, bubbly water, and fluffy towels that looked as soft as clouds.

But then Ruby noticed something that made her tilt her little head. Emma was brushing her teeth, washing her hands with soap, and combing her hair. "Why does she do all these things?" Ruby wondered aloud.

"Tweet-tweet! Excuse me," Ruby said politely to Emma, who was surprised but delighted to see the little bird. "I'm Ruby, and I live outside. I've never seen these activities before. What are you doing?"

Emma giggled and said, "Hi Ruby! I'm getting clean! This is called hygiene - it means taking care of my body to stay healthy and feel good."

Ruby watched with fascination as Emma showed her the toothbrush. "This keeps my teeth clean and strong," Emma explained. Then she demonstrated washing her hands with soap. "And this washes away germs so I don't get sick."

"Wow!" chirped Ruby. "In the forest, I take baths in puddles and streams, but I never knew there were special tools for staying clean!"

Emma smiled and said, "Would you like to try? Birds need to stay clean too!"

Ruby felt a little nervous but also excited. She had been patient, watching and learning. Now she wanted to be assertive and try something new! "Yes, please!" she sang.

Emma filled a small bowl with warm water and placed it on the counter. Ruby carefully dipped her tiny feet in the water, then splashed gently with her wings. "This feels wonderful!" she chirped happily. "The water is so warm and clean!"

As Ruby enjoyed her mini-bath, she discovered that the warm water made her feathers feel softer and shinier. Emma even gave her a tiny drop of gentle soap to try. "This smells like lavender," Ruby sang with joy.

After her bath, Ruby felt fresh and beautiful. Her red feathers gleamed in the morning sunlight streaming through the window. "Thank you for teaching me about hygiene, Emma!" Ruby chirped gratefully.

"You're welcome, Ruby!" Emma said. "Taking care of ourselves makes us feel good inside and out."

Ruby realized she had made an important discovery. Staying clean wasn't just about looking nice - it was about feeling healthy and confident. She had learned that being curious and patient helped her discover new things, and being assertive helped her try new experiences.

"I should fly back outside now," Ruby said. "But I'll remember what I learned! And maybe I can teach other birds about staying clean too!"

Emma waved goodbye as Ruby flew back through the window. From that day on, Ruby made sure to take proper baths in the forest streams, and she taught all her bird friends about the importance of hygiene. She had discovered that learning new things, even in unexpected places, could make life better and more wonderful.

The End.
