# Pippa Parrot’s Sunny Sharing Circle

Pippa was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a sunny yellow beak. She lived in a sunny backyard filled with warm light, soft grass, and sweet orange flowers. The air smelled like fresh lemons, and butterflies danced in the breeze.

One morning, Pippa fluffed her feathers and chirped, “Good morning!” She heard children laughing on the patio and the soft splash of the birdbath. Pippa loved the backyard. She loved the sunshine, the crunchy leaves, and the songs the wind sang in the trees.

Pippa had a special family tradition. Every year, her grandparrot taught her a song. It was a song about kindness and home. Grandparrot would say, “We share this song to remember who we are.” Pippa remembered the warm feeling in her heart when they sang together.

Pippa wanted to share the song with friends. She hopped to the garden fence and called, “Friends! Come and listen!”

Benny the bunny poked his ears up. “What is it?” he asked.

Mimi the mouse scurried out. “Is there cheese?” she squeaked.

Tiko the turtle blinked slowly in the sunshine. “I like sunshine,” he said.

Pippa laughed. “I want to share a tradition,” she said. “A tradition is something special we do again and again. It helps us remember. My family sings a kindness song. Will you join me?”

Benny twitched his nose. “I love songs!”

Mimi clapped her tiny paws. “Me too!”

Tiko smiled. “Slow and steady, I can sing.”

Pippa looked at the soft grass. She had an idea. “Let’s make a Sharing Circle,” she said. “We sit in a circle on this warm blanket. We share the song. And we each share something from our own families too.”

“That sounds fun,” Benny said, hopping in place.

Pippa spread a bright blanket on the grass. It felt cozy and warm. The air smelled like mint from the garden. The leaves made a gentle shhh sound.

Pippa took a small bell from a little basket. “Ding!” she rang. “I will be the leader to start,” she said. “A leader helps everyone take turns. A leader listens, and a leader is kind.”

Pippa stood tall. “This is my family’s song.” She closed her eyes and sang in a soft, clear voice, “Kind hearts, bright hearts, sing together, near or far. Kind words, warm words, make us who we are.”

Her voice floated like a feather in the sunny yard. The friends listened. Their faces were calm and happy.

“That was beautiful,” Mimi said, her whiskers wiggling.

Pippa smiled. “Thank you,” she said. Her heart felt warm. “Now it’s your turn to share. Benny, would you like to go next?”

Benny nodded. “My family has a hopping hello,” he said. He showed them: hop, hop, hop, wiggle ears, and a big bunny smile.

Everyone tried it. Hop, hop, hop, wiggle ears, smile! They laughed. The grass tickled their toes.

“Ding!” Pippa rang the bell gently. “Mimi?”

Mimi stood on the blanket. “My family makes a tiny thankful tap,” she said. She tapped the ground with her paw and whispered, “Thank you, thank you.” Her voice was like a small bell.

They all tapped gently and whispered together. The sound was soft and sweet.

“Ding,” Pippa said. “Tiko?”

Tiko nodded slowly. “My family watches the sunset,” he said. “We breathe in and out. We say, ‘Orange sky, calm hearts.’”

They all looked up at the bright, blue sky. They took a big, slow breath. In and out. They felt calm.

Pippa looked around the sunny backyard. She had another idea. “Let’s make a new tradition together,” she said. “We can join all our traditions in one.”

“That’s innovation!” Benny said proudly. “That means a new idea!”

“Yes,” Pippa said. “We mix the old and new. We share and make something special.”

Pippa flapped her wings gently and became the leader. “Ready?” she asked.

“Ready!” everyone said.

First, they sang Pippa’s kindness song: “Kind hearts, bright hearts, sing together, near or far. Kind words, warm words, make us who we are.”

Next, they did Benny’s hopping hello: hop, hop, hop, wiggle ears, smile!

Then, they made Mimi’s thankful taps: tap, tap. “Thank you, thank you,” they whispered.

Finally, they breathed with Tiko’s calm words: “Orange sky, calm hearts,” even though the sun was still high and bright.

They put it all together. It felt happy and peaceful. The breeze kissed their faces. The flowers smelled sweet. The birdbath splashed as a robin landed with a plip-plip.

Pippa’s eyes shone. “We made a new tradition,” she said. “We will do it every sunny day.”

“Can we share it with others?” Mimi asked.

“Yes,” Pippa said. “Sharing traditions helps friends feel welcome. Sharing shows love.”

Benny hopped high. “You were a good leader, Pippa,” he said.

Tiko nodded. “You listened. You helped.”

Pippa felt proud and kind. “We all led,” she said. “We all shared.”

They did the new tradition one more time. Song, hops, taps, calm breath. Their hearts felt warm and light.

As the shadows grew longer, Pippa tucked the blanket away. The backyard still smelled like lemons. The sky turned soft orange. Pippa said, “Today we learned to share traditions, to lead with kindness, and to try new ideas.”

Everyone smiled. They felt safe. They felt loved.

The End.
