# Panda Poppy Saves the Rooftop

Poppy was a patient panda with soft black-and-white fur and bright, careful eyes. Every afternoon, Poppy liked to go up, up, up the stairs to the sunny rooftop garden. The roof felt warm under her paws. The breeze smelled like fresh leaves and a little bit like apples. Birds sang, “Cheep-cheep!” and tiny bees buzzed by the flowers.

Poppy loved to water the plants and sort the bins. She had three bins: blue for recycling, green for compost, and gray for trash. Poppy would smile and say, “Blue for bottles, green for peels, gray for the rest.” She showed her friends how to use them, one by one, with patience and care.

One day, Poppy opened the roof door and heard a clatter. Clink! Clank! Something rolled across the tiles. A plastic bottle spun near her paw. Poppy blinked. The blue recycling bin was tipped over. The green compost bin was full of paper and cans. The gray trash bin had banana peels. The bins were all mixed up!

“Oh dear,” Poppy whispered. “This is not safe, and it is not fair to our Earth.” She took a deep breath. The breeze tickled her nose. She felt sad for the garden and the bees.

Just then, her friends arrived. Lila the little lark flapped her wings. “What happened, Poppy?”

Milo the monkey hopped and frowned. “This is a mess!”

Poppy stood tall and patient. “Let’s look carefully. If we know the problem, we can find the best way to fix it. We must be safe, fair, and kind. We must recycle right.”

They looked and listened. They heard a soft scraping sound behind a planter box. Scritch. Scratch. Poppy peeked and saw Tico the turtle, slowly pushing a shiny can with his shell.

“Tico,” Poppy said gently, “did you move the bins?”

Tico pulled his head in, then peeked back out. “I… I wanted to help clean up. But the bins were heavy. I pushed and pushed. I got mixed up.” His voice was small, like a whispering leaf.

Poppy’s heart felt soft. “Thank you for trying to help, Tico,” she said kindly. “It is good to help. But we must be safe. And we must be fair to the Earth. Recycling has rules.”

Milo crossed his arms. “It’s not fair that the garden got messy!”

Lila clicked her beak. “And the bees need clean flowers!”

Poppy nodded. “Justice means we make things right in a fair way. We do not yell. We do not blame. We teach. We fix. We keep everyone safe.”

She turned to Tico. “We can fix this together.”

Tico’s eyes grew brighter. “Together?”

Poppy smiled. “Yes. First, safety.” She pointed to the edge of the roof. “We stay far from the edge. We walk, not run. We wear our little gloves so our paws and claws are safe.”

They all put on small gloves from the garden box. The gloves smelled like soap and sunshine. Poppy spoke in a clear voice, slow and calm. “Now, sort with me. Listen to the sounds.”

She tapped a glass jar: “Tink!” She held up a plastic bottle: “Crinkle!” She squeezed a paper bag: “Rustle!”

“Blue bin for tink and crinkle,” Poppy said. “Green bin for peel and pit and leaf.” She held up a banana peel and a little apple core. “Gray bin for things that cannot be reused.”

Lila fluttered near the bins. “I can sing the sorting song!” She sang, “Blue for bottles, green for peels, gray for trash that nothing heals!”

They laughed. Then they worked. Poppy showed Tico how to lift small loads. “Slow and steady,” she said. “You are safe, and you are helping.”

Milo bounced with a broom. “I’ll sweep the tiny bits,” he said. Swish, swish, swish. He stayed in the middle of the roof, far from the edge, just like Poppy said.

The sun warmed their backs. The sky was bright and blue. The garden started to look neat. The bees buzzed happily again.

Soon, the bins were upright and tidy. Blue had bottles and cans. Green had peels and leaves. Gray had only a few things. Poppy clapped her paws softly. “We did it.”

Tico lifted his chin. “I learned the rules. I will not push big bins alone. I will ask for help.”

Poppy nodded. “That is safe and fair. We share the job. We keep the Earth clean.”

Milo grinned. “Can we make signs?”

“Yes,” Poppy said. They drew simple pictures: a bottle for blue, a peel for green, and a little trash can for gray. They taped the signs on the bins. Tap, tap, tap. The tape smelled a little sticky and sweet.

Poppy looked at her friends. “Justice means we all do our part. If someone makes a mistake, we help them learn. We do not let the Earth get hurt. We keep each other safe.”

Lila sang softly, “We sort and care, we’re kind and fair.”

Tico smiled and pat-patted the blue bin. “I like helping the Earth.”

Poppy felt proud. The wind fluttered her fur. The rooftop was clean, the garden was happy, and the friends felt calm and brave.

Poppy said, “What did we learn today?”

Milo said, “Recycling keeps the roof and the Earth clean.”

Lila said, “Safety first. Walk, don’t run. Stay away from the edge.”

Tico said, “Justice is making things right in a kind way.”

Poppy nodded. “Yes. And we do it together.”

They shared cool cups of water. Sip, sip. The water tasted fresh. They watched the sun paint the sky with soft gold. A bee landed on a flower. The rooftop hummed with peace.

From then on, Poppy taught the sorting song to anyone who came up the stairs. And when the bins got full, they called, “Teamwork!” and worked slowly and safely. The garden grew, the birds sang, and the rooftop shone like a clean, happy place.

The lesson was clear: recycle right, be fair and kind, and keep everyone safe.

The End.
