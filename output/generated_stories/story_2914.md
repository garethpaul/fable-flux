# Sunny’s Treehouse Rules

Sunny the squirrel lived in a cozy treehouse high in a friendly oak. The treehouse smelled like pine needles and sweet acorns. Soft leaves brushed the windows. A tiny bell chimed in the breeze. Sunny was resourceful. She fixed wobbly shelves with twigs, tied little ropes for buckets, and sorted her acorns by size and color. “I like my space neat,” Sunny sang. “I like my space safe.”

One bright morning, Sunny hung a small wooden sign near the door. It read, “Sunny’s Space: Please Ask Before You Touch.” She painted the letters in cheerful yellow. “These are my body and my things,” Sunny said to herself. “I can say yes. I can say no.”

Soon, friends came to play. Pip the chipmunk scampered up the ladder. Bree the bluebird fluttered to the window. Nib the bunny peeked in with a basket of berries. “Hi, Sunny!” they called. “Your treehouse smells yummy!”

“It’s pine tea,” Sunny said, smiling. “Come in, friends.”

They played “Acorn Hide-and-Seek.” They sang a clapping song. They tasted warm pine tea with honey. The tea felt smooth and sweet on their tongues.

When Pip found a shiny acorn on a shelf, his eyes grew big. “Ooh! Shiny!” He reached for it.

Sunny took a gentle breath. “Please stop,” she said kindly. “That shiny acorn is special to me. Please ask before you touch.”

Pip froze. His paws hovered in the air. “Oh! I forgot,” he said softly. “May I hold it, Sunny?”

Sunny smiled. “Thanks for asking. Today I want to keep it safe on the shelf. That is my boundary. You can look with your eyes.”

Pip nodded. “Okay,” he said. He leaned in to look. The acorn glowed like a tiny moon.

Bree flapped to the craft table and picked up a blue ribbon. “For my nest!” she chirped.

Sunny touched the ribbon with her paw. “Bree, I care about you. I also care about my things. Please ask before you take. You can use this red ribbon today. The blue one is for a special project.”

Bree tilted her head. “Thank you for telling me kindly,” she said. “May I use the red ribbon?” 
“Yes,” Sunny said. “That feels good to me.”

Nib the bunny bounced to the cozy corner. He loved Sunny’s soft pillow. “Boing!” he laughed and flopped onto Sunny’s lap without asking. Sunny felt squished. She didn’t feel safe.

Sunny put a paw on her chest. She took a slow breath in and out. “Nib,” she said in a calm voice, “my body is my own. I do not want to be climbed on. Please give me space.”

Nib’s ears drooped. “Oh no,” he sniffed. “I didn’t mean to squish you. I’m sorry.”

“Thank you for listening,” Sunny said. “You can sit next to me and we can share the pillow. That feels comfy to me.”

Nib smiled. “Okay,” he said, and scooted to the side.

They played more games. They painted with leaf brushes and nutty colors. Their paws and feathers made soft swish-swish sounds. The air smelled like paint and honey and warm wood.

Suddenly, a gust of wind blew open the little door. The tiny bell clanged. A new friend climbed in—Rascal the raccoon. He wore a big grin and a muddy tail.

“Hi, friends!” Rascal boomed. He rushed across the room. He picked up Sunny’s wooden sign. He spun her acorn sorter. He grabbed the tea cup and slurped. He hugged Sunny without warning. Everything felt too fast.

Sunny’s fur prickled. Her heart beat quick, quick. She didn’t feel safe. She remembered her words. She stood tall, feet steady on the floor. In a clear, kind voice, she said, “Stop, Rascal.”

Rascal blinked. The room went quiet. The wind whispered through the leaves.

Sunny pointed to her sign. “This is my treehouse. Here are my rules. Please ask before you touch my things. Please ask before you hug me. My body is my own.”

Rascal’s smile faded. “I just wanted to play,” he mumbled. “I didn’t know.”

Sunny nodded. “I want to play too. But I need to feel safe. When you ask first, I can say yes or no. That is respect. That is a boundary.”

Rascal shuffled his paws. “I can try,” he said. “May I put your sign back?”

“Yes,” Sunny said. “Thank you.”

Rascal carefully hung the sign. He wiped muddy paw prints from the table with a cloth. “May I have some tea?” he asked.

Sunny felt her body relax. “Yes,” she said. “I will pour it for you.” The tea made tiny steam clouds. It smelled sweet.

“May I look at the shiny acorn?” Rascal asked.

“You may look with your eyes,” Sunny said.

“May I give you a high-five?” he asked.

Sunny smiled. “Yes, a high-five is okay.” Their paws tapped. Clap!

Soon they all made a new project together. Sunny said, “Let’s make a ‘Please Ask’ poster for the treehouse and the whole forest!” They drew simple pictures: a paw asking, a wing waiting, a smile saying yes, a head shaking no. They added easy words: “Ask,” “Wait,” “Listen,” “Respect.”

They carried the poster to the forest square. The air smelled like fresh rain. Friends gathered around. Sunny spoke in a warm voice. “Boundaries keep us safe. We can say yes. We can say no. We ask before we touch. We listen to the answer.”

Rascal raised his paw. “I learned today,” he said. “Asking is kind.”

Pip, Bree, and Nib nodded. “Listening shows care,” they added.

Back in the treehouse, the friends played again. They asked. They waited. They listened. Sunny felt calm and happy. Her space was safe. Her heart was full.

Sunny curled on her pillow as the bell chimed softly. “I can speak up,” she whispered. “I can set my boundary. My friends can learn. We can be kind and have fun.”

And they did.

The End.
