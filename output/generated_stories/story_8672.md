# Shelly's Musical Gift

Shelly the turtle peeked her little green head around the corner of the musical practice room. The room smelled like fresh wood polish and felt warm from the afternoon sun streaming through the windows. She could hear the soft *plink-plink* of piano keys and the gentle *hum* of children practicing their songs.

"Oh no," whispered Shelly to herself, pulling her head back into her shell. Yesterday, she had accidentally knocked over the music stand while trying to reach her favorite tambourine. All the sheet music had scattered everywhere like falling leaves. "Everyone must be so upset with me."

Mrs. Robin, the music teacher, noticed Shelly hiding by the door. "Good morning, Shelly! We missed you at practice yesterday after your little accident. Would you like to join us?"

Shelly's eyes filled with tears. "I'm sorry I made such a big mess. I didn't mean to knock everything down."

"Accidents happen, dear," said Mrs. Robin kindly. "But I have an idea. Instead of feeling sad, what if we make a plan to help fix things?"

Shelly's ears perked up. "A plan?"

"Yes! Planning means thinking ahead about how to solve a problem," explained Mrs. Robin. "First, let's think about what happened. Then, we can figure out how to make things better."

Shelly thought carefully. "Well... the music stand fell down because it was wobbly. And all the papers got mixed up."

"Excellent thinking!" praised Mrs. Robin. "Now, what could we do to help?"

Shelly's mind began to work like a little engine. *Chug-chug-think-think.* "Maybe... maybe I could help build a better music stand? One that won't fall over so easily?"

"What a wonderful idea! And how else could you help the music room?"

Shelly looked around at the scattered instruments and messy shelves. "I could help organize everything! And maybe... maybe I could do something special for all the other children to say sorry?"

Mrs. Robin clapped her wings together. "Now that's what I call good planning! Let's make a list."

Together, they wrote down their plan:
1. Build a sturdy new music stand
2. Organize all the instruments
3. Create a special musical surprise for the class

Shelly spent the whole morning working on her plan. First, she and Mrs. Robin built a strong, wide music stand that wouldn't tip over. Shelly painted it bright yellow like sunshine. The fresh paint smelled sweet and made her feel proud.

Next, she carefully sorted all the instruments. Triangles went *ting-ting* as she placed them in one basket. Maracas went *shake-shake* in another. Everything had its special place.

For her surprise, Shelly practiced a gentle song on the bells. *Ding-dong-ding-dong* - it sounded like a happy raindrop dance.

When the other children arrived for afternoon practice, they gasped with delight.

"Wow! Everything looks so neat and pretty!" said Tommy the rabbit.

"And listen to Shelly's beautiful song!" added Emma the squirrel.

Shelly felt warm and happy inside. "I made a plan to help fix my mistake and do something good for everyone," she said proudly, no longer feeling shy.

Mrs. Robin smiled. "Shelly learned that when we make mistakes, we can plan ways to make things better. Planning helps us think ahead and solve problems together."

From that day on, Shelly always made plans before trying new things, and she loved helping others in the music room.

The End.
