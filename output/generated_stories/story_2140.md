# Oliver Owl and the Fair Share Garden

Oliver Owl sat high in the big oak tree, watching the sunny backyard below. His golden eyes sparkled as he saw three little animals playing together. The warm sun made everything glow, and colorful flowers danced in the gentle breeze.

"Tweet, tweet!" chirped Bella Bird as she hopped around. "Look at all these pretty flowers! I want to make a beautiful crown!"

Benny Bunny wiggled his nose. "I want to make flower soup for my tea party!" he said, his fluffy tail bouncing with excitement.

Squeaky Squirrel scampered up and down. "No, no! I need all the flowers to decorate my tree house!" she chattered loudly.

Oliver watched as the three friends began to argue. Their voices got louder and louder.

"I saw them first!" said Bella, flapping her little wings.

"But I'm the hungriest!" said Benny, thumping his foot.

"I live here!" squeaked Squeaky, gathering flowers in her tiny paws.

Soon, all three animals were pulling at the same bunch of bright yellow daisies. The beautiful flowers began to tear and break apart. Petals scattered on the soft grass like confetti.

"Hoo, hoo," called Oliver gently from above. "What seems to be the problem, little friends?"

The three animals looked up at the wise old owl. His feathers were soft brown and white, and his kind eyes made them feel safe.

"Oliver!" they all called together. "We all want the flowers, but there aren't enough!"

Oliver spread his wide wings and glided down to the sunny grass. The morning air smelled sweet like honey and fresh leaves.

"Let me see," said Oliver thoughtfully, tilting his head. "How many flower patches do you see in this big backyard?"

The animals looked around carefully. "One, two, three, four, five!" counted Benny.

"And how many of you are there?" asked Oliver with a gentle smile.

"Three!" chirped Bella.

"That's right," hooted Oliver wisely. "It seems unfair for one friend to take all the flowers when there are plenty for everyone. What if we found a way to share fairly?"

Squeaky's ears perked up. "But how?"

Oliver's eyes twinkled. "Let's use our creativity! What if each of you chose one flower patch? Then you could all make your special projects together!"

The three friends looked at each other. Bella felt sorry for being grabby. "I'm sorry I tried to take all the flowers," she said softly.

"Me too," said Benny, giving her a gentle hug.

"I forgive you, and I'm sorry too," said Squeaky.

Working together, they divided the flower patches fairly. Bella made her crown with red roses. Benny gathered purple violets for his pretend soup. Squeaky picked white daisies for her tree house.

But the best part was when they decided to help each other! Bella helped Benny arrange his flowers in a pretty bowl. Benny helped Squeaky carry flowers up to her tree. Squeaky helped Bella weave her crown.

"This is much more fun!" laughed Bella as they all crafted together in the warm sunshine.

Oliver watched proudly from his branch. "When we share fairly and work together, everyone is happy," he hooted. "That's what justice means - making sure everyone gets what they need."

As the sun climbed higher in the bright blue sky, the three friends played happily with their flower creations, and Oliver smiled, knowing they had learned something very important about being fair and kind.

The End.
