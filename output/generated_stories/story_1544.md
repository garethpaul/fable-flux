# Ollie Otter’s Balcony Art Garden

The morning air was soft and warm on the balcony. Tiny leaves swayed. Little bells on the wind chime went ting-ting-ting. Ollie the playful otter peeked out the sliding door. His whiskers twitched. His paws were ready for fun.

“Good morning, plants!” Ollie sang. He patted a mint plant and sniffed. “Mmm! Mint smells so fresh.”

Ollie loved his balcony garden. There were pots of sunny marigolds, curly herbs, and a shy tomato plant. There was a shallow tub of water where Ollie liked to splash. Today, the sky looked pale and gray. The flowers looked a little droopy.

Ollie felt a tiny frown. “You look sleepy,” he told the marigolds. “I want to cheer you up.”

His friend, Mina the moth, fluttered in and sat on the railing. “Hello, Ollie,” she buzzed softly. “What are you doing?”

“I want to make our garden bright,” Ollie said. “But I don’t know how.”

Mina flapped her powdery wings. “You can make art,” she said. “Art is a way to show feelings.”

Ollie blinked. “Art? On the balcony?”

“Yes,” Mina said. “Use what you have. Use your heart.”

Ollie put a paw to his chest. His heart felt bouncy. Then he looked around. He saw fallen leaves in a pile. He saw smooth stones by the tub. He saw empty seed packets, a bit of twine, and a tiny brush for the pots.

“I can do it!” Ollie chirped. “I can make balcony art!”

He gathered the leaves. They felt cool and papery. He washed the stones in the tub. Splish, splash! The water sparkled and tickled his fur. He tied the twine to the railing. Ting-ting-ting went the wind chime, like happy claps.

Mina watched with big eyes. “What will you make?”

Ollie took a deep breath. “I will make a flower flag!” He threaded the twine through the leaves. He painted little dots on the stones with wet mud and mashed berries. Plip, plop. The mud was squishy. The berries smelled sweet.

“Look!” Ollie said. He lined the dotted stones in a curve like a smile. He hung the leaf flag so it swayed. The balcony looked brighter already.

Just then, Tiko the tiny turtle peeked from under a pot. “Hi,” Tiko said slowly. “I feel shy today.”

Ollie knelt. “Hi, Tiko. Do you want to make art with us?”

Tiko shuffled his feet. “I am not good at art,” he whispered. His shell trembled a little.

Ollie’s heart squeezed. He remembered feeling unsure a minute ago. He took a gentle breath in. He let it out slowly. “It’s okay to feel shy,” he said. “We can start small. You can choose a stone.”

Tiko looked at the stones. He picked the tiniest one. “This one,” he said quietly.

“Great choice!” Ollie beamed. “What color do you like?”

“Yellow,” Tiko said.

Ollie mashed a yellow marigold petal with a drop of water. Swish, swish. He made a soft golden paint. “Here you go,” he said, handing Tiko the tiny brush.

Tiko dabbed the stone. Dot. Dot. Dot. He blinked. “It looks like sunshine.”

“It does!” Mina cheered. “I feel warm just seeing it.”

Tiko stood a little taller. “I made sunshine,” he said. His voice was brighter now.

Ollie clapped. “Your sunshine stone can be the center.”

They set Tiko’s stone in the middle of the smile curve. Ollie added leaf petals around it. Mina sprinkled a few glittery seeds. The art looked like a giant flower face, smiling up at the gray sky.

A little breeze came by. The leaf flag flapped. The wind chime chimed. The balcony smelled like mint and marigold and a tiny bit of berry jam.

“Wow,” Ollie whispered. “I feel happy.”

Mina nodded. “Art can help us feel our feelings.”

Ollie thought about that. He looked at Tiko. “How do you feel now?”

Tiko touched his sunshine stone. “I feel brave,” he said. “And proud.”

Ollie felt proud too. He wanted to share the good feeling. He filled a small cup with water. He dipped a fluffy herb in it. He brushed the mint leaves so they shone. “Here, plants,” he said. “This is a gift for you. Thank you for colors.”

Mina fluttered to the railing. “I will tell the other balcony friends to come see,” she said. “Sharing art is generous.”

Soon, a ladybug crawled over. A sparrow peeked from above. Even the shy tomato plant stood a little taller. Ollie laughed. “Our art makes friends come together!”

The gray clouds thinned. A patch of blue peeked out. Sunlight touched the stones. They glittered like tiny stars.

Ollie put a paw on his chest. “Today I learned that my ideas can grow, just like plants,” he said. “When I felt unsure, I tried. I used what I had. I shared. And now our garden smiles.”

Mina twirled in the light. “That is creative expression,” she said. “You showed your heart.”

Tiko nodded. “And you helped my heart feel brave.”

Ollie hugged his friends gently. “We can make new art tomorrow,” he said. “We can make music with the wind chime, and paint with berry colors, and build with sticks.”

“Yes!” Mina and Tiko said together.

Ollie looked at the garden, bright and happy. He felt calm, kind, and strong. He whispered to the plants, “Thank you for helping us make something beautiful.”

The End.
