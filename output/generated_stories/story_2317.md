# Bubbles the Dolphin’s Painty Problem

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash, wiggle, and make silly squeaks. Today, Bubbles swam into the creative art room, where there were big windows, bright sunshine, and colorful shelves. Paint pots sat like rainbow shells. Brushes stood like tall sea grass. Paper felt smooth under Bubbles’s flippers. The room smelled like fresh paper and sweet soap.

“Wow!” Bubbles squeaked. “I want to paint a big ocean friend!”

Bubbles bounced happily, flicking a fin. He dipped a brush into blue paint. Drip, drop, drip! “Hee-hee!” Bubbles giggled. The blue looked like tiny waves. He swished the brush across the paper. Swish-swish! He made a big round body. He added little eyes and a smiling mouth.

“I’m painting a happy whale!” Bubbles said.

Bubbles felt excited. He wanted to go faster. He wanted more colors. He dipped into green. Then yellow. Then red. Splat! A big red drop fell on the floor. Bubbles didn’t see it. He was too busy swirling and twirling the brush in the air.

Miss Coral, the art helper, smiled and said, “Bubbles, remember our healthy-habits rules for the art room.”

Bubbles tilted his head. “Healthy-habits? Like eating kelp snacks?”

Miss Coral nodded. “Yes! Healthy-habits help our bodies and our spaces. We wash our fins, we keep our area clean, and we take calm breaths. Clean space, calm body, happy hearts.”

Bubbles wiggled. “Okay!” He washed his fins in the bubbly sink. The water felt warm and tickly. Swish, swish. He dried them on a soft towel. Then he went right back to painting. He wanted to finish the whale’s splash.

Bubbles dipped his brush again. He forgot to tap the extra drops. Drip, drip, drip! More paint dotted the table and the floor. He slid a little on the red drop. “Whoops!” His tail bumped a cup of water.

Uh-oh.

The cup tipped. Water spilled. Brushes rolled. Blue water ran like a tiny river down the table. It splashed onto a friend’s paper. “My sun!” gasped Star the seahorse. Her bright yellow circle turned pale and watery.

Bubbles stopped. His tummy felt fizzy. His cheeks got warm. He looked at the messy floor, the wet table, and Star’s soggy sun. He felt a small sting in his heart.

“I’m sorry, Star,” Bubbles said softly. “I made a big mess.”

Star blinked. She looked sad. “My sun is squishy now.”

Miss Coral swam over with gentle eyes. “Thank you for saying sorry, Bubbles. Saying sorry is a healthy habit for our hearts. Now, let’s use logical thinking to solve this problem.”

Bubbles took a slow, deep breath in. He counted, “One… two… three.” He blew the air out like tiny bubbles. “Puff…” His shoulders relaxed.

Miss Coral asked, “What do we need to make it safe and clean?”

Bubbles looked around. He used his eyes like little helpers. He pointed. “We need towels for the water. We need a trash bin for paper bits. We need to tap-tap the brush so it won’t drip. And we need to move the cup away from the edge.”

“That is smart thinking,” Miss Coral said. “Let’s do it step by step.”

Step one: Bubbles got towels. He placed them on the spill. Squish, squish! The towels drank up the water. It felt cool on his fin.

Step two: He wiped the floor where the red drop was. Rub, rub. The floor looked shiny again.

Step three: He tapped his brush over the paint pot. Tap-tap-tap. No drips! He put the cup of water in the middle of the table. Safe and steady.

Step four: He turned to Star. “Can I help fix your sun?”

Star’s eyes brightened. “Maybe we can make a rainbow sun!”

Bubbles smiled. “Yes! We can use fresh paper.” He brought Star a clean sheet. He held it gently so it did not bend. Star dabbed yellow in the center. Bubbles added soft orange rings. They worked slowly. Tap-tap, swish-swish. No drips now.

Miss Coral handed them a clean sponge for the old paper. “You two made a new plan. That is logical thinking. And you kept the art room healthy by cleaning up.”

Bubbles felt proud. His heart felt light again. “I like our healthy-habits,” he said. “Wash fins. Tap brushes. Keep cups safe. Breathe slow. Say sorry. Clean up.”

Star giggled. “And share!”

Bubbles nodded. “And share.”

They both looked at Bubbles’s whale. It needed one more splash. Bubbles tapped his brush gently. Tap-tap. He painted tiny blue drops jumping high. He wiped a small spot on the table when a teeny dot fell. Wipe-wipe.

The art room smelled fresh again. The light felt warm on their backs. The paper felt smooth and dry. The brushes stood tall and clean.

Miss Coral clapped softly. “You solved your painty problem with healthy habits and kind words.”

Bubbles squeaked happily. “I’m sorry for the mess, Star. Thank you for painting with me.”

Star smiled. “I forgive you, Bubbles. Your idea helped my sun shine again.”

Bubbles took one more deep breath in and out. Calm and steady. He looked at the whale and the rainbow sun. They glittered in the sunshine.

Bubbles whispered, “Clean space, calm body, happy hearts.”

“Happy hearts,” Star echoed.

They hung their art to dry. Then they washed brushes together. Swish, swish in the soapy water. They dried the table one more time. The room glowed with colors and care.

Bubbles flipped his tail and did a tiny, careful splash. Not too big. Just right. He laughed. “I can play and be careful too!”

Miss Coral smiled. “That is the lesson, Bubbles.”

And Bubbles remembered: when a problem spills, use your brain, use your breath, clean your space, say sorry, and try again.

The End.
