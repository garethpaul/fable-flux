# Gigi the Giraffe Learns to Share

In a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls and fluffy white clouds painted on the ceiling, lived a thoughtful giraffe named Gigi. Her long neck reached almost to the top of the room, and her gentle brown eyes sparkled with kindness. The bedroom smelled like lavender and felt warm and safe.

Gigi loved her special corner where she kept her favorite things: colorful picture books, smooth wooden blocks, and a shiny red ball that bounced just right. She would spend hours reading stories and building tall towers with her blocks.

One sunny morning, Gigi heard tiny footsteps in the hallway. *Tap, tap, tap.* Three little mice peeked around the bedroom door. Their whiskers twitched nervously.

"Hello," squeaked the smallest mouse named Pip. "We're new here. Could we... could we maybe play in your room? We don't have any toys."

Gigi's first thought was to say no. These were HER special things! But then she looked at the mice's hopeful faces and remembered how lonely she sometimes felt.

"Well," Gigi said slowly, "I suppose we could talk about it. What would you like to play with?"

The middle mouse, Dot, pointed at the wooden blocks. "Those look fun! We could build something together."

"And maybe read a story?" added the biggest mouse, Max, looking at the colorful books.

Gigi felt worried. "But what if you break my things? What if you don't put them back in the right place?"

Pip's ears drooped. "We promise to be very careful. We just want to play and learn."

Gigi thought hard, her long neck swaying gently. "Okay, let's make a deal. How about we take turns? I'll share my blocks for thirty minutes, then I get them back for thirty minutes. Does that sound fair?"

The mice's eyes lit up. "Yes! That sounds perfect!" they squeaked together.

"And," Gigi continued, "we can all read the books together. I love the sound of different voices reading the same story."

So they began. First, Gigi and the mice built an amazing castle with the wooden blocks. Gigi used her height to place blocks up high, while the mice scurried around adding details at the bottom. The blocks felt smooth and cool in their paws, and they made satisfying *click* sounds when they fit together perfectly.

"This is better than building alone," Gigi realized, watching their beautiful creation grow.

When it was Gigi's turn with the blocks, she built a tall tower. But instead of playing alone, she asked, "Would you like to help me make it even taller?"

The mice cheered and helped steady the blocks as Gigi carefully placed each one higher and higher.

For story time, they all snuggled on the soft, squishy bed. Gigi read in her gentle, deep voice, while Pip squeaked the mouse characters, Dot made sound effects, and Max turned the pages with his tiny paws. The room filled with laughter and the rustling of turning pages.

"This is the most fun I've ever had," said Gigi, her heart feeling warm and full.

"Us too!" squeaked the mice. "Thank you for sharing and working together with us."

From that day on, Gigi learned that talking about problems and making fair deals made everyone happy. She discovered that sharing and teamwork made everything more fun, and that different voices made stories come alive in wonderful new ways.

The End.
