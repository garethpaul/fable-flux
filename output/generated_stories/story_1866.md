# Ollie Otter Helps at the Home Office

Ollie was a playful otter with bright eyes and soft whiskers. He lived with Mia in a cozy house. One sunny morning, Mia said, “I need to work in my home office today.” The office smelled like warm paper and lemony cleaner. The computer hummed. The keyboard went click, click, click.

Ollie clapped his little paws. “I can help!” he squeaked. He wanted to be helpful. He wanted to feel big and brave.

Mia smiled. “Thank you, Ollie. You can help by bringing me sticky notes and pens.” She set a small basket on the soft rug. “You can put supplies here.”

Ollie puffed up. “I can do it!” he said, feeling proud.

He picked up a stack of sticky notes. They were smooth and bright like tiny suns. But his paws were slippery. The notes slid—whoosh!—across the floor like little yellow leaves. “Oh no!” Ollie cried. His tummy felt twisty.

“It’s okay,” said Mia gently. “Try again, slowly.”

Ollie took a deep breath. In and out. He picked up the notes one by one. Pat, pat, pat. He set them in the basket. “I did it,” he whispered, tail wiggling.

Next, Ollie tried to carry three pens. Tap, tap, tap, they rolled in his paws. One pen slipped and plopped into Mia’s water cup. “Splish!”

Ollie’s eyes filled with tears. “I’m not good at this.”

Mia shook her head kindly. “You are learning. Learning takes time. Let’s fish out the pen together.”

They giggled as Mia used a spoon to scoop the pen. Drip, drip, drip. Ollie wiped the pen with a soft cloth. “I can keep trying,” he said, a little braver.

Mia had to join a video call. She put on comfy headphones. “I’ll be right here,” she said. “You can draw while I talk.” She gave Ollie crayons and paper.

Ollie drew a big blue river. He drew fish, and a boat, and a smiley sun. Scritch, scritch, scritch went the crayons. He felt calm and happy. He wanted to show Mia.

He tiptoed with his picture, but his tail bumped the basket. The basket tipped. Sticky notes floated down like butterflies. Pens rolled under the desk. Mia’s papers slid—whoa!—and one paper drifted to the floor.

On the screen, people said, “Hello!” Mia’s eyes grew wide. Ollie’s heart thumped. “I made a mess,” he whispered. His whiskers drooped.

Mia pressed a button to pause the sound. She looked at Ollie. “Are you okay?”

“I messed up,” Ollie said. “I wanted to help. I wanted to show you. I’m sorry.”

Mia knelt and hugged him. He smelled like river and crayons. “Thank you for telling me,” she said. “Accidents happen. You are loved when things go right and when things go wrong.”

Ollie sniffled. “Can I fix it?”

“That’s a great plan,” Mia said. “Let’s make amends together.”

Ollie wiped his cheeks. He crawled under the desk. It was cool and a little dusty. He found the pens. “Found them!” he chirped. He gathered the sticky notes and stacked them, tap, tap, tap. He smoothed the paper with gentle paws. “All neat!”

Mia smiled. “You are being very persistent.”

Ollie looked at his paws. “I can do hard things if I go slow,” he said. He felt warm and strong inside. His self-confidence grew like a bubble, round and light.

Mia went back to the call. Ollie sat close, listening to the soft murmur of voices and the steady hum of the computer. He held his drawing and waited. Waiting was hard. He wiggled, then remembered: slow and steady.

When Mia finished, she turned to Ollie. “Thank you for waiting. May I see your picture now?”

Ollie held up the blue river picture. “This is you in your boat,” he said. “And this is me, swimming and helping.”

Mia clapped. “It’s wonderful! I like how you kept trying. I like how you fixed the mess. That shows courage.”

Ollie grinned. “I felt small. Then I tried again. Now I feel big inside.”

Mia nodded. “That feeling is self-confidence. It grows when you try, even after mistakes.”

Ollie puffed his chest. “I can try again,” he said. He picked up one pen. He walked slowly. Step, step, step. He set it in the basket. Click. He picked up one sticky note pad. Pat. He placed it gently. He looked at Mia. She gave him a thumbs-up.

“See?” said Mia. “You did it.”

Ollie twirled. “I did it!” he sang. The office felt bright. The lemon smell seemed extra sunny. The keyboard clicks sounded like tiny cheers.

He padded to the window and saw the sky, soft and blue. He took a deep breath. In and out. “I can learn. I can help. I can make things right,” he said.

Mia hugged him again. “And I accept you just as you are.”

Ollie hugged back. “And I accept me, too,” he whispered.

They put the supplies in a neat line. They stuck one yellow note on the wall. It said: Try, try, try.

Ollie smiled and tapped the note. “I’m brave,” he said. “I keep going.”

“Yes,” said Mia. “You kept going, you made amends, and you believed in yourself. That’s how we grow.”

Ollie’s whiskers wiggled with joy. “I’m Ollie Otter,” he said. “I can do hard things, one small step at a time.”

The End.
