# Splash the Dolphin's Special Room

Splash the dolphin lived in a magical house where sea creatures could visit land. Today was very special - Grandma Whale was coming to stay in the guest room!

Splash swam excitedly through the hallway, his smooth gray skin glistening. "Click-click-whistle!" he sang happily, which meant "I can't wait!" in dolphin language.

The guest room was beautiful, with soft blue walls that looked like ocean waves and a big, comfortable water bed. Splash had helped his mama decorate it with pretty seashells from around the world - pink ones from warm tropical seas and sparkly white ones from cold arctic waters.

"Splash," called Mama Dolphin from the kitchen, "remember what we talked about?"

"Yes, Mama!" Splash replied, spinning in a happy circle. "The guest room is Grandma Whale's special space when she visits."

But as the day went on, Splash kept forgetting. First, he swam into the guest room to play with his favorite ball. Then he brought all his toys inside to build a coral castle.

"Splash," said Papa Dolphin gently, finding him arranging toy fish on the guest bed. "What are you doing?"

Splash's fins drooped a little. "I wanted to make the room fun for Grandma!"

Papa smiled and sat beside him. "That's very thoughtful, little one. But remember, everyone needs their own special space. When Grandma arrives, this room will be her private place to rest and feel comfortable."

"But I want to play with her!" Splash said, his voice squeaking with excitement.

"And you can!" Papa explained. "In the living room, the kitchen, and the garden. But the guest room will be where Grandma can have quiet time when she needs it."

Splash thought about this. He remembered when his friend Finn the Fish had visited last week. Finn had felt overwhelmed when too many sea creatures crowded into his sleeping space. "Oh!" Splash said, his eyes lighting up. "Like how I feel better when I have my own room to rest in?"

"Exactly!" Papa beamed. "Everyone around the world - dolphins, whales, fish, and even land creatures - needs personal space sometimes."

Just then, Splash had a wonderful idea. His innovative mind started working! "Papa, what if I make Grandma a welcome sign for OUTSIDE her room? Then she'll know we love her, but she'll also know the room is hers!"

"What a creative solution!" Papa said proudly.

Splash quickly gathered colorful seaweed and shells. He carefully arranged them into a beautiful sign that read "Welcome Grandma Whale!" with pictures of hearts and fish.

When Grandma Whale arrived that evening, her eyes twinkled when she saw the sign. "Oh, Splash! This is beautiful! And how thoughtful of you to put it outside my room."

"I wanted you to feel welcome AND have your own special space," Splash explained, bouncing excitedly in the water.

Grandma Whale gave him the biggest, warmest hug. "You know what? That shows you understand something very important - that caring for others means respecting their boundaries too."

That night, Splash felt proud as he swam to his own room. He had learned that showing love sometimes means giving others space, and that this idea worked for creatures all around the world.

"Good night, Grandma!" he called softly from the hallway.

"Good night, my thoughtful little dolphin!" she called back.

And Splash fell asleep knowing he had found the perfect way to be caring and respectful at the same time.

The End.
