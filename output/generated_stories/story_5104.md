# Oliver Otter's Special Discovery

Oliver the otter lived in a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls and fluffy white clouds painted on the ceiling. He had sleek brown fur and bright curious eyes that sparkled when he smiled.

Every morning, Oliver loved to splash in his little water bowl. He would wiggle his whiskers and make happy chirping sounds. "Splash, splash!" he would say as water droplets danced around him.

One morning, Oliver woke up feeling excited. "Today feels special!" he squeaked. He looked around his bedroom and noticed something new on the wooden desk near the window.

There was a shiny red notebook with blank white pages and a bright yellow pencil beside it. The morning sunlight made them glow like treasures.

"What are these?" Oliver wondered aloud. He had never seen a notebook and pencil before. His little paws touched the smooth cover. It felt cool and interesting.

Oliver tried to pick up the pencil, but his paws were still wet from his morning splash. The pencil slipped and fell with a small "thunk" on the carpet.

"Oh dear," Oliver said, looking at his damp paws. He tried again, but the pencil kept slipping. His whiskers drooped with disappointment.

Then Oliver had an idea. "Maybe I need clean, dry paws!" he said. He remembered how good it felt when his paws were clean and dry.

Oliver went to his special towel - soft and fluffy like a cloud. He rubbed his paws carefully, making sure each tiny toe was clean and dry. The towel smelled like fresh flowers.

"Much better!" Oliver chirped. His paws felt fresh and ready.

Now Oliver could hold the pencil properly. It felt smooth and light in his clean paws. He opened the notebook and saw all the empty pages waiting for him.

"I wonder what I can make," Oliver said thoughtfully. He pressed the pencil gently on the paper and made his first mark - a small circle.

"Look! I made something!" Oliver squeaked with joy. The circle looked like a bubble from his water bowl.

Oliver discovered he could make lines too - straight ones and wiggly ones. He drew wavy lines that looked like water. He made dots that looked like raindrops.

As he drew, Oliver felt proud and happy. Each mark was a new discovery. "Writing and drawing are like magic!" he said, his eyes twinkling.

Soon, Oliver had filled a whole page with circles, lines, and dots. It looked beautiful to him - like his own special language.

"I made this all by myself!" Oliver said, holding up his artwork. The paper rustled softly in his clean, dry paws.

From that day on, Oliver always cleaned and dried his paws before writing or drawing. He discovered that clean paws helped him hold things better and made everything more fun.

Every morning after his splash, Oliver would dry his paws and create new pictures in his notebook. He drew fish swimming, clouds floating, and even himself playing.

Oliver learned that taking care of himself helped him discover new and wonderful things. His bedroom became filled with his beautiful drawings, and he felt proud of each discovery he made.

"Clean paws help me create!" Oliver would say happily, wiggling his whiskers as he wrote and drew amazing pictures every day.

The End.
