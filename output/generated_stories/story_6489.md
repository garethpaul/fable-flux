# Bibi Sings in the Laundry Room

Bibi was a tiny singing bird with bright blue feathers and a brave little heart. She loved to sing good-morning songs. One day, she fluttered into a cozy laundry room. It smelled like warm soap and cotton. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer clicked, “Clack, clack.” Piles of soft towels sat like fluffy mountains.

“This is a good place to practice my song,” Bibi chirped. “Tweet, tweet-tee-tweet!”

Bibi hopped onto a laundry basket and peeked around. She saw a big bottle with a picture of a flower. She saw a box with a scoop on top. She saw a paper sign on the wall with big letters that said, “STOP. READ. FOLLOW DIRECTIONS.” Bibi leaned close. “Hmm,” she said. “Directions help you do things the right way.”

Just then, a small sock slipped off a high shelf. “Plop!” It fell behind the washing machine.

“Oh no!” squeaked the sock in a tiny voice. “I’m stuck! I can’t get back to my pair!”

Bibi fluffed her feathers. “Don’t worry, little sock. I will help.”

Bibi peeked down the narrow space. It was dark and dusty. She could hear the washing machine say, “Whirr-wooosh.” The space was too tight for Bibi to fly in. She took a big breath. “I need a plan,” she said. “I need to use my brain, not just my wings.”

Bibi looked around the laundry room. She looked with her eyes, listened with her ears, and thought with her brave little heart.

“What do I have?” she asked. “I have a basket. I have a scoop. I have towels. I have a clothespin. I have… string!”

On a hook, Bibi saw a ball of soft string. Next to it, she saw another sign. Bibi fluttered closer and read slowly, sounding out the words. “Use… tools… safely. Ask… for… help.” Bibi nodded. “I will follow directions.”

She sang a little plan song. “Look, think, try, and ask! Look, think, try, and ask!”

Bibi tugged the string and tied a small loop. She clicked the clothespin on the end. “This can be a tiny grabber,” she chirped.

“But I should ask for help too,” Bibi said. “The washer and dryer are big and loud. They know this room.”

She hopped onto the washer. “Mr. Washer,” Bibi sang, “can you hum a little softer so I can hear the sock?”

The washer hummed kindly. “Whirr… whirr,” it said, a little softer now.

Bibi hopped to the dryer. “Ms. Dryer, please be still while I help.”

The dryer clicked once and stayed quiet.

Bibi listened. “Sock, can you talk to me?”

“I’m here,” the sock squeaked. “It’s dusty and a little scary.”

“You are brave,” Bibi sang. “I am brave, too. We will do this together.”

Bibi lay on her tummy and slowly lowered the string and clothespin into the narrow space. “I will move slowly,” she said. “Slow is safe.” She remembered the sign: Follow directions. She counted. “One… two… three…” Counting helped her go slow.

“Sock, can you wiggle to the sound of my song?” Bibi asked.

“I will try,” said the sock. Bibi sang a soft, steady tune. “Tweet, tweet, tee-tee-tweet.” The sock wiggled closer to the clothespin.

“Almost there,” Bibi whispered. She could smell a tiny puff of dust. “Ah-choo!” she sneezed, but she kept her wings steady. “I can do hard things,” she told herself.

“Clip!” The clothespin grabbed the edge of the sock.

“I got you!” Bibi chirped. “Now I will pull gently.” She pulled with careful, even tugs. “Left, right, left, right.” She listened for scrapes and stops. She felt the string move smooth against her claws.

With one last tug, “Pop!” The sock slid out and flew into the basket with a happy bounce.

“You did it!” cheered the sock. “Thank you!”

Bibi smiled. “We did it together. I used a tool. I followed directions. I listened and I was brave.”

Just then, the laundry room door opened. A kind person walked in and saw the string, the clip, and the happy sock. “Well done, little helper,” they said softly. “You used your knowledge and you were careful.”

Bibi puffed with pride. “I learned something,” she said. “When there is a problem, I can stop, look, and think. I can use what I have. I can follow directions. I can ask for help. That is called resourcefulness!”

The sock giggled. “And I found my pair!” Its matching sock peeked out from the towel mountain. “Hooray!” they sang together.

The washer hummed a cheerful tune. The dryer clicked like a clap. The room smelled fresh and warm, like clean rain and sunshine.

Bibi perched on the basket and sang a new song, bright and proud. “Try, try, tiny friend. Use your tools and ask a friend. Listen close and take your time. Step by step, you’ll do just fine.”

Bibi flapped her wings. “I will remember this,” she said. “Next time there is a tricky thing, I will be calm, I will be kind, and I will be resourceful.”

She gave the little sock a gentle peck. “Good-bye, friend. Stay with your pair.”

“We will!” the socks chimed.

Bibi flew to the window, the warm soap smell behind her and the sun ahead. Her heart felt big and happy.

The lesson was clear: Use your brain, use your tools, follow directions, and ask for help. That is being resourceful and brave.

The End.
