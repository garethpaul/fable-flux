# Ellie’s Porch Plan

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with big soft ears and a curious trunk. She liked to sit on the peaceful front porch with her grandma. The porch smelled like fresh lemons from a pitcher of lemonade. The wood floor felt warm under Ellie’s feet. Birds chirped in the trees. The breeze was quiet and kind.

Today, Ellie had a plan. She wanted to paint a big sunny sign that said, “Welcome, Friends!” She set out her small paint jars. Red, yellow, and blue. She laid soft brushes on a cloth. She put on her tiny apron. “I am ready,” Ellie said. Her trunk gave a happy wiggle.

Just then, her neighbor, Tiko the turtle, clunk-clunked up the porch steps. He wore a dusty cap and carried a small drum. “Hi, Ellie!” he said. “I want to play my drum on the porch.”

“Hi, Tiko,” said Ellie. “I want to paint my sign on the porch.”

They both smiled. But then they both looked at the same porch space. The porch was cozy, but there was one big sunny spot. It was the best spot for painting. It was also the best spot for drumming.

Tiko sat his drum in the sunny spot. “Boom-boom,” he tapped.

Ellie set her sign board in the same spot. “I need the sun,” she said softly. “The paint will dry.”

“I need the sun,” Tiko said. “The drum sounds happy here.”

Ellie’s ears drooped. Tiko’s head tucked a little into his shell. A tight feeling came to Ellie’s tummy. The porch did not feel peaceful now.

Grandma rocked in her chair and hummed a calm song. “Sweet ones,” she said, “I hear two good plans. But one porch.” Her eyes twinkled. “What can we do?”

Ellie took a deep breath. She smelled the lemon lemonade. She felt the warm wood under her feet. She remembered Grandma’s words: “When we have a bump, we can think, talk, and try.”

Ellie said, “Let’s think.” She tapped her trunk on her head. “We can take turns. Or we can share the space. Or we can move something.”

Tiko rubbed his chin. “Hmm,” he said. “What if I drum in the shade?” He tapped the drum in the shadow. “Thump-thump.” It sounded a little dull.

Ellie tried her paint in the shade. “Splot,” went the brush. The paint was slow to dry. She made a small frown.

Tiko peeked at Ellie’s face. “I want your paint to dry,” he said kindly.

Ellie peeked at Tiko’s drum. “I want your drum to sound happy,” she said gently.

Grandma smiled. “You two are kind. Keep thinking.”

Ellie looked around the porch. She saw a small stool. She saw a bright, round mirror on the wall. The mirror caught the sun and made a warm patch on the floor. “Oh!” Ellie said. “What if we move the mirror to make a sun spot over there?”

Tiko’s eyes grew wide. “That is a smart try!”

Ellie and Tiko carefully turned the mirror. A warm patch of light slid across the wood like a golden puddle. It moved into the shady corner where Ellie had put her paints.

Ellie set her sign in the new sun patch. She brushed yellow paint. “Swoosh.” The paint shined and began to dry.

Tiko tried the drum in the original sunny spot. “Boom-boom-bah!” The sound was bright and bouncy.

They both grinned. “We did it!” said Ellie.

“Wait,” said Tiko. “What about the birds? The drum might scare them.”

Ellie looked at the tree. The birds hopped and fluttered. “Good thinking,” she said. “Let’s ask.”

Ellie lifted her trunk and spoke softly, “Hello, birds. We want to make sounds. Will that bother you?”

The birds chirped, “Tweet-tweet!” They tilted their heads. Grandma said, “How about soft drumming? Then the birds can sing, and Ellie can paint.”

Tiko nodded. “Soft drumming,” he agreed. He put a cloth over the drum. “Thup-thup,” went the drum, gentle like a heartbeat.

Ellie painted big, sunny letters. “W-e-l-c-o-m-e,” she said slowly. She made a bright yellow sun with a smile. She painted small blue flowers. She took her time. She breathed. She listened. Thup-thup. Tweet-tweet. Swoosh-splot.

Soon, the sign was dry. Ellie held it up. “Welcome, Friends!” it read. The letters were neat. The colors were bright. Her heart felt warm and proud.

Tiko tapped a happy rhythm. “We solved it,” he said. “We thought. We talked. We tried.”

“We were open,” Ellie said. “We listened to each other.”

Grandma poured lemonade. The cool drink tasted sweet and tart. The cup felt smooth in Ellie’s trunk. The porch felt peaceful again. The birds sang. The breeze kissed Ellie’s ears.

Ellie hung the sign on the porch rail. The porch looked even more friendly now.

Tiko smiled. “Next time, I can bring a small shaker,” he said. “It is quiet.”

Ellie nodded. “And I can set up my paints before I start,” she said. “That will help me be ready.”

Grandma raised her cup. “To kind hearts and smart plans,” she said.

Ellie and Tiko clinked their cups. “To kind hearts and smart plans!”

Lesson: When there is a problem, we can calm our bodies, use our words, listen to others, and try new ideas. That is how we make peace together.

The End.
