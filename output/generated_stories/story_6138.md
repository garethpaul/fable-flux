# Toby the Tortoise and the Rainbow Blocks

In a cozy living room filled with soft cushions and colorful toys, lived Toby the Tortoise. His shell was a beautiful green with golden spots, and he loved spending his days playing with all the wonderful toys scattered around the room.

One sunny morning, Toby slowly made his way across the fluffy carpet to his favorite corner where a big box of rainbow blocks sat waiting. The blocks were red, blue, yellow, green, purple, and orange - so bright and cheerful! Toby loved building tall towers with them.

"Good morning, blocks!" Toby said in his gentle voice. "Let's build something wonderful today!"

Just as Toby picked up a red block with his careful claws, he heard a tiny voice.

"Excuse me," squeaked the voice. "Could I play too?"

Toby looked around and saw a small gray mouse peeking out from behind the sofa. The mouse looked a little nervous, with her whiskers twitching.

"Hello there!" Toby smiled warmly. "I'm Toby. What's your name?"

"I'm Mila," said the mouse softly. "I've been watching you play with those beautiful blocks. They look so fun, but I wasn't sure if you'd want to share."

Toby's kind eyes sparkled. "Of course you can play! There are plenty of blocks for everyone. Come join me!"

Mila scurried over, her tiny paws pattering on the soft carpet. "Thank you, Toby! But I'm so small. I can only carry the tiny blocks."

"That's perfect!" Toby said encouragingly. "Small blocks are just as important as big ones. We can take turns and build together!"

Soon, a fluffy white rabbit hopped into the room. "Hop, hop! What are you doing?" asked the rabbit, her pink nose twitching with curiosity.

"We're building with blocks!" Mila squeaked excitedly. "Want to join us? I'm Mila, and this is Toby!"

"I'm Ruby!" said the rabbit. "I'd love to play, but I can only hop. I might knock things down."

Toby thought for a moment, then his face lit up. "Ruby, your hopping could help us! You could hop around our building and tell us how it looks from different sides!"

Ruby's ears perked up with joy. "Really? I can help that way?"

"Absolutely!" Toby nodded. "Everyone has something special to offer."

The three friends began to play together. Toby carefully placed the big blocks at the bottom with his steady claws. Mila used her tiny paws to add small blocks in just the right spots. Ruby hopped around, suggesting where to add more blocks and cheering them on.

"Your turn to pick the next color, Mila," Toby said, passing the box to her.

"Now Ruby gets to choose where it goes!" Mila added, passing the choice along.

They took turns choosing colors, placing blocks, and sharing ideas. Their tower grew taller and more beautiful with each block. It had red stripes, blue windows, yellow flowers, and a purple flag on top!

"Wow!" Ruby exclaimed, hopping in a happy circle. "We built the most amazing tower ever!"

"And we did it together," Toby said proudly. "Each of us made it special in our own way."

Mila clapped her tiny paws. "It's so much more fun when we all play together!"

From that day on, Toby, Mila, and Ruby played together every day in the living room. They learned that when everyone is included and takes turns, playtime becomes magical. No matter how different they were, they each had something wonderful to share.

The End.
