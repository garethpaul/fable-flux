# Buckle-Up, Little Fawn

A gentle deer fawn named Fern had soft, dappled fur and big, bright eyes. One sunny morning, Fern and her family were getting ready to ride in the family car. The car smelled like pine needles and lemon wipes. The seats were warm from the sun, and the buckles clicked like tiny beetles.

“Today we go to the park,” said Mama Deer, smiling. “But first, we prepare.”

Fern wiggled her hooves. “I want to go now!” she sang in a sing-song voice.

Papa Deer opened the car door. “We will go. We have a little routine. Routine helps us know what to do,” he said calmly.

Fern tilted her head. “What is roo-teen?”

“It’s our plan we follow every time,” Mama said. “It makes trips easy and safe.”

Fern liked easy. She liked safe. But she also liked to sing and draw and play. She looked at the backseat. The seat was cozy and smelled like cookies from last week. Her booster seat waited, soft and snug.

Mama Deer handed Fern a small travel bag. “This is your Prep Bag,” Mama said. “We pack it before we go.”

Fern peeked inside. There was a tiny water bottle, a crunchy apple slice in a cup, a small blanket with yellow stars, and a little notebook with four bright crayons. Red, blue, green, and sunny yellow.

Fern’s eyes sparkled. “I can draw!”

“Yes,” Mama said. “First we do our routine. Then we play.”

Papa Deer held up a hoof. “Step one: Potty and paws.” He wiggled his hooves. “Clean hooves, clean paws.”

Fern giggled and wiped her hooves with a soft wipe. It felt cool and squishy. “Step one done!” she chirped.

“Step two: Buckle check,” Mama said. Fern climbed into her booster. The fabric was smooth and the buckle made a happy click. Click! “Buckle snug?” Mama asked.

Fern pulled the straps. “Snug like a hug,” she said.

“Step three: Prep Bag,” Papa said. “What do we have?”

Fern took a breath. “Water. Apple. Blanket. Notebook. Four crayons.” She held each thing, one by one. The apple smelled sweet. The blanket was warm and fuzzy. The crayons were waxy and bright. She put everything back.

“Step four,” Mama said. “We talk about feelings.”

Fern put a hoof on her heart. “I feel excited,” she said. “I want to draw now.”

Mama nodded. “Thank you for telling us.”

Papa smiled. “We also feel excited. We also want to be safe. How can we do both?”

Fern thought. The car hummed softly as the engine started. A gentle breeze drifted in and smelled like grass. “We can make a plan,” she said.

“That is preparation,” Mama said. “What is your plan?”

Fern wiggled. “I draw when we are on the road. But I wait to uncap my crayons until after we leave the driveway. I keep my blanket on my lap. I take tiny sips of water.”

Papa tapped his chin. “That sounds smart. Here is my idea. Keep the crayons in the seat pocket until we pass the big red barn. Then you can draw. The barn will be our ‘Start Drawing’ sign.”

Fern looked out the window. She knew that barn. It was big and red and shiny. “Deal!” she said. “We made a plan.”

“That’s called negotiation,” Mama said kindly. “We listened. We agreed.”

The car rolled down the driveway. Crunch, crunch went the gravel. Fern felt the seat hug her. She watched birds swoop in the blue sky. Her tummy fluttered with happy jitters.

They turned onto the road. Trees waved hello. “There’s the big red barn!” Fern cheered.

“Start Drawing sign!” Papa said with a chuckle.

Fern reached into the seat pocket and pulled out her notebook and a blue crayon. She drew a little car with deer ears. She drew Mama, Papa, and herself, all buckled and smiling. The crayon made a soft scritch-scratch sound on the paper. It felt smooth and fun.

She showed Mama. “Look! Safe car. Happy deer.”

Mama’s eyes shone. “I love your art.”

Fern took a tiny sip of water. Sip. Sip. She nibbled her apple. Crunch. Crunch. Juice tickled her tongue. She placed the cup back in the bag. “I am prepared,” she said proudly.

Soon they reached the park. The car stopped. The engine went quiet. The air smelled like flowers and mud. Sunlight tickled Fern’s nose.

“Before we get out, what do we do?” Papa asked.

Fern smiled. “We do our routine again. Step one: Pause and plan. Step two: Unbuckle. Step three: Grab Prep Bag. Step four: Check feelings.”

Mama nodded. “How do you feel now?”

Fern touched her heart. “I feel proud. Our plan helped.”

They unbuckled. Click. They climbed out. Thump. Fern tucked her notebook under her arm. She saw ducks in the pond and heard them quack-quack. She wanted to run, but she stopped and took a breath.

“Preparation first,” she whispered. She held Mama’s hoof, and they walked together.

At the end of the day, back in the cozy car, Fern put her crayon drawing on her lap. She smiled at the colorful lines.

Mama said softly, “What did we learn?”

Fern beamed. “Preparation makes trips easy. Routine keeps me safe. And we can make plans together. I can be creative and still be ready.”

Papa added, “When we prepare, we have more time for fun.”

Fern nodded. “Prepared fawns have happy adventures.”

The End.
