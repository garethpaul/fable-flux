# Bouncy Roo and the Bathtub Stadium

Bouncy Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey. He loved to hop, wiggle, and cheer. One sunny morning, Bouncy Roo bounced into the bathroom with a happy thump-thump-thump. The smooth tiles felt cool on his toes. The room smelled like lemon soap, and warm steam puffed like tiny clouds.

“Today is sports day!” Bouncy Roo sang. “I will jump! I will stretch! I will build a place to play!”

Mama Roo peeked in with a smile. “Sports are fun, little one,” she said. “Let’s move our bodies safely. The bathroom can be a good place to practice gentle moves. We will build a soft play space.”

Bouncy Roo’s ears perked. “Build? In the bathroom?”

“Yes,” Mama Roo said. “We can make a bathtub stadium. We will keep it safe and dry.”

Bouncy Roo clapped. “Let’s do it!”

Together, they gathered towels and a rubber bath mat. The towels were fluffy and smelled like sunshine. The rubber mat felt squishy and grippy. Mama Roo laid the mat on the floor. Pat, pat. She put a big towel on the closed toilet lid—soft like a bench. She popped the bath toys into a basket. Clink, clack. Everything had a place.

“We are building our space,” Mama Roo said. “Sports need a good place to move. We make it safe. We make it neat.”

Bouncy Roo nodded. “Safe and neat!”

Mama Roo filled the bathtub with just a little warm water. Swish, swish. “This is our tiny pool,” she said. “Only feet in, and I stay close.”

Bouncy Roo dipped a toe. “Ooh! Warm!” He giggled as tiny waves tickled his ankles.

“First, we stretch,” Mama Roo said. “Stretching helps our muscles get ready.”

Bouncy Roo stood tall. He reached his paws to the sky. “Hello, ceiling!” he said. He touched his toes. “Hello, toes!” He rolled his shoulders. Roll, roll. He took a big breath. In and out. It smelled like minty toothpaste.

“Great job,” Mama Roo cheered. “Now, movement time!”

Bouncy Roo did tiny kangaroo hops on the rubber mat. Boing, boing, boing. His tail swished. His ears flopped. He laughed. “I feel bouncy!”

“Soft knees,” Mama Roo reminded. “We hop gently.”

“Soft knees,” Bouncy Roo echoed, landing like a feather.

“Next is balance,” Mama Roo said. She set a rolled towel on the floor like a balance beam. “Arms out. One foot in front of the other.”

Bouncy Roo stepped slowly. Step. Step. The towel squished under his foot. He wobbled, then steadied. “I can do it!”

“You are focused,” Mama Roo said. “Sports use focus.”

Bouncy Roo smiled. “I am strong and careful.”

They moved to the tub. “This is water marching,” Mama Roo said. “Lift your knees. March, march.”

Bouncy Roo marched in the warm water. Splash, splash. The tiny waves kissed his legs. He felt cool drops on his fur and heard plip-plop-plip. He grinned. “I love water marching!”

“Sports use many moves,” Mama Roo said. “Hopping, balancing, marching. We can also toss.”

“Toss?” Bouncy Roo’s eyes shone.

Mama Roo gave him a soft sponge ball. “Toss to me,” she said, standing by the tub. “Eyes on the ball.”

Bouncy Roo held the squishy ball. It smelled like clean soap. He bent his knees. “Ready, steady, toss!” The ball flew—whoosh!—and landed in Mama Roo’s paws.

“Great toss!” Mama Roo cheered. She tossed it back, gentle and slow. “Catch!”

Bouncy Roo caught it against his chest. “I did it! I did it!”

“You practiced,” Mama Roo said. “Practice helps us learn.”

They played a little game. Toss and catch. Toss and catch. Bouncy Roo missed once, and the ball plopped into the tub. “Oh no!”

“It’s okay,” Mama Roo said kindly. “We try again.”

Bouncy Roo tried again. He watched the ball. He kept his paws ready. Catch! “I kept trying!” he said proudly.

“Sports also use teamwork,” Mama Roo said. “We help each other.”

Bouncy Roo nodded. “We help and cheer.”

“Last, a cool-down,” Mama Roo said. “We breathe and stretch again.” They breathed in slowly and out like a soft sigh. The bathroom hummed with quiet. The warm air hugged them.

Bouncy Roo felt calm and happy. His muscles felt tingly and strong. He patted the rubber mat. “We built our stadium. We moved our bodies. We played a sport game!”

“Yes,” Mama Roo said. “We moved safely. We cleaned as we played. We took turns. We kept trying. That is the sports way.”

They put the ball back in the basket. Clink. They folded the towels. Fold, fold. They drained the tub. Glug, glug. The bathroom was neat again.

Bouncy Roo gave Mama Roo a squeeze. “I learned a big lesson,” he said. “Sports are fun when we build a safe space, warm up, move our bodies, and keep trying. Teamwork helps too!”

Mama Roo kissed his nose. “You learned well, little roo. Your body is strong. Your heart is brave. Your mind is ready.”

Bouncy Roo bounced once more, soft and small. “I am ready for more sports tomorrow!”

“Tomorrow,” Mama Roo agreed, smiling. “And today, we celebrate with a big drink of water.”

They sipped cool water. Sip, sip. It felt crisp and fresh. Bouncy Roo beamed. He felt proud, calm, and happy.

The bathroom was quiet now, but in Bouncy Roo’s mind, the bathtub stadium still cheered. He could hear the soft crowd: Go, Roo, go!

The End.
