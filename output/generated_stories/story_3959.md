# Penny Panda's Garden Helper

Penny the panda loved her balcony garden. She had soft black and white fur that felt like velvet. Every morning, she stepped outside to smell the fresh air. The sun felt warm on her fuzzy ears.

Penny's garden had red tomatoes, green lettuce, and purple flowers. She watered them with her little yellow watering can. The water made gentle splashing sounds. Penny hummed happy songs while she worked.

"Good morning, my beautiful plants," Penny said sweetly. She touched each leaf with her gentle paws. "Time to grow big and strong!"

One day, Penny heard tiny crying sounds. "Sniff, sniff, sob!" She looked around carefully. Behind a big flower pot sat a small brown mouse.

"Hello there," said Penny kindly. "Why are you sad?"

The mouse wiped his tiny eyes. "I'm Milo. I wanted to make a pretty picture for my mama. But I don't have any colors to paint with."

Penny's heart felt warm and caring. She thought about her colorful garden. The flowers had many beautiful colors - red, yellow, purple, and pink.

"I have an idea!" said Penny excitedly. "We can make paint from my flowers. Would you like to try?"

Milo's eyes grew big and sparkly. "Really? You would share your flowers?"

"Of course!" said Penny. "Gardens are for sharing joy."

Penny showed Milo how to pick flower petals gently. They gathered red petals from roses and yellow petals from sunflowers. The petals felt soft and smelled sweet.

"Now we mash them up," explained Penny. She gave Milo a small bowl. "Add a little water and squish, squish, squish!"

Milo giggled as he squished the petals. Purple juice came out! "Look, Penny! It's working!"

They made red paint, yellow paint, and purple paint. Penny found some white paper in her house. Milo dipped his tiny paws in the flower paint.

"I'm painting a heart for mama," said Milo happily. He made careful brush strokes with his paw. The colors were bright and cheerful.

Penny painted too. She made a picture of their garden with all the flowers. "Art is more fun with friends," she said with a big smile.

When Milo finished his painting, he held it up proudly. "Thank you, Penny! Mama will love this so much."

"You're very welcome," said Penny. "But I need to tell you something honest. Some of my flowers might look different now."

Milo looked worried. "Oh no! Did we hurt your garden?"

Penny pointed to the flowers. Some had fewer petals, but they still looked beautiful. "Gardens are strong," she explained. "They grow back even prettier. And we made something wonderful together!"

Milo hugged Penny's fluffy leg. "You're the best friend ever! Can we paint again tomorrow?"

"Yes!" said Penny. "We can paint every day if you want. My garden loves making new friends happy."

As the sun set, Penny watered her plants again. She felt proud of helping Milo. The garden smelled even sweeter than before.

"Thank you, garden," whispered Penny. "You helped me help a friend today."

The flowers seemed to dance in the gentle breeze, as if they were saying "you're welcome" back.

The End.
