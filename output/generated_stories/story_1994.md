# Poppy Penguin Helps in the Attic

Poppy was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a happy waddle. One sunny afternoon, Poppy climbed the creaky stairs to a quiet attic. The attic smelled like old paper and cinnamon. Dust sparkled in the shafts of light. Boxes sat in neat rows, like sleepy little houses.

“Hello, attic!” Poppy chirped. “I’m here to help!”

Poppy was volunteering to help tidy the attic at the community house. Poppy had a big feather duster, a tiny broom, and a small trash bag. Poppy also had a lunch box with carrot sticks, a water bottle, and a soft washcloth.

Grandma Dot, the kind caretaker, smiled from the stairway. “Thank you, Poppy. Please be careful. Take breaks and drink water.”

“I will!” said Poppy, patting the water bottle. “Healthy helpers take care of their bodies.”

Poppy began to dust. Swish, swish! The duster tickled Poppy’s flippers. “Hee-hee!” Poppy giggled. Poppy swept the floor. Sweep, sweep! Little bits of fuzz scooted into a pile. Poppy hummed a happy tune and made the work into a game. “Sweep and sing! Sweep and swing!”

Then Poppy saw a big box labeled, “Buttons and Bells.” Poppy lifted the lid. Ring-a-ding! Tinka-tinka! Bright buttons and tiny bells shone like candies. Poppy’s eyes grew round. “Ooooh!”

Poppy wanted to sort the buttons. Big ones in one bowl, small ones in another. Poppy wanted to practice careful hands. “I can do it,” Poppy said. “Practice makes me proud.”

Poppy reached in, but a dusty puff flew up. Achoo! Poppy rubbed itchy eyes and reached again.

“Wait,” Poppy whispered to self. “Healthy habits first.” Poppy took the washcloth, went to the little sink in the corner, and washed flippers with warm water. Swish, swish, bubble, bubble. Poppy dried them on a clean towel. Poppy also put on a small mask Grandma Dot had set out for dusty jobs. “There,” Poppy said. “Safe and clean.”

Poppy sorted buttons. Click, clack, plink! Some buttons slipped. Some rolled away. “Oh no!” Poppy said. “This is hard.”

A little mouse peeked out from behind a hat box. The mouse wore a tiny blue ribbon. “Squeak-hello,” said the mouse. “I’m Mimi. That bell is mine.” Mimi pointed to a tiny bell under a scarf.

“Oh! Hi, Mimi,” Poppy said. “I’m Poppy. I’m helping.”

Mimi scurried closer. “You dropped a few. Do you need help?”

Poppy felt a little shy. “I want to do it right,” Poppy said. “But my flippers are slippery.”

Mimi nodded. “We can practice. Slow and steady. We can also take tiny breaks.”

Poppy took a sip of water. Glug, glug. “Ahh,” said Poppy. “My body feels good. Thank you, water.”

Poppy and Mimi made a plan. Poppy would pick up one button at a time and say, “Big” or “Small.” Mimi would ring the bell softly when a bowl was filled. Ring-a-ding.

They began. “Big,” said Poppy, plinking a button into the big bowl. “Small,” said Poppy, dropping a tiny one into the small bowl. Mimi rang the bell. Ring-a-ding! They smiled and kept going. Practice felt like a calm dance.

Suddenly, a tall cat named Tully padded in. Tully’s tail swished. “What’s all this?” asked Tully. “Those bells are my favorite toys!” Tully batted a bell. Tinka-tinka! Buttons bounced everywhere.

Poppy’s heart thumped. “Oh no! Our sorting!”

Mimi squeaked and hid behind the box. Tully blinked. “I didn’t mean to make a mess. I just got excited.”

Poppy took a deep breath. In through the beak, out slow. “It’s okay, Tully,” Poppy said. “We are sorting to help Grandma Dot. We want the attic clean. We can share the bells, but we need to finish our job.”

Tully’s ears twitched. “Share? And help?” Tully sat down. “I can help. I can use my soft paws.”

Poppy nodded. “Thank you. Let’s make a rule. We work for five minutes, then play for one minute. We also wash or wipe our hands if they get dusty, drink water, and take calm breaths.”

Mimi peeked out. “That sounds fair.”

They counted together. “One, two, three…” Poppy picked up a button. Tully held it gently so it did not roll away. Mimi guided the bowls. Plink, plink, plink. When five minutes passed, Mimi rang the bell. Ring! They shook out their arms and took sips of water. They smiled.

Grandma Dot came up to check. She sniffed the cinnamon air and smiled. “It looks lovely! And you are all working so well.”

Poppy puffed with pride. “We had a problem,” Poppy said. “Buttons went everywhere. But we used healthy habits. We washed. We drank water. We took calm breaths. We made a plan. We practiced. We shared. We took turns. We made a rule for work and play.”

Tully purred. “I learned to be gentle and to help.”

Mimi squeaked. “I learned to speak up and to take breaks.”

Grandma Dot clapped softly. “Thank you for volunteering. You helped our community.”

They finished the last bowl. Plink! Mimi rang the final bell. Ring-a-ding! The attic looked neat. The air felt fresh. The sun made a warm stripe on the floor. Poppy opened the lunch box and shared carrot sticks. Crunch, crunch. Sweet and crisp.

Poppy patted the water bottle. “Healthy helpers are happy helpers,” Poppy said.

Mimi smiled. “Practice makes our skills grow.”

Tully swished a tail. “Working together solves problems.”

Poppy hugged friends. “Today we learned: take care of your body, be calm, make a plan, and help others. That is a healthy habit.”

They all nodded, tails and ribbons and flippers wiggling with joy.

The End.
