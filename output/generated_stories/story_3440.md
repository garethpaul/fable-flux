# Rascal's Clean-Up Song

In a sunny backyard filled with chirping birds and buzzing bees, lived a little raccoon named Rascal. Rascal loved making music more than anything in the whole world. He would tap-tap-tap on flower pots, shake-shake-shake the garden tools, and sing happy songs all day long.

But there was one big problem. Rascal didn't like taking baths or washing his paws. His fluffy gray fur was getting dirty and messy, and his little black paws were covered in mud from digging in the garden.

"La-la-la!" Rascal sang as he banged on an old bucket. "Music is the best!"

His friend Bella the butterfly fluttered over. "Hi Rascal! Your music sounds wonderful, but... well..." She wrinkled her tiny nose. "You smell a little stinky."

Rascal stopped playing and looked down at his dirty paws. "I don't like getting wet," he said sadly. "Water feels cold and scary."

"But being clean feels so good!" chirped Charlie the cardinal from a nearby tree. "When I splash in the birdbath, my feathers feel light and fluffy!"

Rascal shook his head. "I'm too scared. What if I can't make music if I'm all wet?"

Just then, Mama Raccoon came outside with a big, warm, sudsy bucket. The bubbles sparkled like tiny rainbows in the sunshine. "Time for your bath, little one," she said gently.

"No, no, no!" Rascal cried, hiding behind his favorite drum. "I don't want to!"

Mama Raccoon sat down beside him. "What if we made bath time musical?" she asked with a twinkle in her eye.

"Musical?" Rascal peeked out curiously.

"Yes! We can splash to the beat, scrub-a-dub to a rhythm, and sing washing songs!" Mama said, swishing her paw in the warm, soapy water. "Listen!" She made gentle splashing sounds that sounded just like music.

Rascal's ears perked up. The splashing did sound like a song! Slowly, he came out from behind his drum.

"Can I try?" he asked softly.

"Of course!" Mama smiled. "Let's start with your paws."

Rascal dipped one tiny paw in the warm water. It felt nice and cozy, like a warm hug! He began to splash gently. Splash, splash, splash! It made a wonderful rhythm.

"Now let's add some scrubbing music!" Mama said, showing him how to rub soap between his paws. "Scrub-a-dub-dub, scrub-a-dub-dub!"

Soon Rascal was giggling and singing along. "This is fun!" he laughed as bubbles tickled his nose. The warm water felt amazing on his fur, and the soap smelled like fresh flowers.

When bath time was over, Rascal looked at himself in the puddle. His fur was fluffy and clean, his paws were bright and shiny, and he felt wonderful all over!

"I feel so good!" Rascal exclaimed, doing a little dance. "I'm clean and fresh and ready to make more music!"

He picked up his drumsticks and played the most beautiful song he'd ever made. All his friends gathered around to listen.

"You sound even better than before!" said Bella the butterfly.

"And you smell like sunshine and flowers!" added Charlie the cardinal.

From that day on, Rascal looked forward to bath time. He learned that taking care of himself made him feel happy and confident. And the best part? He discovered that being clean didn't stop his music – it made it even more wonderful!

"Taking care of myself helps me shine!" Rascal sang every day as he splashed in his bath.

The End.
