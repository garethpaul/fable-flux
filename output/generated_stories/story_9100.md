# Bubbles Finds the Beat

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash, sing, and wiggle her flippers. Today, Bubbles swam through a little door shaped like a seashell and slid into the musical practice room. The room was warm and bright. Rainbow lights glowed softly. Shiny drums lined one wall. Tiny bells dangled from strings. A big soft rug felt cozy under Bubbles’ smooth belly as she scooted in.

“Hello, friends!” Bubbles clicked and whistled. Her dolphin voice sounded like tiny bubbles popping.

Inside the room were friends from all over: Tiko the turtle with a gentle smile, Lila the ladybug with red, spotted wings, and Nia the narwhal with a long, spiral horn. They were getting ready for music time.

Coach Coco, a kind crab with a tiny baton, tapped the baton on a shell. Tap-tap! “Today we practice our band song,” Coach Coco said. “We listen. We share. We care. And we celebrate what makes us special.”

Bubbles wiggled with excitement. She loved making music. She took a deep breath. “Eeee-eee,” she sang, a shiny dolphin song.

Tiko blinked. “Um, that sounds funny,” he said softly.

Lila giggled, not to be mean, just surprised. “I’ve never heard singing like that!”

Bubbles’ smile slid away. Her fins drooped. “Is my song wrong?” she asked. Her voice made a small, sad squeak.

Coach Coco held up a claw. “Let’s listen with our hearts,” she said. “Everyone sings in their own way.”

But Bubbles felt fluttery in her tummy. She wanted to fit in. She wanted to sing like her friends. She tried to copy Lila’s tiny hum. “Hmm-hmm.” It came out squeaky and short. She tried to copy Tiko’s deep “bom-bom.” It sounded wobbly, like a soap bubble about to pop. Bubbles made a little splash on the rug. “I can’t do it,” she whispered.

Nia the narwhal swam closer. Her voice was calm like soft waves. “Bubbles,” Nia said, “your song is like the ocean. It tells your story.”

Bubbles blinked. “My story?”

Coach Coco nodded. “Yes. Our music room is for learning, and we all have different languages of sound. Lila hums. Tiko thumps. Nia sings whale words. You click and whistle. We can learn from each other.”

“Language is like music,” Nia added. She sang a warm “ooo-OOO-ooo.” The sound vibrated in the air. Bubbles felt it tickle her ears.

Bubbles took another deep breath. She remembered her healthy habits: breathe in, breathe out, slow and gentle. When she was calm, she felt brave. “I can try again,” she said.

Coach Coco clacked her claws happily. “Band, let’s explore sounds! First, let’s greet in our own ways.”

Lila hummed a tiny “hmm-hm-HELLO!” Tiko tapped the drum twice, “bom-bom.” Nia sang a swirl of notes. Bubbles clicked and whistled, “Eee-eee-click!”

“See?” Coach Coco smiled. “So many ways to say hello!”

Bubbles’ eyes sparkled. “I can teach you dolphin words!” She clicked slowly, then fast. “Eee… eee-eee… click-click!” She pointed her fin to her heart and then to her friends. “This means, ‘Friend.’”

Lila flapped her wings. “Friend!” she hummed, trying the clicks. She sounded like a tiny popcorn machine. Everyone laughed kindly—not at her, but with joy.

Tiko touched the drum with his soft flipper. “I can show turtle rhythm.” He went, “bom… bom-bom… bom,” like steady footsteps. Bubbles tapped her tail on the rug to match the beat. It felt good to move together.

Coach Coco said, “Now, let’s make a song with all our languages.”

They began. Tiko gave the beat. Bom… bom-bom. Lila sprinkled high hums like glitter. Hmm-hmm! Nia poured in long, warm notes, ooo-OOO. Bubbles added bright clicks and whistles, eee-eee-click! The sounds hugged each other, different and beautiful.

But then, a little problem popped up. The song got messy. Everyone grew louder at the same time. The room buzzed and boomed. Bubbles’ head felt busy. She stopped and rubbed her fins. “It’s too much,” she said.

Coach Coco lifted her baton. “Pause and breathe.” Everyone took slow breaths in and out. The room felt calm again.

“Let’s make space,” Coach Coco said. “We listen. We take turns. We celebrate each voice.”

They tried again with a plan:
- Tiko starts: bom… bom-bom.
- Lila hums next: hmm-hmm.
- Bubbles replies: eee-eee-click!
- Nia holds the last note: ooo-OOO.

They played in turns, then all together softly. The music grew like a rainbow. Bubbles could hear every friend. She could hear herself, too. Her heart felt warm.

When the song ended, the room rang with happy silence. Then everyone clapped and tapped and hummed. Coach Coco’s eyes shone. “Beautiful,” she said. “You listened. You cared. You made space for each other.”

Bubbles smiled big. “My dolphin song belongs,” she said.

“It does,” Nia agreed. “Different songs make our band strong.”

Tiko nodded. “When we listen, we understand.”

Lila added, “And trying new sounds is fun!”

Bubbles wiggled. “I learned that being different is something to celebrate. I can share my language and learn yours. We can breathe, take turns, and make music together.”

Coach Coco bowed. “That is our lesson today.”

Bubbles felt brave and bright. She splashed a happy, gentle splash—just a little, to keep the rug dry. She clicked one more word: “Friend.”

All her friends echoed back, each in their own way. Bom. Hmm-hmm. Ooo-OOO. Eee-eee-click!

The End.
