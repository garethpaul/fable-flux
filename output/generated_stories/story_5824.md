# Rosie Rabbit's Garden Helper Adventure

Rosie Rabbit loved helping in the garden, but she had a big problem. Every time Grandpa Bunny asked her to get tools from the garden shed, she would forget what he needed!

"Rosie, can you please bring me the small watering can and the red gloves?" Grandpa asked one sunny morning.

"Yes, Grandpa!" Rosie hopped excitedly toward the wooden shed that smelled like fresh soil and dried flowers.

But when she opened the creaky door, she stopped. The shed was full of so many interesting things! Shiny shovels hung on hooks, colorful pots sat in neat rows, and bags of seeds lined the shelves.

"Ooh, look at these pretty blue pots!" Rosie said to herself, touching their smooth surface. "And this big watering can is so sparkly!"

She grabbed the big silver watering can and some blue gloves, then hopped back to Grandpa.

"Oh dear," Grandpa said gently. "I asked for the small watering can and red gloves. Let's try again, but this time, let's make a plan."

Rosie's ears drooped. "I'm sorry, Grandpa. I keep forgetting!"

"That's okay, little one," Grandpa smiled. "Following directions takes practice. Let me teach you a special trick."

Grandpa knelt down to Rosie's level. "When someone gives you directions, repeat them back. Then count on your paws how many things you need to remember."

"Really?" Rosie's whiskers twitched with excitement.

"Yes! So if I say 'small watering can and red gloves,' what would you say back to me?"

Rosie thought carefully. "Small watering can and red gloves!"

"Perfect! Now count on your paws."

Rosie held up one paw. "Small watering can - that's one." She held up another paw. "Red gloves - that's two!"

"Excellent! Now you can say it three times as you hop to the shed: 'Small watering can and red gloves, small watering can and red gloves, small watering can and red gloves.'"

Rosie practiced the words, then hopped toward the shed, chanting softly. The wooden floor creaked under her paws as she entered.

This time, instead of looking at all the pretty things, she focused on her task. "Small watering can," she said, spotting the little green one on the bottom shelf. "And red gloves!" She found them hanging on a low hook.

"I did it!" she cheered, hopping back to Grandpa with the correct items.

"Wonderful job, Rosie! You followed the directions perfectly," Grandpa said, giving her a warm hug that smelled like sunshine and carrots.

From that day on, Rosie used her special system every time. She would repeat the directions, count on her paws, and say them three times. Soon, she became the best garden helper in the whole neighborhood!

"Rosie," said Mrs. Cottontail from next door, "could you help me get the yellow watering pot and small trowel from my shed?"

"Yellow watering pot and small trowel," Rosie repeated confidently, counting on her paws. "One, two! I'll be right back!"

As she hopped away, chanting the directions, Rosie felt proud and happy. Following directions wasn't hard when you had a good plan!

The End.
