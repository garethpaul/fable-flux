# Binky the Brave Bunny and the Bathroom Mystery

Binky was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and big, bright eyes. He loved to learn new things. He loved to ask, “Why?” and “How?” and “What happens if…?” Today, his curious nose twitched as he hopped into the bathroom. The tiles felt cool under his paws. He could hear the quiet drip, drip, drip of the sink. He smelled lemon soap and warm steam.

“I want to know how everything works,” Binky said to himself. “I will be careful. I will be kind. And I will think, think, think!”

Binky’s best friend, Tilly the turtle, peeked in from the hallway. “What are you doing, Binky?” Tilly asked in a soft, slow voice.

“I am on a bathroom adventure,” Binky said. “Do you want to come?”

“Yes,” said Tilly, smiling. “We can look and learn together.”

Binky and Tilly stood by the sink. The silver faucet was shiny. Binky touched it gently. “It feels cold and smooth,” he said.

“Let’s see what it does,” Tilly whispered.

Binky turned the handle a little bit. The faucet made a soft hiss. Warm water rushed out. It splashed and sparkled. “Ooo!” Binky giggled. “The water is warm. It sounds like tiny rain.”

Tilly put her small foot under the water. “It feels cozy,” she said. “Can we make bubbles?”

Binky looked around. He saw a pump bottle with a picture of a bubble on it. “This must be soap,” he said. “But we should ask first.”

Just then, Mama Rabbit walked by the door. “Mama,” Binky called, “may we make soap bubbles to wash our paws?”

Mama smiled. “Yes, Binky. Use one pump. Be gentle and share the sink with Tilly. And no big mess, please.”

“Thank you, Mama,” Binky said. He felt proud for asking. He pressed the pump, and a small dollop of soap landed in his paw with a soft plop. It smelled sweet, like oranges.

They rubbed their paws together. Bubbles grew. They were shiny and round. “The bubbles look like tiny moons,” Tilly said.

“Let’s rinse,” Binky said. He turned the handle a little more. Water washed the bubbles away. “Now our paws are clean and happy.”

Binky’s eyes moved to a big white bowl with a seat. “I wonder how the toilet works,” he said. “It makes a whoosh sound. Where does the water go?”

Tilly blinked. “I am curious too,” she said. “But we should be careful.”

Binky nodded. “Curiosity means we ask and we think. Kindness means we keep things safe and clean. We can look with our eyes.”

Binky lifted the lid gently. “There is water inside,” he said. He pressed the flush button, just one time. Whooooosh! The water swirled and spun like a little dance. Then it filled up again with a calm glug, glug.

“I think it is a circle,” Tilly said. “Water goes down and then comes back.”

Binky thought hard. “Maybe pipes help the water move,” he said. “Pipes are like tunnels.”

They both listened. Far away, they heard faint gurgles. “It sounds like the bathroom is talking,” Tilly giggled.

On the shelf, they saw a tiny roll of soft paper. Binky picked it up. “This is toilet paper,” he said. He read the small picture chart on the wall Mama had drawn: One, two squares for a wipe. No toys in the toilet. No big wads.

Binky nodded. “We will follow the chart. Rules help us use things the right way.”

Then, they heard a drip, drip, drip again. The sink was dripping! Binky’s ears perked up. “We have a problem,” he said. “The sink is wasting water.”

“What can we do?” asked Tilly.

Binky took a deep breath. “Step one: Look and listen. Step two: Try a small change. Step three: Ask for help if needed.”

He turned the handle just a tiny bit until the drip stopped. They waited. No drip. “We did it!” Binky cheered.

But after a moment, drip, drip. The sound came back. Binky tilted his head. “Hmm. Our small change did not last.”

Tilly raised her hand like in class. “Step three?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Binky. “Let’s ask Mama.”

Mama Rabbit came in. “What is the little mystery?”

“The sink drips,” Binky said. “We tried to turn the handle gently, and it stopped for a bit, but then it dripped again. We want to save water.”

Mama smiled proudly. “You used your curious minds and kind hearts,” she said. “Sometimes a washer is loose. That is a tiny part inside. I will call a helper to fix it. For now, we can put a small cup under the drip and use the water to feed the plants.”

“That is smart sharing,” Tilly said. “We share water with the plants.”

Binky put a cup under the faucet. Drip, plip, drip. The little cup made a cheerful sound. “Now the water will not be wasted,” he said.

They washed their paws one more time, dried them with a soft towel that felt fluffy like clouds, and wiped up any splashes. The bathroom smelled fresh and looked tidy. Binky felt warm with pride.

“Mama,” Binky said, “today we learned to ask before we try, to think in steps, and to share what we have.”

“And we learned that curiosity helps us discover how things work,” Tilly added. “Curiosity is a good helper when we are careful and kind.”

Mama hugged them both. “Yes,” she said. “Curiosity plus kindness plus clear thinking makes a great team.”

Binky’s nose twitched happily. “I am Binky the Brave Bunny,” he said. “I will keep asking good questions, I will keep being kind, and I will keep using my smart steps.”

Tilly smiled. “And I will share in the learning.”

They hopped and toddled out of the bathroom, feeling helpful and bright. They had solved a small problem and learned a big lesson.

The End.
