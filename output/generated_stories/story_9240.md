# Dippy's Feelings Painting

Dippy was a playful dolphin who loved to splash and giggle. One sunny morning, Dippy visited the creative art room at the Sea School. The room smelled like fresh paper and salty water. Bright shells made soft clinks in jars. Paints shone in rainbow puddles. Brushes sat like tiny sea trees.

“Hello, friends!” Dippy chirped. “Let’s make art today!” Dippy swished his tail and made little sparkly drops fly in the light.

Mimi the seahorse waved with her curly tail. “I want to paint the sun,” she said in a tiny voice.

Toto the turtle blinked slowly. “I like clay,” he said. “It feels squishy.”

Coach Coral, their kind teacher, smiled. “Today we will paint our feelings,” she said. “We can use colors to show how we feel inside. And we can listen to each other with kind hearts.”

Dippy’s eyes went wide. “Feelings? I feel silly!” He dipped a brush into yellow and made happy swirls. “Wheee!” The brush whispered over the paper. The yellow smelled like lemons. Dippy hummed a bubbly tune.

Mimi dipped into orange, but her brush made tiny, shaky lines. She looked down. Her eyes were shiny like wet rocks.

Dippy swam closer. “Mimi, look! My painting is silly like a wiggly fish!” he said. He splashed a little splash.

Mimi turned away. “I don’t feel silly,” she whispered. “I feel small.”

Dippy felt his tail slow down. He looked at Mimi’s little lines. He looked at her droopy fins. He thought about the word small. His tummy felt a quiet flutter.

Coach Coral nodded gently. “Let’s be curious,” she said. “Curiosity helps us understand. Dippy, what could you do to help a friend who feels small?”

Dippy took a soft breath. “I can listen,” he said in a quiet voice. He put his brush down. He floated next to Mimi and kept his splashes inside. “Mimi, can you tell me about your painting?”

Mimi touched her paper. “I wanted to make a big sun,” she said. “But my brush made tiny lines. Everyone else makes big, bright things. My fins feel tired. I feel small.”

Dippy’s heart felt warm and a little heavy. He nodded. “I hear you,” he said. “Tiny lines can be beautiful too.”

Mimi peeked up. “Really?”

“Really,” Dippy said. “Tiny lines can be gentle. Like quiet waves.”

Coach Coral smiled. “That is empathy, Dippy. You are trying to feel what Mimi feels. Empathy is when we care about someone’s feelings.”

Dippy’s chest lifted. “Empathy,” he repeated. The word felt like a soft shell.

Toto the turtle nudged a ball of clay. “Maybe Mimi’s sun can have tiny sunny friends,” he said. “Lots of little lights.”

Mimi’s mouth made a small smile. “I like that.”

Dippy looked at the colors. He picked up a small brush and dipped it into light yellow. “May I paint next to you, Mimi?” he asked.

“Yes,” Mimi said.

Together they made tiny sunny dots. Dot, dot, dot. The paper looked like a sky full of sparkles. The room was quiet and cozy. The brushes made gentle sounds like whispers. Dippy’s splashy tail stayed calm, but his eyes were bright.

“Thank you for staying soft,” Mimi said. “It helps.”

Dippy nodded. “I was very wiggly before,” he said. “I didn’t see your small feeling. Now I do.”

Coach Coral clapped softly. “You listened. You understood. You helped.”

When the paintings were dry, the friends hung them on a sea-blue wall. Dippy’s first picture had big yellow swirls. Next to it was the new one he made with Mimi, full of tiny dots like little suns. Mimi’s eyes shone with quiet pride.

“Let’s go outside to the tide garden,” Coach Coral said. “We can look for colors in nature.” They swam to the open pool where sea grasses waved. The water felt cool and tickly on their skin. Crabs tapped like tiny drums on the rocks. The sun made lace patterns on the sand.

“Look!” Dippy pointed with his fin. “Small yellow flowers on the shore!” They were little star-shaped blooms. Dippy leaned close. They smelled sweet like honey and lemons.

Mimi floated beside him. “They are small,” she said, “but they shine.”

Dippy’s heart felt big. “Like your tiny sun dots,” he said.

A little crab scurried by with a shell too heavy for his back. He wobbled and slipped. “Oh!” Dippy said. He felt a tug in his chest again. “Maybe he needs help.”

“Let’s be gentle,” Toto said.

Dippy swam slowly. “Hi, little crab,” he said softly. “Do you want a lighter shell?” The crab clicked and tapped. Together, the friends found a smaller shell. The crab tried it on and did a happy wiggle.

Mimi smiled. “We listened to the crab’s problem. That is empathy too.”

Dippy felt proud and calm. The sea breeze kissed his nose. The waves said shhh like a kind hush.

Back in the art room, Dippy wrote a tiny note on their picture: “We see small feelings. We care.” Mimi drew a tiny heart. Toto added a gentle wave.

Coach Coral read the note aloud. “What did we learn today?”

Dippy lifted his fin. “Empathy means I listen, I understand, and I care. Big splashes are fun, but small feelings matter too.”

Mimi nodded. “And tiny art can shine.”

Toto added, “And curious hearts find ways to help.”

Everyone smiled. The room felt warm and bright, like a hug.

The End.
