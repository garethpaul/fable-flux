# Milo Mouse’s Welcome Basket

Milo was a kind mouse who lived in a cozy guest room. The room smelled like clean sheets and warm tea. Soft light came through the curtains each morning. Milo loved his routine. He dusted the tiny nightstand, straightened the little rug, and tapped his paw to make sure the lamp worked. Tap, tap! “Good morning, guest room,” he squeaked.

One sunny day, Milo heard footsteps in the hall. The door opened with a gentle creak. A suitcase rolled in—rumble, rumble. Milo’s whiskers twitched with excitement. “A new friend!” he whispered.

A family entered. There was a child named Sam, wearing bright socks. Sam’s grandma, Nana Amara, wore a colorful scarf. It had blues and golds and tiny bells that jingled softly. “Welcome,” Milo squeaked from behind the striped curtain, though the people could not understand him. Still, he liked to be polite.

Milo’s routine had one special part: a welcome basket. Every day a guest arrived, he filled a tiny basket with kind things. He gathered a button that looked like the moon, a smooth pebble that felt cool, and a drawing of a smiling sun. He added a small card that said, “You belong.” Milo placed the basket on the nightstand.

Sam looked around the room. “It smells like lemons,” Sam said. “And the blanket is so soft.” Nana Amara smiled. “It feels like a friendly place,” she said. The bells on her scarf made a sweet ting sound.

Milo peeked from the curtain. He saw Sam hugging a stuffed bear. He saw Nana Amara carefully fold her scarf on a chair. Milo wanted to say hello properly, but he remembered his personal boundaries rule. “Ask first,” he told himself. “Keep space until someone says it is okay.”

That afternoon, Milo heard gentle singing. It was Nana Amara’s song, soft and warm. La-la-lu, la-la-lu. The melody floated like feathers. Sam clapped. “Teach me, Nana!” Sam said.

Milo’s heart felt big. He loved new songs. He wanted to include the new sounds in his routine. He had an idea—an innovation! Milo made a tiny drum from a bottle cap and a leaf. Tap, tap. “I can play along,” he squeaked.

He stepped out from the curtain a little. Sam gasped. “A mouse!” Sam’s eyes were wide, but kind. Milo waved his paw. “Hello,” he said softly. He pointed to the drum. Tap, tap. He stayed on the rug, not too close.

Sam looked at Nana Amara. “Can our new friend play with us?” Sam asked. Nana Amara nodded. “Yes, if we are gentle and give space.” She turned to Milo and said in a calm voice, “Little friend, you may play your drum. We will sing. Is this okay with you?”

Milo’s whiskers wiggled. “Yes,” he squeaked. He felt safe and happy. Everyone had asked. Everyone had listened.

They made a small band. Nana Amara sang her song from home. Sam shook a tiny rattle. Milo tapped his leaf drum. Tap, tap, tap. The room filled with music. The bells on the scarf jingled. The rattle whooshed softly. The drum made a tiny pop sound. It felt like sunshine in the room.

After the music, Sam noticed the welcome basket. “Look, Nana!” Sam said. “A basket!” Sam read the card slowly. “You be-long.” Sam smiled. “I belong.”

Nana Amara touched the button moon and the cool pebble. “Someone kind made this,” she said. “Maybe our little drummer.”

Milo’s ears turned pink. He nodded. “I like to include everyone,” he squeaked. He pointed to the basket and then to Sam and Nana Amara. He held his paws to his chest, then held them out, as if to say, “You are welcome here.”

Sam sat on the rug and set the stuffed bear beside them. “Can we make a basket too?” Sam asked. “We can share our things.”

Nana Amara smiled. “Yes. We can share from our culture. We will ask first, and we will take care.”

They made a second small basket. Nana Amara tied a tiny ribbon from her scarf—just a little piece, with permission. The ribbon smelled like spice and flowers. Sam added a sticker with a star. They made a new card. Sam said, “I want to write, ‘We can sing together.’”

Milo clapped his paws. “We are a team,” he squeaked.

Soon, the door creaked again. Another guest peeked in—a shy traveler with a hat. The traveler looked nervous. Their shoulders were small. Milo and Sam and Nana Amara smiled. Sam pointed to the basket. “You belong,” Sam said gently. Nana Amara sang one soft line. Milo tapped his drum. The traveler’s hat lifted a little as they stood taller.

“Thank you,” the traveler whispered. “I feel safe.”

Milo felt warm inside. He had kept his routine and made room for others. He had asked and listened. He had learned a new song. They had shared and cared.

That night, the guest room was quiet and cozy. The blanket felt like a hug. The air smelled like tea and lemons. Milo curled up by the nightstand. He whispered, “Inclusion means we make space. We ask. We listen. We share. Everyone belongs.”

The End.
