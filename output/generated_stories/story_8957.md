# Rocco Raccoon's Noisy Day

Rocco Raccoon loved making music. His tiny paws could tap rhythms on anything! Tap-tap-tap on tables. Bang-bang-bang on boxes. Crash-crash-crash with cymbals!

Today, Rocco bounced into the bright art room. Paintbrushes and colorful papers covered every table. Crayons sparkled in rainbow cups.

"Time to make music!" Rocco shouted. He grabbed two paintbrushes like drumsticks.

BANG! BANG! BANG! He drummed on the art table.

"Oh my!" gasped Miss Owl, the art teacher. "That's very loud, Rocco."

But Rocco didn't hear. He was having too much fun! He drummed louder and louder.

CRASH! A cup of crayons fell over. Red, blue, and yellow crayons rolled everywhere.

"Rocco, please stop!" called Penny Pig from across the room. She was trying to paint a pretty flower.

But Rocco kept drumming. BANG! CLANG! RATTLE!

Soon, all the other animals covered their ears. Nobody could focus on their art projects.

"I can't paint with all this noise!" cried Benny Bear.

"My hands are shaking!" said Sally Squirrel, holding up her wobbly drawing.

Rocco finally stopped drumming. He looked around the art room. Everyone looked upset. Some friends had tears in their eyes.

"What's wrong?" Rocco asked, confused.

Miss Owl sat down next to him. "Rocco, your music is wonderful. But sometimes we need quiet to create art. When it's too loud, our friends can't think clearly."

Rocco's ears drooped. "I didn't mean to upset anyone. I just love making music!"

"I understand," said Miss Owl gently. "Let's think together. How can you make music AND help your friends feel calm?"

Rocco thought hard. He looked at all the art supplies. Soft paintbrushes. Smooth paper. Gentle watercolors.

"What if..." Rocco said slowly, "what if I made quiet music?"

Miss Owl smiled. "That sounds perfect! Show us."

Rocco picked up a soft paintbrush. Instead of banging, he gently tapped it on the table. Tap... tap... tap... The sound was like gentle raindrops.

"That's beautiful!" whispered Penny Pig.

Next, Rocco found tissue paper. He crinkled it softly between his paws. Whisper... whisper... whisper... It sounded like wind in the trees.

"I love that sound," said Benny Bear, relaxing his shoulders.

Rocco discovered he could make music with everything! He rubbed two smooth stones together. He blew gently across an empty jar. He even made music by painting - swish, swish, swish went his brush.

Soon, all the animals were creating art again. But now they had Rocco's gentle music to help them feel calm and creative.

"Your quiet music helps me paint better," said Sally Squirrel, adding finishing touches to her picture.

"It makes me feel peaceful," added Penny Pig.

Rocco beamed with pride. He had learned something important. Music could be loud and exciting. But gentle, quiet music was special too. It helped friends feel calm and happy.

From that day on, Rocco became the art room's official "calm music maker." He learned that thinking about others' feelings made him a better friend. And everyone loved creating beautiful art together with Rocco's soothing sounds.

"Thank you for learning to make calm music," said Miss Owl. "You helped everyone feel peaceful and creative."

Rocco smiled. Sometimes the most beautiful music was the quietest kind of all.

The End.
