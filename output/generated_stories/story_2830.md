# Shelly's Big Voice

In a quiet guest room with soft blue walls and a cozy window seat, lived a little turtle named Shelly. She had a beautiful green shell with yellow spots and kind brown eyes. But Shelly had a big problem - she was very, very shy.

"I want to ask Grandma for a bedtime story," whispered Shelly to herself. She peeked out from under the fluffy white guest bed. "But what if my voice is too quiet? What if she can't hear me?"

Shelly pulled her head into her shell. It felt safe and warm inside, but also very lonely. Through her shell, she could hear Grandma humming in the kitchen downstairs. The sweet smell of cookies floated up to the guest room.

"Maybe I should just stay here," Shelly said softly. Her voice was so quiet, even she could barely hear it.

But then Shelly's tummy rumbled. Those cookies smelled like chocolate chips - her favorite! And she really did want that bedtime story. Grandma told the best stories about brave animals and magical adventures.

"I can try," Shelly whispered. "Maybe I can practice first."

Shelly poked her head out just a little. She looked at the soft teddy bear sitting on the guest room chair. "Hello, Teddy," she said quietly. "Could I please have a cookie?"

The teddy bear smiled back with his button eyes. Shelly felt a tiny bit braver.

"Hello, Teddy!" she said a little louder. "Could I please have a cookie and a story?"

That felt better! Shelly's shell felt less tight around her. She crawled out from under the bed and sat up tall. The afternoon sunlight streaming through the window felt warm on her shell.

"I think I can do this," said Shelly. Her voice was getting stronger. "I just need to practice more."

Shelly walked to the window seat and climbed up. She could see Grandma in the garden below, watering her colorful flowers. "Grandma!" Shelly called out. But her voice was still too quiet.

She took a deep breath. "I can be brave," Shelly told herself. "My voice matters. Grandma wants to hear from me."

Shelly thought about all the times Grandma had smiled at her. Grandma always listened carefully and gave the best hugs. "Grandma loves me," Shelly realized. "She wants to talk with me."

With her heart beating fast but feeling determined, Shelly walked to the top of the stairs. "Grandma!" she called down. This time her voice was clear and strong.

"Oh, hello my dear Shelly!" Grandma called back happily. "I was just thinking about you! Would you like to come down for some fresh cookies?"

"Yes, please!" said Shelly, her voice growing more confident. "And could you tell me a story later?"

"Of course, sweetheart! I'd love to tell you a story. Come down whenever you're ready."

Shelly's whole shell seemed to glow with happiness. She had done it! She had used her voice and asked for what she wanted. And Grandma was so happy to hear from her.

As Shelly slowly made her way down the stairs, she felt proud and brave. "My voice is important," she said to herself. "When I speak up, good things happen!"

From that day on, Shelly still felt shy sometimes, but she remembered that her voice mattered. She learned that asking for what she needed was not scary - it was brave.

The End.
