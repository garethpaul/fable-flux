# Leo the Lion Cub and the Laundry Room Picnic

Leo was a brave little lion cub with bright eyes and soft, golden fur. One rainy afternoon, he padded into the laundry room. The room hummed and whirred. The washer went whump-whump. The dryer spun with a soft whoosh. Warm air smelled like clean shirts and sunshine soap.

Leo’s tummy gave a tiny rumble. “I’m hungry,” he said. His whiskers twitched. “I want a snack!”

On a low shelf, Leo saw a crinkly bag. It was shiny and loud. He poked it. Crinkle-crackle! “This looks yummy,” he said. He tugged the bag open with his little paws. The smell was salty and strong.

Just then, Mama Lion walked in with a basket of towels. “Hello, brave cub,” Mama said with a smile. “What do you have there?”

“A snack!” Leo said proudly. “I found it all by myself.”

Mama crouched down to his level. “You did use your eyes and paws. That shows initiative,” she said. “Taking action is brave. But let’s use our thinking, too. Is this snack good for your body?”

Leo sniffed the bag again. “It smells fun,” he said. He looked at Mama. “How do I know if it is good?”

Mama tapped the bag gently. “We can check. Good snacks help you grow strong, help your mind think, and make your body feel good. They are full of colors from fruits and veggies, protein for strong muscles, and whole grains for long energy.”

Leo looked around the laundry room. He saw the neat shelves, the soap bottles, the basket of clean socks. Everything had a place. He puffed his chest. “I can be a helper,” he said. “I can make a good snack.”

Mama’s eyes shone. “That is civic responsibility,” she said kindly. “We help our family and our home. We keep things safe, clean, and healthy.”

Leo put the crinkly bag back on the shelf. “This can be for a sometimes day,” he decided. “Today I want a grow-strong snack.” His tummy rumbled again. Rum-buh-rum!

“Let’s think,” Mama said. “What do we have that is healthy and close by?”

Leo’s ears perked up at a soft plop-plop sound. He looked at the dryer door. Inside, towels tumbled like fluffy clouds. He imagined a picnic with warm towels as a blanket. He giggled. “A laundry room picnic!” he said. “We can eat here while it rains.”

Mama clapped softly. “What a creative idea.”

Leo padded to the little fridge by the door. He opened it carefully. A cool breeze tickled his nose. He saw bright red apple slices in a clear box. He saw a small cup of yogurt with a blue lid. He saw baby carrots, orange and crunchy. He saw a little bag of round oat crackers.

Leo thought. “I want colors,” he said. He took the apple slices. Sweet smell! He took the yogurt. Mmm, creamy. He took the carrots. Crunch! He took the oat crackers. “This has fruit, dairy, veggies, and whole grains,” he said slowly, proud of his big words.

Mama brought two cups of water. The cups clinked. “Water helps our bodies, too,” she said. “It keeps us happy and hydrated.”

Leo spread a warm towel on the floor. It felt soft and cozy under his paws. He set the snacks on a little tray. “Picnic time!” he cheered.

They sat together. The washer hummed a gentle song. The dryer puffed warm air. Rain tapped the window, tip-tap, tip-tap.

Leo took a bite of apple. Crunch! “Sweet,” he said. He dipped a carrot in a tiny spoon of yogurt. “Silly dip,” he giggled. He nibbled an oat cracker. “Nutty and crispy!”

Mama nodded. “How does your body feel?”

Leo put a paw on his tummy. “Happy,” he said. “My brain feels awake. My muscles feel strong.”

Mama smiled. “You used critical thinking. You asked, ‘Is this healthy? How will it help me?’ You also took initiative to make a better choice and set up a clean, safe picnic. That helps our home.”

Leo looked at the shelf with the crinkly bag. He picked up a small sticky note and a pencil from the counter. He drew a red apple and a green check mark. He stuck the note on the fridge. “This is our healthy snack spot,” he said. Then he drew a blue star and put it on the towel shelf. “This is our clean picnic place,” he added.

Mama beamed. “You are helping everyone know what is healthy and where to keep things tidy. That is good for the whole pride.”

Leo finished his snack and took a sip of water. “When I am hungry,” he said, “I can look for colors, protein, and whole grains. I can drink water. I can help keep our home safe and clean.”

“And if you’re not sure,” Mama added, “you can ask, read, and think.”

Leo stood up tall on the warm towel. The hum of the machines sounded like a purr. The room smelled fresh and kind. He placed the empty cups in the sink and put the tray away. He folded the towel as best as he could—fold, pat, smooth.

“I did it,” Leo said with a proud smile. “I made a healthy choice, I helped, and I cleaned up.”

Mama gave him a soft nuzzle. “Brave, smart, and caring,” she said. “That is my little lion.”

Outside, the rain slowed to a gentle drip. Inside, Leo’s heart felt bright and full.

The clear lesson: Healthy foods help our bodies and minds; we can use our thinking, take action, and help our home and community by choosing well and cleaning up.

The End.
