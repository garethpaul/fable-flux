# Rosie Rabbit's Muddy Problem

In a sunny backyard filled with colorful flowers and tall green grass, lived a little white rabbit named Rosie. She had the softest fur and the brightest pink nose. Rosie loved to hop and play in the warm sunshine every day.

One beautiful morning, Rosie woke up excited to play with her friend Benny the bunny. "What a perfect day!" she said, stretching her little paws. The sun felt warm on her fur, and she could smell the sweet flowers blooming all around.

Rosie hopped quickly toward the garden where Benny was waiting. But oh no! She didn't see the big mud puddle from yesterday's rain. SPLASH! Rosie landed right in the middle of the sticky, brown mud.

"Ew, yuck!" Rosie cried. Her beautiful white fur was now covered in messy, smelly mud. She tried to shake it off, but the mud stuck to her like glue. "This is terrible! I can't play with Benny looking like this!"

Rosie sat down and felt very sad. She looked at her muddy paws and dirty fur. "Maybe I should just go back inside," she whispered.

But then Rosie remembered what her mama always said: "When you have a problem, think of solutions!" Rosie took a deep breath and thought carefully. "What can I do to get clean?"

First, she tried rolling in the dry grass. The green blades tickled her nose, but the mud stayed stuck. "That didn't work," she said. "But I won't give up!"

Next, Rosie found some big leaves and tried to wipe the mud off. The leaves felt rough and scratchy, but they only smeared the mud around. "Hmm, still muddy," she noticed.

Then Rosie had a wonderful idea! She remembered seeing a small stream at the edge of the backyard. "Water!" she exclaimed. "Water can wash away mud!"

Rosie hopped carefully to the babbling stream. The water sparkled in the sunshine and sounded like tiny bells ringing. She stepped into the cool, clear water. It felt refreshing on her muddy paws.

"This feels much better!" Rosie said happily. She splashed gently and rubbed her fur with her clean paws. The mud began to wash away, floating down the stream like little brown clouds.

Soon, Rosie was clean and white again! Her fur was fluffy and soft, and she smelled fresh like the morning air. "I did it!" she cheered. "I solved my problem!"

Just then, Benny hopped over. "Rosie! You look wonderful and clean! Are you ready to play?"

"Yes!" Rosie said proudly. "I got muddy, but I didn't give up. I thought of different ways to get clean, and I found the perfect solution!"

Benny smiled. "You're so smart, Rosie! You didn't let the problem stop you from having fun."

The two friends spent the whole day playing in the sunny backyard. They hopped through the flowers, played hide-and-seek behind the trees, and enjoyed the warm sunshine on their clean fur.

That evening, as Rosie got ready for bed, she felt proud of herself. "When I have a problem," she said sleepily, "I can think of solutions. I can try different ideas until I find one that works!"

From that day on, whenever Rosie faced a problem, she remembered to stay calm, think carefully, and try different solutions until she found the right one.

The End.
