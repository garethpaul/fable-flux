# Sunny Bird Sings in the Workshop

Sunny was a little singing bird with bright yellow feathers. Sunny loved to visit the garage workshop at the edge of the garden. The workshop smelled like pine wood and warm oil. It was full of soft sawdust, shiny screws, and boxes with labels. In the corner, a small window let in a square of golden light.

“Tweet-a-dee! Good morning!” Sunny sang as she fluttered in and landed on a pegboard hook. “What will we learn today?”

Mr. Ben, the friendly neighbor, looked up from a workbench. He wore blue overalls and a smile. “Good morning, Sunny. Today I’m fixing a wobbly chair. And I’m sorting these boxes so we can reuse more things.”

Sunny tilted her head. “Reuse? What does that mean?”

“It means we use something again,” Mr. Ben said. “We don’t throw it away if it can still help. That is good for our Earth.”

Sunny’s eyes shone. “I want to help the Earth too! Tweet-a-dee!”

She hopped along the pegboard, peeking at tools. She smelled wood glue—sweet and sticky. She heard a gentle tap-tap of a small hammer. The workshop felt cozy and calm.

Sunny saw a big cardboard box on the floor. It was full of plastic bottles, paper, and a few shiny cans. Some things were mixed together. Some things had fallen out.

“Oh no,” Sunny said softly. “This looks messy.”

Mr. Ben nodded. “Yes, it is. We can make it better. We can sort, reduce, and reuse. That is being responsible.”

Sunny puffed her feathers with pride. “I can sing a sorting song!”

She flew to the box and sang, “Paper here, plastic there, cans go clink with extra care!”

Mr. Ben laughed. “That’s a great song, Sunny.”

Together, they started sorting. Sunny used her beak to nudge a light paper scrap into one bin. It felt dry and papery. She tapped a can with her beak—clink!—and it rolled into another bin. Mr. Ben rinsed a plastic bottle in a small sink. The water splashed like tiny bells. “Clean things are easier to recycle,” he said.

Sunny noticed a shiny screw on the floor. “This is not trash!” she chirped. “It can be used again.”

“Exactly,” said Mr. Ben. “You are learning fast.”

Suddenly, Sunny heard a drip… drip… drip. She followed the sound to a leaky hose by the door. A small puddle made the floor slippery.

Sunny flapped her wings. “Drip-drip is wasting water. What can we do?”

Mr. Ben crouched down. “First, we choose a peaceful solution. We do not shout. We do not get upset. We look and think.”

Sunny took a deep breath. The air was cool. The puddle felt chilly when a tiny drop touched her toe. “We can turn off the tap,” she said. She pressed the tap gently with her beak. The drip slowed.

Mr. Ben tightened the hose with a wrench. “And we fix the problem. That is responsibility.”

The drip stopped. The puddle still gleamed. Sunny sang softly, “Wipe, wipe, clean and dry. Keep the workshop safe, oh my!” Mr. Ben used a towel to soak it up. The floor felt dry again.

Sunny felt proud. “We saved water. We kept the workshop safe.”

Mr. Ben nodded. “Yes, and we learned to notice, to think, and to act kindly.”

They worked more. Sunny found a small wooden box with a broken lid. “Is this trash?” she asked.

Mr. Ben shook his head. “Let’s reuse it.” He sanded the edges—shhhh, shhhh. The wood smelled fresh. He glued the lid and held it steady. Sunny sang a gentle hum while the glue set. When it was ready, Mr. Ben showed it to Sunny. “Now it can hold extra screws.”

Sunny looked around. The workshop was tidy. Labeled bins stood like little houses: Paper, Plastic, Metal, Reuse. The window light made dust sparkle like tiny stars. The chair no longer wobbled. The hose no longer dripped.

Sunny’s heart felt warm. “Mr. Ben, I learned so much today.”

“What did you learn, Sunny?” he asked.

Sunny took a deep breath and sang her new lesson song. “Sort and save, fix and share. Use things again with love and care. Think first, act kind, help the Earth each day. That is the Sunny way!”

Mr. Ben clapped. “Beautiful. That is sustainability—caring for the Earth by using only what we need, reusing, and fixing.”

Sunny nodded. “And we used peaceful solutions. We looked, we thought, and we helped.”

Mr. Ben winked. “Yes. And you were very responsible.”

The sun began to set, and the square of light on the floor turned orange. The workshop smelled like clean wood and lemon soap. Sunny fluttered to the window. “I will sing this song to my friends,” she said. “We will all help the Earth.”

“See you tomorrow, Sunny,” Mr. Ben said, waving.

“Tweet-a-dee! See you tomorrow!” Sunny chirped, and she flew out into the soft evening air, happy and calm.

The clear lesson sparkled in Sunny’s mind: Sort, reduce, reuse, and fix. Be peaceful. Be responsible. Learn and help every day.

The End.
