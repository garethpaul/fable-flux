# Poppy the Penguin’s Treehouse Turn-Taking Day

Poppy was a cheerful penguin with bright, curious eyes and a happy wiggle. She had a cozy treehouse at the edge of a snowy wood. The treehouse smelled like pine and fresh air. The wooden steps felt smooth and warm in the sunshine. Wind chimes tinkled softly, ding-ding, in the gentle breeze.

Today was Activity Day!

Poppy clapped her flippers. “Yay! We will play and move and have fun,” she sang. “We will take turns, get exercise, and be ready!”

She had prepared the night before. She spread a soft mat on the floor. She filled a basket with jump ropes, beanbags, and a bright red scarf. She made a simple list with big, friendly pictures: First Stretch, Then Jump, Then Toss, Then Snack. She even set a little bell on the table. Ding!

Poppy’s friends came waddling and scampering along the path. There was Benny Bear, who moved slowly but smiled big. There was Tula Turtle, who wore a tiny blue headband. There was Mimi Mouse, light as a feather and quick as a blink.

“Welcome!” Poppy chirped. “Come inside! Shoes off, flippers and paws clean.” She pointed to a small rug. Everyone wiped. Swish, swish.

The treehouse felt lively and bright. Sunlight made golden stripes on the floor. The jump ropes made tiny rustles. The beanbags smelled like cotton and lavender.

“We will have turns,” Poppy said. “We will move our bodies. We will get ready for each part. Ready?” She rang the bell. Ding!

“Ready!” everyone said.

First was Stretch Time. Poppy showed a gentle stretch. “Reach up high like a tall tree.” She reached. Her flippers brushed the air. “Now bend like a rainbow.” They bent slowly. It felt good. Poppy’s tummy felt calm and warm.

Mimi giggled. “I can reach my toes!” Tula smiled. “I can reach my shell!”

Poppy nodded. “Wonderful. We are getting our bodies ready.”

Next was Jump Time with the red scarf. Poppy held the scarf like a ribbon. “We will take turns jumping over the scarf. I will go first to show. Then I will pass the scarf.”

She laid the scarf on the mat. “Jump!” She hopped lightly. Her feet made a soft thup! She smiled, heart happy and bouncy.

“I want to jump now!” Benny Bear said, stepping forward with big paws.

Mimi Mouse bounced up and down. “Me, me, me! I want to go next!”

Tula Turtle blinked. “I can wait,” she said softly.

Poppy took a breath. She looked at her picture-list. She smiled. “We will take turns. I went first to show. Now Benny, then Mimi, then Tula. We will cheer for each friend.”

Benny Bear nodded. He bent his knees. He jumped over the scarf. Thump! “I did it!” he roared, eyes bright.

“Great jump, Benny!” said Poppy. She rang the bell quietly. Ding.

Mimi wiggled. “My turn!” She raced forward and jumped. Boing! “Whee!”

“Nice jump, Mimi!” Poppy clapped. “Now Tula.”

Tula moved slow and steady. “I can do it.” She bent. She hopped! Her feet made a tiny tap. She landed with a smile. “I did it too.”

Everyone cheered. The treehouse felt warm with happy sounds.

Poppy rolled up the scarf. “Now Toss Time with beanbags,” she said. “We toss into the basket. We will take turns again. Before we toss, we prepare our stance: feet on the floor, eyes on the basket, gentle throw.”

She showed how to stand. “Feet steady. Eyes ready. Toss!” Plop. Her beanbag landed in the basket. It made a soft pluff sound.

Mimi grabbed a beanbag. “Me first!”

Poppy shook her head gently. “Remember our plan. We will take turns. Benny went first last time. Tula can go first this time. Then Mimi. Then me again.”

Mimi’s whiskers drooped. “But I want to go now.”

Poppy knelt beside her. “I know it’s hard to wait. Waiting can feel wiggly in our bodies. Let’s try a waiting wiggle. Squeeze your paws. Take a deep breath. In and out.”

Mimi breathed. In and out. Her whiskers lifted a little. “Okay. I can wait.”

Tula stepped up. She planted her feet. She looked at the basket. “Toss,” she whispered. Plop! In it went.

“Great toss, Tula!” Poppy smiled.

Benny tried next. His beanbag went whoosh and missed. “Oh,” he said, ears low.

Poppy patted his paw. “Good try. We can try again after everyone has a turn.” She pointed to the list. “Taking turns means we all get chances.”

Mimi tossed. Plop! She beamed. Benny tried again on his second turn. Plop! He laughed, deep and warm. “I did it!”

The friends felt strong and proud. Their bodies were warm from moving. Their hearts were calm from waiting and cheering.

Poppy rang the bell. Ding. “Snack time!” She brought out little cups of cool water and apple slices. Crunch, crunch. The apple smelled sweet.

“Today was fun,” Mimi said, tail twirling.

“I liked waiting with the waiting wiggle,” Tula said.

“I liked trying again,” Benny said. “Taking turns helped me feel brave.”

Poppy nodded. “When we prepare, we feel ready. When we exercise, our bodies feel good. When we take turns, everyone gets to shine.”

They cleaned up together. Beanbags in the basket. Ropes in a neat loop. The red scarf folded like a small red cloud. The treehouse looked tidy and peaceful.

Poppy looked around. “Thank you for taking turns. Thank you for being patient and kind.”

Outside, the wind chimes sang. Ding-ding. The sun made the tree leaves glow like tiny lanterns.

Mimi hugged Poppy. “Can we have Activity Day again?”

“Yes,” Poppy said. “And we will take turns again, prepare again, and move our bodies with joy.”

Everyone smiled. Their hearts felt steady and happy. They waddled and walked down the smooth wooden steps, one at a time, taking turns even there.

The End.
