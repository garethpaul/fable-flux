# Rocco's Morning Music Lesson

The morning sun streamed through the soft blue curtains of Rocco's cozy bedroom. The little raccoon stretched his furry arms and yawned. His tiny piano sat quietly in the corner, waiting for him to play.

"Good morning, Rocco!" called Mama Raccoon from downstairs. "Time to get ready for your music lesson!"

Rocco loved playing piano, but today felt different. He looked at his striped paws. They had dirt under his tiny claws from yesterday's adventure in the garden.

"I don't want to wash my paws," Rocco mumbled. "They're just going to get dirty again."

Mama Raccoon climbed the wooden stairs. Her footsteps made soft tapping sounds. She peeked into Rocco's room with a warm smile.

"What's troubling you, little musician?" she asked gently.

Rocco wiggled his paws. "My teacher Mrs. Chen is coming today. She's going to teach me a special song from her home in China. But my paws are all dirty."

Mama sat on Rocco's soft bed. The colorful quilt bounced slightly. "Tell me more about this special song."

"It's called 'Mo Li Hua,'" Rocco said carefully. "It means 'Jasmine Flower.' Mrs. Chen says it's very beautiful and very old. Her grandmother taught it to her."

"That sounds wonderful!" Mama said. "Mrs. Chen is sharing something very special from her culture with you."

Rocco nodded but still looked worried. "But what if my dirty paws make the piano keys dirty? What if I can't play the song right?"

Mama's eyes twinkled. "You know what, Rocco? Clean paws are like a clean canvas for an artist. They help you play your very best music."

"Really?" Rocco asked, perking up his little ears.

"Really! And when we keep ourselves clean, it shows respect for our teachers and the beautiful music they share with us."

Rocco thought about this. "Mrs. Chen traveled far to teach me her grandmother's song. I want to show her I care."

"Exactly!" Mama said. "Let's make your paws sparkle clean for this special lesson."

They went to the bathroom together. The warm water felt nice on Rocco's paws. He scrubbed gently with lavender soap that smelled like flowers. His paws became soft and clean.

"Now I'm ready!" Rocco said, admiring his shiny claws.

Soon, Mrs. Chen arrived with her gentle smile and kind eyes. She wore a beautiful red scarf with golden flowers.

"Hello, Rocco! Are you ready to learn about jasmine flowers and their song?"

"Yes!" Rocco said proudly, showing his clean paws. "I washed up special for our lesson."

Mrs. Chen's face lit up. "How thoughtful! Clean hands help us play with care and respect."

She taught Rocco about jasmine flowers in China - how they smell sweet and bloom at night. Then she showed him the gentle melody on the piano. The notes sounded like flowers dancing in the breeze.

Rocco's clean paws moved carefully across the keys. The music filled his cozy bedroom with beautiful sounds.

"You play with such respect, Rocco," Mrs. Chen said. "Your clean paws and caring heart make the music even more beautiful."

After the lesson, Rocco felt proud and happy. He had learned a wonderful song from another culture, and he had shown respect by being clean and prepared.

"Thank you for teaching me, Mrs. Chen," Rocco said with a bow.

"Thank you for being such a respectful student," she replied.

That evening, Rocco played "Mo Li Hua" for his family. The gentle melody filled their home with joy and the spirit of jasmine flowers from far away.

From that day on, Rocco always washed his paws before playing piano. He learned that being clean wasn't just about looking nice - it was about showing respect for his music, his teachers, and the beautiful cultures they shared with him.

The End.
