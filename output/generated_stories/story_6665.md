# Ellie's Splish-Splash Sharing Day

The morning sun peeked through the bathroom window as little Ellie the elephant calf stepped into the warm, bubbly bathtub. The water felt so nice and cozy around her gray skin.

"Splash, splash!" Ellie giggled, using her tiny trunk to spray water droplets that sparkled like diamonds in the sunlight.

Just then, she heard a soft "peep, peep" from outside the window. Ellie looked up and saw three little birds sitting on the windowsill. Their feathers looked dusty and ruffled.

"Oh my!" said Ellie, her kind eyes growing wide. "You little birdies look so thirsty and dirty!"

The smallest bird chirped sadly, "We've been flying all morning looking for water to drink and bathe in. The pond is so far away!"

Ellie's heart felt warm and fuzzy. She wanted to help! But how could she share her bath water with the birds outside?

Then Ellie had a wonderful idea. She filled her trunk with the soapy, warm water and gently sprayed it toward the open window. "Here you go, little friends!"

The birds flapped their wings happily as the gentle shower reached them. "Thank you, Ellie!" they chirped. "This feels amazing!"

But Ellie noticed something. The more water she shared, the less she had for her own bath. Her mama had said bath time was only fifteen minutes, and she was using up her time helping the birds.

"Maybe I should stop," Ellie thought. "I need to wash behind my ears and clean my trunk."

Then she looked at the birds again. They were so happy, splashing and cleaning their feathers. The biggest bird was helping the smallest one wash his tiny wings.

"You know what?" Ellie said with a big smile. "Sharing makes everything more fun!"

She kept spraying water for her new friends, even though it meant her own bath would be shorter. With her trunk, she made it rain gentle droplets, then bigger splashes, then a soft mist.

"This is like magic!" squeaked the middle bird.

Ellie giggled. "It's not magic - it's sharing!"

Soon, Mama Elephant peeked into the bathroom. "Time's up, sweet Ellie!" But when she saw what was happening, her eyes twinkled with pride.

"Mama, look! I'm sharing my bath with the birds," Ellie explained, her trunk still spraying water toward the window.

"What a generous heart you have," Mama said warmly. "But you know what? When we share with others, good things happen to us too."

Mama turned on the faucet, filling the tub with fresh, warm water. "Now you can finish your bath properly, and the birds can keep enjoying their shower!"

The birds sang a beautiful thank-you song while Ellie splashed happily in her refilled tub. She washed behind her big ears and cleaned her trunk until it sparkled.

"Sharing made my bath time even better!" Ellie trumpeted joyfully.

From that day on, whenever Ellie took a bath, she always looked for ways to share the joy with others. Sometimes she made rainbow sprays for the birds, sometimes she shared her rubber ducky with her toy friends.

And every time she shared, Ellie's heart felt as warm and bubbly as her bath water.

"When we give to others," Mama always reminded her, "we get something wonderful back - the happiness that comes from being kind."

Ellie smiled, knowing that sharing made everything more magical.

The End.
