# Painting on the Porch

On a peaceful front porch, a gentle elephant calf named Elli flapped her soft ears. The sun felt warm on her back, and the porch smelled like fresh wood and sweet flowers. Birds chirped, “Tweet-tweet!” and a little breeze made the wind chimes sing, “Ting-a-ling.”

Elli set out her art things. She had a big sheet of paper, safe, washable paints, a big brush for her trunk, and a small brush for details. She also had a cup of water that went “plip-plop” when she dipped her brush. Elli smiled. “Today I will make porch art,” she said in a happy voice. “I will learn and practice.”

Her friend, Mia the mouse, peeked around the porch step. “Hi, Elli! What are you doing?” squeaked Mia.

“I am painting,” said Elli. “I want to paint the porch. The porch is calm and kind. I want to show it with colors.”

Mia clapped her tiny paws. “Can I watch?”

“Yes,” said Elli, “and you can help me be careful. We will keep the porch neat and clean.”

Elli dipped her big brush in blue paint. “Swish,” went the brush. She made a big blue sky. She watched the paint spread. “Ohhh,” she said. “That looks like the morning.”

Mia sniffed the paint. “It smells like soap and berries,” she said.

Next, Elli used sunny yellow. “Dot, dot, dot,” she said as she dotted a bright sun. The porch felt even warmer. The wind chimes sang again. “Ting-a-ling!” Elli listened. “I will paint the sound,” she said. She made soft silver lines that curved like a song.

Just then, Taro the turtle came up the porch steps. “Good day,” he said slowly. “What are you making?”

“A porch picture,” said Elli. “We are learning about art.”

“Art is practice,” said Taro. “Art is patience. May I sit and watch?”

“Yes,” said Elli. “But please sit on this little mat, so my paper stays safe.” Elli pointed with her trunk.

“Thank you for telling me,” said Taro. “I respect your art space.” He tucked his feet in and watched.

Elli tried to paint the wood boards. She dipped into brown and made long lines. “Oh no,” she whispered. Her line got wobbly. “It does not look like the porch.”

Mia patted her trunk. “It is okay,” she said. “You can try again.”

Elli took a deep breath. She felt the warm sun on her back. She heard the birds. She smelled the sweet flowers. “I can slow down,” she said. “I can practice.”

She washed her brush. “Swirl, swirl.” She tapped it on the cup. “Tap, tap.” Then she tried again with gentle strokes. “Slide… slide…” The lines looked smoother.

“You did it!” said Mia.

Taro nodded. “You learned from your mistake. That is skill-building.”

Elli smiled. “I am learning to see, to feel, and to try.” She added little green leaves to a flower pot. She made tiny dots for petals with the small brush. “Dot, dot, dot.”

Just then, Riku the rabbit hopped up. He wiggled his nose. “I like to paint fast!” he said. “Can I try?” He reached out a paw.

Elli pulled her painting back. “Wait, Riku,” she said softly. “This is my picture. I worked hard. Please ask first. Please use your own paper.”

Riku blinked. He looked at her careful lines and her tiny dots. “Oh,” he said. “I am sorry. I did not ask. I will respect your art.”

Elli smiled. “Thank you. Here is another paper and a brush. We can paint side by side. I can share the colors.”

Riku grinned. “Yes! I will paint bouncy clouds!”

They lined up their papers. Elli showed Riku how to wash the brush. “Swirl, swirl. Tap, tap,” she said. “Then pick a color. Paint slow. See and feel.”

Riku tried. “Swirl, swirl. Tap, tap,” he whispered. He made soft puffs. “Look! My clouds look bouncy!”

Mia clapped again. “We are all learning!”

Elli added little details to her porch painting. She painted the wind chimes, the flower pot, and even the shy shadow under the step. She looked closely. “The porch is peaceful,” she said. “I will show peace with gentle colors.”

She mixed a quiet purple by stirring red and blue. “Mix, mix,” she said. She painted a soft shade under the chair. It looked calm. It looked kind.

At last, Elli sat back. She felt proud, like a warm balloon in her chest. “I did it,” she said. “I took my time. I listened. I looked. I learned.”

Taro smiled. “Your art shows care,” he said.

Riku held up his cloud painting. “I asked first. I learned to slow down. I can build my skills.”

Mia hugged Elli’s leg. “We kept the porch neat. We shared. We respected each other.”

Elli washed all the brushes. “Swirl, swirl. Tap, tap.” She put the paints away. The porch smelled clean again. The sun made long, sleepy lines. The birds sang a soft song. The wind chimes whispered, “Ting-a-ling.”

Elli looked at her painting. “Art helps me see. Art helps me grow,” she said. “I will practice every day. I will respect other artists. I will be kind to their work and to mine.”

Her friends nodded. They all felt peaceful. They all felt proud.

The End.
