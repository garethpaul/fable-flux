# Milo's Big Feelings at Dinner Time

Milo the mouse lived in a cozy hole behind the big dining room. Every evening, he loved to peek out and watch the family eat their dinner. The warm yellow light made everything glow, and the delicious smells of food filled the air.

One Tuesday evening, Milo heard loud voices coming from the dining room. He poked his tiny pink nose out to see what was happening.

"I don't want to try that!" shouted little Emma, pushing her plate away. "It looks yucky!"

Emma's grandmother had made special dumplings from her home country. The round, white dumplings sat in a beautiful bamboo steamer, sending up curls of steam that smelled like ginger and vegetables.

"But Emma," said Grandma gently, "these are very special. They're from where I grew up."

"NO!" Emma yelled, her face turning red. "I only want pizza!"

Milo's whiskers twitched with worry. He could see Emma's angry feelings were as big as storm clouds. Her little fists were clenched tight, and tears were starting to form in her eyes.

"Oh my," whispered Milo to himself. "Emma needs help with her big angry feelings."

Milo had an idea! He scurried back to his hole and gathered some of his favorite things: a tiny bell that made gentle sounds, a soft piece of cotton, and a small mirror he had found.

Carefully, Milo crept out and climbed onto the dining room table. Emma was still crying and pushing food around her plate.

"Squeak, squeak!" said Milo softly.

Emma looked up, surprised. "A mouse!"

"Hello, Emma," said Milo in his gentle voice. "I can see you have very big angry feelings right now."

Emma nodded, wiping her eyes. "I don't like new food. It makes me scared."

Milo held up his tiny bell. "When I feel angry or scared, I do something special. Would you like to try?"

Emma leaned closer, curious.

"First," said Milo, "we take three deep breaths." He showed Emma how to breathe in slowly through her nose and out through her mouth. "Feel the air going in... and out... like gentle waves."

Emma tried it. Her shoulders relaxed a little.

"Next," said Milo, holding up the soft cotton, "we can squeeze something soft when we feel angry. It helps our hands feel better."

Emma gently squeezed the cotton. "It's so fluffy!"

"And last," said Milo, showing the mirror, "we can look at ourselves and say something kind. I say, 'Milo, you are brave and you can try new things.'"

Emma looked in the tiny mirror. "Emma, you are brave," she whispered.

Grandma smiled warmly. "What a wise little mouse! Emma, would you like to try just one tiny bite of dumpling? We can eat it together."

Emma looked at the steaming dumpling. She took another deep breath, just like Milo taught her. "Okay, Grandma. Just one tiny bite."

She took a small bite and her eyes grew wide. "It tastes like... like a warm hug!"

"That's because it's made with love," said Grandma, hugging Emma.

Milo clapped his tiny paws. "You were so brave, Emma! And you discovered something wonderful."

From that day on, whenever Emma felt angry or scared, she remembered Milo's special tricks: deep breathing, squeezing something soft, and saying kind words to herself. And she loved trying new foods from different places, especially with Grandma's stories about where they came from.

Milo smiled as he watched Emma enjoy her dinner. Sometimes the best adventures happen when we learn to handle our big feelings with kindness.

The End.
