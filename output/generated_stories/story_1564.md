# Fawn’s Big Breakfast Plan

In a sunny dining room, a gentle deer fawn named Fern stood on soft hooves by a small wooden chair. The table was smooth and warm, and the morning light made little golden squares on the floor. Fern wiggled her ears and took a deep breath. She smelled toast, sweet berries, and warm oatmeal. Her tummy gave a tiny rumble.

“Today, I have a goal,” Fern said. Her voice was soft and brave. “I want to make a strong and happy breakfast. I want to build it, one step at a time.”

Her best friend, a small gray mouse named Pip, peeked from a napkin ring. “A goal?” Pip squeaked. “What’s a goal?”

Fern smiled. “A goal is a plan I want to do. I think it, I say it, and I try it, step by step.”

Pip clapped his tiny paws. “Squeak-tastic! What’s step one?”

Fern tapped the table with her hoof. Tap. Tap. “Step one: Make a list,” she said. She looked around the dining room. The window was open a little. A soft breeze brought the scent of apples and the sound of a faraway bluebird. The table held a big bowl of fruit, a basket of bread, and a little jar of honey that sparkled like sunshine.

Fern spoke slowly. “I need fruit for vitamins. I need grains for energy. I need something with protein to help me grow. I need water to drink.”

Pip nodded. “Good plan. What can we use?”

Fern looked at the fruit bowl. “I see red strawberries, blue blueberries, and a crunchy green apple.” She touched them gently with her hoof. The strawberries felt cool and bumpy. The blueberries were round and smooth. The apple smelled fresh and sweet.

“Step two: Build the plate,” Fern said. “Let’s make a rainbow.”

Pip pointed at the bread basket. “We can add toast for grains!”

Fern giggled. “Yes! Crunchy toast.” She pressed the button on the little toaster. Click. In a moment, the toast popped up with a happy spring. Pop! It smelled warm and toasty, like a hug.

“Step three: Add protein,” Fern said, thinking. She peeked into a small bowl on the table and found creamy yogurt. “This will help me grow strong.”

Pip licked his whiskers. “And honey for sweetness?”

“Just a little,” said Fern. She drizzled a tiny golden line. It glowed in the light.

Together, they started to build. Fern placed toast on a plate. Pip stacked strawberry slices like red bricks. Fern added blueberries like little round roof tiles. She spread yogurt like soft white cement. They were building a breakfast house!

“We are builders!” Pip cheered.

Fern nodded proudly. “We are building our goal.”

She added the crunchy apple slices to make a door and windows. “Knock, knock,” she giggled, tapping the apple door with her hoof. Tap-tap. The plate looked colorful and bright. It smelled fruity and sweet. The toast made a soft crackle when Fern pressed it.

“Step four: Check the plan,” Fern said. “Do we have fruit, grains, and protein? Do we have water?”

Pip looked at a pitcher on the table. The water inside sparkled like tiny stars. He poured a cup. “Sip, sip,” said Fern, taking a drink. The water felt cool and kind.

Pip’s whiskers twitched. “I like tiny bites,” he said. “My teeth are small. Can we make mouse-size pieces?”

Fern nodded. “We are different, and that is okay. We can celebrate it.” She cut tiny strawberry hearts for Pip and kept bigger slices for herself. “We are the same in our goal, but different in our bites.”

Pip smiled. “Different is special!”

Fern stood tall. “Step five: Try and taste!”

She took a bite of toast with yogurt and berries. Crunch. Creamy. Sweet. Her ears tingled with joy. Pip nibbled his tiny heart-shaped strawberry. Nibble-nibble. He squeaked, “Yum!”

Fern felt proud. “I set a goal. I made a plan. I built my breakfast. I did it.”

Pip raised his cup. “To goals!”

Fern raised hers too. “To goals, and to good food!” They clinked cups. Clink.

After they ate, Fern looked at the plate. A few blueberries rolled like little marbles. She gathered them up and popped them into her mouth. “Step six: Clean up,” she said. “Finishing the goal means taking care of our space.”

They wiped the table. Swish, swish. They carried dishes to the sink. Clatter, clink. The dining room felt neat and calm again. The soft breeze touched Fern’s face like a friendly paw.

Pip stretched. “What’s next, Fern?”

Fern’s eyes shone. “New day, new goals. Maybe we can build a snack tower later. But for now, I feel strong, happy, and ready.”

She looked at the sunny table and thought about her steps. Make a list. Build the plate. Check the plan. Try and taste. Clean up. She whispered, “Small steps make big goals.”

Pip nodded. “And friends help, too.”

Fern nuzzled Pip gently. “Yes. We can be different and still work together.”

They smiled, their hearts warm and light, as the bluebird sang from the open window. The dining room glowed with morning cheer, and Fern felt proud of her plan and proud of herself.

The End.
