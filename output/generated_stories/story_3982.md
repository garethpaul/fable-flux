# Ellie’s Bubble Dance

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, Ellie padded into the bathroom. The tiles felt cool on her feet. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. The mirror was shiny like a lake. The air smelled like soap and mint.

Ellie loved to dance. She swayed her trunk and wiggled her little tail. She wanted to make a dance show for her family. But the living room was busy. The kitchen was busy. The bedroom was full of toys. Only the bathroom was quiet.

“I will dance here,” Ellie whispered. She turned on the tap just a little. Swish! Water sang a tiny song. She tapped one foot. Tap, tap. She slid the bath mat a little. Swish, swish. The bath mat felt squishy and safe.

But there was a problem. Ellie's little brother, Pip, wanted a bubble bath. “Ellie, I want bubbles!” Pip said, holding a fluffy towel. “Can I have the tub?”

Ellie felt her tummy twist. She wanted to dance. Pip wanted bubbles. The bathroom was small. There wasn’t space for two big plans.

Mama Elephant peeked in. “What’s the plan, my dancers?” she asked with a warm smile.

Ellie’s ears drooped. “I want to dance,” she said softly. “I want to make a show. But Pip wants a bath.”

Pip held up a bottle. “Bubbles! Bubbles!” he sang.

Mama nodded. “Hmm. We have one bathroom and two ideas. What could we do?”

Ellie took a deep breath. She listened to the drip, drip. She looked at the shiny mirror. She felt the soft bath mat under her toes. Then her eyes sparkled.

“I have an idea!” Ellie said. “I can make a bubble dance! Pip can have bubbles, and I can use them in my show.”

Pip clapped. “Bubble dance! Bubble dance!”

Mama smiled. “That sounds generous, Ellie. You are sharing the space and your show. Let’s make it safe and fun.”

They set the room. Mama put a towel at the edge of the tub. She set out a small stool for Pip to sit on while the tub filled. The floor was dry and cozy.

Ellie opened the bubble bottle. Pop! A sweet soapy smell filled the room. She dipped a big round wand in the bubbly water. Whoosh! A shiny bubble floated up. It looked like a tiny rainbow moon.

“Welcome to the Bubble Dance,” Ellie announced in her best stage voice. “I am Ellie the dancer!”

Pip giggled. “And I am Pip the clapper!”

Ellie began to move. She swayed side to side. Sway, sway. Her feet went tip, tap, tip, tap on the mat. Her trunk drew circles in the air. She blew gently through the wand. Poof! A string of bubbles drifted like stars.

“Look, Pip! Catch the bubble with your breath,” Ellie said.

Pip leaned forward. “Pfffff!” He puffed and the bubble wobbled. Pop! He laughed and clapped. Clap, clap, clap!

Ellie twirled, careful and slow. The mirror showed her a happy dancer with bright eyes and big ears. She felt brave. She felt free. She felt like her inside ideas were coming outside.

She made a new move. “This is the Drip-Drop Step,” she said. “Drip.” She bent one knee. “Drop.” She bent the other knee. The faucet dripped in time. Drip, drop, drip, drop.

Pip copied her. “Drip! Drop!” His towel fluff brushed his cheeks. It felt soft like clouds.

Ellie giggled. “And this is the Soap Bubble Sway.” She held the wand high and swayed slow. Bubbles floated up, spinning and shining. The bathroom light made rainbow spots on the wall. The air felt cool and tickly on her skin.

Mama hummed a gentle tune. “Mmm-mmm,” she sang. The room felt warm and happy.

Then, another problem. The bubbles began to pile up in the tub. The foam grew and grew. It wobbled like a big white mountain.

“Oh no,” Pip said. “Too many!”

Ellie thought fast. “I can use them!” She scooped a handful of foam and shaped it into a hat. “The Bubble Crown!” She placed it on Pip’s head. He beamed.

She made a bubble bracelet for herself. She made a tiny bubble bow for the soap bottle. “Now everyone is in the show,” Ellie said, giggling.

“Generous and creative,” Mama said. “You are sharing your art and helping with the bath.”

Ellie finished with her Sparkle Spin. She turned one time, two times, three. The bubbles floated around her like snowflakes. Pop, pop, pop! Soft, gentle pops like tiny kisses.

“And now,” Ellie said, bowing, “the bath for Pip!”

She helped Pip climb into the warm, bubbly tub. The water hugged him. Swoosh! He kicked his feet. “It’s a bubble sea!” he cheered.

Ellie stood by the tub and hummed. She made small bubble rings and let Pip pop them. She shared her dance wand and taught him the Drip-Drop Step with his toes in the water. Pip laughed and splashed carefully.

When the bath was done, Mama wrapped Pip in his fluffy towel. Ellie wiped the floor and put the bubble bottle away. The bathroom smelled clean and sweet.

Mama hugged Ellie. “You solved the problem with kindness,” she said. “You used your creativity to make room for both of you.”

Ellie’s heart felt warm. “I learned that I can show my feelings with dancing,” she said. “And I can share my art so others can smile, too.”

Pip nodded. “Bubble dance again tomorrow?”

Ellie grinned. “Yes! And maybe a Toothbrush Tango!”

They all laughed. The bathroom was quiet again. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. Ellie’s feet felt happy and light. She had danced, she had shared, and she had helped.

The End.
