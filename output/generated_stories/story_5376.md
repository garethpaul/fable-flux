# Bella Bird Sings in the Study Corner

Bella was a small singing bird with bright blue feathers and a golden beak. She loved to sing soft songs in the study corner by the window. The study corner had a tiny desk, a jar of crayons, smooth paper, and a comfy green rug. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. It smelled like sharpened pencils and orange tea. Outside, the leaves made whispery sounds, shhh-shhh, like they were listening.

Bella hopped onto a pencil cup and hummed. “La-la-la,” she sang. Her song was gentle and sweet, like warm honey. Singing made her chest feel light and happy.

Today, Bella had a big idea. “I will make a Friendship Song,” she chirped. “A song to share and a song to care.” She puffed up her feathers and began, but her notes came out wobbly. “La… laa… oh dear,” she said softly. Her tummy felt twisty. “I wish I had a friend to help me.”

A small voice answered from the hallway. “Hello?”

A child named Sam peeked in. Sam wore a red sweater and held a little drum made from a box and rubber bands. “I heard a pretty song,” Sam said, smiling. “My grandma taught me to tap rhythms. It is our family tradition.”

Bella’s eyes sparkled. “I am Bella. I am making a Friendship Song. I feel shy. Will you help me?”

Sam nodded. “Yes! We can share our traditions. You sing, and I tap.” Sam stepped onto the green rug. Tap-tap, tap-tap. The little drum made soft, bouncy sounds.

Bella tried again. “La-la-laaa,” she sang. The rhythm held her up, like a hand to hold. Her voice felt steadier. “Oh! Thank you, Sam,” Bella chirped. “Your tapping helps my heart feel brave.”

Just then, a gentle wind slid through the window screen. Papers lifted and fluttered. One paper flew to the floor and slid under the desk.

“Oh no,” said Bella. “That paper has our kindness list. We wrote ways to care for our study corner. We must keep our space tidy. That is our responsibility.” She took a breath. “I can feel my wings go fast when I worry.”

Sam crouched down and peeked under the desk. “We can solve it together,” Sam said calmly. “First, let’s take a big belly breath.” Sam put a hand on their tummy. In… and out. Bella copied, fluffing her feathers as she breathed. Her heartbeat slowed.

“I feel better,” Bella said. “Thank you for helping me notice my feelings.”

Sam smiled. “Teamwork time.” Sam reached under the desk and pulled out the paper. It was dusty. “Let’s dust and straighten, too.”

Bella fluttered to the shelf and grabbed a tiny cloth. Swish-swish! Sam wiped the desk. Swipe-swipe! They stacked the papers neatly. They put crayons back in the jar, red first, then blue, then yellow, click-click.

Bella read the kindness list aloud. “Take turns. Use gentle hands. Clean up. Share traditions. Ask before borrowing. Help a friend who is sad.”

Sam nodded. “Those rules keep our study corner healthy and happy. That is wellness.”

Bella felt a warm glow. “And it is caring for our shared space. That is being a good helper to our little community.”

Sam tapped a happy rhythm. Tap-tap, ta-tap! Bella tried her song again, thinking of the list, the clean desk, the sunny squares, and her new friend.

“La-la-la, friends we are,
In the corner, near and far.
We listen, help, and share our ways,
Bright like sunshine, kind all days.”

Sam added a verse. “Tap, tap, together strong,
Traditions make our friendship song.”

Bella giggled. “Your grandma’s rhythm is special. Thank you for sharing it with me.” She thought for a moment. “My family has a tradition too. We greet the morning sun with a soft hello song.” Bella cleared her throat and sang a tiny tune, like a sunrise peeking. “Loo-loo-loo.”

Sam’s eyes went wide. “It sounds like a golden ribbon,” Sam said. “Can we add it?”

“Yes!” Bella chirped. “Loo-loo-loo,” she sang. Sam answered, “Tap-tap-tap,” and the sounds braided together.

Footsteps padded past the door. Their teacher, Ms. Lee, peeked in. “What lovely music,” she whispered. “I hear careful hands and caring hearts. Thank you for keeping the study corner neat and sharing your traditions.”

Bella felt proud. Her feathers fluffed like a happy cloud. “We worked together,” she said. “I felt shy, but Sam helped me be brave. We cleaned up, and we made a song.”

Ms. Lee smiled. “That is friendship and responsibility. Your music makes our room feel safe and well.”

Sam looked at Bella. “When I feel wiggly or worried, can we sing the Friendship Song?”

Bella nodded. “Yes. We can listen, breathe, and sing.”

They sang one more time, soft and sweet. The window breeze smelled like leaves and orange tea. The study corner glowed with afternoon light. Bella’s heart felt calm and strong. She was not alone. She had a friend.

Lesson: Friends listen, share, and help. We can share our family traditions, care for our space, notice our feelings, and be kind. That makes our hearts, and our study corner, a happy place.

The End.
