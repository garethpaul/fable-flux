# Pippa the Parrot and the Busy Garage

Pippa was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a red tail that shimmered in the light. She loved to say, “I have an idea!” and flap her wings with joy. One sunny morning, Pippa perched on a pegboard in a garage workshop. The air smelled like clean wood and warm metal. The floor felt cool on her feet. She could hear gentle clinks, the soft buzz of a tiny fan, and the squeak of a rolling stool.

“Good morning, tools!” Pippa chirped. The tools glinted under the light. There were shiny wrenches, a blue tape measure, and a box of colorful screws. On the workbench sat a pile of wood, smooth and sandy. Pippa had a plan.

“I will make a book stand,” Pippa said. “A place for my picture books. Then I can read and learn new things!”

Pippa picked up the tape measure with her beak. “Measure, measure,” she sang. “We need to know how long.” She stretched the tape across the wood. Click! The tape snapped back. Pippa blinked. “Oops. I will try again.”

Just then, her friends waddled in. Benny the bunny hopped, Flo the frog bounced, and Tiko the turtle slowly slid along the floor. They liked the cool, shadowy garage. It was a good place to build.

“What are you making?” asked Benny, his ears standing tall.

“A book stand,” said Pippa. “We will use it to hold our books. We can read and learn together.”

“I want to hammer!” Benny said, reaching for a little hammer.

“I want to paint!” Flo croaked, eyes wide at the bright jars of paint.

“I want to hold the screws,” Tiko said in his soft voice.

Pippa fluffed her feathers. She was excited too. “Friends, we will take turns,” she said. “Taking turns is kind. And we will learn what to do. Knowledge helps us do it right.”

Benny hopped in place. “But I really want to hammer now.”

Pippa took a deep breath. “We need to measure first,” she said gently. “The measuring tells us where to cut. That is knowledge. It keeps us safe and makes the stand strong.”

Benny’s ears drooped. “Okay. I can wait.”

Pippa smiled. “Thank you, Benny.” She slid the tape across the wood again. “Tiko, can you hold this end still? Your shell is steady.”

Tiko nodded and pressed softly. “I can do that.”

“Three steps,” Pippa said. “Measure, cut, and then hammer. We will say the steps together.” 

They chanted, “Measure, cut, hammer!” Their voices echoed like tiny drums in the garage.

Pippa marked the wood with a pencil. “We need a smooth cut,” she said. “Flo, can you keep the area clear? That is helpful.”

“I can,” Flo said. She gently moved the paint jars to the corner and hummed. The saw made a gentle zzz-zzz sound as Pippa and Benny, with careful hands and feet, cut along the line. The smell of fresh wood tickled their noses.

“Now it is hammer time!” Benny cheered.

“Wait,” Pippa said in a kind but firm voice. “We need to know where the nails go. See these dots? One, two, three. Knowledge first. Then hammer.”

Benny looked closely. “Oh! The dots show me the places.”

“Yes,” Pippa said. “The dots help us do it right.” She handed Benny the small hammer. “Your turn. We will hold the wood.”

Benny tapped. Tap-tap-tap. The sound was bouncy and bright. “I am tapping, not smashing,” he said proudly.

“You are doing great,” Pippa said. “Thank you for taking turns.”

Flo looked at the paint again. “Is it my turn to paint now?” she asked.

“Almost,” Pippa said. “We must sand the wood, so it is smooth.” She gave Flo a soft sanding block. “Rub gently. Feel how it changes.”

Flo rubbed and smiled. “It feels silky now. Like a leaf after rain.”

Pippa nodded. “Good work. Knowledge tells us why we sand. Smooth wood is safe for little fingers.”

Tiko lifted a screw between his careful claws. “I can bring the screws now,” he said.

“Yes,” Pippa said. “And I will show you how the screwdriver turns. Righty-tighty.” She turned the tool slowly. “Hear the little squeak?” Squeee! “That means it’s snug.”

They all took turns turning the screws. Tiko was slow but steady. Benny counted softly, “One, two, three screws.” Flo hummed a happy tune. Pippa kept the steps clear. “We plan. We learn. We do.”

At last, the book stand was ready. It looked neat and bright. They painted little shapes on it—blue stars, green leaves, and red dots. The paint smelled sweet and tangy. Pippa placed a picture book on the stand. The book sat tall and proud.

“We did it!” Pippa chirped. She flapped her wings in joy.

Benny smiled. “We waited and took turns.”

Flo nodded. “We listened and learned.”

Tiko added, “We were gentle and careful.”

Pippa looked at her friends. “We respected each other. We used knowledge to guide us. And I used my strong voice to say what we needed to do. That is being assertive and kind.”

They all sat on the soft mat. Pippa opened the book and read. Her voice was warm and smooth. The story told them about birds and nests. They learned new words, like feather and twig.

As the sun made golden stripes on the floor, Pippa closed the book. “Today we learned that knowledge helps us do things the right way. When we respect each other and take turns, we can make something wonderful.”

Benny, Flo, and Tiko nodded. “Knowledge is our friend,” they said together.

Pippa smiled and tapped the book stand. “And now we have a special place for more learning tomorrow.”

The End.
