# Pippa Parrot’s Rooftop Parade

Pippa the parrot loved the rooftop. It was warm and sunny up there. The wind made her feathers flutter. She could see the whole street below and hear tiny sounds rise up, like a bus “vroom,” a bell “ding,” and children laughing, “ha-ha!” Pippa liked to repeat sounds. “Vroom! Ding! Ha-ha!” she chirped, her voice bright and bouncy.

One morning, Pippa had an idea. “Let’s make a rooftop parade!” she squawked. “We can share music and colors with everyone.”

She looked around. The rooftop had flower pots, a small blue kiddie pool for birds, and a long string for laundry. There were also chalk sticks in a bucket and a big cardboard box.

Pippa flapped to the edge and called to her friends. “Tap-tap, tap-tap!” she pecked on the railing like a drum. Soon, Tico the tiny sparrow, Luna the pigeon, and Benny the bumblebee buzzed up to join her.

“What is a parade?” asked Tico, his eyes wide.

“A parade is a happy line,” Pippa said. “We move together. We sing. We share. We can make sounds and art for everyone to enjoy.”

Luna cooed softly. “That sounds nice. But how do we start?”

Pippa tilted her head. She was a creative parrot, but she knew a secret: big ideas need many helpers. “We start with cooperation,” she said. “We work together. We listen. We take turns.”

“Co-op-er-a-tion,” Benny buzzed, trying the long word. “What does it feel like?”

“It feels like holding wings,” said Pippa. “It feels like sharing our gifts.”

The friends all nodded. The sun felt warm on their backs. The air smelled like tomato leaves and a little bit like soap from the laundry line.

Pippa pointed with her beak. “I can make words. I can sing! Tico, you are fast. You can draw with chalk. Luna, you are strong. You can carry and place things. Benny, you can make a gentle buzz, like a drum roll!”

“Bzzzz-rr-rr,” Benny hummed, very proud.

They got to work. Tico flitted over the concrete and drew a bright path with yellow chalk. “Step, step, step,” he sang, making little footprints for the parade to follow. The chalk felt dusty and made a soft scratch sound: scritch-scratch.

Luna lifted the cardboard box. “I will make a sign,” she cooed. She puffed and pushed, and Pippa painted letters with a wet brush from the bird pool. “R-O-O-F-T-O-P P-A-R-A-D-E,” she spelled. “Rooftop Parade!” She liked the sound of each letter. “R,” she rolled. “P,” she popped.

Benny zipped to the flower pots and sniffed the tiny flowers. They smelled sweet, like honey. He found colorful petals on the ground and brought them to Pippa. “For beauty,” he buzzed gently.

Pippa arranged the petals around the sign. “Thank you! That is generous, Benny.”

“Gen-er-ous,” Benny repeated, smiling. “Sharing makes my heart hum.”

They practiced together. Pippa tried a song. “La-la! Vroom, ding! Ha-ha!” She switched to new words too. “Hola! Bonjour! Hello!” The sounds felt round and friendly in her beak. “Language is music,” she told her friends. “Words can be gifts.”

Tico hopped onto the chalk path and did a tiny dance. “Tap-tap-tap!” His feet made happy dots.

Luna tested the laundry string and clipped the sign to it with careful beak and claws. The string went swish in the breeze, and the sign wiggled but stayed up.

They were ready. But just then, a gust of wind blew. Whoosh! The cardboard sign slipped and spun. “Oh no!” cried Tico.

Pippa flapped hard, but the sign floated away toward the edge. Benny buzzed after it, but it was too big for him alone. Luna dove under the sign and pushed up, cooing, “I’ve got you!” Tico zipped to the clothespins and grabbed one in his tiny beak. Pippa flew beside Luna and steadied the sign with her wing.

“Together!” Pippa called. “One, two, three—lift!” On three, all four friends moved at the same time. Luna pushed, Pippa guided, Tico clipped, and Benny buzzed to keep them steady. The sign snapped back onto the string. Click!

They all panted. Then they all laughed. “Ha-ha!” said Pippa. “We did it. Cooperation saved the day.”

Soon, neighbors peeked out of windows. A child pointed up and clapped. “Look! A parade!” The street cat below flicked her tail and purred, “Mrrr.” The sounds rose to the rooftop like a friendly wave.

Pippa led the parade along the chalk path. She sang, “Hello! Hola! Bonjour!” Tico danced. Luna carried a tiny pot with a red flower, giving it to a window ledge where a woman smiled and said, “Thank you!” Benny made a soft drum roll, “bzzzz-rrr,” before each new hello.

They moved in a line, slow and happy. The sun felt warm. The breeze felt cool. The chalk dust tickled their toes. Everyone felt the same bright feeling: we are together.

After the parade, Pippa sat with her friends by the kiddie pool. They dipped their feet in the cool water and watched the sign sway gently.

“Today was special,” said Luna.

“It was,” said Tico. “We worked as one.”

Benny buzzed softly. “Cooperation is a big word with a warm feeling.”

Pippa smiled and fluffed her feathers. “Yes. Cooperation means we listen, we share, and we help. When we work together, we can make big, beautiful things. And we can give joy to others.”

She looked at her friends. “Thank you for your help. Thank you for your generosity.”

“Thank you for your words,” said Luna.

“Thank you for your ideas,” said Tico.

“Thank you for your song,” buzzed Benny.

Pippa repeated softly, like a song, “Thank you, thank you, thank you.” The words felt like sunshine. She liked how they sounded in every language.

The friends watched the sky turn soft and golden. They felt proud, calm, and close.

They had made a parade. They had shared it with kindness. And they had done it together.

The End.
