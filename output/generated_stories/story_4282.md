# Splash on the Balcony

Dori was a playful dolphin who loved to learn new things. Dori did not live in the ocean today. Dori was visiting a big city. Dori was staying with friends in a tiny home with a sunny balcony garden. The balcony was full of green leaves, red tomatoes, little bells, and soft singing wind chimes.

“Hello, plants!” Dori squeaked. “I am Dori, the playful dolphin!”

Dori flipped her tail in a blue tub of water and made a friendly splash. The splash sounded like a clap. Clap, clap! The air smelled like mint and sweet basil. Bees buzzed softly, and a small scooter hummed in the street below.

Friends came to the balcony garden to play. They were from many places. Mei wore a bright red ribbon from China. Awa had a patterned scarf from Senegal. Luca had a soccer ball from Italy. Priya wore shiny bangles from India. Tomo brought a little drum from Japan. Everyone smiled at Dori.

“Let’s play sports!” said Luca. “We can do balcony games!”

“Yes!” squeaked Dori. “We will share traditions too. We will learn from each other.”

They made a plan. Dori showed initiative. “I will lead,” said Dori. “We will take turns. We will listen. We will be kind.”

First, Luca showed soccer moves. Tap, tap, tap went his feet. “This is dribbling,” Luca said. Dori tried to tap the ball with her tail. Thump! The ball bumped a pot. The pot wiggled.

“Oops,” said Dori. “I will be gentle.” Dori took a deep breath. In, out. Calm.

Mei showed a ribbon dance. The red ribbon swirled like a dragon. “We use it for festivals,” said Mei. Dori swished water in the tub. Swish, swirl, swirl! “It looks like your ribbon,” Dori giggled.

Awa taught a clapping game from her grandma. Clap, clap, pause. Clap, clap, pause. “In my home, we sing with it,” Awa said. They sang a simple song. The song was warm and happy. The basil leaves shook with the beat.

Tomo tapped his little drum. Tum-tum, ta! “This drum is called a taiko,” he said. The sound was deep. It felt like a soft hug in the belly. Dori tapped the tub rim with her flipper. Tap-tap! “We are a team,” she squeaked.

Priya showed a yoga pose her auntie taught her. “This is tree pose,” Priya said. She stood tall and balanced. Everyone tried. Dori tried too. Wobble, wobble, splish! Dori laughed. “I will try again.” She breathed slowly and felt steady.

Then Luca wanted to kick the ball again. “My turn!” he shouted. Tomo said, “But my drum turn isn’t done.” Luca’s face turned red. His eyebrows pulled down. His foot stamped. Thump.

Dori saw the anger. The balcony felt tight and prickly. The wind chime went quiet.

Dori took initiative. She spoke softly. “Pause. Let’s help our feelings.” Dori showed her fins. “We can do calm bubbles. Blow with me.”

Everyone made bubble breaths. In through the nose, out through the mouth. Bubble, bubble. Luca’s face softened. Tomo’s shoulders relaxed.

“I felt mad,” Luca said. “I wanted more turns.”

“Thank you for saying your feeling,” said Dori. “Feelings are okay. We can share. We can wait. We can take turns.”

Tomo nodded. “I will finish my drum in one minute. Then Luca can kick.”

Mei held up her ribbon. “While we wait, I can wave the ribbon like a timer.” The ribbon floated for a short swirl. One minute was done. They all clapped. Luca smiled. “Now I feel ready.”

They made a new rule together. Dori said it slowly. “We will listen. We will take turns. We will share our traditions. We will use calm breaths when we are upset.”

Next, Awa taught a soccer song from her uncle. “In Wolof, we cheer like this!” She sang, and Luca kicked gently. Tap, goal! Everyone cheered. Priya shared a sweet smell. “This is cardamom from my mom’s tea.” They sniffed. Mmm, warm and spicy.

Dori shared too. “In my ocean home, we click and whistle to talk.” Dori clicked and whistled. Click-click, whee-ee! The sound was bright and bubbly. Friends tried the sounds. The bees buzzed along, and the wind chime sang again.

They made a balcony game together. It was a mix of all their traditions. They dribbled the ball to the drum beat. Tap, tum-tum! They froze in tree pose when the ribbon made a loop. They sang Awa’s clap song and did calm bubble breaths if someone felt too excited. They took turns being leader.

The sun felt warm on their cheeks. The mint leaves tickled their noses. The tub water was cool on Dori’s fins. Laughter filled the air like birds.

When it was time to rest, Dori floated in the tub. “Today we learned so much,” she said. “We learned that we are different and the same.”

Mei nodded. “Your clicks are new to me.”

“And your ribbon is new to me,” said Dori.

Awa smiled. “We shared our traditions.”

Priya added, “We used calm breaths when we were mad.”

Luca kicked the ball softly and said, “We made a game with everyone’s ideas!”

Dori squeaked with joy. “That is cultural diversity. It means many ways, many colors, many songs, and we all belong.”

They sat in the soft evening light. The chimes tinkled. The plants whispered. The friends held hands.

Dori said, “When we listen, share, and calm our feelings, we can play together. We can learn together. We can celebrate each other.”

Everyone nodded. “Together is best,” they said.

The End.
