# Koala Kiki Builds With Care

Koala Kiki was a calm koala with soft gray fur and gentle eyes. One sunny morning, Kiki padded into the guest room. The room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. A soft rug tickled Kiki’s toes. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor.

In the middle of the guest room stood a big box of wooden blocks. The blocks were smooth and colorful—red, blue, yellow, and green. “I will build a cozy tower,” Kiki whispered. “I will build it with care.”

Kiki set a wide blue block on the rug. Tap, tap. Then a red block. Then a green one. Kiki breathed in slow and out slow. “Patience,” Kiki said. “One block at a time.”

Just then, there was a gentle knock. Knock, knock. Kiki opened the door. It was Tiny Turtle, holding a small toolbox with stickers. “Hi, Kiki,” Tiny Turtle said softly. “Can I build too?”

“Yes,” said Kiki with a smile. “We can build together. We will be kind. We will be patient. We will be safe.”

Tiny Turtle slid in and set the toolbox down with a soft clink. “I brought soft gloves,” Tiny said. “They help me hold blocks.”

“That is safe,” Kiki said. “Let’s use gentle hands. Let’s keep our bodies calm.”

They washed their hands in the sink by the door. Splash, splash. They dried them on a fluffy towel. Swish, swish. Then they went back to the rug.

Kiki said, “First, we make a strong base. Four big blocks.” Kiki and Tiny Turtle lined up the blocks. Thunk, thunk, thunk, thunk. The base felt steady under their fingers.

Tiny Turtle’s eyes sparkled. “Now we go high! Fast!” Tiny reached for a skinny block and set it on top. The tower wiggled. Wobble, wobble.

Kiki held out a paw. “Let’s pause. Let’s be patient.” Kiki’s voice was calm like a soft song. “We can keep it safe.”

Tiny Turtle took a deep breath. “Okay. What is next?”

Kiki showed Tiny how to press down softly, not hard. “Gentle hands,” Kiki said. “Kind words. Kind bodies.”

They built three more layers. The blocks clicked together. Click. Click. Click. The tower grew as tall as Kiki’s nose. Tiny Turtle giggled. “It looks like a rainbow!”

The door creaked. Squeak. Little Moth fluttered in, with shiny wings that caught the sunlight. “Oh! Blocks!” Little Moth hummed and swooped low. The breeze made the curtains wave. Swish.

The tower shook. Wobble, wobble, wobble!

Tiny Turtle squeaked, “No! Don’t touch!” Little Moth froze in the air, wide-eyed. The tower leaned. Kiki placed a steady paw on the side, like a gentle hug for the blocks.

“It’s okay,” Kiki said softly. “We can be kind. We can be safe.” Kiki looked at Little Moth. “Would you like to help, too?”

“Yes,” Little Moth whispered. “But I flap too fast.”

“That’s okay,” Kiki said. “We can all slow down together.” Kiki showed Little Moth how to flap softly. Flutter, flutter. “Let’s use whisper wings.”

They tried again. Tiny Turtle handed Kiki a flat yellow block. “Here,” Tiny said kindly. “You can place this one.”

“Thank you,” Kiki said. “That was kind.” Kiki slid the yellow block under the leaning side. The tower stood tall again. Strong. Still.

Little Moth hovered close, wings quiet. “I can watch for wobbles,” Little Moth said.

“That is a helpful job,” Kiki said. “We all have a job. Tiny Turtle is our block helper. Little Moth is our wobble watcher. I am the steady builder.”

They worked together. They took turns. They spoke kindly. “Your turn.” “Thank you.” “You did it!” Their words felt warm and soft in the room.

Soon the tower had windows made from square blocks and a little doorway of two tall blocks and one long block. The sunlight made the colors glow like candy. The tower was beautiful.

Tiny Turtle’s tummy rumbled. “I’m hungry,” Tiny said.

Kiki nodded. “Let’s take a safe break.” They stepped back from the tower. They put the extra blocks in the box so no one would trip. They walked slowly around the tower and sat on the rug. Crunch, crunch, they ate apple slices from a small plate. The apple was sweet and juicy.

After the snack, Kiki said, “We can make the roof.” Kiki picked up a big triangle block. Tiny Turtle held the base still with gloved hands. Little Moth watched the top. “No wobble!” Little Moth whispered.

Kiki set the triangle carefully. Tap. The tower did not wiggle. It stood proud and tall.

“We did it!” they cheered. The guest room felt cozy and bright. The tower looked like a friendly little house.

Kiki smiled. “We were kind. We were patient. We were safe. That is how we solved our problem.”

Tiny Turtle nodded. “When I rushed, it wobbled. When we slowed down, it was strong.”

Little Moth twirled. “Whisper wings helped!”

Kiki patted the rug. “Let’s make a sign.” They wrote together on a small card: “Kind Hands. Patient Hearts. Safe Steps.” Kiki taped it to the box of blocks.

They cleaned up the room. The blocks went click-clack into the box. The towel went into the hamper. The toolbox closed with a snap. The guest room smelled fresh and lemony again.

Kiki looked at the tower one more time. “Our tower is a kind tower,” Kiki said. “It was built with care.”

Tiny Turtle and Little Moth smiled. Their hearts felt warm and calm.

And in the quiet guest room, the tower stood strong—because kindness, patience, and safety held it up.

The End.
