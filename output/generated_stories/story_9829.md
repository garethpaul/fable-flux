# Koala Kiko Cleans Up Kindly

Kiko was a calm koala with soft gray fur and bright, gentle eyes. One sunny morning, Kiko padded into the creative art room. The room smelled like sweet glue and fresh paper. The tables were covered with colorful markers, squishy clay, and shiny buttons that sparkled like tiny stars.

“Today I make art,” Kiko whispered, smiling. “I will make a rainbow tree.”

Kiko took a deep breath in and out, slow and steady. “I feel calm,” Kiko said. “I feel ready.”

Kiko dipped a paintbrush in cool blue paint. Swish, swish! The brush made soft sounds on the paper. Kiko tapped bright yellow dots. Tap, tap! Kiko pressed red buttons into sticky glue. Press! Press! It felt bumpy and fun.

Soon, Kiko’s rainbow tree was big and bright. “Oh! I love it,” Kiko said, clapping softly.

But there was a little problem. Kiko had used many things and left them all around. Glue lids were open. Markers rolled on the floor. Crayons lay without their box. Bits of paper made crinkly piles. The table was a mess, and the floor was, too.

Kiko looked at the art. Kiko looked at the mess. Kiko felt a tiny twist in the tummy.

“Hm,” Kiko said, ears drooping a little. “I made the mess. I can fix the mess.”

Just then, Ms. Bella the teacher walked by with a kind smile. She smelled like orange soap. “Hello, Kiko,” she said. “Your rainbow tree is lovely.”

“Thank you,” Kiko said. “But I see a problem. I made art, and now I made a mess.”

Ms. Bella nodded. “I see. What is your plan?”

Kiko put a paw on the heart. “I have a responsible plan,” Kiko said. “I will clean up what I used. I will care for the room. I will care for me.”

“That is responsibility,” Ms. Bella said. “You can do it. One small step at a time.”

Kiko took another slow breath. In. Out. “Step one,” Kiko said. “Close the glue.” Kiko clicked the glue lids on tight. Click! Click! The glue smelled less strong now. That felt better.

“Step two,” Kiko said. “Caps on markers.” Kiko found each cap and pressed it on. Pop! Pop! The markers would not dry out now. They would be ready for next time.

“Step three,” Kiko said. “Crayons in the box.” Kiko slid the crayons in, one by one. Scritch, scritch. The box made a happy sound.

“Step four,” Kiko said. “Paper bits in the bin.” Kiko scooped the crinkly paper. Crinkle, crinkle. Into the bin it went.

“Step five,” Kiko said. “Wipe the table.” Kiko got a soft cloth and a little warm water. Swish, swoosh. The table became smooth and clean. The room smelled fresh.

Kiko looked around. The markers were safe. The glue was closed. The crayons were cozy in their box. The floor was clear. The art room looked peaceful again.

“I did it,” Kiko said, feeling warm and proud. Kiko’s ears perked up, and the tummy twist was gone.

Ms. Bella clapped softly. “Kiko, you took responsibility,” she said. “You used the tools, and you cared for them. You cared for the room, and you cared for yourself.”

Kiko nodded. “Cleaning helps my body feel calm,” Kiko said. “The room looks tidy. I can breathe easy.”

Kiko took a tiny sip of cool water. “Ah,” Kiko sighed. “That feels good.” Kiko stretched arms up high. “My body is happy. My mind is happy.”

Two friends, Pip the puppy and Mina the mouse, peeked in. “Wow!” Pip said. “The room looks so nice.”

Mina’s whiskers twitched. “How did you do it?”

Kiko smiled. “I made a plan. I cleaned what I used. I put things back. That is responsibility.”

Pip wagged his tail. “Can we try?”

“Yes,” Kiko said. “After we make art, we clean our part.”

They all made small pictures. Pip painted a blue boat. Mina drew a strawberry. When they were done, Kiko said, “Now we clean.”

Pip closed the glue. Click! Mina capped the markers. Pop! Kiko wiped the table. Swish! Together they hummed a little tune: “Use it, love it, put it back. Caring keeps us on the track.”

Ms. Bella beamed. “You are responsible artists,” she said. “You keep the room safe. You keep your hearts calm. That is good wellness.”

Kiko hugged the rainbow tree picture. “I learned,” Kiko said. “When I make a mess, I can make it better. I can take care of my things. I can take care of my space. That is responsibility.”

Pip and Mina nodded. “We can do it too!”

Kiko felt peaceful and proud. The art room looked bright and clear. The air smelled sweet. The tools were ready for tomorrow.

Kiko waved to friends. “We made art. We cleaned up. We feel good.”

“Yes,” said Ms. Bella. “Responsible choices help everyone.”

Kiko took one more slow breath. “In. Out. I am calm. I am caring. I am responsible.”

The End.
