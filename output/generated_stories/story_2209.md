# Benny Bear's Closet Gym

Benny Bear loved his special closet hideaway. He had soft blankets on the floor and his favorite toys all around. The small space felt cozy and safe, like a warm hug.

But today, Benny felt wiggly and restless. His paws wanted to move. His body wanted to stretch. The closet felt too small for all his energy.

"I need to exercise," said Benny. "But there's no room in here!" He looked around his tiny hideaway. The walls seemed too close together.

Benny sat down and felt sad. "Maybe I need to go outside," he thought. But outside felt scary and big. His closet was his favorite place to be.

Then Benny had an idea! He stood up tall and stretched his arms high above his head. His paws almost touched the ceiling.

"I can stretch up!" he said happily. Benny reached left and right. He twisted his fuzzy body like a tree in the wind. It felt wonderful!

Next, Benny tried marching in place. His big paws made soft thumping sounds on the blankets. "Left, right, left, right," he counted. The rhythm made him smile.

"What else can I do?" wondered Benny. He looked at his pile of stuffed animals. "I can lift weights!" He picked up his toy elephant with both paws. Up and down, up and down. His arms felt strong.

Benny tried touching his toes. His round belly made it tricky, but he kept trying. "Almost there," he huffed. When he finally touched them, he felt proud.

"I can do jumping jacks too!" Benny spread his arms and legs wide, then brought them back together. The closet was just big enough. Jump, clap, jump, clap!

Soon Benny was breathing hard, but he felt amazing. His whole body was warm and happy. The closet didn't feel too small anymore. It felt just right for his special workout.

"I solved my problem!" said Benny proudly. "I don't need a big space to exercise. I just need to be creative!"

Benny's friend Rabbit peeked into the closet. "What are you doing, Benny? You look so happy!"

"I'm exercising in my hideaway!" said Benny. "Want to try? We can stretch together!"

Rabbit squeezed into the closet. "Show me how," she said.

Benny taught Rabbit all his new exercises. They stretched high like tall trees. They marched like little soldiers. They lifted toy weights and giggled together.

"This is so much fun!" said Rabbit. "You're really smart, Benny. You figured out how to exercise anywhere!"

Benny's chest puffed up with pride. He had used his thinking skills to solve his problem. He didn't need to leave his safe, cozy hideaway to feel good and strong.

From that day on, Benny's closet became his special exercise room. Whenever he felt wiggly or restless, he knew exactly what to do. He would stretch, march, lift, and jump until he felt happy and calm.

"Every problem has a solution," Benny would tell his friends. "You just have to think creatively and believe in yourself!"

And in his cozy closet hideaway, Benny Bear always felt strong, confident, and proud.

The End.
