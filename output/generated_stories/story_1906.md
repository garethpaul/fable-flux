# Bunny Hops in the Art Room

Thump, thump! A determined rabbit named Ruby hopped into the creative art room. The room smelled like lemony paint. Sunshine slid across the floor in bright squares. Paper birds hung from the ceiling and wiggled when the fan went whoosh.

Ruby’s ears stood tall. “Today, I will dance,” she said. “I will dance all by myself.”

On the wall was a big mirror. On the floor were soft mats like puffy clouds. In the corner, a little speaker blinked a blue light. Ruby pressed play. Tinkly music drifted out, like raindrops on a window.

Ruby lifted one paw. She tried a hop, a spin, and a wiggle. Her feet went slip, slip. Her tail bobbed, bump! She tried a big leap. Thud! She landed on her soft bottom. Ruby’s nose scrunched. Her whiskers shook.

“I can’t do it,” she whispered. Her chest felt tight, like a little knot. Her ears drooped.

Just then, the art room door went creak. Ms. Lark, the art and movement teacher, smiled. She wore painty overalls and shiny sneakers. “Hello, Ruby,” she said. “I see a brave dancer.”

Ruby blinked. “I’m trying to dance. I want to do it by myself. But I keep falling.”

Ms. Lark sat on a mat. “Falling is part of learning,” she said gently. “I can show you a way to calm your body and try again by yourself. Would you like that?”

Ruby rubbed her paws together. “Yes, please.”

“First,” Ms. Lark said softly, “we breathe.” She put a hand on her belly. Ruby put a paw on hers. “Smell the flower.” They breathed in, slow. “Blow the candle.” They breathed out, slow. In… and out… in… and out…

Ruby felt the knot melt. Her shoulders dropped. The music sounded softer and kinder. The room smelled like fresh paper and crayons. Her paws felt warm and ready.

“Now,” said Ms. Lark, “pick three moves you can do. Simple and strong.”

Ruby’s ears perked. “I can hop,” she said. “I can wiggle. I can spin.”

“Perfect,” said Ms. Lark. “This is your dance. You are the boss of your body. Try hop-wiggle-spin. Then again. You can add more when you are ready.”

Ruby stood tall. “I am the boss of my body,” she said. She pressed play again. Tinkly music twinkled.

“Hop,” she whispered. She hopped. “Wiggle.” Her tail wiggled like a happy worm. “Spin.” She turned slow, paws light on the mat. She felt the breeze on her cheeks. She heard the soft swish of her feet. She smiled.

She tried again. Hop. Wiggle. Spin. “Oops!” She tipped. Her paws slid. Thump! She landed on her side. Frustration bubbled up. Her eyes got shiny.

Ruby put her paw on her belly. “Smell the flower… blow the candle…” In… out… in… out… The bubbles popped. She felt calm again.

“I can try again,” Ruby said. “I am learning.”

She practiced. Hop. Wiggle. Spin. Hop. Wiggle. Spin. Her feet made gentle tap-tap sounds. Her ears fluttered like ribbons. The hanging paper birds ruffled and cheered in the airflow.

Ruby added a new move. “Hop-hop!” Two hops in a row. She added a soft “slide.” She slid one paw and then the other. Slide, slide. She giggled. “This feels good!”

Ms. Lark clapped. “I see focus. I see choices. I see a dancer making a plan.”

Ruby beamed. “I’m doing it all by myself.”

Soon, other friends peeked in. A fox with paint on his nose, a mouse with glittery cheeks, and a turtle with a tiny scarf. “Can we watch?” asked the mouse.

Ruby took a breath. “Yes. I will show you my dance.”

The room was quiet, like a friendly hush. Ruby started. “Hop. Wiggle. Spin. Hop-hop. Slide.” She moved with the music. Her paws felt sure. Her heart felt proud. When she finished, the friends cheered. Clap-clap-clap!

The turtle asked, “What do you do when it’s hard?”

Ruby smiled. “I breathe. I say, ‘I can try again.’ I pick simple moves. I make my own plan.”

Ms. Lark nodded. “That’s mindfulness,” she said. “You noticed your feelings. You calmed your body. You chose your steps. That is independence. That is strong.”

Ruby’s whiskers twitched with joy. “Dance helps my body and my mind,” she said. “Dance is a sport, and I can practice.”

The fox wiggled. “Can we try?”

Ruby pointed to the mats. “Yes! Pick three moves you can do. Then put them together.”

The friends picked their moves. The mouse chose tiptoe, sway, and twirl. The turtle chose step, pause, and stretch. The fox chose jump, wiggle, and bow. They all breathed in and out. They tried. They slipped. They tried again. The room filled with soft feet sounds, happy huffs, and quiet giggles.

Ruby led one more time. “Smell the flower. Blow the candle.” Everyone smiled. Everyone moved.

When the music faded, the room felt warm and bright. Ruby hugged herself. “I did it,” she said. “I learned a dance. I kept trying. I listened to my body. I made my own choices.”

Ms. Lark gave a thumbs-up. “That’s the lesson: when you feel stuck, breathe, make a simple plan, and try again. Your body is smart. Your mind is kind. You can dance.”

Ruby’s ears stood tall again. “I am a determined rabbit,” she said. “And I can dance.”

The End.
