# Benny Beaver's Big Day

In a cozy nursery filled with soft toys and colorful blocks, lived a busy little beaver named Benny. His brown fur was fluffy and warm, and his big flat tail made a gentle *thump-thump* sound when he walked across the wooden floor.

Every morning, Benny loved to exercise. He would stretch his tiny paws high up to the ceiling and wiggle his nose. "One, two, three!" he would count as he did his morning jumps. *Bounce, bounce, bounce!*

Today felt different, though. As Benny was doing his exercises, he heard some unusual sounds coming from the toy corner. *Squeak, squeak, rustle, rustle.*

"What could that be?" wondered Benny, tilting his fuzzy head to listen better.

He waddled over to investigate and found three new friends he had never seen before. There was a tall giraffe with spots, a small mouse with pink ears, and a colorful parrot with bright green feathers.

"Hello there!" chirped the parrot. "I'm Polly!"

"I'm Grace," said the giraffe in a gentle voice, bending her long neck down low.

"And I'm Max," squeaked the tiny mouse, his whiskers twitching nervously.

Benny looked at these new friends and felt a little unsure. They all looked so different from him! The giraffe was much taller, the mouse was much smaller, and the parrot had feathers instead of fur.

"Um, hello," said Benny slowly. "I'm Benny. I was just doing my morning exercises."

"Exercises sound fun!" said Grace the giraffe. "But I'm so tall, I might bump my head on the ceiling."

"And I'm so small," worried Max the mouse, "I can't jump very high at all."

"My feathers might get all ruffled," added Polly the parrot.

Benny thought for a moment. His first feeling was that maybe they couldn't all exercise together because they were so different. But then he remembered what his mama always told him: "Every friend is special in their own way."

"Wait!" said Benny, his eyes lighting up. "Maybe we can find exercises that work for everyone!"

Benny's tail made an excited *thump* on the soft nursery rug. "Grace, you could do neck stretches since you're so tall! Max, you could do tiny mouse marches! And Polly, you could flap your beautiful wings!"

The friends looked at each other with growing smiles.

"That sounds wonderful!" said Grace, slowly moving her long neck from side to side. It looked like a graceful dance.

"I love marching!" squeaked Max, taking tiny steps in place. His little paws made the softest *pitter-patter* sounds.

"Wing exercises are my favorite!" sang Polly, spreading her colorful feathers wide.

Soon, all four friends were exercising together in the cozy nursery. Benny did his bouncing jumps, Grace did her neck stretches, Max marched in tiny circles, and Polly flapped her wings. The room filled with happy sounds: *bounce, thump, pitter-patter, swoosh!*

"This is the best exercise time ever!" laughed Benny. "I'm so glad we're all different. It makes everything more fun!"

"Me too!" agreed his new friends.

From that day on, Benny learned that being different wasn't something to worry about – it was something to celebrate! Every morning, the four friends would exercise together, each in their own special way, filling the comfortable nursery with joy and laughter.

The End.
