# Joey Roo and the Music Room

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with a springy tail and bright, curious eyes. Today, Joey hopped into the musical practice room. The room smelled like clean wood and lemon polish. Sunlight danced on shiny drums, a big piano, tiny bells, and a soft green rug. On the wall was a sign that said, “Play, Practice, Protect.”

Joey’s ears wiggled. “I want to play a song!” he said. His voice echoed softly, like a whisper in a seashell.

Miss Wattle, the kind teacher, smiled. “Hello, Joey. Pick an instrument. Take your time. Remember—patience helps the music grow.”

Joey nodded. He grabbed two drumsticks. Tap-tap-tap! Boom-boom! The drum sounded like thunder far away. Joey grinned. “I can do it fast!”

He tapped faster and faster. Tap-tap-tap-tap—oops! One stick slipped from his paw and rolled under the piano.

Joey’s cheeks felt warm. “Oh no,” he said.

“It’s okay,” Miss Wattle said gently. “Let’s slow down. Patience.”

Joey took a deep breath. He crouched down and peered into the dusty space. He saw the stick, a fuzzy dust bunny, and a candy wrapper hiding under the piano.

Joey wrinkled his nose. “It’s messy under here.”

Miss Wattle nodded. “Our room is our home for music. We care for it. That’s environmental care.”

Joey scooped up the stick. “I will help,” he said. He found a little brush and dustpan by the door. Swish, sweep, swish! He swept the dust. He put the wrapper in the recycling bin. The room smelled fresher. Joey’s heart felt light. “I did a good job,” he whispered.

“You did,” Miss Wattle said. “You took care of our space. That is kind to the Earth.”

Joey hopped back to the drum. “I can do this,” he told himself. He counted slowly, “One… two…” Tap—pause—tap—pause. The drum sounded round and full, like a big hug.

“Beautiful,” Miss Wattle said.

Joey smiled. His tail gave a happy thump. “I feel proud.”

Next, Joey tried the tiny bells. They sang like little stars: ting-ting-ting. He wanted to make a song. He pressed his paw to his chest. “I will be patient.”

He made a pattern: ting… ting-ting… ting. He listened. He tried again. Ting… ting-ting… ting. He clapped for himself. Clap, clap! “I’m learning!”

Just then, Joey heard a squeak. A small gecko peeked in the window. “Hello,” Joey whispered. The gecko blinked and scampered away, leaving a tiny leaf on the sill.

Joey picked up the leaf. “This belongs outside,” he said. He opened the window a little. Fresh air drifted in, cool and sweet. He set the leaf back on the garden ledge and closed the window gently. “We protect plants too,” he said.

Miss Wattle nodded. “You are careful and kind. That is justice for our Earth—giving nature what it needs.”

Joey’s ears perked up. He heard a low sound from the piano, like warm chocolate melting. “May I try the piano?” he asked.

“You may,” said Miss Wattle. “First, clean hands.”

Joey washed his paws at the tiny sink. Splish, splash. He dried them on the cloth towel. Soft and warm.

He sat at the piano bench. The keys felt cool and smooth. Joey pressed one key. Plink. He pressed two. Plink-plonk. He giggled. “Hello, keys!”

He wanted to play fast. He wanted to play loud. But he remembered. “Patience helps the music grow.”

Joey chose three notes. Plink… plonk… plunk. He played them again. Plink… plonk… plunk. He hummed a simple tune. “Da-da-daa,” he sang.

A bigger kangaroo, Pete, hopped in. Pete wore big headphones. He reached for the drumsticks Joey had used. Joey watched. “Um, Pete,” Joey said softly, “we take turns. That’s fair.”

Pete paused. He looked at Joey. “Oh,” he said. “I didn’t ask. I’m sorry.”

“It’s okay,” said Joey. “You can play after I finish my song. I will be quick and careful.”

“That is fair,” Miss Wattle said. “Fairness is justice.”

Joey sat tall. He felt brave. “I can do this,” he told himself again. He played his three notes. Plink… plonk… plunk. He added the bells: ting. He tapped the drum once: boom. He took his time. He breathed in, and out. The sounds joined like friends holding hands. Plink… plonk… plunk—ting—boom.

Miss Wattle clapped. Pete clapped. Joey’s chest filled up with warm sunshine. “I made a song,” he whispered. “I did it.”

“You practiced with patience,” Miss Wattle said. “You cared for the room and the Earth. You were fair and kind. And you believed in yourself.”

Joey put the sticks back. He wiped the piano keys with a soft cloth. He put the bells in their basket. He looked around. The room was neat. The air felt bright. Joey’s reflection winked at him in a shiny cymbal. He winked back.

Joey hopped to the door. “I am proud of me,” he said. “I can learn. I can wait. I can care.”

“Yes,” Miss Wattle said. “Your music starts inside you.”

Joey smiled. His tail swished. “I am enough,” he whispered. “And I will come back tomorrow.”

The End.
