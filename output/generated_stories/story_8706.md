# Pico the Pantry Parrot

Tap-tap-tap! Rain pitter-pattered on the kitchen window. In the cozy pantry, it was warm and bright. Little glass jars lined the shelves like shiny buttons. Bags of rice crinkled softly. A basket of onions smelled sweet and earthy. The floor was cool and smooth, and the light hummed with a gentle buzz.

Pico the parrot tilted his green head. His bright feathers glittered like tiny leaves. “Squawk! Pantry Parade!” Pico chirped. His voice echoed softly. He loved ideas. He loved colors. He loved making plans.

Today, Pico had a big plan. “I can make a Snack Station,” he said to himself. “I can do it. I can try. I can learn.” His chest felt warm and proud. Self-reliance fluttered in his heart like a small, brave wing.

On the bottom shelf sat a rainbow of helpers. There was a yellow bowl of crackers, a blue basket of apples, a red jar of oats, and a green tin of tea. Next to them, a tiny scoop and a stack of clean napkins waited.

Pico bobbed from toe to toe. “First, safety,” he said. “Safe beaks, safe feet.” He looked around. A tall glass jar wobbled near the edge. “Uh-oh.” Pico gently nudged it back to the middle. “Safe and steady,” he whispered. He checked the floor. A small spill of flour made a white moon on the tiles. “Slippy!” Pico fetched a cloth with his beak, dab-dab-dab, and wiped it up. “Better.”

Chirp-chirp! A little cricket the color of tea leaves peeped from behind a bag of beans. “Hello, Pico,” the cricket said in a soft, squeaky voice. “I am Pip.”

“Hello, Pip!” Pico fluffed his feathers. “I am making a Snack Station. Will you watch while I work? I want to try it myself.”

“I can cheer for you,” Pip said. “Chirp! You can do it!”

Pico looked at the shelves. “I will sort. I will stack. I will save.” He tapped his beak, thinking. “Sustainability means we care for our world. We use what we have. We do not waste.”

He picked up a basket of apples one by one. “Red, green, red, green,” he sang. He checked for soft spots. One apple had a little bruise. “It is still good,” Pico said. “I can cook this one later. No waste.” He placed it in a “Cook Soon” bowl.

Next, he fluffed open a small cloth bag. “Scoot, oats,” he said, pouring oats from the crinkly package into a glass jar with a lid. The oats whispered shhh-shhh as they slid down. “Glass can be used again and again,” Pico told Pip. “Good for our Earth.”

He made neat labels with big letters. OATS. RICE. BEANS. He stuck them on the jars. “Now everyone can read the names,” he chirped. “That helps our diverse friends. Some friends read, some like pictures.” He drew a tiny oat, a rice grain, and a bean next to each word. “Words and pictures help many ways of learning.”

Pip clapped tiny legs. “Chirp-chirp! Clever, Pico!”

Pico set a small plate at beak level. He placed a spoon, a napkin, and a little cup. “Snack Station is ready.” He stood back. His heart beat fast. “I did it myself,” he breathed. “I planned. I worked. I checked for safety. I cared for the Earth. I used labels so everyone feels welcome.”

Just then, two pantry friends peeked in. Miko the mouse wore a bright blue scarf. Suri the snail had a twirly shell with gold flecks. “Hello, Pico,” said Miko. “It smells like apples.”

“It looks so tidy,” said Suri. Her shell gleamed in the light. “May we try your Snack Station?”

“Yes!” Pico nodded. “But first, we wash.” He pointed to a little bowl of water and a clean towel. “Clean hands and clean paws keep us safe.”

Miko washed paws. Suri touched the water with her soft feelers. Pip watched and hummed a tiny tune.

Pico showed them how to scoop oats onto the plate and add apple slices. “Just a little,” he said. “We can always come back for more. That stops waste!”

They crunched. Crunch-crunch! The apples were juicy and sweet. The oats tasted nutty and warm. The napkins felt soft and smelled like sunshine.

“Mmm,” said Miko. “Pico, you made this all by yourself?”

Pico puffed up with pride. “Yes. I asked for no help until I tried. I made a plan. I was careful. I cleaned up. I used jars we can wash and use again. I made labels for everyone. I did it!”

Suri smiled. “Your plan helps all of us. Different friends, one pantry. We can share and be safe.”

Pip chirped, “And we can keep the Earth happy, too!”

Pico looked around the pantry. It gleamed and glowed. The jars were lined up like a little parade. The labels smiled with words and pictures. The floor was clean and dry. The Snack Station stood ready again.

He took a deep breath. He smelled apples, oats, and a tiny hint of tea. “I can make good things,” he said softly. “When I try, I learn. When I care, I help. I can do many things by myself.”

Outside, the rain slowed to a gentle drip. Inside, Pico’s heart felt like a bright, warm lantern.

Pico flapped his wings and called, “Pantry Parade complete!”

All his friends cheered.

The End.
