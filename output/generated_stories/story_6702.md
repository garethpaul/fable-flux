# Kiki Joey and the Calm-Down Colors

Kiki was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a springy tail. She loved to boing, boing, boing. One sunny morning, Kiki hopped into the garage workshop. It smelled like fresh wood and lemon soap. Sunbeams made tiny dust sparkles dance in the air.

On the wall hung brushes, big and small. Jars of paint lined a shelf—blue like the sky, yellow like sunshine, red like a ripe tomato, and green like the grass outside. A big piece of cardboard sat on a low table beside a box of tape, paper shapes, and buttons.

Kiki clapped her little paws. "I’m going to make art all by myself!" she said. "I want to surprise Mom and go outside to show the picture to the birds."

She picked up a small brush. The bristles felt tickly. "Hello, brush," Kiki giggled. "Let’s paint!"

She dipped into blue paint. Drip! A drop fell on her nose. "Cold and squishy," she said, scrunching her face. She laughed and dabbed blue swirls on the cardboard. Swish, swish.

Kiki tried yellow next. The smell was a little sweet. She made big, happy suns. Then she reached for red. Oh no—the red jar was tight. She twisted. Twist, twist, twist. Her paws slipped. Plop! The jar knocked over.

Red paint rushed like a tiny river across the table. It splashed onto the floor with a splat. Kiki’s heart thumped. "Oh no, oh no!" she cried. "My art is ruined! I want to finish fast. I want it perfect now!"

She felt hot in her cheeks. Her tail twitched. Her tummy felt tight, like a knot. Kiki hopped in a circle. "I can’t, I can’t!" she said, almost in tears.

Then Kiki remembered something her grandma taught her in the garden. "When feelings get big," Grandma had said, "stop, sniff a flower, blow a leaf. You can calm your body."

Kiki stopped. She put her paws on her belly. "Smell a flower," she whispered. She breathed in slowly through her nose. Sniff... "Blow a leaf," she said. She breathed out softly through her mouth. Whooo. Her shoulders relaxed. Her tail stopped twitching.

"Again," she told herself. Sniff... whooo. Sniff... whooo.

Kiki looked at the red puddle. It was still messy, but her tummy felt softer, like a cushion. "I can fix this," she said. "I can try one step at a time."

She scanned the workshop. She noticed a roll of paper towels and a small bucket by the sink. She also saw a sign that Mom had made: "Think. Plan. Try." Kiki read it out loud, tapping each word with her paw. "Think. Plan. Try."

"Think," Kiki said. "What do I know?" She pointed to the paper towels. "Paper towels soak up spills." She nodded, feeling proud.

"Plan," she said next. "I will clean the spill, then use the red to make something new." Her ears perked up. "Maybe I can make red flowers or a big red sunset."

"Try," she whispered. She tore a few paper towels. Squish, dab, dab. The towels slurped up the red paint. She wiped the floor with slow, careful circles. She washed her paws in the sink, the water cool and splashy, and dried them on a clean cloth. She took a deep breath. Sniff... whooo.

Kiki looked at her cardboard. The red spill had left a big red shape on one side. It looked like a fuzzy cloud. "Hello, red cloud," Kiki giggled. "Maybe you can be a big red tree!"

She picked the wide brush. Swish-swish. She added a brown trunk with a smooth, warm color. Tap-tap. She pressed green paper leaves on top. Crinkle, crinkle. She glued on shiny buttons for apples. Click, click.

Soon the picture showed a sunny sky, a tall red tree, and a blue river that sparkled with tiny white dots. Kiki hummed as she worked. Her body felt calm and steady. She liked going slow. She liked choosing each color. "I can do this," she told herself. "I am the boss of my body."

When she finished, she stepped back. "Ta-da!" she said. The art was bright and bold. The room smelled like paint and soap and a little like apples from the buttons. Kiki smiled so big her cheeks felt round.

She opened the garage door. Outside, the air felt cool and soft. Birds chirped, "Cheep-cheep!" Kiki carried her art to the porch and leaned it carefully against the step. Sunshine kissed the colors. Her tail made a happy thump, thump on the wood.

Mom came over, wiping her hands. "Oh, Kiki! What a beautiful picture," Mom said. "Tell me about it."

Kiki stood tall. "It was hard," she said honestly. "The red spilled. My tummy got tight. I wanted to cry. But I stopped and did sniff... whooo. I used Think. Plan. Try. I cleaned up. I made a red tree!"

Mom hugged her gently. "That’s wonderful self-regulation," Mom said. "You calmed your body and made a good plan. You fixed the problem all by yourself."

Kiki’s eyes sparkled. "I can take care of big feelings," she said proudly. "And I can make art, too."

"Yes," Mom nodded, "and now you can show your picture to the birds."

Kiki turned to the chirping sound. "Hello, birds," she called. "Look at my art!" The breeze ruffled her fur. She breathed in the fresh, leafy air. Sniff... and she smiled as she breathed out. Whooo.

The birds sang back, and Kiki felt calm and strong, inside and out.

The End.
