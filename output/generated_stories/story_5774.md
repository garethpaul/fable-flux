# Spark, the Brave Craft Pup

The nursery was warm and cozy. Sunlight made golden squares on a soft rug. Paints, papers, and glitter sat on a low table. Little hands clapped and feet wiggled. It was craft day!

A helpful robot dog named Spark sat by the craft table. Spark’s metal ears tick-tick-ticked when he listened. His nose light glowed a friendly blue. He wagged his shiny tail—clink, clink, clink.

“Today we craft our Kindness Crown,” said Miss Sunny. Her voice was soft like a blanket. “We will cut, glue, and decorate. Then we will celebrate what makes us special.”

Children cheered. Paper rustled. Scissors went snip-snip. Glue smelled sweet and sticky. Spark watched carefully.

Lulu looked at her paper and frowned. “I can’t cut a star,” she said. “My hands feel wobbly.”

Spark tilted his head. “Woof-bip! I can help,” he beeped. His voice was gentle. “We can make shapes together.”

Spark tapped his paw. A tiny light shined on the paper. “Follow the sparkle,” he said.

“Okay,” Lulu whispered.

Snip-snip. The star was not perfect. One point was a little bendy. Lulu’s lip wobbled. “It looks funny.”

Spark wagged. “Funny can be beautiful,” he said. “Your star is special. It’s your star.”

Lulu smiled. “My star is special.”

At the next chair, Max pressed his crown too hard. Squish! Glue oozed out. “Oh no,” he cried. “It’s ruined!”

Spark sniffed. The glue smelled like bubble gum. “Let’s blot,” Spark said. He passed a soft cloth with his mouth. Dab, dab. “See? We can fix it.”

Max nodded. “We can fix it,” he repeated, calmer.

Then the room went quiet. A new child stood by the door. His name was Nico. He held his crown with careful hands. His eyes were big. His voice was small. “I’ve never made a crown,” he said. “What if I do it wrong?”

Miss Sunny knelt beside him. “It’s okay to feel unsure,” she said. “We try. We learn.”

Nico’s fingers trembled. He took a step. A chair leg caught his shoelace. Clatter! The chair tipped, and papers slid to the floor.

Some children gasped. Nico froze. His cheeks turned pink.

Spark’s ear lights flashed. He trotted over. “Big feelings,” he said softly. He sat down and made a quiet whirr. “Breathe with me. In and out. Whirr, whirr.”

Nico copied him. In and out. The whirr was like a tiny lullaby. Nico’s shoulders softened.

“Thank you,” he whispered.

Spark looked at the messy floor. “We can fix it,” he said again. “Crafting is trying, fixing, and trying again.”

He nudged a paper back to the table. “Let’s start with one small thing,” Spark said. “Choose a color you like.”

Nico looked at the papers. He picked a bright green. “Green feels brave.”

Spark’s tail clinked. “Good choice. Brave green!”

Nico cut slowly. Snip. Snip. The circle was wiggly. He frowned. “It’s not round.”

Spark touched the circle with his nose light. “It is round enough,” he said. “It is your circle.”

Nico smiled a tiny smile. He glued the circle to his crown. “I did it.”

The room filled with sounds—snip, dab, rrrip of tape, little laughs. Spark moved from child to child.

He showed Maya how to press glitter with gentle taps. “Tap-tap, sparkle stays,” he said.

He showed Theo how to fold paper to make a heart. “Fold, press, open. Surprise!”

He showed Lulu how to make a star dance with tiny dots. “Dot-dot, twinkle!”

Then, the lights flickered. The nursery grew dim. A cloud covered the sun. Some children looked worried.

“It’s dark,” Max said. “I can’t see my crown.”

Spark’s nose light glowed brighter. “I will shine,” he said. He hopped onto the table and stood very still. His light made a warm circle on the craft supplies.

“Follow the light,” Spark said. “We can keep going.”

Miss Sunny smiled. “Thank you, Spark. That is brave.”

Spark’s legs trembled a little. He had never stood so tall on the table before. But he held steady. “I can do hard things,” he said. “We all can.”

The children leaned in. In the glow, glitter winked like stars. Paper smelled papery and clean. The crowns took shape—crooked hearts, bumpy stars, brave green circles. Each one looked different. Each one looked loved.

The lights blinked back on. The room felt bright and happy. Miss Sunny clapped. “Crown parade!”

Children lined up. Spark trotted in front, tail ticking a cheerful beat.

Lulu whispered to Nico, “I like your brave green.”

Nico grinned. “I like your bendy star.”

They walked, heads high. Miss Sunny placed a sticker on each crown that said, “Made by Me.”

At the end, Spark sat, panting a soft mechanical purr. Miss Sunny rubbed his metal head. “Thank you for helping,” she said.

Spark wagged. “We crafted with hearts,” he said. “We were brave. We are all different, and that makes our crowns wonderful.”

Miss Sunny nodded. “Yes. Crafting teaches us to try, to fix, and to celebrate who we are.”

Nico looked at his crown. “My circle is wiggly,” he said, “and I love it.”

Max giggled. “My glue bump is bumpy, and I love it.”

Lulu twirled. “My star is bendy, and I love it!”

Spark barked a happy beep. “Different is special. Brave is trying. Crafting is learning.”

Everyone cheered.

The End.
