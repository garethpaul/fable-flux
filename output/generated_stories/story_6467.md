# Tortoise's Special Art Day

Old Tortoise slowly walked into the bright, colorful art room. His shell gleamed green and brown in the morning sunlight streaming through the big windows. Today was a very special day - he was going to teach the little forest animals how to make beautiful leaf paintings, just like his grandmother had taught him long, long ago.

"Oh my!" said Tortoise, looking around the messy art room. Paint brushes were scattered everywhere. Paper was crumpled on the floor. The paint pots were empty and dirty. "This won't do at all. I must prepare everything properly first."

Tortoise remembered his wise grandmother's words: "Little one, good preparation makes everything better. When we get ready carefully, our work turns out beautifully."

First, Tortoise slowly picked up all the paintbrushes. He washed each one with warm, soapy water until they were clean and fluffy. "Swish, swish," went the brushes in the bubbly water. The room began to smell fresh and clean.

Next, he organized all the paint pots by color - red like apples, yellow like sunshine, blue like the sky, and green like grass. He filled each pot with fresh, thick paint that looked good enough to eat.

"Now for the paper," said Tortoise thoughtfully. He arranged clean white sheets on each little table, making sure every spot was ready for a young artist.

Finally, Tortoise went outside and carefully collected the most beautiful leaves he could find - big maple leaves, tiny oak leaves, and long willow leaves. He arranged them in baskets, each one perfect for painting.

Just as Tortoise finished preparing, little Rabbit hopped in. "Wow, Tortoise! Everything looks so pretty and organized!"

"That's because I prepared everything first," smiled Tortoise. "My grandmother taught me that good preparation helps us do our best work."

Soon, Squirrel, Fox, and Hedgehog arrived too. Their eyes grew wide with wonder at the perfectly prepared art room.

"Today, I'll teach you something special my grandmother taught me," said Tortoise warmly. "We're going to make leaf paintings by dipping real leaves in paint and pressing them on paper."

Fox picked up a red maple leaf. "Like this, Tortoise?"

"First, let's practice the steps together," said Tortoise patiently. "Hold your leaf by the stem. Dip it gently in the paint - not too much! Then press it carefully on your paper and lift it straight up."

Because everything was so well prepared, the little animals could focus on creating beautiful art. Their leaf prints came out perfectly - red maples, golden oaks, and green willows dancing across their papers.

"Look at my tree!" squeaked Squirrel, pressing leaf after leaf to make a colorful forest scene.

"Mine looks like a rainbow!" giggled Rabbit, using leaves of every color.

As they worked, Tortoise shared stories about his grandmother and how she had taught him this special art when he was very young. The room filled with laughter, creativity, and the sweet smell of paint.

When art time was over, the little animals looked at their beautiful creations with pride.

"Thank you for teaching us, Tortoise!" said Hedgehog. "And thank you for making everything so nice and ready for us."

Tortoise smiled his wise, gentle smile. "Remember, little ones - when we prepare well, everything works better. And sharing what we know with friends makes it even more special."

The little animals carefully cleaned up together, preparing the art room for the next day's adventures.

The End.
