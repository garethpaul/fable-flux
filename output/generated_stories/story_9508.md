# Bubbles in the Garden Shed

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash, giggle, and make tiny bubble rings. One sunny day, Bubbles visited a little garden. At the edge of the garden stood a small wooden shed. It smelled like fresh soil and sweet flowers. The door creaked open, “Creeeak,” and warm light shone on shiny tools and colorful pots.

“Hello?” Bubbles called, his voice soft and bubbly. “Is anyone here?”

From a corner, a mouse peeked out. “I’m Miri,” squeaked the mouse. “This is our garden shed. We keep seeds and tools here.”

A fluffy kitten named Kip padded over, paws silent on the wooden floor. “Hi, Bubbles! We’re getting ready for the Garden Sharing Day.”

A green frog hopped in with a plop. “Ribbit! I’m Finn. We’re making gifts for everyone.”

Bubbles’ flippers wiggled. “I want to help!” he said. He smiled so big his eyes sparkled like water in sunshine.

Miri held up a seed packet. “We make seed packets, flower chains, and little hats,” she said. The shed smelled like mint leaves and earthy pots. Ribbons rustled. Paper crinkled. A gentle breeze made wind chimes sing, “Ting-a-ling.”

Bubbles tried to pick up a tiny seed with his smooth flipper. “Oops!” The seed slid away like a raindrop. He tried again. Slip! Slide! The seeds scattered like sprinkles.

Kip tilted his head. “It’s okay,” he purred, but Bubbles felt his cheeks grow warm. “I want to help. I really do,” Bubbles whispered.

Finn pointed to the flower chains. “Maybe try the ribbons?” Bubbles tapped a ribbon with his nose. The ribbon tickled him. “Hee-hee!” he giggled, but the ribbon would not tie.

Miri stood on her tiptoes and patted Bubbles’ fin. “You are trying so hard,” she said kindly.

Bubbles’ happy bubbles slowed. He looked down. “I’m different,” he said softly. “I don’t have little paws or tiny fingers. I feel sad.”

The shed was quiet for a moment. The sun made a warm square on the floor. Kip sat beside Bubbles. “It’s okay to feel sad,” he said. “Feelings can come and go, like clouds.”

Finn nodded. “What can a dolphin do that we can’t?” he asked, eyes bright.

Bubbles blinked. He thought about the sea. He thought about swish, splash, and song. He thought about his big, gentle tail.

“I can splash,” Bubbles said. “I can sing. I can blow bubbles. I can carry big things with my nose.”

Miri’s whiskers twitched. “We need water for the seeds!” she squeaked. “The watering can is so heavy.”

Bubbles smiled. “I can help with that!” He nudged the metal watering can. It was cold and shiny. He balanced it on his nose like a circus star. “Ta-da!”

Kip clapped his paws. “Amazing!”

Bubbles gently tipped the can. Water trickled out: trickle, trickle. The soil smelled rich and happy. The seeds drank with quiet sips.

Finn pointed to a wobbly stack of pots. “We need those over there.”

With careful swishes, Bubbles moved the pots, one by one. Clink, clink. He set them in a neat row. He hummed a soft dolphin song. The room felt full of warm music.

Miri held up a little hat made of leaves. “We also need bubbles for the parade. Pretty bubbles that float and shine!”

Bubbles’ eyes twinkled. He took a breath and blew soft rings. Pop! Pop! Pop! The rings drifted through the sunbeams, catching tiny rainbows. The friends giggled and reached out their paws. The bubbles kissed their noses and went “poof!”

“You’re wonderful, Bubbles,” Kip said. “Your way is different, but it helps so much.”

Bubbles’ heart felt light. “My way is my way,” he said. “I like being me.”

As they worked, Bubbles noticed Miri’s small hands getting tired. He nudged a cushion over. “Here, rest your paws,” he said. That was generous and kind.

Finn rubbed his belly. “I’m hungry,” he croaked.

Bubbles rolled a basket of apples closer with his nose. “Share with me,” he said. The apples smelled sweet. Kip sliced them with a tiny claw, and they each took a crunchy bite. Crunch, munch, smile.

Soon the shed was full of gifts—seed packets stacked like little books, hats lined up like green crowns, and shiny bubbles floating like jewels. The friends stood together, proud and cheerful.

Kip looked at Bubbles. “How do you feel now?” he asked.

Bubbles listened to his feelings. He placed a fin on his chest. “I felt sad before,” he said. “Now I feel happy and proud. Thank you for helping me notice my feelings.”

“And thank you for helping us,” said Miri. “We all have different ways. Different is good.”

Finn added, “Different makes us strong.”

They opened the shed door. The garden glowed. Birds chirped. The air smelled like sunshine and grass. The friends carried their gifts outside. Bubbles followed, making a rainbow of bubbles that led the way.

At Garden Sharing Day, everyone clapped and cheered. “Hooray for the helpers!” they sang.

Bubbles bowed. “We did it together,” he said. “We used our different talents. We were kind. We shared. We listened to our feelings.”

Kip purred, “That’s the best kind of team.”

Bubbles made one last big bubble ring. It floated up, up, up, like a silver moon. Everyone watched with wide, happy eyes.

The End.
