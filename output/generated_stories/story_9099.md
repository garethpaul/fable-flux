# Bubbles Learns New Words at Dinner

The dining room was bright and warm. The long table smelled like lemon polish. Sunlight danced on the spoons, and the napkins felt soft like cotton clouds.

“Splish-splash!” giggled Bubbles, a playful dolphin who loved to hop and twirl. Bubbles didn’t live in the sea today. Bubbles was visiting friends for dinner, right in the dining room! A big bowl of fruit sat in the middle, colorful like a rainbow.

“Welcome, Bubbles,” said Mia, patting a chair. “We’re happy you’re here.”

Bubbles wiggled, flicked a fin, and made little clicking sounds. “Eee-eee! I’m happy too!”

Papa set down a plate. “Tonight we have crunchy carrots, sweet peas, and soft rice,” he said. “Good fuel for big play.”

Bubbles sniffed. The carrots smelled earthy and sweet. The peas were bright and green like tiny marbles. The rice looked like little white snowflakes.

Bubbles tried to copy Papa. “Crunchy… car-rots,” Bubbles said slowly, tapping the plate. “Sweet… peas. Soft… rice.”

Mia clapped. “Great words, Bubbles!”

Bubbles smiled and bobbed. But when Mama brought a little cup, Bubbles felt a flutter in their tummy. “What is that?” asked Bubbles, eyes wide.

“Herbal tea,” Mama said gently. “It’s warm and cozy.”

Bubbles peered at the cup. Steam curled up like tiny clouds. It smelled like mint and a little like lemons. Bubbles dipped a fin and squeaked, “Too hot?”

“Let it cool,” Mama said. She blew softly. “Warm now.”

Bubbles tried a sip. “Mmm,” Bubbles hummed. “Minty!”

Everyone began to eat. Crunch crunch went the carrots. Pop pop went the peas. The rice was soft and quiet. Bubbles loved the sounds. “Crunch! Pop! Soft!” Bubbles sang, tapping the table like a drum.

Mia laughed. “You made a dinner song!”

Bubbles grinned, then frowned a little. “I want to say more,” Bubbles whispered. “But I do not know all the words.” Bubbles felt a tiny pinch in their chest. Being playful was easy. Using new words was hard.

Mia nodded kindly. “It’s okay to learn,” she said. “We can help.” She pointed to the carrots. “These are crunchy.”

“Crunchy!” Bubbles repeated.

Papa pointed to the peas. “These are round.”

“Round!” Bubbles echoed, making a circle with a fin.

Mama pointed to the rice. “This rice is fluffy.”

“Fluf-fee,” Bubbles said, tasting the sound. “Fluffy!” Bubbles smiled. The pinch in their chest began to melt.

Mia held up a napkin. “This napkin is soft.”

“Soft,” Bubbles said.

Papa tapped his cup. “This tea is warm.”

“Warm,” Bubbles repeated, hugging the cup.

Mama smiled. “Words help us share what we feel and what we need.”

Bubbles nodded. “I want… more words,” Bubbles said. “More words help me be understood.”

Mia had an idea. “Let’s play the Word Tasting Game!”

Bubbles clapped. “Yes!”

Mia spoke first. “I taste sweet,” she said, nibbling a strawberry from the fruit bowl.

Bubbles tasted one too. “Sweet!” The juice dripped like tiny raindrops on Bubbles’ chin. It made Bubbles giggle.

Papa tried a carrot. “I taste crunchy.”

Bubbles crunched with him. “Crunchy!” It sounded like little sticks snapping.

Mama sipped tea. “I taste minty.”

Bubbles sipped. “Minty,” Bubbles repeated, feeling coolness dance on their tongue.

Mia licked her lips. “I feel happy.”

Bubbles placed a fin on their chest. “I feel… proud,” Bubbles said slowly. “Proud for learning.”

Papa nodded. “I feel calm.”

Bubbles closed their eyes. The warm tea, the soft rice, the calm voices—all felt safe. “Calm,” Bubbles whispered.

Then, a tiny oops. Bubbles knocked a pea, and it rolled across the table. “Oh no,” Bubbles said. “I feel… worried.”

Mia reached out and caught the pea. “I understand,” she said. “No big problem.” She placed the pea back on the plate. “You can say, ‘Please help.’”

Bubbles tried. “Please help.”

“Of course,” said Mia, smiling. “See? Words can ask for help.”

Bubbles beamed. “Words are like fins for my feelings,” Bubbles said. “They help me swim to you.”

Everyone nodded. “That’s beautiful,” Mama said.

Dinner went on with cheerful chatter. They talked about colors, shapes, and sizes. They named smells—lemony, minty, earthy—and sounds—crunchy, poppy, quiet. They took small bites and sips, listening to their tummies. When Bubbles felt full, Bubbles tried a new sentence. “I am full now,” Bubbles said gently.

“Great listening to your body,” Papa said. “That’s a healthy habit.”

Bubbles patted their tummy. “I listened. I learned. I used my words.”

Mia leaned closer. “What did we learn tonight?”

Bubbles sat tall, fins tucked neatly. “We learned to taste and name. We learned to listen to feelings and tummies. We learned to ask for help. Words help us understand each other.”

Mama nodded. “And sharing words can be fun.”

Bubbles twirled once in the chair, careful and slow. “I love words,” Bubbles said. “They are crunchy, soft, warm, sweet, and kind.”

Everyone laughed softly. The dining room felt cozy and bright. The spoons still shimmered, and the napkins were still soft. Bubbles felt calm, proud, and very understood.

“Thank you for dinner,” Bubbles said.

“Thank you for your words,” said Mia.

Bubbles smiled. “I will keep learning. One word at a time.”

The End.
