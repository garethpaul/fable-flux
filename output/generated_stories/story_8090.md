# Ellie's Colorful Dream

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved the bright, cheerful art room at her school. The walls were covered with rainbow paintings, and shelves held jars of sparkly paint in every color she could imagine. The room smelled like crayons and glue, and sunlight streamed through big windows, making everything glow.

But today, Ellie felt sad. She stood in front of a blank piece of paper, holding a paintbrush in her trunk. All around her, the other animals were making beautiful pictures.

"Look at my butterfly!" chirped Benny the bunny, showing off his purple and yellow painting.

"My flower is so pretty!" squeaked Mia the mouse, pointing to her pink and green creation.

Ellie looked at her empty paper and sighed. "I don't know what to paint," she whispered. "Everyone else is so good at art. I'm just not creative like them."

Mrs. Owl, their kind teacher, noticed Ellie's droopy ears and sad eyes. She waddled over with a gentle smile.

"What's wrong, dear Ellie?" Mrs. Owl asked softly.

"I want to make something beautiful, but I don't have any ideas," Ellie said, her voice wobbly. "I'm not good at being creative."

Mrs. Owl's eyes twinkled. "Oh, my dear, everyone is creative in their own special way! Let me ask you something. What makes you feel happy?"

Ellie thought for a moment. "I love helping my friends. And I love the way flowers smell in the garden. And... and I love making people smile!"

"Those are wonderful things!" Mrs. Owl said. "Now, what if you could paint something that shows all those happy feelings?"

Ellie's eyes grew wide. "But how do I paint feelings?"

"Close your eyes," Mrs. Owl suggested. "Think about helping your friends. What colors do you see?"

Ellie closed her eyes tight. "I see warm yellow, like sunshine! And soft blue, like a cozy blanket!"

"Perfect! Now think about those sweet-smelling flowers."

"Green like grass, and bright red like roses!" Ellie said, getting excited.

"And when you make someone smile?"

"Orange like a happy sunrise! And purple like my favorite toy!"

Ellie opened her eyes, and they sparkled with new ideas. She dipped her brush in yellow paint and made a big, warm circle in the middle of her paper. Then she added blue swirls around it.

"This is me helping my friend who was cold!" she explained to Mrs. Owl.

Next, she painted green stems with red flower tops. "These are the flowers that make everyone happy when they smell them!"

Finally, she added orange and purple dots all around her painting. "These are all the smiles I want to share!"

When she finished, Ellie stepped back to look at her creation. It wasn't like anyone else's painting, but it was uniquely hers. It told the story of her kind heart and generous spirit.

"Ellie, that's absolutely wonderful!" Mrs. Owl exclaimed. "You've painted your feelings and your caring heart. That's the most creative thing of all!"

Benny and Mia came over to see Ellie's painting. "Wow, Ellie! Your painting makes me feel so happy inside!" said Benny.

"It's like a warm hug in picture form!" added Mia.

Ellie's trunk curled up in a big smile. She realized that being creative wasn't about painting like everyone else. It was about sharing what was special inside her heart.

From that day on, Ellie never worried about not being creative enough. She learned that the best art comes from being yourself and sharing your own special gifts with the world.

The End.
