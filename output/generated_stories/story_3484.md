# Spark the Robot Dog’s Bubble Bounce

Spark was a helpful robot dog with shiny silver paws and a wagging metal tail that went tick-tick-tick. One sunny morning, Spark rolled into the bathroom. The tiles felt cool and smooth under his paws. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. The room smelled like fresh soap and minty toothpaste.

“Good morning!” Spark beeped happily. “Today we move, we learn, and we get ready!”

Spark’s little human friend, Mia, peeked in with sleepy eyes. “I don’t want to brush today,” she mumbled. “I’m too slow.”

Spark tilted his round head. His blue light blinked kindly. “When we move our bodies, they wake up,” he said. “Let’s try fun bathroom moves while we get ready. We can learn and prepare for our day!”

Mia smiled a tiny smile. “Okay. But only if it’s fun.”

“Fun it is!” said Spark. He tapped his paw on the mat. “Step one: Preparation. We gather what we need.” Spark opened a drawer with a soft whirr. Out came a tiny toothbrush, a cup, toothpaste, a towel, and a small stool.

Mia climbed onto the stool. “I’m ready!” she said.

“Step two: Movement,” Spark chirped. “Let’s do the Bubble Bounce!” He played a gentle beep-beep beat. “Bend knees, bounce soft, like a bubble.” Mia bent her knees and bounced up and down. Boing, boing. Spark bounced too, his springs whispering shoop-shoop.

Mia giggled. “I feel awake!”

“Now the Circle Arms,” said Spark. “Arms go round like the big mirror.” They swung their arms in slow circles. Whoosh, whoosh. The air felt cool on their skin.

Mia took a deep breath. “I smell soap,” she said. “And I hear drip, drip!”

“Excellent observations,” Spark said. “That is knowledge! We use our senses to learn.”

Spark handed Mia the toothpaste. “Step three: Brush Time. We move small and gentle.” He sang, “Little circles, round and round, clean teeth make happy sound.” Mia brushed in little circles. Scritch, scritch, scritch. Tiny minty bubbles foamed.

“Top teeth,” said Spark. “Bottom teeth. It’s like a dance for your mouth.”

Mia swished water in her cup. Swish, swish. “I did the brushing dance!”

Spark nodded. “Your smile is bright like the bathroom light.” He wagged his tail. Tick-tick-tick.

“Can we do more moves?” asked Mia.

“Yes! Let’s do Towel Twists,” said Spark. He held a soft towel. “Twist-twist, squeeze-squeeze. This helps our hands get strong.” Mia twisted carefully. The towel felt squishy and warm from the water. Drip, drip, drop.

“Now the Toe Taps,” said Spark. “Tap your toes on the bath mat.” Tap-tap-tap went Mia’s toes. Tap-tap-tap went Spark’s metal paws. The mat felt bumpy and cozy.

Mia laughed. “My feet are dancing!”

“Feet that move help bodies groove,” Spark rhymed. “Movement makes us ready.”

Mia pointed at the shelf. “What’s that?”

“That is the hair brush,” Spark said. “Knowledge time! A brush helps hair be smooth. We start at the bottom, then go up.” He showed with a gentle whirr. Mia brushed slowly. Swish, swish. Her hair made a soft whisper sound.

Spark checked the list on his screen. “Preparation check: Teeth brushed? Yes! Hands washed? Not yet!”

Mia turned on the water. Whoosh! It felt cool and slippery. “Soap, please,” she said. Spark squeezed a tiny bubble into her hands. “Rub, rub, rub,” he sang. “Palms and backs, between your fingers, round your thumbs.” The soap smelled like lemons. Bubbles popped with tiny snap sounds.

Mia rinsed. “Clean!” She shook her hands. Sprinkle-sprinkle-sprinkle.

“Last move,” Spark said. “Balance like a toothbrush.” He stood on one metal paw. Mia stood on one foot too. They wobbled and giggled. “Arms out wide,” Spark guided, “eyes on a spot.” Mia steadied. “I did it!” she cheered.

Spark’s screen glowed with stars. “You moved. You learned. You prepared. Now you are ready for the day.”

Mia looked in the mirror. Her eyes were bright. Her hair was smooth. Her teeth sparkled. “I feel strong,” she said. “I can move and do things!”

“Exactly,” Spark beeped. “Movement wakes our bodies. Knowledge helps us do it right. Preparation makes it easy.”

Mia patted Spark’s head. Clink. “Thank you, Spark.”

Spark’s tail wagged again. “Anytime. Want a goodbye stretch?”

“Yes!” said Mia. They reached up high to the ceiling. Stretch, stretch. They folded down to touch toes. Tickle, tickle. They rolled shoulders like tiny waves. Roll, roll.

Mia took one more deep breath. The bathroom smelled clean and bright. The warm towel felt soft on her hands. The quiet drip had stopped. She was calm and happy.

“I’m ready for breakfast now,” Mia said.

“Ready and steady,” Spark agreed. “Today, you will move with care, learn with joy, and be prepared.”

Mia nodded. “Tomorrow, can we do the Bubble Bounce again?”

“Of course,” Spark said. “Every day is a new little adventure.”

The End.
