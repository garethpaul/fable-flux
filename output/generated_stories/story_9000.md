# Dippy Dolphin’s Sunny Snack Parade

Dippy was a playful dolphin who loved to splash, laugh, and try new things. Today, Dippy was visiting a sunny backyard. The grass was warm and tickly. The sky was bright and blue. A gentle breeze smelled like fresh oranges and sweet tomatoes from the garden.

“Hello, backyard!” Dippy chirped, flipping her tail in a little kiddie pool. “I’m ready for a snack adventure!”

Dippy’s friends were there too: Benny the bunny, Miko the monkey, and Tia the tiny turtle. A long blanket was spread on the grass. On it sat empty baskets, colorful plates, and a circle of napkins.

Miko clapped his hands. “Let’s make a snack parade!”

Tia blinked happily. “We can use the garden. It’s full of crunch and color.”

Benny wiggled his nose. “And we can take turns. Taking turns feels fair and friendly.”

Dippy bobbed in the pool. “Let’s organize our snack! We can sort by colors.” She pointed her fin to a sign they made together: Red, Orange, Green, and Purple. Each color had a basket.

They walked—well, Dippy swam in her little pool-wheel—through the garden rows. Bees buzzed softly. Leaves brushed their fingers. The soil smelled rich and earthy.

Miko reached for a shiny red strawberry. “Red basket!” he cheered, placing it in.

“Wait,” said Tia gently. “I haven’t had a turn.”

Miko smiled and moved his hand back. “Your turn, Tia.”

Tia picked a plump tomato. “Red basket,” she whispered, her eyes twinkling.

Dippy wiggled closer and sniffed a carrot top. “Mmm! Orange smells sweet.” She carefully nudged carrots into the Orange basket. Benny hopped to snip a small bell pepper. “Crunch! Into Green,” he said, placing it neatly.

They kept going, taking turns. Apples went into Red. Oranges went into Orange. Cucumbers and lettuce went into Green. Plums and grapes went into Purple.

Dippy felt proud. “We are organized,” she said. “Sorting helps us see our choices.”

A little cloud drifted by. The wind ruffled the napkins. One napkin fluttered to Dippy’s nose. “Achoo!” Dippy giggled. “That tickled!”

When the baskets were full, they returned to the blanket. The colors looked bright like a rainbow. Dippy’s tummy made a funny sound. “Gurgle!” She laughed. “My tummy says it’s snack time!”

Benny lined up the plates. “Let’s make balanced snack plates. We can pick one food from each color to help us grow strong, think clear, and play longer.”

Miko tilted his head. “Do we have to try everything?”

Tia smiled softly. “We can try small bites. One bite tells a story.”

Dippy nodded. “Let’s be open-minded! New foods can be yummy surprises.”

They took turns building plates:

- Dippy chose a red strawberry, an orange carrot coin, green cucumber sticks, and a purple grape.
- Benny chose a red tomato slice, orange bell pepper strips, green lettuce leaves, and purple plum pieces.
- Miko chose a red apple chunk, an orange wedge, green peas, and purple grapes.
- Tia chose tiny bits of each, all in neat corners.

They added little scoops of hummus and a sprinkle of seeds for crunch. Dippy arranged the seeds like stars. “Snack art!” she giggled.

Before eating, they washed their hands with a little soap bucket by the hose. The water felt cool and bubbly. “Healthy habit time,” Benny said. They sang, “Wash, wash, wash, front and back,” as the bubbles popped like tiny balloons.

They sat in a circle. “Taking turns to taste!” Miko announced.

Dippy took a small bite of carrot. Crunch! Sweet and snappy. “This orange helps my eyes see the sky,” she said.

Benny bit the tomato. Juicy drops tickled his whiskers. “This red helps my heart,” he murmured.

Tia nibbled cucumber. Cool and fresh. “Green helps me stay calm,” she whispered.

Miko sniffed the orange slice. He looked unsure. “It smells strong.”

Dippy smiled gently. “One brave bite?”

Miko nodded and licked the juicy edge. His eyes lit up. “It’s sunshine! I like it!”

They all giggled. Dippy tried the grape. Pop! “Purple is powerful,” she said. “It helps my brain think.”

As they ate, they kept the blanket tidy. Peels went in a small compost pail. Plates stayed in a line. Baskets sat by their color signs.

“Staying organized makes it easy,” Tia said, stacking napkins.

A little neighbor kitten peeked through the fence and meowed. Dippy waved a fin. “Would you like to join?” The kitten meowed again, shy.

Benny placed a tiny plate by the fence. “We can share. We’ll take turns choosing kitten snacks too.”

Miko chose a soft banana bite. Tia added a pea. The kitten sniffed, then licked, then purred. Everyone smiled.

The sun warmed their backs. A bird sang. The backyard smelled like oranges and grass. Dippy leaned on the pool edge, feeling happy and full of good food.

“What did we learn?” Dippy asked.

Benny counted on his paws. “We learned to sort and stay neat.”

Tia added, “We learned healthy habits—washing hands and eating colors.”

Miko grinned. “We learned to take turns and try new tastes.”

Dippy flapped her tail. “And we learned that balanced snacks help our bodies and brains. We can be brave, organized, and kind.”

They packed up together: plates stacked, baskets back on the porch, compost pail by the garden. The blanket folded into a tidy square.

Dippy looked at her friends. “A sunny snack parade is the best,” she said. “Tomorrow, let’s try a new fruit!”

“Open minds, happy tummies,” they cheered.

The End.
