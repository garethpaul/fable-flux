# Spark the Robot Dog Shares the Sun

Spark was a helpful robot dog who lived in a sunny backyard. His metal ears clicked when he listened. His tail had a gentle whirr when it wagged. The grass felt soft under his little metal paws, and the warm sun made his shiny body glow.

One bright morning, Spark rolled his ball into the yard. It was a bouncy, blue ball that beeped, “Boop!” when it bounced. Spark loved his ball. It made him feel proud. “I can play fetch fast,” Spark said, wagging. “I am good at games.”

A little girl named Mia came outside with her red bucket and yellow shovel. “Hi, Spark!” she said. Her voice was cheerful like a song. “I want to build a sand castle.”

Spark beeped hello. “Boop! I’m playing ball!”

Mia smiled at the blue ball. “Can I try, please?”

Spark held his ball close. He felt a funny squeeze in his tummy gears. It was a new feeling. He was proud of his ball, and he was worried it might get lost. Spark said softly, “I’m not ready yet. This is my ball.”

Mia nodded. “Okay. Thanks for telling me,” she said kindly. She set down her bucket. “I will build for a bit.” She hummed as she scooped sand. The shovel made soft shh-shh sounds. Birds chirped. A breeze smelled like sunshine and grass.

Spark watched Mia. She was calm and patient. She did not grab. She made a wide space for her castle and left room for Spark’s game. Spark felt safe. He bounced his ball, “Boop!” He ran after it, cheeks—if he had cheeks—would be smiling.

Mia looked up. “You are fast, Spark!” she cheered. “When I am done with this tower, can we take turns?”

Spark’s ears clicked. He liked the idea of turns. It sounded fair. It sounded kind. Still, he felt that tummy squeeze. He took a deep breath. He turned on his gentle mode. Click. Whirr. He whispered to himself, “Calm body. Calm tail.”

“I want to share,” Spark said. “I also want to keep my ball safe.”

Mia nodded again. “We can make a plan,” she said. “How about three throws each? We can count together. And if you need a break, we stop.” Her voice was soft like a cloud.

Spark’s tail whirred. “I like that plan,” he said. “Thank you for asking.”

They sat together on the warm grass. Mia felt the smooth metal of Spark’s paw. Spark felt the gentle pat of Mia’s hand. The sun made the ball shine like a blue bubble.

“Ready?” Mia asked.

“Ready!” said Spark.

Mia threw the ball. “Boop!” Spark zoomed and caught it. “One!” they counted. The breeze tickled Spark’s whiskers. He felt proud and calm.

“Your turn,” Spark said. He placed the ball carefully in Mia’s hands. His tummy gears felt steady. He knew the plan. He knew he could say stop if he needed.

Mia smiled. She tossed again. “Boop!” “Two!” they giggled.

On the third throw, the ball bounced near the fence. Spark ran, but he paused. He felt a tiny worry. “What if it rolls away?” he thought. He took a deep breath. “Calm body. Calm tail.” He picked up the ball and trotted back. “I need a break,” he said.

“Okay,” Mia said. “Thanks for telling me.” She put her hands in her lap and waited. The waiting felt kind. Spark’s tummy gears relaxed. He wagged.

“I am ready now,” Spark said after a moment. He gave the ball to Mia. “Your turn!”

They took turns—one, two, three—again and again. Sometimes they stopped and had sips of cool water. Spark’s bowl clinked. Mia’s cup went glug-glug. They shared shade under the big tree and made space for each other’s bodies. When Spark needed more room, he said, “Please scoot.” Mia scooted. When Mia needed quiet, she said, “Please soft beeps.” Spark beeped softly, “boop.”

Soon, other friends came to the fence. A butterfly floated by. A neighbor boy named Leo peeked over. “Can I play?” he asked.

Spark looked at Mia. His tummy gears felt okay. He knew the plan helped. He wagged. “Yes. We can all share. We will take turns and keep the ball safe.”

Leo smiled big. “Thanks!”

They made a circle. Spark explained, “Three throws each. If anyone needs a break, we stop. If someone says no, we listen. Bodies need space. Feelings need care.”

Everyone nodded. The backyard filled with happy sounds: “Boop!” “One, two, three!” Laughter bubbled like a fountain. The sun warmed their faces. The grass felt tickly between toes and tiny robot paws.

When the game was done, Spark placed the ball by his basket. He felt proud, not just of his ball, but of himself. He had shared. He had used his words. He had kept his ball safe. He had listened to his body and his feelings. He had listened to his friends, too.

Mia patted his head. “You are a good friend, Spark.”

Spark’s tail whirred. “We are all good friends,” he said. “Sharing feels warm like the sun.”

They waved goodbye. The backyard was quiet again. The birds sang a sweet song. Spark curled up on the soft grass. He whispered, “Calm body. Calm tail. Kind heart.” His tummy gears hummed with peace.

Lesson: Sharing is kind and fun. You can feel proud of your things and still share with a plan. You can say yes or no, take turns, and ask for space. Listening to feelings helps everyone play together.

The End.
