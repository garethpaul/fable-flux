# Nutkin's Big Fix

Nutkin the squirrel loved living in the warm, cozy bedroom with soft blue walls and fluffy white curtains. The room smelled like lavender and sunshine, and it belonged to a little girl named Emma who always left out tiny treats for him.

One morning, Nutkin was feeling extra playful. He scampered across Emma's wooden desk, his tiny claws making soft tapping sounds. "What fun things can I explore today?" he chattered to himself.

On the desk sat Emma's favorite music box – a beautiful pink ballerina that twirled when you wound it up. Nutkin had watched Emma play with it every day, her eyes sparkling with joy as the tiny dancer spun around.

"I wonder what makes it work," Nutkin thought, his bushy tail twitching with curiosity.

He climbed onto the music box and began exploring with his little paws. Click, click, click went his claws as he poked and prodded. Suddenly – CRACK! The delicate ballerina snapped right off!

"Oh no!" squeaked Nutkin, his heart beating fast. "What have I done?"

The broken ballerina lay on the desk, and the music box was silent. Nutkin's whiskers drooped with worry. "Emma will be so sad when she sees this. I need to fix what I broke!"

But how could a small squirrel fix such a delicate toy? Nutkin sat very still, thinking hard. His bright black eyes looked around the room for ideas.

"I need a plan," he said to himself. "What can I use to help?"

First, he tried using tree sap from the windowsill plant to stick the ballerina back on. But his paws were too big, and the sap was too sticky. "This isn't working," he sighed.

Then Nutkin noticed Emma's craft supplies in a colorful box. Inside were tiny tubes of glue, perfect for small repairs! But the caps were too tight for his little paws to open.

"I need to think like a squirrel," Nutkin said, his tail fluffing up with determination.

He remembered how he opened tough nuts – by using tools! Nutkin found a bobby pin on the dresser. Using his teeth and paws, he carefully twisted it into a tiny tool. With great patience, he managed to open the glue tube.

Drop by tiny drop, Nutkin applied the glue to the broken ballerina. His paws shook a little, but he worked very carefully. The sweet scent of the glue mixed with the lavender smell of the room.

"Almost there," he whispered, gently pressing the pieces together.

When Emma came home from playing outside, she found something wonderful. Not only was her music box fixed, but there was also a small note made from torn paper with tiny paw prints on it. Nutkin had used a crayon to draw a picture of himself saying "Sorry!"

"Oh my!" Emma giggled, winding up her music box. The ballerina twirled perfectly, and the gentle melody filled the cozy room. "Thank you for fixing it, little friend!"

From his hiding spot behind the curtain, Nutkin's heart felt warm and happy. He had learned that when you make a mistake, the best thing to do is try your hardest to make it right.

That evening, Emma left an extra-special treat on her desk – a tiny acorn with a bow on it. Nutkin knew he was forgiven, and he promised to be more careful with Emma's special things.

The End.
