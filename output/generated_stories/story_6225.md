# Milo's Bathroom Adventure

Milo the monkey loved to swing and play all day long. His soft brown fur was always messy from climbing trees and doing somersaults. But there was one place that made Milo feel a little nervous - the bathroom.

"It's time to get clean, Milo!" called his mama from downstairs. "Your cousin Zara is coming to visit soon!"

Milo's tummy felt fluttery. He slowly climbed down from his favorite branch and walked to the bathroom. The white tiles felt cool under his little feet, and he could hear the gentle drip-drop of water from the faucet.

As Milo reached for his toothbrush, he heard a tiny voice say, "Excuse me!"

Milo looked around but didn't see anyone. "Hello?" he called softly.

"Down here!" squeaked the voice.

Milo looked down and saw a small spider hanging from a silky thread near the sink. The spider had eight tiny legs and was trembling a little.

"Oh my!" said Milo, jumping back. His first thought was to run away, but then he remembered what his mama always said about being brave and kind.

"I'm sorry if I scared you," said Milo gently. "I'm Milo. What's your name?"

"I'm Spinner," said the spider in her squeaky voice. "I live in the corner behind the towel. I was just trying to get a drink of water from the drip-drop."

Milo noticed that Spinner looked very thirsty. Her tiny body was moving slowly, and she seemed tired.

"Would you like me to help you?" asked Milo. "I could move the cup closer to you."

Spinner's eight eyes sparkled with surprise. "You... you want to help me? Most animals run away when they see me."

"That's not very nice," said Milo, shaking his head. "Everyone deserves kindness, no matter how they look or how many legs they have."

Milo carefully moved his water cup to the edge of the sink, making sure not to disturb Spinner's web. The little spider gratefully took a long drink.

"Thank you so much, Milo!" said Spinner. "You're very thoughtful."

"We're sharing this bathroom," said Milo with a smile. "We should take care of each other."

Just then, Milo heard footsteps. His cousin Zara appeared in the doorway, her golden fur shining.

"Who are you talking to, Milo?" asked Zara curiously.

"This is my friend Spinner," said Milo proudly. "She lives here too."

Zara looked nervous at first, but when she saw how gentle Milo was being, she smiled. "Hello, Spinner. I'm Zara."

"Hello, Zara!" squeaked Spinner happily.

The three friends took turns using the bathroom peacefully. Milo brushed his teeth while Spinner watched from her safe corner, and Zara washed her hands carefully so she wouldn't splash Spinner's web.

"This is much better than being alone," said Spinner. "Thank you for including me."

"Everyone belongs somewhere," said Milo, drying his face with a fluffy towel. "And everyone deserves respect and friendship."

As they left the bathroom together, Zara whispered to Milo, "You taught me something important today. Being different doesn't mean we can't be friends."

Milo felt proud and happy. He had been courageous, not by being loud or rough, but by being kind and respectful to someone who seemed different from him.

From that day on, the bathroom became a place of friendship, where Milo, Zara, and Spinner always looked out for each other with gentle hearts and caring actions.

The End.
