# Nutkin's Big Bathroom Problem

Nutkin the squirrel had a very important problem. He lived in a cozy tree house with his family, but there was only one bathroom for everyone to share. Every morning, it seemed like someone was always using it when Nutkin needed it most!

"This is not fair!" Nutkin chittered one Tuesday morning, hopping from paw to paw outside the bathroom door. He could hear the shower running and his sister Hazel singing inside.

"Hazel!" he called through the door. "I need to brush my teeth! I promised Mama I would brush them every morning!"

"I'm washing my fluffy tail!" Hazel called back. "It takes a long time to get all the soap bubbles out!"

Nutkin's whiskers twitched with frustration. He really wanted to keep his promise to Mama, but he also knew Hazel needed to get clean. He sat down on the soft bathroom rug and thought hard.

"Maybe," he said to himself, "there's a way we can both get what we need."

When Hazel opened the door, her tail was indeed very fluffy and clean. Water droplets sparkled on her fur like tiny diamonds.

"Hazel," Nutkin said honestly, "I really need to brush my teeth, but I can see you're not completely finished. What if we share the bathroom?"

Hazel tilted her head. "Share? How?"

"Well," Nutkin explained, "you could dry your tail with the big fluffy towel while I brush my teeth at the sink. We could both use the bathroom at the same time!"

Hazel's eyes lit up. "That's a great idea! But what about tomorrow? And the day after that?"

Nutkin's tail swished as he thought. "How about we make a schedule? You can have the bathroom first on Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays. I can have it first on Tuesdays, Thursdays, and Saturdays. On Sundays, we can share it together!"

"But what if one of us needs extra time?" Hazel asked.

"Then we can talk about it and figure out what's fair," Nutkin said. "We can always work together to solve problems."

Hazel smiled and nodded. "I like that plan! It's honest and fair for both of us."

They shook paws to seal their agreement. The bathroom smelled like Hazel's lavender soap, and the mirror was still a little steamy from her shower. Nutkin carefully brushed each tiny tooth while Hazel fluffed her tail with the soft yellow towel.

"This is much better than arguing," Hazel said, watching Nutkin rinse his mouth with the cool, fresh water.

"And much faster too!" Nutkin agreed, wiping his whiskers dry.

When Mama Squirrel came upstairs, she found both her children happy and ready for the day.

"How did you two solve your bathroom problem?" she asked, her eyes twinkling.

"We talked about it honestly and found a compromise!" Nutkin said proudly.

"We learned that when we work together and share, everyone can be happy," Hazel added.

Mama Squirrel gave them both big, warm hugs. "I'm so proud of you for using your words and being fair to each other. That's what good families do."

From that day on, Nutkin and Hazel never fought about the bathroom again. Whenever they had a problem, they remembered that talking honestly and finding ways to compromise made everything better for everyone.

The End.
