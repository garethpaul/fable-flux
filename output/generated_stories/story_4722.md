# Polly's Perfect Car Trip

Polly the parrot loved bright colors and making beautiful sounds. Her feathers were rainbow-colored - red, blue, green, and yellow! She lived with the Johnson family and was getting ready for her very first long car trip.

"Squawk! Where are we going?" Polly asked, tilting her colorful head.

"We're driving to Grandma's house," said Mom, buckling her seatbelt. "It's a three-hour trip!"

Polly flapped her wings excitedly. Three hours sounded like a very long time. She watched Dad put suitcases in the trunk and wondered what she should bring for such a long journey.

After just ten minutes in the car, Polly started feeling restless. The trees outside looked the same, and her perch felt uncomfortable. "Squawk! Are we there yet?" she called out.

"Not yet, Polly," Dad said gently from the front seat. "We still have a long way to go."

Polly tried looking out the window, but soon she felt bored. Her tummy rumbled, and she wished she had her favorite sunflower seeds. She saw little Emma playing quietly with a coloring book and wondered why she felt so fidgety.

"I'm bored! Squawk!" Polly said, ruffling her feathers.

Mom turned around with a kind smile. "Polly, would you like to know a secret about long trips?"

Polly's bright black eyes sparkled with curiosity. "Yes, please!"

"The secret is preparation," Mom explained. "Emma prepared for our trip by packing her coloring books, crayons, and snacks. When we prepare, we feel happy and comfortable."

"What's preparation?" Polly asked, tilting her head the other way.

"Preparation means getting ready ahead of time," Emma said sweetly. "Like packing things that make you happy!"

Polly thought about this. "But I didn't pack anything!"

Dad pulled over at a rest stop. "Let's help Polly prepare for the rest of our trip," he suggested.

The family gathered around Polly's travel cage. Mom brought out a small bag of sunflower seeds. "For when you're hungry," she said.

Emma gave Polly a tiny mirror. "So you can see your beautiful feathers!"

Dad added a small bell. "For making pretty music!"

Polly felt so much better! "Thank you for helping me prepare! Squawk!"

Back in the car, Polly munched her seeds when hungry, admired her colorful reflection in the mirror, and made gentle bell music. She even started noticing wonderful things outside - fluffy white clouds, red barns, and horses running in fields.

"Look at those horses!" Polly chirped excitedly. "They're brown and white!"

"Great observation, Polly!" Mom said proudly.

When they finally arrived at Grandma's house, Polly felt happy and calm instead of cranky and tired.

"How was your trip, beautiful bird?" Grandma asked, offering Polly a gentle finger to perch on.

"Wonderful! I learned about preparation!" Polly announced proudly. "When we prepare for something new, we feel ready and happy!"

Grandma smiled. "That's very wise, Polly. Preparation helps us enjoy our adventures."

That evening, as Polly settled into her cozy travel cage, she felt proud of herself. She had learned that asking for help politely and preparing ahead of time made everything more fun. Tomorrow, she would help Emma prepare for their trip back home!

"Good night, everyone," Polly whispered softly. "Thank you for teaching me about preparation!"

The End.
