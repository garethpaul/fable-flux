# Roo's Bubble-Time Promise

Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey. He loved bath time. The bathroom was warm and steamy. The mirror looked foggy like a soft white cloud. The tub was full of bubbly water that smelled like orange flowers.

“Splash, splash!” Roo giggled, kicking his feet. Tiny waves tapped the side of the tub. Pop! Pop! Pop! went the bubbles. Roo’s nose twitched. He could smell the soap—sweet and bright, like a fresh orange.

Mama Roo stood by the sink with a fluffy towel. “Keep the water in the tub, Roo,” she said kindly. “Water on the floor can be slippery. We must be careful and show respect for our home.”

“I will, Mama!” Roo chirped. He loved to listen to Mama. He also loved to make big splashes.

Roo looked at his rubber ducky, Sunny. “Ready, Sunny?” he whispered. “Jump!” Roo bounced Sunny high. Sunny landed with a big plop.

WHOOSH!

Water spilled over the tub. It ran across the floor in a shiny river. The bath mat went squish-squash under Mama’s feet.

Mama Roo stepped back. “Oh dear,” she said, eyes wide. “The floor is very wet.”

Roo’s tummy felt wiggly. He remembered Mama’s words. He wanted to be brave and honest, but his cheeks felt hot.

“I’m sorry, Mama,” Roo said in a small voice. “I made a big splash. I didn’t keep it in the tub.”

Mama Roo knelt down. She touched Roo’s wet ear with a gentle paw. “Thank you for telling the truth,” she said. “That is brave.”

Roo’s ears perked. “Brave?”

“Yes,” said Mama. “It is brave to say, ‘I made a mistake.’ It is respectful to our home and to me. Now we can make amends.”

Roo blinked. “Make amends?” he asked.

“It means we fix what we can,” Mama said. “We help, we say sorry, and we do better next time.”

Roo’s tail thumped the tub with a tiny thud. “I want to make amends,” he said. “I want to help!”

Mama smiled. “Thank you, Roo. Let’s start with the water.”

Roo took a deep breath. The bathroom smelled like warm towels and orange soap. He felt brave inside, like a little drum beat. Bum-bum. Bum-bum.

Mama handed him a small towel. “This one is for you.”

Roo dabbed at the floor. Pat, pat, pat. The towel got soggy. “I’m cleaning!” he said, feeling proud.

Mama mopped the bigger puddle. Swish, swish. “You are doing a careful job,” she said. “That shows respect.”

Roo looked at the bath mat. It looked like a sleepy wet cloud. “Sorry, bath mat,” he whispered, giving it a gentle squeeze. Drip, drip. “I will be gentle with water.”

When the floor was dry, Roo climbed out of the tub. Mama wrapped him in the fluffy towel. It felt warm like sunshine. Roo sighed happily. “I like making amends,” he said. “It feels good in my heart.”

Mama nodded. “Our hearts feel light when we fix our mistakes.”

Roo put Sunny back into the tub softly. “Sunny,” he said, “we will make small splashes. Little plips. Little plops.”

He showed Sunny with his paw. Plip. Plop. The water made soft sounds, like rain tapping a window. Roo smiled. “See? No big whoosh!”

Mama Roo watched. “That is smart and safe,” she said. “Thank you for being brave and respectful.”

Roo’s eyes shone. “Mama, can I do more to help?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Would you like to dry the foggy mirror?”

Roo nodded. He stood on a soft stool. He wiped the mirror in big slow circles. Squeak, squeak. His own face appeared—wet fur, bright eyes, a happy grin. “Hello, Roo!” he giggled to his reflection.

Mama chuckled. “Hello, Roo,” she said to the mirror too.

Roo looked around the bathroom. It felt calm and cozy again. The towels were hanging neat and straight. The floor was dry. The air smelled clean.

“I am sorry for the big splash,” Roo said again, looking up at Mama. “I will try to keep the water in the tub.”

“Thank you for your apology,” Mama said. “Thank you for helping. That is how we make amends. That is how we show respect.”

Roo stood tall on his big feet, even though he was small. “I was scared to tell you,” he said. “But I did it.”

“That is bravery,” Mama said, giving him a warm hug. The towel tickled his nose. He giggled.

Roo took Sunny in his paws. “We learned something,” he whispered to the duck. “When we make a mess, we can make it better.”

He looked at Mama. “Can we read a bath book now?”

“We can,” Mama said. “And after, we will hop to bed.”

Roo hopped once. Boing! The bathroom echoed softly. He felt light, like a bubble.

Roo smiled. “I made a mistake, I made amends, and I feel proud.”

Mama smiled back. “That is a big lesson for a little joey.”

The End.
