# Benny Beaver’s Big Idea

Benny was a busy beaver who loved his cozy home office. It was a sunny corner by a window. His desk was smooth wood, and his chair was soft and squeaky. He had jars of colorful crayons, stacks of paper, and a bright blue pencil with a tiny bell that jingled. His computer made a gentle hum, like a bee saying hello.

“Today, I will make a sign!” Benny said. “A big, beautiful sign that says, ‘Welcome Friends!’”

He clapped his little paws. Clap, clap! His tail thumped on the floor. Thump, thump! It sounded like a friendly drum.

Benny spread paper on the desk. He sniffed the crayons. They smelled a little waxy and a little sweet. He picked a red crayon and drew a big W. “W for Welcome!” he cheered.

But the crayon broke. Snap!

“Oh no,” Benny said, eyes wide. His tummy felt wiggly. “I want my sign to be perfect.”

Benny took a deep breath. In… out. He remembered what Mama Beaver always said: “When something breaks, try something new.”

“Okay,” Benny whispered. “I can fix this.” He peeled a little paper off the crayon and kept drawing. The crayon felt bumpy, but it worked. Scritch, scritch. He smiled. “I can do hard things.”

Next, he chose yellow for a sun. He colored in slow circles. Swish, swish. He added a happy face to the sun. “Hello, Sunny!” he said.

Ding! His tiny bell jingled as the pencil rolled. “Oops! Come back,” Benny giggled, catching it. He liked the cheerful sound. It made him feel brave and bright.

Benny drew trees with green tops and brown trunks. He added a little river, blue and shiny. He tapped the paper. “I love my sign,” he said. “But something is missing.”

He looked around his home office. On the shelf, he saw ribbon. It was soft and satiny. He saw stickers—stars, hearts, and tiny beavers. He saw a glue stick with a purple cap.

“I can make my sign special,” Benny said. “I will add ribbon borders.”

He put glue on the paper. Squish, squish. The glue was cool and a little sticky. He pressed the ribbon on the edges. The ribbon curled and wiggled.

“Oh!” he said. The ribbon popped up. Boing!

Benny’s shoulders drooped. His whiskers drooped too. “It won’t stay,” he said. “Maybe I can’t do it.”

He looked at the sign. The sun smiled. The river sparkled. The big W looked proud.

Benny put a paw on his heart. Thump, thump. “I can try a different way,” he said softly. “Trying is how I learn.”

He held the ribbon with one paw and smoothed it with the other. He counted slowly, “One… two… three…” The ribbon stayed. He clapped. “I did it!”

Benny added star stickers around the word “Welcome.” Peel, press. Peel, press. He hummed a little tune. Hum, hum, hummm.

He stepped back. “I like it,” he said. “But I want it to feel like me.”

Benny thought about himself. He liked building dams, nibbling twigs, and helping friends. He liked splashing in water. He liked music and dancing with his tail.

“I will draw me!” Benny said. He smiled and drew a friendly beaver with bright eyes and a big, strong tail. He gave his beaver a tiny bell on the pencil and a happy grin.

Tap, tap, tap went his tail as he added a blue wave. Splish! He imagined cool water on his fur. He added musical notes floating in the air. La, la, la!

Just then, a light breeze came through the window. Whoosh! The sign fluttered. The corner bent.

“Oh no!” Benny cried. “My sign!”

He gently pressed the corner flat. He shut the window a little to stop the whoosh. He found some washi tape with tiny pinecones. He taped the corner down. Smooth and steady.

“There,” he said. “I can solve problems. I can keep going.”

Benny read the sign out loud. “Welcome Friends!” His voice sounded warm and friendly. He felt warm inside, like a hug.

He put the sign on the wall in his home office. He stepped back and looked. The sun smiled. The river flowed. The ribbon borders shined. The beaver waved hello.

Knock, knock! It was his friend, Dot the Duck.

“Hi, Benny,” Dot said. “Your office smells like crayons and glue. It smells like art!”

“Hi, Dot,” Benny said, tail thumping happily. “Look at my sign.”

Dot’s eyes sparkled. “Oh! It’s beautiful. It looks like you made it your way.”

Benny’s cheeks felt rosy. “I did,” he said. “I tried new things. I fixed little problems. And I drew what I love.”

Dot flapped her wings. “That is creative expression,” she quacked. “You showed your ideas. You used your heart.”

Benny smiled big. “When I make art my way, I feel proud,” he said. “I don’t have to make it perfect. I can make it me.”

Dot nodded. “That is a great lesson.”

Benny pointed to the empty spot next to his sign. “Would you like to make one too?”

“Yes!” said Dot. “We can use our own ideas.”

They sat together at the desk. Scritch, swish, peel, press. They giggled. They hummed. They shared stickers and stories. Their home office felt cozy and bright.

Benny whispered, “Trying, fixing, and sharing make me strong.”

Dot whispered back, “Making art our way makes us happy.”

Benny’s heart felt full. He looked at his sign one more time and smiled. “Welcome, friends,” he said. “Welcome to our ideas.”

The End.
