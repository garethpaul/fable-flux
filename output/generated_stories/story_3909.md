# Bella Bear Cubs First Pancake

Bella was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. She stood on her tiptoes in the warm kitchen. The sunlight made golden squares on the floor. The air smelled sweet, like vanilla and cinnamon. Pans clinked softly. The fridge hummed a gentle tune.

“I want to make my own pancake,” Bella said, puffing out her small chest. “All by myself.”

Mama Bear smiled and tied a tiny apron around Bella. “I hear your brave voice,” Mama said. “You can try. I will be near. You can ask if you need help.”

Bella nodded. “Okay. I want to try first.”

On the counter sat a big bowl, a wooden spoon, milk, flour, eggs, and blueberries. The bowl was shiny and cool. The flour felt soft like snow. The milk made a glug-glug sound. The eggs were smooth and fragile, like little moons.

Bella read Mama’s picture recipe. Step one: pour the flour. Bella held the bag with both paws. Puff! Flour whooshed up and tickled her nose. “Achoo!” she sneezed. Flour sprinkled everywhere, like tiny stars.

Bella giggled. “Oops.”

“You are learning,” Mama said gently. “What is your next step?”

“Milk,” said Bella. She lifted the carton. It felt heavy. “I can do it.” She poured slowly. Drip. Drip. Splash! A little milk puddle formed on the counter and slid onto the floor. “Oh no,” said Bella, frowning.

Her cheeks felt warm. Her paws felt wobbly. “Maybe I can’t do it,” she whispered.

Mama passed a soft towel. “Let’s be fair to yourself,” Mama said. “You are new at this. New things take time. You can clean, you can try again.”

Bella took a deep breath. The towel was warm from the sun. She wiped the milk. “I can try again,” she said. “I want to do it.”

Step two: crack an egg. Tap. Tap. CRACK! A little shell fell into the bowl.

Bella looked at the shell. “Hmm,” she said. “I want to be open-minded. Maybe there is a new way.” She used a spoon to scoop the shell out. Plink! Out it came.

“Good idea,” Mama said. “You solved it.”

Bella added blueberries. Plop, plop, plop. The berries smelled sweet and a little tangy. She mixed the batter. Swish, swish. The spoon felt heavy, but Bella kept going. Her arms felt strong.

Now it was time for the hot pan. Sizzle! The sound was like tiny fireworks. Heat rose like warm breath on Bella’s face.

“I want to pour it,” Bella said.

Mama nodded. “I trust you to try. I am here if you need help.”

Bella held the ladle and poured a small round circle. Sizzle! The batter bubbled. A blueberry popped. Pop!

Bella watched closely. The edges turned golden. “When do I flip?” she asked.

“When you see bubbles pop and stay open,” Mama said. “But you decide when you feel ready.”

Bella waited. She felt her heart beat—thump, thump. She felt brave and careful. “Now,” she said. She slid the spatula under the pancake. Her paw shook a little. Flip! The pancake turned over and landed with a soft plop.

“I did it!” Bella cheered. Her smile felt big and warm, like the pan.

She made a second pancake and a third. The kitchen smelled cozy and sweet. The window let in a soft breeze. Birds chirped outside: tweet, tweet, tweet.

Soon, the plate was full of pancakes. Bella felt proud. She had flour on her nose and a blueberry spot on her paw.

Just then, her little friend, Pip the chipmunk, peeked in the door. “I smell pancakes!” Pip said, eyes wide.

“They’re mine,” Bella said. She hugged the plate.

Mama raised an eyebrow. “Let’s think about fairness,” she said softly. “You worked hard. You can choose. What feels fair?”

Bella looked at the stack. There were many pancakes. She remembered the milk spill and the egg shell and how Mama stayed near. She remembered Pip’s wiggly nose and kind eyes.

“I can share,” Bella said. “That is fair. I made them, and I can choose to share.” She put two pancakes on a small plate for Pip. “And I will keep two for me.”

Pip clapped. “Thank you!”

They sat at the table. The chairs were smooth and wooden. The syrup was sticky and sweet. Butter melted like tiny yellow puddles. They took bites. Mmm. Warm and fluffy. The blueberries burst with juice.

Pip said, “These are the best pancakes!”

Bella giggled. “I made them by myself. I asked for help when I needed it. I tried new ways. I was fair.”

Mama hugged Bella. “You were independent and kind. You kept an open mind. You found fair choices.”

Bella licked a bit of syrup from her paw. “I can do new things,” she said. “I can try, I can fix, and I can share.”

The warm kitchen glowed. The plate was almost empty. Bella felt full in her tummy and full in her heart.

The End.
