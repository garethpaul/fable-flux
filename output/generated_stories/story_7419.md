# Rascal's Sharing Song

Rascal the raccoon sat in the cozy study corner, his tiny paws holding his favorite red drum. The soft carpet felt warm under his striped tail. Sunlight streamed through the window, making his drum shine like a ruby.

"Tap-tap-tap!" went Rascal's drum. He loved making music more than anything.

"Can I play too?" asked Penny the rabbit, hopping over with hopeful eyes.

"No!" said Rascal, clutching his drum tighter. "It's MINE!"

Penny's ears drooped down. She sat quietly by the bookshelf, watching Rascal play alone.

Soon, Oliver the owl flew down from his perch. "That drum sounds wonderful! May I try?"

"NO!" Rascal said louder, turning away. "Go find your own drum!"

Oliver's big eyes looked sad. He fluffed his feathers and flew back to his corner.

Then came Benny the bear, waddling over with a big smile. "Wow! Can you teach me that beat?"

"ABSOLUTELY NOT!" Rascal shouted, hugging his drum so tight it couldn't make any sound. "This is MY special drum! Nobody else can touch it!"

Benny's smile faded. He shuffled away slowly.

Now Rascal sat all alone in the study corner. The room felt very quiet. Very empty. He looked around at his friends - Penny reading by herself, Oliver preening his feathers sadly, and Benny staring out the window.

Rascal tried to play his drum again. "Tap... tap..." But it didn't sound happy anymore. It sounded lonely.

His little heart felt heavy. The drum didn't feel special when no one else could enjoy it.

"Wait," Rascal whispered to himself. "Music is supposed to make everyone happy."

He thought about his grandmother's words: "Sharing joy makes it grow bigger, little one."

Rascal took a deep breath. He walked over to Penny first.

"Penny," he said softly, "I'm sorry I was mean. Would you like to try my drum?"

Penny's ears perked up. "Really?"

"Really," Rascal nodded. "But let's make a fair plan. We can each play for five minutes, then pass it to the next friend."

Penny's eyes sparkled. "That sounds perfect!"

They called Oliver and Benny over. Rascal explained the sharing plan.

"Five minutes each?" asked Oliver. "That's very fair!"

"And we can make music together!" added Benny, clapping his paws.

Rascal smiled wide. "Yes! Penny can play drum, Oliver can hoot the melody, Benny can clap the beat, and I can sing!"

They formed a circle in the study corner. Penny played gentle rhythms on the red drum. Oliver hooted beautiful notes. Benny clapped steady beats. And Rascal sang a happy song about friendship.

"This is the best music ever!" laughed Penny.

"Much better than playing alone," agreed Rascal.

The study corner filled with joyful sounds. Other animals heard the music and came to listen. Soon they had a whole audience!

When their concert ended, everyone cheered. Rascal felt warm and proud inside.

"Thank you for sharing," whispered Penny, giving Rascal a gentle hug.

"Thank you for being patient with me," Rascal replied. "I learned that sharing makes everything more fun!"

From that day on, the study corner became their special music place. They took turns with instruments, created songs together, and always made sure everyone felt included.

Rascal learned that being fair and kind made his heart feel as happy as his music sounded.

The End.
