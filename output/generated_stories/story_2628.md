# Tilly the Turtle Tries

Tilly was a shy turtle who lived with her family. One morning, Tilly peeked into the bright, bubbly bathroom. The mirror was shiny. The sink hummed with a soft drip, drip. The air smelled like minty soap.

“Today I will brush my teeth,” Tilly whispered. “I want a sparkly smile.”

Her big brother, Toby, was already at the sink. He turned the water on full blast. Ssssshhhhh! The water rushed like a little waterfall.

Tilly’s eyes grew wide. Loud water made her feel small. “Um… Toby?” she said in a tiny voice. “The water is loud. Can you turn it down?”

Toby did not hear her. He hummed a song and swished his toothbrush. Swoosh, swoosh, swoosh!

Tilly took a deep breath. She looked at the bath mat. It was soft and fluffy under her feet. She liked how it felt. She felt a little braver.

“Excuse me, Toby,” Tilly said, a bit louder. “I am shy. The water scares me. And we should save water for fish and flowers.”

Toby stopped and listened. He turned his head. “Oh! I didn’t hear you,” he said kindly. “Thank you for telling me.”

Mama Turtle came in with a towel that smelled like sunshine. “Good morning, my turtles,” she said. “What do I hear?”

Tilly looked at Mama. “The water is too loud for me. I want to brush. But I want to save water too.”

Mama nodded. “Those are good thoughts, Tilly. Thank you for using your brave voice. Toby, can we find a way that works for both of you?”

Toby scratched his shell. “Hmm,” he said. “I need water to rinse. But I can turn it off while I brush. Like this.” He turned the tap off. The bathroom got quiet. “I can also hum softer.”

Tilly smiled a tiny smile. “That helps,” she said. “But I still feel shy at the sink.”

Mama had an idea. She picked up a small cup with blue fish on it. The cup felt smooth and cool. “How about we fill this cup just a little?” said Mama. “We can use it to rinse. We will keep the water off in between. That will be gentle for Tilly. And we will save water for the earth.”

Toby nodded. “That is a good plan,” he said. He filled the cup halfway. Glug, glug. Then he turned the tap off. Click.

Tilly took her green toothbrush. It smelled like strawberries. “I will try,” she said. She put a tiny dot of toothpaste on the brush. “Pea-sized,” she said proudly.

Toby hummed a soft tune. Mmm-mmm-mmm. Tilly brushed in little circles. “One, two, three,” she counted. The bristles tickled her teeth. “It feels funny,” she giggled.

“Let’s practice together,” said Toby. “Top teeth. Bottom teeth. Chew, chew, chew. Now the tongue.”

Tilly brushed her tongue. “Bleh!” she laughed. The minty taste was cool like a snowflake.

After a moment, Tilly paused. “I need to rinse,” she said. Toby held the cup. “Your turn first,” he said.

Tilly sipped and swished. Swish, swish. She spit into the sink. “All done,” she said. “My mouth feels tingly and clean.”

“Now me,” Toby said. He brushed with the water off. When he needed to rinse, he used the cup too. “This works!” he said. “It is quiet and we save water.”

Mama smiled. “You listened to each other,” she said. “You made a compromise. That means you found a way to share and to care.”

Tilly looked at the sink. A tiny drip still went drip, drip. “Mama,” she asked, “can we fix the drip? I want to help fish and flowers.”

“Yes,” Mama said. She turned the knob and wiped the faucet with a cloth. “There. Better.”

Tilly felt warm inside, like a hug. “Thank you for listening, Toby,” she said.

“Thank you for telling me,” Toby said. “Your shy voice matters.”

The next day, Tilly came to the bathroom again. She took a deep breath and felt the soft mat under her toes. “Practice time,” she said.

Toby brought the little cup. “Compromise time,” he said with a grin.

They brushed together. The bathroom sounded calm: soft hums, little swishes, quiet giggles. The room smelled minty-fresh. The mirror showed two happy turtle smiles.

When they were done, Tilly turned to the light switch. “Click,” she said as she turned the light off when they left. “This helps the earth too.”

Mama gave them both a gentle pat on their shells. “I am proud of you,” she said. “You listened. You practiced. You cared for each other and for the earth.”

Tilly stood taller. “I can be shy and still be brave,” she said. “I can speak up. I can listen. We can find a way together.”

Toby nodded. “Together is best.”

Tilly’s smile sparkled. She felt calm, clean, and kind. And the bathroom felt like a friendly place.

The End.
