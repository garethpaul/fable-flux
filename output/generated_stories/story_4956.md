# Benny Beaver's Big Day Plan

Benny Beaver sat at his little wooden desk in his cozy home office. The morning sun streamed through the window, making his brown fur sparkle. He had a very important day ahead!

"Today I need to help my forest friends," Benny said to himself, tapping his big front teeth thoughtfully. "But how can I help everyone?"

Benny's friend Rosie Rabbit had asked him to fix her fence. Ollie Owl needed help organizing his library. And little Sammy Squirrel wanted to learn how to build a treehouse.

"Oh my!" Benny's tail slapped the floor with worry. "That's a lot to do. I might forget something important."

Benny looked around his office. He saw colorful pencils, a big calendar on the wall, and sheets of paper. Suddenly, his eyes lit up like tiny stars.

"I know! I'll make a plan!" Benny exclaimed happily.

He grabbed a bright blue pencil and drew three boxes on a piece of paper. In the first box, he drew a fence for Rosie. In the second box, he drew books for Ollie. In the third box, he drew a treehouse for Sammy.

"Morning, afternoon, evening," Benny said, pointing to each box. "If I plan my day, I can help everyone!"

But then Benny stopped. His whiskers twitched as he thought harder.

"Wait," he said softly. "What if my friends need different things? I should ask them first!"

Benny picked up his red telephone. Ring, ring!

"Hello, Rosie!" Benny said cheerfully. "What time would be best for fixing your fence?"

"Oh, Benny!" Rosie's voice sounded happy. "Morning would be perfect! That's when I have the most energy."

Next, Benny called Ollie Owl. "Hoot, hoot! Afternoon is ideal for me," Ollie said wisely. "I'm most awake then."

Finally, Benny called little Sammy. "Evening would be great!" Sammy squeaked excitedly. "After my nap!"

Benny felt warm and fuzzy inside. "I'm so glad I asked," he thought. "Now everyone will be happy!"

He updated his plan with the times his friends preferred. Then he packed his toolbox with a hammer, nails, and measuring tape. Everything smelled like fresh wood and metal.

Throughout the day, Benny followed his plan perfectly. In the morning, he helped Rosie fix her white picket fence. The hammer went "tap, tap, tap" as they worked together.

"Thank you for coming when I had energy!" Rosie said, giving Benny a big hug.

In the afternoon, Benny helped Ollie sort books by color and size. The library smelled like old paper and wisdom.

"I appreciate you asking about my best time," Ollie hooted gratefully.

In the evening, Benny taught Sammy how to measure wood and hammer safely. Sammy's little paws felt so proud holding the tiny hammer.

"You're the best teacher, Benny!" Sammy said with sparkling eyes.

As the sun set, Benny sat back in his office chair, feeling tired but very happy. His plan had worked perfectly!

"Planning helped me help everyone," Benny said with a satisfied smile. "And asking what they needed made them even happier!"

He looked at his successful plan on the desk. Tomorrow, he would make another plan for another wonderful day of helping friends.

Benny learned that planning ahead and caring about others' feelings made everything work better. And that made his heart feel as warm as a cozy fireplace.

The End.
