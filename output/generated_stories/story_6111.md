# Leo’s Treehouse Team

Leo was a brave little lion cub. He had bright golden fur and a curious nose. One sunny morning, Leo climbed the ladder to his treehouse. The wood was warm. The leaves whispered, swish-swish. Birds sang, tweet-tweet. Leo smiled. “Today, I will make a flag for our treehouse,” he said. “A flag for everyone!”

He spread colorful cloth on the floor. Red, blue, green, yellow, and purple. He held up a brush. “I will paint the best flag,” Leo said. “But how should it look?”

Leo tapped his chin. Tap, tap. He wanted the flag to show love for all friends. He wanted it to be fair. He wanted it to welcome everyone. But what picture should he choose?

“Hmm,” Leo said. “I need ideas.” He took a deep breath. He used his brave heart and his thinking head. “I will ask my friends!”

Soon, his friends climbed the ladder to the treehouse. There was Tiko the zebra, with stripes like piano keys. There was Bina the bunny, soft and gray. There was Miko the monkey, with quick hands and a big smile. And there was Asha the parrot, with bright rainbow feathers.

“Welcome!” said Leo. “I am making a flag. I want it to show we are all different and all special. What should it look like?”

Tiko swished his tail. “Stripes!” he said. “I love stripes.”

Bina wiggled her nose. “A soft cloud,” she said softly. “Clouds hold rain for plants. They care for the earth.”

Miko clapped. “A banana!” he giggled. “Bananas are yummy.”

Asha fluffed her feathers. “Colors,” she sang. “Many colors. Like me!”

Leo listened carefully. He nodded. “Thank you,” he said. “We have many ideas. Now we must choose.” He looked around the treehouse. He could smell fresh wood. He could hear leaves rustle. He could feel his heart beat, thump-thump.

“We need a plan,” Leo said. “Let’s think step by step.” He pointed to a small chalkboard. “First, what is our goal?”

“Our goal,” said Asha, “is to make a flag for everyone.”

“Yes,” said Leo. “A flag that says we all belong.”

“Second,” Leo said, “we will try ideas. We will practice drawing. We will see what works.”

They began. Tiko drew black and white stripes. Swish, swish. “It looks like me,” he said. “But does it look like everyone?”

Bina drew a soft gray cloud. “It is kind,” she whispered. “But does it show all of us?”

Miko drew a big banana. He giggled. “Yum!” he said. “But not everyone likes bananas.”

Asha painted bright colors in little shapes. “Pretty!” she chirped. “But it needs a message.”

Leo looked at the papers. His whiskers twitched. He felt proud. They had tried many times. They had practiced. But they still needed one idea that fit all.

“Third,” Leo said, “we will use our thinking. We will mix ideas.” He picked up a new piece of cloth. It felt smooth. “What if we make a flag with many parts?”

Tiko tilted his head. “What do you mean?”

Leo smiled. “We can use stripes for Tiko. We can add a soft cloud for Bina. We can add bright colors for Asha. And for Miko, we can add a yellow circle. It does not need to be a banana. It can be a sun! The sun gives light to all.”

Miko grinned. “The sun is for everyone!” he said.

Asha flapped. “Yes! Many colors, plus a sun, plus stripes, plus a cloud. It shows we are different and we shine together.”

They worked as a team. Paint went swish-swish. The brush smelled like berries. The cloth felt cool. Leo painted stripes on one side. Tiko helped with careful lines. Bina painted a soft gray cloud in the corner. Miko painted a bright yellow sun in the middle. Asha dotted many colors around like tiny rainbows.

When they finished, they stepped back. The flag was bright and warm. It had stripes, a cloud, a sun, and many colors. It looked like a hug for every friend.

Leo tied the flag to the top of the treehouse. The wind blew it wide. Whoosh! The flag waved hello to the world.

A squirrel looked up. “Nice flag!” she chittered. “Can I play, too?”

“Yes!” said Leo. “Everyone can play.” He felt happy and strong. He had used bravery to ask for help. He had used practice to try again and again. He had used thinking to make a plan.

Tiko smiled. “We are different,” he said.

Bina nodded. “We belong.”

Miko clapped. “We worked together!”

Asha sang. “We shine together!”

Leo’s heart felt warm. He looked at his friends. He looked at the flag. “Our treehouse is for all,” he said. “Different is good. Different is strong. When we listen, practice, and think, we find the best answer.”

They played under the bright flag until the sun went down. The leaves whispered, the birds sang, and the friends laughed.

The End.
