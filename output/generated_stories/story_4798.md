# Ellie Leads with Gentle Feet

In a cozy, comfortable nursery, the sun made soft squares on the floor. The room smelled like warm blankets and fresh crayons. Little toys waited on shelves. Tiny chairs circled a bright, round rug. On that rug stood Ellie, a gentle elephant calf with big ears and careful eyes. Her feet were soft and quiet, tap-tap on the floor.

Ellie loved the nursery. She loved the soft music that hummed from a small radio. She loved the tickle of the fan’s breeze on her ears. Most of all, Ellie loved her friends: Pip the panda, Tilly the turtle, and Nori the nightingale. They wanted to build a tall block tower.

“Let’s make it reach the moon,” piped Nori.

“Slow and steady,” said Tilly.

“Big blocks first!” said Pip.

Ellie smiled. “We can do it,” she said in a gentle voice.

They began. Click, clack, clunk went the blocks. The tower wobbled and grew. Ellie lifted a block with her trunk. She placed it with care. “There,” she whispered. Her heart felt warm, like a cozy hug.

But soon, Pip grabbed a block and put it sideways. The tower leaned. Nori fluttered and chirped, “My way! My way!” Tilly tucked into her shell. “Too fast,” she murmured.

The tower shook, then—thump!—it fell. Blocks scattered across the rug. Everyone was quiet. Ellie heard the tiny hum of the radio and the soft shush of the fan.

Pip crossed his arms. “It’s not fair,” he said.

Nori flapped. “You didn’t listen to me!”

Tilly sighed. “I want to hide.”

Ellie felt a heavy lump in her tummy. Her ears drooped. She did not like the unhappy voices. She took a slow breath. In and out. In and out. The air smelled like crayons and little lemon wipes.

Ellie wanted to help. She remembered a song from the teacher: “Peaceful steps, peaceful hearts.” She thought, What can I do? I can be a helper. I can be a leader who is kind.

Ellie took one gentle step forward. Tap. She spoke softly. “Friends, let’s use quiet voices. We can fix this together.”

Pip looked at her. Nori paused. Tilly peeked out.

“How?” asked Pip.

Ellie nodded. “First, let’s listen. One at a time.” She sat down on the rug. “Pip, you first.”

Pip’s voice got softer. “I wanted big blocks first.”

“Thank you,” Ellie said. “Nori?”

Nori folded her wings. “I wanted it to be tall, tall, tall!”

“Thank you,” Ellie said. “Tilly?”

Tilly lifted her head. “I like slow and steady.”

Ellie smiled. “We all have good ideas. Let’s make a plan. We can practice a new way.”

She held up her trunk. “Plan step one: Big blocks first on the bottom.” She tapped the thick blocks. “Step two: Slow and steady. We count to three before we add a block. One, two, three.” Her voice was calm and sing-song. “Step three: Take turns. We say, ‘Your turn,’ and ‘Thank you.’”

Pip nodded. “I can try.”

Nori chirped. “I will count.”

Tilly smiled. “I will check if it’s steady.”

Ellie felt the lump in her tummy melt away. She felt brave and kind at the same time. “Let’s practice,” she said.

They began again. Click, clack, clunk. The room filled with happy sounds. Nori sang, “One, two, three.” Pip placed a big block. “Your turn,” he said, and smiled. “Thank you,” said Tilly, checking with a gentle tap. Ellie steadied the edges with her trunk. “Nice and easy,” she said.

The tower grew taller and straighter. The sun-warm rug felt soft under Ellie’s feet. She smelled the wood of the blocks, smooth and friendly. Her heart beat a little faster with joy.

“Almost there,” said Tilly.

“Careful,” whispered Pip.

Ellie took the last block. She paused. She looked at her friends. “Ready?” she asked.

“Ready,” they said together.

“One, two, three,” sang Nori.

Ellie placed the block. It fit! The tower stood strong and tall. The friends cheered. “We did it!”

Ellie laughed, the sound like tiny bells. Pip bounced. Nori flapped gently so the wind would not shake the tower. Tilly patted the base with care.

“Our plan worked,” Ellie said. “We listened. We took turns. We were patient. That is peaceful leadership.”

Pip nodded. “You led us kindly, Ellie.”

Nori chirped, “You used your words, not loud flaps.”

Tilly added, “You helped us think and practice.”

Ellie’s ears wiggled with happy pride. “We all led,” she said. “When you listen, you lead. When you help, you lead. We can all be leaders.”

Just then, the teacher peeked in. The soft music played. The fan hummed. The tower shone in the sunlight. The nursery felt calm and bright.

The friends sat in a circle and shared warm fruit slices. “Mmm,” said Pip. The sweet smell filled the room. Ellie touched the cool cup of water and took a sip. She felt peaceful and strong.

Ellie looked at her friends. “Next time there is a problem, we can use our plan. We can use gentle voices, good thinking, and practice, practice, practice.”

“Yes,” said everyone.

Ellie’s heart glowed. She had led with gentle feet and a caring voice. And that made the nursery a happy place for everyone.

The End.
