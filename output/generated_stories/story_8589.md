# Leo Plans the Big Block Build

In a sunny, playful living room, a brave little lion cub named Leo swished his tiny tail. The rug was soft and squishy under his paws. He could smell warm toast from the kitchen and hear birds peeping outside the window.

“Today,” Leo roared in a little cub voice, “I will build the tallest tower ever!”

He padded to his toy basket. Blocks clacked and rattled when he lifted the lid. There were red blocks, blue blocks, big blocks, small blocks, and one very wobbly block with a little chip.

Leo grabbed the wobbly block first. “Up you go!” he said. He placed it at the bottom and stacked a tall, wobbly tower. Tap, tap, tap—he added more and more.

Sway, sway… crash!

Blocks tumbled like colorful rain. “Oh no,” Leo huffed. His ears drooped. His whiskers tickled his nose, and he felt a little frustrated.

Mama Lion peeked in, wiping her paws on a soft towel. “I heard a crash. Are you okay, brave cub?”

Leo sniffed. “I want a tall tower, but it keeps falling. I’m mad.” He stomped one soft paw, thump.

Mama Lion knelt beside him. “It’s okay to feel mad. Take a deep breath with me.” They breathed in slowly and breathed out softly. Whoooo.

Leo’s shoulders relaxed. “Better,” he said.

Mama smiled. “Before big builds, we prepare. Preparation helps a plan be strong. Let’s make a plan together.”

“Prepare?” Leo blinked. “What do we do?”

“We look, we sort, we choose, and we stack with a strategy,” Mama said. “That means we think about what goes first.”

Leo’s eyes shone. “I can do that!”

First, they looked. Leo used his sharp eyes to see all the blocks. “I see big blocks. I see tiny blocks. I see the wobbly one.”

Next, they sorted. Leo slid blocks across the rug. Swish, swish. “Big blocks here. Medium blocks here. Tiny blocks in a little pile.” He giggled at the clacky sounds.

Then, they chose. Mama tapped the big, heavy blocks. “These make a strong base. A strong base helps balance.”

Leo nodded. “Big blocks first. That is my strategy!” He liked the big word. It felt strong in his mouth.

He set two big blocks side by side. Thunk, thunk. He pressed them gently. “Are you steady?” he asked.

“I feel steady,” he whispered to himself.

He added two more big blocks across the top like a bridge. Tap. Everything felt firm under his paw.

“Now medium blocks,” Leo said. He stacked them carefully, one, two, three. He listened to the little click each time. Click. Click. Click.

The tower grew. It was as tall as Leo’s knee, then as tall as Leo’s belly. He felt proud, and his tail flicked with happy swishes.

He picked up the tiny blocks. They were light and smooth. “These go near the top,” he remembered. He placed them gently. Tip… tap… set.

The tower wiggled just a little.

Leo frowned. “Uh-oh.”

Mama pointed. “What can help?”

Leo looked and thought. “Maybe the wobbly block should not be at the bottom. That made it fall before. I will understand my blocks. The wobbly one can be a roof on top!”

He took a breath, held the tower with one paw, and added the wobbly block carefully. Tink.

The tower stood. It stood tall and proud, shining with blue and red and yellow. Leo’s eyes went big. “I did it! It’s tall!”

Suddenly, a little breeze from the window whispered across the room. The top wiggled again.

Leo felt a bubble of worry. His paws felt warm. “I’m scared it will fall,” he said.

“Use your plan,” Mama whispered. “You are brave, and you are prepared.”

Leo placed one more small block like a brace behind the tower. “This is my helper block,” he said. “It gives support.” He pressed it in with gentle paws.

The tower stopped wiggling. It felt balanced and strong. Leo smiled so wide his whiskers curled. “I used preparation, understanding, and strategy!”

Mama clapped softly. “And courage, when it felt hard.”

Leo nodded. “I felt mad before. I took a breath. I made a plan. I sorted. I chose. I stacked. I checked. I fixed.”

He sat beside his tower and listened. The room was quiet except for the tick-tock of a clock and the purr of the heater. He touched the smooth blocks and smelled a hint of wood. He felt calm and proud.

Mama brought two cups of warm milk. “Builders need breaks,” she said.

They sipped. Mmm. Leo looked at his tower. “Tomorrow I will build a bridge. I will prepare first!”

Mama kissed his head. “My brave little lion cub, your plan made your tower strong, and your calm made your heart strong.”

Leo stood up and patted his tower gently. “I like to plan. Planning helps me do big things.”

He cleaned up the extra blocks and put them in the basket. Clack, clack. He left out the helper block and the wobbly block on top, just where they belonged.

Leo took a deep breath. “I am ready for more.”

The End.
