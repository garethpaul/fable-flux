# Mimi Mouse’s Balcony Boogie

Mimi was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She lived with her family near a sunny balcony garden. The garden had tiny pots of mint, basil, and cherry tomatoes. Bees buzzed. Leaves rustled. Birds sang sweet songs in the morning.

One warm day, Mimi heard music from a radio inside the house. The song floated out to the balcony like a ribbon of sound. Mimi’s whiskers twitched. Her little feet tapped. “I want to dance!” she squeaked with a happy wiggle.

She stepped onto the balcony. The floor was smooth and warm. The basil smelled like pepper and the mint smelled cool, like a tiny breeze. Mimi twirled once, very slow. “Squeak! This feels nice.”

Her friend Nia, a ladybug, landed on the railing. “Hi, Mimi! What are you doing?”

“I’m dancing,” Mimi said. “The music makes my heart feel jumpy and glad.”

Nia smiled. “I love watching you dance.”

Just then, a gust of wind whooshed past. The watering can tipped. A puddle spread across the floor. Mimi did not see it. She spun faster. Her foot slid—whoops! Mimi wobbled and grabbed the tomato stake.

Her tiny heart thumped. “Oh no,” she said. “I almost fell.”

Nia fluttered closer. “Are you okay?”

“I think so,” Mimi said softly. She looked at the shiny puddle and the edge of the balcony. It was high. The birds’ songs sounded far away and a little scary now. Mimi’s ears drooped. “Maybe dancing is not safe.”

Nia shook her tiny head. “Dancing is wonderful. We just need to make it safe.”

Mimi took a deep breath. The music still played. She wanted to dance, but she wanted to be careful too. “How can we make it safe?”

Buzz, a friendly bee, hovered near the mint. “Bzz! I know a few ideas,” he hummed. “First, clean up spills. Wet floors are slippery.”

Mimi nodded. “Good plan.” She grabbed a soft leaf that had fallen from the basil and carefully wiped the puddle. She set the watering can upright. “All dry!” she said.

“Second,” Buzz buzzed, “make space. Move things out of your dance path.”

Mimi looked around. A tiny rake lay near her feet. A small pot sat in the middle. She gently moved the rake to the corner. She scooted the pot beside the tomatoes. “Now I have a dance spot,” she said. It was like a little dance stage, warm and safe.

“Third,” Nia added, “listen to your body. If it feels wobbly, slow down.”

Mimi smiled. “I can do that.”

She stood in her safe spot. The music’s beat was steady: thump, thump, thump. Mimi bent her knees. She wiggled her whiskers. She took a small step to the left and a small step to the right. Tap, tap. Her feet made tiny sounds on the floor. She swung her arms like soft ropes. She felt the sun on her fur and the cool mint smell in her nose. She was dancing again!

“Yes!” Mimi cheered. “This is my Balcony Boogie.”

“Teach us!” Nia said.

Mimi laughed. “Okay! First, safety check.” She pointed with her paw. “No puddles?” 

“No puddles,” Buzz and Nia said together.

“Edge far away?” Mimi asked.

“Edge far away,” they echoed, moving back from the railing.

“Shoes or clean feet?” Mimi wiggled her toes. “Clean feet!”

Now Mimi taught her friends a simple dance:
- Step, step, clap.
- Wiggle, wiggle, tap.
- Spin slowly, stop, and smile.

They tried it together. Step, step, clap! Wiggle, wiggle, tap! Spin slowly, stop, and smile. The birds chirped along. The basil leaves brushed each other, whispering shh-shh in the breeze. The tomato plant bobbed like it was nodding in time.

Mimi noticed a dry leaf on the mint. “We should care for our garden, too,” she said kindly. “The plants give us shade and sweet smells for our dance.”

She picked up the dry leaf and put it in the compost cup. Nia checked the soil with her tiny feet. “This pot needs a sip of water,” she said.

Mimi poured just a little from the watering can. “Not too much,” she whispered. “We share and we care.”

Buzz hummed a soft rhythm, like a drum: bzz-bzz, bzz-bzz. Mimi clapped to the beat. The music from the radio mixed with Buzz’s hum and the birds’ songs. It felt like the whole balcony was in the band.

Then Mimi’s little brother, Max, peeked out. “Can I dance too?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Mimi, “but first we do our safety steps.”

They checked for spills. They kept the edge far away. They made space. Max nodded. “I’m ready.”

“Follow me,” Mimi said. Together they danced the Balcony Boogie. Step, step, clap. Wiggle, wiggle, tap. Spin slowly, stop, and smile! Max giggled. Mimi’s heart felt big and warm.

When the song ended, they took a bow. Mimi patted the pots gently. “Thank you, garden,” she said. “Thank you, music. Thank you, friends.”

Nia clapped her tiny wings. “You taught us how to dance and be safe.”

Buzz buzzed, “And you cared for the plants, too. Kind and careful—that’s the best way to boogie.”

Mimi beamed. “Dancing is fun. Dancing is safe when we think first. Music helps our bodies move, and kindness helps our world grow.”

They all smiled as a new song began, and the balcony garden swayed with them, happy and green.

The End.
