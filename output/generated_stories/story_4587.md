# Rocco's Bathroom Band

Rocco the raccoon loved making music more than anything else in the whole world. He had tiny cymbals on his paws, a little drum around his neck, and maracas tied to his tail. *Shake, shake, CRASH!* went his instruments as he danced around the house.

One sunny morning, Rocco bounced into the bathroom, ready to practice his morning concert. The shiny white tiles made perfect echoes for his music! *BANG, BANG, CRASH!* His drums sounded so loud and wonderful.

"Rocco!" called Mama Raccoon from the kitchen. "Remember the bathroom rules, sweetie. Turn off the water when you brush your teeth, and don't leave the faucet running!"

"Okay, Mama!" Rocco called back, but he was too excited about his music to really listen. He turned on the bathroom faucet and watched the sparkly water flow. *Splash, splash!* It sounded like another instrument joining his band!

Rocco started brushing his teeth while playing his drums. *Brush, brush, BANG!* The water kept running and running, making a cheerful gurgling sound. He was having so much fun that he forgot all about Mama's directions.

After brushing, Rocco left the water running and started his full bathroom concert. He shook his maracas, crashed his cymbals, and beat his drum. The flowing water made the perfect background music!

Twenty minutes later, Mama Raccoon came to check on him. Her eyes grew very wide when she saw the water still gushing from the faucet.

"Oh my!" she gasped, quickly turning off the water. "Rocco, what happened to following directions?"

Rocco's ears drooped down low. His tail stopped shaking the maracas. "I... I forgot, Mama. The water sounded so pretty with my music."

Mama sat down beside him on the bathroom floor. "I understand, honey. Music is wonderful! But when we don't follow directions about saving water, we waste something very precious."

"Precious?" Rocco asked, tilting his fuzzy head.

"Yes," Mama explained gently. "Water helps flowers grow, gives animals drinks, and keeps our earth healthy. When we waste it, there's less for everyone else."

Rocco looked at the faucet, then at his instruments. "I didn't know, Mama. I just wanted to make beautiful music."

"You can still make beautiful music AND follow directions," Mama smiled. "Let me show you how!"

Mama taught Rocco a special song about saving water. "Turn it off, turn it off, when you brush your teeth! Save the water, save the water, for the birds and bees!" she sang in a happy tune.

Rocco's eyes lit up like sparklers! "Can I make that into a real song with my instruments?"

"Of course!" Mama laughed.

From that day on, Rocco practiced his new water-saving song every morning. *Brush, brush* went his teeth, then *click* went the faucet turning off. Then came his instruments: *Shake, shake, BANG!* "Turn it off, turn it off!" he sang proudly.

Soon, all the forest animals came to hear Rocco's special bathroom concerts. They learned the water-saving song too! Papa Bear, Little Bunny, and even Wise Old Owl would sing along: "Save the water, save the water, for the birds and bees!"

Rocco discovered that following directions made his music even more special. He felt proud knowing that his songs helped take care of the earth and all his friends.

"Following directions is like following a musical beat," Rocco told his forest friends. "When everyone does their part, we make beautiful harmony together!"

The End.
