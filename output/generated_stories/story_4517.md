# Shelly's Musical Discovery

Shelly the turtle was very shy. She lived in a cozy guest room with soft blue walls and a big, fluffy bed. The room had pretty curtains that danced in the breeze, but Shelly always hid inside her shell when anyone came to visit.

One sunny morning, Shelly heard something wonderful. *Tap, tap, tap!* It was coming from outside her window. She poked her little green head out just a tiny bit.

"What's that beautiful sound?" she whispered to herself.

The tapping got louder. *Tap-tap-TAP!* Shelly's heart felt happy and jumpy. She had never heard anything like it before. Very slowly, she crawled to the window and peeked outside.

There was a red bird on the windowsill, tapping his beak against the glass! The bird saw Shelly and smiled.

"Hello there!" chirped the bird. "I'm making music! Would you like to try?"

Shelly quickly pulled her head back into her shell. "Oh no," she said quietly. "I'm too shy. I don't know how to make music."

But the tapping continued. *Tap-tap-tap-TAP!* The sound made Shelly's toes tingle with excitement. She had an idea!

Shelly looked around the guest room. There was a wooden dresser, a soft pillow, and some books on a shelf. "Maybe I can make music too," she thought.

First, she tried tapping the dresser with her little claw. *Tap!* It made a deep, woody sound. Then she patted the fluffy pillow. *Puff!* It made a soft, whispery sound.

"This is fun!" Shelly giggled. She felt brave and creative.

The red bird heard the sounds from inside. "That's wonderful music!" he called through the window. "You're a natural!"

Shelly felt warm and proud. She discovered that the books made a *thump-thump* sound, and the window made a *ting-ting* when she tapped it gently.

Soon, Shelly was making a whole song! *Tap-puff-thump-ting!* Her body started to sway back and forth. Before she knew it, she was dancing! Her little feet moved to the rhythm, and her shell wiggled with joy.

"I'm dancing! I'm making music!" Shelly laughed out loud. She felt so happy that she forgot all about being shy.

The red bird clapped his wings. "You found your own special way to make music! You used what was around you and listened to your heart."

Shelly realized something important. She didn't need to be exactly like the bird to make beautiful music. She could use her own ideas and the things around her. Being resourceful meant she could create something wonderful all by herself!

From that day on, whenever Shelly felt shy or scared, she would make music in the guest room. Sometimes she would tap rhythms, sometimes she would hum little songs, and sometimes she would dance around the soft carpet.

The guest room became filled with joy and music. When visitors came to stay, they would hear Shelly's gentle songs and feel welcome and happy.

Shelly learned that music comes from the heart, and everyone can make their own special kind of music. She also discovered that being shy was okay, but sharing her gifts made her feel even more wonderful.

"Music is everywhere," Shelly would tell her reflection in the window. "You just have to listen and be brave enough to try!"

And whenever the red bird visited, they would make music together - each in their own special way.

The End.
