# Luna's Heart Garden

The soft morning light peeked through the curtains of Luna's cozy bedroom. The gentle deer fawn sat on her fluffy pink rug, looking sadly at her favorite drawing tablet. Yesterday, her little brother had scribbled all over her beautiful flower picture with his red crayon.

Luna's ears drooped as she touched the messy red marks. "My pretty flowers are ruined," she whispered, her brown eyes filling with tears. The drawing had taken her all morning to make - careful purple petals, green stems, and a bright yellow sun.

Mama Deer peeked her head through the doorway. "Good morning, sweet Luna. You look upset. What's wrong?"

"Brother messed up my picture," Luna said, holding up the tablet. "Look at all these red scribbles! Now it's ugly and I can't fix it."

Mama Deer's gentle hooves clicked softly on the wooden floor as she came closer. "Oh my, that does look frustrating. How does your heart feel?"

Luna touched her chest. "Heavy and mad. Like a dark cloud is sitting inside me."

"Would you like to try something special?" Mama asked, settling down on the soft rug. "Sometimes when our hearts feel heavy, we can plant seeds of forgiveness to help them feel light again."

"Forgiveness seeds?" Luna tilted her head curiously.

"Yes! Close your eyes and imagine your heart is like a little garden," Mama said in her warm voice. "What do you see?"

Luna squeezed her eyes shut. "I see brown dirt and some weeds that look angry."

"Now, imagine planting tiny seeds of forgiveness. Think about how your brother felt when he saw your beautiful picture. Maybe he wanted to help make it even more colorful?"

Luna's nose twitched as she thought. "Maybe... maybe he thought he was helping?"

"That's a beautiful seed, Luna! Plant it in your heart garden."

Luna imagined dropping a small, glowing seed into the dirt. Suddenly, a tiny green sprout appeared in her mind. "Mama! Something's growing!"

"Wonderful! Now think of one nice thing about your brother."

"He always shares his cookies with me," Luna said, and another seed sprouted in her imagination.

"And how do you think he would feel if he knew you were sad?"

Luna's eyes popped open. "He would feel sad too! Brother doesn't like when I'm upset." She looked at her drawing again, but this time she noticed something different. "Mama, look! The red marks kind of look like butterflies flying around my flowers!"

Mama Deer smiled. "What do you see now?"

Luna grabbed her crayons excitedly. "I can add wings! And maybe some more butterflies!" Her hooves moved quickly as she drew delicate wings around the red scribbles. "Brother accidentally made my picture more magical!"

Soon, Brother Fawn peeked around the door. "Luna? Are you still mad at me?"

Luna looked up from her colorful creation, her heart feeling as light as a feather. "I was mad, but I'm not anymore. Look what we made together!"

Brother's eyes grew wide. "Wow! Those are the most beautiful butterflies ever!"

"Want to help me add more?" Luna asked, scooting over to make room.

As they colored together on the warm rug, Luna felt her heart garden blooming with joy. She had learned that forgiveness wasn't just about saying sorry - it was about planting seeds of understanding that could grow into something even more beautiful.

"Thank you for teaching me about forgiveness seeds, Mama," Luna said.

Mama Deer nuzzled her gently. "You grew them all by yourself, sweet one. Your heart is the most wonderful garden of all."

The End.
