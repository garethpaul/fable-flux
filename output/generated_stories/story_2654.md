# Sunny Day Squirrel Games

In a sunny backyard with soft green grass, a small breeze made the leaves whisper, swish-swish. Birds sang tweet-tweet in the tall tree. The air smelled sweet like warm apples. A resourceful squirrel named Sunny peeped out from her cozy nest. Her tail was fluffy, her eyes were bright, and her heart was full of big ideas.

“I want to play sports today!” Sunny chirped. “I want to hop, and toss, and race!”

Sunny climbed down the tree. She felt the bumpy bark under her paws. She carried a tiny acorn ball she had made, smooth and shiny. She had also tied two little leaves into flags. She placed the flags in the grass and drew a chalk line on the patio with a little white stone.

Sunny looked around the sunny backyard. “Who will play with me?” she asked.

Blue the bluebird fluttered down. “Tweet! I can cheer,” said Blue. “I love to sing.”

Lily the ladybug landed on a yellow dandelion. “I can count,” said Lily. “One, two, three!”

Benny the bunny hopped from behind a bush. His ears flopped. “I can race,” said Benny. “Ready, set, hop!”

Sunny clapped her paws. “Let’s play the Squirrel Games! We will be kind. We will be safe. We will have fun!”

They started with a toss game. Sunny lined up leaves as goals. “Throw the acorn ball into the circle,” she said.

Benny tossed. The acorn ball rolled past the circle. “Oops,” said Benny, ears drooping.

“It’s okay,” said Sunny. “Try again. We can be patient.”

Blue sang, “You can do it! Slow and steady.” Lily counted, “One… two… three…”

Benny tried again. He took a slow breath. He tossed gently. Plop! The acorn ball landed in the circle. “I did it!” he giggled. Sunny cheered, “Great job! Patience helps.”

Now it was Sunny’s turn. She tossed the acorn ball. It bumped the circle and rolled away. “Oh,” said Sunny, her whiskers wiggling. “I want it to go in right now.”

Blue tilted his head. “Tweet-tweet. Take your time.”

Lily said, “Patience, Sunny. One slow breath.”

Sunny put a paw on her chest. She breathed in. She breathed out. She tried again, slow and gentle. Plop! The acorn ball landed right in the circle. Sunny smiled wide. “Patience works!”

Next was a hop race. “From the tree to the flag,” Sunny said. “Ready, set—” She paused. “Wait. We start together.”

Lily counted, “One… two… three… go!”

Benny hopped hop-hop-hop. Sunny hopped hop-hop-hop. The grass tickled their paws, and the sun warmed their fur. Blue chirped a happy song. But suddenly, Sunny heard a small cry.

“Help, please,” squeaked a tiny ant near the path. The ant had a crumb bigger than her head. The crumb was stuck in a crack.

Sunny stopped hopping. She wanted to finish the race. Her paws wiggled with wanting. But she saw the little ant’s worried eyes.

“I can be kind,” Sunny said to herself. “I can be patient.”

Sunny walked to the ant. “I can help,” she said softly. She gently nudged the crumb. Push, push. It moved a little. Benny stopped too. “I will help,” he said. They pushed together. The crumb rolled free!

“Thank you,” squeaked the ant. “You were patient and kind.”

Sunny’s heart felt warm, like sunshine. “You’re welcome.”

They went back to the race. “We can start again,” Sunny said. Lily nodded. “Fair and kind,” she said.

“Ready, set, go!” Lily counted. This time, they both hopped happily. They crossed the finish flag together and held paws high. “We are both winners,” Sunny said. “Kindness is a win.”

Last, Sunny wanted to try a new game. She placed a stick on two little rocks to make a tiny jump. “We’ll jump over,” she said. “But be careful.”

Benny jumped—boing!—and landed with a soft thump. “Fun!” he said.

Sunny wiggled her tail. She ran fast and tried to jump. Her paw clipped the stick. Clatter! The stick fell.

Sunny’s cheeks felt hot. “I want to do it now,” she said, frowning a little. Her tummy felt twisty. She wanted to be fast and good.

Blue fluttered close. “Tweet-tweet. Try slow. Try again. Patience.”

Lily counted softly, “One… two… three…”

Sunny stood still. She felt the breeze on her fur. She listened to the birds. She smelled the sweet grass. She took a slow breath in and a slow breath out. “I can wait. I can try again,” she told herself.

She set the stick back up. She bent her knees. She looked where she wanted to land. She didn’t rush. She hopped—boing!—and sailed right over. She landed light as a leaf. “I did it!” she squealed, tail swishing like a flag.

Benny clapped his paws. Blue sang a bright song. Lily whispered, “Patience wins!”

Sunny smiled at her friends. “Today I learned something big,” she said. “Sports are fun. Being kind is important. And patience helps me do hard things.”

The sun was lower now, soft and golden. The grass felt cool. The backyard was full of happy sounds—giggles, chirps, and tiny claps. Sunny packed the acorn ball and the leaf flags.

“Thank you for playing,” Sunny said. “Thank you for cheering. Thank you for helping.”

“Thank you for being patient,” said the ant, peeking out again.

Sunny nodded. “When I wait, when I breathe, when I try again, I feel strong and calm. I can learn new skills. I can help my friends. I can have fun.”

They all sat together for a rest. They shared tiny berry snacks. The sky turned pink, like a soft blanket. Sunny curled her tail around her. Her heart felt peaceful.

“Tomorrow,” Sunny said, “we can play again. We will practice. We will be patient. We will be kind.”

“Yes!” chirped Blue. “Tweet-tweet!”

“Yay!” said Benny.

“One… two… three… friends,” said Lily with a smile.

Sunny looked at her friends and the sunny backyard. She felt proud. She had waited. She had helped. She had tried again. And that made the day perfect.

The End.
