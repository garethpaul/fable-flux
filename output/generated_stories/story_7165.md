# Polly's Colorful Day

Polly was a bright green parrot who loved to paint in the sunny art room. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds, and her orange beak was always smiling. The art room smelled like sweet crayons and had big windows that let in warm sunshine.

"Squawk! Today I will paint the most beautiful picture ever!" chirped Polly as she flapped her wings excitedly.

But Polly had a big problem. She wanted to do everything at once! She wanted to paint a rainbow, play soccer with her friends, and make a clay sculpture all in one morning.

Polly grabbed her paintbrush and started painting red stripes. "Oh! But I also want to kick the soccer ball," she said, dropping her brush with a *clatter*. She flew to the window and saw her friends playing soccer in the yard below.

"Maybe I can do both!" Polly thought. She tried to paint with one wing while bouncing a small ball with her foot. *Splat!* Red paint splashed everywhere. The ball rolled under the art table.

"Oh no!" squawked Polly. "This isn't working!"

Just then, Mrs. Owl, the wise art teacher, waddled into the room. Her soft gray feathers rustled as she moved. "What's wrong, dear Polly?" she hooted gently.

"I want to paint AND play soccer AND make clay art, but everything is getting messy!" Polly's voice sounded sad and frustrated.

Mrs. Owl smiled with her big, kind eyes. "Polly, let me teach you something special about time. When we try to do everything at once, nothing gets done well. But when we do one thing at a time, we can do our very best!"

"Really?" asked Polly, tilting her colorful head.

"Yes! Let's make a plan together," said Mrs. Owl. "First, you can paint for twenty minutes. Then you can play soccer for twenty minutes. After that, you can work with clay. How does that sound?"

Polly's eyes lit up like tiny stars. "That sounds wonderful!"

First, Polly focused only on painting. She carefully dipped her brush in blue paint and made beautiful sky strokes. The paint felt cool and smooth. She added fluffy white clouds that looked like cotton balls. "Look, Mrs. Owl! When I focus on just painting, it's so much better!"

*Tick-tock, tick-tock* went the big clock on the wall.

"Time for soccer!" announced Mrs. Owl.

Polly flew outside and played soccer with her friends. She kicked the black and white ball with her strong feet. The grass felt soft and springy. "This is so fun when I'm not worried about other things!" she chirped happily.

After soccer time, Polly returned to make her clay sculpture. Her feathers were a little ruffled from playing, but she felt proud and happy. The clay felt squishy and cool in her wings as she shaped a little bird.

"Mrs. Owl, I did it! I finished my painting, played soccer, AND made my sculpture!" Polly danced around the art room, her tail feathers fanning out like a colorful fan.

"You learned something very important today, Polly," said Mrs. Owl. "When we give our full attention to one activity at a time, we feel proud of what we accomplish. This is called managing our time wisely."

From that day on, Polly always made plans for her activities. She felt proud, happy, and successful because she learned that doing one thing well was much better than doing many things poorly.

The End.
