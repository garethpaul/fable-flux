# Leo the Lion Cub Learns to Share

In a cozy living room filled with soft cushions and colorful toys, lived a brave little lion cub named Leo. His golden fur sparkled in the warm sunlight that streamed through the big window. Leo loved playing in his special room, where toy cars zoomed across the soft carpet and building blocks reached up to the sky.

One sunny morning, Leo was playing with his favorite red fire truck. "Vroom, vroom!" he roared happily, pushing the shiny truck around the room. The wheels made a pleasant rolling sound on the wooden floor.

Just then, his little sister Lily toddled into the room. Her eyes grew big and round when she saw the beautiful red truck. "Truck! Truck!" she squealed with excitement, clapping her tiny paws together.

"No!" Leo growled, pulling the truck close to his chest. "This is MY truck! You can't play with it!"

Lily's bottom lip began to wobble, and tears formed in her bright eyes. She sat down on the soft blue rug with a small thump and started to cry. "Waaah!"

Leo felt something funny in his tummy when he heard his sister crying. It wasn't a good feeling at all. He looked at Lily, then at his truck, then back at Lily again.

Mama Lion padded into the room, her warm brown eyes taking in the scene. "What's happening here, my little cubs?" she asked gently, her voice soft like a purr.

"Leo won't share his truck!" Lily sniffled.

"It's MINE!" Leo said, but his voice was quieter now.

Mama Lion sat down on the fluffy carpet between her cubs. "Leo, do you remember how happy you feel when you play with your truck?"

Leo nodded, his little mane bobbing up and down.

"Well," Mama continued, "Lily would feel that same wonderful happiness if she could play with it too. Sharing means helping others feel joy, just like you do."

Leo thought about this. He looked at his sister's sad face, then at his shiny red truck. He remembered how good it felt when his friend Sam shared cookies with him yesterday.

Slowly, Leo walked over to Lily. "Here, Lily," he said, holding out the truck. "You can play with it for a little while."

Lily's face lit up like sunshine! "Thank you, Leo!" she giggled, giving her big brother a warm, fuzzy hug that smelled like baby powder and love.

But then Leo had an even better idea. His eyes sparkled with excitement. "Wait! I know something even MORE fun! We can play together!"

Leo sat down next to Lily on the soft carpet. "You can drive the fire truck to the emergency, and I'll be the brave firefighter who saves the day!"

"Yes! Yes!" Lily clapped her paws together.

They spent the whole morning playing together. Leo made siren sounds - "Wee-ooh, wee-ooh!" - while Lily carefully drove the truck around the room. When the truck needed to rescue Leo's stuffed elephant from the tall bookshelf, they worked as a team.

"This is the best game ever!" Leo laughed, his heart feeling warm and full.

Mama Lion watched her cubs playing happily together and smiled. "When we share," she said softly, "we don't lose anything. Instead, we gain something wonderful - we gain joy with the people we love."

Leo learned that sharing didn't mean giving up his fun. It meant making the fun even bigger by including others. From that day on, whenever someone wanted to play with his toys, Leo remembered how good it felt to see others happy.

The End.
