# Ollie's Helping Paws

In a cozy closet hideaway, behind the winter coats and old shoes, lived a playful otter named Ollie. His soft brown fur tickled against the hanging clothes as he swam through his imaginary water. The closet smelled like cedar and sunshine from the clothes that hung above.

"Splash, splash!" Ollie giggled, pretending the closet floor was his favorite river. He had built a wonderful fort with shoe boxes and old blankets. It was perfect for one little otter.

One morning, Ollie heard sniffling sounds coming from outside his closet door. He peeked out and saw Benny Bunny sitting in the hallway. Tears rolled down Benny's fuzzy cheeks.

"What's wrong, Benny?" Ollie asked, his whiskers twitching with concern.

"My burrow got flooded in the big rain," Benny sobbed. "I have nowhere warm to sleep. All my things are wet and muddy."

Ollie's heart felt heavy like a stone. He looked back at his cozy closet hideaway. It was so warm and dry. But it was just right for one otter, wasn't it?

"Maybe... maybe you could help me think of something," Ollie said slowly. His paws fidgeted with a loose thread on his blanket.

Benny wiped his nose. "I don't know how. I'm not good at building like you are."

Ollie's ears perked up. "Building! That's it!" He clapped his paws together. "We can build together! Your paws can help my paws!"

"But I've never built anything before," Benny said quietly.

"That's okay!" Ollie's eyes sparkled like stars. "I can show you. We learn by trying!" He grabbed Benny's paw. "Come see my hideaway!"

In the closet, Ollie showed Benny how to stack shoe boxes. "You hold this side, and I'll hold this side," Ollie explained. The cardboard felt smooth and sturdy under their paws.

"Like this?" Benny asked, carefully balancing a box.

"Perfect!" Ollie cheered. "You're a great builder!"

Together, they moved blankets and pillows. They made the space bigger and cozier. Benny's long ears helped him reach high places. Ollie's strong paws helped move heavy things.

"This is fun!" Benny laughed as they arranged soft towels for bedding. The closet now smelled like friendship and fresh cotton.

"Helping feels good in my tummy," Ollie said, patting his belly. "Like warm soup on a cold day."

When they finished, the closet hideaway was twice as nice. Two cozy sleeping spots nestled side by side. Moonlight from the hallway made everything glow softly.

"Thank you for sharing your home," Benny whispered. "And for teaching me to build."

"Thank you for helping me make it better," Ollie replied. "I learned that helping others helps me too. My heart feels bigger now!"

They snuggled into their warm beds. The closet felt fuller in the best way - full of friendship and kindness.

"Tomorrow, can we help someone else?" Benny asked sleepily.

"Yes!" Ollie yawned. "Helping is the best adventure of all."

As they drifted off to sleep, both friends dreamed of all the ways they could volunteer and help others. Ollie learned that sharing made everything better, and that everyone can learn to build wonderful things when they work together.

The End.
