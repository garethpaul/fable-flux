# Tiny Hooves at the Table

Sunlight peeked through the window and sprinkled golden dots on the dining room floor. A gentle deer fawn named Dottie stepped in on soft, tiny hooves. Her nose twitched. She smelled warm toast, sweet berries, and cool, clean water.

“Good morning,” whispered Dottie. “Today, I have a big goal.”

She looked at a little list Mama Deer had placed on the table. The list had three simple drawings: a plate, a napkin, and a cup.

Mama Deer smiled from the doorway. “Good morning, Dottie. What is your goal today?”

Dottie lifted her head. “I want to set the table all by myself. I want to discover where everything goes.”

“That is a kind and careful goal,” said Mama. “You can do it step by step. Start with the plate.”

Dottie touched the smooth plate with her hoof. It felt cool and hard. “Plate first,” she said softly. “Where do plates go?” She looked at the big chair and the small chair. Her ears flicked. She listened to the clock tick, tick, tick. She looked again at the list.

“I will discover the best spot,” Dottie decided. She held the plate and took a slow breath. “I can try.” She placed the plate in front of the chair. It made a tiny clink. “Clink!” Dottie giggled.

Mama Deer nodded. “You used your eyes and your ears. Good discovery.”

Dottie looked back at the list. The second picture was a napkin. She found a soft, square napkin. It felt fluffy, like a small cloud. “Napkin next,” she said. “But where?”

She tried placing the napkin on top of the plate. It looked like a little hat. Dottie tilted her head. “Hmm. Is that right?”

She tested another idea. She slid the napkin beside the plate, to the left. The napkin whispered as it moved, “Swish.”

Mama asked, “How does it look?”

Dottie studied her work. “I discovered that the napkin on the side helps keep the plate ready for food,” she said. “I like it here.”

“Careful thinking,” Mama said.

Dottie checked the last picture: a cup. She found a small cup that felt smooth and just a little heavy. She lifted it slowly. “Cup goes… where?” She tried the left side. Then she tried the right side. The cup made a soft tap on the wood. “Tap.”

Dottie closed her eyes and imagined drinking water. She lifted her hoof like she held the cup. “I think the cup should be on the right,” she said. “It feels easy to reach.” She placed it on the right side of the plate.

Mama Deer clapped her hooves. “You set a goal, you tried ideas, and you found the best places. What a discovery!”

Dottie’s heart felt warm. But then she saw the basket of berries. She wanted to help more. “Mama, may I add fruit? I want to complete the task fully.”

“That is thoughtful,” Mama said. “Let’s plan. First, wash your hooves. Then carry the berries carefully. Small steps help big goals.”

Dottie washed her hooves in cool water. Splash, splash. The water felt tickly. She dried them on a towel. Pat, pat. She picked up the berry bowl. The berries smelled sweet and a little tangy. She walked slow, slow, slow. Her hooves made soft tip-taps on the floor. “Tip-tap, tip-tap.”

She set the bowl in the center of the table. “There,” she said. “All set.”

“Almost,” Mama said kindly. She pointed to a spoon for stirring honey. It shined like a tiny sunbeam. “Would you like to place the spoon?”

“Yes,” Dottie said. She looked at the bowl of honey. She thought about sticky drips. She thought about neatness. She put the spoon on a small saucer next to the honey jar, so drips would have a place to land. “This keeps the table clean,” Dottie explained.

Mama’s eyes sparkled. “You made a smart plan.”

They sat down together. Dottie looked around. The plate was ready. The napkin waited like a gentle friend. The cup stood tall. The berries glowed red and blue. The honey shone gold. Everything looked calm and cozy.

Dottie took a sip of water. “I feel proud,” she said in a small, happy voice.

“You worked with a clear goal,” Mama said. “You discovered where things belong. You finished every step. How did you do it?”

Dottie thought. “I looked. I listened. I tried. I changed my idea when I needed to. I took small steps.”

Mama smiled. “That is how we learn every day.”

They ate toast with berries and a drizzle of honey. The toast was crunchy. The berries popped with sweet juice. The honey tasted warm and sunny. Dottie’s tummy felt full, and her heart felt even fuller.

When they finished, Dottie had one more idea. “I can clear the table too,” she said. “I will start with the cup, then the plate, then the napkin. Step by step.”

She did it slowly and safely, humming a little song. Hum, hum, hum. She wiped the table until it felt smooth. Swish, swish.

Mama hugged Dottie. “You completed your tasks. You met your goal. And you made new discoveries along the way.”

Dottie nuzzled Mama’s cheek. “Discovery helps me learn,” she said. “Little steps make big goals come true.”

They looked at the clean, sunny dining room. It smelled like berries and soap and a tiny bit of honey. It felt peaceful and bright.

Dottie smiled. “Tomorrow, I will set a new goal,” she said. “I will discover something new.”

The End.
