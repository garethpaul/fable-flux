# Whiskers the Safety Helper

Whiskers was a very clever orange cat with bright green eyes and soft, fluffy fur. She lived in a cozy house with the Johnson family, and her favorite place to explore was the big pantry where all the yummy food was kept.

One sunny morning, Whiskers heard little Tommy Johnson crying in the kitchen. "Mama! Mama! I want cookies!" he sobbed. Tommy was only two years old and very small.

Whiskers padded quietly into the kitchen on her soft paws. She saw Tommy trying to climb up on a wobbly chair to reach the cookie jar on the high pantry shelf. The chair was tipping back and forth - it looked very dangerous!

"Meow! Meow!" Whiskers called loudly, trying to warn Tommy. But the little boy didn't understand cat language.

Whiskers knew she had to help keep Tommy safe. She was a very clever cat, so she thought carefully about what to do. "I need to be patient and find a safe way to help," she said to herself.

First, Whiskers gently rubbed against Tommy's legs with her warm, furry body. "Purr, purr," she said softly, trying to get him to step down from the dangerous chair.

Tommy giggled and bent down to pet Whiskers. "Nice kitty!" he said, stepping safely onto the floor.

But Tommy still wanted those cookies! He started to climb again. Whiskers knew she needed a better plan.

The clever cat remembered that Mrs. Johnson always kept a special step stool in the pantry - one that was safe and sturdy for little children to use. Whiskers ran into the pantry, where it smelled like cinnamon and vanilla and all sorts of wonderful foods.

"Meow! Meow!" she called from inside the pantry, trying to get Tommy to follow her.

Tommy toddled after Whiskers, curious about what the cat was doing. In the pantry, Whiskers sat right next to the safe step stool and patted it with her paw.

"Oh!" said Tommy, understanding. He climbed carefully onto the sturdy step stool. It didn't wobble at all - it was much safer than the chair!

Just then, Mrs. Johnson came into the kitchen. "Tommy! There you are!" she said. "And Whiskers, what a good helper you are!"

Mrs. Johnson saw the wobbly chair in the kitchen and the safe step stool in the pantry. "Did Whiskers show you the safe way to reach things?" she asked Tommy.

"Kitty helped!" Tommy clapped his hands happily.

Mrs. Johnson gave Tommy one cookie and gave Whiskers a special treat. "Whiskers, you're such a clever cat. You helped keep Tommy safe and showed him patience. That's what good community helpers do - they look out for each other!"

Whiskers purred loudly, feeling proud and happy. Her orange fur felt warm in the sunny kitchen, and her green eyes sparkled with joy.

From that day on, whenever Tommy wanted something from the pantry, he always used the safe step stool. And Whiskers always stayed nearby, watching carefully to make sure everyone was safe.

"Being patient and thinking about safety helps everyone," Whiskers purred to herself. "That's what being a good helper is all about!"

The End.
