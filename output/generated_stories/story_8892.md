# Fawn's Fair Share

Little Fawn loved her special closet hideaway. It was cozy and warm, with soft blankets piled high and her favorite stuffed animals arranged in a perfect circle. The closet smelled like lavender from Mama's sachets, and golden sunlight peeked through the crack under the door.

One morning, Fawn heard crying from outside her hideaway. She poked her spotted head out and saw three little animals sitting sadly in the hallway.

"What's wrong?" asked Fawn gently, stepping out with her hooves clicking softly on the wooden floor.

Rabbit sniffled. "We don't have anywhere special to play. The big animals took our favorite spots."

"Bear said the sunny meadow is only for big animals now," squeaked Mouse, her tiny whiskers trembling.

Raccoon's striped tail drooped. "And Fox claimed the reading nook is just for animals with red fur. That's not fair!"

Fawn's heart felt heavy. She knew this wasn't right. Everyone deserved a special place to play, no matter how big they were or what color their fur was.

"That doesn't sound fair at all," said Fawn thoughtfully. "Fair means everyone gets treated the same way. Come with me!"

Fawn led her new friends to find Bear, who was lounging in the sunny meadow all by himself. The grass felt warm and tickly under their feet.

"Bear," said Fawn politely, "this meadow is big enough for everyone. Sharing makes everything more fun!"

Bear grumbled. "But I was here first!"

"Being first doesn't mean you get everything," explained Fawn. "Fair means we take turns and share. Would you like to play a game with all of us?"

Bear's ears perked up. He had been feeling lonely all by himself.

Next, they found Fox curled up alone in the reading nook, surrounded by colorful books.

"Fox," said Fawn kindly, "books are for everyone to enjoy, not just animals with red fur. Mouse loves stories too!"

Fox looked surprised. "Really? I thought only animals like me could understand the good books."

"That's silly!" giggled Rabbit. "We all love different stories, and that makes sharing even more exciting! Mouse knows the best adventure tales."

Mouse's eyes sparkled. "I do! I know one about a brave little ant!"

Fawn smiled warmly. "How about we all share the reading nook? We can take turns picking stories, and everyone can bring their favorite book."

Soon, all the animals were gathered in Fawn's closet hideaway. The space felt magical with everyone together. Rabbit brought crunchy carrots to share, Mouse contributed her softest blanket, Raccoon shared his collection of shiny buttons, and Fox brought his favorite books.

"This is much better than being alone," admitted Bear, munching on a carrot.

"When we share and treat everyone fairly, we all have more fun," said Fox, reading a story in his gentle voice.

Fawn looked around at all her new friends snuggled together in the cozy closet. The air was filled with happy giggles and the rustling of turning pages.

"Being fair means everyone gets to join in," said Fawn. "It doesn't matter if you're big or small, what color you are, or where you come from. Everyone deserves kindness and a chance to play."

From that day on, all the animals shared their special spaces. The meadow, the reading nook, and Fawn's closet hideaway became places where everyone was welcome. They learned that fairness, sharing, and kindness made everything better for everyone.

The End.
