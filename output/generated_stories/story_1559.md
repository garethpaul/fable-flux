# Panda's Fair Garden Day

Penny the panda woke up to sunshine streaming through her window. She stretched her fluffy black and white arms and smiled. "What a beautiful day for gardening!" she said softly.

Penny lived in a cozy apartment with a special balcony garden. She had planted colorful flowers, green herbs, and tiny vegetables in pretty pots. Every morning, she loved to water her plants and watch them grow.

As Penny stepped onto her balcony, she breathed in the fresh morning air. The sweet smell of lavender tickled her nose, and she could hear birds chirping in the distance. Her garden looked lovely, but something seemed different today.

"Oh my!" Penny gasped. She noticed that some of her plants looked thirsty and droopy, while others looked perfectly fine. The tomato plant in the sunny corner was bright and perky, but the flowers near the shady wall looked sad and wilted.

Penny sat down on her little garden stool and thought carefully. "This isn't fair," she said to herself. "Some plants are getting more water and sunshine than others."

She remembered what her grandmother had taught her: "To be fair means everyone gets what they need, not just what's easy to give."

Penny decided to prepare properly for her garden care. First, she got her special watering can, her soft gardening gloves, and her small notebook. She walked slowly around her balcony, looking carefully at each plant.

"Hello, little tomato plant," she said gently, touching its leaves. "You feel nice and moist. You don't need water yet."

Then she moved to the shady corner. "Oh, poor little flowers," she whispered. "Your soil feels dry and crumbly. You need extra water today."

Penny wrote notes in her notebook about each plant. Some needed water, some needed to be moved to sunnier spots, and some needed their dead leaves picked off.

"Being fair takes time and attention," Penny said as she filled her watering can. "But it's the right thing to do."

She started with the thirstiest plants, giving them long, gentle drinks. The water sparkled like tiny diamonds in the morning light. She could hear the happy bubbling sound as the dry soil soaked up the water.

Next, Penny carefully moved some pots to better spots. The small herb plants went to the sunny side, while the big leafy plants went where they could have some shade.

"There," she said, wiping her furry paws on her apron. "Now everyone has what they need to grow strong and healthy."

As Penny worked, she felt peaceful and calm. Taking care of her garden this way made her heart feel warm and happy. She noticed how the colors seemed brighter and the air smelled even sweeter.

A little butterfly landed on her newly watered flowers. "Thank you, Penny," it seemed to say as it danced from bloom to bloom.

By lunchtime, Penny's balcony garden looked beautiful and balanced. All the plants stood tall and happy, each one getting exactly what it needed.

Penny sat in her favorite garden chair and smiled. "When we're fair and take time to prepare properly, everyone benefits," she said. "My garden is happy, and I feel good inside too."

She closed her eyes and listened to the gentle sounds of her thriving garden, feeling proud that she had learned such an important lesson about fairness and caring.

The End.
