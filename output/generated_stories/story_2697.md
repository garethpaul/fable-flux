# Birdie Builds a Story

In a sunny dining room, with a round table and a soft rug, lived a singing bird named Birdie. Birdie was small and bright blue, with a tiny yellow beak that loved to sing. Every morning, Birdie perched on the back of a chair and sang, “Tweet-a-lee, tweet-a-loo!” The sound felt warm like toast and smelled sweet like the fresh oranges in a bowl.

One morning, Birdie hopped along the table and noticed a basket full of safe, soft blocks. The blocks were red, blue, yellow, and green. Some were smooth squares. Some were long and tall. Birdie fluffed her feathers and chirped, “Today, I will build!”

From the hallway, the floor made a gentle creak. Little feet pattered. Maya, a kind child with curly hair, peeked in. “Hi, Birdie!” Maya said, smiling. “What are you building?”

Birdie sang, “Tweet! A tower! And a story too!”

Maya’s eyes grew round. “A story tower?”

Birdie nodded and hopped closer. “Yes! We build with blocks. We build with words. We build with bodies that move.”

Maya laughed and clapped. “Let’s do it!”

They sat on the soft rug. Birdie nudged a red block with her beak. “First, we make a strong base,” Birdie said. “A base helps us stand tall.”

Maya put down two blue squares. “Like this?”

“Tweet-perfect!” Birdie cheered. “We are builders.”

They stacked a yellow block and a green block. The tower grew as tall as Birdie’s wings. Birdie sang a steady song, “Slow and strong, slow and strong.” The song sounded like a drum, bump-bum, bump-bum, helping them remember to move carefully.

Maya asked, “Why do we go slow?”

“To keep it steady,” said Birdie. “When we build, we think, we try, we move with care.”

Maya nodded. She placed a long block on top. It wobbled. “Oh!” she gasped.

Birdie flapped her wings just a tiny bit. “Let’s try again,” Birdie said gently. “We can fix. Builders fix.”

They took a deep breath together—big in, warm out. Birdie’s feathers shivered with the soft whoosh. Then Maya moved the long block to the middle. It felt smooth, and it sat flat. “We did it!” Maya laughed. The smell of toast floated from the kitchen, and the room felt safe and happy.

“Now,” said Birdie, “let’s build a story to go with our tower. Stories help our ideas stand tall too.”

Maya sat crisscross on the rug. “Once upon a time,” she said, “there was a little tower in a dining room.”

Birdie added, “And a singing bird who loved to help.”

Maya giggled. “The tower wanted to be a castle!”

Birdie hopped in a circle. “Tweet! Movement time! Let’s move like builders. Tap, tap, tap!” Birdie tapped the rug with her feet. Maya tapped with her fingers—tap, tap, tap. They moved their arms like strong cranes, lifting blocks in the air. They stretched up tall like the tower, then crouched low like a base. Their bodies felt warm and wiggly, and they both smiled.

As they moved, the tower wobbled again. “Uh-oh,” Maya said. “I think it needs help.”

Birdie looked closely. The top was heavy. “We can solve this,” Birdie said. “We can try a new plan.”

Maya took off the top block carefully. “New plan,” she repeated.

Birdie said, “Let’s build wide at the bottom and small at the top.”

Maya placed two new blocks at the base. Birdie hummed a calm tune like a soft breeze: “Mmm-mmm, steady and ready.” The tower felt stronger. They added a small block to the top. It stayed!

“Yay!” Maya clapped, and Birdie twirled. “Our castle is ready for our story.”

Maya continued, “The castle had a door.” She set a block like a little door.

Birdie sang, “And the door opened—creeeak!” Birdie made the sound so silly that Maya giggled. “Inside lived friends who helped each other.”

Maya said, “They used kind words and careful hands.”

Birdie nodded. “And when something fell, they didn’t give up. They tried again.”

Maya looked at the tower, then at Birdie. “Birdie, we built with blocks. We built a story. We built with our bodies. We built with our hearts.”

“Tweet-yes,” Birdie sang. “Building is about trying, planning, and sharing.”

Just then, Maya’s elbow bumped the tower. The top block slipped and plop! It fell onto the rug. Maya’s eyes got big. “Oh no!”

Birdie chirped calmly, “It’s okay. We can fix. Let’s breathe and rebuild.”

They took another big breath—big in, warm out. Maya picked up the block. “I can try again,” she said softly.

“You can,” Birdie sang. “I believe in you.”

Together, they set the block back on top, nice and easy. The tower stood tall and proud. The room felt bright, and the air smelled like oranges and toast again. Maya smiled. “We did it!”

Birdie puffed with pride. “What did we learn today?”

Maya counted on her fingers. “One: Build a strong base. Two: Go slow and steady. Three: Try again when it falls. Four: Tell a story to guide our plan. Five: Move our bodies to help our minds.”

Birdie sang, “Tweet-a-lee, tweet-a-loo! Builders think, move, and share too!”

They sat by their tower, finishing their story: “And the friends in the dining room lived happily, helping each other build big dreams, one careful step at a time.”

The End.
