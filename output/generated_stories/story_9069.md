# Ruby Rabbit and the Laundry Day

Ruby Rabbit hopped into the laundry room with a small basket in her paws. The room smelled warm and soapy. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” like a big sleepy bumblebee. The dryer was quiet, round and shiny like a silver moon.

Ruby’s ears stood tall. “Today I do my own laundry,” she said. “I am a big rabbit. I can try.”

She took a deep breath. The air felt warm on her whiskers. She wiggled her nose and looked at the buttons. “Start. Stop. Gentle.” There were so many!

Her mom peeked in from the hallway. “Do you want help, Ruby?”

Ruby thought about her heart. It felt brave and bouncy. “I want to try by myself first,” she said. “Please watch me from the door.”

Mom nodded. “I’m here if you need me.”

Ruby placed her little red scarf into the washing machine. She added her striped socks, her blue shorts, and her favorite yellow T-shirt. She read the small picture labels Mom had taught her. “Cold for colors,” she whispered. She picked up the bottle of soap. The soap smelled like lemons. “Mmm, zesty,” she giggled.

She remembered the measure cup. “Not too much,” she told herself. She poured the soap slowly. Drip, drip, drip. “That is enough.” She put the cup down and wiped a tiny bubble from her paw. The bubble popped with a soft “pff.”

Ruby pressed the “Gentle” button. The machine began to swish. “Swish, swish, whirr,” it sang. Ruby’s chest felt proud. “I did it!”

Just then, Ruby heard a small sniffle. She turned and saw her neighbor, Tiny Tortoise, at the doorway. Tiny held a torn washcloth. “H-hi, Ruby,” Tiny said. “I spilled juice on my cloth. I don’t know what to do.”

Ruby’s ears perked. She wanted to keep watching her own wash. But she also felt a warm tug in her heart. “I can be kind,” she thought. “I can still do my job and help, too.”

“Come in, Tiny,” said Ruby. “Let’s clean it together. I am learning to do laundry. We can learn together.”

Tiny stepped in slowly. His shell made a quiet tap-tap on the tile. “I’m scared of the loud swish,” he whispered.

Ruby nodded. “It is loud. But we can take deep breaths.” She breathed in and out. “In… and out.” Tiny copied her. They both smiled.

Ruby showed Tiny the stain spray. “We spray the spot,” she said. “Just a little spritz.” She squeezed the sprayer. “Psst!” The lemon smell floated up. Tiny wrinkled his nose and giggled. “It smells like sunshine!”

“Now we rub gently,” Ruby said. Tiny dabbed with his small claw. “Good job,” Ruby cheered. “You are careful.”

The machine beeped. “Beep-beep!” The wash was done.

Ruby opened the door. Steam puffed out, warm and soft like a hug. She picked up her clothes. Drip, drip, drip went the water. She shook them. Swoosh! “Now we put things in the dryer,” she said. “But not the red scarf. It might shrink.”

Tiny watched closely. “How do you know?” he asked.

“I ask questions and listen,” said Ruby. “Mom taught me. I remember. That helps me be independent.” She folded the red scarf and set it on the counter. “I will hang this later.”

Tiny held up his washcloth. “Dryer or no dryer?”

Ruby checked the little tag. “It says okay. Let’s put it in.” She turned the dryer knob to “Low Warm.” The knob clicked-clicked under her paw. “Click, click, click.” Ruby smiled. “Low Warm is gentle.”

The dryer started. “Thump, whirr, whoosh.” Tiny flinched, then stood taller. “I can do brave breaths,” he said. “In… and out.”

While they waited, Ruby cleaned up a drip of soap on the floor. She wiped the counter. She closed the soap lid tight. Tiny watched and asked, “Why do you do that?”

“This is our shared room,” Ruby said. “Everyone uses it. We take care of it. That is being a good neighbor.” Tiny nodded slowly. “Civic responsibility,” he said carefully. The words felt big, but Ruby smiled. “Yes. We help our place stay safe and clean.”

The dryer beeped. “Beep-beep-beep!” Ruby opened the door. Warm air whooshed out, like a toastie breeze. She and Tiny reached in. The towels felt fluffy. The shirts felt soft. The washcloth was clean and bright.

Tiny hugged his cloth to his chest. “Thank you, Ruby,” he said. “You helped me, and you let me help, too.”

Ruby’s cheeks felt warm and happy. “You’re welcome, Tiny. You were brave. You did a lot yourself.”

Mom stepped closer with a smile. “I am proud of you, Ruby,” she said. “You tried on your own. You asked for help when needed. You helped a friend. You cleaned up.”

Ruby’s heart felt big and light. She folded her clothes. Fold, pat. Fold, pat. Tiny folded his cloth, too. It looked like a neat little square.

Ruby hung her red scarf by a sunny window. The scarf dripped a tiny “plip-plip” into a bowl. The room smelled clean and bright.

“I can do my laundry,” Ruby said, standing tall. “I can be kind. I can care for our home.”

Tiny nodded. “I can try new things, and I can ask for help.”

Ruby looked at the tidy room. She felt proud, grateful, and calm. “Thank you, machines,” she said softly. “Thank you, Mom. Thank you, Tiny.”

Mom laughed. “And thank you, Ruby, for being responsible.”

Ruby hopped once, happy and sure. “Today I learned I can do things by myself. I can help others, and I can take care of our shared space. That makes me feel big on the inside.”

The End.
