# Benny Beaver's Story Circle

Benny Beaver sat at his tiny desk in Mama's home office. His fluffy brown tail tapped nervously on the floor. Today he wanted to tell stories to his friends, but he felt scared.

"I don't know any good stories," Benny whispered to himself. He looked at the big bookshelf filled with colorful books. "What if my friends don't like what I say?"

Mama Beaver looked up from her computer. "What's wrong, little builder?" she asked with a warm smile.

"I want to tell stories like you do at work," Benny said. His small paws fidgeted with a pencil. "But I don't know how to make them good."

"Ah!" Mama's eyes twinkled. "Let me show you something special." She pulled out a world map covered in bright pictures. "Stories come from everywhere, Benny. Let's plan together."

Benny's nose twitched with curiosity. The map smelled like adventure and far-away places.

"See this blue part?" Mama pointed to the ocean. "In Japan, children tell stories about brave fish." She moved her finger to green mountains. "In Peru, families share tales about dancing llamas."

"Really?" Benny's tail stopped tapping. His button eyes grew wide.

"And here," Mama pointed to red deserts, "children in Morocco tell stories about magic carpets that fly through the clouds."

Benny clapped his little paws together. "Wow! Stories are everywhere!"

"Now," Mama said, "let's practice. What do you see outside our window?"

Benny looked out at their backyard pond. He saw water lilies floating like tiny boats. A family of ducks paddled by, quacking softly. The afternoon sun made everything sparkle like diamonds.

"I see... I see a duck family!" Benny said excitedly.

"Perfect! Now, where might they be going?" Mama encouraged.

Benny thought hard. His whiskers wiggled as he planned. "Maybe... maybe they're swimming to visit their grandmother duck in the next pond!"

"Wonderful! What happens next?"

"The baby ducks get tired," Benny continued, his voice growing stronger. "So the mama duck teaches them a special swimming song from her grandmother's pond in Canada!"

Mama clapped her paws. "Benny, that's a beautiful story! You included family, travel, and different places - just like the stories from around the world."

Benny felt warm and proud inside. His tail began its happy tapping again.

"Can I tell it to my friends now?" he asked.

"Of course! Remember, every story is special when it comes from your heart," Mama said. "And every place has wonderful tales to share."

Benny hugged his mama tight. He smelled her familiar scent of wood chips and love. "Thank you for helping me plan my story!"

That afternoon, Benny gathered his friends by the pond. He told them about the duck family's musical journey. His friends listened with big smiles and asked for more stories.

"Tomorrow, I'll tell you about a little beaver who builds bridges to connect different countries!" Benny announced proudly.

As the sun set, Benny felt happy and confident. He learned that stories could come from anywhere - his backyard, his imagination, and places all around the world. With a little planning and practice, he could share wonderful adventures with everyone.

The End.
