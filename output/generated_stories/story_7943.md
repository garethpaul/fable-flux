# Nutkin's Big Feelings Day

In a cozy study corner filled with soft pillows and colorful books, lived a little squirrel named Nutkin. The study corner had warm yellow walls, a fluffy reading rug, and shelves lined with picture books that smelled like vanilla and adventure.

One sunny morning, Nutkin felt very excited. "Today I want to read ALL the books!" he chattered, his bushy tail twitching with energy. He grabbed three books at once, then four more, then two puzzles, and some crayons too.

But as Nutkin tried to do everything at the same time, he felt his heart beating faster and faster. The books scattered everywhere. The puzzle pieces went flying. His crayon rolled under the bookshelf.

"Oh no!" squeaked Nutkin, feeling overwhelmed. His whiskers trembled, and his little paws felt shaky. "Everything is too much!"

Just then, his friend Owl swooped down from the top shelf. "Nutkin, I can see you're having big feelings," Owl said gently, her voice soft like a warm hug. "Would you like to try something special with me?"

Nutkin nodded, his eyes wide and worried.

"Let's sit on our special breathing pillow," Owl suggested, pointing to a fluffy blue cushion. "Close your eyes and imagine you're smelling a beautiful flower."

Nutkin sat down and took a slow, deep breath through his nose. "I can smell something sweet, like the flowers by the window," he whispered.

"Good! Now blow out slowly, like you're cooling down hot soup," Owl said.

Nutkin puffed out his cheeks and breathed out slowly. "Whooooo," he sighed, feeling his shoulders relax.

They breathed together three more times. With each breath, Nutkin felt calmer and more peaceful.

"How do you feel now?" asked Owl.

"Better," Nutkin said, opening his bright eyes. "My heart isn't racing anymore."

"Wonderful! Now, let's think together. What would you like to do first?" Owl asked.

Nutkin looked around at all the scattered books and toys. "I think... I want to read just one book first. The red one about the friendly bear."

"That sounds perfect," Owl smiled. "And after you finish that one?"

"Then maybe I can do the puzzle. One thing at a time," Nutkin said proudly, his tail swishing happily.

As Nutkin read his book, he felt calm and focused. The words seemed clearer, and the pictures looked brighter. When he finished, he carefully put the book back on the shelf before getting his puzzle.

Later that day, some baby rabbits hopped into the study corner, looking upset and scattered just like Nutkin had been.

"I know how to help!" Nutkin said confidently. "Come sit with me on the breathing pillow."

Nutkin taught the little rabbits the flower-breathing trick. "Smell the flower... cool the soup," he said gently, just like Owl had taught him.

Soon all the baby rabbits were calm and smiling.

"You're a wonderful teacher, Nutkin," Owl said proudly. "You learned to help yourself, and now you're helping others too."

Nutkin's chest puffed up with pride. "When I have big feelings, I can breathe and think. Then I can choose what to do next!"

From that day on, whenever Nutkin felt overwhelmed, he remembered his special breathing spot. He learned that taking care of his feelings helped him think clearly and be a good friend to others.

The End.
