# Whiskers Builds a Bridge

Whiskers was a clever orange cat who loved to explore. One sunny morning, she padded into Grandpa Joe's garage workshop. The workshop smelled like wood shavings and paint, and sunlight streamed through the dusty windows.

"Meow!" Whiskers said, looking around at all the wonderful tools hanging on the walls. There were hammers, screwdrivers, and colorful blocks of wood stacked high.

Suddenly, Whiskers heard a tiny voice crying. "Help! Help!"

She followed the sound with her keen ears. There, on the other side of the workshop, she found three little toy mice trapped on a high shelf. Between Whiskers and the mice was a big gap where a wooden board had fallen down.

"Oh no!" squeaked the first mouse. "We can't get down!"

"The bridge is broken!" cried the second mouse.

"We're scared!" whimpered the third mouse.

Whiskers' whiskers twitched as she thought hard. She was a little scared too – the gap looked big, and she had never built anything before. But the mice needed her help!

"Don't worry, little friends," Whiskers called out. "I'll build you a new bridge!"

First, Whiskers looked around the workshop. She saw wooden planks, some rope, and a small hammer that was just her size. Her heart beat fast, but she took a deep breath.

"I can do this," she said to herself. "Step by step."

Whiskers carefully dragged a wooden plank with her teeth. It was heavy! The wood felt rough against her paws, and she had to rest twice. But she didn't give up.

"You're so brave, Whiskers!" called the mice.

Next, she found some rope. Using her clever paws, she tied one end of the rope around the plank. It was tricky work, and she had to try three times before the knot felt strong.

"Almost done!" Whiskers purred, feeling proud of her hard work.

The hardest part was next. Whiskers had to push the wooden plank across the gap. She put her shoulder against it and pushed with all her might. Her paws slipped on the smooth garage floor.

"Oof!" she grunted. This was harder than she thought!

But Whiskers remembered what Grandpa Joe always said: "Big jobs are just lots of little jobs put together."

So she pushed the plank just a tiny bit. Then a little more. Then a little more. Slowly, slowly, the plank moved across the gap.

"It's working!" squeaked the mice excitedly.

Finally, the plank reached the other side. Whiskers had built a perfect bridge! She tested it carefully with one paw. It felt strong and safe.

"Come on down!" Whiskers called.

One by one, the three little mice scurried across the bridge. They were so happy, they did little mouse dances on the workshop floor.

"Thank you, Whiskers!" they squeaked. "You saved us!"

"You were so brave!" said the first mouse.

"And you didn't give up!" said the second mouse.

"You're the best builder ever!" said the third mouse.

Whiskers felt warm and proud inside. She had been scared at first, but she had been brave. She had learned that building something takes patience and trying again when things get hard.

Just then, Grandpa Joe walked into the workshop. "Well, look at that!" he smiled. "Someone built a perfect bridge. Good job, Whiskers!"

Whiskers purred loudly. She had learned that even when something seems scary or hard, you can do it if you take it step by step and don't give up.

The End.
