# Daisy Fawn's Green Garden Day

Daisy Fawn stepped carefully into the sunny backyard. Her spotted brown coat sparkled in the warm morning light. She sniffed the fresh air with her tiny black nose.

"What a beautiful day!" Daisy said, stretching her long legs.

But something made her sad. Plastic bottles lay scattered on the green grass. Empty cans rolled in the gentle breeze. Candy wrappers stuck to the pretty flowers.

"Oh no," Daisy whispered. "This yard looks so messy. The flowers can't breathe with all this trash."

Daisy's big brown eyes filled with worry. She loved this backyard. Butterflies danced here. Bees buzzed from flower to flower. But now everything looked sad and dirty.

"I want to help," Daisy decided. "But I'm just one little fawn. Can I really make a difference?"

She walked slowly around the yard. Her hooves made soft sounds on the path. Click, click, click. The sun felt warm on her back, but her heart felt heavy.

Then Daisy heard a tiny voice. "Help! Help!"

She looked down. A small ladybug was trapped under a plastic bag.

"Don't worry, little friend," Daisy said gently. She used her nose to lift the bag away. "There you go!"

"Thank you!" buzzed the ladybug. "That plastic was so heavy. I couldn't fly to my favorite flower."

Daisy smiled. "I helped one friend. Maybe I can help more!"

She picked up the plastic bag with her teeth. It tasted yucky, but Daisy didn't give up. She carried it to the recycling bin. Plop! In it went.

"One piece of trash gone!" Daisy cheered.

Next, she found three cans. They were shiny and made loud noises when they rolled. Clank, clank, clank! Daisy pushed them with her nose, one by one, to the recycling bin.

"Two, three, four pieces gone!" she counted happily.

A blue jay landed nearby. "What are you doing, little fawn?"

"I'm cleaning up," Daisy explained. "The yard looks sad with all this trash. The flowers need clean soil to grow."

"That's very kind," said the blue jay. "Can I help too?"

"Yes, please!" Daisy's tail wiggled with joy.

The blue jay picked up candy wrappers in his beak. Daisy gathered more bottles and cans. Together, they worked in the warm sunshine.

Soon, a gray squirrel scampered down from a tree. "This looks like important work. May I join you?"

"Of course!" Daisy and the blue jay said together.

The squirrel was very good at finding small pieces of trash hidden under leaves. His busy paws moved quickly.

After an hour, the backyard looked completely different. The grass was bright green again. The flowers stood tall and proud. The sun made everything sparkle like jewels.

"Look what we did together!" Daisy exclaimed, spinning in a happy circle.

The ladybug flew over. "Thank you, friends! Now I can visit all my favorite flowers. The air smells so much better!"

Bees buzzed contentedly from bloom to bloom. Butterflies danced in the clean, fresh air. Birds sang cheerful songs in the trees.

"We took care of our home," Daisy said proudly. "When we work together and care for the Earth, everyone feels better."

The blue jay nodded. "Every piece of trash we cleaned up helps the plants and animals."

"And it's fair for everyone to have a clean, safe place to live," added the squirrel.

Daisy looked around the beautiful, clean backyard. She felt warm and happy inside. "Taking care of our world is the kindest thing we can do. And it's so much more fun when friends help together!"

The End.
