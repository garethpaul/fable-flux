# The Clean Beat Bounce

Thump-thump! Thump-thump! A joyful kangaroo joey named Kiki bounced into the musical practice room. The room smelled like shiny brass and fresh wood. Drums lined the walls. A piano sat by the window. A triangle sparkled on a soft blue rug.

Kiki wiggled her ears. “I’m ready to make music!” she cheered.

Her friends waved. There was Tilly the timid turtle, Benji the busy beaver, and Momo the mellow monkey. Their teacher, Miss Bell, smiled kindly. “Hello, musicians,” she said. “Today we learn a new song. We start in ten minutes. Let’s get ready.”

Kiki clapped. “Ten minutes! We have time to practice!”

She hopped to the drum and lifted the sticks. But her paws felt sticky. “Hmm?” She sniffed. “Phew.” Her paws smelled like the peanut butter snack she had eaten outside.

Benji wrinkled his nose. “I smell… peanut butter drums.”

Momo giggled softly. “Sticky sticks make squeaky sounds,” he said.

Kiki tapped the drum. Squeak. Smudge. The drumhead looked cloudy. Tilly pulled into her shell. “Oh dear,” she whispered. “The drum is getting messy.”

Kiki’s ears drooped. “I didn’t mean to make a mess.”

Miss Bell knelt and spoke gently. “Sometimes our hands carry food and germs. Instruments like to stay clean. Clean hands make clean music.”

Kiki’s eyes grew round. “Germs? Are they tiny?”

“Yes,” said Miss Bell, nodding. “We can’t see them, but washing helps. We have ten minutes before we start. Who will be the Hygiene Helper?”

Kiki felt her heart thump in a brave, bouncy way. She raised her paw. “Me! I can help!”

Miss Bell smiled. “Wonderful, Kiki. Lead the team.”

Kiki took a deep breath. In-out. Calm and slow. She remembered: calm bodies make clear plans. “Friends,” she said, “let’s wash our hands before we play. That way our music will be bright and clean.”

Tilly peeked out. “Can we do it together?”

“Yes,” said Kiki. “We will line up. We will count slowly. We will sing while we wash, so we use our time well.”

They padded to the sink in the practice room. Water rushed, warm and gentle. Kiki turned the tap and felt the smooth water on her paws. “Ahh,” she sighed. “It feels nice.”

She pumped soap. Squish! Bubbles popped like tiny drums. “Let’s sing the Clean Beat Song,” Kiki said.

“Scrub, scrub, scrub-a-dub,
Fronts and backs,
Between each nub.
Thumbs go round,
Fingers too,
Rinse, rinse, rinse,
All clean for you!”

They sang it twice. The soapy smell was lemony and bright. Momo hummed a calm note. Benji giggled when bubbles tickled his fur. Tilly smiled and swished her little hands with care.

When it was Kiki’s turn to dry, she patted her paws with a soft towel. “Dry, dry, ready to try,” she whispered.

Kiki looked at the clock. “We used our time well. We still have three minutes to set up,” she said proudly. Her friends nodded. Leadership felt warm in Kiki’s chest, like sunshine.

Back at the instruments, Kiki picked up the drumsticks again. Tap-tap! The drum sounded clear and crisp. No squeaks. No smudges. “Listen!” she said. “So clean!”

Miss Bell clapped softly. “Lovely. Clean hands help our instruments last. Clean hands keep us healthy too.”

But then—Momo sneezed. Achoo! He reached for the triangle. Kiki remembered the lesson. She stayed calm and kind. “Momo, let’s wipe the triangle and wash again, just in case,” she said.

Momo nodded. “Good idea.” He used a gentle wipe for the triangle and went to the sink. Kiki felt proud. “We can be Hygiene Helpers together,” she said.

The group gathered. Miss Bell raised her baton. “Ready, musicians?”

“Ready!” they cheered.

They began to play. Tap from Kiki, ting from Momo, gentle thump from Tilly’s soft drum, and plink-plonk from Benji on the piano keys. The room filled with bright sounds. It smelled clean and lemony. Their music felt happy and smooth, like hopping on a soft path.

Halfway through, Kiki saw a little smear on the drumstick from the floor. She paused calmly. “Time for a quick clean,” she whispered. She used a safe wipe from the basket, then counted, “One, two,” and rejoined right on beat. That was smart time-management.

The song ended with a sparkling ting. Everyone smiled.

Miss Bell bowed. “Wonderful music. Kiki, thank you for leading us. You showed calmness. You used your time well. You taught your friends about hygiene.”

Kiki’s tail flicked with joy. “Clean hands, clean plans, clean bands!” she said, giggling.

Benji laughed. “Let’s always sing the Clean Beat Song.”

Tilly nodded. “I like how my hands feel. Soft and safe.”

Momo gave a thumbs-up. “I like how the triangle sounds now.”

Kiki looked around the shiny room. She felt proud, gentle, and strong. “When we wash our hands, we care for ourselves, our friends, and our instruments,” she said. “That is good leadership.”

Miss Bell added, “And good education. You learned, you shared, and you helped.”

Kiki bounced once, softly. “I will be a Hygiene Helper every day.”

Everyone clapped. The music room glowed with happy notes and clean sounds.

The End.
