# Ollie Otter's Garden Helper Day

Ollie Otter loved to play! He splashed in puddles, rolled in the soft grass, and chased butterflies around the garden. But today was different. Today, Farmer Ben needed Ollie's help in the garden shed.

"Ollie," called Farmer Ben, his voice gentle and kind. "I need to go to town. Can you help me organize the garden shed while I'm gone?"

Ollie's whiskers twitched with excitement. "Yes! Yes! I can help!" he squeaked, bouncing on his little paws.

Farmer Ben smiled and pointed to a list on the shed wall. "Here are the directions, Ollie. First, put all the small tools in the red box. Then, stack the flower pots by size. Last, sweep the floor clean. Can you follow these steps?"

"I can do it!" Ollie chirped, but he was already looking at a shiny watering can that caught the morning sunlight.

After Farmer Ben left, Ollie started to follow the directions. He picked up a small trowel to put in the red box, but then he saw some colorful seed packets scattered on the shelf. "Ooh, pretty!" he said, dropping the trowel with a *clank* to look at the pictures of flowers.

Soon, Ollie was playing with the seed packets, making them into little boats. Then he noticed some garden gloves that felt soft and fuzzy. He put them on and pretended to be a superhero, running around the shed making "whoosh" sounds.

Time passed quickly. The shed grew messier as Ollie played instead of following the directions. Tools were everywhere, flower pots were scattered, and dirt covered the floor.

When Farmer Ben's truck rumbled up the driveway, Ollie suddenly remembered his job. His tummy felt funny and worried. The shed looked worse than when he started!

"Oh no," whispered Ollie, looking at the messy shed. "I didn't follow the directions. Farmer Ben will be so disappointed."

Farmer Ben walked into the shed and saw the mess. His face looked sad, not angry. "Ollie, what happened here?"

Ollie's eyes filled with tears. "I'm sorry, Farmer Ben. I got distracted and didn't follow your directions. I wanted to play instead of doing my job."

Farmer Ben knelt down and gently patted Ollie's head. "Thank you for telling me the truth, Ollie. Everyone makes mistakes. What do you think we should do now?"

Ollie wiped his eyes with his fuzzy paw. "Can I try again? This time I'll follow the directions step by step."

"That's a wonderful idea," said Farmer Ben. "I'll help you."

Together, they looked at the list. Ollie carefully picked up each small tool and placed it in the red box with a satisfying *clink*. He felt proud when the box was full of neat, organized tools.

Next, Ollie stacked the flower pots by size - big ones on the bottom, medium ones in the middle, and tiny ones on top. They looked like a perfect tower!

Finally, Ollie swept the floor, pushing the broom back and forth until every speck of dirt was gone. The clean floor sparkled in the afternoon sun.

"Look, Farmer Ben! I followed all the directions!" Ollie beamed with pride.

"You certainly did, Ollie. The shed looks wonderful. Following directions helps us finish our jobs and feel proud of our work."

Ollie learned that following directions wasn't just about rules - it was about being helpful and doing good work. From that day on, whenever someone gave him directions, Ollie listened carefully and followed each step.

And the garden shed stayed beautifully organized all summer long!

The End.
