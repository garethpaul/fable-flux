# Beary’s Gentle Words in the Basement Lab

Beary was a friendly bear cub with soft, warm fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, Beary padded down the wooden steps to the basement lab. Step, step, step. The air felt cool, like a little breeze. The light hummed softly. “Buzzzz,” sang the lights. The room smelled like clean soap and oranges.

In the lab, there were colorful bottles, safe water tubs, and big paper sheets on the walls. There was a long table with paint that was only for little paws, easy to wash. There was a chart with pictures of apples, toothbrushes, and water. Beary’s friend, Dr. Dot, waved. Dr. Dot had a kind smile and a white coat with rainbow buttons.

“Good morning, Beary,” said Dr. Dot.

“Good morning!” Beary said, wiggling a little with joy.

Today was Daily Adventure Day. Beary would learn, make art, and practice healthy habits. But most of all, today would be about respect—kind words, calm bodies, and listening ears.

Dr. Dot pointed to a big sign. It said: “We use gentle words. We use gentle paws. We take turns.”

Beary read it slowly with help. “Gentle words. Gentle paws. Take turns.” The words felt warm in Beary’s mouth.

“First,” said Dr. Dot, “we do our healthy habits. Then we make art. Then we learn new words.”

Beary nodded. “I can do that!”

They went to the sink. The water felt warm and tickly. Swish, swish. Beary washed paws, front and back, between the tiny claws. “I sing while I wash,” Beary said, and sang, “Bubbles, bubbles, go away, make my paws clean for the day.” The soap smelled like lemons. Beary felt fresh and ready.

“Great job,” said Dr. Dot. “That shows respect for your body.”

Next they looked at a basket of snacks. There were apple slices, crunchy carrots, and cool water. “May I have an apple, please?” Beary asked, using a gentle voice.

“Yes, you may,” said Dr. Dot. “Thank you for asking with respect.”

Crunch. The apple was sweet and juicy. Beary smiled. “Yum!”

Now it was art time. Beary saw bright paints: red, yellow, blue, and green. There were soft brushes and big paper. Another friend, a little robot helper named Pip, rolled in with tiny wheels. Whirr-click. Pip had a screen that showed happy faces, and a small speaker voice.

“Hello, Beary,” Pip said. “May I paint with you?”

Beary wanted the big blue brush. Pip also looked at the big blue brush.

Beary reached fast. Pip beeped, surprised. The brush almost fell. Dr. Dot caught it. “Oh dear,” said Dr. Dot gently. “Let’s pause.”

Beary felt a hot, tight feeling in the tummy. Beary wanted to cry. Beary wanted that brush. But Beary remembered the sign: Gentle words. Gentle paws. Take turns.

Beary took a deep bear breath. In through the nose. Out through the mouth. “Hooo… haaa.” The air smelled like oranges again. The tight feeling got softer.

“I’m sorry, Pip,” Beary said in a calm voice. “I grabbed. That was not respectful. May we take turns with the big blue brush?”

Pip’s screen showed a smiling face. “Thank you, Beary. Yes, we can take turns.”

“Wonderful,” said Dr. Dot. “That is respect. You used gentle words. You listened to your feelings. You breathed.”

Beary painted a big blue sky. Smooth, swooshy strokes. Pip painted a round, yellow sun. Together they added green trees and a red flower. The brush felt soft in Beary’s paw. The paint smelled a little like berries. They hummed while they painted. “Mmm-mmm,” went Beary. “Beep-boop,” went Pip.

After art, Dr. Dot showed a little book with words from different languages. “These are ways to say hello with respect,” she said.

“Hello,” said Dr. Dot.
“Hola,” said Pip.
“Bonjour,” Beary tried, slowly. The word felt funny and fun, like a hop in the mouth. Beary giggled.
“Ni hao,” said Dr. Dot.
“Jambo,” said Pip.
“Namaste,” said Beary carefully.

Beary’s ears perked up. “I like the sounds. They are like music.”

“Words can be art too,” said Dr. Dot. “When we try new words, we show respect for other people.”

They made a “Hello Mural.” Beary painted big, friendly letters. Pip added tiny stars. Dr. Dot drew a rainbow. Each hello went on the wall with a little heart.

Soon it was tidy time. Beary wanted to run and play. But Beary remembered respect for the space. “I will help,” Beary said. The water splashed as they washed brushes. Swish, swish. The cloth felt cool as they wiped the table. Wipe, wipe. The floor looked shiny and neat.

At last, they sat on soft mats. Dr. Dot passed water cups. Beary took small sips. Gulp, gulp. The water felt cool and kind to Beary’s throat.

“Beary,” said Dr. Dot, “tell us about your big moment today.”

Beary thought. “I felt mad about the blue brush. I took a breath. I used gentle words. I took turns. That felt better. Pip felt better too. That is respect.”

Pip beeped happily. “I felt safe and happy when you spoke kindly.”

Beary’s heart felt warm, like a hug. “Respect is gentle words. Respect is gentle paws. Respect is taking turns. Respect is caring for our bodies, our friends, and our lab.”

Dr. Dot clapped softly. “That is the lesson.”

Beary smiled a big bear cub smile. “I can do it tomorrow too.”

“Yes,” said Dr. Dot. “Every day.”

The lights hummed. The room smelled sweet and clean. The mural shone with hellos from all around the world. Beary waved. “Goodbye! Adiós! Au revoir!”

The day felt calm and bright. Beary’s paws were clean, the art was beautiful, and the words were kind. Beary knew: respect makes hearts feel safe and happy.

The End.
