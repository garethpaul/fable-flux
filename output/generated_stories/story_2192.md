# Melody's Big Choice

Melody was a small yellow bird who lived in Mrs. Chen's quiet attic. She had the most beautiful voice in the whole house. Every morning, she would sing sweet songs that drifted down through the floorboards.

But Melody had a big problem. The attic was full of old boxes, dusty books, and forgotten treasures scattered everywhere. Nothing had a proper place. Melody couldn't find anything when she needed it!

"Tweet, tweet! Where is my favorite shiny button?" Melody chirped, hopping from box to box. Her tiny feet made soft tapping sounds on the wooden floor.

She looked behind a pile of old photo albums. She peeked under a stack of winter clothes that smelled like lavender. But the button was nowhere to be found.

"This is too messy!" Melody sang sadly. "I can't find anything in here!"

Just then, she heard small voices coming from a corner. Three little mice were huddled together, looking worried.

"We're lost," squeaked the smallest mouse. "We can't find our way back to our cozy nest with all these boxes everywhere."

Melody felt sorry for the mice. She also felt frustrated about her messy home. Then she had an idea.

"I could organize this attic," Melody thought. "But that's a lot of work for one little bird. Should I do it alone, or should I ask for help?"

Melody made her first big decision. "Would you mice like to help me organize the attic?" she asked. "Then we can all find what we need!"

The mice's whiskers twitched with excitement. "Yes! We're very good at organizing small things," they squeaked.

Melody made her second decision. She decided to make a plan. "Let's put books with books, clothes with clothes, and toys with toys," she sang.

Together, they started working. Melody used her sharp eyes to spot items from high places. The mice used their tiny paws to sort small objects. They worked as a team.

"This dusty book goes here," chirped Melody, carrying it in her beak.

"These buttons go in this small box," squeaked the mice, pushing them carefully.

The attic filled with the sounds of their teamwork - Melody's gentle songs, the mice's happy squeaks, and the soft rustling of organized treasures.

After working all morning, they stepped back to look at their work. The attic was transformed! Everything had its own special place. Books stood neatly on shelves. Clothes were folded in labeled boxes. Toys were sorted by color and size.

"Look!" sang Melody joyfully. "I found my shiny button!" It was sitting in a small box marked "Pretty Things."

"And we found our way back to our nest!" squeaked the mice. "It's right behind the 'Winter Clothes' box!"

Melody felt proud of her decisions. She had chosen to ask for help instead of struggling alone. She had chosen to include the mice instead of sending them away. She had chosen to make a plan instead of working randomly.

"Thank you for helping me," Melody sang to her new friends. "Making decisions together made everything better!"

From that day on, Melody's morning songs sounded even more beautiful. The organized attic became a happy home for everyone. And whenever Melody faced a big choice, she remembered that good decisions often include kindness, planning, and friendship.

The mice visited Melody every day, and together they kept their attic home neat and tidy. Melody learned that making good decisions wasn't just about being independent - it was about being wise enough to include others when help was needed.

The End.
