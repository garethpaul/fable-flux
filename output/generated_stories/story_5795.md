# Melody's Big Helper Day

In the cozy guest room with yellow walls and soft blue curtains, lived a little singing bird named Melody. She had bright orange feathers and the sweetest voice in the whole house. Every morning, Melody would perch on the windowsill and sing "Tweet-tweet-good morning!" to start her day.

Today was special because the Johnson family was expecting visitors from far away places. Melody fluffed her feathers excitedly as she watched Mrs. Johnson preparing the guest room.

"Oh my!" chirped Melody, seeing all the work to be done. "The bed needs fresh sheets, the floor needs sweeping, and the welcome basket needs filling!"

Mrs. Johnson heard Melody's concerned tweet and smiled. "Would you like to help me prepare for our guests, little one? They're coming from three different countries - Japan, Mexico, and Kenya!"

Melody's eyes sparkled with curiosity. "Different countries? What does that mean?"

"Well," explained Mrs. Johnson gently, "our world is very big, with many different places where people live. Each place has special foods, languages, and traditions. Our guests will bring wonderful stories to share!"

Melody hopped excitedly. "I want to help make them feel welcome! What can I do?"

Mrs. Johnson thought carefully. "Let's work together fairly. I'll change the bed sheets, and you can help me choose special snacks for our welcome basket. Each guest might enjoy different foods from their home countries."

"That sounds fair!" sang Melody. She flew to the kitchen and carefully selected items with her tiny beak. "These rice crackers might remind our friend from Japan of home. These dried mangoes could be like the tropical fruits from Kenya. And these little cookies with cinnamon might taste like Mexican treats!"

Back in the guest room, Melody helped arrange the colorful snacks in a beautiful wicker basket. The sweet smell of cinnamon mixed with the fresh scent of clean sheets.

"You're such a thoughtful helper," praised Mrs. Johnson. "But we're not done yet. Let's make sure our room is perfectly clean."

Melody had a smart idea. "I can dust the high places with my wings while you clean the low places! That way, we'll finish faster and both do our fair share."

Together, they worked as a team. Melody's wings tickled the lampshade clean while Mrs. Johnson vacuumed the soft carpet. The room began to smell like sunshine and fresh air.

"Tweet-tweet! Look how beautiful everything looks!" sang Melody proudly.

When the guests arrived that evening, Melody perched on her windowsill and sang a welcoming song. The visitors clapped and smiled, speaking in different languages that sounded like music to Melody's ears.

"Arigato!" said the guest from Japan, tasting the rice crackers.

"¡Qué rico!" exclaimed the visitor from Mexico, enjoying the cinnamon cookies.

"Asante sana!" called the friend from Kenya, savoring the sweet mango.

Melody didn't understand the words, but she understood the happy smiles and grateful tone. Her heart felt warm and full.

That night, as Melody tucked her head under her wing, she felt proud. She had learned that the world was full of wonderful, different people who all deserved kindness and fairness. By working together and sharing the chores, she and Mrs. Johnson had created something beautiful.

"Tomorrow," Melody whispered sleepily, "I'll ask our guests to teach me songs from their countries. Music is the same everywhere - it brings joy to everyone!"

And with that happy thought, little Melody drifted off to sleep, dreaming of all the amazing places and people in the big, wonderful world.

The End.
