# Koda's Fair Play Day

Koda the koala loved the big playful living room. Soft rainbow rugs covered the floor. Colorful toy boxes lined the walls. The room smelled like fresh eucalyptus leaves and sunshine.

Every day, children from around the world came to play. There was Maya from India with her bright sari dress. Jin from Japan brought origami paper. Amara from Kenya wore beautiful beaded bracelets. Carlos from Mexico had a tiny guitar.

Koda watched from his cozy corner. His gray fur was soft and fluffy. His round black eyes sparkled with kindness.

One morning, something felt wrong. Koda's nose twitched. He heard upset voices.

"That's not fair!" cried Maya. Her voice sounded shaky.

Koda climbed down from his eucalyptus branch. He waddled over on his short legs. The children sat in a circle, but they looked sad.

"What's wrong, friends?" asked Koda gently. His voice was calm like a warm hug.

Jin pointed to the toy box. "Some children took ALL the building blocks. They won't share with anyone else."

Koda looked around. In the far corner, three bigger children had built a tall wall with blocks. Behind the wall, they had piled every single toy - the dolls, the cars, the puzzles, even the crayons.

"We can't play with anything," whispered Amara. Her beaded bracelets jingled softly.

Koda's heart felt heavy. This wasn't right. Everyone deserved to play and have fun.

"Follow me," said Koda bravely. He walked toward the wall of blocks.

"Excuse me," Koda called over the wall. His voice stayed calm and friendly. "I see you have all the toys. But look around - there are many children who want to play too."

A boy peeked over the wall. "We got here first! Finders keepers!"

Koda nodded thoughtfully. "I understand you want to play. But think about how you would feel if you came to play and found no toys left for you."

The boy's face changed. He looked confused.

"In our living room," Koda continued, "we believe everyone deserves a chance to play. Maya's family in India taught her that sharing brings joy. Jin learned in Japan that working together makes beautiful things. Amara knows from Kenya that when everyone helps, everyone wins."

Carlos strummed his tiny guitar. The gentle music filled the air.

"What if we played together?" suggested Koda. "We could build something amazing with all these blocks!"

The children behind the wall whispered to each other. Slowly, they began taking down their wall.

"We... we didn't think about the other kids," said a girl with red hair. "We were just excited about the toys."

"That's okay," smiled Koda. "Now we can fix it together."

Soon, all the children worked side by side. Maya showed everyone how to fold Jin's origami paper into colorful birds. Amara taught them a clapping game from Kenya. Carlos played music while they built a magnificent castle with the blocks.

The living room filled with laughter and happy chatter. Children shared crayons, took turns with puzzles, and helped each other reach high shelves.

Koda watched from his branch, his heart warm and full. When everyone was treated fairly, when everyone had a chance to play, the whole room became more joyful.

"Thank you, Koda," called Maya. "You helped us remember that fairness feels good for everyone!"

Koda's black nose twitched happily. He had learned something important too - standing up for what's right, even when it's hard, makes the world a better place for everyone.

As the sun set through the big windows, all the children played together peacefully. The living room sparkled with the sounds of friendship from around the world.

The End.
