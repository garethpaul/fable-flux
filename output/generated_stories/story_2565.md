# Leo the Lion Cub's Important Job

Leo was a brave little lion cub with golden fur that sparkled in the sunshine. He lived next to a cozy garden shed where his friend Mr. Rabbit kept all his garden tools. The shed smelled like fresh wood and blooming flowers, and Leo loved to visit there every day.

One sunny morning, Leo bounded over to the shed with his tail swishing happily. "Good morning, Mr. Rabbit!" he called out in his cheerful little roar.

But when Leo peeked inside, he gasped. The shed was very messy! Garden tools were scattered everywhere, seed packets were torn and spilled on the floor, and there was mud tracked all around.

"Oh my!" said Mr. Rabbit, looking very sad. "Someone made quite a mess in here last night. Now I can't find my important watering can, and all the plants in our community garden need water today."

Leo's heart felt heavy seeing his friend so upset. He knew the community garden was special - all the neighbors worked together to grow vegetables that everyone could share. Without water, the plants would be thirsty and sad.

"I want to help!" Leo said bravely, puffing out his little chest. "What can we do?"

Mr. Rabbit smiled. "Well, Leo, being a good neighbor means helping when our community needs us. We could clean up together and make a list of what's missing."

Leo nodded eagerly. "Yes! I can write too!" He was very proud of his writing skills, even though his letters were still a bit wobbly.

Together, they began their important work. Leo carefully picked up each tool, feeling their smooth wooden handles and cool metal parts. He arranged the shovels by size - big, medium, and small. The rakes went in one corner, standing tall like soldiers.

"Now," said Mr. Rabbit, "let's write down what we're missing so we can ask our neighbors if they've seen anything."

Leo took a big piece of paper and a thick crayon. He wrote very slowly and carefully: "MISSING: 1 WATERING CAN (BLUE), 2 SMALL SHOVELS, 1 BAG OF SUNFLOWER SEEDS."

His writing was a bit wiggly, but each letter was made with love and care.

"Excellent writing, Leo!" praised Mr. Rabbit. "Now let's go ask our neighbors."

They visited Mrs. Hedgehog, who had found the blue watering can by her fence. They asked Mr. Squirrel, who had accidentally taken the small shovels thinking they were his. The sunflower seeds were discovered under some leaves where the wind had blown them.

As they returned each item to its proper place in the now-clean shed, Leo felt warm and proud inside. The shed smelled fresh and looked perfect, with everything organized and ready for garden work.

"Leo," said Mr. Rabbit, giving him a gentle hug, "you showed wonderful civic responsibility today. You saw a problem in our community and helped solve it. You used your writing skills and worked hard to complete this important task."

Leo's golden fur seemed to glow even brighter. "It felt good to help!" he said. "When we all work together, we can solve any problem!"

That afternoon, they watered all the plants in the community garden. Leo watched the happy vegetables drink up the cool water, and he knew that being a good neighbor meant always being ready to help others.

From that day on, Leo checked on the garden shed every morning, making sure everything was tidy and ready for the community to use.

The End.
