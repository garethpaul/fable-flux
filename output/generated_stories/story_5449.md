# Polly's Washing Day Stories

In the warm laundry room of the Martinez family lived Polly, a bright green parrot with rainbow feathers on her wings. She loved perching on the washing machine and watching the colorful clothes spin round and round.

"Whoosh, swoosh!" sang Polly as the washing machine hummed. The soapy bubbles smelled fresh like spring flowers. The warm air made her feathers feel cozy and soft.

Every Tuesday was washing day. Mama Martinez would sort the clothes into piles - whites here, colors there, delicates in a special basket.

"Polly, would you like to help me today?" asked Mama Martinez, holding up a tiny sock.

"Yes, yes! Polly helps!" squawked the cheerful parrot, flapping her wings with excitement.

But instead of following Mama's directions, Polly had a different idea. She grabbed a red shirt with her beak and dropped it in the white pile.

"Oh no, Polly!" laughed Mama Martinez. "Red clothes go with the colors. Let me show you again."

Polly tilted her head. She had been so excited to help that she forgot to listen carefully.

"Tell you what," said Mama Martinez, picking up the red shirt. "How about you tell me a story while we sort? Stories help us remember things better."

Polly's eyes sparkled. She loved telling stories!

"Once upon a time," began Polly in her sweetest voice, "there was a little red shirt who wanted to swim with his color friends. The white clothes lived in White Cloud Land, where everything was snowy and clean."

As Polly told her story, she carefully placed the red shirt in the color pile.

"The red shirt was much happier with his rainbow friends - the blue jeans, the yellow dress, and the purple socks," continued Polly, sorting each piece correctly.

"What a wonderful story!" said Mama Martinez. "And look how well you're following directions now!"

Polly felt proud and warm inside. "Stories make everything easier!" she chirped.

When they reached the delicate clothes, Polly created another tale: "The gentle silk scarf was very special. She needed extra soft soap and cool water, just like a princess taking a bubble bath."

Polly gently placed the scarf in the delicate basket, being very careful with her beak.

"Excellent job, Polly!" praised Mama Martinez. "You listened to directions and made washing day fun with your stories."

As the washing machine filled with sudsy water, Polly perched on top and told one more story about the clothes going on a bubble adventure. She described how the soap helped wash away all the dirt and germs, keeping the family healthy and clean.

"Clean clothes mean healthy families," sang Polly. "And following directions makes everything work perfectly!"

The washing machine rumbled to life, and Polly watched the clothes dance in the soapy water through the glass door. The bubbles looked like tiny clouds floating in a blue sky.

"Thank you for being such a good helper, Polly," said Mama Martinez, giving the parrot a gentle pet. "Your stories made our work feel like play."

Polly puffed up her chest with pride. She had learned that following directions was important, and that stories could make any task more fun. Plus, helping keep clothes clean was a healthy habit that helped her whole family.

From that day on, every washing day became story day. Polly would tell tales while carefully following Mama's directions, making laundry time the most magical part of the week.

"The end!" squawked Polly happily, as the washing machine sang its gentle song.

The End.
