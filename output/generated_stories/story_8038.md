# Ollie Otter Says Sorry

Ollie the playful otter slipped into the musical practice room with a happy wiggle. His whiskers twitched. His small paws pat-pat-patted on the shiny wood floor. The room smelled like polished wood and lemon soap. Sunlight poured in through the big window and made a warm square on the floor.

“Wow,” Ollie whispered. “Look at all the drums and shiny bells!” He saw a big mirror, a piano with black and white teeth, and a tall shelf with ribbons and scarves. He heard tiny sounds too—tick-tick from a metronome and soft hums from the ceiling fan.

His friend Bella Bunny was there with her fluffy ears tied in a bow. Max Mole stood by the drums, wearing little glasses. Miss Lark, the music teacher, smiled and clapped gently. “Welcome, friends,” she sang. “Today we will dance to the beat. We will listen, talk, and move our feet!”

Ollie bounced, excited. “I love dancing!” he squeaked. He swished his tail and did a silly slide. Squeeeak! His paws made a smooth sound on the floor. Everyone giggled.

Miss Lark set the metronome. Tick… tock… tick… tock. “First, let’s warm up,” she said. “Wiggle fingers, wiggle toes.” The children wiggled. The room filled with soft giggles and little pats on the floor.

Ollie saw a blue scarf on the shelf. It looked like a small river. He wanted it so much. He forgot to ask. He ran to grab it. His tail swished, and his paws pattered fast—pat pat pat!

“Wait, Ollie,” said Bella, reaching out. “That’s mine. I put it there.”

But Ollie was quick. He twirled with the blue scarf. The scarf swooshed and swished in the air. It felt cool and silky. “Look at me! River dance!” Ollie sang.

Max’s drum went thump…thump…thump. Bella’s nose wiggled. Her voice sounded small. “Ollie, I was using that.”

Ollie kept spinning. He bumped the drum. BOOM! The loud sound made everyone jump. The metronome wobbled and fell—tick-tock-tick—clack! The room got very quiet. Ollie stopped. The scarf slid to the floor like a sleepy wave.

Miss Lark knelt so her eyes were kind and level. “Ollie,” she said softly, “did you hear Bella’s words?”

Ollie’s cheeks felt hot. He looked at Bella’s big eyes. They were shiny with surprised tears. His tummy felt tight, like a knot. He looked at the drum, then at the scarf.

“I… I didn’t listen,” he said in a tiny voice. “I took the scarf without asking. I bumped the drum. I made it too loud. I’m sorry, Bella. I’m sorry, Max. I’m sorry, Miss Lark.”

He picked up the metronome and set it straight. He picked up the scarf with careful paws. “Bella, this is yours,” he said, and he held it out.

Bella sniffled and touched the scarf. “Thank you for saying sorry,” she said. “That helps my heart feel better.” Her ears relaxed.

Miss Lark nodded. “Ollie, that was a strong and kind apology. You told us what happened. You used gentle words. Thank you.” She tapped the drum softly. “And thank you for fixing the metronome.”

Ollie’s knot in his tummy untied a little. “Can I make it right?” he asked. “Can I help?”

Max squeaked, “You can hold the drum steady while I play.”

Ollie smiled. “Okay.” He placed his paws on the drum’s sides, nice and firm. Max tapped: tum-tum-ta. It sounded smooth. Bella waved her blue scarf. Swoosh, swoosh. Miss Lark played a soft piano tune that went plink-plonk-plink.

“Now,” said Miss Lark, “let’s try a talk-and-dance game. We will use our words first, then our feet.”

She pointed to her ear. “Step one: we listen. Step two: we speak. Step three: we dance!”

Bella took a breath. “I would like to lead with my scarf.”

Ollie nodded. “I hear you. I will follow.”

Max said, “I will keep the beat.”

“Wonderful,” Miss Lark said. “Begin!”

Bella swirled the scarf high. Swoosh! Ollie watched and copied with his paws in the air—whoosh! Max kept a steady tum-tum-ta. The metronome clicked along: tick… tock… tick… tock.

Bella dipped low. Ollie dipped low. Their noses almost touched the floor. They both giggled. “Smells like lemons,” Ollie said. “And music,” Bella whispered.

Then Ollie asked, “May I have a turn to lead next?”

“Yes,” Bella said. “Thank you for asking.”

Ollie led a silly otter wiggle. He shimmied and slid, but he stayed away from the drum this time. His tail swished softly. Everyone laughed with happy eyes. Max added a gentle roll on the drum. Rrrrrum. Miss Lark clapped the rhythm—clap, clap.

When the song was done, the room felt warm and bright. Ollie’s heart felt light. He looked at his friends. “I’m glad I said sorry,” he said. “Saying sorry helped us dance better together.”

Miss Lark smiled. “That is our lesson today,” she said. “When we make a mistake, we listen, we say sorry, and we make it right. Good words make good dances.”

Ollie nodded. “Listen. Speak. Dance,” he said softly, like a little song. He squeezed Bella’s paw. “Thank you for forgiving me.”

Bella squeezed back. “Thank you for asking first.”

Max tapped a tiny ending beat—tap! And everyone bowed.

The End.
