# Milo’s Morning Map

Milo was a courageous monkey with bright eyes and soft, fuzzy fur. He lived in a playful living room with bouncy couch cushions, a rainbow rug, and a shelf of colorful blocks. Sunlight danced on the floor. It smelled like warm toast and clean laundry. Milo stretched his little arms and said, “Good morning, living room! Today, I will do my morning jobs all by myself.”

Milo loved to help. He also loved to think. Today, he wanted to use his brain like a brave explorer uses a map.

He sat on the rainbow rug and took a deep breath. “First,” he said slowly, “I will make a plan.”

Milo picked up a crayon and a small notepad from the table. He drew simple pictures. “One,” he said, drawing a tiny sun, “open the curtains.” “Two,” he drew a toy bin, “put toys in the bin.” “Three,” he drew a toothbrush, “brush teeth.” “Four,” he drew a bowl, “eat breakfast.” “Five,” he drew a book, “read with Mama.”

He looked at his pictures and smiled. “This is my Morning Map!”

Just then, his friend, a gentle little mouse named Pippa, peeked from behind a couch pillow. “Squeak! What are you doing, Milo?” she asked, twitching her whiskers.

“I am planning,” Milo said proudly. “I made steps. Steps help me know what to do next.”

Pippa nodded. “Can I watch?”

“Yes,” said Milo, “and you can help me think.”

They went to the window. The curtains were soft and smooth. Milo felt the cool fabric in his fingers. “Step one: open the curtains,” he said. He pulled gently. Sunlight poured in like warm lemonade. “Ahh,” Milo said, eyes twinkling. “Checking my map helped me remember.”

He looked back at the notepad. “Step two: put toys in the bin.” Toys were scattered on the rug—blocks, cars, and a soft banana plush. “How will I do this fast?” Milo asked.

Pippa squeaked, “Maybe sort first?”

Milo nodded. “Good idea. I will put all blocks together, then cars, then soft toys.” He made three little piles. His hands felt the smooth blocks and the tiny wheels. Clack, clack, roll, roll, squish. In a few minutes, the toys were in their home. “Sorting made it easier,” Milo said, feeling proud.

He checked the map again. “Step three: brush teeth.” He padded to the bathroom, Pippa on his shoulder. The minty smell tickled his nose. Milo said, “Two minutes is best.” He set a tiny sand timer Mama had given him. Swish-swish went the brush, round and round. He counted softly, “One, two, three,” and listened to the sand whisper down. He spat, rinsed, and grinned at his shiny teeth in the mirror. “My plan is working.”

Back in the living room, he looked at the notepad. “Step four: eat breakfast.” In the kitchen corner, a small bowl waited. Mama Monkey hummed a sweet tune as she poured crunchy flakes and sliced banana. “Good morning, Milo,” she said. “I see you are following your Morning Map.”

“Yes, Mama,” Milo said. “Planning helps me. It feels calm.”

Mama smiled. “Would you like milk or water?”

Milo thought. He put a finger to his chin. “Hmmm. Milk makes my tummy feel full, but water makes me feel light. Today, I choose milk.” He felt proud for deciding. He took small bites. Crunch, crunch, yum. Pippa nibbled a tiny seed and giggled.

After breakfast, Milo wiped his mouth. “Step five: read with Mama.” He picked a book with a bright blue cover. They snuggled on the couch. The cushions felt bouncy and soft. Mama’s voice was warm like a blanket. “Once there was a small seed,” she read. Milo listened, eyes wide, as the seed used sun and water to grow. He could almost smell dirt and rain from the pictures.

When the story ended, Milo looked at his map. All the steps were done! He drew a happy star next to each picture. “I did it,” he said. “I followed my plan.”

Suddenly, Pippa pointed. A block was under the couch. “Squeak! We missed one.”

Milo felt a little worry in his tummy. But he took a slow breath. “It’s okay,” he said softly. “I can think.” He got a ruler from the table. “The block is far. My hand is short. What can help?” He tapped the ruler. “A long tool!” He slid the ruler under the couch and nudged the block out. Click! “Problem solved,” he said, smiling.

Mama clapped. “You used your brain, your tools, and your calm voice. That is peaceful and smart.”

Pippa squeaked happily. “Your plan worked, and you fixed a surprise!”

Milo hugged his notepad. The room felt bright and tidy. He felt brave and calm inside. He said, “Step six: playtime!” He drew one more little picture—a bouncing ball—and laughed.

Before they played, Milo shared his lesson out loud. “When I make a plan with simple steps, I know what to do. When a problem pops up, I stop, breathe, think, and try a tool. Planning and thinking help me help myself. And talking kindly makes teamwork easy.”

Pippa nodded. “That is a good map for every day.”

Milo winked. “Tomorrow, I will use my Morning Map again.”

The End.
