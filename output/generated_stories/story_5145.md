# Bubbles Builds a Balcony Band

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash, sing, and try new things. One sunny morning, Bubbles visited a little balcony garden. The garden was full of green leaves, tiny tomatoes, and pots that smelled like mint and rosemary. Wind chimes tinkled softly. The warm sun made the flowers glow, and the wet soil smelled rich and earthy.

Bubbles peeked over the balcony rail, swishing her tail. “Hello, garden!” she sang in a bubbly voice. “I want to make music here. I want to play a song!”

Bubbles looked around. There were no drums. There were no guitars. There was no piano. Just pots, sticks, strings, and leaves.

Her smile faded a little. “Hmm,” Bubbles hummed. “No instruments. How can I make music?”

She took a deep breath. The air tasted like sweet basil. She listened. Ting-ting went the wind chimes. Tap-tap went a drip from the watering can. Chirp-chirp sang a tiny bird on the rail.

Bubbles’ eyes sparkled. “I hear sounds!” she said. “Maybe I can build something to play. I can solve this problem!”

She flapped her fin and thought in simple steps. “Step one,” she said softly, “look for things. Step two, try them. Step three, fix and try again.”

Bubbles found a wooden spoon. Clap! She clapped her fins. “This can tap.” She found a string tied to a trellis. Twang! “This can sing!” She found a big empty pot and a small tin cup. Boom and ting!

Bubbles smiled again. “I can build a balcony band!”

She started to set things up. She placed the big pot near the mint. Boom-boom. She tied the string across two small stakes. Twang-twang. She laid the spoon next to the tin cup. Ting-ting.

She tried to play a song. Boom, twang, ting! But the string sagged. It made a floppy sound. “Bluh,” Bubbles giggled. “That sounds like a sleepy whale!”

“Don’t give up,” she told herself. “Try again.” She tightened the string. She moved the stakes closer. Twang! The sound got brighter, like a sunny note.

Next, she tapped the big pot. Boom! But the boom was too loud. It made the soil shake, and a little tomato wobbled on its vine. “Oops! Sorry, tomato,” Bubbles whispered.

She thought again. “How can I make it softer?” She folded a soft leaf and put it under the pot. Boom… boop. The sound was gentle now. The tomato stopped wobbling.

“Good fix!” Bubbles cheered.

She tried the tin cup. Ting! But the spoon slipped from her fin. Clatter! It scared the bird on the rail. The bird fluttered away. Bubbles felt a pinch in her tummy. “Oh no,” she said. “I don’t want to scare friends.”

She paused and took a slow breath. In and out. She looked around. “Maybe I need a holder,” she said. She found a small cloth and wrapped it around the spoon handle. It felt grippy and soft. Ting-ting. The spoon stayed put, and the sound was sweet and light.

The bird came back and tilted its head. “Chirp?”

“Hi, little friend,” Bubbles said softly. “I am making gentle music.”

Bubbles set a simple beat. Boom… ting… twang. She counted, “One, two, three. One, two, three.” The bird chirped along, “Chirp, chirp, chirp.” The wind chimes answered, “Ting-a-ling.”

Then—uh-oh!—a cloud covered the sun, and a breeze pushed the string out of place. Twang turned to twuuup. The music drooped.

“This is hard,” Bubbles sighed. Her tail drooped, too. For a moment, she wanted to stop.

Bubbles remembered her steps. “Step one: look. Step two: try. Step three: fix.” She smiled a small smile. “I can do it.”

She found two clothespins on the line. Click! Click! She used them to hold the string steady. She moved the band closer to the wall where the wind was softer. She tested again. Twang! It sounded strong and happy.

Bubbles played a new rhythm. Boom… ting… twang-twang. She sang a little tune. “We can build, we can try. If it’s hard, we don’t cry. We can fix, we can learn. Then the music will return.”

The bird bobbed and chirped. The mint leaves swished. The chimes danced in the gentle breeze. Bubbles felt warm and proud. Her heart beat with the rhythm. Children below looked up and waved. “We hear the music!” they called.

Bubbles waved her fin. “It’s our balcony band!” she said. “We built it with careful steps.”

When the sun peeked out again, everything looked golden. The big pot glowed. The tin cup sparkled. The string shone like a bright line.

Bubbles finished the song with a soft boom and a happy ting. She clapped her fins. The bird clapped its wings. The chimes gave a last twinkle.

Bubbles took a breath and shared her lesson. “When there is a problem,” she said kindly, “we can stop, think, and try. We can build. We can fix. We can listen. We can make something new.”

She smiled at the garden. “Today, we solved it together.”

The End.
