# Penny's Big Feelings Day

Penny the parrot loved her peaceful front porch. The wooden boards felt warm under her bright orange feet. Soft wind chimes sang gentle songs in the breeze. Purple flowers in hanging baskets smelled sweet and lovely.

Every day, Penny painted beautiful pictures with her special brushes. She mixed red and yellow to make orange. She swirled blue and white to make sky colors. Painting made her heart feel happy and light.

But this morning felt different. Penny's colorful feathers felt ruffled and messy. Her bright eyes looked worried. Her little heart beat fast like a tiny drum.

"I feel all mixed up inside," Penny chirped to herself. "My tummy feels wobbly. My wings feel shaky. What's happening to me?"

Penny tried to paint, but her brush trembled. The colors looked muddy and dark. Nothing felt right at all.

Just then, Oliver the wise old owl landed softly on the porch railing. His feathers were calm and smooth. His big eyes looked kind and caring.

"Hello, Penny," Oliver hooted gently. "You look upset today. Can you tell me what you're feeling?"

Penny's beak quivered. "I don't know! Everything feels wrong and scary. My body feels funny. I can't paint pretty pictures anymore!"

Oliver nodded slowly. "Ah, you're having big feelings, little friend. Sometimes our hearts feel many things at once. That's perfectly okay."

"But it doesn't feel okay," Penny whispered. "It feels yucky and confusing."

"Let's take some deep breaths together," Oliver suggested. "Breathe in slowly... and out slowly. Feel the warm sunshine on your feathers."

Penny closed her eyes. She breathed in the sweet flower smells. She felt the gentle porch breeze ruffle her feathers softly. Slowly, her heart began to calm down.

"Now," Oliver said kindly, "let's name your feelings. What does your heart feel like right now?"

Penny thought carefully. "Maybe... worried? And a little sad? And maybe excited too?"

"Those are all normal feelings," Oliver smiled. "Tomorrow is your first art show, isn't it? Sometimes we feel many things when something new is coming."

"Yes!" Penny chirped. "I want to show my paintings, but I'm scared too. What if nobody likes them?"

"Your paintings are beautiful because they come from your heart," Oliver said. "And feeling nervous about new things shows you care. That's wonderful!"

Penny picked up her brush again. This time, her wing felt steady. She painted a bright yellow sun with orange rays. Then she added a blue sky with fluffy white clouds.

"I'm still a little nervous," Penny said, "but I feel better now. Thank you for helping me understand my feelings, Oliver."

"Feelings are like colors," Oliver explained. "Sometimes they're bright and happy. Sometimes they're dark and stormy. But they all make the beautiful picture of who you are."

The next day, Penny's art show was wonderful. People loved her colorful paintings. But best of all, Penny felt proud because she had learned something important.

Now when big feelings come, Penny takes deep breaths. She names her feelings out loud. She remembers that all feelings are okay, and they help her understand herself better.

The End.
