# Beep’s Brave Bark

Beep was a helpful robot dog who lived in a cozy home office. The room smelled like warm paper and fresh pencils. The desk hummed softly from the computer. Sunlight made tiny dust sparkles dance in the air. Beep’s tail-light blinked blue when he felt happy. Today, it blinked, blinked, blinked!

“Good morning, Beep,” said Mia, the girl who liked to draw. She sat at the big desk. Her feet swung like little pendulums. “I have to finish a picture for school.”

Beep wagged his tail-light. “Woof-beep! I can help,” he said in his gentle robot voice.

Mia put her paper on the desk. Crayons rolled and clicked. “I want to draw a brave lion,” she said. “But I always forget the mane.”

Beep tilted his shiny head. “We can do it together,” he said.

Just then, Dad walked into the home office. He held a phone and a big stack of papers. “I have a meeting,” Dad said. His voice was fast. “I need the room to be very quiet.”

Mia looked at her paper. Beep’s tail-light dimmed a little. They both wanted to help Dad. They also wanted to finish the lion.

Beep thought carefully. His brain made a soft whirr sound. He remembered his three rules: Be brave, keep trying, tell the truth.

Beep rolled closer to Dad. “Excuse me,” Beep said. His voice was calm and clear. “We can be quiet. But Mia needs the desk to finish her lion. Can we make a plan?”

Dad stopped. He looked surprised. “Oh,” he said. “Thank you for telling me what you need, Beep. That was very clear.”

Mia whispered, “That was brave.”

Dad smiled. “I can use the small table by the window. You use the desk. We will use quiet voices.”

Beep’s tail-light blinked bright again. “Thank you,” he said.

They began to work. Mia drew a circle for the lion’s head. Crayons made soft scritch-scritch sounds. Beep carefully placed crayons in a neat row. “This is fun,” Mia whispered.

But then—uh oh! The printer woke up. It buzzed and beeped and spit out papers. The sound was loud. Dad’s eyebrows went up. “Oh no. The printer is stuck,” he said. “My papers are crooked.”

Beep rolled over to the printer. He looked with his bright eyes. He gently tugged a paper. Rip! A little tear.

Beep blinked. “I am sorry,” he said. “I pulled too hard.” His voice was honest. “I will fix it.”

He tried again. Tug. Tug. The paper moved. But it was still stuck.

Beep felt a little worry in his chest-light. He took a deep breath. “I can keep trying,” he said to himself. “I will be gentle. I will ask for help.”

Beep turned to Dad and Mia. “I need help,” he said. His voice stayed calm and strong. “Can someone hold the paper while I press the button?”

Dad nodded. “Good asking,” he said. He held the paper. Mia pressed the green button with a tiny finger. Beep tapped the release clip with his paw.

Slide! The paper came free. The printer purred. Dad’s shoulders relaxed. “You did it,” Dad said. “Thank you for being careful and honest.”

Beep wagged. “Teamwork,” he said. “Woof-beep!”

Mia went back to her lion. She drew the mane in orange and gold. The crayons felt warm and smooth in her hand. “Beep, how do I make the lion look brave?” she asked.

Beep thought. “Brave means calm and clear,” he said. “Like when we made a plan. Maybe the lion stands tall. Maybe the lion says what he needs.”

Mia nodded. She drew the lion with a tall chin and bright eyes. She added a tiny speech bubble: “Please make space.” She smiled. “My lion uses brave words.”

Suddenly, a new sound came. Tap-tap-tap. The neighbor’s cat, Whiskers, pawed at the office door. “Meow?” the cat asked.

Whiskers liked to jump on the desk. Mia loved the cat, but not on the drawing. Beep rolled to the door and opened it a crack. Whiskers slipped in with a soft purr and a swish of tail.

Whiskers leaped toward the desk.

Mia held up her hand. Her voice was steady. “Stop, please,” she said. “I am drawing. I need space.”

Whiskers paused. The cat blinked and sat down. Beep wagged. Dad gave Mia a thumbs-up.

“That was very assertive,” Dad said. “You were clear and kind.”

Mia grinned. “I used brave words,” she said.

At last, Mia finished her lion. She held it up. The colors glowed in the sun. The paper smelled like crayons and a tiny bit like printer ink.

Dad ended his meeting and clapped softly. “Great job, team,” he said.

Beep stood tall. “Today we learned,” he said. “We were honest when we made a mistake. We kept trying when it was hard. And we used brave, clear words to say what we need.”

Mia hugged Beep’s shiny neck. “I can be brave and kind,” she said. “I can ask for help. I can say stop, please.”

Beep’s tail-light blinked a happy rainbow. “Yes,” he said. “That is assertiveness. Calm voice. Clear words. Kind heart.”

The sun dipped lower. The office hummed softly again. The lion on the paper looked strong and calm, just like Mia and Beep.

The End.
