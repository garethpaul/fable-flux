# Tessa Tortoise and the Paint-Brush Plan

In a sunny school, there was a creative art room that smelled like fresh paper and sweet orange soap. Paint pots sat like little rainbows on a shelf. Brushes lay in a basket, soft and tickly. On the big table, there were shiny buttons, leafy stickers, and cups of glue that smelled a little like flowers.

Tessa Tortoise, a wise tortoise with a smooth, green shell, slowly peeked in. “Good morning,” she said, her voice warm like cocoa. “Today we will make food collages and shell art. We will practice. We will follow directions. And we will use only what we need.”

Her friends cheered. Pip the squirrel hopped. Miri the mouse squeaked. Bibi the bunny wiggled her nose. “I want to make a giant picture!” Pip said.

Tessa smiled. “We will do it step by step,” she said. “First we plan. Then we make. Then we clean and save.”

She pointed to a chart with simple pictures:
1. Wash hands.
2. Choose healthy foods for the collage.
3. Use only a little glue.
4. Share brushes.
5. Save scraps for the recycle box.

Bibi bounced. “I remember! Wash hands!” The sink water sounded like a tiny waterfall. Splash, splash. The soap bubbled like tiny clouds. The children laughed as the bubbles popped, pop-pop-pop.

“Good listening,” Tessa said. “Now we choose healthy foods to glue in our collages. Foods that help us grow strong.”

Miri looked at the picture cards. “Carrots are crunchy!” she said, holding an orange card. “And apples are juicy,” Pip added, pressing a shiny red apple card to his cheek. “It’s cool!”

“Broccoli is like little trees,” Bibi whispered, giggling. “And water is the best drink,” Tessa said, tapping a blue water drop picture. “Foods like carrots, apples, beans, and water help our bodies feel good. Candy is a sometimes-food.”

Pip leaned toward the glue. “I will use a lot so it sticks fast!”

Tessa shook her head kindly. “Let’s practice using just a dot,” she said. “Dot, dot, not a lot.” She showed them: one tiny, glittery dot on the corner. “When we use a little, we save glue. That helps our room and our world.”

They tried. Pip squeezed too hard. Squish! Glue bubbled out like a little marshmallow. “Oops,” he said, his ears drooping.

“That’s okay,” Tessa said. “We practice, and we get better.” She handed him a cloth. “Wipe, wipe. Try again. Dot, dot.”

Pip breathed in slowly. “Dot… dot… not a lot,” he said. This time, tiny dots. “I did it!”

“Great practicing,” Tessa said. The room hummed with happy sounds: snip-snip of safe scissors, tap-tap of buttons, soft brush swishes like wind in leaves.

Soon Bibi reached for three brushes. “I need all of them!”

Tessa touched Bibi’s paw gently. “We share. One brush for you. One brush for Miri. One for Pip. We follow directions so everyone can make.”

Bibi’s nose twitched. “Okay,” she said, smiling. “Sharing feels good.”

Miri was making a shell pattern on a paper turtle. She frowned. “My shells keep sliding.”

“Let’s slow down,” Tessa said. “Press and count. One, two, three.” Together they pressed, soft and steady. The shell felt cool and bumpy. “Look,” Miri said. “It stayed!”

Pip looked at the scrap box. “These tiny bits are trash,” he said, reaching to toss them.

“Wait,” said Tessa. “Some scraps can be saved.” She lifted a green leaf sticker piece. “This can be a tiny tree. Saving and reusing helps the Earth. That is called conservation. We can practice caring for our planet.”

Pip’s eyes grew wide. “Tiny trees on my collage!” He glued the little leaf pieces as small trees next to a broccoli forest. “I’m a saver,” he said proudly.

Bibi licked her lips. “I’m hungry after all this practice.”

Tessa nodded. “Let’s have a snack. We have crunchy carrot sticks and cool, sweet apple slices. We also have water to sip.” She passed around a tray. Crunch, crunch! Slurp, slurp! The carrots snapped like little twigs; the apples were juicy and smelled like sunshine.

“Healthy foods help our hands work and our minds think,” Tessa said. “We make strong art and strong bodies.”

After snack, Pip wanted to splash more paint on his picture. He dipped the brush and swung. Drip! A big drop fell onto Miri’s collage.

Miri’s whiskers drooped. “Oh no.”

Pip’s tail curled. “I didn’t follow directions,” he said softly. “I didn’t practice careful painting.”

Tessa put a gentle hand on his shoulder. “We can fix it. Let’s breathe.” They took three slow breaths. In… and out. “Now dab with a cloth.” Dab, dab. The drop became a soft blue cloud.

“It looks pretty,” Miri said, smiling again. “Thank you for helping.”

Pip nodded. “Next time, I will paint slowly and keep my brush low.”

“Good plan,” said Tessa. “Practice makes our hands remember.”

When the art was done, Tessa rang a tiny bell. Ting! “Last step,” she said. “Clean and save.” They washed brushes until the water ran clear, clear. They put caps on glue. They folded clean scraps into the recycle box. The room smelled like soap and apples again.

They held up their art. Pip’s collage had apples, carrots, beans, and tiny trees. Miri’s turtle had a bumpy shell and a blue cloud sky. Bibi’s picture had one careful, shiny brush line.

“What did we learn today?” asked Tessa.

Pip raised his paw. “Practice helps me do things better.”

Miri squeaked, “Following directions helps everyone.”

Bibi wiggled her nose. “Healthy foods help us feel good.”

Tessa smiled. “And saving and reusing helps our Earth.”

They all nodded. The room felt warm and bright. Their hearts felt proud.

Tessa said, “Step by step, with practice and care, we can make beautiful things.”

The End.
