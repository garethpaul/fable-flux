# Rascal's Bathroom Band

Rascal the raccoon loved making music more than anything in the whole wide world. His fluffy gray and black striped tail would wiggle with joy whenever he heard a tune. This morning, Rascal woke up with a wonderful idea.

"I'm going to make the most beautiful music ever!" he chirped, scampering into the bathroom.

The bathroom was perfect for music-making. It had shiny tiles that made sounds echo, and lots of interesting things to tap and shake. Rascal picked up a plastic cup and began drumming on the edge of the bathtub.

*Tap-tap-tap!* went the cup. *Splash-splash!* went the water.

"This sounds amazing!" Rascal giggled, his black mask around his eyes crinkling with happiness.

Soon, his friends heard the music and came to see. First came Bella the bunny, with her soft white fur and pink nose twitching curiously.

"What are you doing, Rascal?" she asked.

"I'm making a bathroom band! Want to join?" Rascal offered her a toothbrush to use as a drumstick.

Next came Oliver the owl, with his big round eyes and brown feathers. "Hoot! This looks fun!" he hooted, grabbing a empty shampoo bottle to shake.

Then Zara the zebra trotted in, her black and white stripes gleaming. "Can I play too?" she asked excitedly.

But when Rascal looked around, he felt frustrated. Everyone looked so different from each other, and they all wanted to play different rhythms.

*Tap-tap-TAP!* went Bella's toothbrush, very fast.

*Shake-shake-shake!* went Oliver's bottle, very slow.

*Stomp-stomp!* went Zara's hooves, right in between.

"Stop! Stop! STOP!" Rascal shouted, his fur standing up with frustration. "You're all doing it wrong! We don't sound good together because we're all too different!"

His friends' faces fell. Bella's ears drooped. Oliver's feathers ruffled sadly. Zara's tail stopped swishing.

"Maybe we should go," whispered Bella.

As his friends started to leave, Rascal felt a heavy feeling in his tummy. The bathroom suddenly felt very quiet and lonely. He sat on the cold tile floor and thought about what he had said.

"Wait!" Rascal called out, his voice echoing off the bathroom walls. "Please come back! I'm sorry I got frustrated."

His friends slowly returned, looking uncertain.

"I was wrong," Rascal said, his voice gentle now. "Being different isn't bad - it's what makes music special! Bella, your fast tapping sounds like raindrops. Oliver, your slow shaking sounds like ocean waves. And Zara, your hooves sound like thunder!"

Rascal took a deep breath. "When we play together, we make something more beautiful than any of us could make alone."

"Really?" asked Bella, her ears perking up.

"Let's try again," suggested Oliver wisely.

This time, Rascal listened carefully to each friend's unique rhythm. Bella's quick *tap-tap-tap* danced with Oliver's gentle *shake-shake-shake*, while Zara's steady *stomp-stomp* held everything together like a heartbeat.

"We sound wonderful!" laughed Zara.

"Like a real orchestra!" hooted Oliver.

"Our differences make us special," said Bella.

Rascal's tail wiggled with pure joy. "And that's what makes the most beautiful music of all!"

From that day on, Rascal's Bathroom Band played together every morning, celebrating how their differences created the most magical music in the whole house.

The End.
