# Ellie's Rainbow Breakfast

Little Ellie the elephant calf stretched her tiny trunk and yawned. The warm morning sun streamed through the kitchen window, making golden patches on the cozy wooden floor. She could smell something wonderful cooking!

"Good morning, my little peanut!" called Mama Elephant from the stove. Steam rose from a big pot, filling the air with the sweet smell of oatmeal. "Are you ready for a special breakfast?"

Ellie's trunk wiggled with excitement. "Yes, Mama! What makes it special?"

"Well," Mama said with a twinkle in her eye, "today we're going to make rainbow oatmeal! But first, we need to wash our hands and brush our teeth. Healthy habits help us grow big and strong!"

Ellie carefully washed her little hooves at the sink, scrubbing between each toe. The warm, soapy water felt bubbly and nice. Then she brushed her small elephant teeth until they sparkled clean.

"Now I'm ready!" Ellie announced proudly.

Mama ladled creamy oatmeal into Ellie's favorite blue bowl. "Close your eyes and take a deep breath," Mama said softly. "What do you smell?"

Ellie closed her eyes tight and breathed in slowly through her trunk. "Mmm... it smells like cinnamon and... something sweet!"

"Very good! Mindful eating means using all our senses," Mama explained. "Now, let's make our rainbow!"

On the counter sat small bowls filled with colorful fruits. Red strawberries that looked like tiny hearts, orange carrot pieces that crunched when Ellie touched them, yellow banana slices as bright as the sun, green apple chunks that smelled fresh and crisp, and purple blueberries that rolled around like little marbles.

"Can I make the rainbow, Mama?" Ellie asked, her trunk reaching toward the fruits.

"Of course! But remember, we eat with our eyes first. Let's arrange them beautifully."

Ellie carefully picked up each piece of fruit with her trunk. She made neat rows of colors across her oatmeal - red, orange, yellow, green, and purple. It looked just like the rainbow she saw after yesterday's rain!

"It's so pretty, Mama! It looks like art!"

"It is art, sweetheart. Healthy food can be beautiful too." Mama sat down with her own rainbow bowl. "Now, let's eat slowly and taste each color."

Ellie took a small spoonful with red strawberries. "The red tastes sweet and juicy!" She tried the orange carrots next. "These are crunchy and a little bit sweet too!"

As she tasted each color, Ellie discovered that the yellow bananas were creamy, the green apples were crisp and tart, and the purple blueberries burst with flavor in her mouth.

"Mama, why do we eat so many colors?" Ellie asked between bites.

"Each color gives our bodies different vitamins and nutrients," Mama explained gently. "Red helps our hearts, orange helps our eyes see better, yellow gives us energy, green makes our bones strong, and purple helps our brains think clearly."

Ellie nodded thoughtfully as she finished her rainbow breakfast. Her tummy felt happy and full.

"Thank you for teaching me about healthy eating, Mama," Ellie said, giving her mother a big trunk hug. "Can we make rainbow lunch too?"

Mama laughed warmly. "Of course, my little artist! When we eat colorful, healthy foods and practice good habits, every meal can be an adventure."

Ellie smiled, already excited about creating her next colorful, healthy masterpiece.

The End.
