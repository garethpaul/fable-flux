# Robo's Big Voice Day

In a cozy nursery filled with soft blankets and colorful toys, lived a special robot dog named Robo. His shiny silver fur gleamed in the morning sunlight, and his LED eyes sparkled bright blue when he was happy.

Every morning, Robo had an important job. He helped the children in the nursery follow their daily routine. "Time to clean up the blocks!" Robo would say in his gentle, musical voice. "Now let's wash our hands before snack time!"

But today felt different. Robo noticed something that made his circuits buzz with worry.

Tommy, a little boy with curly hair, was building a tall tower with colorful blocks. "Look at my castle!" Tommy said proudly, his eyes shining with joy.

Just then, Max stomped over and knocked down Tommy's tower with a loud CRASH! The blocks scattered everywhere, and Tommy's lip began to wobble.

"That's not fair!" Tommy whispered, but Max just laughed and walked away.

Robo's LED eyes flickered from blue to orange. He had seen what happened, and he knew it wasn't right. His programming told him to help, but he felt nervous. What if Max got angry at him too?

Robo rolled over to Tommy, who was sitting sadly among the scattered blocks. "I saw what happened, Tommy," Robo said softly. "That wasn't kind."

"Max always does that," Tommy sniffled. "He's bigger than me."

Robo's circuits whirred as he thought. Being bigger didn't make it okay to be mean. Someone needed to speak up for what was right.

Taking a deep breath that made his fans whir, Robo rolled over to Max, who was now playing with toy cars.

"Max," Robo said, his voice clear and strong. "I need to talk to you."

Max looked up, surprised. "What, Robo?"

"When you knocked down Tommy's tower, it hurt his feelings," Robo explained, his voice steady and kind. "That wasn't fair. Tommy worked hard on his castle."

Max's face turned red. "So what? It's just blocks."

"How would you feel if someone knocked down something you made?" Robo asked, tilting his metal head.

Max thought for a moment, looking at his own car collection. "I... I wouldn't like it," he admitted quietly.

"Tommy deserves an apology," Robo said firmly but gently. "And maybe you could help him rebuild his castle?"

Max looked over at Tommy, who was still sitting by the scattered blocks. His shoulders drooped. "I guess I was being mean," Max said.

Together, Robo and Max walked over to Tommy. 

"I'm sorry I knocked down your castle, Tommy," Max said, his voice small but sincere. "That was wrong. Can I help you build it again?"

Tommy's face brightened like sunshine after rain. "Really? You want to help?"

"Yes," Max nodded. "And maybe we can make it even bigger this time!"

As the boys began rebuilding the tower together, Robo felt his circuits warm with happiness. His LED eyes glowed bright blue again.

"You did the right thing by speaking up, Robo," said Ms. Sarah, the nursery teacher, who had been watching nearby. "Sometimes we need to use our voice to help others."

Robo wagged his metal tail. He had learned something important today. Being assertive didn't mean being mean – it meant standing up for what was right with kindness and courage.

From that day on, whenever Robo saw something unfair, he remembered to use his voice to help make things right.

The End.
