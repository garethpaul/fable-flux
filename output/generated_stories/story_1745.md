# Benny's Big Bounce Band

Benny the kangaroo joey loved to bounce! *Boing, boing, boing!* His little paws clapped as he hopped into the colorful musical practice room at his school.

"Good morning, everyone!" Benny called out cheerfully. The room smelled like fresh crayons and had soft, squishy mats on the floor. Shiny instruments sparkled on the shelves - drums that went *boom-boom*, bells that went *ting-ting*, and maracas that went *shake-shake*.

Today was special. Mrs. Robin, their music teacher, was letting everyone choose instruments for the spring concert. Benny's ears perked up with excitement as he saw his friends gathering around.

"I want the big drum!" shouted Max the elephant, reaching his long trunk toward the biggest, shiniest drum.

"But I saw it first!" squeaked Lily the mouse, standing on her tippy-toes.

Benny watched as more friends joined in. Zara the zebra wanted the golden bells, but so did Sam the squirrel. Everyone was talking at once, and the room got very noisy and jumbled.

"Wait, wait!" Benny bounced gently in place, thinking carefully. "This doesn't feel right. Everyone wants the same instruments, but some friends might not get anything."

Mrs. Robin smiled warmly. "What do you think we should do, Benny?"

Benny took a deep breath, just like his mama taught him when he needed to think clearly. He looked around the room with his bright, kind eyes.

"Maybe we could take turns?" Benny suggested. "And maybe we could try different instruments too! I bet they all make beautiful music."

"That's a wonderful idea," said Mrs. Robin. "But how should we decide who goes first?"

Benny's tail wiggled as he thought. "We could put everyone's name in my mama's hat and pick fairly! That way, nobody feels left out, and everyone gets the same chance."

The friends agreed this sounded fair. Mrs. Robin brought out a soft, purple hat. One by one, each animal friend wrote their name on a colorful piece of paper.

"This is exciting!" whispered Penny the penguin as she dropped her paper in the hat.

Benny reached in with his small paw and pulled out names. "Lily gets to pick first!" he announced. The little mouse clapped her tiny paws with joy.

As each friend chose, something magical happened. Lily picked the triangle instead of the big drum because she loved its gentle *ting* sound. Max discovered he enjoyed the soft, wooden blocks that made peaceful *tap-tap* sounds. Zara found beautiful rhythm sticks that clicked together like her hooves.

"You know what?" said Sam the squirrel, holding up some jingly ankle bells. "These are perfect for my quick feet!"

Benny chose last and found a wonderful set of small drums that were just right for his bouncy energy. *Boom-ba-boom-ba-boom!*

"Let's all play together!" Benny suggested. The room filled with the most beautiful, diverse music as each instrument added its special sound.

Mrs. Robin clapped her wings. "When everyone gets a fair turn and we include all different kinds of music, we make the most wonderful songs!"

Benny bounced happily, feeling warm and proud inside. He had learned that being fair meant making sure everyone felt included and valued. It felt much better than fighting over things.

"Tomorrow, we can switch instruments if we want!" Benny announced. "That way, we can all try everything and learn new sounds!"

All the friends cheered and continued making their joyful music together, each one special and important in their own way.

The End.
