# Miko's Big Mistake

Miko the monkey loved swinging from the curtains in his family's cozy living room. The soft carpet felt bouncy under his feet. Colorful pillows made perfect landing spots. Sunlight streamed through the windows, making everything warm and bright.

"Swing, swing, wheee!" Miko called out happily. He grabbed the curtain rope and sailed across the room.

But today, Miko swung too hard. CRASH! His tail knocked over Mama's favorite lamp. The pretty blue lamp tumbled to the floor and broke into pieces.

"Oh no!" Miko gasped. His tummy felt twisty and scared. The broken pieces sparkled on the carpet like sad tears.

Miko heard Mama's footsteps coming down the hall. "Thump, thump, thump." His heart beat fast like a drum.

"Maybe I can hide the pieces," Miko whispered to himself. He quickly pushed the broken lamp under the couch with his little paws.

Mama walked into the living room. She looked around with curious eyes. "That's strange," she said. "Where did my blue lamp go?"

Miko's cheeks felt hot and red. He knew he should tell the truth, but the words felt stuck in his throat like peanut butter.

"I don't know, Mama," Miko said quietly, looking at his toes.

But telling a lie made Miko's tummy hurt even more. The broken lamp pieces seemed to whisper from under the couch: "Tell the truth, tell the truth."

All afternoon, Miko couldn't play happily. His favorite toys didn't seem fun anymore. Even banana snacks tasted yucky.

Papa noticed Miko's sad face. "What's wrong, little monkey?" Papa asked, sitting on the soft couch.

Miko took a big, brave breath. Being courageous meant doing the right thing, even when it felt scary.

"Papa, I broke Mama's lamp," Miko said with a wobbly voice. "I was swinging on the curtains, and my tail knocked it over. Then I hid the pieces because I was scared."

Miko crawled under the couch and pulled out the broken lamp pieces. They clinked together like tiny bells.

Papa's eyes looked surprised, then gentle. "Thank you for telling me the truth, Miko. That was very brave."

When Mama came back, Miko ran to her with tears in his eyes. "Mama, I'm so sorry I broke your lamp. I was swinging too wildly, and I should have been more careful with your special things."

Mama knelt down and hugged Miko tight. She smelled like cookies and love. "Thank you for apologizing, sweetie. I forgive you. Accidents happen when we're learning."

"But I also told a lie," Miko sniffled. "I hid the pieces instead of telling you right away."

"I'm proud of you for being honest now," Mama said, stroking his soft fur. "That shows you're growing up to be responsible."

Together, they carefully picked up every piece of the broken lamp. Miko helped sweep with a tiny broom. It felt good to clean up his mistake.

"From now on, I'll be more careful when I play," Miko promised. "And I'll always tell the truth, even when it's hard."

That night, Miko felt light and happy again. His tummy didn't hurt anymore. He had learned that saying sorry and being honest made everything better.

"Good night, brave little monkey," Mama whispered, tucking him into his cozy bed.

Miko smiled. Tomorrow he would swing more carefully and always remember to take care of his family's special things.

The End.
