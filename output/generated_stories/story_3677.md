# Penny's Big Discovery

Penny the parrot perched on her favorite swing in Daddy's home office. Her bright green feathers sparkled in the morning sunlight. She loved watching Daddy work at his big computer.

"Good morning, world!" Penny squawked happily. She could speak many words because parrots are very smart birds.

Daddy smiled and turned on his computer for a video call. "Today I'm meeting friends from around the world, Penny."

Penny tilted her colorful head. "Around the world? What does that mean?"

Soon, faces appeared on the computer screen. Penny saw people who looked different from Daddy. Some had dark skin, some had light skin. Some wore colorful clothes she had never seen before.

"¡Hola!" said Maria from Mexico. She had long black hair and a bright smile.

"Namaste!" said Raj from India. He pressed his hands together and bowed.

"Konnichiwa!" said Yuki from Japan. She waved with both hands.

Penny fluffed her feathers nervously. "Daddy, they don't talk like us! They look different too. I don't understand them."

Penny felt scared and confused. She flew to the corner of her cage and hid behind her mirror.

Daddy noticed Penny was upset. "Come here, sweetie," he said gently. "Let me tell you something wonderful."

Penny slowly flew back to her swing. Her little heart was beating fast.

"These are my friends," Daddy explained. "They speak different languages, but we're all the same inside. We all have feelings. We all love our families."

"But they sound funny," Penny whispered.

"Different doesn't mean bad, Penny. It means special! Maria speaks Spanish. Raj speaks Hindi. Yuki speaks Japanese. You speak English and parrot language!"

Penny's eyes grew wide. "I do speak parrot language! Squawk squawk!"

Daddy laughed. "Exactly! And look at you - you have beautiful green and red feathers. I have brown hair. We're different, but we're still best friends."

Penny thought about this. She looked at the screen again. Maria was showing a colorful painting. Raj was holding a cute baby elephant toy. Yuki was folding pretty paper into a bird shape.

"They make beautiful things!" Penny said excitedly.

"Would you like to say hello?" Daddy asked.

Penny felt brave now. She flew closer to the computer. "Hello!" she squawked clearly.

All of Daddy's friends smiled and waved. "Hello, beautiful parrot!" they said in their different languages.

Maria taught Penny to say "Hola." Raj showed her how to put her wings together like "Namaste." Yuki taught her a Japanese word for "pretty bird."

Penny's feathers sparkled with joy. She learned that people from different places could be kind and fun. She learned that different languages sounded like music. She learned that the world was full of wonderful friends.

"Thank you for teaching me," Penny told Daddy's friends. "You are all very nice!"

After the call ended, Penny preened her feathers proudly. "Daddy, I want to learn more about the world!"

"We will, sweetheart," Daddy said, giving her a gentle head scratch. "There are so many amazing people and places to discover."

From that day on, Penny loved meeting Daddy's friends from everywhere. She learned that being different makes the world more colorful and interesting - just like her beautiful rainbow feathers.

"Different is wonderful!" Penny would squawk happily whenever she met someone new.

The End.
