# Benny Bunny’s Big Dining Room Plan

Benny Bunny was a determined rabbit with soft gray fur and big, curious ears. He loved to help. One sunny morning, Benny hopped into the dining room. The room smelled like warm toast and sweet strawberries. The table was shiny. The chairs were tall. The sunshine made little squares on the floor.

Benny wiggled his nose. “Today,” he said, “I will set the table all by myself!” He wanted to be independent. He wanted to feel proud.

Mama Rabbit smiled from the doorway. “That’s kind, Benny. You’re volunteering to help. Thank you.”

Benny puffed out his chest. “I can do it!”

He hopped to the cupboard. The plates were heavy and round. He touched one with his paw. It felt cool and smooth. Clink! The plates made a tiny sound.

“I will put all the blue plates here,” Benny said. He placed one plate, then another. He liked his plan. He had a strategy.

Suddenly, Sister Bunny bounced in, carrying a basket of napkins. She sniffed the air. “I smell strawberries! I want to help too.”

Benny’s ears stood up. He wanted to do it alone. “I said I would do it all by myself,” he said. His voice sounded tight.

Sister Bunny’s whiskers drooped. “But I can help. I like making napkin triangles.”

Mama Rabbit came closer. “Let’s use our words,” she said softly. “Tell each other what you want.”

Benny took a breath. “I want to set the table by myself. I want to show I can do it.”

Sister Bunny nodded. “I want to help because helping is fun. I like to be part of it.”

Benny’s belly felt twisty. He looked at the big table. It was very big. The chairs were very tall. He had many plates and cups to carry. His paws felt a little tired.

He tapped his foot and thought. Thump. Thump. Thump. He remembered Mama Rabbit’s words: “Use your words. Make a plan.”

Benny’s ears perked up. “Sister,” he said slowly, “maybe we can both help. We can make a plan.”

Sister Bunny smiled a tiny smile. “What plan?”

Benny pointed with his paw. “I can put the plates and cups. You can fold the napkins and place the spoons. We can take turns. That is a compromise. I do some. You do some.”

Sister Bunny’s smile grew big. “Yes! A compromise!”

Mama Rabbit clapped her paws softly. “That’s good communication. You listened and shared.”

Benny felt warm and calm. He showed Sister Bunny how he liked the plates. “One plate here. One plate there,” he said. The plates went clink, clink. The cups went tap-tap on the wood.

Sister Bunny folded napkins. “Triangle napkin! Another triangle!” she sang. The napkins were soft and smelled like soap. She placed a shiny spoon on each napkin. Ting! The spoons made a tiny music sound.

Benny and Sister Bunny moved like a team. Hop, place, smile. Hop, place, smile. Sometimes Benny needed help with a tall chair. Sister Bunny held the chair steady. Sometimes Sister Bunny mixed up left and right. Benny showed her with a giggle. “This side, Sister. That’s okay.”

They talked as they worked.

“I like working together,” said Sister.

“I like our plan,” said Benny.

“I like how you listen,” said Sister.

“I like how you fold,” said Benny.

The sunlight felt warm on their fur. The room smelled like toast and strawberries and soap. The table began to look very nice. Blue plates. Clear cups. Soft triangles. Shiny spoons.

When they were done, Benny looked around. He felt proud. He still did many things by himself. He also worked with Sister. He felt independent and kind at the same time.

Mama Rabbit brought a bowl of sweet strawberries and a plate of toast. “Thank you for volunteering,” she said. “You made the dining room ready. You used a good strategy, and you made a fair compromise.”

Benny’s eyes sparkled. “We used our words. We made a plan. We took turns.”

Sister Bunny nodded. “Together is nice.”

They sat down. The chairs didn’t seem too tall now. The toast was crunchy. Crunch! The strawberries were juicy. Mmm! The napkins felt soft on their paws.

Mama Rabbit poured milk. “How does compromise feel?” she asked.

Benny chewed and thought. “Compromise feels warm,” he said. “It feels like sharing and still being me.”

Sister Bunny giggled. “It feels like teamwork!”

Benny reached for a strawberry and passed one to Sister. “Here. We can share.”

Sister Bunny passed him the honey. “And you can have this.”

They smiled. The dining room felt happy and calm. Benny’s heart felt big.

He whispered, “Next time, we can switch jobs. You can do plates. I can do napkins.”

Sister Bunny beamed. “Yes! Another plan.”

Mama Rabbit hugged them both. “Good communication. Good compromise. Good helpers.”

Benny wiggled his nose and sat up tall. He was still a determined rabbit. He could do things by himself. He could also listen, speak kindly, and make a plan with others. That was his new big skill.

The End.
