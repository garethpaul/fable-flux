# Bubbles the Brave at Bedtime

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash and laugh. At night, Bubbles slept in a cozy bedroom under the sea. Soft shells made a comfy bed. Seaweed curtains swished like a whisper. A tiny lamp made warm, golden light that glowed like a friendly moon.

One evening, Mama Dolphin said, “Bubbles, it’s bedtime.”

Bubbles wiggled. “But I want more play time, Mama!”

Mama smiled. “We can talk about it. Let’s plan a bedtime that helps your body grow strong.”

Bubbles took a deep breath. The room smelled like clean ocean water. The lamp hummed softly. Bubbles felt a little flutter inside, like tiny fish bumping around. “I feel nervous,” Bubbles said. “Sometimes the dark feels big.”

“That’s brave to say,” Mama said gently. “Let’s use courage, and also make a plan.”

Bubbles blinked. “What is courage?”

“Courage is doing something kind and right, even when you feel a little scared,” Mama said. “We can do it together.”

Bubbles nodded and took initiative. “I have an idea! Can we make a bedtime map?”

Mama’s eyes shone. “Great idea. Let’s negotiate a plan that works for both of us.”

Bubbles thought and tapped a fin on the shell bed. “First, we brush teeth. Second, we have story time. Third, we hug. Fourth, lights go low, not off. Fifth, I try sleeping.”

Mama said, “That sounds healthy and cozy. I have one change. After teeth, let’s also have water. Our bodies need it.”

Bubbles smiled. “Deal! We both agree.”

They all shook fins. The sea lamp made soft waves of light on the ceiling, like wiggly ribbons.

Step one: teeth time. Bubbles swam to the sink made of a shiny clam. The toothpaste tasted minty and cool. “Swish, swish,” Bubbles sang. “Clean teeth are strong teeth.” Bubbles spit and giggled at the bubbly foam.

Step two: water. “Sip, sip,” said Bubbles. The water was fresh and smooth. It felt good after minty toothpaste.

Step three: story time. Mama opened a book with a pearly cover. “This story is about a small fish who tried new things,” she said. Mama read in a calm voice. The pages smelled like salty paper. Bubbles listened, eyes wide. The story fish felt scared at first, then brave. Bubbles whispered, “I can be brave too.”

Step four: hugs. Bubbles and Mama cuddled close. Their fins were warm and soft. Bubbles felt safe. “Mama,” Bubbles said, “I still feel a little flutter. Can we make a courage plan for the dark?”

“Yes,” said Mama. “What do you want?”

Bubbles took initiative again. “I want the lamp dim, and I want my brave shell.” Bubbles held a small, smooth shell with a tiny star shape. It felt cool and steady in Bubbles’ fin.

Mama nodded. “We can do that. And if you wake, you can take three deep breaths. Smell the sea, blow out gentle bubbles. Then call me if you still need help.”

Bubbles practiced. “Breath in. One. Breath out. Bubble, bubble.” The air tasted like salt and sweet seaweed. Bubbles felt calmer.

Now it was time for step five. The room grew quieter. The lamp was soft and golden. The sea outside whispered, “shhh.” But then a shadow moved on the wall. Bubbles’ eyes opened wide.

“I’m scared,” Bubbles whispered.

Mama swam closer. “Thank you for telling me. What does our courage plan say?”

Bubbles held the brave shell. “Three breaths.” In. Out. Bubble, bubble. In. Out. Bubble, bubble. In. Out. Bubble, bubble.

The shadow still danced. Bubbles tried something new. “Hello, shadow,” Bubbles said. “I am Bubbles. I am brave. Who are you?”

A gentle breeze moved the seaweed curtains. The shadow wiggled and waved hello. “Oh!” Bubbles laughed. “It’s just the curtains.”

Mama smiled. “You used courage. You talked to your worry. You found the truth.”

Bubbles felt proud. “Mama, I did it. But can we make one more deal? If I sleep all night, can we have a dance in the morning?”

Mama clapped her fins softly. “Yes. We can have a morning dance. And I will make a yummy seaweed smoothie.”

“That’s a good healthy habit,” Bubbles said, giggling.

Bubbles settled into the shell bed. The blanket felt smooth and cool. The pillow smelled like fresh sand. Bubbles closed eyes. “Good night, Mama.”

“Good night, Bubbles. I’m right here,” Mama said.

Bubbles felt the brave shell, warm now from holding it. Bubbles whispered, “I am brave. I can rest.” The room hummed softly. The curtains swished. The lamp glowed like a tiny star.

Soon, Bubbles’ breaths were slow and steady. Bubble, bubble. Bubble, bubble. Bubbles dreamed of morning dances and green smoothies. Bubbles dreamed of being brave, kind, and strong.

In the morning, Bubbles woke with a big smile. “I did it!”

Mama cheered. “You used courage, you made a plan, and you worked with me to make a deal. That is initiative and negotiation.”

Bubbles wiggled with joy. “And I brushed teeth and slept well. Healthy habits help me feel good!”

They danced a happy sea dance. Swish, spin, splash!

Lesson: Courage means trying even when you feel worried. You can make a plan, talk it out, and take small brave steps. When you do, your body and your heart feel strong.

The End.
