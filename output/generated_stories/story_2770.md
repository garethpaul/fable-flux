# Bleep's Big Map Mission

The rain tapped gently on the roof of the garden shed. Tap-tap-tap. A little light blinked on. Click! The helpful robot dog, named Bleep, woke up with a soft whirr.

“Good morning, garden shed,” Bleep beeped. His nose light glowed blue. He could smell oil and fresh soil. He could hear the patter of rain and the soft rustle of seed packets.

On the workbench sat a big rolled-up paper. Bleep nudged it. The paper unfurled with a swish. It was colorful and bright.

“A map!” Bleep wagged his metal tail. “So many shapes! So many places!”

He looked closely. The map showed the whole world. There were blue oceans and green lands. There were tiny drawings of animals and foods. A penguin by the cold bottom. A panda near tall bamboo. A mango near warm sun.

Bleep’s friend, Mia, poked her head into the shed. She wore yellow rain boots and a red raincoat.

“Hi, Bleep!” Mia smiled. “Grandpa said we can learn about the world today. We just have to follow directions on the map.”

Bleep nodded. “I am good at directions!” His ears clicked to attention. “Beep-beep! Let’s learn.”

Mia opened a small envelope on the bench. Inside was a list with simple steps.

Mia read the first line. “Step one: Find the cold place at the bottom. What lives there?”

Bleep peered at the map. He saw white ice and blue water. “Cold bottom… here!” He pointed with his soft paw to Antarctica.

“Who lives there?” asked Mia.

Bleep looked at the tiny picture. “A penguin. Waddle-waddle,” he said, imitating a penguin. He waddled around the shed, squeaking. Mia giggled. The rain made little drippy sounds, like penguin steps.

Mia checked the second line. “Step two: Find a warm place with tall bamboo.”

Bleep hummed. “Warm and bamboo… here!” He tapped Asia. “A panda eats bamboo.” He pretended to crunch bamboo. Crunch, crunch. His speaker made a silly munch sound. Mia laughed again.

“Good following,” she said. “Step three: Find where tacos are yummy.”

Bleep’s nose light turned green. “I smell… pretend tacos!” He pressed the map near North America and found a tiny taco picture by Mexico.

“Mexico!” Mia clapped. “People there speak Spanish. Hola means hello.”

Bleep beeped, “Hola!” He wagged. “Around the world, people say hello in many ways.”

Mia smiled. “Yes! Step four: Find a place with drums and dancing.”

Bleep listened. In his memory, he played a soft drum beat. Boom-boom, clap! He pointed to Africa. “Many kinds of music here,” he said gently. “We can dance too.”

They did a little dance on the shed floor. Their boots squeaked. Squeak-squeak! The rain made music on the roof. Tap-tap, drip-drip.

Mia read the next line. “Step five: Find a place with a maple leaf.”

Bleep scanned. “Canada!” he chirped, touching the red leaf. “People there say hello and bonjour.” He wagged slowly. “Two ways to be friendly.”

Mia nodded. “Last step: Find your home.”

Bleep paused. His head tilted, and a question mark blinked on his screen. “Home is where we are,” he said softly. He looked at the map and placed his paw on their country. “Here!”

“Good job,” Mia said. “We followed all the directions.” She lifted the envelope. There was a tiny sticker sheet with flags. “Grandpa said we can match flags to places.”

Bleep was careful. He placed a little penguin sticker by Antarctica, a panda sticker in Asia, a taco near Mexico, a drum on Africa, and a maple leaf on Canada. Mia placed a heart on their home.

“Beep,” Bleep whispered. “The world is big. Many homes. Many friends.”

Mia’s eyes shone. “Yes. People eat different foods. They wear different clothes. They say hello in many ways. And we can be kind to all.”

Bleep’s tail clicked. “How can we be kind from our shed?”

Mia thought. She looked around the bench. There were seed packets with little pictures. Sunflower. Tomato. Beans.

“I know,” she said. “We can grow food and share.” She read the simple rules on a seed packet. “Step one: Add soil. Step two: Plant seeds. Step three: Water a little. Step four: Wait and care.”

Bleep nodded. “I will follow directions.” He gently scooped soil. Scoop, scoop. It smelled earthy and sweet. He tapped the seeds into the pot. Plink, plink. He sprayed water. Spritz, spritz. The water sparkled.

Mia drew small flags on paper and taped them to the pots. “These plants are for our neighbors. Our neighbors are from many places,” she said. “We can share with everyone.”

Bleep’s screen showed a happy face. “Sharing is kind everywhere,” he said. “Hello, world,” he added softly, looking at the map.

The rain slowed. The shed felt warm and bright. Bleep and Mia cleaned up. They put the map in a safe tube. They wiped the bench. They stacked the pots gently.

Mia patted Bleep’s head. “Today we learned about our world,” she said. “We followed directions. We planted with care. We can help others.”

Bleep wagged. “I can learn every day,” he said. “I can follow steps. I can be kind.”

They listened. Drip… drip… stop. The rain was done. The world outside was fresh and ready.

“Let’s share our plants when they grow,” Mia said.

“Beep-yes,” Bleep replied. “Friends everywhere.”

The End.
