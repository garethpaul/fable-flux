# Pippa Penguin’s Music-Room Makeover

Pippa was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a wiggly waddle. One sunny morning, she slid into the musical practice room. The room smelled like wood and lemon polish. Drums sat in a row. A shiny piano winked. Tambourines and maracas made gentle shakers’ sounds when she passed.

“Hello, friends!” Pippa chirped. “I’m ready to practice!”

But when Pippa tapped a drum—thump, thump—she noticed something. The music sheets were all messy. Some were ripped. A box of broken sticks sat in the corner. A pile of empty paper tubes and bottle caps lay near the door.

Pippa tilted her head. “Hmm,” she hummed softly. “These things look like trash, but maybe they can become something new.”

She waddled to the tubes and caps. The tubes felt smooth and papery. The caps clicked and clacked in her flippers. Pippa’s cheeks felt warm with a happy idea.

“I can craft! I can make music helpers!” she said.

Just then, her friend Tilly Tortoise peeked in. “Hi, Pippa,” Tilly said in a gentle voice. “What are you doing?”

“I’m crafting with old things,” Pippa said. “Do you want to help?”

Tilly smiled. “Yes, please!”

Pippa clapped her flippers. “First, we ask before we use,” she said. “We respect personal things.” She waddled to the music teacher’s shelf and called, “Teacher, may we use the empty tubes and caps for crafts?”

From the hallway, Teacher replied, “Yes, Pippa. Thank you for asking first. That shows good boundaries.”

Pippa beamed. “Great! Let’s think.” She tapped her beak. “What does the room need?”

Tilly looked around. “We need holders for the drum sticks,” she said. “And we need a place for the music sheets so they don’t rip.”

Pippa nodded. “Smart thinking! We will make a stick stand and a music tray.” She took a deep breath. The room smelled dusty and a little like crayons. She felt excited and calm.

They spread a clean cloth on the floor. Pippa set out safe glue, soft yarn, and child scissors. She wiggled her toes. “Safety first. Slow and gentle,” she said.

Pippa picked up a big paper tube. “This can be the stand,” she said. “But how will it not tip?” She paused and thought. Her eyes went squinty. “We need a base.”

Tilly tapped a flat piece of cardboard. “This can be the base!”

“Great idea,” Pippa said. She glued the tube to the center of the cardboard. “We will hold it and count to ten.” Together they pressed and counted, “One, two, three…” The glue felt cool and sticky. At “ten,” the tube stood tall.

Next, Pippa wrapped yarn around the tube. “Round and round,” she sang. “Soft and snug.” She tied a simple bow. Tilly added bottle caps around the base. Click, clack, click. “They look like shiny feet,” Tilly giggled.

“Let’s test,” Pippa said. She placed drum sticks into the tube. The stand did not wobble. “It works!”

Now for the music tray. Pippa picked a shallow box from the recycling bin. She smoothed the edges with tape so they were safe. She glued two paper tubes along the sides like rails. “This will help the papers stay flat,” she explained.

Tilly sorted the messy music sheets. “We can fix the rips with little pieces of tape,” she said softly. “And we can put them in the tray.” She was careful and kind to each paper.

As they worked, their friends came in. Benny Bunny hopped. “Can I help?” he asked.

Pippa smiled. “Thanks for asking, Benny. Yes, you can bring us more caps from the bin. Please take only what we need.”

Benny nodded. “Okay!” He brought three caps and set them down gently. “Here you go.”

Mina Mouse squeaked, “Can I add stickers?”

Pippa thought. “We will keep it simple so it’s safe,” she said. “How about you draw music notes on paper, and we will glue them on.”

Mina grinned and drew tiny notes. Pippa glued them on the tray. The tray looked cheerful.

Soon the room felt different. It sounded different too—less clatter, more gentle hums. The drum sticks stood tall in their new stand. The music sheets rested flat in the tray.

Pippa rubbed her flippers, feeling proud. “We used old things to make new helpers,” she said. “We recycled!”

Teacher stepped in and smiled. “I see smart crafters. I see helpers. I see careful choices.”

Pippa waddled to her friends. “Thank you for helping,” she said. “I like how we asked before we used things. I like how we thought first. We solved problems.”

Tilly nodded. “We used our brains and our hearts,” she said. “And our hands!”

Pippa tapped a tambourine, shake-a-shake. “Now let’s try a song.” Everyone took a turn, one at a time. They watched and listened. No one grabbed. If someone said, “No, thank you,” they respected it. The room felt safe and happy.

When the song ended, Pippa whispered, “I learned something today. Crafting can help us think, help us share, and help our world. We can make useful things from old things.”

Teacher said, “Yes, Pippa. You showed critical thinking, helpfulness, and good boundaries. And your crafts will help every day.”

Pippa’s heart felt warm, like a cozy blanket. She looked at the stand and the tray. She looked at her friends. She smiled her biggest smile.

“Let’s clean up,” she said. “Then we can play again tomorrow.”

Everyone cleaned together. The room smelled fresh. The instruments sparkled. The new crafts stood ready.

Pippa gave a happy waddle. “Today’s music was made with kindness and crafts,” she said. “That’s the best kind of music.”

The End.
