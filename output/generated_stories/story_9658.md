# Toby's Big Lab Adventure

Deep underground in a cozy basement lab, Toby the tortoise slowly walked between tall tables covered with colorful bottles and shiny tools. The lab smelled like lemons and fresh air, and soft lights twinkled like stars on the ceiling.

"Oh my!" said Toby, looking up at a high shelf. "I need that special blue bottle for my experiment, but it's so far up there!"

Toby stretched his little green neck as high as he could. "Jump, jump, jump!" he said, hopping on his stubby legs. But the shelf was much too high.

"Hmph!" Toby felt frustrated. His shell felt heavy, and his legs felt too short. "Maybe I'm just too slow and small," he sighed, sitting down with a little thump.

But then Toby remembered what his grandmother always said: "When you feel stuck, take a deep breath and make a plan."

Toby closed his eyes and took three deep breaths. "Whoosh, whoosh, whoosh." The cool basement air filled his lungs and made him feel calmer.

"Let me think," said Toby, tapping his wise head with one claw. "What do I see around me that could help?"

He looked around slowly, moving his head left and right. There was a wooden stool near the wall. "Hmm, that's good, but still not high enough," he said.

Then he spotted a stack of thick books on a lower shelf. "Aha! And there's a rolling cart over there!" 

Toby's eyes sparkled with excitement. "I have an idea! First, I'll push the cart to the wall. Then I'll stack the books on top. Then I'll put the stool on the books. That should make a perfect ladder!"

Toby started moving. Step by step, he pushed the cart across the smooth floor. "Push, push, push," he said, feeling stronger with each step. The wheels made a happy "squeak-squeak" sound.

Next, he carefully carried the books one by one. "Lift, walk, place," he said with each book. "I'm doing great!" The books made a satisfying "thunk" as he stacked them.

Finally, Toby climbed onto his book-stack and placed the wooden stool on top. "Perfect!" he said, clapping his claws together.

Slowly and carefully, Toby climbed his homemade ladder. Step by step, higher and higher. "I can do this!" he said with confidence. "My plan is working!"

At the top, Toby reached out and gently grabbed the blue bottle. "Success!" he cheered, holding it up high. The bottle sparkled in the twinkling lights.

As Toby climbed down safely, he felt proud and happy. His shell didn't feel heavy anymore - it felt strong and protective.

"You know what?" Toby said to himself with a big smile. "Being slow helped me think carefully. Being small helped me notice all the helpful things around me. And making a plan helped me solve my problem!"

Toby did a little happy dance, moving his feet in a slow, joyful rhythm. "Plan first, then move. Plan first, then move," he sang.

From that day on, whenever Toby faced a tricky problem, he remembered to stop, breathe, look around, and make a plan. And you know what? His plans always worked!

The End.
