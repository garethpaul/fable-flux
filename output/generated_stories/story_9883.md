# Poppy Panda and the Fair Share Day

Poppy was a gentle, patient panda who lived in a cozy treehouse high in a big green tree. The treehouse smelled like sweet bamboo and pine. The floor was soft with woven leaves. Sunlight danced on the wooden walls. Birds sang, “Chirp-chirp!” and a soft breeze whispered, “Whoosh.”

Every afternoon, Poppy invited friends to play. There was Tiko the tiny turtle, Mina the bright blue bird, and Ruffles the furry red squirrel. They loved coming to Poppy’s treehouse. They loved the toys. They loved the snacks. Most of all, they loved Poppy’s kind smile.

One sunny day, Poppy set a shiny bell on the table. “Today is Fair Share Day,” Poppy said. “We will take turns with the new stacking blocks. Ding-ding! The bell says it is time to switch.”

Mina flapped her wings. “I can’t wait!”

Ruffles swished his tail. “Me first!” he squeaked and grabbed the blocks.

Tiko blinked slowly. “I can go second,” he said.

Poppy nodded. “We will all get a turn,” she said calmly. “I will wait.”

Ruffles stacked the red block. Tap. He stacked the blue block. Tap-tap. He stacked the yellow block. Tap-tap-tap! The tower grew tall. Mina’s feathers puffed with excitement. Tiko smiled a soft turtle smile. Poppy watched and breathed in and out. Slow and patient.

Ding-ding! Poppy rang the bell. “Time to switch!”

Mina reached out her wings. But Ruffles hugged the blocks. “Not yet!” he said. “I am not done.”

Mina’s eyes looked wet. “But it is my turn,” she chirped softly.

Tiko tucked into his shell a little. He did not like when friends felt sad.

Poppy took a deep breath. She felt a small twist in her tummy. She wanted the game to be fair. She wanted everyone to feel okay. She spoke in a gentle voice. “Ruffles, I hear you. You are having fun. You want more time.”

Ruffles nodded fast. “Yes! Yes!”

Poppy nodded too. “Mina is excited for a turn. She wants a fair share. That is justice. Justice means we all get what is fair.”

Mina whispered, “I just want a turn.”

Tiko peeked up. “Maybe we can understand each other,” he said softly.

Poppy smiled. “Let’s try.” She put her paw on her chest. “I will listen first.”

Ruffles took a big breath. “I like the click sound when blocks go together. Click, click! It makes my ears happy. I don’t want the happy to stop.”

Poppy nodded. “Thank you for telling us. I understand. The sound feels good.”

Mina blinked and stepped closer. “I like flying around the tower. I like to see it from the top. I feel left out when I wait and wait.”

Ruffles looked at Mina. His ears dipped. “Oh,” he said softly. “I didn’t know you felt left out.”

Poppy’s voice was warm. “This is understanding. We listen. We share feelings. Now we can make a fair plan.”

She rang the bell once. Ding. “How about this: Ruffles gets three more clicks. One, two, three. Then Mina gets a turn. After Mina, Tiko gets a turn. I will go last. I can wait. Patience is a helpful friend.”

Ruffles wiggled his whiskers. “Three more clicks? Okay!”

Mina chirped, “Thank you, Ruffles.”

Tiko nodded. “That sounds fair.”

Ruffles stacked. Click. Click. Click. He stopped and placed the blocks in the middle. “Your turn, Mina,” he said with a small smile.

Mina took the blocks. She flapped up and set one on top. Tap! She giggled. “It looks like a little house!”

Tiko watched with wide eyes. “I can add a door,” he said when it was his turn. He placed a green block very carefully. Slide. Tap.

While Poppy waited, she hummed a soft song. Hum-hum-hum. She smelled the warm tea in her tiny cup. It smelled like honey and flowers. She felt calm.

Ding-ding! Finally it was Poppy’s turn. She sat close to her friends. “I will make a bridge so everyone can pass,” she said. She added two long blocks. The bridge was steady and strong.

Ruffles clapped. “It’s a sharing bridge!”

Mina chirped, “We can all cross!”

Tiko smiled. “I understand now. Being fair makes my heart feel light.”

Just then, there was a small thump at the door. A little mouse peeked in. “Excuse me,” squeaked the mouse. “May I come in? I heard laughter.”

Poppy nodded. “Yes. We have a fair plan. Everyone gets a turn. You can have a turn too.”

The mouse stepped in and sniffed the air. “It smells nice in here,” she said. “And it feels kind.”

Poppy’s eyes shone. “Kindness grows when we understand each other. Justice helps us share. Patience gives us time to listen.”

They all took turns again. They counted together. One, two, three. They watched each other’s faces. They asked, “How do you feel?” They answered, “I feel happy,” “I feel seen,” “I feel calm.”

As the sun got sleepy, the treehouse glowed golden. The tower, the bridge, and the little house all stood tall and safe. Everyone had a turn. Everyone felt heard. Poppy poured tiny cups of warm tea. Sip. Sip.

Poppy smiled at her friends. “Today we learned to understand. We listened. We were fair. We were patient.”

Ruffles nodded. “That is justice,” he said proudly.

Mina chirped, “That is friendship.”

Tiko added, “That is peace.”

They all hugged gently. The breeze whispered, “Whoosh,” like a happy sigh.

The End.
