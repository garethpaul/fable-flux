# Momo’s Big Dining Room Map

Every morning, a kind mouse named Momo woke up, stretched his tiny paws, and squeaked, “Good morning, new day!” Momo lived under a warm, round table in a bright dining room. The room smelled like toast, cinnamon, and sometimes oranges. Sunlight made soft squares on the floor. Chairs stood like tall friends, and a big clock went tick-tock, tick-tock.

Momo loved his morning routine. He brushed his whiskers, tidied his crumb basket, and checked his little list. “Breakfast, water, share, and learn,” he said. “That is my plan!”

One day, Momo found a basket on the dining chair. Inside were little snacks from around the world. There was a round rice cracker, a spicy-smelling chip, a sweet date, and a tiny piece of seaweed. A note said, “For everyone. Please share.”

Momo’s eyes sparkled. “From around the world?” he whispered. “The world is big, but we can meet it right here!”

Just then, his friends popped in. Pip the sparrow fluttered onto the chair. Lila the ladybug landed on Momo’s shoulder. And Gus the goldfish swished in his bowl on the table, making tiny bubbles.

“Hello!” chirped Pip. “What’s that yummy smell?”

“Hello!” hummed Lila. “I see shiny things!”

“Blub-blub!” said Gus. “Is there a taste for me?”

Momo smiled. “Friends, we have snacks from many places. Let’s try, learn, and share. Everyone is welcome.”

They washed their paws, wings, and shell in a little drip of water. Momo lined up napkins. “We will be kind. We will listen. We will try new things. If we don’t like a taste, we can say, ‘No, thank you,’” he said.

First, Momo held up the rice cracker. “This is eaten in many places, like Japan.” He took a tiny nibble. Crunch! “It is light and crispy.”

Pip pecked a crumb. “Crunch crunch! It sounds like little footsteps,” he giggled.

Lila took a dot-sized bite. “It tastes gentle,” she said.

Gus watched, his fins fanning. “Blub! I can’t eat, but I can listen and learn.”

Next, they tried the spicy chip. The smell tickled Momo’s nose. “This might be spicy, maybe from Mexico,” he said. He took a brave nibble. “Oh! Tingly!” He fanned his mouth.

Pip flapped. “Hot! My beak feels like dancing!”

Lila sipped a drop of water. “I will try a tiny bit.” She smiled. “Tingles, but I am okay!”

Momo set out water drops for everyone. “We can help each other when things feel new,” he said.

Then, Momo lifted the sweet date. “This fruit is eaten in places like Morocco. It is soft and sweet.” He took a bite. “Mmm!”

Pip’s eyes grew wide. “It’s like a little hug!”

Lila patted her belly. “I like this one.”

Gus bubbled. “Blub-blub! Sweet stories for me.”

Last, they smelled the seaweed. It was green and shiny. “This is eaten in places near big seas, like Korea,” Momo said. He tasted a flake. “Salty! Like ocean air.”

Pip hopped. “I taste the beach!”

Lila nodded. “New and funny, but nice.”

Momo looked around the dining room. The table, chairs, and clock felt like their home. But the snacks felt like tiny doors to places far away. “Friends,” he said softly, “the world is big, but we are all part of it.”

He got an idea. “Let’s make a map!” he squeaked. “A dining room map of the world.”

“Blub! How?” asked Gus.

Momo thought and thought. He drew circles with bread crumbs and lines with string. He placed the rice cracker on one circle, the spicy chip on another, the date on a third, and the seaweed on the fourth. He made little flags from sticky notes and toothpicks.

Pip helped by pushing crumbs into neat paths. “I will keep trying,” Pip said when the crumbs rolled away. He tried again and again until the paths stayed.

Lila placed the flags. “I will be careful and brave,” she said, balancing on a flag like a tiny acrobat.

Gus swished. “I will cheer you on! Blub-blub! You can do it!”

They worked and worked. The clock said tick-tock, tick-tock. Sometimes a string slipped. Sometimes a flag fell. But they kept going. “We can fix it,” Momo said. “We will not give up.” He thought of new ways to hold things down, using a dab of honey for sticky, and a dry leaf for gentle padding. “Innovation!” he giggled. “A new idea for a new problem.”

At last, the map was ready. The dining room was quiet for a moment. Sunlight made the crumbs glow like tiny stars.

Momo took a deep breath. “Friends, this is our map,” he said. “These snacks come from different places. People live in many lands. They have different foods, songs, and stories. We can learn from all of them. We can be kind to everyone.”

Pip chirped, “We tried new tastes!”

Lila whispered, “We worked together.”

Gus bubbled, “We included everyone.”

Momo smiled. “Today, we traveled the world from our table,” he said. “Every day, we can learn something new.”

They cleaned up, shared the last crumbs fairly, and tucked the map safely under the warm round table. Momo checked his list. “Breakfast, water, share, and learn,” he said. “Yes! We did it.”

He looked at his friends and said, “The world is full of friends we have not met yet.”

The End.
