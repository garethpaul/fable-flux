# Bounce, Think, and Try Again

Sunny the joey bounced into the playful living room with a happy thump-thump. Pillows lay like little mountains. Blocks stacked like tiny towers. A soft rug felt warm under Sunny’s feet. The room smelled like fresh soap and oranges. Sunlight made bright dots on the floor.

“Today, I will build the Tallest Tower,” Sunny said with a big grin. “It will be taller than me!”

Sunny picked up a red block. “Clack.” A blue block. “Clack.” A yellow block. “Clack.” The tower started to grow. Higher and higher it went.

Sunny’s little paws wobbled. “Careful, paws. Be steady,” Sunny whispered.

Bounce, bounce, bounce went Sunny’s tail with excitement. The tower shook. “Oh no!” Sunny gasped.

CRASH!

Blocks tumbled everywhere. Sunny’s ears drooped. “My tower fell.”

Just then, Sunny’s friend Pip the puppy trotted in. Pip’s soft fur brushed Sunny’s leg. “Woof! I heard a crash. Are you okay?” Pip sniffed the blocks and wagged.

“I’m okay,” Sunny said, blinking back tiny tears. “But my tower is not okay.”

Pip tilted his head. “Do you want to try again?”

Sunny took a deep breath. “Yes. I will keep trying. I won’t give up.”

Sunny and Pip picked up the blocks together. “We can be a team,” Pip said.

Sunny nodded. “Team Sunny and Pip!”

They started again. “Clack, clack, clack.” Sunny stacked slowly. Pip held the bottom with his paw so it stayed still. The tower grew to Sunny’s tummy, then to Sunny’s chest.

Sunny felt bouncy joy inside. “It’s working!” Sunny cheered.

Sunny looked at two blocks: a tiny purple one and a big green one. “Which block should go next?” Sunny asked.

Pip looked closely. “Hmm. Big blocks on the bottom. Small blocks on top. That’s a good choice.”

Sunny thought. Sunny decided. “Big green first. Then the tiny purple.” Sunny placed the big green block. It fit like a snug hug. Then the tiny purple block went on top.

The tower stood taller than before. Sunny’s heart felt warm and proud.

But Sunny’s tail swished. The rug tickled. Sunny giggled and bounced.

Uh-oh. The tower wiggled. “Steady,” Pip whispered.

CRASH!

Blocks fell again. Sunny felt a tear on a cheek. “I keep trying. It keeps falling.”

Pip licked Sunny’s paw. “It’s okay to feel sad. Let’s rest for one breath.”

Sunny closed eyes and breathed in. The room smelled gentle and sweet. Sunny breathed out and shook the little worry away.

“We can make a plan,” Pip said. “That is a smart step.”

“A plan,” Sunny echoed. Sunny clapped softly. “Good idea!”

They talked in quiet voices.

Sunny said, “Step one: make a strong base.”

Pip said, “Step two: use flat blocks in the middle.”

Sunny said, “Step three: little blocks on top.”

Pip wagged. “Step four: slow and steady. No big bounces.”

Sunny giggled. “I will park my tail.”

They started again. Sunny pressed the bottom blocks firmly. “Clack.” Pip checked with a gentle paw. “Stable,” he said.

They added blocks, one by one. Sunny hummed a calm song. Pip counted, “One, two, three.”

The tower reached Sunny’s chest. Then Sunny’s chin. Sunny’s eyes shone. “We are doing it!”

A round block rolled near Sunny’s foot. “Should we use this one?” Sunny asked.

Pip shook his head. “Round blocks roll. That’s a wobbly choice.”

Sunny nodded. “Good thinking. I choose a flat block instead.”

They kept going. The tower grew to the tip of Sunny’s ears. Sunny felt a tickle of fear and a sparkle of hope.

“Just one more block,” Sunny whispered.

Sunny lifted a golden block. It felt cool and smooth. Sunny held very still. The room was quiet. Even the sunbeams seemed to wait.

Plink.

The golden block slid into place. The tower did not wiggle. It stood tall and proud, taller than Sunny, taller than Pip.

“We did it!” Sunny laughed. Pip barked, “Woof, woof!”

They danced a tiny happy dance with tiny careful steps. The rug felt soft under their feet. The tower glowed in the sunlight. It looked like a shiny castle.

Sunny put a paw on heart. “I kept trying. We worked as a team. We made a plan and made good choices.”

Pip’s tail wagged fast. “That’s persistence,” Pip said. “You did not give up.”

Sunny smiled wide. “Yes. When it fell, I tried again. When I felt sad, I rested and made a plan. I used my brain and my friends.”

Pip nodded. “That’s teamwork and decision-making too.”

Sunny hugged Pip. “Thank you for helping me think and build.”

“You’re welcome,” said Pip. “Now let’s take a picture!”

They looked at their tower one more time. Sunny whispered, “I am proud of us.”

Sunny felt strong inside, like the tower’s base. Sunny felt smart inside, like the good choices. Sunny felt loved inside, like the warm hug with a friend.

Sunny tapped the camera. Click! The picture showed two happy friends and one very tall tower.

Sunny said, “Next time, if something is hard, I will remember this day. I will bounce, think, and try again.”

Pip barked softly, “And I will be there.”

The End.
