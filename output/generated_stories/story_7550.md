# Polly Parrot and the Laundry Song

Polly was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a rainbow tail. Polly loved to sing, paint, and make new games. One sunny morning, Polly fluttered into the laundry room. The room smelled soapy and clean. The washing machine hummed, “Whirrrr.” The dryer clicked, “Tick, tick,” and warm air puffed out like a gentle sigh.

“Hello, machines!” Polly chirped. “Let’s make a song!”

Mama Mia came into the laundry room with a basket of clothes. “Hi, Polly,” she said with a smile. “I’m washing clothes today. Would you like to help?”

“Yes, yes!” Polly flapped. “I can sing, and I can spin, and I can toss, toss, toss!”

Mama Mia chuckled. “Helping is wonderful. But we have to follow directions to keep it safe and clean. Are you ready to listen, look, and wait?”

Polly bobbed. “Listen, look, wait. I can do it!”

Their friends came to visit too. Ben the bunny hopped in, Lola the ladybug buzzed, and Sam the sleepy kitten padded softly on his paws. “Can we help?” they asked.

“Of course,” said Mama Mia. “Everyone can help. We will take turns, include everyone, and follow directions.”

Polly wiggled with ideas. “I have a plan! Let’s put all the clothes in at once! Socks, shirts, hats, and my rainbow feathers too!” Polly puffed and picked up a big pile.

“Pause,” Mama Mia said gently. “Step one: Sort. We sort lights and darks. We keep feathers out,” she added with a wink.

Polly froze, then slowly set down the pile. “Sort. I can sort.” The friends made neat piles. Soft white socks went in one pile. Dark jeans in another. Lola flew down, pointing with her tiny leg. “Light here. Dark there.” Ben the bunny used his ears to push socks into place. Sam the kitten patted a shirt and purred.

Polly laughed. “This is fun! We are a team.”

“Step two: Check the pockets,” Mama Mia said. “We don’t want tissues in the wash.”

Polly reached into a pocket and pulled out a crinkly tissue. “Aha!” she sang. “Found one!” The tissue felt soft and fluffy. Polly giggled as it tickled her beak. Ben found a tiny leaf. “Out you go,” he said.

“Step three: Use the right soap,” said Mama Mia. “Only a little.” She held up the bottle.

Polly wanted to pour a big, bubbly mountain. “Big bubbles make big fun!” Polly chirped.

Mama Mia shook her head with a kind smile. “Just a small cup. We follow directions to keep it healthy and safe. Too much soap is not good.”

Polly took a breath and nodded. “A small cup.” She poured slowly. Drip, drip. “I did it!”

“Step four: Close the door,” Mama Mia said. Polly pushed the door. Click. The machine was ready.

“Step five: Press start,” said Mama Mia. “And we wait.”

Polly’s wings twitched. “Wait? But I want to sing and spin!”

“We can sing while we wait,” said Lola. “We can also drink water,” added Ben. “Healthy habit!” Sam yawned. “And rest.”

Polly took a sip of cool water. The glass felt smooth in her claws. The hum of the washer was like a soft drum. “Whirrrr,” it sang. Polly began a gentle laundry song.

“Sort, check, soap so small,
Close the door, start it all.
Listen, look, wait with me,
Clean and safe, 1-2-3!”

Everyone joined. They clapped wings, paws, and tiny legs. They took turns leading the song. First Lola, then Ben, then Sam. Polly smiled each time a friend took a turn.

When the washer stopped, Mama Mia said, “Great listening! Now, step six: Move clothes to the dryer. One at a time. Take turns.”

Polly wanted to scoop them all at once. But she remembered. Listen. Look. Wait. She said, “Ben first. Then Lola. Then Sam. Then me.” Each friend helped, one by one. The wet clothes felt cool and heavy. Drip, drip. Polly carefully placed a shirt inside. Plop.

“Step seven: Clean the lint,” Mama Mia said, opening a little tray. The lint was soft and gray like a tiny cloud.

Polly brushed it into the bin. “Healthy habit!” she chirped. “Keeps the dryer safe.”

“Step eight: Set the time, close the door, and press start,” said Mama Mia. Polly waited for her turn. Beep. The dryer warmed the room. It smelled like sunshine and soap. “Shhhh,” went the dryer. “Whoosh,” went warm air.

While they waited, Polly created a poster with big, bright pictures: a light shirt, a dark sock, a tiny cup of soap, a door clicking shut, and a big ear for listening. She drew numbers 1 to 8. “These are our laundry directions,” she said proudly. “Everyone can see. Everyone can help.”

Ding! The dryer finished. “Step nine: Fold,” Mama Mia said.

They folded towels together. The towels were warm and fluffy. Polly squeezed one. “Mmm, cozy!” They made small piles for each friend and took turns putting items away. Ben hopped to the shelf. Lola zipped to the basket. Sam curled around socks to keep them from rolling away.

Polly fluffed her feathers. “We did it! We followed directions. We took turns. We included everyone. We used healthy habits.”

Mama Mia hugged Polly. “You listened, looked, and waited. You improved each step. That’s how teams work.”

Polly sang the laundry song once more, soft and sweet. “Sort, check, soap so small, close the door, start it all. Listen, look, wait with me, clean and safe, 1-2-3!”

Polly winked at her friends. “Next time, we can teach others. We can share the directions and the song. Everyone can help.”

The friends cheered. The laundry room felt bright and happy. Polly’s heart felt warm, like the towels.

Polly whispered, “Following directions helps us do big things. It keeps us safe. It helps us include friends. It helps us grow.”

The End.
