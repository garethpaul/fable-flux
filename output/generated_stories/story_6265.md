# Polly's Kitchen Dance Party

In a sunny, warm kitchen filled with the sweet smell of fresh fruit, lived a colorful parrot named Polly. Her bright red, blue, and yellow feathers sparkled in the morning sunlight that streamed through the window. Polly loved to dance and sing, but today she had a big problem.

"Oh my!" squawked Polly, looking at the messy kitchen counter. "Mama asked me to help make a healthy fruit salad for our family picnic, but there are so many tasks to do!" 

The counter was covered with apples, bananas, oranges, and strawberries. There were also mixing bowls, a cutting board, and colorful plates scattered everywhere. Polly felt overwhelmed and didn't know where to start.

"This is too much work," Polly sighed, ruffling her bright feathers. "Maybe I'll just dance instead!" She began to spin and twirl around the kitchen, her feet tapping on the warm tile floor.

Just then, her friend Benny the bunny hopped into the kitchen. His soft gray ears twitched as he smelled the fresh fruit.

"Hi Polly! What are you doing?" asked Benny, his pink nose wiggling.

"I'm supposed to help make fruit salad, but there's so much to do! I don't know where to begin," Polly explained, pointing her colorful wing at the messy counter.

Benny's eyes lit up. "I have an idea! What if we break the big job into smaller tasks? And we can dance while we work!"

"Really? We can dance AND work?" Polly's eyes sparkled with excitement.

"Absolutely! Let's make it fun," said Benny. "First task - let's wash the fruit!"

Polly turned on the warm water and began washing the red strawberries. "Wash, wash, wash!" she sang, moving her head side to side like a dance. The water felt cool and refreshing on her feathers.

"Great job!" cheered Benny. "Next task - let's sort the fruit by colors!"

Together, they danced around the kitchen, placing red apples and strawberries in one bowl, yellow bananas in another, and orange oranges in a third bowl. Polly did little spins with each piece of fruit she moved.

"This is fun!" laughed Polly. "What's next?"

"Now we cut the fruit into small pieces," said Benny. With Mama's help, they carefully cut each piece of fruit. Polly did a little wiggle dance each time they finished cutting one type of fruit.

"Look how colorful and healthy our fruit salad is!" said Polly, admiring their work. The kitchen smelled amazing - sweet like strawberries and fresh like oranges.

Finally, they mixed everything together in a big, shiny bowl. Polly did her biggest dance move yet - a happy spin that made all her feathers flutter.

"We did it!" she chirped proudly. "We completed every single task!"

Benny clapped his paws. "And look how much fun we had! Breaking the big job into small tasks made it easy."

When Mama came into the kitchen, she gasped with delight. "Oh my! What a beautiful, nutritious fruit salad! And the kitchen smells wonderful!"

"We learned that big tasks aren't scary when you break them into small pieces," said Polly, doing one more little dance. "And dancing while working makes everything more fun!"

As they enjoyed their colorful, healthy fruit salad together, Polly felt proud and happy. She had learned that completing tasks feels wonderful, especially when you take them one step at a time and make them fun.

The End.
