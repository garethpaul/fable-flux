# Koala Kori’s Porch Plan

Kori was a calm koala who loved her peaceful front porch. Every morning, she stretched her soft, gray arms, blinked her gentle eyes, and listened. She heard the quiet breeze hum through the gum leaves. She smelled warm eucalyptus and a little hint of sweet flowers. Her wooden porch felt smooth and cool under her fuzzy feet.

Kori had a simple routine. She sang, “Step, sort, sweep,” in a soft voice every day. First, she took one step outside. Next, she sorted the bottles and cans for recycling. Then, she swept leaves into a neat pile. “Step, sort, sweep,” Kori said with a smile. Routine made her feel safe and steady.

One sunny morning, a problem rolled onto the porch. A big blue bin wobbled on one wheel and tipped with a CLUNK. Bottles and cans clinked and clanked everywhere. Some rolled under the chair. Some rolled near the steps. One can spun in a circle and made Kori giggle.

“Oh dear,” Kori said calmly, patting her heart. “I can fix this. Step, sort, sweep.” She took a deep breath. The air tasted fresh and cool.

Kori reached for a shiny bottle. It slipped. Tink! It bumped her paw and rolled away. Kori did not fuss. “I will try again,” she whispered. She bent her knees low, kept her eyes on the bottle, and reached slowly. This time, she caught it. “Thank you, paws,” she said, giving her paws a little squeeze. “That’s good coordination.”

Next, Kori crawled under the chair. Dust tickled her nose. “Ah-choo!” She laughed and tried again. “Slow and steady,” she told herself. She found two cans hiding like tiny turtles. “Hello, little cans,” she said. “Back to the recycling bin for you.”

Kori scooped and sorted, but the blue bin still leaned and wobbled. It was hard to put things in. Each time she tried, the bin teetered. Flop! The lid fell shut. “Hmm,” said Kori, thinking. “I need a new plan.”

Her friend, Pip the pigeon, fluttered down and cooed. “Coo-coo! Need help, Kori?”

“Thank you, Pip,” said Kori. “Yes, please. We can do this together.” She felt grateful for a friend.

Kori looked at the wobbly wheel. It squeaked, eee-eee, and shook like jelly. “I will be patient,” she said. “Step, sort, sweep. And fix.”

Kori found a smooth stone by the porch steps. “This can be a helper,” she said. With careful hands, she nudged the stone under the bin’s corner. The bin steadied a little. Pip hopped to the other side and pressed with a wing. “Coo! It’s better!” Pip chirped.

Kori tried to put a can in. Clink. It stayed! “Thank you, Pip,” Kori said. “Thank you, stone.” She gave a small nod. “I am grateful.”

But a big gust of wind whooshed by. Woosh! Leaves swirled like a tiny storm. The neat pile blew apart. Cans wiggled. The bin shook again.

Kori closed her eyes and took three slow breaths. In... and out. In... and out. In... and out. “I will not give up,” she told herself. “Persistence, Kori. Keep trying.”

She opened her eyes and made a new plan. “We will move in a rhythm,” she said to Pip. “We will use coordination.”

“Coo-coo!” agreed Pip.

Kori counted. “One, two, three.” On one, she held the bin handle. On two, Pip pressed the bin’s side. On three, Kori lifted the lid and slipped in a can. Clink! It worked. They smiled.

Again, Kori counted. “One, two, three.” Another bottle went in. Tink! Again. “One, two, three.” Another can. Clonk! Together, they moved like a little team. Their bodies felt steady and strong.

When the wind blew, they paused. When the wind rested, they worked. “Step, sort, sweep,” Kori sang softly. “And count, one, two, three.” She was patient. She was calm. She kept going.

Soon, the porch looked clean again. The blue bin stood steady with the smooth stone and Pip’s help. The leaves sat in a tidy bundle, ready for compost. The bottles and cans were in the right place for recycling. The air felt clear. The porch smelled like eucalyptus once more.

Kori wiped her forehead and smiled. “We did it. Thank you, Pip.”

“Coo! You did not give up,” Pip said. “You were brave and patient.”

Kori nodded. “I kept trying. That is persistence.” She patted the bin. “And this is recycling. It helps keep the world clean.” She looked at her broom. “This is our routine. Step, sort, sweep.” She tapped her chest. “And we say thank you. Gratitude feels warm inside.”

Pip puffed up with pride. “Coo-coo! I feel warm, too.”

Kori sat on her peaceful front porch. She listened to the gentle breeze. She tasted a cool sip of water. Her paws felt relaxed on the smooth wood. “Friends, plans, and persistence,” she said softly. “That is how we solve problems.”

She waved to the sun and whispered, “Thank you for this day.” Then she added, “Tomorrow, I will do my routine again. Step, sort, sweep.”

And if the bin wobbled again, Kori knew what to do. She would take a breath. She would try a new plan. She would keep going, one, two, three, until it worked.

The End.
