# Gigi's Sharing Tree

High up in the tallest oak tree, Gigi the giraffe had built the most wonderful treehouse. It had bright yellow walls that matched her spots, a cozy reading corner with soft cushions, and shelves filled with her favorite books and toys.

Every morning, Gigi would stretch her long neck up, up, up to her treehouse door. "Good morning, beautiful day!" she would say, breathing in the fresh air that smelled like flowers and sunshine.

One sunny Tuesday, Gigi was arranging her collection of colorful blocks when she heard sniffling sounds below. She peeked over the edge of her treehouse and saw three little woodland friends sitting sadly under her tree.

"What's wrong?" called Gigi gently, her voice floating down like a warm hug.

Benny the bunny looked up with teary eyes. "We don't have anywhere fun to play. The playground is being fixed, and it's raining at our homes."

Rosie the raccoon added, "We just want somewhere cozy to play together."

Sam the squirrel nodded. "Your treehouse looks so wonderful up there."

Gigi's heart felt heavy seeing her friends so sad. She looked around her special treehouse - her private, perfect space. For a moment, she wanted to keep it all to herself. It was HER treehouse, after all.

But then Gigi took three deep breaths, just like her mama had taught her when making important choices. "Think with your heart," she whispered to herself.

"I have an idea!" Gigi called down. "But first, let's make sure everyone can get up here safely."

Gigi carefully lowered her special rope ladder - the one with big, easy-to-grab knots. "Hold on tight and climb slowly," she instructed. "I'll watch to make sure you're safe."

One by one, her friends climbed up. Benny's ears wiggled with excitement. Rosie's tail swished happily. Sam chittered with joy.

"Welcome to my treehouse!" said Gigi, spreading her long arms wide. "But we need some rules to keep everyone safe and happy."

Together, they made their rules: gentle hands only, take turns with toys, and always ask before using something special.

"Now," said Gigi with a big smile, "who wants to build a block tower together?"

The friends spent the whole afternoon playing. They built castles, read stories, and shared Gigi's delicious acorn cookies. The treehouse filled with giggles and the warm feeling of friendship.

When it was time to go home, Benny hugged Gigi's long leg. "Thank you for sharing your special place!"

"You made our sad day into the best day ever!" said Rosie.

Sam added, "You're the kindest friend in the whole forest!"

As Gigi watched her friends climb down safely, her heart felt bigger and warmer than ever before. Her treehouse felt even more special now that it was filled with happy memories of friendship.

"Mama was right," Gigi said to herself, touching her heart. "Sharing makes everything more wonderful."

From that day on, Gigi's treehouse became the forest's favorite place to play. And Gigi learned that the more joy you share, the more joy you have.

The End.
