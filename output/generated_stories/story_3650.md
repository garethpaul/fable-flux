# Ellie’s Little Big Dinner Plan

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and bright, curious eyes. One evening, she padded into the dining room on quiet, careful feet. The wooden table smelled like lemon polish. The room felt warm, with golden light shining on shiny spoons and colorful plates.

Mama Elephant hummed a happy tune. “Dinner soon,” she sang, tapping a spoon like a tiny bell. Ting-ting!

Ellie loved music. She swayed her small tail and tapped the floor with a soft thump. “I want to help!” she said.

Mama smiled. “Thank you, Ellie. Helping is fair. We all do our part. Please set the napkins, and we will use only what we need.”

Ellie nodded. “Only what we need,” she repeated. The words felt important, like a song to remember.

On the shelf was a big pack of paper napkins. They were fluffy and white like little clouds. Ellie reached out with her trunk and took a big handful. She loved how soft they felt. She giggled. “Clouds for everyone!”

Just then, her best friend, Finny the Finch, fluttered in the window and landed on a chair. “Tweet! I love clouds!” Finny chirped.

Ellie placed two napkins at each seat. “Two is better,” she said. “More is nice.”

Papa Elephant stepped in, sniffing the yummy carrot stew. “Mmm, smells like sunshine and pepper,” he said. He saw the table. “Ellie, this is a lot of napkins.”

Ellie’s ears drooped a little. “I wanted it to be special,” she said softly.

Mama knelt beside her. “It is special because you are helping,” she said. “But using more than we need is not fair to our trees. Napkins come from trees. Trees give homes to birds and clean air to breathe.”

Finny fluttered his wings. “Tweet! Trees are my favorite homes!”

Ellie blinked. “Napkins come from trees?” She looked at the fluffy stack. She could almost hear whispery leaves. She felt a warm wish in her heart to take care of them.

Mama nodded. “Yes. When we take only what we need, we help the forest. That is called conservation.”

Ellie tried the new word carefully. “Con-ser-va-tion,” she said. It felt big in her mouth, but also kind.

Papa tapped a gentle rhythm with his trunk on the table—dum, dum, dum—like footsteps in a forest. “Let’s make a plan,” he said. “We have a problem. We used too many napkins. What is our solution?”

Ellie thought. She looked at the clean cloths hanging on a peg. They were soft and blue and had little stitched stars. “We can use cloth napkins,” she said. “We can wash them and use them again.”

Mama’s eyes sparkled. “That is a wonderful idea.”

Finny chirped, “Tweet-yea! Save the trees, please!”

Ellie picked up the extra paper napkins with her trunk. She counted slowly, “One, two, three…” The paper felt crinkly and light. She put them back on the shelf. Then she set one cloth napkin at each place, smoothing them gently. Swish, smooth, swish.

“Fair and enough,” Ellie said. “One for each.”

Papa smiled. “That is fair.”

Mama added a small pitcher of water and a bowl with lemon slices. The lemon smelled bright and zippy. “We can also save water,” Mama said. “Let’s pour only a little at a time. If we are still thirsty, we pour more. We do not waste.”

Ellie nodded. “Only what we need.” She touched the cool pitcher. She felt proud and calm.

Finny hopped onto the back of a chair. “Can we sing while we do our chores?” he asked.

“Yes,” Ellie said. She loved music and chores together. She made up a song:

“Take just a little,
Leave some for the trees.
Use, wash, share,
Thank you, please!”

Finny chirped a high harmony. “Tweet, tweet, tweet!” Papa drummed the table softly—dum, dum, dum. Mama tapped the spoon—ting-ting! The dining room sounded like a tiny band. Ellie’s heart felt full and happy.

They sat to eat. The carrot stew was warm and sweet. The bread was soft and smelled toasty. Ellie dabbed her mouth with her cloth napkin. “It works,” she said, giggling.

After dinner, Ellie asked, “What are our chores now?”

Papa said, “We clear the table together. Fair and fun.”

Ellie carried the spoons carefully, clink, clink, clink. Mama folded the cloth napkins to wash later. Finny gathered crumbs with a tiny brush. “Tweet! Crumb dance!” he chirped, hopping in a circle.

When they finished, the table shone again. Ellie looked around the dining room. It looked neat and cozy. She felt warm and proud inside.

Mama hugged Ellie with her trunk. “You helped our home and the forest.”

Ellie smiled. “Conservation means helping the trees and using only what we need,” she said. “That is fair to everyone.”

Finny puffed his feathers. “And chores can be music time!”

Papa winked. “Little choices make a big difference.”

Ellie swayed and sang softly as they turned off the lights. “Take just a little, leave some for the trees.” The room grew quiet, like a forest at night—safe and peaceful.

Ellie whispered, “Tomorrow I will remember again.”

The End.
