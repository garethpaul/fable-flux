# Penny Penguin’s Tabletop Tournament

Penny was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a tiny red scarf. One sunny afternoon, she waddled into the dining room. The wooden table gleamed. The chairs stood like tall teammates. The room smelled warm, like fresh toast and sweet berries.

“Today is sports day!” Penny chirped. “Right here in the dining room!”

She clapped her flippers. Clap, clap! Her heart felt bouncy and brave.

Penny set up her tabletop tournament. She lined up soft napkins as a running track. She placed shiny spoons as hurdles. She stacked colorful cups into towers for targets. She even drew a finish line with a piece of string. The spoons jingled when she tapped them. Ting, ting!

“This will be fun,” she said. “I can run, jump, and toss!”

Her friend, a little stuffed seal named Sal, watched from a chair. “Go, Penny! You can do it!” Sal squeaked in a tiny voice. Penny smiled. Encouragement felt warm in her chest.

Penny started with spoon-hurdle jumps. She took a deep breath. In. Out. Her feet pitter-pattered on the napkin track. Pitter, patter, pitter! She bent her knees and hopped.

Clink!

Her belly bumped a spoon. The spoon fell and spun on the table. Spin, spin, spin!

“Oh,” Penny said softly. Her cheeks felt hot. “I bumped it.”

Sal the seal said, “Try again, Penny. Sports is practice!”

Penny nodded. “I can try again. I can improve.”

She fixed the spoon with a gentle clink and tried a smaller hop. Hop! This time she cleared the spoon. She landed with a soft thump. “I did it!” she cheered.

Next came the cup toss. Penny made little paper balls from napkins. Rustle, rustle. She aimed at the colorful cup towers. “One, two, toss!” she said. The paper ball flew and tapped the cup. Tap! The cup wobbled but did not fall.

Penny puffed out her cheeks. “Almost.”

She tried again. “One, two, toss!” Tap! Wobble, wobble… plop! A cup fell into Penny’s flippers. She giggled. “I got one!”

Sal clapped his tiny flippers. “Great aim, Penny!”

Penny felt proud. Her tummy felt like a happy drum. Boom, boom—gentle and good.

Then Penny made up a new game. “Creative sports!” she sang. She placed a napkin like a cape. She spun around the chair and slid across the smooth table. Swish! She made a new rule. “Slide, spin, and freeze like a statue!”

She slid and spun, then froze with a silly face. Her beak made a big grin. Her eyes were wide. She wobbled, then steadied. “Balancing is a sport, too,” she said.

She added music by tapping a spoon on a cup. Ting-ting, ting-ting! “This is my rhythm,” she said. “I move to the beat!”

Penny drew a small star on a sticky note and placed it by the finish line. “The star is a goal. I will try to reach it with my best form.”

She bent her knees, held her flippers out, and ran the napkin track. Pitter, patter! She jumped the spoon. Hop! She tossed a paper ball. Toss! She spun with her napkin cape. Swish! Then she reached the star and touched it lightly. Tap.

“I did the whole course!” Penny cheered. Her voice rang like a bell. “I improved because I practiced.”

Sal smiled. “You were strong, careful, and creative!”

Penny sipped a cup of cool water. Sip, sip. It felt fresh and kind. “Water is good after sports,” she said. She took a deep breath. In. Out. She stretched her wings. Stretch, stretch. “Stretching helps my body.”

Mama Penguin peeked into the dining room. She smelled the toasty air and saw the neat course. “What is all this, Penny?”

Penny waved. “It’s my Tabletop Tournament! I ran, jumped, tossed, and balanced. I made new games, too!”

Mama smiled. “I hear happy sounds. Ting-ting! Tap-tap! I see your bright eyes. You look proud.”

Penny nodded. “I tried. I practiced. I improved.”

Mama clapped softly. “That is the heart of sports. Try your best, be safe, and have fun. Your ideas make it special.”

Penny gave Mama a big penguin hug. “Sports can be creative,” she said. “I can make rules. I can change them. I can learn.”

She put the spoons back, stacked the cups, and folded the napkins. Rustle, rustle. The dining room felt calm and tidy again. Penny looked at the little star sticky note. She pressed it to her scarf. “This star is for effort,” she said. “I will keep it close.”

Sal waved from the chair. “Tomorrow, new games?”

Penny beamed. “Yes! Tomorrow, I’ll practice again and cheer for you, too!”

The dining room glowed in the soft afternoon light. Penny’s heart felt strong. She learned that sports mean moving your body, using your mind, and being kind to yourself. Practice brings improvement. Creative ideas make it fun. Believing in yourself makes you brave.

Penny waddled out, humming her spoon song. Ting-ting, ting-ting. “I am proud of me,” she whispered. “And I will keep trying.”

The End.
