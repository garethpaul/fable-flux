# Panda Pippa on the Rooftop Garden

Panda Pippa was a patient panda. She lived in a tall bamboo house with a soft, sunny rooftop. On the rooftop was a tiny garden. The garden had crunchy carrots, sweet strawberries, and a little patch of green, wiggly lettuce. The air smelled fresh, like mint and rain.

Every morning, Pippa climbed the steps slowly. Step, step, step. She listened to the birds sing, “Tweet, tweet!” She felt the cool breeze tickle her fur and the warm sun kiss her cheeks. Pippa watered the plants with a blue watering can. “Sip, sip, sip,” went the water. “Grow strong,” she whispered. “Good food helps bodies grow.”

One day, Pippa made a plan. “I will make a rainbow snack,” she said. “Red strawberries, orange carrots, green lettuce.” She patted her tummy. “Healthy food gives me energy and courage!”

Pippa set out a small bamboo basket. She hummed a happy tune. But just then, a quick shadow whooshed over the garden. “Whoosh!” Something rustled the lettuce. “Rustle, rustle!” Pippa peeked. Her eyes grew wide.

“Pip! Pip! Pip!” chirped a little bird with bright eyes. It pecked, pecked, pecked at a strawberry. A tiny squirrel skittered, “Skrit-skrit!” It nibbled a carrot top. The strawberry rolled. The carrot fell. The lettuce leaves drooped.

Pippa’s heart felt hot and thumpy. “Oh no,” she said softly. “My snack!” Her mouth turned down, and her eyes got wet. She took a slow breath. In, out. In, out. Pippa was patient, but she was also sad.

The little bird hopped closer. “Pip?” it chirped, tilting its head. The squirrel tucked its paws. “S-sorry,” it squeaked. “We were hungry. We saw the food. We didn’t ask.”

Pippa looked at the garden. She smelled the crushed leaves, like a green tea smell. She listened to the wind. She touched a soft strawberry that had a tiny bite. Her tummy rumbled, but her heart softened. She remembered her lesson: Healthy bodies need food. Kind hearts forgive.

Pippa sat on the warm roof tiles. “I feel upset,” she said in a calm voice. “I worked hard to grow this food. I wanted to share, but we need to ask first.”

The bird drooped its wings. “Pip-pip. I was not brave enough to ask,” it said.

The squirrel’s tail trembled. “I was scared you would say no,” it whispered.

Pippa blinked. She took another deep breath. She felt the warm sun, like a hug. “It takes courage to ask,” Pippa said gently. “And it takes courage to forgive.”

She looked at the bird and the squirrel. She looked at the garden. Pippa put her paws together. “I forgive you,” she said. “Next time, please ask. We can make a plan.”

The bird chirped, “Thank you! Pip-pip!” The squirrel sighed with relief. “Thank you,” it said, shoulders relaxing.

“Let’s be brave together,” Pippa smiled. “Let’s fix the garden and make a snack.”

They got to work. Pippa pressed the soil. Press, press. The squirrel patted seeds with tiny paws. Pat, pat. The bird fetched a small straw piece for a plant bed. Flip, flap, drop! They watered the sprouts. “Sip, sip,” sang the watering can. The garden smelled clean and new.

Soon, they picked just enough: three strawberries, two carrots, and a handful of lettuce. Pippa washed the food in a little bowl. Splash, swish! She cut soft slices with care. Snip, snip. She arranged the colors in a rainbow shape on a bamboo plate.

“This is our Courage Snack,” Pippa said. “Red for brave hearts, orange for strong bodies, green for calm minds.”

They sat in a circle on the rooftop. The tiles were warm. The breeze was cool. The skyline looked like blue blocks. “Crunch!” went the carrots. “Mmm,” went the strawberries. The lettuce was crisp and tickly on their tongues.

The bird peeped, “Pip, I will ask next time. I will be brave.”

The squirrel nodded. “I will too. We can help the garden grow. We can share.”

Pippa felt light and happy. “Forgiving made my heart feel better,” she said. “Healthy food helped my body feel strong. And asking took courage.”

After their snack, they drew a simple sign together. Pippa wrote, “Please ask before you nibble.” The bird added a tiny strawberry picture. The squirrel drew a carrot. They hung the sign by the garden. The wind fluttered it. Flap, flap.

They looked at their work. The garden was not perfect, but it was cared for. The friends smiled at each other. The sun slid lower, painting the sky peach and gold. The rooftop smelled like sweet leaves and evening air.

Pippa hugged the bird and the squirrel. “Thank you for helping,” she said.

“Thank you for forgiving,” said the squirrel.

“Thank you for sharing,” sang the bird.

Panda Pippa climbed down the steps. Step, step, step. Her tummy was happy. Her heart was calm. She whispered, “Forgiveness helps friends grow. Courage helps us ask. Good food helps us feel strong.” And that night, she slept with a smile.

The End.
