# Tully’s Tidy Shell Plan

Tully the turtle was very shy. He liked to peek from his shell and watch butterflies dance in the sunny backyard. The grass felt soft under his feet, and the warm sun made his shell cozy. Birds sang sweet songs. The air smelled like fresh flowers and clean laundry.

Tully loved little treasures. He kept shiny buttons, smooth pebbles, a tiny yellow leaf, and a red ribbon inside his shell. But his treasures were all mixed up.

One bright morning, Tully wanted to go see his best friend, Bibi Bunny. He peeked out and said, “I’m coming soon, Bibi!”

“Okay!” Bibi called from the carrot patch. “Let’s play bakery. Bring your red ribbon for a pretend cake!”

Tully pulled into his shell to find the ribbon. He looked left. He looked right. He looked under a pebble. He bumped a button. Clink! Clatter! Everything tumbled.

“Oh no,” Tully whispered. “I can’t find anything.”

His shell felt crowded and messy. He felt a little sad. He wanted to cry, but he took a slow breath. He could smell the clean laundry again, and it made him feel calm.

“I need a plan,” Tully said softly. “A tidy plan.”

Just then, Sunny the bluebird landed on the fence. “Tweet? Are you okay, Tully?”

“I lost my red ribbon,” Tully said. “My shell is a mess.”

Sunny tilted her head. “Let’s organize! We can make little spots. Spots help things go home.”

Tully blinked. “Spots?”

“Yes,” Sunny sang. “One spot for buttons, one for pebbles, one for keepsakes. And we can wash the dusty things, too. Clean things feel nice.”

Bibi Bunny hopped over with a happy thump-thump. “I can help! We can plan together. Step one: sort. Step two: clean. Step three: label. Step four: play!”

Tully smiled a tiny smile. “That sounds good.”

They sat in the sunny backyard. The grass tickled their toes. Tully carefully took his treasures out and set them on a leaf blanket. He felt the smooth pebbles, cool and round. He heard the buttons go click-click as he stacked them. He smelled the dusty old ribbon and wrinkled his nose.

“Step one: sort,” Bibi said. “Buttons in one pile.”

Tully placed the buttons together. “Click, click.”

“Pebbles in one pile,” Sunny chirped.

Tully made a pebble pile. “Plop, plop.”

“Special keepsakes in one pile,” Bibi said. “Like the yellow leaf.”

Tully put the yellow leaf and a tiny bell in a special pile. His shy smile got bigger.

“Step two: clean,” Sunny said. “Let’s give them a bath!”

They brought a small bowl of warm water. The water felt gentle on Tully’s hands. Bubbles tickled his nose. He giggled. “It smells like soap!”

Sunny dipped the buttons. “Swish, swish.” Bibi wiped the pebbles with a soft cloth. “Shiny!”

Tully washed the red ribbon. “Swoosh.” It turned bright and clean. He held it up. “I found it!”

Bibi clapped. “Hooray! But we’re not done.”

“Step three: label,” Sunny chirped. “Labels help us remember.”

Tully was shy about asking for help, but he tried. “Bibi, can we make picture labels? I don’t read words yet.”

Bibi nodded. “Yes. We can draw pictures. That is good planning.”

They used small leaf cards. Bibi drew a button picture: a big circle with dots. Sunny drew a pebble picture: oval shapes. Tully drew a ribbon picture: a wavy line with a bow. He felt proud and brave.

Tully looked inside his shell. “I need spots.” He made tiny pockets from clean leaves and little sticks. He placed the picture labels on each pocket.

Sunny said, “Where does the ribbon go?”

Tully put the ribbon in the wavy-line pocket. “Right here.”

“Where do the buttons go?” Bibi asked.

“Click-click in the circle pocket,” Tully said.

“Where do the pebbles go?” Sunny asked.

“Plop in the oval pocket,” Tully said.

His shell felt roomy and neat. He could hear the birds again. He could feel the gentle wind and the warm sun. He felt calm inside.

“Step four: play!” Bibi cheered. “But first, may we use the ribbon for the pretend cake?”

Tully’s shy voice was soft. “I like the ribbon safe in its spot. But we can make a plan.” He took a brave breath. “We can use the ribbon for the cake. Then we put it back when we’re done.”

Bibi nodded. “That is good negotiation. We share and then we tidy.”

Sunny smiled. “A plan we all like!”

They played bakery in the sunny backyard. They pretended the pebbles were chocolate chips. The buttons were cookies. The ribbon was a sweet bow on the cake. Tully laughed with his friends. His shyness felt smaller.

When they finished, Tully said, “Clean-up time!”

They washed pretend crumbs off the buttons and pebbles. They dried the ribbon carefully. “Swish, swish. Pat, pat.” Tully put each treasure back in its spot. Click. Plop. Swoosh.

Bibi said, “Your shell looks so tidy!”

Sunny chirped, “Organization makes room for fun!”

Tully stood tall, as tall as a shy turtle could. “My tidy plan helped me find my ribbon, play with friends, and clean up. I can sort, clean, label, and put things back.”

He looked at his friends. “Thank you for helping me plan.”

Bibi hugged him. Sunny sang a happy tune. The sun felt warm. The backyard smelled fresh. Tully felt proud and peaceful.

From that day on, Tully used his four steps: sort, clean, label, and put back. His shell stayed neat, and his heart felt light.

The lesson Tully learned: when we plan and organize, we can find what we need, keep it clean, share kindly, and have more time to play.

The End.
