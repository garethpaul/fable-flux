# Benny's Big Bathroom Fix

Benny the beaver loved to build things. His big orange teeth were perfect for chewing wood. His flat tail made a great tool for patting things into place. But today, Benny felt worried.

"Oh no!" cried Mrs. Rabbit from the bathroom. "The sink is dripping! Drip, drip, drip!"

Benny heard the water drops hitting the floor. Splash, splash, splash. He wanted to help, but he felt scared.

"I'm just a little beaver," Benny whispered to himself. "I don't know how to fix sinks."

Mr. Bear poked his head out of the bathroom. "The water won't stop dripping," he said. "We need help!"

Benny's heart beat fast. Thump, thump, thump. All his friends needed him, but what if he couldn't fix it?

"Maybe I should try," Benny said quietly. He walked slowly to the bathroom.

The bathroom smelled fresh and clean, like soap and mint. The white tiles felt cool under Benny's paws. He looked up at the tall sink. Water dripped from the shiny faucet.

"It's so high up," Benny worried. "And I'm so small."

But then Benny remembered something important. "I am good at building," he told himself. "I can figure this out!"

Benny looked around the bathroom carefully. He saw a step stool by the bathtub. Perfect! He pushed it over to the sink with his strong paws.

"First, I need to see the problem," Benny said. He climbed up on the step stool. Now he could reach the faucet!

Benny touched the handle gently. It felt loose and wobbly. "Aha!" he said. "The handle needs to be tighter."

Mrs. Rabbit brought Benny a small wrench. It felt heavy and cold in his paws.

"You can do this, Benny," she said kindly.

Benny took a deep breath. The air smelled like lavender soap. He carefully turned the wrench. Click, click, click.

"Is it working?" asked Mr. Bear.

Benny turned the faucet handle. No more drips! The water stayed inside where it belonged.

"I did it!" Benny shouted happily. His tail slapped the floor with excitement. Thwap, thwap, thwap!

"Hooray for Benny!" cheered Mrs. Rabbit.

"You saved the day!" said Mr. Bear.

Benny felt warm and proud inside. His chest puffed out with confidence. "I was scared at first," he said. "But I remembered that I'm good at fixing things."

Mrs. Rabbit hugged Benny gently. "You are brave and helpful," she said.

"And smart too!" added Mr. Bear. "You made a plan and solved the problem."

Benny smiled big. His orange teeth gleamed in the bathroom light. "When I felt scared, I reminded myself what I'm good at," he said. "Then I made a plan. First the step stool, then the wrench, then fix the handle!"

"That's perfect planning," said Mrs. Rabbit.

From that day on, whenever Benny felt unsure about helping, he remembered the bathroom fix. He would think about what he was good at. He would make a plan. And he would try his best.

"I can help my friends," Benny would say to himself. "I am capable and brave!"

And you know what? Benny was right. He helped fix many more things around their home. Each time, he felt more confident and proud.

The bathroom stayed dry and happy, and so did Benny.

The End.
