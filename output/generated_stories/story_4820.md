# Ollie Otter’s Quiet Bubbles

Ollie was a playful otter with bright eyes and a shiny whiskered smile. He lived in a cozy house with a special place downstairs. The basement lab smelled like warm soap and fresh lemons. Lights blinked. Buttons clicked. Tiny machines hummed, “whirr-whirr.” Ollie loved it there. He called it his Bubble Lab.

“Good morning, bubbles!” Ollie giggled as he slid down the banister. Plop! He landed on a soft mat. “Today I will make the biggest bubble of all!”

He put on his safety goggles. Click! He tied his little apron. Swish! He took a deep breath. His whiskers tickled. “I am ready,” he said.

In the lab, there was a bubble table, a bubble book, and a blue bottle marked “Soap Mix.” There was also a timer that beeped kindly. Beep-beep! Ollie opened his bubble book. The pages smelled like paper and a little like sugar. He read the simple steps.

“Step one,” said Ollie. “Wash paws.” He stopped. He wanted to skip. He wanted to hurry. He wanted to blow bubbles right now. His paws felt a bit sticky from breakfast honey. The honey felt thick and sweet.

Ollie wiggled. “Maybe I can skip washing,” he whispered. He picked up the bubble wand. His paws slipped. The wand dropped with a clink. The soap splashed. “Oh!” He touched his face. Sticky. Slick. Silly.

Just then, the timer beeped again, like a gentle friend. Beep-beep. Ollie looked at the book. He read the little note: “Self-care helps us care for our work.”

Ollie nodded slowly. “Self-care first,” he said. “I will be kind to me.”

He went to the sink. The water felt warm and soft on his paws. “Swish, swish,” he sang. The soap smelled like oranges. He scrubbed between his fingers. He washed his whiskers, too. He dried with a fluffy towel. Rub-rub. His paws felt light and clean. He smiled. “Ahh,” he said. “This feels nice.”

Back at the table, Ollie read “Step two: Mix and wait.” Waiting was hard for a playful otter. He wanted to bounce. He wanted to blow. But the book said, “For strong bubbles, wait three beeps.”

“Patience,” said Ollie. He set the timer. Beep. He looked around. The humming machines made a cozy song. The lemon smell was soft and bright. Ollie took slow breaths. In and out. He hugged himself gently. “I will be calm,” he said. “I can be patient.”

He remembered his friend Dot the beetle, who liked quiet. Dot lived in a tiny jar-home on the shelf, with a leaf for a bed. “Good morning, Dot,” Ollie whispered. Dot waved a tiny leg.

Beep two. Ollie’s tummy rumbled. “Snack time is self-care, too,” he said. He nibbled a crunchy apple slice. Crunch, crunch. Sweet juice dripped on his tongue. He sipped cool water. Sip, ahh. He felt strong.

Beep three. “Time!” Ollie cheered. He dipped the wand. The soap shimmered like rainbows. He took a gentle breath. He blew. Whooo. A bubble grew—round, smooth, shiny. It floated up like a slow moon.

“Wow,” said Ollie. He clapped softly so the bubble would not pop. He blew another. Pop! It burst right away. “Hmm,” he said. “Why did that bubble pop?”

He opened his bubble book. “When bubbles pop, learn why,” it said. “Knowledge helps us grow.” He read little pictures. One showed big breaths. One showed tiny breaths. One showed a fan blowing too hard.

Ollie tried again with a small breath. Whoo. The bubble floated. He smiled. He tried a big breath. WHOOO—pop! “Oh,” said Ollie. “Too strong.” He learned something new. “Gentle helps.”

Ollie looked at Dot’s jar. The fan by Dot was whirring. “Whirr-whirr.” It made the air windy. The bubbles wiggled and popped. Ollie frowned. “Dot likes air. Bubbles like calm.” He thought. He wanted big bubbles. Dot wanted cool air.

Ollie took a breath. He felt a little tug inside. He wanted both things. He needed a plan. “Compromise,” he said, reading a small word in his book. “Compromise means we both get a little of what we need.”

Ollie spoke softly. “Dot, I will move the fan to low and tilt it to your leaf, not the bubble table. Is that okay?” Dot waved again. “Tippity-tap,” her tiny feet said on the glass.

Ollie turned the fan to low. Click. He angled it away. The air near the bubble table became calm and gentle. The air near Dot felt cool. “We did it,” said Ollie.

Now the lab felt just right. Ollie stood tall. He washed his paws one more time. Swish. He stretched his neck and shoulders. “Self-care helps me feel ready,” he said.

He dipped the big wand. He closed his eyes for a moment. He felt his breath. In. Out. Calm and kind. He blew slowly. Whooooo.

A giant bubble grew, smooth as jelly and bright as a rainbow. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple danced on its skin. It floated past the blinking lights. It carried a tiny lemon smell and a little soap sparkle. It floated and floated. Ollie and Dot watched with wide eyes. “Ohhh,” they whispered together.

The bubble drifted and then gently touched the mat. It rested there, round and happy, before it finally went, “pop,” like a soft kiss.

Ollie clapped. “I did it! Self-care, patience, knowledge, and a kind compromise,” he said. His heart felt warm. His paws felt clean. His mind felt smart. Dot tapped the glass, like a tiny cheer.

Ollie cleaned the table. Wipe, wipe. He put away the wand. He closed the book. He yawned a little. “Rest is self-care, too,” he said. He turned off the lights. Click. The lab hummed a sleepy song.

As he climbed the stairs, Ollie smiled. “When I take care of me, I can do great things,” he said. “And I can be kind to others, too.”

The End.
