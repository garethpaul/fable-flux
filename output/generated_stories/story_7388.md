# Benny Beaver’s Backyard Beat

Benny was a busy beaver with bright eyes and a big, bristly tail. He lived near a sunny backyard where birds sang, flowers nodded, and the grass felt warm and tickly. One morning, Benny peeked through the fence and wiggled his whiskers. “Today, I will play outside and make music!” he said.

The sun was golden and soft. The air smelled like sweet grass and juicy strawberries. A gentle breeze went whoooosh, and the leaves whispered shhh-shhh. Benny took a deep breath in and let it out slowly. “In… and out… I am calm and ready,” he whispered. He put on his little red cap. He tapped his tail on the ground. Tap-tap-tap! It made a friendly sound.

In the sunny backyard, Benji the bluebird fluttered down. “Cheep-cheep! Can I play, too?”

“Yes,” said Benny. “We will make a backyard band!”

Daisy the bunny hopped by. “Boing! I like to drum on the watering can.”

“Welcome!” said Benny. “We will all get a turn.”

Soon, Molly the squirrel skittered along the fence. “I can shake the seed rattle,” she said, tail swish-swish.

“Great,” said Benny. “Let’s set up. We will play outside, move our bodies, and listen with our ears.”

Benny lined up the backyard treasures: a metal watering can, a wooden bucket, smooth sticks, and a seed rattle made from an old tin with pebbles. He pointed to each one and spoke slowly. “First, we warm up our bodies. Then, we follow directions. We will play fair and share.”

They stretched their paws and wings. Reach up, wiggle, shake, and stomp! The grass felt cool and squishy under their feet. The sun gave their cheeks a toasty kiss.

Benny said, “Rule one: We listen for my count. One, two, three, play! Rule two: We take turns. Rule three: If it feels too loud, we use quiet paws.”

Benji chirped, “Cheep! I can do that.”

Daisy nodded. “I can be gentle.”

Molly whispered, “Me too.”

Benny counted, “One, two, three, play!”

Tap-tap on the can. Thump-thump on the bucket. Shake-a-shake with the seeds. Benji sang, “Cheep-cheep-cheeeep!” The sounds danced together in the sunny air. Benny smiled. He loved making music outside.

“Now freeze!” said Benny. Everyone stopped. The backyard got quiet. They could hear the breeze, the bees, and a far-away lawnmower humming mmmmmm.

Benny took another slow breath. “That was good listening,” he said. “Now we will march around the yard. Follow me. Walk to the beat.”

They stepped in a line—step, step, step—around the garden stones. Daisy kept the watering can steady. Molly shook the seeds softly. Benji flapped from fence post to flower. Benny tapped his tail, snare-tail, tap-tap!

But then Daisy saw a shiny stick. “I want that one now!” she said and grabbed it while Molly was still using it.

Molly’s ears drooped. “Hey… that was my turn.”

Benji’s chirp got small. “Cheep?”

The music wobbled, then stopped.

Benny took a calm breath. In… and out. He placed his paw on his chest. “Let’s pause,” he said softly. “We can be fair. Daisy, how do you feel?”

Daisy looked at the shiny stick. She shuffled her feet. “I feel excited. I want the shiny stick.”

Benny nodded. “Thank you for telling us. Molly, how do you feel?”

Molly hugged the seed rattle. “I feel sad. I was using the stick first.”

Benny smiled kindly. “We can solve this. Fair means we share and take turns. Let’s use a timer song. We will each play for one short song.”

Benji chirped, “I can sing the timer!”

Benny said, “Great! And we follow directions. First Molly plays while Benji sings. When the song stops, we switch. Daisy waits with quiet paws.”

Daisy took a deep breath. In… and out. “Okay. I can wait.”

Benji sang, “Cheep-cheep-cheep—cheep-cheep-cheep—stop!” Molly played while Benji sang, then stopped. Daisy’s turn! Daisy smiled and tapped the shiny stick to the beat. Molly shook the seeds.

“Thank you for being fair,” said Benny. “That felt good.”

They started a new song. Benny counted them in. “One, two, three, play!” The backyard filled with happy sounds. The sun warmed their backs. The grass smelled green and fresh. Their feet went step-step. Their hearts felt light.

After a while, Benny said, “Water break!” They sipped cool water and sat in the shade. Benny asked, “What did we learn today?”

Daisy said, “Playing outside feels good in my body.”

Molly said, “Sharing and waiting my turn is fair.”

Benji said, “Listening to directions helps our band.”

Benny added, “When we feel big feelings, we breathe. In… and out. Then we choose kind actions.”

They stood up for one last march. “Backyard Beat Parade!” shouted Benny. They moved around the yard, waving, tapping, shaking, and singing. Neighbors peeked over the fence and smiled at the cheerful music.

At the end, Benny held up his paw. “Freeze!” Everyone stopped. Quiet again. They could hear the leaves, the birds, and their own gentle breaths.

Benny grinned. “We had an outdoor adventure. We used our bodies, our ears, and our hearts. We were fair. We followed directions. We made music together.”

“Cheep-cheep hooray!” sang Benji.

“Boing-boing hooray!” hopped Daisy.

“Swish-swish hooray!” squeaked Molly.

Benny patted the grass. “Thank you, sunny backyard.”

They packed up the tools, gave high-fives and wing-fives, and promised to play again tomorrow.

The clear lesson: Playing outside helps our bodies and hearts. When we listen, take turns, and breathe, everyone has fun.

The End.
