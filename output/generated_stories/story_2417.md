# Gigi the Giraffe’s Gentle Office Day

Gigi the giraffe had a long, kind neck and very curious eyes. She worked in a cozy home office. The room smelled like fresh paper and sweet tea. The window was open, and a soft breeze tickled her ears. Click-clack went her keyboard. Tap-tap went her pencil. Gigi smiled. “Today, I will be calm and kind,” she whispered.

On her desk, Gigi kept colored boxes. Blue for papers. Yellow for crayons. Green for glue and tape. “Everything has a home,” she said. She liked to keep things neat, so her ideas could find space to grow.

Ding-dong! The doorbell sang. Gigi blinked. “I wonder who that is.”

She stretched her long neck to the door. There stood two friends: Poppy the penguin and Zuzu the zebra. Poppy wore a tiny scarf. Zuzu wore big, bright socks.

“Hi, Gigi!” said Poppy. “Can we work in your office?”

“We want to make a big card,” said Zuzu. “It’s for Miss Owl’s birthday.”

Gigi’s heart felt warm. “Yes! Come in. We can make a card together.”

They waddled and trotted to the home office. The breeze fluttered the curtains. Sunlight made soft stripes on the floor, just like Zuzu’s socks. Gigi pointed to the boxes. “Let’s be organized. Blue for papers, yellow for crayons, green for glue.”

Poppy clapped. “I like boxes!”

Zuzu sniffed the crayons. “Mmm, they smell like apples and rainbows.”

They began to make the card. Poppy cut shapes with careful snips. “Snip, snip.” Zuzu colored with big, bold strokes. “Scritch, scritch.” Gigi wrote neat letters: “Happy Birthday, Miss Owl!”

Then, oops! Zuzu’s crayon rolled under the desk. Zuzu reached for it and bumped the blue paper box. The papers flew like soft birds. Whoosh!

“Oh no,” said Poppy. “The box fell.”

Zuzu’s ears drooped. “I’m sorry,” Zuzu whispered. “I didn’t mean to.”

Gigi felt a pinch in her tummy. She liked her things tidy. She looked at the papers, the box, and Zuzu’s sad eyes. Gigi took a deep breath, slow and gentle. In... and out. The room smelled like paper and sweet tea again.

“It’s okay,” Gigi said softly. “Accidents happen. We can fix it together.”

“That is very tolerant,” said Poppy, nodding. “You are being patient and kind.”

Gigi smiled. “We all like different things. Zuzu likes big, bold coloring. I like neat lines. Poppy likes careful shapes. We can respect each other. We can share space and ideas.”

Zuzu peeked up. “Can I help pick up the papers?”

“Yes,” said Gigi. “Let’s make a plan. First, we put papers back in the blue box. Next, crayons in yellow. Last, glue in green. We can do it step by step.”

They picked up the papers. Rustle, rustle. They sorted crayons. Clink, clink. They clicked the glue cap shut. Click!

Zuzu said, “I want to make amends.” Zuzu held out a sticker shaped like a star. “I will add this to the card to say sorry for the mess.”

Gigi’s eyes shone. “Thank you, Zuzu. That is a kind choice.”

Poppy waddled closer. “We can learn from this. Next time, we keep crayons near the middle.”

Gigi nodded. “Yes. That’s a growth mindset. We learn, and we try again.”

They returned to the card. Gigi whispered, “We can use our different styles. Zuzu, you make big, bright borders. Poppy, you make small shapes. I will write the words.”

They worked together. The room was full of happy sounds. Scritch for coloring. Snip for cutting. Tap for writing. The sunshine warmed their backs. The card looked lovely—bright borders, tiny stars, and neat words. Right in the corner, Zuzu’s shiny star sticker sparkled.

“Let’s read it,” said Poppy.

Gigi read in a clear, gentle voice. “Happy Birthday, Miss Owl! We made this together with love.”

They all smiled. Zuzu’s ears perked up. “I feel better,” Zuzu said. “Thank you for being patient, Gigi.”

Gigi touched Zuzu’s shoulder with her soft nose. “Thank you for saying sorry and helping fix it.”

Poppy flapped a little. “We respected each other’s ways. We were organized. We learned and grew!”

They cleaned the desk again. Blue papers in blue. Yellow crayons in yellow. Green glue in green. Gigi looked around. The office felt calm and tidy. Her heart felt calm and tidy, too.

“Friends,” Gigi said, “being tolerant means we stay kind, even when things are different or a little messy. We listen. We help. We make amends. We keep trying.”

Poppy and Zuzu nodded. “We will remember,” they said together.

They carried the card to the door. The breeze whispered goodbye. The sweet tea smelled extra sweet.

Gigi waved her long neck. “Happy day!”

“Happy day!” sang Poppy and Zuzu.

The End.
