# Panda Poko’s Rooftop Choice

Panda Poko liked to be patient. Poko took deep breaths, counted slowly, and thought before acting. One sunny morning, Poko climbed the stairs to the rooftop garden. The air felt warm and soft. A tiny breeze tickled Poko’s ears. The rooftop smelled like fresh leaves and wet dirt from yesterday’s rain. Birds chirped, “Chee-chee!” and clouds looked like fluffy dumplings in the blue sky.

“Today I will learn a new skill,” Poko said. “I will practice hopping over the soft mats.”

On the rooftop, three colorful mats lay in a row: a red mat, a blue mat, and a green mat. A short balance beam stood beside them, and a little bell hung at the end. If you touched the bell, it rang, “Ding!”

Poko’s friend, Miko the sparrow, fluttered down. “Hi, Poko! What will you do first?”

Poko smiled. “I am deciding. I have three choices. I can hop on the mats. I can walk the beam. I can ring the bell.”

Miko tilted her head. “Which one?”

Poko put a paw on the red mat. It felt springy and bouncy. “Hmm,” Poko hummed. “I will make a plan. Step one: Look at the choices. Step two: Think about what I can do. Step three: Choose. Step four: Try. Step five: Check how it feels.”

Miko chirped, “That is a good plan!”

Poko closed his eyes and took a slow breath. In. Out. He opened his eyes and looked at the mats, the beam, and the bell. “I want to move my body. I want to learn. I also want to be safe.”

Poko looked at the red mat. “Red mat is closest. That is easy to start.” He looked at the blue mat. “Blue mat is in the middle. That is a little harder.” He looked at the green mat. “Green mat is last. That is the farthest jump.”

Poko nodded. “I choose the red mat first.”

Poko bent his knees. He felt the warm sun on his fur. He heard the wind whisper. “Hop!” Poko hopped onto the red mat. The mat squeaked, “Squeak!” Poko landed with a soft thump. He smiled. “I did it.”

Miko flapped her wings. “Good job! What next?”

Poko sat on the mat and thought. “Step five: Check how it feels. My legs feel strong. My heart feels happy. I can try the blue mat now.”

Poko stood up. He wiggled his toes inside his soft paw pads. “Hop!” He hopped to the blue mat. The blue mat felt cool and smooth. “Thump!” He wobbled a little. Poko paused, took a breath, and put his paws out to balance.

Miko called, “Do you want help?”

Poko shook his head gently. “Thank you, Miko. I will try again. I am patient.”

Poko bent his knees deeper. “Hop!” He landed steadier this time. “I did it again,” he said softly. His eyes shined.

Now Poko looked at the green mat. The space between the mats looked bigger. The bell at the end glowed in the sun, “Ding” waiting.

Poko said, “This is a bigger jump. I will decide slowly. If I do not feel ready, I can practice more. If I feel ready, I can try once.”

Poko listened to his body. His legs felt a little shaky, but strong. His tummy felt fluttery, like little butterflies. Poko put a paw on his chest. “Hello, feelings,” he whispered. “Thank you for talking to me.”

Miko chirped softly, “What does your body say?”

Poko nodded. “My body says, ‘Warm up more.’ So I choose more practice.”

Poko practiced small hops on the red and blue mats. He counted, “One, two, three, four, five.” With each hop, the squeaks and thumps sounded like a friendly drum. His breath went in and out, slow and steady. His paws felt steady. His smile grew.

After more hops, Poko stood tall. “Now my body says, ‘I feel ready to try the green mat.’”

Poko bent his knees, swung his arms, and looked at where he wanted to land. “I can do this,” he said. “I am patient and I choose wisely.”

“Hop!” Poko jumped to the green mat. For a tiny moment, Poko felt like he was floating. Then, “Thump!” He landed on the green mat. He wobbled. He froze. He took one slow breath. He placed one paw wide, then the other. He felt steady again.

Miko cheered, “You did it, Poko!”

Poko giggled. “I did it because I chose a good plan.”

Poko looked at the balance beam next. It was not high, but it was narrow. “Do I go on the beam now?” Poko asked himself. He did Step One again: Look. Step Two: Think. Step Three: Choose. “I will try the beam with tiny steps and my arms out.”

Poko put one paw on the beam. The wood felt smooth and warm. He took tiny, careful steps. Tap-tap-tap. He looked at the bell. “Almost there.”

Miko flew beside him. “You are doing great!”

Poko reached the end and touched the bell. “Ding!” sang the bell, bright and sweet. The sound made Poko’s heart glow.

Poko hopped off and sat on the soft grass by the garden box. He sipped cool water from his bottle. “I moved my body. I built my skills. I made careful choices,” he said.

Miko asked, “What did you learn, Poko?”

Poko smiled. “When I make a plan and listen to my body and feelings, I make good decisions. I can practice, be patient, and try again. That helps me feel strong and brave.”

Miko chirped, “That is a big lesson!”

Poko nodded. “Big lessons can come from small hops.”

The sun dipped lower. The breeze felt gentle, like a soft hug. Poko waved to the bell. “Thank you for the ding.” He patted the mats. “Thank you for the squeaks.” He patted his chest. “Thank you, patient heart.”

Poko and Miko watched the clouds turn peach and pink. Poko felt calm, proud, and sure.

Today, Poko chose well.

The End.
