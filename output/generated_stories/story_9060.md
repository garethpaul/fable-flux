# Polly's Pantry Problem

Polly the parrot loved her colorful feathers that sparkled green and blue in the morning sunlight. She lived with the Johnson family and had her very own perch right next to the kitchen pantry. Every day, she watched the family open and close the pantry door, and she was very curious about what was inside.

One sunny Tuesday morning, Polly noticed something troubling. Mrs. Johnson opened the pantry door and sighed deeply. "Oh my," she said, shaking her head. "Look at all this wasted food!"

Polly tilted her head and peered inside. She could see bread that had gone moldy, bananas that were brown and mushy, and milk that smelled sour. Her bright eyes grew wide with concern.

"Squawk! That's not good!" Polly chirped. She flapped her wings and thought carefully about what she had observed. Every week, she watched the family throw away food that had gone bad. This made her little parrot heart feel sad.

Polly knew she had to help. She was a smart bird who loved solving problems! She flew down to the pantry shelf and began to examine everything carefully. The crackers felt stale and crunchy under her feet. The apples looked wrinkled and soft. Some cans were dusty because they had been forgotten for so long.

"I need to talk to the family," Polly said to herself. She had learned that the best way to solve problems was to communicate kindly and clearly.

When little Emma came into the kitchen for breakfast, Polly spoke up assertively but gently. "Emma! Emma! Food waste, food waste!" she squawked, pointing her wing toward the pantry.

Emma giggled. "What are you trying to tell me, Polly?"

Polly flew to the pantry and tapped her beak on the old bread. "Bad food! Waste! Save! Save!" she repeated, using her most caring voice.

Emma's eyes lit up with understanding. "Mom! Dad! Come quick! I think Polly is trying to tell us something important!"

When the whole family gathered around, Polly put on quite a show. She pointed to the fresh apples with her wing, then to the old ones. She made happy chirping sounds near the good food and sad, quiet sounds near the spoiled food.

"I think Polly wants us to use our food better," said Dad thoughtfully. "She's showing us that we're wasting too much."

Mrs. Johnson nodded. "You're absolutely right, Polly! We should check our food more often and use the older items first."

From that day forward, the Johnson family created a new system. They organized their pantry with older items in front and newer items in back. They checked expiration dates weekly and made shopping lists to avoid buying too much food.

Polly became the family's official "Food Conservation Helper." Every morning, she would inspect the pantry with her keen eyes and alert the family if anything needed to be used soon. She felt proud knowing that she was helping save food and protecting the environment.

The family learned that when they wasted less food, they also saved money and helped take care of the Earth. Emma even started a chart to track how much food they saved each week.

"Thank you, Polly," said Mrs. Johnson, gently stroking her feathers. "You taught us that even small changes can make a big difference."

Polly puffed up her chest proudly and squawked happily. She had used her voice, her thinking skills, and her compassion to help her family solve an important problem through peaceful communication.

The End.
