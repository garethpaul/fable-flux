# Squeaky’s Warm Kitchen Surprise

Squeaky the squirrel lived in a warm kitchen with sun on the floor and a gentle cinnamon smell in the air. The oven hummed softly. Pots made tiny clinks. The wooden spoons looked like tall trees to Squeaky. He loved to wiggle, hop, and scurry. He also loved to make things.

One bright morning, Squeaky felt a happy buzz in his paws. “Today I will make a fruit banner!” he chirped. “It will dance when I move!”

He set tiny bowls on the table. Plop went the blueberries. Plink went the grapes. Soft bananas made a squish sound, and apples gave a little crunch when sliced. Squeaky tied a bit of string around a spoon, then around a cup, making a line across the kitchen. He planned to hang fruit shapes on it—stars, hearts, and circles.

Just then, his friends came to visit. Flo the fox trotted in with light steps. Pip the pigeon flapped, flap-flap, and landed by the window. Tilly the turtle slowly slid across the smooth floor, shell scraping gently, shhhk-shhhk.

“Hello!” Squeaky squeaked. “Let’s make and move!”

Flo wagged her tail. “I like to jump!” she said.

Pip puffed his feathers. “I like to flap!”

Tilly smiled shyly. “I like to go slow,” she said in a soft voice.

Squeaky clapped his little paws. “We can all help! We will craft a banner. Then we will do a movement parade. Jump, flap, and go slow!”

They washed their paws and feathers with warm, soapy water. Splash, swish, rinse! The kitchen smelled like oranges now.

Squeaky showed the craft. “We press the fruit on soft paper. We cut shapes. Then we hang them.” He held up a red apple heart. “Ta-da!”

Flo jumped high. “I can press the fruit!” she said.

Pip flapped twice. “I can hang the shapes up top!”

Tilly looked at the scissors. She took a tiny breath. “I’m not fast at cutting,” she whispered. “My paws are slow.”

Squeaky nodded. He felt the warm oven hum. He heard the clock tick-tock. He looked at Tilly’s careful eyes. “Slow is okay,” he said. “Slow can be careful. Slow can be kind.”

But when they started, Flo bounced so much that a bowl tipped. Plop! Blueberries rolled under the table. Pip tried to hang a star, but the string wobbled. The star fell. Thump! Tilly began to cut a circle. Snip… pause… snip… pause. It took a long time.

Flo sighed. “I want to move fast.”

Pip fluttered. “I want to be up high.”

Tilly pulled her paws back a little. “Maybe I should stop,” she murmured.

Squeaky felt a tight twist in his tummy. He wanted his banner to shine. He wanted his friends to smile. He put his paws on his chest and took a big breath. In. Out. He listened to the oven hum and the clock tick. He felt the warm floor.

Then he had an idea. “We can make a plan!” he said. “We can accept how each of us moves. We can use our ways.”

He smiled at Tilly. “You cut the tiny shapes. Your slow hands are steady and neat.”

He turned to Flo. “You can press the fruit stamps. Your jumps can be soft presses. Up and down, up and down.”

He looked at Pip. “You can hang the shapes. You flap so well. You can reach high and gently clip.”

Squeaky placed sticky tape and small clips in a row. He set a mat for Flo to hop on so the bowls stayed still. He placed a comfy pad for Tilly, with a little light just for her. He tied the string tighter so Pip had a good grip.

They tried again.

Flo bent her knees and pressed. Thump… press! Thump… press! Blue hearts, purple circles, golden stars appeared on the paper.

Pip flapped up once, twice, and clipped each shape to the string. Clip. Clip. Clip. The banner swayed like a gentle wave.

Tilly snipped slowly. Snip… snip… round and smooth. Her circles were the neatest circles anyone had seen. “I did it,” she said, eyes bright.

“You did,” Squeaky said, smiling. “Your slow is strong.”

The kitchen glowed. Cinnamon, orange, and sweet fruit smells filled the air. Sunlight made the banner shine.

“Movement parade time!” Squeaky cried.

Flo led, bouncing soft steps, boing-boing! Pip followed, flap-flap-glide! Tilly moved last, slow and steady, shhhk-shhhk. Squeaky scampered beside them, arms wide, tail swishing.

They danced under the fruit banner. The shapes twirled. The clips clicked. Everyone laughed. Their different moves made a happy rhythm.

When the music of their feet and wings grew quiet, Tilly looked up. “Thank you for waiting for me.”

Squeaky nodded. “We accept each other. Fast, high, or slow—we all belong here.”

Pip puffed his chest. “Together, we made something beautiful.”

Flo wagged. “And we moved in our own ways!”

Squeaky picked up the last apple heart and held it to his chest. “Today I learned that when we accept how friends move and make, we can help each other. We can craft with care. We can dance with joy. We all fit.”

They smiled, and the oven hummed a warm, happy note. The banner swayed like a little rainbow across the kitchen. The friends felt safe, loved, and proud.

The End.
