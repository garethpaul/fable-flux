# Nutkin's Special Treehouse

High up in a tall oak tree lived a clever little squirrel named Nutkin. His treehouse was the coziest home in the whole forest, with soft moss cushions and tiny acorn cups for drinking rainwater.

One sunny morning, Nutkin woke up and stretched his fluffy tail. "What a beautiful day!" he chirped, looking out his round window. The leaves rustled gently in the warm breeze, and birds sang happy songs all around.

But as Nutkin looked down at the forest floor, his smile turned into a worried frown. "Oh no!" he gasped. There were candy wrappers, plastic bottles, and paper scattered everywhere under his tree.

"This won't do at all," Nutkin said to himself. "Our beautiful forest home needs help!"

Nutkin scampered down his tree trunk, his tiny claws clicking against the bark. He picked up a shiny wrapper that crinkled in his paws. "This doesn't belong here," he said firmly.

Just then, his friend Bella Bunny hopped over. "Good morning, Nutkin! What are you doing?"

"I'm cleaning up our forest," Nutkin replied, showing her the wrapper. "All this trash is making our home messy and unsafe for everyone."

Bella's pink nose twitched with concern. "Oh my! That's terrible. How can I help?"

"We can work together!" Nutkin said excitedly. "Many paws make light work!"

Soon, Oliver Owl flew down from his branch. "Hoot! What's all this activity about?"

"We're taking care of our forest home," Nutkin explained. "Would you like to help too?"

"Of course!" hooted Oliver. "I can carry things in my talons!"

The three friends worked all morning. Nutkin used his quick paws to gather small pieces of trash. Bella hopped around collecting bottles with her strong teeth. Oliver flew high to spot litter caught in tree branches.

"Look!" said Nutkin, wiping his furry brow. "We can put all the bottles in this pile for recycling. The paper can go here, and the other trash goes there."

"You're so smart, Nutkin!" said Bella admiringly. "You know just what to do with everything."

As they worked, more forest friends joined them. Chippy Chipmunk brought his whole family. The raccoon twins, Rascal and Rosie, rolled up their sleeves to help. Even grumpy old Badger came out of his burrow to lend a paw.

"This is wonderful!" squeaked Nutkin happily. "When we all work together and share the job, we can take better care of our home."

By afternoon, the forest floor sparkled clean. The stream bubbled clearly, and the flowers seemed to stand taller and more colorful.

"Thank you, everyone!" called Nutkin from his treehouse window. "Our forest is beautiful again because we all cared enough to help."

"And now we know to put our trash in proper places," added Oliver wisely.

"Yes!" agreed Bella. "We must always take care of our home so all the forest animals can be happy and healthy."

That evening, Nutkin sat in his treehouse, feeling proud and content. He had learned that being resourceful meant using his skills to solve problems. But even better, he had learned that sharing the work with friends made everything easier and more fun.

"Tomorrow," Nutkin said to himself as he curled up in his soft moss bed, "I'll teach the young squirrels how to keep our forest clean too. When we all take care of our home together, everyone wins!"

The forest settled into peaceful sleep, clean and beautiful once more.

The End.
