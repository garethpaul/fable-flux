# Nutmeg’s Neat Guest Room Adventure

Nutmeg was a small, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail. One sunny morning, Nutmeg peeked into the house through a tiny open window. “Hello?” she chattered softly. The guest room smelled like clean sheets and lavender. Sunlight made golden squares on the soft rug.

Nutmeg wiggled inside and looked around. “Oh my! What a busy room,” she said. A suitcase sat open on the bed. Socks peeked out like little puppets. Books were in a wiggly pile on the floor. A teddy bear lay on its back with a sleepy smile.

Nutmeg loved to help. “I can be helpful here,” she decided. Her heart felt warm and brave. She rubbed her paws together. “Let’s make this room neat!”

Just then, the door creaked. Little Lina and her grandma walked in. “We’re getting ready for our guest,” Grandma said kindly. Lina waved. “Hi, little squirrel!”

“I am Nutmeg,” the squirrel chirped. “May I help?”

Grandma smiled. “Yes, please. We would love help.”

Lina clapped. “We can make a plan!” she said.

Nutmeg’s whiskers twitched. “A plan keeps things tidy.” She pointed to the suitcase. “First: clothes.” She pointed to the books. “Next: books.” She patted the teddy bear. “Last: friends.”

“Friends?” giggled Lina.

“Toys can be friends,” Nutmeg said with a nod. “They like cozy spots.”

They began with the clothes. Nutmeg picked up a soft blue sock. It felt warm and stretchy. She put it with its partner. “Sock, sock, match, match!” she sang.

Lina joined in. “Sock, sock, match, match!”

Grandma folded shirts that smelled like sunshine. “Fold, fold, smooth and hold,” she hummed.

Nutmeg made labels from sticky notes. She wrote big simple words with a thick pen. “Socks,” she said slowly, tapping each letter. “S-O-C-K-S.”

Lina traced the letters with her finger. “Socks,” she repeated. “I can read it!”

Nutmeg’s eyes sparkled. “Words help us. Words are friends too!” She stuck the label on a drawer. “Labels tell us where things go. That is organization.”

They moved to the books. The covers were shiny and colorful. One book had a red apple on it. Another showed a brave ladybug. Nutmeg sniffed a page. “Mmm, paper smells like the forest,” she said.

Lina stroked the pages. “This one goes night-night,” she whispered, placing a bedtime story near the lamp.

“Good idea,” said Grandma. “Let’s sort: bedtime books by the bed, picture books on the low shelf.”

Nutmeg made more labels. “Bedtime,” she said. “B-E-D-T-I-M-E.” She sounded it out slowly. Lina repeated the sounds and smiled. “I can say big words!”

Nutmeg nodded. “Language is like music. We listen. We say. We learn.”

They picked up the teddy bear and a small toy train. “Friends go together,” Nutmeg chirped. They placed the teddy on the pillow and set the train in a box with soft tissue paper. The box had a label too: “Toys.”

“Toy, toy, box, box!” Lina sang, and Nutmeg clapped her tiny paws.

Grandma opened the window a little wider. A gentle breeze carried the scent of pine trees. The curtains swished and whispered. “What a fresh room,” Grandma said. “Thank you, helpers.”

Nutmeg looked around. The rug was smooth. The bed was neat. The suitcase was zipped. Drawers had clear labels. Books were happy in their spots. The guest room felt calm and ready.

But Nutmeg saw one more thing. A little note on the table said, “Welcome.” The letters were wobbly. “Did you write this, Lina?” she asked.

Lina nodded. “I tried.”

“It’s wonderful,” Nutmeg said. “Words say hello. Words make hearts smile.” She added a tiny acorn sticker to the note. “Now it says, ‘Welcome, with love.’”

“Ding-dong!” The doorbell rang. The guest had arrived. Auntie Mei stepped in with a big grin and a small bag. “Hello, hello!” she said. “What a lovely room!”

Auntie Mei picked up a book with a ladybug on it. “I speak two languages,” she told Lina. “In my language, ladybug is called ‘mariquita.’ Can you say ‘mariquita’?”

“Ma-ri-qui-ta,” Lina said slowly. She giggled. “It tickles my tongue!”

Nutmeg bounced. “New words are fun!” She tapped the label that said “Books.” “Books bring words. Labels help us find. Helping makes everything shine.”

Auntie Mei saw the labels. “Socks. Bedtime. Toys,” she read. “This room is so organized. Thank you, little team.”

Nutmeg felt proud. Her tail made a happy swish. “We made a plan. We used kind hands. We used kind words.”

Grandma hugged Lina. “We learned that helping is caring,” she said softly. “We learned that words can guide us. We learned that neat spaces feel good.”

Lina hugged Nutmeg. “Thank you for helping.”

“You’re welcome,” Nutmeg chirped. “Helping is my favorite adventure.”

They all sat together on the rug. Auntie Mei read the ladybug book, first in one language, then in another. The words sounded like gentle rain, patter-patter, happy and bright. The guest room felt cozy and full of love.

Nutmeg curled up near the window as the sun began to set. “Today we were helpful,” she whispered. “Today we learned new words. Today we made order.” Her eyes grew sleepy and glad.

The room was ready, the hearts were full, and the labels smiled from their places.

The clear lesson: Helping others, using kind words, and keeping things organized makes everyone feel safe, calm, and happy.

The End.
