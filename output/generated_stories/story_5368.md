# Nutmeg’s Study Corner Surprise

Nutmeg was a small, resourceful squirrel with bright, curious eyes and a fluffy tail that swished like a soft paintbrush. She lived near a sunny study corner inside a cozy house. The study corner had a little desk, a comfy chair, and a big basket of crayons. Sunbeams made warm stripes on the rug, and the air smelled like paper and fresh apples.

“Good morning, study corner!” Nutmeg chirped. She loved to collect tiny treasures: smooth acorns, shiny buttons, and bits of ribbon. She kept them in neat jars on the shelf. Each jar had a label. Nutmeg liked labels. Labels helped her share and save.

Today, Nutmeg had a plan. “I will make a picture chart,” she said. “It will show how we can share and care!”

Her friends Peanut the mouse and Bluebell the bluebird fluttered in through the open window. Peanut wore a tiny red scarf. Bluebell’s wings glimmered like raindrops.

“What are you doing, Nutmeg?” asked Peanut, sniffing the apple smell.

“I’m making a Sharing Chart,” Nutmeg said proudly. “We will share crayons, paper, and ideas. And we will save, not waste!”

Bluebell tilted her head. “Save? Like saving seeds?”

“Yes,” Nutmeg said, nodding. “We use only what we need. We put things back so others can use them too.”

Nutmeg placed a big sheet of paper on the desk. She drew a smiling sun in the corner. The crayons felt smooth in her paw, and they made soft scritch-scratch sounds. Bluebell sang a little tune. Peanut hummed along.

Soon, other friends peeked in. Daisy the rabbit hopped to the rug. Tumble the turtle slowly slid beside the chair. “Can we make pictures too?” Daisy asked, her nose twitching.

Nutmeg’s heart felt warm. “Yes! But let’s share. One crayon at a time. When we finish, we pass it on.”

Daisy grabbed five crayons at once. “I need all the colors!” she said.

Nutmeg’s tail curled. She took a breath, like a tiny breeze. “Daisy,” she said kindly, “if you take all the colors, there are none left to share. Let’s try one or two. Then pass them, please.”

Daisy’s ears drooped. “But I want a rainbow.”

“You will still get a rainbow,” Nutmeg said with a smile. “We can make a rainbow together.”

Daisy slowly put three crayons back. “Okay,” she whispered.

They began to draw. Peanut made a tiny boat. Bluebell made blue clouds. Tumble drew a careful green leaf. Nutmeg drew a tree with big branches. The crayons went from paw to wing to paw. Pass, draw, pass. The room was full of gentle sounds: scritch-scratch, hum-hum, chirp-chirp.

Suddenly, Tumble frowned. “The paper is gone,” he said. The stack was empty. Daisy had three sheets in front of her, but two were still blank.

Nutmeg touched Daisy’s paw. “Daisy, we need to save paper. Can we use one paper at a time?”

Daisy looked at the blank sheets. “But I like to have them ready.”

“I know,” Nutmeg said softly. “Let’s keep one for now and put two back. Then everyone can make a picture. That is sharing. That is caring for our things. That is conservation.”

“Con-ser-vay-tion,” Daisy repeated, sounding each part. “That means we don’t waste?”

“Yes,” said Nutmeg. “We use just what we need. We keep our corner neat and happy.”

Daisy nodded and put two papers back. “I can wait,” she said. Her whiskers twitched with a shy smile.

Bluebell fluttered to the window and brought a soft breeze inside. It smelled like leaves and sunshine. “We are doing great!” she chirped.

Then Peanut bumped the crayon basket. Oh no! The crayons spilled like colorful rain, rolling under the chair and across the rug.

Peanut covered his eyes. “I’m sorry,” he squeaked.

Nutmeg patted his tiny shoulder. “It’s okay. Accidents happen. Let’s clean up together.”

Everyone got to work. Tumble reached under the chair. Bluebell found a stray purple crayon near the window. Daisy picked up two and passed one to Peanut. They said, “Share and care,” as they found each piece.

Nutmeg made little jars for broken crayons. “We can still use the pieces,” she said. “Nothing wasted!”

At last, the study corner was tidy again. Sunlight sparkled on the clean desk. The crayons sat in a neat row, like a rainbow parade. Nutmeg pinned up the Sharing Chart:

- Take one, then pass it on.
- Use only what you need.
- Put things back where they belong.
- Say, “Thank you.”

“Let’s read it together,” Nutmeg said.

They read in small, happy voices. Then they drew one big picture on one big sheet: a tall tree with enough branches for every friend. Nutmeg added a tiny nest. Bluebell added sky. Peanut drew a boat on a little pond. Daisy colored flowers, one petal at a time. Tumble made a bright green leaf with room for a ladybug.

When they finished, everyone clapped. The picture felt warm and friendly, like a hug you can see.

“We shared,” Daisy said, beaming.

“We saved,” Tumble added.

“We cared,” Peanut squeaked.

“And we made something beautiful,” Bluebell sang.

Nutmeg’s tail swished with joy. “Sharing makes our study corner happy. Sharing helps our friends. And sharing helps the Earth, too, when we don’t waste.”

They hung the big picture on the wall. It glowed in the sun. Nutmeg passed around slices of apple. “One slice for each friend,” she said.

“Thank you,” they all said, crunching together. Crunch, crunch, crunch. Sweet and crisp.

Nutmeg looked around the cozy study corner. Her heart felt as warm as the sunlight. “Today we learned,” she said, “that sharing, friendship, and conservation go paw in paw.”

Everyone nodded. They smiled. They felt proud.

The End.
