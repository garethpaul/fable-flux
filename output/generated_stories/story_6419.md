# Ollie Otter’s Plan in the Basement Lab

Ollie was a playful otter with bright, curious eyes. He lived by a river, but today he padded downstairs to a cozy basement lab. The lab smelled like lemon soap and warm wood. Lights hummed softly. Beakers clinked gently. A big paper chart hung on the wall with the words: “Ollie’s Goal.”

Ollie tapped the chart with his paw. “I want to build a little raft,” he said. “I want to ride it on the river this afternoon.”

His whiskers wiggled. His heart felt excited and bouncy, like bubbles. Then he looked at the pile of wood pieces, rope, and glue. The pile looked huge. Ollie’s tummy felt twisty.

“This is a big job,” Ollie whispered.

From a shelf, a timer clicked. Tick. Tick. Tick. The sound was calm and steady, like a slow drum.

Ollie took a deep breath in. He smelled fresh pine from the wood. He breathed out slowly. “I can make a plan,” he said. “A goal needs steps.”

He picked up a marker and drew boxes on the chart.

Step 1: Gather pieces.
Step 2: Tie the boards.
Step 3: Test in a small tub.
Step 4: Take it outside.

Ollie smiled. “Little steps,” he said. “One at a time.”

He turned on a small fan to keep the room breezy. It made a soft whoosh. He put on safety goggles. “Eye bubbles!” he giggled.

First, he gathered pieces. Thump, thump, thump went the wood as he stacked it. Clink went the rope rings. Squish went the glue bottle when he checked the cap. He counted out loud. “One, two, three boards. One, two ropes.” Counting felt good and clear.

Then he paused. He put a paw on his heart. “How do I feel?” he asked. “I feel calm. I feel ready.” He smiled. “Mindful Ollie.”

He started Step 2. He tied the boards. “Left loop, right loop,” he sang. But the rope kept slipping. “Oh no,” said Ollie. “It’s too loose.”

His whiskers drooped. He felt a tear on his cheek. Drip.

Ollie sat crisscross on the floor. He closed his eyes. He breathed in for three. One, two, three. He breathed out for three. One, two, three. He felt the cool floor under his paws. He listened to the hum of the lights and the tick of the timer. His tummy stopped twisting.

“What is the problem?” he asked himself. “The rope is slippery.” He wagged a paw. “What is a new idea?”

He looked around the basement lab. He saw a little chalkboard. He drew a picture of a knot. He drew a big, round knot and a small, tight knot.

“I can try a double knot,” Ollie said. “And I can use a towel to hold the rope.”

He dried the rope with a soft towel. He tied a double knot. Pull, snug, pull. The rope squeaked a little and then held tight. “Yes!” Ollie cheered. He wiggled his tail. “Problem solved.”

Step 3 was testing in a small tub. Ollie filled a plastic tub with cool water. Splash, splash. He set the tiny raft on the water. It bobbed like a leaf. He added a small pebble as a pretend rider. The raft stayed up. “Float, float,” he said. “Good raft!”

He checked his chart. Step 4: Take it outside. He paused again. Paw on heart. “How do I feel now?” he asked. “I feel proud. I feel ready.”

Ollie carried the raft up the stairs and out to the sunny yard. Warm air hugged his fur. He heard birds chirp, chirp, chirp. The river sparkled like silver ribbons.

He put the raft in the shallow edge. He placed a paw on it and pushed gently. The raft glided. Swish. Ollie climbed on. The water felt cool on his toes. The sun was bright on his whiskers. He giggled. “I did it!”

He paddled a little, staying near the edge. He watched the clouds. He listened to the wind in the grass. He took slow breaths to stay calm and safe. In, two, three. Out, two, three. He smiled at the trees and waved to a dragonfly. “Hello, friend!”

After a happy ride, Ollie pulled the raft back to shore. He shook his fur. Drip-drip. He looked up at the sky and then down at his chart he had brought outside.

“I had a big goal,” he said. “I made small steps. I took mindful breaths. I solved problems. I went outside and moved my body. I did my best.”

Ollie drew a big gold star next to each step. Star, star, star, star. His heart felt warm and full, like cocoa in a mug.

He patted the raft and whispered, “Thank you, plan. Thank you, breath. Thank you, river.”

Then he looked at the chart and added new words at the bottom: “Next goal: Invite a friend to ride with me.”

Ollie laughed. “One step at a time.”

The End.
