# Owl Hoots for Help

In a cozy, comfortable nursery, a wise old owl named Olive perched on a soft, blue rocking chair. The nursery smelled like clean soap and warm laundry. Sunlight sprinkled across the carpet, making bright little squares. A gentle clock went tick-tock, tick-tock, like a calm heartbeat.

Olive the owl had big golden eyes and soft, speckled feathers. She loved to take care of others. She reminded the stuffed animals to sit up straight, the picture books to rest on their shelf, and the tiny plants to sip their water. But sometimes, Olive forgot to take care of herself.

One morning, Olive tried to do everything at once. She fluffed pillows, straightened blankets, and told the toys, “Good morning!” She read three storybooks aloud, hooted a lullaby to the doll, and then looked at her feathers in the mirror.

“Oh my,” said Olive. “I feel a little droopy.”

Her tummy felt rumbly. Her eyes felt blinky. Her wings felt heavy. But Olive still wanted to write her daily letter. Every day, she wrote a little note to the nursery to say kind things. She dipped her feather quill in a jar of purple ink and began to write on a smooth paper.

Hoot hoot, scratch scratch.

But the letters wiggled, and the words got sleepy. Olive yawned a big owl yawn. “Hooo-um.”

Just then, the soft clock went tick-tock, and the night-light hummed a gentle song. The window showed a sky of fluffy clouds. Olive blinked again and whispered, “I want to write, but I feel tired.”

A small plush bunny named Button waved a floppy ear. “Olive? Do you need a break?”

Olive tilted her head. “A break? But I have so much to do! I want to write kind words for everyone.”

Button hopped closer. “Kind words are best when your heart feels steady and your body feels comfy.”

The wooden giraffe nodded, its painted eyes twinkling. “Self-care helps you share.”

Olive listened. The room smelled like fresh crayons and warm hugs. She felt the soft cushion under her feet. “What is self-care?” Olive asked gently.

“It means being kind to yourself,” said Button. “It means resting when you are tired. It means drinking water, stretching your body, and asking for help when you need it. Then you can do your best.”

Olive thought about the purple ink, the wiggly letters, and her heavy wings. She took a slow breath in. She let a slow breath out. “I can practice self-care,” she said.

First, Olive sipped cool water from a tiny cup. “Sip, sip.” Her mouth felt fresh and happy. Next, she nibbled a crunchy apple slice from the snack tray. “Munch, munch.” The apple tasted sweet, like sunshine. Then Olive stood up and stretched her wings. “Stretch, stretch.” Her feathers fluffed with a soft rustle, like a tiny breeze.

Button clapped her little paws. “How do you feel now?”

Olive blinked her golden eyes. “I feel calmer,” she said. “But I still feel a bit tired.”

“That’s okay,” said the giraffe. “You can rest.”

Olive curled up on the blue rocking chair. The cushion was soft like a cloud. She pulled a small blanket over her toes. The clock went tick-tock, tick-tock. The night-light hummed. Olive closed her eyes and took three slow breaths.

In, out. In, out. In, out.

After a short rest, Olive opened her eyes. The nursery looked bright and friendly again. Her tummy felt peaceful, and her wings felt light.

Button smiled. “Hello again! Ready to write?”

Olive nodded. She sat at the little table. The paper felt smooth and cool. The quill felt like a soft feather in her talons. She dipped the quill into the purple ink. “I will write a letter to the nursery,” she said. “I will use kind words. I will also practice good communication.”

“What is communication?” asked Button.

“Communication is how we share our thoughts and our feelings,” Olive said. “We can use words. We can use listening ears. We can ask for help. We can say thank you.”

Olive began to write, speaking the words as she wrote them:

“Dear Nursery,
Today I felt tired. I took a break. I had water. I ate a snack. I stretched. I rested. Now I feel better. Thank you for being patient. Love, Olive.”

Her writing looked neat, round, and clear. The purple ink shone softly in the light.

Olive read her letter out loud. Her voice was warm and calm. Button and the giraffe listened with bright eyes. The doll smiled. The room felt safe and happy.

“That was good communication,” Button said. “You told us how you felt. You told us what you did. You said thank you.”

Olive nodded. “It helped me too,” she said. “When I take care of me, I can take care of others.”

Olive taped the letter to the wall near the bookshelf. Then she drew a little picture under the words: a cup of water, a crunchy apple, a small pillow, and a big heart. She added a tiny owl feather and a big smile.

“Self-care helps me share,” Olive sang softly.

Button twirled. The giraffe tapped a tiny wooden hoof. The nursery smelled like apples and ink and felt cozy and bright. Olive’s feathers rustled with joy.

Olive looked around and said, “Friends, if you feel tired, it’s okay to say, ‘I need a break.’ It’s okay to drink water, eat a snack, stretch, and rest. Then your kind words can fly.”

The friends all hooted and clapped. Olive felt proud and peaceful. She rocked gently in her chair and watched the sunlight dance on the floor.

The nursery was quiet and happy. And so was Olive.

The End.
