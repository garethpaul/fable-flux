# Panda Poppy’s Gentle Day in the Treehouse

Panda Poppy woke up in her cozy treehouse. The morning sun painted the walls gold. The leaves whispered, “Shhhh,” in the soft breeze. Poppy stretched and smiled. “Good morning, world,” she said. “I will be patient today.”

Poppy lived in a sturdy treehouse with a tiny kitchen, a reading corner, and a craft table. She loved to paint. She loved to share. She loved to listen.

She washed her paws with warm, soapy water. “Bubble, bubble,” she giggled. “Clean paws help me stay healthy.” She drank cool water from a cup and munched on crunchy bamboo. “Munch, munch,” went the bamboo. “I am ready,” Poppy said.

Outside, she heard footsteps on the ladder. “Knock, knock,” said a small voice.

Poppy opened the door. It was her friend, Niko the squirrel. His tail flicked fast. His eyes were shiny. “Hi, Poppy!” Niko squeaked. “Can we paint a big world picture today?”

Poppy clapped her paws. “Yes! Let’s paint our big world.” She pointed to a big paper on the wall. “We can paint oceans, forests, deserts, and snowy mountains. We can paint places where our friends live. We can paint people all around the world.”

Niko bounced. “I want to paint the ocean!” he said. He grabbed a blue brush. But when he dipped it, a drip fell on his paw. “Oh no,” said Niko. “Drip!”

Poppy smiled. “It’s okay,” she said softly. “Drips happen. We can wipe it.” She handed Niko a soft cloth. “Wipe, wipe.”

Niko tried again. This time, the blue brush made a big splash on the paper. “Splat!” Niko’s tail drooped. “I can’t do it,” he said. “I feel mad.”

Poppy put a gentle paw on Niko’s shoulder. “I see you feel mad,” she said. “Your tail tells me. Your eyes tell me. I am here. Let’s take a belly breath.”

They breathed in slowly. “In…” They breathed out slowly. “Out…” The leaves whispered along, “Shhhh.”

Poppy nodded. “When I feel mad, I breathe. Then I try again. You can try again too.”

Niko took another breath. “I will try,” he said.

They painted together. Poppy made soft, round mountains. Niko made smooth waves. “Swish, swish,” went the brushes. The paper smelled like fresh paper and sweet paint. Sunlight warmed their paws.

Soon, another friend climbed up. “Hello!” called Asha the bird. Her wings were bright like little flags. “Can I paint, too?”

Poppy smiled. “Yes, Asha. We are painting the big world. What would you like to paint?”

Asha looked at the paper. “I will paint a desert,” she chirped. “With a cactus and a golden sun.” She dipped into yellow and green. “Dot, dot, swoop.”

As they painted, Niko bumped his cup. “Uh-oh!” Water splashed. “Splash!” The water ran toward Asha’s desert. Asha’s feathers puffed. “Oh no! My sun is wet,” she cried.

Niko’s ears fell. “I am sorry,” he said. “I did not see the cup. I feel bad.”

Poppy put a paw over her heart. “Asha, I hear you. You feel sad. Your sun is wet,” she said. “Niko, I see you are sorry. Thank you for saying it.”

She handed them both towels. “We can fix this together,” Poppy said. “We can dab the water. Dab, dab.” They dabbed the paper. The sun looked a little blurry, like a warm, sleepy sun.

Asha looked closer. “It looks soft now,” she chirped. “Like a sun in the morning.”

Niko smiled a little. “I can paint a bright edge,” he said. “To help.”

Asha nodded. “Thank you, Niko. I feel better.”

Poppy’s eyes were kind. “This is empathy,” she said. “Empathy is when we listen to feelings and try to help.”

They kept painting. They added a forest with tall trees. They added a snowy mountain with sparkles of white. Poppy painted tiny people holding hands around the paper earth. “Around the world, people have feelings,” Poppy said. “We can be gentle everywhere.”

They took a break. Poppy passed apple slices and cool water. “Crunch, crunch,” went the apples. “Healthy snacks help us feel calm,” Poppy said. “Water helps us think.”

After the snack, Niko tried a tricky wave again. His brush slipped. “Oh!” He felt the mad feelings come back. His tail twitched.

Poppy saw his face. She whispered, “Belly breath?” Niko nodded. They breathed in and out. Poppy hummed a soft tune. “Mmm-mmm.” The tune felt like warm tea.

Niko tried again. This time, the wave curved just right. “I did it!” he cheered. “Thank you for waiting, Poppy.”

“I am happy to wait,” Poppy said. “Patience is a friend.”

They stepped back to look. The big paper world was bright and kind. The ocean swished. The desert glowed. The forest whispered. The mountains sparkled. Little people waved. It was a map of feelings and places.

Asha fluffed her wings. “We made this together,” she said. “We listened. We helped.”

Niko nodded. “When I got mad, we breathed. When I made a mess, I said sorry. When Asha felt sad, we helped. That is empathy.”

Poppy smiled, her eyes warm. “Yes,” she said. “Today we learned to see and hear each other’s hearts. We can be gentle with our friends, with our world, and with ourselves.”

They hung the painting by the window. Sunlight kissed the colors. The treehouse felt calm and happy. The leaves sang, “Shhhh,” in the wind.

Poppy hugged her friends. “Thank you for painting the world with me.”

“Thank you for being patient,” said Niko.

“Thank you for caring,” said Asha.

Poppy nodded. “When we care, we share. When we listen, we learn. When we breathe, we can try again.”

They all smiled. It was a gentle, good day.

The End.
