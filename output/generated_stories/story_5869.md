# Benny Beaver’s Balcony Garden

Benny was a busy beaver with bright eyes and a big, flat tail. He lived with his family in an apartment with a sunny balcony garden. The garden had little pots, a tiny watering can, and a small wooden table that Benny had helped build. The air smelled like wet soil and mint leaves. Bees hummed softly, and little birds chirped from the rail.

Today was a big day. Benny wanted to grow his very own salad. He wanted to try doing it by himself. He took a deep breath and said, “I can do this. I will try, try, try!”

Mama Beaver smiled from the doorway. “I’m here if you need help,” she said. “But I know you can try on your own.”

Benny nodded. He felt proud and a little wiggly inside. He put on his tiny sun hat. It felt warm on his head. He picked up a packet of seeds. The seeds rattled like rain in the paper. “Lettuce seeds,” he read slowly.

He lined up three pots. “First, good dirt,” he said. He scooped soil with his small shovel. Plop, plop, plop. Some soil landed on his toes. It felt cool and crumbly. “Oops!” Benny giggled.

He poked little holes with his finger. “One, two, three.” He dropped in the seeds. They were like tiny dots of hope. “Sleep well, little seeds,” he whispered.

He covered the seeds with soft soil and patted it gently. Pat, pat, pat. “Now water,” Benny said. He lifted the small watering can. The spout sparkled in the sun. He tilted it slowly. Drip, drip, drip. “Not too much,” he reminded himself. “Seeds like a little drink.”

Tippy the sparrow hopped on the rail and chirped, “Cheep! What are you doing, Benny?”

“I’m growing my salad,” Benny said. “I’m doing it myself.”

“Cheep-cheep! That is smart,” Tippy said. “I will cheer you on!”

Benny smiled. He set a tiny stick sign in the pot. He wrote, “Benny’s Lettuce.” The letters were a little wobbly, but they made him proud.

Each morning, Benny checked his pots. He used his finger to feel the soil. “Is it dry? Is it wet?” he asked. If it was dry, he gave it a little water. If it was wet, he waited. Waiting was hard. Benny’s tail went tap-tap-tap on the floor. “I want salad now,” he sighed.

Mama Beaver touched his shoulder. “Plants grow slowly,” she said. “Patience is part of the work.”

Benny took a breath. “I can wait,” he said. He hummed a little song. “Grow, grow, grow, little seeds. I will help with what you need.”

Days passed. The sun warmed the balcony. The air smelled sweet and green. One morning, Benny saw tiny specks of green peeking up. “Sprouts!” he squeaked. His heart felt bubbly. “Hello, little leaves!”

Poppy the ladybug landed on a leaf. “Hello,” she said in a tiny voice. “Nice garden.”

“Thank you,” Benny whispered. “I’m taking care of it myself.”

Benny kept working. He turned the pots so the leaves got even sun. He picked off a dry leaf. He shooed away a nibbling snail by moving it to a shady pot of mint. “Here you go, Mr. Snail. You can have mint, not my lettuce,” he said kindly.

Some afternoons, the wind blew strong. The leaves wiggled. Benny made a small wind screen from a piece of cardboard and two sticks. He tied it with string. His paws worked carefully. “There,” he said. “Safe and steady.” He felt clever and strong.

One hot day, Tippy chirped, “Cheep! I am thirsty.”

Benny looked at the water in his pail. He had saved some for the plants. He thought. He felt the soil. Still moist. Benny smiled. “I can share.” He poured a little water into a saucer for Tippy. “We can both be okay,” he said.

“Cheep-cheep! Thank you,” Tippy sang. “You are generous.”

The lettuce grew and grew. The leaves were crisp and bright. When Benny touched them, they felt cool and curly. The garden smelled fresh, like rain and herbs. Benny’s tail thumped happily. “It’s time,” he said.

He washed the leaves in a bowl. Swish, swish. He patted them dry with a clean towel. He put the salad on a plate and added tiny carrot coins and three mint leaves. He made a little sign: “Benny’s Salad!”

Mama Beaver came out. So did Papa and Baby Bea. Tippy fluttered down to the rail. Poppy sat on a leaf like a red jewel.

Benny took a deep breath. “I grew this,” he said. “I did the work. I asked for help only when I needed it. I waited. I shared water. I used what I had.”

Mama Beaver hugged him. “You were self-reliant,” she said. “You were patient and kind.”

Benny smiled big. “Let’s share,” he said. He passed little leaves to everyone. Crunch, crunch. The salad tasted cool and yummy. Benny felt warm inside, like sunshine.

Tippy chirped, “Cheep! Sharing makes it better.”

Benny nodded. “I can do things by myself,” he said. “And I can be generous, too.”

He looked at his garden. He could hear bees buzzing and feel the soft wind on his fur. He could smell mint and soil. He felt proud, calm, and ready for tomorrow’s work.

The lesson was clear in Benny’s heart: I can try, I can care, I can share.

The End.
