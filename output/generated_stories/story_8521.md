# Miko's Special Art Stand

Miko the monkey loved to sit on her peaceful front porch every morning. The warm sun tickled her fuzzy brown fur, and the sweet smell of flowers filled the air. Today, she had spread out all her favorite art supplies on the wooden porch floor.

"I'm going to make beautiful pictures for everyone!" Miko said happily, picking up her bright red crayon.

She drew colorful butterflies, smiling suns, and rainbow flowers. Each picture made her feel proud and excited. Miko carefully arranged her artwork on a small table she had made from an old cardboard box.

"Art for everyone!" she called out cheerfully. "Come see my pretty pictures!"

Soon, her friend Benny the bear walked by. His shoulders were drooped, and he looked very sad.

"What's wrong, Benny?" Miko asked, swinging her tail gently.

"Someone took my lunch money again," Benny whispered. "Now I can't buy anything to eat."

Miko's heart felt heavy. She knew this wasn't fair. "That's not right, Benny. No one should take things that don't belong to them."

Miko thought hard, tapping her finger on her chin. Then her eyes lit up with an idea!

"I know how to help!" she said bravely. "Let's make something special together."

Miko gathered her art supplies - soft clay, colorful paint, and sparkly stickers. She showed Benny how to roll the clay into small bowls and cups.

"We can make these beautiful things and sell them," Miko explained. "Then you'll have money for lunch!"

Benny's eyes grew wide with wonder. "Really? We can do that?"

"Yes!" Miko said confidently. "When we work together and use our hands to create, we can solve problems."

They spent the morning crafting. Miko painted tiny blue flowers on the clay bowls while Benny added shiny star stickers. The sweet smell of paint mixed with the fresh porch air.

When they finished, Miko stood up tall and took a deep breath.

"Now we need to tell Mrs. Owl about the lunch money problem," she said firmly. "It's important to speak up when something unfair happens."

Benny looked nervous. "But what if they get mad at me?"

"I'll be right beside you," Miko promised, holding his paw. "Being brave means doing the right thing, even when it feels scary."

Together, they walked to Mrs. Owl's house. Miko spoke clearly and kindly about what had happened. Mrs. Owl listened carefully and promised to help fix the problem.

"Thank you for being so brave and telling me," Mrs. Owl said warmly.

Back on the porch, they set up their craft stand. Soon, neighbors came to buy their beautiful handmade bowls and cups. The coins clinked happily in their jar.

"We did it!" Benny cheered, his smile as bright as the sunshine.

Miko felt proud and warm inside. "When we use our creativity and speak up for what's right, amazing things happen!"

That evening, as the golden sun set behind the trees, Miko and Benny counted their earnings. They had enough for lunch and extra to share with other friends who might need help.

"Crafting isn't just about making pretty things," Miko said thoughtfully. "It's about using our hands and hearts to make the world better."

Benny nodded, holding up their beautiful clay bowl. "And being brave helps too!"

Miko smiled, feeling grateful for their special day of creating, caring, and courage.

The End.
