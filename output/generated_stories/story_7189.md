# Teddy Bear Cub’s Quiet Dance Talk

Teddy the bear cub lived with Mama Bear and Papa Bear. Today, the home office felt busy. The room smelled like warm tea and paper. The computer hummed softly like a bee. Sunlight made little gold squares on the floor. Click, click went the keyboard. Tap, tap went Papa’s pen.

Teddy loved to dance. His paws twitched. His fuzzy ears wiggled. He wanted to spin. He wanted to leap. He wanted to clap his paws fast. But Papa Bear was working in the home office. Papa’s brow made a little line. His eyes looked at the screen.

Teddy took a step. The floor felt smooth and cool. He whispered, “Shuffle, shuffle.” He made a tiny wiggle dance. A pencil rolled on the desk. Papa Bear looked up.

“Hi, Teddy,” said Papa in a calm voice. “I am working now. I need quiet.”

Teddy’s face felt hot. He wanted to dance big, not small. His heart thumped like a drum-drum-drum. He thought, I want to dance! But he also remembered what Mama Bear always said: “Use your words. Use gentle words.”

Teddy took a deep breath. In… and out… His chest felt softer. He padded closer and spoke slowly. “Papa, I feel wiggly. My body wants to dance.”

Papa Bear nodded. “Thank you for telling me how you feel,” he said. “That is peaceful talking.”

Teddy liked those words. Peaceful talking. It felt warm, like a hug. He tried again. “I want to dance, but I don’t want to make work hard for you. Can we make a plan?”

Papa Bear smiled. “Yes, we can make a plan. That is safe and kind. I need ten quiet minutes to finish a call. See the clock? When the little hand moves to the twelve, I will be done.”

Teddy peeked at the round clock. Tick-tick, tick-tick. The sound was soft and steady. “Okay,” he said. “What can I do while I wait?”

Papa Bear pointed to a basket. “You can draw a dance map. Use crayons on paper. Plan your steps. Also, our safety rule: no dancing near the cords and the swivel chair.”

Teddy’s eyes grew wide. He saw the long cords like snaky vines. He saw the chair that could spin. He nodded. “No cords. No spinny chair. Safety first.”

Teddy sat at a little table. He picked a blue crayon. Scritch, scritch. He drew a big circle. “This is my twirl spot,” he said. He picked a red crayon. Scritch, scritch. He drew two little paw prints. “These are my clap steps.” He drew a yellow sun for a happy jump. He hummed a tiny tune. Mmm-mmm, mmm-mmm.

Soon the clock’s little hand moved to the twelve. Papa Bear finished his call. He closed the laptop with a soft click. He rolled his shoulders and smiled. “You waited so nicely. You spoke calmly. You made a plan. That was peaceful communication.”

Teddy’s tail nub wiggled. “Can I dance now?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Papa. “Let’s dance together, but let’s keep it safe. We’ll use your dance map. We’ll clear a space.” Papa Bear pushed the chair to the wall. He tucked the cords away. He set a soft rug on the floor. “Our safety circle,” he said.

Mama Bear peeked in with a tray. “Snack time soon,” she said. “I hear happy voices. What’s the plan?”

Teddy pointed to his paper. “We will twirl in the circle. We will clap on the paw prints. We will jump like sunshine. We will keep the office quiet enough. We will watch our feet.”

Mama Bear clapped softly. “I love your plan. Let’s use quiet feet and gentle music.” She tapped her phone and played a soft song. The music floated like a feather. Ding, ding, ding, like tiny bells.

Teddy held Papa’s paw. They stepped on the paw prints. Tap, tap. They twirled in the circle. Swish, swish went Teddy’s soft fur. Teddy giggled. His giggle sounded like fizzy bubbles. Mama Bear spun slowly, too. Her dress brushed the rug. Whoosh.

Teddy remembered safety. He looked down. He kept away from the cords. He checked the chair. “Safe space,” he said. He watched Papa’s eyes. “Are you okay, Papa?”

“I am great,” said Papa. “Thank you for checking in. That is caring and safe.”

They danced small and gentle. They used quiet claps. They took turns making moves. Teddy said, “My turn. Wiggle paw.” Papa said, “My turn. Bear bounce.” Mama said, “My turn. Slow swirl.”

When the song ended, they placed paws on hearts. They took a deep breath together. In… and out… The room felt calm and happy. The tea smelled sweet. The sun made the rug warm on Teddy’s toes.

Mama Bear set out apple slices and peanut butter. Crunch went the apples. Teddy licked his lips. “Today I felt wiggly,” Teddy said. “I wanted to dance. I used my words. We made a plan. We kept safe.”

Papa Bear nodded. “That is the lesson. When we have big feelings or big wiggles, we can use peaceful communication. We can say how we feel. We can ask for a plan. We can keep everyone safe.”

Teddy smiled. “Peaceful talk helps my dance.” He hugged Mama and Papa. His hug was snug and cozy.

The home office was still a working place, and now it was a dancing place too—safe, calm, and full of kind words.

The End.
