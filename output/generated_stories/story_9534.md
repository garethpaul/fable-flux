# Ollie Otter Says “Thank You”

Ollie the playful otter loved bubbles. He loved splish-splash water, foamy soap, and shiny tiles. One sunny morning, Ollie padded into the bathroom with a big grin. His whiskers twitched. The air smelled like fresh lemon soap. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. Plip, plop, plip went the water on the floor.

“Good morning, sink!” Ollie giggled. “Good morning, tub! Are you ready for bubbles?”

Mama Otter peeked in. “Good morning, Ollie. Remember our bathroom rules,” she said softly. “We listen, we share, and we leave the bathroom clean for others. That shows respect. And we say thank you.”

Ollie nodded. “I can do that! I can listen. I can be thankful.” His paws clapped with a little slap-slap.

Mama handed him a small cup, a soft brush, and a tiny towel. “These are your tools,” she said. “You’re learning new skills. Turn the water on slowly. When you’re done, turn it off. We care for our home. That is civic responsibility.”

Ollie liked the big words. “Civ-ic re-spon-si-bi-li-ty,” he hummed. “That means we help take care of our place!”

He turned the handle just a tiny bit. “Trickle, trickle,” he whispered. The water made a gentle hiss. Ollie smiled. “Thank you, water,” he said. “You are cool and clear.”

He added a drop of soap. Plop! Foam grew into tiny clouds. Ollie dipped his brush and scrubbed his paws in slow circles. Swish, swish. “Hello, bubbles!” he chirped. “You tickle my paws.”

“Good listening,” Mama said. “And kind words.”

Ollie rinsed his paws and turned the handle back. The faucet stopped. “I did it!” he said. He dried his paws with the small towel. “Thank you, towel. You make me warm and dry.”

Then Ollie saw the mirror. It was foggy from a bath last night. He drew a smiley face with one paw. “Hee hee!” he squeaked. But then he paused. “Someone else might want a clean mirror,” he said, remembering respect. He took his towel and wiped gently in small circles. “Shine, shine,” he sang.

Mama smiled. “That was thoughtful. You are thinking about others.”

Ollie’s friend, Tori Turtle, peeked around the door. “Hi, Ollie,” she said. “May I wash my shell?”

“Yes!” Ollie said. “Let’s take turns.” He held out the brush. “You can go first.”

Tori smiled. “Thank you.”

Ollie felt a warm glow in his belly. “You’re welcome,” he said. He liked how those words felt.

Tori turned the water just a little. “Trickle, trickle,” she said. She scrubbed her shell in small, careful circles. “Swish, swish.” Then she turned the water off. “Thank you, water,” she whispered. She dabbed with a towel. “Thank you, towel.”

Ollie clapped again. “We are good listeners,” he said. “We take care of our bathroom. We say thank you.”

Tori nodded. “We show respect.”

Just then, Ollie noticed a tiny spill by the tub. “Uh-oh,” he said. “The floor is wet. Someone might slip.”

He looked at Mama. “What should we do?”

Mama said, “First, we listen and look. Then we act with care. What tool could help?”

Ollie bounced on his toes. “A towel!” He picked a bigger towel from the hook. He patted the floor. Pat, pat, pat. The towel drank up the water. “There,” he said. “All dry.” He hung the towel neatly. “Thank you, towel, for helping.”

Tori smiled. “That keeps everyone safe. That is civic responsibility.”

Ollie’s whiskers twitched. He felt proud. His heart felt big and cozy, like a hug. “I am grateful,” he said. “I am thankful for water, soap, towels, and friends.”

He looked at Mama. “Thank you for teaching me,” he said.

Mama knelt and hugged him. Her fur smelled like sea breeze. “Thank you for listening,” she said. “Thank you for helping.”

Ollie and Tori washed the little cup and brush. Clink, clink. They set them in a basket. Ollie wiped the edge of the sink. Swipe, swipe. He put the soap back in its dish. Plop. He closed the cabinet softly. Click.

He looked around. The tiles shone. The mirror sparkled. The floor was dry. The bathroom felt calm and clean.

Ollie took a deep breath. “I like how it feels when we care for our place,” he said. “It smells fresh. It looks bright. My paws feel soft.”

Tori nodded. “And my shell feels smooth.”

Ollie turned to the bathroom. “Thank you, bathroom,” he said. “You help us every day. We will take care of you.”

Mama laughed kindly. “That is a grateful heart,” she said. “Gratitude helps us notice good things. When we are thankful, we are kind to others and to our home.”

Ollie stood tall. “Today I learned to listen. I learned to take turns. I learned to clean up. I learned to say thank you.”

Tori added, “And we had fun!”

Ollie grinned. “Yes! Bubbles and buddies and big thank-yous!”

Mama tapped the light switch. Click. The bathroom glowed softly. “Time for snack,” she said.

Ollie waved at the room. “Good-bye, sink. Good-bye, tub. Thank you for the splashy fun!”

He took Tori’s fin—well, flipper to shell—and they waddled and wiggled out together, feeling proud, safe, and very, very thankful.

The End.
