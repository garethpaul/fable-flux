# Pippa Parrot Solves the Scribble

Pippa the parrot loved colors. She lived in a cozy home office with bright sticky notes, neat pens, and a shiny keyboard that clicked like tiny tap shoes. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. The air smelled like paper and lemon tea.

“Good morning!” Pippa chirped, fluffing her green, blue, and sunny-yellow feathers. She hopped onto the desk and listened to the gentle hum of the computer. Her human friend, Max, waved. “Hi, Pippa! I have a big report to finish. But the printer is sleepy today.”

The printer sat like a quiet box. “Beep,” it said softly, but no paper came out.

Max sighed. “Hmm. I need my report for work. I don’t know what to do.”

Pippa tilted her head. She loved a puzzle. “We can solve it!” she squawked. “We can try, try, try.”

Max smiled. “Okay, Helper Pippa. First, I’ll check the cables.” He bent down. “They are all plugged in.”

Pippa hopped closer. “Maybe it needs paper?” She peered into the paper tray. It was empty. “Aha! Clue number one!”

Max laughed. “Good eye.” He put a stack of paper in. “Let’s try again.”

The printer whirred. “Whrrrrr-click.” One page came out, but it was covered in wiggly gray lines. Scribbles!

Pippa blinked. “That looks like spaghetti,” she said. “But we want words.”

Max nodded. “You’re right. It still isn’t working.”

Pippa felt a flutter in her tummy. Solving problems could feel big, like standing on the edge of a high perch. She took a deep breath and puffed her feathers. “When a problem is big, we make it small,” she told herself. “Step by step.”

She looked around the home office. She listened to the quiet buzz and smelled the lemon tea again. She saw crayons in a cup, a rainbow stack of sticky notes, and a little brush for dust.

“Maybe it’s dusty,” Pippa said. She picked up the tiny brush with her beak and gently brushed the printer’s tray. “Sweep, sweep, sweep,” she sang.

Max opened a side door on the printer and found a soft gray puff. “Dust bunnies!” he said. He dusted carefully. “Let’s try again.”

The printer whirred. “Whrrrrr-click.” Another page. More scribbles!

Pippa didn’t give up. She patted her chest with a wing. “I am brave. I can do hard things.”

Max rubbed his chin. “Maybe the ink is low.”

Pippa hopped to the shelf. She smelled something inky and sweet, like markers. She found a tiny box with color drops on it. “Ink!” she chirped.

Max changed the ink. “Okay, one more try.”

The printer whirred again. “Whrrrrr-click.” This time, clear words came out. But then, halfway, the paper jammed. “Crinkle-crackle!” The page got stuck.

Pippa watched Max gently open the printer. He slowly pulled the paper out. It had a big wrinkly line. “Oh dear,” he said.

Pippa felt a pinch of worry. She didn’t want Max to be sad. She spread her wings and made a gentle breeze. “We are not done,” she said softly. “We are still trying.”

Pippa looked at the wrinkly paper. She looked at the crayons. She had an idea that felt like a warm sparkle in her head. “If the printer is slow, we can make the page by hand! We can write and draw the report.”

Max blinked. “Make it by hand? The report needs simple words and a chart.”

Pippa nodded. “We can do simple. We can draw the chart with boxes. We can use colors. We can take turns.”

Max’s eyes lit up. “That’s very creative, Pippa.”

They cleared a space. Pippa pulled the crayons closer with her beak. “Scritch-scratch,” went the crayons on smooth paper. Max spoke the words slowly. “Title: Toy Sharing Plan.”

Pippa repeated the words and drew neat boxes. “Red box for cars, blue box for blocks, yellow box for books,” she said. She drew happy faces to show “Wait,” “Ask,” and “Trade.” She added little arrows. Max wrote the words. Pippa drew tiny pictures to match. The air smelled like waxy crayons, and the paper felt warm under her toes.

“Listen,” Pippa said. “Flip-flip.” She turned each page so the ink could dry. “Tap-tap.” She lined them up.

Max smiled the biggest smile. “Pippa, this is even better. It’s clear, colorful, and kind. It shows how to solve problems by asking, waiting, and trading. This helps everyone.”

Pippa’s chest felt light and proud. “We did it,” she chirped. “We tried different steps. We stayed calm. We used our ideas.”

Max stapled the pages. “Click.” He held the report up. “You used problem-solving and creative expression. And you believed in yourself.”

Pippa nodded. “I can solve problems. I can be creative. I am enough.”

Max leaned down. “Thank you, Pippa. You taught me to take small steps and to look for new ways.”

Pippa fluffed. The sun made her feathers shine like little leaves. The printer sat quiet, not scary at all. Pippa patted it gently. “Maybe next time,” she said kindly. “We’ll try again.”

They set the colorful report on the desk. The room felt bright and peaceful. Pippa took a sip of cool water. “Ahh,” she said. “Teamwork tastes good.”

Lesson: When a problem feels big, take small steps, try new ideas, and believe in yourself. You can solve it.

The End.
