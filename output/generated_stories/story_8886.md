# Fair and Sunny Steps

In a sunny backyard, a gentle deer fawn named Fern blinked her big brown eyes. The grass was soft on her hooves. The air smelled like warm clover and sweet flowers. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” and a friendly breeze tickled Fern’s ears.

Fern loved to play in the sunshine. She loved to listen to the bees buzz and the leaves whisper. Today, she had a big idea. “Let’s make a leaf path!” Fern said. Her tail twitched with joy. “We can line up leaves from the gate to the big oak tree.”

Her friends popped up. Pip the squirrel bounced from the fence. “I’ll help!” he squeaked. Miri the bluebird fluttered down. “Me too!” she sang. Tully the turtle blinked slowly from a shady spot. “I can carry,” he said in a quiet, steady voice.

They started to work. Fern gathered red and yellow leaves. Pip raced, his tiny paws quick. Miri sang a happy song and picked tiny green leaves with her beak. Tully moved along with a soft slide sound, carrying a little pile on his shell.

The leaf path grew and grew. It looked like a rainbow ribbon on the grass. Fern smiled. “It’s so pretty,” she said. She felt proud, like a warm glow in her chest.

Suddenly, a thump-thump-thump sounded on the path. A big rabbit named Rolo hopped in. He wore a bright blue scarf. “Make way!” he shouted. He bounced right down the leaf path, kicking leaves into the air. Swish! Crunch! The ribbon broke into messy piles.

Fern’s heart dropped. “Oh no,” she whispered. Pip’s tail puffed out. “Hey! You messed it up!” he squeaked. Miri fluttered in circles. “Oh dear, oh dear!” Tully blinked and looked at the scattered leaves.

Rolo stopped and looked around. “What? I was just hopping,” he said. He sounded a little loud. Fern took a deep breath. She felt sad and a bit mad. Her ears felt hot. But she remembered being gentle. She remembered being fair.

Fern stepped forward. “Rolo,” she said softly, “we made this leaf path together. We worked hard. It is not fair to break it. Justice means we treat everyone right. When something is not fair, we fix it.”

Rolo twitched his whiskers. “I didn’t mean to make a mess,” he said. He looked at his feet. “I just wanted to hop fast.”

Fern nodded. “I hear you,” she said. “We like to hop too. But we also need to be careful. Being responsible means we take care of our choices. We can play, and we can be fair.”

Pip crossed his little arms. “So what now?” he asked. Miri tilted her head. “Can we fix it together?” Tully took a slow breath. “We can,” he said.

Rolo looked at the scattered leaves. He looked at Fern’s kind eyes. He listened to the birds and the breeze. He took a small, slow breath. “I want to help,” he said. “I want to make it right.”

Fern smiled. “Thank you,” she said. “That is justice. When we make a mistake, we help to fix it. That is being responsible.” She swished her tail and showed Rolo how to line up the leaves. “One by one, like sunny steps,” she said.

They all worked together. Pip placed big red leaves. Miri set small green leaves. Tully carried yellow leaves on his shell, soft as a humming sound. Rolo used his big paws to pat the leaves flat. Fern guided them. “Slow and gentle,” she said. The leaves made a happy rustle, like a tiny clap.

As they worked, Rolo spoke softly. “I am sorry,” he said to Pip and Miri and Tully. “I did not look. I will be careful.” Pip’s tail relaxed. “Thanks,” he said. Miri sang, “We forgive you.” Tully nodded. “We learn,” he added.

The path became bright and smooth again. It shone under the sun like a golden road. Fern placed the last leaf. “There,” she said. “All done.”

Rolo bounced once, very lightly. “May I hop here, slow and safe?” he asked. “Yes,” Fern said. “That is fair for everyone.” Rolo hopped gently. Pip walked beside him. Miri fluttered above. Tully slid along the side.

They made a new rule together. Fern said, “We take turns on the path.” Pip said, “We go slow.” Miri said, “We watch where we go.” Tully said, “We fix any mess.” Rolo added, “We say sorry and we help.”

Fern felt that warm glow again. The sun touched her back. The grass smelled sweet. She heard the soft rustle of leaves and the happy cheep of birds. She looked at her friends and smiled.

“Today we learned about justice,” Fern said. “Justice is fair for all. We also learned about responsibility. We take care of what we do. We fix mistakes. We are kind and we are careful.”

Rolo’s ears perked up. “Justice feels good,” he said. “It feels like the sun on my fur.” Everyone giggled.

They walked the leaf path together, one gentle, sunny step at a time.

The End.
