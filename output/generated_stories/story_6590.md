# Squeaky on the Front Porch

Squeaky the squirrel lived near a peaceful front porch. The porch was warm in the sun and cool in the shade. It smelled like sweet flowers and fresh leaves. Birds sang, and a soft breeze tickled Squeaky’s whiskers. Every morning, Squeaky made a little plan.

“I wake up, I stretch, I clean, I snack,” Squeaky sang. “That is my routine!”

Squeaky’s routine was special. He swept tiny leaves off the porch with a little broom made from a twig and grass. He put acorn caps in a neat line. He checked a blue bucket for rainwater and used a leaf cup to water the porch plants. He said, “Thank you, porch. Thank you, plants. Thank you, birds!” Squeaky liked to show gratitude every day.

One sunny morning, there was a new problem. A big paper bag had blown onto the porch. It was crinkly and loud. It covered the porch steps like a sleeping dragon. Squeaky wrinkled his nose. The bag smelled like salty chips, not like the clean porch.

“Oh no,” Squeaky said. “This does not belong here. It could blow into the garden. It could go into the stream. That would not be kind to the Earth.”

Squeaky tried to push the bag with his paws. “Push, push,” he said. The bag flapped and flew back. “Flap, flap!” It slapped Squeaky’s tail. Squeaky squeaked and jumped.

He tried to pull the bag by the string on the top. “Pull, pull,” he said. The string slid out and tickled his nose. “Ah-choo!” The bag puffed open and covered his head. It was crinkly and dark inside.

Squeaky sat very still. He took a slow breath. He remembered his routine. “I can do hard things,” he whispered. “I can be safe. I can keep trying.”

Squeaky wiggled free and peeked around. The porch was quiet. The breeze was soft. A robin called, “Cheer up! Cheer up!”

“Thank you, robin,” Squeaky said. “I will cheer up. I will try again.”

Squeaky looked around for help. A long stick leaned by the steps. A smooth rock sat by the flower pot. The blue bucket was by the railing. Squeaky’s eyes shined. He was a resourceful squirrel.

“I can make a plan,” he said. “I can use my brain and my body. I can use coordination.”

First, Squeaky moved the rock with both paws. “Roll, roll,” he said, rolling it close to the bag to keep it from blowing away. Next, he picked up the stick. He slid it under the edge of the bag. “Lift, lift,” he said. The bag rose a little.

“Now the bucket,” Squeaky said. He nudged the bucket next to the rock. “Tip, tip.” He tipped the bucket on its side. The bag slid toward the bucket, but then the breeze huffed. “Huff!” The bag puffed up again.

Squeaky put his paw on his chest. He felt his heart. Thump, thump. He took another slow breath. “I will keep trying,” he said. “I will be patient. I will be gentle.”

Just then, his friend Pip the chipmunk poked out from under the porch. “Hi, Squeaky! What are you doing?” Pip asked. His cheeks were full of seeds.

“I am saving the porch, the garden, and the stream,” Squeaky said. “I am moving this bag to the bin. Will you help me, please?”

Pip nodded. “Yes! We can do it together.”

“Thank you,” said Squeaky. “I am grateful for your help.”

They made a new plan. Squeaky held the stick under the bag edge. Pip held the other side of the bag with his tiny paws. “One, two, three,” Squeaky counted. They lifted together. “Up, up!”

The bag crinkled loudly. “Crinkle-crankle!” The sound was funny. Pip giggled. The bag almost slipped. Squeaky said, “Hold steady. We can do this.”

“Steady,” Pip said. They took small steps. Step, step, step. They moved like a little dance. The bag slid into the tilted blue bucket. “Plop!”

“We did it!” Pip cheered.

“Not yet,” said Squeaky. “We still need to put it in the recycling bin.” He pointed his tail to the big blue bin at the edge of the porch. “We will care for the Earth.”

They pushed the bucket together. “Push, push,” they said. The bucket rolled with a soft bump sound. Bump, bump, bump. When they reached the bin, Squeaky tipped the bucket and Pip guided the bag. “Slide, slide,” Pip said. The bag fell into the bin. “Whoosh!”

Squeaky and Pip looked at each other. Their eyes were bright. Their hearts felt big.

“We did it,” Squeaky said. “We stayed calm. We kept trying. We worked together. We took care of our porch and our Earth.”

Pip clapped his tiny paws. “I am proud,” he said.

Squeaky smiled. “I am grateful,” he said. He looked at the porch. The steps were clear. The flowers smiled with bright colors. The air smelled fresh again. The birds sang a happy song. The breeze felt gentle and kind.

Squeaky finished his routine. He swept the last little leaves. He lined up the acorn caps. He checked the blue bucket and set it back by the railing. He picked up his twig broom and said, “Thank you, porch. Thank you, plants. Thank you, Pip. Thank you, Earth.”

Pip bowed. “Thank you, Squeaky.”

Squeaky stretched and took a crunchy nut from his snack pile. “Munch, munch,” he said. “Today I learned: When there is a problem, I can be patient. I can keep trying. I can ask for help. I can take care of my home and our Earth.”

Pip nodded. “We can do hard things, one small step at a time.”

“Yes,” said Squeaky. “That is our routine.”

They sat on the peaceful front porch. The sun felt warm on their fur. The world felt clean and safe. And their hearts were full.

The End.
