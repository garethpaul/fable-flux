# Benny's Big Understanding

Benny the beaver loved his cozy study corner. He had soft cushions and bright books. Colorful crayons and smooth paper too. Every day he worked on his special projects. He drew pictures and practiced writing his name.

"Snack time, Benny!" called Mama Beaver.

Benny looked up from his drawing. He was making the most beautiful picture of their pond. The water was blue. The lily pads were green. He wasn't finished yet.

"But I'm not hungry," Benny said, his little paws still holding his crayon.

"Your tummy needs food to help you grow big and strong," Mama said kindly.

Benny's ears drooped. He really wanted to finish his picture. The sun in his drawing needed yellow rays. The fish needed silver scales.

He walked slowly to the kitchen. The apple slices looked crunchy. The cheese cubes smelled good. But Benny still felt sad about leaving his work.

"I wish I could draw and eat," Benny sighed.

Mama Beaver's eyes twinkled. "Maybe we can find a way," she said.

She brought a small tray to Benny's study corner. On it were his apple slices and cheese cubes. A little cup of water too.

"Now you can take care of your body AND finish your beautiful picture," Mama said.

Benny's tail wiggled with joy. "Really?"

"Really. But we need to be very careful. Food and art supplies don't mix well," Mama explained. "Can you eat first, then draw?"

Benny nodded. He understood now. His body needed food just like his mind needed creativity. He could do both if he was careful.

He took a big bite of apple. It was sweet and crunchy. The cheese was creamy and smooth. His tummy felt happy.

"Now I'm ready to draw!" Benny said, wiping his paws clean.

He picked up his yellow crayon. Carefully, he drew bright rays around his sun. His paws felt steady. His mind felt clear.

"This is the best picture ever," Benny said proudly.

"And you took such good care of yourself too," Mama said, giving him a warm hug.

Just then, Benny's little sister Bella peeked into the study corner. Her eyes grew wide at the beautiful picture.

"Can I draw too?" Bella asked hopefully.

Benny looked at his special corner. Then he looked at Bella's excited face. He understood that sharing could make things even more fun.

"Of course! But we take turns with the crayons," Benny said. "And we both need to eat our snacks first."

Bella clapped her tiny paws. "I understand!"

They sat together in the cozy corner. First they ate their healthy snacks. Then they took turns with the crayons. Benny helped Bella draw a purple butterfly. Bella helped Benny add pink flowers.

"Understanding makes everything better," Benny said, looking at their beautiful picture together.

Mama Beaver smiled from the doorway. Benny had learned that taking care of himself was important. He had learned that sharing and taking turns made things more fun. Most of all, he had learned that understanding others' needs helped everyone feel happy.

The study corner felt even more special now. It was a place for creativity, for self-care, and for sharing with others.

The End.
