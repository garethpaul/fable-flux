# The Fawn Who Found a Fair Song

The warm kitchen glowed like morning toast. Sunlight buttered the wooden table. A gentle deer fawn named Fern stood on a soft rug, her small hooves tap-tapping. The air smelled like cinnamon and soap. Pots and pans hung like shiny moons. A kettle sang a tiny song—peep, peep, peep.

Fern loved music. She hummed, “La-la-la,” and the sound bounced off the walls. “Today,” she said, “I will sing in the kitchen band!”

On the counter sat three friends: a wooden spoon, a clean bowl, and a jingly bell. They were ready to make music. But in the sink, a sticky pan sat with icky crumbs. It did not shine. It gave a sour smell, like old milk. Fern wrinkled her nose. “Phew.”

Mama Deer stepped in with a warm smile. “Good morning, Fern,” she said, her voice like a soft song. “I hear the kitchen band wants to play.”

Fern nodded. “Yes! I will sing. The spoon can tap. The bell can jingle.” She looked at the pan and frowned. “But that pan is yucky.”

Mama Deer washed her hooves with warm water and bubbly soap. The bubbles giggled as they popped. “Before music, we make things clean,” Mama Deer said. “Clean hands. Clean tools. Clean space. That is fair to everyone.”

“Fair?” asked Fern, ears lifting.

“Fair means justice,” said Mama Deer kindly. “Everyone gets a turn and the same care. Even the pan. No one is left sticky.”

Fern thought. She looked at her own hooves. They had a little flour on them. “I will wash,” she said. “La-la, wash-wash!” The warm water hugged her hooves. The soap smelled springy, like lemons. She watched bubbles dance. “This is fun,” she giggled.

Now clean, Fern tiptoed to the sink. She looked at the sticky pan. It looked back, dull and tired. “You can still shine,” Fern whispered. She picked up a soft sponge. Squish, squish. Circle, circle. She scrubbed gently. The water whooshed. The crumbs went away. The pan began to glow like a small sun.

“This is improvement,” Mama Deer said. “You made it better.”

Fern smiled. Her chest felt warm, like cocoa. “I can improve my singing too,” she said. “I used to sing too fast. I will slow down and breathe.”

She took a deep breath. In. Out. The kitchen smelled sweet, like cinnamon toast and lemon soap. “La… la… la,” Fern sang slowly, her voice smooth and bright.

The wooden spoon tapped the clean bowl—tink… tink… tink. The bell jingled—jingle jingle! The kettle joined—peep, peep!

Fern closed her eyes and listened. “We sound happy,” she said.

A small ant on the windowsill peered in. “May I listen?” the ant squeaked.

Fern opened one eye. “Yes! But first, please walk on the dry mat,” she said gently. “We keep the floor clean. That is fair to everyone so no one slips.”

The ant nodded and marched onto the mat. “Thank you,” it said.

A bluebird at the door chirped, “Tweet? Can I sing too?”

Fern looked at the spoon, the bowl, and the bell. “We have space for one more voice,” she said. “But we share. One at a time. Everyone gets a turn.” Her ears wiggled. “That is justice in our band.”

The bluebird sang a soft tweet. Fern waited, then added her “La.” The spoon tapped after. The bell jingled last. Round and round the turns went. No one rushed. No one pushed. The music felt like warm cookies—sweet and fair.

Then—clunk! The door wiggled. A little gust of wind blew in dust. The clean bowl got a sprinkle of crumbs. “Oh no,” said Fern. The music stopped.

The bluebird looked worried. “Is the song broken?”

Fern shook her head. “No. We can fix it.” She took a cloth and wiped the crumbs gently. “We clean again. We improve again.” She washed her hooves once more. The water sang, splish splash. The soap bubbled, pop pop.

Mama Deer watched, eyes kind. “You are learning,” she said. “You are calm. You are fair. You are careful. That is how we grow.”

Fern felt brave and proud. “I was a little fawn,” she said softly. “Now I am a little better fawn.”

They began again. Fern sang, “Laaaa,” steady as a warm hug. The spoon tapped, the bell jingled, the bluebird trilled, the kettle peeped. Even the shiny pan hummed along, happy to be clean.

When the song ended, the kitchen was quiet and cozy. The air was fresh. The floor was dry. The tools were clean. Everyone had a turn. Everyone felt seen.

Fern lifted her head. “Today I learned three things,” she said. “First, wash up to keep us safe and healthy. Second, be fair. Take turns and care for all. Third, keep improving. Clean, try again, and sing better each time.”

Mama Deer nuzzled her. “That is a beautiful lesson,” she whispered.

Fern smiled and whispered back, “La-la-good-job,” to herself. It felt like a soft star inside—bright and calm.

They shared warm cinnamon toast. The butter melted and the toast crackled. The band rested. The kitchen glowed.

And the little fawn knew: with clean hooves, a fair heart, and a gentle song, she could always make the day better.

The End.
