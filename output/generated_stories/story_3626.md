# Ruby Rabbit’s Balcony Plan

Ruby was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She lived with her family in a cozy home with a sunny balcony garden. The balcony smelled like fresh mint and wet soil. Little wind chimes went tinkle-tinkle in the breeze. Ruby loved to hop outside and wiggle her nose at the flowers.

One bright morning, Ruby had a big idea. “I want to make garden art!” she said, her whiskers twitching. “I will paint a flower sign for our balcony.” She imagined a sign with yellow sunbeams, red tulips, and a happy blue sky.

Ruby grabbed her paintbrush. She hopped to the paint tray. She bounced to the flower pots. She looked at the wall where the sign could go. There were so many things to think about! Ruby felt excited, but also a little jiggly inside.

Mama Rabbit peeked out. “What are you making, Ruby?” she asked, smiling.

“I want to paint a beautiful sign,” Ruby said. “Right now! Right away!”

Mama knelt beside her. “That sounds wonderful,” Mama said. “What is your plan?”

“My plan?” Ruby blinked. She had not made a plan. She had only the big idea bouncing around like a rubber ball in her head.

Mama nodded. “Planning helps big ideas grow,” she said softly. “Let’s make a simple plan. First, we think. Then, we gather. Next, we do. And last, we clean.”

Ruby took a deep breath. The wet soil smelled earthy and kind. “Okay,” she said. “First, think.”

Ruby sat on a little stool. The stool felt smooth and cool under her paws. She closed her eyes. She pictured the sign: big, bright, and easy to read. “I want the sign to say Garden,” she whispered. “I want a red tulip, a yellow sun, and a tiny green leaf.”

“Good thinking,” said Mama. “Now, gather.”

Ruby made a list, saying each word out loud as she touched each thing:
“Paintbrush.” Tap.
“Paints.” Tap-tap.
“Water cup.” Swish.
“Rag.” Soft pat.
“Cardboard for the sign.” Rustle.

Then she looked at the flowers on the balcony. She loved them all. She wanted to pick one to trace its shape. Her paw reached toward a bright red tulip. The petals were velvety and smelled sweet.

But Ruby stopped. She remembered honesty. She remembered the rule: “Ask before you pick.” Her tummy felt warm with truth.

Ruby hopped to Mama. “Mama,” she said, “I want to trace the tulip. May I pick one?”

Mama looked at the tulip and then at Ruby. “Thank you for asking,” Mama said. “We need the tulip to grow seeds. Could you trace a fallen petal instead?”

Ruby looked around. She saw a soft petal on the floor near the pot. It was red like a tiny heart. “Yes!” she said. “I will use this petal.”

“Good choice,” said Mama.

Ruby set her cardboard on a low table. The table was bumpy, so the cardboard made a crunchy sound. She dipped her brush in water. Swish. She dabbed yellow paint. Dab-dab. “First, the sun,” she said, planning each step. “Next, the tulip. Last, the word Garden.”

She painted slow and steady. She counted in a gentle voice. “One, two, three rays of sun. Four, five, six.” The sun looked warm and happy. She traced around the fallen petal to make the tulip shape. It made a soft, curvy red line. Then she added a tiny green leaf with a little curl.

Ruby tried to write the word Garden. The G looked like a giggling noodle. The R was a little wiggly. Ruby frowned. “Oh no,” she said. “It’s not perfect.”

Mama touched Ruby’s shoulder. “Planning helps, and practice helps too,” Mama said. “Would you like to sketch the letters in pencil first?”

Ruby nodded. “Yes, please.” She sketched slowly: G-A-R-D-E-N. She said each letter out loud. “G makes guh. A makes ah.” The pencil made a soft scratch-scratch sound. Then she painted over the letters with careful strokes.

At last, the sign was done. Ruby held it up. The paint smelled a little like fruit. The colors looked sunny and bright. The wind chimes went tinkle-tinkle again, as if they were clapping.

Ruby smiled. “I followed my plan,” she said proudly. “Think, gather, do, clean.”

Mama smiled too. “You also told the truth and asked before picking. That was honest. And you made lovely art.”

Ruby looked at the brushes and the paint cup. “Now I clean,” she said. She washed the brush. Swish-swish. She wiped the table. Wipe-wipe. She put the paints away. Click, clack.

When everything was tidy, Ruby and Mama hung the sign on the balcony wall. It felt a little rough under Ruby’s paws as she pressed it in place. The sun warmed her fur. The sign brightened the garden like a smile.

Papa Rabbit stepped outside. He sniffed the mint and grinned. “What a beautiful sign! How did you do it, Ruby?”

Ruby stood tall. “I made a plan,” she said. “I asked before I took the tulip. I made art with care.”

Papa clapped his paws. “That is the smart way.”

Ruby’s ears stood up high. She felt peaceful and proud. “Planning helps big ideas grow,” she said.

Mama added, “And honesty makes hearts feel safe.”

Ruby nodded. “And art makes the world bright.”

They all looked at the balcony garden together. The flowers swayed. The chimes sang. Ruby’s sign shone in the sunshine.

That night, as the sky turned purple, Ruby whispered to her sign, “Tomorrow, I will plan again.”

The End.
