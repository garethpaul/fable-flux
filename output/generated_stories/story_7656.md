# Rascal's Safe Art Adventure

Rascal the raccoon loved making music. He had tiny cymbals on his paws that went *clink-clink* when he walked. Today, he discovered something wonderful - the creative art room!

"Wow!" Rascal said, his black eyes growing wide. "Look at all these colors and treasures!"

The art room smelled like crayons and glue. Bright papers covered the tables. Paintbrushes stood tall in jars like colorful flowers. Best of all, there was a big box labeled "Recycling Treasures" filled with empty bottles, cardboard tubes, and shiny cans.

"I can make the most beautiful music instrument ever!" Rascal said excitedly. His cymbals went *clink-clink* as he reached for a sharp can.

"Wait!" called Ms. Robin, the art teacher. Her voice was gentle but firm. "That can has sharp edges, little one. It could hurt your paws."

Rascal felt his whiskers droop. "But I want to make music with it," he said, his voice getting wobbly.

Ms. Robin smiled warmly. "Let me show you something special." She picked up the can with special gloves. "See these jagged edges? They're like tiny teeth that could cut you."

Rascal looked closer. The can's edge was indeed sharp and scary-looking.

"But don't worry," Ms. Robin continued. "We can make it safe!" She carefully covered the sharp edges with colorful tape. "Now it's smooth and safe to touch."

Rascal gently touched the taped edge. It felt soft and bumpy, not sharp at all. "It's safe now!" he said happily.

"That's right! Now, what else do you need for your instrument?"

Rascal looked around the recycling box more carefully this time. He found smooth cardboard tubes, plastic bottles with no sharp parts, and colorful bottle caps.

"These are all safe," he said proudly, checking each piece.

"Excellent! You're being very careful," Ms. Robin praised.

Rascal began building. He put rice in a plastic bottle to make a shaker. *Shake-shake-shake!* He taped bottle caps to a cardboard tube to make a rattle. *Tap-tap-tap!* But when he tried to attach the safe can, it kept falling off.

"Grrrr!" Rascal growled, his fur standing up. "This is too hard!"

He wanted to throw the can, but he remembered it was still heavy and could hurt someone.

"I see you're feeling frustrated," Ms. Robin said kindly. "When I feel upset, I take three deep breaths. Want to try?"

Rascal breathed in slowly through his nose. *Sniff.* He breathed out through his mouth. *Whoosh.* One... two... three times.

"I feel calmer now," Rascal said, his fur lying flat again.

"Good! Now, what if we try a different way?" Ms. Robin suggested.

Together, they used strong tape and let it dry. While they waited, Rascal practiced his other instruments. The shaker went *shake-shake*, the rattle went *tap-tap*, and his cymbals went *clink-clink*.

"My recycled orchestra!" Rascal laughed.

When the can was ready, Rascal tapped it gently with a safe wooden spoon. *Bong-bong-bong!* It made the most wonderful sound.

"I did it!" Rascal cheered. "I made beautiful music safely!"

Ms. Robin clapped her wings. "You learned to check for safety, cope with frustration, and create something amazing from recycled materials!"

Rascal played his new instruments together - *shake-tap-clink-bong!* The music filled the art room with joy.

"Safety first makes everything more fun," Rascal said wisely, his cymbals sparkling in the light.

The End.
