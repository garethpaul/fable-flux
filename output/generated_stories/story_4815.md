# Ollie Otter’s Rooftop Rest-and-Play Day

Ollie the playful otter loved to wiggle, splash, and giggle. He lived in a cozy house with a big, flat rooftop garden. The rooftop smelled like mint and sweet flowers. Soft wind tickled his whiskers. The city hummed below like a gentle song.

One sunny morning, Ollie bounced up the stairs. Thump-thump-thump! He carried a small paint set, a cup of water, and his favorite soft brush.

“I’m going to paint the sky!” he cheered.

On the rooftop, Ollie’s best friends were waiting. Mimi the mouse waved a tiny paw. Pip the pigeon fluffed his feathers. Gigi the gecko blinked with bright eyes.

“Hi, Ollie!” they called.

“Hi, friends!” Ollie said, wiggling his tail. “Let’s make art!”

They set up a long paper roll on a low table. The paper felt smooth under their paws and feet. The paint smelled like fresh berries. Ollie dipped his brush into blue paint. Swish, swish! The sky on paper looked bright and happy.

Pip cooed, “Let’s paint clouds!”

Mimi squeaked, “And flowers!”

Gigi whispered, “I’ll paint tiny stars.”

They painted and laughed. Ollie sang a silly song. “Paint, paint, wiggle and wink! Sky so blue, what do you think?”

But after a while, Ollie’s whiskers drooped. His tummy made a little grumble. His paws felt tired. His brush slipped and made a splat. Splat!

“Oh no,” Ollie sighed. “I want to keep painting, but I feel slow.”

Mimi peeked up. “Maybe your body wants a rest.”

Pip tilted his head. “A drink of water might help.”

Gigi softly said, “We can take care of ourselves and still have fun.”

Ollie blinked. “Take care of myself? Even when I want to play?”

“Yes,” his friends said together. “Self-care helps us feel good.”

Ollie looked at the sky. The sun felt warm on his fur. The breeze smelled like basil and rain. He took a deep breath in. He let a long breath out. “I can try,” he said.

Mimi brought a small basket. Inside were crunchy apple slices and tiny carrot sticks. Pip carried cool water in a cup. Gigi rolled out a soft mat.

Ollie sat on the mat. The mat felt snug and comfy. He sipped water. Sip, sip. It tasted clean and cool. He munched an apple slice. Crunch! Sweet juice tickled his tongue. He smiled.

“This feels nice,” Ollie said.

“Let’s do a wiggle break,” Pip cooed.

They stood tall, paws and wings and tiny toes. Gigi showed a gentle stretch. “Arms up like the sun.” They reached up, up, up. “Arms down like the rain.” They floated their arms down. “Belly breath like a balloon.” They breathed in, big and round. They breathed out, soft and slow.

Ollie felt lighter. His tummy felt calm. His paws felt strong again.

Mimi handed him a small towel. “We can wash and dry our paws before painting more,” she said. The warm water felt silky. The towel felt fluffy. Ollie’s paws were clean and ready.

Ollie giggled. “Self-care is tasty, stretchy, and clean!”

Pip nodded. “And friendly. We remind each other to rest.”

Gigi added, “That is wellness. Our bodies and hearts feel good.”

They went back to the table. The paper waited like a bright path. Ollie picked up his brush. He painted the softest cloud. He painted a sun with gentle rays. He painted an otter taking a nap on a tiny rooftop.

Mimi painted a bowl of fruit. Pip painted blue swirls like wind. Gigi painted teeny stars around a big moon. The colors looked calm and joyful.

Soon, Ollie felt a yawn coming. Yaaaawn. He smiled. “I think I need a quiet minute.”

“Good idea,” Mimi said.

They made a cozy corner with pillows. Ollie lay down and closed his eyes. He listened. Whoosh went the breeze. Hum went the city. Tap-tap-tap went Pip’s small feet. He felt safe and warm.

After a short rest, Ollie sat up. “I feel fresh! Thank you.”

“Happy hearts need rest,” Gigi said.

Ollie and his friends looked at their painting. It showed sky, sun, fruit, wind, and stars. It showed friends caring for each other. It showed a little otter who learned to listen to his body.

Ollie dipped his brush one last time and painted big, friendly letters at the bottom: “Rest, Sip, Stretch, Smile.”

He turned to his friends. “Today I learned a big thing. I can play and paint. I can also pause and care for me.”

Mimi cheered, “That’s self-care!”

Pip cooed, “That’s friendship.”

Gigi smiled, “That’s wellness.”

Ollie hugged his friends. His fur felt soft against their cheeks. The sun warmed their faces. The painting dried in the gentle wind, colors shining.

Ollie wiggled with happy pride. “When I rest, I feel my best. When I share, I feel I care. When I breathe, I do believe—I can try again.”

They laughed and made a plan. “Tomorrow,” Ollie said, “we paint the moon first, and we take snack breaks together.”

“Yes!” said Mimi.

“Yes!” said Pip.

“Yes!” said Gigi.

Together, they cleaned the brushes. Swish in water. Pat with towel. They rolled up the paper and tied it with a ribbon.

Ollie looked at the sky once more. “Thank you, rooftop,” he whispered. “Thank you, friends. Thank you, self-care.”

He felt calm, strong, and loved.

The End.
