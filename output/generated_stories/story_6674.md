# Ellie's Colorful Clean-Up

Ellie the elephant calf stood in the doorway of the creative art room. Paint brushes lay scattered on tables. Colorful paper scraps covered the floor like confetti. Glue bottles had their caps off, and crayons rolled everywhere.

"Oh my!" Ellie said, her trunk swaying gently. The room smelled like paint and markers. It looked like a rainbow had exploded!

Mrs. Robin, the art teacher, walked in behind Ellie. "What a mess we made today," she said with a tired smile. "I have so much cleaning to do before tomorrow's class."

Ellie watched Mrs. Robin pick up paint brushes one by one. The teacher's shoulders looked heavy. Ellie's big heart felt sad seeing her favorite teacher so tired.

"Mrs. Robin," Ellie said softly, "can I help you clean up?"

Mrs. Robin looked surprised. "That's very kind, Ellie, but cleaning isn't fun. You should go play instead."

Ellie shook her big gray head. "I want to help! We made this mess together. We should clean it together too."

Mrs. Robin smiled. "Well, if you really want to help..."

Ellie clapped her trunk excitedly. "Yes! What should I do first?"

"Let's start with the crayons," Mrs. Robin said. "Can you use your trunk to pick them up?"

Ellie nodded eagerly. She carefully wrapped her trunk around each crayon. Red ones. Blue ones. Yellow ones. She sorted them into the big crayon box by color. The crayons felt smooth and waxy against her trunk.

"You're so good at this!" Mrs. Robin said. "Your trunk is perfect for picking up small things."

Next, they tackled the paper scraps. Ellie used her trunk like a vacuum cleaner, gathering up all the colorful pieces. Some were sticky with glue. Others had paint splotches. She didn't mind at all.

"This is actually fun!" Ellie giggled as she picked up a purple paper butterfly.

Mrs. Robin laughed too. "You know what? You're right! Cleaning is more fun when we do it together."

They worked as a team. Mrs. Robin wiped down tables while Ellie organized art supplies. She put caps back on markers. She stacked paper neatly. She even used her trunk to dust the high shelves that Mrs. Robin couldn't reach.

The room began to sparkle. The floor was clean. The tables were tidy. All the art supplies were in their proper places.

"Wow!" said Mrs. Robin. "We finished so quickly! Thank you, Ellie. You turned a big chore into something wonderful."

Ellie felt warm and proud inside. Her trunk wiggled with happiness. "I liked helping! Can I help clean up every day?"

"Of course!" Mrs. Robin said. "Would you like to be my special cleaning helper?"

"Yes, please!" Ellie trumpeted softly.

Just then, Tommy the turtle peeked in. "The art room looks amazing! How did you clean it so fast?"

"Ellie volunteered to help," Mrs. Robin explained. "She showed me that chores can be fun when friends work together."

Tommy's eyes grew wide. "Can I help tomorrow too?"

"The more helpers, the better!" Mrs. Robin said.

Ellie beamed. By doing her part and helping with chores, she had made Mrs. Robin happy and inspired Tommy to help too. The clean, organized art room smelled fresh and felt peaceful.

"Thank you for teaching me that helping others feels good," Ellie said to Mrs. Robin.

"And thank you for teaching me that chores are better with friends," Mrs. Robin replied.

As they turned off the lights, Ellie felt proud. Tomorrow, the art room would be ready for more creative fun, and she had helped make it happen.

The End.
