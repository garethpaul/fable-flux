# Rooftop Hops and Calm Down Stops

A joyful kangaroo joey named Kip loved to hop. One sunny afternoon, Kip hopped up the stairs to the tall rooftop garden. The roof smelled like mint leaves and warm sun. Little bells on the fence went ting-ting in the breeze. Kip’s soft tail brushed the flowers as he bounced. He giggled, “Boing, boing, boing!”

On the rooftop, friends were meeting for Story Time. There was Lulu the ladybug, Pip the pigeon, and Nia the neighborhood kid with curly hair. They sat on colorful cushions. A big basket of picture cards lay in the middle. Each card showed a place, a feeling, or a friend.

Nia clapped her hands. “Let’s tell a story together!”

Kip’s eyes sparkled. “I want to start! I love stories!”

Pip puffed his chest. “Coo! Me too.”

Lulu zipped her wings. “We can all share.”

Nia smiled. “We will take turns. We will listen. We will show respect. If we feel mad, we will use calm-down steps.”

Kip nodded, but he was very excited. His feet wiggled and his ears twitched. The mint leaves smelled extra minty. The sun felt warm like a soft blanket.

Nia pulled a card. It showed a brave seed. “Once there was a tiny seed,” she began.

Kip bounced. “And the seed—”

Nia held up a friendly finger. “Wait, Kip. It’s my turn. You will get a turn too.”

Kip’s tummy felt tight. He wanted to talk now. His face felt hot, like a warm tomato. He gripped his tail. He huffed a little huff.

Lulu noticed. “I hear a huff,” she said softly. “Is that an angry huff?”

Kip nodded. “I want to tell the story. I have big ideas.”

Nia said, “We respect each other’s turns. When we wait, we listen. When we listen, our story grows.”

Pip cooed, “Coo, coo. We can share words like seeds.”

Kip took a slow breath. He remembered: Calm-down steps. Nia taught them last week. He put his paws on his belly. He breathed in through his nose—sniff, sniff—like smelling warm cookies. He breathed out through his mouth—whooo—like blowing a bubble. He looked at the sky. The clouds were puffy and friendly.

He whispered, “Smell the cookies, blow the bubble.”

The tight feeling started to melt. His cheeks cooled. He nodded. “Okay. I can wait.”

Nia smiled. “Thank you, Kip. That is respect.”

She continued the story. “The tiny seed felt small on a windy rooftop.”

Lulu buzzed, “But the seed listened to the wind.”

Pip pecked a card with a sun. “The sun said, ‘I am warm. I will help you grow.’ Coo!”

Now it was Kip’s turn. Nia handed him a card with a watering can. Kip held it carefully. The card felt smooth and cool.

Kip said, “The seed got a little drink. Drip, drop.” He made soft drip, drop sounds with his tongue.

Everyone smiled. Kip glowed inside. He had waited, and now his words fit just right.

The story grew and grew. They added a kind ant and a funny worm. Kip did the worm’s silly voice. “Wiggle, wiggle!” Everyone laughed. The bells went ting-ting-ting.

Then came a tricky part. Pip wanted the next turn, but Kip wanted it too. Kip grabbed a card fast. Pip’s feathers fluffed. “Coo? My turn!”

Kip felt that hot tomato face again. His paws squeezed the card. The wind seemed louder. The mint smell felt sharp.

He remembered the calm-down steps. “Smell the cookies, blow the bubble,” he whispered. He took a big breath. In. Out. He counted softly, “One, two, three.”

He looked at Pip. “I’m sorry,” Kip said. “I grabbed. You can go. I will wait.”

Pip’s feathers settled. “Thank you, Kip. That is respect.”

Nia nodded. “That is anger-management. You noticed your mad. You calmed your body. You used kind words.”

Kip felt proud. His heart did a happy hop. He watched Pip add a card with a rainbow. The story got brighter and brighter.

At the end, the tiny seed grew into a rooftop flower with red petals and a golden center. The flower said, “Thank you, friends, for your stories. You listened. You shared. You helped me grow.”

Kip hugged his tail. “We did it!” he cheered.

Nia clapped. “Storytelling is like gardening. We plant words with respect. We water them with listening. We calm our feelings so our story can bloom.”

Lulu buzzed a gentle song. Pip cooed a soft coo. The sun dipped orange and pink. The mint leaves danced in the breeze. The bells whispered ting-ting, like tiny applause.

Kip stood up and spoke clearly, “I learned three things today:
- Tell stories together.
- Show respect and take turns.
- When I feel mad, I can breathe and use kind words.”

Nia grinned. “Beautiful lesson, Kip.”

They packed the cards, waved goodbye, and hopped down the stairs. Kip’s steps were light. His heart felt calm and bright. He whispered to himself, “Smell the cookies, blow the bubble. Share the story, let it double.”

On another day, they would make a new story. But tonight, the rooftop was quiet, and the flower glowed like a tiny lantern of gold.

The End.
