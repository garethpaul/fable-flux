# Benny Bunny’s Treehouse Day

Benny was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived near a tall oak tree with a cozy treehouse tucked between the branches. The wind rustled the leaves, whispering shhh, shhh, and the wood smelled warm and sunny.

“This is my big day,” Benny said, twitching his whiskers. “I will host a play-and-snack time in my treehouse!” He bounced in a circle and took a deep breath. “I can do it if I get ready.”

Benny began to prepare. He swept the floor with a tiny broom. Swish, swish. He laid out soft leaf mats. Pat, pat. He set up a basket of carrots and apples. Crunchy and sweet! He set a little sign by the ladder that said, “Welcome, friends!”

He also made a plan. He looked at his list and tapped it with his paw. “Snacks ready? Yes. Games ready? Yes. Kind words ready?” Benny smiled. “Yes! I will use kind words. I will listen with my big bunny ears. I will show respect.”

Soon, his friends arrived. Tilly the turtle climbed slowly up the ladder. “Hello, Benny,” she said in a gentle voice.

“Hello, Tilly! Take your time,” Benny said. He held the ladder steady with both paws. He looked into Tilly’s eyes and smiled. “I’m happy you’re here.”

Next came Pip the chipmunk, chattering fast. “I brought nuts! I brought songs! I brought a dance!” Pip’s tail swished like a broom.

“Thank you, Pip,” said Benny. “Your nuts look yummy. Your ideas sound fun!”

Last came Mo the mole. Mo blinked under his tiny hat. “It is bright up here,” he said softly.

Benny nodded. “I hear you, Mo. I will close the window shade a little.” He pulled the shade down. The light became soft and calm. “Is that better?”

Mo smiled. “Yes. Thank you for listening.”

Benny felt proud. He was prepared. He was showing respect.

They all sat on leaf mats. “First, we share,” Benny said. “Everyone gets a turn. We listen when a friend talks. We use kind, gentle words.”

Pip wiggled. “I want to go first! I want to go first!”

Benny put a paw on his chest. “I hear you, Pip. You are excited. Let’s let Tilly share first, because she climbed so slowly and worked so hard. Then it will be your turn. Does that feel fair?”

Pip looked at Tilly. Tilly smiled and nodded. Pip took a deep breath. “Okay. I can wait.”

Tilly shared her story. “Today I saw a shiny pebble. It felt cool and smooth.” She passed it around. Benny held it and felt its smooth, round face. “Thank you for sharing,” he said.

Then Pip sang a short song. His voice was bright like tiny bells. Benny listened, still and quiet, ears tall. When Pip finished, Benny clapped softly. “That was lovely. Thank you, Pip.”

Mo spoke next. “Sometimes loud sounds scare me,” he said. “I like quiet.”

Benny said, “Thank you for telling us, Mo. We can be gentle and quiet here.” He looked at Pip. Pip nodded and whispered, “I can sing softly.” They all smiled.

Soon it was game time. Benny brought out a small basket and some soft cloth balls. “We will play ‘Roll and Tell,’” he said. “We roll the ball. When you catch it, you say one kind thing.”

Pip rolled the ball to Tilly. “You are patient,” Pip said.

Tilly rolled to Mo. “You are brave to share your feelings.”

Mo rolled to Benny. “You are a good host.”

Benny’s heart felt warm. “Thank you,” he said. “You are all special.”

After the game, Benny served snacks. He looked at the basket. “We have enough for everyone,” he said. “We will pass the plate and wait our turn.” He passed the carrots to Tilly, then Pip, then Mo. Finally, he took one for himself. Crunch, crunch. The apples were juicy and sweet. Drip, drip, went the juice on their chins. They laughed and wiped with napkins.

Then a little problem came. Pip wanted to hang his new drawing on the wall. “I will put it here,” Pip said, reaching over Tilly’s head.

Benny saw Tilly lean back, surprised. He hopped close and spoke kindly. “Pip, please ask before you reach over Tilly. We need to keep her safe and comfortable. That is respect.”

Pip’s cheeks puffed. He looked at Tilly. “I’m sorry, Tilly. May I put my drawing up now?”

Tilly nodded. “Yes, please. Thank you for asking.”

Pip smiled and taped the drawing. It showed a big sun and four happy friends. Benny patted Pip’s shoulder. “Good asking,” he said. “Good listening, Tilly.”

The sun slipped lower outside, and the treehouse felt cozy and calm. Benny looked around. “We did it,” he said. “We prepared. We listened. We took turns. We asked before we acted. That is respect. When we show respect, everyone feels safe and happy.”

Mo nodded. “I felt calm today.”

Tilly said, “I felt seen.”

Pip said, “I felt proud.”

Benny’s whiskers twitched. “I felt confident,” he said. “I can host again because I can prepare and be kind.”

They put away the games and cleaned up together. Swish, pat, stack. Benny opened the door, and a soft evening breeze drifted in, smelling like leaves and apple peels.

“Thank you for coming,” Benny said at the ladder. “I respect you. You matter.”

“Good night!” called his friends as they climbed down, one by one.

Benny looked around his tidy treehouse and smiled. “Respect makes our day bright,” he whispered. His heart felt big and brave. He knew he could learn and grow, one kind choice at a time.

The End.
