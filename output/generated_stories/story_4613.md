# Fawn’s Little Garden Job

A gentle deer fawn named Dottie stepped onto the sunny balcony garden. Warm light kissed her soft ears. The air smelled like mint and wet soil. Bees hummed. Tiny pots lined the railings, filled with basil, cherry tomatoes, and bright yellow marigolds.

Today, Dottie had a job. She held a tiny watering can with blue stars. It felt cool and a little heavy in her hoof. “I can do this,” Dottie whispered to herself. Her heart felt fluttery, like butterfly wings.

“Good morning, plants,” she said. “I will water you and check your leaves.” The leaves rustled as if they said, “Hello!”

Dottie started with the basil. Drip, drip, drip. The soil turned dark and damp. She sniffed. “Mmm, minty and fresh.” She smiled. “I am careful and kind.”

Then she saw the cherry tomato plant. Tiny round tomatoes hung like red beads. But some leaves were droopy. Dottie’s ears dipped. “Oh no. Did I do something wrong?” She took a slow breath the way Mama Deer taught her. In and out. “I will ask for help. I can speak up.”

Dottie called softly, “Excuse me, Sparrow. Can you help me?” A little brown sparrow landed on the railing and tilted his head. “Chirp! What do you need, Dottie?”

“I want to take care of the tomatoes,” Dottie said. “Some leaves look sad. I feel unsure. Can you show me what to do?”

“That is brave to ask,” said Sparrow. “Touch the soil. Is it dry?”

Dottie pressed a careful hoof. “It feels dusty and crumbly.”

“Then a little more water,” Sparrow chirped. “Not too much. Just a gentle rain.”

Dottie lifted her blue-star can and poured slowly. Drip, drip. The plant seemed to stand taller. Dottie felt a warm, proud glow in her chest. “I did it,” she said. “Thank you for helping me know what to do.”

“You asked for what you needed,” Sparrow said. “Good self-advocacy!”

Dottie giggled at the big word. “Self-ad-vo-ca-cy,” she said, tapping each sound. “That means I can use my voice.”

She moved to the marigolds. They smelled like sunshine and pepper. A ladybug crawled across a petal. “Hello, little dot friend,” Dottie said. “Are you busy?”

“I am,” said the ladybug in a tiny voice. “I eat the tiny bugs that nibble the leaves. You water. I will guard.” Dottie nodded. “Teamwork!”

Dottie checked each pot. She made a small plan in her mind:
- First water.
- Then wipe leaves.
- Last, tidy the garden mat.

“I can do it, step by step,” she told herself. She felt calm. Her breath was steady. She hummed a little tune: “Sip, sip, little plants. Grow, grow, little plants.”

As she wiped a big green leaf, a gust of wind whooshed across the balcony. Whoosh! The watering can wobbled. It tipped. Water splashed onto Dottie’s legs and the garden mat. “Oh!” Dottie squeaked. Her eyes grew wide. “I made a mess.”

Her tummy did a tiny flip. She wanted to hide. But then she remembered her plan. She stood tall, as tall as a small fawn could. “It’s okay,” she whispered. “I can fix this. I am learning.”

She looked around and called, “Excuse me, Breeze. Please be gentle. I am working here.”

The wind softened to a kind sigh. “Hoo,” said the Breeze. “Thank you for telling me what you need.”

Dottie smiled. She found a cloth and dabbed the mat. Pat, pat, pat. She set the watering can on a flat tile, safe and steady. “There,” she said. “I can solve problems.”

Sparrow chirped. “You kept going. That builds self-esteem!”

Dottie stood near the railing and checked her list in her mind. Water, done. Wipe, done. Tidy, almost done. She picked up a fallen leaf and put it in the compost pot. She lined up the plant pots so every plant could see the sky.

Squirrel peeked over the railing with shiny eyes. “Need help?” he asked, twitching his tail.

“Thank you,” Dottie said kindly. “I want to finish this myself today. I am practicing completing my job.” Her voice was small but strong. “But you can cheer!”

Squirrel clapped his tiny paws. “Go, Dottie, go!”

Dottie’s hooves made a happy tap on the tiles as she did the last step. Tap, tap. She looked at the garden. The leaves were clean. The soil was damp, not soggy. The air smelled green and good. Bees hummed again, like a soft song of “Well done.”

Dottie’s heart felt big and bright. She said out loud, “I am proud of me. I asked for help when I needed it. I used my voice. I stayed calm. I finished my task.”

Sparrow fluffed his feathers. “You grew your garden, and you grew yourself.”

Dottie nuzzled a marigold gently. “Thank you, garden,” she said. “Thank you, friends.”

She took one more deep breath. Warm sun on her back. Cool tile under her hooves. Sweet plant smells all around. Dottie smiled. “I can do little jobs. Little jobs help me feel big inside.”

And from the railing, the garden seemed to whisper, “Yes, Dottie. You can.”

The End.
