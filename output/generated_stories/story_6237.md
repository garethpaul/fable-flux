# Rosie Rabbit's Messy Masterpiece

Rosie Rabbit loved to paint. Her fluffy white ears bounced as she hopped into Mama's home office, carrying her favorite paintbrush in her tiny paws.

"Can I paint here today, Mama?" Rosie asked, her pink nose twitching with excitement.

Mama looked up from her computer. "Of course, sweetie. But remember our painting rules."

Rosie nodded quickly. "Stay clean and tidy!" But she was already thinking about the beautiful rainbow she wanted to create.

Rosie spread her paper on the small table by the window. The morning sunlight made everything sparkle. She dipped her brush in bright red paint and began to swirl and swoosh across the paper.

"This is the most wonderful red!" she giggled, painting big circles.

But then... SPLAT! Red paint dripped on her white fur.

"Oops," Rosie whispered. She kept painting.

Next came sunny yellow paint. Rosie loved how it looked next to the red. She painted faster and faster, her little heart beating with joy.

SPLISH! Yellow paint splashed on her paws.

"Just a little mess," Rosie said to herself. The painting was looking so pretty!

Blue came next, then purple, then green. Rosie's brush danced across the paper like magic. Colors mixed and swirled together like a beautiful dream.

But soon, paint covered Rosie's ears. Paint dotted her whiskers. Paint stained her fluffy tail. She looked down at herself and gasped.

"Oh no!" Rosie's eyes filled with tears. "I'm all messy!"

Mama turned around and saw her little rabbit covered in rainbow colors.

"Rosie!" Mama said with surprise. "What happened?"

"I'm sorry, Mama," Rosie sniffled. "I forgot to stay clean. I was having so much fun painting that I didn't notice the mess."

Mama's face softened. She walked over and knelt beside Rosie. "Your painting is absolutely beautiful, sweetheart. But look at yourself."

Rosie looked at her reflection in Mama's computer screen. Paint covered her from ears to tail. She looked like a walking rainbow.

"I feel yucky," Rosie admitted. "And sticky. And my fur feels heavy."

"When we don't keep ourselves clean while creating art, it doesn't feel good, does it?" Mama asked gently.

Rosie shook her head. "My paws feel icky, and I can smell the paint in my fur."

"Let's get you cleaned up," Mama said with a warm smile. "Then I'll show you how to paint and stay clean at the same time."

In the bathroom, warm soapy water washed away all the paint. Rosie felt fresh and light again. Her white fur was fluffy and soft once more.

"Much better!" Rosie said, wiggling her clean whiskers.

Back in the office, Mama showed Rosie how to wear an old shirt while painting. She taught her to keep a wet cloth nearby for wiping her paws and to dip her brush carefully.

"Being curious and creative is wonderful," Mama explained. "But taking care of ourselves while we create makes everything more enjoyable."

Rosie started a new painting, this time wearing her special painting shirt. She wiped her paws often and kept her brush neat. The new painting was even more beautiful than the first one!

"I can paint AND stay clean!" Rosie announced proudly. "And it feels so much better!"

"I'm proud of you for learning," Mama said, giving Rosie a gentle hug. "And I forgive you for the mess. Everyone makes mistakes while they're learning."

Rosie beamed. Her new painting showed a happy rabbit family, all clean and colorful and bright.

The End.
