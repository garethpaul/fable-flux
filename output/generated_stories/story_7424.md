# Rocco Raccoon Finds Fair

Rocco was a musical raccoon with bright eyes and soft, stripey paws. He loved to tap rhythms and hum songs. His favorite place to make music was the cozy home office. It had a comfy chair, a sunny window, and a big wooden desk that smelled like pencils and paper. The keyboard clicked, the printer hummed, and the clock tick-tocked like a patient drum.

“Tap-tap-tap,” sang Rocco, drumming gently on the desk with two pencils. “Hum-hum-hum,” he sang along. The notes felt like sunshine in his chest. The home office was quiet but friendly. Rocco always asked permission before using things, because that was safe and fair.

On the desk sat a shiny, silver metronome that went tick… tock… tick… tock. It helped Rocco keep the beat. There was also a blue jar of colorful paper clips. They looked like tiny rainbows. Rocco loved how they sounded when they jiggled—clink, clink, clink!

Rocco’s friend, Miri the mouse, hurried in. Her whiskers twitched. “Rocco! I need the paper clips for Mama’s work,” she squeaked. “She has to mail papers today.”

Rocco looked at the blue jar. “I’m using them to make a little song,” he said. “Listen!” He shook the jar—clink-clink!—and tapped the pencils—tap-tap!

Miri stomped her tiny foot. “But I saw them first this morning,” she said. “I asked Mama, and she said I could use them. It’s not fair if you keep them.”

Rocco’s ears drooped. He wanted to be fair. He also wanted to finish his song. His tummy felt tight, like a knot in a string. He took a slow breath, like a soft “whoooo.” Self-care helps feelings soften, he remembered.

“Let’s be safe and calm,” Rocco said. “Let’s use our words.” He placed the jar on the desk. “I did not know you asked Mama. Thank you for telling me.”

The metronome ticked steadily. Tick… tock. The steady sound helped Rocco’s heart slow down. He hummed and thought. Justice means being fair to everyone, he remembered. It means we listen. It means we share what is right.

Miri rubbed her paws. “I feel worried,” she said. “Mama needs the clips now.”

Rocco nodded. “I hear you,” he said kindly. “Your need is important. My music is important too. How can we make it fair?”

They both looked around the home office. The window showed a little bird hopping on the sill, chirp-chirp! The printer gave a soft whirr. The room felt patient, like it was waiting for a good idea.

Rocco said, “What if we count the clips and share them?” He smiled. “Half for you, half for me. And when you’re done, you can bring them back.”

Miri’s whiskers wiggled. “That could work. But Mama needs a big bunch. Maybe I need more than half.”

Rocco thought again. “Justice means giving what is needed, not just the same amount,” he said, patting his chest. “If you need more for Mama’s work, you should get more.”

Miri’s eyes shone. “That sounds fair.”

Rocco poured the clips onto the desk. They made a cheerful clatter. Together they counted slowly, “One, two, three…” The colors made a little rainbow river. There were twenty clips. Rocco said, “How many do you need?”

Miri squeaked, “Fifteen, please.”

Rocco nodded. “You can have fifteen. I will keep five to finish my rhythm. When you’re done, bring back any extras. That is safe for the office and fair for everyone.”

Miri smiled wide. “Thank you, Rocco! I will hurry and be careful.” She scooped the clips into a small basket. The clips jingled softly, like bells. “I’ll tell Mama we shared in a just way.”

After Miri left, Rocco sat in the comfy chair. He noticed his shoulders felt tense. He remembered self-care. “First, breathe,” he told himself. He took three slow breaths—whoooo in, whoooo out. He sipped cool water from a cup and stretched his paws. He wiggled his toes and shook out his arms. “I am calm,” he whispered. The metronome ticked along like a friendly friend.

Rocco tried his song again with five clips. Clink-clink-clink… tap-tap… hum-hum. It sounded sweet and simple, like a gentle rain. He smiled. “Sometimes less is more,” he giggled.

Soon Miri came back, tail flicking happily. “Mama used twelve,” she said. “Here are three extra clips!” She placed them on the desk. “Thank you for being fair.”

Rocco beamed. “Thank you for telling me your need,” he said. “We solved our problem together. That is justice. We listened. We shared. We kept the home office safe and neat.”

They put the clips back in the jar and closed the lid with a soft click. They tidied the desk, pushed in the chair, and put the pencils away. Safety first!

“Want to play the metronome beat with me?” asked Rocco.

“Yes!” said Miri. They tapped softly so they didn’t disturb anyone. Tick… tock… tap… tap… hum… hum. Their music filled the room like warm cocoa.

Rocco felt light and proud. “Today I learned that justice is being fair and kind,” he said. “It means we listen and give what is needed. We use our words and keep each other safe.”

Miri nodded. “And we take care of ourselves, too,” she added. “Breathe, stretch, and drink water!”

They laughed together, paws and whiskers wiggling. The home office glowed in the afternoon sun. The problem was solved. The music played on.

The End.
