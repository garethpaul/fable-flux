# Daisy's Big Dinner Goal

Little Daisy the deer fawn stood on her tippy-hooves, peeking over the shiny wooden dining room table. Her brown eyes sparkled with excitement as she watched Mama Deer arrange colorful plates and cups.

"Mama, what are you doing?" Daisy asked, her soft voice filled with curiosity.

"We're having a special dinner tonight, sweetie," Mama explained, smoothing the pretty tablecloth. "Our new neighbors from around the world are coming to visit. There's Mrs. Chen from China, Mr. Patel from India, and the Rodriguez family from Mexico."

Daisy's ears perked up. "Can I help? I want to make everything perfect!"

Mama smiled warmly. "That's a wonderful goal, Daisy. But helping means being patient and careful. Can you do that?"

"Yes, yes!" Daisy nodded eagerly, her little tail wiggling.

Mama gave Daisy a special job: arranging the colorful napkins. Red ones from China, orange ones from India, and green ones from Mexico. Daisy felt so important as she carefully placed each napkin.

But then... CRASH! Daisy's hoof accidentally knocked over a beautiful blue vase. Water splashed everywhere, and pretty flowers scattered across the floor.

"Oh no!" Daisy cried, her eyes filling with tears. "I ruined everything! I'm sorry, Mama!"

Mama Deer rushed over and gently nuzzled Daisy's head. "Accidents happen, little one. The important thing is that you were trying to help."

"But my goal was to make everything perfect," Daisy sniffled.

"Sometimes our goals change, sweetheart," Mama said softly. "Your new goal can be to help clean up and try again."

Together, they mopped up the water and picked up the flowers. Daisy felt better when she saw Mama's proud smile.

Soon, the doorbell rang with a cheerful ding-dong! Their guests arrived, carrying wonderful smells and warm smiles.

Mrs. Chen brought dumplings that smelled like ginger and soy sauce. "In China, we share food to show friendship," she explained, teaching Daisy how to hold chopsticks.

Mr. Patel carried a pot of curry that made Daisy's nose tingle with spices. "This is my grandmother's recipe from India," he said kindly. "The yellow color comes from turmeric."

The Rodriguez family brought fresh tortillas that were warm and soft. "In Mexico, we make these by hand," little Sofia Rodriguez showed Daisy, letting her touch the smooth dough.

As everyone sat around the dining room table, Daisy noticed something wonderful. Even though the vase was gone, the table looked beautiful with all the different foods and happy faces.

"Daisy helped make this dinner special," Mama announced. "She set a goal to help, and even when things didn't go perfectly, she kept trying."

Mrs. Chen patted Daisy's head gently. "In my country, we say that trying your best is more important than being perfect."

Mr. Patel nodded. "And when we make mistakes, we learn and grow stronger."

Sofia giggled. "I break things all the time! But my mama always forgives me."

Daisy's heart felt warm and full. She had learned that goals could change, that mistakes were okay, and that friends from different places could teach her wonderful new things.

As they all shared the delicious meal, Daisy made a new goal: to always try her best and be kind to others, no matter where they came from.

"Thank you for forgiving me, Mama," Daisy whispered.

"Thank you for being helpful and brave, my little fawn," Mama whispered back.

The dining room filled with laughter, new friendships, and the sweet smell of foods from around the world.

The End.
