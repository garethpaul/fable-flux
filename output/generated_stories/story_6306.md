# Tilly Turtle's Quiet Song

Tilly was a shy turtle who loved quiet. She lived in a cozy bedroom with soft pillows, a warm blanket, and a little lamp shaped like a star. At night, the lamp made a gentle glow. It looked like honey light. It felt safe.

Tilly had a small blue drum and a tiny toy xylophone with rainbow bars. Ting, tang, ting! She liked to tap soft songs when no one was watching. The sound was sweet, like rain on leaves. But when others were near, Tilly tucked her head into her shell. Her cheeks got warm, and her tummy felt fluttery.

One cool evening, Tilly’s friend Benny Bunny came to play. His paws were fluffy, and his ears were long and kind. Benny sniffed the air. “It smells like warm cookies in here,” he said.

“Grandma baked them,” Tilly whispered. The room smelled like vanilla and cinnamon. It made Tilly smile.

Benny saw the blue drum. His eyes grew wide. “Can I try?” he asked.

Tilly’s shell felt tight. She loved her drum. It was her special thing. “Um… I don’t know,” she said softly. Her voice was tiny, like a leaf rustle.

Benny’s ears drooped. “It’s okay,” he said. “We can do something else.”

Tilly looked at Benny’s droopy ears. Her heart felt a little pinch. She wanted to be kind. She wanted to be brave. She looked at the drum again. She took a deep breath. In…and out. She put a hand on her shell and felt it slow down. “I feel nervous,” Tilly said. “I’m shy about my music. But I want to share.”

Benny smiled a small, careful smile. “We can go slow,” he said. “We can take turns.”

Tilly nodded. “Yes. We can practice sharing.”

She slid the drum over. Her hands shook a little. The star lamp made warm light on the drum top. Benny tapped the drum—pat, pat—very soft. “Is this okay?” he asked.

Tilly listened. Pat, pat. The sound was gentle, like a friendly knock. Her shoulders loosened. “That is kind tapping,” she said. “Now me?”

They took turns. Pat, pat, ting, tang. The cozy bedroom filled with soft beats and tiny chimes. The blanket felt extra warm. The pillow smelled like clean soap. The music felt safe and sweet.

“Can we try a song?” Benny asked.

Tilly blinked. “A song together?”

Benny nodded. “We can make a sharing song. You start. I will follow.”

Tilly tapped three slow pats. Pat…pat…pat. Benny echoed. Pat…pat…pat. Tilly smiled. “Now the xylophone.” She picked up the little mallet. Ting…ting…ting. The notes were like colorful drops. Benny listened, then tapped the drum quiet and steady.

Tilly felt brave. She peeked out fully from her shell. “I like this,” she said. “My tummy feels calm now.”

Benny’s ears stood tall. “Me too!”

Just then, Grandma Turtle peeked in. She wore soft slippers that went swish on the rug. “Oh my,” she whispered. “What beautiful music. It sounds like a bedtime hug.”

Tilly blushed, but she didn’t hide. “We are sharing and practicing,” she said proudly.

Grandma nodded. “I hear taking turns. I hear kind hearts.” She placed a plate of warm cookies on the bedside table. The cookies smelled buttery and sweet. “Would you like to share these, too?”

Tilly looked at Benny. Benny looked at Tilly. They both giggled. “Yes!” they said together.

Tilly broke a cookie in half. “Half for you, half for me,” she said. Benny did the same with his cookie. Crunch! The edges were crisp. The middle was soft. The taste was cozy and happy.

After their snack, Tilly picked up a small shaker. “Would you like to try this?” she asked.

Benny smiled. “Thank you for sharing.”

Tilly nodded. “I was scared at first. My feelings were big. But I took a breath. I said my feeling. Then we made a plan. Sharing felt better when we went slow and took turns.”

They played one more song. Pat, pat. Ting, tang. Shake, shake. Their music was like soft rain and tiny stars. It filled the cozy bedroom with a calm, friendly sound. Tilly felt the brave glow grow warm inside her.

When it was time for Benny to hop home, he waved at the door. “I learned something,” he said. “Sharing makes music more fun.”

Tilly hugged her drum. She didn’t hide at all. “I learned something too,” she said. “When I share and practice, my shy feeling gets smaller. My brave feeling gets bigger. And playing together feels wonderful.”

Grandma tucked Tilly into bed. The star lamp glowed. “Good night, little musician,” Grandma whispered. “You shared your music and your heart.”

Tilly smiled, eyes sleepy. “Sharing turns quiet songs into happy songs,” she murmured.

And in the cozy bedroom, everything felt soft, sweet, and kind.

The End.
