# Polly's Problem-Solving Adventure

Polly the parrot perched on the windowsill in Grandma's guest room. Her bright green feathers sparkled in the morning sunlight. She held a small pencil in her colorful beak.

"Oh no!" Polly squawked. She looked at the messy guest room. Toys were scattered everywhere. Books lay open on the floor. The trash can was tipped over.

Grandma walked in and gasped. "What happened here? My guests arrive this afternoon!"

Polly's heart felt heavy. She knew her little cousin Tommy had played here yesterday. But Tommy was too little to clean up properly.

"I need to think about this," Polly said to herself. She tapped her pencil on the window. "What would be fair? What would help?"

Polly closed her bright yellow eyes. She thought step by step. First, she needed to understand the problem. The room was messy. Grandma needed help. Tommy didn't know how to clean properly.

"I know!" Polly chirped happily. "I'll make a plan!"

Polly flew to the small desk. She spread out a piece of white paper. Carefully, she drew pictures with her pencil. First, she drew toys going into a box. Then she drew books going on the shelf. Last, she drew the trash going in the can.

"A cleaning map!" Polly said proudly. "This shows the right way to clean up."

Polly called to Tommy, who was playing in the hallway. "Tommy! Come see what I made!"

Tommy toddled into the room. His eyes grew wide when he saw Polly's drawings.

"Pictures!" Tommy clapped his small hands.

"Yes," Polly said gently. "These pictures show us how to clean up. Look, toys go here." She pointed with her wing. "Books go there. Trash goes in the can."

Tommy nodded seriously. "I help!"

Together, they followed Polly's plan. Tommy picked up each toy and looked at the picture. Then he put the toy in the right box. Polly helped by flying the lighter items to their proper places.

"One, two, three blocks in the box," Tommy counted happily.

"Good thinking!" Polly praised. "You're following the plan perfectly."

When they finished with the toys, they moved to the books. Polly's pictures helped Tommy remember where everything belonged.

"Heavy book," Tommy grunted as he lifted a picture book.

"I'll help," Polly said. She grabbed one corner with her beak. Together, they placed it on the shelf.

Finally, they cleaned up the trash. Tommy carefully put each piece in the can.

"All done!" Tommy cheered.

Grandma returned and smiled brightly. "Wow! How did you clean up so well?"

"Polly made pictures," Tommy explained. "Pictures show us how!"

Grandma looked at Polly's drawing. "What a smart idea, Polly! You thought through the problem and found a solution that helped everyone."

Polly felt warm and proud. "I used my thinking brain," she said. "First, I looked at the problem. Then I made a plan. Then we worked together!"

"That's called logical thinking," Grandma said. "You solved the problem step by step."

Tommy hugged Polly's soft feathers. "Thank you, Polly!"

From that day on, whenever there was a problem to solve, Polly would get her pencil and paper. She learned that thinking step by step and making plans helped everyone.

The guest room sparkled clean, and Polly felt happy knowing she had helped her family through careful thinking and teamwork.

The End.
