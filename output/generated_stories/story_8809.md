# Ellie's Colorful Choice

Little Ellie the elephant calf sat in the middle of her family's cozy living room, surrounded by soft cushions and bright toys. Her trunk swayed back and forth as she watched her big brother Max bounce a soccer ball against the wall.

"Come on, Ellie!" Max trumpeted excitedly. "Let's practice kicking! You need to get better at sports!"

Ellie's ears drooped a little. She loved her brother, but soccer balls felt too hard under her feet, and running around made her feel dizzy. Instead, her eyes sparkled as she spotted the box of colorful crayons sitting on the low coffee table.

"I want to draw," Ellie said softly, reaching for a bright red crayon with her trunk.

"Drawing is boring!" Max said, still bouncing his ball. "Sports are way more fun! Everyone loves soccer!"

Ellie felt her tummy get tight and worried. She picked up the red crayon and started making gentle circles on a piece of paper. The smooth feeling of the crayon moving across the paper made her feel calm and happy.

"Look, Max!" Ellie said, showing him her red swirls. "I made a flower!"

Max barely glanced over. "That's nice, but come play ball with me!"

Ellie's trunk touched the yellow crayon next. She loved how bright and sunny it looked. As she drew yellow petals around her red center, she felt warm and giggly inside.

"I really like making art," Ellie said, her voice a little stronger now. "It makes me feel good."

"But you should like sports instead," Max insisted. "All the cool elephants play soccer!"

Ellie put down her crayon and looked at her brother with her big, kind eyes. She took a deep breath and stood up tall on her four little legs.

"Max," she said clearly, "I love you, but I don't want to play soccer right now. I want to make beautiful pictures. Art makes me happy, and that's okay."

Max stopped bouncing his ball and looked surprised. "Really?"

"Really," Ellie nodded. "Watch this!" She picked up a blue crayon and added a bright blue sky above her flower. Then she grabbed the green crayon and drew wiggly grass at the bottom.

"Wow!" Max said, coming closer to look. "Those colors are really pretty, Ellie. How did you know what colors to use?"

Ellie's trunk wiggled with excitement. "I just feel it in my heart! The red feels warm like hugs, and the blue feels cool like water, and the green feels fresh like morning!"

Max sat down next to his little sister. "Can you teach me how to draw a flower too?"

"Of course!" Ellie trumpeted happily. She handed Max a purple crayon. "You can make your flower any color you want!"

As they drew together, Ellie felt proud and strong. She had used her voice to tell Max what she really wanted, and now they were both having fun.

"You know what, Ellie?" Max said as he finished his purple and orange flower. "You're really good at art. And it's okay that we like different things."

"And maybe sometimes I'll try soccer with you," Ellie said with a smile. "But right now, I love making art!"

Their mama elephant walked into the room and gasped with delight. "What beautiful artwork! You both should be proud of expressing yourselves."

Ellie beamed, knowing that being true to herself and speaking up for what she loved had made everything better.

The End.
