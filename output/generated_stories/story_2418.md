# Gigi Listens at the Table

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with big kind eyes and very long legs. She lived with her family in a sunny home. One morning, the smell of warm pancakes and sweet berries filled the dining room. The table looked bright with a yellow tablecloth, shiny spoons, and a little pitcher of syrup that sparkled like amber.

“Breakfast time!” called Mama Giraffe, her voice soft and happy.

Gigi trotted in, her hooves clicking on the floor. Her head reached the hanging lamp, and she ducked politely. “I’m so hungry!” she giggled, licking her lips.

Papa Giraffe smiled. “Let’s sit and listen to each other while we eat.”

Gigi loved to help. She stretched her long neck to pass plates. She reached, reached… and her tall neck bumped the syrup. The pitcher wobbled. It wiggled. Then—plop!—the syrup spilled, sticky and brown, across the tablecloth and onto baby brother Jax’s plate.

“Uh-oh,” whispered Gigi. Her tummy felt squiggly. Jax’s lip wobbled. “My pancake,” he sniffled.

Mama Giraffe took a gentle breath. “Accidents happen,” she said, dabbing the syrup with a soft cloth. “Gigi, what can you do now?”

Gigi remembered: When something goes wrong, I can make it right. She looked at Jax’s messy plate and felt a tiny tear in her eye. “I’m sorry, Jax,” she said in a clear, quiet voice. “I bumped the syrup.”

Jax looked up. “Sticky,” he murmured, touching his fingers together.

“Sticky indeed,” Papa said kindly. “Thank you for saying sorry, Gigi. What’s the next step?”

Gigi thought and thought. Being thoughtful meant listening, too. She bent down so she could look at Jax. “What would help you, Jax? Do you want a new pancake?”

Jax nodded. “New pancake, please.”

“I can fix it,” Gigi said. She stood tall and went slowly this time. Step, step—no rushing. She listened to the soft clink of plates, the quiet hum of the refrigerator, the little chirps of birds outside the window. She took a clean plate, slid a warm pancake onto it, and added three blueberries—one, two, three—just how Jax liked.

“Here you go,” she said, setting it in front of him. “Would you like a tiny bit of syrup? I’ll pour very slowly.”

Jax smiled a small smile. “Slow syrup,” he agreed.

Gigi held the pitcher with both hooves. She watched, listened, and counted. “Drip… drip… stop.” Jax clapped his little hooves. “Perfect!”

Mama Giraffe winked. “You are making amends, Gigi. That means you are fixing a hurt and making it better.”

Gigi felt warm inside. “I am sorry, and I am helping,” she said, repeating the words like a soft song.

They all began to eat. The pancakes were fluffy, and the berries were juicy and cool. The table smelled like cinnamon. Gigi chewed slowly. She listened when Papa shared a story about a cloud shaped like a boat. She listened when Mama asked, “Who wants orange slices?” She listened when Jax said, “Me! Me!” in his tiny happy voice.

After a few bites, Gigi remembered how she had bumped the syrup. She wanted to say more. “Mama,” she asked, “how can I keep the syrup safe next time?”

Mama smiled. “That is a smart question. Let’s make a little syrup spot.” She moved the pitcher to the middle of the table, far from the edge. “And we can take turns, one at a time, and use slow hands.”

Gigi placed a small napkin under the pitcher. “A cozy napkin bed,” she said. “Now the syrup won’t slide.”

Papa nodded. “You listened, you said sorry, and you helped. That is respect.”

Gigi’s ears perked. “Respect means caring about people and their things,” she said proudly.

Jax held up a blueberry like a tiny jewel. “Thank you, Gigi,” he said, and popped it into his mouth with a giggle.

Soon, the plates were almost empty. The dining room felt calm and bright. The sunlight made little squares on the floor. Gigi stood and collected the sticky napkins. She brought a bowl of warm soapy water to the table. The bubbles smelled like lemons and made soft pops. “I will clean the syrup spot,” she said.

“Teamwork,” said Mama, handing her a cloth.

Gigi wiped slowly. Swish, swish. She checked with her eyes and her hooves. Clean and smooth. She hummed a happy hum. Her tummy felt steady now, not squiggly at all.

When everything was tidy, Papa asked, “Gigi, what did you learn today?”

Gigi thought again, the way a thoughtful giraffe does. “I learned to stop and listen. I learned to say ‘I’m sorry’ when I make a mistake. And I learned to make amends by helping to fix the problem.”

Mama kissed Gigi’s forehead. “That is a beautiful lesson.”

Gigi smiled at Jax. “Next time, I will pour with slow hands and careful eyes.”

Jax tapped his spoon on the table—tap, tap. “And I will say, ‘Stop,’ if I need less syrup,” he added.

“We will all listen,” said Papa.

Gigi felt proud and peaceful. Breakfast ended with soft laughter, clean hands, and happy hearts. The dining room glowed in the morning light. Gigi hugged her family with her long, gentle neck.

She whispered, “When we listen and make amends, we make our home feel safe and sweet.”

And everyone agreed.

The End.
