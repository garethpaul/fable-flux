# Ellie’s Gentle Trunk and the Big Oops

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a small, wiggly trunk. Today, Ellie stepped into the creative art room. The room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Paints gleamed like bright rainbows. Brushes whispered, “Swish, swish,” from a cup.

“Good morning,” Ellie said. “I have a big goal today!”

“What’s your goal?” asked Ms. Fern, the kind art teacher with green apron strings.

“I want to make a Healthy Heroes poster,” Ellie said. “I will draw fruits and veggies, and I will use recycled paper. That is good for our Earth.”

Ms. Fern smiled. “That is a wonderful goal. It helps your tummy and our planet.”

Ellie set a piece of recycled paper on the table. It was smooth and a little crinkly. She picked up a brush. “Swish.” She painted a juicy red tomato. “Swish.” She painted a sunny banana. “Swish.” She painted a big leafy lettuce.

Her friend Tiko the turtle rolled in with his paint set. “Hi, Ellie,” he said. “Your poster looks yummy!”

Ellie giggled. “You cannot eat the paint, Tiko, but we can eat real fruits and veggies later.”

Tiko nodded. “Snack time will be so tasty.”

Ellie hummed a happy tune. Her trunk swayed. She reached for blue paint to draw a big water droplet. “Water is good for us,” she said. She whispered to the paper, “I can do it. I can meet my goal.”

Then it happened.

Bump!

Tiko’s paint jar tipped. “Oh no!” he cried.

Splish-splash! Purple paint spilled onto Ellie’s poster. It made a big puddle star. Purple ran over the tomato, the banana, and the lettuce. The paper felt cold and wet.

Ellie froze. Her ears drooped. Her belly felt tight. “My goal,” she whispered. “My poster.”

Tiko looked small inside his shell. “I am so, so sorry,” he said. “I was careless. I didn’t keep my jar steady.”

Ellie stared at the purple puddle. Her trunk trembled. She wanted to shout, but her heart felt heavy and sad. She took a slow breath. She could smell the sweet paint and hear the soft tick-tick of the wall clock.

Ms. Fern came close. “This is a problem,” she said softly. “What can we do to solve it?”

Ellie looked at Tiko. His eyes were shiny. He blinked. “I can help clean,” he whispered. “Please forgive me.”

Ellie thought about her goal. She thought about being kind to the Earth. She thought about being kind to friends, too.

She took another slow breath. In… and out. The tight feeling began to melt like ice in the sun.

“I forgive you, Tiko,” Ellie said gently. “Accidents happen. Let’s fix it together.”

Tiko’s shell seemed to glow. “Thank you,” he said. “I will be very careful now.”

Ms. Fern handed them cloths made from old shirts. “These are reused rags,” she said. “Reusing helps our planet.”

Together, they dabbed. “Pat, pat, pat,” went the cloths. The purple puddle became a soft cloud.

Ellie had an idea. “We can make the purple part into grapes!” she said. “Grapes are healthy, too.”

Tiko grinned. “And we can add a little sign that says, ‘Snack smart. Be kind to the Earth.’”

Ellie’s trunk swished with joy. “Yes! And we can glue the scraps into a frame so we do not waste paper.”

They gathered colorful scraps from the recycle bin. Some were bumpy. Some were smooth. They felt crunchy-dry under Ellie’s toes. Snip, snip went the safety scissors. Glue smelled a little sweet. They made a patchwork frame around the poster.

Ellie painted little grapes from the purple cloud. She added carrots, peas, and a water bottle with a tiny leaf to show it could be used again. Tiko drew a sunshine with smiling rays.

Ms. Fern clapped softly. “You solved the problem with calm hearts and bright ideas. You kept your goal and cared for our Earth.”

Ellie nodded. “My new goal is to forgive fast and fix problems with friends.”

Tiko raised a brush. “And my goal is to move slowly and keep lids on tight!”

They laughed. Their laughter sounded like bells. “Ding-ding!”

When the class gathered for snack, Ellie passed around apple slices and crunchy carrots. “Let’s eat colors,” she said. “Red apples, orange carrots, green cucumbers!”

Tiko poured water from a big reusable jug into little cups. “Sip, sip,” went the water. It felt cool and fresh.

Ellie looked at the finished poster on the wall. The grapes sparkled. The scraps made a bright frame. At the bottom, in big friendly letters, it said: “Eat well. Forgive well. Care for our Earth.”

Tiko tapped his shell. “Thank you for forgiving me, Ellie.”

Ellie wrapped her gentle trunk around him in a soft hug. “Friends make mistakes,” she said. “Forgiveness helps hearts feel light. Goals help us grow. Caring for our Earth helps everyone.”

Ms. Fern smiled. “That is a beautiful lesson.”

Ellie’s ears wiggled with pride. She felt calm and happy. The art room smelled like crayons and apples. The sunlight felt warm on her back. The clock went tick-tick, and Ellie hummed again.

She had met her goal—with a kind heart and a gentle trunk.

The End.
