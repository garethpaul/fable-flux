# Melody's Big Choice

High up on a red brick rooftop, a little yellow bird named Melody loved to sing. Every morning, she would flutter her bright feathers and chirp the most beautiful songs. "Tweet-tweet-trill!" she sang, watching the sun paint the sky orange and pink.

Melody felt so happy when she sang that sometimes she forgot about everything else. She would sing louder and LOUDER until her voice echoed across all the rooftops in the neighborhood.

One sunny morning, Melody was singing her favorite song when she heard a tiny voice below. "Please... could you sing a little quieter?" squeaked a small voice.

Melody looked down and saw Mrs. Mouse peeking out from a crack in the roof tiles. Her whiskers were twitching, and she looked very tired.

"Oh! Hello, Mrs. Mouse," chirped Melody. "Why do you look so sleepy?"

Mrs. Mouse rubbed her tiny eyes. "My baby mice were up all night because your singing was so loud yesterday. They couldn't sleep, and now they're cranky and tired."

Melody's heart felt heavy. She loved singing more than anything, but she didn't want to hurt her neighbors. "I'm sorry, Mrs. Mouse. I didn't know my singing was keeping your babies awake."

"It's okay," said Mrs. Mouse gently. "Your voice is beautiful, but maybe you could sing a little softer in the early morning and evening when little ones are sleeping?"

Melody thought hard. She could feel her excitement bubbling up inside her like fizzy bubbles. She REALLY wanted to sing her loudest, most joyful song right now. But she also wanted to be a good neighbor.

"I need to think about this," Melody said to herself. She took three deep breaths, just like her mama had taught her. "Breathe in... breathe out... breathe in... breathe out."

As she breathed, Melody felt the fizzy feeling in her chest calm down. She thought about how sad the baby mice must feel when they're too tired to play. She thought about how Mrs. Mouse was being so kind even though she was tired too.

"You know what, Mrs. Mouse?" Melody said with a warm smile. "I can sing softly during quiet times. That's what good neighbors do!"

Mrs. Mouse's whiskers twitched happily. "Thank you, Melody! That would be wonderful."

From that day on, Melody learned to control her excitement. In the early morning and evening, she sang gentle lullabies that sounded like a soft breeze. "Tweet-tweet-whisper," she would sing quietly.

During the day, when everyone was awake, she sang her big, joyful songs. "TWEET-TWEET-TRILL!" The whole neighborhood loved her music then.

Mrs. Mouse's babies grew strong and healthy with plenty of sleep. They would even dance to Melody's daytime concerts! Other birds on nearby rooftops started copying Melody's kindness too.

"I feel even happier now," Melody realized one evening as she sang a soft goodnight song. "When I think about others and control my excitement, everyone feels good!"

As the stars twinkled above the rooftop, Melody tucked her head under her wing. She had learned that being responsible and thinking of others made her heart feel as warm as sunshine.

The End.
