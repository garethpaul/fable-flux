# Joey Roo and the Garden Shed Surprise

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with a bouncy tail and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, Joey hopped to the garden shed behind the house. The shed smelled like fresh wood and clean soil. Little sunbeams danced across shelves full of boxes, pots, and tools.

“Hello, shed,” Joey whispered. “Let’s see what we can do today!”

Joey peeked inside a basket. It held old jars, ribbon pieces, and buttons. On a high shelf sat a small watering can with a dent. A soft humming came from the window where bees buzzed around flowers. Joey felt happy and calm. He placed a paw on his chest and took a slow breath.

“In… and out,” Joey said gently. “I will look, listen, and be kind to things.”

Joey’s friend Pip the pigeon fluttered to the window and cooed, “Hello, Joey! What are you making?”

“I don’t know yet,” Joey said, smiling. “I want to help our garden. But I will use what we already have. That is being resourceful.”

Pip tilted her head. “Resource-ful?”

“It means I use my brain and the things around me,” Joey explained. “I don’t toss things away. I find new jobs for them!”

Pip clapped her wings. “Coo! Let’s try!”

Joey found a cracked pot. He tapped it. “Tap, tap.” It made a funny sound. Inside the pot were tiny stones. Joey listened to the gentle clink. He smiled. “This pot can become a home for seeds!”

“But it’s cracked,” Pip said.

“That’s okay,” Joey said. “We can line it with an old jar lid to hold soil.”

They worked together. Joey placed the jar lid inside the pot. Pip brought dry leaves for the bottom. The shed smelled leafy and warm. Joey scooped soil with the small dented watering can. It made a soft “shhh” sound as it poured. Joey giggled. “It still works!”

Pip nodded. “We are using what we have. That is good for the Earth.”

Joey found a short wooden stick and a ribbon piece. “Let’s make a plant sign,” he said. He tied the ribbon on the stick and drew a tiny sun with a piece of charcoal from the grill. “This sign can tell us to give the plant sunshine and love.”

Pip cooed, “What about water?”

Joey looked at the corner. There was a big bucket under a pipe. Drip, drip, drip. Rainwater from last night gathered in the bucket. “We can use rainwater,” Joey said. “That saves water from the tap.”

Pip looked shy. “Can I pour first?”

Joey smiled kindly. “Let’s take turns. You pour first, and I will count.”

Pip lifted the dented watering can and poured. “One, two,” Joey counted softly. The soil looked dark and rich. Then Joey took his turn and poured just a little. “Three.” They nodded. “Just enough,” Joey said. “Not too much. That is mindful.”

From a tiny tin, Joey found seeds. “These are from our last pumpkin,” he said. “We saved them. We can plant them now.”

Pip puffed with pride. “We are gardeners!”

They poked little holes with their paws and beak. The soil felt cool and crumbly. “Hello, seeds,” Joey whispered. “We will care for you.”

On a shelf was a box of buttons. Joey picked four bright ones. “These can be decorations for the pot,” he said. They glued the buttons around the rim, making a smiley face. The pot looked cheerful.

Just then, Mia the mouse peeped in. “Squeak! I need a tiny bed for my baby mice. Do you have something soft?”

Joey looked around. He saw a basket with old fabric scraps. He thought, then grinned. “We can make a cozy nest from these. No need to buy new.”

They shredded the soft fabric into gentle strips. It felt fluffy and warm. Mia sniffed it and smiled. “It smells like lavender!” she said. “Thank you.”

Pip looked at the messy table. “We should clean up.”

Joey nodded. “Yes. We can sort things.” They placed paper in one box, plastic in another, and metal in a third. “We recycle to help the whole world,” Joey said. “That is being a good neighbor to everyone, everywhere.”

They took slow breaths again. “In… and out,” Joey said, feeling calm and proud.

Outside, the sun glowed. Bees hummed happily. Joey placed the pot on the sunny step. “We will check it each day,” he said. “We will share the pouring and take turns watching.”

Mia waved. “You used old things and made new treasures,” she said.

Joey beamed. “We were resourceful! We helped the garden, saved water, and cleaned up. We were mindful too.”

Pip cooed, “And we shared!”

Joey hugged his friends gently. “Today we learned: Use what you have, take care of the Earth, breathe and think, and take turns. Little choices help our big world.”

They all watched the pot. A tiny hope grew in their hearts, like a seed in the soil.

“Good job, team,” Joey whispered.

The End.
