# Koala Kiko’s Quiet Concert

Kiko was a calm koala with soft gray fur and gentle eyes. One sunny afternoon, Kiko arrived at Auntie Roo’s house. “Welcome, Kiko,” said Auntie Roo, smiling. “Your room is the guest room. It has a big window and a comfy chair.”

Kiko padded in. The guest room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. The curtains were light and airy. A small plant sat on the windowsill. “Thank you, Auntie,” Kiko said. “It feels cozy.”

Kiko loved music. He had a small blue ukulele. The strings went twang, twang, twang. He also had little clappy sticks that went click, click. Kiko wanted to play a song to say thank you.

Soon, Auntie Roo’s neighbor, Baby Joey, came over with his mama. Baby Joey was a sleepy kangaroo. He rubbed his eyes and yawned. “Shh,” whispered Mama Kangaroo. “Baby Joey needs a nap.”

Kiko’s ears perked up. He looked at his ukulele. He felt excited, but he also felt unsure. “I want to play music,” Kiko said softly. “But I want to be kind.”

Auntie Roo patted Kiko’s paw. “Respect means we think about others,” she said. “We can always ask first.”

Kiko nodded. He walked to Mama Kangaroo. “Excuse me,” he said in a calm voice. “Is it okay if I play a little music in the guest room?”

Mama Kangaroo smiled. “Thank you for asking,” she said. “Baby Joey needs quiet right now. But gentle music might help him sleep.”

Kiko’s heart felt warm. “I can play soft,” he said. He held his ukulele and plucked very gently. Twiiing… twiiing… The sound was light and sweet, like a tiny bell.

Baby Joey’s ears wiggled. He snuggled into his mama’s pouch. “Hmm,” he hummed. His eyes started to close.

Kiko kept his strums slow. He swayed a little. The curtains swished in the soft breeze. The plant leaves whispered. The guest room felt peaceful.

Then Kiko remembered his clappy sticks. He picked them up and tapped. Click… click… click! The sound was a bit sharp. Baby Joey blinked and sat up. His nose twitched. “Oh,” Kiko said. “That was not soft.”

Auntie Roo peeked in. “Oops,” she said kindly. “That sound is a little loud.”

Kiko took a deep breath. He felt a tiny pinch in his tummy. “I made a mistake,” he whispered. “I want to fix it.” He put the clappy sticks down. “I’m sorry, Baby Joey,” Kiko said. “I will play more gently.”

Mama Kangaroo nodded. “Thank you for your respect,” she said. “Let’s try again.”

Kiko looked around the guest room. He had an idea. He put a soft scarf over the ukulele body. He strummed. Twuumm… twuumm… The scarf made the sound cozy and quiet. Kiko smiled. “A peaceful solution,” he said.

Baby Joey’s eyelids drooped. He let out a happy sigh. Kiko hummed, “La, la, laaa,” like a soft cloud. He played a slow, sleepy tune. The room smelled sweet and felt warm. The breeze was gentle. The only sounds were Kiko’s soft strums and Baby Joey’s tiny snores. Zzz…

Mama Kangaroo tiptoed to the door. “Thank you, Kiko,” she whispered. “Your music is kind.”

Kiko felt grateful. “Thank you for telling me what you need,” he whispered back. “I like to help.”

After Baby Joey fell asleep, Kiko set his ukulele down and rubbed his paws together. He wrote a small thank-you note on a card. It said, “Thank you, Auntie, for the lovely guest room. Love, Kiko.” He drew a little heart and a music note.

When Baby Joey woke up, he stretched and smiled. “Nap good,” he said. Kiko grinned. “Would you like a happy song now?” he asked. “We can clap softly.”

Mama Kangaroo said, “Yes! Now it’s playtime.”

Kiko checked with Auntie Roo. “Is it a good time for music, Auntie?”

Auntie Roo nodded. “It is a very good time.”

Kiko strummed a cheerful tune. Twang-twang! He tapped the clappy sticks—softly. Click, click. Baby Joey clapped, pat, pat. Everyone smiled. The guest room was full of joy and respect.

When the song ended, Kiko bowed. “Thank you for listening,” he said. “I am grateful we found a quiet way and a fun way.”

Auntie Roo hugged Kiko. “You showed respect,” she said. “You asked first. You listened. You found a peaceful solution.”

Kiko hugged back. His calm heart felt big and bright. “Music can be kind,” he said. “Respect makes everyone feel safe and happy.”

That night, Kiko tucked into the soft sheets. The moon made silver shapes on the floor. He whispered, “Good night, guest room. Thank you.” He felt thankful, peaceful, and proud.

The End.
