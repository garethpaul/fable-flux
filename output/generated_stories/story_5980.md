# Pebble the Penguin Finds Their Voice

Pebble the penguin loved their cozy bedroom. The blanket felt soft like clouds. The night-light glowed warm and yellow, like a tiny moon. Pebble’s favorite music box sat on the shelf. When it played, it sounded like tinkly snowflakes.

“Good morning, room!” Pebble chirped. Pebble wiggled their little wings and did a happy penguin shuffle on the rug. “Waddle, slide, spin!” Pebble giggled. Tap-tap-tap went Pebble’s feet on the floor.

Mama Penguin peeked in. “Good morning, Pebble. Time to brush your beak and wash your flippers.”

Pebble sniffed the minty toothpaste. “Mmm! It smells like cool winter.” Pebble squeezed a little bit—squish!—and brushed in tiny circles. “Swish, swish,” sang Pebble. Water splashed, and the soap made tiny bubbles that tickled.

After washing up, Pebble looked in the mirror. “Clean beak, clean flippers! I feel fresh and brave.”

Today was Dance-at-Home Day in Pebble’s cozy bedroom. A soft rug made a perfect dance floor. Mama set the music box to a gentle beat. “We’ll practice the Snowy Shuffle,” Mama said. “I’ll show, then you try.”

Pebble loved to dance. Pebble also loved to choose. Sometimes Pebble liked slow spins. Sometimes Pebble liked big jumps. Pebble’s tummy fluttered with excitement.

Mama danced first. “Step, step, slide,” she said. Pebble tried. “Step, step—oops!” Pebble slipped and bumped a pillow. “I feel wobbly,” Pebble whispered.

Mama smiled. “It’s okay. Try again?”

Pebble took a deep breath. The air smelled like clean sheets and a little bit like soap. “Mama, can I tell you what I need?” Pebble asked.

“Yes, Pebble,” Mama said. “Use your strong, kind voice.”

Pebble put a flipper on their chest. “I need a slower song,” Pebble said. “And I need you to show me one small part.”

“That is great self-advocacy,” Mama said. “You said what you need.”

Mama turned the music box to a slower tune. “Let’s try just the step,” she said.

Pebble smiled. “Thank you.” Pebble stepped. “Step.” Pebble stepped again. “Step.” Then Pebble slid. “Slide!” Pebble’s feet made a soft shhh sound on the rug. Pebble felt proud and warm inside.

Soon, Pebble wanted to add a spin. “Mama, I want to try a spin now,” Pebble said. “But I need more space.”

Mama moved a basket of toys. “You asked for space. That keeps your body safe.”

Pebble spun—whee! The room blurred like snowy swirls. Pebble laughed and clapped their flippers.

Just then, Pebble’s cousin, Poppy, waddled in. “I want a turn!” Poppy said. Poppy ran to the music box and turned it up—too fast and too loud. The music went boom-boom-boom!

Pebble covered their ears. The sound felt prickly. Pebble’s tummy felt tight. Pebble looked at Poppy and took a calm breath. “Poppy,” Pebble said, “the music is too loud for me. I need it softer.” Pebble used a strong, kind voice.

Poppy blinked. “Oh.” Poppy’s cheeks looked sorry. “I didn’t know. I was excited.”

“It’s okay,” Pebble said. “Please turn it down.”

Poppy turned the knob. The music became soft again. Pebble dropped their flippers from their ears and smiled. “Thank you for listening.”

Poppy shuffled a little. “Can I dance too? I want to do big jumps.”

Pebble looked at the cozy bedroom. “We can share the rug,” Pebble said. “But I need a corner for my spins. Let’s make dance spots.”

Mama put two pillows on the rug. “Pebble’s spot. Poppy’s spot,” Mama said.

Pebble nodded. “That works for me.” Pebble felt brave for speaking up.

They danced together. Pebble did slow spins and slides. Poppy did big jumps like popcorn. The music chimed like tiny bells. The rug felt snug and warm under their feet. They laughed. “We are a good team!”

Then—whoop! Poppy’s jump landed on Pebble’s flipper. “Ow!” Pebble cried. It hurt a little.

Poppy’s eyes grew wide. “I’m sorry,” Poppy said. “I didn’t see your flipper.”

Pebble held their flipper. Pebble took a breath. In…out. “Thank you for saying sorry,” Pebble said. “I forgive you. Next time, please look at my flippers. I need safe feet.”

Poppy nodded. “I will.” Poppy moved back to their pillow spot.

After dancing, Pebble felt sticky and warm. “I need to wash,” Pebble told Mama. “My body feels sweaty.”

“That is good self-advocacy,” Mama said. “You noticed what your body needs.”

Pebble washed their flippers with gentle soap. Bubbles popped like tiny kisses. Pebble wiped their beak with a soft towel. “Clean again!” Pebble cheered. Poppy washed too. The minty smell made them smile.

Back in the cozy bedroom, Pebble cuddled under the blanket with Poppy. The music box played a quiet lullaby. Mama hugged them both.

Pebble said, “Today I learned to use my strong, kind voice. I can say what I need. I can listen to others. I can dance and keep clean and be safe.”

Poppy said, “And I learned to listen and say sorry. Thank you for forgiving me.”

Pebble wiggled their feet. “My body feels calm. My heart feels brave.”

Mama nodded. “That’s the power of self-advocacy, care, and kindness.”

Pebble smiled. “Tomorrow, we can dance again. I will ask for what I need.”

The room felt warm and happy. The night-light glowed like a tiny moon. The cozy bedroom was full of soft whispers and gentle breaths.

Pebble’s last thought before sleep was simple and strong: “My voice matters.”

The End.
