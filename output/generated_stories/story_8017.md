# Polly's Perfect Plan

Polly the parrot loved colors and creativity. Her bright green feathers sparkled in the morning sunlight that streamed through the dining room window. She lived with the Johnson family, and every morning she watched them eat breakfast at the big wooden table.

"Good morning, Polly!" said little Emma, munching on her cereal. The spoon clinked against the bowl with a cheerful sound.

"Good morning! Good morning!" Polly squawked happily from her perch near the window.

But Polly noticed something that made her colorful feathers droop. After breakfast, the family always left their dishes on the table. Crumbs scattered everywhere like tiny white snowflakes. Sticky jam spots dotted the smooth wood surface. The dining room looked messy and sad.

"I wish I could help clean up," Polly sighed, looking at her small wings. "But I'm just a bird. I can't carry heavy plates or wipe the table."

Day after day, Polly watched the mess grow bigger. She felt smaller and smaller. Her confidence flew away like a leaf in the wind.

One sunny Tuesday, Polly had a wonderful idea! Her eyes sparkled like bright buttons.

"I may be small, but I'm creative!" she chirped to herself. "Maybe I can help in my own special way!"

When the family finished breakfast and rushed off to school and work, Polly flew down to the table. She couldn't lift the heavy plates, but she could do something else.

First, she picked up all the napkins in her beak and dropped them in a neat pile. The soft paper rustled as she worked. Then, she used her sharp eyes to spot every single crumb. One by one, she pecked them up with her orange beak.

"Peck, peck, peck!" she sang as she worked. The rhythm made the chore feel like a fun game.

Next came Polly's most creative idea. She flew to the kitchen and found colorful cloth napkins. Carefully, she arranged them in a beautiful rainbow pattern across the clean table. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue – each color bright and cheerful.

When the Johnson family came home, they gasped in surprise.

"Wow!" exclaimed Emma. "Look how pretty our dining room looks!"

"Did you do this, Polly?" asked Mom, her voice full of wonder.

Polly puffed up her chest with pride. "Yes! Yes! Polly helped!" she squawked joyfully.

Dad smiled warmly. "Polly, you found such a creative way to help our family. Thank you!"

From that day on, the Johnsons and Polly worked together as a team. The family learned to put their dishes in the dishwasher right after eating. Polly continued her special job of arranging the colorful napkins and keeping watch for any missed crumbs.

"We all have different talents," Mom explained to Emma. "Polly showed us that even small helpers can make a big difference."

Polly's confidence soared higher than ever. She learned that being small didn't mean being helpless. By using her creativity and finding her own special way to contribute, she became an important part of the family team.

Every morning, the dining room sparkled clean and bright, decorated with Polly's rainbow napkin arrangement. And every morning, Polly felt proud knowing she had found her perfect way to help.

The End.
