# Ollie Otter’s Balcony Garden Parade

Ollie was a playful otter who loved to splash in a blue bucket on the balcony garden. The balcony was bright and sunny. Little pots sat in neat rows. Basil smelled sweet. Mint smelled cool. Tiny tomatoes hung like red beads.

“Good morning, plants!” Ollie chirped. He wiggled his whiskers and patted the soil. “I’m your helper today.”

Ollie wore a small green apron. It felt soft on his fur. The sun felt warm on his nose. The breeze felt tickly on his ears. He picked up his little watering can. It made a gentle slosh-slosh sound.

“Sip, sip,” Ollie said, pouring carefully. “Not too much. Not too little.” He watched the water sparkle. He watched the soil turn dark and cozy. He smiled. “I can do it myself.”

A pigeon landed on the railing with a flutter-flap. “Coo-hello,” she said. “I’m Pippa.”

“Hi, Pippa!” said Ollie. “I’m watering my plants.”

Pippa tilted her head. “What are those?”

Ollie sniffed the mint. “This is mint. Smell!” Pippa leaned in. “Mmm. Fresh!” she cooed.

Next, a tiny ladybug crawled onto Ollie’s paw. “I’m Lala,” said the ladybug in a tiny voice. “I like these leaves.”

“Hi, Lala!” Ollie giggled. “You can help the plants too.”

Ollie set out little cups of water for the birds and bugs. He wiped his paws on his apron. “I can share,” he said.

A voice came from the next balcony. “Hola!” A small boy waved. “I’m Nico!”

Ollie waved back. “Hi, Nico! I’m Ollie.”

Nico held up a pot with a green sprout. “I’m growing cilantro,” he said. “It smells like my grandma’s soup.”

Ollie’s eyes grew wide. “I have basil and mint and tomatoes,” he said proudly. “We are balcony neighbors!”

Nico smiled. “We are garden friends.”

Ollie felt a warm, happy thump-thump in his chest. He looked around. Above him, the sky was big and blue. Below, the street hummed with faraway sounds. On one side, he heard Nico. On the other side, he heard a gentle laugh.

“Namaste,” said a girl with a braid. She leaned over her railing. “I’m Asha. I am growing turmeric.” She held up a golden root. “It makes warm milk at night.”

“Wow,” Ollie said. “Our gardens are different and the same!”

Asha nodded. “We all help our plants.”

Pippa flapped her wings. “I fly over many balconies,” she said. “I see peppers, lemons, and beans. So many plants. So many helpers.”

Ollie’s whiskers wiggled. “That is like a big team,” he said. “A world team!”

Lala the ladybug tickled his paw. “We all share the sun. We all need water. We all take care. That is being good neighbors.”

Ollie thought hard. He set his watering can down. “I can learn and help,” he said softly. “I can be part of the world team.”

He had an idea. “Let’s have a Balcony Garden Parade!” he squeaked. “We can share smells and tastes. We can learn words.”

Nico clapped. “Sí!” Asha smiled. “Haan, yes!”

Ollie made little signs from paper. He drew a leaf, a drop of water, and a happy sun. He taped a sign to each pot with gentle paws. He hummed a bouncy tune. “Step, step, sniff, and share.”

First, Ollie sniffed basil. “Sweet,” he said. “In my language, I say yummy-yum!” He giggled.

Nico held up cilantro. “Cilantro,” he said. “In Spanish, we say cilantro.” He breathed in. “¡Delicioso!”

Asha crushed mint between her fingers. The scent floated cool and bright. “In Hindi, mint is pudina,” she said. “Poo-dee-na.”

“Ooo,” Ollie said. He tried the word slowly. “Poo-dee-na.” He grinned. “I am learning.”

Pippa dipped her beak in a water cup. “Hydrate,” she cooed. “Healthy habit!”

Ollie nodded. “Drink water. Eat greens. Move your body.” He did a little otter wiggle. “These are good for me.”

Lala crawled onto a tomato leaf. “Gentle hands,” she whispered. “We care for small friends.”

Ollie looked at his watering can, his signs, and his tidy pots. “I can do many things by myself,” he said. “I can also ask and share.”

He poured a little water for Asha’s turmeric. “Here you go.”

Asha smiled. “Dhanyavaad. Thank you.”

Nico passed a tiny pinch of cilantro to Ollie. “For your lunch,” he said.

Ollie passed a basil leaf back. “For your soup,” he said.

They all took a slow breath together. The air smelled green and bright. The sun made the leaves shine. A soft breeze brushed their cheeks.

Ollie looked at the big blue sky again. “We are small,” he said, “but we are part of a big world. We can help it grow.”

Pippa spread her wings. “You are a good global neighbor, Ollie.”

Ollie hugged his watering can. His heart felt full. “Today I learned new words. I shared water. I cared for plants. I did my best.”

He waved to Nico and Asha. “See you tomorrow!”

“Hasta mañana!” called Nico.

“Kal milte hain!” called Asha.

Ollie giggled. “Tomorrow, we learn more.”

He tucked his tools away. He washed his paws with warm, soapy water. The bubbles smelled like oranges. He ate a tiny tomato with a happy crunch. “Healthy habits,” he said with a smile.

Ollie looked at his garden, his friends, and the open sky. “We all belong,” he whispered. “We all help.”

The End.
