# Panda Paws in the Car

Poppy the panda was a patient panda. She had soft black ears, a gentle smile, and a small backpack with a snack and a book. Today, Poppy climbed into the family car with Mama Panda and Baby Bro, Pip.

The car smelled like oranges from Mama’s hand wipes. The seats were warm from the sun. Poppy buckled her seat belt. Click! Pip’s seat belt went click-click! The engine hummed. Vroom, vroom, purrr.

“Today we help at the park,” said Mama Panda. “We will pick up tiny pieces of trash. That is community service. We care for our home.”

Poppy nodded. “We can do it together.”

Pip wiggled his feet. “I want the window!” he squeaked.

Poppy looked at the little window button. She wanted to feel the breeze too. She took a slow breath. In, hold, out. She remembered her calm routine.

“Let’s use peaceful words,” Poppy said softly. “We can share.”

Pip crossed his little arms. His face scrunched. “I want it now.”

Poppy felt a tingle in her tummy. She wanted to press the button. But she touched her seat belt and waited. “Pip,” she said, “I hear you. You want wind. Me too. How about we take turns? You can have the window on the counting of ten. Then I can have it on the next ten.”

Mama Panda glanced in the mirror. “I like how you are speaking kindly, Poppy.”

Pip peeped, “Ten is big.”

Poppy smiled. “I can count with you. One… two… three…” Her voice was calm like a little song.

Pip listened. His face relaxed. He held up his tiny paws and counted too. “Four… five… six…”

Wind came in like a whisper when Pip pressed the button. Whoosh! It smelled like trees and morning. The air kissed their fur. Pip giggled. “Wind is tickly!”

When they reached ten, Poppy said, “Now it’s my turn, please.”

Pip pressed the button again. “Okay, Poppy.” He smiled, proud. The window moved a little for Poppy. She felt the breeze on her nose. It felt cool and kind. She said, “Thank you, Pip.”

The car rolled to a stop at a red light. Beep! Another car made a loud honk. A baby in that car started to cry. The sound was sharp, like a whistle.

Pip’s eyes grew wide. “Loud,” he whispered.

Poppy nodded. “Yes, loud can feel scary.” She took his paw. “We can breathe together.” They took three slow breaths. In, hold, out. In, hold, out. In, hold, out. The red light changed to green. The cars moved on. The street smelled like rain on warm road.

“Peaceful-communication helps our bodies too,” Mama Panda said. “Thank you for helping each other.”

Soon they were at the park. The car door made a thump. Birds chirped, chip-chip! Leaves rustled. The sun felt soft, like a hug on their backs. Mama gave them little gloves and small bags. “We are helpers today.”

Poppy saw a little fox family near the swings. One fox kit held a sign that said, “Thank you, helpers!” Poppy waved. The fox kit waved back with a happy wiggle.

They began their routine: look, pick, drop in the bag. Poppy sang a quiet song. “Pick and plop, pick and plop, we love our park, we never stop.” Pip copied her. “Pick an’ pop!” His giggle was bubbly.

Near the slide, Pip saw a shiny candy wrapper. Two squirrel friends reached for it too. Their paws bumped. “Mine!” squeaked one squirrel.

Poppy walked over slow and kind. “Hi, friends. We can share the job. We can take turns. One can pick it up. The other can hold the bag.”

The squirrels blinked. One squirrel’s tail flicked fast. The other squirrel’s ears drooped. Poppy added, “I hear you both want to help. Let’s use peaceful words.”

The fast-tail squirrel said, “I want to pick.”

The droopy-ear squirrel said, “I want to help too.”

Poppy smiled. “You can both help. Pick, then drop. Teamwork!”

The squirrels nodded. Pick. Drop. Crinkle! Into the bag it went. They both grinned. “We did it!” they chirped together.

Mama Panda clapped softly. “Good guiding, Poppy.”

After filling their bags, they washed paws at the park sink. The water was cool and splishy-splashy. Poppy felt proud. Her chest felt warm and light.

Back in the family car, Pip yawned a big, squeaky yawn. “I am sleepy.”

Poppy buckled up. Click. “Me too. We worked as a team.”

As they drove home, the sky turned pink like cotton candy. The car hummed. Vroom, purrr. Poppy looked at Pip. “Thank you for taking turns with the window.”

Pip smiled, sleepy. “Thank you for counting with me.”

Poppy said, “When we listen and use gentle words, we feel safe. We solve problems. We help friends.”

Mama Panda said, “That is peaceful-communication. We use it in our car, in our park, and in our hearts.”

Poppy held Pip’s paw. She felt calm, like the humming car. She whispered, “We can do it every day.”

Lesson: Use peaceful words, take turns, listen, and breathe. It helps you, your family, and your community too.

The End.
