# Milo Monkey's Morning Mix-Up

Milo Monkey loved his cozy living room. Every morning, he followed his special routine. First, he would swing from the soft purple couch to the fuzzy yellow chair. Then he would organize his toys in neat little piles.

"Good morning, living room!" Milo chirped happily. His brown tail swished as he looked around.

But today, something was very different. The room was a big mess! Toys were scattered everywhere. Empty juice boxes sat on the coffee table. Banana peels lay on the carpet.

"Oh my!" Milo scratched his head. "This doesn't look right at all."

Milo's little sister Maya peeked around the corner. Her eyes looked sad and worried.

"I'm sorry, Milo," Maya whispered. "I played in here last night. I forgot to clean up. I made a big mess."

Milo felt upset at first. His morning routine was all mixed up! But then he looked at Maya's sorry face. He remembered how Mama always said forgiveness makes hearts feel better.

"It's okay, Maya," Milo said gently. "Everyone makes mistakes. Want to help me fix it?"

Maya's face brightened like sunshine. "Yes, please!"

Milo put on his thinking cap. He looked around the messy room with his smart monkey eyes.

"Let's think logically," he said. "First, we need to sort everything. Toys go in the toy box. Trash goes in different places."

Maya nodded eagerly. "What about these juice boxes?"

"Those can be recycled!" Milo explained. "We put them in the special blue bin. That helps take care of our Earth."

The two monkeys worked together. *Clink-clank* went the juice boxes into the recycling bin. *Thud-thump* went the toys into their proper homes.

"Look, Maya!" Milo pointed to the banana peels. "These go in the compost bin. They'll help grow new plants!"

As they cleaned, Milo felt his body getting stronger. Moving around felt good! His muscles were happy to stretch and work.

"Cleaning is like exercise," Milo puffed cheerfully. "It keeps our bodies healthy and strong!"

Soon, the living room sparkled again. The purple couch looked fluffy and inviting. The yellow chair sat ready for morning swings. Everything was in its proper place.

"Now we can do our morning routine together!" Milo said.

Maya clapped her little hands. "Can you teach me your routine?"

"Of course!" Milo beamed. "First, we swing from couch to chair. Then we organize our toys. Having a routine helps us feel calm and happy."

They swung together, giggling as their feet touched the soft carpet. They arranged their toys in colorful rows. Maya learned that routines make days feel safe and special.

"Thank you for forgiving me," Maya said, giving Milo a big hug.

"Thank you for helping me clean," Milo replied. "We make a great team!"

From that day on, Maya joined Milo's morning routine. They learned that forgiveness brings families closer. They remembered to recycle and compost. They discovered that cleaning together is fun exercise.

Most importantly, they learned that having a daily routine helps everything feel just right.

"Routines are like magic," Milo said as they snuggled on the purple couch. "They make every day feel wonderful!"

The End.
