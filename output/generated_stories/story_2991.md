# Bubbles the Dolphin Takes a Calm Breath

In a comfy nursery with soft rugs and tiny chairs, a playful dolphin named Bubbles splashed quietly in a round, warm water tub. The tub was shiny blue and smelled like clean soap. Sunlight made little sparkles on the water. Soft music hummed from a toy radio. It sounded like “la-la-la” with gentle bells.

Bubbles clapped his fins. “I want to play everything!” he squeaked. “I want to stack blocks! I want to sing! I want to snack!” His tail swished water that went plip-plip onto the floor.

Miss Lila, the kind teacher with a warm smile and a rainbow scarf, chuckled. “We can do many things, Bubbles,” she said. “But let’s do one thing at a time.”

Bubbles tried to stack blocks. Red, yellow, blue! But he heard the soft music and wanted to sing. He opened his mouth to sing, and the blocks fell down. Clack-clack! “Oh no!” he cried.

He swam in a fast circle. “I want a snack now. I want to sing now! I want to build now!” His tummy made a little rumble. The room smelled like banana and warm toast. The music chimed again. The colors of the blocks looked so bright and exciting. Bubbles felt wiggly and buzzy inside.

His friend Mia, a small child with curly hair and gentle eyes, said, “Bubbles, it’s okay. We can slow down.”

Bubbles shook his head. “I can’t slow down! I am too bouncy!”

Miss Lila knelt by the tub. She dipped her fingers in the water. “The water is warm,” she said. “Let’s try a calm breath. We can learn a little trick.”

“A trick?” Bubbles blinked. He loved tricks.

“It is a mindfulness trick,” Miss Lila said. “Mindfulness means noticing what is here, right now. We use our eyes, ears, nose, tongue, and skin. We do one thing at a time.”

Bubbles tilted his head. “How do I do that?”

Miss Lila held up her hand. “First, we listen. What do you hear?”

Bubbles closed his eyes. The room felt quieter. He heard the toy radio sing soft bells. Ting-ting-ting. He heard his water go swish. He heard Mia’s gentle breathing. “I hear music and water and Mia,” he whispered.

“Good noticing,” said Miss Lila. “Now we look. What do you see?”

Bubbles opened his eyes. He saw the shiny blocks, the bright rug with green leaves, and Miss Lila’s rainbow scarf. He saw the snack plates with banana slices and crunchy carrots. “I see many colors,” he said, smiling.

“Now we smell,” Miss Lila said.

Bubbles sniffed with his little dolphin nose. Sniff, sniff. “Banana. Soap. Warm toast,” he said. His tummy rumbled again.

Mia giggled. “Now taste comes later,” she said. “We wait our turn.”

Bubbles nodded. “Okay. What next?”

“Now we feel our body,” Miss Lila said. “Let’s take a belly breath. Put your fin on your tummy.”

Bubbles placed his fin on his round, splashy belly. The water felt cozy and warm.

“In through your nose, slow like smelling a flower,” Miss Lila said. “Out through your mouth, slow like blowing a bubble.”

Bubbles breathed in. The air smelled sweet. He breathed out. A tiny bubble popped. Plop! He giggled.

Again, in… and out… The music felt softer. The room felt bigger and calmer. The colors were not too loud now. His tummy rumble felt friendly, not fussy.

“How do you feel?” Mia asked.

“I feel calm,” Bubbles said. “I can choose one thing.”

Miss Lila smiled. “That is knowledge. You learned a skill. Mindfulness helps us choose.”

Bubbles looked at the blocks. “First, I build,” he said. “Then, music. Then, snack.”

Mia clapped softly. “I’ll help you build. We can make a tall tower.”

They stacked slowly. One block. Breath. Two blocks. Breath. The tower grew steady and strong. “I am patient,” Bubbles said. “I am careful.” When the tower was done, it was taller than the toy giraffe.

“Now the music,” Mia said.

Miss Lila handed them little egg shakers. They felt bumpy and made soft sh-sh sounds. “Let’s play quietly, so others can read,” Miss Lila reminded. “That shows respect.”

Bubbles nodded. “I can be gentle.” They shook the eggs in a small rhythm to the la-la-la song. Bubbles smiled. “This feels nice.”

“Snack time,” Miss Lila said at last. She set out bowls with banana slices, crunchy carrots, and a small cup of cool water.

Bubbles looked at the snacks. He remembered mindfulness. “I will taste slowly,” he said. He picked up a carrot with his fin. Crunch! It was crisp and sweet. He smiled. “My body likes this. It gives me energy.” He ate a banana slice. Soft and creamy. “This helps my brain learn,” he added proudly.

Mia sipped her water. “We are taking care of our bodies,” she said.

Miss Lila nodded. “Mindfulness, knowledge, and good nutrition help us feel our best. And when we feel our best, we can be kind to others.”

Bubbles looked around. “Thank you for waiting for me,” he told Mia. “And thank you, Miss Lila, for the breathing trick.” His heart felt warm like sunshine on the water.

“You’re welcome,” said Miss Lila. “What will you do next time you feel buzzy?”

Bubbles took a slow breath. “I will stop. I will notice. I will choose one thing. In, and out.”

The room was calm and happy. The tower stood tall. The music hummed softly. The snack tasted good. Bubbles splashed once, gently. “I can do one thing at a time,” he said. “I am mindful.”

The End.
