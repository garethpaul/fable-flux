# Leo the Lion Cub Helps in the Laundry Room

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, he padded into the laundry room. The air smelled warm and soapy. The washing machine hummed like a busy bee, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer made a gentle “thump, thump” sound.

Leo’s friend, Miri the mouse, peeked from a basket of clean towels. “Hi, Leo!” squeaked Miri. “I’m making a sock puppet. Want to help?”

Leo’s whiskers twitched with excitement. “Yes! Let’s craft and help with chores too.”

They looked around. There were shirts in a pile, socks on the floor, and a big basket of clean clothes to fold. A little note on the wall said, “We are helpers. We are friends. We finish what we start.”

Leo puffed out his chest. “We can do it together.”

“Together,” nodded Miri, smiling.

First, they sorted the laundry. “Colors in this basket, whites in that one,” said Miri. Leo picked up a red shirt. “This goes with colors,” he said. He picked up a white sock. “This goes with whites.” He liked the soft feel of the fabric on his paws. Miri held up two tiny socks. “These are friends,” she giggled. “They go together.”

Leo found a lonely blue sock. “This sock needs a friend,” he said. He felt a little worried. “What if we can’t find it?”

Miri patted his paw. “Friends help friends. We will look.”

They searched under a fluffy towel. “Not here.” They looked behind the dryer. “Dust bunnies!” They giggled as they saw tiny dust tumble and tickle their noses. “Achoo!” sneezed Leo. He wiped his nose with a tissue and washed his paws at the sink. The water felt cool and splashy. “Self-care,” he said proudly. “I took care of me.” Miri washed her little paws too. “Me too!”

“Let’s finish sorting,” said Miri. “Then we craft.”

They worked side by side. Leo folded a soft T-shirt. He pressed the sides in, then folded it in half. “Fold, fold,” he sang. Miri rolled the socks into pairs. “Roll, roll,” she sang back. Their voices made a happy song with the machines: “Whirr, thump, fold, roll.”

At last, the piles were neat. Leo smiled. “We are completing our tasks!”

“Teamwork,” said Miri. “Now let’s make your sock puppet.”

Leo chose the lonely blue sock. “We can give it a new friend,” he said. Miri brought out a little box with buttons, yarn, and glue. Leo touched the buttons. They were cool and smooth. He felt the yarn. It was fuzzy and tickly.

“First, we plan,” said Miri. “Planning helps us do things in the right order.”

Leo nodded. “Order helps.”

They made a plan:
- Glue two buttons for eyes.
- Tie yarn for hair.
- Draw a smile with a fabric marker.
- Make a tiny paper bow tie.

“Ready?” asked Miri.

“Ready!” said Leo.

Miri squeezed a tiny dot of glue. “Just a little,” she said. “We use safe amounts.” Leo pressed the buttons on the sock. “Hello, eyes!” He gently patted them. The glue was sticky and cool.

Next, Leo picked purple yarn. “Purple hair!” He giggled as it tickled his paw pads. Miri tied the yarn on. “Snip, snip,” went her little scissors. “We keep our paws away from the sharp part,” she reminded.

Leo drew a big smile. The marker smelled a little sweet. “This puppet looks kind,” he said.

Miri folded a tiny bow tie from a scrap of blue paper. “For style!” she chirped.

They slipped the sock on Leo’s paw. “Hi, I’m Blue Buddy,” said Leo in a silly voice. Miri laughed. “Hello, Blue Buddy! I’m Miri Mouse.”

Blue Buddy wiggled. “I love helping and being a friend!”

Leo looked at the laundry baskets. “We did it. We sorted. We folded. We crafted. We cared for ourselves. We learned to plan.”

“And we did it together,” said Miri.

Just then, a small squeak came from behind a basket. “Squeak! I’m lost!” It was the other blue sock! It had fallen behind. Leo’s ears perked up. “We found your friend!” He paired the socks. “Now no sock is lonely.”

Miri clapped her tiny paws. “Friendship means we look out for each other.”

They put the clean, folded clothes away. The shelves looked neat. The room smelled fresh and warm. The washer and dryer grew quiet.

Leo took a deep breath. He felt proud and calm. “We finished our tasks. We made something new. We helped each other.”

Miri smiled. “When we are kind and work together, chores can be fun.”

Leo hugged his new puppet. “Blue Buddy says thank you, friend.”

Miri hugged Leo’s paw. “You’re welcome, friend.”

Leo’s heart felt big and cozy. He learned that friendship is helping, sharing, and finishing things together. He learned that planning and caring for yourself make work easier. He learned that crafting can bring smiles—and bring lost socks new life!

“Tomorrow,” said Leo, “let’s be helpers again.”

“Together,” said Miri.

The End.
