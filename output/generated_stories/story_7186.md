# Polly’s Balcony Dance Parade

Polly the parrot lived on a sunny balcony garden. The air smelled like mint and lemon leaves. The wind chimes tinkled softly, and little pots of flowers glowed red, yellow, and purple. Polly was a very creative parrot. She loved colors, songs, and new ideas. But today, she wanted to learn a new kind of dance.

“Good morning, garden!” Polly chirped. “Who wants to dance?”

The marigolds bobbed in the breeze. The basil leaves swished. A blue butterfly fluttered by. “I do!” Polly squawked. “I want a dance that is happy, careful, and fun!”

From the doorway, Mia, the little girl who cared for the garden, stepped out with a watering can. She wore bright socks and had a big smile. “Hi, Polly! Do you want to learn my garden dance?” she asked.

“Yes, please!” Polly fluffed her green feathers. “Teach me! Teach me!”

Mia set down the watering can. “We will follow directions. Following directions helps us move together and stay safe,” she said kindly. “Ready?”

Polly nodded. “Ready!”

Mia clapped a gentle beat. Clap, clap… clap, clap. The wind chimes chimed along.

“Step one,” Mia said. “Feet still. Wings still. Listen.”

Polly stood very still. He heard the chimes, the buzzing of a honeybee, and the soft splash of water. His feet felt cool on the tiles. “Listening,” he whispered. “I can do that!”

“Step two,” Mia said. “Tap-tap. Tap your toes two times.” She tapped her shoes. Tap, tap.

Polly looked down at his birdy toes and tapped the tiles. Tap, tap. “Tap-tap!” he said proudly.

Mia smiled. “Great! Step three: Sway side to side. Slow and smooth, like a tall flower in the breeze.”

Polly swayed left, then right. His feathers brushed the air. He felt the warm sun on his back and smelled the sweet mint. “Sway, sway,” he sang.

“Step four,” said Mia. “Now add a spin. One gentle spin. No bumping the pots!”

Polly peeked at the pots of tomatoes and basil. He made a careful circle. Whoosh! His tail feathers tickled the air. He did not bump a single pot. “Spin!” he cheered.

“Step five,” Mia said. “Clap two times. Clap, clap. Then freeze!”

Mia clapped. Polly clacked his beak two times. Clack, clack! Then he froze. Even his tail stayed still. He giggled. “This is fun!”

“You’re doing great,” Mia said. “Now we put it together. Listen, tap-tap, sway, spin, clap-clap, freeze!”

Polly took a deep breath. He followed each step. He listened. Tap, tap. Sway, sway. Whoosh, spin. Clack, clack. Freeze! The wind chimes jingled like tiny bells. The butterfly fluttered in circles as if cheering.

Polly felt brave and bright. “I followed the directions!” he squawked. “I can dance!”

Mia nodded. “Yes! When we follow directions, our bodies learn the moves. Then we can add our own ideas.”

Polly’s eyes sparkled. “My own ideas? Oh! I want to be creative too!”

Mia laughed. “Let’s make it a balcony dance parade. First we follow the steps. Then we add a fun twist.”

Polly flapped with joy. “Parade! Parade!”

They started again. “Listen,” Mia said.

Polly listened. The honeybee hummed a steady hum. Hmmmmmm.

“Tap-tap,” Mia said.

Tap, tap went Polly’s toes.

“Sway,” Mia said.

Polly swayed like a tall sunflower.

“Spin,” Mia said.

Polly spun carefully, the world a blur of colors—red tomatoes, purple flowers, green leaves.

“Clap-clap, freeze!”

Clack, clack! Still like a statue.

Mia clapped. “Now add your creative move, Polly!”

Polly thought. He felt the sun. He smelled lemon leaves. He heard the bee’s hum and the chimes. He puffed up and added a happy wiggle—wiggle, wiggle—while he swayed. Then he made a tiny hop—hop!—before the spin. Wiggle, hop, spin!

Mia cheered. “I love your wiggle-hop! That’s your creative idea.”

Polly beamed. “Can you add a move too?”

“Yes!” Mia said. “After clap-clap, I’ll make a star shape with my arms.” She stood tall. “Star!”

They danced the parade across the balcony tiles. Listen. Tap-tap. Sway. Spin. Clap-clap. Freeze. Wiggle-hop. Star! The mint leaves brushed their ankles. The sun warmed their faces. The chimes made the music bright.

Soon a tiny snail peeked out from under a pot. A ladybug landed on a leaf. Even the butterfly floated along. The garden felt alive and happy.

Polly called, “Friends, you can dance with us! Just follow the steps. Then add one small idea that is safe and kind.”

The ladybug bobbed. The butterfly looped. The snail made a slow slide wiggle. Everyone followed the directions first. Then everyone added one gentle, creative move. No one bumped a pot. No one got in the way. The parade moved like a friendly ribbon across the balcony.

After the last freeze, Mia bowed. Polly bowed too. They took a deep breath. The air smelled fresh and green.

Mia said, “Today we learned: First we listen and follow directions. That keeps us safe and together.”

Polly added, “Then we add our own ideas. That makes our dance special and creative!”

They both smiled. “Dance can be careful,” Mia said, “and dance can be fun.”

Polly fluffed his feathers. “I am a careful, creative dancer!”

The wind chimes sang a soft, happy song. The garden glowed. The butterfly waved a tiny wing. Polly felt proud, calm, and brave.

“Tomorrow,” he chirped, “we dance again!”

The End.
