# Ollie's Amazing Invention Corner

Ollie the otter loved his special hideaway in the big closet. It was cozy and warm, with soft blankets and his favorite stuffed fish. But today, Ollie had a big problem.

"Oh no!" Ollie squeaked, looking around his messy hideaway. Toy blocks were scattered everywhere, old juice boxes sat in corners, and his art supplies were all mixed up. "Mama Otter asked me to clean up, but this is too much work!"

Ollie's whiskers twitched as he thought hard. "There has to be a better way," he said to himself. "What if I could make cleaning fun AND help the Earth too?"

His dark eyes sparkled with an idea. Ollie remembered seeing his big sister sort things into different boxes. "I can be an inventor!" he chirped excitedly.

First, Ollie found three empty cardboard boxes from the recycling pile. With his crayons, he drew pictures on each box. A green leaf for things to recycle, a toy car for things to keep, and a trash can for real garbage.

"This is my Sorting Machine!" Ollie announced proudly, clapping his little paws together.

He picked up a plastic water bottle. "Hmm, this goes in the green box because plastic can be made into new things!" Plop! In it went.

Next, he found his favorite red ball. "This stays with me!" he giggled, bouncing it into the "keep" box. Boing, boing!

An old, broken crayon went into the trash box. "Goodbye, little crayon. You helped me make pretty pictures!"

As Ollie worked, he made up a happy song: "Sort, sort, sort all day! Making cleaning fun to play! Recycle, keep, or throw away! In my boxes they will stay!"

Soon, his friend Penny Penguin waddled into the closet. "Wow, Ollie! What are you doing? It smells so fresh and clean in here!"

"I invented a Sorting Machine!" Ollie beamed, showing off his colorful boxes. "Want to help? We can set a goal to clean the whole closet before snack time!"

"That sounds amazing!" Penny flapped her wings. "Can I sort too?"

Together, they worked as a team. Penny found old newspapers that could be recycled. Ollie discovered broken toys that needed to go in the trash. They kept all their favorite books and games in the "keep" box.

"Look how much we can recycle!" Ollie pointed to the green box, now full of bottles, cans, and paper. "These will become new things instead of hurting our planet!"

When Mama Otter peeked into the closet, her eyes grew wide. "Ollie! Your hideaway looks wonderful! How did you clean up so quickly?"

"I used innovation!" Ollie said proudly, puffing out his furry chest. "I made cleaning into a fun game with my Sorting Machine!"

Mama Otter hugged him tight. "You're such a smart little otter! You turned a chore into an adventure and helped the Earth too!"

From that day on, Ollie used his Sorting Machine every week. He taught other young animals how to sort and recycle. His closet hideaway became the cleanest, coziest spot in the whole house.

"When we use our brains to solve problems," Ollie told his friends, "we can make everything better and more fun!"

The End.
