# Nutkin's Bathroom Helper Adventure

Nutkin the squirrel lived in a cozy oak tree next to the Miller family's house. He loved watching the children through the windows and learning about their daily routines. One sunny morning, Nutkin heard crying coming from the bathroom window.

"Oh no!" sniffled little Emma Miller. "I can't reach the soap to wash my hands, and Mommy said I need to follow the hand-washing steps!"

Nutkin's fluffy tail twitched with concern. He scampered closer to the open window and peeked inside. The bathroom smelled fresh and clean, like lavender and mint. Emma stood on her tippy-toes at the big white sink, but the soap dispenser was too high for her small hands to reach.

"Don't worry!" called Nutkin in his cheerful, squeaky voice. "I'm a very good listener, and I love following directions. Maybe I can help you!"

Emma's eyes grew wide with wonder. "A talking squirrel! But how can you help me wash my hands?"

Nutkin's whiskers wiggled as he thought. "First, let me listen carefully to what your mommy taught you. What are the steps for washing hands?"

Emma wiped her tears and recited slowly: "First, turn on the water. Second, get soap. Third, scrub for twenty seconds. Fourth, rinse with water. Fifth, dry with a clean towel."

"Perfect!" Nutkin exclaimed. "You remembered all the steps! Now, let me help you follow them." 

The clever squirrel noticed a small step stool tucked behind the toilet. Using his strong paws and teeth, he carefully dragged it across the smooth tile floor. The stool made a soft scraping sound as it moved.

"Oh, thank you!" Emma giggled. "I forgot about my step stool!"

Emma climbed onto the stool and reached the faucet easily. "Step one - turn on the water!" she announced proudly. The water made a gentle splashing sound as it flowed into the sink.

Nutkin watched as Emma pressed the soap dispenser. Sweet-smelling bubbles appeared in her palms. "Step two - get soap! Check!" she said with a big smile.

"Now comes the fun part," Nutkin encouraged. "Let's count to twenty together while you scrub!"

Emma rubbed her hands together, making lots of foamy bubbles. "One, two, three..." she counted with Nutkin's help. The bubbles felt slippery and smooth between her fingers.

"Eighteen, nineteen, twenty!" they finished together.

"Step three done!" Emma cheered. She rinsed her hands under the warm water, watching the bubbles swirl down the drain. "Step four - rinse!"

Nutkin pointed his tiny paw toward a fluffy yellow towel hanging nearby. "Don't forget step five!"

Emma dried her hands thoroughly with the soft towel. "All done! I followed every single direction!"

"You did wonderfully!" Nutkin praised. "Following directions helps us do things safely and correctly. And asking for help when we need it is very smart!"

From that day on, whenever Emma needed to wash her hands, she remembered Nutkin's lesson about following directions step by step. She even taught her little brother the same five steps, helping him just like Nutkin had helped her.

Nutkin felt proud knowing he had helped Emma learn something important. He discovered that following directions and helping others made him feel warm and happy inside, just like finding the perfect acorn on a beautiful autumn day.

The End.
