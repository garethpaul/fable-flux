# Cubby Counts the Clues

Cubby was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and a bright red backpack. Today, Cubby climbed into the family car with Mama Bear, Papa Bear, and Baby Sis. The car smelled like warm seats and fresh pine from the air freshener. The sun made little sparkles on the windows.

“Seat belts, everyone,” said Papa Bear.

“Click, click!” Cubby and Baby Sis said together. Their buckles made happy snappy sounds.

Mama Bear passed a small snack bag to Cubby. “We have a job to do,” she said. “We need to drop a healthy lunch to Grandma at the park. She forgot her lunch at home!”

Cubby’s ears perked up. “We can help!”

Papa turned the key. “Vroom,” went the car. The wheels hummed on the road.

Inside the snack bag, Cubby saw a banana, crunchy carrot sticks, cheese cubes, and a tiny note. The note said, “To Grandma, with love.”

Baby Sis pointed to a toy map on her lap. “We go park?”

Cubby smiled. “Yes! But we have to think, step by step.”

“What do you mean, Cubby?” asked Mama.

Cubby held up one paw. “First, we check the lunch. Is it all here?”

“Let’s count,” said Papa. “Counting is smart thinking.”

Cubby opened the bag carefully. “One banana. One, two, three, four carrot sticks. One, two, three cheese cubes.”

Baby Sis clapped. “Three cheeses!”

Cubby nodded. “So the lunch is ready. Now step two: Keep it safe. It should not squish.”

Mama passed a small container. “We can use this box.” The box clicked shut over the cheese and carrots. The banana sat on top, yellow and smooth.

“Step three,” Cubby said, tapping his chin. “Find the fastest way to the park.”

Papa Bear smiled at the rear-view mirror. “The big road is busy today. Maybe a quiet street is better.”

Cubby looked out the window. He could see tall trees, a bakery window with round bread, and a red light blinking. “Hmm. If the big road is busy, we might stop a lot. Quiet street might be faster.”

Baby Sis waved her toy map. “Turn left!” she chirped.

Cubby traced with a paw. “Left, then straight, then right at the big tree. That’s three steps.” He held up three claws. “One, two, three.”

Papa turned left. The car made a soft “whoosh.” The quiet street smelled like flowers. A bird sang, “Cheep-cheep!”

“Step four,” Cubby said. “Check time.” He looked at the clock on the dashboard. The numbers glowed green. “We want to get there before lunch time.”

Mama nodded. “Good thinking. It’s almost noon.”

Suddenly, the car slowed down. A shiny orange cone blocked the road. A sign said, “Road Work.”

“Oh no,” said Baby Sis. “No go!”

Cubby took a deep breath. “It’s okay. We can think. What can we do?”

Papa stopped the car. “We need a new plan.”

Cubby held up a paw and spoke slowly. “Let’s use logic. What do we know? One: The park is to the right of the big tree. Two: This road is closed. Three: We can go around.”

Mama pointed to a side street. “That road goes around the block.”

Cubby nodded. “Yes! Step five: Try another way.”

They turned down the side street. The car tires made a soft “crunch” on tiny pebbles. Cubby watched for the big tree. “I see it!” he said. The big tree had wide branches and cool shade.

“Right turn,” Papa said, turning the wheel.

They reached the park. Children laughed. Swings squeaked. A grill sizzled, and the air smelled like warm corn and fresh grass. Grandma Bear waved from a bench under the big tree.

“You made it!” Grandma smiled.

Cubby held up the lunch box proudly. “We used steps and thinking. We checked the lunch. We kept it safe. We picked the best streets. When the road closed, we changed the plan.”

Grandma gave him a hug. “That is wonderful teamwork.”

Baby Sis handed Grandma the banana. “Yellow smile fruit!”

Grandma chuckled. “Thank you, dear.” She opened the lunch. “Carrots for crunch, cheese for strength, and banana for sweet energy. This is a healthy lunch.”

Cubby’s tummy rumbled. “I like carrots, too.” Mama Bear shared a few extra sticks with Cubby and Baby Sis. The carrots were cool and crisp. “Crunch, crunch,” went their teeth.

Papa Bear smiled. “We finished our task together.”

Cubby looked up at the leaves and felt proud. He thought about each step like a puzzle. “When we think clearly,” he said, “we can solve problems.”

Grandma nodded. “And when we work as a team, we finish faster and happier.”

Baby Sis patted Cubby’s paw. “Team!”

On the way home, the car hummed softly. The sun felt warm on Cubby’s fur. He whispered, “Step by step. Think, choose, and try again.”

Mama Bear squeezed his shoulder. “That’s logical thinking, Cubby.”

Cubby grinned. “Logical thinking helps me help others. It helps me eat healthy, too!”

Papa chuckled. “And it makes adventures fun.”

They all laughed as the car rolled along, full of happy sounds and carrot crunches.

The End.
