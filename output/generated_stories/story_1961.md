# Polly's Special Family Recipe

Polly the parrot loved bright colors and trying new things. Her beautiful green and red feathers sparkled in the morning sunlight as she perched in the cozy dining room. Today was special - Grandma Parrot was coming to teach the family their old recipe for seed cookies!

"Squawk! I can't wait!" chirped Polly, flapping her wings excitedly. The dining room smelled like cinnamon and vanilla from Mama's baking earlier.

When Grandma Parrot arrived, she carried an old wooden recipe box. "This recipe is one hundred years old," she said proudly. "My grandmother taught it to me, and now I'll teach it to you."

Polly watched carefully as Grandma mixed sunflower seeds, oats, and honey. "First, we measure two cups of seeds," Grandma explained, her wise eyes twinkling.

But Polly had a creative idea! "Grandma, what if we add colorful berries too? They would make the cookies look like rainbows!"

Grandma's feathers ruffled. "Oh my! We've never changed this recipe before. It's always been the same."

Polly felt sad. Her bright idea seemed to upset Grandma. "I'm sorry, Grandma. I just thought it might taste good."

The dining room grew quiet except for the tick-tock of the old clock on the wall. Polly could smell the sweet honey and hear Grandma's gentle breathing.

Then something wonderful happened. Grandma looked at Polly's droopy feathers and remembered being young and creative too.

"You know what, little one?" Grandma said softly. "Maybe traditions can grow and change. Tell me more about your berry idea."

Polly's eyes lit up like little stars! "Well, we could use blueberries for blue, strawberries for red, and maybe yellow corn for sunshine colors!"

Grandma smiled. "That sounds beautiful! Let's try it together. We'll keep the special old recipe and add your new touch."

So they worked side by side. Grandma showed Polly how to measure carefully, just like her grandmother had taught her. Polly added her colorful berries, making each cookie look like a tiny rainbow.

"Mix gently," said Grandma. "Feel how the dough gets softer?"

"Yes! And it smells like a flower garden!" Polly giggled.

When the cookies finished baking, the whole dining room filled with the most amazing smell - sweet, nutty, and fruity all together.

The family gathered around the big wooden dining table. Everyone took a bite of the new rainbow cookies.

"Mmm! These are delicious!" said Papa Parrot.

"The old recipe tastes even better with Polly's new ideas," said Mama Parrot.

Grandma hugged Polly tight. "I learned something important today. Traditions are like seeds - they grow best when we share them and let them change a little."

Polly felt warm and happy inside. "And I learned that old recipes are special because they connect us to our family!"

From that day on, Polly and Grandma baked together every week. Sometimes they made the cookies the old way, and sometimes they tried new ingredients. But always, they shared stories and love.

The recipe box now held both the original recipe and Polly's rainbow version, side by side.

"Traditions are meant to be shared and grow," Polly would tell her friends. "Just like our family!"

The End.
