# Koda the Koala and the Magic Music Box

In a cozy, quiet attic filled with dusty sunbeams and old treasures, lived a calm koala named Koda. The attic was Koda's favorite place because it was peaceful and full of wonderful things to discover. Soft golden light filtered through a small round window, making everything glow like magic.

One sunny morning, Koda heard excited voices coming up the creaky wooden stairs. *Thump, thump, thump!* Three little mice scurried into the attic, their tiny paws pattering on the old floorboards.

"Look what we found!" squeaked Pip, the smallest mouse, pointing to a beautiful music box sitting on an old trunk. The music box was painted with swirling colors and had a tiny dancing ballerina on top.

"I want to wind it up first!" shouted Max, the biggest mouse, pushing past the others.

"No, I saw it first!" cried Mia, tugging on the golden key. "It's my turn!"

"But I'm the oldest!" Max argued, grabbing for the music box. "I should go first!"

The three mice began to push and pull, their voices getting louder and louder. The peaceful attic suddenly felt very noisy and upset.

Koda watched quietly from his soft corner, his fuzzy gray ears twitching. He could see that nobody was happy, even though they had found something wonderful to share.

"Friends," Koda said in his gentle, calm voice. "May I help you?"

The mice stopped arguing and looked at the wise koala. Koda's kind eyes sparkled in the dusty sunlight.

"We all want to play with the music box," Pip explained, his little whiskers drooping sadly.

"But we can't agree on who goes first," added Mia, sitting down with a small *plop*.

Koda nodded thoughtfully and climbed down from his cozy spot. "I understand. The music box looks very special. But tell me, what happens when you all try to use it at the same time?"

"Nobody gets to enjoy it," Max admitted quietly, looking at his paws.

"And we feel grumpy instead of happy," said Mia.

"Exactly," Koda smiled warmly. "But what if we found a fair way for everyone to have a turn? Would that work better?"

The mice's eyes lit up. "How?" they asked together.

Koda pointed to three colorful leaves that had blown into the attic. "Let's each pick a leaf. The reddest leaf goes first, then the yellowest, then the most orange. That way, it's fair and nobody chooses for themselves."

"That's a great idea!" squeaked Pip excitedly.

Each mouse carefully selected a leaf. When they compared them, Mia had the reddest, Pip had the yellowest, and Max had the most orange.

"I'll go first, then Pip, then Max," Mia said happily. "And we can each wind it up three times!"

As Mia gently turned the golden key, the most beautiful tinkling melody filled the quiet attic. The tiny ballerina spun gracefully, and all the mice watched with wonder and joy.

When Mia's turn ended, she carefully passed the music box to Pip. "Your turn, friend!"

Each mouse enjoyed their special time with the music box, and when Max finished his turn, they all clapped and cheered together.

"Thank you, Koda," said Pip. "Taking turns made it much more fun!"

"And everyone got to enjoy the beautiful music," added Max.

Koda settled back into his cozy corner with a peaceful smile. "When we listen to each other and take turns fairly, everyone can be happy. That's what good friends do."

The attic filled with warm laughter and friendship as the mice played together, taking turns with all the wonderful treasures they discovered that day.

The End.
