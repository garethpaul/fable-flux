# Sprig the Squirrel and the Laundry Room Fairness

Sprig was a small, fluffy, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a twitchy nose. One rainy day, Sprig scurried into the laundry room to stay dry. The room was warm and smelled like soap and clean cotton. The washing machines hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryers sang, “Thump-a-thump,” as socks tumbled inside.

“Hello, laundry room,” Sprig whispered. “What a cozy place.”

Sprig loved being helpful. He liked to make things fair. He liked to make things work. He sat on a shelf and watched the day.

Soon, Fluffy the Cat padded in, tail high. “I will use the washer first,” Fluffy purred. She put her tiny blanket in a basket by the washer.

Then Tilly the Turtle slowly slid in with a little towel on her back. “Excuse me,” Tilly said, “I was next in line yesterday. Today is my turn.”

Hopper the Bunny bounced in, ears wiggling. “I need to wash my soccer socks,” Hopper said. “I’m fastest. Fastest should go first!”

A soft squeak came from the corner. Pip the Mouse peeked out, holding a little scarf. “I have been waiting quietly,” Pip said. “I think first should be the one who waited.”

Fluffy flicked her tail. “But I am ready now,” she said.

Hopper hopped in place. “Speed wins!” he said.

Tilly blinked. “I was promised a turn,” she reminded them.

Pip hugged the scarf and looked down.

Voices grew louder. The hum of the machines felt shaky. The warm room did not feel cozy anymore. Sprig’s whiskers twitched. This was not fair. This was not kind. This was not just.

Sprig took a deep breath. He could feel the softness of warm towels under his paws. He could smell the lemony soap. He remembered a big word: justice. Justice means fair for everyone. Justice means keeping promises. Justice means listening.

Sprig climbed onto a laundry basket. “Friends,” he called in a clear, friendly voice. “Let’s pause. Let’s be fair. Let’s make a plan.”

The room got quiet. Even the dryer seemed to thump more softly.

Sprig smiled. “I can help,” he said. “I will lead us to a fair turn. We will make a list. We will follow the list. No pushing. No grabbing. Everyone will be heard. That is justice.”

Fluffy sat and curled her tail. “I like a list,” she said.

Tilly nodded slowly. “A list keeps promises,” she added.

Hopper wiggled his nose. “Okay,” he said. “But it must be fast.”

Sprig found a clean towel tag and a piece of chalk that had fallen from the shelf. Resourceful Sprig made a little sign on the floor. Scritch, scratch. He wrote: “Laundry Turns.”

“Now tell me,” Sprig said kindly. “Who was first to wait?”

Pip squeaked. “I have been waiting since morning,” he said softly.

Tilly lifted her head. “Yesterday I was told I could be next today,” she said.

Fluffy purred. “I came in ready with my blanket,” she said.

Hopper bounced. “And I am very fast,” he said.

Sprig nodded to each friend. “I hear you,” he said. “Justice means we honor waiting and promises. This is the fair plan: Pip goes first because he has waited the longest. Tilly goes second because she was promised a turn today. Fluffy goes third because she is ready and has a small load. Hopper, you go fourth. Your socks will be clean soon. Speed is great on the field, but fairness is great here.”

Hopper’s ears drooped, then lifted. “I can wait,” he said. “I will practice hopping in place.”

Fluffy smiled. “Third is fine,” she said. “My blanket likes a little more time to purr.”

Tilly’s eyes shone. “Thank you for keeping the promise,” she said.

Pip beamed. “Thank you for seeing me,” he whispered.

Sprig clapped his tiny paws. “We have a plan,” he said. “Let’s help each other.”

Pip carefully put his scarf in the washer. Sprig showed him how to press the gentle button. Click. The washer hummed, “Whirr.” The scarf swirled like a little fish.

Tilly went next. Her towel smelled like the pond and the sun. Sprig added just enough soap. “Plop,” went the soap. “Whirr,” sang the machine.

Fluffy placed her blanket in with care. “Thump-a-purr,” went the dryer later, and the blanket came out warm and fluffy like a cloud.

Hopper waited and counted hops. “One, two, three…” Sprig cheered him on. “You are a leader in patience,” Sprig said.

Soon, Hopper’s socks were spinning, too. He watched the little window. Round and round they went like tiny moons.

While they waited, Sprig taught a song:
“Fair and kind, fair and kind,
We take turns and peace we find.
We listen, we share,
We show we care.
Justice keeps us all in line.”

They sang together. Their voices mixed with the hum of the machines. The laundry room felt warm again. It smelled like soap and smiles.

At last, all the clothes were clean and dry. Pip wrapped his scarf around his neck. “It is soft and fair,” he said happily.

Tilly folded her towel. “Thank you for keeping the promise,” she told Sprig.

Fluffy fluffed her blanket. “It is cozy, like a hug,” she purred.

Hopper tugged on his soccer socks. “Ready to run, ready to be fair,” he said.

Sprig felt proud, but not proud only for himself. He was proud of his friends. “We used justice,” he said. “We listened. We kept a promise. We took turns. That is what leaders do. That is what friends do.”

They all nodded. The machines were quiet now. The room was peaceful. The rain outside had stopped.

Sprig looked around with bright eyes. “Remember,” he said, “justice means fair for everyone. When we are fair, we all feel safe, heard, and happy.”

They waved goodbye and left the laundry room clean and calm. Sprig’s tail twitched with joy as he skipped to the door.

“Whirr, thump, song, and smiles,” Sprig said. “That is the sound of justice.”

The End.
