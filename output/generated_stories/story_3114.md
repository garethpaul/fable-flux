# The Brave Build in the Busy Garage

In a sunny little town, there was a clever cat named Coco. Coco had bright green eyes, soft gray fur, and a twitchy tail. Coco loved to explore. One day, Coco padded into the garage workshop. The garage smelled like pine wood and warm oil. Sunbeams made dusty sparkles in the air. Coco’s whiskers wiggled with curiosity.

“Me-ow! What is all this?” Coco asked, peeking around.

On the workbench, there were shiny screws, a small hammer, gentle sandpaper, and colorful wooden pieces. There was a buzzing sound from a tiny fan and a soft drip-drip from a leaky sink. Coco’s ears perked up. Coco saw a big, empty space on a shelf and a note that said, “Build a little cart to carry seeds to the garden.”

“A cart! I can try,” said Coco with a brave smile. “I want to help.”

Coco looked around. There were wheels in a blue box, and there was a yellow rope on a hook. There were wooden boards stacked neatly. Coco put a paw on a smooth board. It felt cool. Coco sniffed. It smelled like a tree after rain.

Coco’s heart thumped. This was new. Coco felt a tiny flutter of worry. “What if I make a mistake?” Coco whispered.

Then Coco took a deep breath. In went the air. Out went the fear. “I can be brave,” Coco said. “Being brave means trying, even when I feel a little scared.”

Coco made a plan. “First, I will think. Then, I will try. If it’s hard, I will try again.”

Coco drew a simple picture on a scrap of paper: a box, four wheels, and a rope to pull. “Good plan,” Coco purred.

Coco checked the pieces. “Four wheels, one, two, three, four.” The wheels were cool and smooth. They rolled with a gentle rattle. “Two axles,” said Coco, tapping them. “One box for seeds.” Coco found a small wooden crate. It smelled sweet like fresh wood.

Coco put on tiny safety goggles. “Safety first,” Coco said, feeling proud and brave.

“Need help?” asked a friendly voice. It was Pip the mouse, peeking from behind a paint can.

“Hi, Pip,” said Coco. “I’m building a seed cart. I’m a little nervous, but I can be brave.”

“I can hand you screws,” said Pip with a squeak.

“Thank you!” said Coco.

Coco lined up the crate. “Hmm. The wheels must go here.” Coco held an axle under the crate. It wobbled and fell with a clink.

“Oh no,” said Coco. Coco’s tail drooped. “It fell.”

Pip squeaked, “It’s okay. Try again. You are brave.”

Coco breathed in. “Being brave means I keep going.” Coco tried again. This time, Coco used a soft pencil to mark two dots. “I will make the spots even.” Coco gently tapped with the hammer—tap, tap, tap. Coco felt the wood vibrate and heard the happy little taps.

Coco slid the axle through the marks and added two wheels. They spun with a quiet whoosh. “It works!” Coco cheered.

“Yay!” squeaked Pip, clapping tiny paws.

Coco added the second axle and two more wheels. The cart rolled a little to the left. “Hmm,” said Coco. “It turns funny.”

Coco sat and thought. Coco put a paw on the cart and watched the wheels. “I see. One wheel is loose.” Coco tightened the small nut with a soft twist. “Click,” it went. Now the cart rolled straight. Coco smiled.

Coco tied the yellow rope to the front. “This will help me pull.” The rope felt rough on Coco’s paw, but it was strong.

Just then, the garage light flickered. Shadows danced on the walls. The drip-drip seemed louder. Coco felt a pinch of fear. “It’s a little dark,” Coco said.

Pip looked worried too. “Maybe we should stop?”

Coco’s whiskers shook. “I feel scared, but I can be brave. I will turn on the lamp.” Coco reached up and clicked on a small desk lamp. Warm light made a soft glow. “There. Better.”

Coco and Pip gently sanded the edges. The sandpaper made a shh-shh sound, and the wood turned silky. They painted a bright red stripe down the side. The paint smelled a little strong, so they opened the window. Fresh air blew in. It felt cool and nice.

“Time to test,” said Coco. Coco placed a tiny bag of seeds in the cart. “Ready?”

Pip hopped in to watch. “Ready!”

Coco pulled the rope. The cart rolled across the floor—smooth, steady, brave. “It works!” Coco purred. The cart went over a little bump. It wobbled. Coco slowed down and guided it carefully. “Slow and steady,” Coco said. “I can do it.”

Coco and Pip brought the cart to the garden door. Sunshine poured in. The birds sang, “Tweet-tweet!” The cart shone with its red stripe.

Coco’s heart felt big and warm. “I did it,” said Coco. “I was brave. I tried, I thought, and I did not give up.”

Pip beamed. “You were very brave, and very clever!”

Coco nodded. “Being brave means trying new things, asking for help, and keeping safe. It also means I can build, learn, and help others.”

Coco pushed the seeds into the sunlight. “Let’s plant,” Coco said, happy and proud.

The End.
