# Ellie's Sharing Tree Adventure

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved her special treehouse. It sat high up in the biggest oak tree in the forest, with wooden walls painted sunshine yellow and a roof made of green leaves. Every morning, Ellie would climb up the rope ladder with her trunk, her big feet making soft *thump-thump* sounds on each wooden step.

Today was extra special because Mama had packed Ellie's favorite snacks in her red polka-dot basket. There were crunchy apples that smelled like sweet sunshine, golden bananas that felt smooth and curved, and tiny blueberries that popped like little bubbles in her mouth.

"What a perfect day for a treehouse picnic!" Ellie said happily, swinging her trunk as she arranged her treats on the small wooden table.

Just as Ellie was about to take her first big bite of apple, she heard a tiny voice below.

"Excuse me," squeaked the voice. "That smells so yummy!"

Ellie looked down through the treehouse window and saw a little gray mouse sitting at the bottom of the tree. The mouse's tummy made a loud *grumble-rumble* sound.

"Oh my!" said Ellie. "Are you hungry, little friend?"

"Yes," said the mouse sadly. "I've been looking for food all morning, but I can't find anything. My name is Max."

Ellie's kind heart felt warm and fuzzy. She remembered what Mama always said: "When we share our good things, happiness grows like flowers in spring."

"Would you like to come up and share my snacks?" Ellie called down. "There's plenty for both of us!"

Max's tiny eyes sparkled with joy. "Really? You would share with me?"

"Of course!" Ellie lowered her trunk like a gentle elevator. "Hop on!"

Up, up, up went Max, giggling with delight as Ellie's trunk carried him safely to the treehouse. The wooden floor felt warm under Max's tiny paws from the morning sunshine streaming through the windows.

"Wow!" Max gasped, looking around at the cozy treehouse. "This is the most beautiful place I've ever seen!"

Ellie felt proud and happy. She cut her big apple into small pieces with her special plastic knife. The apple made a satisfying *crunch* sound and smelled even sweeter when shared.

"Here you go, Max," Ellie said, offering him a perfect apple slice. "And some blueberries too!"

Max nibbled the apple carefully. "Mmm! This is the most delicious apple in the whole wide world! Thank you, Ellie."

As they ate together, Max told Ellie about all the places he had traveled - through flower gardens where bees buzzed busy songs, across streams that sparkled like diamonds, and under bushes where butterflies danced in rainbow colors.

"The world is so big and full of friends we haven't met yet," said Max wisely.

Ellie nodded, her ears flapping gently. "And when we're kind and share, we make new friends everywhere we go!"

After their lovely meal, Max helped Ellie clean up by carrying tiny crumbs in his small paws. They laughed and played games until the sun began to set, painting the sky in soft pink and orange colors.

"Thank you for sharing with me, Ellie," said Max as Ellie lowered him back down the tree. "You made my day magical!"

"Thank you for being my friend, Max!" Ellie called. "Come back anytime!"

That night, as Ellie snuggled in her cozy bed, she felt extra happy and warm inside. She had learned that sharing her good things didn't make them smaller - it made her joy bigger and brighter, just like Mama said.

The End.
