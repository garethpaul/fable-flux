# Ellie Speaks Up

In a warm, cozy nursery, a gentle elephant calf named Ellie sat on a soft, blue rug. The room smelled like clean blankets and sweet soap. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. A lullaby hummed softly from a tiny music box, and the air felt calm and safe.

Ellie loved the nursery. There were blocks, soft pillows, and a big basket of books. Her favorite was a picture book with a rainbow on the cover. Ellie’s ears were large and floppy, and her feet made little thump-thump sounds when she walked. She liked to hum and sway her tail when she felt happy.

One morning, Ellie wanted to build a tall tower. She picked up red, blue, and yellow blocks. Tap, tap, tap—they stacked up high. “I’m making a rainbow tower,” Ellie said to herself, smiling.

Her friend, a tiny mouse named Mip, hurried over. “Blocks! Blocks!” squeaked Mip. He reached for the red block Ellie was using.

Ellie felt a twist in her tummy. “Um,” she said softly, “I’m using that block.”

Mip didn’t hear her. He tugged the block. The tower wobbled. Wiggle-wobble—crash! The blocks fell with a clatter. Ellie’s eyes filled with tears. Her ears drooped, and her chest felt tight.

Mip blinked. “Oh,” he said, holding the red block. “Blocks fell.”

Ellie sniffled. “I feel sad,” she whispered. “I worked hard.”

Mip’s whiskers drooped. “I didn’t know,” he said. “I only heard the blocks, not your words.”

Just then, Nani, the gentle nursery caregiver, walked over. She smelled like warm cookies and wore a soft, green sweater. “I see blocks on the floor,” Nani said in a calm voice. “And I see two friends with big feelings.”

Ellie took a deep breath. In… out. Nani knelt down so her eyes were level with Ellie and Mip. “Let’s use our talking tools,” Nani said. “We can use our words, our ears, and our kind hearts.”

Ellie wiped her eyes with the tip of her trunk. “I want to say what I feel,” she said.

“That’s brave,” Nani smiled. “Use ‘I’ words.”

Ellie looked at Mip. “I feel sad when my tower falls,” she said. “I was using the red block.”

Mip’s ears perked up. He listened. “I hear you,” he said slowly. “I didn’t listen before. I feel sorry.”

Nani nodded. “Thank you for listening, Mip. Thank you for speaking up, Ellie. Now, let’s solve the problem together.”

Ellie’s tummy felt better. The music box played a gentle ding-ding tune. The sunlight felt warm on her back. “Maybe,” Ellie said, “we can make a tower together.”

Mip’s eyes sparkled. “We can! We can!” he squeaked. “Can I ask first?”

“Yes,” Ellie said. “Please ask me.”

Mip held up the red block. “Ellie, can I use this red block?”

Ellie smiled. “Yes! After I place this blue one.” She gently set the blue block down. “Your turn.”

Mip placed the red block carefully. Tap. “Thank you for telling me,” he said. “I like when you use your words.”

Nani clapped softly. “You are both using kind words and calm bodies. That helps our wellness. How do your bodies feel now?”

Ellie wiggled her toes on the rug. “I feel calm. My tummy is not twisty.”

“I feel light,” said Mip, hopping once. “Like a feather.”

They stacked more blocks. “My turn?” asked Mip.

“Yes,” Ellie said. “Your turn.”

“Can I help?” Mip asked when a tall piece was hard to steady.

“Yes, let’s hold it together,” Ellie replied. They both used gentle hands. The tower grew higher and brighter. Red, blue, yellow, green. It looked like a candy rainbow.

A breeze from the window made the curtains swish. The ribbon on Nani’s sweater fluttered. The tower wobbled a little.

“Oops,” said Ellie. “I feel a little worried.”

“What can we do?” asked Nani.

“Let’s talk,” Ellie said. “Mip, can you hold the bottom while I put the top?”

“I can!” Mip said. “I am strong for a mouse.”

Together they counted. “One, two, three.” Ellie placed the last block. Mip held steady. The tower stood tall and proud.

“We did it!” Ellie cheered. Her ears flapped with joy. Mip squeaked happily and did a tiny dance.

Nani smiled. “You used good communication. You asked. You listened. You took turns. You helped each other. How does that feel?”

“It feels warm like the sun,” Ellie said.

“It feels smooth like this block,” Mip said, rubbing the yellow piece.

They sat on the soft rug and looked at their tower. Ellie’s heart felt soft and full. “Mip,” she said, “next time, if you want a block, you can say, ‘May I have a turn?’”

Mip nodded. “And if I don’t hear, you can say, ‘Mip, please listen.’ We can keep our bodies calm.”

Ellie giggled. “Yes. Calm bodies, kind words, good listening.”

Nani placed a small star sticker on each of them. “Stars for strong communication,” she said.

Ellie and Mip hugged. Their tower sparkled in the sunlight. The nursery felt extra cozy and safe. They picked up the extra blocks and put them in the basket, working side by side.

Ellie whispered, “Talking helps.”

Mip whispered back, “Listening helps too.”

The End.
