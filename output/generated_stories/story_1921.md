# Benny Bunny Helps the Art Room

Benny Bunny was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved to hop-hop-hop and help-help-help. One sunny morning, Benny visited the community art room. The door was open, and he smelled paint that was sweet like berries and glue that was a little bit sour. Inside, colorful paper fluttered like leaves, crayons rolled like tiny logs, and brushes lay in sleepy puddles of water.

“Hello?” Benny called, twitching his nose. “Is anyone here?”

Ms. Lila, the art teacher with paint on her apron and a warm smile, waved. “Hi, Benny! We are getting ready for the neighborhood art show.”

Children gathered around: Mia, with blue sparkles on her cheeks. Leo, with smudgy green fingers. Suni, with a ribbon in her hair. They all looked excited, but the room looked very, very messy.

Benny’s ears stood up tall. “I want to help!” he said. “I am a helper rabbit.”

Ms. Lila nodded. “Thank you, Benny. We want to make art for our neighbors. We will hang pictures at the library and the bakery. But first, we need to clean and organize the art room, so we can find things. Can you listen and help us make a plan?”

Benny took a big breath. He put his paw on his chest to remember: Listen first. Help next. “I’m listening,” he said.

Ms. Lila clapped softly. “Step one: Pick up crayons and sort by color. Step two: Wash and dry the brushes. Step three: Put paper in neat stacks. Step four: Make a shared art box for the show.”

Benny nodded. “I can do it!”

Mia giggled. “Let’s sing while we work!” She sang a silly tune: “Red with red and blue with blue. Clean the brushes, one and two!”

Benny hopped to the crayons. He heard them click and clack as they rolled on the table. “Wait, crayons,” he said kindly. “You need a home.” He found small cups. “Red here, blue here, yellow here.” He listened to Ms. Lila’s steps. He sorted slowly and carefully. His paws felt waxy and warm. “I am organizing,” he whispered, proud.

Leo brought a bucket of water. “Brush time!”

Benny dipped a brush. “Swish-swish,” it went. The water turned swirly purple. “Oh!” Benny said. “The brushes like a bath.”

Suni handed him a soft towel. “Dry, please.”

Benny patted the brushes dry. “Pat-pat. Pat-pat.” The bristles felt tickly. He lined them up like little soldiers. “Stand tall, brushes. You look ready.”

Ms. Lila smiled. “Great listening, Benny. What is step three?”

Benny touched his ear. “Paper in neat stacks.” He gathered big paper, medium paper, and tiny paper. The paper made a whisper sound: shhh-shhh. He stacked them neatly. He even made a sign. He wrote in big letters: PAPER. He drew a star. “So everyone can see,” he said.

Mia cheered. “We can find everything now!”

Then they heard a bump. A tower of glue sticks toppled, and the glitter box slid open. Sparkles sprinkled the floor like little stars. Benny’s eyes grew wide. “Oh no!”

A little worry hopped into Benny’s tummy. He wanted to fix it fast, but he remembered to listen. He turned to Ms. Lila. “What should we do?”

Ms. Lila took a breath. “We will clean together. Step by step.”

Benny nodded. “I will sweep the sparkles.” He held a tiny broom. “Swish, swish.” The sparkles made soft crunchy sounds. Leo picked up the glue sticks. Mia closed the glitter box gently. Suni wiped the table with a warm, soapy cloth that smelled like lemons.

Soon the floor shone. The room felt calm, like a quiet hug.

“Now step four,” Ms. Lila said. “Our shared art box for the show. It will go to the library and the bakery. Everyone can make art there.”

Benny’s whiskers twitched. “Community service means we help our neighbors,” he said. “We share our things. We make it easy for others.”

“Yes,” said Ms. Lila. “That is community service.”

They filled the box with crayons, clean brushes, and neat paper. Benny added stickers and friendly signs. He drew pictures of hands helping hands. He added labels: BRUSHES, CRAYONS, PAPER. He used bright colors so little kids could see.

Mia clapped. “We did it together!”

But then Benny saw a small bunny drawing on the floor. It was wrinkly and torn. He picked it up gently. “This is mine,” he whispered. His ears drooped. “It is messy.”

Ms. Lila knelt beside him. “You listened. You helped. You organized. You can try again.”

Benny smoothed the paper. He took a breath. He dipped a clean brush. The bristles felt soft and ready. Stroke by stroke, he painted a big heart. He added a sunny yellow circle. He drew many small hands around the heart. He drew a rabbit giving a crayon to a friend.

Suni peered over. “I like your heart.”

Leo nodded. “It shows helping.”

Mia sang, “Sharing is caring. Helping is kind.”

Benny smiled. “Yes. That is our message.”

They carried the art box to the door. It felt heavy, but they held it together. Step by step, hop by hop, they moved safely. Ms. Lila opened the door. Fresh air flowed in, cool and clean. Benny smelled grass and sunshine.

At the library, the librarian beamed. “Thank you for this art box!”

At the bakery, the baker waved a floury hand. “The children will make beautiful pictures while they wait.”

Benny’s chest felt warm and proud. “We helped our neighbors,” he said. “We listened. We kept things in order. Now everyone can make art.”

Ms. Lila patted his shoulder. “Benny, you are a determined helper. You showed community service, organization, and wonderful listening.”

Benny’s ears stood tall. “I can help again tomorrow,” he said.

“Me too!” said Mia, Leo, and Suni.

Benny grinned. “We can help together.”

The End.
