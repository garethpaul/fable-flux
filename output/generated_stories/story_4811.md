# Ollie's Porch Art Day

Ollie the otter sat on the sunny front porch. His soft brown fur felt warm in the gentle morning light. He had lots of art supplies spread around him - colorful paints, brushes, and big white paper.

"Today I'm going to paint all day long!" Ollie said excitedly. He dipped his brush in bright blue paint and started making swirls on the paper.

The porch was peaceful and quiet. Butterflies danced in the flower garden. A soft breeze made the wind chimes sing gentle songs. Ollie painted happy pictures of fish swimming in the river.

After a while, Ollie's tummy started to rumble. "Grumble, grumble," it said loudly.

"Oh no," Ollie said. "I'm hungry, but I don't want to stop painting. My picture is so pretty!"

Ollie kept painting. He added green seaweed and yellow sunshine to his picture. But his tummy rumbled even louder. "GRUMBLE, GRUMBLE!"

Then Ollie felt very thirsty too. His mouth felt dry like sand. But he still didn't want to stop painting.

"Just a few more fish," Ollie said to himself. He painted one more fish, then another, then another.

Soon Ollie felt dizzy. His paws started to shake. The paintbrush felt heavy in his hand.

"Oh my," said Mama Otter as she came onto the porch. "Ollie, you look tired! Have you eaten lunch yet?"

"No," Ollie said quietly. "I wanted to keep painting. My picture is almost perfect!"

Mama Otter sat down next to Ollie. She looked at his beautiful painting. "Wow! This is wonderful, Ollie. But your body needs food and water to stay healthy and strong."

"But what if I lose my good ideas?" Ollie asked.

"Your good ideas will still be there after you take care of yourself," Mama said gently. "When we eat good food and drink water, our brains work even better!"

Ollie looked at his painting, then at his empty tummy. "Maybe you're right, Mama."

"How about this," Mama suggested. "Let's make a plan. You can paint for a little while, then take a break to eat and drink. Then you can paint some more!"

Ollie's eyes lit up. "That sounds like a good compromise!"

They went inside together. Ollie washed his paws with soap and warm water. The water felt good on his tired hands. Then he ate a yummy sandwich and drank a big glass of cool water.

"Ahh," Ollie said, feeling much better. "My tummy feels happy now!"

After his break, Ollie went back to the porch. He felt strong and full of energy again. His hands were steady, and his mind was clear.

"Now I can paint my best fish ever!" Ollie said happily.

He painted beautiful fish with bright scales and happy smiles. The colors looked even more beautiful than before.

When the sun started to set, Ollie looked at all his paintings. They covered the whole porch!

"Taking care of myself helped me paint even better pictures," Ollie realized. "When I eat good food and rest, I can do my best work!"

From that day on, Ollie always remembered to take care of his body while doing the things he loved. He learned that the best art comes when we feel healthy and strong.

The End.
