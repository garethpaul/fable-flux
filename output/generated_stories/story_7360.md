# Ellie's Big Cleanup

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved playing in her cozy nursery. The soft yellow walls were painted with dancing butterflies, and fluffy clouds hung from the ceiling. Her favorite toys were scattered everywhere - colorful blocks, squeaky balls, and her special teddy bear named Peanut.

Every morning, Mama Elephant would remind Ellie, "Remember, sweetie, after playtime comes cleanup time. Everything has its special place."

"Okay, Mama!" Ellie would trumpet cheerfully. But when playtime was over, Ellie often forgot all about putting her toys away.

One sunny Tuesday morning, Ellie was having the most wonderful time. She built tall towers with her blocks - *click, clack, stack!* She rolled her red ball across the soft carpet - *bounce, bounce, roll!* She snuggled with Peanut and told him stories about jungle adventures.

"Ellie, it's time for your nap," called Mama Elephant from the doorway. "Please put your toys away first."

But Ellie was so tired from playing. Her little elephant eyes felt heavy, and all she wanted to do was curl up in her comfy bed. "I'll do it later, Mama," she yawned, leaving all her toys scattered across the floor.

When naptime was over, Ellie woke up excited to play. But as she stepped out of bed - *CRASH!* - she tripped over her blocks. "Ouch!" she cried, rubbing her trunk.

Then Mama Elephant came in to check on her. *SQUISH!* Mama accidentally stepped on Ellie's squeaky ball. "Oh my!" said Mama, looking around at the messy room. "This isn't safe for anyone."

Ellie's ears drooped low. She felt a heavy feeling in her tummy - not the good kind from eating peanuts, but the sad kind that comes when you know you've made a mistake.

"Mama, I'm sorry," Ellie said in a small voice. "I didn't put my toys away like I promised. Now you got hurt, and I got hurt too."

Mama Elephant's eyes were kind but serious. "Thank you for saying sorry, little one. But saying sorry is just the first step. What can you do to make things better?"

Ellie thought hard, swishing her little tail. "I can clean up my toys right now! And I can be more careful next time!"

"That's exactly right," smiled Mama. "Making amends means fixing what went wrong."

Ellie got right to work. She picked up each colorful block - red ones, blue ones, yellow ones - and stacked them neatly in their special box. *Clink, clink, clink!* went the blocks as they found their home.

She rolled her ball to the toy basket - *bounce, roll, plop!* - and tucked Peanut safely on her bookshelf where he could watch over the room.

"There!" said Ellie, looking around her tidy nursery. The soft sunlight streaming through the window made everything sparkle clean. "Now it's safe for everyone!"

"I'm proud of you for making things right," said Mama, giving Ellie a warm trunk hug. "You showed responsibility and kindness."

From that day on, Ellie remembered her cleanup routine. Every time she finished playing, she would sing her special song: "Toys away, toys away, everything goes in its place today!"

And whenever she made a mistake, Ellie knew just what to do - say sorry and work to make things better.

The End.
