# Moonlight Mornings on the Rooftop

The city was quiet, and the moon was round and bright. On a cozy rooftop garden, a wise old owl named Ollie blinked his big golden eyes. The night air smelled like mint leaves and rain. The soft wind tickled his feathers.

“Whooo,” said Ollie, stretching his wings. “It’s almost time.”

Every night, Ollie had a routine. He checked the stars, counted three potted plants, and hummed a gentle song. He greeted whoever came to the rooftop. This made everyone feel safe. This made Ollie feel calm. It was his Moonlight Morning, because night was his morning.

But tonight, something was different.

A small gray mouse peeked from behind a brick. “H-hi, I’m Miri,” she squeaked. Her tiny whiskers trembled. “I want to learn the night routine. But I’m scared of the dark.”

Ollie blinked kindly. “Welcome, Miri. You are included. Our rooftop is for everyone.”

Just then, a shy black kitten named Nib appeared, his paws silent on the tiles. “Can I learn too?” he whispered. “I’m new here.”

“And me,” croaked a green frog named Pop, hopping from a flowerpot. “I lose my way at night. Maybe a routine can help.”

Ollie puffed softly with pride. “Yes,” he said. “We’ll learn together. We start with a gentle plan. A routine is a friendly map.”

He tapped his talon on the rooftop. “First, we listen.”

Everyone grew quiet. The wind whispered. Leaves rustled. A faraway bell rang, ding-dong.

“I hear the bell,” said Miri. She smiled a little.

“Second, we look,” Ollie said. He pointed with a wing. “See the big star? We say hello to the North Star.”

Nib squinted up. “Hello, bright one,” he said, his voice tiny but brave.

“Third,” Ollie continued, “we count our plants. One mint, two lavender, three tomatoes.”

Pop hopped to each pot. “One, two, three!” His throat bubbled with a happy croak.

“Fourth, we sing our Rooftop Song,” said Ollie. “It goes soft and slow.” He hummed, whooo-hooo, hum-hum, and the friends hummed along. The tune felt warm, like a blanket.

“Last,” Ollie said, “we check feelings. We say how we feel, and we help each other.”

Miri took a breath. “I feel a little scared,” she confessed.

Nib nodded. “I feel shy.”

Pop wiggled his toes. “I feel bouncy and lost.”

Ollie bowed his head. “I feel proud to be with you. Thank you for sharing. That is courage.”

They practiced again: listen, look, count, sing, share. The stars shimmered. The moon slid through a thin cloud. The night felt friendly.

Then, a sudden gust of wind whooshed across the rooftop. The mint plant tipped. A garden light flickered and went out. The shadows grew long. Miri jumped. Nib tucked his tail. Pop hid behind a pot.

“Oh no!” Miri squeaked. “The dark is bigger now!”

Ollie’s feathers ruffled, but his voice was calm. “We have a routine,” he said. “Let’s use it.”

“First, we listen,” Nib whispered. They all listened. The wind calmed to a soft hush-hush.

“Second, we look,” Pop said. “We look together.” Ollie pointed his wing to the moon. The silver light made a slow path on the tiles.

“Third, we count our plants,” Miri said, her voice stronger. “One, two, three… Are you okay, little mint?” She patted the pot, and Nib steadied it with his paw.

“Fourth, we sing,” Ollie hummed. The Rooftop Song floated in the air. Whooo-hooo, hum-hum. Pop croaked along. The flickering light glowed steady again.

“Last, we share feelings,” Ollie said gently.

Miri took a slow breath. “I still feel a little scared,” she admitted, “but I also feel brave.”

Nib blinked. “I feel helpful,” he said, smiling. “My paws can steady things.”

Pop puffed proudly. “I feel found. I know what to do!”

Ollie spread his wings wide. “You used the routine. You used your courage. You helped each other. That is inclusion,” he said. “Everyone’s voice matters. Everyone has a place.”

They repeated the steps once more, just to be sure. Listen… look… count… sing… share. Each time, the rooftop felt safer. The air smelled like mint and hope. The moon peeked from the cloud, bright again.

Miri stood taller. “Can this be our new nightly plan?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Ollie. “A good routine is like a friend. It helps us when things are easy, and it helps us when things are hard.”

Nib purred softly. “I like our plan,” he said. “It tells me what to do.”

Pop clapped his webby hands. “Our plan helps me remember the way.”

Ollie nodded. “Tomorrow night, we will do it again. And if you forget, we will do it again. Practice makes strong paths in our hearts.”

They all looked up at the North Star once more. The city lights twinkled below. The rooftop felt warm and safe, even under the cool night sky.

“Thank you, Ollie,” Miri said. “I’m not just scared. I’m also brave.”

“You are both,” said the wise old owl. “And you are part of our rooftop family.”

They hummed the Rooftop Song one last time. Whooo-hooo, hum-hum. The wind swayed the plants like a quiet dance.

Their routine was simple. Their courage was real. And everyone was included.

The End.
