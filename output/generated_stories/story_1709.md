# Polly’s Colorful Laundry Day

Polly was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a curious mind. She loved to chirp, “Squawk! I can make new things!” Today, Polly waddled into the laundry room. The room was warm and smelled like clean soap. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer went, “Thump, thump, thump.” The floor felt cool under Polly’s little feet.

“Hello, clothes!” Polly sang. “Time to wash and share!” In a basket, there were socks, shirts, and a rainbow of tiny towels. Some towels were soft and fluffy. Some were flat and thin. Some had dots, and some had stripes. Polly smiled. “So many kinds! All different. All welcome.”

Her friends, a small group of stuffed animal buddies, watched from a shelf. There was Bear with brown fur, Kitty with blue whiskers, and Bunny with long, floppy ears. They liked to pretend wash their little scarves and hats.

Bear said, “Polly, I need the big fluffy towel. I am the biggest.” Kitty wiggled her whiskers. “I want the same towel too!” Bunny hopped. “Me too!”

Polly’s wings twitched. “Oh… we all want the same towel?” She looked at the pile. There was only one big, fluffy rainbow towel. Polly felt her tummy get tight. Her feathers puffed up. She wanted to help everyone. But how?

“I feel frustrated,” Polly whispered. “Squawk.” She wanted to grab the towel fast. Her beak opened, and then… she paused.

Polly remembered her calm steps. She had learned them when the toaster once got stuck. “Stop. Breathe. Think.” She took a slow breath in, like smelling warm bread. She let it out, like blowing a bubble. In, out. In, out. Her shoulders softened.

Polly spoke softly, “Friends, I feel frustrated. We all want the same thing. It feels unfair.”

Bear nodded. “I feel grumpy.” Kitty said, “My whiskers are twitchy.” Bunny whispered, “I feel wobbly.”

Polly listened. “Thank you for telling me. Your feelings matter. All our feelings matter.” The washer hummed. “Whirr, whirr.” The sound was steady, like a kind song.

Polly thought. “We can make a fair plan,” she said. “But first, let’s see what we have.” She looked at the towels. Big ones, small ones, dotted ones, striped ones. So many shapes and colors. “We are all different,” she said. “And the towels are different too. That can help us.”

Kitty asked, “How?”

Polly’s eyes sparkled. “Innovation time! Let’s make the towels into a team.” She flapped her wings and gathered supplies: clothespins, a soft rope, and a little bell. “Squawk! We can make a towel train.”

“A towel train?” Bunny perked up.

“Yes!” Polly said. “We clip one towel to another. We take turns sitting on different parts. Everyone gets soft. Everyone gets comfy. And we can share the big, fluffy one.”

Bear tilted his head. “Will it be fair?”

Polly nodded. “We will use a timer. When the dryer goes ‘ding!’ we switch spots. Everyone gets a turn on the fluffy car.”

Bear smiled. “That sounds fair.” Kitty purred. Bunny clapped.

Polly worked carefully. She clipped the striped towel to the dotted towel, and the dotted towel to the big, fluffy rainbow towel. She tied the soft rope like a train track across the floor. The dryer thumped, “Thump, thump,” as if saying, “Go, go.”

“All aboard!” Polly chirped. “Bear, you start on the fluffy car. Kitty on the striped car. Bunny on the dotted car. When the dryer dings, we switch.”

They climbed on. The towels felt warm from the dryer. The fluffy towel hugged Bear like a cloud. The striped towel felt smooth under Kitty’s paws. The dotted towel tickled Bunny’s toes. Polly rang the little bell. “Choo-choo!”

They took a ride. Polly gently tugged the rope. The towel train slid across the tiles. “Squeak,” went a clothespin. “Whirr,” sang the washer. The smell of lemon soap filled the air.

“Ding!” went the dryer. Polly smiled. “Switch!” Everyone moved one spot. Now Kitty snuggled into the fluffy towel. “Mmm, cozy.” “Ding!” They switched again. Now Bunny got the fluffy car. “So soft!” Bear was on the striped towel and smiled anyway. “I like this one too.”

When the ride was done, Polly asked, “How do you feel now?”

Bear said, “I feel calm.” Kitty said, “I feel included.” Bunny said, “I feel happy.” Polly nodded. “I feel proud. We used calm steps. We shared. We were fair.”

Polly gathered her friends close. “We are all different. Big, small, whiskery, floppy, feathery. That makes our team strong.” She pointed to the towels. “These are different too. Dots, stripes, rainbow. All are useful. Together, they made something new.”

Bear hugged the fluffy towel and then handed it to Kitty. “Your turn.” Kitty passed it to Bunny. Bunny passed it to Polly. Polly pressed her cheek to the soft fabric. “Thank you,” she said. “Sharing feels good.”

Polly looked at the laundry room. It was warm and bright. The basket was empty. The towels were folded neatly in a rainbow stack. The room smelled like clean cotton and lemons. The washer hummed a sleepy tune.

Polly chirped, “When we feel frustrated, we can stop. We can breathe. We can think. We can make a fair plan. We can include everyone.” Her friends nodded and chimed together, “We can share!”

They all did a happy wiggle. The dryer gave one last tiny, “Ding!” It sounded like a cheer.

The End.
