# Cub’s Big Home Office Game

In a sunny home office, with a soft rug and a humming computer, lived a friendly bear cub named Cub. The walls were bright with pictures from around the world: a kite from India, a drum from Ghana, a fan from Japan, a soccer scarf from Brazil, and a dancing shoe from Spain. Cub loved to visit Mama Bear’s home office. It smelled like warm tea and paper. The keyboard went click-click, and the printer purred like a kitten.

One rainy day, Cub peered in. “Mama, can I play?” Cub asked, swishing a little tail and hugging a fuzzy ball.

Mama smiled. “I am on a long call, sweet Cub. I need quiet,” she whispered, putting a finger to her lips. “Maybe later we can play.”

Cub’s ears drooped. “I feel wiggly,” Cub said softly. The rain tapped on the window, tap-tap-tap. The chair squeaked. Cub’s paws felt itchy to move. Inside, Cub felt a little storm of frustration. The storm said, “I want to play now!”

Cub took a deep breath. “Smell the tea. Hear the tap-tap,” Cub told self. “I can calm my body.” Cub had learned to breathe in like smelling a flower, and out like blowing a candle. In and out. The little storm got smaller.

Cub looked around the home office. There was space by the cozy rug. There were the world pictures. There was a stack of soft scarves. Cub had an idea. “I can use my own idea. I can start something kind. I can take initiative!” Cub whispered. The word felt big and brave in Cub’s mouth: “In-ni-sha-tive.”

Cub tiptoed and gathered a scarf from each picture. The green scarf for the soccer game from Brazil. The red scarf for dancing from Spain. The blue scarf like the sky for kites from India. The yellow scarf like sunny drums from Ghana. The white scarf for cool fans from Japan.

Cub laid the scarves in a circle on the rug. “I will make a quiet, gentle game,” Cub said. “A World Sports Circle!” Cub’s eyes sparkled. Cub placed a soft note for Mama: “I am playing a quiet game. Love, Cub.”

Cub began the game with a whisper. “Now we try sports from around the world. Quiet and gentle,” Cub told self, patting the fuzzy ball. “Ready, set, brave!”

First, Cub stepped onto the green scarf. “Soccer,” Cub whispered. Tap, tap, tap. Cub used gentle paws to roll the ball with feet, side to side, slow like a turtle. “Goal!” Cub breathed, but very soft. Cub imagined children in Brazil cheering, “Goooal!” Cub lifted paws like the scarf was a flag. Cub felt proud.

Next, Cub twirled on the red scarf. “Flamenco dance,” Cub said. Swish, swish! Cub stomped lightly—tap! tap!—and swirled the scarf like a cape, then froze like a statue. Cub smiled and whispered, “Olé!” The swish felt like wind. Cub felt brave and strong.

On the blue scarf, Cub held a paper sheet like a kite. “Kite flying,” Cub said. Whoosh. Cub waved arms slowly, making the paper kite glide through air. “Namaste, kite,” Cub whispered, thinking of friends in India who love colorful kites. The air felt cool on Cub’s nose.

Then came the yellow scarf. “Drumming,” Cub said. But the office needed quiet. Cub put paws on knees and made a soft pat-pat rhythm, like gentle rain on leaves. Pat-pat, pat. Cub hummed a warm tune, thinking of drum circles in Ghana and friends who love to share music. Cub’s chest buzzed like a happy bee.

On the white scarf, Cub fanned gently with a paper fan. “Fan dance,” Cub whispered. Flutter, flutter. The paper clicked softly. Cub bowed and thought of friends in Japan who dance with fans. Cub felt calm and smooth, like a little stream.

Cub was doing it! Cub had turned wiggly feelings into a quiet game. But then the fuzzy ball rolled under the desk. It bumped the printer. The printer shook and beeped. Beep! Beep!

Cub froze. “Oh no,” Cub said. The little storm tried to grow again. “I feel scared,” Cub whispered. Cub placed a paw on heart. “I can be brave and kind. I can fix it,” Cub told self.

Cub took a deep breath. Smell the tea. Blow the candle. In. Out. Cub crawled slowly under the desk. It was dark and smelled like dust and crayons. Cub’s fur brushed the carpet. “Hello, ball,” Cub whispered. “Come back, please.” Cub reached and got it, then gently moved the printer cord back to its place, careful and slow. The beeping stopped.

Mama Bear ended her call and turned around. She saw the scarf circle, the soft note, and Cub crawling out with the ball.

“Mama, I made a quiet World Sports Circle,” Cub said, eyes wide. “I played soccer, danced flamenco, flew a kite, made soft drum beats, and did a fan dance. I used initiative. I fixed a beep, too.”

Mama’s eyes shone. “Cub, you showed initiative. You had a good idea, and you started it yourself. You were brave and careful. You respected our quiet home office. And you enjoyed sports and dances from many cultures. I am so proud.”

Cub’s cheeks felt warm. “I felt frustrated,” Cub said. “I breathed. I made a plan. I tried. I kept trying.”

Mama hugged Cub. The hug was big and cozy, like a warm blanket. “That is how we learn,” Mama said. “When we feel wiggly or stuck, we can breathe, make a kind plan, and take the first step. That is initiative. And trying new things from around the world helps us grow.”

“Can we play together now?” Cub asked.

“Yes,” Mama said. “I will be the kite, and you be the wind.”

Cub giggled. Whoosh! Together they played, soft and happy. The rain kept tapping, but inside the home office, Cub felt bright like sunshine.

The End.
