# Leo Listens to His Tummy

The brave little lion cub was named Leo. Leo lived with his family in a cozy den with a bright dining room. The dining room smelled like warm corn cakes and sweet mango. The table felt smooth under Leo’s paws, and the chairs were soft and squeaky.

“Dinner time,” said Mama Lion in a gentle voice. She placed a bowl in front of Leo. “Carrot stew with soft noodles.”

Leo’s tummy made a happy glug-glug sound. He liked noodles. But the stew was orange and shiny, and Leo wasn’t sure about the carrots. He scrunched his nose. “Do I have to eat it now?” he asked.

Papa Lion smiled. “We can take our time,” he said. “Let’s be calm, like quiet rain.”

Leo looked at the bowl. He wanted to gobble the noodles, but he also wanted to roar and play tag around the chairs. His paws wiggled. His tail swished-swish. “I feel jumpy,” Leo whispered.

Mama Lion nodded. “Let’s try our calm breath. In slow like smelling flowers.” She sniffed. “And out slow like blowing bubbles.” She puffed.

Leo copied. In… snifffff. Out… pffffff. His chest felt warm. The clink of spoons and the soft hum of the ceiling fan sounded friendly now.

Baby Lila, Leo’s little sister, tapped her cup. “Tap-tap,” she sang. “Soup is warm!” Steam curled like tiny clouds. Leo giggled. “It smells sweet,” he said. He lifted his spoon and touched the stew. It felt cozy-hot, not too hot. He blew. “Ffffff.” He took one tiny sip. “Hmm!”

“What do you notice?” asked Papa Lion.

“It tastes like sunshine and soft carrots,” Leo said, eyes big. “And the noodles are slippery!”

He ate another small spoonful. Then he saw the mango slices on a plate. “I want mango now,” he said, reaching.

Mama Lion put a gentle paw over his. “Mango will wait,” she said softly. “Let’s practice patience.”

“What is patience?” Leo asked.

Papa Lion’s whiskers twitched. “Patience is giving things time. Like when seeds sleep in the dirt and then grow into flowers.”

Leo looked at the mango. He looked at his stew. He took a calm breath. “I can wait,” he said, sitting tall. His tail gave a small, proud flick.

Baby Lila splashed a tiny drop of stew. “Oops,” she said. A carrot landed on the table with a soft plop. Leo watched. He felt a little grumpy bubble in his chest. He wanted everything to be perfect and quick. But he remembered the quiet rain.

“In… smell the flowers,” Leo whispered to himself. “Out… blow the bubbles.” He handed Lila a napkin. “It’s okay,” he said kindly. “We can clean together.” Lila smiled and patted the napkin. “Pat-pat.”

Leo tasted more stew. “I like it more when I go slow,” he said. “My tummy feels happy.”

Papa Lion nodded. “When we are calm, we can hear our tummies and our hearts.”

Soon, the stew bowl was almost empty. Leo waited, paws in his lap, while Mama Lion passed him the mango. He noticed the mango’s golden color. He pressed his nose close. “It smells like sweet sunshine,” he said. He took a bite. The juice was cool and sticky. “Yum!”

A soft breeze came through the window. The curtains went swish-swish. Outside, the evening birds chirped. Inside, the dining room felt safe and warm. Leo’s jumpy feelings had turned into cozy feelings.

Mama Lion placed a tiny corn cake on a plate. “Would you like to spread honey?” she asked.

Leo nodded. “Yes, please.” He dipped a little spoon into the honey. It glowed like amber. “I will go slow,” he said. The honey made a quiet drizzle sound. Drip… drip… drip. He waited for the last drop. He didn’t rush. He took a small bite. Crunch, then soft. “Mmm!”

“I did it,” Leo said. His eyes sparkled. “I was patient. I was calm.”

Papa Lion ruffled Leo’s mane. “You listened to your body and your feelings. That is brave.”

After dinner, Leo helped carry the small bowls to the sink. He liked the warm water on his paws and the bubbles that tickled. “The bubbles sound like tiny pops,” he said. Pop-pop-pop! Lila giggled and clapped. “Pop!”

When the table was clean, Leo sat back down in his chair. He felt proud and peaceful. The dining room smelled like honey and soap. The fan hummed a sleepy tune.

Mama Lion looked at Leo. “What did you learn tonight, brave cub?”

Leo thought. He placed a paw on his chest. “When I feel jumpy, I can breathe slow. I can wait my turn. I can try one small taste,” he said. “Calm helps me make good choices.”

“And acceptance?” Papa Lion asked gently.

Leo smiled at his bowl. “I can accept that dinner is dinner time. I can accept carrots are different from noodles. Different can be good.”

Mama Lion kissed his forehead. “Yes,” she said. “Calmness, acceptance, and patience make our hearts feel safe.”

Leo yawned a big, soft yawn. “I am a brave, calm lion cub,” he said.

“You are,” said Papa Lion.

Leo listened to the quiet room—the soft fan, the tiny bird songs, the happy hum in his tummy. He felt ready for the rest of the evening, one calm step at a time.

The End.
