# Rainbow Posters in the Cozy Bedroom

In a cozy bedroom with soft pillows and a bright yellow rug, a determined rabbit named Rumi twitched her whiskers. The air smelled like crayons and clean sheets. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Rumi’s long ears perked up.

“Today I make a show!” Rumi said. Her voice was small but strong. “A bedtime art show for everyone.”

Rumi spread paper on the bed. She lined up paints, markers, and glue. “I can do it,” she told herself, tapping her paw. Tap-tap. “I will make posters and tickets and a stage.”

Her little lamp glowed. It made the room feel safe, like a hug.

Rumi drew a blue star. She painted a red heart. She stuck tiny cotton tails on bunny pictures. “Ta-da!” she giggled. The scissors made snip-snip sounds. The marker made a squeak as it slid across the paper.

Rumi wanted friends to come. She wanted everyone to feel welcome. But she felt a worry hop into her tummy.

“What if some friends feel left out?” Rumi whispered. Her ears drooped. “What if the bed is too high? What if someone doesn’t like loud songs?”

Rumi fluffed her pillow and thought hard. Problem, meet solution, she decided. She was a determined rabbit, after all.

First, Rumi made a quiet corner on the soft rug. She put a squishy blanket there and a tiny lamp that glowed like a firefly. “This is the Cozy Corner,” she said. “If you need quiet, you can rest here.”

Next, she made a low stage on the floor with two big books and a folded blanket. “Now little legs can step up,” she said, patting it. “No big jumps.”

Then she drew a big, colorful sign: “Everyone can join!” She added pictures of many kinds of friends—bunnies, mice, turtles, birds. Some wore glasses. Some used a little wagon. Some had big ears, some had small. All were smiling.

Rumi made tiny tickets. She used soft colors and big, friendly shapes. “Tickets for all,” she sang softly. “Blue, green, yellow—hello!”

Knock-knock!

Rumi hopped to the door. Her friends stood there in the hallway.

Milo the mouse sniffed the air. “Mmm, I smell crayons!”

Tina the turtle blinked slowly. “Is it bright inside?”

Bella the bird fluffed her feathers. “I like music. But not too loud, please.”

Rumi smiled wide. “Welcome, friends! Come in. This is our cozy bedroom. This is our show!”

They stepped on the yellow rug. The floor felt warm. The lamp gave gentle light.

Rumi handed a ticket to each friend. “You belong here,” she said, looking into their eyes. “Everyone belongs.”

Milo looked at the stage. “It’s not too tall! I can climb.” He squeaked happily.

Tina looked at the Cozy Corner. “A soft blanket. Thank you.” She touched it with her little foot and smiled.

Bella peeped at the paints. “Can I sing and paint?”

“Yes,” said Rumi. “You can sing soft or sing strong. You can paint with your wings or your feet. You can be you.”

The show began. Rumi tapped a wooden spoon on a toy drum, very gently. Tap… tap… tap. “Welcome to our bedtime art show!”

Milo went first. He told a tiny joke. “Why did the cheese cross the road? To get to the crackers!” He giggled. Everyone giggled too.

“Your joke is yummy,” said Rumi, clapping softly.

Tina slowly painted a rainbow with wide, careful strokes. Swish… swish… The room smelled sweet like paint. Tina hummed a low song. “I go slow,” she said. “Slow is good for me.”

“Slow is great,” said Rumi. “We have time.”

Bella hopped up. She sang a gentle song like a breeze. “Tweet-tweet, sleep-sleep.” Her voice floated like a feather. Rumi turned the drum down to a whisper so Bella’s song could shine.

“Beautiful,” said Rumi, her ears high with joy.

A new friend peered in, a shy hedgehog named Pip. He held a tiny pillow and looked at the floor.

“Hi, Pip,” said Rumi softly. “Do you want to watch? Do you want to draw? Do you want to sit in the Cozy Corner?”

Pip nodded at the corner. He tiptoed to the blanket and cuddled up. “I like quiet,” he murmured.

“You can still be part of our show,” Rumi said. She brought over soft crayons and big paper. “Draw from here.”

Pip drew small stars. Scratch, scratch, scratch. He held up his picture. The stars were bright and shiny with little yellow smiles.

“Ta-da,” Pip whispered.

Everyone clapped gently. Clap, clap, clap.

Rumi felt warm in her chest. “We did it,” she said. “We all joined in. We all shared our way.”

Then Rumi had one more idea. She made a “Helping Paws” sign. “If you need help, hold up your ticket,” she said. “A friend will come.”

During the last song, Tina’s paint cup rolled. It wobbled toward the rug. “Oh no,” said Tina.

Milo held up his ticket. Rumi saw it and hopped fast. She steadied the cup. “Teamwork,” she said. “We help each other.”

The show ended with a big, soft group hug. They felt the smooth bedspread, the fuzzy blanket, the tickle of feathers, the gentle poke of tiny hedgehog quills. They giggled together.

Rumi looked around her cozy bedroom. “Our show is for everyone,” she said. “Different is welcome. Slow, quiet, fast, loud. We can make space. We can take turns. We can help.”

Her friends nodded. “We belong,” they said together.

The lamp clicked low. The room turned sleepy and sweet.

“Good night, friends,” Rumi whispered. “Thank you for joining in.”

The End.
