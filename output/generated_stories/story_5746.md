# Nutkin Learns to Listen

In a cozy quiet attic, high above a warm house, lived a little gray squirrel named Nutkin. The attic was filled with old boxes, soft blankets, and dusty sunbeams that danced through the small window. Nutkin loved exploring every corner of his special home.

One sunny morning, Nutkin heard his mama calling from across the attic. "Nutkin, my little one, please stay away from the big wooden box in the corner today. It's not safe for little paws."

But Nutkin was busy chasing a floating dust bunny. "Okay, Mama!" he called back, not really listening to her words. The dust bunny looked so fluffy and fun!

Later, Mama Squirrel tried again. "Remember, Nutkin, the wooden box is off-limits. There are sharp things inside that could hurt you."

"Uh-huh, Mama," Nutkin said, but he was watching a spider spin a sparkly web. The spider's legs moved so quickly! He wasn't paying attention to Mama's important words.

After lunch, when Mama went to gather acorns from downstairs, Nutkin's curious eyes spotted the big wooden box in the corner. It was brown and mysterious, with a heavy lid that was slightly open.

"I wonder what's inside," Nutkin whispered to himself. His fluffy tail twitched with excitement. "Just a tiny peek won't hurt."

Nutkin scampered over to the box. He pushed the heavy lid with his small paws. *Creak!* The lid opened wider. Inside, he saw shiny tools with sharp edges that glinted in the sunlight.

"Ooh, sparkly!" Nutkin reached his paw toward a pointy tool.

Suddenly, he heard a gentle voice behind him. "Little squirrel, be careful!"

Nutkin turned around and saw Mrs. Mouse, an elderly mouse who lived in the attic walls. Her whiskers were gray, and her eyes were kind but worried.

"Those tools are very sharp," Mrs. Mouse said softly. "They could hurt your little paws badly. Didn't your mama tell you to stay away?"

Nutkin's ears drooped. "Yes, she did. But I... I wasn't really listening." His voice became very small. "I was thinking about other things."

Mrs. Mouse nodded wisely. "When someone who loves us gives us important information, we need to use our ears AND our hearts to listen. Your mama wasn't trying to spoil your fun. She was trying to keep you safe."

"I understand now," Nutkin said, stepping away from the dangerous box. "Mama knows things that can help me stay safe. I should listen with my whole self, not just pretend to listen."

Just then, Mama Squirrel returned. When she saw Nutkin near the box, her eyes grew wide with worry.

"I'm sorry, Mama," Nutkin said quickly. "I wasn't really listening when you told me about the box. Mrs. Mouse helped me understand that listening keeps me safe. I promise to listen better."

Mama Squirrel hugged Nutkin tight. "Thank you for being honest, my brave little squirrel. Listening shows respect for others and helps us learn important things."

From that day on, Nutkin always used his ears, eyes, and heart when others spoke to him. He learned that good listening was like a superpower that kept him safe and showed others he cared about them.

And whenever he saw the wooden box, Nutkin remembered the important lesson: listening with love keeps everyone happy and safe.

The End.
