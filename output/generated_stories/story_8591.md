# Leo's Kitchen Adventure

In a cozy house with yellow walls and sunshine streaming through the windows, there lived a brave little lion cub named Leo. Every morning, Leo loved to help in the warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon and fresh bread.

"Good morning, Leo!" called Mama Lion from the stove. Steam rose from her cooking pot, making the kitchen feel extra warm and snug. "Would you like to help me make breakfast?"

Leo's golden fur fluffed with excitement. "Yes, please!" he roared softly, his little paws pattering across the smooth tile floor.

But as Leo reached for his special step-stool, he saw his big sister Luna already using it to reach the counter. She was stirring something that smelled like sweet pancakes.

"Luna, I need the step-stool too," Leo said, his voice getting a little growly. He felt his whiskers twitch with frustration.

"I'm using it first!" Luna said, not looking away from her mixing bowl. "You have to wait!"

Leo felt his chest getting tight and warm. He wanted to roar loudly or push Luna away from the step-stool. His paws clenched into little fists. But then he remembered what Papa Lion had taught him about peaceful solutions.

Leo took three deep breaths, just like Papa showed him. In... out... in... out... in... out. The sweet smell of pancakes filled his nose, and he felt calmer.

"Mama," Leo said in his clearest voice, "Luna and I both want to use the step-stool. Can you help us find a peaceful solution?"

Mama Lion smiled proudly at her little cub. "What a wise question, Leo! Let's think together. What are some ways you both could help in the kitchen?"

Leo's ears perked up as he thought hard. "Maybe... maybe Luna could stir the pancakes on the step-stool, and I could help you with something else?"

"That's excellent thinking!" Mama said. "Luna, how much longer do you need to stir?"

Luna looked at her bowl. "Just two more minutes, then the batter will be smooth!"

"Perfect!" said Leo, his tail swishing happily. "I can set the table while I wait, and then we can take turns!"

Leo padded over to the cabinet and carefully took out the colorful plates and cups. He felt proud as he placed each one on the wooden table. The plates made a gentle *clink* sound that he loved.

"Time's up!" Luna called. "Your turn, Leo!"

Leo climbed onto the warm step-stool and helped Mama pour the pancake batter into the sizzling pan. It made a happy *hiss* sound, and Leo giggled.

"You know what, Leo?" Luna said, watching him work. "I'm sorry I didn't want to share at first. You found such a good solution!"

"Thank you for listening," Leo said with a big smile. "Now we both got to help!"

As they sat down to eat their fluffy pancakes together, Mama Lion gave Leo an extra big hug. "I'm so proud of you for using your words and finding a peaceful way to solve the problem."

Leo's chest puffed out with pride. He had learned that even when he felt frustrated, he could take deep breaths, use his voice, and work together with others to find solutions that made everyone happy.

The End.
