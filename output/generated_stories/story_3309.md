# Poppy the Penguin Takes a Calm Breath

Poppy was a cheerful penguin with shiny black feathers and a bright orange beak. She loved snack time in the dining room. The dining room smelled like warm toast and sweet strawberries. Sunlight made little sparkles on Poppy’s spoon.

“Yum! Fishy crackers and fruit!” Poppy chirped, clapping her flippers.

Mama set a big bowl of fishy crackers on the table. “Please share,” Mama said kindly. “Everyone can have some.”

Poppy’s best friends, Benny the Bunny and Tilly the Tortoise, sat beside her. Their chairs made soft squeaks on the floor. Benny’s ears wiggled. Tilly blinked slowly.

Poppy reached for the bowl. But the bowl slid away to Benny’s side. Benny said, “My turn!” and scooped a big handful. Then Tilly took a few, very slow, one… two… three.

Poppy’s tummy rumbled. Her face felt hot. Her flippers squeezed tight. Her cheeks puffed. “Hey! I want some now!” she shouted.

Her words sounded loud in the quiet room. The spoon clinked. The clock ticked. Poppy’s eyes felt watery, and her beak pinched into a frown.

Mama knelt beside Poppy. “I see big feelings,” Mama said in a soft voice. “Are you feeling angry?”

Poppy nodded. “I am mad,” she said. “I am hungry, and I did not get a turn.”

Mama touched Poppy’s flipper gently. “It’s okay to feel mad. Let’s help your body feel safe. Try my Calm-Down Three.”

Poppy sniffled. “What is that?”

“First,” Mama whispered, “Put your hand on your belly.” Poppy placed a flipper on her soft belly. “Now breathe in like you smell a yummy flower.” Poppy breathed in. She smelled strawberries and toast. “Breathe out like you blow a bubble.” Poppy breathed out slow. “Pop!”

“One,” Mama said. “Now again.” Poppy breathed in like smelling flowers. She breathed out like blowing big, shiny bubbles. “Two.” They did it one more time. “Three.”

Poppy’s shoulders got soft. Her face cooled. Her tummy didn’t feel so rumbly. The room seemed less noisy.

“How do you feel now?” Mama asked.

“Calmer,” Poppy whispered. “Still hungry. But calmer.”

“Now we can use words,” Mama said. “You can say what you need.”

Poppy looked at Benny and Tilly. She took one more gentle breath. “Friends,” she said, “I felt mad. I need a turn too. Can we share so everyone gets some?”

Benny’s ears drooped. “Sorry, Poppy,” he said. “I grabbed fast.” He pushed the bowl to the middle. Tilly nodded slowly. “We can take turns,” Tilly said.

Poppy smiled a small smile. “Thank you.” She took a little handful of fishy crackers. Crunch, crunch! They were salty and yummy. The fruit was cool and sweet like tiny kisses on her tongue.

After snack, there were crumbs on the table and floor. Little cracker bits stuck to the plates. Poppy looked around. “It’s messy,” she said.

Mama smiled. “You noticed a problem. What could we do?”

Poppy tapped her beak, thinking. “We can help! We can be dining room helpers.” Her eyes sparkled. “We can volunteer to clean up!”

Benny hopped. “I can pick up crumbs!” Tilly smiled. “I can carry plates. Slow and steady.”

Poppy took a deep, flower-bubble breath and said, “I will wipe the table.” Mama gave her a soft cloth. It felt squishy and cool in Poppy’s flipper. Poppy wiped in gentle circles. Swish, swish. Benny used a little brush. Sweep, sweep. Tilly carried plates to the sink. Clink, clink.

They worked together. Poppy hummed a happy tune. The room began to sparkle. The trash smelled a little stinky, but the air also smelled like soap and strawberries. The floor felt smooth under Poppy’s feet.

When they finished, Mama clapped softly. “What resourceful helpers! You saw a problem, you calmed your bodies, and you found a solution. Thank you for volunteering.”

Poppy felt warm and proud. “My body feels calm and strong,” she said. “When I got mad, I did the Calm-Down Three. Then I used my words. Then we helped.”

Benny nodded. “I will try the flower-bubble breath next time.”

Tilly added, “Me too. Slow breaths make slow, kind choices.”

Poppy looked at the table. It was clean and shiny. She looked at her friends. They were smiling. She put her flipper over her belly and took one more peaceful breath, in and out.

“I learned something,” Poppy said. “Feelings are big, but I am bigger when I breathe, talk, and help.”

Mama hugged them all. “That is a wonderful lesson.”

Poppy giggled. “Next time I feel mad, I know what to do! Flower-bubble breath, use my words, and find a way to help.”

The clock ticked a happy tick-tock. The dining room felt cozy and bright. Poppy’s heart felt light like a bubble that would not pop.

The friends sat together, sipping water. Sip, sip. They shared a plate of strawberries. Sweet, juicy, and red. Poppy smiled and said, “We are a good team.”

“Yes,” said Benny.

“Yes,” said Tilly.

“Yes,” said Mama. “Calm, kind helpers.”

The End.
