# Bubbles Helps in the Warm Kitchen

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash and sing. One sunny morning, Bubbles visited a warm kitchen. The kitchen smelled like sweet bananas and cozy oatmeal. Pots hummed. A kettle whispered. The light made the butter shine like sunshine.

“Hello!” Bubbles chirped, flicking his tail with a happy swish. He wore a tiny apron with little fish on it. His friend Mia stood on a step stool by the counter. Mia’s grandma, Nana Rosa, stirred a pot.

“We are making a healthy breakfast,” said Nana Rosa. “Banana oatmeal with berries.”

Bubbles’ eyes sparkled. “I want to help! Helping feels good,” he sang.

Mia smiled. “We need a helper who listens and uses gentle fins.”

Bubbles bobbed his head. “I can listen. I can be gentle.” He took a big breath and felt the warm, cinnamon air. It smelled like hugs.

Nana Rosa pointed to a bowl. “First, we mash the bananas. Bubbles, please hold the bowl still. Mia will mash.”

Bubbles wanted to mash, too. His fins wiggled. He started to reach, but Mia said softly, “Bubbles, this is my job right now. Please let me mash.”

Bubbles paused. He remembered: help means listening. Help means following directions. Help means respecting friends. He pulled his fin back and smiled. “Okay, Mia. I will hold the bowl.”

Mia mashed the soft bananas. Squish, squish. “Thank you, Bubbles,” she said. “You’re helping me do my job.”

Bubbles felt warm inside. “I like helping.”

Nana Rosa added oats and milk to the pot. “The oatmeal needs to cook slowly,” she said. “Slow and gentle. Just like kind words.”

Bubbles swam a tiny circle in the air, as if the kitchen were a calm sea. He sniffed again. “It smells sweet and cozy.”

Mia pointed to the berry bowl. “We can wash the berries. But, Bubbles, the water is my job, and the strainer is your job. Okay?”

“Okay,” said Bubbles. “I will hold the strainer. You can pour the water.” He held the strainer steady. The water splashed, trickle-trickle. The berries glowed red and blue.

Bubbles saw a shiny spoon. “Ooo! Shiny!” He picked it up and tapped, ting-ting. Nana Rosa turned and smiled. “That spoon is for stirring later. Please put it back for now.”

Bubbles blinked. He wanted to play music. But he let the spoon rest. “I can wait,” he said. “I am a helper who follows directions.”

Nana Rosa clapped softly. “Thank you for listening.”

Soon, the oatmeal bubbled, blup-blup. Steam floated like tiny clouds. Nana Rosa said, “Time to stir.” She looked at both helpers. “I will stir first. Then Mia. Then Bubbles.”

Bubbles felt excited. He wiggled again. “My turn will come,” he whispered to himself. “I can be patient.”

Nana Rosa stirred. Mia stirred. Then Nana Rosa handed the spoon to Bubbles. “Your turn.”

Bubbles stirred in slow circles. The spoon felt warm. The oatmeal felt thick and soft. “Round and round,” he sang. “Healthy and kind.”

Mia put bowls on the table. “Bubbles, may I have a hug?” she asked.

Bubbles stopped and looked at her. “Yes, I would like that,” he said. They shared a small, gentle hug. Then Bubbles asked Nana Rosa, “May I taste the oatmeal?”

“Thank you for asking,” said Nana Rosa. “Yes, but it is hot. Please blow first.”

Bubbles blew tiny puffs. Fffff. He took a small taste. “Mmm! Sweet banana. Warm oats. Happy belly!”

Nana Rosa nodded. “Healthy foods give us energy. Berries help our bodies. Oats help our tummies.”

Bubbles wanted to pour the berries into the pot. He looked at Mia. “May I pour them?”

Mia smiled. “Yes, Bubbles. Please pour them slowly.”

Bubbles poured slowly. Plop, plop, plop. Purple swirls danced in the oatmeal. He wiped a tiny drip with a towel.

Then Bubbles reached for Mia’s spoon. Mia stepped back. “Please don’t take my spoon,” she said calmly. “That is my tool.”

Bubbles froze. He remembered personal boundaries. He put his fin down. “I’m sorry, Mia. I will not take your spoon. May I use a different one?”

“Yes,” Mia said, handing him another spoon. “Thanks for asking.”

They set the table together. Bubbles carried napkins. Mia carried cups. Nana Rosa poured warm oatmeal into bowls. “Thank you, helpers,” Nana Rosa said. “You listened. You asked. You waited. You were gentle.”

They sat together. The kitchen felt safe and bright. Bubbles tasted his bowl. “Helping makes my heart feel big,” he said.

Mia nodded. “Helping is kind. We can use soft voices and kind words.”

Nana Rosa raised her spoon. “To helpful fins and helping hands.”

They all giggled and took a bite. Yum.

After breakfast, Bubbles helped rinse the dishes. Swish, swish. He asked, “What can I do next?”

Nana Rosa smiled. “You can rest now. Helpers also rest.”

Bubbles curled on a soft mat near the warm oven. He listened to the quiet kitchen and felt proud.

Today, Bubbles learned that helping means listening, following directions, and respecting friends’ space. Helping makes work easier, bodies healthy, and hearts happy.

The End.
