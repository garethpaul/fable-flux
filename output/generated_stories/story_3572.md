# The Gentle Guest Room

In a quiet house, there was a soft, sunny guest room. The curtains were light and floaty. The bedspread was smooth and smelled like clean soap. A tiny clock tick-tick-ticked on the table. This was where Mimi, a kind mouse, liked to visit. Mimi had soft gray fur, big round ears, and the warmest smile. She loved to help. She loved to listen. She loved to be kind.

One morning, Mimi peeked into the guest room. “Hello, room,” she squeaked, “I brought crumbs of cracker for a snack.” She stepped onto the fluffy rug. It felt like a little cloud under her paws. The window was open a crack, and a sweet breeze whispered in. The breeze smelled like grass and lemons.

“Okay, paws on the floor, tail still, ears open,” Mimi said to herself. “Mindful mouse mode.” Mimi liked to be mindful. She took a slow breath in—siiiip—and then out—whoooosh. Her heart felt calm.

Just then, Mimi heard a small sound. It was a sniff. Then a tiny hiccup. “Hello?” Mimi called softly. “Is someone here?”

From behind a stack of pillows, a little dust-bunny popped out. It was fluffy and gray and a bit wiggly. It had two shy eyes and a bendy smile. “Hi,” the dust-bunny whispered. “I’m Dodo. I got blown in by the breeze. I feel scared. This room is big. I feel like I don’t belong.”

Mimi’s ears perked up. “Oh, hello, Dodo,” she said gently. “Thank you for telling me how you feel.”

Dodo sniffled. “Are you going to chase me away? People sweep me. Brooms swoosh me. I get tossed.”

Mimi shook her head. “I won’t chase you,” she said. “I can see you are worried. Your eyes look wet. Your voice is small. I hear your heart.” Mimi sat down on the rug, tail curled. “Would you like to sit with me?”

Dodo wiggled closer. “I am a mess,” he said. “I am not neat like this room.”

Mimi looked around. The pillows were tidy. The bed was straight. The clock ticked kindly. “This room is for guests,” Mimi said. “You are a guest right now. Guests can be different and still belong.”

Dodo took a shaky breath. Mimi noticed. “Let’s take slow, gentle breaths,” she said. “Smell the flower.” Mimi lifted her nose and breathed in. “Blow the candle.” She breathed out slowly, like a soft “whooooo.” Dodo copied. In. Out. In. Out. With each breath, Dodo’s wiggles grew calmer.

“How do you feel now?” Mimi asked.

“Better,” Dodo said. “My tummy is not so tight.”

“I’m glad,” said Mimi. “When I feel worried, I listen to my breath. I listen to the clock. Tick, tock. Tick, tock. The sounds help me feel safe.”

They sat together. The rug felt warm. The sun made tiny golden squares on the floor. The room smelled like lemon and a little like crackers. Their tummies rumbled.

“Would you like a crumb?” Mimi asked. “We can share.”

Dodo’s eyes grew round. “Share? With me?”

“Yes,” Mimi said. “Sharing is a way to show care.”

They munched. Crunch, munch, crunch. Dodo giggled. “Your crumbs are tasty,” he said.

Mimi smiled. “Thank you for saying please and thank you,” she said. “Kind words help hearts feel soft.”

Just then, there was a swish at the door. A soft broom peeked in. “Sweep, sweep,” it whispered, sliding onto the rug. Dodo stiffened. “Oh no,” he squeaked. “The broom!”

Mimi stood tall on her tiny paws. “Hello, Broom,” she called. “Please move slow. We have a worried guest.”

The broom paused. “I am here to clean,” it said in a hushy voice. “But I can be gentle.” The broom waited, and the bristles didn’t scratch. “I see a dust-bunny,” it said. “Do I need to sweep?”

Mimi looked at Dodo. “What do you need?” she asked.

Dodo took a breath. “I need time,” he said softly. “I need a place to be me for a little while.”

Mimi nodded. “Broom, can we make a cozy corner? A little safe spot by the plant?”

The broom thought for a moment. “Yes,” it said. “I can sweep a circle and leave it neat.” Swish, swish. The broom made a small, tidy circle by the big green plant. The light there was soft. The air smelled fresh.

Dodo looked at the cozy corner. “Is this for me?”

“For now,” Mimi said. “You are our guest. We will accept you as you are. When you are ready to move on, you can. Until then, you can rest.”

Dodo scooted into the circle. He sighed. “Thank you,” he whispered. “You listened. You cared. I feel seen.”

Mimi’s eyes were warm. “That is empathy,” she said. “Empathy means I try to feel with you. I try to understand your heart.”

The broom nodded. “I learned something, too,” it said. “Cleaning can be kind.”

They sat together. Mimi listened to the tick-tock. Dodo breathed slow. The broom leaned quietly by the door.

At last, Dodo smiled. “I think I am ready,” he said. “The breeze is calling me.” He wiggled happily. “Because you were kind, I am not scared.”

Mimi walked him to the window. “Good-bye, gentle guest,” she said. “You belong in the world, just as you are.”

The breeze kissed Dodo and carried him away. Whooosh. The room felt peaceful. The clock ticked. Mimi’s heart felt big and bright.

Mimi turned to the broom. “We did it,” she said. “We listened, we breathed, we made space. That is empathy, mindfulness, and acceptance.”

The broom smiled in its bristly way. “Yes,” it said. “What a kind guest room.”

The End.
