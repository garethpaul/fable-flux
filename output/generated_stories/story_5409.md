# Benny Beaver Says Thank You

Benny was a busy beaver with bright, shiny teeth and a warm, wiggly tail. He lived near a sunny backyard where flowers opened like little suns, birds sang sweet songs, and a gentle breeze smelled like fresh grass and oranges.

“Work, work, work!” Benny hummed as he scurried along the fence. “So many sticks to move! So many leaves to tidy!”

In the sunny backyard, a little garden grew. Plump tomatoes blushed red. Carrots peeked up with feathery green tops. Blueberries sat like tiny buttons on a bush. A bird bath sparkled in the sunshine, and a tall tree made leafy shade.

Benny liked to help. He picked up fallen twigs. He swept leaves into neat piles with his flat tail. He stacked small sticks to make cozy spaces for bugs and worms.

But Benny was so busy that he forgot to look up. He forgot to smile at the sun. He forgot to say thank you.

A robin landed near him. “Cheep! Hello, Benny! Isn’t the sun warm and nice?”

Benny didn’t stop. “No time! Work, work, work!”

A ladybug zipped by on tiny wings. “Hello, Benny! Smell the flowers. Mmm, sweet!”

Benny shook his head. “No time! Sticks to stack!”

Benny’s tummy made a little rumble. “Gr-rroowl.”

He put a paw over his belly. “Maybe a snack,” he said. He found a crunchy carrot in the garden. Crunch, crunch! It was sweet and orange and delicious. He found a ripe tomato. Pop! Juice dripped on his chin. He licked it with a giggle. He plucked a few blueberries. Plink, plink, plink! They tasted like purple sunshine.

But Benny gobbled and rushed. “Eat fast! Work fast!”

Soon, the sky felt hotter. The sun tickled Benny’s fur. His paws felt tired. His tail drooped. He bumped a stick pile. Clatter! The sticks tumbled and rolled.

“Oh no,” Benny sighed. “I worked so hard, and now it’s all messy.”

A soft breeze whispered through the leaves. The tall tree swayed and tossed cool shadows across the grass. The bird bath splashed as the robin dipped and shook sparkles into the air. The garden leaves rustled.

A wise turtle peeked from under a big leaf. “Hello, Benny,” the turtle said slowly. “You look worn out.”

“I am,” said Benny. “I tried to do everything. Now my sticks fell down, and I feel grumpy.”

The turtle blinked kindly. “Sometimes when we hurry, we forget to be thankful. Gratitude makes work gentle.”

“Gratitude?” Benny asked. The word felt soft in his mouth.

“It means we say thank you,” said the turtle. “Thank you for help. Thank you for food. Thank you for shade and water. Gratitude helps our hearts smile. When our hearts smile, our paws work better.”

Benny looked around. He felt the cool shade on his fur. Ahh. He heard the robin’s song. Trill, cheep, tweet! He smelled the carrot tops and the sweet tomato vines. He saw the blueberries glowing like tiny stars.

Benny took a slow breath. In. Out. His belly felt calm.

“Thank you, sun, for warm light,” he said softly.

The sun shined brighter.

“Thank you, tree, for shade,” Benny said.

The leaves whispered, shhh, shhh.

“Thank you, water, for splash and sip,” Benny added, dipping his paw into the bird bath. The cool drops tickled.

“Thank you, garden, for yummy food that helps me grow strong,” he said to the carrots, tomatoes, and blueberries.

The robin hopped closer. “Cheep! Thank you, Benny, for caring for the yard.”

Benny smiled. “And thank you, little friends.”

He turned to his tumbled sticks. “I can fix this,” he said. “But this time, I will go slow, and I will care for our home.”

Benny began to rebuild. He placed each stick gently. Tap. Pat. He made little homes for the bugs and worms. “Thank you, bugs, for helping the soil,” he said. He put a leaf blanket on the garden soil. “This keeps you cozy and moist,” he whispered. He carried a tiny bucket of water and gave the plants a careful drink. “Sip, sip, sip,” he sang.

The turtle nodded. “That is environmental care,” he said. “You are helping the earth.”

Benny’s tummy felt happy, not rumbly. He picked a carrot, washed it, and took a slow bite. Crunch. “Thank you, carrot, for vitamins,” he said. He tasted a tomato slice with a sprinkle of herbs. “Thank you, tomato, for juicy goodness.” He popped a blueberry in his mouth. “Thank you, blueberry, for sweet energy.”

The backyard seemed to hum with joy. Bees buzzed. The breeze smelled like mint and soil. Benny’s heart felt big and warm.

He finished his work and rested in the tree’s shade. The robin sang a bedtime tune. The turtle tucked in with a tiny yawn.

Benny folded his paws. “Today I learned something important,” he said. “When I say thank you—to the sun, the shade, the water, the plants, and my friends—I feel calm. I take good care of our home. I eat good foods that help me grow. Gratitude makes everything better.”

He wiggled his tail happily. “Thank you, day,” he whispered.

And the sunny backyard whispered back, “You’re welcome, Benny.”

The End.
