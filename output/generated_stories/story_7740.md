# Panda Bao’s Big Thank-You Song

In a comfortable nursery with soft pillows, cozy blankets, and shelves of picture books, a patient panda named Bao sat on a round, green rug. The rug felt warm under Bao’s paws. Sunlight made golden stripes on the floor. A little radio played gentle music from faraway places—soft drums, twinkly bells, and a happy guitar.

Bao loved to listen. “I can wait my turn,” Bao said with a calm smile. “I am patient.”

Children from many homes came to this nursery. There was Amina, who liked to hum. There was Luca, who clapped. There was Mei, who shook tiny bells. There was Jamal, who tapped a drum. Each child brought a sound from their family and their world.

Today, Teacher Rosa opened a colorful box. “We will make a thank-you song,” she said. “We will share sounds from around the world. We will listen with kind ears. We will say thank you. We will try again if mistakes happen.”

Bao’s black-and-white ears perked up. “A thank-you song,” Bao whispered. “That feels warm in my heart.”

Teacher Rosa gave each child an instrument. Bao got a soft bamboo shaker. It was smooth and cool. It made a hush-hush sound like wind in tall grass.

“Ready?” said Teacher Rosa. “First, we listen. Then, we play together. Remember self-control: breathe in, breathe out.”

Bao placed a paw on the shaker and took a slow breath. In…and out.

The music began. Amina hummed a tune from her grandmother. Luca clapped softly. Mei rang bells like tiny stars. Jamal tapped a steady boom-boom. Bao waited. The sounds blended like a big, bright hug.

But then—oops! The radio switched to a fast song. Luca clapped too fast. Jamal banged too loud. Mei rang-rang-rang without stopping. The sounds bumped and banged. Amina frowned. “I can’t hear my hum.”

Bao felt a wiggle of worry in their tummy. The room got noisy. The soft rug didn’t feel so calm now. Bao wanted to shake-shake-shake the bamboo fast, too.

Bao placed the shaker in their lap. “Breathe in…breathe out,” Bao told themself. “I can be patient.” Bao saw Amina’s eyes look sad. Bao’s heart squeezed.

Teacher Rosa raised a hand. “Pause,” she said kindly. “Let’s use our calm.”

Everyone took a slow breath. The room grew quiet again. The radio played a gentle beat.

Bao lifted a paw. “Friends,” Bao said softly, “I made a plan. Let’s take turns. We can hear everyone. We can be one big world family.”

“What is the plan?” asked Mei.

Bao smiled. “We go around the circle. Each person teaches us a little sound from their home. We will copy it, then say thank you. Then the next person goes.”

Everyone nodded. “Yes!” said Luca. “I like that.”

They began with Amina. She hummed a smooth, round “mmm-mmm.” It felt like warm tea. Everyone hummed with her. “Thank you, Amina,” they said together.

Next was Luca. He clapped, clap…clap-clap. It sounded like happy feet. “Thank you, Luca,” they said.

Mei shook the bells in a soft ring…ring…ring. The bells sparkled like tiny rain. “Thank you, Mei.”

Jamal tapped boom…boom-boom…boom. It felt like a friendly heartbeat. “Thank you, Jamal.”

When it was Bao’s turn, Bao shook the bamboo: hush…hush…hush. It sounded like leaves saying hello. “Thank you, Bao,” everyone said.

Now they put the sounds together, slow and gentle. Hummm. Clap-clap. Ring. Boom-boom. Hush. The nursery filled with a sweet, warm song. It smelled like fresh crayons and clean blankets. It felt safe.

Then—oh no! Luca clapped too soon. Jamal followed, and the beat tumbled over the hum. Amina’s hum got lost again. Mei’s bells jingled nervously.

Bao felt a flutter. “I want to help,” Bao thought. Bao put a paw on the shaker and took another breath. In…and out.

“I’m sorry,” Bao said, even though Bao had not rushed. Bao knew an apology can open hearts. “We got mixed up. Let’s try my calm idea again. And I will count us in. One…two…three.”

Luca looked at Bao. “I’m sorry too,” he said. “I got excited.”

“Me too,” said Jamal. “I was loud.”

“Thank you for saying sorry,” said Bao. “Thank you for trying again.”

Bao counted softly, “One…two…three,” and waved a gentle paw. They played again: Hummm. Clap-clap. Ring. Boom-boom. Hush. The sounds took turns. The song felt like a big, soft blanket that covered everyone.

Teacher Rosa smiled. “Listen,” she said. “We are sharing. This is global citizenship. We take care of each other. We listen to sounds from many places. We say thank you. We say sorry. We try again. We make music together.”

Bao’s heart glowed. “We are a world family,” Bao said. “We are different, and we belong.”

They finished with a happy whisper: hush…hush…hush. The room stayed still for a moment, warm and bright.

Amina grinned. “Thank you, Bao, for the plan.”

“Thank you, friends, for your sounds,” said Bao.

Luca handed Mei a bell. “Would you like a turn to lead?” he asked.

Mei nodded. “Yes, please.” She smiled at Bao. “You helped us be calm.”

Bao felt proud and grateful. The bamboo shaker felt smooth in Bao’s paws. Bao hugged it gently. “When we listen, say sorry, and say thank you, we can make a good world,” Bao said softly.

“Yes,” said everyone. “Together.”

The End.
