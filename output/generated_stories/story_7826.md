# Benny Beaver’s Laundry Plan

Benny the busy beaver had a shiny orange bucket and a little blue clipboard. Clip, clap, went the pen on the paper. Benny loved to build, but today he had a new job. Today, he would wash clothes in the laundry room.

Swish, hum, buzz went the big washing machine. The laundry room smelled like warm soap and clean cotton. A sunny stripe of light came through the window and made the dust sparkle. Benny’s tail thumped softly on the floor. Thump, thump. “I am ready,” he said. “I have a plan!”

Benny read his clipboard. “Step one: sort the clothes.” He smiled. “I can do that.” He made piles: whites in one pile, colors in another, and towels in a fluffy mountain. “Soft!” he giggled, burying his paws in the towels. The towels smelled like lemons.

Then Benny heard a knock. Tap, tap. His friend Dot the duck peeked in. “Hi, Benny! Want to come splash in the pond?”

Benny looked at the sunny stripe and the clipboard. He took a little breath. “I want to splash. But I planned laundry. I made a promise to myself. I will finish first.”

Dot waddled closer. “It’s okay to say no,” she said kindly. “I’ll wait.”

Benny nodded. “Thank you, Dot.” He read his clipboard again. “Step two: check pockets.” He patted pants. Pat, pat. He checked shirts. Rustle, rustle. “Oh!” He found a tiny red crayon. “Crayons can melt,” Benny said. “I will put it away.” He placed the crayon in a safe cup. He found a shiny coin. “Clink!” He put the coin in a bowl.

Dot watched. “You are careful,” she said.

Benny smiled. “Planning helps me do things right.”

He checked the list. “Step three: measure soap.” He picked up the big soap bottle. It was heavy and smelled like flowers. He poured a little bit, just to the line on the cup. “Not too much,” he said. “Not too little.” Glug, glug.

Next was “Step four: set the machine.” Benny turned the knob to “colors,” and pressed the button. Beep! The washer woke up. Swish, swish, swish went the water. The machine hummed a sleepy song. Benny felt proud. His tail thumped. Thump, thump.

Then Benny saw a basket he forgot. It was full of Daddy Beaver’s work shirts. “Oh no!” Benny felt his tummy twist. He had promised to do all the laundry. He looked at his clipboard. “I missed a step,” he whispered. His eyes got a little wet.

Dot put a soft wing on his shoulder. “It’s okay, Benny. You found the problem. Now you can fix it.”

Benny sniffed and nodded. “Step five: check every basket.” He added it to his plan. Scribble, scribble. He looked under the table. He looked behind the door. He found one more tiny sock. He giggled. “Peekaboo, little sock!”

He sorted the new basket. Whites to whites, colors to colors. He checked the pockets again. Pat, pat. He found a sticky candy wrapper. “This does not go in the wash,” he said. He threw it in the trash. He felt better. He was honest with himself and did the right thing.

The washer went ding! The first load was done. Benny moved the wet clothes to the dryer. Plop, plop, plop. “Step six: clean the lint trap,” he read. He pulled out the fuzzy gray fluff. “It feels like a cloud,” he laughed. He put it in the bin.

“Step seven: start the dryer.” He turned the knob to “low.” Beep! Whoosh went the warm air. The room smelled extra cozy now. Benny and Dot sat on the rug. They listened to the gentle tumble—thump, whirr, thump.

Dot said, “I like your plan. It helps you remember.”

Benny nodded. “When I plan, I don’t rush. I do each step. I keep my promises.”

The dryer went ding! Benny folded small shirts. Fold, pat. He rolled socks into happy pairs. Roll, boop. He stacked towels like a soft tower. He put Daddy Beaver’s shirts on hangers. “Smooth and neat,” he said.

He checked his clipboard one more time. “Step eight: put things away.” He carried baskets—one, two—down the hall. The house smelled like fresh rain and lemons. He put the clothes in the right rooms. He came back and looked around. The laundry room was tidy. His clipboard had little check marks all down the page.

Dot flapped her wings. “You did it!”

Benny’s heart felt light and warm. “Yes! I followed my plan, I fixed my mistake, and I kept my promise.” He looked at the window. The sun still shone. “Now I can splash.”

They went to the pond. The water went plip, plop, splash on their toes. They laughed and played. Benny felt calm and happy.

On the way home, Benny said, “Planning helps me do my work, even when it is hard. Being honest helps me fix problems. When I plan first and do the right thing, there is still time for fun.”

Dot quacked. “That is a good plan, Benny.”

Benny smiled and tapped his clipboard. Clip, clap. “I will make a plan tomorrow too.”

The End.
