# Leo's Big Game Plan

Leo was a brave little lion cub with a fluffy golden mane and bright, curious eyes. He lived in the coziest living room you could imagine, filled with soft cushions, colorful toys, and plenty of space to play.

One sunny morning, Leo bounced on his favorite red cushion and announced, "Today I'm going to play the BEST game of indoor soccer ever!" He had set up a special course using pillows as goals and toy blocks as obstacles.

Leo kicked his soft, squeaky ball toward the pillow goal. *Thump!* The ball bounced off a block and rolled under the coffee table instead.

"Hmm," Leo said, scratching his little head. "That didn't work."

He tried again, this time kicking harder. *Bounce, bounce, CRASH!* The ball knocked over his tower of blocks and still missed the goal.

Leo's ears drooped. "Maybe I'm not good at this game," he sighed, sitting down with a *plop* on the soft carpet.

Just then, his wise grandmother, Nana Lion, padded into the room. Her warm, golden fur smelled like sunshine and cookies.

"What's wrong, my brave little cub?" she asked gently.

"I can't get the ball in the goal, Nana," Leo said sadly. "I've tried and tried, but it keeps going the wrong way."

Nana Lion smiled and sat beside him. "Tell me, Leo, when you're hunting for your favorite toy mouse, do you just run around everywhere, or do you have a plan?"

Leo's eyes lit up. "Oh! I make a plan! First, I look under the couch, then behind the cushions, then in my toy box!"

"Exactly!" Nana said, her eyes twinkling. "That's called using strategy. It means thinking before you act and making a smart plan."

Leo jumped up excitedly. "So I need a strategy for soccer too!"

"That's right, my clever cub. Look at your course and think: what's the best way to get the ball to the goal?"

Leo studied his setup carefully. He noticed the blocks were lined up in a straight path, but there was an open space on the left side.

"Aha!" Leo roared softly. "Instead of kicking straight through the blocks, I can kick the ball around them!"

He positioned himself at an angle and gently tapped the ball. It rolled smoothly around the blocks and right into the pillow goal!

"I did it! I did it!" Leo danced around the room, his tail swishing with joy.

"You certainly did," Nana purred proudly. "And how do you feel now?"

"I feel amazing! I feel smart and strong!" Leo beamed. "Using strategy made me better at the game!"

Leo spent the rest of the morning practicing his new approach. Sometimes he kicked around the left side, sometimes the right. He even discovered he could bounce the ball gently off the wall to change its direction.

Each successful goal made Leo feel more confident. His roars of celebration echoed through the cozy living room, and his golden mane seemed to shine even brighter.

As the sun began to set, painting the room in warm orange light, Leo curled up next to Nana on the big, soft couch.

"Thank you for teaching me about strategy, Nana," he said sleepily.

"You're welcome, brave little one. Remember, when something seems hard, take a moment to think and make a plan. Your brain is just as strong as your paws!"

Leo smiled and closed his eyes, dreaming of all the wonderful games he would play tomorrow with his new strategy skills.

The End.
