# Miko the Mouse Builds a Dinner Helper

Miko the mouse lived behind the tall clock in the dining room. The room smelled like warm bread and sweet apples. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. The big table stood in the middle, with chairs like friendly giants.

Every evening, the family ate dinner here. Miko was a kind mouse. He liked to help. He liked to build little things from tiny bits he found, like paper clips, buttons, and clean bottle caps.

One day, the dining room was very busy. The family was setting the table. Forks clinked. Napkins rustled. The little child, Lily, was learning to help too.

“I will carry the cups,” Lily said. She lifted a cup, then another. Her hands were small and careful. But the cups were smooth and slippery. One cup wobbled. “Oh no!” said Lily.

Miko peeked from behind the clock. He felt a little flutter in his tummy. “I can help,” he whispered to himself. He watched, listened, and sniffed. He smelled soup. He heard the soft hum of the fridge. He saw the wobbling cup.

Miko ran along the baseboard, tiny feet pat-patting. He waved at Crumbs the ant, who was marching by. “Hello, Crumbs!”

“Hello,” said Crumbs. “Busy day?”

“Very busy,” said Miko. “Lily wants to help. The cups slip. We need… a builder’s brain.”

Crumbs tapped his feelers. “A plan!” he said.

Miko nodded. “Yes, a plan.” He took a deep breath. He remembered his three builder steps: Look, Think, Try.

First, Miko looked. He saw a small napkin on a chair. He saw a clean rubber band by the flower vase. He saw a smooth coaster that had fallen to the floor.

Next, Miko thought. “If cups are slippery, we can make them grippy,” he said. He touched the coaster. It felt rough. “This helps cups stay still.” He touched the rubber band. It was stretchy. “This can hug the cup.”

Crumbs clicked his tiny feet. “Smart thinking!”

Then, Miko tried. He dragged the coaster to the center of the table leg. “Huff, huff,” he puffed. He looped the rubber band around the cup Lily was holding, then tugged the coaster to where she would set it down.

Lily came closer. “I can do it,” she said softly. She saw the coaster on the table edge. She saw the rubber band around the cup. “What’s this?”

Miko squeaked kindly, “It’s a helper.”

Lily looked left, looked right. She could not see the small mouse. But she heard a tiny squeak, like a friend. “Okay,” she said, smiling. She placed the cup on the coaster. It sat still. No wobble.

Mom clapped softly. “Nice job, Lily.”

Lily beamed. “I did it!” she said. “The cup stayed!”

Miko’s whiskers twitched with happy pride. “Look, Think, Try,” he whispered. He felt warm inside.

Now Lily wanted to carry the napkins. The napkins slid off her little stack. They floated down like soft feathers. “Oh,” she sighed. “They won’t stay.”

Miko looked again. He saw a clean ribbon on the chair. He thought. “Ribbon can tie. A bow can hold.” He tried. He tugged the ribbon over to Lily’s hands.

Lily blinked. “A ribbon?” She wrapped it around the napkins and made a simple bow. “Stay, napkins.” They stayed! She walked to the table. Swish, swish, went the ribbon tails.

“Great thinking,” Dad said.

“Thank you,” Lily said. She patted her chest. “I am a helper.”

Crumbs waved his feelers. “Teamwork!” he cheered.

Then Miko heard a sniffle. He looked under the table. Chair the Chair was creaking. “My foot feels wobbly,” the chair creaked in a tired voice.

Miko felt empathy. “Oh, Chair,” he said gently. “You have worked hard.” He touched the wobbly foot. It was uneven. He looked around. He found a tiny clean cork chip near the baseboard. He thought. “If the foot is short, we can build it taller.” He tried. He slid the cork chip under the foot and pressed.

The chair stood steady. “Ahh,” said Chair, relieved. “Thank you, little builder.”

“You’re welcome,” said Miko.

Soon the table was set. Cups were safe. Napkins were neat. The chair did not wobble. The room smelled like soup and baked carrots. Miko’s tummy rumbled. Lily climbed into her seat.

“Dinner looks ready,” Mom said.

Lily looked at the coaster, the ribbon, and the steady chair. “We built helpers,” she said. “We used our brains.”

Miko smiled from the clock. He felt calm and strong. He had used his three steps. He had helped a friend. He had kept the room safe and well.

Mom ladled soup. Steam floated like tiny clouds. “What did we learn today?” Mom asked.

Lily held her spoon. “Look, Think, Try,” she said. “Building makes jobs easier. Helping feels good.”

Miko whispered, “And being kind makes everyone steady.”

Crumbs did a happy dance. Tap-tap, tap-tap.

They all ate together. Slurp, slurp. Crunch, munch. The dining room glowed with warm light and warm hearts.

The End.
