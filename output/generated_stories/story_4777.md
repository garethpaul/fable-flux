# Fawn’s Sunny Backyard Craft

In a sunny backyard, a gentle deer fawn named Fawn stepped softly on cool green grass. The sun felt warm on Fawn’s back. The air smelled like sweet flowers and fresh dirt. Birds sang, “Tweet, tweet!” A soft breeze said, “Whoosh.”

Fawn blinked big brown eyes. “I want to make a craft,” Fawn said. “I want to make something new.”

Fawn looked around. There was a big box that said, “Re-use Me!” Inside the box were things that people were going to throw away. There were clean paper rolls, shiny bottle caps, a piece of soft fabric, and a small cardboard tray. They were not trash. They were treasures!

Fawn smiled. “I can craft with these. I can be kind to Earth.”

A ladybug landed on Fawn’s nose. “Hello,” Fawn giggled. “Do you want to help?”

The ladybug wiggled. “Yes! What will we make?”

Fawn thought. “Hmm. I want to make a backyard music band! Music is like talking with sounds. That is language too. We can share feelings with songs.”

The ladybug clapped tiny feet. “Let’s do it!”

Fawn pulled out a paper roll. It felt smooth and light. “This can be a shaker,” Fawn said. Fawn gathered clean, dry seeds that had fallen under a sunflower. The seeds smelled nutty. “Listen.” Fawn poured the seeds into the roll. “Shhh-shhh.” The sound was soft, like rain on leaves.

Fawn found a small piece of cloth. It was blue like the sky. Fawn tied the cloth over the end of the roll with a long grass twist. “Tie, tie, tie,” Fawn said slowly. “Safe and snug.” The shaker rattled. “Chika-chika!” Fawn laughed.

Then Fawn picked up two shiny bottle caps. “These can be cymbals.” Fawn slid a thin stick through both caps and wrapped a leaf around the middle to keep them apart. “Tap-tap!” The sound was bright, like little stars.

The ladybug buzzed by the cardboard tray. “What about this?”

Fawn sniffed the tray. It smelled like paper and sunshine. “We can make a drum!” Fawn stretched the soft fabric over the tray and tied it with vine string. Fawn tapped it with gentle hooves. “Tum-tum-tum.” The drum boomed like a kind, slow heartbeat. Fawn felt happy.

Just then, a problem came. A stray gust of wind blew. “Whoosh!” The shaker rolled away and bumped the garden gate. The gate made a squeak. The cloth on the shaker slipped. Seeds spilled. “Pitter-patter!” Seeds scattered on the ground.

Fawn’s ears drooped. “Oh no. My craft is broken.”

The ladybug landed on Fawn’s cheek. “We can fix it. We can try again. We can be careful. We can be smart.”

Fawn took a deep breath. The air smelled like mint leaves. “I will not give up,” Fawn said. “I will solve the problem.”

Fawn looked for a new way. Fawn found a small reusable jar lid in the box. “This lid can be a cap for the shaker,” Fawn said. Fawn put the seeds back into the roll, gently, gently. “Plink-plink.” Fawn pushed the roll into the jar lid and wrapped it tight with a strip of fabric and a twist of vine. “Twist, twist.” Fawn shook it. “Chika-chika!” It stayed closed.

“Yay!” cheered the ladybug. “You fixed it.”

Fawn stood the drum on a flat stone so the wind could not blow it. “Safe and steady,” Fawn said. Fawn tucked the cymbals under a leaf when not using them. “We take care of our crafts,” Fawn whispered.

Soon, a soft rabbit hopped by. “What is that nice sound?” asked Rabbit.

Fawn smiled. “It is my backyard band. I made it with things we already had. This is called re-use. It helps Earth. It is sustainable.”

Rabbit’s nose twitched. “Can I join?”

“Yes!” said Fawn. “Tap the drum like this. Tum-tum.”

Rabbit tapped. “Tum-tum-tum.” Rabbit’s ears wiggled with joy.

A bluebird landed on the fence. “Tweet! I can sing the words,” Bluebird said. Bluebird sang, “We craft with care, we love to share!”

Fawn shook the shaker. “Chika-chika!” The cymbals went “Clink!” The drum went “Tum!” The backyard was full of happy sound. The sun felt warm. The grass felt tickly on Fawn’s legs. The friends smiled.

After the song, Fawn said, “Let’s teach a word.” Fawn spoke slowly and clearly. “Re-use means we use things again in a new way. Innovate means we try new ideas. Craft means we make things with our hands and hearts.”

Rabbit repeated, “Re-use.” Bluebird repeated, “Innovate.” The ladybug repeated, “Craft.” They all laughed and clapped.

Fawn looked at the box. There were more treasures. “We can make a wind chime, too!” Fawn tied bottle caps and small sticks to a string and hung it from a tree. The wind blew. “Ting-ting-ting.” It sounded like little bells.

“Beautiful,” said Rabbit.

“Smart,” said Bluebird.

“Kind to Earth,” said the ladybug.

Fawn’s heart felt warm. “We solved our problem with care. We made music. We learned new words. We re-used and did not waste. We worked together.”

The sun began to slide down. The backyard glowed gold. Fawn packed the crafts neatly in a basket made of old twigs. “We will play again tomorrow,” Fawn said.

Fawn looked at the friends. “Today we learned: We can make fun things with simple, safe items. We can try again when there is a problem. We can be gentle with Earth. Crafting, re-using, and new ideas help us and our world.”

Everyone nodded. The wind chime sang a soft, “Ting-ting,” like a bedtime smile.

Fawn whispered, “Thank you, sunny backyard.”

The friends waved. “See you soon!”

The End.
