# Sunny Bird Sings at Supper

Sunny was a small, yellow singing bird with a bright, warm voice. She lived near a big house with a cozy dining room. The dining room smelled like fresh bread and sweet apples. The table was shiny, the napkins were soft, and a little clock went tick-tick-tick on the wall.

Sunny loved to sing. “Tweet-a-lee! Tweet-a-loo!” she sang from the window sill. Her song sounded like bubbles and sunshine. One day, Sunny heard the family say, “Let’s have a special supper with music tonight.”

Sunny’s heart fluttered. “I can sing for them!” she chirped. She flapped to the dining room window and peeked inside. She saw Mama setting plates, Papa pouring water, and two children, Mia and Max, putting out flowers. The room glowed golden from the lamp.

Sunny puffed up her feathers. “I will sing a big, beautiful song right now!” she said. And she took a deep breath. But—hic!—she squeaked. Her tummy rumbled. Her throat felt dry.

“Uh-oh,” Sunny whispered. “I didn’t drink water. I didn’t warm up. I didn’t plan what to sing.” She felt a little droop in her wings.

Just then, Mama looked up. “Oh! A sweet bird,” she said softly. “Hello, little singer.”

Sunny chirped, “Hello! I wanted to sing for your supper, but I am not ready.”

Mia tilted her head. “It’s okay,” she said. “We can help.”

Sunny took a tiny sip from a small bowl of water by the window. Sip, sip. Her throat felt better. “Thank you,” she said. “I think I need a plan.”

Papa smiled. “Good singers prepare,” he said. “What is your goal?”

Sunny blinked. “My goal is to sing a gentle song while you eat,” she said. “Not too loud. Not too long. Just right.”

Max clapped softly. “Let’s make steps,” he said. “Step one: drink water.”

“Step two: warm up,” said Mia. “Do your tweet-tweets!”

Sunny nodded. “Tweet, tweet, tweet,” she sang, soft and slow. She hummed low like a purring cat. Mmmmmm. She hummed high like a tiny bell. Ting-ting-ting! Her voice felt smooth and shiny, like a clean spoon.

“Step three,” Mama said, “pick the best time.”

Sunny peered at the clock. Tick-tick-tick. “I will sing after you take your first bites,” she said. “So you won’t be too hungry.”

“Step four,” Papa added, “make a plan for loud and soft.”

Sunny took a deep breath. “I will sing soft like a whisper wind,” she said. “If you need me to pause, I will pause.”

Mia and Max held up their thumbs. “Good plan!” they cheered.

Soon, the food came to the table—warm soup, crunchy carrots, and sweet apples. The smell was cozy. Sunny felt excited. She fluffed her feathers. “I am ready,” she whispered.

But when the family lifted their spoons, Sunny forgot her plan. She burst into a big, bright song. “TWEET-A-LEE! TWEET-A-LOO!” It was too loud. Max jumped. The soup wobbled. A carrot rolled onto the table.

Sunny stopped. Her cheeks—if birds had cheeks—felt hot. “I am so sorry,” she said quickly. “I forgot my plan. I sang too loud.”

Mia smiled kindly. “Thank you for your apology,” she said. “We know you want to help.”

Sunny tucked her wings. “Can we make a new plan together?” she asked. “Maybe we can agree on a sign.”

Papa nodded. “Let’s negotiate. We will set a sign,” he said. “If we put our hands on our ears, sing softer. If we tap the table once, pause. If we tap twice, sing again.”

Sunny chirped, “Yes! That is fair.” She felt lighter. She took a small breath and waited.

The family took their first bites. Crunch, slurp, munch. Papa tapped the table twice. Tap-tap. Sunny began a soft, swaying song. “Tweet-a-lee, tweet-a-loo,” like a warm breeze. The sound floated around the room, gentle and sweet.

Mia smiled. Max nodded. Mama put her hand on her ear. Sunny sang even softer, like snowflakes. Papa tapped once. Sunny paused and waited. After a moment, tap-tap. She sang again, a little brighter, like morning sun.

The dining room felt calm and happy. The clock went tick-tick-tick. The soup smelled good. The apples tasted crisp. Sunny’s song wrapped the room like a hug.

When the meal was done, everyone clapped quietly. “Bravo, Sunny,” said Mama. “Your song was just right.”

Sunny’s eyes sparkled. “Thank you for helping me prepare,” she said. “My goal was to sing a gentle song, and I did it.”

Max held up a tiny notebook. “Let’s write our plan,” he said. “Goal: gentle supper song. Steps: drink water, warm up, choose time, agree on signs.”

Mia added, “And remember to say sorry if we make a mistake.”

Sunny nodded. “And listen with empathy,” she said. “I listened to your signs. You listened to my song.”

Outside, the sky turned soft blue. Sunny felt proud and peaceful. “Preparation helps me do my best,” she chirped. “And apologizing and making a fair plan help friends feel good.”

She gave a final, tiny trill, as light as a feather. “Tweet,” she whispered.

The End.
