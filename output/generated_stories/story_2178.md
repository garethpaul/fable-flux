# Tully the Turtle Chooses

Tully was a shy turtle with a smooth, shiny shell. One rainy day, Tully peeked into the pantry. The pantry was cool and quiet. It smelled like cinnamon and warm bread. Little lights blinked on the shelf. Crinkle, crinkle went paper bags. Tap, tap went raindrops on the window.

Tully liked the pantry. It felt safe. But today was special. Today, Tully had to make a choice.

On the pantry shelf were jars and boxes from friends all around the world. Each friend had shared a snack for a big picnic. There was a jar of salsa from Sofia, who loved spicy tomatoes. There were sweet mango slices from Mohan, soft dumplings from Mei, crunchy plantain chips from Pedro, and a bowl of hummus from Hana. So many smells! So many colors!

Tully’s tummy gave a tiny rumble. “I want to help,” Tully whispered. “I want to pick a snack to bring to the picnic.” But Tully felt shy and unsure. What if the snack was too spicy? What if no one liked it? What if Tully chose the wrong thing?

He pulled his head into his shell. It was quiet inside. He listened to his heart go thump-thump, thump-thump.

“Hi, Tully!” said a soft voice. It was Mei the mouse, peeking from behind a rice basket. She wore a little blue scarf. “You look thoughtful.”

“I need to pick a snack,” Tully said, peeking out. “But I don’t know which one. I’m scared to decide.”

Mei smiled. “Deciding is like taking small steps. We can practice.”

“Practice?” Tully blinked.

“Yes,” said Mei. “First, look. Then, smell. Then, taste. We can try tiny bites. We can choose together.”

Tully took a slow breath. He looked at the shelf. He saw the bright red salsa, the golden mango, the puffy dumplings, the green bowl of hummus, and the tall bag of plantain chips.

He reached for the mango slices first. They were sticky and orange. “They look sunny,” Tully said.

“Let’s smell,” said Mei.

Tully sniffed. “Mmm! Sweet like sunshine.” He took a tiny taste. “Oh! It is soft and sweet.”

“Good practicing,” Mei said, clapping her tiny paws.

Next, Tully looked at the dumplings from Mei’s family. They were warm and bouncy. “They look like clouds,” Tully said.

“Smell,” Mei reminded.

Tully sniffed. “Mmm! Savory and cozy. Like soup in a hug.” He took a tiny nibble. “So soft!”

They tried a dot of hummus. It was creamy and lemony. “It tastes like a sunny garden,” Tully said. They crunched a plantain chip. “Crunch, crunch!” said Mei. It was salty and fun. They dipped the chip in hummus. “Yum!” they both said.

Tully looked at the salsa last. He was nervous. It was red and sparkly with little green bits. “I’m scared it’s too spicy,” he whispered.

Mei nodded. “We can try a tiny-tiny taste. We can have milk nearby if it’s hot.”

Tully took a small breath. He touched the tip of his tongue to a dot of salsa. “Tingle!” he said, eyes wide. He waited. “It is a little spicy… but also very tasty. Tomato, onion, lime.”

Mei poured a sip of milk. “You were brave,” she said.

Tully felt his shell warm with pride. “I practiced. I looked, smelled, and tasted. I made tiny choices.”

Mei pointed to the picnic basket. “Now, what will you bring?”

Tully thought. He closed his eyes. He remembered each taste, each smell, each feeling. Sweet mango. Cozy dumplings. Creamy hummus. Crunchy chips. Sparkly salsa. He opened his eyes and smiled.

“I will bring a mix,” he said. “I will bring plantain chips with hummus to dip. I will add mango for sweet. And I will bring a little salsa on the side for friends who like spicy. Then everyone can try. Everyone can choose.”

Mei’s whiskers wiggled. “That is a kind decision.”

Tully packed the basket carefully. Crunch, crunch went the chips. Swirl went the hummus. Plop went the mango. The little salsa jar jingled softly. Tully placed a small spoon and tiny cups so friends could taste first. He even made a sign that said: “Try a little. Choose what you like!”

At the picnic, friends gathered under the big umbrella tree. Sofia smiled when she saw the salsa. Mohan cheered for mango. Hana dipped a chip in hummus and hummed. Pedro crunched and laughed. Mei shared dumplings with everyone.

“Tully, this is perfect,” Sofia said. “You made a brave and smart choice.”

Tully’s cheeks felt warm. “I practiced making a decision,” he said. “I took small steps. I thought about everyone.”

Mei patted his shell. “You did it.”

The rain slowed to a whisper. The pantry door stood open, and the world smelled like fresh earth. Tully looked around. His shy heart felt bigger. He had been brave. He had practiced. He had chosen with care.

Tully smiled at his friends. “When a choice feels big, we can use small steps,” he said. “Look, smell, taste. Think. Then decide.”

Everyone nodded. They ate, they shared, and they learned about flavors from many places. The picnic was full of giggles and crunchy, creamy, sweet, and a tiny bit spicy bites.

And Tully the shy turtle? He felt proud, and a little less shy.

The End.
