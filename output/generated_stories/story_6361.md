# Panda Poppy’s Sunny Backyard Dance

In a sunny backyard, a patient panda named Poppy stepped onto the warm grass. The sun felt cozy on her black-and-white fur. Birds sang, “Chee-chee!” The breeze smelled like sweet flowers and fresh soil. Poppy stretched her paws up high and took a slow breath.

“Good morning, world,” Poppy said softly. “I am ready to learn and help.”

Mama Panda called from the porch, “Poppy, it’s backyard chore day!”

Poppy smiled. “I can help,” she said. She loved helping. Helping made her heart feel big and strong.

Mama handed Poppy a little basket. “We will pick up leaves, water the garden, and hang the laundry. Then we can dance.”

Poppy’s eyes sparkled. “Dance time! Maybe a dance from around the world!”

They started with the leaves. The leaves were crisp and crinkly. “Crunch, crunch,” they sang under Poppy’s paws. Poppy took her time. She did not rush. She picked up one leaf, then another.

A tiny ant marched by with a crumb on its back. Poppy knelt down. “Hello, little ant. You work hard too.” The ant wiggled its antennae and kept going. Poppy smiled. “We all help our homes.”

By the fence, their neighbor, Mr. Gómez, waved. “Buenos días, Poppy!” he said with a kind grin.

Poppy waved back. “Buenos días means good morning,” she told Mama. “People say hello in many ways.”

“That’s right,” said Mama. “We are all neighbors in this big world.”

When the leaves were done, they watered the garden. Poppy held the little watering can. Water whispered, “Drip, drop, drip,” onto the thirsty plants. The earth smelled rich, like warm chocolate cake. Poppy took small steps so the water went to each plant.

A butterfly with blue wings fluttered close. Poppy whispered, “Hello, butterfly. Thank you for visiting our flowers.” She felt calm and proud. Helping the garden helped the whole world a tiny bit.

Next, they hung laundry on the line. Soft shirts flapped like flags. Poppy touched each one. “This one is smooth. This one is bumpy.” She clipped them carefully. “Clip, click,” went the clothespins.

Mama clapped gently. “You are patient and careful, Poppy.”

Poppy nodded. “I am learning. I can grow a little every day.”

Just then, music floated from the radio on the porch. A drum went “dum-dum,” a guitar went “ting-ting,” and a flute went “whoooo.” Poppy’s ears perked up.

Mama smiled. “Now it’s time for a world dance break. Dancing makes our bodies and hearts feel well.”

Poppy bounced. “Yes! I want to learn dances from many places.”

Mama tapped the button. A happy beat began. “This is a cumbia from Colombia,” Mama said. “People dance it at parties and in the street. Around the world, people share music and joy.”

Poppy swayed her hips and stepped side to side. “Step, step, swish,” she whispered. She held the watering can like a pretend skirt and giggled. Mama laughed and joined her.

Next, a soft song played. “This is a hula from Hawai‘i,” Mama said. “Hands tell stories.”

Poppy lifted her paws and moved them like waves. “Whoosh, whoosh.” She imagined the big ocean. “In Hawai‘i, people care for the land and the sea,” Mama added. “We can care for our backyard too.”

Poppy nodded. “We are all part of one big home.”

The music changed again. A lively beat with claps and stomps filled the air. “This reminds me of flamenco from Spain,” Mama said.

Poppy straightened her back and clapped, “Clap, clap,” then stomped gently on the grass, “stomp, stomp.” She felt strong and brave.

After dancing, Poppy breathed in and out. “I feel happy,” she said. “My body feels warm. My heart feels open.”

Mama gave her a cool sip of water. “That’s wellness,” she said. “We care for our bodies, our feelings, and our world.”

Poppy looked around the sunny backyard. The leaves were tidy. The garden was watered. The laundry waved hello. She felt a small worry. “Did I do enough?” she asked. “The world is so big.”

Mama knelt down. “We start small,” she said. “We help at home. We learn new words. We listen to music from other places. We move our bodies with care. These small steps help us be kind global citizens.”

Poppy took that in slowly, like a warm sip of tea. “I can be patient with myself,” she said. “I can grow a little every day.”

Just then, Mr. Gómez came to the fence with a shiny red pepper. “Para ti, for you,” he said. “Thank you for keeping the garden neat. The bees like it.”

Poppy accepted the pepper with two paws. “Gracias,” she said carefully. “Thank you.”

Mr. Gómez smiled. “De nada. You’re welcome.” He showed a simple clap-and-step from his childhood. Poppy copied him. “Clap, step. Clap, step.” They laughed together across the fence.

Poppy felt a warm glow in her chest. She loved learning from friends. She loved caring for her backyard. She loved dancing across the world with her feet on home grass.

As the sun began to drift lower, Mama said, “What did you learn today, Poppy?”

Poppy thought. “I learned that people around the world say hello in different ways. We dance different dances. We share food and stories. When I help at home and listen and learn, I am a helper to the whole world.”

Mama hugged her. “Yes, my patient panda. That is global citizenship.”

Poppy looked up at the bright blue sky. “I will keep helping,” she said. “I will keep learning. I will keep dancing.”

And the backyard, warm and sunny, seemed to hum, “Yes.”

The End.
