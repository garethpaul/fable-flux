# Paws and Pancakes

In a warm kitchen, a friendly bear cub named Bibi wiggled her nose. The air smelled like cinnamon, butter, and sweet berries. Sunlight made cozy patches on the wooden floor. The kettle hummed a soft song.

“Pancakes!” Bibi cheered, clapping her paws. “I want the biggest stack!”

Mama Bear stirred a bowl. “Good morning, Bibi,” she said with a smile. “Pancakes take teamwork.”

“But I am hungry now,” Bibi said. Her tummy gave a happy rumble.

Mama scooped batter. The pan sizzled. “Ssssss,” it sang. “While we wait, let’s do our morning chores,” Mama said. “We set the table, we wash the berries, we wipe the crumbs.”

Bibi frowned. “Chores are slow. I just want to eat.”

Just then, there was a gentle knock at the back door. Tap-tap. Mama opened it. Three friendly neighbors peeked in, bringing warm smiles and colorful bowls.

Nana Goose waddled in with a jar. “Maple syrup from my tree,” she said. “In my home, we say, ‘Thank you, tree,’ before we pour.”

Milo Mouse carried a tiny bowl. “I brought nut butter,” he squeaked. “My grandpa taught me to stir it smooth.”

Auntie Panda held a basket of fruit. “These are dragon fruit and mango,” she said. “My family slices them in pretty shapes. We say, ‘Thank you, growers.’”

Bibi’s eyes grew wide. “Wow,” she whispered. “So many yummy things!”

Mama Bear nodded. “Many paws help make a meal,” she said. “Let’s think about what we need.”

Bibi looked around. The table was empty. The sink had a pile of blue cups. The counter had a few crumbs.

Bibi took a deep breath. “Okay,” she said. “I can help.” She put on a small apron with honeybees on it. The fabric felt soft on her fur.

“What should I do first?” Bibi asked.

“Good question,” said Mama. “Let’s use our thinking paws.”

Bibi wriggled her paws and looked at the kitchen. “First, we need clean cups,” she decided. “Then we need plates. Then the napkins.”

“Excellent plan,” Mama said.

Bibi washed the cups. Splash-swish, splash-swish. The water was warm on her paws. She set them on a towel. Tap-tap, the drops fell like tiny rain.

“I will set the plates,” Bibi said. She counted slowly. “One, two, three, four, five.” She placed a plate for everyone. The plates made a gentle clink. She put napkins next to each one, folding them into triangles.

Milo climbed onto a stool. “I can stir the nut butter,” he said. Thup-thup went the spoon.

Auntie Panda cut the dragon fruit into stars. The kitchen smelled bright and sweet. Nana Goose polished the syrup jar. “Thank you, tree,” she honked kindly.

Bibi looked at the pan. “Is it time now?” she asked.

Mama Bear flipped a pancake. Swish-flip! The pancake puffed and turned golden. “Almost,” Mama said. “We still have crumbs to wipe.”

Bibi picked up a cloth. Wipe, wipe. The counter felt smooth now. “I did it!” she said.

“Your helping paws are strong,” Mama said. “How does your body feel?”

“Warm and proud,” Bibi said. Her chest felt big and happy. Her tummy rumbled again.

They all sat down. The pancakes were soft and fluffy. The syrup smelled like a sweet forest. Bibi poured a little, and whispered, “Thank you, tree.” She spread nut butter, and said, “Thank you, Milo and Grandpa.” She added star fruit and mango, and said, “Thank you, growers.”

Bibi took a bite. “Mmm,” she said. “This tastes like love.”

Mama smiled. “What made it taste so good?”

Bibi thought. She touched her chin. “Chores helped,” she said. “We washed and set and wiped. We thought first. We worked together. And we said thank you.”

Nana Goose nodded. “Gratitude makes food sweeter.”

Auntie Panda clapped softly. “You used your brain and your paws,” she said. “That is skill-building.”

Milo Mouse squeaked, “You planned! That is critical thinking.”

Bibi’s ears perked up. “I used my planning paws!” she said with a giggle.

When the plates were clean and the tummies were full, Bibi stood and looked at the sink. “There are sticky plates,” she said. “We can help again.”

Mama Bear winked. “Your thinking paws are still working.”

Together, they cleaned. Splash-swish, stack-stack, wipe-wipe. The kitchen felt warm and shiny. The kettle hummed a goodbye note.

Bibi hugged Mama. “Thank you for pancakes,” she said.

Mama hugged back. “Thank you for helping.”

Bibi turned to their friends. “Thank you for the syrup, the nut butter, and the pretty fruit stars,” she said. “Thank you for your ways from your homes. I learned new words and new shapes!”

Nana Goose smiled. “Gratitude grows like a tree.”

Bibi grinned. “Next time, I will say thank you first. Then plan my chores. Then use my strong paws.”

Mama Bear nodded. “That sounds like a good plan.”

Bibi’s heart felt cozy and bright. She looked at the clean kitchen and the happy friends. “Gratitude makes our home warm,” she said.

The End.
