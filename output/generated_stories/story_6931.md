# Gigi's Tall Tale

Gigi the giraffe loved to help in the kitchen pantry. Her long neck could reach the highest shelves where the colorful sprinkles and special baking supplies lived. But today, something was bothering her.

"Time for our cooking class!" announced Mrs. Bear, the teacher. All the little animals gathered around the low table in the pantry. There were rabbits, squirrels, hedgehogs, and mice - all much shorter than Gigi.

Gigi bent her long neck down, down, down to see the mixing bowls on the table. Her knees bumped into the shelves. Her head felt heavy from looking down so much.

"Ouch," whispered Gigi softly. Her neck was starting to hurt.

Little Mouse was measuring flour. "Can you pass the vanilla, Gigi?" she squeaked.

Gigi stretched up to the high shelf and carefully brought down the small brown bottle. The sweet smell of vanilla filled the air. But when she bent back down, her neck ached even more.

"Are you okay, Gigi?" asked Bunny, noticing Gigi's sad face.

Gigi wanted to say something, but she felt shy. She didn't want to cause trouble. "I'm fine," she said quietly, even though she wasn't fine at all.

As the cooking lesson continued, Gigi's neck hurt more and more. She couldn't mix the colorful batter properly because she had to bend so far down. Her beautiful yellow spots felt all twisted up.

Finally, Gigi took a deep breath. She remembered what her mama had taught her: "When something doesn't feel right, it's okay to speak up for yourself."

"Mrs. Bear?" Gigi said in her gentle voice. "I have something important to say."

Mrs. Bear looked up with kind eyes. "What is it, dear?"

"The table is too low for me," Gigi explained. "My neck hurts from bending down so much. I want to help and have fun too, but I need something different."

Mrs. Bear's face lit up with understanding. "Oh my! Thank you for telling me, Gigi. That's very brave of you to speak up."

"Can we help?" asked the little animals together.

Mrs. Bear clapped her paws. "I have an idea! Gigi, what if we make a special tall cooking station just for you?"

Working together, they moved some sturdy wooden crates to make a higher surface. Gigi could stand comfortably without bending her neck. The sweet smell of cinnamon and sugar filled the pantry as everyone got back to work.

"This is perfect!" said Gigi, her neck feeling much better. She could reach the high ingredients and work at her own level too.

"Now we can all cook together comfortably," said Squirrel, stirring chocolate chips into the mix.

As they decorated their cookies with colorful icing, Gigi felt proud and happy. The cookies looked like little rainbows - red, blue, yellow, and green swirls dancing together.

"Thank you for speaking up, Gigi," said Mrs. Bear. "When you told us what you needed, it helped everyone. Now our pantry works better for all of us."

Gigi smiled, her long eyelashes fluttering. "It felt scary at first, but I'm glad I said something."

The little animals cheered, and they all enjoyed their beautiful, delicious cookies together. The pantry smelled like happiness and friendship.

From that day on, Gigi always remembered that speaking up for herself wasn't selfish - it was important and helpful for everyone.

The End.
