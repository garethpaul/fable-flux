# Rocco’s Backyard Band and the Sparkly Wash

Rocco was a musical raccoon with a stripey tail and bright, curious eyes. He lived by a sunny backyard where flowers nodded and bees hummed. Every morning, the grass felt cool and tickly on his toes, and the air smelled like sweet oranges and warm dirt. Rocco loved to make music. He tapped on pots, strummed rubber bands, and shook seed pods that sounded like tiny maracas.

“Good morning, band!” Rocco sang to his backyard friends. “Let’s play a happy song!”

Chirp the little bird fluttered down to the fence. “Chirp-chirp! I’ll sing with you!”

Buzzy the bee buzzed near the clover. “Bzz-bzz! I’ll make a gentle drum!”

And Willa the snail peeked from under a leaf. “I can keep the beat,” she whispered, “very slowly.”

Rocco grinned. He picked up his shiny spoon drum. But today, the spoon was not shiny. It was sticky and dull. Rocco’s paws were sticky too, with jam from his breakfast berries and smudges from painting a picture of the sun.

He tried to play. Tap-tap. The spoon stuck to his paw. It made a funny schlop sound.

Chirp tilted her head. “Your drum sounds sleepy.”

Buzzy buzzed in a circle. “Bzz? Your paws look messy.”

Rocco looked at his paws. They were brown and purple and a little muddy. His whiskers had paint dots, and his mask had a jelly kiss. He frowned. “But I want to play now.”

He shook a seed pod. Instead of a happy shake-shake, it went clump. A leaf had glued itself to the seeds with sticky jam.

“My music isn’t sparkly,” Rocco sighed. “It sounds like a nap.”

Chirps sang softly, “Maybe your band needs a little… wash?”

Rocco’s ears perked. He remembered Mama Raccoon’s words: “Clean paws help you do your best. Wash, brush, rest, and then you can shine.”

He looked around the sunny backyard. There was a rain barrel by the fence, a bar of soap on a little dish, and a soft towel hanging on a branch. The water in the barrel shimmered like a blue drum. It smelled fresh, like rain on leaves.

Rocco took a deep breath. “Friends, I have a plan. If I care for me, my music will care for you.”

Buzzy buzzed “Yes!” Willa nodded slowly. Chirp chirped a cheerful “Do it!”

First, Rocco set his instruments neatly on a dry blanket. “Art needs care,” he said. “So do I.” He put the sticky leaf in the compost bucket. “Conservation means we help the earth, too.”

Then Rocco rolled up his sleeves. Splash! He dipped his paws into the cool water. “Oooh!” The water felt soft and tickly. He added some soap. Squish-squish. Bubbles grew like tiny stars. They smelled like lemons. Rocco laughed. “Hello, bubble stars!”

He rubbed his paws, front and back. “Round and round, wiggle the thumbs, scrub-scrub-scrub,” he sang. He cleaned between his fingers. He washed under his nails. He rinsed until the water ran clear, and the bubbles floated away like clouds.

Chirp hopped closer. “Don’t forget your face.”

Rocco nodded. He dipped a corner of the towel, wiped his whiskers, and pat-pat-patted his mask. He brushed his teeth with a little brush from his pack. Swish, swish. Minty fresh! “Ahh,” he said, smiling. “My mouth feels like a clean bell.”

He combed his fur with gentle strokes. Smooth and soft! He took a little rest in the shade, closing his eyes for a moment. A breeze whispered through leaves. The world felt bright and calm.

When he felt ready, Rocco looked at his instruments. “Your turn,” he said kindly. He wiped the spoon drum. Shine! He rinsed the seed pods and set them in the sun to dry. He brushed dust off his rubber band guitar. Twang! He even put a bowl under the rain barrel spout to catch drips. “We save water by not wasting it,” he told his friends. “Conservation helps everyone.”

Willa clapped her tiny feelers. “You cared for you. You cared for your tools. You cared for the earth.”

Rocco’s eyes sparkled. “I feel fresh. I feel ready. Let’s make art!”

They began to play. Tap-tap-tap! The spoon drum danced. Shake-shake-shake! The seed pods chimed. Twang-twang! The rubber bands sang. Chirp’s voice swooped, sweet and clear. Buzzy’s gentle bzz-bzz was the softest drum. Even the wind hummed along. The music sounded bright, like sunshine on water.

Rocco laughed with joy. “My clean paws move fast and light. My drum shines and sings!”

They played a song about washing hands. They played a song about brushing teeth. They played a song about saving water and loving the backyard.

When the songs were done, Rocco bowed. “Thank you, friends. Today I learned a big thing. Self-care is caring. When I wash, brush, rest, and tidy my tools, my music is better, and I feel happy.”

Chirp chirped, “Clean helps you shine!”

Buzzy buzzed, “Care helps us all!”

Willa smiled slowly. “Small steps, big glow.”

Rocco nodded. “I will make a little routine. After I eat, I wash. After I paint, I clean. I save water, I put scraps in compost, and I take breaks. That is my kind care.”

The sun warmed their backs. The backyard smelled like lemon bubbles and green grass. Rocco lifted his spoon drum for one last tap. It rang like a bright bell.

“Self-care helps me share,” he said softly.

The End.
