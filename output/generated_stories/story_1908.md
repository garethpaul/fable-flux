# Benny Bunny's Big Art Adventure

Benny Bunny loved to hop and play. He was the fastest runner in the meadow and always won at hopscotch. Today, he bounced into the bright, colorful art room at school, his fluffy tail wiggling with excitement.

"Look at all the pretty colors!" Benny said, his pink nose twitching. The art room smelled like sweet crayons and fresh paper. Rainbow paintbrushes stood in tall jars, and glittery stickers sparkled on the shelves.

Mrs. Owl, their teacher, clapped her feathery wings. "Today we're making friendship paintings! You'll work in teams of two."

Benny's ears perked up. Teams? That was like sports! He loved winning games.

"I want ALL the red paint!" Benny announced loudly, grabbing three big bottles. "Red is the fastest color, just like me!"

His partner, Lily Mouse, looked sad. Her whiskers drooped. "But Benny, I need red too. I want to paint strawberries."

"No!" Benny hugged the paint bottles tight. "I'm the best at sports, so I should get the best colors!" His foot thumped on the floor - thump, thump, thump.

Lily's eyes filled with tears. "That's not fair, Benny."

Benny started painting, but something felt wrong. His picture looked empty with just red paint. He wanted to add a blue sky and yellow sun, but Lily was using those colors for her side of their shared paper.

"Your painting looks so pretty," Benny said quietly, watching Lily paint beautiful purple flowers and green grass.

Mrs. Owl hopped over. "How are you two doing?"

"Benny won't share the red paint," Lily sniffled.

Mrs. Owl's big eyes looked kind but serious. "Benny, what happens in sports when team members don't work together?"

Benny thought hard. His ears drooped. "The team loses?"

"That's right," Mrs. Owl nodded. "And what makes a painting beautiful?"

Benny looked at the other tables. Friends were laughing, mixing colors, and creating amazing pictures together. Their paintings had every color of the rainbow!

"Lots of colors working together?" Benny asked softly.

"Exactly!" Mrs. Owl smiled.

Benny felt his tummy feel funny - not angry anymore, but sorry. He took a deep breath, just like his mom taught him when he felt upset.

"Lily," Benny said, his voice gentle. "I'm sorry I was mean. Want to share all our colors?"

Lily's face lit up like sunshine. "Really?"

"Really! We can make the most beautiful painting ever - together!"

Benny carefully poured some red paint into a cup for Lily. She shared her blue and yellow with him. Soon they were mixing colors, creating orange and purple and green.

"Look!" Lily giggled. "When we mix your red with my yellow, we get orange!"

"Wow!" Benny's eyes grew wide. "It's like magic! And mixing is like strategy in sports - we have to think and plan together!"

Their painting showed a beautiful garden with red strawberries, yellow sun, blue sky, and purple flowers. It was the most colorful, happy painting in the whole room.

"We make a great team," Benny said, giving Lily a gentle high-five with his paw.

"The best team!" Lily agreed.

Mrs. Owl hung their painting on the wall. "This shows what happens when friends share and work together."

Benny learned that sharing made everything more fun and beautiful. Just like in sports, the best victories came from working as a team.

From that day on, Benny always remembered: sharing colors, sharing toys, and sharing kindness made every day brighter.

The End.
