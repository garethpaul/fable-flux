# Ollie's Garden Workout

Ollie the otter lived in a cozy apartment with his family. Every morning, Mama Otter would say, "Time for exercise on the balcony, Ollie!"

But today, Ollie didn't want to move. He curled up in his soft blanket and made grumpy sounds. "Grr! I don't want to exercise! It's boring!"

His whiskers twitched with anger. His little paws crossed tight over his chest.

"Exercise helps us feel happy and strong," said Mama Otter gently. "Come see our beautiful garden."

Ollie shuffled slowly to the balcony. The morning sun felt warm on his brown fur. Colorful flowers danced in the breeze - red roses, yellow sunflowers, and purple lavender.

"I still don't want to exercise," Ollie grumbled. His tail slapped the ground with frustration.

Just then, their neighbor Mrs. Chen appeared on her balcony next door. She was doing slow, graceful movements with her arms.

"Good morning, Ollie!" Mrs. Chen smiled. "Would you like to try tai chi with me? It's like dancing with the wind."

Ollie watched her gentle movements. They looked like swimming motions in the air. His anger started to melt away like ice cream in sunshine.

"What's tai chi?" Ollie asked, his curiosity growing.

"It's exercise from my home country, China," Mrs. Chen explained. "We move slowly and breathe deeply. It makes our bodies and minds peaceful."

Ollie tried copying her movements. He stretched his arms up high like reaching for clouds. He bent down low like diving for fish. His breathing became calm and steady.

"This feels nice!" Ollie giggled. "My anger is floating away!"

From another balcony, their friend Carlos called out, "¡Hola! Want to try some salsa dancing? It's from my family in Mexico!"

Carlos showed them quick, happy steps. Ollie bounced and wiggled, his tail swishing with joy. The flowers seemed to dance along with them.

"Dancing makes my heart feel bouncy!" Ollie laughed.

Mrs. Kim from upstairs leaned over her railing. "In Korea, we do morning stretches too! Try reaching for the sky like growing bamboo!"

Soon all the neighbors were exercising together on their balconies. Ollie learned stretches from Korea, breathing exercises from India, and jumping games from Brazil.

"Look at our garden community!" Mama Otter smiled. "Everyone exercises differently, but we all feel better together."

Ollie's anger was completely gone now. His body felt strong and wiggly. His mind felt clear and happy.

"I love our morning exercise time!" Ollie declared. "Can we do this every day?"

"Of course!" said Mama Otter. "When we exercise regularly, it becomes a happy habit."

The next morning, Ollie jumped out of bed before Mama could even call him. "Time for our balcony workout!" he announced cheerfully.

He waved to all his neighbors as they began their morning exercises. The garden sparkled with dewdrops, and the air smelled fresh and sweet.

Ollie had learned that exercise wasn't boring at all. It was a wonderful way to start each day, meet friends, and learn about different cultures. Most importantly, when he felt angry or grumpy, moving his body helped those feelings swim away.

From that day forward, Ollie looked forward to his daily exercise routine in their beautiful balcony garden, surrounded by friends from around the world.

The End.
