# Rooftop Rainbow Roar

Little Leo was a brave lion cub with bright eyes and a soft golden mane. One sunny morning, he padded up the stairs to the rooftop. The air was warm. The breeze was gentle. “Mmm,” Leo sniffed. “I smell flowers and fresh bread.” He could hear the city humming below—beep, beep, chug, chug, chatter, chatter.

On the rooftop, there were pots of green plants, a big water barrel, and a gray wall that looked plain and sleepy. Leo tilted his head. “This wall looks bored,” he said. “Maybe it wants some color.”

Just then, Leo heard tiny paws pattering. His neighbors popped up from the stairwell: Mina the monkey with curly tail, Tiko the turtle with a shiny shell, and Afi the pigeon with speckled wings. They all lived in different homes on the same street. They spoke in different ways, but they were friends.

“Hi, Leo!” Mina chirped. “What are you doing?”

“I want to make the wall smile,” Leo said. “I want to make art.”

Afi cooed, “Art on the rooftop? Coo, coo! That sounds nice.”

Tiko blinked slowly. “We should ask,” he said. “The roof is for everyone.”

Leo nodded. “You are right. We must be careful and kind.” He went to find Ms. Hana, the building helper. She was watering the plants with a blue can.

“Ms. Hana,” Leo said, standing tall but gentle. “May we make art on the rooftop wall? We will keep it clean. We will share space. We will make it beautiful for everyone.”

Ms. Hana smiled. “What a brave and thoughtful idea, Leo. Yes, you may, if you use safe paint and keep the path clear.”

“Thank you!” Leo’s whiskers wiggled with joy. He ran back to the roof. “We can do it!”

Mina clapped. “But we don’t have paint!”

Leo looked around. The breeze brushed his ears. “We can be resourceful,” he said. “Let’s see what we have.”

They searched the rooftop. Afi found old chalk sticks in a tin. Mina found a box with brushes and big paper. Tiko found fruit peels from the compost—purple beet peels and bright orange peels. Leo found small pots with crushed flowers from last week’s bouquet.

Leo’s eyes sparkled. “We can make colors!”

They crushed berries in a bowl. Squish, squish. They mixed flower petals with water. Swish, swish. They rubbed beet peels on paper. Squeak, squeak. The rooftop smelled sweet and earthy. The colors were soft and bright—pink like sunrise, green like leaves, blue like the sky.

Afi fluttered. “What will we draw?”

Leo whispered, “Let’s draw our street. Let’s draw our friends. Let’s draw how we feel.”

Mina hopped. “I will draw drums! Boom, boom!” She tapped the paper with her brush.

Tiko smiled. “I will draw a garden. Leaf by leaf, slow and steady.”

Afi cooed, “I will draw the sky, with birds from many places. Coo, coo.”

Leo dipped his brush. He painted a big, gentle lion roar. But it was not a loud roar. It was a rainbow roar! Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple waves rolled across the gray wall. The colors made the wall look awake and happy.

Neighbors came to see. Mr. Ali brought mint tea that smelled fresh. Grandma Mei brought steamed buns, warm and soft. Little Zuri clapped to a tiny drum. “Pretty!” she said.

Leo looked around. He saw brown hands, tan hands, pale hands, furry paws, and feathery wings. He heard many words—hello, jambo, ni hao, hola. His heart felt big. “We all bring our colors,” he said. “We all bring our songs.”

A small cloud drifted by, and a cool wind blew. The paper started to flap. “Oh no!” Mina cried. “Our art will fly away!”

Leo took a calm breath. “We can fix it together.” He looked at the plant pots. “We can use clean stones to hold the paper down.” Tiko nodded and helped place smooth stones at the corners. Afi held the edges with gentle wings. Mina tied a soft string from the railing to the paper. The paper stayed. The colors stayed. The art stayed.

Ms. Hana came back and checked the path. “Good job keeping it clear,” she said. “You made a safe art place.”

When the sun began to set, the rooftop glowed gold. The wall shone with the rainbow roar, the dancing drums, the green garden, and the flying birds. People smiled. A baby giggled. Someone hummed a tune.

Leo felt brave and proud, but also kind and calm. He stood in front of the wall and said, “This is our story. We used what we had. We shared. We asked. We cared. We made beauty for everyone.”

Mina grinned. “Art can help us speak.”

Tiko nodded. “Art can help us help.”

Afi cooed, “Art can hold all our wings.”

Leo lifted his chin. “When we make art, we show our hearts.”

They cleaned up together. They washed the bowls. They stacked the brushes. They put the stones back by the plants. The rooftop was tidy and bright.

As stars peeked out, Leo whispered, “I learned something today. I can be brave and kind. I can use my ideas to make the world better. My roar can be colors.”

Everyone cheered softly, “Yes.”

And the rooftop, once plain and sleepy, now smiled in rainbow light.

The End.
