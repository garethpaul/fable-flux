# Finn's Rooftop Adventure

Little Finn the fox kit loved to explore. His orange fur sparkled in the morning sun as he climbed up, up, up to the rooftop of the old red barn. The rooftop was his favorite place to play!

"Wow!" Finn said, looking around with his bright amber eyes. "I can see everything from up here!" He could smell the sweet flowers below and feel the warm breeze tickling his whiskers.

On the rooftop, Finn found something wonderful - a pile of shiny acorns that the squirrels had stored there. His fluffy tail wagged with excitement. "These are perfect for my collection!" he said. "I want to gather ALL of them!"

Finn set a goal: "I will collect every single acorn and keep them all for myself!" He began stuffing the acorns into his small backpack, one by one. *Clink, clink, clink* went the acorns as they bumped together.

Just then, he heard a tiny voice. "Excuse me," squeaked Rosie the red squirrel, poking her head over the roof's edge. Her little paws were empty, and her tummy rumbled loudly. "Those are my family's acorns. We've been saving them for winter. Could I please have some back?"

Finn hugged his backpack tighter. "No way! I found them first. They're mine now!" His ears drooped as he turned away from Rosie.

Rosie's eyes filled with tears. "But my baby brothers and sisters are so hungry," she whispered. "We worked all summer to gather those acorns."

Finn felt something funny in his tummy - not hunger, but something that didn't feel good. He watched Rosie's sad face and heard her tummy growling. The warm sunshine didn't feel as nice anymore.

"Wait," Finn said softly. He thought about how sad he would feel if someone took all his favorite toys. "Rosie, I'm really sorry. I was being selfish."

Finn opened his backpack and looked at all the acorns. They made a lovely *rustle* sound as he moved them around. "You know what? I have a new goal," he announced. "I want to share these acorns with you and your family!"

Rosie's whiskers twitched with surprise. "Really? You'd share with us?"

"Yes!" Finn smiled, his tail wagging again. "Sharing feels much better than keeping everything for myself." He carefully divided the acorns into two piles. "Here's half for your family, and I'll keep half for mine."

Rosie clapped her tiny paws together. "Thank you, Finn! You're so kind!" She gave him a warm, fuzzy hug that smelled like pine needles and sunshine.

"And you know what?" Finn added, his eyes twinkling. "Maybe we can be rooftop friends! We could play games up here together!"

"I'd love that!" Rosie giggled. "I know the best spots to watch the sunset!"

As they sat on the rooftop sharing the acorns, Finn felt warm and happy inside. The acorns tasted even more delicious when shared with a friend. The gentle wind carried the sweet smell of flowers, and both friends felt their tummies getting full and happy.

"I learned something important today," Finn said, crunching on a tasty acorn. "Sharing makes everything better - even the sunshine feels warmer!"

Rosie nodded, her cheeks full of acorn. "And saying sorry when we make mistakes helps us become better friends!"

From that day on, Finn and Rosie met on the rooftop every afternoon, sharing snacks and watching the clouds drift by like fluffy white pillows in the sky.

The End.
