# The Fair Share in the Cozy Chair

In a comfortable nursery, soft and warm, a determined rabbit named Remy twitched his nose. The room smelled like clean blankets and apple soap. A sunbeam rested on the rug. Pillows made a little hill. Books lined the low shelf. Toys waited in tidy baskets.

Remy loved story time. Today, the big comfy chair by the window felt extra cozy. Remy hopped closer, ears up and whiskers wiggling. “I will sit and read,” he said. His paws felt soft on the round rug. He hugged his favorite book, The Moon Carrot.

But just then, Teddy Bear plopped into the big chair first. “Mine!” Teddy said, patting the armrest. His fur was fuzzy and smelled like lavender.

Remy’s heart thumped. His tummy felt tight. “I was going to sit,” Remy said. His voice shook a little. “It was my turn yesterday.”

Teddy crossed his paws. “I got here first,” he said. “I want the whole chair.”

Remy’s face grew warm. He felt a pinch of frustration. He wanted to stomp. He wanted to shout. Instead, he took a deep breath. In and out. He rubbed his ears. “I feel mad,” he whispered. “I need help.”

Just then, their friends peeked from the pillow hill. Mina Mouse, with tiny pink ears. Lila Lamb, soft and calm. Timmy Tortoise, slow and wise. They heard the upset voices.

“What’s wrong?” asked Mina Mouse, skittering close.

“We both want the big chair,” Remy said. “I think it is my turn. Teddy says it is his.”

“That feels unfair,” Lila Lamb said gently. “We can make it fair.”

“Justice means fair for everyone,” Timmy Tortoise said. His shell clicked softly on the floor. “We can use teamwork.”

Remy listened. His paws stopped shaking. “Teamwork?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Mina. “We can figure it out together.”

Remy nodded. “Okay.” He looked at Teddy. “My tummy feels tight. I want a turn. How do you feel?”

Teddy’s button eyes blinked. He looked at Remy’s ears, droopy and sad. “I feel grabby,” Teddy said slowly. “I thought I would miss my turn. I got here first because I was worried.”

Remy took another breath. “Thank you for telling me,” he said. “I want justice. I want fair.”

Timmy tapped his chin. “Let’s make a plan. We can share the chair. Or we can take turns. Or we can make two cozy spots.”

Mina Mouse squeaked, “I have an idea! We can use the timer. When it dings, we switch.”

Lila Lamb smiled. “And we can add pillows to make another cozy spot, so no one feels left out while they wait.”

Remy’s whiskers twitched. He liked plans. “Yes. That is fair.” He looked at Teddy. “Do you agree?”

Teddy hugged his knees. He looked at the sunny chair. He looked at the pillow hill. He took a slow breath like Remy. “I agree,” he said. “Fair means both get a turn.”

They worked together. Teamwork felt good. Mina and Timmy gathered pillows that smelled like sunshine. Lila spread a soft blue blanket on the rug. Remy set the little egg timer on the windowsill. Teddy scooted to one side of the big chair to make room for the next turn.

“First, Remy,” said Lila, “for three dings. Then, Teddy, for three dings.”

Remy climbed into the chair. The fabric felt warm. The book was smooth in his paws. He read out loud. “The Moon Carrot shines bright,” he said. His voice grew steady. The friends listened. The timer went tick, tick, tick. Remy’s frustration floated away like a little cloud.

Ding! Ding! Ding!

Remy hopped down. He smiled at Teddy. “Your turn,” he said. His stomach felt calm now. He felt proud. He had used his words. He had waited. He had been fair.

Teddy climbed up. “Thank you,” he said. He patted the seat beside him. “And you can sit on the pillow hill and read with me.”

Remy settled on the soft pillows. They were squishy and smelled like fresh cotton. Lila cuddled close. Mina perched on the arm. Timmy tucked by the blanket edge. Teddy read in a deep, cozy voice. “The Moon Carrot shines for all.”

As they read, they used their imaginations. The nursery became a night garden. Silver light covered the rug. The pillows turned into gentle hills. Stars winked on the bookshelf. A cool breeze felt like a hug. They all looked up and pretended to nibble the glowing moon carrot. Crunch, crunch! It tasted like sweet pear and snow.

Ding! Ding! Ding!

Teddy stopped and smiled. “Remy, do you want to finish the story together?” he asked.

“Yes,” Remy said. “We can read it as a team.” He hopped back into the chair beside Teddy. They shared the space, shoulder to shoulder. They took turns with the words. Their voices danced like little bells.

When the book ended, everyone clapped softly. The nursery felt even warmer. Remy’s heart felt big and light.

Lila Lamb said, “We made a fair plan.”

Timmy Tortoise nodded. “That is justice. We listened. We took turns. We shared.”

Mina Mouse added, “And we helped when feelings were hard.”

Remy hugged Teddy. “Justice means fair for everyone,” he said. “Teamwork helps. Breathing helps too.”

Teddy smiled. “We can do it again tomorrow.”

They put the book back on the shelf. The sunbeam slid across the floor like a golden ribbon. The friends curled up on the pillow hill, peaceful and proud.

The End.
