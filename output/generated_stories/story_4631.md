# Panda Patches and the Fix-It Routine

Panda Patches lived next to a little garage workshop that smelled like warm wood, shiny metal, and orange soap. Every morning, Patches padded in on soft paws. Tap-tap went his toes on the floor. Clink-clink went the tools on the wall. The bright window let in a stripe of sun that felt cozy on his fur.

Patches loved to build and fix. He was a patient panda. He took deep, slow breaths that sounded like “hoo—haa,” and he liked to do things in a calm, steady way. He had a routine. He sang it as a song.

“First I look. Then I plan.
Then I gather what I can.
I try, try slow, and check each part.
I finish proud with a happy heart.”

“Good morning, Patches!” chirped a tiny blue bird on the window ledge.

“Good morning, Bluebird,” said Patches, smiling. “It’s Fix-It Time!”

Today, a small red wagon sat in the center of the workshop. One wheel was wobbly. The wagon creaked. Eee-eee! It made a sad squeak.

A little bunny hopped in, nose twitching. “Can you fix my wagon, please?” the bunny asked. “I want to pull carrots.”

Patches waved. “Yes, we can fix it. We will use my routine.”

Bluebird tilted her head. “Maybe just push it and see if it works!” she tweeted.

Patches shook his head kindly. “First I look.” He crouched down and looked at the wheel. He felt it with his paws. The metal felt cool. The rubber felt bouncy. He listened. Eee-eee!

“Then I plan,” Patches said. He imagined the steps in his head. He thought, “I will need a rag, a wrench, some oil, and a new nut.”

The bunny bounced. “Can we skip to the end?” he asked.

Patches smiled. “Routine helps us do it right. Routine keeps us safe.”

He pointed to his shelf. “Then I gather what I can.” He picked up a soft rag. He chose a small wrench that fit his paw. He got a tiny bottle of oil that smelled like lemons. He found a shiny new nut in a small blue box.

“Now I try, try slow,” he said. He wiped the wheel clean. Swish-swish. He dabbed a drop of oil. Drip. He held the wheel still with one paw and turned the old nut with the wrench. Click… click… clack! The nut was loose. He took it off and put it in a cup.

Bluebird fluttered closer. “Can I help?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Patches. “Please hold the light.” Bluebird held a little lamp. The warm yellow light made the silver shine. The bunny held the wagon steady with gentle paws.

Patches slid on the new nut. He turned the wrench. Tight, but not too tight. “Check each part,” he hummed. He wiggled the wheel. It felt snug. He spun it. Whirr… smooth and soft. No more squeak!

The bunny tried the wagon. He pulled it a little. Then he pulled it fast. The wheel spun and did not wobble. “It works!” he cheered.

“Wait,” said Patches. He put the wrench back on the hook. Clink. He cleaned up the rag. Fold. He closed the oil. Twist. He put the cup of old nuts on the shelf. Tap. “Finish proud with a happy heart.”

Bluebird whispered, “Why do you always put things away, Patches?”

Patches patted his chest. “My routine helps me find tools next time. It keeps the workshop safe. It builds my skills. Every day, I learn a little more.”

Just then, another problem rolled in. A tiny scooter leaned by the door. Its handlebar was crooked.

Bluebird chirped, “Let’s fix it fast!”

Patches took a deep breath. Hoo—haa. He made a decision. “We will use the same routine.”

“First I look.” He saw a loose bolt.

“Then I plan.” He would need a small screwdriver and a soft cloth.

“Then I gather what I can.” He found the tools and a strip of tape to mark the straight line.

“I try, try slow.” He lined up the handlebar with the tape. He turned the bolt. Turn, turn. Stop. He checked each part. He made sure the bar was straight. He tested it. The scooter rolled smooth. The bunny giggled, “Vroom!”

The garage smelled like orange soap and lemons. The sun stripe moved on the floor as time passed. Patches felt calm and happy. He had used his routine. He had built his skills. He had made careful choices.

Bluebird flapped her wings. “Patches, your routine works like magic!”

Panda Patches shook his head and grinned. “Not magic. Practice. Plan. Patience.”

The bunny hugged his wagon. “Thank you, Patches! I will make a wagon routine too. First I look, then I plan…”

Patches nodded. “A routine helps with big jobs and small jobs. It helps our hands and our hearts. It helps us decide what to do next.”

Bluebird sang the song with him as they swept the floor together.

“First I look. Then I plan.
Then I gather what I can.
I try, try slow, and check each part.
I finish proud with a happy heart.”

They turned off the lamp. Click. They closed the door. Creak. The garage was neat and quiet.

Panda Patches felt proud. He was ready for tomorrow’s Fix-It Time.

The End.
