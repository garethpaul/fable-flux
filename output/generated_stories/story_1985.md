# Penny Penguin Builds a Band Stand

Penny was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a happy wiggle. One breezy afternoon, she waddled into the musical practice room at the community center. The room smelled like wood and lemon polish. Sunlight made tiny sparkles on the shiny drums. Guitars rested on a stand, and a big piano sat quietly, like it was holding a secret song.

“Hello, room!” Penny chirped. “I’m ready to practice!”

She flapped her little wings with joy. But when Penny looked around, her smile got small. The music sheets were messy and crinkled. The stools were wobbly. A pile of wooden planks and screws lay in a box.

“Oh no,” Penny said softly. “The room needs help.”

Penny waddled closer. She touched a wobbly stool. It rocked and squeaked. Squeak, squeak! She peeked into the box. A note lay on top. It read, “Please help build a new band stand for the drums. Thank you, Volunteers!”

Penny’s eyes sparkled. “I can help! I love building things.”

Just then, the door swung open with a swish. Tiko the tiger cub and Bella the bunny hopped in. Tiko carried drumsticks. Tap-tap. Bella carried a violin in a soft case. Zip.

“Hi, Penny!” Tiko roared softly. “I’m here to practice drums!”

“I’m here to practice violin,” Bella said in a gentle voice.

Penny waved. “Hi! I want to practice too. But the drums need a new stand. Look at this box. We can build it!”

Tiko’s whiskers twitched. “But I want to drum now,” he said, tapping the sticks on his paw. Tap-tap-tap.

Bella’s ears drooped. “I practiced all week. I want to play my new song.”

Penny took a deep breath. She felt a little twist in her tummy. She wanted to practice too. But she also wanted to help. Penny remembered the note: “Thank you, Volunteers!”

Penny said, “We can do both. We can build first. Then we can play. If we build together, it will be fast!”

Tiko crossed his arms. “I don’t like waiting.”

Bella looked at the floor. “I get nervous when I wait.”

Penny waddled between them. “Let’s listen to each other. We all want to make music. The drums need a strong stand so they don’t fall. If we build, the music will sound better and be safe.”

Tiko looked at the drums. They wobbled on the old stand. Wobble-wobble. He frowned. “I don’t want the drums to fall.”

Bella rubbed the violin case. “I don’t want anyone to get hurt.”

Penny smiled. “We can build with respect. We can take turns. We can talk in kind voices. We can make a plan.”

“What’s the plan?” Tiko asked, tapping slower. Tap… tap.

Penny pointed to the box. “Step one: Count the planks. Step two: Find the screws. Step three: Read the picture directions. Step four: Hold, drill, and tighten. Step five: Try it out!”

Bella’s ears perked up. “I like steps.”

Tiko took a deep breath. “Okay. I will help. But can I tap a quiet rhythm while we work?”

Penny nodded. “Yes. Quiet taps are okay.”

They opened the box. The wood smelled sweet. Bella counted the planks. “One, two, three, four.” Tiko found the screws. Click, click, click. Penny spread out the paper directions with big friendly pictures.

“First, we build the base,” Penny said.

Tiko held the plank steady with his big paws. “I will hold.”

Penny lined up the screws. “I will start them.”

Bella turned the little screwdriver. Twist, twist. “I will tighten.”

They worked like a small team. Tap-tap-tap went Tiko, very softly. Twist-twist went Bella. Penny checked the picture. “Good job! We are building!”

The base got strong. Then the legs went on. Penny used a soft cloth to wipe away dust. The sunlight made the wood glow. The room felt warm and busy, like a humming bee.

Suddenly Tiko tugged too hard. The leg wiggled. The screw slipped. Skitter! Bella gasped. “Oh!”

Tiko pulled back. “I’m sorry. I was too fast.”

Penny placed a gentle flipper on his paw. “It’s okay. We can fix it. Let’s slow down. We can count together.”

They counted. “One, two, three.” Tiko held steady again. Bella turned the screwdriver carefully. Twist, twist, twist. Penny checked the line. “Yes! It’s straight.”

“Last step,” Penny said. “Top plate for the drums.”

Together, they lifted the flat top. Up, up. Set. Penny gave two final twists. Click. Click. She tapped the stand with her flipper. Thump. It felt solid.

Tiko’s eyes shone. “Can I try the drums now?”

Penny nodded. “Yes, but first, let’s test the stand.”

Tiko sat behind the drums. He tapped a gentle beat. Dum, dum, dum. The stand did not wobble. It stood tall and proud.

Bella smiled. “Now my turn?” She tucked her violin under her chin. She played a soft song, light as feathers. La-la-la.

Penny shook little bells from a drawer. Jingle, jingle. The room filled with music. It sounded happy and bright.

When they finished, they all clapped. Clap, clap, clap!

Tiko said, “Thank you for the plan, Penny.”

Bella said, “Thank you for listening, Tiko.”

Penny said, “Thank you for helping, Bella.”

They cleaned up the wood bits and put the tools away. The practice room looked tidy. It smelled like fresh wood and lemon again.

Penny smiled big. “We built something useful. We solved a problem. We were kind.”

Tiko nodded. “Building together felt good.”

Bella added, “I liked taking turns.”

Penny looked at her friends. “Today we learned: When we build with respect, talk kindly, and help each other, we make strong things and happy music.”

They all wiggled, tapped, and hummed. The new stand shone in the sun, ready for many songs.

The End.
