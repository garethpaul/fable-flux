# Fawn's Friendly Festival Shelf

In a comfortable nursery, soft sunlight made golden shapes on the rug. The room smelled like clean cotton and warm wood. A gentle deer fawn named Fawn slept in a cozy basket bed. Fawn’s fur was the color of warm toast, and little white spots dotted her back like tiny snowflakes.

“Good morning, room,” Fawn whispered as she woke. She stretched her long legs and booped her nose on a mobile that chimed tinkly notes. The air felt calm, and Fawn’s ears twitched happily.

Today was a special day. The nursery had a big shelf by the window. On the shelf were treasures from friends: a bright red fan from Mei, a tiny drum from Samir, a woven bracelet from Ayo, a small rainbow flag pin from Tita, and a soft scarf from Lila. Each treasure told a story of family and home.

Fawn loved the shelf. She called it the Friendly Festival Shelf. Every day, she looked and listened. “Good morning, treasures,” Fawn said. “I will take good care of you.”

But this morning, the shelf was messy. The red fan lay on the floor. The tiny drum hid under a pillow. The scarf was tangly. The bracelet was tucked inside a shoe!

“Oh dear,” said Fawn softly. “The treasures need help. They need to be safe and easy to see.” Her heart beat a little faster. She felt small in the big room. But she took a brave breath. “I can do it,” she told herself. “I can be gentle and brave.”

Fawn began with her nose and hooves. Tap, tap. She picked up the red fan. It smelled like peach tea. When Fawn opened it, the air said whoosh. “I will put you back where the sun kisses you,” Fawn said. She found a spot on the shelf where the light made a warm square. She set the fan there and smiled.

Next, she looked for the tiny drum. “Drum, where are you?” she called. The pillow answered with a little thump. Fawn nudged the pillow, and the drum rolled out, dum-dum-dum. “There you are!” Fawn laughed. “Samir showed me how to tap you softly.” She tapped a slow beat. Dum... dum... dum. The sound was kind and steady, like a heartbeat. Fawn placed the drum near the fan, side by side, like good friends.

She found the woven bracelet in the shoe. It smelled like grass and sunshine. “Ayo’s grandma made you,” Fawn said. “You are strong and bright.” She slipped the bracelet over a small peg so it could hang and dance in the light.

The scarf was last. It felt soft as clouds. Lila had said, “In my family, we wear scarves for special days.” Fawn hugged it gently and folded it slow, smooth-smooth, corner-corner. She laid it on the shelf like a soft rainbow bed.

As Fawn worked, she made little labels from sticky notes. She could not write words, but she drew simple pictures: a tiny fan shape, a little drum, a circle bracelet, a wavy scarf. “This is our plan,” Fawn said. “We keep things in their places.” Her voice sounded proud, and pride felt warm in her chest.

Just then, Fawn heard a rustle. The door softly creaked. Mei, Samir, Ayo, and Lila peeked into the nursery with curious eyes. They had come to play.

“Hello, Fawn!” Mei said. “What are you doing?”

Fawn’s ears tipped shyly. “I made a tidy shelf,” she said. “I want to see everyone’s special things. I want to remember your homes.”

Mei’s eyes sparkled. “That red fan is from my grandma,” she said. She showed Fawn how to open and close it slowly. Swish—pause—swish. “We use it at parties to dance.”

Samir smiled and tapped the drum. Dum-da-da, dum-da-da. “We play this at our family gatherings,” he said. “We sing and clap. It makes us feel together.”

Ayo lifted the bracelet. “My grandma weaves these,” he said proudly. “Each color is a story. Green is for fields. Blue is for sky.”

Lila picked up the scarf and rubbed it on her cheek. “We wear this at special times,” she said. “It helps us remember kindness.”

Fawn listened with her whole body. Her ears felt warm. Her nose smelled peach tea and cotton and wood. Her heart felt big. “Your treasures are special to you,” she said. “Thank you for telling me.”

Mei looked at the shelf and the little picture labels. “It’s so neat,” she said. “We can find everything.”

“We can share and learn,” Samir added.

“And we can put things back,” Ayo said. “So we can see them tomorrow, too.”

Lila tied the scarf into a gentle bow and placed it on its spot. “We can keep each other’s stories safe.”

Fawn nodded. “That is our shelf rule,” she said. “We keep the treasures in their places. We listen. We learn. We are gentle and brave.”

They all clapped softly so the drum could whisper dum. The room felt bright and full. Fawn’s spots tingled with happy pride.

At the end of the day, golden light turned sleepy and soft. Fawn looked at the tidy shelf. The fan, the drum, the bracelet, and the scarf all rested in their spots. The little picture labels smiled back.

“Good night, treasures,” Fawn whispered. “Thank you for teaching me about your families. I will keep you safe. I will keep you neat.”

Her friends waved. “Good night, Fawn,” they said. “Thank you for caring.”

Fawn curled in her basket bed. The mobile chimed. The room smelled like warm wood and sweet dreams. Fawn felt brave, and Fawn felt kind.

The Friendly Festival Shelf shone in the moonlight, and everyone’s stories felt close.

The End.
