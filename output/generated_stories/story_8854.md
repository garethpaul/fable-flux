# Bouncy's Big Room Adventure

Bouncy the kangaroo joey woke up in a new place. He rubbed his sleepy eyes and looked around. "Where am I?" he wondered out loud.

He was in Grandma Roo's guest room! The walls were painted soft yellow like sunshine. There was a big, fluffy bed with purple pillows. A wooden rocking chair sat by the window, and colorful books filled a small bookshelf.

"Good morning, little one!" called Grandma Roo from the kitchen. "Come hop down for breakfast!"

But Bouncy felt shy. This room was different from his own cozy pouch. Everything looked so big and new. He pulled the blanket over his head.

"I don't know how to get down by myself," Bouncy whispered.

Then his tummy made a loud rumbling sound. "Grrrrowl!" It sounded like a friendly monster! Bouncy giggled. He was getting very hungry, and he could smell something delicious - sweet pancakes and fresh berries!

Bouncy peeked out from under the blanket. The morning sun was streaming through the window, making everything sparkle. He noticed how the wooden floor looked smooth and perfect for hopping.

"Maybe I can try," he said to himself. "Mama always says I'm a good hopper!"

Bouncy sat up tall and wiggled his strong legs. He remembered how good it felt to bounce and jump. His legs were made for moving! He took a deep breath that smelled like flowers from the garden outside.

First, he tried a tiny hop on the soft bed. "Boing!" The mattress was springy and fun. Then he hopped a little higher. "Boing, boing!" Each bounce made him feel braver and stronger.

"I can do this!" Bouncy said with a big smile.

He carefully hopped from the bed to the rocking chair. The chair rocked back and forth - "Creak, creak!" - like a gentle swing. From there, he made a perfect hop to the bookshelf, then down to the smooth wooden floor.

"Look at me go!" Bouncy cheered as he bounced across the room. His feet made happy tapping sounds on the floor. "Tap, tap, hop! Tap, tap, hop!"

He discovered that moving around the room was actually really fun! He hopped in circles, making his own little dance. He bounced high and low, feeling his muscles get stronger with each jump.

When Bouncy finally hopped into the kitchen, Grandma Roo clapped her hands with joy.

"What a brave and independent joey you are!" she said, giving him a warm hug. "You figured out how to explore all by yourself!"

Bouncy's chest puffed up with pride. "I was curious about everything in the guest room, and my legs helped me discover it all!"

"Your body is amazing," Grandma Roo said as she served him fluffy pancakes. "Moving and hopping makes you strong and happy!"

Bouncy took a big bite of pancakes. They tasted like vanilla and sunshine. "Thank you for letting me stay in the special guest room, Grandma. I learned that I can be brave and try new things!"

"And I learned that moving my body feels wonderful," he added, doing a little happy hop in his chair.

From that day on, Bouncy loved exploring new places. He knew that his strong legs and curious heart could take him anywhere. Every hop was an adventure waiting to happen!

The End.
