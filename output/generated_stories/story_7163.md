# Dippy the Dolphin Says “I’m Sorry”

Dippy the Dolphin was playful and bright. He had smooth, shiny skin and a happy flip-flap tail. Today, Dippy visited the creative art room. The room smelled like crayons and glue. Soft music hummed like waves. Sunlight peeked in and made the glitter sparkle.

“Wow!” Dippy squeaked. “Look at all the colors!” He puffed little bubbles when he was excited. Pop, pop, pop!

On the big table were blocks, paint, paper, and shiny buttons. Friends were busy making a tall, tall block tower. It was blue, red, and yellow, like a rainbow building. Lina the ladybug lined up tiny stickers. Max the mouse placed a roof block very gently. Tiko the turtle held a glue stick and smiled slowly.

“Can I help?” Dippy asked, flapping his fins.

“Yes,” said Max. “We are building a happy house.”

“Please be careful,” said Tiko. “The blocks can fall.”

Dippy nodded and swam in the air, pretending the floor was the sea. He wiggled closer, smelling the fresh paint and hearing the soft click of blocks. He felt big feelings inside—excited and bouncy.

“I want to put the top star!” Dippy cheered.

He reached his fin. His tail swished—flip, FLOP!

Oh no!

Dippy’s fin bumped the table. The tall tower wobbled. Wibble-wobble… CRASH! Blocks tumbled. Stickers fluttered like tiny leaves. The roof skidded across the table. The happy house fell down.

Silence.

Dippy’s tummy felt twisty. His cheeks felt hot. He wanted to hide under a blue piece of paper. He blinked at the mess and his eyes felt watery, like little waves.

Lina looked at the scattered stickers. Max hugged the roof block. Tiko took a slow breath.

Dippy whispered, “I didn’t mean to.” He looked at his friends. “I made a big mess.”

Lina said softly, “I worked hard on the stickers.”

Max squeaked, “And I balanced the roof.”

Tiko nodded. “We all feel sad.”

Dippy’s heart thumped. He remembered a gentle voice from home: “When you make a mistake, stop, breathe, and say sorry. Then try to fix it.”

Dippy took a slow breath in. He blew it out like a tiny bubble. Pop.

He looked at Lina, Max, and Tiko. “I am sorry,” he said clearly. “I bumped the table. I knocked it down. I didn’t listen to ‘be careful.’ I will help fix it.”

The room felt quiet. Then Lina’s wings relaxed. “Thank you for saying sorry, Dippy.”

Max nodded. “Can you help me find the roof?”

“Yes,” Dippy said. His tummy untwisted a little. “I can help.”

Together, they picked up blocks. Clack, clack. They peeled stickers gently. Peel, press. Dippy sorted colors: “Red goes here. Blue goes there.” He moved slowly this time, holding his tail still. Flip… stop.

“I like how you are being careful,” said Tiko. “That helps our building.”

Lina smiled. “You can place the star when we are ready.”

Dippy’s eyes shone. He felt a warm glow in his chest. “I can do it,” he told himself. “I can be careful. I can help. I can try again.”

They started building. The blocks clicked and stacked. Click. Click. The tower grew. Taller. Stronger.

Dippy asked, “Is this a good spot?” He waited.

Max said, “Yes, right there.”

Dippy placed a block slowly. He watched his fin. He held his breath. The block stayed. He grinned. “I did it!”

Lina added stickers in a neat row. “Sparkle, sparkle,” she sang. The glitter twinkled like tiny stars.

Tiko used glue for the door. “Smooth and steady,” he said.

Dippy whispered, “Smooth and steady,” and pressed a window block just right.

Soon, the happy house was back! It was even better than before. It had a sticker sun, a tiny button doorknob, and a blue roof that shone in the sunlight.

Lina clapped. “We built it together.”

Max squeaked, “Teamwork!”

Tiko smiled. “And careful fins.”

Dippy looked at his friends. “Thank you for helping me fix my mistake,” he said. “I felt bad before. Now I feel proud. I can say sorry. I can make it right.”

Lina said, “Saying sorry helped my heart.”

Max added, “Helping fix the building helped my feelings.”

Tiko nodded. “We all make mistakes. We can breathe. We can say sorry. We can try again.”

Dippy held the shiny star. “May I put the star now?” he asked.

“Yes,” said everyone.

Dippy moved slowly. He felt the smooth star in his fin. He heard the quiet music. He smelled the friendly, gluey smell. He saw his friends’ kind faces. He placed the star on top. It sat still and bright.

The room filled with happy sounds. Clap, clap. Squeak, squeak. Tap, tap. Dippy’s tail gave a tiny, quiet wiggle—flip… shhh.

Dippy smiled. “I learned something,” he said. “When I make a mistake, I can say, ‘I’m sorry,’ and then help to fix it. I can be careful. I can do hard things. I am a good friend.”

Everyone nodded. The happy house gleamed. The glitter sparkled like sea foam in sunshine.

“Play time!” said Lina.

“Build time!” said Max.

“Kind time,” said Tiko.

Dippy laughed. “All together time!”

They played and built and felt proud of themselves. And every time Dippy felt bouncy, he took a breath, held his tail still, and remembered: say sorry, fix it, and try again.

The End.
