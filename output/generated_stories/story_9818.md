# Ellie's Rainbow Breakfast

Little Ellie the elephant calf woke up to the warm sunshine streaming through the kitchen window. Her tummy rumbled loudly. "Grumble, grumble!" it said.

"Good morning, Mama!" Ellie called, swinging her little trunk happily.

Mama Elephant was busy at the stove, stirring something that smelled wonderful. "Good morning, my sweet Ellie! Are you hungry?"

"Yes! My tummy is making funny noises!" Ellie giggled, patting her round belly with her trunk.

Mama smiled. "Well, let's make you a special breakfast. But first, can you help me pick the best foods for your growing body?"

Ellie's eyes sparkled with excitement. "Yes! I want to help!"

Mama opened the big refrigerator. Inside, Ellie could see so many colorful foods. There were bright red strawberries that looked like little hearts, orange carrots that crunched when you bit them, yellow bananas that curved like smiles, green spinach leaves that felt soft and cool, and purple grapes that looked like tiny balloons.

"Wow!" Ellie gasped. "It's like a rainbow in there!"

"That's right!" Mama said. "Each color gives your body different vitamins and minerals to help you grow big and strong. Red foods like strawberries help your heart. Orange foods like carrots help your eyes see better. Can you pick one food from each color?"

Ellie carefully used her trunk to select foods. She chose strawberries because they smelled so sweet, carrots because they were crunchy, a banana because it made her smile, spinach because Mama said it would make her strong like a superhero, and grapes because they were fun to pop in her mouth.

"Perfect choices!" Mama praised. "Now, what should we make?"

Ellie thought hard, tapping her trunk against her forehead. "Can we make a rainbow smoothie bowl?"

"What a wonderful idea! You're so creative, Ellie."

Together, they washed the fruits and vegetables. The water felt cool and refreshing. Ellie loved the sound of the strawberries splashing and the way the grapes glistened like jewels.

Mama helped Ellie put the banana, strawberries, and spinach into the blender. "The spinach will make it green, but you won't even taste it!" Mama explained.

"Really?" Ellie asked, amazed.

"Really! Sometimes healthy foods can hide in yummy foods."

They blended everything together. The kitchen filled with a sweet, fruity smell that made Ellie's mouth water. The smoothie turned a beautiful purple-green color.

Mama poured the smoothie into Ellie's favorite bowl – the one with little flowers painted on it. Then Ellie carefully arranged the remaining fruits on top in rainbow stripes: red strawberries, orange carrots, and purple grapes.

"Look, Mama! It really is a rainbow!" Ellie clapped her trunk excitedly.

Ellie took her first big spoonful. The smoothie was cold and creamy and tasted like sunshine. The strawberries were sweet, the carrots added a fun crunch, and the grapes burst with flavor.

"Mmm! This is the best breakfast ever!" Ellie said with her mouth full.

"And it's making you healthy and strong," Mama said, giving Ellie a gentle hug. "You did such a good job helping and choosing nutritious foods."

Ellie felt proud and happy. Her tummy felt satisfied and her body felt energized. "Can we make rainbow food every day?"

"Of course! When we eat lots of different colored foods, we give our bodies everything they need to grow and play and learn."

Ellie finished every last drop of her rainbow breakfast, already excited to help make tomorrow's colorful meal.

The End.
