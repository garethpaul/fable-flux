# Kora Koala Helps in the Warm Kitchen

Kora was a calm koala with soft gray fur and bright, gentle eyes. She lived near a cozy house where the kitchen was always warm. The kitchen smelled like cinnamon toast and sweet apples. The sun made orange squares on the floor.

One morning, Kora peeked in through the doorway. She heard soft clinks and clatters. She saw a big mixing bowl on the counter. She saw a tall stack of clean cups. She also saw a sign on the fridge. It read, “Soup for Friends: Today!”

Kora blinked slowly. “What is Soup for Friends?” she asked in a quiet voice.

Mrs. Maple, the neighbor with kind hands and a flour-dusted apron, smiled. “Hello, Kora. We are making soup and bread for our neighbors. Some people are busy or tired today. We will share our food. Would you like to help?”

Kora’s ears twitched. She felt a little wiggly inside. “I am calm. I move slowly,” she said. “Can I still help?”

Mrs. Maple nodded. “Yes. Volunteering means we give our time and our care. We each help in our own way.”

Kora took a deep breath. The kitchen felt warm on her fur. The soup pot bubbled softly. “Blub, blub,” it said. The smell was rich and cozy, like hugs.

“Okay,” said Kora. “I want to help.”

Mrs. Maple clapped once. “Wonderful! Let’s start with washing our paws.” The water ran with a whoosh. It felt warm and slippery on Kora’s hands. Splash, swish, clean!

Kora dried her paws on a soft towel. “What can I do now?” she asked.

Mrs. Maple showed her a basket of carrots. “We need tiny carrot coins. I can cut, and you can place them in the bowl. That is safe.”

Kora nodded. “I can do that.” She watched Mrs. Maple cut, snip, snip. Kora picked up each little orange coin and counted. “One, two, three.” The carrots felt cool and smooth. Plop! They went into the bowl.

Soon, Kora learned a rhythm. Snip, plop. Snip, plop. She felt proud. “I am getting good at this,” she said.

“You are building a skill,” said Mrs. Maple. “You are careful and calm. That helps the whole team.”

Kora’s cheeks warmed with happy blush. “What next?”

“Would you like to stir?” Mrs. Maple handed Kora a wooden spoon. The spoon was big and round. Kora held it with both hands and stirred slowly. The soup swirled. It smelled like carrots, onions, and love. “Mmm,” Kora hummed.

From the doorway came a small voice. “Can I help too?” It was Theo, a tiny turtle with a shiny shell.

“Of course,” said Kora. “We are volunteers! We work together.”

Mrs. Maple smiled. “Theo, you can stack the cups by size. Big to small.”

Theo’s shell made a gentle tap on the tiles as he moved. Tap, tap. He stacked. Kora stirred. Mrs. Maple baked.

Suddenly, the bread timer beeped. Beep! Beep! Mrs. Maple took out warm rolls. The air smelled like butter clouds. Kora’s tummy rumbled. “I am hungry,” she giggled.

“We will taste later,” said Mrs. Maple. “First we share.”

Kora felt a tiny worry. “What if I spill? What if I am too slow?”

Mrs. Maple touched Kora’s paw. “We take our time. Slow and steady is good. Helping is not a race.”

Kora took another deep breath. She felt her calm come back, like a soft blanket. “I can do this.”

They packed soup into jars. Kora held each jar steady. Theo put lids on with a careful twist. Twirl. Click. Kora tied ribbons. The ribbons felt silky. “These look pretty,” she said.

Soon, neighbors came by the warm kitchen door. Mr. Finch, the mail carrier, smiled. “Thank you. I had a long day.” Ms. Rae, the teacher, sighed with relief. “This will help my family.”

Kora’s heart felt big and soft. She looked at Mrs. Maple. “Our volunteering helps people feel cared for.”

“Yes,” said Mrs. Maple. “And you learned new kitchen skills. Look how well you stir and count and tie.”

Theo bobbed his head. “And I can stack and twist!”

Kora handed a jar to a little boy with sleepy eyes. He held it close and whispered, “Thank you.”

“You’re welcome,” Kora said. Her voice was calm and kind. She felt strong inside, like a steady tree.

When the last jar was given, they all sat down. Mrs. Maple passed a warm roll to Kora. The crust crackled softly. Kora tasted a tiny bite. The bread was fluffy and sweet. The soup was cozy and bright.

Kora smiled. “Helping feels good,” she said. “Volunteering is sharing time, care, and skills.”

Mrs. Maple nodded. “And when we help, we learn. We grow. We make our kitchen, and our world, warm.”

Kora wrapped her paws around her bowl. The steam tickled her nose. She breathed in calm. She breathed out joy.

“I will help again,” she said.

The End.
