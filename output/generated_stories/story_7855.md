# Koko Koala’s Gentle Lab Day

In a quiet house on a sunny street, there was a cool, cozy basement lab. It smelled like fresh paper and clean soap. Little lights blinked softly. Shelves held jars of marbles, feathers, buttons, and bright leaves. There were soft mats on the floor and a big table like a friendly island.

Koko the calm koala climbed down the stairs slowly. Step, pause. Step, pause. He took a deep breath. “Mmm,” Koko said. “Smells safe and clean.” His round ears twitched. His nose wiggled. He felt curious and calm, like a small bubble floating.

Pinned to the wall was a big poster with three kind rules:
- Be safe
- Be gentle
- Be curious

Koko patted the poster with his paw. “I can do that,” he said.

His friends were already there. Miko the mouse zipped around in tiny sneakers. Zuzu the zebra stood by the table, her stripes like piano keys. Lali the ladybug sat on a leaf pad, dots shiny like buttons. They all wore little safety goggles that made soft tap-tap sounds when they smiled.

“Hi, friends,” Koko said in his calm voice. “Today we discover.”

“What do we discover?” Miko squeaked, bouncing.

“We discover bubbles,” Koko said. “And kindness. And how to be safe.”

He pointed to the safe things: goggles for eyes, gloves for paws and hooves, and aprons with big pockets. “We protect our bodies,” Koko added. “We protect our friends.”

They put on their gear with gentle hands. Zuzu helped tie Koko’s apron. Koko tightened Zuzu’s goggles so they wouldn’t slip. Lali tugged Miko’s glove snug. Everyone felt seen and cared for.

Koko showed a tray with bowls. One bowl had warm, soapy water. Another had clear water. A little dish held salt. There was a tiny dropper of blue food coloring. There were wide spoons and a soft whisk.

Koko tapped the table. “We look with eyes. We listen with ears. We smell with noses. We take turns. We say please and thank you.”

“Please can I stir?” asked Zuzu.

“Thank you for asking,” Koko said. “Yes. Slow and gentle.”

Zuzu stirred the soapy water. Swish, swish. Bubbles shimmered like tiny moons. Miko leaned close. “They look like rainbows!” he said.

“Let’s listen,” Lali whispered.

They listened. The bubbles made very soft pop-pop sounds, like kisses from the air.

Miko reached too fast toward the dropper. Koko raised a calm paw. “Pause,” he said kindly. “We use one drop. We count slowly. We keep the dropper away from eyes.”

Miko took a deep breath. “One,” he counted. He squeezed just a tiny bit. Plink! A blue drop bloomed, spreading in the bowl like a flower waking up.

“Ooooh,” said Zuzu. “It’s like a sky swirl!”

Koko nodded. “Discovery is patient,” he said. “Discovery is safe.”

They took turns. Lali sprinkled a pinch of salt into the clear water. The crystals fell like snow. “It shines!” she said.

“Let’s feel the whisk,” Koko offered. “We whisk slow. We don’t splash.”

Miko whisked gently. Swirl, swirl. The water made little waves. “I hear a shhh sound,” Miko said, ears perked.

“It’s the water whispering,” Lali giggled.

A small jar of feathers waited on the shelf. Koko reached for it, then stopped. He read a label: “Borrow only. Ask first.”

“We ask before we use,” Koko said. “These feathers belong to Ms. Owl.”

Zuzu lifted her hoof. “May we borrow the feathers, please?” she asked the quiet room.

Just then, Ms. Owl’s soft voice floated from the stairs. “Yes, you may,” she hooted kindly. “Thank you for asking.”

“Thank you, Ms. Owl,” the friends said together.

Koko opened the jar and passed it around. The feathers were fluffy and smelled like sunshine and air. Each friend chose one. A long stripey feather. A tiny red-tipped feather. A bright blue feather like the sky drop.

Koko set up two little fans. “We test how feathers fly,” he said. “We keep paws away from the fans. We stand behind the tape line.”

They dropped feathers in front of the fans. Whoosh! The feathers danced and spun. Zuzu’s feather twirled like a ballerina. Miko’s feather floated and wiggled. Lali’s feather did a tiny hop and then glided like a sleepy leaf.

“How do they feel?” Koko asked.

“Soft and tickly,” Lali said, touching her cheek.

“How do they sound?” Zuzu listened. “Whish-whish,” she said, “like a hush song.”

“How do they move?” Miko watched closely. “They float,” he said. “Not fast. Not hard. Just light.”

Koko smiled. “We discovered,” he said. “We used eyes, ears, nose, and gentle touch. We asked before we used. We stayed safe.”

They cleaned up together. Miko held the bowls. Zuzu wiped the table. Lali counted spoons. Koko put feathers back and left a little thank-you note for Ms. Owl with a tiny heart.

Ms. Owl peered in and nodded. “I see safety,” she said. “I hear kindness. I feel respect.”

Koko’s heart felt warm. His calm spread like tea in a cup. He looked at his friends. They were all different—small and big, stripey and spotty, furry and smooth. But their smiles matched.

“We are a good team,” Koko said softly. “We are safe. We are gentle. We are curious.”

“What did we learn today?” asked Ms. Owl.

Koko answered. “Discovery is fun when we do it safely. Discovery is kind when we share and ask. Discovery is bright when everyone belongs.”

They all nodded. The lights blinked cozy. The lab smelled like soap and a little like feathers. Their hearts felt like bubbles—soft, light, and ready for more.

“Tomorrow,” Koko said, “we will discover sound.”

“Yay!” said Miko, Zuzu, and Lali.

Koko pointed to the poster once more. Be safe. Be gentle. Be curious. He patted it, and the friends patted it too.

Then they turned off the fans, washed their paws, and climbed the stairs together, step by step.

The End.
