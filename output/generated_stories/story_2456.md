# Rocco Raccoon and the Quiet Button

Rocco was a little raccoon with a big, musical heart. He loved to tap rhythms on the window, hum silly songs, and shake his tiny paw tambourine. Today, Rocco sat buckled in the back seat of the family car. The seat was soft. The sun made warm patterns on the seat belts. The car smelled like orange snacks. The tires hummed a low song on the road: vrrrrr, vrrrrr.

Mama drove. Buckle clicked. Signal blinked. Blip, blip, blip.

“Let’s sing a car song!” Rocco chirped, his masky eyes bright. He shook his little tambourine. Tinka-tinka-ting!

Mama smiled. “I love your music, Rocco. But I have to listen to the road. My ears need to be calm. Can you help me with a quiet car?”

Rocco’s paws wiggled. The music inside him was bouncy like popcorn. “I can try,” he said. He took a deep breath. In… and out. The air smelled sweet and citrusy.

He put the tambourine down. The car sounded softer. Vrrrrr. Blip, blip.

Rocco looked out the window. Trees waved. Clouds looked like fluffy drums. His toes tapped. Tap, tap. He wanted to play. He wanted to sing.

He whispered, “Just a tiny song?” His toes tapped louder. Tap, tap, tap. His paws started to clap. Clap-clap!

Mama peeked in the mirror. “Rocco, I need your brave quiet. Can you use your calm tools?”

Rocco blinked. Brave quiet? He wanted to be brave! He wanted to help. But the music inside felt like a busy bee. Buzz-buzz-boom!

He felt squirmy. His cheeks felt warm. “Mama,” he said in a small voice, “my music is big. I want to be quiet, but it’s hard. I almost clapped. I almost forgot.” His tail drooped.

“Thank you for telling me,” Mama said. “That was honest. That was brave.” Her voice was warm like a blanket. “Let’s try your quiet tools together.”

“What tools?” Rocco asked.

“Your Quiet Button,” Mama said, tapping her own chest. “Press it and breathe. In for four. Out for four. And your Counting Beat. Tap one finger at a time on your leg. Soft. Quiet. Try it with me.”

Rocco pressed his chest. Beep! He pretended he had a button. He breathed in: one, two, three, four. He breathed out: one, two, three, four. The air felt cool in his nose. His shoulders dropped down.

He tapped one finger at a time on his jeans. Tap… tap… tap… tap. Soft like rain. The road hummed with him. Vrrrrr. The blinker blinked. Blip, blip. His body felt slower.

“You’re doing it,” Mama whispered. “That is self-regulation. You are the boss of your body and your music.”

Rocco smiled a little. “I’m the boss?”

“You are,” Mama said. “And bosses choose.”

Rocco chose. He chose quiet fingers. He chose slow breaths. He chose to listen. He could still hear his music inside, but now it was gentle. A sleepy lullaby.

A big truck drove by. Whoosh! The sound made Rocco jump. His paws grabbed the tambourine. Tinka— He stopped. He felt the grab. He felt the jump.

“Quiet Button,” he told himself. Beep! In, two, three, four. Out, two, three, four. Tap, tap, tap, tap.

He set the tambourine down. “I almost played,” he said. “But I paused.”

“That’s knowledge,” Mama said. “You noticed your body. You knew what to do. That’s very smart.”

Rocco’s tail curled in a happy swoop. He looked at the window again. The sky looked like a soft drum skin. He sang a song in his head, so quiet only he could hear.

Minutes passed. The car smelled like crackers now. The sun felt warm on his fur. He felt proud. Brave. Calm.

Soon, the car slowed. Mama turned on the blinker. Blip, blip. “We’re stopping for gas,” she said. “When the car is parked and safe, you can play a song.”

Rocco’s ears perked. “Really?”

“Really,” Mama said.

The car stopped with a soft shhh. The engine went quiet. Rocco unbuckled with a click. He held the tambourine. He looked at Mama. “May I play now?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Thank you for helping with a quiet car. That was honest and brave.”

Rocco smiled so big his whiskers wiggled. He shook his tambourine. Tinka-tinka-ting! He kept a steady, soft beat. He sang a little song:

“I can press my Quiet Button,
I can breathe and wait.
I can tap my Counting Beat,
I can make a great choice.”

Mama clapped. “Beautiful!”

Rocco giggled. “It was hard,” he said. “I wanted to play so much. But I waited.”

“That’s self-regulation,” Mama said. “You noticed. You paused. You chose. You were honest about your feelings and brave to try.”

Rocco felt tall, even in his little seat. He hugged his tambourine. He learned a big lesson in a small car: the music can wait, and he can be the boss.

When they drove again, Rocco pressed his Quiet Button. Beep! He breathed with the road. Vrrrrr. Blip, blip. Tap, tap, tap, tap.

He smiled out the window. The music inside was his friend. He knew what to do.

The End.
