# Gigi Giraffe's Tall Problem

Gigi Giraffe loved spending time in her sunny backyard. The warm sunshine felt wonderful on her golden spots. Her long neck stretched high above the fence, and she could see everything around her neighborhood.

Today was extra special. Gigi's friend Bunny was coming over for a tea party! Gigi had set up a pretty little table with tiny cups and pretend cookies. The table sat perfectly under the apple tree, where cool shade mixed with warm sunbeams.

*Knock, knock!* 

"Come in, Bunny!" called Gigi excitedly.

Little Bunny hopped through the gate. Her fluffy white tail wiggled with joy. "Hi, Gigi! I'm so excited for our tea party!"

But when they reached the table, Bunny looked up and up and up. Gigi's head was so high in the sky! The little table seemed very far away from Gigi's mouth.

"Oh dear," said Gigi softly. Her voice sounded worried. "I can't reach the tea cups. My neck is too long and the table is too low."

Bunny's ears drooped. "Does this mean we can't have our tea party?"

Gigi felt sad. Her long neck was usually helpful. She could see over fences and reach tall apples. But right now, it felt like a big problem. She wanted to have fun with her friend, but how could they share tea when she couldn't even reach the table?

"Wait," said Gigi thoughtfully. She closed her eyes and took a deep breath. The sweet smell of flowers helped her think clearly. "Let me use my thinking skills."

Gigi looked around the sunny backyard. She saw garden boxes, flower pots, and a small wooden stool. Her mind started working like a puzzle.

"I have an idea!" Gigi said brightly. "Bunny, can you help me stack these flower pots?"

Together, they carefully built a tower of pots. One pot, then two pots, then three! The tower grew taller and taller. Gigi tested it gently with one hoof. It felt strong and steady.

"Now I can put the table on top!" said Gigi.

They moved the little table onto their flower pot tower. Now the table was much higher! Gigi could bend her long neck down comfortably. The tea cups were just right for both friends.

"You're so smart, Gigi!" said Bunny, clapping her paws. "You solved the problem!"

Gigi felt proud and happy. Her neck wasn't too long after all. She just needed to think creatively! "Sometimes when we have a problem, we need to look around and use what we have," she said wisely.

The friends enjoyed their perfect tea party. They sipped pretend tea and nibbled pretend cookies. The afternoon sun made everything golden and warm. Birds chirped happy songs in the apple tree above them.

"This is the best tea party ever," said Bunny.

"It is," agreed Gigi. "And I learned something important today. When something seems impossible, I can take a deep breath, look around, and find a solution. Problems can become possibilities!"

As the sun began to set, painting the backyard in soft orange light, Gigi felt grateful. She had solved her problem, helped her friend, and learned to believe in herself.

The End.
