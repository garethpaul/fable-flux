# Bounce, Bump, and "I'm Sorry"

Sunny Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey. He lived in a cozy home with a big, playful living room. The rug was soft like clouds. Bright pillows were stacked high. A tiny drum sat by the window, and a basket of blocks waited like colorful candy.

“Boing, boing!” sang Sunny Roo as he bounced on his springy tail. His ears wiggled and his paws clapped. “Today is a good day for a game!”

His friend, Milla the mouse, peeked from behind a pillow. “Let’s build a tall block castle,” she squeaked. “We can make windows and a big door.”

Sunny Roo nodded. “Yes! A castle for kings and queens and squeaky mice.”

They started to build. Sunny Roo stacked red, blue, and yellow blocks. Milla added tiny square windows. The castle grew taller and taller. It made a gentle clack-clack sound each time a block touched another. The room smelled like warm toast from the kitchen. Sunlight made shiny puddles on the floor.

“Ta-da!” said Milla, eyes wide.

“It is the best castle,” said Sunny Roo. His tail twitched. He felt so full of happy energy. He wanted to bounce again.

“Let’s bounce around it,” he said. “Just once!”

Milla’s whiskers wiggled. “Careful, Sunny. The blocks are wobbly.”

“I will be gentle,” Sunny Roo promised. He took a small hop. Then another. His feet went pat, pat. His tail went boing. The soft rug made him feel safe, so he hopped just a little higher.

“Sunny…” Milla said softly.

Boing! Sunny Roo’s tail bumped a pillow. The pillow toppled, and fwump! It bumped the castle. The blocks rattled. Clack-clack-clatter!

Down came the castle. Blocks rolled under the couch. A blue block bumped Milla’s paw.

Milla squeaked and pulled her paw close. Her eyes looked shiny and sad. “My castle,” she whispered. “I worked so hard.”

Sunny Roo’s smile slid away. His ears drooped. His tummy felt twisty, like a knot. The room felt too quiet, even with the sunny light. He wanted to hide behind a pillow.

“I didn’t mean to,” he said in a small voice. He looked at Milla’s paw. “Did it hurt?”

“A little,” Milla said, rubbing it gently. She sniffed. “And I feel sad.”

Sunny Roo took a deep breath. He remembered what Mama Roo had said: “When you make a mistake, stop, look, and use kind words. Then make it right.”

He stopped. He looked at Milla’s face. He saw her watery eyes and droopy whiskers. He listened to her tiny breaths. He felt the soft rug under his toes. He knew what to do.

“Milla,” he said clearly, “I am sorry. I bumped the pillow. I knocked down your castle. I see you feel sad. I care about your paw and your feelings.”

Milla blinked. Her whiskers began to lift a little. “Thank you for saying that,” she said softly.

Sunny Roo nodded. “I want to make it right. Can I help fix the castle? And would you like a cool cloth for your paw?”

Milla thought. Then she smiled a tiny smile. “Yes, please.”

Sunny Roo hopped carefully to the kitchen and brought back a small, cool cloth. He held it out with gentle paws. Milla placed it on her paw and sighed, “Ahh.” Sunny Roo felt the knot in his tummy loosen. The room felt warm again.

“Let’s find the blocks,” said Sunny Roo. “We can make it even better.”

Together, they listened for little rolling sounds. They lifted a pillow and found a yellow block. They peered under the couch and saw a red one. “I’ll reach,” said Sunny Roo. He lay on the rug and stretched his long arms. “Got it!”

As they built, Sunny Roo spoke softly. “This time I will use my walking feet near the castle.”

Milla giggled. “And I will say, ‘Stop, please,’ if I feel worried.”

“Good plan,” said Sunny Roo.

Clack, clack, click. The castle grew again, taller and steadier. Sunny Roo held each block with two paws. Milla pressed them gently, checking the corners. Their faces were calm and bright.

When they were done, the castle had a round window and a tiny flag made from a paper triangle. Sunny Roo’s heart felt light. Milla’s eyes sparkled.

“It is beautiful,” said Sunny Roo.

“And strong,” said Milla. “Thank you for helping. Thank you for your kind words.”

Sunny Roo smiled. “I made a mistake, and I said I’m sorry. I listened to your feelings. I helped fix the problem.”

Milla nodded. “That is what friends do.”

They stood side by side and looked at their work. The living room felt full of soft sounds: the gentle tick of a clock, the whisper of the curtain, and the quiet thump of Sunny Roo’s happy tail on the rug.

“Want to play drum music for the castle?” Sunny Roo asked.

“Yes!” Milla chimed. “Soft drum music.”

Sunny Roo tapped the tiny drum in a slow, gentle rhythm. Dum, dum, dum. Milla swayed her whiskers and hummed. They both laughed, light as bubbles.

Sunny Roo whispered, “Next time I will ask, ‘Is it okay if I bounce?’ And I will wait for your answer.”

Milla grinned. “And I will say how I feel. We can keep each other safe.”

They did a little happy wiggle. The castle stood tall. Their hearts felt even taller.

The End.
