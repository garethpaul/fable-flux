# Leo Learns to Wait

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and a pink, boop-able nose. One morning, he padded into the bathroom on quiet paws. Drip, drip went the faucet. Swish went the shower curtain. The bright towels were stacked like a rainbow.

Leo wiggled his whiskers. “I want to brush my teeth fast!” he said. He bounced on his paws and reached for his lion toothbrush. It had a tiny mane painted on the handle.

Mama Lion peeked in. “Good morning, Leo,” she said softly. “I see you’re ready.”

“I want shiny teeth now!” Leo said. He squeezed the toothpaste tube. Squish! Too much minty paste plopped onto the sink. It smelled cool and tingly, like little snowflakes in his nose.

“Oh dear,” said Mama Lion kindly. “Let’s slow down. Patience helps us do things well.”

Leo took a deep breath. He watched the water drip, drip, drip. His tail swished. Waiting felt hard.

Mama Lion handed him a small cup. “First, let’s get organized,” she said. “Toothpaste in pea-sized dots. One dot for you.” She showed Leo how to squeeze gently. “Squeeze… and stop.”

“Squeeze… and stop,” Leo repeated. He felt proud when a tiny toothpaste dot sat like a little cloud on his brush.

“Now, patience,” Mama Lion said. “Little circles. Top, bottom, front, and back. We count to ten for each part.”

Leo opened his mouth wide. “Ahhh!” The toothbrush tickled his gums. Scritch-scratch, scritch-scratch. The mint tasted cool. He wanted to rush. His paws wiggled.

“Can I go fast?” he asked.

“Let’s try slow and steady,” Mama Lion said. “We’re practicing patience.”

Leo tried again. “One… two… three…” he counted. He brushed tiny circles on each tooth. He listened to the scritch-scratch and felt the bubbles pop, pop, pop. It was like the brush was humming a quiet song.

When Leo reached “ten,” he smiled a foamy smile. “Top done!”

“Great work,” Mama Lion said. “Now the bottom.”

Leo nodded. He brushed the bottom teeth. “One… two… three…” He peeked at the mirror. His eyes were big and brave. His ears twitched at the drip, drip, drip. He wanted to be done, but he kept going. “Eight… nine… ten!”

“Front and back,” Mama Lion reminded him.

Leo made tiny circles on the front teeth. His tongue felt the bubbles. The mint was cold like a snowflake dance. “One… two… three…” He thought of a patient turtle taking slow steps. “I can be like the turtle,” he whispered.

“You are being patient,” Mama Lion said with a warm smile.

At last, “Ten!” Leo rinsed with a swish and a spit. Swish, swish. Spit! The sink sparkled. The air smelled clean and minty.

Leo touched his teeth with his tongue. “Smooth!” he giggled.

“Now we put things in their places,” Mama Lion said. “Organization helps us find things next time.”

Leo looked at the counter. The toothpaste cap lay on its side. The towel peeked over the tub. The cup was near the soap. He made a little game. “Cap on, twist-twist!” He put the toothpaste in a small basket. “Brush goes here,” he said, placing it next to the toothpaste. He folded the towel. “Fold, pat, smooth.” The bathroom looked neat and cozy.

“Ready for your mane?” Mama Lion asked, holding a small brush.

Leo puffed his tiny mane. “I want it big!” He grabbed the brush and tugged. “Ow!” The brush got stuck. His eyes got watery.

Mama Lion took a calm breath. “Let’s try again with patience. Gentle strokes. Start at the ends, then move up.”

Leo nodded. He held still. “One… two… three gentle strokes,” he counted. The brush slid through. Swish, swish. His mane felt fluffy. He smelled Mama’s warm fur and the clean soap.

“You did it!” Mama Lion cheered softly.

Leo stood tall on the bath mat. It felt squishy under his paws, like a mossy rock. He looked at the mirror. A brave cub with bright eyes and a tidy mane smiled back.

“I was patient,” Leo said. “I brushed slow. I counted. I put things away. I didn’t give up.”

Mama Lion nuzzled his cheek. “You improved because you were patient and organized.”

Leo’s tummy rumbled. “Breakfast time?” he asked.

“Almost,” Mama Lion said. “One more check. Did we turn off the water?”

Leo listened. Silence. No drip, drip, drip. He grinned. “All done!”

They padded out of the bathroom together. Leo felt proud inside, like a warm sun glowing in his chest.

He whispered to himself, “Patience helps me do things well. Organization helps me find things. When I practice, I improve.”

Mama Lion heard and smiled. “That’s a big cub lesson.”

Leo wagged his tail. “Tomorrow I will brush again, slow and steady.”

“Slow and steady,” Mama Lion agreed.

The bathroom was neat. The air smelled fresh. Leo’s teeth shined. His mane was soft. And his brave little heart felt calm, because he had learned to wait.

The End.
