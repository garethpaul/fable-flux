# Mango's Fair Art Day

The art room sparkled with rainbow colors. Paintbrushes stood tall in colorful cups. Paper sheets waited on wooden tables like blank clouds ready for pictures.

Mango the monkey swung into the bright room. His brown fur shone in the sunny light. Today was special art day at school!

"Good morning, artists!" said Miss Robin, their kind teacher. "Today we're making friendship paintings. Everyone gets to use the special glitter paints!"

Mango's eyes grew wide and happy. He loved making art with sparkly things. But when he looked at the art table, his smile turned into a frown.

Three little rabbits sat at the big table. They had ALL the glitter paints lined up in front of them. Red glitter, blue glitter, gold glitter - everything!

"Can I please use some glitter paint?" Mango asked politely. His tail swished nervously.

"No way!" said Ruby Rabbit, hugging the paint bottles tight. "We got here first. These are ours now!"

The other rabbits nodded and turned their backs to Mango. They squeezed glitter paint onto their papers, making it drip and waste.

Mango felt his heart sink like a heavy rock. This wasn't fair at all! His paws felt shaky, but he remembered what Mama Monkey always said: "Be brave and do what's right."

Mango took a deep breath. The air smelled like sweet paint and paper. He walked over to Miss Robin, who was organizing colorful crayons.

"Miss Robin," Mango said in his clearest voice. "The rabbits won't share the glitter paints. Everyone is supposed to use them, right?"

Miss Robin looked over at the table. She saw the rabbits hoarding all the special paints while other animals sat with empty hands.

"Oh my!" Miss Robin said gently. "That's not how we share in our art room."

She walked over to the rabbits with Mango. "Ruby, Emma, and Benny," she said kindly but firmly. "These special paints are for everyone to enjoy. How can we make this fair?"

Ruby's ears drooped down. "But we really, really wanted to use them all!"

"I understand," Miss Robin smiled. "But think about how Mango and the other friends feel. What if someone took all your favorite crayons?"

Emma Rabbit's nose twitched thoughtfully. "That would make me feel sad and left out."

"Exactly!" said Miss Robin. "When we share, everyone gets to create beautiful art."

Benny Rabbit stood up slowly. "We're sorry, Mango. We can take turns with the glitter paints."

Mango's face lit up like sunshine. "Thank you! We can make amazing pictures together!"

The rabbits carefully divided the glitter paints into small groups. Ruby gave Mango the golden paint. "This one makes the prettiest stars," she said with a shy smile.

Soon, all the animals were painting happily. Mango painted a bright jungle with golden banana trees. The rabbits made sparkly garden scenes with silver flowers.

"Look how different and wonderful everyone's art is!" Miss Robin announced. "When we share fairly, we all get to show our special creativity."

Mango looked around the room. Every painting was unique and beautiful. His heart felt warm and proud. He had been brave enough to speak up for fairness, and now everyone was happy.

"Thank you for helping us remember to share," Ruby whispered to Mango. "You're a good friend."

As they cleaned their brushes in soapy water, Mango learned something important. Being fair meant everyone got a chance to shine. And when brave friends spoke up for what was right, wonderful things happened.

The End.
