# Ellie's Garden Sharing Day

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved spending time on Grandma Elephant's sunny balcony garden. The colorful flowers smelled so sweet, and the green leaves felt cool under her tiny trunk. Today was extra special because her cousin Benny was visiting too!

"Look at all these beautiful flowers!" squeaked Ellie, her eyes sparkling with joy. She reached her little trunk toward a bright red tulip.

"I want to pick that one!" trumpeted Benny, stretching his trunk toward the same flower. "It's the prettiest!"

"But I saw it first!" said Ellie, her trunk trembling a little. The two little elephants stood nose to nose, both wanting the same special flower.

Grandma Elephant watched from her rocking chair, her wise old eyes twinkling. "Oh my," she said gently. "What seems to be the problem, little ones?"

"We both want the red tulip!" they said together, their voices getting louder.

Ellie felt her tummy get tight and worried. She really wanted that flower, but she could see that Benny wanted it just as much. His little ears drooped sadly.

"Hmm," said Grandma Elephant, standing up slowly. "This reminds me of a story from when I was young. Would you like to hear it?"

Both little elephants nodded eagerly. They loved Grandma's stories!

"Long ago," Grandma began, settling back in her chair, "two young elephants found the most delicious mango tree. But there was only one perfect, golden mango left."

"What did they do?" asked Ellie, sitting down on the soft garden mat.

"Well, first they argued, just like you two. But then they had a wonderful idea. They decided to share the mango - one elephant ate half, and the other ate half. And you know what? It tasted even sweeter because they enjoyed it together!"

Benny's ears perked up. "That's smart thinking!"

Ellie looked at the red tulip, then at Benny's hopeful face. She thought hard, her little forehead wrinkling with concentration.

"Benny," she said slowly, "what if we both take care of the flower together? We could take turns watering it each day!"

Benny's trunk wiggled with excitement. "Yes! And we could both smell it whenever we want!"

"That's perfect!" trumpeted Ellie. "We can share!"

Grandma Elephant clapped her big ears together. "What wonderful problem-solving! You found a way for both of you to be happy."

The two little elephants gently touched the red tulip with their trunks, then gave each other a big hug. The flower seemed to shine even brighter in the warm sunshine.

"Sharing feels good in my heart," whispered Ellie.

"Mine too," agreed Benny. "And now we can enjoy the whole garden together!"

They spent the rest of the afternoon exploring every corner of the balcony garden, smelling the lavender, touching the soft moss, and listening to the bees buzz happily. When they found other beautiful flowers, they made sure to enjoy them together.

As the sun began to set, painting the sky orange and pink, Ellie snuggled close to Grandma Elephant.

"Thank you for the story, Grandma. It helped us think of a good solution."

"Stories have special magic," said Grandma, stroking Ellie's head with her trunk. "They help us learn and grow. And when we share and compromise, everyone feels happy."

Ellie smiled, already excited to visit the garden again tomorrow with Benny. She had learned that sharing made everything more wonderful, and that thinking together could solve any problem.

The End.
