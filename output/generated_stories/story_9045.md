# Gigi the Giraffe Visits the World Room

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with bright eyes and a gentle smile. One sunny morning, she padded softly into the guest room at Auntie Bea’s house. The room felt cozy and cool. The curtains were soft and blue. A tiny fan hummed, “Whirrr.” The rug felt bouncy under Gigi’s hooves.

On the wall, there was a big map with many colors. Little flags poked from different places. A basket held scarves with patterns—dots, stripes, and zigzags. Gigi’s long neck stretched tall. She loved to learn. She loved to listen. She loved to think about big ideas.

“Good morning, guest room,” Gigi said in a quiet voice.

“Good morning, Gigi,” said a small voice back.

Gigi blinked. “Who said that?”

A globe on the shelf wobbled. “I did,” the globe giggled. “I’m Tippy the Globe. I spin! I show the world.”

Gigi smiled. “Hello, Tippy! You are very round.”

“Would you like to visit?” Tippy asked. “You can’t fly, but you can move your body, look, listen, and learn.”

Gigi felt brave. “Yes! I want to learn how to be a good neighbor to the whole world.”

Tippy spun gently. “Let’s start with a step.” Gigi marched in place. “Step, step, step,” went her hooves on the rug. The sound was soft and steady. Her tail swished—swish, swish.

“We are in a home,” said Tippy. “Many homes are different. Some homes take off shoes at the door.”

Gigi nodded. “I can do that.” She carefully took off her little house slippers. “It shows respect,” she said. “I ask, I listen, I follow.”

“Good,” said Tippy. “Being a global citizen means we care about people everywhere. We notice, we ask, we share, and we help.”

Gigi took a deep breath. She smelled lemon soap from the clean sheets. She felt calm and ready.

“Let’s learn a hello,” said Tippy. “In Spanish, people say ‘Hola.’”

Gigi practiced. “Hola!” Her voice was cheerful.

“In Swahili,” Tippy continued, “people say ‘Jambo.’”

“Jambo!” Gigi said, lifting her head proudly.

“In Mandarin, people say ‘Nǐ hǎo.’”

Gigi tried, slow and careful. “Nee how.” She smiled. “I am learning! My voice is brave.”

Tippy clapped with a tiny tap-tap. “You are being assertive. You speak clearly and kindly. That is courage.”

Gigi stood taller. “I can say what I need, and I can listen, too.”

Just then, a knock sounded—knock, knock. “Is someone in the guest room?” asked a little penguin peeking in. “I am Pika. I am visiting from far away. I feel shy.”

Gigi stepped closer with gentle feet. “Hola, Jambo, Nǐ hǎo, Pika! I am Gigi.” She kept her voice warm and soft.

Pika waddled on the rug. Squeak, squeak went his tiny feet. “I like your map,” he said.

“Would you like a scarf?” Gigi asked. “We can choose together.” She held up a bright scarf. “This one has dots.”

Pika shook his head. “I get warm. May I have water?”

Gigi nodded firmly. “Yes. I will ask Auntie Bea.” She trotted to the doorway. Clip, clop, clip. “Excuse me, Auntie Bea,” Gigi said clearly, “our guest needs water, please.”

“That is assertive and kind,” Auntie Bea said, bringing a cool cup. “Thank you for asking.”

Pika sipped. “Ahh.” He smiled. “Thank you.”

Gigi beamed. “We care for each other. That is global-citizenship.”

Tippy the Globe wobbled with joy. “Would you like to move like friends from around the world?”

“Yes!” said Gigi and Pika together.

“Let’s march like drummers,” said Tippy.

Gigi and Pika marched—boom, boom—soft feet on the rug. Gigi’s neck bobbed. Pika’s wings flapped. They giggled.

“Let’s sway like trees,” Tippy said.

They swayed side to side—whoosh, whoosh—like wind in the curtains.

“Let’s bow to say thank you.”

Gigi and Pika bowed. “Thank you,” they said to each other. They felt kind and strong.

Pika looked at the scarves again and picked one with tiny snowflakes. “This one reminds me of home,” he said. “May I keep it?”

Gigi took a breath. She wanted to say yes, but it was Auntie Bea’s scarf. Gigi stood tall. “I need to ask first,” she said. “I will speak up.”

She found Auntie Bea folding towels. “Auntie Bea,” Gigi said, steady and polite, “Pika likes the snowflake scarf. Is it okay if he borrows it?”

Auntie Bea smiled. “Yes, he may borrow it today.”

Gigi hurried back. “Good news, Pika! You can wear it today.” Pika wrapped the scarf and twirled—spin, spin.

Gigi felt warm inside. She had been brave and fair. She had moved, asked, and listened. She had cared for a friend.

Tippy hummed. “Gigi, what did you learn on today’s adventure?”

Gigi looked around the guest room—the blue curtains, the neat bed, the friendly globe, her new friend. She spoke clearly. “Being a global citizen means I care about people everywhere. I say hello in many ways. I ask before I take. I help when someone needs help. I use my brave voice. I move with respect. I listen and share.”

Pika nodded. “And we have fun together!”

Gigi laughed, her hooves tapping a happy rhythm. “Yes, we do.”

The fan whispered. The map glowed with many colors. Gigi felt proud and peaceful. “Tomorrow,” she said, “we can learn how to say goodbye in new ways.”

“Adiós,” said Tippy.

“Kwaheri,” said Pika.

“Zàijiàn,” said Gigi, careful and kind.

They all waved, big and small, near and far, together.

The End.
