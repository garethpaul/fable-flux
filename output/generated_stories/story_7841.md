# Paws and the Guest Room Giggle Quest

In a cozy house with soft rugs and warm smells, a friendly bear cub named Paws wiggled his nose. Today was special. Grandma Fern was visiting. She had a bright scarf with tiny stars and a gentle laugh like wind chimes. Paws loved Grandma Fern.

“Good morning, Paws,” Grandma Fern said, her scarf rustling like leaves. “Would you like to help me set up the guest room?”

Paws clapped his fuzzy paws. “Yes, please! I can help. I’m a careful helper.”

They walked down the hall. The guest room door stood open. The room smelled like clean sheets and lavender. Sunlight made gold shapes on the carpet. A small fan hummed, and the curtains swished softly.

Paws peeked inside. He saw a tall lamp, a suitcase with stickers from many places, a stack of books, and a tiny step stool. He also saw something shiny—two little coins under the bed—and a loose cord wiggling near the wall.

“Before we start,” Grandma Fern said, kneeling to eye level, “let’s do our Safety Scan.”

“What’s a Safety Scan?” Paws asked, ears perking.

“It’s when we look, listen, and think,” Grandma Fern said. “We ask, ‘Is it safe? What can we do?’ Let’s do it together.”

Paws stood tall. “Look, listen, think,” he repeated.

He looked at the room. He listened to the fan. He thought hard. “I see a loose cord,” he said. “It might make someone trip.”

“Good eyes,” Grandma Fern said. “What can we do?”

Paws thought. “We can tuck it away.”

They gently clipped the cord along the wall so no paws or feet would catch it. The clip made a little click sound.

Next, Paws spotted the shiny coins. “I see tiny things,” he said. “Little ones might put them in their mouths.”

“That’s right,” Grandma Fern said. “Tiny things are not for mouths.”

Paws picked up the coins and dropped them into a lidded jar. Clink, clink.

The room felt safer already.

Grandma Fern opened her suitcase. Inside were soft socks, a music box, and a bright pillow with stripes—purple, green, gold, and blue. Paws’ eyes grew wide. “Your pillow looks like a rainbow!”

“It came from a faraway market,” Grandma Fern said. “People there wear clothes in many colors. We all like different things, and that makes the world interesting.”

Paws smiled. “I like your scarf and your pillow. I like my fuzzy fur. We are all different—and special!”

They kept working. Paws noticed the tall lamp wobbling. He placed a steadying hand on it. “This lamp is wiggly.”

“Hmm,” Grandma Fern said. “If it falls, someone could get bonked.”

“What can we do?” Paws asked, tapping his chin.

“Let’s think,” Grandma Fern said. “Look, listen, think.”

Paws looked at the base. “We can move it to the corner,” he said. “And we can tighten this twisty part.”

They carefully moved the lamp and tightened the knob. The lamp stood straight and strong.

Suddenly, Paws smelled something sweet. Sniff, sniff. “Cookies?” he asked, eyes bright.

From the hallway came a giggle. It was their neighbor, little Jojo, waving from a wheelchair decorated with stickers—stars and moons and a friendly dragon. Jojo’s mom held a plate. “We brought welcome cookies,” Jojo said. “May I help too?”

Paws grinned. “Yes! But we’re doing a Safety Scan first.”

Jojo rolled into the doorway and paused. “There’s a bump in the rug,” they said. “My wheels could get stuck.”

Paws crouched and smoothed the rug’s curled edge. “Good thinking, Jojo.”

“Everyone sees different things,” Grandma Fern said. “That’s why we work together.”

They set the cookie plate on a low table. Paws sniffed warm cinnamon and sugar. Jojo took a deep breath too. “Mmm!”

Grandma Fern looked around. “Almost done. What about the window?”

Paws padded over. The window was open a crack. A cool breeze tickled his whiskers. The screen was strong, but the chair was right under the window.

Paws paused. He remembered a rule: Windows are for fresh air, not for climbing. He looked, listened, and thought. “The chair should be away from the window,” he said. “Someone might climb and fall.”

“Excellent,” Grandma Fern said.

They moved the chair and locked the window latch to a safe spot. Click.

Jojo clapped softly. “You are a safety star, Paws.”

Paws felt warm and proud. “We did it together.”

When everything was safe, they rested on the bed. The quilt felt soft like clouds. The music box played a gentle tune—plink, plink, plink. Grandma Fern tucked the bright pillow near the headboard.

“Thank you, helpers,” she said. “Before we snack, tell me our safety steps.”

Paws held up three claws and counted. “One: Look. Two: Listen. Three: Think. And then we fix what we can or ask a grown-up.”

Jojo added, “And we all help, because we all notice different things.”

Grandma Fern nodded. “Perfect.”

They shared cookies. The cinnamon tickled their tongues. Paws giggled when a sugar crumb stuck to his nose. Jojo laughed, and Grandma Fern dabbed it away with a napkin.

Paws curled his tail and took a happy breath. The guest room felt cozy and safe. The fan hummed. The curtains whispered. The rainbow pillow shone like a little sunrise.

“We made the room ready,” Paws said. “Safe and comfy.”

“And we used our brains and our hearts,” Grandma Fern said. “That’s the best way.”

Paws smiled. “Tomorrow, can we do a Safety Scan in the kitchen?”

“We can,” Grandma Fern said. “Safety is our daily adventure.”

Paws nodded. “Look, listen, think,” he whispered, and took another tiny bite of cookie.

The End.
