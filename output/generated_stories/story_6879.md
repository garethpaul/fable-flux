# Rascal's Big Game Day

Rascal the raccoon loved two things more than anything: making music and playing sports! He had soft gray fur with black rings around his tail, and he always carried his tiny drum wherever he went. *Tap-tap-tap!* went his drum as he bounced around the cozy guest room where he lived.

Today was special - it was the Forest Olympics! All the woodland animals would compete in fun games like running, jumping, and ball tossing. Rascal had been practicing for weeks.

"I'm going to win every single game!" Rascal announced proudly, beating his drum. *BOOM-BOOM-POW!* "I'm the fastest, strongest, most amazing athlete in the whole forest!"

But when Rascal arrived at the meadow, he saw something that made his tummy feel funny. Bella the bunny was hopping so high she almost touched the clouds! Oliver the owl was throwing pinecones farther than anyone had ever seen! And Sammy the squirrel was running so fast he looked like a furry blur!

"Oh no," whispered Rascal, his drum suddenly feeling very heavy. "Maybe I'm not the best after all."

When the first race began, Rascal tried his hardest. His little paws moved as fast as they could, but Sammy the squirrel zoomed past him like lightning. Rascal came in third place.

*Thump.* Rascal's heart felt sad.

In the jumping contest, Rascal stretched his legs and leaped with all his might. But Bella the bunny bounced so high she could see over the tallest trees! Rascal came in second place.

*Thump-thump.* Now Rascal felt even sadder.

During the pinecone toss, Rascal threw as hard as he could. But Oliver the owl's strong wings helped him throw much farther. Rascal came in last place.

Rascal sat down on a soft patch of grass, his drum silent beside him. Tears began to roll down his furry cheeks. "I'm no good at sports," he sniffled. "Everyone is better than me."

Just then, Bella the bunny hopped over. "Rascal, why are you crying?"

"I didn't win anything," Rascal said sadly. "I thought I was the best, but I was wrong."

Oliver the owl flew down and perched nearby. "But Rascal, you did something wonderful today!"

"I did?" Rascal looked up, confused.

Sammy the squirrel scurried over too. "You cheered for all of us! When I won the race, you clapped and played your drum. When Bella won the jumping contest, you made happy music. When Oliver won the tossing game, you celebrated with us!"

Bella nodded. "You made everyone feel special and happy. That's the most important part of sports!"

Oliver spread his wise wings. "Sports aren't just about winning, Rascal. They're about having fun, trying your best, and being a good friend to everyone."

Rascal wiped his eyes and picked up his drum. "Really? Even if I don't come in first place?"

"Especially then!" said Sammy. "You showed us what good sportsmanship looks like. You were kind and encouraging to everyone."

Rascal began to smile. He felt warm and grateful inside. "Thank you for being such good friends. I'm sorry I only cared about winning."

"That's okay," said Bella. "We all learned something today!"

From that day on, Rascal played sports not to be the best, but to have fun with his friends. And whenever someone felt sad about losing, Rascal would play a happy song on his drum to remind them that trying your best and being kind was the real victory.

*Tap-tap-tap!* went his drum, and all the forest friends danced together.

The End.
