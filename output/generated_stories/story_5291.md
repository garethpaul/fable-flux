# Benny Beaver’s Treehouse Day

Benny the busy beaver woke up in his cozy treehouse. The morning sun peeked through the round window, painting warm, golden dots on the wooden floor. Benny stretched his small paws and listened. He heard birds sing, “Chirp, chirp!” and leaves whisper, “Shhh, shhh,” in the soft breeze.

“Good morning, treehouse,” Benny said. He patted the smooth railing. “Thank you for keeping me safe.”

Benny loved to help. Today, he had a plan. “I will take care of my home,” he said. “I will care for the trees, the water, and the animals. That is called caring for the environment.”

He put on his tiny red hat and his bright yellow safety vest. “Safety first,” Benny said, snapping the vest closed. He grabbed his little broom and a small basket.

Down the ladder he went, step, step, step. The bark felt bumpy under his paws. Grrrrowl went his tummy. “Breakfast later,” he giggled. “First, I clean.”

Outside the treehouse, Benny saw a few bits of paper caught in the grass. “Oh no,” he said softly. “Litter makes the forest sad.” He picked up the paper and put it in his basket. Rustle, rustle went the leaves as he moved. A blue butterfly fluttered by. “Hi, Benny!” it seemed to say.

“Hello, Butterfly,” Benny smiled. “I’m cleaning up. Clean forest, happy forest!”

Benny swept the wooden porch. Swish, swish. Tiny pine cones rolled like little marbles. He stacked them in a neat pile. “These can be toys for later,” he said. He found a shiny can near a flower. “This goes in recycling,” he said. “We sort to save. We sort to care.”

“Good plan,” chirped Robin from a branch. “Thank you, Benny.”

Benny looked up and waved. “Thank you, Robin, for your song.”

He carried his basket to his tiny sorting bins: one for trash, one for recycling, one for compost. He held up a banana peel. “Compost,” he said. Plop! He held up the shiny can. “Recycling.” Clink! He held up a broken string. “Trash.” Drop!

“Sorting helps the Earth,” Benny said proudly. His whiskers twitched with joy.

Next, Benny checked his treehouse ladder. He wiggled each rung. Wiggle, wiggle. “Safe and strong,” he said. He tied a bright ribbon on the top step. “We look. We check. We stay safe.”

A soft wind blew, and Benny smelled something sweet. “Mmm, flowers,” he sighed. He tiptoed to the garden patch under his treehouse. Little yellow flowers smiled at him. Bees hummed, “Bzzzz,” like tiny songs.

“Hello, bees,” Benny whispered. “Thank you for helping the flowers grow.” He filled a small watering can. Drip, drip, drip went the water, sparkling in the sun. “We use just what we need,” he said. “Save water. Share water.”

“Benny!” called a voice. It was Tilly Turtle, carrying a small bag. “I found this near the stream. It was stuck on a stick.”

Benny took the bag carefully. “Thank you, Tilly. You kept the water safe.”

“The bag was scary,” Tilly said, eyes wide. “I moved slowly and stayed on the path.”

“You did the safe thing,” Benny nodded. “Together we care.”

They walked to the stream. The water gurgled, “Glug, glug,” over shining stones. Benny saw a plastic bottle on the bank. He put on his little gloves and picked it up. “Gloves help keep us safe,” he explained. “We never touch sharp things with bare paws.”

Tilly smiled. “I like helping.”

“Helping feels good,” Benny said. He pointed to a sign by the stream. It read, “Be kind to the Earth.” Benny traced the letters with a paw. “We can all be Earth helpers,” he said. “We can pick up litter, plant flowers, and use only what we need.”

They heard a squeak. “Eee!” It was Pip the mouse, holding a tiny seed packet. “I want to plant a tree,” Pip said.

“Great idea!” Benny cheered. “Trees give us air to breathe and shade to rest.”

They chose a sunny spot by the treehouse. Benny pressed the soil. Cool, soft dirt hugged his paws. Pip dropped the seeds in. Tilly watered carefully. Drip, drip. Benny patted the ground. “Grow strong, little tree,” he whispered.

Benny looked around. The yard was tidy. The stream was clear. The garden was happy. Birds sang louder. The air smelled fresh, like rain and pine.

“I feel grateful,” Benny said. “Thank you, trees, for shade. Thank you, water, for your song. Thank you, friends, for helping.”

“We feel grateful too,” said Tilly and Pip. They all gave a gentle hug.

The sun climbed high. Benny’s tummy rumbled again. “Snack time!” he laughed. They climbed the safe ladder, one at a time. Step, step, step. In the treehouse, Benny shared crunchy apples and nutty muffins. Crunch, munch, yum!

Pip pointed at a picture on the wall. It showed Benny sweeping. “That’s you,” Pip giggled.

Benny nodded. “Yes. I clean, I sort, I plant, I save water, and I say thank you. That is how I care for our world.”

The friends watched the little garden through the window. A ladybug landed on the glass. Tap, tap. The treehouse felt warm and kind.

Benny yawned. “What did we learn today?” he asked.

Tilly counted on her toes. “Be safe. Wear your vest and gloves. Check ladders.”

Pip squeaked, “Be grateful. Say thank you to friends and to nature.”

Benny smiled. “Care for the environment. Pick up litter, sort it, save water, and plant trees.”

They all nodded. “We are Earth helpers,” they said together.

The day felt complete. The forest whispered, “Thank you,” and the stream hummed, “Good job.”

Benny curled up on his soft blanket. “Tomorrow,” he whispered, “we will help again.”

The End.
