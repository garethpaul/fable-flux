# Tulsi's Sharing Blanket

Tulsi was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and bright, curious eyes. One breezy afternoon, Tulsi padded into the guest room. The room smelled like lemon soap and fresh flowers. The curtains swished like whispers. On the bed lay a colorful blanket, covered in tiny hand-stitched stars.

Tulsi touched the blanket with her trunk. It felt warm and bumpy. “Mama,” she called softly, “what is this blanket?”

Mama Elephant peeped in with a smile. “That is our Family Sharing Blanket,” she said. “When guests come, we spread it on the floor and share our stories and songs.”

Tulsi’s eyes grew wide. “Stories and songs? Can we share today?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Our friends are visiting. We will teach them our tradition.”

Soon, the doorbell chimed like a tiny bell bird. Two friends arrived: Rina the rabbit and Kito the kitten. The guest room filled with the smell of carrot muffins and warm milk. Rina hopped in with a basket. Kito tiptoed, his whiskers twitching.

“Welcome,” said Tulsi. “Please, come in!”

Kito looked at the blanket. “What is this?” he asked.

Rina’s nose wrinkled. “It looks old,” she said. “Maybe we should play outside instead.”

Tulsi’s ears drooped. She wanted to share the tradition. She wanted to be kind too. Her tummy fluttered like a little bird.

Mama touched Tulsi’s shoulder. “Use your words,” she whispered. “Tell your friends what matters to you.”

Tulsi took a deep breath. “This blanket is special. My grandma stitched stars for every story we share. We sit on it together. We listen. We take turns. That is our tradition.”

Kito tilted his head. “But we wanted to run outside. The sun is bright!”

Rina nodded. “And I brought a ball.”

Tulsi thought and thought. She wanted to be fair. She wanted everyone to feel happy. That felt like justice to her—making sure everyone’s needs mattered.

“I have an idea,” Tulsi said. “First, we share one story on the blanket. Then we all go outside and play your game. We can do both. We can be fair.”

Kito and Rina looked at each other.

“That sounds fair,” said Kito.

“I can try,” said Rina. “I will listen.”

They sat on the soft blanket. It smelled like clean cotton and sunshine. Mama lit a small lantern that glowed like a friendly moon.

Tulsi began in a quiet voice. “This is my grandma’s star,” she said, touching a blue stitch. “She sang about listening with big ears and a big heart.” Tulsi hummed a gentle tune. The notes were slow and cozy. Kito’s tail curled. Rina’s ears relaxed.

“Now it’s your turn,” said Tulsi. “We take turns on the Sharing Blanket.”

Kito whispered, “I can tell a kitten story.” He told a story about a windy day and a paper kite. His paws made little swish-swish sounds as he showed how the kite flew. Rina clapped softly.

Rina spoke next. “I will share a snack. In my family, we share carrot muffins.” She handed them out. The muffins were warm and sweet. Crumbs tickled Tulsi’s tongue.

Tulsi smiled. “Thank you. Sharing makes the stars brighter.”

Kito looked around the guest room. “I like this tradition,” he said. “It feels safe and cozy.”

Rina nodded. “I like taking turns. It helps me listen.”

Tulsi felt proud. Her ears wiggled. “Now it’s time for your turn outside!”

They carefully folded the blanket. Tulsi patted it with her trunk. “We will come back later,” she promised.

Outside, the sun kissed their faces. The air smelled like grass and pine. Birds chirped tip-tip-tip. Rina rolled the ball. Kito chased it with quick, happy steps. Tulsi ran in gentle thumps, feeling the warm ground under her feet. They laughed together, and their laughter sounded like bells and bubbles.

When they grew tired, they went back to the guest room for cool water. Kito leaned on the doorframe. “Tulsi, today we learned your tradition. Tomorrow, can you learn ours?”

Tulsi’s eyes sparkled. “Yes! We can share traditions every day. We can take turns, indoors and outdoors.”

Mama smiled. “That is the heart of tradition-sharing. We show respect. We listen. We make space for each other. That is justice and kindness.”

Rina nibbled a last crumb. “I thought the blanket was old,” she said softly. “But now I think it is beautiful.”

“It is beautiful because we share,” Tulsi said. “When we share, we glow like stars.”

Kito purred. “I feel warm inside.”

Tulsi hugged her friends with her soft trunk. “Me too.”

That night, Mama stitched a tiny new star on the blanket. It shone gold. Tulsi watched, eyes sleepy, heart full.

“What will this star mean?” Tulsi asked.

Mama winked. “It means today we learned to share traditions, be fair, and play together—inside and outside.”

Tulsi curled up, listening to the gentle hush of the curtains and the sweet lemony air. “I will remember,” she whispered. “I will share, I will listen, and I will be kind.”

The End.
