# Max's Morning Mix-Up

The morning sun painted the rooftop golden yellow. Max Monkey loved his daily exercise time up here. The cool breeze tickled his fuzzy brown fur as he stretched his long arms high above his head.

"One, two, three!" Max counted loudly as he did his jumping jacks. His feet made thumping sounds on the rooftop tiles.

Down below, Mrs. Robin was trying to feed her three tiny babies in their nest. The loud thumping scared the little birds.

"Peep! Peep!" cried the baby birds, flapping their small wings nervously.

Mrs. Robin flew up to the rooftop. "Excuse me, Max," she chirped politely. "Could you please exercise more quietly? My babies are frightened by the loud sounds."

Max stopped jumping and put his hands on his hips. "This is MY exercise time!" he said firmly. "I always exercise here every morning. It's my routine!"

Mrs. Robin's feathers ruffled sadly. "I understand, but—"

"No buts!" Max interrupted, swinging from the antenna. "I was here first! Find somewhere else for your nest!"

Mrs. Robin's eyes filled with tears. She flew away without another word.

Max continued his exercises, but something felt different. The sunshine didn't seem as bright. The breeze felt cooler. His tummy felt twisty and uncomfortable.

He did his sit-ups, but kept thinking about Mrs. Robin's sad face. He tried his stretches, but remembered how scared the baby birds looked.

"Maybe I was too loud," Max whispered to himself. He touched his chest where his heart was beating. "Maybe I hurt their feelings."

Max climbed down from the rooftop and found Mrs. Robin sitting quietly on a branch. The baby birds were still peeping nervously.

"Mrs. Robin?" Max called softly. His voice was gentle now, not loud and bossy.

She looked at him with tired eyes. "Yes, Max?"

Max's cheeks felt warm and red. He twisted his tail nervously. "I'm sorry I was mean to you. I'm sorry I was so loud. I didn't think about how my exercising was scaring your babies."

Mrs. Robin's expression softened. "Thank you for saying sorry, Max. That means a lot to me."

"I still want to exercise every morning," Max said carefully. "But maybe we can find a way to share the rooftop? I could exercise more quietly, or maybe at a different time?"

Mrs. Robin's eyes brightened. "That would be wonderful! What if you exercised on the other side of the rooftop? It's farther from our nest."

Max clapped his hands together. "That's a great idea! And I'll do quiet exercises like stretching and yoga poses instead of jumping!"

The next morning, Max climbed to the quiet side of the rooftop. He did gentle stretches and peaceful yoga poses. The baby birds chirped happily in their nest, and Mrs. Robin sang a beautiful morning song.

"This feels much better," Max said to himself, reaching his arms toward the warm sun. "Being kind feels better than being loud."

From that day on, Max and Mrs. Robin shared the rooftop peacefully. Max learned that saying sorry when you hurt someone's feelings is brave and important. He also learned that sharing and thinking about others makes everyone happier.

Every morning, Mrs. Robin would chirp "Good morning, Max!" and Max would wave gently back. The rooftop became a place of friendship, respect, and peaceful exercise for everyone.

The End.
