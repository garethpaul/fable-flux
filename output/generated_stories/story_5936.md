# Daisy's Rooftop Adventure

Little Daisy the deer fawn had the softest brown fur and the biggest, kindest eyes in the whole forest. One sunny morning, while playing hide-and-seek with her forest friends, Daisy took a big leap and landed right on top of a tall building's rooftop!

"Oh my!" gasped Daisy, looking around with wide eyes. The rooftop was flat and gray, with little puddles from last night's rain that sparkled like tiny mirrors in the sunshine. Far below, she could see the green treetops of her forest home. "How will I ever get down?"

Daisy's legs felt wobbly with worry. She tried to jump down, but it was much too high and scary. She sat down carefully and felt tears starting to fill her eyes.

"Help! Help!" called Daisy in her sweet, small voice.

Soon, she heard a gentle flapping sound. A wise old pigeon named Pete landed softly beside her. "Hello there, little one. What's wrong?"

"I'm stuck up here!" Daisy explained. "I jumped too high during our game, and now I can't get home to my mama."

Pete cooed thoughtfully. "Don't worry, Daisy. I have an idea, but we'll need to work together. Are you brave enough to try?"

Daisy nodded, feeling a little spark of courage in her heart. "Yes, I'll try!"

"First," said Pete, "we need to practice moving together. Can you walk slowly and carefully across the rooftop while I fly beside you?"

Daisy stood up on her thin legs and took one small step, then another. Pete flew right next to her, his wings making soft whooshing sounds. "That's wonderful!" he chirped. "You're doing great!"

They practiced walking and flying together until Daisy felt confident and coordinated. Her steps became steady and sure.

"Now," said Pete, "I'm going to call my pigeon friends. When they arrive, we'll make a soft, feathery bridge from the rooftop to that big oak tree. But you'll need to be very brave and trust us."

Soon, the sky filled with the gentle sounds of many wings. Pete's friends arrived - there was Penny with her shimmery gray feathers, Paul with his speckled chest, and many more kind pigeons.

"Thank you all for coming to help me!" said Daisy, her heart feeling warm and grateful. "You're so kind!"

The pigeons lined up in a row, their wings touching, creating a soft bridge of feathers from the rooftop to the strong oak tree. Daisy could smell the fresh morning air and feel the gentle breeze ruffling her fur.

"I'm scared," whispered Daisy, "but I'm grateful you're all here with me."

"We believe in you," cooed all the pigeons together.

Taking a deep breath, brave little Daisy stepped carefully onto the feathery bridge. One step, two steps, three steps - she moved slowly and steadily, just like she had practiced. The pigeons' soft feathers felt warm and safe under her hooves.

"I did it!" Daisy cheered as she reached the oak tree and climbed down to the soft forest floor. Her mama was waiting with the biggest hug.

"Thank you, thank you, dear friends!" called Daisy to all the pigeons. "I'm so grateful for your help and kindness!"

From that day on, Daisy always remembered to be thankful for the friends who helped her and to be brave when facing new challenges.

The End.
