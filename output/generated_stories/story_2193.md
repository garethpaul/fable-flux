# Melody's Big Decision

In a cozy, quiet attic filled with dusty boxes and golden sunbeams, lived a little yellow bird named Melody. She had the most beautiful voice in the whole house, and she loved to sing sweet songs that echoed softly through the wooden beams.

"Tweet-tweet-trill!" Melody sang happily every morning, her tiny chest puffing out with pride. The attic was her special home, warm and peaceful, with soft shadows dancing on the walls.

One sunny day, Melody discovered something wonderful. Behind an old trunk, she found a pile of colorful ribbons, shiny buttons, and sparkly beads scattered everywhere. "Oh my!" she chirped excitedly. "These are so pretty!"

But then Melody noticed a problem. Three little mice lived in the attic too - Pip, Squeak, and Nibbles. They were all looking sadly at the pretty treasures.

"We found these first," squeaked Pip, his whiskers twitching nervously.

"But we can't agree on how to share them," added Squeak, her tiny paws fidgeting.

"Pip wants all the ribbons, I want all the buttons, and Nibbles wants all the beads," explained Squeak. "Now we're all upset and can't play together."

Melody fluffed her bright yellow feathers thoughtfully. She could see that her friends were unhappy, and the beautiful treasures were just lying there in a messy pile instead of bringing joy.

"Hmm," Melody sang softly. "I think I can help, but I need to make a good decision."

Melody thought carefully. She could take the treasures for herself - after all, she was bigger than the mice. Or she could fly away and ignore the problem. But neither of those choices felt right in her little bird heart.

"I know!" Melody chirped brightly. "Let me show you something fair and fun!"

She hopped down to the pile of treasures, her tiny claws clicking on the wooden floor. "Let's organize these together! We can sort them into groups, and then everyone can choose what they like best."

Melody began separating the items with her beak. "Red things here, blue things there, big things in this pile, small things in that pile."

The mice watched with growing excitement. "That's so smart!" squeaked Nibbles.

"Now," sang Melody, "let's take turns choosing. Pip, you pick first, then Squeak, then Nibbles, then me. We'll keep taking turns until everything is shared fairly."

The mice's eyes sparkled with happiness. "What a wonderful idea!" they all squeaked together.

One by one, they took turns choosing treasures. Pip picked a red ribbon, Squeak chose a shiny silver button, Nibbles selected a blue bead, and Melody picked a tiny golden bell that would make her songs even more beautiful.

Soon, all the treasures were shared equally, and everyone had something special they loved. The attic felt warm and friendly again, filled with the sounds of happy squeaking and gentle singing.

"Thank you, Melody!" said the mice. "You made such a good decision to help us!"

Melody puffed out her chest proudly. "When we work together and think about being fair, everyone can be happy," she sang sweetly. "Making good decisions means thinking about others, not just ourselves."

From that day on, whenever there was a problem in the quiet attic, all the friends remembered Melody's lesson. They learned that the best decisions are the ones that help everyone and bring friends together.

And Melody's songs sounded even more beautiful, because they came from a heart full of kindness and wisdom.

The End.
