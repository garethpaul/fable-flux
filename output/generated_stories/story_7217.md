# Hoot in the Guest Room

In a quiet house with soft rugs and sunny windows, there was a cozy guest room. The guest room smelled like clean sheets and lavender. The curtains were light and fluttery. A small lamp glowed warm and yellow. On a pillow sat a wise old owl named Hoot. Hoot had round, kind eyes and soft, speckled feathers. He liked to read and to help.

One day, a little child named Mia tiptoed into the guest room. Mia’s slippers went pat, pat, pat on the wooden floor. She saw a shiny glass snow globe on the bedside table. Inside the globe, glitter sparkled like tiny stars. Mia loved sparkles.

“Oh!” said Mia. “So pretty!”

Hoot blinked and smiled. “Hoo-hoo, hello, Mia,” he said in a gentle voice. “Please be careful in here. The guest room has special things.”

Mia nodded. “I just want to look,” she said. Her fingers itched to touch the globe. She reached out with one finger. Tap.

The snow globe rocked. The glitter swirled faster. Tap, tap. The globe wobbled again.

“Mia,” Hoot said softly, “two taps is too many.”

“I won’t tap again,” Mia said.

But Mia’s hand felt curious. She wanted to know how the glitter moved. She picked up the globe to peek closer. Uh-oh. Her hands were small. The globe felt big and slippery.

Slip!

Clunk!

The globe fell to the rug. The top cracked just a little. A tiny line. A drip of water shined.

Mia’s tummy felt twisty. Her cheeks got hot. “Oh no,” she whispered. Her eyes filled with tears. She put the globe back and hid her hands behind her back.

Hoot tilted his head. He saw the tiny crack. He heard the tiny drip. “Hoo-hoo,” he said. “Something happened.”

Mia looked down at her slippers. “I… I don’t know,” she said very softly. She felt scared. She did not want to get in trouble.

Hoot hopped closer on the pillow. “Mia,” he said in a calm voice, “our words should be true. Honesty helps us learn and fix things. When we tell the truth, we grow wise. When we hide, we stay stuck.”

Mia sniffled. She rubbed her sleeve over her eyes. “I’m sorry,” she whispered. “I picked it up. I dropped it. I was curious. I should have asked. I didn’t tell the truth right away.” Her face felt warm, but her heart felt a tiny bit lighter.

Hoot nodded and smiled kindly. “Thank you for being honest,” he said. “That was brave. Curiosity is good. It helps us learn. But we must be careful and ask before we touch.”

Mia took a deep breath. She listened to the quiet room. She heard the tiny drip again. Plip. Plip. “What do we do now?” she asked. “How can we fix it?”

“Good question,” said Hoot. “Let’s use knowledge. Let’s learn.” He fluffed his feathers and pointed a wing at a small basket under the nightstand. “There is a towel, some tape, and a card for notes.”

Mia knelt on the soft rug. She felt the towel—fluffy and dry. “It’s soft,” she said. She dabbed the drip. Dab, dab. The lavender smell floated up again.

Hoot said, “We can write a note and tell what happened. That is being honest. We can also tape the crack so it won’t drip more. That is using what we know.”

Mia nodded. Her shoulders relaxed. “I can do that,” she said. She pressed a tiny piece of clear tape over the crack. Smooth, smooth. The dripping stopped. She smiled a small smile.

“Now the note,” Hoot said. He gave Mia a card and a pencil. The pencil felt cool and a little bumpy in her hand. She wrote slowly: “I am sorry. I dropped the globe. I tried to fix it. Love, Mia.”

Mia read the words out loud. Her voice was quiet but steady. Hoot listened and hummed. “Hoo-hoo, that is an honest note,” he said. “You told the truth. You said sorry. You tried to help. That is wise.”

Just then, Mama walked in. She smelled like apples and soap. She looked at Mia. She looked at Hoot. She looked at the globe. “What happened here?” she asked.

Mia held out the note with two small hands. “I dropped the globe, Mama,” she said. “I was curious. I didn’t ask. I was scared, and I almost did not tell. I am sorry. Hoot helped me learn. We dabbed the drip. We used tape. We wrote a note.”

Mama knelt down and hugged Mia. The hug felt warm and safe. “Thank you for telling the truth,” said Mama. “That is very brave and kind. Accidents happen. We can learn from them.”

Mia hugged back. Her tummy felt calm again. Hoot blinked happily. “Hoo-hoo,” he said. “Honesty opens the door to learning. With truth, we find good answers.”

Mama smiled. “We will fix the globe together,” she said. “Next time, please ask before you touch.”

“I will,” said Mia. She looked at the lamp’s warm glow and the soft curtains. The guest room felt friendly again.

Hoot perched tall on the pillow. “Today we learned three things,” he said. “One: Be honest. Two: Ask before you touch. Three: Use knowledge to solve problems.”

Mia nodded. “Honesty helps us learn,” she said. “And learning helps us fix things.”

Hoot fluffed his feathers and gave a happy hoot. Mia giggled. The room smelled sweet and safe. The glitter in the globe still sparkled in the light.

The End.
