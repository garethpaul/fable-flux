# Polly Paints a Boundary

Polly was a creative parrot who lived in a bright home office. The room smelled like fresh paper and lemon cleaner. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Click-click went the keyboard. Whirr went the little fan. Tap-tap went Polly’s beak on a jar of crayons.

“I love colors!” Polly squawked happily. “Squawk! Let’s make art!”

Polly’s best friends, Mia and Jay, liked to play in the home office too. Mia wore soft socks that slid on the smooth floor. Jay’s shirt had little stars that sparkled. They danced and wiggled when they were happy. Today, they wanted to spin and draw with Polly.

Mia ran in. “Polly! Can we dance and color with you?” she asked.

“Squawk-yes!” said Polly. “We can move our bodies and make pictures!”

They put paper on the rug. Crayons rolled and bumped. Blue, red, yellow, green—so many colors! Polly flapped her wings and wiggled her tail. Mia tiptoed and twirled. Jay hopped like a bunny.

But the home office had a desk where Dad worked. His laptop sat very still. A mug of tea steamed with a soft, sweet smell. A sticky note said, “Quiet time: 2 to 3.” The clock ticked like a tiny drum.

Dad peeked in. “Friends, I’m working,” he said gently. “Please be careful around my desk. This is my workspace.”

Polly nodded. “Squawk, we will be careful.”

They tried to stay on the rug. But the music in their hearts felt big. Mia spun a little too far. Jay hopped a little too high. Polly flapped, flap-flap, with a swirl of crayons.

Oops! A bright red crayon rolled under the desk.

“I’ll get it!” Polly chirped. She waddled under the desk with a rustle of feathers.

Her wing brushed the mug. The mug wobbled. “Oh!” said Polly.

Splash! The tea tipped. It dripped like brown rain onto a stack of papers. Drip, drop, plop.

Dad looked surprised. “Oh no! My papers,” he said. His voice was not loud, but it was tight, like a tied shoe.

Polly’s heart went thump-thump. Her feathers felt hot. “I’m sorry,” she whispered. “I did not stay in the play space.”

Mia’s eyes got wide. “We didn’t remember the rule,” she said softly.

Jay hugged Polly’s wing. “We can fix it together,” he said.

Dad took a deep breath. In…and out. He knelt down so his face was kind again. “Thank you for saying sorry,” he said. “Accidents happen. Let’s clean up. After, we can make a plan to help everyone.”

They moved their bodies with a job. Mia got a towel. Jay picked up crayons. Polly flapped gently to dry the papers. Swish, pat, dab. Clean, clean, clean. Working together felt good, like a team dance.

When the floor was dry and the papers were safe, Dad smiled. “Teamwork!” he said. “Now, let’s talk about personal boundaries.”

Polly tilted her head. “Boundaries?” she asked.

“A boundary is a safe space,” Dad explained. He put a long, soft scarf on the floor and made a big square around the rug. “This is the play space. Inside the scarf, you can dance and color. Outside the scarf is my work space. Please ask before you step out.”

Polly nodded. “A boundary keeps us safe and kind. Squawk!”

Mia asked, “Can we move our bodies inside the scarf?”

“Yes,” said Dad. “You can hop, stretch, and wiggle. Just watch each other. If someone says ‘Stop’ or ‘Space, please,’ you pause and give room.”

Jay tried it. He stretched his arms like a starfish. “Space, please,” he said gently.

Polly stepped back. “Okay! I hear you,” she said. She felt proud of her listening.

To remember the plan, Polly had a bright idea. “Let’s make a boundary sign!” she chirped. She drew a green circle with a happy face for “Go” inside the scarf, and a yellow triangle with a thinking face near the desk for “Ask first.”

Mia colored the edges. Jay added arrows that showed where to move. Dad wrote the words: “Play Space” and “Work Space.”

They tried again. Music played softly. Mia twirled inside the scarf square. Jay hopped and clapped. Polly fluttered and tapped her beak to the beat: tap-tap-tap. When the red crayon rolled toward the edge, Polly called, “Stop!” Everyone froze like statues. Then Polly said, “Ask first,” and looked at Dad.

Dad smiled. “Yes, you may get the crayon. I’ll hold the mug.”

Polly waddled carefully, picked up the crayon, and went back. No spills. She felt light as a feather.

After playtime, Polly ruffled her feathers and looked at Dad. “Thank you for forgiving me,” she said.

“I forgive you,” Dad said. “You listened. You helped. You learned. That’s what families do.”

Polly looked at Mia and Jay. “We make art, we move, and we respect boundaries,” she said proudly.

Mia giggled. “And we ask for space when we need it.”

Jay nodded. “And we help each other.”

The home office felt calm and happy. The lemon smell was back. The fan went whirr. The keyboard went click-click. The scarf boundary sat like a cozy hug for the play space.

Polly hung their sign on the wall. “Our plan keeps everyone safe,” she said. “Squawk! Boundaries are kind. Teamwork makes it work.”

The End.
