# Benny Beaver's Colorful Meal

Benny Beaver sat at his wooden dining room table, staring at his plate. Every day, he ate the same thing - plain brown bark chips. *Crunch, crunch, crunch.* The sound echoed through his cozy dining room.

"This is so boring," Benny sighed, pushing the bark around his plate with his tiny paw. "I wish my meals were more exciting!"

Benny's tummy rumbled, but he didn't feel hungry for the same old food. He looked around his warm dining room with its checkered tablecloth and sunny windows. Outside, he could see his garden full of colorful vegetables growing tall and bright.

"Wait a minute!" Benny's eyes sparkled with an idea. "Maybe I can try something new!"

Benny waddled outside to his garden. The morning sun felt warm on his fuzzy brown fur. He saw bright orange carrots peeking from the soil, green lettuce leaves dancing in the breeze, and red tomatoes hanging like little lanterns.

"These look so pretty and smell so fresh!" Benny said, sniffing the sweet garden air. "I wonder if they taste good too?"

First, Benny picked a crunchy orange carrot. He took a small bite. *Crunch!* It was sweet and made his teeth feel strong. "Wow! This gives me energy!" he cheered.

Next, he tried a leafy piece of lettuce. It was cool and refreshing, making his mouth feel clean and happy. "This makes me feel fresh!" Benny giggled.

Then he picked a juicy red tomato. When he bit into it, sweet juice ran down his chin. "Mmm! This tastes like sunshine!" he laughed, wiping his whiskers.

Benny gathered all the colorful vegetables in his little basket. Back in his dining room, he arranged them on his plate like a rainbow. Orange carrots, green lettuce, red tomatoes, and even some purple berries he found.

"This looks like art!" Benny clapped his paws together. "And it smells amazing!"

As Benny ate his colorful meal, he felt something wonderful happening. The orange carrots made his eyes feel bright and sharp. The green lettuce made his body feel light and strong. The red tomatoes made his heart feel happy and full of energy.

"I feel like I can build the biggest dam ever!" Benny exclaimed, flexing his little muscles.

Just then, his friend Rosie Rabbit hopped by the window. "Benny! You look so healthy and happy today! What's different?"

"I decided to try new foods!" Benny said proudly, showing her his colorful plate. "Look at all these beautiful colors! Each one makes me feel strong in a different way!"

"That's so smart, Benny!" Rosie smiled. "Different colored foods give our bodies different kinds of help!"

From that day on, Benny always made sure his meals looked like rainbows. He learned that orange foods helped his eyes, green foods gave him energy, red foods made his heart strong, and purple foods helped him think clearly.

"Eating different colors isn't just pretty," Benny told himself as he enjoyed his daily rainbow meal. "It's the best way to help my body grow big and strong!"

Benny felt proud that he had been brave enough to try something new. His dining room was now filled with the happy sounds of crunching colorful vegetables instead of boring bark chips.

The End.
