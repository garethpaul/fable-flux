# Toby the Tortoise and the Colorful Chaos

In a bright and cheerful art room filled with rainbow paintbrushes, sparkling glitter, and colorful papers, lived Toby the Tortoise. Toby was very wise and moved slowly and carefully through the room. He loved watching the children create beautiful art every day.

The art room smelled like sweet crayons and fresh paint. Sunlight streamed through big windows, making the glitter jars twinkle like stars. Toby's favorite spot was on a soft cushion near the easels, where he could see everything happening.

One Tuesday morning, something went wrong. Two little friends, Emma and Jake, were both reaching for the same bright red paint jar.

"I need the red paint!" Emma said loudly, pulling on one side of the jar.

"No, I was here first!" Jake replied, tugging on the other side. "I need it for my fire truck!"

"But I'm painting roses!" Emma's voice got louder. "Red roses need red paint!"

The jar wobbled back and forth between them. Other children stopped their painting to watch. The room became noisy and tense.

Toby watched quietly from his cushion. He took a slow, deep breath through his nose, smelling the familiar art supplies. Then he began to move slowly toward the children.

"Oh my," said Toby in his gentle, calm voice. "It sounds like we have a problem here."

Emma and Jake both turned to look at the wise tortoise. They were still holding the paint jar, but they stopped pulling.

"Toby!" Emma said. "Jake won't share the red paint!"

"Emma grabbed it from me!" Jake added, his cheeks red and puffy.

Toby nodded slowly. "I can see you both really want to use the red paint. That must feel frustrating." He settled down on the floor near them. "Let me ask you something. When I feel upset, do you know what I do?"

"What?" both children asked together.

"I take three slow breaths, just like this." Toby demonstrated, breathing in slowly through his nose and out through his mouth. "Would you like to try it with me?"

Emma and Jake looked at each other, then set the paint jar down safely on the table. They took three deep breaths with Toby.

"Ahhhh," said Emma. "That feels better."

"Yeah," Jake agreed. "My tummy doesn't feel tight anymore."

"Wonderful!" Toby smiled. "Now, let's think together. What are some ways you could both use the red paint?"

Emma thought for a moment. "We could take turns?"

"That's a great idea," said Toby. "What do you think, Jake?"

Jake nodded. "Emma can use it first for her roses. Then I can paint my fire truck."

"But wait," said Emma, looking more carefully at Jake's picture. "Your fire truck is really cool! What if we painted together? I could help you make the fire truck, and you could help me with my roses."

Jake's eyes lit up. "Really? That sounds fun!"

Toby clapped his flippers together slowly. "What wonderful problem-solving! You both stayed calm, listened to each other, and found a solution that makes everyone happy."

The two friends spent the rest of art time painting together, sharing not just the red paint, but all the colors. They laughed and created the most beautiful fire truck with roses painted on its side.

From his cushion, Toby watched with a warm feeling in his heart. The art room smelled sweet again, and peaceful music seemed to float through the air.

"Remember," Toby called softly to all the children, "when problems come up, take a deep breath, listen with your heart, and think together. There's always a way to solve things with kindness."

The End.
