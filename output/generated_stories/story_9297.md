# Gigi’s Fair Dance

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with long, gentle eyelashes and soft, spotted fur. She lived in a comfortable nursery filled with sunshine, squishy rugs, and the sweet smell of clean blankets. The nursery had a big basket of instruments, a rainbow of pillows, and a shiny music box that played dancing songs.

One morning, the teacher, Miss Star, said, “Today we will have a dance party!” The music box twinkled. The lights were soft and warm. Gigi’s heart went thump-thump with happy excitement.

Her friends gathered on the rug—Bibi the bunny, Max the monkey, and Tia the turtle. Gigi bent her long neck and whispered, “I love to dance, but I also like space to move.”

Miss Star smiled. “Good dancers listen, share, and give everyone room. That is fair.”

Gigi nodded. She cared about fairness. Justice means making sure everyone gets what is right and kind. Gigi wanted that.

The music began. Ting-ting, swish-swirl! Gigi stepped lightly. Tap, tap. She stretched her legs and swayed her neck. It felt like wind in tall grass. Bibi bounced, Max twirled, and Tia slid in slow circles. Everyone giggled.

But soon Max spun very fast. He slid close to Gigi and bumped her knee. “Oops!” he said, still spinning.

Gigi wobbled. “I need space, please,” she said softly.

Max didn’t hear. The music was loud. He bumped Bibi’s ears. Bibi frowned. “Ow. My ears need room.”

Tia’s shell tapped the floor. “Too tight,” she said. “I want to dance, too.”

Gigi felt a warm squeeze in her chest. This wasn’t fair. Justice means we all get to dance safely. She took a deep breath. She could think of a way to help.

“Pause!” Gigi called in her gentle, giraffe voice. She reached her long neck to the music box and pressed stop. The room grew quiet. They could hear the soft whoosh of the air and the tick-tick of the clock.

Miss Star nodded. “Thank you for pausing, Gigi. What do you need?”

Gigi said, “Max, I like your spins. They are fun to watch. But I need space so I don’t get hurt. Bibi needs space for her ears. Tia needs space for her shell. That is fair.”

Max’s eyes got big. He looked at his feet. “I didn’t mean to bump. I got excited.”

“I know,” said Gigi kindly. “Excited is okay. Bumping is not. Let’s make a fair dance plan.”

Miss Star clapped softly. “Great idea. How can we make it fair?”

Gigi looked around. The squishy rug felt soft under her hooves. The pillows were bright and puffy. She had an idea. “We can make dance circles! We can use pillows to mark our space. We can decide where spins go and where slow moves go.”

“That sounds just,” said Tia, smiling. “Just means fair.”

Bibi hugged a pink pillow. “I will help!”

They worked together. Gigi placed pillows in a wide square on the rug. Bibi lined up two more to make a spin spot. Tia slid a pillow near the wall for slow, smooth moves. Max put a pillow near the instruments for tap-tap toe dances.

Gigi said, “Let’s talk about rules. Rules help justice happen.”

Miss Star wrote the rules on a small white board:
- Use your own dance circle.
- Ask before coming close.
- If someone says “Stop,” you stop.
- If you bump, say “Sorry,” and give space.

Gigi asked, “Does everyone agree?”

Max raised his hand. “I agree.”

Bibi and Tia nodded. “We agree.”

Gigi added, “We can also take turns in the spin spot. One, then two, then three, then four.”

Miss Star smiled. “You made a fair plan with clear choices. That is good decision-making and cooperation.”

The music began again. Ting-ting, swish-swirl! Gigi swayed in her circle. She could smell the lemon-clean floor and feel the soft rug under her hooves. Max waited at the spin spot. “My turn?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Gigi. “You’re first.”

Max spun. Whirr! He stayed in his pillow circle. Bibi bounced in her space like a tiny drum. Tap-tap! Tia glided slowly, her shell gleaming in the warm light.

“Gigi, may I dance next to you?” Bibi asked.

Gigi smiled. “Yes, but just one step away.” Bibi nodded and kept the space. It felt safe. It felt fair.

After a while, Max forgot and slid a toe over Gigi’s pillow line. Gigi lifted her head. “Stop, please,” she said calmly.

Max stepped back. “Sorry,” he said. “I will stay in my circle.”

“Thank you,” said Gigi. Her chest felt light again. Everyone was smiling. The nursery hummed with happy sounds—music, giggles, and the gentle swish of feet.

When the song ended, they all clapped. Miss Star said, “What did we learn today?”

Gigi said, “Justice means being fair. We make room for everyone. We listen to words like ‘Stop’ and ‘Please.’ We share space, take turns, and make good choices.”

Max added, “Justice feels good. No bumps!”

Bibi said, “My ears are happy.”

Tia said, “My shell is safe.”

Gigi’s heart felt warm. She had used her careful thinking to help her friends. Together, they made a fair dance. Together, they kept personal boundaries and cooperated.

Miss Star gave them each a shiny star sticker. “You were kind and just today.”

Gigi tucked her sticker on her soft fur and whispered, “Fair can be fun.”

The End.
