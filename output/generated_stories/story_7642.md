# Pip's Colorful Discovery

Pip the penguin waddled into the bright art room with a big smile. The morning sun streamed through tall windows, making the colorful paints sparkle like jewels. Today was painting day, and Pip couldn't wait to create something beautiful!

"Good morning, Pip!" called Mrs. Robin, the art teacher. "What would you like to paint today?"

"I want to paint a rainbow!" Pip chirped excitedly, flapping his little wings. "The biggest, most beautiful rainbow ever!"

Mrs. Robin helped Pip put on his special painting apron. It was blue with yellow stars, just like Pip's favorite. She placed a big white paper on the easel and set out brushes and paints in every color Pip could imagine.

Pip dipped his brush in red paint and started at the top. "Red is for strawberries!" he sang happily. But when he tried to make a perfect curve, the paint dripped down in wobbly lines.

"Oh no!" Pip said, his voice getting a little shaky. "That doesn't look right."

He tried again with orange paint. "Orange is for... for..." But this time, the brush felt too big in his flipper, and orange paint splashed onto the red.

Pip's cheerful smile began to fade. His little heart started beating faster. "This is too hard!" he huffed, and before he knew it, he threw his brush down on the table with a loud CLAP!

Paint splattered everywhere – on the table, on his apron, and even on Mrs. Robin's feathers!

"Oh my!" Mrs. Robin said gently, wiping orange paint from her wing.

Pip looked at the mess he had made. His eyes filled with tears. "I'm sorry, Mrs. Robin," he whispered. "I didn't mean to make such a big mess. I just wanted my rainbow to be perfect, and it wasn't working!"

Mrs. Robin knelt down next to Pip. "It's okay to feel frustrated, Pip. Sometimes when things don't go the way we want, we feel upset inside. But throwing things isn't the best way to handle those big feelings."

"What should I do instead?" Pip asked, sniffling.

"When you feel frustrated, you can take three deep breaths," Mrs. Robin showed him. "In through your beak... and out. In... and out. In... and out. Feel better?"

Pip nodded. The tight feeling in his chest was going away.

"And if something is still too hard, you can ask for help," Mrs. Robin continued. "Would you like me to show you a trick for painting rainbows?"

Together, they cleaned up the splattered paint with warm, soapy water. The bubbles tickled Pip's flippers and made him giggle.

Mrs. Robin showed Pip how to use a smaller brush and how to paint gentle curves by moving his whole flipper, not just his wrist. "Art takes practice," she said. "Even grown-ups make wobbly lines sometimes!"

This time, when Pip painted, he remembered to breathe deeply when things felt tricky. When the yellow mixed with the green by accident, instead of getting upset, he said, "Mrs. Robin, could you help me with this part?"

By the end of art time, Pip had created a wonderful rainbow. It wasn't perfect, but it was colorful and bright and made him feel proud.

"Thank you for teaching me how to handle my frustrated feelings," Pip said, giving Mrs. Robin a gentle hug. "And thank you for helping me with my rainbow."

"You're very welcome, Pip. Remember, taking deep breaths and asking for help are always good choices when things feel hard."

Pip waddled home with his rainbow painting, excited to show his family what he had learned – not just about painting, but about handling big feelings too.

The End.
