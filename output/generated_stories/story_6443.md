# Milo the Mouse Makes a Plan

In a comfortable nursery filled with soft rugs and sunlit curtains, a kind little mouse named Milo peeked out from a small hole in the wall. The nursery smelled like warm blankets and fresh crayons. Toy blocks made colorful towers. A rocking chair creaked gently. It was a calm and cozy place.

Milo had shiny whiskers and bright button eyes. He loved when the baby napped and the room hummed with quiet. Today, though, the quiet was broken by a sound.

Tap, tap, tap!

Milo shivered a little. “What is that?” he whispered.

A tall toy robot stood in the middle of the room. Its silver arms clicked. Its light blinked red. The robot blocked the path to a tiny basket of crumbs near the window. Milo’s tummy rumbled. He needed just a few crumbs for dinner.

“I can be brave,” Milo told himself. “I can be calm.” He took a slow breath in. He let a slow breath out. “I can use my words.”

The robot’s head turned with a soft whirr. “Stop,” the robot said in a low, tinny voice. “This area is closed.”

Milo stood up straight, even though he felt small. “Hello,” he said kindly. “My name is Milo. I would like to pass, please. I only need a little food.”

“Closed,” the robot repeated. Its light blinked again. “No passing.”

Milo blinked back. His heart did a tiny thump-thump, but he kept his voice gentle. “I am not here to break. I am here to share. Can we make a plan together?”

The rocking chair creaked. The curtains swished. Milo listened to the soft sounds and felt calmer. He thought of a plan. He remembered what Mama Mouse said: “When you feel stuck, make a bridge with words.”

“What does a fair plan look like?” Milo asked the robot. “You can guard. I can nibble. We can both be okay.”

The robot paused. “Plan?” it asked.

“Yes,” said Milo. “A plan. We can take turns. Or we can trade. You like order. I can help with order.”

The robot’s light turned from red to soft orange. “I like order.”

Milo nodded. “I can line up the toy blocks in a neat row. I can place the crayons back in the cup. Then, you can let me pass to the little basket. I will take only three crumbs. I will count them. One, two, three. Then I will go home.”

The robot clicked and whirred. “Count. Order. Trade.” It looked at the scattered blocks and the crayons rolling under the shelf. “Acceptable?”

Milo rubbed his whiskers. He spoke slowly and clearly. “Let me say our plan. I clean up. You watch. After that, you let me pass to the basket. I take only three crumbs. We both feel good.”

The robot stood still, listening. “Plan accepted,” it said at last.

Milo smiled. He felt warm and brave. “Thank you,” he said. “You made a good choice.”

He got to work. He pushed the blue block with his tiny paws. Scrape, scrape. He nudged the red block next to it. Tap. He lined up yellow, green, and purple. He found the crayons under the shelf, one by one, and rolled them back into the cup. Click, click, click. The nursery looked tidy again.

The robot watched closely. “Neat,” it said. Its light turned a friendly green.

Milo sat down and took another calm breath. “Now it is your turn,” he said kindly.

The robot moved aside with a smooth whoosh. “You may pass.”

Milo walked to the little basket by the window. The sun warmed his fur. He reached in and chose three tiny crumbs. “One,” he counted softly. “Two. Three.” He put them in a leaf pouch.

He looked back at the robot. “I kept the plan,” he said.

The robot’s head nodded with a soft click. “Plan kept. Good teamwork.”

Just then, a teddy bear on the shelf tumbled and bumped a small bell. Ting! The bell’s sound made the baby in the crib stir and let out a sleepy mmm. Milo froze. The robot’s light flashed yellow. “Quiet mode,” it whispered.

Milo nodded. He pressed a gentle paw to his chest and breathed slowly. The baby sighed and drifted back to sleep. The room felt calm again. The curtains swayed. The rocking chair gave one tiny creak and rested.

Milo tiptoed back to the robot. “Thank you for working with me,” he whispered. “You guarded well, and I cleaned well.”

“Thank you for asking with kind words,” the robot replied. “You were brave and calm.”

Milo’s eyes shone. “When we use calm voices and make a fair plan, we both win,” he said.

The robot’s light glowed green. “We both win,” it agreed.

Milo waved and scampered to his little home in the wall. He shared the three crumbs with Mama Mouse and Baby Pip. “I used my words,” he told them proudly. “I made a plan. I was brave and calm.”

Mama Mouse hugged him. “That is real courage,” she said. “You solved a problem with kindness.”

Milo curled up in a soft cotton bed, warm and happy. Outside, the nursery was neat and peaceful. He closed his eyes and whispered, “Next time, I will use my words again.”

And the room, and the robot, and the little mouse all felt safe and good.

The lesson: When you feel stuck, breathe, use calm words, and make a fair plan. That is brave. That is negotiation.

The End.
