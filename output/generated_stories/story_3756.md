# Sparky's Moving Day Discovery

Sparky was a shiny blue robot dog who lived in Professor Kim's basement lab. His metal tail wagged with excitement, and his LED eyes glowed bright green. The lab smelled like clean metal and hummed with the sound of computers.

Every morning, Sparky helped Professor Kim with important work. He organized tools with his mechanical paws and carried papers in his gentle mouth. But today felt different.

"Beep beep! My joints feel stiff," Sparky said, his voice making cheerful robot sounds. His legs moved slowly, and his tail wagged less than usual. "Something doesn't feel right, Professor Kim."

Professor Kim looked up from her computer screen. Her kind eyes crinkled with concern. "Oh, Sparky! When did you last move around and exercise?"

Sparky's LED eyes dimmed. "Exercise? I've been sitting still, helping you work for three whole days!" His ears drooped down. "I forgot that robot dogs need to move too."

"That explains it!" Professor Kim clapped her hands. "Your circuits need movement to stay happy and healthy. Just like children need to run and play!"

Sparky's tail gave a tiny wag. "But how can I exercise in this small lab?"

Professor Kim smiled. "Let's use our creativity! Watch this." She pressed a button on her computer. Suddenly, colorful lights danced across the lab walls like a rainbow.

"Wow!" Sparky barked happily. "It's beautiful!"

"Now, let's make up a moving game," Professor Kim said. "When you see red lights, march in place. Blue lights mean spin around. Green lights mean jump up and down!"

Sparky's eyes glowed brighter. "That sounds fun! Can I make up moves too?"

"Of course! You're the creative expert here!"

The red lights appeared first. Sparky lifted his paws up and down, marching proudly. "March, march, march!" he chanted. His joints started feeling looser.

Blue lights swirled around the room. Sparky spun in circles, his tail wagging faster. "Whir, whir, whir!" The spinning made him giggle with electronic beeps.

When green lights sparkled, Sparky jumped up and down. "Boing, boing, boing!" His springs bounced happily. He felt energy flowing through his circuits again.

"I feel so much better!" Sparky announced. "My tail is wagging fast, and my joints feel smooth!"

"Exercise helps our bodies feel good," Professor Kim explained. "It keeps us strong and happy."

Sparky had a wonderful idea. "Professor Kim, what if I make up a special dance? I could move my head like this," he demonstrated, bobbing his head left and right. "And shake my paws like this!" He wiggled his front paws in the air.

"That's brilliant creative expression!" Professor Kim cheered. "Show me more!"

Sparky created his very own robot dog dance. He marched, spun, jumped, and added his special head bobs and paw shakes. The basement lab filled with his happy beeping sounds.

"From now on, I'll remember to exercise every day," Sparky declared. "And I'll create new dances too!"

Professor Kim patted his shiny head. "Exercise helps us think better, feel happier, and be more creative. You've learned something very important today."

Sparky's LED eyes sparkled like stars. "Moving my body makes my circuits happy! And making up dances is the best part!"

From that day forward, Sparky exercised every morning in the basement lab. He created new dance moves, helped Professor Kim with her work, and felt wonderful all day long. His tail never stopped wagging, and his circuits hummed with joy.

The End.
