# Squeaky's Big Clean-Up Day

Squeaky the mouse lived in a cozy corner of the guest room. Every morning, he liked to tidy up his little home before breakfast. Today felt special because his friend Bunny was coming to visit!

"Oh my!" squeaked Squeaky as he looked around. The guest room was very messy. Pillows were scattered on the floor, books were tumbled everywhere, and dust bunnies danced in the corners.

Squeaky's whiskers twitched with worry. "How can I clean all this before Bunny arrives? It's too much work for one little mouse!"

He started by trying to move a big pillow. "Push, push, push!" he grunted. But the pillow was three times bigger than him! It barely budged an inch.

"This is impossible!" Squeaky said, feeling his tiny heart beat faster. He sat down and felt angry tears in his eyes. "I'll never finish in time!"

Just then, he remembered what Mama Mouse always told him: "When things feel too hard, take three deep breaths and try again."

Squeaky closed his eyes. "Breathe in... breathe out... breathe in... breathe out... breathe in... breathe out." His little chest felt calmer now.

"Maybe I don't need to move the whole pillow," he thought. "Maybe I can push it bit by bit!"

So Squeaky pushed one corner of the pillow. It moved a little! Then he pushed another corner. It moved again!

"I'm doing it!" he cheered, his tail wiggling with excitement.

Next came the books. They were heavy too, but Squeaky had an idea. Instead of carrying them, he rolled them across the smooth wooden floor. "Roll, roll, roll!" The books made a fun rumbling sound as they moved.

When he reached the dusty corners, Squeaky used his fluffy tail like a feather duster. "Swish, swish, swish!" The dust tickled his nose and made him giggle.

Halfway through cleaning, Squeaky heard a gentle knock at the door.

"Squeaky? It's me, Bunny!" called a sweet voice.

"Oh no!" worried Squeaky. "I'm not finished yet!"

But when Bunny hopped in, she smiled warmly. "What a wonderful job you're doing! Can I help you finish?"

"Really? You'd help me?" asked Squeaky, his eyes sparkling.

"Of course! Friends help friends," said Bunny. "Teamwork makes everything easier and more fun!"

Together, they finished cleaning in no time. Bunny used her strong legs to hop and move the last few pillows, while Squeaky organized the books by color.

"We did it!" they cheered together, high-fiving with a tiny paw and a soft paw.

As they sat down to share some cheese and carrots, Squeaky felt proud and happy. The guest room sparkled like sunshine, and the air smelled fresh and clean.

"I learned something important today," said Squeaky thoughtfully. "When something feels too hard, I can take deep breaths, try different ways, and ask friends for help. I don't have to give up!"

"That's right!" agreed Bunny. "You kept trying even when it felt difficult. That's called persistence, and it's a very special skill!"

From that day on, whenever Squeaky faced a big challenge, he remembered his cleaning adventure. He would breathe deeply, try step by step, and remember that asking for help made everything better.

The End.
