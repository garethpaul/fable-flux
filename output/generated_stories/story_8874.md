# Penny's Rooftop Art Garden

On top of a tall apartment building lived a patient panda named Penny. Her black and white fur was soft like velvet. Her round ears twitched when she heard sounds. Today, Penny felt excited but also a little worried.

"Today is the day!" Penny said to herself. She had been planning something special for weeks. On the sunny rooftop, she had set up an art station with colorful paints, brushes, and big sheets of paper.

Penny wanted to invite all the neighborhood animals to make art together. But she felt nervous butterflies dancing in her tummy.

"What if they don't want to share?" Penny wondered aloud. "What if they fight over the red paint?"

Just then, she heard little paws climbing up the fire escape. Three young raccoons appeared, their eyes wide with curiosity.

"Wow!" squeaked the smallest raccoon named Rascal. "Look at all those pretty colors!"

"Can we paint too?" asked Ruby, reaching for a brush.

Penny took a deep breath. She noticed her heart beating fast. "I feel excited and nervous at the same time," she said honestly. "I want everyone to have fun, but I'm worried about sharing."

The third raccoon, Rex, tilted his head. "Why do you feel worried, Penny?"

"Sometimes when friends share art supplies, they might grab or argue," Penny explained. "That makes me feel sad inside."

Ruby nodded. "I understand. When I feel worried, my tummy feels funny too."

Penny smiled. Talking about her feelings made them feel smaller. "Let's make a plan together," she suggested. "We can take turns with each color. When someone is using red paint, we wait patiently."

"That sounds fair!" Rascal clapped his tiny paws.

Penny arranged the paints in a circle. The warm sun made the colors sparkle like jewels. The fresh rooftop breeze carried the sweet smell of flowers from the garden below.

"I'll start with blue," Penny said, dipping her brush. She painted gentle waves across her paper. "Who wants to use yellow next?"

"Me, please!" Ruby raised her paw politely.

As Ruby painted bright sunshine, Rex waited quietly. When he felt impatient, he said, "My body wants to grab the paint, but I'm choosing to wait."

"That's very brave," Penny praised. "Noticing our feelings helps us make good choices."

The raccoons took turns with each color. When Rascal accidentally dripped green paint, he felt upset.

"My painting is ruined!" he cried, his eyes filling with tears.

Penny put down her brush and sat beside him. "I can see you feel frustrated," she said gently. "Your shoulders are tight and your face looks sad."

"Yes," Rascal sniffled. "I wanted it to be perfect."

"Sometimes accidents happen," Penny said. "Would you like to try turning that drip into something beautiful? Maybe a leaf or a flower?"

Rascal's face brightened. With Penny's help, he turned the green drip into a lovely tree. Soon he was giggling with joy.

As the afternoon sun grew warm, all four friends had created beautiful paintings. The rooftop smelled like paint and happiness.

"Thank you for teaching us about feelings," Ruby said, admiring everyone's artwork.

"And for sharing so generously," added Rex.

Penny felt proud and peaceful inside. "When we notice our feelings and talk about them, we can solve problems together," she said. "And when we take turns and share, everyone has more fun."

The friends hung their paintings on a clothesline to dry in the gentle breeze. The colorful art fluttered like flags of friendship against the blue sky.

"Tomorrow, let's paint again!" suggested Rascal.

"Yes," Penny agreed, her heart full of warmth. "And we'll remember to share our feelings and our supplies."

The End.
