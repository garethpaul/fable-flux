# Nutty Snacks on the Go

Squeak was a small, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail. Today, Squeak buckled into the back seat of the family car. The seat was soft, the belt clicked, and the car smelled like warm sunshine and a hint of orange peel. Vroom, vroom—the car hummed like a sleepy bee.

“Where are we going?” Squeak asked, wiggling with excitement.

“To the park,” said Dad, smiling in the mirror. “Do we have snacks?”

Squeak’s tummy gave a little rumble. “Snacks!” Squeak patted a tiny backpack. “I packed them myself.”

The car rolled down the street. Trees waved. A gentle breeze came through the open window, bringing the crisp smell of leaves. Squeak felt proud and independent. “I can help. I planned our snacks.”

Mom turned around and asked, “What did you pack, clever Squeak?”

Squeak unzipped the backpack. Rustle, rustle. “Apples slices, crunchy carrots, soft cheese cubes, whole-grain crackers, and a little bag of nuts.” Squeak held up two bottles. “And water! One for me, one for you, so it’s fair.”

“That sounds like a rainbow of healthy foods,” said Mom. “Great nutrition!”

Squeak nodded. “Food helps us play! Carrots help eyes. Apples help smiles. Water keeps us strong.”

The car stopped at a light. A soft ding came from the dashboard. “Oops,” said Dad. “We forgot the big snack box.”

Squeak blinked. “It’s okay! We can share my backpack snacks.” Squeak felt brave. “I can figure it out.”

At the park, the family stayed in the car a little longer to plan. The sun warmed the windows. Birds chirped outside like tiny flutes. Squeak spread a napkin on the seat like a little table.

“Teamwork time,” said Squeak. “We will divide the snacks. Everyone helps, everyone shares.”

“How will we make it fair?” asked Mom.

Squeak thought, tail twitching. “We will count. One, two, three! Everyone gets the same number of slices and crackers. And we listen to each other.”

Dad nodded. “Good plan, leader Squeak.”

Squeak gently opened the snack containers. Crunch! The carrot sticks snapped when Squeak set them down. The apple slices smelled sweet and juicy. The cheese cubes were soft and a little bouncy. The crackers made a happy rustle.

Squeak said, “First, let’s wash hands.” They used a little bottle of hand gel. Rub, rub, rub—hands felt cool and clean.

Squeak passed out napkins. “One for you, one for you, and one for me.” Then Squeak counted apple slices. “One, two, three for Mom. One, two, three for Dad. One, two, three for me.” Squeak smiled. “Fair!”

“Thank you,” Mom said. “You’re very careful.”

Squeak sipped water. The cold sip felt fresh and bright. “Mmm. Water helps our bodies.”

Dad opened a small packet with a crinkle. It was a shiny wrapper from an old candy. “Oh,” he said. “We don’t need this.”

Squeak held the wrapper. “This goes in recycling,” Squeak said. “But wrappers can’t always be recycled. We can put it in the trash so the park stays clean.”

“You are right,” said Mom. “We recycle bottles and paper, and we throw away wrappers if they can’t be recycled.”

Squeak looked at the two water bottles. “When we are done, we put them in the recycling bin. That helps the Earth.”

They ate together, crunching carrots and munching crackers. “Crunch, crunch,” sang Squeak. “Yum, yum.” The family smiled. Their tummies felt happy.

A little tap came at the window. Tap, tap. A small raccoon friend, Ria, waved. “Hello! I forgot my snack,” Ria said softly. “Do you have any to share?”

Squeak thought again. “We planned for three. Now we are four.” Squeak counted the food. There was just enough—if they worked as a team.

Squeak said, “We can make it fair. We will each get a little less, but everyone will have some.” Squeak split the cheese cubes in half and broke the crackers gently. “Snap.” The pieces were smaller, but there were more to share.

Ria’s eyes sparkled. “Thank you.”

Squeak nodded. “Healthy snacks help us all.”

They ate and chatted. The car felt cozy and bright. The air smelled like apples and clean seats. Everyone took small bites and small sips. No one was too full, and no one was left out.

When they finished, Squeak held up the empty containers. “Teamwork cleanup!” They put apple cores in a compost bag, bottles in the recycling bin, and the tiny wrapper in the trash. The napkins went in the right place too. The car stayed tidy.

Dad smiled. “You showed independence by planning. You showed teamwork by sharing. You showed fairness by counting. And you chose good nutrition.”

Squeak’s tail swished with joy. “Healthy food helps me think and play. Sharing makes my heart feel warm.”

Ria waved. “Let’s play at the park now!”

Squeak giggled. “Yes! Our bodies are ready.”

They hopped out of the car, hands clean, tummies happy, and smiles wide. The sun shone, the breeze whispered, and the day felt bright and kind.

Squeak whispered, “Today I learned: choose healthy foods, share fairly, and take care of our Earth.”

The End.
