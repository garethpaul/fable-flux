# Ellie's Musical Heart

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved music more than anything in the whole world. Every morning, she would trumpet a happy tune and swing her trunk like she was conducting an orchestra. Her favorite place was the cozy musical practice room at the animal school, where colorful instruments lined the walls and soft sunlight streamed through the windows.

One sunny Tuesday, Ellie walked into the practice room, her big ears flapping with excitement. She wanted to practice her trumpet for the upcoming spring concert. But when she got there, she saw Benny the bunny sitting at the piano, playing the same song over and over again.

*Plink, plink, CLANK!* went the piano keys.

"Oh no," whispered Ellie softly. The music room had a rule that only one animal could practice at a time, and Benny was already there. Ellie's trunk drooped sadly. She really needed to practice her trumpet piece today.

Ellie felt frustrated bubbling up in her tummy like fizzy bubbles. For a moment, she thought about using her big voice to tell Benny to leave. But then she remembered what her mama always said: "When we have problems, we solve them with kindness, not loudness."

Taking a deep breath that filled her whole elephant chest, Ellie walked over to Benny. She noticed he looked worried and kept making mistakes.

"Hi Benny," said Ellie gently, her voice soft as a whisper. "Your music sounds lovely. Are you getting ready for the spring concert too?"

Benny's ears drooped. "Hi Ellie. Yes, but I keep messing up this tricky part. I've been here all morning trying to get it right." His little paws trembled on the keys.

Ellie's heart filled with understanding. She knew exactly how that felt! Instead of being upset about not getting to practice, she had a wonderful idea.

"You know what?" said Ellie, her trunk curling up in a smile. "I need to practice my trumpet too. What if we help each other? I could listen to your piano piece and help you find the tricky spots. Then maybe you could listen to my trumpet song?"

Benny's eyes lit up like little stars. "Really? You would help me?"

"Of course!" trumpeted Ellie happily. "Music is always better when friends make it together!"

So Ellie sat down beside the piano bench, her big ears listening carefully as Benny played. When he got to the tricky part, Ellie gently hummed along, helping him find the right rhythm. Her trunk swayed back and forth, keeping perfect time.

"There! You've got it!" cheered Ellie when Benny played the whole song perfectly.

Then it was Ellie's turn. She picked up her shiny trumpet while Benny listened with his whole heart. When Ellie felt nervous about the high notes, Benny encouraged her with little claps.

"Your trumpet sounds like sunshine!" said Benny.

By the end of their practice time, both friends had learned their songs perfectly. But more importantly, they had learned something even better - that sharing and kindness could turn problems into wonderful solutions.

"Thank you for being so kind, Ellie," said Benny as they packed up their instruments. "You turned my worried morning into the best practice ever!"

Ellie's heart felt warm and happy. "Thank you too, Benny. I learned that helping friends feels even better than practicing alone."

From that day on, Ellie and Benny always practiced together, making beautiful music and even more beautiful friendship.

The End.
