# Joey Jumps In to Help

Tap, tap, tap went the keys. Click, click went the mouse. The home office was comfy and bright. A soft lamp glowed. Papers sat in neat stacks. A plant smelled fresh and green. A big clock went tick-tock on the wall.

Joey, a joyful kangaroo joey, peeked in with a bounce. His tail swished. His ears perked up. He loved to help. He loved to learn. He loved to jump!

“Good morning, Mum!” Joey chirped. “It’s work time, hop-a-doodle-doo!”

Mum smiled from her chair. “Good morning, Joey. I have lots to do,” she said. “I need quiet for a little while. Then we can play.”

Joey nodded. “Okay! I can help. I can be Quiet Joey,” he said softly, pressing a finger to his lips.

Joey looked around. He smelled warm toast from the kitchen. He heard the clock. He saw a basket with crayons and paper. He saw a tiny bell, a tidy trash bin, and a sticker chart with sunny stars.

Joey remembered the family routine. The chart said: Morning Hugs. Breakfast. Quiet Time. Snack. Play. Tidy. Story. Nap.

He hugged Mum. “Morning hug!” he said. Hug, squeeze. It felt warm and safe.

Then he whispered, “Quiet time.” He tiptoed to the rug. He took a deep breath. In and out. His feet felt the soft, fuzzy carpet. His hands felt the smooth crayons. He began to draw a big, bright sun.

But soon, a problem hopped in.

Mum’s papers slid. Swoosh! One flew to the floor. Another paper turned sideways. The tiny bell rolled under the desk. Mum frowned, just a little. “Oh dear,” she said. “I need those papers in order.”

Joey looked up. He felt a wiggle in his tummy. He wanted to jump. He wanted to help. But Mum needed quiet.

Joey took a small hop. “I can help with quiet,” he whispered. “Quiet help. Soft help.”

He tiptoed to the desk. “Mum, may I cooperate?” he asked, sounding out the big word slowly. “Co-o-per-ate means we help each other.”

Mum’s eyes lit up. “Yes, Joey. Thank you. Let’s work as a team,” she said, smiling.

Joey made a plan. He pointed to the sticker chart. “Routine time! First, we breathe.” They both took three soft breaths. In… out. In… out. In… out. Their shoulders relaxed.

“Next,” Joey whispered, “we sort.” He picked up the paper on the floor with gentle paws. He felt the paper smooth and cool. “Is this the first?” he asked.

Mum nodded. “Yes. It has the red dot.”

Joey looked for dots. Red, then blue, then green. “Red on top. Blue next. Green last,” he said, tapping each one softly. “Tap, tap, tap.” He made a neat pile.

The tiny bell still hid under the desk. Joey lay on his belly and peeked in. It was dark and dusty. He felt the floor cool on his tummy. “I see it!” he whispered.

“How can we get it?” Mum asked.

Joey thought. “We can cooperate. You hold the light. I will reach.” Mum turned on her phone light. A soft white glow lit the small space. Joey reached in, slow and careful. Clink! His paw touched metal. He rolled the bell out. Ding! It made a tiny sound like a silver star.

“Teamwork!” Joey cheered quietly. Mum gave a thumbs-up.

Joey saw the tidy trash bin. Some tiny paper bits were on the rug. “I can help more,” he said. He pinched the bits and dropped them in. Swish, swish. The bin smelled like clean paper. He lined up the crayons in a rainbow row. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple. It looked happy.

Mum kept typing. Tap, tap, tap. She looked calmer now. Joey felt proud and steady. He remembered the next part of the routine. Snack time.

He tiptoed to the kitchen and poured water into his cup. Glug, glug. He peeled a banana. Peel, peel. Sweet smell, soft fruit. He brought Mum a sip of water, too.

“Thank you, helper,” Mum said. She sipped. “You are making my work time smooth.”

Joey grinned. “We are a team! We cooperate.”

Then there was one last problem. The big clock went tick-tock, tick-tock, and Joey’s legs got wiggly again. He wanted to jump high, so high!

He tapped the sticker chart. “Routine says play is next after quiet,” he said. “When the big hand is on the 12, can we have a jump break?”

Mum checked the clock. “Yes,” she said. “Five more minutes. Can we count together?”

Joey nodded. “We can help each other wait.”

They counted slow. “One… two… three…” Joey hugged his tail to keep it still. Mum rubbed his back. The lamp hummed softly. The plant leaves were shiny in the light. Joey breathed in. He breathed out. He waited with Mum.

When the big hand reached the 12, Mum stood up. “Jump break!” she cheered.

Joey bounced, boing, boing, boing! Mum hopped once, too. They laughed. Their worry was gone. The room felt bright and light.

After the jump break, Joey put a sunny star on the sticker chart. “We followed our routine,” he said. “We helped each other. We cooperated. That made the work easier and the waiting easier too.”

Mum knelt to Joey’s eye level. “Yes,” she said softly. “Cooperation means we listen, we help, and we take turns. With teamwork and a routine, we can solve problems.”

Joey hugged Mum. “Teamwork makes it smooth,” he said. “I like being a helper.”

Mum hugged back. “I like being on your team.”

The clock went tick-tock. The lamp glowed warm. The plant smelled green. Joey felt happy and calm. He knew that helping, waiting, and working together made the day sweet.

The End.
