# Ollie's Pantry Ball Game

Ollie the otter loved to play! He had shiny brown fur and bright, curious eyes that sparkled when he found something fun to do. Today, he discovered something amazing in the big pantry where all the food was kept.

"Wow!" squeaked Ollie, looking at all the round cans and boxes. "These look just like balls! I can play sports in here!"

Ollie picked up a can of soup. It was smooth and cool in his little paws. He rolled it across the floor like a bowling ball. "Strike!" he giggled as it knocked over some crackers.

Next, he found a bag of oranges. "Perfect for basketball!" Ollie said, tossing an orange high into the air. But instead of catching it, the orange bounced off a shelf and knocked down a jar of pickles.

CRASH!

"Uh oh," whispered Ollie, looking at the mess. Pickle juice was everywhere, and the pantry looked like a tornado had visited.

Just then, Mama Bear walked in. She saw the spilled pickles, scattered crackers, and oranges rolling around the floor.

"Ollie!" she said, her voice firm but kind. "What happened here?"

Ollie's whiskers drooped. "I wanted to play sports, but I made a big mess instead. I'm sorry, Mama Bear."

Mama Bear knelt down and looked at the sad little otter. "I can see you love sports, Ollie. But the pantry isn't the right place to play ball games. The food could get hurt, and someone might slip on the spills."

"But where can I play?" asked Ollie, his eyes filling with tears.

Mama Bear smiled warmly. "Let's clean this up together first. Then I'll show you the perfect place for sports!"

Ollie and Mama Bear worked as a team. They wiped up the sticky pickle juice with soft towels. The smell was sour but not too bad. They carefully put the crackers back in their box and gathered all the oranges in a basket.

"Teamwork makes cleaning faster!" said Mama Bear as they organized everything back on the shelves.

When the pantry was neat and tidy again, Mama Bear took Ollie outside to the big backyard. The grass was soft and green under their feet, and the sun felt warm on their fur.

"This is your sports area!" announced Mama Bear. "Here you can play ball games safely without breaking anything."

She brought out a soft rubber ball that bounced just right. "This ball is perfect for outdoor sports. It won't break anything if it bounces around."

Ollie's eyes lit up like stars. "Can we play catch?"

"Of course!" laughed Mama Bear.

They threw the ball back and forth, and Ollie practiced his catching skills. When he missed, the ball just bounced harmlessly on the grass. They played soccer, rolling the ball with their paws, and even tried some gentle bowling with empty cardboard boxes.

"This is so much better than the pantry!" panted Ollie happily. "The ball doesn't break anything, and I have so much room to run!"

As the sun began to set, painting the sky pink and orange, Ollie hugged Mama Bear tight.

"Thank you for helping me find the right place to play sports," he said. "And thank you for teaching me that when I make a mistake, I should clean it up and try to do better next time."

"That's what friends do," smiled Mama Bear. "We help each other learn and grow."

From that day on, Ollie always played sports in the backyard, and the pantry stayed neat and organized. And whenever he made a mistake, he remembered to clean it up and ask for help when he needed it.

The End.
