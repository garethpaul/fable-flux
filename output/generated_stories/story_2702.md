# Rollo the Rabbit's Spin and Win

Rollo the rabbit hopped into the laundry room with bright eyes and a brave little heart. His soft ears perked up at the steady hum. “Whirr, whirr, whirr,” sang the washing machine. The room smelled warm and clean, like sunshine on cotton. Baskets of clothes made soft hills, and a pile of socks looked like tiny sleeping snakes.

“I want to help,” Rollo said. “I’m small, but I’m strong and smart.”

Rollo’s friend Dot the duck waddled in, little feet pat-pat on the tile. “Hi, Rollo! What are you doing?”

“I want to move the heavy basket to the dryer,” Rollo said, pointing to a big basket full of damp towels. The towels were squishy and cool. “But it’s very heavy.”

Dot looked at the basket. “That’s a big job,” she said. “Maybe we can do it together.”

Rollo took a deep breath. He put a paw on his chest and said, “Stop, breathe, choose.” He had learned those words when his feelings got too big. He smiled. “I will try a safe plan.”

He wiggled his whiskers and bent his knees. “I will push with slow, strong legs,” he said. “Ready, body?” His feet made a soft thump on the floor as he got into position.

Rollo pushed the basket. It slid a tiny bit. “Squeeeak,” went the plastic on the tile.

“I moved it!” Rollo cheered, his tail wiggling.

Dot clapped her wings. “Great job! Can I help with some movement too?”

“Yes!” Rollo said. “Let’s make it fun. We can move like workers. We can squat, we can lift, we can wiggle.”

They made a plan:

- Rollo would push with steady legs.
- Dot would pull the towel corners like ropes.
- They would count their moves to stay calm.

“One, two, push through!” they sang.

Rollo bent his knees—down, up—down, up. It felt good to use his muscles. Dot pulled gently, wings tucked, feet firm. The basket slid more. “Squeak, squeak, squeak.”

“I feel strong,” said Rollo. “My legs are working!”

“I feel steady,” said Dot. “My wings are helping!”

Then the dryer beeped. “Beep-beep!” It was ready and waiting. Rollo felt excited. He also felt a little wiggly and rushed. He grabbed a heavy towel too fast and stumbled. “Ooof!” he said, sitting on his fluffy tail.

Dot stopped and looked at him with kind duck eyes. “Are you okay?”

Rollo nodded, ears drooping a bit. “I went too fast.”

He put a paw on his belly and took a slow breath. In, out. “Stop, breathe, choose,” he said softly. “My body needs slow.”

Dot breathed with him. “We can move slow together.”

Rollo smiled. “Let’s try a sock game. Small things first. We can march to the dryer.”

They picked up socks and marched, heel-to-toe, heel-to-toe. “March, march, gentle and large,” they sang. The socks felt soft like clouds in their paws and wings. They tossed them into the dryer with tiny swishes. “Whoosh, plop!”

“Now the shirts,” said Dot. “We can stretch long like tall trees.”

They reached up high. “Stretch!” Their bodies felt long and light. They shook the shirts out. “Flap-flap!” The shirts whispered like leaves in wind. Into the dryer they went.

Rollo’s chest felt warm. “I’m doing it,” he said. “I’m careful and strong.”

Finally, the towels. Rollo bent his knees again. He wrapped his paws around one towel. “Lift with legs, not with back,” he said. “Legs, please help.” He felt the towel’s cool weight and took one step, then another, slow and steady. Dot guided the edge. Together, they slid the towel into the dryer. “Thump… and in!”

They did a towel dance after each one: step, step, spin! Rollo giggled. “Movement makes me happy!” Dot quacked with joy.

At last, the basket was empty. Rollo pressed the dryer button. “Beep!” The machine rumbled. “Whrummm,” it sang, soft and warm.

Rollo and Dot sat on the floor, ears and feathers relaxed. The laundry room smelled like clean rain. The hum was cozy. Rollo touched his chest. His heart beat slow and proud.

“I had a big problem,” Rollo said. “The basket was too heavy.”

“You found a plan,” Dot said. “You moved your body in safe, strong ways. You used your breath. And we worked as a team.”

Rollo’s ears stood tall. “I asked for help, and I helped back. I can do hard things with my body. I can wait and move slow when I need to.”

Dot nodded. “That is self-regulation,” she said carefully. “It means you notice your feelings and choose your moves.”

Rollo smiled. “I like that. Stop, breathe, choose.”

They bumped paw to wing. “Teamwork!” they said together.

The dryer finished with a happy “Ding!” Rollo opened the door. Warm air puffed out, like a hug. He and Dot folded a towel, corner to corner, with slow hands. It felt soft and sunny. Rollo’s voice was calm and sure. “I am small,” he said, “and I am strong. My body helps me. My friends help me. I can help too.”

He did a little hop-hop-hop and a gentle spin. “Movement is my superpower!”

Dot laughed. “And your heart is brave.”

Rollo grinned. “Both together make big jobs feel just right.”

The End.
