# Monkey Mio’s Kind Dance

Monkey Mio was small, brave, and very curious. He wore a bright blue ribbon on his tail. Today, he opened the door to the musical practice room. The room was full of sounds and colors. Tappy shoes sat by the wall. Shiny drums blinked under the lights. A big mirror showed Mio’s smile.

“I am ready to dance,” Mio said. His voice echoed, “Dance, dance.”

He took a deep breath. The room smelled like lemon polish and warm wood. He heard a soft hum from the air vent. He wiggled his toes. He wiggled his fingers. He felt brave.

“I will learn a new step today,” Mio told himself. “Step, step, turn!”

He played a little drum—tap-tap-tap—and counted, “One, two, three!” His feet shuffled on the smooth floor. Swish, swish, swish. He tried a twirl. “Woooo!” He giggled when his ribbon spun like a blue wheel.

Then Mio stopped. He saw something on the floor. A trail of shiny beads lay near the music stand. Next to the beads was a fallen broom, and a small dustpan was upside down.

“Oh no,” said Mio. “The room is messy. Someone needs help.” He looked around and called, “Hello?”

A tiny voice answered from behind the piano. “I’m here.”

Out came Cricket Pip, rubbing one leg. Pip’s eyes were wet and shiny. “I tried to clean up, but I dropped the beads, and the broom fell, and the dust is tickly.”

Mio knelt down. “Are you hurt?”

“My leg is okay,” Pip said softly. “But I made a big mess. I wanted the room to be nice for dancing.”

Mio put a gentle hand near Pip so he could climb onto his palm. “You tried your best. That was kind,” Mio said. He looked at the beads and the broom. He thought, and his tail ribbon bobbed. “We can solve this together. First, we breathe.”

They took slow breaths. In. Out. The air smelled like lemon and a little bit like dust. Mio smiled. “Now we make a plan.”

He spoke slowly so Pip could follow:
- “One: We pick up the big beads.”
- “Two: We sweep the tiny crumbs.”
- “Three: We check the floor for safety.”
- “Four: We dance!”

Pip’s antennae perked up. “A plan!”

Mio made a little song to help. “Pick and plink, sweep and swish, safe and clear, dance your wish!” He sang it with a gentle beat: tap-tap, swish-swish.

“Pick and plink!” Pip echoed, and they began. Pip pushed the beads with his tiny legs, plink, plink, into a small bowl. Mio used two careful fingers to gather the rest. “We are a good team,” Mio said.

“Swish and swish,” Mio sang as he lifted the broom. He held the dustpan steady, and Pip brushed the crumbs in with tiny strokes. Swish, swish, soft and neat. The broom bristles whispered on the floor, and the dustpan clicked shut.

They looked at the floor in the mirror. It was shiny and clear. Mio nodded. “Step three: safety check.” He took slow steps. Tap. Tap. Tap. No slip. No trip. “All safe!”

Pip chirped a happy note. “Step four?” he asked.

Mio bowed. “Dance!”

He turned the drum to a calmer beat. Tap-tap… tap. “Let’s make a kindness dance,” he said. “It can show how we helped.”

They made simple moves:
- A gentle bend to say, “I see you.”
- A soft hand to the heart to say, “I care.”
- A sweeping arm to say, “I help.”
- A bright twirl to say, “We did it!”

Mio showed each move slowly. “Bend,” he said. “Hand to heart.” He felt warm inside, like sunshine. “Sweep.” His arm whooshed like wind. “Twirl!” His ribbon spun, and Pip did a tiny jump.

Pip added a little wiggle that looked like a smile. “This is our compassion wiggle,” Pip said. “It feels gentle.”

Mio laughed. “Perfect!” They put it all together:
“See you,” bend.
“I care,” hand to heart.
“I help,” sweep.
“We did it,” twirl.
“Compassion wiggle,” wiggle-wiggle!

They danced it again, counting, “One, two, three, and breathe.” The drumbeat made the floor hum under their feet. The mirror showed two happy friends moving like music.

When they finished, Mio and Pip hugged. “Thank you, Mio,” Pip said. “You were brave and kind.”

“You were brave too,” Mio said. “You asked for help. And we did our chores first, so the dance was safe and fun.”

The door opened a crack. A soft breeze brought in a new smell, like fresh rain. A mouse teacher peeked in. “I heard a sweet song,” she said. “Your room looks lovely. Your dance is full of heart.”

Mio’s cheeks felt warm. “We cleaned together,” he said. “Then we danced our kindness dance.”

The mouse teacher smiled. “That is real courage. You used your mind to make a plan. You used your heart to help a friend. And you used your feet to dance your story.”

Mio looked at Pip. Pip looked at Mio. They both grinned.

Mio said, “Today I learned: do chores first for safety, use kind eyes and kind words, make a plan when there is a problem, and dance can share love.”

Pip chirped, “And dancing feels extra good in a clean room!”

Mio tied his ribbon in a neat bow. “Ready to practice again?”

“Ready!” Pip said.

They began their kindness dance once more. Tap-tap. Swish. Twirl. Wiggle.

The End.
