# Ruby Rabbit's Big Helper Heart

Ruby Rabbit loved her cozy burrow home, especially the bright yellow bathroom with its sparkly mirror and soft blue towels. Every morning, she would hop in to brush her fluffy white teeth and wash her pink nose.

But today was different. As Ruby hopped toward the bathroom, she heard a tiny, sad sound.

"Sniff, sniff, sob."

Ruby's long ears perked up. She listened carefully with her whole body still. There it was again - someone was crying!

Following the sound, Ruby discovered a little field mouse sitting behind the toilet, tears rolling down his whiskers.

"Hello there," said Ruby gently, crouching down to be at the mouse's level. "I'm Ruby. What's your name?"

"I'm Max," whispered the mouse, his voice shaky. "I'm lost and scared. I was looking for water, and now I don't know how to get home."

Ruby's heart felt warm and heavy at the same time. She remembered feeling scared when she got lost in the garden once. "Oh Max, being lost feels really frightening, doesn't it?"

Max nodded, his tiny body trembling. "Everything here is so big and loud. The water makes whooshing sounds, and I can't reach anything."

Ruby looked around the bathroom with new eyes. The sink that seemed just right for her was like a tall mountain for Max. The toilet was enormous, and even the soap dispenser towered above him.

"You know what?" said Ruby, sitting down completely so she wasn't towering over Max either. "When I feel scared, it helps to have a friend nearby. Would you like me to stay with you while we figure this out?"

Max's eyes brightened just a little. "Really? You'd do that?"

"Of course!" Ruby smiled. "Everyone needs help sometimes. Now, let's think together. Where did you come from?"

Ruby waited patiently while Max thought. She noticed he was very thirsty, so she cupped her paws and brought him some water from the sink. She made sure it wasn't too much - just the right amount for a little mouse.

"Thank you," Max said, feeling much better after drinking. "I think I came from the kitchen. I remember smelling something sweet."

"Ah!" said Ruby. "My mom was baking carrot cookies this morning. That must be what you smelled. The kitchen is just down the hall."

But Max looked worried again. "It's so far, and everything looks the same to me."

Ruby thought carefully. How could she help Max feel safe on the journey home?

"I have an idea," she said. "What if I carry you on my back? That way you'll be up high enough to see where we're going, and you won't feel alone."

Max's whiskers twitched with relief. "You would really do that for me? But you don't even know me very well."

"Sometimes the best thing we can do is help someone who needs it," Ruby explained. "When we're kind to others, it makes everyone feel better - even us!"

Carefully, Ruby helped Max climb onto her soft, fluffy back. She moved slowly and gently, making sure he felt secure.

"Hold on tight, but don't worry - I'll be very careful," Ruby said.

As they traveled down the hallway, Ruby pointed out landmarks to help Max remember the way. "See that family photo? And there's the plant by the kitchen door."

Soon they reached the kitchen, where Max could smell those wonderful carrot cookies.

"There's the little hole by the cabinet!" Max exclaimed. "That's my home!"

As Max scampered down and headed toward his family, he turned back to Ruby.

"Thank you for being so kind and patient with me, Ruby. You made me feel safe when I was scared."

Ruby's heart felt full and happy. "You're very welcome, Max. Remember, whenever someone needs help, listening with our hearts and being gentle makes all the difference."

From that day on, Ruby and Max became the very best of friends, and Ruby always remembered how good it felt to help someone in need.

The End.
