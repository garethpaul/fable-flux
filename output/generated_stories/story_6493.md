# Bella Bird's Big Problem

Bella Bird loved to sing. Every morning, she would perch on the windowsill of the warm laundry room and sing beautiful songs. Her yellow feathers sparkled in the sunlight. Her little beak opened wide as sweet melodies filled the air.

"Tweet-tweet-trill!" Bella sang happily. She loved how her voice echoed off the white walls.

But today was different. Today, Bella had a big problem.

"Oh no!" Bella chirped, looking down at the laundry room floor. "My favorite shiny button is gone!"

Bella had found the pretty blue button yesterday. It was round and sparkly, just perfect for her nest. But now it had rolled somewhere, and she couldn't find it anywhere.

"Where could it be?" Bella fluttered around the room. She looked behind the washing machine. Nothing there. She checked under the dryer. Still nothing.

Bella felt her feathers getting all ruffled. Her little heart beat faster. "This is so frustrating!" she chirped loudly. "I've looked everywhere!"

She flew around in circles, getting more and more upset. The warm, soapy smell of the laundry room usually made her feel calm, but not today.

"Tweet-SQUAWK!" Bella made an angry sound she had never made before.

Just then, she heard a gentle voice. "What's wrong, little bird?"

Bella looked down and saw Mrs. Chen, the kind lady who did laundry here every Tuesday. Mrs. Chen had gray hair and gentle brown eyes.

"I lost my special button," Bella chirped sadly. "I've looked everywhere, and I can't find it!"

Mrs. Chen smiled. "When I feel frustrated, I take three deep breaths. Want to try with me?"

Bella nodded. Together, they breathed in slowly through their noses. "One... two... three," Mrs. Chen counted softly.

Bella felt her ruffled feathers smooth down. Her heart stopped racing.

"Better?" asked Mrs. Chen.

"Yes," Bella chirped more quietly. "But I still don't know where my button is."

"Sometimes when we're upset, we don't think clearly," Mrs. Chen said wisely. "Let's think together. Where did you last see your button?"

Bella closed her eyes and thought carefully. "I was playing with it near the basket of clean towels."

"Ah!" said Mrs. Chen. "Did you check inside the towels?"

Bella's eyes grew wide. She hadn't thought of that! She flew to the fluffy white towels and gently moved them with her beak.

"There it is!" Bella sang joyfully. Her blue button had rolled between two soft towels.

"You found it by staying calm and thinking smart," Mrs. Chen said proudly.

Bella picked up her button carefully. "Thank you for helping me! I learned that when I feel frustrated, I should take deep breaths and think clearly."

"That's right," Mrs. Chen nodded. "And you know what? Instead of taking that button, why don't you help me? I have some old buttons that fell off clothes. You could use those for your nest instead."

Bella's eyes sparkled. "That would be wonderful! Then I'm helping recycle too!"

Mrs. Chen gave Bella three pretty buttons that had come loose from old shirts. Bella felt so happy. She had learned to cope with frustration, and she had helped the environment too!

"Tweet-tweet-trill!" Bella sang her most beautiful song. She had turned a frustrating day into a wonderful one, just by staying calm and asking for help.

From that day on, whenever Bella felt frustrated, she remembered to breathe deeply and think clearly. And she always sang extra sweetly for her friend Mrs. Chen.

The End.
