# Poppy the Patient Panda on the Rooftop

Poppy was a patient panda who loved to help. One sunny morning, Poppy climbed the safe stairs to the rooftop garden. The rooftop felt warm under her paws. The breeze was gentle and smelled like fresh leaves and sweet flowers. Bees buzzed softly. The sky was wide and blue.

“Good morning, garden,” Poppy said. She always spoke kindly to the plants. “I am responsible for you.”

Poppy wore a little orange safety vest. It was bright like a carrot. She also wore a soft hat to shade her eyes. On the door was a big sign that said, “Rooftop Rules.” Poppy read the rules out loud.

“Rule one: Walk, don’t run. Rule two: Stay away from the edge. Rule three: Water carefully. Rule four: Ask for help if you need it.”

Poppy nodded. “I can do that,” she said. She took a deep breath and felt calm.

Poppy checked her list. “Today I water the tomatoes, feed the fish in the tiny pond, and pick up any small trash.” She tapped the list with her paw. “Being responsible means doing my jobs, even when I want to play.”

She picked up the little watering can. It was not too heavy. She filled it with water. Whoosh, the water sounded like a whispering stream. Poppy walked, not ran. She watered the tomatoes at their roots. Drip, drop, drip. The soil drank the water. The leaves looked shiny and happy.

“Thank you, Poppy,” said a little voice.

Poppy looked around. It was Mimi, a tiny bird, sitting on the fence. Her feathers were the color of dawn.

“You’re welcome, Mimi,” Poppy said. “I am taking care of the garden.”

Mimi hopped closer. “The wind is stronger today,” she chirped.

Poppy felt the wind on her whiskers. It did feel stronger. She checked the “Rooftop Rules” again. “Stay away from the edge,” she read. She saw a small red ball near the edge of the roof. It wiggled with the wind.

“Oh,” said Poppy. “That is not safe.”

Mimi fluttered. “It might fall!”

Poppy wanted to run to grab the ball. But she stopped. “Rule one: Walk, don’t run,” she said. “Rule two: Stay away from the edge.” Poppy felt her heart beat fast. She put her paw on her chest. “I will be patient. I will be careful. I will be responsible.”

Poppy looked around. She saw a long stick for picking fruit. She saw a short rope. She made a plan. “I will not go to the edge,” she said. “I will use tools. I will ask for help.”

Poppy waved to Mr. Lee, the friendly neighbor. He was checking the solar panels on the other side of the roof. “Mr. Lee,” called Poppy, “can you help me please? The ball is near the edge.”

Mr. Lee came over slowly. He smiled. “Good job asking for help, Poppy,” he said.

Poppy tied the short rope to the handle of the stick. She held one end of the rope. Mr. Lee held the other end. Poppy reached out with the stick and gently tapped the ball. Tap, tap. The ball rolled toward her, away from the edge. Poppy and Mr. Lee pulled the rope together. Scoot, scoot. The ball came back to safety.

Mimi chirped a happy note. “You did it!”

Poppy hugged the ball. It smelled like rubber and sunshine. “We did it together,” she said. “We were safe.”

She put the ball in a basket far from the edge. “Things have a home,” she said. “Balls go in the basket. Tools go on the hook. Trash goes in the bin.”

Poppy kept working. She fed the fish in the tiny pond. Plip, plop, plip. Their little mouths made bubbles. She picked up a crinkly wrapper and put it in the trash. Crinkle, crinkle. She swept the path with a soft brush. Swish, swish.

Mimi watched. “You do every job,” she said.

Poppy smiled. “Being responsible means I take care of what needs to be done. I do it safely. I do it kindly.”

When the jobs were done, Poppy sat on a soft mat. She shared sweet orange slices with Mimi and Mr. Lee. The oranges smelled bright and tasted juicy.

“Thank you for sharing,” said Mr. Lee.

Poppy nodded. “Sharing is generous. Helping is kind. Being safe is smart. Being responsible makes everyone feel good.”

Mimi tilted her head. “I learned something today,” she chirped. “When there is a problem, we can be patient, make a plan, and ask for help.”

Poppy hugged her list. “Yes,” she said. “Today’s problem was the windy ball. The solution was to follow the rules, use tools, and work together.”

The sun began to rest. The sky turned pink and gold. The breeze felt cooler and soft. Poppy locked the rooftop door and checked the sign one more time.

“Rule five,” she added with a smile, writing neatly at the bottom: “Put things back where they belong.”

Mimi clapped her wings. Mr. Lee gave a thumbs-up.

Poppy felt proud and calm. “I took care of the rooftop garden,” she said. “I kept it safe. I was responsible.”

She waved goodbye to the sky. “See you tomorrow,” she whispered.

And the garden, the wind, and the little fish seemed to whisper back, “Thank you, Poppy.”

The End.
