# Nutkin's Laundry Day Helper

Nutkin the squirrel loved exploring the big house where he lived. His fluffy gray tail twitched with excitement as he scampered down the hallway. Today, he heard a strange sound coming from the laundry room - *thump, thump, whoosh!*

"What could that be?" Nutkin wondered, his tiny paws pattering on the wooden floor.

When he peeked around the corner, Nutkin saw Mrs. Robin struggling with a huge pile of wet towels. The washing machine had stopped working, and water was everywhere!

"Oh dear, oh dear!" chirped Mrs. Robin, her feathers all ruffled. "How will I ever get these heavy towels dry? My little chicks are coming home soon, and they need their bath towels!"

Nutkin's heart felt warm and fuzzy. Mrs. Robin looked so worried! He remembered how sad he felt when he couldn't find his acorn collection last week. 

"Don't worry, Mrs. Robin!" called Nutkin, hopping into the room. "I want to help you!"

Mrs. Robin looked surprised. "That's very kind, Nutkin, but these towels are so heavy. I don't think a little squirrel can carry them."

Nutkin's whiskers twitched as he thought hard. Then his eyes sparkled with an idea! "I can't carry them alone, but maybe we can work together!"

He scurried outside and called to his friends. "Benny Bunny! Penny Possum! Come quick! Mrs. Robin needs our help!"

Soon, all the woodland friends gathered in the laundry room. The air smelled fresh and soapy, and puddles of warm water tickled their paws.

"Mrs. Robin's washing machine is broken," explained Nutkin. "Her little chicks need dry towels, but they're too heavy for her to carry outside alone."

Benny Bunny's long ears perked up. "We can make a chain! I'll carry one end!"

"And I'll take the middle!" squeaked Penny Possum.

"I'll grab the other end!" chirped Nutkin.

Working together, they carefully lifted the first heavy towel. Step by step, they carried it outside to the sunny clothesline. The warm sunshine felt good on their fur and feathers.

"Heave-ho! Heave-ho!" they chanted, making the work feel like a fun game.

Mrs. Robin watched with tears of joy in her bright eyes. "You're all so kind! I feel much better now."

One by one, they hung all the towels on the line. The colorful towels flapped gently in the breeze, making happy *flap-flap* sounds.

"Look how the sunshine will dry them perfectly!" said Nutkin, feeling proud and happy.

Just then, three little robin chicks flew home. "Mama! We're ready for our bath!" they chirped.

"Thanks to my wonderful friends, your towels will be dry and fluffy soon!" said Mrs. Robin, giving each helper a gentle hug.

Nutkin's tail felt extra fluffy with happiness. "It feels so good to help a friend," he said.

"When we work together, big problems become small!" added Benny Bunny.

"And helping others makes our hearts feel warm and happy," said Penny Possum.

Mrs. Robin smiled. "You've all learned something very special today. When we care about others and work as a team, we can solve any problem!"

As the friends watched the towels dancing in the sunshine, Nutkin knew he would always remember this special day. Helping others felt even better than finding the biggest acorn!

The End.
