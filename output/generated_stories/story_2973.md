# Roo's Attic Parade

Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a bouncy tail. One sunny afternoon, Roo hopped up the wooden stairs to the quiet attic. The attic smelled like old paper and lavender. Dust danced in the sunbeams. “Hello, attic!” Roo whispered. “Let’s have an adventure.”

In the corner sat a big trunk with stickers from all over the world. Roo tapped the lid. Tap, tap. “May I look inside?” Roo asked politely. The trunk creaked open. Inside were colorful things: a soft red scarf, a tiny drum, a straw hat, a shiny blue ribbon, a little bamboo fan, and a cloth bag of seeds.

Roo’s nose twitched. “Mmm, it smells like spices and flowers.” Roo smiled. “These must be treasures from many places.”

Roo picked up the drum. “Boom-boom,” Roo tapped softly. The sound was warm and round. “I can make music!” Roo said. Roo tied on the red scarf. It felt smooth and silky. “I can dance!” Roo spun in a little circle, tail swishing.

Then Roo heard a tiny voice. “Hello down there!” A little spider peeked from the rafters. “I’m Dot.”

“Hi, Dot!” said Roo. “Do you want to play?”

Dot dangled down on a silver thread. “What are you doing?”

“I’m making a parade,” said Roo. “A parade to celebrate friends from everywhere.”

Dot clapped tiny feet. “A cultural parade! That means we share and learn from many places.”

“Yes!” Roo said. “We can learn and be kind.”

Roo put on the straw hat. It smelled like sunshine and grass. Roo opened the bamboo fan and felt a cool breeze. “Swish, swish,” went the fan. “Ahh,” sighed Roo. “That is nice.”

Next, Roo found a little book at the bottom of the trunk. The book had pictures of children smiling. They wore different clothes and held different foods. “Look, Dot,” Roo said. “We are all different and all special.”

Dot nodded. “We can listen, learn, and share.”

Roo felt brave. “I will sing a welcome song.” Roo took a deep breath. “La-la-la, hello to you! La-la-la, we learn new too!” Roo giggled. The attic echoed with sweet sound.

Suddenly, a gust of wind from the window blew. “Whoosh!” The red scarf slid toward the edge of the attic floor. “Oh no!” Roo gasped.

Roo peeked over the edge. The scarf hung on a nail, far across a small gap. The boards creaked softly. Roo’s heart thumped. “Boom-boom,” like the drum.

Dot said, “You can do it, Roo. Be brave and careful.”

Roo looked around. “I will use the blue ribbon.” Roo tied one end to a beam. “Safety first,” Roo said. Roo held the ribbon tight, took small hops, and stretched a little paw. “I am brave. I am calm,” Roo whispered.

With one last gentle reach—got it! Roo pulled the scarf back and hugged it. “Thank you, ribbon,” Roo said. “Thank you, brave heart.”

Roo placed the scarf neatly, then looked inside the cloth bag. Tiny seeds rolled into Roo’s paw. They felt smooth and alive. “Seeds can grow,” Roo said. “We can care for our home, the Earth.”

Dot smiled. “We can plant them later by the window in a little pot.”

Roo nodded. “Yes! Caring for the Earth is a gift to everyone.”

Roo set up the attic parade. The drum sat in the middle. The fan and ribbon made a bright path. The straw hat and scarf made the parade costumes. Roo placed the book open to a page with many smiling faces.

“Dot, will you be the bell?” Roo asked.

Dot rang a tiny lost bell with her thread. “Ding-ding! The parade begins!”

Roo marched slowly. “Step, step.” Roo beat the drum. “Boom-boom.” Roo waved the fan. “Swish.” Roo wore the straw hat and red scarf and sang, “Welcome, welcome, come and see! Different ways make one big we!”

The attic felt warm and happy. Roo danced a soft dance and then stopped to speak. “Friends from many places teach us songs, clothes, foods, and stories. We listen. We share. We are kind.”

Dot added, “When we care for the Earth, we care for everyone, everywhere.”

Roo smiled. “We can draw our friends.” Roo found old crayons and paper. Roo drew a child with braids and beads. Roo drew a child with a turban and a bright smile. Roo drew a child with curly hair and a soccer ball. Roo drew the Earth with green and blue. Roo drew the seeds in a pot by the window.

The sun slid a little lower. The attic glowed golden. Roo whispered, “I feel proud. I feel brave. I feel kind.”

Dot said, “Today, you learned to celebrate cultural diversity, to be brave and careful, to make art, and to care for our world.”

Roo nodded. “Different is beautiful. Sharing is kind. Brave hearts try. Little hands help the Earth.”

Roo closed the trunk gently. “Thank you, treasures,” Roo said. Roo placed the seeds in a cup of soil near the attic window and gave them a sip of water. “Grow well, little seeds.”

Roo and Dot watched the tiny pot. The air smelled like damp soil and sunshine. Roo hummed the welcome song.

“Tomorrow,” said Roo, “we can invite more friends to our attic parade. We can listen to their songs too.”

“Yes,” said Dot. “Our world is big. Our hearts are big.”

Roo hugged the drum and smiled. “We are different. We are together.”

The End.
