# Ellie's Musical Solution

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved music more than anything. Her big ears would flap happily whenever she heard pretty sounds. Today was her first day in the musical practice room at the animal school.

The practice room was filled with wonderful instruments. Shiny drums sat in one corner, making soft *thump-thump* sounds when touched. Colorful xylophones sparkled under the bright lights, and a beautiful piano stood proudly in the center.

"Good morning, everyone!" chirped Mrs. Robin, their music teacher. "Today we'll practice for our spring concert!"

Ellie's trunk wiggled with excitement. She had been dreaming about playing music with her friends. But when she looked around, she saw a problem.

Benny the bear was holding TWO tambourines, shaking them loudly. *Jingle-jangle, jingle-jangle!*

"I need both of these!" Benny said firmly. "They sound better together!"

Meanwhile, Rosie the rabbit sat quietly in the corner with no instrument at all. Her little nose twitched sadly as she watched the other animals play.

"That's not fair!" squeaked Max the mouse. "Rosie doesn't have anything to play!"

"But I was here first!" Benny replied, clutching the tambourines tighter.

The room grew noisy with arguing voices. Some animals took Benny's side, others felt sorry for Rosie. The beautiful music stopped, replaced by upset chatter.

Ellie watched with her kind, brown eyes. Her heart felt heavy seeing her friends argue. She remembered what her mama always said: "When there's a problem, think of a solution that helps everyone."

Ellie took a deep breath and stepped forward. "Wait, friends," she said in her gentle voice. "I have an idea."

The room grew quiet. Everyone looked at the little elephant calf.

"Benny, you love making music with tambourines, right?" Ellie asked.

"Yes!" Benny nodded, his tambourines jingling softly.

"And Rosie, you want to make music too?"

"Oh yes, very much," Rosie whispered.

Ellie's trunk curled thoughtfully. "What if we take turns? Benny could play both tambourines for the first song, then give one to Rosie for the second song. That way, everyone gets to make the music they love!"

"But..." Benny started, then stopped. He looked at Rosie's hopeful face. "I guess that could work."

"And," Ellie continued, her voice growing more excited, "maybe you could teach Rosie how to play! You're really good at tambourine!"

Benny's chest puffed up with pride. "I AM pretty good, aren't I?"

"The best!" agreed Max the mouse.

Slowly, Benny walked over to Rosie and handed her one tambourine. "Here," he said with a small smile. "Shake it like this - *jingle-jangle-jingle*!"

Rosie's eyes sparkled as she copied Benny's rhythm. Soon, both friends were playing together, their tambourines creating a beautiful harmony.

Mrs. Robin clapped her wings. "What a wonderful solution, Ellie! You helped your friends work together instead of against each other."

The practice room filled with joyful music again. Ellie picked up some maracas and joined in, her big ears flapping to the beat. The *shake-shake* of maracas, the *jingle-jangle* of tambourines, and the happy laughter of friends created the most beautiful song of all.

As they played, Ellie felt warm and proud inside. She had learned something important: when friends disagree, listening to everyone and finding a fair solution makes everything better.

The End.
