# Benny Beaver's Attic Adventure

High up in the quiet attic of an old farmhouse, Benny Beaver was very busy. He had found the perfect spot to build his winter home - a cozy corner where the morning sunlight streamed through a dusty window.

"Tap, tap, tap!" went Benny's strong teeth as he chewed on wooden beams. The attic smelled like old books and sunshine. Benny loved the peaceful quiet up here, away from all the noise downstairs.

But Benny had a big problem. He needed more wood to finish his home, and he had seen some perfect pieces on the other side of the attic. The trouble was, those pieces belonged to someone else.

In the far corner lived Rosie Rabbit, who had been collecting those same wooden scraps for her own winter project. Benny watched her from across the attic, wondering what to do.

"I really need that wood," Benny whispered to himself. "But Rosie was here first. What should I do?"

For three whole days, Benny worked quietly, but his home wasn't getting finished. He felt frustrated and a little sad. The wooden beams he had weren't quite right, and his home felt wobbly.

On the fourth day, Benny took a deep breath. His mama had always told him, "When you need something, the best thing to do is ask nicely and speak up for yourself."

Benny walked across the creaky attic floor to where Rosie was sorting through her wood pile. His heart went "thump, thump" in his chest.

"Hello, Rosie," Benny said in his kindest voice. "I'm Benny Beaver, and I live over there." He pointed to his corner with his flat tail.

Rosie looked up with bright, curious eyes. "Oh! I didn't know anyone else lived up here. It's so quiet, I thought I was alone!"

"I've been watching you work," Benny continued, feeling braver. "You have some beautiful wood pieces. I was wondering... could we maybe share some? I really need help finishing my winter home."

Rosie's nose twitched thoughtfully. "What kind of help do you need?"

Benny explained about his wobbly home and showed Rosie exactly which pieces would help him. "I have some nice smooth stones I found," he offered. "Maybe we could trade?"

Rosie's eyes lit up. "Smooth stones? I love smooth stones! And you know what? I have more wood than I can use. I was worried about what to do with it all!"

The two new friends spent the whole afternoon working together. Benny shared his smooth stones, and Rosie shared her extra wood. They talked and laughed as they worked.

"This is so much more fun than working alone," said Rosie, as she helped Benny place a perfect wooden beam.

"You're right!" agreed Benny. "And my home is much stronger now. Thank you for sharing with me."

When the sun began to set, both friends had finished their winter homes. Benny's home was strong and cozy, and Rosie had built a beautiful storage area for her stones.

"I'm so glad I asked you to share," said Benny. "I was scared at first, but speaking up was the right thing to do."

"And I'm glad you did!" said Rosie. "Sharing made both our homes better, and now I have a wonderful friend too."

From that day on, Benny and Rosie worked together in their quiet attic, sharing materials and helping each other with their projects. Benny learned that when you speak up kindly and ask for help, amazing things can happen.

The End.
