# Beebo's Busy Little Plan

Beebo the busy beaver woke up with a happy wiggle. He stretched his small paws and listened. Drip, drip, drip went the rain outside his window. Tap, tap, tap went his tiny tail on his blanket. It smelled like warm wood and fresh pine in his snug room.

“Good morning, Beebo,” hummed Mama Beaver from the kitchen. “What’s our morning routine?”

Beebo grinned. “First we wash. Then we munch. Then we study. Then we build!” he said in a bright voice.

“Yes,” said Mama. “Routine helps our day feel steady and strong.”

Beebo brushed his teeth—scrub, scrub, scrub. He ate crunchy bark pancakes—crunch, crunch, crunch—with sweet berries that popped like little songs. Then he padded to his study corner.

His study corner was cozy and neat. A small lamp glowed like sunshine. A little clock ticked, ticked, ticked. On the desk sat a tiny basket of pencils, a stack of picture cards, and a soft blue cushion for Beebo’s tail.

Beebo whispered, “Hello, study corner.” He liked the quiet feel. It smelled like paper and cedar. He felt calm.

Beebo opened his picture cards. Today’s card said: Today we learn shapes for building. Beebo traced a square with his paw. “Square,” he said. He traced a triangle. “Triangle.” He traced a circle. “Circle.” His paws made gentle shhh sounds on the paper.

Mama peeked in. “You’re focused,” she smiled.

Beebo nodded. “Study time helps my brain,” he said proudly.

Tick, tick, tick went the clock. When it chimed, Beebo clapped. “Study time is done. Outdoor-activity time now!”

He put on his little orange vest. It felt snug and safe. The air outside was cool and damp. Birds sang tweet-tweet. The river hummed glug-glug. Beebo’s whiskers tingled with the breeze.

Beebo carried his small tool bag. Inside were smooth sticks, a tiny wooden ruler, and soft twine that smelled like grass. “I will build a book bench,” he said. “A spot to read and rest.”

On the riverbank, Beebo measured with his ruler. “Square for the seat. Triangle for the brace. Circle for the wheels.” He giggled. “Just kidding, no wheels today!”

He placed sticks in a tidy line. Tap, tap, tap went his tail as he thought. He tied twine with careful loops—around and over, around and over. The bench began to grow, like a puzzle clicking into place.

“Hello, Beebo!” chirped Pip the sparrow. Pip hopped close, his little feet making tickly prints in the mud. “What are you building?”

“A book bench,” said Beebo. “It’s in my plan. Study, then build.”

Pip tilted his head. “Can I help?”

Beebo smiled. “Yes, we can help each other. That is kind.” He passed Pip a short stick. “Hold this steady, please.”

Together they worked. Pip peck-peck-pecked tiny holes. Beebo looped twine through. The bench felt strong and smooth. Beebo rubbed it with his paw. “Nice and safe,” he said. “No splinters.”

Just then, Tilly Turtle slowly scooted near. “Hello, friends,” she said. “I am tired from my long walk. My shell is heavy today.”

Beebo’s heart felt warm. He patted the bench. “You may rest here, Tilly. This bench is for sharing.”

Tilly smiled and settled onto the seat. “Ahh,” she sighed. “Thank you, Beebo. Thank you, Pip.”

Beebo’s tummy made a tiny rumble. He checked his pocket. His snack apple was shiny and red. “Snack time,” he said. He took one bite—crunch! Then he looked at Pip and Tilly. He thought about generosity.

Beebo cut the apple into three little pieces. “Let’s share,” he said.

Pip chirped with joy. Tilly’s eyes sparkled. “You are a generous beaver,” she said.

After the snack, Beebo brushed crumbs away. He checked his routine chart in his tool bag. “Next is tidy time,” he read. He swept leaves from the bench with gentle swishes. He coiled the twine and counted the sticks. One, two, three, four. Everything went back in the bag.

The sun peeked through the clouds and warmed Beebo’s fur. He felt proud and peaceful. “Study, build, share, tidy,” he sang softly. “My routine helped me do my best.”

Mama Beaver walked down the path. She saw the bench and clapped. “Beebo, you followed your routine. You studied shapes in your study corner. You did outdoor activity. You built something useful. And you shared.”

Beebo’s cheeks felt glowy. “I did it step by step,” he said.

Pip flapped his wings. Tilly tapped her shell. “We did it together,” they added.

Mama sat on the bench. “This is sturdy and kind,” she said. “May we read a story here this evening?”

“Yes!” said Beebo. “After dinner and brush time.” He giggled. “That is our bedtime routine.”

As the river sang and the birds chirped, Beebo packed his bag and gave the bench one last pat. Smooth, safe, and ready. He waved to his friends. “See you later!”

On the way home, Beebo felt grateful. His steps were light. His heart felt big.

At the door, he looked back at the bright day. “Routine helps me learn,” he whispered. “Routine helps me build. Routine helps me share.”

The lesson felt soft and strong inside him, like the twine around his sticks.

The End.
