# Fair Turns in the Living Room

In a sunny, playful living room, a gentle deer fawn named Fern visited her friends for an afternoon playdate. The room felt warm and bright. Sunbeams made little squares on the soft rug. Pillows were piled high like colorful clouds. Blocks clinked and clacked in a big basket. The air smelled like cinnamon cookies cooling on a plate.

Fern’s friends were ready to play. Buzz the bouncy bunny thumped his feet. Mia the tiny mouse squeaked with excitement. Sam the careful squirrel held a toy drum. “Let’s build a tall tower!” said Fern, her brown eyes shining.

They all agreed. “Tower time!” Buzz cheered. “We can make it reach the couch,” Mia said, wriggling her whiskers.

Fern placed the first block. Tap. Tap. Mia added a blue block. Clink. Sam set down a red block very slowly. Buzz hopped and tried to stack three blocks at once—tumble! The blocks fell and rolled across the rug.

Fern took a slow breath in. She remembered what her mama always said: “Slow breaths help your body feel calm.” She breathed in through her nose and out like a soft puff of wind through leaves. “Let’s try again,” she said kindly. “One at a time.”

They began again. The tower grew. Tap-clink, tap-clink. It reached Fern’s knee, then her belly. Fern felt proud and happy. But just then, Buzz spotted a shiny race car on the shelf. “I want the car now!” he cried, rushing over. “Me too!” said Mia, scurrying after him. Sam reached out a paw as well. All three friends grabbed the same toy at once.

The car squeaked, and so did the friends. “Mine!” Buzz said. “No, mine!” Mia said. “But I didn’t get a turn last time,” Sam said, his tail twitching.

Fern’s heart thumped fast. Her ears felt hot. She really wanted to keep building. She also wanted her friends to feel happy and safe. She took another slow breath and listened. She heard the tick-tick of the clock, the soft hum of the fridge, and the tiny scratch of their paws on the rug.

“This doesn’t feel fair,” Fern said gently. “We are pulling and shouting. That can hurt feelings.”

Buzz’s ears drooped. “I just really, really want the car.” Mia hugged her little paws to her chest. “I want to go fast too.” Sam looked down. “I want a turn like everyone else.”

Fern blinked and thought. Justice means being fair to everyone, even when it’s hard. Justice means we all get a turn. “Friends,” Fern said, “let’s make a fair plan.”

“What kind of plan?” asked Mia.

“A turn plan,” Fern said. “We can set a timer. Everyone gets the same time. We can cheer for each other and wait with calm bodies.”

Buzz bounced once, then stopped himself. He put his paws on his belly and breathed like Fern. “I can try,” he said. “Calm bodies.”

Sam nodded. “I like a plan.”

Fern found a little sand timer on the shelf. She shook it and watched the sparkly sand fall. “When the sand is gone, the turn is done,” she explained. “Buzz first, then Mia, then Sam. I will go last. That is fair because I chose the order.”

“Thank you,” said Sam softly. Mia clapped her tiny paws. “Okay!”

Buzz went first. He zoomed the car around the pillow mountain. “Vrrrrooom!” He made careful circles and stopped when the last bit of sand fell. He handed the car to Mia. “Your turn.”

Mia’s whiskers twitched with joy. She zig-zagged the car across the rug. “Zee-zee-zoom!” When the sand ran out, she smiled and gave the car to Sam. “Your turn, Sam.”

Sam rolled the car in slow, straight lines. “Roll, roll,” he whispered, watching the wheels spin. When his time ended, he placed the car in Fern’s hooves. “Now you.”

Fern felt warm inside. She drove the car up the block tower, pretending it was a hill. “Beep-beep!” She stopped when the sand was gone and put the car on the shelf again.

“Now no one is left out,” Fern said. “That is justice. It means fair turns and kind choices.”

Buzz’s ears stood tall. “I liked cheering for my friends,” he said. “It helped me wait.” Mia nodded. “And breathing made my body feel soft again.” Sam smiled. “I felt safe when we had a plan.”

They returned to the tower together. Tap-clink, tap-clink. The tower got taller than the couch! They all stepped back. “We did it as a team,” Fern said. “Because we were fair and patient.”

Just then, the cookies were ready. Sweet cinnamon filled the air. Fern looked at the plate. “Let’s use our plan again,” she said. “One cookie each, then we see if any are left.” Everyone agreed. They each took one cookie and said, “Thank you.” There were two cookies left, so they split them into tiny halves so all four friends could have more.

Fern licked a crumb from her nose and giggled. “Justice feels good,” she said. “It looks like fair turns, calm bodies, and kind hearts. It includes everyone.”

Her friends nodded, cheeks full of cookie. The sunbeam on the rug felt extra warm. The tower stood tall. The living room felt happy and safe.

The End.
