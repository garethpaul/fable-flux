# Koala Kori’s Brave Craft Plan

In a comfortable nursery with soft rugs and sunny windows, a calm koala named Kori sat at a little table. The air smelled like crayons and fresh paper. The window was open, and Kori could hear birds chirping, “cheep-cheep,” and feel a gentle breeze tickle his fuzzy ears.

On the table was a big sheet of paper, glue, and a basket of colorful leaves. Kori wanted to make a leaf kite. He dreamed of running outside and watching his kite dance in the sky like the birds.

Kori patted the paper. “I can do this,” he whispered.

He picked a shiny green leaf and put glue on it. “Squish, squish,” went the glue. He pressed the leaf down. It stuck! Kori smiled. He added a red leaf, then a yellow leaf. The colors looked bright and happy.

But when Kori tried to lift the paper, the leaves slid. The kite shape flopped. “Oh no,” said Kori. “It’s not working.”

His tummy felt tight. His cheeks felt warm. He wanted to crumple the paper. His paws squeezed the edge. He felt a big feeling. It was frustration.

Kori took a slow breath. “In… and out…” he whispered. He put his paw on his chest and felt his heartbeat slow down. He remembered what his teacher said: “When you feel stuck, stop, breathe, and make a plan.”

Kori nodded. “I will try a plan.”

He looked around the cozy nursery. The light made the glue sparkle. He could smell orange peels in a snack bowl. He could hear a soft “tick-tock” from the wall clock.

Kori talked to himself in a calm voice. “Step one: breathe. Step two: ask for help. Step three: try again.”

“Miss Bree?” Kori called. His teacher walked over. She had gentle eyes and a sweater with tiny clouds. “Yes, Kori?” she said.

“My kite is floppy,” said Kori. “I feel frustrated. Can you help me make a plan?”

Miss Bree smiled. “That is very brave, Kori. You used your words. Let’s think. What could make the kite strong?”

Kori looked at the basket. He saw sticks by the plants. He saw tape in a shiny dispenser. “Maybe sticks,” he said. “Maybe tape.”

“Good strategy,” said Miss Bree. “Let’s try a frame.”

They gathered two thin sticks that smelled like rain and earth. Kori held them in a cross and Miss Bree helped tape them tight. “Sticky-zip!” went the tape.

Kori took another breath. “In… and out…” He spread the paper over the sticks and tapped glue around the edges. “Pat, pat, pat,” went his soft paw.

He placed the leaves again. Green. Red. Yellow. He pressed down and counted, “One… two… three…” The glue held. The kite looked strong and colorful.

Kori tied a string to the bottom. “Swish,” it whispered as it brushed his arm. He smiled, but then he looked at the door. Outside, the yard was big and bright. The sky was wide. A tiny flutter of worry tickled his belly.

“I feel a little scared to try it outside,” Kori said softly.

“That’s okay,” Miss Bree said. “Being brave means feeling scared and trying anyway, with a plan.”

Kori nodded. “Plan: hold tight. Walk slowly. Watch the wind. Ask a friend.”

His friend Mia, a little mouse with pink ears, scampered over. “I can help!” she squeaked. “I can hold the string, too.”

They stepped outside together. The warm sun kissed Kori’s nose. The grass felt cool and tickly under his feet. A breeze whooshed by, smelling like flowers.

“Ready?” Mia asked.

Kori felt his tummy flutter. He put his paw on his chest. “In… and out…” he breathed. “I am brave. I have a plan.”

“Ready,” he said.

They lifted the kite. The wind caught it. “Whoooosh!” Up, up it went. The string hummed in Kori’s paw. The leaves glittered in the sun, like tiny stars. The kite danced and dipped. Kori laughed. “We did it!”

Just then, the kite bobbed too hard and swooped down. Kori felt a jolt of frustration again. “Oh!” he cried.

He stopped. He breathed. “In… and out…” He remembered his steps. “Step one: breathe. Step two: make a plan.”

“Let’s move to the open spot,” he said. “Less trees. More space.”

Mia nodded. “Good strategy!”

They walked to a wide, clear patch of grass. Kori held the string low, then lifted it high as the wind puffed. “Now!” he said. The kite rose smooth and steady. It floated like a colorful leaf-bird in the blue sky.

Kori’s calm came back. His worry melted like ice on a sunny day. He felt brave and proud. He felt strong and steady.

Back in the nursery, Kori wrote a tiny note and taped it to the wall: “Stop. Breathe. Make a plan. Try again.”

Miss Bree smiled. “What did you learn today, Kori?”

Kori hugged his soft kite and said, “When I feel frustrated, I can stop, breathe, make a plan, and try again. That is brave.”

Mia clapped her tiny paws. “And crafting with friends is fun!”

Kori looked at the sunny window. He heard the birds sing. He touched the smooth string and smelled the sweet glue. He felt calm.

“Tomorrow,” he said, “we can make a leaf boat.”

“And we will have a plan,” Mia giggled.

Kori nodded. “In… and out…”

The End.
