# Leo the Lion Cub Finds His Voice

Leo was a small, fluffy lion cub with golden fur that sparkled in the morning sunshine. He loved sitting on his family's peaceful front porch, watching the colorful butterflies dance around the flower boxes and listening to the gentle chirping of birds.

Every morning, Leo's big sister Luna would bounce onto the porch with her notebook and crayons. "Move over, little cub!" she would say loudly, taking up the whole cozy reading corner where Leo liked to sit.

Leo would quietly scoot to the hard wooden steps, feeling sad in his tummy. The steps weren't comfortable, and he couldn't see the pretty garden as well from there. But Leo didn't know how to tell Luna how he felt.

One sunny Tuesday morning, Leo watched Luna spread out all her art supplies in his favorite spot again. His little heart felt heavy, like a rock was sitting in his chest. He looked at the uncomfortable steps and sighed a big, whooshy sigh.

"Why do you look so droopy, Leo?" asked Mama Lion, settling into her rocking chair with a warm cup of tea that smelled like honey.

Leo's ears drooped down. "Luna always takes my favorite reading spot," he whispered. "But I don't want to be mean."

Mama Lion's eyes twinkled kindly. "Oh, sweet cub, using your voice isn't mean when you speak with kindness. Let me teach you about being assertive."

"What's 'sertive?" Leo asked, tilting his fuzzy head.

"Assertive means speaking up for yourself in a nice, clear way," Mama explained. "It's like being brave with your words. You can tell someone how you feel without being loud or unkind."

Mama Lion helped Leo think of the right words. They practiced together, with Leo's voice getting stronger and clearer each time.

When Luna came bouncing out with her supplies, Leo took a deep breath that filled up his whole little chest. His paws felt tingly, but he remembered Mama's words about being brave.

"Luna," Leo said in his clear, kind voice, "I feel sad when I can't sit in the reading corner. It's my favorite spot too. Could we please take turns?"

Luna stopped and looked at her little brother with surprise. "Oh! I didn't know you wanted to sit there too, Leo. I was just excited about my drawing."

Leo's tail wiggled with hope. "Maybe we could share? I could sit there in the mornings, and you could have it in the afternoons?"

"That sounds fair!" Luna smiled, her whiskers twitching happily. "I'm sorry I didn't ask before."

Leo felt warm and proud inside, like sunshine was glowing in his heart. He had used his voice, and it worked! He curled up in the soft reading corner, feeling the gentle morning breeze ruffle his fur.

Later, Leo got his own special notebook and drew a picture of himself speaking up. He drew himself standing tall and proud, with a big smile and words floating in a bubble above his head that said "Please" and "Thank you."

"I'm proud of you for using your assertive voice," Mama Lion purred, nuzzling Leo's head. "Speaking up for yourself with kindness takes real courage."

From that day on, Leo knew he could always use his voice when something didn't feel right. He learned that being assertive meant being both brave and kind, and that his feelings mattered too.

The End.
