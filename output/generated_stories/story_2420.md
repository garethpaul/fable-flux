# Gentle Necks and Kind Hearts

The morning light made soft stripes on the dining room floor. The table smelled like warm toast and sweet jam. A thoughtful giraffe named Gigi leaned her long neck low. She loved to help. She loved to think. Today was chore day.

Clink, clink went the plates. Swish, swish went a little sponge in a bowl of bubbly water. Gigi took a deep breath. The bubbles smelled like lemons. “Good morning, dishes,” she whispered. “We will make you shiny.”

Little Max sat at the table with a cup of milk. He kicked his feet and frowned. “I don’t like chores,” he said. “They are too hard. They are not fun.”

Gigi blinked her kind eyes. “Hmm,” she said softly. “Your face looks rainy, like a little cloud. Are you feeling upset, Max?”

Max shrugged. “I wanted to play trains.”

Gigi nodded. “I understand. Play time is important. Feelings are important too.” She moved a napkin with the tip of her soft nose. The napkin felt crisp and dry. “Let’s talk to our feelings. Hello, Upset. I see you.”

Max giggled a little. “You are talking to feelings?”

“Yes,” said Gigi. “Feelings like to be seen. When we see them, they calm down. Like this.” She breathed in. She breathed out. “Whoosh,” she said.

Max breathed with her. “Whoosh,” he said. His shoulders felt less tight.

Mama came in with a warm plate of toast. “Thank you for helping set the table, Gigi,” she said.

Gigi smiled. “We can all help. Our dining room is our team room.” She looked at Max. “Do you want to be on the team?”

Max looked at the shiny forks. “Maybe. But I am small.”

Gigi bent her long neck so her eyes were level with Max. “I am tall. You are small. We can both help in our own way. That is teamwork.”

A little clatter sounded from the chair. The chair wobbled. A stack of napkins slid to the floor. Fluff, fluff, flutter. Max’s eyes went wide. “Oh no!”

Gigi heard the soft flaps and saw Max’s face turn pink. “It’s okay,” she said gently. “Napkins like surprises.”

Max blinked. “Do they?”

“They do when we treat them kindly,” Gigi said with a tiny smile. She lowered her head. “Let’s listen to how the napkins feel.”

Max knelt. He touched a napkin. It felt soft like a kitty’s ear. “Maybe they feel scared,” he said. “They fell.”

Gigi nodded slowly. “If I fell, I might feel scared too. What could we do to help them feel safe?”

“Pick them up,” Max said. “And pat them.”

“Good idea,” said Gigi. “That is empathy. Empathy means we imagine how someone else feels, and then we help.”

Together they picked up the napkins. Pat, pat, pat. Max placed them in a neat little square pile. He smiled. “They look cozy now.”

“They do,” said Gigi. “You helped them feel better.”

Mama set down the jam. “Thank you, Max. Thank you, Gigi. The table looks lovely.”

A tiny clink came from the spoon. The spoon was on the edge of a bowl. It looked like it might slip. Gigi blinked again. “I think the spoon feels wobbly.”

Max leaned close. The spoon made a small t-t-t sound. “It is wobbly,” he said. “If I were a spoon, I would want a safe spot.”

“What is a safe spot for a spoon?” asked Gigi.

Max thought. He touched the smooth, cool spoon. “On the napkin!” he said. He moved it gently. The spoon settled with a quiet tap. “There.”

“Kind and careful,” Gigi said. “That is empathy, too.”

After breakfast, it was time for cleaning. Mama wiped the table. Swish, swish. Gigi carried plates to the sink. Clink, clink. Max held a small basket for crumbs. “I am the crumb catcher,” he said proudly.

He crouched and looked under the table. He saw a lonely grape. It was purple and a little wrinkly. “Hello, grape,” Max said. “Are you lost?”

Gigi lowered her head and peered under the table with him. “If I were a little grape, I might feel left out.”

Max nodded. “I feel left out sometimes, too.” He picked up the grape and put it in the compost bowl. “Now you can go back to the garden.”

Gigi’s heart felt warm. “You used your feelings to help the grape,” she said. “That is big empathy.”

Max stood tall. “Empathy is like a gentle neck,” he said, touching Gigi’s long neck and giggling. “It reaches to help.”

Gigi laughed softly. “Yes. A gentle neck and a kind heart.”

They finished the chores together. The dining room smelled clean and lemony. The table felt smooth and dry. The forks shone like tiny moons. Max clapped. “We did it! Chores can feel good.”

Mama gave them both a hug. “When we help, we care for our home and for each other,” she said.

Gigi looked at Max. “What did we learn today?”

Max grinned. “Empathy means I think about how others feel. Then I help. Chores are easier when we do them together. And breathing helps when I feel upset.”

Gigi nodded. “That is a very big lesson for a very big heart.”

They all sat for a story at the shiny table. The room was calm. The feelings were calm, too.

The End.
