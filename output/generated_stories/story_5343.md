# Koko's Sharing Garden

The morning sun warmed the peaceful front porch where Koko the koala sat quietly. She rocked gently in her favorite wicker chair, watching the colorful butterflies dance around her potted plants.

"What a beautiful day," Koko said softly, her fuzzy ears twitching in the gentle breeze.

Koko loved her front porch garden. She had bright red tomatoes, sweet yellow corn, and leafy green lettuce growing in big clay pots. The plants smelled fresh and earthy, and tiny dewdrops sparkled like diamonds on the leaves.

*Rumble, rumble.* Koko's tummy made a hungry sound. "Time for breakfast!" she said cheerfully.

Just as Koko reached for a ripe, red tomato, she heard soft crying. *Sniff, sniff.* The sound came from behind the big sunflower pot.

"Hello?" Koko called gently. "Is someone there?"

A tiny field mouse peeked out, tears rolling down his whiskers. "I'm sorry," squeaked the mouse. "I'm just so hungry, and I can't find any food."

Koko's kind heart felt warm and fuzzy. "Oh my! You poor little thing. What's your name?"

"I'm Pip," said the mouse shyly. "I've been looking everywhere for food to take back to my family."

Koko smiled her biggest, warmest smile. "Well, Pip, you've come to the right place! Look at all these wonderful vegetables growing on my porch."

Pip's tiny eyes grew wide. "They look delicious! But... but they're yours."

"The best things in life are meant to be shared," said Koko wisely. She carefully picked the biggest, juiciest tomato. "Here, take this home to your family."

Pip's whiskers wiggled with joy. "Really? You'd share with me?"

"Of course!" Koko laughed, her voice like tinkling wind chimes. "But I have an even better idea. Would you like to help me tend the garden? We could grow enough food for both our families!"

Pip clapped his tiny paws together. "I'd love to help! I know all about digging and planting seeds."

From that day forward, Koko and Pip worked together every morning. Pip's small paws were perfect for planting tiny seeds, while Koko's gentle touch helped the plants grow tall and strong. The sweet smell of fresh soil and growing plants filled the peaceful porch.

"Look how much we've grown!" said Koko proudly, admiring their expanded garden. Colorful vegetables now filled every corner of the porch.

"And look who's coming to visit!" squeaked Pip excitedly.

Three more hungry animals approached the porch - a baby rabbit, a young raccoon, and a little bird with a hurt wing.

"Welcome, friends!" called Koko warmly. "There's plenty of food for everyone, and plenty of work to share too!"

Soon, all the animals were working together, digging, planting, and watering. The porch buzzed with happy chatter and the *pat-pat-pat* of tiny paws in the soil.

"Sharing makes everything better," said Koko, watching her new friends munch on fresh vegetables. "When we work together and share what we have, there's always enough for everyone."

Pip nodded, his cheeks full of sweet corn. "And helping others feels even better than helping yourself!"

As the sun set over their beautiful shared garden, all the animals gathered on the peaceful porch. They had full bellies, new friends, and hearts overflowing with joy.

"Tomorrow we'll plant even more," said Koko contentedly. "Because the more we share, the more we grow - not just our garden, but our happiness too."

The End.
