# Nutkin's Story Time Surprise

Nutkin the squirrel lived in a cozy tree house with a special room called the home office. This room had a big wooden desk, colorful books on tall shelves, and a soft blue chair where Nutkin liked to sit and think.

One sunny morning, Nutkin's fluffy tail twitched with excitement. "Today is story time with my forest friends!" he chirped, jumping up and down. All the little animals in the forest were coming to hear Nutkin tell his favorite tale about the brave acorn who saved the day.

But when Nutkin looked around his office, he felt his whiskers droop. "Oh no!" he squeaked. His desk was messy with scattered papers. His story book was nowhere to be found. Even his special storytelling hat was missing under a pile of leaves he'd brought inside yesterday.

"Chitter-chatter, what a mess!" Nutkin said, his bushy tail swishing nervously. He could hear little voices outside getting closer. "We're here for story time!" called Benny Bunny. "Is it ready, Nutkin?"

Nutkin's cheeks turned pink. He opened the window and called down, "Um... could you wait just a tiny bit? I need a few more minutes!"

The resourceful squirrel knew exactly what to do. First, he gathered all the scattered papers into a neat pile. The papers made a satisfying *swoosh* sound as he stacked them. Next, he looked high and low for his story book, finally finding it tucked behind his favorite acorn jar. The book felt smooth and familiar in his small paws.

"Where's my storytelling hat?" Nutkin wondered aloud. He moved the pile of crunchy leaves and found his bright red hat underneath. When he put it on, it felt just right on his fuzzy head.

Now Nutkin had everything ready, but he realized something important. He sat in his blue chair and opened the book, practicing the story quietly. "Once upon a time, there was a brave little acorn..." he whispered, making sure he remembered all the exciting parts.

When his friends knocked on the door, Nutkin felt prepared and confident. He opened the door wide with a big smile.

"Hello everyone!" Nutkin said warmly. "Before we start, I want to say I'm sorry for making you wait. I wasn't ready when I should have been."

"That's okay, Nutkin!" said Luna the lamb, her woolly coat soft and white. "We don't mind waiting for a good story!"

Nutkin led his friends to the cozy office. They sat in a circle on the soft carpet while Nutkin sat in his blue chair, wearing his special red hat. The room smelled like pine trees and fresh air from the open window.

"Are you ready for the story of the brave acorn?" Nutkin asked, holding up his book.

"Yes!" all the animals cheered, their eyes bright and eager.

Nutkin told the most wonderful story. His voice rose and fell with excitement, and he made funny voices for each character. Because he had practiced and prepared everything just right, the story was magical. His friends listened carefully, gasping at the exciting parts and giggling at the funny moments.

When the story ended, everyone clapped their paws, hooves, and wings. "That was the best story ever!" said Benny Bunny.

Nutkin felt proud and happy. He had learned that being prepared made everything better - and more fun too!

The End.
