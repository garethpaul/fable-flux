# Benny's Rooftop Art Show

Benny the bear cub loved to paint. His soft brown paws held brushes just right. He mixed colors on his palette - red like strawberries, blue like the sky, yellow like sunshine.

Every morning, Benny climbed up to the rooftop of his apartment building. The wind felt cool on his fuzzy ears. He could see the whole city spread out below. Cars looked like tiny toys. People walked like busy ants.

"This is the perfect place to paint," Benny said, setting up his easel. The rooftop was quiet and peaceful. Sunlight made everything sparkle.

But today was different. When Benny arrived, three older cubs were already there. They had claimed the best spot - the corner with the prettiest view.

"You can't paint here," said Max, a big gray cub. "We were here first."

"Find somewhere else," added Luna, flicking her black ears. "This spot is ours."

Benny's heart felt heavy. His favorite painting corner was taken. The other spots didn't have the same beautiful view. How could he paint the city if he couldn't see it properly?

For a moment, Benny wanted to leave. His paws felt shaky. But then he remembered what Mama Bear always said: "Use your voice, little one. Tell others what you need."

Benny took a deep breath. "Excuse me," he said, walking closer to the older cubs. "I come here every morning to paint. Could we maybe share this space?"

Max laughed. "Share? There's no room for a little cub like you."

But Benny didn't give up. "What if we worked together?" he asked, his voice getting stronger. "I have lots of paint colors. You could use them too. We could all paint different parts of the city."

Luna tilted her head. "You'd share your paints?"

"Yes!" Benny's eyes sparkled. "I have more colors than I can use. And look - the rooftop is big enough for all of us if we arrange our easels in a circle."

The third cub, a small brown bear named Pip, perked up. "I've never tried painting before. Could you teach me, Benny?"

Benny's tail wagged with excitement. "Of course! Painting is more fun when you share it with friends."

Slowly, the older cubs began to listen. Max looked at Benny's bright paints. Luna noticed how the little cub's eyes shone with kindness.

"Okay," Max said finally. "Let's try working together."

Soon, all four cubs were painting side by side. Benny showed Pip how to hold a brush. Luna painted the tall buildings. Max created fluffy white clouds. Benny worked on a rainbow stretching across the city.

"This is amazing!" Pip giggled, getting purple paint on his nose. "I love how the colors blend together."

The wind carried the sweet smell of their paints. Birds chirped approval from nearby trees. The sun warmed their fur as they created beautiful art together.

"You know what?" Luna said, stepping back to admire their work. "This is much better than painting alone."

"Benny, thank you for speaking up," Max added. "Your idea to work together was really smart."

Benny beamed with pride. He had used his voice to solve the problem. Instead of giving up, he had asked for what he needed. And by being willing to share, he had made new friends.

As the morning sun climbed higher, the four cubs put their paintings side by side. Together, they had created the most beautiful rooftop art show the city had ever seen.

"Tomorrow, let's paint together again," Benny suggested.

"Yes!" all the cubs agreed, their voices happy and excited.

Benny learned that speaking up for himself could lead to wonderful things. When you use your voice with kindness and creativity, amazing adventures can happen.

The End.
