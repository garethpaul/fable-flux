# Rosie Rabbit's Honest Heart

Rosie Rabbit lived in the coziest bedroom you could imagine. Her soft blue blanket smelled like lavender, and her favorite carrot-shaped pillow was always perfectly fluffed. Sunlight streamed through the polka-dot curtains, making everything feel warm and happy.

One morning, Rosie woke up to find something wonderful on her nightstand - a shiny red marble that sparkled like a tiny rainbow. "Oh my!" she gasped, her whiskers twitching with excitement. "Where did this beautiful marble come from?"

The marble felt smooth and cool in her small paws. It was the prettiest thing she had ever seen! Rosie's heart began to beat faster as she thought about keeping it forever.

"Maybe... maybe someone dropped it here by accident," she whispered to herself. "But it's so beautiful, and I found it in my room!"

Rosie held the marble up to the sunlight, watching the colors dance across her bedroom walls. Red, blue, green - it was like holding a tiny piece of the rainbow! She wanted to keep it so very much.

But then, something didn't feel quite right in her tummy. It felt twisty and uncomfortable, like when she ate too many carrots too quickly.

"Hmm," Rosie said, sitting down on her soft rug. She took three deep breaths, just like Mama Rabbit had taught her when she felt worried. "In... and out... in... and out..."

As she breathed calmly, Rosie remembered what Mama always said: "A rabbit with an honest heart feels happy inside and out."

"But how can I find out who this marble belongs to?" Rosie wondered aloud. She looked around her cozy room, thinking hard. Her ears perked up as she got an idea!

Rosie hopped to her bedroom door and listened carefully. She could hear her little brother Benny crying in the next room.

"Benny sounds very sad," she said to herself. "I wonder why?"

Being very resourceful, Rosie decided to investigate. She hopped quietly down the hallway, still holding the beautiful marble. Through Benny's open door, she could see him looking under his bed and behind his toy box.

"Benny, what's wrong?" Rosie asked gently.

Benny's eyes were full of tears. "I lost my special marble! Grandpa Rabbit gave it to me yesterday, and now it's gone! I looked everywhere!" He sniffled loudly.

Rosie's heart gave a little jump. She looked down at the red marble in her paws, then back at her sad little brother. The twisty feeling in her tummy got stronger.

Taking another deep breath, Rosie made her choice. "Benny," she said softly, "is this your marble?" She held out the sparkling red treasure.

Benny's face lit up like sunshine! "Yes! That's my marble! Oh, Rosie, where did you find it?"

"It was on my nightstand this morning," Rosie explained. "I really wanted to keep it because it's so pretty, but I knew in my heart that wouldn't be right."

Benny hugged his sister tight. "Thank you for being honest, Rosie! You're the best big sister ever!"

As Rosie gave Benny his marble back, the twisty feeling in her tummy disappeared completely. Instead, she felt warm and happy all over - much better than keeping the marble would have made her feel.

"Being honest feels much better than being sneaky," Rosie said to herself, smiling.

That night, as Rosie snuggled under her lavender-scented blanket, she felt proud of her choice. Her heart was as light as a feather, and she slept peacefully, knowing she had done the right thing.

The End.
