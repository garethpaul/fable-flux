# Ollie's Garden Helper Adventure

In a sunny backyard with a sparkling blue pool lived a playful otter named Ollie. Ollie had silky brown fur and tiny webbed paws. He loved to splash and slide all day long.

But Ollie had a big problem. He did not like to follow directions. When Grandma asked him to help in the garden, Ollie would run away. When she said "Please water the flowers gently," Ollie would splash water everywhere instead.

"Following directions is boring," Ollie said, shaking his wet fur. "I want to play my own way!"

Grandma was patient but worried. "When we follow directions, we help things grow beautifully," she said kindly. "Will you try to listen today?"

One sunny morning, Grandma's friend Mrs. Chen came to visit. She brought special seeds from her home in China. The seeds smelled like sweet jasmine and looked like tiny pearls.

"These are very special moon flowers," Mrs. Chen explained. "They only bloom at night and smell like heaven. But they need very careful planting."

Ollie's eyes grew wide. Night-blooming flowers sounded magical! "Can I help?" he asked, bouncing on his paws.

"Yes," said Mrs. Chen with a warm smile. "But you must listen carefully to every step."

Mrs. Chen began to explain: "First, dig holes exactly two inches deep. Then, place one seed in each hole. Cover gently with soft soil. Finally, sprinkle just three drops of water on each spot."

But Ollie was too excited to listen. He grabbed the seeds and started digging big holes everywhere. Dirt flew through the air! He dumped all the seeds in one giant hole and splashed water all around.

"Oh no!" cried Mrs. Chen. "The seeds won't grow if they're planted wrong!"

Ollie looked at the messy garden. Mud covered the pretty flowers. The special seeds were all mixed up in one muddy puddle. Mrs. Chen looked very sad.

"I'm sorry," Ollie whispered, his ears drooping. "I didn't listen to your directions. I ruined your special seeds."

Grandma put her gentle paw on Ollie's shoulder. "Mistakes help us learn," she said. "What can we do now?"

Ollie thought hard. "Can we try again? I promise to follow every direction this time."

Mrs. Chen's face brightened. "I have more seeds! But this time, we must work as a team."

This time, Ollie listened carefully. He dug holes exactly two inches deep, counting "One... two!" He placed one seed in each hole, very gently. He covered them with soft soil and sprinkled exactly three drops of water.

"Perfect!" said Mrs. Chen, clapping her hands. "You followed every direction beautifully!"

That evening, Ollie, Grandma, and Mrs. Chen sat by the garden. As the moon rose high, tiny white flowers began to bloom. They glowed like little stars and smelled like sweet dreams.

"Wow!" gasped Ollie. "They're the most beautiful flowers ever!"

Mrs. Chen smiled. "When we follow directions and work together, wonderful things happen. These flowers teach us that patience and listening create magic."

From that day on, Ollie always listened carefully to directions. He helped Grandma plant tomatoes, water herbs, and tend the vegetables. The garden grew more beautiful each day.

And every night, Ollie would sit by Mrs. Chen's moon flowers, remembering that following directions helped create something truly special.

The End.
