# Piper Parrot’s Treehouse Band

High in a leafy tree, there was a snug, wooden treehouse that smelled like pine and sunshine. Inside lived Piper, a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a rainbow tail. Piper loved music. She loved the soft tap of raindrops, the whoosh of wind, and the plink-plonk of tiny seed shells falling on the floor. “Music is everywhere!” Piper sang.

One warm morning, Piper fluffed her feathers and said, “I want to make a treehouse band!” She fluttered to the window and called, “Friends! Come play music with me!”

Soon, Coco the squirrel scampered up with a nut drum. Tippy the turtle slowly climbed with a tiny triangle that went ting-ting. Buzz the bee buzzed in with a gentle hum. Mimi the mouse carried two little sticks for clapping. Everyone squeezed into the cozy treehouse. It felt warm and safe, and it smelled like fresh leaves and nut cookies.

Piper flapped. “Let’s play a song together!” she chirped. “I will lead. We will listen. We will follow directions.”

Coco bounced. “I want to play fast!”

Tippy blinked. “I like slow.”

Buzz zipped. “Bzzzz-zz-zz! Loud!”

Mimi squeaked. “I like quiet.”

Piper tilted her head. “We are different,” she said kindly. “That is okay. But we need a plan.”

They all nodded. Piper was the leader, but everyone had ideas. Piper pulled out a little chart she made with colorful leaves. “First,” she said, pointing with a feather, “we practice the beat. Then we add our sounds. Last, we play together.”

Coco’s tail twitched. “I can do the beat!” He thumped his nut drum. Boom-boom-boom! It was very fast. The treehouse shook. Buzz buzzed louder. Mimi clapped too quickly. Tippy’s triangle tinged off the beat. It sounded messy, like shoes in a dryer.

Piper’s ears twitched. Her tummy felt twisty. She took a deep breath. “Pause, please,” she said in a calm voice. “Let’s try again. Follow my wings.”

Piper lifted one wing up and down, steady as a clock. Up, down. Up, down. “Boom… boom… boom… boom,” she counted. “Coco, play soft and slow with my wing. Friends, wait and listen.”

Coco watched. Boom… boom… boom. The sound was softer, like a gentle heartbeat. The treehouse felt steady. “Good,” Piper smiled. “Now, Buzz, hum on boom-number-two. Only number two.”

Buzz hovered and listened. “Bzz… BZZ… bzz… BZZ…” On the second boom, he hummed a little louder. It tickled the air like a fuzzy blanket.

“Great listening,” Piper said. “Mimi, clap on boom-number-four.” Mimi waited, ears twitching. “Clap!” on four. Her clap was crisp, like a tiny snap.

“Tippy,” Piper said kindly, “ting on boom-number-one and hold it long.” Tippy raised his flipper. Ting—! The sound was bright and shiny, like a star.

They tried together. Boom (ting)— bzz— boom— clap! The sounds lined up. Piper’s heart felt warm. “We did it! We are following directions,” she chirped. “Let’s try our song.”

Just then, Coco’s eyes sparkled. “Can we play faster now?” he asked.

Mimi squeaked, “I still like quiet.”

Buzz buzzed, “I still like loud!”

Tippy blinked slowly. “Slow is nice.”

Piper thought. She wanted everyone to feel happy. “We can compromise,” she said. “That means we take a little of what each friend wants. We share the plan.”

Piper drew a new leaf chart. “Part one: slow and quiet. Part two: a little faster and louder. Part three: back to slow and soft. We will change together when I tap my beak.”

Tap-tap-tap. Everyone listened.

They began. Part one was slow. Boom (ting)— bzz— boom— clap. The music felt like a cuddle. The air smelled sweet and green. Sunlight made golden stripes on the floor. Piper sang softly, “Listen, wait, and play.”

Tap-tap-tap. Time for part two. Coco sped up just a little. Boom-boom— bzz— boom— clap! Buzz hummed brighter. The treehouse filled with happy sound. Mimi smiled with her whole face. Tippy tinged with a twinkle.

Tap-tap-tap. Part three. They slowed down together, softer and softer, until the last ting floated like a bubble and popped into silence.

Everyone grinned. Coco’s tail swished. “That felt good,” he said. “I followed your wings.”

Buzz buzzed softly, “I listened and waited.”

Mimi squeaked, “We worked together.”

Tippy nodded. “Compromise is kind.”

Piper bowed. “You all did wonderful listening. You followed directions. We cooperated. We made music as a team.”

They played the song again. And again. Birds outside chirped along. Leaves rustled like tiny shakers. Even the sun seemed to smile through the window.

When they finished, Piper laid out nut cookies on a little leaf plate. They munched and giggled. The treehouse smelled like warm nuts and sweet sap. Piper felt proud. Her heart beat boom… boom… just like their song.

Coco said, “Next time, can I try leading one part?”

Piper nodded. “Yes. We can take turns. Leaders listen, too.”

Buzz buzzed, “We can add a soft part and a loud part.”

Mimi squeaked, “And we can practice waiting.”

Tippy chimed, “We will follow directions together.”

Piper fluffed her rainbow tail. “Music is more than sounds,” she said. “Music is listening. Music is working together. Music is sharing and being kind.”

They all said, “Yes!”

As the sun went down, the friends packed their instruments. The treehouse felt calm and cozy. Piper looked around at her happy band and sang, “With listening ears and gentle hearts, we make the sweetest music.”

The End.
