# Splash the Dolphin Learns to Share

In a cozy study corner by the big blue ocean, there lived a playful dolphin named Splash. The study corner had soft cushions, colorful books, and toys scattered everywhere. Splash loved to play there every day, jumping and spinning in the shallow water that filled the special room.

"Wheee!" squeaked Splash as he dove through a hoop. "This is MY special place!"

One sunny morning, Splash heard tiny voices outside his study corner. Three little fish swam up to the window - Finn the yellow fish, Coral the pink fish, and Bubbles the purple fish.

"Excuse me," said Finn politely. "We heard there are wonderful books here. Could we please come in and read?"

Splash puffed up his chest. "No way! This is MY study corner. Go find your own place to read!"

The little fish looked sad. Their fins drooped down, and Bubbles started to sniffle. "But we don't have anywhere else to go," whispered Coral. "The library is too far away."

Splash turned away. "Not my problem!" he said, splashing water everywhere.

The three fish swam away slowly, their tails dragging behind them.

For the rest of the day, Splash tried to play alone. He threw his ball against the wall - *thunk, thunk, thunk* - but it wasn't fun without friends. He looked at his favorite picture book about friendship, but the colorful pages made him feel lonely.

"Hmm," thought Splash, tapping his fin against his chin. "Maybe I was being mean."

The next morning, Splash peeked outside his window. He saw Finn, Coral, and Bubbles sitting on a rock, trying to read a soggy book that was falling apart.

"Oh no," gasped Splash. "They really do need help!"

Splash's heart felt warm and tingly. He had an idea - a really good idea!

He swam quickly to his window and called out, "Hey! Little fish! Would you like to come in?"

The three fish looked surprised. "Really?" asked Bubbles, her eyes sparkling.

"Yes!" said Splash with a big smile. "I have lots of books, and reading is more fun with friends!"

Soon, all four friends were in the study corner together. Splash showed them his favorite books with bright pictures of sea creatures. The little fish taught Splash how to read the tiny words he couldn't see very well.

"You're really good at reading big words!" said Finn to Splash.

"And you three are great at reading small words!" replied Splash. "We make a good team!"

They took turns picking books. When Coral wanted to read about seahorses and Splash wanted to read about whales, they decided to read both books together.

"This is so much better than playing alone," laughed Splash, doing a happy flip.

From that day on, Splash shared his study corner with all his new friends. He learned that being kind and sharing made everything more fun. When other sea creatures asked to join them, Splash always said, "Of course! There's room for everyone!"

Splash discovered that being a good leader meant helping others and including everyone. He felt proud when he saw his friends smile and learn new things together.

"Sharing makes everything better!" Splash would tell anyone who would listen. And he was absolutely right.

The End.
