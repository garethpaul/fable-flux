# Milo's Big Fix

Milo the monkey loved playing in the cozy living room with his best friend Emma. The soft carpet felt wonderful under his furry feet, and the warm sunlight streamed through the big windows, making everything bright and cheerful.

"Look what I built!" Emma said proudly, showing Milo her tall tower of colorful blocks. Red, blue, yellow, and green blocks stacked high, reaching almost to her knees. "It took me all morning!"

Milo's eyes grew wide with excitement. "Wow! That's the tallest tower ever!" He wanted to see it up close, so he swung from the soft couch armrest, just like he did in the jungle.

But oh no! Milo swung too fast and too far. His long tail knocked right into Emma's beautiful tower.

CRASH! CLATTER! BANG!

The blocks tumbled everywhere across the carpet. Some rolled under the coffee table, others scattered behind the bookshelf. The room suddenly felt very quiet.

Emma's eyes filled with tears. "My tower! You broke my special tower, Milo!" She sat down on the fluffy rug and began to cry.

Milo's tummy felt all twisty and upset. He had hurt his best friend's feelings, and that made him feel terrible. "Emma, I... I didn't mean to," he said softly, his voice shaking.

"But it's broken now," Emma sniffled. "All my hard work is gone."

Milo looked at all the scattered blocks. He thought about how sad Emma looked, and he knew he had to do something. Being courageous meant doing the right thing, even when it was hard.

"Emma," Milo said, sitting down beside her on the soft carpet. "I'm really, really sorry I broke your tower. I was being too excited and not careful enough."

Emma wiped her eyes with the back of her hand. "It's okay to say sorry, but my tower is still broken."

Milo's face lit up with an idea. "What if... what if I help you build an even better tower? We could work together!"

Emma looked up at him. "Really? You'd help me?"

"Yes!" Milo said, already crawling around to gather the blocks. "I'll be super careful this time. And maybe you can teach me the best way to stack them so they don't fall down."

Emma's frown slowly turned into a small smile. "Well... you could hand me the blocks, and I could show you how to make the bottom really strong."

For the next hour, Milo and Emma worked side by side. Milo carefully passed Emma each block, and she taught him about making a wide base and balancing the colors. The room filled with their happy chatter and the gentle clicking sounds of blocks fitting together.

"Look!" Emma giggled as they placed the very last block on top. "Our new tower is even taller than my first one!"

Milo clapped his hands with joy. "And we built it together! That makes it extra special."

Emma hugged her monkey friend tight. "Thank you for saying sorry and helping me fix it, Milo. That's what good friends do."

"I learned something important today," Milo said thoughtfully. "When I make a mistake, I should say sorry and try to make it better."

Emma nodded. "And I learned that building with a friend is more fun than building alone!"

As the afternoon sun made their tower sparkle with pretty colors, both friends felt warm and happy inside.

The End.
