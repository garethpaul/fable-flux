# Panda Poppy’s Porch Plan

Poppy was a patient panda. She loved to sit on her peaceful front porch. The porch was cool and smooth. The wood smelled like sunshine and clean rain. A soft breeze tickled her ears. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” Leaves whispered, “Shhh-shhh.”

Poppy rocked in her little chair. She sipped warm bamboo tea. “Ahh,” she said. “I like calm.”

Then Poppy saw a little squirrel. The squirrel tugged on a long string. The string was wrapped around a baby bird’s wing! The baby bird cried, “Peep! Peep!” The string came from a shiny balloon stuck in the porch tree.

Poppy’s heart felt full and tight. “Oh dear,” she said. “You are scared.” She put her cup down. “I will help. I can wait. I can be gentle.”

The squirrel chattered, “I wanted to play. But the string grabbed the bird. I feel bad.”

Poppy touched the squirrel’s paw. “I can see you are worried,” she said. “Thank you for telling me. That is brave.” She looked up at the balloon. It wobbled and bumped the leaves. The leaves rustled, “Hush-hush.”

Poppy spoke softly to the baby bird. “I hear your peeps. I know you hurt. I am here. I will go slow.”

Poppy took a deep breath. In, out. In, out. “Patience helps,” she whispered.

She looked around the porch. She saw a basket, a scarf, and a pair of safe, soft scissors. She made a plan.

“First, we keep you calm,” Poppy told the baby bird. She hummed a warm, low song. “Hmm-hmm-hmm.” The baby bird stopped shaking. The little body felt warm and fluttery in Poppy’s paws.

“Next, we keep you safe,” Poppy said. She wrapped the bird in the soft scarf like a snug hug. “There, cozy,” she smiled.

“Now, we snip the string,” Poppy said. She showed the scissors to the squirrel. “Tools can help, but we must be careful and kind.”

The wind blew, “Whooo,” and the balloon bobbed. Poppy waited for the wind to slow. She counted, “One… two… three.” Snip! She cut the string from the branch. Then, with tiny snips, she freed the string from the bird’s wing.

“Peep?” said the bird. The wing wiggled. The bird blinked. The squirrel sighed, “Thank you. I was scared I made it worse.”

Poppy nodded. “You told the truth. You stayed to help. That is responsibility.” She smiled at both friends. “We all learn.”

The balloon drifted down to the porch with a soft “pap.” Poppy picked it up. “This balloon came from a party,” she said. “Balloons can be fun, but strings can hurt animals and trees.”

The squirrel’s eyes grew wide. “We can do better,” he said.

“Yes,” Poppy said. “We can be brave and change.” She set the balloon on the porch table. “Let’s make art,” she said. “Let’s make a ‘Kind Porch’ sign. We can tell friends to care for birds, trees, and the whole big world.”

Poppy got her art box. It smelled like crayons and fresh paper. She spread out colors: green like leaves, blue like sky, yellow like sun. She gave the squirrel a brush. She gave the baby bird a tiny stamp shaped like a leaf.

They painted together. Swish, swish went the brush. Tap, tap went the leaf stamp. Poppy drew a tree with smiling branches. The squirrel drew a nest with cozy lines. The baby bird stamped little green leaves all around.

Poppy wrote big, simple words: “Be Kind. Be Careful. Care for Nature.”

She looked at the balloon. “We can use it safely,” she said. “Let’s make a flower.” She snipped the balloon into petals. She glued the petals around a yellow circle. “Now the balloon will not fly away and hurt any more wings,” she said. “This is conservation. We use things again in a safe way.”

The porch smelled like glue and crayons and sweet tea. The breeze felt soft. The birds sang, “Peep-peep, cheep-cheep!” The baby bird flapped. The wing worked! “Peep!” it cried, happy now.

Poppy opened her paws. “You can go when you are ready,” she said. The baby bird hopped to the rail. It looked at Poppy and the squirrel. It chirped, “Thank you,” and flew to its nest. The squirrel waved its tail. “Good-bye!”

They hung the “Kind Porch” sign on the front porch. The wood felt warm under their paws. The colors shone bright in the sun.

A little rabbit hopped by. “What is this?” asked the rabbit.

Poppy said, “It is our Kind Porch plan. We help. We wait. We listen. We clean up. We make art to teach others.”

The rabbit read the words slowly. “Be Kind. Be Careful. Care for Nature,” the rabbit said. “I can do that!”

Poppy smiled. “When we feel what others feel, that is empathy,” she said. “Empathy helps us take care with our hands and our hearts.”

The squirrel nodded. “Next time, I will pick up strings,” he said. “I will ask, ‘Is this safe for birds?’”

Poppy patted the porch rail. “We can be gentle and brave every day,” she said.

The sun set soft and pink. The porch glowed. Poppy rocked in her chair. She sipped her tea. “Today, we listened. We helped. We made art. We cared for nature,” she said. “That is a good day.”

The End.
