# Mango’s Backyard Band Helps Out

Mango the courageous monkey lived in a sunny backyard full of bright flowers and buzzing bees. The grass felt soft under his toes, and the warm sun tickled his furry ears. Every morning, Mango stretched his arms, took a deep, happy breath, and said, “Today, I will be brave and kind!”

One sunny day, Mango heard a soft clink and clatter. “Ting-ting, tap-tap,” went the sound. He peeked around the big sunflower and saw a neighbor, Grandma Dot. She stood by a wobbly wagon filled with food. The wagon held crunchy apples, soft bread, and jars of jam that glowed red in the sun.

“Oh dear,” sighed Grandma Dot. “I want to take this food to the Little Birds’ Nest, where tiny birds need snacks. But my wagon is wobbly, and my feet are tired.”

Mango’s heart felt warm. He wanted to help. “I can help!” he said. His tail curled with courage. “We can do it together.”

Grandma Dot smiled. “Thank you, Mango. But the wagon is heavy, and the path is bumpy.”

Mango tapped his chin. He looked around the sunny backyard. He saw his friends: Tiko the turtle, Bella the bluebird, and Pippa the puppy. He had an idea that sparkled like sunshine.

“Friends!” Mango called. “Let’s make a Helping Band! We can move together, keep a steady beat, and help Grandma Dot. One, two, we can help you!”

Bella fluttered down. “A band? Tweet, tweet! I can sing!”

Tiko blinked slowly. “I can keep time,” he said. “Slow and steady.”

Pippa wagged her tail. “I can pull and push!” she barked. “Woof, woof!”

Mango clapped his hands. “Music helps us move together. That’s called coordination. We listen. We watch. We step in time!”

He found a little drum made from a tin and a stretchy lid. Tap-tap! It sounded bright and happy. Pippa wore a jingly collar. Jingle-jingle! Bella’s song was light and clear: “La-la-la!” Tiko tapped a tiny stick on a stone: “Tok… tok… tok.”

Mango stood tall. “Ready? We will help our community. That means we help our neighbors. We share and care.”

Grandma Dot’s eyes shone. “You are kind friends.”

They started down the backyard path. The sun felt warm like a hug. The wind smelled like sweet clover. Bees hummed, “Bzzzz,” as if they joined the song.

Mango tapped the drum. “Tap-tap, tap-tap.” He said, “Step, step, together.”

Pippa pushed gently with her shoulder. “Jingle, jingle, push!” she sang.

Tiko moved in time. “Tok… tok… steady.”

Bella flew above and called, “Left, right, left, right!” Her voice sounded like a silver bell.

The wagon rolled forward. The wheels creaked, “Eee-eee,” but they kept moving. When they reached a bump, the wagon shook. The jars rattled. Mango felt his tummy tighten.

“I feel a little worried,” Mango said. “But I can be brave and careful.”

“Slow and steady,” said Tiko. “Tok… tok…”

“Let’s make our beat slower,” Mango said. He tapped softer. “Tap… tap…” Everyone matched the beat. They coordinated their steps. Together, they rolled the wagon over the bump, safe and sound.

“Yay!” cheered Pippa. “We did it!”

They came to a sunny patch where the grass was tall. It brushed their legs with a tickle. Bella swooped low. “Watch out!” she sang. “Tall grass ahead!”

Mango smiled. “We can swish-swish through. Keep the beat!”

They swished and sang. “Tap, tap, swish, swish. Help, help, we wish-wish!”

At last, they reached the Little Birds’ Nest. Tiny birds chirped, “Peep! Peep!” Their eyes were bright and hopeful. The nest smelled like warm straw. The little birds’ tummies made soft grumble sounds.

Grandma Dot opened the wagon. She handed out apple slices, small crumbs of bread, and tiny spoonfuls of jam. The little birds pecked happily. “Peep, peep, thank you!”

Mango felt his heart glow. “We did community service,” he said. “We helped our neighbors. We worked together.”

Grandma Dot patted Mango’s shoulder. “You were all brave and kind. Your music helped you move as one.”

Bella sang a cheerful tune. Pippa wagged in time. Tiko nodded. “Coordination is good,” he said. “Slow and steady together.”

Mango bowed like a band leader. “Helping can be fun. Music can guide our steps. And when we help, we make the world warm and bright.”

They started the walk home. The sun touched the tops of the flowers. A soft breeze smelled like oranges and grass. Mango tapped a happy rhythm. “Tap-tap, thank you. Tap-tap, we care.”

Grandma Dot waved. “You are my Helping Band!”

Back in the sunny backyard, Mango sat with his friends under the big sunflower. They shared a bowl of sweet banana slices. Mango said, “Today I learned: community service means we help others. With coordination and music, we can do big jobs together.”

Pippa licked a banana smear. “Woof! Helping feels good.”

Bella sang softly, “La-la, kind hearts shine.”

Tiko smiled. “We did it, one beat at a time.”

Mango looked at the blue sky. “Tomorrow, we can help again,” he said. “Together.”

The End.
