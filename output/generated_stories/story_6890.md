# Koa the Calm Koala Says “I’m Sorry”

Koa the calm koala loved the creative art room. The room smelled like fresh paper and sweet glue. Sunlight made rainbow spots on the floor. Paint pots lined the table like little colorful soldiers—red, yellow, blue, and green. Soft music hummed, “la-la-la,” from a tiny speaker.

“Good morning, colors,” Koa said, patting the blue paint. Koa spoke softly, like a warm whisper. “Today, I will paint a sky.”

Koa dipped a brush into blue. Swish, swish. The brush felt smooth and tickly in Koa’s paw. Koa smiled. “So calm. So nice.”

Just then, Tilly the turtle rolled in a cart of paper. “Hi, Koa,” Tilly said. “I made a special rainbow paper for my art show.”

Koa’s eyes went big. The paper was shiny and bright. It shone like a bubble. “It is beautiful,” Koa said. “I like the sparkle.”

Tilly nodded proudly. “I will put it here to dry,” she said, laying the rainbow paper on a low shelf. “Please don’t touch. It is still wet.”

Koa’s ears twitched. “I will not touch,” Koa promised. Koa took a deep breath and went back to painting the blue sky.

Soon, Benny the bunny bounced in. “Boing, boing! I found glitter!” Benny giggled, shaking a little jar. Glitter sprinkled into the air like tiny stars. “Oops!”

Some glitter landed on Koa’s painting. “Oh,” said Koa, “my calm blue sky is sparkly now.” Koa smiled. “That is okay. It looks pretty.”

Benny hopped toward the shelf. “What is this shiny rainbow?” he asked.

“It is Tilly’s,” Koa said. “Please don’t touch.”

Benny stopped. “Okay! I won’t.”

Koa felt proud. Koa was respectful and careful. Koa knew the art room had rules: ask, share, and be gentle.

Then Koa saw a little drip on the floor. Plip. Plop. It looked like water near Tilly’s rainbow paper. “Hmm,” Koa whispered. “I will clean it so no one slips.”

Koa reached for a cloth. In a hurry to help, Koa’s soft paw brushed the shelf. The shelf wobbled. The rainbow paper slid—shoop!—and landed face-down on Koa’s blue paint.

“Oh no,” breathed Koa. Koa’s heart thumped fast. The pretty rainbow was now smudged with blue. Koa felt hot in the cheeks. “I did not mean to,” Koa said, voice small.

Tilly came back with stickers. “I am ready to decorate—” She gasped. “My rainbow! It is smudged!”

Benny’s ears drooped. “Uh-oh.”

Koa looked at Tilly. Koa took a slow, deep breath, just like when clouds move. “Tilly,” Koa said, “I am sorry. I bumped the shelf. I wanted to clean the drip. I made a mistake. I did not ask for help. I feel sad that I hurt your art.”

Tilly blinked. Her eyes were shiny, like she might cry. “You said sorry,” she whispered. “You told the truth.”

Koa nodded. “I want to fix it. May I try? I can help make a new rainbow. Or I can help un-smudge. I will do what you like.”

Tilly was quiet. She touched the paper. It felt sticky and cool. “It is still wet,” she said. “Maybe we can make it into a sky-after-rain picture.”

Koa’s ears lifted. “A rainbow after rain! That is a good idea.”

“I will help too,” Benny said. “I can sprinkle glitter like little raindrops.”

Koa smiled a small smile. “Thank you. I will start.” Koa showed initiative. Koa got clean paper towels, fresh water, and a new brush. Swish, dab, pat. Koa gently lifted the extra blue. Tilly added red and yellow. Benny shook a tiny bit of glitter. Sparkle, sparkle.

They worked slowly. The room sounded like soft brushing and quiet breaths. The smell of paint mixed with the sweet smell of stickers. “Look,” Tilly said at last. “The smudge looks like soft clouds now.”

“And the rainbow peeks through,” Koa said. “Hello, rainbow!”

Tilly looked at Koa. “Thank you for saying sorry,” she said. “You were brave and respectful.”

Koa felt warm inside. “Thank you for listening,” Koa said. “I will be more careful. Next time, I will ask for help before I reach.”

Benny clapped his paws. “Team art!”

They made a little sign that said, “Please Ask Before Touching.” Koa drew a tiny koala face. Tilly added a turtle. Benny drew a carrot. They laughed.

When the picture was dry, they hung it on the wall. The sunlight made the glitter twinkle like rain. The colors were bright and kind. The art room felt safe and happy again.

Ms. Lark, the teacher, peeked in. “What a lovely ‘Rainbow After Rain’!” she said. “I see respect. I see good choices. I hear kind words.”

Koa stood tall but calm. “I learned to say ‘I’m sorry’ when I make a mistake,” Koa said. “I learned to fix what I can, and to ask before I touch.”

Tilly smiled. “I learned to forgive.”

Benny giggled. “I learned to sprinkle carefully.”

They all said, “Thank you,” to each other. Gratitude filled the room like a cozy hug.

Koa took one more deep breath. The art room smelled like paper and paint and friendship. The rainbow picture glowed. Koa felt peaceful.

The clear lesson floated in Koa’s mind: When we make a mistake, we say sorry, we show respect, and we take action to make it right.

The End.
