# Ellie Says "I'm Sorry"

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a tiny, wiggly trunk. She played in a comfortable nursery with bright rugs, wooden blocks, and soft stuffed animals. The room smelled like clean soap and warm milk. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor.

Ellie loved to stack blocks. Tap, tap, tap went her little feet as she walked. Clink, clink went the blocks as she built a tall tower.

Her friends played nearby. Mia the mouse drew with crayons that smelled like sweet berries. Benji the bunny hugged a fuzzy blanket. Tiko the turtle watched from a beanbag chair.

“Let’s build together,” Ellie said, her eyes shining. “I can make a super tall tower!”

“I can add a blue block,” Mia squeaked.

“I can add a green one,” Benji said.

“I’ll cheer,” Tiko smiled.

Ellie felt big and brave. She wanted to be a leader and help her friends. “I will show you how,” she said. Her trunk reached out. Her heart thumped with excitement.

But Ellie was so excited, she didn’t see Mia’s crayon box near the blocks. Her foot bumped the box. The crayons rolled. The tower wobbled.

“Careful!” Mia squeaked.

“Oh!” Ellie gasped.

Crash! The tower tumbled down. Blocks bumped the floor. Clatter clatter clatter! A block knocked Mia’s picture. A crayon made a purple line across it.

Mia’s eyes got shiny with tears. “My picture,” she whispered.

Benji’s nose twitched. “That was scary,” he said softly.

Tiko tucked his head. “It was loud,” he murmured.

Ellie’s tummy felt tight. Her ears drooped. “I didn’t mean to,” she whispered. She felt warm and wobbly inside. She wanted to hide under a blanket.

Ellie looked at her friends. She remembered what her caregiver always said: “Leaders are gentle. Leaders use brave words and kind hands.”

Ellie took a slow breath. In. Out. She felt her heart slow down.

“I made a mistake,” she said. “I knocked the tower. I messed up your picture, Mia. I am sorry.”

Mia sniffled. “You mean it?” she asked.

“Yes,” Ellie said, standing tall. “I am really, really sorry. I didn’t watch my feet. I will make it right.”

“How?” Benji asked.

Ellie thought. Her trunk curled and uncurled. The sun felt warm on her back. She smelled the berry crayons. She had an idea.

“I can help fix the picture,” Ellie said. “Mia, can I hold the paper while you draw a new line? I can bring you a fresh white sheet, too.”

Mia nodded slowly. “Okay.”

Ellie used her gentle trunk to bring a clean paper. She held the edge steady. Mia colored carefully. Scritch, scratch went the crayons. Ellie hummed a soft tune. Benji fetched a little cup of water. Tiko fanned the picture to dry the crayon.

Soon, the new picture looked bright and happy. It was a big purple heart with a tiny mouse inside. Mia smiled. “Thank you,” she said. “I like this one even more.”

Ellie’s chest felt light. “I am glad,” she said.

Then Ellie turned to the blocks. “Friends,” she said, “I want to build the tower again. But we will do it safely. I can be a kind leader.”

“How?” Tiko asked, peeking out.

Ellie pointed with her trunk. “We can make a clear path. No boxes near the blocks. We can take turns. We can use quiet feet and slow hands.”

“That sounds good,” Benji said.

Mia placed the crayon box on a shelf. “Safe and neat,” she said.

Ellie swept the floor with her trunk, making a soft shhh sound as she moved a small brush. She set the blocks in a little circle. “Mia, you choose the first block,” she said. “Benji, you choose the next. Tiko, you cheer and tell us if the tower leans. I will wait and help.”

They began. Tap, tap, tap. Clink, clink. The tower grew. Mia added blue. Benji added green. Ellie added a yellow block with slow, careful hands. Tiko watched closely.

“Leaning left,” Tiko said.

Ellie steadied the tower. “Thank you,” she said. “Good eyes!”

The tower got taller and taller. Everyone smiled. Their hearts felt brave and soft at the same time. Ellie felt proud, but not the loud kind of proud. She felt the kind of proud that is gentle and warm, like a blanket on a cool day.

At last, the tower stood tall and steady. It was even taller than Ellie’s ears!

“We did it,” Mia said.

“We did it together,” Ellie added. “Thank you for letting me fix my mistake. Saying sorry was hard, but it was the brave thing to do.”

Benji patted her leg. “You were a good leader,” he said.

Tiko nodded. “You used kind words and careful steps.”

Ellie’s ears lifted. “I learned something,” she said. “When we make a mistake, we can say, ‘I’m sorry,’ and then make it right. That is courage. That is leadership.”

The friends cuddled on the soft rug. The nursery felt extra cozy. It smelled like crayons and warm milk. Sunlight kissed the tower with a golden glow.

Ellie closed her eyes for a moment. Her heart felt calm and happy. She listened to her friends breathe. In. Out. Together.

The End.
