# Finn the Dolphin Solves the Drippy Mystery

Finn was a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a happy squeak. Finn loved to learn. One rainy morning, Finn splashed down a slide that led to a cozy basement lab. The lab smelled like clean soap and lemon. Lights hummed softly. Jars clicked on shelves. Paper rustled like leaves. Tools lay in tidy bins: tape, string, crayons, and little wooden sticks.

“Hello, lab!” Finn chirped. “Let’s make something!”

Finn wiggled to a big table and made a little craft boat from a cup, tape, and sticks. “My boat can help clean the pond,” Finn said. “I want to help my town.” Finn felt proud and warm in the tummy. Helping felt good.

Plink. Plink. Finn’s fins paused. “What is that sound?” Plink. Plink. Drip!

Finn turned and saw a tiny puddle under a pipe. A drop fell and made a soft splash. Finn sniffed. It smelled like rainwater.

Finn took a calm breath. “I can think this through,” Finn said. “I will be a problem-solver.” Finn smiled. “Step by step.”

Finn talked aloud. “First, I will look.” Finn looked up at the pipe. The drop came from a shiny bend. “Next, I will listen.” Finn listened—drip, drip, drip. “Then, I will touch.” Finn gently felt the pipe with a soft fin. Cool and wet.

Finn drew a quick picture on paper. “I will make a plan,” Finn said. “Plan: find the leak, catch the drips, fix the drip safely.”

Finn looked for help tools. There was a bucket, a towel, and a roll of plumber’s tape. There was also a bright yellow sign that said, “Caution.” Finn nodded. “Safety first,” Finn said. “That is being a good helper to everyone.”

Finn slid the bucket under the drip. Plink—into the bucket! “Good,” said Finn. “Now the floor is safe and dry.”

Finn looked at the pipe again. “Why is it dripping?” Finn asked. Finn tapped a little, very gentle. “Too loose? Too hot? Too cold?” Finn hummed. “I will check the knob.” A small knob was near the bend. Finn read the label slowly. “Water… off.” Finn made a thoughtful face. “If I turn it, the water may stop.”

Finn remembered another rule. “Ask for help if you are not sure.” Finn called out, “Is anyone here?”

From upstairs, a friendly voice answered. “I’m here!” It was Jo, the neighbor who ran the lab. Jo came down the steps. The steps creaked like little drums. Jo wore blue gloves and had kind eyes. “Hi, Finn! I heard drips.”

Finn pointed. “I found the drip. I made a plan. Can we do the knob together?”

Jo smiled. “Great thinking, Finn. Yes, we will do it together.” Jo gently turned the knob. The drip slowed. Drip… stop. The lab became quiet and cozy again.

Finn clapped fins. “We did it!”

Jo nodded. “You used your eyes, your ears, your touch, and your brain. You asked for help. That is smart and safe.”

Finn’s heart felt big like a bubble. “I can think. I can help.”

Jo looked at Finn’s little craft boat. “What is this?”

Finn beamed. “A clean-up boat! It can carry tiny trash from the pond. That is good for the town.”

Jo said, “That is civic responsibility. It means you care for everyone.”

Finn liked the new words. “Civic… responsibility.” Finn said them slow and happy.

Jo showed a box of felt, glue, and paper tubes. “Would you like to craft a tiny filter for your boat?”

Finn squeaked. “Yes, please!” They worked side by side. The glue smelled sweet. The felt felt soft. The paper tubes were smooth. Finn cut a small square of felt and glued it into the cup boat like a gentle net.

Finn tested the boat in a sink. The water swished. The boat bobbed. Little paper bits stuck to the felt. “It works!” Finn laughed. “We made it.”

Jo cheered. “You planned, you tested, and you tried again. That is critical thinking.”

Suddenly, Finn heard a faint drip again. “Oh!” Finn looked. A second, tiny drip was coming from another joint. Finn breathed in and out. “Step by step,” Finn said softly. “Look. Listen. Touch. Plan. Ask.”

Finn pointed to the new drip. “Jo, may we put a towel here and use the special tape?”

Jo nodded. “Yes.” They dried the pipe with the towel. Finn held the tape while Jo wrapped it snug. They turned the water on just a little. No drip! The lab stayed quiet. Finn felt calm and proud.

Jo put up the yellow “Caution” sign near the pipe with a smile. “You helped keep the lab safe for everyone, Finn. That is brave. That is kind.”

Finn tucked the clean-up boat into a bag. The bag rustled softly. “I will take this to the pond and help pick up tiny bits. I will tell friends, too.”

Jo patted Finn’s fin. “You are a thoughtful helper. Your brain and your heart make a great team.”

Finn swished toward the slide. The lemon smell and the warm lights made the lab feel like a hug. Finn looked back and said, “Today I learned to slow down, make a plan, and ask for help. I can solve problems. I can care for my town.”

“Exactly,” said Jo. “Think first, then act with care.”

Finn slid away with a happy splash, ready to help, ready to think, and ready to share.

The End.
