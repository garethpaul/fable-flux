# Toby's Big Sorry

Toby the tortoise lived in a busy basement. Boxes stacked high like towers. Old toys scattered everywhere. The washing machine hummed a gentle song. Toby loved his cozy home.

Today felt different. Toby's shell felt heavy with worry. Yesterday, he had knocked over Mama Mouse's winter food pile. All her acorns rolled under the big furnace. Now her three babies were hungry.

"I was rushing to my reading corner," Toby whispered sadly. "I didn't look where I was going."

The basement smelled like soap and old books. Toby could hear little mouse squeaks from behind the water heater. His heart felt achy. He knew he had to fix his mistake.

"I need to say sorry," Toby decided. "And help find new food."

Toby crawled slowly to the mouse family's corner. His feet made soft tapping sounds on the concrete floor. Mama Mouse sat with her babies, looking worried.

"Mama Mouse," Toby called gently. "I'm very sorry I knocked over your food. It was an accident, but I should have been more careful."

Mama Mouse looked up. Her whiskers twitched. "Thank you for saying sorry, Toby. But my babies are still hungry."

Toby's wise old eyes sparkled. "I have an idea! Let me help you find new food. I know every corner of this basement."

Together, they made a plan. Toby would use his strong shell to move heavy boxes. Mama Mouse would squeeze into small spaces. They would search for dropped crackers and forgotten snacks.

"Teamwork makes everything better," Toby smiled.

They started near the old toy chest. Toby pushed aside a heavy box with his shell. "Oomph!" he grunted. Behind it, they found three peanuts!

"Hooray!" squeaked the baby mice.

Next, they checked under the stairs. The space smelled dusty and old. Mama Mouse found breadcrumbs from last week's picnic basket.

"You're so good at finding food," Toby told her. "I'm learning new things about our basement home."

By the furnace, they discovered the best treasure yet. A whole bag of sunflower seeds had fallen behind the warm pipes. The bag crinkled as they opened it.

"This is enough food for many days!" Mama Mouse danced with joy.

As they worked together, Toby learned about Mama Mouse's homeland. "I came from a farm far away," she shared. "Mice all over the world work hard to feed their families."

"Animals everywhere take care of each other," Toby nodded wisely. "Just like we're doing now."

The baby mice munched happily on their new feast. Their tiny cheeks puffed out like balloons. The basement felt warm and friendly again.

"Thank you for making things right," Mama Mouse said. "You didn't just say sorry with words. You helped with actions too."

Toby's shell felt light now. His worry had melted away like ice cream in sunshine. "When we make mistakes, we can always try to fix them," he said.

From that day on, Toby always looked carefully before moving through the basement. He and Mama Mouse became the best of friends. They helped each other and shared stories about the big world above.

"Saying sorry and helping others makes everything better," Toby told himself each night. The basement hummed its gentle song, and everyone slept peacefully.

The End.
