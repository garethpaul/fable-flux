# Polly's Fair Share Day

In a bright and colorful living room filled with soft cushions and rainbow toys, lived a creative parrot named Polly. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds in the sunshine that streamed through the big window. Polly loved to paint pictures with her tiny brush and create beautiful art on her easel.

One sunny morning, Polly was excited to start a new painting. She flapped her wings happily and squawked, "Today I'll paint the most beautiful garden ever!" But when she looked at her paint palette, her bright eyes grew sad. All her paint tubes were empty!

"Oh no!" chirped Polly. "How can I paint without any colors?"

Just then, her friend Benny the bunny hopped into the living room, carrying a big box of brand-new paint tubes. The tubes were shiny and full of every color Polly could imagine - red like strawberries, blue like the sky, and yellow like sunshine.

"Wow, Benny! Those paints look amazing!" said Polly, her feathers ruffling with excitement.

Benny clutched the box tightly to his fluffy chest. "These are all mine," he said. "I'm going to use every single color for my own painting. You can't have any!"

Polly's heart felt heavy. She watched as Benny squeezed out huge globs of paint onto his palette - much more than he could ever use. The beautiful colors dripped and wasted onto the floor. Soon, the soft carpet was spotted with rainbow puddles.

"Benny," said Polly gently, "you're using so much paint that it's going to waste. And I have no paint at all to create my garden picture."

But Benny just turned away and kept squeezing out more and more paint. The room began to smell strongly of the paint, and colorful drops splattered everywhere.

Polly felt frustrated, but then she had an idea. She flew to the bookshelf and pulled out a picture book about sharing. "Benny, let me show you something," she said kindly.

Together, they looked at pictures of animals sharing food, toys, and art supplies. "See how happy everyone looks when they share?" asked Polly, pointing with her wing tip.

Benny's ears drooped as he looked at all the wasted paint around him. The beautiful colors were drying up and couldn't be used anymore. "I made such a mess," he whispered. "And I have way more paint than I need."

"It's okay," said Polly warmly. "We can clean up together and share what's left. When we share fairly, there's enough for everyone, and nothing goes to waste!"

Benny's eyes lit up. "You're right! Sharing is much better than being greedy."

They carefully cleaned up the spilled paint with soft cloths, making sure to put the colorful mess in the recycling bin. Then Benny shared his remaining paints with Polly, giving her exactly half of each color.

Together, they created the most beautiful paintings ever! Polly painted her dream garden with flowers of every color, while Benny painted a sunny meadow. The living room filled with the sweet scent of their creativity and the sound of happy chirping and giggling.

"This is so much more fun than painting alone," laughed Benny.

"And look how much we saved by not wasting paint!" added Polly proudly.

From that day on, Polly and Benny always shared their art supplies fairly. They learned that when everyone gets their fair share, nobody goes without, nothing gets wasted, and everyone is happier. The living room became a place where fairness and friendship painted the most beautiful pictures of all.

The End.
