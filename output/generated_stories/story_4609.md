# Miko's Garden Helper Day

Miko the monkey lived in a cozy apartment with his family. Every morning, he loved to swing out onto their sunny balcony garden. The colorful flowers smelled sweet like candy, and the green leaves rustled softly in the breeze.

"Good morning, little plants!" Miko chirped happily, touching the smooth petals with his tiny fingers.

One Tuesday morning, Miko's neighbor Mrs. Chen knocked on their door. She was an elderly lady who lived next door with her own beautiful balcony garden.

"Hello, Miko," Mrs. Chen said with a worried voice. "I hurt my back yesterday and can't take care of my plants today. Could you help me water them? It's very important that they get water every day, or they might get sick."

Miko's eyes grew wide. "Me? Help with your garden?" He felt excited but also a little nervous. "What if I do it wrong?"

"I believe you can do it," Mrs. Chen smiled gently. "Let me show you exactly what to do."

Mrs. Chen gave Miko a small blue watering can that fit perfectly in his hands. "First, fill this with cool water from the kitchen sink," she explained. "Then, pour just a little bit on each plant's soil - not too much, not too little."

Miko nodded seriously. This was an important job!

He carefully carried the watering can to the sink. The water made a pleasant splashing sound as it filled up. The can felt heavier now, so Miko used both hands to carry it safely to Mrs. Chen's balcony.

"Remember," Mrs. Chen said, "start with the tomato plants in the big pots, then the flowers, and finish with the herbs. Take your time."

Miko began his task. He poured the water slowly, watching it soak into the dark soil. The tomato plants had tiny yellow flowers that smelled fresh and green. "Drink up, little plants," he whispered.

Next came the colorful flowers - red, yellow, and purple blooms that felt soft like velvet. The water made tiny rainbows in the morning sunlight.

Finally, Miko watered the herb plants. Mrs. Chen had taught him their names: basil, mint, and rosemary. Each one smelled different and wonderful.

"I'm almost done!" Miko called out proudly.

But then - oh no! Miko accidentally tipped the watering can too much. Water splashed everywhere, soaking his fur and the balcony floor.

"Oops!" Miko felt his cheeks get warm with embarrassment.

Mrs. Chen appeared with a soft towel. "That's okay, dear. Everyone makes mistakes when they're learning. The important thing is that you kept going and finished your job."

She helped Miko dry off. "Look at all the plants you helped today! They're already looking happier."

Miko looked around at the glistening garden. The plants did look more colorful and fresh. He felt proud and tall, even though he was just a little monkey.

"Thank you, Miko," Mrs. Chen said. "Because you completed this important task, my plants will stay healthy. You're a wonderful helper."

That evening, Miko told his family all about his garden adventure. "I finished the whole job, even when I made a mistake," he said with a big smile.

"We're so proud of you," his mama said, giving him a warm hug. "When we complete our tasks, we help others and feel good about ourselves too."

From that day on, Miko helped Mrs. Chen with her garden every week. He learned that finishing what you start makes you feel strong and happy inside.

The End.
