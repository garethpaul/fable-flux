# Benny's Big Helper Day

Benny the rabbit had soft brown fur and the biggest, brightest eyes in the whole garden. He loved to hop and jump and help everyone around him. Today was a very special day because Mama Rabbit had asked him to clean the old garden shed.

"Benny," said Mama Rabbit, giving him a gentle pat on his fluffy head. "I need you to organize the shed before lunch time. First, put all the tools on the left shelf. Then, stack the flower pots on the right. Last, sweep the floor clean. Can you follow these steps?"

"Yes, Mama! I'm the best helper ever!" Benny said, thumping his back foot excitedly. He hopped toward the wooden shed, his cotton-tail bouncing.

The shed smelled like fresh dirt and old leaves. Sunlight streamed through the dusty window, making everything glow golden. Benny looked around and saw tools scattered everywhere, flower pots piled in corners, and leaves covering the floor.

"This will be so easy!" Benny said to himself. But instead of following Mama's steps, he decided to do everything at once. He grabbed a rake with one paw, picked up three flower pots with the other, and tried to sweep with his foot all at the same time.

CRASH! The flower pots tumbled down. CLANG! The rake fell on his toe. "Ow, ow, ow!" Benny hopped around, holding his hurt foot.

He tried again, this time running back and forth very fast. He moved one tool, then ran to move a pot, then swept one corner, then moved another tool. Soon, Benny felt dizzy and tired. His heart was beating fast, and he was breathing hard.

"Oh no!" Benny gasped, looking around. The shed looked even messier than before! Tools were mixed with pots, dirt was everywhere, and he had knocked over a watering can that was now dripping water on the floor.

Just then, Mama Rabbit peeked into the shed. "How are you doing, little one?"

Benny's ears drooped low. "I'm sorry, Mama. I tried to do everything fast, but I made a big mess. I didn't follow your directions."

Mama Rabbit hopped inside and gave Benny a warm hug. "It's okay, sweetie. Sometimes when we try to rush, we make more work for ourselves. Would you like to try again, but this time following the steps slowly?"

Benny nodded, wiping his eyes with his paw. "Yes, please help me."

"Remember," Mama said gently, "first the tools, then the pots, then sweep. One step at a time."

Benny took a deep breath. First, he carefully picked up each tool - the rake, the shovel, the watering can - and placed them neatly on the left shelf. The metal tools clinked softly as he arranged them.

Next, he gathered all the flower pots, feeling their smooth clay surfaces, and stacked them carefully on the right shelf. They made a satisfying clicking sound as they nested together.

Finally, Benny swept the floor with long, steady strokes. The broom swished back and forth, gathering all the leaves and dirt into a neat pile.

"I did it!" Benny cheered, looking at the clean, organized shed. Everything was in its proper place, and the floor sparkled in the sunlight.

"You certainly did!" Mama Rabbit smiled proudly. "You learned that taking time to follow directions step by step helps us do our best work."

Benny hugged his mama tight. "Next time, I'll remember to slow down and follow the steps. It feels so good to finish something the right way!"

From that day on, whenever Benny had a job to do, he remembered to take his time, follow directions, and do one thing at a time.

The End.
