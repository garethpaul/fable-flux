# Brave Beaver Break Time

Benny was a busy beaver. He worked in his cozy home office by the river. His desk was smooth wood. His chair was soft and round. The window was open, and he could hear birds sing, “Tweet-tweet!” The sun made warm puddles of light on the floor.

Benny tapped on his keyboard. Tap-tap-tap! He clicked his mouse. Click! He stacked papers in neat piles. Shuffle-shuffle! He wore big round glasses, and his tail thumped when he thought hard. Thump. Thump.

Today, Benny had a big plan. After work, he wanted to try a new sport with his friends. He had never played before. It was a friendly game called riverball. It was a little like soccer but played by the river bank with a soft ball. His friends came from many places. Mei the duck from the big lake. Kofi the turtle from a warm pond. Asha the fox from the red hill. They all liked to share games from their homes.

But Benny felt a tickle in his tummy. It felt like butterflies. “What if I fall?” he whispered. “What if I’m not good at riverball?”

His phone buzzed. Bzzz! A message popped up from Coach Nana, a kind otter. It said, “Practice at 4! Bring your brave heart. We learn together.”

Benny looked at his work. So many papers. So many taps and clicks. He wanted to finish everything before playtime. He worked faster. Tap-tap-tap! Click-click! But the butterflies fluttered more. Flitter-flutter.

His friend Mei paddled by and waved through the window. “Hi, Benny!” she quacked. “Are you coming to riverball?”

“I’m busy,” Benny said. He looked at the papers. He looked at the ball by the door. “And I’m a little scared.”

Mei nodded. “It’s okay to feel scared,” she said softly. “Let’s breathe together.”

They both took a slow breath in. Siiiip. Then a slow breath out. Hooosh.

Benny felt the butterflies slow down. “Thank you,” he said. “That felt nice.”

Kofi the turtle knocked on the door. Knock-knock. “Benny,” he said in a deep, gentle voice, “my grandma taught me a brave saying. He put a hand on his shell and said, ‘Small steps, strong heart.’ Want to try?”

Benny put a paw on his chest. “Small steps,” he repeated. “Strong heart.” His tail made a happy thump.

Asha the fox peeked in with a bright scarf. “I brought the soft ball,” she said. “It’s squishy! Want to feel?”

Benny squeezed the ball. Squish-squish! It smelled like new rubber. It made a tiny squeak. “It’s not scary,” he said, surprised.

Coach Nana popped her head in, water droplets sparkling on her whiskers. “Team,” she said kindly, “sports are for learning. We cheer. We try. We rest when we need.”

Benny looked at his desk. He looked at his friends. He listened to the birds. Tweet-tweet. He took another slow breath. Siiiip. Hooosh.

“I will be brave,” he said. “I will try riverball.”

“Yay!” everyone cheered. “Brave Benny!”

They walked to the river bank. The grass was cool and tickly under Benny’s feet. The water made a gentle hush-hush. The warm sun hugged his fur. Benny’s butterflies came back a little, but he remembered, “Small steps, strong heart.”

Coach Nana clapped. “Let’s learn the steps,” she said. “First, listen. Second, breathe. Third, try. Fourth, cheer!”

They practiced gentle kicks on soft sand. Tap. Tap. The ball rolled slow and safe. Asha showed a kick from her hill team. Mei showed a paddle pass from her lake. Kofi showed a careful block from his pond. “We all share,” Coach Nana said. “Different places, different skills. Together, we grow strong.”

Benny tried a kick. The ball rolled into the water—plop! “Oops,” he said. His cheeks felt warm. He wanted to hide behind his tail.

Coach Nana smiled. “Breathe with me, Benny.”

Siiiip. Hooosh.

Benny tried again. Tap. The ball rolled to Mei. She quacked, “Great try!” Asha wagged her tail. Kofi gave a thumbs-up with his little foot.

Soon they played a slow, friendly game. Benny ran a little. He stopped to breathe when his tummy felt fluttery. He listened to his feet on the grass. Pat-pat-pat. He felt the soft wind on his face. Whoosh. He heard happy laughs. He tasted cool water from his bottle. Ahhh.

The ball came to Benny. His heart thumped. Thump-thump. He remembered, “Small steps, strong heart.” He took one step. He took one breath. He tapped the ball. Tap! It rolled to Kofi, who gently nudged it into the goal. Plunk!

“Goal!” shouted Coach Nana. “Teamwork!”

Benny’s smile felt big and warm. “I did it,” he said. “I was brave.”

After the game, they sat in a circle on the soft grass. Coach Nana asked, “What did we learn?”

Mei said, “We can breathe when we feel scared.”

Kofi said, “Small steps help us try.”

Asha said, “We can share our skills from our homes.”

Benny said, “Bravery is trying, even when my tummy has butterflies. Mindfulness helps the butterflies calm down. Sports are for learning and fun.”

Coach Nana nodded. “Yes. Bravery grows when we practice. Mindful breaths guide us. Teamwork makes us strong.”

Benny went back to his home office as the sky turned pink. He stacked his papers with a happy shuffle-shuffle. He tapped his keyboard with a calm tap-tap. He smiled at the soft ball by the door.

“I can be busy,” he said, “and I can be brave. I can work, and I can play. I can breathe, and I can try.”

And the butterflies? They were still there, but they were gentle now, like tiny, friendly wings.

The End.
