# Benny's Big Kitchen Project

Benny the beaver loved to build things. He had big orange teeth that were perfect for chewing wood. He had a flat tail that made a loud *SLAP* when he was excited. Today, Benny wanted to build something special in Mama's warm kitchen.

The kitchen smelled like fresh cookies. The yellow walls were sunny and bright. Mama was making lunch at the big wooden table. Steam rose from her soup pot, making the windows foggy.

"Mama!" said Benny, carrying a pile of wooden sticks. "I want to build a dam right here!" He pointed to the middle of the kitchen floor.

Mama looked at Benny's excited face. She saw his pile of muddy sticks. "Oh my," she said gently. "A dam would be wonderful, Benny. But look around our kitchen. What do you see?"

Benny looked around. He saw Mama's clean floor. He saw the table where they ate meals. He saw Baby Sister's high chair. "I see... a kitchen," he said slowly.

"Yes," said Mama. "And what would happen if you built a dam here?"

Benny thought hard. His whiskers twitched. "The sticks would get in the way," he said. "And the mud would make everything dirty." His tail drooped. He felt sad and disappointed.

"I really want to build something," Benny said. His voice was small and quiet.

Mama knelt down next to Benny. "I can see you're feeling sad," she said. "You really want to create something special, don't you?"

Benny nodded. "Building makes me happy. But I don't want to mess up our kitchen."

Mama smiled. "What if we found a way to do both? You could build something, and I could keep cooking. That's called a compromise."

"What's a compromise?" asked Benny.

"It's when we both give a little to make everyone happy," Mama explained. "Let me show you."

Mama got out a big cardboard box. She put it on the table. "What if you built a mini dam in here? You could use clean craft sticks instead of muddy ones."

Benny's eyes grew wide. "Really? A table dam?"

"Yes!" said Mama. "And I have blue ribbons that look like water. We could make it together."

Benny felt his excitement bubble up again. *SLAP* went his tail on the floor. "That sounds perfect!"

They worked together for an hour. Benny carefully placed each craft stick. Mama helped him weave the blue ribbons through the sticks. The kitchen filled with the sound of their happy chatter.

"Look!" said Benny. "It looks like real water flowing!"

Baby Sister clapped her hands. "Pretty!" she squealed.

"You know what, Benny?" said Mama. "This compromise worked perfectly. You got to build something amazing. I got to keep our kitchen clean. And we had fun together."

Benny hugged his mama tight. "I like compromises," he said. "They make everyone feel good."

That night at dinner, Benny's craft stick dam sat proudly on the counter. Every time he looked at it, he remembered how good it felt to find a solution that made everyone happy.

"Tomorrow," said Benny, "can we compromise again and build a bridge?"

Mama laughed. "I think we can work something out!"

The End.
