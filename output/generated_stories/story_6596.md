# Rosie Rabbit and the Porch Plan

Rosie Rabbit loved her peaceful front porch. Every morning, the porch smelled like warm wood and fresh leaves. The breeze tickled her whiskers. Birds peeped. A bee hummed. Rosie would breathe in and say, “Good morning, porch!”

One sunny day, Rosie had a plan. “I will sweep the porch, water the plants, and tidy the shoes,” she said. “I want a clean, happy porch every day.” She wiggled her nose and picked up a tiny broom.

But the broom was long, and Rosie was small. When she started to sweep, swish, the broom slid past the dust. Crunchy leaves skittered away. A little pile of crumbs blew back under the bench.

“Oh no,” said Rosie. “This is harder than I thought.” Her ears drooped.

Just then, her friend, Tilly Turtle, peeked up the steps. “Hi, Rosie,” Tilly said in her slow, warm voice. “What are you doing?”

“I’m trying to care for my porch,” said Rosie. “I want to help the earth by keeping it clean and safe. But my broom keeps slipping. I can’t get the leaves in a pile. I keep missing. I feel all wiggly.”

Tilly blinked. “When I feel wiggly,” she said, “I slow down. I make a plan. Step by step.”

Rosie took a deep breath. The porch smelled like mint from the little plant near the rail. “A plan,” she said. “I can try that.”

Rosie tapped her chin. “First, I will sweep. Then, I will pick up. Then, I will water. Every morning, I will do the same thing.” She smiled. “A routine!”

Tilly nodded. “A routine helps our bodies and minds work together.”

Rosie stood tall. “I am a determined rabbit,” she said softly to herself. “I will keep trying.”

She moved her feet apart. She held the broom with two paws. She bent her knees, just a little. Swish, swish. The broom made a soft whiskery sound on the wood. Rosie looked at where the leaves went. She used her eyes, paws, and feet together. Left paw, right paw, step, sweep. Her body felt steady. That is called coordination.

The leaves started to make a small, neat pile. “I did it!” Rosie cheered. The wind puffed, and a few leaves floated away. Rosie giggled. “Nice try, wind. I will try again.”

Swish, swish. Step, sweep. Rosie kept going. When she felt tired, she paused and wiggled her paws. “I can rest, then try again,” she told herself. “That is persistence.”

Tilly brought a little basket. “Here,” said Tilly. “Put the leaves in this. We can make leaf compost for the garden. That helps the earth.”

Rosie’s eyes shone. “We won’t throw them away! We will feed the soil.” She scooped the leaves into the basket. Crunch, crunch went the dry leaves. The sound made her smile.

Next, Rosie picked up little things on the porch. A lost button. A tiny wrapper. A shiny bottle cap. “Trash goes in the bin,” she said. “Caps can be reused.” She felt proud to care for her home and the earth.

Then Rosie turned to the plants. The mint leaves were thirsty. The green fern drooped. Rosie got her small watering can. She felt the cool metal in her paw. She poured slowly. Drip, drip, drip. The soil turned dark and smelled rich. “Not too much,” she said. “Just enough.” She checked each pot and tilted her head to listen. In her heart, she imagined the plants saying, “Thank you, Rosie.”

Her neighbor, Benny Bluebird, fluttered down to the rail. “Tweet! The porch looks neat,” he chirped. “How did you do it?”

Rosie beamed. “I made a plan. I practiced holding the broom. I used my eyes and paws together. I kept trying, even when it was hard. I have a routine now. I sweep, I pick up, I water.”

Benny bobbed. “That sounds smart. Tweet!”

Tilly smiled. “You were patient and kind to yourself.”

Rosie looked around. The porch boards were smooth and clean. The air smelled like mint and rain. The shoes were lined up like little soldiers by the door. The plants stood taller. The basket of leaves sat ready for the garden.

Rosie patted the broom. “Thank you, broom,” she said. “We make a good team.”

The next morning, Rosie did her routine again. She swept. She picked up. She watered. Some days, the wind blew extra hard. Some days, a new mess showed up. But Rosie kept going. Step by step. Swish, swish. Drip, drip. Crunch, crunch. And each day, it got a little easier.

One afternoon, a small rabbit named Pip hopped by. Pip looked worried. “I tried to help at my porch,” Pip said. “I dropped the broom. I splashed water on my toes. I want to give up.”

Rosie knelt and smiled gently. “I used to feel that way too,” she said. “Let’s try together. Hold the broom with two paws. Feet apart. Bend your knees a little. Watch where the leaves go. Step, sweep. That’s coordination.”

Pip copied Rosie. Swish! A tiny pile appeared. Pip’s eyes lit up. “I did it!”

“Yes,” said Rosie. “You kept trying. That is persistence. And we can do a little each day. That is a routine. When we care for our porches, we care for the earth too.”

Pip grinned. “I want to help every day!”

Rosie’s heart felt warm. The porch felt peaceful and bright. A soft breeze brushed her fur. The mint leaves danced. Benny sang. Tilly waved.

Rosie whispered, “Little by little, we can do big things.”

And every day after that, Rosie and her friends took care of the porch together—sweeping, picking up, watering, and smiling—step by step, never giving up.

The End.
