# Tessa Tortoise Shares the Paints

In a sunny garden, behind the flowers and buzzing bees, there was a small wooden shed. The shed smelled like fresh soil and warm wood. Inside, sunlight slipped through the window and made bright squares on the floor. Brushes, little jars, and colorful paper sat on a low shelf. This was Tessa Tortoise’s art corner.

Tessa was a wise tortoise with gentle eyes and a smooth, shiny shell. She loved to paint pictures. She painted slow and careful, one brush stroke at a time. She hummed a soft song her grandmother taught her as she worked. “Brush, brush, swish,” she sang. “Share the colors, share the wish.”

One morning, Tessa opened a jar of blue paint. “Today, I will paint the sky,” she said. She dipped her brush and made a long blue swish. Blue smelled a little like fresh rain. Next, she opened a jar of yellow. “Now the sun,” she whispered, tapping her brush and making round, warm circles.

Knock, knock! The shed door wiggled. A little rabbit peeked in. “Hi, Tessa! I’m Remy,” he said. His ears were tall and tickly, and he held a small napkin with a carrot drawing on it.

“Hello, Remy,” Tessa said with a smile. “Come in.”

Remy’s nose wiggled. “It smells nice in here. Like dirt and flowers and paint.” He looked at the brushes with big, curious eyes. “Can I paint too?”

Tessa blinked. Her paints were special. Her grandmother had given them to her. “These are my paints,” Tessa said slowly. “They are very special to me.”

Remy’s ears drooped. “Oh,” he said. “I only wanted to make a picture for my grandma. She loves carrots and sunshine.”

Tessa looked at Remy’s droopy ears. She remembered how her grandmother shared a small blue shell bead with her. “We share to show we care,” Grandma used to say. Tessa took a deep, slow breath. She listened to the soft creak of the shed and felt the warm sun on her shell.

“I want to be careful with my paints,” Tessa said. “They are part of my family’s art. But I can share. We can use them together. I will show you how.”

Remy’s ears perked up. “Really? Thank you!”

Tessa moved a little stool next to her. “Sit here. We will paint side by side.” She pushed a jar of water closer. “Step one,” she said, “dip the brush in water. Tap, tap. Not too drippy.” Tap, tap went the brush.

“Step two: ask for a color with kind words,” Tessa said. “I say, ‘Remy, may I please use the yellow when you are done?’ And you can say, ‘Yes, when I am done, I will share.’ That is how we take turns.”

Remy nodded and tried the words. “Tessa, may I please use the blue when you are done?”

“Yes, Remy,” Tessa said kindly. “When I am done, I will share.”

They painted with small, careful strokes. Remy made a carrot garden with orange dots and green tops. Tessa made a big sky with a sunny smile. The shed filled with quiet sounds: swish, swish of brushes, plink of water drops, soft humming from Tessa’s song.

Soon, the door creaked again. A little ladybug named Lila flew in and landed on a flower pot. “What a lovely smell! Is that paint?” she asked. The tiny wings made a whispery buzz.

Remy smiled. “We are painting. It feels happy.”

Lila looked shy. “I don’t have a brush,” she said softly.

Tessa tipped her head. She remembered being small and shy too. “We can share a brush,” Tessa said. “We will take turns. I paint for a little while, then Remy, then Lila. We say, ‘Your turn,’ when we finish.”

Lila’s dots seemed to sparkle. “Thank you. I would like to make tiny red hearts.”

They all smiled and began. Tessa painted a blue wave. “Your turn, Remy.”

Remy painted a green leaf. “Your turn, Lila.”

Lila dipped very gently. “Thank you,” she whispered, making tiny hearts that looked like little seeds of love.

As they worked, Tessa told a story. “My grandmother taught me to paint slowly. She also taught me to share. She said, ‘When we share, our art grows. When we share, our hearts glow.’” Tessa felt warm inside as she said the words.

Remy nodded. “When you shared, I felt brave.”

Lila added, “When you shared, I felt seen.”

Tessa looked at their papers. Remy’s carrots had bright green tops. Lila’s hearts danced like ladybug spots. Tessa’s sky held a big, friendly sun. They smiled at the colors together. The shed seemed brighter, and the air felt soft and kind.

Then Tessa had an idea. “Let’s make one big picture,” she said. “We will each add something. We will share space and share time.”

Remy clapped his paws. “Yes!”

Lila buzzed happily. “Yes!”

They taped a big paper to the wall. Tessa painted a wide blue sky. Remy made a tall carrot house with a tiny door. Lila dotted the sky with red heart-clouds. They paused when someone needed a color. They waited with patience. They used kind words each time.

“May I please have the red?”

“Yes, when I am done.”

“Your turn.”

“Thank you.”

The shed filled with kindness, like warm light. When they finished, they stepped back. The painting showed a sunny garden, a carrot house, and heart-clouds that rained happy drops. It looked beautiful.

Tessa felt her heart glow. “We shared our paints. We shared our time. We shared our ideas. Look what we made together.”

Remy hugged the air, so excited. “Sharing makes art better!”

Lila fluttered. “Sharing makes friends.”

Tessa smiled and hummed her grandmother’s song. “Share the colors, share the wish,” she sang softly. “We care when we share.”

They cleaned the brushes, tap, tap, and put the lids on jars, twist, twist. The shed felt calm and tidy again. They hung the big picture on the wall, where the sun could kiss it.

Tessa looked at her new friends. “Today we learned to share. Sharing shows love. Sharing helps us make something wonderful together.”

Remy and Lila nodded. “We will remember,” they said.

Outside, the bees buzzed, and a warm breeze slipped through the door. Inside the little garden shed, three friends smiled at their art, and at each other.

The End.
