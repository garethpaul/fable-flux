# Fawn’s Pantry Peek-and-Listen

In a quiet cottage at the edge of the woods, a gentle deer fawn named Fern tiptoed through the back door. The air smelled like warm bread, sweet berries, and a little bit like cinnamon. Fern’s soft hooves went clip-clip on the cool floor as she peeked into a small, cozy room.

“This must be the pantry,” Fern whispered. The pantry felt like a tiny treasure cave. Shelves stood tall, holding jars and boxes and baskets. Little dust sparkles danced in a sunbeam. Somewhere, a drip went plip… plip… from a leaky faucet in the sink nearby.

Fern’s velvety ears twitched. “I’m brave,” she told herself. “I can explore.” Her tummy gave a tiny rumbly growl. “And maybe I can share a snack with a friend,” she added, remembering how sharing made her heart feel warm.

On the lowest shelf, Fern saw a basket of pears, green and shiny. On the middle shelf, she saw a jar of oats. On the highest shelf, way up high, sat a jar of golden honey. It glowed in the sunbeam like a little sun in a jar.

Fern stretched her neck. “Ooo, honey,” she said softly. “Just a little taste?” She rose onto her tiptoes. The shelf creaked. She wobbled. Her heart fluttered fast, like a small bird.

“Fern?” came a gentle voice. It was Nana Dove, the kind bird who kept the cottage pantry tidy. Her soft gray feathers brushed the doorframe as she peeked in. “What are you doing, dear?”

Fern froze. Her ears drooped. “I… I wanted to try the honey,” she said. “I didn’t mean to wobble the shelf.”

Nana Dove tilted her head. “I understand you’re curious,” she cooed. “Did you hear my rules about the pantry?”

Fern blinked. “Rules?”

Nana Dove fluffed her feathers. “Yes, sweet Fern. We have safe steps in here. We look with our eyes first. We ask for help with high things. We listen to directions so everyone stays safe.” Her voice was warm like toast.

Fern took a little breath. The air tasted like oats and sugar. “I didn’t listen,” she admitted. “I forgot to ask.”

“That was a brave truth,” said Nana Dove, smiling. “Listening helps our ears keep us safe, and it helps our hearts be kind. Would you like to try again?”

Fern nodded. “Yes, please. I can listen.”

Nana Dove pointed her wing. “First, we pick a snack from the low shelf. Next, we ask before we touch. And we share with friends.”

Fern’s ears perked up. “I can do that.” She looked at the low shelf. The pears shone softly. Next to them was a small bag of crunchy crackers. Fern’s tummy gave another rumbly growl. “May I choose pears and crackers?”

“You may,” said Nana Dove. “Good listening.”

Fern carefully picked two pears and a handful of crackers. She set them on a little plate. “May I have a tiny taste of honey too, if you help me?”

Nana Dove nodded. “Yes. I will help.” She fluttered up, lifted the honey jar gently, and set it on the counter. The lid made a soft pop. The room filled with a sweet, sunny smell. “Just a drizzle,” she said.

Fern watched closely. “Just a drizzle,” she repeated, listening with both ears. The honey made a golden ribbon on the pears. It sparkled in the light. Fern’s eyes went wide. “It looks like sunshine.”

“Now,” Nana Dove said, “who will you share with?”

Fern looked toward the door. “My friend Pip the field mouse is outside,” she said. “He likes crunchy things.”

“Let’s invite him,” Nana Dove smiled.

Fern trotted to the door and called, “Pip! Snack time!” A tiny nose peeked in. Pip’s whiskers trembled. “It smells yummy,” he squeaked.

Fern held the plate. “We listened to the pantry rules. We asked for help. And now we share.”

Pip’s eyes shone. “That’s kind. May I have a cracker?”

Fern nodded. “Yes, and a pear slice with honey.”

They sat on a little rug by the pantry door. Fern took a small bite. The pear was cool and juicy. The honey felt warm and silky. Crunch, crunch went the cracker. Pip made a happy squeak. Nana Dove sipped a tiny cup of tea and watched with soft eyes.

“I was a little scared of the high shelf,” Fern said between bites. “My knees felt wiggly.”

“That’s okay,” Pip said. “You were brave to say ‘I need help.’”

“And brave to listen,” added Nana Dove. “Listening is courage too. It means you care.”

Fern’s ears lifted. She felt a glow inside, like the honey glow. “Listening keeps me safe,” she said. “Listening helps me share. Listening helps me be brave.”

When the snack was done, Fern licked a sticky drop from her hoof. “Thank you for helping, Nana Dove,” she said. “Next time I will look with my eyes, ask with my words, and listen with my ears.”

Nana Dove nodded. “And your kind heart will guide you.”

Fern helped tidy the plate and put the lid back on the honey. Pop! The pantry felt neat and calm. The sunbeam had moved, and dust sparkles danced in a new spot.

“Let’s go play,” Fern told Pip. “We can practice listening outside too. We can take turns and share the red ball.”

Pip grinned. “I’ll listen for your turn, and you listen for mine.”

Fern’s tail gave a happy flick. “Deal!”

They scampered out, brave and careful, with listening ears and sharing hearts, ready for a sunny day.

The End.
