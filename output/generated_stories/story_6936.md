# Gigi the Giraffe’s Pantry Plan

Gigi the giraffe was tall, kind, and very thoughtful. She loved helping her friends and she loved snacks. One sunny morning, Gigi stood in her pantry. The pantry smelled like cinnamon and fresh oats. Jars clicked softly. Paper bags rustled. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor.

Gigi peeked at the clock. “Tick-tock,” it sang. “I promised to bring fruit crunch to the park at ten,” Gigi said. “But where is everything?”

She opened one shelf. Crackle! A box of cereal leaned, then slid. “Oops!” Gigi giggled. “Careful there.” She reached her long neck and caught it with her nose.

On another shelf, the raisins danced away from the oats. The spoons hid under napkins. The honey sat way up high, sticky and shy. “Oh my,” said Gigi. “My pantry is messy. My snacks play hide-and-seek!”

Gigi felt a wiggly worry in her tummy. The clock went, “Tick-tock, tick-tock.” She looked at the door. She looked at the shelves. “I want to be on time,” she whispered, “but I can’t find what I need.”

She took a deep breath. In. Out. The cinnamon smell made her smile. “I can do this,” she said. “I have a plan.” Gigi liked plans. Plans helped her feel calm.

She talked to herself, softly and sweetly. “First, I will make a list. Next, I will set a little timer. Then, I will put things in easy places. Last, I will make the snack.”

Gigi found a chalkboard on the wall. Scritch-scratch, her chalk wrote:
- Oats
- Raisins
- Honey
- Spoons
- Bowls

She set a tiny timer shaped like a berry. Beep! “Ten minutes to find. Ten minutes to sort. Ten minutes to make,” she said. “I can do it step by step.”

“Step one: find.” Gigi sang a little finding song. “Oats, oats, where do you hide? Come out, come out, I say with pride.” She stretched her long neck. Her ears twitched. Her eyes scanned gently. She spotted a bag with a blue ribbon. “Oats!” she cheered.

“Raisins?” she asked. She sniffed the air. Sweet and wrinkly smells drifted from a red tin. “Found you!” she laughed.

“Honey,” Gigi called. The honey jar glowed golden on the top shelf. Gigi reached. A little too far. “Wobble, wobble,” she said. She stepped back and used her stool. Step, step. “I can try a new way,” she told herself. She climbed carefully. “Got it!” Sticky sweetness tickled her nose.

“Step two: sort.” Beep! went the timer. Gigi pressed it again. She made little baskets. She put stickers on them: a sun for breakfast things, a moon for bedtime tea, a star for snacks. She placed the oats with the sun. The raisins with the star. The honey with the star and the sun. “You can share,” she told the honey. “We can all share.”

She slid the spoons into a bright yellow cup and set it by the bowls. “Now I know where you live,” she said kindly. The pantry looked neat. The shelves smiled. The rustle turned quiet. The clock sounded friendly now.

“Step three: make.” Beep! went the timer again. Gigi poured oats. Plip, plip. She sprinkled raisins. Pat-pat. She drizzled honey. Drip, drip, drip. She stirred with a gentle clink. The crunch smelled warm and sweet. Gigi tasted a tiny spoon. “Mmm!” Her eyes sparkled.

A knock came. Tap-tap. It was Tilda the turtle and Benny the bunny at the door. “Are you ready for the park?” asked Benny, bouncing.

Gigi looked at the clock. “I am on time!” she said, proud and calm. She packed the fruit crunch into little boxes. She put napkins on top. She placed everything in a basket by the door. “All set,” Gigi said.

At the park, leaves whispered in the breeze. Children laughed. Tilda took a bite. Crunch! “This is tasty,” she smiled. “And you were right on time!”

Gigi blushed, happy. “I used a plan,” she said. “I made a list. I set a timer. I put things in easy places. I worked step by step. When it was hard, I tried a new way.”

Benny nodded. “That’s smart. Can you show me?”

Back home, Gigi showed her pantry. “See the baskets? See the stickers?” Benny and Tilda clapped. Gigi made a new list: wash bowls, close jars, put basket by the door for tomorrow. She set the timer for five minutes. Beep! They moved like a team. Swish, click, tap-tap. Soon, everything was tidy again.

Gigi felt light as a leaf. “When I manage my time and prepare,” she said, “I feel calm. I can be independent and kind.”

On the way back to the park, Gigi hummed. The clock went, “Tick-tock,” but it sounded like music now. She smiled. “I can make a plan any time,” she told herself. “If I forget, I can try again. I can grow and learn.”

And the sun kept shining, warm and bright.

The End.
