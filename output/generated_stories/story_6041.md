# Sunny Bird’s Practice Song

Sunny was a small singing bird with bright yellow feathers. She lived near a musical practice room where children learned to play instruments. Every morning, Sunny perched on the window ledge and peeped inside. The room had shiny drums, a green piano, and shelves of music books. The air smelled like clean wood and lemon polish. Sunlight made dust sparkles dance in the air.

Sunny loved to sing. Her voice was sweet and clear. But lately, her songs sounded tired and squeaky. She felt worried. “Why does my song wobble?” she chirped.

Coach Melody, the music teacher, opened the window a little. A soft breeze came in. “Good morning, Sunny,” she said kindly. “You sound a bit scratchy today.”

Sunny blinked. “I want to sing strong and bright,” she said. “But my song gets squeaky.”

Coach Melody nodded. “Let’s listen and learn,” she said. “We can try a routine.”

Sunny tilted her head. “What is a routine?”

“A routine is a plan we do the same way each day,” Coach Melody said. “It helps our bodies and brains. It helps our voices, too.”

Sunny fluffed her feathers. “I can try!”

Coach Melody clapped a gentle rhythm. Tap tap—pause—tap tap. “First, we warm up the body,” she said. “A warm body helps a strong voice.”

Sunny stretched her wings wide. She rolled her tiny shoulders. She hopped in place—hop, hop, hop. Her feet made soft pat-pat sounds on the window ledge.

“Good exercise,” Coach Melody smiled. “Now breathe in slowly like you are smelling a flower.” Sunny breathed in. The air smelled like lemon and a tiny hint of dust.

“Now breathe out like you are blowing bubbles,” Coach Melody said.

“Poooo,” Sunny breathed. Her chest felt calm.

“Next, humming,” Coach Melody said. “Mmmmmm.” The piano hummed with her.

Sunny hummed too. “Mmmmmm.” It tickled her beak in a nice way.

But just then, Sunny noticed the floor. Candy wrappers and tiny paper bits were under the chairs. A plastic bottle rolled and clinked near the drums.

Sunny’s eyes went wide. “Oh! The room is messy.”

Coach Melody sighed. “You are right. We forgot our clean-up routine after class.”

Sunny listened to the little clink-clink of the bottle. It made her song feel wobbly again. “The sounds and smells bother me,” she chirped. “I can’t focus.”

Coach Melody nodded. “Listening is part of learning. You noticed a problem.” She smiled. “Let’s solve it with our routine.”

Sunny puffed up with brave energy. “I can help!”

They made a simple plan:
- Exercise and stretch
- Breathe and hum
- Quick clean-up
- Practice singing
- Quiet rest and drink water

“Let’s try the clean-up step,” Coach Melody said.

Sunny fluttered down onto a drum stool. She pecked gently at the rolling bottle and nudged it toward a blue recycling bin. Clink! It fell in. “Recycling helps the Earth,” Sunny chirped proudly.

Coach Melody picked up paper bits and placed them in the trash. She gathered wrappers and wiped the piano keys with a soft cloth. Swish, swish. “The Earth is our home,” she said. “We care for it by sorting trash and keeping spaces clean.”

Sunny found a small leaf that had blown in. She carried it to the compost bin near a plant. “For the soil,” she sang.

Soon the room looked neat and bright. The floor was clear. The air smelled fresh again.

Sunny breathed in long and slow. “Ahhh,” she sighed. Her chest felt big and light.

“Now back to the routine,” Coach Melody said. “Exercise, breathe, hum, then sing.”

Sunny stretched again—hop, hop, hop. She breathed in like a flower, out like bubbles. “Mmmmmm,” she hummed, soft and smooth. Then she opened her beak.

“La-la-laaaa!” Her song floated like a ribbon. It sounded clear, not squeaky. The piano chimed gently with her. Ding-ding-ding.

Sunny smiled with her eyes. “I did it!”

Coach Melody clapped softly. “You followed a routine. You listened to your body. You helped the Earth. You solved a problem.”

Sunny felt warm and proud. “I will do this every day,” she said. “First I stretch and breathe. Then I clean and recycle. Then I sing and rest.”

She fluttered up to the window ledge again. The sun touched her feathers, and they glowed like buttercup petals. She took a sip of water from a little cup Coach Melody placed by the window. The water was cool and sweet.

A gentle hush filled the room. Sunny listened. The quiet made space for her song. She sang again, softer this time. “La, la, la,” she murmured like a lullaby. It felt easy. It felt kind.

Before she flew home, Sunny looked around the tidy practice room. “Thank you, room,” she chirped. “We will keep you clean.”

Coach Melody waved. “See you tomorrow, Sunny. Same routine!”

Sunny nodded. “Same routine,” she echoed. “It helps me grow.”

That night, Sunny dreamed of clean floors, soft breaths, kind humming, and bright songs. In her dream, the Earth smiled.

The next morning, Sunny returned right on time. Hop, hop, hop. Breathe in, breathe out. Hum, clean, sing, rest. Her voice rang clear and happy.

Sunny learned that a simple routine can make hard things easier. It helps our bodies, our songs, and our Earth.

The End.
