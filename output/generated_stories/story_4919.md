# Buzzy Bubbles and Brave Feelings

Bear Cub Benny padded into the bathroom on soft paws. The shiny floor felt cool under his feet. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. The room smelled like minty soap and warm towels.

“I’m curious,” Benny said, tilting his head. “What makes bubbles so bubbly?”

He turned the shiny handle. Water hissed and whooshed. Steam rose like a tiny cloud. Benny giggled. “Hello, little cloud!”

He squeezed a bottle of bubble soap. Glop! A ribbon of soap slid into the water. Soon, the tub bloomed with big, round bubbles. They popped with gentle puffs. Pop. Pop. Pop.

Benny’s nose twitched. “Mmm, they smell like lemons!” He dipped his paw. “Ooh! Slippery and squishy.”

He hummed a song. “Buzzy bubbles, up you go—”

Suddenly, the bubbles grew and grew. They stacked like fluffy mountains. The water climbed, too. It sloshed over the edge. Splish—splash! A puddle spread across the floor.

Benny’s tummy felt twisty. His ears felt hot. “Oh,” he whispered. “I think I made a big mess.”

He looked at the door. He looked at the puddle. He looked at the bottle. His heart thump-thump-thumped.

“I feel worried,” Benny said softly. “My paws are shaky. My face feels warm. That means my feelings are talking.”

He took a slow, deep breath like Mama had taught him. In through the nose. Out through the mouth. “Hoo… haa…”

“Let me name my feelings,” Benny said. “I feel scared. I also feel sorry. I was curious, and I squeezed too much soap.”

Benny sat on the bath mat. It felt fluffy under his bottom. The water glimmered like tiny stars. He listened again. Drip, drip, drip. “I can think of a plan,” he said. “I can use calm and smart steps.”

He held up one paw. “Step one: Turn off the water.” Click! The hiss stopped.

He held up the second paw. “Step two: Make it safe.” He slid the mat closer to the puddle so he wouldn’t slip.

He held up a third paw. “Step three: Ask for help and tell the truth.”

Benny opened the door and called, “Mama Bear? I need you, please.”

Mama Bear came with soft steps. She smelled like cinnamon and hugs. She looked at Benny, then at the puddle and the bubble mountain. “Oh my,” she said, with kind eyes. “What happened here, little cub?”

Benny’s voice wobbled. “I was curious. I wanted to see big bubbles.” He took another deep breath. “I made a mess. I feel scared and sorry. I turned off the water. Can you help me clean, please?”

Mama knelt and touched Benny’s paw. “Thank you for telling the truth,” she said. “That was very brave. I can see you feel worried. Your eyes look shiny. Let’s breathe together.”

They breathed in and out. Hoo… haa…

“I feel calmer,” Benny said. “My tummy is not so twisty now.”

“That’s emotional-awareness,” Mama said. “You noticed your feelings. You named them. You used them to make a smart plan.”

Benny’s ears perked. “Emo… emo-tional aware-ness?”

Mama smiled. “It means paying attention to your feelings, just like you listen to the drip, drip, drip. Feelings help us learn.”

Benny nodded. “I like learning.” He looked at the towels. “Step four: Clean up.”

Together, they laid towels on the puddle. Squish! The towels drank the water. Benny patted them with his paws. “Slurp, slurp,” he said, pretending the towels were thirsty whales.

Mama chuckled. “Good work, Helper Cub.”

They scooped the extra bubbles into a bucket. Pop! Pop! Bubbles tickled Benny’s nose. “Hee-hee!” he giggled. “The bubbles kiss my whiskers!”

When the floor was dry and the tub was safe, Mama pointed to the soap bottle. “What did we learn about bubbles?”

Benny tapped his chin. “We learned: Too much soap makes too many bubbles. Also, water rises when bubbles build. That can make a mess.”

Mama nodded. “How can we do it better next time?”

Benny thought hard. “Logical-thinking time!” he said. “One small squeeze. Ask a grown-up to watch. Keep a towel ready. And turn off the water sooner.”

“Excellent plan,” Mama said. “And what about feelings?”

Benny smiled. “When I feel twisty, I can stop, breathe, and name my feeling. I can tell the truth, even if I’m scared.”

Mama hugged him. Her fur felt warm and soft. “That is honesty and courage,” she said. “I’m proud of you.”

Benny’s heart felt light, like a little soap bubble floating up. “I’m proud of me, too.”

Later, Mama turned the water back on, just a little. Benny squeezed a tiny dot of soap. Drip, drop. Glop! Small, gentle bubbles bloomed. They smelled like lemons again.

“Hello, little bubbles,” Benny said. “Let’s play safe.”

He splashed with careful paws. He made a bubble hat. He made a bubble beard. “I am Captain Bubbly!” he boomed, and Mama laughed.

Benny looked at Mama. “I feel happy,” he said. “My body feels light. My cheeks feel smiley. My heart feels calm.”

Mama winked. “That is the feeling of doing the right thing.”

Benny nodded. “Today I learned: Feelings are helpers. Honesty is brave. Curiosity is good when I use smart steps.”

He blew one tiny bubble into the air. It floated, then softly popped.

“Pop!” Benny said. “And that’s okay.”

The End.
