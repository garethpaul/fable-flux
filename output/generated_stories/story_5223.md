# Mousey’s Quiet Porch Plan

On a warm, soft morning, a kind mouse named Mousey sat on a peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like sweet flowers. The wooden steps felt smooth and warm under Mousey’s tiny feet. A gentle breeze kissed his whiskers. Birds sang, “Chee-chee-chee,” and the wind chimes whispered, “ting, ting, ting.”

Mousey took a slow breath in and out. “Ah,” he sighed. “This porch is a calm place.” He sipped cool water from a little cup and watched a cloud float by. It looked like a fluffy sheep.

Just then, Mousey heard tiny feet. Tip-tap, tip-tap. His friend, Cricket, hopped onto the porch. “Hi, Mousey!” chirped Cricket. “I lost my shiny pebble, and I feel all wiggly inside!”

Mousey put his paws on his tummy and nodded. “Let’s try something calm before we look,” he said kindly. “We can breathe together.”

Cricket blinked. “Breathe?”

“Yes,” said Mousey. “Smell the flower.” Mousey breathed in slowly through his nose. “And blow out a candle.” He blew out gently, like a soft puff. “Let’s do it three times.”

Cricket copied him. In… and out. In… and out. In… and out.

Cricket’s shoulders softened. “I feel better,” he chirped. “Thank you. Can you help me find my pebble?”

“Of course,” said Mousey. “Let’s use calm eyes and calm ears.” He cupped his large round ears. “What do we hear?”

They listened. The porch creaked a tiny “cree-eak.” The chimes went “ting.” A bee buzzed, “bzzz,” near a flower pot.

“I hear the bee,” said Cricket.

“I hear our calm,” said Mousey with a smile. “Now, let’s talk about a plan.”

They sat side by side on a soft porch mat. Mousey drew a little map with a twig in a patch of dust. “We’ll look under the chair, by the boots, and near the flower pots,” he said. “We will move slowly. We will use our listening ears. If we feel wiggly, we will breathe.”

Cricket nodded. “Good plan.”

They started under the rocking chair. The chair moved slowly, creak, creak, creak. “Hello, chair,” Mousey whispered. “We are looking for a pebble.” He peeked. No pebble, just a curl of dry leaf.

By the boots, they found a feather. “Soft!” said Cricket, rubbing it on his cheek. It tickled. He giggled and then took a calm breath. “Still no pebble.”

Near the flower pots, they smelled dirt, warm and rich. Mousey brushed away a bit of soil. “Hello, worms,” he said gently. The worms wiggled. “We will not bother you.” He listened. The bee buzzed away. The porch felt extra quiet now.

Suddenly, a squirrel clattered onto the porch railing. “Clack-clack!” He dropped an acorn, and it rolled. “Sorry! I was in a hurry,” he said, tail flicking fast.

Mousey smiled and put a paw on his heart. “It’s okay. Would you like to be calm with us?”

The squirrel tilted his head. “Calm? How?”

“Smell the flower, blow the candle,” Cricket chirped, showing him. All three breathed in and out together. The squirrel’s tail slowed and relaxed. “Oh,” he said softly. “That feels nice.”

Mousey nodded. “When we are calm, our brains can think and our eyes can see.”

The squirrel looked around with new, gentle eyes. “What are you looking for?”

“A shiny pebble,” said Cricket.

The squirrel tapped his chin. “I saw something shiny by the step.” He pointed to the bottom step with a tiny claw.

They walked there together. The wood felt warm. The bottom step had a teeny crack. Mousey crouched down and peered in. Something sparkled, like a tiny star.

“I see it!” whispered Mousey. “Cricket, can you talk me through it?”

Cricket took a steady breath. “Okay. You can slide the twig in. Move slow. Keep breathing, Mousey.”

Mousey slid the twig. He breathed in and out, in and out. He nudged the shiny thing. It wiggled closer, closer, and then—plink!—the pebble popped out onto the porch.

“My pebble!” Cricket chirped, hopping with joy. He held it up. It glowed in the sunlight like a drop of water. “Thank you, Mousey. Thank you, Squirrel.”

“You did it,” said Mousey. “You used calm breaths. You talked about a plan. You listened. You were mindful.”

“Mindful?” asked the squirrel.

“It means we pay attention to right now,” said Mousey. “We feel our feet on the porch. We hear the chimes. We see the sunlight. We breathe. When we are mindful, we feel safe and strong.”

Cricket put the pebble in a tiny pouch. “I feel safe,” he said. He touched his tummy. “It’s quiet in here now.”

The squirrel smiled. “I’m going to breathe before I rush,” he said. “Smell the flower, blow the candle.”

Mousey looked at his friends. The breeze was soft. The chimes sang. The porch smelled like flowers and cool water. “Our porch plan worked,” he said.

“It did,” said Cricket. “Calm helps.”

Mousey nodded. “When we feel wiggly or worried, we can stop, breathe, and talk. We can make a plan. We can listen. Calm helps our hearts, our minds, and our friends.”

They sat together on the peaceful front porch, sharing a bowl of tiny blueberries. Pop! Pop! Sweet juice burst on their tongues. They smiled at the sky.

The End.
