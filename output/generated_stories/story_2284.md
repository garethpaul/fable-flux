# Miko's Pantry Adventure

Miko the monkey loved exploring, and today he discovered something amazing - the big pantry in his house! The pantry smelled like sweet apples and crunchy crackers. Colorful boxes and jars lined the tall shelves from floor to ceiling.

"Wow!" Miko said, his brown eyes growing wide. "So many yummy things!"

As Miko reached for a jar of peanut butter on a high shelf, he heard tiny voices squeaking below.

"Help! Help!" called the voices.

Miko looked down and saw three little field mice trying to reach a box of oats that had fallen behind a heavy bag of rice.

"We've been trying to move this bag for hours," puffed the smallest mouse, whose whiskers twitched with worry. "Our baby mice are hungry, but we can't reach our food!"

Miko wanted to help right away, but he also really wanted that peanut butter. His tummy was rumbling loudly - "GRUMBLE, GRUMBLE!"

Then Miko had a smart idea. "I know! Let's take turns helping each other. First, I'll help you get your oats. Then, maybe you can help me reach the peanut butter jar?"

"That sounds fair!" squeaked the mother mouse gratefully.

Miko used his strong monkey arms to push the heavy rice bag. "One, two, THREE!" he grunted. The bag slid across the smooth pantry floor with a soft "whoosh" sound.

"Hooray!" cheered the mice as they scurried to their oats. "Thank you, Miko!"

Now it was the mice's turn to help. They thought carefully about how three tiny mice could help one big monkey.

"I have an idea!" said the father mouse. "We can't reach high places, but we're great at finding things!"

The clever mice scattered around the pantry, their little paws pattering on the wooden shelves. Soon they found a step stool hidden behind some soup cans.

"Look what we found!" they called together.

Miko climbed onto the sturdy stool and easily reached the peanut butter jar. "Perfect! Thank you, my new friends!"

As they all enjoyed their snacks, Miko noticed the mice carefully putting their oat crumbs in a small container.

"We don't like to waste food," explained the mother mouse. "We save every bit to help our earth stay clean and healthy."

"That's very smart," said Miko, making sure to catch his peanut butter crumbs too. "I want to take care of our earth as well!"

The smallest mouse looked up at Miko with sparkling eyes. "Even though we're different sizes and like different foods, we make a great team!"

"Yes!" agreed Miko, his tail swishing happily. "When we take turns and help each other, everyone gets what they need."

From that day on, whenever Miko visited the pantry, he always checked if his mouse friends needed help first. And the mice always helped Miko reach high places when it was his turn.

The pantry became filled with the sounds of friendship - gentle squeaks, happy monkey chatter, and the soft sounds of friends sharing and caring for each other.

"Taking turns makes everything more fun!" Miko would tell anyone who would listen. "And it feels so good to help others!"

The End.
