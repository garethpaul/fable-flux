# Oliver Owl's Dancing Lesson

Oliver the wise old owl sat high in the big oak tree, watching the sunny backyard below. His soft brown feathers rustled in the warm breeze as he spotted something that made him worry.

Little Lily Rabbit was spinning and twirling on the wooden deck, her pink tutu flowing around her. But she was dancing very close to the edge where the steps went down to the garden.

"Whoo-whoo!" called Oliver gently. "Hello there, little dancer!"

Lily stopped spinning and looked up with bright eyes. "Hi, Mr. Oliver! Look at my new dance moves!" She began to leap and spin again, getting closer and closer to the steps.

Oliver's wise old heart felt concerned. He spread his wings and glided down to a low branch near the deck. "Your dancing is beautiful, Lily! But I notice you're very close to those steps. That looks a bit dangerous."

"I'm fine!" Lily said, doing another big spin. "I'm a great dancer! Watch this!" She jumped high in the air, landing just inches from the edge.

Oliver's big round eyes grew wider. "Oh my! Lily, I understand you love to dance, and you are very talented. But even the best dancers need to think about safety first."

Lily stopped and tilted her head. "Safety? But dancing is fun!"

"Dancing IS fun," Oliver said with a warm hoot. "That's exactly why we want to keep you safe so you can dance for many, many years! Let me show you something."

The wise owl flew down to the middle of the deck, away from the steps. "See this spot here? This is a perfect dancing space. You have room all around you, and there's nothing to trip over or fall from."

Lily hopped over to where Oliver was standing. "But there's less room here."

"Ah," said Oliver, "but watch this!" He began to move his wings gracefully, showing Lily how to dance in the safe space. "When we practice in a safe spot, we can focus on making our moves even more beautiful. We don't have to worry about falling."

Lily's eyes sparkled with understanding. "Oh! So I can practice my spins without looking around for danger?"

"Exactly!" Oliver hooted happily. "The best dancers always choose safe spaces to practice. It shows they respect themselves and their art."

Lily began to dance in the middle of the deck. She spun and leaped and twirled, her movements flowing like water. "This feels so much better! I can really focus on my dancing!"

Oliver clapped his wings together. "Beautiful! See how much more graceful you look when you're not worried about the edge?"

"Thank you, Mr. Oliver," Lily said, giving a little bow. "I understand now. Safety first, then dancing!"

"That's right, little one," Oliver said, his voice full of pride. "And remember, asking for help or advice doesn't mean you're not good at something. It means you're smart and want to get even better!"

From that day on, Lily always chose safe spaces for her dancing practice. She became an even more wonderful dancer because she could focus all her energy on her beautiful moves instead of worrying about getting hurt.

And Oliver? He loved watching her dance safely in the sunny backyard, knowing that wisdom and fun could go hand in hand.

The End.
