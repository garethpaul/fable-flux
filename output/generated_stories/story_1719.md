# Ruby's Rooftop Song

High up on a red brick rooftop lived a little bird named Ruby. She had bright orange feathers and the biggest dream in the whole wide world - she wanted to sing the most beautiful song ever!

Every morning, Ruby would hop to the edge of the rooftop and try to sing. "Tweet-tweet-SQUAWK!" came out instead of the pretty melody she heard in her head. The sound was so loud and scratchy that it made the other birds cover their ears.

"Oh no!" Ruby sighed, her little orange head drooping down. "My song doesn't sound nice at all."

From the next rooftop over, an old wise crow named Chester heard Ruby's troubles. "Good morning, little one," he called gently. "I heard you trying to sing. Would you like some help?"

Ruby felt embarrassed. "My voice is too loud and squawky. I'll never sing beautifully like the other birds."

Chester flew over and perched beside her. "Ruby, every great singer started just like you. Even I had to practice every single day to learn my songs."

"Really?" Ruby's bright black eyes grew wide with surprise.

"Oh yes! Listen to this," Chester said. He began to sing a deep, rich melody that floated across the rooftops like warm honey. "But when I was young, I sounded like this - CAW-CAW-SCREECH!"

Ruby giggled at the funny sound. "That did sound pretty silly!"

"The secret," Chester explained, "is practice, practice, practice. And being patient with yourself while you learn."

From that day on, Ruby practiced every morning. Chester taught her to take deep breaths and start with quiet, gentle sounds. "Breathe in slowly," he would say. "Now let the air flow out like a soft breeze."

At first, Ruby's songs were still a bit wobbly. Some days she felt frustrated and wanted to give up. But Chester reminded her, "Every day you practice, you get a little bit better. Be kind to yourself, little Ruby."

Soon, other birds began to gather on nearby rooftops to listen. There was Pip the sparrow, Luna the dove, and even a family of chatty robins. They all encouraged Ruby with gentle chirps and happy wing-flutters.

"We love hearing you practice!" chirped Pip. "Your song gets prettier every day!"

Ruby learned that the other birds weren't laughing at her mistakes - they were cheering her on! She felt warm and happy knowing she belonged with this kind group of feathered friends.

After many weeks of daily practice, something magical happened. Ruby opened her beak and out came the most beautiful, clear melody anyone had ever heard. It was sweet like morning sunshine and gentle like a summer breeze.

All the birds joined in with their own voices, creating a wonderful rooftop chorus. The music was so lovely that people stopped on the sidewalk below to listen and smile.

"Ruby," said Chester proudly, "your patience and practice have paid off. But remember, even the best singers keep practicing every day."

Ruby nodded happily. "I will! And Chester, thank you for being so kind and helping me learn. Now I know that with practice and good friends, I can do anything!"

From that day forward, Ruby sang her beautiful song every morning, and the rooftop became a place where all birds felt welcome to practice and learn together.

The End.
