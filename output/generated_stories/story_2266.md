# Milo the Helper Monkey

In a cozy garden behind a yellow house, there stood a wooden shed filled with wonderful smells of soil and flowers. This was where Milo the monkey loved to play. Milo had soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes that sparkled when he smiled.

One sunny morning, Milo swung down from his favorite apple tree and heard something unusual coming from the garden shed. *Squeak, squeak, scratch, scratch!* 

"Oh my!" said Milo, his ears perking up. "Someone sounds upset in there!"

Milo pushed open the creaky wooden door and saw three little animals huddled in different corners of the shed. A tiny gray mouse was squeaking loudly, a green frog was croaking with worry, and a brown rabbit was thumping his foot on the dusty floor.

"What's wrong, friends?" asked Milo in his kindest voice.

The mouse squeaked, "The frog took my favorite hiding spot behind the flower pots!"

The frog croaked back, "But the rabbit scared me away from my lily pad outside!"

The rabbit thumped again, "I was just trying to find a quiet place to rest, but everyone keeps making noise!"

Milo scratched his head thoughtfully. The shed smelled like fresh dirt and old wood, and sunlight streamed through the small window, making dust sparkle in the air. He could see that all three animals looked sad and frustrated.

"I think I can help," said Milo with a gentle smile. "But first, let's all sit down together and talk."

The three animals looked at each other uncertainly, but Milo's warm voice made them feel safe. They gathered in a circle on an old garden mat that smelled like lavender.

"Mouse, can you tell us why that spot is special to you?" asked Milo.

The little mouse's whiskers twitched. "It's warm and cozy, and I can see the pretty flowers."

"And Frog, why do you need a quiet place?" Milo continued.

"I get scared easily," admitted the frog, his green skin glistening. "When I'm frightened, I need somewhere safe to hide."

"Rabbit, what about you?" asked Milo kindly.

The rabbit's ears drooped. "I just wanted to take a nap. I didn't mean to scare anyone."

Milo clapped his hands together. "I have an idea! What if we make this shed a special sharing place for everyone?"

The animals looked curious as Milo pointed around the shed. "Mouse, you could have the cozy spot behind the flower pots in the morning when the sun makes it extra warm. Frog, you could use it in the afternoon when you need quiet time. And Rabbit, the soft pile of garden blankets over there would be perfect for naps!"

"But what if we need our spots at the same time?" asked the mouse.

"Then we can talk about it, just like we're doing now," said Milo. "We can take turns and help each other. That's what good neighbors do!"

The frog hopped excitedly. "I could help Mouse reach the high shelves!"

"And I could keep watch while Rabbit naps," squeaked the mouse.

"And I could share my vegetables with everyone!" said the rabbit, his cotton tail wiggling with joy.

From that day on, the garden shed became the happiest place in the whole garden. The four friends learned that when they talked kindly to each other and shared, everyone could be happy together.

And whenever other animals had problems, they knew they could come to the shed and find Milo the helper monkey, who would always listen with his heart and help them find a way to get along.

The End.
