# Rooftop Rainbow Band

On a warm, breezy evening, a musical raccoon named Riff climbed up the ladder to the rooftop. His tail was striped, his nose was shiny, and his backpack was full of little instruments. The sky was soft orange, like peach jam, and the city hummed below.

Riff wiggled his whiskers. “Tonight, I make art,” he said softly. “Art with music. Art with color. Art to share!”

He opened his backpack. Out came tiny bells, a small drum, a wooden flute, and a tin paintbox with bright, safe roof-chalks. He spread them on a cozy blanket. The blanket was squishy and smelled like sunshine.

Riff tapped the drum. Tum-tum. He shook the bells. Ching-ching. He blew the flute. Toot-toooot! The sounds floated up, light and happy, like bubbles.

“Hello, rooftop,” Riff said. “Let’s make a sound painting.”

He took a chalk and drew a long, wavy line across the flat roof. “This is wind,” he whispered. He drew dots of pink. “These are giggles.” He drew a big yellow circle. “This is my song.”

A gentle coo came from the railing. It was Pippa the pigeon. Her feathers were gray-silver, and her eyes were bright. “Coo-coo! What are you doing, Riff?”

“I am making art,” said Riff. “I make pictures and sounds. Do you want to help?”

Pippa bobbed her head. “Coo! I like art.”

Riff smiled. “Art is for sharing,” he said. “Art is for learning, too. We can learn colors, shapes, and feelings.”

They played a little game. Riff tapped the drum—tum!—and drew a square. “Square drum,” he said. Pippa shook the bells—ching!—and drew a triangle. “Triangle bells!” she cooed. Riff blew the flute—toooot!—and drew a long blue line. “This is a note that goes far.”

The rooftop felt alive. The breeze smelled like fresh leaves and warm bricks. The sky became deeper and deeper blue. Lights blinked on in the windows, like little stars.

Then a small paw peeked over the edge. It was Mimi the mouse, carrying a tiny basket. “Hi,” squeaked Mimi. “I brought snacks. My teacher says good food helps bodies grow strong and brains think well.”

“Yay!” said Riff. “That is nutrition. Good food helps us make good art.”

Mimi opened the basket. Inside were crunchy carrot sticks, sweet apple slices, and tiny cups of water. The carrots made a snap! The apples were juicy and smelled like candy. Riff washed his paws with a wipe from his backpack. “Clean paws for art and snacks,” he said.

They sat and shared. Crunch, munch, gulp. “Thank you for sharing, Mimi,” said Pippa.

“Sharing makes everything brighter,” Riff added. “When we share, our hearts feel big.”

After the snack, Riff stood up. “Let’s paint a rooftop rainbow band,” he said. “We will use sound and color to show our feelings. Art lets feelings come out in a kind way.”

Riff tapped the drum softly. Tum. He drew a red stripe. “Red is brave,” he said. Pippa shook the bells gently. Ching. She drew an orange stripe. “Orange is friendly,” she cooed. Mimi blew across a tiny bottle to make a whoooo sound and drew a yellow stripe. “Yellow is happy,” she squeaked.

They kept going. Green was calm like leaves. Blue was peaceful like the evening sky. Purple was cozy like a hug. Each color had a sound. Each sound had a feeling.

“Listen,” Riff whispered. The wind made a shhh sound. The city made a hum. Their bells and drum and flute made the rainbow dance. The chalk felt dusty and smooth under their paws and wings. Their eyes sparkled at the bright stripes. Their hearts felt full.

Suddenly, they heard a little whimper. A raccoon kit peeked from behind a chimney. It was Tiko, Riff’s young cousin. “I want to make art too,” Tiko said, “but I don’t know how.”

Riff knelt down and smiled. “Art is for everyone, Tiko. Art does not have to be perfect. Art is trying. Art is sharing your inside feelings in a kind way.”

Tiko nodded. “Can I try the bells?”

“Yes,” said Pippa, handing them over. “I will share.”

Tiko shook the bells—jing-jing!—and drew a small pink heart. “This is love,” Tiko said.

Riff’s eyes warmed. “See? You are an artist.”

They added finishing touches together: tiny stars, dotted notes, and smiley faces. Mimi tapped the drum, Pippa drew clouds, Tiko made hearts, and Riff played the flute. The rooftop rainbow band was complete.

Riff looked at his friends. “What did we learn today?”

Mimi raised a paw. “Good food helps us make and think.”

Pippa fluffed her feathers. “Sharing makes art better.”

Tiko chimed, “Art is for everyone, even me!”

Riff nodded. “Yes. Art helps us show feelings, learn colors and shapes, and play together. When we share and take care of our bodies with healthy snacks, our art shines.”

They held paws, wings, and tiny hands and took a deep breath. The night air was cool. The chalk art glowed softly in the moonlight.

“Thank you,” Riff said. “We made beauty together.”

They packed up, left the rooftop neat and clean, and climbed down slowly. Their hearts were light, like music.

The End.
