# Penny Penguin's Big Heart

Penny Penguin waddled down the wooden stairs to the busy basement. The basement was full of wonderful things! There were colorful boxes stacked high like towers. Shiny tools hung on the walls. A warm yellow light made everything glow.

"Oh my!" said Penny, looking around with her bright orange beak. "What a busy place!"

This basement belonged to Mrs. Walrus, who fixed broken toys for all the children in the neighborhood. Every day, children brought their favorite toys that needed help.

Today, the basement was extra busy. Broken dolls sat on one table. Toy cars with missing wheels lined another. A teddy bear with a torn arm waited patiently in the corner.

Mrs. Walrus looked very tired. Her whiskers drooped down. She rubbed her flippers together slowly.

"There are so many toys to fix," Mrs. Walrus sighed. "I don't think I can help them all today."

Penny's heart felt warm and fuzzy. She wanted to help! But then she remembered something sad. Yesterday, Mrs. Walrus had been grumpy with her. Penny had accidentally knocked over a jar of buttons while trying to help.

"You're too little to help," Mrs. Walrus had said. "Please stay out of my way."

Those words had hurt Penny's feelings. She had waddled home feeling very sad.

Now, seeing Mrs. Walrus looking so tired, Penny forgot all about yesterday. Her heart told her to be kind.

"Mrs. Walrus," Penny said in her sweetest voice, "may I please help you today?"

Mrs. Walrus looked surprised. Her eyes grew wide like two big marbles.

"But Penny," she said softly, "I was not very nice to you yesterday. I said mean words."

Penny smiled her biggest smile. "That's okay! Everyone has bad days. I still want to help my friend."

Mrs. Walrus felt tears in her eyes. "You have such a generous heart, little Penny. I'm sorry I was grumpy."

"I forgive you!" Penny said, clapping her flippers together. "Now, what can I do?"

Mrs. Walrus showed Penny how to sort the buttons by color. Red buttons went in one jar. Blue buttons in another. Penny's little flippers worked carefully.

"You're doing wonderfully!" said Mrs. Walrus.

Together, they worked all morning. The basement smelled like glue and polish. They could hear the tap-tap-tap of the hammer and the whirr of the sewing machine.

Penny helped organize the screws. She passed Mrs. Walrus the right tools. She even sang happy songs while they worked.

"Your singing makes everything more fun," laughed Mrs. Walrus.

By afternoon, all the toys were fixed! The dolls had bright new dresses. The cars had shiny new wheels. The teddy bear's arm was good as new.

When the children came to pick up their toys, their faces lit up like sunshine.

"My car looks better than ever!" shouted little Tommy.

"Thank you so much!" said Sarah, hugging her doll tight.

Mrs. Walrus smiled at Penny. "We couldn't have done this without your generous heart, dear friend."

Penny felt warm and happy inside. Being generous and helping others felt better than anything in the whole world.

"Tomorrow, can we help more friends together?" asked Penny.

"Of course!" said Mrs. Walrus. "You're the best helper I could ask for."

From that day on, Penny and Mrs. Walrus worked together every day, fixing toys and spreading happiness throughout their community. Penny learned that being generous and forgiving makes everyone's heart feel full of joy.

The End.
