# Ellie Elephant Writes Peaceful Words

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a wiggly tail. She loved the sunny backyard. The grass felt cool on her feet. The sun was warm on her back. A sweet breeze smelled like flowers and clean laundry. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” and a butterfly floated by, yellow and bright.

Ellie had a big piece of paper on the picnic table. She had chunky crayons in many colors—blue like the sky, green like the grass, pink like a strawberry. She wiggled her trunk and smiled. “I’m going to write a happy letter,” she said softly. “I will write kind words.”

Her friend Pip, a tiny sparrow, hopped on the table. “Tweet! What are you writing, Ellie?”

“A note to my friend Mila,” Ellie said. “I want to tell her I care.” Ellie took a slow breath in and out. She liked to breathe slowly before she began. It made her feel calm. In… and out… She felt her body slow down. Her ears relaxed. Her heart felt warm.

Just then, a little tortoise named Tuck came by. He was carrying a red ball. “Play ball?” he asked.

Ellie wanted to play, but her trunk was still writing. She wrote, “Dear Mila, I hope you smile today. Love, Ellie.” She drew a sun with long, cozy rays. “I will play soon,” she said kindly. “Let me finish my letter.”

Tuck rolled the ball, but it bumped the table—bump! The crayons rolled—clack-clack! The paper slid and got a smudge. Ellie’s eyes went wide. Her tummy felt tight.

“Oh no,” Ellie whispered. “My letter.”

“I’m sorry,” said Tuck. He pulled his head in a little. “I didn’t mean to.”

Ellie felt two choices inside: she could shout, or she could breathe and use peaceful words. She touched her chest and remembered her calm breaths. In… and out… She counted softly, “One, two, three.” The tight feeling got smaller.

Pip chirped, “You can say how you feel.”

Ellie nodded. “Tuck, I feel sad that my letter is smudged,” she said gently. “I worked hard on it. I want to keep it neat.”

Tuck peeked out. “I hear you,” he said. “I want to help. I can hold the paper with my little feet.”

Ellie smiled. “Thank you for listening.” She gave Tuck a kind look. “Let’s make a plan. Can we put the ball over there while I write?”

“Yes,” Tuck said. He rolled the ball to a soft patch of grass.

Ellie took another slow breath, in and out. She picked up the blue crayon with her trunk. She wrote slowly, carefully. She said her words out loud: “Dear Mila, today the sun is warm. I am thinking of you.” She drew a heart. She drew a butterfly. She felt proud.

A soft bee buzzed by—bzzzz—and the flowers smelled sweet, like honey. The world felt peaceful again.

Pip hopped closer. “Can I add a word?” he asked.

“Yes,” Ellie said. “Let’s write together.” She smiled. “Writing can be sharing.” Pip dipped a tiny feather in a safe, washable ink pad and made a tiny dot like a happy chirp. “Cheep!”

Tuck used his careful feet to hold the paper steady. “I can help too,” he said. “Teamwork!”

Ellie looked at Tuck. “Thank you for being careful. I like it when we work together.”

When the letter was done, Ellie read it out loud in her soft voice. “Dear Mila, today the sun is warm. I am thinking of you. I hope you smile today. I drew a sun, a heart, and a butterfly for you. Love, Ellie.”

“It sounds kind,” Pip said.

“It feels kind,” Ellie said. She noticed her body felt light and cozy, like a blanket hug. “My calm breaths helped me use peaceful words.”

Tuck nodded. “Your peaceful words helped me listen.”

Ellie folded the letter. She put a leaf sticker on it. It smelled like mint when she pressed it down. “Now we can play ball,” she said.

They made a new plan. “Let’s set a play area,” Ellie said. She placed small pebbles in a circle far from the table. “This will keep the writing safe.”

They played gently. Tuck rolled the ball—roll, roll. Ellie tapped it back with her soft foot—tap. Pip cheered, “Cheep-cheep, hooray!”

After the game, Ellie wrote one more line on a new paper. “Peaceful words make hearts feel safe.” She hung the paper on the fence with a clothespin. The words wiggled a little in the breeze.

Pip read it. “I like that.”

Tuck read it slowly. “Me too.”

Ellie took a calm breath and smiled. “Today I learned to say how I feel, listen, and make a plan. That is peaceful communication.” She touched her letter to Mila. “Writing helped me share my heart. Breathing helped me stay calm.”

The sun gave a warm, golden glow. The backyard felt happy and quiet. Birds sang. The flowers nodded. Ellie felt proud and peaceful.

Pip fluttered to her shoulder. “What will you write tomorrow?”

Ellie giggled. “Maybe a thank-you note. I will write, ‘Thank you for helping me.’”

Tuck smiled. “I will help keep the paper safe.”

Ellie nodded. “We can always use calm breaths, kind words, and gentle plans.”

And they did.

The End.
