# Ellie's Big Clean-Up Day

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved her cozy nursery. The walls were painted soft yellow like sunshine, and her favorite stuffed animals sat on fluffy cushions. But today, Ellie looked around and felt worried. Her toys were scattered everywhere!

"Oh my!" said Ellie, swishing her little trunk. "My blocks are under the rocking chair, my books are on the floor, and my crayons rolled under the toy chest. How will I ever clean all this up?"

Ellie's mama peeked into the nursery. "What's wrong, little one?" she asked gently.

"Mama, my room is so messy!" Ellie trumpeted softly. "I want to clean it, but there's so much to do. I don't know where to start!"

Mama Elephant smiled and sat down beside Ellie. "When we have big jobs to do, we can make them easier by planning first. Would you like me to show you?"

Ellie nodded eagerly, her big ears flapping.

"First," said Mama, "let's look around and make a plan. What different types of things do you see?"

Ellie looked carefully with her bright eyes. "I see books, blocks, crayons, and stuffed animals!"

"Perfect!" said Mama. "Now, let's plan which task to do first, then second, then third, and last. What would you like to start with?"

Ellie thought hard, tapping her trunk on her chin. "The books look easy to pick up. Can I start there?"

"That's wonderful planning!" said Mama. "Easy tasks first can help us feel good about our progress."

Ellie began picking up her colorful picture books with her trunk. One by one, she placed them neatly on her bookshelf. "Look, Mama! I finished my first task!" she said proudly.

"You did! How does it feel?" asked Mama.

"It feels great! And now I can see my pretty rug again," Ellie giggled.

"What's your plan for the second task?" Mama asked.

Ellie looked around. "The crayons are small, so they might be quick too!" She carefully gathered all the red, blue, yellow, and green crayons and put them in their special box.

"Two tasks done!" cheered Ellie, doing a little dance.

For her third task, Ellie chose the blocks. She stacked them in neat towers, making satisfying clicking sounds as they fit together. "These blocks sound so nice when they're organized," she said, listening to the gentle taps.

Finally, Ellie gathered her soft, cuddly stuffed animals. She hugged each one before placing them on their cushions. "There! Mr. Bear, Mrs. Bunny, and Little Mouse are all in their cozy spots."

Ellie stepped back and looked at her nursery. Everything was clean and organized! The afternoon sunlight streamed through the window, making everything sparkle.

"Mama, I did it! I completed all my tasks!" Ellie trumpeted happily.

"You certainly did!" said Mama, giving Ellie a warm hug. "How did planning help you?"

"When I made a plan, the big job became four small jobs," Ellie explained. "And finishing each small task made me feel happy and strong!"

"That's exactly right," said Mama. "When we plan our tasks and complete them one by one, even big jobs become manageable."

That night, as Ellie snuggled in her clean, cozy nursery, she felt proud and peaceful. She had learned that with good planning, any task could be completed successfully.

The End.
