# Joey's Fair Share Adventure

Little Joey the kangaroo bounced excitedly in the back seat of Mama's blue car. His fluffy ears wiggled as he looked out the window at the tall green trees zooming by. Today was snack day at playgroup, and Joey had his favorite honey crackers in a shiny red box on his lap.

"Are we there yet, Mama?" Joey asked, patting his crackers with his tiny paws.

"Almost, sweetie," Mama Kangaroo said with a warm smile. "Remember to share nicely with your friends today."

When they arrived at playgroup, Joey hopped out with his crackers held high. Inside the colorful room, his friends Bella Bear and Max Monkey were already sitting in a circle on the soft rainbow carpet.

"Look what I brought!" Joey announced proudly, showing his shiny box.

But when snack time came, something didn't feel right. Joey noticed that Bella only had three small apple slices, and Max had just two tiny cookies. Meanwhile, Joey had a whole box full of delicious honey crackers - at least twenty of them!

Joey's tummy felt funny, but not from hunger. He felt a warm, uncomfortable feeling in his chest. "This doesn't seem fair," he thought, looking at his friends' small snacks.

At first, Joey felt a little angry. "Why should I give away MY crackers?" he grumbled quietly, hugging his box tighter. His ears drooped down, and his nose scrunched up.

But then Joey remembered what Mama always said: "When something doesn't feel fair, we can make it better."

Joey took three deep breaths, just like Mama taught him. "Whoosh, whoosh, whoosh." The angry feeling in his chest started to melt away like ice cream in the sun.

"I have an idea!" Joey announced, his eyes sparkling bright again.

He opened his red box and began counting. "One for Bella, one for Max, one for me. Two for Bella, two for Max, two for me." He made three equal piles of crackers on the carpet.

"But Joey," said Bella softly, "these are YOUR special crackers."

"Sharing makes them even MORE special!" Joey replied, bouncing a little. "Besides, it's not fair if I have so many and you have so few. Friends should have equal treats!"

Max clapped his hands. "That's so kind, Joey! Thank you!"

As they munched their crackers together, Joey felt a warm, happy glow in his heart - much better than the uncomfortable feeling from before. The crackers even tasted sweeter when shared with friends.

"You know what?" Joey said, getting another creative idea. "Let's draw pictures of our favorite snacks and make a friendship recipe book!"

The three friends spent the rest of snack time drawing colorful pictures of fruits, cookies, and crackers. They used bright crayons to create a beautiful book they could all share.

When Mama came to pick up Joey, she noticed his big smile. "You look extra happy today, little one."

"I learned something important, Mama," Joey said, bouncing into the car. "When things aren't fair, I can help make them fair! And sharing makes everything better - even my tummy feels happier!"

Mama Kangaroo's eyes twinkled with pride. "That's my wise little joey. You discovered that being fair and kind makes everyone feel good inside."

As they drove home, Joey watched the trees again, but this time he felt proud and peaceful. He had learned that justice means making sure everyone is treated fairly, and that sharing and kindness can fix unfair situations.

The End.
