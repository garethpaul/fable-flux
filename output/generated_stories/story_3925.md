# Kira the Koala's Fair Share Day

Kira the koala sat quietly in her cozy home office, surrounded by soft cushions and colorful books. The morning sun streamed through the window, making her gray fur look silvery and warm. She was getting ready for a very special video call with her friends from around the world.

"Ring, ring!" The computer chimed with a happy sound. On the screen appeared three smiling faces: Raj from India, Maria from Mexico, and Chen from China. They were all part of Kira's online playgroup.

"Good morning, friends!" Kira said in her gentle voice, waving her fluffy paw.

"Buenos días, Kira!" called Maria, her dark curls bouncing as she waved.

"Namaste!" said Raj, pressing his hands together with a warm smile.

"Ni hao!" Chen giggled, showing off a colorful toy dragon.

Today was sharing day, and each friend had brought something special to show. But there was a problem. Kira noticed that Raj looked sad and wasn't holding anything.

"What did you bring to share today, Raj?" asked Maria kindly.

Raj's shoulders drooped. "I... I don't have anything special. My family doesn't have many toys like yours."

Kira's heart felt heavy. This didn't seem fair. She watched as Chen excitedly showed his shiny new robot, and Maria displayed her beautiful handmade doll. The computer screen felt cold suddenly, and Kira could see Raj trying not to cry.

"Wait!" Kira said, her calm voice filled with determination. "This isn't fair. Sharing day should be fun for everyone."

She thought carefully, her small black nose twitching as she concentrated. Then her eyes lit up with an idea.

"Friends, I have a better idea for sharing day. Instead of showing things we own, let's share something we all have - our special talents and stories!"

Maria clapped her hands. "That's a wonderful idea, Kira!"

"Raj," Kira said gently, "would you like to share first? I know you tell the most amazing stories."

Raj's face brightened like sunshine breaking through clouds. "Really? You want to hear one of my stories?"

"Yes, please!" all three friends said together.

Raj sat up tall and began telling a magical tale about a brave elephant who helped his village. His voice grew stronger and more excited with each word. When he finished, everyone cheered.

"That was incredible!" Chen said, his eyes wide with wonder.

"My turn!" Maria sang a beautiful lullaby her grandmother had taught her in Spanish. The melody was so sweet that Kira felt like she was floating on a cloud.

Chen shared how to fold a paper crane, moving his fingers carefully as he taught them each step. "In my culture, cranes bring good luck and peace," he explained.

Finally, Kira taught everyone a breathing exercise. "When we breathe slowly like this," she demonstrated, taking deep, calm breaths, "it helps us feel peaceful and safe, just like when I'm hugging my eucalyptus tree."

As they all breathed together, the computer screen seemed to glow with warmth and friendship.

"Thank you, Kira," Raj said, his smile bright and genuine. "This was the best sharing day ever. I felt left out before, but now I feel special."

"We all have different gifts," Kira said wisely, her voice soft and caring. "When we share what makes us unique, everyone feels included and valued. That's what fairness really means."

"And I'm grateful for all of you," Maria added. "You taught me that the best treasures aren't things we can hold, but the love and kindness we share."

The friends waved goodbye, promising to have another fair sharing day soon. Kira leaned back in her office chair, feeling proud and happy. She had learned that true fairness isn't about everyone having the same things, but about making sure everyone feels valued and included.

The End.
