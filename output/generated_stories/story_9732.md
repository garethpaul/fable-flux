# Owl Hoots and Bubble Boots

In a sunny little house, there was a bright blue bathroom. The tiles felt cool like smooth pebbles. The mirror was shiny. The sink sang a soft drip-drip song. In the window, a wise old owl named Obo perched on a towel rack. Obo had soft silver feathers, big kind eyes, and a calm hoot that sounded like a warm hug.

Every morning, Obo helped the bathroom do its big job: staying clean and safe. “Hoo-hoo,” Obo said, puffing his feathers. “We have tasks to do!”

On the counter, there were building blocks shaped like small boats. On the floor, there were tiny puddles from last night’s bath. The soap smelled like oranges. The bubble bath smelled like sweet berries.

“First, we finish our tasks,” Obo hooted. “Then we play.” He gently tapped a little chart on the wall. It had pictures: a toothbrush, a towel, a brush, a tiny broom, and a little clock.

From the doorway, a small voice grumbled. It was Pip, a young raccoon who visited every day to learn with Obo. Pip had striped paws and a curious nose. Today, Pip’s tail was twitchy.

“I want to build a big boat now!” Pip said, stomping a paw. “I don’t want to wipe the floor. It’s boring.” His cheeks puffed. His ears drooped.

Obo blinked slowly. “I hear your big feelings,” he said. “Let’s take a calm breath. In through the nose … out through the mouth.”

Pip took a big sniff. “Mmm,” he said. “The bathroom smells like orange soap.”

“Good,” said Obo. “Again. In … and out.”

Pip’s tail stopped twitching. “Okay,” he whispered.

“Now,” Obo said, “we have a plan. We complete tasks step by step. Step one: brush teeth.” He held up a tiny toothbrush with blue bristles.

Pip frowned. “But the blocks!”

“Blocks wait,” Obo said gently. “Teeth first, then towel, then brush fur, then wipe the floor. When we finish, we feel proud. That is justice for our bathroom. We care for the places that care for us.”

Pip blinked. “Justice means fair?”

“Yes,” Obo hooted. “Fair to the bathroom. Fair to you.”

Pip looked at the mirror. “I can do it,” he said. He squeezed a pea of toothpaste. Scritch-scritch went the brush. Minty bubbles tickled his tongue. He giggled. “It tastes tingly!”

“Two corners, other corners, little circle, big circle,” Obo guided. “Complete the task.”

When they were done, Pip spat and rinsed. “Step two?” he asked.

“Dry with the towel,” Obo said. The towel was fluffy and warm from the sun. Pip patted his face. “It feels like a soft cloud,” he said.

“Step three: brush fur,” Obo said, handing a small brush. Pip brushed his fur until it was smooth. Swish-swish. “I look neat,” he said, smiling into the shiny mirror.

“Step four: wipe the floor,” Obo said. He gave Pip a small mop and put on little rubber “bubble boots” with tiny stars. Squish! They made funny sounds on the wet tiles.

Pip’s smile faded. “I don’t like this part,” he muttered. His tail twitched again. He looked at the blocks. He stomped a boot. Splash!

Obo lifted a wing. “Pause. Feeling stormy?”

Pip nodded. “Yes. I’m mad.”

“Name it,” Obo said softly. “Say, ‘I feel mad, but I can calm.’”

“I feel mad,” Pip said. He took a breath. “But I can calm.”

“Good,” Obo said. “Now count your swishes. One, two, three. That helps the mad move along.”

Pip tried. Swish-one. Swish-two. Swish-three. The mop smelled like lemon. The floor began to shine. Pip’s mad feeling got smaller. “Hey,” he said. “I’m doing it.”

“Persistence,” Obo hooted. “You keep going, even when it’s not fun.”

They moved together like a tiny team. Obo hummed a low hoot-song. Pip swished and counted. The floor dried. The sink sparkled. The towels were hung straight like flags.

“Task complete,” Obo said, tapping the chart. “All done!”

Pip bounced. “Now boat time?”

“Now boat time,” Obo smiled.

Pip stacked the blocks on the dry bath mat. He built a tall red base, a blue middle, and a green top. “This is the Justice Boat,” he said. “It will carry soap and towels to help the bathroom.”

Obo clapped his wings softly. “A fair and helpful boat.”

Pip pushed the boat along the mat. It made a soft hush sound. He added a paper flag that said, “We finish our tasks!” He laughed. “Hooray!”

Then a tiny drip came from the faucet. Drip. Drip. Pip looked at Obo. “Is that fair to the bathroom?”

Obo shook his head. “No. The bathroom needs care.” He showed Pip the handle. “Turn until the drip stops. Small task, big help.”

Pip turned the handle gently. The drip stopped. The room was quiet and cozy again. “I did it,” he said, chest puffed.

Obo nodded. “You completed tasks. You managed anger. You kept going. You built something helpful. That is wise.”

Pip hugged Obo. “When we finish tasks, we get to play, and the bathroom feels happy.”

Obo hooted a soft lullaby. Sunlight made tiny rainbows in the mirror. The orange soap smelled bright. The floor was smooth and safe. The Justice Boat sailed on the bath mat sea.

Pip saluted. “Captain Pip will always finish the job!”

Obo smiled. “And Captain Obo will always help you breathe, count, and persist.”

They put the blocks away, turned off the light, and closed the door with a gentle click. The bathroom felt proud.

The End.
