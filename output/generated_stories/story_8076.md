# Leo the Lion Cub and the Painty Paws

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright curious eyes. Today, he padded into the creative art room, his tail swishing with excitement. The room smelled like fresh paper and sweet glue. Sunlight shone on jars of colorful paint. Brushes stood like little trees in a cup. Crayons lay in rainbow rows. A sink with shiny soap sat in the corner, humming quietly with dripping water.

Leo’s friends were there too. Mia the monkey liked to draw swirly lines. Tiko the turtle took slow, careful strokes with his brush. Nala the giraffe wore a tiny apron with stars. Everyone smiled.

“Let’s make a big friendship picture!” Leo roared softly. He was brave, but he used a kind voice. “We can paint a garden with birds and bugs.”

“Yes!” cheered Mia. “I’ll draw the sky.”

“I’ll do flowers,” said Nala, her long neck bending to see the colors.

“I’ll paint a ladybug,” said Tiko.

Leo put on his small blue apron. He dipped his paw in red paint. It felt cool and squishy, like pudding. “Squish, squish,” he giggled. He pressed his paw on the paper. A bright red paw print bloomed.

Mia tapped the blue paint. “Drip, drop,” went her brush. Tiko made tiny dots. “Tap, tap.” Nala mixed yellow and made a big sun. “Swish,” went her brush.

Leo felt very happy. He wanted to help everyone. He hurried to the green paint. He forgot to wash his paw. Red and green mixed into a muddy brown. “Oops,” he whispered, but he didn’t stop. He ran to the purple. He reached over Tiko’s careful dots. His painty paw slipped.

Splat!

A smear of brown-red-purple landed on Tiko’s ladybug and across Mia’s sky. The colors ran like little rivers.

Mia’s eyes got big. “Leo! You messed up my sky!”

Tiko’s mouth trembled. “My ladybug…”

Leo’s ears went flat. His tummy felt twisty. The room seemed too quiet except for the soft drip of the sink. He didn’t mean to hurt their art. He just wanted to help.

“I’m s-sorry,” Leo said with a small roar. “I forgot to wash my paws. I moved too fast. I wanted to help, but I made a mess.”

Nala took a breath. She looked at the sink. “We can fix it,” she said gently. “But first, clean paws.”

Leo nodded. He walked to the sink. The water felt warm on his fur. “Shhh,” it whispered. He pumped the soap. “Squeeze.” Bubbles puffed up like little white clouds. He scrubbed between his toes. “Rub, rub, rub.” He sang, “Scrub the paws, front and back, make the paint go swish and slack.” He rinsed until the water ran clear. He dried with a soft towel. “Pat, pat.”

When Leo turned around, Mia and Tiko were still sad. Leo took a deep breath. His brave heart told him to try again, with kindness.

“Mia, Tiko,” he said. “I see your faces. I see I hurt your work. I am sorry. How can I help fix it? I will listen.”

Mia looked at the smeared sky. She blinked. “Maybe the brown line can be a tree trunk,” she said slowly.

Tiko touched the smear on his ladybug. “Maybe the big smear can be a rock. My ladybug can sit on the rock.”

Nala smiled. “Good ideas. Leo, use your clean paw to paint leaves for the tree.”

Leo nodded. “I will move slow. I will ask first.”

He waited. “May I add leaves here?” he asked Mia.

“Yes,” Mia said.

“May I make a smooth rock shape?” he asked Tiko.

“Yes,” Tiko said.

Leo dipped a clean brush in green. “Swish, swish,” he painted soft leaves around the brown trunk. Mia added blue around the leaves to make the sky bright again. “Brush, brush.”

Tiko used black to outline the rock. “Tap, tap.” Then he painted his ladybug sitting happily on the rock. “Dot, dot,” went the tiny spots.

Nala added flowers under the tree. “Swirl, swirl.” The picture grew even more beautiful than before.

They stepped back. The paper showed a sunny garden with a strong tree, a happy ladybug on a smooth rock, and a big bright sky. It was different now, but it was special. It showed their teamwork.

Leo felt his tummy unknot. His ears lifted. “Thank you for forgiving me,” he said. “Next time, I will wash my paws before I touch the paints. I will move slow. I will ask before I reach.”

Mia nodded. “We felt upset. But we listened. We found a way to fix it together.”

Tiko smiled a small smile. “I forgive you, Leo.”

Nala clapped her hooves. “We solved the problem. Clean paws, kind words, and calm steps!”

They laughed. Leo washed his paws again before cleaning up. “Squeeze, scrub, rinse, pat,” he sang. Everyone joined in. The room smelled like soap and paint and felt warm and safe.

At the end, they wrote Kind Friends at the bottom of the picture. Leo drew a little heart. Mia drew a tiny cloud. Tiko dotted one more spot. Nala added a star.

Leo whispered, “When we make a mess, we can say sorry, forgive, and fix it. And we can keep our paws clean.”

Everyone nodded. The art room glowed with happy colors.

The End.
