# Benny Beaver's Rooftop Adventure

Benny Beaver was the busiest beaver in the whole neighborhood. He loved to build and fix things with his strong teeth and clever paws. One sunny morning, Benny climbed up to the rooftop of the tall apartment building where he lived.

"Wow!" said Benny, looking around at all the space. "This rooftop is perfect for my biggest project ever!"

Benny had big dreams. He wanted to build a huge dam right there on the rooftop, complete with sticks, mud, and a little pond. He started gathering materials, dragging branches and collecting rainwater in buckets.

Soon, his friend Ruby Robin flew by. "Hi Benny! What are you doing up here?" she chirped.

"I'm building the most amazing dam ever!" Benny said excitedly, his tail slapping against the roof. "It's going to be enormous!"

Ruby tilted her little red head. "But Benny, this rooftop belongs to all the animals who live in this building. Don't you think you should ask them first?"

Benny paused, a stick hanging from his mouth. He hadn't thought about that. "But I really want to build here," he said. "There's so much space!"

Just then, Oliver Owl hooted from the roof's edge. "Benny, your pile of sticks is blocking the door where Mrs. Squirrel hangs her laundry to dry in the sunshine."

Benny looked around and saw that his building supplies were everywhere. The sweet smell of Mrs. Squirrel's flower garden was being covered by the muddy scent of his dam materials.

"And your pond might leak through the roof," added Carla Cat, who had climbed up to see what all the noise was about. "The families below need their homes to stay dry and cozy."

Benny's ears drooped. He felt sad and confused. "But where can I build my special project?" he asked.

Ruby Robin had an idea. "Benny, let's think together! What if we found a place that's just right for your dam and doesn't take away space that others need?"

Oliver Owl nodded wisely. "That's called respecting boundaries, Benny. It means making sure our actions don't hurt others or take away what belongs to them."

Benny thought hard, tapping his flat tail on the roof. Suddenly, his eyes lit up! "I know! There's an empty lot by the creek where no one lives. I could build my dam there and help make the water cleaner for everyone!"

"That's perfect!" chirped Ruby. "And we can all help you!"

The friends worked together to carefully move Benny's materials down from the rooftop. Mrs. Squirrel was so happy to have her laundry space back that she baked them all fresh acorn cookies.

At the creek, Benny built the most wonderful dam ever. It helped clean the water and gave many forest animals a safe place to drink. Ruby Robin brought soft moss, Oliver Owl found the strongest sticks, and Carla Cat helped pat down the mud.

"Thank you for helping me understand about boundaries," Benny told his friends as they watched fish swimming in the clean water. "Now everyone is happy, and my dam helps our whole neighborhood!"

From that day on, Benny always remembered to think about others before starting his projects. He learned that the best building happens when everyone works together and respects each other's space.

The End.
