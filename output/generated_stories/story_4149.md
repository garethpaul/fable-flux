# Whiskers the Cat's Big Reading Goal

Whiskers was a fluffy orange cat with bright green eyes. She loved her cozy study corner. Soft cushions made a perfect reading spot. Sunlight streamed through the window, making everything warm and bright.

"Meow!" said Whiskers happily. "Today feels special!"

On the wooden shelf sat many colorful books. Some were thick. Some were thin. Some had big pictures. Whiskers looked up at them with wonder.

"I want to read ALL these books!" she declared. "Every single one!"

Whiskers counted the books. "One, two, three... ten books! That's so many!" Her whiskers twitched with excitement.

She pulled down the biggest book first. It was heavy! The words looked very long and squiggly. Whiskers tried to read the first page.

"The... cat... sat... on... the..." Whiskers felt tired already. "This is too hard!" she sighed.

The clever cat sat back on her cushion. She put her paw on her chin. "Hmm," she thought. "Maybe I need a different plan."

Whiskers looked at the shelf again. This time, she noticed the smallest book. It was thin and had bright pictures of fish.

"I'll start with this one!" she said. "It looks just right for me."

The little book was perfect! Whiskers could read every word. "The fish swims in the blue water," she read aloud. "I did it! I read the whole page!"

Her tail swished with pride. The words felt smooth like purring.

"Now I know my plan," said Whiskers. "I'll read one small book first. Then a bigger one. Then an even bigger one!"

She made a chart on paper. She drew ten boxes in a row. "I'll color one box for each book I finish," she decided.

Whiskers read her first book completely. She colored the first box bright yellow. "One down, nine to go!" she cheered.

The next day, she chose a slightly bigger book. This one had longer words, but Whiskers felt ready. "I can do this," she told herself. "I learned new words yesterday!"

Page by page, she read about a brave mouse. When she finished, she colored the second box green. "I'm getting stronger at reading!" she purred.

Each day, Whiskers picked the next book carefully. Some days were easier. Some days were harder. When big words felt tricky, she would say, "I'm still learning. That's okay!"

Her grandmother cat visited the study corner. "What are you doing, Whiskers?" she asked.

"I'm reading ALL the books!" Whiskers showed her chart. Seven boxes were colored now. "I started with tiny goals. Now I can read bigger books!"

"You're so wise," said Grandmother Cat. "You learned that big dreams need small steps."

Finally, Whiskers reached for the very last book. It was the biggest one from the first day! But now, after reading nine other books, it didn't look scary anymore.

"I know so many words now," she said confidently. "I can do this!"

Whiskers read the final book slowly and carefully. When she finished, she colored the last box bright purple.

"I did it!" she meowed joyfully. "I read every single book!"

The study corner felt magical. Sunbeams danced on her completed chart. Whiskers felt proud and happy.

"I learned that big goals are just lots of little goals put together," she said wisely. "And I can do anything if I keep trying!"

The End.
