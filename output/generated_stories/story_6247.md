# Nutkin's Fair Share

In a cozy, warm kitchen that smelled like fresh-baked cookies, lived a little gray squirrel named Nutkin. His bushy tail twitched with excitement as he watched the golden sunlight stream through the window onto the wooden countertop.

Nutkin had a big problem. He had found three shiny acorns under the kitchen table, but his two best friends, Chippy the chipmunk and Hazel the field mouse, were coming for lunch. How could he share just three acorns fairly among three friends?

"Oh dear," Nutkin chittered, scratching his fuzzy head. "Three acorns for three friends. That seems easy, but what if someone is extra hungry today?"

Just then, he heard tiny paws scampering across the kitchen floor. Chippy bounced in through the pet door, his cheeks already puffy with nuts. "Hi Nutkin! I brought some sunflower seeds to share!"

"That's wonderful!" squeaked Nutkin. But then he noticed something. Chippy's cheeks were so full of food, he probably wasn't very hungry at all.

Soon after, little Hazel squeezed through a crack near the cupboard. Her pink nose twitched as she sniffed the air. "Something smells yummy! But I'm so hungry, I haven't eaten anything all morning."

Nutkin's curious mind began to work. Being fair didn't just mean giving everyone the same amount - it meant thinking about what each friend really needed!

"Friends," announced Nutkin, washing his paws in a drop of water from the faucet, "I have three acorns to share, but I want to be really fair about it."

He looked at Chippy's full cheeks, then at Hazel's thin little body. "Chippy, you've already had breakfast. Would it be okay if Hazel had two acorns since she's extra hungry, and you and I each had one?"

Chippy's eyes sparkled with understanding. "That's a great idea! I'm not very hungry anyway. Being fair means caring about what our friends need!"

But then Hazel shook her head. "That's very kind, but I made a mistake by not eating breakfast. Maybe I should just have one acorn like everyone else. I need to learn to take better care of myself."

Nutkin's tail swished thoughtfully. "What if we do something different? Let's each take one acorn now, and I'll show you both where I found them. Tomorrow we can look for more together!"

"Yes!" squeaked Hazel and chittered Chippy together.

As they munched their acorns, Nutkin led his friends to the spot under the table. "Look! There are more acorns here, and some cookie crumbs too! If we clean up these crumbs and save them in my storage box, we'll have snacks for many days."

The three friends spent the afternoon carefully collecting crumbs and acorns, making sure to keep the kitchen tidy. They put everything in Nutkin's special storage box made from a recycled yogurt container.

"This is the best lunch ever," said Hazel, giving Nutkin a tiny hug. "Thank you for thinking about what I needed."

"And thank you for teaching us that being fair means being thoughtful," added Chippy.

Nutkin felt warm and happy inside. He had learned that fairness wasn't just about equal sharing - it was about caring for his friends and thinking about what each one needed. By working together and being curious about solutions, they had found a way for everyone to be happy and well-fed.

The End.
