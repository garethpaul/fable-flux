# Kody Koala's Pantry Workout

Kody the koala woke up feeling sleepy, just like every morning. He stretched his fuzzy gray arms and yawned a big yawn. "I need to get my snacks for breakfast," he said to himself, padding slowly to the kitchen pantry.

The pantry was tall and narrow, with wooden shelves reaching up, up, up to the ceiling. Kody could smell the sweet honey crackers on the very top shelf. His tummy rumbled loudly. "Grumble, grumble!"

"Oh my," said Kody, looking up at the high shelf. "Those crackers are so far up there. How will I reach them?"

Kody tried standing on his tippy-toes, stretching his arms as high as they could go. His fluffy ears tickled the second shelf, but the crackers were still too far away.

"I could climb up there," thought Kody. But then he remembered what Mama Koala always said: "Safety first, little one. Ask for help when you need it."

Kody felt frustrated. His tummy was getting hungrier. "Grumble, grumble, GRUMBLE!"

Then Kody had an idea. "Wait! I know what I can do. I can exercise to make myself stronger and taller!"

Kody started with jumping jacks. "One, two, three!" he counted, his gray fur bouncing as he jumped. His arms went up and down, up and down. The wooden floor creaked softly under his feet. "This feels good!" he giggled.

Next, Kody did stretches. He reached his arms way up high, standing on his toes. "Stretch, stretch, stretch!" he said. He could feel his muscles waking up. The morning sunlight streaming through the pantry window felt warm on his fur.

"Maybe I should try marching in place," thought Kody. He lifted his knees up high, one at a time. "Left, right, left, right!" His heart started beating faster, and he felt more awake than before.

After a few minutes of exercising, Kody tried reaching for the crackers again. He stood tall and stretched his arms up high. His fingertips could almost touch the bottom shelf, but the crackers were still too high.

"Hmm," said Kody, thinking carefully. "I exercised and I feel stronger, but I still can't reach safely. I think it's time to ask for help."

Kody walked to the kitchen where Papa Koala was making coffee. The rich, warm smell filled the air.

"Papa," said Kody in his calm, clear voice, "I did my morning exercises, but I still can't safely reach the honey crackers on the top shelf. Could you please help me?"

Papa Koala smiled proudly. "Of course, Kody! I'm so proud of you for exercising first and then asking for help when you needed it. That's very smart and safe."

Papa Koala easily reached the crackers and handed them to Kody. "Here you go, little one."

"Thank you, Papa!" said Kody, hugging the cracker box. "My exercise made me feel strong and awake, even though I still needed help with the high shelf."

"That's exactly right," said Papa Koala. "Exercise makes our bodies strong and healthy. And speaking up when we need help keeps us safe."

Kody munched his honey crackers happily. They tasted sweet and crunchy. "Tomorrow I'll do my pantry exercises again," he decided. "And I'll always ask for help when I need it!"

From that day on, Kody started every morning with exercises in the pantry, and he never felt shy about asking for help when he needed it.

The End.
