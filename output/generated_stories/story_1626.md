# Rocco’s Sparkly Song

Rocco the raccoon loved music. He had a tiny drum, a jingling bell, and a shiny harmonica. His cozy bedroom was warm like a hug. The curtains were soft and blue, and his bed felt fluffy like a cloud. Sunlight slid in through the window and made little dust sparkles dance.

“Good morning, music!” Rocco sang. He blew a note on his harmonica. Toot-tee-toot! It sounded happy.

Rocco’s nose wiggled. “Hmm,” he said, sniffing his paws. “Uh-oh. My paws smell like last night’s snack.” He frowned a tiny bit. “I want to play music, but my paws feel sticky.”

Mama Raccoon peeked in. She smiled and patted Rocco’s head. “Good morning, my musical star. Before you play, it’s hygiene time. Clean paws make healthy music.”

“Hygiene?” Rocco asked, tilting his head. The word felt new and bouncy in his mouth.

“Hygiene means taking good care of your body,” Mama said. “We wash our paws, brush our teeth, and keep our room tidy. It helps us stay healthy and strong.”

Rocco nodded. He liked learning new words. He tapped his drum gently. Tap-tap. “Can hygiene be fun?” he asked.

Mama’s eyes twinkled. “Let’s make it a song.”

Rocco clapped. “A hygiene song!”

They went to the small sink in Rocco’s cozy room. The soap smelled like oranges. Rocco sniffed. “Mmm, it smells sunny!” The water was warm and tickly on his fur.

Mama sang, “Top and bottom, in-between,” and Rocco sang back, “Bubbles make our paws so clean!” They rubbed their paws together. Swish, swish. The bubbles were soft and foamy. Rocco watched the bubbles pop—plink, plink—like tiny stars.

Rocco giggled. “My paws are dancing!”

“Now rinse,” Mama said. “Good job.” The water washed the bubbles away. Rocco shook his paws. Drip, drop. He felt proud.

Next, Mama held up a little, soft toothbrush. It was blue with a tiny silver star on the handle. “Teeth time,” she said.

Rocco touched his cheeks. “I want my smile to shine,” he said.

The toothpaste smelled like mint leaves. Cool and fresh. Mama sang, “Little circles, gentle and slow,” and Rocco hummed along. Brush-brush, swish-swish. He looked in the mirror and made a silly face. “I’m a minty musician!” he giggled. His breath felt cool, like a tiny breeze.

Rocco rinsed and smiled at his reflection. “My teeth go ding!” he said, tapping his harmonica. Toot! The note sounded bright.

“Now for tidy time,” Mama said, pointing to the floor. Socks and music sheets were in a small pile near the bed. Rocco blinked. “Oops.”

Rocco looked at his drum. He had an idea. “Let’s play the Clean-Up Beat!”

He tapped a steady rhythm. Tap-tap, tap-tap. He made a little chant: “Pick it up, put it back. Clean room, happy track!”

It was Rocco’s innovation. He made a new way to clean—using music! He and Mama danced around, putting socks in the drawer, stacking music sheets on the desk, and setting the bell and harmonica on a soft cloth. The bell jingled. Jingle-jing! The room felt calm and comfy again.

Rocco stopped and sniffed the air. The room smelled like orange soap and fresh blankets. He took a deep breath. His chest felt big and brave.

“I learned something,” Rocco said. He held up his clean paws. “Hygiene helps me feel good. Clean paws, bright teeth, tidy room.”

Mama hugged him. “Yes. Hygiene keeps us healthy. It also shows we care for ourselves and our home.”

Rocco bounced on his toes. “Can I make a special song so I remember?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “That’s a smart idea.”

Rocco sat on the fluffy rug. He tapped his drum and sang softly:

“Wash-wash, bubbles bright,
Brush-brush, teeth so white.
Tidy-tidy, clean and neat,
Healthy body, happy beat!”

He played his harmonica. Toot-tee-toot! The notes tickled his ears. He felt warm and proud.

There was a soft knock. Papa Raccoon peeked in with two small cups. “Warm milk for my musicians,” he said. The milk smelled sweet and cozy. Rocco took a sip. Mmm. It felt smooth and warm on his tongue.

Papa looked around. “Oh! The room is sparkling.”

Rocco stood tall. “I used education and innovation,” he said carefully. “I learned about hygiene, and I made a Clean-Up Beat.”

Papa clapped. “Wonderful, Rocco! Your song helps your body and your room.”

Rocco yawned a tiny yawn. He pulled his blanket over his legs. The fabric was soft like a whisper. He held his harmonica close. “I feel clean, calm, and ready,” he said. “My music shines when I shine.”

Mama tucked him in and kissed his forehead. “That’s the lesson, little star: take care of yourself, and you’ll feel bright inside and out.”

Rocco smiled. “Wash, brush, tidy,” he whispered. “Healthy body, happy beat.”

He blew one gentle note. Toot. The sound was clear and sweet in the cozy bedroom.

The End.
