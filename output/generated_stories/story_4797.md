# Ellie's Safe Room Adventure

Little Ellie the elephant calf was visiting her grandmother's house for the very first time. Her trunk wiggled with excitement as Grandma Elephant showed her to the special guest room where she would sleep.

"This room is just for you, my dear," said Grandma, her kind eyes twinkling. "But remember, Ellie, we must always be careful and safe, especially in new places."

The guest room was cozy and warm, with soft yellow walls and a big, fluffy bed. But as Ellie looked around with her bright, curious eyes, she noticed something that made her feel worried.

"Grandma," Ellie said in her small, gentle voice, "I see some things that might not be safe."

Grandma Elephant smiled proudly. "What do you see, little one?"

Ellie pointed her tiny trunk toward the corner. "That electrical cord is lying across the floor where someone might trip. And look—that heavy picture frame is sitting too close to the edge of the nightstand."

"My goodness!" said Grandma. "You have very good eyes for safety, Ellie."

But then Ellie noticed something else. A family of field mice had made a small nest behind the dresser. Some elephants might have been scared or wanted the mice to go away, but gentle Ellie had a different idea.

"Hello, little mice," Ellie whispered softly. "I see you live here too."

The smallest mouse peeked out nervously. "We're sorry, big elephant. We know some animals don't like us."

Ellie's heart felt warm and kind. "I think we can all share this room safely. But I noticed that electrical cord might be dangerous for your tiny paws too."

The mama mouse nodded gratefully. "We were worried about that too, but we didn't know what to do."

Ellie thought carefully. Even though she was young, she knew she could help everyone stay safe. "Grandma," she called, "may I help make this room safer for everyone?"

"Of course, dear. What's your plan?"

Ellie used her trunk very gently to move the electrical cord against the wall where no one could trip. Then she carefully pushed the picture frame away from the edge of the nightstand.

"Now," said Ellie to the mouse family, "you can run along the wall safely, and I can walk across the room without worrying about tripping."

The mice squeaked happily. "Thank you, Ellie! You're so kind and smart."

Grandma Elephant wrapped her trunk around Ellie in a warm hug. "You showed wonderful leadership today, my dear. You kept everyone safe, you were kind to the mice even though they're different from us, and you took charge to solve the problems."

That night, Ellie slept peacefully in the safe, cozy guest room. The mice slept safely too, and they all felt happy knowing they could share the space together.

"Safety first, kindness always, and helping others makes everyone happy," Ellie whispered to herself as she drifted off to sleep.

From that day on, whenever Ellie visited new places, she always looked for ways to keep everyone safe. She learned that being a good leader meant caring for others, no matter how big or small they were.

And the mouse family? They became Ellie's very best friends, proving that different animals can live together safely and happily when they show kindness and understanding to one another.

The End.
