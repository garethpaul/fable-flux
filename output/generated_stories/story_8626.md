# Tortoise's Fair Share Tree House

High up in the branches of the biggest oak tree in Sunny Meadow lived a very wise tortoise named Shelly. Her beautiful treehouse had wooden walls that creaked softly in the breeze and windows that sparkled like diamonds in the morning sun.

One bright Tuesday morning, Shelly heard tiny voices arguing below her treehouse. She poked her wrinkled green head out the window and saw three little animals standing in the grass.

"It's MY turn to use the swing!" squeaked Pip the mouse, stamping his tiny feet.

"No, it's MINE!" chirped Bella the bluebird, flapping her bright blue wings.

"I was here first!" growled Rex the raccoon, crossing his furry arms.

The rope swing hung from a branch near Shelly's treehouse, swaying gently in the warm breeze. All three friends wanted to play, but nobody wanted to wait their turn.

"Oh my," said Shelly in her slow, gentle voice. "What seems to be the problem down there?"

The three friends looked up at the wise tortoise. Their faces were red and pouty.

"We all want to swing, but there's only one swing!" said Pip, his whiskers twitching with frustration.

Shelly slowly climbed down her wooden ladder, her shell making soft tapping sounds against each rung. When she reached the ground, she smiled kindly at the three friends.

"I see," she said thoughtfully. "You know, when I was a young tortoise, I learned something very important about sharing and being fair."

The three friends gathered around Shelly, curious to hear what she had to say.

"Being fair means everyone gets a turn," Shelly explained. "But first, we need to finish the job of making sure everyone knows the rules."

Shelly walked over to a smooth patch of dirt near the swing. With her claw, she drew three circles in the soft earth.

"Let's make a fair plan together," she said. "Each circle represents one of you. We'll take turns in order, and each friend gets to swing for the same amount of time."

"But how do we know when our time is up?" asked Bella, tilting her small blue head.

Shelly smiled and pulled out a small hourglass from her shell. The sand inside sparkled like tiny stars.

"This magic timer will help us," she said. "When all the sand falls to the bottom, it's time to switch. That way, everyone gets exactly the same amount of swing time."

Rex's eyes grew wide with wonder. "That's so fair!"

"Now," said Shelly, "who would like to go first?"

All three friends raised their hands, then looked at each other and giggled.

"How about we decide fairly?" suggested Pip. "We could count to three and point to who we think should go first."

They all agreed this was a wonderful idea. After counting, they all pointed to Bella because she was the smallest.

Bella swung happily while the hourglass counted down. The sand made a soft whooshing sound as it fell. When her turn ended, she hopped off with a big smile.

"That was perfect!" she chirped. "Rex, you're next!"

One by one, each friend took their fair turn. They cheered for each other and felt proud of completing their task of sharing nicely.

As the sun climbed higher in the sky, Shelly watched the three friends playing together peacefully.

"You see," she said warmly, "when we're fair and take turns, everyone has fun. And when we complete the task of making good rules together, we all feel happy."

From that day forward, the three friends always remembered Shelly's lesson about fairness, and the treehouse swing became a place of joy and friendship for everyone.

The End.
