# Polly's Dancing Day

Polly was a bright green parrot with beautiful red feathers on her wings. She lived in a cozy treehouse high up in an old oak tree. The treehouse had colorful ribbons hanging from the ceiling and wind chimes that sang gentle songs.

Every morning, Polly loved to dance. She would spread her wings wide and spin around. Her feet would tap-tap-tap on the wooden floor. Dancing made her feel happy and free.

"Good morning, world!" Polly chirped as she began her morning dance. She flapped her wings up and down. She hopped from one foot to the other. The ribbons swayed as she moved.

But today was different. Polly heard crying sounds coming from below her treehouse. She looked down and saw three little squirrels sitting on a branch. They looked very sad.

"What's wrong?" called Polly, flying down to meet them.

"We're trying to learn how to dance for the forest talent show," said the first squirrel, wiping his eyes. "But we keep bumping into each other."

"Our tails get all tangled up," said the second squirrel.

"We're too clumsy," said the third squirrel. "We'll never learn."

Polly's heart felt warm with kindness. "Don't worry, little friends. Dancing takes practice and patience. Would you like me to help you?"

The squirrels nodded eagerly. Polly flew back to her treehouse and invited them up.

"First," said Polly gently, "we need to start slow. Watch me." She lifted one wing, then the other, very slowly. "Dancing is like breathing. Nice and easy."

The squirrels tried to copy her. Their movements were wobbly, but Polly smiled encouragingly.

"That's wonderful! Now let's add our feet. Step to the right, step to the left." Polly showed them how to move without rushing.

At first, the squirrels still bumped into each other. One little squirrel got frustrated. "This is too hard!" he squeaked.

"Remember," said Polly in her sweetest voice, "patience is the secret ingredient to dancing. Every dancer needs time to learn. Even I had to practice many, many times."

She showed them how to take deep breaths when they felt upset. "When we feel calm inside, our bodies can move better too."

Slowly, the squirrels began to understand. They watched Polly's graceful movements. They listened to the gentle rhythm of the wind chimes. They felt the warm breeze on their fur.

"Let's try together now," suggested Polly. "We'll go very slowly. If someone makes a mistake, we'll just smile and keep dancing."

The four friends began to dance together. Polly's colorful feathers sparkled in the sunlight. The squirrels' fluffy tails swished in perfect harmony. When one squirrel stumbled, the others waited patiently for him to catch up.

"Look at us!" giggled the first squirrel. "We're really dancing!"

"And we're not bumping into each other anymore!" said the second.

"This feels magical," whispered the third squirrel.

Polly felt so proud and happy. "You see? When we help each other and stay patient, we can solve any problem. Dancing isn't about being perfect. It's about having fun and moving with joy."

The squirrels danced until sunset, their movements becoming smoother and more confident. They learned that working together peacefully was much better than getting frustrated.

"Thank you, Polly," they said. "You taught us that patience and kindness make everything better."

As the stars began to twinkle, Polly tucked her new friends into soft leaf beds in her treehouse. She had learned something too: sharing her love of dance made it even more wonderful.

The End.
