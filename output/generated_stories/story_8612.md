# Tommy Tortoise Takes Charge

In a cozy nursery filled with soft toys and colorful books, lived Tommy the tortoise. His shell was a beautiful green with golden spots, and he had the kindest brown eyes. Tommy loved to help in the nursery, but today he felt very sleepy.

"Good morning, Tommy!" called Miss Lucy, the nursery teacher. "Could you please help me organize the toy bins before story time?"

Tommy yawned a big yawn. "Maybe later, Miss Lucy. I'm too tired right now." He tucked his head into his shell and closed his eyes.

Miss Lucy smiled gently. "Okay, Tommy. Rest for now."

While Tommy napped, the other children played. Soon, toys were scattered everywhere! Blocks tumbled across the soft carpet, stuffed animals lay in messy piles, and puzzle pieces hid under the reading corner cushions.

When story time came, Miss Lucy looked around the messy room. "Oh my," she said softly. "We can't have story time with all these toys on the floor. Someone might trip and get hurt."

Tommy poked his head out and saw the chaos. His heart felt heavy. The children looked sad - they really wanted their story!

"I should have helped earlier," Tommy thought. "Now everyone is disappointed because of me."

Tommy took a deep breath and made a big decision. He slowly walked to the middle of the room.

"Miss Lucy," Tommy said in his gentle voice. "I'm very sorry I didn't help when you asked. I want to fix this mess right now!"

Miss Lucy smiled warmly. "Thank you for apologizing, Tommy. But this is a big job for one little tortoise."

"I have an idea!" Tommy said, his eyes twinkling. "Everyone, let's work together! I'll show you how to sort everything quickly."

Tommy began organizing. "Red blocks go in the red bin," he called out cheerfully. "Blue blocks in the blue bin!"

The children giggled and joined in. "This is fun!" said little Emma, dropping blocks into the right containers with satisfying *clinks*.

"Stuffed animals go on the soft shelf," Tommy continued, demonstrating by carefully placing a fluffy bunny in its spot.

Soon, everyone was helping. The room filled with happy chatter and the *swoosh* of toys being put away. Tommy felt proud as he watched everyone work together.

"Puzzle pieces in the puzzle box," called out Jake, shaking the pieces with a gentle *rattle*.

In just ten minutes, the nursery was tidy again! The carpet felt soft under their feet, and everything had its special place.

"Wonderful work, everyone!" Miss Lucy clapped her hands. "Tommy, thank you for taking charge and helping us fix the problem."

Tommy's shell seemed to glow with pride. "I learned something important today," he said. "When someone asks for help, it's better to do it right away. And when I make a mistake, I can always apologize and work hard to make things better!"

"That's called taking initiative," Miss Lucy explained. "It means doing the right thing without being asked twice."

The children gathered on the soft reading rug, and Miss Lucy began their favorite story. Tommy listened happily, knowing that taking initiative and saying sorry when needed made everything better.

From that day on, Tommy always helped right away, and the nursery stayed neat and cheerful for everyone to enjoy.

The End.
