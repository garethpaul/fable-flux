# Pip's Big Helper Day

Pip the kangaroo joey bounced happily in the sunny backyard. His soft brown fur sparkled in the warm sunlight. He loved hopping from the green grass to the colorful flower beds.

"Mama, can I help you today?" asked Pip, his big dark eyes shining with excitement.

Mama Kangaroo smiled. "Of course, my little joey! Today we're going to clean up our beautiful backyard."

Pip looked around. He saw empty juice boxes under the oak tree. Plastic bottles sat near the fence. Candy wrappers danced in the gentle breeze.

"Where did all this trash come from?" wondered Pip.

"Sometimes the wind blows litter into our yard," explained Mama. "It's our job to keep our home clean and safe for all the animals who visit."

Pip's pouch felt warm with pride. "I want to help! I'm big enough now!"

Mama gave Pip a small blue bag. "You can collect the light trash, and I'll get the heavy things."

Pip bounced to the oak tree. He carefully picked up a juice box with his tiny paws. It felt smooth and cool. Into the bag it went!

"Look, Mama! I got one!" Pip called out happily.

"Wonderful job, Pip!" said Mama. "You're being very responsible."

Hop, hop, hop! Pip bounded to the fence. He found three plastic bottles. They made a crinkly sound as he put them in his bag. The bottles were bigger than he expected, but Pip was determined to help.

"These are heavy," puffed Pip. "But I can do it!"

As Pip worked, he noticed a tiny field mouse watching from behind a flower pot.

"Hello there," said Pip gently. "Are you okay?"

The little mouse squeaked, "I was looking for food, but there's so much trash everywhere. It's hard to find safe places to play."

Pip's heart felt sad for his new friend. "Don't worry! I'm cleaning up our backyard. Soon it will be beautiful again!"

The mouse's whiskers twitched with happiness. "Thank you, Pip! You're very kind."

Pip continued hopping around the yard. He found candy wrappers that smelled sweet and sticky. He discovered paper napkins that felt soft but dirty. Each piece of trash went into his blue bag.

The sun felt warm on Pip's back as he worked. His paws got a little dirty, but he didn't mind. He was helping his home and his animal friends!

After an hour, Pip's bag was full. His legs felt tired, but his heart felt proud.

"Mama, look!" Pip called out, holding up his heavy bag. "I collected all this trash by myself!"

Mama hopped over and gave Pip a big hug. "Oh, Pip! You did such important work today. You helped keep our backyard clean and safe."

Together, they put the trash in the big recycling bins. Pip learned that plastic bottles could become new toys, and paper could become new books.

"Taking care of our environment is everyone's job," said Mama. "Even little joeys like you can make a big difference."

Pip looked around the clean backyard. The grass looked greener. The flowers smelled sweeter. A butterfly landed on his nose, making him giggle.

"I did that all by myself!" said Pip proudly. "I'm a big helper now!"

The little field mouse peeked out from behind the flower pot and waved. "Thank you, Pip! Our backyard is beautiful again!"

That night, Pip felt very proud as he snuggled with Mama. He had learned that even small joeys could do big, important things when they tried their best.

The End.
