# Momo's Feelings Flag

High up on a sunny rooftop, a brave little monkey named Momo sat with his tail curled around his feet. The warm breeze tickled his fuzzy ears, and he could see the whole neighborhood below. But today, Momo felt mixed up inside.

"I feel happy and sad and mad all at once!" Momo said to himself, scratching his head. "My tummy feels twisty, and I don't know why."

Just then, his friend Bella the bird landed nearby with a flutter of bright blue wings.

"Good morning, Momo!" chirped Bella. "You look puzzled. What's wrong?"

Momo's shoulders drooped. "I have so many feelings today, Bella. When I woke up, I felt excited about playing. Then I felt sad when I couldn't find my favorite banana. Then I felt angry when the wind blew my hat away. Now I feel confused!"

Bella tilted her little head. "Oh my! That's a lot of feelings for one morning. But you know what? Having lots of feelings is perfectly normal!"

"Really?" Momo's eyes grew wide.

"Really!" Bella nodded. "I have an idea. Let's make something special to help you understand your feelings better."

Bella flew down and returned with colorful pieces of fabric, some string, and a small stick. "We're going to craft a Feelings Flag!"

"A Feelings Flag?" Momo asked, his curiosity growing.

"Yes! Each color will represent a different feeling," Bella explained. "Red for angry, blue for sad, yellow for happy, and green for calm."

Momo clapped his hands together. "That sounds wonderful! But how do I make it?"

Bella showed him how to tie the colorful fabric strips to the stick. As they worked, she taught him feeling words.

"When you feel angry, your face might get hot and your hands might make fists," Bella said, demonstrating. "That's the red feeling."

"Like when the wind took my hat!" Momo said, tying a red strip carefully.

"Exactly! And when you feel sad, your eyes might get watery and your heart might feel heavy," Bella continued. "That's the blue feeling."

"Like when I couldn't find my banana," Momo nodded, adding a blue strip.

They worked together in the gentle sunshine, the fabric pieces dancing in the breeze. Momo learned that yellow meant the giggly, bouncy feeling of happiness, and green meant the peaceful, quiet feeling of being calm.

"Now," said Bella, "whenever you have big feelings, you can look at your flag and find the right color. Then you can say, 'I feel red-angry' or 'I feel blue-sad.'"

Momo held up his beautiful Feelings Flag. The colorful strips fluttered like tiny rainbows. "This is amazing! I can name my feelings now!"

"And remember," Bella added gently, "all feelings are okay to have. The important thing is to notice them and talk about them."

Momo practiced with his flag. "Right now, I feel yellow-happy because I made something beautiful, and green-calm because I understand my feelings better!"

As the sun climbed higher in the sky, Momo planted his Feelings Flag in a pot on the rooftop. Every day, he would look at it and practice naming his emotions.

"Thank you, Bella," Momo said with a big smile. "Now I know that feelings come and go like clouds in the sky, and that's perfectly okay!"

From that day forward, whenever Momo felt confused about his emotions, he would visit his Feelings Flag and remember that understanding his feelings made him even more courageous than before.

The End.
