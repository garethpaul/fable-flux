# Koko's Big Mistake

Koko the koala sat quietly in Grandma's cozy guest room, looking at the soft blue walls and the pretty white curtains that danced in the breeze. The room smelled like lavender and sunshine, just like Grandma always did.

But today, Koko didn't feel happy at all. His fuzzy gray ears drooped down, and his big round eyes looked very sad.

"Oh no, oh no," Koko whispered to himself, staring at Grandma's favorite lamp on the nightstand. The pretty glass shade had a big crack right down the middle. "What have I done?"

Earlier that morning, Koko had been playing with his bouncy red ball in the guest room. He threw it up high, higher and higher, until... CRASH! The ball hit the lamp and broke it.

Koko's tummy felt all twisty and worried. He thought about hiding the broken lamp under the bed, but that didn't feel right. He thought about blaming his little sister Kira, but that wasn't true.

"I need to think about this," Koko said, touching his soft nose with his paw. "What should I do?"

He remembered what Mama always said: "When we make mistakes, the brave thing to do is tell the truth and try to make things better."

Koko took three deep breaths, just like Mama taught him. "I can be brave," he said to himself. "I can make this right."

He carefully picked up the broken lamp and walked slowly to the kitchen, where Grandma was making her famous eucalyptus cookies. The sweet smell filled the air.

"Grandma?" Koko's voice was very small and shaky.

Grandma turned around with her warm smile. "Yes, my dear Koko?"

Koko's heart was beating fast like a little drum. He held up the broken lamp with both paws. "I... I broke your special lamp. I'm really, really sorry, Grandma. I was playing with my ball, and I threw it too high, and..."

Tears started rolling down Koko's fuzzy cheeks.

Grandma knelt down and gave Koko the biggest, warmest hug. "Oh, my brave little koala," she said softly. "Thank you for telling me the truth. That took a lot of courage."

"But I broke your favorite lamp!" Koko sniffled.

"Yes, you did," Grandma said gently. "But you were honest about it, and that means so much more to me than any lamp."

Koko wiped his eyes. "Can I help fix it? Or... or maybe I can give you my piggy bank money to buy a new one?"

Grandma's eyes twinkled. "That's very generous of you, Koko. How about we fix it together? We can use special glue to mend it, and it will have a beautiful silver line where it was broken. Sometimes broken things become even more special when they're fixed with love."

Koko's ears perked up. "Really?"

"Really," Grandma smiled. "And next time you want to play ball, let's do it outside in the garden where there's lots of space."

"Yes, Grandma! And I promise to be more careful," Koko said, feeling much better.

They spent the afternoon carefully gluing the lamp back together. When they finished, the silver line looked like a pretty design.

"See?" Grandma said, plugging in the lamp. It glowed beautifully. "Sometimes our mistakes can teach us important lessons and bring us closer together."

Koko hugged Grandma tight. He learned that being brave enough to say sorry and trying to make things better always feels much better than hiding or lying.

The End.
