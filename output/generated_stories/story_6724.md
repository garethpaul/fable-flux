# Joey's Big Workshop Adventure

Joey the kangaroo was a bouncy little fellow who loved to hop, hop, hop everywhere he went. His mama had told him he could help Grandpa Roo in the garage workshop today, and Joey was SO excited!

"Boing, boing, boing!" Joey bounced into the workshop, his fluffy tail wiggling with joy. The garage smelled like wood shavings and had shiny tools hanging on the walls. There were hammers, screwdrivers, and a big workbench covered with interesting things.

"Welcome to my workshop, little Joey!" said Grandpa Roo with a warm smile. "Today we're going to build a special birdhouse together."

Joey's eyes grew wide. "A birdhouse? Can I hammer? Can I saw? Can I do everything RIGHT NOW?" He started bouncing faster and faster around the workshop.

"Whoa there, speedy!" chuckled Grandpa Roo. "Building takes patience and careful steps. First, we need to slow down and think."

But Joey was too excited to listen. He grabbed a hammer and started swinging it wildly. CLANG! It hit a can of nails, spilling them everywhere with a loud CRASH!

"Oh no!" Joey's bottom lip started to wobble. "I made a big mess!"

Grandpa Roo knelt down beside Joey. "It's okay, little one. Sometimes when we're excited, our bodies want to move fast, but our minds need to catch up. Let's try something special."

"What's that?" sniffled Joey.

"It's called the 'Stop and Breathe' game," said Grandpa Roo. "First, we stop moving." Joey froze like a statue. "Now, we take three big breaths like this: in through our nose... and out through our mouth."

Joey breathed in deeply, smelling the sawdust. He breathed out slowly, feeling his bouncy energy settle down like bubbles floating to the ground.

"How do you feel now?" asked Grandpa Roo.

"Calmer," said Joey, surprised. "My heart isn't beating so fast!"

"Perfect! Now let's clean up together, and then I'll show you how real builders work."

They picked up every single nail, counting them as they went. "One, two, three..." Joey loved the clicking sound they made dropping into the can.

"Now," said Grandpa Roo, "let's imagine we're master builders. What do master builders do first?"

Joey thought hard, his little face scrunched up. "They... they look at the plan?"

"Exactly!" Grandpa Roo showed Joey a simple drawing of the birdhouse. "See? First the bottom, then the sides, then the roof. Step by step."

Joey practiced the "Stop and Breathe" game every time he felt his excitement bubbling up. When he wanted to grab all the wood pieces at once, he stopped and breathed. When he wanted to hammer super fast, he stopped and breathed.

Slowly and carefully, they built the most beautiful little birdhouse. Joey held the pieces steady while Grandpa Roo hammered. Joey painted the roof bright blue like the sky.

"I did it!" Joey said proudly, admiring their work. "I was brave and patient!"

"You sure were," said Grandpa Roo, giving him a big hug. "You learned that sometimes the best way to go fast is to go slow first. Your mind and your body worked together like a team."

Joey smiled wide. He had learned something very important: when his body wanted to bounce and rush, he could stop, breathe, and help his mind catch up. That made him feel strong and proud inside.

From that day on, whenever Joey felt too excited or bouncy, he remembered his workshop adventure and played the "Stop and Breathe" game.

The End.
