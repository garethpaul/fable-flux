# Otto Otter’s Little Lantern Lesson

Otto the playful otter wiggled into his study corner with a happy splashy step. The study corner was cozy and bright. Sunlight made warm squares on the rug. The air smelled like fresh paper and wood. Soft pillows hugged the floor. A tiny table held crayons, scissors, and shiny stickers. On the wall, a calendar showed a golden moon.

Otto clapped his paws. “Today is Lantern Night!” he chirped. His whiskers twitched with excitement. “Grandma taught me a special Lantern Night song. She sang it when she was small.” Otto loved this old family tradition. Each year, they made a paper lantern and sang the song.

He opened a box. Inside were orange paper, a safe little battery light, and ribbon that felt smooth and cool. “I will make my lantern,” he said. “Then I will share our Lantern Night with my friends.”

Otto heard little footsteps. His friends—Pip the chipmunk and Tulla the turtle—peeked in. Pip’s nose wiggled. “It smells like crayons!” Pip said. Tulla blinked slowly. “The room feels warm. Is it craft time?”

Otto smiled. “Yes! Today is Lantern Night at my house. We make lanterns, sing a song, and then take a tiny walk. We watch the lights glow.” His voice was gentle and proud. “It is our family tradition. Would you like to share it with me?”

Pip and Tulla nodded. “Yes, please!” they chimed.

Otto passed out supplies. “We will be fair,” he said. “We take turns. We share the stickers. We each get the same number.” He counted softly, tapping each sticker with a paw. “One for Pip, one for Tulla, one for Otto. One-two-three, fair and free!”

Pip grinned. “That feels kind.” Tulla smiled. “That feels safe.”

Otto hummed the Lantern Night song as they worked. “Glow, glow, little light. Kind hearts make the dark bright.” His voice was warm and sweet. Pip added tiny star stickers. Tulla pressed soft moon shapes. Otto punched holes and tied the ribbon. The ribbon slid like silk over his paw.

“Listen,” Otto said. “Grandma told me this tradition came from her grandma. We say thank you. We share stories. We walk slowly and breathe deep.” He took a slow breath in. “Smell the clean paper?” He breathed out. “Feel the quiet?”

Pip copied him. “In… out…” Pip’s shoulders relaxed. Tulla closed her eyes. “I feel calm,” she whispered.

Otto nodded. “Healthy habit time. Before we go, we wash our paws.” He pointed to a small sink in the corner. Water rushed like a tiny waterfall. “Clean paws keep us well.” They rubbed soap. It smelled like lemons. Bubbles tickled their fingers. They rinsed and dried with soft towels.

Back at the table, Pip reached for the glitter glue. “Me first!” Pip squeaked.

Otto held up a paw. “Fair turns. We can count to five. Then switch.” Pip paused, then nodded. “Okay.” Tulla smiled. Otto counted. “One… two… three… four… five.” Pip passed the glitter glue to Tulla. “Thank you,” Tulla said. Otto used it last, making gentle dots like fireflies. Sharing made the lanterns look extra special.

Soon, three lanterns sat on the table, glowing softly with their little battery lights. The study corner felt cozy and golden. Otto’s heart felt big. “Now,” he said softly, “we share the song.”

He showed them the motions Grandma taught. “Hands over hearts,” he said. Pip and Tulla placed small hands and flippers over their chests. “We think of people we love. We say thank you for our light. We walk three tiny steps. We sing together.”

They stood in a little line. The rug felt warm under their feet. Otto started the song, his voice slow and clear.

“Glow, glow, little light,
Kind hearts make the dark bright.
Step by step, we share our way,
Lantern love will guide our day.”

Pip’s eyes shone. Tulla’s shell gleamed. They took three small steps around the study corner, lanterns bobbing. The lights shimmered on the walls like golden fish. Otto felt happy and calm. He felt close to Grandma, even though she was not there.

When the song ended, Otto said, “Now we tell one kind thing we did today.” Pip piped up, “I waited my turn.” Tulla said, “I shared my stickers.” Otto said, “I taught our tradition.”

Otto opened the window a tiny crack. Cool air drifted in, smelling like rain and leaves. “Deep breath,” he said. They breathed in and out together, slow and easy. It felt good.

Pip looked at his lantern. “Can we do this again next week?” he asked.

Otto nodded. “Yes. And maybe we can learn your family ways too. We can share your traditions. We can be fair and take turns teaching.” His whiskers twitched with joy. “Sharing traditions brings us close. It helps us learn. It keeps our hearts kind and our bodies calm.”

Tulla patted her lantern. “I will ask my Nana about our tea clap song,” she said.

Otto smiled. “We will listen. We will learn. We will share.”

They put their lanterns on the shelf. They cleaned the table, wiped the glue, and washed their paws again. The study corner looked neat and bright. The lights glowed like small suns.

Otto hugged his friends. “Thank you for joining Lantern Night,” he said. “Today we learned to share traditions, to keep healthy habits, and to be fair.”

Pip and Tulla hugged back. “Glow, glow,” they whispered.

The End.
