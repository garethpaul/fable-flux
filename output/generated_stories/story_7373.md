# Benny Beaver’s Balcony Band

Benny was a busy beaver with big front teeth and a warm, wiggly nose. He lived in an apartment with a tiny, sunny balcony garden. The balcony smelled like mint leaves and wet soil. Little pots lined the rail: tomatoes, marigolds, and a tall bamboo plant that whispered shhh when the wind passed.

Benny loved to tap, hum, and drum. “Music makes my heart feel bright,” he said, patting his chest. He made a drum from a flowerpot, a shaker from a jar of beans, and a soft bell from a spoon and a teacup.

One morning, Mrs. Owl from downstairs taped a paper to the hallway door. “Balcony Quiet Rules,” it said in big letters with a smiley face. It showed simple pictures: a sun for daytime music, a moon for quiet time, and a tiny ear for listening to others. At the bottom it said, “Please follow directions so all neighbors can be peaceful.”

Benny nodded. “I can do that!” he said. His tail thumped once, a happy thump.

He went to the balcony. The sun felt warm on his fur. Bees buzzed over the marigolds. Benny tapped his flowerpot drum. Tippy-tap. Tippy-tap. He hummed a tune like a gentle river. Hum-hum, hum-hum.

“Lovely,” said Miss Cat next door, watering her basil. “That music is calm.”

“Thank you!” Benny smiled.

Then a big idea popped into his head like popcorn. “I will build a bigger drum!” he said. He picked up the watering can and turned it upside down. He grabbed a wooden spoon. Tap… BOOM! Tap… BOOM! The sound was loud and bouncy.

From below came Mr. Squirrel’s squeaky voice. “Eek! Too loud! My babies are napping!”

Benny’s ears drooped. “Oh no,” he whispered. He looked at the paper again. There was the sun, but also a little picture of two people talking. At the bottom, in tiny letters, it said, “Check with neighbors before big sounds.”

Benny took a deep breath. He smelled mint and damp dirt. “I can fix this,” he said.

He padded downstairs with his soft beaver feet and knocked on Mr. Squirrel’s door. Knock-knock.

Mr. Squirrel opened it, rubbing his eyes. Two tiny squirrels peeked out, holding blankets.

“I’m sorry,” Benny said. “I made big BOOMs. I did not follow all the directions. How can I help?”

Mr. Squirrel’s whiskers twitched. “Thank you for asking, Benny. Could you play gentle songs when the babies nap?”

Benny nodded. “Yes. I will play soft like a cloud.” He held his spoon like a flute and blew a slow whooo through his teeth. The babies giggled.

Back upstairs, Benny made a plan. He drew simple signs for his balcony: a blue sign with a cloud that said “Soft Music Time,” and a yellow sign with a sun that said “Band Time.”

He set the blue cloud sign on the table. He put cotton inside the watering can drum. Tap… puff. Tap… puff. “Nice and soft,” he said.

Later that day, Miss Cat and Mrs. Owl came by to see the garden. “We brought a bell and a gentle rattle,” said Mrs. Owl.

Benny’s eyes shone. “Let’s make a Balcony Band!”

He showed the signs. “When the cloud is up, we play soft. When the sun is up, we play a little louder, but not too loud. We follow the directions, and we listen with our ears and our hearts.”

Everyone nodded.

They began with the cloud sign. Benny brushed the bamboo with a leaf. Swish-swish. Miss Cat tapped the teacup very lightly. Ting… ting… ting. Mrs. Owl shook the rattle slow. Shhh… shhh… shhh. The music sounded like rain on leaves. Benny felt calm. His tail made a soft thup on the floor.

When the sun sign went up, Benny counted, “One, two, ready, go!” He remembered to keep the beat and watch his friends. He tapped the flowerpot, not the big drum. Tippy-tap, tippy-tap. Miss Cat meowed a melody, “La, la, la,” and Mrs. Owl hooted a harmony, “Hoo, hoo.” They smiled and swayed. A butterfly landed on the marigold and fluttered like a tiny dancer.

Suddenly, a gust of wind made the spoon bell clink too fast. Clink-clink-clink! The sound got busy and messy. Benny raised his paw. “Stop,” he said kindly. “Let’s try again. We can solve this peacefully.”

They took a breath together. In, out. Benny said, “Let’s follow the directions. I will count us in. We will keep a steady beat. Soft hands, strong listening.”

He tapped his nose. “Nose knows!” he giggled.

“One, two, ready, go.” The band began again. This time, the sounds fit like puzzle pieces. Swish. Ting. Shhh. Tippy-tap. The music felt smooth and friendly. Even Mr. Squirrel opened his window and smiled. The babies clapped tiny paws.

When the sun started to slide down and the sky turned peach, Benny switched the sign back to the cloud. “Quiet time,” he whispered. He put the spoon down and listened to the crickets chirp. Chirp… chirp… chirp.

Mrs. Owl patted Benny’s shoulder with a soft wing. “You made beautiful music, and you followed directions.”

Miss Cat purred. “You also used peaceful solutions. You asked, you listened, and you changed your plan.”

Benny’s whiskers lifted. “Music is for sharing,” he said. “When we follow directions and listen, our songs feel good for everyone.”

He looked at his garden, glowing in the soft light. He smelled mint and marigolds. He heard quiet night sounds and felt proud and calm.

“Balcony Band again tomorrow?” asked Mrs. Owl.

“Yes,” Benny said. “We will make gentle, joyful music. We will count, we will listen, and we will be kind.”

And they did.

The End.
