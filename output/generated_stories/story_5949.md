# Daisy's Rooftop Garden Mystery

Daisy was a gentle deer fawn who lived on a special rooftop garden. The rooftop had tall green plants, colorful flowers, and a small pond that sparkled in the sunshine. Daisy loved to nibble the fresh lettuce and carrots that grew there.

Every morning, Daisy would hop around the garden, checking on all the plants. She would sniff the sweet-smelling flowers with her soft black nose. The morning sun felt warm on her spotted brown fur.

But today, something was very wrong! All the healthy vegetables were disappearing. The crispy lettuce had big bite marks. The orange carrots were half-eaten. Even the juicy tomatoes were missing!

"Oh no!" said Daisy, her big brown eyes wide with worry. "Who is eating all our healthy food? This isn't fair to the other animals who need these vegetables too!"

Daisy felt sad and confused. She sat down next to the pond and thought carefully. Her ears twitched as she listened to the sounds around her.

"I need to think about this like a detective," Daisy said to herself. "Let me look for clues."

First, Daisy looked at the bite marks in the lettuce. They were very small and neat. "These are tiny bites," she noticed.

Next, she examined the ground around the vegetables. There were little scratchy marks in the dirt. "These look like small paw prints," Daisy observed.

Then Daisy heard a soft rustling sound coming from behind the big sunflower. She tiptoed over quietly, her hooves making gentle tapping sounds on the rooftop.

Behind the sunflower, Daisy found three little field mice. They were stuffing their cheeks with vegetables. Their tiny whiskers twitched nervously when they saw Daisy.

"Please don't be angry!" squeaked the smallest mouse. "We're so hungry, and these vegetables taste so good!"

Daisy felt her heart soften. She remembered feeling hungry before too. But she also knew it wasn't fair to take all the food without asking.

"I understand you're hungry," said Daisy gently. "But when you take all the vegetables, there's nothing left for the other animals. That's not fair or kind."

The mice looked down at their tiny paws. "We didn't think about that," admitted the biggest mouse. "We just saw the yummy food and started eating."

Daisy smiled warmly. "Let me show you something better. If we work together, we can plant more vegetables! Then there will be enough healthy food for everyone."

The mice's eyes lit up with excitement. "Really? We can help grow food?"

"Yes!" said Daisy. "And eating vegetables at the right times helps our bodies grow strong. We should eat some in the morning, some at lunch, and some at dinner."

Together, Daisy and the mice planted new seeds in the soft, dark soil. The mice used their small paws to dig tiny holes. Daisy used her hooves to gently pat the soil. They watered the seeds with cool water from the pond.

"This feels so much better than just taking food," said the middle mouse happily.

As the sun began to set, painting the sky orange and pink, Daisy and her new friends sat together. They shared some vegetables fairly - just enough for each of them to feel satisfied and healthy.

"Thank you for teaching us to think before we act," said the smallest mouse. "And for showing us how to be fair."

Daisy felt proud and happy. By thinking logically about the problem, listening to others' feelings, and finding a fair solution, she had made new friends and saved the garden.

From that day on, the rooftop garden grew bigger and more beautiful, with enough healthy food for everyone to share.

The End.
