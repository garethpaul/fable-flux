# Polly's Colorful Discovery

Polly the parrot loved making art. Her bright green feathers sparkled as she flew into the sunny art room. The room smelled like crayons and paint, and colorful pictures hung on every wall.

"Today I want to make the most beautiful painting ever!" Polly squawked happily. She picked up a paintbrush with her orange beak and dipped it in red paint.

Splash! The paint dripped everywhere except where Polly wanted it to go.

"Oh no!" Polly flapped her wings. She tried again with blue paint. Splish-splash! More paint dripped on the table.

Polly's feathers ruffled up. "This is too hard!" she screeched. "I can't make anything pretty!"

Just then, her friend Maya the mouse scurried in. Maya had soft brown fur and carried a tiny paintbrush.

"Hi Polly! What's wrong?" Maya asked in her squeaky voice.

"I'm trying to paint, but it's all messy!" Polly said, her voice getting louder. "I want to throw this brush away!"

Maya's whiskers twitched. "When I feel frustrated, my grandmother taught me to take three deep breaths. Want to try?"

Polly tilted her colorful head. "Three breaths?"

"Yes! Breathe in slowly... hold it... and breathe out," Maya demonstrated. "My family has done this for many years. It always helps me feel calmer."

Polly closed her eyes. She took a big breath in through her beak. "One..." She held it. "Two..." She breathed out slowly. "Three..."

"How do you feel now?" Maya asked gently.

Polly's feathers smoothed down. "Better! But I still don't know how to paint nicely."

Just then, Carlos the chameleon changed from green to purple as he walked in. "Did someone say painting?" he asked. "In my family, we paint with our fingers and feet! It's a special tradition from my homeland."

"With fingers?" Polly's eyes grew wide.

"And I paint with my tail!" added Zara the zebra, trotting in with paint on her black and white stripes. "My family taught me that art doesn't have to be perfect. It just has to come from your heart."

Polly looked at all her friends. They were all different colors and sizes, and they all made art in different ways.

"Can you show me?" Polly asked, feeling curious instead of frustrated.

Maya dipped her tiny paws in yellow paint and made little paw prints. Carlos rolled in blue paint and made swirly patterns with his body. Zara dipped her tail in green paint and made long, sweeping lines.

Polly watched and smiled. "I want to try something new too!" She dipped her wing feathers in purple paint and gently brushed them across the paper. Beautiful, soft strokes appeared.

"Wow!" all her friends said together.

"Each of us makes art differently," Polly realized. "And that's what makes it special!"

Soon, their paper was covered in paw prints, swirls, tail strokes, and feather brushes. It looked like a rainbow garden full of friendship.

"When I felt frustrated, breathing helped me calm down," Polly said proudly. "And learning about your family traditions helped me find my own special way to paint!"

"That's right," Maya squeaked. "When we're frustrated, we can breathe, ask for help, and try new things."

Polly hung their beautiful painting on the wall. Every time she looked at it, she remembered that being different makes the world more colorful, and taking deep breaths helps solve big problems.

The End.
