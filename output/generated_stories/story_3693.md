# Leo’s Little World Classroom

In a cozy, comfortable nursery, a brave little lion cub named Leo woke up to the soft hum of a fan and the sweet smell of warm milk. Sunlight made gentle squares on the rug. The rug felt fluffy under his paws. He stretched and yawned. “Today is learning day,” he whispered. His whiskers twitched with excitement.

All around the nursery were shelves with books, soft blocks, and a big world map on the wall. The map had bright colors and tiny stars. Leo loved the map. It looked like a friendly puzzle.

Miss Nia, the teacher, rang a small bell. “Good morning, friends,” she sang. Her voice sounded like a soft song. “Today, we learn about our big, beautiful world. We also learn how to listen and share.”

Leo’s ears perked up. Learning was his favorite adventure.

His friends gathered in a circle: a quiet panda named Bao, a giggly bunny named Mina, a curious giraffe named Tiko, and a shy turtle named Amari. They sat on round cushions that smelled like clean soap and lavender.

Miss Nia held up a picture book. “This book shows children from many places,” she said. “Some say hello like this: ‘Hello!’ Some say, ‘Hola!’ Some say, ‘Jambo!’ Some say, ‘Ni hao!’”

Leo bounced a little. “I want to say them all!” he roared softly.

Bao raised a paw. “Can I try too?”

Mina wiggled her nose. “Me too, please!”

“Of course,” said Miss Nia. “We will take turns.”

Leo felt brave and proud. He wanted to go first. He stood tall. “Hello!” he said. Then he said, “Hola!” His voice was loud and cheerful.

Tiko stretched his long neck. “Jambo!” he called, smiling.

Mina whispered, “Ni hao.” Her voice was tiny but bright.

Amari tucked into his shell, then peeked out. “Bonjour,” he said slowly.

Leo clapped his paws. “We are world friends!” he cheered.

Miss Nia smiled. “Yes, Leo. We are world friends. We respect others by listening and sharing. We learn from many voices.”

It was time for a hands-on activity. Miss Nia rolled out a big paper map on the floor. It felt smooth and cool under Leo’s paws. She put out crayons in many colors. The crayons smelled waxy and sweet.

“Let’s draw children from around the world,” she said. “Draw what they like to read, sing, and play. Everyone gets a turn with the colors.”

The basket of crayons sat in the middle like treasure. Leo’s eyes sparkled. He reached for the shiny blue crayon. “I’ll use blue first!” he said. He held it tight.

Bao reached out too. “Blue is my favorite,” Bao said softly.

Leo pulled the crayon close. “But I got it first,” he said. His tail swished. He wanted his picture to be perfect. He began drawing a smiling child with a blue hat.

Bao’s eyes looked sad. He lowered his paw. “Okay,” he whispered.

Miss Nia knelt beside them. “Leo,” she said gently, “what do we do when we learn together?”

Leo looked at the map. He remembered the hellos. He remembered the circle time. He remembered Miss Nia’s words: We respect others by listening and sharing.

He took a slow breath. The room smelled like crayons and clean cotton. “I… can share,” he said. He turned to Bao. “Do you want to take turns? I draw the hat, then you draw the sky?”

Bao’s eyes brightened. “Yes. Thank you, Leo.”

They made a plan. Leo used the blue to color the hat. The crayon made a soft scratch-scratch sound. Then he passed the blue crayon to Bao. Bao colored a big, calm sky. Scratch-scratch. It sounded like quiet rain.

Mina chose yellow. “I will draw sunshine,” she giggled. Tiko picked green for grassy fields. Amari chose red and drew a kite. Everyone took turns. Sometimes they waited. Sometimes they helped. Leo noticed the picture was better when many hands worked together.

Miss Nia held up another card. It showed a classroom far away. Children sat in a circle, just like them. “In many places,” she said, “children learn in different ways. Some clap and sing. Some read quietly. Some draw pictures. But they all learn and grow. We are part of one big world.”

Leo listened. His heart felt warm. “We can learn from each other,” he said. “When we share, we all get more.”

They finished their map picture. It showed children singing, reading, and playing in many colors. The paper smelled a little like wax and a little like hope.

Leo turned to Bao. “What word do you say to be kind?”

Bao smiled. “Xie xie means thank you.”

Leo tried it. “Sh-eh sh-eh,” he said carefully.

Bao giggled. “Almost! Xie xie.”

Leo tried again. “Xie xie.”

Miss Nia clapped softly. “Wonderful, Leo. Learning takes practice. Being kind takes practice too.”

The little bell rang for snack time. Crunchy crackers and sweet bananas waited on the table. Leo passed a napkin to Bao. “Xie xie for sharing the blue,” he said.

“Thank you for taking turns,” Bao replied.

Leo looked at the big paper map. It felt like a window to the world. He felt brave and happy. He had learned new words. He had learned to share. He had learned that friends everywhere like to learn too.

Miss Nia smiled at the class. “What did we learn today?”

Leo raised his paw. “Education helps us grow. We listen. We share. We respect others, near and far. We are world friends.”

Everyone nodded. The nursery felt extra warm and bright.

The End.
