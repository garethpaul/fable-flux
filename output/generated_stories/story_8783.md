# Miko’s Garden Shed Games

In a cozy garden shed, a kind mouse named Miko made a tiny home in a wooden toolbox. The shed smelled like fresh soil and sweet hay. Sunlight peeked through a dusty window and made sparkly dots in the air. Outside, birds chirped. Inside, tools hung like shiny friends.

“Good morning,” Miko squeaked, brushing sawdust from his whiskers. “Today is game day!”

Every week, Miko hosted Garden Shed Games. Friends came from the yard to play. There was a little ladder to climb, a curved hose to balance on, and paper leaf flags to catch in the breeze. It was all for fun and for moving bodies with care.

First came Bella the beetle, black and shiny. “Click-click!” went her small feet. Next rolled in Tumbles the roly-poly. He wiggled and could roll into a perfect ball. Then fluttered Nia the moth with soft wings like fuzzy rainclouds. Last, Pip the sparrow hopped on the windowsill. “Chirp! I can’t wait!” he said.

Miko clapped his tiny paws. “Welcome, friends. Today we try the balance hose, the leaf flag toss, and the tiny tool climb. We will move, help, and cheer. We are different, and that is great!”

Everyone smiled. The hose was smooth and green. It smelled like grass. The floor was cool and wooden. A leaf flag rustled from a rake handle.

“First, the balance hose,” said Miko. “Step, step, step.”

Pip hopped up. “Watch me!” he chirped. He fluttered his wings and wobbled across. “Easy-peasy!”

Bella tried. Her shiny feet went click-click-click. She moved slowly and carefully. “I am steady,” she said.

Tumbles rolled onto the hose. He tried to walk, but his feet tickled. “Oh!” he squeaked and curled into a ball. He rolled off softly, bump. “I can’t do it!”

Nia fluttered to the hose and landed light as a whisper. The hose moved. “Oops!” Nia said, flapping. She slid down gently like a snowflake.

Pip puffed his feathers. “Just hop, like me!” he chirped. His voice got loud. “It’s easy!”

Nia’s antennae drooped. Tumbles hid in his ball.

Miko felt a warm prickle in his tummy. Anger tried to grow like a hot pepper. He wanted to shout, “Stop!” But Miko took a big breath in through his nose. He smelled sweet hay. He let the breath out slow. He listened to the birds. The warm feeling cooled.

Miko spoke softly. “Pip, we all move in different ways. Your wings help you hop. Bella’s feet help her grip. Tumbles can roll. Nia can glide. Different is not easy or hard. Different is different.”

Pip blinked. “Oh,” he said. “I forgot.”

Miko nodded. “Let’s try again, with help. We can use our differences.”

He placed a tiny cloth on the hose. “Nia, your soft wings can keep this cloth still.”

Nia fluttered near and fanned her wings. The hose stopped wiggling so much.

“Bella, your click-click legs can make a steady beat,” said Miko. “Step, step, pause. We will follow you.”

Bella tapped a slow rhythm. Click-click, pause. Click-click, pause.

“Tumbles,” Miko smiled, “you roll very straight. You can be our guide at the end. Roll to the finish and show us where to go.”

Tumbles uncurled. “I can do that!” He rolled gently to the end of the hose and waited, a little ball of courage.

Pip fluffed. “I can cheer,” he chirped. “Chirp, chirp, you can do it!”

Together, they crossed. Miko stepped with tiny paws. Bella clicked the beat. Nia’s wings made the air calm. Pip’s cheers felt like sunshine. Tumbles waited and wiggled happily. One by one, they made it. When Tumbles’ turn came, he rolled along the side, then popped up at the end. “Ta-da!”

Everyone clapped and tapped. The shed echoed with tiny cheers.

“Leaf flag toss next,” said Miko. He placed the paper leaf flags in a basket. “We catch with care.”

Pip swooped fast. “I got it!” he chirped, grabbing a flag right away.

Nia tried to catch one, but the flag drifted. “Oh dear,” she said softly.

Pip started to zip again, but he paused. He took a breath like Miko did. He felt his feathers smooth. “Nia,” he said gently, “you float slowly. I will toss it slow, like a cloud.”

They tried again. Nia floated under the flag and caught it with a soft smile. “Thank you.”

Bella held two flags steady with her strong legs so Tumbles could practice bopping one with his nose. Tap! The flag landed on his back. “I did it!” he squeaked.

Finally came the tiny tool climb: a small step ladder leading to a wide shelf. The wood felt warm. The metal felt cool. Sun dust danced in the air.

Miko went first, showing careful steps. “One, two, pause. Hold on,” he said.

Bella climbed with the rhythm. Nia fluttered beside them to keep them company. Tumbles waited below, cheering in wiggly circles. Pip stayed near the top to guide with chirps. Everyone reached the shelf and looked out the window. The garden looked green and golden. Bees hummed. A breeze carried the smell of mint.

Miko’s heart felt full. “Friends,” he said, “we did it because we are not the same. We helped in different ways.”

Pip nodded. “I was fast, but fast is not always best. Slow can be smart.”

Nia smiled. “Soft can be strong.”

Bella clicked happily. “Steady can be kind.”

Tumbles spun once. “Round can be brave!”

Miko wrapped his tail around his paws. “When we feel mad or rushed, we can stop, breathe, and listen. We can use our bodies in the way that fits us. We can cheer for differences.”

They packed up the games and shared a tiny picnic: crunchy seeds, sweet crumbs, and a drop of water that looked like a shining marble. Laughter filled the shed like music.

As the sun moved, the sparkly dots of light slid across the floor. Miko looked at his friends. “Today,” he said, “we learned that different is beautiful. Working together takes good listening, good breathing, and good hearts.”

Everyone agreed. “Different is beautiful,” they said.

The End.
