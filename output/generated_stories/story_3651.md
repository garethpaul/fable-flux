# Ellie's Big Helper Decision

Little Ellie Elephant stood in the bright dining room, looking around with her big brown eyes. The morning sun streamed through the tall windows, making everything glow golden. But Ellie felt worried.

The dining room was a mess! Colorful plates sat scattered on the long wooden table. Cups and spoons were everywhere. Crumbs covered the floor like tiny pebbles under Ellie's feet.

"Oh my," Ellie said softly, wrapping her trunk around herself. "Mama asked me to help clean up after breakfast. But there's so much to do! Where do I even start?"

Ellie's trunk drooped down. She felt overwhelmed. Her heart beat fast like a little drum. The big room seemed even bigger when she felt so small.

"I could put the plates away first," Ellie thought out loud. "Or maybe sweep the crumbs? Or wash the cups?" She swayed back and forth, feeling confused.

Then Ellie remembered what Mama always said: "When you feel worried, take a deep breath and think step by step."

Ellie took a big breath through her trunk. "Whoooosh!" The air felt cool and calming. She closed her eyes and thought carefully.

"I need to make a good decision," Ellie whispered to herself. "What would be the best way to help?"

She opened her eyes and looked around again. This time, she noticed something important. The plates were heavy and breakable. The cups were slippery when wet. But the crumbs on the floor? Those looked perfect for her trunk!

"I know!" Ellie said, feeling excited. "I'll start with what I do best!"

Ellie carefully positioned herself near the biggest pile of crumbs. She took another deep breath and gently used her trunk like a vacuum cleaner. "Whoosh, swoosh!" The crumbs tickled as they went up her trunk.

"This feels good!" Ellie giggled. Her trunk was perfect for this job. She moved around the table, picking up every crumb she could find. Under chairs, near the window, by the door - Ellie's trunk reached everywhere!

As she worked, Ellie felt proud and happy. Her worried feeling melted away like ice cream in sunshine. She was helping! She was making good choices!

Just then, Mama Elephant walked into the dining room. "Oh my goodness, Ellie!" Mama said with a warm smile. "Look how clean the floor is! You made such a wonderful decision about where to start."

Ellie's cheeks felt warm with pride. "I was worried at first, Mama. There was so much to do! But then I thought about what I could do best."

"That's exactly right, sweet girl," Mama said, giving Ellie a gentle trunk hug. "When we feel overwhelmed, we can take deep breaths and choose one thing to start with. You chose perfectly!"

Together, Mama and Ellie finished cleaning. Mama carefully stacked the plates while Ellie organized the napkins with her trunk. They worked as a team, moving together like a gentle dance.

"You know what, Ellie?" Mama said as they put the last spoon away. "Making good decisions takes practice. Today you practiced thinking, breathing, and choosing wisely. I'm so proud of you!"

Ellie beamed with happiness. The dining room sparkled clean and bright. But even better, Ellie felt confident and brave.

"Next time I feel worried about a big job," Ellie said, "I'll remember to breathe, think, and start with what I do best!"

"That's my smart little helper," Mama said with a loving smile.

From that day on, whenever Ellie faced a big decision, she remembered her dining room success. Take a breath, think it through, and trust herself to choose well.

The End.
