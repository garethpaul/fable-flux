# Benny Beaver Helps at Nursery

Benny was a busy beaver with bright, curious eyes and a soft, flat tail. He loved the comfortable nursery where he played each day. The nursery had warm yellow walls, a round blue rug, and shelves full of books and blocks. Sunlight made little squares on the floor, and the room smelled like crayons and fresh soap.

“Good morning, Benny,” said Miss June, the teacher. Her voice was gentle like a song. “Today we are building, painting, and reading. We help each other here.”

Benny nodded. “I can help!” he said. He liked to nibble on ideas and build with his paws. He wanted to be helpful.

First, Benny saw Mia with a tall block tower. The blocks wobbled. Clack, clack. Mia’s hands shook. “Oh no,” she whispered.

Benny hurried over. His tail swished on the rug. “Do you want help?” he asked.

Mia looked at her tower. She took a breath. “Yes, please,” she said.

Benny smiled. He put one steady paw on the tower and held it still. “I will hold. You can stack.”

“Thank you,” said Mia. She placed a red block, then a green block. Tap, tap. The tower stood tall and strong. They clapped softly so it wouldn’t fall.

Miss June smiled. “You asked before helping, Benny. That was a good decision.”

Benny felt warm and proud. Asking first was smart. It felt kind.

Next, Benny heard a soft sniffle by the window. Liam sat with a picture book about frogs. “The words are hard,” Liam said. His cheeks were pink. “I feel a little sad.”

Benny sat beside him. The window felt warm on his fur. He listened to the birds outside: chirp, chirp. “Do you want to read together?” Benny asked.

Liam nodded. “Yes, please.”

Benny pointed to the picture. “This frog is green,” he said slowly. “Gr-een.”

Liam tried. “Green,” he said, smiling.

They took turns. Benny sounded out a tiny word. Liam said the next one. Their voices were soft like whispers. When they finished the page, Liam grinned. “We did it!”

“You are brave to try,” said Benny. He felt happy for Liam. Helping made the room feel bright.

Across the rug, Tia opened the art cupboard. “I want to paint a rainbow!” she sang. She had red paint in one hand and blue in the other. The jars clinked. “Benny, can you help me carry these?”

Benny walked over and reached for both jars at once. The jars slid. Oops! His paws were a little sticky from blocks, and the jars were smooth and cold.

“Wait,” Benny said to himself. He took a calm breath. He made a choice. “I will carry one at a time,” he decided. “Slow and careful.”

He gave Tia a small smile. “I will help. I will go slow.”

They carried the jars, one by one. Plop went the jars on the art table. No spills! Tia giggled. “Thank you, Benny.”

Benny watched Tia paint. Swish, swish went her brush. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple. The colors looked like candy and flowers. Tia dipped her brush in water. It made a soft, bubbly sound. “Do you want to try a new color?” she asked.

Benny blinked. He always chose brown. He loved brown like tree bark. But Tia held out a bright blue. “Okay,” Benny said. “I can try blue.”

He made a gentle swoop with the brush. The blue shone like a clear pond. “I like it!” Benny laughed. It felt good to try something new.

Just then, the music shelf made a rattle. The maracas rolled off and landed with a pat-pat on the rug. “Oh dear,” said Miss June. “We need to clean up before snack.”

Benny looked around. Mia’s tower was still tall. Liam smiled at his book. Tia painted a rainbow. Benny felt his heart grow big. He wanted to help again.

“Friends,” he called, “let’s clean up together!”

Mia looked at her tower. “I don’t want it to fall,” she said.

Liam looked at his book. “I want to finish the story,” he said.

Benny thought. He tapped his tail softly, thump, thump. He made another choice. “We can take turns,” he said. “I will put away the maracas. Mia can guard the tower. Liam can read one more page. Then we all help.”

Miss June nodded. “That is open-minded, Benny. You listened to your friends and made a fair plan.”

Benny picked up the maracas. They felt bumpy and light. Shake, shake, into the bin they went. He hummed a little tune. After one page, Liam closed the book. Mia waved. “Ready!”

They worked together. Benny stacked books. Mia put blocks in boxes. Liam wiped the table with a warm, soapy cloth. Tia washed brushes. Swish, swish, bubble, bubble. The nursery smelled like oranges and paint water. The room looked neat and happy.

Miss June clapped softly. “You helped others, you made good choices, and you tried new things. That is wonderful.”

Benny’s chest felt warm and proud again. He smiled at his friends. “Helping makes our nursery feel good,” he said.

“Yes,” said Mia. “Helping is kind.”

“Yes,” said Liam. “Helping is brave.”

“Yes,” said Tia. “Helping is fun!”

Benny wiggled his tail. He learned that asking before helping is kind, listening to others is fair, and trying new things is exciting. Helping-others, making good decisions, and being open-minded made their day bright.

The End.
