# Nutkin's Nighttime Helper

Nutkin the squirrel lived in the big oak tree outside Emma's bedroom window. Every morning, he would peek through the glass and watch the little girl play with her toys. The bedroom was so cozy with its soft pink walls, fluffy pillows, and warm sunlight streaming in.

One chilly morning, Nutkin noticed something different. Emma wasn't playing. She was sitting on her bed, sniffling softly. Tears rolled down her cheeks like tiny raindrops.

"Oh my," whispered Nutkin, pressing his fuzzy nose against the cool window. "Emma looks so sad. I wonder what's wrong?"

Nutkin's heart felt heavy seeing his friend upset. He wanted to help, but how could a small squirrel make a big difference?

Just then, Emma's mom came into the room. "Sweetie, I know you're sad about your teddy bear," she said gently. "Mr. Buttons fell behind your bed, and we can't reach him."

Emma sobbed louder. "I can't sleep without Mr. Buttons! He keeps the scary dreams away!"

Nutkin's ears perked up. He knew exactly where that teddy bear was! Yesterday, he had seen it tumble down into the narrow space between the bed and the wall.

"I can help!" Nutkin chittered excitedly to himself. "I'm small enough to fit in tiny spaces!"

But first, he needed to get inside. Nutkin scampered down the tree trunk, his tiny claws clicking against the bark. He squeezed through a small crack near the window frame that he had discovered weeks ago.

Inside the warm bedroom, everything smelled like lavender and sunshine. Nutkin could hear Emma's quiet sobs and felt his heart squeeze with worry.

He crept carefully to the bed, trying not to make noise. The space behind the bed was dark and dusty, but Nutkin wasn't scared. He thought about how much Emma needed her special bear.

"Be patient, Nutkin," he told himself. "Take your time."

Slowly, he squeezed into the narrow gap. It was tight, and his fluffy tail got stuck twice, but he didn't give up. Finally, he spotted Mr. Buttons wedged way back in the corner, covered in dust bunnies.

Nutkin grabbed the teddy bear's soft arm with his teeth and pulled. It was heavy! Much heavier than an acorn or walnut. But Nutkin kept trying, thinking about Emma's tears.

"I won't give up," he puffed, tugging harder. "Emma needs her friend."

After many tries, Nutkin managed to drag Mr. Buttons out from behind the bed. The teddy bear was dusty but safe.

Now came the tricky part. How could he let Emma know her bear was found?

Nutkin gently pushed Mr. Buttons into the middle of the bedroom floor where Emma would see him. Then he knocked over a small toy block to make a soft sound.

"What was that?" Emma looked up, wiping her eyes.

Her face lit up like sunshine when she saw Mr. Buttons! "Mom! Look! Mr. Buttons is back!"

Emma hugged her teddy bear tight, spinning around with joy. "Thank you, thank you, whoever helped me!"

From his hiding spot, Nutkin felt warm and happy inside. Seeing Emma smile was better than finding the biggest acorn ever.

That night, as Emma slept peacefully with Mr. Buttons, Nutkin curled up on his tree branch. He had learned something wonderful: when someone you care about is sad, even small acts of kindness can make the biggest difference.

The End.
