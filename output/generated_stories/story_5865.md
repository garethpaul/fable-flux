# Benny's Beautiful Balcony Garden

Benny the beaver loved his cozy balcony garden. He had tiny tomato plants, colorful flowers, and herbs that smelled like sunshine. Every morning, Benny would rush outside with his little watering can.

"Water, water, water!" Benny chattered excitedly. He splashed water everywhere - on the plants, on the floor, and even on himself! His fluffy brown tail got soaking wet.

But something wasn't right. His tomato plants looked droopy. The flowers seemed sad. Even the mint leaves were turning yellow.

"Oh no!" Benny squeaked. "Why aren't my plants happy?"

Mrs. Chen, his neighbor, peeked over from her balcony. Her garden was full of beautiful green plants and bright red tomatoes.

"Good morning, Benny," she said kindly. "I see you're working hard in your garden."

"My plants look sick," Benny said sadly. "I water them every day, but they don't grow like yours."

Mrs. Chen smiled. "Would you like to learn a special secret about gardening?"

Benny's eyes grew wide. "Yes, please!"

"In my country, we have an old saying: 'Patience makes the garden grow,'" Mrs. Chen explained. "Plants need just the right amount of water - not too much, not too little. And they need time to rest between waterings."

Benny's tail twitched with understanding. "You mean I should slow down?"

"Exactly! Let me show you." Mrs. Chen demonstrated how to touch the soil. "Feel here, Benny. Is the dirt dry or wet?"

Benny pressed his tiny paw into the soil. "It's very wet and squishy!"

"That means your plants have enough water for today. Tomorrow, check again. If the soil feels dry on top, then it's time to water."

Benny took a deep breath. This was hard! He wanted to water his plants right now. But he remembered Mrs. Chen's wise words.

"I can wait," Benny said slowly. "I can be patient."

The next day, Benny checked his soil carefully. Still wet! He felt proud that he didn't water his plants. Instead, he sang them a little song and told them about his day.

On the third day, the soil felt dry. "Now it's time!" Benny said happily. He watered each plant gently, giving them just enough to drink.

Days passed. Benny practiced checking the soil every morning. Sometimes he wanted to water anyway, but he would count to ten and remind himself: "Patience makes the garden grow."

Soon, something magical happened. Green shoots appeared on his tomato plants! The flowers stood up tall and proud. The herbs smelled stronger and more delicious.

"Mrs. Chen! Mrs. Chen! Look!" Benny called excitedly.

Mrs. Chen clapped her hands. "Wonderful, Benny! You learned to listen to your plants and control your excitement. That's called self-regulation."

"Self-what?" Benny giggled.

"Self-regulation means stopping to think before you act. You wanted to water every day, but you learned to wait and check first. That's very grown-up!"

Benny's chest puffed with pride. He had set a goal to grow healthy plants, and by learning patience and self-control, he had succeeded!

From that day on, Benny's balcony garden became the most beautiful one in the whole building. And whenever young animals visited, Benny would share Mrs. Chen's wisdom: "Patience makes the garden grow - and that goes for little gardeners too!"

The End.
