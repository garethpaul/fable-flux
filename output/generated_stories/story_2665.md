# Leo’s Quiet Roars

In a cozy study corner, soft like a cloud and bright with warm sunlight, lived a brave little lion cub named Leo. His study corner had a tiny desk, a basket of crayons, a stack of picture cards, and a fuzzy rug that tickled his paws. There was also a small bell that chimed, “ting!” when tapped. The air smelled like crayons and fresh paper, and the window let in a gentle breeze that whispered, “You can do it.”

Leo wanted to learn new skills. He wanted to clap rhythms, draw shapes, and practice his letters. But sometimes, his brave heart beat fast, and his paws felt wiggly. “I want to learn,” Leo said, “but I feel busy inside.”

Mama Lion peeked in with a warm smile. “Hello, my brave cub,” she said. “Let’s get ready. Preparation is a skill too.”

“Preparation?” Leo asked, twitching his little whiskers.

“Yes,” Mama said. “We will get your body ready, your breath ready, and your mind ready. That helps you learn.”

Leo nodded. “Okay! I am brave. I can try.”

First, they did a tiny exercise to get the wiggles out. “Stretch like the sun,” Mama said. Leo stretched his paws up, up, up. His back felt long. “Now shake like the rain.” Leo shook his paws—tap, tap, tap—on the rug. His tail swished. “Now stand tall like a mountain.” Leo planted his paws and felt steady and strong.

“I feel warm and tingly,” Leo giggled.

“Good,” Mama said. “Your body is ready.”

Next, they practiced mindfulness. “Listen,” Mama whispered. The room was quiet. Leo heard a soft bird outside: tweet, tweet. He heard the tiny bell: ting. He heard his own breath: in… out… in… out…

“Smell,” Mama said gently. Leo sniffed. Crayons smelled sweet and waxy. Paper smelled clean. “Feel,” Mama prompted. Leo rubbed the fuzzy rug. It felt cozy and safe.

“My breath is slow,” Leo said softly. His busy feeling got smaller, like a little bubble that popped.

“Your breath is your friend,” Mama said. “Now your mind is ready.”

“Now I learn?” Leo asked with bright eyes.

“Now you practice the skill,” Mama said. “Let’s start with clapping patterns.” She clapped, “Clap, clap… pause… clap!” Leo listened, then tried: “Clap, clap… pause… clap!” His paws made a happy sound, pat-pat… pause… pat!

“I did it!” Leo cheered.

“Yes,” said Mama. “Try a new one.” She tapped the bell, “ting,” then clapped, “clap.” Leo copied: “ting… clap.” He smiled. “This is fun!”

They moved to drawing shapes. Mama showed a circle card. “Round and round, smooth and slow.” Leo held a crayon. He took a slow breath. He drew a circle. It was a little wobbly.

“Oh,” Leo frowned. “It’s not perfect.”

Mama touched his paw. “Skill-building takes time. We try, we breathe, we try again.”

Leo nodded. “I can try again.” He shook his wiggles out—little exercise—then breathed in… out… and drew another circle. Rounder this time.

“Circle!” Leo grinned.

“Wonderful,” Mama said. “Now a square.” Leo drew line, turn, line, turn. The crayon made a soft scritch-scratch sound. “Scritch-scratch!” he giggled. “I like that sound.”

They practiced letters next. Leo traced a big L. “Down, then across.” He went slow and steady. “L for Leo,” he said proudly.

“L for Lion,” Mama added, nuzzling his ear.

Leo looked at his desk. There was one more card: a little checklist with tiny pictures—stretch, breathe, listen, try, rest. “What is this?” he asked.

“This is your preparation plan,” Mama said. “A plan helps you get ready every day.”

Leo tapped the bell, “ting,” and read the pictures. “First, I stretch like the sun.” He reached up. “Then I breathe in and out.” He closed his eyes and felt calm. “Then I listen.” He heard the birds again. “Then I try.” He traced another L. “And then I rest.” He put his paws on his heart and smiled.

His brave heart felt steady. His mind felt clear. His paws felt strong.

“Leo,” Mama said softly, “how do you feel now?”

“I feel ready,” Leo said. “I can learn one little step at a time.”

He looked at his shapes and letters. He listened to his breath. He felt the fuzzy rug. He stretched his paws. He clapped the pattern. “Clap, clap… pause… clap!” The bell chimed, “ting!”

“I learned new skills,” Leo said, “because I prepared, I moved my body, and I used calm breaths. When I get wiggly, I can do it again.”

Mama smiled. “That is the lesson, my brave cub. Preparation, mindfulness, and exercise help you learn. Practice makes skills grow.”

Leo hugged Mama. “I am a skill-builder!”

“Yes,” Mama whispered. “And your quiet roar is strong.”

Leo took one more slow breath. In… out… He traced one more L, neat and proud. The study corner felt cozy and bright. The breeze whispered again, “You can do it,” and Leo believed it.

The End.
