# Tap-Tap Tumble Tunes

In a sunny little house, there was a cozy laundry room. It smelled like soap and warm towels. The washing machine hummed, “Hmmmm,” and the dryer sang, “Whirrrr.” A determined rabbit named Rilla hopped in with a basket bigger than her belly.

“I can do it,” Rilla said. Her whiskers wiggled with courage. “I can wash the big blanket all by myself.”

The big blanket was fluffy and blue. It was heavy too. Rilla tugged it. Tug, tug. Plop! The blanket fell to the floor with a soft thump.

“Oh!” Rilla’s ears drooped for a moment. She took a deep breath. “I will try again.”

She crouched low. She pushed with her paws. She pulled with her teeth. “I can do it. I can do it,” she whispered. The washer door was open like a round mouth. But the blanket was too floppy. It slid and flopped and flumped right back down.

From the doorway, a small voice called, “Need a paw?”

It was Pip, a tiny mouse with bright eyes. Behind him waddled Dot, a gentle duck who wore a red scarf. And on the shelf peered Lolo, a sleepy ladybug who loved tapping rhythms with her tiny feet.

Rilla shook her head. “Thank you, friends, but I want to do it by myself. I am determined.”

Dot tilted her head. “Being determined is brave,” she quacked softly. “Being a team is brave too.”

Pip twitched his whiskers. “We can make a plan,” he squeaked. “A teamwork plan!”

Rilla looked at the big blue blanket. She felt a little twist in her tummy. The washer hummed, “Hmmmm,” again, like a calm song. Rilla took another breath. “Okay,” she said. “Let’s try together.”

Lolo fluttered down. “We can make music to stay calm,” she said. “Tap-tap, tap-tap.” She tapped a beat on the soap box: tap-tap, tap-tap.

Dot smiled. “Music helps me move slow and steady,” she said.

Pip squeaked, “Music helps me feel brave!”

Rilla’s ears perked up. “Music helps me feel strong,” she said. Her heart felt lighter.

“Team plan,” said Pip. “Rilla lifts one corner. Dot nudges with her soft wing. I pull this string tied to the blanket. Lolo makes our calm beat.”

Tap-tap, tap-tap. Lolo’s tiny feet made a gentle drum. The washer kept humming. The room felt peaceful.

“Ready,” Rilla said. “One, two, three!”

Rilla scooped the corner. Dot nudged the middle. Pip pulled the string. The blanket rose, slow and steady.

“Scoop, nudge, pull,” Dot sang softly. “Scoop, nudge, pull.”

Tap-tap, tap-tap. Hmmmm.

They moved together. The blanket slid toward the washer. Almost there—oops! It drooped to the side. Rilla’s nose wrinkled. “Oh no,” she said. “It fell again.”

Lolo tapped faster once, then slowed. “We can try again,” she whispered. “Calm and steady.”

Rilla nodded. “We can try again,” she said. “We will keep going.” She felt the word in her chest: persistence.

“One, two, three,” Pip squeaked.

They tried again. Scoop, nudge, pull. Tap-tap, tap-tap. Hmmmm.

This time, the blanket lifted higher. It slid into the round washer mouth. Plop! It landed inside. Everyone cheered.

“We did it!” Rilla laughed. Her ears stood tall. “We did it together!”

Dot flapped her wings happily. Pip did a tiny dance. Lolo drummed a happy roll: tap-ta-ta-tap!

Rilla reached for the soap. “Can we keep the music going?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Lolo. “Music helps us work as one.”

Rilla poured a little soap. It smelled like fresh lemons. Dot closed the washer door with a gentle click. Pip pressed the big round button. Beep!

The washer began to swish. Swish-swish, swish-swish. The window showed water and bubbles. The bubbles sparkled like tiny stars. Rilla and her friends watched, noses close to the glass.

“Listen,” Rilla said softly. “The washer is singing with us.”

Tap-tap, swish-swish, hmmmm.

They waited together. Pip told a silly joke about socks that liked to hop. Dot hummed a soft tune. Lolo tapped along. Rilla felt calm and proud. Her paws were not tired anymore. Her heart felt warm.

When the washer stopped, they loaded the blanket into the dryer. It was still a little heavy, but now they knew the plan. Scoop, nudge, pull. Tap-tap. Hmmmm. They moved together, smiling.

Soon the dryer went whirrrr. Warm air filled the room. It smelled cozy, like sunshine on towels. When the dryer dinged, Rilla reached inside. The big blue blanket was dry and fluffy. She hugged it. It felt like a cloud.

“Thank you, team,” Rilla said. “I was determined to do it alone. But with you, I did it better. I feel strong.”

Pip squeaked, “Teamwork makes big jobs small!”

Dot nodded. “Teamwork keeps hearts calm.”

Lolo tapped a soft heartbeat. “Teamwork makes music,” she said.

Rilla spread the blanket on the floor. They all climbed on. They made a blanket mountain and a blanket boat. They sang their teamwork song: “Scoop, nudge, pull! Calm and kind and full! Tap-tap, swish-swish, we can do it all!”

Rilla smiled. “I learned something,” she said. “Being determined is good. Asking for help is good too. Together, we can do big things.”

They snuggled into the warm blanket. The laundry room hummed a happy tune. Everyone felt peaceful and proud.

The End.
