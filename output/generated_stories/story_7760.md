# Ollie the Otter Learns to Help Himself

In a cozy nursery with soft blue walls and fluffy white clouds painted on the ceiling, lived a playful otter named Ollie. He had sleek brown fur and bright curious eyes. His whiskers twitched whenever he was excited, which was almost all the time!

Ollie loved splashing in his special water bowl and sliding across the smooth wooden floor. But there was one big problem - whenever something went wrong, Ollie would cry loudly for help instead of trying to fix it himself.

"WAAAH! HELP ME!" Ollie would shout when his favorite fish toy got stuck under the rocking chair. "SOMEONE COME QUICK!" he would cry when his water bowl was empty.

One morning, Ollie was playing with his colorful building blocks. He stacked them high, higher, highest! The tower wobbled and... CRASH! All the blocks tumbled down, scattering across the soft carpet.

"HELP! HELP!" Ollie cried, his whiskers drooping sadly. "My tower fell down! Someone fix it for me!"

But today, nobody came running. The nursery was quiet except for the gentle tick-tock of the clock and the soft rustling of leaves outside the window.

Ollie sniffled and looked around. The blocks lay everywhere - red ones by the toy chest, blue ones near the window, yellow ones under the changing table. His bottom lip trembled.

"Maybe... maybe I could try to pick them up myself?" Ollie whispered to his reflection in his water bowl.

He took a deep breath that smelled like lavender from the nursery's soft blankets. Slowly, Ollie waddled over to the red blocks. His little paws carefully picked up one block, then another. 

"I did it!" he squeaked with surprise. "I picked up two blocks all by myself!"

Feeling braver, Ollie gathered the blue blocks next. Then the yellow ones. His whiskers began to twitch with excitement again. Soon, all the blocks were in a neat pile.

"Now I can build again!" Ollie said proudly. This time, he built his tower more carefully. He placed each block gently, making sure it was steady before adding the next one.

When his tower was finished, it was smaller than before, but it stood strong and tall. Ollie clapped his paws together with joy.

Just then, Mama Bear peeked into the nursery. "Oh my, Ollie! What a wonderful tower! Did you build that all by yourself?"

Ollie's chest puffed up with pride. "Yes! And when it fell down, I cleaned up the blocks by myself too!" Then his whiskers drooped a little. "I'm sorry I used to cry so loudly for help all the time."

Mama Bear smiled warmly. "Thank you for that apology, little one. It's okay to ask for help when you really need it, but trying things yourself first makes you grow big and strong!"

"I feel proud inside my tummy," Ollie said, patting his furry belly. "Like warm sunshine is glowing in there!"

From that day on, whenever something went wrong, Ollie would take a deep breath and ask himself, "Can I try to fix this myself first?" Usually, he could! And when he really did need help, he asked politely instead of crying loudly.

Ollie learned that being self-reliant felt wonderful. He was grateful for his own clever paws and brave heart. The nursery felt even cozier now that it was filled with Ollie's proud, happy splashing and playing.

The End.
