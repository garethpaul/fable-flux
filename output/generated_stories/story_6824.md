# Mira Mouse and the Rooftop Parade

Mira was a kind mouse with bright eyes and tiny whiskers that tickled when the wind blew. She lived in a snug nest under the roof tiles of a tall, tall house. The rooftop was her favorite place. Up there, Mira could feel the warm sun on her fur, smell fresh bread from the bakery below, and hear birds chirp, “Cheep-cheep!” in the morning.

One sunny day, Mira peeked over the edge and squeaked, “Oh!” The neighbors were planning a rooftop parade. Long ribbons flapped. Little flags waved. People from many homes climbed up with careful feet. They brought drums, soft bells, and colorful scarves from their families and faraway places.

Mira’s heart did a happy skip. “A parade! I want to join,” she said. She wiggled her toes and did a tiny hop. But then she saw the tall gap between two roof lines. The parade would cross it on a narrow plank. The plank looked long. The plank looked wobbly.

Mira’s tummy did a nervous flip. “I am small,” she whispered. “I am scared.”

She hid behind a chimney and watched. A boy named Sami came with a blue drum from his grandfather. “Boom, ba-boom!” went his drum. A girl named Lila twirled a red scarf from her auntie who lived by the sea. “Swish, swish!” went her scarf. A grandpa named Kenji shook tiny bells from his childhood. “Ting-a-ling!” went the bells. They all smiled at each other even though their scarves, drums, and bells were different.

“It’s a sharing parade,” said Lila. “We bring something special from our families.”

Mira’s whiskers twitched. She had something special too. In her nest was a small seed rattle her cousin sent from a far field. It made a soft, friendly sound. “Shika-shika,” it sang.

Mira took a breath. The wind smelled like bread again. Her paws felt the warm tiles. “I can be brave,” she told herself, “and I can move my body strong.”

She picked up a tiny stick and touched it to the roof. Tap. Tap. Tap. “I will practice balancing,” Mira said. She stood on one foot. Then the other foot. She bent her knees low. She stood tall like a tree. She took three little steps forward and three little steps back. Her tail helped her balance. The sun warmed her shoulders. Her breath went in and out like a gentle wave.

Sami peeked around the chimney. “Hi, little friend,” he said softly. “Do you want to join the parade?”

Mira squeaked, “I am scared of the plank.”

Sami smiled. “I was scared of drumming in front of people,” he said. “My grandpa said, ‘We are all different, and that makes the song big.’ He helped me practice. We can practice together.”

Lila came over and swirled her scarf slowly so it whispered, “Sssswish.” “I get shy too,” she said. “But when I twirl, I feel brave. Let’s move together.”

Grandpa Kenji rang his bells, “Ting-a-ling,” and bowed. “Courage grows when we share,” he said kindly.

Mira’s ears perked up. “Okay,” she said. “Let’s practice.” They made a little exercise game on the safe, flat part of the roof.

“Step-step,” said Sami, marching his feet. “Boom, ba-boom.”

“Stretch-stretch,” said Lila, reaching her arms up like the sky. “Swish, swish.”

“Balance-balance,” said Grandpa Kenji, standing on one foot, then the other. “Ting-a-ling.”

Mira copied her friends. Step-step. Stretch-stretch. Balance-balance. She felt her legs grow steady. Her paws felt sure. Her heart felt brave.

Then came the big moment. The parade lined up. The narrow plank waited. The wind hummed softly in Mira’s ears. She held her little seed rattle. “Shika-shika,” it sang. “I can do this,” she whispered.

Sami went first, drumming a slow beat to help everyone step together. “Boom… step. Boom… step.”

Lila followed, scarf twirling like a friendly flame. “Swish… step.”

Grandpa Kenji crossed with smiling eyes. “Ting… step.”

Mira put one paw on the plank. The wood felt smooth and warm. She looked at the far roof. She took a breath. Step. Step. “Shika… step,” went her rattle.

“Go, Mira!” called Sami.

“You can do it!” cheered Lila.

“Little steps make big courage,” called Grandpa Kenji.

Mira kept moving. Step. Step. The plank did not feel so wobbly now. Her tail helped her balance. Her body remembered the practice. Her rattle sang, “Shika-shika,” as if it were smiling.

At last, her paws touched the other roof. She made it! The crowd clapped and cheered. “Hooray!”

Mira’s cheeks felt warm and happy. “Thank you,” she squeaked. “I was scared, but I tried. We moved together, and we shared our special things.”

The parade began. Drums boomed. Scarves swished. Bells rang. Mira shook her seed rattle. People danced little steps, big steps, and silly steps. They taught each other moves from their homes and families. Sami showed a side-step from his grandpa. Lila taught a sea-twirl from her auntie. Grandpa Kenji shared a bell-bow. Mira showed a tiny tail-balancing dance from her cousins in the fields.

Everyone smiled. The rooftop felt like a warm hug. The air smelled like bread and flowers. The sky looked wide and blue.

Mira stood tall and said, “We are all different, and that makes our parade big and beautiful. When we share and move together, we learn and grow brave.”

And from that day on, whenever the wind sang on the rooftop, the neighbors had a little parade. They listened, learned, and danced with kind hearts and strong steps.

The End.
