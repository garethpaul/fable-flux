# Bubbles in the Bathroom

Splash the dolphin was small, shiny, and very playful. One sunny morning, Splash visited a bathroom that felt like a tiny ocean. The tiles were smooth and cool. The tub was a big white whale shape. Warm steam floated like little clouds. Drip, drop, drip, sang the faucet. The whole bathroom smelled like oranges from the soap.

“Hello, tub!” Splash chirped, flicking his tail. “Hello, bubbles!”

He pressed the silver tap with his nose. Water gushed out with a gentle roar. The tub filled with warm water that hugged him like a cozy blanket. Splash spun in a circle, making swooshes and tiny waves. “Whee!”

Just then, a little rubber duck bobbed near the edge. “Quack?” said the duck.

“Hi!” Splash smiled. “I’m Splash. What’s your name?”

“I’m Ducky,” said the duck, bouncing. “I love to float. But I can’t reach my friend, Brushy. He’s the toothbrush by the sink. I want to say good morning.”

Splash peeked over the tub. On the sink, a bright red toothbrush stood tall in a cup. His bristles were neat, and he looked proud and clean.

“Hello down there!” Brushy called. “I like to keep teeth shiny. But today, I feel lonely. Could someone bring me closer to my friends?”

Splash thought. He loved making friends feel happy. He took a deep breath and listened to the bathroom. Drip, drop. Swish, swish. His tummy felt fluttery. This was a problem to solve.

“I can help,” Splash said. “But we must be careful. Water can splash and make the floor slippery.”

“That’s a good decision,” said Ducky. “Slow and safe.”

Splash looked around. He saw a small blue cup by the sink. He saw a soft towel with little fish embroidered on it. He also saw a tiny boat toy with a colorful flag. The flag had patterns like zigzags and dots. The colors reminded Splash of different places in the big ocean.

“I have an idea!” Splash said. “Let’s make a kind, careful plan, like team friends.”

“What’s the plan?” asked Ducky, paddling closer.

“First, I will make gentle waves to move the little boat. The boat can carry you, Ducky. The towel will be the dock, so no one slips. Then we ask Brushy to hop into the cup. The cup can slide to the edge, and we can say hello together.”

“Quack-tastic!” Ducky giggled.

Brushy wiggled in his cup. “I’m ready! And I have a song from my family. We sing it every morning when we brush: Scrub, scrub, smile so bright. Teeth feel clean, day feels light!”

“I like your brushing song,” Splash said. “In my ocean family, we have a morning song too. It goes: Flip, flap, swim and play. Help a friend and share your day.”

“Let’s share our songs,” Brushy said. “Different songs from different homes make our hearts big.”

Splash placed the towel on the side of the tub, making a soft landing. He nudged the boat with his nose. Swoosh. The boat bobbed gently. Ducky climbed aboard. “Quack! Captain Ducky here!”

“Easy does it,” Splash whispered. He pushed the boat with small, careful waves. Swish, swish. The boat glided across the tub. The colorful flag fluttered like a tiny rainbow fish.

The boat reached the towel-dock. Ducky hopped onto the towel and wobbled, then steadied. “I’m okay!” he laughed.

“Your turn, Brushy,” Splash called. “Can you slide your cup closer to the edge?”

Brushy hummed his brushing song as he scooted. “Scrub, scrub, smile so bright…” He slid very slowly, very safely, until—plop!—the bottom of the cup nestled into the towel’s soft folds.

Splash clapped his fins. “We did it! Teamwork!”

Ducky nuzzled Brushy. “Good morning, friend.”

“Good morning,” Brushy said happily. “Thank you, Splash. You made a safe plan. You listened. You helped.”

Splash’s heart felt warm, like sunshine on water. “That’s what friends do. We think, we choose, we help.”

They all sat together on the towel-dock, watching steam curl like dancing ribbons. The bathroom smelled like oranges and a little like mint now too. Tap-tap went the faucet, calm and steady.

“Let’s share our songs,” Ducky said. “First Brushy’s, then Splash’s.”

They sang together, taking turns. Brushy’s song made their mouths feel tingly and clean. Splash’s song made their tails (and duck tails and bristles!) wiggle with joy. The boat’s little flag waved like it was clapping too.

“Even though our songs are different,” Splash said, “they both make us feel good. Different ways can both be special.”

“That’s cultural appreciation!” Brushy chimed. “We can love each other’s songs and learn from them.”

“I appreciate your song,” Ducky said softly. “It teaches me to care for my smile.”

“And I appreciate your floating,” Splash giggled. “You are steady when I need a dock.”

After singing, Splash peeked at the water level. “Time to tidy up,” he said. “Let’s put things back so the bathroom stays safe.”

“Good decision,” said Brushy. He slid back to the sink with Ducky guiding the towel. Splash turned the tap off with his nose. Drip, drop… stopped. The room grew quiet and cozy.

“Thank you for being my friends,” Splash said.

“Thank you for helping,” Ducky quacked.

“Thank you for listening and planning,” Brushy added.

Splash took a happy, deep breath. The warm air felt soft. His heart felt full.

Today we learned: Friends listen. Friends make safe plans. Friends can enjoy different songs and still sing together. When we use kind words and careful choices, everyone feels welcome.

The three friends smiled.

“Scrub, scrub, smile so bright,” Brushy sang.

“Flip, flap, share your light,” Splash replied.

“Quack, quack, that feels right,” Ducky finished, giggling.

The End.
