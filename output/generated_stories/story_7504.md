# Koko's Wellness Garden

Koko the koala loved spending time in Grandpa's old garden shed. The wooden walls smelled like fresh earth and sunshine, and colorful flower pots lined the shelves like a rainbow.

One morning, Koko found his friend Benny the bunny sitting sadly in the corner of the shed.

"What's wrong, Benny?" Koko asked in his gentle, calm voice.

"I feel grumpy and tired," Benny said with a big frown. "My tummy hurts, and I don't want to play."

Koko sat down slowly next to his friend. He took a deep breath, smelling the sweet lavender growing in the window box. "Sometimes when I feel that way, I do special things to help my body feel better. Would you like to try them with me?"

Benny nodded, his ears drooping.

"First," said Koko, "let's drink some fresh water." He reached for a small watering can filled with cool, clean water. "Our bodies need water like flowers need rain." They each took small sips, and Koko showed Benny how the water felt refreshing as it went down.

"That does taste good," Benny said, sitting up a little straighter.

Next, Koko picked up a basket of colorful fruits and vegetables from the garden. "Eating rainbow foods helps our bodies grow strong," he explained. "Red strawberries, orange carrots, green lettuce – each color gives us different vitamins!"

They shared sweet strawberries and crunchy carrots. Benny's eyes began to brighten as he tasted the fresh, juicy flavors.

"Now for my favorite part," Koko said with a warm smile. "Let's move our bodies!" He stood up and began to stretch his arms high like tree branches. "Stretching feels so good for our muscles."

Benny giggled as they stretched together, reaching for the shed's ceiling and bending side to side like flowers swaying in the breeze.

"And when we feel worried or upset," Koko continued, "we can breathe slowly and deeply." He showed Benny how to breathe in through his nose, counting to three, then breathe out through his mouth. "Feel how the air fills your belly like a balloon."

They sat quietly together, breathing and listening to the gentle sounds of birds chirping outside the shed.

"You know what else helps?" Koko asked. "Talking about our feelings with friends. When we use kind words and listen to each other, we both feel better."

"Thank you for listening to me, Koko," Benny said, his voice much happier now. "I feel so much better! My tummy doesn't hurt anymore."

"That's wonderful!" Koko replied. "Taking care of our bodies and feelings is like taking care of a garden. We need to give ourselves good food, fresh water, movement, rest, and love."

Benny hopped up excitedly. "Can we do these wellness activities every day?"

"Of course!" Koko said. "Every morning, we can meet here in the garden shed and take care of ourselves together."

From that day on, Koko and Benny made wellness their daily adventure. They drank water, ate rainbow foods, moved their bodies, practiced calm breathing, and always talked kindly to each other.

The garden shed became their special wellness place, where they learned that taking care of themselves helped them feel happy, strong, and ready for each new day.

The End.
