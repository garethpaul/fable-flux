# Polly Speaks Up

Polly the parrot loved her home in the cozy office. Sunlight streamed through the big window. Her colorful feathers shimmered green, red, and yellow. She could see everything from her tall perch.

Every morning, Polly watched Mom work at her desk. The keyboard clicked softly. Papers rustled. Mom always had a steaming cup of coffee that smelled rich and warm.

But Polly had a problem. A big problem.

"Squawk! Good morning!" Polly chirped happily.

"Good morning, Polly," Mom said, typing away. "Here's your breakfast."

Mom poured plain seeds into Polly's bowl. The same boring brown seeds. Every. Single. Day.

Polly's tummy rumbled. The seeds tasted dry and bland. She pecked at them sadly. Through the window, she could see other birds eating juicy berries and crunchy nuts from the oak tree.

"I want different food," Polly thought. But she didn't know how to tell Mom.

For three days, Polly barely ate. Her bright feathers looked dull. She felt tired and sleepy.

"Polly, you're not eating," Mom noticed. "Are you sick?"

Polly tilted her head. How could she explain? She was a smart parrot. She could think of a way!

First, Polly flew to the window. She tapped her beak on the glass. "Look! Look!" she squawked.

Mom looked up from her computer. "What is it, Polly?"

Polly pointed her wing at the birds outside. They were eating colorful berries. "Yum! Yum!" Polly said clearly.

Mom scratched her head. "You want to go outside?"

"No, no!" Polly flew back to her food bowl. She pushed the plain seeds around with her beak. Then she made a sad face and shook her head. "Blah!"

Next, Polly had another idea. She flew to Mom's desk and gently tugged on her sleeve with her beak.

"What's wrong, sweetie?" Mom asked.

Polly marched to the kitchen. She stood by the fruit bowl full of red apples and orange oranges. She flapped her wings excitedly. "Yes! Yes!"

"Oh!" Mom's eyes grew wide. "You want fruit?"

"YES!" Polly squawked loudly. She did a happy dance on the counter.

Mom smiled big. "Polly, why didn't you tell me sooner? I didn't know you were tired of just seeds!"

Mom washed a crisp apple. She cut it into small pieces. The apple smelled fresh and sweet. Polly's mouth watered.

"Here you go, my smart girl," Mom said.

Polly took a big bite. The apple was crunchy and juicy. "Mmm! Thank you!" she chirped.

From that day on, Mom gave Polly healthy variety. Colorful berries, crunchy nuts, fresh vegetables, and yes, some seeds too. Polly's feathers grew bright and shiny again. She felt strong and happy.

"I'm proud of you for speaking up," Mom said, scratching Polly's head gently. "You used your brain to solve your problem. You showed me exactly what you needed."

Polly puffed out her chest proudly. She had learned something important. When something wasn't right, she could find ways to communicate. She could ask for help. She could speak up for herself!

"Good job, Polly!" she said to herself.

And every morning after that, Polly enjoyed her colorful, healthy breakfast in the sunny office, knowing that her voice mattered.

The End.
