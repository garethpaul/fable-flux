# Bellie Bird and the Quiet Treehouse

Bellie was a small, blue singing bird with a bright yellow beak. She loved to sing. Her favorite place to sing was the cozy treehouse high in the old oak tree. The treehouse smelled like pine and warm sunshine. Soft leaves brushed the windows, and the wooden floor felt smooth under tiny bird feet.

One breezy morning, Bellie flapped to the treehouse to practice a big song. “Trill-a-la! Tweet-tweet-tee!” she sang, her feathers puffing with joy.

Down on the rope ladder, she saw her friends. Pip the squirrel carried a basket of crunchy acorns. Miri the mouse pushed a tiny broom. Tavi the turtle wore a little red scarf.

“Hi, friends!” Bellie chirped. “Let’s get ready for our music day!”

Pip wiggled his tail. “I’m hanging streamers!”

Miri squeaked, “I’m sweeping the floor!”

Tavi smiled slowly. “I’ll set up the chairs.”

Bellie felt a sparkle of excitement. She wanted to sing right away. Her chest felt big and bubbly. “Trill-a-la!” she burst out. “Tweet-tee-tee!” Her song rang bright and loud.

Miri dropped her broom and covered her ears. “Oh! That was loud next to me.”

Pip blinked. “Bellie, I can’t hear where the streamers go.”

Tavi frowned a little. “My head feels busy with so many sounds.”

Bellie stopped. Her wings drooped. The room felt different now, crowded with sounds and little sighs. She felt a squeeze in her tummy, like a small knot. “I’m sorry,” she whispered. “I was excited. I didn’t listen.”

Miri took a slow breath and nodded. “I like your singing, Bellie. But the broom needs quiet.”

Pip added, “We can work together if we take turns.”

Tavi said softly, “Maybe we can make a plan.”

Bellie’s heart thumped. She sat on the window sill and looked out at the leaves fluttering in the soft wind. She listened. Swish-swish went the leaves. Tap-tap went the broom. Creak went the rope ladder. Her tummy knot felt a little smaller.

“I’m feeling excited,” Bellie said, touching her chest with a wing. “My body gets loud when I’m excited. I want to sing. But I also want to help. Can we make a quiet plan?”

Pip’s eyes lit up. “Yes! A plan!”

Tavi nodded. “We can use listening ears.”

Miri smiled. “Let’s share turns.”

Bellie hopped to the center. “How does this feel? First, quiet for sweeping. Then, normal voices for hanging streamers. Last, singing time.” She looked at her friends and listened for their words and faces.

Miri’s shoulders relaxed. “That feels good to me.”

Pip clapped his paws. “Me too!”

Tavi gave a slow thumbs-up. “Good plan.”

Bellie took a deep, gentle breath. In through the beak, out through the beak. She felt her chest soften. “I will hum very softly while I wait,” she said. “Like this.” She closed her eyes and hummed a tiny sound, “mmm-mmm,” soft as a cotton cloud.

Sweep-sweep went Miri’s broom. Pip tied green streamers in a neat line. Tavi placed little leaf chairs in a circle. Bellie hummed smooth and quiet, listening to the rhythm of her friends.

When the floor was clean, Miri squeaked, “All done!”

When the streamers hung like vines, Pip grinned. “Done!”

When the chairs were in place, Tavi smiled. “Ready.”

Bellie felt the hum in her chest turn into a bright warm glow. “Now it’s singing time,” she said. “Would you like to sing with me?”

Pip nodded fast. “Yes!”

Miri’s whiskers twitched. “I will sing and clap.”

Tavi took a deep breath. “I will sing slow and low.”

Bellie asked, “How loud feels good?” She looked at each friend.

Miri touched her ears. “Soft to medium.”

Pip bounced, then paused. “Medium is okay.”

Tavi said, “Soft feels calm.”

Bellie listened and thought. “We will start soft,” she said. “If it feels okay, we can go to medium. If it feels too loud, we can go soft again.”

They all agreed. Bellie raised her wing, then lowered it like a gentle curtain.

“Trill-a-la, soft and sweet,” sang Bellie.

“Clap-clap, pat-pat,” went Miri, keeping the beat like tiny raindrops.

“Ta-ta-ta,” hummed Tavi, deep as a drum.

“Tweet-tweet!” sang Pip, bright as a twinkle.

The treehouse filled with warm, cozy sound. It felt like honey and sunshine. Bellie watched her friends’ faces. When Pip’s eyes got big, she softened her voice. When Miri smiled wider, she kept the gentle rhythm. When Tavi looked calm, she felt proud inside.

After the song, everyone sighed a happy sigh. The leaves outside went swish-swish, like they were clapping too.

Bellie said, “I learned something today. My feelings matter, and your feelings matter. When I feel excited, I can take a breath, say my feeling, and listen. Then we can make a plan together.”

Pip nodded. “Listening helps us work together.”

Miri added, “Taking turns feels kind.”

Tavi said, “Soft can be strong.”

Bellie fluffed her feathers. The knot in her tummy was gone. She felt light and peaceful. “Thank you for helping me,” she chirped. “Now our music day is ready.”

They shared one more soft song, and this time, it fit just right.

The End.
