# Joey Roo and the Balcony Garden Bounce

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a springy tail. He lived with Mama Roo in a cozy home. Their favorite place was the balcony garden. It had green pots, crunchy carrot tops, soft mint leaves, and a tiny rain barrel that went drip, drip, drip.

Every morning, Joey hopped onto the balcony and sniffed the plants. The air smelled fresh and leafy. Little bees buzzed past, “Bzz, bzz,” on their way to tiny purple flowers. Sun warmed Joey’s fur, and the tiles felt smooth under his feet.

One sunny day, Joey found a surprise. “Oh no!” he gasped. “My lettuce is flat!” The leaves were squished and brown. A small plastic bag was stuck in the soil. A broken toy car lay on the path. A soda can rolled, clink clink, in the wind.

Joey’s cheeks got hot. His tummy felt stormy. “I’m mad!” he shouted. His tail thumped, thump, thump. He wanted to kick the can off the balcony.

Mama Roo came outside and put a gentle paw on his shoulder. “Big feelings,” she said softly. “Let’s keep our bodies safe. No kicking off the balcony. What can we do first?”

Joey squeezed his paws. He took a big breath in—whoosh—and a long breath out—whooo. “I can calm my body,” he said. He hugged himself. His tummy felt less stormy.

“Good plan,” said Mama. “Now, let’s think. What is the problem?”

“There is trash in our garden,” said Joey. “It hurts the plants.”

“What do we want?” asked Mama.

“A clean, happy garden,” said Joey.

“How can we solve it safely?” Mama smiled. “Let’s use smart steps.”

Joey nodded. “Step one: stay safe.” He put on his little garden gloves. Mama put on hers too. They looked at the balcony rail. “Do not drop things,” Joey reminded himself. “People walk below.”

“Step two: sort,” said Mama.

Joey picked up the plastic bag and the soda can. “This goes to recycling,” he said. “Plastics and cans can be used again!” He placed them in the blue bin. He held up the broken toy car. “Hmm. The wheels are off.”

“Let’s see what we can reuse,” said Mama.

Joey wiggled the wheels. “They roll!” He smiled. “We can make a plant label with the car top.” He washed the toy and stuck it on a stick. He drew a little carrot on a paper circle and taped it on. “Carrot corner!” he cheered.

“Step three: care for the plants,” said Mama.

They touched the soil. It felt dry and dusty. Joey listened. The rain barrel went drip, drip. “We can save water,” he said. “Let’s use the rain barrel.”

They filled a small watering can. Joey held it with both paws. He poured slowly, plip, plip, around the roots. “Sip, don’t splash,” he whispered to the plants. The leaves perked up. The mint smelled cool and sweet.

A breeze blew. The soda can on the floor wobbled and clinked. Joey remembered. “Step four: stop future mess!” he said. “We need a plan.”

Mama nodded. “What could help?”

Joey looked around. “A lid for the bin, so the wind can’t blow things out.” He searched the cupboard and found a snug lid. Click! “And a sign!” Joey drew a bright picture: a happy plant and a bin with arrows. He wrote simple words with Mama’s help: “Please recycle. Keep balcony safe.”

He taped the sign inside the door so every guest could see it. He also found a small net to cover the soil of the lettuce pot, so light bits couldn’t fly in. “Safe and snug,” he said.

“Step five?” Mama asked.

Joey thought. “Share and teach!”

They knocked on the neighbor’s door. Mrs. Birdie opened it with a chirp. “Tweet-hello, Joey!”

Joey held up his sign. “We are keeping our balcony garden clean and safe,” he said. “We sort, reuse, and water with rain. Will you help?”

Mrs. Birdie smiled. “Tweet yes! I will put my cans in the bin and keep bags inside.”

Back on the balcony, Joey checked the lettuce again. He touched the leaves. They felt cool and soft. He sniffed the mint. Mmm! He heard bees humming and water dripping softly. His heart felt calm and proud.

“I was mad before,” Joey said. “I took breaths. I used my thinking steps. I did not kick. I helped the Earth.”

Mama hugged him. “That is sustainability,” she said. “We care for our garden, we reuse, we recycle, we save water, and we keep everyone safe.”

Joey grinned. “I am a Green Roo!” He bounced, boing, boing, but only on the soft mat so it was safe.

That evening, the sky turned pink and golden. Joey and Mama shared a tiny salad from the balcony. “Crunch, crunch,” went the carrots. “Fresh,” said Joey. “Tasty,” said Mama.

Joey patted the rain barrel and the blue bin. “Thank you,” he whispered to the garden. “We work together.”

He looked at his sign and took a slow, happy breath. “When I feel mad, I can pause, breathe, and make a plan. When I care for plants and sort my trash, I help the Earth.”

Mama nodded. “Little steps make a big difference.”

Joey smiled at the twinkly stars. “I’ll keep bouncing smart,” he said, “and growing green.”

The End.
