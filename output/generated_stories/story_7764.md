# Polly's Patient Practice

Polly the parrot loved to sing beautiful songs. Her bright green feathers sparkled in the morning sunlight as she perched on her favorite spot - the bathroom windowsill. The bathroom had wonderful sounds that echoed and made her voice sound extra special.

"La-la-la!" Polly sang happily, fluffing her colorful tail feathers. She wanted to learn a new song she had heard on the radio, but it was very tricky. The tune had lots of high notes and low notes that twisted and turned like a winding river.

"I want to sing it perfectly RIGHT NOW!" Polly squawked loudly. She tried to sing the whole song at once, but it came out all jumbled. "Squawk-la-tweet-chirp!" It sounded terrible!

Polly felt frustrated. Her orange beak drooped down sadly. "Why can't I sing this song? I'm a good singer!" she complained, stamping her little orange feet on the smooth windowsill.

Just then, Mama Bird flew to the bathroom window. She had soft gray feathers and kind, twinkling eyes. "What's wrong, my dear Polly?" she asked gently.

"I can't learn this new song!" Polly huffed. "It's too hard, and I want to sing it perfectly right now!"

Mama Bird tilted her head thoughtfully. "Listen carefully, sweet Polly. Do you hear the water dripping from the faucet?"

Polly stopped squawking and listened. "Drip... drip... drip..." went the faucet. Each drop made a tiny, clear sound.

"Does the water try to drip all at once?" Mama Bird asked with a warm smile.

"No," Polly said slowly. "It goes one drop at a time."

"That's right! Learning a song is just like those water drops. You learn it one small piece at a time, with patience," Mama Bird explained. "Let's try together. First, let's just learn the first line."

Mama Bird sang softly: "Twinkle, twinkle, little star."

Polly listened carefully, her head tilted to one side. The bathroom made the notes echo beautifully. She tried singing just those words: "Twinkle, twinkle, little star." It sounded much better!

"Wonderful!" Mama Bird chirped. "Now let's add the next line."

They practiced slowly, adding one line at a time. Polly discovered that when she listened carefully and took her time, the notes came out clear and sweet. The bathroom's echo made their voices blend together like a magical choir.

"How I wonder what you are," Polly sang, and this time it sounded beautiful!

After practicing patiently for a while, Polly could sing the whole song. Her voice rang out clearly in the bathroom, bouncing off the tiles and creating lovely echoes. She felt so proud and happy!

"Thank you, Mama Bird!" Polly chirped gratefully. "I learned that being patient helps me learn better. And listening carefully makes everything sound more beautiful!"

"You're very welcome, dear," Mama Bird said, giving Polly a gentle wing-hug. "Remember, the most beautiful things take time to learn. Just like how flowers grow slowly in the garden, your singing grows more beautiful with patient practice."

From that day on, whenever Polly wanted to learn something new, she remembered the patient water drops. She would take her time, listen carefully, and practice bit by bit. Her bathroom concerts became the most beautiful music in the whole house!

And Polly always felt grateful for Mama Bird's wise lesson about patience.

The End.
