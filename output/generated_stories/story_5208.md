# Breezy Porch Choices

On a sunny morning, a kind mouse named Miko sat on a peaceful front porch. The porch boards felt warm under Miko’s tiny feet. A gentle breeze whispered, shhh, shhh, through the leaves. The air smelled like fresh flowers and a little like toast from the kitchen.

Miko loved this porch. He liked to listen. He liked to notice. He liked to decide what to do each new day.

“Good morning, porch,” Miko said softly. “I feel calm. I feel ready.”

A blue butterfly fluttered by. Flit, flit. It danced over the porch rail and rested on a flower pot.

“Hello, Butterfly,” Miko whispered. “You move so gently.”

“Hello, Miko,” the butterfly seemed to say with its wings. “Where will you go today?”

Miko placed a paw on his chest and took a slow breath in. He let it out slowly. In and out. In and out. Calm breathing helped Miko think.

“I have choices,” Miko said. “I could sweep the porch. I could draw with chalk. I could bring lemonade to friends.”

Just then, a little squirrel named Suri scampered up the steps. Her tail swished fast. Tap-tap-tap went her feet on the wood.

“Miko! Miko!” Suri squeaked. “There is a big leaf pile in the yard! Come jump right now!”

Miko smiled. He liked Suri. He liked leaf piles too. But he also liked to choose with care.

“Hello, Suri,” Miko said in a warm voice. “I hear your idea. It sounds fun. I want to think.”

Suri bounced. “Think later! Jump now!”

Miko felt his tummy wiggle with the fast energy. He put his paw on his belly. He took a slow breath. In and out.

“I will use my calm,” Miko said. “When I am calm, I can choose.”

Suri stopped bouncing and watched. “What are you doing?” she asked.

“I am being still for a moment,” Miko said. “I breathe. I listen. I talk kindly. Then I decide.”

Suri blinked. The breeze tickled her whiskers. “Okay. I can try too,” she said. She put her paws on her belly and breathed in and out, just like Miko. Her tail slowed.

Miko looked around the porch. He felt the warm boards. He smelled the flowers. He heard a faraway bird sing, tweet, tweet. He listened to his own voice inside.

“I want to help, play, and rest,” Miko said. “We can do all three.”

“How?” asked Suri.

Miko spoke clearly. “First, let’s sweep the porch together. That helps. Then, let’s draw a map with chalk. That is play. The map can show a path to the leaf pile. After that, we can rest with lemonade and a book on the porch.”

Suri’s eyes lit up. “A map! A leaf path! Lemonade!” she cheered. “Yes!”

Miko smiled. “Thank you for listening. Thank you for waiting. Let’s begin.”

They found a small broom. Swish, swish, swish. Dust danced in the sun. Tiny petals fell like pink snow. Miko hummed a calm tune. Suri swept slowly, and the porch looked tidy and bright.

“All clean,” said Miko. “Now the map.”

Miko and Suri picked bright chalk sticks: blue like the sky, green like grass, yellow like sunshine. Scritch, scratch, the chalk made friendly lines on the wooden step by the edge. Miko drew the porch. Suri drew the yard. Together, they drew a big, happy leaf pile with a smiling face.

Miko tapped the map with his paw. “This line is the safe path. We walk, not run, down the steps. We check left and right. We keep space for each other.”

Suri nodded. “Walk, check, space,” she repeated. “Got it!”

They followed their chalk map. Step, step. The grass felt cool on their toes. The breeze smelled like leaves and a hint of mint. A bird hopped nearby, watching.

“Left, right,” Miko said, looking both ways. “Safe to go.”

They reached the leaf pile. It rustled like soft paper. Swish, swish. They touched it first.

“Hello, leaves,” Miko said kindly. “We will jump gently.”

“One at a time,” Suri added.

Miko counted, “One, two, three, jump!” He floated into the leaves. Shhhh—soft and cozy. Suri waited and then jumped too. The pile hugged them with a crunchy cuddle.

They laughed and popped up. Miko’s whiskers tingled. Suri’s nose had a tiny leaf hat.

“Again?” Suri asked.

“Let’s do two more,” Miko said. “Then lemonade and rest.”

They jumped two more times, calm and happy. Then they walked back to the porch, following the chalk map. The boards felt warm again. The butterfly fluttered by, flit, flit.

Miko poured lemonade into tiny cups. The cups felt cool. The drink tasted sweet and sunny.

Suri sipped. “Mmm. I like your plan, Miko,” she said. “How did you make such a good choice?”

Miko smiled and spoke gently. “I stayed calm. I used my breath. I listened to my feelings. I used kind words. I made a plan. That is good decision-making.”

Suri nodded slowly. “Calm helps me too,” she said. “When I am calm, I can hear you. I can hear me.”

They leaned back. The porch breeze said, shhh, shhh. The bird sang, tweet, tweet. The chalk map glowed bright at their feet. The day felt peaceful and full.

Miko looked at Suri. “When we are calm and we communicate, we make good choices,” he said.

Suri smiled. “Let’s remember that every day.”

Miko raised his cup. “To calm hearts and kind words.”

They clinked cups softly. Tink. The butterfly danced once more, and the porch was quiet and happy.

The End.
