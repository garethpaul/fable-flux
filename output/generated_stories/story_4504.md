# Shelly's Musical Discovery

Shelly the turtle peeked through the crack in the door of the musical practice room. Inside, she could hear beautiful sounds - *ting-a-ling* went the triangle, *boom-boom* went the drums, and *la-la-la* sang the children.

"Oh my," whispered Shelly, pulling her head back into her shell. "I wish I could make music too, but I'm too shy to go inside."

Every day, Shelly would listen from outside the practice room. The music made her little heart feel happy and warm, like sunshine on her green shell. Her tiny feet would wiggle when she heard the dancing songs, but she was too scared to join the other animals.

One rainy afternoon, Shelly discovered something wonderful. The practice room was empty, but someone had left the door open just a crack. 

"Maybe... maybe I could peek inside?" she wondered aloud.

Slowly, very slowly, Shelly pushed the door open with her nose. *Creak* went the door. The room was full of shiny instruments - drums that looked like big pots, bells that sparkled like stars, and maracas that looked like colorful eggs.

"Wow!" gasped Shelly. "So many beautiful things!"

But when she tried to reach the drums, they were too high. When she tried to shake the maracas, they were too heavy for her little flippers. Even the triangle was hanging too far up.

Shelly felt sad. "I'm too small and too slow," she sighed. "I'll never be able to make music."

Then something caught her eye - her own shell! When a raindrop from the window fell on it, it made a gentle *plink* sound.

"That's interesting," said Shelly. She wiggled her body, and her shell made a soft *tap-tap* sound against the floor.

Curious now, Shelly looked around the room with new eyes. She noticed some small wooden sticks on the floor - drumsticks that had been dropped. Using her mouth, she picked one up and gently tapped it against her shell.

*Tap-tap-tink!* 

"Oh my! That sounds lovely!" she exclaimed.

Shelly discovered she could make different sounds by tapping different parts of her shell. The top made a higher *ting* sound, and the sides made a deeper *tock* sound. When she moved her flippers, they made a gentle *swish-swish* like soft rain.

Soon, Shelly was making her very own music! She swayed her body back and forth, creating a gentle dance. *Tap-ting-tock, swish-swish, tap-ting-tock!*

Just then, Mrs. Robin, the music teacher, peeked in. "What beautiful music! Who's making those lovely sounds?"

Shelly quickly pulled her head into her shell, but Mrs. Robin had already spotted her.

"Hello there, little turtle," said Mrs. Robin kindly. "That was wonderful music you were making!"

"Really?" whispered Shelly, poking her head out just a little.

"Oh yes! You used your shell like a drum and created your very own instrument. That's very clever and resourceful!"

From that day on, Shelly became the special shell-drummer in the music class. She learned that being different wasn't something to hide from - it was something that made her special. Her unique shell-music added a beautiful new sound that no other instrument could make.

"I may be shy and small," Shelly would tell her new friends, "but I found my own way to make music. Sometimes the best solutions come from using what you already have!"

The End.
