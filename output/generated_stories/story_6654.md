# Daisy's Block Tower Plan

Little Daisy the deer fawn tiptoed into Mama's home office on her tiny hooves. The morning sun streamed through the big window, making golden patches on the soft carpet. Daisy's brown eyes sparkled as she spotted something wonderful - a big box of colorful building blocks sitting on the floor!

"Oh my!" whispered Daisy, her fluffy white tail wiggling with excitement. The blocks were red, blue, yellow, and green, all different shapes and sizes. They looked like the most amazing toys ever!

Mama Deer looked up from her computer and smiled. "Good morning, little one. Would you like to play with the blocks while I work?"

"Yes, please!" Daisy said, clapping her small hooves together. "I want to build the tallest tower in the whole world!"

Daisy rushed over and grabbed a big red block. She placed it on the carpet with a soft *thump*. Then she reached for a tiny yellow triangle and balanced it right on top.

*Crash!* Down fell the little yellow block, rolling across the office floor.

"Hmm," said Daisy, tilting her head. "That didn't work very well."

Mama Deer turned in her chair. "Sometimes building takes a special plan, sweetie. What do you think might work better?"

Daisy looked at all the blocks scattered around her. She picked up the big red square again, then looked at a medium blue rectangle, and finally at the tiny yellow triangle.

"Maybe..." Daisy said slowly, "maybe I should use the big blocks first?"

"That sounds like a smart strategy!" Mama said encouragingly. "Big blocks can hold up smaller ones better."

Daisy carefully placed the big red square down first. *Thunk!* It sat perfectly still. Then she chose a medium blue rectangle and gently set it on top. It stayed! Next came a smaller green circle, then finally the tiny yellow triangle.

"Look, Mama! It's working!" Daisy giggled, her tail swishing happily.

But Daisy wanted her tower to be even taller. She reached for more blocks, but her tower started to wobble. *Wobble, wobble, sway!*

"Oh no!" Daisy gasped. "It's going to fall!"

Just then, Papa Deer peeked into the office. "What's happening in here?"

"I'm building a tower, but it's wobbly," Daisy explained, holding her breath as the blocks swayed back and forth.

Papa smiled. "Would you like to take turns building together? Sometimes two builders can make an even better tower!"

"Yes!" Daisy said excitedly. "You can have the next turn!"

Papa carefully added a wide purple rectangle to make the base stronger. Then it was Daisy's turn again. She chose a medium orange square. Back and forth they went, taking turns, thinking carefully about each block.

"This is so much fun!" Daisy laughed as their tower grew taller and stronger. "Taking turns makes it better!"

When they finished, their tower reached almost to Daisy's nose! It had big blocks on the bottom, medium blocks in the middle, and small blocks on top, all perfectly balanced.

"We did it!" Daisy cheered, dancing around on her little hooves. "Our strategy worked! Big blocks first, then taking turns, then thinking before we build!"

Mama and Papa clapped their hooves together. "You learned something very important today, Daisy," Mama said. "Good planning and working together makes everything better!"

Daisy nodded proudly, admiring their beautiful, strong tower. She had discovered that the best adventures happen when you think first, then act!

The End.
