# Benny Beaver's Sharing Garden

Benny Beaver lived in a cozy apartment with a beautiful balcony garden. Every morning, he would waddle outside with his tiny watering can, listening to the happy chirping of birds and feeling the warm sunshine on his fuzzy brown fur.

"Good morning, little plants!" Benny said cheerfully, sprinkling cool water on his tomatoes, carrots, and lettuce. The green leaves sparkled with dewdrops like tiny diamonds.

One sunny day, Benny noticed something wonderful. His garden had grown so much! Red tomatoes hung heavy on their vines, orange carrots peeked out from the soil, and crispy lettuce leaves danced in the gentle breeze.

"Wow!" Benny clapped his small paws together. "I have so much food! But this is way too much for just me."

Just then, he heard a tiny voice. "Excuse me, Mr. Beaver?"

Benny looked down and saw a little gray mouse family standing by his balcony railing. The mama mouse held two tiny baby mice close to her.

"We're new to this neighborhood," said Mama Mouse softly. "We've been walking all day looking for food for my babies. They're so hungry, and we don't know where to find any."

Benny's heart felt warm and fuzzy. He remembered when his grandpa beaver had taught him something very important: "When we have enough, we should always share with those who need help, no matter where they come from."

"Of course!" Benny said with a big smile. "Please, take some vegetables from my garden!"

But then Benny heard a loud voice from the next balcony. "Hey! Those mice don't belong here! Send them away!"

It was Mr. Grumpy Cat, who always complained about everything.

Benny stood up tall on his hind legs and spoke clearly. "Mr. Cat, these mice are hungry and need help. My garden has plenty of food to share. Everyone deserves kindness, no matter if they're new or different."

Mr. Grumpy Cat's whiskers twitched. "But... but they're not from around here!"

"That makes it even more important to help them," Benny explained patiently. "When we're kind to everyone - our neighbors and visitors from far away - we make our whole world a better place."

Benny carefully picked the biggest, juiciest tomato and the crunchiest carrot. He handed them to Mama Mouse, whose eyes filled with happy tears.

"Thank you so much, Benny," she squeaked. "You have such a generous heart."

The baby mice nibbled the sweet vegetables and giggled with joy. Their tiny tummies felt full and happy.

Even Mr. Grumpy Cat's expression began to soften as he watched the little family share their meal.

"Maybe... maybe I could share some of my fish treats too," Mr. Cat said quietly.

Soon, all the balcony neighbors were sharing food and stories. Benny learned that Mama Mouse's family had traveled from very far away, looking for a safe place to live.

As the sun set, painting the sky pink and orange, Benny felt proud and happy. His balcony garden had brought everyone together.

"When we share what we have and welcome everyone with kindness," Benny thought, "we create a beautiful community where everyone belongs."

From that day on, Benny's balcony became known as the "Friendship Garden," where neighbors from near and far always felt welcome.

The End.
