# Roo’s Bouncy Helping Heart

Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a big soft pouch. One sunny morning, Roo’s family got a visitor. Auntie Em, a kind emu, came to stay in the guest room. The room was cozy and calm. It smelled like fresh soap and clean cotton sheets. A little lamp made a warm, golden glow. A window showed a round green tree and a blue sky.

Roo bounced in, thump-thump, thump-thump. “Welcome, Auntie Em!” Roo chirped. “Do you like the guest room?”

Auntie Em smiled, but her feathers drooped. “Thank you, Roo. The room is lovely,” she said softly. “My legs feel tired today. I came to rest.”

Roo stopped bouncing. “Oh,” Roo said, ears tilting. “Rest is good.”

Mama Kangaroo gave Roo a gentle pat. “Auntie Em needs quiet and comfort,” Mama whispered. “Let’s help her feel safe here.”

Roo nodded, tail swishing. The guest room felt warm and friendly, but there was a little problem. Auntie Em needed help, and Roo was not sure how to help. Roo loved to bounce and dance and giggle. Roo did not want to make loud noise in the guest room. Roo wanted to be useful.

Roo took a deep breath. The room smelled like lemon tea. A small clock tick-ticked like a tiny drum. Roo had an idea. “Maybe I can do a quiet dance,” Roo whispered. “A gentle dance for Auntie Em. And I can ask our friends to help.”

Roo hopped to the door and peeked into the hall. “Friends?” Roo called softly. “Can you help me make Auntie Em feel happy and safe?”

Soon, friends arrived. Tilly the turtle slid in, shell shining. Pip the possum peeked down from the doorframe. Buzz the bee hummed at the window. “We can help,” they all said in soft voices.

Roo’s heart felt warm. “Let’s make the guest room a caring place,” Roo said. “We can do community service. That means we help together.”

Tilly nodded slowly. “I can dust the side table,” she said, brushing with a soft cloth. Swish, swish.

Pip brought a tiny basket of lavender from the garden. The purple flowers smelled sweet and calm. “For the pillow,” Pip said.

Buzz zoomed in little loops, humming like a lullaby. “Bzz-bzz, I will sing a gentle song.”

Roo folded a small blanket and placed it at the foot of the bed. The fabric felt fuzzy like a cloud. “Auntie Em, would you like a soft blanket?” Roo asked.

“Yes, thank you,” Auntie Em said, smiling a little. Her eyes looked less tired.

Roo still wondered, “What about my dance?” Roo loved to dance with big jumps and spins, but this was a quiet room. Roo tapped one foot on the carpet. The carpet felt like a field of tiny flowers. The tap made a soft tap-tap, like raindrops.

Roo had a new idea. “I can make a quiet dance. A kindness dance!” Roo said. “A dance that says, ‘We care for you.’”

Roo showed the friends the steps. “Step… pause… smile,” Roo whispered. “Sway… sway… hug your arms. Then a little bow.”

They practiced. Step… pause… smile. Sway… sway… hug. Bow. Their feet made soft sounds like whispers. Their smiles warmed the room like sunshine. The lamp’s glow seemed brighter. The lavender scent floated like a tiny purple cloud.

Roo peeked at Auntie Em. “Would you like to watch our kindness dance?” Roo asked.

Auntie Em’s eyes sparkled. “I would love that.”

The friends lined up at the end of the bed. Roo took a breath. The clock ticked. Buzz hummed. Roo began. Step… pause… smile. Sway… sway… hug. Bow.

Auntie Em clapped gently. “Oh my,” she said. “That was beautiful. I feel calm. I feel loved.”

Roo’s chest felt big with joy. “We wanted to help,” Roo said. “We can bring tea too!” Roo poured warm lemon tea into a cup. The steam curled up like a tiny ribbon. The tea smelled bright and sweet. Tilly added a coaster. Pip fluffed the pillows. Buzz hummed a little thank-you tune.

But then, the clock ticked louder. Auntie Em yawned. “I think I need a nap,” she whispered.

Roo blinked. “Oh! We should be extra quiet now,” Roo said. “This is a peaceful place.”

Roo gave soft directions. “Let’s tiptoe out. We can put a sign on the door.” Pip wrote with big round letters: “Shh. Rest Time.” Tilly placed the sign just right. Buzz closed the curtain a little, so the light was cozy, not too bright.

Auntie Em tucked in and smiled at Roo. “Thank you for your care,” she said. “Your dance was gentle. Your help was perfect.”

Roo’s heart felt like it was doing happy little hops. “We did it together,” Roo whispered to the friends. “That is community service. We look, we listen, and we help.”

After the nap, Auntie Em came out, feathers fluffed and eyes bright. “I feel better,” she said. “Your kindness helped me rest. Your friendship gave me strength.”

Roo bounced once, very softly. “Helping together makes our hearts dance,” Roo said.

Mama Kangaroo hugged Roo. “You learned a big lesson today,” she said. “Community service is love in action. Friends listen. Friends care. And even a dance can be quiet and kind.”

Roo nodded. “We can help again,” Roo said. “We can make soup for Mr. Wombat next door. We can pick up sticks in the park. We can share our smiles.”

Everyone cheered in soft voices. The guest room glowed with peace. Roo felt brave and proud, with a bouncy, caring heart.

The lesson was clear: When we work together, we can help others feel safe and loved. That is community service. That is friendship. And that is the best dance of all.

The End.
