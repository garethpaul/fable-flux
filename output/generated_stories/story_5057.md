# Poppy Panda’s Brave Little Heart

In a comfortable nursery with soft rugs and sunny windows, Poppy the patient panda sat at a low table. The room smelled like clean soap and orange slices. Gentle music hummed like a warm hug. Crayons, safety scissors, and glue sticks waited on the table, like colorful friends.

“Today we make brave badges!” said Ms. Nina, the kind teacher. She smiled and showed a round, shiny paper badge with a star in the middle. “A brave badge helps us remember to try new things and keep calm.”

Poppy touched her badge paper. It felt smooth under her paws. “I want to be brave,” Poppy whispered. Her tummy felt fluttery, like tiny butterflies. Outside, the wind tickled the window, tap tap tap.

Poppy picked a purple crayon. “I will draw a big star,” she said. Her paw moved carefully. But the crayon slid a little. The star looked wiggly.

“Oh no,” Poppy sighed. “It’s not perfect.”

Ms. Nina knelt beside her. “Wiggly can be wonderful,” she said softly. “Let’s take a brave breath.”

“What is a brave breath?” Poppy asked.

“Watch,” said Ms. Nina. She breathed in slowly through her nose, fill, fill, fill, and breathed out slowly through her mouth, whoosh. “Brave breaths help our bodies feel calm.”

Poppy closed her eyes. She felt the chair under her, steady and strong. She took a brave breath in… and a slow whoosh out. The butterflies in her tummy fluttered more softly.

“You did it,” said Ms. Nina.

Poppy smiled a tiny smile. She tried again. She drew another line for her star. It fit better this time.

At the next table, friends were busy. Tiko the tiger cub snipped paper shapes. “Snip, snip!” The scissors made a happy sound. Luna the lamb stuck a circle on her badge. “Stick!” The glue felt cool and squishy.

Poppy watched. “I can try snipping,” she said. She picked up the safety scissors. They felt new and a little tricky in her paws. Snip. The paper wiggled. Snip. The shape was not a perfect star. It looked like a cloud.

Poppy’s ears drooped. “I can’t do it.”

Ms. Nina touched Poppy’s shoulder gently. “Sometimes we feel stuck,” she said. “That feeling is called frustration. Let’s try three brave steps. Step one: brave breath. Step two: ask for help. Step three: try again.”

Poppy nodded. She took a brave breath. In… and whoosh out. “Ms. Nina, can you help me hold the paper?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Ms. Nina. She held the paper steady. “You’re very brave to ask.”

Snip. Snip. Poppy made a pointy star this time. It was not perfect, but it was her star. “I did it!” she said. Her chest felt warm, like sunshine.

“Your star is strong,” said Tiko. “Like you!”

Poppy glued her star onto the round badge. The glue was cool and sticky. She pressed, press, press. Then she added a little heart. “This is my brave heart,” she said.

“Let’s add healthy sparkle,” said Ms. Nina. She set out tiny stickers shaped like apples and toothbrushes.

“Healthy sparkle?” Poppy giggled.

“When we take care of our bodies, we help our brave hearts,” Ms. Nina said. “We eat colorful foods, drink water, rest well, and wash our paws.”

Poppy chose an apple sticker. “Crunch, crunch,” she pretended to eat. “I like apples.”

She picked a water drop sticker. “Gulp, gulp,” she giggled, pretending to drink. She added a toothbrush sticker. “Brush, brush,” she sang. The room filled with happy sounds—snip, stick, giggle, hum.

Later, it was snack time. Ms. Nina brought orange slices and water. The orange felt cool and juicy in Poppy’s paw. “I’m trying a new fruit,” Poppy said. She took a small bite. Sweet and bright! “Mmm.” She took a brave breath, then another bite. “I like it!”

After snack, it was quiet rest time. The lights were soft. Poppy snuggled into a cozy pillow. “Rest helps my brave heart,” she whispered. She took a slow brave breath and felt her body relax.

When rest time ended, Ms. Nina had one more challenge. “Who would like to stand up and show their brave badge?”

Poppy’s tummy fluttered again. The room seemed big. Her paws felt warm. She looked at her badge. Star. Heart. Apples. Water drop. Toothbrush. She remembered the three brave steps.

“Brave breath,” Poppy whispered. In… and whoosh.

“Ask for help,” she whispered. “Tiko, will you stand near me?”

“I will,” said Tiko, standing with a grin.

“Try again,” Poppy said to herself. She stood up. Her legs felt wobbly, but she stood tall.

“I made a brave badge,” Poppy said in a small voice. She took another brave breath. “My star is wiggly, but it’s mine. I took brave breaths, asked for help, and tried again. I eat fruits, drink water, brush my teeth, and rest. These help my brave heart.”

The room was quiet, warm, and kind. Then Luna clapped. Tiko clapped. Ms. Nina clapped. Poppy smiled big. Her brave heart felt strong and soft at the same time.

Ms. Nina placed the badge gently on Poppy’s shirt. “You were brave many times today,” she said. “Bravery is trying, even when it feels hard.”

Poppy patted her badge. It felt light, like a little star hug. “I can be brave tomorrow, too,” she said. “I can breathe, ask, and try again.”

And the butterflies in her tummy? They felt like friends now, fluttering happy little cheers.

The End.
