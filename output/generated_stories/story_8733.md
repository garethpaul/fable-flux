# Koko’s Kitchen Plan

In a warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon and toast, a calm koala named Koko sat on a soft stool. The morning sun made golden squares on the floor. A kettle hummed. The fridge hummed too. Koko rubbed sleepy eyes and took a deep breath. The air felt cozy and safe.

Koko had a big goal. “Today,” Koko whispered, “I will bake banana muffins by myself.” Koko’s round ears wiggled with excitement. “I can be brave and learn new skills.”

Koko washed paws in warm, soapy water. Splish, splash. Then Koko set out the things needed: a mixing bowl, a wooden spoon, ripe bananas with brown spots, flour like soft snow, sugar that sparkled, and a tiny bottle of vanilla that smelled sweet.

Just then, Koko’s little cousin, Kip, came in. Kip was a bouncy koala. His feet pitter-pattered on the tile. “Koko! I want to help! Can I stir? Can I pour?” Kip reached for the spoon with quick paws.

Koko felt a bump in the tummy. This was a problem. Koko had a plan and a goal. But Kip wanted to do everything right now. The spoon tugged between their paws.

“I want to stir,” said Kip, tug tug.

“I had a plan,” said Koko softly, holding the spoon. Koko felt cheeks grow warm. The kettle clicked. The clock ticked. The room felt tight.

Koko took a slow breath. In… and out. Calm, calm, calm. “We have a conflict,” Koko said. “We both want the same spoon.”

Kip wrinkled his nose. “I want to stir first!”

Koko nodded. “I hear you,” Koko said. “You want to stir first. I want to follow my plan. Let’s solve this together.”

“How?” Kip asked, ears drooping a little.

Koko tapped the wooden spoon on the bowl. Tap, tap. “We can make a plan with turns. That is a fair way. First, we mash the bananas. Then we stir the dry things. Then we mix it all together. We can set a timer for turns.” Koko held up a tiny timer shaped like a lemon. It made a happy click.

Kip’s eyes lit up. “I like turns! I can mash,” he said, bouncing again.

“Great idea,” Koko said. “You mash. I will hold the bowl still. Then I will measure the flour. Then you can pour the milk.”

They started. Kip peeled bananas. Peel, peel. The bananas made a sweet, mushy smell. Squish, squish—Kip mashed with a fork. Koko steadied the bowl and smiled. “Nice mashing! So smooth.”

The timer clicked for the next turn. Koko scooped flour with careful paws. Whoosh—flour puffed like a tiny cloud. Koko coughed and giggled. “Snowy noses!” Kip laughed too and wiped a white spot from Koko’s nose.

Next, Kip poured the milk with both paws. Glug, glug. A little milk splashed on the counter. Kip’s eyes went wide. “Oh no!”

“It’s okay,” said Koko, calm as a warm blanket. “Spills happen when we learn. Let’s wipe it up together.” They pressed a soft cloth on the splash. Pat, pat. Clean again.

They added sugar that glittered and a drop of vanilla. Drip—mmm, sweet smell! Koko cracked an egg. Crack! Kip watched closely. “I want to try next time,” he said.

“You’re brave to try new things,” Koko said. “We can practice with a pretend egg later.”

They took turns stirring. Swirl, swirl, swirl. The batter grew thick and yellow. The timer clicked again. “My turn is done,” Koko said. “Your turn to spoon the batter into the cups.”

Kip scooped carefully. Plop. Plop. Plop. Some cups were big and some were small. “They look funny,” Kip said.

“They look perfect for us,” Koko said with a grin. “All shapes can be tasty.”

They slid the muffin tray into the warm oven. A soft whoosh of heat came out, like a cozy hug. Koko set another timer. “Now we wait,” Koko said. “Waiting is a skill too.”

They cleaned the counter. Wipe, wipe. They washed the dishes. Swish, swish. The kitchen smelled sweeter and sweeter. The timer sang, ding-a-ling!

Koko used big oven mitts with strawberries on them. “Hot,” Koko said. “I will be careful.” Koko bravely pulled out the tray. The muffins were golden and puffy. Steam curled up like little clouds.

Kip clapped. “We did it!”

Koko placed two muffins on a plate to cool. They blew softly. Hoo, hoo. They took tiny bites. The muffins were warm and soft. Banana and vanilla danced on their tongues.

Kip leaned on Koko’s shoulder. “I liked our plan. I felt heard.”

Koko nodded. “I felt heard too. We solved our conflict with calm words, turns, and a timer. We built skills together. That was brave.”

Kip smiled. “Next time, I can try cracking the egg.”

Koko patted Kip’s paw. “Yes. We will set a goal and take turns again.”

They sat in the warm kitchen, tummies happy, hearts happy, and minds proud. The sun made golden squares on the floor again. The kettle hummed softly.

Koko said, “When we have a problem, we can talk, listen, and make a plan. That is how we solve conflicts.”

Kip nodded. “And we can be brave and keep trying.”

They bumped noses and giggled. Outside, a bird chirped a tiny song. Inside, everything felt bright and kind.

The End.
