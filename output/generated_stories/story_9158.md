# Ellie's Sweet Solution

Ellie the elephant calf stood in the big pantry. Her gray trunk swayed gently as she looked around. The pantry smelled like cookies and apples. Wooden shelves reached up high, filled with colorful jars and boxes.

But Ellie had a problem. She really, really wanted a cookie from the tall cookie jar. The jar sat way up on the highest shelf. It was much too high for a little elephant to reach.

"Oh no," Ellie said sadly. "I can smell those yummy chocolate chip cookies, but I can't get them."

Ellie tried standing on her tippy-toes. She stretched her trunk as high as she could. But the cookie jar was still too far away.

Then Ellie had an idea. She could push a chair over to the shelf. She could climb up and grab the cookies all by herself!

But as Ellie started to push the wooden chair, she stopped. Her mama had taught her to always ask before taking food from the pantry.

"That wouldn't be honest," Ellie said to herself. "I should ask first."

Just then, Oliver the owl flew into the pantry through the open window. His soft brown feathers ruffled in the breeze.

"Hello, Ellie," Oliver hooted kindly. "You look worried. What's wrong?"

Ellie's ears drooped down. "I really want a cookie, but the jar is too high. I thought about climbing up, but that wouldn't be right without asking."

Oliver's big round eyes sparkled. "That's very honest of you, Ellie. Being truthful is always the best choice."

"But now I'm stuck," Ellie sighed. "And I'm getting so hungry."

Oliver tilted his feathered head. "Maybe we can work together! I can fly up high, and you can catch what I drop down."

Ellie's trunk wiggled with excitement. "That's a wonderful idea! But we should still ask Mama first."

The two friends walked to the kitchen together. Ellie found her mama making lunch. The kitchen smelled like fresh bread and soup.

"Mama," Ellie said politely, "may Oliver and I please have some cookies? We found a way to work together to reach them."

Mama Elephant smiled warmly. "Of course, my sweet Ellie. I'm so proud that you asked first instead of taking them. That shows real kindness and honesty."

Back in the pantry, Oliver flew up to the cookie jar. His wings flapped softly as he carefully picked up two cookies in his beak. He dropped them gently down to Ellie.

"Catch!" Oliver called.

Ellie caught both cookies perfectly with her trunk. The cookies felt warm and smelled like vanilla.

"Thank you so much, Oliver!" Ellie said happily. "You're such a kind friend."

"And thank you for being honest," Oliver replied. "That made me want to help you even more."

The two friends sat together in the sunny pantry. They munched their cookies and talked about their favorite games. The cookies tasted extra sweet because they had been shared with kindness.

Ellie learned something important that day. When you're honest and ask for help nicely, friends are happy to work together with you. Being kind and truthful always leads to the best solutions.

From that day on, whenever Ellie had a problem, she remembered to be honest and ask for help. And Oliver was always ready to lend a wing to his thoughtful elephant friend.

The End.
