# Tilly Turtle Finds Her Team

Tilly was a shy turtle with a smooth, green shell. One sunny morning, she climbed the creaky stairs to a quiet attic. The attic was soft with dust. Sunbeams made golden lines in the air. Tilly sniffed. It smelled like old books and warm wood.

“Hello?” Tilly whispered. Her voice sounded small in the big, quiet room.

Under a stack of boxes, Tilly heard a tiny sneeze. “Achoo!”

“Who is there?” Tilly asked, peeking around a wobbly lamp.

A little brown mouse popped out. He had bright eyes and a pink nose. “I’m Milo,” he said, twitching his whiskers. “It’s dusty up here!”

Tilly smiled, but she looked down shyly. “I’m Tilly. I am… a little shy.”

“Me too,” said Milo softly. “But maybe we can be shy together.”

They both giggled. The attic felt friendlier already.

Suddenly, Tilly saw a kite stuck high on a wooden beam. It was red and blue with a yellow tail. The tail hung like a ribbon. “Oh,” Tilly said, pointing. “Someone’s kite is stuck.”

A light flapped near a window. A moth with pale, powdery wings floated gently. “I’m Marnie,” the moth hummed. “That kite blocks the window. I can’t see the moon at night. Could you help?”

Tilly’s heart thumped. “I want to help,” she said. “But the beam is very high.” She looked at her short legs and felt shy again.

Milo touched Tilly’s shell with a tiny paw. “We can try together,” he said kindly. “We can cooperate.”

“Cooperate?” Tilly tasted the new word. “What does that mean?”

“It means we help each other,” Marnie said softly. “We use our different strengths. We are a team.”

Tilly took a brave breath. “Okay,” she said. “We will be a team.”

They made a plan. Milo could climb. Marnie could fly and see from above. Tilly was strong and steady.

First, Tilly moved a small stool under the beam. The stool scraped on the wooden floor with a shhh sound. “Careful,” she said. “We go slow.”

Marnie floated up to the beam. “The kite string is looped,” she called down. “I can show you the best spot!”

Milo hopped onto Tilly’s shell. “Is this okay?” he asked.

“Yes,” Tilly said. “I am steady.” She felt proud to be helpful.

“Ready,” Milo said.

“One, two, three,” Tilly counted in a calm voice. She stepped onto the stool. Her feet went tap, tap. She kept her balance. Milo reached up and wiggled the string. It was tight.

“It’s stuck,” Milo said. “I need a little more height.”

Tilly looked around. She saw a soft old pillow. “We can add the pillow,” she said. “Then I will be a little taller.”

“That’s smart,” Marnie said. “Good teamwork!”

They slid the pillow on the stool. Tilly climbed again. The pillow felt squishy. “Easy does it,” she said. “Slow and steady.”

Milo reached higher. Marnie hummed near the knot and pointed with her wing. “Right there,” she said. “That’s the tricky part.”

Milo tugged the string. It did not move. Tilly felt her legs shake. “I’m scared I will fall,” she said in a small voice.

“You are brave, Tilly,” Marnie said kindly. “Being brave means trying, even when you feel scared.”

“You are not alone,” Milo added. “I’ve got you.”

Tilly breathed in. She felt the cool, dusty air fill her chest. She breathed out. “I can do this,” she said.

“Let’s cooperate again,” Milo said. “On three, you lift a tiny bit, and I pull gently.”

“Okay,” Tilly said. “One… two… three!”

Tilly lifted her neck slowly. Milo pulled just a little. Marnie guided them with soft words. “A little left. Now a little right.”

Pop! The string slipped free. The kite drifted down, light as a feather, and landed on the floor with a soft whoosh.

“We did it!” Milo squeaked.

“We cooperated,” Marnie cheered, fluttering in a happy circle.

Tilly smiled wide. Her shy heart felt warm and big. “We did it together,” she said.

They folded the kite neatly. Tilly brushed dust off with gentle strokes. “Let’s put it by the door,” she said. “Someone will find it.”

As they worked, they talked kindly.

“Thank you for your courage, Tilly,” Marnie said.

“Thank you for your smart idea,” Milo added.

“Thank you for your help,” Tilly said. “I felt brave because we were a team.”

Marnie flew to the window. The moonlight peeked in now. The attic looked bright and friendly. It smelled less dusty and more like fresh night air.

Tilly listened. The creaks sounded like music now. The golden beams of light felt like warm hugs.

“I learned something,” Tilly said. “Cooperation means we help each other. We share ideas. We take turns. We are kind. That makes us strong.”

Milo nodded. “When we cooperate, big jobs feel small.”

Marnie smiled. “And shy friends can be brave together.”

Tilly tucked into her shell for a cozy hug, then peeked out with a grin. “I may be shy,” she said, “but with friends, I can do brave things.”

They all looked at the kite by the door and the clear window by the moon. The attic was calm and happy.

“Teamwork makes courage grow,” Tilly said softly.

“Yes,” said Milo. “And kindness makes teams.”

They sat together in the quiet attic, proud and peaceful, knowing they had done a good, kind thing by cooperating.

The End.
