# Roary’s Closet Camp

Roary was a brave little lion cub with a soft golden mane and bright, curious eyes. He loved to roar, but his roar sounded more like a tiny “rawr!” Today, Roary had a big job to do.

In the hallway, there was a closet hideaway. It was a cozy space with a soft rug, a tiny lamp shaped like a moon, and shelves full of boxes. The closet smelled like clean cotton and a little bit like cinnamon cookies. This was Roary’s favorite place to play and learn.

Mama Lion touched Roary’s nose gently. “Roary,” she said, “today we tidy the closet hideaway and pack a box for our friends far away.”

Roary’s ears perked up. “A box for friends?” he asked.

“Yes,” Mama said. “Kids in other places need warm socks, crayons, and stories. We can share. First task: tidy. Second task: sort. Third task: pack and label.”

Roary took a deep breath. “I can do it,” he said. His belly fluttered like little butterflies, but he stood tall. “I am brave.”

Inside the closet hideaway, Roary saw toy drums, puzzle pieces, crayons, and tiny socks. The lamp glowed soft and warm. The rug felt squishy under his paws. Roary whispered, “Hello, closet. Let’s get to work.”

He made a plan like Mama said. “First tidy,” he murmured. He put all the puzzle pieces into a green box. Click, click, click. He lined up books on the shelf. Slide, slide. He folded tiny socks. Fold, pat. Roary hummed a little tune. “Tidy, tidy, one by one, I can do it, job well done.”

His friend, a stuffed zebra named Zuzu, peeked from the top shelf. “Neigh?” Zuzu didn’t really talk, but Roary liked to pretend.

“I’m on task, Zuzu,” Roary told him. “I finish what I start.”

Just then, Roary saw something shiny under the rug. He lifted a corner and gasped. “A marble!” It rolled and glimmered like a blue planet. Roary wanted to play. He reached out, then pulled his paw back. “Focus, Roary,” he whispered. “First tidy, then play.” He put the marble in a small “to sort” cup.

Mama Lion peeked in. “How’s it going?”

Roary puffed his chest. “I’m completing my tasks, Mama. Tidy is almost done.”

Mama smiled. “I see your hard work.”

“Now sorting,” Roary said. He made three piles: keep, share, and recycle. He touched the crayons. “We have many. We can share some.”

He rubbed a crayon on paper. Scritch scritch. “These colors can make happy pictures for friends far away,” he said. He placed new crayons into the share box. He added warm socks that still smelled like sunshine. He added a small book with animal pictures. “This book shows animals from many places,” Roary said. “It shows we all belong.”

Roary paused at a shiny toy drum. “I love this drum,” he whispered, tapping it. Tum-tum. “But I have another drum.” He closed his eyes and felt his brave heart. “Someone else might love this, too.” He placed the drum in the share box.

Zuzu the zebra tipped to the side, as if nodding. Roary giggled.

Mama came back with labels and string. “Ready for the third task?” she asked.

“Yes,” Roary said. “Pack and label!”

Together, they folded soft tissue paper. Swish, swish. They laid the crayons and socks gently inside. They tucked in the book and the drum. Roary added a small note he had drawn with a big heart and a tiny lion paw print. He said softly, “Hello, friend. I am Roary. I am sharing with you.”

Mama tied the string. “Listen,” she said.

Roary listened. He could hear the soft hum of the house, the tiny click of the lamp, and his own steady breath. He felt calm and proud.

Mama pointed to the box. “This will travel to another place in our big world,” she explained. “People everywhere are part of one big family. We help each other. That is global citizenship.”

Roary traced the letters on the label with his paw. “Glo-bal ci-ti-zen-ship,” he sounded out slowly. “We live in one big world.”

He looked around the closet hideaway. It was neat and cozy now. The books stood straight like little soldiers. The rug was smooth. The marble rested in the cup, waiting for playtime.

Mama smiled. “You finished each task. How do you feel?”

Roary took a deep breath. “I feel brave. I feel kind. I finished what I started.”

He picked up Zuzu and gave him a cuddle. “Zuzu, our closet is tidy, and our friends will smile.”

Mama stroked Roary’s mane. “You showed generosity by sharing. You showed respect for our diverse world by thinking of others. And you learned that completing tasks, step by step, makes big jobs easy.”

Roary nodded. “First tidy. Then sort. Then pack and label,” he repeated. “I can do hard things when I do them one by one.”

They turned off the little moon lamp. Click. The closet hideaway felt peaceful and clean. Roary’s paws felt light. His heart felt big.

“Playtime now?” Mama asked.

Roary grinned. “Yes! After my tasks, I play. I kept my promise.” He rolled the blue marble on the smooth rug. It sparkled like a tiny Earth. Roary whispered, “Hello, world.”

He and Mama listened to the marble roll, soft and sweet. Roary’s brave little roar filled the cozy space. “Rawr! I can help. I can finish. I can share.”

The End.
