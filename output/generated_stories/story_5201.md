# Hootie’s Calm Clock

In a cozy study corner, soft and quiet, lived a wise old owl named Hootie. The study corner smelled like fresh paper and warm tea. The lamp glowed like a little moon. Books lined the walls like friendly trees. Hootie’s feathers were soft gray, and his eyes were kind and round.

Every afternoon, little animals came to the study corner to read and draw. Hootie helped them. He loved to say, “Let’s learn together. Hoot, hoot!”

One sunny day, the room felt busy. Pages rustled. Crayons rolled. The clock said tick, tick, tick. Hootie had promised to help three friends: a squirrel named Pip, a bunny named Bibi, and a tiny mouse named Dot.

Pip hopped in first, his tail twitching. “Hootie, I need help counting acorns! Right now!”

Bibi bounced down the hall, ears flopping. “Hootie, I need a story! The one with the rainbow!”

Dot squeaked from the doorway, holding a small book. “Hootie, I have to read today. Can you listen?”

All at once, their voices filled the room. Pip, Bibi, and Dot talked over each other. The lamp seemed brighter. The clock ticked louder. Hootie felt his chest get tight. His face got warm. His wings felt stiff.

“I can’t do it all at once,” Hootie said. His voice shook. He felt mad bubbles rise inside, like fizzy soda. His feathers puffed.

Pip frowned. “But I was first!”

Bibi stamped a little foot. “But I want my rainbow!”

Dot’s whiskers drooped. “But I practiced…”

Hootie’s eyes closed. He remembered his calm plan. He had made it for days just like this.

First step: Stop.
Second step: Breathe.
Third step: Choose kind words.

Hootie put one wing on his chest. He stopped. He took a long, slow breath in. The air smelled like tea and paper. He let the breath out. He took another. In… and out. His chest felt softer. The mad bubbles popped away.

He opened his eyes. “Friends,” he said gently, “I feel upset. My body is tight. I need a calm moment.” He took one more breath. “Okay. Thank you for waiting. Let’s make a fair plan.”

Pip’s tail slowed. Bibi’s foot stopped stamping. Dot stood still, listening.

Hootie tapped a small wooden timer on the desk. It was smooth and round and made a soft click. “This is our Calm Clock,” he said. “We can share time. We can take turns. That is fair.”

He drew three small circles on a paper. He wrote: Pip—5 minutes. Bibi—5 minutes. Dot—5 minutes. He smiled. “We will help one by one. While you wait, you can volunteer to help a friend.”

“Volunteer?” asked Bibi, ears high.

“It means you choose to help,” said Hootie. “Helping makes the room kind.”

Pip gulped a breath. “I can wait five minutes,” he said. “I will volunteer to sharpen crayons for Dot.”

Dot’s whiskers lifted. “Thank you, Pip!”

Bibi nodded. “I will sort books by color. Red, blue, green!” She giggled. “Rainbow!”

Hootie’s heart felt warm. “That is good leadership,” he said. “You are showing kind choices.”

The timer clicked. The first five minutes began. Tick, tick, tick.

Hootie sat with Pip. “Let’s count slow and steady,” he said. “One acorn, two acorns…” Pip’s tail moved in a calm sway. When Pip’s five minutes were done, Hootie smiled. “You did it. Thank you for waiting and helping.”

Next, Hootie turned to Bibi. “Time for a rainbow story,” he said. He pointed to the colors outside the window. “Red flower, orange leaf, yellow sun.” Bibi’s eyes shone. When the timer clicked again, she clapped softly. “That was perfect!”

Finally, Hootie looked at Dot. “Your turn, little one.” Dot read her book in a tiny, brave voice. “The cat sat.” Hootie nodded. “Yes. You are strong and steady.”

Everyone had a turn. Everyone felt seen. The study corner felt quiet and warm again. The clock ticked in a friendly way. The tea smelled sweet. The lamp glowed soft.

Pip said, “Hootie, how did you stop being mad?”

Hootie touched his chest. “I felt the mad feeling. I stopped. I breathed in, and out. Then I used kind words. I made a fair plan with time. That helped my anger. My body felt better.”

Bibi asked, “Was sharing time the fair thing?”

“Yes,” said Hootie. “Fair means each friend gets what they need. Today, we took turns. We used the Calm Clock. We volunteered to help. That made our time work.”

Dot squeaked, “I liked helping, too. It made waiting nice.”

Hootie nodded. “Helping while you wait is a good choice. That is leadership. You showed others how to be kind.”

The friends smiled. They cleaned up together. Pip stacked acorns in a neat line. Bibi tucked books by color. Dot put crayons in a small tin that made a happy clink-clink.

Before they left, Hootie hung a bright sign on the wall. It said: Stop. Breathe. Kind Words. Fair Turns. Help While You Wait.

He read it with them. “When we feel mad, we can choose calm. We can share time. We can help. We can be fair.”

Pip waved his tail. “I will try the calm steps at home.”

Bibi twirled. “I will use the Calm Clock with my toys.”

Dot whispered, “I will take turns and help.”

Hootie smiled wide. “Hoot, hoot! That is the lesson. Anger can visit, but we do not have to stay angry. We can calm down, make a plan, and be kind. That is wise.”

The study corner glowed. The friends felt proud. The day felt light and sweet.

The End.
