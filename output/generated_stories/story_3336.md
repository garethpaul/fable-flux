# Gigi Plans a Big Art Day

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with long, gentle lashes and warm brown eyes. Today, she stood in the creative art room. The air smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight made bright squares on the colorful floor.

Gigi whispered, “Today, I will make my own art. I will plan it. I can do it by myself.”

She felt a tiny flutter in her tummy. It was not a scared flutter. It was an excited flutter.

“First, I make a plan,” Gigi said softly. She took a deep breath and looked around. The room had paint bottles, soft brushes, glue sticks, scissors with round tips, and a rainbow of paper. There were also smocks hanging like little capes.

She found a small chalkboard on the wall. Gigi picked up a fat white chalk. Scratch, scratch. She wrote her steps:

1. Put on smock.
2. Pick colors.
3. Draw a idea.
4. Paint and glue.
5. Clean up.

Gigi smiled. “I can do this.”

She pulled a big blue smock over her long neck. Swish! It felt smooth and a little cool. She tied the strings herself. It took a moment. Tug, tug. The bow was a little crooked, but it held. “I did it on my own,” she said, proud.

Next, she picked colors. She touched the paint bottles and said their names in a soft song. “Sunny yellow, grassy green, sky blue, berry red.” The caps clicked open. She squeezed small dots onto a palette. Plip. Plop. Plip.

“Now I draw my idea,” Gigi said. She closed her eyes and imagined. She saw a tall tree, a warm sun, and friends making art outside. She opened her eyes and smiled. “Yes. A picture of a big art picnic.”

Gigi took a fat crayon. It felt warm in her hoof. Scribble, swirl, swoop. She drew the round sun. She drew the tall tree. She drew a blanket with little squares. She drew a small cup of brushes. Her crayon made a friendly scratchy sound.

Tap, tap. Gigi’s friend, Tali the tiny turtle, peeked in. “Hi, Gigi,” Tali said in a small voice. “What are you doing?”

“I am making art,” Gigi said. “I made a plan. I am trying to do it myself.”

Tali blinked. “Can I watch?”

“Yes,” said Gigi. “But I will try to work on my own first. I am practicing being independent.”

Tali nodded. “I can sit and cheer.”

Gigi dipped a brush in blue paint. The bristles felt soft and tickly. She made the sky. Swish, swish. She cleaned her brush in a jar of water. Swirl, swish. The water turned cloudy. She patted the brush on a towel. Tap, tap.

She reached for green. Splish. She painted the grass. She added red and yellow dots for flowers. Dot, dot, dot. They looked like tiny happy buttons.

Then Gigi had a tricky part. She wanted to glue a leaf she found on the windowsill onto the tree. The glue bottle was new. The cap was tight. Gigi held it with both hooves. Twist. Twist. It did not open.

Gigi felt the flutter in her tummy again. This time it felt a little worried. She took a slow breath. “I can try a plan,” she told herself.

She looked at her chalkboard list. She added a tiny note under number four: “Ask for help if needed, then try again.”

Gigi smiled. “Being independent means I can try, make choices, and ask for help when it is safe and smart.”

She tried a rubber grip from the drawer. Slip it on. Twist. Pop! The cap came loose.

“You did it!” Tali clapped her little hands.

Gigi squeezed a small line of glue. Squish. She pressed the leaf down. It felt cool and a little bumpy. “Thank you for cheering, Tali,” she said. “I planned, I tried, and I fixed my plan.”

Next, Gigi imagined birds in the sky. “I will make them from paper.” She chose soft blue paper and cut two simple bird shapes. Snip, snip. The scissors made a snippy song. She glued the birds and added tiny eyes with a marker. Dot, dot.

“Your art looks joyful,” Tali said. “And the sun seems warm.”

“It feels warm to me,” Gigi said, touching the golden circle. “Now I will add the friends at the art picnic.” She painted a little turtle, a bunny, and a giraffe with a blue smock. She gave them happy smiles.

At last, Gigi stepped back. Her heart felt full. The picture showed a bright day, a tall tree, and friends making art together. It looked like her idea. It looked like her plan.

Tali sniffed. “I smell oranges,” she said.

Gigi laughed. “That is the orange paint. It smells a little like fruit.” She wiggled her nose. “The room smells like crayons and fun.”

She checked her list again. “Last step: clean up.” Gigi washed her brushes. Swirl, swish. She wiped the table with a soft cloth. Wipe, wipe. She closed the paint caps. Click. She hung up her smock. Swish.

Tali smiled. “You did it, Gigi. You made a plan. You did your steps. You asked for help in a smart way.”

Gigi nodded. “I feel proud. I can be independent. I can plan and use my imagination. I can finish and clean up, too.”

They stood together and looked at the art. The sun made it glow. Gigi whispered, “My plan helped my dream become real.”

Tali said, “Can we have a real art picnic next time?”

“Yes,” said Gigi. “Let’s plan it together. We can each do our part.”

Gigi felt calm and happy. She learned that being independent means making a plan, trying first, fixing problems, and asking for help when needed. With imagination and friends, she could do big things, one small step at a time.

The End.
