# Rocco’s Ready Rhythm

Rocco was a musical raccoon with bright curious eyes and soft striped paws. He lived in a playful living room filled with cozy pillows, a bouncy rug, and a basket of instruments. There were shakers that went shaka-shaka, a little drum that went boom-boom, and a tiny bell that went ting-ting!

Today was Music Share Day. Rocco’s grandma, Nana Raccoon, had a special song to teach. It was a family tradition, a song that made hearts feel warm and paws tap fast. Rocco wanted to play along and share his own little tune too.

“I want to play my drum for everyone,” Rocco said, patting the drum with a gentle boom. “I want to feel brave.”

Nana Raccoon smiled and wiggled her whiskers. “You will feel brave when you are ready, little rhythm star. Let’s prepare.”

“Prepare?” Rocco tilted his head.

“Prepare means getting ready step by step,” said Nana. “We make a plan. We practice. We pack what we need. Preparation helps our hearts feel calm and strong.”

Rocco’s tail swished. “I like calm and strong.”

They looked around the playful living room. Sunlight danced on the walls, making bright squares. The air smelled like warm cookies from the kitchen. The soft rug felt squishy under Rocco’s paws.

Nana set out a little list on a big, friendly notepad.

“Step one,” she said. “Pick your instrument.”

Rocco tried the shakers. Shaka-shaka! He giggled. He tapped the drum. Boom-boom! He rang the bell. Ting-ting!

“I choose the drum,” Rocco said, hugging it. “It feels like my heartbeat.”

“Good choice,” said Nana. “Step two: check your drum.”

Rocco looked closely. The drum skin was smooth. The strap was a bit loose.

“Oh!” said Rocco. “The strap is floppy.”

“That’s why we prepare,” said Nana. “We fix little things before they become big things.”

They tightened the strap together. Rocco tested it. “Snug,” he said proudly.

“Step three,” Nana said, “practice.”

Rocco sat with straight shoulders. He took a big, slow breath. In and out. He tapped a simple pattern: boom, boom, pause. Boom, boom, pause.

“Listen with your ears and your heart,” Nana whispered.

Rocco listened. The drum sounded warm and round. He tried again, a little faster. Boom-boom, boom-boom! He smiled wider. His tail made a happy swish-swish on the rug.

“Step four: pack and plan,” said Nana. “Where will you sit? What will you say?”

Rocco looked at the bouncy rug. “I will sit in the middle. I will say, ‘Hello! I am Rocco. I am ready to share my song.’”

Nana clapped softly. “Perfect. Preparation gives you a plan, and a plan gives you peace.”

Ding-dong! Friends arrived. Miri the mouse brought a pink ribbon shaker. Tavi the turtle rolled in with a tiny triangle. Bibi the bunny had fuzzy ears and a sweet voice.

“Hi, Rocco!” they said.

Rocco waved. “Hi! I prepared. I picked my drum, I fixed my strap, I practiced, and I planned.”

Miri sniffed the air. “Mmm, cookies!” The room smelled extra yummy now.

Bibi bounced on the rug. “I feel wiggly.”

Rocco nodded. “Me too. But my plan helps my wiggles.”

Nana raised her paws. “Welcome to Music Share Day! First, our family tradition song. Then, we will share our own sounds.”

She sang the old song, soft and warm: “Ting-a-ling, tap-tap, hearts together, clap-clap.” The bell went ting-ting. The drum went tap-tap. Everyone clapped and smiled. Rocco felt the song travel from Nana’s voice to his paws. It felt like a hug made of music.

“Your turn, Rocco,” said Nana.

Rocco took a breath. “Hello! I am Rocco. I am ready to share my song.”

He tapped his drum. Boom, boom, pause. Boom, boom, pause. He watched his paws. He listened with his ears and his heart. He kept the beat steady, just like he practiced. Miri shook her ribbon shaker gently. Shaka-shaka. Tavi pinged the triangle. Ting! Bibi hummed along.

The room filled with sound. It felt like sparkles in the air. It sounded round and bright. Rocco’s cheeks felt warm, but in a happy way. His chest felt big and proud.

When he finished, Rocco looked at Nana.

“You did it,” Nana said. “You prepared, and your music was strong.”

Rocco grinned. “Preparation helped me be brave.”

Miri nodded. “Can you show me how to prepare too?”

“Of course,” said Rocco. “Step one: pick. Step two: check. Step three: practice. Step four: plan.”

He helped Miri tighten the ribbon on her shaker. He counted slow for Bibi while she practiced humming. He helped Tavi find a comfy spot to sit.

Soon, it was a little circle of sharing. Everyone took turns. Everyone felt heard. Everyone felt proud.

After the music, they shared cookies. Crunch, munch! The chocolate chips were melty. The milk was cool and sweet.

Rocco licked a crumb from his paw. “Sharing music feels good. Sharing steps feels good too.”

Nana nodded. “When we prepare, we show care for ourselves and for others. Preparation builds self-esteem. It helps us feel ready to share.”

Rocco hugged his drum. He felt calm. He felt strong. He felt kind.

He looked at his friends. “Next time, let’s prepare together again. We can make a new tradition.”

“Yes!” said Miri.

“Yes!” said Tavi.

“Yes!” said Bibi.

Rocco tapped a tiny good-bye rhythm. Boom, tap, boom. The living room glowed with smiles and soft sunlight. The day felt cozy, full, and bright.

Lesson: Preparation helps us feel brave, do our best, and share with joy.

The End.
