# Della Dolphin’s Bedtime Bubble Plan

In a cozy bedroom with star stickers on the ceiling, Della the playful dolphin bounced on a sea-blue blanket. Her bed was shaped like a little boat and smelled like fresh soap. A nightlight glowed warm and golden. The curtains swished softly, like waves saying shhh.

Della loved to splash in the tub and sing loud songs. But tonight, bedtime felt big. Pillows were everywhere. Her favorite book hid under the blanket. Her toothbrush was on the shelf, high and far.

“I want help,” Della called. “Can someone do it for me?”

No one answered. The house was quiet. She could hear the gentle hum of the heater and the soft tick-tick of a tiny clock.

Della’s flippers drooped. “Bedtime is hard,” she whispered. “I can’t do everything.”

She tried to wiggle under the blanket, but her tail poked out. She tried to reach the toothbrush with her nose, but it wobbled and stayed put. She felt a bubble of worry pop in her chest.

Then Della took a small, brave breath. “I am little,” she said to herself, “but I can still try.” She remembered something her teacher said: “You can ask for help, and you can help yourself.” That sounded like a good plan.

Della made a Bedtime Bubble Plan. She whispered, “First, I will tidy. Then, I will brush. Then, I will cozy up.”

She looked around her cozy bedroom. It smelled like clean sheets and a little like oranges from her bath soap. Her toys were soft and smiling.

“Step one: tidy!” Della told herself. She used her flippers to push the pillows to the head of the bed. Thump, thump. She stacked her storybooks on the little table. Tap, tap. She put her stuffed starfish in a neat line. Plop, plop. The room felt calmer. Della felt calmer too. The tick-tick of the clock sounded friendly now.

“Step two: brush!” Della looked at the high shelf. She stood tall on her boat-bed and still couldn’t reach. She stayed calm. “I can say what I need,” she decided. That is self-advocacy.

She called out in a clear voice, “I need my toothbrush down here, please!”

Her grandma, who was knitting in the next room, peeked in with a smile. “You used your strong voice,” Grandma said. “That’s smart.” Grandma placed the toothbrush on a lower shelf. “You’re doing so much on your own. I’m proud.”

Della grinned. “Thank you!” She added toothpaste that smelled like mint leaves and tiny bubbles. She brushed, swish-swish, swish-swish, until her teeth felt squeaky and bright. She spit gently and rinsed. “Minty!” she giggled, tasting the cool tingle.

“Step three: cozy up,” Della said. She pulled the blanket straight. She fluffed the pillow with soft, whooshy sounds. She found her favorite book under the blanket and hugged it tight. It smelled like paper and adventure.

Grandma said, “Do you want me to read to you?”

Della thought. She liked Grandma’s voice. But she also wanted to try. “I will start,” Della said, standing tall and sure. “If I get stuck, I will ask.”

“That is very assertive,” Grandma said. “You told me your plan.”

Della opened the book. The pages made a gentle flap. She read the little words she knew. “Sun. Fish. Nap.” When she saw a big word, she tapped the page. “Grandma, can you help with this one?”

“Of course,” Grandma said kindly. She read the tricky word, and Della repeated it, one slow sound at a time. Della felt her worry bubble float away.

Soon the story was done. The room felt warm and safe. The nightlight made tiny stars dance on the ceiling. Della listened to the quiet hush of the house and the soft swoosh of the curtains.

Grandma tucked the blanket around Della. “How do you feel, little dolphin?”

Della smiled, her eyes sleepy. “I did it myself, and I asked when I needed help,” she said. “I made a plan. I used my voice. I was brave.”

Grandma kissed Della’s forehead. “That is self-reliance,” she said. “You took care of your bedtime. That is self-advocacy and assertiveness too.”

Della wiggled happily. “I can do many things,” she whispered. “I can try first. I can say what I need. I can finish strong.”

She looked around her tidy, cozy bedroom one more time. The pillow smelled fresh. The blanket felt soft against her fins. Her teeth felt minty. Her heart felt proud.

“Goodnight, stars,” Della said. “Goodnight, Grandma. Goodnight, brave me.”

Grandma turned off the bright lamp. The nightlight glowed like a little moon. Della closed her eyes. She pictured bubbles floating up, each one a tiny cheer.

One bubble said, “Try.”
One bubble said, “Speak up.”
One bubble said, “You can.”

Della drifted to sleep with a peaceful smile, wrapped in her own strong, gentle plan.

The clear lesson: When something feels big, you can make a small plan, try by yourself, and ask for what you need. That is being brave and taking care of you.

The End.
