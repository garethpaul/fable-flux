# Milla Mouse and the Treehouse Map

Milla was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright button eyes. She lived in a cozy treehouse that smelled like warm wood and cinnamon toast. The treehouse had tiny windows, a rope ladder, and a little bell that went ding-ding when friends came to visit.

One sunny morning, Milla found a note on her quilted bed. It was taped to a shiny, rolled-up paper. The note said: “Dear Milla, please bring this basket of muffins to Grandma Squirrel. Follow the map. Love, Auntie Bee.” Milla sniffed the basket. Mmm! Blueberry muffins. Sweet and warm.

Milla unrolled the map. It showed a path from her treehouse to Grandma Squirrel’s stump home. There were little pictures: a red leaf, a bumpy log, a sparkling stream, and a big round rock. Next to each picture was a small star and a number. “One, two, three, four,” Milla read softly. “I can do this.”

As Milla tied on her blue scarf, her friend Pip the sparrow fluttered by the window. “Chirp! Where are you going, Milla?”

“I’m taking muffins to Grandma Squirrel,” Milla said. “Auntie Bee says to follow the map.”

Pip tilted his head. “I know a short way. We can fly and skip the log and the rock!”

Milla looked at the map. The paper felt smooth under her paw, and the drawings were neat and clear. She remembered Auntie Bee’s words: Follow the map. Milla took a slow, thinking breath. “Thank you, Pip. But the note says to follow the map. It has numbers. I think that means go in order.”

Pip chirped, “Okay! I’ll come and sing while you walk.”

They climbed down the rope ladder. Leaves whispered in the breeze. The sun felt warm on Milla’s whiskers. The first picture on the map was a red leaf. Soon they reached a big red leaf lying on the path. “Number one,” Milla said. “Left at the red leaf.” She turned left. Pip sang a happy tweet-tweet tune.

Next was the bumpy log. Milla read, “Number two: cross the bumpy log slowly.” She put one paw, then another. The log felt rough and scratchy. “Slow and steady,” Milla said. Pip flapped nearby. “You’re doing it!”

After the log came the sparkling stream. The map showed a tiny bridge. “Number three: use the little bridge.” The water splashed and giggled. Sunlight made the stream look like diamonds. Milla walked over the bridge, holding the basket tight. “Almost there,” she smiled.

Just then, they heard a soft cry. “Help! Help!” It was Benny Bunny, sitting by the path. His long ears drooped. “I dropped my ribbon,” he said, pointing to a brambly bush. A shiny blue ribbon was tangled in the thorns.

Milla looked at the map. The next step was the big round rock. She felt the basket handle in her paw. She also felt her heart caring for Benny. “We can help,” Milla said. “But we must be careful and follow safe steps.”

Pip fluttered close. “What should we do first?”

Milla made a decision. “First, I will put the basket down so it doesn’t fall. Next, we will look for a stick to lift the ribbon. Then, we will pull slowly.” She spoke clearly so everyone knew the plan.

They found a smooth stick. Milla held it steady. Pip lifted the ribbon with his beak. Benny gently tugged. Snip-snap! The ribbon came free without tearing.

“Thank you,” Benny said, his ears perking up. “You followed good steps.”

Milla smiled. “We followed directions. Directions help us stay safe and kind.” She checked the map again. “We are at number four now: the big round rock.”

They reached the rock, warm from the sun. The map said to turn right at the rock and knock three times at Grandma Squirrel’s door. Milla’s paws felt excited and a little wiggly. Pip hummed. Benny hopped along to say hello.

Soon they saw Grandma Squirrel’s stump home. Flowers danced in the breeze, and the door smelled like pine. Milla knocked one-two-three. Knock, knock, knock!

Grandma Squirrel opened the door with a friendly smile. “Oh my, muffins! And little helpers too. Come in, darlings.”

Inside, the room was cozy. A kettle sang. The table was smooth and warm. Milla gave the basket to Grandma. “Auntie Bee said to follow the map. We did, step by step.”

Grandma Squirrel nodded. “You listened. You chose carefully. You helped a friend. That shows respect for others and for the plan.”

They shared muffins. Crumbs were sweet on Milla’s tongue. Pip sipped a tiny drop of tea. Benny tied his ribbon into a neat bow. Everyone felt happy and calm.

Before they left, Grandma gave Milla a small gold sticker shaped like a star. “For following directions,” she said. “When you follow directions, you learn, you stay safe, and you can help.”

On the way home, Milla looked at the map again. The paper rustled softly. She felt proud and peaceful. “Today I learned to follow directions, to think before choosing, and to be kind,” she said.

Pip chirped, “And muffins taste better when we do the right thing!”

Milla giggled. The leaves whispered yes, yes. The sun lowered, painting the path with honey light. Together, they walked back to the treehouse, ready to follow good directions another day.

The End.
