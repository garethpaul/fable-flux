# Bubbles in the Busy Basement

Splash was a playful dolphin with bright, shiny eyes and a giggly squeak. One sunny afternoon, Splash wiggled down a long slide that led to a busy basement playroom. The basement was full of color and sound. Toy bins clicked. A laundry machine hummed. A little fan whirred, making a soft breeze. There were blocks, crayons, drums, and a big blue tub of water toys that smelled like fresh soap bubbles.

“Wow,” said Splash, flicking her tail with a swish-swish. “So many things to try!”

Children and animals were already playing. Mia stacked blocks. Leo colored a big rainbow. Sammy the squirrel rolled a toy car that went vroom. A soft rug tickled Splash’s tummy as she scooted closer.

“I want to see everything,” Splash said, her voice round and bubbly. “I want to touch, and smell, and hear, and learn!”

In the corner stood a shiny bubble machine. It was silver and round with a big button that looked like a juicy blueberry. It blinked and blinked. Splash’s eyes grew wide. Pop! Pop! Pop! Little leftover bubbles clung to the machine and smelled sweet, like berries.

“What’s that?” Splash asked, her curiosity twinkling.

“It’s the Bubble Buddy,” said Mia. “We all take turns. We share the bubbles.”

“Share?” Splash tasted the word, like a tiny bubble on her tongue. “Share means we all get some?”

“That’s right,” said Leo, nodding. “One turn for each friend.”

Splash clapped her fins. “I love bubbles! I want a big, big bubble storm!” She wiggled fast, excited. Without asking, she pressed the blue button.

Whoosh! The Bubble Buddy began to buzz and glow. Bubbles floated up and up. They were cool and wet on Splash’s snout. They smelled sweet and made the air sparkle. The room filled with giggles and soft pops. Pop! Pop! Pop!

Splash squealed, “More bubbles for me!” She pressed the button again and again. Bubbles swirled like shiny snow. Soon the bubbles grew so many that Sammy could not see his car. Mia’s block tower got slippery and tipped… clatter! Leo’s paper got wet and wrinkly.

“Hey,” Mia said with a frown. “We were saving the button for turns.”

“I can’t find my car,” said Sammy, tail twitching. “It’s lost in the bubbles.”

“My rainbow is soggy,” Leo sighed, holding up the drippy page.

Splash blinked. She felt a squish in her tummy. She had wanted to have fun. But now her friends looked sad. The bubbles tickled her nose, but her smile slid away.

“I… I was excited,” Splash whispered. “I wanted to see what the button did. I forgot to ask. I forgot to share.”

The bubble machine hummed softer and softer. The fan whirred. Drip, drip went the bubbles on the floor. The room smelled like berries and a little bit like soap.

Mia took a breath. “It’s okay to be curious,” she said kindly. “We like that too. But we all need a turn. Sharing means you wait, you ask, and you listen.”

Splash nodded. “I can do that. I want to include everyone. Can we make a plan?”

Leo smiled. “Yes! Let’s make a sharing plan.”

They made a little line on the soft rug. Mia held a timer shaped like a lemon. Tick-tick-tick. “Each friend gets one minute,” she said. “Then we pass the turn.”

“I will go last,” Splash said. “I want to help first.”

Splash used her tail to push bubbles toward Sammy. “Here is your blue car,” she said. “Vroom-vroom!” She gently dabbed Leo’s rainbow with a dry towel. “I’m sorry,” she said. “Your colors are still bright.” She helped Mia restack the blocks, one, two, three, tall.

Sammy’s whiskers wiggled. “Thanks, Splash.”

“It feels good to help,” Splash said. The squish in her tummy warmed into a glow.

Now it was Bubble Buddy time. Mia went first. She pressed the button once. Whoosh! Small bubbles drifted. Mia danced in a circle, shoes squeaking on the rug. “Pop! Pop!” she said, laughing.

Tick-tick-tick. The lemon timer dinged. “My turn!” said Sammy. He chased a shiny bubble and caught it on his nose. Plop! He giggled.

Then it was Leo’s turn. He held his paper far away and watched bubbles float around his rainbow like tiny moons. “Pretty,” he whispered.

Finally, it was Splash’s turn. She pressed the blue button slowly. Whoosh. Bubbles rose like little stars. She waited, watching her friends. “Who wants to pop with me?” she asked.

“Me!” said Mia.

“Me!” said Leo.

“Me too!” said Sammy.

They all popped together. Pop! Pop! Pop! The room was full of giggles and soft, sweet smells. The bubble machine hummed happily. The fan kept the air cool and breezy. Splash felt light as a feather, even in a busy basement.

When the bubbles faded, Splash placed her fin on the button and looked around. “Does anyone want an extra turn?” she asked.

Mia smiled. “You shared your turn. That was kind.”

Leo nodded. “Sharing makes play better.”

Sammy did a tiny hop. “Sharing makes friends feel seen.”

Splash’s eyes sparkled. “I learned something today. Curiosity can be kind when I ask, wait, and share. When we include everyone, fun grows and grows.”

They cleaned up the floor with soft towels, swish-swish, and put the toys back in bins with a click. The basement felt cozy and calm. Splash gave a happy squeak.

“Next time,” Splash said, “let’s try the drums—and share the sticks!”

Everyone cheered. They had learned, they had helped, and they had played together.

The End.
