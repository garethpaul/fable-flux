# Tessa Tortoise Talks It Out

In a cozy garden shed, Tessa Tortoise woke up to the soft smell of fresh soil and the sweet scent of wood shavings. The sun peeked through a dusty window and made little sparkles float in the air. Tessa stretched her legs slowly and smiled. “Good morning, little shed,” she said in her warm, gentle voice.

The shed was full of interesting things. There were shiny watering cans, soft twine, a friendly broom, and a little box of seeds that rattled like tiny maracas when the wind tapped the wall. Tessa loved her tidy corner with a small basket bed and a smooth, flat stone for thinking time.

“Today I will water the seedlings,” Tessa decided. “They will love a sip.”

Just then, she heard a rustle and a clatter. “Clink, clank, clunk!” A tall rake leaned over. A stack of pots wobbled. A quick, chatty mouse named Pip zipped across the floor and bumped the pots again.

“Oops! Oops! So sorry!” squeaked Pip, his whiskers twitching. “I’m in a hurry. I need a soft place for a nest. These pots look perfect!”

The pots toppled like a tiny tower. “Clatter!” A pot rolled right into Tessa’s basket bed. Her smooth stone slid away and bumped her toes. She pulled into her shell for a moment. It felt safe and quiet inside.

When she peeked out, she took a slow breath. In... and out. “I can use my calm voice,” Tessa reminded herself. “I can say what I need.”

“Pip,” said Tessa, speaking softly but clearly, “I feel upset when the pots crash. The noise hurts my ears, and my bed is messy now. I need our shed to stay safe and tidy.”

Pip froze, his tiny ears turned toward her. “Oh,” he said, blinking. “I didn’t know it hurt your ears. I was only thinking about my nest.”

Tessa nodded. “I understand you want a cozy home. Let’s find a way that is kind for both of us.”

The rake gave a little creak, like a kind old uncle. “A-hem,” it groaned. “Teamwork can help.” The broom whispered, “Sweep, sweep, tidy and neat!”

Pip’s tail swished. “What can I do?” he asked.

“First,” said Tessa, “let’s make the shed quiet again. Pip, can you help me pick up the pots? We can stack them softly.” She smiled to show she was not angry, just firm.

“Yes!” Pip chirped. He lifted a tiny pot in both paws and set it down with a gentle tap. “Tap.” Tessa used her steady legs to nudge the bigger pots into a neat stack. “Slide, slide.”

“Thank you, Pip,” Tessa said. “Next, let’s find you a nest spot that won’t bonk and clatter.”

Pip’s nose twitched. “I like soft and warm,” he said.

Tessa looked around. The seed box rattled. The twine looked cozy. In the corner, behind the watering cans, was a small shelf with a folded cloth. It smelled like sunshine. “What about that little shelf?” Tessa asked. “It is snug and still. We can make a nest there.”

Pip’s eyes shone. “Oh! Yes, please!”

Together, they stretched the cloth into a little pocket. Pip tucked in a bit of twine, soft as a cloud. Tessa added a dry leaf that smelled like tea. “There,” Tessa said. “A quiet nest.”

Pip sighed happily. “Thank you, Tessa. You spoke so kindly. You told me how you felt. You told me what you needed. That helped me listen.”

Tessa’s heart felt warm, like a sunbeam on her shell. “Peaceful talking helps everyone,” she said. “I can be calm and strong at the same time. That is being assertive.”

A soft drip-drop started outside. Rain tapped the roof. The shed felt safe and cozy again. Tessa set her smooth stone back in place and poured a gentle drink for the seedlings. “Sip, sip,” went the soil.

Pip peeked from his new nest. “If I ever get too fast again,” he said, “will you remind me with your calm words?”

“Yes,” said Tessa. “I can say, ‘Pip, I feel worried when things crash. Please move more slowly.’ And you can tell me what you need too.”

Pip nodded. “I will say, ‘Tessa, I need help finding a safe spot.’”

They both smiled. The broom hummed its tidy song. The rake stood tall and quiet.

Tessa settled on her smooth stone and closed her eyes for a moment. She felt her breath go in and out. In... and out. Her tummy felt peaceful. Her mind felt clear.

“Today,” Tessa whispered, “we learned to use calm words, to listen, and to make a plan. That keeps our bodies well and our hearts happy.”

Pip yawned in his snug nest. “And our shed safe,” he added.

“Safe and kind,” Tessa agreed.

The rain sang softly, and the seedlings stood a little taller. Tessa and Pip rested, knowing they could talk things out, anytime.

The End.
