# Sunny Nuts and Honest Truths

In a sunny backyard, a little resourceful squirrel named Sprig zipped along the fence. The grass felt cool under his tiny feet. The sun warmed his back like a soft blanket. Birds chirped, “Cheep, cheep!” and the leaves whispered in the breeze.

Sprig loved to collect acorns. He kept them in a neat little pile by the big flowerpot. “I am ready for snack time!” he squeaked, patting his pile with his fluffy tail.

One bright morning, Sprig spotted a shiny red ball by the sandbox. It glowed in the sun like a cherry. “Ooo!” Sprig said. “What a round treasure!” He nudged it with his nose. Boink! It bounced and made a happy sound. Sprig giggled and rolled it toward his acorn pile.

Just then, a small voice called, “Hey! That’s my ball!”

A little chipmunk named Pip stood on the patio. Pip’s whiskers shook, and his eyes looked worried. He hugged his tiny paws to his chest and said, “I was playing with it. Then it rolled away.”

Sprig froze. His ears twitched. He liked the shiny ball. He wanted to keep it. A little whisper in his tummy said, “Say it is yours.” But another whisper, a stronger, kinder one, said, “Tell the truth.”

Sprig felt warm in his cheeks. He took a deep breath. The air smelled like fresh grass and sweet flowers. “I found it by the sandbox,” Sprig said softly. “I wanted it. But it is not mine. I am sorry, Pip.”

Pip’s face relaxed. “Thank you for telling me,” he said. “I got scared I would never see it again.”

Sprig nodded. “It is yours. I should not keep it,” he said. He pushed the ball toward Pip. Boink. It rolled with a friendly bounce.

Pip smiled. “Do you want to play with me?” he asked. “We can take turns.”

Sprig’s tail swished with joy. “Yes, please!”

They played a rolling game in the sunny yard. Sprig pushed. Boink. Pip caught. Boink. They laughed when the ball tickled their toes. The birds chirped along, and a butterfly fluttered by, like a tiny yellow kite.

After a while, Sprig sighed and sat down by the flowerpot. “Pip,” he said, “I wanted your ball so much. It felt hard to say the truth.”

Pip nodded. “It can be hard,” he said. “But it feels better after.” He patted the ball. “When you tell the truth, friends can trust you.”

Sprig looked at his acorn pile. He wanted to learn. He wanted to be a good friend. He had an idea. “Maybe we can make a plan,” he said. “A plan to share.”

Pip tilted his head. “What kind of plan?”

“A turn plan,” said Sprig. “We can roll the ball for five leaf-counts each. One, two, three, four, five. Then switch. That is fair.”

Pip smiled wide. “I like that plan!”

They placed a bright green leaf by the ball. Sprig counted, “One leaf, two leaf, three leaf, four leaf, five leaf.” Then he passed the ball. Pip counted too. Soon they did not need the leaf. They could feel the rhythm, like a little drum in their hearts. Roll. Catch. Share.

Later, they rested by the flowerpot. The sun felt cozy. A honey scent drifted from the flowers. A soft breeze carried the giggles of children playing at the far fence. Sprig nibbled an acorn and thought about the morning.

“I learned something,” Sprig said.

“What did you learn?” asked Pip.

Sprig looked at his paws. “I learned that telling the truth is brave,” he said. “It makes a tight feeling in my tummy go away. And I learned that when we share and make a plan, we both get to play.”

Pip nodded. “That is learning. That is being honest,” he said. “Honesty keeps friends close.”

Just then, a tiny blue jay hopped near. “Peep?” it chirped, eyeing an acorn.

Sprig chuckled. “Would you like an acorn, little bird?” he asked.

The blue jay peeped again. Sprig pushed a small acorn over. Pip clapped. “Sharing is caring,” Pip sang.

Sprig grinned. “And honesty is best,” he added.

When the sky turned soft and golden, Sprig stood up. “Thank you for playing,” he told Pip. “And thank you for letting me learn.”

Pip bounced his ball. Boink. “Thank you for telling the truth,” he said. “Friends tell the truth. Friends share. Friends learn together.”

They bumped paws. Tap.

As the sun slid low, Sprig tucked his acorns neatly. His heart felt light and warm. He whispered to the backyard, “Today I was honest. Today I learned. Today we made a good plan.”

The backyard seemed to smile back with rustling leaves and a sweet, sunny glow.

And from that day on, whenever Sprig felt a little tug to keep something that wasn’t his, he took a deep breath, listened to the kind whisper in his tummy, and chose honesty. He remembered the fun of sharing and the peace of a fair plan.

That made every sunny day even brighter.

The End.
