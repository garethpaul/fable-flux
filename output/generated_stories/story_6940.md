# Gigi the Giraffe Says “I’m Sorry”

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with long, gentle eyelashes and a neck that stretched up, up, up. One sunny morning, Gigi visited the community pantry. The pantry was a cozy room with shelves full of cans, boxes, and baskets. It smelled like warm bread and sweet apples. People from many places came to the pantry to share and to take what they needed.

Gigi tiptoed inside. Her hooves made soft clippy-clop sounds on the floor. She whispered, “I’m here to help.” Her heart felt big and kind. She wanted to do a good job.

Mr. Otter, the pantry helper, waved. “Good morning, Gigi! Today we are sorting food. We need to be careful. Some food goes here, and some food goes there.”

Gigi nodded. “Careful. I can do careful.” She smiled and reached her long neck toward the top shelf. She loved reaching high.

Gigi started stacking boxes of pasta. She hummed a little tune. “Do-be-do, do-be-do.” The boxes were light. The shelf was tall. Gigi felt proud.

Then—uh-oh! Her tail swished. Her hoof slipped just a tiny bit. A tower of spaghetti boxes wobbled and tumbled—clatter, rustle, whoosh! The boxes fell like crunchy leaves. One box bumped a basket of apples. The apples rolled across the floor like red marbles.

A little mouse named Mimi squeaked and jumped back. “Eep! Rolling apples!”

Mr. Otter blinked. “Oh dear.”

Gigi’s cheeks grew warm. Her ears drooped. “I made a mess,” she said in a small voice. “I didn’t mean to.”

Mimi the mouse peered up. “Are you okay?”

Gigi took a deep breath. She felt a twist in her tummy. She wanted to hide, but she remembered she was thoughtful. Being thoughtful meant thinking about others and making things right.

Gigi said, “I’m sorry.” She looked at Mr. Otter and Mimi. “I made a big mess. I want to make amends.” The words felt brave and kind.

Mr. Otter nodded softly. “Thank you for saying sorry, Gigi. Making amends means we fix what we can.”

“How can I help?” asked Gigi.

Mimi smiled. “We can pick up the apples together!”

So they did. Gigi lowered her long neck. She used her gentle lips to lift the apples one by one. “Crunchy, shiny, round,” she whispered as she placed them back. Mimi rolled the apples along the floor with her tiny paws—bump, bump, bump! Mr. Otter stacked the pasta boxes, this time in small, steady piles.

Gigi listened and learned. “Maybe we need a plan,” she said. “A plan is preparation.”

Mr. Otter’s whiskers twitched. “Good idea!”

They made a simple plan:
- Heavy things on lower shelves.
- Light things up high.
- No tall towers.
- One shelf at a time.

Gigi repeated the plan out loud. “Heavy low, light high, no tall towers, one shelf at a time.” Saying it made it stick.

They worked together. The pantry became neat and calm again. Gigi felt the twist in her tummy melt away. She felt steady and useful.

A family came in—a fox dad, a bunny mom, and a little panda child. The panda wore a blue scarf and looked a little sleepy. The family spoke softly in different words. Gigi did not know those words, but she understood their smiles.

“Welcome,” Gigi said gently. She pointed to the shelves and made a friendly sweeping motion. “Please, take what you need.”

Mr. Otter added, “We share food with friends from near and far.”

Mimi showed the family a basket of rice with a picture of a globe on it. “This rice comes from far away,” she said. “We take care of each other, all around the world.”

Gigi felt her heart grow warm again. She thought, We are global neighbors. We help each other.

When the family chose apples, Gigi carefully reached high and handed them down, one by one. “Here you go,” she said. The little panda giggled. “Thank you!”

Gigi smiled back. “You’re welcome.” She felt proud, but this time her pride felt calm and careful.

When the pantry was quiet again, Gigi turned to Mr. Otter and Mimi. “I am sorry for my mistake,” she said once more. “Thank you for helping me fix it. I learned to slow down, to make a plan, and to make amends.”

Mr. Otter patted her hoof. “You did a kind and brave thing, Gigi.”

Mimi nodded. “Saying ‘I’m sorry’ is caring. Fixing the problem is caring, too.”

Gigi looked around at the tidy shelves. They looked safe and ready. The air smelled like apples and hope. She took a deep breath and felt peaceful.

She whispered to herself, “When I make a mistake, I can make it right. I can help my community. I can be a good neighbor to the whole world.”

Outside, the sun shone bright. Inside, the pantry felt bright, too.

The End.
