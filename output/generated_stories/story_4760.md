# Kody the Koala's Moving Day

Kody the koala sat very still on top of the washing machine in the warm laundry room. He loved sitting quietly and watching the colorful clothes spin round and round through the glass door. The machine made a gentle "whoosh-whoosh" sound that made him feel sleepy.

"Kody!" called his friend Bella the bunny from the doorway. "Come play outside! We're having a dance party in the garden!"

Kody shook his furry gray head slowly. "No thank you, Bella. I like sitting here. It's nice and peaceful."

Bella's ears drooped a little. "But moving and dancing is so much fun! You'll love it!"

"I don't think so," Kody said softly. "I'm not good at moving around like you are."

After Bella left, Kody felt a little sad. He watched the clothes tumble and spin, and something didn't feel right in his tummy. Through the small laundry room window, he could see his friends hopping, skipping, and dancing in the sunshine. They looked so happy!

Just then, Mama Koala walked into the laundry room carrying a basket of fresh, warm towels that smelled like flowers.

"Kody, sweetie, why the long face?" she asked, setting down the basket.

"I hurt Bella's feelings," Kody admitted. "She wanted me to dance with everyone, but I said no. I don't like to move around much."

Mama Koala sat beside him on the washing machine. "You know, moving our bodies is very important. It helps us grow strong and feel happy. Would you like to try some gentle movements right here in the laundry room?"

Kody looked around the cozy room. "Here? But there's not much space."

"That's perfect for starting small," Mama smiled. "Let's make a goal together. First, can you stretch your arms up high like you're reaching for eucalyptus leaves?"

Kody slowly raised his arms above his head. "Like this?"

"Perfect! Now, can you march in place like the washing machine drum?" Mama demonstrated, lifting her knees up and down.

Kody giggled at the silly comparison and started marching. His feet made soft "thump-thump" sounds on the floor. "This feels good!"

"Now let's sway side to side like clothes hanging on the line," Mama said, moving gently back and forth.

As they moved together, Kody felt warm and tingly all over. His body felt awake and happy! The laundry room filled with their soft laughter.

"I want to try more movements!" Kody said excitedly. "Can we spin like the washing machine?"

They spun slowly in circles until they felt dizzy and giggly.

"Mama, I think I'm ready to apologize to Bella and maybe try dancing outside," Kody said, feeling proud of himself.

"That's wonderful, sweetie. Remember, we can start with small movements and build up to bigger ones. Every little step counts!"

Kody hopped down from the washing machine and walked to the garden. He found Bella sitting alone under a tree.

"Bella, I'm sorry I said no earlier," Kody said. "I was scared to try moving, but I learned that it feels really good! Could you teach me some gentle dance moves?"

Bella's face lit up like sunshine. "Of course! Let's start slow and work our way up!"

From that day on, Kody set small movement goals for himself every day, and he discovered that his body loved to move just as much as it loved to rest.

The End.
