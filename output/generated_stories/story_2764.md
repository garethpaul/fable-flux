# Splash the Dolphin's Big Voice

Splash the dolphin lived in a magical garden shed that was filled with water instead of tools. The shed had sparkly blue walls that looked just like the ocean, and rainbow fish swam around the corners. Splash loved to play here every day, spinning and diving through the cool, clear water.

One sunny morning, Splash woke up feeling excited. "Today is going to be special!" he clicked happily, making his dolphin sounds echo through the shed. The water felt warm and bubbly against his smooth gray skin.

As Splash was practicing his best jumps, he heard tiny voices calling for help. "Help us! Help us!" squeaked the voices. Splash looked around with his bright, curious eyes and saw three little hermit crabs stuck under a heavy shell near the shed door.

"Oh no!" Splash said, swimming quickly over to them. "Are you okay, little friends?"

"We're stuck!" cried the smallest crab, whose name was Shelly. "This big shell fell on us, and we can't move it!"

Splash tried to push the shell with his nose, but it was too heavy even for a strong dolphin like him. He felt worried. What could he do?

Then Splash remembered something important his mama had taught him. "When you need help, use your voice! Ask for what you need," she had said.

Splash took a deep breath and called out loudly, "Help! I need help in the garden shed! My friends are stuck!"

Soon, he heard footsteps. A kind girl named Emma opened the shed door. "What's wrong, Splash?" she asked, kneeling down by the water.

"My crab friends are stuck under this heavy shell," Splash explained, pointing with his fin. "I tried to move it, but I'm not strong enough. Could you please help us?"

"Of course!" Emma said with a big smile. She carefully lifted the heavy shell, and the three little crabs scurried out safely.

"Thank you, Splash!" said Shelly. "You were so brave to ask for help!"

"And thank you, Emma!" added the other crabs, doing happy little dances.

Emma sat by the water's edge. "Splash, did you know that dolphins like you live all around the world? In warm oceans and cool seas, dolphins help each other just like you helped these crabs today."

Splash's eyes grew wide with wonder. "Really? There are dolphins everywhere?"

"Yes! And they all work together, just like we did," Emma explained. "When we share our help and use our voices to ask for what we need, we make the whole world a better place."

Splash felt proud and happy. He had learned that speaking up wasn't scary - it was helpful! "I used my voice to help my friends," he said, doing a little spin in the water.

The hermit crabs brought Splash some of their favorite seaweed snacks to share. "This is for being such a good friend," Shelly said.

As the sun set through the shed windows, making the water glow golden, Splash felt grateful. He had learned that asking for help was brave, sharing made everyone happy, and dolphins all over the world were probably helping their friends too.

"Tomorrow, I'll remember to use my voice whenever someone needs help," Splash promised, settling down for a cozy night's sleep in his magical garden shed.

The End.
