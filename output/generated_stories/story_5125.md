# Joey's Clean Writing Adventure

In a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls and fluffy white clouds painted on the ceiling, lived a joyful kangaroo joey named Pip. His favorite spot was the little wooden desk by the sunny window, where he loved to draw and write stories.

One morning, Pip hopped excitedly to his desk. "Today I'll write the best story ever!" he chirped, grabbing his favorite red crayon.

But when Pip looked at his hands, he wrinkled his tiny nose. "Yucky!" His paws were covered in sticky jam from breakfast, dirt from playing outside, and mysterious purple smudges.

Pip tried to write anyway. *Scribble, scribble.* But the crayon slipped in his messy paws. Purple smudges got all over his clean white paper. The jam made the crayon stick and skip.

"Why won't my story look pretty?" Pip wondered aloud, his ears drooping sadly.

Just then, Mama Kangaroo hopped into the bedroom. She saw Pip's frustrated face and the messy paper covered in smudges.

"Oh my little joey," Mama said gently, "what's troubling you?"

"I want to write a beautiful story, but everything looks yucky!" Pip sniffled. He held up his dirty paws. "My hands are all messy, and my story looks terrible!"

Mama Kangaroo smiled warmly. "I think I know just the thing to help. Follow me to the bathroom, sweet Pip."

In the bathroom, Mama turned on the warm water. *Splash, splash!* "Clean hands make everything better," she explained. "When our paws are clean, we can create wonderful things!"

Pip watched the warm, soapy water swirl around his paws. The purple smudges washed away. The sticky jam disappeared down the drain. Soon his paws felt soft and clean.

"Wow!" Pip giggled, wiggling his sparkling clean paws. "They feel so nice and smooth!"

"Now," Mama said, helping him dry his paws with a fluffy towel, "let's try writing again."

Back at his desk, Pip picked up his red crayon with his clean paws. This time, the crayon glided smoothly across a fresh piece of paper. No slipping! No sticky spots! No purple smudges!

"Look, Mama!" Pip exclaimed joyfully. "My letters are so neat and pretty!"

He wrote in big, careful letters: "I LOVE CLEAN HANDS!"

Then Pip drew a picture of himself washing his paws, with soap bubbles floating all around. The drawing was clear and beautiful because his clean hands could hold the crayon just right.

"This is my best story ever!" Pip bounced happily in his chair.

Mama Kangaroo hugged her little joey. "When we keep our hands clean, we can do amazing things. Clean hands help us write, draw, eat, and play better!"

From that day on, Pip always washed his paws before writing his stories. He discovered that clean hands made everything more fun - from holding crayons to turning book pages to giving the best hugs.

Every morning, Pip would hop to the bathroom first, wash his paws with warm soapy water, and then bounce to his desk ready to create wonderful stories with his clean, happy hands.

"Clean paws, clean stories, clean fun!" became Pip's favorite saying.

The End.
