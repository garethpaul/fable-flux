# Polly Paints with Patience

Polly the parrot loved colors. She fluffed her green feathers and flapped into the creative art room. The room smelled like sweet glue and soft crayons. Sunlight danced on glitter jars. Brushes stood like little trees in a forest of color.

“Squawk! Hello, colors!” Polly chirped. “I’m ready to make art!”

Today was Volunteer Art Day. Friends from the neighborhood came to make pictures for the community center. Ms. Lila, the kind art helper, smiled. “We will make a big mural to share,” she said. “Everyone can add something special.”

Polly puffed with pride. “I will paint a bright rainbow,” she said. “I can do it fast!” She dipped a brush in red and swooped it across the paper. Swish! But the line wobbled. Drip. A big red drop slid down.

“Oh no,” Polly whispered, wings drooping. “It’s not perfect.”

Her friend Ben the bunny hopped over. “It’s okay,” he said softly. “We can fix it.”

Polly shook her head. “I can’t. I’m not good,” she said. Her tummy felt tight. Her cheeks felt hot.

Ms. Lila knelt beside her. “Polly,” she said calmly, “our art room is for trying, not for perfect. We can grow with practice. Let’s use our growth minds.”

“What is a growth mind?” Polly asked, tilting her head.

“It’s a mind that says, ‘I can learn,’” Ms. Lila said. “It says, ‘I’m not good yet, but I can try again.’”

Polly blinked. “Not good yet,” she repeated. The word “yet” felt soft and kind, like a warm blanket.

“Let’s take a breath,” Ms. Lila said. They breathed in slow. The room smelled like paper and paint. They breathed out slow. Polly’s tummy felt softer.

Polly looked around. Friends were sharing ideas. Ami the cat hummed an old song from her grandma and painted tiny golden stars. “These are festival stars from my family,” Ami said. “We hang lanterns, and we sing.”

“Wow,” Polly said. “That is your tradition.”

Ben the bunny drew a big carrot garden. “My grandpa grows these,” he said. “He taught me how to pat the soil.”

Polly felt a spark in her chest. “I have a tradition too,” she said. “My family makes feather patterns for special days. We say, ‘Feathers together!’”

“Let’s add your feather patterns to the mural,” Ms. Lila said. “Would you like help?”

Polly nodded. “Yes, please,” she said. “But I am worried. What if I drip again?”

Ms. Lila handed her a small brush. “Small brush, small steps,” she said. “When it’s hard, we can say, ‘I can try.’”

Polly tapped the brush on a napkin. Tap tap. No drips. She dabbed a tiny blue leaf shape. Dab. Dab. “I can try,” Polly whispered. She added a green feather. Then a yellow one. Swish, dab, dab.

A little owl named Oona fluttered close. “Polly, I want to paint near you,” Oona said. “But I need space, please.”

Polly looked at the crowded table. “Let’s talk kindly,” she said. “I feel squished. Can we move the water cup?”

Oona hooted softly. “Yes. I can move the cup.” They both smiled. They slid the cup to the middle. No more elbow bumps.

Polly’s feathers fluffed. “Peaceful talking helps,” she said.

Soon, other friends gathered. “Polly, will you show us your feather pattern?” Ben asked.

Polly gulped. “I’m not good yet,” she said. Then she took a brave breath. “But I can try. Feathers together!” She taught them the simple shapes: leaf, leaf, dot, swirl. She showed them how to tap the brush to stop drips. Tap tap.

Everyone painted a row of feather patterns beside the rainbow and the garden. The mural began to glow. There were festival stars, carrots, and feathers dancing side by side. The room sounded happy—soft hums, gentle brush swishes, and tiny giggles.

Polly made a mistake. A big green smudge! Her heart bumped. “Oh no,” she said.

Ms. Lila was there with a smile. “What can we do?” she asked.

Polly looked closely. The smudge was a funny shape. “We can make it into a leaf,” she said. She added a stem and two little dots. “Ta-da!”

“Great idea,” Oona said. “That’s problem solving!”

Polly felt a warm glow in her chest. “I can learn,” she told herself. “I can try. Mistakes help me grow.”

When the mural was done, everyone stepped back. The colors seemed to sing. The air smelled like orange peels from snack time and fresh paint. Ms. Lila clapped. “You all worked with caring hearts,” she said. “You shared traditions. You used peaceful words. You helped our community.”

Polly stood tall. “We volunteered,” she said. “We made art to share.”

Ben nodded. “And we didn’t give up.”

Polly smiled at the rainbow, the stars, the garden, and the feather patterns. She touched her feather badge on her chest. “I feel proud,” she said. “I am still learning, and that is good.”

Ms. Lila added a small sign under the mural: “We grow when we try.”

Polly read the sign out loud. “We grow when we try,” she chirped. “Feathers together!”

Everyone cheered. The mural shone, bright and brave, ready to make the community center smile.

The End.
