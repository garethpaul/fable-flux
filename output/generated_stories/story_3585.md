# Mimi Mouse’s Guest Room Day

Mimi was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She lived behind a tiny picture frame in a cozy guest room. The guest room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. Sunshine made warm squares on the rug, perfect for tiny mouse toes.

“Today, I will do things by myself,” Mimi said, puffing up with brave feelings. “I can try. I can learn.” Her whiskers tickled with excitement.

On the shelf, a big puzzle waited in a box. The picture showed a picnic under a tree. Mimi tugged the box open. “I will start the puzzle on my own,” she said. The pieces felt smooth and cool. They made a gentle clicky sound when she tapped them.

Her friend, Timmy the tiny turtle, peeked from under a slipper. “Hi, Mimi! Can I play too?”

“Yes,” Mimi said, smiling. “We can take turns. I will try first, and then it’s your turn.”

Mimi looked at the puzzle piece with the bright red apple. “Hmm,” she said softly. “Where do you belong?” She turned it around, around, and around. She listened to her thinking. She felt proud for trying. “I can do this,” she whispered.

Click! The piece fit. Mimi clapped her paws. Timmy clapped his little feet.

“Now my turn!” said Timmy. He picked up a piece with blue sky. He nudged it here, then there. “It’s tricky,” he said.

“You can try,” Mimi said kindly. “Take your time.” She waited, humming a tiny tune. Waiting was hard, but Mimi breathed in the lemony air and stayed patient. She felt strong inside.

Click! Timmy smiled. “I did it!”

They took turns—Mimi, then Timmy, then Mimi again. The picture grew: green grass, a basket, a tree. Laughter bubbled up like warm cocoa.

Then they found a piece with a shiny strawberry. Mimi blinked. “Oh! I love strawberries.” She wanted to grab it. Her paws wiggled. But she remembered their plan.

“I had the last turn,” she said. “It’s your turn, Timmy.” She pushed the strawberry piece toward him. “Here. You try.”

Timmy’s eyes shone. “Thank you, Mimi.” He tried, and click! It fit! Mimi felt a warm glow. Sharing felt good. Waiting felt brave.

After the puzzle, Mimi said, “Let’s make a cozy fort!” The guest room had a soft chair, a stack of folded towels, and colorful scarves. The towels smelled like sunshine. The scarves were silky and swishy.

“We can build it ourselves,” Mimi said. “I will think of a plan.” She looked around, tapping her chin. “Chair first. Towels next. Scarves on top.”

Timmy nodded. “I can carry the small towels.”

Mimi dragged a towel. It was heavy for a mouse. Her paws slid a little, but she kept going. “I can try,” she told herself. She didn’t call for big help. She used her own little muscles. Tug, tug, tug. She paused to breathe and smiled. “I did a little. I can do a little more.”

They took turns placing towels. Mimi spread them smooth. Timmy tucked in corners. Mimi tied a scarf to the chair leg, then to the lamp’s base. The scarf made a gentle whoosh. Inside the fort, it felt cozy and cool. Light made colorful patches like shy rainbows.

“Let’s decorate,” Mimi said. She drew tiny pictures on scraps of paper—stars, a smiling sun, a red apple. Timmy made a wiggly turtle shape. It looked like a dancing jellybean. They laughed and taped the pictures up.

Mimi found a shiny button on the floor. She liked it a lot. It felt cold and round in her paw. But she remembered seeing it on the guest pillow earlier. “This belongs to the pillow,” she said. “It is not mine.” Her heart felt honest and tall. “I will put it back.” She climbed up the pillow and set the button by the stitch. “Doing the right thing feels good,” she whispered.

“Story time in the fort?” Timmy asked.

“Yes,” said Mimi. “We can make our own story!” Mimi started. “Once upon a time, a mouse and a turtle had a picnic.”

“My turn,” said Timmy. “They ate juicy strawberries.” He made a slurp sound. They giggled.

“Then,” Mimi said, “they cleaned up by themselves.” She folded a pretend napkin. “They were independent and kind.”

Timmy nodded. “They helped each other and waited their turns.”

The fort felt safe. The guest room smelled calm. A breeze made the scarves flap like soft wings. Mimi yawned a tiny, happy yawn.

“We did so much by ourselves today,” Mimi said. “We tried. We waited. We shared. We told the truth.”

“And we were creative!” Timmy added. “We built a fort and made a story.”

Mimi’s heart hummed. “When I try to do things on my own, I learn and grow. I can ask for help if I need it, but I can start by trying.”

Timmy smiled. “That is our lesson.”

Mimi nodded. “Being independent means I believe in me, I take turns, I use my ideas, and I do the right thing.”

They snuggled in their colorful fort. The puzzle picture smiled up at them. Mimi whispered, “I can do it, and I can do it kindly.”

Outside, the hallway was quiet. Inside, Mimi and Timmy felt brave, honest, and proud.

The End.
