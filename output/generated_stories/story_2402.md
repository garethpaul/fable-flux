# Gertie's Big Cleanup

Gertie the giraffe loved sitting on her peaceful front porch. The wooden boards felt warm under her long legs, and she could see all the way down the sunny street from up high. Her porch was her favorite place to think and watch the world go by.

This morning, Gertie noticed something was wrong. Her beautiful porch was messy! There were toy blocks scattered everywhere, crayons rolling around, and her favorite books were in a big pile. Even worse, some of the toys belonged to her friend Benny the bunny, who lived next door.

"Oh my!" said Gertie, her long neck drooping with worry. "I made such a mess yesterday when I was playing. And I forgot to give Benny back his toys!"

Gertie remembered how excited she had been yesterday. She had pulled out all her toys to play a big adventure game. She had colored pictures and built tall towers with blocks. But when dinner time came, she had rushed inside and left everything scattered on the porch.

Just then, Benny hopped over to visit. His little nose twitched as he looked around the messy porch.

"Hi Gertie," Benny said quietly. "Have you seen my red fire truck and my puzzle pieces? I can't find them anywhere."

Gertie felt her tummy do a little flip. She could see Benny's fire truck under a pile of her books, and his puzzle pieces were mixed up with her crayons. Benny looked sad, and that made Gertie feel sad too.

"Benny," Gertie said, bending her long neck down to look at her friend. "I'm really sorry. I have your toys here on my porch. I was playing with them yesterday and forgot to put them away properly."

Benny's ears perked up a little. "You do?"

"Yes, and I made a big mess too," Gertie admitted. "I should have been more careful with your special things. And I should have cleaned up before going inside."

Gertie felt much better after telling the truth, even though she was embarrassed about the mess.

"Let me help fix this," Gertie said with determination. "We can clean up together, and I'll make sure your toys get back to you safe and sound."

Benny smiled. "That would be nice, Gertie. Thank you for saying sorry."

Together, they began organizing the porch. Gertie used her long neck to reach toys that had rolled far away. She carefully picked up Benny's red fire truck and wiped it clean with a soft cloth.

"Here's your fire truck," Gertie said gently. "I'm sorry I didn't take better care of it."

"It's okay," Benny said, hugging his toy. "Thank you for cleaning it."

They sorted the puzzle pieces into a neat pile and put Gertie's crayons back in their box. Gertie stacked her books carefully and arranged her blocks in a tidy tower.

"Wow," said Benny, looking around. "Your porch looks beautiful now!"

Gertie felt proud and happy. The wooden boards gleamed in the sunshine, and everything had its proper place.

"I learned something important today," Gertie said thoughtfully. "When I make a mistake, it feels much better to say sorry and fix it than to hide from it."

"And cleaning up together was actually fun!" added Benny.

From that day on, Gertie always remembered to put her toys away carefully and to take good care of things that belonged to her friends. Her peaceful porch stayed organized, and her friendship with Benny grew even stronger.

The End.
