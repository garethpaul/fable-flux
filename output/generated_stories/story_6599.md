# Cubby Cleans the Porch

Cubby was a friendly bear cub with soft, honey-brown fur. He loved to sit on the peaceful front porch of his den. The porch smelled like pine trees and warm wood. Bees hummed in the garden. The breeze felt cool on Cubby’s nose.

“Good morning, porch,” Cubby said. He patted the smooth step with his paw. “Let’s have a snack!”

Cubby brought a basket of juicy berries and crunchy crackers. He munched and smiled. “Mmm! Berries are sweet, and crackers go crunch-crunch,” he giggled. Red berry juice stained his paws. Cracker crumbs sprinkled the floor like tiny snowflakes.

After snack time, Cubby was excited to play. He wiggled. He bounced. He forgot about the crumbs and sticky spots on the porch. He ran to his toy truck and rolled it along the railing. “Vroom-vroom!”

A little bluebird landed on the porch rail and peeped, “Good day!”

“Hi, Bluebird!” Cubby waved. “Want to watch my truck?”

Bluebird looked at the porch floor. “Peep? There are crumbs and berry blobs. Ants might come. Your porch is home for many friends.”

Cubby looked down. He saw red smears, tiny crumbs, and a tipped-over cup. He also saw a line of ants marching. “Oh,” Cubby said softly. “I made a mess.”

Just then, Mama Bear opened the front door. She sniffed the air. “I smell berries,” she said kindly. She looked at the porch. “I also see a sticky floor.”

Cubby’s ears drooped. “I was excited to play, Mama. I forgot to clean up.”

Mama Bear knelt and gave him a gentle hug. “Everyone forgets sometimes,” she said. “What can we do when we forget?”

Cubby thought. He felt a little twist in his tummy. He remembered that the porch was a peaceful place for everyone—birds, bees, and bears. “We can make it right,” he said. “We can clean it up.”

“That sounds like a caring plan,” Mama Bear said. “I will help you think, and you will do the work. That is how we learn.”

Cubby nodded. “I can try.”

He took a deep breath. He listened to the soft wind and the buzzing bees. He felt brave.

First, Cubby picked up the cup and set it upright. “Cup safe,” he said. He picked up the basket and put it by the door. “Basket safe.”

Next, he found a little broom next to the mat. The broom felt light in his paws. Swish-swish went the bristles. The crumbs gathered into a tiny pile. “I am sweeping my mess,” Cubby told Bluebird.

Bluebird peeped, “Thank you. The porch feels better already.”

Now Cubby looked at the sticky berry spots. He wrinkled his nose. “Ew, it smells like extra-sweet jam.” He got a small bucket of warm, soapy water and a soft cloth. Mama Bear showed him how to dip and wring. Dip, swish. Wring, twist. Drip-drip went the cloth.

Wipe, wipe. The red smears faded. Wipe, wipe. The floor shone. Cubby smiled. “I can see the wood again!”

Little Bunny hopped by the front steps. “What are you doing, Cubby?” Bunny asked.

“I’m making amends,” Cubby said proudly. He sounded out the big words slowly. “That means I fix my mistake.”

Bunny twitched his nose. “Can I help?”

Cubby thought. He wanted to be kind and careful. “Yes,” he said, “but please be gentle. This porch is a peaceful place. We must respect it.”

Bunny nodded. He held the dustpan while Cubby swept in the crumbs. Together they dumped the crumbs into the compost bin in the yard. It smelled earthy and warm. “The crumbs will turn into soil food,” Cubby said. “That helps the garden grow.” He felt happy to care for the earth.

When they were done, the porch looked neat and calm again. The wood was smooth and clean. The air smelled like soap and sunshine. The ants had wandered away to the grass. Bluebird sang a bright song. “Peep-peep! Thank you, friends!”

Mama Bear came back with a little sign made from a leaf and a stick. It said, “Please keep our porch clean.” She smiled. “What should we do next to care for our home?”

Cubby’s eyes lit up. “Community service!” he said, clapping his paws. “We can help the neighbors, too.”

Mama Bear nodded. “What is your idea?”

Cubby pointed to a small basket near the door. “I will put a tiny brush and a cloth in here. If anyone makes a spill, they can clean it right away.” He added a friendly note: “We all share this space. Let’s keep it nice.”

Bunny brought a little flower from the yard and set it on the step. “A welcome flower!” he said. It smelled sweet and brightened the porch.

Later, Squirrel scampered over with a cracked acorn shell. “Oops,” Squirrel said. “I dropped this.”

Cubby smiled. “It’s okay. Let’s clean it up together.” They swept, they wiped, and they giggled when the broom tickled their toes. The porch stayed peaceful and tidy.

As the sun began to set, the wood felt warm beneath Cubby’s paws. He sat on the step and sipped cool water. He felt calm and proud.

Mama Bear sat beside him. “What did you learn today?” she asked.

Cubby hugged her. “I learned to care for the earth,” he said. “I clean my mess, I share tools, and I help my friends. That is respect. That is making amends.”

Bluebird sang one last soft peep. The porch glowed golden, and everyone felt safe and happy.

Cubby whispered, “A clean porch is a peaceful porch.”

The End.
