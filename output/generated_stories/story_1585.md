# Benny Bear's Rooftop Adventure

Benny was a small, fluffy bear cub with golden brown fur and curious black eyes. He loved to explore, but sometimes his curiosity got him into trouble.

One sunny morning, Benny climbed up to the rooftop of his family's cozy house. The wind felt cool on his fuzzy ears, and he could see the whole forest below. "Wow!" he said, spreading his little arms wide. "I can see everything from up here!"

Benny found a pile of shiny red apples that his mama had put on the roof to dry in the sun. His tummy rumbled loudly. "Grumble, grumble!" Without thinking, he grabbed ALL the apples and started eating them quickly.

"Mine, mine, mine!" Benny said with his mouth full of sweet, juicy apple. Sticky apple juice dripped down his chin.

Just then, his little sister Bella climbed up to the rooftop too. "Benny!" she called. "Mama said we could share the apples for our snack!"

But Benny didn't want to share. He felt angry and hugged all the apples close to his chest. "NO!" he shouted, his face turning red. "They're MINE!"

Bella's eyes filled with tears. "But Benny, sharing is caring," she said softly.

Benny was so angry that he stood up quickly and started jumping around with the apples. But the rooftop was slippery from the morning dew! His paws slipped on the wet surface.

"Whoa!" Benny cried as he started to slide toward the edge of the roof. The apples rolled everywhere - bump, bump, bump! His heart beat fast like a drum. "Help!" he called, feeling scared.

Bella quickly grabbed a long rope that was nearby. "Benny, hold on!" she said. Even though he hadn't shared with her, she wanted to help her brother stay safe.

Benny caught the rope just in time. Bella pulled and pulled with all her might until Benny was safe in the middle of the roof again. They both sat down, breathing hard.

"Thank you, Bella," Benny said, his voice shaky. "I'm sorry I was mean and didn't share with you."

Bella hugged her brother tight. "It's okay, Benny. I'm just happy you're safe!"

Benny looked at the apples scattered around the roof. Then he looked at his kind sister. His angry feelings melted away like ice cream in the sun.

"Bella," Benny said, "would you like to share these apples with me? And maybe we should sit down while we eat them. Mama always says to be careful on high places."

Bella's face lit up with a big smile. "Yes! And let's sit far away from the edge where it's safe."

The two bear cubs sat in the middle of the roof, sharing the sweet, crunchy apples. The sun warmed their fur, and they could hear birds chirping happy songs in the trees below.

"Sharing makes everything taste better," Benny said, offering Bella the biggest, reddest apple.

"And being safe makes everything more fun!" Bella added.

From that day on, Benny always remembered to share with others and to be careful in high places. He learned that when he felt angry, he should take deep breaths and think about being kind instead of grabbing everything for himself.

And whenever he climbed up high, he always sat down and stayed away from edges, because being safe was the most important thing of all.

The End.
