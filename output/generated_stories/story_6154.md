# Pippa Parrot’s Calm-Down Colors

Pippa the creative parrot lived in a playful living room with bright rugs and soft pillows. Sunlight danced on the floor. Crayons, paper, and stickers sat on a low table. The air smelled like lemon soap and warm toast.

“Art time!” Pippa squawked happily. “Let’s make a rainbow!” She flapped her green wings and hopped to the crayons. Her feathers shimmered like shiny leaves.

Pippa’s friend, Ben, sat cross-legged on the rug. He was small, with curly hair and curious eyes. “I want the blue crayon,” Ben said.

“I want blue too!” Pippa chirped. She reached out with her beak at the same time Ben reached with his hand. Their fingers and beak bumped. The blue crayon rolled under a pillow.

“Oh no,” Ben said. His lip wobbled.

Pippa’s tummy felt tight. “I need blue now,” she squawked. Her voice got louder. Her heart thumped like a drum. Boom-boom, boom-boom.

Ben looked worried. “I want blue now too.”

The room felt noisy and squishy. The clock ticked. The fan whirred. Pippa’s head buzzed like a bee. Her wings twitched. She wanted to grab, but she stopped. She remembered something Nana Parrot had taught her.

“When feelings get big,” Pippa whispered to herself, “I can make them small.”

She placed her feet flat on the rug. It was soft and fuzzy under her toes. She touched her belly with a feathered wing.

“Breathe in… like smelling a flower,” she said softly. She sniffed the lemony air. “Breathe out… like blowing a bubble.” She puffed a gentle whoosh. Pop.

Ben watched. His shoulders dropped a little. “Can I try?” he asked.

Pippa nodded. “In like flowers… out like bubbles.” They breathed together. In, out. In, out. The room felt slower and calm, like a quiet song.

“Let’s find the blue crayon,” Pippa said in a peaceful voice.

Pippa lifted the pillow with her wing. “Peek-a-crayon!” she chirped. The blue crayon rolled into the light. It was smooth and cool. Pippa held it up. “We both want this. Let’s make a plan.”

Ben smiled a tiny smile. “A plan?”

“Mm-hmm,” Pippa said. “We can share. We can take turns. Or we can mix colors to make new blues.”

Ben blinked. “Mix colors?”

Pippa pointed to the crayons. “Dark blue, light blue, and green next to yellow can make ocean colors. We can draw together.”

Ben thought. He tapped the rug with one finger. “I like turns,” he said. “You first for five big breaths. Then me for five big breaths.”

Pippa’s eyes sparkled. “Deal!” She held out a wing tip. Ben touched it with his finger. Tap.

Pippa counted, “One flower smell, one bubble blow. Two flowers, two bubbles.” She drew wavy sky lines. The crayon felt waxy. It made a gentle scritch-scratch sound. “Three… four… five.” She passed the blue to Ben.

Ben drew a round pond. “Scritch-scratch,” he said with a grin. He counted his breaths out loud. “One, two, three, four, five!” Then he handed the crayon back.

They kept going, breathing and passing the blue. Pippa added a yellow sun with orange rays. Ben added a green frog with big eyes. Pippa stuck on star stickers for shiny splashes. Ben used purple for a tiny boat. They giggled when the sticker sheet crackled under their fingers.

Suddenly, a sticker stuck to Pippa’s beak. “Mmmph!” she said, eyes wide. Ben laughed and gently peeled it off. “Thank you,” Pippa chirped, and they both giggled again.

After a while, Pippa felt her wings get wiggly. “My body is buzzy,” she said. “I want to flap.”

She stopped, touched her belly, and breathed. “Flower in… bubble out.” Her body felt softer. “I can wait and take my turn,” she said. “I can choose calm.”

Ben nodded. “Me too.”

They finished their art together. The paper showed a big blue sky, a pond with ripples, a smiling frog, and a little boat. The sun looked warm and golden. The room smelled like crayons and lemon and felt bright and cozy again.

Pippa tilted her head. “We did it,” she said. “We made a plan. We shared. We stayed calm.”

Ben clapped softly. “We used breaths,” he said, “and we took turns.”

Pippa puffed her chest proudly. “When my feelings got big, I stopped, I breathed, and I made a plan.”

Ben held up the picture. “Team art!”

They taped the picture to the wall with a gentle rip-rip of tape. It hung right above the soft pillows. Pippa did a small happy hop. “Next time we want the same thing,” she said, “we can talk, breathe, and make a plan again.”

Ben smiled. “Deal.”

The living room was peaceful. The clock ticked kindly. The fan hummed like a lullaby. Pippa felt light, like a leaf in warm air.

The End.
