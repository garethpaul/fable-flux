# Gigi’s Tick-Tock Plan

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe who lived in a cozy, playful living room. Bright pillows were piled like soft mountains. A round rug felt fuzzy under hooves. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. A big clock on the wall went tick…tock…tick…tock.

“Good morning, clock,” Gigi whispered. She liked how the sound made her feel calm. Today, Gigi had many things to do.

She tapped her hoof and made a list out loud. “First, tidy toys. Then, water the plant. Then, make thank-you cards for the helpers on our street. Then, play and read. Then, help set the table.” She stretched her long neck proudly. “That is my tick-tock plan!”

Gigi picked up blocks. Clack, clack, clack. She put them in a basket. She folded a soft blanket. Swish. She smiled. “One job done,” she said.

Just then, her friend Pip the puppy slid across the rug. “Zoom!” Pip wagged his tail so fast it went thump-thump-thump.

“Hi, Gigi! Want to play chase?” Pip panted.

Gigi looked at the clock. Tick…tock. She took a sip of cool water from her cup and felt the chill on her tongue. “I do want to play,” she said kindly, “but I have my plan. Playtime is after I water the plant.”

Pip’s ears flopped. “Okay,” he said, “I’ll help!” He fetched the little blue watering can with his teeth. Drip, drip, drip. Gigi poured just enough water. The green plant looked happy and shiny.

“Two jobs done,” Gigi said. She felt warm pride in her chest.

Knock, knock! The door tapped lightly. It was Ms. Rosa, the mail carrier. She had a gentle smile and bright blue hat. “Good morning, friends,” she said. “I’m dropping off letters. The street is extra busy today. Please keep toys off the walkway.”

Gigi nodded. “Thank you for telling us. We will help.” She glanced at the clock. Tick…tock. “Ms. Rosa, we are making thank-you cards for helpers. One is for you!”

Ms. Rosa’s eyes sparkled. “How thoughtful. I must go now. See you later!” She waved and left.

Gigi spread out paper and crayons on the coffee table. The crayons smelled a little like wax and rainbows. “Let’s draw,” she said.

Pip drew a big heart. Gigi drew the blue hat and a tiny letter with a smile. They wrote, with careful letters, “Thank you!” Gigi looked at the clock again. “Card time is ten minutes,” she whispered. “When the big hand reaches the red dot, we move to the next job.”

They drew, colored, and giggled. “Time check,” Gigi said softly when the big hand slid to the red dot. “Card time is done.”

Pip’s tail swooshed. “Let’s play now?”

Gigi took a breath and listened to the steady tick…tock. “Not yet. We promised to keep toys off the walkway. That is civic responsibility. It means we take care of our place and our neighbors.”

Pip tilted his head. “Like good helpers?”

“Yes,” said Gigi. “We can help our whole street.”

They went to the doorway. Bright sun warmed their faces. The step felt a little gritty with sand. Gigi spotted three toy cars near the path. She picked them up gently. Pip carried a ball in his mouth. Together, they placed everything in the toy basket inside.

A distant siren sang woo-woo, then faded. Gigi felt glad. “Now people can walk safely,” she said. “We kept our promise.”

Back on the fuzzy rug, Gigi checked the clock again. Tick…tock. “Now it is playtime!” she cheered.

Pip barked, “Hooray!” They played a counting game with soft blocks. “One, two, three!” Gigi stacked them high. Pip gently nudged the tower. It wobbled and wiggled and then—plop!—fell into a heap. They laughed so hard their bellies felt warm and wiggly.

When the big hand reached the next red dot, Gigi took a deep breath. “Time for reading,” she said. She opened a picture book. The paper felt smooth. The pages smelled like stories. Pip curled up, warm and sleepy, by her side. Gigi read with a calm voice. “Once there was a star…” Pip sighed, happy.

Soon, Mama’s voice floated from the kitchen. It smelled like warm soup and bread. “Gigi, Pip,” Mama called, “it’s time to set the table.”

Gigi smiled. “That is our last job.” She and Pip carried napkins. Soft. Folded. They placed little forks and spoons with care. Clink. Clink. Everything looked neat and kind.

Mama came in and hugged Gigi’s long neck. “You followed your plan,” she said. “You helped at home, and you helped our street. I am proud of you.”

Ms. Rosa peeked in the open door. She held the card and waved. “Thank you for the lovely card and for keeping the path clear,” she said.

Gigi’s heart felt big and bright. She looked at the clock one more time. Tick…tock. “Making a plan helped me do what matters,” she said. “I did my jobs, I helped others, and I still had time to play.”

Pip wagged. “Time plans make room for fun,” he barked.

Gigi nodded. “Yes. When we use time well, we can care for ourselves, our home, and our neighbors.”

They sat down to warm soup, feeling safe, helpful, and happy.

The End.
