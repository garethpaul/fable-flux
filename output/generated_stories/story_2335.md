# Ollie Otter's Garden Day

Ollie the otter stretched his furry arms and yawned as the warm morning sun tickled his whiskers. He lived in a cozy apartment with his family, and today was special - it was Garden Day on their balcony!

"Good morning, sunshine!" called Mama Otter from the kitchen. "Are you ready to help plant our new flowers?"

Ollie bounced excitedly on his soft paws. "Yes! Yes! I want to dig and plant and water everything!"

But when Ollie rushed outside to the balcony garden, he stopped. There were so many things scattered around - flower pots, bags of soil, tiny seed packets, and a big watering can. Where should he start?

"Whoa there, speedy!" laughed Papa Otter, stepping onto the sunny balcony. "Before we begin our garden adventure, what do you think we should do first?"

Ollie scratched his head with his little paw. "Um... start digging?"

"That's a good idea," said Papa Otter gently, "but let's think about getting ready first. What do we need to prepare?"

Mama Otter joined them, carrying a basket. "Remember when we bake cookies, Ollie? What do we do before we mix the ingredients?"

"Oh! Oh!" squeaked Ollie, his dark eyes sparkling. "We get everything ready first! We wash our paws and get all the things we need!"

"Exactly right!" said Mama Otter, giving him a warm hug that smelled like lavender.

Together, the otter family prepared for their garden day. First, they washed their paws in the small outdoor sink. The cool water felt refreshing on Ollie's fur. Then they put on their special garden aprons - Ollie's was bright green with tiny fish on it.

"Now, let's gather our tools," said Papa Otter. "Ollie, can you help us organize everything we need?"

Ollie nodded eagerly. He carefully carried the small hand shovels to one corner of the balcony. Mama Otter arranged the flower pots in neat rows. Papa Otter lined up the seed packets so they could read all the pretty pictures.

"Look how organized everything is now!" said Ollie proudly. "It's so much easier to see what we have!"

"You're absolutely right," said Mama Otter. "When we prepare first, everything goes much smoother."

Working together, the otter family planted beautiful marigolds, sweet-smelling basil, and colorful petunias. Ollie's favorite part was gently patting the soft, dark soil around each tiny plant. The earth felt cool and crumbly between his paws.

"My tummy is rumbling," said Ollie after they finished planting.

"Mine too!" laughed Papa Otter. "Good thing we prepared snacks earlier!"

Mama Otter brought out a tray with sliced apples, crackers, and fresh water. They sat together on the balcony, admiring their beautiful new garden while they ate.

"Preparing made everything so much better," said Ollie, crunching happily on an apple slice. "We worked together, we took care of ourselves with snacks and clean paws, and now look at our gorgeous garden!"

The afternoon sun made the new plants sparkle with water droplets. Butterflies began visiting their balcony, dancing from flower to flower.

"Tomorrow, we'll need to water our plants," said Papa Otter.

"And I'll prepare by getting the watering can ready tonight!" said Ollie confidently.

As the sun set, painting the sky in soft orange and pink colors, Ollie felt proud and happy. He had learned that taking time to prepare made everything more fun and successful.

The End.
