# Owl's Letters From the Treehouse

High in a cozy treehouse, a wise old owl named Oriel fluffed her soft feathers. The morning air smelled like pine and warm toast. Sunlight danced through the leaves and made golden dots on the floor.

“Good morning, treehouse,” Oriel hooted softly. “Today is writing day.”

Oriel loved writing. She had a little wooden desk. She had smooth paper that felt cool to touch. She had a bowl of shiny acorns and a cup of sweet tea. She also had a tidy box with three labels: To Write, To Send, and To Keep.

“Let’s be organized,” Oriel said. She lined up her pencils in a neat row. Tap, tap, tap went the tips on the desk. She put new paper in the To Write box. She smiled. “All ready.”

A little breeze wiggled a string of colorful flags. The flags came from many places. One was bright red with a sun. One had tiny stars. One had a green leaf. Oriel touched each flag gently.

“These are from my friends around the world,” she said. “We share stories. We help each other. We care.”

Knock, knock! A squirrel named Pip peeked in. His tail twitched like a fluffy broom.

“Hi, Oriel!” Pip chirped. “What are you doing?”

“I am writing letters,” Oriel said. “Would you like to help?”

“Yes!” said Pip. He hopped inside and sniffed the air. “Mmm, tea smells nice.”

“We can drink tea later,” Oriel said with a wink. “First, we write. We are citizens of the world when we share and listen.”

Pip tilted his head. “What is a citizen of the world?”

Oriel’s eyes shone. “It means we belong to one big, wide world. We are kind and helpful to friends near and far. We learn about each other. We take care of our home, Earth.”

Pip smiled. “I want to do that!”

“Good,” said Oriel. She pulled out a map. It felt smooth and crinkly. Tiny pictures showed oceans and mountains. “Look, this is the world. My friend Asha lives near the big, blue ocean. My friend Ben lives by snowy hills. My friend Mei lives where flowers bloom early.”

Pip’s whiskers wiggled. “Can we write to them?”

“Yes,” said Oriel. “But first, let’s organize.” She pointed with her wing. “Paper here. Pencils here. Stamps here. Letters go in the To Send box.”

Pip clapped his paws. “I can put stamps on!”

“Wonderful,” Oriel said. “Let’s think: What do our friends need today?”

They sat very still. They could hear birds singing. Cheep, cheep. They could hear leaves whisper. Shhh, shhh.

“I know,” said Oriel. “Asha likes to clean the beach. She picks up tiny bits of trash. We can cheer her on.”

Oriel began to write. Scratch, scratch went her pencil.

Dear Asha,
Thank you for caring for the ocean. The fish and the crabs are happy. We will pick up litter in our woods today. We are proud of you.

Love,
Oriel and Pip

Pip read the letter. “It feels warm,” he said. “Like a hug.”

“Letters can be hugs,” Oriel said with a smile. She slid the letter into the To Send box.

Next, Pip said, “Ben likes to share books with his friends. Can we write to him?”

Oriel nodded. “Yes. He is a world helper too.”

They wrote:

Dear Ben,
We love how you share books. We will share our stories at the treehouse. We will take turns and listen.

Love,
Oriel and Pip

Pip placed a stamp carefully. “Stick!” he said. He put the letter into the To Send box.

They wrote to Mei:

Dear Mei,
Your flower pictures are beautiful. We learned the names. Daisy, tulip, and lily! We will water our treehouse plants. Thank you for teaching us.

Love,
Oriel and Pip

Pip looked at the map again. “We help them, and they help us,” he said softly.

“Yes,” said Oriel. “That is global citizenship. We learn and we care together.”

Just then, a little robin fluttered in with a tiny envelope. Flutter, flutter. The envelope smelled like lavender.

“For you,” chirped the robin. “From far away.”

Oriel opened it carefully. The paper crinkled. She read aloud:

Dear Friends,
Today we planted a small tree. We organized a team. We took turns holding the shovel. We sang a song. The tree will give shade and fruit. What did you do today to help?

Love,
Your friends across the sea

Pip’s eyes grew big. “Let’s help too!”

Oriel nodded. “Yes. We will pick up litter under the treehouse. We will sort it into bins. Blue for paper. Green for glass. Brown for leaves.”

They went outside. The ground felt cool and bumpy. They heard bees buzz and smelled fresh earth. Pip picked up a wrapper. Oriel picked up a bottle. They put each item in the right bin.

“We are organized helpers,” said Oriel.

When they finished, the forest looked clean. The air felt bright. Pip giggled. “The trees look happy.”

Back inside, Oriel wrote one more note:

Dear Friends,
Today we cleaned our woods. We sorted the litter. We took care of our home. Thank you for your letter. We feel close to you, even far away.

Love,
Oriel and Pip

They placed the letters in the To Send box. Oriel poured tea. The steam smelled sweet like honey. Pip took a tiny sip. “Ahh,” he said.

Oriel fluffed her feathers. “What did we learn today?”

Pip held up his paws. “Be organized. Write kind letters. Help our big world.”

Oriel nodded. “Yes. When we listen, share, and care, we are good citizens of our world.”

The sun dipped low. The treehouse glowed with soft, golden light. Oriel and Pip felt warm and proud.

“Tomorrow,” Oriel said, “we will read letters from new friends.”

“And we will help again,” Pip said.

Together, they watched the leaves sway and the flags dance. The world felt close and kind.

The End.
