# Kora the Calm Koala and the Garden Shed Day

In a sunny backyard, beside soft grass and buzzing bees, stood a little garden shed. It smelled like wood and flowers. Inside, tools hung like shiny silver smiles, and clay pots waited like sleepy cups. A calm koala named Kora lived nearby in a gum tree, but today she climbed down for a little adventure.

Kora liked slow breaths and gentle steps. “Hello, shed,” she whispered. “Let’s have a peaceful day.”

She pushed the door. Creak! The light slipped in like warm honey. Kora blinked and smiled. “I will tidy and rest,” she said. “That is good for wellness.” She put a small mat on the floor. She put a bowl of cool water nearby. She set a tiny bell on a shelf.

Just then, she heard a soft rustle. A shy snail peeked out from a flowerpot. “Hello,” said the snail. “I am Nia.”

“Hello, Nia,” said Kora. “I am Kora. Would you like to be calm with me?”

Nia nodded slowly. “I move slow. I like calm,” she said.

They both listened. Outside, a butterfly fluttered. Drip, drop went a little leak into a bucket. It was peaceful.

Suddenly, the door burst wide. Bang! A bouncy rabbit hopped in. “Hi! I’m Riff!” he shouted. His ears shook like little flags. “Let’s jump and drum! Tap-tap-tap!” He drummed on a pot with a stick. Clang, clang!

Nia pulled into her shell. Kora’s ears twitched. She placed a soft paw on her heart. “One calm breath,” she whispered to herself. In… and out. The air smelled like mint leaves. Kora felt steady.

She turned to Riff and smiled. “Hi, Riff. I like your energy. It sounds like thunder drums.”

Riff grinned. “Yes! I am fast! I am loud! I am fun!” He hopped. “Come on!”

Kora spoke kindly. “Riff, I like calm time now. Nia likes calm too. Can we talk and make a plan?”

Riff paused and looked around. “I just want to play,” he said, a little softer.

Kora nodded. “I hear you. You want to move and make music. I want quiet and slow breathing. Nia wants gentle sounds. We are all different. That is special.”

Riff’s nose twitched. “Different is good?”

“Yes,” said Kora. “We can celebrate our differences. We can take turns. That helps our bodies feel well.”

“How?” asked Riff.

Kora picked up the tiny bell. “We can use this bell. When it rings once, we have calm time. When it rings twice, we have movement time. We can care for our wellness together.”

Nia peeped out. “I like that,” she said. “It feels safe.”

Riff bounced in place. “Okay! I will try.”

Kora rang the bell once. Ding.

“Calm time,” Kora said. “Let’s do koala breaths. In through the nose, out through the nose.” She closed her eyes. “Smell the eucalyptus. Warm air out.”

Nia breathed slow. Riff tried. He took a big breath, then giggled. “Again?” he asked.

“Again,” said Kora. They breathed together. The shed felt like a hug. The light on the wooden floor made a soft yellow glow. Kora’s belly moved like a tiny wave. Riff’s paws stopped tapping. Nia stretched her little body.

Kora smiled. “How do you feel?”

“Soft,” said Nia.

“Like a calm drum,” said Riff. “My heart is quieter.”

Kora nodded. “That is wellness. We care for our bodies and feelings.”

Then Kora rang the bell twice. Ding-ding.

“Movement time!” Riff cheered.

They made gentle music. Riff tapped a pot with a soft brush. Tap… tap. Nia tapped the bucket with her shell, a tiny tick-tick. Kora shook a seed rattle. Shhh… shhh. It was not too loud. It was happy.

Riff hopped in circles, but he watched his friends. “Is this okay?” he asked.

Kora gave a thumbs-up. “Yes. Thank you for checking in.”

After a while, Kora rang the bell once. Ding.

They sat on the mat. “Water break,” said Kora. She poured a little into a leaf for Nia and a cup for Riff. “Sips, not gulps,” she said, smiling. The water felt cool on their tongues.

“Riff,” Kora said kindly, “I like your big energy. It brings joy.”

Riff beamed. “And I like your calm. It feels safe.”

Nia added, “I like that we talk. It helps my heart.”

They made a sign for the shed. Kora wrote, “Calm and Move Shed.” Riff drew bouncing stars. Nia drew a slow spiral. They hung it on a nail. The sign swayed gently in the warm air.

Kora rang the bell a last time. “We did it,” she said. “We cared for wellness with breaths, water, and kind words. We used peaceful communication. We celebrated our different ways.”

Riff hugged Kora and Nia. “I can be loud sometimes,” he said. “But I can listen, too.”

“And I can be quiet,” Nia said, “and also try new sounds.”

Kora’s eyes shone. “We are a good team.”

Outside, a breeze whispered through leaves. Inside the garden shed, three friends smiled. They felt calm, happy, and strong.

The End.
