# Milo the Monkey Speaks Up

In a cozy nursery filled with soft toys and colorful books, lived a little monkey named Milo. The nursery smelled like lavender and sunshine, with fluffy clouds painted on the ceiling and a rainbow rug on the floor.

Every day, Milo watched the other toy animals play together. The teddy bears built tall towers with blocks, the dolls had tea parties, and the toy cars raced around the track. But Milo always sat quietly on his shelf, wishing he could join in.

"I want to play too," Milo whispered to himself, swinging his long tail nervously. But he felt too shy to ask.

One sunny morning, the teddy bears were building the biggest tower ever. Milo's eyes sparkled as he watched them stack block after block.

"Wow, that looks so fun!" Milo said softly, but no one heard him.

The tower grew taller and taller until... CRASH! All the blocks tumbled down.

"Oh no!" cried Benny Bear. "We can't figure out how to make it stay up!"

Milo knew exactly how to help. He had been watching and learning for weeks! He took a deep breath and felt his heart beating fast like a little drum.

"Um, excuse me," Milo said in a small voice. The bears didn't hear him.

Milo remembered what Mama always said: "When you have something important to say, speak up clearly and kindly."

This time, Milo climbed down from his shelf and walked over to the bears. He stood up tall and spoke in his clearest voice.

"Excuse me, Benny! I know how to help with your tower!"

All the bears turned to look at Milo. His cheeks felt warm, but he kept going.

"I've been watching you build, and I learned that if you put the biggest blocks on the bottom and the smallest ones on top, it won't fall down!"

"Really?" asked Benny Bear, his button eyes twinkling. "You've been learning about building?"

"Yes!" Milo said, feeling braver. "I love watching and learning new things. Can I show you?"

"Of course!" said Bella Bear. "We'd love your help!"

Milo's hands felt a little shaky with excitement as he picked up the biggest block. He placed it carefully on the soft rug, then chose the next biggest one.

"See? Big blocks make a strong bottom," Milo explained, his voice growing stronger. "Then we add smaller blocks as we go up!"

The bears watched with wonder as Milo showed them his building technique. Soon, they were all working together, and their tower stood tall and proud!

"Milo, you're so smart!" cheered Benny. "Thank you for speaking up and teaching us!"

"And thank you for including me," Milo said with a big smile. "I was scared to ask before, but I'm glad I found my brave voice!"

From that day on, Milo always spoke up when he wanted to join in or had ideas to share. He learned that using his voice to ask for what he needed wasn't scary – it was how he made friends and shared his knowledge with others.

The nursery became even more magical with Milo's ideas and courage, and all the toys loved having such a thoughtful friend who wasn't afraid to speak up.

The End.
