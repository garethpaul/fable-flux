# Poppy the Parrot’s Attic Band

Poppy was a bright, creative parrot. Her feathers were red, green, and gold, like a box of crayons. She lived in a quiet attic at the top of a cozy house. The attic smelled like old books and warm wood. Sunlight slipped in through a tiny round window and painted soft gold shapes on the floor.

Poppy loved making music. She had a small drum, a shiny triangle, a toy piano, and a basket of jingling bells. She also had crayons, papers, and ribbons she used to make little flags for her “attic band.”

One morning, Poppy flapped her wings and chirped, “Music time! Tweet-tweet!” She pecked at the toy piano. Plink, plonk! She tapped the drum. Tap-tap-tap! She shook the bells. Jingle-jingle!

But something felt wrong. Her triangle was missing. Her ribbon flags were tangled. The drum sticks were under a pile of papers. The attic looked like a windy storm had blown through it.

“Oh no,” Poppy sighed. “I cannot find my triangle. I cannot see my drum sticks. My music is… stuck.”

Poppy’s heart beat fast. Her chest felt tight. She fluttered from box to box. “Where is it? Where is it?” The more she flapped, the messier it got. Papers slid to the floor with a hush-hush sound. Crayons rolled away and bumped the wall. Knock, knock.

Poppy paused. She took a slow breath. In… and out. In… and out. The attic grew quiet again. She listened. She could hear the soft ticking of the old clock and the tiny hum of the house.

“Calm helps me think,” Poppy whispered to herself. “Calm helps me find.”

Just then, a friendly squeak came from a corner. It was Mimi the mouse, with soft gray fur and bright eyes. She wiggled her whiskers. “Hi, Poppy. Do you need help?”

Poppy nodded. “I want to make music, but I can’t find my things. It feels too big.”

Mimi looked around. “Hmm. The attic is busy. Let’s make it simple.”

“How?” asked Poppy.

Mimi tapped her tiny paw on the floor. “Let’s sort and put away. Then we can play. We can make homes for everything.”

“Homes?” Poppy tilted her head.

“Yes,” said Mimi. “A home for bells. A home for sticks. A home for ribbons. Like a map for your things!”

Poppy’s eyes lit up. “A map for my things! I like that.”

They made a plan:
- First, they took slow breaths together. In… and out.
- Next, they made three little piles: Music, Art, and Cozy. The Music pile had the drum, triangle, bells, and piano. The Art pile had crayons, paper, and ribbons. The Cozy pile had a soft blanket and a pillow.
- Then, they found three boxes. Poppy decorated each box with a simple picture. She drew a drum on one box, a crayon on another, and a star for the cozy box.

“Now the things have homes,” Mimi said with a happy squeak.

Poppy smiled and put the drum sticks in the drum box. Tap. The bells went into the music box. Jingle. The crayons slid into the art box. Skritch-scratch. The blanket and pillow went into the cozy box. Floof.

When they finished, the attic felt bigger and lighter. The sun made bright, calm shapes on the clean floor.

“I can see!” Poppy cheered. She lifted the lid of the music box. There was the shiny triangle, resting like a little moon. “I found you!”

Poppy tapped it with a tiny stick. Ting! The sound was clear and sweet. Mimi clapped her paws. “Beautiful!”

Poppy fluffed her feathers. “Thank you, Mimi. You shared your idea. You helped me feel calm.” She looked at the three boxes. “Now I can share, too.”

Poppy opened the attic window a crack. A soft breeze came in. It smelled like leaves and cookies from the kitchen below. Poppy called, “Friends, do you want to play in my attic band?”

Up the steps came Benny the bunny and Lila the ladybug. “We will be gentle,” Benny said. “We will put things back,” Lila promised.

Poppy showed them the boxes. “Music box here. Art box here. Cozy box there. When we are done, we put each thing in its home.”

They nodded. “Okay!”

The band began. Poppy played the triangle. Ting, ting. Benny tapped the drum. Tap, tap. Mimi shook the bells. Jingle, jingle. Lila hummed a tiny tune. Mmm-mmm.

The sounds mixed like colors in a painting. It was happy. It was calm. When they finished each song, they put every piece back in its box. Ting goes here. Tap sticks go there. Jingles go home. Easy, easy.

After the music, they snuggled on the cozy blanket. The attic felt peaceful and neat. Poppy’s heart felt light.

“I learned something today,” Poppy said softly. “When I am calm and I organize, I can find what I need. When I share my space and my plan, we can make music and have fun.”

Mimi nodded. “Organization helps our play.”

Benny and Lila smiled. “We like your attic, Poppy.”

Poppy gave a happy chirp. “I like it too. Everything has a home. My friends have a place. My music has room to sing.”

The End.
