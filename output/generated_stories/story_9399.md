# Ollie Otter's Big Day Surprise

In a cozy nursery filled with soft toys and colorful books, lived a playful little otter named Ollie. The nursery smelled like lavender and had sunshine streaming through the windows. Ollie loved to slide down the rocking chair and splash in his water bowl, making happy chirping sounds all day long.

One morning, Ollie's mama came into the nursery looking very excited. "Ollie, sweetie," she said in her gentle voice, "Grandma Otter is coming to visit today! She's bringing her famous fish cookies."

Ollie's whiskers twitched with joy. "Yay! When is she coming, Mama?"

"In just two hours," Mama said. "We need to get ready for her visit."

Ollie bounced up and down on his favorite cushion. "I'm ready! I'm ready!" he squeaked.

But when Mama looked around the nursery, she saw toys scattered everywhere. Ollie's blocks were under the bookshelf, his stuffed fish was behind the toy chest, and his favorite ball was nowhere to be seen.

"Oh my," Mama said softly. "Ollie, what do you think Grandma will say when she sees our messy nursery?"

Ollie stopped bouncing. He looked around and saw the mess too. His little heart felt worried. "Will Grandma be sad?" he asked, his voice small and quiet.

"Well," Mama said, sitting down beside him, "let's talk about this. What could we do to make Grandma feel welcome?"

Ollie thought hard, his tiny paws pressed against his furry cheeks. "Maybe... maybe we could clean up?"

"That's a wonderful idea!" Mama said, clapping her paws together. "But cleaning takes time. How long do you think it will take us?"

Ollie looked at all the toys. There were so many! "A really, really long time?" he guessed.

"Let's make a plan," Mama suggested. "If we work together and prepare step by step, we can make it fun and fast."

Ollie's eyes sparkled. "Can we make it like a game?"

"Absolutely!" Mama said. "Let's count as we put things away. You love numbers!"

They started with the blocks. "One block, two blocks, three blocks!" Ollie counted in his squeaky voice as he stacked them neatly. The blocks made satisfying clicking sounds as they fit together.

Next, they found the stuffed fish. "This fishy smells like the ocean," Ollie giggled, hugging it before placing it on the toy shelf.

"You're using such wonderful words to describe things," Mama said proudly. "Grandma loves it when you talk about how things look, smell, and feel."

As they worked, Ollie asked, "Mama, what if we don't finish in time?"

Mama smiled. "Then we'll explain to Grandma that we're still learning to prepare for visitors. She'll understand because she loves us."

"And maybe she can help us finish!" Ollie added excitedly.

When they found the last toy - Ollie's red ball under the rocking chair - the nursery looked beautiful. Everything was tidy, and the afternoon light made the room glow warmly.

Just then, they heard a gentle knock at the door. "That's Grandma!" Ollie squealed.

Grandma Otter came in carrying a basket of delicious-smelling fish cookies. "Oh my!" she exclaimed, looking around the clean nursery. "What a lovely, welcoming space! I can see you prepared for my visit."

Ollie beamed with pride. "We cleaned together, Grandma! And we counted, and we talked about how things smell and feel!"

"You learned that preparing ahead makes special times even more special," Grandma said, giving Ollie a warm hug.

As they shared cookies together, Ollie felt happy and proud. He had learned that preparing for important things - and working together - made everything better.

The End.
