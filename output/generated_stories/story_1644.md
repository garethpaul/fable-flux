# Bixby Beaver Shares the Words

Bixby was a busy beaver. He wore bright blue goggles and a tiny lab coat. He worked in a basement lab under the library. The lab smelled like warm wood and clean paper. Little lights twinkled like fireflies. Machines hummed softly. Click. Whirr. Beep.

Bixby loved words. He kept jars of words on shelves. Each jar had a label. “Hello,” “Please,” “Thank you,” and “Friend.” Bixby liked to listen to the jars. When he opened a lid, the word would float out and whisper, “Hello!” or “Friend!” in a friendly voice. It made Bixby smile.

“I am busy,” Bixby said to himself, tapping his tail. “Today I will build a Word Bridge. It will help everyone talk and share.”

He set up blocks from wood, soft clay, and shiny foil. He used a gentle glue that smelled like apples. He read his plan, written in big, simple letters. Step 1: Share a word. Step 2: Listen. Step 3: Build together.

Knock, knock.

Two friends peeped down the stairs into the basement lab. Pip the puppy wore a red scarf. Momo the mouse wore tiny pink shoes. Their noses twitched. They heard the hum. They saw the jars. Their eyes grew wide.

“Hi!” said Pip. His tail wagged. “What are you making?”

“A Word Bridge,” Bixby said. “It lets words cross from me to you. It helps us be kind.”

Momo whispered, “I like that. I like kind.”

Bixby looked at his jars. He felt a little tight inside. He had collected these words for a long time. He liked to keep them safe. He liked to hear them when he was alone. He hugged one jar close. It was the “Thank you” jar. The words inside glowed like honey.

Pip sniffed the air. “It smells like apples,” he said. “Can we help?”

Bixby’s paws paused. “I… I do not know,” he said. Sharing felt big. What if the jars got sticky? What if he ran out of words?

Bixby looked at his plan. Step 1: Share a word.

He took a deep breath. He opened the “Hello” jar. A soft voice rose up. “Hello!” It echoed, like a warm hug.

Bixby held out the jar. “I can share this,” he said. “Hello is a good start.”

Pip smiled. “Hello!” he barked back. Momo giggled. “Hello, hello!”

The lab felt brighter. The lights blinked happily. Bixby’s tight feeling loosened.

“Let’s build,” Bixby said. He showed the blocks. “Wood here. Clay here. Foil on top. It will be shiny like a moon.”

Pip pushed the wood blocks with his nose. Thunk, thunk. Momo patted the clay with her tiny paws. Squish, squish. Bixby smoothed the foil. Shhhrrrp. The sounds made a little basement song.

But the bridge wobbled. Wiggle, wobble, whoa!

“Oh no,” said Momo. “It is dizzy.”

Bixby frowned. “We need strong words,” he said. “Words can hold us up.” He looked at the shelves. He reached for “Please.” He opened the lid. “Please,” the word said, quiet and sweet.

“Please pass the clay,” said Bixby.

Pip passed the clay. “Please hold the foil,” said Momo.

“Please lift,” said Pip.

With each “Please,” their paws and paws and paws worked together. The bridge stood taller. It did not wobble as much.

Now Bixby opened the “Thank you” jar. The jar glowed like sunshine. “Thank you,” the words sang. The lab felt warm, like a blanket.

“Thank you, Pip,” said Bixby.

“Thank you, Momo,” said Pip.

“Thank you, Bixby,” said Momo.

The bridge shone. But there was still a gap in the middle. A little space. A little silence.

Bixby looked at the highest shelf. There was a jar with a pink ribbon. The label said, “Share.” It was his favorite jar. He had never opened it with friends. He hugged it again. His heart went thump-thump.

He remembered his plan. Step 3: Build together.

Bixby smiled. “I can share,” he whispered. He opened the jar.

“Share,” sighed the word. It drifted like a soft cloud. It wrapped around them. It filled the gap in the bridge. The blocks settled. Clack. The clay hugged the wood. The foil smoothed. Shhh.

Pip’s ears perked. “It works!”

Momo tiptoed onto the bridge. “Listen,” she said. “The bridge talks.” When they stepped, the bridge spoke kind words. “Please.” “Thank you.” “Share.” “Friend.”

The three friends stood together. They felt calm and proud. The lab hummed with happy sounds.

Bixby held up one more jar. “This one says hello in new ways,” he said. He opened it. The lab filled with gentle voices. “Hola.” “Bonjour.” “Jambo.” “Ni hao.” “Salam.” “Namaste.” The words were music. Pip wagged in rhythm. Momo squeaked with joy.

Bixby said, “We can greet friends in many languages. We can be kind in many ways.”

They walked across the Word Bridge. Each step said a friendly sound. Hola. Please. Merci. Thank you. Share.

Bixby’s heart felt big. He had given his words. He had shared his best jar. And the bridge became strong because they worked as a team.

Pip yawned. “I feel cozy,” he said. Momo nodded. “I feel brave.”

Bixby cleaned the lab. He put the lids on the jars, but not too tight. He wanted to share again tomorrow.

“We did it together,” said Bixby. “When we share, we make strong things. When we use kind words, we feel close. Words can be gifts.”

Pip and Momo smiled. “We will use our words,” they said. “We will share.”

The lights blinked goodnight. The machines hummed a soft lullaby. The basement lab smelled like apples and wood and happy.

The End.
