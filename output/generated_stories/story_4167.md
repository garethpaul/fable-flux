# Benny Bear's Big Feelings Day

Benny Bear was a fluffy brown cub who lived in the coziest nursery you could imagine. His room had soft yellow walls, a warm blue rug, and shelves filled with colorful books and toys. This morning, sunlight streamed through his window, making everything glow like honey.

"Good morning, Benny!" called Mama Bear from downstairs. "Time to clean up your room before breakfast!"

Benny looked around his nursery. Oh my! His blocks were scattered everywhere like a rainbow explosion. His stuffed animals were having a tea party on the floor instead of sitting on his bed. His favorite books lay open, their pages fluttering in the morning breeze from his window.

"This is too much!" Benny huffed, his little paws clenched into fists. He felt his tummy get tight and his face grow warm. "I don't want to clean up! It's too hard!"

Benny was about to throw himself on the floor when he remembered something Mama Bear had taught him. When big feelings come, take three deep breaths like a sleepy bear.

"Sniff... sniff... sniff," Benny breathed in slowly, smelling the sweet morning air. "Whoosh... whoosh... whoosh," he breathed out, letting his shoulders drop down.

His tummy felt better already! Now he could think more clearly.

"Okay, Benny," he said to himself in his gentlest voice. "Let's make a plan. Mama says the best way to eat honey is one lick at a time!"

First, Benny decided to put away his blocks. He picked up the red ones, then the blue ones, then the yellow ones. "Red, blue, yellow," he sang as he worked. "What a pretty rainbow!" The blocks clicked and clacked as they went into their special box.

Next came his stuffed friends. "Come on, Mr. Rabbit," Benny said, giving his bunny a gentle hug. "Time to hop back to bed!" He placed each animal carefully on his pillow, arranging them just right. The soft fur felt nice under his paws.

Finally, it was time for his books. Benny loved his books so much! There was one about a dancing elephant, another about a singing frog, and his very favorite about a little bear who learned to ride a bicycle.

"Thank you for the wonderful stories," Benny whispered to each book as he closed it gently and placed it on the shelf. "I'll read you again soon!" He ran his paw along their smooth spines, reading the colorful titles.

When Mama Bear came upstairs, she gasped with delight. "Oh, Benny! Your room looks beautiful! How did you manage to clean up so much?"

Benny's chest puffed up with pride. "I took deep breaths when I felt upset, and then I made a plan! I did one thing at a time, just like eating honey one lick at a time!"

"That's wonderful self-control," Mama Bear said, wrapping Benny in the warmest, fuzziest hug. "You listened to your feelings and then chose what to do. That's what big bears do!"

As they walked downstairs together, Benny felt proud and happy. His room was clean, his books were safe on their shelf, and he had learned something important about himself.

"When big feelings come," Benny said to himself, "I can breathe, make a plan, and take it one step at a time!"

The End.
