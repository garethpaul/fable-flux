# Cub's Tiny Steps in the Tinkering Lab

In a cozy house on a sunny street, there was a squeaky door that opened to the basement lab. The lab smelled like lemon soap and warm wood. Little lights blinked on a big pegboard. Jars of buttons, springs, and colorful beads sat on a shelf, clink-clink, like tiny bells when moved.

A friendly bear cub named Cub padded down the steps. His paws made soft thump-thump sounds on the stairs. Cub wore a little apron with pockets. He wiggled his ears and smiled. Today felt special.

“I have a big idea,” Cub said. His voice echoed, hello-hello, around the room. “I want to build a Good-Morning Helper!”

Cub’s eyes shone. “My helper will ring a bell when it’s time to brush my teeth, do stretches, and drink water. It will help me do healthy things every day!”

Cub took a deep breath. The lab felt cool and calm. He wrote on a bright sticky note with a fat blue crayon: Goal: Make a Good-Morning Helper.

He stuck the note on the wall where he could see it. “That is my goal,” Cub said proudly. “I will do it, step by step.”

Cub looked around the lab. He heard a gentle hum from a tiny fan. He felt the smooth table under his paws. He saw a little red button, a silver bell, some yarn, and a small clock that tick-tick-ticked.

“Step one,” Cub said. “Make a plan.” He drew three boxes on a paper.
- Box 1: Ring bell for tooth time.
- Box 2: Stretch time song.
- Box 3: Drink water reminder.

Cub nodded. “I can do this.”

He tried to tie the bell to the clock. The yarn slipped—whoops! The bell fell and bounced, ding-ding-ding, across the table. Cub’s nose wrinkled. “Oh,” he said softly. His ears drooped.

He looked at his sticky note again. Goal: Make a Good-Morning Helper.

Cub put a paw on his chest. “I can try again,” he whispered. “Tiny steps.”

He tried a different way. He looped the yarn through the bell’s little hole. He pulled it gently. He asked out loud, “What comes next?” The lab was quiet, like it was listening. Cub smiled. “Next is the button.”

He pressed the red button. Click! It felt smooth and firm. He taped the button near the bell. “When I press this in the morning, the bell will ring for tooth time.” Cub practiced pressing it. Ding! The cheerful sound made him giggle.

“Time for the stretch song,” Cub said. He found a small music chip that played a bouncy tune. He connected it to a tiny speaker with bright green wires. The wires felt rubbery and cool. He pressed play. La-la-la! The music filled the lab like warm sunshine.

Cub clapped his paws. Then he frowned a little. “How will I remember to drink water?” He tapped his chin. Tap, tap.

He looked at the little clock. “You can help!” he told it. He set the clock hand to point at a blue sticker with a water drop on it. “When the hand gets here,” he said, “the bell will ring and the light will blink.”

He added a small blue light. He pressed some putty to hold it. The putty smelled a tiny bit like mint. “Blink when it’s water time,” he whispered to the light. The light did not answer, but Cub felt brave.

He tested everything. Press. Ding! Tooth time. Play. La-la-la! Stretch time. Tick-tick-tick. Blink! Ding! Water time. Cub’s heart felt happy and big.

But when Cub tried to lift the helper, it wobbled. The bell slid. The button tilted. “Uh-oh,” he said. “It’s not steady.”

Cub sat down on the cool floor and took a slow breath in. He let it out, whooo. “Tiny steps,” he reminded himself. “I can fix it.”

He found a wooden board. It was smooth and smelled like pine trees. He gently glued the parts onto the board. He pressed them down with soft, strong paws. He waited. Waiting was hard. He hummed along with the little fan, mmm-mmm-mmm, to pass the time.

Finally, Cub touched each part. They felt snug and safe. “Ready,” he said.

He picked up his Good-Morning Helper. It was steady and bright. He set it by the stairs where morning sun would shine.

The next day, rosy light peeped into the lab. Cub yawned a big bear yawn. He pressed the red button. Ding! “Tooth time,” he said. He brushed his teeth—brush, brush, brush—until they felt squeaky clean. He pressed play. La-la-la! He stretched tall, then wide, then wiggly, smiling at how good his body felt. The clock hand moved. Blink! Ding! Cub sipped cool water. “Ahhh,” he said. His tummy felt happy.

Cub looked at his sticky note again. Goal: Make a Good-Morning Helper.

“I did it,” Cub whispered. His fur felt warm with pride. His ears perked up. “I can set goals and reach them, little by little.”

He added a new sticky note: Next Goal: Teach my friend how to make one, too!

Cub waved at the blinking light. “Thank you for helping,” he told the helper. Then he turned to the stairs, ready for a bright day.

The End.
