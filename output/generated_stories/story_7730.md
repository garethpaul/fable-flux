# The Little Lion Cub and the Wobbly Wheels

In a cozy garage workshop, a brave little lion cub named Lio liked to visit. The garage smelled like warm wood and clean soap. Sunlight made bright stripes on the floor. Tools hung on the wall—shiny wrenches, silver screws, and a red toolbox that clicked when it closed. Lio’s paws made soft pat-pat sounds on the smooth floor.

“Wow,” Lio whispered. “So many things to fix!”

Mr. Badger owned the workshop. He wore tiny glasses and a blue apron. “Hello, Lio,” he said kindly. “Today we help our friends.”

Lio puffed up with pride. “I am brave! I will help.”

The first friend arrived. It was Tika the turtle, pushing a small wagon with wobbly wheels. “My wagon shakes-shakes,” Tika said, her voice a little trembly. “It makes me scared.”

Lio’s tail swished. He wanted to fix it fast. He jumped up. “I can do it! I am brave!”

Mr. Badger smiled. “Being brave is good. Being gentle is good, too. Let’s listen first.”

Tika’s eyes were big and worried. Lio crouched down. He touched the wheel. It squeaked. Eeee. He felt the rough edge. He smelled a bit of metal oil—slippery and sharp. Lio looked at Tika. “Does it shake when you turn left?” he asked softly.

Tika nodded. “Yes. Left is hard.”

Lio took a deep breath. “Thank you for telling me.”

Just then, Rocco the raccoon came in carrying a broken toy truck. “My truck won’t roll,” he huffed. “I was here first!”

Tika looked down. Rocco’s face was scrunched. Lio’s ears twitched. He felt a tug inside. He wanted to be fair.

“Hmm,” Mr. Badger said. “We can make a plan.”

Lio stood tall. “We will be fair,” he said. “Tika was already on the floor with the wagon. Rocco, you can sit by the green chair. We will help you next. Is that okay?”

Rocco sighed. “Okay,” he said. “But please hurry. I feel sad.”

Lio nodded. “I hear you.” He turned back to Tika. “Let’s help your left turns.”

Lio and Mr. Badger worked together. Lio held the wagon still. Mr. Badger loosened the squeaky wheel. Squeak, squeak. Lio found a soft cloth that smelled like lemon soap. He wiped the axle. Then he dabbed a little oil. Drip, drop. Lio spun the wheel with one paw. Whirr. It felt smooth.

“Try it,” Lio said.

Tika pulled the wagon in a small circle. No wobble. She smiled. “It works! Thank you, Lio.”

Lio’s chest felt warm. He liked helping. He turned to Rocco. “Your turn now.”

Rocco brought his truck. “I tugged the wheel too hard,” he mumbled. “I was mad. I broke it.”

Lio blinked. He remembered yesterday, when he pulled a drawer too hard and made a loud bang. He had felt bad then. He knew Rocco felt bad now.

“It’s okay to make mistakes,” Lio said. “We can make amends. That means we try to fix what we did.”

Rocco’s ears perked up. “Make amends?”

“Yes,” Lio said gently. “We fix it and we say sorry. We try to be careful next time.”

Rocco nodded. “I’m sorry, truck.” He gave a small laugh. “And I’m sorry, Mr. Badger.”

Mr. Badger smiled. “Thank you, Rocco. Let’s fix this together.”

They looked at the truck. A little pin had popped out. Lio held a tiny magnet. Click! The pin stuck to it. He felt proud and careful. Mr. Badger showed Lio where the pin went. “Here, steady now.”

Lio’s paws were soft and slow. Tap. The pin slipped in. Rocco held the truck steady, his mask-striped face calm. They took turns. They breathed in and out. In, out. The garage was quiet except for a gentle tap and the low hum of a small fan.

“Try it,” Lio said at last.

Rocco rolled the truck across the floor. Rrrrroll. It moved smooth and straight. Rocco grinned. “It works! Thank you, Lio. Thank you, Mr. Badger.”

Lio beamed. Then a squeak came from the corner. Pippa the mouse peeked out, holding a tiny bell. “I dropped my bell,” she whispered. “It makes no ring.”

Lio knelt down so his eyes were kind. “We will help,” he said. “But first, Rocco, will you help Tika load her wagon?”

Rocco nodded. “Yes.” He picked up soft blocks and placed them in Tika’s wagon. “I want to be helpful, too.”

Lio looked at Pippa’s bell. A bit of dust hid inside. He blew gently. Pffff. He wiped it with the lemon cloth. Then he rang it. Ting-ting! Pippa’s whiskers wiggled with joy.

“Thank you,” Pippa said.

Lio felt his heart glow. He had been brave. He had listened. He had been fair. He had helped others. He had made amends with calm hands and kind words.

Mr. Badger patted Lio’s shoulder. “What did you learn today, brave cub?”

Lio thought. He looked at Tika, Rocco, and Pippa, all smiling. “Compassion,” he said. “Compassion means I care how others feel. I listen. I am fair. If I make a mistake, I say sorry and fix it. We can help each other.”

Everyone cheered. The garage smelled like lemon and hope. The tools shone in the light. Lio’s tail swished happily.

“Let’s clean up together,” Lio said.

They swept, they stacked, they put the tools back with tiny clicks. The workshop looked neat and calm. Lio waved goodbye to his friends, his brave heart full and gentle.

The End.
