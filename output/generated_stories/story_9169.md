# Joey's Big Feelings Adventure

In a cozy study corner filled with colorful books and soft cushions, there lived a little kangaroo joey named Bounce. Bounce loved to hop around the reading nook, his fluffy tail swishing as he explored picture books with his friends.

One sunny morning, Bounce was excited to read his favorite book about space rockets. He hopped over to the book shelf, his little paws reaching up high. But when he got there, he saw his friend Ollie the owl already reading it.

"That's MY book!" Bounce said loudly, his ears drooping down. His chest felt hot and tight, like a balloon filling up with air.

"I was here first," hooted Ollie gently, adjusting his tiny glasses. "Would you like to read it together?"

But Bounce felt his anger growing bigger and bigger. His face turned red under his soft brown fur. "NO! I want it NOW!" he shouted, stamping his big feet on the soft carpet. THUMP, THUMP, THUMP!

The study corner suddenly felt very quiet. Bounce's friend Zara the zebra looked up from her puzzle, and Sam the slow turtle peeked out from behind a cushion. Everyone looked worried.

Bounce felt embarrassed. His tummy felt all twisty, and his eyes started to water. "I... I don't know why I got so mad," he whispered.

Mrs. Wise, the kind librarian bunny, hopped over with a gentle smile. "Oh, Bounce," she said softly, "big feelings are normal. Everyone gets angry sometimes."

"Even you?" asked Bounce, sniffling.

"Even me," Mrs. Wise nodded. "But I've learned some special tricks. Would you like to try them?"

Bounce nodded eagerly, wiping his nose with his paw.

"First, let's take three deep breaths together," Mrs. Wise said. "Breathe in through your nose... smell the lavender flowers by the window. Now breathe out through your mouth... like you're blowing bubbles."

Bounce breathed in deeply. The lavender smelled like his mama's fur after bath time. He breathed out slowly, imagining purple bubbles floating away. His chest started to feel lighter.

"Good job!" said Mrs. Wise. "Now, let's count to five while we hop in place. One hop, two hops, three hops, four hops, five hops!"

Bounce giggled as he hopped. His anger was melting away like ice cream in the sunshine.

"Feeling better?" asked Mrs. Wise.

Bounce nodded and looked around at his friends. Ollie was still holding the space book, but now he was smiling. Zara had saved a puzzle piece for Bounce to place. Sam was slowly making his way over with a different book about rockets.

"I'm sorry I yelled," Bounce said to Ollie. "Can we really read together?"

"Of course!" hooted Ollie happily. "I love sharing stories!"

Soon, all the friends were snuggled together in the study corner. Ollie read about rockets, Zara showed everyone her finished puzzle of different animals, and Sam shared fascinating facts about stars. Bounce realized that sharing made everything more fun.

"Thank you for teaching me about big feelings," Bounce told Mrs. Wise as they cleaned up.

"Remember," she said with a warm smile, "when anger comes visiting, just breathe deep, count and hop, then think of ways to solve the problem together."

From that day on, whenever Bounce felt angry, he remembered his special tricks. And the study corner remained a happy place where all different kinds of friends could learn and play together.

The End.
