# Tick-Tock, Robo-Pup!

In a warm, comfortable nursery, a helpful robot dog named Robo-Pup wagged his shiny tail. The nursery smelled like clean blankets and orange soap. Sunlight made little squares on the soft rug. Toys sat in neat baskets. A big round clock went tick-tock on the wall.

“Good morning!” beeped Robo-Pup in a friendly voice. His ears glowed blue. “I love mornings! What is our plan?”

Robo-Pup had a plan board with pictures. There was a picture of a toothbrush, a bowl, a book, a hat, and a ball. Under each picture was a tiny clock.

Robo-Pup pointed with his paw. “First, brush teeth. Then breakfast. Then books. Then outdoor play. Then tidy up.” He smiled. “We can do it!”

Baby Mia sat on her cushion and clapped. “Play now?” she asked.

Robo-Pup’s tail lights blinked. “We will play outside soon,” he said gently. “First things first. We can practice self-regulation. That means we calm our bodies and follow our plan.”

He tapped his chest. “I can help. I use my timer and my breath.”

“Breath?” Mia asked.

Robo-Pup nodded. “Smell the flower, blow the pinwheel!” He held up a soft felt flower and a tiny paper pinwheel. “Ready?”

They both took a slow breath in. Sniff, sniff. Then a slow breath out. Ffffff. The pinwheel spun. Mia giggled.

“Great job,” said Robo-Pup. “Now, teeth time.”

They walked to the little sink. The water went trickle-trickle. Robo-Pup hummed a gentle tune. “Two minutes,” he said, tapping his timer. “Tick-tock.” Mia brushed. Bubbles of mint smelled fresh. The timer chimed. Ding!

“Breakfast time!” Robo-Pup cheered. The oatmeal was warm and cinnamon-sweet. The spoon made a tiny clink, clink. Mia wanted to hurry. She bounced in her chair.

Robo-Pup put a soft paw on the table. “Let’s show self-regulation. Sit still. Scoop, bite, sip.” He spoke slowly and smiled. “When we slow down, we feel good.”

Mia copied him. Scoop, bite, sip. She took a breath. Ffffff. “All done,” she said with a proud grin.

“Book time,” Robo-Pup said. He rolled to the reading nook. The pillows were squishy. The blanket was fuzzy. They read a short book about a kite. The pages whispered as they turned. Whoosh went the pretend wind.

Mia looked at the window. The sky was bright blue. “Outside now?” she asked.

Robo-Pup checked the plan board and the clock. “Yes! Outdoor play for twenty minutes.” His ears blinked green. “We can practice time-management. That means we make a plan for our time.”

He showed Mia a picture card. “Five minutes for hat and shoes. Ten minutes for ball and run. Five minutes to come back in.”

“Okay!” Mia nodded.

Robo-Pup helped her put on a sun hat. The hat felt soft. Shoes went tap-tap on the floor. Outside, the air smelled like grass. Birds chirped, peep-peep. The ball was bouncy and blue.

“Let’s run to the tree and back,” said Robo-Pup. “Ready, set—go!”

They ran. The ground felt springy under feet and paws. Mia laughed. The ball boinged. Robo-Pup beeped, “You are fast!”

Then Mia saw a puddle. It was shiny like a mirror. She wanted to splash, but the timer chimed. Ding! Ten minutes were almost done.

Mia pouted. “Splash now!”

Robo-Pup paused. He took a breath. “Smell the flower,” he said softly. Mia sniffed. “Blow the pinwheel.” Ffffff. She calmed a little.

Robo-Pup knelt so his eyes met hers. “Let’s use resourcefulness and compromise. We cannot splash a lot right now. But we can do three quiet splashes. Then we go in. Tomorrow we can plan more splash time.”

Mia held up three fingers. “Three?”

“Three,” Robo-Pup agreed, holding up his paw.

Mia made one gentle splash. Plip. Two gentle splashes. Plip. Plip. Three gentle splashes. Plip-plop. She smiled. “All done.”

“Wonderful self-regulation,” Robo-Pup said. “You waited. You breathed. You made a good choice.”

They walked back in. Shoes off. Hat off. The room felt cool and cozy. The clock went tick-tock.

“Last job: tidy up,” Robo-Pup said. “We can sing our tidy song.”

They sang, “Pick up, put in, clap and grin!” Toys went thump, thump into baskets. The rug looked neat again. Mia clapped. “We did it!”

Robo-Pup gave a happy beep. “Yes! We followed our plan. We took turns. We used our time well. We calmed our bodies when we felt big feelings.”

Mia hugged Robo-Pup. His metal felt warm from the sun. “I can do it,” she whispered.

Robo-Pup wagged. “You can. When you feel wiggly, breathe in and out. When you want to do something now, check the plan. You can compromise and choose a small way to play. That is self-regulation.”

He pointed to tomorrow’s picture cards. “Tomorrow, we will plan longer puddle time.”

Mia smiled a sleepy smile. The nursery smelled like soap and sunshine. The clock’s tick-tock was soft. Robo-Pup turned on a gentle lullaby, and blue stars twinkled on the ceiling.

“Today was a good day,” Robo-Pup said.

“A good day,” Mia agreed.

They closed their eyes, calm and proud, ready for another little adventure.

The End.
