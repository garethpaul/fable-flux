# Fawn’s Rooftop Rescue

A gentle deer fawn named Dottie tiptoed up the stairs to the sunny rooftop garden. Her small hooves went clip-clop on the steps. When she pushed open the door, warm air hugged her face. The rooftop smelled like mint leaves, wet soil, and sweet flowers. Bees buzzed. Flags fluttered. The city below hummed softly, like a big purring cat.

Dottie’s friend, a bright bluebird named Pip, landed on the edge of a big planter. “Good morning, Dottie! Tweet-tweet!” Pip chirped. “Today is Clean-Up Day!”

Dottie smiled. “I love Clean-Up Day,” she said in her soft voice. “We can make the rooftop sparkle.”

They looked around. Oh dear! The rooftop was messy. A breeze had tossed wrappers, empty bottles, and crinkly cans all over the garden paths. A cardboard box leaned against a pot of basil. A paper cup rolled in a circle, bumping Dottie’s hoof.

Dottie’s ears drooped. “This is a lot,” she whispered, feeling a little worried. “We wanted to finish before the afternoon sun gets too hot. I don’t know where to start.”

Pip tilted his head. “Let’s make a plan,” he sang. “When we make a plan, we save time!”

Dottie took a deep breath. She loved making plans. It helped her feel calm inside. “Okay,” she said. “We will sort, then we will share, then we will celebrate.”

“Sort, share, celebrate!” Pip echoed, flapping his wings. “That sounds smart.”

Dottie found three big bins by the door. She read the simple labels: Recycle, Compost, Trash. She tapped each one with her hoof. “Recycling means we turn old things into new things,” she explained. “We keep good materials out of the trash. That helps our Earth.”

Pip puffed up with pride. “I’ll help!” he chirped.

They started to sort. Dottie picked up a shiny can. It felt cool and smooth. “Aluminum can,” she said, smiling. “This goes in Recycle.” Clink! The can dropped into the blue bin.

Pip nudged an apple core with his beak. “This is food. It can be compost,” he said, pushing it into the green bin. Squish! The apple core slid in.

Dottie found a leaky paper cup with a plastic lid. She pulled the lid off. “Plastic lid goes to Recycle,” she said. “The soggy paper cup goes to Compost.” Pip cheered. “We are learning about materials! We are helping the Earth!”

They worked and sang a little sorting song:
“Sort and drop,
Clink and plop,
We help the Earth,
We never stop!”

Soon the sun climbed higher. The air grew warmer. Dottie felt a bead of sweat on her nose. She looked at the clock on the rooftop wall. “We have one hour until lunch,” she said. “Let’s use our time wisely.”

Pip nodded. “How can we do that?”

Dottie looked around. “We will clean in circles,” she said. “First the far corner, then the middle path, then the garden chairs.” She drew three small circles in a patch of soil with her hoof. “We follow the circles. We don’t run back and forth.”

“That saves time!” Pip said. “And it saves energy.”

They followed the plan. Dottie scooped paper into the bin. Pip carried little bits of foil. They took small breaks to sip cool water. They smiled at each other and kept going. The rooftop began to look tidy and bright.

Then Dottie heard a soft meow. She turned and saw a little kitten peeking from behind the basil pot. The kitten’s whiskers wiggled. Her paws were dusty. Next to the kitten was a stack of empty cardboard tubes and a pile of yarn.

The kitten meowed again. “I wanted to make toys for the shelter kittens,” she said shyly. “But I can’t finish before lunchtime.”

Dottie’s heart felt warm. She liked helping others. “We can share our time,” she said gently. “Sharing time is generosity.”

Pip hopped closer. “We can use recycled things,” he chirped. “Recycling and helping at the same time!”

Dottie checked the clock. “We have thirty minutes,” she said. “If we work together and keep sorting, we can help the kittens and finish the cleanup.”

They made a new plan:
- Ten minutes to sort the last cans and bottles
- Ten minutes to make toys with the tubes and yarn
- Ten minutes to put the bins away and sweep

“Ready?” Dottie asked.

“Ready!” Pip chirped. “Ready!” meowed the kitten.

They moved quickly and kindly. Clink went the bottles. Crunch went the cardboard. Then they rolled the tubes in yarn, tied little knots, and added a jingle bell from a broken necklace they found in the Recycle bin. The toys were soft and safe.

The kitten batted one. “This is perfect,” she purred. “Thank you for sharing your time.”

Dottie felt proud but calm. “We used time wisely,” she said. “We recycled to help the Earth. And we shared to help friends.”

Pip looked around the rooftop. The paths were clear. The bins were sorted. The plants swayed happily in a gentle breeze.

Dottie rang a tiny bell. “Clean-Up Day is complete!” she announced. “Let’s celebrate with a cool drink and crunchy carrot sticks.”

They sat in the shade. The rooftop smelled like mint again. The city hummed softly. Dottie smiled at her friends. “Today I learned,” she said, “that recycling turns old things into new things, time plans help us finish our work, and sharing makes hearts feel full.”

Pip chirped, “Sort, share, celebrate!”

The kitten purred, “Purr-fect.”

And the rooftop garden sparkled in the sunshine.

The End.
