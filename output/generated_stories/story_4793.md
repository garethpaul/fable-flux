# Ellie’s Words Parade

In a playful living room full of soft rugs and bright pillows, a gentle elephant calf named Ellie wiggled her round toes. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. The air smelled like clean soap and crayons. Toys rested in baskets, and a big box held old magazines and cardboard tubes to recycle later.

Ellie loved sounds. She loved the hush of the curtains, the squeak of a rubber duck, and the tap-tap of tiny feet. Today, her friends had come over to play. There was Benji the bunny, Lila the ladybug, and Max the mouse.

“Let’s make a parade!” Benji shouted, thumping his feet.

“A quiet parade?” Lila asked, her tiny wings humming.

“A kind parade,” Ellie said, her voice gentle. “And a words parade too.”

“Words?” Max squeaked, twitching his whiskers. “Do words march?”

Ellie smiled and nodded. “Words can dance in our mouths. Words can help. Words can be friends.”

They all liked that. They picked up empty cardboard tubes from the recycling box and pretended they were trumpets. Ellie sniffed the cardboard. It smelled like paper and a little bit like cereal.

“Toot!” Benji blew. “Toot-toot!”

“Buzz-buzz,” Lila hummed.

“Peep peep!” Max squeaked.

Ellie raised her trunk like a leader. “Let’s choose happy words,” she said. “Words that make hearts feel good.”

They began to march around the living room. The rug felt soft under Ellie’s feet. The pillow mountain looked cozy and inviting.

“Kind!” Ellie said clearly.

“Share!” Benji thumped.

“Please!” Lila sang.

“Thank you!” Max squeaked.

Their words floated up like bubbles. Ellie felt warm pride in her belly. She loved how the words sounded. She loved how they made her friends smile.

On the coffee table, a stack of old magazines wobbled. “Careful,” Ellie said. “We can recycle these later.”

“Recycle!” Lila echoed, happy to try a big word. “Re-cy-cle!”

Just then, a small voice came from the doorway. It was Poppy the puppy. Her ears drooped. “I want the shiny drum!” she barked. She grabbed a metal bowl and a wooden spoon.

“May I have a turn, please?” Max asked, looking up.

“No! Mine!” Poppy barked again, louder this time. Bang, bang! The bowl rang like a bell. Max covered his ears.

Ellie’s tummy felt tight. The room did not sound kind now. Benji’s whiskers trembled, and Lila folded her tiny wings.

Ellie took a breath. She remembered words can help. She stepped forward, slow and steady. “Poppy,” Ellie said softly, “we are having a words parade. Our words are kind. Our words are fair.”

“Fair?” Poppy blinked.

“Fair means justice,” Ellie said carefully. “Justice means we all get turns. We use words to make it right.”

Poppy looked down. “But I like the drum.”

“I like it too,” Max said. “We can make a plan.”

Ellie nodded. “I can be the leader and help. We can make a turn chart.” She picked up a cardboard tube and flattened it into a soft card. She took a crayon. “We will write our names. See?”

She wrote big, simple letters. “E-L-L-I-E.” The crayon smelled waxy. “Writing is a way to show words.”

Benji bounced. “B-E-N-J-I!”

Lila buzzed. “L-I-L-A!”

Max squeaked. “M-A-X!”

Poppy wagged slowly. “P-O-P-P-Y?”

“Yes,” Ellie said, smiling. “Now we have turns. Each name gets one song with the drum. We will use words to ask and to thank.”

Poppy’s tail swished. “Okay,” she whispered. “I can try.”

They set the bowl drum on a pillow so it would not fall. Ellie lifted her trunk like a baton. “Ready? I will say the starting word.”

She breathed in and sang, “Begin!”

Max tapped gently. Tink, tink. “Thank you for my turn,” he said when he finished.

“Your turn, Poppy,” Ellie said. “Please play.”

Poppy tapped softly. Ting, ting. She smiled. “Your turn,” she told Benji. Her ears perked up. “Please.”

Benji thumped a happy beat. Lila followed with a hum like a little motor. The living room felt bright again. The words felt like sunshine.

Between songs, Ellie showed more word-magic. She picked up old magazine pages. “Look,” she said. “Pictures and letters!” She cut out big letters and glued them on a cardboard sign from the recycling box. “We can make a Words Parade banner.”

They all helped. The glue smelled sweet. The paper felt smooth. The banner read: “KIND WORDS, FAIR TURNS.”

Ellie held it up and said each word slowly. “Kind. Words. Fair. Turns.”

Poppy repeated, sounding proud. “Kind. Words. Fair. Turns.” She gave the drum to Max without being asked. “Thank you for waiting,” she added.

Max beamed. “Thank you for sharing.”

Ellie’s heart felt light. She saw that language could lead. Words could make justice. Words could guide friends like a gentle drumbeat.

“Parade time!” Ellie called. They marched around the couch, under the table tunnel, and past the window’s warm light. Their banner rustled. Their voices blended.

“Please, thank you, share, fair!” they chanted.

The parade ended at the recycling box. Ellie bowed. “We used our words. We led with kindness. We made it fair. And we recycled, too.”

Poppy wagged hard. “Words are strong,” she said. “They help us be friends.”

Ellie smiled and wrapped her trunk around her friends in a soft hug. “Yes,” she said. “Words help us listen, lead, and love.”

The End.
