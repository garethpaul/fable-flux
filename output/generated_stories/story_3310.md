# Fern the Fawn Finds a Way

Fern was a gentle deer fawn with soft, speckled fur and big, listening ears. One rainy afternoon, Fern visited a busy basement where neighbors met to sort books and toys for a community give-away. The basement hummed with sounds—whirring fans, clinking boxes, and cheerful voices. It smelled like warm dust, crayons, and a little bit of cinnamon from someone’s tea.

Fern’s hooves clicked softly on the floor. “I’m here to help,” she said in a shy, sweet voice.

“Thank you, Fern,” said Mia, a kind volunteer. “We are sorting donations. Books go on this shelf. Toys go in these bins. We want everything neat so families can find what they need.”

Fern nodded. “I can do it!”

She trotted to a wobbly stack of puzzle boxes. The tower leaned like a sleepy tree. “Careful,” whispered Fern. She reached to move the top box when—plop!—the bottom one slid out. Pieces scattered like raindrops pattering on the floor.

“Oh no,” Fern gasped. Her heart thumped. Her cheeks felt hot. She stomped one tiny hoof. “I ruined it!”

Mia looked over. “It’s okay, Fern. Mistakes happen.”

But inside, Fern felt a little storm. “I’m mad at myself,” she said. Her ears drooped. The busy basement seemed louder now—the clacks, the chatter, the fan’s whoosh. It felt too much.

Mia knelt, speaking softly. “Let’s pause. Place a hoof on your chest and take belly breaths with me. In and out.”

Fern put her hoof over her thumping heart. “In,” she whispered. “Out.” She felt her belly rise and fall, like a small boat on gentle waves. The storm inside became a quiet drizzle.

“Better?” Mia asked.

“A little,” Fern said. Her eyes were wet, but her breathing was slow. “I want to fix it.”

“That’s resourceful,” Mia smiled. “Let’s look, think, and act.”

Fern repeated, “Look, think, act.”

First, Fern looked. She noticed puzzle pieces in bright colors across the floor. She noticed empty baskets, rubber bands, and sticky notes on a nearby table. She noticed tape, markers, and a big sign that said, “Thank you, Volunteers!”

Next, Fern thought. “What can help me sort fast and keep pieces safe?” she wondered. She remembered how squirrels use different holes to store nuts. “I can give each puzzle its own safe place!”

Now, act. Fern nudged three baskets close. “Basket for the farm puzzle. Basket for the rainbow puzzle. Basket for the animal shapes,” she said. She read little pictures on the box lids to match them. “Cow goes to the farm. Blue arc goes to the rainbow. Triangle ear goes to the animal shapes.”

“Smart sorting,” said Mia. “You’re using your eyes and your plan.”

Fern felt a warm glow in her chest. The fan’s whoosh sounded friendly now, like a soft song. “Click, clack,” went her hooves as she gathered pieces. “Swish,” went the broom as Mia helped sweep. They hummed together, “La, la, la.”

Soon, three neat piles sat in the baskets. “But the boxes are ripped,” Fern said. Her brow wrinkled. “The pieces might spill again.”

Fern looked, thought, and acted once more. She found zip-top bags in a drawer. “These will hold the pieces,” she said. She used markers to draw little pictures on sticky notes: a cow for the farm puzzle, a rainbow for the rainbow puzzle, and a fox for the animal shapes. She slid each note into a bag with the matching pieces and sealed it tight. “Zzzzip!”

Mia clapped softly. “You are being very resourceful. You used what we had and made a new system!”

Fern’s tail twitched with pride. “I can make a shelf label too,” she said. She wrote, with careful strokes, “Puzzles—All Pieces Inside” on a big sticky note and pressed it on the shelf. “Pat,” went her hoof.

Just then, a little boy named Theo came in with his dad. “I like puzzles,” Theo said, eyes wide.

Fern bent down. “These puzzles are ready,” she said. “All pieces are safe in bags. See the pictures?”

Theo smiled. “Thank you! That makes it easy.”

Fern’s heart fluttered like a happy leaf. The busy basement felt bright and helpful. Other volunteers noticed. “Great idea, Fern,” said one. “Your system can help with games, too,” said another.

Fern giggled. “We can do it together.” They started placing game pieces into bags, drawing tiny notes, and making clear labels. The room filled with calm, steady work—soft footsteps, gentle laughter, the sweet smell of cinnamon tea, and the rustle of sticky notes.

At the end, the shelves looked neat and welcoming. Fern took one more belly breath, in and out, just to feel peaceful.

Mia touched Fern’s shoulder. “Today you learned to calm your mad feelings with mindful breathing. You looked, thought, and acted to solve a problem. That is resourcefulness. And you helped others. That’s volunteering with heart.”

Fern beamed. “When things fall apart, I can look, think, and act,” she said. “I can breathe. I can try again.”

They turned off the fan. The basement grew quiet and cozy. Rain tapped the window like a soft goodbye.

Fern trotted toward the stairs. “See you next time,” she called. “I’ll bring my big ears and my brave breaths.”

“See you, Resourceful Fern!” everyone cheered.

The End.
