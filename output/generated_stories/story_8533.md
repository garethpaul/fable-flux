# Panda Poppy in the Busy Basement

Poppy the panda was patient and gentle. One rainy day, Poppy padded down the wooden steps into a busy basement. The basement hummed and buzzed. Drip, drip went the pipes. Whirr went the washer. Click-clack went tiny toy blocks. The air smelled like soap, dust, and a little bit like warm toast.

“Hello, basement,” Poppy whispered. “I’m ready to make art.”

Poppy wanted to build a tall tower with boxes and make a big painting. But the basement was crowded. Boxes were stacked like little mountains. A fan spun slowly. Shelves held jars of buttons, old crayons, and paper. There were soft blankets in a basket and a little window with raindrops racing down the glass.

Poppy put on a bright blue smock. “I can do this,” she said.

But when Poppy reached for a paint brush, three brushes fell with a clatter. Clatter, clatter! The sound made Poppy’s ears twitch.

“Oh,” Poppy said softly. “That startled me.”

Poppy took a slow breath in. She let the breath out. “I feel a little jumpy. That’s okay.”

She tried again. This time, she set the brushes in a cup. “There,” she said, patting the cup. “Safe and still.”

Next, Poppy tried to find the biggest box for her tower. She tugged one box. Another box slid and boop—tapped her foot.

“Ouch,” Poppy said. “I feel upset.”

Poppy sat on a soft blanket for a moment. The washer went whirr, whirr. The fan hummed. Poppy put her paws on her belly. She took a breath in like a balloon. She let it out like a gentle breeze. “I am patient,” she told herself. “I can try a new way.”

Poppy looked around with curious eyes. She noticed a sign on a jar: “Old Buttons.” The buttons were shiny, bumpy, smooth, and colorful. She noticed a box of paper scraps. Some pieces were shaped like clouds. Some were shaped like leaves.

“I can make art with what I find,” Poppy said. “I can make my tower slowly, safely.”

Poppy made a plan. “One thing at a time,” she whispered. “First, I clear a space.”

She slid the boxes a little, one by one, using both paws. Scoot, scoot. She stacked light boxes on a shelf. She made a tiny path on the floor. “This is my art spot,” she said with a smile.

Poppy found a small tray and set it near the window. Raindrops tapped the glass. Tap-tap-tap. The sound was soft and steady. It made her feel calm.

She painted a circle with yellow. “This looks like the sun,” Poppy said.

She painted a blue swish. “This looks like the rain.”

Then she had an idea. “I can use the buttons to make eyes!” she giggled. She glued two shiny buttons on her painting. “Hello, rainy sun.”

When glue dripped on her paw, Poppy frowned. “Sticky,” she said. “I feel a little yucky.”

Poppy got a warm cloth from a basket. She wiped her paw. “There,” she said kindly to herself. “All clean.”

She felt proud. She felt calm. She felt ready.

Now Poppy built her box tower. She picked a strong box for the bottom. Thump. She pressed it gently. She chose a lighter box next. Plop. She checked with both paws. “Stable,” she said.

A small box wobbled. Wobble, wobble. Poppy’s heart went thump-thump. She wanted to rush. She wanted to say, “Oh no!”

Instead, she placed her paw on her chest. “Hello, feelings,” she said softly. “I see you. I hear you. Let’s slow down.” She took a breath in. She let it out. She added a book as a flat base. The wobble stopped.

“I did it,” Poppy whispered.

Poppy looked at the jar of buttons again. She had another idea. “I can make a garden from things we already have,” she said. She used green paper scraps for stems. She used purple buttons for flowers. She used a cardboard roll as a tree trunk. She drew leaves with a stubby crayon.

“This is good for the Earth,” Poppy said. “I am reusing things.” She felt warm and happy inside.

Suddenly, the fan squeaked. Squeak! Poppy blinked. “That sound is sharp,” she said. “I feel bothered.” She clicked the fan to a slower speed. The squeak stopped. The hum was soft again.

Poppy smiled at the busy basement. It felt friendly now. The washer still went whirr. The raindrops still went tap-tap. The buttons clicked softly as she moved them. Click, click. Poppy swayed her tail.

Footsteps came down the stairs. It was Poppy’s friend, Milo the mouse. “Wow,” Milo squeaked. “You made a tower and a garden!”

Poppy nodded. “I felt jumpy and upset sometimes,” she said. “But I took breaths. I talked to my feelings. I tried new ways. I used old things to make new art.”

Milo touched a purple button. “It’s beautiful,” he said. “I like how you listened to yourself.”

Poppy felt proud and peaceful. “Thank you, Milo. When it is busy, I can be patient. When I feel big feelings, I can name them. I can breathe. I can make a plan. I can use what I have and take care of the Earth.”

Milo smiled. “Can we make a tiny tower for me?” he asked.

“Yes,” Poppy said. “We can do it together. One step at a time.”

They built a tiny tower with tiny boxes. Plip, plop, stack. They laughed. They listened to the soft basement sounds. The busy place felt safe and calm because Poppy was patient and kind to her feelings.

Lesson: It helps to name our feelings, take slow breaths, and try one step at a time. We can be creative, discover new ideas, and care for the Earth by reusing things. And we can accept ourselves and others, just the way we are.

The End.
