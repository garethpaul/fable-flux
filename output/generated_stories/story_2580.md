# Bubbles in the Garden Shed

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to make friends. One sunny day, a big rain cloud came. “Splash-splash!” went the rain. The tide rose up, up, up, and carried Bubbles along a little stream. The stream curved past a house and bumped gently against a small, wooden garden shed.

“Click, creak,” went the shed door as the wind pushed it open. Bubbles slid inside with a soft flop. “Oh!” Bubbles chirped, wiggling his tail. “This is not the sea!”

The garden shed smelled like fresh soil and lemony soap. It was cozy and dim, with strips of sunlight peeking through tiny windows. On the shelves were shiny tools, colorful pots, a red watering can, and a blue bucket with seashell stickers. The floor felt cool and a little rough under Bubbles’s smooth skin.

“Hello?” called Bubbles. His voice echoed, “Hello-hello!”

No answer came. Bubbles looked around and felt a bubble of worry. “How do I get back to the water?” he wondered. The stream was close, but the ground was dry now. The rain had stopped, and the door had swung shut. “Click.”

Bubbles took a deep breath. “I can be resourceful,” he said. “I can use what I have!”

He peeked at the shelves. He saw string, a soft towel, a tiny fan, a box of seeds, the red watering can, and the blue bucket. He heard a quiet “rustle-rustle.” In the corner, a little mouse peeked out. The mouse wore a small bandana with pretty patterns.

“Hola,” squeaked the mouse, waving. “Hello!”

Bubbles smiled. “Hello! I am Bubbles. I need help to get back to the water.”

The mouse nodded. “Me llamo Luna,” she said. “That means, my name is Luna.”

Bubbles tried the words. “Meh yah-moh Luna!” He giggled. “I like your bandana. It is beautiful.”

“Gracias,” said Luna. “That means thank you.” She pointed to the red watering can and the blue bucket. “We can help you. Use water!”

Bubbles clapped his fins. “Yes! Water will make me slippery. Water will help me slide!”

They worked together. Luna tugged the watering can to the sink in the corner. Drip-drip, water filled the can. Bubbles lifted the towel with his nose and laid it on the floor by the door. “Soft and smooth,” he said. “Good for sliding.”

“Muy bien,” said Luna. “That means very good!”

Bubbles repeated, “Moo-ee bee-en!” He grinned. “I am learning new words.”

Luna pushed the blue bucket closer. “We will pour water on the towel. Then you slide, and I will open the door.”

Bubbles nodded. “We have a plan!” He felt brave. He felt calm. He felt proud to use his brain.

Luna counted, “Uno, dos, tres!” She poured. Splash! The water splashed on the towel. The shed smelled fresh and cool. Bubbles wiggled. “Ready!”

But the door was stuck. “Creak… stuck,” said Luna, pulling. “Hmm.”

Bubbles looked again. He spotted the string and the tiny fan. “We can be more resourceful,” he said. “We can make a gentle push.”

He showed Luna how to tie the string to the door handle. Luna tied neat little knots. “Nudos,” she said. “Knots.”

Bubbles nudged the tiny fan toward the door. “We can use the breeze to help a little,” he said. “The fan blows. You pull the string. I slide!”

Luna smiled. “Sí!”

Luna pulled the string. The fan hummed, “Whirr-rrr,” and sent a soft breeze to the door. The breeze made the door wiggle. “Click!” The latch popped. The door opened a crack. Sunlight streamed in, warm and golden.

“Go, Bubbles!” cheered Luna.

Bubbles took a deep breath. “Here I go!” He flopped onto the wet towel and slid. “Wheee!” The towel felt cool and swishy. The water sparkled like little stars. He slid through the door and onto the soft, damp grass. The grass tickled his belly. He giggled.

Luna hurried out, too. “This way!” She pointed with her tiny paw. They could hear “glug-glug” from the stream nearby.

Bubbles and Luna worked together once more. Luna poured the last of the water on the grass to make it slippery. Bubbles wiggled and slid, wiggled and slid, all the way to the stream.

“Splash!” Bubbles slipped into the water. It felt like home—cool, bubbly, and blue. He twirled his tail. “Thank you, Luna!” he cheered.

“De nada,” said Luna. “You are welcome.” She waved. “You were very resourceful.”

Bubbles nodded. “We used our eyes. We used our minds. We used what we had. We listened. We learned new words, too. Hola means hello. Gracias means thank you. Nudos are knots. I like learning.”

Luna smiled. “Different words, same friends.”

Bubbles made happy clicks. “Today I learned to be resourceful. I used simple things to solve a problem. I also learned new words from your culture. That helps me understand and care.”

He splashed a friendly wave. “I will remember: Look, think, use what you have, and ask kind friends for help.” Bubbles blinked in the sunlight. “And say thank you. Gracias!”

“Adiós,” said Luna, waving.

“Good-bye!” sang Bubbles, and he swam off, feeling brave, smart, and kind.

The End.
