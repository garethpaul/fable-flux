# Tully the Turtle Finds a Team

In a cozy kitchen pantry, jars stood in neat rows. Spices smelled warm, like cinnamon and pepper. Bags of flour felt soft like clouds. A small light glowed. In the quiet, a shy turtle named Tully tucked inside his smooth, green shell.

Tully liked the pantry. It was calm and cool. He could hear the hum of the fridge and the soft tap, tap of a leaky sink. He sang tiny songs to himself. “La, la, la,” he whispered. His voice was small but sweet, like a bell.

One day, the family put a basket in the pantry. Inside the basket were a little drum, two wooden spoons, a shiny whistle, and a soft red ball.

Tully peeked out. His big eyes blinked. “Oh,” he said softly. “New things.”

The door swung open. In came Pip the mouse, quick and cheerful. “Hi, Tully!” squeaked Pip. “We’re making a pantry parade! Music and sports fun! Will you join?”

Tully felt his chest flutter. He pulled his head in. “I’m shy,” he whispered from inside his shell. “What if I am too slow? What if I make a mistake?”

Pip sat beside him and listened. “It’s okay to be shy,” Pip said kindly. “We accept you just as you are. You can go slow. You can try a small part.”

The door creaked again. Bina the beetle clacked her tiny feet. “I made a plan,” she buzzed, holding a crinkly paper. “We will prepare! First, warm-up. Then practice music. Then gentle sports. Step by step.”

Tully peeked out more. He liked plans. Plans felt safe. “What is a warm-up?” he asked.

Bina smiled. “Warm-up is to get ready. We stretch and breathe.” She showed: in and out, in and out. Her shiny shell gleamed in the pantry light.

Pip tapped the wooden spoons. “Tap, tap, tap!” The sound was bright and happy. The pantry seemed to giggle.

Tully listened. He felt the beat in his feet. He took a small step out. “I can try,” he said.

They began to prepare. They stretched. Tully rolled his shoulders. He wiggled his toes. He breathed in the cinnamon smell and out the pepper tickle. “In… out… I can do this,” he told himself.

Pip gave Tully the little drum. “You can keep the beat,” Pip said. “You choose the pace.”

Tully’s hands trembled. “Tap… tap.” The drum sounded soft and round, like gentle rain on a tin roof. He tried again. “Tap-tap, tap.” His heart felt steady.

Bina blew the shiny whistle. “Toot!” It was bright like sunshine. Pip banged the spoons in time. The pantry was full of friendly sound.

“Now ball time!” said Bina. She rolled the soft red ball. It made a hush-hush sound over the floor. “We roll to each other. Slow and careful. Ready?”

Tully pulled back a little. “What if it bumps me?” he asked.

Pip nodded. “We will go slow,” he said. “You can say stop anytime. We listen and accept.”

Tully felt brave. “Okay. I will try,” he said. He placed his shell steady and held out his hands.

Bina rolled the ball very gently. The ball kissed Tully’s fingers. “Oh!” Tully giggled. “It is soft.”

He rolled it back, slow as a sleepy snail. Pip caught it and cheered. “You did it!”

They practiced more. Tap-tap on the drum. Toot on the whistle. Roll, roll, roll of the red ball. Tully felt the rhythm. He made a little song. “Tap, roll, tap-tap, roll.” He smiled. His smile felt warm, like fresh bread.

Soon, the pantry door opened again. A tiny breeze brought smells of oranges and soap. Mimi the cat peeked in. “What’s all this?” she purred.

Pip stood tall. “It’s our pantry parade! Music and sports. Everyone is welcome.”

Mimi’s tail swished. “May I join?” she asked.

Tully looked at his friends. He took initiative. He spoke up. “Yes,” he said clearly. “We accept you. We go slow. We prepare first.”

Mimi stretched with them. She breathed in and out. Tully showed her the plan. He tapped the drum to start. Tap… tap… tap. “Ready,” he said.

They began. Bina tooted the whistle: toot! Pip clacked the spoons: clack! Mimi rolled the red ball with a soft paw. Tully kept the beat. The pantry hummed with happy sound. The jars seemed to dance. The flour bag puffed a tiny cloud—poof! It smelled warm and tickly. Tully laughed, and the laugh rang like a bell.

Then, oops! The red ball rolled too fast. It bumped Tully’s shell. “Thump!”

Tully froze. He felt shy again. He wanted to hide.

Pip put a gentle paw on the ball. “Are you okay?” he asked. “We can slow down. We accept your pace.”

Tully breathed in, then out. The drum was still in his hands. He tapped once, soft. “Tap.” He looked up. “I’m okay,” he said. “Let’s try again. Slow.”

They tried again. Slow and steady. Tap-tap, roll, toot. Tully’s beat led the way. He felt proud. He felt safe. He felt accepted.

When they finished, everyone cheered. Mimi purred. Bina buzzed. Pip squeaked. Tully’s cheeks felt warm.

“You did it, Tully!” said Bina. “Your plan and your beat helped us all.”

Tully nodded. “We prepared. We went slow. We listened. We accepted each other.” He looked at his friends. “Thank you for accepting me. I will accept me, too.”

He hugged the drum. The pantry felt like home, full of soft sounds and kind hearts.

The lesson: We accept friends and ourselves, just as we are. With preparation and small steps, we can try new things. And when we take initiative to speak up and make a plan, everyone can have fun together.

The End.
