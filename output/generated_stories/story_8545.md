# Koala Kori’s Basement Busy-Day Plan

Kori the koala lived with her family above a busy basement. The basement hummed with noises. Drip, drip went a leaky tap. Whirr went a little fan. Click-clack went a wobbly shelf. Kori’s soft gray ears twitched. She was a calm koala who liked quiet and clean spaces.

One sunny morning, Mama said, “Kori, can you help me tidy the basement? We have tasks to do.”

Kori took a deep breath. “Yes, Mama. I can do my tasks, one by one.”

They went downstairs. The basement smelled like old boxes and fresh soap. Dust sparkled in the light. There were toys, books, and bins all around.

Mama pointed to a big list on a yellow paper. “Here is our plan,” Mama said. “Number one: turn off the dripping tap. Number two: sort toys into bins. Number three: stack books to give and to keep. Number four: sweep, sweep, sweep!”

Kori smiled. “I can do it. I will finish my tasks.”

But then Kori heard a loud crash! A tower of blocks fell down. Clatter-clatter! “Oh no,” Kori whispered. Her tummy felt tight. Her paws felt hot.

Kori remembered her calm breath. “Smell the flower,” she told herself, sniffing in. “Blow the candle,” she said, puffing out. In, out. In, out. The tight feeling got smaller.

Mama knelt next to her. “How do you feel, Kori?”

“I felt mad for a moment,” Kori said softly. “But I used my breath. I want to try again.”

“That is wise,” Mama said. “Let’s do task number one.”

Kori went to the tap. Drip. Drip. She gently turned the handle. The drip stopped. Plink! Silence. Kori grinned. “No more water wasted,” she said. “We are saving water for the trees and the fish!”

Mama clapped. “Conservation helps everyone.”

Task number two was toys. There were soft bears, rolling cars, and puzzle pieces. Kori made three bins: Keep, Share, and Fix.

She picked up a tiny red car. “Vroom!” It still rolled. Into Keep it went. She found a puzzle with missing ears. “Hmm,” she said. “This needs help.” Into Fix it went. She held a doll she no longer played with. “Another child can love you,” Kori told the doll. Into Share it went.

Suddenly, Kori saw a tangled string of beads. It caught on her paw. “Ouch!” She frowned. Her face grew hot again. She wanted to toss it.

She stopped and touched her belly. “Smell the flower.” In. “Blow the candle.” Out. She looked at the beads. “I feel frustrated,” she said. “Can I ask for help?”

Mama nodded. “Asking for help is a smart step.”

Together they untangled the beads slowly. Kori’s paws moved carefully, loop by loop. “I can do hard things, a little at a time,” she whispered. The beads lay smooth and neat. Kori smiled big.

Task number three was books. Kori stacked “Koala Songs” and “Counting Leaves” to keep. She stacked “Little Boats” and “Sharing is Caring” to give. The books smelled like paper and sunshine. She patted each stack. “These will make new friends happy,” she said. “Sharing is kind.”

Then the fan went whirr-whirr-whirr, too fast. The sound made Kori’s ears twitch. She wanted to shout, “Stop!” but she remembered her plan.

She placed her paws on her cheeks. “Name it to tame it,” she said. “I feel annoyed.” In. Out. Calm returned like a soft blanket.

She walked to the fan switch. Click. The fan hummed low. “Ah,” Kori sighed. “Gentle sound.”

Mama smiled. “You are finishing each task. What comes next?”

“Task number four,” Kori said. “Sweep, sweep, sweep!”

Kori held a little broom. Swish, swish. The dust danced. She swept crumbs into a pan. She found a shiny button. “Treasure!” she giggled. She put the button into the Fix bin for later sewing.

At last, Kori looked around. The basement felt different. It smelled fresh. The shelves were steady. The tap was quiet. The toys and books were in neat bins with little paper labels. KEEP. SHARE. FIX.

Kori held up the yellow list. “Check, check, check, check!” she sang, tapping each box with her finger. “I did my tasks. I finished them one by one.”

Mama gave Kori a gentle hug. “How did you do it, sweet koala?”

Kori thought. “I made a plan. I took calm breaths when I felt mad. I asked for help. I did one task at a time. And I saved water and shared things.”

Mama nodded. “That is how we care for our home and our world.”

They carried the Share box to the door. Sunlight warmed Kori’s fur. A little bird chirped outside. “Bye-bye, toys and books,” Kori said. “Have fun in your new home!”

Kori stretched and yawned. “The busy basement is a calm basement now.”

Mama winked. “Just like you.”

Kori giggled. “When I feel big feelings, I can breathe. When I have many jobs, I can finish them step by step. When I care for things, I help the Earth.”

She patted the yellow list and placed it on the shelf. “Let’s make a new plan for tomorrow,” she said.

“Together,” Mama replied.

The End.
