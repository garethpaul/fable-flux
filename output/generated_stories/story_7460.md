# Miri Mouse and the Talking Time

In a sunny, playful living room, a kind mouse named Miri woke up under a cozy couch. The couch smelled like clean cotton. The rug felt soft and tickly under her tiny paws. Sunbeams made warm squares on the floor.

“Good morning, room!” Miri squeaked in a happy whisper. She was small, with gray fur and bright eyes. She liked to help, and she loved to talk things through.

Today, Miri had a plan. “I will make a toy town!” she said. She pulled little blocks from a basket. Clink, clack, clunk. She set up a tiny bridge, a pretend bakery, and a little park made from green buttons.

Just then, a friendly breeze from the window fluttered the curtains. They swooshed like gentle waves. Miri looked up. “Oh! The window is open. Fresh air is nice,” she said, taking a deep sniff. It smelled like grass and sunshine.

Across the room, a fluffy cat pillow with tassel whiskers sat on the couch. It was just a pillow, but Miri liked to talk to it. “Hello, Captain Whisker Pillow,” Miri giggled. “Would you like to visit my toy town?”

She set the pillow near her bridge. The blocks wobbled a little. Miri felt a little flutter in her tummy. “Hmm,” she said softly. “I need to go slow. I can do it.”

Suddenly, Miri heard a rustle. Scratch-scratch. Her best friend, Pip the paper puppet, peeked from a book. Pip had a drawn-on smile and little folded arms. “Miri!” Pip said in his crackly paper voice. “I want to play too!”

Miri clapped her paws. “Yes, Pip! Let’s build together. But first, let’s share words. Let’s tell each other our ideas.”

Pip nodded. “My idea is a tall tower.”

“My idea is a small, safe bridge,” Miri said. “Let’s say our plan out loud. Then we know, and then we grow!”

They took turns. Miri spoke slowly. “We build the bridge first. Then, we add the tower next to it.”

Pip agreed. “We can do it. We can talk if we get stuck.”

They started building. Tap, tap, tap went the blocks. Miri’s whiskers twitched with joy. But then, a round red ball rolled in from under the chair. It bumped the tower. Tock!

The tower tumbled. Crash! Blocks scattered like little raindrops. Pip gasped. “Oh no!”

Miri put a paw on her chest. She took a big, slow breath. In… and out. “It’s okay,” she said. “We can fix it. Let’s talk.”

Pip still looked worried. “I wanted a tall tower,” he whispered.

Miri scooted closer. She kept her voice soft. “I hear you. You wanted tall. I wanted safe. Can we find a plan that feels good for both of us?”

Pip’s paper edges rustled. “Maybe the tower can be tall but strong,” he said. “We can use the big blocks at the bottom.”

Miri smiled wide. “Yes! Thank you for telling me your idea. Talking helps.”

They sorted the blocks. Clunk, clink. Miri picked up a cracked block. It was chipped on one side. “Hmm,” she said. “This is not trash yet. We can use it for the park bench.” She brushed it clean with a tiny cloth. “We save, we mend, we use again.”

Pip grinned. “That is good for the world.”

“And good for us,” Miri said. “We are careful. We are kind.”

A little squeak came from the toy basket. Squeak-squeak! It was a tiny wind-up mouse, stuck under a ribbon. Miri rushed over. “Oh dear,” she said. “You are trapped.”

Pip waved his paper arms. “I will help!”

They worked together. Miri held the ribbon. Pip tugged. The wind-up mouse popped free. Click! Its key spun. It wobbled on its little wheels and bumped Miri’s paw like a thank-you hug.

Miri laughed. “You’re welcome, little friend.” She stroked its smooth, cool back. “Helping feels warm inside.”

Miri looked at the window. The breeze was stronger now. The curtains flapped, flap-flap. The blocks shivered. “The wind is too wild,” she said. “We need to close the window a little to keep our town safe. But I also like fresh air.”

“Let’s say our plan,” Pip said.

Miri nodded. “We will close the window halfway. We keep some fresh air and keep our town safe.” She went to the window, pulled gently, and listened to the soft click. The breeze became a whisper. The room felt calm again.

Back at the town, they built a new tower with strong bottom blocks. They made a steady bridge. They used the cracked block as a little bench. They made a tiny garden with paper leaves from an old magazine. Snip-snip. “We reuse,” Miri said. “We do not waste.”

When they finished, the town looked bright and neat. The sun made their blocks glow. Miri felt proud. Pip did too.

“I have one more idea,” Miri said. She looked at the basket. There were blocks left. “Let’s share with the little wind-up mouse.”

They rolled some blocks to the toy’s corner. “These are for you,” Miri said. “You can make a tiny house.”

Pip clapped. “That is generous!”

The wind-up mouse clicked happily, like a tiny drum.

Miri and Pip sat on the soft rug. They sipped pretend tea from bottle-cap cups. Miri spoke gently. “Today we learned something big.”

Pip leaned in. “We learned to talk and listen.”

Miri nodded. “Talking kindly helps us work together. Sharing makes hearts big. Saving and reusing helps our home and the world. And when things fall down, we breathe, we plan, we try again.”

The living room felt peaceful and sweet. The sun gave them a golden hug.

Miri smiled at Pip and at the little wind-up mouse. “We can do hard things when we use our words,” she said.

The End.
