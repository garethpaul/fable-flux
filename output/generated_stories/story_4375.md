# Koala Kori's Fair Kitchen Dance

In a warm kitchen, a calm koala named Kori sat on a soft stool. The air smelled like sweet cinnamon and warm toast. The sun made yellow squares on the floor. The kettle hummed a little song.

Kori blinked slowly and smiled. “Today, I will make fruit toast,” Kori said. “And I will dance while I wait.”

Kori opened a little basket. Inside were two slices of bread, a small pat of butter, and one bright red strawberry. The strawberry looked juicy and shiny. “Mmm,” Kori said softly. “This will be tasty.”

Just then, two friends poked their noses around the doorway. One was Pip the tiny possum, with quick feet and big curious eyes. The other was Lulu the wombat, with soft fur and strong little paws.

“Hi, Kori!” Pip chirped. “It smells yummy in here!”

“Hello,” Lulu said. “My tummy is rumbly.”

Kori’s ears twitched. “I have fruit toast,” Kori said. “But I only have one strawberry.”

Pip hopped closer. “Can I have the strawberry?”

Lulu shuffled forward. “I love strawberries too. Can I have it?”

Kori looked at the single berry. It was smooth and red like a heart. Kori’s own tummy made a tiny growl. “I want it too,” Kori admitted in a calm voice. “But we all want it. How can we be fair?”

Pip’s eyes grew wide. “Fair means everyone gets the same?”

“Fair can mean we all feel okay about what happens,” Kori said. “Let’s think.”

The toaster popped with a cheerful click! The bread turned golden and smelled toasty. Butter melted and made little shiny rivers. Kori spread the butter slowly. “We can share the strawberry,” Kori said. “But we need a fair way.”

Lulu nodded. “We could cut it.”

Pip wiggled. “We can also dance while we think!”

Kori smiled. “Yes. A thinking dance. A fairness dance.”

Kori tapped the wooden spoon on the counter. Tap-tap. Tap-tap. Pip wiggled toes. Wiggle-wiggle. Lulu swayed side to side. Sway-sway. The kettle hummed. Hummm.

Kori sang softly, “Share, care, we’ll be fair.” Tap-tap. Wiggle-wiggle. Sway-sway. All three friends laughed. The kitchen felt warm and happy.

“I have an idea,” Kori said. “We will be resourceful. We will use what we have.”

“What do we have?” Lulu asked.

Kori looked around. “We have one strawberry, two toasts, a butter knife, and… oh! We have a banana on the counter.”

Pip clapped. “More fruit!”

Kori nodded. “I will slice the strawberry into three tiny pieces,” Kori said. “One for each of us. Then I will slice the banana for extra fruit. That way, each friend gets fruit on toast.”

“That sounds fair,” Lulu said.

“That sounds yummy,” Pip added.

Kori used the butter knife carefully. Slice. Slice. Slice. The red strawberry made tiny red hearts on the cutting board. The banana slices were soft and sweet-smelling.

Kori placed food on three small plates: a triangle of toast for Kori, a triangle for Pip, and a triangle for Lulu. Each plate had one little piece of strawberry and two banana rounds.

Kori paused. “Is this fair, friends?”

Pip peered at the plates. “We all have the same!”

Lulu sniffed. “And it smells so good.”

Kori smiled. “Then we have fairness.”

They sat together at the little table. The chairs creaked a friendly creak. The butter felt warm on the toast. The strawberry tasted bright and sweet. The banana tasted soft and mellow.

“Mmm,” Pip said. “I like our fairness dance.”

“Me too,” Lulu said. “It helped us think.”

Kori licked a crumb from a paw. “Fairness means we care about each other. When we think and share, we all feel good.”

Pip tapped the spoon again. “Can we dance again?”

Kori stood up on the stool and held the spoon like a baton. “Yes. A happy dance.”

They danced in the warm kitchen: tap-tap with the spoon, wiggle-wiggle with little toes, sway-sway with round bellies. The sunlight made soft patterns on the floor. The kettle sang a gentle hum. Their laughter sounded like tiny bells.

When the dance ended, Kori looked at the plates. Only crumbs were left. Kori felt cozy and calm. “We used what we had,” Kori said. “We were resourceful.”

“And we were fair,” Lulu added.

“And we danced!” Pip giggled.

Kori nodded. “Today’s lesson: when we all want the same thing, we can slow down, think, and share. Fairness makes friends feel safe and happy. Being resourceful helps make enough for everyone. And a little dance helps our brains.”

Pip and Lulu cheered. “Share, care, we’ll be fair!”

Kori wrapped the butter, washed the knife, and wiped the table. The kitchen smelled like soap and sweet fruit. Outside, a bird chirped a tiny “tweet-tweet.” Inside, three friends felt full and bright.

Kori patted both friends gently. “Thank you for being fair with me,” Kori said.

“Thank you for leading the dance,” Pip said.

“Thank you for the toast,” Lulu added.

Kori gave a calm, cozy smile. “The best taste is fairness.”

The End.
