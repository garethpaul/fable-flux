# Milly Mouse and the Missing Muffin

On a warm, peaceful front porch, a kind little mouse named Milly lived in a tiny hole beneath the wooden steps. The porch smelled like sweet flowers and fresh morning air. Milly loved to watch the world wake up each day.

One bright morning, Milly peeked out from her cozy home. She saw something wonderful on the porch table - a golden blueberry muffin sitting on a pretty blue plate!

"Oh my whiskers!" squeaked Milly. "That muffin smells so yummy!"

Her little pink nose twitched as she breathed in the sweet, warm scent. The muffin had plump blueberries bursting from its fluffy top. Milly's tummy rumbled loudly.

Just then, she heard heavy footsteps. A kind old lady named Mrs. Rose came outside, looking worried.

"Oh dear," said Mrs. Rose, looking around the porch. "I left my special muffin right here for my friend who's coming to visit. Where could it have gone?"

Milly's heart began to beat fast. She really, really wanted that muffin. It would be so easy to take just one tiny bite while Mrs. Rose wasn't looking. But something in her tummy felt funny - and it wasn't hunger.

"Should I take it?" Milly whispered to herself. "Mrs. Rose might not even notice a small bite."

But then Milly remembered what her mama had taught her: "Always tell the truth, little one. Honesty makes your heart feel light and happy."

Milly took a deep breath. She knew what she had to do, even though it was hard.

She scurried out from her hiding spot and stood bravely in front of Mrs. Rose. In her tiniest, squeakiest voice, she said, "Excuse me, Mrs. Rose!"

Mrs. Rose looked down and smiled. "Why, hello there, little mouse! What can I do for you?"

"I... I saw your muffin," Milly said, her whiskers trembling. "I was thinking about taking a bite because I'm very hungry. But I know that wouldn't be right. The muffin belongs to you."

Mrs. Rose's eyes twinkled with kindness. "Oh my, what an honest little mouse you are! You know what? I was just thinking this muffin is too big for one person anyway."

Mrs. Rose broke off a small piece of the muffin and placed it gently on the porch floor. "Would you like to share it with me? We could have a little porch picnic together!"

Milly's eyes grew wide with joy. "Really? You want to share with me?"

"Of course!" laughed Mrs. Rose. "Honest friends always deserve kindness."

As they shared the delicious muffin together, Mrs. Rose told Milly stories about the birds and flowers in her garden. Milly learned about different types of berries and which ones were best for little mice to eat.

"You know," said Mrs. Rose, "if you help me exercise my garden by carrying small seeds to different spots, I'll always have treats to share with you."

From that day on, Milly visited Mrs. Rose every morning. She would run back and forth across the porch, carrying tiny seeds and getting lots of healthy exercise. Mrs. Rose taught her about plants, weather, and friendship.

Milly's heart felt warm and happy. She learned that being honest wasn't just about telling the truth - it was about making real friends and feeling good about herself.

"Being honest feels so much better than being sneaky," Milly told herself as she curled up in her cozy hole that night, her belly full and her heart happy.

The End.
