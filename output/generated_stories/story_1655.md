# Bubbles and Bathroom Beats

Dori was a playful dolphin who loved splashing and singing. One day, Dori visited a bright, clean bathroom. The floor was shiny like a little lake. The big tub was white and round. A soft blue towel hung like a tiny ocean wave. The sink whispered drip, drip, drip. The air smelled like sweet soap and warm vanilla.

“Hello, bathtub!” Dori chirped, flipping her tail. “Hello, rubber duck!” The duck bobbed in the water. “Squeak!” it said.

Dori hummed a silly tune. “La-la, la-la, splash!” She swished her fin and made water dance. The bathroom echoed her voice. La-la, la-la, la-la! It sounded big and bouncy, like her song had wings.

Then a frown wrinkled Dori’s nose. “Hmm,” she said softly. “My song is messy. It feels too fast. It makes my tummy wiggle in a not-nice way.” She tried again. “La-la—oh no!” The sounds got tangled like noodles. Her heart thumped, and the water sloshed too hard.

Dori sighed. “I want my music to feel calm,” she whispered. “I want my song to be gentle, like a hug.”

Rubber Duck tilted its head. “Squeak?”

Dori nodded. “I have a problem. My music is wild. I need a way to make it soft and smooth.”

She looked around for help. The mirror glowed with steamy clouds. The towel felt fluffy when she touched it with her nose. The tub made a hollow sound when she tapped it. Tok, tok, tok. The sink went drip, drip, drip. The fan hummed a steady whirr.

Dori blinked. Her eyes sparkled. “Oh! The bathroom has music already. I can listen.”

She floated still, letting her body rest. “What do I hear?” she asked herself in a small, calm voice.

Drip, drip, drip, went the sink. Whirr, whirr, whirr, went the fan. Tok, tok, tok, went the tub when the duck bumped it. Squeak, squeak, said the duck. Dori’s heart slowed down. Her tail moved gently. Swish… swish…

“Hello, little beats,” Dori whispered. “Can I sing with you?”

She tried a tiny note. “Loooo.” The sound bounced softly off the tiles. She matched the sink. “Drip… looo. Drip… looo.” She counted in her head. One… two… drip. One… two… drip. Her voice floated like a bubble.

Dori smiled. “That feels nice.”

She added the fan. “Whirr, whirr,” she hummed. “Looo on drip. Humm on whirr.” She swayed. Swish… swish… The duck squeaked on the tok of the tub. Squeak on tok! Squeak on tok!

Dori giggled. “We are a bathroom band!”

She made a plan. “Step one: listen. Step two: breathe slow. Step three: sing with the sounds.”

She put a fin on her belly and took a big, gentle breath. In… one, two. Out… one, two. Her body felt light, like she was floating on a quiet sea. She sang a soft pattern. “La… la… laaa.” She tapped the tub with her fin. Tok… tok… tok… The duck answered. “Squeak!”

Soon the bathroom was full of cozy sound. The mirror stopped fogging so fast. The towel swayed a little, like it was dancing. The water made tiny rings that tickled Dori’s nose. The vanilla soap smell felt warm and happy. Dori’s music was calm now. It felt like rocking in a safe boat.

But then the sink made a big drip—plop! Dori jumped. Her song wobbled. She almost rushed again. “Oh no,” she said. “I’m getting fast.”

She remembered her plan. “Listen. Breathe. Sing with the sounds.”

Dori closed her eyes. In… one, two. Out… one, two. She counted with the fan. Whirr—one, two, three, four. Whirr—one, two, three, four. She lowered her voice. “Loooo… loooo…” She tapped slower. Tok… tok… Pause. Tok…

The plop faded back to drip. Dori felt steady. “I did it,” she said, proud and bright. “I can calm my music when it gets too big.”

Rubber Duck puffed out its chest. “Squeak!”

Dori made up a gentle song:

“Drip-drop, don’t you rush,
Swish-swash, soft and hush.
Whirr-whirr, steady breeze,
Sing with sounds, feel at ease.”

Her song was clear. Her body felt peaceful. Her heart felt brave. She knew how to use music to help her feel calm.

A small voice came from the door. It was a little child peeking in. “I like your song,” the child whispered. “It makes me feel quiet inside.”

Dori beamed. “Thank you. I listened to the bathroom. The bathroom helped me make a calm beat. You can do it too! Listen. Breathe. Sing with the sounds around you.”

The child nodded and tapped the sink gently. Drip… tap… drip… tap… Together they made a tiny duet. The duck squeaked right on time. The fan hummed like a friendly bee. Everyone smiled.

Dori swished her tail. “Music is for feelings,” she said. “We can make soft music when we need calm. We can make happy music when we feel bright. We can use sounds around us to help.”

She looked at the shiny floor and the cozy towel and the little rubber duck. “Thank you, bathroom band,” she said.

Dori took one more slow breath and sang her calm song again. The bathroom glowed with gentle notes, like warm light on water. Dori felt safe, kind, and proud.

And every time Dori swam into a new place, she listened first. She found the beat. She made music that helped her and her friends feel good—peaceful, brave, and full of joy.

The clear lesson echoed soft and true: When you listen, breathe, and create with care, music can calm your heart and help you share.

The End.
