# Splash the Helper Dolphin

In a cozy study corner by the sea, there lived a playful dolphin named Splash. The study corner had soft blue cushions, colorful books, and big windows that let in the warm sunshine. Splash loved to peek through the window and watch the children reading together.

"Click, click!" Splash chirped happily as she swam near the window. She could see Mrs. Marina, the librarian, reading a story to a group of little children. But Splash noticed something that made her feel sad.

A little boy named Tommy sat all alone in the back corner. He looked lonely and couldn't reach the books on the high shelf. His wheelchair made it hard for him to join the other children on the reading carpet.

"I want to help!" Splash said to herself, her heart full of kindness. She had an idea that made her fins tingle with excitement.

Splash swam quickly to find her friend, Oliver the octopus, who was organizing books with his eight helpful arms.

"Oliver! Oliver!" Splash called out, splashing the water gently. "I saw a little boy who needs help in the study corner. Can we volunteer to make things better for him?"

Oliver's eyes sparkled. "What a wonderful idea, Splash! How can we help?"

Splash explained about Tommy and how he couldn't reach the books or join the reading circle. Together, they swam to Mrs. Marina's window and tapped gently with their fins.

"Oh my!" said Mrs. Marina, opening the window. "Hello, Splash and Oliver! What brings you here today?"

"We want to volunteer!" Splash said proudly, doing a little spin in the water. "We saw Tommy needs help, and we want to make the study corner better for everyone!"

Mrs. Marina's face lit up with a big smile. "That's so thoughtful of you both! Volunteering means using our time and talents to help others. What ideas do you have?"

Oliver stretched his arms wide. "I can use my long arms to get books from high shelves!"

"And I can push Tommy's wheelchair with my nose so he can join the reading circle!" Splash added, clapping her fins together.

"Those are perfect ideas!" Mrs. Marina said. "Let me ask Tommy if he'd like your help."

Tommy's face broke into the biggest smile when he heard about Splash and Oliver's offer. "Yes, please!" he said excitedly. "I love dolphins and octopuses!"

From that day on, Splash and Oliver became the best volunteer helpers in the study corner. Oliver would carefully bring down books for all the children who couldn't reach them. Splash would gently push Tommy's wheelchair so he could sit with his new friends during story time.

"This feels so good in my heart," Splash told Oliver one sunny afternoon as they watched all the children reading together happily.

"When we volunteer and help others," Oliver replied, "we make new friends and spread kindness everywhere we go!"

Tommy waved at his new friends through the window. "Thank you, Splash and Oliver! You made me feel included and happy!"

Splash did her favorite spinning trick in the water, feeling proud and joyful. She had learned that volunteering wasn't just about helping others – it also filled her own heart with happiness and made wonderful friendships bloom.

The study corner became a place where everyone felt welcome, thanks to two caring sea friends who chose to volunteer their time and hearts.

The End.
