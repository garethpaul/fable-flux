# Melody's Big Feelings

In a cozy home office with tall bookshelves and a warm wooden desk, lived a little yellow bird named Melody. She had the most beautiful voice in the whole house, with feathers that sparkled like sunshine and tiny black eyes that twinkled when she sang.

Every morning, Melody would perch on the windowsill and practice her songs. "La-la-la! Tweet-tweet-tweet!" she would sing happily, watching the clouds drift by outside.

But today was different. Today, Melody felt very frustrated.

"I want to sing the perfect song for Mama's birthday tomorrow," Melody chirped to herself. "But nothing sounds right!"

She tried singing high notes. "TWEET-TWEET-TWEET!" Too loud.

She tried singing low notes. "Chirp, chirp, chirp." Too quiet.

She tried singing fast. "Tweetweetweetweet!" Too jumbled.

Melody's little chest began to feel hot and tight. Her feathers ruffled up, and she felt like stomping her tiny orange feet.

"This is too hard!" she squawked, flapping her wings. "I'll never get it right!"

Just then, Papa walked into the office carrying a steaming cup of coffee that smelled warm and nutty.

"Oh my," Papa said gently, sitting down in his big office chair. "Melody, you sound upset. What's making you feel frustrated, little one?"

Melody's voice trembled. "I want to sing the most beautiful song for Mama's birthday, but everything I try sounds wrong! My feelings are all mixed up inside!"

Papa nodded thoughtfully. "You know, Melody, when I feel frustrated, I like to take three deep breaths. Would you like to try that with me?"

"Okay," Melody whispered.

Together, they breathed in slowly through their noses - "Whoooosh" - and out through their mouths - "Ahhhhhh." They did this three times, and Melody felt her ruffled feathers smooth down.

"Better?" Papa asked with a warm smile.

"A little," Melody admitted. "But I still don't know what song to sing."

"Well," Papa said, "sometimes when I'm stuck, I tell myself a story about what I'm trying to do. Would you like to tell me a story about the perfect birthday song?"

Melody tilted her little head. "A story?"

"Yes! What would this special song sound like? How would it make Mama feel?"

Melody closed her eyes and began to speak softly. "The song would sound like... like morning sunshine streaming through the window. It would be gentle like Mama's hugs and warm like her smile. It would tell her how much I love her."

"That sounds beautiful," Papa said. "Now, what if you sang exactly what you just told me?"

Melody's eyes opened wide. She had been so focused on making perfect notes that she forgot the most important part - singing from her heart!

She took another deep breath and began to sing: "Good morning, sunshine Mama, you make me feel so bright. Your hugs are warm and gentle, you fill my world with light!"

The melody was simple but sweet, flowing naturally like a gentle stream.

"Oh, Melody!" Papa clapped his hands. "That was perfect because it came from your heart and told a story about love."

Melody learned that when big feelings like frustration bubble up inside, she could take deep breaths, ask for help, and remember that the best things come from being kind and respectful to herself and others.

The next morning, Mama cried happy tears when she heard Melody's heartfelt birthday song.

The End.
