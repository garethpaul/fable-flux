# Panda Paws and the Windy Day

Poppy the panda lived in a cozy house with a sunny backyard. The grass felt soft like a blanket. The sun felt warm on Poppy’s fur. Birds sang, “Tweet-tweet!” and a gentle wind said, “Whooo.” The air smelled like sweet flowers and clean leaves.

Poppy loved to play outside. Today, Poppy had a big plan. “I will make a leaf circle,” Poppy said. “I will make it round and neat.” Poppy smiled and held a small rake. It was just right for panda paws.

Poppy raked the crunchy leaves. Rustle, rustle. The pile grew bigger. Orange, yellow, and red leaves made a bright and happy blanket. “Almost done,” said Poppy.

Then the wind blew a little stronger. “Whooooo!” Leaves flew up and danced away. The circle broke apart. Leaves went under the garden bench. Leaves went on the path. Leaves went everywhere.

Poppy’s cheeks felt hot. Poppy’s tummy wiggled. “I worked so hard,” Poppy said. “My circle is gone!” Poppy’s paws curled tight. Poppy wanted to roar. Poppy wanted to throw the rake.

Just then, a butterfly with blue wings floated by. It fluttered near Poppy’s nose. “Hello,” the butterfly seemed to say. Poppy took a small breath. In and out. The butterfly landed on a yellow flower. The flower smelled sunny and sweet.

Poppy remembered a helper song. It was the Calm-Down Song. Poppy sang softly, “Stop and breathe. One, two, three. I can wait. I can see.” Poppy put the rake down. Poppy placed paws on a soft belly and took slow breaths.

In… and out.
In… and out.
In… and out.

Poppy’s cheeks cooled. Poppy’s tummy felt steady. “I still feel sad,” Poppy said, “but I can choose gentle.” Poppy looked at the leaves. They were not bad. They were just moving with the wind.

Poppy had an idea. “I can work with the wind.” Poppy waved to the leaves. “Let’s try again, but different.”

Poppy set up a little path with smooth stones. Tap, tap, tap. The stones made a border to catch leaves. Poppy picked up fallen twigs and made tiny fences. “This will help,” Poppy said. “I can be patient. I can practice.”

Poppy raked again. Rustle, rustle. The wind puffed. Whooo. Some leaves ran away, but many stayed inside the stone path. “It’s okay,” said Poppy. “I can try once more.”

Poppy took a sip of cool water. It tasted fresh and crisp. “Thank you, body,” Poppy said. “You are calm.” Poppy took three more breaths. In and out. In and out. In and out.

Poppy worked slowly. Scoop, pat, smooth. “Round and neat,” Poppy said. “Round and neat.” The sun tickled Poppy’s ears. A robin hopped near and pecked at the ground. “Cheer-up! Cheer-up!” it sang.

A little neighbor, Mia, peeked over the fence. “Hi, Poppy!” she called. “What are you doing?”

“I am making a leaf circle,” Poppy said. “The wind knocked it over. I felt mad. I sang my Calm-Down Song. Now I am trying again.”

Mia nodded. “Can I help?”

“Yes,” said Poppy. “Let’s care for the yard together. Leaves can be used for good things.”

Mia smiled. “We can make a cozy bed for worms,” she said. “Worms like leaves!”

Poppy’s eyes shined. “Great idea. The earth will be happy.”

Together they raked. Rustle, rustle. They made one neat circle and one small leaf bed near the flowers. Poppy pressed the leaves down with gentle paws. Mia patted them flat with tiny hands. “Sleep tight, worms,” Poppy whispered.

A bigger gust whooshed by. Whooooosh! A few leaves tried to run. Poppy felt a little spark in the tummy again. Cheeks warmed up. “Oh no,” said Poppy.

Poppy paused. “Stop and breathe. One, two, three,” Poppy sang. Mia joined, “I can wait. I can see.”

They watched the leaves dance and then settle. “See?” Mia said softly. “They came back.”

Poppy nodded. “Thank you, breath. Thank you, patience.”

They added two more stones to the border. Tap, tap. Then they picked up a plastic bottle from the grass. “This does not belong here,” said Poppy. “Let’s take care of our backyard.”

They carried the bottle to the recycling bin. Clink!

When they were done, the yard looked clean and bright. The leaf circle was round and lovely. The leaf bed snuggled next to the flowers. The wind blew softly now. The air smelled like dirt and sunshine. Poppy’s heart felt light.

Mia clapped. “We did it!”

Poppy grinned. “We did it with calm. We did it with care.”

They sat on the warm step and shared cool cucumber slices. Crunch, crunch. The cucumbers tasted juicy and green. The butterfly flew by again. The robin sang a happy note.

Poppy said, “Today I learned a big thing. When I feel mad, I can stop. I can breathe. I can wait. I can try again. Being patient helps my body and my heart.”

Mia added, “And we can care for our world. We can keep our yard clean. We can use leaves to help little creatures.”

Poppy nodded. “Yes. I can be a patient panda.”

The sun dipped a little, and the backyard glowed gold. Poppy felt proud, calm, and kind.

The End.
