# Momo the Honest Mouse

Momo was a kind little mouse who lived behind the big cupboard in a sunny dining room. Every morning, golden light danced on the table. The air smelled like warm toast and apples. The wooden chairs made soft creaks, like tiny songs.

One morning, Momo peeked out from his mouse door. “Good morning, dining room!” he whispered. His whiskers twitched. His tummy rumbled, but just a little. “I will do my chores first,” he said. “Then I will have a snack. That keeps me healthy and calm.”

Momo had small chores every day. He swept crumbs from under the chairs with a tiny leaf broom. He folded napkins that fell to the floor. He lined up shiny buttons he found, into neat rows. “Sweep, sweep. Fold, fold. Line, line,” he sang in a gentle voice.

On the table sat a bright red bowl filled with ripe strawberries. They looked juicy. They smelled sweet, like summer. Momo’s nose wiggled. “Oh my,” he said. “Those look so yummy.”

He stood very still and took a deep breath. “I can wait,” he told himself. “First chores, then snack.” He wiggled his toes and counted softly, “One… two… three.” That helped his body feel calm.

As Momo swept, he heard a tap and a roll. Tap. Roll. Something tiny fell off the table and bounced across the floor. It was a small silver spoon! It rolled under a chair and stopped by Momo’s feet.

Momo picked it up. “Hello, little spoon,” he giggled. The spoon felt cool and smooth. He looked around the quiet dining room. No one was there.

He thought, I could keep the spoon. It is shiny. But then he felt a warm squeeze in his chest. “Wait,” he said out loud. “Whose spoon is this? Is it mine? No. I must be honest.”

Momo sat on his tail to think. He used simple steps, like a tiny detective.

Step 1: “What happened?” he said. “The spoon fell.”

Step 2: “What do I know?” He looked up at the table. “The strawberries are Mama’s. The red bowl belongs to the family. The spoon must be theirs too.”

Step 3: “What is kind and right?” He nodded. “Tell the truth. Give it back.”

Momo carried the spoon with two paws. It clinked softly. Clink, clink. He climbed a chair leg, then a cushion. “Careful,” he said. “Slow and safe.” He moved his body with control, just like he practiced.

When he reached the table, Momo saw a strawberry had rolled away and left a tiny red juice spot. He wiped the spot with a leaf napkin. “Clean and tidy,” he smiled. “That is part of my chores.”

Just then, the door opened. Gentle footsteps. It was the family. A little girl named Lina walked in with her Mama. “Breakfast time,” Mama said. Lina wiggled into a chair.

Lina looked at the table. “Oh no,” she said. “My small silver spoon is missing!”

Momo’s heart thumped. He stood tall and squeaked, “Hello, Lina! Hello, Mama!” He lifted the spoon high. “The spoon fell. I found it under the chair. I wanted to keep it… because it is shiny. But it is not mine. I am telling the truth.”

Lina’s eyes got big. “You are an honest mouse,” she said. She smiled so wide her eyes crinkled. “Thank you!”

Mama nodded. “Thank you for being truthful, Momo. Thank you for taking care of the dining room too.”

Momo felt warm and light, like a bubble. He took another slow breath. “It felt hard to tell,” he said. “But telling the truth feels good in my heart.”

Lina clapped softly. “Honest hearts are brave hearts,” she said. “Would you like a strawberry as a thank-you?”

Momo’s tummy gave a happy grumble. “Yes, please,” he said politely. Lina washed a small strawberry and patted it dry. It was cool and red and smelled sweet. She set it on a tiny saucer.

Momo looked at the strawberry. He paused, checked his body, and noticed he felt ready to eat. “First, I finish my last chore,” he said. He folded one more napkin that had slipped to the floor. Fold, fold. Then he sat, took a small bite, and chewed slowly. “Mmm,” he hummed. “Juicy and kind.”

Lina giggled. “Juicy and kind is a funny thing to say!”

After breakfast, Momo and Lina played a quick “Think Steps” game.

Lina asked, “What if a marble rolls away? What do we do?”

Momo held up one paw. “Step 1: What happened? The marble rolled.”

He held up another paw. “Step 2: What do we know? It is Lina’s marble.”

He held up a third paw. “Step 3: What is kind and right? Tell the truth and give it back.”

“Exactly!” Lina laughed. They both felt proud.

The sun shone brighter on the table. The dining room smelled like toast and strawberries. Momo swept the last crumbs, humming. He felt calm, strong, and honest.

“I can be truthful, I can think with steps, and I can take care of my body,” Momo said. “That is wellness for a mouse!”

Lina waved. “See you later, Momo! Thank you for being honest today.”

Momo waved back. “Honesty makes the day shine,” he said.

The End.
