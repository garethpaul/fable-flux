# Fawn's First Big Helper Day

Little Fawn stood on her tippy-toes in Grandma Rabbit's warm kitchen. The morning sun streamed through the yellow curtains, making everything glow like honey. The kitchen smelled wonderful - like fresh bread and sweet jam.

"Oh my!" said Grandma Rabbit, holding her fluffy white paw to her forehead. "I promised to make cookies for the forest picnic today, but I'm feeling quite tired."

Fawn's big brown eyes grew wide. She loved the forest picnic! All her friends would be there - Squirrel, Hedgehog, and Baby Bear. But without cookies, it wouldn't be the same.

"Don't worry, Grandma Rabbit," Fawn said softly. "I can help you rest."

But as Grandma Rabbit settled into her cozy chair for a nap, Fawn looked at the big mixing bowl on the counter. Her heart felt fluttery, like butterflies were dancing inside.

"I wish I could make the cookies myself," she whispered. "But I'm too little. I don't know how."

Fawn felt sad and worried. She wanted to help, but she didn't think she could do something so big all by herself.

Then Fawn took a deep breath. The warm kitchen air filled her nose with the smell of cinnamon and vanilla. She looked at her small hooves, then at the recipe card propped against the sugar jar.

"Wait," she said out loud. "Maybe I can try! I won't know if I don't try."

Fawn pushed a sturdy wooden stool over to the counter. Climb, step, climb - up she went! Now she could reach everything.

She read the recipe card slowly, sounding out each word. "One cup flour. Two eggs. Sugar." She could read some of the words Grandma Rabbit had taught her!

"I can do this," Fawn told herself. "One step at a time."

First, she cracked the eggs - CRACK! - into the big bowl. A tiny piece of shell fell in. "Oops!" she giggled, fishing it out with a spoon.

Next came the flour. POOF! A white cloud puffed up, dusting her brown fur. She looked like she was wearing a silly white hat!

When she felt confused about how much sugar to add, Fawn didn't give up. Instead, she gently woke Grandma Rabbit.

"Grandma, I'm making the cookies myself, but I need to ask - is this enough sugar?" Fawn held up the measuring cup proudly.

Grandma Rabbit's eyes twinkled with surprise and joy. "Oh my dear Fawn! You're doing this all by yourself! That's perfect."

Fawn stirred and mixed, her little hooves working hard. The wooden spoon felt heavy, but she kept going. Soon, the kitchen filled with the most delicious smell as golden cookies baked in the oven.

When her friends arrived for the picnic, they couldn't believe their eyes.

"Fawn! Did you really make these cookies all by yourself?" asked Squirrel.

"I did!" Fawn said proudly. "I felt scared at first, but I tried anyway. When I needed help, I asked for it. And look what I made!"

The cookies were perfectly golden and tasted like sunshine.

"You're so brave, Fawn," said Baby Bear. "You showed us that we can do big things too!"

Fawn's heart felt warm and happy. She learned that being self-reliant didn't mean doing everything alone - it meant trying her best, asking for help when needed, and believing in herself.

From that day on, whenever Fawn felt something was too hard, she remembered her cookie day and said, "I can try! One step at a time."

The End.
