# Benny Beaver's Big Pantry Problem

Benny Beaver was the busiest beaver in the whole forest. He loved to build things with his strong teeth and busy paws. One sunny morning, Benny waddled into the forest pantry where all the animals kept their food.

"Oh no!" squeaked Benny, looking around with his big brown eyes. The pantry was a terrible mess! Acorns rolled everywhere on the wooden shelves. Honey jars were sticky and dirty. Berries were scattered all over the floor like colorful marbles.

Benny's fluffy tail slapped the ground with worry. "How will I ever clean this big pantry all by myself?" he wondered aloud. The sweet smell of spilled honey mixed with the earthy scent of scattered nuts made his nose twitch.

Just then, Rosie Rabbit hopped in. Her pink nose wiggled when she saw the mess. "What happened here, Benny?"

"I don't know," said Benny sadly. "But it's such a big job. I don't think I can fix it alone."

"Maybe I can help!" chirped Rosie. "I'm really good at sorting things with my quick paws."

Soon, Oliver Owl flew in through the window. His wise yellow eyes looked around carefully. "Hoot! What a mess! But you know what? I can reach the high shelves with my wings."

Then Sammy Squirrel scampered down from a tree branch. His bushy tail twitched excitedly. "I can gather all the nuts! I know exactly where each type belongs."

Benny felt warm and happy inside. "You would all help me?"

"Of course!" they said together. "We're friends!"

The friends worked as a team. Benny used his strong teeth to fix broken shelves. *Gnaw, gnaw, gnaw!* went his busy teeth. Rosie sorted berries by color - red strawberries here, blue blueberries there. Oliver flapped up high to dust the tall shelves with his soft feathers. Sammy gathered nuts faster than lightning, his tiny paws moving like magic.

"This is fun!" laughed Benny as they worked. "It's much easier with friends helping."

"And faster too!" giggled Rosie as she arranged the berries in neat rows.

While they cleaned, each friend shared a story. Benny told about building his first dam. Rosie shared about learning to hop. Oliver hooted about his first flight. Sammy chattered about finding his favorite acorn tree.

The stories made the work feel like play. Before they knew it, the pantry sparkled clean! Every jar gleamed. Every shelf was organized. The wooden floor shone like new.

"Wow!" said Benny, clapping his paws together. "Look what we did together! Thank you, everyone. I couldn't have done this without you."

"That's what friends are for," smiled Rosie.

"Working together makes everything better," hooted Oliver wisely.

"And more fun too!" added Sammy, doing a little dance.

Benny felt grateful in his heart. He had learned something important that day. Big problems become small when friends work together. And sharing stories while working makes even hard jobs feel like fun.

From that day on, whenever any forest friend had a big job, they all helped each other. The pantry stayed neat and clean because everyone took care of it together.

"Thank you, friends," Benny said every day. "Together, we can do anything!"

The End.
