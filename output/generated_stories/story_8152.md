# Koala Kai and the Quiet Buttons

Koala Kai was a calm koala with soft, silvery fur. He lived with his family above a basement lab. Down the wooden stairs, the lab smelled like lemon soap and warm toast from the kitchen. Lights blinked. Little fans hummed. Shelves held jars of shiny beads, smooth rocks, and tiny flags from many places around the world.

One bright morning, Kai’s teacher, Ms. Lina, invited the class to visit the basement lab. “Today we will make Learning Boxes,” she said. “We will share sounds and stories from our families.” She smiled. “We will listen, learn, and be kind.”

Kai felt happy and curious. He loved to learn. He loved to stay calm. He patted his belly and whispered, “Slow breaths help me.” In through the nose. Out through the mouth. Whooo. He felt the air cool and warm, like a tiny breeze.

In the lab, each child got a small box with buttons. The buttons were red, blue, and green. When you pressed a button, it could play a sound or show a picture. Ms. Lina said, “We will add one special sound from home. We will take turns.”

Bashir pressed his green button. It played a drum beat from his family’s music. Boom, ba-boom! The sound was low and warm. “This is a doumbek,” he said. “My uncle plays it.”

Lina from the next street pressed her red button. It sang a lullaby in Spanish. “Mi abuela sings this,” she said. The song was soft like a blanket.

Ava pressed her blue button. It chirped like a cricket from the summer nights. “We listen to crickets on our porch,” she said.

Kai smiled at each friend. He clapped quietly. He felt the drum in his chest. He felt the lullaby in his ears. He felt the cricket in his toes.

Then it was Kai’s turn.

He held his box. He pressed his red button.

It was too loud. A sudden BEEP! filled the lab. BEEP! BEEP! The lights flashed. The fan buzzed faster. The jar lids rattled. The buttons blinked all at once.

Kai’s ears felt hot. His paws felt tingly. He did not like the loud beep. His calm felt like it was sliding away.

Ms. Lina said, “It’s okay, Kai. We can fix it.”

But the beep kept going. BEEP! BEEP!

Kai wanted to hide. He wanted to cry. His tummy felt tight.

Then he remembered: “Slow breaths help me.” He placed a paw on his belly. In through the nose. One... two... three. Out through the mouth. Whooo.

He looked around and saw a small poster on the wall. It had pictures of “Quiet Buttons.” It showed a koala pressing pause, a koala hugging itself, and a koala counting.

Kai whispered, “Pause. Hug. Count.” He pressed the pause button on his box. It did not work. BEEP!

He gave himself a gentle hug. His fur was soft. His arms were warm. He rocked a little. Rock, rock. His feet felt the cool floor.

He began to count. “One... two... three... four...” His voice was slow and kind. “Five.”

The beep did not stop, but Kai felt his body slow down. He could hear Ms. Lina’s calm voice under the noise, like a river under wind. “We are safe. We will solve this together,” she said.

Bashir held up a round sticker. “Maybe cover the speaker?”

Lina brought a tiny flag from Chile and waved it. “We can make it a game. Follow the quiet flag!”

The class watched Ms. Lina switch off a silver switch. The fan grew soft. She pulled a little cord. The lights stopped flashing. Then she knelt beside Kai’s box. “I see the problem,” she said gently. “The red button is stuck.”

Kai pressed his paws together. “May I try?” he asked.

Ms. Lina nodded. “Yes. You are the engineer.”

Kai took one slow breath. He counted to three. He pressed the button softly, not hard. Click. The beep ended.

Ahhh. The lab felt warm again. The air smelled like lemons again. Kai’s shoulders lowered. Everyone cheered, but softly, like gentle rain. Clap, clap.

Kai smiled. “I got too loud inside,” he said. “I used my quiet buttons.”

“What are your quiet buttons?” Ava asked.

Kai showed the poster. “Pause. Hug. Count. And breathe.” He patted his belly. “Like this. In, out.”

Ms. Lina said, “Wonderful self-regulation, Kai. You noticed your feelings. You chose calm steps. That is smart.”

Bashir added, “And brave!”

Lina said, “Now let’s add Kai’s sound.”

Kai opened a small pouch from home. Inside was a tiny eucalyptus leaf charm and a recording. He pressed the green button. A gentle rustle played—wind through gum leaves—and a soft, plucked tune from his grandma’s ukulele in Fiji. Swish, swish. Plink, plink.

“This is my family sound,” Kai said. “Leaves from Australia, music from Fiji. Both are me.”

Everyone swayed to the soft music. The Learning Boxes glowed like little suns.

Ms. Lina said, “We learned about music from many places. We learned to listen. We learned to be calm.”

Kai felt proud. He accepted the loud beep had scared him, and that was okay. Feelings are okay. Choices help.

He looked at his friends and said, “When it gets too loud, we can use quiet buttons. We can listen to each other’s stories. We can learn together.”

The class nodded. “We can,” they said.

They put a tiny sticker on each box: a koala hugging itself. It was the “quiet button” badge.

The basement lab hummed softly. The jars sat still. The flags fluttered a little as the air moved, swish, swish. Kai took one more slow breath and smiled.

The End.
