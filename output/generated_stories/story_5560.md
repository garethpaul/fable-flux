# Tilly Turtle Finds Her Song

Tilly was a shy turtle with a small, smooth shell and gentle eyes. She walked slowly, step-scritch, step-scritch, down the hallway to the musical practice room. Inside, the room was bright and warm. The air smelled like polished wood and clean strings. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor.

Her friends were already there. Benny the bunny tapped a drum, thump-thump, thump. Mia the mouse held a tiny triangle that went ting! ting! Kavi the kitten plucked a guitar, twang-twang. Miss Lark, the music teacher, smiled. “Welcome, everyone. Today we will take turns making music and telling a tiny story with our sound.”

Tilly tucked her head a little. Her heart went pitter-pat. She liked music, but she was shy. “What if I make a mistake?” she whispered to herself.

Miss Lark clapped gently. “We will go in a circle,” she said. “We listen. We wait. We take turns. And we cheer for each friend.”

Benny went first. “My sound is a galloping horse!” he shouted happily. Thump-thump, thump-thump, thump! The drum sounded big and bouncy. Everyone clapped.

Mia went next. “Mine is a twinkly star.” Ting… ting… ting! The soft triangle sparkled. “Lovely,” Miss Lark said.

Kavi grinned. “My sound is a purring cat.” Twang-twang, rowr-rowr, twang. His whiskers twitched. “Nice purr,” Mia giggled.

Tilly’s turn was coming. Her stomach felt fluttery, like tiny butterflies. She looked around. The shelves held shiny cymbals, colorful shakers, and a deep brown cello that hummed like a friendly bear. In the corner sat a small drum, not too loud, not too fast.

Miss Lark’s eyes were kind. “Tilly, would you like to try?” she asked in a soft voice.

Tilly took a slow breath in. In… and out. “I… I can try,” she said. Her voice was quiet, but it was there.

She picked up the small drum. Her hand trembled a little. Tap… pause… tap-tap. The sound was gentle, like tiny raindrops on a leaf. Tap… tap-tap… tap. It made her think of a story—her story.

“I am a little turtle,” Tilly whispered. “I walk slow. Step-scritch, step-scritch. I hide when I’m scared. But I keep going.”

Tap… tap-tap… tap. The room was very still. Everyone listened. Tilly felt warm in her chest. She kept going.

“Then I see a rainbow,” Tilly said. “It is bright and kind. I feel brave.”

Tap-tap-tap! She made a tiny rainbow rhythm. Mia smiled wide. Kavi’s tail swished.

When Tilly finished, the room filled with soft claps. “Beautiful,” Miss Lark said. “Your music told a story of courage.”

Tilly peeked out from her shell a little more. “I did it,” she thought. Her heart calmed. Pitter-pat became pitter… pause… pat.

Benny hopped closer. “I liked the raindrop sound,” he said. “Can you show me?”

Tilly nodded. “Tap with soft fingers,” she said, tapping slow so he could see. She felt helpful, and that felt good.

Miss Lark said, “Let’s make a group song. We will tell a story together. We will take turns. We will listen.”

They sat in a circle. The floor felt smooth under Tilly’s feet. The sunlight felt warm on her shell. Miss Lark began. “Once there was a quiet forest…”

Kavi went first: twang, twang—leaves rustling. Mia added ting—stars peeking out. Benny added thump-thump—little feet walking. Tilly listened. She waited, breathing slow. It was almost her turn.

She remembered her tiny raindrops. She remembered the rainbow. “I can try again,” she told herself. “I can grow.”

When her turn came, Tilly lifted the drum. Tap… tap-tap… tap. “A shy turtle walks,” she said softly. “She is scared. But she keeps going.”

Everyone leaned in. Tilly felt their listening like a hug. The room hummed with kind quiet.

Then she added a new sound. Tap-tap, tap-tap-tap. “She finds friends,” Tilly said. “They wait for her. They cheer for her. They take turns.”

Kavi smiled. “The turtle is brave,” he purred. Mia’s eyes shone. “The turtle is me sometimes,” she said. Benny nodded. “Me too.”

At the end, Miss Lark lifted her hands. “Together,” she said. They all made one last gentle sound. Thump. Ting. Twang. Tap. The notes floated up like soap bubbles and popped with a happy hush.

Tilly’s smile grew. She felt tall inside her small shell. She had tried. She had kept trying. She had told a story with music. She had waited, listened, and taken turns with her friends.

Miss Lark spoke softly. “What did we learn today?”

Tilly raised a little hand. “When we feel shy, we can breathe and try again,” she said. “Trying helps us grow.”

“And when we take turns and listen,” Benny added, “everyone gets to shine.”

Mia squeaked, “We include friends. We cheer for them.”

Kavi purred, “We keep going, even when it’s hard.”

Tilly hugged her drum. The room still smelled like warm wood. The sun still made golden squares. But now, inside Tilly, there was a new light. It glowed like a rainbow.

“I can be brave again tomorrow,” she whispered.

“Yes,” Miss Lark said. “Step by step. Beat by beat.”

The End.
