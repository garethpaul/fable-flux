# Penny Parrot's Practice Place

In a cozy house on Maple Street, there lived a bright green parrot named Penny. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds, and she loved to create beautiful things. Today, Penny flew down to the busy basement where her family kept all sorts of wonderful treasures.

"Squawk! What a perfect place to practice!" chirped Penny, looking around with her bright orange eyes.

The basement was full of exciting sounds. *Whirr* went the washing machine. *Hum* buzzed the dryer. *Drip, drop* sang the water from a leaky pipe. But Penny wasn't bothered by the noise. She had important work to do!

Penny wanted to learn how to paint beautiful pictures, just like the ones she saw in her favorite books. She had tried yesterday, but her painting looked more like colorful mud than a pretty flower.

"I felt so sad when my painting didn't look right," Penny said to herself, remembering how her tummy had felt heavy and her wings had drooped. "But Mama always says practice makes progress!"

Penny set up her easel near the window where golden sunlight streamed in. She arranged her paints in neat rows - red like strawberries, blue like the sky, and yellow like sunshine. The sweet smell of the paints made her beak tingle with excitement.

"First, I need to take care of myself," Penny remembered. She took three deep breaths, just like her friend Dr. Robin had taught her. *In... out... in... out...* Her heart felt calmer already.

As Penny began to paint, her neighbor's cat, Mr. Whiskers, peeked through the basement window.

"What are you doing, Penny?" meowed Mr. Whiskers.

"I'm practicing my painting!" Penny replied cheerfully. "Yesterday I felt frustrated, but today I'm going to keep trying. Practice helps me get better!"

Penny dipped her brush in the blue paint. This time, she moved slowly and carefully. When she made a wobbly line, she didn't feel angry. Instead, she took another deep breath and tried again.

"Oh my!" gasped Mr. Whiskers. "That looks like a beautiful sky!"

Penny felt proud and happy. Her chest felt warm and light, like a balloon filled with sunshine. She added some green for grass and yellow for a bright sun.

Just then, Penny heard a *drip, drop* sound getting louder. She looked up and saw water dripping from the ceiling into a bucket.

"Oh dear," said Penny. "I should tell Papa about this leak. Taking care of our home is everyone's job!"

Penny carefully cleaned her brushes with soapy water - another healthy habit she had learned. She put her paints away neatly and flew upstairs to find Papa.

"Papa! Papa!" she called. "There's a drip in the basement that needs fixing!"

"Thank you for telling me, Penny!" said Papa with a big smile. "You're such a responsible helper!"

Later, as Papa fixed the leak, Penny showed him her painting.

"Wow, Penny! This is much better than yesterday's painting!" Papa said proudly.

"I practiced and practiced," Penny beamed. "And when I felt frustrated, I took deep breaths. Practice really does make progress!"

That night, Penny hung her painting on the basement wall. Every time she looked at it, she remembered that with practice, patience, and taking care of herself and her home, she could do amazing things.

The End.
