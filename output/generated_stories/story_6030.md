# Kira the Koala's Crafty Solution

Kira the koala loved staying in Grandma's cozy guest room. The soft yellow walls made her feel warm and happy, and the big window let in lots of sunshine. But today, something was wrong.

"Oh no!" Kira said, looking around the messy room. Her craft supplies were scattered everywhere! Colorful paper scraps covered the fluffy carpet, glue sticks rolled under the bed, and her favorite scissors were nowhere to be found.

Kira's little cousin Max was coming to visit tomorrow, and they always made crafts together in this special room. But how could they create anything fun with such a big mess?

Kira sat on the soft bed and took a deep breath, just like Mama Koala taught her. "When things feel overwhelming, breathe slowly and think calmly," she whispered to herself.

Looking around again, Kira noticed something. "Maybe this mess can become something wonderful!" she said with a smile.

First, Kira gathered all the paper scraps. There were shiny gold pieces, soft pink hearts, and bright blue circles. "These would make beautiful collages," she thought, sorting them by color into neat piles.

Next, she found three glue sticks hiding in different corners. "Perfect! Now I know where they are," Kira said, placing them in a small basket.

Under the guest bed, Kira discovered not just her scissors, but also some forgotten crayons and stickers. "What a nice surprise!" she giggled, her fuzzy ears wiggling with excitement.

But Kira still had a problem. How could she keep everything organized so she and Max could find things easily tomorrow?

Kira thought and thought. Then she spotted some empty shoeboxes in the closet. "That's it!" she exclaimed.

Carefully, Kira covered each box with pretty wrapping paper. She used her scissors to cut the paper just right, and her glue stick to make it smooth and neat. The boxes felt bumpy under her paws as she smoothed out every wrinkle.

On each box, Kira drew a picture with her crayons. A crayon picture for the crayon box, a scissors picture for the cutting tools, and a glue drop for the glue supplies. The waxy crayons smelled sweet as she colored.

"Now everything has its own special home," Kira said proudly, arranging the boxes on the desk.

She made labels with her best writing: "PAPER," "GLUE," and "TOOLS." Each letter was carefully drawn in bright colors.

Finally, Kira created a special craft schedule. She drew pictures showing the steps: First, choose your materials. Second, plan your project. Third, create together. Fourth, clean up.

The next morning, when Max arrived, his eyes grew wide. "Wow, Kira! This room looks amazing! Everything is so organized and pretty!"

"Thank you!" Kira beamed. "I learned that when we take time to organize our things, crafting becomes even more fun. Plus, we can find everything we need!"

Max and Kira spent the whole day making beautiful art projects. They created paper flower gardens, painted rock families, and built towers with recycled boxes. Because everything was organized, they had more time to be creative and less time searching for supplies.

"This is the best craft day ever!" Max said, hugging his calm cousin.

Kira smiled, feeling proud and peaceful. She had learned that problems can become opportunities, and that taking time to organize and plan makes everything work better.

From that day on, Kira kept her craft routine: organize first, create second, and always clean up together. The guest room became the most wonderful crafting space, where imagination could bloom in an organized, happy place.

The End.
