# Otter Ollie’s Backyard Boogie

Otter Ollie loved to wiggle, hop, and twirl. “I want to dance in the big backyard show!” he squeaked, whiskers twitching with joy. The sunny backyard smelled like warm grass and sweet flowers. Bees buzzed softly. A breeze tickled the leaves and made them whisper, shhh-shhh.

Ollie clapped his little paws. “I can dance fast! I can dance now!” He bounced onto the soft, green lawn. He spun in a circle. He hopped high. He tried a fancy tail wiggle. Then—plop!—he slipped on the smooth patio and landed on his belly.

“Oof,” Ollie giggled. “That was silly.” But his smile wobbled. His paws felt a little shaky.

From the porch, Nana Turtle peeked over her glasses. “Hello, Ollie,” she said in her gentle, slow voice. “Are you getting ready?”

“I don’t need to get ready,” Ollie said. “I want to dance right away!”

Nana Turtle blinked kindly. “Even the sun waits for morning to rise. Patience helps us shine.” She tapped a little drum by the steps. “Would you like help?”

Ollie looked at the bright, sunny yard. He heard kids cheering in the street. He wanted to hurry. But his belly still felt oops-y from the plop. He took a deep breath. He smelled mint from the garden and felt the warm sun on his fur.

“Okay,” he said softly. “Yes, please.”

Nana Turtle smiled. “Good choice. Let’s prepare together.”

They made a plan. First, stretch. Then, practice. Then, rest. Then, dance.

“Stretch like the sun waking up,” Nana Turtle said.

Ollie reached his paws up, up, up. “Ahhh,” he sighed. The sky looked so blue. The clouds looked like fluffy boats.

“Stretch like a tree,” she said.

Ollie stood tall and wiggled his tail like a branch in the breeze. Leaves rustled. He felt steady.

“Stretch like a tiny seed,” she said.

Ollie curled small, then slowly opened. “I feel calm,” he whispered.

Next, practice. Nana Turtle tapped the drum, boom-boom, boom. “Let the beat be your friend. Listen. Wait. Then move.”

Ollie’s whiskers twitched. He wanted to jump fast. But he listened. Boom-boom, boom.

“Step, step, wiggle,” Nana Turtle said.

Ollie waited for the beat. Step. Step. Wiggle. His paws tapped softly on the grass. Tap tap. His tail swished. Swish swish.

“Good,” said Nana Turtle. “Again. Slowly.”

Ollie’s nose smelled cookies from a neighbor’s kitchen. His tummy rumbled. He wiggled too soon. “Oops!” He stepped on a leaf and slid.

“It’s okay,” Nana Turtle said. “Patience.”

Ollie nodded. He took a deep breath. He watched a butterfly land on a yellow flower. The butterfly waited. Then it fluttered away. Slow to start. Then—whoosh!

“Like the butterfly,” Ollie whispered.

He listened to the drum. Boom-boom, boom. He counted in his head. One, two, wait. Step. Step. Wiggle. Turn. Pause. Smile.

His feet felt sure. The grass felt springy under his pads. The sun warmed his back. “I’m getting it!” Ollie beamed.

Now, rest. They sipped cool water with lemon. The cup felt chilly in his paws. “Mmm,” Ollie said. “Rest feels nice.”

Nana Turtle nodded. “Rest helps your body remember.”

Neighbors gathered by the fence. A bluebird sang from the birdbath. The backyard show was starting. Ollie’s heart went thump-thump with excited beats.

“I’m ready,” Ollie said. “But I also feel a little wiggly-worried.”

Nana Turtle patted his shoulder. “Patience is your friend. Trust your plan.”

Ollie stood in the sunny spot on the lawn. The grass tickled his toes. He saw smiling faces. He heard claps like tiny raindrops—pat, pat, pat.

He waited. He listened. Boom-boom, boom.

Ollie counted. One, two, wait.

Step. Step. Wiggle.

Turn. Pause. Smile.

He felt the beat like a gentle wave. He rode it with his paws and tail. Swish, swish. Tap, tap. He made a little star shape with his arms. He did a soft hop and landed light.

The crowd cheered. “Go, Ollie, go!” The bluebird chirped along.

Ollie wanted to zoom into a fast spin. But he remembered patience. He breathed in the warm, flowery air. He waited for the drum. Boom-boom, boom. Then he spun once, smooth and round. He finished with a calm bow.

Everyone clapped louder. Nana Turtle’s eyes shone. “That was beautiful.”

Ollie’s cheeks felt warm and happy. “I did it!” he laughed. “Being patient helped me dance better.”

Nana Turtle nodded. “You prepared. You practiced. You rested. You waited for the right moment. That is patience.”

Ollie looked at the kids watching. “Do you want to try?” he asked.

They nodded. Ollie showed them the steps. “Step, step, wiggle. Turn. Pause. Smile.” They moved together, slow and steady. The backyard felt like a happy song. The sun felt like a hug.

When the dance was done, Ollie said, “I learned that patience helps me do my best. When I wait, listen, and prepare, I feel strong and ready.”

Nana Turtle smiled. “And that makes the dance even more fun.”

Ollie grinned. “Next time, I’ll be patient from the start.”

He took one last deep breath. He smelled grass and flowers. He tasted a tiny bit of lemon on his tongue. He felt the soft breeze on his fur. He heard the friendly drum. He saw his friends smiling.

“Thank you, Patience,” he whispered.

The End.
