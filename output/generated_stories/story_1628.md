# Rocco Raccoon Finds His Voice

Rocco was a small, musical raccoon. He had soft gray fur, bright eyes, and clever little paws. Rocco loved to make music. He tapped tiny drums. He shook jingly bells. He hummed warm, happy notes that sounded like “la-la-laaa.”

Rocco lived in a comfortable nursery. The room was cozy and safe. Sunlight made soft squares on the rug. A fluffy reading chair sat by the window. Shelves held baskets of toys. There was a gentle smell of clean blankets and warm milk. Best of all, a blue box held Rocco’s instruments.

One morning, Rocco opened the blue box. Inside were the tiny drum, a wooden xylophone, and jingly, shiny bells. “Today I will play a quiet lullaby,” he whispered. “I will practice, then share.”

He planned his steps. “First, warm up,” he said. “Next, practice slow and soft. Then, ask friends to listen.” Rocco felt proud. Planning helped him feel ready.

Just then, friends came into the nursery. Pip the bunny wore a yellow bow. Tilly the turtle tucked into a purple shell. Max the kitten bounced like a spring. “Let’s play!” they all cheered.

Rocco smiled. “I want to make music. I have a plan,” he said in a calm voice.

Max grabbed the bells. They rang loud-loud-loud. “Jingle-jangle! Jingle-jangle!” Pip clapped fast. Tilly thumped the drum too hard. “BOOM! BOOM! BOOM!”

Rocco’s ears felt full. The sounds were too big. His heart beat fast. He wanted to say, “Stop!” but the word hid in his throat.

He tried to step back, but the noise followed him. “La—la—” he tried to hum, but it was lost in the jangles and booms.

Rocco took a deep belly breath. In through the nose. Out through the mouth. He put a paw on his chest and felt it go up and down. He remembered his plan. “First, warm up. Next, practice slow and soft. Then, ask friends to listen.” He needed to speak up. He needed to be brave and kind.

Rocco stood tall. He kept his voice steady and clear. “Friends,” he said, “I like music. I want to play a quiet lullaby. The loud sounds hurt my ears. Please stop for a moment.”

The room grew still. The bells stopped. The drum was quiet. Pip’s ears perked up. “Oh,” Pip said softly. “We didn’t know it was too loud.”

Max’s whiskers drooped. “Sorry, Rocco,” Max meowed. Tilly peeked from her shell. “How can we help?” she asked.

Rocco’s shoulders relaxed. He smiled. “Thank you for listening,” he said. “I have a plan. First, I warm up. Next, I practice slow and soft. Then, we can all play together—quiet and gentle, like a bedtime song.”

Pip nodded. “I can clap softly,” she said, patting her paws like tiny raindrops on the window.

Tilly touched the drum with just one finger. “Tap… tap,” she whispered. The drum made a cozy sound, like a heartbeat.

Max held the bells carefully. “Jingle,” he whispered, just one, like a star twinkling.

Rocco picked up the xylophone mallet. “Ting… ting… ting,” he played in a little rainbow of sounds. He hummed, “La-la-laaa,” low and smooth, like warm cocoa.

They all followed the plan. They played slow and soft. The nursery felt calm. The sunlight was golden. The soft rug tickled Rocco’s toes. The gentle music floated like feathers in the air.

Pip smiled. “I like your lullaby,” she said.

Max purred. “I like your plan,” he added.

Tilly nodded. “I like how you asked with kind words,” she said.

Rocco beamed. “Thank you,” he said. “It was hard to speak up. I felt shy. But I used my brave voice. I said what I needed. And I listened to you, too.”

They played the lullaby again. They took turns. Rocco counted, “One, two, play.” Pip clapped soft. Tilly tapped slow. Max jingled tiny-tiny. The sound was sweet and safe.

When they finished, they all took a bow. The room felt peaceful. The friends felt proud.

Then Rocco put the instruments back in the blue box. He made a little plan card with pictures: a drum with a tiny tap, a bell with a tiny jingle, a mouth making a calm “please.” He taped the card to the box.

“This card will help us remember,” Rocco said. “We can be gentle. We can take turns. We can say how we feel. We can also listen.”

Pip reached for a blanket. “I feel sleepy,” she giggled.

Max stretched on the rug. “The music was cozy,” he purred.

Tilly tucked into her shell with a smile. “Good plan,” she whispered.

Rocco hummed one last soft note. He felt warm and brave. He had used assertive words. He had been kind and clear. His friends were kind, too. They showed tolerance by listening and trying his quiet way. Their plan made the nursery calm and happy.

Lesson: Use your brave, kind voice to say what you need. Make a simple plan. Listen to others, and play together in a gentle, fair way.

The End.
