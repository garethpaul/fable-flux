# Toby the Tortoise's Big Game Plan

Toby the Tortoise sat in the back seat of his family's blue car, his little shell pressed against the soft cushions. The car smelled like his mom's lavender air freshener, and the gentle hum of the engine made him feel cozy.

"Are you excited for your first soccer game, Toby?" asked his dad from the driver's seat.

Toby's tummy felt all fluttery like butterflies were dancing inside. "I... I don't know, Dad. What if I'm too slow? What if the other animals laugh at me?"

His mom turned around with a warm smile. "Oh sweetie, remember what makes you special?"

Toby touched his green shell. "I'm... I'm wise?"

"That's right!" said Mom. "And wise animals know something very important. What do they know?"

Toby thought hard, watching the trees whoosh by outside the window. The leaves looked like they were waving hello to him. "Um... they know how to plan?"

"Exactly!" Dad cheered. "Tell me, what did we practice this week?"

Toby's eyes lit up like little stars. "We practiced! I learned where to stand when the ball comes to me. I learned to pass to my teammates. And I learned that soccer isn't about being the fastest!"

"What's it about then?" asked Mom, her voice gentle and encouraging.

"It's about working together and having fun!" Toby said, feeling a little braver. The car smelled like his dad's coffee, warm and comforting.

As they pulled into the parking lot, Toby saw lots of other young animals with their families. There was a little rabbit bouncing excitedly, a young bear adjusting his shin guards, and a fox practicing kicks with her dad.

"I'm still nervous," Toby admitted honestly, his voice small.

Dad parked the car and turned around. "You know what, buddy? It's okay to feel nervous. Even the best soccer players feel nervous sometimes. But you've prepared, you've practiced, and you have a plan. That's what matters."

"What's my plan again?" Toby asked, wanting to hear it one more time.

Mom counted on her fingers. "First, listen to Coach Owl. Second, stay in your position. Third, pass the ball to your teammates. Fourth, cheer for everyone. And fifth?"

"Have fun!" Toby giggled, feeling the butterflies in his tummy settle down.

As they walked toward the field, the grass felt soft and springy under Toby's feet. He could hear other families cheering and the sound of soccer balls being kicked.

"You know what?" Toby said, standing a little taller. "I think I'm ready. I have a good plan, I practiced hard, and I told you the truth about being nervous. That makes me feel strong!"

"That's our wise little tortoise!" Dad said proudly.

When Toby joined his team, he felt confident. He knew exactly what to do because he had planned and practiced. During the game, he made three great passes, helped his team score a goal, and cheered loudly for everyone.

After the game, as they drove home, Toby smiled big. "Planning really works! I felt so much better when I knew what to do."

"And being honest about your feelings helped too," added Mom.

"Yes!" Toby agreed. "Now I can't wait for next week's game. I'm going to plan and practice again!"

The End.
