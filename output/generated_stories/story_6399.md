# Koala Kiki’s Colorful Laundry Day

Kiki was a calm koala who loved to paint. Her gray fur was soft like clouds. Her round nose smelled like warm soap and sunshine. Today, Kiki padded into the laundry room with a gentle smile.

“I want to make art,” she whispered. “I want to paint a happy day.”

The laundry room hummed and whirred. The washer went swish-swish. The dryer went hum-hum. Clean towels smelled like lemon and fresh air. On a shelf, there were empty cardboard boxes, a basket of old socks, and a jar of buttons. The floor was cool on Kiki’s paws.

Kiki set a big piece of paper on the floor. “This is my art space,” she said. “I will keep it neat.” She stuck tape around the paper like a little frame, so paint wouldn’t spill too far. She put a small bowl of water to rinse her brush. She placed a cloth by her side to wipe drips. She smiled. “I will care for the planet by reusing and not wasting. I can turn boxes into shapes! I can use old socks as puppets!” Her heart felt warm.

Her friend, Pip the parrot, fluttered in and squawked softly, “Art! Art! Can I join?”

Kiki nodded. “Yes, Pip. We can share. But please ask before touching my things. Personal space and gentle hands.” She hugged herself to show her space.

Pip bobbed his head. “May I sit here?” he asked, pointing to a clean mat.

“Yes,” Kiki said. “Thank you for asking.”

Kiki dipped her brush into blue paint. Swish. The brush felt smooth and cool. “I will paint a river,” she said. She added green for trees. Dab, dab. “Trees help us breathe.” She took a deep breath. In and out. Calm and happy.

Pip watched. “May I use the yellow?” he asked.

“You may,” said Kiki. “Please keep the lid on tight when you are done. We save paint when we close the lid.”

Pip painted sunshine. The laundry room felt bright and cozy. The washer hummed a soft song. The dryer clicked, and warm air smelled like clean cotton. Kiki’s tummy rumbled.

“I need a snack,” she said. “Art needs energy.”

Kiki opened a small box with carrot sticks and apple slices. Crunch, crunch. “Healthy food helps me think and paint,” she said. She offered Pip a slice. “Would you like some apple?”

“Yes, please,” Pip chirped. “I will chew carefully and then wipe my beak.”

They wiped their hands with a cloth. “Clean hands keep the art clean,” Kiki said.

Kiki reached for red paint. But the red paint wasn’t there. The jar was open. A drip on the floor made a tiny red spot.

“Oh,” said Kiki. “We have a problem.”

Pip’s feathers fluffed. “I took the red,” he said, eyes wide. “I forgot to ask. I got too excited.”

Kiki put a paw on her chest. She took a slow breath. “I feel a little sad,” she said, “and a little worried about the floor. Please ask before you take my paint. That keeps my feelings safe and our space safe.”

“I’m sorry, Kiki,” Pip said softly. “I will ask next time. I will help clean now.”

“Thank you,” Kiki said. “Let’s solve it together.”

They pressed a clean rag on the red spot. Dab, dab, dab. The spot got smaller. Kiki poured a tiny splash of water. Swish, wipe. The floor looked clean again. Kiki smiled. “We can fix mistakes.”

Pip nodded. “May I use the red now?”

“Yes,” Kiki said. “I will hold the paper for you. Please paint on the paper only.”

Pip painted a red heart next to Kiki’s blue river and green trees. “This is our art,” Pip said.

Kiki clapped softly. “Let’s add a message.” She cut a leaf shape from a cardboard box and glued it on. “We reused the box. That helps the Earth.”

Pip glued a button sun in the corner. “We reused buttons, too!”

Kiki painted a little sign at the top: “We care for our world. We care for each other.”

The dryer beeped. Beep! Warm towels flopped into a basket. Kiki folded one and made a soft place for the art to dry. “Art needs rest, like us,” she said. “We will keep it safe.”

Kiki washed the brushes in the bowl. Swish-swish. The water turned a rainbow swirl. She poured the used water into a plant pot outside the door where it was safe for the soil, not into the street. “We protect our water,” she said.

Pip tightened all the lids. Twist, twist. “We save paint for another day.”

Kiki looked at their picture: a blue river, green trees, a button sun, a red heart, and a leaf of cardboard. It was bright and kind. “Our art tells a story,” she said. “It says: Ask before you touch. Share kindly. Eat good food to feel strong. Reuse and clean to care for Earth.”

Pip fluffed happily. “I learned to ask first. I learned to help clean. I learned that old things can become new art.”

Kiki hugged her friend gently. “I feel calm and proud,” she said. “Art helps our hearts.”

They taped their picture on the laundry room wall where the clean smell and warm hum made a cozy gallery. Kiki took one last deep breath. “Today, we solved a problem with calm voices and caring hands.”

Pip nodded. “And we made beautiful art.”

The End.
