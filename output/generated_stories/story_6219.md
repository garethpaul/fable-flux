# Monkey Miles and the Garage Gym

Miles was a courageous monkey with bright eyes and quick feet. He lived with his family near a small, sunny garage workshop. The garage smelled like pine wood and orange soap. It felt warm and safe. Tools hung on the wall like shiny silver fish. A soft mat covered the floor, and a jump rope lay in a neat loop. There was music from a little radio, a happy beat that made tails twitch.

“Today is Move Day!” Miles cheered. He clapped his hands. “I want to be strong and calm.”

Mama Monkey smiled. “Let’s make the garage a gym,” she said. “We can lift, stretch, and jump. Exercise helps our bodies and our feelings.”

Miles nodded. He felt brave. He liked to move. He liked to learn. He bounced into the garage and sniffed the lemony air. “Mmm! It smells clean and bright,” he said.

His friend Pip, a tiny mouse with big ears, peeked from a toolbox. “Squeak! Can I join?” Pip asked.

“Yes!” said Miles. “Let’s exercise together. We can take turns.”

They started with warm-ups. “Arms up, arms down,” sang Mama. Miles reached high and touched his toes. Pip wiggled his whiskers and stretched his little paws. The radio beat went thump-thump, tap-tap.

“Now we jump like frogs,” said Miles. He bent his knees and sprang. “Boing! Boing!” The mat went soft under his feet. Pip made little hops. “Boop! Boop!” They giggled.

Then they tried the jump rope. Miles held the handles. “I can do it,” he said. He swung the rope. Swish-swish. He jumped once, twice, three times. “Yes!” Miles cheered.

“May I try?” Pip asked.

Miles felt a tight squeeze inside. He liked the rope. He did not want to stop. His face grew warm. “But—I’m not done!” he said. His tail flicked. His hands gripped the handles.

Mama put a gentle hand on Miles’s shoulder. “Breathe in. Breathe out,” she said softly. “We can share. Exercise is fun together.”

Miles took a deep breath. The lemony air felt cool in his nose. He let the breath out slow. The tight squeeze got smaller. “Okay,” he said. “We can take turns. I will count for you.”

Pip smiled. “Thank you, Miles!”

Miles swung the rope for Pip. Swish-swish. Pip jumped. “One, two, three,” counted Miles. “Great jumps!”

Next, they made a game. “Garage Gym Stations!” said Mama. “We can move around the room.”

Station One was the Balance Beam, a low plank on the floor. Miles put his arms out. The wood was smooth under his feet. “Step-step,” he said. Pip tried too. “Tip-tip,” he squeaked. When Pip wobbled, Miles reached out a steady hand. “I’ve got you,” he said. They both smiled.

Station Two was the Tire Push. A small tire rolled slowly on the mat. Miles pushed with strong arms. “Grrr-rrr!” he growled playfully. His muscles felt warm. Pip helped with a tiny shoulder push. Together, they moved it. “We are a team!” said Miles.

Station Three was Calm Corner. A soft blanket lay on the floor. A picture of a happy sun hung on the wall. “We can breathe here,” said Mama. “In through the nose, out through the mouth.” They sat, breathed, and listened to the radio’s gentle beat. Tap-tap, rest-rest.

Then came the Big Build. They wanted to stack foam blocks to make a tunnel. “I will lift the big block,” said Miles. He bent his knees, just like Mama taught. “Lift with legs.” The block felt squishy and light.

Pip placed small blocks on top. “Click, click,” they went. The tunnel grew tall. Miles felt proud. He felt strong. He felt calm too.

But suddenly, the top block slipped. Thump! The tunnel fell down. Blocks rolled across the mat. Pip squeaked, “Oh no!” Miles’s heart thumped fast. His cheeks got hot. He stamped his foot. “I worked so hard!” he cried. His tail lashed.

Mama knelt beside him. “Big feeling,” she said. “Let’s cool it. Smell the flower.” She held up her fingers like a tiny flower.

Miles sniffed. In. The lemony air filled his nose.

“Blow the candle,” said Mama.

Miles blew. Out. His shoulders dropped. The heat melted away. He looked at Pip, who looked worried and small.

“I felt mad,” Miles said. “But I can try again. Will you help me?”

Pip nodded. “Squeak, yes! Together.”

“Together,” Miles agreed. They sorted the blocks. “Big on bottom, small on top,” said Miles. They counted. “One, two, three blocks.” They pressed gently. “Pat-pat.” The tunnel stood tall and steady.

Miles crawled through. “Whoosh!” he laughed. Pip scurried after him. “Zip!”

They finished with a cool-down. “Arms up, arms down,” sang Mama again. They touched toes and circled shoulders. The radio hummed softly. The garage smelled like orange soap and victory.

“How do you feel?” Mama asked.

Miles put a hand on his chest. “I feel strong,” he said. “I moved my body. I breathed when I was mad. I shared with Pip. Exercise helps my body and my feelings.”

Pip added, “Exercise is more fun together!”

Mama smiled. “That is the lesson. Move your body, share the space, breathe when big feelings come. Exercise gives us strong muscles and a calm heart.”

Miles grinned. “Tomorrow, can we make a new station?”

“Yes,” said Mama. “We can run, hop, and stretch again.”

Miles gave Pip a high-five. “Teamwork!” he said.

“Teamwork!” Pip echoed.

They turned off the radio. Tap-tap stopped. The garage felt cozy and quiet. The mat was soft under their toes. The tools on the wall shone like stars.

Miles whispered, “I am brave. I am calm. I love to move.”

The End.
