# Polly Plans a Helper Day

Sunlight sprinkled the playful living room like warm confetti. Soft pillows made little mountains. Crayons rested in a rainbow cup. The rug felt fluffy under tiny toes. In the middle of it all stood Polly, a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a curious, twinkly eye.

“Squawk! Helper Day!” Polly chirped. She loved to make art and silly songs, but today she had a new idea. “We all share this room. We all keep it nice. That’s caring for our home. That’s civic responsibility!” Her feathers rustled with pride.

Polly opened a big, friendly chart she had drawn with bold shapes. “First, we follow directions,” she said. “Then we can do some by ourselves.”

Mama’s voice came from the kitchen. “Polly, remember the living room rules.”

Polly nodded. “Yes, Mama! Rule one: toys in the bin. Rule two: books on the shelf. Rule three: ask for help if it’s too big.”

Polly flapped to the toy bin. Blocks were scattered like crunchy cereal. “Clack, clack,” went the blocks as she picked them up with her feet and beak. “One block, two block, in you go!” she sang.

A soft squeak came from behind the couch. It was Momo the plush monkey. His little button eyes looked worried. “Eee-eee. I made a mess. I took out all the stickers. I wanted to decorate. Now they’re everywhere!”

Polly looked at the floor. Stickers stuck like tiny stars on the rug. She felt a tug in her chest. “Hmm. I can help,” said Polly. “But first, we follow directions. Stickers go on paper, not on the rug.”

Momo’s ears drooped. “I forgot.”

“That’s okay,” Polly said kindly. “We can fix it. We care for our space together.”

Polly checked her chart. “Step one: ask for help. Mama!”

Mama walked in, smiling. She smelled like warm toast and honey. “What’s the plan, Polly?”

Polly stood tall. “We are doing Helper Day! We will clean and care. It is our living room. We share it. We keep it safe and tidy.”

Mama nodded. “Good plan. First, peel stickers off the rug. Put them on this paper. Then wash hands.”

“Following directions!” Polly saluted with her wing. She showed Momo how to slide a sticker off gently. “Slow and steady,” she whispered.

“Eee-eee,” said Momo, trying. Peel, peel. The room was quiet except for soft peels and little giggles when a sticker tickled their fingers.

Soon the rug felt smooth again. Polly puffed her chest. “We did it! Now, books.”

On the sofa, books sat open like little tents. Polly tapped the covers. “Books sleep on the shelf.” She made a game of it. “Goodnight, book! Goodnight, pages!” She flapped to the shelf and lined them up, small to tall, like a parade.

Momo clapped. “I can do one!” He picked up a tiny board book and carefully placed it on the shelf. He beamed. “I did it by myself!”

Polly smiled. “Self-reliance! You tried and did it.”

A crinkle sound came from the art corner. Paper scraps crunched underfoot. Polly felt a wiggle of worry. “So much to do,” she murmured.

She took a deep breath. In came the smells of crayons and soap. Out went the worry. “We can do it step by step,” she said. “I’ll sweep. You hold the bag.”

Swish, swish went the little brush. Scritch, scritch went the paper into the bag. Momo held the bag wide, like a friendly mouth.

When they were done, the living room looked bright and calm. The rug felt soft. The bookshelf stood neat. The toy bin was happy and full.

Then they heard a small whirr. The window fan was dusty. “Oh!” said Polly. “It’s too high.”

She checked her chart. “Rule three: ask for help if it’s too big.”

“Mama?” Polly called.

Mama came with a soft cloth. “I’ll do the high parts,” she said. “You can dust the low table.”

Polly dusted in little circles. Momo wiped a tiny leg on the table. They both sang a quiet dusting song. “Round and round, clean and bright, we care for our space, we do it right.”

When everything shined, Polly made a final touch. She drew a big heart on a piece of paper and taped it to the wall at kid-height. The heart said, “We Care.”

Mama clapped gently. “Polly, you led with care. You followed directions. You helped a friend. You knew when to ask for help. That is civic responsibility.”

Polly’s chest felt warm and glowy. “We all share this room,” she said softly. “We keep it safe and clean. We are helpers.”

Momo hugged Polly’s wing. “Eee-eee. I can follow the chart tomorrow too!”

Polly nodded. “Every day can be Helper Day. We can make a plan, follow directions, and try by ourselves. When it’s too big, we ask. When it’s our job, we do it.”

The playful living room hummed with happy quiet. The pillows were plump, the crayons were capped, and the air smelled like fresh lemon.

Polly looked around with pride. “Squawk! Our living room is ready for play. Ready for reading. Ready for friends.”

Mama smiled. “And ready for rest.”

Polly curled up on a soft pillow. Her feathers felt smooth. Her eyes fluttered. “We care for our home,” she whispered. “That’s how we care for each other.”

The End.
