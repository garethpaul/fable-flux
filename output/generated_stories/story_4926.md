# Brave Bubbles in the Bathroom

Little Bramble was a friendly bear cub with soft, fuzzy fur and bright, curious eyes. One rainy afternoon, Bramble padded down the hall. Drip, drop, drip! He followed the sound to the bathroom. It smelled like warm soap and minty toothpaste. The shiny tiles felt cool under his paws.

Bramble peeked in. The big white tub looked like a boat, and the mirror gleamed. But there was something new on the counter. A buzzing toothbrush hummed, “Bzzzz!” It wiggled and vibrated in its cup.

Bramble’s ears perked up. “What is that?” he whispered.

His friend, Tilly the turtle, poked her head over the edge of the sink. “It’s a toothbrush,” Tilly said softly. “It’s good for your teeth.”

Bramble took a tiny step closer. “It’s loud,” he said. “It sounds like a bee.”

Tilly nodded. “It does. But it helps make teeth strong and clean.”

Bramble wanted strong teeth. He loved munching crunchy apples and sweet berries. But the buzzing made his tummy feel wiggly. He didn’t want to say he was scared, but his paws told the truth. They felt sticky on the tile.

Mama Bear stepped into the bathroom with a warm smile. She smelled like honey and clean laundry. “Hello, my brave cub,” she said. “Time for bath and brush.”

Bramble pressed his paws together. “Mama,” he said, “the brush goes bzzzz. I don’t like the noise.”

Mama Bear knelt and gave him a gentle hug. Her fur was soft, and Bramble felt safe. “Thank you for telling me,” she said. “Being brave means trying new things even when you feel nervous. We can try together.”

Tilly climbed onto a folded towel like a tiny stage. “I’ll cheer for you,” she said. “Go, Bramble, go!”

Bramble took a deep breath. He smelled bubbles and peppermint. “Okay,” he said, “but can we make it less buzzy?”

Mama Bear nodded. “We can start with quiet. We can touch it when it is off.” She held the toothbrush out. It was smooth and a little cool. “You can feel it with your paw.”

Bramble reached out. Tap, tap. “It’s not scary when it’s still,” he said.

“Now I will turn it on, but we won’t touch yet,” said Mama Bear. She pressed the button. Bzzzz! The sound buzzed, soft like a sleepy bee. Bramble’s ears twitched. He squeezed his eyes, then peeked. “It sounds like the rain,” he said slowly. “Like the washing machine.”

Tilly swayed side to side. “Bzzzz means busy, not bad,” she sang.

Bramble giggled. “Busy, not bad.”

Mama Bear smiled. “Would you like to hold the handle while it buzzes?”

Bramble took another breath. His tummy felt wiggly, but his heart felt warm. “I will try,” he said. He wrapped his small paw around the handle. It hummed gently in his paw. “It tickles!”

Tilly clapped her little feet. “Brave Bramble!”

“Now a tiny brush on your teeth,” Mama Bear said. “We will count to five. One, two, three, four, five. If you want to stop, we stop.”

Bramble opened his mouth like a little lion. “Roar,” he whispered. Mama Bear touched the brush to his tooth.

Bzzzz, tickle-tickle!

Bramble squealed. He tasted mint. He felt fizz and foam. It was funny and strange. “It tickles my tooth!” he laughed. “Again!”

They counted together. “One, two, three, four, five.” The bubbles made tiny pops. Bramble could hear the water dripping, the brush humming, his own giggles.

“Switch sides,” Mama Bear said. “You are doing it!”

Bramble nodded. “I am brave,” he said proudly. “Busy, not bad.”

They finished brushing, top and bottom, little and big teeth. Then Mama Bear turned off the brush. The sudden quiet felt soft, like a blanket. Bramble swished water. Swish, swish! He spat into the sink. Plink!

He looked in the mirror. His smile looked shiny and strong. “Hello, clean teeth,” he said.

“Hello, brave bear,” said Tilly.

Next was bath time. Bramble slid into warm water. The bubbles were like tiny snowflakes on his fur. “The water is a hug,” he said. He played with a yellow duck that went squeak. He made a bubble hat. He washed behind his ears and under his chin. He sang, “Scrub-a-dub, brave and clean!”

Tilly floated on a soap dish like a little boat. “You showed your feelings,” she said. “You tried a little, then a little more.”

Bramble nodded. “My tummy felt wiggly, but I still tried. That is brave.”

When bath time was done, Mama Bear wrapped Bramble in a fluffy towel. It felt warm and cozy. She kissed his forehead. “I am proud of you,” she said. “You listened, you learned, and you took care of yourself. That is wellness.”

Bramble wiggled his toes. “I was curious. I asked questions. I tried new things. And I had help from my friend.” He smiled at Tilly. “Thank you for cheering.”

Tilly beamed. “Any time.”

Bramble looked at the toothbrush on the counter. It wasn’t scary now. It was a helper. “Tomorrow,” he said, “I can do it again. I can be brave again.”

Mama Bear nodded. “Bravery grows when we practice.”

Bramble took a final deep breath. Peppermint and soap filled the air. His heart felt big and calm. “Busy, not bad,” he whispered. “Brave and clean.”

He padded out of the bathroom, tail high, feeling strong, curious, and proud.

The clear lesson: Being brave means trying new things even when you feel nervous. Ask for help, take small steps, and you can do it.

The End.
