# Gigi the Giraffe's Clean-and-Creative Garage

Gigi the giraffe was tall and thoughtful. Her long neck peeked through the high window of the garage workshop. Sunlight sprinkled dust like tiny stars. The garage smelled like warm wood, lemon soap, and a little bit of paint. Today was a busy day.

“Good morning, tools,” Gigi said kindly. “We have a big plan.”

Gigi loved to make art from old boxes, buttons, and ribbon. She also loved to help her friends. Today she wanted to build a bright sign for the neighborhood playground that said, “Wash, Rinse, Smile!”

She looked at the big clock on the wall. It went tick-tock, tick-tock. Gigi tapped her hoof and thought, I can do it if I make a plan.

She drew a simple list on a big paper:

1. Wash up
2. Set a timer
3. Create the sign
4. Clean the mess

Her ears wiggled with calm. “Step by step,” she whispered.

Gigi put on her blue apron. It was soft and smelled clean. She tied it carefully. Then she filled a little bucket at the sink. Swish, swish! The water was cool and sparkly.

“Time to wash up,” Gigi said. She pumped soap. Squish! The bubbles smelled like oranges. She scrubbed her hooves and her long, gentle hands. Scrub, scrub, scrub! She sang a song, “Tops and bottoms, in between, scrub until my hands are clean.” She counted, “One… two… three…” all the way to twenty. Rinse, rinse. Dry, dry with a fluffy towel.

Her friend, Momo the mouse, peeked in through the door. “Hi, Gigi! Can I help?” Momo squeaked.

“Yes, please!” said Gigi. “First, we wash. Clean hands help us stay healthy.”

Momo nodded. “Healthy hands, happy hearts!” He washed his tiny paws with a big grin.

Gigi smiled. “Now we set our timer. We have one hour before lunchtime.” She turned the little kitchen timer. Tick-tick-tick. It blinked. “The timer helps us use our time wisely,” she explained.

On the big table, Gigi put out colored markers, bright paints, and a clean wooden board. She smelled the pine wood—sweet and fresh. “Let’s begin,” she said softly.

Gigi painted the word Wash in big, blue letters. Stroke, stroke. Smooth and steady. Momo dotted little bubbles around the word. Dot, dot, dot! They looked like floating soap circles.

“Rinse,” Gigi painted in green. Swish! “Smile,” she wrote in yellow sunshine letters. She added a little giraffe brushing teeth and a splashy sink.

Momo giggled. “Your giraffe is smiling with so many shiny teeth!”

Gigi laughed too. “Teeth feel happy when they’re clean.” She held up a small toothbrush from her supply box. “After we paint, we’ll brush our teeth and wash our hands again. Paint can be messy.”

Just then, the garage door creaked. “Eee-eee.” In came Tika the turtle and Benny the bunny. They wore curious faces.

“What are you making?” asked Benny.

“A sign for the playground,” said Gigi. “It says, ‘Wash, Rinse, Smile!’ It will help everyone remember to keep clean.”

Tika looked at his paws. “I touched chalk. They feel dusty.”

Gigi nodded kindly. “Let’s take a wash break.” Everyone lined up at the sink. Pump, pump went the soap. Scrub, scrub went the hands. Tika liked the warm water. Benny loved the soft towel. Momo hummed the washing song. Gigi checked the clock. Still plenty of time.

Back at the table, they added ribbon borders and star stickers. Gigi guided them. “Place the stars in a row. We can count them.” They counted together, “One, two, three, four, five!” Counting made the work feel calm and fun.

The timer went ding! “Time check,” said Gigi. “We have ten minutes to finish and five minutes to clean.” She pointed to the list. “Step by step.”

They all nodded. Momo glued the last ribbon. Tika signed his name with a tiny turtle shell doodle. Benny added a smiling soap bar.

Gigi looked closely. “Beautiful! Now we clean our space.” She passed out wipes and a little broom. Swish went the broom. Wipe went the table. Cap, cap went the paints.

“Don’t forget ourselves,” said Gigi gently. “Clean space, clean hands, clean smiles.” She led them to the sink again. Scrub, rinse, dry. Gigi also brushed her teeth. She used a pea-sized bit of minty paste. Brush front teeth, brush back teeth, tiny circles. She counted to twenty again. Rinse. Smile!

Her friends followed, brushing gently with tiny brushes Gigi kept in a clean cup. The garage smelled minty and bright.

They carried the sign outside. The sun warmed their backs. The playground sparkled in the light. Gigi hung the sign near the water fountain.

Benny read it slowly. “‘Wash, Rinse, Smile!’ It makes me feel happy,” he said.

Tika touched his clean paws together. “My hands feel fresh.”

Momo grinned. “We finished on time!”

Gigi’s heart felt warm. “We made art. We used our time well. And we kept our bodies clean. That keeps us healthy.”

They all took a deep breath. The breeze smelled like grass and lemon soap. Then they laughed and clapped. The playground bell rang for lunch, right on time.

As they waved goodbye, Gigi said, “Remember: Wash before you start, wash when you’re done, and smile in between.”

The friends nodded and skipped away, hands clean, teeth shiny, and hearts proud.

The End.
