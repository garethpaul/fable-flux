# Polly Paints the Busy Basement

In a busy basement full of boxes, tools, and tick-tock clocks, lived a creative parrot named Polly. The basement smelled like warm wood and fresh paint. Lights buzzed softly. Fans whirred. Tiny dust sparkles danced in the light.

Polly loved to make art. She wore a tiny blue scarf and kept bright feathers tidy. “Squawk! Colors, colors!” Polly chirped. She set out paper, brushes, and safe, washable paint on a low table. Drip, drop! Red, blue, yellow plopped onto a tray.

Today, friends came to visit. Tumble the turtle slid slowly across the cool floor. “Hello, Polly,” Tumble said, voice calm and low.

Bree the bunny bounced-bounce-bounced, ears flopping. “Let’s paint fast!” Bree giggled.

Squeak the mouse peeked from a small box. “Is it safe?” Squeak asked in a tiny voice.

“It is safe,” Polly said kindly. “We will paint together. We will share. We will be fair.”

The basement was busy. A washing machine hummed, “Hmmmm.” A dryer clicked, “Clack, clack.” Air smelled like soap and lemon. The noise made Squeak’s whiskers twitch. Bree’s feet thumped-thump from excitement. Tumble blinked slowly and waited.

Polly took a big, calm breath in. She let a soft breath out. “We can do this,” she said. “We can take turns. We can listen. We can be kind.”

They began. Bree dipped a brush and whooshed bright swirls. “Wheee!”

Squeak wanted a tiny brush. “I like small dots,” Squeak said. “Pop, pop, pop.”

Tumble wanted to paint slow lines. “Smooth and steady,” Tumble murmured.

Soon, three voices filled the busy room.

“My turn!” Bree shouted.

“No loud, please,” Squeak squeaked, covering ears. “Too fast, too noisy.”

“I will wait,” Tumble said. “But I do want a blue.”

Polly felt her tummy flutter. Her wings ruffled. The humming, the clacking, the voices—so much at once! She wanted to shout, “Stop!” But she paused. She placed her feet firm on the floor. She folded her wings and took three soft breaths. In. Out. In. Out. In. Out.

“I can choose calm,” Polly told herself. “I can decide with care.”

Polly tapped the table gently. Tap, tap. “Friends,” she said in a warm voice, “we are different. Bree likes fast. Squeak likes quiet. Tumble likes slow. Different is okay. We can be fair.”

“How?” Bree asked, bouncing smaller bounces.

“We can make a plan,” Polly said. “We can take turns. We can make a quiet sign. We can share colors.”

Polly drew a tiny chart with pictures. She drew Bree’s ears, Squeak’s whiskers, Tumble’s shell, and her own bright feathers. She pointed to each picture in turn. “First Bree, then Squeak, then Tumble, then Polly,” she said. “We each get a turn with the big brush and the blue. When it is not your turn, you can choose a small brush, a new color, or you can stretch and breathe.”

Squeak still looked worried. The machines hummed and clacked. “The noise,” Squeak whispered.

Polly nodded. “Good telling,” she said. “We can make a quiet corner.” She laid a soft towel by a box and set out cotton earmuffs. “This is safe and cozy. You can rest there any time.”

Squeak smiled a tiny smile. “Thank you.”

Bree wiggled. “Can I help?” Bree asked.

“Yes,” Polly said. “You can be the ‘Turn Helper.’ When you hear me tap—tap, tap—you say, ‘Switch, please,’ in a friendly voice.”

Bree stood tall. “I can do that!”

Tumble raised a slow hand. “I can remind us to breathe.”

Polly’s feathers smoothed. “We have a plan,” she said. “That is fair.”

They tried it. Bree whooshed bright swirls for one minute. Tap, tap. “Switch, please,” Bree said softly. Squeak took the big brush next but used tiny strokes. Pop, pop. When the hum grew loud, Squeak padded to the quiet corner, put on earmuffs, and took a calm breath. Tumble painted slow waves like the sea. Polly dotted stars.

Sometimes Bree forgot and bounced too big. Her tail bumped the water cup. Splash! Water dripped on the floor.

Polly blinked. She felt a fast flutter again. She chose to pause. She took one long breath. “Accidents happen,” she said. “We can clean together.”

“I’m sorry,” Bree said, ears drooping.

“It’s okay,” Polly said. “Thank you for saying sorry. Let’s fix it.” They wiped the spill. The floor felt cool and smooth under the cloth. The smell of lemon came back.

Time passed gently. The chart helped. The taps helped. The breathing helped. Little by little, the busy basement felt friendly, not too loud and not too fast. Their picture grew and grew: fast swirls, tiny dots, slow waves, and twinkly stars. It looked like a rainbow ocean sky.

At last, they stepped back. The machines grew quiet. The air felt calm.

“We did it,” Tumble said, smiling.

“We were fair,” Squeak said, cuddling the earmuffs.

“We made choices,” Bree said. “I used a quiet voice. I took turns.”

Polly fluffed her feathers. “We practiced tolerance,” she said. “That means we accept that friends can be different. We listen. We share. We wait. We are kind.”

Bree nodded. “Different is okay.”

Squeak nodded. “Different is good.”

Tumble nodded. “Different makes beauty.”

Polly hung the art to dry. Drip, drip, drip. The colors glowed. “When we use gentle words, take turns, and take calm breaths,” Polly said, “we turn a busy basement into a brave, kind place.”

They touched wings, paws, and flippers in a little circle. “Together,” they said.

The End.
