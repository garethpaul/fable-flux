# Pippa the Parrot Discovers a Better Way

Pippa was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a rainbow tail. She loved colors. She loved the way red was warm like a hug, and blue felt cool like water on a hot day. Pippa flapped into the creative art room one sunny morning and breathed in the sweet smell of glue and the lemony scent of clean tables. The room was full of paper, paints, pom-poms, and soft brushes. Sunlight made tiny sparkles on the jars of glitter.

“Good morning, colors!” Pippa squawked happily. “I can’t wait to make!”

Ms. Dot, the gentle art teacher, smiled and tapped a bell that made a soft ding. “Today, we will make collage pictures,” she said. “We will cut, glue, and share the supplies. Remember to use kind hands and kind words.”

Pippa bobbed her head. “Kind hands, kind words. Got it!”

Her friends, Mousey Max and Bunny Bea, sat nearby. Max rubbed his tiny paws together and said, “I love shiny paper.” Bea’s long ears wiggled. “I love fluffy pom-poms.”

Pippa loved everything. She discovered a basket of bright buttons—clicky, round, lovely buttons. They were red, blue, yellow, and sparkly silver. “Ooooh!” Pippa whispered. “I discovered treasure!”

Pippa wanted all the buttons. She scooped the whole basket with her wings and brought it to her spot. “I will use them all,” she chirped, eyes wide. “My picture will be amazing.”

Max looked up. “Can I have two buttons?” he asked softly.

Bea leaned close. “I want a yellow one, please.”

Pippa felt a big burst of excitement in her chest. “I found them,” she said. “I discovered them. I need them.”

She glued buttons onto her paper in a shiny, bumpy path. Click. Plop. Press. The glue smelled sweet, and the buttons felt cool on her feathers. Her collage grew and grew, but Max and Bea were quiet now. Max’s whiskers drooped. Bea’s ears went flat.

Ms. Dot walked by and looked at the table. “Pippa,” she said gently, “I see you discovered the buttons. I’m glad you looked and found something new. But I also see friends who need some too.”

Pippa stopped. She looked at Max’s empty paper. She looked at Bea’s hopeful eyes. She felt a flutter-frown in her tummy. “But… I found them,” she repeated, softer now.

Ms. Dot nodded. “Discovery is wonderful,” she said. “You looked. You noticed. You were curious. That is how artists learn. And discovery can be kind. We can share what we find. When we share, others can discover with us.”

Pippa blinked. She hadn’t thought of that. She picked up one button and listened. The room hummed with quiet voices. Scissors snipped. Brushes swished. Pippa heard the tiny clink of the button in her wing. She thought about how Max loved shiny paper and how Bea loved pom-poms. She thought about how she would feel if someone kept all the rainbow feathers.

Her heart felt heavy. “Oh,” she said. “I didn’t think about you.”

Pippa turned to Max and Bea. Her voice was small but brave. “I am sorry,” she said. “I didn’t share the buttons. I didn’t use kind hands. I didn’t use kind words. I was excited, and I forgot about your feelings.”

Max’s whiskers lifted. “Thank you for saying sorry,” he said.

Bea smiled and touched Pippa’s wing. “That was kind.”

Pippa took a deep breath. “I want to make it better,” she said. “Let’s discover together.” She pushed the basket to the middle of the table. The buttons made a cheerful clatter. “We can sort them by color. We can count them. We can share them fair.”

Max clapped. “I can count,” he said. “One, two, three!”

Bea bounced. “I can sort. Yellow here, blue here, red here!”

Pippa felt the heavy feeling float away, like a balloon rising. “I can pass them around,” she said. “We each get some. And we can trade if we want.”

They worked together. The buttons clicked and chimed as they moved. Pippa discovered that red looked happy next to yellow. Bea discovered that small buttons made tiny, cute eyes on her bunny picture. Max discovered that two silver buttons made the perfect moon.

Ms. Dot came back and smiled. “I see a team,” she said. “I see sharing and care.”

Pippa’s chest felt warm, like sunshine through the window. “Discovery is better with friends,” she said. “I learned that when I find something, I can ask, ‘Who else wants to discover this with me?’”

When they finished, Pippa’s collage showed a button path that led to a painted rainbow nest. Max’s picture had a shiny moon over a blue pond. Bea’s picture was a fluffy bunny with silly button eyes. They held up their art, and the room seemed to sparkle.

Pippa turned to Max and Bea. “Thank you for forgiving me,” she said.

Max nodded. “Thank you for sharing.”

Bea hugged her. “Thank you for caring.”

Pippa smiled with her bright beak. “Next time I discover something new, I will share my find. I will say sorry if I forget. I will listen to your feelings.”

Ms. Dot rang the bell. Ding. “Artists,” she said, “today you discovered colors, shapes, and the power of kindness.”

Pippa flapped her rainbow tail. “Yes,” she chirped. “Discovery, apology, and empathy. That is our art.”

The End.
