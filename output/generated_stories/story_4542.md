# Miko’s Little Green Dinner

Miko was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived behind the big clock in a warm, cozy dining room. Every evening, Miko peeked out to see the family eat dinner at the long wooden table. The room smelled like fresh bread, sweet apples, and warm soup. The lights were gentle, and the tablecloth felt smooth under Miko’s tiny paws.

One day, Miko heard Mama say, “Let’s set the table. It’s time for dinner!”

The children, Lily and Ben, ran in. Their feet made soft thump-thump sounds on the floor. Miko watched from his tiny hole and whispered, “I want to help too. I can be a helper mouse.”

Miko scurried out when no one was looking. He tugged a light napkin to the edge of the table leg. “Little napkins for little messes,” he squeaked. He heard water running in the kitchen and thought, That sounds like a lot of water.

Miko called softly, “Lily?”

Lily looked down and smiled. “Oh! Hello, little mouse.”

“I’m Miko,” he said with a shy twitch of his whiskers. “May I help?”

Ben crouched with wide eyes. “You can help me carry the napkins.”

Miko beamed. “I like to help. Helping is kind.”

When dinner began, the room was full of happy sounds. Spoons clinked. Plates slid. The soup smelled like carrots and peas. The bread was warm and fluffy. Miko sat near the table leg and watched.

After a while, Lily pushed away her plate. “I’m full,” she said. “I can’t finish my peas.”

Miko peeped, “Can we save them?”

“Save them?” asked Ben.

“Yes,” Miko said. “Saving food is good. We do not waste. Wasting is not kind to our world.”

Mama smiled. “You’re right, Miko. We can save the peas in a little container.”

Miko sighed with relief. “Thank you. That is good for our Earth.”

When dinner was done, Papa picked up the dirty plates. He turned on the faucet. Water rushed fast and loud. Miko’s ears perked up. “Splash-splash! That is a lot of water.”

Papa paused. “Should I turn it down?”

Miko nodded. “Yes, please. We can use just what we need. We can make the water go shhh, not WHOOSH.”

Papa turned the water to a small, gentle stream. “Better?”

“Much better,” said Miko. “We care for water. Water helps plants grow. Water helps us too.”

Lily said, “What else can we do, Miko?”

Miko’s nose twitched. He smelled the apple peels in a bowl. They smelled sweet and fresh. “Those peels can go to a compost,” he said.

“What’s compost?” asked Ben.

“It is a special place for old food,” Miko explained. “It turns into rich, dark soil. The soil helps plants. Plants give us food and air. It is a big, kind circle.”

Mama clapped softly. “We have a small compost bin. Let’s use it.”

They put the apple peels and carrot tops into the bin. The bin made a soft thud as it closed. Miko felt proud. His heart felt warm and light, like a small sun.

Then Miko saw the napkins. Some were still clean. Some had tiny crumbs. “We can use cloth napkins again,” he said. “We can shake the crumbs outside for the birds.”

Ben opened the door. Cool night air brushed Miko’s whiskers. The sky smelled like fresh rain. They shook the crumbs gently. Tiny chirps answered from the dark. “Thank you, kind birds,” Miko whispered.

Back inside, Lily wiped the table with a damp cloth. “I’m helping!” she sang.

“That is a good chore,” said Miko. “Chores keep us healthy and calm. Clean spaces make our hearts feel good.”

Papa turned off the big light. The room glowed softly with a small lamp. “We can use one light,” he said. “It saves energy.”

Miko smiled. “Yes. We use only what we need. That is caring.”

Ben yawned. “I feel cozy.”

“Me too,” said Lily. “Miko, I like helping. It feels nice.”

Miko nodded. “Helping is wellness. It helps our bodies and our hearts. It helps our Earth too.”

Mama gave Miko a tiny, clean bottle cap with warm water. “Here is a little drink.”

Miko sipped. The water felt cool on his tongue. “Thank you,” he said. “You are kind.”

Before bed, they all checked the dining room. The table was clean. The leftovers were in containers. The compost bin was full of peels and tops. The faucet was off. The light was soft.

Miko stood tall and said, “Today we learned to be gentle with our world. We saved food. We saved water. We used the compost. We used one light. We shared. We cleaned up. We were kind.”

Lily whispered, “Little steps make a big difference.”

“Yes,” Miko said. “Little steps, little paws.”

He waved his tiny paw and scurried back to his cozy spot behind the clock. The room smelled like soap and apples. It felt peaceful and safe.

Miko curled up and closed his eyes. He felt happy. He felt proud. He dreamed of green gardens, singing birds, and a bright, kind Earth.

The End.
