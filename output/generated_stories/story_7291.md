# Leo Helps at the Dinner Table

The brave little lion cub was named Leo. Leo’s paws were soft, and his fur was warm like sunshine. One evening, Leo padded into the dining room. The big table was shiny and smooth. He could smell warm veggie soup, sweet apples, and fresh bread. The chairs made a gentle scrape on the floor. The room felt cozy and bright.

Mama Lion said, “Dinner is almost ready!”

Papa Lion said, “We have guests tonight.”

Leo’s ears perked up. He loved guests. He loved stories, too. But the table was bare. No plates. No spoons. No napkins. The soup pot bubbled, “Blub, blub.”

Mama Lion sighed. “Oh dear. We are running late.”

Leo felt a brave idea roar inside his chest. “I can help! I will be a Helper Hero!”

Mama Lion smiled. “Thank you, Leo. That is volunteering. Volunteering means helping because you want to.”

Leo stood tall. “I want to help!”

He imagined he wore a bright red helper cape. In his mind, the dining room turned into a busy helper station. The chairs were friendly trees. The table was a wide, kind hill. Leo’s heart went thump-thump with excitement.

He trotted to the cupboard. “Plates, please,” he whispered.

The plates felt cool and smooth. Clink, clink. He set them around the table with care. “One for Mama, one for Papa, one for each guest, and one for me,” he counted softly.

“Good counting,” said Papa Lion.

“Now forks and spoons,” said Leo. He imagined the spoons were little moons and the forks were tiny sunbeams. The metal felt chilly in his paws. Tink, tink. He lined them up, left and right, neat and straight.

Mama Lion stirred the soup. “Thank you, Leo. This helps my feelings feel calm.”

Leo liked that. Helping felt warm inside, like a snuggle. He took napkins next. They were soft like clouds. He folded them into simple triangles. “Triangle napkins for everyone,” he said.

Just then, the doorbell rang—ding-dong! The guests arrived: Zebra Zoe, Giraffe Gabe, and Turtle Tia. “Hello!” they called, smiling.

“Welcome,” said Papa Lion. “Please, have a seat.”

Zebra Zoe looked at the table. “Oh! I like my fork on the right,” she said kindly. “At home, we do it that way.”

Giraffe Gabe said, “I like extra napkins, please. My long neck can be, um, drippy.”

Turtle Tia peeked up. “I eat slowly. Could I have a small spoon?”

Leo felt a little swirl in his tummy. He wanted everyone to be happy. But everyone wanted different things. He took a brave breath. “We can work it out,” he said. “Let’s talk and make a plan.”

He remembered a special helper word: negotiate. Mama Lion taught him it means talking and listening to find a way that works.

Leo said, “Zoe, your fork can be on the right. Gabe, I’ll bring extra napkins. Tia, I will find a small spoon.” He looked at everyone. “Is that okay?”

They all nodded. “Thank you, Leo,” they said together.

Leo padded back to the drawer. The small spoon made a tiny clink. He set Zoe’s fork on the right side. He placed a little tower of napkins by Gabe’s plate. He imagined he was a gentle waiter in a friendly jungle café. The dining room smelled like soup and bread and apples. His tummy rumbled—grr-rumble!

When they sat down, Mama Lion said, “Before we eat, let’s stretch.” Everyone stretched their paws, hooves, neck, and shell. “Ahhh,” they sighed. Leo learned that a small stretch helps the body feel well. Wellness is taking care of our bodies and feelings.

They began to eat. “Mmm,” said Zoe. “The soup tastes cozy.”

“Crunch,” said Gabe, biting an apple slice.

“Sip, sip,” said Tia with her small spoon.

Papa Lion said, “Leo, you helped set the table. You listened to everyone. You found a way for all.”

Leo’s whiskers twitched with pride. “I liked helping. It made my heart feel big.”

Turtle Tia smiled. “You were patient with me. Thank you.”

Zebra Zoe said, “You respected my way. That is very kind.”

Giraffe Gabe added, “You used your imagination, too. I saw your helper cape!”

Leo giggled. “Helping is fun. Volunteering makes dinner better.”

When dinner was done, there were crumbs and little drips. Leo stood up. “I will volunteer again. I can carry plates to the sink.”

“Let’s all help,” said Mama Lion.

They made a happy line. Clink, step, clink, step. The warm water whooshed. The soap smelled like lemons. Splish, splash, swish!

Soon the table was clean and shiny. The dining room felt calm and bright again.

Mama Lion gave Leo a gentle hug. “You were brave, kind, and smart. You used negotiation and tolerance. You cared about everyone’s needs.”

Leo hugged back. “Helping made me strong on the inside.”

Papa Lion said, “That is the magic of volunteering.”

Leo smiled. He imagined his red helper cape fluttering. “Tomorrow, I can help again,” he said. “I can set the table, share snacks, or read a story to Tia.”

Everyone cheered, “Helper Hero!”

Leo’s heart purred. He learned that volunteering means choosing to help, listening to others, and taking care of bodies and feelings. Helping together makes the room—and the world—feel warm and good.

The End.
