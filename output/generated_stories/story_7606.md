# Miko the Rooftop Helper

Miko was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright button eyes. He lived on a sunny rooftop garden. The roof felt warm under his tiny paws. The air smelled like mint leaves and sweet tomatoes. Bees buzzed. A little fountain splish-splashed. Miko loved his rooftop home.

Every morning, Miko did exercise. He ran along the long roof ledge. Tap tap tap went his feet. He stretched his tail. He wiggled his whiskers. “Strong body, strong helper,” he said proudly.

One day, the sun was very hot. The fountain gurgled less. The plants drooped. The mint smelled faint and tired. “Oh no,” said Miko. “The water is running low.”

Miko peeked into the fountain. Drip. Drip. Just little drops. He heard a soft cry. “I am thirsty,” sighed a tiny sparrow. “Tweet, tweet.” A beetle clicked its shiny wings. “I am hot.” The tomato vines rustled. “We need a drink.”

Miko’s heart felt full and kind. “I want to help,” he said. He looked at the water bucket by the door. It was heavy and big.

Miko took a deep breath. “I can do it,” he said. He tried to lift the bucket. “Oof!” It was too heavy. Water sloshed. A little splash wet his paws. He slipped and fell on his fluffy bottom.

Miko sat up. He felt sad and a little scared. “I want to save water, and I want to help my friends,” he whispered. “But I can’t carry this big bucket.”

Just then, the door creaked. Mrs. Nia, the roof gardener, stepped out. She wore a wide hat and a bright smile. “Hello, little neighbor,” she said. “Is everything okay?”

Miko’s ears drooped. He took a brave breath. “Mrs. Nia, I tried to move the big bucket,” he said honestly. “I wanted to give water. I slipped. I am sorry.”

Mrs. Nia knelt down. Her hat smelled like straw. Her voice was warm. “Thank you for telling the truth,” she said. “You are honest and kind. Let’s think of a better way.”

Miko wiped his paws. “We need to save water,” he said. “The sun is hot, and the fountain is slow.”

“You are right,” said Mrs. Nia. “We can use less water and still help.” She pointed to a big blue barrel. “This is a rain barrel. It catches rain from the roof. That is called conservation. We save water for dry days like today.”

Miko’s eyes shone. “We can use the rain water!”

“Yes,” said Mrs. Nia, “but we must share and plan. That is a good compromise. We water a little in the morning and a little at night, when the sun is soft. We give sips, not gulps. We can also cover the soil with straw to keep it cool.”

Miko nodded. “Sips, not gulps,” he repeated. “And straw to keep water in.”

Miko wanted to help more. He needed practice. Mrs. Nia found a tiny cup, just mouse-size. It was light and easy to hold. “Let’s practice carrying water, little by little,” she said.

Miko dipped the tiny cup in the rain barrel. Swish. The water felt cool. A drop tickled his whiskers. “Hee-hee!” he giggled. He walked slowly. Tap… tap… tap. The cup did not spill.

“Great job,” said Mrs. Nia. “Slow steps. Strong steps.”

Miko made a little plan. He drew tiny boxes in chalk on a flat brick. “One cup for mint. One cup for tomatoes. One cup for the bird bath,” he said. “We share.”

He carried cup after cup. Swish. Tap. Swish. Tap. The sparrow drank a tiny sip. “Tweet, thank you!” The beetle fanned cool air with its wings. The mint stood up straighter and smelled fresh again. The tomatoes glowed red and happy.

Miko’s legs felt warm and strong from all the moving. “This is good exercise,” he puffed, smiling. “My body gets strong, and my friends get water.”

Mrs. Nia spread straw around the plants. Rustle, rustle. “This keeps the soil cool,” she said. “It helps us save water.”

Miko found a little leak in the fountain hose. Drip. Drip. “Mrs. Nia, look,” he said.

“Good eyes,” said Mrs. Nia. She turned off the hose and fixed the leak with a snug band. “No more waste,” she said. “That is conservation too.”

When the sun began to set, the air felt gentle. The sky turned pink and gold. The rooftop smelled sweet and green. Miko and Mrs. Nia sat and listened. Bees hummed softly. The fountain bubbled again. The sparrow sang a happy song.

“I learned a lot today,” said Miko. “I learned to tell the truth. I learned to practice with small cups. I learned to share and make a plan. I learned to save water.”

Mrs. Nia nodded. “You found a problem and a solution,” she said. “You were honest, you practiced, you made a fair compromise, and you helped everyone.”

Miko curled his tail and smiled. “Sips, not gulps,” he whispered. “Save water, help friends, and try, try, try.”

The rooftop felt safe and bright. Miko’s heart felt light. He looked at the rain barrel, the happy plants, and his tiny cup.

“We did it,” he said.

The End.
