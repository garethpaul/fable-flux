# Nutmeg's Big Workshop Day

The morning sun made tiny sparkles on the garage door. Inside the garage workshop, it smelled like fresh wood and orange soap. Tiny dust specks floated in the warm light. Tools hung on a wall like shiny silver stars.

A resourceful squirrel named Nutmeg lived in a nest outside the window. Nutmeg had bright eyes, soft brown fur, and a bushy tail that flicked when she was excited. Today, Nutmeg was very excited.

“Today is fixing day!” Nutmeg chattered. “I will make something useful.”

Nutmeg scampered inside through a little crack. Tap-tap went her paws on the smooth floor. She looked around carefully. A soft blue cloth, a roll of tape, a wooden box, buttons, rubber bands, and little screws waited on a table. A radio hummed a gentle tune. The fan went whirr, whirr.

Just then, two neighbors came to the open door. Pip the mouse wore a tiny red scarf. Tilly the turtle had a yellow shell with a small chip on the side.

“Hi, Nutmeg,” Pip squeaked. “My wagon wheel is loose.”

“Hello,” Tilly said slowly. “My shell chip keeps catching on my blanket. Can you help?”

Nutmeg’s tail swished. “I can try. I like to learn. I like to make. Let’s see.”

Pip rolled in a little wooden wagon. The wheel wobbled. Wobble, wobble. Nutmeg touched it gently. “Hmm,” she said. “We need a peg.”

She found a short stick and a tiny screw. She held the screw in her little paws. Tap-tap with a small hammer. Twist-twist with a toy screwdriver. The wagon wheel turned. Spin! It felt smooth.

Pip’s eyes shone. “You did it!” He hopped in and rolled a little. “Whee!”

Nutmeg smiled, but then she looked at Tilly’s shell. The chip was sharp. “I want to help you too,” Nutmeg said. “But I am not sure how yet.”

“It’s okay,” Tilly said kindly. “We can think together.”

Nutmeg took a breath. She listened to the soft radio. She smelled the sweet wood. She felt brave, but also a tiny bit worried. “What if I can’t fix it?” she whispered.

Pip nodded. “It’s okay to not know right away,” he said. “We accept learning steps.”

Nutmeg blinked. Those words felt warm, like a cozy blanket. “Yes,” she said. “We accept where we are. We can learn.”

Nutmeg tried a piece of soft blue cloth. She pressed it on the chip. It fell off. Plop.

“That didn’t work,” she said.

“That’s a good try,” Tilly said. “What next?”

Nutmeg tried tape. She pressed the tape over the cloth. It stuck for a moment, then peeled. Peel, peel.

Nutmeg’s ears drooped. “Oh,” she sighed.

Pip rolled his wagon close. “Let’s test smaller pieces,” he suggested. “Little steps!”

Nutmeg cut a tiny circle from the cloth. Snip, snip. She found a smooth button, round and gentle. She wrapped the cloth around the button like a soft cap. Then she used a rubber band to hold it tight. Stretch, snap! Not too tight, just right.

She looked at Tilly. “May I try this soft cap on your shell chip?”

“Yes,” said Tilly.

Nutmeg pressed the soft button-cap on the chip. She added a dab of kid-safe glue from a little bottle on the shelf. Dab, dab. They waited. The fan whispered. The radio sang. The glue dried.

Tilly took a tiny step. The cap stayed. Tilly brushed the blanket over her shell. No snag! “It feels smooth,” Tilly said, smiling. “It doesn’t hurt.”

Nutmeg’s tail puffed with joy. “We did it together!” she chirped.

From the corner, a big wooden stool wobbled. Wobble, wobble. Nutmeg noticed. “Should we help the stool too?” she asked.

Pip and Tilly nodded. Nutmeg crouched and felt the short leg. She slid a little cardboard square under it. Still wobbly. She tried a thicker piece. Still wobbly. She paused.

“It is okay to try many times,” Nutmeg said. “I accept that practice takes time.”

She stacked two thin wooden coins, smooth and light. Tap-tap with a bit of glue. The stool stood steady. No wobble at all.

Nutmeg giggled. “Steady as a tree!”

Pip clapped his tiny paws. Tilly hummed a happy tune.

When the sun moved higher, the garage felt cozy and bright. The tools looked friendly now. The air smelled like warm wood and a little bit of glue. Nutmeg packed the extra pieces into the wooden box. She labeled it with big letters: Fix-It Friends.

Pip tugged his wagon. Tilly moved with ease. Nutmeg walked them to the door.

“Thank you, Nutmeg,” said Tilly. “You helped me feel safe.”

“Thank you,” said Pip. “You showed me how to try, and try again.”

Nutmeg looked at her friends. “Thank you for helping me too,” she said. “I learned that it is okay to not know at first. We accept ourselves, and we learn new skills. Little steps can do big things.”

They all smiled. The fan went whirr, and a gentle breeze brushed Nutmeg’s fur. Outside, leaves rustled like soft applause.

That night, Nutmeg tucked a note into her nest. It said, “Accept, learn, practice, and help.” Her heart felt calm and proud.

The next morning would bring new fixing fun. Nutmeg was ready, one small step at a time.

The End.
