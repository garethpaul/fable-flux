# Ruby Rabbit's Rooftop Library

Ruby Rabbit lived in a cozy burrow beneath Mrs. Chen's apartment building. Every morning, she would hop up the winding stairs to the sunny rooftop where she loved to read her favorite books. The warm sunshine felt wonderful on her soft, brown fur, and the gentle breeze made the pages flutter like butterfly wings.

One bright Tuesday morning, Ruby discovered something wonderful on the rooftop. Someone had left a big cardboard box filled with colorful children's books! Her pink nose twitched with excitement as she sniffed the fresh smell of new pages.

"Oh my carrots!" Ruby squeaked with joy. "Look at all these amazing books!"

She spent the whole morning reading story after story. There were books about brave knights, silly elephants, and magical gardens. Ruby's eyes sparkled as she turned each page, learning new words and discovering exciting adventures.

But as the days passed, Ruby noticed something sad. Other young animals from the neighborhood would climb up to the rooftop, hoping to find books to read. A little squirrel named Sam looked disappointed when he saw Ruby had taken all the books to her corner.

"I wish I could read a story too," Sam sighed, his fluffy tail drooping.

Ruby's ears perked up. She looked at her pile of books, then at Sam's sad face. Her heart felt heavy, like a stone in her chest.

"Maybe I could share just one book," Ruby thought. But then she worried, "What if I don't get it back? What if someone tears the pages?"

The next day, more animals came looking for books. A baby fox named Finn and twin raccoons named Rita and Rico all climbed the stairs with hope in their eyes. But when they saw Ruby reading alone with all the books around her, they turned around and left quietly.

Ruby's tummy felt funny - not hungry funny, but worried funny. She realized that keeping all the books to herself wasn't making her as happy as she thought it would.

"Wait!" Ruby called out to her friends. "Come back! I have an idea!"

She hopped to the center of the rooftop and arranged the books in a big circle. "Let's make a rooftop library together! We can all read and share stories!"

Sam's eyes lit up like tiny stars. "Really? You'd share your books with us?"

"They're not just my books," Ruby said with a warm smile. "Books are meant to be shared! And sharing makes stories even more fun!"

Soon, all the young animals were sitting in the sunshine, reading together. Ruby discovered something magical - when she read stories aloud to her friends, the adventures felt twice as exciting! The little fox giggled at the funny parts, and the raccoon twins gasped at the surprising endings.

"Ruby," said Sam, "you're the most generous rabbit I know. Thank you for sharing!"

Ruby's heart felt as warm as the sunshine on her fur. She learned that taking care of herself meant taking care of her friends too. And the best way to enjoy her love of reading was to share it with others.

From that day on, Ruby's rooftop became the neighborhood's favorite library, where all the young animals could discover the joy of stories together.

The End.
