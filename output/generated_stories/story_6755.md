# Leo Waits His Turn

In a bright bathroom with shiny tiles and a sunny window, a brave little lion cub named Leo padded in on soft paws. The room smelled like soap and warm towels. Water dripped, plip-plop, into the sink.

Leo’s golden fur was messy from play. His whiskers were sticky with jam. “I need to wash up,” Leo said. He puffed his tiny chest. “Brave lions take care of themselves!”

The bathroom was busy. Leo’s big sister, Lila, stood on a step stool, brushing her teeth. Scritch-scritch went the brush. Bubbles of minty foam popped on her lips.

“I want to wash my paws now!” Leo said, hopping closer.

Lila pointed to the sink. “I’m almost done, Leo. Please wait,” she said kindly.

Waiting was hard. Leo’s paws itched. His nose wrinkled at the jam. He felt wiggly, like a bouncing ball. He tapped his tail on the mat. Thump, thump.

Mama Lion peeked in with a warm towel. “Good morning, brave cub. The bathroom is busy. Let’s practice patience,” she said in a gentle voice.

“What is patience?” Leo asked, ears perked.

“Patience means waiting calmly,” Mama said. “It means taking turns and caring for yourself and others.”

Leo looked at the sink. He looked at Lila. He took a slow breath in. He let it out like a quiet roar: “Roooaaar…” It sounded like a soft purr. The warm towel smelled like sunshine. It made Leo feel a little better.

“I can wait,” Leo said. “I am brave.”

To pass the time, Leo looked around. The mirror shone like a silver lake. The soap looked like a blue gem. The bubble bath smelled like oranges. He hummed a little song. Hum-hum-hummm.

Lila rinsed her brush. Swish, swish. “Almost done,” she said.

Just then, Baby Timo toddled in. He was a tiny lion with big eyes and a tiny mane. He held his favorite yellow duck. “Quack!” squeaked the duck. Timo pointed to the potty. “I need to go,” he said in a small voice.

Leo looked at the potty. He looked at the sink. He felt a tug in his heart. Waiting was still hard, but Timo was dancing from foot to foot.

“I can share my turn,” Leo said. “Timo can go first.”

Lila smiled. “That is kind,” she said.

Timo hurried to the potty with Mama’s help. Leo stepped back and held the door open. The bathroom smelled a little funny, but Mama opened the window. Fresh air whooshed in. Leaves swished outside.

“Quack,” said the duck again. Timo giggled. “All done!”

“Good job, Timo,” Mama said. She washed Timo’s tiny paws. Splish-splash. The water felt warm and sounded like a little rain.

Now it was Leo’s turn at last. He climbed onto the step stool. He turned the handle slowly. The water made a happy hiss and turned warm. He rubbed his paws with soap. Squish-squish. Bubbles popped like tiny stars. Pop! Pop!

Leo stared at the mirror. Jam spots speckled his whiskers. He felt a little squeeze in his tummy. “Mama,” he said softly, “I was going to push in front at first. I didn’t tell the truth to myself. I said I could not wait. But I can wait.”

Mama nodded. “Thank you for being honest, Leo,” she said. “You waited. You shared. That is brave.”

Leo rinsed. Swish. He dried his paws on the fluffy towel. It felt like a warm cloud. “I like clean paws,” Leo said. He smiled so big his whiskers wiggled.

Lila handed him the toothbrush. “Would you like to share my step stool?” she asked.

“Yes, please,” Leo said. “We can take turns.”

Lila brushed first. Scritch-scritch. She handed the brush to Leo. “Your turn.”

Leo brushed slowly. “One, two, three,” he counted. Minty bubbles tickled his tongue. He wanted to spit right away. But he remembered patience. He hummed his song again. Hum-hum-hummm. He finished the top teeth and then the bottom. “All done!”

He spat gently. Patooey. He rinsed his mouth. Swish. He wiped a tiny drip with the towel. He put the toothbrush back in the cup. Clink.

“Leo,” Mama said, “how do you feel?”

“I feel proud,” Leo said. His heart felt warm, like sunshine on fur. “I waited my turn. I shared with Timo. I told the truth. I took care of myself.”

Timo clapped with tiny paws. “Brave Leo!” he cheered. “Quack!” said the duck.

Lila patted Leo’s mane. “Being patient made everything smoother,” she said.

Leo nodded. “Patience is like a soft roar in my tummy. It helps me be calm.”

Mama wrapped both cubs in a big, cozy hug. The bathroom smelled sweet and clean. The window breeze kissed their noses.

“Today,” Leo said, “I will practice patience again. I will wait. I will share. I will be honest. I will care for me and for you.”

“That’s our brave little lion,” Mama said.

Leo smiled at the shiny sink one more time. He wiggled his clean paws. He felt ready for a new day.

The End.
