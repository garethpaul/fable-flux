# Fawn’s Fair Pantry Day

In a sunny cottage pantry, a gentle deer fawn named Fawn poked her soft nose inside. The pantry smelled like warm oats, sweet apples, and a tiny hint of cinnamon. The shelves were tall and tidy, and jars sparkled in the light.

Fawn’s hooves clicked softly on the cool floor. “Tap, tap,” she whispered, listening. She was curious. She loved to look, to sniff, and to learn.

From the doorway, Mama Deer called, “Good morning, Fawn! Today is pantry day.”

“Pantry day?” Fawn’s ears perked up. “What do we do?”

“We do chores,” said Mama Deer kindly. “Chores help everyone. We share the work. That is fair.”

Fawn blinked her big brown eyes. “What is fair, Mama?”

Mama Deer smiled. “Fair means we share. Fair means we take turns. Fair means we help. When everyone helps, everyone feels good.”

Fawn nodded. Her heart felt warm. “I want to help!”

Just then, their friends arrived. Squeak the mouse peeked in with bright eyes. Buzz the bee hummed a happy tune. Cluck the chicken fluffed her feathers and clucked, “Good morning!”

“Good morning!” Fawn said. “We are doing chores today. We will be fair.”

Buzz buzzed in a circle. “I can dust the jars with my tiny wings!”

Squeak raised a paw. “I can stack the little boxes. I am small and fast.”

Cluck flapped proudly. “I can count the eggs. One, two, three, cluck!”

Fawn looked around. She wanted a job too. She sniffed the apples in a wooden bowl. They smelled crisp and sweet. “I can sort the apples,” she said. “Red apples in one basket. Green apples in another.”

Mama Deer nodded. “That sounds fair.”

Everyone began. Buzz hummed, “Bzz-bzz,” as he brushed flour from a jar label. Squeak scooted boxes of tea. “Slide, slide,” he said. Cluck counted. “One egg, two egg, cluck!” Fawn lifted apples gently. “Soft and smooth,” she whispered, as she put red with red and green with green.

After a while, Fawn saw something shiny behind a big flour sack. Her heart tickled with curiosity. “What is that?” she wondered. She nudged the sack. It wobbled and made a poof! A tiny cloud of flour flew into the air like snow. Fawn sneezed. “Ah-choo!”

Squeak giggled. Buzz zoomed through the flour cloud and made little white swirls. Cluck shook flour off her tail. “Cluck! That was funny!”

Behind the flour sack lay a small honey jar with a smiling bee on it. The golden honey glowed. Fawn’s eyes grew wide. “Oh! Honey!”

Buzz hovered close. “That is my favorite!”

Cluck licked her beak. “Honey is yummy on oats.”

Squeak rubbed his tummy. “I want a taste too.”

Fawn felt a tug in her tummy. She had found the honey. She wanted the first taste. But she remembered: Fair means we share. Fair means we take turns. She took a deep breath. The air smelled sweet, like warm toast and flowers.

“Mama,” Fawn said softly, “we found honey. How can we be fair?”

Mama Deer stepped closer. She gave Fawn a proud look. “What do you think, Fawn?”

Fawn thought about chores. She thought about sharing. She thought about everyone helping. “We can make a plan,” she said. “First, we finish our jobs. Then, we all taste together. We can use tiny spoons. We can say, ‘You first, then me.’ We can take turns.”

Buzz buzzed with joy. “That sounds fair!”

Squeak clapped his tiny paws. “Fair feels good.”

Cluck nodded. “Cluck! I like turns.”

They finished their chores. Fawn placed the last shiny apple in the basket. Buzz dusted the last jar. Squeak stacked the last tea box straight and neat. Cluck counted, “Ten eggs, cluck, all safe!”

Mama Deer brought four tiny spoons. Fawn lined them up, one for each friend. Her heart felt big and happy. “Buzz first,” she said, “because honey is special to bees. Then Squeak, then Cluck, then me.”

Buzz dipped his spoon and tasted. “Bzz, sweet and sunny!”

Squeak took a turn. “Mmm, warm flowers!”

Cluck pecked carefully. “Cluck, silky and nice!”

Fawn tasted last. The honey felt thick and smooth on her tongue. It tasted like sunshine and flowers and friendship. “Yum,” she whispered.

Buzz looked at Fawn. “Thank you for being fair.”

Squeak nodded. “You shared and took turns.”

Cluck fluffed her feathers. “We all helped. The pantry is tidy. That is fair.”

Mama Deer nuzzled Fawn’s cheek. “I am proud of you. You were curious and careful. You did your chores. You chose fairness.”

Fawn’s ears wiggled with joy. She looked around the clean pantry. The jars shined. The apples glowed. The air smelled sweet. Everyone smiled.

Fawn said, “Fair means we share, we help, and we take turns. It makes work easy. It makes hearts happy.”

They all agreed. “Yes!”

The End.
