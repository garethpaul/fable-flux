# Tally the Tortoise Saves the Day

Tally was a wise tortoise who wore tiny round glasses. She lived in a cozy basement lab. The lab smelled like warm tea and clean soap. Soft lights hummed. Shelves held jars of buttons, gentle brushes, and little tools that clicked and clacked.

“Good morning, lab,” Tally said, patting a friendly broom. “Today we will make something useful.”

Her friends came to visit. Pip the mouse squeaked, “Hi, Tally!” Buzz the bee buzzed softly, “Bzz-hello!” Dot the spider waved a tiny leg. They all loved to help. Tally smiled. “Everyone is welcome here,” she said. “We take turns. We follow directions. We care for our home.”

On the table sat a big box of scraps—paper, string, old bottle caps, and colorful cloth. “We reuse things,” Tally said. “That is called sustainability. We care for Earth by using things again.”

Pip’s whiskers twitched. “Can I pour the glue?” he asked.
“Not yet,” said Tally kindly. “First, we plan. We listen. We follow steps.”

Tally drew a simple picture. “Step 1: Sort the scraps. Step 2: Wash what is messy. Step 3: Make a tool to pick up litter.”

Buzz tapped the paper. “Bzz—pick up litter?”
“Yes,” said Tally. “Trash does not belong on the ground. We help our home stay clean.”

They began to sort. Paper went in a paper pile. Caps went in a cap pile. Soft cloth went in a cloth pile. The piles made a gentle rustle and a soft clink.

Pip’s paws wiggled. He grabbed the glue. “I can do it fast!”
But the bottle tipped. “Oh!” Glop, glop—sticky glue spilled. It slid like a slow river across the table and dripped with a plop onto the floor.

Buzz buzzed in surprise. Dot squeaked, “Sticky!”

Pip’s ears drooped. “I’m sorry,” he whispered.
Tally put a warm hand on his shoulder. “Thank you for saying sorry. Now we make amends. That means we fix what we can.”

Tally took a deep breath. “Friends, we can clean this up together.” She spoke gently and clearly. “Step 1: Paper towels. Step 2: Warm water. Step 3: Wipe, rinse, and dry.”

They all followed Tally’s directions. The paper towels felt soft and squishy. The warm water felt cozy on their hands. Swish, swish. Wipe, wipe. Soon the table was clean. The floor was clean too.

Pip’s eyes were shiny. “I feel better,” he said.
Tally nodded. “You listened. You helped. That is making amends.”

They went back to sorting. Tally hummed a happy tune. “We will build a litter picker,” she said. “We will use the old tongs, two bottle caps, some string, and soft cloth for a handle.”

“May I tie the string?” asked Dot.
“Yes,” said Tally. “Your webs are neat and tidy.”
“May I test the tongs?” asked Buzz.
“Yes,” said Tally. “You can fly and see small things.”

Pip looked worried. “May I help too? I want to follow directions.”
Tally smiled. “Yes, Pip. You can hold the handle and count. Counting helps us stay calm.”

Tally spoke step by step. “Dot, tie the string around the tongs—one, two. Buzz, press the bottle caps on the ends—soft, not hard. Pip, count to three—one, two, three—and then we pause.” The tongs felt cool and smooth. The cloth handle felt warm and soft.

They finished. The litter picker looked shiny and helpful.

“Let’s try it,” said Tally. They went to the laundry corner of the basement. A small paper cup lay by the drain. “Trash does not belong there,” Tally said. “We will put it in the bin.”

Buzz hovered. “There it is!” Pip held the handle. His paws were steady. “One, two, three,” he counted slowly. Tally guided his hands. The tongs squeezed with a gentle click. They picked up the cup! Pip smiled wide. “We did it!”

They found a crinkly wrapper under a chair. Click! Into the bin it went. They found a loose button near the steps. “Not trash,” said Tally. “We can reuse this.” She put the button in a jar. The jar made a happy clink.

Soon the basement lab was tidy. It smelled like fresh air and lemon soap. Everyone’s hearts felt light and warm.

Tally gathered her friends. “Today we learned big things,” she said softly.
Pip stood tall. “I learned to follow directions.”
Dot waved. “I learned to make amends.”
Buzz buzzed. “I learned everyone can help.”

Tally nodded. “And we learned about sustainability. We reuse. We reduce waste. We keep our home clean. We care for Earth together.”

Pip said, “I made a mistake. I fixed it. I feel included. Thank you for letting me help.”
Tally hugged him gently. “There is room for everyone in this lab. We listen, we share, and we try again.”

They set the litter picker by the door. A little sign read: “For Everyone.” The letters were big and friendly. Tally smiled. “Every day, we can do small things. Small things help the big, big Earth.”

The friends drank warm tea and honey water. The cups felt cozy in their hands. They giggled and told silly jokes. The basement hummed with happy sounds.

Tally looked around her clean, kind lab. “We did good work today,” she said. “We took care of our place, and our place will take care of us.”

Everyone nodded. Their smiles were bright as the soft lights.

The End.
