# Benny Bunny Tries a New Way

In a cozy bedroom with soft yellow walls, Benny Bunny wiggled his nose. His bed was fluffy like a cloud, and a small lamp glowed like a warm moon. On the rug, toy blocks made a colorful city. The window was open just a little, and a gentle breeze carried the smell of fresh hay and clean laundry.

Benny was a determined rabbit. He liked to do things his way. He woke up, stretched his long ears, and said, “Today, I will build the tallest tower ever!”

His friend Tilly Turtle peeped from a basket by the bed. “Good morning, Benny,” she said in a small, calm voice. “I like towers too.”

Benny hopped to the blocks. Tap, tap, tap went his paws as he stacked red on blue, then green on yellow. “See, Tilly? Straight up, straight up, so tall!” he said proudly.

Tilly watched, blinking slowly. “Maybe make a wide base?” she suggested. “It could help it stand.”

Benny shook his head. “No, thank you,” he said, standing tall. “I know my way.”

The tower grew and grew. It wobbled just a little. Benny’s whiskers tickled with excitement. He smiled and added one more block.

Clatter! The tower tumbled down in a bright rain of colors.

Benny’s ears drooped. “Oh no,” he said. His chest felt tight, and his cheeks felt warm. He wanted to cry, but he took a deep breath. “I am determined,” he whispered. “I will try again.”

He started stacking. Tap, tap, tap. The lamp hummed softly. The blanket on his bed felt cozy against his leg. The tower rose, higher and higher.

Tilly tilted her head. “Benny, would you like to hear my idea?” she asked gently.

Benny paused. He was still determined. He remembered his last try. He puffed his chest and said in a brave, steady voice, “I want to make a tall tower. I will listen to your idea, but I may still choose my way.” He felt proud to use his words.

Tilly smiled. “That’s very assertive, Benny. My idea is to start with four blocks on the bottom. That makes a strong, wide base.”

Benny rubbed his chin. His whiskers twitched. He listened. He thought. He looked at the fallen blocks. “Hmm,” he said. “I never tried that.” His ears lifted. “Okay. I will be open-minded. I will try your way.”

Together, they set four blocks side by side. The base looked big and steady. Benny stacked more blocks on top, pressing gently with his paw. “One, two, three, four,” he counted. The tower didn’t wobble. It felt solid, like a tree stump.

“I like this!” Benny said. “It’s tall and strong.”

Tilly nodded. “When we learn and try new ideas, we grow.”

Benny’s mama hopped into the cozy bedroom with a tray. Warm oatmeal smelled sweet with cinnamon. “Good morning, builders,” Mama said. “I see teamwork.” Her voice was like a soft blanket.

Benny’s tummy rumbled. “Mama, I was determined to build my tower. It fell. Tilly had an idea. I listened. We tried it. Now it stands!” He pointed proudly.

Mama smiled. “That is open-mindedness,” she said. “You kept your goal and stayed kind and brave. You learned something new, and you chose to try it.”

Benny took a small bowl of oatmeal. The spoon felt cool in his paw. The oatmeal was warm and smooth. He licked a bit off his whiskers and giggled. “Learning tastes good,” he said.

After breakfast, Benny wanted to read. He picked a big book with pictures of shapes. The pages felt crisp. He saw a triangle, a square, and a rectangle. “Look, Tilly!” he said. “A square base is strong.”

Tilly clapped her tiny hands. “That matches what we did.”

Benny nodded. “We used our eyes, our minds, and our kind words. That is education,” he said, carefully sounding out the big word. “Ed-u-ca-tion.”

He read a little more, then closed the book and hugged it to his chest. His heart felt warm and light. He set the book down and looked at the tower again. It reached almost to his lamp. It did not wiggle. It stood tall and proud, just like he felt.

Benny turned to Tilly. “Thank you for your idea,” he said. “I like to do things my way. But I can also try a new way. That helps me learn.”

Tilly beamed. “And thank you for saying what you wanted,” she said. “You were clear and kind.”

Benny hopped in a little circle, his feet soft on the rug. He sang a tiny song, “Try, try, try, and listen too. That’s how we learn something new.”

The cozy bedroom glowed. The gentle breeze made the curtains dance. Benny’s ears swayed happily. He felt calm, smart, and brave.

He looked at the tower and whispered, “Being open-minded helped me reach my goal.”

Mama hugged him. “Yes, bunny. You were determined, you spoke up, and you listened. That’s the way.”

Benny smiled and patted the sturdy tower. “I can be strong and open,” he said. “I can learn every day.”

The tower stood tall, the room felt safe, and Benny’s heart felt big with joy and learning.

The End.
