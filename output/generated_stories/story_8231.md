# Pancakes, Pots, and Panda Plans

In a warm kitchen, a patient panda named Poppy padded in on soft, quiet feet. The sun painted golden stripes on the floor. The air smelled like sweet vanilla and warm butter. A little radio played gentle music—tap, tap, tap went the beat.

Poppy Panda wiggled her round ears. “Good morning, kitchen,” she said. “I’m ready to make pancakes!” She set a bowl on the counter, and the bowl made a tiny clink.

Poppy opened the flour jar. Puff! A white cloud floated up and sprinkled her nose. She giggled. “Achoo!” The radio hummed a happy tune. Poppy tapped the spoon like a drum. Tap-tap, tap-tap. “Music helps me think,” she said.

Then—oh no!—Poppy looked for the eggs. The egg carton was empty. “Hmm,” said Poppy, tapping her chin. “No eggs. That is a problem.” She took a slow breath. “I am a patient panda. I can think of a plan.”

Poppy remembered her steps for solving problems. She sang them softly with the radio beat:
“Stop and breathe,
Look and see,
Think of choices,
Pick carefully.”

She stopped and took another deep breath. She looked around the warm kitchen. She saw banana spots on a banana. She saw a jar of applesauce. She saw milk, flour, and a smiling whisk.

Poppy spoke out loud to help her think. “Choice one: I can wait for eggs. That will take a long time. My tummy is rumbly now.” Her tummy made a little growl. “Grrr-rumble.”

“Choice two: I can ask a friend for eggs,” she said. She looked at the window. Outside, birds sang. But Poppy wanted to try by herself. “I can be independent. I can do it.”

“Choice three: I can use something else.” She tapped the spoon to the radio beat again. Tap, tap. “Some friends use banana or applesauce instead of eggs. Should I try?”

Poppy nodded. “Let’s test it. I can be a kitchen scientist!”

She set up two little bowls. “Test A: Banana,” she said, mashing banana with her fork. Squish, squish, squish. “It smells sweet.” She sang, “Squish and mash, do not rush,” to the music.

“Test B: Applesauce,” she said, scooping shiny applesauce. Plop! “It looks smooth and shiny.”

Poppy measured carefully. “One cup flour. One cup milk. One spoon sugar. A tiny shake of salt.” She stirred the banana bowl. Swirl, swirl. She stirred the applesauce bowl. Swirl, swirl. The batter made soft plop sounds.

She warmed the pan. Ssssss went the butter as it melted. The kitchen smelled like apple pie and banana bread. Poppy smiled. “I am calm. I am thinking. I am trying.”

Poppy poured a little banana batter. Plip! She waited. She watched. “Wait for bubbles,” she told herself. Bloop, bloop. She flipped the pancake. Flip! It turned golden.

She poured a little applesauce batter. Plip! Bloop, bloop. Flip! Another golden circle.

Just then, her friend Millie Mouse peeked in the door. “Hi, Poppy! I heard music,” squeaked Millie. “It smells yummy.”

Poppy waved. “Hi, Millie! I had a problem. No eggs! I tried two choices. Banana and applesauce.” Poppy pointed to the stacks. “Would you like to help me test?”

Millie’s whiskers twitched. “Yes, please!” She washed her tiny paws. “How did you decide?”

Poppy swayed to the radio. “I used my steps,” she said. “Stop and breathe, look and see, think of choices, pick carefully.” She tapped the counter in time. Tap, tap, tap.

Millie nodded. “That is smart.”

They tasted the banana pancake first. Poppy took a bite. “Mmm. Soft and sweet.” Millie took a nibble. “Yummy, but a little heavy,” she said.

They tasted the applesauce pancake. Poppy chewed and smiled. “Mmm. Light and fluffy.” Millie clapped. “This one is my favorite!”

Poppy thought. “If we want fluffy pancakes, we should choose applesauce today.”

Millie pointed to a small milk drip on the floor. “Oops, I see a spill.”

Poppy’s eyes grew wide. “I dripped when I poured. I did not see. I am sorry.” She grabbed a towel. “I will make amends.” Wipe, wipe, wipe. “Thank you for telling me,” she said.

Millie smiled. “You listened. That is good communication.”

Poppy nodded. “Talking and listening help us solve problems.”

They made more pancakes, pouring applesauce batter. Plip, plip, plip. Flip! Flip! The radio played a bouncy song. Poppy and Millie sang:
“Think it through,
Try a few,
Talk it out,
Find what’s true!”

Soon the plate was full of warm, golden pancakes. Steam curled up like ribbons. Poppy set the table. She put out tiny cups for Millie and a big cup for herself. They drizzled syrup. Drip, drip. The pancakes tasted just right—fluffy and sweet with a hint of apple.

Millie patted her belly. “Your plan worked, Poppy.”

Poppy grinned. “I stayed calm. I looked for choices. I tested them. I listened and I fixed my mistake. That is critical thinking.”

Millie wiggled her ears to the music. “And we made music while we worked!”

Poppy lifted her cup. “To patience, to plans, and to pancakes!”

They clinked cups. Clink!

The warm kitchen felt even warmer, full of good smells, happy music, and kind friends.

The End.
