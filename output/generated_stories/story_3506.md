# Daisy's Cookie Dream

Little Daisy the deer fawn loved visiting Grandma Rabbit's warm kitchen. The yellow walls were sunny and bright, and the air always smelled like cinnamon and fresh bread. Today, something special sparkled in Daisy's big brown eyes.

"Grandma Rabbit," said Daisy, bouncing on her tiny hooves, "I want to make cookies all by myself! Big, beautiful cookies just like yours!"

Grandma Rabbit smiled and patted Daisy's soft spotted fur. "That's a wonderful goal, little one. Let's think about how to make it happen step by step."

"Step by step?" asked Daisy, tilting her head.

"Yes, dear. First, we wash our hooves. Then we get our ingredients. After that, we mix them together. Finally, we bake them in the oven. One step at a time makes big dreams come true!"

Daisy nodded eagerly. She washed her hooves in the warm, soapy water until they were squeaky clean. Then she carefully carried the flour, sugar, and butter to the big wooden table. Everything felt so important and grown-up!

But when Daisy tried to crack the first egg, it slipped right out of her small hooves. SPLAT! Egg goo spread across the counter like a yellow puddle.

"Oh no!" cried Daisy. Her ears drooped down. "I messed up already!"

"That's okay, sweetie," said Grandma Rabbit gently. "Everyone makes mistakes when they're learning."

Daisy tried again with the second egg. This time she squeezed too hard. CRACK! Pieces of eggshell fell into the bowl with the egg.

"This is too hard!" Daisy stamped her little hoof. "I can't do it! I'm not big enough!" Her voice got louder and shakier. "I want to make perfect cookies RIGHT NOW!"

Grandma Rabbit sat down next to Daisy. "I can see you're feeling frustrated. That's a big feeling, isn't it?"

Daisy nodded, tears making her eyes shiny.

"When we feel frustrated, we can take three deep breaths," said Grandma Rabbit. "Want to try with me?"

Together, they breathed in slowly through their noses, smelling the sweet kitchen air. Then they breathed out through their mouths. One... two... three.

"Better?" asked Grandma Rabbit.

"A little," whispered Daisy.

"Now, remember your goal. You wanted to make cookies. We can pick out the eggshell pieces and keep going, one small step at a time."

Daisy looked at the messy counter. "I'm sorry I got angry and made such a big mess, Grandma Rabbit."

"Thank you for saying sorry, dear one. Now, shall we try the next step together?"

With Grandma Rabbit's gentle help, Daisy picked out the eggshell pieces. She stirred the batter slowly, watching it become smooth and creamy. The mixer whirred happily as chocolate chips got folded in.

When the timer dinged and they opened the oven, the most delicious smell filled the kitchen. Golden-brown cookies sat on the baking sheet, perfectly round and warm.

"I did it!" squealed Daisy, clapping her hooves. "I made cookies! Well, we made cookies together."

"You set a goal, you kept trying even when it got hard, and you asked for help when you needed it," said Grandma Rabbit proudly. "That's exactly how dreams come true!"

As they shared the warm, gooey cookies with cold milk, Daisy smiled. She had learned that big goals happen one small step at a time, and that's perfectly wonderful.

The End.
