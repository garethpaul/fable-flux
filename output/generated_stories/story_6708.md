# Joey Roo and the Calm-Down Colors

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with big bouncy feet and a curious nose. One sunny afternoon, Joey hopped into Grandpa’s garage workshop. It smelled like fresh wood and orange soap. Sunbeams made dusty sparkles in the air. Tools hung on the wall like shiny silver fish.

“Wow,” Joey whispered. “So many things!”

Grandpa wore blue overalls and a kind smile. “Hello, little hopper,” he said. “Would you like to make art today? We can paint a brave rainbow sign.”

“Yes, please!” Joey clapped his soft paws. Joey loved colors. Red like apples, yellow like sun, blue like Grandpa’s overalls.

Grandpa set out a small wooden board, safe brushes, and little pots of paint. He also put a tiny timer on the table and a soft towel by Joey’s seat. “These are our helper tools,” Grandpa said. “We use them to stay calm and safe.”

Joey tilted his head. “Helper tools?”

Grandpa nodded. “When we feel big feelings, we can use calm-down tools. Let’s paint and practice.”

Joey dipped his brush in red. “Swoosh, swoosh,” he sang. The brush made soft tickly sounds. “This is fun!”

But then—plop! A red drop fell on Joey’s paw. It felt cold and sticky. Joey’s ears twitched. He tried blue next, but the lid was tight. Twist, twist—stuck. Joey’s tummy felt scrunchy.

“Hmm,” Joey said. “I can’t open it.”

Grandpa watched. “How does your body feel?”

Joey looked at his belly. “Scrunchy.” His tail wiggled fast. “My paws feel hot.”

“That’s a feeling,” Grandpa said gently. “Maybe frustration. Let’s name it.”

“Fru… frus… frus-tray-shun,” Joey tried. The word was big. His eyes felt watery.

The timer ticked softly, like a beetle: tick, tick, tick.

Joey wanted to yank the lid. He wanted to stomp. His heart thumped, thump, thump.

Then Grandpa pointed to the towel. “You are brave when you pause,” he said. “Bravery can be quiet. Let’s try our helper tools.”

Joey nodded. He was a brave joey.

First, Joey closed his eyes. He put a paw on his belly. “In, two, three,” Grandpa counted softly. “Out, two, three.” Joey breathed in the orange soap smell. He breathed out slow. His tail stopped wiggling so fast.

Next, Joey whispered, “I feel frustrated, and I can calm down.” The words felt warm, like a hug.

He wiped his paws with the towel. The towel was fluffy and gentle. Then he stretched his arms up, then down. “Shake, shake,” he giggled, shaking his paws. The scrunchy feeling got smaller.

“Good job,” said Grandpa. “Try again when you’re ready.”

Joey tried his brush with a different color. Yellow opened easily. Swish! A bright sun appeared on the board. Joey smiled. “Hello, sunny!”

He looked at the blue lid again. Joey put the jar on the table. He held it with two paws. “I can ask for help,” he said. “Grandpa, can you help me, please?”

“That is brave,” Grandpa said. He showed Joey how to twist slow and steady. Pop! The lid opened. “You did the brave part by asking.”

Joey dipped into the blue. “Splish.” He painted a calm blue sky. Then came green for grass and purple for a cozy cloud. His brush made soft whisper sounds: whoosh, sweep, dot, dot.

Suddenly, the timer rang—ding! Joey jumped and made a purple streak across the sun. “Oh no!” His cheeks got hot. The scrunchy feeling came back.

Joey froze. He looked at the towel, the timer, and Grandpa’s kind eyes. “I feel surprised,” Joey said. “And a little mad.”

Grandpa nodded. “You noticed. What can you do?”

Joey took a belly breath. In, two, three. Out, two, three. He counted five little screws on the table. “One, two, three, four, five.” His heart slowed down.

“I can fix it,” Joey said. He dipped in orange and made the streak into a brave ribbon. Then he added tiny stars around it. “Ta-da!” The sun looked even happier.

Grandpa clapped. “You turned a mistake into art.”

Joey beamed. “With calm-down tools!”

They painted big letters on the board: BE BRAVE, BE CALM. Joey dotted the letters with red, blue, and gold. The board smelled like paint and hope. The colors danced in the sun.

“Let’s hang it up,” Grandpa said. They used safe hooks and soft taps. Tap, tap. The sign hung on the wall, bright and proud.

Joey put a paw on his belly. He felt soft and steady. “Grandpa, I learned something today.”

“What did you learn, little hopper?”

“When feelings get big,” Joey said, “I can stop, breathe, name my feeling, and ask for help. That is brave.”

Grandpa hugged him. “Yes. That is self-regulation. And you are a joyful, brave artist.”

Joey giggled. He bounced once, twice, just a little. “Bounce-bounce.”

“Want a snack?” Grandpa asked.

“Yes, please,” Joey said. They shared crunchy apple slices that tasted sweet and cool. The garage felt warm and safe. The rainbow sign smiled down at them.

Joey looked at his painted paws. “My feelings can be big,” he said. “But my calm can be big too.”

Grandpa winked. “And your art is the bridge.”

Joey smiled. “I can do it. I can be calm and brave.”

The End.
