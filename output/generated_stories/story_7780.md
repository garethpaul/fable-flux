# Ollie Otter's Bubble Bath Wait

Ollie the otter loved bath time more than anything! He would splash and play with his rubber duck, making happy squeaking sounds. But today was different. When Ollie rushed to the bathroom, he found his big sister Olivia already in the bathtub.

"I want my bath NOW!" Ollie said, stomping his little webbed feet on the fluffy bath mat. The soft mat felt squishy under his paws, but he was too upset to enjoy it.

Olivia looked up from her bubble bath. The bubbles sparkled like tiny rainbows in the warm light. "I just got in, Ollie. I need a few more minutes to wash my fur."

"But I NEED my bath!" Ollie whined, his whiskers twitching with frustration. "I'm all muddy from playing by the pond!"

Mama Otter heard the fuss and swam over. Her gentle voice was like a warm hug. "What's happening here, my little ones?"

"Olivia won't share the bathtub!" Ollie said, his brown eyes filling with tears. "I want to splash and play with my toys!"

Mama Otter knelt down and looked into Ollie's eyes. "I can see you're upset, sweetie. It's hard to wait when we really want something. But what could you do while you wait?"

Ollie sniffled. He looked around the cozy bathroom. The mirror was steamy, and he could smell the sweet lavender soap Olivia was using. "I... I don't know."

"Well," Mama said softly, "you could use your words to ask Olivia nicely how much longer she needs. Then you could find something fun to do while you wait."

Ollie took a deep breath. The air smelled warm and clean. "Olivia," he said in his politest voice, "how much longer do you need for your bath?"

Olivia smiled. "Just five more minutes to rinse my tail, little brother. Thank you for asking nicely!"

"What can I do for five minutes?" Ollie wondered aloud.

Mama pointed to the bathroom window. "Look! You can watch the raindrops race down the glass. Or you could organize your bath toys in the basket."

Ollie's eyes lit up. He loved organizing! He carefully arranged his rubber duck, his floating boat, and his squeaky fish in neat rows. The toys felt smooth and clean in his paws. As he played, he started humming a happy tune.

"One, two, three little ducks," he sang softly, making his toys dance.

Before he knew it, Olivia was climbing out of the tub. "All done! The bathtub is ready for you, Ollie. I even left some bubbles for you!"

"Thank you, Olivia!" Ollie squeaked with joy. He hugged his sister, feeling proud that he had waited so patiently.

As Ollie slipped into the warm, bubbly water, Mama smiled. "I'm proud of you, Ollie. You used your words politely, and you found a fun way to wait. That's what patience looks like!"

Ollie splashed happily, watching the bubbles pop around him. The water felt perfect - not too hot, not too cold. "Waiting wasn't so bad after all," he giggled. "And now my bath feels extra special!"

From that day on, whenever Ollie had to wait for something, he remembered to use his words kindly and find something fun to do. Patience, he learned, made everything better in the end.

The End.
