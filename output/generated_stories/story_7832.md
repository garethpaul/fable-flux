# Benny Beaver's Guest Room Adventure

Benny was a busy beaver with bright, shiny teeth and a wide, warm smile. Today, Mama Beaver said, “We have a visitor coming, Benny. Aunt Willow will sleep in the guest room tonight.”

Benny’s eyes sparkled. “Can I help, Mama? I’m a busy beaver!” he said, tapping his tail on the floor. Thump-thump! The sound was soft and happy.

“Yes, you can help,” Mama said kindly. “The guest room needs tidying. And Aunt Willow loves surprises.”

Benny waddled to the guest room. It smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. The window let in golden light. The bed was fluffy like a white cloud. But the room felt quiet, almost shy.

Benny blinked. “Hello, guest room,” he whispered. “Let’s make you cozy.”

He looked around. There was a small chair, a soft rug, a basket of blankets, and a big empty space on the floor. The room made a little echo when he stepped. Tap, tap. Benny smiled. “I can use my imagination!” he said. “Imagination makes everything more fun.”

He spread a blanket on the floor. Swish. “This is a river,” he giggled. “Aunt Willow will cross it to the bed.” He wiggled his paws. “But we need a bridge!”

Benny gathered couch cushions from the hallway—poof, poof!—and lined them up. “Bridge ready!” He practiced stepping across. “Tip, tap, tip!” His tail swished. He was moving and balancing, using his strong beaver body.

Mama peeked in. “That looks fun,” she said, smiling.

“It’s an Imagination Bridge,” Benny explained. “It helps visitors travel safely.”

Mama nodded. “What a kind idea.”

Benny looked at the chair. “A boat!” he gasped. He pushed it gently so it made a little scrape sound. “Skrrp.” He placed a folded towel as a sail. “Sail, sail, sail,” he sang softly. “The Imagination River is calm and blue.”

He paused. “Aunt Willow might want a snack,” he said. “Beavers share!” He waddled to the kitchen, tail whispering along the floor. He picked apples and a small bag of nuts. Crunchy apples smelled sweet. He set them in a bowl by the bed. He poured water into a cup. “Aunt Willow will feel welcome,” he said proudly.

Then Benny noticed the basket of blankets. He rubbed one against his cheek. “So soft,” he giggled. “Like a cloud.” He rolled one into a round shape. “This is a cozy log,” he decided. “For reading.” He set it near the lamp.

Suddenly, he had a big idea. “The guest room needs a welcome sign!” He found paper and crayons. The crayons smelled waxy and warm. Scritch-scratch went the red crayon. Swirl went the blue. He drew a beaver waving and a shiny sun. He wrote, slowly and carefully, “WELCOME, AUNT WILLOW.” He taped the sign to the door.

To test the adventure, Benny stood at the doorway. “Hello, I am Aunt Willow,” he said in a gentle voice, pretending. He walked in with tiny steps. “Oh my, what a lovely room!” he practiced. He crossed the bridge, arms out wide, toes touching each cushion. “Tip, tap, tip,” he whispered. He sat in the boat-chair and wiggled. “Comfy.” He reached the bed and patted the blankets. “Soft like clouds.” He took a pretend bite of apple. “Crunch! Thank you, Benny. You were so generous.”

Benny felt warm all over. He twirled on the rug, swinging his arms, taking big breaths. The rug felt bouncy under his paws. He laughed. “Imagination makes my body move and my heart feel good,” he said.

Just then, there was a gentle knock. Knock-knock.

“Aunt Willow!” Benny ran to the door, tail thumping softly.

Aunt Willow stepped in. She smelled like pine trees and fresh air. Her eyes were kind. “Hello, my busy beaver,” she said, giving him a careful hug.

“Come see the guest room,” Benny chirped. He took her paw and led her in.

Aunt Willow gasped. “Oh!” Her eyes grew wide at the bridge, the boat-chair, the cozy log, and the welcome sign. “This is magical,” she whispered.

“It’s the Imagination River,” Benny explained. “You can cross the bridge. Do you want to try?”

Aunt Willow lifted her paws and balanced. Tip, tap, tip. She giggled. “I feel brave!”

Benny clapped. “You did it!”

Aunt Willow sat on the bed and picked up the apple. Crunch! “Delicious. You shared with me. That is very generous.”

Benny’s cheeks warmed. “I wanted you to feel happy,” he said.

“I do,” Aunt Willow said. “You used your imagination to make this room friendly. You moved your body to build and balance. And you shared, so I felt loved.”

Benny’s smile grew bigger. “Imagination can turn a quiet room into an adventure,” he said softly.

Mama peeked in again. “What a wonderful helper.”

Benny curled his tail around his paws. “Today I learned something,” he said. “When I use my imagination, I can make kind surprises. Moving my body helps me build them. And sharing makes others smile.”

Aunt Willow nodded. “That is a perfect lesson.”

They all sat together for a moment, listening to the soft tick-tick of the wall clock and the gentle whoosh of the wind at the window. The room did not feel shy anymore. It felt full of love.

The End.
