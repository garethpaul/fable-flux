# Ruby's Cozy Closet Concert

Ruby was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and big, listening ears. She loved to hop. She loved to snack on crunchy carrots. But today, Ruby had a different plan. Today, she would make her closet hideaway clean and calm.

Her closet hideaway was a tiny, cozy space under the stairs. It smelled like fresh laundry and a little bit like pine. A soft blanket made a nest on the floor. A string of paper stars hung from the wall, and a little lantern gave a warm, gentle glow. It was Ruby’s quiet place.

Ruby peeked inside. “Oh no,” she whispered. Toys were scattered like little raindrops. Buttons, socks, and puzzle pieces were everywhere. Her drum was upside down, and her maracas were hiding under a scarf.

“I can do this,” Ruby said to herself. “I am determined.”

Mama Rabbit called from the hallway, “Ruby, it’s tidy time!”

Ruby took a big, slow breath in. She felt the air cool her nose. She let the breath out. “I will clean my closet,” she said. “I will be calm.”

She turned on her tiny radio. Soft music floated into the closet—la, la, la, like a warm breeze. The notes sounded like sunshine. Ruby’s ears perked up.

“I will make my chores a little concert,” Ruby giggled. “A tidy concert!”

First, she picked up the socks. “Plop, plop,” into a small basket. The socks felt soft and squishy. She hummed to the music. “Plop, plop, tidy-tidy, plop.”

Next, she found her puzzle pieces. Click, click. The pieces made happy sounds as they slid into their box. Ruby whispered, “Thank you, pieces. You have a home now.”

Her drum looked at her from the corner. It was dusty. Ruby wiped it with a cloth. Swish, swish. The dust smelled a little like the attic. Ruby tapped the drum gently. Dum-dum. The sound was deep and friendly. “Hello, drum,” she said. “You can help me keep the beat.”

Ruby set a rhythm: dum-dum, plop-plop, click-click. She moved slowly and carefully. Her body felt calm. Her mind felt clear. The music and the drum helped her hands keep going.

Mama peeked in. “How’s it going, Ruby?”

Ruby smiled. “I’m having a closet concert! I’m doing my chores with music. It helps me be calm.”

Mama nodded. “What a great idea. Music can help our bodies move and our minds focus.”

Ruby found the maracas. Shake-shake! They sounded like gentle rain. She put them on a shelf. She folded her scarf—smooth, smooth—and placed it in a bin. She lined up her books so the spines made a rainbow. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple. The colors made her heart feel happy.

She took another slow breath. In through the nose, out through the mouth. She noticed the lantern’s warm light. She noticed the soft blanket under her feet. She noticed the music’s cozy hum.

But then Ruby saw the button jar. The lid was off, and buttons had rolled into every corner. Little blue buttons. Big brown buttons. Shiny silver buttons. Ruby’s nose twitched. “This is hard,” she whispered.

She stopped. She put her hand on her drum. Dum… dum… A steady, slow beat. “I can do hard things,” she told herself. “I can go slow. One button at a time.”

Ruby scooped a blue button. Plink! Into the jar. She scooped a brown one. Plink! She made a button song. “Plink-plink, tidy-tidy, plink,” she sang softly. The song made her smile. Her shoulders relaxed. Her paws moved steady.

Soon, the last shiny button went in. Plink! Ruby screwed the lid on tight. Twisty-twist. She gave herself a small cheer. “I did it!”

She looked around. The closet hideaway was neat and calm. The blanket was smooth. The paper stars swayed gently. The drum sat next to the maracas, waiting for music time. Ruby turned down the radio. The song faded to a soft whisper.

Mama came back and looked inside. “Ruby, this is lovely,” she said. “How do you feel?”

Ruby placed a paw on her chest. “I feel calm,” she said. “I feel proud. Chores are like little steps. Music helps me take them. And breathing helps me be steady.”

Mama smiled. “That is a smart lesson.”

Ruby curled up on her blanket. She held her maracas and tapped the drum softly. Shake-shake, dum-dum. The closet smelled clean and sweet. The light felt warm. Her ears heard only soft sounds. Her heart felt peaceful.

She whispered, “When I do my chores, my space is safe and cozy. When I go slow and breathe, I can do hard things. And music can help me finish with a smile.”

Ruby closed her eyes and hummed her tidy song, happy in her clean, calm closet hideaway.

The End.
