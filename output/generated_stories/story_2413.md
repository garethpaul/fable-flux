# Ginger Giraffe’s Gentle Work Day

Ginger the giraffe was tall and thoughtful. She had big soft eyes and a long, kind neck. Today, she was working in her home office. The sun shined through the window, warm and golden. Her desk smelled like fresh paper. Click, click, went her keyboard. Tap, tap, went her pencil.

Ginger whispered, “I can do this. I will take my time.”

On her desk, there were neat baskets. One basket said “To Do.” One basket said “Done!” There was a small bell that went ding. A tiny plant sat by the lamp. Its leaves were shiny and green.

Ginger started to feel her tummy wiggle. Her heart went thump-thump faster. Papers were on the floor from yesterday. Stickers were stuck to her chair. Her tail swished with worry.

“I feel… big,” Ginger said softly. “Big feelings.”

She closed her eyes and took a slow breath in. In goes the air, cool and sweet. She let the breath out. Out goes the air, warm and soft.

“In, two, three. Out, two, three,” she hummed. “I can name my feeling. I feel overwhelmed.”

Her phone buzzed. “Bzzzz.” It was her friend, Timo the tiny turtle. “Hi, Ginger,” Timo said. “I made a drawing for you, but it got smudged. I feel sad.”

Ginger listened with her big ears. “I hear you, Timo,” she said gently. “Your drawing smudged. That feels sad.”

“Can you still like it?” asked Timo. His voice was small.

“I still like it,” Ginger said. “I like your hard work. I respect your feelings too.”

Timo sniffled. “Thank you.”

Ginger smiled. “Would you like to visit my home office on the screen? We can breathe together.”

“Yes, please,” said Timo.

They looked at each other on the screen. Ginger showed Timo her baskets. “When I feel overwhelmed, I get organized,” she said. “It helps my feelings be calm.”

Ginger put the papers in the “To Do” basket. “Plop.” She put finished drawings in the “Done!” basket. “Plink.” She peeled the stickers off her chair. “Rip, rip.” She dusted the plant leaves. “Wipe, wipe.”

“How do you feel now?” asked Timo.

Ginger paused. She felt the warm sun on her fur. She smelled her tea—sweet mint. She put a hoof on her chest. “My heart feels slower,” she said. “I feel calm and proud.”

Timo nodded. “I want to try,” he said. He held up his smudged drawing. “First, I name my feeling. I feel sad.” He breathed in. “In.” He breathed out. “Out.” Then he said, “I can try again later. I can put this in my ‘To Try Again’ folder.”

“That’s a great idea,” said Ginger. “You are very brave.”

Just then, there was a rustle at Ginger’s door. Her neighbor, Bibi the baby bird, peeped in. “Peep? Peep?” Bibi held a tiny letter in her beak. The letter was bent and wrinkly.

“I think Bibi feels worried,” Ginger said to Timo. “Look at her little wings.”

Bibi chirped, “My letter is wrinkly. Will the mail mouse be mad?”

Ginger lowered her head to Bibi’s eye level. “I hear you,” she said. “You worked hard. You feel worried about the wrinkles.”

Bibi nodded fast. “Peep, peep.”

Ginger smiled kindly. “I respect your care. Let’s help the letter feel safe.” She smoothed the paper. “Smooth, smooth.” She found a folder. “This folder will keep it straight. We will put it in the ‘To Mail’ basket.”

Bibi’s wings relaxed. “Peep! Thank you.”

“Would you like to ring the bell?” Ginger asked.

“Ding!” went the bell. Bibi giggled and flew off.

Timo clapped his little turtle hands. “You helped Bibi feel better.”

Ginger felt warm inside. “We listened. We named the feeling. We made a small plan. Organization can help our hearts.”

They both smiled. Ginger checked her list. “One: tidy desk. Two: finish letter. Three: thank friends.” She wrote, “Thank you, Timo,” on a bright yellow note. She stuck it on her screen. “Stick.”

Timo held up a fresh paper. “I will draw again after a snack,” he said. “First, I will drink water.”

“Good plan,” said Ginger. “Being kind to yourself is important.”

The room was quiet and cozy. The lamp made a soft glow. Ginger’s plant looked proud too. Ginger took one more breath. In. Out.

“How do we end our work time?” Ginger asked.

Timo thought. “We can say how we feel.”

Ginger nodded. “I feel calm, organized, and loved.”

“I feel hopeful and brave,” said Timo.

Ginger added, “And we show respect by listening and helping.”

They waved goodbye. “Bye, friend!”

Ginger put her pencil to paper. The words came easier now. Tap, tap. She smiled and whispered, “Big feelings can be gentle when I notice them, name them, and make a plan.”

Outside, a soft breeze moved the curtains. The bell sat quiet. The baskets were neat. Ginger’s heart felt light.

The End.
