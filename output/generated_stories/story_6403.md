# Ollie Otter Says “I’m Sorry”

Ollie was a playful otter with soft, sleek fur and bright, curious eyes. One rainy afternoon, Ollie padded down the creaky stairs to a basement lab. The air was cool and smelled like lemons and glue. Lights blinked. Beep-beep! Whirrr! Little machines hummed. Shelves held jars of buttons, colorful wires, and fluffy brushes.

“Hello, lab!” Ollie giggled, swishing his tail. “Let’s play!”

On the big table stood a special box with a friendly smile drawn on it. A little sign said, “Language Helper. Please be gentle.” The box had a red button and a yellow knob. Ollie loved buttons.

“I will just look,” said Ollie. His whiskers twitched. He poked the yellow knob. Click. He tapped the red button. Boop!

Suddenly the box sang, “Hola! Bonjour! Hello!” It made a happy ding. Ollie clapped. “Wow! It knows many ways to say hello!”

Ollie hopped around the table. His paws slid on a shiny tray. Clink-clatter! Paintsticks rolled. A blue cup tipped. Splash! A little wave of purple paint slid across the table and dripped—plip, plop—onto the Language Helper.

“Oh no,” whispered Ollie. His tummy felt twisty. The box sputtered. “H-h-h—” Then it went quiet. The friendly smile looked smudged with purple.

Footsteps thumped on the stairs. Ms. Bea, the kind volunteer who ran the lab, came down holding a stack of clean towels. She wore bright green glasses and a soft sweater that smelled like soap.

“Hi, Ollie,” Ms. Bea said with a warm smile. Then she saw the table. She saw the purple paint. She saw the quiet box. Her smile faded a little. “Oh dear. What happened?”

Ollie’s cheeks grew hot. His tail drooped. He wanted to hide under the table. He wanted to say, “It was not me.” But he took a deep breath. He remembered the sign: “Please be gentle.”

In a small voice, Ollie said, “I made a big mess. I touched the button. I slipped. The paint spilled. I am sorry.” His voice shook, but he looked at Ms. Bea with honest eyes.

Ms. Bea’s face softened. She knelt so her eyes met Ollie’s. “Thank you for telling the truth,” she said gently. “Saying ‘I’m sorry’ is brave. We can fix mistakes. We can make it better.”

Ollie blinked. “We can?”

“We can,” said Ms. Bea. “Let’s solve this together.”

They got two towels. Swish, swish. They wiped the table. They dabbed the box. Dab, dab. The Language Helper blinked, then coughed, “H-hello?” It sounded sleepy.

Ollie smiled a tiny smile. “Hello,” he whispered back.

Ms. Bea showed Ollie a small, soft brush. “This brush is for careful cleaning. Can you help?”

“Yes,” Ollie said. He felt a warm glow in his chest. He wanted to help. He wanted to make things right.

They worked as a team. Ms. Bea unplugged the box. Ollie held the brush and gently, gently brushed the purple spots away. Whoosh. He hummed a little tune. “Hum, hum, hum.” Ms. Bea hummed too. Hum, hum.

“Thank you for volunteering to help,” Ms. Bea said. “When we help, we are volunteers. Helpers make the lab happy.”

Ollie’s whiskers lifted. “I am a helper!”

When the box was clean and dry, Ms. Bea plugged it back in. The light blinked green. Beep! The Language Helper spoke in a bright voice, “Hello! Hola! Bonjour! Jambo! Ni hao!”

Ollie’s eyes went wide. “It talks many ways!”

Ms. Bea nodded. “These are different languages. People say hello in many ways. Each word is special.”

Ollie tried the sounds, slow and careful. “Hello. Hola. Bonjour.” He tasted the sounds like little candies. “They feel different on my tongue,” he giggled. “Bonjour is soft. Hola is bouncy.”

Ms. Bea clapped softly. “Wonderful! Language is a treasure. We listen. We learn. We show care with our words.”

Ollie took a breath. He stood tall. “Ms. Bea, I am sorry I touched the button without asking. I am sorry I made a mess. Next time I will ask first. I will be gentle.”

Ms. Bea smiled. “I forgive you, Ollie. Thank you for your clear apology. You said what happened. You said you are sorry. You said what you will do next time. That is a strong, kind apology.”

Ollie’s heart felt light. He wanted to keep helping. “Can I put the paintsticks back?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Ms. Bea. “And we can make a ‘Please Ask First’ sign together.”

They made a bright sign with big friendly letters. Ms. Bea wrote the words. Ollie added tiny otter paw prints at the edges. Tap, tap. They read the sign together: “Please ask first. Be gentle. Use kind words.”

At the end, they pressed the red button together. The Language Helper sang, “Thank you!” in many voices. “Gracias! Merci! Asante! Xie xie!”

Ollie repeated, “Thank you. Gracias. Merci.” He smiled. “Words can help.”

Ms. Bea nodded. “Yes. Words can help fix problems. Words can say ‘I’m sorry.’ Words can say ‘I can help.’”

Ollie took a deep, happy breath. The lab smelled like lemon again. The lights blinked friendly blinks. Beep-beep! Whirrr!

He waved to the box. “Goodbye! Adiós! Au revoir!”

Together, Ollie and Ms. Bea climbed the stairs. Ollie’s steps felt bouncy, not heavy. He had made a mistake. He had said he was sorry. He had helped to fix it. He had learned new words. He was a helper now.

And that felt good.

The End.
