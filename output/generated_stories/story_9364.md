# Kiki the Calm Koala Tells a Story

Kiki the calm koala sat on the peaceful front porch of her little gum-leaf house. The porch was warm and smooth. The wooden floor felt friendly under her soft paws. Birds chirped sweet songs. A breeze smelled like eucalyptus and sunshine. Kiki took a deep breath in and a slow breath out.

“Ahh,” she said. “This is a good porch for stories.”

Kiki loved stories. She kept a big, cozy basket of soft pillows by her chair. She also kept a little basket with water, a tiny bell, and a comb. “Self-care helps me share care,” Kiki would say. She sipped cool water and brushed her fluffy ears. Then she rang the tiny bell—ding!—to let friends know Story Time was near.

Thump-thump-thump! Footsteps came up the path. Pip the possum peeked over the fence. “Hi, Kiki! Can we hear a story?”

“Yes,” Kiki said with a calm smile. “Please sit on a pillow.”

Pip sat. Then Lila the lizard slid onto the porch with a soft swish. Gus the kookaburra flapped down and clicked his beak. “Hee-hee-hoo! I love stories,” Gus laughed.

Kiki held her paws together. “Before we start, I will care for me. I will sit tall, drink water, and take three slow breaths. Would you like to try with me?”

“Yes!” said the friends.

“In,” said Kiki. They breathed in. “Out,” said Kiki. They breathed out. The porch felt even more peaceful.

Gus wriggled. “Can you tell a silly story? A super silly one with jumping jelly beans?”

Kiki nodded slowly. “Silly is fun. I also want a story with kindness. We can have both.”

Pip raised a paw. “Can we be in the story?”

“Yes,” said Kiki, “but we take turns. I will start. If you want to add a part, please say, ‘May I add?’ That is being assertive and polite.”

Pip smiled. “May I add?”

“Soon,” said Kiki kindly. “I will begin, then I will call on you.”

Kiki closed her eyes. She felt the warm sun on her fur. She smelled the sweet leaves. She began in a gentle voice. “Once, on a peaceful porch, there lived a calm koala named Kiki. She loved to tell stories that helped hearts feel cozy. One day, a tiny wind came to visit.”

Kiki made a tiny whoosh with her lips. The friends giggled.

“The wind said,” Kiki continued, “‘Hello, Kiki. Please tell a story that helps me slow down. I am a fast wind, and I am tired.’”

Gus flapped. “Hee-hee-hoo!”

Kiki held up a paw. “Gus, would you like to add?”

Gus sat tall. “May I add a sound?”

“Yes,” Kiki said.

Gus made a soft breezy “hoo-hoo.” It was gentle and sweet.

“Thank you,” said Kiki. “That was helpful.”

She went on. “Kiki said to the wind, ‘First, we drink water.’ So the wind sipped a raindrop.” Kiki took a sip from her cup. “Then they took three slow breaths.” The friends breathed again. “Then the wind felt better. But the wind also felt wiggly.”

Pip bounced. “May I add?” he asked.

“Yes,” Kiki said.

Pip squeaked, “The wind tickled a feather!”

Kiki smiled. “And the feather said, ‘Please be gentle.’ The wind listened. Listening is helpful.”

Lila lifted her little chin. “May I add, please?” Her voice was small, but brave.

“Yes,” said Kiki. “Thank you for asking.”

Lila spoke slowly. “The feather told a story, too. A soft story.”

Kiki nodded. “The feather said, ‘Once I flew high. I felt scared. I told my story to the sky. The sky held me.’ When we tell our stories, our hearts feel held.”

The porch was very quiet. The breeze felt like a hug.

Gus wiggled again. “What about jumping jelly beans?” he burst out.

Kiki placed a gentle paw on her heart. “I hear you, Gus. You want silly.” She took a breath. “We can have a little silly now.”

Gus beamed.

Kiki grinned. “Suddenly, a bowl of jelly beans came hopping up the steps—boing, boing!” She tapped the porch for each boing. The kids laughed. “They said, ‘We want to bounce in the story!’”

Pip clapped. Lila’s tail swished.

Kiki spoke clearly. “Kiki said to the beans, ‘Welcome. We take turns and we stay gentle. No bumping.’ That is being assertive. Kiki set a kind rule.” She looked at her friends. “Rules help stories grow.”

The jelly beans bounced softly—boing, soft, boing, soft. They made rainbow shadows on the porch. The wind hummed a calm tune. The feather floated like a quiet dancer. Everyone listened and added, one by one.

Kiki paused and checked her body. “I feel thirsty,” she said. “I need a sip and a stretch.” She drank water and rolled her shoulders. “Self-care keeps my story voice steady.”

Pip whispered, “I need water too.” They all sipped. No one felt rushed.

Then Kiki finished the tale. “The wind learned to slow. The feather felt brave. The jelly beans learned gentle bouncing. And Kiki learned that a good story has room for silly and kind, if we take turns and speak up with care.”

Gus fluffed his feathers and laughed softly. “Hee-hee-hoo. I feel calm and happy.”

Lila smiled. “I felt brave when I asked to add.”

Pip nodded. “I liked the turn-taking.”

Kiki rang the tiny bell—ding! “Friends, what did we learn?”

Pip said, “Tell your story.”

Lila said, “Care for yourself.”

Gus said, “Speak up kindly.”

Kiki hugged them with her eyes. “Yes. We listen. We take turns. We care for ourselves. We are brave and gentle. That is how stories help hearts.”

The porch glowed in the late sun. The breeze was sweet. The friends sat together on soft pillows, happy and calm.

The End.
