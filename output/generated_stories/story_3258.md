# Toby's Special Welcome Gift

Toby the tortoise lived in a sunny garden behind a big house. He had a beautiful green shell with golden spots and kind, twinkling eyes. Toby loved making things with his careful, steady hands.

One morning, Toby heard voices from the guest room window. "We have visitors coming!" said Mrs. Chen, the house owner. "A little girl named Lily is staying with us. She's very shy and doesn't speak our language yet."

Toby's heart felt warm. He wanted to help the little girl feel welcome. "I know!" he said to himself. "I'll make her something special!"

Toby crawled slowly to his craft corner under the rose bush. He had colorful paper, smooth pebbles, and pretty ribbons. But when he started working, everything went wrong.

"Oh no!" Toby sighed. His paper flowers looked crumpled. The pebbles wouldn't stick together. His ribbon kept tangling up in his claws.

Toby felt sad. "I'm too slow," he mumbled. "My hands are too big. I can't make anything beautiful."

Just then, Rosie the rabbit hopped over. "What's wrong, Toby?" she asked with her soft, gentle voice.

"I want to make a welcome gift for the new little girl," Toby explained. "But I'm not good at crafting. Everything looks messy."

Rosie's nose twitched thoughtfully. "Can I help? We could work together!"

"Really?" Toby's eyes brightened. "But you're so fast, and I'm so slow."

"That's perfect!" Rosie smiled. "You're careful and thoughtful. I'm quick and energetic. Together we'll make something wonderful!"

They worked side by side. Toby carefully chose the most beautiful flowers from his garden. His steady hands arranged them perfectly. Rosie quickly gathered soft moss and shiny leaves. Her nimble paws tied delicate bows.

"This feels so much better," Toby said, feeling proud. "We each do what we're good at!"

Soon, Benny the bluebird flew down. "What are you making?" he chirped curiously.

"A welcome gift!" Toby and Rosie said together.

"I want to help too!" Benny sang. He flew high and brought down the softest feathers and sweetest-smelling petals.

Together, they created a beautiful welcome basket. It had colorful flowers, smooth worry stones to hold, soft feathers to touch, and a picture made from leaves showing all the garden friends.

That afternoon, little Lily arrived. She looked nervous and stayed close to her parents. Toby slowly approached the guest room window with his friends.

"Hello," Toby said gently, holding up the basket. "Welcome to our garden."

Lily's eyes grew wide with wonder. She touched the soft feathers and smiled. She picked up a smooth stone and held it tight. Even though she didn't speak their language, she understood their kindness.

"Thank you," Lily whispered in her own language, but her happy smile said everything.

From that day on, Lily visited the garden every morning. She learned to craft with Toby, run with Rosie, and sing with Benny. Soon she was laughing and playing, feeling at home.

Toby learned something important: when we work together and use our different talents, we can create something more beautiful than we ever could alone. Everyone has special gifts to share, and kindness is a language everyone understands.

The End.
