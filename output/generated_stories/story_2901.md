# Owl Hoots and the Sunny Sort

The sun was warm and bright in the backyard. Flowers smelled sweet. Bees buzzed softly. A gentle breeze tickled the grass. In the big oak tree, a wise old owl blinked her golden eyes.

“Hoo-hoo! Good morning,” she called. “I am Miss Olive Owl. Today is a busy day!”

Children from many homes came to play. There was Amir with a red cap, Mei with sparkly shoes, Ayo with a green ball, and Sofía with a purple ribbon. Their grown-ups smiled from the porch, sharing snacks and stories. The backyard looked like a happy rainbow of friends.

Miss Olive Owl fluffed her feathers. “Friends,” she hooted, “we have a job and an adventure. We will clean up and recycle! We will use our time well. Hoo-hoo!”

“What is re-cy-cling?” asked Sofía, her eyes wide.

“Recycling means we give old things a new life,” Miss Olive Owl said. “A bottle can become a bench. A paper can become a book. We help the Earth. We help each other.”

Amir held up a crinkly bottle. “Like this?”

“Yes,” said Miss Olive Owl. “We will sort. We will put things in the right bins. Listen and look.” She pointed her wing to three bright bins. One was blue, one was green, and one was brown.

Mei clapped. “Blue, green, brown!”

“The blue bin is for paper,” said Miss Olive Owl. “It goes shhh like a page.” She slid a newspaper in. “Hear that?”

“Shhh,” Ayo whispered, giggling.

“The green bin is for glass and plastic bottles. They go clink and plunk.” She dropped in a bottle. Plunk!

“Plunk!” the children said together.

“The brown bin is for scraps. Banana peels, apple cores, and leaves. They go munch to the worms and make soil.”

Sofía sniffed a banana peel. “It smells sweet.”

Miss Olive Owl nodded. “Now, we will use our time in three small parts. Time is like slices of fruit. We do a little, then a little more. First, five minutes to pick up. Next, five minutes to sort. Last, five minutes to make art with clean things. Hoo-hoo!”

Amir bounced. “I like slices!”

“Me too,” said Mei. “Let’s start!”

The children spread out. The sun made the grass warm on their toes. A cool shadow from the tree felt nice on their faces. Birds chirped. A sprinkler went tss-tss-tss.

“Bottle here!” Ayo called, holding a bottle up high.

“Plunk!” laughed Sofía as she dropped it in the green bin.

“I found paper!” said Mei. She put a shiny flyer into the blue bin. “Shhh.”

Amir picked up a banana peel with a silly face. “Hello, Mr. Peel!” he said, making everyone giggle. He put it in the brown bin. “For the worms. Munch.”

Miss Olive Owl watched with a happy heart. “You are doing so well. You are kind to the Earth. Hoo-hoo!”

A bell on her branch went ting-ting. “Time for sorting,” she said. “Check the bins. Look, listen, and think.”

Ayo held a metal can. “Where does this go?”

Miss Olive Owl tapped the can. “Tink-tink. That is metal. It can go with bottles in the green bin here. In some homes, metal has its own bin. Today, green is good.”

Amir had a sticky cup. “It is messy.”

“Let’s rinse,” Mei said. She and Amir took the cup to the porch and swished it in a little tub. Swish-swish! Sofía wiped it with a cloth. “Now it is clean,” she said proudly.

Miss Olive Owl smiled. “You used teamwork. You used time. Hoo-hoo!”

The bell went ting-ting again. “Art time!” Miss Olive Owl laid out safe, clean items: paper rolls, big buttons, and clean lids. She brought glue sticks and string.

“Let’s make a time train,” said Ayo. “Choo-choo!”

“Let’s make a world,” said Sofía, pointing to a round lid. “So many people. So many friends.”

“Let’s make an owl,” said Mei, grinning at Miss Olive Owl.

They worked side by side. Amir glued paper rolls like a train. Sofía made a blue and green world with a smile. Mei and Ayo made an owl with wide eyes and soft feather shapes from paper. The air smelled like glue sticks and fresh grass. Fingers felt sticky, then dry. Everyone hummed a happy tune.

Miss Olive Owl looked at their art. “Beautiful! The train shows time moving. The world shows our big family. The owl shows wisdom. And all from old things! Hoo-hoo!”

Sofía hugged Mei. “We did it together,” she said.

Amir nodded. “We used our time like slices.”

Ayo patted the bins. “We sorted. We saved. We helped.”

Miss Olive Owl flapped her wings gently. “Today you learned three big ideas. One, recycling gives old things a new life. Two, friends from many places can work together. Three, when we plan our time, we can do big jobs in small steps.”

The sun began to soften in the sky. The backyard was clean and calm. The bins were neat. The art shone bright on the porch.

“Hoo-hoo,” Miss Olive Owl said softly. “Remember: Look, listen, and think. Sort with care. Share with friends. Use your time in little parts. The Earth thanks you.”

The children waved. “Thank you, Miss Olive Owl!”

“See you tomorrow,” she hooted. “There will be more small slices of time, and more big smiles.”

The End.
