# Sunny Song in the Treehouse

High in a friendly oak, a little singing bird named Sunny woke up in her cozy treehouse. The morning light peeked through a round window. Sunny stretched her wings and chirped, “Good morning, day! I am ready to sing!”

Sunny had a soft nest bed, a tiny water cup, and a little basket for snacks. She loved her treehouse. It smelled like fresh leaves. The breeze made the curtains swish-swish. Sunny touched her beak to the window. “Cool glass,” she giggled.

Sunny had a plan. “Today I will sing for my friends,” she said. “But first, I take care of me.” Sunny remembered her wellness plan. She kept it simple and kind.

“Drink water,” she sang, sipping her cup. “Sip-sip.” The cool water felt good. Her throat said ahhh.

“Stretch and breathe,” she chirped. Sunny opened her wings wide. In... and out... slow and gentle. “My body feels ready,” she said. She did three tiny hops. Hop, hop, hop!

“Healthy snack,” she hummed. Sunny took a bite of a juicy berry. “Sweet and bright!” The red berry made her cheeks feel happy.

“Check the weather,” Sunny said, peeking outside. The sky was sunny, but the wind whispered shhhh. “It might be breezy,” she noticed. “I’ll be prepared.”

Sunny packed her little bag. She put in:
- A tiny scarf for her throat.
- A spare water cup.
- A little note that said, “I can say no.” It helped her remember to be brave and clear.

She fluffed her feathers and flew to the treehouse door. Tap-tap-tap! Her friends arrived. Hoot the owl blinked kindly. Pip the squirrel bounced. Bella the butterfly fluttered soft and bright.

“Hi, Sunny!” Pip chattered. “Let’s sing now, now, now!”

Sunny smiled. “I am excited too. First, a warm-up,” she said. “Warm-ups keep us well.”

Hoot nodded. “Wise idea.”

They hummed low. “Mmmmmm.” They hummed high. “Meeeeee.” Sunny shook her shoulders gently. “Loose and light,” she giggled.

Outside, the wind brushed the leaves. Whooooosh. Sunny felt it on her feathers. “I brought a scarf,” she told her friends. “If our throats feel cold, we can use it.”

Bella fluttered near. “Thank you, Sunny. You are prepared.”

They went to the singing spot, a wide branch with a soft moss rug. Sunny placed her little water cup on a leaf plate. “Take sips,” she said. “Sips help our voices.”

Pip bounced again. “Let’s sing a big, loud song!”

Sunny took a calm breath. “I like to start gentle,” she said softly. “Gentle keeps our voices safe.” She looked at Pip’s bright eyes. “I will start soft. You can join me.”

Sunny sang a sweet morning song. La-la-la, light and warm. Birds in nearby trees tilted their heads. The sun felt warm on Sunny’s back. The moss felt cool under her feet. Her song floated like bubbles.

Pip sang loud-loud. His voice squeaked. “Eeeep!”

Sunny held up a wing. “Pause, friend,” she said kindly. “Let’s try soft first. Your voice is special. It needs care.”

Pip blinked. “Okay,” he said, a little shy.

They sang softly together. La-la-la. Pip smiled. “That feels nice.”

Suddenly, a big crow swooped close. “Sing louder!” the crow cawed. “Louder, louder!” His voice was rough and windy.

Sunny felt her tummy tighten. She put a feather on her scarf and stood tall. “No, thank you,” she said, calm and clear. “Loud is not safe for our voices right now. We are warming up.”

The crow huffed. “But I want loud.”

Sunny breathed in slowly. She remembered her note: I can say no. “I hear you,” she said. “We choose soft singing now. You can listen, or you can fly to another branch.”

The crow looked surprised. He looked at the soft moss. He looked at Sunny’s kind eyes. “Hmph,” he said, and flew away with a flap-flap.

Bella whispered, “Sunny, that was brave.”

Sunny nodded. “I used my voice to keep us well.”

They sang their next song a little stronger, but still gentle. Hoot kept the beat with a soft hoo-hoo. Bella danced in the air. Pip grinned and stayed steady.

After the songs, Sunny passed her water cup around. “Sip-sip,” she said. She tucked the scarf around her neck as the breeze blew cooler. Whooooosh.

“Snack time,” Pip chirped. They shared berries and tiny nuts. Crunch, munch, yum.

The wind suddenly pushed a small branch onto the moss. Thump! Sunny checked everyone. “Is everyone okay?”

“Yes,” said Hoot. “All good,” buzzed Bella. “I’m okay!” piped Pip.

Sunny looked at the sky. A gray cloud drifted in. “It’s time to go inside,” she said. “We can finish our music in the warm treehouse.”

Inside, it smelled like wood and sweet leaves. Sunny closed the door softly. The rain began to patter. Pit-pat, pit-pat. The friends sat on the nest rug.

Sunny smiled. “We planned. We warmed up. We said no when we needed to. We took care of our bodies.”

Pip nodded. “I learned to start soft.”

Hoot blinked. “I learned that being prepared helps us feel calm.”

Bella fluttered. “I learned to use my voice kindly and clearly.”

Sunny sang a gentle good-bye song. La-la-la, soft and bright. The rain answered with its own song. Pit-pat, pit-pat.

Sunny felt warm and proud. “Wellness means caring for our bodies, our voices, and our feelings,” she said. “Preparation helps. And being assertive keeps us safe.”

The friends hugged. “We did great,” Sunny chirped.

They all smiled, cozy in the treehouse, ready for another happy day.

The End.
