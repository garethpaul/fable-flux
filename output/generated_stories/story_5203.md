# The Quiet Click of Calm

In a cozy study corner, a kind mouse named Miri lived behind a stack of soft notebooks. The study corner smelled like paper and warm crayons. Sunlight made little squares on the desk, and a clock went tick-tick in a friendly way.

Every morning, Miri peeked out and listened. Today, the pencils were standing tall in a cup. The glue stick wore its cap like a hat. A small timer sat on the desk and blinked a red dot. On the chair was a blue cushion that looked like a puffy cloud.

Miri liked to help. She brushed crumbs off the desk with her tiny paws. “Good morning, study corner,” she squeaked kindly. “Let’s have a calm day.”

“Click!” went the timer, starting a soft beep-beep. It was Reading Time.

Miri nodded. “I will follow directions,” she said to herself. “First reading, then drawing.” She remembered the picture chart taped to the wall: a book, a pencil, and a star. It told the plan.

Miri opened a little picture book. The pages smelled like vanilla and dust. She read the pictures with her eyes and smiled. “Look, a duck! Quack-quack,” she giggled.

Suddenly, a big thump shook the desk. The pencil cup wobbled. “Hey!” cried a green pencil. “I was standing up!”

Out popped Nib, a tiny nib of a broken crayon. Nib zipped across the desk like a little stripe of color. “I want to draw now!” Nib squeaked. “I don’t want to wait. I don’t like the beep-beep!”

The timer kept going. Beep, pause, beep. Miri felt her whiskers wiggle. Her heart did a quick pitter-patter. She wanted to say, “Stop!” in a loud voice. But she remembered the calm picture chart.

Miri put a paw on her chest. “Hand on heart,” she whispered. She took a slow breath in. She let a slow breath out. In and out. Her tummy moved like a soft balloon.

“Nib,” Miri said in a gentle voice, “I hear you. You want to draw. The timer says we read first, then we draw. Can we talk?”

Nib hid behind the eraser. “I don’t like the beep-beep,” Nib said. “It makes my head feel bouncy.”

Miri listened. The room smelled like clean paper. The sun felt warm on her fur. She took another slow breath. “Thank you for telling me,” she said. “We can use calm. Let’s try a quiet plan.”

“What plan?” Nib peeped.

“We can make the beep softer,” said Miri. She pressed the timer’s soft button once. The beep-beep turned into a tiny blink. “There,” she smiled. “Now it’s silent, and we can watch the red dot go around.”

Nib peeked out. “Oh,” Nib said. “I feel better.”

Miri showed the chart on the wall. “Look, first the book, then the pencil.” She touched each picture with her paw. “We can follow directions. When we follow directions, everyone gets a turn.”

“But I want my turn now,” Nib huffed, rolling in a little circle.

“I hear that,” Miri said. “Let’s find a calm waiting job.” She looked around. The glue stick sat quiet. The paper clips were shiny like tiny fish. “Nib, can you help me count paper clips while we wait?”

Nib blinked. “Count?” Nib liked numbers. “Okay.”

They counted softly. “One, two, three, four.” The paper clips felt cool and smooth. The counting made a gentle rhythm, like the clock’s tick-tick. Miri’s heart slowed. Nib’s rolling stopped.

When the red dot on the timer reached the top, it flashed. Reading Time was done.

Miri clapped her tiny paws. “You did it! You waited!” she cheered. “Now it’s Drawing Time. Thank you for using calm and talking.”

Nib wiggled with joy. “My turn!”

Miri placed a clean sheet of paper on the desk. It smelled fresh, like a new day. She held Nib carefully. “What shall we draw?”

“A sharing picture,” Nib said. “We can draw us!”

They drew a bright sun with smiling faces. They drew a book and a pencil, and a little red dot going around. Miri hummed a soft tune. The pencil made a shhh-shhh sound on the page. Nib made bold, friendly lines.

Just then, the pencil cup tipped again. A blue pencil slid out and grumbled, “Hey, I’m next!”

Miri felt a tiny flutter in her chest. She put her paw back on her heart. In and out. Calm. “Blue,” she said kindly, “we are taking turns. You are after Nib. Look at the chart.” She pointed. “Nib draws, then Blue draws. You can do a waiting job too. Would you like to sort erasers by color?”

Blue looked at the pink and white erasers. “Okay,” Blue said, calmer now. “I can sort.”

They all worked together. The study corner felt peaceful. The clock ticked. The sun made warm squares. The air smelled sweet and safe.

When it was Blue’s turn, Miri said, “Thank you for waiting. You followed directions and used calm words. That was good communication.”

Blue smiled. “I feel proud.”

Miri smiled too. “When we feel wiggly, we can breathe. We can use calm words. We can follow the plan. Talking helps.”

At the end of the day, the picture chart had a big star. Miri stuck the star on with a tiny pat. “We did it,” she said. “We stayed calm, we talked, and we followed directions. That is how we solve problems.”

Nib, Blue, and the whole pencil cup cheered, “Hooray for calm!”

The study corner glowed with quiet joy.

The End.
