# Panda Poppy’s Pillow Plan

Panda Poppy lived in a cozy bedroom with soft blankets, a small bookshelf, and a cheerful night-light shaped like a moon. The room smelled like fresh cotton and warm tea. Sunlight peeked through the curtains and made golden stripes on the floor.

“Good morning,” Poppy said softly, patting her fluffy pillow. “Today is Fix-It Day!”

Poppy looked around. Her favorite bedtime book had a torn page. Her night-light wobbled and tilted. And her pillow had a little rip, with a tiny puff of fluff peeking out like a white cloud.

“Oh dear,” said Poppy. “I want everything to feel safe and snug for bedtime.”

Poppy took a slow breath in and out. She was patient and calm. “I can do this,” she said. “I will use what I have.”

She opened her little supply box. Inside were buttons, ribbon, paper, tape, and a small roll of soft yarn. A gentle breeze brushed the curtains. The room felt still and friendly.

First, Poppy held up the torn page in her book. “I love this story,” she whispered. “I want to keep it nice.”

She looked at the tape. The tape was shiny and smooth. She touched it with the tip of her paw. “Tape can help.”

Poppy gently lined up the torn edges, pressed the tape on the back, and smoothed it with a soft paw. “All better,” she said, smiling. “Thank you, tape.”

Next, Poppy looked at the wobbly night-light. “You are my moon,” she told it. “Let’s help you stand tall.”

She carefully checked the base. “Hmm,” she hummed. “It feels loose.” Poppy used her open mind and tried new ideas. She stacked two tiny books under one side. The light stood a little straighter, but still wobbled.

Poppy did not feel upset. She was patient. “Let me try again.”

She found a small ribbon in her box. It was silky and blue. “Maybe I can tie the night-light to the bedpost to help it stay still,” she said.

She tied a gentle bow. The night-light did not wobble now. It glowed warm and steady. “Thank you, ribbon,” Poppy said. The room felt cozy and safe.

Then Poppy turned to her pillow. The little rip peeked at her. “Hello, little rip,” she said kindly. “We will help you too.”

Poppy looked in her box. No needle. No thread. Just soft yarn.

“I will be resourceful,” Poppy said. “I will use what I have.”

She thought and thought. She tried to poke the yarn through the fabric, but the yarn was thick and fuzzy. “This is tricky,” she said. “I need a tool.”

She looked around the room with open eyes and an open mind. On her shelf, she saw a paper straw from a tea party. She saw a small button. She saw a paper clip shaped like a heart.

Poppy picked up the paper clip. “Hello,” she said to it. “You can be a helper.” She gently bent the paper clip into a tiny loop and slid the yarn through. Now she had a simple, safe loop to guide the yarn. She moved slowly and carefully.

Poppy whispered, “In…out…pull…pause.” Her paws worked in a calm rhythm. The yarn made soft, quiet sounds like swish, swish. The pillow felt warm and puffy under her paws. The room smelled like clean sheets.

Almost done, Poppy stopped. She looked at her work. “Is this strong? Is this neat? Am I being honest and careful?” she asked herself. Poppy believed in integrity. She wanted to do her best and tell the truth.

She tugged gently. One stitch was loose. “I will fix it,” Poppy said. She did not rush. She did not give up. She tied a small knot and tucked the yarn end inside. The pillow looked snug and happy. A tiny line of blue yarn made a cute smile on the corner.

Just then, Poppy’s friend, Little Sparrow, tapped on the window. Tap-tap. “Poppy, can I come in?” chirped Sparrow.

“Yes,” said Poppy, opening the window. A cool breeze brought in the smell of morning leaves.

Sparrow looked around. “Your book is fixed. Your moon is steady. Your pillow is smiling! How did you do all this?”

Poppy smiled. “I used what I had. I tried one idea, then another. I asked myself to be patient and honest. I talked kindly to my things, and I listened to my ideas.”

Sparrow chirped, “I have a loose feather on my nest. I was worried.”

Poppy nodded. “Let’s talk and think together. What do you have?”

“I have twigs, a ribbon, and a leaf,” Sparrow said.

Poppy said, “You can use the ribbon to help the feather stay. Try gently tying it to a twig.”

Sparrow flapped with joy. “Thank you! You are so calm and smart.”

Poppy said, “We can be calm together. We can try new ideas, talk kindly, and do our best.”

They sat on the bed, listening to the soft hum of the night-light. The room felt warm, bright, and peaceful. Poppy hugged her pillow. It was soft and safe.

Poppy said, “Today I learned that resourcefulness means using what you have, trying new ideas, and staying patient. Open minds find new ways. Kind words help us work together. And being honest helps our work stay strong.”

Sparrow chirped, “That is a good lesson.”

Poppy smiled. “Now bedtime will feel cozy and brave.”

They both took a slow breath in and out. The moon-night-light glowed like a friendly smile.

The End.
