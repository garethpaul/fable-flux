# Ollie Otter Speaks Up

Ollie the otter loved his cozy bedroom with its soft blue blankets and fluffy pillows that smelled like fresh laundry. Every morning, he would slide across the smooth wooden floor in his fuzzy socks, making happy chirping sounds. But today, something was wrong.

"Mama! Papa!" Ollie called out, his whiskers twitching with worry. "My favorite water bowl is empty, and I'm really thirsty!"

He waited and waited, listening for footsteps in the hallway. The house was quiet except for the gentle tick-tock of the clock on his nightstand. Ollie's throat felt dry and scratchy, like when he played too hard in the sun.

"Maybe they didn't hear me," Ollie whispered to his stuffed fish friend, Finny. He tried calling again, a little louder this time. "Mama! Papa! I need water, please!"

Still no answer. Ollie could hear the washing machine humming downstairs and knew his parents were busy with chores. His tummy started to feel worried and grumbly.

Ollie sat on his bed, swinging his little webbed feet back and forth. "What should I do, Finny?" he asked his toy. "I'm really, really thirsty, but Mama and Papa always fill my water bowl for me."

He looked at his empty bowl sitting on the low table by his window. The afternoon sunlight made rainbow patterns on the wall, and Ollie remembered something important his teacher had said at otter school: "When you need help, it's okay to ask. And sometimes, you can help yourself too!"

Ollie's brown eyes lit up with an idea. "I know! I can get water myself!" He slid off his bed, his paws making soft patting sounds on the carpet.

But then he stopped. The bathroom sink seemed so high up, and he wasn't sure if he was allowed to get water by himself. His whiskers drooped a little as he thought about what to do.

"I'll be very careful," Ollie decided, talking to himself in his squeaky otter voice. "And I'll tell Mama and Papa what I did so they know I was being responsible."

Slowly and carefully, Ollie picked up his special step-stool from beside his toy chest. It was painted with little fish and seaweed, just perfect for a young otter. He carried it to the bathroom, his arms wrapped tightly around it.

Step by step, Ollie climbed up to reach the sink. The faucet handle was cool under his paws as he turned it gently. Clear, fresh water flowed out, making a pleasant splashing sound that reminded him of playing in the river.

Ollie filled his bowl to just the right level – not too full, not too little – just like he had watched his parents do so many times. The water felt cool and refreshing as he took small, careful sips.

"I did it!" Ollie chirped happily, his tail wiggling with pride. "I got water all by myself!"

Just then, Mama Otter appeared in the doorway. "Ollie! I heard you calling earlier, but I was hanging laundry outside. I'm sorry I didn't hear you right away."

Ollie's eyes sparkled as he explained, "I was really thirsty, Mama, and when you didn't come, I remembered I could help myself! I used my step-stool and got water very carefully."

Mama Otter smiled warmly and gave Ollie a big, soft hug that smelled like sunshine and soap. "I'm so proud of you for speaking up when you needed something, and for being patient and thinking of a safe solution. You're becoming such a responsible little otter!"

That night, as Ollie snuggled into his cozy bed, he felt happy and proud. He had learned that it's important to ask for help when you need it, but sometimes you can solve problems yourself too.

The End.
