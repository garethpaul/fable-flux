# Chirpy's Clean Song

Chirpy was a bright yellow bird who loved to sing. She lived in a cozy cage in the Miller family's playful living room. Colorful toys were scattered on the soft carpet, and sunshine streamed through the big windows.

Every morning, Chirpy would wake up and sing her favorite song. "Tweet-tweet-tra-la-la! Good morning, world!" she would chirp happily.

But today was different. When Chirpy tried to sing, her voice sounded scratchy and rough. "Caw-caw-grumble," came out instead of her beautiful melody.

Little Emma, who was three years old, noticed right away. "Mama, why does Chirpy sound funny?" Emma asked, pointing at the bird cage.

Mama walked over and looked at Chirpy carefully. "Oh my," she said gently. "I think Chirpy needs to clean herself. Look at her feathers - they're all messy and dusty."

Chirpy looked down at herself. Her bright yellow feathers were indeed dirty and tangled. She had forgotten to preen and clean herself for many days. No wonder her throat felt scratchy!

"But I don't know how to get clean," Chirpy chirped sadly. "I've forgotten what my mama bird taught me."

Emma's eyes lit up. "I know how to get clean! Mama teaches me every day!"

Emma ran to the bathroom and came back with a small cup of fresh water. "First, you need clean water," she explained to Chirpy. "Just like I brush my teeth with clean water."

Mama helped Emma pour the fresh water into Chirpy's water dish. "That's right, Emma. Clean water is very important for staying healthy."

Chirpy dipped her beak into the cool, fresh water. It felt wonderful! She began to splash gently, letting the water reach her dusty feathers.

"Now you need to clean each feather," Emma said, pretending to comb her own hair. "Like when I brush my hair every morning!"

Chirpy remembered now! Her mama had taught her to use her beak like a tiny comb. She carefully preened each feather, smoothing them down and removing the dust and dirt.

As Chirpy cleaned herself, she felt better and better. The scratchy feeling in her throat began to go away.

"In my family," said Mama, "we always say 'Clean body, happy heart.' My grandmother taught that to me, and her grandmother taught it to her."

Emma clapped her hands. "Clean body, happy heart!" she repeated. "That's a nice saying!"

After Chirpy finished cleaning all her feathers, she felt amazing. Her yellow feathers were bright and fluffy again. She took a deep breath and opened her beak.

"Tweet-tweet-tra-la-la! Good morning, world!" Her beautiful song came out clear and sweet, just like before.

"Chirpy, you sound wonderful!" Emma giggled and danced around the living room.

Chirpy was so grateful. "Thank you for reminding me to stay clean," she sang to Emma. "From now on, I'll clean my feathers every single day, just like you brush your teeth!"

Emma smiled proudly. She had helped her feathered friend remember something very important.

From that day on, Chirpy made cleaning part of her daily routine. Every morning after singing her wake-up song, she would carefully clean her feathers and drink fresh water. And Emma would always say, "Clean body, happy heart!" whenever she saw Chirpy preening.

Chirpy's songs became more beautiful than ever, and she felt healthy and happy every single day.

The End.
