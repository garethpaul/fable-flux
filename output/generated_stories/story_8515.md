# The Fair Porch Parade

On a warm, bright morning, a gentle elephant calf named Elli sat on the peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like fresh bread from the kitchen and felt smooth under Elli’s soft feet. A light breeze tickled her ears. Birds sang little songs, “Chirp, chirp,” and a wind chime went, “Ting, ting, ting.”

Elli loved words. She whispered them to herself, testing how they felt. “Hello. Thank you. Fair.” She liked how “fair” felt round and kind in her mouth. Elli also loved to craft. When Mama set out a small basket with colored paper, yarn, and shiny stickers, Elli clapped her big ears. “We can make porch flags!” she squeaked.

Mama smiled. “Yes, sweet Elli. Let’s make flags that say kind words. Words can help us be fair. Words can help us be just.”

Elli nodded. “Just means fair,” she said softly. The new word felt strong, like her little legs.

Soon, two friends padded up the steps. Tiko the tiny turtle clicked his shell on the wood. Mimi the moth fluttered softly. “Hi, Elli!” they chimed.

“Hi!” Elli said. “We’re making flags. Everyone can make one for the Porch Parade!”

Tiko stretched his neck. “I want a blue flag with a big star.”

Mimi hummed. “I want a sparkly flag with two shiny stickers.”

Elli’s eyes grew wide. “We have to share the shiny stickers. We only have four.” She counted with her trunk. “One, two, three, four.”

Tiko whispered, “I want two shiny stickers too.”

Mimi folded her wings. “But I asked first.”

Elli felt a little twist in her tummy. She wanted to make everyone happy. She wanted to be fair. She rubbed her trunk on the smooth porch rail and took a slow breath. “We can talk,” she said. “Talking helps us find what is just.”

Mama nodded from the doorway. “Use your words. You can do it.”

Elli put the stickers in the middle. “We have four shiny stickers and three friends,” she said. “Justice means we share in a fair way. What is a fair way?”

Tiko tapped the wood: tap, tap. “Maybe we all get the same?” he said.

Mimi’s wings made a soft “fuff.” “But I asked first,” she reminded them.

Elli listened. Her ears felt warm in the sun, and the wind chime sang again, “Ting, ting.” She thought about words: “listen,” “share,” “fair,” “first,” “same.”

She smiled gently. “Let’s use two fair rules,” she said. “Rule one: We listen to each other. Rule two: We take turns.”

Tiko and Mimi nodded.

Elli spoke slowly. “Mimi asked first. Mimi can choose one sticker first. Then Tiko can choose one. Then I can choose one. Then we will see what is left.”

Mimi chose a star that glittered like sunshine. Tiko chose a moon that gleamed like milk. Elli chose a tiny silver dot that shone like dew.

One sticker sat in the middle, winking.

Tiko looked from the sticker to Mimi to Elli. “Who gets the last one?” he asked.

Elli thought again. She felt the porch under her feet. She heard the birds. The world felt quiet and kind as she chose. “We will talk again. Who needs it most for their flag idea?” she asked.

Mimi blinked softly. “I have one,” she said. “One is enough for me.”

Tiko rubbed his shell. “I wanted two… but one is okay. My flag can still be nice.”

Elli felt a warm glow. “Then I will not take it,” she said. “Tiko, you can have the last one. That feels fair to me because Mimi and I are happy with one. Justice is not only the same for all. Justice is choosing what is fair for all.”

Tiko’s eyes shone. “Thank you. That is just.”

They began to craft. Snip, snip went the scissors. Shuffle, shuffle went the paper. Glue made a soft “squish.” Elli’s trunk held yarn while Tiko tied knots, and Mimi pressed stickers oh-so-gently. The porch smelled like glue and lemon wood polish. Their flags danced in the breeze: Elli’s flag said “Kind,” Mimi’s said “Listen,” and Tiko’s said “Share.”

A tiny ant line marched across the porch. Elli watched. The ants took turns carrying a crumb. “They know about responsibility,” Elli said. “They all help.”

Mimi nodded. “We can be responsible too.”

Tiko puffed up with pride. “My job is to carry the flags to the rail,” he said.

“I will hold the basket,” Mimi buzzed. “I will not drop it.”

“I will check we have all we need,” Elli said. She counted again. “Three flags, one basket, four shiny stickers used. We did it right.”

They hung the flags. The wind chime sang, “Ting, ting, ting,” and the flags fluttered like soft bird wings.

Mama clapped. “What did you learn today, little ones?”

Elli lifted her trunk. “We learned justice,” she said. “Justice means fair. We listened, we shared, and we took turns.”

Tiko added, “We were responsible. We did our jobs and we were careful.”

Mimi whispered, “Kind words help. ‘Please,’ ‘thank you,’ ‘your turn,’ and ‘fair’ are good words.”

Elli felt proud. Her heart felt big and calm, like the porch itself. She wrapped her trunk around her friends in a gentle hug. “This was a good Porch Parade.”

The flags shimmered. The porch felt peaceful. The friends smiled.

Elli spoke one more word, slow and happy: “Justice.”

The End.
