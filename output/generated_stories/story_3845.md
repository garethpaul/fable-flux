# Benny the Beaver's Big Problem

In a sunny backyard filled with tall green grass and colorful flowers, lived a busy beaver named Benny. Benny had soft brown fur and big white teeth that gleamed in the sunshine. He loved to build things with sticks and mud.

One bright morning, Benny woke up to a terrible sight. The little stream that flowed through the backyard was almost dry! The water barely trickled over the smooth stones.

"Oh no!" said Benny, his whiskers twitching with worry. "The fish need water to swim, and the flowers need water to drink. What can I do?"

Benny sat on a warm rock and thought very hard. He scratched his furry head with his tiny paw. "I need to think like a smart beaver," he said to himself.

First, Benny looked around carefully. He noticed that the water was flowing away too fast through a big crack in the stream bank. "Aha!" he squeaked with excitement. "The water is escaping!"

But how could one little beaver fix such a big problem? Benny thought and thought. Then he remembered something important his mama had taught him: "When you have a big problem, break it into little pieces."

Benny made a plan. "First, I need to gather materials. Then, I need to build something to slow down the water."

He waddled around the sunny backyard, collecting fallen twigs with his strong teeth. The twigs smelled like fresh wood and felt smooth in his mouth. He also gathered soft mud from the stream edge. The mud felt cool and squishy between his paws.

"Now for the building part!" Benny said happily.

Carefully, Benny began weaving the twigs together, just like his family had taught him. He placed them across the crack in the stream bank. The work was hard, and sweat dripped from his furry forehead in the warm sunshine.

"This is tricky," Benny huffed, "but I won't give up!"

Next, he packed the soft mud around the twigs, pressing it down firmly with his flat tail. Pat, pat, pat! The mud made a squishy sound as he worked.

Just then, a little blue bird landed nearby. "What are you doing, Benny?" chirped the bird.

"I'm fixing the stream so everyone has water," Benny explained proudly. "The fish, the flowers, and all the backyard animals need it."

"That's very kind of you!" said the bird. "You're helping the whole community!"

As Benny finished his work, something wonderful happened. The water began to slow down and pool behind his little dam. Soon, the stream was flowing gently again, with plenty of water for everyone.

The fish swam happily in the deeper water. The flowers lifted their colorful heads as their roots drank. A family of rabbits came to take a cool drink in the shade.

"I did it!" Benny cheered, clapping his small paws together. "When I broke the big problem into smaller steps, I could solve it!"

The sun shone warmly on Benny's successful project. He felt proud and happy, knowing that his careful thinking and hard work had helped everyone in the backyard community.

From that day on, whenever Benny faced a problem, he remembered to stop, think, make a plan, and work step by step. And the backyard stayed green and beautiful for all the animals to enjoy.

The End.
