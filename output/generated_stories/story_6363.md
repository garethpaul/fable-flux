# Panda Pippa’s Sunny Backyard Dance

Panda Pippa woke up with a soft yawn and a big smile. “Today feels sunny and special,” she said. She patted her tummy. “I feel a dance inside me!”

She stepped into her sunny backyard. The grass was warm and tickly under her paws. A sweet breeze smelled like oranges and flowers. Birds chirped, “Chee-chee!” The sun felt like a gentle hug.

Pippa lifted her arms and swayed. “Sway, sway,” she sang. She tapped her toes. “Tap, tap.” She twirled slowly so she wouldn’t get dizzy. “I love dancing,” she whispered. “I can be patient. I can learn.”

Her friend, Tiny Turtle Tim, peeked from under a leaf. “Hi, Pippa,” Tim said. “What are you doing?”

“I’m dancing in the sun,” Pippa said. “Would you like to join?”

Tim smiled. “Yes, but I am slow.”

“That’s okay,” Pippa said kindly. “We can dance slow together.” She showed Tim a simple step. “Sway left. Sway right. Tap, tap.”

Tim tried. “Sway… sway… tap, tap.” His shell made a gentle “clack” sound. He giggled. “I did it!”

Next, Bunny Bella hopped in. “Can I dance too?” she asked, ears wiggling.

“Yes!” said Pippa. “Everyone can dance. We can share the sunshine and the music in our hearts.” She clapped a soft beat. “Clap, clap, clap, clap.” The sound was crisp and happy.

Bella hopped to the beat. “Hop, hop!” Her feet made little thumps on the soft ground.

Squirrel Sammy ran along the fence with a flick of his tail. “I want to spin!” he squeaked.

Pippa nodded. “Let’s try a gentle spin. We spin slowly so we stay safe. If you need space, you can say, ‘Please give me room.’”

Sammy twirled once and giggled. “I like gentle spins!”

Soon, more friends came by. Bluebird Bea chirped a song that sounded like tiny bells. “Ting-a-ling!” The leaves whispered “shhh” as they swayed. The sun made the grass sparkle.

Pippa took a deep breath. “Friends,” she said, “let’s make a backyard dance show! We will dance for each other. We will be kind and share. We will use our voices if we need help.”

“Yay!” everyone cheered.

Pippa found a bright blanket. She spread it like a stage. “We can take turns,” she said. “We can give and we can receive. That is generosity.”

Tim raised his hand slowly. “May I go first?” he asked.

“Yes, Tim,” Pippa said. “Thank you for asking.”

Tim did the sway-sway-tap-tap. The friends clapped. “Clap, clap!” Tim’s eyes shone. “I did it because I practiced,” he said.

Bella bounced on the blanket. “May I do bunny hops?” she asked.

“You may,” said Pippa. “Please keep your hops soft.”

Bella hopped in a gentle rhythm. “Hop, hop, hop.” She smiled. “I like sharing my dance.”

Sammy stepped up. “I need space for my spin,” he said clearly. “Please move back a little.”

“Good self-advocacy,” Pippa said. “You used your words.” Everyone took a step back. Sammy twirled slowly and then bowed. “Ta-da!”

Now it was Pippa’s turn. She felt a flutter in her tummy. “I want to try a new move,” she said. “But I feel a little shy.”

Bea sang softly, “You can do it, Pippa.”

Pippa nodded. “I can be brave and patient.” She started with sway-sway, then tap-tap, then a tiny tiptoe. Her paws made soft puffs on the grass: “puff, puff.” She added a gentle arm wave like a floating cloud. “I did it!” she said, surprised and happy.

The friends cheered. “Hooray for Pippa!”

Just then, a big gust of wind whooshed through the backyard. “Whooooosh!” It lifted the blanket stage and flipped it over the daisies.

“Oh!” said Bella. “Now what do we do?”

Pippa took a calm breath. The breeze smelled crisp, like fresh leaves. “We can solve this together,” she said. “Please help me hold the corners.”

Tim said, “I am slow, but I can hold one corner.” He waddled to the edge.

“I can hold a corner too,” said Bella.

“I can find a rock to keep it down,” said Sammy, zipping off.

“I will sing a steady beat,” said Bea. “La-la-la.” Her song helped everyone move together.

Soon the blanket was flat again, and a smooth rock held it still. Pippa smiled. “Thank you for helping. That was generous.”

“I have a request,” Tim said softly. “Could we do the dance one more time? I want to practice my taps.”

“Yes,” said Pippa. “Thank you for telling us what you need.”

They danced again, slower this time. Pippa counted with a warm voice. “One, two. Sway, sway. Three, four. Tap, tap.” The sun glowed on their faces. The grass smelled sweet. Their feet made friendly sounds on the ground.

After the dance, Pippa shared juicy orange slices from a small basket. “Let’s share a snack,” she said. The slices were bright and sticky-sweet. “Mmm,” said everyone.

Pippa wiped her paws and looked around. “Friends, today we learned something,” she said.

“What did we learn?” asked Bella, licking her fingers.

Pippa smiled. “Dance is for everyone. We can be patient when we learn. We can be generous and share turns and help. We can use our voices to ask for space or more time. When we dance and care for each other, our hearts feel sunny, just like today.”

Everyone nodded. “Let’s dance again tomorrow,” said Sammy.

“Yes,” said Pippa. “We can practice, be kind, and speak up.”

They all did one last gentle sway together. “Sway, sway,” they whispered, happy and calm.

The End.
