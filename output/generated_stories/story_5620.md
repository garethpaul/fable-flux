# Joey Roo and the Closet Club

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with soft brown fur and a bouncy tail. He loved to hop and hum little songs. One rainy morning, Joey Roo wiggled into the hallway closet. It was warm and cozy, like a tiny cave. The closet smelled like clean cotton and wooden shelves. Soft scarves tickled his nose. “Ah-choo!” he giggled. “This is my hideaway!”

Inside the closet, there were boxes, buttons, and a flashlight. Joey clicked the flashlight on. The light made a little circle on the wall. “Hello, Closet Club,” he whispered. “What can we build today?”

Mama Roo opened the closet door a crack. “Joey, sweetheart,” she said with a smile, “the neighborhood is having a Share and Care Day. We are building a Little Snack Library for everyone. It will hold healthy snacks so friends can eat well. But first, we need to tidy the closet and gather supplies.”

Joey’s ears perked up. “A library for snacks? Yum!” He rubbed his tummy. It made a tiny rumble. “Can I help?”

“Yes,” said Mama Roo. “But we must follow directions and complete each task. When we finish one job, we can go to the next. That is how we build good things.”

Joey Roo clapped. “I can do tasks! I am a Task Kangaroo!”

Mama Roo handed him a simple list with pictures: a broom, a box, a cloth, a snack, and a hammer. She pointed her paw. “First: sweep. Second: sort. Third: wipe. Fourth: pack healthy snacks. Fifth: bring the tools so we can build.”

Joey read the pictures with his eyes and said the words out loud. “Sweep. Sort. Wipe. Pack. Bring.” He took a deep breath. He could smell orange soap. “I can do it!”

He started to sweep. Swish, swish, swish went the broom. Dust bunnies danced away. “Bye-bye, fluff!” Joey sang. His tail thumped softly in the tiny space. When he finished, he set the broom back. “Task one, done!”

Mama Roo peeked in. “Great job, Joey. What comes next?”

Joey looked at the list. “Sort!” He opened two boxes. One box had crayons, tape, and stickers. The other had mixed buttons, ribbons, and a little bell. Joey made neat piles. “Buttons here. Ribbons there. Bell goes ding!” The bell made a happy tink sound. Joey smiled. He loved how the stickers felt smooth and the ribbons felt silky. “Task two, done!”

“Wonderful,” said Mama Roo. “What is task three?”

“Wipe!” Joey sprayed a tiny spray of orange soap on the shelf. Spritz! The closet smelled fresh and sunny. He wiped in little circles. Wipe, wipe, wipe. The shelf shined. Joey wiggled with pride. “Task three, done!”

Mama Roo nodded. “You are following directions. Now, we need snacks for the Little Snack Library. What snacks should we pack?”

Joey thought. He loved crunchy crackers shaped like stars. He also liked carrot sticks and apple slices. Mama Roo said, “We need healthy foods that help us grow strong. Colors and crunch are good clues.”

Joey tapped his chin. “Apples are red and sweet. Carrots are orange and crunchy. Crackers can be whole grain.” His tummy hummed again. “Can we share these?”

“Yes,” said Mama Roo. “Let’s pack them.” Joey washed his paws with warm water. Splash, splash. He dried them on a soft towel. Pat, pat. Then he helped Mama Roo put applesauce cups, whole grain crackers, and dried carrot chips into a box. The box rustled as the packets slid inside. “Task four, done!” Joey cheered. “We are helping our community. Sharing is caring!”

“Last task,” Mama Roo said kindly. “We need building tools for the Little Snack Library. Let’s bring the small hammer, the safe nails, and the paint.”

Joey’s eyes grew big. He carefully carried a small toolbox. “I will be gentle,” he whispered. The metal jingled a little. He liked the steady feel of the handle in his paw. “Task five, done!”

They hopped to the porch where neighbors were waiting under a long, dry awning. Mr. Bear waved a big paw. “Hello, Joey! Ready to build?”

Joey nodded. “We followed the directions. We completed our tasks!”

Ms. Duck quacked happily. “Let’s put it together.” Mama Roo guided Joey: “Hold the board. Tap, tap, tap. Paint a bright sign.”

Joey held, tapped, and painted. The paint smelled like lemons. He drew a big red apple and an orange carrot on the sign. “Little Snack Library,” he said slowly as Mama helped him write the words. His tail swished with excitement.

When they finished, they placed the snacks inside. The box looked neat and welcoming. A little bunny hopped up. “May I have an applesauce?” the bunny asked softly.

“Yes,” said Joey. “This is for everyone.”

The bunny smiled and took one. “Thank you!”

Joey’s chest felt warm and full, like sunshine in his hideaway. Mama Roo squeezed his paw. “You did it, Joey. You followed directions, completed your tasks, and helped the community.”

Joey peeked back at the house. He could see the cozy closet from the open door. He remembered the sweep-swish, the spritz-wipe, the sort-click, and the gentle tap-tap. He grinned. “Tasks help us build good things,” he said. “When we do one step at a time, we finish strong.”

The rain stopped. A rainbow spread across the sky, soft and bright. Joey bounced once, twice, three times. “I am a Task Kangaroo!” he laughed.

Mama Roo laughed too. “Yes, my joyful joey. And your kind heart makes our whole town hop with happiness.”

The End.
