# Ruby's Reading Adventure

In a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls and fluffy white curtains, lived a determined little rabbit named Ruby. Her bedroom was filled with colorful toys, but Ruby had her eyes on something else - a big bookshelf full of books.

Ruby loved looking at the pretty pictures in books, but she had a big problem. She couldn't read the words yet, and it made her feel sad.

"I want to read stories like Mama and Papa," Ruby said, touching her long ears nervously. "But the letters look like squiggly lines to me."

Every night, Mama Rabbit would read bedtime stories to Ruby. Ruby would snuggle under her warm, soft blanket and listen to the magical tales. But deep inside, she wished she could read them herself.

One evening, Ruby felt very brave. She hopped over to her parents, who were sitting on the big reading chair.

"Mama, Papa," Ruby said in her sweetest voice. "I want to learn to read. Will you teach me?"

Papa Rabbit smiled. "Reading takes lots of practice, little one. Are you ready to work hard?"

Ruby nodded so fast her ears bounced. "Yes! I'm very determined!"

But Mama looked worried. "Learning to read can be frustrating sometimes. What if you get upset?"

Ruby thought carefully. She was good at solving problems. "How about this," she said, using her best negotiating voice. "If I practice reading for ten minutes every day, can we have extra story time when I get frustrated?"

Mama and Papa looked at each other and smiled. "That sounds like a fair deal," Papa said.

The next morning, Ruby started her reading adventure. Mama showed her how letters make sounds. "A says 'ah' like in apple," Mama explained, pointing to a red apple picture.

Ruby tried hard, but some letters were tricky. When she saw the letter 'b', she got confused. "This looks like a 'd' to me," she said, feeling discouraged.

"That's okay," Papa said gently. "Even brave rabbits need practice. Remember our deal?"

Ruby nodded and took a deep breath. She touched the smooth pages of her book and tried again. "B says 'buh' like in... bunny!" she said proudly.

Day after day, Ruby practiced. She traced letters with her tiny paw, sounded out simple words, and asked lots of questions. When she felt frustrated, her parents would read her an extra story, just like they promised.

After two weeks of practice, something magical happened. Ruby was looking at her favorite picture book when she realized she could read some words!

"Cat... sat... on... mat!" Ruby read out loud, her voice full of excitement. "I can read! I can really read!"

Mama and Papa hugged Ruby tight. "You were so brave to keep trying," Mama said. "And you were very smart to negotiate a plan that helped you succeed."

Ruby felt proud and happy. Her cozy bedroom felt even more special now that she could read the books on her shelf. She had learned that being determined, brave, and willing to work with others could help her achieve her dreams.

That night, Ruby read her very first bedtime story to her stuffed animals. As she snuggled under her soft blanket, she smiled. Tomorrow, she would read even more books in her cozy bedroom.

The End.
