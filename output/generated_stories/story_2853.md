# Ollie Otter’s Caring Break

Ollie the playful otter loved the study corner. It was a cozy spot by the window. The soft rug felt warm on his paws. The little lamp made a gentle, golden glow. Crayons, books, and paper were in tidy baskets. The air smelled like fresh paper and lemony clean shelves.

“Time to learn!” Ollie chirped. His whiskers wiggled with joy.

Today, Ollie wanted to make picture cards for the neighborhood book box. “I’m volunteering,” he said. “I will make happy cards for people who love to read.” He dipped his paw in blue paint. “Swoosh!” The paint felt cool and slippery. He giggled and drew a big smiling sun.

Soon his friend Pip, a small sparrow, fluttered in. “Hi, Ollie! What are you doing?”

“I’m making cards to share,” Ollie said. “Do you want to help?”

“Yes!” Pip peeped. “You’re a great leader, Ollie.”

Ollie’s cheeks felt warm. He liked helping. He liked leading too. “Let’s make ten cards,” he said. “We can do it!”

They got busy. Ollie made flowers and rainbows. Pip stamped tiny leaf shapes with a sponge. The soft sounds of coloring filled the room: scratch-scratch, tap-tap, swish-swish. The lamp hummed softly. It felt peaceful and good.

After a while, Ollie’s paws felt tired. His eyes felt heavy. His tummy made a little rumble. He frowned. “No time to stop,” he said. “We must finish.”

Pip tilted her head. “You look droopy, Ollie.”

“I’m okay,” Ollie said. He reached for another card, but his lines got wiggly and crooked. His shoulders felt tight. He dropped a crayon. “Oops.”

Pip fluttered closer. “Maybe we need a break.”

Ollie shook his head. “Leaders keep going,” he said. “Helpers keep going too.”

Pip chirped softly. “Leaders also take care of themselves.”

Ollie blinked. He thought about the word “care.” He sniffed and noticed he was thirsty. His nose twitched at the smell of peanut butter in the snack basket. His paws tingled. His heart thumped fast.

A little worry cloud floated in. “What if we don’t finish?” Ollie whispered. “I want to be helpful.”

Pip placed a tiny wing on his paw. “Self-care helps us help others. When we rest, we do our best.”

Ollie looked around the study corner. He saw the comfy pillow by the window. He saw the water bottle with the blue lid. He saw the timer on the shelf. He took a slow breath. In and out. In and out. The breath felt cool in his nose and warm as he let it go. His chest relaxed.

“Okay,” Ollie said. “Let’s try a caring break.”

They set the timer for five minutes. Tick… tick… tick…

Ollie took a sip of water. “Ahh,” he said. He nibbled a crunchy apple slice from the snack basket. “Crunch-crunch.” Sweet juice dribbled on his chin. He wiped it with a soft napkin. Pip sipped from a tiny cup. They stretched paws and wings. Ollie rolled his shoulders. “Stretch up, stretch wide,” he sang in a silly, sweet voice.

They sat on the pillow and listened. Outside, leaves whispered. A faraway car hummed. Inside, the lamp glowed like honey. The tick-tick-tick of the timer was steady and calm. Ollie felt cozy. His tummy felt full. His paws felt light.

The timer chimed. Ding!

Ollie smiled. “I feel better,” he said. “My body says ‘thank you.’”

Pip fluttered to the table. “Ready to lead again?”

Ollie nodded. “Yes! First, let’s make a plan. Good leaders make simple plans.”

He made three steps on a sticky note:
1. Work for a bit.
2. Take a caring break.
3. Check our feelings.

Pip peeped, “That’s a caring plan!”

They got back to work. Ollie’s lines were steady now. His rainbow was bright and smooth. Pip’s leaves were neat and green. They hummed a happy tune together. When Ollie’s paws felt buzzy, they stopped. They stretched. They sipped. They smiled. Then they started again.

Soon, ten beautiful cards lay in a row. Stars and suns sparkled. There were kind words too: “You are brave.” “You are kind.” “Have a happy read!”

Ollie looked at the cards and wiggled with joy. “We did it!”

Pip flapped. “Your self-care helped us finish. You led with kindness.”

Just then, two neighbors peeked in—Milo the mouse and Nia the newt. “Do you need help carrying the cards to the book box?” Milo asked.

“Yes, please,” Ollie said. “Thank you for volunteering!”

They all walked together. The cards rustled softly. The evening light glowed peach and pink. The air smelled like grass and warm pavement.

At the book box, they slid the cards inside. A child nearby smiled and clapped. “So pretty!” the child said.

Ollie’s heart felt big and warm. He turned to his friends. “Today I learned something,” he said. “Self-care is not stopping the help. Self-care is how we keep helping. Good leaders listen to their bodies. Good helpers take caring breaks. Then we can share even more.”

Pip cheered. Milo and Nia nodded. They all took one more big, calm breath together.

Ollie patted the study corner pillow when they returned. “Thank you, break spot,” he whispered. He set the timer on the shelf for next time.

The End.
