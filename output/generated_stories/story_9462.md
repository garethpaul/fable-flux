# Oliver Owl's Morning Muddle

Oliver Owl lived in a cozy home office filled with colorful papers, shiny scissors, and jars of glue that sparkled like stars. Every morning, Oliver loved to make beautiful crafts at his big wooden desk by the sunny window.

But today was different. Oliver woke up feeling all mixed up and confused.

"Oh my feathers!" hooted Oliver, ruffling his soft brown wings. "I can't remember what I'm supposed to do first!"

He looked around his messy office. Papers were scattered everywhere like fallen leaves. His favorite blue scissors were hiding under a pile of ribbons. The glue jars were all mixed up - big ones next to little ones instead of lined up nicely.

Oliver's big yellow eyes grew wide with worry. "How can I make my morning craft if everything is so jumbled?" he wondered aloud.

He tried to start anyway. First, he reached for some red paper, but it was buried under green paper. Then he looked for his scissors, but they weren't in their special spot. When he finally found them, he couldn't find any glue!

"This is too hard!" Oliver said, feeling frustrated. His feathers drooped sadly. "Maybe I should just give up."

But then Oliver remembered something very important. His grandmother had once told him, "When things feel scary or hard, little owl, remember that being brave means trying again, even when it's difficult."

Oliver took a deep breath that smelled like the fresh morning air coming through his window. "I can be brave," he said to himself. "I just need to make a plan!"

First, Oliver decided to clean up his office. He put all the red papers together in one neat pile. Then he gathered all the blue papers. The green papers went in their own special stack too.

"Red, blue, green - all in a row!" Oliver sang happily as he worked.

Next, he put his scissors in their special drawer. The big glue jars went on the left side of his desk, and the small ones went on the right side.

"Big and small, each in their place!" Oliver hooted with joy.

Finally, Oliver made a colorful chart with pictures showing his morning routine:
1. First, clean the desk
2. Then, choose the paper colors
3. Next, get the scissors ready
4. Last, pick the perfect glue

Oliver hung his chart right where he could see it every morning. The bright colors made him smile.

"Now I'm ready to craft!" Oliver said proudly.

He followed his routine step by step. Clean desk - check! Choose paper - he picked sunny yellow today. Get scissors - they were right in their drawer! Pick glue - the medium-sized jar was perfect.

Oliver made a beautiful paper flower that looked like sunshine. As he worked, he felt calm and happy. Everything was in its right place, and he knew exactly what to do next.

"Following my routine makes everything easier!" Oliver realized. "When I do things in the same order every day, I feel brave and ready for anything!"

From that day on, Oliver used his routine every morning. His office stayed neat and tidy, and he created the most wonderful crafts. Whenever something felt difficult, Oliver remembered that having a routine helped him feel brave and confident.

And every time he looked at his colorful routine chart, Oliver smiled, knowing that good habits make every day brighter.

The End.
