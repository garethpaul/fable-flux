# Ruby Rabbit and the Slippery Sink

Ruby Rabbit was small, soft, and very determined. Her ears stood tall like two pink sails. One sunny morning, she hopped into the bright, tile-shiny bathroom. The floor felt cool under her paws, and the air smelled like minty soap.

Ruby had a job to do. “Today, I will brush my teeth all by myself,” she said. She wiggled her nose and looked at the big sink. The sink was tall. The toothbrush sat in a cup far, far up.

Ruby stood on her tiptoes. She stretched. She reached. “Almost…” she whispered. But the toothbrush was still too high.

Ruby’s tummy felt twisty. “Hmm,” she said. “This is a problem. I can solve it.”

She looked around the bathroom with her bright bunny eyes. She listened to the dripping tap—drip, drop, drip. She smelled the lemony towel. She felt the smooth wall with her paw. Ruby took a deep breath. “Think, think, think,” she said softly. “I will use what I have.”

First, Ruby tried the bath mat. She folded it. She stepped on it. Squish! “Wobbly,” she said, and hopped off. “I need something steady.”

Then she spotted a little stool by the tub. It was short and sturdy with rubber feet. “Yes!” Ruby said. She pushed the stool to the sink. Squeak, squeak. She climbed up. Her paws gripped the edge. She was just tall enough!

Ruby reached for her toothbrush. As she grabbed it, a soft knock came at the door. It was her friend, Tiny Turtle. “Ruby? Can I come in?” Tiny asked in a quiet voice.

Ruby remembered a big rule Mama Rabbit had taught her. “In the bathroom, we keep our bodies safe and private,” Mama had said. “Always ask. Always listen.”

Ruby looked at the door. “I am in here, Tiny,” she called. “Please wait. I need privacy.”

“Okay, I will wait,” Tiny said. He hummed a little song in the hallway.

Ruby felt proud. She used her voice and kept her personal space safe. She turned back to the sink. She opened the toothpaste cap. Plip! A tiny dot landed on her paw. “Oops,” she giggled. She washed it off and put a pea-sized dot on her brush. Squish! The minty smell tickled her nose.

“Brush, brush, brush,” she sang. “Little circles, front and back.” Bubbles foamed like tiny clouds. She spat into the sink. Swish, swish went the water.

Then she saw it. On the counter, a puddle of water spread toward the edge. Ruby’s heart thumped. “Oh no,” she said. “I made a mess.”

She wanted to hide it. She wanted to run and say nothing. But Ruby remembered another rule: Be honest.

She took a deep breath. “I will fix what I can, and I will tell the truth,” she said.

Ruby looked for a towel. The big towel was for drying bodies. The hand towel was small, just right for spills. She reached for the hand towel and patted the puddle. Dab, dab, dab. The towel drank the water. The counter felt dry and clean.

Ruby hopped down from the stool and opened the bathroom door a little. Tiny Turtle peeked in with curious eyes. “Is it safe to come in?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Ruby. “But may I tell you something first?”

Tiny nodded. “I will listen.”

“I made a water puddle,” Ruby said. “I cleaned it with the hand towel. Next time I will turn off the tap faster.”

“Thank you for telling me the truth,” Tiny said. He smiled. “That was brave.”

Just then, Mama Rabbit’s voice floated down the hall. “Ruby, how is it going?”

Ruby opened the door wide now. “Mama, I brushed my teeth by myself. I used the stool to reach. I kept my privacy. I cleaned a spill. And I told the truth.”

Mama Rabbit knelt and hugged Ruby. Ruby felt warm and safe. The bathroom smelled minty and lemony, and the little stool sat proudly by the sink.

“I’m so proud of your resourcefulness,” Mama said. “You used what you had. You kept your boundaries. You were honest. And you listened.”

Ruby’s ears perked up. “Can I tell you the story of how I did it?” she asked.

“Yes, please,” said Mama. Tiny Turtle clapped his little flippers. “Storytime!”

Ruby stood tall on the mat. She told her story like a brave storyteller. “First, I saw a big sink. I felt small. I needed a plan. I looked and listened. I found a stool. I reached my brush. I kept my space. I cleaned my mess. I told the truth.”

Mama and Tiny cheered softly in the shiny bathroom. Ruby’s whiskers twitch-twitch-twitched with joy.

Ruby smiled. “I learned that I can solve problems by thinking, using safe tools, and asking for help if I need it. I learned to keep my body private and to be honest.”

Mama kissed her forehead. “That is a very good lesson, little rabbit.”

Ruby laughed, light as bubbles. “Tomorrow, I will put the stool back when I’m done,” she added. “That is part of my plan.”

And she did.

The End.
