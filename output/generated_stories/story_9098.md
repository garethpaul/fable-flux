# Benny Beaver's Musical Day

Benny Beaver loved to build things with his strong teeth and busy paws. Every day, he would chop wood and stack logs by the river. But today was different – today Benny had his first music lesson!

"I'm so excited!" Benny said, bouncing on his flat tail as he walked into the bright musical practice room. The room smelled like fresh wood polish and had colorful instruments everywhere. There was a shiny piano, drums that looked like tree stumps, and guitars hanging on the wall like golden leaves.

Mrs. Melody, the music teacher, smiled warmly. "Welcome, Benny! Are you ready to learn the violin?"

Benny nodded eagerly, but when Mrs. Melody handed him the violin, his tummy started to rumble loudly. "GRUMBLE, GRUMBLE, GROWL!"

"Oh my!" laughed Mrs. Melody. "It sounds like someone forgot to eat breakfast!"

Benny's cheeks turned pink. He had been so excited about music class that he rushed out of his dam without eating. "I was too busy to eat," he said quietly. "I wanted to get here early to practice."

"That's wonderful that you're eager to learn," said Mrs. Melody gently, "but our bodies need fuel just like instruments need to be tuned. When we don't eat, we can't think clearly or hold our instruments steady."

Benny tried to hold the violin, but his paws were shaky. When he drew the bow across the strings, it made a scratchy "SCREEEECH!" sound instead of beautiful music.

"I can't do it!" Benny cried, feeling frustrated. His empty tummy rumbled again, even louder this time.

Mrs. Melody sat down beside him. "Benny, being brave doesn't mean doing everything perfectly right away. Sometimes being brave means taking care of ourselves first. Would you like to share my healthy snack?"

She pulled out a basket filled with crunchy apple slices, cheese cubes, and whole grain crackers. "These foods give our brains and bodies energy to learn new things."

Benny's eyes lit up. "Really? Food helps us learn music?"

"Absolutely!" Mrs. Melody said. "When we eat healthy foods, our minds become clear and our hands become steady. It's like giving our body the right tools to build beautiful music, just like you use the right tools to build your dams!"

Benny carefully ate the apple slices – they were sweet and made satisfying "CRUNCH, CRUNCH" sounds. The cheese was creamy and the crackers were nutty. As he ate, he felt his energy returning.

"Now," said Mrs. Melody, "let's try again. But first, let's learn some musical words! In music, we say 'forte' when we want to play loudly, and 'piano' when we want to play softly."

"For-tay! Pee-ah-no!" Benny repeated, loving how the new words rolled off his tongue.

With his tummy full and happy, Benny picked up the violin again. This time, his paws were steady and strong. When he drew the bow across the strings, it made a sweet, gentle sound like wind through the trees.

"Beautiful!" cheered Mrs. Melody. "You see? When we take care of our bodies with good food, we can do amazing things!"

Benny beamed with pride. "From now on, I'll always eat breakfast before doing important things. Taking care of myself IS being brave!"

"That's right, Benny. Healthy habits help us be our best selves every day."

The End.
