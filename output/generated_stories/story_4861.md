# Rocco’s Guest Room Band

Rocco the musical raccoon loved sounds. He loved the soft tap of raindrops, the warm hum of a fan, and the plinky-plonk of his tiny toy piano. One sunny morning, Rocco was staying in the guest room at Aunt Lina’s house. The guest room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. The window was open, and a breeze tickled the curtains. Rocco’s whiskers twitched with a happy wiggle.

“I will play a welcome song!” Rocco said. He set out his drum, his shaker, and his tiny piano on the soft rug. Plink, plonk, tap-tap, shake-shake!

Knock, knock! Friends peeked in. Mei the mouse rolled a bell. Samir the sparrow fluffed his feathers with a cheerful chirp. Amina the kitten padded in, her paws quiet as clouds. “Can we play too?” they asked.

“Yes!” said Rocco. “We can be a band. We will play together and make everyone feel at home.”

They all smiled. The guest room was a special room for visitors from near and far. Aunt Lina said the guest room was for sharing. “In our house,” she always said, “we welcome the world.”

Rocco passed out instruments. “We will make a song about welcome,” he said. “We will use kind voices and kind paws. We will take turns.”

They began. Tap-tap went Rocco’s drum. Ting-ting went Mei’s bell. Chirp-chirp sang Samir. Prrr-purr hummed Amina. The sounds filled the room like warm cocoa.

But soon, the song got wobbly. Mei rang her bell very fast. Samir chirped very loud. Amina batted the shaker right under the bed. Rocco banged the drum as hard as he could. The music turned into a jumble. The curtains shook. The lamp wobbled. Rocco’s ears drooped.

“Oh no,” said Rocco. “This does not sound like welcome. It sounds like a storm.”

Aunt Lina opened the door. She smiled gently. “Sounds are special,” she said. “But we must share the room. The guest room is for everyone. Can you try again with teamwork?”

Rocco took a deep breath. He felt the cool breeze on his nose. “Friends,” he said, “we have a problem. We are all playing at once. It is not fair. We cannot hear each other. How can we fix it?”

Mei held her bell close. “We can take turns,” she squeaked.

Samir tilted his head. “We can listen first, then sing,” he chirped.

Amina’s whiskers twitched. “We can keep the shaker safe,” she purred, pulling it out from under the bed.

Rocco nodded. “Let us make a plan. We will use negotiation. We will talk and choose together.”

They sat in a little circle on the rug. The rug felt soft and warm under their toes and paws. Rocco drew a quick plan on a notepad. “First, a quiet start,” he said. “Mei, you tinkle the bell. Then, I tap the drum softly. Next, Samir sings. Last, Amina shakes, gentle like rain. After that, we all play together, but we keep it kind and fair.”

“Fair means we all get a turn,” said Mei.

“And responsibility means we care for the room and the things in it,” added Amina, placing the shaker on a folded towel so it would be soft and safe.

“And global-citizenship means we welcome everyone,” said Samir. “We make a song that says hello to friends from everywhere.”

Rocco beamed. “Yes! We will make a welcome song for the world.”

They tried again. Mei went ting… ting… soft and sweet, like tiny stars. Rocco followed with a gentle tap-tap, like a heart. Samir sang a bright chirp-chirp that fluttered like sunshine. Amina shook the shaker—shh shh—like a little rain on leaves.

Then they all played together. The sounds blended and smiled. The lamp stood still. The curtains waved like they were dancing. Rocco felt warm and proud.

Knock, knock! New visitors peeked in: a turtle with a suitcase sticker from faraway seas, and a bunny with a scarf from snowy hills. They listened with wide eyes.

“Welcome,” Rocco said softly. “This is our guest room band. We play with teamwork.”

The turtle nodded slowly. “It sounds fair,” he said. “Everyone has a turn.”

The bunny’s nose wiggled. “It sounds kind,” she said. “I feel at home.”

When the song ended, the room was quiet and happy. Rocco looked at his friends. “We did it,” he said. “We solved our problem by talking, listening, and sharing. We used negotiation, fairness, and responsibility.”

Aunt Lina clapped from the door. “That is teamwork,” she said. “You worked like good neighbors to the whole wide world.”

Rocco put the instruments away neatly. Mei folded the towel. Samir closed the window just a little, so the breeze was soft. Amina brushed the rug smooth with her paw. The guest room smelled like lemon and music.

Rocco smiled at his friends and the new guests. “When we listen and share, our song says welcome,” he said. “Teamwork makes room for everyone.”

They all nodded. The turtle and bunny joined the circle. Together, they hummed a gentle goodbye tune for the day, sweet and calm.

The End.
