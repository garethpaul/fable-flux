# Rocky Raccoon’s Calm Cleanup Song

Rocky was a musical raccoon. He wore tiny headphones and a bright, blue scarf. He loved to make songs with pots, pans, and paintbrushes. Today, Rocky padded into the creative art room. The room smelled like lemon soap and crayons. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor.

Rocky wiggled his striped tail. “Time to make art!” he hummed. He tapped a paint jar. Tink! He shook a shaker. Sha-sha-shake! He beat a soft drum. Boom-boom-bum!

Soon, his friends came in. Mia Mouse carried glitter. Pablo Pig rolled a basket of crayons. Lina Llama brought paper that felt smooth and cool. Everyone giggled and sang. “Paint, draw, glue, and play! Art and music, hip-hooray!”

They made a big, bright mural. Swish went the brushes. Squish went the glue. Glitter twinkled like tiny stars. Crayons slid across the paper—scritch, scratch. The room was full of sounds and color and laughter.

When the mural was done, they all cheered. “We did it!” said Mia.

But then, they looked around. Oh no.

Paint lids were open. Brushes were sticky. Paper scraps were on the floor like little clouds. Crayons rolled under tables. A cup tipped over. Drip, drip, drip. The room looked messy and tired.

Pablo frowned. “This is too big,” he said. “I don’t know where to start.”

Lina’s ears drooped. “I feel worried,” she whispered.

Mia wrung her tiny paws. “I want to run away.”

Rocky took a deep, slow breath in. He let it out slowly. “We can be calm,” he said. “We can make a plan. We can use a strategy.”

“What is a strategy?” asked Mia.

“It’s a smart plan,” Rocky said. “A step-by-step way to solve a problem. We do one small step, then another. We stay calm.”

Rocky tapped his drum, softly. Bum… bum… bum. “First, let’s stop and breathe,” he said. “In… and out.” They breathed together. Their bodies felt softer and steady.

“Next,” said Rocky, “we sing a cleanup song. The song will be our plan.”

He sang in a gentle voice:
“Sort, stack, scoop, and sweep,
Do a little, nice and neat.
Pick a job and do your part,
Clean with kindness, calm and smart.”

Mia’s whiskers perked up. “I can sort!” she squeaked.

“Great,” said Rocky. “Mia, sort crayons by color: reds with reds, blues with blues.”

Mia nodded. “Red, blue, green, yellow,” she sang. Click, click went the crayon boxes.

“Pablo,” said Rocky, “you can stack. Put papers in a neat pile. Lids on paint. Stack brushes in a cup. Stack, stack, stack.”

Pablo smiled. “I can do that.” The brushes made a gentle clink as he stood them up. The paint lids went twist, click.

“Lina,” said Rocky, “you can scoop. Scoop glitter with the spoon. Scoop scraps into the bin.”

Lina hummed as she scooped. Scoop, plop. Scoop, plop. The floor began to show again, smooth and shiny.

“What will you do?” asked Mia.

“I will sweep,” said Rocky. He picked up a small broom. Swish, swish, swish. He moved slowly and calmly, like a soft song.

They worked to the beat of Rocky’s drum. Bum… bum… bum. They sang the plan:
“Sort, stack, scoop, and sweep,
Do a little, nice and neat.”

Sometimes they bumped paws. “Oops, sorry,” said Pablo.

“It’s okay,” said Lina. “We are a team.”

Sometimes they felt stuck. A glitter jar did not want to close. Twist, twist. “It’s too tight,” said Mia.

Rocky paused. “Let’s try a new strategy,” he said. “When something is hard, ask for help.”

Pablo held the jar. Mia turned the lid. Together they went twist… click! “We did it!” they cheered.

Bit by bit, the room changed. The tables felt smooth, not sticky. The air smelled like clean soap again. The crayons clicked into tidy boxes. The floor was clear and bright in the sun.

Rocky looked around. “We solved the problem,” he said. “We used calm. We used a strategy. We did chores together.”

Lina smiled. “I feel proud.”

Pablo nodded. “I feel strong.”

Mia twirled. “I feel happy!”

Rocky tapped a joyful beat. Tap-tap-TAH! “Now,” he said, “let’s make a new song. A strategy song for any big mess.”

They clapped and sang:
“When a mess is big and wide,
Take a breath, don’t run and hide.
Make a plan, one step, two,
Work as friends—me and you.”

They put their hands in a circle—paw, hoof, paw, paw. “Team!” they shouted.

Before they left, Rocky stuck a bright chart on the wall. It had four pictures:
1. Breathe
2. Make a plan
3. Do one small job
4. Ask for help

He drew a tiny broom and a happy face. “This is our strategy chart,” said Rocky. “We can use it every day.”

They turned off the lights. The room was quiet and calm. The mural smiled from the wall, full of colors. Rocky hummed as they walked out, tails and ears and feet in a gentle beat.

“We did chores with music,” Mia said.

“We solved conflict with words,” Lina added.

“We used strategy,” Pablo said, tapping the chart.

Rocky grinned. “And we stayed calm.”

The End.
