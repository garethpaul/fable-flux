# Ellie's Calm Kitchen Colors

In a warm kitchen, sunlight hugged the walls like a cozy blanket. The air smelled like sweet bananas and cinnamon toast. Ellie, a gentle elephant calf, padded in on soft feet. Her big ears fluttered. Her trunk wiggled with a happy sniff.

“Good morning, kitchen,” Ellie whispered. “You smell yummy.”

On the table sat a bowl of bright fruit—red strawberries, yellow bananas, green kiwi, and little blue blueberries. Beside it was a stack of soft, round rice cakes. Next to the fruit lay finger paints, thick paper, and a cup of water with a tiny brush.

Ellie’s eyes sparkled. “Today, I will make a snack and a picture,” she said. “I can start by being calm and kind.”

Mama’s voice drifted from the hallway. “I’ll be back in a minute, Ellie. Use your calm breathing if you get excited.”

Ellie nodded. “I can do it.”

She put her front feet on the stool and climbed up, slow and steady. “Step… pause… breathe,” she said softly. She took a deep, gentle breath through her trunk. In… and out. It felt like warm tea in her tummy.

Ellie picked up a banana. It felt smooth and cool. Peel, peel—plop! The peel slid off like a little yellow coat. She giggled. “Hello, banana.” She sliced it with a safe, dull butter knife, slow and careful. “One… two… three.” Each slice made a soft, squishy sound.

Her tummy gave a little rumble. “I am hungry,” Ellie said. “But I will be mindful.” She pressed her feet into the floor. She felt the firm tiles. She listened to the tiny hum of the fridge. She smelled the fruity air. “I am here. I am calm.”

She washed her hands. Swish, swish. The water was warm like a hug. “Clean hands help my body,” she said.

Ellie made a rainbow snack. She laid the fruit on a rice cake: red, yellow, green, blue. “I’m making fruit art!” she cheered. She tried to go fast, but a strawberry slipped. Sploot! It landed on the paper.

Ellie’s ears drooped. “Oh no.”

She stopped. She placed her trunk on her tummy. “It’s okay,” she whispered. “I can take a breath.” In… and out. She felt her heart slow. She looked at the red mark on the paper. It looked like a tiny heart.

Her eyes lit up. “I have an idea!” She took initiative, all by herself. She dipped her brush in a little water and touched the berry spot. It made a soft pink swish. She added a blueberry dot. Plip! A purple circle bloomed. The paper smelled fruity and fresh.

Ellie smiled. “I can make kitchen art.”

She painted with calm, slow strokes. Swish, swish. She made green kiwi leaves with little lines. She pressed a banana slice on the page and lifted it to see a pale yellow moon shape. “Moon banana,” she giggled.

She paused between each move. “Stop. Breathe. Look.” She noticed the colors shining in the sun. She heard a tiny bird outside, peep-peep. She felt happy bubbles in her chest. “Mindful Ellie,” she said. “I am noticing everything.”

Ellie looked at her snack again. “My body needs good food,” she said. “Fruit gives me energy to create.” She picked up her rainbow rice cake. She smelled the sweet strawberry. She took a small bite. Crunch, squish, yum. She chewed slow. “I taste red. I taste yellow. I taste green. I taste blue!” She laughed. “It tastes like a sunny rainbow.”

Mama walked in and smiled wide. “Oh, Ellie! What a calm kitchen. What a beautiful picture. And what a smart snack!”

Ellie’s ears flapped with pride. “I made fruit art and a fruit snack. I used my calm breathing when the strawberry fell. I took my time.”

Mama knelt and looked at the painting. “Tell me about your art.”

Ellie pointed. “This is a berry heart. This is a moon banana. These are kiwi leaves. And this purple dot is a blueberry planet.”

Mama clapped softly. “You used creativity, and you were mindful. How does your body feel?”

Ellie closed her eyes. She listened to her breath. In… and out. She felt her feet on the floor. She felt her tummy feeling full and happy. “I feel calm. I feel strong. I feel proud.”

Mama nodded. “When we slow down and notice, we can do hard things with care.”

Ellie added one last blueberry dot. “Plip.” She giggled. “My picture is done.”

Together they cleaned up, one piece at a time. Wipe, wipe. Rinse, rinse. Ellie hummed a soft tune. The kitchen smelled like soap and fruit. The sun made a warm square on the floor. Ellie stepped into the sunny square and took one more deep breath.

“I can be calm, I can start things on my own, and I can make art that helps my heart,” she said.

Mama hugged her. “That is a wonderful lesson.”

Ellie grinned. “Today, I learned to stop, breathe, and notice. It helps my hands, my tummy, and my art.”

They set the painting to dry and shared another rainbow snack, bite by slow bite, smiling in their warm kitchen.

The End.
