# Joey's Big Workshop Dream

Joey the kangaroo joey loved visiting Grandpa's garage workshop. The smell of wood and paint filled the air. Tools hung neatly on the walls, and sunlight streamed through the dusty windows.

Joey had a big dream. He wanted to build a special toy car for his best friend Benny Bear's birthday.

"I can do this!" Joey said, bouncing excitedly around the workshop. His fluffy tail wiggled with joy.

Joey grabbed a hammer. BANG! BANG! BANG! He hit the wood very hard and very fast.

"Ouch!" Joey cried as the hammer slipped. The wood split in two pieces.

Joey felt frustrated. His ears drooped down. He tried again, this time even faster.

CRASH! The wood pieces flew everywhere. One piece knocked over Grandpa's paint can. Blue paint splashed across the floor.

"Oh no!" Joey's eyes filled with tears. "I made a big mess!"

Grandpa Roo hopped into the workshop. He saw the spilled paint and broken wood. But he didn't look angry.

"Joey, what happened here?" Grandpa asked gently.

"I wanted to make Benny a toy car," Joey sniffled. "But I broke everything! I'm sorry, Grandpa."

Grandpa's eyes twinkled. "Making things takes time, little joey. Come sit with me."

They sat on Grandpa's old wooden stool. Grandpa's pouch felt warm and safe.

"When I build things," Grandpa said, "I take three deep breaths first. Like this." He breathed in slowly through his nose. "In... and out."

Joey tried it too. "In... and out." His heart felt calmer already.

"Good!" said Grandpa. "Now, let's think step by step. What do we need for a toy car?"

Joey closed his eyes and imagined. "Four wheels, a body, and maybe a little seat!"

"Perfect! Let's make a plan," Grandpa smiled. "First, we'll clean up this paint. Then we'll choose the right wood. Finally, we'll work slowly and carefully."

Together, they cleaned the blue paint with soft rags. The garage smelled fresh again.

Grandpa showed Joey how to hold the hammer gently. "Like holding a baby bird," he said.

Joey took another deep breath. This time, he tapped the nail softly. Tap, tap, tap. The wood stayed together!

"I did it!" Joey whispered, trying to stay calm.

Step by step, they built the little car. Joey sanded the wood until it felt smooth as silk. He painted it bright red, Benny's favorite color. Grandpa helped him attach four round wheels.

"Look, Grandpa! It's perfect!" Joey said proudly.

The toy car was small but beautiful. It had a tiny steering wheel and a soft cushion seat.

"You did wonderful work, Joey," Grandpa said. "What helped you succeed?"

Joey thought carefully. "Taking deep breaths made me feel calm. Making a plan helped me know what to do. And going slowly made everything work better!"

The next day, Joey gave Benny the special toy car. Benny's eyes grew wide with happiness.

"You made this for me?" Benny asked.

"Yes! And I learned something important," Joey said. "When things feel hard, I can take deep breaths and stay calm. Then I can do anything!"

Benny hugged Joey tight. They spent the whole afternoon playing with the little red car in the sunshine.

Joey learned that the best way to reach his goals was to stay calm, make a plan, and take his time. And saying sorry when he made mistakes helped too.

The End.
