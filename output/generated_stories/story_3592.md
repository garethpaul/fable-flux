# Shelly's Big Heart

Shelly the turtle lived in a busy basement. The basement was full of sounds - *drip, drop* went the water pipes, *whirr, buzz* went the washing machine, and *thump, thump* went footsteps upstairs.

Shelly was very shy. She kept her head tucked inside her green shell most of the time. The basement felt safe and cozy to her.

One morning, Shelly heard a tiny *squeak, squeak* sound. She peeked her head out just a little. Near the corner, by the old cardboard boxes, sat a small gray mouse.

The mouse was crying. "I'm lost!" he squeaked. "I can't find my family!"

Shelly wanted to help, but she felt scared. What if the mouse didn't like her? What if she said something wrong?

She pulled her head back into her shell. *Maybe someone else will help him,* she thought.

But the mouse kept crying. *Squeak, squeak, sniffle.*

Shelly's heart felt heavy. She knew how it felt to be alone and scared. Slowly, very slowly, she stretched her neck out of her shell.

"Um... hello," Shelly whispered in her softest voice.

The mouse looked up with big, wet eyes. "Oh! A turtle! I didn't see you there."

"I... I heard you crying," Shelly said. "Are you okay?"

The mouse wiped his tiny nose. "I'm lost. I came down here looking for crumbs, but now I can't find the way back to my family's nest."

Shelly felt her heart grow warm. She understood being scared and lost. "I live here," she said, feeling a little braver. "Maybe I can help you look?"

The mouse's eyes lit up. "Really? You would do that for me?"

Shelly nodded. She knew every corner of the basement. "Tell me about your home. What does it look like?"

"It's warm and soft," the mouse said. "Made of cotton and paper bits. It's near something that smells like soap."

Shelly thought hard. *Soap smell... that must be near the laundry area!*

"Follow me," Shelly said. She moved slowly on her short legs, but she was determined.

They passed the rumbling furnace and the stack of paint cans. The mouse stayed close behind Shelly's shell.

"There!" Shelly pointed with her nose. Behind the detergent bottles was a cozy nest made of dryer lint and torn newspaper.

"My home!" squeaked the mouse happily.

Just then, three other mice poked their heads out. "Charlie! We were so worried!" they called.

Charlie the mouse turned to Shelly. "Thank you so much! You saved me!"

Shelly felt proud and happy. Her shell felt less heavy now.

"Would you like to be our friend?" asked Charlie. "We could share our crumbs with you!"

Shelly smiled - a real, big smile. "I would like that very much."

From that day on, Shelly wasn't so shy anymore. She learned that when you care about others and try to help them, you make wonderful friends. The busy basement became filled with laughter and friendship.

Every evening, Shelly and the mouse family would gather to share stories and snacks. Shelly discovered that being kind and caring about others' feelings made her heart feel bigger and brighter than ever before.

The End.
