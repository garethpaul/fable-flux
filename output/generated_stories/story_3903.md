# Sunny's Big Helper Day

Sunny was a small, fluffy squirrel with a bright, busy tail. Sunny lived in a cozy home office with soft carpet, a whirring computer, and a big window that smelled like clean morning air. The desk was covered in colorful sticky notes, a cup of pencils, and acorns Sunny liked to count. Click-click went the keyboard. Hum-hum went the printer. Sunny loved the sounds.

One day, papers were scattered like white leaves. Crayons rolled under the chair. There was a tiny spill of apple juice on the floor, sticky and sweet. Sunny wiggled his whiskers. “Oh my,” he said. “These chores look so big!”

Sunny looked at the list on a bright pink sticky note:
- Tidy papers
- Wipe spill
- Feed plant
- Sort crayons

Sunny’s heart thumped. “I am just a little squirrel,” he whispered. “What if I can’t do it?” His tummy felt fluttery, like leaves in a breeze.

Then Sunny took a brave, deep breath. In went the air—cool and fresh. Out went the worry—soft and slow. “I can try,” Sunny said. “Trying is brave.”

First, he tackled the papers. Rustle, rustle, the papers sounded as Sunny gathered them. He stacked them into neat piles: “Done,” “To read,” and “To draw.” “I can be a helper,” Sunny chirped. “Helpers make small jobs into big smiles.” The desk looked smoother already.

Next came the sticky spill. Sunny got a soft cloth. “Hello, cloth,” he giggled. He dipped it in warm, soapy water. The soap smelled like tiny lemons. “Swish, swish,” sang Sunny, wiping in gentle circles. “Round and round.” The floor changed from sticky to squeaky clean. Sunny touched it with a paw. “Not sticky!” he said with a grin.

Then Sunny saw the thirsty plant on the windowsill. Its leaves drooped like sleepy ears. Sunny poured a little water. “Sip, sip,” he told the plant. “You are part of the office too.” Sunlight sprinkled the leaves, and the plant perked up. “Thank you,” the leaves seemed to whisper. Sunny’s chest felt warm and proud.

But under the chair, the crayons waited in a colorful jumble. Red, blue, yellow, green, and a tiny shy purple. Sunny scooted closer. The chair looked big and a little wobbly. “What if it rolls?” Sunny asked. His tail puffed.

“Be brave and be smart,” Sunny told himself. “I can ask for help. That is brave too.”

Just then, Clickety the keyboard clacked. “You’ve got this, Sunny,” it seemed to say.

Sunny nodded. He put a small book behind the chair wheels so it would not roll. “Safe and steady,” he said. He used a ruler to reach the crayons. Tap, tap—out they slid, one by one. “Hello, little purple,” Sunny whispered kindly. “You are not lost anymore.”

Sunny sorted the crayons into a cup. “Red with red, blue with blue.” He found an open mind made sorting fun. “Maybe,” he said, “I can sort by size too!” He tried a new idea. Tall crayons in one cup, short crayons in another. Sunny smiled. “I like trying new ways.”

All at once, there was a tiny sound. Skitter-skatter! A paperclip spilled over the edge of the desk and jingled onto the floor. Sunny blinked. The paperclips were many and small, like shiny fish. “This is a lot,” Sunny said softly. “I feel a little worried again.”

He paused. He listened. Hum-hum went the printer. Chirp-chirp sang a bird outside. Sunny put a paw on his chest. “Brave heart, open mind,” he said. “I can make a plan.”

Sunny made a plan:
- Use a magnet to pick up clips
- Put clips in a tin
- Take a stretch break

He found a tiny magnet on the fridge. Zzzzip! The paperclips jumped to it, clinging like friends. “Whee!” Sunny laughed. He dropped them into a tin that went ping! when they landed. Clip by clip, plan by plan, the floor got clear.

After the chores, the home office felt different. The air smelled like lemons and sunshine. The desk looked calm. The plant stood tall. The crayons and clips rested in their places. Sunny stood in the middle with his paws on his hips and his tail waving like a flag.

“I did it!” Sunny cheered. “I was brave. I tried new ideas. I did my chores!”

The computer gave a happy beep. The chair sat still and safe. The window let in a warm breeze that tickled Sunny’s ears.

Sunny made one more list, this time on a yellow sticky note:
- Take a stretch
- Drink water
- Say thank you

He stretched tall. He sipped cool water. “Thank you, tools,” he said to the cloth, the ruler, and the magnet. “Thank you, Sunny,” he added with a giggle. “You helped.”

Sunny curled up by the sunny window. His whiskers twitched with joy. He felt strong and calm. He knew something important now.

“When we do our chores,” Sunny whispered, “we care for our place and for ourselves. Being brave means trying, asking for help, and staying safe. Being open-minded means using new ideas. Little steps make big changes.”

Outside, the bird sang a sweet goodbye song. Inside, the tidy home office hummed a happy tune.

Sunny closed his eyes and smiled. “Tomorrow, I will help again,” he said.

The End.
