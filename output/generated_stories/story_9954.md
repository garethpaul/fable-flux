# Melody's Turn to Share

In a bright musical practice room filled with shiny instruments, lived a little yellow bird named Melody. She had the most beautiful voice in the whole music school, and she loved to sing more than anything else in the world.

Every morning, Melody would fly to her favorite spot on top of the grand piano. "La-la-la!" she would sing, her sweet voice echoing through the room. The sound made her feel happy and warm inside.

But today was different. When Melody arrived at the practice room, she found three other young birds already there. There was Ruby the robin with her red chest, Blue the jay with his bright feathers, and Pip the sparrow with her tiny brown wings.

"Good morning!" chirped Ruby. "We're here for singing practice too!"

Melody's heart sank a little. She had always practiced alone. "But... but this is MY practice room," she said, her voice getting a bit shaky. "I always sing here by myself."

Blue fluffed his feathers. "The music teacher said we could all use this room today. We want to practice for the spring concert too!"

Melody felt her tummy get tight and twisty. She didn't want to share her special singing space. "I need to practice my solo song," she said, flying to the piano. "I'll sing first, and you can wait."

She began to sing her beautiful song, but something felt wrong. The other birds sat quietly in the corner, looking sad. Pip's little head was down, and Ruby wasn't chirping her usual happy sounds.

Melody stopped singing. She looked at her new friends and noticed how their shoulders drooped. "How are you feeling?" she asked softly.

"We're sad," said Pip in a tiny voice. "We want to practice too, but there's no room for us."

Melody tilted her head and thought carefully. She remembered how happy singing made her feel. Maybe... maybe the other birds felt the same way about music?

"I have an idea!" Melody said, her eyes brightening. "What if we take turns? I could sing for a little while, then Ruby could practice, then Blue, then Pip. We could each have our own special time!"

Ruby's red chest puffed up with excitement. "That sounds wonderful! How long should each turn be?"

Melody thought hard. "How about we each sing one song? That way everyone gets the same amount of time."

"Yes!" chirped Blue. "And we could listen to each other and maybe learn new songs!"

So Melody sang her solo first, her voice filling the room with golden notes. When she finished, she flew to a branch and said, "Ruby, it's your turn!"

Ruby sang a cheerful morning song that made everyone want to dance. Then Blue shared a jazzy tune that had everyone tapping their feet. Finally, little Pip sang the sweetest lullaby that made the room feel cozy and warm.

"This is so much better than singing alone!" Melody realized. "I learned three new songs, and I made three new friends!"

The four birds decided to practice together every day, always taking turns and sharing the beautiful music room. Melody discovered that sharing made her feel even happier than keeping everything to herself.

From that day on, whenever Melody felt like she didn't want to share, she remembered how good it felt to take turns and make others happy too.

The End.
