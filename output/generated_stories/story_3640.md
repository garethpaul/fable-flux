# Ellie’s Elbow Room

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a swishy little tail. One sunny afternoon, Ellie and her friends gathered in the big dining room for snack time. The room smelled like warm banana bread and sweet oranges. The long table was shiny, and colorful napkins waited at each chair.

“Yum!” said Ellie, her trunk wiggling with happy sniffs.

Mr. Otter, the helper, clapped his paws. “Friends, please sit. We will share snacks and be fair. Everyone gets a turn.”

Ellie sat between Tavi the turtle and Mina the monkey. The chairs made tiny squeaks on the floor. Dishes clinked. Ellie’s tummy gave a friendly rumble.

Mr. Otter brought a big bowl of banana slices and a plate of crunchy carrot sticks. “We pass to the left,” he said, smiling. “Remember gentle hands. Remember elbow room.”

Ellie did not know what “elbow room” meant. She scooted closer to the bowl. Her round shoulder brushed Tavi.

“Oof,” said Tavi softly. “Ellie, you’re squishing my shell.”

“Oh!” Ellie pulled back. “Sorry, Tavi.”

The bowl came to Ellie. It smelled so good. She reached with her trunk and scooped a big bunch. Plop, plop, plop! The bananas landed on her plate.

Mina’s eyes grew wide. “Ellie,” she said, “I want bananas too!”

Ellie blinked. “I like bananas. They are my favorite.” She pushed the bowl to Mina, but the bowl bumped Mina’s elbow. “Oops!” Ellie said. A banana slice slid onto Mina’s lap. Mina squeaked.

Mr. Otter walked over. “Let’s take a breath,” he said kindly. “In…and out.” Everyone breathed slowly. The dining room felt calm again.

“Ellie,” said Mr. Otter, “do you know what personal space is?”

Ellie tilted her head. “Is it like…a little circle around me?”

“Yes,” said Mr. Otter. “Your body has a safe circle. Others have a safe circle too. We give each other space. We ask before we touch. That is called a boundary.”

Ellie touched her chest. “My circle. Tavi’s circle. Mina’s circle.”

“Right,” said Mr. Otter. “And when we share snacks, we use fairness. Fair means everyone gets some, not too little, not too much.”

Ellie looked at her plate. She had lots of bananas. Mina had only two. Tavi had one, and it was very small. Ellie felt a warm blush in her cheeks.

“I took too many,” Ellie whispered.

“That’s okay,” said Mr. Otter. “You can fix it.”

Ellie nodded. “I can help.”

Ellie carefully picked up a few slices and put them back in the bowl. She looked at Mina. “May I pass them to you?” she asked.

“Yes, please,” said Mina, smiling.

Ellie passed the bowl gently, keeping the bowl in the middle. Mina took some. Then Mina asked Tavi, “Would you like more?”

“Yes, please,” Tavi said. “Thank you for asking.”

The plate of carrots came next. Ellie waited. She kept her elbows tucked in. She asked, “Is it my turn?” Mr. Otter smiled and nodded.

Ellie took two carrots. Crunch! Crunch! They tasted fresh and sweet. She slid the plate to the center. “Mina, Tavi, your turn.”

“Thank you for the space,” said Tavi. He sat taller, not squished.

Ellie noticed the table was a little messy. A few banana slices were on the table. Some napkins had fallen on the floor. She wanted to help.

“Mr. Otter,” Ellie said, “may I clean up? I want to help our dining room.”

“That is kind,” said Mr. Otter. “Helping the room helps everyone.”

Ellie asked, “Mina, may I pick up the napkin near your tail?”

“Yes,” said Mina. “Thank you for asking first.”

Ellie picked up the napkin. She wiped a sticky spot. Swish, wipe, swish! The table shone again. Tavi held the trash bin steady with his strong shell. “Teamwork,” he said.

Mr. Otter brought out a small cup of yogurt for each friend. “Before we start,” Ellie said, “let’s make a plan so it’s fair.” She pointed to the spoons. “Everyone gets one spoon. We scoop slow. We keep our circles.”

“Agreed!” said Mina.

“Agreed,” said Tavi.

They ate together. Scoop, taste, smile. The dining room hummed with happy munching. No bumps, no squishes, just kind voices and careful hands.

After snack, Mr. Otter asked, “How did it go?”

Ellie took a breath. “At first, I forgot about circles. I took too much. That was not fair. Then I asked, I shared, and I helped clean. That felt good.”

Mina nodded. “I liked when you asked before passing the bowl.”

Tavi added, “I liked when you gave me elbow room.”

Mr. Otter clapped softly. “You practiced personal boundaries. You cared about fairness. You did community service by cleaning the room. Well done!”

Ellie’s ears flapped with joy. “I learned a rule,” she said. “I can keep my circle. I can respect your circle. I can share so it’s fair. And I can help our space.”

The friends stacked their plates. Ellie asked, “Group hug?”

Mina giggled. “May we hug, Tavi?”

Tavi smiled. “Yes, we may. Gentle hug.”

They hugged gently, with plenty of elbow room, and lots of happy hearts.

The End.
