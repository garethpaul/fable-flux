# Panda Pao’s Calm and Cozy Night

Panda Pao lived in a cozy bedroom with soft blankets, a tiny paper lantern, and a bamboo pillow that smelled sweet and green. The lantern glowed warm and orange, like a little sunset in the room. Outside, the moon shone through the window, round and friendly.

Pao loved bedtime. He brushed his teeth with minty paste. “Minty fresh!” he giggled. He patted his round tummy. He liked to be patient, to move slow and gentle. Tonight, he tried a new bedtime song from Grandma, who lived far away. Grandma sent the song in a little note with a red string. The note said, “Breathe in. Breathe out. Think of bamboo and bright moonlight.”

Pao climbed into bed and pulled up his soft blanket. “I am ready,” he whispered. But as he closed his eyes, he heard a soft sound. Tap-tap. Tap-tap-tap.

Pao opened one eye. “Who’s there?” he asked.

“It’s me,” squeaked Miko the little mouse, peeking from the corner. “I can’t sleep. My tummy feels wiggly. My cheeks feel hot. I think I’m worried.”

Pao sat up and patted the bed. “Come sit,” he said in a calm voice. “Let’s name the feeling. You feel worried. That’s okay.”

Miko climbed up. His whiskers trembled. “I don’t like this feeling,” he said. “I want it to stop now.”

Pao nodded slowly. “Feelings come and go, like clouds in the sky,” he said. “We can listen to them. We can help them.”

Miko blinked. “How?”

Pao took a deep breath, in and out. “We can use Grandma’s song.” He showed Miko the note with the red string. “It is special from my culture. It helps me feel calm. Would you like to try?”

“Yes, please,” Miko whispered.

Pao tapped the lantern gently. It swayed and made a soft, warm glow. The room smelled like fresh cloth and clean sheets. The night crickets sang a quiet chirp. Pao began, “Breathe in through your nose. One… two. Breathe out through your mouth. One… two.”

Miko copied. In… two. Out… two. His whiskers stopped shaking so much.

Pao smiled. “Now, let’s put a hand on our tummies. Feel your tummy lift and fall.” They breathed together, feeling the rise and fall. Pao said, “What color is your worry?”

Miko thought. “It is prickly orange.”

Pao nodded. “Let’s paint it soft. Let’s breathe in blue, gentle and cool. Breathe out orange.” They imagined blue air coming in, and orange air going out like a tiny cloud. In… blue. Out… orange.

Miko sighed. “It’s smaller now.”

“Good noticing,” said Pao. “You are brave to look at your feeling.”

Tap-tap. Another visitor! Lili the ladybug crawled in through the window. “I heard your song,” she said. “I feel sad. My favorite red scarf is torn. I worked hard to make it. Now it has a hole.”

Pao patted the bed again. “Come sit,” he said kindly. “Let’s say the feeling: you feel sad.”

Lili nodded. “Yes. My wings feel heavy.”

Pao said, “Sad is okay. Sad tells us we care.” He reached into a small box under his pillow. Inside were tiny needles, a bit of thread, and a patch with a flower from Grandma’s village. “We can help. We can fix the scarf.”

Lili sniffled. “But I don’t know how.”

“We can learn together,” said Pao. “We have a growth mindset. We can try, step by step.” He threaded the needle slowly. “First, we breathe.” In… two. Out… two. “Then we stitch. In, out. In, out.”

Miko watched with bright eyes. “Can I help hold the scarf?”

“Yes,” said Pao. “Helping hands, helping hearts.” He showed Miko and Lili how to keep the cloth smooth. Stitch by stitch, the hole closed. Pao added the tiny flower patch. “This flower is from my family’s stories,” he said. “It reminds me of home.”

Lili smiled. “It is beautiful. My sad feels softer now.”

Pao said, “You named your feeling. You asked for help. You tried something new. That is strong.”

They all listened to the quiet night. The lantern made a gentle hum. The blanket felt fluffy and warm. Pao yawned. “Now it is time to sleep,” he said. “Let’s check our feelings again. Miko?”

Miko put a paw on his chest. “My worry is small and calm. It feels pale yellow now, like a soft sunrise.”

“Lili?” asked Pao.

Lili touched her scarf. “I feel proud and peaceful. My wings feel light.”

Pao smiled. “I feel glad to share and to help. I feel close to you both.”

They curled up on the bed. Pao whispered Grandma’s song. “Breathe in moonlight. Breathe out soft smiles.” The lantern glowed, and the room smelled like bamboo and thread and bedtime.

As their eyes closed, Pao said, “Feelings are friends with messages. We can listen. We can breathe. We can help. We can grow.”

Miko and Lili nodded sleepily. “Thank you, Pao.”

“Goodnight,” said Pao, patient and warm.

The End.
