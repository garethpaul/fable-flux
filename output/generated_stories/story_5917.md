# Ellie’s Turn in the Treehouse

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a curious trunk. One sunny morning, she walked down a leafy path to her friends’ treehouse. The wooden steps felt warm under her feet. The air smelled like pine and sweet flowers. Birds sang, “Chee-chee!” in the branches.

Ellie peeked up at the treehouse window. “Hello?” she called in a soft voice.

“Come in, Ellie!” chirped Pip, a tiny squirrel with a bushy tail. “We’re playing market!”

Inside, the treehouse was cozy and bright. Sunlight made golden shapes on the floor. There were baskets of pretend fruit and shiny buttons and a bell that went “ding!” when you pressed it. Tula the turtle wore a little apron. Buzzy the bee buzzed near a flower drawing.

Ellie smiled. “This looks fun.”

“It is,” said Pip. “We take turns being the shop helper. I’m first, then Tula, then you, then Buzzy.”

“Okay,” Ellie said, nodding. She felt happy to be included. She liked taking turns. It made games fair.

Pip rang the bell. “Ding! My turn to help! Who wants a pretend apple?”

“Me, please,” said Ellie.

Pip put a red wooden apple in Ellie’s basket. “Thank you for waiting your turn,” Pip said. Ellie’s trunk lifted with pride. It felt good to wait and then be seen.

When Pip’s turn ended, Tula stepped forward slowly. “It’s my turn now,” Tula said, smiling. She moved carefully to the counter. “I am ready to help.”

Ellie waited again. She listened to the bell. She smelled the sweet wooden fruit. She heard Buzzy’s wings hum, “zzzz.” Waiting can be hard, she thought. But it also felt calm, like a big slow breath.

Soon, Tula nodded to Ellie. “Now it’s your turn, Ellie.”

“My turn!” Ellie’s heart felt warm. She moved to the counter. Her trunk brushed the bell and it went “ding!” She liked that sound.

Buzzy zipped closer. “I want a bunch of grapes!” Buzzy buzzed.

Ellie looked at the line. “Buzzy, Pip is next in line,” she said kindly. “Then you. I will help everyone. We are taking turns.”

Buzzy hovered, then nodded. “Okay. I will wait.” Buzzy hummed a gentle tune while waiting.

Ellie gave Pip a grape bunch. Then she gave Tula a pretend lemon. “Thank you for waiting,” she told them. She felt proud to be fair.

Just then, Rusty the raccoon poked his head through the window. His paws were muddy. His eyes were bright. “Wow!” he said. “I want the bell! I want it now!”

Rusty climbed in and reached for the bell. Ellie’s ear twitched. She took a small step back. “Rusty,” she said in a steady voice, “it is my turn to use the bell. You can have a turn after Buzzy.”

Rusty frowned. “But I don’t want to wait.” He grabbed the bell. The bell wobbled in his paws and clanged, “di—di—ding!”

Ellie felt a funny twist in her tummy. She knew it was not right to take something when it was not your turn. She took a gentle breath and spoke clearly, “Rusty, please give the bell back. That is my boundary. I am using it now.”

Pip and Tula stood close to Ellie. Buzzy buzzed softly, “We take turns, Rusty.”

Rusty looked at the bell. He looked at Ellie’s kind eyes. He looked at the neat line of friends. His ears drooped a little. “I… I saw the shiny bell and I forgot,” he said. “I’m sorry.”

“Thank you for being honest,” Ellie said. “That is integrity. Integrity means telling the truth and doing the right thing, even when it is hard.”

Rusty’s shoulders relaxed. He held the bell out. “Here, Ellie. It’s your turn. I can wait.” He wiped his muddy paws on a cloth by the door so he wouldn’t make a mess.

Ellie took the bell back with a smile. “Thank you, Rusty. You can be after Buzzy.”

Rusty smiled. “Okay.”

Ellie rang the bell once—“ding!”—and kept helping. She handed out pretend blueberries that smelled faintly like the painted wood. She listened to the happy bird songs outside. She felt the smooth counter under her trunk.

When Buzzy’s turn ended, Ellie turned to Rusty. “Now it’s your turn,” she said.

Rusty stood tall. “My turn,” he said softly. He rang the bell one gentle time. “Ding.” He did not grab. He did not rush. He smiled at his friends. “Thank you for waiting with me.”

They all cheered. “Hooray!”

After the game, the friends sat in a cozy circle on a soft rug. They ate crunchy crackers and smelled sweet orange slices. Sunlight warmed their backs.

Pip said, “Today we learned to take turns.”

Tula nodded. “And we learned to speak up with kind words when someone takes something that is not their turn.”

Buzzy buzzed, “And we learned integrity is telling the truth and fixing mistakes.”

Rusty looked at Ellie. “Thank you for being brave and gentle,” he said. “I felt safe when you told me your boundary.”

Ellie’s ears wiggled. “Thank you for listening,” she said.

They cleaned up together. They put the fruit in baskets and wiped the counter. They took turns with the cloth, one wipe each, sharing the job. The treehouse smelled fresh, like orange peels and wood.

As they hugged goodbye, Ellie felt peaceful. Taking turns made the game fair. Honest words made hearts light. Kind boundaries made everyone feel safe.

Ellie waved her trunk and smiled. “We can play again tomorrow,” she said.

“Yes!” everyone cheered.

The End.
