# Fawn’s Polite Guest Adventure

The gentle deer fawn tiptoed into the guest room on soft, quiet hooves. The room was calm and cozy. Sunlight made a golden square on the rug. A clean bed stood tall with a fluffy pillow. A little table held a pretty bowl of shiny apples and tiny oranges. The air smelled like fresh soap and fruit.

“Hello, guest room,” whispered Fawn. “I will be very gentle.”

Fawn was visiting Auntie Doe’s house. The guest room was special. It was for visitors to rest. Auntie Doe had said, “This room is for guests. We keep it tidy. We ask before we touch things.” Fawn had nodded, but now the apples looked so red and round. Fawn’s tummy gave a little rumble.

Fawn’s ears twitched. “I feel hungry,” Fawn said to the quiet room. “But I did not ask.” Fawn remembered the rule: Ask first. Respect means we care about other people’s things and feelings.

Fawn took a deep breath. “I can be brave and do the right thing,” Fawn told the pillow. “I can ask.”

Fawn padded down the hallway. “Auntie Doe?” Fawn called softly.

Auntie Doe peeked from the kitchen. The kitchen smelled warm, like oatmeal and cinnamon. “Yes, little one?”

“I saw a bowl of apples in the guest room,” Fawn said. “They look so yummy. May I have one, please?”

Auntie Doe smiled. “Thank you for asking. The apples are for our visiting friend, Owl. But we can share. Let’s wash one together and slice it.”

“Share means we care,” Fawn said, feeling proud.

They washed an apple. The cool water splashed Fawn’s hooves. Auntie Doe cut the apple into little moons. Crunch! The slices were juicy and sweet. “Apples help our bodies grow strong,” Auntie Doe said. “That is good nutrition.”

Fawn nodded. “Crunch, crunch. Healthy snack!”

After the snack, Auntie Doe asked, “Would you like to make the guest room extra nice for Owl?”

“Yes!” said Fawn. “I will show respect.”

They carried a tiny vase with a yellow flower. It smelled like sunshine. Fawn smoothed the blanket and fluffed the pillow. Fawn lined up the tiny oranges in a happy circle. “The room feels peaceful,” Fawn whispered.

Just then, Fawn saw something shiny under the table. “Oh!” Fawn leaned closer. It was Owl’s little reading glasses case. It had a small blue feather painted on it. Fawn picked it up and turned it around. “It is very pretty,” Fawn said. “I want to look inside.”

Fawn opened the case with a click.

Inside were the round glasses, clean and bright. Fawn blinked at the big, round lenses. “Hello, glasses,” Fawn giggled. “You look like two little moons.”

Suddenly Fawn remembered: Ask first. This is Owl’s thing.

“Oh no,” Fawn whispered, ears drooping. “I opened it without asking.” Fawn’s tummy felt twisty. Fawn wanted to hide. But then Fawn took a brave breath. Courage means telling the truth and making it right.

Fawn carried the glasses case to Auntie Doe. “Auntie,” Fawn said, voice small, “I opened Owl’s glasses case without asking. I forgot the rule. I am sorry.”

Auntie Doe’s eyes were kind. “Thank you for telling me. That is very brave. Let’s close the case, put it back, and plan a polite apology for Owl.”

They closed the case with a soft click and set it on the table. Fawn made a tiny note on a leaf-shaped paper: “Dear Owl, I opened your case. I am sorry. From Fawn.”

That evening, Owl arrived with a gentle hoot. The guest room looked bright and welcoming. Owl’s feathers were soft and gray, and Owl’s eyes were warm.

Owl saw the little note. “Hoo-hoo,” Owl said, reading it. Owl turned to Fawn. “Thank you for your honest words.”

Fawn looked at the floor. “I forgot to ask. I will be more careful. I respect your things.”

Owl nodded. “Respect keeps friends feeling safe,” Owl said. “Thank you for your apology. I forgive you.”

Fawn’s heart felt light. “Thank you.”

Auntie Doe brought a snack tray. There were carrot sticks, apple slices, and a small bowl of nuts. The carrots were crisp, the apples sweet, and the nuts smelled toasty. “Let’s share a healthy snack together,” Auntie Doe said.

They sat on the soft rug. Crunch, munch, crunch. “Good snacks help us play and learn,” Owl said. “Good manners help us live together.”

Fawn smiled. “Today I learned to ask before I touch. I learned to say sorry. I learned that food can be healthy and yummy.”

Owl patted Fawn’s hoof with a feathered wing. “That is a big day,” Owl said.

The guest room felt calm and happy. The flower glowed in the soft light. The pillow looked extra fluffy, and the little oranges still made a happy circle.

Fawn yawned a tiny yawn. “Respect means care,” Fawn whispered. “Apology means we try again. Good food helps us grow. I can be brave and kind.”

Auntie Doe turned off the lamp. “Yes, little one. You did all of that today.”

Fawn curled up on the rug for a moment, listening to the quiet room. It smelled like clean sheets and sweet fruit. Fawn felt safe, loved, and proud.

The End.
