# Pebble the Penguin Writes a Story

In a playful living room full of pillows and soft rugs, Pebble the cheerful penguin waddled in with a happy wiggle. The room smelled like warm cookies. Sunlight made bright squares on the floor. A tiny fan hummed. A rainbow of crayons waited on a low table. A big notebook sat open, its pages clean and white like fresh snow.

Pebble clapped his little flippers. “Today, I will write a story!” he chirped. His voice sounded like a bell in the cozy room.

He tapped a blue crayon on the paper. Tap, tap. “Hmm,” he said. “What will I write?” He looked at the window, at the soft couch, at the teddy bear wearing a tiny scarf.

“Hello, Pebble,” said the teddy bear in his imagination. Pebble smiled. “Hello, Teddy. I want to write, but my words feel stuck.”

Pebble’s tummy fluttered. “I feel a little wiggly inside,” he said. “That feeling is worry.”

He took a slow breath. In, out. He pressed his flippers on his chest. Thump, thump. “It’s okay to feel worried,” he told himself. “I can try. I am brave.”

He drew a small circle on the page. “Step one,” he said in a whisper. “Think.” He closed his eyes. “What do I love?” He loved snow. He loved friends. He loved snacks. He loved the big world map on the wall that showed many places. He loved sharing.

“Step two,” Pebble said, tapping the crayon again. “Plan.” He made three dots. “Beginning, middle, end,” he sang softly. “I can do this.”

He drew a tiny penguin. “Beginning: A penguin says hello.” He smiled. “That penguin can be kind.” He drew a smiling sun. “Middle: The penguin meets new friends from far away.” He drew three little shapes: a fox, a koala, and a panda. “End: They help each other and share.”

Pebble felt warmth in his cheeks. His worry wiggled smaller. “Okay, words, let’s go,” he said.

He wrote slowly, saying each word out loud. “Hi,” he whispered as he wrote H-I. “I am Pip,” he said as he wrote I A-M P-I-P. The crayon felt waxy and smooth. Scritch, scritch. The notebook smelled like paper snowflakes.

He read his first line. “Hi, I am Pip.” Pebble clapped. “Yay! I wrote a sentence!” He felt proud. His heart felt like a warm cookie.

Then he frowned. “But what if it’s not good?” A little cloud came into his mind. His flippers drooped.

Pebble looked at the big world map on the wall. He waddled closer. “Hello, world,” he whispered. “There are many friends out there.” He put a flipper on a blue ocean. “Some friends say hello like ‘Hola.’ Some say ‘Ni hao.’ Some say ‘Jambo.’” He smiled. “Everyone’s words are different. My words can be mine. That is special.”

He waddled back to the table. “I will keep going,” he said. “When I feel unsure, I try again.”

He wrote the middle. “Pip meets Fox, Koala, and Panda. They say hello in many ways.” Pebble said the greetings out loud: “Hola! Ni hao! Jambo!” The sounds felt like tiny songs. He giggled.

He drew a red heart. “They all want a snack,” he wrote. “But there is one small plate.”

Pebble paused. “This is a problem,” he said. His head tilted. “What is the solution?” He tapped his crayon. Tap, tap, tap. Logical-thinking time! He made a list on the side.

- Take turns
- Share
- Make more snacks
- Ask for help

Pebble nodded. “I like share and take turns.” He wrote, “They share the snacks and take turns choosing. They feel happy.”

He wrote the end. “They write thank-you notes to each other. They draw pictures and say kind words. They feel proud and cozy.”

Pebble read his whole story out loud. His voice was calm and clear. He listened to the soft scritch of the pages as he turned them. He felt the cool crayon in his flipper. He smelled the sweet cookie air.

Then the worry cloud tried to come back. Pebble pressed his flippers to his chest again. “How do I feel?” he asked. “I feel proud. I feel brave. I feel curious.”

He nodded. “I did it.”

Just then, the little fan blew a tiny breeze. The paper fluttered. “Thank you, fan,” Pebble giggled. “You clapped for me.”

He put a star sticker on the page. “Writing helps me share my ideas,” he said. “Writing helps me know my feelings.” He pointed to the world map. “Writing can travel everywhere. It can say hello to friends far away.”

Pebble stood tall on his short legs. “Dear me,” he said kindly, “you tried. You planned. You wrote. You solved a problem. You are a writer.”

He closed the notebook gently. Pat. He hugged Teddy. “Teddy,” he said, “tomorrow I will write again.”

“What will you write?” asked Teddy in his imagination.

Pebble smiled. “I will write a thank-you note to the world.”

He looked at the bright squares of sun on the floor. He waddled through the warm light. His feathers felt soft. His heart felt steady.

Pebble waved at the map. “Hola, Ni hao, Jambo, Hello,” he said. “We can all write. We can all share. We can all be kind.”

The End.
