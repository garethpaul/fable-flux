# Dori the Dolphin’s Shed Surprise

Dori was a playful dolphin who loved to splash and squeak. One sunny morning, Dori swam up the stream that tickled the edge of a small garden. At the end of the garden stood a cozy wooden shed. It smelled like pine and warm soil. The door was open, and a soft ray of light made dust sparkle like tiny stars.

“Hello? Anyone here?” Dori clicked and chirped. Her voice echoed gently in the quiet shed.

Inside, there were boxes and baskets, brushes and buttons, ribbons and paper. Dori’s eyes shone. “A crafting place!” she squealed. Her tail flicked with happy swishes that sprayed cool mist in the air. She could hear the leaves rustle and the hum of a little fan. She could feel the smooth wood floor under her fins as she scooted in on a puddle of water.

On the workbench lay a sign that said: Craft Corner. Please Ask Before Using. Dori sounded out the words slowly. “Please… ask… before… using.”

Just then, a small voice peeped from a shelf. “You read the sign! Thank you!”

Dori blinked. A fluffy garden mouse popped her head out from a basket. She wore tiny glasses and a green apron dotted with paint. “I’m Millie,” said the mouse. “This is my shed. I make things here.”

“I’m Dori,” said the dolphin. “Your shed is wonderful. May I craft with you, please?”

Millie’s whiskers twitched with a smile. “Yes, thank you for asking. That’s kind.” She scurried down, her little feet pat-pat-patting. “What would you like to make?”

Dori tapped her chin with a fin. “Hmm. I want to make a sign for the garden. A Justice Sign. It will remind friends to be fair and kind.”

“I like that,” Millie said. “Let’s plan first.” She handed Dori a big piece of cardboard and a box of markers that smelled like berries. “What will the sign say?”

Dori closed her eyes to think. She listened to the soft buzz of bees outside and the clink of buttons in a jar. “It could say: We Share. We Ask. We Help.”

Millie nodded. “Good rules. That’s justice—being fair.”

Dori smiled. “And we use our skills to help each other.”

They spread out supplies. The cardboard felt sturdy. The markers felt smooth and cool in Dori’s fin. Millie opened a glue bottle with a gentle twist. “Let’s practice first,” she said. “We’ll sketch the letters in pencil so we don’t make big mistakes.”

Dori took a deep breath. “Yes. Step by step.” She drew big friendly letters. Swoosh, loop, line. When a letter tilted too much, she erased, giggling. “Try again,” she whispered to herself. “I can do it.”

They were almost ready to color when a buzzing beetle zipped in. “Make me a kite!” the beetle said, scooting right onto the bench. He grabbed a ribbon. “I want it now!”

Millie held up a tiny paw. “Stop, please. This is my craft space. You need to ask first.”

The beetle frowned. “But I want it!”

Dori puffed a little spray, careful not to splash. She spoke gently. “We ask before using. We wait our turn. That’s fair.”

The beetle wriggled his antennae. “Hmph.” He tugged at the ribbon again.

Millie’s voice was calm but firm. “My ribbon. My rules. Please let go.”

The beetle froze. He looked at the sign-in-the-making. He read the pencil words aloud, slow and shaky. “We… Share. We Ask. We Help.”

He let go of the ribbon. “I didn’t ask. I’m sorry,” he buzzed softly.

“Thank you for saying sorry,” said Millie. “Would you like to help us finish the sign? Then we can plan a kite together after.”

The beetle brightened. “Yes! I can place buttons!”

Dori smiled. “That’s a good plan. First we finish. Then we craft your kite—with asking, sharing, and waiting.”

They went back to work. Dori traced the letters with blue and green markers. The colors smelled like blueberries and fresh grass. Millie dabbed glue in careful dots—dot, dot, dot. The beetle pressed shiny buttons around the edges—click, click, click. They stood back to look. The sign was bright and kind, with a little painted dolphin, a tiny mouse paw print, and a beetle wing shape.

“Now we seal it,” Millie said, brushing a smooth coat over the top. It shone like morning dew.

Dori lifted the sign with her strong fins. “Let’s place it by the garden gate.”

They went outside. The air felt warm. A butterfly fluttered past. Dori posted the sign. The words glowed in the sun: We Share. We Ask. We Help.

A hedgehog rolled by and stopped. “What a fair sign,” he said. “It makes me feel safe.”

The beetle buzzed proudly. “I helped! And I waited my turn.”

Millie nodded. “You used your skills. Asking, planning, and making—those are skills too.”

Dori’s heart felt big and light. “Crafting can teach us justice,” she said. “We make with our hands and with our hearts.”

Millie clapped her little paws. “Now, about that kite…”

Dori smiled. “Let’s make it together. First, we ask. Then, we plan. Then, we craft!”

The wind whispered through the garden. The shed door creaked softly, like it was smiling too.

The End.
