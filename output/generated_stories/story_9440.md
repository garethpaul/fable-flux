# Tilly Turtle Listens

Tilly was a shy turtle with a soft green shell. She liked quiet places. Today, Tilly was in the laundry room. It smelled like fresh soap and warm towels. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer sang, “Thumpity-thump.”

Tilly peeked around a basket of socks. “Hello?” she whispered.

Coach Dot, a kind spotted ladybug, waved from the shelf. “Hi, Tilly! Ready for music time?” Coach Dot wore tiny headphones and carried a bright blue clipboard.

Tilly wiggled her toes inside her shell. “Music time sounds loud,” she said softly.

Coach Dot smiled. “We will keep it gentle. We will listen, breathe, and follow directions. That way, music feels safe.”

Tilly nodded. She wanted to be brave. She wanted to learn.

Coach Dot pointed to a simple sign on the wall. It had three pictures: an ear, a nose, and a heart.

“Step 1,” Coach Dot said, tapping the ear, “Listen to the words.”

“Step 2,” she said, tapping the nose, “Take a slow breath.”

“Step 3,” she said, tapping the heart, “Do the safe move.”

Tilly looked at the sign. The humming washer made tiny bubbles that went pop-pop. The room felt warm and cozy.

“Let’s try,” Coach Dot said. She clicked a soft song. The tune was calm, like a hug. “When I say ‘tap,’ we tap our feet. When I say ‘clap,’ we clap softly. When I say ‘shell,’ we hug our shell and breathe.”

Tilly listened. Her heart went pitter-pat. “I can try,” she whispered.

“Tap,” Coach Dot said.

Tilly listened to the word. She breathed in slowly. She tapped her feet—tap, tap—on the clean floor. It felt smooth and cool.

“Clap,” Coach Dot said.

Tilly listened. She breathed out slowly. She clapped softly—clap, clap—like tiny raindrops.

“Shell,” Coach Dot said.

Tilly listened. She took a big belly breath. She hugged her shell. Her shoulders relaxed. “Mmm,” she hummed.

“You did it!” Coach Dot cheered. “You followed the directions.”

Tilly’s shy smile grew. “I did,” she said.

The song changed to a faster beat. The dryer went thumpity-thump with the music. Socks swirled like a tiny dance party inside.

“Now we add the laundry moves,” Coach Dot said. “When I say ‘fold,’ we fold our arms. When I say ‘spin,’ we turn once, slow. When I say ‘pause,’ we freeze like a statue and breathe.”

Tilly peeked at the sign again. Ear. Nose. Heart. She could do this.

“Fold,” Coach Dot said.

Tilly listened. She breathed. She folded her arms. It felt neat and tidy.

“Spin,” Coach Dot said.

Tilly listened. She breathed. She turned once, slow, like the washer drum. The room swished by. She giggled.

“Pause,” Coach Dot said.

Tilly listened. She stopped and took a slow breath. In and out. Calm and quiet.

“You are a star listener,” Coach Dot said.

Just then, the song grew bouncy. Tilly got excited. She wanted to do all the moves at once! She started clapping and spinning and tapping together. Her feet got twisty. Her shell bumped a basket. Socks flew—plop! A red sock landed on her head.

“Oh!” Tilly said. She felt her cheeks get warm. She wanted to hide in her shell.

Coach Dot fluttered down. “It’s okay, Tilly,” she said kindly. “Let’s solve this problem. What helps when we feel mixed-up?”

Tilly looked at the sign. Ear. Nose. Heart. She took a little breath. “Listen,” she said. “Breathe. Do the safe move.”

Coach Dot nodded. “Yes. Let’s make a plan. We will follow directions, one step at a time.”

The music softened. Coach Dot spoke clearly. “First, ‘Pause.’”

Tilly listened. She breathed in and out. She felt her body calm, like warm towels on a rainy day.

“Next, ‘Fold.’”

Tilly folded her arms. The red sock slid off her head. Plop! She giggled again.

“Now, ‘Tap.’”

Tap, tap. Her feet felt steady.

“Then, ‘Clap.’”

Clap, clap. Soft like bubbles.

“Last, ‘Spin.’”

Tilly turned once, slow. No bumping. No flying socks. Just smooth and safe.

The basket sat still. The socks were quiet. Tilly felt proud inside. Her shell felt light.

Coach Dot clapped. “You did it, Tilly! You followed the directions and solved the problem.”

Tilly took a calm breath and smiled. “Listening helps,” she said. “Breathing helps too.”

“Music and mindfulness go together,” Coach Dot said. “When we listen and breathe, our bodies know what to do.”

The song ended with a gentle ding. The dryer stopped. The room was peaceful. It smelled like sunshine and clean cotton.

Coach Dot pointed to the sign one more time. “What are our steps?”

Tilly stood tall. “Step 1: Listen to the words. Step 2: Take a slow breath. Step 3: Do the safe move.”

“Perfect,” Coach Dot said. “Now you can teach others.”

Tilly tucked a folded sock into the basket. “I can help,” she said. She hummed the calm tune. Tap, clap, shell. Fold, spin, pause. She moved with care.

She looked up at Coach Dot. “I am shy,” she said, “but I can still follow directions.”

Coach Dot smiled. “That makes you brave.”

Tilly’s heart felt warm. She learned that following directions, using music, and taking mindful breaths helped her feel safe, calm, and proud.

The End.
