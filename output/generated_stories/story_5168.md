# Ellie’s Little World Shed

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray skin and tiny, careful feet. One sunny morning, she found a cozy garden shed behind the big mango tree. The shed smelled like fresh wood and warm soil. Inside, there were jars of buttons, bright paints, ribbon spools, and small tools that went click and clack.

Ellie peeked in and whispered, “Hello?” Her voice sounded like a soft drum. “Is anyone here?”

From a corner, a tiny voice answered, “Hello!” It was Pip, a bright green bug wearing a leaf hat. Pip waved his little hand. “Welcome to the Make-and-Share Shed.”

Ellie’s ears flapped gently. “Make and share?” she asked.

“Yes!” Pip chirped. “We make things together and share them with friends. Would you like to join?” He smiled with his twinkly bug eyes.

Ellie’s heart felt warm. “Yes, please. I like to be gentle and helpful.”

They looked at the shelves. Paints looked like rainbows in bottles. Fabrics felt soft like clouds. Ellie’s trunk smelled the lemons in the soap jar. “Mmm,” she hummed. “It smells friendly.”

Knock, knock! The door opened, and two more friends came in. Mina the mouse wore a tiny scarf with stars. Kwaku the sparrow hopped in with shiny blue feathers. “Hi, Ellie!” they sang together. “We heard there’s a project.”

Pip clapped. “Let’s build a Friendship World Map. We can show where our friends live and what they like. Everyone can see we belong together.”

Ellie smiled. “That sounds kind.”

Kwaku chirped, “I can bring twigs from the yard for borders.”

Mina squeaked, “I can cut shapes with my safe snip-snip scissors.”

Ellie said, “I can hold things steady with my trunk.” She swayed happily. She liked to help without bumping.

They laid a big cardboard on the floor. It felt bumpy under Ellie’s feet. “Soft steps,” she reminded herself. She pressed her trunk down gently to keep the cardboard still.

Pip pointed to a globe picture in a book. “Here is Earth, our home. So many places! So many people!” His wings buzzed with joy.

Ellie’s eyes grew round. “We can make it colorful.”

Together, they worked. Kwaku placed twig lines like rivers. “Cheep-cheep, steady,” he sang.

Mina cut soft shapes for countries. Snip, snip, snip. “This one is long and thin,” she giggled. “This one is round like a cookie.”

Ellie dabbed glue with her trunk. It smelled sweet and sticky. “I will count to three so the glue holds,” she said. “One… two… three.” Tap, tap.

Friends from the garden peeked in. Li the ladybug rolled in red beads. “These can be friendship dots,” she said.

Sami the snail slid slowly, leaving a shiny trail. “I can polish the edges,” he said kindly.

Nao the frog brought a small, smooth stone. “This is from the pond,” he croaked. “For the place where water friends live.”

Everyone had something to share. Ellie’s heart felt like a warm blanket. “Thank you,” she said. “We are a team.”

They added tiny pictures. A kite for a windy hill. A rice bowl for a faraway farm. A drum for a busy city. Pip wrote simple words on tiny flags. “Hello,” “Hola,” “Jambo,” “Namaste.” He read each one. “These words all mean hello.”

Ellie tried saying them softly. “Hola,” she said, and her trunk made a happy curl. “Jambo!” Her voice rumbled like a friendly purr. Everyone laughed with joy.

But then—oh no! A gust of wind whooshed through the open window. The papers fluttered like butterflies. A flag flew under a shelf. The twig border slid sideways.

Ellie took a deep breath. “We can fix it.” Her voice was calm. “We will help each other.”

Mina nodded. “I will find the flag under the shelf.” She scooted in and pulled it out with a proud squeak.

Kwaku held the window partly closed with a stick. “I’ll slow the breeze,” he chirped.

Ellie used her trunk like a steady tree. “Glue time again,” she said. “One… two… three.” Tap, tap. With careful teamwork, the map lay flat and bright.

They stepped back. The Friendship World Map glowed with color. It showed places near and far. It showed foods, songs, and games. It showed hello in many ways.

Pip asked, “What did we learn?”

Ellie thought. She listened to the soft hum of the shed. She smelled the lemon soap and the sweet glue. She felt the cool floor under her feet and the warm smiles of her friends. “We learned we can make big things when we work together,” she said. “We learned friends can come from many places. We learned every friend can help.”

Mina added, “We include everyone.”

Kwaku chirped, “We share and we care.”

Ellie smiled with her whole self. “Friendship is saying, ‘Come join us.’ Friendship is listening. Friendship is gentle and strong.”

They hung the map on the shed wall. It shone like a window to the world. Pip said, “Every day, we can say hello and help someone. That is being a good neighbor to the whole world.”

Ellie wrapped her trunk in a gentle hug around her friends. “I am happy,” she whispered. “Today we built a little world. Together.”

Outside, the mango leaves rustled like soft claps. The shed felt safe and bright. The friends packed up their tools and washed their hands in a tiny bowl that smelled like lemons.

“See you tomorrow,” said Pip.

“See you tomorrow,” echoed Ellie, Mina, and Kwaku.

Ellie looked at the map one more time. “Hello, world,” she said kindly.

The End.
