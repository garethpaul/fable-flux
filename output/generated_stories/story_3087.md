# Polly's Paint Problem

Polly the parrot loved colors more than anything in the world. Her bright green feathers sparkled in the sunny art room where she spent every day. The room smelled like fresh paint and had rainbow pictures covering every wall.

Today was special. Polly wanted to paint a beautiful picture for her friend Maya's birthday. She had seen the most amazing sunset yesterday - all orange and pink and purple swirled together like cotton candy.

"I'll make the most wonderful painting ever!" Polly squawked happily.

She dipped her brush in bright orange paint. Splash! The paint dripped everywhere except where she wanted it. She tried again with pink. Splat! The colors mixed into a muddy brown blob.

"Oh no!" Polly flapped her wings. "This isn't right at all!"

Her beautiful sunset looked like a messy mud puddle. Polly felt her chest getting tight and hot. Her feathers ruffled up. She wanted to throw the paintbrush across the room.

"I can't do this!" she cried. "It's supposed to be pretty, but it's all wrong!"

Just then, her friend Oliver the owl flew into the art room. His wise brown eyes noticed Polly's upset feathers right away.

"What's wrong, Polly?" Oliver asked gently.

"I'm trying to paint Maya's birthday picture, but everything is going wrong!" Polly's voice was shaky. "Look at this mess!"

Oliver looked at the painting carefully. "You know, Polly, when I feel frustrated like this, my grandmother taught me something special. She said to take three deep breaths and count to ten."

"Really?" Polly sniffled.

"Try it with me," Oliver said kindly. "Breathe in... one, two, three. Breathe out... four, five, six."

Polly breathed slowly with Oliver. The tight feeling in her chest started to go away. Her ruffled feathers smoothed down.

"Feel better?" Oliver asked.

Polly nodded. "A little bit. But my painting is still a mess."

"My grandmother also taught me that mistakes can become something beautiful," Oliver said. "What does your painting look like to you now?"

Polly tilted her colorful head and looked again. The muddy swirls suddenly looked different. "It looks like... like chocolate ice cream! Or maybe tree bark!"

"Exactly!" Oliver hooted. "You could add some green leaves and make it a beautiful tree!"

Polly's eyes lit up. She carefully painted green leaves around the brown swirls. Then she added a bright blue sky and yellow sun. Soon, she had painted the most wonderful tree Maya had ever seen.

"It's perfect!" Polly chirped. "Thank you for teaching me your grandmother's breathing trick, Oliver."

"That's what friends are for," Oliver smiled. "And now you can share this tradition with other friends when they feel frustrated too."

When Maya saw her birthday painting, she gasped with joy. "Polly, this tree is so beautiful! It looks just like the one where we first met!"

Polly felt warm and proud inside. She had learned that when things don't go as planned, taking deep breaths and looking at problems differently can turn mistakes into something wonderful.

From that day on, whenever Polly felt frustrated, she remembered Oliver's grandmother's wisdom. And she always shared the breathing trick with any friend who needed help feeling better.

The End.
