# Kora the Calm Koala Helps in the Study Corner

Kora was a calm koala with soft gray fur and a gentle smile. She loved the quiet study corner at the community library. The study corner smelled like paper and crayons. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Little chairs sat in a circle, and a basket held pencils, glue, and bright stickers.

Kora came to read and help. She liked to volunteer. “I can share,” she would say. “I can help.” Her heart felt warm when she helped.

One morning, Kora found a problem. The study corner was messy. Pencils rolled on the floor. Papers were crumpled. The sticker box was empty. Three friends looked upset: Benny the bunny, Dot the duck, and Miko the mouse.

Benny stomped his foot. “My drawing got squished!” he cried.
Dot flapped her wings. “I can’t find a clean page!” she quacked.
Miko squeaked, “I don’t know what to do.”

Kora took a slow breath in and out. In, out. Calm. She listened. She looked. She wanted to understand.

“Tell me what happened,” Kora said softly.

Benny’s ears drooped. “I was excited. I colored fast. I knocked the pencils.”
Dot shook her head. “I grabbed a lot of stickers. I wanted the shiny ones. Then they spilled.”
Miko wrung his tiny paws. “I felt small, so I hid my paper. Now it’s wrinkly.”

Kora nodded. “Thank you for telling me. I understand. You were excited. You wanted things to be special. It’s okay to feel that way.”

Benny sniffled. “Are you mad?”
Kora smiled. “No, little friend. I am calm. We can solve this together.”

Kora patted the soft rug with her paw. “Let’s make a plan. First, we breathe. In, out.” The friends copied her. In, out. Their bodies felt softer. Their faces relaxed.

“Next,” Kora said, “we will clean and sort. We can volunteer to help the study corner. Helping makes the space feel happy.”

“I will pick up pencils,” said Benny. His ears stood tall.
“I will stack clean paper,” said Dot, smoothing each page.
“I will gather glue and crayons,” said Miko. He squeaked a brave little squeak.

Kora hummed a gentle tune. She made three baskets: one for pencils, one for paper, and one for crayons. “We can be generous,” she said. “We share tools so everyone can make art.”

They worked together. The pencils made a soft clink. Paper made a crinkle sound. Crayons smelled a little sweet and waxy. Sunlight warmed their backs. The study corner began to look tidy and bright.

Dot found a shiny star sticker under a chair. She looked at her friends. “I wanted this for me,” she said, “but I will share.” She put the sticker in a little cup. “One each,” she quacked. Her feathers fluffed with pride.

Benny tugged on Kora’s arm. “I can be careful,” he said. “I will color slow.” He showed a smooth page. “Look! No tears.” His nose twitched happily.

Miko held up his wrinkled paper. “I feel shy,” he whispered, “but I want to try again.”

Kora’s eyes were kind. “I understand, Miko. Trying again is brave. Your feelings matter.” She gave him a fresh page. “We will make a cozy plan for you.”

“What is a cozy plan?” asked Miko.

Kora tapped her chin. “Healthy habits help us feel good. We can sit with straight backs. We can drink a sip of water. We can take breaks. We can ask for help.”

Kora set a little timer shaped like an apple. Tick, tick. “We draw for five minutes,” she said, “then we stretch our paws and wings.” She showed them how to roll their shoulders. She wiggled her fuzzy ears. Everyone giggled.

Soon, the study corner was neat. The baskets were full. The friends were ready. “Let’s begin,” said Kora.

Benny colored a slow, smooth rainbow. “I feel proud!”
Dot drew a duck pond with careful blue waves. “I feel calm.”
Miko drew a tiny mouse house with a door and two windows. He drew a smiling mouse. “I feel brave,” he whispered.

The apple timer dinged. They stretched, took sips of water, and smiled. Kora clapped softly. “You did it. You helped each other. You cared for the space. You cared for yourselves.”

A little girl peeked into the study corner. “Can I draw too?” she asked.

“Yes!” said Kora. “We share.” She handed the girl a pencil. “We have a kind rule here: We listen. We ask. We understand.”

The girl nodded. Benny made space. Dot shared stickers. Miko showed the new friend where the paper was. The study corner felt warm and friendly again.

Kora looked around. The problem was solved, and everyone felt good. “When we understand each other,” she said, “we make strong hearts.”

Benny hugged Kora. “Thank you for being calm.”
Dot smiled. “Thank you for helping us learn.”
Miko held his drawing up high. “I can do hard things,” he said.

Kora’s smile grew wide. “Yes, you can. We all can. We listen, we help, we share, and we take care.”

The End.
