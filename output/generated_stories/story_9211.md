# Tortoise Tim Cleans the Lab

In a quiet house on a rainy day, a wise tortoise named Tim lived in a cozy basement lab. The lab was full of gentle hums and tiny beeps. Glass jars clicked softly on shelves. A small fan whirred, “whirr, whirr.” The air smelled a little dusty.

Tim wore round safety goggles. He spoke slowly and kindly. “Good work needs clean hands,” he liked to say. He cared for plants and made little science projects. He also taught little helpers about listening and caring.

One day, Tim’s young neighbor, Mia, tiptoed down the stairs. “Hi, Tim!” she chirped. “Can I help?”

Tim smiled. “Hello, Mia. I am testing plant water. It must be very clean. Please wash your hands first.”

Mia bounced. She saw a shiny button. She saw a squishy sponge. She saw a jar of blue liquid. “Ooo!” she said. “Can I touch?”

“After we wash,” said Tim. He pointed his wise head toward the sink. “Soap, scrub, rinse, dry. That is the way.”

Mia wrinkled her nose. “But my hands look fine!”

Tim blinked gently. “Germs are tiny. We cannot see them. They can make plants and people sick. Washing is a helpful chore, like putting toys away and turning off lights.”

Mia looked at her hands. They had a little glitter from art class. “Okay,” she said. But then she heard the rain, drip-drop, drip-drop. She smelled the sweet blue liquid. She felt excited.

“I will be quick,” she whispered. She poked a jar lid. “Clink.” She tapped a tray. “Tap-tap.” She pressed the shiny button. “Beep!”

Suddenly, a soft “Whoosh!” blew from a tube. Dust puffs floated into the air like tiny gray clouds. A plant leaf drooped. The blue liquid wobbled. Mia jumped back.

“Oh no!” she gasped. Her tummy felt twisty. “I didn’t listen.”

Tim turned off the button. “Click.” He nodded slowly. “It is okay. We can solve problems,” he said in his calm voice. “First lesson: we pause and breathe.”

They both took a big breath. In. Out. The fan whispered, “whirr.” The rain tapped, “tap-tap.”

Mia said, “I am sorry, Tim.”

“Thank you for saying sorry,” said Tim. “Now, we will fix the mess. Step one is hygiene. Clean hands help us clean the lab.”

Mia walked to the sink. Tim taught a hand-washing song. “Soap on, scrub, scrub, scrub. Front and back and thumbs. Finger, finger, nail-beds too. Rinse the bubbles. Dry them, woo!”

Mia giggled as they sang. The warm water felt nice. The soap smelled like lemons. She scrubbed for the whole song. She dried with a soft towel.

Tim held up a small brush and a bin. “Step two is chores. We clean up our space. We wipe, sort, and put away.”

Mia nodded. “I can wipe!” She wiped the table in slow circles. “Swish, swish.” The dust went into the bin. Tim lifted the droopy leaf and misted it with clean water. “Pshh, pshh.” The leaf perked up.

“Step three,” said Tim, “is care for our Earth. We do not waste. We keep the air and water clean.” He pointed to three little bins. One for trash. One for recycling. One for compost. The labels were big and bright.

Mia sniffed the air. It smelled fresher now. “Where does this paper towel go?”

“Trash,” said Tim softly. “And this empty bottle?” He tapped it.

“Recycling!” said Mia.

They worked together. They listened. They moved slowly and safely. Tim wiped the button and covered it with a safe cap. “Buttons are for grown-ups or helpers who listen,” he said with a smile.

Mia placed the sponge back on its tray. She lined up the jars. “Clink, clink.” She even swept the floor with a tiny broom. “Swish, sweep.”

When the lab was tidy, the little plants looked happy. The lights glowed warm and gold. The blue liquid was put away in a closed cabinet. The fan hummed a gentle tune.

Tim washed his hands again. “We wash after cleaning, too,” he said. “Hygiene is before and after.”

Mia washed too. “Soap on, scrub, scrub, scrub,” she sang. She felt proud and calm.

Tim nodded. “You listened. You helped. The lab is safe and clean. The plants will grow strong.”

Mia smiled. “I learned three things! One: wash my hands to keep me and others safe. Two: do my chores to care for my space. Three: sort our waste to care for Earth.”

Tim’s eyes twinkled. “And one more: when we make a mistake, we say sorry, listen, and fix it.”

Mia gave Tim a careful hug. His shell felt smooth and cool. She looked around the bright, clean lab and took a deep breath. It smelled like lemons and rain.

“Can I come help again?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Tim. “Next time, we will make bubbles, after we wash.”

They both laughed. The rain slowed to a soft patter, and the basement lab felt cozy and kind.

The End.
