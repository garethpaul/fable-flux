# Miko’s Recycle Dance

In a comfortable nursery with sunny curtains and a cozy reading nook, a courageous monkey named Miko sat on a soft, green rug. The room smelled like clean soap and crayons. Gentle music played from a small speaker, and the afternoon light made warm shapes on the floor.

Miko loved to dance. He wiggled his tail and tapped his toes. Today, he wanted to do something brave and helpful, too. He looked around the nursery. Blocks sat in a neat basket. Dolls and cars rested in their places. But one thing was not neat at all.

“Oh,” Miko said. “The trash bin is full!”

Paper, bottles, and snack boxes were all mixed together. Some rolled onto the floor. One crunchy bottle rolled under the table and bumped his toe.

“Ouch! Hello, little bottle,” Miko giggled. “You are noisy.”

Miss June, the kind teacher, walked in with a smile. Her scarf smelled like oranges. “Hello, Miko,” she said. “I see a brave helper. Do you want to learn something new today?”

“Yes!” said Miko. His eyes were big and bright. “I want to help. I want to be brave. What can I do?”

“We can recycle,” said Miss June. She pointed to three colorful bins by the window. One bin was blue, one was green, and one was yellow. “Recycling means we put things in special bins so they can be used again. That helps our Earth.”

Miko’s tail curled with excitement. “I can do that! I am a courageous monkey! I can learn.”

Miss June showed him the bins. “Paper goes in the blue bin. It feels flat and soft.” She held a paper drawing. “Listen.” She crinkled it gently. “Shh-rrr.”

“Paper goes in blue,” Miko repeated. He touched the paper. “Flat and soft.”

“Plastic bottles go in the yellow bin,” Miss June said. She squeezed a bottle. “Crunch, crunch.”

“Bottles go in yellow,” Miko said. He grinned at the funny sound.

“Cardboard boxes go in the green bin,” Miss June said. She tapped a snack box. “Tap, tap. It is thick and a little rough.”

“Boxes go in green,” Miko said proudly. “I can do it!”

Miss June clapped softly. “You can. And you will. I believe in you.”

Miko felt warm and tall, like sunshine inside. But the trash pile looked big. He took a deep breath. He wiggled his toes. “I will make it a dance,” he said. “A recycle dance!”

He started with the paper. “Step, step, slide to blue,” he sang. He picked up drawings and napkins. “Paper in blue! Shh-rrr.” He dropped them in the bin. The paper made a soft flutter. His feet made little tap sounds on the rug.

Next came the bottles. “Bounce, bounce, hop to yellow,” he giggled. He found the crunchy bottle under the table. “Got you!” He squeezed it. “Crunch, crunch.” Into the yellow bin it went. “Good job, Miko,” he whispered to himself.

Now the boxes. “Spin, spin, tap to green.” He stacked boxes in his arms. They smelled like apples and crackers. “Tap, tap,” he said, tapping the side of the green bin. In went the boxes, neat and tidy.

Miss June watched with bright eyes. “You made a plan. You are careful and kind. You are helping our Earth.”

Miko’s cheeks felt warm with pride. But then he saw something silly. A shiny bottle cap sat in the wrong place. It was in the blue bin with the paper.

“Oh no,” Miko said softly. “I made a mix-up.”

He felt a little twist in his tummy. He looked down at his toes. Then he remembered Miss June’s words. “I can learn,” he said. “I am brave.”

He reached in gently and picked up the cap. It felt cool and smooth. “This goes in yellow with the bottles,” he said. He put it in the right bin. Then he took a deep breath. “When I make a mistake, I can fix it.”

Miss June nodded. “That is courage,” she said. “You keep trying.”

Miko smiled. “I can keep trying!”

Soon, a group of little friends came in. They smelled the orange scarf and heard the happy music. “What are you doing, Miko?” asked a small giraffe plush come to life in their pretend play.

“I am doing the Recycle Dance!” Miko said. “Step to blue, hop to yellow, spin to green! Do you want to try?”

“Let’s do it!” the children cheered.

They all danced, stepping and hopping to the bins, sorting paper, bottles, and boxes. The room filled with giggles, crunchy bottle sounds, and soft paper flutters. The bins looked neat and colorful. The floor was clean and calm.

Miss June clapped a joyful rhythm. “You are recycling heroes! You are helping the Earth. You are helping our nursery.”

Miko stood tall. He felt strong and happy. His heart beat to the music. “I did it,” he said. “I learned something new. I was brave. And I danced!”

Miss June gave him a gentle high-five. “You showed courage and care. Remember: When we recycle, we give old things a new life. That helps our planet. And when we try, even after a mix-up, we grow strong inside.”

Miko nodded. “Paper in blue, bottles in yellow, boxes in green,” he said. “I can teach my friends and my family.”

He did one last twirl. The sunlight made a warm circle around him. “Step to blue, hop to yellow, spin to green,” he sang. “Recycling is our happy team!”

The nursery felt fresh and bright. Miko’s tail swished with pride. He knew he could learn new things every day, and he could help the Earth with every little dance.

The End.
