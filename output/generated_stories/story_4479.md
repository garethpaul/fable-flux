# Tiko the Tortoise and the Pantry Parade

Tiko was a wise tortoise with a smooth, shiny shell the color of warm tea. He lived in a quiet house where the pantry smelled like cinnamon, apples, and fresh bread. The shelves were tall and tidy, and the floor felt cool under Tiko’s careful feet.

Each morning, Tiko visited the pantry to prepare his small basket of snacks. He liked to be ready for the day. “Slow and ready,” he would say with a smile. “That is my way.”

One sunny day, Tiko opened the pantry door with a gentle push. Creak. The room felt cozy. He heard the soft rustle of paper bags and the tiny hum of the fridge nearby. He sniffed the sweet smell of bananas. “Mmm,” he said. “Hello, snacks.”

On the lowest shelf, a jar of peanut butter sat beside a loaf of bread. A bunch of bananas leaned like a little yellow moon. Tiko pulled his basket close. “We will be brave today,” he told himself. “We will be kind. We will be ready.”

As Tiko reached for a banana, he heard a squeak. “Help!” cried a small voice. “I’m stuck!”

Tiko blinked. “Who is there?” he asked, his voice soft and calm.

From behind a bag of flour peeked a tiny field mouse with gray whiskers and bright eyes. A loop of paper had slipped around her tail. “I’m Mimi,” she said, squeaking. “I just wanted a crumb. I wanted to be brave. But now I’m stuck, and I’m scared.”

Tiko nodded kindly. “Being brave can feel scary. You asked for help. That is brave, too,” he said. “Let’s see what we can do.”

Tiko looked around. He saw a short wooden spoon. He saw a clean cloth. He saw a little bottle of water. He took a slow breath. “We will prepare,” he said. “We will be gentle.”

He slid the spoon under the loop to lift it a little. “Mimi, can you wiggle your tail?”

Mimi sniffed. “I can try.” She wiggled, but the loop held tight.

Tiko wrapped the cloth around the spoon so it would be soft. He dripped a tiny drop of water on the paper loop. “Paper loosens with water,” he explained. “We can help it let go.”

Mimi wiggled again. The damp paper softened. Slip! Her tail popped free.

“I’m free!” Mimi squealed, hopping in a happy circle. “Thank you! You are so kind!”

Tiko smiled. “Kindness makes our hearts warm,” he said. “Now, let us clean up.” He folded the loose paper, tied it in a neat knot, and put it in the bin. “Preparation keeps us safe,” he added. “We put things away so tails don’t get stuck.”

Mimi’s nose twitched. “It smells sweet in here,” she said.

“It does,” Tiko agreed. “Would you like to share a snack?”

Mimi’s eyes grew big. “Share? With me?”

“Yes,” said Tiko. “We share when we can. And we take care of our things.”

He set his small cutting board on the floor. He washed his hands at the little water bowl by the door. Drip, drip, drip. He dried them on the clean cloth. “First, we prepare,” he said. He sliced a banana into soft, tiny circles. He spread a thin layer of peanut butter on a small square of bread. He placed one banana circle on top. “Banana buttons,” he said with a grin.

Mimi clapped her tiny hands. “Banana buttons!”

They sat together in the cozy pantry. The bread felt soft. The banana tasted sweet. The peanut butter smelled nutty and made their tongues stick a little. They giggled.

“Thank you for being brave,” Tiko said. “You asked for help. That was smart.”

Mimi nodded. “You helped me kindly. You were brave, too.”

Tiko tilted his head. “Sometimes being brave means moving slowly and thinking. Sometimes it means speaking up. And sometimes it means standing up for what is right.”

Just then, they heard a rattle on the top shelf. Rattle, rattle. A bag of pasta leaned too close to the edge. Tiko looked up. “That bag could fall,” he said. “We should be ready.”

He took a deep breath. “I will climb the step stool,” he decided. “Slow and steady.” He tucked his basket aside and placed his feet carefully, one step at a time. Tap. Tap. Tap. “I can do hard things,” he whispered.

Mimi watched with wide eyes. “Go, Tiko!”

At the top, Tiko reached out his sturdy shell and nudged the bag back to a safe spot. He lined up the jars and boxes, making a neat row. “Preparation keeps us safe,” he repeated. “And it keeps the pantry kind for everyone.”

When Tiko climbed down, Mimi gave him a tiny high-five. “You did it!”

Tiko smiled. “We did it together.”

They packed up the board, the cloth, and the spoon. They closed jars with soft clicks. They put the basket by the door. The pantry felt calm and tidy.

Mimi took a deep breath. “I learned today,” she said. “Kindness helps friends. Preparation keeps us safe. And being brave can be quiet.”

Tiko nodded. “Yes,” he said. “Kindness first. Then careful steps. We can be kind to others and ourselves.”

They shared one last banana button and laughed when the peanut butter made Mimi’s whiskers wiggle.

“Thank you, Tiko,” Mimi whispered.

“Thank you, Mimi,” Tiko replied.

They opened the pantry door together. The warm hallway light spilled in, like sunshine on their faces.

The End.
