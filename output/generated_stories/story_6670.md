# Gigi's Special Organization Day

Gigi the giraffe loved her cozy closet hideaway. It was tucked behind the winter coats, where she could stretch her long neck up to the high shelves. But today, her special place looked very messy.

"Oh my!" Gigi said, looking around. Toys were scattered everywhere. Books lay open on the floor. Her art supplies had spilled out of their box. Even her favorite purple blanket was bunched up in a corner.

Gigi's mom called from downstairs. "Gigi, your cousin Leo is coming to play in one hour!"

Gigi's heart sank. She wanted to show Leo her hideaway, but it was too messy. What if he couldn't find a place to sit? What if he stepped on her crayons?

"I need to clean this up," Gigi said to herself. "But how can I do it all by myself?"

Gigi looked up at the high shelves with her long neck. She could see empty boxes up there. "I have an idea!" she said.

First, Gigi made a plan. She would sort everything into groups. Books in one pile, toys in another, and art supplies together.

"One, two, three books," Gigi counted as she stacked them carefully. The books felt smooth and cool in her hooves.

Next came the toys. Gigi's stuffed elephant was dusty, so she gave it a gentle pat. Her wooden blocks clinked together as she gathered them up.

"This is working!" Gigi said proudly.

But then she faced a big problem. The art supplies were too heavy to carry up to the high shelf all at once. Gigi thought hard.

"I know!" she exclaimed. "I'll use my long neck to help!"

Gigi carefully picked up the art box with her mouth and stretched her neck way up high. She placed it gently on the top shelf where it belonged.

For the toys, Gigi found a medium shelf that was just right. She could reach it easily without stretching too far.

The books went on the low shelf where she could see all the colorful covers.

Just as Gigi was folding her purple blanket, she heard footsteps.

"Gigi? Where are you?" called Leo.

"I'm in my closet hideaway!" Gigi called back. "Come see!"

Leo peeked through the coats. His eyes grew wide. "Wow, Gigi! This place is amazing! Everything is so neat and organized!"

"Thank you," Gigi said, feeling warm and proud. "I cleaned it all by myself. I made a plan and used my long neck to help."

Leo sat down on the soft purple blanket. "Can you teach me how you organized everything? My room is really messy too."

Gigi smiled big. "Of course! First, we make a plan. Then we sort things into groups. And if something seems too hard, we think of a creative way to solve the problem."

"You're so smart, Gigi," Leo said. "And responsible too!"

As they played together in the clean, cozy space, Gigi felt happy and proud. She had taken responsibility for her mess. She had made a good plan. And she had found a creative solution when things got tricky.

"Taking care of my things feels really good," Gigi thought to herself. "And now Leo and I can have so much fun in my special hideaway!"

The two cousins spent the whole afternoon reading books, building with blocks, and drawing pictures in Gigi's perfectly organized closet hideaway.

The End.
