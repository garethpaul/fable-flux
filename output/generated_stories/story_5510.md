# Rocco’s Bubble-Time Beat

Rocco the raccoon loved music. He drummed on pots, hummed in halls, and tapped rhythms with his striped tail. Today, Rocco was excited. He wanted to record a new song with his friends after breakfast. But first, he had to get ready in the bathroom.

Rocco scooted in, his paws pat-pat on the tile. The room smelled like mint and warm soap. The mirror was foggy from Papa’s shower, and the sink shone like a tiny moon.

Rocco’s fluffy bandmates waited in the hallway: Tia the turtle with a tiny tambourine, Amir the armadillo with a shaker egg, and Mei the mouse with a bright blue kazoo. They all loved making music together.

“Rocco, we can jam in ten minutes,” said Tia softly. “Meet us outside!”

“Ten minutes?” Rocco’s ears perked up. “I can do it!” He patted his pockets for a plan, but pockets do not hold plans. Plans live in our heads.

Rocco bounced to the faucet. “I’ll brush for one quick second!” he said. He squeezed a big mountain of toothpaste onto his brush. Some splooped onto his nose.

“Minty!” he giggled, licking his nose. He began to strum his toothbrush like a guitar. “Ching-ching! I’m a superstar!”

He forgot to brush.

He peeked at the tub. Bubbles! A shiny bottle sat on the edge. It smelled like oranges and sunshine. Rocco turned the tap. Water rushed—whooosh!—like a river in a song. He wiggled his tail and banged the soap dish like a drum. “Ba-dum-dum! Ba-dum-dum!”

Minutes slipped by like little fish.

Tia knocked gently. “Rocco, five minutes!”

“Okay!” Rocco called, heart jumping. He grabbed his brush. “Scrub-scrub-scrub—wait! I need my hair pick!” He climbed onto a stool and peered at his reflection. His masky eyes looked big and bright. His hair was a wild forest. He began to fluff and pose. “Rocco Rockstar!”

He forgot to brush again.

Amir peeked in with kind eyes. “Do you need help, Rocco?”

Rocco’s cheeks warmed. “I want to be fast, but I keep stopping. I want to play music so bad.” His voice got wobbly. “I feel…grr…frustrated.”

Amir nodded. “Sometimes my shell straps twist, and I feel twisty too. We can make a plan beat.”

“A plan beat?” Rocco blinked.

Amir tapped the doorframe. Tap… tap-tap… tap. “We use a beat for time. One beat for each step.”

Mei scurried up with the blue kazoo. “We can help count! It’s kind to help friends.” She smiled. “We all get ready in different ways. That’s okay.”

Rocco took a deep breath. In… out. The breath smelled like minty hope. “Okay. What’s the plan?”

Tia held up her tambourine. “Three steps. Brush. Wash. Comb. We give each step a little song.”

Rocco’s tail swished. “I like that!”

Amir started the beat. Tap… tap-tap… tap.

“Step one: Brush!” sang Mei.

Rocco squeezed a pea of toothpaste this time. “Not a mountain, just a pea,” he said. He held the brush to his teeth. Tia shook the tambourine slow. Jing… jing… jing… “Two minutes,” she said. “Let’s count to twenty, three times.”

“Scrub in circles,” Amir whispered. “Front teeth, side teeth, back teeth.”

Rocco brushed. Tiny bubbles foamed. The mint felt tingly and cool. “One, two, three…” he counted with his friends. The sound of counting made him calm. His shoulders relaxed. He spit—ptoo!—and rinsed with a sloshy swish.

“Step two: Wash!” Mei sang.

Rocco pumped soap. It smelled like oranges and clean rain. “Wash paws,” he said. “Tops and bottoms.” He rubbed until the soap felt slippery like a fish. The water whooshed again, but now it was part of the song: whoosh-whoosh, drip-drip.

“Step three: Comb!” chanted Tia.

Rocco picked his hair. Pick, fluff, smooth. He made a little beat with the comb’s teeth. Tik-tik-tik. He smiled at his tidy face. “I did it!”

He looked at the clock sticker on the wall. A friendly sun smiled back. “We still have two minutes!”

Rocco’s heart did a happy drum. Ba-bump, ba-bump. His frustration felt smaller now, like a crumb. He turned to his friends. “Thank you for being kind.”

Amir grinned. “We all need help sometimes.”

Mei tooted a tiny kazoo flourish. “Ta-da!”

Tia nodded. “Do you want to practice the plan again tomorrow? Practice makes skills strong.”

Rocco bounced. “Yes! I’ll make a song for it.” He grabbed his little drum from the bathroom shelf. “It goes like this: Brush-brush, wash-wash, comb-comb—go!”

They marched to the door together, paws, hooves, claws, and tiny feet tapping in time. Rocco turned off the tap and put the cap on the toothpaste. “I can be fast and gentle,” he said. “Fast is not rush. Fast is plan.”

Outside, the jam began. Tia’s tambourine jingled. Amir’s shaker egg shushed. Mei’s kazoo buzzed. Rocco kept the steady beat. He felt proud and peaceful. He had used his time well, and he had been kind to himself when he felt frustrated.

After the song, Rocco said, “My plan beat helped. When I felt grr, I took a breath. I asked for help. I did one step at a time.”

Tia smiled. “That’s time-management!”

Amir added, “And frustration-coping.”

Mei squeaked, “And kindness!”

Rocco giggled. “And practice. Tomorrow we do it again.”

They bumped elbows, tails, and paws in a happy, wiggly cheer. The bathroom still smelled like mint and oranges, and the mirror showed four smiling friends who were all a little different and all together.

The End.
