# Oliver Owl's Big Goal

Oliver Owl lived in a cozy guest room at Grandma Rose's house. His soft brown feathers rustled as he perched on the windowsill, watching the morning sun peek through the flowery curtains. The guest room smelled like lavender and felt warm and safe.

"Hoot, hoot!" Oliver said excitedly. "Today I want to learn how to fly all the way around the big oak tree in the yard!"

Oliver had been practicing flying for weeks, but he could only make it halfway around the tree before getting tired. His little wings would flap and flap, but then he'd have to rest on a branch.

"I'm going to do it today!" he declared, puffing out his fluffy chest.

Oliver flew out the window and zoomed toward the oak tree. He flapped his wings as hard as he could. "Flap, flap, flap!" But just like before, he got tired halfway around and had to stop on a branch.

"Oh no!" Oliver hooted sadly. His wings felt heavy and his heart felt disappointed. "Why can't I do it? I want to fly around the whole tree!"

Oliver felt so frustrated that his feathers ruffled up. He wanted to give up and hide back in the guest room under the soft quilted blanket.

Just then, Grandma Rose came outside with a cup of tea that smelled like honey.

"What's wrong, little Oliver?" she asked kindly, sitting on the garden bench.

"I want to fly around the whole oak tree, but I can't do it!" Oliver said honestly. "My wings get too tired!"

Grandma Rose smiled warmly. "That sounds like a wonderful goal, Oliver. But tell me, how are you preparing for such a big flight?"

"Preparing?" Oliver tilted his head, confused.

"Yes, dear. Big goals need good preparation," Grandma Rose explained. "Just like when I bake cookies, I need to gather all my ingredients first. What do you think your wings need to get stronger?"

Oliver thought carefully. "Maybe... practice?"

"Exactly! And what else do strong flyers need?"

"Food? And rest?" Oliver's big round eyes grew brighter.

"Very good thinking!" Grandma Rose clapped. "Why don't you make a plan? Start with shorter flights around smaller trees, eat healthy seeds, and get plenty of sleep. Then work your way up to the big oak tree."

Oliver felt excited again. "So I should set smaller goals first?"

"That's right! Small steps lead to big achievements."

The next day, Oliver started his new plan. First, he flew around the little maple tree - success! Then the medium birch tree - he did it! Each day, he practiced a little more, ate nutritious seeds, and rested well in his cozy guest room.

After a week of preparation, Oliver felt ready. His wings were stronger, and he felt confident.

"Today's the day!" he hooted happily.

Oliver took a deep breath and began flying around the big oak tree. His wings felt strong and steady. Around and around he went - once, twice, three times!

"I did it! I did it!" Oliver celebrated, landing gracefully on the windowsill of the guest room.

Grandma Rose cheered from below. "Congratulations, Oliver! You set a goal, made a plan, and worked hard to achieve it!"

Oliver learned that big dreams come true when you prepare well, practice every day, and never give up. Even when things feel hard, taking small steps makes big goals possible.

The End.
