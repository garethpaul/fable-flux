# The Fawn and the Sticky Paint Plan

In a bright, sunny creative art room, a gentle deer fawn named Feli peeked inside. The room smelled like sweet paste and fresh paper. Soft music hummed. Brushes stood in cups like tiny trees, and colored paints shone like rainbows in little trays.

Feli’s hooves clicked softly on the floor. “Hello?” she said in her small, musical voice.

“Hi, Feli!” chirped Pip the sparrow, fluttering by the window.

“Hi!” squeaked Mallow the mouse from a basket of ribbon.

Feli smiled. Her big ears twitched with excitement. “I want to make a big, bright picture,” she said. “I want a sun, and leaves, and clouds, and flowers. But I only have this one big paper. I don’t want to make a mistake.” Her soft tail flicked with worry.

Mallow sat on a spool. “You can do it!”

Pip nodded. “Yes, you can!”

Feli took a deep breath. “Okay.” She dipped a wide brush into yellow paint. The yellow was shiny and thick. “Oh!” she said. The brush felt heavy and slippery in her hoof.

She touched the paper. Splish! A big blob of yellow paint dropped down. It spread like a puddle.

Feli stepped back. “Oh no,” she whispered. “I wanted a small sun, but this blob is too big.”

Her heart beat faster. She wanted to crumple the paper. She wanted to run. But she stopped. She remembered what her mama deer said: “When there is a problem, stop. Look. Think. Then try a plan.”

Feli took another deep breath. In. Out. Her chest felt calmer.

“Stop,” she told herself.

She looked at the messy yellow blob. It glowed warm and bright. “Look,” she said. “What is this like?” She tilted her head. The blob was round and shiny.

“It looks like a sun!” Pip chirped.

Feli’s eyes lit up. “Yes! The blob can be my sun.” She smiled. “Now, I will think. How can I help the sun look neat?”

She picked a small brush. “I will use a tiny brush for tiny lines.” Carefully, she dipped it in orange paint. She tapped, tap, tapped little sun rays around the blob. “Ray, ray, ray,” she sang.

The rays looked happy and warm. “My first plan worked,” Feli said with pride.

Next, Feli wanted green leaves. She reached for the green paint. But the green tray was dry and sticky. “Oh,” she said. “This paint is too sticky. It won’t move.”

“That is a problem,” Mallow said, whiskers twitching.

“Stop. Look. Think,” Feli whispered. She looked at the sink. She heard water trickling. She thought, “Water makes paint softer.”

“Maybe a tiny bit of water,” she said. She dipped her brush in water, then in the green. She swirled gently. Swish, swish. The paint turned smooth and shiny.

She painted leaf shapes. “Soft strokes,” she murmured. The leaves curved like smiles.

“Good thinking!” Pip cheered.

Feli giggled. Her worry started to melt away.

Now she wanted soft, fluffy clouds. She tried to make clouds with a brush. But the points looked sharp. “These are not soft,” she said, frowning.

“Hmm,” she hummed. “Stop. Look. Think.” She looked around the room. She saw a bag of cotton balls on a shelf. They looked like tiny, white puffs.

“Maybe I can dab the paint with cotton,” Feli said. She dipped a cotton ball into white paint and pressed it on the paper. Dab, dab, dab. The paint made soft, round puffs. The clouds looked cozy and gentle.

“Oooh,” Mallow squeaked. “Fluffy clouds!”

Feli nuzzled the paper. It smelled like clean paint and cotton. She felt warm and brave inside.

Then, as she reached for blue paint, her hoof bumped the water cup. Tip! The water wobbled. Splash! A puddle spilled on the paper near the bottom.

Feli froze. “Oh no! My picture!” Her eyes felt prickly with tears.

Pip fluttered close. “It’s okay. You can fix it.”

Feli nodded slowly. “Stop. Look. Think.” She looked at the splash. It was watery blue. It made wavy shapes.

She thought of a pond where she drank cool water with Mama. She could hear the soft plop of frogs, see the ripples in the sun.

“A pond,” Feli whispered. “The splash can be a pond.” She smiled again. “I need a plan to make it neat.”

She found a sponge. It was soft and bouncy. She pressed it around the edge of the splash. Dab, dab. The edges turned smooth and round. She added tiny dots with a small brush. “Hello, pebbles,” she said. She drew a little lily pad. She dotted a pink flower on top.

The picture now had a big sun, green leaves, fluffy clouds, and a shiny pond. Feli stepped back and looked. Her ears twitched with joy. “I did it,” she said. “I solved each problem with a plan.”

Pip and Mallow clapped their wings and tiny paws. “You were patient,” Pip sang.

“You used your thinking steps,” Mallow added. “Stop. Look. Think. Try!”

Feli nodded. “And I did not give up,” she said. “Mistakes can become ideas.”

She wrote her name in the corner with a careful hoof. F-E-L-I.

Feli smiled at her friends. “Thank you for cheering me on.”

“Any time!” they said.

She hung her picture on the wall. The sun glowed. The clouds puffed. The pond shone. The room smelled like a happy day.

Feli turned to the door. “Today I learned to be calm and to make a plan,” she said. “When I have a problem, I will stop, look, think, and try again.”

Pip chirped, “That is a bright idea!”

Mallow giggled, “Like your sun!”

Feli’s heart felt light. She trotted out, ready for the next picture, the next plan, and the next smile.

The End.
