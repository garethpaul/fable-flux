# Squeaky Squirrel Follows the Steps

In a warm, comfortable nursery, the air smelled like clean soap and sleepy lavender. Soft lamps glowed like cozy moons. The carpet felt fluffy under tiny toes, and wooden blocks clicked-clacked like little drums. In the corner, a small window let in a breeze that whispered, shhh.

Squeaky the resourceful squirrel lived in this nursery. He had soft gray fur and a big, bright tail. Squeaky loved to help. He loved to build, fix, and sign up for jobs. He wore a tiny apron with pockets for string, stickers, and a little safety whistle. “I can do it!” Squeaky would say, patting his apron.

One sunny morning, Miss Dot, the gentle teacher with red glasses, rang the bell. “Ding-ding!” The children and Squeaky gathered on the carpet. “Today,” Miss Dot said, smiling, “we will hang the Happy Day Banner. But we must follow the steps. Following directions keeps us safe and helps us do our best.”

Squeaky’s tail swished with excitement. “I can help! I can hang it high!” He bounced on his little paws.

Miss Dot nodded. “Thank you, Squeaky. Step one: We clear the space. Step two: We ask a grown-up for the step stool. Step three: We hold the banner with two hands. Step four: We clip it to the string. Step five: We check with a friend. We do it slowly and safely.”

Squeaky listened, but his paws felt extra bouncy. The banner was rainbow-bright, with glitter stars that sparkled like tiny suns. He loved shiny things. He scooped up the banner and whispered, “I can do it super fast.”

He zipped to the corner without clearing the space. The blocks made a little mountain there. Squeaky tiptoed around them and reached up, up, up on his tippy toes. “Almost there,” he squeaked.

Crunch! His paw nudged a block. The mountain wobbled. Clatter-clatter! Blocks tumbled like popcorn. Squeaky froze. His heart thumped thump-thump, and the banner drooped to the floor. The glitter stars looked sad.

Miss Dot walked over calmly. “Are you okay, Squeaky?”

Squeaky’s ears drooped. “I wanted to help. I didn’t follow the steps.”

Miss Dot crouched to his level. Her voice was warm like the lamp light. “Thank you for being honest. Guess what? We can try again. Let’s practice following directions to keep everyone safe.”

Squeaky took a deep breath. The room smelled like crayons and apples today. He puffed his cheeks and let the breath out slow. “Okay. Step one: Clear the space.”

Together, they picked up the blocks. “Clink, clack, clink,” went the tidy puzzle of blocks back into the bin. Squeaky smiled a little. “That sounds nice,” he said.

“Step two,” said Miss Dot, “ask a grown-up for the step stool.”

Squeaky stood tall and respectful. “Miss Dot, may I please have the step stool?”

“Yes,” said Miss Dot. “Thank you for asking.” She placed the small stool on the carpet. “We will open it and check it.” Squeaky touched the stool with one paw. It felt cool and smooth. “Click,” it locked in place.

“Step three,” Squeaky said, his tail settling into a happy curve, “hold the banner with two hands.”

He held the banner carefully. The glitter stars tickled his paws. He giggled. “It’s sparkly and soft.”

“Step four, clip it to the string,” Miss Dot reminded.

Squeaky climbed one step. “One, two,” he counted. The stool was steady. He reached up with both hands. “Clip,” went the first side. “Clip,” went the second side. The banner hung like a rainbow smile.

“Step five,” Squeaky said, feeling proud but still careful, “check with a friend.”

“Friend check!” said Leo, the little lion plush who sat by the window. Squeaky held him up like a helper. Miss Dot chuckled. “I’ll be your friend check.” She gently tugged the banner. “Secure and safe.”

Squeaky’s chest felt warm, like sunshine on fur. “We did it!” he squeaked. He looked at the spot where the blocks had been. “Clearing the space made it easy. Asking for the stool kept me safe. Doing it slowly helped me do it right.”

Miss Dot nodded. “You improved because you listened. You understood the directions, and you respected the rules. That keeps everyone safe.”

Later, snack time smelled like cinnamon toast. The children munched, and Squeaky sipped warm milk. He told a tiny mouse friend, Mina, what he learned. “When we follow steps,” he said, “our work gets better, and our bodies stay safe.”

Mina’s whiskers wiggled. “What if I forget a step?”

Squeaky smiled kindly. “It’s okay to try again. We can ask for help. We can go slow. We can listen with our ears and our hearts.”

Miss Dot clapped softly. “Friends, look up.” The Happy Day Banner shone with glittery stars. The room felt even cozier. Everyone cheered. “Hooray for following directions!”

Squeaky’s tail swished like a happy metronome. He tugged his apron and blew his tiny safety whistle once. “Tweet!” Not too loud, just right. “I’m a helper who listens,” he said.

Miss Dot gave him a sticker shaped like a golden acorn. It felt smooth and proud on his apron. “For safety, understanding, and respect,” she said.

Squeaky hugged Mina and Leo. “Next time,” he said, “we’ll follow the steps from the start.”

The nursery hummed with soft music and friendly voices. The lamps glowed. The banner smiled. Squeaky felt calm, brave, and ready to help again—one careful step at a time.

The End.
