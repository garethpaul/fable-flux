# Squeaky-Clean Squirrel Saves the Day

In a bright, bubbly bathroom, a small, resourceful squirrel named Squeaky lived on a soft towel by the window. The bathroom smelled like lemon soap. Sunlight made tiny rainbows on the shiny mirror. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet, like a slow little song.

Squeaky loved to be ready. “Prepared means proud!” he chirped, patting his tiny bag. Inside were a mini toothbrush, a tiny comb, a drop of soap, and a soft acorn cloth. He practiced words he heard from the family: “soap,” “rinse,” “scrub.” He liked how they felt in his mouth. “So-ap,” he said slowly, tasting the sounds.

One morning, the door swung open. In came Milo, a messy puppy with muddy paws and a wiggly tail. “I’m late! I’m late!” Milo yipped. Mud splattered on the floor. Plop! Splat! The tub looked like a muddy pond.

Squeaky’s eyes grew wide. “Oh my whiskers,” he whispered. “This is a big problem.”

Milo bounced and barked, “No time to wash! I have to go to the park!” He hopped onto the bathmat, leaving brown paw prints like little stars.

Squeaky took a deep breath. He put his little paw on his chest and felt it rise and fall. “We can solve this,” he said. “We can be quick and clean.”

Milo frowned. “But washing takes forever,” he grumbled.

“Not if we prepare,” said Squeaky. He opened his tiny bag. He lined up tools on the counter. They gleamed in the light. “We will use simple steps and simple words. Watch and say them with me.”

“Okay,” Milo sighed, his ears drooping.

Step one, said Squeaky, “Rinse.” He turned the water on. Whoosh! Warm water made a soft hiss. He splashed a little on Milo’s paws. “Rinse,” he said slowly.

“Rinse,” Milo repeated, tail swishing. The mud slid away like chocolate melting.

Step two, said Squeaky, “Soap.” He pumped a dab of lemon soap. Plop! He rubbed it into bubbles. “Soap.”

“So-ap,” Milo said carefully. The bubbles tickled. Pop! Pop! Pop! He giggled. “It feels like tiny kisses!”

Step three, said Squeaky, “Scrub.” He scrubbed in little circles. Swish, swish. “Scrub.”

“Scrub,” Milo chirped, copying the circles with his paws. The bathroom smelled clean and bright.

Step four, said Squeaky, “Rinse again.” Whoosh! The water rinsed the suds away. Milo watched the foam spin down the drain like a ribbon. “Rinse,” he said, smiling now.

Step five, said Squeaky, “Dry.” He handed Milo a fluffy towel. “Dry,” they said together. Rub, rub. The towel was warm and soft, like a hug.

Milo looked at his paws. They were shiny and clean. “I did it,” he yipped. He wagged so hard his tag jingled. “I feel fresh. I smell like lemons!”

Squeaky nodded. “Clean and ready is fast and steady,” he said, pleased. He packed up the tools. Click, click, zip.

Then Squeaky noticed the muddy spots on the floor and the bathmat. His whiskers drooped. “Oh dear,” he said softly. “The bathroom is still messy.”

Milo’s ears went flat. “I made the mess,” he whispered. “I was in a hurry. I’m sorry, Squeaky.”

Squeaky’s heart felt warm. He put a tiny paw on Milo’s nose. “I forgive you,” he said kindly. “Thank you for saying sorry. Now we can fix it together.”

They prepared again. Squeaky made a plan. “We will use our words and our steps,” he said. “Wipe. Rinse. Dry.” He pointed to each spot.

“Wipe,” Milo repeated, taking a cloth. Swipe, swipe went the cloth. The muddy stars disappeared.

“Rinse,” Squeaky said, dipping the cloth. Swirl, swirl in the water.

“Dry,” Milo chimed, pressing the towel down. Pat, pat, pat.

Soon the bathroom sparkled. The mirror shined. The floor felt smooth and clean. The air smelled like lemon and a little like wet puppy.

Milo wagged. “We were a team,” he said proudly.

Squeaky smiled. “Yes. We prepared. We cleaned. We forgave. And we learned new words.”

Milo practiced them again. “Rinse. Soap. Scrub. Rinse. Dry. Wipe.” He liked how the words made a rhythm. “It’s like a song!”

Squeaky tapped his tail like a drum. “Rinse, soap, scrub, rinse, dry,” he sang. Milo howled a happy note. The faucet dripped in time: drip, drip, drip.

The door opened, and a little girl peeked in. She saw the sparkling bathroom and the shiny puppy. “Wow,” she said softly. “So clean!” She stroked Milo’s head. “Good job, friends.”

Milo stood tall. “Clean and ready!” he yipped.

Squeaky waved his tiny comb like a flag. “Prepared means proud!” he added.

They shared a quick hug. The problem was solved. The bathroom was beautiful. Milo was set for the park, and Squeaky felt calm and ready for the day.

Lesson: Good hygiene keeps us healthy and happy. Being prepared makes cleaning quick and easy. Saying “sorry” and “I forgive you” helps hearts feel light. And using simple words helps us learn and share.

The End.
