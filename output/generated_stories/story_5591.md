# Bunny Lanterns in the Guest Room

Bibi was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and big, brave ears. Today, Bibi had a plan. “I want to share my family’s Lantern Night,” Bibi said, patting a little box of colorful paper. “I can show our friends in the guest room!”

The guest room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. A soft rug tickled Bibi’s paws. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Bibi set the box on a low table and took a deep breath. “I can do this,” Bibi whispered.

Friends came to visit. Tiko the squirrel, Lulu the duckling, and Miro the mouse scampered in. “Hi, Bibi!” they said together.

“Welcome,” said Bibi. “Today I want to share my family’s tradition. It is called Lantern Night. We make paper lanterns, tell kind wishes, and sit together with safe candles.”

Tiko’s eyes got big. “Lanterns? I like lights!”

Lulu flapped a little. “I like wishes!”

Miro sniffed the lemon smell. “I like sitting together!”

Bibi smiled and opened the box. Inside were sheets of red, yellow, and blue paper, safe battery tea lights, blunt child scissors, and a small roll of tape. “We will be safe,” Bibi said carefully. “We use only these little pretend lights. No real fire. We sit at the table and take turns with scissors.”

“Okay!” said the friends.

Bibi showed the first step. “Fold the paper. Press, press.” The paper made a soft crinkle sound. “Now cut little lines. Snip, snip. Not too close to the edge.” Bibi spoke slow and clear.

Tiko grabbed the scissors fast. “I go first!” he said, reaching across the table.

Lulu puffed. “No, me first! I want a blue lantern!”

Miro squeaked and pulled the tape. “I need tape now!”

Voices got loud. The table wobbled. A tea light rolled to the edge.

Bibi felt her ears go hot. “Oh no,” she thought. “This is not our way.” Bibi put a paw on her chest and took a big slow breath, just like Mama Rabbit taught. In… and out… Bibi remembered: Lantern Night means we share and listen.

“Friends,” Bibi said in a calm voice, “In my family’s tradition, we have gentle hands and gentle words. We take turns. We keep safe.”

Tiko stopped. Lulu blinked. Miro held still. The room got quiet, like soft feathers.

Bibi nodded. “Let’s fix this together. First, we make a turn list.” Bibi held up three small sticky notes. “Watch me write your names: Tiko, Lulu, Miro. We will go in that order. I will help each friend.”

Tiko’s tail twitched. “But I want to go now.”

“You will go first,” Bibi said, smiling. “Right after we sit with steady paws.”

“Okay,” Tiko said, sitting down.

“Next, we make a safe space,” Bibi said. She set the tea lights in the middle, far from the edge. “Lights stay in the center. Scissors point down when we pass them. We keep our paws away from faces.”

Lulu nodded. “Safe is smart.”

“Third, we share a wish when we are done,” Bibi said. “In my family, we share wishes for kindness.”

Miro’s whiskers wiggled. “I like wishes.”

They began. Tiko folded his red paper. The crinkle sounded like crunchy leaves. Bibi guided his paws. “Snip, snip,” Tiko said, cutting careful lines. “I did it!”

Lulu went next with blue paper. “Press, press,” she whispered. She giggled when the paper made a soft whoosh. “Snip, snip.” Bibi helped her tape the ends. The lantern looked like a blue smile.

Miro chose yellow. He cut slow and steady. “Snip… snip.” The room smelled like paper and lemon soap. The tea lights glowed softly in the middle.

Now it was Bibi’s turn. She chose red and blue together. “This is our friendship lantern,” Bibi said, taping the pieces as her friends watched. “We made these together.”

They set the lanterns over the little tea lights. The room filled with gentle, warm glows—gold, ocean blue, and sunset red. The soft lights made the rug look like a tiny star field.

“Now we share wishes,” Bibi said. “In my family’s tradition, we hold our lantern close and speak kind words.”

Tiko held his red lantern and said, “I wish for calm turns and sharing snacks.”

Lulu held her blue lantern and said, “I wish for gentle words and warm hugs.”

Miro held his yellow lantern and said, “I wish for safe paws and happy friends.”

Bibi held the friendship lantern and said, “I wish to share traditions and learn yours too.”

Tiko perked up. “I have a nut-dance tradition!”

Lulu quacked softly. “We have a feather song!”

Miro smiled. “We make tiny cheese stars!”

Bibi clapped. “We can take turns sharing traditions each week. We will listen, be safe, and be kind.”

They all nodded. “Yes!”

Bibi looked around the guest room. It felt cozy and bright. The air was warm with light and love. The friends had learned to take turns. They had learned to keep the table safe. They had learned that sharing a tradition means sharing hearts.

“Thank you for teaching us,” said Lulu.

“Thank you for listening,” said Bibi. “Sharing makes us strong.”

They sat on the soft rug and watched the lanterns glow. The lights were small, but the feeling was big. Their voices were quiet, their smiles were wide, and their hearts were light.

Lesson: Sharing traditions helps us learn about each other. We use gentle words, we take turns, and we stay safe.

The End.
