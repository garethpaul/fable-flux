# Rosie Rabbit's Messy Room Adventure

Rosie Rabbit loved her cozy bedroom with its soft pink walls and fluffy white carpet. But there was one big problem - her room was very, very messy!

Toys were scattered everywhere like colorful confetti. Her art supplies rolled under the bed. Books lay open on the floor with their pages fluttering in the breeze from the window. Even her favorite stuffed elephant, Mr. Peanuts, was buried somewhere in the pile of clothes.

"Oh my whiskers!" said Rosie, twitching her nose as she looked around. "I can't find anything in here!"

Just then, her little brother Benny Bunny hopped into the room. "Rosie, can I play with you?" he asked with hopeful eyes.

"I'm sorry, Benny," Rosie sighed. "My room is too messy. There's nowhere for us to play together."

Benny's ears drooped sadly. "Maybe I can help you clean up?"

Rosie's eyes lit up like sunshine. "What a wonderful idea! Two rabbits are better than one!"

First, Rosie looked around thoughtfully. "We need a plan," she said, tapping her fuzzy chin. "Let's sort everything into groups!"

The two rabbits started their organizing adventure. They made it into a fun game!

"All the books go here!" called Rosie, pointing to the bookshelf. The books felt smooth and cool in her paws as she arranged them by size - big ones on the bottom, small ones on top.

"I found your crayons!" squeaked Benny, gathering the waxy, colorful sticks. They smelled like happiness and creativity. Together, they put all the art supplies in a special box with rainbow stickers.

"Now for the toys!" said Rosie. They sorted the toys by type - soft stuffed animals in one basket, building blocks in another, and puzzles on the shelf. Each toy found its perfect home.

As they worked, Rosie discovered something magical. "Benny, this is actually fun! And look - we can see the pretty carpet again!"

The fluffy white carpet felt wonderful under their paws, like walking on a cloud.

Finally, they found Mr. Peanuts hiding under a pile of socks. "There you are!" laughed Rosie, hugging her beloved elephant tight.

When they finished, the room sparkled like a jewel. Everything had its own special place. The books stood proudly on their shelf. The art supplies waited patiently in their rainbow box. The toys sat neatly in their baskets, ready for playtime.

"Now we have room to play together!" said Rosie, doing a happy bunny hop.

Benny clapped his paws with joy. "And it looks so beautiful!"

The two rabbits spread out a puzzle on the clean carpet. The afternoon sun streamed through the window, making everything glow warmly. They could hear birds chirping outside and smell the fresh flowers from the garden.

"You know what, Benny?" said Rosie as they worked on the puzzle together. "Keeping things organized means we have more time to play and create together. And everything is so much easier to find!"

"Plus, it feels good to work as a team!" added Benny with a big smile.

From that day on, Rosie made sure to put everything back in its special place. Her room stayed cozy and organized, and there was always room for friends and family to join in the fun.

The End.
