# Benny Beaver's Big Bathroom Project

Benny Beaver loved to build things. He built dams with sticks, towers with blocks, and bridges with logs. Today, he wanted to build something special in his bathroom - a soap dispenser made from recycled bottles!

Benny gathered his materials: empty shampoo bottles, colorful tape, and a small pump. He spread everything out on the bathroom floor, his big flat tail tapping excitedly on the cool tiles.

"This will be the BEST soap dispenser ever!" Benny said, his brown eyes sparkling.

But when Benny tried to cut the first bottle, it slipped from his wet paws. CRASH! The bottle rolled into the bathtub, making a loud echoing sound.

"Grrrr!" Benny's face turned red. His little paws clenched into fists.

He tried again, but the tape wouldn't stick to the slippery bottle. It kept peeling off and sticking to his fur instead.

"This is SO HARD!" Benny shouted, his voice bouncing off the bathroom walls. He felt hot and frustrated, like steam rising from a warm bath.

Just then, his friend Rosie Rabbit hopped into the bathroom. Her soft pink nose twitched as she smelled Benny's anger.

"Benny, you look upset," Rosie said gently, her voice as soft as bubbles. "What's wrong?"

"Nothing's working!" Benny huffed, his chest going up and down quickly. "I wanted to make something beautiful to share with everyone, but it's all going wrong!"

Rosie sat down next to Benny on the fluffy bath mat. "When I feel angry like that, I do something special. Want to try it?"

Benny nodded, his ears drooping.

"Let's take three deep breaths together," Rosie said. "Breathe in slowly... smell the lavender soap... and breathe out like you're blowing bubbles."

Benny breathed in deeply. The bathroom smelled clean and fresh, like rain on leaves. He breathed out slowly, imagining colorful bubbles floating away with his anger.

"One more time," Rosie encouraged. "In... and out..."

Benny felt his shoulders relax. The hot feeling in his chest cooled down like stepping out of a warm bath.

"Better?" Rosie asked.

"Much better," Benny smiled. "But I still don't know how to fix my project."

"Maybe I can help," Rosie offered. "Two friends working together can solve any problem!"

Rosie held the bottles steady while Benny cut them. She helped dry them with a soft towel so the tape would stick. Together, they decorated the dispensers with colorful tape that looked like rainbow stripes.

"Look!" Benny exclaimed, holding up their creation. "It's even better than I imagined!"

The soap dispenser was beautiful - green and blue like a pond, with a pump that worked perfectly. They filled it with gentle, earth-friendly soap that wouldn't hurt the environment.

"Should we make more to share with our neighbors?" Rosie suggested.

"Yes!" Benny's tail slapped the floor happily. "Everyone should have nice soap dispensers made from recycled bottles!"

They spent the afternoon making dispensers for all their friends, turning trash into treasures.

That evening, as Benny washed his paws with his new dispenser, he felt proud and calm.

"Thank you, Rosie," he said. "You taught me that when I feel angry, I can breathe slowly and ask friends for help."

"And you taught me how to build amazing things while helping our planet!" Rosie replied.

From that day on, whenever Benny felt frustrated, he remembered to stop, breathe deeply, and reach out to friends. Together, they could build anything!

The End.
