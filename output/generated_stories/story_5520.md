# Pippin's Bubble Bath Adventure

Pippin the penguin loved bath time! His orange beak would wiggle with excitement every evening when Mama called, "Bath time, Pippin!"

Tonight was special. Mama had given him new bubble soap that smelled like sweet strawberries. Pippin waddled into the warm bathroom, his black and white feathers already ruffling with joy.

"I'm going to make the BIGGEST bubble mountain ever!" Pippin announced, squeezing the pink bottle. Squeeze, squeeze, squeeze! He poured and poured until the bottle was nearly empty.

But something was wrong. Where were all the bubbles? The water stayed flat and boring. Pippin's happy flippers drooped.

"This is so frustrating!" Pippin huffed, splashing the water with his wing. "Why won't it work? I want bubbles NOW!"

His little penguin heart felt hot and angry. He wanted to throw the bottle across the bathroom!

"Take a deep breath, little one," said Mama penguin gently from the doorway. "Remember what we do when we feel frustrated?"

Pippin closed his eyes and took three slow breaths. "In... and out... in... and out..." The angry feeling in his chest got a little smaller.

"Good job! Now, what do you think might help?" Mama asked.

Pippin looked at the still water. Then he had an idea! "Maybe I need to mix it up?" He began stirring the water with his flipper, round and round, making little whirlpools.

Still no bubbles.

"Hmm," Pippin said, not getting upset this time. "What else could I try?"

He remembered watching Mama make bubbles before. She always turned on the water faucet WHILE adding the soap. 

"Oh! I need running water!" Pippin exclaimed. He turned the faucet handle and added just a tiny bit more soap. The water splashed and danced, and suddenly... POP! POP! POP!

Beautiful, fluffy bubbles began appearing everywhere! They sparkled like tiny rainbows in the bathroom light.

"I did it! I figured it out!" Pippin giggled, clapping his flippers together. The bubbles tickled his belly and made him laugh even more.

He scooped up handfuls of bubbles and pretended they were snow clouds. "I'm flying through the sky!" he chirped, blowing bubbles off his wing. Some landed on his beak, making him cross-eyed as he tried to see them.

"You know what, Pippin?" Mama said, sitting on the edge of the tub. "When you felt frustrated, you did something very grown-up."

"I did?" Pippin asked, a bubble beard covering his orange beak.

"Yes! Instead of staying angry, you took deep breaths, thought about the problem, and tried different solutions. That's what smart penguins do!"

Pippin beamed with pride. The warm, bubbly water felt wonderful on his feathers, and his heart felt light and happy again.

"And you know what else?" Mama continued. "Sometimes the best things take a little practice and patience."

"Like learning to slide on ice!" Pippin remembered.

"Exactly! And now you know how to make perfect bubbles."

As Pippin played with his mountain of bubbles, he felt proud and peaceful. When something didn't work the first time, he didn't have to get upset. He could breathe, think, and try again.

"Tomorrow I'll teach Papa how to make bubble mountains too!" Pippin said, creating a bubble hat on his head.

And from that day on, whenever Pippin felt frustrated, he remembered his bubble bath adventure and knew exactly what to do.

The End.
