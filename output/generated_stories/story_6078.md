# Foxy's Colorful Day of Sharing

Little Foxy was a curious fox kit with bright orange fur and the biggest, most sparkly eyes in the whole forest. Today was extra special because it was her first day visiting the creative art room at the community center!

"Wow!" gasped Foxy as she stepped through the colorful door. The art room smelled like crayons and paint, and sunshine streamed through big windows. Rainbow paintings covered the walls, and tables were filled with brushes, papers, and jars of every color imaginable.

"Welcome, Foxy!" said Ms. Rabbit, the art teacher. Her apron was splattered with purple and yellow paint. "We're making friendship cards today. You can sit anywhere you'd like!"

Foxy's fluffy tail wiggled with excitement as she looked around. She spotted a table with the most beautiful art supplies - shiny markers, glittery stickers, and smooth, white paper that felt like clouds.

But as Foxy sat down, she noticed something that made her ears droop. At the table next to her, a little bear cub named Bruno was trying to draw with a broken crayon. His paper was wrinkled, and he only had two colors - brown and gray.

"My picture doesn't look very pretty," Bruno whispered sadly, holding up his card. "I wanted to make something colorful for my grandma."

Foxy looked at her pile of perfect supplies, then at Bruno's sad face. Her heart felt warm and tingly - the same feeling she got when Mama Fox gave her extra honey with her breakfast.

"Hmm," thought Foxy. "What should I do?"

She remembered what Papa Fox always said: "The best way to make your heart happy is to make someone else's heart happy too."

Foxy made her decision. She picked up half of her beautiful markers and walked over to Bruno's table.

"Hi Bruno!" she said with a bright smile. "Would you like to share my markers? We could make our cards together!"

Bruno's eyes grew wide and sparkly. "Really? You would share with me?"

"Of course!" Foxy giggled. "Sharing makes everything more fun! Plus, I bet you know some colors in different languages. My grandpa taught me that 'red' is 'rojo' in Spanish!"

Bruno clapped his paws together. "Yes! My mom taught me that 'blue' is 'bleu' in French!"

The two new friends spent the morning creating the most wonderful cards. Bruno drew a beautiful blue sky using Foxy's markers, while Foxy made red roses with Bruno's brown crayon for the stems. They taught each other color words in different languages and laughed as they painted.

"Look!" said Bruno, holding up his finished card. It was covered in rainbows, hearts, and the words "I love you Grandma" written in three different languages.

"It's beautiful!" said Foxy. "Your grandma will love it so much!"

Ms. Rabbit hopped over to see their work. "What wonderful cards! And Foxy, that was very kind of you to share your supplies. Kindness makes everything more beautiful."

As they cleaned up together, Bruno whispered to Foxy, "Thank you for being so kind. You made my day special."

Foxy's heart felt warm and full, like a cozy blanket on a cold day. She learned that when you choose to be kind and share what you have, you don't lose anything - you gain a friend and a happy heart.

From that day on, Foxy and Bruno were the very best of friends, and they always remembered to share and be kind to others in the art room.

The End.
