# Ruby Rabbit's Honest Garden Plan

Ruby Rabbit lived in a cozy burrow beneath Mrs. Chen's balcony garden. Every morning, she would hop up through a small hole in the wooden deck to smell the beautiful flowers and watch the colorful butterflies dance around the tomato plants.

One sunny Tuesday, Ruby noticed something wonderful. Mrs. Chen had left several empty yogurt containers, plastic bottles, and old newspapers near the garden shed. Ruby's whiskers twitched with excitement as a brilliant idea popped into her head.

"I could make the most amazing garden decorations!" Ruby squeaked to herself. "But wait..." She paused, her fluffy tail drooping slightly. "These things belong to Mrs. Chen. I should ask first."

Ruby's friend Squeaky the sparrow landed on the railing nearby. "What's wrong, Ruby? You look worried."

"I want to use these containers to make planters for my own little garden corner," Ruby explained, pointing with her pink nose. "But I know I should ask Mrs. Chen first. What if she says no?"

Squeaky chirped thoughtfully. "Well, what if she says yes? You won't know unless you're honest and ask!"

Ruby took a deep breath. The sweet smell of basil and mint filled her nose, giving her courage. She hopped over to the sliding door and gently tapped with her paw.

Mrs. Chen opened the door, surprised to see the little rabbit. "Oh my! Hello there, little one."

Ruby's heart beat fast like a tiny drum. "Mrs. Chen," she said in her sweetest voice, "I was wondering... could I please use some of your empty containers? I want to make a small recycled garden, but I know they belong to you."

Mrs. Chen's face lit up with a warm smile. "What a polite and honest little rabbit you are! Of course you may use them. I was actually planning to recycle them anyway."

"Really?" Ruby's ears perked up high with joy.

"Yes! And I'll help you plan your garden. We can drill holes in the containers for drainage and paint them with bright colors."

Ruby hopped in happy circles. "Thank you, thank you! I promise to take very good care of them!"

Together, they spent the afternoon planning. Mrs. Chen helped Ruby choose which containers would work best for different plants. They drew pictures of how the garden would look, with the yogurt containers holding herbs and the plastic bottles turned into hanging planters for cherry tomatoes.

"You know what, Ruby?" Mrs. Chen said as they worked. "Because you were honest and asked permission, this project is much more fun. We can work together and make it even better than you imagined!"

Ruby's nose twitched with happiness. The afternoon sun felt warm on her fur, and the sound of bees buzzing around the flowers made everything feel perfect.

As the sun began to set, painting the sky orange and pink, Ruby looked at their plan. "Mrs. Chen, I'm so glad I told the truth and asked first. Now we're friends, and my garden will be even more special!"

"Honesty always leads to the best adventures," Mrs. Chen agreed, gently patting Ruby's soft head.

From that day on, Ruby and Mrs. Chen worked together every week, recycling containers and growing the most beautiful balcony garden in the whole neighborhood. And Ruby learned that being honest and asking permission always led to better outcomes than taking things without asking.

The End.
