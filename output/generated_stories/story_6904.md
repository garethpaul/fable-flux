# Ollie's Big Attic Adventure

Ollie the otter loved to explore. His sleek brown fur was always clean, and his whiskers twitched when he was excited. Today, he climbed the creaky wooden stairs to the quiet attic above his family's cozy den.

"Wow!" Ollie whispered. Dust particles danced in the golden sunlight streaming through a small window. Old boxes sat everywhere, and cobwebs hung like lace curtains in the corners.

Ollie's nose twitched. He smelled something sweet and familiar. "Mmm, that smells like Mama's fish cookies!" he said, following his nose deeper into the attic.

Behind a tall stack of boxes, Ollie found a small tin container. His tummy rumbled. The tin was exactly like the one Mama used for her special treats.

"Maybe just one cookie," Ollie thought. But when he tried to open the tin, it was stuck tight. He pulled and pulled with his little paws, but it wouldn't budge.

Ollie felt frustrated. His whiskers drooped. "I could ask Papa for help," he said to himself. "But then he might ask whose cookies these are."

Suddenly, Ollie remembered something important. Last week, his big sister Olive had been saving her birthday cookies in a special tin. "Oh no," Ollie gasped. "These might be Olive's cookies!"

Ollie's heart felt heavy. He really wanted a cookie, but taking something that wasn't his would be wrong. Even if no one would know, Ollie would know.

"I need to think about this," Ollie said aloud. He sat down on a soft, dusty cushion and put his paw on his chin, just like Mama did when she was thinking hard.

"What would make me feel good inside?" Ollie asked himself. "Taking cookies that aren't mine, or finding the right thing to do?"

Ollie's whiskers perked up. He had an idea! He carefully carried the tin downstairs, his little feet patting softly on each wooden step.

"Olive!" Ollie called. "I found something in the attic!"

Olive swam over quickly, her eyes wide with surprise. "My birthday cookies! I forgot where I put them!" She hugged the tin tightly. "Thank you for bringing them to me, Ollie!"

"I smelled them and thought they might be yours," Ollie explained honestly. "I wanted one really badly, but I knew I should ask first."

Olive's face lit up with a big smile. "You know what? Since you were so honest and kind, would you like to share some cookies with me?"

"Really?" Ollie's tail wiggled with excitement.

"Really!" Olive opened the tin with a satisfying *pop*. The sweet smell of fish cookies filled the air. "Honest otters deserve the best cookies!"

They sat together by the sunny window, munching on the delicious treats. The cookies tasted even better because Ollie had done the right thing.

"I'm proud of you for thinking through your problem," Olive said, giving Ollie a gentle pat on the head. "You could have just taken a cookie, but you chose to be honest instead."

Ollie felt warm and happy inside. His tummy was full of yummy cookies, but his heart was even fuller. He had solved his problem by being truthful and thinking about what was right.

"Next time I have a problem, I'll remember to stop and think," Ollie said with a smile. "And I'll always try to do what's right, even when it's hard."

From that day on, whenever Ollie faced a tricky situation, he would sit quietly, put his paw on his chin, and ask himself: "What choice will make me feel proud inside?"

The End.
