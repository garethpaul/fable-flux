# Rosie Rabbit's Big Garden Project

Rosie Rabbit hopped into the old garden shed. The wooden walls smelled like earth and flowers. Sunlight peeked through dusty windows, making dancing spots on the floor.

"Today I'm going to plant the most beautiful garden ever!" Rosie announced. Her fluffy tail wiggled with excitement.

The shed was full of gardening tools. There were shovels, watering cans, and colorful seed packets scattered everywhere. A big poster on the wall showed pictures of how to plant seeds properly.

Rosie's eyes sparkled as she grabbed a handful of carrot seeds. "I don't need to read those silly directions," she said. "I'm smart enough to figure it out myself!"

She hopped outside and started digging holes everywhere. Dig, dig, dig! She scattered seeds here and there, not paying attention to how deep or far apart they should be.

"This is easy!" Rosie giggled, her paws getting muddy.

But when Grandpa Bunny came to visit the next week, Rosie's garden looked very sad. Some seeds hadn't grown at all. Others were crowded together in tiny, weak sprouts.

"Oh my," said Grandpa Bunny gently. "What happened here, little one?"

Rosie's ears drooped. "I don't understand. I planted so many seeds, but nothing grew right."

Grandpa Bunny's whiskers twitched kindly. "Did you follow the planting directions, Rosie?"

"Well... no," Rosie admitted, her voice getting quieter. "I thought I could do it by myself."

"Let's go look at those directions together," Grandpa suggested.

Back in the garden shed, they studied the colorful poster. It showed exactly how deep to plant each type of seed, how far apart they should be, and when to water them.

"See here?" Grandpa pointed with his paw. "Carrot seeds need to be planted just this deep, and this far apart. That gives them room to grow big and strong."

Rosie nodded, feeling a bit embarrassed. "I should have read this first."

"Following directions isn't about not being smart," Grandpa explained. "It's about learning from others who have done this before. They want to help you succeed!"

The next morning, Rosie woke up early. She hopped to the shed and carefully read every direction on the poster. She organized her tools neatly: small shovel here, watering can there, seeds lined up by size.

"First, I'll measure the right distance," she said to herself, using a ruler to mark spots in the soil.

She dug each hole exactly the right depth. Her paws moved slowly and carefully. When she planted each seed, she whispered, "Grow strong, little seed!"

After planting, Rosie made a schedule. "Water every other day," she read aloud. "Check for sprouts in one week."

Day by day, Rosie followed her schedule. She watered gently, just like the directions said. She even made a chart to mark off each day.

Two weeks later, tiny green shoots poked through the soil! Rosie clapped her paws with joy.

"Look, Grandpa!" she called when he visited. "My garden is growing!"

Beautiful rows of carrots, lettuce, and radishes grew in perfect lines. Each plant had plenty of space to grow big and healthy.

"I'm so proud of you, Rosie," Grandpa Bunny smiled. "You learned that following directions and being organized helps us do our best work."

Rosie beamed. "And taking time to do things right makes them turn out even better than I imagined!"

From that day on, whenever Rosie started a new project, she always read the directions first. She learned that following instructions wasn't about not being smart – it was about being smart enough to learn from others and do things the best way possible.

The End.
