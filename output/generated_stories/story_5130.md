# Milo's Musical Discovery

Milo the monkey sat at the big dining room table. His fuzzy brown tail swished back and forth. The morning sun made pretty patterns on the wooden floor. But Milo felt grumpy.

"I don't want to eat breakfast," Milo said with a frown. He pushed his banana around his plate. The yellow fruit made a soft scraping sound on the dish.

Mama Monkey sighed. "Milo, you need to eat to grow big and strong."

Milo crossed his little arms. "Breakfast is boring!" he declared. His voice echoed in the big dining room.

Just then, Milo heard something magical. *Tap tap tap.* It came from the kitchen. *Clink clink clink.* The sounds had a rhythm, like music!

Milo's ears perked up. His curiosity made him brave. He climbed down from his chair and tiptoed toward the kitchen door. The sounds got louder and more interesting.

*Tap tap, clink clink, whoosh whoosh.*

"What's that beautiful music?" Milo whispered to himself.

He peeked around the corner. There was Mrs. Robin, the neighbor bird, washing dishes at the sink. But she wasn't just washing – she was making music!

Mrs. Robin tapped spoons against bowls. She clinked plates together gently. She swished water in a special rhythm. Her feathers sparkled as she moved.

"Oh!" Milo gasped. "You're making music with dishes!"

Mrs. Robin turned around with a warm smile. "Hello, little Milo! Yes, I love making kitchen music. It makes washing dishes so much fun!"

Milo's eyes grew wide with wonder. "Can you teach me?"

"Of course!" Mrs. Robin chirped happily. "Music is for everyone to share."

Mrs. Robin showed Milo how to tap gently on different bowls. Each bowl made a different sound – some high, some low. The big bowl went *BONG* and the little bowl went *ting*.

"Listen carefully," Mrs. Robin said. "Music is about listening with your heart."

Milo tried tapping the bowls. At first, it sounded messy. But Mrs. Robin was patient and kind. She showed him how to make steady beats.

"Try this pattern," she said gently. "*Tap tap rest, tap tap rest.*"

Milo concentrated hard. His little monkey hands learned the rhythm. Soon, beautiful music filled the dining room!

"I'm making music!" Milo laughed with joy. His grumpiness melted away like ice cream in the sun.

Mrs. Robin smiled. "Music makes everything better, doesn't it? Even breakfast time!"

Milo gasped. He had forgotten all about his breakfast! But now he had an idea.

"Can I make music while I eat?" Milo asked excitedly.

"What a wonderful idea!" Mrs. Robin said.

Milo ran back to the dining room table. He tapped his spoon gently against his plate. *Tap tap tap.* Then he took a bite of banana. The fruit tasted sweeter when he ate it to a rhythm!

Mama Monkey came back and saw Milo eating happily. "My goodness! What changed?"

"I learned about music!" Milo said with his mouth full of banana. "Mrs. Robin taught me that music makes everything more fun!"

Mrs. Robin flew into the dining room. "Milo was very brave to try something new. And he listened with kindness."

From that day on, Milo made music at every meal. He shared his musical discovery with all his friends. The dining room became filled with joyful sounds and laughter.

Milo learned that music brings happiness to ordinary moments. And when we share what we learn with others, the joy grows even bigger!

The End.
