# Bubbles the Otter Takes a Calm Breath

Bubbles was a playful otter who loved to splash in puddles and draw swirly pictures. He lived with his family above a basement lab. The lab was a bright, busy place with soft humming machines, shiny buttons, and the gentle drip-drip of a sink. The floor felt cool and smooth under Bubbles’s paws. The air smelled like clean soap and oranges.

“Today I will make rainbow foam art!” Bubbles squeaked, his whiskers twitching with joy.

Mama Otter put on a little apron for Bubbles. “Sweet pea,” she said softly, “remember our calm plan. We use slow breaths, slow paws, and slow words.”

Bubbles nodded fast. “Slow, got it!”

On a wide table, he set out cups with bubbly soap, safe food colors, and big white paper. He gently tapped the cups. Tap, tap. He squeezed a tiny drop of blue. Plip! The blue swirled. He added pink. Plip! The colors made soft clouds that smelled like berries.

Bubbles giggled. “It’s like a fizzy rainbow!”

He dipped a straw and blew. Fff-ff-ff! Piles of sweet-smelling foam grew like mountains, soft and tickly on his paws. The bubbles made little crackle sounds, pop-pop-pop, like tiny kisses.

He pressed his paper down and lifted it up. “Ta-da!” Swirly circles spread over the page. It looked like a soft sunset.

Then Bubbles saw it—on the shelf, high and shiny—Mama’s big mixing jar with a silver lid. It had a picture of a star on it. It looked perfect for giant bubbles.

“I want that jar,” Bubbles whispered, eyes wide. He reached up on his tippy paws. Stretch, stretch. The jar wobbled. Wiggle-wobble. Bubbles’s paw slipped. The jar slid off the shelf and fell—CLINK—into a tray of water! Cold drops splashed his nose. The foam mountain toppled and smeared his picture. His paper tore with a rip.

Bubbles’s belly felt hot. His cheeks puffed. His paws balled into tight, tight fists. “My picture!” he shouted. “It’s ruined! I want to stomp!”

His tail thumped. Thump. Thump. The room felt loud and prickly. The gentle hum of the machines sounded like buzz-buzz-buzz in his ears. A tear rolled down his fur.

Mama Otter stepped close, her voice soft like a warm blanket. “I see a big feeling,” she said. She crouched to his level. “Is it mad?”

Bubbles sniffed. “Mad and sad,” he said, his voice wobbly. “My art is messy now.”

Mama nodded. “Mad and sad are okay. Your feelings are welcome here. Let’s use our calm plan.”

Bubbles remembered. Slow breaths. Slow paws. Slow words.

Mama touched her paw to his paw. “Smell the flower,” she whispered. “Blow the bubble.”

Bubbles closed his eyes. He breathed in through his nose—sniff, sniff—like smelling a sweet flower. He breathed out through his mouth—whooo—like blowing a round bubble. He did it again. Smell the flower. Blow the bubble.

His shoulders dropped. The buzz-buzz got quiet. He felt his belly soften. The lab did not feel prickly now. The air was still orange-clean and safe.

“Would you like a squeeze?” Mama asked.

Bubbles nodded. They shared a gentle hug. His tail stopped thumping.

Mama pointed to a small poster on the wall. It had three pictures: a turtle, a cloud, and a heart. “Step one,” she said, pointing to the turtle, “go slow like a turtle. Step two,” she pointed to the cloud, “breathe like a cloud. Step three,” she pointed to the heart, “choose a kind next step.”

Bubbles looked at his torn paper. He touched the soft foam. He listened to the drip-drip and the purr of the tiny fan. “My kind next step is… to clean a little,” he said, “and then try again.”

Mama smiled. “That’s patience,” she said. “Patience means waiting and trying in a calm way.”

They wiped the table with warm, soapy cloths. Swish, swish. The water sparkled. Bubbles’s paws felt busy and helpful. He hummed a little song: “Slow paws, slow words, slow breaths.”

He set out new paper. He chose smaller cups this time. He did not reach for the high jar. “Not safe to grab,” he said. “I can ask.”

“May I please use a safe big cup?” he asked.

“Yes,” Mama said, handing him a sturdy plastic bowl. “Great asking.”

Bubbles added colors again. Plip, plip. He blew gently. Fff. The foam grew round and puffy like soft clouds. He pressed the paper and lifted it. This time, the swirls were bigger, brighter, and even more beautiful. The picture looked like dancing waves.

“I did it,” Bubbles whispered, his eyes shiny with happy. He pressed his paw to his chest. His heart felt warm. His anger had been a big wave, but he had floated on it.

Mama clapped softly. “You used your calm plan. You were patient. You chose safe, kind steps. That is wellness—taking care of your body, your feelings, and your choices.”

Bubbles looked at the shelf. “If I want something up high,” he said, “I will ask for help.”

“And if a picture tears?” Mama asked.

“I can take a breath, clean up, and try again,” Bubbles said proudly.

They hung the new art to dry. It dripped tiny stars of color. Pop-pop-pop went the last bubbles. The lab felt cozy and bright again.

Bubbles wiggled his whiskers. “Slow breaths, slow paws, slow words,” he sang. “That’s my calm plan.”

He touched noses with Mama. “I feel proud,” he said.

“And calm,” Mama added.

Bubbles smiled. “And ready to make more art—one gentle bubble at a time.”

The End.
