# Kiki Koala Tries New Things

In a comfortable nursery with soft rugs and sunny curtains, Kiki the calm koala woke up from a nap. She stretched her fuzzy arms. She smelled clean soap and warm milk. She heard gentle music go “la-la-la” from a little radio. The room felt safe, like a cozy hug.

“Good morning, Kiki,” said Teacher Mia with a smile. “Today we will try new activities.”

Kiki blinked her big brown eyes. “New?” she asked softly.

“Yes,” Teacher Mia said. “We will paint with big brushes. We will sing a song with hand motions. And we will build a block tower together.”

Kiki held her soft leaf-shaped blanket. She liked her blanket. She liked quiet. She liked when things stayed the same. Her tummy felt a tiny flutter.

Benny Bear bounced over. “Let’s paint a rainbow!” he roared happily, but not too loud because he remembered inside voices.

Pip Penguin waddled close. “And we can splash the water cup,” Pip said, flapping her little wings.

Kiki took a slow breath in. She let a slow breath out. “I like my quiet corner,” she whispered. “I am not ready yet.”

Teacher Mia nodded. “That’s okay, Kiki. We accept our friends, even when they need time. You can watch first.”

Kiki sat on a soft beanbag. The room smelled like sweet apples. The brushes made soft swish-swish sounds on paper. Benny painted a big red arch. Pip dotted blue spots like tiny raindrops.

Kiki watched. Her tummy flutter felt smaller. She could see the colors shine in the light. “Red looks warm,” she said. “Blue looks cool.”

Benny looked up. “Do you want the green brush?” he asked.

Kiki held her blanket. She thought. “I am not ready yet,” she said. “But I like to watch you paint.”

“That’s okay,” said Benny. “We can share the space.”

Teacher Mia smiled. “That is acceptance. We let friends be themselves.”

After painting, Teacher Mia clapped softly. “Now it’s time for the song with hand motions.”

Pip giggled. “Flap-flap! Spin-spin!”

Kiki’s ears wiggled. The music started. It went “tap-tap, clap-clap.” The children moved their hands. Kiki watched their fingers open and close like little flowers.

Kiki whispered, “I feel shy.”

Teacher Mia knelt beside her. “You can join a little bit. Maybe just one clap.”

Kiki took a slow breath in. She let a slow breath out. She lifted her paws. “Clap,” she said, and she clapped once, very softly. It made a tiny sound, like a gentle pat on a pillow.

The music ended. Pip waddled over. “I liked your tiny clap,” she said. “It was soft like a cloud.”

Kiki smiled. Her cheeks felt warm. “Thank you for accepting my tiny clap,” she said.

Next came block time. The blocks clicked together with a happy “tock-tock.” Benny and Pip started to build a tall tower.

“Want to add a block, Kiki?” Benny asked.

Kiki looked at the tall tower. It wobbled a little. Wobble-wobble. Her tummy fluttered again. “What if it falls?” she asked.

“Then we try again,” Pip said. “We use resilience,” Teacher Mia added. “That means we keep going, even when things change.”

Kiki nodded. She took the smallest block. It felt smooth and cool in her paw. She set it gently on top. “Plink.” The tower wobbled. Everyone held their breath.

The tower stood!

Kiki’s eyes grew wide. “I did it,” she whispered.

“You did,” said Teacher Mia. “You were calm and careful.”

Then Benny reached for a big block. The tower wobbled again. Wobble-wobble—crash! The blocks fell with a soft clatter like little wooden rain.

Pip gasped, then giggled. “Oops!”

Kiki looked at the pile. She felt the flutter, but it was smaller now. She took a slow breath in. She let a slow breath out. “It fell,” she said. “That is okay. We can build again.”

Benny smiled. “We accept what happened.”

Pip nodded. “We try again.”

Together they stacked the blocks. Kiki added one block, then two. She hummed the quiet “la-la-la” song. Her paws moved steady and slow. The tower grew as tall as a chair. They all clapped—Kiki clapped twice this time. Clap-clap!

Teacher Mia brought cups of cool water. It tasted fresh. “How do you feel, Kiki?” she asked.

Kiki hugged her blanket and looked around at her friends. The nursery felt even cozier now. The colors were bright. The music was soft. The blocks stood tall.

“I feel calm,” Kiki said. “I tried a tiny clap. I placed small blocks. When the tower fell, we tried again. You accepted me when I needed time. I accept when things change.”

Teacher Mia nodded. “That is a wonderful lesson.”

Benny grinned. “We accept friends.”

Pip chimed in. “We stay calm.”

Kiki added, “We keep trying.”

They touched paws and hands and flippers in the middle. “Together!”

Kiki nibbled a crunchy apple slice. It was sweet and juicy. She felt brave in her quiet way.

She whispered, “I can try new things, little by little. And it’s okay to be me.”

The End.
