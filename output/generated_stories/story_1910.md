# Ruby Rabbit's Science Routine

Ruby Rabbit loved her basement lab. She had test tubes that sparkled like rainbows. She had a microscope that made tiny things look huge. Best of all, she had her special notebook where she wrote down all her discoveries.

Every morning, Ruby hopped down the creaky wooden stairs. The basement smelled like chalk and lemon cleaner. She always did the same things in the same order. First, she put on her tiny white lab coat. Then she organized her tools. Finally, she opened her notebook to plan her experiments.

"Today I will mix colors!" Ruby said to herself. She felt excited and ready.

But this morning was different. Ruby's little sister Rosie came bouncing down the stairs.

"Ruby! Ruby! Can I help? Can I help?" Rosie squeaked. Her pink ears wiggled with excitement.

Ruby's whiskers twitched. She felt frustrated. "No, Rosie. This is MY lab. I have MY routine. You'll mess everything up!"

Rosie's ears drooped. "But I want to learn science too."

"Go away!" Ruby said firmly. She turned back to her test tubes.

Rosie sadly hopped back upstairs. Ruby tried to start her experiment, but something felt wrong. She mixed red and blue paint, but it didn't seem as fun. She looked through her microscope at a flower petal, but it felt lonely.

Ruby sat on her little stool and thought hard. She remembered how happy she felt when she first discovered science. Maybe... maybe Rosie would feel happy too?

"Wait!" Ruby called upstairs. "Rosie! Come back!"

Rosie's fluffy tail appeared first, then her whole body bounced down the stairs. "Really? I can help?"

Ruby nodded. "But we need to follow my routine. Routines help us stay organized and safe in the lab."

"What's a routine?" Rosie asked, tilting her head.

"It's when we do the same helpful things in the same order every time," Ruby explained. "First, we put on lab coats."

Ruby helped Rosie put on a tiny lab coat. It was a bit big, but Rosie giggled with joy.

"Next, we organize our tools," Ruby continued. "Everything has a special place."

The sisters arranged the test tubes by size. They lined up the brushes from biggest to smallest. Rosie was very careful and gentle.

"Now we plan our experiment in the notebook," Ruby said. "What should we try?"

"Rainbow bubbles!" Rosie suggested.

Ruby smiled. "Perfect! We'll mix soap, water, and food coloring."

They worked together step by step. Ruby measured the soap while Rosie added drops of color. When they blew the bubbles, beautiful rainbow spheres floated through the basement air.

"Look, Ruby! They're dancing!" Rosie laughed as she tried to catch a purple bubble.

Ruby felt warm and happy inside. "You're a great lab partner, Rosie. Following our routine helped us make something amazing together."

"Can we do this every day?" Rosie asked hopefully.

"Yes," Ruby said. "Our routine is even better when we share it."

From that day on, Ruby and Rosie followed their lab routine together every morning. Ruby learned that sharing her knowledge made her feel proud and helpful. Rosie learned that routines made big projects feel easier and more fun.

The basement lab filled with laughter, colorful experiments, and the happy sounds of two little scientists working as a team.

The End.
