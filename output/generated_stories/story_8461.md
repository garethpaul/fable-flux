# Tick-Tock Tails in the Closet Hideaway

In a cozy house, there was a tiny closet with a soft rug, twinkly string lights, and a little shelf of crayons. It was a quiet hideaway. It smelled like clean cotton and a hint of lavender. In the hideaway lived a helpful robot dog named Tock.

Tock had shiny silver ears and a tail that wagged with soft beeps. Beep-beep! His nose lights blinked blue when he had an idea and green when it was time to start. Tock loved to help. He loved to plan. He loved to say, “Tick-tock! Let’s use our time like a clock!”

One morning, a small child named Mia crawled into the closet hideaway with a frown. She hugged her soft bear. “I have so much to do,” she sighed. “I want to clean my toys, draw a picture for Grandma, and read my book. But I don’t know what to do first.”

Tock sat up and wagged. Beep-beep! “Hello, Mia,” he said in a gentle, friendly voice. “Let’s make a plan. We will set goals. We will use our time. We will do one thing at a time.”

Mia sniffed the cozy air and listened. Far away, the washing machine hummed, mmm-mm. The clock in the hall went tick, tock, tick, tock. The closet felt safe. “Okay,” she said. “What is step one?”

Tock’s nose blinked blue. “Step one: make a list,” he said. He pulled a small chalkboard from the shelf with his little paw. Scratch, scratch. The chalk felt dusty and fun. “Goal one: tidy toys. Goal two: draw for Grandma. Goal three: read your book.”

Mia smiled. “That looks nice. But I still feel wiggly.”

Tock took a slow, calm breath. “Let’s try mindfulness,” he said softly. “We breathe in like we smell a flower. We breathe out like we blow a bubble.”

Mia breathed in. Sniff. She breathed out. Hooo. Her shoulders dropped. She felt ready.

Tock’s nose turned green. “Now we set a timer,” he said. He tapped his collar. Ding! A gentle bell rang. “Ten minutes for toys. Are you ready?”

“Ready!” Mia said.

They crawled out of the closet and into the playroom. Sunlight warmed the floor. Tiny cars and blocks were everywhere. Tock wagged. “We sort by color! We sing as we go!”

Mia clapped. “Red here, blue there!” she sang.

“Tick for toys, tock for time,” Tock sang in his robot woof. The timer glowed. They moved fast but not rushed. Mia’s hands felt busy and strong. Tock picked up blocks with a soft whirr. Whirr-click! He put them in a box.

Ding! The bell rang. “Stop,” Tock said kindly. “Time is up. Great job!”

Mia looked around. The floor looked neat. She felt proud. “What’s next?”

“Back to the hideaway,” Tock said. They crawled in. The string lights twinkled like tiny stars. “We check our list,” Tock said. He put a big smiley sticker next to Goal one.

Mia giggled. “Sticker time is happy.”

Tock nodded. “Goal two: draw for Grandma. We give it ten minutes.”

They set the timer. Mia picked purple, yellow, and green crayons. Scratchy-scritch, the crayons moved on the paper. Tock wagged and gently hummed. “What are you drawing?” he asked.

“A garden,” Mia said. “With a big sun and Grandma’s smile.”

“Beautiful,” Tock said. The paper smelled like wax. The colors felt bright and soft. Mia made a tiny heart in the corner.

Ding! The bell rang. Mia put the picture on the shelf. “Done!”

“Back to the hideaway,” Tock said. They stuck another smiley on the list. “Goal three: read your book. Ten minutes.”

Mia curled up on the soft rug. She opened her book. The pages made a whisper sound, flip, flip. Tock lay beside her, warm and steady like a little motor purring. Prrr. Mia traced the big letters with her finger. “The cat sat,” she read.

“You are calm and focused,” Tock said. “One thing at a time.”

Ding! The bell chimed. Mia closed the book and hugged Tock. “We did all the things! It was not too hard.”

Tock’s tail beeped happily. “We set goals. We used time. We took breaks in our quiet place. That is good time-management.”

Mia thought. “When I try to do everything at once, I feel tangled. When I do one thing at a time, I feel clear.”

Tock nodded. “Yes. Plan, breathe, do, and review.”

Mia peeked at the list. Three smiley stickers grinned at her. She added a star. “You helped me, Tock. You are very clever.”

Tock blinked blue. “We were a team. That is innovation, too,” he said. “We used a timer, a list, and our hideaway to make a new way that works for you.”

Mia smiled big. “Can we do this tomorrow?”

“Tick-tock,” said Tock. “We can do it every day!”

They cuddled in the cozy closet. The lights twinkled. The house hummed softly. Mia felt calm, proud, and ready for playtime. She knew she could make a plan, use her time, and reach her goals.

The End.
