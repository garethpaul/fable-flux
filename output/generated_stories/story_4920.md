# Brave Bubbles and the Big Brush

Baby Bear was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and bright curious eyes. One sunny morning, Baby Bear padded into the bathroom. The floor tiles felt cool on his paws. The mirror was shiny. The soap smelled like sweet oranges. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet.

Mama Bear called from the hallway, “Baby Bear, it’s brushing time! Two minutes, then breakfast.”

Baby Bear’s tummy rumbled. “Two minutes is so long,” he whispered. He looked at the tall cup with the big, bristly toothbrush. “That brush looks huge.”

He picked it up. The toothbrush felt heavy in his little paw. He squeezed the toothpaste. Squeeeze! A squiggle of minty paste plopped out. “Oops,” he giggled, but then he frowned. “What if I can’t do it right? What if I take too long?”

Baby Bear’s ears drooped. He felt a wiggle of worry in his chest.

Just then, a tiny bubble floated up from the sink. “Plink!”

“Hello?” said Baby Bear.

“Hello!” chimed the bubble. “I’m Brave Bubble. I help with bathroom bravery.” Brave Bubble wobbled and sparkled in the light. It smelled like oranges too.

Baby Bear blinked. “I need help,” he said softly. “Brushing feels hard. The brush is big. Two minutes is long. I don’t think I can do it.”

Brave Bubble bobbed near his nose. “You can do hard things,” it sang. “Take a deep breath. In… and out…” Baby Bear took a big breath. The minty and orange smells tickled his nose.

Brave Bubble twirled. “Let’s make a plan! Plans help with time. Plans help with courage. Plans make you feel strong.”

Baby Bear’s ears perked up. “A plan?” he asked.

“Plan number one,” said Brave Bubble, “Let’s make brushing fun. We can count together! One… two… three…”

Baby Bear smiled. “I like counting.”

“Plan number two,” said Brave Bubble, “Let’s try new ideas. That is being open-minded. We will try small circles. Then we will try side swipes. We will see what works best.”

Baby Bear nodded. “I can try.”

“Plan number three,” Brave Bubble chimed, “We will use a timer song. Two minutes is the length of our silly brush song!”

Baby Bear looked at the mirror. He saw his own small face. He stood tall on the fuzzy bath mat. “I can do this,” he said. His voice felt a little bigger.

“Let’s start,” said Brave Bubble. “Ready…”

Baby Bear put the brush on his teeth. The bristles felt tickly. “Hee-hee!” he giggled.

“Brush the fronts,” sang Brave Bubble. “One, two, three, four… small circles galore!”

Baby Bear moved the brush in tiny circles. Swish, swish, swish. Minty foam bubbled. It felt cool and tingly. He watched the mirror and smiled at his foamy grin.

“Now the sides,” Brave Bubble hummed. “Five, six, seven, eight… you’re doing great!”

Baby Bear tried side swipes. “This is a new way,” he said. “I am trying something new.” He felt proud.

“Now the tops,” Brave Bubble chimed. “Nine, ten, eleven, twelve… brave bear, be yourself!”

Baby Bear brushed the tops of his teeth. Scritch-scritch-scritch. “I am brave,” he whispered. His shoulders relaxed. He felt calm and strong.

“Halfway!” said Brave Bubble. “Take a tiny pause. Breathe in. Breathe out. You are on time.”

Baby Bear peeked at the clock on the wall. The second hand went tick, tick, tick. He nodded. “I am using my time.”

“Back to the bottoms,” sang Brave Bubble. “Thirteen, fourteen, fifteen, sixteen… keep them clean!”

Baby Bear moved the brush slowly and carefully. He did not rush. He did not stop. He was steady. The bathroom smelled extra minty now, and the faucet went drip, drip, drip.

“Seventeen, eighteen, nineteen, twenty!” Brave Bubble’s voice was happy and bright. “Almost done.”

Baby Bear rinsed and spit. Swish, ptoo! He wiped his mouth with a soft towel. He looked in the mirror. His teeth looked shiny and clean.

Mama Bear peeked in. “I heard the song,” she said with a smile. “You brushed the whole time!”

Baby Bear stood tall. “I made a plan. I tried new ways. I counted. I used my time,” he said. “I did it!”

Mama Bear gave him a warm hug. “I am proud of you,” she said. “How do you feel?”

Baby Bear put a paw on his chest. His heart felt warm. “I feel brave. I feel strong. I can do hard things.”

Brave Bubble floated near his ear. “That is self-confidence,” it whispered. “You believed in you.”

Baby Bear nodded. “Tomorrow, I can do it again.”

Mama Bear said, “Yes. You can. And if something feels hard, you can make a plan, try new ideas, and take your time.”

Baby Bear smiled. He picked up the cup and took a sip of cool water. Ahhh. He listened to the little drip drop. He smelled the sweet soap. He felt the soft towel. The bathroom felt like a brave place now.

“Thank you, Brave Bubble,” said Baby Bear.

“You did it,” Brave Bubble replied. “I just sang. You were the brave one.”

Baby Bear’s tummy rumbled again. “Breakfast time!” he laughed.

He hopped off the bath mat, ready for the day, with a bright, shiny smile and a brave, shiny heart.

The lesson: Believe in yourself, make a simple plan, try new ideas, and use your time well. You can do hard things, one small step at a time.

The End.
