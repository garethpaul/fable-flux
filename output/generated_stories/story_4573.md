# Benny Bear’s Porch Plan

Benny was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved his peaceful front porch. It smelled like pine and warm tea. The porch was where Mama Bear rocked in a chair and hummed. The porch was where the wind made tiny whispers and the sun made golden stripes on the floor.

One sunny morning, Benny clapped his paws. “I have a big goal!” he said. “I want to tie my red porch ribbon all by myself.”

His red ribbon fluttered in the breeze. It felt smooth and silky in his paws. Benny’s tummy felt excited and a little twisty. He wanted to do it today.

Mama Bear smiled. “That is a wonderful goal, Benny,” she said. “A goal is a wish with a plan. We can make a plan and follow directions. We can practice. We can grow our brains.”

Benny wiggled his ears. “Grow my brain?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Mama. “When it is hard and you try, your brain gets stronger. That is called a growth mindset. We say, ‘I can learn. I can try. I can get better.’”

Benny took a deep breath. He smelled honey toast from the kitchen. He felt brave. “Okay, Mama. I will try.”

Mama Bear sat beside him on the porch step. Her chair creaked softly. The breeze tickled their noses. Birds sang peep-peep in the big tree.

“First,” Mama said, “let’s set our steps. Step one: cross the two ribbon ends. Step two: make a loop. Step three: wrap the other end around the loop. Step four: pull both loops tight.”

Benny nodded. He liked the clear steps. He liked having a plan.

He held the ribbon ends. “Step one,” he said. He crossed them. The ribbon slid and slipped. “Oops!”

“That’s okay,” Mama said. “When it slips, we learn. Try again.”

Benny tried again. Cross, loop, wrap, pull. The bow fell apart. His ears drooped. “It’s too hard,” he whispered.

Mama put a warm paw on his shoulder. “It is hard, and you are learning. What can we say?”

Benny looked at the glowing sun stripes. He listened to the wind. He felt the porch under his paws. “I can learn. I can try. I can get better,” he said softly.

“Good,” Mama said. “Let’s break it into little bits. We can practice just step one. Cross.”

Benny crossed the ends. This time he held them tight. “Cross!” he cheered.

“Now step two. Make a loop.”

Benny made a little ear with one end. It felt wiggly. He giggled. “It looks like a bunny ear.”

“Perfect,” said Mama. “Step three. Wrap the other end around the loop.”

Benny wrapped it. It squeezed his paw. He stuck out his tongue and focused. The birds peeped like tiny helpers.

“Step four. Pull both loops,” Mama said. “Gentle, gentle.”

Benny pulled. The ribbon made a soft swish. Then—plink!—it slipped again.

His eyes got shiny. “I can’t do it.”

Mama shook her head kindly. “You can’t do it yet. Yet is a magic word. Yet means you are on the way. Let’s rest and try again.”

They rocked in the chair. The wood felt smooth. The air smelled like apples. Benny’s breath slowed. He felt calm.

After a little rest, Benny sat up. “I want to try again,” he said. “That is my goal.”

“Great!” said Mama. “Let’s add one more direction: keep your paws close to the bow.”

Benny smiled. “Okay. Cross. Loop. Wrap. Paws close. Pull.”

He pulled slowly. The ribbon made a tiny whisper. The loops grew like two little bear ears. The bow held!

“I did it!” Benny shouted. His heart felt warm and big. He touched the bow. It felt snug and neat.

Mama clapped. “You followed directions. You set a goal. You kept trying. Your brain got stronger.”

Benny hugged her. “At first it was hard,” he said. “Then I rested. Then I tried again. I used my steps.”

Mama nodded. “That is a growth mindset. When something is hard, we say, ‘I can’t do it yet.’ We make a plan. We follow directions. We practice. We keep going.”

Benny looked at his red bow. The sun made it sparkle. The breeze made it flutter. He felt proud and brave.

He stood up on the porch step. “I have a new plan,” he said. “Tomorrow, I will try to tie it two times.”

Mama smiled. “That is a great goal. We can write it down.”

They wrote it on a small card with a blue crayon. Benny sniffed the crayon. It smelled sweet and waxy. He traced the letters with his paw.

Later, Benny sat and watched the sky change to soft pink. He listened to the peep-peep birds. He touched his bow and felt its gentle knot.

“I can learn,” he whispered. “I can try. I can get better.”

“Yes,” Mama said. “And you did.”

The End.
