# Bubbles the Dolphin Makes a Plan

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash, giggle, and spin. One sunny day, Bubbles visited a house with a big, bright laundry room. The floor was cool and smooth. The air smelled like clean soap and warm cotton. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer went, “Thump, thump, thump.”

Bubbles wiggled her fins and whispered, “What a fun room! So many soft towels! So many baskets!” She liked to play, but she also remembered what her friend, Turtle Tom, always said: “Think first. Make a plan.”

Bubbles saw a mountain of clothes piled high on a tall basket. On top was a tiny red sock with a smiley face. The little sock looked stuck. It wobbled, then teetered, and almost fell behind the big machines.

“Oh no!” Bubbles gasped. “If the little sock falls, it might get lost.” She wanted to help, but the space behind the machines was small and dark.

Bubbles took a deep breath. She felt brave, but she also felt a little worried. “I will not rush,” she said softly. “I will make a plan.”

She looked around the laundry room. She listened and smelled. She touched the smooth baskets, the fluffy towels, and the cool floor. She made a plan step by step.

“Step one,” said Bubbles, tapping her fin to her nose. “Ask for help if I need it.” She looked for an adult. No one was there, but there was a phone on the wall. “If I need help, I can call,” she decided.

“Step two,” said Bubbles. “Make the floor safe.” She saw a small puddle of water near the machines. “Slippery,” she said. She used a towel to pat-pat the puddle until it was dry.

“Step three,” she said. “Use tools.” Bubbles found a long, soft duster, a gentle grabber, and a laundry basket. She placed the basket under the tall pile, just in case anything fell.

“Step four,” Bubbles whispered. “Check my boundaries.” She pointed to a yellow line painted on the floor near the machines. A sign said, “Do not climb behind.” Bubbles nodded. “Personal boundaries keep me safe. I will not go behind the machines.”

She smiled. “I can be brave and clever without breaking rules.”

The little sock wobbled again. “Help!” it squeaked in a tiny voice.

“I’m coming,” Bubbles said kindly. “But I will go slow and follow my plan.”

Bubbles stood on the safe side of the yellow line. She held the long, soft duster. “I will nudge, not push,” she said. With a gentle sweep, she brushed the sock closer. “Swish, swish.” The sock slid toward the edge, but the pile started to lean. “Uh-oh.”

Bubbles paused. She felt her heart flutter, like bubbles popping. She took a deep breath in, then let it out. “I can do this,” she said. “Brave and careful.”

She moved the laundry basket closer to the leaning side. “Catch if it falls,” she told the basket. Then she picked up the gentle grabber. “Clack, clack,” it went. She aimed at the sock’s smiley face. “Almost there…”

“Got you!” Bubbles cheered as the grabber clasped the tiny sock. The big pile wobbled, but the basket held it safe. The little sock giggled. “Thank you!”

Bubbles hugged the sock gently. It felt warm and soft. “You are safe now,” she said. She placed the sock on top of a folded towel so it wouldn’t move.

Just then, the dryer stopped. “Thump.” The room got quiet and cozy. An adult, Ms. Sunny, walked in. “Oh! Hello, Bubbles,” she said with a smile. “I heard the machines. Are you all right?”

“I am,” said Bubbles. “I made a plan. I dried the puddle. I used tools. I stayed on the safe side of the line. I did not go behind the machines.”

Ms. Sunny nodded. “That is smart and safe. You were brave to help, and you respected the rules. Thank you for saving the little sock.”

Bubbles blushed and twirled. “I also asked for help if I needed it,” she added, pointing to the phone. “But I could do it with my plan.”

Ms. Sunny clapped softly. “Planning first makes tricky things easier,” she said. “And you took care of yourself and the laundry room.”

The little red sock wiggled and sang, “I feel happy and snug! Thank you, Bubbles!”

Bubbles giggled. The clean, warm air felt nice on her skin. The towels looked like fluffy clouds. The baskets sat in neat rows. Everything felt calm.

Bubbles looked at the yellow line again. “Boundaries keep me safe,” she said. “Bravery means doing the right thing, even when I feel a little scared. Planning helps me know what to do. And tools help me be resourceful.”

Ms. Sunny gave Bubbles a sticker shaped like a shining star. “For smart planning,” she said.

Bubbles put the star on her fin. “I will remember my steps,” she said proudly. “Think first. Make a plan. Stay safe. Ask for help. Use tools. Try again.”

The washing machine started humming once more, “Whirr, whirr.” Bubbles and the sock danced a tiny dance. The laundry room felt bright and friendly.

Bubbles smiled. “Planning helps me be brave and kind,” she said. And every time she heard “Whirr, whirr,” she remembered her plan and her brave, careful heart.

The End.
