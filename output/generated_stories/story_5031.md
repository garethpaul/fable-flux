# Benny's Big Kitchen Problem

Benny the rabbit loved his warm kitchen. The sunny yellow walls made him feel happy. The smell of fresh carrots filled the air. But today, Benny had a big problem.

"Oh no!" Benny said, looking at his kitchen table. "This won't work at all!"

Benny wanted to bake carrot cookies for his friends. But his little sister Rosie wanted to paint pictures on the same table. And his big brother Max needed the table to build his block tower.

"I need this table first!" said Benny, thumping his foot. "I promised cookies to everyone!"

"But I want to paint!" cried Rosie, holding her colorful brushes. "My art is important too!"

"My tower needs to be finished!" said Max, carrying his wooden blocks. "I've been working on it all week!"

All three rabbits stared at the kitchen table. It was the only big table in their cozy home. The warm afternoon sun made it the perfect spot for all their activities.

Benny's whiskers twitched as he thought hard. He really wanted those cookies to be perfect. But he could see that Rosie and Max wanted to use the table too.

"This isn't fair to anyone," Benny said quietly. He looked at his sad siblings. Rosie's ears drooped down. Max's blocks sat in a messy pile.

Then Benny had a wonderful idea! His ears perked up with excitement.

"Wait! I have a plan!" Benny announced. "What if we share the table? We can each use part of it!"

"How?" asked Rosie, wiping her eyes.

Benny hopped around the table, pointing with his paws. "Look! The table has three sections. Rosie, you can paint on this end by the window. The light is perfect for art!"

Rosie's tail wiggled with joy. "Oh yes! I can see all my colors so clearly here!"

"And Max," continued Benny, "you can build your tower on this side. It's near the wall, so your blocks won't fall off."

Max smiled big. "That's perfect! My tower will be safe there!"

"And I'll bake my cookies on this middle part," said Benny. "We can all work together!"

The three rabbits got busy right away. Rosie painted beautiful flowers in the bright sunlight. Max carefully stacked his colorful blocks. Benny mixed flour and grated carrots for his special cookies.

"This smells amazing!" said Max, watching Benny work.

"Your tower looks so tall!" said Benny, admiring Max's building.

"And Rosie, your flowers are so pretty!" both brothers said together.

As the cookies baked in the warm oven, the kitchen filled with the most wonderful smells. The three rabbits cleaned up their spaces and helped each other.

"You know what?" said Rosie, washing her paintbrushes. "This was much more fun than working alone!"

"I agree!" said Max, organizing his blocks. "We all got to do what we wanted!"

Benny pulled the golden cookies from the oven. They were perfect! "And the best part is, we all learned something important today."

"What's that?" asked his siblings.

"When we share and work together, everyone can be happy," said Benny with a warm smile. "We just needed to think of a fair way to solve our problem!"

The three rabbits sat together at their shared table. They ate warm carrot cookies, admired Rosie's art, and marveled at Max's tall tower. The kitchen felt cozier than ever before.

From that day on, whenever the rabbit siblings had a problem, they remembered Benny's lesson about sharing and finding fair solutions that made everyone happy.

The End.
