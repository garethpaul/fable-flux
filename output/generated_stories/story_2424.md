# Gigi’s Porch Pockets

Gigi the thoughtful giraffe loved her peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like fresh orange peels from the little tree nearby. The wood felt warm under her hooves. A soft breeze tickled her ears, and the rocking chair creaked in a friendly way. “Good morning, porch,” Gigi whispered, blinking her long lashes. “Today will be a tidy day.”

Gigi liked to think and to care. She cared about her friends. She cared about her feelings. And today, she wanted to care for her things. But her porch had a problem. Toys were scattered. Crayons rolled under the chair. Paintbrushes leaned against the flower pot. A tiny drum sat on the step. Gigi felt a squiggle in her tummy.

“This mess makes my head feel foggy,” she said softly. “I want to see the floor and feel calm.”

Her friend Poppy the porcupine waddled up, humming. “Hi, Gigi! Can we paint today?” Poppy sniffed the air. “Mmm, oranges!”

“Hi, Poppy,” Gigi said. “I want to paint too. But I can’t find the red brush. And the green crayon is hiding.”

Poppy peered around. “Oh,” she said, quills drooping a little. “It is messy.”

Just then, Tiko the tiny turtle clattered onto the porch, shell clicking on the wood. “Gigi! I brought stickers!” He held up a sparkly star. “Where do I put them?”

Gigi took a long, slow breath. In, out. The breeze brushed her cheeks. “I feel frustrated,” she said, placing a hoof on her chest. “I want to play, but I need to organize first.”

Poppy nodded. “We can help.”

Tiko blinked. “What does organize mean?”

“It means giving each thing a home,” Gigi said with a gentle smile. “When everything has a place, we can find it. And our hearts feel peaceful.”

Gigi looked around her porch. “Let’s make Porch Pockets,” she said, eyes bright. “Little baskets and boxes for our things.”

Poppy clapped. “Yes! I like pockets.”

They gathered three small baskets and a box. The baskets smelled like straw and sunshine. Gigi tied bright ribbons on them. Red ribbon for brushes. Green ribbon for crayons. Blue ribbon for toys. She drew a sticker label for the box: STICKERS.

“Here is our plan,” Gigi said kindly. “We sing, sort, and smile.”

“What’s sing-sort-smile?” Tiko asked.

“We sing while we sort to make it fun,” Gigi explained. “And we smile when we finish, because being organized feels good.”

Gigi started a simple tune. “Brushes in red, crayons in green, toys in blue, keep it clean!”

Poppy sang too, her voice soft and bouncy. Tiko hummed along, tapping his shell like a drum. Tap-tap, hum-hum. The front porch seemed to dance.

They picked up a paintbrush. “Red ribbon!” Gigi said, placing it in the red basket.

A crayon rolled by. “Green ribbon!” Poppy grinned and popped it in the green basket.

A toy car zoomed from under the chair. “Blue ribbon!” Tiko laughed and parked it in the blue basket.

Soon, the floor showed its warm wood stripes. The rocking chair could rock. The orange tree’s leaves shivered happily. Gigi felt the fog in her head lift. “I can think again,” she said. “I feel calm.”

Then Gigi saw something else—a little pile of stickers stuck to the step. Some were wrinkled. Some were dirty. She remembered yesterday. She had told Tiko, “Don’t touch my stickers.” She had said it in a sharp voice. Tiko’s eyes had gotten big then. Gigi felt a small pinch in her heart.

“Tiko,” Gigi said gently now, bending her long neck. “Yesterday I spoke in a rough voice. I am sorry. I was upset about the mess, but I did not use kind words. I want to make amends.”

Tiko rubbed the porch with his tiny foot. “I was sad,” he said. “But I feel better now.”

“Thank you for telling me,” Gigi said. “You matter. My words matter. Next time I will breathe and use a soft voice.”

Poppy nodded. “We all learn. We all try again.”

Gigi smiled at her friends. “We are different, but we fit,” she said. “You drum. Poppy hums. I plan. That is our porch team.”

They finished sorting. Gigi placed the sticker box near the rocking chair. “Stickers live here,” she said. “Brushes live here. Crayons live here. Toys live here.”

Poppy sniffed the orange tree and giggled. “The porch smells neat!”

Tiko looked around with wide turtle eyes. “I can see everything!”

Gigi’s heart felt warm, like sunlight on the wood. “Now we can paint,” she said.

They painted a big sign together. Gigi drew tall letters. Poppy dotted the corners. Tiko added sparkly stars. The sign said: Porch Pockets.

Gigi hung the sign. The ribbons fluttered in the breeze. The porch looked friendly and bright.

“Let’s make a porch promise,” Gigi said. “After we play, we put things back.”

They placed their hooves and paws and tiny turtle foot together. “Porch promise!” they cheered.

As the sun began to set, the sky turned peach and pink. The rocking chair creaked a happy good-night. Gigi tidied the last brush into the red basket. Poppy tucked the crayons into green. Tiko slid the toy car into blue and closed the sticker box with a click.

Gigi took a slow breath. In, out. “I feel proud,” she said. “We organized. We were kind. We made amends. Our different ways helped us.”

Poppy smiled. “And now we can always find the red brush.”

Tiko patted the sticker box. “And the stars!”

Gigi nuzzled her friends. “When everything has a home, our hearts can rest.”

The End.
