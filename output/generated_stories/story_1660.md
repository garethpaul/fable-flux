# Shelly's Dancing Discovery

In a cozy home office filled with books and a big computer, lived a very shy turtle named Shelly. She had a beautiful green shell with yellow spots, but she always kept her head tucked inside when anyone came near.

Shelly's mom worked at the big desk every day, typing on her computer. "Click, click, click" went the keyboard. Shelly would peek out just a tiny bit to watch, but whenever Mom looked her way, she'd quickly pull her head back in.

One sunny morning, Mom turned on some happy music while she worked. "La, la, la!" sang the cheerful tune. The music made Shelly's little heart feel warm and tingly.

"Oh, what a lovely song!" said Mom, swaying gently in her chair. "This music makes me want to dance!"

Shelly had never heard anything so wonderful. The music seemed to tickle her toes inside her shell. She wanted to move, but she was too scared. What if she looked silly? What if she wasn't good at dancing?

The music played on: "Boom, boom, tap, tap!" Shelly's foot started to wiggle just a little bit. Then her other foot joined in. Before she knew it, both her feet were tapping quietly against her shell.

"That's strange," said Mom, looking around. "I hear a little drumbeat, but I don't see any drums!"

Shelly froze. Had Mom heard her? She pulled her head in even deeper.

But the music was too wonderful to resist. Soon, Shelly's feet were tapping again, this time a little louder. "Tap, tap, wiggle, wiggle!"

Mom smiled and looked down at Shelly's corner. "Is that you making music, little one?"

Shelly's heart beat fast like a tiny drum. She was so embarrassed! But then she noticed something amazing - Mom was smiling, not laughing at her.

"Your tapping sounds beautiful!" said Mom kindly. "Would you like to dance with me?"

Very slowly, Shelly poked her head out just a little. The music was still playing its happy tune. Mom was moving her arms gently, like she was swimming in the air.

"It's okay to be shy," said Mom softly. "But sometimes trying new things can be really fun. Want to try moving just one foot?"

Shelly thought about it. The music did make her feel happy inside. Maybe she could try just a tiny bit.

She stuck out one green foot and tapped it gently. "Tap!"

"Wonderful!" cheered Mom. "Now try the other foot!"

Shelly felt braver. She stuck out her other foot. "Tap, tap!"

The music seemed to wrap around her like a warm hug. Soon, Shelly was tapping both feet and bobbing her head to the beat. She even stuck her neck out a little further.

"Look at you go!" said Mom, clapping her hands. "You're a natural dancer!"

Shelly felt something new and wonderful growing in her chest - it was confidence! She wasn't perfect at dancing, but she was getting better with each tap and wiggle.

From that day on, whenever Mom played music in the office, Shelly would come out of her shell and dance. Each day, she moved a little more and felt a little braver. She learned that it's okay to start small and that practice makes everything better.

"The best part about trying new things," Mom told her one day, "is that you get to improve and grow!"

Shelly smiled big. She had discovered that being shy was okay, but being brave enough to try was even better.

The End.
