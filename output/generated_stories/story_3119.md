# The Brave Build in the Busy Garage

In a sunny, busy garage workshop, a clever cat named Coco padded across the cool floor. The floor smelled like pine wood and orange soap. Sunbeams made bright stripes on boxes and tools. Little sparks of dust danced in the light.

Coco wore a small blue bandana. Her whiskers twitched. Her ears pricked up. Coco loved to learn new things. She loved to build, too. Today, she wanted to make a Bird Buddy Box. It would be a safe, cozy home for small birds outside the window.

“Meow! I can do it,” Coco said to herself. “I will be brave. I will be careful. I will be kind.”

On the big workbench, there were wood pieces, soft sandpaper, a tiny hammer, and shiny screws. There was also a bright yellow tape measure that clicked, and a pencil that smelled like cedar. The garage hummed with a low fan. Somewhere, water dripped: plip, plop, plip.

Coco looked at the plan. The paper had simple pictures with numbers. “Step one,” she read aloud, tapping the paper with her paw. “Measure and mark.” She stretched the tape measure. It made a whirring sound: zzzzip! She marked the wood with the pencil. Scritch, scritch.

Then Coco saw the next step: “Cut the wood.” She looked at the small hand saw on the bench. It was shiny and sharp. Coco’s tail curled. Her heart thumped a little faster. “I feel a tiny bit scared,” Coco whispered. “Sharp tools can be scary.”

Coco took a slow breath in. The air smelled like clean wood. She let the breath out. “Being brave does not mean I am not scared,” she said softly. “Being brave means I am careful and I try.”

Just then, a little mouse named Mino peeked from behind a paint can. “Squeak! Hello, Coco,” Mino said. “What are you building?”

“A Bird Buddy Box,” Coco smiled. “I want birds to feel safe.”

“That is kind,” said Mino. “Do you need help?”

Coco nodded. “Yes, please. I feel a little scared of the saw.”

Mino’s eyes were warm. “You are brave to say how you feel,” he said. “Let’s make a plan.”

Coco stood tall on a stool. “Plan: we will be safe. We will go slow. We will wear gear.” She put on little soft ear muffs and tiny goggles that made her eyes look big and round. Mino put on a tiny acorn helmet.

Coco taped the wood down so it would not move. “Hold the wood steady with the clamps,” she said. The metal clamps clicked. Mino held the end with gentle paws. “Ready,” he squeaked.

Coco wrapped her paws around the saw. Her paws felt a little shaky. She took another slow breath. “In… and out…” she said. “I can do hard things with care.”

Zzzip, zzzip, went the saw. Sawdust drifted down like cinnamon sugar. It tickled Coco’s nose. “Achoo!” she sneezed. Mino giggled. “Bless you!”

The first cut was straight and smooth. “I did it!” Coco cheered. She felt brave and calm. Her heart slowed. Her tail swished in a happy way. “Thank you for helping me,” she told Mino.

“Anytime,” said Mino. “You were safe and brave.”

Coco sanded the edges. Shh, shh, shh. The wood became soft to touch. She hummed a little tune. The garage felt warm and friendly now. The fear felt small.

“Now we build,” Coco said. She placed the sides together. Tap, tap, tap went the tiny hammer. “Oops,” Coco said when a nail tipped sideways.

“It’s okay,” Mino said. “We can try again.”

Coco took a break. She sipped cool water. Self-care time, she thought. She stretched her paws. “When I rest, I do my best.”

After the break, Coco tried again. Tap, tap, tap. The nail went in straight. Piece by piece, the Bird Buddy Box grew. It smelled like fresh wood and a hint of lemon from the oil Coco used to rub the surface. She added a little door and a round window. She even painted a small heart on the side, bright red.

“Last step,” Coco said. “We make it kind.” She tucked soft cotton inside so baby birds would have a cozy bed. She tied a ribbon on top.

Mino looked proud. “Coco, you were brave. You learned. You cared for yourself. You built something kind.”

Coco smiled so wide her whiskers wiggled. “I was scared, and I asked for help. I went slow and was careful. I took a rest. That is brave.”

They carried the Bird Buddy Box to the window. The sun warmed their fur. They heard cheerful chirps outside. “Tweet, tweet!” A little bird fluttered down and looked at the box. It tilted its head and hopped inside. The bird’s feathers brushed the smooth wood, whisper, whisper.

Coco and Mino held their breaths. Then they heard a happy chirp. “It likes it!” Coco purred. Her chest buzzed like a small motor. “We did it.”

Mino nodded. “When we are brave and kind, we can help others.”

Coco placed a small sign by the bench: “Be Brave. Be Safe. Be Kind.” She tapped the sign into place. Tap, tap.

She looked around the busy garage workshop. It still smelled like wood and orange soap. The fan still hummed. But now it also felt like a place of courage and care.

Coco turned to Mino. “Next time, we can build a Mouse House for you,” she said.

Mino smiled. “With a tiny window and a tiny door!”

“Yes,” Coco said. “We will be brave. We will learn. We will build with love.”

The End.
