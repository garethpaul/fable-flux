# Polly Plans It Out

Polly the parrot loved the study corner. It was a cozy spot by the window with a small desk, a sunny yellow chair, and a soft green rug. Books sat in a neat basket. Crayons rested in a tin cup. A little plant, with shiny leaves, stood proudly in a clay pot. The plant’s name was Sprout.

“Good morning, Sprout!” Polly chirped, fluffing her bright feathers. “Let’s make art today!”

Polly spread paper on the desk. She wiggled her tail and sang, “Squawk-a-doodle-doo! I will paint a rainbow for you!” She reached for her paint box and brushes.

But when she opened the paint, Polly frowned. “Oh no,” she whispered. “The water cup is empty. And the brush is sticky. And—oh dear—the trash bin is full. My study corner is a mess!”

Polly looked around. There were crayon bits on the rug. There were paper scraps on the chair. A little paint cup lay on its side. The sun was warm on Polly’s feathers, but she felt a little worried inside.

Polly took a deep breath. “I am a creative parrot,” she told herself. “I can think. I can plan. I can solve this.”

She tapped her beak gently on the desk. Tap, tap, tap. “First, I will look. Then, I will think. Then, I will do. Step-by-step!”

Sprout’s leaves rustled by the window. Polly listened. The room was quiet, like a hush. She could hear the gentle buzz of a fly outside and the soft tick-tick-tick of the clock.

“Step one: What is the problem?” Polly asked aloud.
She pointed with her wing. “Sticky brush. Empty water. Full trash. Messy rug.”

“Step two: What do I need?” she asked.
She nodded. “I need clean water. A tidy space. A place for scraps. And I must care for Sprout.”

“Step three: What can I do that is kind to Earth?” Polly looked at Sprout and smiled. “I will not waste. I will reuse. I will recycle.”

Polly picked up the tipped paint cup. “This cup can be cleaned,” she said. “I will rinse it with a little water. Not too much, just enough.” She tiptoed to a small sink in the corner. Swish, swish. The water sounded soft. Polly watched the paint swirl like a tiny cloud, then fade. She placed the clean cup back with a happy “Squawk!”

Next, Polly looked at the paper scraps. She held them in her wings and felt the crinkly edges. “These can be used for small art,” she said. “Little feathers! Little leaves! I will make a scrap box.” She found an empty cereal box from the recycle bin. It smelled a bit like oats. “Perfect!” she chirped. She labeled it with a big P for “Pieces.”

Polly opened the full trash bin. “Hmm,” she said. “Some things are trash. Some things can be recycled.” She sorted slowly. “Plastic cap here. Paper there. Real trash in the bin.” She moved each piece with care. The soft rustle of paper made a friendly sound, like a tiny wave.

Now the rug. Polly shook out the crayon bits. Click-click went the crayons as they dropped into the tin cup. She patted the rug smooth. It felt warm from the sun. “Almost ready,” she said.

Polly checked her list in her head. “Sticky brush!” She dipped the brush in the clean water. Swirl, swirl. The bristles wiggled and softened. “There you go,” she cooed. “All clean.”

Polly smiled at Sprout. “Would you like a sip, too?” She felt the soil with her toe. It was dry, like a cracker. “Just a little water,” Polly said. She poured gently until the soil felt like soft cake. “Caring for you helps the Earth,” she whispered. Sprout’s leaves looked extra shiny in the light.

Polly clapped her wings. “I used steps. I used my brain. I was kind to Earth. Now I can paint!”

She dipped her brush in red. Swish. Then orange. Swish. Yellow, green, blue, and purple. The colors smelled like sweet fruit and summer grass. Polly hummed as the rainbow curved across the page. In the corner, she glued a tiny green paper leaf from the scrap box. “A leaf for Sprout,” she said.

Just then, a little friend peeked in. It was Mina the mouse. “Hi, Polly,” Mina squeaked. “Your study corner is so tidy! How did you do it?”

Polly puffed with pride. “I used a plan,” she said. “First, I looked. Then I thought. Then I did. Step-by-step. I reused and recycled. I saved water. And I cared for Sprout.”

Mina’s eyes sparkled. “That is smart! Can you teach me?”

“Yes!” said Polly. “Let’s make a list.”
She spoke slowly and Mina wrote in big letters:
- Look at the problem.
- Think of what you need.
- Choose kind-to-Earth ideas.
- Do the steps, one by one.

Mina smiled. “I will try this at my desk, too.”

Polly pinned the list above the desk. She felt calm and happy. The room smelled fresh and clean. The sun made the rainbow painting glow.

Polly looked at Mina and Sprout. “When we think and plan,” she said, “we can solve problems. When we care for our things and our Earth, we help everyone.”

Mina clapped. Sprout’s leaves rustled. Polly bowed and sang, “Step-by-step, we do our best!”

The End.
