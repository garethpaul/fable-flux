# Polly's Colorful Dining Room Dance

Polly the parrot loved making beautiful things. Her bright green feathers sparkled in the morning sunlight as she perched on her favorite chair in the dining room. Today felt special - like a day perfect for creating something wonderful!

"Good morning, dining room!" Polly chirped happily. "What shall we make together today?"

The dining room was quiet and peaceful. Sunbeams danced across the wooden table, making pretty patterns on the wall. Polly flapped her colorful wings and noticed how her shadow moved like a dancer.

"Oh my!" she squawked with excitement. "Look at my shadow dancing! I have an idea!"

Polly hopped down from her chair and began to move around the dining room. She stretched her wings wide, then brought them close to her body. Her shadow on the wall looked like it was telling a story.

"I can make shadow pictures!" Polly said, her voice full of wonder. She moved her wings to make the shadow look like a butterfly. "Flutter, flutter, little butterfly!"

But then Polly had a problem. She wanted to make more shadow shapes, but she couldn't see all the walls at once. How could she share her shadow art with everyone?

Polly thought carefully, tilting her colorful head. "Hmm... I need to think of a solution."

She looked around the dining room with curious eyes. The chairs! The table! The walls! Everything could be part of her creative adventure.

"I know!" Polly chirped. "I'll make a shadow dance show!"

Polly began to hop and flutter from chair to chair around the dining table. At each spot, she made a different shadow shape on the wall. First, she made a tall tree by stretching up high. Then she made a round sun by spreading her wings in a circle.

"Hop, hop, hop!" Polly sang as she moved. "This is great exercise too!"

As she danced around the dining room, Polly discovered she could make her shadows tell a whole story. The tree shadow grew flowers when she wiggled her wing feathers. The sun shadow smiled when she tilted her head just right.

"Look at all the beautiful pictures I can make!" Polly said, feeling proud and happy. "My body is like a paintbrush, and the wall is my canvas!"

Soon, Polly's family came into the dining room for breakfast. They gasped with delight when they saw Polly's shadow show.

"Polly, what beautiful art you've created!" said her mom. "You used your imagination and your whole body to make something special."

"And you solved the problem of sharing your art by moving around the room," added her dad. "That was very clever!"

Polly felt warm and grateful inside. She had discovered that creativity could happen anywhere, even in the dining room! She had used her curiosity to explore, her body to exercise, and her imagination to create something beautiful.

"Thank you for watching my shadow dance show," Polly said with a happy bow. "Creating art makes me feel so joyful! Tomorrow I'll think of something new to make."

As the family sat down for breakfast, Polly perched proudly on her chair. She had learned that being creative means using what you have around you, moving your body, and never being afraid to try new ideas.

The End.
