# Nutmeg Solves the Study Corner Puzzle

Nutmeg was a small, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail. She loved to learn new things. One sunny morning, Nutmeg made a cozy study corner in her room. She put a tiny table by the window, a soft pillow to sit on, and a basket of crayons that smelled like sweet wax. The light was warm and golden. It made her fur look extra shiny.

“Today I will make a learning chart,” Nutmeg said. “I will draw shapes and write my name!”

She opened a book and ran her paw over the smooth pages. “Mmm, the paper feels cool,” she whispered. She picked up a blue crayon, but the crayon rolled right off the table. Plink! It fell to the floor and rolled under the bookshelf.

“Oh no,” said Nutmeg. “My crayon!”

She looked around. The table was a little wobbly. Her basket of crayons kept sliding. Her chart paper kept curling up like a sleepy leaf. Nutmeg took a deep breath. She felt a little worried in her chest. “I can fix this,” she told herself. “I will try.”

Nutmeg remembered her three S’s: Stop, See, and Solve. Her teacher taught her the three S’s for problem solving.

“Stop,” Nutmeg said softly. She paused and sat on her pillow.

“See,” she whispered, looking closely. She saw the wobbly table leg. She saw the slippery basket. She saw the curly paper.

“Solve,” Nutmeg said with a smile. “I can choose what to do.”

First, she decided to fix the wobbly table. Tap, tap, tap went her paw as she pushed the table. “Hmm,” she said. “It needs something flat.” She looked in her craft box and found a folded paper square. She slid it under the short leg. The table stood steady. Thump. No wobble!

“I did it,” Nutmeg said. Her tail twitched with pride.

Next, she decided to stop the basket from sliding. She felt the table. It was smooth and slippery, like a river stone. Nutmeg looked around. She saw a piece of sticky tape. “I can use this,” she decided. She tore a strip—riiiip!—and stuck it under the basket. She pressed it down. Press, press, press. The basket stayed put.

“Two problems solved,” she sang.

But the paper still curled up. It made little loops and would not lie flat. Nutmeg put her paw on the paper. “Stay,” she said. The paper kept curling. “Hmm. I can try again.”

Nutmeg thought of teamwork. She peeked out the window and called, “Bluejay! Do you have a minute?”

Bluejay fluttered in and landed on the windowsill. “Hi, Nutmeg! What’s up?” he chirped.

“I’m being self-reliant,” said Nutmeg, “but I also know I can ask for ideas. My paper curls, and I want it to be flat. What do you think?”

Bluejay tilted his head. “You are doing great! Maybe try little helpers. Like tiny weights.”

Nutmeg nodded. “Good idea. Thank you!” She looked around her study corner and spotted smooth pebbles in a jar. She set one on each corner of the paper. Clink, clink, clink, clink. The paper relaxed and lay flat.

“It worked!” Nutmeg cheered. “I made a choice. I tried a plan. I solved the problem.”

Bluejay smiled. “You used your own ideas first. That is self-reliance. And you used teamwork to get a helpful idea.”

Nutmeg beamed. “Yes! I can do things on my own, and I can also listen and decide.”

She picked up a new blue crayon, and this time it did not roll away. She drew a circle. Round and smooth. She drew a square with four equal sides. She drew a triangle with three pointy corners. She wrote N-U-T-M-E-G at the top in big letters. The crayon felt warm in her paw. The wax smelled sweet. The paper made a soft shhh sound as she colored.

Bluejay watched. “Nice shapes,” he said.

Nutmeg giggled. “Thank you! I feel calm and proud.” She touched her chest. Her heart felt steady and happy.

When the chart was done, she taped it to the wall above her tiny table. She stepped back and looked. The sun made the colors glow. Blue, red, yellow, and green danced on the page.

Bluejay waved a wing. “I must fly,” he said. “Keep solving and keep smiling!”

“Bye, Bluejay! Thank you for the idea,” Nutmeg called. She cleaned up her study corner. She put the pebbles back in the jar, smoothed her pillow, and tucked the tape away. Then she sat and admired her work.

Nutmeg whispered, “When I Stop, See, and Solve, I can do hard things. I can choose. I can try. I can ask for ideas and decide what works.” Her tail curled like a happy question mark.

She hugged her learning chart. “I did it myself,” she said, “with a smart plan and a helpful friend.”

The End.
