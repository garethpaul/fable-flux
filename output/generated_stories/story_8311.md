# Leo Listens in the Home Office

Leo was a brave little lion cub. He lived with Mama in a cozy house. Today, Mama was working in the home office. The room was bright and sunny. The computer hummed softly. Papers rustled like leaves. The air smelled like warm tea and fresh apples.

Leo padded in on soft paws. Tap, tap, tap. His tail swished. His tummy made a tiny rumble. “Mama,” Leo said, “my tummy is talking. It says, grrrr!”

Mama smiled. “Your tummy is hungry, Leo. Let’s listen to it and feed it good food.” She pointed to a rainbow chart on the wall. “We eat colors for strength. Red, orange, green, and more.”

Leo’s eyes got big. “I want colors!” he said. “Can I have a snack now?”

Mama nodded. “Yes, brave cub. But first, listening ears.” She touched her ears. “We listen to our bodies and to each other.” Leo copied her and giggled. “Listening ears on!”

Mama opened a little basket in the office. Inside were crunchy carrot sticks, red apple slices, green cucumber rounds, and a small cup of hummus. There was also a bottle of water and a reusable napkin with tiny leaves on it.

“Wow,” Leo whispered. He sniffed. “It smells sweet like apple and fresh like the garden.”

Mama said, “These snacks help you play, learn, and grow. Crunchy carrots help your eyes. Apples give you energy. Cucumber is cool and juicy. Hummus helps your muscles.”

Leo picked up a carrot stick. Crunch! “I am strong!” he said. He dipped it in hummus. “Mmm. I am brave and strong!”

Just then, a small sound came from the trash bin. Rustle, rustle. Leo tilted his head. “Mama, did you hear that?”

Mama listened. “I hear it too. Let’s use quiet feet.”

They looked inside the bin. A little paper ball lay on top. Under it, a tiny ant was caught in a sticky bit of tape. The ant wiggled and tapped the bin with its tiny feet. Tap, tap.

“Oh no!” Leo gasped. “The ant is stuck. That is not fair.” His whiskers drooped. “We must help. Justice means fair for everyone.”

Mama nodded. “Yes, Leo. Helping is right. Let’s be gentle and kind.”

Leo took a deep breath. “I will listen to the ant with my eyes and ears.” He looked closely. “The tape is sticky.” He remembered how Mama said to protect nature. “We care for the Earth and its tiny friends.”

Mama said, “First, let’s make a soft place.” She put a clean scrap of paper on the desk. “Now, a tiny drop of water to loosen the tape.” She squeezed a drop from the bottle. Plip.

Leo held the ant’s tiny corner with a leaf from a plant trimmings cup. He whispered, “It’s okay, little friend. We are here.” The water glimmered. The tape let go. The ant wriggled free!

“Hooray!” Leo cheered. The ant crawled onto the clean paper. It tickled Leo’s paw. He giggled. “That feels like tiny footsteps!”

Mama smiled. “You listened and helped. That is brave and just.”

Leo looked at the sticky tape. “Sticky trash can hurt. We should put it in the right place.” Mama handed him a small box labeled Recycle and another labeled Trash. There was also a little bin labeled Compost.

Leo pointed. “Apple cores go in compost to feed the Earth. Paper can be recycled. Sticky tape goes in trash.” He placed each item carefully. “We care for our home and the Earth.”

His tummy rumbled again. “Grrr!” Leo laughed. “Oops! More colors, please.” He ate a red apple slice. Crunch! Sweet juice ran down his chin. He wiped it with the leaf-pattern napkin. “Cold and yummy!”

Mama said, “Water sip?” Leo nodded. Gulp, gulp. “Water helps our bodies work well.”

A soft breeze came through the window. It smelled like grass. Leo closed his eyes. “I feel calm,” he said. “I listened to my tummy. I listened to the ant. I listened to you.”

Mama hugged him. “You did three good things today. You chose healthy foods. You cared for nature. You made things fair for a tiny friend.”

Leo’s ears perked up. “And I used my listening ears!”

Mama chuckled. “Yes, brave cub. Listening helps us learn. Good nutrition gives us energy to help and play. Caring for the Earth keeps our world safe.”

Leo stood tall on his paws. “I am Leo the Listener,” he said. “I eat colors, drink water, sort my trash, and help friends.”

The computer hummed. The papers were neat. The little ant crawled out the window and back to the garden, safe and free.

Leo finished his snack and patted his belly. “Thank you, carrots. Thank you, apple. Thank you, water.” He waved at the window. “Bye-bye, little ant! Be safe!”

Mama kissed his head. “Time for a story break,” she said.

Leo nodded. “Yes! And later, I will help set the table with veggies.”

They held hands and walked out of the home office. The room felt bright and happy. Leo’s heart felt full.

He whispered, “Listening, nutrition, and caring make a strong, kind lion.”

The End.
