# Ollie's Thank You Dinner

Ollie the otter wiggled his whiskers as he swam into the cozy dining room. The wooden table gleamed in the warm sunlight streaming through the window. His tummy rumbled loudly.

"Grumble, grumble!" went his belly. "I'm so hungry!" Ollie said, patting his round tummy with his small paws.

On the table sat a beautiful plate filled with his favorite foods: fresh fish, crunchy carrots, and sweet berries. The fish smelled like the clean river where he loved to swim. The orange carrots looked bright and cheerful, and the purple berries sparkled like tiny jewels.

Ollie's eyes grew wide. "Wow! All this food looks amazing!" He quickly climbed onto his special chair and reached for the biggest fish.

But just as his paw touched the plate, Ollie stopped. He remembered something important his mama had taught him.

"Wait," Ollie said to himself, pulling his paw back. "Mama always says to think about where our food comes from before we eat."

Ollie looked at the shiny fish. "Thank you, little fish, for swimming in the river and growing strong," he said softly. "You will help me grow big and healthy too."

He picked up a bright orange carrot and held it gently. "Thank you, carrot, for growing in the earth with your green leaves reaching toward the sun. You will make my eyes bright and help me see clearly when I dive underwater."

Then Ollie held a plump berry between his paws. "Thank you, sweet berry, for growing on the bush and getting so juicy and delicious. You will give me energy to play and swim all day long."

Ollie felt warm and happy inside. His heart felt as full as his hungry tummy wanted to be.

"And thank you to all the people who helped bring this food to my table," Ollie continued. "Thank you to the fisherman who caught the fish carefully. Thank you to the farmer who grew the carrots with love. Thank you to the person who picked the berries when they were perfectly ripe."

As Ollie spoke these words of thanks, the food seemed to smell even better. The fish looked more golden, the carrots more orange, and the berries more purple.

"Now I can eat!" Ollie said happily. He took small, careful bites, chewing slowly and tasting every flavor. The fish was tender and flaky. The carrots were sweet and crunchy. The berries burst with juice that made him smile.

"Mmm, mmm, mmm!" Ollie hummed as he ate. "Everything tastes so much better when I remember to say thank you first!"

When Ollie finished eating, he felt wonderful. His tummy was full, but more importantly, his heart felt full of gratitude. He carefully carried his empty plate to the sink, just like Mama had taught him.

"Thank you for this wonderful meal," Ollie said one more time, looking around the sunny dining room. "I am so lucky to have healthy food to eat every day."

Ollie's whiskers twitched with joy as he realized something special: when he took time to be thankful, everything in his day felt more magical and meaningful.

From that day on, Ollie always remembered to say thank you before every meal, and his food always tasted like the most delicious feast in the whole wide world.

The End.
