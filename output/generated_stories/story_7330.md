# Bear Cub on the Rooftop Garden

The sun was warm and golden on the rooftop. A friendly bear cub named Bumble padded across the soft green grass of the rooftop garden. He could smell sweet strawberries growing in a big box. Bees hummed. A light breeze tickled his nose. “Mmm,” said Bumble. “Today feels happy.”

Bumble had a small blue basket. He filled it with three big red strawberries. They were shiny and juicy. His tummy gave a tiny rumble. “I will eat one now, and save two for later,” he said. Bumble liked to plan and to share, but he also liked to keep his own things safe.

Just then, a pigeon fluttered down. Flap, flap, flap! The pigeon had soft gray feathers and bright eyes. “Hello,” cooed the pigeon. “I am Pip. Those strawberries smell nice.”

Bumble smiled. “Hi, Pip! They do smell nice.”

Pip leaned closer. “Can I have all of them?” Pip asked quickly.

Bumble blinked. His paws hugged the basket. He felt a little twist in his tummy. He wanted to be kind. He also wanted to keep his plan. He took a deep breath. The breeze felt cool on his fur. “I can share,” Bumble said softly. “But not all. One is for me now, and one is for later. I can give you one.”

Pip puffed his feathers. “Only one? I am so hungry,” he cooed.

Bumble’s ears drooped. He did not like to see a friend upset. He looked at his basket. He looked at Pip. Bumble remembered something he learned: I can be kind and keep my own plan. That is a boundary. Boundaries keep me safe and help me feel good.

Bumble tried again. “I hear you,” he said. “You are hungry. I want to help. I will share one strawberry. The other two are mine. That is my plan.” He hugged the basket gently. “This is my boundary.”

Pip shuffled his feet. “But one is small,” he cooed.

Bumble felt a little pinch in his chest. He did not want to argue. He took another slow breath. In and out. The air smelled like mint from the garden. “I can make the one special,” said Bumble. “We can cut it and make it fancy. We can add mint leaves. We can eat together on the soft blanket.” Bumble’s eyes brightened. “And I can show you how to grow more strawberries. Then there will be many!”

Pip tilted his head. “Grow more?”

“Yes,” said Bumble with a smile. “We can plant seeds. That is a growth mindset. We can learn. We can try. We can make more.”

Pip looked at the basket, then at Bumble’s kind eyes. He cooed more softly. “Okay. One strawberry. Fancy. And learn to grow.”

Bumble clapped his paws. “Thank you for understanding.” His heart felt warm. He found a little wooden board. He asked, “Pip, do you agree to wait while I cut?” Pip nodded and kept his wings tucked. Bumble felt safe. His boundary was respected.

With a small child-safe knife, Bumble cut the strawberry into nice red slices. Juices sparkled in the sun. He placed the slices on a leaf plate. He added two tiny mint leaves. “Smell,” he said.

Pip sniffed. “Mmm! Fresh and sweet.”

They sat together on a soft blue blanket. Bumble tasted a slice. It was cool and juicy. Pip took a slice and cooed happily. “Thank you for sharing,” Pip said. “It tastes better together.”

After their snack, Bumble showed Pip the planter box. The dirt felt cool and crumbly under Bumble’s paws. “We make a little hole,” Bumble said. “We drop the seeds in. We cover them. We give them water and sun. We try, and we try again.”

Pip pecked gently and helped. Drip, drip, drip went the watering can. The water smelled like rain. Bumble smiled. “We will have more strawberries. It may take time. That is okay.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “I like learning. I can wait.”

Later, a small squirrel named Squeak popped up from behind a pot. “Hi!” squeaked Squeak. “I saw your strawberries. Can I have all of them?”

Bumble remembered his plan. He stood tall and calm. “I can share one,” he said kindly. “The others are mine. That is my boundary.”

Squeak looked at Pip. Pip nodded. “Bumble shares and also keeps his plan. We can grow more!” Pip chirped.

Squeak’s eyes shone. “Okay! One now, and we grow more.” They all smiled.

They made another little plate with one strawberry slice for Squeak, and they planted more seeds together. The breeze sang. The bees buzzed. The sun felt soft and warm on their fur and feathers.

Bumble felt proud. He had shared. He had kept his boundary. He had helped friends learn to grow. Together, they had found a kind solution.

That night, the rooftop was quiet and cozy. Bumble curled up with his basket safe by his side. He whispered, “Sharing feels good. Boundaries keep me safe. Learning makes more.” His heart felt full like a ripe strawberry.

The End.
