# Rocco Raccoon and the Fair Fix

Rocco was a musical raccoon. He wore a tiny red hat and carried a shiny silver tambourine. On sunny days, he played happy beats in the sunny backyard. The grass tickled his toes. The flowers smelled sweet, like honey. Birds chirped along, “Tweet-tweet!”

“Tam-ta-tam!” Rocco shook his tambourine. “Music time!” he sang.

One bright morning, Rocco saw something that made his whiskers wiggle. A pile of cups, wrappers, and crinkly bottles lay by the sandbox. The sandbox was messy. The sun sparkled on the plastic, and the wind made the wrappers whisper, “Whooo.”

Rocco put his paws on his hips. “This is not fair,” he said. “Someone made a mess, and that is not just. Everyone should share the clean yard.”

His friend Bella Bluebird fluttered down. “Tweet? What happened, Rocco?”

“There is trash by the sandbox,” Rocco said. “It is not right. It is not fair to the yard or to us.”

Rocco felt big feelings in his chest. He wanted to shout, “Who did this?” But he remembered his mama’s words: “Justice means we listen, we ask, and we make things right. We are kind and fair.”

Rocco took a deep breath. He shook his tambourine softly. “Tam… tam.” The sound felt calm, like a warm hug.

Milo Mouse peeked from under the slide. “I smell banana peels,” he squeaked. “I don’t like stinky piles.”

Rocco nodded. “Me too. Let’s find out what happened. We can talk. We can be fair.”

They looked around. They heard the hose drip, drip, drip. They smelled soap from the garden sink. They saw the bright blue recycle bin by the fence.

Just then, Gigi Goat trotted in with a cardboard box. “Hi, friends! I brought craft stuff,” she said, smiling. She spotted the messy pile and her ears drooped. “Oh no. I think that is from my picnic. I was in a hurry. I dropped the box, and the wind whooshed the cups away. I meant to clean up, but I ran to find tape. Then I forgot. I’m sorry.”

Rocco felt his shoulders relax. He was glad he asked. He was glad Gigi could speak. Justice likes the truth.

“Thank you for telling us, Gigi,” Rocco said. “We all use this yard. It needs to be fair and clean for everyone.”

Gigi nodded. “You are right. I want to fix it. Can I make it right?”

“Yes,” said Rocco. “Let’s do it together.” He tapped his tambourine in a happy rhythm. “Clean-up beat!”

“Tam-ta-tam! Tam-ta-tam!” The friends moved to the music.

Bella picked up paper. “Paper goes in here,” she chirped, dropping it in the blue recycle bin.

Milo gathered bottles with tiny clinks. “Plastic in the bin!” he squeaked.

Gigi tied a bag for the banana peels. “Food goes to compost,” she said. The bag smelled a little icky, but the garden worms would like it.

Rocco used a little broom. “Sweep-sweep,” he sang. The sand felt soft under his paws. The sun felt warm on his back. A butterfly landed on his red hat. He giggled.

Soon, the yard was tidy. The sandbox was smooth. Everyone smiled.

“Next,” Rocco said, pointing to the garden sink, “we wash our paws. Hygiene helps us stay healthy.”

They lined up. The water was cool. The soap smelled like lemons. “Rub-rub, rinse-rinse,” Rocco sang. “Clean paws dance!”

Gigi looked at the ground and then back at her friends. “Thank you for being fair,” she said. “You did not shout. You asked me what happened, and you listened.”

Rocco nodded. “Justice means we listen to everyone. We do not blame fast. We find the truth. Then we fix it together.”

Bella flapped her wings. “And we talk. Communication helps!”

Milo twitched his whiskers. “And we recycle and keep the yard clean!”

Rocco smiled wide. “We did all of that. I feel proud.”

Gigi reached into her craft box. “I have an idea,” she said. She held up a big piece of cardboard and bright markers. “Let’s make a sign. It can help everyone remember.”

They drew together. Rocco drew a blue bin with smiling bottles. Bella drew a hand washing under drops of water. Milo drew a big ear to show listening. Gigi wrote the words, slow and clear:

“Be fair. Ask. Listen. Recycle. Wash hands.”

Rocco hung the sign by the fence. The wind fluttered it like a flag. The letters shone in the sun.

To celebrate, Rocco shook his tambourine. “Tam-ta-TAH!” He sang, “Justice is fair for me and you! We listen, speak, and clean up, too!”

Everyone clapped. The birds chirped along. The backyard felt bright and kind.

Gigi smiled. “I learned today. When I make a mess, I can say sorry and fix it. That is fair.”

Rocco patted her shoulder. “We all make mistakes. Justice helps us make it right.”

The friends played in the clean sandbox. They built a tall sand castle with a little blue-flag leaf on top. The castle sparkled in the sun, just like Rocco’s tambourine.

Rocco looked around and felt happy. The yard was clean. His friends felt safe. Their voices felt heard. That was justice.

The End.
