# Tilly the Turtle Finds Her Beat

Tilly was a shy turtle with a smooth green shell. She liked quiet. She liked gentle. But one day, her class went to the musical practice room. The room was full of soft drums, shiny bells, and a big black piano. There were mirrors on the wall and rainbow scarves in a basket.

Tilly peeked in and felt her cheeks grow warm. The room smelled like fresh wood and a little like lemons. The floor felt cool under her feet. She heard a low hum from the piano. “Thum, thum,” went the big drum when someone tapped it. It sounded like a heartbeat.

Miss Lark, the teacher, smiled. “Today, we dance to music,” she said. “We move with our feelings. We can be slow like turtles, or quick like mice!”

Tilly pulled her head into her shell just a little. “I’m not quick,” she whispered.

Her friend Pip, a small mouse with soft whiskers, squeaked, “That’s okay, Tilly. Your slow can be special.”

Miss Lark played a gentle tune on the piano. It sounded like raindrops. The notes made tiny sparkly tingles in the air. The children began to move. Pip did little hops. Lina the bunny twirled with a rainbow scarf. Tilly watched. Her tummy felt twisty.

“I want to try,” Tilly told herself softly. “But I feel shy.”

Miss Lark noticed. She walked over and sat on the floor beside Tilly. “How do you feel, Tilly?” she asked in a soft voice.

Tilly took a deep breath. “I feel small,” she said. “My heart is thumping. I don’t know what to do.”

Miss Lark nodded. “Thank you for telling me. That’s brave. When I feel shy, I begin with a tiny move. Would you like to try a tiny turtle move?”

Tilly looked at her feet. “Maybe,” she said.

Miss Lark picked up a thin ribbon from the basket. It was sea-green like Tilly’s shell. “Let’s make ocean waves,” Miss Lark said. “Slow and smooth.”

Tilly held the ribbon. It was cool and silky. She lifted her arm, just a little. The ribbon made a soft swoosh. Swoosh. The sound was sweet and quiet.

“That’s it,” Miss Lark said. “Can you sway, side to side, like sea grass?”

Tilly swayed, just a little. Sway, sway. Her shell felt heavy but safe. The piano notes tickled the room like rain. Tilly thought, My slow is okay. My slow is me.

Pip hopped over. “Can I sway with you?” he asked.

Tilly nodded. “Yes,” she said, a small smile peeking out. Pip held a blue ribbon. Together, they made tiny waves. Swoosh, swoosh.

Miss Lark changed the music. The tune grew brighter, like sun on water. “Now we try a turtle step,” she said. “Step, slide. Step, slide. Listen for the drum. Thum… thum…”

Tilly listened. Thum. She stepped her foot. Thum. She slid her other foot. Step, slide. Step, slide. It felt like walking on soft sand. The mirrors showed her moving slowly and steady. She saw her own smile growing.

Lina twirled in and giggled. “Look at Tilly’s turtle dance!” she said. “It’s smooth!”

Tilly felt warm in a new way. Not scared-warm. Happy-warm. “I can dance in my way,” she said.

Then Miss Lark tapped the big drum faster. Thum-thum! Thum-thum! The room buzzed with energy. Some friends moved quick. Tilly’s heart sped up again. She paused. “This is too fast for me,” she thought. “What can I do?”

She looked at the drums. She looked at the scarves. She looked at her ribbon. Tilly had an idea. “I can make a turtle beat,” she thought. “Slow and steady. I can dance to that.”

She went to a small hand drum and picked it up. It felt smooth and warm. She tapped it gently. Thum… thum… thum… Slow and even. Then she moved her feet to her own beat. Step, slide. Step, slide. Swoosh went the ribbon, like sea waves.

Miss Lark clapped softly. “Tilly, that is resourceful,” she said. “You made your own steady beat.”

Pip tapped with her on a bell. Ding… ding… He matched Tilly’s slow rhythm. Lina slowed her twirl and waved a scarf like a turtle fin. Soon, the whole class tried the turtle dance. The room filled with slow, kind sounds. Thum… ding… swoosh…

Tilly stood a bit taller. “I feel calm,” she said to Miss Lark. “I feel brave.”

“You listened to your feelings,” Miss Lark said. “You found a way to dance that works for you. That is smart. That is strong.”

At the end, the music softened to a whisper. The room was quiet and cozy. Everyone sat in a circle. Tilly’s shell glowed in the light.

Miss Lark asked, “What did we learn today?”

Pip raised his paw. “We can dance slow or fast,” he said. “Both are good!”

Lina nodded. “We can help friends by dancing with them.”

Tilly placed her hand on her shell. “When I feel shy,” she said, “I can take a breath. I can start tiny. I can make my own beat. My way is okay.”

Miss Lark smiled. “Dance is for everyone,” she said. “Dance can show feelings. Dance can help us grow.”

Tilly smiled back. She looked at the mirror. She saw a shy turtle who tried, who listened, and who moved with heart. She knew she could dance again tomorrow, slow and steady, brave and true.

The End.
