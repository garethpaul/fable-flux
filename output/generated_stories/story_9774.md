# Ollie the Otter Leads the Backyard Parade

Ollie was a playful otter with bright eyes and a swishy tail. He lived near a sunny backyard with soft green grass, warm light, and a friendly breeze. Bees buzzed. A sprinkler hissed and made tiny rainbows. The air smelled like oranges and fresh soil.

One morning, Ollie clapped his little paws. “Let’s have a backyard parade!” he cheered. His friends looked up.

Mina the cat, with jingling bells on her collar, purred, “A parade? I can bring music.” Ting-ting went her tiny bell.

Ravi the rabbit wiggled his nose. “I can hop high and carry flags,” he said.

Lina the ladybug fluttered. “I can make a dance in the air!”

Tariq the turtle peeked out. “I am slow,” he said softly, “but I can carry a sign.”

Ollie smiled. “We can all help. I will be the leader. A leader listens and helps everyone do their best.”

They began to plan. The sun warmed their backs. The grass tickled their feet. “What song should we play?” Mina asked.

“I like drums,” said Ravi, thumping a bucket. Boom, boom.

“I like soft chimes,” whispered Lina, tapping a spoon on a glass. Tinkle, tinkle.

“I like a deep hum,” said Tariq, humming low. Mmmmmm.

Ollie heard different sounds. He felt a flutter in his tummy. “There are many sounds,” he said, “from many places. We can make a world song.”

“What is a world song?” asked Ravi.

Ollie’s whiskers twitched. “A song with pieces from different places. We can share and take turns. We can listen to each other. That is good leadership.”

Mina nodded. “We can start with chimes, then drums, then a hum.”

“And I can clap!” said Ollie. Clap, clap!

They tried. Tinkle, boom, mmmm, clap. But soon everyone played at once. It got too loud. The bees buzzed away. The sprinkler hissed louder as if to say shhh.

Ravi stamped. “I can’t hear my drum!”

Lina drooped. “My chime is lost.”

Tariq tucked in his head. “It is too much for me.”

Ollie took a deep breath. He remembered: a leader stays calm. He looked at each friend. “I hear you,” he said kindly. “Let’s make a plan. We will use hand signs. When I hold up one paw, Mina chimes. Two paws, Ravi drums. Three paws, Tariq hums. When I swish my tail, we all clap.”

Mina smiled. “That is a fair plan. We can take turns.”

Ollie also thought of the parade line. “Some of us are fast. Some of us are slow. We can make a middle speed so we stay together. We can walk, hop, flutter, and roll in a line.”

Tariq peeked out. “Thank you for thinking of me,” he said. “I will carry the sign that says, ‘We All Belong.’”

They tried again. Ollie lifted one paw. Tinkle, tinkle. He lifted two paws. Boom, boom. He lifted three paws. Mmmmmm. He swished his tail. Clap, clap! The sounds took turns and danced together. The air felt happy again.

“Now costumes!” said Mina. She brought ribbons with many colors—red, blue, gold, and green. “These ribbons come from my grandma in a faraway city,” she said. “They are special to me.”

Ravi brought tiny flags from his family. “This one is from where my uncle was born,” he said. “This one is from where my aunt lives now.”

Lina dotted a leaf with paint. “These dots are like my wings.”

Tariq found a small drum from his cousins. “They taught me this rhythm.” Mmm-mm, mmm-mm.

Ollie listened and smiled. “We are sharing pieces of our homes. We are learning about the big world in our sunny backyard.”

They lined up. Ollie at the front, Mina behind, Ravi hopping next, Lina fluttering above, and Tariq rolling slowly with the sign. The sun warmed their backs. The grass brushed their toes. The sprinkler made a tiny rainbow over their heads.

“Ready?” asked Ollie. His heart thumped. He felt brave and kind. He held up one paw. Tinkle. Two paws. Boom. Three paws. Mmmmm. Tail swish. Clap!

Neighbors peeked over the fence. Mrs. Park waved. “What a lovely parade!”

Mr. Silva smiled. “I hear many sounds. It feels like the whole world.”

A little chick from next door chirped, “Can I join?”

Ollie nodded. “Yes! We take turns. Follow the signs.”

The chick joined the line and shook a small rattle. Shaka-shaka. Ollie added a new sign: a nose wiggle for the rattle. Everyone watched and learned.

They moved together, not too fast, not too slow. Ravi made room for Tariq. Mina paused so Lina could swoop. The chick listened for the nose wiggle. Shaka-shaka at just the right time.

At the end, they circled the big oak tree. They took a bow. The neighbors clapped. Clap, clap, clap.

Ollie looked at his friends. “We did it,” he said. “We listened. We shared. We took turns. We stayed together.”

Tariq smiled. “You were a good leader, Ollie. You helped us work as one.”

Mina purred, “Our parade had many colors and many sounds.”

Ravi wiggled his whiskers. “We learned to make space for each other.”

Lina sparkled in the sun. “We made a world song in our backyard.”

Ollie felt warm and proud. “When we lead with kind hearts, we all belong, and everyone can shine.”

The End.
