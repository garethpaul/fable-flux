# Mango the Brave in the Garden Shed

Mango the monkey was small, brave, and very curious. One sunny morning, Mango tiptoed to the garden shed. The wooden door smelled like warm tree bark. The handle felt cool and smooth. “Hello, little shed,” Mango whispered. “May I come in?”

Creak! The door opened. Inside, the air was dusty and sweet, like dry leaves and flowers. Sunlight made little sparkles in the floating dust. Tools hung on hooks like shiny silver fish. A red watering can sat on the floor. A blue broom leaned in the corner.

Mango’s tummy fluttered. “I can do brave things,” Mango said. “I will help in the garden!”

Mango grabbed the red watering can. Slosh, slosh. Water splashed on Mango’s toes. “Oh! Cold!” Mango giggled. Then Mango tried to lift a big bag of seeds. The bag was heavy. “Unh!” Mango pulled. “Unh!” Mango pushed. The bag did not move.

Mango felt a pinch of frustration. The pinch felt hot like a little spark. Mango’s tail twitched. “This is hard,” Mango said, frowning.

Just then, a tiny mouse peeked from a box. “Squeak! Hello. I am Pip. Do you need help?”

Mango took a deep breath. In. Out. The spark got softer. “Hi, Pip. I am Mango. I want to carry the seeds, but it is too heavy.”

Pip looked at the bag. Pip looked at the broom. “We can find a peaceful way,” Pip said. “Maybe we can use the broom like a lever.”

“A lever?” Mango blinked. “What is that?”

“It is a helper stick,” Pip said. “It helps lift things.”

Mango smiled. “Let’s try.”

They slid the broom under the bag. Mango pressed down on the other end. Creak… lift! The bag wiggled. “We did it!” Mango cheered. A sprinkle of seeds spilled and made a soft patter on the floor. It sounded like rain on a roof.

Mango scooped the seeds into a bowl. “Thank you, broom,” Mango said. “Thank you, Pip.”

Pip’s whiskers twitched. “You’re welcome!”

They set the seeds by the door. Mango puffed out a breath. “I still feel a little grumpy inside,” Mango said. “I wanted to do it all by myself.”

Pip nodded. “It’s okay to feel grumpy. Let’s listen to the sounds in the shed.”

They were quiet. Drip, drip, drip went a leaky tap. Buzz went a sleepy fly. The wood went creak, like a gentle song. Mango’s tight feelings got softer. “Listening helps,” Mango said.

Mango looked around and saw a tangled green hose like a big snake. “Oh no. The hose is stuck.”

Mango tugged. Tug, tug. The hose tightened. Mango’s face got hot. “Grrr,” Mango growled. Mango stopped. “Wait. I need a calm plan.”

Mango put a hand on the heart. Tap, tap. “I am safe. I can try again,” Mango said softly. “I am thankful for my brave body. I am thankful for Pip.”

Pip smiled. “And I am thankful for you.”

They worked together. Pip held one loop. Mango lifted another. Slide, slide. The hose came free. “Yes!” Mango clapped. The rubber felt smooth and cool. The hose smelled like fresh rain.

Mango and Pip carried the hose to the door. The garden outside waited with bright flowers and wet soil. Birds sang, “Tweet, tweet!”

“Let’s water the flowers,” Mango said. Mango turned the tap. Whoosh! Water arched like a rainbow. Drops sparkled on Mango’s fur. “Brrr! Cold!” Mango laughed. Pip danced in the spray. The flowers nodded as if to say, “Thank you.”

When the watering was done, Mango and Pip went back into the shed. Mango swept the floor with the blue broom. Swish, swish. Dust bunnies ran away. Mango put the tools back on their hooks. Clink, clink. The shed smelled clean and leafy.

Mango placed a little note on a shelf. It said, “Thank you, shed.” Mango patted the wooden wall. “You keep the tools safe and dry. I am grateful.”

Pip tilted a tiny head. “You found many things to thank today.”

Mango counted on fingers. “I am thankful for you, Pip. I am thankful for the broom. I am thankful for my breath that helps me calm down. I am thankful for water and flowers. I am thankful for my brave heart.”

Pip clapped tiny paws. “Gratitude makes the day bright.”

Mango nodded. “When I felt frustrated, I stopped, I breathed, and I used a peaceful plan. Being thankful helped me feel strong again.”

They stepped outside. Warm sun hugged their faces. A butterfly floated by. Mango waved. “Hello, butterfly!” The wings looked like a tiny painted fan.

Pip said, “Let’s have a snack.”

They shared a banana on the garden bench. The banana was sweet and soft. Mango licked sticky fingers and giggled. “Today I learned that big problems can be solved with calm, kind ideas. And saying ‘thank you’ helps my heart feel happy.”

Pip smiled. “That is a brave lesson.”

Mango looked back at the shed and whispered, “Thank you, thank you, thank you.”

The End.
