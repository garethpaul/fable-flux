# Gigi Listens with Her Long Ears

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with soft brown spots and very long ears. Today, she stepped into the creative art room. It smelled like fresh paint and clean paper. Sunlight made warm, golden stripes on the floor. Brushes stood like tiny trees in cups, and colorful crayons lay in neat rows. Gigi’s heart felt bouncy with happy excitement.

“Good morning, class,” said Miss Dot, a kind lady with a polka-dot apron. Her voice was gentle like a song. “Today we will make a big Friendly Forest mural together. We will paint, glue, and add words that show how we feel.”

Gigi’s ears perked up. She loved words. She loved the way words sounded. She whispered, “Friendly Forest,” and tasted the soft F sound on her tongue. “F-f-friendly,” she said with a smile.

Miss Dot clapped softly. “First, we listen. Listening helps us know what to do. Then, we speak with kind, strong voices.”

Gigi nodded. Listening was the main job, she told herself. Listen first, then speak.

Miss Dot gave directions. “We will have three teams. Paint Team will make the tall trees. Paper Team will cut green leaves. Word Team will write kind words to hang in the forest.”

Gigi listened with her whole body. She kept her eyes on Miss Dot. She kept her ears open. She kept her mouth quiet. The room hummed with quiet sounds—paper swish, brush tap, crayon scratch.

“Gigi, you are on Word Team,” said Miss Dot. “You will write and say words that make hearts feel safe and brave.”

Gigi felt warm and proud. “Yes, Miss Dot,” she said. “I will listen and choose good words.”

At the Word Table, Gigi found soft markers that smelled like berries. She sniffed, giggled, and chose a blueberry-blue marker. Her friend Benny the bunny hopped over. “Let’s write big words!” Benny said. “Like ‘ROAR!’”

Gigi tilted her head. She remembered: listen first. “What words do we need?” she asked. She looked over at Miss Dot and listened again.

Miss Dot said, “Choose words like please, thank you, hello, and sorry. Words that tell the truth. Words that help us listen.”

Gigi turned to Benny. “Let’s write ‘please’ first,” she said. “Please is a soft, friendly word.” She wrote slowly: p-l-e-a-s-e. The letters felt smooth under her hoof. “Please,” she said out loud, and the word sounded like a gentle hug.

Benny nodded. “Okay! And ‘thank you’ next.” He held the paper while Gigi wrote. “Thank you,” Gigi whispered, and she felt a warm glow.

Suddenly, Zig the zebra zoomed by. “Move! I need the blue marker!” he neighed. He grabbed at Gigi’s marker.

Gigi pulled the marker close. She felt a tiny twist in her tummy. She wanted to be kind, but she also wanted to be brave and fair. She took a slow breath and remembered: listen first, then speak.

She looked at Zig’s face. His eyes were wide and rushed. “Zig,” Gigi said softly, “I hear you want the blue marker. I am using it right now. Please wait, or we can take turns.”

Zig stomped once. “I need it now for the sky!”

Gigi stood tall, but her voice stayed gentle. “I will finish this word, then you can use it,” she said. “That is fair.”

Miss Dot came closer. “What good listening, Gigi,” she said. “You heard what Zig wanted. You also used your strong, kind voice. That is called being assertive.”

Zig’s ears drooped. “I’m sorry,” he said. “I did not listen. I grabbed.”

Gigi smiled. “Thank you for telling the truth,” she said. “You can have the marker after I write ‘sorry.’” She carefully wrote s-o-r-r-y. “Sorry,” she said, and the word felt like a soft blanket.

Zig waited. When Gigi finished, she passed the marker to him. “Here you go.”

“Thank you,” said Zig. “I will bring it back.”

As the mural grew, the room filled with happy sounds: “Please pass the glue,” “Thank you,” “Excuse me,” “Hello,” and “I can wait.” The Friendly Forest came to life—tall painted trees, a paper river that swished when you waved it, and bright leaves with brave, honest words.

At the end, Miss Dot gathered everyone. The mural covered the wall, green and blue and full of kind words. It looked like it could sing.

“Class,” said Miss Dot, “what did we learn?”

Gigi’s ears wiggled. She raised her head. “We learned to listen first,” she said. “Listening helps us know what others need. Then we use our voices to be kind and strong. We tell the truth. We take turns.”

Benny added, “And words can make hearts feel safe!”

Zig nodded. “I listened and said sorry.”

Miss Dot smiled wide. “Yes. Listening, assertiveness, and integrity—telling the truth and doing the right thing—make our art and our hearts bright.”

Gigi looked at the mural. She whispered the words like a song. “Please. Thank you. Sorry. Hello.” The sounds felt sweet and brave on her tongue. She liked how they sounded. She liked how they helped.

Gigi pressed a leaf with the word listen at the very center of the forest. “This is our heart leaf,” she said. “We listen first.”

Everyone clapped. The room smelled like berries and paint. The Friendly Forest seemed to rustle with happy, honest wind.

Gigi’s heart felt calm and proud. She had listened. She had used a clear, kind voice. She had told the truth. And together, they made something beautiful.

The End.
