# Milo's Story Circle

Milo the monkey swung into the cozy nursery. The soft blue walls were covered with pictures of animals and rainbows. Colorful books filled the shelves, and a big round rug sat in the middle of the room.

"Story time!" called Miss Sarah, the nursery teacher. All the little children sat in a circle on the fluffy rug.

Milo loved stories, but today he felt different. He noticed that Emma was sitting by herself in the corner. Her eyes looked sad, and she wasn't joining the circle.

"Once upon a time," Miss Sarah began reading about a brave dragon.

But Milo couldn't focus. He kept looking at Emma. She was new to the nursery and seemed lonely.

Milo raised his furry hand. "Miss Sarah, can I help Emma feel better?"

Miss Sarah smiled. "That's very kind, Milo. What would you like to do?"

Milo thought carefully. He remembered how stories always made him happy when he felt sad. "Can I tell Emma a special story?"

"That's a wonderful idea," said Miss Sarah.

Milo walked over to Emma. His feet made soft padding sounds on the carpet. "Hi Emma," he said gently. "Would you like to hear a story?"

Emma looked up with big, curious eyes. "What kind of story?"

"A story about making friends," Milo said, sitting down beside her. "Once there was a little elephant who felt scared on her first day at a new place."

Emma's face brightened a little. "What happened to the elephant?"

"Well," Milo continued, "a friendly monkey came over and shared his favorite banana with her. Soon, all the other animals wanted to be friends too!"

Emma giggled softly. "That's a nice story."

"Would you like to join our story circle?" Milo asked. "We can sit together."

Emma nodded. Hand in hand, they walked to the circle. The other children scooted over to make room.

"Welcome, Emma!" said Tommy.

"We're glad you're here," added Lucy.

Miss Sarah continued reading, but now Emma was smiling. She clapped when the dragon saved the day and laughed at the funny parts.

After story time, Emma hugged Milo. "Thank you for helping me feel brave," she said.

"That's what friends do," Milo replied, his chest puffing up with pride.

Miss Sarah gathered everyone around. "Milo showed us something very important today. When we see someone who needs help, we can use our words and kindness to make them feel better."

"Like community helpers!" said Tommy.

"Exactly," said Miss Sarah. "We can all help our nursery community by being kind and including everyone."

From that day on, Milo made it his special job to look out for new children. Every morning, he would check if anyone looked lonely or scared. He would share stories, offer hugs, and always make sure everyone felt included.

Emma became one of his best friends. Together, they started a "Welcome Committee" to help other new children feel at home.

The nursery became an even happier place because Milo learned that helping others made his own heart feel warm and fuzzy inside.

"Helping others is the best feeling ever," Milo told his mom that evening.

And every day after that, Milo continued to use his courage and kind words to make the nursery a better place for everyone.

The End.
