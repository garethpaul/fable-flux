# Tiko the Wise Tortoise Tries New Fuel

In a quiet house, down creaky wooden steps, there was a basement lab. The lab smelled like fresh oranges and clean soap. Little lights blinked. Bottles clinked. Papers rustled. In the middle, on a soft blue mat, lived Tiko the wise tortoise.

Tiko wore tiny round glasses. He moved slowly, but he thought carefully. He loved learning. He listened to the hum of a small fan. Whirrr. He watched bubbles in a beaker. Plop, plop.

“Good morning, lab,” Tiko said. “Today, I will test my fuel. Food is my fuel!”

On a shiny tray were choices. Crunchy carrots. Sweet strawberries. A bowl of leafy greens. A cup of cool water. There was also a crinkly bag that said, “Super Zippy Candy.”

Tiko touched his chin. “Hmm. My tummy is rumbling. I must choose with care,” he said. His shell felt warm. His heart felt brave. He wanted to make a good decision.

Just then, a tiny robot rolled in. It was round and silver with a wiggly antenna. “Beep-beep! I am Dot,” it said in a happy voice. “Experiment time! We must power the Bubble Maker!”

The Bubble Maker was big and blue. It had a red button. It made rainbow bubbles that smelled like lemons. Tiko loved the Bubble Maker.

Dot blinked. “We need energy, Tiko. What will you eat?”

Tiko looked at the choices again. The candy bag was shiny. It sparkled. It seemed easy. “Maybe candy?” he whispered.

From the shelf, a kind voice spoke. “Hello.” It was Dr. Bloom, a plant in a pot with a little label. The plant’s leaves waved. “I am full of vitamins,” Dr. Bloom said. “Candy is tasty, but it is not the best fuel for your body.”

Tiko took a slow breath. He was brave enough to listen. “I want to do my best,” he said. “But I also feel curious.”

Dot beeped softly. “We can test and learn,” the robot said. “Be brave, make a plan, and make a good choice.”

“Okay,” said Tiko. “First, I will try a little candy.” He nibbled one tiny piece. It was very sweet. His tongue tingled. “Zippy!” he said.

He pressed the Bubble Maker button. Bloop! One bubble came out. It wobbled and popped. The machine made a tired sound. Brrrr… “Oh,” said Tiko. “That didn’t last long.”

Tiko listened to his tummy. It felt jumpy, not strong. “This fuel is too quick,” he said.

Dot nodded. “Data collected,” Dot said. “Quick sugar, quick stop.”

Tiko touched the carrots. They were crisp and orange. Crunch! “These taste fresh,” he said. He tried leafy greens. They tasted cool and earthy. He ate strawberries. They were juicy and bright red. “Mmm,” he said, licking his lips. He took sips of water. Sip, sip. “Ahh. My body feels happy.”

“Now press the button,” Dr. Bloom said kindly.

Tiko pressed the big red button. Whoosh! The Bubble Maker sang. La-la-la! Bubbles floated up like tiny moons. They smelled like lemons and sunshine. The bubbles were strong. They did not pop right away. They drifted and gleamed.

Dot spun in a circle. “Success! Steady energy!”

Tiko smiled. He felt calm. He felt brave. He had made a smart choice. He looked at the tray and nodded. “Real food gives me power,” he said. “Colors help, too. Orange carrots, green leaves, red strawberries, and clear water!”

Just then, a light flickered. The room went a little dark. Click. The fan slowed. The Bubble Maker hummed low. Tiko blinked.

“We have a problem,” Dot said. “The power is low.”

Tiko’s heart thumped. Thump, thump. He felt a little scared. But he stood tall in his shell. “I can be brave,” he told himself. “I will think and choose.”

He saw a small hand-pump on the floor. It had a big handle. It was heavy, but not too heavy. Tiko took a deep breath. He ate two more bites of greens and one more crunchy carrot. Crunch, crunch. He sipped water. Sip.

“I am fueling my body,” he said. “Now I can help.”

He waddled to the pump. Step, step. He pushed the handle. Push. Push. The pump made a sound like hooosh. A little green light turned on. Dot cheered. “You did it!”

Tiko pressed the red button again. The Bubble Maker filled the room with soft, lemony bubbles. Dot and Dr. Bloom clapped their leaves and little robot arms.

Tiko laughed. “My body works better when I choose good food. Candy is a treat, but fruits, veggies, and water help me do big jobs.”

Dot recorded the lesson. “Note: Wise tortoise used brave heart and good choices. Result: strong body, steady bubbles, happy lab.”

Tiko looked around the cozy basement lab. He felt proud. He shared a strawberry with Dot. He misted Dr. Bloom’s leaves with water. Spritz, spritz. The lab smelled fresh and bright.

Tiko said, “Today I learned: I can be brave. I can think. I can choose foods that help me grow and go.”

Dr. Bloom rustled. “That is wisdom.”

Dot beeped. “That is power.”

Tiko nodded. “That is health.”

He snuggled onto his soft blue mat. The fan hummed again. Whirrr. A last bubble floated by. Pop!

Tiko whispered, “Good choices make me strong.”

The End.
