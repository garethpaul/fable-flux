# Tippy Bird and the Big Little List

In a cozy bedroom with soft yellow walls, sun stripes peeked through the curtains. A small fan hummed. The room smelled like clean sheets and warm toast. On the dresser sat a tiny paper list with big words and little checkboxes.

“Good morning!” sang Tippy, a bright blue bird who lived on the windowsill. Tippy loved to sing. He chirped, “Tweet tweet! Today is for sweet, neat feats!”

On the bed, Mia rubbed her eyes. “Hi, Tippy,” she said, smiling. “We have a list today.”

Tippy bounced along the window latch. “A list? Oh, I like lists! They go one, two, three!”

Mia held up the paper. “Today we will help Grandma with a food basket for the community center.” She pointed to the boxes. “It says: eat a healthy breakfast, make the bed, pack good foods, be honest, and deliver the basket.”

Tippy’s feathers puffed. He felt excited and a tiny bit nervous. “That is a big little list,” he sang softly. “We can do it.”

First: breakfast. The cozy bedroom smelled like oatmeal and bananas from the kitchen. Mia brought a small bowl to her room and sat on the rug. “Would you like to watch, Tippy?”

Tippy nodded. “Healthy helps us help,” he sang.

Mia took a bite of oatmeal and a slice of banana. “Mmm, warm and sweet.” She chewed slowly. Tippy pecked at a single oat that had dropped. “Crunch!” he chirped. “Nutri-nice!”

Mia giggled. “Nutrition helps us be strong.”

Tippy fluffed his wings. Inside, he felt a warm, steady beat. Eating first made his song feel brighter. “Check,” he sang. Mia put a checkmark by “eat a healthy breakfast.”

Next: make the bed. The sheets were soft and cool. The blanket was fuzzy like a cloud. Tippy tugged a corner with his beak while Mia smoothed the top. “Pull, puff, pat!” Tippy sang. They pressed the pillow with their hands and wings. The bed looked neat and calm.

“Check!” Mia marked the box.

Tippy looked at the next line. “Pack good foods.” On the floor sat a wicker basket. It smelled like clean straw. Mia collected apples, a bag of rice, a can of beans, and a small jar of peanut butter.

Tippy looked at a shiny wrapper in the corner of the nightstand. It was a candy bar. His eyes sparkled. “Shiny,” he whispered. He wanted to put it in the basket. It would be quick. It would make the basket look full. He hopped over and nudged it with his beak.

Mia watched. “Tippy,” she said softly, “is candy the best food for a family who needs good meals?”

Tippy’s wings drooped. He felt a little twist in his tummy. He liked sweet. But the list said “good foods.” He took a deep breath. The fan hummed. The room felt quiet. Tippy knew the answer.

“No,” he said, his voice small. “Candy is tasty, but not good for strong bodies.” He looked up at Mia. “I tried to add it anyway. I’m sorry.”

Mia knelt down. “Thank you for telling the truth, Tippy. That is honesty.”

Tippy’s chest felt lighter. He sang a soft, brave note. “Honesty makes hearts feel sunny.”

Mia smiled and patted his head. “Let’s choose foods that help, okay?”

Together they added carrots, oatmeal packets, and a box of tea. The basket looked colorful and healthy. Red apples. Orange carrots. White rice. Brown beans. The smells were fresh and earthy.

“Check,” said Mia, tapping the list.

Now came “be honest.” Tippy stood tall and sang, “I wanted candy in the basket, but I told the truth.” Mia wrote a tiny heart by the words. “Honesty, check.”

Last: deliver the basket. The cozy bedroom door creaked open. The hallway smelled like soap and sunshine. Tippy flew low beside Mia as she carried the basket carefully.

But then—uh-oh! Tippy saw a feather duster on the chair. The bedroom had a little mess: a sock under the bed and a book on the floor. They had not picked up those small things. The list did not say “tidy toys” today, but Tippy’s heart peeped. “We should finish our tasks,” he sang. “A job done right feels light.”

Mia looked back at the cozy room. “We have time,” she said. “Let’s complete it.”

They tiptoed back in. Soft thump, soft sweep. Tippy tugged the sock out with his beak. Mia placed the book on the shelf. They made one last check on the list: “complete tasks.”

Outside, the sun felt warm. The air smelled like cut grass. At the community center, people smiled. “Thank you,” said Mr. Lee. “These good foods will help many families.”

Tippy sang a proud, bright song. “Tweet-a-deed! We did each needful deed!”

Mia laughed. “We finished our list, we told the truth, and we helped our community.”

Back in the cozy bedroom, the bed was smooth, the list was full of checks, and Tippy’s song floated like a ribbon in the air. He felt calm. He felt strong. He felt kind.

Mia taped the list on the wall. “Let’s remember how this feels,” she said.

Tippy tucked his head under his wing and whispered, “When we complete our tasks, we help ourselves and others. We eat smart foods, we tell the truth, and we show we care.”

He peeked out and sang one last gentle line. “Little steps make big good.”

The End.
