# Nutkin's Book of Many Languages

Nutkin the squirrel lived in a cozy oak tree next to a big house. One sunny morning, he scampered through an open window into the guest room. His fluffy tail twitched with excitement as he explored.

"Oh my!" squeaked Nutkin, his bright eyes growing wide. "What are all these colorful things?"

The guest room was full of old books scattered everywhere. Some had pictures of faraway places, others had strange squiggly letters Nutkin had never seen before. He picked up a red book and sniffed it. It smelled like vanilla and adventure.

"These smell wonderful," he chittered, "but I can't understand any of these marks on the pages."

Nutkin felt sad. He loved stories, but these books were written in languages he didn't know. There were books in Spanish with pictures of dancing children, books in French with images of cheese and bread, and books in Japanese with beautiful drawings of cherry blossoms.

Just then, he heard voices outside. Three children were walking toward the house - Maria, who spoke Spanish, Pierre, who spoke French, and Yuki, who spoke Japanese.

Nutkin's whiskers twitched with an idea. "I know!" he said, clapping his tiny paws together. "I'll make something special!"

The clever squirrel began gathering the old, torn pages that had fallen from the books. Instead of throwing them away, he carefully collected each colorful page. His small paws worked quickly, sorting the pages by language and pictures.

"This page about tacos goes here," he said, placing it in the Spanish pile. "This page about croissants goes there," he added, putting it with the French pages.

Nutkin found some old ribbon in a drawer and began tying the pages together. He created three special books - one for each language. Each book had the most beautiful pictures and simple words.

When the children arrived at the guest room, they gasped with delight.

"¡Qué bonito!" exclaimed Maria, which means "How beautiful!" in Spanish.

"C'est magnifique!" said Pierre, which means "It's magnificent!" in French.

"Kirei desu!" giggled Yuki, which means "It's pretty!" in Japanese.

Nutkin peeked out from behind a curtain, his heart warm with happiness. The children sat in a circle and began sharing their books with each other.

Maria read her Spanish book aloud, teaching Pierre and Yuki new words like "sol" for sun and "luna" for moon. Pierre shared French words like "étoile" for star and "ami" for friend. Yuki taught them Japanese words like "sakura" for cherry blossom and "tomodachi" for friend.

"Listen!" whispered Nutkin to himself. "They're learning that different languages are like different colored flowers in a garden - all beautiful in their own way!"

The children spent the whole afternoon reading, laughing, and learning from each other. They discovered that even though they spoke different languages, they could still be friends and share stories together.

As the sun began to set, Nutkin felt proud. He had turned old, forgotten books into something magical. He had helped the children discover that every language is special and worth learning about.

"Every word is like a tiny treasure," Nutkin said softly, "and every language helps us understand each other better."

From that day on, the guest room became a special place where children came to share stories in many different languages, all thanks to one resourceful squirrel who believed that all languages are beautiful.

The End.
