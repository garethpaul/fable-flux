# Ollie's Splish-Splash Dance Party

Ollie the otter loved to play in the sunny backyard. His soft, brown fur sparkled in the warm sunlight as he rolled around on the green grass. Today felt perfect for dancing!

"La-la-la!" sang Ollie as he wiggled his whiskers and spun in circles. His little paws tapped the ground like tiny drums. "I'm going to have the best dance party ever!"

But when Ollie looked down at his paws, he noticed something yucky. They were covered in mud and grass stains from all his rolling around. His fur had bits of leaves stuck in it too.

"Oh no," Ollie said, wrinkling his nose. "I'm all dirty! But I don't want to stop dancing to clean up."

Just then, his friend Mama Robin flew down from her nest in the big oak tree. She chirped sweetly, "Hello, Ollie! You look like you're having fun, but you seem a little messy."

Ollie's ears drooped. "I know, Mama Robin. I want to keep dancing, but I'm all dirty. What should I do?"

Mama Robin tilted her little head thoughtfully. "Well, what if we make a deal? How about you clean up first, and then I'll teach you a special new dance?"

Ollie's eyes grew wide with excitement. "A special dance? Really?"

"Really!" chirped Mama Robin. "But clean paws and fur make the best dancers. When we're clean, we feel fresh and ready to move!"

Ollie thought about this. He really wanted to learn the new dance, and he did feel a bit uncomfortable with all the dirt on his paws.

"Okay!" Ollie agreed. "But can you make cleaning fun too?"

Mama Robin clapped her tiny wings. "Of course! Let's make it a cleaning dance!"

Ollie waddled over to the small pond in the corner of the backyard. The water felt cool and refreshing against his paws. Mama Robin sang a cheerful tune: "Scrub-a-dub-dub, clean those paws! Splash and wash, that's how it goes!"

Ollie giggled and started moving to the rhythm. He splashed his paws in the water, washing away all the mud. "This is fun!" he laughed, shaking the water droplets from his whiskers.

Next, he dunked his whole body in the pond, letting the cool water clean his fur. He rubbed and scrubbed while Mama Robin sang, "Wash your fur from head to toe! Clean and fresh, now you can go!"

When Ollie climbed out of the pond, his brown fur was shiny and clean. His paws were spotless, and he smelled like fresh water and sunshine.

"Now I feel amazing!" Ollie exclaimed, doing a little twirl.

Mama Robin flew in a happy circle. "Perfect! Now you're ready for the special dance. It's called the Clean-and-Gleam Wiggle!"

Together, they danced in the sunny backyard. Ollie wiggled his clean paws, spun his fresh fur, and felt so much better than before. The dance was even more fun because he felt comfortable and clean.

"Thank you, Mama Robin!" Ollie said, catching his breath. "I learned that being clean makes everything more fun. And making a deal with you was the best idea ever!"

From that day on, Ollie always cleaned up before his dance parties. He discovered that taking care of himself made him feel happy, confident, and ready for any adventure.

The End.
