# Monkey Miles and the Car Ride Traditions

Monkey Miles buckled his little seat belt in the family car. Click! The buckle made a happy sound. The car smelled like warm banana muffins from breakfast. The seats felt smooth and cool. Outside, the sun peeked through the windows like a shy friend.

Mama drove. Papa sat in the front seat with a map. Miles sat in the back in his car seat with his backpack. He was a courageous monkey, brave and curious, with bright eyes and a big smile.

“Miles,” Mama said, “we are going to Grandma’s house today.”

“I love Grandma!” Miles clapped his furry hands. “She tells stories. They feel like hugs.”

As the car rolled along, the tires hummed a soft song. Hummm. Hummm. Miles wiggled with excitement. Then he took a deep breath. He had an idea.

“I want to start a car ride tradition,” Miles said. “A special thing we do every time we drive.”

Mama looked in the mirror. “That’s a wonderful idea,” she said. “What tradition do you want to share?”

Miles sat up tall. “Three things,” he said. “A hello song, a snack share, and a story time!”

Papa turned back and smiled. “You took great initiative, Miles. You made a plan!”

Miles grinned. “Yes! First, the hello song.” He cleared his throat. “La-la-la!” His voice was small but brave.

He began to sing. “Hello, seat belt. Click, click, click. Hello, windows. Shine, shine, quick. Hello, family. Hug, hug, hug. Hello, car. Vroom, vroom, snug!”

Mama sang along. Papa tapped the map like a drum. Tap-tap. The car filled with happy sound. Miles felt warm in his tummy. Traditions felt like home.

“Second,” Miles said, “snack share!” He opened his backpack. He pulled out a little lunch box. Snap! It opened with a crisp sound. Inside were apple slices, carrot sticks, and a small water bottle. They were bright and fresh. The apples smelled sweet. The carrots were crunchy like tiny sticks in the forest.

“Healthy-habits time,” Miles said proudly. “I will pass them around.” He handed a napkin to Mama. He gave a little bag to Papa. He kept some for himself. “Chew, chew,” he said. “Slow and kind.”

“Thank you, Miles,” Mama said. “Sharing snacks is a lovely tradition.”

Papa took a bite. Crunch! “These taste like sunshine,” he said.

Miles took a sip of water. Gulp, gulp. The water felt cool and clean. He patted his belly. “My body feels strong.”

The car passed tall trees. The leaves waved hello. The sky looked like a big blue blanket. Miles listened to the engine purr. Purr-rrrr. He felt safe.

“Third,” Miles said, “story time.” He held up a small wooden bead necklace. It was Grandma’s. It smelled like pine and cookies. “Grandma gave me this,” he said softly. “She told me we have a family story tradition. We pass stories like we pass hugs.”

Mama nodded. “Yes, our family shares stories on car rides. My Mama told me. Her Papa told her. It is our tradition.”

Miles smiled. “I will start today.” He took a brave breath. “Once upon a time, a courageous monkey rode in a family car. He felt the cozy seat. He heard the humming wheels. He wanted to share something kind. So he made a tradition. He sang hello. He shared healthy snacks. He told stories from his heart. The end.”

Papa clapped. “Bravo!” Mama wiped a happy tear. “Miles,” she said, “you kept our old tradition and added new parts. That is tradition-sharing. Old and new together.”

Miles nodded. “Old and new, like bread and jam.”

The car bumped over a little bridge. Thump-thump. Miles looked out at the water. It sparkled like tiny stars. He had another idea.

“Can we share Grandma’s story too?” he asked. “I will start it, and you can help.” He held the bead necklace close. It felt smooth and warm.

Mama began. “When I was small, Grandma and I sang a rainbow song.”

Miles sang softly, “Red, orange, yellow, green…”

Papa joined, “Blue, indigo, violet. We all shine, and we don’t forget!”

They all laughed. The car felt full of love.

Miles added, “After the song, we say what we are thankful for.”

“I am thankful for our safe car,” Mama said.

“I am thankful for our healthy snacks,” Papa said.

Miles thought. His tail curled with joy. “I am thankful for our family traditions,” he said. “They make my heart feel brave.”

Soon, the car turned onto Grandma’s street. The tires crunched on little stones. Crunch-crunch. The house smelled like cinnamon and tea even from the driveway.

Grandma came out with open arms. “Welcome, my travelers!” she said. “Did you share our car stories?”

“Yes!” Miles cheered. “And we added new parts! A hello song and snack share.”

Grandma hugged him. “Traditions grow when brave hearts care,” she said. “You showed initiative, my little monkey.”

Miles stood tall. “Can we show you our tradition?” he asked.

“Please do,” Grandma smiled.

They sang the hello song. They shared apple slices and water. Crunch, gulp, giggle. Then they took turns telling a story. The words felt soft and strong. The day tasted sweet and sounded bright.

Grandma tapped Miles’s nose gently. “Our family tradition is safe with you,” she said.

Miles snuggled close. He felt proud and peaceful. “Sharing traditions helps us remember love,” he whispered. “And healthy habits help us feel good while we do it.”

Everyone nodded.

The End.
