# Koala Kori’s Sunny Backyard Circle

Kori the koala was calm and cuddly. Kori lived in a sunny backyard with soft green grass, a big shady tree, and a small garden that smelled like sweet tomatoes and mint. Every morning, Kori followed a kind routine. Kori stretched, sniffed the warm air, and said, “Good morning, sun. Good morning, friends.”

The backyard was full of gentle sounds: bees buzzing like tiny songs, leaves rustling like whispers, and the sprinkler making soft pat-pat drops. Kori liked routines because they felt safe. After breakfast, Kori picked up a little green bucket and tiny tongs. It was cleanup time.

“Clean yard, happy hearts,” Kori hummed. Kori wore a wide smile and a floppy hat.

Soon, friends came to play. Tia the turtle rolled her toy truck. Benji the bunny bounced with a ball. Pip the pigeon fluttered down, cooing. “Coo-coo! Can I join?” Pip asked.

“Yes,” said Kori. “Everyone belongs in our sunny backyard.”

Kori set out a bright mat in the shade. “We’ll have a sharing circle,” Kori said. “We talk. We listen. We care.” That was part of the morning routine too.

Benji sat and hugged the ball. Tia parked the truck with a gentle clink. Pip ruffled feathers and cooed happily.

Kori pointed to a tiny sign made from a leaf and a stick. It said, “Kind words. Gentle hands. Everyone welcome.” Kori tapped the sign and smiled. “This is our kindness sign.”

Then a little wind puffed. It carried a crisp paper cup and a wrinkly bag into the yard. The paper cup rolled, flop-flop. The bag crinkled and stuck to the mint. The bees buzzed away.

“Oh no,” said Tia softly. “The garden looks sad.”

Benji sniffed. “It smells like plastic,” he said with a wrinkle nose.

Pip hopped back. “Coo… litter,” Pip whispered.

Kori took a slow breath. In. Out. Calm. “We have a problem,” Kori said. “Litter is not kind to our garden. The bees don’t like it. The mint can’t breathe.”

Benji hugged the ball tighter. “I want to play, not clean,” he said, ears drooping.

Pip peeked at the crinkly bag. “I’m scared to touch it,” Pip said.

Tia tucked into her shell a little. “I don’t know where it goes,” she said.

Kori nodded. “Thank you for telling me how you feel,” Kori said. “Feelings are welcome here too. We can solve this together. In our yard, everyone helps, everyone belongs.”

Kori showed the green bucket. “This is our sorting bucket. We sort to care for our Earth. Sorting is part of our routine. It is kind to our home.”

Kori held up three little boxes. They had bright picture labels.

“Paper goes to the blue box,” Kori said, tapping the picture of a book. “Plastic goes to the yellow box,” Kori said, pointing to a bottle picture. “Food scraps go to the brown bin,” Kori said, patting a picture of an apple core. “We turn scraps into soil. That is called compost. It helps our garden grow.”

Benji’s ears lifted a little. “I can hop fast,” he said. “I can get the paper cup to the blue box!”

Pip fluffed up. “I can use my beak to lift the bag,” Pip said. “But I might need help.”

Tia rolled her truck closer. “I can carry the boxes with my truck,” she said, smiling a small turtle smile.

Kori clapped softly. “Teamwork! Let’s try. We will be gentle and safe. I will hold the bucket and tongs.”

Together, they moved slowly. The sun felt warm and the grass tickled their toes. Kori used the tongs to pick up the cup. “Benji, ready?” Kori asked.

“Ready!” Benji hopped, thump-thump, and placed the cup in the blue box. “Paper to blue!” he cheered.

Pip took a brave breath. “Coo… I will lift the bag.” Pip nudged the plastic bag with Kori’s help. It crinkled like a tiny drum. “Plastic to yellow,” Pip said, placing it in the yellow box with a proud coo.

Tia drove the boxes to the side of the yard. “Vroom-vroom,” she hummed. The boxes made a happy clack when they lined up.

Kori looked at the mint leaves. A few were brown. Kori touched the soil. It felt dry and sandy. “Our garden needs a drink,” Kori said. “And a blanket of compost.”

Benji brought the brown bin. It smelled earthy, like rain and leaves. “Compost for the garden,” he said.

They sprinkled the compost softly. Pip flew to the sprinkler and tapped the lever. Water sprayed in sparkly arcs. Everyone laughed as cool drops kissed their fur, feathers, and shell. Drip-drop, plip-plop.

“The garden is smiling,” Tia said, peeking out of her shell.

Kori nodded. “You were all brave and kind,” Kori said. “We solved the problem together. Everyone helped in a way that felt right.”

Benji bounced the ball. “I liked helping,” he said. “Can everyone play ball now?”

“Everyone,” Kori said. “We can take turns.”

They made a big circle on the bright mat. “Pass, catch, cheer,” Kori sang. Benji passed to Tia. Tia tapped to Pip. Pip nudged to Kori. No one was left out. Everyone was in.

When they finished, Kori pointed to the kindness sign again. “What did we learn today?” Kori asked.

Pip cooed, “Include everyone.”

Tia added, “Be kind and gentle.”

Benji said, “Sort trash to help the Earth.”

Kori smiled. “Yes. Inclusion means everyone belongs. We listen. We help. We take turns. That is our routine in this sunny backyard.”

The bees came back to the mint. The air smelled fresh again. The friends sat in the shade, sipping cool water and sharing crunchy apple slices. The sun felt warm. Hearts felt warm too.

Kori closed eyes for a moment and took a calm breath. “Good work, friends,” Kori said. “When we include everyone, we can solve any problem, and our world grows happy and green.”

The End.
