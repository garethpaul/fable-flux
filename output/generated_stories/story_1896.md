# Gigi Builds on the Rooftop

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with long, gentle eyelashes and a soft, warm smile. One sunny morning, she climbed the wide, safe stairs to her building’s rooftop. The air was fresh and cool. A little breeze tickled her ears. She could hear birds chirping and smell warm toast from a kitchen window below.

Gigi looked around the rooftop. It was flat and bright, with a soft mat on the ground and a fence all around. “Today, I will build a cozy reading nook,” she said. “A special place to sit, to think, and to read.”

She had boxes of colorful blocks, a small toolbox with a tiny hammer, and a rolled-up shade cloth. Gigi tapped her hoof on the ground. Tap, tap. “I will be careful,” she whispered. “I will be responsible. I will clean up, too.”

Her friend Pip, a playful pigeon, landed with a flap. “Coo! What are you building, Gigi?”

“A reading nook,” Gigi said. “I have a plan. Four tall walls, no windows, and one small door.”

Pip tilted his head. “No windows? But I like light. Light feels happy on my feathers.”

Gigi blinked. She had not thought about windows. She felt a little tight feeling in her tummy. She took a deep breath. The breeze smelled like soap and flowers. “Maybe,” she said slowly, “I can listen to new ideas.”

Pip cooed, “What if we make one window? A small one?”

Gigi smiled. “I can be open-minded. Let’s try it.”

They began to build. Block by block. The blocks clicked together with soft clacks. Gigi stacked them carefully, keeping them straight. Pip held a small level with his wing. “It’s even!” Pip cheered.

Mina, a tiny mouse with bright eyes, peeked from behind a plant pot. “Squeak! Can I help?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Gigi. “We are being builders today. We are also being safe and tidy.” She pointed to a basket. “Blocks go here when we are done.”

Mina touched the shade cloth. “This cloth feels scratchy,” she said. “Maybe we can add a soft rug, too?”

Gigi felt that tight tummy feeling again. She had not planned a rug. But she remembered her big idea of listening. “I will be open-minded,” she said. “A soft rug could be nice.”

They built some more. Pip flew up high and sang a happy tune. Mina pushed blocks with her tiny paws. Gigi used her long neck to reach carefully. The wall grew up and up.

Soon, the door was ready. Gigi looked at the wall. “We made one window,” she said. “Now, the sun can say hello.”

The sun peeked through and made a warm, golden square on the floor. It felt cozy on Gigi’s legs. She smiled. “That feels very nice.”

Then a cloud covered the sun. The wind puffed a little stronger. The shade cloth flapped, flap-flap. Pip frowned. “Coo, it might fly away!”

Gigi nodded. “We must be responsible. We need to tie it tight.” She took string from her toolbox. “Double knot,” she said. “Safe and snug.” Pip and Mina helped hold the corners. Together, they tied firm knots. The cloth settled with a soft hush.

Next, Mina brought a small, fuzzy rug from her bag. It was sky-blue. Gigi spread it on the floor. “Ooh,” said Gigi, pressing her hoof. “Soft like clouds.”

Pip whispered, “I have another idea. Could we make a tiny shelf for books and seeds?”

Gigi paused. Her plan did not have a shelf. But her heart felt open, like the window. She took a deep breath, smelling the cool air. “Yes,” she said. “Let’s try a little shelf.”

They stacked short, strong blocks and made a small shelf. Gigi placed her favorite picture book on it. Pip added a few shiny seeds. Mina put a tiny bell. It chimed, ding, ding, in the breeze.

At last, they stepped back. The nook was ready. It had a window for light, a rug for soft feet, a shelf for books and little treasures, and a shade cloth tied tight.

Gigi sat down and opened her book. Pip and Mina sat close. The sun warmed their faces. The wind made gentle music with the bell. Gigi read in a slow, kind voice. The pages smelled like paper and adventure.

When the story ended, Gigi looked at her friends. “Thank you,” she said. “Your ideas made our nook better. I had a plan, but listening helped us build something wonderful.”

Pip puffed his chest. “Coo! We were safe, we cleaned up, and we worked together.”

Mina nodded. “And we tried new ideas. That felt brave and smart.”

Gigi touched the shelf with her hoof. “Today I learned to be open-minded. When we listen, we grow. When we care, we build well. Being responsible keeps our space safe.”

They put extra blocks in the basket, rolled up the leftover string, and brushed crumbs from the rug. The rooftop was neat and calm. The sky turned a soft pink as the day ended.

Gigi smiled at the window glow. “Our cozy nook is for everyone,” she said. “We can read, rest, and dream here.”

Pip cooed softly. Mina rang the tiny bell. Ding. Ding.

Gigi felt warm and proud. She had listened, learned, and built with care.

The End.
