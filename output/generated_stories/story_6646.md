# Sunny's Calm Clicks

Sunny the squirrel lived in a cozy home office at the top of an old oak tree. The room was bright and tidy. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. A tiny fan hummed, “whirr, whirr.” Papers were stacked in neat piles. A pencil cup smelled like fresh wood. Sunny loved to work here.

“Today,” said Sunny, patting her fluffy tail, “I will fix the wobbly chair and sort the paper for recycling.” She put on her little tool belt and clicked open a toolbox. Click, clack!

Sunny hopped to the desk. The keyboard made soft tap-tap sounds. The chair wiggled. “Wobble, wobble,” it went. Sunny giggled. “I can fix that.”

Just then, Pip the chipmunk poked his head in the window. “Hi, Sunny! Can I help?”

“Yes!” said Sunny. “You can stack the papers. White paper here. Colored paper there. We will reuse the blank sides.” Sunny set two boxes down. One box said, “Use Again.” The other said, “Recycle.”

Pip saluted. “Got it!”

Sunny found a tiny screw under the chair. She tried to twist it in. Twist, twist. But the screw fell. Ting! It rolled under the rug.

“Oh nuts,” Sunny said. She lifted the rug and looked. “Where did it go?” She reached and reached. Her whiskers tickled the rug. “Ah-choo!”

Pip giggled. “Bless you!”

Sunny tried again. Twist, twist. The screw slipped and bounced onto the floor. Ting-ting! The chair wobbled harder. Wobble, wobble, wobble!

Sunny felt something hot in her tummy. Her ears felt warm. Her tail puffed up. “I am getting mad,” she said in a small voice.

Pip’s eyes grew wide. “Do you need help?”

Sunny stomped one tiny paw. “I can do it!” The stomp made the pencil cup wobble. The pencils clattered like rain. Clack, clack, clack! A green pencil rolled across the desk and plopped into the paper box.

Pip jumped back. “Oh!”

Sunny’s face felt prickly. “This is too hard!” she shouted. Her voice boomed in the little room. The fan went “whirr?” as if it was surprised.

Pip took a step toward the window. He looked a little scared. “Sunny… I can go if you want.”

Sunny saw Pip’s worried eyes. Her heart squeezed. She took a breath in. In, two, three. Out, two, three. She remembered a trick: Stop, breathe, name the feeling.

She put a paw on her chest. “Stop. Breathe.” She breathed in through her nose. Sniff. She breathed out slowly. Hoo. “I feel angry,” she said softly. “I feel frustrated.”

The fan hummed again. Whirr, whirr. Sunny listened to the sound. She smelled the pencil wood. She felt the smooth desk. She took another slow breath.

Pip peeked in. “Are you okay?”

Sunny nodded. “I used my calm breaths.” She looked at the pencils on the floor and the green pencil in the box. “I am sorry I stomped and scared you,” she said. “I will make it right.”

Pip’s shoulders relaxed. “Thanks for saying sorry.”

Sunny picked up each pencil. Click, click, click. She placed them gently back in the cup. She smoothed the rug. She picked up the screw and placed it in a small bowl so it would not roll away. “I will try a different way,” she said. “I can use the right tool. I can ask for help.”

Pip smiled. “I can hold the chair steady.”

Sunny chose a small screwdriver that fit the screw just right. She lined it up. “Lefty loosey, righty tighty,” she whispered. Pip held the chair. Sunny turned the screwdriver. Twist… twist… snug! The chair stood tall and still.

“Yay!” Pip cheered. “No more wobble!”

Sunny sat in the chair and gave a tiny bounce. It did not wobble. She grinned. “Thank you, Pip.”

They moved to the paper boxes. Some pages had drawings on one side. “We can use the blank sides,” Sunny said. “That saves paper. Trees will be happy.” She stroked the wooden desk. “Thank you, tree.”

Pip held up a crumpled page. “Recycle?”

Sunny smoothed it out. “Use again!” She placed it in the “Use Again” box. “We can draw on it later.”

They worked and sang a little song. “Sort, sort, sort. Use again, recycle, sort.” The fan kept the beat. Whirr, whirr, whirr.

Soon the home office was neat again. The papers were in the right boxes. The pencils stood tall in their cup. The chair was steady. The sun made warm light on the floor. The room smelled like clean paper and pine soap.

Pip tugged his ear. “Sunny, how did you make your mad go away?”

Sunny patted her chest. “I did not make it go away fast. I noticed it. I named it. I took slow breaths. I listened to the fan. I felt the desk. Then I asked for help and tried a new way.”

Pip nodded. “I will try that next time I feel mad.”

Sunny smiled. “Feelings are okay. We can choose kind actions.”

They drew a picture together on the “Use Again” paper. Sunny drew a tree with a big smile. Pip drew a tiny screw with a happy face. They both laughed.

Sunny looked at Pip and said, “I am glad we fixed the chair without waste. We saved paper, too. We made amends for my stomp. We solved the problem together.”

Pip held up his paw for a high-five. “Teamwork!”

Slap! Their paws met. The fan cheered, “whirr!”

Sunny stretched and yawned. “What a good day,” she said. “I learned to calm my angry feelings. I said sorry. We took care of our things and our tree home.”

Pip waved goodbye. “See you tomorrow!”

Sunny sat in her steady chair. Tap-tap went the keyboard. She smiled and whispered, “Stop, breathe, name it, choose kind.” The words felt warm and calm, like sunshine on her fur.

The End.
