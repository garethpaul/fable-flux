# Miko's Morning Garden Adventure

Miko the monkey stretched his little arms and yawned as the warm sunshine tickled his face. "Good morning, sun!" he chirped, swinging from his cozy tree branch to the colorful balcony garden below.

The garden smelled like fresh dirt and sweet flowers. Red tomatoes hung like tiny balloons, green lettuce leaves danced in the breeze, and purple carrots peeked out from the soil like shy friends saying hello.

"Time for my morning chores!" Miko said happily, remembering what Grandpa Monkey had taught him. "A healthy garden needs love every day, just like our bodies need healthy food every day."

First, Miko grabbed his small watering can. The water made a gentle *splash-splash* sound as he poured it carefully on each plant. "Drink up, little tomatoes!" he giggled. "Water helps you grow big and strong, just like it helps me!"

Next came his favorite part - picking the ripe vegetables. Miko's tiny fingers wrapped around a bright red tomato. *Pop!* It came off easily. "You're perfect!" he told the tomato, placing it gently in his basket.

"Miko!" called Grandpa Monkey from the kitchen window. "Are you following our family tradition?"

"Yes, Grandpa!" Miko replied proudly. "I'm taking care of our garden, just like you taught me, and just like your grandpa taught you!"

Grandpa Monkey climbed down to join him. His whiskers were gray and his hands were gentle. "My little grandson, you're doing such a good job. Our family has always grown our own healthy food. It's much better than the sugary snacks from the store."

Together, they picked crisp lettuce leaves that made a satisfying *crunch* sound. Miko found orange carrots hiding under the green tops. "Look, Grandpa! These carrots are the same color as my fur!"

"That's right!" Grandpa chuckled. "And carrots help your eyes stay healthy and strong. Just like how we monkeys need good eyes for swinging through trees!"

When their basket was full of colorful vegetables, Grandpa showed Miko how to pull tiny weeds. "Weeds take food away from our good plants," he explained. "It's like how too much candy takes away our appetite for healthy food."

Miko worked carefully, his small hands pulling each weed. The soil felt cool and crumbly between his fingers. "I like helping our vegetables grow," he said.

"And they help you grow too," Grandpa smiled. "When we eat fresh vegetables from our garden, our bodies get vitamins and energy. That's why you can swing so high and run so fast!"

After finishing their garden chores, they washed their hands with soap and cool water. The vegetables sparkled clean in their basket - red tomatoes, green lettuce, orange carrots, and purple radishes.

"Now for the best part," Grandpa said. "Let's make a rainbow salad!"

In the kitchen, Miko helped wash and tear the lettuce. He arranged the colorful vegetables in a big bowl, making patterns like a beautiful painting. "It looks like a rainbow!" he exclaimed.

"And it tastes even better," Grandpa said, taking a big bite. "Mmm! Fresh, crunchy, and full of good energy!"

Miko munched happily on his rainbow salad. Each bite was crispy and sweet. "I love our family tradition, Grandpa. Taking care of our garden and eating healthy food makes me feel strong and happy!"

"That's the best lesson of all," Grandpa said, hugging his little grandson. "Healthy habits help us grow, just like our beautiful garden."

The End.
