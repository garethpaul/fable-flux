# Sunny Squirrel Cleans the Cozy Closet

Sunny the squirrel loved to collect shiny buttons, soft socks, and tiny twigs. One rainy day, pitter-patter went the drops on the window. Sunny scampered into a closet hideaway—soft, dark, and snug. The closet smelled like old shoes and dusty blankets. “This is my cozy corner,” Sunny chirped, twitching her whiskers.

Sunny piled her treasures into a nest. “I’ll make a little bed!” she said. She fluffed a scarf. She rubbed a button until it glowed. She tucked in a twig with a swirl like a curly noodle. The air felt warm and still.

But after a while, Sunny wrinkled her nose. “Pee-yew! What is that smell?” She poked a sock. It was sticky. A crumb clung to her paw. “Oh no,” Sunny said softly. “My cozy corner is messy. It’s not clean.”

A small moth fluttered out. “Hello,” said the moth in a whispery voice. “I like dark, dusty places.”

Sunny blinked. “I do not want dusty. I want clean and safe. Clean keeps me healthy!”

From the shelf above came a friendly voice. “I’m Brushy, the little broom,” it said, bristles rustling. “I can help sweep.”

A soft cloth peeked from a box. “I’m Wipey,” it giggled. “I can help wipe.”

Sunny felt her heart thump like raindrops. “I made this mess,” she said. “I will fix it. I can make amends.” Her tail curled with courage. “I can be brave and try again.”

She took a small breath in. The closet smelled dusty, like old leaves. She breathed out slow. “Let’s make a plan,” she said. “Step one: sort. Step two: clean. Step three: keep it nice.”

Brushy nodded. “Sweep, sweep!”

Wipey chimed, “Wipe, wipe!”

Sunny clapped her tiny paws. “Let’s go!”

First, Sunny sorted. “This sock has a hole,” she said. “It can be a puppet!” She drew two eyes with a bit of safe, washable marker and made the sock giggle. “Hello, Sock Puppet!”

She found three buttons. “These can be saved,” she said. She dropped them in a little jar with a gentle clink, clink, clink. “We reuse to help our Earth,” Sunny said. “That is called sustainability.” She smiled proudly. “We take care of things so they last.”

Next, Sunny cleaned. Brushy swept the floor. Swish, swish went the broom. Dust danced like tiny clouds, then landed in a pile. “Into the bin!” Sunny said. The bin went plop as the dust fell in.

Wipey helped with a small bowl of warm, soapy water. Sunny dipped the cloth. Drip, drip. “Wipe, wipe,” she sang. She wiped the shelf. She wiped the doorknob. She wiped the jar. The soap smelled like lemons. The wood smelled like trees after rain. “This is good hygiene,” Sunny said. “Clean hands, clean space, healthy face!”

She looked at her paws. “Time to wash.” Sunny went to the little sink by the door. “Water on,” she said. Swish. “Soap on,” she said. Squish. She rubbed her paws—front, back, between, and thumbs. “Scrub-a-dub, scrub-a-dub,” she sang, counting to 20 softly. She rinsed until the bubbles were gone. She shook her paws. Drip, drip, drip. “Dry, dry,” she said, patting them on a clean towel.

Back in the closet, Sunny found the sticky sock spot. “Oh dear,” she whispered. “I forgot to ask Mama about snacks in the closet.” She felt a little droop in her tail. “I made a mistake.”

Sunny took a brave breath. “I can make amends.” She carried the sticky sock to Mama Squirrel in the kitchen. “Mama, I made a mess in my closet hideaway. I brought snacks there. I am sorry,” Sunny said, eyes big and honest. “I cleaned up. Can we keep snacks at the table next time?”

Mama Squirrel hugged her. “Thank you for telling me,” Mama said, warm and kind. “I am proud of you. Yes, snacks stay at the table. That keeps bugs away and helps with hygiene.”

Sunny’s tail lifted high again. “I will remember,” she said.

They went back to the closet together. Sunny placed a small basket for clean treasures. She made a “Keep” basket and a “Fix” basket. “Fix means we repair,” Sunny said. “We don’t throw away what we can mend.” Mama showed her how to stitch a sock with tiny, careful pokes. Poke, pull. Poke, pull. “We mend to help the Earth,” Mama said.

Sunny put a little sign in the closet: “Clean Paws, Clean Space, Happy Face!”

She opened the door wide. Fresh air puffed in. “Hello, breezy air!” Sunny laughed. She lined up her twigs into a neat row. She set her button jar on the shelf. She folded the scarf into a tidy square. The closet now felt bright and calm, like a quiet hug.

The moth peeked in again. “It is too clean for me,” it sighed, and fluttered away. Sunny waved. “Goodbye, little moth!”

Sunny snuggled into her clean, soft nest. It smelled like lemons and wood. It felt safe and warm. She smiled. “I fixed my mess. I made amends. I kept my space clean. I can do hard things,” she said.

Brushy whispered, “Sweep, sweep, any day you need.”

Wipey giggled, “Wipe, wipe, we’re on your team!”

Sunny nodded. “I will clean a little every day. I will wash my paws. I will reuse and repair. I will ask for help when I need it.”

And in the cozy, clean closet hideaway, Sunny closed her eyes, proud and peaceful.

The lesson was clear: Keep clean for health, care for the Earth by reusing and fixing, say sorry and make it right, and try again with a brave heart.

The End.
