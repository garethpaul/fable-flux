# Bolt the Brave in the Garden Shed

Bolt was a helpful robot dog. His nose light blinked blue, his ears went beep, and his tail made a soft whirr-whirr. He lived by a little garden shed that smelled like fresh soil and mint leaves. The shed was full of tools, seed packets, and a tiny desk with paper and crayons.

One sunny morning, the wind whooshed, and the shed door went creak. Bolt trotted inside. “Good morning, shed!” he said in a friendly beep voice. He loved to help. He loved to learn. He was curious.

On the desk was a note. The note said, “Please help! Tomatoes are missing. We need lunch. —Milo and Mei.” Bolt’s nose light blinked brighter. “A problem,” he said. “I can help!”

He sniffed the air. It smelled like tomato vines, sweet strawberries, and damp wood. He listened. Rustle-rustle. Something moved behind a stack of pots.

Bolt’s metal paws clicked on the floor. “Hello?” he called. “I am Bolt. I can help.” His voice was calm and kind.

A tiny squeak answered. Out popped a little mouse with big shiny eyes. The mouse held a cherry tomato. Her whiskers trembled. “I’m Mimi,” she said. “I took tomatoes. I was hungry. I’m sorry.”

Bolt tilted his head. “Thank you for telling me, Mimi,” he said. “You were brave to speak up.” His tail whirred gently. “Let’s solve this together.”

Mimi sniffled. “I didn’t know who to ask. The shed felt big and dark. I got scared.”

Bolt looked around. The corners were shadowy. The rain barrel dripped, plip-plop. The rake looked tall like a giant comb. “Sometimes big places feel scary,” Bolt said. “But we can be brave. Brave means trying, even when we feel scared.”

Mimi nodded, but her paws still shook. “What if someone is mad?”

“We use words,” Bolt said. “We listen. We share. That is brave, too.”

Bolt picked up a crayon with his soft grabber paw. He wrote on a paper: “We need to share and eat healthy.” He drew a red tomato, a green leaf, and a little mouse smiling. Mimi watched, eyes wide.

“What is healthy?” Mimi asked.

Bolt beeped happily. “Healthy food helps our bodies. Tomatoes and leaves are full of goodness. Crunchy carrots help teeth. Berries help eyes. Water helps us feel strong.” He pointed his nose light at a basket. “Let’s make a plan so everyone eats.”

Mimi took a deep breath. “Okay,” she said. “I will be brave with you.”

They made a menu in big, simple words:
- Tomato salad
- Carrot sticks
- Leafy greens
- Sweet berries
- Water

Bolt drew pictures next to each word. The paper looked bright and friendly. “This is writing,” Bolt said. “Writing helps us share ideas.”

They rolled the paper and tied it with twine. Bolt wagged his tail. “Now we talk to Milo and Mei.”

Out in the garden, the sun warmed their backs. Bees buzzed. Basil smelled spicy. Milo and Mei were near the tomato rows, looking worried.

Bolt trotted up. “Hello, friends. We have news,” he said. Mimi hid behind a watering can, then peeked out.

Mei saw Mimi’s little face. “Oh!” she said softly. “Hello, Mouse.”

Milo smiled. “Are you okay?”

Mimi stepped forward. Her whiskers quivered, but her voice was clear. “I took tomatoes because I was hungry. I’m sorry. I want to help. I want to share.”

Bolt handed them the rolled paper. Mei opened it. “A menu!” she said. “With pictures!”

Milo read the words slowly. “Tomato… salad,” he said. “Carrot sticks. Leafy greens. Sweet berries. Water.”

Bolt nodded. “We can pick some for everyone,” he said. “We can plant extra for Mimi. We can make a tiny garden for small friends.”

Mei clapped. “Yes! Let’s do it.”

They worked together. Bolt held a basket. Mimi nibbled a tiny spinach leaf. “Mmm,” she squeaked. “Green tastes fresh.” Milo washed carrots. Crunch, crunch! Mei sliced tomatoes, red and juicy. The air smelled sweet and earthy. The birds sang tweet-tweet.

They set the food on a little table by the shed. Bolt poured water into cups. “Cheers to brave hearts,” he said.

Mimi looked up at Bolt. “My tummy feels happy,” she said. “My heart feels brave.”

Milo placed a small pot on the ground. “Mimi, this is your pot,” he said. “We will help you plant lettuce.”

Mei gave Mimi a tiny sign that said, “Mimi’s Garden.” Bolt wrote the words and drew a green leaf next to them. Mimi touched the sign. “Thank you for listening,” she said. “Thank you for sharing.”

Bolt’s nose light glowed warm. “We used our words. We were brave. We were curious. We learned about good food,” he said. His tail whirred a happy tune.

The shed did not feel scary now. It felt safe and bright. The tools looked helpful. The desk looked friendly. The paper and crayons waited for more ideas.

Bolt looked at his friends. “When we feel scared,” he said, “we can take a breath, use our words, and ask for help. That is brave.”

“And we can eat yummy healthy food,” Mimi added, taking a tiny sip of water.

Everyone laughed. The garden breeze was gentle. The tomatoes glowed like little suns. Together, they made a promise to share, to listen, and to be brave every day.

The End.
