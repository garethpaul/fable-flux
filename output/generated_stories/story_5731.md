# Koa the Calm Koala and the Pantry Plan

Koa the koala was very calm. Koa liked quiet places. One day, Koa visited a big, cozy house with a bright, clean pantry.

The pantry smelled like warm oats and sweet apples. Shelves held jars that clicked when turned. Boxes made soft crinkle sounds. The light above hummed like a happy bee.

Koa whispered, “Hello, pantry.” Koa always used a gentle voice.

Koa looked around and saw friends. Pip the possum peeked from a basket. Tulla the turtle tapped her shell on the floor, tap-tap. Buzz, a tiny bee puppet on a stick, leaned against a jar. Buzz did not fly, but children used Buzz to pretend.

“Hi, friends,” said Koa. “Would you like a snack?”

“Yes, please!” Pip squeaked. “I want lots of crackers!”

“I want the apple slices,” said Tulla slowly. “Crunch, crunch.”

Buzz did not talk, but Koa wiggled the puppet and made a buzz sound. “Bzz! I want honey!”

Koa smiled. “We can share. Let’s take turns.”

Pip hopped to the cracker box and reached in with both paws. Crinkle! Crinkle! “I’m very hungry,” Pip said. Pip took a big handful.

Tulla reached for the apple jar. Click! The lid turned. “I am hungry, too,” she said. She took three slices and began to chew. Crunch. Crunch. Crunch.

Koa reached for the honey jar for Buzz. The glass was cool. The jar was heavy. Koa read the label slowly. “Honey. One jar left.”

Koa looked at the apples. Only a few slices were left. The cracker box looked light and a little empty.

Koa’s ears twitched. “Friends,” Koa said softly, “we have a problem.”

Pip froze. “A problem?”

Tulla stopped chewing. “What kind of problem?”

Koa pointed to the shelves. “We are using many snacks very fast. If we eat too much now, there will be none for later. We need to save some. We need to take turns and not waste.”

Pip’s eyes grew big. “But I am so, so hungry.”

Koa nodded. “I hear you, Pip. We can eat. But we can eat in a smart way.”

“How?” asked Tulla. She blinked slowly.

Koa sat on a soft mat on the floor. The mat felt warm and smooth. “We can make a Pantry Plan,” Koa said. “We can share, we can take turns, and we can save food. Saving food is called conservation. It helps the planet and helps us, too.”

Pip tilted his head. “Con-ser-vay-shun?”

“Conservation,” Koa said gently. “It means we use only what we need. We do not waste. When we do that, there is enough for everyone, and enough for later days.”

Tulla nodded. “That sounds kind.”

Koa clapped quietly. Pat, pat. “Let’s make the plan now.”

Koa took out three small bowls. Tap, tap, tap. Koa poured a few crackers into each bowl. Koa counted, “One, two, three, four.” Koa sliced the last apple into tiny pieces. The apple smelled sweet and fresh. Koa put a small drizzle of honey on each bowl. Drip, drip. “A little is enough.”

Pip’s nose twitched. “It smells so yummy!”

Koa smiled. “We will take turns. Pip, you may have your bowl first. Then Tulla. Then Buzz. I will have some last.”

Pip wiggled. “I get to go first?” His tail swished. “Okay! I can wait next time.”

Tulla smiled. “I will go second. I can wait, too.”

Buzz, the puppet, wiggled when Koa moved the stick. “Bzz-bzz, I can wait as well,” Koa buzzed playfully.

They ate slowly together. Crunch, crunch went the crackers. Slurp went the tiny honey drips. Pip licked a crumb from his paw. “Mmm. I am happy.”

Tulla took small bites. “I feel full,” she said after a while. “I did not need a lot.”

Koa nodded. “Our plan works.”

Then Koa looked at the shelves again. “Let’s also think about sustainability,” Koa said. “That is a long word. It means we make choices that can last a long time and help the Earth.”

Pip blinked. “How do we do that?”

“We can put snack crumbs in the compost bin,” Koa said. “Com-post helps plants grow. We can put empty boxes in the recycling bin to be used again. We can close jars tight so food stays fresh. Click!” Koa showed them the click.

“Click!” echoed Pip.

“Click,” said Tulla, smiling.

Koa showed a small picture chart on the wall. The chart had three simple pictures: a leaf for compost, a circle arrow for recycle, and a clock for taking turns. “We can follow this chart every snack time.”

Pip pointed. “Leaf for peels and crumbs.”

Tulla pointed. “Circle arrow for boxes and jars.”

Koa pointed. “Clock for taking turns.”

They cleaned up together. Pip brushed crumbs into a little green bin. Swish. Tulla folded the cracker box and put it in the blue bin. Rustle. Koa wiped the table with a damp cloth. Wipe, wipe. The pantry smelled fresh again.

Koa placed the remaining snacks neatly on the shelf. “Now we have some for tomorrow,” Koa said. “And the next day.”

Pip hugged Koa. “Thank you for the Pantry Plan.”

Tulla patted Koa’s paw. “I like saving and sharing.”

Koa felt warm and calm. “We solved the problem together. We took turns, we saved food, and we helped the Earth.”

They all smiled. The light hummed softly. The jars sat in tidy rows. The pantry felt peaceful and kind.

Koa whispered, “Conservation means care.” Pip and Tulla whispered back, “We care.”

The End.
