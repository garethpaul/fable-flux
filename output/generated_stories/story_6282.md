# Hops in the Pantry

Hops was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and springy feet. One sunny morning, Hops peeked into the pantry. It smelled like warm oats, sweet raisins, and crunchy crackers. The shelves looked tall, like little mountains of boxes and jars.

Mama Roo called from the kitchen, “Hops, today we have three pantry tasks. We can do them together.”

Hops clapped his tiny paws. “I like tasks! What are they?”

Mama opened the door wider. “Task one: put the cereal boxes in a neat row. Task two: sort the fruit cups by color. Task three: wipe the little crumbs from the bottom shelf. We will do one task at a time.”

“One at a time,” Hops repeated, bouncing softly. His tail swished on the cool floor. “I can do it!”

Hops reached for the cereal boxes. They felt smooth under his paws. “Hello, Crunchy Corn!” he giggled. “Hello, Oaty O’s!” He set them side by side. But a cookie box winked from the corner. “Psst, Hops! I am tasty,” it seemed to say.

Hops sniffed. The cookie box smelled like cinnamon. His tummy made a tiny rumbly sound. He took a small hop toward it, but Mama’s kind voice floated in. “Hops, remember your boundary. Cookie time is after tasks. Right now is work time.”

Hops stopped and placed a paw on his chest. “My boundary: work first, cookie later,” he whispered. He turned back to the cereal. He slid each box into a straight line, edges touching, like train cars on a track.

Click. Click. Click.

“All done with task one!” Hops cheered. He felt proud, like sunshine in his chest.

Mama smiled. “Great persistence, my joey. You stayed with the job.”

“Task two!” Hops said. He opened a basket of fruit cups. Red strawberry cups. Yellow peach cups. Orange mandarin cups. The cups squished softly when he held them, and the lids went crinkle-crinkle.

“I will sort by color,” Hops said. “Red with red. Yellow with yellow. Orange with orange.” He made three happy towers. As he stacked, a tall jar of sprinkles sparkled like tiny rainbows. It made a little tinkle sound when he bumped the shelf.

“Sprinkles!” Hops gasped. “They look like tiny stars!”

His paws reached out, then stopped. He took a slow breath and tapped his tail two times on the floor. Tap. Tap. “Boundary check,” he told himself. “I do my task first. I can look at sprinkles later.”

He kept sorting. Plop—red cup. Plop—yellow cup. Plop—orange cup. Soon the towers stood straight and bright.

Mama peeked in again. “You are focusing, Hops. You’re completing your task.”

Hops wiggled with joy. “Task two is done!”

Mama gave a gentle nose boop. “On to task three: wipe the crumbs.”

Hops took a small cloth. It felt soft and a little damp. He crouched low on the cool tile. The bottom shelf had tiny crumbs like sandy dots. Swish, swish, swish went the cloth. He gathered the crumbs into a little pile.

A big bag of crunchy chips rustled. Rrrrustle, rustle. It sounded like leaves in the wind. Hops wanted to peek inside. He bounced once, then remembered. “I can say no to peeking,” he told himself. “No, thank you, chips. I am cleaning right now.” He smiled. “That is my personal boundary.”

Swish, swish, swish. The shelf grew shiny and clean. Hops wrung out the cloth. Drip, drip went the water into a tiny bucket.

“All clean!” Hops said, standing tall. His chest felt warm and proud again. “I finished all three tasks!”

Mama Roo came in with a happy hum. “I saw your persistence, Hops. You kept going, even when sprinkles and cookies called your name.”

“They were very loud,” Hops giggled. “But my boundary voice was louder. It said, ‘One task at a time.’”

Mama nodded. “That is a strong skill. Completing tasks helps our home feel calm and safe. It also helps our hearts feel proud.”

Hops looked around. The cereal train was straight. The fruit cups were in bright towers. The shelf was neat and clean. The pantry felt cozy. It smelled like sweet fruit and fresh cloth now.

Mama said, “Now that the tasks are done, it is snack time. What would you like?”

Hops thought. His tummy gave a friendly rumble. “One cinnamon cookie, please,” he said. “And two slices of peach.”

“Good choice,” Mama said. She set a small plate on the counter. The cookie was warm and soft. The peach slices were cool and juicy.

Hops took a bite. Crunch and yum! Juice dribbled a little on his chin. He wiped it with the cloth, then put the cloth back in the wash basket. “I can finish my snack, then help put the cloth away,” he said.

Mama smiled. “You’re completing each job all the way. That is persistence.”

Hops licked his lips and looked at the shiny shelf. “I learned: do one task at a time, say no when I need to, and keep going until the job is done.”

He gave a happy hop. “Work first, then play. Boundaries help me every day!”

Mama hugged him close. “Yes, my joyful joey.”

Hops felt light as a feather and strong as a big kangaroo. He bounced once more and whispered, “I can do hard things, one small step at a time.”

The End.
