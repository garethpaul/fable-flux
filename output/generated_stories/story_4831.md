# Leo’s Porch of Quiet Roars

The sun was warm and soft on the peaceful front porch. A brave little lion cub named Leo sat on a striped cushion. The wood boards felt smooth under his paws. The porch smelled like sweet lemonade and fresh flowers. Birds chirped, “Chee-chee!” Leaves whispered, “Shhh,” in the gentle breeze.

Leo’s tail swished. He was building a tower with smooth, colorful blocks. “I can do it,” he said. “I am brave and calm.”

Mama Lion brought out a cool pitcher. The ice clinked, “Tink, tink.” “I’m right here on the porch,” she said kindly. “Have fun, little roar.”

Leo stacked one block. Then two. Then three. His tower wobbled a teeny bit. He took a slow breath. “In…and out,” he whispered. “Like the breeze.”

A fly buzzed close to his ear. “Bzzzz!” The tower shook. “No, no, no!” Leo said. The top block fell. Plop. Then the next one slid. Clack. The whole tower tumbled down. Crash!

Leo felt hot inside, like a tiny fire. His ears got prickly. His paws got tight. “I’m mad!” he growled. “I worked so hard!”

He lifted a block to throw it. His paw trembled. His little heart beat fast—thump, thump, thump. He heard a dove coo softly in the tree, “Coo, coo.” The porch wind said again, “Shhh.”

Mama Lion’s voice was gentle. “I see a brave cub with big feelings.”

“I want to roar loud,” Leo said, eyes shiny. “I want to smash the blocks.”

Mama nodded. “Feelings are okay. Let’s use a calm plan.”

“A plan?” Leo blinked. He liked plans. Plans felt safe.

“Three calm steps,” Mama said, holding up three soft claws. “Step one: Pause your paws. Put the block down gently.”

Leo placed the block on the cushion. Thump—soft and safe.

“Step two,” Mama said. “Breathe like the wind. In for four, out for four.”

Leo breathed in slowly. One, two, three, four. He breathed out slowly. One, two, three, four. His chest felt like a small boat, rocking on a quiet pond. The hot feeling cooled.

“Step three,” Mama said. “Talk to your feelings. Use your calm words.”

Leo put a paw on his chest. “Hello, Anger. I see you. Thank you for telling me I care. I will calm down now.”

The breeze kissed his whiskers. The birds sang a tiny song. Leo’s paws relaxed. His tail made a soft swish-swish instead of a smack-smack.

Mama smiled. “Brave and calm.”

Leo nodded. “Can we try again?” His voice was small but steady.

“Let’s add one more strategy,” Mama said. “When it wobbles, we stop and make it steady. That is called a ‘steady-check.’”

Leo grinned. He liked the sound of that. “Steady-check!”

He began to build again. One block. Steady-check. Tap, tap, gentle paws. Two blocks. Steady-check. He wiggled them just right. Three blocks. Steady-check. Breathe in…breathe out. The tower stood taller.

The fly buzzed back. “Bzzzz!” Leo’s ear twitched, but he remembered. He paused his paws. He breathed like the wind. He said, “Hello, Anger. I will stay calm.” Then he did a steady-check. Tap, tap. The tower stayed up!

Leo’s eyes sparkled. “I did it!”

Mama clapped softly. “I saw your brave calm.”

Leo added one more block. Four! The tower glowed in the sun. He could smell the lemony air. He could hear the porch boards creak, “Creak,” as he scooted closer.

Then a squirrel chattered on the fence. “Chik-chik!” The porch dog next door gave a happy “Woof!” The tower wobbled again. Leo felt a tiny bubble of mad pop in his tummy.

“Pause paws,” he whispered. He set his paws down flat. “Breathe like the wind.” In…out. “Use calm words.” He smiled at his tower. “We can fix it.” He did a steady-check—tap, tap—and slid a bigger block at the bottom.

The tower stood strong. Leo stood strong too.

“Let’s celebrate,” Mama said. She poured a little cup of lemonade. The cup felt cool in Leo’s paw. He took a sip. “Mmm.” Sweet and sunny.

“Mama,” Leo said, “when I got mad, the world felt loud. Then I got calm, and the world felt soft.”

Mama nodded. “Calm helps your brave show up.”

Leo looked at the porch, at the soft sunshine, at the kind breeze brushing his fur. “I’m a calm lion with a smart plan,” he said proudly.

“What’s your plan called?” Mama asked.

“Pause Paws, Wind Breath, and Steady-Check,” Leo said. He tapped his nose. “I can use it anytime.”

He stacked one more tiny block on top. Five! The birds cheered. “Chee-chee!” The leaves whispered, “Shhh.” The porch felt peaceful again.

Leo hugged Mama. Her fur was warm and cozy. “When I feel angry,” he said, “I can pause, breathe, and use a smart strategy. Then I can try again.”

Mama hugged him back. “That is a strong lion lesson.”

Leo smiled at his tall, steady tower. “I am brave,” he said, “and I am calm.”

The End.
