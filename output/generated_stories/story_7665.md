# Ollie Otter’s Crafty Study Corner

Ollie was a playful otter with bright eyes and busy paws. He loved to splash, he loved to laugh, and he loved to make things. Today, Ollie wiggled into his cozy study corner. It was a small, sunny space with a low table, soft cushions, and a basket of craft supplies. Paper rustled, glue smelled sweet and sticky, and little buttons clicked like tiny raindrops in a jar.

“Today is a craft day!” Ollie squeaked. “I will make a pretend boat!”

He spread out blue paper, brown cardboard, shiny stickers, and string. He tapped his whiskers. “I need a plan,” he said. “Step one, make a boat shape. Step two, make a sail. Step three, decorate!”

Ollie drew a simple shape. Scritch, scritch went his crayon. He snipped the cardboard. Snip, snip went his scissors. He glued the sides. Squish, squish went the glue. But the cardboard flap flopped. The boat leaned to one side like a sleepy fish.

“Oh no,” said Ollie. “My boat is wobbly.”

He felt a little droop in his tummy. His paws felt sticky and slow.

Just then, a soft breeze came through the window. It smelled like warm sun and paper. The curtains whispered, “Try again, Ollie.”

Ollie nodded. “I can try again,” he said. “I can learn.”

He looked around his study corner. On the wall was a small sign he made with his caregiver: Try, Think, Try Again. Ollie tapped each word with his paw. “Try. Think. Try again.”

“Maybe I need a stronger bottom,” Ollie said. He looked in his basket. He found a thin wooden stick and some tape. “I have a new idea,” he said. “I will make a strong beam.”

He taped the stick under the boat. Press, press. He waited. He counted slowly, “One… two… three.” He smiled. “I am using a strategy! I press and wait.”

He set the boat down. It stood straighter, but the sail kept falling. Flop, flop.

Ollie took a deep breath. He smelled the sweet glue and the papery paper. “It’s okay,” he whispered. “I can be open to new ways.”

He tried a straw for the mast. “This might work,” he said. He poked the straw into a soft sponge piece and taped it inside the boat. The sponge felt squishy and cool. The straw stood tall.

“Hello, Sail!” said Ollie with a giggle.

He cut a triangle of blue paper. Snip, snip. He drew a friendly fish on it. “Hi, Fish!” he said. He taped the sail to the straw. The sail fluttered when the breeze kissed it. Flutter, flutter. Ollie clapped. “It stands!”

Now it was time to decorate. Ollie stuck round buttons like little portholes. Click, click. He drew waves with a wiggly crayon. Wiggle, wiggle. He tied a bit of string like a tiny rope. “My boat needs a name,” he said. “I will call it Brave Try!”

Just then, Ollie tried to move the boat, and the front bent down. “Oh dear,” he said. “Still wobbly.” His eyes felt hot. A small tear peeked out.

He paused and put his paw on his heart. Thump, thump. “I can calm my body,” he said softly. He took a deep breath in and out. “I can grow. I can learn.”

Ollie looked around again. On his shelf was a small, flat piece of foam. “Maybe a base will help.” He put the boat on the foam and taped gently around the edges. He pressed the tape smooth. Smooooth. He gave the boat a tiny push across the table. It slid like a happy duck on a pond. “Whee!” said Ollie.

He grinned so big his whiskers curled. “My strategy worked! I tried, I thought, I tried again.”

He held up Brave Try and listened. The paper sail whispered. The buttons clicked. The straw mast made a tiny hollow sound when he tapped it. “Tonk, tonk,” Ollie giggled.

His caregiver peeked in. “Ollie, I love your boat,” they said. “Tell me how you made it.”

Ollie puffed with pride. “I had a plan. It went wobbly. I felt sad. I took a breath. I tried a new idea. I made a beam. I used a straw and sponge. I made a base. I kept going!”

“That is open-minded,” said the caregiver. “You listened to new ideas. That is a growth mindset.”

Ollie nodded. “Yes! I can learn new ways.”

They set Brave Try on the window ledge. Sunlight made the stickers sparkle. The room smelled like crayons and warm paper. Ollie wiggled his tail. “I want to make a tiny flag next,” he said. “I will use small steps. I will try, think, and try again.”

The caregiver smiled. “That is a smart strategy.”

Ollie waved his little flag and whispered to the boat, “We did it, Brave Try.”

He looked at his sign again: Try, Think, Try Again. He traced the words with his paw and felt calm and proud.

Lesson: When something is hard, we can stop, breathe, and try a new idea. Crafting is for trying, learning, and having fun. Being open-minded and using a plan helps us grow, one small step at a time.

The End.
