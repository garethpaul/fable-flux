# Dori the Dolphin Visits the Shed

Dori was a playful dolphin with bright, curious eyes. One breezy morning, she splashed out of a puddle after a rainy night and popped into a little garden shed. The door was open just a tiny bit, and the air smelled like fresh soil and lemony soap.

“Oooh,” Dori whispered. “What a cozy cave!” She clicked and squeaked with joy. Her flippers flapped softly on the wooden floor. Tap, flap, tap!

Inside, the shed was full of things: a jump rope hung on a nail, a shiny watering can sat on a shelf, and soft rags lay in a basket. Sunlight made golden stripes on the walls. Dust floated like tiny sparkles. Dori loved the sparkle.

“I will play pretend,” Dori said to herself. “I will use my big dolphin imagination!”

She looked at the jump rope. “This can be seaweed,” she giggled. “Wiggle, wiggle!” She wrapped it loosely around her tail and shimmied. Swish, swish! It tickled, and she laughed.

Then she saw the watering can. “This is a sea trumpet,” she decided. She put the spout near her mouth and made a silly sound. “Toot-a-toot!” Her squeaks echoed in the shed. It felt good to make music.

Dori swam—in her mind—through a make-believe ocean. She pretended the broom was a tall coral tree. She pretended the rake was a friendly crab. She wiggled and stretched and spun in small circles. Exercise felt fun. Her fins and tail moved, and her body felt strong and warm.

Just then, little feet pattered on the path. Two neighbors, Max and Lina, peeped into the shed. Max wore bright yellow boots. Lina held a soft, blue ball.

“Hello?” Max said. “Who’s in our shed?”

Dori turned and smiled a big dolphin smile. “Hi! I’m Dori. I am visiting your cozy cave.”

Lina’s eyes grew wide. “A dolphin in our shed?” she gasped. “Wow!”

Max grinned. “What are you doing, Dori?”

“I’m playing ocean,” Dori said. “This rope is seaweed. The watering can is my sea trumpet. Want to play with me?”

Max and Lina looked at each other. Max took a tiny step in. “We do want to play,” he said. “But those are our things. We use them, too.”

Dori froze. Her flippers stopped. “Oh,” she whispered. “I did not ask. I was excited. I forgot to share and take turns.” Her tummy felt fluttery. She looked down. The shed felt quiet.

Lina hugged the blue ball. “It’s okay,” she said softly. “We can still play. But we need to share and keep things safe.”

Dori took a deep breath. She made a gentle click. “I’m sorry,” she said. “Can we share? Can we make a plan?”

Max nodded. “Yes! Let’s make a plan.” He held up his fingers. “We can use a timer. One song long for a turn.”

Lina smiled. “We can sing the Twinkle song! When we finish, we switch.”

Dori’s eyes sparkled again. “I like that. We will share and take turns. That is fair.”

They set the rope on the floor. Dori went first. She wiggled it like seaweed and stretched her fins up high. “Stretch to the sky!” she sang. Max and Lina copied her, lifting their arms. They all took deep breaths. In and out. Their bodies felt alive and strong.

“Twinkle, twinkle, little star…” they sang together. Their voices were soft and sweet. The rope made a gentle swoosh on the floor.

When the song ended, Dori passed the rope to Lina. “Your turn,” she said, smiling. “I will watch and cheer.”

Lina hopped over the rope. Hop, hop, hop! Her ponytail bounced. “I am a dolphin jumping waves!” she laughed.

Dori clapped her fins. “Great jumps!”

Then it was Max’s turn. He used the rope like a finish line. He ran in place, pumped his knees, and zoomed through. “Zoom!” he cried. They all giggled. Their cheeks felt warm, and their hearts beat fast. Exercise felt happy.

Next, they shared the watering can. Dori held it gently. “May I toot?” she asked.

Max said, “Yes, after my turn.” He tipped it like he was watering pretend sea flowers. “Drip, drop, drip!”

Lina took her turn next. She made tiny rain sounds with her tongue. “Pitter-patter,” she said.

Then Dori lifted the can and made a trumpet noise. “Toot-a-toot!” She kept it short and careful. They laughed at the funny sound.

They made a new game together. The rope was seaweed. The watering can made rain. The blue ball became a bubble. They popped the bubble with gentle taps. Pop, pop, pop! Their pretend ocean swirled with color and sound.

“Sharing makes more fun,” Lina said, smiling.

“It makes more ideas, too,” Max added. “We can build the game together.”

Dori felt warm and brave. “I learned something,” she said. “When I ask first and share, we all play. When we take turns, everyone feels happy.”

The sun moved, and the golden stripes grew long. Dori listened to the soft creak of the shed door and the tiny buzz of a fly. She smelled the sweet wood and the lemony soap again.

“I must go back to my puddle,” Dori said gently. “Thank you for sharing with me.”

Max waved. “Come visit again!”

Lina nodded. “We’ll make a new game next time.”

Dori squeaked a goodbye. Splash! She slipped back to her puddle, heart light and tail swishing.

As she swam away, Dori sang, “Share, ask, and care. We can all play, everywhere.”

The End.
