# Leo and the Sparkly Sink Adventure

In a bright, bubbly bathroom, a brave little lion cub named Leo padded in on soft paws. The tiles felt cool under his feet. The room smelled like lemon soap. Sunlight danced on the mirror and made tiny rainbows.

“Good morning, toothbrush,” Leo said, touching the soft bristles. “Today we brush, splash, and shine!”

A tiny rubber duck on the edge of the tub squeaked, “Squeak!” Leo smiled. “Hello, Ducky. Want to be my friend today?”

Squeak! The duck sounded happy. Leo’s brave heart felt warm. Friends make every day better.

Leo climbed onto a little step stool. He could see his whiskers in the mirror. “Roar!” he giggled softly. “I am brave and kind.”

He turned the water knob. Splish, splash! Warm water ran over his paws. It felt smooth and cozy. “Ahh,” Leo sighed. He squeezed a pea of toothpaste that smelled like mint leaves. “Minty fresh!” he said.

Just then, a tiny voice whispered from the corner. “Hello?” It was a shiny silver cup. “I’m Cup. I hold toothbrushes. Today I feel a little messy.”

Leo looked around. The sink had drips. Towels were on the floor. Toys sat across the tub. The soap was sleeping in a puddle. “Oh my,” said Leo. “This bathroom needs some love.”

He smiled at Cup. “Don’t worry. We can help. Friends help each other.”

Squeak! Ducky bobbed closer. “We can be a team,” Leo said. “A friendship team!”

Leo finished brushing. “Brush, brush, brush,” he sang. He rinsed with a gentle swish. The mint tingled like tiny bubbles. “All clean,” he said, showing a big lion grin to the mirror.

Then he heard a bump. The mirror wobbled. “Careful,” said the mirror, in a soft, shiny voice. “Please wipe me gently. I want to see your smile clearly.”

“I will,” said Leo. He took a soft cloth from a shelf and wiped in small, careful circles. Swish, swish. The glass sparkled. Leo could see his bright eyes. “Thank you,” the mirror hummed.

“Now,” Leo said, “let’s organize. Organize means we put things where they belong.”

Squeak! Ducky wiggled in agreement.

Leo picked up the towels from the floor. They felt fluffy and a little damp. “Towels hang here,” he said, placing them on hooks. “Hooks help towels dry.”

He lifted the soap and set it on a small dish. “Soap, this is your cozy bed,” he said. The soap smelled sweet and clean. “Thank you,” it seemed to say.

He gathered the bath toys into a basket. Clack, clack, clack went the toys as they snuggled together. “Toys sleep in the basket,” Leo told Ducky.

“Where do I go?” Ducky asked.

“In the basket too,” said Leo. “You like to be with your friends.” Squeak! Ducky settled in with a happy sound.

Leo turned to Cup. “Let’s help you.” He rinsed Cup with warm water. The water felt like summer rain. He shook off the drops. “Toothbrush goes in Cup,” Leo said, placing it upright. Cup shined proudly. “I feel neat,” said Cup.

Leo noticed little puddles around the sink. He pressed the cloth down. Squish! The cloth soaked up the water. “Dry, dry, dry,” Leo sang. He wrung it out carefully and hung it neatly.

Leo took a deep breath. The bathroom looked calm and bright. The sink sparkled. Towels hung like colorful flags. The floor felt safe and dry. The air smelled fresh like lemons and mint.

“Friends,” Leo said, “we did it together.”

Squeak! cheered Ducky. “Thank you for helping,” said Cup. The mirror shimmered with a soft glow. “I can see you clearly, brave Leo.”

Just then, Leo’s neighbor, a small cheetah cub named Chi, peeked in. “Hi, Leo,” Chi said, paws tapping. “Can I wash my paws? Mine are muddy.”

Leo nodded. “Yes! Let’s wash together.”

Chi climbed the step stool. Leo turned the water to a gentle stream. “Warm, not too hot,” Leo said. Chi giggled as the water tickled. “It feels nice!” she said.

“Here is soap,” Leo showed. “Rub, rub, rub. Bubbles!” The bubbles felt soft like clouds. They smelled like lemons. Chi laughed. “I love bubbles!”

Leo handed Chi a towel. “We dry our paws so we don’t slip.” Chi patted her paws. “Thank you, Leo. You are a good friend.”

“We are friendship helpers,” Leo said. “We help, we share, and we put things away.”

Chi looked around. “Everything is neat,” she said. “It makes me feel calm.”

“Me too,” Leo said. “When we organize, we can find what we need. When we care for ourselves, we feel strong. When we help friends, our hearts feel big.”

Chi smiled. “Can I help next time?”

“Yes,” said Leo. “We can always help each other.”

The bathroom glowed with clean, happy light. Ducky snuggled in the basket. Cup stood tall. The mirror smiled back at them.

Leo’s brave heart felt full. “Friendship makes work feel fun,” he said. “Discovery makes each day new. Organization keeps our space safe and comfy. And caring for ourselves helps us shine.”

Squeak! said Ducky.

Leo and Chi bumped paws gently. “Teamwork,” they said together.

The End.
