# Beaver Benny’s Porch Plan

Beaver Benny woke up with the sun. The morning air was cool on his nose. He stretched his furry arms and wiggled his flat tail. “Today is a porch day,” he said. “I will sweep, I will sort, and I will share.” Benny loved his peaceful front porch. It smelled like fresh wood and pine. Birds chirped softly in the trees. The porch floor felt smooth and warm under his paws.

Benny brought out his little broom. Swish, swish. Dust danced in the light. “I like to keep things neat,” he said, humming a happy tune. He set out three small baskets. One basket held shiny pebbles. One basket held soft pinecones. One basket held bright leaves. Each basket made a soft rattle when he moved it. “These are for porch games,” said Benny. “We will play and move our bodies. It will be fun!”

Soon, friends came by. Squirrel Sunny skipped up the steps. “Hi, Benny! I hear seeds crunching in my tummy,” she giggled. “Can we play a game?”

Chipmunk Chippy peeked from the railing. “Let’s race!” he squeaked. “I’m fast, fast, fast!”

Turtle Tula climbed slowly onto the porch. Tap… tap… tap. “I like to play too,” she said with a shy smile. “But I walk slow. That is okay, right?”

Benny nodded. “Yes, Tula. Everyone can play,” he said. He looked at his baskets. He looked at his friends. He wanted the game to be fair. Fair means everyone gets a kind chance.

Benny clapped his paws. “Let’s make a porch plan,” he said. “We will decide together.” He pointed to the baskets. “We can play Pebble Toss, Pinecone Balance, or Leaf Stretch. What do you think?”

Chippy jumped high. “Race! Race! Race!” He wiggled his whiskers.

Sunny tilted her head. “Pebble Toss sounds fun,” she said. “Plink, plink!”

Tula took a breath. “Leaf Stretch,” she said softly. “I can stretch slow.”

Benny felt a squeeze in his tummy. They all wanted different things. “Hmm,” he said. He sat on the warm porch step. He could smell the sweet wood. He listened to the birds again. He took a fair-think breath in and out.

“We need a fair choice,” Benny said. “Fair means we listen, we share, and we take turns.” He smiled at his friends. “Let’s try all three games. We will decide the order together.”

“How?” asked Chippy.

Benny had an idea. He held up three leaves: one red, one yellow, one green. They felt smooth in his paws. “We will pick a leaf,” he said. “Red is Pebble Toss, yellow is Pinecone Balance, green is Leaf Stretch. We will pick with eyes closed. That is fair.”

Sunny clapped. “I like that! It is a fair pick.”

Benny put the leaves in a hat. He shook the hat. Rustle, rustle. “Tula, you may pick,” he said kindly.

Tula closed her eyes and drew out a green leaf. “Leaf Stretch,” she said. “Yay.” Her smile was slow and warm.

“Leaf Stretch first!” cheered Benny. He showed them how. “Arms up like tree branches,” he said. The sun felt cozy on their fur and shells. “Now bend side to side. Sway like the trees.” Swoosh, swoosh. They giggled. Their bodies felt strong and calm.

After a few stretches, Benny said, “Next we will pick again.” He held out the hat. Sunny picked the yellow leaf. “Pinecone Balance!” she laughed.

Benny put a pinecone on his head. “Walk to the rocking chair and back,” he said. “Slow steps.” Tula smiled. “Slow is good for me,” she said. Chippy tried to go fast, but the pinecone fell. Plop! He giggled and tried again, slower this time. “Slow can be fun,” Chippy said, surprised. The porch boards felt bumpy under their careful feet.

Last, the red leaf came out. “Pebble Toss!” shouted Chippy. Benny drew a chalk circle on the porch floor. The chalk felt dusty on his paw. “Stand behind the line,” said Benny. “One, two, three, toss!” Plink, plink, plink went the pebbles in the basket. They cheered for each friend. “Good try, Sunny!” “Nice toss, Tula!” “Great focus, Chippy!”

When the games were done, they sat together on the step. The porch smelled like sunshine and pine. A soft breeze touched their faces. Benny passed around cold water in little cups. Sip, sip. It felt cool and nice.

Sunny said, “That was fair. We all got what we wanted.”

Chippy nodded. “And I learned to go slow sometimes. That helped me!”

Tula smiled. “And I felt brave to play with friends.”

Benny’s heart felt big and light. “We listened. We took turns. We decided together. That is fairness,” he said. “Fairness helps everyone have fun.”

They bumped paws and shell softly. “Team Porch!” they cheered.

Benny put the baskets away. Swish, swish went the broom one last time. The porch was peaceful again. The birds sang a sweet song. Benny felt proud. “Today we practiced fairness, decision-making, and cooperation,” he said. “We can do it again tomorrow.”

The End.
