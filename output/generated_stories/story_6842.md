# Sunny Paws and the Helpful Shed

Sunny Paws was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and a tiny round nose. One bright morning, he padded to Grandma Maple’s garden shed. The shed smelled like warm wood and sweet soil. Birds chirped. A breeze tickled Sunny Paws’ ears.

Grandma Maple waved. “Hello, Sunny Paws! I’m sorting seeds for the neighborhood garden. Would you like to help?”

Sunny Paws’ eyes sparkled. “I can help! I love to help,” he said. He felt a wiggle of happy energy in his tummy.

Inside the shed, jars lined the wall. The jars were full of rattling seeds—sunflowers, beans, and bright marigolds. The floor was dusty. A basket of gloves was tipped over. A shovel leaned against the door and made a tiny clink.

Grandma Maple smiled. “Today, we can volunteer. That means we help for free because we care. We help our neighbors grow food and flowers.”

“Volunteer,” Sunny Paws repeated slowly. “Help because we care.”

Grandma Maple showed him a calm breath. “Before we start, let’s be mindful. That means paying attention. Let’s use our bear breaths. In… one… two. Out… one… two.”

Sunny Paws closed his eyes and listened. He heard the wind whisper shh, shh. He smelled the woody shed and the lemon soap on Grandma’s hands. He felt the cool, smooth jar in his paws. In… one… two. Out… one… two. His wiggles turned into warm, steady calm.

“Ready,” he said with a smile.

“Let’s begin a tradition,” said Grandma Maple. “Every spring, our family sorts seeds together. My grandmother taught me. Now I teach you. We sing a seed song, too.”

Grandma Maple hummed a gentle tune. “Little seeds, small and round, find your home in the soft brown ground.” Sunny Paws joined in, humming softly. The song made the work feel cozy.

“First job,” Grandma said. “Pick up the gloves.”

Sunny Paws gathered the gloves and patted them into a neat pile. “Soft,” he said, brushing the fuzzy fabric on his face.

“Thank you,” Grandma said. “Next, let’s sort sunflower seeds. They are big and striped.”

Sunny Paws tipped a jar. Rattle, rattle, rattle went the seeds in his paw. “They look like tiny boats,” he giggled.

“Good noticing,” said Grandma Maple. “That is mindfulness. We see, we hear, we feel.”

They made a little assembly line. Grandma poured. Sunny Paws sorted. He placed the sunflower seeds into a bowl with a sun sticker. He placed the bean seeds into a bowl with a green leaf sticker. He placed the marigold seeds into a bowl with a flower sticker.

“Cooperation means we work together,” Grandma said. “See how fast and fun it is when we share the job?”

Sunny Paws nodded. “Teamwork!” he said. His paws went pat, pat as he placed each seed.

A gentle knock sounded at the door. Knock, knock. It was Mr. Robin with a red chest. “Cheep! Do you need help?” he asked.

Sunny Paws smiled. “We’re volunteering! We sort seeds for everyone.”

Mr. Robin hopped in and used his beak to point at spilled twine. “Cheep-cheep! I can roll the string.” He nudged and rolled until the twine was a tidy ball.

“Thank you,” Sunny Paws said. “Cooperation!”

Soon, a small bunny named Clover peeked in. “I brought a carrot snack,” she said shyly. “I can share.”

Grandma Maple clapped softly. “Sharing is caring. We have apple slices, too.” The snack was crunchy and sweet. Sunny Paws licked a drop of juice from his chin. “Mmm,” he said.

After the snack, Sunny Paws saw a muddy footprint on the floor. He took a broom. Swish, swish. He took a cloth. Wipe, wipe. The shed began to shine in the soft sun. He felt proud.

Grandma Maple handed him a tiny bell. “In our family tradition, we ring this bell when the shed is ready, to say thank you for the work.”

Sunny Paws looked at the bell. It was smooth and cool in his paw. He took a bear breath. In… one… two. Out… one… two. Then he rang the bell. Ting-a-ling! The sound was bright and happy.

“Now,” Grandma said, “let’s make seed packets for our neighbors. We will write their names and draw pictures.”

Sunny Paws drew a big yellow sun on one packet for Mr. Sun, the turtle. He drew a pink flower for Ms. Daisy, the cow. He put a few seeds in each packet. Tap, tap, tap. He felt the tiny bumps. He whispered, “Grow well, little seeds.”

At the end, the shed was neat. The jars were full and labeled. The broom rested in the corner. The gloves sat in a basket like cozy kittens.

Neighbors arrived one by one. Mr. Sun the turtle smiled slowly. Ms. Daisy mooed softly. “Thank you, Sunny Paws,” they said. “Thank you, Grandma Maple.”

Sunny Paws’ heart felt full and warm. He looked around at his friends. He listened to the birds and the bell’s last tiny echo. He smelled apples and soil. He felt the smooth jar and the soft glove. He felt calm and proud.

Grandma Maple patted his shoulder. “What did we learn today?”

Sunny Paws thought. “Volunteering means helping because we care,” he said. “Mindfulness helps us pay attention. Traditions help us remember and share. Cooperation makes work easy and fun.”

Grandma Maple beamed. “Yes. Helping hands make happy hearts.”

Sunny Paws smiled wide. “I like to help. I will volunteer again.”

The neighbors cheered. “Hooray for Sunny Paws!”

Ting-a-ling went the little bell, sounding like a bright thank you.

The End.
