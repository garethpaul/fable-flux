# Leo’s Gentle Roar of “I’m Sorry”

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved the tiny study corner in his den. It had a small table, a basket of crayons, a stack of picture books, and a big, cozy pillow. Sunlight made warm stripes on the floor, and the air smelled like crayons and paper. Leo’s tail swished with excitement. Today was his “Big Learning Day.”

“Today,” Leo told himself, “I have a goal!” He put a sticker chart on the wall. “Goal one: listen with my ears. Goal two: use gentle paws. Goal three: say sorry if I make a mistake.”

His best friends came to visit: Tiko the tortoise, who moved slowly and loved quiet, and Zuzu the zebra, who loved patterns and talked fast when she got excited.

“Hi!” Leo said with a brave smile. “Let’s learn together!”

Tiko blinked and smiled. “Slow and steady,” he said in his soft voice.

Zuzu pranced in, her hooves making soft tap-tap sounds. “Let’s draw! Let’s read! Let’s sing!”

Leo set out paper and crayons. “We can do all three,” he said. His heart felt big. He liked taking initiative. He liked making a plan.

They started with drawing. Leo held a red crayon and drew a bright sun. Zuzu drew zigzags and circles. Tiko carefully colored a green leaf. The crayons made scratchy, happy sounds.

“I am curious,” Leo said. “What are you drawing, Tiko?”

“A leaf,” Tiko said, smiling. “Leaves are calm.”

“I’m making a super zippy zebra road!” Zuzu laughed, her stripes almost dancing.

Leo clapped his paws. “We are different, and that is good,” he said. “We can celebrate our differences!”

They moved to picture books. Leo opened a book about stars. The pages smelled like ink and adventure. Zuzu leaned close. Tiko took his time, one page at a time.

Then Zuzu saw a small drum in the corner. “Can we play?” she asked.

Leo’s eyes shone. “Yes! After we read one more page,” he said. He was trying to follow his goals.

But Leo felt extra brave and a little too fast. He grabbed the drum and gave it a big THUMP. The sound boomed around the study corner—boom, boom!

Zuzu giggled. “Loud!”

Tiko pulled his head into his shell. “Oh,” he whispered. The loud sound made him feel scared.

Leo’s heart thumped like the drum. He hadn’t used gentle paws. He had not listened to Tiko’s need for quiet. He saw Tiko’s shell tremble. Zuzu stopped giggling and looked worried.

Leo took a slow breath. He looked at his sticker chart. Goal three: say sorry if I make a mistake.

He put the drum down. He walked to Tiko and sat on the floor. He spoke softly. “Tiko,” he said, “I made a loud boom. It was too loud for you. I am sorry.”

Tiko peeped out, his eyes warm. “Thank you for saying sorry,” he said. “That helps.”

Leo nodded. “I will try again. I will use gentle paws. Can we make a quiet beat together?” He tapped the drum with just one finger—tap… tap… tap. The sound was soft, like raindrops.

Zuzu smiled. “I can clap softly,” she said, clapping like a butterfly. “Flap, flap.”

Tiko tapped the table with a tiny toe—tip… tip… tip. “This feels safe,” he said.

Leo felt lighter. His apology was like a gentle roar that made room for everyone. “We are all different,” he said. “That is special! Tiko likes calm. Zuzu likes zippy. I can like both. I can be brave and gentle.”

They made a quiet song together. Tap… flap… tip… The study corner felt cozy again. The sun stripes made the floor look like a golden zebra path. The crayons smelled sweet, and the soft sounds made Tiko smile.

“New plan,” Leo said. “Let’s set a goal for each of us.” He pointed to the chart. “Tiko’s goal: feel safe and calm. Zuzu’s goal: have fun with sounds. Leo’s goal: listen and be gentle.”

Zuzu nodded. “And we can switch! We can try each other’s way.”

They took turns. First, quiet time: Leo read in a soft voice, and Tiko relaxed on the pillow. Then, fun time: Zuzu led a gentle dance with whisper claps. Leo watched his friends carefully and matched their pace.

When they finished, Leo added a sticker to each goal. “We did it,” he said. “We met our goals together.”

Tiko smiled wide. “Thank you for saying sorry and for listening.”

Zuzu nuzzled Leo’s shoulder. “You are brave and kind,” she said. “Different is wonderful.”

Leo felt proud and peaceful. “When we make a mistake,” he said, “we can say ‘I’m sorry,’ make a new plan, and try again.”

The friends hugged. The study corner glowed in the warm, golden light. Leo’s heart felt like a soft drum, steady and kind.

The End.
