# Nutmeg’s World Lab Day

In a cozy house on Maple Street, there was a basement lab that hummed softly. It smelled like warm cookies from upstairs and a little bit like crayons. Lights blinked. Buttons clicked. Maps hung on the wall.

A resourceful squirrel named Nutmeg lived there. Nutmeg wore a tiny tool belt and a bright blue scarf. Her tail was fluffy and tickly. She loved to solve problems.

“Good morning, lab!” Nutmeg chattered. “Today is World Day!”

She pushed a big green button. Beep! The World Table lit up. It showed small models: little houses, tiny trees, tiny boats, and tiny bridges. Each model made a gentle sound. Ding. Bloop. Clink.

Nutmeg’s best friends, a curious ladybug named Dot and a kind mouse named Milo, scurried in.

“What is World Day?” asked Dot, her wings whispering, “whirr, whirr.”

“World Day means we learn how everyone belongs,” Nutmeg said. “We listen. We share. We help, even if we are far apart.”

Milo sniffed the warm cookie air. “Mmm. I like to share.”

Nutmeg pointed to the World Table. “Look. The tiny bridge is stuck. The little boat cannot pass.” The boat made a soft “blub blub.”

“We can help,” said Dot. “But how?”

“First, we use our thinking steps,” Nutmeg said. “Step one: Look and listen.”

They leaned close. The stuck bridge squeaked, “Eee.” The boat’s bell went, “ding-ding.” Tiny figures on the shore waved tiny flags from many places. Some flags had stars. Some had stripes. Some had suns.

“What are the flags saying?” Milo whispered.

Nutmeg turned a knob. Click. Small speakers whispered many gentle voices. “Hello!” “Hola!” “Ni hao!” “Jambo!” “Namaste!” The words sounded different. They felt like little songs. Dot smiled. “So many ways to say hello!”

“Step two: Ask kind questions,” Nutmeg said. She spoke into the talk-tube. “Hello, friends. We see your bridge is stuck. How do you open it?”

A tiny voice answered, “We sing to start. Then we turn the handle.” Another voice added, “We bow before we begin.” Another said, “We count to three together.”

Nutmeg nodded. “Different ways. All special.”

Milo’s whiskers twitched. “Which way is right?”

Nutmeg’s eyes were bright. “All ways can be right. We can make room for each way.”

“Step three: Make a plan,” Nutmeg said. She took out a pencil that smelled like cedar. “Plan: We will listen to every voice. We will add every step.”

Dot fluttered. “Let’s try!”

Nutmeg spoke into the tube again. “Friends, we will do all your steps together. We will sing. We will bow. We will turn. We will count.”

Tiny cheers floated up. “Yay!”

Nutmeg raised her paw. “Ready, team? First, we sing.” She sang a simple tune, “La-la-la,” soft and warm. Dot hummed, “mmm.” Milo tapped a tiny drum. Tump-tump.

“Now we bow,” Nutmeg said. They bowed gently. Their noses almost touched the table. It smelled a little dusty.

“Now we count,” said Milo. “One, two, three!” His voice was small but brave.

“Now we turn!” Nutmeg grasped the model’s little handle. It was cool and smooth. She turned it slowly. Creak… creak… creak…

The bridge lifted! The boat’s bell rang bright. Ding-ding! The tiny people waved. Confetti popped—soft “poof!” sounds and paper stars floated.

“We did it!” Dot giggled. Her wings went “whirr-whirr-whirr!”

“We did it together,” Nutmeg said. Her heart felt warm and big. “We used listening. We used kindness. We used thinking.”

The World Table glowed again. A tiny garden looked dry. The flowers drooped like sleepy kittens.

Dot peered closer. “They need water.”

Milo looked at the map. “Some friends keep water in a tall jar. Some use small cups. Some save rain in a barrel.”

Nutmeg nodded. “All good ideas. Let’s blend them.” She reached for her mini-tools. Clink! She built a little path. A big jar poured into small cups. Drip, drip, drip. The cups poured into the barrel. Plip-plop. The barrel tipped and sent a gentle shower. Shhhhh.

The flowers lifted their heads. The garden smelled fresh. It smelled like mint and wet leaves. Dot giggled as a tiny rainbow winked. “We did it again!”

Nutmeg patted her tool belt. “We respected many ways. We shared the job. We made a new way together.”

Milo smiled. “We are good world friends.”

A tiny beep-beep sounded. The World Table showed a circle of hands in many colors. A soft voice said, “Thank you for caring. Thank you for listening. Thank you for being fair.”

Nutmeg’s tail swished happily. “That is global citizenship,” she said. “It means we care for our big world. We listen to different people. We tell the truth. We take turns. We make plans that help everyone.”

Dot hovered near Nutmeg’s ear. “It also means we say hello in many ways!” She buzzed, “Hello, hola, jambo!”

They all laughed. The sound felt like sunshine.

Nutmeg turned off the lights. Click. The lab was quiet, cozy, and safe. The cookie smell drifted down again from the kitchen. Their tummies rumbled.

“Snack time,” said Milo.

“Yes,” Nutmeg said. “We will share. One for you. One for me. One for Dot. Fair and kind.”

They sat together, nibbling. Crunch, crunch. Crumbs tickled their paws. Nutmeg looked at her friends and at the resting World Table.

“Today we learned a big lesson,” she said softly. “When we listen and include everyone, the world works better. That is how we are good neighbors to all.”

Dot nodded. “We can do it again tomorrow.”

“Yes,” said Nutmeg. “Every day can be World Day.”

The End.
