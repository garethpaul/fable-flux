# Whiskers Tells the Truth

The home office was quiet and cozy. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. The computer hummed softly. Papers rustled when the fan blew. A clever cat named Whiskers liked this room very much.

Whiskers had bright green eyes and soft gray fur. He wore a tiny blue bandana. “I help,” he liked to say. Today, he wanted to help his best friend, Sam, who was drawing a picture at the big desk.

Sam held up a crayon drawing. “Look, Whiskers! It’s a tree with birds. We can hang it by the window.”

Whiskers sniffed the crayons. They smelled sweet and waxy. He purred. “It’s beautiful, Sam.” He curled his tail and jumped onto the chair. His paws landed with a soft thump.

On the desk was a shiny water bottle and a small green plant. “We keep plants happy,” Sam said. “We give them a little water, not too much.” There was also a box of paper with a label: “Use Both Sides!”

Sam tapped the box. “We save paper to help trees. Only take a page if you need it.”

Whiskers nodded. “Save trees. Be kind to Earth.”

Sam smiled. “I’m going to get some tape,” he said. “Don’t touch the computer, okay?”

“Okay,” said Whiskers, swishing his tail.

When Sam left, the room felt very still. Whiskers listened. Tick-tick went the clock. Hummm went the computer. Drip? Whiskers heard a tiny drip. He sniffed the air. The plant’s little saucer was wet. The water bottle had tipped over. A shiny bead of water rolled across the desk.

“Oh no,” whispered Whiskers. He reached out a paw. The bead touched his fur. It felt cold and slippery. The water rolled, rolled, and touched the corner of Sam’s drawing. The paper curled.

Whiskers gasped. His heart went thump-thump. “I must fix it!” He grabbed a soft cloth from the shelf and dabbed, dabbed, dabbed. The paper got less wet, but the corner still wrinkled.

Just then, his paw bumped the keyboard. The screen lit up with a bright click. A page popped open. “Me-ow!” Whiskers froze. He hadn’t meant to touch it.

Footsteps! Sam came back with tape. “Whiskers, did you touch the computer?” he asked, eyes wide. He saw the wrinkled drawing corner and the damp cloth.

Whiskers felt warm and wiggly inside, like a worry worm. He thought, Maybe I should say, ‘I didn’t.’ But he remembered Sam’s words about the plant and the paper. Be kind. Be careful. Tell the truth. His whiskers drooped.

He took a deep breath. “Sam,” he said softly, “I tried to help. The water bottle tipped. The plant got splashed. The drawing got a little wet. I bumped the keys. I am sorry.”

Sam looked at Whiskers. He looked at the water bottle. He looked at the cloth. He touched the wrinkled corner. Then he knelt down so his eyes were level with Whiskers.

“Thank you for telling me the truth,” Sam said gently. “That was brave.”

Whiskers blinked. “You’re not mad?”

“I’m a little surprised,” Sam said honestly. “But I’m not mad. Accidents happen. Honesty helps us fix them.”

Whiskers’s tail gave a hopeful flick-flick. “How can I help now?”

“Let’s work together,” said Sam. “We can save the picture and save paper.”

They set the drawing in the sun patch to dry. It warmed the room and smelled like dust and crayons. “Mmm, sunny,” purred Whiskers. Sam smiled and smoothed the wrinkled corner with gentle fingers. “We’ll use a scrap for the part that’s too crinkly,” he said, opening the “Use Both Sides!” box. He took a small leftover piece and drew a tiny leaf. “We can patch it. That way we don’t waste a whole new page.”

Whiskers nodded. “Save paper. Save trees.”

“Exactly,” said Sam. They taped the little leaf over the wrinkled corner. It looked like a secret treasure on the tree. They laughed together. The laughter sounded like soft bells.

Next, they checked the plant. The soil felt damp and cool. Sam tipped the saucer to pour extra water back into the bottle. “We reuse water when we can,” he said. “Plants like a sip, not a bath.”

Whiskers sniffed the soil. “Earthy,” he said, wrinkling his nose. He pressed his paw to the keyboard and then quickly pulled it back. “Oops!”

Sam chuckled. “Let’s make a new rule,” he said kindly. “Paws off the keys. But paws can pat the cloth, hold the paper, and help me recycle.”

“Paws promise,” said Whiskers, placing a paw on his heart.

They made a little sign together: “Tell the Truth. Use Both Sides. Little Sips for Plants.” Sam drew pictures, and Whiskers added tiny paw prints. They taped the sign above the desk. The tape made a soft rip sound. Rip, press. Rip, press.

The drawing was dry now. The tree with birds looked happy. The patched corner made the tree look even more special, like it wore a leaf badge. Sam held it up to the window. Sunlight shined through, and the colors glowed. Whiskers’s chest felt warm.

“I’m proud of you,” Sam said. “You were honest. You helped fix the problem. You cared for our things and our planet.”

Whiskers purred loud and long. “I told the truth. We saved paper. We helped the plant. And we are friends.”

“We’re best friends,” said Sam, giving Whiskers a gentle hug. Whiskers smelled soap and oranges on Sam’s shirt. It made him feel safe.

They hung the picture by the window. The computer hummed. The plant stood tall. The home office felt calm again.

Whiskers curled up in the sun patch. “Honesty feels good,” he whispered, eyes closing.

“It sure does,” said Sam. “Honesty helps friends, and it helps the Earth too.”

The End.
