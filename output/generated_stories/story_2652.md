# Poppy Panda’s Pause and Play

Poppy the panda sat on a round, soft stool in the musical practice room. The room smelled like shiny wood and lemon polish. Sunlight peeked through the tall window, drawing warm squares on the floor. Drums were stacked like pancakes. A big gong shimmered like a golden moon. Bamboo flutes lay in a neat line, smooth and cool.

Poppy held a tiny drum. Tap-tap-tap, she played a little beat. Her black-and-white ears perked up. “I want to play fast! Like thunder!” she said.

Grandma Mei, a wise old panda, stood by the window. She wore a red scarf with tiny golden leaves. “We will share a tradition today,” she said softly. “We will learn a spring song for the picnic parade.” She held up a bamboo flute and played a gentle tune. It sounded like wind in tall grass. It sounded like soft rain on leaves.

Poppy tried to copy. Tap-tap—oops! Her drumstick slipped. It clacked on the floor. “Grrr,” Poppy grumbled. Her cheeks felt hot. Her paws felt tight.

Grandma Mei smiled. “Breathe, little bamboo. In and out. We practice with patience.”

Poppy took a big breath in. She smelled lemon polish and wooden drums. She breathed out. The warm air tickled her whiskers. “Okay,” she said.

Grandma Mei showed a slow pattern. “Tap… tap… rest,” she said. “We listen in the rest. Listening is part of music.”

Poppy tried again. Tap… tap… rest. She listened. She heard the clock go tick-tock. She heard a bird sing outside. She heard her own heart go thump-thump. She smiled a little.

But when Grandma added a new beat, Poppy’s paws rushed. Tap-tap-tap—clack! The drumstick fell again. “This is hard!” Poppy’s voice got loud. Her tail flicked. She stomped a paw.

Grandma Mei nodded. “It is hard. That is okay. When we feel big feelings, we can pause.” She tapped her chest. “We pause here.”

“How?” asked Poppy.

“We go outside,” said Grandma Mei. She opened the window. A soft breeze came in. It smelled like green grass and sweet flowers. “Outside helps our inside calm down.”

Poppy’s eyes lit up. “Outside?” she said. She loved the garden. She loved tall bamboo and bright butterflies.

They stepped into the garden. The air felt warm. The sun kissed Poppy’s fur. Leaves rustled like quiet hands clapping. Poppy wiggled her toes in the soft dirt. “It feels nice,” she whispered.

Grandma Mei pointed to a smooth stone path. “We will do a tradition walk,” she said. “Step and count. One, two. Pause and breathe. Smell the flowers. Listen.” They walked. Step, step. Pause. Poppy smelled a pink blossom. It smelled like candy and rain. She listened. Bees hummed. A little stream giggled over rocks.

Poppy’s shoulders dropped. Her chest felt light. “Grandma, I feel better.”

Grandma Mei smiled. “Yes. The outside helps us practice patience. Now we try the song again.”

They went back to the musical practice room. It was still and bright. The drums waited. The gong glowed. Poppy sat on the soft stool. She took one deep breath. She remembered the garden. Bees hummed. The stream giggled. She felt calm.

“Tap… tap… rest,” said Grandma Mei.

Poppy played. Tap… tap… rest. She listened to the quiet. She smiled.

“Now add the new beat,” Grandma said. “Tap-tap, tap—rest.”

Poppy tried. Tap-tap, tap—rest. The drum felt warm under her paw. The drumstick felt steady. She did not rush. She did not stomp. She paused and breathed. She thought of stepping on the stone path. One, two. Pause. Breathe.

Grandma Mei lifted her flute and played with Poppy. The room filled with sweet notes and soft taps. The sound wrapped around them like a hug. Poppy’s ears tingled. Her heart felt proud.

“Again!” Poppy said, giggling. They played the spring song three times, then four. Each time, Poppy paused and listened in the rests. Each time, she felt strong and calm.

Grandma Mei clapped her paws. “You did it, little bamboo. You used patience. You used the outside to calm your inside. That is our tradition.”

Poppy beamed. “Can we share this tradition at the picnic parade?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Grandma Mei. “We will play the song. And we will invite everyone outside to move and breathe before we play.”

On parade day, Poppy and Grandma Mei met friends in the park. The sky was bright and blue. The grass was cool on bare paws. Poppy said, “Step and count with me! One, two. Pause and breathe. Smell the flowers. Listen.” Children and grown-ups walked the stone path. They smiled. Their shoulders relaxed.

Then Poppy tapped her drum. Tap… tap… rest. Grandma Mei played her flute. The song floated through the trees. Birds sang along. The crowd clapped and swayed. Poppy felt the sun on her fur and the wind on her face. She felt calm and happy.

After the song, Poppy said, “When I get mad or rush, I take a pause. I go outside. I breathe. Then I try again.” Her friends nodded.

Grandma Mei hugged her. “Patience grows, little bamboo. And outdoor play helps it grow.”

Poppy hugged back. “I will share our walk and our song,” she said.

And she did.

The End.
