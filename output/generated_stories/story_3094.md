# Polly's Painting Problem

In a cozy study corner filled with books and art supplies, lived a bright green parrot named Polly. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds in the warm sunlight that streamed through the window. Polly loved to create beautiful pictures with her paintbrush held carefully in her beak.

"Tweet, tweet! Today I'll paint the most wonderful picture ever!" chirped Polly excitedly, flapping her colorful wings.

Polly dipped her brush in bright red paint and began to work on her canvas. But oh no! A big blob of paint dripped right in the middle of her picture.

"Oh my!" squawked Polly, her feathers ruffling with frustration. "This isn't going right at all!"

She tried again with blue paint, but her wing bumped the water cup, spilling it everywhere. The blue paint ran across her paper like a little river.

"Squawk! This is too hard!" Polly cried, feeling her heart beat fast with worry. "I'll never make a good painting!"

Just then, she heard a tiny voice. "Excuse me," squeaked a small field mouse who lived behind the bookshelf. "I couldn't help but notice you seem upset."

The little mouse had big brown eyes and a twitchy pink nose. She looked up at Polly with a kind expression.

"I'm trying to paint, but everything keeps going wrong!" Polly explained, her voice shaky. "The paint drips and spills, and my picture looks messy!"

The mouse smiled warmly. "You know what? When I feel frustrated, I take three deep breaths. Want to try it with me?"

Polly nodded. Together, they breathed in slowly through their noses - one, two, three - and then breathed out. Polly felt her feathers settle down and her heart slow to a gentle rhythm.

"That feels better," Polly admitted. "But I still don't know how to fix my painting."

"Maybe," suggested the mouse gently, "you could turn those drips and spills into something beautiful. What if that red blob became a big, round sun? And that blue streak could be a flowing river!"

Polly's eyes grew bright and sparkly. "Oh! You're so kind to help me think of new ideas!"

"Sometimes the best art comes from happy accidents," the mouse said with a giggle.

Polly picked up her brush again, but this time she felt calm and excited. She painted yellow rays around the red blob, turning it into a cheerful sun. She added green grass along the blue streak, making it a beautiful river. She painted purple flowers and orange butterflies dancing in the sky.

"Look!" Polly called out joyfully. "It's the most beautiful garden scene ever!"

The kind mouse clapped her tiny paws together. "You did that all by yourself! You didn't give up, and you turned your frustration into something wonderful."

Polly felt proud and happy. She had learned that when things don't go as planned, taking deep breaths helps her feel better. She also learned that being kind to others - just like the mouse was kind to her - makes everyone feel good.

"Thank you for being so kind and helping me," Polly said warmly. "Would you like to paint with me tomorrow?"

"I would love that!" squeaked the mouse.

From that day on, Polly remembered that with kindness, patience, and deep breaths, she could solve any problem that came her way.

The End.
