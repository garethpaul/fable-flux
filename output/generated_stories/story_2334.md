# Koala Kori’s Balcony Garden Day

Kori the koala was calm, gentle, and loved the quiet balcony garden. The balcony had tiny pots of mint, lemon balm, and sweet cherry tomatoes. The air smelled like sunshine and leaves. Bees buzzed far below. A soft breeze tickled Kori’s round ears.

“Today is garden day,” Kori said with a small smile. “I will water, weed, and taste one tomato.” Kori patted the watering can. “I am ready.”

Kori peeked at the sky. “Clouds might bring rain later,” Kori murmured. “I will prepare.” Kori put on a little sun hat, set a towel near the door, and placed a tiny umbrella by the pots. “Prepared and calm,” Kori whispered.

Just then, Kori’s friend, Pip the parrot, fluttered onto the railing with a happy squawk. “Let’s splash, Kori! Big water! Big fun!”

Kori blinked. “I planned small sips for the plants,” Kori said softly. “Too much water can make roots soggy.”

Pip puffed feathers. “But I love splashes! Can we do my way?”

Kori took a slow breath. The mint leaves brushed Kori’s paw and smelled fresh and cool. “I hear you,” Kori said. “You want big splashes. I want small sips. Maybe we can find a way that works for both of us.”

Pip tilted his head. “How?”

Kori looked around. The watering can was heavy. A little spray bottle sat on the shelf. “We can use the spray for fun mist,” Kori said, “and the can for gentle drinks.”

Pip’s eyes sparkled. “Mist is silly and soft! Can I spray?”

“Yes,” said Kori, smiling. “First, we prepare.” Kori set a tray under the pots to catch extra drops. “This helps the floor stay dry.”

Pip nodded. “Preparation! I like that word.”

Kori filled the can. “I will count to three for each pot. One, two, three.” The water made a happy glug-glug sound. The soil turned dark and smelled earthy and cozy.

Pip sprayed the air. Pssst, pssst. A tiny rainbow blinked in the sunlight. “Look, Kori! A rainbow mist!”

Kori giggled. “Beautiful. The plants get sips, and you get mist.”

They worked together. Kori trimmed a brown leaf. Snip. Pip gathered the leaf and sang, “Clean and green, clean and green!”

Then the wind picked up. Whoosh. A tomato vine wobbled. A small pot tipped and rolled toward the edge. Kori’s heart thumped. “Oh!”

Pip flapped fast and tapped the pot back with his beak. Clink. It stopped. Kori placed a little rope around the pots. “We will tie them gently. That is good preparation for wind.”

Pip breathed out. “We did it. We kept calm.”

Kori nodded. “We stayed steady. That is resilience. We try, even when it is windy.”

Pip fluffed. “And we can try again if something tips.”

Kori smiled. “Yes.”

Pip looked at the tomato plant. “Is it time to taste?”

Kori touched a bright red tomato. It was warm from the sun. “We can share one.” Kori paused. “You wanted to splash more, Pip. I wanted to water softly. We found a way to do both. That is called compromise.”

Pip blinked. “Com-pro-mise. A word like a bridge.”

“Yes,” said Kori. “A bridge between your idea and my idea.”

Pip chirped. “Then let’s share the tomato like a bridge too!”

Kori picked the tiny tomato. It made a soft pop as it came off the vine. Kori washed it with a small splash from the can. Pip held a leaf plate. They split the tomato. Squish! Juice dripped on Kori’s paw and smelled sweet.

“Mmm,” Pip said. “It tastes like sunshine.”

Kori nodded. “And teamwork.”

Clouds rolled in, gray and puffy. A few raindrops tapped the railing. Tap, tap. Pip shivered. “Rain!”

Kori opened the tiny umbrella over the delicate mint. “We prepared for rain,” Kori said. “The tray will catch extra water. The rope will keep pots safe.”

They stood under the doorway, listening. The rain sounded like soft drums. Pip leaned close. “I had fun with the mist.”

“I had fun with the gentle watering,” Kori said. “We both had fun because we compromised.”

The rain slowed, and the air smelled extra fresh. Kori took a deep breath. “Today we learned three things. One: prepare. Hats, trays, ropes, and umbrellas help us be ready. Two: be resilient. When wind comes, we try again and stay calm. Three: compromise. We make a bridge between ideas.”

Pip bobbed. “Prepare, be resilient, and compromise. I can remember that!”

Kori patted the railing, feeling it cool and smooth. “Next garden day, you can choose the music we play,” Kori said, “and I will choose the tools we use.”

Pip clapped wings. “Another compromise!”

They laughed together as a sunbeam slipped through the clouds and warmed the balcony garden. The mint leaves sparkled with tiny drops. The tomato plant stood tall and tied, safe and green.

Kori folded the umbrella, put away the tools, and took the towel inside. “All clean,” Kori said. “All ready for next time.”

Pip perched on Kori’s shoulder. “We make a good team.”

“We do,” Kori agreed, calm and happy. “When we listen and share, everyone grows.”

The End.
