# Wiggles in the Car

The little fox kit, Fenn, buckled into the back seat of the family car, wiggled from nose to tail. His fur was soft like a warm blanket. The seat felt smooth under his paws. The car smelled like pine gum and apple snacks. Raindrops tapped on the windows—tap, tap, tippity-tap.

“I feel wiggly,” Fenn said, his bright eyes wide. “Can we dance?”

Mama Fox smiled in the driver’s seat. “We’re driving, dear. In the car, we use safe bodies. Dancing has to wait.”

Fenn puffed a tiny sigh. “But my feet want to say hello.”

Papa Fox looked in the mirror. “Feet can talk later. For now, let’s help them whisper.” He handed Fenn a small blanket. “Wrap your wiggles.”

Fenn tucked the blanket over his lap. He pressed his paws together. He breathed in. He breathed out. The raindrops made a soft song. Fenn listened.

“Tap, tap, tippity-tap,” he whispered. “Hello, rain.”

Mama turned on the radio. A gentle drum beat came on. Thum, thum, thum. A singer’s voice floated like a feather. “La-la-la.”

Fenn’s ears perked up. “What does la-la-la mean?”

“It’s a sound for singing,” said Mama. “People sing with words and sounds. Dancing is like talking with your body.”

Fenn’s tail swished. “I want my body to talk!”

Papa chuckled. “I know. Dancing is wonderful. It can tell the truth about how you feel. That’s called integrity—being honest and kind, even with your body words.”

Fenn blinked. “Inte-gri-tee?”

Mama nodded. “Integrity means we keep our promises and use our words and moves the right way, at the right time.”

Fenn looked at his paws. “I promised to be safe in the car.”

“You did,” said Papa. “How can your body keep that promise and still enjoy the music?”

Fenn thought. The car hummed. The rain sang. The drum went thum, thum, thum. He wiggled one toe under the blanket. He tapped it very softly. Tap… tap…

“I can do quiet toe taps,” he said.

“That’s self-regulation,” Mama said gently. “You are steering your feelings like I steer the car. You can slow down, stop, or go.”

Fenn smiled. “I am steering my wiggles.”

The song changed to a cheerful tune with tiny bells. Ting, ting, ting! “Hola,” sang a voice. “Bonjour.” The words were bright and friendly.

“What are those words?” Fenn asked.

“They mean hello,” said Papa. “Hola is Spanish. Bonjour is French. Different languages say hello in different ways.”

Fenn tried the sounds. “Oh-la. Bon-zhoor.” The words felt bouncy on his tongue. He giggled. “My mouth is dancing!”

Mama clapped once, softly. “That is language appreciation. We listen and try new sounds. We show respect.”

Fenn sat up taller. “Can I dance with my words and toes?” he asked.

“You can, as long as it is safe,” Mama said. “Soft voice. Gentle toes. Seat belt on.”

Fenn nodded. He sang a tiny hello. “Hola.” Tap. “Bonjour.” Tap-tap. “La-la-la.” He made a small rhythm with his feet under the blanket and moved his paw tips like little waves. His shoulders wanted to bounce, but he glued them to the seat with his mind. “Stay,” he told them.

“Good steering,” Papa said.

The car slowed for a red light. Outside, a puddle shimmered. Two ducks splashed. Sploosh! Fenn’s eyes lit up. “They are dancing in the water!”

“Yes,” Mama said. “They are showing joy.”

Fenn closed his eyes and listened to the next song. It had a fast beat. His chest felt fizzy like soda. The fizz wanted to pop out as big kicks and spins. But he remembered his promise. He pressed his paws to the blanket, and he breathed with the beat. In, two, three. Out, two, three. The fizz calmed into tiny bubbles.

“I did it,” he whispered. “I kept my dance small.”

“That’s integrity,” Mama said. “You kept your promise even when it was hard.”

“And you found a way to dance with your breath,” Papa added. “That is a very smart dance.”

Soon, the car turned into a quiet parking lot next to a little park. The rain had stopped. A bright rainbow curved in the sky. Mama parked and unbuckled. “Now it is a good time and a good place,” she said. “Let’s dance on the soft grass.”

Fenn hopped out. The air smelled like wet earth and sweet leaves. The grass tickled his paws. He heard birds chirp, “Peep-peep!” He felt the wind hug his fur. He looked at Mama and Papa. “May I dance big now?”

“Go for it,” Papa said.

Fenn spun in a circle, slow like the moon. He hopped like the ducks—sploosh!—but on grass. He made shapes with his arms: a hello circle, an I-love-you heart. He sang, “Hola! Bonjour! Hello!” He tapped little raindrop steps. Tap, tap, tippity-tap. He swished his tail like a ribbon. His body told the truth: I am happy. I am safe. I kept my promise.

Mama danced too, swaying side to side. Papa did a silly knee bend that made Fenn giggle. They all danced together, each in their own way, each with kind, honest moves.

When they were done, Fenn took a deep breath. The rainbow faded to soft sky. He felt calm and proud.

“I learned,” Fenn said. “Dance is talking with my body. I can use integrity and self-regulation. I can wait for the right time. I can love words from many places. Hola! Bonjour! Hello!”

Mama kissed his forehead. “Yes, little dancer. You listened. You waited. You told the truth with your moves.”

Papa squeezed his paw. “And you found your own rhythm.”

Fenn placed a paw on his chest. Thum, thum, thum. “My heart drum,” he said. “It helps me steer.”

Mama smiled. “Your heart drum and your brave brain make a great team.”

Fenn nodded. “I can dance anywhere, even in the car, when I choose the right dance.”

They climbed back into the car, buckled up, and rode home with soft toe taps and gentle hellos. Fenn watched the trees sway like dancers outside, and he smiled.

The End.
