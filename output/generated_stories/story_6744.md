# Ruby Rabbit's Big Dinner Wait

Ruby Rabbit sat at the big dining room table. Her fluffy white ears twitched with excitement. The wooden chair felt hard under her soft fur. She could smell something wonderful cooking in the kitchen - something warm and yummy that made her tummy rumble.

"When will dinner be ready?" Ruby called out, bouncing a little in her seat.

"Soon, sweetie," came Mama's voice from the kitchen. "It needs more time to cook."

Ruby's pink nose wiggled. She could hear *sizzle, sizzle, sizzle* sounds from the kitchen. She could smell carrots and herbs floating through the air. Her favorite orange carrots! But waiting was so hard.

Ruby drummed her little paws on the shiny table. *Tap, tap, tap.* She swung her legs back and forth. The dining room felt so quiet and still. The big window showed the sun getting lower in the sky.

"Is it ready now?" Ruby asked again, her voice getting louder.

"Not yet, little bunny," Mama replied gently. "Good food takes time."

Ruby's ears drooped down. She wanted to hop and jump around the dining room. She wanted to run to the kitchen and peek at the food. Waiting made her feel wiggly all over!

She tried counting the flowers on the tablecloth. One daisy, two daisies, three daisies. But her mind kept thinking about dinner. She tried looking out the window at the birds, but her tummy kept rumbling.

"Mama, I'm really, really hungry!" Ruby called out, feeling frustrated.

Mama appeared in the doorway, wiping her paws on her apron. She had kind, understanding eyes. "I know waiting is hard, Ruby. But let me teach you something special."

Mama sat down next to Ruby at the table. "Close your eyes and take a deep breath with me," she said softly.

Ruby closed her eyes tight. She breathed in slowly through her pink nose. The kitchen smells filled her nose - warm, comforting, delicious.

"Now breathe out slowly," Mama whispered. "Feel how your body gets calm and peaceful."

Ruby breathed out. Her shoulders relaxed. Her wiggly feeling started to go away.

"Good things are worth waiting for," Mama said gently. "When we cook food slowly and carefully, it tastes much better. When we wait patiently, we feel proud and happy."

Ruby opened her eyes. She felt different - calmer and more peaceful. "Can we breathe together again?" she asked.

"Of course!" Mama smiled. They breathed slowly together, listening to the gentle cooking sounds from the kitchen.

Ruby noticed new things while they waited. The way the evening light made golden patterns on the wall. The soft *tick, tick* of the clock. The cozy feeling of sitting with Mama.

"You know what?" Ruby said thoughtfully. "Waiting isn't so bad when I breathe slowly."

Just then, a wonderful smell filled the dining room. "Now dinner is ready!" Mama announced.

Ruby's eyes sparkled with joy. The food smelled even more amazing than before. As Mama brought in a steaming bowl of carrot soup, Ruby felt proud. She had waited patiently, and it felt really good.

"This tastes like the best soup ever!" Ruby said, taking a warm, delicious spoonful.

Mama hugged her gently. "Good things really are worth waiting for, aren't they?"

Ruby nodded happily. She had learned that being patient made everything better - and more delicious too!

The End.
