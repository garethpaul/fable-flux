# Bear Cub's Living Room League

In a cozy, playful living room, a friendly bear cub named Benny bounced on his soft, squishy rug. The room smelled like warm cookies and clean blankets. Sunlight made bright squares on the floor, and toys waited in happy piles. Benny’s favorite ball, red and shiny, went boing, boing, boing.

“I love sports!” Benny cheered. “Today, I will make a living room league!”

He set up a small basket by the couch for hoop toss. He lined up pillows for hurdles. He put tape on the floor for a running lane. His fuzzy tail wiggled with excitement.

Mama Bear clapped from the armchair. “Go, Benny! Try your best. Think smart and be kind.”

Benny took a deep breath. He held the ball in his soft paws. “I can do it,” he whispered. “Bounce, look, toss.”

Boing. Boing. He tossed. Thunk! The ball hit the rim and rolled away. Benny’s ears drooped. “Oh no.”

He tried again. Boing. Boing. Toss! The ball bumped the basket and fell to the floor. “Ohhh,” Benny sighed. His tummy felt twisty.

Just then, two friends padded in: Tilly the tiny turtle and Max the squirrel with a swishy tail. They smelled the cookies and giggled.

“Hi, Benny!” said Tilly in a small, sweet voice. “What are you playing?”

“My living room league,” Benny said. “But I keep missing the basket.”

Max tapped his chin. “Let’s use a plan. Sports need a plan. That’s called strategy.”

Benny blinked. “Strah-teh-jee?”

“It means we think first,” Tilly said. “We look, we aim, then we toss.”

Mama Bear nodded. “Good teammates help each other.”

Benny felt a little braver. He wiped his paws on the rug. “Okay. Strategy. I will look, aim, toss.”

Tilly rolled closer. “Benny, bend your knees a little.”

Max pointed. “And aim for the square of sunlight by the basket. Try a gentle toss.”

Benny bent his knees. He focused his eyes on the warm, golden square. Boing. Boing. He tossed gently.

Swish!

The ball slipped through the basket. Benny’s eyes grew wide. “I did it!” He laughed and clapped his paws. His heart felt big and bubbly.

“Great job,” Mama Bear said. “What’s next in your league?”

“Pillow hurdles!” Benny lined up the puffy pillows. “We hop and we don’t knock them down.”

Max bounced first. “Hop! Hop! Hop!” He knocked one pillow. Thud. He frowned.

Tilly tried next. “Hop… hop… oops.” She bumped a pillow and hid her face in her shell.

Benny patted Tilly’s shell. “It’s okay. We can try again.”

Max twitched his tail. “Strategy again! Small hops. Soft feet.”

They giggled and tried together. Hop, hop, hop! Their feet made little thump-thump sounds on the rug. The pillows stayed up. “We did it!” Benny cheered. Tilly peeked out and smiled.

“Last game!” Benny said. “The running lane. We run from the door to the couch and back. Three tries. We keep trying, even if we get tired.”

Tilly took a deep breath. “I am small, but I can be strong.”

Max grinned. “I am fast, but I will go steady.”

Benny said, “I will be brave and keep going.”

They ran. Pat-pat-pat went their feet. The room smelled like warm air and a little bit of sweat. Benny puffed. “Whew!”

On the first try, Max ran too fast and slid into a pillow. “Whoa!” he squeaked.

On the second try, Tilly got tired and slowed down. “I need a plan,” she said softly.

On the third try, Benny’s paw slipped. “Oh no!” He wobbled but did not fall.

Max took a slow breath. “Let’s plan together. We count our steps to the couch. One, two, three, four, five. Then we turn gently.”

Tilly smiled. “And we cheer for each other.”

Benny nodded. “And if someone wins, we share the prize.”

“What’s the prize?” asked Max.

Benny sniffed the sweet air. “Cookies!”

They tried again. “One, two, three, four, five,” they counted. Turn. Run back. Pat-pat-pat.

They finished together, paws and feet in a happy pile. They laughed and hugged.

Mama Bear brought a plate. “Warm honey cookies! You all worked hard.”

Benny looked at the cookies. His mouth watered. He felt proud, like sunshine inside. “We all win,” he said. He gave the first cookie to Tilly. “For trying and trying.” He gave the next cookie to Max. “For smart planning.” He took the last cookie for himself. “For not giving up.”

They munched. Crunch, munch, yum. The cookies were sweet and sticky. Crumbs tickled their noses. They giggled.

Mama Bear smiled. “What did you learn today?”

Benny licked a crumb from his paw. “Sports are fun. We use strategy to play smart. We keep trying. And we share.”

Tilly nodded. “Small steps can be strong steps.”

Max swished his tail. “Being a good teammate feels best.”

Benny bounced his red ball one more time. Boing, boing. “Living room league forever!”

They all cheered, “Go team!”

The End.
