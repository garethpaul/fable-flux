# Koko the Koala and the Cookie Jar Mystery

Koko the koala loved helping Mama in the kitchen. With his soft gray fur and gentle smile, he was always ready to lend a paw. Today, Mama asked him to organize the pantry while she baked cookies.

"The pantry smells so good!" Koko said, sniffing the sweet scent of vanilla and cinnamon. He could hear the cookies baking in the oven - *tick, tick, tick* went the timer.

As Koko arranged the colorful boxes and jars on the shelves, he noticed something strange. The big cookie jar that usually sat on the middle shelf was almost empty! Only three cookies remained at the bottom.

"That's odd," Koko thought. "Mama just filled this jar yesterday with twelve chocolate chip cookies. I counted them myself!"

Just then, his little sister Penny bounced into the pantry. "Hi Koko! Whatcha doing?"

"Penny," Koko said calmly, "do you know what happened to the cookies in this jar?"

Penny's eyes grew wide. "I... I don't know," she whispered, but her whiskers twitched the way they always did when she wasn't telling the truth.

Koko felt a little upset, but he took a deep breath like Mama taught him. "Penny, it's important to be honest. Did you take the cookies?"

Penny looked down at her tiny paws. "Maybe I took one... or two... or maybe more," she mumbled. "But I was so hungry, and they smelled so yummy!"

Koko could have gotten angry, but instead he sat down beside his sister. "I understand you were hungry, Penny. But taking so many cookies without asking isn't fair to our family."

"What do you mean?" Penny asked, looking confused.

"Well," Koko explained gently, "when we share things fairly, everyone gets some. Mama made those cookies for all of us to enjoy together. When you took most of them, there weren't enough left for everyone."

Penny's bottom lip trembled. "I didn't think about that. I just wanted the cookies."

Koko hugged his sister with his fuzzy arms. "It's okay to make mistakes, Penny. But now we need to make it right. What do you think we should do?"

Penny thought hard, her little nose twitching. "Maybe... maybe I could help Mama make more cookies? And say sorry?"

"That's a wonderful idea!" Koko said proudly. "And next time, you can ask for a cookie instead of taking them. That way, everyone gets treated fairly."

Together, they walked to the kitchen where Mama was pulling fresh cookies from the oven. The warm smell filled the air like a cozy hug.

"Mama," Penny said bravely, "I need to tell you something. I took too many cookies from the jar without asking. I'm sorry."

Mama smiled and knelt down. "Thank you for being honest, Penny. How can we fix this?"

"Koko helped me think of a plan," Penny said. "I want to help make more cookies for everyone!"

"What a fair solution," Mama said, giving them both a warm hug. "And Koko, thank you for helping your sister learn about fairness and honesty."

As they mixed the cookie dough together, Koko felt proud. He had stayed calm, been assertive but kind, and helped his family solve their problem fairly.

"Being fair means everyone gets treated nicely," Koko said as he stirred the dough.

"And being honest makes everything better!" Penny added, giggling as flour dusted her whiskers.

Soon, the pantry jar was full again, and everyone enjoyed warm cookies together - the fair way.

The End.
