# Ellie's Special Words

Little Ellie the elephant calf was staying in Grandma's cozy guest room. The soft blue walls were covered with pictures of faraway places, and the bed had the fluffiest pillows that smelled like lavender flowers.

But Ellie felt sad. She curled up her little trunk and made a worried sound. "Mmph, mmph."

"What's wrong, sweet Ellie?" asked Grandma, peeking through the doorway. Her kind eyes twinkled behind her glasses.

Ellie's big brown eyes filled with tears. "Grandma, I can't talk like the other animals. The birds chirp beautiful songs. The cats say 'meow' so clearly. Even the dogs bark loud and proud. But I just make trumpet sounds that nobody understands."

Grandma sat on the soft carpet next to Ellie. She gently patted Ellie's gray head. "Oh, my dear little one. Did you know that every animal has their own special way of talking?"

"Really?" Ellie sniffled, her trunk wiggling with curiosity.

"Yes! Come, let me show you something magical." Grandma opened a big, colorful book on the guest room's wooden table. The pages made a gentle rustling sound.

"Look here," Grandma pointed to a picture of elephants in a sunny field. "Elephants have the most wonderful language of all! You can trumpet loudly to call your family from far away. You can rumble softly to say 'I love you.' You can even stomp your feet to make music!"

Ellie's ears perked up. "I can do all that?"

"Let's practice!" Grandma clapped her hands together. "Try making a happy trumpet sound."

Ellie took a deep breath and lifted her little trunk high. "PAAAAAA!" The sound echoed around the guest room, making the curtains flutter.

"Beautiful!" Grandma cheered. "Now try a gentle rumble to say hello."

Ellie made a soft, rumbly sound deep in her throat. "Rrrrrummmmm."

"Perfect! And what about stomping to make rhythm?"

Ellie carefully lifted one foot, then another, making a gentle pat-pat-pat on the carpet. She giggled as she made the pattern faster. Pat-pat-PAT! Pat-pat-PAT!

"Grandma, this is fun! I'm making music with my feet!"

"You certainly are! And look—" Grandma turned the page to show pictures of elephants talking to each other. "Elephants also touch trunks to say hello, flap their ears to stay cool and show excitement, and even paint pictures with their trunks!"

Ellie's eyes grew wide with wonder. "We can paint too?"

"Oh yes! Every part of your elephant language is special and important. Your trumpets, rumbles, stomps, and touches—they're all beautiful ways to share your feelings."

Ellie practiced all afternoon in the guest room. She trumpeted joy, rumbled contentment, and stomped happy rhythms on the soft carpet. The afternoon sunlight streamed through the window, making everything feel warm and golden.

"Thank you, Grandma," Ellie said, gently touching Grandma's hand with her trunk. "I understand now. My elephant language isn't different—it's wonderful!"

"That's right, sweet Ellie. Every language is special, and with practice, yours will grow stronger every day. The most important thing is to keep trying and never give up."

That night, Ellie fell asleep peacefully in the guest room, dreaming of all the beautiful elephant words she would learn tomorrow.

The End.
