# Melody's Big Helper Day

In a cozy living room filled with soft cushions and colorful toys, a little yellow bird named Melody perched on the windowsill. The morning sun made her bright feathers sparkle like tiny golden stars.

"Tweet-tweet! Good morning, world!" Melody sang cheerfully, flapping her small wings.

Through the window, she could see children from the neighborhood playing outside. There was Emma with her red curly hair, Ahmed wearing his favorite blue cap, and little Rosa carrying her stuffed elephant. But today, something seemed different.

Melody tilted her tiny head and listened carefully. She heard sniffling sounds coming from outside.

"Oh my!" chirped Melody. "Someone sounds sad. I wonder if I can help?"

She flew closer to the window and saw that Ahmed had dropped his lunch box. Apples and crackers were scattered all over the sidewalk, and tears rolled down his cheeks.

"I can't eat my lunch now," Ahmed said sadly. "And I'm so hungry."

Melody's heart felt warm and fluttery. She wanted to help her friend, even though she was just a small bird.

"Tweet-tweet! I have an idea!" she sang.

Melody flew into the kitchen area of the living room where the family kept a fruit bowl. Using her tiny beak, she carefully picked up a small, clean apple slice that had fallen near the bowl.

"This is heavy for a little bird like me," Melody puffed as she carried it. "But I can do it! I want to help Ahmed feel better."

She flew back to the window and gently dropped the apple slice onto the windowsill, then tapped on the glass with her beak.

"Tap-tap-tap!"

Ahmed looked up and saw the little yellow bird and the apple slice. His eyes grew wide with surprise.

"Did you bring this for me?" Ahmed asked softly.

"Tweet-tweet-tweet!" Melody sang, which meant "Yes! Friends help friends!"

Emma and Rosa noticed what was happening and came running over.

"Look! The little bird is sharing her food!" said Emma, clapping her hands.

"That's so kind!" added Rosa, hugging her elephant tighter.

Ahmed carefully took the apple slice from the windowsill. "Thank you, little bird. You're the nicest friend ever!"

Melody felt her chest puff up with pride. Even though she was small, she had made a big difference in her friend's day.

"I learned something important today," Ahmed said to his friends. "When we help each other, we all feel happier. Just like how this little bird helped me!"

Emma nodded. "My mom says that's what good neighbors do - we take care of each other."

"Even if we're different," added Rosa, "we can still be kind friends."

Melody sang her sweetest song, feeling proud and happy. She had completed her important task of helping a friend, and it made her feel wonderful inside.

As the children shared their snacks with Ahmed, Melody realized that being helpful wasn't about being big or strong. It was about having a caring heart and doing what you could to make others smile.

"Tweet-tweet-tweet!" she sang joyfully. "Being a good friend to everyone makes the whole world brighter!"

From that day on, Melody made it her special job to watch over all the children in the neighborhood, ready to help whenever someone needed a friend.

The End.
