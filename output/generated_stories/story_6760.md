# Leo’s Cozy Kitchen Adventure

The sun peeped through the window of a warm kitchen. Pots and pans gleamed. The air smelled like sweet oatmeal and cinnamon. A brave little lion cub named Leo padded in on soft paws. His nose twitched. Sniff, sniff! “Mmm,” Leo purred. “Breakfast smells yummy!”

Mama Lion hummed and stirred a pot. The spoon went tap-tap on the side. “Good morning, Leo,” she said with a smile. “Today is a big day. We will make a healthy snack together."

Leo’s eyes sparkled. “I want to help! I’m brave and strong!”

Mama nodded. “Yes, you are brave. And brave cubs take care of themselves first. Self-care helps us feel good and ready to help others.”

Leo blinked. “What is self-care?”

Mama lifted Leo onto a cozy stool. The wood felt warm under his paws. “Self-care means taking care of your body and your feelings. We clean up, eat good food, rest, and listen to our needs.”

Leo sat up tall. “I can do that!”

Mama pointed to a small shelf. On it were baskets with little picture labels: a toothbrush picture, a brush picture, a paw towel picture, and a water bottle picture. “This is our self-care shelf,” Mama said. “Everything has a place.”

Leo purred. “That’s organization! Like putting toys in a box.”

“Exactly,” Mama said. “When we are organized, it’s easier to take care of ourselves.”

Just then, a cheerful chirp came from the open window. Leo’s friend, Pip the parrot, flapped in with a flutter and a whoosh of air. Pip’s green feathers shone in the light. “Good morning!” Pip squawked. “I love this warm kitchen. It smells like hugs!”

Leo giggled. “Pip, we are doing self-care before snack time!”

Pip tilted his head. “Self-care? Can I learn too?”

“Of course,” Mama said. “Friends help friends.”

Mama handed Leo a soft paw towel. “First, we wash our paws.” The water went splash-splash. The bubbles were warm and tickly. Leo rubbed between his toes. He watched the suds swirl down the drain. “Clean paws feel happy,” he said, wiggling them.

“Happy paws, happy parrot!” Pip chirped, dipping his little claws in a tiny bowl of water. Drip, drop. He shook them dry with a tiny shake-shake.

Next, Mama gave Leo his small brush from the brush basket. “Brush your mane,” she said. “Slow and gentle.” Leo brushed. Swish, swish. His little mane fluffed up, soft like a cloud. He smiled. “I feel neat!”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “I will smooth my feathers. Smooth and neat!”

Mama pointed to the water bottle basket. “Now, a sip of water.” Leo took a sip. Cold and fresh! Pip took a tiny sip too. “Ahh,” they both said together.

“And a big breath,” Mama added. “Breathe in through your nose, out through your mouth. In… and out…” Leo’s tummy rose and fell. He felt calm, like a sleepy purr.

Pip closed his eyes and breathed with a soft whooo. “I feel light,” Pip said.

Leo looked at the shelf. One basket was messy. The toothbrush picture basket had combs and a ribbon stuck inside. Leo frowned. “This basket is mixed up. Things are lost.”

Mama nodded. “This is a discovery! When things are not in the right place, self-care is harder.”

Leo hopped down. “We can fix it! We are a team.” He and Pip emptied the basket on the counter. Clink, clink went the combs. The ribbon slid like a red snake. Leo read the pictures. “Comb goes in brush basket. Ribbon goes in hair ribbons jar. Toothbrushes go in the cup with the toothbrush picture.”

Pip cheered, “Organization station!” He sang a little song: “Find the place, take your time, put it right, it’s so fine!”

They sorted and put things away. The baskets looked neat and tidy. Leo’s chest felt warm. “I like when things have a home,” he said.

Mama smiled. “You discovered how organization helps self-care. Now it’s snack time!”

They mixed oats, banana, and a little honey. Squish, squish went the banana under Leo’s paw. The bowl smelled sweet and cozy. Pip pecked gently at a blueberry and rolled it into the bowl. “Blueberry ball!” he chirped.

They formed little round snack bites. Roll, roll. Their paws and claws felt sticky, and they giggled. “We will wash again,” Mama reminded them. “Self-care is every time we need it.”

After washing, Mama set the snacks on a plate. Leo took a bite. Chew, chew. “Yum,” he said. “My body feels happy.”

Pip munched a tiny piece. “My feathers feel brave!”

Leo looked at Pip. “Thank you for helping, friend.”

Pip bobbed. “Friends help friends take care of themselves.”

Mama hugged them both. The kitchen felt extra warm now, like sunshine and cinnamon. “What did we learn today?” she asked.

Leo thought. His tail swished. “I learned that self-care means washing, brushing, drinking water, and breathing calm breaths. I learned that being organized makes it easy. And I learned that friends can help each other.”

Pip added, “And I learned that discovery can be fun! We found a messy basket and made it neat!”

Leo grinned. “When I take care of me, I feel strong and ready to help.”

Mama nodded. “That is the best kind of brave.”

They stacked the clean bowls. Tap, tap. They put the baskets back in order. Everything had a place. The warm kitchen hummed with happy sounds—soft purrs, little chirps, and a gentle breeze at the window.

Leo took one more big, calm breath. In and out. He felt proud.

The End.
