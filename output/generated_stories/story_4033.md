# Poppy Parrot Finds a Place for Everything

Poppy the parrot loved colors. She loved the soft swish of paintbrushes, the tickle of feathers, and the sweet smell of glue. Poppy lived in a bright creative art room where sunbeams danced on the floor. Shelves held paints, paper, buttons, and beads. Cups held crayons and brushes. The room was a rainbow of happy things.

“Squawk! Art time!” Poppy chirped every morning. She flapped her green wings and hopped to the big table.

One windy day, Poppy was very excited. “I will paint a jungle!” she said. She opened blue paint. She opened green paint. She opened yellow paint. She pulled out glitter, pom-poms, feathers, and stickers. She spread them all over the table. She spread them on the floor. She spread them on a chair.

“Squawk! So many colors!” Poppy laughed. She dipped her brush. Swish, swish. She sprinkled glitter. Sprinkle, sprinkle. She glued feathers. Sticky, sticky.

But then Poppy wanted a small brush. “Where is my tiny brush?” she asked. She looked on the table. She looked on the chair. She looked on the floor. There were big brushes. There were crayons. There were buttons. But no tiny brush.

Poppy felt her tummy get tight. “Oh no,” she whispered. The room felt messy. Her wings felt fluttery. She made a small squawk that sounded worried.

Her friend, Miss Dot, peeked in. Miss Dot was a calm helper with kind eyes. She smiled and breathed in slowly. “Hello, Poppy. What a colorful room,” she said softly. “I hear little wings flapping fast. What happened?”

“I can’t find my tiny brush,” Poppy said. “I want to paint little leaves. I can’t find it anywhere! Squawk!”

Miss Dot nodded. “I know a trick,” she said. “When we cannot find, we can tidy our minds. We can tidy our space. Let’s be calm. Breathe with me.”

Poppy breathed in. One, two, three. She breathed out. One, two, three. Her tummy felt softer. Her wings felt slow and smooth.

“Good,” said Miss Dot. “Now we use knowledge. We will sort. We will put things where they belong. When everything has a home, we can find what we need. That is called organization.”

“Or-ga-ni-za-tion,” Poppy tried. The word felt big, but friendly.

Miss Dot pointed to the shelves. “Look. Brushes can live in the cup with the blue dot. Crayons can live in the box with the green stripe. Glue sticks can live in the tray with the sticky star. Paper can live in the bin with the paper picture.”

Poppy blinked. “Homes for things,” she said. “Like a nest!”

“Exactly,” said Miss Dot. “Things feel safe when they have a place. And so do we.”

They began to sort. Poppy fluffed her feathers and sang a tidy song. “Brushes in the blue cup, tap tap tap. Crayons in the green box, snap snap snap.”

She picked up brushes. Tap tap tap. She put them in the blue cup. She gathered crayons. Snap snap snap. Into the green box they went. She stacked paper. Shuffle, shuffle. Into the paper bin. She put glue sticks into the sticky star tray. Plop, plop. She shook pom-poms back into their jar. Plink, plink. The sounds were happy sounds.

“Let’s share the job,” said Miss Dot. “I’ll hold the jar. You pour.” Poppy poured the beads. Tinkle, tinkle. She smiled. “Thank you for sharing, Miss Dot.”

“And thank you for working with me,” Miss Dot said. “Sharing makes jobs lighter.”

Soon the table was clear. The floor was clear. The chair was clear. The room felt big again. Sunbeams danced again.

Poppy looked around. “Tiny brush, where are you?” she asked in a calm voice.

She saw a small handle peeking from the blue cup. “There you are!” Poppy cheered. “You were at home all along!”

Poppy dipped the tiny brush. Swish, swish. She painted tiny leaves. Tickly, tickly. She added a small yellow bird. Dot, dot. She glued two feathers for wings. Soft, soft. Her jungle painting looked bright and neat.

A little bunny friend named Nib came in, holding a crayon. “Can I draw too?” Nib asked shyly.

“Yes!” said Poppy. “Crayons live here.” She pointed to the green box. “When you are done, they go back home. That is our plan.”

Nib nodded. “I can do that.”

They drew together. They shared the crayons. They took turns with the glue. They used the blue cup for brushes. When they finished, Poppy said, “Let’s tidy together.” Nib smiled and helped. Brushes tap tap tap. Crayons snap snap snap. Paper shuffle, glue plop. The room stayed neat.

Poppy felt proud. “I feel calm,” she said. “I know where things go. I can find what I need. I can share with friends.”

Miss Dot clapped quietly. “You used organization. You used knowledge about where things belong. You used calm breaths. Look how well you worked.”

Poppy puffed her chest. “I have a tidy nest!” she sang. “A place for everything, and everything in its place.”

The sunbeams winked. The room smelled like clean paper and a little like orange soap. The jars made soft tinkle sounds when Poppy put on the lids. The shelves stood like helpers, holding each thing in its home.

Poppy looked at Nib and Miss Dot. “Tomorrow we can make a sky,” she said. “We will use blue paint. We will use cotton clouds. And when we finish, we will put everything back.”

Miss Dot smiled. “That is a wonderful plan.”

Poppy flapped her wings happily. “Squawk! Organized art is happy art!”

And from that day on, whenever Poppy started to make, she started to sort. She took a calm breath. She set out just what she needed. She made her art. And she put things back in their homes. It was fun. It was friendly. It was easy.

The lesson Poppy learned was simple: When we stay calm, use what we know, and keep things in their place, we can find what we need, share with friends, and have more time for fun.

The End.
