# Benny Bear Builds on the Rooftop

Benny was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, he climbed the wooden stairs to the rooftop garden of his apartment building. The air felt warm on his nose. A soft breeze tickled his ears. He could smell fresh soil and sweet flowers.

“Good morning, rooftop!” Benny said, waving his paw.

On the rooftop, there were planters, birdhouses, and small benches. But one corner was empty and bare. It looked a little lonely.

“I want to build something helpful,” Benny said. “I will build a little shade house for the plants, so they don’t get too hot.”

Benny took a deep breath. “I will be careful. I will be responsible,” he told himself. He remembered what Mama Bear always said: “Plan first, build second.”

Benny sat down with a small notepad. He drew a simple picture. “Four posts, a roof, and a door,” he said. “It needs to be safe and sturdy.” He tapped his chin. Tap, tap, tap. “How tall should it be?” He looked at the plants. “Not too tall, not too small.”

Just then, his friend, Miki the mouse, peeked out from a flower pot. “Squeak! What are you doing, Benny?”

“I am building a shade house,” Benny said. “Would you like to help?”

Miki squeaked happily. “Yes! Let’s plan together.”

They counted the wooden boards. “One, two, three, four,” Benny said. “We have enough for the posts. We need more for the roof.”

Benny checked the storage box on the rooftop. He found a small bag of nails, a tiny hammer, some string, and an old sheet. He sniffed the wood. It smelled like pine trees. He touched the boards. They felt smooth.

“Let’s reuse what we can,” Benny said. “It is good for the Earth when we use things again.”

Miki nodded. “That is environmental care!”

Benny put on his little safety hat. “Step one,” he said, “we choose the spot.” He looked around. “Not too close to the edge. Not in the walkway. Here is good.” He marked an X with chalk.

“Step two,” he said, “we make sure it is safe.” He swept the floor so there were no loose pebbles. The broom made a soft swish-swish sound.

“Step three,” he said, “we build.” Benny lifted a board carefully. “I am strong, but I will go slow.” He held the board while Miki tied string to help keep it steady. Tap, tap, tap went the little hammer. The sound echoed across the rooftop. Tap, tap, tap.

A pigeon fluttered down. “Coo! What are you building?” she asked, tilting her head.

“A shade house for the plants,” Benny said. “Do you need shade too?”

The pigeon cooed. “Sometimes. My eggs get warm.”

Benny smiled. “Then we will make the roof nice and wide.”

As they worked, a small gust of wind whooshed by. The old sheet flapped like a big white cloud. “Whoosh!” Benny giggled. “We need to tie it tight.”

Miki said, “Let’s be logical. The wind comes from that way. We tie the sheet on the windy side first.”

“That is smart thinking,” Benny said. He held the corner. Miki tied a strong knot. The sheet stayed still. “Good teamwork.”

They added a little door made from a short board and some string. Benny tested it. “Open.” He pushed gently. “Close.” He pulled the string. “It works!”

Just then, Benny heard voices. It was the squirrel twins, Pip and Pop. They carried a tiny watering can.

“What is that?” Pip asked.

“It looks fun!” Pop said.

“It is a shade house for the plants,” Benny explained. “Would you like to help water them when we are done?”

Pip and Pop nodded. “We can be responsible too!”

Benny smiled. “Thank you. Responsible builders clean up.” He picked up extra nails and put them back in the bag. He rolled up leftover string. He folded the empty bag and put it away. “We keep the rooftop tidy.”

When the shade house was finished, Benny stepped back. The plants looked happy under the soft, cool shade. A small bee buzzed by. Bzz, bzz. She landed on a flower and wiggled with joy.

Benny felt proud. His heart felt warm, like a hug. “We built something helpful,” he said. “We used what we had. We worked safely. We took care of the Earth.”

The pigeon cooed, “Thank you for sharing your shade.” She tucked her eggs nearby.

Benny turned to his friends. “This shade house is for everyone,” he said. “For plants, for birds, for friends. That is generosity.”

Pip poured water gently. “Drip, drip,” went the can. Pop held the door open. Miki checked the knots. Benny wrote a little sign: “Plant Shade House. Please be kind.”

As the sun lowered, the rooftop felt calm. The sky turned peach and purple. Benny packed the tools and locked the storage box. He looked at the tidy space and the happy plants.

“I learned today,” Benny said softly, “that building starts with a good plan. Being careful is being responsible. Sharing what we build is generous. And using things again helps our Earth.”

Miki squeaked, “We did it together!”

Benny grinned. “Yes. Together is the best way to build.”

They waved goodnight to the rooftop garden and went downstairs, feeling proud, helpful, and kind.

The End.
