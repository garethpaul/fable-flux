# Whiskers Learns to Listen

In the bright art room, Whiskers the clever cat sat by the paint jars. Her orange fur gleamed in the sunny light. She loved making colorful pictures with her tiny paws.

"Meow! I want to paint a rainbow!" Whiskers said excitedly. She dipped her paw in red paint. Splish-splash went the brush in the water cup.

Mrs. Robin, the art teacher, was talking to all the animal friends. "Today we will work together to make a big mural for the school," she chirped sweetly.

But Whiskers wasn't listening. She was too busy making swirly red circles on her paper. The paint felt cool and wet on her paws. "Look at my pretty picture!" she purred.

"Whiskers," called Bunny from across the room. "Mrs. Robin needs help mixing colors for everyone."

"Not now," Whiskers meowed. "I'm making my own art." She added blue paint to make purple swirls. The colors mixed like magic on her paper.

Soon, Whiskers heard sniffling sounds. She looked up with her bright green eyes. Little Mouse was crying by the empty paint cups.

"What's wrong?" Whiskers asked, tilting her furry head.

"I wanted to paint flowers," Mouse squeaked sadly. "But all the yellow paint is gone. I can't make my sunshine petals."

Whiskers looked around the art room. Bunny was sharing brushes. Puppy was helping clean up spills. Everyone was working together except her.

"Oh no," Whiskers whispered. Her ears drooped down. "I wasn't listening to Mrs. Robin. I was only thinking about myself."

Mrs. Robin flew over with gentle wings. "Whiskers, we need your clever paws to help mix more yellow paint. Will you listen and help us?"

Whiskers nodded her fuzzy head. "Yes! I want to help Mouse make beautiful flowers."

She put down her own paintbrush. Her paws felt sticky with paint, but she didn't mind. "Mouse, let's make yellow together!"

Whiskers carefully mixed white and red and a tiny bit of orange. "Stir, stir, stir," she said as the colors swirled. Soon, bright yellow paint appeared in the cup.

"Oh, thank you!" Mouse squeaked happily. "Now I can paint my sunshine flowers!"

"And I can help everyone else too," Whiskers purred. She listened carefully as Mrs. Robin explained how to make green for leaves and brown for tree trunks.

Whiskers shared her yellow paint with Duckling. She helped Frog mix blue for the sky. Her clever paws were busy helping all her friends.

"This is much more fun than painting alone," Whiskers realized. The art room buzzed with happy sounds - friends laughing, brushes swishing, and colors mixing.

When the big mural was finished, it was the most beautiful picture ever. It had sunshine flowers, blue sky, green trees, and a bright rainbow.

"We did it together!" all the friends cheered.

Mrs. Robin smiled with her bright eyes. "Whiskers, thank you for listening and helping everyone. Your kindness made our art even more special."

Whiskers felt warm and happy inside. Her orange fur seemed to glow with joy. "Listening to others and helping friends feels wonderful," she purred.

From that day on, Whiskers always listened carefully. She learned that sharing and helping others made everything more fun and beautiful.

The End.
