# Splash the Dolphin's Porch Adventure

On a sunny morning, Splash the dolphin wiggled out of his special water tank and flopped onto Grandma Ocean's peaceful front porch. The wooden boards felt warm under his smooth gray skin, and he could smell the sweet jasmine flowers blooming nearby.

"Good morning, Splash!" called Grandma Ocean from her rocking chair. She was a kind old woman who took care of sea animals. "Would you like to help me teach the neighborhood children about the ocean today?"

"Oh yes!" squeaked Splash excitedly, clapping his flippers together. "I love meeting new friends!"

Soon, three children came running up the porch steps. There was Maya from India, Carlos from Mexico, and Aisha from Kenya. They all spoke different languages, but they were learning English together.

"Children," said Grandma Ocean gently, "Splash has some very important ocean lessons to share. But first, we must practice following directions. Can you all sit in a circle on the soft porch cushions?"

The children scrambled around excitedly, but Grandma Ocean held up her hand. "Remember, one at a time. Maya, please choose your cushion first."

Maya carefully picked a blue cushion and sat down quietly. "Now Carlos, your turn," said Grandma Ocean. Carlos chose a green one. Finally, Aisha selected the yellow cushion.

"Wonderful job taking turns!" praised Grandma Ocean. "Now, Splash will teach you his special ocean dance, but you must listen carefully to each step."

Splash wiggled with joy. "First," he said in his squeaky voice, "wave your arms like ocean waves - slowly, like this." He demonstrated with his flippers moving gently back and forth.

The children watched carefully and copied his movements. The porch creaked softly as they swayed together.

"Next," continued Splash, "make the sound of whales singing - 'Whooooo!'" The children giggled and made whale sounds that echoed across the porch.

"Now, the most important part," said Splash seriously. "Pretend to pick up trash from the ocean. This is how dolphins help keep our home clean!"

The children bent down, pretending to collect invisible garbage. "In my country," said Maya, "we have rivers that flow to the ocean too!"

"And in Mexico," added Carlos, "my grandmother says we must protect the sea turtles!"

"In Kenya," said Aisha proudly, "we learn that all water connects to the ocean, even our lakes!"

Splash's eyes sparkled. "You're all so smart! The ocean connects every country in the world. When we follow directions to keep it clean, we help dolphins, whales, and fish everywhere!"

Grandma Ocean smiled warmly. "Now children, let's practice following directions one more time. Splash will call out ocean movements, and you must do exactly what he says."

"Touch your nose like a dolphin!" squeaked Splash.

All three children touched their noses.

"Flap your arms like a seagull!"

They flapped their arms carefully.

"Sit down quietly like a sleeping seal!"

The children sat down softly on their cushions.

"Perfect!" cheered Splash. "When we follow directions, we can learn amazing things about our beautiful world!"

As the sun climbed higher, the children hugged Splash goodbye. They had learned that following directions helps everyone learn together, and that children from all around the world can work together to protect the ocean.

Splash slipped back into his tank with a happy splash, knowing he had made three new friends who would remember to listen carefully and take turns sharing their ideas.

The End.
