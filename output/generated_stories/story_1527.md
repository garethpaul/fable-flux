# Ellie's Dancing Day

Little Ellie the elephant calf woke up feeling wiggly and jiggly. Her trunk swished back and forth, and her big feet wanted to stomp and dance!

"Good morning, sunshine!" called Mama Elephant from the kitchen. "What would you like to do today?"

Ellie looked around their cozy living room. Soft cushions were scattered on the fluffy carpet, and colorful toys filled the toy chest. Sunlight streamed through the big windows, making dancing shadows on the walls.

"I want to MOVE!" trumpeted Ellie excitedly. "My body feels like it needs to wiggle and giggle!"

Mama Elephant smiled. "That sounds wonderful! But first, let's listen to our bodies. What is your body telling you?"

Ellie stopped and closed her eyes. She felt her heart beating like a gentle drum. Her trunk felt tingly, and her legs felt bouncy. "My trunk wants to swing, and my feet want to dance!" she said.

"Perfect!" said Mama. "Let's start with some gentle movements. Can you move your trunk like a snake?"

Ellie swayed her trunk slowly from side to side. "Ssssss," she hissed softly, pretending to be a friendly snake. The movement felt smooth and calming.

"Now try lifting one foot, then the other," suggested Mama. "Like you're walking through thick, sticky honey."

Ellie lifted her front feet one at a time, moving very slowly. "This feels nice and peaceful," she said, taking deep breaths.

But soon, Ellie's energy bubbled up again. "Now I want to move FAST!" she giggled.

"Okay," laughed Mama. "But let's listen first. Put your trunk on your chest. What do you hear?"

Ellie pressed her trunk to her heart. "Thump-thump, thump-thump! It's beating faster now!"

"That's right! When we move fast, our hearts work harder. Let's march around the room like brave elephants!"

Ellie stomped her feet and swung her trunk as she marched in circles around the soft cushions. "Left, right, left, right!" she chanted happily. Her heart pumped faster, and she felt strong and proud.

After marching, Ellie felt warm and excited. "More, more!" she trumpeted.

"Wait," said Mama gently. "Let's listen to our bodies again. How do you feel now?"

Ellie paused and noticed her breathing was faster. "I feel warm and my heart is beating quick-quick-quick!"

"When our bodies work hard, they need rest too," explained Mama. "Let's try some calm movements now."

They sat on the soft carpet together. Mama showed Ellie how to stretch her trunk up high like a tree reaching for the sky, then slowly bring it down like a gentle waterfall.

"Breathe in when you reach up," whispered Mama. "Breathe out when you come down."

Ellie followed along, feeling her heart slow down to a peaceful rhythm. The stretching felt like a warm hug for her muscles.

"I like moving fast AND slow," said Ellie thoughtfully. "My body feels happy when I listen to what it needs."

"That's exactly right, my smart little elephant," said Mama, giving Ellie a gentle trunk hug. "Moving our bodies is wonderful, but listening to what they need makes us feel our very best."

Ellie curled up on a soft cushion, feeling perfectly content. Her body felt relaxed and strong, and her heart beat with a calm, happy rhythm.

"Tomorrow, can we dance and stretch again?" asked Ellie sleepily.

"Of course," smiled Mama. "Every day is a perfect day for moving and listening to our bodies."

The End.
