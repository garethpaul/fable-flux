# Polly’s Basement Band

Polly was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a red, smiling beak. She lived with her family above a busy basement. The basement was full of sounds. Tap-tap went the pipes. Hum-hum went the heater. Drip, drip went a tiny leaky sink. It was a music place, and Polly loved it.

One rainy afternoon, Polly flapped down the creaky wooden stairs. “Squawk! Music time!” she chirped. The air smelled like warm dust and a little like soap. String lights glowed like tiny stars.

On a shelf, Polly saw shiny things: pots, pans, a tin can, and a wooden spoon. She also saw a small drum, a little xylophone, and a globe with blue seas and many shiny countries. The globe could spin with a gentle whirr.

Polly tapped the pan with the spoon. Ting! She tapped the drum. Boom! She ran her wing over the xylophone. Plink, plonk, plink! “Listen!” Polly sang. “The basement is a band!”

Her friends came to visit. Miko the mouse squeaked, “I hear music!” Lila the ladybug buzzed, “I see lights!” Tari the turtle smiled slowly, “I feel the drum in my shell. Boom, boom.”

They began to play. Miko shook a jar of buttons. Click-a-click! Lila rang a tiny bell. Ding-ding! Tari tapped the drum. Boom, boom. Polly sang, “La, la, la!” Her song was smooth and happy.

On a table sat Mama’s shiny metronome. It ticked back and forth. Tick-tock, tick-tock. Polly liked the beat. She wanted to lead. She puffed her feathers and said, “I can make our song even better!”

Polly looked at the globe. She spun it gently. Whirr. The blue seas swirled. She tapped the drum like ocean waves. Boom… boom… boom. “This is sea music,” she said.

Then she stopped the globe with her beak on a warm sandy place. “Listen to island drums,” she chirped, and played a quick, happy pattern. Boom-bada-boom! Miko squeaked, “I hear dancing!” Lila buzzed, “I feel sunshine!”

Polly spun the globe again. Whirr. Her beak landed on a snowy place. She rubbed the xylophone with her wing. Ting-ting-ting! “This is snow music,” she whispered. Tari smiled. “It sounds like tiny stars.”

They played and laughed. The basement sounded full and bright. Tap, hum, drip, boom, ting, ding, click! The music danced around them like warm cocoa steam.

Then Polly saw something special: a new silver whistle. It lay in a basket by Mama’s toolbox. It was smooth and shiny. It looked like it could sing. A small card was tucked under it. The card said: “For Mama’s class.”

Polly felt a flutter in her chest. “Just one toot,” she said softly. “I will put it back.” She picked up the whistle and blew. Toooot! The sound was loud and sweet. It echoed. Toooot… Toooot…

The whistle slipped! Clink! It fell and got a tiny dent. Polly’s feathers drooped. Her tummy felt twisty. Miko squeaked, “Oh no.” Lila’s wings paused. Tari blinked slowly.

Polly wanted to hide the whistle. She wanted to say, “It was not me.” But her heart felt heavy. She thought about music. Music is true. Music is honest. Music tells feelings. Polly took a deep breath.

“I did it,” Polly said softly. “I used Mama’s whistle. I did not ask. It has a dent. I am sorry.”

Footsteps came down the stairs. Thump, thump. Mama looked at the whistle. She looked at Polly. “Thank you for telling the truth,” Mama said. Her voice was calm and kind. “I feel sad about the dent. But I am proud you were honest.”

Polly felt a tiny bit lighter. “Can we fix it?” she asked. “I want to make it right.”

“Yes,” Mama said. “We can try. We can also learn.” Mama showed them soft cloth, warm water, and gentle taps. Tap-tap. They cleaned the whistle. They smoothed the dent a little. It still had a small mark, like a tiny moon.

“I forgive you,” Mama said. “Next time, please ask.” Polly nodded. “I will,” she said. “I will grow and do better.”

Then Mama smiled. “Let’s use the whistle in the right way,” she said. “We can make a song for the world.”

Polly’s eyes shone. “A world song!” she chirped. She spun the globe. Whirr. “We will listen to many places,” Polly said. “We will share kindness and truth.”

They made a new basement band plan:
- Ask before using things.
- Practice with the metronome. Tick-tock.
- Try, try again. Growth takes time.
- Play songs from many places and say, “We see you. We hear you.”

Polly lifted the whistle. She asked, “May I play now?” Mama nodded. “Yes.”

Toooot! The whistle called. Miko shook buttons. Click-a-click! Lila rang her bell. Ding-ding! Tari tapped the drum. Boom, boom. Polly sang, “La, la!” They played sea music, snow music, sunshine music, and city music with beeps and claps. They listened to each other. They left space. They kept the beat.

The busy basement felt like the whole world. It sounded honest and bright. It smelled like warm dust and soap. It glowed like tiny stars.

Polly smiled. “Music helps us tell the truth,” she said. “Music helps us care for the world. When we make a mistake, we can say sorry, try again, and grow.”

Mama hugged Polly’s wings. “Yes,” she said. “That is our song.”

The End.
