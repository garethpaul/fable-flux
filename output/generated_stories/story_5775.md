# Robo-Pup Cleans the Study Corner

In a cozy study corner, with soft lamplight and crayons that smelled like sweet wax, lived a helpful robot dog named Sparky. Sparky had shiny ears, a wagging metal tail, and a gentle beep that sounded like a tiny bell. His nose light blinked blue when he was ready to help.

Lina, a little girl with curly hair and bright eyes, sat at the small desk. Papers, crayons, and puzzle pieces were everywhere. A snack plate with apple slices and carrot sticks sat nearby. The plate smelled fresh and crunchy.

“Sparky,” Lina said with a sigh, “I want to color and learn about the world. But the study corner is so messy. I can’t find my globe sticker book!”

Sparky’s nose light blinked. Beep-beep! “I can help,” he said in his warm robot voice. “We can do our chores here. We can be a team.”

Lina crossed her arms. “Chores are boring,” she grumbled. “I want to play now.”

Sparky sat down and tilted his shiny head. “May I tell you something?” he asked softly.

Lina nodded.

Sparky wagged. “In homes all around the world, families do little jobs to care for their things. In big cities and small villages, kids help too. Chores keep spaces safe, clean, and happy. We can clean together, and then play. We can do it kindly and quickly.”

Lina peeked at the messy floor and felt her tummy get twisty. “I feel frustrated,” she said. “I don’t know where to start.”

Sparky’s ears perked. “Thank you for telling me how you feel,” he said. “Let’s use gentle words to make a plan. May I suggest three steps?”

“Yes, please,” Lina said.

“Step one,” Sparky said, tapping his paw. “Sort. Papers in a stack, crayons in a cup, puzzle pieces in a box. Step two. Wipe and sweep crumbs so ants don’t come. Step three. Snack and learn. We’ll use your globe stickers to see different foods from around the world.”

Lina took a deep breath. “I want to help, but I need a turn to choose a job,” she said. “I choose the crayons!”

“That’s assertive and kind,” Sparky replied. “You spoke up with calm words. I’ll stack papers. Let’s begin.”

The room filled with happy sounds. Click, clack went the crayon cups. Swish, swish went the little brush as Sparky swept. The apple slices made a crisp crunch when Lina took a bite. “Crunch!” She giggled. “Apple power!”

Sparky hummed a gentle tune. “Beep-beep, tidy up. Beep-beep, fill the cup.”

Soon, the crayons were lined like a rainbow. The paper stack stood tall and neat. Puzzle pieces clicked into their box. The desk felt smooth under Lina’s small hand.

“Look!” Lina smiled. “Our study corner is ready to learn!”

Sparky nodded. “Yes. You did your chore with care. That helps our home. Now, where is your globe sticker book?”

Lina opened a drawer. “Here!” She held up a bright book with shiny stickers. “Let’s learn about food from different places!”

They opened to a page with pictures of rice fields, bread loaves, and colorful fruits. “This is rice from Asia,” Lina read slowly. “This is bread from Europe. This is a papaya from tropical places. Foods grow in different soils and sunshine.”

Sparky wagged. “And we can nourish our bodies with many foods. Your carrots crunch. Your apples are sweet. In some homes, families eat beans, lentils, or corn. Cleaning before we eat keeps our food safe.”

Lina placed a sticker of a tiny bowl next to a map. “Sparky, I feel proud,” she said. “The study corner is clean. My tummy feels calm.”

Sparky’s nose light glowed softly. “I feel proud too,” he said. “You used your voice to choose a job. You listened, and we worked as a team.”

Just then, Lina’s little brother, Niko, peeked in with big curious eyes. “Play?” he asked.

Lina smiled. “Yes! But the study corner stays tidy. You can use the puzzle on the mat. When you’re done, we put it back. That’s our family rule.”

Niko nodded. “Okay.” He picked up the box.

Sparky watched, tail swishing. “You spoke clearly and kindly. That is peaceful communication. You set a rule, and you helped Niko understand. Now everyone can enjoy this clean, happy space.”

Lina took a last apple bite. “Crunch!” She laughed. “Sparky, can we make a clean-up song for next time?”

Sparky beeped a cheerful melody. “Clean, clean, little team, make it bright, make it gleam. Sort and stack, clap-clap-clap! Snack and learn, tap-tap-tap!”

They sang together, clapping softly. The study corner felt warm and calm. The lamp glowed like a friendly moon. The crayons smelled sweet. The desk felt smooth and cool. The air tasted like apples and a tiny bit like carrots.

Lina hugged Sparky’s shiny neck. “Thanks, helper friend. Doing chores was not boring at all. It helped me learn and play.”

Sparky wagged. “Chores show love for our home, for our family, and for our world. Little hands can help in big ways.”

Lina nodded. “Today I learned to clean, to speak up kindly, and to try new healthy snacks. Tomorrow, we can help again.”

“Yes,” Sparky said. “One small chore, one big smile, every day.”

The End.
