# Olive Owl and the Night of Choices

In a quiet attic that smelled like old books and cedar wood, a wise old owl named Olive blinked her big golden eyes. Moonlight slipped through a tiny window and painted soft silver shapes on the floor. The attic was peaceful. It was Olive’s favorite place to think.

“Hooot,” Olive hummed softly. “Tonight I have many choices.”

Olive kept a tidy attic nest with a small broom, a shiny water bowl, and a basket of fresh fruit. Shelves held maps and soft quilts. A little clock tick-tick-ticked with a gentle sound. In the corner, a round globe sat on a stand. It showed the big blue oceans and many countries far away.

Olive spread her wings and looked around. She had three plans for the night. Plan A: clean her nest. Plan B: practice flying quietly so she could be strong and healthy. Plan C: read stories about children around the world and make a kind letter.

But she could not do everything at once.

“What should I do first?” Olive asked the moon. “I want to be clean, I want to be strong, and I want to be kind.”

A little breeze slipped under the attic door. It smelled like fresh night air. Olive closed her eyes and took a slow breath in and out. “I will make a good decision,” she said. “I will use a simple plan.”

Olive hopped to a low table. On it was a small chalkboard and a tiny piece of chalk. Tap, tap, tap went her claws on the wood. She drew three boxes and wrote in big, careful letters:

1. Clean nest
2. Exercise fly
3. Kind letter

“Now I will ask three questions,” Olive said. “Is it healthy? Is it helpful? Is it kind?”

She touched the first box. “Clean nest. Is it healthy?” She looked at a dusty corner and sneezed. “Ah-choo! Yes, it is healthy. Dust makes me sneeze.” She smiled. “Is it helpful?” She imagined a tidy nest where she could find her fruit and books. “Yes, helpful. Is it kind?” She thought of her attic friends: a shy mouse named Pip and a spider named Netty who liked quiet. “Yes, a clean space is kind to friends.”

She touched the second box. “Exercise fly. Is it healthy?” Olive flapped her wings. Swish, swish. “Yes, very healthy. Strong wings help me feel good.” She giggled. “Is it helpful?” Strong flying would help her carry small notes and seeds. “Yes, helpful. Is it kind?” She thought, “If I am strong, I can help others.” She nodded. “Yes, kind.”

She touched the third box. “Kind letter. Is it healthy?” She tilted her head. “It is healthy for my heart.” She felt warm and calm. “Is it helpful?” She looked at the globe. “Friends far away might smile.” She thought of children who drink clean water, eat veggies, and play outside. “Yes, helpful. Is it kind?” Olive’s eyes shone. “Oh yes.”

Olive looked at the clock. Tick, tick. “I need an order,” she said. “I will plan.”

She listened to her tummy. It made a tiny rumble. “First, a sip of water and a bite of fruit,” she said. “Healthy habits help me think.” She took a cool sip. The water felt fresh on her tongue. She ate a crunchy apple slice. Crunch! Sweet juice dripped. “Now I can decide.”

She clapped her wings softly. “Plan: I will clean first, then fly, then write.”

Olive got her broom. Swish-swish went the broom on the wooden floor. She sang, “Sweep-a-weep, dust away, clean and tidy, happy day!” Pip the mouse peeked out from behind a hat box.

“Hi, Olive,” squeaked Pip. “What are you doing?”

“I am making choices,” said Olive. “I cleaned first.”

Pip sniffed. “It smells nice. Can I help?”

“Yes,” said Olive. “Teamwork is a smart strategy.” Pip carried tiny bits of lint to a bin. Netty the spider waved from her web.

“Please leave my web,” said Netty. “It is my home.”

“Of course,” Olive said. “We clean with care.”

Soon the attic gleamed. Olive’s feathers felt less sneezy. She smiled. “Step one done!”

“Now exercise!” Olive whispered. She opened the small window. Cool night air brushed her face. She flapped her wings slowly—up, down, pause. She glided to one beam, then to another. Swish, glide, swish. Her heart felt strong and steady. Pip clapped his tiny paws. “Go, Olive!”

Olive landed and laughed softly. “Step two done! I feel strong.”

She looked at the globe. “Step three: kindness.” Olive turned the globe gently. It spun with a soft whirr. “So many places,” she whispered. “So many friends.” She took a little card and wrote in big, simple letters:

“Hello, friends! I am Olive the owl. Tonight I made a plan. I cleaned my nest. I exercised. Now I send you a wish: Drink water, eat fruits, rest well, and be kind. We share one big world. Hoot hoot, from my quiet attic to you.”

Olive tucked the card into a small envelope. She tied it to a blue ribbon and hung it by the window, where the night breeze could carry her wish to hearts everywhere.

Pip curled up in a cotton puff and yawned. “Olive,” he said, “how did you pick what to do first?”

Olive blinked kindly. “I asked good questions. I made a plan. I took care of my health, helped my home, and shared kindness with the world.”

Pip smiled. “That is a smart way.”

Olive nodded. “Good decisions make a gentle night.” She stretched her wings, took one last sip of water, and settled in her soft nest. The moonlight felt like a blanket. The clock went tick-tick-tick, easy and calm.

In the quiet attic, Olive whispered, “When we think, plan, and choose with care, we feel safe, strong, and kind. That is the wise way.”

The End.
