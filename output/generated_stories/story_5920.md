# Gigi the Giraffe’s Home Office Helpers

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe. She had a sunny home office with a big window and a small, green plant on the desk. The plant smelled fresh, like rain. Gigi loved to think, write notes, and help neighbors from her home office.

One morning, the sun made warm squares on the floor. Gigi stretched her long neck and said, “Good morning, plant. Let’s do good work.” She took a deep breath. The air smelled like paper, pencils, and a little bit of cinnamon tea.

Knock, knock! “Come in,” said Gigi gently.

Three friends peeked in. There was Poppy the Panda with soft black-and-white fur, Tiko the Tiny Bird with bright blue feathers, and Sam the Snail with a shiny shell.

“Hi, Gigi!” chirped Tiko. “We want to help in your home office today!”

Gigi smiled. “Helping is kind. We can all help in our own way.”

Poppy hugged a stack of old papers. “I can carry heavy things,” Poppy said. “I am strong.”

Tiko fluttered. “I can sing and remind everyone to take breaks,” Tiko said. “I am small but loud.”

Sam moved slowly but steady. “I can sort paper clips,” Sam said. “I am careful.”

Gigi clapped her hooves softly. “Our team is different, and that is wonderful,” she said. “We all have special ways to help. That is called diversity.”

Gigi pointed to a big box. “First, we will sort this box. Some paper can be used again. Some can be recycled. Re-using and recycling help the Earth. That is called sustainability.”

Poppy smiled and set the box on the desk. Thump! The plant’s leaves rustled. “Oops, sorry plant,” said Poppy.

“It’s okay,” Gigi said. “We will be gentle.”

They started to work. Tiko sang a tiny song. “Sort, sort, sort. We care for the Earth. Sort, sort, sort. We know our worth.”

Sam slid a paper clip onto a little pile. Click. “I’m doing my best,” Sam said.

“You are doing great,” said Gigi. She liked to say kind words.

Soon the office felt busy and happy. The window let in warm light. The pencils smelled woody. The floor tickled Gigi’s hooves.

Then Tiko flew to Gigi’s head. “Perch!” said Tiko.

Gigi blinked. “Tiko, I like my head to be my space,” she said softly. “Please ask before you perch.”

Tiko paused. “Oh! I forgot. May I perch on your head?”

Gigi smiled. “Thank you for asking. Today, I say no. My head needs a rest. You can perch on the chair.”

Tiko nodded and landed on the chair. “I can do that,” Tiko said. “I will ask next time.”

Gigi felt proud. She had set a boundary in a kind way. “We can keep our bodies safe,” she said. “We can say yes or no.”

Poppy raised a paw. “I found markers,” Poppy said. “They are all different colors!”

Gigi’s eyes shone. “Different colors make bright pictures,” she said. “Different friends make bright teams. Diversity makes us strong.”

Just then, the power went out. Click! The computer went dark. The room got quiet. No hum. No beep. Only the soft rustle of leaves.

“Oh no,” whispered Sam. “What now?”

Gigi took a breath. “We can be calm,” she said. “We can be resilient. That means we keep trying.”

She opened the window. A cool breeze swished in. The plant leaves danced. “We have sunlight. We can still sort. We can still draw with markers. We can still help.”

Tiko sang a soft brave song. Poppy made a little rainbow picture. Sam kept sorting paper clips. Click, click.

Gigi wrote a note with her pencil: “Use both sides of paper.” She turned a used paper and wrote on the back. “This saves trees,” she said. “Sustainability!”

The power came back on. Bzzzt! The computer blinked. “Hooray!” said Tiko.

Gigi smiled. “Thank you for staying calm,” she told her friends. “We are a diverse team. We care for the Earth. We respect each other’s space. We keep going when things are hard.”

Poppy held up the rainbow picture. “This is for your wall,” Poppy said.

Gigi taped the rainbow near the plant. The tape made a tiny rip sound. “It’s beautiful,” she said. “Our different colors and ideas make our office happy.”

Sam looked at the plant. “Does the plant need water?” he asked.

Gigi touched the soil. It felt a little dry, like sand. “Yes,” she said. She poured just a little water. Plip, plip. “We give just what is needed. That is good care.”

Tiko flapped. “And we ask before perching,” Tiko said.

“And we use both sides of paper,” added Poppy.

“And we keep trying,” said Sam.

Gigi nodded. “Today we learned that diversity is our strength. We learned to care for the Earth with small steps. We learned to keep our own space safe and to ask before touching. And when things get hard, we take a breath and try again.”

The sun moved across the floor. The warm squares turned into long golden stripes. Gigi closed her notebook and smiled at her friends.

“Thank you,” she said. “Our different ways made this day great.”

Everyone cheered. The plant looked very green.

The End.
