# Gina Giraffe Solves the Guest Room Riddle

Gina the thoughtful giraffe blinked her big gentle eyes and peered into the guest room. The room smelled like fresh soap and lemon. The curtains were soft and blue. A tiny fan hummed, “whirr, whirr,” in the corner. Today, Gina had an important job.

“Grandma is coming to visit,” Gina said to herself. “I will make the guest room cozy.” She stretched her long neck to dust the high shelf. She patted the pillows until they were fluffy like clouds. She set a glass of water on the little table.

Then Gina found a note on the desk. The note was on creamy paper. The words were neat and straight. Gina loved writing. She loved reading too. She read the note out loud, slowly and clearly.

“Hello, helper,” the note said. “There is a tiny riddle to solve. Where should Grandma’s favorite book go? Look at the clues. Use your thinking steps. Love, Mom.”

Gina’s ears perked up. “A riddle! I like riddles,” she said. Her heart felt warm and excited. She tapped the paper with her hoof. “I will use my good thinking.”

She read the three clues written below.

Clue 1: “The book should be near light for reading.”
Clue 2: “It should be close to water for sipping.”
Clue 3: “It should be safe and not fall.”

Gina looked around the guest room. She wanted to guess fast. But she stopped. She took a deep breath. She remembered her thinking steps.

“Step one,” she whispered. “I will look and listen.” She looked at the lamp by the bed. “That is light.” She looked at the table with the glass of water. “That is water.” She touched the shelf. “Is it safe?”

“Step two,” Gina said. “I will make a plan.” She wrote on a sticky note with her favorite purple pen. Her letters were big and round.

- Lamp gives light.
- Table has water.
- Shelf is high. Maybe not safe?
- Bedside table is not wobbly.

“Step three,” she said softly. “I will try and check.”

Just then, her little friend Pip the mouse peeked from behind the curtain. Pip’s whiskers twitched. “Hi, Gina!” Pip squeaked. “What are you doing?”

“I am solving a riddle,” Gina said. “I need to find the best place for Grandma’s book.”

Pip sniffed the lemon air. “I like games! Put it on the tall shelf.”

Gina smiled kindly. “That is an idea. Let’s check the clues.”

She lifted the book. It smelled like paper and a hint of cinnamon. She gently set it on the tall shelf. The shelf was high and near the window. Sunlight splashed on the wall.

“Clue 1,” Gina said. “Is it near light for reading?” She looked. The shelf was near the window, but at night there would be no sun. “Hmm. The lamp is by the bed. The shelf is not near the lamp.”

Pip nodded. “Oh.”

“Clue 2,” Gina read. “Is it close to water?” The glass on the little table was far from the shelf. “No,” Gina said. “Not close.”

“Clue 3,” she read. “Is it safe and not fall?” Gina gently nudged the shelf. The book wobbled. “Not so safe,” she said.

Pip’s ears drooped. “Oops.”

“It is okay,” Gina said with a smile. “We are learning. Learning is brave.” Her heart felt calm. She wrote on another sticky note:

- Shelf: light sometimes, water far, a little wobbly.

Pip pointed to the bed. “Put it on the soft blanket!”

Gina tested that idea. She put the book on the bed. She tried the clues.

“Near light?” The lamp stood next to the bed. “Yes, near the lamp,” she said.
“Near water?” The little table with the glass of water was also next to the bed. “Yes,” she said.
“Safe?” Gina pressed the blanket. The book slid a little down the puffy pillow. “Hmm. It might fall,” she said gently.

Pip’s tail curled. “Oh no!”

“It is okay,” Gina said again. “We are getting closer. We can keep trying.” She smiled and wrote:

- Bed: light yes, water yes, safe maybe not.

Gina looked at the bedside table. It had a smooth top and four sturdy legs. The lamp sat on it. The glass of water was on it too.

Gina’s eyes lit up. “Let’s test the bedside table!”

She placed the book between the lamp and the water, with room on each side. She checked the clues.

“Near light?” Gina flicked the switch. The lamp glowed warm and golden. “Yes.”
“Near water?” The glass stood close, but not too close. “Yes.”
“Safe?” She wiggled the table. It did not wobble. The book did not slide. “Yes!”

Pip clapped his tiny paws. “We solved it!”

Gina felt proud. Her chest felt big and bright. “We used steps,” she said. “We looked, we planned, we tried, and we checked. That is logical thinking.”

Just then, Mom walked by the door. “How is the guest room, dear?”

Gina lifted her head and smiled. “Ready and cozy,” she said. “We found the best spot for Grandma’s book.”

Mom sniffed the lemon air and looked around. “It looks lovely. And your notes are so clear.” She gave Gina a warm hug. The hug felt like a soft blanket. Pip smiled too.

Gina placed her sticky notes in a little row on the desk. She wrote one more note:

- I can think in steps. I can solve problems. I am thoughtful.

She read it aloud, and her voice sounded strong and happy. Pip whispered, “Me too.”

Gina looked at the lamp, the water, and the book resting safely. She felt calm and ready. “Grandma will love this,” she said.

And when Grandma arrived, she smiled and said, “What a perfect place for my book. Thank you, Gina.” Gina’s heart hummed like the little fan, steady and sweet.

Lesson: When we slow down, use kind words, and think in steps—look, plan, try, and check—we can solve problems and feel proud.

The End.
