# Kobi's Fair Share Day

Kobi the koala sat quietly in his mama's home office, watching the morning sunlight stream through the big window. The warm rays made everything look golden and cozy. Mama was typing on her computer, making soft clicking sounds with her fingers.

"Kobi," said Mama, looking up from her screen, "I have a special job for you today. Can you help me sort these colorful blocks for the children at the daycare?"

Kobi's round black eyes sparkled with excitement. "Yes, Mama! I love helping!"

On the soft carpet lay three big baskets and a mountain of wooden blocks in red, blue, and yellow. They smelled like fresh cedar wood and felt smooth under Kobi's fuzzy paws.

"Each basket needs the same number of blocks," explained Mama. "That way, every child gets a fair share to play with."

Kobi nodded thoughtfully. He understood fairness – it meant everyone getting what they needed, just like how he and his sister always shared their eucalyptus leaves equally.

At first, Kobi started grabbing blocks quickly, tossing them into baskets without counting. Red blocks went flying everywhere! Blue blocks rolled under the desk! Yellow blocks scattered across the floor with little wooden *clinks* and *clacks*.

"Oh my!" giggled Mama. "What happened here?"

Kobi looked around at the messy office. Blocks were everywhere except where they should be. His fluffy ears drooped a little.

"I wanted to finish fast," Kobi said softly. "But now it's all mixed up."

"That's okay, little one," Mama said gently, giving him a warm hug that smelled like lavender. "Sometimes when we rush, we make more work for ourselves. Let's try a different strategy."

"What's a strategy?" asked Kobi, tilting his head.

"A strategy is a smart plan to solve a problem," Mama explained. "Let's make a plan together."

They sat cross-legged on the carpet. Mama showed Kobi how to count slowly: "One, two, three..." Her voice was patient and encouraging.

"First, let's gather all the blocks back into one pile," suggested Mama.

Kobi carefully collected each block, feeling their different textures and listening to their gentle sounds as they clinked together. Some blocks were perfectly smooth, others had tiny scratches that told stories of other children who had played with them.

"Now," said Mama, "let's count out ten blocks for each basket. That will be fair for everyone."

Kobi took his time this round. "One red block for basket one," he said carefully, placing it gently. "One red block for basket two... one red block for basket three."

The office filled with Kobi's quiet counting and the soft *tap-tap* of blocks being placed just right. He continued with blue blocks, then yellow ones, making sure each basket got exactly the same amount.

"Look, Mama!" Kobi called out proudly. "Each basket has ten blocks – that's fair!"

Mama clapped her hands. "You did wonderfully, Kobi! You learned that fairness takes patience and a good strategy."

Kobi felt warm and happy inside, like drinking hot cocoa on a cold day. He had learned that rushing made things harder, but taking time and making a plan helped everything work out fairly.

"The children will be so happy," said Kobi, admiring the three equal piles of colorful blocks.

"Yes," agreed Mama, "because you made sure everyone will get a fair share. That's what makes you such a thoughtful koala."

Kobi smiled, feeling proud of his patient work and his new understanding of fairness.

The End.
