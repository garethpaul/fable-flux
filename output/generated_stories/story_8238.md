# Mango's Pantry Project

Mango the monkey was small, brave, and curious. One sunny morning, Mango tiptoed into the pantry. The door creaked softly. “Eee-eee!” he chirped. Inside, the air smelled like sweet bananas and warm bread. The shelves were tall and full of jars. Jars of rice, jars of beans, jars of jam. Crackers crunched softly in a box when Mango brushed them. Crinkle, crackle!

Mango loved the pantry. It was quiet and cozy. But the shelves were messy. Boxes leaned. Jars wobbled. A bag of flour slumped to the side. Mango’s tail curled with worry. “Oh no,” he whispered. “Things might fall. Someone could get hurt.”

Mango’s heart thumped, but he was courageous. “I can help! I will build something strong.” He pictured a neat shelf. He pictured a safe step-stool. He rubbed his paws together and smiled.

He pulled a small wooden crate from the corner. Thump. He found a stack of lids. Clink, clink. He found some string. He found cardboard. He found a roll of tape. Rrrip! Mango felt proud. “I can build!” he said.

Just then, Penny the parrot peeked in. “Squawk! What are you doing, Mango?” she asked, tilting her green head.

“I am building a helper shelf,” Mango said. “It will hold jars so they don’t wobble.” He patted the crate. Pat, pat.

Penny flapped her wings. “Be careful,” she said. “Ask first. This is not our pantry.”

Mango paused. He looked around. The pantry belonged to Grandma Gigi. Mango liked Grandma Gigi very much. She always shared warm bread. He should ask. But Mango’s courage grew bigger than his patience. He wanted to help right now. “I will fix it fast,” he said. “Then I will say sorry if needed.”

Mango stacked the lids like little feet under the crate to make it even. He taped cardboard into small walls so jars would not roll. He tied string across the front like a tiny fence. He pressed the tape hard. Rrrip, press. Rrrip, press. He hummed a happy building song. Tap, tap, pat!

Penny watched. “It looks neat,” she said. “But did you ask?”

Mango lifted a jar of jam and set it on the new shelf. It felt steady. “Look! It works!”

Just then, Grandma Gigi opened the door. The pantry light clicked on. “Oh my,” she said, eyes wide. She sniffed the sweet jam and the flour dust. “What is happening here?”

Mango froze. His ears drooped. “I wanted to help,” he said softly. “The jars were wobbly. I built a helper shelf.” He held his paws together. “I did not ask first. I am sorry.”

Grandma Gigi looked at the crate shelf. She touched the string fence. Twang! She pressed a corner of the tape. Sticky. She knelt down to Mango’s eye level. “Thank you for wanting to help,” she said gently. “That is very kind. But respect means asking before we change someone’s things.”

Mango’s tail curled around his ankles. “I understand,” he said. “May I fix it with you the right way?”

Grandma Gigi smiled. “Yes. Let’s build together. We can make it strong and safe.”

Mango’s heart felt warm. He had been brave, but now he would be respectful too. “Penny, will you help?” he asked.

“Squawk! Yes!” Penny chirped.

Grandma Gigi brought a small toolbox. Click. A tiny screwdriver. A little level with a bubble. A soft cloth. She brought extra wood slats and safe child tape. “First, we plan,” she said. “We look. We measure. We ask.”

They looked at the space. Mango held the level. The bubble danced left, then right. “Center,” said Grandma Gigi. The bubble smiled in the middle. Mango giggled. “It likes the middle!”

They counted jars together. “One, two, three, four…” Mango’s fingers tapped each cool glass jar. Tap, tap, tap. They drew a simple plan on cardboard. Mango made a big square for the shelf and small squares for jar spots. Penny added a tiny picture of a banana. “Banana spot!” she squawked.

“Next, we build,” Grandma Gigi said. She showed Mango how to line up the wood slats. He held the slat steady. She turned the screwdriver with slow, careful twists. Tika-tika-tik. Penny held the tape. “Ready!” she sang.

They made small dividers so each jar had its own place. They added soft pads so the shelf would not slide. Mango wiped flour dust with the cloth. Swish, swish. The pantry smelled clean and cozy.

“Now we put things back,” Grandma Gigi said. Mango lifted each jar with two hands. “Thank you, jam,” he said. “Go to your spot.” He placed the rice, the beans, the tea. Plunk, plunk, plunk. The shelf was strong. Nothing wobbled.

Grandma Gigi nodded. “You were brave to help. You were respectful to ask. And you made amends by fixing it with me.” She gave Mango a warm hug. Mango’s cheeks felt toasty. Penny nuzzled his ear. “Good building!” she chirped.

Mango looked at the pantry. It was neat and safe. He took a deep breath. It smelled like vanilla and happy work. “I learned,” he said. “First, ask. Then plan. Then build together.”

Grandma Gigi smiled. “That is the builder’s way.”

Mango grinned. “And if I make a mistake, I can make amends.”

“Yes,” said Grandma Gigi. “We can always try again with respect.”

Mango did a little monkey dance. His feet pattered on the floor. Tap-tap-tap. “Builder Mango!” he cheered.

They closed the pantry door gently. Creak… click. The house felt calm. The shelf stood strong.

The lesson was clear: Be brave, be respectful, and build together. When we make a mistake, we can make amends.

The End.
