# Leo Listens in the Little Garage

Tap, tap, tap. The brave little lion cub named Leo padded into the neighborhood garage workshop. The air smelled like warm wood and orange soap. Sunbeams made dancing dust sparkle. Tools hung on the wall like shiny, sleepy birds.

“Good morning, Mr. Bear!” Leo called. Mr. Bear ran the workshop. He wore big blue gloves and a kind smile. “I want to help the neighborhood today!”

Mr. Bear nodded. “That’s wonderful, Leo. Helping is community service. Today, we fix the Little Free Story Box so kids can share books.” He pointed to a small wooden box with a glass door. The door was loose, and the paint was scratched.

Leo’s tail swished. “I can do it! I am brave.”

Mr. Bear chuckled. “Brave and calm is best. Let’s use mindful paws.” He showed Leo how to breathe. “In through the nose, out through the mouth. Smell the flower. Blow the feather.”

Leo closed his eyes. He smelled a pretend flower. He blew a pretend feather. “Ahhh.” His shoulders felt soft.

“We will need screws, a small screwdriver, sandpaper, and paint,” Mr. Bear said. “First decision: tidy the space or start right away?”

Leo looked around. Nails in a tin. Brushes in a jar. A little spill of blue paint on the floor. His paws itched to start, but he remembered mindful paws. He put his paw on his chest. “I will pause, then choose.” He took a breath. “Let’s tidy. Clean space, clear mind!”

They hummed while they cleaned. Clink went the nails in the tin. Swish went the broom on the floor. The garage felt safe and ready.

A line of neighbors peeked in. A rabbit with floppy ears. A turtle with a tiny backpack. A fox holding a book. “Is the Story Box open?” asked Rabbit.

“Soon,” Leo said. “We are fixing it for you!”

Leo checked the door. It wobbled like jelly. “Screwdriver, please.” He held the tool. His paw shook. “It’s hard,” he whispered.

Mr. Bear placed a gentle paw on Leo’s shoulder. “Mindful first.”

Leo took three slow breaths. In. Out. In. Out. In. Out. The screwdriver felt lighter. He turned the screw—slow and steady. Squeak, squeak. The door held tight.

“Yay!” cheered Rabbit. Turtle clapped slowly. Fox wagged his tail.

“Next,” said Mr. Bear, “we sand the rough spots.”

Leo rubbed the wood with sandpaper. Scritch, scritch. Dust tickled his nose. “Achoo!” He giggled and stepped back. “Mindful paws,” he said, brushing dust away. He noticed the sound of a bird outside. He felt the smooth wood under his paw. He smelled the orange soap again. “I am here. I am calm.”

“Now paint,” Mr. Bear said. “What color?”

Leo looked at the shelves. Red like a sunset. Blue like the sky. Yellow like a sunny smile. He paused to decide. He closed his eyes and pictured kids smiling. “Yellow!” he said. “Yellow is happy.”

Neighbors brought little gifts. Rabbit brought stickers of stars. Turtle brought a tiny bell. Fox brought a small story about a brave whale.

“Let’s make this a storytelling box,” Leo said. “We can paint a sun, add stars, and hang the bell as a gentle chime. Ding!”

They worked together. Brush, brush. Leo painted careful strokes. He breathed slow and steady. Ding went the bell in the doorway breeze. The box glowed warm and bright.

Soon, a little mouse tugged Leo’s tail. “I lost my book,” she squeaked. “I am sad.”

Leo knelt. “Let’s breathe together first.” They smelled the flower. They blew the feather. Mouse’s whiskers stopped shaking.

“Now we think,” Leo said softly. “Where did you have it last?”

“In the corner by the rakes,” Mouse said.

Leo listened. The garage whispered small sounds—tick of a clock, drip of a tap. He looked with slow eyes. Under a cloth, he saw a corner of a red cover. He lifted the cloth. “Here it is!”

Mouse hugged her book. “Thank you!”

Leo smiled. “Mindful looking helps us find and fix.”

At last, the Story Box stood ready: clean, bright, and strong. Mr. Bear opened the door. “Who would like to share the first story?”

Leo cleared his throat. “I will.” He sat on a soft mat and held Fox’s whale story. Neighbors gathered close. The garage smelled like warm paint and orange soap. The bell went ding in the breeze.

“In the big blue sea,” Leo read, “a brave whale took a deep, calm breath and found the way home.” Leo paused and took a breath, too. Everyone breathed with him. In. Out. Smiles grew wide.

When the story ended, Rabbit whispered, “Leo, you fixed the box. You helped Mouse. You read to us. How did you do it all?”

Leo touched his chest. “I used mindful paws. I paused. I breathed. I listened. Then I chose.”

Mr. Bear nodded. “That is skill-building and good decision-making. Mindfulness helps our hearts and hands.”

Leo rang the bell softly. Ding. “The Story Box is open for everyone. Let’s share, help, and breathe together.”

The neighbors cheered. The sun felt warm. The little garage workshop felt like a bright, kind home.

The End.
