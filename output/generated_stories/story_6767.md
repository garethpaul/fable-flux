# Sunny Squirrel’s Kitchen Surprise

Sunny the squirrel lived in a warm kitchen with soft yellow light and the sweet smell of cinnamon toast. The floor felt smooth under her tiny paws. The kettle hummed a gentle song. Sunny was small, quick, and very resourceful. She loved to plan. She loved to fix. She loved to share—most of the time.

One bright morning, Sunny peeked into the big fruit bowl. “Oh! So many shiny apples,” she whispered. The apples were red, round, and cool to touch. “I will make apple slices for everyone!”

Her friends knocked on the back door. Tap-tap-tap! “Sunny, can we play?” asked Bree the bluebird.

“We can play and snack,” Sunny said. Her tail flicked with joy. “But first, we need a plan.”

Sunny pulled out a small notepad. “Let’s make a list,” she said. “Lists help us stay calm and kind.” She wrote in big, clear letters:
1. Wash hands
2. Wash apples
3. Slice safely
4. Share

Bree chirped, “I like your list!”

Pip the chipmunk sniffed the air. “Mmm, cinnamon!” he giggled. “I’m hungry!”

Sunny nodded. “Me too. But we will follow our plan. We can do it together.”

They washed their paws and wings with warm water and bubbly soap. The bubbles smelled like oranges. “This is self-care,” Sunny said. “We care for our bodies.”

“Self-care,” Bree repeated, flapping happily.

Sunny set the apples on a clean towel. She lined up plates in a neat row: red plate, blue plate, green plate. “Organization helps us share,” she explained. “When things are in order, we can see what we have.”

Pip bounced. “Can I have the biggest slice?”

Sunny paused. Her whiskers twitched. She wanted the biggest slice too. Her tummy made a tiny rumble. For a moment, her heart felt tight. She took a slow, deep breath in and out. “I can grow my sharing muscles,” she told herself. “I can choose to be kind.”

Sunny smiled at her friends. “Let’s make fair slices,” she said. “We will share.”

She asked Bree, “Can you count the friends?”

Bree hopped. “One, two, three!”

Sunny nodded. “Three friends. We will make three plates.”

She carefully sliced the apples with a safe, small cutter while an adult nearby watched. “Crunch!” went the apples. The smell was fresh and sweet. Sunny sprinkled a tiny bit of cinnamon. The kitchen felt even warmer.

Sunny placed equal slices on each plate. Then she saw one extra slice, small and shiny. Pip’s eyes grew wide. Bree’s eyes grew wide too. Sunny felt that tight feeling again. She held the little slice in her paw. She could keep it. She could eat it fast.

“I have an idea,” Sunny said. “A peaceful solution!”

“What is it?” Pip asked.

“We can make a sharing plate,” said Sunny. “The extra slice can go there. Anyone who is still hungry can ask, and we can decide together.”

Bree chirped, “That feels fair!”

Pip nodded. “We can use our words.”

They sat at the small round table. The chairs were soft and made a cozy squeak. Sunny took one slice and tasted. “Crunch.” Her tummy felt warm and happy. “Thank you for waiting and sharing,” she said.

Pip ate his slice and smiled. “I feel calm,” he said.

Bree pecked her slice gently. “I feel proud,” she sang.

Soon, the extra slice sat alone on the sharing plate. Pip looked at it. Bree looked at it. Sunny looked at it.

Sunny asked, “Is anyone still hungry?”

Pip raised a paw. “I am a little hungry,” he said. “May I have the sharing slice?”

Bree listened to her tummy. “I am full,” she said.

Sunny checked in with herself too. “I am okay,” she said. “Pip, you may have it.”

Pip took the slice slowly. “Thank you, Sunny,” he said. “I will take small bites.” He did, and the crunch sounded like tiny music.

After snack, Sunny clapped her paws. “Let’s clean up together!” She pointed to her list. “Organization keeps our kitchen safe and nice.”

Bree carried plates to the sink, wings careful and steady. Pip wiped the table with a soft cloth, round circles, swish-swish. Sunny put the apple cores in the compost bin. “We are a team,” she said. “We plan, we share, we clean.”

When the kitchen was tidy, Sunny opened a drawer. Inside were small labels and markers. She wrote: Plates, Cups, Snacks. She stuck the labels onto shelves. “Labels help us remember where things go,” she said. “When we know where things go, sharing is easy.”

Bree tilted her head. “We learned a lot today.”

Pip nodded. “We used peaceful words. We made a plan. We shared!”

Sunny felt light and bright. “And we cared for ourselves,” she added. “We washed hands. We ate slowly. We listened to our tummies.”

They all hugged. The kettle hummed again, and the warm kitchen smelled like apples and cinnamon and soap and sunshine.

Sunny looked at her list and drew a star at the bottom. She said, “Today I grew my sharing muscles. When I feel that tight feeling, I can breathe, make a plan, and choose to share.”

Bree chirped, “Sharing makes friends feel safe.”

Pip grinned, “Sharing feels good!”

Sunny’s tail swished with joy. “Sharing helps everyone,” she said. “I’m glad we did it together.”

The End.
