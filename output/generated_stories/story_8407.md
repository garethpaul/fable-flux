# Squeaky Follows the Steps

In a comfortable nursery with soft rugs and sunny windows, a resourceful squirrel named Squeaky lived in a cozy basket by the bookshelf. The room smelled like clean cotton and warm apples. Gentle music hummed, and a ticking clock went tick-tick-tick.

Squeaky had bright eyes and a fluffy tail. He loved to help. He liked the feel of smooth wooden blocks and the sound they made: clack, stack, tap! One morning, Teacher Mia smiled and set a colorful puzzle on the low table. It showed a picture of a big tree with tiny acorns.

“Friends, it’s puzzle time,” Teacher Mia said kindly. “We will follow the steps on the little card. Following directions helps us finish our work.”

Squeaky’s ears perked up. “I can do it!” he chirped. He wriggled his whiskers and read the simple picture steps on the card:

1. Corners first.
2. Straight sides next.
3. Middle last.

Squeaky looked at his friends. Pip the penguin waddled close, and Bella the bunny hopped near. Pip said, “Can we start with the big tree piece? It looks fun!”

Squeaky took a deep breath. He wanted to grab the tree piece too. It was shiny and green and made a soft papery sound. But he looked at the card again. The little pictures showed corners first.

Squeaky said, “Let’s follow the steps. Corners first.” He touched the card with his tiny paw. “Step one.”

Bella wrinkled her nose. “What if we skip the corners? We could finish faster.”

Squeaky shook his fluffy tail. “Hmm. I want to decide wisely. The card is here to help us. When we follow directions, things fit.”

Teacher Mia nodded. “Good thinking, Squeaky. Try the corners.”

Squeaky picked up a corner piece. It felt cool and smooth. He placed it at the top-left. Click! The piece fit nicely. Pip found another corner and pushed it in. Click! Bella giggled and found the last two corners. Click, click!

Squeaky’s heart felt warm. He smiled. “Step two: straight sides next.” He tapped the card. Pip and Bella reached for side pieces. The sides made a gentle sliding sound as they moved across the table. Swish, swish, click!

Suddenly, they heard a sniffle. Little Dot, the shy hedgehog, sat near the window. Her puzzle looked all mixed up. Her spines trembled. “I can’t do it,” she whispered. “I forgot the steps.”

Squeaky looked at his almost-finished frame. He wanted to keep going, but he paused. He thought, I can help and still follow directions. He made a choice.

Squeaky scampered to Dot with the step card. “Hi, Dot. Do you want help? We can follow the directions together.”

Dot blinked. “Will it be hard?”

Squeaky shook his head. “We’ll go slow. Corners first, sides next, middle last.”

Dot nodded. “Okay.”

They searched for corner pieces in her box. Dot’s paws felt nervous. Squeaky said, “Listen. Hear how the piece clicks when it’s right?” Click! Dot’s face brightened. “Oh! I heard it!”

Squeaky and Dot laughed softly. They finished the corners, then the sides. “Now step three,” Squeaky said. “Middle last.” Dot picked up the middle tree piece. It smelled like fresh cardboard. She placed it gently. Click! The picture showed a happy tree with tiny acorns, just like Squeaky’s puzzle.

“Great job, Dot,” Squeaky said. “You followed the directions!”

Dot smiled wide. “Thank you, Squeaky. You helped me.”

Squeaky hurried back to his puzzle. Pip and Bella had waited for him. “We saved the last piece for you,” Pip said.

Squeaky’s chest felt warm and proud. He lifted the final middle piece. Everyone leaned in. He pressed it down. Click!

Teacher Mia clapped softly. “You did it. You followed the directions. You made good decisions. And you helped a friend.”

Squeaky’s tail swished happily. “The steps kept us calm,” he said. “Corners, sides, middle. It works!”

Bella nodded. “I’m glad we didn’t skip.”

Pip waddled in a circle. “Following directions made it easier—and more fun!”

Teacher Mia set out a small snack of apple slices. The sweet smell filled the room. As they crunched, Squeaky thought about the day. He had wanted to start in the middle. He had wanted to finish fast. But he chose to follow the steps. He chose to help Dot. Both choices felt good.

Squeaky held up the step card. “These little pictures are like a map,” he said. “Maps help us know where to go.”

Dot hugged the card carefully. “Next time, I’ll use the map too.”

The clock went tick-tick-tick. The music hummed. The nursery felt safe and bright. Squeaky curled his tail and smiled. “When we follow directions,” he said, “we can solve problems, make smart choices, and help each other.”

Everyone nodded. Their puzzles looked neat and complete, and their hearts felt full.

The End.
