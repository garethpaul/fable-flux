# Mimi the Brave and the Busy Basement

Mimi was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She lived in a cozy nest behind a warm dryer in a busy basement. The basement hummed and buzzed. The washing machine sloshed, “Whoosh, whoosh,” and the dryer sang, “Hum, hum.” Boxes stacked high smelled like cardboard and soap. The floor felt cool on Mimi’s tiny paws.

One morning, Mimi poked her nose out and sniffed. “Good morning, basement!” she squeaked. “I will find crumbs for breakfast.”

She scurried along the wall, whiskers twitching. She heard clinks and clanks from a toolbox. She smelled fresh laundry, sweet and soapy. She saw a shiny puddle near a big bottle. “Oh!” said Mimi. “That looks like water.” She started to step closer.

Just then, her friend Pip, a tiny roly-poly bug, rolled by. “Hi, Mimi! What are you doing?” asked Pip.

“I am thirsty,” said Mimi. “I see a puddle.”

Pip peeked. “Mimi, that bottle has a scary picture. It might be soap or cleaner. It smells strong. It is not safe.”

Mimi stopped. She took a careful step back. “You are right,” she said. “If I don’t know what it is, I won’t touch it.” Her heart thumped, but she felt proud. She had used safe thinking.

She heard a rustle by a pile of boxes. It was Noodle the worm, wiggling and smiling. “Hello, Mimi,” Noodle said. “Do you want to play slide? We can slide down that shiny ramp!”

Mimi looked. The shiny ramp was a long board leaning on a stack of boxes. It looked fun, but it wobbled. “It looks wiggly,” Mimi said. “It could fall.”

Noodle wiggled faster. “But it looks so fun!”

Mimi took a deep breath. She remembered a rule she made for herself: Be safe, be kind, be brave. She stood tall on her tiny feet and said, “I want to be safe. The ramp is not steady. Let’s find a safe game.”

Noodle paused. “Okay,” he said, with a little sigh. “What safe game can we play?”

Mimi smiled. “Listen! I hear buttons go beep!” The washing machine blinked little lights. “Let’s play Beep Copy,” said Mimi. “When it goes beep, we say beep!”

Beep! “Beep!” sang Mimi, Pip, and Noodle. They laughed and clapped. They felt happy and safe.

Soon, a cold breeze snuck in from a door at the top of the steps. The door opened, and a person’s boots stomped. A heavy box came down the stairs. Thump, thump, thump. The person put the box on the floor and turned on a bright light. The light made the basement glow.

Mimi sniffed again. She smelled something sharp and hot. She looked and saw an orange cord tangled in a loop. The cord lay under the dryer, close to a dusty, fuzzy clump. The fuzzy clump looked like a tiny gray cloud—lint!

Mimi’s whiskers prickled. “Friends,” she said, “I see a tangle and fuzz near the cord. That could be a fire danger.”

Pip gulped. “What do we do?”

Mimi felt a little scared, but she was brave. “We will be safe and kind,” she said. “We will not touch the cord. We will tell the big person.”

Mimi scampered to the bottom of the stairs and squeaked up, “Excuse me! Excuse me!” Her voice was small, but her heart was big. The big person did not hear at first. Mimi tried again, louder and clear. “Excuse me! There is a tangle and fuzzy fluff by the cord!”

The big person bent down and looked. “Oh!” they said kindly. “Thank you, little mouse.” They unplugged the cord, swept up the lint, and tossed it away. They wrapped the cord neat and tight. Then they wiped up the shiny puddle and put the bottle on a high shelf with a lid. They even put the wobbly board flat on the floor.

Mimi felt warm with pride. Pip and Noodle cheered, “Yay, Mimi!”

The big person smiled. “Safety helpers deserve a treat,” they said, and they set a tiny dish of clean water and a few crumbs of cracker by the wall. “Stay safe, little friends.”

Mimi sipped. The water was cool and fresh. The cracker crumbs were crunchy and tasty. “Thank you!” she squeaked.

Later, the friends made a Safety Song. Mimi tapped her paws. Pip clacked his little legs. Noodle hummed softly.

Mimi sang:
“When I see a thing,
And I do not know,
I stop, I think,
I do not go.

I use my voice,
I say it loud,
I ask for help,
I feel so proud.”

Pip added:
“Kind and brave,
Safe and smart,
We help each other
With a caring heart.”

They danced in a little circle. The dryer hummed along, “Hum, hum,” like a happy drum. The basement felt bright and friendly now.

At bedtime, Mimi curled up in her nest behind the warm dryer. She felt cozy and calm. She whispered, “Today I was safe. Today I was kind. Today I was brave.” Her whiskers twitched with a tiny smile.

Outside, the lights blinked softly. Inside, Mimi fell asleep, knowing a big truth:

Being safe is being strong. Using your voice is brave. Helping others is kind.

The End.
