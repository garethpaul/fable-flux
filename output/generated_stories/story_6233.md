# Nutkin's Clean Adventure

In a cozy bathroom with white tiles and a big mirror, a little gray squirrel named Nutkin sat on the edge of the bathtub. His fluffy tail was covered in mud, and his tiny paws were sticky from climbing trees all morning.

Nutkin looked at himself in the shiny mirror. "Oh my!" he squeaked. "I'm so dirty and messy!"

The warm bathroom smelled like lavender soap. Soft towels hung on silver hooks, and a yellow rubber duck bobbed in the bathtub. Everything looked so clean and fresh.

But Nutkin felt scared. He had never taken a bath before. "What if the water is too cold? What if I slip?" he worried, his whiskers twitching nervously.

Just then, Mama Squirrel peeked through the bathroom door. Her fur was clean and fluffy, and she smelled like flowers.

"Nutkin, sweetie, it's time for your bath," she said gently. "I can see you're worried. Would you like to tell me what's wrong?"

Nutkin's ears drooped. "Mama, I'm scared of the water. And I don't know how to get clean properly."

Mama Squirrel smiled warmly. "Thank you for being honest with me, little one. Let's learn together, step by step."

She turned on the faucet. The water made a gentle splashing sound as it filled the tub. Steam rose up like tiny clouds.

"First, we test the water," Mama explained, dipping her paw in. "It should feel warm, not hot or cold. Would you like to try?"

Nutkin slowly reached out his tiny paw. The water felt nice and cozy, like a warm hug. "It's perfect!" he giggled.

"Now, let's add some bubbles," said Mama, squeezing sweet-smelling soap into the water. The bubbles popped and fizzed, making Nutkin laugh with delight.

"This looks fun!" Nutkin said, his curiosity growing stronger than his fear.

Mama helped Nutkin into the warm water. It felt wonderful on his muddy fur. She showed him how to use a soft washcloth to scrub gently behind his ears and between his toes.

"Why is it important to stay clean?" asked Nutkin, splashing playfully.

"When we wash away dirt and germs, we stay healthy and feel good," Mama explained. "Clean fur and paws help us feel fresh and happy."

Nutkin discovered that washing could be fun! He made bubble beards and gave his rubber duck friend a ride on his tail. The warm water made his muscles feel relaxed and sleepy.

"Don't forget to wash your face," reminded Mama, handing him a special gentle cloth.

Nutkin carefully cleaned around his bright black eyes and his little pink nose. "I can do it myself!" he said proudly.

After the bath, Mama wrapped Nutkin in the softest, fluffiest towel. It felt like being hugged by a cloud. She helped him brush his clean fur until it was smooth and shiny.

"Look at yourself now," said Mama, lifting him up to the mirror.

Nutkin gasped with joy. His gray fur sparkled, his tail was fluffy and beautiful, and he smelled wonderful. "I look amazing! And I feel so good inside too!"

"That's because taking care of yourself makes you feel proud and happy," said Mama, giving him a gentle kiss on his forehead.

From that day on, Nutkin looked forward to bath time. He learned that being clean wasn't scary at all. He discovered that asking questions and being honest about his feelings helped him learn new things.

Every evening, Nutkin would carefully wash his paws and face before bed. He brushed his teeth with his tiny toothbrush and combed his fluffy tail.

"Taking care of myself feels wonderful," Nutkin would say, looking at his clean reflection with a big smile.

And whenever his woodland friends felt nervous about trying something new, Nutkin would share his story and remind them that it's always okay to ask for help and learn together.

The End.
