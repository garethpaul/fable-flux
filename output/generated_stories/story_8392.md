# Benny Bear's Big Helper Day

Benny Bear was a small, fluffy cub with golden fur and bright brown eyes. He loved spending time in Grandpa Bear's garage workshop, where the smell of wood shavings and the sound of gentle hammering filled the air.

"Good morning, Grandpa!" Benny called out as he padded into the cozy workshop. Sunlight streamed through the dusty windows, making everything glow warmly.

"Good morning, little helper," Grandpa Bear smiled, his big paws carefully sanding a wooden birdhouse. "I have lots of work to do today. Would you like to help me fix some old toys for the forest friends?"

Benny's eyes sparkled with excitement. "Yes, yes! I want to help everyone!"

Grandpa Bear chuckled and pointed to a pile of broken toys in the corner. There was a wobbly rocking horse, a puzzle with missing pieces, and a stuffed rabbit with a torn ear.

"These toys need our care," Grandpa explained. "Instead of throwing them away, we can fix them and give them new life. That's much better for our forest home."

Benny bounced on his toes. "Let's start right now!"

But as Benny reached for the heavy rocking horse, Grandpa Bear gently caught his paw. "Wait, little one. Before we help others, we need to take care of ourselves first."

"What do you mean?" Benny asked, tilting his fuzzy head.

"Well," Grandpa said kindly, "have you eaten breakfast today?"

Benny's tummy rumbled loudly. "Oops! I forgot!"

"And did you wash your paws this morning?" Grandpa asked with a knowing smile.

Benny looked down at his dusty paws and giggled. "I forgot that too!"

Grandpa Bear led Benny to a small sink in the corner. "When we take care of ourselves first, we have more energy to help others. Let's wash up and have some honey toast."

The warm, soapy water felt good on Benny's paws. He scrubbed them clean while humming a happy tune. The sweet smell of honey filled the workshop as Grandpa prepared their breakfast.

"Mmm, this tastes so good!" Benny said, taking big bites of the golden toast. He felt his energy coming back with each delicious bite.

"Now I feel strong and ready!" Benny announced, wiping honey from his whiskers.

Together, they worked on the toys. Grandpa showed Benny how to sand the rocking horse gently, making the wood smooth again. Benny helped glue the puzzle pieces back together, his small paws perfect for the delicate work.

"This is fun!" Benny said as they stitched up the stuffed rabbit's ear. "And we're saving these toys instead of throwing them away!"

"That's right," Grandpa nodded. "Taking care of our things and fixing them helps take care of our whole forest."

When Little Rabbit and Squirrel came to pick up their repaired toys, their faces lit up with joy.

"Thank you, Benny!" they cheered. "Our toys look brand new!"

As the sun began to set, Benny yawned sleepily. "Grandpa, I learned something important today."

"What's that, little helper?"

"I need to take care of myself first - eat good food, stay clean, and rest - so I can help my friends better," Benny said proudly.

Grandpa Bear hugged him tight. "That's exactly right, Benny. When we care for ourselves, we can care for others and our world too."

Benny smiled, feeling happy and proud as they cleaned up the workshop together.

The End.
