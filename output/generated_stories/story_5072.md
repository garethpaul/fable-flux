# Kody the Koala Makes Things Right

In the cozy guest room with soft blue walls and a fluffy white bed, lived a calm koala named Kody. He had gray fuzzy fur and gentle brown eyes that always looked sleepy. Kody loved to help keep the guest room tidy for visitors.

Every morning, Kody would fluff the pillows. *Puff, puff!* He would smooth the blankets. *Whoosh!* He would even dust the nightstand with his soft paws. The guest room always smelled fresh like lavender and looked perfectly neat.

But one busy Tuesday morning, Kody felt very tired. His eucalyptus breakfast had made him extra sleepy.

"I'll just rest for a tiny bit," Kody yawned, climbing onto the guest bed. "Then I'll do my chores."

Kody curled up in a fuzzy ball and fell fast asleep. He dreamed of climbing tall eucalyptus trees under the warm sunshine.

*Ring, ring!* The doorbell woke Kody up hours later. 

"Oh no!" Kody gasped, looking around the messy room. The pillows were squished flat. The blankets were all wrinkled and twisted. Dust covered the nightstand like tiny gray sprinkles.

Through the window, Kody saw Grandma Rose walking up the path with her purple suitcase. She was coming to stay in the guest room!

"Welcome, Grandma Rose!" called Mom from downstairs.

Kody's tummy felt all twisty and worried. Grandma Rose was so kind and always brought honey cookies. But now her room was messy because Kody had been lazy.

"I made a big mistake," Kody whispered to himself. "I need to make this right."

Kody heard footsteps coming up the stairs. *Thump, thump, thump.*

"Here's your room, Mom," said Kody's mother, opening the door.

Grandma Rose's kind blue eyes grew wide when she saw the messy room. "Oh my," she said softly.

Kody took a deep breath and stepped forward. His paws felt shaky, but he knew what he had to do.

"Grandma Rose," Kody said in his gentle voice, "I'm very sorry. I was supposed to keep your room clean and ready. But I got sleepy and took a nap instead of doing my chores. I made a mistake, and I feel bad that your room isn't nice for you."

Grandma Rose's wrinkled face broke into a warm smile. "Oh, sweet Kody. Thank you for telling me the truth. Everyone makes mistakes sometimes."

"Can I please fix it?" Kody asked hopefully. "I want to make your room perfect for you."

"That would be wonderful," Grandma Rose nodded. "But let me help you. Working together is more fun!"

Kody's heart felt lighter already. Together, they fluffed the pillows until they were puffy like clouds. *Puff, puff!* They smoothed the blankets until they were flat and neat. *Whoosh!* Grandma Rose dusted while Kody organized her things.

"There!" said Grandma Rose, looking around the fresh, clean room. "It's perfect now. And Kody, I'm proud of you for saying sorry and fixing your mistake."

Kody's fuzzy cheeks felt warm with happiness. "I feel much better now. Saying sorry helped my tummy stop feeling twisty."

That evening, Kody and Grandma Rose shared honey cookies in the beautiful, clean guest room. The room smelled like lavender again, and everything sparkled.

"Tomorrow I'll do my chores first, then rest," Kody promised. "Taking care of others feels good."

Grandma Rose hugged Kody tight. "You're such a thoughtful koala. Sweet dreams!"

As Kody drifted off to sleep, he felt peaceful and proud. He had learned that saying sorry and fixing mistakes made everything better.

The End.
