# Mango’s Music Routine

Mango the courageous monkey loved the musical practice room. It smelled like polished wood and bananas from his snack. Sunlight peeked through the window, making the drum skins shiny. Guitars hummed softly on the wall, and the big piano waited with quiet keys.

Every morning, Mango followed his routine. He said, “Good morning, room.” He washed his paws. He stretched his arms, his tail, and his smile. He checked his list on the wall, written with big, friendly pictures:

1. Warm-up clap
2. Drum beat
3. Piano practice
4. Clean up
5. Snack time song

Mango tapped the list and said, “Step by step. I can do it!”

One day, when Mango clapped, the sound was soft and sticky. “Oh!” he said. “My paws are banana-y.” He giggled, but he did not skip his routine. “Wash first,” he said, and he washed again. Clap, clap! “Much better.”

Mango sat at the drum. “Boom-boom, boom-boom,” he played. The drum went thump and the air tickled his ears. He smiled. “Next, piano.”

But when he looked for the key chart—a paper with colored dots to show which keys to press—it was gone. Mango frowned. “Hmm. My chart is missing.”

Mango felt a wibble in his tummy. “I want to play. I need my chart.” He took a deep breath. “I am courageous. I can think.”

He looked at his list. He said, “I will follow my routine and solve the problem.” He asked himself little questions, like a helper. “What do I know? What can I try?”

Mango stood tall like a leader and called gently, “Friends, is anyone here?” A tiny mouse named Pip poked out from a tambourine. “Squeak! I saw paper fly near the window,” said Pip.

“Thank you, Pip,” said Mango. He walked to the window. The breeze smelled like flowers. He listened. Rustle, rustle. He peeked behind the piano and under the benches. No chart.

Mango used resourceful thinking. “I can make a new chart,” he said. He looked at the colored stickers on the shelf. Red, blue, yellow, green. He looked at the piano keys, black and white like a zebra.

Pip squeaked, “Can I help?”

“Yes,” said Mango. “We will work together. I will lead us with the routine.”

Mango made a mini-plan:
- Find the sounds
- Match colors
- Stick new dots
- Try the song
- Check the list

He pressed a key. “Ping!” It sounded like a raindrop. He smiled. “This key can be red.” He pressed another. “Plonk!” It sounded like a frog. “This key can be green.” He pressed a low key. “Dum,” like a big sleepy drum. “Blue,” he said. A high key went “Ting!” like a tiny bell. “Yellow.”

Pip handed stickers. Mango placed them carefully. His tail curled with focus. He hummed his warm-up notes. “La-la-la.” He followed his routine steps, slow and steady.

Just then, a gust of wind whooshed. A paper flapped on the floor. Mango picked it up. “My old chart!” he cheered. He looked at the piano. He looked at his new stickers. He did not feel upset. He felt proud.

“I used my brain and my routine to make a new tool,” Mango said. “Now I have two ways to learn!”

Pip clapped his tiny paws. “Squeak! That was smart.”

Mango smiled. “Thank you, Pip. Now, piano practice.”

He set the old chart on the stand and kept the new stickers on the keys. He pressed red. “Ping!” He pressed blue. “Dum.” He pressed green. “Plonk.” He pressed yellow. “Ting!” The room filled with happy sounds. Mango’s heart felt brave and calm.

“Let’s play the Snack Time Song,” Mango said. “Ready?”

They followed the list. Step 1, clap. Clap-clap-clap! Step 2, drum. Boom-boom! Step 3, piano. Ping, dum, plonk, ting! Step 4, clean up. Mango wiped the keys. Pip put the stickers back in the box. Step 5, snack time song. Mango sang, “Step by step, we do our best. We think, we try, we lead with rest.”

When they finished, Mango checked the room. The guitars were quiet, the drums were tidy, and the air smelled like clean wood and a little like bananas again.

Pip said, “Mango, you were a good leader.”

Mango nodded. “I used my routine to stay calm. I asked good questions. I tried new ideas. I cleaned up. Now I understand that routines help me think and solve problems.”

Pip smiled. “And they make music fun.”

Mango laughed. “Yes! Tomorrow, we will follow the steps again.”

He waved to the room. “Good job, team,” he said to Pip and to himself. Mango felt strong, smart, and safe. He packed his snack wrapper, washed his paws, and turned off the light.

Outside the window, the sun was warm. Inside the musical practice room, the piano waited with two helpers: the old chart and the new colorful keys. Mango’s routine was ready for another brave day.

The clear lesson is simple: Routines help us stay calm, think clearly, and solve problems, step by step.

The End.
