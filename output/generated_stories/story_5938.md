# Soft Steps in the Quiet Attic

A gentle deer fawn named Fern tiptoed up the creaky wooden steps. Creak, creak. The quiet attic smelled like old books and dry leaves. Dust danced in thin sunbeams that peeked through a small round window. The floor felt cool and smooth under Fern’s tiny hooves.

Fern’s ears twitched. “Hello?” she whispered. Her voice sounded soft and small in the big, still room.

She had a plan. Downstairs, the little ones—two mice, Pip and Dot, and a shy spider named Lacy—wanted a puppet show. Fern promised to bring down a big, colorful puppet curtain she had seen in the attic. But the attic was full of boxes, ropes, and a tall stack of sleepy hats that smelled like lavender.

Fern spotted the curtain rolled up on a high shelf. It was bright red with golden stars. “There you are,” she said, feeling brave. She took a deep breath. The air tasted a little dusty, like cinnamon without sugar.

Fern tried to reach. Tap, tap went her hooves. Stretch, stretch went her neck. The curtain sat just out of reach.

Pip and Dot scampered up the steps behind her. Their tiny feet made patter-patter sounds. “We came to help!” squeaked Pip.

“We want the puppet show!” whispered Dot, eyes shining.

Lacy lowered herself from a beam on a fine, silver thread. She hung like a tiny twinkling star. “I can help too,” she said in a voice as light as a breeze.

Fern smiled. “Thank you. We need the red curtain. But it’s high, and the floor is slippery. We must move carefully. Let’s make a plan.”

She pressed her ears forward and looked around. Boxes, ropes, brooms, and the stack of sleepy hats stood like a tiny forest.

Fern said, “First, let’s decide what each of us will do. That way we can move together.”

Pip nodded fast. “I can climb!”

Dot hugged her tail. “I can carry small things!”

Lacy spun gently. “I can tie and hold with my silk.”

Fern’s heart felt warm. “Good choices,” she said. “We will use coordination. That means we move and think together, like a dance.”

They all took a slow breath. In… out. Fern listened to the still attic. Creaks, a soft wind, and tiny mouse squeaks. She felt calm and ready.

“Step one,” Fern said softly. “We clear a safe path.” She nudged brooms aside with her nose. Swish. Pip and Dot pushed a small box together. “One, two, push!” they squeaked. The box slid with a shoop sound.

Lacy floated down to the sleepy hats. “Careful,” Fern said. “We will move the hats together on three.” Fern counted, “One, two, three.” She lifted the top hat with her nose while Pip and Dot steadied the brim. Lacy tied a little silk loop to keep it from wobbling. The hat made a gentle thump as it sat safely by the wall.

Fern smiled. “Good coordination. We did it at the same time.”

“Step two,” said Fern. “We build a reaching place.” She gently rolled a soft rug to the foot of the shelf so her hooves wouldn’t slip. Pip and Dot stacked two sturdy books on the rug. Plunk, plunk. Lacy tested the stack with a tiny tap. “Steady,” she chirped.

“Step three,” Fern said. “We climb and hold.” Fern placed one hoof on the books. “I will climb slowly. Pip, you stand on my left. Dot, you stand on my right. If I wobble, tell me. Lacy, you can tie a silk guide from the shelf to the rug.”

“Like a safety string!” said Pip.

Fern nodded. “Yes, a safety string.”

Fern took a careful step. The books felt firm. She took another step. Her heart thumped—thump-thump—but she kept her breath slow. Pip squeaked, “Left is good!” Dot said, “Right is good!” Lacy’s silk line shimmered in the light, a soft, silver path.

Fern reached up, up. Her nose touched the curtain. It smelled like sunshine and orange peels. She could almost taste the brightness.

“I have it,” she said. “But it’s heavy.”

“Decision time,” Fern said calmly. “We can pull it down fast or lower it slowly. Which is safer?”

“Slowly,” Pip and Dot said together.

“Slowly,” echoed Lacy.

Fern smiled. “We choose slow.”

Lacy looped silk around one end of the curtain. Pip and Dot held the other end with tiny paws. “One, two, three,” Fern said. Together they lowered the curtain. The fabric whispered, shhh, shhh, as it slid down. No crash, no slip—just soft, safe sounds.

They placed the red curtain on the rug. Golden stars winked at them. Fern’s tail gave a happy swish.

“We did it!” Dot cheered.

Fern nuzzled them gently. “We moved as a team. We made a plan. We decided together. That is coordination.”

They carried the curtain downstairs, step by careful step. Creak, creak, creak. In the cozy room below, they hung the curtain across two chairs. The golden stars shined like little smiles.

Pip popped up behind the curtain with a sock puppet. “Hello!” he squeaked. Dot giggled and made her puppet wave. Lacy dangled a tiny paper moon on her silk.

Fern sat close, warm and proud. The puppet show was silly and sweet. Everyone laughed. The room smelled like apple tea, and the laughter sounded like bells.

When the show ended, Fern said, “We solved our problem by working together. We listened, we planned, and we moved at the same time. That’s coordination. It helps us be safe, kind, and strong.”

Pip and Dot nodded. Lacy sparkled in the light. Their hearts felt full.

The End.
