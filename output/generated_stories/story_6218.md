# Momo’s Gentle Guest Room Routine

Momo was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and soft, fuzzy fur. One sunny morning, Momo visited Auntie Bea’s house. Auntie Bea smiled and said, “Momo, you’ll nap in the guest room today. We have a special routine.”

Momo loved adventures. He peeked into the guest room. It smelled like lavender. A little fan hummed softly. Sunlight made golden squares on the carpet. The bedspread felt smooth and cool. Momo wiggled his toes and giggled.

Auntie Bea showed a chart on the wall. “Our calm guest room routine goes like this,” she said. “First, tidy. Next, wash hands. Then, quiet play. After that, story time. Finally, rest.”

Momo nodded bravely. “I can do that!”

First, tidy. Momo placed his backpack on a chair. He lined up his crayons in a neat row. Click, click, click went the crayons. He folded his little blanket. “Tidy feels nice,” Momo whispered.

Next, wash hands. The water was warm like a hug. Swish, swish. The soap smelled like oranges. Bubble mountains covered Momo’s fingers. “All clean,” he sang.

Then, quiet play. Momo found a soft puzzle of animals. The pieces made gentle tapping sounds on the low table. “Where does the lion go?” he asked himself. “Roar… but softly.” He placed the lion piece and smiled.

But Momo noticed a shiny bell on the dresser. Ding! He wanted to ring it. He wanted to leap and twirl. His tail twitched. “I am courageous,” Momo said. “I can make good choices.” He took a deep breath in… and out. The fan hummed, the room smelled calm, and the sunlight felt warm on his fur.

Auntie Bea came back with a book. “Story time,” she said.

Momo clapped, but softly. Auntie Bea sat on the bed and patted the spot next to her. “Hop up, dear.”

Momo hopped up. The bed squeaked a tiny squeak. Auntie Bea opened the book. The pages whispered, flip, flip. She read about a kind turtle who moved slowly and stayed calm.

Momo listened, eyes wide. He could almost hear ocean waves in the turtle story. Shhhhhhh. He felt his breathing slow.

After the story, Auntie Bea smiled. “Now rest.”

Momo wanted to be brave and follow the routine. But his feet felt bouncy. His tail felt springy. He did a tiny hop. Then another tiny hop. The bed went boing! The shiny bell winked at him. Ding! He tapped it with one curious finger. Ding-ding!

Auntie Bea’s eyebrows lifted. The fan kept humming. The lavender smell still floated in the air. Momo froze. He remembered the chart. He remembered “quiet play,” “story,” and “rest.”

Momo placed his hand on his heart. Thump-thump. He took a slow breath. In…and out. He looked at Auntie Bea and said, “I forgot the calm part. I’m sorry.”

Auntie Bea’s face softened. “Thank you for your apology, Momo. That was very kind and brave.”

Momo nodded. “Can I try again?”

“Of course,” said Auntie Bea. “Let’s do our calm steps.”

Together they whispered the steps:
- “Tidy.” Momo set the bell gently back on the dresser and straightened the blanket.
- “Wash hands.” Swish, swish. Cool water this time. It felt refreshing.
- “Quiet play.” Momo finished the last puzzle piece. Tap. Done.
- “Story time.” They turned one page back and read the turtle’s last part again. “Slow and steady,” said the turtle.
- “Rest.” Momo lay down on the cool, smooth bedspread. He tucked in his tail. He placed his hands on his tummy. In…and out. He listened to the fan’s hum and the soft bird song outside the window. Tweet…tweet…

Auntie Bea dimmed the light. The golden squares on the carpet turned peach and soft. Momo felt cozy. His brave heart felt gentle. He whispered, “Routine helps me feel calm.”

Auntie Bea nodded. “Routine is like a friendly map. It guides us.”

Momo blinked slowly. He felt proud. He followed the routine. He said sorry when he forgot. He used calm breaths. He learned that courage is not just jumping high. Courage can be being gentle and making good choices.

After rest time, Momo sat up and stretched. “Auntie Bea,” he said, “thank you for the chart. Can I make a little song to remember it?”

“Yes!” Auntie Bea laughed.

Momo sang softly:
“Tidy, wash, play so quiet,
Story, rest, peaceful diet.
Breathe in, breathe out, nice and slow,
Calm and kind is how we go.”

They clapped very softly together. Clap, clap.

Momo hugged Auntie Bea. “I like the guest room,” he said. “It smells nice. It sounds soft. It feels safe.”

“I’m proud of you,” said Auntie Bea. “You followed the routine. You apologized. You stayed calm.”

Momo grinned. “Tomorrow, I’ll follow the routine again.”

The End.
