# Benny Beaver's Garden Surprise

The morning sun warmed the cozy balcony garden. Bright red tomatoes hung heavy on their vines. Yellow sunflowers swayed in the gentle breeze. This was where Benny Beaver loved to work.

"Tap, tap, tap!" went Benny's busy teeth as he nibbled on a small branch. He was building tiny plant supports for his garden friends who lived on the balcony below.

"Good morning, Benny!" chirped Rosie Robin from her nest in the corner. "What are you making today?"

"I'm building bridges for the ants," Benny said proudly. His brown fur gleamed in the sunlight. "They need help carrying seeds to their home."

Benny had planned everything carefully. He made a list in his head: bridges for ants, water dishes for birds, and shade covers for the tender seedlings. His flat tail slapped happily against the wooden deck.

"Sniff, sniff!" Benny's nose twitched. Something smelled wonderful! Fresh strawberries were ready in Mrs. Chen's garden next door.

"Oh my!" said Mrs. Chen, stepping onto her balcony. "Benny, look how big my strawberries have grown!"

Benny's tummy rumbled. The red berries looked so juicy and sweet. But he remembered his mama's words: "A good beaver always asks before taking."

"Mrs. Chen," Benny said politely, "your strawberries smell amazing. They look so yummy!"

Mrs. Chen smiled. Her eyes crinkled at the corners. "Would you like to try one, Benny? You've been such a helpful neighbor."

"Really?" Benny's eyes grew wide. "Thank you!"

Mrs. Chen picked the biggest, reddest strawberry. Benny took a small bite. Sweet juice ran down his chin. "Mmm! This is the best strawberry ever!"

Then Benny had a wonderful idea. His whiskers twitched with excitement.

"Mrs. Chen, I have fresh lettuce and carrots in my garden. Would you like some? We could share!"

"That sounds perfect!" Mrs. Chen clapped her hands. "I love fresh vegetables."

Benny scurried to his garden patch. He carefully picked the crispiest lettuce leaves and the smallest, sweetest carrots. His strong paws worked quickly but gently.

"Here you go!" Benny said, offering his vegetables. "I grew them myself!"

"Thank you, Benny! You're so generous," Mrs. Chen said. "Would you like to take some strawberries to your other friends?"

Benny's heart felt warm and fuzzy. "Yes, please! Rosie Robin and the squirrel family would love them!"

Together, they filled a small basket with strawberries and vegetables. The basket smelled like sunshine and rain and growing things.

"Tweet, tweet!" Rosie Robin flew down when she saw the treats. "Oh, Benny! These look wonderful!"

"They're for everyone," Benny said happily. "Mrs. Chen and I are sharing our gardens!"

Soon, all the balcony friends gathered around. The squirrel family chittered with joy. The butterflies danced around the flowers. Even the busy ants stopped to enjoy tiny strawberry pieces.

"This is better than eating alone," Benny realized. His tail slapped the deck with happiness. "When we share, everything tastes sweeter!"

Mrs. Chen patted Benny's head gently. "You planned this garden so well, Benny. And sharing your hard work makes it even more special."

As the sun climbed higher in the sky, Benny looked around at his friends enjoying the feast. His heart felt as full as his tummy.

"Tomorrow," Benny said to himself, "I'll plan an even bigger sharing party!"

The balcony garden buzzed with happy sounds. Friends eating together, birds singing, and Benny's contented munching filled the air. It was the most wonderful morning ever.

Benny learned that the best gardens grow not just vegetables and fruits, but friendship and joy too.

The End.
