# Melody the Dolphin's Musical Garden

Melody was a bright blue dolphin who lived in a magical water tank on Mrs. Chen's sunny balcony garden. The garden was full of colorful flowers, buzzing bees, and singing birds. Melody loved splashing in her tank and watching the pretty butterflies dance around the roses.

Every morning, Melody would make beautiful music by slapping her tail on the water. *Splash, splash, splash!* The sounds were like drums. She would click her tongue to make whistling noises too. *Click, whistle, click!* The flowers seemed to dance to her rhythm.

But there was one big problem. When the neighborhood children came to visit the garden, they were very loud. They would shout and run around, making so much noise that nobody could hear Melody's lovely music.

"Listen to my song!" Melody would call out, but the children kept talking loudly. They picked flowers without asking. They scared away the singing birds. Poor Melody felt sad and invisible.

One warm afternoon, a little girl named Emma came to the garden with her grandmother. Emma had curly red hair and wore a yellow dress with musical notes on it. She loved music just like Melody did.

"Grandma, do you hear that?" Emma whispered, putting her ear close to Melody's tank. "The dolphin is making music!"

Melody's heart jumped with joy. Finally, someone was listening! She made her prettiest sounds. *Splash, click, whistle, splash!*

"That's beautiful!" said Emma softly. "But I can barely hear it because those other children are being too loud."

Emma looked at the noisy group of children. They were still shouting and being rough with the flowers. Emma felt nervous, but she knew she had to speak up for her new friend.

"Excuse me," Emma said in a clear, brave voice. "Could you please be quieter? The dolphin is trying to make music for us, but we can't hear it when we're loud."

The children stopped and looked at Emma. One boy said, "We're just having fun!"

"We can have fun AND listen to the music," Emma explained kindly. "It's not fair to the dolphin when we're too noisy. She wants to share her songs with us."

The children looked at Melody, who made a gentle clicking sound. They had never really listened before.

"Oh wow!" said a girl with pigtails. "She really is making music!"

"Let's sit quietly and listen," suggested Emma. "Then maybe we can make music together!"

All the children sat down on the soft grass. The garden became peaceful. Birds started singing again. Bees hummed as they visited flowers. And Melody began her most beautiful concert ever.

*Splash, splash, click, whistle!* Her tail created rhythms on the water. Her clicks made melodies that mixed with the bird songs. The children clapped softly along.

"This is amazing!" whispered the boy. "I'm sorry we were too loud before."

Emma smiled. "When we're kind and listen to each other, everyone gets to share their gifts."

From that day on, the balcony garden became known as the most musical place in the neighborhood. Melody taught the children that music is meant to be shared, but only when everyone listens with respect and kindness.

Every afternoon, they would gather for "Melody's Music Time." The children learned to use their voices gently, the birds sang along, and Melody splashed her happiest rhythms in her sparkling tank.

The End.
