# Benny Beaver’s Helpful Treehouse Day

Benny was a busy beaver with bright eyes and strong teeth. He lived in a cozy treehouse by the river. The treehouse smelled like warm wood and pine. Sunlight made golden spots on the floor. Birds sang, “Tweet-tweet!” outside the window.

Benny looked at his list. “Today I will sweep, water my plants, and tidy my tool box,” he said. He wore a little green bandana. It felt soft on his neck. His tail thumped—tap, tap—because he was excited.

As Benny swept, he heard a tiny knock. “Knock-knock.”

Benny opened the door. It was Dot the duckling. Dot’s feathers were fluffy and damp. “Hi, Benny,” quacked Dot. “My little wing hurts. I can’t reach my nest. Can you help me?”

Benny looked at his list. He wanted to finish his chores. He took a deep breath. Helping is important, he thought. He smiled. “Yes, Dot. Helping friends is first.”

Benny grabbed a small leaf pouch with bandages and a cool water bottle. “Let’s go slowly,” he said. “Sip some water. It helps your body feel good.”

Dot sipped. “Sip, sip.” She smiled. “Thank you, Benny.”

They walked to the river path. The air smelled fresh and leafy. The ground was soft and squishy—squish, squish. Benny saw a shiny candy wrapper under a bush. He frowned a little. “Hmm. Litter makes the forest sad,” he said. He picked it up and put it in his pocket. “We care for our home.”

Dot nodded. “Quack. Caring is kind.”

At the pond, Dot pointed up. “My nest is there,” she said. It was on a low branch by the water. The branch wiggled a bit in the breeze.

Benny knelt. “Let me check your wing.” He was gentle. He wrapped a soft bandage around Dot’s little wing. “There,” he said. “We will move slow.”

Benny looked at the wiggly branch. “I will make a safe ramp,” he said. He collected fallen sticks and strong leaves. He did not take living branches. “We use what is already on the ground,” he told Dot. “That keeps trees healthy.”

Tap, tap, tap went Benny’s little wooden peg as he pressed the sticks together. The ramp smelled like clean bark. Dot took careful steps—step, step—and reached her nest.

“Thank you!” Dot quacked. “You helped me feel safe.”

“You’re welcome,” said Benny. “Rest and drink water. Wellness means caring for our bodies.”

On the way home, Benny saw his friend, Tilly Turtle, by the path. Tilly’s shell was dusty. She looked worried. “Benny,” she said slowly, “I am late for my sun spot. Can you help me get there?”

Benny looked at the sky. He still had chores. He also wanted to help. “Yes, Tilly,” he said. “Friends help friends.”

Benny used a big leaf to fan a cool breeze. “Whoosh, whoosh.” He showed Tilly a shady path with soft moss. “This way. It is smooth and safe.” Tilly smiled and moved along—shuffle, shuffle. At the sun spot, warm light made the ground feel cozy.

“Thank you,” Tilly said. “You helped my body feel good.”

“You’re welcome,” said Benny. “Sun time is healthy for you.”

Benny waved goodbye and hurried back to his treehouse. Inside, his tools were still messy. His broom was by the door. He remembered something. In the morning, he had told himself, “I will do all my chores.” But he did not. He felt a small wiggle in his tummy. It was the honesty wiggle.

Benny put a paw on his heart. “I was busy helping,” he said to himself. “I will be honest about my day.”

Just then, Grandpa Beaver knocked. “Knock-knock.” Grandpa stepped in. He smelled like cedar and river water. “How is my busy beaver?”

Benny looked at the tools. “Grandpa, I did not finish my chores,” he said. “I helped Dot and Tilly. I cleaned some litter too. I want to finish now.”

Grandpa’s eyes crinkled kindly. “Thank you for telling the truth,” he said. “Helping others is a good job. I can help you, too.”

They worked together. Sweep, sweep went the broom. Clink, clink went the toolbox. Benny watered his plants. The leaves looked shiny and green. He opened the window. Fresh air whooshed in—whoosh—and the treehouse felt bright and happy.

When they were done, Benny’s tail thumped—thump-thump. His heart felt warm. He had helped his friends, cared for the forest, and told the truth.

Grandpa smiled. “What did you learn today, Benny?”

Benny thought. He took a deep breath. The treehouse smelled like clean wood and mint from his plant. “I learned that helping others comes first,” he said. “Helping keeps friends safe and well. I learned to care for our home by picking up litter and using fallen sticks. And I learned honesty feels good in my heart.”

Grandpa nodded. “Those are strong lessons.”

Benny peeked out the window. Dot rested in her nest. Tilly basked in the sun. The river whispered, “Shhh.” The trees swayed, “Swish.”

Benny waved and called, “If you need help, I am here!”

The forest seemed to smile back.

The End.
