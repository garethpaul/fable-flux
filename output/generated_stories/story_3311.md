# Buzzy Beaver’s Bubble Pause

Buzzy the beaver was very busy. His tail went thump-thump when he hurried, and his paws went tap-tap when he worked. One morning, Buzzy rushed into the bathroom. It was bright and steamy. The mirror was foggy like a cloud. The bath smelled like sweet soap and minty toothpaste. Plip-plop, the faucet dripped a little song.

“I have to brush! I have to splash! I have to be fast!” Buzzy said. His whiskers wiggled. His heart felt buzzy, buzzy, buzzy.

Mama Beaver knocked gently. “Good morning, Buzzy,” she said. “Slow is okay.”

“I can’t slow,” Buzzy huffed. “I need to do everything right now.”

He grabbed his blue toothbrush. He squeezed the toothpaste. Squish! Too much came out—squiggly and white—plop on the sink and plop on the floor.

“Oh no!” cried Buzzy. “I made a mess!” His tummy tightened. His tail thumped too fast. Thump-thump-thump.

Mama opened the door a little. “I hear big feelings,” she said softly. “Your body is telling you it needs a pause.”

“I don’t have time for a pause,” Buzzy said. “I need to fix it now.”

Then, Buzzy’s paw bumped the cup. Clink! The cup tipped. Water splashed—splash, splash—over the counter and onto the floor. Cold water tickled his toes.

Buzzy’s eyes got watery. “I ruined everything,” he whispered.

Mama knelt beside him. “You are not ruined. You made a mess. Messes can be cleaned. Feelings can be calmed. Let’s try a Bubble Pause.”

“What’s a Bubble Pause?” Buzzy sniffed.

“It’s a tiny break for your brain and body,” Mama said. “We breathe slowly and notice what we feel. Then we choose our next step.”

Buzzy looked at the shiny bubbles in the sink. He nodded.

Mama held up one finger. “First, feel your feet. Are they warm or cold?”

“Cold,” Buzzy said. “Splashy-cold toes.”

“Good noticing. Second, listen. What do you hear?”

“Drip-drop,” Buzzy whispered. “And my heart… thump… thump…”

“Third, breathe,” Mama said. “In like you smell a flower… and out like you blow a bubble.”

Buzzy breathed in through his nose. Sniff. He smelled mint and soap. He breathed out slowly. Whooo. A tiny soap bubble wobbled on the water. It popped with a soft “pip!”

Again. Flower… bubble. Flower… bubble.

Buzzy’s shoulders softened. His tail slowed. Thump… thump…

“How do you feel now?” Mama asked.

“Less buzzy,” Buzzy said. He smiled a small smile. “I can choose.”

“Yes,” said Mama. “You can choose kind actions.”

Buzzy looked at the toothpaste scribble. “I will clean it. I can wipe and wash.” He took a small cloth. Swish, swish. He wiped the sink. He used both paws, slow and steady. He set the cup upright and filled it halfway. Glug, glug. No rushing.

“I spilled,” Buzzy said, peeking up at Mama. “I’m sorry.”

“Thank you for saying sorry,” Mama said, giving his head a gentle pat. “I forgive you. And I am proud of your Bubble Pause.”

Buzzy’s cheeks felt warm and happy. Forgiven felt light, like a bubble in his chest.

He picked up his toothbrush again. “Flower… bubble,” he whispered. He squeezed the toothpaste—just a pea-sized dot. “Plop.” He grinned. “Just right!”

Buzzy brushed in tiny circles. “Round and round, little teeth,” he sang. The mint tasted cool. The foam felt tickly. He listened to his brush go swish-swish-swish. He counted slow. “One… two… three…” No rushing, just steady brushing.

When he rinsed, he took one small sip. Swish, swish. He spit—ptoo!—right into the sink. He wiped his chin with a soft towel. The towel felt fluffy on his fur.

Then Buzzy looked at the tub. The bath water was warm and bubbly. He wanted to jump in right away. His paws twitched. His tail wiggled fast again.

He stopped. “Bubble Pause,” he reminded himself.

He felt his feet on the mat—soft and dry. He listened—drip-drop and Mama humming. He breathed in—flower. He breathed out—bubble. His body calmed.

“I will test the water first,” Buzzy said. He dipped a paw. “Just right.” He climbed in slowly. “Ahhh,” he sighed. The water hugged him warm. The bubbles popped like tiny kisses—pop, pop, pop!

Buzzy made a little boat from a cup and a washcloth. He sailed it gently. He bumped it into the side by mistake. “Oops,” he said. “I forgive me. I can try again.” He set it straight and watched it glide.

When bath time was done, Buzzy pulled the plug. The water swirled—whoosh, whoosh—like a little whirlpool. He wrapped himself in a big towel. “I feel calm,” he said.

Mama smiled. “What did you learn today?”

Buzzy stood tall. “When I feel too fast, I can do a Bubble Pause. I can feel my feet, listen, and breathe. I can choose kind actions. I can say sorry and forgive myself, too.”

Mama hugged him. “That is self-regulation,” she said. “You are growing your calm.”

Buzzy’s tail gave one happy thump. “I like growing my calm,” he said.

And that night, when the bathroom was quiet and the mirror was clear, Buzzy whispered to himself, “Flower… bubble.” He felt peaceful from whiskers to tail.

The End.
