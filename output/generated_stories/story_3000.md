# Melody's Colorful Song

Melody was a bright yellow bird who lived near a cozy nursery window. Every morning, she loved to sing beautiful songs for baby Emma who slept inside. The nursery had soft pink walls and fluffy white clouds painted on the ceiling.

But Melody had a problem. She wanted to make something special for baby Emma, but she didn't know how. All she could do was sing the same songs every day.

"Tweet, tweet, la-la-la," Melody sang sadly. "I wish I could make pretty pictures like the ones on Emma's walls."

Through the window, Melody could see colorful crayons scattered on a small table. There were red ones and blue ones and purple ones too. They smelled waxy and sweet.

"Those look wonderful," Melody chirped. "But I don't have hands like people do."

Melody felt her heart sink. Her orange beak wasn't good for holding crayons. Her tiny feet couldn't grip them either.

Just then, Emma's mama walked into the nursery. She picked up the crayons and started drawing on a big white paper. Melody watched carefully through the glass.

"Oh!" Melody gasped. "She's making art with those colorful sticks!"

Melody thought and thought. She looked at her reflection in the window. Then she had a wonderful idea.

"I may not have hands," she said, "but I have something else special!"

Melody flew to the garden below. She found a shallow puddle from yesterday's rain. The water was cool and clear. Next to it grew bright red berries on a bush.

Melody carefully dipped her tiny feet in the berry juice. The red liquid felt cool and sticky. Then she hopped onto a big white rock.

"Look!" she chirped happily. "My feet make pretty red dots!"

Step by step, Melody created a pattern of red dots on the rock. They looked like tiny flowers dancing in a circle.

Next, she found purple grapes that had fallen from a vine. She rolled them gently with her beak, making purple smears. The grape juice smelled sweet and fruity.

"This is so much fun!" Melody sang.

She discovered yellow dandelion petals and blue flower juice too. Soon, her rock was covered with a beautiful rainbow painting. The colors swirled together like a sunset.

Melody was so excited! She flew back to the nursery window and began to sing. But this time, her song was different. She sang about red dots and purple swirls. She sang about blue skies and yellow sunshine.

Baby Emma opened her sleepy eyes and smiled. She pointed at the window where Melody perched.

"Mama, look!" Emma's mama said softly. "The little bird is singing about colors!"

Melody's heart felt warm and happy. She had found her own special way to make art. She didn't need to use crayons like people did. Her feet and beak and voice were perfect for creating beauty.

From that day on, Melody painted on rocks every morning. Then she sang songs about her colorful creations for baby Emma. Each song told a story about the pictures she made.

"Every creature has their own special way to make art," Melody chirped proudly. "I just needed to use my imagination!"

Emma clapped her tiny hands and giggled. Melody's songs helped her learn about colors and shapes. Together, they shared the joy of creativity through the nursery window.

Melody learned that being different wasn't a problem - it was a gift. Her unique way of making art brought happiness to everyone around her.

The End.
