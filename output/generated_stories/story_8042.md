# Rocco Raccoon Says “I’m Sorry”

Rocco was a musical raccoon with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved drums, he loved bells, and he loved the shiny piano keys. Every day, Rocco went to the musical practice room. The room smelled like fresh wood and lemon polish. Sunlight made the dust sparkle, and music stands stood like little trees.

Today was a big day. Rocco and his friends were going to play a song for their class. Rocco bounced in with his drumsticks. Tap-tap! Tap-tap! He hummed a happy tune.

“Good morning, Rocco,” said Ms. Melody, the kind music teacher. She wore a scarf with tiny notes on it. “Please set up your drum and check your sticks.”

Rocco nodded, but he saw a new shiny cymbal. “Ooo!” he squeaked. “Shiny!” He ran to the cymbal and tapped it. Crash! The sound was loud and bright. He forgot to set up his drum. He forgot to check his sticks.

Lila the little lion cub was tuning a small violin. “I like that sound,” she said. “But we need to be ready.”

“Ready, ready!” said Benny the bunny, who held jingle bells. He shook them. Jingle-jingle!

Ms. Melody clapped gently. “Friends, let’s warm up and prepare.”

Rocco felt a wiggle in his feet. He wanted to try every instrument. He grabbed Benny’s jingle bells. “Just a little shake,” he said.

But Benny was using them. “Oh! I need those,” Benny whispered.

Rocco kept shaking. Jingle-jingle-jingle! He giggled and spun. Then, oops! The bells slipped from his paws and rolled under a chair.

Benny’s ears drooped. “I can’t reach them.”

Rocco giggled again, but when he looked at Benny’s sad face, his tummy felt tight. The room went quiet. Ms. Melody kneeled down next to Rocco.

“Rocco,” she said softly, “did you ask before taking the bells?”

Rocco looked at his paws. “No.”

“Did you prepare your drum like we practiced?” she asked.

Rocco shook his head. “No.”

Ms. Melody nodded kindly. “It’s important to be ready. It’s important to be helpful. And when we make a mistake, we say, ‘I’m sorry,’ and we fix it.”

Rocco’s ears drooped. He felt warm in his cheeks. He took a deep breath. “Benny,” he said in a small voice, “I’m sorry I took your bells. I didn’t ask. I wasn’t careful.”

Benny looked at him. His ears lifted a little. “Thank you for saying sorry,” Benny said. “Can you help me get them back?”

“Yes,” Rocco said. He dropped to his belly. The floor was cool on his fur. He stretched his paw under the chair. “I feel them!” he said. The bells felt cold and smooth. He pulled them out and dusted them off. “Here you go.”

“Thank you,” said Benny. He shook the bells gently. Jingle!

Rocco turned to Lila. “I’m sorry I made a loud crash before everyone was ready,” he said.

Lila smiled. “Thank you for saying sorry. Let’s get ready together.”

Rocco’s heart felt lighter. He picked up his drum. He checked the little screws. Tight. He checked his sticks. Smooth. He placed the drum on the stand. “I am preparing now,” he said. He even looked at the music page with big black notes. “Tap-tap, rest. Tap-tap, rest.”

Ms. Melody smiled. “Wonderful initiative, Rocco. You are taking action to fix your mistake and prepare.”

Rocco nodded. He wanted to be helpful too. He saw that Lila’s music stand was wobbly. “May I help?” he asked.

“Yes, please,” said Lila.

Rocco twisted the knob. Twist-twist. The stand felt steady. “All set.”

“Thank you,” Lila said. She plucked her violin. Plink-plink.

Soon everyone was ready. The practice room hummed with soft sounds: a gentle drum tap, a tiny violin plink, a quiet bell jingle. The air smelled warm, like wood and a little bit like orange from a cleaner. Sunlight made the cymbal glow like a small moon.

Ms. Melody raised her hand. “One, two, ready, play!”

Rocco watched, listened, and played his part. Tap-tap, rest. Tap-tap, rest. He kept the beat like a steady heartbeat. Lila’s violin sang sweet notes. Benny’s bells sparkled.

The song ended with a happy crash on the cymbal—this time at the right moment. Crash! Everyone smiled.

Ms. Melody clapped. “Friends, that was lovely. Rocco, I am proud of you. You said ‘I’m sorry.’ You prepared your drum. You helped your friends. That is what musicians do.”

Rocco’s eyes shone. “I learned that saying sorry helps hearts feel better,” he said. “I learned to prepare before I play. I learned to ask, ‘May I help?’”

Benny hopped. “And I learned to forgive.”

Lila nodded. “And we all learned to work together.”

Rocco hugged his drum with his striped arms. He felt calm and happy. He looked at Benny and Lila. “Next time, I will ask before I take something. I will get ready first. I will be helpful.”

Ms. Melody smiled. “That is a beautiful plan.”

Rocco tapped a tiny rhythm. Tap-tap. “I’m ready.”

The practice room seemed to hum with a soft, warm song. Everyone packed up together. Rocco helped close the cases. Click. He wiped the drum with a cloth. Swish. He placed the sticks in a cup. Clink.

Before they left, Rocco turned to his friends. “Thank you for listening,” he said. “I’m glad I said sorry.”

“We are glad too,” said Benny and Lila.

They waved goodbye, and Rocco’s paws made gentle pat-pat sounds in the hall as he went home, feeling proud, prepared, and kind.

The End.
