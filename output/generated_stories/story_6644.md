# Nutkin's Big Problem

Nutkin the squirrel sat on the windowsill of the cozy guest room. His fluffy gray tail twitched as he looked around. The room smelled like fresh flowers from the vase on the dresser. Soft sunlight streamed through the white curtains.

"Oh no!" Nutkin squeaked. He had been collecting acorns all morning, but now he had a big problem. His favorite acorn had rolled under the heavy wooden bed. The space was too small for him to squeeze through.

Nutkin's whiskers drooped. "How will I get my acorn back?" he wondered aloud. The acorn was special - it was perfectly round and golden brown, just like his grandmother's favorite.

He tried reaching with his tiny paws. "Stretch, stretch!" he grunted. But his arms were too short. The acorn sat far in the middle, just out of reach.

Next, Nutkin tried pushing the bed. He put his shoulder against the wooden leg and pushed with all his might. "Oof!" he puffed. The bed was much too heavy. It didn't budge even a little bit.

Nutkin sat down and put his paw on his chin. He needed to think like a smart squirrel. "There must be a way," he said to himself. "I just need to use my brain!"

He looked around the guest room carefully. On the nightstand, he spotted a long wooden ruler. His dark eyes lit up with an idea.

"That's it!" Nutkin chittered excitedly. He scampered up to the nightstand and carefully picked up the ruler with his teeth. It was smooth and cool.

Back at the bed, Nutkin lay on his belly. He slid the ruler under the bed slowly and gently. "Easy does it," he whispered to himself.

The ruler touched the acorn with a soft tap. Nutkin moved it carefully, nudging the acorn bit by bit. "Come on, little acorn," he encouraged. "Come back to me!"

Slowly but surely, the golden acorn rolled closer and closer to the edge. Nutkin's heart beat faster with excitement. His plan was working!

Finally, the acorn rolled out from under the bed. "Hooray!" Nutkin cheered, doing a little happy dance. He hugged his precious acorn tight against his fluffy chest.

Just then, Mrs. Robin flew to the window. "What's all the excitement about, Nutkin?" she chirped sweetly.

"I had a big problem, but I solved it!" Nutkin said proudly. "My acorn was stuck under the bed, but I didn't give up. I thought of a plan and used this ruler to get it back!"

Mrs. Robin smiled with her bright orange beak. "That's wonderful, Nutkin! You showed great thinking. When we have problems, we can always find solutions if we stay calm and use our minds."

Nutkin nodded wisely. "And it's okay to try different ideas until one works!" he added. He felt proud and confident.

The warm afternoon sun made the guest room feel even cozier. Nutkin carefully placed his special acorn in his collection pile by the window. He had learned something important today.

"Every problem has a solution," Nutkin said to himself. "I just need to think, stay calm, and keep trying." He curled up his bushy tail and settled down for a well-deserved nap, feeling very proud of his problem-solving skills.

The End.
