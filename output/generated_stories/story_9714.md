# Benny Beaver Builds a Band

Benny the busy beaver loved to make things with his paws and teeth. One bright morning, he waddled into the musical practice room at the community center. The room smelled like fresh wood and lemon polish. Sunlight danced on shiny music stands. Drums, flutes, and a big piano waited quietly.

Benny wiggled his flat tail. “I want to play music too!” he said. He picked up a drumstick. Tap, tap, tap! The sound was loud and bouncy. But when he looked around, he saw some cracked drum heads, a flute with a bent mouthpiece, and a broken maraca. A sign on the wall said, “Please be gentle with instruments.”

Benny’s ears drooped. “Oh no,” he whispered. “These instruments are tired.”

Miss Melody, the music teacher, peeked in. She wore bright green shoes and smelled like oranges. “Good morning, Benny,” she sang. “Ready to make music?”

Benny shook his head. “The drum is ripped. The maraca is broken. I can’t play.”

Miss Melody smiled kindly. “This is a problem. What could we do?”

Benny took a deep breath. He remembered what his mama always said: “Try, try, try. If it doesn’t work, ask why.”

“I can fix things!” Benny said. “I am a crafter. I build with care. I can make instruments from safe, clean, recycled things.”

Miss Melody clapped softly. “What a sustainable idea! Let’s reuse what we have. Let’s craft!”

Benny and Miss Melody opened the supply cupboard. Inside were boxes of safe, clean items: empty oatmeal cans, paper tubes, rubber bands, bottle caps, fabric scraps, wooden sticks, and a small bag of dry beans. There was also child-safe glue, tape, and markers.

Benny’s whiskers twitched with excitement. He touched the smooth oatmeal can. “This feels cool,” he said. He shook the dry beans. “Rattle, rattle!” His eyes sparkled. “We can make a band!”

Miss Melody nodded. “Let’s make a plan. What do we need?”

Benny thought. “We need drums, shakers, and maybe a guitar sound.”

He drew simple pictures on scrap paper. He labeled them: Drum, Shaker, String Thing. His letters were wiggly, but clear.

“First,” Benny said, “we wash and dry everything.” He and Miss Melody cleaned the items with warm, soapy water and set them on a towel. The room smelled fresh and a little bubbly.

Then they began.

Benny wrapped fabric around the oatmeal can and taped it tight. He stretched a clean piece of recycled paper over the top like a drum skin and secured it with a big rubber band. Tap, tap! “It works!” Benny giggled.

Next, he made shakers. He put a few beans into a small bottle, then added colorful paper confetti. He twisted the cap on tight and taped it closed. He shook it. Rattle, rattle, swish! The sound was soft like rain.

“For the string thing,” Benny said, “we can use a tissue box and rubber bands.” He slid rubber bands over the empty box. Twang! Twang! The sounds were bouncy and bright.

Miss Melody smiled. “You are a creative crafter, Benny.”

Benny blushed. “Thank you. But I want to fix the old maraca too.” He looked at the broken handle. The crack looked like a tiny smile. He tried tape. It fell off. He tried glue. It wobbled.

Benny’s tail drooped again. “It’s still broken,” he sighed.

Miss Melody knelt beside him. “What can we try next?”

Benny tapped his chin. “Try, try, try. If it doesn’t work, ask why.” He looked closer. “The handle is too weak. It needs a stronger helper.”

He slid a small wooden stick inside the handle like a brace, then wrapped it with fabric and tape. He shook it gently. Rattle-rattle! It held together. “I did it!” Benny cheered.

Soon, other children peeked into the room. “What’s that sound?” asked Mia.

“Come play with us!” Benny said. He handed out the crafted instruments. “These are reused. We saved them from the trash. That is good for the Earth.”

The children felt the instruments. The drum felt bumpy under the fabric. The shaker felt warm in little hands. The string thing felt stretchy and fun.

“Let’s make music,” said Miss Melody.

Benny counted, “One, two, three, four!”

Tap, rattle, twang! The room filled with happy sounds. Benny led a simple song:
“Shake, shake, shake,
Tap, tap, tap,
Twang-twang-twang,
Clap, clap, clap!”

They played softly like snow. They played loudly like thunder. They played fast like hopping bunnies. They played slow like drifting clouds. Everyone smiled. Even the piano seemed to hum.

After the song, Benny took a bow. “We fixed, we crafted, and we didn’t waste. We tried many ways. We kept going.”

Miss Melody nodded. “Today we learned three things: We can craft with care. We can help the Earth by reusing and repairing. And when a problem is hard, we try, try, try again.”

Benny’s heart felt big and warm. He hugged his drum. “I’m a busy beaver with a growth mindset!” he said proudly. “I can learn and build with love.”

The children cheered. The musical practice room glowed with joy, and the shelves now held a brand-new, handmade band.

The End.
