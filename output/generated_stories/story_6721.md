# Benny's Big Bounce Workshop

Benny the kangaroo joey loved to bounce! *Boing, boing, boing!* He bounced everywhere he went. His fluffy brown fur would flutter in the wind as he hopped around the yard.

One sunny morning, Benny discovered something exciting - Grandpa Roo's garage workshop! The big wooden doors were wide open, and inside he could see shiny tools hanging on the walls and a workbench covered with interesting things.

"Wow!" Benny said, his eyes growing wide. "Look at all those cool tools!"

Without thinking, Benny bounced right into the workshop. *BOING!* He knocked over a jar of screws. *CRASH!* Then he bounced into a stack of wood pieces. *CLATTER!*

"Oh my!" called Grandpa Roo, rushing in from outside. "Benny, what happened here?"

Benny's ears drooped down. "I'm sorry, Grandpa. I just got so excited about your workshop. I wanted to see everything!"

Grandpa Roo smiled kindly and patted Benny's head. "I understand, little one. But workshops have special rules to keep everyone safe. Would you like to learn about them?"

"Yes, please!" Benny said, bouncing just a tiny bit.

"First," said Grandpa Roo, "we need to use our 'thinking pause.' When we feel excited, we stop, take a deep breath, and think before we act."

Benny practiced taking a slow, deep breath. "Like this? *Inhaaaale... exhaaaaale...*"

"Perfect! Now, let's clean up together, and then I'll teach you how to use some tools properly."

They picked up all the scattered screws, and Benny carefully stacked the wood pieces. The workshop smelled like sawdust and metal - a wonderful, busy smell.

"Now," said Grandpa Roo, "let's practice walking instead of bouncing in here. Can you show me your best careful walking?"

Benny put one paw in front of the other, walking slowly and steadily. "Look, Grandpa! I'm being careful!"

"Excellent! Now you're ready to learn about tools." Grandpa Roo showed Benny a small hammer. "This is for building things. We hold it like this, and we tap gently."

Benny held the hammer carefully with both paws. *Tap, tap, tap* went the hammer on a piece of wood. "I did it! I used the tool the right way!"

"You certainly did! And because you took your time and listened carefully, you're ready for a special project."

Grandpa Roo brought out some smooth wood pieces and child-safe tools. "Let's build a bird feeder for the backyard! The birds will love having fresh seeds."

Working together, Benny learned to measure, sand the wood with gentle strokes, and help attach the pieces. Every time he felt excited and wanted to bounce, he remembered to take his thinking pause.

"*Inhale... exhale...* Now I can work carefully," Benny said to himself.

When they finished, the bird feeder was beautiful! It had a little roof and a perfect spot for seeds.

"Can we hang it outside now?" Benny asked.

"Of course! Now you can bounce all you want in the yard!"

They hung the bird feeder on a tree branch, and Benny celebrated with his biggest, happiest bounces. *BOING, BOING, BOING!*

Soon, colorful birds came to eat the seeds. Benny watched them quietly, feeling proud of what he had built.

"Thank you for teaching me about thinking pauses, Grandpa," Benny said. "Now I know when to be calm and when to bounce!"

Grandpa Roo hugged him tight. "You learned that different places need different behaviors, and that's very wise!"

The End.
