# Ella’s Rooftop Steps

The sun was soft and golden on the rooftop garden. Wind chimes tinkled like tiny bells. Flower pots stood in neat rows. A gentle elephant calf named Ella stood there, her big ears flapping. She could feel the warm tiles under her feet and smell mint and tomatoes growing in pots. “Today,” she said, “I have a big goal.”

Ella’s trunk wiggled with nerves and excitement. “I want to learn rooftop steps! Step, step, step, and turn. I will dance carefully and strong.” She tapped one toe, then another. Tap, tap. The wind brushed her cheek like a friendly hand.

Her neighbor, a kind pigeon named Pip, landed on the railing. “Coo! Hello, Ella. What is your plan?” Pip asked.

Ella smiled. “My goal is to dance the rooftop steps by sunset.”

Pip tilted his head. “That is a big goal. How will you do it?”

Ella thought. The chimes sang again. “First, I will make a small plan,” she said. “I will warm up, then practice in little pieces, then take a rest, and then try again.”

“Coo! That sounds smart,” Pip said.

Ella stretched. She rolled her shoulders. She swayed her trunk like a ribbon. “Warm up,” she said. “This is self-care. I am kind to my body.” She took a sip of cool water. The water felt fresh and sweet.

“Ready?” Pip asked.

“Ready.” Ella placed her feet on two tiles. “Step, step, turn,” she said. She tried to move light, but her big foot went “clomp!” A pot wobbled. Dirt sprinkled out like chocolate crumbs.

“Oh no!” Ella’s ears drooped. “I am too clumsy.”

Pip flapped his wings. “It’s okay, Ella. Everyone learns with practice. Let’s try a smaller goal.”

Ella nodded. “Yes. First goal: Two soft steps.” She breathed in slowly. “Step.” She lifted her foot and set it down like a feather. “Step.” Softer this time. The tiles felt smooth and warm, like toast.

“Coo! Good job,” Pip said. “How do you feel?”

“I feel proud,” Ella said. Her cheeks glowed. “Now I will rest for one minute. Rest is part of my plan.” She sat on a cushion. She closed her eyes. She listened to the chimes, the city hum, and a faraway dog bark. Her heartbeat slowed. She took another sip of water.

On the next roof, a family of cats watched. The smallest kitten called, “You are big! You can’t dance!”

Ella felt a pinch in her tummy. Pip puffed up. “That was not kind,” he said.

Ella took a deep breath. “I will try tolerance,” she whispered. She looked at the kitten and spoke gently. “We are all different. I am an elephant. I can be careful and kind and still dance my way.”

The kitten blinked. The mama cat stepped forward. “We are sorry,” she said. “We will watch quietly.”

Ella’s heart felt light again. “Thank you,” she said, and she smiled. “Now, second goal: Step, step, turn with a pause.” She stood tall. “I can do this. I will go slowly.”

“Coo! I will count for you,” Pip offered. “One… two… pause… and turn!”

Ella moved. Step—soft like a cloud. Step—soft like a whisper. Pause—she breathed in and out. Turn—she swivelled gently. Her ear brushed a string of beads, and they made a pretty shhh-ting sound.

“Did I do it?” Ella asked, eyes bright.

Pip clapped his wings. “You did it! How do you feel now?”

“I feel calm and strong,” Ella said. “But my feet feel warm. I need another rest.” She stretched her toes and wiggled them. She dabbed a bit of cool water on her forehead. “Self-care helps me keep going.”

The sun moved across the sky. Shadows stretched like long cats. Ella stood again. “Final goal: Rooftop steps all together. Step, step, turn, and a bow.”

Pip hopped closer. “Remember your plan. Break it into parts. Be kind to your body. Be kind to yourself.”

Ella nodded. “Thank you, coach Pip.” She giggled. “Okay. Ready.”

She listened to the wind chimes and let them be her music. “Three, two, one,” she whispered. Step—soft. Step—soft. Turn—gentle. Bow—slow. Her trunk curled like a vine, and her tail gave a happy swish.

From the next roof, the kittens cheered, “Hooray!” The mama cat purred. “You did it, dancer!”

Ella’s eyes sparkled. Her heart felt warm, like sunshine on the tiles. “I did it! I reached my goal.” She took a big sip of water, then nibbled a crunchy apple slice from her snack bag. The apple tasted sweet and cozy.

Pip fluttered up and circled. “Coo! You set a goal. You made a plan. You practiced in small steps. You rested when you needed. You were patient with others and with yourself. That is how goals grow.”

Ella touched a wobbly pot and tucked the soil back in with her gentle trunk. “I also learned to be careful and to fix small messes,” she said. “That is part of practice.”

The sun slipped low, painting the clouds peach and pink. The rooftop felt peaceful. Ella sighed a happy sigh. “Tomorrow, I will set a new goal,” she said. “Maybe I will learn a hop.”

Pip laughed. “Coo! One step at a time.”

Ella smiled. “Yes. One small step, then another.”

Lesson: Set a goal. Make a simple plan. Practice in small pieces. Be kind to your body and your heart. Be patient and tolerant. Rest, then try again. With time and care, you can do big things.

The End.
