# Nutkin's Big Rooftop Challenge

The morning sun warmed the red brick rooftop where Nutkin the squirrel lived. His fluffy gray tail twitched with excitement as he looked at the big pile of acorns he had collected. Today was sharing day with his rooftop friends!

"Good morning, everyone!" chirped Robin from her nest in the chimney.

"Morning!" squeaked Mouse, poking his tiny head out from under a loose shingle.

Crow flapped down with a loud "CAW! CAW!" His shiny black feathers gleamed in the sunlight.

Nutkin had planned a fun game. "Let's take turns rolling acorns down the roof slope!" he said. "We can see whose acorn goes the farthest!"

Robin clapped her wings. "That sounds wonderful!"

But when Nutkin set up the starting line, Crow pushed right in front. "I go first!" Crow cawed loudly. "I'm the biggest!"

Mouse squeaked sadly from behind. He was too small to push past Crow.

Nutkin's whiskers twitched. His heart beat fast. Speaking up to Crow felt scary - Crow was so much bigger! But this wasn't fair to Mouse and Robin.

Nutkin took a deep breath. "Wait, Crow," he said in a clear, brave voice. "We need to take turns. That's how games work best for everyone."

Crow ruffled his feathers. "But I want to go first!"

"I know," said Nutkin kindly. "But Robin was here first, then Mouse, then you. Taking turns means everyone gets a chance to have fun."

Robin nodded. "Nutkin's right! When we take turns, no one feels left out."

Mouse's tiny voice piped up. "I was worried I wouldn't get to play at all."

Crow looked at his friends' faces. Mouse looked sad. Robin looked hopeful. Nutkin looked patient but firm.

"Oh," said Crow, his voice getting softer. "I didn't think about that. I just got really excited about the game."

Nutkin's tail fluffed up happily. "That's okay! Being excited is good. Now, let's line up properly. Robin, you're first!"

Robin carefully rolled her acorn. It tumbled down the slanted roof - bump, bump, bump - and landed in the rain gutter. "Hooray!" she chirped.

Mouse went next. His tiny paws could barely hold the acorn, but he tried his best. The acorn rolled slowly and stopped halfway down. "I did it!" he squeaked proudly.

Now it was Crow's turn. He pushed his acorn with his strong beak. It rolled fast and far, flying right off the roof! "WOW!" everyone cheered.

Finally, Nutkin took his turn. His acorn rolled steadily and landed right next to Robin's in the gutter.

"That was so much fun!" said Mouse. "Can we play again?"

"Yes!" said Crow. "And this time, I'll wait for my turn without pushing."

Nutkin smiled big. His heart felt warm and proud. Speaking up had felt scary at first, but it helped everyone have more fun together.

"You know what?" said Robin. "When Nutkin spoke up, it made the game better for all of us."

Mouse nodded. "Sometimes the brave thing to do is speak up for what's right."

Crow bobbed his head. "And I learned that waiting my turn made the game more fun, not less fun!"

As the friends played more rounds, taking careful turns, Nutkin felt proud and happy. He had been brave when it mattered. He had helped his friends learn something important too.

The sun climbed higher in the sky, warming the rooftop where four good friends played together, each one taking turns and each one feeling special.

The End.
