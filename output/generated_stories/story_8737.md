# Kora the Calm Koala’s Plan

In a quiet study corner, soft light shone on a little desk. The desk smelled like fresh paper and lemon-clean shelves. Kora the calm koala sat in a cozy chair with a tiny pillow. Her fur was silver-gray and fluffy, and her ears were round and soft. She took a deep breath in—sniff, sniff—and let it out slow. Kora liked slow.

On the desk lay crayons, a stack of picture cards, and a small puzzle with bright animal shapes. It was time to sort the cards and finish the puzzle. Kora wanted to do a good job. She wanted to feel proud.

But the cards were mixed up. The puzzle pieces were all over the place. A giraffe piece peeked from under a paper. A lion piece sat by her water cup. Kora’s tummy felt tight for a moment.

“This looks hard,” Kora whispered. Her voice was as soft as cotton.

Her friend, a tiny ladybug named Luli, landed on the pencil cup and wiggled her little antennae. “Hard does not mean no,” Luli said. “It means plan.”

Kora blinked. “Plan?” she asked.

“Yes,” Luli buzzed gently. “A plan is a smart way. We can make steps. Step by step.”

Kora took another slow breath. She remembered her healthy habits: sip water, stretch, and use kind words to herself. She took a small sip—cool and fresh—and did a tiny stretch—arms up, wiggle toes. “I can try,” Kora said. “I can be brave.”

Luli nodded. “Let us make a strategy,” she said.

Kora liked the word. It felt steady. “Stra-te-gy,” she said, tapping the desk lightly. Tap, tap, tap.

“What is our first step?” asked Luli.

Kora looked at the mess and listened to the quiet hum of the lamp. She listened to her calm breath. “Step one,” she said, “we make space.” She gently pushed the crayons into a cup. Scritch-scratch went the crayons. She stacked the paper. She wiped a tiny crumb with a soft cloth. The desk looked open and clear.

“Good step,” Luli said. “Step two?”

“Step two,” Kora said, “we sort the puzzle pieces.” She picked up the giraffe piece. It felt smooth and warm in her paw. “Animals go on the left. Cards go on the right.” She slid pieces into neat little lines. Click, click. The sounds were nice.

“Step three?” Luli asked.

“Step three,” Kora smiled, “we do the corners first.” She found the puzzle corners—round tree, blue sky, green grass, yellow sun. She placed them with gentle taps. “Corners make a frame,” she said. “The frame helps me see.”

A tiny worry poked Kora when she could not find the last corner. “Oh,” she said quietly. “Where are you, little sun?”

“Breathe,” Luli reminded. “Brave and calm.”

Kora put her paw on her chest and felt it go up and down. “In... out,” she said. She peeked under the paper stack. “Ah!” The yellow sun corner smiled up at her. “Found you.” She placed it. Tap.

“Step four,” Kora went on, “we match colors.” She looked for all the green pieces for the grass. “Green with green,” she hummed. The pieces clicked together like friends.

As she worked, the study corner felt cozy and good. The lamp hummed. The pages gave a soft papery whisper when she moved them. Kora’s water cup made a tiny clink when she set it down.

A big piece didn’t fit. Kora tried to push. It wouldn’t go. Her cheeks got warm. “Maybe I can’t,” she said in a small voice.

Luli fluttered closer. “You are brave when you pause,” she said. “Try a new idea.”

Kora nodded. “New idea.” She turned the piece around and looked at the shape of the edges. “Step five,” she said, “we try a gentle turn.” She lined up the bumps with the notches. Click! It fit.

Kora’s eyes shone. “It works!”

Soon the animals had faces and tails. The giraffe stood tall. The lion looked proud. The sky was smooth and blue. Kora sat up a little straighter. She could feel her self-esteem grow, like a small light inside her chest.

“Now the cards,” Luli reminded.

“Yes,” Kora said. “Step six: make groups.” She made a group for red, a group for blue, a group for yellow. She made a group for circles, and a group for squares. She giggled when a circle card tried to hide under a square card. “Hello, silly circle,” she said, sliding it into the right group.

Kora stretched again, slow and happy. “Last step,” she said, “we tidy up.” She put the sorted cards into small boxes with little labels. “Colors,” said one label. “Shapes,” said the other. She put the puzzle back in its tray and wiped the desk once more. The lemon-clean smell made her nose crinkle with joy.

Luli clapped her tiny wings. “You used a strategy,” she said. “You were brave. You were kind to you. You made a plan and you followed it.”

Kora’s smile felt warm. “I did,” she said. “Step by step.”

She looked at the tidy study corner. It looked peaceful. It felt safe. Kora took one last sip of water and patted her pillow into a soft puff. “When things look big,” she told herself, “I make a plan. I take a breath. I try one step. Then another.”

“That is a strong habit,” Luli said. “Healthy mind. Healthy body.”

Kora nodded. “And a happy heart.”

The End.
