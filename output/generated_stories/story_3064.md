# Sunny’s Soft-Paw Surprise

Sunny was a small, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail. One morning, Sunny scampered into the guest room in the big, cozy house. The guest room smelled like fresh soap and warm blankets. The curtains were soft and yellow. A stack of pillows sat on the bed like puffy clouds.

“I love this room,” Sunny whispered. “It feels calm.”

Sunny had a little backpack. Inside were tiny tools: a string, a button, a leaf fan, and a roll of gentle tape. Sunny liked to solve small problems. It made Sunny feel brave and helpful.

Sunny heard a quiet sound: “Sniff… sniff.” It was soft and sad.

Sunny’s ears perked up. “Who is there?” Sunny asked, stepping softly across the rug. The rug felt bouncy under Sunny’s paws.

From behind a pillow, a small stuffed rabbit peeked out. The rabbit had one floppy ear and one stuck-up ear. A single thread hung from its arm.

“My name is Blue,” said the rabbit in a tiny voice. “I’m a guest in the guest room. I’m scared. My arm feels loose. I don’t want to fall apart.”

Sunny sat down, tail curling around like a question mark. “Hi, Blue,” Sunny said softly. “I hear that you feel scared. Thank you for telling me. I want to understand how you feel.”

Blue sniffled. “When people come, they hug me. I like hugs. But with my arm like this, I worry I will break. I feel alone.”

Sunny nodded. “That sounds hard. I would feel worried too if my arm felt loose.” Sunny put a tiny paw on Blue’s soft head. “You are not alone. I am here.”

Blue’s little button eyes grew brighter. “You understand?”

“Yes,” Sunny said. “I understand your feelings. That is called empathy. It means I try to feel what you feel.”

Sunny opened the backpack. “Let’s see how we can help.” Sunny spoke slowly so Blue would not be scared. The guest room was quiet. The clock went tick-tock, tick-tock. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor.

Sunny held up the gentle tape. “This is soft,” Sunny said. “It will not hurt.”

Blue nodded. “Okay.”

Sunny looked at the loose thread. “I will be careful,” Sunny said. “Please tell me if anything feels too tight.”

Sunny worked with soft paws. The tape made a tiny “shhh” sound. Sunny wrapped it just enough to hold the arm snug. “How does that feel?” Sunny asked.

Blue wiggled the arm. “It feels safe,” Blue whispered.

Sunny smiled. Sunny felt proud, but not bossy. “You did great telling me how you felt,” Sunny said. “You helped me help you.”

Blue’s floppy ear bounced. “Thank you,” Blue said. “You were gentle.”

A draft blew in from the window. The curtains fluttered like yellow butterflies. Blue shivered. “Sometimes the room feels too big,” Blue said. “Visitors come and go. I don’t know what will happen.”

Sunny listened. “It sounds like you feel small,” Sunny said. “Would a cozy corner help?”

“Yes, please,” Blue said.

Sunny looked around. There was a chair with a soft blanket draped over it. Sunny tugged the blanket down, making a little nest on the rug. Sunny fluffed the pillows into a comfy wall. Sunny used the leaf fan to waft warm air. “This can be your snuggle spot,” Sunny said.

Blue climbed in with a small hop. “It smells like clean cotton,” Blue said, smiling. “It feels soft.”

Sunny tucked in a corner of the blanket. “When new people come,” Sunny said, “you can rest here. You can take deep breaths.”

Sunny showed Blue how to breathe. “In… like the smell of a warm cookie,” Sunny said, sniffing. “Out… like blowing a bubble.” Sunny and Blue breathed together. In and out. The tick-tock of the clock matched their calm breaths.

Footsteps sounded in the hallway. “Guests!” Blue whispered.

Sunny nodded. “You can choose what feels okay,” Sunny said. “Would you like to wave from your nest?”

Blue sat up taller. “Yes. I can wave.”

The door opened just a little. Two friendly voices said, “What a lovely room!” They did not see Sunny, who was small and quiet. But they saw Blue in the cozy nest. Blue lifted the safe arm and gave a brave wave.

“Aww,” one guest said. “What a happy bunny.”

Blue’s cheeks felt warm with joy. When the door closed, Blue turned to Sunny. “I did it!” Blue said. “I was brave.”

Sunny clapped tiny paws. “You were brave and honest,” Sunny said. “You shared your feelings. You took care of yourself. That is self-confidence.”

Blue hugged Sunny gently. “You listened to me,” Blue said. “That is empathy.”

Sunny’s tail swished. “And you let me help. That is helpfulness we did together.”

They sat in the soft light. The room smelled sweet and clean. The blanket felt warm. The clock sang tick-tock like a lullaby.

Blue snuggled into the pillows. “I’m not alone,” Blue said.

Sunny nodded. “You are not alone. When we listen, we understand. When we understand, we can help. And when we help, we feel strong.”

Blue smiled with sleepy eyes. “Thank you, Sunny.”

Sunny packed the tiny tools and gave a gentle wave. “Call me if you need me,” Sunny said. “I will come with soft paws and open ears.”

Blue yawned. “I will.”

Sunny scampered to the door, heart full and bright. Sunny felt proud, not just for fixing, but for caring.

The guest room was quiet again, calm and kind, ready for any guest who might need a soft-paw friend.

The End.
