# Benny Bear and the Pantry Mystery

Benny Bear was a curious little cub with soft brown fur and bright, twinkling eyes. One sunny morning, he wandered into Mama Bear's pantry, sniffing the wonderful smells of honey, berries, and fresh bread.

"Mmm, everything smells so yummy!" Benny said, his little nose wiggling with excitement.

As he explored the cozy pantry with its wooden shelves and colorful jars, Benny heard a tiny voice.

"Help! Help me, please!"

Benny looked around, his ears perking up. "Who's there?" he called softly.

"Down here! By the flour jar!"

Benny looked down and gasped. There was a little mouse with gray fur and a pink nose, stuck under a heavy lid that had fallen from a jar.

"Oh no!" Benny exclaimed. "Are you hurt, little mouse?"

"I'm okay, but I can't get out," squeaked the mouse. "I was just looking for some crumbs to feed my babies. They're so hungry!"

Benny's first thought was what Mama Bear always said: "Mice don't belong in our pantry!" But as he looked at the scared little creature, his heart felt warm and fuzzy. The mouse had kind eyes and was only trying to take care of her family.

"What's your name?" Benny asked gently.

"I'm Rosie," said the mouse. "I know bears don't usually help mice, but—"

"That's not true!" Benny interrupted. "Good friends help each other, no matter how big or small they are!"

Benny carefully lifted the heavy lid with his strong paws. "There you go, Rosie! You're free!"

Rosie scurried out and looked up at Benny with grateful eyes. "Thank you so much! You're very kind."

"Why were you looking for food?" Benny asked curiously.

Rosie's whiskers drooped sadly. "The big storm last week washed away all our winter food. My three little babies are hungry, and I don't know what to do."

Benny's heart felt heavy. It wasn't fair that Rosie's family was hungry when the pantry was full of food. He thought about what would be right and just.

"Wait here!" Benny said with determination.

He gathered some fallen crumbs, a few berries that had rolled behind a jar, and a small piece of cheese that had been forgotten on a low shelf. He made a neat little pile.

"This food was already on the floor or forgotten," Benny explained. "It's perfect for you and your babies!"

Rosie's eyes sparkled with joy. "Oh, Benny! You're the kindest bear I've ever met!"

Just then, Mama Bear walked into the pantry. "Benny, what are you doing?"

Benny took a deep breath. "Mama, this is my friend Rosie. Her family lost their food in the storm. I gave her some crumbs and forgotten food to help her babies."

Mama Bear looked at the little mouse, then at her thoughtful cub. A warm smile spread across her face.

"Benny, you did exactly the right thing," Mama Bear said proudly. "Helping others in need is always important, no matter who they are."

From that day on, Rosie and her family became welcome visitors to the pantry. They helped keep it clean by eating fallen crumbs, and Benny had new friends to play with.

"I learned something important today," Benny told Mama Bear at bedtime. "Everyone deserves kindness and help, even if they're different from us."

"That's right, my sweet cub," Mama Bear said, giving him a cozy hug. "Acceptance and kindness make the world a better place for everyone."

The End.
