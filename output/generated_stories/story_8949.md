# Miko the Monkey Makes a Plan

The creative art room smelled like fresh paper and lemon soap. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Paint pots sat in a rainbow line. Brushes stood up like little soldiers. Stickers sparkled in a big jar.

Miko the monkey swung his tail happily. “Today I will make a giant jungle collage!” he chattered. His brown eyes were bright, and his heart felt brave. He loved to try big things.

Miss Lila, the art teacher, smiled. “That sounds wonderful, Miko. Remember, big projects need a smart plan.”

Miko nodded fast. “I can do it!” He bounced to the big table. He grabbed green paper, blue paint, glue, scissors, and a lot of glitter. He wanted everything at once.

Soon the table looked busy. Paper dropped to the floor. Glue made sticky puddles. Glitter sprinkled like tiny stars everywhere. Miko’s tail twitched. His chest felt tight. His hands buzzed.

“Oh no,” he whispered. “This is too much.”

His friend Tilly the turtle peeked up. “Miko, your face looks scrunchy. Are you okay?”

“I want a jungle collage,” Miko said, “but I don’t know where to start.”

Miss Lila walked over softly. Her shoes made a tap-tap sound. She spoke in a calm, warm voice. “Let’s take a pause breath.”

“What is a pause breath?” Miko asked.

“It is a tiny rest for your brain,” Miss Lila said. “Breathe in like you smell a flower.” She sniffed gently. “Breathe out like you blow a leaf.” She blew slowly.

Miko copied her. He breathed in. He smelled paper and lemon soap. He breathed out. His shoulders felt less tight. He did it again. In. Out. In. Out.

“There you go,” Miss Lila said. “Now, use monkey mind magic: stop, look, plan.”

Miko rubbed his chin. “Stop, look, plan,” he said. The words felt strong, like a little drumbeat.

“First,” said Miss Lila, “stop and sit.” Miko sat on the stool. His feet dangled. He smiled a little.

“Second, look. What do you really need right now?”

Miko looked at the mess. He looked at his picture in his head. He pictured big green leaves, a blue sky, and a smiling sun. He did not need glitter yet.

“I need just three things,” he said. “Green paper for leaves, blue paper for sky, and glue.”

Tilly clapped slowly. “Good plan.”

“Third,” Miss Lila said, “plan your steps.”

Miko held up his fingers. “Step one: clean the table. Step two: make a sky. Step three: make leaves.”

“That sounds like a strong strategy,” Miss Lila said. “A strategy is a smart way to do a big thing.”

Miko felt courage in his belly now, like a warm cocoa cup. He hummed a tiny tune.

He started with step one. He put extra things back on the shelf. The glitter went into its jar with a soft shhh. The scissors went in the basket. He wiped up the glue with a cloth. The table felt smooth.

“Step two,” he said. He glued a big blue paper on the top half. The glue smelled sweet and sticky. He pressed it flat. Swish, press, smooth.

“Step three,” he said. He cut green paper into big leaf shapes. Snip, snip, snip. Pieces fell like quiet rain. He glued the leaves around the bottom.

Tilly watched, smiling. “It looks like a real jungle!”

Miko added a bright yellow sun in the corner. He did not rush. He took tiny pause breaths when his tail started to wiggle too fast. In. Out. In. Out. His monkey mind felt steady.

He noticed his tummy rumble. “I think I need a snack break,” Miko said.

“That is self-care,” Miss Lila said. “Taking care of your body helps your brain.”

Miko washed his sticky hands with warm water. The water felt cozy. He dried them on a soft towel. He drank cool water and ate a banana from his lunch box. Yum. He felt fresh and ready.

“Time for details,” he said. He used a brown crayon to draw tree trunks. He added tiny red flowers. He put down a little bit of glitter on the sun. Just a sparkle, not a storm.

At last, Miko stepped back. The collage showed tall trees, big leaves, and a bright sun. It looked calm and happy, like a jungle morning.

Miss Lila clapped. “Miko, you used a clever strategy. You stopped, looked, and planned. You took pause breaths. You cared for your body. You finished your big idea.”

Miko’s cheeks felt warm with pride. “I did it,” he said. “When I feel busy inside, I can use my plan. Stop, look, plan.”

Tilly nodded. “And snack breaks!”

Everyone laughed. Miko hung the collage on the wall. Sunlight touched the glitter, and it twinkled hello.

Miko smiled at his friends. “I am a courageous monkey,” he said, “and I make a plan.”

The lesson was clear: when a big task feels too big, take a breath, make a simple plan, and care for yourself. Then do one small step at a time.

The End.
