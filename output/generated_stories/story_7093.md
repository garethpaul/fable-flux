# Brave Little Songs in the Guest Room

In a sunny house, there was a quiet guest room with soft blue walls and a big cozy bed. A small window let in warm light. A vase of flowers smelled sweet, like honey. A soft rug tickled toes. This room felt calm and safe.

In the window sat a singing bird named Lilu. Lilu had bright green feathers and a tiny golden beak. She loved to sing gentle songs. “Tweet-tee, tweet-tee,” she sang, like silver bells. Today, the house was buzzing. Many friends were visiting from faraway places. They all came to the guest room to rest and share stories.

First came Maya with a red scarf. “Hello,” she said softly. “I like your song.”

Then came Tomás with a big smile. “Hola,” he said. “I like your song too.”

Amina waved. “Salaam,” she said, her bracelets jingling like tiny chimes.

Kenji bowed. “Konnichiwa,” he said, and he grinned.

Lilu looked at all the friendly faces. Her heart beat fast. She wanted to sing a big song for everyone. But her tummy felt fluttery, like little wings. “What if my song is not good?” she whispered. “What if I am too small?”

Maya sat on the rug. “It is okay to feel shy,” she said. “We can help.”

Tomás nodded. “We are a team,” he said. “Team means we help each other.”

Amina listened to the breeze in the window. “We can share our songs too,” she said. “We can learn together.”

Kenji smiled. “We can clap softly,” he said. “Clap, clap. Like raindrops.”

Lilu took a tiny breath. The guest room was quiet and kind. She smelled the sweet flowers and felt the sun on her feathers. Still, her tummy fluttered. “I want to be brave,” she said. “But how do I do it?”

Maya pointed to her red scarf. “When I feel shy,” she said, “I hold my scarf and count to three. One, two, three.” She squeezed the scarf with a gentle hug.

Tomás touched his heart. “When I feel shy,” he said, “I think of my family. They say, ‘You can do it.’”

Amina tapped the rug. “When I feel shy,” she said, “I breathe in like smelling soup. Mmm. Then I breathe out slowly, like blowing on the soup so it cools.”

Kenji made a tiny bow. “When I feel shy,” he said, “I remember to try, even if it is hard.”

Lilu listened. These were good ideas. “I will try the soup breath,” she said. She breathed in: sniff, sniff. She breathed out: whooo. The fluttery feeling got smaller.

“Would you like a soft clap?” asked Kenji.

“Yes, please,” said Lilu.

Clap, clap. Clap, clap. The sound was gentle, like rain on leaves.

“I will start,” said Maya. She sang a simple tune from her home. “La-la, la-la.” It was warm and bright.

Tomás sang next. “Do-do, do-do.” His song danced like a happy ball.

Amina sang. “Na-na, na-na.” Her song was smooth, like a quiet river.

Kenji sang. “Ta-ta, ta-ta.” His song stepped like little feet.

Lilu felt her heart slow down. The guest room was full of different sounds, like a rainbow of music. Each song was special. Each song was loved. Lilu took another breath. “I am ready,” she said softly.

She opened her golden beak. “Tweet-tee, tweet-tee.” Her voice was small but clear, like a tiny bell. The sun felt warm. The flowers smelled sweet. The soft rug felt safe. Lilu kept going. “Tweet-tee, tweet-tee, twee.” She remembered her friends. She remembered the soup breath. She remembered to try.

When she finished, the room was quiet for a little moment. Then everyone smiled. Clap, clap. Clap, clap. This time, the claps were a little louder, like a happy pat on the back.

“That was beautiful,” Maya said.

“Muy bien!” said Tomás.

“Mashallah,” said Amina, her eyes shining.

“Sugoi!” said Kenji, clapping again.

Lilu felt warm and proud. “Thank you,” she chirped. “Your songs helped me. Your claps helped me. Your ideas helped me be brave.”

Maya nodded. “We learned from you too,” she said. “You showed courage.”

Tomás added, “Courage means trying, even when you feel shy.”

Amina smiled. “We all have different songs,” she said. “We can share and learn. That is education.”

Kenji said, “Our songs come from many places. That is our big world.”

Lilu hopped to the window. “I have a new idea,” she said. “Let’s make a team song.”

They all sang together. “La-la, do-do, na-na, ta-ta, tweet-tee!” Their voices mixed like colors in a painting. The guest room felt bright and full of joy. The air tasted like sweet tea and sounded like laughter.

At last, they rested on the soft rug. Lilu tucked her head under her wing. She felt calm and brave.

Today, Lilu learned: Courage is trying, even when you feel shy. Friends can help. We can learn from many places and many people. Together, we are strong, and our world is bright.

The End.
