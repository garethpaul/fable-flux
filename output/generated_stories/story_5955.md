# Pebble the Penguin and the Rooftop Picnic

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with a bright orange beak and soft, squeaky feet. One sunny afternoon, Pebble waddled up the stairs—step, step, step—to the building’s rooftop. The roof felt warm under Pebble’s little webbed feet, and the wind whooshed and whooed, tickling Pebble’s feathers. Pebble loved the rooftop because you could see the blue sky, fluffy clouds, and hear city birds chirp, “Tweet-tweet!”

Pebble was excited. It was picnic day with friends! On a comfy blanket, Pebble set out crunchy carrot sticks, cool cucumber slices, and a small bottle of water. Pebble hummed a happy tune, “Waddle-waddle, snacky-snack,” and clapped flippers with joy.

Soon, friends arrived. Lulu the ladybug buzzed softly, “Hi, Pebble!” Max the kitten padded over, purr-purr, his whiskers twitching. Benny the bunny hop-hop-hopped and wiggled his nose. Everyone smiled. The sun felt warm, and the breeze smelled like fresh air and a little bit like flowers.

Pebble said, “Welcome to our rooftop picnic! We can eat, drink water, and then play a game.”

Max blinked. “Can we play first? I want to chase my tail!” he meowed, swishing it in a circle.

Benny giggled. “I want to hop-hop-hop now!”

Lulu zipped and zapped in a tiny loop. “Let’s fly and dance right away!”

Pebble’s tummy made a little rumbly sound. “Grumble,” it said.

Pebble took a deep breath. The air felt cool in, warm out. Pebble remembered: Taking care of your body helps you play better. Pebble also remembered: It’s important to use your voice and listen to friends too.

Pebble smiled and spoke clearly. “Friends, I hear you want to play first. I want to play too. But my tummy is hungry, and my mouth is dry. I need a snack and water to have energy. Can we find a plan that works for all of us?”

Lulu landed on the blanket. “Hmm. What could we do?”

Max rolled onto his back, paws in the air. “Maybe a tiny play first?”

Benny did a slow hop. “Maybe a tiny snack first?”

Pebble nodded. “Let’s share ideas. I will go first.”

Pebble held up one flipper. “Idea one: We eat a big snack now, then play.”

Pebble held up the other flipper. “Idea two: We play a long time first, then eat.”

Pebble tapped the blanket gently. “Idea three: We take turns. We do a little eating and a little playing.”

Everyone thought. The wind brushed their faces like a soft hand. Far below, a car honked, “Beep!” and a kite fluttered, “Flap-flap,” above them.

Max flicked his tail. “I like idea three. I can play a little, then I will be ready to eat.”

Benny’s ears wiggled. “I like idea three too! Hop, crunch, hop!”

Lulu buzzed. “Idea three sounds fair. We will all get what we need.”

Pebble felt proud and calm. Pebble had used words to share needs. Pebble had listened too. That was good self-advocacy and good friendship.

“Great!” Pebble chirped. “Here is our plan. First, we each take three sips of water.” Pebble held up the bottle. The water went, “Glug, glug, glug.” It felt cool and fresh on Pebble’s tongue. “Ahh!”

Max lapped a sip from a little cup. “Sip-sip-sip.” Benny drank, “Slurp-slurp.” Lulu took tiny droplet tastes, “Tink-tink.”

“Next,” said Pebble, “we crunch two carrot sticks. Then we play tail-chase, hop-hop, and loop-de-loop for five minutes. Then we come back for cucumber slices and a short rest. After that, we play again!”

Max smiled wide. “That plan sounds balanced.” He tested the word. “Bal-anced!”

Benny clapped paws. “Balanced means fair!”

Lulu twirled. “Balanced means we listen and take care of our bodies.”

Pebble grinned. “Yes! It also means we make a plan together.”

They all crunched. The carrots were crisp: “Crunch! Crunch!” The noise made them giggle. Pebble felt energy wake up, like a little sun inside. The cucumber smelled fresh and watery, like rain on grass.

“Ready?” Pebble asked.

“Ready!” the friends cheered.

They played. Max chased his tail, round and round, his bell collar jingling, “Jingle-jingle.” Benny hop-scotched over chalk squares, “Thump-thump.” Lulu zoomed in loops, tiny wings, “Bzzzz!” Pebble waddled fast and flapped, “Swoosh!”

After a few minutes, Pebble waved. “Pause time!” Pebble used a kind voice. “Let’s check our bodies.”

Max touched his chest. “My heart goes thump-thump. I need a sip.”

Benny patted his tummy. “I feel hungry for more cucumber.”

Lulu brushed her wings. “I feel a little warm. I need shade.”

Pebble nodded. “Thank you for sharing your needs.” Pebble poured water, passed cucumber slices, and scooted the blanket to a shady corner by a big potted plant. The plant leaves rustled, “Shhh.”

They rested. The city hummed, “Mmm,” around them. A cloud shaped like a turtle drifted by. Pebble felt calm and happy. The plan was working.

“Friends,” Pebble said softly, “what did we learn?”

Max lifted a paw. “We can talk and listen to make a plan.”

Benny added, “We can say what we need.”

Lulu chimed, “Water and snacks help our bodies feel strong.”

Pebble nodded. “That is negotiation. We share ideas, we listen, and we choose a fair plan. That is self-advocacy too. We use our voices kindly.”

They all smiled and did a gentle group hug. Pebble’s feathers felt soft, Max’s fur was warm, Benny’s ears were velvety, and Lulu’s tiny feet tickled.

“Now,” Pebble said, “shall we play again?”

“Yes!” they cheered.

They followed their balanced plan: a little play, a little rest, a little water, and a little snack. The sun began to dip, turning the sky peach and pink. The rooftop felt cozy and safe. Pebble’s heart felt full.

Pebble looked at friends and said, “We made a plan together. We took care of our bodies. We were kind and fair. That is the penguin way!”

Everyone laughed, “Waddle-wonderful!”

The End.
