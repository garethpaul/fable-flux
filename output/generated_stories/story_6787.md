# Melody's Big Soccer Dream

Melody was a bright yellow canary who loved to sing. She lived in a cozy nest in an oak tree in the sunny backyard. Every morning, she would chirp beautiful songs that made everyone smile.

One warm day, Melody heard exciting sounds from the yard below. "Kick! Pass! Goal!" shouted happy voices. She fluttered down to see what was happening.

Three children were playing soccer on the green grass. There was Sam from next door, Maria who spoke Spanish, and Kai who had just moved from Japan. They were laughing and running with a black and white ball.

"That looks so fun!" Melody chirped to herself. "I wish I could play too!"

But when Melody tried to kick the ball with her tiny bird feet, she couldn't move it at all. The ball was much too big and heavy for her small body.

"Tweet, tweet!" she called sadly. "I'm too little to play soccer like the children."

Melody felt left out. She perched on a fence post and watched the children play. Sam kicked the ball to Maria. Maria passed it to Kai. They were all working together as a team.

"I have an idea!" chirped Melody suddenly. She had been thinking hard about how to solve her problem.

Melody flew to each child and sang a special song. "Tweet-tweet-chirp! Follow me! Tweet-tweet-chirp! You'll see!"

The curious children followed Melody to a corner of the yard. There, she had gathered small pebbles, acorns, and flower petals.

"Look!" sang Melody. "We can make a tiny soccer field for me! And you can teach me the rules!"

Sam clapped his hands. "What a great idea!"

Maria smiled. "¡Sí! We can be coaches!"

Kai nodded excitedly. "In Japan, we call soccer 'football.' I can teach you both names!"

Working together, they built a mini soccer field. They used twigs for goal posts and made a tiny ball from rolled-up leaves. Melody practiced dribbling the leaf-ball with her beak.

"In soccer, we work as a team," explained Sam. "Everyone helps everyone."

"And people all over the world play soccer," added Maria. "It brings us together!"

"When you want something, you should speak up and ask for help," said Kai wisely.

Melody learned to "kick" by pushing the leaf-ball with her beak. She learned to "pass" by dropping it near her friends' feet. Soon she was scoring tiny goals!

"Tweet! I did it!" sang Melody happily. "Thank you for helping me!"

The children cheered. They had learned something too - that everyone can play sports in their own special way, no matter how different they are.

From that day on, Melody joined the children's soccer games. She would fly above the field, calling out encouragement and singing victory songs. She even learned to say "goal" in Spanish ("¡gol!") and Japanese ("gooru!").

"Sports are for everyone," Melody sang to the other birds in the neighborhood. "When we work together and help each other, we can all have fun and learn new things!"

And whenever new children moved to the neighborhood, Melody would sing her welcoming song and invite them to play. Because she learned that speaking up for what you want, and including others, makes everything more fun.

The sunny backyard became a place where children from all over the world could come together, play sports, and be friends.

The End.
