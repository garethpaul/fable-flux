# Ruby's Big Sorry

Ruby the rabbit hopped down the wooden stairs into Grandma's busy basement. *Thump, thump, thump* went her fluffy feet. The basement smelled like old books and fresh laundry.

"Wow!" Ruby gasped. The basement was full of amazing things! Colorful boxes lined the walls. Beautiful fabrics hung from strings. Strange instruments sat on shelves.

Ruby's nose twitched with excitement. She had never seen so many interesting things before!

In one corner, she spotted a shiny drum with pretty patterns. Ruby had seen drums like this in her picture books about faraway places. Her paws itched to touch it.

"I'll just look," Ruby whispered to herself.

But looking turned into touching. And touching turned into *BANG!*

The drum fell over with a loud crash! Beautiful beads scattered everywhere like tiny rainbows across the floor.

"Oh no!" Ruby's heart pounded fast. Her ears drooped down. She had broken something special.

Footsteps hurried down the stairs. Grandma appeared with her friend Mrs. Chen, who had kind eyes and gray hair.

"What happened here?" Grandma asked gently.

Ruby wanted to hide behind a box. Her whiskers trembled. But she remembered what Mama always said: "Brave bunnies tell the truth."

Ruby took a deep breath. "I'm sorry, Grandma. I touched the pretty drum and it fell down. I broke it." Her voice was very small.

Mrs. Chen knelt down beside the scattered beads. Ruby expected her to be angry. Instead, Mrs. Chen smiled.

"This drum is from my home country," Mrs. Chen said softly. "My grandmother gave it to me when I was little, just like you."

Ruby's eyes filled with tears. "I'm so, so sorry, Mrs. Chen. I didn't mean to break your special drum."

Mrs. Chen picked up Ruby's small paws. "I can see you're truly sorry, little one. That takes courage to admit."

"Can we fix it?" Ruby asked hopefully.

"Yes!" Mrs. Chen's eyes sparkled. "In my culture, we believe broken things can become even more beautiful when we fix them together."

Grandma brought a special glue that dried clear. Mrs. Chen showed Ruby how to carefully place each bead back in its spot. Ruby's small paws worked very gently.

"Tell me about your drum," Ruby said as they worked.

Mrs. Chen smiled. "This drum played at celebrations in my village. We used it for happy dances and special songs."

"What kind of songs?" Ruby asked, her ears perking up.

Mrs. Chen hummed a gentle melody. It sounded like wind chimes and flowing water. Ruby had never heard anything so beautiful.

When they finished fixing the drum, it looked almost like new. The tiny cracks made pretty patterns, like silver rivers.

"It's even more special now," Ruby said with wonder.

"Yes," Mrs. Chen agreed. "And you helped make it that way. Your apology and hard work turned a mistake into something beautiful."

Ruby hugged Mrs. Chen's knees. "Thank you for teaching me about your drum and your songs. And thank you for forgiving me."

"Thank you for being brave enough to say sorry," Mrs. Chen replied.

That day, Ruby learned that saying sorry takes courage. She learned that people from different places have beautiful traditions. And she learned that when we work together to fix our mistakes, we can create something even more wonderful.

From then on, Ruby always asked before touching special things. And sometimes, Mrs. Chen would visit and teach Ruby songs from her faraway home.

The End.
