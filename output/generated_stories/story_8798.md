# Milo Mouse and the Busy Garden Shed

Milo was a kind little mouse who lived near a sunny garden. Every morning, he stretched his tiny paws, twitched his whiskers, and peeked into the garden shed. The shed smelled like fresh soil and wood. Sunbeams made dust sparkle in the air like tiny stars.

One bright day, Milo squeaked, “Good morning, shed!” He loved to visit and help. Inside, garden tools rested on a big wooden wall. A watering can sat by the door. A bag of seeds rustled softly when the breeze slipped in.

Milo took a deep breath. He smelled green leaves and damp earth. “Today I will be helpful,” he said. “I will enjoy the outdoors and move my body. I will be honest and careful.”

Just then, Ella the robin hopped onto the window ledge. “Chirp! Hello, Milo,” she sang. “Will you come outside and help me? The carrot patch is thirsty.”

“Yes!” said Milo. “Outdoor time is good for me. Let’s go move and learn.”

Milo carried the small watering can with both paws. It was cool and heavy. “Splash, splash,” went the water as he stepped outside. The sun warmed his fur, and the grass tickled his toes. Milo felt proud. He was helping in the fresh air, and his body was busy and strong.

At the carrot patch, Ella pointed with her beak. “Chirp! Not too much water. Just enough.”

Milo nodded. “I will be gentle and respectful.” He tipped the can. “Drip, drip, drip.” The little carrot tops nodded like they were saying, “Thank you!”

When the watering was done, Milo heard a soft clatter from the shed. “Clink, clank.”

Milo ran back, paws patter-pattering. Inside, he saw a shiny trowel on the floor. It had fallen from its hook. Beside it sat a tiny wooden box with a lid. Milo blinked. He had never seen the box before.

He picked up the trowel. “This tool belongs on the wall,” he said. “I will be responsible.” He reached up high, stood on his tippy toes, and hung the trowel carefully. “Click.”

Then Milo looked at the wooden box. “Hmm,” he whispered. “Is this for me? Or does it belong to someone else?” He touched the lid. It felt smooth and warm from the sun.

Ella fluttered in. “Chirp! What is it?”

“I don’t know,” said Milo. “I want to look inside. But I did not ask. I want to be honest and respect others.”

Milo took a deep breath. He stepped back from the box. “I will not open it without asking. It might be special.”

Just then, Mr. Green, the gardener, opened the shed door. He wore muddy boots and a kind smile. “Hello, little friends,” he said. “What are you doing today?”

Milo stood tall. “We watered the carrots. We moved our bodies and helped outside. And I put the trowel back. But I found this box. I did not open it. Does it belong to you?”

Mr. Green’s eyes crinkled. “Thank you for telling me the truth, Milo. Yes, this is my seed box.” He knelt down. “You were honest, and you respected my things. That is very kind.”

Milo’s whiskers wiggled. “I wanted to be good and responsible.”

“You are,” said Mr. Green. He opened the box and showed them tiny round seeds. They smelled nutty and dry. “Would you like to help me plant these? It’s a wonderful outdoor activity. It helps our bodies grow strong and our hearts feel happy.”

“Yes, please!” chirped Ella.

“Yes!” squeaked Milo. “I love to be outside!”

They went to a sunny spot. The soil was warm and soft. Mr. Green gave Milo a little spade. “Dig small holes,” he said. “Not too deep.”

Milo dug: “scoop, scoop.” The earth crumbled gently. Ella sang a planting song: “Chirp-chirp, cheep-cheep, seeds down deep.” Milo giggled.

Mr. Green handed Milo some seeds. “These are for radishes.”

Milo held them carefully. “I will be gentle,” he said. He kept his paws steady and dropped one seed into each hole. “Plink. Plink. Plink.” He covered them with soil. “Pat, pat, pat.”

They watered the new row. “Drip, drip,” went the can. The sun warmed Milo’s back. A breeze brushed his ears. He felt calm and proud. He had worked outside. He had moved and stretched. He had been honest and respectful.

When they were done, Mr. Green said, “Thank you, helpers. You were careful with the tools, and you asked before touching the box. That shows responsibility.”

Milo smiled. “Being outside makes me happy. I can help, I can learn, and I can be honest.”

Ella fluttered her wings. “Chirp! And we can come back each day to check the seeds. We will walk, water, and watch them grow!”

Milo nodded. “A little outdoor activity every day is good for our bodies. It is also good for the garden.”

They put the tools away. Milo hung the spade and set the watering can by the door. “Everything in its place,” he said. “That is respectful and safe.”

Mr. Green closed the seed box and tucked it on a shelf. “And thank you for not opening this without asking. You showed honesty and respect.”

Milo’s heart felt warm. “Today I learned to enjoy the outdoors, to tell the truth, and to care for others’ things.”

Ella sang a happy note. “Chirp! Chirp!”

Milo waved his paw. “Good night, garden shed. See you tomorrow for another outdoor adventure.”

The shed glowed in the soft evening light. The garden sighed with a gentle hush. Milo scampered home, tired and smiling, ready for a cozy rest.

The lesson was clear: Play and help outside every day, be honest, and respect others’ things. That is how small friends grow big hearts.

The End.
