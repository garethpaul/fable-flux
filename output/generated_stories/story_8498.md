# The Cozy Bedroom Art Parade

In a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls, a little lamp glowed like a warm moon. The bed had a fluffy cloud blanket, and the rug felt like a gentle marshmallow underfoot. A determined rabbit named Remy sat in the middle of the rug with a basket of crayons, a stack of paper, and a bright idea.

“I will make the best art poster all by myself,” Remy said, twitching his whiskers. He tapped his paw and nodded. “I can do it. I am determined.”

Remy pulled out a red crayon. Scritch, scritch. He drew a big, happy sun. He used yellow. Swish, swish. He added shiny rays. He picked blue. Swoop, swoop. He drew a friendly sky. Remy’s nose wiggled with the sweet smell of crayons and paper.

Just then, there was a tiny knock at the bedroom door. Knock, knock.

“Come in,” Remy called.

The door creaked open. In stepped Mia Mouse with sparkly stickers. Behind her came Gus Goose with a cup of safe, washable paint. Tali Turtle peeked in, carrying soft brushes like tiny feathers.

“Hi, Remy!” said Mia. “Can we make art with you?”

Remy’s ears stood tall. “I want to finish my poster by myself,” he said. “I want it to be perfect.”

Mia’s whiskers drooped a little. Gus’s big feet shuffled on the rug. Tali tucked her head for a moment, then peeked out again.

Remy felt a small squeeze in his chest. He looked at his friends. He looked at his poster. He looked at the cozy bedroom. The lamp hummed softly. The air smelled like vanilla and crayons. The room felt warm and full of friendship.

“I am determined,” Remy said softly. “But I can be determined and kind. Maybe we can work together.”

Mia’s eyes sparkled. “We can make a team!”

Gus flapped his wings gently. “Honk-yes! I can paint smooth blue swirls.”

Tali smiled slow and sweet. “I can make gentle lines.”

Remy took a deep breath. “Okay. We will make the art poster together. We will use cooperation. That means we work as a team and listen.”

“Co-op-er-a-tion,” Mia repeated, sounding it out. “I like that.”

Remy pointed to the big sun. “I drew the sun. Mia, you can place stickers. Gus, you can paint the sky. Tali, you can brush the grass. And we will all share.”

Mia bounced. “I have star stickers and heart stickers!”

“Let’s try hearts near the sun,” Remy said. “But we will ask each other first. We respect each other’s ideas.”

Mia nodded. “Respect means we listen and we are kind.”

Gus dipped a brush in blue. Swish, swoosh. “Is it okay if I paint a calm, blue wave under the sun?”

Remy smiled. “Yes, Gus. That looks peaceful.”

Tali dipped her brush in green paint. Stroke, stroke. “Is it okay if I make the grass soft and low?”

“Great idea,” said Remy. “Thank you for asking.”

As they worked, the bedroom filled with happy sounds. Scritch of crayons. Swish of brushes. Peel of stickers. Little giggles. The lamp made a cozy glow on the paper. Remy’s ears relaxed. His heart felt light.

Mia held up a star sticker. “Can I put a star near the sun?”

Remy thought. “The sun is bright. A star might get lost. What if we put the star on the blue sky?”

Mia nodded. “Yes! Thank you for helping me choose.”

Remy added orange to the sun. “I like your sticker sparkle,” he said.

Mia beamed. “Thank you for your kind words.”

Gus painted a smooth, windy line. “Oops,” he said softly. “My paint touched the heart sticker.”

Remy looked. The heart had a tiny blue smudge. He felt a pinch of worry. He took another breath.

“It is okay,” Remy said. “Art can have surprises. We can fix it. Or we can make it part of the picture.”

Tali dipped her brush in white. Dab, dab. “I can make a small cloud to hug the heart.”

They all watched. The cloud looked soft and sweet. The smudge turned into a cozy shadow. Everyone smiled.

Remy’s nose wiggled. “Thank you, Tali. That was a kind help.”

Tali nodded. “We are a team.”

They kept going. Mia made a line of stars like a parade. Gus painted a smooth river under the grass. Tali brushed tiny flowers with careful taps. Remy drew a little rabbit family holding paws. The room smelled like clean paint, crayons, and a hint of cookies from the kitchen.

When the poster was done, they all sat back on the marshmallowy rug. The paper shone with color and care. It looked friendly. It looked like them.

Remy touched the edge of the poster with his paw. “This is the best art poster,” he said, “because we made it together.”

Mia clapped her tiny hands. Gus flapped softly. Tali smiled her slow smile.

Remy looked at his friends. “I learned something,” he said. “Being determined is good. But being determined to work together is even better. Cooperation helps us include everyone and respect each other.”

Mia added, “When we include friends, our art gets bigger.”

Gus said, “When we respect ideas, we feel safe.”

Tali said, “When we work together, problems become new plans.”

They hung the poster on the cozy bedroom wall. The lamp light kissed the colors. The poster glowed with hearts, stars, clouds, and friends.

Remy’s ears wiggled. “Teamwork makes our art shine.”

Everyone nodded. They shared warm hugs. The bedroom felt extra cozy now, like a nest full of care.

The End.
