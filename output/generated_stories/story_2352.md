# Polly Plans a Rooftop Parade

Polly was a creative parrot who loved colors, sounds, and making new things. She lived on a sunny rooftop with potted flowers, flapping laundry, and a blue sky that looked close enough to touch. The wind smelled like warm bread from the bakery below. Pigeons cooed, bees hummed, and a little bell on the clothesline went ting-a-ling in the breeze.

One morning, Polly puffed her green feathers and said, “Goal! Today I will make a rooftop parade. A kind parade with music, colors, and smiles!” Her eyes shone like tiny stars. She hopped to the edge and called, “Friends, let’s plan together!”

From the water tank, a shy pigeon named Pip peeped, “Parade?”  
From the chimney, a tabby cat named Miso stretched and purred, “Me-ow, music?”  
From a flower pot, Busy Bee buzzed, “Bzz-zz, colors!”

Polly nodded. “Yes! We will have a soft drum, a ribbon dance, and a happy song. Our goal is to share joy. But we must use peaceful words and listen to each other. Teamwork!”

They all came close. Polly spread a blue scarf like a tiny stage. “Step one,” she said gently. “We plan with calm voices. Step two, we make a list. Step three, we practice. Step four, we parade!”

Pip fluffed his neck. “I can coo-coo a beat.”  
Miso swished her tail. “I can carry a bell.”  
Busy Bee buzzed circles. “I can paint petals with flower dust!”

Polly clapped her wings. “Great ideas. Let’s talk and take turns.”

She drew pictures in chalk on a warm tile: a drum, a bell, a ribbon, a flower. The chalk felt dusty and cool under her claw. “Pip, you start the beat.” Pip cooed, “Coo, coo—coo,” like soft raindrops. The sound made the air feel cozy.

Then Miso rang the bell, but it was too loud. DING-DING! Busy Bee shot up. “Bzz! Too big, too big!”

Miso’s ears went low. “I only wanted to help.”

Polly took a slow breath. The breeze brushed her cheek like a kind hand. “Let’s use peaceful words. Busy Bee, please try softer words.”

Busy Bee hovered lower. “I feel surprised, Miso. The bell is loud for my tiny ears. Can we make it softer, please?”

Miso lifted her head. “Thank you for telling me kindly. I can wrap the bell with a leaf.”

Together, they found a big green leaf. Polly tapped it gently around the bell. Miso tried again: ding-a-ling, soft and sweet. Pip cooed with it, and the sounds slid together like warm honey.

“Teamwork feels good,” Polly said, swaying. Her feathers tickled in the wind.

Next, Busy Bee spread golden dust across the scarf, making shapes like suns and flowers. The dust sparkled. Pip waddled closer, but slipped. “Oh!” He landed on his bottom, and his coo turned into a tiny squeak. Tears filled his bright eyes.

Polly’s heart felt tight. She slid near and spoke softly. “Pip, are you hurt?”

Pip sniffed. “My tail feels ouch.”

Miso brought a dry cloth with her teeth. Busy Bee fanned cool air with her wings. Polly said, “We care for each other. That is compassion.” She dabbed Pip’s tail with the cloth. “Better?”

Pip nodded. “Better. Thank you.” He managed a little smile.

Polly looked at the sparkly floor. “We need a safe path. Let’s talk about what we need.” She pointed to her list. “Goal: a safe, happy parade. What can we change?”

Busy Bee raised a tiny leg. “I can keep the glitter on the scarf only, not the floor.”  
Miso added, “I can lick my paw and smooth the path.”  
Pip said, “I can walk slowly and watch my steps.”

Polly beamed. “Good peaceful plan.”

They practiced again. Pip cooed a gentle beat. Miso rang the soft bell. Busy Bee painted the scarf with careful swirls. Polly danced with a long ribbon made from an old red sock. The ribbon whispered in the wind—swish, swish—like a shy creek. The sun warmed their backs, and the smell of mint from a nearby plant made everyone feel calm.

Then they heard a sharp squawk from a corner. A grumpy crow named Crag watched with a frown. “Parades are silly,” he cawed. “You’re wasting time.”

Busy Bee trembled. Pip hid behind a pot. Miso’s tail puffed up.

Polly stood tall. She spoke in a clear, kind voice. “Hello, Crag. We are making a parade to share joy. We use peaceful words here. Would you like to tell us how you feel, using kind words?”

Crag blinked. “I… I feel left out,” he muttered. “No one asked me.”

Polly’s eyes softened. “Thank you for telling us. I’m sorry you felt left out. We want you here. Would you like a part?”

Crag shuffled. “I can tap-tap with my beak on the pipe.”

“Great idea,” said Polly. “Let’s add it to our plan.”

They practiced one more time: coo-coo, ding-a-ling, tap-tap, swish-swish, and a soft bzz. The sounds blended like a friendly hug.

At last, Polly chirped, “Parade time!”

They marched around the rooftop. The wind carried their music down to the street. A baker waved. A child giggled and pointed up. The bell chimed, the ribbon fluttered, the beak tapped, the coo rolled, and the bee’s sparkle shone like morning dew.

When they finished, everyone cheered. Pip flapped happily. Miso rubbed her head against Polly. Busy Bee buzzed a tiny song. Crag’s frown was gone. He looked proud.

Polly gathered them close. “Today we met our goal because we used peaceful communication. We shared feelings with kind words. We listened. We cared for each other. That is compassion. That is teamwork.”

Crag nodded. “I felt grumpy. Now I feel glad.”

Polly smiled. “When we talk kindly, we make space for every voice.”

They watched the sun paint the sky pink and gold. The rooftop felt warm and safe. The bell gave one last gentle ting-a-ling, like a goodnight kiss.

Polly whispered, “New goal tomorrow: more kindness.”

Everyone agreed.

The End.
