# Rocco’s Tune of Tidying

Rocco was a musical raccoon with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a playful living room filled with pillows, crayons, toy blocks, and a little drum set. Sunlight made warm squares on the carpet. The air smelled like clean laundry and a hint of fruity juice. When Rocco tapped his drum, the room hummed with happy echoes.

Every morning, Rocco sang, “Tap-tap-tee, tidy with me!” But today, he felt bouncy and busy. He wanted to make music, not clean up. He shook his maracas—shaka-shaka—then set them down on the couch. He jangled his tiny cymbals—ting-ting!—and left them under a pillow. He plucked his ukulele—plink-plonk—then hurried off to try the drum—boom-boom!

Soon the playful living room did not feel so playful. Blocks made a wobbly path. Crayons rolled like tiny logs. A stuffed bunny lay on the drum stool. Rocco stepped and slipped—whoa! He caught himself on a pillow and giggled, but his tummy felt twisty.

“Rocco,” said Mama Raccoon, her voice warm like cocoa, “I hear big sounds. Is your heart making music or is the mess playing the song?”

Rocco’s ears perked up. “I want to play a new song, Mama! A big, big song.”

Mama smiled. “Big songs need tidy places. That is being responsible. Responsibility means taking care of your things and your space.”

Rocco wrinkled his nose. “But cleaning is slow.”

“Cleaning can be music,” Mama said. She tapped a gentle beat on the coffee table—tap, tap, ti-tap. “Try a tidy tune.”

Rocco blinked. “A tidy tune?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Make a song while you put things back.”

Rocco’s eyes lit up like little stars. He stood tall. “I can do that!”

He took a deep breath. He felt the soft rug under his toes. He smelled the cozy room. He heard the clock tick-tick, like a tiny metronome. Rocco began to sing, his voice small and bright.

“Pick up, put back,
tap-tap-tee!
Toys in baskets,
neat as can be!”

He tapped the drum—boom-boom—then slid it gently to the corner. He hummed as he found the drum sticks and placed them in a little cup. He giggled when the sticks clicked together—click!

He scooped up crayons—red, blue, yellow—rolling them into their box with a soft rattle-rattle. “Goodnight, crayons,” he said. “See you later!”

He lifted the stuffed bunny from the stool and gave it a hug. “Thanks for listening,” he whispered. He put Bunny on the couch and fluffed a pillow. The pillow smelled like sunshine. It whooshed—fwump—when it landed.

Rocco tapped his maracas—shaka-shaka—then tucked them into their basket. “Rest now, maracas. We will dance after snack.”

He made up words as he moved, his voice growing strong. “I can do it, yes, I can! I’m a tidy-tune raccoon!”

Mama watched, her eyes warm. “You are being very responsible, Rocco.”

Rocco felt big inside. His tail swished with pride. The room began to open up. The floor looked clear. The light felt brighter.

Just then, there was a knock at the door. Knock-knock!

Rocco peeked. It was his friend, Tilly Turtle, with a small ukulele sticker on her shell. “Hi, Rocco,” Tilly said softly. “Can we play music?”

Rocco looked around. “Yes! But first, we finish our tidy tune.” He smiled. “We can do it together.”

Tilly looked unsure. “I am slow,” she said in a quiet voice.

Rocco nodded kindly. “That’s okay. We can go slow. Slow is still good.” He hummed a gentle rhythm for Tilly—hum-hum, hum-hum. “We all have our own beat.”

Tilly smiled a little. “Okay.”

Together they moved to the music. Rocco rolled the blocks into a bin—clack, clack. Tilly carried two at a time—clack…clack. Rocco wiped the little table with a soft cloth—swish, swish. Tilly folded a tiny blanket—fold…pat. They listened to each other’s sounds and matched them like a friendly duet.

When they were done, the playful living room felt happy again. The carpet looked like a soft, green field. The drum gleamed in its corner. The baskets sat like cozy homes for toys.

Rocco took a proud breath. “We did it!” he cheered.

Tilly nodded. “We were responsible,” she said, tasting the new word.

Mama clapped lightly—pat-pat-pat. “You cared for your things, you cared for your space, and you cared for each other. That is responsibility. And that shows respect and tolerance too—everyone can help in their own way and at their own pace.”

Rocco lifted his ukulele. “Now our big song!” He strummed—plink-plonk—and felt brave. Tilly tapped a tiny beat on the table—tip-tip. Their music filled the room like warm light.

Rocco sang, “When we tidy, we feel strong. When we care, we sing our song.” His heart felt tall. He knew he could try new things and do them well. He felt good about himself.

After the song, Rocco put the ukulele back on its hook. Tilly set the little drum sticks back in the cup. Rocco smiled. “Look, we kept being responsible.”

Tilly grinned. “Yes! And now the room is ready for more fun.”

Rocco hugged Mama. “I like our tidy tune.”

Mama hugged back. “I’m proud of you. You made music, you were responsible, and you were a kind friend.”

Rocco swished his tail. “Tap-tap-tee, tidy with me!” he sang one more time. He felt calm, safe, and happy in the playful living room.

The clear lesson rang like a bell in Rocco’s heart: when we take care of our things and our space, we can make our best music, feel proud, and play together.

The End.
