# Bubbles the Brave in the Laundry Room

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to wiggle, splash, and sing. One sunny morning, a big wave whooshed Bubbles through a shiny round door—right into a laundry room!

“Whoooa!” Bubbles squeaked. “This room smells like warm soap and soft clouds!” The air was sweet and clean. The floor felt cool and smooth. Machines hummed and whirred. A dryer thumped-thump, thump-thump, like a friendly drum. A washer swooshed, swoosh-swish, like the ocean at night.

Bubbles looked around with wide eyes. “Hello?” he called. “Is anyone here?”

A sock popped from a basket. “Boing!” It landed on Bubbles’s nose.

“Hee-hee!” Bubbles giggled. “Hello, little sock.”

From behind a stack of towels came a tiny voice. “Help! Help!” It was a small, shy glove. It peeked out with a little wave. “I am lost,” the glove whispered. “My friend, the other glove, is missing. I am scared.”

Bubbles felt his heart do a flutter-flip. He was in a strange room with big, loud machines. He was a dolphin without the sea. He wanted to hide in the basket of soft, warm towels. But he looked at the little glove, shaking like a leaf.

Bubbles took a slow, brave breath. In, out. In, out. “I can help,” he said. “I can be brave. Let’s set a goal! Goal number one: find your friend. Goal number two: do it safely. Goal number three: don’t give up.”

The glove sniffled. “Okay,” it said. “I believe in you.”

“Let’s move, let’s groove!” Bubbles sang, flipping his tail. He slid over the cool floor, swish-swish. He peeked under a chair. “Not here,” he said. He wiggled behind a basket. “Not here,” he hummed.

The machines hummed louder. The washer gurgled. The dryer glowed warm like a sunshine belly. Bubbles felt a little worry bubble up. His fins trembled. He whispered, “Brave means trying, even when I feel wobbly.”

He gave himself a little pep song. “Be brave, be bright, use soft dolphin light!” He made a silly dance with little tail taps—tap, tap, tap—and a flip—whoop! The glove giggled.

“Good movement!” said the sock on his nose. “Can I come too?”

“Yes!” said Bubbles. “We can all help. We can move and look with our eyes, ears, and noses.” He sniffed. “Mmm, warm and soapy.”

They checked the shelf. Bubbles stretched long and tall. Reach, reach. “I see a clothespin parade,” he said. “But no glove.”

They listened near the dryer. Thump-thump, thump-thump. Bubbles pressed his ear. “Hello, drum-belly,” he said. “Have you seen a little glove?” The dryer gave a warm, windy sigh. Whoooosh.

Bubbles felt the warm air brush his cheeks. It smelled like sunshine on a blanket. He had an idea. “Let’s make a brave plan! We will search in three places: under, behind, and inside.”

The glove’s tiny eyes went round. “Inside?” it whispered.

“We will do it safely,” said Bubbles. “We ask first.” He tapped the button. The dryer slowed and stopped. The door clicked. Click!

Bubbles took another slow breath. “I can do hard things,” he said. “One small step.” He opened the door. Creak. Warm air puffed out. Whooosh.

He peeked in. “Hello?” he called softly.

From deep in the fluffy tumbling cave came a tiny voice. “I’m here! I’m here!” Out rolled a little glove, soft and shy, smelling like clean rain. It tumbled into Bubbles’s fins.

“You found me!” said the little glove. “I was scared. It was dark and bumpy.”

Bubbles smiled big. “We used our brave plan. We set our goal. We moved our bodies and our hearts.”

The two gloves hugged. “Thank you,” they said together. “You were very brave.”

Bubbles felt warm and proud, like sunlight in his chest. “When I feel scared, I can breathe, make a plan, and take small steps,” he said.

“Let’s celebrate!” cheered the sock.

Bubbles wiggled. The gloves clapped. The sock bounced. They made a laundry room dance. Swish, tap, clap! Swish, tap, clap! Bubbles led a brave dance parade:
- Big stretch up high! Reach for the shelf.
- Little scoot down low! Peek under the chair.
- Gentle spin around! Check behind the basket.
- Soft stomp-stomp! Feel your feet.
- Dolphin flip—whoop! Smile and shout, “I can!”

They laughed and sang. The washer hummed a happy song. The dryer made warm wind that tickled their faces. The towels were a soft mountain to climb. The room felt friendly now.

A door across the room opened with a quiet creak. Bubbles saw a round, shiny window with blue water beyond. “Home!” he said. “It’s time to go.”

The gloves waved. The sock bowed. “Thank you, Bubbles the Brave,” they said.

Bubbles popped his nose up high. “Remember our brave steps,” he said. “Breathe. Make a goal. Move and try. Ask for help. Don’t give up.”

He slid to the round door. He looked back and smiled. “I was scared, and I tried. That is bravery.”

Then Bubbles splashed through the door. Whooosh! Cool blue water hugged him. He swam with strong, happy strokes. Swish-swish-swoosh!

As he swam, he sang, “Be brave, be kind, make goals in your mind! Move your body, do your best, and take a little rest.”

The End.
