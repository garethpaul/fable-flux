# Bear Cub's Quiet Breath and Big Drum

Boom-boom, tap-tap! The musical practice room was full of sounds. Shiny drums lined the wall. There were soft bells, smooth wooden blocks, and a bright red shaker that went shake-shake. A friendly bear cub named Bruno stood in the middle, his small paws twitching with excitement.

“I want to play all the sounds!” Bruno said. His brown fur puffed as he bounced. “I want to play now!”

In came Ms. Lark, the gentle music teacher with warm eyes. She smiled. “Hello, Bruno. Today, our friends will share songs from home. We will listen. We will learn. We will breathe.”

Bruno nodded fast. He picked up a big drum. Boom! The drum sounded deep and round like a giant heartbeat. But Bruno’s paws kept going—boom-boom-boom-BOOM! The sound grew too loud. His ears felt tight. His tummy felt wiggly.

“I can’t stop,” Bruno said, frowning. “I feel too fast inside.”

Ms. Lark knelt beside him. “It sounds like your feelings are loud. Let’s try a quiet breath.” She touched her chest. “Breathe in… like smelling a flower.” She sniffed. “Breathe out… like blowing a feather.” She blew softly.

Bruno copied. In… out… He felt the cool air in his nose and the warm air out. His paws slowed. Boom… boom. The drum sounded friendly now.

Friends came in with bright smiles and special instruments. A girl named Amara held little metal bells. “These are from my family,” she said. “We ring them for dancing.”

Tariq stepped forward with a small drum tied with string. “My Gramma taught me this rhythm,” he said. “It feels like walking.”

Sora hugged a bamboo flute. “My grandpa showed me how to make the sound,” she whispered. “It is like wind.”

Bruno looked from friend to friend. He wanted to hear every song at once. His paws buzzed again. He grabbed the red shaker and shook it fast—shake-shake-SHAKE!

“Bruno,” Ms. Lark said softly. “We will take turns. Let’s listen with our whole bodies. That is mindfulness.”

“What is mind-full-ness?” asked Bruno, his ears tilting.

“Mindfulness is paying kind attention,” Ms. Lark said. “We listen with our ears, our eyes, our nose, and our heart. We feel our breath. We slow down. We notice.”

Bruno looked at the shaker in his paw. He took a slow flower breath. In… out… He set the shaker down. “I can try,” he said.

Amara went first. She rang her bells—ding-ding—soft and bright like tiny stars. Bruno watched her hands move smooth and light. He listened to the clear sound. He felt it tickle his ears. “Your bells sound like rain,” Bruno said, smiling.

“Thank you,” said Amara. “In my family, we ring them when we dance together.”

Tariq played next. Tap-tap ta-tap! The drum made a walking beat. Bruno put his paw on his chest. His heart matched the rhythm. Tap-tap ta-tap. He swayed gently. “Your drum sounds like feet on a path,” said Bruno.

“Yes,” Tariq laughed. “We clap and sing with it at home.”

Then Sora raised her bamboo flute. She blew a long, soft note. Hoooo. The sound drifted like a breeze through trees. Bruno closed his eyes. He felt the air on his nose. He smelled wood and a little lemon from the clean floor. He felt calm, like a leaf floating.

When Sora finished, Bruno opened his eyes. “Your flute is like wind hugging my ears,” he said.

Sora’s eyes shone. “Grandpa says the flute helps us breathe and listen.”

Now it was Bruno’s turn. He looked at his big drum. His tummy fluttered again. What if he played too loud? He touched his fur and felt its warmth. He took a flower breath. In… out… One more. In… out…

He lifted his paws and played. Boom… pause… boom-boom… pause. The drum beat was gentle and steady. He felt the sound in his paws and in the floor. “My Grandma hums when we make honey cookies,” Bruno said. “I think my drum can hum too.”

Boom… mmm… boom. Bruno hummed a low bear song. The room filled with cozy sound.

Everyone listened. They smiled. Ms. Lark swayed. “I can hear your story,” she said. “Your drum tells a warm, sweet song.”

Bruno’s cheeks felt sunny. He wasn’t rushing. He was noticing. He was sharing.

“Let’s make a circle,” said Ms. Lark. “We will play together. We will listen for spaces. We will leave room for each other.”

They made a circle. Amara’s bells went ding after Bruno’s boom. Tariq’s drum walked between. Sora’s flute wind flowed over all. Bruno watched, listened, breathed. When his tummy felt wiggly, he took a quiet breath. In… out… He waited for the space, then added a gentle boom.

The sounds danced. They were different but friendly. The room smelled like wood and lemon and a hint of honey from Bruno’s fur. The floor felt smooth under paws and feet. The music felt like a big hug.

When the last note floated away, Ms. Lark whispered, “Let’s notice the quiet.” They all breathed together. The quiet was soft and nice.

Bruno grinned. “Mindfulness helps me listen. Mindfulness helps me share.”

“And when we listen,” said Amara, “we learn each other’s songs.”

“We share our traditions,” Tariq added.

“We make new music together,” Sora said.

Ms. Lark nodded. “Different songs. One happy room.”

Bruno hugged his drum. “I can slow down,” he said. “I can breathe. I can listen. I can play with friends.”

They all smiled and tapped their hearts. In… out…

The End.
