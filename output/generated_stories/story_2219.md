# Spark the Robot Dog and the Cozy Closet Quest

Spark was a helpful robot dog with shiny silver ears and a warm, waggy tail that hummed, “whirr-whirr.” He loved to help. He loved to learn. Most of all, he loved his cozy closet hideaway. It was a small space with soft blankets, a basket of toys, and twinkly sticker stars on the door. It smelled like clean laundry and a little bit like lavender soap.

One sunny morning, Spark trotted into the closet hideaway. “Click-click,” went his gentle paws. He planned a quiet rest. But when he looked in his toy basket, he blinked his bright blue eyes. His favorite bedtime book was missing!

“Oh no,” said Spark. “Where is ‘Goodnight, Little Moon’? I read it every day.”

Spark’s tail slowed. “Whirr…” He felt a tiny worry inside. He took a deep, brave breath. “I can solve this,” he said softly. “I am a helper. I can try.”

Spark turned on his small nose light. “Beep!” The closet glowed. He listened and sniffed. He heard the soft rustle of hangers and smelled the sweet lavender again.

“First, I will look high,” Spark said. He stood up on his tippy toes. “Tip-tip.” He peeked on the top shelf. Socks. A hat. No book.

“Next, I will look low,” said Spark. He lay down flat and peeked under the blanket pile. “Swoosh.” He found a fuzzy mitten and a tiny blue button. Still no book.

Spark sat and thought. His ears made a little “tink” sound when he tapped them. “When did I read the book last?” he asked himself. “Last night, in my closet. Then I drifted to sleep. Maybe the book slipped away.”

He looked around with open, curious eyes. “I will try a new idea,” Spark said. “I will ask for help.” Asking for help was kind and smart.

He poked his head out of the closet. “Hello?” he called. “Is anyone nearby?”

A soft voice answered, “Me! I’m Pip the Pillow. I live right here in the closet.” Pip looked fluffy and smelled like sunshine.

“Hi, Pip,” said Spark. “I’m looking for my bedtime book. Have you seen it?”

Pip puffed up proudly. “I saw something slide under the door last night. It made a tiny ‘swish’ sound.”

Spark’s tail buzzed happily. “Thank you, Pip! That is a helpful clue.”

He scooted to the bottom of the door and peered through the crack. He saw a sliver of blue. “Beep-beep! I see it!”

But the book was out in the hallway. The door was almost closed, and Spark didn’t want to scratch it. He thought again. “I will be gentle. I will be kind to the door,” he said.

He tried his first plan. He reached a paw under the door. “Tap-tap.” His paw was too big. The book did not move.

He tried a second plan. He blew a slow puff of robot air. “Hooo.” The book wiggled a little, but not enough.

Spark did not give up. He smiled. “I can do hard things,” he whispered. “I will find a smart tool.”

He looked around the closet hideaway with open-minded eyes. “What could help?” He saw a shoelace. He saw a soft scarf. He saw a long, flat ruler tucked behind a box.

“The ruler!” Spark wagged. “It is long and gentle. I can push, not pull. That might work.”

He slid the ruler under the door. “Sssss.” He moved it slowly. “I will go easy,” he said. “Easy, easy.” He felt the edge of the book. “Click!” He nudged it. The book slid closer. “Scritch-scritch.” A little more, a little more.

Spark kept trying, patient and calm. “I can do this. I can do this,” he sang in a soft, happy way.

At last, the blue book slipped back into the closet. “Plop!”

Spark’s eyes shone bright. “I did it!” he cheered. He nuzzled the book kindly. “Thank you, Pip, for your clue. You helped me.”

Pip puffed with pride. “You solved it with a good plan and gentle hands!”

Spark wagged. “First I looked. Then I asked. Then I tried different ideas. I stayed calm. That was smart problem-solving.”

He curled up with his book on the soft blankets. The closet felt safe and warm. The sticker stars seemed to glow a little brighter. He opened the book and read in a soothing voice, “Goodnight, Little Moon…”

When he finished, Spark tucked the book into a small box so it would not slide away again. He added a label that read, “Books Sleep Here.” He patted the box. “Kind and tidy,” he said.

Pip yawned. “You believed in yourself, Spark.”

Spark nodded. “Yes. I can be brave and gentle. I can try new ways. And I can ask friends for help.” His tail hummed a happy “whirr-whirr.”

The closet hideaway was very quiet now. Spark closed his eyes. He felt proud, kind, and calm.

That day, Spark learned: When a problem pops up, take a breath. Look. Ask. Try new ideas. Believe in yourself. With kindness and patience, you can find a way.

The End.
