# Bubbles Helps in the Basement Lab

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a happy squeak. Today, he splashed into the big tank in Dr. Dot’s basement lab. The lab smelled like clean soap and salty water. Lights blinked. Machines hummed. The floor felt cool and smooth.

“Good morning, Bubbles!” said Dr. Dot. She wore a white coat and blue shoes. “We have an important day. The tiny sea-bots need help. They fell on the floor. We must fix them before the school visit.”

Bubbles clicked and squeaked. “I can help! I want to help!” His tail swished. Gentle waves rippled across the tank. Helping made his heart feel warm and big.

A little turtle robot beeped sadly. “Beep-beep. I’m stuck under the table,” it said in a small voice.

Bubbles lifted his head above the water. He looked around. The basement lab was full of things—wires, boxes, a big table, and a soft blue towel. The air smelled like oranges from a cleaner. He saw the turtle robot under the table, its light blinking.

“I will volunteer!” Bubbles said. “I will help for free. I want everyone to learn and smile.”

Dr. Dot nodded. “Thank you, Bubbles. That is volunteering. You give your time. You use your kind heart.”

Bubbles took a deep breath. “I can do it,” he told himself. “I am brave.” He pressed a big red button. A little lift moved the tank closer to the table. Clunk. Whirr.

But the turtle robot was still far away. Bubbles frowned. “Hmm.” He looked at a long grabber tool with a soft claw. It was on a hook. It looked shiny and a little heavy.

“I will try,” he said. He held the grabber in his mouth. Clack. The metal tasted cold. He stretched his neck. He reached. He missed.

“Oops!” Bubbles said. The grabber slipped into the water with a splash. Drip-drip-drip. Bubbles shook his head. Water drops sparkled like tiny stars.

Dr. Dot smiled gently. “It’s okay, Bubbles. Try again. You are learning. You are being persistent.”

Bubbles took a slow breath. “I will try again.” He picked up the grabber. He moved slowly, slowly. “I am calm. I am kind. I can be brave,” he whispered.

He reached under the table. He felt dust tickle his nose. “Ah-choo!” He giggled. He opened the soft claw and closed it around the turtle robot’s fin. Click.

“I got you!” Bubbles cheered.

The turtle robot beeped happily. “Beep-beep! Thank you!”

Bubbles lifted the turtle robot onto a towel. He patted it gently with his fin. The towel felt fluffy and warm. “You are safe now,” he said.

Dr. Dot clapped. “Well done! Your courage and kindness helped a friend.”

“Who else needs help?” Bubbles asked. His eyes shone. He liked this volunteering job.

A little crab bot wiggled in a box. “My legs are loose,” it chirped. “Click-click.”

“I will help you too,” said Bubbles. He looked at a tiny screwdriver. He took it carefully in his mouth. The handle felt rubbery. He moved slow. He listened to the hum of the lab. He watched the small silver screws. Turn, turn, turn. The screws went snug. The crab bot stood tall.

“Click-click! Thank you!” it cheered.

Bubbles felt proud. His chest felt warm like sunshine. He wiggled his tail. Splash-swish!

Then the lights blinked fast. Buzz-buzz. The big door stuck halfway. A box of sea-bot parts sat on the other side. The school kids would come soon. “Oh no,” said Dr. Dot. “We need that box. But the door is stuck.”

Bubbles looked at the door. It was heavy and gray. He felt a little scared. His fins trembled. He took a deep breath. “I can try. I will be brave. I will be careful,” he said.

Dr. Dot handed him a soft rope. “We can pull together,” she said. “Teamwork and courage.”

Bubbles bit the rope. Dr. Dot held the other end. “One, two, three—pull!” they said. They pulled. The rope felt rough. The door didn’t move.

Bubbles blinked. “I will not give up,” he said. “I will be persistent.”

They tried again. “One, two, three—pull!” Creak. The door moved a little. They smiled. “One, two, three—pull!” Creak. Slide. The door opened wide.

“Yay!” cheered Bubbles. He swam in a happy circle, making tiny waves. The box of parts was right there. He used the grabber to lift it. He set it gently on the table.

Soon, the sea-bots buzzed happily. Clicks and beeps filled the lab like tiny songs. The room smelled like sweet oranges and warm towels. The school kids’ voices floated down the stairs. “Wow!” they said when they saw the bots swim and wiggle.

Dr. Dot patted Bubbles. “You volunteered today. You were kind. You were brave. You kept trying.”

Bubbles smiled. “Volunteering feels good,” he said. “Helping makes hearts happy.”

The kids waved. “Thank you, Bubbles!” they called.

Bubbles squeaked and splashed. “You’re welcome! We can all help. We can be brave. We can be kind. We can keep trying!”

The End.
