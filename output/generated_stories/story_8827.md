# Coco Cat Finds Their Voice

Coco was a clever cat with bright green eyes and soft, stripy fur. Coco lived in a cozy treehouse with a round window, a tiny blue door, and a rope ladder that swished and creaked. The treehouse smelled like pine cones and sunshine. Inside, there were soft pillows, clean towels, a tiny sink, and a mirror that made Coco’s whiskers look extra long.

“Good morning, tree!” Coco purred, stretching paws and tail. The leaves whispered back, shhhhhh, and a warm breeze tickled Coco’s nose. Today was Clean-and-Play Day, a special day Coco made to keep the treehouse fresh and happy.

As Coco hummed a little song, there was a thump-thump on the ladder.

“Hi, Coco!” called Pip, a bouncy puppy with muddy paws.

“Hi, Coco!” chirped Dot, a bluebird with sparkly wings.

“Hi, Coco!” squeaked Nib, a shy mouse with big round ears.

Coco smiled. “Welcome to my treehouse! Let’s clean, then play. We can all help.”

Pip wagged. “I want to help with snacks!”

Dot fluffed. “I want to sing while we sweep!”

Nib peeped softly, “I can put toys in the basket.”

Coco pointed to a sign by the tiny sink. The sign had pictures: a paw, a wing, a tail, and bubbles. “First,” Coco said gently, “we wash our paws, wings, and tails. We keep our space clean. It helps us feel good.”

Pip made a face. “But washing is splashy. I don’t like the cold water.”

Dot tilted her head. “I fly fast. I don’t need washing.”

Nib hopped from foot to foot. “I’m nervous. The sink is big.”

Coco felt a flutter in the tummy. Coco wanted everyone to be happy. But Coco also wanted to keep the treehouse clean and safe. Coco took a deep breath. The air smelled like soap and leaves. Coco remembered the lesson: I can use my voice, kind and clear.

Coco spoke up. “Friends, I hear you. Washing can be tricky. But this is my treehouse, and I need it clean. I am asking you to wash, please. Clean paws keep our snacks safe. Clean wings keep our pillows fresh. Clean tails keep our toys nice. I can help you.”

Pip stopped wagging and listened. Dot folded her wings. Nib peeked up with wide eyes.

Coco showed a warm bowl of water. “Feel this,” Coco said, dipping a paw. “It’s warm like sunshine.”

Pip touched the water. “Oh! Not cold. Okay.” Pip giggled as bubbles popped, pop-pop-pop. “It smells like oranges!”

Dot sniffed. “Mmm. Orange bubbles? I can try.” She fluttered close, and Coco dabbed her tiny claws with a soft cloth. “Tickly!” Dot chirped, and she laughed.

Coco placed a small step stool by the sink. “Nib, this is for you. You can be brave. I will stand right here.”

Nib climbed up. Swish, swish. “It feels slippery,” Nib said.

Coco nodded. “You are doing it. I am proud of you.”

Soon, everyone was clean and shiny. The towels were fluffy and warm. The treehouse smelled bright and sweet.

“Thank you for listening,” Coco said. “I used my voice to share what I need. That is called speaking up. It helps me, and it helps us.”

Pip’s tail thumped softly. “I didn’t want to wash, but now I feel light and comfy.”

Dot fluffed her clean feathers. “I learned something new. I can try new things when friends explain.”

Nib smiled shyly. “I was scared. Coco helped me. I can ask for help.”

They sang as they cleaned. Sweep-sweep went the little broom. Tap-tap went the toy blocks into the basket. Crunch-crunch went the apple slices as Coco set the snack plates. The sun made golden patches on the floor, warm like a nap.

Then a small problem popped up. Pip wanted to play ball inside. Zoom! The ball bounced off the clean pillows. Thump! It bumped the mirror.

Coco’s whiskers twitched. Coco took another deep breath. “Pip, I need to say something. The ball can bump and break things. I feel worried. I want to keep everyone safe. Let’s play ball outside on the soft grass.”

Pip paused, ears up. “But I like it in here.”

Dot landed on Pip’s head and giggled. “Let’s try outside. The breeze will make the ball zoom!”

Coco stayed calm. “Thank you for listening, Pip. It’s okay to want things. It’s also okay for me to say what I need.”

Pip nodded. “Okay. I can be open to a new plan.”

Outside, the grass was cool and tickly. The leaves whispered cheer-cheer-cheer! They tossed the ball in the shade, and Dot sang a happy tune. Nib clapped tiny paws. Coco felt proud and peaceful.

When they went back inside, they washed paws, wings, and tails again, quick and easy. Swish, dab, dry. They ate apple slices and crunchy carrots. They sipped cool water. Everything felt fresh and good.

Coco curled on a pillow and smiled at friends. “Today we learned big things,” Coco said. “We used kind words to say what we need. We tried new ideas. We kept clean to stay healthy.”

Pip wagged. “Speaking up can be gentle.”

Dot chirped. “New things can be fun.”

Nib nodded. “Clean and brave feels nice.”

Coco purred. “When we use our voices and listen to others, we all feel safe and happy.”

The End.
