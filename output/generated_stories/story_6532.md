# Pippa the Fair Parrot

In a quiet study corner, the lamp glowed soft and warm. The room smelled like crayons and paper. Pencils stood up in a cup, like tiny trees. Little books sat in neat rows. Pippa the parrot fluttered in with a happy squawk. She was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of red on her tail. She loved to make art and solve puzzles.

“Good morning, study corner!” Pippa chirped. “I am ready to help!”

Her friends were there too. Benny the bunny tapped his foot. “We must put stickers on the chart,” he said. “We all help with tasks today.”

Suki the squirrel twitched her fluffy tail. “We also color the big poster,” she added. “We need to finish before snack time.”

Pippa clapped her wings. “I love projects! Let’s do it!”

Benny held up the sticker sheet. Each sticker was shiny and smelled like strawberries. There were stars, hearts, and smiley faces. “We will each get one sticker when we finish a job,” Benny said.

Pippa’s eyes sparkled. “Can we choose our sticker?”

Benny shook his head. “No. Only stars. Stars are best. Everyone gets a star.”

Suki looked down at her paws. “But I like hearts,” she whispered. “Hearts make me feel warm.”

Pippa tilted her head. She felt a flutter in her chest. “Hmm,” she murmured. “Is that fair?”

Benny put a star by his name. “Stars are fair because we all get the same,” he said.

Pippa looked at the stickers again. She sniffed the sweet strawberry smell. She brushed a feather over a shiny heart. “Fair can mean same,” she said softly. “But fair can also mean we get what helps us do our best.”

Suki nodded. “Hearts help me try.”

Benny’s ears wiggled. “I don’t know,” he said. “I like simple rules.”

Pippa thought for a moment. She looked around the study corner. She heard the gentle hum of the lamp and the scratchy sound of crayons on paper. “Let’s have a plan,” she chirped. “A peaceful plan.”

“What plan?” asked Benny.

Pippa hopped to the chart. “We will all do three tasks,” she said. “1) Sort pencils by color. 2) Match books by size. 3) Color the poster. We can finish together. When we finish each task, we choose a sticker that fits us. That is fair because it helps each of us feel proud.”

Benny frowned. “But what if someone takes all the stars?”

Pippa smiled. “We can set a kind rule,” she said gently. “We share the stickers. No one takes them all. We listen, and we take turns.”

Suki perked up. “That sounds fair to me,” she said.

Benny tapped his foot, thinking. The floor felt cool under his toes. He looked at the neat pencils, the short books, the tall books, and the bright poster waiting. He took a deep breath. “Okay,” he said at last. “Let’s try.”

They began with the pencils. Pippa felt the smooth wood as she sorted greens with greens, blues with blues. “Green pencils smell like leaves,” she giggled.

Suki scurried, placing reds with reds. “These look like cherries,” she squeaked.

Benny lined up yellows. “Sunshine row,” he said, grinning.

They finished task one. “Choose your sticker,” Pippa chirped.

Suki picked a heart. “It makes my heart happy,” she said.

Benny chose a star. “It makes me feel bright,” he said.

Pippa chose a smiley face. “It reminds me to be kind,” she said.

Next, they matched books by size. Big books thumped softly. Small books made tiny taps. “Tall here, short there,” Pippa said. Her feathers brushed the book spines. She liked the smooth feel.

They finished task two. Suki chose another heart. Benny chose another star. Pippa chose a rainbow. “I like to celebrate differences,” she said. “Look at our stickers—so many shapes!”

Last, they colored the poster. Pippa made blue waves and green trees. She hummed a happy tune. Suki dotted pink blossoms. Benny colored a golden sun. “We are a good team,” Benny said, smiling.

They stepped back. The poster shined with colors. The study corner felt warm and bright. They finished task three!

Benny reached for a sticker and paused. “Suki, do you want a heart?” he asked.

Suki’s eyes lit up. “Yes, please!”

Benny nodded. “I will try a smiley face this time,” he said. “New things can help me, too.”

Pippa clapped her wings. “That is fair,” she said. “We each get what helps us do our best. We share. We listen. We take turns.”

They placed their last stickers on the chart. It looked like a garden of shapes. Stars twinkled. Hearts glowed. Smileys beamed. Rainbows curved like hugs.

Benny wiggled his ears. “Fair is not just the same,” he said slowly. “Fair is kind. Fair helps everyone.”

Suki hugged her tail. “And differences can be happy,” she added. “Hearts for me, stars for you, smiles for Pippa!”

Pippa puffed her feathers with pride. “We finished our tasks,” she chirped. “We improved our plan. We found a peaceful solution.”

They shared a crunchy apple snack. The slices were cool and sweet. The room was quiet and safe. The chart shone in the lamp’s soft light.

Pippa looked at her friends. “Today we learned,” she said. “Fair means we care.”

The End.
