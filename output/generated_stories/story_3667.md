# Benny the Busy Beaver’s Big Video Call

Benny the busy beaver worked in a cozy home office. His desk was made of smooth wood he gently chewed and shaped himself. The room smelled like fresh pine. Sunlight slid across his papers, and his computer hummed like a quiet bee. Click, clack, tap went Benny’s little paws on the keyboard.

“Today is a big day,” Benny said, his whiskers twitching. “I’m having a video call with helpers from all over the world!” He looked at his list. “We will plan a new playground by the river. I want everyone to feel welcome.”

Benny took a deep breath. “I can do this,” he whispered. He checked his camera. He tested his mic. “Hello, hello!” he said. His voice sounded clear and warm. He set out colorful sticky notes. He filled a cup with cool water. He even put a basket of crunchy willow sticks by his chair. Crunch, crunch!

Knock, knock! The screen lit up. First came Miko, a tiny mouse with round ears and bright eyes. “Hi, Benny!” squeaked Miko. “I like building little tunnels.”

Next came Tala, a tall giraffe who wore bright headphones. “Hello!” said Tala in a gentle voice. “I can see far and reach high places.”

Then Zuzu, a zebra with musical stripes, joined in. “Clip-clop hello!” said Zuzu. “I love to paint bold lines and soft swirls.”

Last came Pika, a cheerful penguin in a cozy sweater. “Honk hello!” said Pika. “I’m great with slides and making safe paths on slippery spots.”

Benny’s tail thumped happily on the floor. Thump, thump. “Welcome, friends! I’m Benny. I chew wood really well and make strong beams. We are all different. That is good. Our differences will help us.”

He showed a picture of the river bank. “We need a playground for everyone,” Benny said. “Some kids run fast. Some roll in wheelchairs. Some like quiet spaces. Some like bright colors. How can we do it?”

Miko squeaked, “I can make a tiny tunnel for toy cars and small hands!”

Tala nodded slowly. “I can help with a tall lookout that has easy ramps, not just stairs.”

Zuzu swished his tail. “I will paint signs with pictures and colors, so everyone can understand.”

Pika flapped. “I’ll plan a cool slide with safe sides. Also a calm, quiet nook for rest.”

Benny smiled. “Great ideas!” He wrote on his sticky notes. His paws moved fast. “We are prepared. We have a plan.”

Then, the screen flickered. Zzzzt. The sound crackled. Benny’s computer froze. His happy face stuck in a silly shape. Everyone blinked.

“Oh no,” whispered Benny. His tummy felt wiggly. His tail went still. “What if I can’t fix it? What if I mess up the meeting?”

He remembered his plan. He had prepared for problems. “I have a backup!” he said softly. He took a calm breath. In… and out… He checked his list. He found his spare battery. He plugged in his little hotspot. Click. He restarted the computer.

The screen warmed up with a soft glow. Zuzu’s stripes, Tala’s long neck, Miko’s bright eyes, and Pika’s sweater popped back. “You did it!” cheered Miko.

Benny blushed under his fur. “Thank you for waiting. I prepared because sometimes things go wrong. That’s okay.”

“Different ways to connect,” said Tala. “Just like different ways to play.”

They kept planning. Benny asked, “What about sounds? Some friends like quiet.”

“I’ll add soft wind chimes that can be turned off,” said Zuzu. “And paint quiet symbols.”

“I’ll make a comfy reading log with smooth wood,” said Benny. “It will be low and wide.”

“I’ll put gentle, bouncy mats,” said Pika, patting the air. “Safe and soft.”

“And I’ll make tunnels with windows,” said Miko. “So no one feels stuck.”

They looked at the picture together. It had ramps that curved like smiles, a slide with high sides, bright signs with shapes and colors, quiet shade spots, and a big, sturdy reading log. It felt friendly. It looked fun.

Benny’s nose twitched with happy tears. “We did this because we are not the same,” he said. “Our differences are our superpowers.”

The friends beamed. The computer hummed. The sun felt warm on Benny’s back. He could almost hear river water giggle outside. Shhhh, gurgle, shhhh.

“Let’s meet at the river tomorrow,” said Benny. “Bring your tools and your ideas. We will build it together.”

“Together!” everyone cheered.

The next day, they met by the sparkling water. Benny chewed and shaped wood. Miko measured tiny spaces. Tala lifted rails gently with her long neck. Zuzu painted bright, welcoming signs. Pika tested the slide with a happy honk and a wiggle.

Children came—big, small, fast, slow, quiet, chatty. Some rolled, some ran, some waved. All smiled. Everyone found a place to play.

Benny’s tail thumped again. Thump, thump. “We prepared. We listened. We celebrated our differences,” he said.

He looked at his friends. “When we make room for everyone, everyone belongs.”

The End.
