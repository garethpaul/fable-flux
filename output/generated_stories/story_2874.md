# Sunny Sings and Solves

Sunny was a small singing bird with bright yellow feathers and a warm, twinkly voice. She lived near a musical practice room where children learned songs and played instruments. The room smelled like fresh wood and lemon polish. Soft sunlight came through the window, and the floor hummed when drums were tapped. Sunny loved to visit and sing along.

One morning, the music teacher, Mr. Leo, opened the door. “Good morning, Sunny!” he said, smiling. “We have a music show today. We must practice and keep the room tidy.”

Sunny fluttered in and landed on a music stand. “Tweet-a-dee! I can help,” she chirped. She felt proud. Helping felt good.

Mr. Leo gave simple jobs to everyone. “Piper the mouse, please stack the rhythm sticks. Nia the bunny, please roll the drum to the corner. Sunny, please gather the sheet music into one neat pile.”

Sunny nodded. “I can do it! I am responsible,” she sang.

But the shiny tambourine caught her eye. It glittered with little mirrors. “Jingle-jingle!” Sunny laughed, tapping it with her wing. She forgot about the papers. She danced and twirled. The tambourine jingled and the air smelled like sweet orange from Mr. Leo’s tea.

Piper finished stacking. Nia rolled the drum. Mr. Leo checked the clock. “Almost time,” he said. “Is the sheet music ready?”

Sunny looked down. Papers were everywhere—on the floor, under a chair, even inside the open piano. Her tummy felt twisty. “Oh no,” she whispered. “I forgot my job.”

Mr. Leo knelt beside her. “Sunny,” he said gently, “being responsible means doing your job first. We all count on each other.”

Sunny’s eyes felt hot. She wanted to sing, but no song came out. “I am sorry,” she chirped softly. “I want to fix it.”

“That’s a good start,” said Mr. Leo. “Let’s solve this together.”

Sunny took a deep breath. She listened. She heard the soft shush of papers when she flapped her wings. She watched how the air moved the sheets. An idea sparkled in her mind. “I can use my song to help!” she said. “We can make a music plan.”

Piper’s whiskers twitched. “A music plan?”

“Yes,” said Sunny. “I will sing a slow, steady beat. You and Nia can gather the papers to the beat. We will make the work feel like a song.”

Mr. Leo clapped. “That is innovative thinking. Let’s try.”

Sunny perched on the music stand. She began to sing, low and even. “Tweet… tweet… twoo… tweet… tweet… twoo…” Her voice was warm like sunlight and soft like a blanket. Piper and Nia moved their paws and feet to the beat. Shush-shush. Pick. Stack. Shush-shush. Pick. Stack.

“Jingle when you find the next page,” Sunny added. Piper shook the tambourine once for each new page. Jingle. Jingle. Nia giggled. “This is fun!”

Sunny’s song grew brighter. “Verse for the violins,” she sang, pointing with a wing. Piper slid those pages into a neat pile. “Chorus for the choir,” Sunny sang, and Nia made another tidy stack. Mr. Leo gathered the pages under the chair and hummed along.

Soon, the papers were in order. The room looked calm and ready. It smelled like wood and orange tea again. Sunny felt her tummy untwist.

Mr. Leo smiled kindly. “Sunny, you made a mistake. Then you took responsibility. You also solved the problem in a smart, new way.”

Sunny’s feathers fluffed with pride. “I forgot, but I fixed it,” she said. “Next time, I will do my job first, then play.”

Piper squeaked, “Your song helped me move fast.”

Nia wiggled her ears. “Your voice is different from mine, and that made our team better.”

Sunny’s eyes shone. “Our differences can help us. We can celebrate them!”

Mr. Leo tapped the baton. “Group, let’s practice our show piece.”

They played and sang. The drum went boom-boom. The tambourine went jingle-jingle. Sunny’s voice floated like a ribbon. The room filled with happy sound. When they finished, everyone clapped.

Mr. Leo opened the door. Families peeked in. “We are ready now because we worked together,” he said.

Sunny fluttered to the front. “Friends,” she chirped, “today I learned: being responsible means doing my job, even when something shiny calls me to play. If I forget, I can say sorry, make a plan, and fix it. My song is different, and that helps the team. Your ways help too!”

The families smiled and nodded. The children tapped their toes. Sunny felt warm and brave.

When the show began, Sunny did her job first—she gathered the last pages and set them on the stand. Then she sang her sweetest song. Everyone joined in. The music room glowed with joy.

After the show, Sunny put the tambourine back in its place. “First I care for my job,” she whispered. “Then I play.”

Mr. Leo winked. “That is responsibility.”

Sunny chirped a gentle good-bye. Outside, the sky was pink like cotton candy. She flew home, humming her steady, happy tune.

The End.
