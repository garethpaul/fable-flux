# Tessa Turtle Finds Her Beat

Tessa was a shy turtle with a smooth, shiny shell. One bright afternoon, she peeked into the musical practice room. The room smelled like clean wood and lemon polish. Soft light fell on shiny drums, a sleepy piano, and rows of tiny bells. A big mirror covered one wall.

Thump-thump. Tessa’s heart beat fast. “I want to dance,” she whispered. “But I feel so small.”

Inside the room, a friendly mouse named Max waved. “Hi, Tessa! Come in! We’re making music.” Max held a little drum. Tap-tap-tap, it sounded happy.

A gentle rabbit named Rosie stood by the piano. “We can dance too,” said Rosie, her ears wiggling. “Let’s try a wiggle dance!”

Tessa tucked her head a little. “I’m shy,” she said. “What if I look silly?”

Rosie smiled. “Feelings are okay,” she said softly. “We can listen to your feelings together.”

Max tapped the drum. Tap-tap-tap. “You can dance with your shell,” he said. “Just a tiny move. Little steps count.”

Tessa took one slow step. Slide… step. Her shell made a quiet scritch on the floor. The mirror showed a turtle with wide eyes. “That’s me,” she whispered.

Rosie pressed a piano key. Plink. The note was bright, like a sunbeam. “Let the sound help you,” she said. “Plink means a small hop.”

Max tapped again. Tap… tap-tap. “Tap means a toe step,” he said.

Tessa listened. Plink. Tap-tap. Her toes wiggled. Her shell swayed. She smiled a tiny smile.

Then—BOOM! A big drum on a stand rolled a little and thumped. The sound was loud. Tessa jumped and pulled into her shell with a soft clack.

“I’m scared,” Tessa said from inside. The room felt too big. The mirror felt too shiny. Her cheeks felt hot. Her heart went thump-thump-thump.

Rosie knelt by Tessa. “Thank you for telling us,” she said. “Let’s take a slow breath.”

Max sat down too. “In through the nose,” he said. “Out through the mouth.” They breathed together. In… and out. In… and out.

Tessa peeked out. The room did not feel so big now. “I still feel shy,” she said. “I still want to dance.”

Rosie nodded. “We can help,” she said. “What do you need?”

Tessa looked around. She saw a box of scarves, soft and colorful. She saw sticky notes on a shelf. She had an idea. “Can I make a quiet corner?” she asked. “A little place for me?”

“That is very smart,” Max said. “Yes!”

Together they built a Cozy Corner. They put a blue scarf on the floor. They put a purple scarf on a chair to make a tiny tent. Max wrote a note and stuck it on the mirror: “Quiet Feet Near Corner.” Rosie put a small bell by Tessa’s corner. “Ring if you need help,” she said.

Tessa crawled to her corner. It smelled like clean cloth. It felt soft and safe. She took one breath. In… and out.

“Let’s make a dance map,” said Rosie. She drew shapes on sticky notes: a circle, a square, a star. “Circle means sway. Square means step-step. Star means spin.”

Max tapped the drum softly. Tap. Rosie played a soft note. Plink. “Follow the map,” Max said.

Tessa looked at the circle. Sway-sway. Her shell moved like a boat on water. She felt calm. She looked at the square. Step-step. Her feet went left-right. She felt brave. She peeked at the star. Spin? “I’m not ready,” she said.

“That’s okay,” Rosie said. “We can wait.”

Tap-tap. Plink-plink. The room filled with gentle sound. Tessa swayed and stepped. Her shy feeling was still there, but now it felt smaller, like a tiny pebble in her pocket.

After a while, Max smiled. “Would you like to share your dance?” he asked.

Tessa looked at her corner. She looked at the mirror. She touched the bell but did not ring it. “I will try,” she said.

She took her dance map to the middle of the room. Sway-sway for the circle. Step-step for the square. Her shell caught the light and looked like a little moon. The piano sang plink-plink-plink. The drum whispered tap… tap… tap.

Tessa stopped at the star note. She pressed her toes into the floor. “Spin?” she asked herself. She felt her shy pebble. She felt her breath. In… and out.

“I can spin small,” she said. She made a tiny spin. Her shell went swirl. She laughed. It sounded like bubbles.

Max and Rosie clapped. Clap-clap-clap! “You did it!” they cheered.

Tessa stood taller. “I danced,” she said. “I listened to my feelings. I made a plan. And I danced!”

Rosie hugged her gently. “You were resourceful,” she said. “You found what you needed.”

Max nodded. “And you learned that dance can help big feelings feel smaller.”

Tessa smiled. “Dance is for me,” she said. “When I feel shy, I can breathe, make a cozy space, and take tiny steps. Then I can try a small spin.”

They danced together—sway, step, tiny spin. The musical practice room felt warm and bright. The mirror showed three friends moving with the music.

Tessa’s shy pebble was still there, but now it felt like a friend saying, Go slow. That was okay. Tessa had her beat.

The End.
