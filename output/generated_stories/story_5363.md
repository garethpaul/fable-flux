# Nutkin's Big Day

High up in the tallest oak tree, Nutkin the squirrel lived in the coziest treehouse. His home had tiny windows that sparkled in the morning sun and a little door painted bright red. Nutkin loved his treehouse, but today he had a big problem.

"Oh no!" squeaked Nutkin, looking at his empty acorn jar. His fluffy tail twitched with worry. "I forgot to save enough acorns for breakfast!"

Nutkin's tummy rumbled loudly. "Grumble, grumble, GRUMBLE!" He could smell the sweet morning air and hear birds chirping, but all he could think about was food.

"I know what to do," said Nutkin, puffing out his chest. "I can find my own breakfast! I'm a big squirrel now."

He scampered down the rough bark of the oak tree, his tiny claws clicking against the wood. The grass felt cool and damp under his paws as he searched for acorns.

"Look, look, look," Nutkin sang to himself as he hopped around. Under a pile of golden leaves, he found three perfect acorns. "Hooray! I found them all by myself!"

Just then, he heard a tiny voice crying. "Sniff, sniff, sob!"

Behind a mushroom sat Pip, a very small chipmunk. Tears rolled down her furry cheeks.

"What's wrong, Pip?" asked Nutkin kindly.

"I'm so hungry," whispered Pip. "I'm too little to climb the tall trees to find acorns. My mama is sick and can't help me today."

Nutkin looked at his three acorns, then at sad little Pip. His heart felt warm and fuzzy. "I know! We can share! I found these acorns all by myself, and now I can share them with you."

Pip's eyes grew big and bright. "Really? You would do that for me?"

"Of course!" smiled Nutkin. "Sharing makes everything better!"

They sat together on a soft patch of moss and crunched their acorns. The nuts tasted sweet and nutty. "Crunch, crunch, crunch!"

After breakfast, Nutkin had another idea. "Want to play acorn ball? I can teach you!"

"What's acorn ball?" asked Pip, tilting her head.

"Watch this!" Nutkin picked up an empty acorn shell and tossed it gently to Pip. "We take turns throwing and catching. It's fun exercise!"

They played and played, giggling as they ran around the tree. "Catch, Pip!" "Got it, Nutkin!" The acorn shell flew back and forth between them.

"This is the best game ever!" laughed Pip, her sadness completely gone.

As they played, Nutkin carefully collected the fallen acorn shells. "We should save these," he explained. "We can plant them to grow new oak trees! Then there will be more acorns for everyone."

"You're so smart, Nutkin!" said Pip. "You found food by yourself, shared with me, taught me a fun game, AND you're helping the forest!"

Nutkin felt proud and happy. His chest puffed out like a fluffy balloon. "When we take care of ourselves and help others too, everyone wins!"

As the sun climbed higher in the sky, Nutkin waved goodbye to Pip and climbed back up to his treehouse. He looked out his little window at the forest below, feeling proud of his big day.

"I can take care of myself," he said with a big smile. "And when I share what I have, I make new friends and help others too!"

That night, Nutkin fell asleep with a full tummy and a happy heart, dreaming of acorn ball and new oak trees growing tall and strong.

The End.
