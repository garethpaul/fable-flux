# Gigi the Helpful Giraffe Builds a Box of Help

Gigi the thoughtful giraffe sat in her cozy home office. Her desk was tall, with a comfy chair and soft blue cushions. A little fan hummed. The window was open, and warm light made stripes on the floor. Gigi smelled sweet paper and fresh crayons. Tap, tap, tap went her long hooves on the keyboard.

"I like to think," Gigi said softly. "I like to plan."

On the wall hung a big paper calendar. Today had a bright yellow star. It said, “Helping Day!” Gigi smiled. “I want to volunteer,” she whispered. “I want to help my neighbors.”

Gigi looked around her office. There were boxes, string, tape, and colorful sticky notes. She took a deep breath. “First, I will think. Then, I will build something to help.”

Just then, her phone buzzed. “Bzzz!” She picked it up with her gentle mouth.

“Hi, Gigi,” said Tiko the turtle on the screen. “My books keep falling down. Can you help?”

Gigi nodded. “Of course, Tiko. I am volunteering today. I will make a plan.”

Her phone buzzed again. “Bzzz!” It was Bea the bee. “Gigi, our flower seeds need a safe place. The wind is too wild.”

Then it buzzed once more. “Bzzz!” It was Poppy the puppy. “My crayons roll away. I can’t find them!”

Gigi’s ears perked. “Three friends need help,” she said. “I can help them all if I think carefully.”

She pulled a big notepad close. She wrote with her favorite blue crayon.

“Problem 1: Books fall down,” she said.
“Problem 2: Seeds blow away.”
“Problem 3: Crayons roll away.”

Gigi tapped her chin. Tap, tap. “What is the same?” she asked. She drew three little pictures: books, seeds, crayons. She noticed they were all small things that needed a safe place. “Ah!” she said. “They all need a box!”

Gigi smiled. “I will build three special boxes.”

She opened a cardboard box with a zip—rrrip! She smelled the cardboard. It smelled warm and papery. She pressed tape. Stick, stick. She cut with snip-snip scissors, very carefully.

“For Tiko’s books, the box must be strong,” Gigi said. “Books are heavy.” She added a firm bottom with extra cardboard. She taped the corners tight. She drew a little turtle on the side. “There,” she said, “a strong book box.”

“For Bea’s seeds, the box must be light and cozy,” Gigi said. She poked tiny air holes so the seeds could breathe. She glued soft cloth inside so the seeds would not slide. She drew bright flowers on the lid. “There,” she said, “a gentle seed box.”

“For Poppy’s crayons, the box must be tidy,” Gigi said. She made little dividers—one, two, three, four—so each color had a place. Red, blue, yellow, green. She added a flap that clicked shut. Click! “There,” she said, “a tidy crayon box.”

Gigi looked at her three boxes. She felt warm and proud. “I used my thinking,” she said. “I matched each problem to a plan. That is logical thinking. That is careful thinking.”

She packed the boxes in a wagon. The wheels went creak, creak. The sun shone bright on the path. Birds sang. Gigi hummed along.

First, she stopped at Tiko’s house. Tiko opened the door slowly. “Hi, Gigi,” he said.

“Ta-da!” Gigi said. She set the strong book box on Tiko’s shelf. They slid the books inside. One, two, three, four. The books stayed up tall.

Tiko’s eyes sparkled. “Thank you, Gigi,” he said. “You volunteered to help me. Now my books are safe.”

Next, she fluttered to Bea’s garden. Bea buzzed around Gigi’s head. “Bzzz! Hi, Gigi!”

Gigi placed the seed box in a quiet corner. Bea poured the tiny seeds in. They rustled like whispers. “Now the wind can’t blow them away,” Bea said. “Thank you, Gigi. You gave your time. That is volunteering!”

Last, Gigi trotted to Poppy’s home. Poppy wagged and wagged. “Is that a crayon box?”

Gigi opened the tidy box. The flap went click. Poppy put each crayon in a cozy slot. “Red has a bed! Blue has a bed!” Poppy said, tail thump-thump-thumping. “Thank you, Gigi. You helped me be neat.”

Gigi pulled the empty wagon back to her home office. The fan still hummed. The room smelled like tape and crayons. She sat in her chair and took a deep, happy breath.

“I used my brain,” she said. “I listened to my friends. I made a plan. I built boxes. I volunteered.”

Her phone buzzed one more time. “Bzzz!” It was a little message from all three friends. The message said, “Thank you, Gigi! You helped us. We feel good. We learned that asking for help is okay, and giving help is kind.”

Gigi smiled. Her heart felt warm, like sunshine on her desk. “Helping feels good,” she said. “When we volunteer, we use thinking and caring. We can build solutions. We can make our world better.”

She put a big gold heart sticker on her calendar. “Helping Day,” she read. “Let’s do it again.”

The End.
