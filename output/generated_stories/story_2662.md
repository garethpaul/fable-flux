# Benny's Musical Moves

Little Benny Bear sat in the cozy music room at his school. Shiny instruments lined the walls - drums, bells, and colorful xylophones. The room smelled like wood polish and felt warm and safe.

"Today we'll learn a new song!" said Miss Robin, his music teacher. Her voice was as sweet as honey.

Benny loved music. He picked up his small drum and tried to play along. *Tap-tap-tap!* But something felt wrong. His paws felt heavy. His tummy felt tight.

"I can't do it right!" Benny huffed. His furry face scrunched up. The drumsticks felt slippery in his paws.

Miss Robin noticed Benny's frown. "What's wrong, little bear?"

"My drum sounds funny. I feel grumpy and tired," Benny said. His ears drooped down sadly.

Miss Robin smiled kindly. "I think I know what might help. Have you moved your body today?"

Benny shook his head. "I sat in the car. Then I sat for story time. Then I sat for snack time."

"Ah!" Miss Robin said. "Your body needs to move to feel good. Let's try some bear exercises!"

"Exercises?" Benny's eyes grew curious.

"Yes! Bears love to move. Watch me!" Miss Robin stood up and stretched her arms high like tree branches. "Reach for the sky!"

Benny giggled and reached up too. His muscles felt tingly and good.

"Now let's march like strong bears!" Miss Robin marched in place, lifting her knees high. *Stomp, stomp, stomp!*

Benny marched too. His heart started beating faster. He felt warmer and happier.

"Bear stretches next!" Miss Robin bent down to touch her toes. "Stretch those sleepy muscles!"

Benny bent down too. "This feels nice!" he said. His back felt loose and wiggly.

"Now shake like you're getting water off your fur!" Miss Robin wiggled all over.

Benny shook his whole body. His paws, his tummy, even his tail! He laughed out loud. "I feel bouncy!"

"Perfect! Now let's take three deep breaths like calm bears." Miss Robin breathed in slowly through her nose. "Smell the forest air."

Benny breathed in deep. The air felt cool and fresh in his nose. He breathed out slowly. His body felt relaxed and ready.

"How do you feel now?" Miss Robin asked.

Benny picked up his drumsticks again. They felt light in his paws. His tummy felt loose and happy. "I feel great! Can we try the song again?"

"Of course!" Miss Robin started the music.

This time, Benny's drum sounded perfect. *Tap-tap-TAP!* His whole body felt awake and strong. He smiled big, showing his little bear teeth.

"I did it!" Benny cheered. "Moving my body made me feel better!"

Miss Robin clapped. "That's right! When we feel frustrated or tired, exercise helps us feel good again. Our bodies need to move every day."

Benny nodded. "Like bears in the forest! They walk and climb and play."

"Exactly! You're a smart little bear."

From that day on, whenever Benny felt grumpy or stuck, he remembered to move his body first. He would stretch tall, march strong, and breathe deep. Then everything felt easier and more fun.

The music room became his favorite place to learn and grow.

The End.
