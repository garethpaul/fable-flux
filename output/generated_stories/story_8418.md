# Roo Follows the Steps

Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a bouncy tail. One sunny morning, Roo and friends were building a cozy reading corner inside their treehouse. The treehouse smelled like fresh wood and sweet sap. Leaves whispered outside, and a soft breeze tickled Roo’s nose.

“Let’s make a snug nook!” Roo cheered, his paws twitching with excitement.

Mina the mouse held a paper list. “I wrote the steps,” she squeaked. “We need to follow directions. Step one: sweep. Step two: lay the rug. Step three: stack books. Step four: hang the lantern.”

Benji the bear cub nodded. “We can do it together.”

Roo bounced high. “I can do it fast! I’ll hang the shiny lantern now!” He grabbed the lantern. It was smooth and cool, and it flashed a tiny rainbow.

Mina shook her head gently. “Roo, please wait. We have steps.”

Roo’s ears perked. He wanted to help. He wanted to be first. “I’ll be careful,” he said, hopping toward the hook.

But Roo’s big tail brushed a pile of colorful books. Thump! Books slid. Plop! A cushion fell. The lantern wobbled with a scary wobble.

“Oh no!” Benji said, catching the lantern with his soft paws. “That was close.”

Roo’s cheeks felt warm. “I just wanted to help.”

Mina’s voice was kind. “You are a good helper, Roo. But we need to follow directions. The steps keep us safe and fair.”

Roo took a slow breath. He listened to the leaves. He smelled the sweet wood. He looked at the list in Mina’s little paws. “Okay,” he said softly. “Let’s follow the steps.”

Mina smiled. “Step one: sweep.”

Roo picked up the tiny broom. Swish, swish, swish. Dust danced like little clouds in the sunbeams. Benji held the dustpan. “I’ll catch the crumbs,” he said. Roo moved slower this time and watched where his tail went.

“Step two: lay the rug,” Mina read.

The rug was fuzzy and red like a warm apple. “Let’s do it together,” Benji said. “One, two, three—pull!”

They tugged gently. The rug made a soft whoosh and landed flat. Roo patted it with his paws. “So soft!” he giggled. His toes felt cozy.

“Step three: stack books,” Mina said with a happy squeak.

Roo wanted to make a tall tower. He looked at Mina’s list again. “Should we make a short stack first?” he asked.

“Yes,” Mina said. “Short stacks are safe.”

Roo and Benji stacked books in neat little piles. Thunk, thunk, thunk. The colors were bright: blue, green, yellow, and orange. Roo liked making the spines line up. It felt calm and right.

“Step four: hang the lantern,” Mina read.

Roo looked at the lantern. He remembered the wobble. “Can I try,” he asked, “if I follow the steps?”

Mina nodded. “Yes. Benji, please hold the stool. I will hold the list. Roo, climb slowly.”

Roo placed his paws on the stool. His heart thumped. Benji’s big paw held the stool steady. “I’ve got you,” Benji said.

Roo climbed one step. Then another. He listened to Mina’s gentle voice. “Left paw up. Right paw up. Clip the hook. Good job.”

Click! The lantern hung straight and bright. Warm light filled the treehouse like honey. Roo’s chest felt proud and calm, not jumpy.

“We did it together,” Mina said.

Roo smiled. “Following directions helped me do it right,” he said.

Just then, Tiko the tiny turtle peeked in. “Can I read too?” he asked in a shy whisper.

“Of course,” Benji said. “But we have a line. It’s fair to take turns.”

Roo remembered fairness. He bounced once, then stopped and folded his paws. “Mina first, because she made the list. Benji next, because he held the stool. I’ll go after. Tiko, you can sit with me, and we can share.”

Tiko’s eyes shone. “Thank you.”

Mina chose a book that smelled like fresh paper and new crayons. Her small voice read about stars and sleepy owls. Roo listened. He hugged a cushion. The lantern glowed warm on his fur. He felt safe, loved, and patient.

When it was Roo’s turn, he picked a book about kangaroo adventures. He sat close to Tiko and held the book so both could see. “Look,” Roo said softly, pointing to a picture. “Big jump!”

Tiko giggled. “I see.”

After reading, Roo stood up. “Friends,” he said, “I learned something. When I didn’t follow the steps, things almost fell. When I listened and followed directions, we were safe. It was fair. We helped each other. And it felt good.”

Mina clapped her tiny hands. “That is a strong lesson.”

Benji nodded. “Following directions shows respect.”

Roo’s tail swished gently, not wildly. He looked at the list and smiled. “Next time, I will look, listen, and then leap.”

Outside, the leaves whispered like they were clapping too. The treehouse glowed with light and soft rug and tidy books. It was ready for stories and friends.

Roo hugged Mina and Benji and patted Tiko’s shell. “Thank you for helping me learn,” he said.

“And thank you for helping us,” said Mina.

They all curled up in the cozy nook. The lantern hummed a soft, warm hum. The breeze smelled like pine and sunshine. Roo closed his eyes for a moment and felt peaceful.

Following directions made their treehouse safe, fair, and happy. And that was the best feeling of all.

The End.
