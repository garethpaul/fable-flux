# Daisy the Brave Little Fawn

In a cozy guest room with soft blue walls and fluffy white pillows, lived a gentle deer fawn named Daisy. She had the most beautiful spotted coat that sparkled like tiny stars, and the kindest brown eyes you ever did see.

One sunny morning, Daisy heard a tiny "squeak, squeak" coming from under the big wooden bed. She tiptoed closer on her delicate hooves, her heart beating fast like a little drum.

"Hello?" whispered Daisy softly. "Is someone there?"

Out peeked the tiniest mouse she had ever seen. The little mouse was shivering and looked very scared. His whiskers were dirty, and his fur was all messy.

"Please don't hurt me!" squeaked the mouse. "I'm just looking for somewhere safe to stay."

Daisy's heart filled with warmth. Even though she felt a little nervous about meeting someone new, she wanted to help. "I won't hurt you," she said gently. "My name is Daisy. What's yours?"

"I'm Pip," said the mouse, still hiding mostly under the bed. "I've been living outside, but it's getting so cold. I don't know what to do."

Daisy took a deep breath. She knew helping Pip was the right thing to do, even though it felt scary to take charge. "Pip, would you like to stay here with me? This guest room is warm and safe."

Pip's tiny eyes grew wide with hope. "Really? You would share your home with me?"

"Of course!" said Daisy, feeling braver with each word. "But first, let's get you all clean and cozy. Come with me to the bathroom."

Daisy led Pip to the small sink. She turned on the warm water very gently, making sure it wasn't too hot or too cold. "See how the water feels nice and warm?" she said, dipping her hoof in first to show Pip it was safe.

"Washing helps keep us healthy and feeling good," Daisy explained as she helped Pip clean his little paws and face. The warm water made Pip's fur fluffy and soft again.

"This feels wonderful!" squeaked Pip happily. "Thank you for being so kind to me, Daisy."

After Pip was all clean and dry, Daisy showed him around the guest room. She shared her favorite sunny spot by the window where they could watch the birds outside. She even gave Pip some of her soft blanket to make a cozy nest.

That evening, as they snuggled together in the warm guest room, Pip said, "Daisy, you're the bravest friend I've ever met. You weren't afraid to help someone you didn't know."

Daisy smiled, her heart glowing with happiness. "Being brave doesn't mean you're not scared, Pip. It means you do the right thing even when you feel nervous. And taking care of each other makes us both feel better."

From that day on, Daisy and Pip became the very best of friends. Daisy learned that being a good leader meant being kind and helpful, and Pip learned that keeping clean and tidy made him feel strong and confident.

Every night, they would tell each other stories about their adventures, and every morning, they would help each other stay clean and ready for a new day of friendship.

The End.
