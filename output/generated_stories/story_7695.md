# Rabbit Roo Builds a Green Home Office

Rabbit Roo lived in a cozy home office. The room had a small desk, soft lamp light, and a big window with sunshine. Roo loved to draw plans on paper and tap-tap with tiny tools. Roo was determined. When Roo had a problem, Roo said, “I can figure it out!”

One warm morning, Roo felt the air. It was stuffy and a little smelly, like old socks. The trash bin was full of crinkly paper and snack wrappers. The lamp was very bright, even though the sun was shining. The printer hummed and buzzed all day.

Roo wrinkled a nose. “Hmm. This doesn’t feel good. It doesn’t feel kind to our Earth,” Roo said. The room felt hot, the air felt heavy, and Roo felt worried.

A little breeze from the window whispered, “Shhhh.” Roo listened. “I want to help,” Roo said. “I want a clean, green home office.”

Roo made a plan. Roo loved plans! The plan had four steps. Roo held up a paw and counted.

“One, reduce,” said Roo.
“Two, reuse,” said Roo.
“Three, recycle,” said Roo.
“Four, rebuild,” said Roo, with a happy wiggle.

Roo clapped. “Let’s start.”

Step One: Reduce.
Roo looked at the bright lamp. Outside, the sun poured in like warm honey. Roo clicked the lamp off. Click! The room felt calm. Golden light slid across the desk. “Ahh,” Roo sighed. Roo also peeked at the printer. “Do I need to print?” asked Roo. “Nope!” Roo smiled and put the printer to sleep. The hum stopped. The room grew quiet and peaceful.

Step Two: Reuse.
Roo checked the trash bin. “So much paper,” Roo said. Roo felt the paper. It was smooth on one side and empty on the back. “I can use the backs!” Roo made a neat pile of “Back-to-Back Paper.” Roo found a glass jar from old carrot sauce. It was shiny and clear. “This can be a pencil jar,” Roo said. Clink, clink—the pencils stood tall and proud. Roo washed a small box that once held tea. “This can hold clips,” Roo said. Snap, snap—the clips went inside.

Step Three: Recycle.
Roo sorted the rest with careful paws. “Paper here,” said Roo, placing paper in a blue box. “Plastic here,” said Roo, putting clean wrappers in a green bin. “Metal here,” said Roo, dropping a can with a soft ping. The room smelled fresher already. Roo took a deep breath. “Mmm, clean air.”

A tiny helper popped in. It was Ladybug Dot, red and shiny. “Hello, Roo,” Dot said in a soft, buzzy voice. “What are you doing?”

“I am helping our Earth,” said Roo. “I am reducing, reusing, and recycling.”

Dot tilted her tiny head. “That is smart,” said Dot. “What is ‘rebuild’?”

Roo’s ears perked up. “Come and see!”

Step Four: Rebuild.
Roo looked at the cardboard box that used to hold printer paper. “I will build a sorter,” Roo said. Roo drew a simple plan with a big square and three small spaces. “One for paper, one for plastic, one for metal.”

Roo worked with care. Snip, snip went the scissors. Fold, fold went the flaps. Tape made a gentle rrrrip sound. Roo labeled each space with big letters. Roo even drew little pictures: a sheet for paper, a bottle for plastic, and a can for metal. Dot watched with wide eyes.

“You built a helpful thing from an old box,” said Dot.

“Yes!” said Roo. “I rebuilt!”

Roo placed the new sorter by the desk. It looked neat and kind. Roo also put a small plant by the window. The leaves were shiny and green. Roo patted the soil. “Hello, little plant,” Roo whispered. The plant smelled like rain and felt cool and damp. “You can help the air,” said Roo.

Dot buzzed in a happy circle. “Your room feels light,” Dot said.

Roo nodded. “Let’s test it,” Roo said. Roo sat at the desk and drew a new plan on back-to-back paper. The pencil made soft scratchy sounds. When Roo finished a snack, Roo put the wrapper in the right box. When clouds came, Roo turned on the lamp for a little while, then turned it off when the sun came back. Click. It all felt just right.

Soon, Mama Rabbit peeked in. She smelled the fresh air and smiled. “Roo, your home office looks wonderful,” she said. “What did you do?”

Roo sat up tall. “I used my brain and my heart,” Roo said. “I reduced the lights and the printing. I reused jars and boxes. I recycled paper, plastic, and metal. And I rebuilt an old box into a sorter. I even planted a plant!”

Mama hugged Roo. Her fur was warm and soft. “You are determined and kind,” she said. “You helped our Earth, and you helped our home.”

Roo’s whiskers twitched. “I can do this every day,” Roo said. “Small steps help in big ways.”

Dot landed on the plant leaf and waved a tiny leg. “I will tell my bug friends about your plan,” she said.

Roo giggled. “Thank you, Dot. Let’s all care for our Earth.”

The room was quiet, bright, and clean. The air felt fresh, like morning. Roo felt calm and proud. The plan had worked.

Lesson: When we think, sort, and build with care, we help our Earth. We can reduce, reuse, recycle, and rebuild at home, one small step at a time.

The End.
