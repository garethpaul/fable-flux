# Ollie Otter's Sharing Garden

On a bright, sunny morning, Ollie the otter splashed happily in his little kiddie pool in the backyard. The warm sun made the water sparkle like tiny diamonds, and Ollie loved how it felt on his soft, brown fur.

"What a perfect day to play!" Ollie said, clapping his little paws together.

Ollie had collected many wonderful treasures around his pool. There were smooth, colorful stones, shiny shells, bouncy balls, and even some pretty flowers he had picked. He had organized them all in neat little piles on his favorite blue towel.

"Look at all my beautiful things!" Ollie said proudly, running his paws through his collection.

Just then, he heard sad sniffling sounds coming from the fence. Ollie's ears perked up, and he saw three little woodland friends peeking through the fence holes.

"What's wrong?" Ollie asked gently, swimming closer to the fence.

Bella the bunny wiped her eyes. "We don't have any toys to play with today. We're so bored and sad."

Sammy the squirrel nodded. "We watched you playing with all those wonderful things, and we wished we could play too."

Little Pip the mouse squeaked softly, "Your pool looks so fun and cool on this hot day."

Ollie looked at his friends, then at his pile of treasures. His heart felt heavy seeing them so sad. He thought for a moment, tapping his chin with his paw.

"I have an idea!" Ollie said with a big smile. "Why don't you all come into my backyard? We can share everything together!"

"Really?" Bella asked, her ears standing up straight with excitement.

"Of course!" Ollie said. "Sharing makes everything more fun!"

Ollie opened the gate and welcomed his friends in. But then he realized something - three friends plus himself meant they needed to organize things differently.

"Let's be patient and take turns," Ollie said thoughtfully. "We can all fit in the pool if we're careful and kind to each other."

Ollie began sharing his treasures. He gave Bella some bouncy balls, handed Sammy the prettiest shells, and offered Pip the smoothest stones. He even picked fresh flowers for each friend.

"But Ollie," Sammy said, "now you don't have as many things for yourself."

Ollie's whiskers twitched with happiness. "That's okay! Watching you all smile makes me happier than keeping everything to myself."

The friends splashed gently in the pool together, taking turns diving and floating. They organized games with the stones and shells, and made beautiful flower crowns for each other. The sunny backyard filled with laughter and joy.

"This is the best day ever!" Pip squeaked happily.

"Thank you for sharing with us, Ollie," Bella said, giving him a warm hug.

As the sun began to set, painting the sky in pretty orange and pink colors, the friends helped Ollie organize all the toys back into neat piles.

"You know what?" Ollie said, looking around at his happy friends. "Tomorrow, let's all bring something to share! We can have an even bigger sharing party!"

All the friends cheered and promised to return the next day with their own special things to share.

That night, as Ollie curled up in his cozy bed, he felt warm and happy inside. He had learned that sharing his treasures didn't make him have less - it made him have more friends, more fun, and more joy than ever before.

The End.
