# Fern's Peaceful Morning

Little Fern the fawn stood in Grandmother Rabbit's warm kitchen, feeling very excited. The morning sun streamed through the yellow curtains, making everything glow like golden honey. The kitchen smelled wonderful - like fresh bread and sweet berries.

"Today is the big Forest Fair!" Fern said, bouncing on her tiny hooves. "I can't wait to run and play with everyone!"

Grandmother Rabbit smiled as she stirred a pot of oatmeal. "That sounds lovely, dear. But first, let's have a calm breakfast together."

Fern's heart was beating fast with excitement. She wanted to dash outside right away! But when she tried to eat her breakfast, her hooves were shaking so much that she spilled berries all over the wooden table.

"Oh no!" Fern cried. "I made a mess!"

"It's okay, little one," Grandmother Rabbit said gently. "When we feel very excited, sometimes our bodies get wiggly. Would you like to try something special with me?"

Fern nodded, her big brown eyes curious.

"Let's take three slow, deep breaths together," Grandmother Rabbit suggested. "Breathe in through your nose... smell the warm cinnamon in the air... and breathe out slowly through your mouth."

Fern tried it. In... she could smell the cozy kitchen scents. Out... she felt her shoulders relax a little.

"Again," Grandmother Rabbit said softly. "In... feel your feet on the warm floor. Out... let your whole body feel peaceful."

After the third breath, Fern felt much calmer. Her hooves stopped shaking, and she could eat her breakfast without spilling.

"That feels so much better!" Fern said. "My tummy feels warm and happy now."

"Being calm helps us enjoy things more," Grandmother Rabbit explained. "When we rush, we might miss the beautiful details around us."

At the Forest Fair, Fern remembered Grandmother Rabbit's lesson. When she felt excited about the games, she took deep breaths. When Bear cub wanted to cut in line for the acorn toss, Fern stayed calm and said kindly, "Everyone gets a turn. That's fair for all of us."

She met Ping the panda, who had traveled very far to visit their forest. "In my home in the mountains," Ping shared, "we have a saying: 'Still water reflects the sky clearly.' It means when we're calm inside, we can see the world more clearly."

Fern loved learning about Ping's faraway home. Being calm helped her listen carefully to the new friend's stories about bamboo forests and misty mountains.

Later, when some young squirrels were arguing loudly about sharing nuts, Fern showed them the breathing exercise. "Let's all take three calm breaths together," she suggested. Soon, the squirrels were sharing peacefully and laughing together.

As the sun began to set, Fern walked slowly back to Grandmother Rabbit's kitchen. She had run and played, but she had also taken time to breathe deeply, listen to new friends, and help others feel peaceful too.

"How was your day, dear?" Grandmother Rabbit asked.

"Wonderful!" Fern said, settling into her cozy chair. "I learned that being calm helps me be a better friend. When I breathe slowly and feel peaceful inside, I can share fairly, listen carefully, and help others feel happy too."

Grandmother Rabbit hugged her gently. "You discovered something very important today, little Fern. Calmness is like a gift we can give ourselves and share with the whole world."

The End.
