# Miri Mouse and the Gentle No

In a sunny garden, behind bright flowers and buzzing bees, there was a little shed. The shed smelled like fresh wood, dry straw, and tomato leaves. Inside, in a cozy corner near a smooth, round stone, lived a kind mouse named Miri.

Miri had soft gray fur and tiny pink ears. She loved to sweep her little space with a grass broom. She lined up her seeds in neat rows and folded a leaf blanket on her bed. It felt calm and safe.

One morning, the shed door creaked. Light spilled in. Miri’s whiskers twitched. “Good morning!” she chirped to herself. “Today I will plant my sprout and sip warm dandelion tea.” She patted the soil in a tiny pot and hummed a gentle tune.

Thump, thump, thump! A big brown boot landed near Miri’s corner. It was Gus the friendly goat, peeking in from the yard. He liked to visit and play. His voice was loud and happy. “Hello, Miri! Let’s play tag! Let’s run, jump, and bump!”

Miri smiled. She liked Gus. He was silly and kind. But she looked at her neat seeds and her tiny bed. She felt a flutter in her tummy. “This is my small space,” she thought. “I want it to stay safe and tidy.”

“Hi, Gus,” Miri said softly. “I’m glad to see you. I’m not ready to play bumping games in my space.”

Gus blinked. “Not ready? But I want to play now!” He trotted in, his hooves tap-tap-tapping on the floor. His tail swished. He bumped a bucket. Clang!

Miri’s ears drooped. The bucket noise was too loud. The air felt big and busy. She took a slow breath in. She let a slow breath out.

“I need to try my brave words,” Miri told herself. “Kind and clear.”

She stood up tall, as tall as a mouse can be. “Gus,” she said, “I like you. I like to play. But I do not like bumping in my corner. This corner is my space. I need it to be calm.”

Gus stopped. His ears wiggled. “Your space?” he asked.

“Yes,” Miri said. She held her paws close to her chest. “This is my nest and my bed and my seed shelf. I feel safe when it is quiet here. I need you to stay by the door for now.”

Gus shuffled his hooves. “Oh,” he said. His voice got smaller. “I did not know. I just felt so bouncy!”

Miri nodded. “I understand. Thank you for listening.” Her words were gentle, and her whiskers relaxed. “We can still play. We can choose a game that is gentle and outside.”

Gus looked at the open door and the warm grass beyond. He took a breath. “Okay,” he said. “We can play outside. But first, can I see your sprout? I will not bump.”

Miri thought for a moment. She checked in with her body. Her tummy felt calm now. “I will show you from here,” she said. “Please stay by the line.” She pointed to a little crack in the floor. “This is the line.”

Gus nodded. He stood by the crack. He lowered his head and peeked. “Oh! A tiny green leaf!”

Miri smiled. “Yes. It is small and brave.” She poured a sip of water. Plip, plip. “Just like my words.”

Gus smiled too. “Your words are small and brave?”

“Yes,” Miri said. “A gentle no can be brave. It keeps me safe. It tells you what I need.”

Gus’s tail swished in a slow, happy way. “I like your gentle no,” he said. “It helps me know.”

The shed felt calm again. The sun made warm stripes on the floor. The breeze smelled like mint. Miri’s heart felt steady.

“Would you like to help me carry this leaf cup outside?” Miri asked. “Then we can play a quiet game.”

Gus grinned. “I can carry it on my nose.” He lowered his soft nose, and Miri set the little leaf cup on top. They walked side by side into the garden. Crunch, crunch went the dry leaves. Buzz went a bee. Cheep, cheep went a bird.

“Let’s play ‘Follow the Slow Steps,’” Miri said. “We walk slowly. We stop when we hear a sound. We breathe. Then we go again.”

Gus tried it. Step, step. Stop. “Buzz!” They breathed in. They breathed out. Step, step. Stop. “Cheep!” They giggled.

After the game, they sat by the mint. Gus flicked his ears. “Miri,” he said, “what if I forget the line? What if I feel bouncy again?”

Miri thought. She picked up a smooth stick and drew a small heart just inside the shed door. “This is my heart spot,” she said. “When you see the heart, remember: ask first.”

Gus nodded. “Ask first,” he said. “May I come in?”

Miri looked at her body again. She felt calm. “Yes,” she said, “for a little visit. We can sit and sip dandelion tea. Then we play outside.”

They had sweet, warm tea. They talked in quiet voices. When the cup was empty, Gus stood. “Thank you for telling me,” he said. “I want to be a good friend.”

“You are,” said Miri. “Good friends listen. Good friends ask. Good friends say gentle no’s when they need.”

Gus smiled. “And good friends play slow steps.” They giggled and went back to the sun.

That night, Miri tucked in her leaf blanket. Her seeds were in neat rows. The shed smelled like mint and tea. She felt proud and peaceful. “I can say what I need,” she whispered. “I can be kind and brave.”

Lesson: Your space and your feelings matter. You can use calm, clear words to set a boundary. You can say a gentle no. Good friends will listen, and you can still have fun together.

The End.
