# Fern’s New Tastes

Fern was a gentle deer fawn with soft, speckled fur and curious eyes. Today, Fern was visiting a friendly house. The guest room was quiet and cozy. A big window let in sunlight that made warm patches on the rug. The air smelled like clean sheets and a tiny bit like cinnamon.

Fern peeked around the room. There was a fluffy chair, a small bed with a quilt, and a basket filled with colorful things. Her ears twitched. “What is that?” she whispered.

A smiling voice answered, “Welcome, Fern!” It was Mira, the child who lived in the house. Mira had bright eyes and a calm smile. She held a small tray. On it were little bowls with green leaves, orange sticks, and red slices.

Fern sniffed the air. Crunchy, fresh, and sweet smells tickled her nose. “I only like my usual clover,” Fern said softly. “I’m not sure about new things.”

Mira sat on the rug so she was small and not scary. “That’s okay,” she said. “We can try with tiny bites. We can be open and brave together.” She wiggled her toes in the sunlight. “We can also go outside after, if you want. The backyard is breezy and fun.”

Fern’s tail flicked. “Outside sounds nice,” she said, thinking of soft grass and gentle wind. But she still looked at the bowls with a little worry.

Mira nodded kindly. “Let’s start with sniffing,” she said. “No pressure.” She lifted a crisp green leaf. “This is spinach. It smells like rain and garden.”

Fern leaned in. Sniff, sniff. The leaf smelled earthy, like morning dew on the forest floor. “It smells like a tiny forest!” she said, surprised.

Mira giggled. “Yes! Now a tiny taste?” She tore a very small piece and held it out.

Fern opened her mouth just a bit. Crunch. The leaf was cool and soft. “Hmm,” Fern hummed. “It’s gentle. Not scary at all.”

Next, Mira picked up an orange stick. “Carrot,” she said. “It is crunchy like a twig but sweet, too.”

Fern liked the word sweet. She tried a tiny nibble. Crunch! The sound was loud in the quiet guest room. Fern’s ears perked up. “Oh! It goes crunch like dry leaves. And it tastes like sunshine.”

Mira smiled wide. “You are very good at trying.”

Fern felt warm and proud. “I am trying,” she said. “I am being open.”

Mira offered a red slice. “Apple,” she said. “It is juicy.”

Fern touched it with her nose first. The smell was flowery and bright. She took a small bite. The juice dripped a little on her chin. “Cold and sweet!” Fern said, licking her lip. She giggled.

“Let’s have some water,” Mira said. She poured water into a tiny bowl. The water made a friendly glug-glug sound. Fern lapped it up. It tasted fresh, like a creek.

Fern looked at the basket in the guest room. A little ball peeked out, red and rubbery. She nudged it with her nose. “What about outside now?” she asked, eyes shining.

Mira nodded. “Yes! Outdoor time helps our bodies feel strong.” She opened the door. A soft breeze came in, carrying the smell of grass and a hint of flowers.

They stepped onto the back lawn. The sun felt warm, and the dirt felt cool under Fern’s hooves. Birdsong rose and fell like tiny bells. In the shade, the grass tickled her belly when she lay down to roll. “This is lovely,” Fern sighed.

“Let’s play a gentle game,” Mira said. “We roll the ball slowly and chase it.” She set the red ball on the grass and nudged it. It rolled with a soft whoop. Fern trotted after it, ears up, tail flicking. She nudged it back. Boop! Mira laughed and clapped.

They played in the breeze. Fern felt the wind brush her fur and heard leaves whisper. She smelled earth and flowers and a faint whiff of apples from the kitchen window. Her legs felt strong and happy.

After a while, they rested under a tree. Mira passed Fern another tiny piece of carrot. “Snack break,” she said.

Fern munched thoughtfully. “I’m glad I tried new things,” she said. “I thought I would only like clover. But spinach is gentle. Carrot is sunny. Apple is juicy.”

Mira nodded. “Being open helps us learn. Trying new foods can help our bodies grow. Going outside helps our hearts feel calm. And listening to new ideas helps our minds.”

Fern blinked slowly. “Sometimes I feel unsure,” she admitted. “New things make my tummy flutter.”

Mira patted the grass. “Me too, sometimes,” she said. “We can start small. We can sniff, we can take tiny bites, and we can take tiny steps. We can say, ‘I will try.’”

Fern smiled, showing her small teeth. “I will try,” she repeated.

They went back inside to the cozy guest room. The sun had moved, and the warm patches were in new places. The room smelled like cinnamon and fresh air now. Fern curled up on the rug, feeling calm and brave.

“I learned a lot today,” Fern said. “I learned to be open. I learned that outdoor play feels good. I learned that colorful foods can be yummy and helpful.”

Mira tucked a small blanket around Fern. “You did great,” she whispered.

Fern closed her eyes. Her heart felt steady and light.

Today’s lesson: When we are open to new things, we find new joys—on our plates, in the breeze, and in our hearts.

The End.
