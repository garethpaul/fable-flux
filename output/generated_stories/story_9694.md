# Milo the Brave Monkey’s Nursery Day

Milo was a courageous monkey with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a comfortable nursery full of cozy rugs, twinkly lights, and shelves of colorful toys. The room smelled like warm bananas and clean soap. Sunlight made friendly squares on the floor.

“Good morning, Milo,” sang Miss Lila, the caregiver with the gentle voice. “It’s time to start our day.”

Milo stretched his arms and wiggled his toes. “I can do it!” he said. He loved to try things by himself. He brushed his fuzzy teeth with a tiny toothbrush that tasted like sweet mint. “Brush, brush, brush,” he hummed. The soft bristles tickled his gums. He washed his hands in warm water. Splash! Splish! He felt proud. Self-care made him feel strong.

At the breakfast table, bowls clinked. Milo smelled oatmeal with cinnamon and bananas. His tummy rumbled. He used a small spoon and scooped carefully. “Slow and steady,” he whispered. Oops! A little drip slid down his chin.

Miss Lila smiled. “Napkin time, Milo.”

Milo dabbed his chin. “I’m taking care of me,” he said with a grin. His friend, Pip the parrot, squawked, “Share, share, share?” Pip peered at the banana slices.

“Yes, Pip. We can share,” Milo said, moving his bowl closer. “Let’s split it.” They counted together, “One, two, you, me.” Sharing felt warm, like the sun on his fur.

After breakfast, Miss Lila clapped softly. “Circle time! Today we learn about caring for our bodies.” She held up a poster with pictures: washing hands, brushing teeth, drinking water, asking for help.

Milo sat up tall. “I know about that!” he said. “First, we wash. Then, we brush. Then, we drink.” He patted his water bottle. “Sip, sip.” Cool water felt fresh on his tongue.

“Wonderful knowledge,” said Miss Lila. “Knowing what to do helps us stay healthy.”

Next came the Balance Path. Pillows, soft blocks, and a wiggly line of tape made a fun trail. “Use your eyes. Use your feet. Use your breath,” Miss Lila reminded them.

Milo placed one foot on a pillow. Squish! He balanced his tail for steady help. He took a slow breath in and out. “I can go carefully,” he told himself. Pip fluttered beside him, “You’ve got this!”

Halfway across, Milo felt wobbly. His tummy did a tiny flip. He paused and hugged himself. “I need a moment,” he said in a clear voice. “Please wait.”

Miss Lila nodded. “Thank you for using your words. That is self-advocacy.”

Milo stood still, counting softly, “One, two, three.” He looked where to step next. He listened to his breathing. When he felt ready, he took another step. Tap, squish, step. He reached the end and smiled so big his cheeks felt round. “I did it with care and patience!”

Story time came with a big, soft blanket and the sweet smell of crayons. Everyone colored a picture of a healthy helper. Milo drew himself washing his hands. He added blue bubbles and a shiny gold star. “This is me taking care of me,” he said proudly.

Then it was quiet rest. Milo lay on his cozy mat and hugged his striped blanket. He whispered to Miss Lila, “I feel thirsty.”

“Thank you for telling me,” she replied, handing him his bottle. The water was cool and soothing. “You listened to your body. That’s smart self-care.”

Later, a small scrape happened. Pip bumped a block and said, “Ow.” Milo heard and hurried over. “Are you okay?” he asked gently. He remembered the poster. “We can wash and tell a grown-up.” He got a soft cloth and called, “Miss Lila, Pip needs help.” Together they cleaned the tiny scrape. Pip chirped, “Thank you.” Milo smiled. “We take care of ourselves and each other.”

Snack time brought crunchy carrot sticks and hummus. “Crunch, crunch,” went Milo. He offered the bowl. “Would you like to share?” he asked the shy turtle, Tilda. “You can say yes or no.”

Tilda peeked out. “Yes, please.” They dipped slowly so the hummus didn’t plop. Milo showed Tilda how to wipe a drip with a napkin. “We keep things tidy,” he said. Tilda beamed. “I learned something new!”

Before going home, Miss Lila asked, “What did we learn today?”

Milo held his tail high. “I learned to care for my body. I brush, wash, drink water, and rest. I use my words when I need a break. I move slowly and with balance. I share and ask, and I help friends.”

Miss Lila gave him a sticker shaped like a little heart. “Your courage helps you try. Your care helps you grow.”

Milo hugged his backpack. The zipper made a soft zzzzip. He waved to his friends. “Tomorrow I will keep listening to my body,” he said. “I can take care of me!”

The End.
