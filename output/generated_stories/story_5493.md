# Toby's Musical Garden Band

The old garden shed smelled like fresh soil and sunshine. Toby the wise tortoise lived there happily among the flower pots and watering cans. His green shell sparkled in the morning light streaming through the dusty window.

"Tweet! Tweet!" chirped the sparrows outside. "Buzz! Buzz!" hummed the busy bees. But today, something was different. The garden sounds were all mixed up and loud!

Toby poked his wrinkled head out of his cozy corner. "Oh my," he said in his slow, gentle voice. "What's all this noise about?"

Outside the shed, he saw Rosie the robin flying in circles. "I want to sing my morning song NOW!" she chirped loudly.

Benny the bee zoomed around angrily. "No! I need to buzz to my flowers FIRST!" he buzzed.

Freddy the frog hopped up and down by the pond. "Ribbit! I want to make music with my splashing! Everyone be quiet!"

All the garden friends were making noise at once. No one was listening. No one was happy.

Toby stretched his neck up tall. "Friends," he called in his wise, calm voice. "May I help?"

The garden grew quiet. Everyone loved Toby. He always knew what to do.

"We all want to make beautiful music," Toby said slowly. "But we're all trying to do it at the same time. How can we work together?"

Rosie fluffed her red feathers. "But I sing the prettiest songs!"

"And I make the best buzzing sounds!" said Benny.

"My splashing is the most fun!" croaked Freddy.

Toby smiled his gentle smile. "What if we take turns? What if we make music together instead of alone?"

The friends looked at each other. That sounded interesting.

"Let's try this," suggested Toby. "Rosie, you sing first. Then Benny, you buzz along. Then Freddy, you add your splashing rhythm."

Rosie tilted her little head. "But what if they don't like my song?"

"Let's ask them," said Toby kindly. "Benny and Freddy, would you like to hear Rosie's song first?"

Benny's tiny wings fluttered. "Yes! I love robin songs!"

Freddy hopped excitedly. "Me too! Then can I splash?"

"Of course," said Toby. "We'll all take turns and listen to each other."

Rosie began to sing her sweet morning song. "La-la-la-tweet-tweet!" Her voice was like silver bells.

When she finished, Benny buzzed a happy harmony. "Buzz-buzz-hum-hum!" His sound was warm and fuzzy.

Then Freddy splashed in the pond with perfect timing. "Splash-ribbit-splash!" His rhythm made everyone want to dance.

"Now," said Toby, clapping his flippers together, "let's try all together!"

The three friends made the most beautiful garden music. Rosie's singing, Benny's buzzing, and Freddy's splashing created a wonderful symphony.

Other garden creatures gathered around to listen. Butterflies danced. Flowers seemed to sway. Even the old garden shed seemed to hum along.

"This is much better than making noise alone," said Rosie happily.

"We sound amazing together!" buzzed Benny.

"And everyone gets to join in!" croaked Freddy with joy.

Toby beamed with pride. "When we talk together and listen to each other, we can solve any problem. And we can make something even more beautiful than before."

From that day on, the garden friends held morning concerts together. They learned that sharing and taking turns made everything more fun. And Toby's shed became the perfect place for their daily music meetings.

The End.
