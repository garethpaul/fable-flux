# The Little Mouse and the Bathroom Plan

Squeak was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and shiny eyes. He lived with his mouse family behind the warm wall near a big, bright bathroom. The bathroom smelled like soap and fresh towels. The floor was cool and smooth. The sink hummed with drips. Drip, drip, drip.

One sunny morning, Squeak peeked out. “Today I will be brave,” he whispered. “Mama gave me a list. I will follow every step.” He gently patted the tiny paper in his paw. The list said: 
1. Look and listen first. 
2. Go slow. 
3. Stay dry. 
4. Ask for help if needed.

Squeak’s tail wiggled. “I can do it,” he said.

He scurried to the bathroom door. The big room shone with light. He heard the soft whoosh of the fan. He smelled lemon soap and fluffy towels. He saw a shiny toothbrush and a little blue cup by the sink.

His friend, Dot the ladybug, buzzed down and landed on the door frame. “Hi, Squeak!” Dot chirped. “What is your plan?”

Squeak held up the list. “I will follow directions. I want to bring back a tiny square of tissue for my baby sister. She needs a soft pillow for her nap.”

Dot nodded. “A good plan. Remember safety.”

Squeak smiled. “Step one: Look and listen.” He peeked around the corner. No people. No big feet. “All clear,” he said.

“Step two: Go slow.” Squeak took small steps. Pat, pat, pat. His paws felt the cool tile. He listened. Drip, drip, drip from the faucet. Whoosh from the fan. He stayed calm and brave.

“Step three: Stay dry,” Squeak read. He saw a shiny puddle near the tub. It looked slippery. He did not run through it. He looked for a safe path. He saw a dry bath mat like a green island.

Dot pointed with a tiny leg. “The mat looks safe.”

Squeak nodded. “I will use a strategy.” He took a deep breath. He hopped from one dry spot to the next. Tap, tap, tap. He made it to the mat. “I did it! I followed the step.”

He reached the tall tissue box on the counter. It was very high. He could not reach it. He felt a little worried. His whiskers trembled. “Step four: Ask for help if needed,” he read.

“Dot,” he said, “can you help me, please?”

Dot smiled. “Yes. I can fly up and pull one corner down. But we must be careful. We must follow directions too.”

Squeak nodded. “We will use teamwork. I will hold the tissue so it does not fall into the sink.”

Dot flew up, wings buzzing. She tugged the corner of a tissue. Squeak stood on the soap tray, but he remembered a rule. “No standing on slippery soap,” the list in his mind said. He paused. “Wait, Dot. The soap is slippery. I should find a better place.”

He climbed down and looked around. He saw a dry, sturdy cup. He moved it close to the counter edge and climbed up slowly. His paws held firm. “This is safer,” he said.

Dot tugged again. The tissue slipped down, soft as a cloud. It brushed Squeak’s nose. “Achoo!” he sneezed. They giggled.

Then Squeak heard it. Footsteps. Thump, thump. A tall person came into the bathroom. The person turned the faucet. Water rushed. The sink began to fill.

Squeak’s heart beat fast. “We need to be safe,” he whispered. “Follow the plan.”

He held the tissue close and stayed still. Then he remembered another good step: move away from danger. He scanned the path. The bathtub puddle was bigger now. The fan whooshed. The person hummed softly.

Squeak whispered, “Dot, we will wait. Then we go when it is quiet and dry.”

They waited. They breathed slow. The person washed hands, then turned off the faucet. The water stopped. The person wiped the counter. The puddles shrank. The footsteps went away. The door clicked.

Squeak smiled. “Now we go.” He climbed down carefully, using the cup like a ladder. He stepped on the dry mat. He looked and listened again. All clear. He walked around the tiny shiny spots. He did not run. He held the tissue high so it stayed clean and dry.

Back at the warm wall, Mama Mouse met them. “Squeak, you are back!” she said. She touched the soft tissue. “It is perfect for your baby sister.”

Squeak’s baby sister snuggled on her new pillow. She squeaked a happy squeak. Squeak felt proud and calm.

Mama asked, “How did you do it?”

Squeak held up his list. “I followed directions. I looked and listened. I went slow. I stayed dry. I asked for help. I used a good plan.”

Dot buzzed and added, “And he was brave and safe.”

Mama smiled. “Following directions helps us stay safe. Using a strategy helps us solve problems. Asking for help shows strength.”

Squeak nodded. “I learned that following directions makes hard jobs easier. It keeps me and my friends safe. I can be brave and careful at the same time.”

He tucked the little list into a tiny pocket. “I will keep my plan,” he said. “Next time, I will follow it again.”

The End.
