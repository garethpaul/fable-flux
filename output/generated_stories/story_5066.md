# Fern Fawn Says “I’m Sorry”

Fern was a gentle deer fawn with soft, speckled fur. One rainy afternoon, Fern visited a bright guest room at her friend Lily’s house. The guest room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. A small lamp made warm light. A basket of toys sat by the bed with a fluffy blue blanket.

“Welcome, Fern!” said Lily, a cheerful bunny. Her long ears wiggled. “You can play while I help Mama in the kitchen.”

Fern’s hooves clicked softly on the wooden floor. She saw blocks, a puzzle, and a round, red ball. “Ooh,” Fern whispered, nose twitching. “The ball looks fun!” The ball felt smooth and cool when she nudged it. It made a soft thump against the rug.

Fern rolled the ball gently at first. Thump. Thump. It made a funny sound, and Fern giggled. “Just a little more,” she said. She pushed harder.

The ball rolled fast—whoosh! It bumped the small lamp. The lamp wobbled. “Oh!” Fern gasped. The lamp tipped and fell onto the bedside table. Clink! The lampshade slipped off and landed on the floor with a soft bump.

Fern’s tummy felt fluttery, like tiny wings. Her ears drooped. “I didn’t mean to,” she whispered. She stood very still and listened. The rain tapped the window. Her heart went thump-thump.

Lily hopped in. “Fern? Are you okay?” Lily’s nose twitched as she looked around. She saw the lamp. She saw the lampshade. Her eyes grew wide.

“I’m sorry,” Fern blurted, her voice small. “I rolled the ball too fast. I made a mess.”

Lily looked at Fern’s worried face. She took a slow breath. “Thank you for telling me,” Lily said kindly. “Are you hurt?”

Fern shook her head. “No. But I feel sad. I didn’t mean to make trouble.”

Lily came closer and gave Fern a gentle hug. “Everyone makes mistakes,” Lily said softly. “When we say ‘I’m sorry,’ we help make things better. Let’s see what we can do.”

Fern blinked. “We can fix it?”

“Let’s think first,” Lily said. “We can use our thinking ears and eyes.” She put a paw to her chin. Fern copied her and put a hoof to her chin too.

“What is the problem?” Lily asked.

“The lamp fell, and the lampshade is on the floor,” Fern said.

“What can we try?” Lily asked.

“We can pick up the lampshade,” Fern said. “And we can place the lamp safely.”

“Good plan,” Lily said with a smile. “I’ll hold the lamp. Can you lift the shade?”

Fern nodded. The lampshade felt smooth and papery. It smelled a little like dust. She lifted it carefully and set it on the table. Lily held the lamp steady and slid the shade back on with a soft swish. Click! The lamp stood tall again.

Lily tapped the switch. The warm light glowed. The guest room looked cozy once more. Fern’s tummy felt calmer.

Fern looked at the red ball. “I should not roll the ball near the lamp,” she said. “It is not a safe place.”

“That’s smart thinking,” Lily said. “The rug by the door has lots of space. And we can roll softly.”

Fern nudged the ball very gently. Thump… slow and quiet. She smiled. “Better!”

Just then, Lily’s mama came by. “Hello, little ones,” she said with a friendly smile. “I heard a clink. Is everything alright?”

Fern took a brave breath. “I’m sorry,” she said, looking up with honest eyes. “I rolled the ball too fast, and the lamp fell. We fixed it, and we are playing gently now.”

Lily’s mama knelt and patted Fern’s shoulder. “Thank you for telling the truth and saying sorry,” she said warmly. “That is brave and kind. You also used good thinking to solve the problem. I’m proud of you both.”

Fern’s chest felt warm, like sunshine after rain. “Thank you for listening,” she said softly.

Lily’s mama placed a basket on the bed. “Here are soft beanbags,” she said. “They don’t roll too far. You can toss them onto the blanket island!”

Fern’s eyes lit up. “A blanket island?” she asked.

Lily giggled. “Let’s be open to new games!” She spread the fluffy blue blanket on the rug. “We toss the beanbags onto the island. If they miss, we try again.”

Fern picked a green beanbag. It felt squishy, like a little pillow. “Ready,” she said. She tossed it gently. Plop! It landed on the blanket island. “I did it!”

“Great job,” Lily cheered. “Your gentle toss was helpful and safe.”

Fern passed a beanbag to Lily. “Your turn. I can help by holding the edge of the blanket.”

They played and laughed. The room felt bright and happy. The rain outside sounded like soft drumming. Fern felt light and calm.

When it was time to rest, Fern whispered, “I learned something today. When I make a mistake, I can say, ‘I’m sorry.’ I can think of safe ways to fix it. I can try a new idea.”

Lily nodded. “That is open-minded and kind.”

Fern smiled. “And helpful too.”

They bumped noses, giggling.

The End.
