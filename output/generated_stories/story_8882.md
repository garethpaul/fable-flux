# Fawn's Rooftop Garden

Little Fawn loved to explore. One sunny morning, she discovered a tall ladder leaning against a big building. Her curious brown eyes sparkled as she carefully climbed up, up, up to the rooftop.

"Wow!" Fawn gasped. The rooftop was covered with colorful flower pots, vegetable boxes, and tiny trees. It smelled like fresh dirt and sweet flowers. "What a beautiful garden!"

Suddenly, she heard angry voices coming from behind some tall sunflowers.

"You can't plant those weird purple flowers here!" squeaked a small voice.

"This is OUR garden!" chirped another voice loudly.

Fawn's soft ears twitched as she tiptoed closer. Behind the sunflowers, she saw three little animals arguing. There was Rabbit with fluffy white fur, Squirrel with a bushy gray tail, and Mouse with tiny pink ears.

In the middle sat Skunk, holding a small pot of beautiful purple flowers. Her black and white fur looked sad, and her eyes were full of tears.

"But I just wanted to help make the garden prettier," Skunk whispered. "These are my grandmother's special flowers."

"No way!" Rabbit stamped his foot. "You smell funny, and your flowers are the wrong color!"

Fawn felt her heart hurt seeing Skunk so sad. She knew this wasn't fair. Taking a deep breath, she stepped forward on her thin, wobbly legs.

"Hello everyone," Fawn said gently. "I'm Fawn. I couldn't help but hear you talking."

The three animals looked surprised to see the spotted deer fawn.

"Skunk wants to add her flowers to your garden," Fawn continued. "That sounds wonderful to me. Gardens are most beautiful when they have lots of different colors and smells."

"But she's different!" Squirrel chattered nervously.

Fawn nodded thoughtfully. "Yes, we're all different. I have spots, you have a fluffy tail, Rabbit has long ears, and Mouse has tiny paws. Different can be beautiful."

She walked over to Skunk's purple flowers and sniffed them gently. "These smell like sweet honey! And look how they shimmer in the sunlight."

Fawn turned to the other animals. "When we share our garden with everyone, it becomes more special. Each of us can bring something unique."

Mouse's whiskers twitched as she thought. "Well... I do like honey smells."

"And purple IS a pretty color," Rabbit admitted quietly.

Squirrel's tail swished. "Maybe... maybe different isn't so bad."

Fawn smiled warmly. "Why don't we all work together? We can find the perfect spot for Skunk's flowers."

Soon, all five friends were digging and planting together. Skunk's purple flowers looked perfect next to Rabbit's orange carrots and Mouse's yellow daisies. The garden smelled like a rainbow of wonderful scents.

"Thank you, Fawn," Skunk said, her eyes now bright and happy. "You helped them see that being different is okay."

"We all have a responsibility to be kind," Fawn said, brushing dirt off her hooves. "When we include everyone and treat them fairly, we make the world more beautiful."

As the sun set, the five friends sat together admiring their colorful garden. They realized that their differences made them stronger as a team.

"Tomorrow, let's plant more flowers together!" Mouse squeaked excitedly.

"Yes!" they all agreed, already planning their next garden adventure.

Fawn learned that standing up for what's right and including everyone makes the best kind of magic.

The End.
