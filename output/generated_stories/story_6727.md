# Joey Jumps into Helping

A joyful kangaroo joey named Kip hopped into the dining room with a happy thump-thump. The room smelled like warm toast and sweet jam. Sunlight made little golden squares on the floor. Kip’s tail made a soft swish as he looked at the big table. Plates, cups, and napkins waited in a neat stack.

“Mama, can I help?” Kip asked, his ears perked up.

Mama Roo smiled. “Yes, Kip. Let’s set the table together.”

Kip clapped his small paws. “I can do it! I can do it all by myself!”

He grabbed a pile of napkins. They felt soft like clouds. He hopped around the table. “One napkin for you, one napkin for me,” he sang. But when he got excited, he hopped too high. Thump! The napkins fluttered down like little butterflies and landed everywhere.

“Oh no,” said Kip. “I made a napkin storm.”

Mama Roo came close and touched Kip’s shoulder. “It’s okay, honey. We can fix it. Helping is a skill. Skills take practice.”

“What is a skill?” Kip asked, tilting his head.

“A skill is something you learn to do,” Mama Roo said. “You learn with steps. You learn with calm. You learn with trying again.”

Kip took a deep breath. He felt the air go cool in his nose. “I can try again,” he whispered.

“Let’s make a plan,” Mama Roo said. “First, we talk. Then, we do. Communication helps our plan.”

Kip nodded. “Talk first. Do next.”

Mama Roo pointed to the table. “We need plates, cups, and napkins. We set them in the same place each time. That helps us remember.”

Kip’s eyes sparkled. “I can remember! Plate in the middle, cup at the top, napkin on the left!”

“You’ve got it,” said Mama Roo.

Kip picked up one plate. It was cool and smooth. He set it in the middle of the first chair spot. Then he put a cup at the top. He placed a napkin on the left. “I did it!” he giggled.

“Great job,” said Mama Roo. “Now, do it again. Step by step.”

Kip went to the next seat. “Plate, cup, napkin,” he said softly. His voice sounded like a tiny drum, tap-tap-tap. He felt proud inside, warm like sunshine.

But at the third seat, he mixed up the cup and the napkin. He frowned. “Oops.”

Mama Roo knelt next to him. “What can we do when we get mixed up?”

Kip thought. He tapped his chin. “We can stop. We can look. We can switch!”

“Yes,” Mama Roo said. “You understood the steps. You can fix it.”

Kip carefully switched the cup and napkin. “I fixed it!” He smiled so big his cheeks felt round and bouncy.

Soon, all the plates, cups, and napkins were in place—almost. One spot was empty.

Kip’s ears drooped. “I missed one.”

“That’s okay,” said Mama Roo. “You noticed. Noticing is a skill, too.”

Kip hopped to the cabinet. He opened the door slowly. The wood made a tiny creek sound. Inside, the last plate shone like a moon. He lifted it with two careful paws. “Slow and steady,” he said. He set it down in the last spot with a soft clink.

“Ta-da!” Kip cheered.

“You set the table,” Mama Roo said. “How do you feel?”

“I feel proud,” Kip said. His tail gave a happy thump. “I used steps. I used calm. I used fixing.”

Mama Roo nodded. “You used creativity, too.”

Kip blinked. “Creativity?”

“You made a little song,” Mama Roo said. “Plate, cup, napkin. That helps your brain remember. That is creative.”

Kip grinned. “Plate, cup, napkin,” he sang again in a gentle tune. He spun in a tiny circle, his feet whispering on the floor.

Just then, Kip saw spoons. “Oh! We need spoons for jam!”

“Good noticing,” Mama Roo said. “How many do we need?”

Kip counted seats. “One, two, three, four.” He tapped each spot with his paw. “Four spoons.”

He picked up the spoons. They were cool and shiny. “One for you, one for me,” he whispered. He set a spoon by each napkin, the metal clinking softly.

Kip stepped back and looked at the table. Everything looked neat and ready. The room felt calm and cozy. He smelled toast again. His tummy made a little rumble. “I’m hungry!”

Mama Roo laughed. “Me too. Before we eat, let’s check our skills. What did we learn?”

Kip held up his fingers. “Talk first. Do next. Steps help. Slow and steady. Notice and fix. Use a song.”

“Wonderful,” Mama Roo said. “That is skill-building. You understand the job. You used creativity. You used communication.”

Kip put his paw in Mama’s paw. “I helped. I can help again tomorrow.”

“Yes,” Mama Roo said. “Skills grow every day.”

They sat down together. Kip spread jam on his toast. The jam was sweet and sticky. He took a bite. “Mmm,” he said. “Helping tastes good.”

Mama Roo winked. “Helping feels good, too.”

Kip wiggled with joy. “I’m a helper joey! Plate, cup, napkin!”

And the dining room seemed to hum with his happy little song.

The End.
