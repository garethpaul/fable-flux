# Binky Beaver’s Bright Breaks

Binky was a busy beaver with a bright yellow hat. He loved his basement lab. It was cool and quiet. The floor felt smooth under his little paws. Lights blinked. Buttons clicked. Paints, paper, and glue sat on a big table. It smelled like fresh paper and oranges from his water cup.

“Click, tap, snip!” Binky sang as he worked. “I will make art that moves!” He was building a spinning rainbow wheel for the wall. He was also drawing a big sun with smiling eyes. He wanted to finish everything today.

Binky’s friend, Dot the duck, waddled down the steps. “Hi, Binky! Quack-quack! Your lab looks busy,” she said. Her feathers were soft and shiny. She carried a small bell on a ribbon.

“I am a busy beaver,” said Binky. “I must finish. No time to stop!”

Dot tilted her head. “Even busy helpers need healthy breaks. Water, stretch, and snack. Then art gets better.”

Binky shook his head. “I can work and work. See?” He pressed a button. The rainbow wheel spun. “Wheee!” he cheered. The colors swirled like candy.

Bzzzt! The wheel slowed and squeaked. Binky frowned. “Hmm. Maybe more glue.” He squeezed the bottle hard. “Squish-squish!” Glue dripped on his paw.

Dot rang her bell. Ding! “Water break?” she asked.

“No time,” Binky said. He rushed to his crayons. “Color, color, fast!” His paw felt sticky. His eyes felt heavy. His tummy made a little rumble. Grumble-grumble.

Binky kept working. “Snip, clip, twist.” His shoulders felt tight. His back felt stiff. His tail drooped. He rubbed his eyes. The room looked fuzzy.

Dot waddled closer and spoke gently. “Binky, your body is telling you something.”

“My body?” Binky blinked. “What does it say?”

Dot put a wing on Binky’s shoulder. “It says, ‘I need a sip, a stretch, and a snack.’ Healthy habits help art. They help brains think and paws make.”

Binky looked at his sticky paw. He sniffed. “I do feel a little wobbly,” he said quietly. “And my tummy is noisy.”

“Teamwork?” asked Dot. “We can make a Healthy Helper Plan.”

Binky’s ears perked up. “A plan? I love plans!”

Dot smiled. “Step one: water. Step two: wiggle and stretch. Step three: snack. Step four: wash-up. Then we make art again—with happy bodies.”

Binky nodded. “Let’s try!”

They clinked cups. Sip, sip. The cool water tasted sweet and clean. Binky sighed. “Ahh.” He felt the coolness in his mouth and tummy.

“Stretch time!” Dot said. They reached up high. “Hello, ceiling!” They touched their toes. “Hello, floor!” They rolled their shoulders. “Roll, roll.” They wiggled like noodles. “Wiggle-wiggle!” They giggled.

“Snack time,” Dot said. She set out apple slices and crunchy carrots. “Crunch!” went Binky. The apple was juicy and crisp. The carrots were loud and fun. Crunch-crunch! His tummy was happy now.

“Wash-up,” Dot sang. They went to the sink in the corner. The water felt warm and bubbly. They scrubbed paws and wings. Rub-rub, rinse-rinse. Binky dried his paws on a soft towel. “All clean,” he said.

Binky looked around the lab. The lights blinked softly. The paints looked bright again. He felt calm. He felt strong. He smiled. “I am ready.”

“Teamwork time,” Dot said.

Binky handed Dot a brush. “You paint the sun,” he said. “I will fix the wheel.”

They worked together. “Swish-swish,” went the brush. Dot added orange and gold. The sun’s smile looked warm and kind. Binky cleaned the glue and used just a little. “Dot, can you hold this?” he asked.

“I’ve got it,” Dot said. Her wing was steady.

Binky pressed the button. The wheel spun. Whirr! The colors danced around the room like rainbows on the walls. Binky’s eyes were bright. “It works!”

“Your plan worked too,” Dot said. “How do you feel?”

“I feel great,” said Binky. He patted his tummy. “Full.” He wiggled his shoulders. “Loose.” He smiled big. “Happy.”

Binky added a small sign to the wall. It had a big heart and four little pictures: a cup, a stretch, a snack, and soap with bubbles. He read it out loud. “Sip. Stretch. Snack. Wash.” He tapped the sign gently. Tap. “This is our Healthy Helper Plan.”

Dot nodded. “Healthy habits help our art. Healthy habits help our hearts.”

Binky looked at the sun painting and the spinning wheel. “And healthy habits help teamwork too,” he said. “Thank you, Dot.”

“Quack-quack, you’re welcome,” Dot said. She rang her bell. Ding! “Break time again?”

Binky laughed. “Yes! Little breaks make big ideas.”

They sipped. They stretched. They munched. They washed. Then they made more art—soft clouds with cotton puffs and a blue river with shiny paper. The basement lab felt bright and cozy. It smelled like apples and soap. It sounded like giggles and gentle whirrs. Binky’s tail swished with joy.

At the end of the day, Binky looked around. “We did it,” he said. “We made beautiful art. We used teamwork. And we used healthy habits.”

Dot smiled. “That is a bright day.”

Binky pointed to the sign again. “I will follow our plan every day. Healthy habits help me do my best.”

The End.
