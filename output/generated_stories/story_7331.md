# Poppy Parrot’s Cozy Closet Cave

Poppy was a creative parrot with bright green feathers, a splash of blue on her wings, and a sunny yellow beak. Poppy loved to make art with feathers, ribbons, and shiny buttons. Her favorite place to create was a special closet hideaway. It was snug and soft, with a little lamp that glowed warm and golden. Inside the closet were baskets of craft supplies, a tiny pillow nest, and a sign on the door that said, “Poppy’s Cozy Closet Cave.”

One morning, Poppy fluffed her feathers and chirped, “I will make a rainbow crown!” She gathered soft ribbons that smelled like clean laundry and picked buttons that clicked like tiny raindrops in a cup.

Just then, Poppy’s friends, Miri Mouse and Benny Bunny, hopped down the hallway. They stopped by the closet door and sniffed the air. “It smells like crayons and cotton,” Miri whispered. “It smells like cozy,” Benny said, ears wiggling.

Miri knocked, knock-knock. “Poppy, can we come in?”

Poppy’s heart felt squishy and mixed-up. She liked her friends. She also liked her quiet time in her closet cave. Poppy peeked out and said softly, “This is my special cozy time. I need space.”

Benny smiled, but his big feet did a little tap-tap. “We want to play with you,” he said.

Miri nodded. “We can help make your rainbow crown!”

Poppy looked at her pillow nest, her baskets, and her sign. Her tummy fluttered like paper in a breeze. “I like you,” she said gently. “I want to make my crown by myself right now.”

Miri’s whiskers drooped. Benny’s ears tilted down. “Oh,” they said together.

Poppy felt a pinch in her chest. She didn’t want to hurt feelings. She also wanted a quiet space. She took a deep breath. The closet smelled like lavender and crayons and made her calm.

“I have an idea,” Poppy said. “First, I will have quiet time in my closet cave. That is my boundary. My body and my space need calm. After that, we can make something together. We can share the big craft box in the hallway.”

Miri looked up. “How long is quiet time?”

Poppy thought. “Quiet time lasts five songs,” she chirped. “I will sing them. When I finish the fifth song, I will open the door.”

Benny’s ears perked. “Okay,” he said. “We can count songs with you.”

“Thank you for listening,” Poppy said. “Please wait out here. You can plan our group craft.”

Poppy pulled the closet door mostly closed. She left a little crack so she could still hear her friends. Then she sang her first song, a soft “tweet-tweet” that sounded like wind in leaves. She tied a red ribbon to her crown. She sang her second song, bright and bouncy. She glued a blue button, then a green one that felt cool and smooth under her wing.

Outside, Miri and Benny whispered. “We can draw a big map,” Miri said. “A map to the Cozy Closet Cave!”

“We can use the long paper roll,” Benny replied. “And the big crayons that smell like apples.”

Poppy sang her third and fourth songs, sweet and steady. She added a purple feather that tickled her cheek. She smiled. Her heart felt calm and warm, like tea in a mug.

Before the fifth song, Miri called softly, “Poppy, thank you for telling us your boundary. We will wait.”

Poppy’s eyes sparkled. She sang the last song, gentle as a lullaby. Then she opened the door wide. Golden light spilled into the hallway. “All done,” she chirped. “Thank you for giving me space.”

Miri and Benny hopped closer. “Your crown is beautiful!” Benny said. The feathers swished like soft brushes. The buttons clicked happily when Poppy moved.

Poppy placed the crown on her head. “Now I am ready to share and cooperate!” she said. “Let’s make your map.”

They spread the long paper in the hallway. Poppy brought out the big crayons. “We can share these,” she said. “Let’s take turns.”

“I will draw the door,” Miri said, gripping a purple crayon.

“I will draw the lamp,” Benny said, choosing yellow.

Poppy drew little stars to mark “quiet zones.” She said, “These stars mean we use quiet voices here. This is another boundary. It keeps our cave cozy.”

Benny nodded. “I can do quiet,” he whispered.

Miri smiled. “Me too.”

They worked together. They asked, “Is this okay?” and “Do you like this?” and “May I use the green now?” They listened to yes and no. When someone said no, they said, “Okay,” and tried a new idea. The crayons made soft scritch-scratch sounds. The paper smelled like fresh wood. Their paws and wings brushed lightly, and everyone scooted to make room.

When the map was done, they taped it by the closet door. It showed a big red heart and three small stars. Under the stars, Poppy wrote slowly, “We ask. We listen. We share.”

Poppy placed her rainbow crown on Miri’s head. “Your turn,” she said. “Do you want this on your head?”

Miri giggled. “Yes, please.” The feathers fluttered. Benny clapped with quiet paws.

Poppy chirped, “Today we learned: My body and my space are mine. I can say yes or no. I can ask for quiet time. And after quiet time, we can share and cooperate.”

Miri hugged Poppy gently. “Your boundary helped all of us,” she said.

Benny grinned. “We made something wonderful together!”

Poppy’s heart felt like sunshine. In her cozy closet cave, with caring friends and clear words, she felt safe, kind, and creative.

The End.
