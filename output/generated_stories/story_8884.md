# Penny Panda's Rooftop Rescue

High up on the tall apartment building, Penny Panda sat quietly on the rooftop garden. She loved watching the fluffy white clouds drift by and feeling the warm sunshine on her black and white fur. The rooftop was her favorite place to think and be patient.

"Tweet! Tweet! Help!" came a tiny voice from above.

Penny looked up and saw a small blue bird flying in circles. The bird looked very worried and kept chirping loudly.

"What's wrong, little friend?" Penny called out in her gentle voice.

The bird flew down and landed near Penny's paw. "My baby fell from our nest! He's stuck on the edge of the roof, and I can't lift him back up. Please help!"

Penny's heart felt heavy with worry. She wanted to help right away, but she knew she needed to be careful. The edge of the roof was very high and dangerous.

"Don't worry," Penny said softly. "I want to help, but first we need to make sure everyone stays safe. Let me think for a moment."

Penny closed her eyes and took three deep breaths, just like her mama had taught her. When she opened them, she had a plan.

"First, let's get some grown-ups to help," Penny said. "This is too dangerous for just us."

Penny carefully walked to the rooftop door, staying far away from the edges. She knocked loudly with her paw. "Help! Help! We need a grown-up!"

Soon, Mr. Johnson the building manager came running up the stairs. "What's happening, Penny?"

"A baby bird is stuck on the roof edge," Penny explained. "We need help to rescue him safely."

Mr. Johnson smiled at Penny's smart thinking. "You did exactly the right thing by getting help first. Safety is always most important."

Mr. Johnson brought a long, soft net and some rope. He tied the rope around his waist so he wouldn't fall, then carefully crawled to the roof edge.

"I can see the little one," he called. "He's scared but not hurt."

Very gently, Mr. Johnson scooped the tiny bird into the soft net. The baby bird was no bigger than Penny's thumb, with fuzzy gray feathers and bright yellow eyes.

"Peep! Peep!" chirped the baby bird.

"There you go, little one," Mr. Johnson said, bringing the baby bird back to safety.

The mama bird flew down quickly. "Oh, thank you! Thank you so much!" she sang happily.

Penny gently offered her paw as a safe place for the bird family to rest. "I'm so glad everyone is safe," she said.

"You were very wise to get help, Penny," said Mr. Johnson. "You showed great respect for safety rules and generosity by wanting to help."

The mama bird chirped, "And you took time to think before acting. That's what kept everyone safe!"

From that day on, Penny became the rooftop safety helper. She made sure all the animals knew the rules: stay away from edges, always get grown-up help for dangerous situations, and take time to think before acting.

The bird family visited Penny every day, bringing her pretty flowers and singing sweet songs to thank her for her kindness and smart thinking.

Penny felt proud and happy. She learned that being patient and thinking about safety first was the best way to help others.

The End.
