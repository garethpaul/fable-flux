# Polly's Colorful Classroom

Sunlight danced across the soft rug in the comfortable nursery. The room smelled like fresh crayons and warm oatmeal. Books with bright pictures lined a tiny shelf. Blocks clicked, pages rustled, and a small clock tick-tocked, tick-tocked.

On the window perch sat Polly, a creative parrot with feathers like a rainbow. She tilted her head, fluffing her green and blue wings. “Good morning!” Polly chirped. “Today, I want to learn!”

Polly loved to make art and sing. She loved to talk, too. But today, she felt something flutter in her chest. It was a curious feeling. “I want to be a teacher!” she squawked, tapping her beak gently on the windowsill. “I can teach my friends words, colors, and kind ways.”

Polly hopped down to the rug. Her feet went tap-tap on the wood floor. “Friends!” she called. “Class is starting!”

Benny the teddy bear blinked his button eyes from the reading corner. Lila the soft llama lifted her fuzzy head. Mia the doll with brown braids sat up straight. A round, bouncy ball rolled over with a boing. Even the toy drum gave a tiny, happy thum.

“Welcome, class,” Polly said, spreading her wings like a bright cape. “Today we learn with ears, eyes, and hearts.”

“What is a heart lesson?” asked Lila in a warm, woolly voice.

“A heart lesson means we show respect,” Polly said softly. “We listen. We share. We use kind words.”

Benny raised his paw. “Can we learn colors, too?”

“Yes!” sang Polly. She pointed to a basket of crayons. “We will learn colors in many languages. It helps us be global friends.”

Polly pulled out a red crayon. “This is red,” she said. “In Spanish, rojo. Can you say rojo?”

“Ro-ho!” said Benny.

“Roro,” tried the ball.

“Good try,” Polly smiled. “We keep learning. We try again. Ro-jo.”

“Ro-jo,” said everyone together. The nursery felt warm with happy voices.

Polly took a blue crayon. “Blue,” she said. “In French, bleu.” She shaped the sound with her beak. “Bluh.”

“Bluh,” echoed Lila, her ears twitching. She giggled. “It feels funny in my mouth.”

“That is okay,” Polly said. “New words can feel new on our tongues. We practice with respect.”

Mia reached for a yellow crayon. “Yellow,” she said. “What is yellow in Swahili?”

Polly thought, her feathers shivering with excitement. “Yellow is njano,” she chirped carefully. “N-ja-no.”

“N-ja-no,” Mia repeated, slow and sweet. “I like it.”

Benny wiggled closer to the crayons. His paw knocked the basket. Crayons rolled like tiny logs. “Oh no!” he gasped. “I made a mess.”

Polly’s eyes were kind. “That is okay. We can fix it together. Learning can be messy, and that is part of the day.”

They picked up the crayons, one by one. The red crayon felt warm and smooth. The blue crayon smelled a little like paper and rain. The yellow crayon squeaked a bit as it rolled back into place.

“Thank you, class,” Polly said. “Now, let’s draw our world.”

She spread big sheets of paper on the rug. “What do we see in our world?” she asked.

“I see the sun,” said Mia.

“I see the ocean,” said Lila.

“I see a park with swings,” said Benny.

“I see friends,” said the bouncy ball, bouncing gently so it did not shake the crayons again.

“Wonderful,” Polly said. “Let’s draw it all.”

They colored slowly. The sun became a bright njano circle. The ocean turned deep bleu waves. The park’s flowers popped in rojo dots.

As they drew, Polly added more word treasures. “Friend in Spanish is amigo,” she said. “In French, ami. In Swahili, rafiki.”

“Rafiki,” whispered Lila, smiling. “I like how that sounds.”

Benny paused. “What if I say it wrong?”

Polly leaned close. “We learn by trying. We listen. We ask. We try again. That is how we grow our minds.”

The clock went tick-tock, tick-tock. Their papers filled with color and care. The nursery hummed with soft crayon scritch-scritch sounds.

When they finished, Polly looked at the pictures. Her eyes shone. “Class, what did we learn today?”

Mia raised her hand. “We learned new words.”

Lila nodded. “We learned to listen and help.”

Benny hugged his picture. “We learned that it’s okay to make mistakes.”

The ball bounced once. “And we learned to be gentle, so we don’t bump the crayons.”

Polly puffed her chest with happy pride. “Yes! We learned with ears, eyes, and hearts. We showed respect. We loved language. And we saw our big, beautiful world.”

She pinned their art on the low corkboard where everyone could see. The pictures glowed like little windows.

“Class,” Polly said softly, “education is a daily adventure. We learn a little each day. We share. We care. We try. That is how we become smart and kind global friends.”

“Amigo,” said Benny.

“Ami,” said Lila.

“Rafiki,” said Mia.

“Friends,” Polly chirped, and her voice rang clear and bright as a bell.

They all held their papers close. The room felt safe and cozy, like a gentle hug. The clock ticked, the crayons rested, and the sunlight made tiny stars on the floor.

“Tomorrow,” Polly said with a wink, “we learn numbers and songs from around the world.”

“Yay!” everyone cheered.

The End.
