# Polly's Amazing Discovery

Polly was a bright green parrot who loved living on Mrs. Chen's sunny balcony garden. Every morning, she would fluff her colorful feathers and look out at all the beautiful flowers and plants around her.

"Good morning, world!" Polly would squawk happily, spreading her wings wide.

One day, Polly noticed something strange. The leaves on her favorite sunflower plant looked droopy and sad. The bright yellow petals were turning brown at the edges.

"Oh no!" chirped Polly. "What's wrong with my sunflower friend?"

Polly hopped closer to investigate. She used her sharp eyes to look carefully at the plant. The soil felt dry and dusty under her orange feet. The water bowl nearby was empty and had green, slimy stuff growing in it.

"Yuck!" said Polly, wrinkling her beak. "This water bowl is very dirty. I think I found the problem!"

But Polly felt nervous. Mrs. Chen was inside making breakfast, and Polly had never tried to solve a problem by herself before. She usually just waited for help.

"I can do this," Polly told herself, puffing out her chest feathers. "I'm smart and brave!"

First, Polly decided to clean the dirty water bowl. She used her strong beak to tip it over, letting the yucky water spill out. Then she rubbed the bowl with her wing until it sparkled clean in the sunshine.

"Much better!" she squawked proudly.

Next, Polly needed fresh water. She remembered seeing Mrs. Chen use the watering can by the door. Polly grabbed the handle with her beak and carefully dragged it over to the rain bucket. The bucket was full of clean, fresh rainwater that smelled like morning clouds.

Polly dipped the watering can into the bucket. It was heavy, but she was determined. Drop by drop, she filled the clean bowl with fresh water.

"There!" panted Polly. "Clean water for drinking."

Then Polly gently watered the thirsty sunflower. She watched as the cool water soaked into the dry soil. The plant seemed to stand up a little straighter already.

But Polly wanted to remember what she learned. She found a stick and used her claw to scratch letters in the dirt:

"CLEAN WATER = HAPPY PLANTS"

Just then, Mrs. Chen came outside with her coffee.

"Oh my goodness, Polly!" she exclaimed. "Did you clean the water bowl and water the plants all by yourself?"

"Yes!" squawked Polly proudly. "I discovered the problem and fixed it! The sunflower was thirsty and the water was dirty."

Mrs. Chen smiled and gently stroked Polly's soft head feathers. "You are such a smart, helpful parrot. You used your eyes to discover what was wrong, you kept everything clean and healthy, and you even wrote yourself a note to remember!"

"I was brave too!" added Polly. "I didn't wait for help. I solved the problem myself!"

Over the next few days, the sunflower grew tall and strong again. Its petals turned bright yellow, and it faced the sun each morning just like Polly did.

Every day after that, Polly made sure to check all the plants in the balcony garden. She kept the water bowls clean and fresh. She watered any thirsty plants. And she always wrote little notes in the dirt about what she discovered.

"I love being a garden detective!" Polly would sing happily, dancing from plant to plant.

Mrs. Chen was so proud of her clever parrot who learned that being curious, staying clean, and speaking up to solve problems made the whole garden a happier place.

The End.
