# Kora the Koala's Story Time

Kora the koala loved her cozy front porch. Every morning, she would sit in her favorite rocking chair with a soft blue cushion. The porch smelled like sweet eucalyptus leaves and fresh morning air.

"What a beautiful day for stories!" Kora said, stretching her fuzzy gray arms.

But today felt different. Kora wanted to tell a story to her forest friends, but every time she tried to think of one, her mind felt all jumbled up.

"Once upon a time..." she started, then stopped. "No, that's not right."

She tried again. "There was a little... um... a big... oh dear!"

Kora felt her tummy get tight and her breathing get fast. Her paws started to feel warm and tingly. She was getting frustrated!

"Why can't I think of a good story?" she huffed, slumping in her chair.

Just then, her friend Benny the bunny hopped up the porch steps. His nose twitched as he sniffed the eucalyptus.

"Hi Kora! You look upset. What's wrong?" Benny asked, his ears flopping forward with concern.

"I want to tell you a story, but I can't think of anything good," Kora sighed. "My brain feels all mixed up."

Benny's eyes sparkled. "Maybe you need to take care of yourself first! My mama always says that when I feel frustrated, I should take three deep breaths."

"Really?" Kora asked.

"Yes! Want to try together?" Benny sat down on the porch.

Kora nodded. They both took a deep breath in through their noses, held it for a moment, then breathed out slowly through their mouths.

"Ahhhh," Kora felt her shoulders relax. "That feels better already!"

"Now," said Benny, "close your eyes and think about something that makes you happy."

Kora closed her eyes and felt the warm sunshine on her fur. She heard birds chirping in the trees and felt the gentle breeze tickling her ears. She thought about her favorite eucalyptus cookies and how they tasted sweet and crunchy.

"I feel much calmer now," Kora smiled. "Thank you, Benny!"

"So, what story would you like to tell?" Benny asked, settling in to listen.

Kora's eyes lit up. "I know! I'll tell you about what just happened to us!"

She began: "Once upon a time, there was a koala who lived on a peaceful porch. One day, she felt frustrated because she couldn't think of a story. But then her kind friend taught her how to take deep breaths and think happy thoughts. When she felt calm again, she realized she had a wonderful story to tell - the story of learning to take care of herself!"

Benny clapped his paws. "That's a perfect story, Kora! And it's true too!"

"You know what I learned?" Kora said, rocking gently in her chair. "Sometimes when we feel frustrated or stuck, we need to be kind to ourselves first. Taking deep breaths and thinking happy thoughts helps our minds feel clear again."

"And then the stories come naturally!" Benny added.

From that day on, whenever Kora felt frustrated, she would sit on her peaceful porch, take three deep breaths, and think of something happy. Soon, wonderful stories would fill her mind, and she would share them with all her friends.

"Taking care of ourselves helps us take care of others too," Kora would always say.

The End.
