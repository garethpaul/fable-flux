# Toby's Musical Discovery

Toby the turtle was very, very shy. When Grandma invited him to stay in her cozy guest room, he pulled his head deep into his shell. The room felt so big and new!

"Don't worry, little one," Grandma said softly. "This room has something special waiting for you."

Toby peeked one eye out. The guest room had a soft blue bed, fluffy white curtains that danced in the breeze, and... what was that shiny thing in the corner?

After Grandma left, Toby slowly crept across the wooden floor. His little feet made tiny *tap-tap-tap* sounds. In the corner sat a beautiful music box with a tiny ballerina on top.

"I wonder what this does," Toby whispered to himself. He was curious, but his shy feelings made his tummy feel fluttery like butterflies.

Toby touched the music box with one careful claw. *Click!* Suddenly, the most wonderful tinkling music began to play. The tiny ballerina spun around and around, her pink dress twirling.

"Oh my!" Toby gasped. The music sounded like raindrops on leaves and birds singing in the morning. It made him feel warm and happy inside.

But then Toby noticed something amazing. His foot was tapping along with the music! *Tap-tap-tap* went his foot. *Ting-a-ling-ling* went the music box.

"The music is making me want to move!" Toby said, feeling surprised and excited.

He tried moving his other foot. *Tap-tap* Then he swayed his shell back and forth. The music made him feel brave and joyful, not shy at all!

Soon, Toby was dancing all around the guest room. He spun like the ballerina, he marched like a soldier, and he even wiggled like a happy worm. The music filled his heart with sunshine.

"I'm dancing! I'm really dancing!" Toby laughed out loud.

Just then, Grandma peeked into the room. "My goodness, Toby! You found my special music box!"

Toby quickly pulled into his shell, feeling shy again. But the music was still playing, and it still made him feel happy.

"Don't hide, sweet turtle," Grandma said gently. "Music is meant to be shared. Would you like to dance with me?"

Toby poked his head out. "But I'm too shy, Grandma."

"Music helps us express our feelings," Grandma explained. "When you're shy, music can help you feel brave. When you're sad, music can help you feel better. And when you're happy, music makes it even more fun!"

Grandma began to sway to the music. She moved her arms like tree branches in the wind. Toby watched and felt the music calling to him again.

Slowly, he came out of his shell. First one foot tapped, then the other. Soon, he was dancing with Grandma, spinning and swaying together.

"This feels wonderful!" Toby said. "The music makes my whole body feel happy!"

"That's the magic of music, dear one," Grandma smiled. "It helps us understand our feelings and share them with others."

From that day on, whenever Toby felt shy or scared, he remembered Grandma's music box. He learned that music could help him feel brave, express his emotions, and connect with others. The shy little turtle discovered that dancing and music were like keys that unlocked the joy in his heart.

And every time he visited Grandma's guest room, the first thing he did was wind up that special music box and dance.

The End.
