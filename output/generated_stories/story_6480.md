# Melody's Morning Concert

On a red brick rooftop high above the busy street, lived a small yellow bird named Melody. Her bright feathers sparkled in the morning sun. Melody loved to sing more than anything in the whole world.

Every day, Melody would perch on the edge of the rooftop. She would listen to all the sounds below. *Beep beep!* went the cars. *Ring ring!* went the bicycles. *Chatter chatter!* went the people walking by.

"I want to sing too!" chirped Melody. "But what if my voice isn't pretty enough?"

Melody had been practicing her songs quietly. She would sing softly to herself when no one could hear. Her voice sounded sweet and clear, like tiny silver bells.

One sunny morning, Melody heard something special. Down in the park across the street, children were gathering for story time. They sat in a big circle on a soft green blanket.

"Tweet tweet!" called another bird from a nearby tree. "Why don't you sing for them, Melody?"

Melody's little heart fluttered fast. "Oh my! Sing for real people? I'm too scared!" she whispered.

The wise old bird smiled. "But Melody, you practice every single day. Your voice is beautiful! The children would love to hear you sing."

Melody looked down at the happy children. They were laughing and clapping their hands. The warm breeze ruffled her yellow feathers gently.

"Maybe... maybe I could try," Melody said softly.

She took a deep breath. The air tasted fresh and clean. Melody opened her tiny beak and began to sing. At first, her voice was quiet and shaky.

*"La la la, good morning sun!
La la la, let's have some fun!"*

A little girl in the park looked up. "Listen!" she said. "Do you hear that pretty singing?"

Melody felt braver. Her voice grew stronger and clearer. She sang about the fluffy white clouds. She sang about the dancing leaves. She sang about friendship and sunny days.

*"Tweet tweet, hello down there!
Music fills the morning air!"*

Soon, all the children were looking up at the rooftop. They pointed and smiled. Some started clapping along with Melody's rhythm.

"More! More!" called the children.

Melody's chest puffed up with pride. She felt warm and happy inside. Her fear melted away like ice cream in sunshine.

She sang song after song. Each one was different and special. Melody sang about rainbows and butterflies. She sang about playing and laughing.

The children giggled and danced. Their teacher smiled too. "What a wonderful concert!" she said.

When story time ended, the children waved up at Melody. "Thank you, little bird! Your songs made us so happy!"

Melody fluffed her feathers proudly. She had found her courage! All that practice had helped her share her gift.

From that day on, Melody sang every morning for the children. She learned that sharing her creative gifts made everyone happy - including herself.

"Being creative means being brave," Melody chirped to her reflection in a puddle. "And practice makes everything better!"

The sun shone brighter, the breeze felt sweeter, and Melody's songs filled the whole neighborhood with joy.

The End.
