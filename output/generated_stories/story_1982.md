# Rocky Raccoon Builds a Song

Rocky was a musical raccoon with bright eyes and stripy paws. He loved to make music. One sunny morning, Rocky padded into the musical practice room. The room smelled like fresh wood. Shiny drums, soft shakers, and a little toy piano waited on colorful rugs. Sunlight made the cymbals sparkle.

“I will make a big song today!” Rocky said. His tail swished with joy. He grabbed a drum. Boom-boom! He shook a shaker. Chika-chika! He pressed a piano key. Plink!

But all the sounds mixed together into a messy noise. Boom-chika-plink-plonk! Rocky’s ears drooped.

“This is too much,” he sighed. “My song is not working.”

Just then, his friend Bella Bunny peeked in. “Hi, Rocky! I brought water and carrot sticks,” she said. “Crunch, crunch!”

Rocky sniffed. He felt a little grumpy. “I want a big song now,” he said. “But it sounds like a jumble.”

Bella sat on a soft cushion. “Maybe we can build your song,” she said. “When I build a block tower, I do it step by step.”

“Build a song?” Rocky blinked. “How do we build a song?”

“We plan,” Bella said. She drew a simple plan on a sticky note with a bright crayon. “First, pick the beat. Then, add a melody. Then, add soft sounds. Last, we take a break and listen.”

Rocky nodded slowly. “Step by step,” he repeated. His whiskers twitched with hope.

They started with the beat. Rocky tapped the big drum with gentle paws. Boom… boom… boom… The sound was deep and warm, like a friendly heart. He smiled. “This feels steady.”

Bella clapped along. Clap… clap… clap… “Nice and slow,” she said. “It’s easier to build when we go slow.”

Next, they added a melody. Rocky pressed one white key on the toy piano. Plink… plink… plink… He tried a black key. Plonk… plonk… plonk… He listened to how the sounds went together.

“I like plink with my boom,” Rocky said. He played: Boom… plink… boom… plink… The room felt cozy, and Rocky’s tail made happy wiggles.

“Now soft sounds,” Bella said. She gave Rocky a tiny bell. It chimed like a star. Ting… ting…

Rocky closed his eyes and listened. Boom… plink… ting… boom… plink… ting… The sounds made a pattern. It felt like building a little music house, one piece at a time.

Suddenly, Ziggy Squirrel rushed in, tail whooshing. “I want to play fast!” he squeaked. He hit the cymbal. Crash! He shook the maracas super quick. Shaka-shaka-shaka!

The music house wobbled. Rocky’s ears flattened. “Wait, Ziggy,” he said, trying to stay calm. “We are building a song slowly.”

“I like fast,” Ziggy said, bouncing. “Fast is fun!”

Rocky took a slow breath like Bella had shown him before. In… out… “We can make a plan,” he said. “Then your fast part can fit. Will you help us build?”

Ziggy stopped bouncing. “A plan?” He tilted his head. “Okay. Where do I go?”

Bella smiled. “We will have fast at the end,” she said. “First slow beat, then melody, then soft bell, then a water break, then fast fun.”

“Water break?” Ziggy asked.

“Yes,” said Rocky. “Healthy breaks help us play better.” He took a sip from his bottle. Glug-glug. Bella crunched a carrot. Crunch! Ziggy tried a sip too. “Ahh,” he said. “That feels good.”

They looked at the sticky note plan. It had four boxes: Beat, Melody, Soft, Fast. Rocky put a little paw print in each box as they finished. One, two, three…

“Now it’s time for fast,” Bella said, pointing to the last box.

“Ready!” Ziggy chirped.

Rocky tapped the drum steady. Boom… boom… Bella played the piano melody. Plink… plink… Rocky added the bell. Ting… ting… Then Ziggy added fast maracas right on the beat. Shaka-shaka! Shaka-shaka!

The sound was bright and happy. It wasn’t messy now. It was a song! The room hummed with joy. Rocky’s heart felt warm.

“We built it!” Rocky laughed. He bounced a little, but kept the beat steady. Boom… boom…

Ziggy grinned. “I like the plan. My fast fits.”

Bella nodded. “And we took a healthy break. That helped us listen.”

They played the song again. This time it felt even better. Rocky noticed how the drum and the piano and the bell made a cozy shape in the air. He could almost see the song like blocks: bottom beat, middle melody, top sparkle, and a fast flag on top!

When they finished, they cleaned up together. Rocky wiped the drum. Wipe, wipe. Bella put away the crayons. Click. Ziggy stacked the shakers in a small basket. Rattle, rattle. Teamwork felt nice.

Rocky put the sticky note plan on the wall. “Next time, we can build again,” he said. “Step by step.”

Bella patted her tummy. “And take water and snack breaks,” she added.

Ziggy nodded. “And talk when we want to go fast,” he said. “We can listen and make a plan.”

Rocky smiled. “Today I learned that building takes time. Planning helps. Healthy habits help too. When we listen and work together, we can solve problems.”

They all did a little bow to their music house of sound.

“Let’s play it one more time,” Rocky said softly.

They breathed in and out together. Then the room filled with Boom… plink… ting… shaka! It was balanced, bright, and beautiful.

The End.
