# Rosie Rabbit's Big Helper Day

Rosie Rabbit bounced excitedly in the back seat of her family's red car. Her soft gray ears wiggled as she looked out the window at the fluffy white clouds.

"Where are we going, Mama?" Rosie asked, her pink nose twitching with curiosity.

"We're driving to your cousin Benny's soccer game," Mama Rabbit said with a warm smile. "He's been practicing very hard!"

Rosie clapped her little paws together. "I want to help Benny win!"

As they drove down the bumpy road, Rosie noticed something troubling. Through the car window, she saw Benny sitting alone on a bench at the soccer field. His shoulders looked droopy, and his ears hung down sadly.

"Oh no!" Rosie gasped. "Benny looks very sad. Can we stop, please?"

Papa Rabbit parked the car, and Rosie hopped out as fast as her little legs could carry her. The grass felt cool and tickly under her paws.

"Benny! Benny!" she called, running over to her cousin. "What's wrong?"

Benny looked up with watery eyes. "Hi, Rosie. I'm supposed to play soccer today, but I forgot my water bottle. Coach says I can't play without water to drink. I feel so thirsty already!"

Rosie's heart felt heavy seeing her cousin so upset. She thought hard, her little brain working like busy gears. Then her eyes lit up like sparkly stars!

"I have an idea!" Rosie announced. "I brought my special water bottle in the car. You can use it!"

"Really?" Benny's ears perked up hopefully. "But then what will you drink?"

Rosie smiled her biggest, brightest smile. "We can take turns! When you're playing soccer, I'll cheer for you. When you need water, we'll share. That's what helpers do!"

Rosie ran back to the car, her fluffy tail bouncing. She grabbed her favorite purple water bottle with rainbow stickers.

"Here you go!" she said, handing it to Benny. "Now you can play!"

Benny took a long, refreshing gulp. "Ahh! Thank you so much, Rosie! You're the best helper ever!"

During the game, Rosie cheered loudly from the sidelines. "Go, Benny, go!" Her voice was as clear as a little bell. Whenever Benny ran over for water, Rosie would take a small sip too, and they would share happy smiles.

Benny played wonderfully! He kicked the black and white ball with strong, confident hops. His team scored three goals, and Benny made one himself!

After the game, Benny ran over to Rosie, his face glowing with joy. "We won! And it's all because you helped me, Rosie!"

"I'm so proud of you!" Rosie giggled, giving Benny a big, warm hug.

Coach Carrot came over and patted both little rabbits on their heads. "Rosie, you showed wonderful kindness today. Helping others is just as important as winning games."

As they drove home in the car, Rosie felt warm and happy inside, like sunshine filling her heart. Mama Rabbit smiled in the rearview mirror.

"You learned something very special today, Rosie," Mama said gently. "When we help others reach their goals, we feel good too. That's the magic of being helpful."

Rosie nodded wisely, already thinking about who she could help tomorrow. Outside the car window, the sunset painted the sky in beautiful orange and pink colors, just like the happy feeling in her heart.

The End.
