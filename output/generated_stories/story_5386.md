# Whiskers and the Missing Library Books

Whiskers was a very clever orange cat who lived in the cozy study corner of the town library. The soft carpet felt warm under his paws. Tall bookshelves reached up to the ceiling like friendly giants.

Every day, Whiskers watched children come to read. He loved hearing their happy voices and seeing their bright smiles when they found special books.

But today was different. Mrs. Chen, the librarian, looked worried. Her forehead had little wrinkles, and she kept sighing.

"Oh dear," Mrs. Chen said to herself. "Ten picture books are missing! The children's reading time is in one hour, and we don't have enough books."

Whiskers' green eyes grew wide. He loved the children's reading time. It was the best part of his day!

"Meow!" Whiskers said softly. He wanted to help, but how could a cat find missing books?

Whiskers sat very still and thought hard. His whiskers twitched as he made a plan. First, he would use his super nose to smell for books. Then, he would use his quiet paws to search everywhere.

"I can do this," Whiskers whispered to himself. "The children need their books."

Whiskers started his search. He sniffed near the front desk. Nothing. He checked under the reading chairs. Still nothing. His heart felt heavy.

Then Whiskers remembered something important. Yesterday, he saw some older children playing near the back door. Maybe they forgot to put books back?

Whiskers padded quietly to the back of the library. His nose twitched. He could smell something papery and sweet - like books!

Behind a tall plant, Whiskers found a big cardboard box. Inside were all ten missing picture books! A note on top said: "Surprise books for reading time - From the Big Kids Club."

"Meow! Meow!" Whiskers called excitedly.

Mrs. Chen hurried over. "Whiskers! What did you find?"

She read the note and smiled big. "Oh my! The older children wanted to surprise the little ones with extra books. They hid them here as a gift!"

Whiskers purred loudly. His strategy worked! He used his thinking, his nose, and his brave heart to solve the problem.

Soon, children filled the study corner. They sat on the soft carpet, just like Whiskers. Mrs. Chen read from the found books, and everyone laughed and clapped.

"Thank you, Whiskers," whispered a little girl with pigtails. She gently petted his orange fur. "You saved reading time!"

Whiskers felt warm and happy inside. He learned that when there's a problem, thinking of a good plan helps. Being brave enough to try, even when you're not sure, is important too.

The older children came to watch reading time. "We're sorry we worried you, Mrs. Chen," they said. "We just wanted to help the little kids love books more."

"That's very kind," Mrs. Chen smiled. "Next time, let's plan surprises together."

Everyone agreed. The big kids, little kids, Mrs. Chen, and even clever Whiskers made a great team.

As the sun set through the library windows, Whiskers curled up in his favorite spot. He felt proud. Today he learned that helping others feels wonderful, and that thinking carefully before acting makes everything better.

"Tomorrow," Whiskers purred sleepily, "I'll help again if anyone needs me."

The End.
