# Milo the Brave Little Monkey

Milo was a small brown monkey with bright, curious eyes and a fluffy tail that wiggled when he was excited. He lived in the coziest living room you could imagine, with soft cushions scattered everywhere, colorful toys in every corner, and warm sunlight streaming through big windows.

One sunny morning, Milo heard a tiny sound coming from behind the big blue sofa. "Squeak! Squeak!" went the little voice.

Milo's ears perked up. "Hello?" he called softly, his voice gentle and kind. "Is someone there?"

The squeaking stopped. Milo felt his heart beat a little faster. The space behind the sofa looked dark and scary. But that little voice sounded sad and frightened.

"I'm Milo," he said, taking a small step closer to the sofa. "Are you okay back there?"

A whisper came from the shadows: "I'm Luna. I'm just a tiny mouse, and I'm stuck! My tail is caught under this heavy book."

Milo's tummy felt fluttery with worry. The dark space behind the sofa seemed so big and mysterious. What if there were other scary things back there? But Luna sounded like she really needed help.

Milo took a deep breath and remembered what his mama always told him: "Being brave doesn't mean you're not scared. It means you do the right thing even when you feel scared."

"Don't worry, Luna!" Milo called out, his voice growing stronger. "I'm coming to help you!"

Carefully, Milo squeezed behind the sofa. It was dark and dusty, and spider webs tickled his fur. "Achoo!" he sneezed, but he kept going.

There, in the corner, was the tiniest gray mouse Milo had ever seen. Her little pink tail was indeed trapped under a big red book that had fallen from the shelf above.

"Oh my!" said Milo. "That book is much bigger than both of us! But don't worry, Luna. We can figure this out together."

Luna's tiny black eyes filled with tears. "I've been stuck here all night. I was so scared and cold."

Milo felt his heart fill with kindness. He gently patted Luna's tiny paw with his finger. "You're very brave for calling for help. Now, let's think..."

Milo looked around and spotted a long wooden ruler that had fallen nearby. "I have an idea!" he said excitedly.

Using all his strength, Milo pushed one end of the ruler under the book. Then he pressed down on the other end like a seesaw. Slowly, slowly, the book lifted just enough for Luna to wiggle her tail free!

"I'm free! I'm free!" squeaked Luna, dancing in tiny circles. "Thank you, Milo! You're the bravest monkey ever!"

Milo felt warm and proud inside. "We both were brave today," he said with a big smile. "You were brave to ask for help, and I was brave to help you, even though I felt scared."

Luna hugged Milo's finger with her tiny paws. "Will you be my friend?" she asked hopefully.

"Of course!" laughed Milo. "The best adventures happen when friends help each other!"

From that day on, Milo and Luna played together every day in the sunny living room. And whenever either of them felt scared about trying something new, they remembered that being brave means caring about others and doing the right thing, even when your tummy feels fluttery.

The End.
