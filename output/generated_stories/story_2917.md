# Polly's Special Writing Space

High up in the big oak tree, Polly the parrot had the most wonderful treehouse. It had bright yellow walls, a cozy reading corner with soft cushions, and best of all—a special writing desk by the window where morning sunlight streamed in like golden honey.

Every day, Polly loved to sit at her desk and write stories. She would dip her colorful feathers in rainbow ink and create magical tales about flying adventures and friendship. The gentle rustle of leaves outside made the perfect writing music.

"Tweet-tweet-write, write-write-tweet!" Polly sang happily as she worked on her newest story about a brave little butterfly.

One sunny morning, Polly's friends Benny the bear and Rosie the rabbit climbed up to visit. They loved hearing Polly's stories, but today they wanted to do something different.

"Let's play tag!" called Benny, bouncing on Polly's soft cushions.

"Or hide and seek!" squeaked Rosie, opening all of Polly's story boxes.

Polly's colorful feathers ruffled with worry. "But I really want to finish my butterfly story today," she said softly. "Writing makes me feel happy and calm."

"Writing is boring!" laughed Benny, accidentally knocking over Polly's ink bottles. Purple and green ink splashed everywhere! "Come play with us instead!"

Rosie grabbed Polly's special writing feathers. "You can write later! We want to play RIGHT NOW!"

Polly felt her tummy get tight and twisty. She loved her friends, but she also loved her writing time. The treehouse felt too crowded and noisy. She couldn't think of any butterfly words with all the bouncing and grabbing.

"Stop!" said Polly, spreading her beautiful wings wide. "This is my special writing space, and I need quiet time to create my stories."

Benny stopped bouncing. Rosie put down the feathers. They both looked surprised.

Polly took a deep breath that smelled like fresh leaves and sunshine. "I love playing with you both, but right now I need my writing time. My stories are important to me, just like your favorite things are important to you."

"But we missed you!" said Rosie, her ears drooping.

Polly's heart felt warm. "I missed you too! How about this—let me finish my butterfly story first. Then we can all play together in the big meadow below. There's much more space to run and jump down there!"

Benny's eyes lit up. "That sounds fair! And maybe you could read us your butterfly story when it's done?"

"I would love that!" chirped Polly happily.

While Polly cleaned up her ink and continued writing, her friends quietly looked at her other finished stories. They whispered "wow" and "amazing" as they saw all the colorful pictures and exciting words.

When Polly finished her butterfly story, she read it aloud. Her friends clapped and cheered! Then they all climbed down to play tag in the meadow, where there was plenty of room for everyone.

That evening, as the sun painted the sky orange and pink, Polly felt proud and happy. She had protected her special writing time AND enjoyed playing with her friends.

"When we say what we need kindly but clearly, everyone can be happy," Polly told herself, snuggling into her cozy treehouse bed.

From that day on, Polly's friends always asked before visiting during writing time. And Polly always made sure to plan fun play time with them too. Everyone's needs mattered, and that made their friendship even stronger.

The End.
