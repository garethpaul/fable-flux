# Miko’s Tidy Adventure

Morning sunshine peeked through the curtains of the comfortable nursery. A soft hum from the window fan tickled the air. Miko, a kind little mouse with bright whiskers and a tiny blue vest, popped out of his cozy sock-bed. He stretched his paws and sniffed. The room smelled like clean blankets, warm crayons, and a hint of apples from snack time.

“Good morning, toys,” Miko squeaked. He loved this nursery. It was safe, warm, and full of friends. There were blocks, books, and baskets with labels. There were soft rugs that felt like clouds under paws.

But today, something was different.

Miko stepped onto the rug and—oof!—his paw bumped a rolling car. “Oh dear,” he said. He looked around. Blocks were scattered like little mountains. Crayons were sleeping on the floor instead of in their cup. Puzzle pieces hid under the table. A teddy’s button-eye stared up from near the door.

“My friends will trip,” Miko whispered. “Someone could fall. The room needs help.”

He felt a flutter in his tummy. The mess looked big. Very big. “Can I do it?” he asked his tiny self.

Miko put a paw on his vest and took a brave breath. “I will try. I am small, but I am brave. I can be a helper.”

He clapped his paws. “Tidy time!” his voice sang. It sounded cheerful, like a bell.

First, Miko listened. Tap-tap from the rain on the window. Rustle-rustle from the curtains. He heard the soft squeak of his own paws on the floor. He made a plan. Plans help big jobs feel smaller.

“Step one,” he said. “Sort by kind.” He smiled. “Blocks with blocks. Books with books. Crayons with crayons.”

Miko pushed a red block toward the block basket. “Red block to your home,” he said. He pushed a blue block next. “Blue block, too.” The blocks made a happy clack as they landed together. Clack, clack!

He hummed a little tidy song. “Push, place, and pat. Push, place, and pat.” His tail swished to the beat. It felt good to move with a rhythm.

He picked up crayons, one by one. They smelled waxy and sweet. “Back to your cup,” he told them. “You will draw rainbows later.” Plink! Plunk! The crayons chimed as they went back into the cup.

Under the table, Miko found a puzzle picture of a duck. “Quack, quack,” he giggled. He looked for the box with the duck on it. “Match the picture,” he reminded himself. “Match, match.” The pieces fit inside with a soft slide. Swish!

Then Miko saw a problem. A big box of blocks was too heavy to move. He pushed with both paws. He leaned his shoulder. The box did not budge. His whiskers drooped.

“It is okay to ask for help,” Miko said softly. He took another brave breath. “I can share the job.”

He cupped his paws around his mouth. “Friends! I need helpers!” His voice was small, but it carried.

From behind a curtain, a shy stuffed rabbit peeked out. From a shelf, a wooden owl blinked painted eyes. Down from the rocking chair, a tiny toy hedgehog rolled.

“Hello,” said Rabbit in a gentle voice. “We heard your song.”

“We want to help,” hooted Owl.

“We like tidy,” snuffled Hedgehog.

Miko’s heart felt warm. “Thank you,” he said. “We can share the work.”

He gave simple jobs. “Rabbit, can you pick up the soft things? Owl, can you watch for sharp things? Hedgehog, can you roll the cars into their garage?”

Rabbit hugged the teddy and found his button-eye. “Here you go,” she whispered, pressing the button-eye onto teddy’s paw for safe keeping.

Owl pointed with a wooden wing. “Careful, a tiny bead.” Miko picked it up and put it in a small-lids bin. “Thank you, Owl,” he said. “We stay safe.”

Hedgehog snuffled cars into a basket that looked like a tiny garage. Vroom, vroom—click! The cars were parked.

Miko and his friends worked and chatted.

“This is fun,” said Rabbit.

“It is neat,” said Owl.

“It smells fresh now,” said Hedgehog, sniffing the room.

Miko felt proud. “We are a great team,” he said. “We are brave. We are smart. We are kind.”

Soon, the nursery looked different. The rug looked like a cloud again. The blocks stood in their basket like a little city. The crayons smiled from their cup. Books sat on the shelf, tall and straight. The air felt light, like a gentle hug.

Miko placed the last item—teddy’s button-eye—into a small “fix-it” cup. “We will ask a grown-up to sew this,” he said.

He rubbed his paws. “Now we can play safely,” he said. He looked at his friends. “Let’s share a story.”

He pulled a book with a bright yellow sun on the cover. “Everyone ready?” he asked.

Rabbit nestled beside him. Owl perched on the arm of the chair. Hedgehog curled at Miko’s feet. Miko opened the book. The pages made a soft whisper. Shhhh. He read in a gentle voice, and the room felt peaceful.

When the story ended, Miko stood up. He put the book back on the shelf, spine out, picture front. “Books go home after reading,” he said with a smile. “That is our tidy rule.”

Rabbit nodded. “I like rules that help.”

Owl hooted softly. “Order is cozy.”

Hedgehog yawned. “A neat place feels safe.”

Miko patted the rug. “We did it together. We were brave when the mess felt big. We shared the work. And we kept everything in its home.”

He looked around one more time. The nursery gleamed. Miko’s heart felt as warm as the sunshine.

He whispered, “A tidy room makes space for fun.”

The End.
