# Gigi the Giraffe and the Painted Feelings

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with long eyelashes and even longer legs. Today, she tiptoed into the creative art room. The room smelled like fresh paper and lemony soap. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. Paints sat in bright cups—red, blue, yellow, and green. Soft brushes waited in a jar, like flowers in a vase.

“Good morning, brushes,” Gigi whispered, smiling. She loved to think about how things felt. She touched the smooth paper. She listened to the quiet hum of the air. She felt calm.

Her friends were there too. Pip the penguin shuffled by the glue sticks. Mimi the mouse sorted fuzzy pom-poms. Tavo the turtle stacked crayons in a neat rainbow. Everyone was busy and happy.

“Let’s make a friendship mural,” said Ms. Bee, their teacher, buzzing with joy. “A big picture we make together. We will share the table and share our ideas.”

Gigi’s heart thumped in a friendly way. “I want to paint a tall tree,” she said softly. “With a nest for everyone.”

“I want stars!” Pip chirped. “Shiny stars!”

“I want tiny dots,” squeaked Mimi. “Dot, dot, dot!”

“I like lines,” Tavo said slowly. “Straight lines. Safe lines.”

They all giggled. The table felt a little crowded, but it felt warm too.

Gigi dipped her brush in green paint. Swish, swish. The brush made a soft scratchy sound on the paper. A tall tree grew from her brush. She hummed a small tune. She was thinking about the nest. “A nest needs to be soft,” she said. “Like a hug.”

Suddenly, Pip reached across with a big brush. Splish! A blue star bloomed right on Gigi’s tree.

“Oh!” Gigi blinked. She felt a bump in her tummy. She looked at Pip. Pip’s eyes sparkled, but he didn’t see Gigi’s surprise.

Mimi scooted closer. “Dot, dot, dot,” she said. Plip, plip. Tiny dots landed on the leaves and on Tavo’s careful lines.

Tavo pulled in his head a little. “My lines,” he whispered. “I worked hard.”

Gigi took a slow breath. In through her nose. Out through her mouth. She looked at her friends. Pip was excited. Mimi looked proud. Tavo looked worried.

Gigi touched her chest. How do we feel? she wondered. She felt a little sad about her tree. She felt happy for Pip’s star. She felt worried for Tavo. Many feelings at once!

Ms. Bee smiled from the sink. “Artists, use your words and your ears,” she buzzed. “We can make room for every feeling.”

Gigi nodded. She stood tall, but her voice was kind. “Friends,” she said, “I want to share. But when the star splashed on my tree, I felt surprised. My tummy felt tight.”

Pip looked up. His brush paused. “Oh,” he said softly. “I didn’t see your tree getting a nest. I was excited. My flippers got too zoomy.”

Gigi gave a gentle smile. “Thank you for listening, Pip. I like your star. Can we plan where the next one goes?”

Pip nodded fast. “Yes! Where should it shine?”

Tavo peeked out. “My lines got dots,” he said. “I feel worried. My lines were my plan.”

Mimi’s whiskers drooped. “I didn’t know,” she squeaked. “I can move the dots. I can choose a new spot.”

Gigi put her long neck low so she could see them eye to eye. “We can make a feelings map,” she said. “We point to where our art can go. We ask, ‘Is this okay?’ before we paint on someone’s part.”

“That’s a good plan,” Ms. Bee buzzed. “That is empathy. Empathy means we try to feel what others feel, and care about it.”

Gigi nodded. “Let’s try. Pip, your stars can go in the sky above my tree. Mimi, your dots can be berries near the tree. Tavo, your lines can be the path under the tree.”

Pip’s eyes shone. “Sky stars! Yes!”

Mimi perked up. “Berry dots! Yum!”

Tavo’s shell relaxed. “A safe path,” he said, smiling slowly. “I like that.”

They all dipped their brushes. Swish, plip, slide. The room sounded like soft rain. Gigi watched her friends’ faces. She looked for smiles. She listened for quiet yeses.

“Is this spot okay?” Pip asked Gigi, pointing with his brush.

“Yes,” Gigi said. “Thank you for asking.”

“Are berries okay here?” Mimi asked Tavo.

“I think so,” Tavo said. “Maybe a little to the left.” Mimi moved them. “Perfect,” Tavo said.

The mural grew and grew. A tall tree with a cozy nest. Stars that shimmered. Dots like sweet berries. Lines that made a gentle path. It looked like everyone’s ideas holding hands.

Gigi felt warm inside. Her tummy bump was gone. She had used her words. She had listened. She had cared about her friends’ feelings and her own.

When they were done, they stepped back. Ms. Bee clapped. “Beautiful,” she buzzed. “You shared space, shared ideas, and shared care. That is kind. That is brave.”

Gigi smiled at her friends. “We made room for each other,” she said. “That made our art better.”

Pip touched the star with his eyes. “I learned to ask first.”

Mimi patted her dots. “I learned to look at faces.”

Tavo traced the path. “I learned to say what I need.”

Gigi nodded. “I learned to listen and to speak up kindly.”

They cleaned the brushes. The water swirled like a tiny rainbow. The room smelled like oranges and paint. Gigi’s heart felt big and bright, like the sky above the tree.

The mural dried on the wall. Under it, Ms. Bee wrote, “We care how you feel.”

Gigi read the words and whispered, “Me too.”

The End.
