# Nutty Plans in the Cozy Bedroom

Sunny the squirrel lived in a cozy bedroom at the top of a little house. The room smelled like warm blankets and fresh apples. A soft lamp made golden light on the walls. Sunny’s bed was a tiny basket with a leaf blanket. Beside it stood a small wooden box, her snack box.

One rainy morning, raindrops tapped the window like tiny drums. Sunny woke up, stretched her fluffy tail, and peeked into the snack box.

“Oh no,” Sunny whispered. “Only one hazelnut and two raisins.” Her tummy gave a small rumble. “I need a plan.”

Sunny was a resourceful squirrel. She liked to think before she did things. She tapped her paw on her chin. Tap, tap, tap.

“First, I will make a list,” she said. Sunny grabbed a crayon and a piece of paper. She drew simple pictures because pictures were easy to understand.

“Plan,” she read softly. “Step one: Count snacks. Step two: Sort snacks. Step three: Share and trade. Step four: Make a healthy plate.”

She smiled. “A plan is like a map for my day.”

Just then, her friend Pip the mouse peeked from under the bed. Pip’s whiskers twitched. “Hi, Sunny! What are you doing?”

“I am making a plan,” Sunny said. “I want good food for my body and brain. I have one nut and two raisins.”

Pip looked worried. “I have one cracker and a tiny carrot. That’s not much.”

Sunny’s ears perked up. “We can cooperate. We can work together.”

Sunny and Pip sat on the soft rug. They placed their snacks in a neat row. The rug felt fuzzy on their paws. They sniffed. The carrot smelled sweet and earthy. The hazelnut smelled toasty. The cracker smelled buttery. The raisins smelled like sunshine.

Sunny pointed to her paper. “Step one: Count. We have one nut, one cracker, one carrot, and two raisins. That makes five snacks.”

Pip nodded. “Step two: Sort.”

They sorted by type and color. Crunchy things in one group. Chewy things in another. Orange things here. Brown things there. Sunny clapped softly. “Sorting helps me see what we have.”

Pip’s tummy grumbled. “Step three?”

“Share and trade,” Sunny said kindly. “If we trade, we can make a balanced plate. Nut for crunch and strong bodies. Carrot for vitamins. Cracker for energy. Raisins for sweet bites.”

Pip smiled. “Good strategy! We think first, then we eat.”

They made two tiny plates on a blue napkin. Sunny put half the carrot on each plate. Pip split the cracker into two squares. Sunny placed the nut between them to share later, and two raisins became one for each.

Pip’s eyes shone. “We did it!”

Sunny nodded. “But wait. We need water.” She poured water from a small cup into two acorn-top cups. “Water is important,” she said. “It helps our bodies.”

They sat on the bed. The blanket felt soft and warm. Rain tapped the glass. Tap, tap, tap. The room smelled like cinnamon from a candle on the shelf.

“Before we eat,” Sunny said, “let’s check our plan. Step four: Make a healthy plate. We have crunchy, chewy, sweet, and fresh. That’s balanced.”

Pip took a nibble of carrot. Crunch! “Yum,” he said. “My body says thank you.”

Sunny took a sip of water. “My brain says thank you, too. Good food helps me think and play.”

Suddenly they heard a soft knock. Knock, knock. A tiny head peeped through the bedroom door. It was Bella the bird, with a little scarf.

“Hello,” Bella chirped. “It is rainy. I brought a slice of pear. But I am not sure how to share. Can you help me?”

Sunny’s eyes sparkled. “Yes! We have a plan. Strategy time!”

She drew a quick picture of the pear and cut it into three little pieces with a butter knife, with a grown-up nearby to watch. “We will add fruit to our plates,” Sunny said. “Fruit gives us vitamins and sweet smiles.”

Bella fluffed her feathers. “I like your plan. It is kind and smart.”

They all ate together. Crunch, nibble, sip. The cozy bedroom felt even warmer, full of happy friends and quiet rain music.

After lunch, Sunny cleaned up. She put the napkin in the wash basket. She stacked the cups. She tucked the plan paper into a small folder labeled “Plans.”

Pip asked, “Sunny, why do you make plans?”

Sunny brushed crumbs from her whiskers. “Plans help me think. Strategy is thinking before doing. It helps me use what I have. It helps me share. It keeps my body healthy.”

Bella chirped, “And it brings friends together!”

Sunny’s tail swished with joy. “Yes. Strategy, nutrition, and cooperation make a great team.”

The rain slowed. The lamp glowed softly. Sunny curled into her basket with a happy heart. “Tomorrow,” she whispered, “I will make a breakfast plan.”

She smiled, warm and cozy. She had used her brain, shared with friends, and made a healthy, balanced meal. She knew she could do it again.

The lesson was clear: Think first, plan your steps, choose healthy foods, and work together. When we use strategy and kindness, small snacks become big smiles.

The End.
