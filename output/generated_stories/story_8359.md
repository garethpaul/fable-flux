# Poppy the Problem-Solving Parrot

Down the creaky stairs, beneath the kitchen, there was a cozy basement lab. It smelled like cinnamon soap and warm paper. Little lights blinked and beeped. Bottles clinked. Soft whirring sounds hummed like a lullaby.

Poppy the parrot lived there with their friend, Dr. Dot. Poppy had bright green feathers with a splash of blue and gold. Poppy loved to make things. Poppy loved to think. Most of all, Poppy loved to ask, “Why?”

One rainy morning, Poppy fluttered to a tall table. “Squawk! I have an idea,” Poppy chirped. “Let’s make a Snack Sorter! It will put raisins in one bowl and crackers in another.”

Dr. Dot smiled and adjusted her round glasses. “Great idea, Poppy. Let’s think it through. What do we need?”

Poppy puffed up. “We need two bowls. We need a slide. We need a little door. And we need rules.”

“Rules?” asked Dr. Dot.

Poppy bobbed. “Rules help the sorter think. If snack is round and sticky, it goes left. If snack is flat and crunchy, it goes right. That is logical thinking!”

Dr. Dot clapped. “Yes! We make a plan. We test our plan. We fix our plan if it does not work.”

Poppy flapped happily. “Plan, test, fix!”

They set two red bowls on the table. They taped a smooth slide between them. They made a tiny door from a plastic card. Poppy wrote the rules on a sticky note with big letters: ROUND + STICKY = LEFT. FLAT + CRUNCHY = RIGHT.

Poppy dropped a raisin down the slide. Plink! It rolled to the left. “Hooray!”

Poppy dropped a cracker. Click! It skated to the right. “Hooray!”

Poppy felt proud. “We did it! We are smart. We used logic.”

Just then, a soft knock came from the stairs. It was Poppy’s cousin, Pip the parakeet. Pip hopped down, eyes wide. “Wow! What a lab! Can I try?”

Poppy fluttered. “Yes, but this is my project,” Poppy said gently. “Please ask before touching.”

Pip nodded quickly. “Okay! May I try one raisin?”

“Yes,” said Poppy. “One raisin.”

Pip reached into the raisin bag. But Pip loved raisins. Pip took a big handful and poured them all down the slide at once.

Sploosh! The raisins stuck together. The tiny door jammed. The machine groaned. The bowls clattered and slid. A cracker fell to the floor and cracked in half.

Poppy’s feathers fluffed. Poppy felt hot and tight in the chest. “Pip! You didn’t follow the rule. You took more than one. This is my project. I said one raisin.”

Pip’s eyes got wet. “I’m sorry. I got excited. I forgot. I should have asked again. I didn’t mean to break it.”

Dr. Dot put a hand on Poppy’s shoulder. “Let’s pause,” she said softly. “Take a breath. In… and out.”

Poppy breathed in and out. The hot feeling cooled. “Okay,” Poppy said. “I feel calmer. We can fix this. We will use logic.”

Poppy looked at the sticky note. “Step one: See the problem. The door is jammed. The raisins are stuck.”

“Step two,” said Dr. Dot. “Think of ideas.”

Poppy tapped the table. “Idea one: Pull the door. Idea two: Make the door bigger. Idea three: Sort one by one, not a big rush.”

Pip sniffled. “Idea four: Use a little flour so sticky raisins don’t clump.”

Poppy blinked. “Good idea, Pip.”

They tried idea one. Poppy gently pulled the tiny door. It moved, but it still stuck. They tried idea two. Dr. Dot trimmed the plastic just a little. Snip, snip. The door swung better.

They tried idea three. “One by one,” Poppy said. Plink. Plink. The raisins rolled left.

They tried idea four. Pip dusted a pinch of flour on the raisins. They did not clump. “It works!” Pip cheered.

Poppy fluffed with pride. “We used steps. We thought carefully. We tested. We fixed.”

Pip looked down. “And I will remember to ask and to wait my turn. I didn’t respect your project. I’m sorry, Poppy.”

Poppy listened to the soft whirring of the machines. Poppy felt the calm hum in their belly. “Thank you for saying sorry,” Poppy said. “I forgive you. Next time, we set clear rules. We can make a sign.”

They made a little sign with a happy blue border. Poppy wrote: ASK FIRST. ONE AT A TIME. FOLLOW THE RULES. Pip drew a tiny raisin and a cracker smiling.

Poppy taped the sign on the table. “Now we both know the boundary,” Poppy said. “A boundary is a kind rule that keeps people and projects safe.”

Pip nodded. “A boundary helps me remember. It is kind to ask.”

They tested the Snack Sorter again. Plink to the left. Click to the right. The bowls filled up with neat little piles.

“It works beautifully,” said Dr. Dot. “You used logical thinking. You practiced forgiveness. You learned about boundaries. And you educated each other.”

Poppy puffed with happy feathers. “Plan, test, fix, and be kind,” Poppy chirped. “That is good science and good friendship.”

Pip peeped, “And good snacks!”

They all laughed. The basement lab hummed softly. It smelled like cinnamon and crackers. The blinking lights blinked, and the tiny door swung just right. Poppy felt proud, calm, and full of ideas.

Poppy looked at Pip. “Want to help me make a Fruit Flipper next?” Poppy asked.

Pip smiled. “Yes. I will ask before I touch. One at a time.”

Poppy nodded. “Deal.”

The Snack Sorter clicked and plinked as they worked together, using their steps, their words, and their kind hearts.

The End.
