# Little Ebo’s Sunny Yard Parade

The sun was warm and bright in the backyard. The grass felt tickly on little Ebo’s feet. Ebo was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a happy, splashy trunk.

“Good morning, yard!” Ebo trumpeted in a friendly, quiet way. He did not want to scare the butterflies. They fluttered like tiny dancing leaves. A small breeze smelled like oranges from the tree. Birds sang, “Tweet-tweet, hello!”

Ebo loved his sunny backyard. Each day felt like a little adventure. Today, Ebo heard new sounds by the fence. Tap-tap. Rustle-rustle.

“Who is there?” Ebo asked softly.

A tiny snail peeked out. “I am Susi,” said the snail. Her shell shone like a tiny moon. “I move slow. That is okay.”

Next, a bright green frog hopped over. “Ribbit! I am Pako,” said the frog. “I like to jump and splash.”

Then a little red bird flew down. “Chirp! I am Lani,” she sang. “I sing songs from my far-away family.”

Ebo’s eyes grew big. “Welcome, friends! You are all different. That is wonderful.”

Susi smiled. “I came from the shady garden.”

Pako puffed up. “I came from the cool pond.”

Lani fluffed her wings. “I came from the tall tree.”

Ebo nodded. “We all share this sunny backyard. We can be kind neighbors.”

The friends heard another sound. Clink-clink. A small wagon bumped along the path. Inside the wagon were tiny flags in many colors. Red, blue, green, yellow, orange, purple! They flapped like happy fish tails in the breeze.

“What is that?” asked Pako.

“It is my Parade of Friends,” said Ebo. “We can celebrate that we are different and we all belong. We can help each other. We can learn from each other. That is being a good neighbor to the whole wide world.”

“Like global friends?” Lani chirped.

“Yes,” Ebo said. “Global friends!”

Ebo tugged the wagon, but it was heavy. The wheels went “Squeak-squeak.” He tried again. “Oof!”

“I can help!” said Pako. He pushed with his strong legs. “Ribbit!”

“I can guide,” said Susi. “I will show a smooth path.” She moved slowly but surely.

“I can watch from above,” said Lani. “I will look for bumps.” She flew up and sang, “This way! Smooth ground here!”

Together, the wagon rolled with a happy creak. Ebo smiled. “Thank you. Helping each other makes big jobs easy.”

They reached the sunny spot by the orange tree. Ebo gave each friend a tiny flag.

Susi held a blue flag. “It looks like a clear sky,” she said.

Pako took a green flag. “It looks like the pond!”

Lani chose a red flag. “It looks like a warm sunset.”

Ebo held a rainbow flag. “It looks like all of us together!”

They began the parade. Step, hop, glide, slide. The grass felt cool on Ebo’s feet. The sun felt like a soft hug. The flags swished and whispered. The bird sang. The frog ribbited. The snail hummed a tiny tune. Ebo trumpeted gently, “Toot-toot!”

They passed the garden. Ants lined up like tiny beads. “We march too!” the ants cheered.

They passed the sandbox. A shy beetle waved. “May I join?” asked the beetle.

“Yes,” said Ebo. “All friends are welcome.”

They passed the fence. A breeze brought a new smell, like sweet flowers. A ladybug landed on Ebo’s ear. “Hello,” she said. “I come from the other side.”

“Welcome,” said Ebo. “We share the sun.”

The parade reached a small puddle. Pako splashed. “Oops!” Water sprinkled everyone. Susi’s path got wet and slippery.

Susi wiggled. “I am slow. I might slip,” she said, a little worried.

Ebo bent his knee. “Climb on,” he said kindly. “My back is wide and safe.”

Susi climbed up. “Thank you, Ebo. I feel safe now.” She smiled, and her shell shone in the sun.

“That is global friendship,” Ebo said. “We make space for each other. We help when someone needs it.”

They kept going until they reached a shady spot near the fence. The friends rested. Ebo shared orange slices. The juice was sweet and drippy. “Mmm,” said Pako. “Delicious!”

Lani dabbed her beak. “Where I am from, we share fruit too,” she said.

Susi nibbled slowly. “In my garden, we share leaves.”

Ebo nodded. “We all share what we have. We all listen. We all care.”

The friends talked about their homes. They talked about their foods. They talked about their songs.

“I learned a new word,” said Ebo. “Neighbor. A neighbor is a friend near you. But I also learned another word: world. The world is big, with many neighbors. We can be kind to all of them.”

They finished the orange slices and stood up.

“Let’s end our parade with a promise,” said Ebo.

They raised their flags.

“I will listen,” said Lani.

“I will help,” said Pako.

“I will be patient,” said Susi.

“I will welcome everyone,” said Ebo. “Near or far, we are global friends.”

The sun began to yawn toward afternoon. The flags fluttered softly. The friends felt warm and happy.

Ebo smiled and said, “Today I learned that being a good global citizen means being kind, helpful, and respectful to all friends, from every place.”

The friends cheered together, “We belong together!”

The End.
