# Polly Paints the Road

Polly was a bright, creative parrot with feathers like crayons. Her wings shimmered green, blue, and a little gold. She loved to chirp, “Squawk! I make art!” Today, Polly hopped into the family car with Mia and Dad. The seats were soft and warm. The seatbelt clicked. The car hummed, “Vroom,” and the air smelled like lemon wipes and a little bit like snacks.

Polly peeked into her little bag. She had sticker stars, tiny crayons, and a small notepad. “Art time!” she sang. “Squawk!”

Mia buckled her teddy bear, Mr. Paws. “We’re going to the big park,” Mia said. “We can run and stretch and play.”

“Exercise!” Dad said with a smile in the rearview mirror. “We can move our bodies and take deep breaths.”

Polly stretched her wings. “Flap, flap, flap! Strong wings, strong body,” she chirped. She liked to flap gently in her seat to feel the air on her feathers.

The car rolled past trees and signs. Sunlight made warm spots on Polly’s toes. She pulled out her sticker stars. “I will make a road picture,” she said. “I will put one star on my notepad for every big tree we pass.”

Mia watched. “Can I have a star, too?” she asked.

Polly blinked. She loved her stars. They were shiny and new. “They are mine,” Polly said softly. “I want to make my road picture by myself.”

Mia looked out the window. Her smile drooped a little. Polly felt a tiny twist in her tummy. The car hummed. The tires whispered, “Whirr, whirr.” A gentle wind came through the window and tickled Polly’s cheek.

Dad said, “We all have special things. When we share, we grow together.”

Polly puffed her feathers. “But… I planned to use them,” she said. “I had an idea.” She tapped her notepad. Tap, tap.

Mia nodded. “You can make your picture,” she said. “I can watch.”

Polly looked at Mia’s hands. They were empty. She looked at her own hands, full of colorful things. Polly took a deep breath, like Dad had taught her. In. Out. In. Out. She felt calmer. “I can be creative,” Polly said, “and I can share.” She held out the sticker sheet. “We can make road art together.”

Mia’s eyes lit up. “Thank you, Polly!” she said. Her smile came back like sunshine.

Polly gave Mia three stars. “You can put stars for the blue signs. I’ll put stars for big trees,” Polly said. “We can be a team.”

The car turned a corner. “Blue sign!” Mia said, pressing a star on the notepad. Sticky. Press. Pop. The little sound made them giggle.

“Big tree!” Polly chirped, adding a gold star. The page began to sparkle. They shared crayons, too. Mia colored a tiny car, red and round. Polly drew a squiggly road that wound across the page. “Twist, turn, swoop!” she sang.

Dad peeked again. “Nice teamwork,” he said. “That’s independence and sharing. You make your own choices, and you help each other.”

Polly felt proud and warm. She made space for Mia’s ideas. Mia drew a little sun with a smile. “Look, our sun is happy,” Mia said.

Polly nodded. “Our sun is grateful,” she said. “Thank you, sun, for warm toes.”

Mia giggled. “Thank you, car, for smooth rides,” she added.

Polly tapped her beak. “Thank you, stickers, for sticking,” she said. “Thank you, wings, for flapping.”

They all laughed. The car smelled like fresh air now. The seatbelt felt snug, like a hug.

Suddenly, the car hit a tiny bump. The crayon rolled to the floor. “Oh no!” Mia said. “I can’t reach.”

“I can try,” Polly said. She leaned a little, kept her belt on, and stretched her toes. Tap. Tap. “Almost!” she chirped. She tried again. “I can’t get it… yet.” Her eyes were bright with the word yet. She wiggled her toes, took a deep breath, and tried once more. Tap—got it! “I did it!” she cheered, passing the crayon to Mia.

“Great growth mindset,” Dad said. “You kept trying.”

Polly felt strong. She felt helpful. She felt kind. She shared, and she could do hard things.

When they got to the park, the grass smelled sweet, and the sky looked big and blue. Mia and Dad did gentle stretches. Polly flapped her wings and did tiny hops on the seat while the car door stayed open and the breeze came in. “Hop, hop, hop!” she sang. “Exercise makes me happy.”

Before they went to play, Polly looked at the finished picture. Stars. Roads. Sun. Tiny red car. It was beautiful. It was theirs.

Mia held the notepad. “We made it together,” she said.

Polly smiled. “Sharing made it better,” she chirped. “I shared my stickers, and we shared ideas. When we share, we all shine.”

Dad nodded. “That’s the big lesson for today.”

Polly fluffed her feathers. “I am grateful for you,” she told Mia and Dad.

“We’re grateful for you, Polly,” Mia said, giving her a gentle finger hug.

Polly chirped, “Squawk! Sharing is caring, and caring makes art!”

They laughed, they stretched, and they went to play, feeling brave, kind, and strong.

The End.
