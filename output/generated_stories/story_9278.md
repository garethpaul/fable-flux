# Jump-Joy Joey and the Garden Shed Mystery

Jump-Joy Joey was a joyful kangaroo joey. His tail swished when he was happy, and today it swished like a broom. “Boing-boing!” he hummed as he hopped toward the garden shed. He loved the shed. It smelled like fresh soil and lemon soap. The wooden walls felt cool and smooth. Sunlight made tiny golden squares on the floor.

Joey peeked inside. “Hello, shed!” he giggled.

Inside the shed lived his friends: Penny the polite porcupine, Buzz the busy bee, and Tilly the tiny turtle. They all liked to help Grandma Garden keep tools tidy.

But today, something was wrong.

“Oh no,” whispered Joey. The big watering can was missing. The shelf was messy. A trowel lay on the floor with a little clink. A muddy trail dotted the boards.

Penny puffed gently. “It looks like someone forgot the rules.” She tapped a sign that said, “We share. We care. We put things back.”

Buzz buzzed in a worried swirl. “Grandma needs the can to water the beans! The beans are thirsty!”

Tilly blinked slowly. “We can figure this out,” she said. “Let’s be calm.”

Joey felt his heart beat fast. He took a deep breath. In…out. He placed a paw on his chest. “I feel worried,” he said. “But I can use my feelings to help. Let’s be a team.”

Penny nodded. “Teamwork means we listen and help each other.”

“Let’s look for clues,” said Joey. His ears twitched. “I hear soft drips.”

Drip. Drip. Drip.

They followed the muddy dots and the gentle drip to the back of the shed. Joey sniffed. It smelled like wet earth and mint. He brushed aside a burlap sack. Behind it, a small door stood open to the garden. A cool breeze tickled their noses.

“Look!” Buzz pointed with a tiny leg. Outside, in the herb patch, sat the big watering can. It was tipped on its side. A little puddle sparkled in the sun.

Joey hopped closer. He saw short, round footprints in the mud. He saw a leaf stuck to the handle. He blinked. “I don’t want to guess wrong,” he said. “That would not be fair.”

“That’s respectful,” Tilly said. “We should ask before we blame.”

Penny tapped her quills lightly. “Let’s hold a tiny team meeting.”

They made a small circle in the warm grass. The bees hummed. The mint leaves smelled sweet and sharp.

Joey said, “What do we know?”

Buzz buzzed, thinking. “We know the watering can is here. We know someone moved it. The beans are still thirsty.”

Tilly added, “We also know the shelf is messy, and the door was left open.”

Penny said, “What can we do?”

Joey’s tail swished. “We can clean the shelf. We can bring the can back. We can find who used it, and we can be kind when we ask.”

Everyone agreed. They put their paws, wings, and flippers together. “Teamwork!”

They worked as one. Joey lifted the can with a soft grunt. “Heave-ho!” Penny sorted tools gently, trowel by trowel. Buzz zipped up high and dusted the sign so it shone. Tilly closed the little door with a slow, careful push. Click.

Just then, a small voice squeaked, “Um… excuse me.”

A young hedgehog peeked from the thyme. His eyes were wide, and his paws were muddy. A mint leaf clung to his back like a tiny cape.

“I’m Nib,” he said. “I took the watering can this morning. I wanted to help the herbs. I didn’t know the rules. Then the can felt heavy, and I dropped it. I was scared and ran away. I didn’t clean up. I’m sorry.”

Joey felt his worried feeling change to a warm one. He smiled softly. “Thank you for telling the truth, Nib. That is brave.”

Buzz hovered by Nib’s ear. “It’s fair to fix what we break,” Buzz said kindly. “That is justice.”

Penny nodded. “And we show respect by teaching, not yelling.”

Tilly looked at Nib. “Would you like to join our team? We can show you the shed rules.”

Nib’s prickles relaxed. “Yes, please.”

Joey hopped in a happy circle. “Let’s decide together what to do next.”

They made a plan:
- Nib would help clean the muddy spots.
- Joey and Tilly would water the beans, slow and even. Splash, swish.
- Penny and Buzz would draw a picture chart of the rules: share, care, put back.

They worked side by side. Joey held the can steady while Tilly guided the spout. The water sounded like soft rain. The soil smelled rich and sleepy. The beans lifted their leaves as if they were smiling.

Nib wiped the floor with a cloth. “Wipe, wipe,” he sang. Penny stuck the picture chart on the wall. Buzz added a tiny star sticker.

When they finished, the shed looked neat and calm again. Sunlight made the tools twinkle.

Grandma Garden peeked in with a gentle smile. “Oh my,” she said. “Teamwork made this place shine!”

Nib stepped forward. “I made a mistake,” he said. “But I told the truth, and I helped fix it.”

Grandma nodded. “That is fair and kind.”

Joey’s chest felt warm and proud. He looked at his friends. “We solved the problem together. We listened. We were respectful. We made a good choice.”

Everyone cheered. “Teamwork!”

Joey swished his tail and bounced once. “Boing! When we work as a team,” he said, “hard things feel smaller, and friends feel bigger.”

The End.
