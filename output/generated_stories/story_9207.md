# Tiko the Tortoise Listens

In a cozy bedroom, soft and warm like a nest, the nightlight glowed like a tiny moon. A wise tortoise named Tiko lived there with his friends, three little stuffed animals: Berry the Bunny, Pock the Panda, and Star the Seal. Tiko’s shell was smooth and green, and it smelled a little like clean leaves after rain.

“Bedtime tidy-up,” hummed Tiko in a gentle voice. “Then wash, brush, and hush.”

Berry bounced on the quilt. “Play more!” she squeaked.

Pock rolled a toy car across the rug. “Vroom, vroom!”

Star hugged a shiny bottle. “I found this!” He shook it and it clinked. It was an empty bottle that had rolled under the bed.

Tiko blinked slowly. He liked to think before he talked. He listened to the tiny hum of the nightlight. He listened to the soft rattle of the bottle. He listened to his friends’ voices. Listening helped him choose kind words.

“We can play tomorrow,” Tiko said. “Now we do our chores. We tidy toys, put trash in the bin, and put clean things in the recycle box.”

“What is re-sy-kull?” asked Berry, tilting her head.

“Re-cy-cle,” Tiko said, smiling. He spoke slowly so the words felt safe. “We give bottles, paper, and cans a new life. They go in the blue box, not the trash. It helps our Earth.”

Star held up the bottle. “New life?” His whiskers twitched.

“Yes,” Tiko said. “Like a story that starts again.”

Pock huffed. “But I want to race. I don’t like stopping.”

Tiko took a small breath. He listened to Pock’s feelings. Pock felt wiggly and sad to stop. “I hear you,” Tiko said. “You want more fun. Let’s make tidy-up a race. We can sing a tidy song.”

Tiko began a soft song with a cozy beat. “Pick and place, take your space. Clean and kind, a calm, safe place.”

They all sang. Berry put crayons in a tin. The crayons made a cheerful clack-clack. Pock rolled cars into the basket. The wheels whispered shh-shh. Star carried the bottle to the blue box. Clink! It sounded like a little bell.

“Re-cycle!” Star cheered. “I did it!”

“Good listening,” Tiko said, and his eyes shone like the nightlight. He turned to the small trash bin. “Wrappers go here,” he said. “Not on the bed.”

Berry’s ears drooped. “I left a wrapper on the pillow,” she whispered. “I forgot.”

Tiko nodded. “Thank you for telling me. We all forget sometimes. We can fix it. Let’s be gentle with ourselves.”

Berry smiled and picked up the wrapper. “Trash, not bed,” she said. The bin made a soft thump.

Soon the room smelled like lemon from a clean cloth. The quilt felt smooth under little hands. The blue recycle box stood proud near the door.

“Now hygiene,” Tiko said. He made the word sound friendly. “We wash hands and brush teeth. Clean hands help keep us well.”

Pock frowned. “I don’t like the cold water.”

Tiko listened again. “You don’t like cold. We can use warm water.” He turned the tap, and warm mist floated like tiny clouds. The water sounded like a tiny river, hush-hush. Pock giggled and rubbed soap. Bubbles popped—pop, pop! Berry washed too, and Star brushed his little seal teeth with minty foam.

“Mint tastes tingly,” Star said, smacking his lips.

Tiko brushed slowly, round and round. “We are kind to our bodies,” he said between gentle brushes. “We listen to them, and we care for them.”

Back in the cozy bedroom, Pock saw a block near Star’s tail. “Move,” Pock said, a little too loud. Star flinched. “I was keeping it safe,” he whispered.

Tiko felt the room get tight. He listened to the quiet feelings. He heard Pock’s hurry and Star’s worry. He spoke softly. “Let’s try kind words. Pock, you can say, ‘Please.’ Star, you can say, ‘I will share.’ We listen to each other. We take turns.”

Pock took a breath. “Please, can I have the block?”

Star held it out. “I will share.” Their paws and flippers touched. Warm and soft.

“Thank you,” Pock said. His voice was calm now.

Tiko smiled. “That is tolerance. We all like different things. We can still be kind and listen.”

They climbed into the quilt. The fabric felt like a hug. Outside, the wind whispered through the window screen. The nightlight hummed its tiny song.

“Tiko,” Berry murmured, “listening feels nice.”

“Yes,” Tiko said. “When we listen, we learn. We help our Earth by recycling. We help our friends with kind words. We help our bodies with clean hands and teeth. Listening shows love.”

Star yawned. “I hear the night.”

Pock tucked in his cars. “I hear my sleepy.”

Tiko closed his wise eyes. “Good night. Tomorrow we will listen again.”

The room was neat, the blue box was ready, and everyone felt safe. They had a problem—too much noise, mess, and fuss—but listening helped them find a gentle solution.

The End.
