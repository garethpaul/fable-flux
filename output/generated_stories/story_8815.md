# Ellie Speaks Up

Little Ellie the elephant calf sat quietly in the corner of the bright, colorful living room. Soft sunlight streamed through the big windows, making rainbow patterns on the fluffy carpet. Around her, all the other animal friends were having fun.

"Let's play soccer!" trumpeted Max the monkey, bouncing a red and white ball.

"I want to paint pictures!" chirped Bella the bunny, holding up paintbrushes that sparkled in the light.

"Story time! Story time!" sang the twin birds, flapping their wings excitedly.

Ellie watched with her big, kind eyes. She wanted to join in, but she felt too shy to speak up. Her trunk swayed nervously as she thought, "What if they don't want to play with me? What if I'm not good enough?"

The room filled with happy sounds - the thump-thump of the soccer ball, the swish-swish of paintbrushes, and the gentle rustle of turning pages. But Ellie just sat there, feeling left out.

"Ellie looks sad," whispered Bella, noticing the little elephant's droopy ears.

Max stopped bouncing his ball. "Why don't you come play with us, Ellie?"

Ellie's cheeks turned pink under her gray skin. "I... I don't know what I'm good at," she said in a very quiet voice.

"What do you mean?" asked the twin birds, tilting their heads.

Ellie took a deep breath. Her mama had taught her to use her words when she needed help. "Well," she said a little louder, "I love to paint, but I think my trunk is too big for the brushes. And I love soccer, but I'm worried I might accidentally step on the ball with my big feet."

"Oh!" said Bella, her nose twitching with excitement. "Your trunk isn't too big - it's perfect! You could paint the most beautiful, swirly pictures!"

"And your feet are great for soccer!" added Max. "You could be our goalie! Your big feet would help stop the ball!"

Ellie felt a warm, happy feeling in her tummy. "Really? You think so?"

"Of course!" chirped the birds. "And you could read stories to us with your loud, clear voice!"

Ellie stood up tall, her trunk swinging with confidence. "I would love to try painting first! But could you show me how to hold the brush with my trunk?"

"Absolutely!" said Bella, hopping over with a fat, easy-to-hold brush. "Here, try this one!"

Ellie wrapped her trunk gently around the brush. It felt just right! She dipped it in bright blue paint and made a big, swooping line across the paper. "Look! I did it!"

"That's beautiful!" everyone cheered.

After painting, they played soccer. Ellie discovered she was an amazing goalie, using her trunk to catch the ball and her big feet to block shots. Then she read a story in her clear, strong voice while everyone gathered around.

As the sun began to set, painting the living room walls with orange and pink light, Ellie felt proud and happy.

"Thank you for helping me," she said to her friends. "I learned something important today."

"What's that?" asked Max.

"When I use my words to tell others how I feel and what I need, good things happen! Speaking up for myself helped me find out what I'm good at."

Bella nodded wisely. "Everyone has special talents. Sometimes we just need to be brave enough to try!"

From that day on, whenever Ellie wanted to join in or needed help, she remembered to speak up clearly and kindly. And she discovered she was good at many wonderful things!

The End.
