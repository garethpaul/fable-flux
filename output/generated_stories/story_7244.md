# Tia Turtle’s World Stories

Tia was a shy turtle. She had a smooth green shell and gentle eyes. She lived near a big oak tree with a cozy treehouse. The wooden steps felt warm in the sun and cool in the shade. Birds sang, leaves rustled, and the air smelled like sweet pine.

One bright morning, Tia peeked out from her shell. “Today,” she whispered, “I will visit the treehouse.”

Up the steps she went, slow and careful. Tap… tap… tap. Inside the treehouse, friends were waiting on soft pillows: Niko the squirrel, Bella the bluebird, and Jamal the bunny. A globe sat on the floor, round and shiny, like a big blue marble.

“Hi, Tia!” chirped Bella.

“Want to share a story?” asked Niko.

Tia tucked her head a little. “I’m shy,” she said softly. “But I want to try.”

Jamal smiled. “We take turns. That’s fair.”

They all nodded. “Fair means everyone gets a chance,” said Bella.

Niko spun the globe gently. It hummed under his paws. “Let’s tell world stories,” he said. “We can learn about places far away.”

Tia touched the globe with a careful claw. It felt smooth and cool. “The world is big,” she whispered. “Are the people far away like us?”

“Let’s find out,” said Jamal. “We can share what we know, and listen.”

Bella went first. “I will tell a story from the sky!” She fluffed her feathers. “Once, I flew over the sea. The air smelled salty, and waves swished, swish, swish. I saw boats. People waved. They wore hats to shade the sun. They smiled. Smiles are the same everywhere!”

Everyone clapped. Niko’s tail twitched. “My turn,” he said. “I read a book about a market in a faraway place. People sold bright fruit—mangoes and bananas. The colors were yellow and orange and pink. Children shared sweets. They lined up, so everyone got some. That was fair.”

Jamal thumped his foot. “My story is about music,” he said. “I heard a drum in a video. Boom-boom, tap-tap! Kids danced in a circle. They took turns in the center. No pushing. That was fair and fun.”

Tia listened. Her heart felt warm, like sunshine on her shell. “I like your stories,” she said. “They sound different and the same.”

Bella tilted her head. “Would you like to tell a story, Tia?”

Tia looked at the globe. She took a slow breath. In and out. “I can try,” she said.

Her voice was small but clear. “I met a girl at the pond,” Tia began. “Her name was Asha. She wore a bright scarf. It was soft and red. She showed me how to carry water in a little cup without spilling. We walked slow. We shared sips. We talked about our homes. Asha said her grandma tells stories at night. I told her my treehouse has a globe. We both like stories.”

“How did it make you feel?” asked Jamal.

“Happy,” said Tia. “And calm. Asha taught me a word from her home. ‘Namaste.’ It means hello. I taught her ‘hello’ too. We smiled. Our words were different, but our smiles were the same.”

Niko clapped softly. “That’s a beautiful world story.”

Bella hopped in place. “Let’s play a game! We will share and be fair.”

She put a bowl of round crackers in the center. They smelled buttery. “We take turns,” Bella said. “One, two, three. Everyone gets the same.”

Tia counted in a tiny voice. “One for Bella, one for Niko, one for Jamal, one for me.” The crackers crunched. Crunch, crunch. They sipped cool water. Sip, sip.

“Let’s make a kindness map,” said Jamal. He drew a big circle on paper, like the globe. “We will mark places we learn about.”

Niko made a yellow dot and wrote “market.” Bella made a blue dot and wrote “sea.” Jamal made a red dot and wrote “music.” Tia made a green dot and drew a little cup and a scarf. “This is for Asha,” she said. “We learned from each other.”

They hung the kindness map on the treehouse wall. Sunlight made it glow. The room felt warm with friends and stories.

“I was shy,” Tia said, “but I told my story.”

Bella smiled. “Your voice matters.”

Niko nodded. “And we listened. That is fair.”

Jamal added, “Now we know more about our big world. People eat different foods. They wear different clothes. They use different words. But we can still be kind, share, and take turns.”

Tia lifted her chin. “We can say hello in many ways,” she said. “Hello. Namaste. Hola. Jambo.” The words felt like little bells. Ding, ding, ding.

They all giggled. The leaves whispered outside. A sweet breeze tickled their noses.

Tia touched the globe again. “The world is big,” she said, “and my heart can be big too.”

“Yes,” said her friends together. “We are fair friends in a wide, wonderful world.”

The End.
