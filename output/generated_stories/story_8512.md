# Milo's Big Feelings Day

Milo the monkey sat on his favorite wooden rocking chair on the peaceful front porch. The morning sun felt warm on his fuzzy brown fur, and he could smell the sweet flowers blooming in the garden. But today, Milo didn't feel happy like usual.

"My tummy feels funny," Milo said to himself, touching his belly with his small hands. "And my heart is beating fast like a drum - thump, thump, thump!"

Milo's friend Bella the bird landed on the porch railing. "Good morning, Milo! Want to play hide-and-seek?"

Milo shook his head. "No thank you, Bella. I don't feel like playing today."

Bella tilted her tiny head. "Oh! What's wrong? You look sad."

Milo thought carefully about how he felt inside. "I think... I think I'm nervous about going to my first swimming lesson today. My hands feel shaky, and my stomach has butterflies in it."

"That sounds scary," said Bella kindly. "But you know what? It's okay to feel nervous. Everyone gets nervous sometimes!"

Just then, Grandpa Monkey came out onto the porch carrying two glasses of cool lemonade. The ice cubes clinked together like tiny bells.

"Here you go, little one," Grandpa said, sitting in the chair next to Milo. "You look worried about something."

Milo took a sip of the sweet, tangy lemonade. "Grandpa, I'm scared about swimming lessons. What if I can't do it? What if the water is too deep?"

Grandpa's eyes crinkled with a warm smile. "Milo, it's very brave of you to tell me how you feel. Do you know what courage means?"

Milo shook his head, his tail swishing back and forth.

"Courage doesn't mean you're not scared," Grandpa explained. "Courage means you feel scared, but you try anyway. And you know what helps when we feel nervous?"

"What?" asked Milo, leaning forward.

"We can take deep breaths like this," Grandpa showed him. "Breathe in through your nose... and out through your mouth. Feel how that makes your body calm down?"

Milo tried it. "Wow! My tummy feels better already!"

"And," continued Grandpa, "we can tell ourselves brave words. Say this with me: 'I am brave. I can try new things. It's okay to feel scared.'"

Milo repeated the words, and his voice got stronger each time. "I am brave! I can try new things! It's okay to feel scared!"

Bella chirped happily. "You sound so confident now, Milo!"

"I still feel a little nervous," Milo admitted honestly. "But now I know that's normal. And I want to learn to swim!"

Grandpa patted Milo's back gently. "That's my brave monkey! Remember, if you feel scared during your lesson, you can always tell your teacher. Good teachers want to help you feel safe."

"I will!" said Milo, standing up tall. "And if I don't learn everything today, I can keep trying tomorrow, right?"

"Exactly!" laughed Grandpa. "Learning takes practice and patience."

As they walked to the swimming pool together, Milo felt proud. He had learned something very important: feelings have names, it's okay to share them with people who care, and being brave means trying even when you feel scared.

"Thank you for helping me understand my feelings, Grandpa," Milo said, swinging their hands together.

"Anytime, my courageous little monkey," Grandpa replied with a smile.

The End.
