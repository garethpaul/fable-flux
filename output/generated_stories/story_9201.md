# Leo's Garden Friends

Leo was a brave little lion cub who loved spending time in his family's balcony garden. The morning sun felt warm on his golden fur as he padded between the colorful flower pots and vegetable boxes.

"Good morning, garden!" Leo said cheerfully, his small tail swishing with excitement. He loved to help water the plants and watch the butterflies dance from flower to flower.

But today, something was different. As Leo approached his favorite sunny spot by the tomato plants, he heard a tiny voice.

"Excuse me," squeaked the voice. "You're standing on my home!"

Leo looked down and saw a small brown snail with a beautiful spiral shell. The snail's eyes were stretched up on tiny stalks, looking worried.

"Oh no!" Leo gasped, quickly moving his paw. "I'm so sorry! I didn't see you there."

The snail sighed. "Nobody ever sees me. I'm too slow and too small. All the other garden creatures think I'm just in the way."

Leo's heart felt sad. He sat down carefully, making sure not to squish anything. "What's your name?"

"I'm Shelly," said the snail. "I've been trying to help in the garden, but I can't fly like the bees or hop like the grasshoppers. I feel so useless."

Leo thought for a moment. His mama had taught him that everyone was special in their own way. "Maybe we can find something you're really good at, Shelly. Want to explore the garden together?"

Shelly's eyes brightened. "Really? You want to be my friend?"

"Of course!" Leo smiled. "Come on, climb on my back. We'll go on an adventure!"

As they moved through the garden, Leo noticed something amazing. Wherever Shelly had crawled, the plants looked extra healthy and shiny.

"Look, Shelly!" Leo pointed with his paw. "The leaves you've been on are the greenest ones in the whole garden!"

Shelly looked surprised. "Really?"

Just then, Mama Lion appeared on the balcony. "Leo, what are you doing?"

"Mama, this is my new friend Shelly! Look how she makes the plants healthy!"

Mama Lion smiled warmly. "Oh my! Shelly, you're helping the plants by eating the tiny bugs that make them sick. And your slime trail gives them special nutrients! You're a wonderful garden helper."

Shelly beamed with pride. "I never knew I was helping!"

"Everyone has special gifts," Mama Lion explained. "Sometimes we just need to look closely to find them."

Leo had an idea. "Shelly, would you like to be my garden partner? I can carry you to the places that need your help, and you can show me which plants need extra care!"

"That sounds perfect!" Shelly agreed. "We can share the work!"

From that day on, Leo and Shelly worked together every morning. Leo learned to move slowly and gently, while Shelly gained confidence in her unique abilities. The other garden creatures soon saw how well they worked as a team.

"You know what, Shelly?" Leo said one sunny afternoon as they finished watering the herbs. "Being different makes us special, not strange."

Shelly nodded happily from her spot on Leo's head. "And working together makes everything better!"

The garden had never looked more beautiful, and Leo had learned that accepting others and sharing talents made the whole world brighter.

The End.
