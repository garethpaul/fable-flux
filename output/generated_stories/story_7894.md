# Kip the Joey Learns to Share

Kip was a happy little kangaroo joey who loved bouncing on the sunny rooftop of his apartment building. *Boing, boing, boing!* His strong legs carried him high into the warm air, and he giggled with joy every time he landed.

The rooftop was Kip's favorite place to play. It had soft green grass, colorful flowers in pots, and the most wonderful view of the city below. But Kip had a big problem - he didn't like sharing his special rooftop with anyone else.

One bright morning, Mrs. Rabbit came up to the rooftop carrying a basket of dirty clothes. "Good morning, Kip!" she said cheerfully. "I need to hang my laundry to dry in the sunshine."

"No!" Kip said loudly, crossing his arms. "This is MY rooftop! Go away!" He felt angry and his heart was beating fast.

Mrs. Rabbit's ears drooped sadly. "Oh my," she whispered, and slowly walked back downstairs with her heavy basket.

Next, old Mr. Turtle climbed slowly up to the rooftop. His shell gleamed in the sunlight. "Hello there, young joey," he puffed. "I was hoping to tend to these beautiful flowers. They need fresh water."

"NO!" Kip shouted even louder. "These are MY flowers! You can't touch them!" His face felt hot and red.

Mr. Turtle blinked his wise old eyes sadly and turned around, moving very slowly back down the stairs.

Then little Mouse family scurried up, carrying tiny soap bubbles and washcloths. "We just want to give our babies a bath in the sunshine," squeaked Mama Mouse. "The warm air helps them stay healthy and clean."

"GO AWAY!" Kip yelled, stomping his feet. "This whole rooftop belongs to ME!"

The Mouse family's whiskers trembled, and they hurried away with their squeaky babies.

Kip bounced alone on his rooftop, but something felt different. The sunshine didn't seem as bright. The flowers looked droopy without Mr. Turtle's care. The rooftop felt... empty and sad.

Kip sat down and thought hard. His tummy felt funny - not happy-funny, but worried-funny. He remembered how Mrs. Rabbit's ears had drooped, how slowly Mr. Turtle had walked away, and how the little mouse babies had squeaked sadly.

"Oh no," Kip said to himself. "I made everyone feel bad. That wasn't very nice of me."

Kip hopped downstairs to find his neighbors. First, he knocked on Mrs. Rabbit's door. "I'm sorry I was mean," he said softly. "Would you like to hang your clean clothes on our rooftop? I can help you carry the basket!"

Mrs. Rabbit's ears perked up. "That's very kind of you, Kip!"

Next, he visited Mr. Turtle. "I'm sorry I yelled," Kip said. "The flowers DO need your help. Will you teach me how to water them?"

"Of course, my boy," smiled Mr. Turtle. "We can take turns caring for them."

Finally, Kip found the Mouse family. "I'm sorry I was selfish," he said. "Your babies should have clean baths in the sunshine. Can I help make bubbles?"

"Oh yes!" squeaked the little mice happily.

Soon, the rooftop was full of friends working together. Mrs. Rabbit hung her fresh-smelling laundry while Kip held the clothespins. Mr. Turtle showed Kip how to gently water each flower. The Mouse babies splashed in their tiny tubs while Kip made the biggest, most beautiful soap bubbles.

"This is much more fun than playing alone," Kip laughed, bouncing happily. "When we share and help each other, everyone feels good!"

From that day on, Kip's rooftop became the happiest place in the whole building, where friends worked and played together with respect and kindness.

The End.
