# Toby the Tortoise and the Rainy Day Rescue

In a cozy garden shed filled with the sweet smell of fresh flowers and earthy soil, lived Toby the Tortoise. His shell was green and brown like the leaves outside, and his eyes sparkled with kindness. The wooden shed was his special home, with soft hay for his bed and a little water dish that caught raindrops from the roof.

One gray morning, Toby heard something new. *Tap, tap, tap!* It wasn't the usual gentle rain on the shed roof. It sounded like tiny voices crying.

"Help! Help!" squeaked the voices.

Toby slowly poked his wrinkled head outside. The rain was falling hard, making puddles everywhere. Near the shed door, he saw three little field mice shivering in the cold water. Their tiny brown fur was soaking wet, and their whiskers trembled.

"Oh my!" said Toby in his deep, gentle voice. "You poor little ones! Come inside my warm shed right away."

The smallest mouse looked up with big, scared eyes. "But... but we're just mice. Why would you help us?"

Toby's heart felt warm and fuzzy. He remembered when he was young and got caught in a storm. A kind rabbit had helped him then. "Because when someone needs help, we help them," Toby said softly. "That's what friends do."

The mice scurried into the shed, their tiny feet pattering on the wooden floor. But they were still shivering! Toby thought hard. How could he make them feel better?

"I know!" Toby said. He carefully pulled some of his softest hay from his bed. "Here, snuggle in this warm hay. It smells like sunshine!"

The mice buried themselves in the golden hay, and their shivering stopped. But their tummies made loud rumbling sounds.

"Oh dear, you must be hungry too," Toby noticed. He had been saving some delicious lettuce leaves for later, but seeing the hungry mice made him want to share. "Would you like some fresh lettuce? I picked the crunchiest pieces!"

The mice's eyes grew wide. "You would share your food with us?"

"Of course!" Toby smiled. "Food tastes better when we share it with friends."

As the mice munched happily, making tiny crunching sounds, the middle mouse asked, "Toby, how did you know we needed help?"

Toby settled down on his hay bed, his shell making a soft *thump* on the ground. "I listened with my heart," he explained. "When I heard scared voices, I felt worried too. When I saw you shivering, I remembered how cold feels. That's called empathy - feeling what others feel."

The rain kept falling outside, but inside the shed, everyone felt warm and safe. The mice told Toby about their home that had flooded, and Toby shared stories about other garden friends he had helped.

When the sun finally peeked through the clouds, the mice were dry and full. "Thank you, Toby," they said together. "You saved us!"

"And now we know how to help others too," added the smallest mouse. "We'll listen with our hearts, just like you!"

Toby watched his new friends scamper home to rebuild their nest. His heart felt as warm as sunshine. He had learned something important: when we help others, we feel happy too. And the mice had learned that kindness grows when we share it.

From that day on, Toby's garden shed became known as the kindest place in the whole garden, where anyone who needed help would always find a friend.

The End.
