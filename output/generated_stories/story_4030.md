# Pico the Parrot Finds a Gentle Song

In a bright creative art room, sunshine spilled in like warm lemonade. Paints sat in tidy rows. Crayons rested in little cups. Paper smelled like fresh rain. In the middle of the room stood Pico, a creative parrot with rainbow feathers and curious eyes.

Pico loved to make art. He also loved to sing. “Squawk! Color, color, tap and hum!” he chirped, hopping from table to table. His claws clicked on the floor. His wings fluttered like soft fans.

Today, Pico wanted to make a big music picture. He tapped paintbrushes like drums. Tap tap! He shook a jar of buttons. Rattle rattle! He blew gently across a bottle. Whooo. “Listen!” Pico sang. “I can make music with art things!”

Lulu the ladybug rolled by on a tiny toy car. “Hi, Pico,” she buzzed. “I like your sounds.”

Pico puffed up with pride. “Thank you, Lulu! I am a music maker.” He flapped and added more sounds. Tap, rattle, whooo, clack, clink!

But the sounds got louder. The room felt buzzy and busy. Markers rolled off the table. A stack of paper slid to the floor with a soft fffft. Tiny beads wobbled in a dish and then spilled like rain. Tink, tink, tink!

Toby the turtle peeked out from under a chair. “It is too loud,” he whispered. He pulled his shell tighter.

Pico stopped. His heart thumped fast. He looked around. “Oh no,” he said softly. “The room is messy. My friends are not calm.” He felt a little twist in his tummy. “I did not mean to make trouble.”

Lulu picked up a crayon. “Maybe we can fix it,” she said.

Pico took a deep breath. In. Out. His feathers settled. “Yes,” he said. “First, I will say sorry.”

He walked to Toby. “Toby,” he said in a gentle voice, “I am sorry I made it too loud. I was excited. I did not see how you felt.”

Toby slowly smiled. “Thank you, Pico,” he said. “I feel better when it is calm.”

Pico nodded. “I want to learn a calm song,” he said. “I want to make music that helps, not hurts.”

He looked at the art room with new eyes. He made a discovery. The brushes, cups, and papers could be friends with his music if he used soft hands and a quiet heart.

“Let’s clean and sort,” said Lulu. “We can put things back.”

Together, they worked. Pico used his beak to lift paper back into a neat stack. Swish. Lulu rolled markers into their cup. Click, click. Toby nudged beads into a little bowl. Plink, plink. The sounds were small and gentle, like tiny rain on a leaf.

Pico felt calm in his chest. He listened. The room had a sweet hush. He discovered new sounds in the quiet. The soft brush on paper made a shhh sound. Tapping a wooden block with one claw made a small tok. Blowing across the bottle very softly made a low whoooo that felt like a warm hug.

Pico smiled. “I found a calm song,” he said. “Listen.”

He stood still. He took a slow breath. “In… and out…” he whispered. Then he tapped the block: tok… tok. He brushed the paper: shhh… shhh. He blew the bottle: whoooo.

Toby’s eyes grew wide. “That is nice,” he said. “It feels like a nap cloud.”

Lulu giggled. “A nap cloud! I like that.”

Pico added words in a soft voice. “Tap so tiny, brush so slow. Breathe in, breathe out, let it flow.” His wings moved like leaves. The room glowed with calm.

They painted as they played. Pico dipped his brush in blue and made smooth waves. Shhh. Lulu dotted yellow suns. Tip, tip. Toby rolled green paint with his shell to make a hill. Mmmm, smooth.

Pico organized the tools as they used them. “Brushes in the cup,” he said. “Markers in the bin. Beads in the bowl.” He made little labels with pictures: a brush picture on the cup, a marker picture on the bin, a bead picture on the bowl. “This helps us find and put away,” he told his friends. “Organization keeps us calm.”

When they finished, the art room was tidy again. The air smelled like clean paper and a little bit of lemon soap. The sun made soft squares on the floor.

Pico turned to Toby and Lulu. “Thank you for helping,” he said. “I am happy we made calm music.”

Toby nodded. “I feel safe,” he said.

Lulu buzzed in a circle. “Me too!”

Pico fluffed his feathers. “I learned something today,” he said. “Discovery can be loud or quiet. I discovered that gentle music and clean spaces make hearts feel good. When I make a mistake, I can say sorry and make it right. I can slow down, breathe, and organize.”

He sang his new soft song again, and they all joined in.

“Tap so tiny, brush so slow.
Breathe in, breathe out, let it flow.
Put things back, nice and neat.
Calm and kind from head to feet.”

They smiled, and the creative art room smiled too.

The End.
