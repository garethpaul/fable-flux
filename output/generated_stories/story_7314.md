# Squeak the Squirrel Says What He Needs

In a busy basement, full of hums and soft clanks, lived a resourceful squirrel named Squeak. The basement smelled like warm wood and clean laundry soap. There were boxes, baskets, a whirring washing machine, and a patch of sunlight coming through a small window. Squeak loved to explore. He loved to solve little problems and discover new things.

One morning, Squeak found a shiny red button on an old toy train. “Click?” he wondered, touching it with one tiny paw. The train chimed, ding-ding. “Ooo!” Squeak giggled. “A new sound!”

Just then, his friends scrambled in. Tippy the mouse squeaked, “Let’s race toy cars!” Hop the cricket chirped, “Let’s sing loud songs!” Pip the pigeon cooed, “Let’s build a tall tower!”

The basement buzzed with energy. Cars zoomed. Songs bounced. Blocks clacked. Squeak’s ears felt full. His tail twitched. He wanted space to listen to his little train. He wanted to think.

Squeak tried to squeeze into a quiet corner behind a soft blanket. “Maybe it will be calm here,” he whispered. But the blanket slipped, whoosh, and dropped. “Oops,” said Tippy. “Race time!”

Squeak’s heart went thump-thump. He liked his friends. He liked their games. But he also liked his plan. He wanted to press the red button and learn the sounds. He wanted to ask for a turn and a little space.

Squeak took a deep breath. He put his paw on his chest and felt it rise and fall. In, out. In, out. He remembered a helpful idea: Be brave. Use kind words. Say what you need.

Squeak stepped forward. “Friends,” he said in a clear voice, “I need a turn. I need a little quiet space. I want to test this train.”

Tippy paused, one tiny car in each hand. Hop stopped mid-chirp. Pip tilted his head. The room felt softer for a moment. The washing machine hummed, mmmmmm.

Tippy asked, “You need a turn?”

“Yes,” said Squeak. “Please wait a bit. Then we can all play.”

Hop whispered, “I can sing quietly. Chirp… chirp.”

Pip nodded. “We can make a space.”

They moved a box, scoot, scoot, to make a little corner just for Squeak. They put a cushion there. They called it the thinking spot. The light from the window fell on it like a friendly blanket.

“Thank you,” said Squeak. His whiskers wiggled with relief. He pressed the red button. Ding-ding. He listened. He pressed again. Ding-dong. He tried holding the button longer. Diiiiing. He smiled. “It makes different sounds,” he said. “Short press, short sound. Long press, long sound.”

“That is smart thinking,” said Pip. “Logical thinking,” added Squeak with a grin. “I look. I test. I learn.”

He lined up three bottle caps as music notes. “One, two, three,” he said, and tapped the button three times. Ding, ding, ding. He tried a pattern: short, short, long. Ding, ding, diiiing. He clapped softly. “A sound pattern!”

Tippy’s eyes shone. “Can I try the pattern after you?”

“Yes,” Squeak said, “after I finish this test. Then it’s your turn.” His voice was firm and kind. He felt proud. He had asked for what he needed.

Hop tiptoed closer. “Can I sing to match the sounds? Quietly?”

“Yes,” said Squeak. “Soft singing is okay.”

They worked together. Squeak pressed: ding, ding, diiiing. Hop sang: la, la, laaa. Pip counted in a gentle coo. Tippy waited with a smile and his little cars resting in his lap.

After a while, Squeak nodded. “I’m done with my test. Thank you for waiting. Now, Tippy’s turn.” He passed the train with both paws.

Tippy pressed, ding! “My turn feels good,” he said.

Squeak felt warm inside. He looked around the busy basement. It was still full of sounds and movement, but it felt friendly, too. It felt accepting. He had used his words. His friends had listened. They all belonged here.

Pip fluffed his feathers. “We made a plan,” he said. “We took turns. We kept it kind.”

Hop added, “We noticed feelings. We made space. That’s caring.”

Squeak nodded. “When it’s too busy, I can say, ‘I need a turn. I need space.’ That is being assertive. Assertive means I speak up for myself with kind words.”

He looked at his friends. “And I listen when you need something, too.”

Tippy squeaked, “I need a snack break.”

“Snack break!” they all cheered. They shared crunchy seeds and sweet apple slices that smelled like sunshine. Crunch, munch, yum. The washing machine finished with a happy beep. The room felt calm and bright.

Squeak curled his tail. “Today I discovered new sounds. I used logical thinking. I was brave and asked for what I needed. We all accepted each other. That makes the basement a good place to play.”

The friends bumped paws, wing, and antenna. “Team!” they said.

Squeak pressed the button one last time. Ding! It sounded like a tiny bell of thanks.

The End.
