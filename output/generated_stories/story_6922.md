# Splash's Attic Adventure

High up in a dusty old attic, something magical was happening. A small, shimmery pool of water had appeared between the wooden boxes and forgotten treasures. In this special pool lived Splash, a playful young dolphin with bright blue skin and the biggest smile you ever did see.

"Click-click-whistle!" Splash sang happily as she swam in circles. The attic was quiet and cozy, with golden sunbeams dancing through the small window. Dust particles floated like tiny stars in the warm air.

But today, Splash noticed something that made her fins droop sadly. In the corner of the attic sat a family of toy mice - Mama Mouse, Papa Mouse, and three little mouse children. They looked very sad indeed.

"What's wrong, little friends?" Splash asked gently, poking her head above the water.

Mama Mouse wiped her tiny eyes. "Oh, Splash! The mean toy cat, Whiskers, took all our cheese crackers. Now our babies are hungry, and we don't know what to do."

Splash's heart felt heavy. "That's not fair at all! Everyone deserves to have enough food."

The little dolphin thought carefully. She could feel the soft attic floorboards creaking under the water, and she could smell the old wooden boxes filled with memories. An idea began to grow in her clever mind.

"I have a plan!" Splash announced, her voice echoing softly in the quiet attic. "But first, we need to work together. Can you help me?"

The mouse family nodded eagerly.

"Papa Mouse, can you show me where Whiskers hides the crackers?" Splash asked.

"Yes! Behind the big red box," Papa Mouse squeaked.

"Mama Mouse, can you gather all the other toy animals? We need everyone to hear this," Splash continued.

Soon, all the attic toys gathered around Splash's pool. There was Whiskers the cat, looking grumpy with cracker crumbs on his whiskers. There were teddy bears, toy soldiers, and even a wise old owl.

Splash took a deep breath. Speaking up felt scary, but she knew it was the right thing to do.

"Friends," Splash said in her clearest, strongest voice, "taking food from others isn't kind. When we share, everyone feels happy and safe."

Whiskers crossed his furry arms. "But I found those crackers first!"

"Finding something doesn't mean keeping it all," Splash explained patiently. "How would you feel if someone took your favorite toy?"

Whiskers thought for a moment. His whiskers twitched as he remembered feeling sad when his ball went missing last week.

"I... I guess I wouldn't like that," Whiskers admitted quietly.

Splash smiled warmly. "That's called empathy - understanding how others feel. Now, let's make a fair plan together!"

The wise old owl hooted approvingly as Splash suggested they create a sharing schedule. Every toy would take turns finding treats and sharing them equally.

"And if anyone feels something isn't fair," Splash added, "they should speak up! Using our voices to ask for what's right is called self-advocacy."

Whiskers slowly walked to his hiding spot and brought back the crackers. "I'm sorry, Mouse family. Here's your share."

The little mouse children squeaked with joy as they munched their crackers. The attic filled with happy sounds and warm feelings.

As the golden sunset painted the attic walls orange and pink, Splash felt proud. She had used her voice to help her friends, made a fair plan, and taught everyone about kindness.

"Thank you, Splash!" called all the toys together.

Splash dove underwater with a happy splash, knowing that speaking up for what's right always makes the world a better place.

The End.
