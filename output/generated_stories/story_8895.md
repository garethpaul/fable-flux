# Sunny Steps to Fairness

In a sunny backyard, soft grass tickled little hooves. A gentle deer fawn named Daya lifted her nose and sniffed the warm air. She heard birds sing, “Tweet, tweet!” and felt a friendly breeze brush her ears. Daya loved this backyard. It was bright, safe, and full of happy sounds.

Today, Daya had a plan. “I will make a stepping-stone path,” she said. “Then everyone can hop to the shade and share the cool water bowl.” She had smooth stones, a small shovel, and a tiny basket of berries for snacks. She felt ready. Preparation made Daya feel calm and brave.

Her friend Pip, a gray squirrel, skittered close. “Can I help?” Pip squeaked.

“Yes,” Daya said with a smile. “We can take turns. One stone at a time. That is fair.” Her heart felt warm and light.

They placed the first stone. Thump. The second stone. Thump. Daya hummed. Pip hummed too. The air smelled like fresh leaves. The sun touched their backs, warm as a hug.

Just then, Rumble the raccoon waddled in with quick feet and excited eyes. “I love stones! I will do it fast!” Rumble shouted. He grabbed three stones at once and dropped them in a hurry. Clack-clack-clack! One stone bumped Daya’s hoof. “Ouch,” Daya whispered.

Pip’s tail puffed up. “Hey! That’s not fair,” Pip said. “We were taking turns.”

Rumble blinked. “But I want to help. Fast is good!” He did not mean to be rough. He just didn’t stop to listen.

Daya took a deep breath. In. Out. She remembered peaceful communication. She softened her voice and let her feelings show. “Rumble,” she said kindly, “I feel sad and a little scared when the stones drop fast. It hurts my hoof. Justice means fair turns and safe bodies. We need fairness for everyone.”

Rumble looked down at his paws. “I didn’t know,” he murmured. His ears drooped. “I feel sorry.”

Daya nodded. “Thank you for telling me your feeling,” she said. “We can make a plan so it is fair for all of us.”

Pip wagged his tail. “A plan!”

Daya pointed to the shovel. “First, we line up. One, two, three. I go, then Pip, then Rumble. We carry one stone each. We place it down softly. Thump. We listen and we wait. That is justice: everyone gets the same chance, and we keep each other safe.”

Rumble took a slow breath, just like Daya. “In… out,” he said. “I can try.”

They tried the plan. Daya lifted a stone. Thump. Pip lifted a stone. Thump. Rumble lifted a stone. Thump. The sounds were soft now, like a drum played with gentle paws. The sun shone. A butterfly fluttered by, yellow as a lemon. Daya smiled. “This feels fair.”

Soon, a blue jay flew down. “Squawk! I want the shady spot first!” the jay cried. He flapped his wings and hopped ahead.

Daya stepped forward and spoke in a calm voice. “Hello, Blue Jay. We are making a path so everyone can reach the shade and water. We have turns. You can join the line. That is fair.”

The blue jay’s feathers ruffled. “But I am hot.”

“I hear you,” Daya said. “You feel hot. I feel hot too. Justice means we do not push or take more. We share so that everyone is okay. If we follow the plan, we all get shade.”

Pip nodded. “You can sip my berry water while you wait,” he offered. “Just a tiny sip.”

The blue jay blinked, then calmed. “Okay,” he said. “I will wait.” He sipped and cooled down. He waited in line. He felt proud.

They worked together. Daya checked the space for each stone. She gently pressed it with her hoof. “Safe and steady,” she said. Pip brushed away pebbles so no one would slip. Rumble counted, “One, two, three,” to keep the rhythm slow. The backyard smelled like sweet berries and green grass. The soft thump-thump-thump of stones made a happy beat.

At last, the path reached the shade. The water bowl sparkled. Everyone cheered, “Hooray!”

Blue Jay hopped onto the first step. “Thank you for being fair,” he said. “Justice feels kind.”

Rumble dipped his paws in the cool water. “I feel calm when we take turns,” he said. “I learned to slow down.”

Pip crunched a berry. “I learned to speak up, but in a gentle way,” he said. “That helps everyone listen.”

Daya took a sip of water and felt the coolness slide down. She looked at her friends. “Today we used preparation, feelings, and peaceful words,” she said. “We made a fair plan. We waited. We shared. Justice means everyone gets a safe chance. When we are fair, we all feel good.”

They rested together in the shade. The wind whispered through the leaves. The stones felt cool under their feet. Daya’s heart was happy. She had solved the problem with kindness and courage.

She smiled and said, “Let’s always use fair turns, kind voices, and ready plans. That is justice.”

Everyone nodded. “Yes,” they said. “Justice for all.”

The End.
