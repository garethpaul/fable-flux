# Polly's Colorful Solution

In a cozy study corner filled with soft cushions and warm sunlight, lived a beautiful parrot named Polly. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds and rubies, and she loved to create art with her bright crayons and paints.

Every morning, Polly would sit at her tiny easel, humming happy songs while she painted pictures of flowers, rainbows, and butterflies. The study corner smelled like fresh paper and sweet lavender from the nearby bookshelf.

One sunny Tuesday, Polly's friend Benny the bunny hopped into the study corner. His ears drooped down, and his whiskers twitched sadly.

"Hello, Benny!" chirped Polly cheerfully. "Would you like to paint with me today?"

Benny shook his head. "I can't paint, Polly. Every time I try, my pictures look all messy and wrong. It makes me feel so frustrated!" His voice cracked a little, and his paws trembled.

Polly tilted her colorful head thoughtfully. She could see that Benny's eyes were watery, and his fluffy tail wasn't bouncing like usual.

"Oh, Benny," said Polly gently. "I understand feeling frustrated. Sometimes my paintings don't look the way I want them to either."

"Really?" sniffled Benny, wiping his nose with his soft paw.

"Really!" Polly nodded. "But you know what helps me when I feel frustrated? Taking deep breaths that smell like the lavender books, and remembering that making art is about having fun, not being perfect."

Polly hopped down from her perch and waddled over to Benny. "Would you like me to show you a special way to paint? We can do it together!"

Benny's ears perked up just a tiny bit. "Together?"

"Yes!" Polly's eyes sparkled with kindness. "We can make handprint flowers! You press your paw in the paint, and I'll use my wing. Then we smoosh them on the paper to make pretty petals."

Polly dipped her bright wing in yellow paint that felt cool and smooth. "See? Like this!" She gently pressed her wing onto the white paper, leaving a beautiful yellow mark.

Benny watched with wonder. Slowly, he dipped his paw in pink paint. It felt squishy and fun between his toes. "Like this?" he asked, pressing his paw next to Polly's wing print.

"Perfect!" cheered Polly. "Look how beautiful our flower is already!"

Together, they made paw-print and wing-print petals in purple, blue, and orange. The colors swirled together like a magical garden. Benny giggled as the paint tickled his paws, and his tail began to bounce again.

"This is so much fun!" laughed Benny. "I'm not frustrated anymore!"

"That's because we're being kind to ourselves," explained Polly wisely. "When we feel frustrated, we can take deep breaths, ask friends for help, and remember that trying our best is what matters most."

They painted three more handprint flowers together, each one different and special. The study corner filled with their happy laughter and the sweet smell of colorful paints.

"Thank you for being so kind to me, Polly," said Benny, hugging his friend with his paint-covered paws. "You helped me feel better and taught me that art is about joy, not perfection."

"That's what friends do," smiled Polly, nuzzling Benny's soft fur. "We help each other when things feel hard, and we share kindness like we share our favorite colors."

From that day on, Benny and Polly painted together every Tuesday, creating beautiful art and even more beautiful memories in their cozy study corner.

The End.
