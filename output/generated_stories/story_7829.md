# Milo Monkey’s Big Guest Room Goal

Milo the monkey loved to visit Grandma’s house. Today, Grandma had a surprise. She opened the door to the guest room. It smelled like clean sheets and lemon polish. The bed looked big and fluffy, like a white cloud. Soft slippers sat by the rug. The window let warm sunlight spill in, making golden squares on the floor.

“Milo,” said Grandma with a gentle smile, “this is the guest room. I need your brave help today.”

Milo’s ears perked up. “My brave help?” he asked, his tail swishing.

Grandma nodded. “I hung a paper rainbow banner. But one star fell behind the big chair. I can’t reach it. Do you think you can?”

Milo looked at the chair. It was tall and wooden, with a quilt draped over the back. He felt a tiny flutter in his tummy. It was a little scary. The space behind the chair was a narrow shadowy gap. He could smell dust and hear a tiny tick-tick from the wall clock.

“I feel wiggly in my belly,” Milo said softly.

“That’s okay,” Grandma said. “Brave doesn’t mean not scared. Brave means you try, even when it’s a little hard. Let’s make a plan.”

Milo liked plans. He rubbed his hands together. “A plan! Yes! My goal is to find the star and hang it up again.”

Grandma clapped quietly. “Good goal. Let’s break it into steps.”

Milo held up one finger. “Step one: Look at the space.”

He crouched low and peeked behind the chair. He squinted. He could see the shiny gold star, halfway under a folded blanket. Dust tickled his nose. “Ah-CHOO!”

Grandma giggled. “Bless you!”

Milo held up a second finger. “Step two: Move carefully.”

He took a deep breath. “In through my nose… out through my mouth.” The breath felt warm in his chest. It made him feel steady. “I can do this,” he whispered.

He set his goal in his mind: Find the star. One small move at a time.

Milo placed his left foot on the rug. It was soft, like warm grass. He placed his right foot on the wooden floor. It was smooth and cool. He pressed his hands on the chair and felt the bumps of the wood. “I’m moving slow and strong,” he said.

He slid sideways into the gap. His tail curled for balance. “Step three: Reach and grab,” he said.

His fingers stretched toward the shiny star. He could almost touch it. His heart thumped, but his breath stayed slow. “I am brave. I am careful,” he told himself.

The star was just a tiny bit too far. Milo paused. “New idea,” he said. “Step three-a: Use a helper.”

He looked around and saw a soft, long scarf on the bed. “Grandma, may I use the scarf to pull it closer?” he asked.

“Smart thinking,” Grandma said. She handed him the scarf.

Milo slid the scarf under the star and tugged. Scratch, scratch, went the paper. The star moved closer. He reached again. His fingers closed around the star. It felt smooth and a little crinkly.

“I got it!” Milo cheered.

He backed out slowly. “Step four: Put the star back up.”

Grandma held the small step stool steady. “You can do it,” she said.

Milo climbed up. One foot. Then the other. He felt tall and brave. The guest room smelled like fresh soap up high. The banner rustled softly. Milo pressed the star back on the string. Stick, went the tape.

He climbed down carefully. His feet touched the floor with a quiet thump. The banner waved in the sunlight. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue. The gold star sparkled happily.

Grandma hugged him. “You were so brave, Milo. You made a plan. You moved your body carefully. You reached your goal.”

Milo grinned. “I felt scared at first. But I took deep breaths. I made steps. I tried. That is brave.”

Grandma nodded. “That is brave.”

Milo looked around the guest room. He noticed the slippers again. “New goal,” he said. “Put the slippers by the bed for guests.”

He picked them up and marched with funny, high steps, making Grandma laugh. Thump-thump, went his feet. He set the slippers neatly side by side. Then he patted the fluffy pillows, smoothing them with slow strokes. Whoosh, went the pillow air.

“All ready,” he said proudly. “The guest room feels cozy.”

Grandma’s eyes sparkled. “You turned a little fear into a big win.”

Milo touched the gold star. “When I feel wiggly in my belly, I can breathe, make a plan, and move step by step.”

Grandma kissed his forehead. “That’s a brave monkey.”

Milo’s tail curled in a happy hug. He felt warm and light, like sunshine on the rug. He whispered to the room, “Hello, guests. Welcome to our brave and cozy place.”

The guest room seemed to glow a little brighter.

The End.
