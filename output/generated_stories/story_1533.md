# Tilly Turtle Listens

Tilly was a shy turtle with a soft green shell and tiny, careful steps. One sunny morning, Tilly’s mom said, “It’s bath time!” Tilly nodded. She liked the warm water and the gentle bubble sounds.

They went to the bathroom. The bathroom smelled like sweet soap. The light shone on shiny tiles. The mirror looked like a quiet lake. Tilly wiggled her toes on the cool floor.

“Let’s make this peaceful,” Mom said softly. “We will listen with our ears and our hearts.”

Tilly took a deep breath. She heard a small drip-drip from the faucet. She heard a soft whoosh from the fan. She heard her own tiny feet tap-tap-tap. Listening made her feel calm inside.

Mom turned on the water. “Tell me when it sounds right for you,” she said.

Tilly tilted her head. The water went shhhh like a gentle rain. “A little softer, please,” Tilly whispered.

Mom smiled and turned the knob. The shhhh became shh. “How is this?”

Tilly listened again. “Yes, that’s gentle,” she said. She felt proud for speaking up with kind words.

Mom sprinkled bubble soap. Plop, plop! Bubbles popped and giggled. Tilly sniffed. The bubbles smelled like lemons and sunshine. She reached out one shy finger and touched the foam. It was fluffy, like clouds.

Tilly climbed into the tub with help. Warm water hugged her legs. “Mmm,” she said. “My shell feels cozy.”

Mom handed Tilly a tiny cup. “We can rinse with this. Would you like to try?”

Tilly looked at the cup. She had two choices. She could splash fast, or she could go slow and listen. She took a calm breath. “I will go slow,” Tilly decided.

She dipped the cup and poured a little. Plip-plip-plip. She paused to listen. The water made a happy sound. “I like that sound,” she said, smiling.

Knock-knock! Someone tapped on the door. It was Tilly’s little brother, Toby. “Can I come say hello?” he asked in a small voice.

Tilly felt shy. She hid her chin under a bubble. Mom whispered, “Let’s listen to our feelings, and then use peaceful words.”

Tilly put her hand on her heart. She listened. Her heart said, I need quiet. She took a breath and spoke kindly, “Toby, I want quiet bath time. You can talk through the door.”

“Okay!” Toby said. “I will talk from here.” His voice sounded happy to help.

Tilly smiled. She felt brave. She had listened to her feelings and used calm words. Toby hummed a little song on the other side. It sounded like a bumblebee. Bzz-bzz, soft and sweet.

Mom brushed Tilly’s shell with a soft sponge. Swish-swish. “How is the sponge?” Mom asked.

Tilly listened to her skin. “It tickles a little,” she giggled. “Can we do gentle circles?”

“Gentle circles it is,” Mom agreed. Swish, circle, swish. The sponge smelled like clean rain. The bubbles clung like tiny stars.

“Now hair,” Mom said. “Should I count so you know what comes next?”

“Yes, please,” Tilly said. Listening to counting helped her feel calm.

“One,” Mom said, wetting Tilly’s head. The water was warm and friendly.
“Two,” Mom added the shampoo. It smelled like apples.
“Three,” Mom paused. “Ready to rinse?”

Tilly listened to the quiet. She noticed her shoulders were tight. She breathed in and out, slow like a turtle. “I’m ready,” she said.

Mom poured water with the little cup. Tilly closed her eyes and held the edge of the tub. She listened to the water fall. Plip-plip. It was okay. She felt safe.

“There,” Mom said. “You did it!”

Tilly’s cheeks felt sunny. “I listened to the sounds. I listened to my feelings. I listened to you,” she said.

“And I listened to you,” Mom replied. “Listening helps us be calm and kind.”

Toby knocked again. “Is it time for a towel hug?” he asked.

Tilly looked at Mom. Mom raised her eyebrows as if to ask, What do you think? Tilly smiled. “Yes. A towel hug sounds nice.”

Mom opened the door. Toby stayed at the doorway and waved. “Hi, Tilly!”

“Hi, Toby,” Tilly said. “Thank you for waiting.”

“Thank you for telling me what you needed,” Toby said.

Mom wrapped Tilly in a big, soft towel. It was warm like sunshine on a stone. Tilly sighed, “Ahh.” The bathroom was quiet. The fan hummed. The bubbles rested.

Tilly stood by the mirror and saw a small, brave turtle looking back. “I am shy,” she said softly, “and I am strong. When I listen, I know what to do.”

Mom nodded. “Listening helps us make good choices. It helps us talk peacefully. It helps us feel calm.”

Tilly took her tiny cup and set it on the edge of the tub. “Today I chose slow and gentle,” she said. “That felt right.”

Toby clapped. “Slow and gentle is my favorite!”

Tilly giggled. “Mine too.”

They turned off the light. The bathroom grew quiet again. Tilly felt cozy, clean, and proud. She had listened to sounds, to Mom, to Toby, and to her own heart. She made calm, kind choices.

That night, Tilly whispered to herself, “Listening is my superpower.”

And it was.

The End.
