# Sunny Squirrel and the Cozy Closet

Sunny the squirrel had soft, gray-brown fur and a fluffy tail like a feather duster. She lived with a kind family and liked to explore the house. One rainy afternoon, pitter-patter sounds tapped the windows. Sunny twitched her nose. “I hear the rain,” she said. “It smells like wet leaves!”

Sunny scampered down the hallway and nudged open a door. It was the hallway closet, a little hideaway full of coats, soft scarves, a basket of buttons, and a shoebox of shiny things. It was dark and cozy. Sunny wiggled inside.

“Oh!” Sunny squeaked. “This is a secret nest.” She made a tiny bed from a fuzzy scarf. The scarf was purple and smelled like lavender. The closet felt like a hug.

Then Sunny heard quiet sniffles. “Sniff, sniff.”

“Hello?” Sunny peeked around a tall boot. There sat Mia, the little girl of the house. She was curled up on a folded quilt. Her cheeks were shiny with little tears.

Sunny tiptoed closer. “Hi, Mia,” she said softly. “I am Sunny. I like this cozy closet.”

Mia wiped her eyes. “I’m sad,” she whispered. “My block tower fell. It went crash. I felt small. So I came here.”

Sunny put a tiny paw on Mia’s hand. “Thank you for telling me. Your feelings are important,” Sunny said. “When I feel sad, I take slow breaths.”

Sunny breathed in. “Sloooow.” She breathed out. “Softer.” Mia copied her. In, out. In, out. They listened to the rain. Pitter-patter. Drip. Drop.

Mia’s shoulders relaxed. “That helps,” she said. “But I still have a problem. I wanted to build a tall tower. I couldn’t do it alone.”

Sunny’s ears perked up. She was a resourceful squirrel. She liked to help. She looked around the closet hideaway. Buttons. Scarves. A shoebox. Coats. Hangers.

“I have an idea,” Sunny said. “We can solve the problem together. We can make a new tower. And we can make it strong.”

Mia looked hopeful. “Together?”

“Together,” Sunny said with a smile.

They began to plan. Sunny cheeped, “First, let’s make a calm space.” She tugged the purple scarf and spread it like a soft rug. “This will be our building spot.”

Mia giggled. “It’s so soft,” she said, rubbing the scarf. “It smells nice.”

“Next,” Sunny said, “we gather helpful things.” She pointed to the shoebox. Mia opened it. Inside were smooth stones, clothespins, and a tiny wooden spool. Sunny found cardboard from an old tissue box. She also found a ribbon.

“This is a discovery,” Mia said. “We can use these!”

Sunny nodded. “We will include blocks, too. Let’s invite Max,” she added. Max was Mia’s little brother. Sunny knew everyone feels good when they are included.

Mia called, “Max! Do you want to build with us?”

Max peeked in. His eyes were wide. “In the closet?” he asked.

“Yes,” Mia said. “It is a cozy hideaway. We are building a strong tower together.”

Max grinned and brought a basket of blocks. Clunk, clunk went the blocks as he set them down. The closet smelled like pine wood and lavender. The children and Sunny sat close.

Sunny winked. “We can be builders. I can be the helper squirrel.”

They began. Sunny showed them how to make a wide base. “Wide at the bottom,” she said, patting the cardboard base, “means strong at the top.”

Mia placed big blocks in a square. Max stacked smaller blocks. Sunny used the ribbon to gently tie a wobbly part. “A little support,” she chirped.

The tower grew. “Tap, tap,” went Sunny’s tiny paws as she checked the corners. “I am checking for wobbles,” she said.

Max’s hands shook. “I’m nervous,” he said. “What if it falls again?”

Sunny looked kindly at Max. “It is okay to feel nervous,” she said. “Feelings are like clouds. They come and go. We can breathe. We can ask for help.”

Max nodded and took a slow breath. Mia squeezed his hand. “We are a team,” she said.

Sunny added the smooth stones on the base to make it steady. Mia placed the wooden spool on top like a crown. Max put a shiny button in the center. The tower stood tall and still. It was beautiful.

“We did it!” Mia cheered. Max clapped. Sunny’s tail swished with joy. The rain outside sounded like soft applause.

Suddenly, a tiny wobble. “Uh-oh,” said Max.

Sunny smiled. “We can fix it. Problems are chances to be helpful.” She slid a coat hanger under the cardboard to make a flat floor. The wobble stopped. The tower stood strong again.

Mia hugged Sunny. “You helped us. You listened. You had ideas. You included Max. Thank you, Sunny.”

Sunny hugged back with gentle paws. “You asked for help. You breathed. You shared. You were kind. Thank you.”

They all sat in the cozy closet, looking at their tower. It was not just tall. It was a Helpful Tower.

Mia said, “When I feel sad again, I can breathe. I can ask for help. I can help others, too.”

Sunny nodded. “Helping makes hearts feel warm,” she said. “And when we include others, the tower—and our team—gets stronger.”

They listened to the last raindrops. Drip. Drop. The house smelled like warm cookies now. The closet hideaway felt safe and bright.

The End.
