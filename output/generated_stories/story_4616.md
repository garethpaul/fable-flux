# Milo the Monkey Finishes the Morning

Milo was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and soft, fuzzy fur. He lived in a comfortable nursery filled with warm sunshine, cozy rugs, and shelves of colorful books. The room smelled like clean cotton and sweet bananas. A round clock tick-tocked softly on the wall.

Tick. Tock.

“Good morning, Milo,” said Miss Lila, the gentle teacher with a kind smile. “Today we have our Morning List.”

Milo looked at the big chart with pictures. It showed five tasks with little stars next to them: brush fur, make bed, snack time, puzzle time, and tidy toys.

Milo’s tail swished. “Five things?” he asked, his voice small.

Miss Lila nodded. “Yes. One by one, we can do it. We will talk about each step. You are brave, and you can finish.”

Milo put his hand on his chest. He took a deep breath. In… and out. The air smelled like warm toast. “I will try,” he said.

First was brush fur. The soft brush felt tickly on his cheeks. Swish, swish. “This is nice,” Milo giggled, brushing his arms and tail. His fur became smooth and shiny.

Miss Lila clapped quietly. “Well done, Milo. One task done.”

Milo smiled. His belly felt warm, like sunshine. A little star sticker went next to the picture. “I did it!” he said. “What’s next?”

“Make bed,” Miss Lila said, pointing to the picture of a blanket.

Milo looked at his little bed by the window. The blanket was a tangle of blue and yellow stripes. He tugged, but the corner flopped down. His ears drooped. “It’s too hard,” he whispered.

Miss Lila came closer and knelt. “Let’s use our words. Tell me what you need.”

Milo lifted his chin. “I need help,” he said. “Can you show me how?”

“Of course,” Miss Lila said warmly. She showed him how to pull the blanket tight and smooth it down. “Hands wide, pull, and pat. We do it together.”

“Hands wide, pull, and pat,” Milo repeated. Pat, pat. The blanket lay flat and neat.

“Two tasks done,” Miss Lila said, adding another star. “How do you feel?”

Milo’s tail curled in a happy loop. “I feel proud,” he said. The word felt big and bright in his mouth.

His tummy gave a little rumble. Snack time! The nursery smelled like banana slices and cinnamon toast. “Yum,” Milo said. He washed his hands. Splash, splash. The water felt cool and bubbly. He peeled a banana. Peel, peel. “Thank you, Miss Lila,” he said.

“You remembered your manners,” Miss Lila replied. “That’s good communication.”

Crunch, munch, gulp. Milo ate slowly and neatly. When he finished, he put the peel in the bin. Another star!

Next was puzzle time. Milo loved puzzles, but this one had tiny animal pieces. Click, clack, clack. He tried to fit the tiger piece, but it didn’t fit. His eyebrows squished together.

“I feel frustrated,” he said, noticing his tight shoulders. He took another deep breath. In… and out. “Can I ask for a hint?”

“You can,” Miss Lila said. “Look at the colors and the shapes.”

Milo turned the tiger piece. He saw a little orange corner. “Ah!” Click! It fit. Piece by piece, the puzzle grew. Zebras, birds, a big green tree. “I’m doing it,” Milo whispered. His heart thumped in a happy beat.

When the last piece clicked, Milo clapped for himself. Clap, clap! Miss Lila clapped too. “You kept trying. That’s courage.”

The star chart had four bright stars now. One more task: tidy toys.

Milo looked around. There were blocks like tiny houses, crayons like colorful sticks, and soft animals snoozing on the floor. It looked like a lot.

Milo said, “It feels big. Can we make a plan?”

“Yes,” said Miss Lila. “We can. Let’s break it into small jobs. Blocks first, then crayons, then animals.” She held up three fingers.

Milo nodded. “Small jobs.” He sang a little tidy song, soft and bouncy. “Blocks in the box, tap tap tap.” The blocks made a happy clink. “Crayons in the cup, plink plink plink.” The crayons smelled waxy and sweet. “Animals on the shelf, cuddle and rest.” The soft animals felt velvety in his hands.

Soon the room looked neat and calm. The rug felt fluffy under Milo’s feet. The air felt light, like a gentle hug.

Miss Lila placed the last star on the chart. “Milo,” she said, “you finished your Morning List.”

Milo stood tall. His fur shone. His smile spread wide. “I did each step,” he said proudly. “I asked for help. I used my words. I kept trying. I finished my tasks!”

Miss Lila gave him a high-five. Slap! “That is how we grow. When we finish tasks, we feel proud. When we talk about our feelings and ask for help, we work better together.”

Milo nodded. “I am brave,” he said. “And I can finish things, one by one.”

The clock went tick-tock, but it sounded like music now. Milo looked at the tidy room, the shiny stars, and Miss Lila’s warm eyes. His heart felt full, like a big, bright balloon.

He whispered to himself, “Small steps, brave heart, and kind words.”

Then, with a happy wiggle of his tail, Milo picked up a book from the shelf. “Can we read?” he asked.

“Yes,” Miss Lila smiled. “You earned a cozy story.”

Milo curled up on the soft rug, listening to Miss Lila’s gentle voice, feeling safe, proud, and ready for new tasks tomorrow.

The lesson was clear in Milo’s mind: finish one thing at a time, talk about feelings, ask for help, and feel proud when you’re done.

The End.
