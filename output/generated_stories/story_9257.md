# Mango's Musical Mission

Mango the monkey swung through the open window of the music room. The morning sun made the instruments sparkle like jewels. Today was special - the children's concert was tomorrow!

"Oh no!" gasped Mrs. Chen, the music teacher. She was from China and taught the children beautiful songs. "The drum set is all mixed up! How will the children play together?"

Mango saw the problem right away. The big bass drum was where the small snare should be. The cymbals were upside down. The drumsticks were scattered everywhere like pickup sticks.

"Don't worry," said Mango, puffing out his furry chest. "I can help!"

But when Mango tried to move the heavy bass drum, his hands and feet got all tangled up. CRASH! He tumbled backward.

"Coordination is tricky," said Mrs. Chen kindly. "Let's try together, step by step."

Mrs. Chen showed Mango how to use his left hand and right hand at the same time. "Left hand holds, right hand pushes," she said slowly.

Mango tried again. This time, he moved his hands together like a team. The drum rolled smoothly to its right spot!

"I did it!" Mango cheered, doing a little dance.

Next came the cymbals. They were shiny and made beautiful ringing sounds. But they were heavy! Mango had to use both hands and both feet.

"Bend your knees," coached Mrs. Chen. "Use your whole body together."

Mango felt his muscles working as one big team. His arms lifted while his legs stayed strong. The cymbals found their perfect place on top.

The hardest part was the drumsticks. There were so many! Mango had to pick them up two at a time, walking carefully so he wouldn't drop them.

"One foot, then the other," Mango said to himself. "Eyes watching, hands holding tight."

Step by step, Mango carried all the drumsticks to their special box. His coordination was getting better with each trip!

Just then, children from many countries came into the room. There was Aisha from Kenya, Carlos from Mexico, and Yuki from Japan. They were going to play music from their homelands.

"Thank you, Mango!" said Aisha. "Now we can practice our African drums!"

"¡Gracias!" called Carlos. "Our mariachi music will sound perfect!"

"Arigato!" said Yuki. "The taiko drums are ready!"

Mrs. Chen smiled big. "Mango, your coordination helped our whole music community. When we work together with our hands, feet, eyes, and hearts, we can do amazing things!"

The children began to play. The African drums went boom-boom-boom. The mariachi music went strum-strum-strum. The taiko drums went thump-thump-thump. All the sounds mixed together like a beautiful rainbow of music.

Mango clapped his hands and tapped his feet to the beat. His whole body moved together in perfect coordination. He felt proud and happy.

"I learned something important today," Mango told Mrs. Chen. "When all parts of my body work together, I can help my friends and my community!"

Mrs. Chen nodded. "And when we all work together, we make beautiful music for everyone to enjoy."

As the sun set, Mango swung home through the trees. His arms and legs moved together like a perfect dance. Tomorrow's concert would be wonderful, and he had helped make it happen.

The End.
