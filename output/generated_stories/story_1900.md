# Giggles the Giraffe and the Tumble of Feelings

In a sunny house, near the back door, there was a busy laundry room. It smelled like soap and lemon. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer sang, “Thump, thump, thump.” Warm steam puffed like tiny clouds.

A thoughtful giraffe named Giggles peeked inside. Giggles had soft spots, bright eyes, and a gentle heart. She liked to think before she did things. Today, she wanted to help wash her favorite red scarf.

“I can do it,” Giggles whispered. “I can take care of me and my scarf.” She smiled and stepped carefully on her long legs.

Giggles opened the washer door. “Hello, washer,” she said. “I am Giggles. I want my scarf to be clean and cozy.”

The washer just hummed.

Giggles put the scarf inside. She grabbed the soap. The soap smelled like fresh rain and flowers. But the bottle felt heavy.

“Oh no,” she grunted. “It’s stuck!” Giggles tugged. The soap bottle slipped. A little splat of soap landed on her hoof.

“Oops!” said Giggles. “That’s slippery.” Her heart thumped fast. Her cheeks felt warm. “I am feeling… frustrated.”

Giggles took a slow breath. She remembered something she liked to do when feelings tumbled. She told herself a tiny story.

“Once upon a time,” she whispered, “a brave giraffe took a breath as big as a balloon. In… and out…” She breathed in slowly. She breathed out slowly. She felt a little lighter.

She tried the soap again. “I will be gentle,” she said. She squeezed a small dot into the cup. Plip! “That’s enough,” she said kindly to herself. “I don’t need too much.”

She reached to start the washer but bumped the basket. Socks toppled out—striped socks, tiny socks, a sock with a star. They flopped on the floor like sleepy fish.

“Oh dear,” said Giggles. Her tail swished. “More frustration.”

She put her hoof on her chest. “Hello, feelings,” she said softly. “I see you.” She looked around the laundry room. The dryer was warm like a hug. The towels smelled sweet. She felt the soft towel with her hoof. “Mmm,” she said. “Soft helps.”

Giggles made another tiny story. “Once upon a time, a giraffe made a plan.” She smiled. “Step one: pick up socks. Step two: press the button. Step three: take a sip of water.”

She bent her long neck and picked up one sock at a time. Plop in the basket. Plop, plop. “I can do small steps,” she said. “Small steps help me care for me.”

“Ow!” She bumped her head on the shelf. “Bonk.” It didn’t hurt much, but it surprised her. Her eyes felt watery.

“Pause,” she told herself. She put her hooves flat and closed her eyes. “In… and out…” She counted softly, “One… two… three.” She wiggled her ears. She smiled a tiny smile. “I am safe. I am learning.”

When the socks were back, Giggles pressed the button. Beep! The washer spun. Water swished. Bubble smells floated up.

“I did it,” she said, her voice like a happy hum.

She took a sip from her water cup. The water felt cool and kind. “Thank you, water,” she said.

While the washer worked, Giggles sat on a folded blanket. She made up a new story, because stories helped her heart.

“Once upon a time,” she began, “a giraffe named Giggles met a mountain of socks. The mountain looked tall. But Giggles was taller in her mind. She used slow breaths. She used gentle words. She used small steps. And the mountain turned into a molehill.”

The dryer thumped a calm rhythm: “Thump, thump, thump.” Giggles tapped her hoof to the beat. She felt peaceful.

The washer stopped with a cheerful click. Giggles moved the scarf to the dryer. The scarf was heavy with water. “Squish,” it said.

“This scarf will be warm and ready,” Giggles told it. “And so will I.”

While the scarf tumbled, Giggles stretched her long neck. She rolled her shoulders. She wiggled her toes. “Stretching feels good,” she said. “Self-care is listening to my body.”

Soon, the dryer sang, “Ding!” The room smelled like sunshine. Giggles opened the door. Warm air hugged her. She lifted the red scarf and rubbed it on her cheek. “Mmm, cozy.”

She wrapped the scarf around her neck. “Thank you, me,” she said. “I was patient. I was kind. I took care of my feelings and my scarf.”

She turned to the laundry room and spoke softly. “Thank you, washer. Thank you, dryer. Thank you, towels and socks. Today, we learned together.”

Giggles looked at the basket. No more mess. No more slippery spots. Just neat, clean clothes and a happy heart.

She smiled and told one last story. “Once upon a time, a thoughtful giraffe felt frustrated. She paused. She breathed. She made a plan. She asked her body what it needed—water, a stretch, a gentle word. She finished her job and felt proud. The end.”

Giggles nodded. “Yes,” she said. “That is my story.”

And from that day, whenever feelings felt big, Giggles remembered her laundry room lesson: take a breath, make a plan, and be kind to yourself.

The End.
