# Koala Kiki’s Porch Plan

The morning sun yawned over the gum trees. Tiny birds peeped, “Peep-peep!” and a soft breeze smelled like sweet eucalyptus. On a peaceful front porch, a calm koala named Kiki sipped warm leaf tea from a small cup.

Kiki loved her porch. The wooden floor felt smooth under her paws. Her rocking chair creaked gently, “cree-eak, cree-eak.” A blue bowl of crunchy leaves sat by her side. She listened to the wind chimes sing, “ting-ting-ting.”

Every morning, Kiki had a happy routine. She stretched. She rocked. She watered her porch plants. She said, “Good morning, porch. Thank you for the shade. Thank you for the breeze. Thank you for the birds.” Kiki liked to feel grateful, right from the start.

But today, Kiki noticed a problem.

A crinkly plastic bottle rolled under the chair. A shiny can sat by the steps. A squished paper cup hid behind her flower pot.

“Oh dear,” said Kiki in a calm, small voice. “This porch is messy. My routine feels wobbly. Where did all these things come from?”

A gust of wind rustled the trees. The loose bottle made a “tup-tup” sound as it bumped the floor.

Kiki blinked slowly. “I will stay calm. I will fix this. I can make a plan.”

Just then, her neighbor, Wally the wombat, waddled by on the path. “Morning, Kiki!” he called. “Your porch smells nice. Is that leaf tea?”

“Yes,” said Kiki, smiling. “And thank you for walking by. I found a problem. My porch has bottles and cans and cups. I want my tidy, grateful morning back. I want to help the Earth too. What should I do?”

Wally scratched his head. “Hmm. Maybe put them in a bag?”

Kiki patted her heart. “I think we can do better. We can recycle.”

“Re-cycle?” Wally rolled the word around like a pebble. “What is that?”

Kiki’s eyes shone softly. “Recycling means we give things a new life. We sort paper, cans, and plastic into special bins. Then they can be used again. That helps the Earth. That helps our porch!”

Wally nodded. “That sounds kind. Can I help?”

Kiki’s ears perked. “Yes, please. First, let’s make a porch plan.”

They looked around. Kiki took a deep breath. She could smell fresh water from her watering can. She could hear bees humming by the flowers. She felt steady and brave.

“Step one,” said Kiki. “Find three bins.” She pointed to three baskets by the door. “This basket is for paper. This bucket is for cans. This tub is for plastic.” She tied colored ribbons on each one—green, silver, and blue.

“Step two,” she said. “We sort.”

Kiki picked up the paper cup. “Paper,” she said, dropping it into the green basket. “Shhff!”

Wally picked up the shiny can. “Can,” he said, dropping it into the silver bucket. “Clink!”

Together they reached for the crinkly bottle. “Plastic,” they said, popping it into the blue tub. “Plop!”

Kiki smiled. “Step three: We rinse if sticky. Step four: We put the bins by the gate. The recycling truck will come. It will roar ‘vrrr-rr-rr’ and take the bins to become new things.”

Wally’s eyes grew wide. “New things? Like what?”

“Like new cans, new paper, new bottles,” said Kiki. “Maybe even a shiny new watering can one day!”

They kept sorting. “Shhff! Clink! Plop!” The porch began to look neat and bright. The wind chimes sang again, “ting-ting-ting,” like a thank-you song.

Kiki wiped her paws on a soft cloth. “Now my routine can feel happy again.”

She rocked in her chair. “cree-eak, cree-eak.” She took a warm sip of tea. “Ahh.” She watered the plants. “Sip-sip,” went the soil.

“Thank you, porch,” she said. “Thank you, Wally. Thank you, bins. I am grateful for helpers.”

Wally’s cheeks puffed with pride. “I like this plan,” he said. “Can I make my own bins at home?”

“Yes,” said Kiki. “I can share ribbons with you. Green for paper. Silver for cans. Blue for plastic. We can make recycling part of our daily routine.”

Just then, Lila the little lizard scampered up the step. “What are you doing?” she squeaked.

Kiki waved. “We are caring for our porch and our planet. We sort. We rinse. We recycle. Would you like to help with a tiny task?”

Lila nodded. “Tiny task for a tiny lizard!”

Kiki handed her a small paper tag. “This tag says ‘paper.’ Can you tie it to the green basket?”

Lila tied it carefully. “There!” she chirped.

Kiki clapped softly. “Thank you, Lila. I am grateful for your careful hands.”

They all sat for a moment. The porch smelled clean and leafy. The breeze felt cool and kind. The birds sang, “Peep-peep!” The three friends smiled.

Kiki said, “When we have a problem, we can stay calm, make a plan, and ask for help. When we recycle, we give things a new life. When we keep a routine, our day feels safe and sweet. And when we say thank you, our hearts feel warm.”

The recycling truck rumbled in the distance. “Vrrr-rr-rr!” Wally waved. Lila giggled. Kiki rocked and whispered, “Good job, team.”

The End.
