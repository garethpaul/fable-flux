# Panda Pao’s Rooftop Wiggle

Panda Pao was a patient panda. He lived with Granny Lin in a cozy blue house. On top of the house was a sunny rooftop. The rooftop was flat and warm, like a big blanket. The wind made little whooshing sounds. Pao loved the rooftop.

One morning, Granny Lin called, “Pao, sweet pea, it’s chore time. Let’s wash the rooftop mats.”

“Yes, Granny!” said Pao, clapping his paws. He liked helping. Helping made his heart feel big and warm.

Granny Lin carried a bucket. Splash, splash! Soapy water sloshed with a sweet lemon smell. Pao wrung a cloth. Squish, squish! The foam was tickly on his paws.

Pao looked around. The rooftop was messy. Leaves danced in a circle. A tiny hat had blown up from somewhere. A red paper lantern rolled, bonk, bonk, bonk.

“Oh dear,” said Granny Lin. “The wind made a busy mess.”

“We can do it,” said Pao. “Slow and steady.”

Granny smiled. “You are patient and strong. Let’s move our bodies and our hearts.”

They started. Pao bent his knees. He took a deep breath. In, out. He felt calm.

First, he needed to pick up the leaves. But the leaves kept skittering away. Whoosh! The wind laughed. The leaves flew to the edge of the rooftop.

“I can’t catch them,” Pao whispered. His ears drooped.

Granny Lin put a soft paw on his shoulder. “Try a leaf dance,” she said. “Use your body. Big steps. Gentle hands.”

Pao nodded. He stood tall. He spread his arms wide like wings. “Leaf dance!” he sang. He took big side steps. Step, step. He scooped with careful paws. Scoop, scoop. He turned in a slow circle. Spin! The leaves were caught in his bucket.

“I did it!” Pao smiled. His chest felt proud and light.

Next was the tiny hat. A big gust sent it scooting under a bench. Pao got down on his tummy. The rooftop felt warm on his fur. He stretched his arm. Stretch, stretch. He could almost touch it.

“My arm is not long enough,” he said.

“Try a reach and wiggle,” said Granny Lin. “Use your body in a new way. You are clever.”

Pao bent one knee. He wiggled his hips. He scooted forward like a little inchworm. Wiggle, wiggle. His paw touched the hat. “Got you!” he cheered.

Then the red lantern rolled toward a puddle. “Oh no!” said Pao. He hopped to the left. Hop! He hopped to the right. Hop! He made a little hopping path. His paws went tap, tap, tap. He gently trapped the lantern between his feet.

“Safe,” he whispered, hugging it. The lantern smelled like paper and a tiny bit of spice from last night’s soup.

They washed the mats next. Pao dipped the cloth. Swish, swish. The water was cool on his paws. He scrubbed in circles. Round and round. Bubbles popped. Pop, pop, pop. He pushed the heavy mats to the railing so they could dry. Push, push. His legs felt strong.

The sun moved higher. The wind sang a softer song. The rooftop began to shine.

Then Pao saw a small bird on the railing. It chirped, “Cheep, cheep,” and fluffed its wet feathers. It looked worried.

“Oh, little bird,” said Pao. “Are you okay?”

The bird’s wing was tucked funny. It could not fly.

Granny Lin said, “We can help. Move with care.”

Pao crouched low. He held out his paws. “It’s okay,” he said softly. “I will be gentle.” He took slow, quiet steps. Step… step… step. He could feel his breath going in and out. He could hear the bird’s tiny peeps. He scooped the bird into a dry towel. The towel was soft and warm.

“There,” Pao said. “You are safe.” He patted the towel. Pat, pat.

Granny Lin smiled. “You moved with kindness. That is strong.”

They made a cozy corner. Pao set the towel near the sunny wall. He brought a shallow cup of water. He moved carefully so it did not spill. Walk, balance, place. The bird sipped. Cheep!

The chores were almost done. Pao swept the last leaf. Swish, swish. He shook out the mats. Flap! He stacked the buckets. Clink. The rooftop looked clean and bright. It smelled like lemon and fresh air.

Pao stood tall and took a deep breath. In, out. “We did it,” he said. “My body can do many things. I can step. I can scoop. I can hop. I can push. I can be gentle.”

Granny Lin hugged him. “Yes, Pao. Your patient body helps. Your kind heart helps, too.”

Just then, the little bird hopped to the edge and flapped, flap, flap. It lifted into the air. It flew in a little circle and chirped. “Cheep-cheep!” It was saying thank you.

Pao waved. “Good-bye, friend!”

They sat on the warm rooftop and drank cool tea. Sip, ahh. Pao’s muscles felt tired, but in a happy way.

Granny Lin tapped his paw. “What did you learn today?”

Pao smiled. “Moving helps me help. Moving makes me strong. I can try new moves when there is a problem. I can be patient. I can do my chores. I can help others.”

Granny Lin nodded. “That is a bright lesson.”

Pao giggled. “Tomorrow, can we do a broom dance?”

“Yes,” said Granny Lin. “We will dance and we will help.”

The wind hummed. The sun glowed. Pao felt proud and calm. The rooftop sparkled, and so did his heart.

The End.
