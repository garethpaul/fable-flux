# Joey's Honest Helper Day

Joey the kangaroo was a bouncy, happy little joey who loved to help. His soft brown fur was fluffy, and his big dark eyes sparkled with excitement. Today, Mama Kangaroo had a special job for him.

"Joey, sweetie," Mama called from downstairs. "Could you please clean up the quiet attic? Put all the old toys in the blue box, and all the books on the wooden shelf."

Joey's ears perked up. "Yes, Mama! I'll do it right away!" he said, hopping up the creaky wooden stairs. *Thump, thump, thump* went his little feet.

The attic smelled like old wood and dust. Sunlight streamed through a small window, making everything look golden and warm. Joey saw toys scattered everywhere - teddy bears, blocks, and toy cars. Books were piled in corners like little mountains.

"This looks easy!" Joey said to himself, starting to bounce around the room.

But as he began working, Joey noticed something shiny under an old blanket. He pulled it away and gasped. "Wow! A box of colorful marbles!"

The marbles were so pretty - red like apples, blue like the sky, and green like grass. Joey forgot all about his cleaning job. He sat down and began rolling the marbles across the floor. *Click, click, roll* they went, making musical sounds.

"These are so much fun!" Joey giggled, watching them sparkle in the sunlight.

Time passed quickly. Soon, Joey heard Mama's voice calling from downstairs.

"Joey! How is the cleaning going?"

Joey looked around. The toys were still scattered everywhere. The books were still in messy piles. He had only been playing with marbles!

Joey's tummy felt funny - like butterflies were dancing inside. He wanted to call back, "Almost done, Mama!" But that wouldn't be true.

Joey remembered what Mama always said: "Honest joeys are happy joeys."

He took a deep breath. "Mama," he called down honestly, "I found some pretty marbles and started playing. I haven't finished cleaning yet. I'm sorry."

Joey heard Mama's gentle footsteps coming up the stairs. She appeared in the doorway with a warm smile.

"Thank you for being honest, Joey," Mama said, giving him a soft hug. "I'm proud of you for telling the truth. Would you like some help finishing your job?"

"Yes, please!" Joey said, feeling much better. "But first, let me put these marbles away."

Together, they worked as a team. Mama helped Joey put the colorful toys in the blue box - *plop, plop, plop* they went. Joey carefully placed each book on the wooden shelf, making neat rows.

"Look, Mama! We did it!" Joey said proudly, bouncing up and down.

"We sure did," Mama smiled. "And because you were honest about not finishing, and because you listened to my instructions, you completed your task perfectly. How does that feel?"

Joey's whole body felt warm and happy. "It feels wonderful! Being honest feels much better than hiding the truth."

"That's right, my sweet joey," Mama said. "Honest helpers are the very best helpers."

From that day on, whenever Joey had a job to do, he remembered to listen carefully, work hard, and always tell the truth - even when it was difficult.

The End.
