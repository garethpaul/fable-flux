# Little Trunks Tries Again

In a cozy, comfortable nursery, a gentle elephant calf named Little Trunks blinked his long eyelashes. The nursery was warm and bright. Soft rugs covered the floor like puffy clouds. Colorful blocks made neat towers. Tiny chairs were lined up like a parade. The air smelled like fresh bananas and warm milk, and a clock went tick-tick, softly and steady.

Today was circle time. Little Trunks sat with his friends in a big circle on the rug. There was Mimi the mouse with whiskers like tiny paintbrushes. There was Boldo the bear cub with a fuzzy belly. There was Lila the lizard with sparkly green scales, and Pip the penguin with smooth, cool feathers. Everyone was different. Everyone was special.

Teacher Nia, a kind giraffe with a rainbow scarf, smiled and said, “Today, we will learn a new song with hand claps and toe taps!”

Little Trunks loved music. His big ears flapped happily. But when he looked down at his big, round feet and his long, wiggly trunk, he felt shy. “My feet are so big,” he whispered. “My trunk is so long. What if I can’t clap or tap like everyone else?”

Mimi’s whiskers twitched. “We can try together,” she squeaked.

Boldo grinned. “We can practice,” he rumbled.

Lila flicked her tongue. “We are all different. That makes our music interesting!”

Pip waddled closer. “I can’t clap with hands,” he chirped. “But I can flap my flippers. Listen!” Flap-flap!

Teacher Nia nodded. “We all have different bodies. We can all join in, in our own way. Diversity means we are not the same, and that is wonderful.”

The music started. Tap-tap went the drum. Ding-ding sang the triangle. Teacher Nia sang softly, “Clap, clap, tap your toes. Wiggle fingers, touch your nose.”

Mimi clapped tiny paws. Clap-clap!
Boldo tapped his toes. Tap-tap!
Lila wiggled her fingers. Wiggle-wiggle!
Pip touched his beak. Boop!

Little Trunks lifted his big feet to tap. Thump! The floor shook a little. He tried to clap, but his hands were not like Mimi’s. He had wide, soft feet and a long trunk. He felt warm in his cheeks. “Oh no,” he whispered.

He tried again. Thump-thump. His toes taps were very loud. He tried to clap by tapping his trunk to his chest. Thoop! It made a funny sound. Some blocks wobbled. Little Trunks’ ears drooped.

“I’m too different,” he said, voice small.

Teacher Nia knelt so her eyes met his. “Different is not bad, Little Trunks. It is brave to try. Let’s practice together. What can your trunk do?”

Little Trunks sniffed. His trunk could pick up a feather. It could blow gentle air. It could make a soft trumpet. He breathed in. He blew out a little puff. Whoosh. The air brushed Mimi’s whiskers.

“That tickles!” Mimi giggled.

Boldo clapped his paws. “That whoosh is great!”

Pip flapped. “You can make wind music!”

Lila smiled. “Try a pattern. Whoosh-whoosh, then thump.”

Little Trunks’ ears lifted. “I can try.” He took a deep, slow breath. Whoosh-whoosh. Then he tapped one big foot. Thump. The sound was deep and soft, like a comfy drum.

Teacher Nia led the song again. “Clap, clap, tap your toes. Wiggle fingers, touch your nose.”

This time, everyone used their own way:
- Mimi clapped tiny paws: clap-clap!
- Boldo tapped and hummed: tap-tap, mmm-mmm!
- Lila clicked her tongue: click-click!
- Pip flapped his flippers and beeped: flap-flap, beep!
- Little Trunks blew a gentle whoosh-whoosh and tapped: thump.

The nursery filled with different sounds. It was like a rainbow you could hear. Little Trunks smiled. His tummy felt warm and proud.

Then the drum beat got faster. Tap-tap-tap! Little Trunks tried to keep up. He missed his turn. He made a loud trumpet by mistake. Brrr-RAAAP! Everyone paused. The blocks shivered.

Little Trunks froze. “Sorry,” he whispered. A tear almost spilled.

Teacher Nia shook her head kindly. “No need to be sorry. We are practicing. When we practice, we make mistakes. Mistakes help us learn.”

Boldo nodded. “I fall down when I learn to roll.”

Mimi squeaked. “I drop cheese when I learn to share.”

Pip waddled in a circle. “I slip on ice sometimes.”

Lila flicked her tail. “I mix up left and right.”

Little Trunks took a brave breath. “I can try again,” he said, a little stronger.

They started the song once more, slower this time. Teacher Nia counted, “One, two, ready, go.”

Whoosh-whoosh, thump.
Clap-clap.
Tap-tap.
Click-click.
Flap-flap, beep.

It fit! The sounds worked together like puzzle pieces. Little Trunks felt the beat in his feet. He felt the gentle air in his trunk. He felt happy. He felt included.

When the song ended, everyone cheered. “We did it!” they cried.

Teacher Nia wrapped her rainbow scarf around the circle for a soft group hug. “What did we learn today?” she asked.

Little Trunks lifted his trunk with pride. “We are all different,” he said. “That makes music better. When I practice, I get better, too. And when I make a mistake, I try again.”

Mimi nodded. “Diversity is beautiful!”

Boldo added, “Practice makes us strong.”

Pip chirped, “Trying again shows big courage.”

Lila smiled. “Together, we make a bright, happy song.”

Little Trunks looked around the comfortable nursery—the soft rugs, the colorful blocks, the warm light. He heard the gentle tick-tick of the clock and the soft breaths of his friends. He felt safe. He felt loved. He felt proud to be himself.

He wrapped his trunk in a little hug around his friends. “Thank you,” he said. “I like my whoosh.”

“We like it, too,” they said.

And they sang one more time, with all their different sounds, making music that felt like sunshine.

The End.
