# Leo’s Turn at the Table

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny afternoon, the smell of warm bread and sweet fruit drifted through the dining room. The wooden table was big and shiny, and it was set with colorful plates, tiny cups, and a tall pitcher of cool apple juice. Leo’s tail swished with excitement.

Mama Lion called, “Time to eat!”

Leo padded into the dining room with his big brother, Max, and his little cousin, Mina. The chairs made soft scrapes on the floor as they sat. The bread was crusty and warm. The fruit salad sparkled with strawberries and blueberries. There was a bowl of crunchy carrots, too.

“My tummy is rumbling,” said Leo, patting his belly.

“I’m thirsty,” said Mina, looking at the tall pitcher of juice.

Max reached for the bread basket. Leo reached for the juice. Their paws bumped.

“Ow!” said Mina, surprised.

Leo pulled his paw back. His ears twitched. “I’m sorry. I’m just so thirsty.”

Mama Lion smiled gently. “I know you are. But remember, we can take turns.”

Leo took a deep breath in through his nose. He smelled the sweet apples and the warm bread. The smell made his mouth water. He slowly let the breath out. He remembered what Mama had said yesterday: “When you feel hurry-hurry, breathe slow-slow.”

Max looked at the pitcher. “I can pour first,” he said. “I’m big.”

Leo felt a little spark in his chest. He wanted to pour. He wanted to show he could do it. But he also remembered that taking turns means everyone gets a chance. He had a choice.

Leo said softly, “We can make turns. Max can pour first, then me, then Mina.”

Mina clapped her paws. “Yes! I like turns.”

Mama Lion nodded. “That is a kind decision. Thank you, Leo.”

Max poured the juice for Mina. The juice made a little glug-glug sound, and ice cubes chimed like tiny bells. Mina smiled. “Thank you, Max.”

“Now it’s my turn to pour,” said Leo, sitting up tall. His paws were small, and the pitcher was heavy. He felt a little worry bubble in his tummy. What if he spilled?

He took another slow breath. In-n-n. Out-t-t. His whiskers twitched. He felt calm and brave.

Mama Lion said, “Use two paws. Go slow. You are responsible and careful.”

Leo held the pitcher with two paws. He poured a little juice for Max. Glug. He poured a little juice for himself. Glug. A drop dripped on the table. Plip!

“It’s okay,” said Mama Lion. She handed Leo a napkin. “We clean up our spills.”

Leo dabbed the drop. He felt proud. “I poured! I cleaned! I can help!”

They passed the bread basket. Max took one piece. Mina took one piece. Leo wanted two pieces. The bread smelled so good, like sunshine and butter. But he remembered: taking turns also means sharing and waiting.

“I will take one now,” Leo said, “and I will wait for my next turn.”

Mina grinned. “You are generous, Leo.”

As they ate, the chairs creaked gently. The carrots crunched. The fruit was cold and juicy. Leo licked a drip of blueberry juice from his whiskers. He felt warm and peaceful inside.

After a while, Mama Lion placed a small plate in the center of the table. On it was a round honey cookie, golden and shiny. Just one cookie.

Mina’s eyes grew wide. “A cookie!”

Max licked his lips. “I want it.”

Leo looked at the cookie. He could almost taste the sweet honey. His heart thumped. He wanted it too. He looked at Mina. He looked at Max. He could choose to grab it. Or he could choose to take turns again.

Leo put a paw on his chest and breathed. In. Out. He thought, I can be mindful. I can listen to my body. I can listen to others.

“I have an idea,” Leo said. “We can decide together. Who had a turn first with the juice?”

“Me,” said Max.

“Who had a turn pouring next?” asked Leo.

“Leo,” said Mina.

“And who got the first bread?” asked Leo.

“Mina,” said Max, smiling.

Leo nodded. “So now, we can give Mina the first cookie bite. Then Max. Then me.”

Mina looked surprised. “Really?”

Leo smiled. “Yes. We all get a piece.”

Mama Lion carefully broke the cookie into three even pieces. Crack. She gave one to Mina, one to Max, and one to Leo. The honey taste was sweet and soft on Leo’s tongue. His tummy felt happy, and his heart felt even happier.

“You made a fair plan,” said Mama Lion. “That is good decision-making. You showed responsibility and generosity.”

Leo’s tail swished with pride. “Taking turns makes everyone smile.”

They finished their meal. Leo carried his plate to the sink. The water ran warm over his paws as he rinsed. He wiped the table with a soft cloth, circles and swishes. He felt calm and strong.

Before nap time, Mama Lion hugged him. Her fur was warm and cozy. “What did you learn today, my brave little lion cub?”

Leo nuzzled her and said, “I learned to breathe when I feel rushy. I learned to make a plan. I learned to take turns and share. When we take turns, everyone gets a chance, and that feels good.”

Mama Lion purred. “That is a wonderful lesson.”

Leo curled up in a sunny patch on the rug. He felt safe, full, and kind. As he closed his eyes, he thought, Tomorrow, I can take turns again.

The End.
