# Sunny Hops and Kind Words

Kip is a joyful kangaroo joey. He lives in a cozy home with a sunny backyard. The backyard smells like sweet flowers and fresh grass. The sky is bright and blue. Bees buzz softly. A warm breeze tickles Kip’s ears.

Kip wiggles his little paws. “Today is our Clean-Up Day!” he says. He is excited. He wants to help his neighbors and make the yard tidy. He has three jobs. First, he will pick up leaves. Then, he will water the flower pots. Last, he will share lemonade with everyone who helps.

Mama Roo smiles. “Good plan, Kip. Let’s use kind words and calm voices today. That helps everyone feel safe and happy.”

Kip nods. “Kind words and calm voices,” he repeats.

Kip hops to the leaf pile. The leaves are crunchy and smell like earth. Rustle, rustle. He scoops them into a big bag. “I can do this!” he says.

Suddenly, he hears a squeak. “Kip! Kip!” It is Dot the duckling from next door. Dot waddles through the gate, her little feet patter-pattering. She carries a tiny rake. “I want to help! Can I help now?”

Kip smiles. “Yes, let’s work together.” They rake. They giggle. They make two neat piles.

Then Pip the possum peeks over the fence. “Can I hang the bird feeder?” Pip asks. His tail curls like a question mark. He holds a shiny feeder that smells like sunflower seeds.

Kip looks at his list. Leaves first, water second, lemonade last. The bird feeder is not on the list. He feels a squeeze in his tummy. “Um, maybe later,” he says, a little fast. Pip’s ears droop.

Just then, Bella the blue butterfly flutters around Kip’s head. “You promised me a flower shower,” she whispers. “Remember the water?” Her wings shimmer in the sun.

Kip’s tummy squeeze gets tighter. Dot wants to rake. Pip wants to hang the feeder. Bella wants water. His list feels big. His heart beats thump-thump. He wants to help everyone. He wants to be kind. But his head feels busy.

Mama Roo comes outside with a gentle smile. “I see many helpers,” she says. “I hear many wishes.”

Kip takes a slow breath in. He lets it out softly. “I need to use my kind words,” he whispers to himself. He pats his chest. “Calm voice.”

He turns to his friends. “Thank you for wanting to help,” Kip says, slow and clear. “I have a plan. First leaves, then water, then lemonade. We can also hang the bird feeder after the leaves.” He holds up three fingers. “One, two, three.”

Dot nods. “I can rake more leaves!” she chirps.

Pip’s eyes brighten. “So I can hang the feeder after the leaves?”

“Yes,” Kip says. “After the leaves. Thank you for waiting.”

Bella twirls in the air. “And then water for flowers!”

Kip grins. The tight feeling in his tummy loosens. He has a plan, and he said it with kind words.

They work together. Rustle, rustle go the rakes. Plop, plop go the leaves into the bag. The sun feels warm on their backs. Birds sing cheep-cheep in the tree. When they finish the leaves, Kip claps. “Great job, team!”

“Now it’s feeder time,” Kip says. He holds the ladder while Pip climbs carefully. Pip hangs the shiny feeder. It swings gently and smells like seeds. A tiny sparrow lands and pecks. “Cheep! Thank you!”

Bella flutters by. “Flower shower?” she hums.

“Water time!” Kip says. He turns on the hose. The water is cool and sparkly. The flowers drink and stand tall. Bella dances in the spray. “This feels like a rainbow!” she laughs.

The yard looks neat and bright. The flowers shine. The feeder glimmers. The birds sing. Kip wipes his forehead. “We did it!”

Mama Roo brings a pitcher of lemonade. The lemons smell zesty and sweet. Kip pours drinks into little cups. “Last job,” he says with a smile, “share lemonade!” He gives a cup to Dot, to Pip, to Mama, and even holds a tiny drop for Bella on a leaf.

They sit in the shade. The grass feels soft. The lemonade tastes cool and yummy.

Dot says, “We worked fast!”

Mama Roo nods. “You worked well. Kip, I saw your kind words and calm voice. You made a plan. You listened. You helped your friends help you.”

Kip’s cheeks feel warm and proud. He looks at his friends. “When I felt busy, I talked kindly,” he says. “I told the plan.” He points to his list. “First leaves, then feeder, then water, then lemonade. We did it together.”

Pip sips his drink. “Your words helped my ears and my heart,” he says. “I knew when it was my turn.”

Bella flutters close. “Peaceful words make sunny days.”

Kip nods. “Peaceful talking helps me and my friends. Making a plan helps time. Helping the yard helps everyone.”

They clink their cups softly. Sip, sip. The sun glows. The birds cheer. The backyard feels happy and calm.

Kip smiles. “Today we used kind words. We took turns. We helped our place.” He gives a little hop. “That is our sunny way.”

The End.
