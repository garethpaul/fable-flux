# Toby the Tortoise and the Moving Day

In a busy basement filled with boxes and old furniture, lived a wise old tortoise named Toby. The basement was always full of sounds - *thump, thump, thump* - as people moved things around upstairs.

One sunny morning, Toby heard new sounds. *Crash! Bang! Bump!* A family of mice had moved into the corner of the basement. But something was wrong. The little mice looked very sad and tired.

"Hello there," said Toby in his slow, gentle voice. "I'm Toby. Welcome to our basement!"

The smallest mouse, named Pip, sniffled. "Hi, Mr. Toby. I'm Pip, and these are my brothers and sisters. We're too tired to unpack our things. We've been sitting in our box all day."

Toby looked around and saw the mouse family surrounded by tiny boxes, but none of them were moving. They all looked sleepy and droopy.

"Why are you so tired?" asked Toby, stretching his wrinkled neck.

"We haven't moved our bodies in days," squeaked Pip's sister Mia. "We just sit and sit and sit. Our legs feel wobbly, and our arms feel weak."

Toby nodded wisely. His shell gleamed in the dim basement light. "Ah, I see the problem! Your bodies need exercise to feel strong and happy. Let me show you something wonderful."

Even though Toby was old and moved slowly, he began to stretch his legs one by one. "First, we stretch like this," he said, extending each leg forward and back. "Feel how good that feels!"

The mice giggled and copied Toby, stretching their tiny paws.

"Now," said Toby, "let's march in place!" He lifted one foot, then the other, moving steadily. *Step, step, step!* The mice joined in, and soon they were all marching together.

"This is fun!" laughed Pip, his whiskers twitching with joy.

"Exercise makes our hearts pump strong," explained Toby. "It helps our muscles grow and makes us feel happy inside!"

After marching, Toby showed them how to reach up high like they were picking apples from a tree, then bend down to touch their toes. The basement filled with happy squeaks and giggles.

"I feel so much better!" said Mia, spinning in a circle.

"Me too!" agreed Pip. "My legs don't feel wobbly anymore!"

Toby smiled. "Now you have energy to unpack your boxes. But remember - you must take care of yourselves every day. No one else can exercise your body for you. You must do it yourself!"

The mice understood. They spent the rest of the day unpacking, taking exercise breaks whenever they felt tired. They marched around their boxes, stretched their paws, and even did little mouse jumping jacks.

By evening, their new home was all set up, and they felt wonderful.

"Thank you, Toby!" called Pip. "Will you exercise with us every morning?"

"Of course!" said Toby. "We can help each other stay healthy and strong. That's what good neighbors do!"

From that day on, every morning in the busy basement, you could hear the happy sounds of Toby and the mice exercising together. *Step, step, step!* They learned that taking care of their bodies made everything else easier and more fun.

And whenever new animals moved into the basement, Toby and the mice taught them the same important lesson: exercise every day to feel your best!

The End.
