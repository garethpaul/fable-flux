# Bubbles from Around the World

Kiki the calm koala loved quiet mornings. She padded into the bright bathroom with her soft, furry feet. The floor tiles felt cool. The air smelled like mint and warm soap. Sunlight made little squares on the shiny mirror. Drip, drip, went the faucet, like a tiny drum.

“Good morning, little drops,” Kiki whispered, smiling. She liked to organize things. Her comb, her cup, and her toothbrush sat in a neat line. But today, something was new.

On the counter stood a basket with many soaps. There were round soaps, square soaps, and tiny soaps wrapped in colors. Some had letters from different languages. Some had flowers and swirls. A paper said, “Soap Share Day.”

Kiki’s ears perked up. “Soap Share Day?” she said. “What is that?”

Her friend Poppy the parrot fluttered to the towel rack. “Squaaawk! We share soaps from our families! Smell and learn!” Poppy chirped, flapping happily.

Kiki sniffed. “Mmm.” One soap smelled like oranges. Another smelled like lavender. A blue soap smelled like the ocean. She read the nice tags.

“From my Nana in Mexico,” said one tag with bright dots. “From our auntie in India,” said a tag covered in gold. “From my friend in Ghana,” said a tag with pretty kente patterns. “From my cousin in Japan,” said a tag with a tiny fan.

Kiki felt warm in her heart. “These soaps come from many places,” she said. “We can learn about each other.”

Then Kiki noticed a problem. The basket was tipping. Boxes and bottles were everywhere. The sink was splashed. The soap dish was sticky. A little bottle rolled, clink, clink, toward the drain.

“Oh no!” Kiki reached just in time. The bottle smelled like sweet rice and flowers. “We need to keep these safe,” she said. “We need to keep them clean and neat.”

Poppy squawked, “Help! Help!” More friends came: Tavi the turtle, Zuzu the zebra, and Miko the mouse. They all peered at the messy basket.

Zuzu wrinkled her nose. “It’s too messy. I can’t see.”

Miko squeaked, “I want to try the bubble soap from Ghana!”

Tavi blinked slowly. “We must not waste. We must be gentle.”

Kiki took a calm breath. She felt her belly move in and out, slow and soft. “We have a problem,” she said. “The soaps are special. The basket is messy. The sink is wet. We need a peaceful plan.”

“What’s the plan?” asked Zuzu.

Kiki touched the cool counter. “Step one: We listen. What does each soap need? Step two: We organize. We make a safe place for each. Step three: We share and learn—one at a time.”

The friends nodded.

Kiki got two dry washcloths. “Soft beds for soaps,” she said. She laid them on the counter, pat pat. Tavi brought a small tray. “A home for little bottles,” he said.

“Let’s sort by story,” Kiki smiled. “We match each soap with its tag. We keep the tags close, so we remember.”

They read together. Poppy read the tag from Mexico. “This orange soap is called naranja. It smells like fruit and sunshine.”

Zuzu read the tag from India. “This soap has sandalwood. It is used for calm and prayer.”

Miko read the tag from Ghana. “This black soap helps clean gently.”

Tavi read the tag from Japan. “This soap smells like cherry blossoms in spring.”

Kiki listened to the sounds of their voices. She felt proud. “We will place each soap on a cloth. We will stand each bottle in the tray. No crowding.”

They worked slowly. Drip, drip went the faucet. Swish went the washcloth. Clack went the tray. The bathroom felt peaceful again. The counter looked neat and bright. Every tag sat by its soap, like a tiny book with a tiny friend.

“Now we can share,” Kiki said. “But only one soap at a time. We will each choose one, wash our paws, and say thank you to the place it came from.”

Poppy chose the naranja soap. “I will wash and say, Gracias, Mexico!” he chirped. The orange smell filled the air, sweet and happy.

Zuzu tried the sandalwood soap. She rubbed her hooves gently. “Namaste, India,” she said softly. The scent was warm, like a hug.

Miko took a tiny dab of black soap. It bubbled, soft and rich. “Medase, Ghana,” he squeaked, grinning.

Tavi chose the cherry blossom soap. He closed his eyes and smiled, “Arigatou, Japan.”

Kiki washed last. She picked a little piece with lavender and honey from France. “Merci, France,” she whispered. The bubbles felt silky. The room smelled like flowers and tea.

They all rinsed with care. They dried with soft towels. No spills. No waste. The tags stayed dry, and the soaps stayed safe.

Zuzu said, “We learned new words!”

Poppy sang, “We learned new smells!”

Miko squeaked, “We learned to be careful!”

Tavi nodded. “We learned to share.”

Kiki felt her heart glow like the sun on the mirror. “We learned to appreciate,” she said. “Each soap is special. Each place is special. When we listen and organize, we can care for everyone’s things. We can help our community bathroom stay clean and kind.”

They made a little sign: “Soap Share Day—One at a time, with care.” Kiki taped it gently near the sink. The letters were big and friendly.

The friends looked at their neat counter and their happy soaps. The bathroom smelled like a garden and a fruit stand and a breeze. It felt like many places together, right here.

Kiki hugged her friends. “Thank you for sharing your cultures,” she said. “Thank you for peaceful solutions.”

Everyone smiled. Their hearts were calm.

The End.
