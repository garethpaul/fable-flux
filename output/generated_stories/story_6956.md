# Tiko the Tortoise Counts to Calm

Tiko was a wise tortoise with a shiny, smooth shell. He walked slowly, but he thought carefully. One sunny afternoon, Tiko pushed open the door to the musical practice room. The room was warm and bright. It smelled like fresh wood and a little like lemon polish. Drums sat in a corner. A piano waited with black and white keys. Colorful shakers rested in a basket. Soft sunlight made dust sparkle like tiny stars.

“Hello, music,” Tiko said in his gentle voice. “I am ready to listen and learn.”

Just then, two young friends, Bunny and Fox, rushed in. Bunny held a small drum. Fox held a shiny triangle. Their ears and tails twitched with energy.

“I want to play loud!” Bunny shouted. Boom, boom, boom! The drum thumped. The floor buzzed and Tiko’s shell hummed.

“I want to play first!” Fox snapped. Ting, ting, ting! The triangle rang sharp and bright. It tickled Tiko’s ears.

The sounds crashed together. Boom! Ting! Boom-ting! Bunny’s nose scrunched. Fox’s eyes narrowed. The music did not feel happy. It felt too spicy and prickly.

Tiko raised a slow hand. “Friends,” he said, “I hear big feelings. Let’s take a breath.”

“I’m mad,” Bunny said, stomping a furry foot. “I waited all day.”

“I’m mad too,” Fox said, tail flicking. “I had the idea first.”

Tiko nodded. “Thank you for telling me. Mad feelings are real. We can help mad feelings. We can make a plan.”

Bunny huffed. “A plan?”

Fox tilted their head. “What kind of plan?”

Tiko took a long, calm breath. He closed his eyes and counted in a soft voice. “One… two… three.” He opened his eyes and smiled. “When I feel mad, I stop, I breathe, and I count to three. Then I choose what to do. That is called decision-making.”

“De-ci-sion… making,” Bunny said slowly.

“It means choosing,” Tiko said. “We can choose kind actions.”

Bunny held the drum close. “What should we choose?”

Tiko tapped his shell, thinking. Tap, tap. “First choice: We can listen. Second choice: We can take turns. Third choice: We can make music together.”

Fox blinked. “How do we choose?”

“We try each choice,” Tiko said. “We listen to our feelings. We listen to each other. Then we pick the best choice for everyone. That is respect.”

Bunny’s ears drooped a little. “I can try.”

Fox’s tail slowed. “I can try too.”

“Good,” said Tiko. “Let’s start with listening. No playing yet. Just ears.”

The room got quiet. Bunny and Fox held still. Tiko tapped the drum with one gentle finger. Tap… tap… tap. The sound was soft and round, like a heartbeat.

“Now the triangle,” Tiko said, nodding to Fox.

Fox lifted the metal stick and tapped. Ting… ting… ting. The sound was bright and shiny, like sunlight on water.

Bunny smiled a tiny smile. “I like that sound.”

Fox whispered, “I like the drum too.”

“Thank you for listening,” Tiko said. “How do your bodies feel?”

“My tummy is less tight,” Bunny said.

“My face is not scrunched now,” Fox said.

Tiko nodded. “Listening helps.”

“Now the second choice: take turns,” Tiko said. He set a small sand timer on the piano. The sand whooshed softly. “Bunny first for one turn. Then Fox.”

Bunny looked at Fox. “Okay?”

Fox took a breath. “Okay.”

Bunny played. Boom… boom… boom-boom. The drum thumped warm and friendly. The sand fell in a soft hiss. When the last grain dropped, Tiko smiled.

“Now Fox’s turn,” Tiko said.

Fox lifted the triangle and played. Ting… ting… tingle-ting. The sound sparkled like tiny bells. When the sand finished, they stopped.

“How do you feel now?” Tiko asked.

Bunny wiggled. “Better!”

Fox nodded. “Me too.”

“Third choice: play together,” Tiko said. “But we need a plan. What kind of music do we want? Loud or soft?”

Bunny thought. “Soft.”

Fox thought. “Happy.”

Tiko grinned. “Soft and happy. Good plan. Let’s decide the pattern. Drum goes ‘boom… rest… boom… rest.’ Triangle goes ‘ting on the rest.’ Can you try?”

Bunny tapped: “Boom… rest… boom… rest.”

Fox waited and then: “Ting! Ting!”

They tried again. Boom… ting… boom… ting. The sounds fit like a hug. The room felt warm and sweet, like a cozy blanket. Tiko swayed as he counted, “One, two, three, four.”

Bunny giggled. “We did it!”

Fox beamed. “We made soft, happy music!”

Tiko clapped slowly. “You made a good plan. You made good choices. You helped each other. You showed respect. You calmed mad feelings.”

Bunny looked at Fox. “I’m sorry I shouted.”

Fox nodded. “I’m sorry I snapped.”

Tiko smiled. “Saying sorry is kind. Thank you.”

Bunny asked, “Tiko, what should we do next time we feel mad?”

Tiko tapped his shell again. “You can Stop. Breathe. Count to three. Then choose. Choices can be: listen, take turns, make a plan together.”

Bunny and Fox said together, “Stop, breathe, count, choose!”

The piano keys glowed in the light. The drum felt warm under Bunny’s paws. The triangle felt cool in Fox’s hand. The room hummed with calm and joy.

“Can we play our song again?” Fox asked.

“Yes,” Bunny said. “Soft and happy!”

Tiko winked. “I will keep the beat.”

He lifted a shaker and made a soft swish-swish. Bunny played boom… rest. Fox played ting on the rest. Tiko swished. They smiled. They listened. They chose kind actions.

And the music smiled back.

The End.
