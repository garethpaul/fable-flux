# Tilly's Rooftop Rainbow Day

High up on the sunny rooftop of the city library, Tilly the wise tortoise was painting beautiful pictures with her colorful brushes. The warm sun felt nice on her green shell, and she hummed a happy tune as she dipped her brush in bright yellow paint.

"What a perfect day for making art!" Tilly said to herself, carefully painting a big, cheerful sunflower.

Suddenly, she heard loud voices coming from the other side of the rooftop. Tilly slowly walked over to see what was happening. She found three young friends sitting in a circle, but they didn't look happy at all.

"I want to paint the sky BLUE!" shouted Maya, a little girl with curly hair. Her face was red and her hands were in tight fists.

"No way! The sky should be PURPLE!" yelled Sam, crossing his arms and stamping his foot. "Purple is the best color ever!"

"You're both wrong!" cried Zoe, throwing her paintbrush down. "The sky has to be PINK! That's the most beautiful color!"

All three children were breathing fast and looking very angry. Their art supplies were scattered everywhere, and their painting was a big, muddy mess.

Tilly took a deep breath and walked slowly toward them. "Hello, little artists," she said in her calm, gentle voice. "I can see you're all feeling very upset. When I feel angry, I like to take three deep breaths. Would you like to try that with me?"

The children looked at the wise tortoise. Something about her peaceful voice made them want to listen.

"Breathe in slowly through your nose," Tilly demonstrated, making a soft whooshing sound. "Now breathe out through your mouth. Let's do it together."

All four friends took three big, slow breaths. Maya's fists unclenched. Sam's shoulders relaxed. Zoe picked up her paintbrush.

"That feels better," Maya said quietly.

"Now," said Tilly, "I heard you each have wonderful ideas about what color the sky should be. Maya likes blue, Sam loves purple, and Zoe thinks pink is beautiful. You know what? You're all right!"

"But how can we all be right?" asked Sam, tilting his head.

Tilly smiled and pointed to the real sky above them. "Look up! Sometimes the sky is blue during the day. At sunset, it can be pink and orange. And have you ever seen a purple sky during a storm?"

The children looked up with wide, amazed eyes.

"What if we work together?" suggested Tilly. "We could paint a sky that changes colors, just like the real one does!"

Zoe's eyes lit up. "We could make a rainbow sky!"

"I could paint the blue part," offered Maya.

"And I could add purple clouds," said Sam excitedly.

"And I'll paint pink and orange for the sunset part!" added Zoe.

Working together, the three friends created the most beautiful, colorful sky painting anyone had ever seen. They shared their paints, took turns with the brushes, and helped each other reach the high parts of their big canvas.

"When we work together and share our different ideas," said Tilly, admiring their masterpiece, "we can create something even more wonderful than any of us could make alone."

The children beamed with pride at their rainbow sky, full of blues, purples, pinks, and every color in between.

"Thank you, Tilly," they said together. "We learned that feeling angry is okay, but taking deep breaths and working together feels much better!"

As the sun set over their beautiful rooftop art studio, Tilly smiled. It had been a perfect day for learning and creating together.

The End.
