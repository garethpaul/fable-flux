# Koala Kori’s Study-Corner Snack Plan

Kori was a calm koala with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. Every day after nap time, Kori padded to the cozy study corner. The study corner was a sunny nook with a little table, a blue beanbag, and a basket of crayons that smelled like sweet berries. A small window let in warm light, and a tiny fan hummed gently, “whirr, whirr,” keeping the air cool.

On this day, Kori had a plan. “Today I will learn shapes,” Kori whispered, patting the page of a shape book. The paper felt smooth under Kori’s paw. “Circle, square, triangle,” Kori said softly, tapping each one. Tap, tap, tap.

Just then, Kori’s friend, Pip the little parrot, fluttered in. Pip’s bright green feathers rustled like leaves. “Squawk! Snack time! Let’s have crunchy crackers,” Pip chirped, landing on the back of the chair.

Kori took a calm breath. In… out. “I like crunchy crackers,” Kori said. “But I also want something juicy. Maybe apple slices?”

Pip tilted his head. “Crackers! Crackers are the best,” he sang, hopping from foot to foot. His tiny claws clicked on the chair, click, click.

Kori’s tummy gave a small rumble. “I feel hungry,” Kori said. “My body wants good fuel. Good food helps me think and learn.”

Pip puffed his chest. “Crackers help me flap fast,” he squawked.

Kori closed the shape book and smiled. “Let’s talk,” Kori said in a gentle voice. “We can share ideas.”

Pip wiggled his wings. “But I want crackers only,” he chirped.

Kori pointed to the study table and the little snack tray. “We can find a way that feels good for both of us,” Kori said. “This is called a compromise. It means we both give a little, and we both get a little.”

Pip blinked. “Comp-ry-mise?” he tried.

“Com-pro-mise,” Kori said slowly. “We listen. We are open to new things. We choose together.”

Pip looked at the tray. There were round crackers, a shiny red apple, a small carrot stick, and a tiny cup of hummus. The apple smelled sweet. The carrot was bright and crunchy. The crackers were golden and crisp.

Kori gave a little nod. “What if we have crackers and apple slices? We can dip the crackers in hummus for protein, and the apple gives us vitamins. The carrot can help us crunch and chew.”

Pip leaned close to the apple. He took a tiny sniff. “It smells like sunshine,” he chirped. He pecked the carrot lightly. “Crunch!”

Kori’s ears twitched happily. “Will you try the apple if we also have crackers?”

Pip thought for a moment. “Hmm,” he hummed. “I like the sound of crunch. I like the smell of sweet. Okay! I will try apple if we also have crackers.”

Kori clapped softly. “That is a kind compromise. We are open-minded. We try new foods.”

They washed their paws and claws. The warm water felt nice on Kori’s pads. “Swish, swish,” went the water. Kori sliced the apple into tiny moons. Pip counted, “One, two, three, four!”

They sat in the study corner together. The fan went “whirr.” The crayons smelled like berries. The table felt cool. Kori took a bite of cracker with hummus. Crunch. Creamy. Yummy. Pip watched, then took a small peck of apple. “Mmm!” he chirped, surprised. “It is juicy! It is sweet! It makes my tongue tingle.”

Kori smiled. “How do you feel?”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “Happy,” he said. “And brave. I tried something new.”

Kori nodded. “Your body likes food that helps you learn. Crackers give energy. Apple gives vitamins. Carrot helps our eyes. Hummus gives protein for strong muscles.”

Pip chomped the carrot. “Crunch, crunch.” He laughed. “I like the crunch song.”

Kori opened the shape book again. “Now we can learn shapes,” Kori said. “Circle, square, triangle.”

Pip peered at the page. “The apple slice looks like a moon shape,” he chirped.

“Yes,” Kori said. “A crescent. We can find shapes in our snacks.”

They made a little game. Kori placed a round cracker on the circle picture. “Circle!” Kori said. Pip placed a carrot stick along the side of the square. “Line!” he chirped. Kori made a tiny triangle with two cracker pieces and a carrot tip. “Triangle!” they said together. They giggled. The study corner felt warm and friendly.

After the game, Kori asked, “What did we learn today?”

Pip thought. He tapped his beak softly. “We learned shapes,” he said. “And we learned a big word. Com-pro-mise.”

Kori’s eyes sparkled. “Yes. We listened to each other. We were open-minded. We tried new foods. We chose a snack that was good for our bodies and happy for our tummies.”

Pip nodded. “I got crackers, and I tried apple. You got apple, and you shared crackers. We both got what we needed.”

Kori took a deep, calm breath. “Compromise helps friends,” Kori said. “It makes learning fun. It helps us grow strong and kind.”

Pip flapped his wings and sang, “Compromise! Open minds! Yummy, healthy snack time!”

Kori laughed softly. “Let’s clean up,” Kori said. They put the crumbs in the bin, wiped the table until it felt smooth again, and stacked the shape book neatly.

The study corner hummed with the fan and the happy feeling of teamwork. Kori and Pip cuddled into the beanbag, warm and cozy.

“Tomorrow,” Kori whispered, “we can try a new shape… and maybe a new fruit.”

Pip grinned. “I will listen. I will try. We will share.”

They smiled at each other. Their tummies felt full, and their hearts felt light.

The End.
