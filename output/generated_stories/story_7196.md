# Daisy's Garden Adventure

Little Daisy the deer fawn stretched her spotted legs as the warm morning sun tickled her nose. She lived near a cozy apartment building with the most wonderful balcony garden she had ever seen. The garden belonged to Mrs. Chen, a kind lady who grew colorful vegetables and herbs in bright pots.

"Good morning, Daisy!" called Mrs. Chen from her balcony. "Would you like to help me in the garden today?"

Daisy's ears perked up with excitement. "Oh yes, please! I love helping!"

Mrs. Chen smiled and pointed to her garden. "I need to water all my plants, but I hurt my back yesterday. It's hard for me to reach the tall tomato plants and carry the heavy watering can."

Daisy looked around the balcony garden. There were red tomatoes hanging high up, leafy green lettuce in low pots, orange carrots peeking from the soil, and purple eggplants growing in the corner. Everything smelled fresh and earthy.

"I want to help, but I'm too small to reach the high plants," Daisy said sadly, looking at her short legs.

Just then, Benny the blue jay landed on the balcony railing. "Tweet! Tweet! Did someone say they needed help?"

"Oh, Benny!" Daisy exclaimed. "Mrs. Chen needs help watering her garden, but the tomatoes are too high for me to reach."

Benny fluffed his bright blue feathers. "I can fly up high! But I can't carry the heavy watering can with my tiny beak."

Ruby the rabbit hopped onto the balcony from the fire escape. "Hop, hop! I heard you talking about teamwork! I'm great at carrying things, but I can't fly like Benny."

Mrs. Chen clapped her hands together. "What wonderful friends! Maybe if you all work together, you can help water my entire garden."

Daisy thought carefully. "I have an idea! Ruby, you're strong. Can you help me carry the watering can to different spots?"

"Absolutely!" Ruby said, twitching her pink nose.

"And Benny," Daisy continued, "you can fly up and tell us which high plants need water most."

"Tweet! That sounds perfect!" Benny chirped.

The three friends got to work. Ruby and Daisy worked together to carry the watering can around the balcony. Daisy used her gentle mouth to tip the can while Ruby steadied it with her paws. Benny flew high above the tomato plants.

"The big red tomato up here is very thirsty!" Benny called down. "And the yellow cherry tomatoes too!"

Ruby stood on her hind legs and helped Daisy aim the water up high while Benny guided them to the thirstiest plants.

When they finished watering the tall plants, Daisy watered the lettuce and carrots at her level. Ruby helped by digging small holes around the plants so the water could soak in better.

"Look how happy all the plants look now!" Daisy said, admiring the glistening water drops on the green leaves.

Mrs. Chen brought out a basket filled with fresh vegetables. "Thank you, my dear friends! Because you worked together, my plants are healthy and happy. These vegetables will help people grow strong and healthy too."

She gave each friend a special treat: sweet clover for Daisy, sunflower seeds for Benny, and crisp lettuce for Ruby.

"We did something really good together," Daisy said, munching her clover. "I couldn't reach high, Ruby couldn't fly, and Benny couldn't carry heavy things. But together, we could do everything!"

"That's what teamwork means," said Mrs. Chen. "When friends help each other and work together, they can accomplish wonderful things."

As the sun set, Daisy felt proud and happy. She had learned that being honest about what she couldn't do alone, and working with friends, made everything possible.

The End.
