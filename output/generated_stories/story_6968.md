# Milo the Helpful Monkey

Milo was a brave little monkey who lived in a cozy house with his family. He had soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes that sparkled when he wanted to help someone. Today, Milo heard a strange sound coming from the laundry room.

"Sniff, sniff, sob!" came the sound from behind the white washing machine.

Milo's ears perked up. Someone needed help! He swung down from his favorite climbing rope and scampered across the smooth tile floor of the laundry room. The room smelled fresh and clean, like soap bubbles and sunshine.

"Hello?" called Milo in his gentle voice. "Is someone there?"

A tiny voice answered back. "Yes... it's me, Squeaky the mouse. I'm stuck!"

Milo looked around the big washing machine and found a very small, very dirty mouse trapped between two heavy laundry baskets. Poor Squeaky was covered in dust and looked very sad.

"Oh my!" said Milo. "How did you get so dirty, little friend?"

Squeaky's whiskers drooped. "I was looking for crumbs behind the washing machine, but I got stuck when the baskets fell. Now I'm all dusty and my family won't want to hug me because I'm not clean."

Milo's kind heart felt sorry for Squeaky. He knew that being clean was important, and he also knew that everyone deserved help when they needed it.

"Don't worry, Squeaky!" said Milo with a big smile. "I'll help you get unstuck AND get clean!"

First, Milo used his strong monkey arms to carefully move one of the heavy baskets. "There!" he said. "Now you're free!"

Squeaky wiggled out, but he was still very dusty. "Thank you, Milo, but I'm still too dirty to go home."

Milo thought carefully. He looked around the laundry room with his smart monkey brain. He saw the sink, some gentle soap, and fluffy clean towels.

"I have an idea!" said Milo. "Let's get you nice and clean!"

Milo gently helped Squeaky climb up to the sink. He turned on the warm water - not too hot, not too cold - just right for a little mouse. The water made a pleasant splashing sound.

"This feels wonderful!" squeaked Squeaky as Milo carefully helped him wash with a tiny drop of gentle soap. The soap smelled like lavender flowers.

After Squeaky was all clean, Milo wrapped him in a corner of a soft, fluffy towel. "There you go!" said Milo proudly. "Now you're clean and dry!"

Squeaky looked at himself in the shiny faucet. His gray fur was sparkling clean, and his whiskers were neat and tidy. "Oh, Milo! Thank you so much! You're the kindest friend ever!"

"I'm happy to help," said Milo. "Everyone deserves to feel clean and comfortable."

Just then, Squeaky's family appeared at the laundry room door. "Squeaky!" they called. "We've been looking for you!"

"Milo helped me when I was stuck and dirty," explained Squeaky. "He was so kind and respectful!"

Squeaky's family thanked Milo with warm smiles. "You're a true friend," said Squeaky's mama.

As Milo watched Squeaky reunite with his family, he felt warm and happy inside. He had learned that helping others, even in small ways, made everyone feel better.

From that day on, whenever Milo heard someone who needed help, he always remembered: being kind, respectful, and helpful makes the world a better place for everyone.

The End.
