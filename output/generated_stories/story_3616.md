# Rainbow Art on the Balcony

Bunny Pip was a small, determined rabbit with big ears and bright eyes. Pip lived on a cozy balcony garden with pots of mint, tomatoes, and tiny flowers. The sun felt warm on Pip’s fur. The air smelled like sweet basil. Bees hummed softly, and a little wind made the leaves whisper, shhh, shhh.

One morning, Pip bounced to the railing and looked at the gray wall. “Hmm,” Pip said. “This wall looks sleepy. I want to make art! I want to make a happy rainbow picture for everyone.”

Pip took a deep breath. “I will plan,” Pip said out loud. “Good art needs a good plan.”

Pip got a small notebook and a stubby pencil. Pip drew a simple plan: a big rainbow with smiling flowers and a helpful sun. Pip made a little checklist with boxes.

“Step one: collect safe things to use,” Pip read.
“Step two: sort and clean.”
“Step three: make the art together.”
“Step four: share and tell the truth.”
“Step five: tidy up.”

Pip hopped around the balcony. Under a chair, Pip found a clean jar lid. Near the watering can, Pip found a short piece of blue ribbon. Next to the plant pots, Pip found a cardboard box, a paper cup, and a shiny button that had been lost.

Pip sniffed each thing. “Smells fine!” Pip said. “But I must ask and be honest.”

Pip knocked on the sliding door to the home inside. “May I use old boxes and safe bits for art?” Pip asked the grown-up inside.

“Yes,” said the kind voice. “Use only clean and safe things. No sharp things. Thank you for asking.”

Pip felt proud. “I told the truth,” Pip whispered. Check! The first box on the plan was done.

Pip then called to friends in nearby balconies. “Hello! Do you have safe recycling for art? We can make a balcony rainbow!”

A turtle named Tori waved. “I have clean bottle caps!”
A bird named Bibi chirped, “I have colored paper scraps!”
A mouse named Momo squeaked, “I have a soft yarn ball!”
A ladybug named Lala zipped over. “I can bring tiny, tiny leaves that fell.”

“Everyone is welcome,” Pip said. “Big, small, slow, or fast. We will make art together.”

They all met on Pip’s balcony garden. The sun made their noses warm. The mint leaves smelled fresh. They spread a cloth on the floor. “Step two: sort and clean,” Pip read.

They sorted the bottle caps by color. Red, blue, green, yellow! They wiped the jar lids. They flattened the cardboard. Pip dipped a cloth in soapy water and washed the paper cup. “Squeaky clean!” Pip laughed. The bubbles popped—pop, pop, pop!

“Step three: make the art,” Pip read.

They laid the cardboard on the gray wall like a big canvas. Pip used soft glue that smelled like vanilla cookies. “We will make a rainbow,” Pip said. “Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple.”

Bibi placed red paper scraps in a curve. “Red!”
Tori set orange caps next to the red. “Orange!”
Momo rolled yellow yarn and pressed it down. “Yellow!”
Pip added green leaves from Lala. “Green!”
Lala dotted blue with tiny blue beads they found in the craft box. “Blue!”
Together, they lined up purple ribbon pieces. “Purple!”

They added smiling flowers from bottle caps and lids. They used the shiny button for the sun. The sun looked kind and warm. Pip made sure each friend had a job. “We include everyone,” Pip said. “We use all hands, paws, and wings.”

Then Pip saw a bright gold sticker on the table. It was new, not from the recycling bin. Pip liked it a lot. “This will look so pretty,” Pip thought. Pip reached out.

Pip stopped. “I did not ask about the sticker,” Pip whispered. Pip’s ears drooped. Pip wanted the art to shine. But Pip also wanted to be honest.

Pip turned to the grown-up at the door. “May I use this new gold sticker?” Pip asked. “I found it in the craft box. I did not ask before.”

“Thank you for telling the truth,” said the grown-up. “New stickers are for special days. But today is special too! You may use one golden sticker if you promise to plan first next time.”

“I promise,” Pip said. Pip felt light and happy. Pip placed the gold sticker in the middle of the sun. It sparkled like a warm giggle.

They stepped back. The rainbow art glowed on the gray wall. The balcony smelled like mint and glue. The wind tickled their faces. Everyone smiled.

“Step four: share and tell the truth,” Pip read. Pip called to neighbors. “We made this with clean, safe recycling. We planned. We worked together. We asked when we were not sure.”

The neighbors clapped. The bees hummed along.

“Step five: tidy up!” Pip sang. They put scraps in the right bins. They washed hands. They folded the cloth. The balcony looked neat and happy.

Tori said, “I learned to plan before we start.”
Bibi chirped, “I learned that old things can make new art!”
Momo squeaked, “I learned to be honest and ask.”
Lala buzzed, “I learned that everyone’s ideas matter.”

Pip’s heart felt big. “I am a determined rabbit,” Pip said, “and art can make our world bright. We can plan, we can recycle, we can tell the truth, and we can include everyone.”

They all looked at the rainbow sun. It smiled back.

The End.
