# Rocky Raccoon Says “I’m Sorry”

In a bright musical practice room, a musical raccoon named Rocky tapped his striped tail on the floor. Tap, tap, tap! The room smelled like polished wood and shiny brass. Sunlight made tiny dust sparkles dance in the air. Stands held music papers, and drums, a xylophone, and a big piano waited quietly.

Rocky loved music. He wore a tiny red scarf and kept little drumsticks in his pocket. “Today is dance day!” Rocky chirped. His voice sounded like a happy whistle. He wiggled his paws and twirled. “I will make big brave dance moves!”

His friends were there too. Bella the bunny tuned a small violin. Ping the penguin stretched his wings. Tiko the turtle tapped a triangle, ding! ding! Their teacher, Miss Melody, a gentle robin, fluffed her feathers. “Let’s practice our music and dance together,” she sang. “We will listen, we will try, and we will be kind.”

Rocky bounced with excitement. His heart thumped like a drum. “I can do a super spin!” he said. He ran to the middle, faster and faster. His tail swished, swoosh! He did a big spin, whoosh!

“Oh!” Bella gasped.

Crash! Rocky’s paw bumped Bella’s violin. It slid off the chair and clattered on the floor. The sound was sharp and scary. Bella’s ears drooped, and her little nose trembled.

Rocky froze. His tummy felt twisty and hot, like a drum too tight. He looked at his paws. He wanted to hide. He wanted to pretend it didn’t happen. “I didn’t mean to!” he blurted. His voice felt small.

Miss Melody tilted her head and spoke softly. “Accidents can happen,” she said. “What helps when we make a mistake?”

Rocky stared at Bella. He saw her eyes were shiny. He felt a prickly worry in his chest. His brave dance felt far away. He wanted to be brave in a new way now. He took a slow breath. In through his nose. Out through his mouth. In. Out. His tail stopped shaking.

He stepped closer to Bella. “Bella,” Rocky said, “I am sorry I bumped your violin.” He spoke clearly and gently. “I hurt your feelings, and I might have hurt your violin. I didn’t look where I was spinning.”

Bella hugged her violin and checked it. She plucked a string. Twang! It still worked, but a string was loose. She sniffled. “I was scared,” she whispered. “The crash was loud.”

“I understand,” said Rocky. “The noise scared me too.” He put his paws on his chest. “I will fix my mistake. Can I help you? Can I hold the music while you fix the string?”

Miss Melody smiled. “That was a brave apology, Rocky. You used honest words. You looked at your friend. You said what happened. You asked to help.”

Rocky nodded. “I will move slowly now,” he said. He picked up Bella’s music with careful paws. The paper felt soft and a little crinkly. He held it steady while Bella tightened the string. Ping stood close and hummed a low, warm note. Tiko tapped the triangle very softly, ting… ting… like tiny stars.

Soon the violin was ready. Bella gave it a gentle stroke. The bow made a sweet sound, like a kitten purring. “Thank you, Rocky,” she said. “Your help was kind. I feel better.”

Rocky’s chest felt light. “I feel better too,” he said. “Saying I’m sorry was scary, but I did it.”

Miss Melody clapped her wings. “Now, let’s try our music and dance again. This time, we make a plan. We keep our space safe.”

She placed colorful tape lines on the floor. The tape smelled a little like lemons. “Everyone dance in your square,” she said. “Look with your eyes. Move with care.”

Rocky stood in his square. He wiggled his paws. “I will be a careful dancer,” he said. “I can be brave and gentle.”

The music began. Bella’s violin sang a bright tune. Ping beat a soft drum, bump-bump. Tiko chimed the triangle, ding! Rocky took a deep breath. He stepped, step, step. He swayed, swish, swish. He spun in a small circle, whoosh—but he kept his tail inside the tape line. He watched his friends. He smiled.

“Great job, Rocky!” called Bella. “Your dance looks happy.”

Rocky grinned. “Thank you! Your violin sounds like sunshine.”

They kept playing. The room filled with warm sounds and happy feet. The floor felt smooth under Rocky’s paws. The air smelled sweet and safe. His heart beat with the music, brave and calm.

When the song ended, they all bowed. Miss Melody said, “Today we learned something important. When we make a mistake, we can be brave. We can say, ‘I’m sorry.’ We can ask, ‘How can I help?’ We can fix what we can and try again.”

Rocky raised his paw. “I learned to look with my eyes when I dance. I learned that saying sorry makes hearts feel better.”

Bella nodded. “And I learned that friends help friends.”

They laughed and did one last gentle dance. Step, sway, smile.

The End.
