# Jump, Joey, Jump!

In a playful living room full of colorful pillows and soft rugs, a joyful kangaroo joey named Kiki bounced with a happy thump-thump. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. The air smelled like fresh apples and cinnamon toast. Kiki wiggled her little paws and giggled. “Today, I learn a new skill!” she said. “I want to jump and land softly on the big blue pillow.”

Her best friends—Milo the mouse and Tessa the turtle—peeked out from a blanket fort. Milo’s whiskers twitched. Tessa’s tiny feet shuffled. “We can help!” squeaked Milo. “We are great helpers,” said Tessa gently.

Kiki looked at the big blue pillow. It was fluffy and wide like a small cloud. “I can do it,” she whispered. She bent her knees. She bounced. Oh! She landed with a wobble and tumbled onto the rug. Thump-flop!

Kiki blinked. Her ears drooped a little. “That was hard,” she said softly.

Milo scampered over and patted Kiki’s paw. “It’s okay. We will try again,” he said. Tessa nodded. “Learning takes practice,” she said. “We can build your jumping skill.”

Kiki took a deep breath. She smelled the sweet apple slices on the coffee table. Her tummy gave a tiny rumble. “I feel a little hungry,” she said.

Tessa smiled. “Food helps our bodies learn. Let’s make a snack.” She pointed to the table. “We can cooperate. Teamwork!”

Together, they washed their paws and hands. The water ran warm and soft. Splish, splash! Kiki dried her paws on a fluffy towel. Milo washed the red apple. Tessa peeled a small banana, slow and careful. Kiki broke crunchy crackers into little squares.

“Snack time!” sang Milo. He placed the apple slices on a plate. Tessa added banana coins. Kiki set the crackers around them like a sunny circle.

They tasted their snack. Crunch! Sweet! Creamy! “Mmm,” said Kiki. “Apples are juicy. Bananas are soft. Crackers are crunchy.” Her ears perked up again. “My body feels ready.”

Milo drew a tiny plan on a piece of paper with a purple crayon. “Step one: Bend knees. Step two: Arms out like wings. Step three: Soft landing on blue pillow.” He showed the paper and smiled. “We can practice each step.”

Tessa placed small pillows in a row like tiny islands. “You can jump from one island to the next,” she said. “Small jumps first.”

Kiki nodded. “I will try.” She stood on the rug. The room smelled like clean soap and fruit. Sunbeams warmed her fur. She bent her knees. She held her arms out. She made a small hop to the first pillow. Boop! She landed. “I did it!” she cheered.

“Great job!” said Milo. “You are building your jumping skill.”

Kiki tried again. Hop to the second pillow. Hop to the third. Each time, she landed a little softer. Her paws learned what to do. Her legs felt strong. She smiled with bright eyes.

Now it was time for the big blue pillow. Kiki felt a flutter in her tummy. “I’m nervous,” she whispered.

Tessa came close and spoke in a calm voice. “It’s okay to feel nervous,” she said. “I feel slow sometimes, and friends help me too.” Milo nodded. “We believe in you.”

Kiki took a deep breath. In… and out. “Thank you,” she said. “Your feelings matter to me, too. I want us all to feel brave.” She looked at her friends with warm eyes. “When I land, we all cheer.”

She bent her knees. She held her arms out. She looked at the big blue pillow. “Step one… step two…” she said softly. She hopped a little. Then a little more. Then—jump!

Thump—fwump! Kiki landed right in the middle of the big blue pillow. It whooshed a gentle puff of air and hugged her paws. She wiggled to balance. She stayed steady. She did it!

Milo squeaked, “Hooray!” Tessa clapped her tiny feet. Kiki bounced once more and giggled. “I learned my skill!” she said. “I practiced steps. I ate a healthy snack. I had help from my friends.”

They all did a happy wiggle dance on the soft rug. The room sounded like laughter and gentle thumps. Kiki hopped down and gave Milo and Tessa a cozy hug. “Thank you for helping,” she said.

Milo smiled. “That’s cooperation.” Tessa added, “And empathy. We cared about your feelings.” Kiki nodded. “And nutrition helped my body and brain.”

Kiki looked at the pillow islands. “Let’s make a jumping game we can all play,” she said. She moved the small pillows closer so Tessa could step, and she drew tiny arrows for Milo. “We can take turns.”

They played together. Kiki jumped. Milo zipped. Tessa stepped and smiled. When Tessa moved slowly, Kiki waited and clapped. When Milo wobbled, Kiki offered her paw. Everyone felt safe and proud.

As the sun faded to a soft golden glow, Kiki whispered, “Today I learned: practice builds skills, healthy food gives energy, and friends help each other.” She touched her heart. “I will use my new skill kindly, and I will help you with your skills too.”

They curled up on the big blue pillow, warm and happy. The living room felt like a friendly hug.

The End.
