# Sunny’s Song in the Treehouse

Sunny was a small, yellow singing bird with bright eyes and a soft, warm voice. Sunny lived near a cozy treehouse with a little round window, a rope ladder, and a wind chime that tinkled like tiny bells. The treehouse smelled like pine needles and fresh leaves. When the breeze blew, the treehouse creaked gently, like it was humming along.

One morning, Sunny fluttered to the treehouse and perched on the window sill. “Tweet-a-lee! Good morning!” sang Sunny. Today was a big day. Sunny wanted to sing a special song for all the forest friends. The song was called the Feelings Song. It had soft parts, loud parts, and a happy ending. Sunny felt excited. Sunny also felt a little nervous.

“I want it to be perfect,” Sunny whispered, holding a tiny feather to the chest. The wind chime tinkled. The rope ladder swayed. The sun made warm dots on the wooden floor.

Sunny practiced the song. “When I feel happy, I smile and sing, la-la-la!” The voice sounded sweet. But when Sunny sang the next line—“When I feel sad, I slow down and breathe”—the words got stuck. Sunny’s wings drooped. The heart felt tight, like a little knot.

“Oh no,” Sunny said. “What if I forget the words? What if my voice shakes?” The belly felt wiggly. The cheeks felt hot. Sunny flapped in a small circle. “I am feeling worried.”

Just then, two friends climbed the rope ladder. Tippy, a tiny chipmunk, came first with a small pouch of acorns. Bree, a blue butterfly, floated behind, her wings shimmering in the sunlight.

“Hi, Sunny!” chirped Tippy. “We heard your practice. It sounded lovely!”

Bree landed on a beam and said softly, “Your voice is like a warm blanket.”

Sunny’s eyes brightened, but the worry stayed. “Thank you. I am happy to see you. But I am also nervous. My tummy is flip-flopping. I keep forgetting the words to my Feelings Song.”

Tippy put a paw on Sunny’s wing. “It sounds like you are worried,” Tippy said, naming the feeling. “I feel worried sometimes too.”

Bree nodded. “I feel fluttery when I try something new. It helps when I breathe slow.”

Sunny took a deep breath in, feeling the cool air in the chest, and a slow breath out, feeling the shoulders relax. “That feels better,” Sunny said. “I can name my feeling. I am worried. Now I can help it.”

Tippy clapped tiny paws. “Let’s prepare together! I brought acorns for a snack. And I have a tiny pencil. We can write the words.”

Bree twirled in the air. “We can make a plan. First, we breathe. Second, we practice. Third, we sing together!”

Sunny felt a small smile grow. “Cooperation makes me feel safe,” Sunny said. The three friends sat on the warm wooden floor. It smelled like sunshine and sap.

Bree spoke gently. “Sunny, what is the first verse?”

Sunny hummed and sang softly, “When I feel happy, I smile and sing, la-la-la.”

Tippy wrote the words on a little leaf with a tiny charcoal tip. “Happy—smile—sing,” Tippy said, tapping each word. “What is the next verse?”

Sunny breathed in. “When I feel sad, I slow down and breathe.” Sunny paused. “I can remember with a picture. Sad is like a small rain cloud. Slow down. Breathe.” Sunny touched the chest and breathed again. The knot felt looser.

Bree’s wings brushed Sunny’s feathers. “I like that. Let’s add hand—or wing—motions. For happy, we clap. For sad, we hug ourselves.”

Sunny clapped little wings. Tippy hugged his cheeks. They all giggled. The wind chime tinkled, like it was laughing too.

They practiced more verses. “When I feel mad, I stomp my feet—then count to three.” Tippy stomped softly: one, two, three. “When I feel scared, I look around—see what’s true.” Bree looked left, right, up, and down. “I am safe,” Bree whispered.

They added motions and pictures. They put the leaf-notes in a tiny row by the window. They took sips of cool water. They ate crunchy acorns. Each step felt calm and kind.

Soon, other forest friends gathered under the treehouse: a small rabbit, a shy turtle, and a giggly squirrel. Sunny peered out the window. The tummy fluttered again. “I feel a little nervous,” Sunny said.

Bree smiled. “Name it, breathe it, plan it.”

Together, they took a deep breath in. The pine smell filled their noses. They breathed out, slowly, slowly.

Sunny hopped to the window and sang, voice clear and warm:

“When I feel happy, I smile and sing, la-la-la!
When I feel sad, I slow down and breathe, ahh-ahh-ahh.
When I feel mad, I stomp and count—one, two, three.
When I feel scared, I look around—safe as can be.”

The friends clapped. The rabbit bounced. The turtle nodded. The squirrel squeaked, “Again!”

Sunny’s eyes sparkled. “I did it! I felt worried, but I used my plan. I named my feeling, I breathed, I prepared, and I asked for help. Cooperation helped my courage grow.”

Tippy grinned. “And your song helped us, too!”

Bree fluttered in a happy circle. “Feelings are visitors. We can notice them, and we can care for them.”

Sunny looked at the leaf-notes, the wind chime, and the smiling friends. The treehouse felt safe and bright. “Thank you for helping me prepare,” Sunny said. “When we work together, hard things feel softer.”

The friends sang the Feelings Song once more, softer this time, like a hug you can hear. The sun slid through the leaves, warm on their faces. The wind chime whispered along.

Sunny felt calm and proud. “Today I learned to listen to my feelings, to ask for help, and to make a plan,” Sunny said. “That is how we grow brave.”

The End.
