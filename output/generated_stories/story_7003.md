# Pebble the Penguin and the Treehouse Try

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a happy wiggle. One sunny morning, Pebble waddled to a cozy treehouse. It smelled like pine needles and warm wood. The leaves whispered, “Shhh,” and a little rope ladder hung down like a smile.

“I want to climb!” Pebble chirped. “I can do it!”

Pebble grabbed the first rung. It felt rough and scratchy. Pebble’s flippers shook a little. “Flap, flap,” went Pebble’s wings. Pebble pulled up one step. The rope creaked. The tree leaves tickled Pebble’s cheeks.

Halfway up, Pebble slipped. “Oh!” Pebble squeaked, sliding back to the grass with a gentle thump.

Pebble’s tummy felt twisty. Pebble’s eyes got watery. “That was hard,” Pebble whispered.

Just then, Squirrel peeked from a branch. “Hi, Pebble! Do you need help?”

Pebble took a deep breath. The air smelled like sweet sap. “Thank you, Squirrel,” Pebble said kindly. “I want to try by myself first.”

Squirrel nodded. “Okay! I’ll watch and cheer. You can do it!”

Pebble wiped the watery eyes and patted the brave tummy. “I can try again. I am strong. I am learning.” Pebble said the words slowly, like little stepping stones.

Pebble put one foot on the first rung. “Step.” Pebble grabbed the next one. “Grip.” The rope felt better now. Pebble’s flippers squeezed tight. “I will go slow,” Pebble told the ladder. “Slow is okay.”

Leaf shadows danced on Pebble’s back. A breeze kissed Pebble’s face. “You’re doing great!” called Squirrel.

Pebble reached the middle again. The ladder wiggled. Pebble stopped and took three big penguin breaths. “In… out… in… out… in… out.” Pebble’s heart slowed. Pebble smiled. “I can keep going.”

Then Bunny hopped over with soft thumps. “Hi, Pebble! I can pull you up!”

Pebble shook a little, then stood taller. “Thank you, Bunny. That is kind,” said Pebble. “I want to do this by myself. If I need help, I will ask.”

“Okay!” Bunny said. “We will cheer quietly.” Bunny and Squirrel waved tiny paws.

Pebble climbed another rung. The rope rubbed Pebble’s flippers. “Ouch,” Pebble said softly. Pebble looked down. The grass smelled fresh and safe. Pebble looked up. The wooden floor of the treehouse was so close! Pebble felt a flutter in the chest. “I can try one more step,” Pebble said. “Just one more.”

Pebble lifted a foot. “Step.” Pebble hugged the rope. “Squeeze.” Pebble pushed up. “Push.” Pebble reached the top and peeped over the edge.

“I did it!” Pebble laughed. The laugh sounded like little bells.

Squirrel and Bunny clapped and cheered. “You did it! You kept going!”

Pebble wiggled onto the treehouse floor. It felt warm and smooth. Inside, there was a tiny window. The sky looked big and blue. Pebble looked at the world from up high and felt calm and proud.

Bunny called up, “Can we come up too?”

Pebble smiled. “Yes! Now I am ready to help.”

Pebble lowered a little basket with a soft rope. “One at a time,” said Pebble. “Please wait your turn. That is my rule.”

Squirrel nodded. “We will follow your rule.” Squirrel climbed, and Pebble held the rope steady. Bunny climbed next, and Pebble kept the space safe. “Thank you,” Bunny said. “I feel safe when you use your rules.”

Inside the treehouse, the friends sat on a small rug. They shared a snack of crunchy apples. “Crunch, crunch,” they went. The apples smelled sweet and juicy. Pebble poured water from a tiny cup. “Sip, sip.”

Squirrel asked, “Pebble, how did you keep going when it was hard?”

Pebble thought and tapped the beak gently. “I took slow breaths. I tried again. I used kind words to myself. I said, ‘I can try one more step.’ I did not give up.”

Bunny said, “You were brave and kind. You said yes to help when you were ready, and you said no when you needed to try alone.”

Pebble nodded. “That’s my boundary. I can choose. I can say yes or no. I can ask for help, and I can try by myself. I can be kind to me.”

They looked out the window. Leaves rustled like a happy song. The sun made warm stripes on the floor. Pebble’s heart felt big and strong.

Pebble stood up and said, “Today I learned something. When a task is hard, I can be patient. I can use slow breaths. I can try again. Step by step, I can do it. That is resilience.”

Squirrel and Bunny smiled. “We learned that too!”

Pebble hugged both friends. The hug was cozy and soft. “Thank you for cheering and following my rules,” Pebble said. “Friends are kind, and friends listen.”

Squirrel and Bunny hugged back. “And you listened to yourself,” said Bunny. “That helps you feel safe and brave.”

They played a gentle game with blocks, building a tiny tower. When it wobbled, Pebble said, “Let’s try again.” They fixed it, one block at a time.

As the sun began to yawn, Pebble whispered, “I feel proud.” The treehouse smelled like happy wood and apples. Pebble looked at the ladder and smiled. “I can do hard things.”

The End.
