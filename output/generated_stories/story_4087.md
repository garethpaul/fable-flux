# Nutkin's Big Decision

Nutkin the squirrel peeked through the window of the cozy dining room. His fluffy gray tail twitched with excitement as he spotted something wonderful - a big bowl of mixed nuts sitting right on the wooden table!

"Oh my!" squeaked Nutkin, pressing his tiny paws against the cool glass. "Those nuts look so yummy and crunchy!"

But as Nutkin scampered through the pet door, he discovered he wasn't alone. Three other hungry animals were already in the dining room: Bella the bunny, Max the mouse, and Oliver the owl.

"I saw those nuts first!" chirped Max, standing on his tiny hind legs.

"But I'm the hungriest!" said Bella, thumping her soft foot on the carpet.

"Whooo says we can't share?" hooted Oliver wisely from his perch on a chair.

Nutkin's whiskers twitched as he thought carefully. He could smell the delicious, nutty scent filling the warm air. His tummy rumbled loudly - *grumble, grumble!*

"Wait, friends," said Nutkin, climbing onto the table with quick, bouncy movements. "We all want the nuts, but fighting won't help anyone. Let me think of a good solution."

Nutkin sat very still, his bushy tail curled around him like a cozy blanket. He looked at each of his friends with his bright, dark eyes.

"I have an idea!" Nutkin announced, jumping up and down excitedly. "What if we work together instead of arguing?"

"How?" asked Bella, tilting her head curiously.

Nutkin pointed to the different nuts with his tiny paw. "Look! There are walnuts, almonds, pecans, and acorns. We each like different kinds best!"

Max's whiskers twitched with interest. "That's true! I love the tiny pieces."

"And I prefer the sweet almonds," added Bella.

"Whooo wouldn't want some crunchy pecans?" hooted Oliver.

Nutkin nodded enthusiastically. "Exactly! And I love acorns most of all. But here's my idea - let's sort them together, then we can each have our favorites AND try some of the others too!"

The friends looked at each other, then smiled. This sounded much better than fighting!

"You're so smart, Nutkin!" said Max, scurrying up to help.

Working as a team, they carefully moved around the table. Nutkin used his strong paws to crack open the harder shells. Bella hopped back and forth, sorting the nuts into neat piles. Max squeezed into small spaces to reach scattered pieces. Oliver used his sharp eyes to spot any nuts they missed.

"This is fun!" giggled Bella as she bounced from pile to pile.

"And much faster than doing it alone," squeaked Max happily.

Soon, four perfect piles of nuts sat on the smooth table. The dining room filled with the sounds of happy crunching and munching.

"Thank you for helping us make a good choice, Nutkin," hooted Oliver. "You showed us that thinking before acting is very important."

Nutkin's chest puffed up with pride. "When we have a problem, it's always better to stop, think, and find a solution that makes everyone happy!"

As the sun streamed through the dining room window, the four friends sat together, sharing their nuts and telling stories. They learned that different friends could like different things, and that was perfectly okay.

"From now on, let's always try to solve problems together," said Bella.

"That's the best decision of all!" agreed Nutkin, his tail swishing contentedly.

The End.
