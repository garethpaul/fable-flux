# The Rabbit Who Said, "I Can Speak Up!"

In a warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon and fresh bread, a determined rabbit named Ruby stood on a soft rug. The sunlight made little squares on the floor. The kettle hummed. The clock went tick-tock. Ruby’s ears perked up.

“Today, I will help,” Ruby said. “I can do it. I can speak up.”

On the big table sat a mixing bowl, a wooden spoon, and a bag of flour. Ruby’s friends were there too. Gus the goose flapped his wings. Mina the mouse stood on a spoon like a tiny captain. Benny the bear wore a small apron that said, “Helper.”

“We are making carrot muffins,” said Benny, licking a paw. “I love muffins!”

Ruby hopped closer. “I know a special family recipe,” she said. “It has cinnamon, carrots, and a dash of orange zest.”

Gus honked, “I can stir fastest!”

Mina squeaked, “I can measure so well!”

Benny rumbled, “I can lift the heavy bowl!”

Ruby looked at her paws. “I can speak up for my idea,” she whispered to herself. She took a breath, like a warm sip of tea.

But then—thump! The flour bag tipped. A white puff filled the air like soft snow. “Ah-choo!” Mina sneezed. The counter turned snowy and sweet. Everyone started talking at once.

“I will stir now!” honked Gus.

“I will measure more!” squeaked Mina.

“I will lift the bowl!” rumbled Benny.

Ruby’s whisk whisked in her paws, but no one looked at her. Her whisk made a tiny tap-tap on the bowl. Tap. Tap. She felt small.

Ruby’s heart made a gentle drum beat. She remembered what Mama Rabbit taught her: “When you have something important to say, stand tall, use kind words, and say it clearly.”

Ruby straightened her ears. She hopped onto a small stool. She put her paw on her chest. “Friends,” she said in a calm, brave voice, “I have an idea, and I would like to share it.”

The kitchen went quiet. The kettle sighed. The sunlight made the butter shine.

Gus lowered his wings. Mina held her spoon still. Benny set down the big bowl. “We are listening, Ruby,” said Benny.

Ruby smiled. “Thank you. It is fair when everyone gets a turn. I have a family recipe. It needs cinnamon, carrots, and a tiny bit of orange zest. I can show you. We can make jobs for each of us.”

Gus tilted his head. “Jobs?”

“Yes,” Ruby said. “Gus, you can whisk gently. Mina, you can measure with your little spoon so it is just right. Benny, you can lift and pour. I will guide us and check the steps.”

Mina’s eyes sparkled. “That sounds fair,” she squeaked.

Gus nodded. “I can whisk softly,” he honked.

Benny smiled. “You are the leader, Ruby,” he rumbled. “Tell us what to do.”

Ruby’s whisk went swoosh-swoosh in the bowl. “First, two cups of flour,” she said. “Mina?”

Mina scooped. “Scoop, level, pour!” she sang. The flour fell like gentle snow into the bowl.

“Next, a pinch of cinnamon,” said Ruby. “It smells warm and cozy.”

Gus fluttered his wings. “I smell it,” he said. “It smells like hugs.”

“Now the carrots,” said Ruby. She grated them carefully. The carrots made bright orange curls. “They look like tiny sunshine ribbons.”

Benny held the bowl steady. “I’ve got it,” he said. “Strong and careful.”

Ruby took an orange and rubbed the peel. “This is zest,” she said. “It makes a little zing!” The kitchen filled with a fresh, happy smell.

They mixed together. Swoosh, tap, scoop, whisk. The batter looked smooth and soft like a cloud. Ruby peeked into the oven as Benny opened it with a gentle paw. A warm whoosh of air touched their noses like a friendly hug.

As the muffins baked, Ruby’s tummy rumbled. “I feel proud,” she said. “I used my voice. I asked to share. That was brave.”

Mina patted Ruby’s paw. “You spoke up kindly,” she said.

Gus honked happily. “You made it fair. Everyone had a turn.”

Benny nodded. “That is justice,” he said. “Fair turns, fair chances.”

Ruby’s eyes grew wide. “Justice is when things are fair,” she said. “I like that.”

Ding! The oven sang. Benny opened the door. The muffins were golden and round. The kitchen smelled sweet and bright. Steam curled up like tiny stories.

Ruby placed a muffin on a plate. “Let’s use our imagination,” she giggled. “These are magic muffins. One bite makes you brave, kind, and fair.”

They took a bite. The tops were soft, the edges a little crisp, the inside warm and gentle. “Mmm,” said everyone together.

Gus flapped once. “I feel brave!”

Mina squeaked, “I feel kind!”

Benny rumbled, “I feel fair!”

Ruby laughed. “We used teamwork and skills. We listened. We shared. We spoke up. That is real magic.”

They cleaned the kitchen together. Wipe, wash, dry. The counters shone again. The kettle hummed a happy tune.

Ruby hopped off the stool. Her heart beat steady and glad. “Today I learned,” she said, “that I can speak up with kind words. I can ask for a turn. I can make space for others too. That is self-advocacy. That is justice. That is us.”

Her friends cheered. “Hooray for Ruby!”

The warm kitchen glowed. The clock said tick-tock, like claps. The muffins cooled, waiting for another happy snack.

The End.
