# Rusty's Clean Workshop Adventure

Rusty was a little fox kit with bright orange fur and a fluffy tail. He loved exploring new places with his big, curious eyes. One sunny morning, he discovered Grandpa Fox's garage workshop behind their cozy den.

"Wow!" Rusty whispered, peeking inside. The workshop smelled like wood shavings and paint. Shiny tools hung on the walls, and colorful bottles lined the shelves. Everything looked so exciting!

Grandpa Fox was wearing special goggles and gloves while fixing a wooden birdhouse. "Good morning, Rusty!" he called out cheerfully. "Would you like to help me today?"

Rusty's tail wiggled with excitement. "Yes, yes, yes! Can I touch everything?"

"Hold on, little one," Grandpa Fox said gently. "Before we start, we need to follow some important rules to stay safe and clean."

Rusty tilted his head. "Rules?"

"First, we must wash our paws with soap and warm water," Grandpa Fox explained, walking to a small sink in the corner. "The workshop has dust and chemicals that could make us sick if we don't clean up properly."

The warm, soapy water felt bubbly and tickly on Rusty's paws. "This feels funny!" he giggled, scrubbing between his tiny claws just like Grandpa Fox showed him.

"Excellent! Now we put on these special gloves to protect our paws," Grandpa Fox said, helping Rusty with small, colorful gloves. "And these safety goggles protect our eyes from flying wood chips."

Rusty looked at himself in the workshop mirror. "I look like a real builder!"

As they worked together, Rusty noticed his gloves getting dusty and dirty. "Ew, Grandpa! My gloves are yucky!"

"That's exactly why we wear them," Grandpa Fox smiled. "They're keeping the dirt off our clean paws underneath. When we're done, we'll throw these gloves away and wash our paws again."

Halfway through their project, Rusty felt thirsty. He started to put his gloved paw near his mouth, but then stopped. "Wait! Grandpa said not to touch my face with dirty gloves," he remembered.

"I'm thirsty, Grandpa, but my gloves are dirty," Rusty said bravely.

"What a smart fox kit!" Grandpa Fox praised. "Let's take off your gloves safely, wash your paws, have a drink, then put on fresh gloves."

At the sink, Rusty carefully washed his paws again, singing a little song: "Scrub, scrub, scrub between my toes! Rinse, rinse, rinse, and away dirt goes!"

After their refreshing water break, they finished building the beautiful birdhouse together. When all the work was done, Grandpa Fox showed Rusty how to clean up properly.

"First, we put away all the tools," Grandpa Fox said. "Then we sweep up the wood chips. Finally, we wash our paws one more time with soap and warm water."

Rusty followed each step carefully. "Being clean feels good!" he announced, admiring his squeaky-clean paws.

"You were very brave today, Rusty," Grandpa Fox said, giving him a warm hug. "You listened to directions, asked questions when you weren't sure, and kept yourself clean and safe."

Rusty's chest puffed up with pride. "Can we build something else tomorrow, Grandpa?"

"Of course! And now you know exactly how to stay clean and safe in the workshop."

That night, Rusty told Mama Fox all about his adventure. "I learned that being clean isn't just about looking nice," he said sleepily. "It's about staying healthy and safe too!"

The End.
