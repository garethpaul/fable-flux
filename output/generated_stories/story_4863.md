# Jumping Joey and the Big World Mural

Joey was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a bouncy tail. He loved to hop, hop, hop. Today, Joey hopped into the creative art room. The room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight spilled across the tables. Paints, brushes, and colorful markers waited like little rainbows.

“Good morning!” Joey sang. He wiggled his ears. He liked to sing when he felt excited.

Ms. Lila, the art teacher, smiled. “Good morning, Joey. Today our class will paint a Big World Mural. We will show how we care for people all over the world.”

Joey clapped his tiny paws. “A mural? Wow!”

Friends arrived and put on their paint smocks. Soft music played on a small drum and a gentle shaker. Tap-tap. Shh-shh. It sounded like a friendly heartbeat.

Ms. Lila pointed to a giant sheet of paper on the wall. “Our mural will show global citizenship. That means we are kind, helpful, and safe with everyone, near and far. We share, we listen, and we take care of our world.”

Joey’s tail swished with a happy swoop. He picked up a wide blue brush. “I will paint the ocean,” he said. “The ocean hugs many lands.”

He dipped his brush. Swish, swish—the blue looked like waves. “Hello, ocean,” Joey whispered.

His friend Nia shook a rattle. “I will paint houses,” she said. “Homes for families.” Tap-tap went the drum in the background, steady and warm.

Suddenly, Joey saw a problem. The paint trays were crowded on one small table. A tall jar of water wobbled near the edge. A long cord from the music player curled across the floor where children walked.

Joey blinked. “Oh! This is not safe,” he said. “Someone might trip. The water might spill.”

His tummy felt fluttery. He wanted to help, but he did not know what to do. He looked at Ms. Lila. He took a deep breath. Slow in, slow out. He remembered: Global citizens keep each other safe.

“Ms. Lila,” Joey said softly. “May I share a safety idea?”

“Yes, Joey,” Ms. Lila said. “Thank you for speaking up.”

Joey pointed. “Can we move the cord by the wall and put the water on a tray? Then no one trips, and the paints do not spill.”

Ms. Lila nodded. “Great idea! Friends, let’s make a safe art space.” She unplugged the cord and taped it along the wall. Joey and Nia carefully carried the water jar to a big tray with a towel under it. Pat, pat. The towel felt soft and dry.

Everyone clapped. Joey felt warm inside. His tail made a tiny thump on the floor. Thump-thump. It sounded like a drum saying thank you.

They kept painting. Little hands brushed red for hearts, green for trees, and yellow for sunshine. Joey added tiny boats on the blue waves. “These boats bring books and food,” he said. “They help people.”

Nia painted a small park with a sign. “Park Clean-Up Day,” she read out loud. “We can pick up litter. That is community service.”

“I can show sharing,” said Omar. He painted two children with a big bowl of soup. One child held a spoon out. “Here you go!”

Joey added music notes floating in the sky. “Music travels everywhere,” he said. “We can sing together, even if our words are different.”

Tap-tap. Shh-shh. The drum and shaker kept the rhythm, and Joey hummed along. His hum sounded like a lullaby for the world.

Then another problem arrived. The green paint ran out. “Oh no,” Nia said. “We still need leaves.”

Joey felt a pinch of worry. But he remembered: Global citizens solve problems together. “Let’s mix colors,” he said. “Blue and yellow can make green.”

They tried it. Plop of blue. Plop of yellow. Swirl, swirl. A fresh green appeared like magic. “We did it!” everyone cheered.

Joey’s smile grew wide. But he had one more thought. “Our mural should have people from many places,” he said. “We can show different clothes, different foods, and different songs. All friends.”

The class nodded. They painted bright scarves, cozy hats, and tasty bowls of noodles and rice. They added drums, flutes, and a big guitar. Joey painted a kangaroo family waving hello to a penguin family, a panda family, and a child planting a tree.

Finally, Ms. Lila said, “Time to add our promise.” She gave Joey a marker. Joey’s paw shook just a little. He took another slow breath. Then he printed big, clear words:

“We care for everyone, near and far. We share, listen, and keep each other safe.”

The class read it together. Their voices were soft and sweet, like the shaker’s shh-shh. Joey’s eyes felt a little sparkly. He was proud.

Ms. Lila smiled. “Joey, you led with kindness. You helped keep us safe. You worked to serve the community in our art and in our room. That is global citizenship.”

Joey’s tail gave a happy hop. “We did it together,” he said. “We are one big world team.”

They took a step back and looked at the Big World Mural. It shined with colors, music notes, and smiling faces. The room smelled like clean water and paint. The drum tapped a gentle goodbye. Tap… tap…

Joey whispered, “Hello, world. We care.”

And the mural seemed to whisper back, “Thank you.”

The End.
