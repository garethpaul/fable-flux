# The Brave Bunny in the Bubbly Bathroom

Plip. Plop. The bathroom was quiet except for the tiny drip-drip of the faucet. The tiles were smooth and cool, and the air smelled like sweet soap and minty toothpaste. A little white rabbit named Rilla hopped in on soft paws. Her ears were tall. Her nose wiggled. Her heart thumped, thump-thump.

“I am a determined rabbit,” Rilla whispered to herself. “I can do hard things.”

Today was bath day. Rilla loved bubbles, but she did not like the loud whoosh when the drain went glug-glug-glug. The sound made her tummy flip. She wanted to be brave. She wanted to try washing by herself, just like a big bunny.

Mama Rabbit stood by the doorway and smiled. “I’m here if you need help,” Mama said in a warm, gentle voice. “Remember to listen to your steps.”

Rilla nodded. She listened. The bathroom hummed softly. She could hear the little fan whirr. She could smell orange shampoo. She could feel the fuzzy bath mat under her feet. She could see the bright yellow duck toy smiling at her from the tub. Quack!

“I will go slow and steady,” Rilla said. “I will be brave.”

She turned the faucet a tiny bit. Shhhhhh! Warm water began to pour. It felt like a soft rain on her paw. Steam curled like little clouds. Rilla took a small breath in. In and out. “I can do this,” she said.

She climbed in carefully. Splash! The water hugged her legs. “Ooh, cozy,” Rilla giggled. She squeezed bubble soap. Plop! White foam bloomed like puffy clouds. The bubbles tickled her whiskers. “Hello, Bubble Mountain!” she cheered.

Rilla washed her ears. Swish, swish. She washed her paws. Rub, rub. She sang a tiny song. “Scrub-a-dub, little bun, I am brave and getting it done.”

Then she looked at the drain. The drain looked back like a little shiny eye. Rilla knew that later, the water would go down with a big slurp. Her tummy wiggled. Her ears drooped a little.

Mama Rabbit spoke softly from the doorway. “What do you hear, Rilla?”

Rilla listened. She heard splish-splash. She heard the fan go whirr. She heard her own breath going slow and calm. She did not hear the scary glug yet. She was okay. “I hear water and my breathing,” Rilla said.

“That’s right,” Mama said. “You are safe. You are strong. Use your steps.”

Rilla nodded. “My steps are: wash, rinse, breathe, and then pull the plug.”

She finished washing. She poured a little water over her ears. Tinkle-tinkle. The water felt warm like a hug. She took one paw and placed it on her chest. Thump-thump. “Be brave, bunny,” she told herself. “Be patient. Go slow.”

She was almost ready to pull the plug. Her paw trembled. She remembered her plan. She would count to three. She would look at her yellow duck. She would listen to Mama’s words in her head: You are safe. You are strong.

“Do you want me to help?” Mama asked.

Rilla thought. She wanted to try by herself first. She wanted to be self-reliant. “I will try,” Rilla said. “Please stand close and listen with me.”

“I’m right here,” Mama said.

Rilla put her paw on the shiny plug. The metal felt cool and smooth. She took a deep breath. In. Out. “One,” she whispered. “Two.” Her heart beat steady. “Three!”

She pulled the plug.

WHOOSH. The water began to swirl and twirl. Glug-glug-glug. The sound was big. The water tickled her toes as it went away. Rilla held her duck and looked at Mama. Her ears trembled for just a moment.

She kept breathing. In. Out. She listened to the sound. It was only water going home. It was not a monster. It was not a scary thing. It was just the drain doing its job.

“I’m okay,” Rilla said, surprised and proud. “I did it!”

The last bubbles made tiny pops like little kisses. Pop, pop, pop. The tub was almost empty. Rilla giggled. “The drain says glug because it’s thirsty!”

Mama clapped softly. “You were so brave, Rilla. You listened to your body. You were patient. You tried by yourself, and you asked me to be near. That is courage.”

Rilla stepped out onto the warm towel Mama held open. The towel was fluffy and smelled like sunshine. Mama wrapped her tight. Snug as a bun in a blanket! Rilla’s fur felt warm and clean. Her heart felt light and proud.

“I used my steps,” Rilla said. “Wash, rinse, breathe, and pull the plug. I was scared, but I tried anyway.”

“That’s what courage is,” Mama said. “Being afraid, listening, and doing the right thing safely.”

Rilla stood tall on the bath mat. She brushed her teeth with minty paste. Swish, swish. She combed her ears smooth and neat. She looked in the mirror and smiled at the brave bunny she saw.

“I can do hard things,” Rilla said, wiggling her nose. “I can be patient. I can listen. I can be brave and do it myself.”

Mama kissed the top of her head. “Yes, you can.”

That night, when Rilla hopped to bed, she remembered the glug-glug-glug. She remembered that she stayed calm and strong. She felt proud, like a little star shining.

If you feel scared, you can be like Rilla. You can stop and listen. You can breathe. You can go slow. You can try by yourself, and you can ask someone you love to stay near. That is courage.

The End.
