# Tilly Turtle’s Closet Plan

Tilly was a shy turtle with a soft green shell and gentle eyes. She loved quiet places. Her favorite spot was the closet hideaway. It was cozy and calm. The clothes felt like soft clouds. The air smelled like clean cotton and a little bit like lavender soap.

“Hello, little light,” Tilly whispered to the tiny nightlight plugged in near the floor. It made a warm, safe glow. Tilly liked how it lit up her small world.

Today, Tilly wanted to have an adventure. But she also wanted to feel safe. “I can plan,” she said, touching her shell with a tiny tap. “A plan helps me be brave.”

She took out a small notepad and a crayon. The crayon was purple and smelled waxy. “Step one,” Tilly said. “Make a cozy map.” She drew a simple picture: a slipper mountain, a scarf river, and a shoebox castle. She smiled. Planning made her tummy feel calm and warm.

“Step two,” she said. “Pack what I need.” She packed:
- A tiny flashlight
- A snack: two carrot sticks
- A soft ribbon for measuring
- A painted pebble friend named Dot

Dot was smooth and cool. Tilly held Dot and listened to the quiet. Far away, the house made soft creaks, like a cat purring. “It’s okay,” she told herself. “I planned.”

Tilly peeked out of her shell and began. “Adventure time,” she whispered.

First, she visited Slipper Mountain. The slippers were fuzzy and smelled like vanilla. She climbed slowly, one careful step at a time. “Left foot, right foot,” she said. At the top, she looked around. “I can see Scarf River!” The scarves hung in a wavy line, tickling her nose when she got close. “Achoo!” Tilly giggled. “That tickles!”

She checked her plan. “Step three: Use my imagination.” She closed her eyes. The closet became a big, friendly forest. The scarves were bright vines. The shoebox was a castle with tiny flags. The nightlight was a golden moon.

“Hello, Castle,” Tilly said. “May I visit?”

She stopped, then added, “I will ask first. I like to have gentle space.” Tilly was practicing personal boundaries. She made a small sign and set it by the door: “Tilly’s Space. Please ask to come in.”

Just then, she heard a soft scuttle. It was Moth, the quiet, dusty moth who sometimes fluttered near the coats. “Hello, Tilly,” Moth whispered. Her wings made a hush-hush sound.

“Hi, Moth,” said Tilly. “I am playing adventure in my closet hideaway. I made a plan. Would you like to play too?”

“Yes,” said Moth. “Can I come in your castle?”

Tilly looked at her sign. “Thank you for asking,” she said. “Yes, you can come in. Let’s keep it calm and gentle.”

They stepped into the shoebox castle. It smelled like cardboard and crayons. Tilly used the ribbon to measure the door. “It is five ribbons wide,” she said. “That fits both of us.”

Moth looked worried. “What if we get lost in the coats?”

Tilly patted Dot the pebble friend. “I planned for that! Step four: Make a path.” She tore a sheet of paper into little stars and set them on the floor. “We can follow the star path home.”

“Star path,” Moth echoed, smiling.

Together, they crossed Scarf River. The scarves swished like soft rain. “Swish, swish,” Tilly sang. They climbed down Slipper Mountain, “Left foot, right foot,” until they reached the castle again.

Suddenly, the house made a louder creak. Tilly’s heart thumped. She pulled into her shell, just a little. The closet felt bigger for a moment.

Moth waited and spoke softly. “Do you need space, Tilly?”

Tilly peeked out. “Yes, please. I need a little quiet.” She took a deep breath in and out. “I can say what I need.”

Moth nodded and sat still. After a moment, Tilly felt better. “Thank you for giving me space,” she said. “That was kind.”

Moth smiled. “You planned for feelings too.”

Tilly checked her notepad. “Step five: Snack and rest.” She shared a carrot stick with Moth. Crunch, crunch. The sound was happy and small. The sweet carrot taste made Tilly’s smile grow.

“Step six,” Tilly read, “Say when the game is done.” She looked at Moth. “I had fun, and now I am ready to rest alone. Is that okay?”

Moth nodded. “Okay. Thank you for playing. I will follow the star path out.”

“Bye, Moth,” said Tilly. “Thank you for asking before coming in. That felt safe.”

When Moth fluttered away, Tilly tucked the stars into a tiny jar. She folded her map and put Dot on top. She turned off her flashlight and watched the warm nightlight glow.

Tilly whispered, “Today I planned my adventure. I used my imagination. I asked for space when I needed it. I shared, and I rested. Planning helped me feel brave and kind.”

She rubbed her soft shell and closed her eyes. The closet hideaway felt like a hug.

The End.
