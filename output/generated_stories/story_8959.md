# Tiko the Wise Tortoise Fixes the Morning

In a sunny little town, there was a cozy garage workshop that smelled like warm wood and orange oil. The floor was smooth and cool. Tools hung in a neat row—tap, tap hammers, soft-brush paint, and shiny screws that chimed like tiny bells.

Inside lived Tiko, a wise tortoise with bright eyes and a gentle smile. Tiko moved slowly on purpose. He liked to say, “Slow is calm. Calm is strong.”

One busy morning, three friends rushed into the garage workshop. Pippa the puppy bounced, her paws pat-pat on the floor. Niko the squirrel zipped in with a swish of his fluffy tail. Mina the mouse hurried, whiskers twitching.

“Help, Tiko!” Pippa barked. “Our parade cart is broken! The parade starts soon.”

“The wheels wiggle,” Niko chattered. “The music box squeaks!”

“And the flag keeps falling,” squeaked Mina, holding a colorful flag with bright patterns from her grandmother’s country. It had stars, circles, and a sun that looked like a smile.

Tiko took a slow breath. He smelled the sweet orange oil. He felt the cool floor under his feet. “Friends,” he said, “let’s be calm. Calm helps us think.”

“But we don’t have time!” Pippa yipped, tail wagging fast-faster-fastest.

Tiko nodded. “We will save time by being calm.” He lifted his head and said, “First, we do our calm breaths. Then we use our strong bodies with gentle exercise. Then we talk with kind words and make a plan.”

Pippa, Niko, and Mina looked at each other. They nodded.

Tiko showed them. “In through the nose, out through the mouth.” He breathed in—slow, cool air. He breathed out—soft, warm air. “Smell the orange, blow the candle,” he said.

They all copied. In… out. Smell the orange… blow the candle. Their shoulders softened. Their hearts felt slower.

“Good,” Tiko said. “Now some gentle moves.” He stretched his neck left and right, slow and smooth. “This wakes up our bodies and keeps our minds calm.”

Pippa stretched her paws long-long-long. Niko rolled his tiny shoulders in quiet circles. Mina did little toe-taps—tip, tap, tip, tap.

Tiko smiled. “I hear calmer hearts. Now we talk. One at a time. Peaceful voices.”

Pippa took a turn. “The cart wobbles. It scares me.”

Niko spoke softly. “The music box squeaks and hurts my ears.”

Mina held the flag close. “I want the flag to stay. It shows my family’s songs and stories.”

Tiko nodded to each friend. “Thank you for using calm voices. Now we decide together.”

He touched the cart wheel. It wiggled and made a clink-clink. “This needs a strong hug from the wrench.” He dabbed a bit of orange oil. Twist, twist—gentle, steady. The wheel sat straight and proud.

He turned to the music box. It squeaked eee-eee. Tiko listened. “A tiny drop of oil and a soft cloth,” he said. Drip. Wipe. He turned the little crank. The music played a sweet tune—ding-ding, la-la—like wind chimes. Niko’s ears perked up. “Much better,” he whispered.

Mina held up the flag again. “It keeps slipping.”

Tiko looked closely. “This flag is special,” he said. “Tell me about it.”

Mina smiled. “The sun is for warmth. The circles are for dancing. The stars are for wishes.”

“What a beautiful story,” Tiko said. “Let’s give your flag a safe place.” He found a soft ribbon with tiny stars and a wooden clip. “We will make a careful clip so it won’t fall.”

Mina’s eyes shone. Tiko measured, clip-clip, then tied the ribbon with a gentle pull. The flag stood tall, colors bright, swish-swish.

Pippa bounced and then paused. She remembered calm. She took a slow breath. “Thank you, Tiko.”

Niko nodded. “We fixed the cart together.”

Tiko patted the cart. “We did. Because we were calm, we could think. Because we moved our bodies, we felt strong. Because we spoke kindly, we made good choices.”

He looked at the friends. “One more thing. Let’s practice our parade steps.”

They all did tiny exercises together:
- March-march, soft feet on the floor.
- Reach-reach, long arms to the sky.
- Twist-twist, gentle like tall grass.

“Ready,” said Tiko. “Now we go.”

They rolled the cart outside. The sun felt warm like a hug. The street smelled like fresh bread. The band was lining up, drums thum-thum, flutes tweet-tweet. The cart moved smooth-smooth. The music box sang clear. Mina’s flag waved high, sharing her family’s colors with everyone.

People smiled and clapped. “What a fine cart!” they cheered.

Mina waved. “This flag is from my grandmother’s home,” she said. “It tells our story.” The crowd listened and smiled. A child waved a small ribbon back. Pippa’s tail wagged in a slow, happy beat. Niko kept the crank turning gently. Tiko walked steady, calm and proud.

When the parade ended, the friends gathered in the garage workshop again. It was cool and quiet. They could still smell the orange oil and hear the soft tick-tick of the clock.

Tiko asked, “What did we learn?”

Pippa said, “Calm helps me be brave.”

Niko said, “Exercise helps my body and ears feel good.”

Mina said, “Kind words help us decide together. And sharing our flag shares love.”

Tiko nodded. “Slow is calm. Calm is strong. With calm minds, we fix problems.”

They all took one more slow breath. Smell the orange. Blow the candle. Their hearts felt light.

The End.
