# Ellie's Wonderful Wellness Morning

Little Ellie the elephant calf sat on Grandma's peaceful front porch. The wooden boards felt warm under her gray bottom. Birds chirped sweet songs in the big oak tree above.

"Good morning, sunshine!" said Grandma Elephant, bringing a tray outside. "Time for our morning wellness routine!"

Ellie's trunk drooped down. "I don't feel very well today, Grandma. My body feels sleepy and grumpy."

Grandma set down the tray with a gentle thump. It had colorful fruits, a water pitcher, and soft yoga mats. "Oh my, that sounds like your body needs some special care. Shall we help it feel better together?"

"How?" asked Ellie, perking up her big ears.

"First, let's drink some cool water," Grandma said. "Our bodies are like flowers - they need water to feel strong and happy."

Ellie used her trunk to sip the fresh water. "Mmm, it tastes like morning dew!" The cool water made her feel more awake.

"Wonderful words, Ellie! You described it beautifully," Grandma smiled. "Now, let's stretch our bodies. Can you reach your trunk up high like the oak tree?"

Ellie stretched her trunk toward the sky. "I'm as tall as the clouds!" she giggled. Her muscles felt tingly and good.

"Perfect! Now let's breathe deeply. In through your trunk... and out slowly," Grandma demonstrated.

Ellie took big, deep breaths. The morning air smelled like fresh grass and blooming flowers. "Breathing feels like hugging my insides," she said.

"What a lovely way to say it!" Grandma clapped her ears. "Your words paint beautiful pictures."

Just then, their neighbor Mrs. Rabbit hopped by looking very tired.

"Good morning, Mrs. Rabbit!" called Ellie. "Would you like to join our wellness time? We have extra fruit and yoga mats!"

Mrs. Rabbit's ears perked up. "That sounds wonderful, but I don't know how to do yoga."

"That's okay!" Ellie said with confidence. "Grandma can teach us both. Everyone deserves to feel good!"

Mrs. Rabbit hopped onto the porch. Ellie shared her orange slices and showed Mrs. Rabbit how to stretch gently.

"This feels amazing," said Mrs. Rabbit. "Thank you for being so generous, Ellie."

But then Ellie noticed something. "Mrs. Rabbit, you keep looking at the road. Are you worried about something?"

Mrs. Rabbit nodded. "I need to ask the mail carrier for help, but I feel too shy."

"You can do it!" encouraged Ellie. "Taking care of ourselves means asking for help when we need it. That's being brave and smart!"

When the mail carrier came by, Ellie stood next to Mrs. Rabbit for support. "Go ahead," Ellie whispered. "I believe in you."

Mrs. Rabbit took a deep breath and politely asked the mail carrier her question. The mail carrier was happy to help!

"Thank you, Ellie," said Mrs. Rabbit. "You taught me that wellness means taking care of my feelings too, not just my body."

As they finished their morning routine, Ellie felt proud and energized. Her body felt strong from stretching and drinking water. Her heart felt warm from helping a friend.

"Grandma, I feel wonderful now!" Ellie announced. "My whole self feels happy and healthy."

"That's the magic of wellness, dear one," Grandma said, hugging Ellie with her trunk. "When we take care of our bodies, minds, and hearts, and help others do the same, we all feel better together."

Ellie trumpeted joyfully. She had learned that wellness was like a special gift - it felt even better when you shared it with friends.

The End.
