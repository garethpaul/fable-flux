# Sunny's Calm Plan on the Balcony Garden

Sunny the squirrel lived near a small balcony garden. The garden smelled like mint and damp soil. The leaves were shiny and green. Bees hummed softly, zzz-zzz, and the wind whispered shhh through the tall herbs. Sunny loved this place. He visited every morning to see the tomato vines and the little water barrel that made a happy drip, drip.

One sunny day, Sunny found a surprise. A strong, clever crow stood on the railing. The crow had a shiny bead in his beak. He cawed loudly, Caw! Caw! The sound made Sunny’s ears twitch.

Sunny climbed up the wooden trellis. He saw a basket of nuts the gardener had put out for birds and squirrels. “Oh! Nuts!” Sunny’s tail flicked with joy.

But the crow hopped closer. With a quick flap, he knocked the basket. Nuts rolled and bounced. One fell into the water barrel. Plop!

Sunny’s happy chest turned tight and hot. His whiskers trembled. “Hey!” he shouted. “That was not kind!”

The crow puffed his feathers. “Caw! I saw first. Mine,” he said.

Sunny’s paws clenched. His heart thumped fast, thump-thump. He felt a big rush of mad, like a storm inside. He wanted to shout louder. He wanted to grab. His tail lashed. He could almost taste the salty edge of anger.

Then Sunny remembered his calm plan. He had made it on a windy day when a chipmunk took his acorn. The plan had three steps:
- Stop and still your body.
- Breathe in like smelling a flower. Breathe out like blowing a feather.
- Use gentle words and a smart idea.

Sunny placed his paws on his belly. “Stop,” he whispered. He stood very still. He breathed in, sniffing the mint. Ahhh. He breathed out, slow, like he was blowing a soft dandelion. Whoooo. He did it again. In. Out. His chest felt cooler. His tail softened into a fluffy curl.

“Okay,” Sunny told himself. “Gentle words. Smart idea.”

He looked around the balcony. He saw the water barrel with the floating nut. He saw empty clay pots, a small scoop, and a bundle of twine. He heard the bees and a far car rumble. He smelled soil and warm tomatoes.

Sunny turned to the crow. “I feel upset,” he said, soft and clear. “I want to share the nuts. We can make a plan.”

The crow tilted his head. “Caw? A plan?” His black eyes blinked.

“Yes,” said Sunny. “We can sort the nuts. Half for you. Half for me. And we can save some for later.” He pointed to a shady corner. “That is good for the garden. We don’t waste. We can plant a few, too. New trees grow from nuts.”

The crow looked at the rolling nuts and at the barrel. “Caw… but the wet one is lost.”

Sunny smiled. “I can help.” He took the twine and tied it to the scoop, making a little dipper. His paws worked fast and careful. Plop-plop went the twine against the barrel. Sunny lowered the scoop. He watched the shiny circle wiggle on the water. Scoop, lift—drip, drip. He caught the floating nut.

“Got it!” Sunny held it up. The crow leaned close. “Caw! Clever.”

Sunny nodded. “Thank you. Let’s sort.”

They sat by a blue mat. “One for you,” Sunny said, sliding a nut to the crow. “One for me.” Tap, tap went the nuts on the mat. The crow counted, “Caw-one, caw-two,” in a funny voice that made Sunny giggle.

They found two broken shells. Sunny said, “These can go into the soil. Crunch them.” He crushed them gently and sprinkled the pieces around the tomato stems. “This helps the plants. It is called compost,” he explained. He patted the soil. “Good for the Earth.”

The crow watched. “Caw. Good for garden.”

When they finished, there were neat piles. Sunny wrapped a few nuts in a leaf bundle and tucked them under a pot. “For a rainy day,” he said. “Saving is smart.”

The crow pecked the shiny bead, then placed it near the water barrel. “Gift,” he said. The bead sparkled in the sun, like a tiny star.

Sunny felt warm and calm now, like sunlight on fur. “Thank you,” he said. “I felt angry before. I stopped, I breathed, and I used gentle words. That helped me. It helped us.”

The crow nodded. “Caw. Sharing helped me.”

They ate together. Crunch, crunch. The nuts were buttery and sweet. A gentle breeze brought the smell of basil. The bees sang. The water barrel went drip, drip, drip, like a slow drum.

After their snack, Sunny picked three nuts and pressed them into a deep pot. “We plant,” he said. “Maybe a tiny tree will grow.”

The crow hopped in a circle. “Caw! New trees!”

Sunny looked at the garden, at the neat nut piles, and at his new friend. He felt proud. He had used his calm plan. He had taken action with care. He had turned a problem into a plan.

Sunny waved his fluffy tail. “When I feel mad, I can stop, breathe, and speak kindly. Then I can make a smart plan.”

The crow lifted his wings. “Caw. Calm makes good choices.”

They watched a small cloud drift by, soft as cotton. The balcony felt safe and bright.

The End.
