# Bolt the Robot Dog’s Snack Plan

In a sunny garage workshop, a helpful robot dog named Bolt blinked his bright blue eyes. His ears were little metal triangles that went “ting-ting” when he wagged. The air smelled like warm wood, orange peels, and a tiny bit of motor oil. Tools hung on the walls, shiny and neat. A little fan hummed, “whirr, whirr.”

“Good morning!” said Mia, Bolt’s best friend. She wore a green apron with paint dots. “Today we build a kite!”

“Kite day! Woof-beep!” Bolt wagged and his tail light blinked, “blink-blink.”

Mia opened a box. “We need sticks, string, paper, and… snacks! Building makes us hungry.”

“Snack plan!” said Bolt. He sat up straight. Inside his chest was a tiny screen that showed a smiling apple. “We choose fuel for our bodies. Healthy fuel helps us work and play.”

Mia giggled. “I want cookies.”

Bolt tilted his head. “Cookies are tasty, but they are sometimes snacks. For building, we need energy from fruits, veggies, and protein.” He tapped his paw. “Let’s prepare together. We can make a snack tray.”

Mia looked at the cookie jar. It was round and red and smelled sugary-sweet. “Can we have one cookie at the end?”

Bolt’s eyes blinked. “Compromise accepted! We prepare healthy snacks first. After we build and taste our colors, we can share one small cookie together.”

Mia smiled. “Deal!”

They washed their hands in a little sink. The water felt cool. “Splash-splash.” Soap made tiny bubbles that smelled like lemons.

“Step one: preparation,” said Bolt. “We wash, we plan, we chop.”

Mia opened the mini-fridge. “We have carrots, cucumbers, yogurt, cheese, and blueberries.”

“Color power!” Bolt beeped. “Orange, green, white, yellow, blue!”

They set out a wooden board. Mia crunched a carrot stick. “Crunch! It’s loud.”

“Carrots help our eyes,” said Bolt, making his eyes glow brighter. “Cucumbers are fresh and give us water. Blueberries are tiny and sweet. Cheese gives protein to build strong muscles. Yogurt is creamy and helps our tummy.”

Mia dipped a cucumber in yogurt. “Mmm, cool and smooth.”

Bolt’s nose light sniffed. “Sniff-scan! This snack will help us build and fly.”

They started the kite. The paper was sunny yellow. The glue smelled a little like flowers. “Sticky-sticky,” Mia said, pressing the paper edges.

“String time!” said Bolt. He used his gentle clamp paw to hold the string. “Hold here, Mia.”

“I’ll tie it,” Mia said. “One loop, two loop.” She pulled tight. “There!”

The fan blew a soft breeze. “Whirr.” The paper made a quiet flap-flap. Bolt’s tail light blinked, “blink-blink.” His metal paws felt cool on the table. He hummed a happy tune, “doo-doo beep.”

After a little while, Mia rubbed her tummy. “I am hungry.”

“Snack break,” said Bolt. “We prepared well.” He placed the snack tray on the workbench. It looked like a rainbow: orange carrots, green cucumbers, blue blueberries, yellow cheese squares, and a white bowl of yogurt.

“Let’s make food pictures,” Mia said, eyes twinkling. “Creativity!”

Bolt wagged. “Yes! We can make faces.” Mia made a smile face with blueberries for eyes, a cheese nose, and a carrot smile. Bolt used cucumber slices for ears. “Woof!” he said.

They counted together. “One, two blueberries. Three, four carrot sticks.” Mia dipped a carrot in yogurt. “Crunch, dip, yum!”

“How does your body feel?” asked Bolt.

“Happy and strong,” said Mia, flexing her little arm. “I can build!”

“Lesson check,” said Bolt, tapping his screen. “Healthy snacks give steady energy. Preparation makes it easy. Creativity makes it fun.”

They finished the kite tail with colorful fabric bows. “Pink bow, blue bow, green bow,” Mia said, tying carefully.

“Teamwork,” Bolt said. “You tie. I hold.” His clamp paw stayed steady. Glue sparkled in the sun light.

At last, the kite was ready. It glowed yellow like a small sun. Mia looked at the cookie jar and then at the tray. “We ate our rainbow.”

“Compromise time,” said Bolt gently. He opened the jar with a soft click. “One cookie to share.”

They broke the cookie into two small halves. It smelled warm and sweet. “Crunch,” Mia said. Bolt made a happy beep. “Beep-yum.”

“Thanks for helping me choose,” said Mia. “I like our plan. Prepare first. Eat colors. Share a treat.”

Bolt’s screen showed a little heart. “Good choices make strong bodies and calm minds,” he said. “Now we can test the kite!”

They stepped just outside the garage. A soft breeze brushed their cheeks. It smelled like grass and sunshine. Mia held the string. Bolt trotted beside her, paws tapping, “tink-tink.”

“Ready?” Mia asked.

“Ready,” said Bolt.

Mia ran a few tiny steps. The kite lifted and rose. “Whoosh!” It danced in the sky, bright and high. Bolt wagged so hard his metal ears went “ting-ting-ting!”

“We did it!” Mia laughed. “Our snack gave us energy. Our plan helped us work. Our compromise felt fair.”

Bolt nodded. “Today we learned: Prepare healthy snacks. Choose colorful foods. Work together. Compromise when we want different things. Then we can create and play.”

Mia squeezed Bolt in a gentle hug. “Thank you, helper.”

“Always,” said Bolt. “Woof-beep!”

Back in the cozy garage, they cleaned up the tools and put the tray in the sink. The fan still hummed, and the afternoon sun made golden squares on the floor. Mia felt proud, full, and ready for more play.

Healthy choices, happy day.

The End.
