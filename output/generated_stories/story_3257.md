# Tiko the Tortoise Paints the Guest Room

Tiko was a wise tortoise with a smooth, shiny shell. He moved slowly and smiled often. One rainy afternoon, Tiko peeked into the guest room. It was quiet and cozy. The lamp made a warm, golden glow. The bed had soft, blue blankets. The air smelled like clean soap and crayons.

On a small table, Tiko saw paper, brushes, and bright paints. Red, yellow, blue, and green—so many colors! Tiko’s eyes twinkled. “Today, I will make art,” he said softly. “I will paint a happy picture for our guests.”

Tiko dipped a brush in red paint. Drip, drip. He touched the paper. Swoosh. The brush slid, but the line went wiggly and wide.

“Oh,” said Tiko. “That is not what I wanted.”

He tried again with yellow. Swoosh. Now a blob plopped on the corner. Plop!

Tiko took a tiny breath. “I feel a little upset,” he whispered. “But I can try again.”

He made a small circle with blue. The circle looked like an egg. He wanted a round sun, but it looked squashed. His tummy felt tight, and his face felt warm.

A little voice from the doorway said, “Hi, Tiko! What are you painting?” It was Mina the mouse, with pink ears and soft steps. She sniffed the sweet smell of paint. “Mmm, it smells like lemons and rain.”

“I am painting a sun for our guests,” Tiko said. “But my sun looks like a blueberry egg.”

Mina giggled kindly. “It is a nice blueberry egg,” she said. “But if you want a sun, maybe you can practice. My mama says, ‘Little steps make big art.’”

Tiko nodded. “Little steps,” he repeated. He closed his eyes and felt his breath go in and out. In… and out… slow, like a turtle walk.

He looked around the guest room. The curtains were soft and white. They moved like clouds when the window breeze whispered. The rug felt fuzzy under his feet. The lamp hummed quietly, zzz, like a sleepy bee.

“I will make little shapes first,” he said. “Circle, circle, circle.”

Tiko dipped his brush in water. Swish, swish. He tapped it on the rim. Tap, tap. Then he tried a circle. It was better. He tried another circle. Better again. Mina clapped. “You did it!”

But then, drip! A big drop of water fell on the paper. The colors ran together. The circle turned muddy brown.

Tiko’s eyes got wet. “Oh no,” he said softly. “My circles are gone.”

Mina put a tiny paw on his shell. “It’s okay to feel sad,” she said. “You can take a pause.”

Tiko nodded. He set down the brush. He counted to five. One… two… three… four… five. He listened to the rain on the window. Pitter-patter, pitter-patter. His body felt calm again.

“I will try a new idea,” he said. “I will paint in layers. First light, then bright.”

He turned the paper over. Fresh and clean. He made a light yellow circle, very gentle. Swoosh. He waited. He watched the paint dry. Waiting felt long. He wanted to go fast, but he remembered: “I am a tortoise. Slow can be strong.”

To pass the time, he hummed a song. “Slow and steady, brush so ready,” he sang in a tiny voice. The lamp’s warm light made the wet paint shine like a little moon. When it was dry, Tiko added bright yellow. Swoosh, swoosh. He made small, careful rays. One ray. Two rays. Three rays. He stopped to breathe. He smiled.

“It is a sun!” Mina cheered.

Tiko felt proud. His heart felt big and warm, like cocoa.

He added a blue sky with soft strokes. He mixed a little white to make the sky gentle. He made small green hills, round and cozy. He remembered his first wiggly line. He tried again, very slow. Swoooosh. The line curved just right.

Then—oops! The brush slipped, and a tiny red dot landed in the sky. Boop!

Mina gasped. “A dot!”

Tiko blinked. “Maybe it is a balloon,” he said. He painted a little string. He added a tiny smile on the balloon. The dot became a happy balloon floating over the hills.

Mina giggled. “I love the silly balloon.”

Tiko added one more thing. He looked at the guest room and saw the blue blanket on the bed. He painted a little blue blanket on the hillside, like a picnic. He put a small basket on it with tiny red apples. He could almost smell them—sweet and crisp.

He leaned back and looked. The sun was bright. The sky was soft. The hills were round. The balloon smiled. The picnic looked cozy. The whole picture felt happy.

“I did it,” Tiko said. “I tried, I paused, I practiced, and I fixed my oops.”

Mina clapped and did a tiny dance. “Guests will love this art,” she said. “It shows your care.”

Tiko carefully set the picture on the table to dry. He washed his brush. Swish, swish. The water turned rainbow for a moment, then clear again.

Tiko smiled. “Art takes patience,” he said. “Little steps, slow breaths, and try again.”

Mina nodded. “And oops can become balloons!”

They both laughed. The rain outside slowed to a soft drip. The guest room felt warm and bright, full of color and calm.

Lesson: When we make art, we can go slow, practice, and keep trying. Mistakes can become new ideas, and patience helps us grow.

The End.
