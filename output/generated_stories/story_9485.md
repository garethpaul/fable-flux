# Feather's Gentle Nursery Plan

Morning light slipped through soft curtains in the comfortable nursery. The room smelled like fresh cotton and warm sunlight. Toys rested in tidy baskets. A tiny plant sat by the window, its leaves shiny and green.

On the rocking chair, a small singing bird named Feather fluffed her blue feathers. She hummed a sweet tune. “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo,” she sang, happy and bright.

Feather lived in the nursery with Baby Pip, who giggled and clapped. “Bir! Bir!” Pip squealed. Feather chirped back, “Good morning, Pip!”

Feather loved songs and love and gentle air. But today, something felt wrong. The trash bin was full, and the toy basket was too. The plant’s leaves drooped a little. A night-light hummed softly, still on from the dark night.

Feather tilted her head. “Hmm,” she said. “This does not feel kind to our cozy nest.”

She hopped to the windowsill and listened. The nursery was quiet, like a soft blanket. Feather made a plan song, low and calm. “Tweet-plan, sweet-plan,” she sang. “We can care. We can share. We can make it fair.”

Baby Pip waved a tiny sock. “Plan!” he said. Feather nodded. “Yes, a plan! We will care for our room and our world.”

First, Feather flew to the plant. She touched a leaf with her beak. It felt dry. “You are thirsty,” she whispered. “I hear you.” She listened to her own tummy too. It rumbled—cheep. “And I am hungry! We both need care.”

Feather lifted a little watering can with her beak. Drip, drip, drip. The water smelled cool. The plant perked up, leaf by leaf. “Thank you,” it seemed to say, rustling softly. Feather pecked some seeds from her tiny dish. Crunch, crunch. “We care for us, and we care for you,” Feather sang. “Self-care helps us care for the world too.”

Next, Feather looked at the trash. Crinkle, crinkle—there were paper scraps, a shiny bottle, and a soft cloth. Feather frowned kindly. “This all in one bin is not a good plan,” she said. “We need to sort.”

She flapped to the toy shelf and found three small baskets. She put a blue sticker on one, a green leaf sticker on the next, and a yellow sun sticker on the last.

“This is our strategy,” Feather told Pip. “Blue is for paper. Green is for bottles. Yellow is for cloth that we can wash and use again.” She spoke slow and simple. “We sort to save. We use again to help the Earth.”

Baby Pip clapped. “Sort!” he said.

Together, they made it a game. Feather sang, “Paper to blue, crinkle-doo!” Pip giggled and dropped paper in the blue basket. “Bottles to green, shiny-clean!” Feather chirped, and in went the bottle. “Cloth to yellow, soft and mellow!” Pip patted the soft cloth into the yellow basket.

Feather’s heart felt warm. The room looked calm. The plant looked proud.

Then Feather heard a buzz-hum. The night-light was still on. Feather blinked. “Little light, sun is here,” she said gently. “You may rest now.” She turned the switch with her beak. Click. The room felt even softer. “When we are not using you, we let you rest. That saves energy.”

But just then, the window made a squeak. A cool breeze slipped in. Squeak, squeak! The curtain fluttered like a sleepy butterfly.

Feather thought, “Should I close the window?” She paused. She looked at the plant, which waved happily in the fresh air. She took a deep breath. The air smelled clean.

Feather made a peaceful plan. “We will open the window in the day for fresh air,” she said. “And we will close it at nap time to keep us warm.” She smiled. “We can listen and choose. That is a peaceful solution.”

Pip yawned. “Nap,” he murmured.

Feather nodded. “First, we wash our hands,” she reminded. They went to the tiny sink. Splash, swish, rub-rub-rub. Feather hummed a hand-washing song. “Clean hands, happy day, wash the worries all away.” They patted dry with a small towel and hung it up to dry. “We can use this towel again,” Feather said. “That is kind to our home.”

At last, Feather tucked Pip’s blanket snug. She placed the yellow sun basket by the door for laundry later. She peeked at the blue and green baskets, neat and light.

Feather sang a soft lullaby. “Care for me, care for you, care for Earth, we all do.” Pip smiled, eyes fluttering closed.

Feather looked around the comfortable nursery. The air felt fresh. The light was off. The plant stood tall. The baskets stood ready. Feather’s feathers felt smooth and settled.

She whispered, “We did it with a kind heart. We used a plan. We listened. We shared. We saved.”

The lesson floated like a feather in the room: When we care for ourselves and sort our things, we help our home and our Earth. Small gentle choices make a big gentle world.

Feather curled her tail and rested. “Tweet-a-dee,” she hummed, very soft. “We can do this every day.”

The End.
