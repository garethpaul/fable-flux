# Milo the Brave Monkey Speaks Up

Milo was a small, fluffy monkey with bright brown eyes and a long, curly tail. He lived in a cozy home office with tall bookshelves, a big wooden desk, and a comfortable chair that spun around and around. Milo loved to swing from the lamp to the bookshelf, but today something was wrong.

"Ouch! Ow! Ouch!" Milo said as he tried to climb. His little paws hurt when he grabbed onto things. He looked down at his hands and saw they were all red and sore.

Milo's friend Emma, a kind lady who worked at the big desk, was typing on her computer. Click-click-click went the keys. She was very busy and didn't notice Milo's problem.

Milo sat quietly on his favorite shelf, holding his sore paws close to his chest. "Maybe Emma is too busy," he thought. "Maybe I shouldn't bother her." He tried to climb again, but it hurt even more. "Ow!"

The little monkey felt sad and scared. His paws were getting more sore, and he couldn't play or move around like he loved to do. He watched Emma typing and wondered what to do.

Then Milo remembered something his mama had taught him: "When something is wrong, it's important to speak up and ask for help. That's what brave monkeys do!"

Milo took a deep breath. He swung down from the shelf and gently tapped Emma's arm with his tail. "Emma," he said in his small, clear voice. "I need help, please."

Emma stopped typing and looked down. "Oh, Milo! What's wrong, little friend?"

"My paws hurt," Milo said, showing her his red, sore hands. "I can't climb or swing anymore. It makes me sad."

Emma's eyes grew wide with concern. "Oh my! Thank you for telling me, Milo. I'm so glad you spoke up. Let me help you right away."

Emma gently picked up Milo and looked at his paws. "I think you might have hurt them yesterday when we moved those heavy books," she said softly. "You were so helpful, but maybe the books were too rough for your little hands."

Emma got a soft, warm cloth and some gentle cream. "This will help your paws feel better," she said, carefully cleaning Milo's hands. The cream felt cool and soothing. "Ahh," sighed Milo as the pain began to go away.

"You know what, Milo?" Emma said with a smile. "You were very brave to tell me about your problem. Some people and animals are afraid to ask for help, but you did the right thing."

Emma found some soft, colorful gloves that were just Milo's size. "These will protect your paws while they heal," she explained, slipping them on his hands. They were bright blue and had tiny bananas on them!

Milo wiggled his fingers in the new gloves. They felt wonderful! He carefully climbed up to his shelf and then swung gently to the lamp. No more pain!

"Thank you, Emma!" Milo chirped happily. "I'm glad I told you about my problem."

"Me too," Emma said. "Remember, Milo, it's always okay to speak up when something is wrong or when you need help. That's how we take care of each other."

From that day on, Milo always remembered to use his voice when he needed help. He learned that being brave meant speaking up for himself, and that asking for help was a smart and important thing to do.

The End.
