# Milo the Brave Monkey Says the Truth

Milo was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and soft brown fur. He had a cozy study corner at home. It was sunny and warm, with a little desk, a wobbly chair, and a basket of crayons that smelled like sweet berries. A clock went tick-tock on the wall. Milo liked the quiet hum of the fan as it spun and spun.

Today was Goal Day. Milo had a big goal. He tapped a star chart on the wall and whispered, “I want to learn all my shape cards by Friday.” He stuck a golden star next to the word “Try.”

Mama Monkey smiled. “That’s a strong goal, Milo. You can do it with practice. Be honest with yourself and others. That is the bravest way.”

Milo nodded. He shuffled his shape cards. Circle, square, triangle. He set a timer. “Five minutes,” he said. Beep! The timer began.

Soon, Milo heard a soft sound—tap, tap, tap. It was his tail flicking, flicking. He felt wiggly. He wanted to run. But he took a breath. “I can try again,” he told himself. “I can be brave.”

Milo practiced. He traced the shapes with his finger. The circle was smooth like a banana. The square felt pointy on his fingertip. “I will get it,” he said.

Just then, his friend Tika the turtle poked her head in. She carried a basket of tiny books from the community book shelf outside. “Hi, Milo! I’m taking these books back to the book swap box,” Tika said. “Do you want to help?”

Milo’s eyes lit up. He loved helping. He loved the sunshine on his fur. But he looked at his goal chart. He looked at his cards. He wanted both. He wanted to help the community and practice.

“I will finish one more round,” Milo decided. “Then I will help.” He flipped the cards again. Circle, square, triangle. When the timer went beep-beep, he smiled. “Now I can help!”

They walked together. The air smelled like fresh leaves. The path felt warm under Milo’s toes. At the swap box, a small crowd waited—Mrs. Parrot, Mr. Bear, and Baby Squirrel.

“Oh dear,” said Mr. Bear. He held up a torn book. “Someone ripped this page and did not tell anyone.”

Milo felt a squeeze in his tummy. He remembered. Yesterday, he had borrowed that same book. He turned the pages too fast. Rip! He got scared and put it back. He had said nothing. His cheeks felt hot now. His tail drooped.

Mrs. Parrot asked, “Does anyone know what happened?”

Milo looked down at his toes. He heard his heart thump. Thump-thump. He wanted to hide. He wanted to say, “I don’t know.” But Mama’s words floated in his head. Be honest. That is the bravest way.

Milo took a breath. He lifted his head. “I did it,” he said softly. “I turned the page too fast. I ripped it. I felt scared. I did not tell you. I am sorry.”

The world seemed very quiet. Even the leaves stood still.

Then Mrs. Parrot blinked. “Thank you for telling the truth, Milo,” she said. Her voice was gentle. “That was honest and brave.”

Mr. Bear nodded. “We can fix torn things. We cannot fix secrets.”

Tika smiled at Milo. “We can repair it together.”

Milo felt a tiny light in his chest. It glowed warm. “I want to help,” he said.

They set up a little repair station in the study corner. The fan hummed. The tape made a soft shh as it rolled. The paper felt crinkly under Milo’s careful fingers. Tika held the page. Milo smoothed it flat. Mr. Bear pressed the tape. Mrs. Parrot read the words slowly so they knew where the picture went.

“Teamwork!” cheered Baby Squirrel, clapping tiny paws.

When the book was mended, everyone smiled. The page was neat. The picture was bright again.

Mrs. Parrot said, “Milo, your honesty helped our community. You told the truth, and we worked it out.”

Milo’s tail swished happily. “I was scared,” he said. “But telling the truth helped my heart feel calm.”

On the walk home, Tika asked, “Will you still practice your shapes?”

Milo grinned. “Yes. My goal is important. I will try again.”

Back in the study corner, he set the timer—beep! “Circle,” he said. “Square. Triangle.” He tapped each card. He made a plan on his star chart: Practice two times a day. Help the book box on Wednesdays. Tell the truth always.

Mama peeked in. “How did it go?”

Milo stood tall. “I told the truth about the torn book. We fixed it together. I felt brave.”

Mama hugged him. Her fur was soft and smelled like warm cookies. “Honesty shines,” she whispered. “When we tell the truth, we grow strong inside.”

That night, the fan whispered, the clock went tick-tock, and the crickets sang. Milo placed one more golden star on his chart. He smiled and closed his eyes.

“I can be honest,” he murmured. “I can try again. I can help my friends.”

His heart felt light. His study corner felt safe and bright. Tomorrow, he would practice shapes again. He would help at the book swap box. And he would tell the truth, even when it felt hard.

Because Milo the courageous monkey knew: honesty makes problems smaller, friends closer, and goals possible.

The End.
