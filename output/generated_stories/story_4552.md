# Penny Penguin Makes It Fair

Penny was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a wiggly walk. One sunny morning, she waddled into the dining room. The room smelled like warm toast and sweet apples. The table was shiny. The chairs were cozy. The window let in golden light that tickled Penny’s feathers.

“Good morning!” Penny chirped.

Mama Penguin smiled. “Good morning, Penny. It’s breakfast time.”

Penny climbed into her chair. “Yum!” she said. She tasted crunchy toast. She sipped cool water. “It feels like tiny bubbles on my tongue,” she giggled. Her tummy felt happy and warm.

At the end of breakfast, Mama said, “It’s Chore Time. We take turns. We make it fair.”

Penny nodded. “Fair means everyone gets a turn.”

Mama put up the Chore Chart on the wall. It had pictures and stickers. It showed four jobs:
- Wipe the table
- Carry the napkins
- Put cups in the bin
- Sweep tiny crumbs

Penny looked at the chart. Today, the sticker showed that wiping the table was her job. Penny loved wiping the table. She loved the swoosh-swoosh sound.

But then, Papa walked in. He had a plate and a big smile. “I can wipe the table super fast!” he said, grabbing the cloth.

Penny felt a bump in her chest. “But that’s my turn,” she squeaked. She held her wings close. Her face felt hot. Her heart went thump-thump.

Mama saw Penny’s eyes. “Let’s pause,” Mama said softly. “Let’s take a slow penguin breath. In… and out…”

Penny breathed in. She breathed out. “I can use my calm,” she whispered.

Papa looked surprised. “Oh! I didn’t see the chart,” he said. He put the cloth down. “You’re right, Penny. Justice means fair turns. I will wait.”

Penny smiled a tiny smile. “Thank you, Papa.”

She picked up the cloth. Swoosh-swoosh went the cloth. The table felt cool and smooth. Penny made little circles. “Round and round,” she sang. “Round and round, make it shiny, make it sound!”

Papa clapped softly. “Go, Penny!”

Then a new problem popped up. Penny saw her little cousin, Pip, carrying the heavy cups. Pip’s flippers shook. “I want to help! I want to be fast!” Pip huffed.

Penny watched. One cup wobbled. “Uh-oh,” said Pip. The cup tipped. Clink! Water splashed on the floor. Pip’s eyes got big. “I’m sorry,” Pip sniffled. His beak trembled.

Penny felt the bump in her chest again. She wanted to shout, “No!” But she remembered her penguin breath. In… and out… She put a wing on her tummy. Calm came back like a soft blanket.

Penny waddled over. “Pip, it’s okay,” she said. “We can fix it. Justice means we help and we follow the chart. Today, cups are my job.”

Pip blinked. “Justice?”

“It means fair and kind,” Penny said. “We all get turns. We do the job that is ours.”

Mama nodded. “And we clean up our spills. That keeps us safe and well.”

Penny gave Pip a napkin. “Let’s pat the splash. Pat, pat, pat.” The floor felt cool and wet under the napkin. Pip giggled. “Pat, pat, pat!”

When the puddle was gone, Penny picked up the cups. “I will carry two. You can carry the napkins. That’s your job today,” she said.

Pip stood taller. “Napkins are my job,” he said proudly. He liked the soft, papery rustle in his flippers.

They worked together. Swoosh-swoosh went the cloth. Rustle-rustle went the napkins. Tap-tap went the cups in the bin. Sweep-sweep went the tiny broom as Mama helped with crumbs.

Papa watched the chart. “I will take my turn next time,” he said. “Fair is fair.”

When the dining room was clean, the sunlight danced on the table. It looked like sparkles on ice. Penny felt light and happy. Her body felt strong and calm. Her tummy felt peaceful.

Mama asked, “How do we feel?”

“Safe and well,” said Penny.

“Fair and proud,” said Pip.

“Grateful,” said Papa.

Penny nodded. “Justice helped us. We took turns. We fixed the spill. We used calm breaths.”

Pip hugged Penny. “You showed me,” he said.

Penny hugged back. “We can show each other.”

Mama put a golden star sticker on the chart. “This star is for justice,” she said. “Justice is when everyone is treated fair and kind.”

Penny’s eyes shone. “Justice feels good,” she said. “Like warm toast. Like a clean table. Like a big penguin hug.”

They all laughed. Penny looked at the chart again. “Next time, I can sweep crumbs,” she said. “I will wait for my turn.”

Mama smiled. “We will all wait. We will all help. We will all be well.”

Penny took one more penguin breath. In… and out… She tapped her chest softly. “I can be calm. I can be fair. I can do my chores,” she whispered.

And in the shiny dining room, with sunlight and giggles and clean, happy smells, Penny the cheerful penguin felt proud. Justice lived in their home, one small turn at a time.

The End.
