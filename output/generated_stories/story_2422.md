# Tall Steps to Make Things Right

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with a long neck and kind eyes. She loved to help. She loved to think. Today, Gigi was in the dining room. The sun made warm squares on the floor. The wooden table smelled like lemon soap. Plates waited like little moons. Forks and spoons twinkled with tiny lights.

“Dinner soon,” said Mama Giraffe. “Let’s set the table.”

“I can do it!” said Gigi. Her tail swished with excitement. Her hooves made a soft tip-tap on the floor.

Gigi reached her long neck to the shelf. She lifted a stack of plates. “I am helpful,” she whispered. She felt proud and tall.

But the plates were heavy. Gigi wobbled. “Uh-oh,” she said. The plates slid—clatter, clatter, clatter! One plate slipped and fell. It hit the floor. CRACK!

The room went very quiet. Gigi’s ears drooped. Her cheeks felt hot. She looked at the broken plate. Little pieces looked like puzzle parts.

“I… I broke it,” Gigi said in a small voice.

Mama Giraffe walked over. She looked gentle, not angry. “Are you okay, Gigi?” she asked.

Gigi nodded. “Yes. But I made a mess. I feel bad. I am sorry.”

Mama breathed in. “I hear your words. Thank you for saying sorry,” she said. “Saying sorry is a good start. Now, what can we do to make it right?”

Gigi thought. Her tail stopped swishing. “I can help clean,” she said. “I can fix my mistake.”

“That is a brave idea,” said Mama. “Let’s do it safely.”

Mama brought a little broom and a dustpan. She also brought a soft brush. “We will work together,” she said.

The tiny pieces made a tink-tink sound as Gigi swept. “I am careful,” Gigi whispered. She listened to the whisk of the broom. She watched the pieces slide into the pan like shiny snow.

When all the pieces were up, Mama wiped the floor. “All clean,” she said. The room smelled like lemon again.

Gigi looked at the table. One plate was missing. There were four forks but only three plates. Her heart felt heavy. “I still feel sorry,” she said.

Mama nodded. “Making amends means we try to fix, help, and learn,” she said. “What could you do next?”

Gigi’s eyes brightened. “I can set the table slowly,” she said. “One plate at a time. I can use both hooves. I can ask for help.”

“That is a smart plan,” said Mama.

Gigi took a deep breath. In, out. She picked up one plate. She held it with two hooves. Tip-tap, tip-tap, she walked to the table. She placed it gently. Tap. The plate sat safe and still.

She did it again. And again. The spoons and forks followed. Clink, clink. The napkins were soft and folded like little clouds. Gigi smoothed each one.

“May I borrow one of the snack plates?” Gigi asked. “It can be my plate tonight, so everyone has one.”

“That is very thoughtful,” Mama said. “Yes, you may.”

Soon the table looked ready. The room felt calm and cozy. The window showed a pink sky. The air smelled like warm carrots and sweet bread.

Papa Giraffe came in. “What a lovely table,” he said. “Thank you, Gigi.”

Gigi looked at the floor where the plate had broken. It was clean now. “I made a mistake,” she told Papa. “I said sorry. I helped clean. I set the table slowly. I learned to ask for help.”

Papa smiled. “That is making amends,” he said. “That is respect for our home and for each other.”

Gigi’s ears perked up. “It felt hard,” she said. “But I kept trying.”

“That is a growth mindset,” Mama said. “You kept going. You learned a new way.”

They all sat down. The chairs made a friendly scrape on the floor. The bread was warm and soft. The carrots were sweet and orange. Gigi took a bite. Crunch! “Mmm,” she said.

After dinner, Gigi stood up. “I want to help with chores,” she said. “I can clear the napkins. I can carry the cups.”

“Thank you,” said Mama. “We can sing while we work.”

They sang a soft, happy song. The cups went clink. The water went swish. The towels went rub-rub. The dining room glowed with clean and care.

When everything was done, Gigi hugged Mama. “I still feel a little sad about the plate,” she said.

Mama hugged back. “It is okay to feel sad,” she said. “You made amends. You learned. You grew. That is what matters.”

Gigi smiled. Her heart felt light again. “Next time, I will go slow,” she said. “And I will ask for help.”

Mama nodded. “That is a wonderful plan.”

Gigi looked at the shiny spoons, the soft napkins, and the safe, steady plates. She felt proud, not just tall. She felt ready for tomorrow.

The End.
