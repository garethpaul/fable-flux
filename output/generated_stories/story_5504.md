# Rhythm in the Bubbly Bathroom

Rusty the raccoon loved music. His striped tail twitched when he heard a beat. His ears perked up at any little song. Today, Rusty had a big plan. He would make a bathroom band!

Rusty padded into the shiny bathroom on quiet paws. The floor felt cool and smooth. “Hello, echo!” he called. His voice bounced back, soft and wiggly. Drip, drip went the faucet. Swish went the fluffy towels when the air moved. Rusty giggled. “So many sounds!”

He looked at the tub. “You can be my drum,” he told it. He tapped the side with a gentle paw. Bonk, bonk. He smiled. He tapped the soap dish with a spoon. Ting, ting! He blew across a bottle. Woo-oo. The mirror fogged a little from his warm breath, and he drew a tiny music note with his finger.

Rusty set his tools in a line: a hairbrush, a plastic cup, a rubber duck, and a comb. “Band, are you ready?” he asked in a brave voice.

“Quack!” said the rubber duck.

“Perfect,” Rusty said. “One, two, three!”

He tapped the tub. Bonk. He shook the comb by the cup. Scritch-scritch. He made the bottle sing. Woo-oo. But then the spoon slipped. Clang! The cup fell into the sink with a loud plop. Water splashed his nose. Cold drops made him squeak. His tail drooped. “Oh,” he whispered.

He tried again. “I can do this.” He tapped softer. Bonk, bonk. He shook the comb. Scritch. He reached for the bottle—slip! The bottle rolled away and thumped the toilet paper. The roll tumbled to the floor and bounced. Rusty’s whiskers trembled. “It’s not working!” he said. His face felt hot. His heart felt tight.

He sat on the bath mat. It was fluffy and soft. He hugged his tail. “I’m mad,” he said. “I wanted to make a song. But everything falls. I don’t like this feeling.” His eyes felt watery.

Just then, he heard tiny paws on tile. His friend Mina the mouse peeked around the door. She wore a bright blue bow. “Hi, Rusty,” Mina squeaked kindly. “I heard clinks and clanks. Are you okay?”

Rusty sniffed. “I’m trying to make music,” he said. “But my spoon slips. The cup falls. The bottle runs away. I feel frustrated.”

Mina nodded. “Frustrated is a big feeling,” she said softly. “I feel it when my bow won’t tie. My whiskers twitch and I want to shout. But I don’t shout. I stop. I breathe. Want to try with me?”

Rusty blinked. “Okay.”

“Breathe in through your nose,” Mina said. “Smell the soap. One… two… three.” Rusty sniffed. The soap smelled like lemons and sunshine.

“Now breathe out slowly,” Mina said. “Blow the bubbles on the duck. One… two… three.” Rusty blew. The duck wobbled and made a tiny squeak. Rusty smiled a little.

“Again,” Mina said. They breathed in. They breathed out. The bathroom felt calm. The drip, drip sounded like a soft metronome.

“My tummy feels softer now,” Rusty said. “Thank you.”

“You’re welcome,” Mina said. “We can try again. We can practice. We can go slow. And we can be kind to ourselves.”

Rusty stood up. He wiped a tear with his paw. “Let’s make a plan.”

Mina clapped her tiny hands. “We can use teamwork. We can use our different talents. I am small. I can hold the cup steady. You have strong paws. You can tap the tub.”

Rusty nodded. “And we can use imagination. The towel can be a cloud. The tub can be a big bass drum. The comb can be tiny rain.”

They set the band again. Mina held the cup with both paws. Rusty held the spoon with one paw and the comb with the other.

“First, a slow beat,” Mina said. “Tap, tap.” Rusty tapped. Bonk… bonk. They counted together. “One… two… one… two…”

“Now, a gentle scritch,” Mina said. Rusty dragged the comb softly. Scritch-scritch. It sounded like soft rain on a window.

“Bottle time,” Mina squeaked. “I will hold it.” She hugged the bottle with her little arms so it wouldn’t roll. Rusty blew. Woo-oo. It floated like a whale song.

Rusty’s eyes lit up. “We did it!” he cheered. He swished his tail in time. He felt proud.

“Let’s add the duck,” Mina said. Rusty gave the duck a tiny squeeze on beat. Quack… quack. The music grew, layer by layer, like building blocks.

“Listen, Mina,” Rusty said. “The faucet drips like a clock. Drip… drip…”

“And the fan hums like bees,” Mina added. “Hmmmmm.”

They smiled at each other. Their bathroom band made a gentle, happy song. Bonk, scritch, woo-oo, quack, drip, hmmmmm. It sounded like a cozy storm with sunshine behind it.

Then Rusty’s spoon slipped again. Clink! He froze. His ears flattened. He felt the tight feeling tiptoe back.

Mina touched his paw. “It’s okay,” she said kindly. “Remember our steps. Stop. Breathe. Try again. Practice makes progress.”

Rusty breathed in, lemon and soap. He breathed out, slow and long. He picked up the spoon carefully. “I can try again,” he said. “I can practice. I can be patient.”

They played the song one more time. It was not perfect. But it was theirs. It was brave. It was kind.

When they finished, Rusty bowed to the rubber duck. “Thank you, band,” he said. “Thank you, Mina. We used teamwork. We used our different strengths.”

Mina bowed back. “And we took care of our big feelings.”

Rusty felt warm and proud. “When I feel frustrated, I can stop, breathe, and try again,” he said. “I can ask for help. I can practice. I can be kind to myself and others.”

“Exactly,” Mina said.

Rusty looked at the shiny mirror. He saw two friends, different and special, smiling together. The bathroom felt like a concert hall. The air smelled fresh. The tiles felt cool and safe. The echo sounded like a cheer.

“Band practice tomorrow?” Mina asked.

“Yes,” Rusty said. “We will keep learning, one small beat at a time.”

The End.
