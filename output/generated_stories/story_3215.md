# Poppy the Patient Panda's Cozy Feelings Night

Poppy the panda lived in a cozy bedroom with soft, squishy pillows and a warm, fuzzy blanket. The room smelled like clean cotton and a little bit like sweet honey. A tiny nightlight glowed like a friendly firefly. Poppy was patient and kind, and she liked to take care of her feelings, just like she took care of her soft, black-and-white fur.

One evening, Poppy’s friend, Tiko the turtle, came for a sleepover. Tiko had a bright green shell and big curious eyes. Poppy clapped her paws. “Hi, Tiko! Welcome to my cozy room,” she said.

“Hi, Poppy,” Tiko said, scooting inside. “Your room feels warm. It smells nice too!”

Poppy smiled. “Thank you. I like it calm and clean.” She pointed to a small basket. “We put toys here when we’re done. That is our room rule. It’s called responsibility.”

Tiko nodded slowly. “I can be responsible.”

On Poppy’s soft rug, they built a tower with colorful blocks. Clack, clack, clack. The tower got tall and wobbly. “Look!” Tiko cheered.

Suddenly, the tower tipped and tumbled—clatter, clatter! Blocks rolled under the bed. Tiko’s face scrunched. “Oh no,” he whispered. “I feel… funny.”

Poppy took a gentle breath. She put a paw on her chest and another on her belly. In and out. Slow and soft. “I feel a little surprised,” she said. “My heart feels bouncy.”

Tiko’s eyes felt watery. “My tummy is tight. I think I feel upset.”

Poppy nodded. “Thank you for telling me. Let’s name our feelings. I feel surprised and a bit sad that the tower fell. You feel upset.”

Tiko sniffled. “Yes.”

Poppy looked at him with kind eyes. “Let’s use our patience. We can pause. We can breathe.” She showed Tiko. “In… and out.” They breathed together. The nightlight hummed softly, and the room felt calm again.

Tiko’s shoulders drooped less. “I feel a little better,” he said.

Poppy smiled. “Feelings change. They move like little clouds.” She touched a block and listened. “Click!” It felt cool and smooth. “We can try again when you’re ready. It’s okay to take time.”

Tiko blinked. “I am ready now.”

They built the tower again, slower this time. “One block. Two blocks. Good job,” Poppy said. “You are careful. I am patient.”

When the tower was done, Poppy clapped quietly. “We did it together.” Tiko giggled. “Together feels good.” He touched his shell. “Sometimes I move slowly. I worry I make people wait.”

Poppy tilted her head. “Your slow is special. Your careful is helpful. We are different, and that is good.” She tapped her paw. “I can wait. You can think. We are a team.”

Tiko’s eyes shone. “Thank you for celebrating my slow.”

Poppy’s tummy rumbled. “Snack time!” She brought out apple slices and a tiny jar of honey. Crunch, crunch. Sweet and crisp. “Mmm,” Tiko said. “Your snacks are kind.”

After snacks, Poppy picked up a small basket. “Now we clean up. Toys go home in the basket.” She sang a little song. “Pick up, put in, smile and spin.” Tiko swayed and joined the song. Their paws and little turtle feet went tap, tap, tap on the rug.

When the room was tidy, Poppy held up a toothbrush. “Next is hygiene time. Brush, brush!” The bathroom smelled like mint. The water was warm. Poppy showed Tiko small circles. “Around and around,” she said. “We take care of our teeth.” Tiko tried too. Swish, swish. “Minty!” he laughed.

Back in the cozy bedroom, Poppy put on her soft pajamas with tiny bamboo pictures. Tiko had a snug, blue sleep shirt with a moon on it. Poppy patted the bed. “Would you like a feelings check-in?”

Tiko nodded. “Yes, please.”

Poppy said, “I feel calm. My body is soft. My heart feels warm. I also feel proud that we cleaned up.”

Tiko placed a hand on his shell. “I feel sleepy and safe. I also feel happy that you waited for me.”

Poppy smiled. “Thank you for telling me. Sharing feelings helps us know what we need. When we know, we can help.”

Tiko looked at the tower they built, now resting in the basket as neat blocks. “I learned that when I feel upset, I can breathe, and I can say my feeling.”

Poppy nodded. “Yes. And I learned that being patient helps friends. Differences make our team strong.”

They turned off the big light. The nightlight glowed softly. The blanket felt fluffy and warm. Poppy whispered, “If feelings get loud at night, we can put a paw on our belly and breathe. We can say, ‘Hello, feeling. You can sit with us.’”

Tiko yawned a tiny yawn. “Hello, cozy feeling.”

Poppy hummed a gentle tune. The room was quiet except for the soft hum and their slow breaths. Poppy whispered, “We took care of our room, our teeth, and our hearts. That is responsibility, hygiene, and emotional-awareness.”

Tiko snuggled closer. “And we helped each other.”

Poppy smiled in the dark. “Yes. We helped, we waited, and we celebrated our differences.”

They both felt safe, clean, and loved. The night felt like a soft hug. Poppy said, “Tomorrow is another day for patient hearts.”

“Good night, Poppy,” Tiko said.

“Good night, Tiko,” Poppy answered.

The End.
