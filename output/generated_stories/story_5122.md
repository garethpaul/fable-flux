# Ollie's Clean Writing Adventure

Ollie the otter loved to play in his cozy bedroom. The soft blue blankets felt warm and fuzzy against his whiskers. His favorite toy boats floated in a small water bowl by his bed, making gentle splashing sounds.

One sunny morning, Ollie found something special on his wooden desk - a bright red crayon and clean white paper!

"Oh wow!" squeaked Ollie, clapping his little paws together. "I want to draw a picture!"

But when Ollie picked up the crayon with his paws, something felt yucky. His paws were muddy from playing outside yesterday, and they left brown smudges all over the pretty white paper.

"Uh oh," said Ollie, looking at the messy marks. "This doesn't look like the beautiful picture I wanted to make."

Ollie tried again, but his dirty paws made more smudges. The crayon slipped and slid in his muddy grip. Soon, the paper looked all brown and messy instead of colorful and pretty.

"I can't make anything nice," sighed Ollie, his whiskers drooping sadly. "My paws are too dirty."

Just then, Mama Otter peeked into the cozy bedroom. She saw Ollie looking sad at his desk, surrounded by smudged papers.

"What's wrong, my little swimmer?" asked Mama Otter in her gentle voice.

"I want to write and draw, but my paws make everything messy," said Ollie, showing her his muddy paws.

Mama Otter smiled warmly. "I know exactly how to help! Come with me to the bathroom."

In the bright bathroom, Mama Otter turned on the warm water. It made a cheerful bubbling sound as it filled the sink.

"First, we wash our paws with soap," said Mama Otter, squirting sweet-smelling soap onto Ollie's paws. "Rub, rub, rub!"

Ollie giggled as the bubbly soap tickled between his tiny fingers. The warm water felt so nice and cozy. He rubbed his paws together, watching the brown mud swirl down the drain.

"Now rinse with clean water," said Mama Otter.

Ollie held his paws under the flowing water. All the soap bubbles washed away, leaving his paws squeaky clean and shiny.

"Look, Mama! My paws are all clean!" said Ollie, wiggling his fresh, pink paws in the air.

"Perfect!" said Mama Otter. "Now you can make beautiful pictures and practice writing your letters."

Back in his cozy bedroom, Ollie picked up the red crayon with his clean paws. This time, it felt perfect in his grip - not slippery or yucky at all.

Carefully, Ollie drew a big, bright sun on his paper. The yellow crayon made smooth, beautiful lines. Then he drew blue waves and green grass. Everything looked so colorful and pretty!

"I did it!" cheered Ollie, dancing around his bedroom. "My clean paws helped me make something beautiful!"

Next, Ollie practiced writing the letter "O" for his name. His clean paws held the crayon steady, making perfect round circles on the paper.

"Writing is so much easier with clean paws," discovered Ollie happily.

From that day on, Ollie always washed his paws before writing or drawing. He learned that clean hands help us create beautiful things and make writing much more fun.

Every morning, Ollie would wash his paws first, then sit at his cozy desk to practice letters and draw wonderful pictures of his swimming adventures.

The End.
