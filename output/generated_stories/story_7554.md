# Pippa Penguin’s Splashy Bathroom Mural

Pippa was a cheerful penguin with a bright yellow scarf. She loved to hum and wiggle her little flippers. One morning, Pippa slid into the bathroom. The tiles were cool and smooth under her feet. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. The air smelled like minty soap.

“Hello, bathroom!” Pippa chirped. “Today, I make art!”

She had a small basket of bath paints and a sponge shaped like a star. The paints were safe and washable. The labels said, “Just add a little water.”

Pippa turned the faucet on. Whoosh! Water rushed out, fast and loud.

“Splash!” giggled Pippa. “I can paint the wall with bubbles!”

She dipped the sponge, splish-splash, splish-splash, and dabbed blue stars on the white tiles. The stars twinkled when the light shone on the wet paint. Pippa swayed and sang, “Star here, star there, stars for all to share.”

Suddenly, there was a soft tap-tap at the door.

“Pippa?” whispered Tilly Turtle. Tilly moved slowly, with a gentle smile. “Can I make art too? I am slow, but I love colors.”

Pippa bounced. “Yes! Come in, Tilly! We share the bathroom and the paints.”

Tilly scooted in, and her shell gleamed green. She looked at the running water. The faucet still roared. Drip-drip had turned into gurgle-gush.

“Um,” said Tilly. “The water is loud. I feel a little worried. That is a lot of water.”

Pippa blinked. She hadn’t noticed. “Oh! You are right. It is whooshy.” She turned the faucet down to a thin, gentle stream. The sound softened to a happy trickle.

“Thank you,” said Tilly. “I breathe better now.”

“I like to help,” said Pippa. “We can save water too.”

They giggled and dabbed. Tilly patted the sponge slowly. “Plip, plop,” went the paint. Pippa made swirls like wind. Tilly made tiny leaves. The bathroom smelled a little like oranges now, from the orange paint.

Another tap-tap.

“Hi,” murmured Buzzy Bee, peeking in. “May I join? I fly fast, but I can be gentle. I brought a tiny brush.”

Pippa opened her flippers wide. “Yes, Buzzy! This bathroom is for everyone.”

Buzzy buzzed softly, “Bzz-bzz,” and painted tiny yellow dots that looked like seeds. The mural began to look like a garden of stars and leaves and seeds.

Soon, a little shadow slipped by the doorway. It was Sly the shy Seal. He peeked with big eyes.

Pippa smiled and spoke softly. “Hi, Sly. Do you want to help?”

Sly whispered, “I am… messy. I splash too much. Maybe I should watch.”

Pippa waddled closer. “In this bathroom, we include everyone. We can make a plan together. We can make art your way.”

Sly took a deep breath. “Okay.” He flopped into the room and sat on a bath mat that felt squishy and warm.

Pippa thought. This was the problem. Sly worried about mess. The water worried Tilly. The space was small, and Buzzy buzzed fast. How could everyone make art together, kindly and cleanly?

Pippa said, “Let’s have bathroom art rules. Kind rules help everyone.”

“Rule one,” said Tilly, “Turn water off when we don’t need it.”

Pippa nodded and turned the faucet off. The trickle stopped. The room felt calm.

“Rule two,” buzzed Buzzy, “Use little paint at a time. Dip, dab, done!”

Sly raised a flipper. “Rule three… I can use a big soft brush on the lower tiles. I can take slow breaths. If I splash, I wipe.”

Pippa clapped. “Yes! Rule four: We listen. If something feels too loud or too much, we say so. Then we change it.”

They began again. Pippa dipped the star sponge, gently. Tilly made green vines in slow lines. Buzzy dotted tiny flowers. Sly brushed wavy water shapes near the floor. Swish, swish. The paint felt cool and smooth. They shared colors. They took turns. They spoke softly.

“I like your vines,” said Pippa.

“Thank you,” said Tilly. “I like your stars.”

“I hear you,” said Buzzy to Sly. “I will buzz quieter.” Buzzy’s wings hummed like a lullaby.

Sly smiled. “I feel included.”

Soon the bathroom wall showed a whole picture: a garden under a sky full of friendly stars, with a small blue river that Sly made. The river sparkled when a sunbeam peeked in. It looked like the whole world on white tiles.

“Let’s clean the brushes,” said Pippa. She turned the tap on, just a little. They rinsed, rub-rub, and turned the water off again. Pippa wiped drops with a cloth. Sly squeezed the cloth. Drip! The floor was dry.

They stepped back. Their hearts felt warm. The bathroom smelled like soap and oranges. It was quiet and happy.

Pippa said, “We did it together. We saved water. We listened. We included everyone.”

Tilly nodded. “We showed respect.”

Buzzy buzzed, “We made art!”

Sly added, “We understood each other.”

Pippa wrapped a flipper around her friends. “This bathroom is for all of us.”

They hugged. Their mural shone bright.

The End.
