# Miko’s Balcony Garden Notes

Miko was a kind little mouse. He lived in a cozy nest near a sunny balcony garden. The garden smelled like mint and lemon. The pots were full of red tomatoes, purple flowers, and green, tickly leaves.

Every morning Miko squeaked, “Good morning, plants!” He liked to help water them. He liked to count the ladybugs. He liked to listen to the soft buzz of bees.

One warm day, the wind blew hard. Whoosh! The watering can tipped. Plop! Water splashed everywhere. A pot slid. Thump! Soil tumbled onto the floor. A tiny tomato rolled away like a red marble.

“Oh no,” said Miko, his whiskers drooping. He felt his tummy get tight. His paws balled up. “I worked so hard! I am mad.”

He stamped one tiny foot. Stamp. Stamp.

Then Miko stopped. He put his paw on his chest and took a slow breath in. Sniff. He let a slow breath out. Whooo.

“I have big feelings,” Miko said softly. “I can calm down.” He counted, “One… two… three…” His tummy felt softer. His paws opened up.

“I can make a plan,” he said. “I can help. I can write it down.”

Miko loved to write. He had a small blue notebook and a red pencil. The notebook smelled like paper and glue. He opened it to a clean page. The page was smooth under his paw.

On the top he wrote, “Garden Fix Plan.”

He read the words out loud, sounding them slowly. “Gar-den. Fix. Plan.” It made his heart feel steady.

First he wrote, “1. Breathe when mad.” Next he wrote, “2. Pick up soil.” Then, “3. Set pot straight.” Then, “4. Ask for help.” He added a smiley face.

“Good plan,” Miko said. He felt proud. His anger had turned into action.

Just then, his neighbor, a shy snail named Nell, peeked from behind a pot. “Hello, Miko,” Nell whispered. “I can help too.”

“Thank you, Nell,” said Miko. “We can volunteer to help the garden together.” He smiled. “Let’s read the plan.”

Miko read each step, and they did it. They breathed. Sniff. Whooo. They picked up the soft, cool soil. It felt crumbly and damp. They set the pot straight. They rolled the little tomato back to its vine. Miko patted the soil gently. Nell held the stem steady.

“Teamwork!” Miko cheered.

A tiny bird landed on the railing and chirped, “Tweet! Need help?” The bird was Pip, the balcony bird.

“We are helping the garden,” said Miko. “We are volunteers today!”

Pip brought a bit of twine. “Tweet! Tie the stem,” Pip said. Miko wrote, “5. Tie the stem gently.”

They tied the stem soft and snug. The plant stood tall. The balcony felt calm again. The sun warmed their backs. The mint leaves smelled sweet.

Nell smiled. “Your writing helped us.”

Miko felt warm in his heart. “Writing helps me think,” he said. “Writing helps me when I feel mad.”

Miko turned another page. He drew the garden. He drew the pot, the tomato, and the smiling sun. He wrote, “We did it!” in big letters.

Then they heard a tiny squeak. It was a little ant, carrying a crumb. The crumb was too big. The ant slipped. The crumb fell. Plop.

The ant looked sad. Miko’s whiskers twitched. He remembered his own mad feeling. He walked over slowly and said, “Hello, friend. We can help. We are volunteers.”

He opened his notebook and wrote, “Help Plan for Ant.” He read it out loud, “1. Smile. 2. Lift together. 3. Walk slow.”

Miko, Nell, and Pip lifted the crumb. They walked slow. Step. Step. Step. The ant cheered, “Thank you!”

Miko felt happy. He wrote, “We helped the ant!” and drew a tiny heart.

In the afternoon, the wind came back. Whoosh! The leaves shook again. Miko felt a flicker of anger. His tummy got tight for a moment. But he knew what to do.

He put his paw on his chest. He breathed in. Sniff. He breathed out. Whooo. He opened his notebook. He wrote, “Windy Day Plan.”

He wrote, “1. Move pots close. 2. Ask Pip to watch. 3. Read a calm poem.”

“What is a poem?” asked Nell.

“It is little music with words,” said Miko. He wrote a small poem:

“Wind goes whoosh,
plants sway, shush.
Breathe in slow,
let it go.”

Miko read the poem in a soft voice. The words felt like warm tea. Nell relaxed. Pip tucked his wings.

They moved the pots close together. They checked the ties. They shared a laugh when a leaf tickled Pip’s beak. “Tweet! That tickles!”

By evening, the garden was neat and safe. The air smelled like wet soil and lemon leaves. The sky turned pink.

Miko closed his notebook. He patted it gently. “Today I learned,” he said, “When I feel mad, I can breathe. I can write my plan. I can help others. Writing helps my heart and my hands.”

Nell nodded. “Your notes help the garden.”

Pip chirped, “Tweet! Your words help us too.”

Miko smiled. “We can all write. We can all help. We can all be kind.”

They sat together on the balcony garden, listening to the soft buzz and the quiet wind, feeling safe, calm, and proud.

The End.
