# Leo's Beautiful Bird House

Leo the lion cub loved sunny days in his backyard. The warm sunshine tickled his golden fur, and the grass felt soft under his little paws. Today felt extra special because Leo wanted to make something wonderful.

"I'm going to build a bird house!" Leo roared happily, puffing out his fluffy chest. He had seen pretty birds flying around, chirping sweet songs, and he wanted to help them have a cozy home.

Leo gathered sticks and leaves from around the yard. The sticks felt rough and bumpy in his paws, and the leaves rustled with a soft *crinkle-crinkle* sound. He found some colorful flower petals that smelled like honey and vanilla.

"This will be the most beautiful bird house ever!" Leo said, his tail swishing with excitement.

But as Leo worked, he got a little careless. He pulled leaves from plants that were still growing. He broke branches that weren't on the ground. Soon, the pretty garden looked messy and sad.

Leo's mama came outside and saw the scattered plants. "Oh my," she said gently. "Leo, what happened to our garden?"

Leo looked around and his ears drooped. The flowers looked wilted where he had taken their petals. The bushes had broken spots where he had pulled off branches. His tummy felt funny and tight.

"I... I was making a bird house," Leo whispered, his voice very small. "But I think I hurt the plants."

Mama Lion sat down next to Leo and nuzzled his head. "It's wonderful that you want to help the birds, little one. But we need to take care of all living things, including our plants."

Leo's eyes filled with tears. "I'm sorry, Mama. I didn't mean to hurt anything. I just wanted to make something pretty."

"I know, sweetheart," Mama said. "Would you like to learn the right way to craft and help our garden feel better?"

Leo nodded eagerly. Together, they watered the hurt plants with cool, refreshing water. Leo gently patted soil around the roots, whispering, "I'm sorry, little plants. Please feel better."

Then Mama showed Leo how to find materials that had already fallen naturally. "Look," she said, "the wind has given us these perfect twigs, and these leaves fell down all by themselves."

Leo's eyes grew bright. "Oh! So I can use things that nature is ready to share!"

"Exactly!" Mama purred.

Leo carefully collected only the fallen materials. His paws moved slowly and gently. The natural materials felt just as nice - maybe even better because he knew he wasn't hurting anything.

As they crafted together, Leo learned new words. "This is called 'weaving,'" Mama explained as they tucked twigs together. "And this is 'arranging' when we place the soft moss inside."

The bird house turned out beautiful! It had walls of woven twigs, a roof of overlapping leaves, and a soft moss bed inside. Best of all, Leo felt proud because he had made it the right way.

The next morning, Leo heard gentle chirping. A little blue bird was exploring his bird house! "Thank you," the bird seemed to sing.

Leo smiled big and roared softly, "You're welcome! I made it with love and care."

From that day on, Leo always remembered to craft responsibly, using only what nature was ready to share. And the garden grew more beautiful than ever, filled with happy birds and proud little lions.

The End.
