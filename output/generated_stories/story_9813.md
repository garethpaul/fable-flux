# Ella's Warm Kitchen Helpers

In a warm kitchen, sunshine slid across the floor like a golden blanket. Pots hummed softly. A wooden spoon tapped a happy beat. A gentle elephant calf named Ella tip-tapped in on her soft, round feet. Her ears wiggled. Her trunk sniffed. The air smelled like sweet bananas and toasty oatmeal.

“Mama, my tummy says good morning,” Ella giggled, patting her belly.

Mama Elephant smiled, her eyes kind like warm tea. “Good morning, Ella. We will make a strong breakfast. Strong tummies need good food.”

Ella clapped. “I want super-elephant pancakes!”

“Super pancakes can be healthy,” said Mama. “Let’s use oats and banana. We will add blueberries for belly smiles.”

Ella nodded. “Healthy means my body feels good.”

Mama washed her hands. The water ran warm and splishy-splashy. “First, clean hands,” she said.

Ella reached for the soap pump. Squeeze! Too much soap plopped into the sink. Bubbles popped and tickled her trunk. “Oops,” Ella whispered, ears drooping.

Mama chuckled softly. “It’s okay, little one. Oops can be cleaned. Try again.”

Ella took a small pump this time. She rubbed, rubbed, rubbed until her hands were clean and shiny. “I can learn,” she said proudly.

They mixed oats in a bowl. Swish, swish. Banana went in next. Squish! Ella mashed with her trunk, careful and slow. “It smells like banana hugs,” she said, nose wiggling.

Blueberries rolled like tiny marbles. “Plop, plop,” they went into the bowl.

Then the door creaked. In waddled Piku, the tiny parrot from next door. “Hello! Hello! Breakfast? Breakfast?” he chirped, hopping onto the counter.

“Hi, Piku!” Ella smiled. “We’re making super pancakes.”

Piku bounced with excitement. His little feet tapped the spoon. Tap-tap—clatter! The spoon fell, and batter splashed! Splat on Ella’s cheek. Splat on the floor. A blueberry bounced under the table and hid.

“Oh no,” whispered Ella. Her eyes felt watery. She wanted to cry. “My pancakes!”

Piku froze. His feathers drooped. “Sorry. Piku is sorry. I was too bouncy.”

Mama knelt beside Ella and dabbed her cheek with a warm cloth. “Big feelings are okay,” she murmured. “Take a deep breath in. One, two. Now blow out like a gentle wind.”

Ella breathed in. She blew out, soft and slow. Her chest felt calm and light. She looked at Piku. His round eyes were worried.

“I forgive you, Piku,” Ella said. “Accidents happen. We can fix it together.”

Piku’s feathers fluffed with relief. “Thank you. Piku will help.”

“Helping is a healthy habit too,” Mama said. “We clean up, then try again.”

Ella and Piku sang a little clean-up song. “Wipe, wipe, wipe. Sweep, sweep, sweep.” The rag felt warm and damp in Ella’s hand. The floor smelled like lemons. Piku pecked up the runaway blueberry, but Mama smiled and put it in the compost bowl. “We’ll use fresh ones,” she said.

They washed their hands again. Splish, splash. Dry, pat, pat. Ella measured more oats. “Small scoops,” she told herself. Piku watched with quiet feet this time.

Mama heated the pan. It made a gentle ssssss sound. Ella poured small circles of batter. “Little pancakes for little helpers,” she said. Bubbles popped on top, like tiny windows. Flip! The pancakes turned golden and smelled cozy and warm.

“While we wait, let’s drink water,” Mama said, handing cups. “Water helps our bodies wake up.”

Ella sipped. Gulp, gulp. “Ahhh.”

Piku sipped. “Sip-sip! Fresh.”

Soon, the table was ready. Warm pancakes sat with sliced strawberries, a bowl of spinach leaves for a green side, and a cup of yogurt for dipping. “Colors help our bodies,” Mama said. “Red strawberries for hearts, blue blueberries for brains, green leaves for strong muscles.”

Ella poked a spinach leaf. “Do elephants eat leaves?”

“Yes,” said Mama. “Elephants love greens. Try a little bite.”

Ella took a bite. Crunch! It tasted cool and leafy. She dipped pancake pieces into yogurt. Creamy and sweet. “My mouth is happy,” she said.

Piku nibbled a blueberry. “Pop! Blue brain food!”

They ate slowly, chewing well. Between bites, Ella wiped her trunk and sipped water. “This is a healthy habit,” she said. “Wash hands, help clean, eat colorful foods, drink water, and chew slowly.”

Mama beamed. “You are learning wonderful habits, Ella.”

When breakfast was done, Ella placed her plate by the sink. Piku carried his tiny cup carefully. “Thank you for forgiving me,” he chirped. “I will move with gentle feet next time.”

Ella hugged him with her soft trunk. “We all learn. We can try again.”

Mama gave them each a small sticker shaped like a blueberry. “For healthy choices and kind hearts,” she said.

Ella looked around the warm kitchen. It smelled like cinnamon and sunshine. Her tummy felt strong. Her heart felt soft and bright.

She patted her belly and whispered, “Healthy habits help me grow.”

Piku fluffed his wings and echoed, “Healthy and kind!”

They giggled together, ready for a brand-new day.

The End.
