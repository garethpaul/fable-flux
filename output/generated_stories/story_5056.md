# Daisy's Healthy Habits Chart

Little Daisy the deer fawn lived in the coziest nursery in the whole forest. Her room had soft pink walls, fluffy white clouds painted on the ceiling, and a warm, squishy rug that felt like moss under her tiny hooves. But lately, Daisy had been feeling grumpy and tired.

"I don't want to brush my teeth!" Daisy stomped her little hooves on the soft rug. "And I don't want to eat my carrots either!"

Mama Deer peeked into the nursery with worried eyes. "Oh my, Daisy. You seem very upset. What's wrong, little one?"

Daisy flopped down on her bed, making the springs squeak softly. "I forget to do my healthy things, and then I feel yucky. My tummy hurts, and my teeth feel fuzzy. But remembering is too hard!" She buried her face in her pillow that smelled like lavender and fresh air.

Mama Deer sat down gently beside her fawn. "Hmm, that does sound frustrating. But I have an idea that might help. Would you like to make something special together?"

Daisy lifted her head, her big brown eyes curious. "Make something? What kind of something?"

"A healthy habits chart! We can craft it together with colorful paper and stickers. It will help you remember all your healthy habits every day."

Daisy's ears perked up. "That sounds fun! But what if I mess it up? What if I can't do it right?"

"Being brave means trying new things, even when we feel scared," Mama said softly. "And crafting together means we help each other. Come on, let's gather our supplies."

They spread out rainbow-colored paper on Daisy's small table. The paper crinkled pleasantly under their hooves. Mama brought out glittery star stickers that sparkled like dewdrops in the morning sun.

"First, let's think of all your healthy habits," Mama said. "What keeps your body strong and happy?"

Daisy thought hard, tapping her hoof on her chin. "Brushing my teeth makes them clean and white!"

"Excellent!" Mama helped Daisy draw a big tooth with a happy smile.

"And eating my vegetables makes me grow big and strong!" Daisy added, carefully gluing a green paper carrot next to the tooth.

"What about getting enough sleep?" Mama asked.

"Oh yes! Sleep makes me feel good in the morning!" Daisy drew a cozy bed with a fluffy pillow.

They worked together, adding pictures for drinking water, washing hands, and playing outside. Daisy's chart grew more colorful with each healthy habit they added.

"Now," said Mama, "every time you complete a healthy habit, you can put a sparkly star sticker next to it!"

Daisy clapped her hooves together. "This is the most beautiful chart ever! And it will help me remember!"

That evening, Daisy brushed her teeth without being asked. *Sparkle!* went the first star. She ate all her carrots at dinner. *Sparkle!* went the second star. She even washed her hooves before bed. *Sparkle!* went the third star.

"Look, Mama!" Daisy pointed proudly at her shimmering chart. "I remembered all by myself! And I feel so much better!"

"You were very brave to try something new," Mama said, nuzzling Daisy's soft fur. "And look how your healthy habits made you feel strong and happy."

From that day on, Daisy loved using her special chart. When she felt frustrated about remembering her healthy habits, she would look at her beautiful creation and feel brave again. Her healthy habits became as easy as breathing, and she felt wonderful every single day.

The End.
