# Leo Learns to Ask First

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft, golden fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, Leo padded into the creative art room. The room smelled like sweet glue and orange paint. Sunlight danced on shelves filled with crayons, brushes, and jars of glitter. Paper fluttered like butterfly wings on the tables.

“Wow,” Leo whispered, his whiskers twitching. “So many colors!”

Ms. Panda, their gentle teacher, clapped her furry paws. “Today, we will make a big friendship collage,” she said. “We will cut, paste, paint, and share. Remember: Use kind hands, kind words, and ask first.”

Leo’s tail swished with excitement. He loved to help. He loved to be brave and try new things. He ran to a table where his friends sat. There was Tilly the turtle, who moved slowly and smiled softly. There was Max the monkey, who bounced and giggled. And there was Noor the nightingale, who hummed a happy tune.

On the table were colored papers—red like apples, blue like the sky, and green like fresh grass. A glue stick rolled gently. Paint brushes rested in small cups of paint—swish, swish went the water jar.

“I want to make a shining sun!” Leo said. He grabbed a bright yellow circle he saw near Tilly’s hand.

Tilly pulled back her little flipper. “Oh,” she said in a tiny voice. “I was using that.”

Leo froze. His paws felt warm. He looked at Tilly’s wide eyes. They looked a bit watery. “I’m sorry,” he said quickly, placing the circle down. “I thought it was for everyone.”

Ms. Panda came closer. “Thank you for saying sorry, Leo,” she said softly. “Let’s remember to ask first. Our things have places. Our bodies have spaces.”

“Our bodies have spaces,” Leo echoed, thinking hard. He looked around. Max was cutting paper into a star shape—snip, snip. Noor was painting soft feathers—brush, brush. Tilly was gluing a shell shape onto the collage—tap, tap.

Leo wanted to help. He wanted to be kind and brave. He took a deep breath and tried again. “Tilly,” he asked, “may I use a yellow circle too?”

Tilly smiled and nodded. “Yes. There are more in the basket.”

“Thanks!” Leo said. He found another yellow circle and pressed it onto the paper. Sticky glue made a funny smell. His paws felt cool and squishy as he smoothed the paper down.

Soon, Leo heard Noor humming, “La, la, la,” while she painted. Her wings were close to the edge of the table. Leo reached over to move her cup of water so it wouldn’t spill.

“Wait,” Noor said gently. “Please ask before you touch my things.”

Leo’s ears drooped. “Oh! I was trying to help,” he said.

“I know,” Noor chirped. “Thank you for wanting to help. Please ask first. I like it when I can say yes or no.”

Leo nodded. “May I move your water so it is safe?”

“Yes,” Noor said. “Thank you for asking.”

Leo moved the cup. It made a tiny clink. “I asked first,” he whispered to himself. He felt a warm, proud glow in his chest.

Max was bouncing with a long strip of tape. “Look at my sticky road!” Max giggled. The tape stuck to Leo’s tail—zip!

Leo blinked. He felt a tug. “Max,” he said, “please don’t stick tape on my tail.”

Max stopped and tilted his head. “Oh! Sorry, Leo. I was silly.”

“It’s okay,” Leo said. “My tail is part of my body. My body has space. Please ask before you touch me.”

Max nodded. “I will ask next time.”

Ms. Panda smiled. “You are all learning about understanding,” she said. “We listen. We ask. We help in kind ways.”

Leo’s heart felt big and brave. He looked at the collage. It was almost done, but there was a blank corner. “Friends,” he said, “may I add my sun here?” He pointed to the empty space.

Tilly looked. Noor peered. Max leaned in. “Yes,” they said together. “That’s a perfect spot.”

Leo pressed the sun into the corner. Then he saw Tilly struggling with a glue cap. Her flippers slipped—twist, twist—no luck.

Leo remembered. Ask first. “Tilly,” he said softly, “may I help you open the glue?”

Tilly sighed with relief. “Yes, please.”

Leo held the cap and turned—pop! “Here you go.”

“Thank you,” Tilly said. “You asked first. That felt nice.”

The collage sparkled with stars, feathers, shells, and a big bright sun. Ms. Panda hung it up high. Everyone stepped back. The room smelled like wet paint and orange peel. The picture shone in the sunshine.

Ms. Panda clapped. “What did we learn today?”

Leo raised his paw. “We learned understanding,” he said. “We ask before we take. We ask before we help. We ask before we touch. Our things have places. Our bodies have spaces.”

Tilly nodded. “Asking makes me feel safe.”

Noor chirped, “Asking lets me say yes or no.”

Max grinned. “Asking helps me be a good friend.”

Leo felt proud and brave. He looked at his friends. “I like asking first,” he said. “It is kind. It is brave.”

Ms. Panda smiled warmly. “When we understand each other, we can help each other. That is how we make beautiful things—like our collage and our friendships.”

They all held up their paws, wings, and flippers and cheered. In the creative art room, the colors seemed even brighter, and everyone’s hearts felt light and cozy.

The End.
