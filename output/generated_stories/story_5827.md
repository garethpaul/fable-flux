# Remy Rabbit Leads the Shed Team

Remy was a determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved to hop, listen, and learn. One sunny morning, Remy peeked into the garden shed. The wooden door creaked, “Creeeeak,” and the smell of fresh soil and sweet hay tickled his nose.

Inside the shed were friendly helpers. There was Dot the duck, who waddled and wiggled. There was Tilly the tiny turtle, slow and steady. There was Buzz the bumblebee, who hummed, “Bzzzz,” by the window. Shelves held bright red buckets, green watering cans, and shiny tools that glinted in the sun.

“Hello, friends!” Remy said, ears up. “Let’s clean the shed and make a play space. We can do it together.”

Dot flapped. “Quack, I want to splash in the water first!”

Buzz zipped. “Bzzz, I want to fly in circles!”

Tilly blinked. “I want to nap,” she whispered.

Remy took a deep breath. He remembered what Mama Rabbit taught him: A good leader listens, shares a plan, and shows respect. He wiggled his whiskers and smiled.

“I hear you,” Remy said kindly. “We all want fun. If we work together, we can be safe and fast. I have a plan. Would you like to hear it?”

Dot, Tilly, and Buzz nodded.

“Okay,” Remy said. “Step one: We move the light things first. Step two: We put sharp things high, on the shelf. Step three: We make a clear path so we can hop, waddle, and roll without bumping.”

He pointed to a picture chart he drew in chalk on the floor: a little bucket for step one, a safe shelf for step two, and a path for step three. The chalk felt dusty on his paws, and the floor felt cool and smooth.

“That looks smart,” Buzz hummed.

“Quack, I can carry the buckets!” Dot said.

“I can push this small box,” Tilly said, pressing gently.

Remy clapped his paws. “Great! I will lead the line. Dot, you go behind me with the buckets. Tilly, you follow Dot. Buzz, please watch from above and tell us if the path is clear. We will use slow, safe moves. We will follow directions. Ready?”

“Ready!” they cheered.

Remy stood tall, even though he was small. He led with calm steps. “Hop, stop. Hop, stop,” he said, making a steady rhythm.

Dot waddled behind. “Waddle, waddle, quack.”

Tilly pushed softly. “Slide… slide…”

Buzz hovered. “Bzzz—Clear to the left. Bzzz—Watch the rake!”

Remy nodded. “Thank you, Buzz.” He stopped at the rake and said, “Sharp things go high.” He looked at everyone. “We will lift together. One, two, three.”

They counted. “One, two, three!” Together they lifted the rake and put it on the top shelf. The metal felt cool and a little heavy, but their teamwork made it safe.

Remy noticed Dot was getting excited. She wiggled fast and splashed a tiny puddle from a watering can.

“Dot,” Remy said gently, “I see you love water. We will have splash time after we finish step three. Can you help by closing the lids?”

Dot took a big breath. “Okay, Remy. I can wait.” She pressed the lids tight. “Click.”

Remy turned to Tilly. “How are you doing?”

Tilly smiled slowly. “I am steady and strong. But I need a small rest.”

“A good leader makes time for rests,” Remy said. “Let’s take a sip of water and stretch.”

They stretched tall like sunflowers. They wiggled fingers and toes. They took three deep breaths in the grassy, earthy air. In, out. In, out. In, out.

“Back to the plan,” Remy said. “We are almost done!”

They moved the last small box. Buzz called, “The path is clear now! Bzzz-zz!”

Sunlight spilled across the floor. The shed looked neat and bright. There was a wide, open space where they could move and play.

Remy smiled. “Great work, team! We followed directions. We showed respect. We stayed safe. Now it’s time for fun.”

He set up a little game. On the floor, he drew three shapes with chalk: a circle, a square, and a triangle.

“Game time!” Remy said. “When I say ‘circle,’ we hop in a circle. When I say ‘square,’ we march in straight lines. When I say ‘triangle,’ we take three tiny steps and touch our noses.”

He looked at his friends. “I will lead the first round. Then each of you can be the leader.”

“Circle!” Remy called.

They hopped around. Hop, hop, hop! Their feet made soft taps. They giggled.

“Square!” he called.

They marched with stiff little legs. March, march, march!

“Triangle!”

They took three tiny steps. One, two, three! Then they touched their noses. Boop!

“Now Dot is the leader,” Remy said. He stepped back and listened.

Dot stood tall. “Quack—Square!”

They marched again, and Remy followed with respect. He did not talk over Dot. He watched and waited for the next direction.

“Buzz is next,” Dot said.

“Bzzz—Triangle!” Buzz hummed, and they took three tiny steps together.

“Tilly’s turn,” Remy said.

Tilly lifted her head. “Circle,” she whispered.

They hopped slowly, matching Tilly’s speed, so no one was left behind. Remy felt proud. He felt warm in his chest, like sunshine.

When the game ended, Remy gathered his friends. “We did it,” he said. “We cleaned the shed, we moved safely, and we took turns being leaders. A good leader listens, makes a plan, helps friends, and shows respect. A good team follows directions and cares for each other.”

Dot quacked happily. “And we had fun!”

Buzz buzzed softly. “Bzzz—Together is best.”

Tilly nodded. “I felt included.”

Remy looked around the tidy shed. It smelled sweet and clean. The tools were safe. The path was clear. His friends were smiling.

“Today,” Remy said, “we learned to lead with kindness.”

They all cheered, and the garden birds sang along outside.

The End.
