# Della the Dolphin Discovers the Pantry

Della the Dolphin was curious and bouncy. She loved to splash, twirl, and explore. One rainy morning, Della slid through a shiny silver doorway and found herself in a brand-new place.

“Whoa,” Della whispered. “What is this cozy room?”

It was the pantry. The air smelled like cinnamon and warm bread. Soft light glowed on tall shelves. Jars clicked gently when the house hummed. Della’s flippers tingled with excitement.

“I discovered a pantry!” she chirped. “What will I learn here?”

Della peeked at a row of jars. They were glassy and smooth. She pressed her nose to one. Tap, tap. It sounded like a tiny drum.

“Hello, jar,” Della giggled. “What’s your name?”

“I’m Oats,” said a whispery voice from inside. “I help make warm breakfasts.”

“Nice to meet you, Oats!” said Della. She swished her tail and looked up. A soft bag hung on a hook. The bag smelled sweet and sunny.

“I’m Flour,” the bag rustled. “I like to puff and float!”

“Hi, Flour!” Della laughed. “You smell like cookies.”

Farther along, a jar of rainbow sprinkles sparkled like tiny stars. “We’re Sprinkles,” they chimed. “We make things colorful!”

Della’s eyes shone. “This pantry is full of treasures,” she said. “I want to make a snack. I want to learn how.”

She felt her heart beat in a happy rhythm. Pat-pat, pat-pat. She set a little goal right there. “Goal one,” she said, holding up a flipper. “Find safe, simple things. Goal two: Mix them. Goal three: Make a tasty, tiny snack!”

“Good plan,” hummed a round jar. “I’m Peanut Butter. I’m sticky and strong.”

“I’m Banana,” smiled a yellow bunch in a fruit bowl by the door. “I’m soft and sweet.”

Della nodded. “I can do this. I can build skills. I will go slow.”

She practiced taking deep belly breaths. In… and out. The pantry smelled like peanut and banana and comfort.

“First, I need tools,” said Della. She looked around and spotted a small plastic bowl and a safe, soft spoon in a low drawer.

“Skill step!” Della cheered. She carefully pulled the drawer. Squeak. She lifted the spoon. Light and smooth. She held the bowl. Cool and round.

“Next, ingredients,” she said. She checked labels with big letters. “Banana. Peanut Butter. Oats.”

“Choose me!” sang Oats.

Della giggled. “Yes, Oats. You can help make chewy bites.”

She placed the bowl on the floor. Clink. She peeled the banana. Peel, strip, swoosh. The banana smelled like sunshine. She mashed it with the spoon. Smush, smush. It felt soft and mushy.

“Good mashing,” said Banana kindly.

“Thank you,” said Della. “Now a scoop of Peanut Butter.”

She reached for the jar. It was a little heavy. Uh-oh. Della paused. “This is tricky,” she said. “But I have a plan. I will ask for help.”

A friendly voice floated from the doorway. “Do you need a hand?” It was Nana, with warm eyes and flour on her cheek.

“Yes, please,” said Della. “I’m learning. My goal is to make a snack. I can scoop, but I need help opening.”

Nana smiled. “Asking for help is a smart skill.” Pop! The lid came off. The pantry filled with a nutty, cozy smell.

Della scooped a small blob. Plop. Into the bowl it went. She sprinkled some oats. Pitter-patter like soft rain. Then she stirred. Swirl, swirl. The spoon went round and round. The mixture became thick and happy.

“My flippers are getting strong,” said Della. “I am practicing. Stirring is a skill!”

“You’re doing great,” said Nana. “What’s your next step?”

Della checked her list in her head. “Goal three: Make a tiny snack.” She rolled little balls with the spoon and her flipper. Roll, roll. They felt squishy and cool. She added a few rainbow sprinkles on top. Tinkle, tinkle. The sprinkles shone like tiny confetti.

“Ta-da!” Della said. “I discovered how to make no-bake bites!”

They tasted together. Mmm. Sweet banana, smooth peanut, gentle oats. The bites were soft and a little chewy.

“How do you feel?” asked Nana.

Della’s eyes sparkled. “Proud. I learned new words. I used my senses. I set a goal. I practiced skills. I asked for help. And I made something good!”

The pantry seemed to glow. The jars and bags rustled happy sounds. The air was warm and safe. Della washed the spoon and bowl with bubbly water. Swish, swish, swish. She wiped the floor with a soft cloth. Wipe, wipe.

“Another skill,” she said. “Cleaning up!”

Della looked around. She had started with a question. She had discovered answers by exploring, trying, and listening. She patted the bowl. “Thank you, Pantry,” she whispered. “You are full of discoveries.”

Nana nodded. “Discovery helps our brains grow. Setting goals and practicing skills make big jobs feel small.”

Della beamed. “Tomorrow, I will set a new goal. Today, I discovered how to make a snack. I can learn a little more each day.”

She gave a playful twirl. The sprinkles winked. The oats hummed. The pantry door made a soft click as they left, leaving sweet smells behind.

And Della’s heart felt bright and brave.

The End.
