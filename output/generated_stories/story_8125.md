# Whiskers and the Windy Window

In a quiet attic at the top of a small house, a clever cat named Whiskers liked to explore. The attic smelled like old books and sweet cedar. Dusty sunbeams floated in the air like tiny glitter. Boxes made soft mountains. A round window looked out over trees and roofs. Sometimes the window made a little whistle sound when the wind blew, like “whoooo.”

One calm morning, Whiskers found a jingly ball. It was red and shiny. “Perfect,” purred Whiskers. “Let’s play.”

Whiskers batted the ball with a paw. Jingle-jingle! The ball rolled across the wooden floor. Tap, tap, tap. It zoomed under a big box and got stuck. “Oh,” said Whiskers, peeking under. The space was small and dark. Only whiskers could fit.

Whiskers reached a paw in. “I can do it,” whispered Whiskers. The ball did not move. Whiskers pushed harder. The box did not budge. The jingle stayed quiet.

Whiskers’ ears went back. A warm feeling rose in Whiskers’ chest. The tail twitched. “I want my ball!” said Whiskers, a little louder. Whiskers scratched at the box. Scritch-scritch. The ball did not come out.

“Whoooo,” sang the window. The sound tickled Whiskers’ ears. “Not now, wind,” said Whiskers. “I’m busy.”

Whiskers tried again and again. Paw, push, scritch. No ball. The warm feeling grew hotter and bigger. Whiskers’ eyes felt watery. The clever cat did not feel clever now.

“I feel mad,” said Whiskers. “I feel stuck.” A little growl came out. “Grrr.”

Just then, a friendly moth fluttered in a sunbeam. Flitter-flitter. “Hello, Whiskers,” the moth hummed softly. “You look upset.”

Whiskers sniffed. “My ball is under the box. It won’t come out. I tried and tried. I feel so frustrated.”

The moth landed on the corner of a book. “Frus-tra-ted,” buzzed the moth gently. “That’s a big feeling. Big feelings need small steps.”

“Small steps?” asked Whiskers, blinking.

“Yes,” said the moth. “First, pause.” The moth folded its wings. “Let’s breathe.”

Whiskers sat down. The floor felt cool. The attic smelled like cinnamon and dust. Whiskers put a paw on the chest. “In,” said Whiskers, breathing in slowly. 1…2…3. “Out,” whispered Whiskers, breathing out slowly. 1…2…3.

The warm, hot feeling cooled a little. The tail stopped twitching. The ears relaxed. “Again?” asked the moth.

“In,” breathed Whiskers. 1…2…3. “Out.” 1…2…3.

Whiskers felt calmer. “Thank you,” said Whiskers. “Now I can think.”

“Second,” hummed the moth, “use kind words for yourself.”

Whiskers nodded. “It’s okay to feel mad. I can try again. I can ask for help.”

“Third,” the moth said, “make a plan.”

Whiskers looked around the quiet attic. There was a broom by the door. There was a soft blanket in a basket. There was a thick book under the window. “I will not scratch the box,” said Whiskers. “The box is old. I will be gentle.”

Whiskers picked up the broom with careful paws. “Excuse me, broom,” said Whiskers. “Can you help?”

The broom did not answer, but it was long. Whiskers slid the broom under the box and nudged, very slowly. Tap…tap…tap. “I am moving the ball,” said Whiskers, peeking. A tiny jingle sounded. Jingle…

“Almost,” whispered the moth.

Whiskers nudged again, gentle and steady. Jingle-jingle! The red ball rolled out from under the box. Whiskers’ eyes grew bright. “I did it!” cheered Whiskers.

Whiskers pounced and caught the ball. “Thank you, moth,” said Whiskers. “Breathing helped. A plan helped.”

“Whoooo,” sang the window again, a little louder now. Whiskers turned to the round window. A thin crack was open. Wind brushed Whiskers’ whiskers and made the fur ruffle. It felt tickly and cool.

Whiskers walked to the window and put a paw up, then paused. “Window,” said Whiskers kindly, “I like your song. But it is too loud for me right now. I need quiet.” Whiskers pushed the crack closed, very gently. Click. The whoooo became a tiny whisper.

“Thank you,” said Whiskers to the window. “I set a boundary.” The moth nodded. “You told the wind what you need,” the moth hummed. “That is a peaceful solution.”

Whiskers rolled the ball again. Jingle-jingle! Then Whiskers stopped and looked at the old box. It looked a little wobbly. “Box,” said Whiskers, “I will not bump you hard. I will play in a safe space.” Whiskers moved the blanket to make a soft play spot away from the box and the window. “This is my play area,” said Whiskers. “This is safe.”

The moth flittered in a happy circle. “Small steps, calm breaths, gentle choices,” it hummed.

Whiskers purred. The purr was low and warm, like a tiny drum. “Today I learned: When I feel frustrated, I can stop, breathe in and out, use kind words, and make a plan. I can set boundaries. I can choose peaceful solutions.”

The attic felt cozy again. The sunbeams looked like golden ribbons. Whiskers curled on the blanket, the jingly ball tucked close.

“I feel proud,” said Whiskers softly.

“And calm,” whispered the moth.

Jingle. Purr. Quiet.

The End.
