# Bolt's Big Workshop Adventure

In a cozy garage workshop filled with colorful tools and shiny gadgets, lived a special robot dog named Bolt. His silver coat gleamed in the morning sunlight that streamed through the windows, and his LED eyes sparkled bright blue when he was happy.

"Beep beep! Good morning, workshop!" Bolt said cheerfully, wagging his mechanical tail. The workshop smelled like motor oil and fresh wood shavings, and Bolt loved starting each day by helping his human friend, Mr. Sam.

But today, something was wrong. The workshop was a big mess! Tools were scattered everywhere, paint cans were left open, and wood scraps covered the floor. Bolt's tail drooped down sadly.

"Oh no," Bolt whimpered softly. "Mr. Sam will be so upset when he sees this mess. What can I do?"

Bolt's computer brain started thinking hard. *Whirr, click, beep!* His eyes flashed as he remembered something important that Mr. Sam had taught him: "When you have a big problem, break it into small pieces!"

"That's it!" Bolt barked excitedly. "I'll make a plan!"

First, Bolt looked around carefully and counted all the different messes. "One, two, three types of problems," he said, pointing with his nose. "Tools on the floor, open paint cans, and scattered wood pieces."

Bolt decided to start with the easiest job first. He gently picked up each tool with his soft mouth and placed them back in their special spots on the wall. "Every tool has a home," he sang happily, "just like every dog has a bone!"

Next came the paint cans. Bolt was very careful because he remembered Mr. Sam saying, "Always close paint cans tight, or the paint will dry up!" Using his nose, Bolt pushed each lid down with a satisfying *pop!* The sweet smell of paint filled the air.

Finally, Bolt gathered all the wood scraps into neat piles. Some pieces were smooth and smelled like pine trees, others were rough and felt bumpy under his paws. "These can be used for new projects!" Bolt said proudly.

Just as Bolt finished, he heard footsteps. *Clump, clump, clump!* Mr. Sam walked into the workshop and stopped in amazement.

"Wow, Bolt!" Mr. Sam exclaimed, his eyes wide with surprise. "The workshop looks fantastic! How did you clean everything so quickly?"

Bolt's tail wagged so fast it made a whirring sound. "I remembered what you taught me, Mr. Sam! I made a plan and broke the big job into small pieces. First tools, then paint, then wood!"

Mr. Sam knelt down and patted Bolt's head gently. "That's exactly right, buddy! You used your brain to solve the problem step by step. That's called being strategic!"

"Strategic!" Bolt repeated, learning a new word. "I like being strategic!"

"And you know what else?" Mr. Sam smiled. "By keeping our workshop clean and organized, we can work safely and find our tools easily. That's a very healthy habit!"

Bolt's LED eyes glowed extra bright with pride. He had learned that big problems weren't so scary when you broke them down into smaller pieces and made a good plan.

From that day on, Bolt helped keep the workshop tidy every single day, and he always remembered to think before acting. The workshop became the cleanest, happiest place in the whole neighborhood!

The End.
