# Penny's Bathroom Art Adventure

Penny the kangaroo joey bounced into the bright bathroom. The white tiles felt cool under her tiny paws. She loved bath time, but today felt different. Today, she had a big idea!

"Mama!" Penny called out. "Can we make something special for bath time?"

Mama Kangaroo hopped in, carrying fluffy towels. "What did you have in mind, little one?"

Penny's eyes sparkled. "I want to make colorful bath paints! But I need help. I can't reach the high shelf."

"That sounds wonderful!" Mama said. "Let's work together. What do we need?"

Penny thought hard. "We need soap, food coloring, and bowls. But there's a problem." She pointed up high. "The soap is way up there, and the bowls are in the kitchen."

"Hmm," Mama said, tapping her chin. "This sounds like a job for teamwork. What if we each take different tasks?"

Penny's tail wiggled with excitement. "Yes! You're tall, so you can get the soap. I'll hop to the kitchen for bowls!"

"Perfect plan!" Mama reached up and grabbed the gentle baby soap. It smelled like lavender flowers. "I'll meet you back here."

Penny bounced to the kitchen. She carefully carried three small bowls, one at a time. The plastic bowls were light and colorful - red, blue, and yellow.

Back in the bathroom, they set everything on the counter. The afternoon sun streamed through the window, making everything glow.

"Now what?" Mama asked.

"We squeeze soap into each bowl," Penny explained. "Then we add different colors!" She tried to squeeze the soap bottle, but her little paws couldn't make it work.

"Let me help with that," Mama offered. "You can tell me how much to squeeze."

"A little bit... a little more... perfect!" Penny directed. The white soap plopped into each bowl with soft splashing sounds.

Next came the fun part. Mama held the tiny food coloring bottles while Penny chose the colors.

"Three drops of red in this bowl," Penny said carefully. "Blue in that one, and yellow in the last one!"

They stirred each bowl together. Penny used a small spoon while Mama used a bigger one. The soap transformed into beautiful, swirling colors.

"Look, Mama! We made rainbow paint!" Penny clapped her paws together.

"We did it by working as a team," Mama smiled. "You had the creative idea, I helped with the tall reaches, and we both did the mixing."

Penny filled the bathtub with warm, bubbly water. Steam rose up like little clouds. She dipped her paws in the red paint and made handprints on the tub walls.

"This is the most fun bath ever!" she giggled. "And we made it together!"

Mama painted a big heart with the blue paint. "When we cooperate and communicate, we can create amazing things."

Penny swirled yellow paint in the water, watching it dance and twirl. "I love how we shared the work. It made everything easier and more fun!"

They painted and splashed until the bathroom walls were covered in colorful, soapy art. The warm water felt wonderful, and the bathroom smelled like lavender and creativity.

"Thank you for helping me, Mama," Penny said as they cleaned up together. "My idea was good, but it was even better when we worked together."

"That's the magic of cooperation, sweet joey," Mama said, wrapping Penny in a soft towel. "When we communicate and help each other, we can make wonderful things happen."

Penny looked at their beautiful, colorful bathroom art and smiled. Tomorrow, she already had another idea for something they could create together.

The End.
