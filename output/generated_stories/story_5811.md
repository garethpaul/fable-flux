# Ollie's Organized Adventure

Ollie the otter loved to play in the garden shed. The wooden walls smelled like fresh dirt and flowers. Sunlight peeked through the dusty windows, making everything sparkle.

But today, Ollie had a big problem. His friend Rabbit was coming to play at three o'clock. Mama Otter had given him a list of chores to finish first.

"Clean your toy corner, put away the garden tools, and sweep the floor," Mama had said. "Then you can play with Rabbit."

Ollie looked at the messy shed. Toy boats floated in puddles. Shovels and rakes lay everywhere. The floor was covered with leaves and mud.

"This will take forever!" Ollie groaned. He felt worried in his furry tummy.

The big clock on the wall said twelve o'clock. Ollie had three whole hours! But instead of starting his chores, he decided to play with his favorite rubber ball.

Bounce, bounce, bounce! The ball hit the shed walls. Ollie giggled and chased it around the messy room.

Soon the clock said one o'clock. Ollie's tummy felt funny. He started to worry. "I should start cleaning," he thought. But the ball looked so fun!

Bounce, bounce, splash! The ball landed in a puddle. Ollie dove in after it, getting his whiskers all wet and muddy.

When Ollie looked up, the clock said two o'clock! Only one hour left!

"Oh no!" Ollie squeaked. His heart beat fast like a tiny drum. "Rabbit will be here soon, and I haven't done anything!"

Ollie felt scared, but he remembered what Papa Otter always said: "When you feel scared, take a deep breath and be brave."

Ollie took a big, deep breath. The shed smelled like wet earth and old wood. He felt a little calmer.

"I can do this!" Ollie said out loud. His voice sounded stronger now.

First, he gathered all his toy boats. One, two, three, four! He put them in their special box. The corner looked much better already.

Next, Ollie picked up the garden tools. The rake was heavy, but he used both paws. The shovel was muddy, but he wiped it clean with an old rag.

"I'm doing it!" Ollie cheered. He felt proud and strong.

Finally, Ollie swept the floor. Swoosh, swoosh went the broom. Leaves and dirt flew into a neat pile. The wooden floor began to shine.

Just as Ollie finished, he heard a knock on the shed door.

"Ollie! It's me, Rabbit!" called a friendly voice.

Ollie opened the door with a big smile. The shed sparkled behind him. Everything was clean and organized.

"Wow!" said Rabbit, hopping inside. "Your shed looks amazing! How did you clean it so fast?"

Ollie's chest puffed up with pride. "I was scared I wouldn't finish in time. But I took a deep breath, followed Mama's directions, and worked really hard!"

"You're so brave and organized!" Rabbit said. "Now we have a beautiful clean place to play!"

Ollie and Rabbit spent the afternoon playing with the toy boats and building block towers. The clean shed made everything more fun.

That evening, Mama Otter hugged Ollie tight. "I'm so proud of you," she said. "You learned that when we manage our time well and follow directions, we can do anything!"

Ollie smiled sleepily. Tomorrow he would remember: start early, be brave, and follow the plan. That's how to get things done!

The End.
