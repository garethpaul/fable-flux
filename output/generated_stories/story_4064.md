# Melody's Messy Treehouse

High up in a big oak tree lived a cheerful yellow bird named Melody. She loved to sing beautiful songs that made all the forest animals smile. Melody had built herself a cozy treehouse with twigs, leaves, and soft moss.

"Tweet-tweet-trill!" Melody sang happily as the morning sun warmed her feathers. But when she looked around her treehouse, she frowned. Scattered everywhere were old seeds, dirty feathers, and muddy twigs. Her home was very messy!

Just then, Melody heard a gentle knock on her treehouse door. "May I come in?" asked a soft voice. It was Ruby, a bright red cardinal who had just moved to the neighborhood.

"Oh no!" Melody thought. "My treehouse is too messy for a visitor!" But she didn't want to be rude. "Um, maybe you could come back later?" Melody called through the door.

Ruby's feelings were hurt. "Did I do something wrong?" she wondered sadly. She flew away, feeling unwelcome.

The next day, Ruby tried again. This time she brought some fresh berries to share. "Hello, Melody! I brought a friendship gift!"

Melody peeked through a crack in her door. She really wanted to make friends with Ruby, but she felt embarrassed about her messy home. "I'm... I'm too busy right now," Melody said quietly.

Ruby flew away again, even sadder than before. She began to think that Melody didn't like her because she was a different color bird.

On the third day, Melody watched Ruby sitting alone on a branch, looking very lonely. Melody's heart felt heavy. She realized she was being unkind by not welcoming her new neighbor.

"This isn't right," Melody said to herself. "Ruby seems very nice. I should talk to her honestly."

Melody took a deep breath and flew over to Ruby. "Ruby, I'm sorry I haven't been friendly. The truth is, my treehouse is very messy, and I was embarrassed to let you see it."

Ruby's eyes lit up with understanding. "Oh, Melody! I thought you didn't like me because I look different from you."

"Different?" Melody gasped. "I think your red feathers are beautiful! I was just worried about my messy home."

Ruby smiled warmly. "You know what? I love organizing and cleaning! Maybe we could work together to tidy up your treehouse?"

"Really? You would help me?" Melody felt so grateful.

"Of course! Friends help each other," Ruby chirped happily.

The two birds flew back to Melody's treehouse. Ruby wasn't bothered by the mess at all. Together, they made a plan. First, they would throw away old, moldy seeds. Then they would organize the good seeds in neat piles. Finally, they would sweep out the dirty feathers and replace them with fresh, clean ones.

"Let's take our time and do it right," Ruby suggested wisely.

As they worked, Melody sang cheerful songs, and Ruby hummed along. The treehouse began to smell fresh and look beautiful. They washed their wings and beaks in a nearby stream to stay clean while working.

When they finished, Melody's treehouse sparkled! "Thank you so much, Ruby. I learned something important today."

"What's that?" Ruby asked.

"I learned that being different makes us special, not scary. And I learned that talking honestly about problems is much better than avoiding them," Melody said. "Will you be my friend?"

"I would love to be your friend!" Ruby sang joyfully.

From that day on, Melody and Ruby were the best of friends. They kept the treehouse clean together, shared meals, and sang beautiful duets that filled the whole forest with harmony.

The End.
