# Sunny Sings in the Living Room

Sunny was a small, singing bird with bright yellow feathers and a tiny blue scarf. Sunny lived with Mia and Max in a playful living room. The room had a soft, bouncy rug, a red couch, and a basket of toys that jingled and rattled. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. The air smelled like fresh crayons and soap.

“Good morning!” chirped Sunny. “Chirp-chirp! Today I will sing a new song.”

Mia clapped. “I can’t wait, Sunny!”

Max wiggled his toes on the rug. “Your songs make my feet dance!”

Sunny puffed up with happy feathers. “I have a big note to sing. It goes laaaa!” Sunny opened their beak wide. “La—” But the note came out small and squeaky. “Li…eep.”

Sunny blinked. “Oh. That was not the big note.”

Mia smiled kindly. “It’s okay. New notes need practice.”

Sunny nodded. “I can practice. My voice needs warm-ups. Like tiny stretches!” Sunny took a breath that smelled like vanilla cookies from the kitchen. “Loo-loo, la-la, lee-lee,” Sunny sang softly.

Max shook a jingle ball. It went shaka-shaka. “I can help. Do you want the music loud or soft?”

Sunny fluffed their feathers and said, “Soft, please. I need to hear my voice.” Sunny used a brave voice. “I can say what I need.” Sunny smiled. “That’s self-advocacy!”

Max grinned and shook the jingle ball very softly. Shika-shika.

Sunny tried again. “La—” The note wobbled like jelly. Sunny felt a little twist in their tummy. “I feel nervous,” Sunny said. “My tummy feels squiggly.”

Mia nodded. “I feel squiggly sometimes too. Let’s take slow breaths. In and out.”

Sunny breathed in. The living room smelled like the lemon plant by the window. Sunny breathed out. “I can do hard things if I practice.”

Sunny sang, “Loo-loo. La-la. Lee-lee.” The sounds bounced off the red couch and came back gentle. Sunny felt calmer.

Max held up a soft pillow like a stage. “You can stand here, Sunny!”

Sunny hopped onto the pillow. “Thank you, friends.” Sunny looked at their tiny feet. “I will try the big note again.”

From the hallway, the family cat, Whiskers, padded in. Whiskers’ fur was silky. Whiskers’s bell collar went tinkle-tinkle. Whiskers curled on the rug and purred, prrrr.

Sunny giggled. “That is a nice bass note, Whiskers.”

Sunny sang the song quietly first. “Morning sun, hello to you. Chirp and cheer, the sky is blue.” Sunny’s voice sounded sweet and light. The words felt smooth, like sliding on the rug.

Mia whispered, “Beautiful.”

Sunny smiled. “Now the big note.” Sunny took a deep breath. “La—” The note was bigger but still a little shaky. “La…aah.” Sunny stopped. “I need more practice.”

Max gave a thumbs-up. “Practice time!”

Sunny made a plan. “Step one: warm up. Step two: drink water.” Sunny sipped from a tiny cup, cool on their beak. “Step three: try again.”

They practiced in short turns. Sunny sang “la” soft, then medium, then a bit louder. Mia counted, “One, two, three,” in a gentle voice. Max kept the jingle soft and steady. Whiskers purred like a tiny drum.

Sunny paused. “Friends, can we make it even quieter? I want to hear my breath.”

Mia clicked off the noisy fan. The room grew still. Sunny could hear a clock tick-tick and the faraway hum of a car.

“Thank you,” Sunny said. “I feel ready.” Sunny raised their wings a little. “La—” The note slid, but this time Sunny caught it. “Laaaaa!” It rang warm and clear, like sunshine on the floor. Sunny’s feathers tingled with joy.

Mia twirled. “You did it!”

Max hopped. “You practiced and you did it!”

Sunny laughed, a bright chirp. “Practice helps me grow.” Sunny looked at their friends. “Thank you for helping. And thank you for listening when I said what I needed.”

Mia hugged a pillow. “That is friendship. We help each other.”

Max nodded. “And you spoke up. That’s self-advocacy!”

Sunny fluttered down to the rug. “I want to sing the whole song now.”

The living room felt cozy and kind. The sunlight made everything golden. Sunny began, clear and proud:

“Morning sun, hello to you.
Chirp and cheer, the sky is blue.
Warm my wings, help me grow.
Practice, practice, watch me go!
Friends beside me, kind and true,
I find my voice because of you.”

Sunny held the big note again. “Laaaaa!” It flowed like warm honey. When the note ended, the room was quiet for a moment, soft and happy.

Then Mia and Max clapped and giggled. Whiskers blinked slow cat blinks and purred louder.

Sunny bowed. “Today I learned: when I practice little by little, I get better. When I tell friends what I need, they can help me. And with friends, practice feels fun.”

Mia said, “Let’s have a dance party!”

They danced on the squishy rug. The jingle ball went shika-shika. The couch pillows went fwump-fwump. Sunny’s song floated through the playful living room like a bright ribbon.

Sunny felt proud and calm. “Tomorrow, I will practice again,” Sunny said. “I can do hard things. I can ask for help. And I can be a good friend too.”

The End.
