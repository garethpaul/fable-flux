# Whiskers and the Wobbly Door

Whiskers was a clever cat with bright green eyes and soft gray fur. One sunny afternoon, Whiskers padded into the guest room. The guest room was quiet and cozy. The curtains were light and fluttery. The bed was big and bouncy. A tiny night light made a warm, golden glow. Whiskers loved to explore.

“I am curious,” purred Whiskers. “What treasures are in here today?”

He sniffed the air. It smelled like clean sheets and a little like lavender. He heard a soft clock tick-tick-tick on the dresser. He swished his tail and peeked under the bed.

“Oh!” he meowed. “A little red ball rolled under there.”

Whiskers reached with his paw. Tap, tap. The ball rolled farther. He reached again. Tap-tap-tap. The ball rolled all the way to the wall. Whiskers crawled under the bed. It was shadowy and a little dusty.

“Achoo!” he sneezed. Dust tickled his nose. “I will get that ball!”

He batted the ball out from under the bed and chased it across the soft rug. The ball bounced, bonk, bonk, bonk, and rolled toward the door. Whiskers pounced and bumped the door.

Creeeeak.

The guest room door opened a tiny crack, then stopped. Whiskers tried to push it open with his head. Bump. It did not open more. He tried to pull it with his paw. Scritch-scratch. It did not move.

Whiskers wrinkled his whiskers. He looked at the door. A heavy bag of laundry was behind it. The door was stuck.

“Oh no,” said Whiskers. “The door is wobbly, and the bag is heavy. How do I get out?”

He sat down and thought. His tail curled like a question mark. He felt a little worried. His heart went thump-thump. The room felt bigger and quieter.

“I will not give up,” he told himself. “I am a clever cat. I will use my brain. I will be resourceful.”

Whiskers looked around. He saw a stack of pillows on the chair. He saw a rolled-up rug by the closet. He saw a wooden ruler on the desk. He saw a basket of soft socks. He heard footsteps in the hallway, far away.

“Meow?” he called. No one answered.

Whiskers sniffed the wooden ruler. It was smooth and cool. “Maybe this can help me push the bag.”

He slid the ruler under the door. Tap-tap. He felt the end bump the laundry bag. He pushed. Nudge. The bag moved a tiny bit. He pushed again. Nudge-nudge. The bag moved a little more.

“This is working!” said Whiskers. “I will keep trying.”

But the ruler slipped. Clatter. It slid back under the door. Whiskers sighed. “Hmm. I need more help.”

He looked at the rolled-up rug. “This can make me taller,” he thought. He rolled the rug near the door and climbed on. Now he could reach higher with his paw. He wiggled his paw through the crack and felt the cool doorknob.

“Too round,” he meowed. “I cannot turn it. But I can push again.”

He put the ruler on the rug like a slide. He pushed the ruler under the door, high and steady. Push, push. The bag moved more. Thump. The door opened a little wider.

Just then, a small voice peeped from the hallway. “Squeak! Do you need help?”

It was Pip, a tiny gray mouse who lived in the safe little hole by the baseboard. Pip was shy, but he liked Whiskers. They were friends. They liked to share sunspots.

“Yes, please,” said Whiskers kindly. “I am stuck. The bag is heavy. Can you help me push?”

“I am small,” said Pip, “but I can try.”

Pip put his tiny paws on the bag and pushed. Squeak-push. Whiskers pushed with the ruler. Push-push. The door opened a tiny bit more. A sliver of bright hallway light shone in. Whiskers smelled warm toast from the kitchen. His tummy rumbled.

“I will get my friends too!” Pip called. He tapped on the baseboard with his little tail. Out came Dot the ladybug and Buzz the sleepy moth from the guest room plant.

“We can help!” said Dot in a tiny, cheerful voice.
“Mmhm,” hummed Buzz, flapping gentle wings.

Whiskers smiled. “Thank you, friends. We can do this together.”

Whiskers pushed with the ruler. Pip pushed the bag. Dot and Buzz flew into the crack and tickled the bag with their tiny feet, making it wiggle. The door squeaked. The gap grew. The warm hallway air flowed in like a soft whoosh.

“One, two, three—push!” said Whiskers.

Push! The bag slid away from the door. The door swung open with a happy creak. Light filled the guest room. Whiskers felt the soft breeze on his whiskers. Pip squeaked with joy. Dot twirled. Buzz hummed a little song.

“We did it!” said Whiskers. His eyes shone. His heart felt big and proud.

Whiskers picked up his red ball and batted it to Pip. Pip giggled and rolled it back. They all laughed. The guest room did not feel big and quiet anymore. It felt friendly and bright.

Whiskers sat down and licked his paw. “I learned something,” he said. “When there is a problem, I can be curious. I can look around. I can try new ideas. I can use tools. And I can ask for help. Being resourceful and working together makes hard things easier.”

Pip nodded. Dot sparkled. Buzz hummed, “Yes-yes-yes.”

Whiskers gave his friends a gentle nuzzle. “Thank you for helping. Everyone can help in their own way.”

Outside the guest room, the home was warm and happy. Whiskers trotted into the hallway with his red ball. He felt calm, brave, and clever.

The End.
