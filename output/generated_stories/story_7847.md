# Kiki Koala Visits the Guest Room

Kiki was a calm koala with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She loved quiet mornings, warm tea, and gentle stories. One sunny afternoon, Grandma Bear said, “Kiki, we have guests coming tonight. Please help me tidy the guest room.”

Kiki’s ears perked up. “The guest room?” she whispered. The door to that room was usually closed. It smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. Today, the door was open. A cool, fresh breeze drifted out.

“Remember,” Grandma Bear said kindly, “this is a special room for guests. We show respect by keeping things neat and safe.” She smiled. “You can explore, but be gentle.”

Kiki nodded. “I will be very gentle.” She held her paws close and took small, careful steps. Her soft feet made little shh, shh sounds on the carpet.

Inside, the guest room felt calm and cozy. The bed was big and fluffy, with a quilt like a patchwork rainbow. Kiki touched it with one finger. “Soft,” she said. A small lamp glowed like a warm candle. There was a basket on the dresser with tiny soaps, a shiny red comb, and a note card.

Kiki read the note slowly. “Welcome, dear guest. We are happy you are here.” She smiled. “This room is a gift for our friends,” she said. “We need to show care.”

On the nightstand sat a blue puzzle box with a silver latch. Kiki loved puzzles. Her whiskers twitched with wonder. “Discovery time,” she whispered. “But I must be respectful.”

She looked around. “Grandma, may I open the puzzle box?” she called.

Grandma Bear peeked in. “Thank you for asking, Kiki,” she said. “That is respectful. Yes, you may open it. Be careful and put everything back.”

Kiki’s heart felt warm. She clicked the latch and lifted the lid. Inside were smooth wooden shapes: a circle, a square, and a triangle. They smelled like pine trees. “Ooh,” Kiki breathed. “They look like cookies, but they are not for eating.”

She set the shapes on the rug and made a little train. “Choo-choo,” she giggled. Then she noticed something else: a tiny bell on a ribbon.

“What’s that bell for?” Kiki asked.

Grandma Bear said, “That bell is for guests to call if they need help at night. It is for safety.”

Kiki nodded. “Safety is important.” She touched the bell gently. Tinkle, tinkle. The sound was soft, not loud. “I will not ring it for play. I will save it for help.”

Just then, Kiki saw a little picture frame turned sideways. A photo of Aunt Bunny smiled out, but it was a bit dusty. Kiki’s nose wrinkled. “Achoo!” she sneezed softly. “Grandma, may I dust the picture?”

“Yes, thank you,” said Grandma Bear. “Use the soft cloth, please.”

Kiki took the cloth and wiped in slow circles. “Round and round,” she sang. The glass shone. “Aunt Bunny can see clearly now,” Kiki said, feeling proud.

Kiki looked under the bed to see if it was tidy. She found a small, forgotten sock. It was blue with tiny stars. “Grandma, I found a sock,” she called.

“Good eyes,” Grandma said. “Let’s put it in the wash.”

They walked together to the laundry basket. Kiki held the sock up high like a flag. “We are helpers!” she said. Grandma laughed.

Back in the guest room, Kiki noticed the window was open a little. The curtains swayed like white waves. A gentle breeze carried the smell of fresh grass. But the window screen had a tiny tear.

“Grandma,” Kiki said softly, “there is a little rip in the screen. Is that safe?”

“Thank you for checking,” Grandma said. “A bird could get in, or someone could get a bug bite. Let’s fix it.”

Grandma got tape, and Kiki held the edges while Grandma patched the tear. “Teamwork,” Kiki said. “Cooperation!” They smiled at each other.

When the room was tidy and safe, Kiki looked at the puzzle shapes again. She wanted to play more. Then she remembered: This room was for guests. “I want to play,” she said, “but I want to respect our guests and their things.”

Grandma Bear nodded. “What could we do instead?”

Kiki thought. “We can borrow the idea!” she said. “Let’s make our own puzzle in the playroom. Then we won’t use the guest things.”

“Wonderful plan,” said Grandma.

Kiki carefully put the shapes back in the box, closed the latch, and smoothed the quilt. She whispered, “Thank you, guest room. See you later.”

In the playroom, Grandma and Kiki drew shapes on cardboard. Snip, snip went the scissors. Tap, tap went the tape. Kiki colored the circle sunny yellow, the square sky blue, and the triangle leaf green. They made a tiny bell from a paper cup and a bead.

Tinkle, tinkle. Kiki smiled. “Now we can play safely and respectfully,” she said.

That evening, the guests arrived. Aunt Bunny hugged Kiki. “The guest room looks lovely,” she said. “Thank you for taking such good care.”

Kiki’s chest felt warm and proud. “We cleaned, we checked for safety, and we used our own toys,” she said. “We showed respect.”

Grandma Bear squeezed Kiki’s paw. “Yes, you did.”

Kiki looked at the guests and said, “Welcome. We are happy you are here.”

The room felt calm and cozy again. The lamp glowed. The quilt was smooth. The tiny bell waited, just in case.

Kiki yawned. “Today I learned to respect other people’s things, to ask before touching, to be safe, and to work together,” she said.

Grandma Bear kissed her head. “That is a very good lesson.”

Kiki smiled a sleepy smile. “I will remember,” she whispered.

The End.
