# Panda Poppy’s Gentle Treehouse Day

Panda Poppy lived in a cozy treehouse high in a big green tree. Leaves shushed in the breeze. The sun peeked through the windows like golden ribbons. The wood floor smelled like warm pine. Poppy liked to breathe in and smile.

“Good morning, treehouse,” Poppy said softly. “Let’s have a peaceful day.”

Poppy had chores to do. She dusted the shelves with a soft cloth. She folded tiny blankets. She watered a little mint plant. The mint smelled cool and sweet.

Her friend, Squeak the squirrel, scampered up the ladder. Tap-tap-tap went his tiny feet. “Hi, Poppy! Can I help?” he squeaked.

“Yes,” said Poppy. “Let’s sweep the floor. Slow and steady.”

They held small brooms. Swish, swish. The brooms made whisper sounds. Poppy hummed a calm song. Squeak hummed too, a little faster.

Then Squeak saw Poppy’s globe. It was a round ball painted with blue seas and green lands. “What’s this?” he asked.

“It is a globe,” Poppy said. “It shows the world. People live in many places. We all share the Earth.”

Squeak’s eyes grew big. He spun the globe. Spin, spin! His tail twitched with joy.

“Let’s look gently,” Poppy said. “Gentle hands, gentle hearts.”

Squeak nodded, but he was excited. He spun it again, too fast. The globe knocked a bowl of blueberries. Plop! Splatter! Blue dots landed on the floor, on Poppy’s blanket, on Poppy’s feet. The sweet berry smell filled the room.

Squeak froze. His ears drooped. “Oh no,” he whispered. “I made a mess. I’m sorry.”

Poppy’s heart felt hot for a moment. Her blanket was blue and sticky. Her chores got harder. She wanted to say, “Why did you do that?” Her face felt tight.

Poppy placed her paw on her belly. She took a slow breath in. One… two… three. She let it out. One… two… three.

She remembered: Pause like a panda. Be patient.

“It’s okay, Squeak,” Poppy said gently. “Accidents happen. Thank you for saying sorry.”

Squeak blinked. “You’re not mad?”

“I felt upset,” Poppy said. “But I can choose peace. We can fix this together.”

Squeak’s tail lifted. “How?”

“First, we breathe,” Poppy said. “In… and out.” They breathed together. The room felt quiet and soft again.

“Now, we clean,” Poppy said. She handed Squeak a damp cloth. It was cool and squishy.

They wiped the floor. Wipe, wipe. They rinsed the blanket in a bucket. Swish, swish. The water turned purple like grape juice. Squeak giggled a little.

“I didn’t mean to spin it so fast,” Squeak said. “I just got excited.”

“I know,” said Poppy. “Your heart was happy. Next time, gentle hands.”

Squeak nodded. “Next time, gentle hands.”

When the floor was clean and the blanket was hung to dry, Poppy and Squeak sat by the window. Warm light curled around them. Birds chirped outside. The mint plant smelled fresh again.

Poppy turned the globe with one paw. It made a soft, slow sound. “See the blue?” she asked. “That is the ocean. Fish live there. See the green? That is land. People and animals live there.”

Squeak leaned close. “Do pandas live here?” He pointed.

“Pandas live in bamboo forests far away,” Poppy said. “And squirrels live in many trees. Even though we live in different places, we all share the same world.”

Squeak smiled. “We share the world. We can share the treehouse too.”

“Yes,” said Poppy. “Sharing means caring for each other. When someone makes a mistake, we can forgive. Forgiveness is like a soft blanket for the heart.”

Squeak placed a paw on his chest. “My heart likes that blanket.”

They decided to make a “Peace Plan.” Poppy took out a small paper and a crayon. The crayon felt smooth in her paw.

“Peace Plan,” she said. “One: Gentle hands. Two: Slow breaths. Three: Say sorry. Four: Forgive and help.”

Squeak repeated, “Gentle hands, slow breaths, say sorry, forgive and help.”

They taped the paper to the wall with a leafy sticker. It looked friendly.

To celebrate, Poppy made mint tea. The steam felt warm on their faces. They sipped. Mmm. Sweet and calm. Squeak shared a bowl of nuts. Crunch, crunch.

“Thank you for forgiving me,” Squeak said.

“Thank you for helping me clean,” Poppy said. “We solved it together.”

They listened to the wind in the leaves. Swoosh. The treehouse felt peaceful again.

Poppy looked at the globe one more time. “All around the world,” she said, “people make mistakes. All around the world, people can forgive. Forgiveness helps hearts heal. It helps homes feel safe.”

Squeak nodded. “Our treehouse feels safe.”

Poppy smiled and gave Squeak a gentle hug. “We had a daily adventure,” she said. “We learned to be mindful and kind.”

Squeak yawned. “I like peaceful days,” he whispered.

“Me too,” said Poppy. “Peace grows when we forgive.”

They put away the globe with careful paws. They folded the clean blanket. It smelled like sun and mint. The treehouse glowed in the soft afternoon light.

Poppy and Squeak sat quietly, breathing in and out, feeling cozy and loved.

The End.
