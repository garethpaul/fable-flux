# Tiko the Tortoise and the Quiet, Kind Beat

In a bright musical practice room, the air hummed like a friendly bee. The big drum went boom. The little bell went ting. The shiny piano keys went plink-plonk. Soft sunlight made the wooden floor warm and smooth. The room smelled a little like lemon soap and a little like old music books.

Tiko the Tortoise came in slowly, step, step, step. Tiko was wise and kind. His shell was patterned like gentle waves. He wore a small blue scarf to keep his neck cozy. He smiled at the instruments and whispered, “Good morning, sounds.”

Inside the room were friends: Pip the puppy with waggy ears, Mina the mouse with a squeaky giggle, and Leo the lion cub with soft paws. They were excited to play. They all wanted to practice for Music Sharing Day.

“Let’s all play!” Pip barked. He grabbed the drumsticks. Boom! Boom! Boom-boom-BOOM!

Leo puffed up and roared, “I want the drum too!” He reached for a drumstick.

Mina squeaked, “I want the bell! Ting! Ting!” She shook the bell fast-fast-fast.

Soon, the room sounded messy. Boom! Ting-ting! Plink-plonk-plunk! Everyone tried to be the loudest. No one listened. The noise got bigger and bigger. Pip bumped into Mina. Leo’s tail knocked over a music stand. A dust cloud puffed up. Achoo!

Tiko blinked. His wise eyes were calm. He took a deep breath. In, two, three. Out, two, three. He tapped his shell with a knuckle. Knock. Knock. The sound was small and kind.

“Friends,” Tiko said in a soft voice, “my ears feel tired. Do your ears feel tired too?”

Pip tilted his head. “Maybe,” he said.

“My whiskers are wiggly,” Mina said. “The noise tickles them.”

“My heart is thumping too fast,” Leo said, placing a paw on his chest.

Tiko nodded. “Let’s use our thinking. How can we make music that is kind to all ears? How can we show respect? Respect means we care how others feel.”

Pip sat on his waggy tail. “We can take turns?”

Mina raised a tiny paw. “We can listen with quiet bodies.”

Leo looked at the drumstick in his paw. “We can share,” he said slowly. “And we can use inside sounds.”

“Good ideas,” Tiko said. “Let’s try a plan.”

Tiko made a soft beat on his shell: tap… tap… tap-tap. “We will go one by one,” he said. “We will listen with our eyes and ears. We will smile when it is a friend’s turn. If we forget, we will take a breath.”

Pip and Mina and Leo nodded.

“First,” Tiko said, “we wash our paws and hands. Clean paws are kind to instruments and kind to friends.”

They went to the sink in the corner. The water went whoosh. The soap smelled like lemons. Bubble-bubble-bubble. Pip sang, “Scrub-a-dub-dub,” and wiggled his paws. Mina rubbed her tiny fingers. Leo used a towel and patted dry. Tiko used a soft cloth on his shell edges and scarf.

“Clean and ready,” Tiko said, smiling. “Now, we begin.”

Mina went first with the bell. She held it gently. Ting… ting… ting-ting. The sound was light like little stars. Pip and Leo listened. Pip folded his ears back to hear better. Leo tucked his tail. Tiko swayed slowly.

Mina finished. She bowed. “Thank you for listening,” she said.

Pip clapped softly. “Your bell sounds like sparkles,” he said.

Leo smiled. “I felt calm,” he said.

“Next,” Tiko said, “Pip and the piano.”

Pip pressed one white key. Plink. Then one black key. Plonk. He played a tiny song: plink-plink, plonk, plink. His paws moved carefully. He kept his tongue inside his mouth so he would not drool on the keys. He remembered the hygiene rule. He sat up tall like Tiko showed him.

When he finished, Mina squeaked, “It sounded like raindrops.”

Leo nodded. “I liked the soft parts.”

Tiko tapped his gentle beat. “Good sharing. Good respect.”

“Now Leo,” Tiko said. Leo picked up one drumstick. He did not grab both. He looked at Tiko and asked, “May I play now?”

“Yes,” said Tiko. “Thank you for asking.”

Leo made a quiet boom. Boom… boom. He felt the drum in his chest. He tried not to roar. He let the drum speak. Boom… boom-boom. The sound was like a slow heart.

When Leo ended, Pip whispered, “That drum was like marching feet.”

Mina smiled. “I felt brave.”

Tiko looked at them. “How did that feel?” he asked.

Pip said, “My ears are happy.”

Mina said, “My whiskers are calm.”

Leo said, “My heart is steady.”

“Why did it work?” Tiko asked.

Mina tapped her chin. “We took turns.”

Pip wagged. “We listened.”

Leo nodded. “We respected each other.”

“Exactly,” Tiko said. “Respect is kind and gentle. It makes room for every voice.”

They practiced more with the same plan. They shared. They waited. They asked, “May I?” They cleaned their paws when they sneezed or touched their noses. They kept the room tidy. Clack went the music stand as they set it straight. Swish went the broom as they picked up dust.

At the end, they played together. Tiko kept the quiet beat: tap… tap… tap-tap. Mina tinged the bell, Pip plinked the piano, Leo boomed the drum softly. The sounds fit like puzzle pieces. The room felt warm and safe. The music floated like a friendly kite.

Tiko smiled. “Friends, what did we learn today?”

Pip said, “Respect means I wait and listen.”

Mina said, “Respect means I share and use gentle hands.”

Leo said, “Respect means I ask, and I care how you feel.”

Tiko nodded. “When we respect others, we help them. We help ourselves too. That is wise.”

They all hugged Tiko’s shell. It felt smooth and strong. The lemony smell mixed with happy music in the air.

“Music Sharing Day will be great,” Pip said.

“It will be kind,” Mina added.

“It will be respectful,” Leo finished.

“Yes,” Tiko said, tapping his quiet, kind beat. “Together, we make the best song.”

The End.
