# Polly's Workshop Surprise

Polly the parrot loved spending time in Grandpa Joe's garage workshop. The colorful bird would perch on the workbench, watching as Grandpa fixed broken toys and built wonderful things with his tools.

"Good morning, Polly!" said Grandpa Joe, putting on his work apron. "Today I'm fixing Mrs. Chen's music box for her granddaughter."

Polly fluffed her bright green feathers excitedly. "Fix it! Fix it!" she squawked, bobbing her head up and down.

The workshop smelled like wood shavings and metal polish. Polly loved the gentle tap-tap-tap of Grandpa's hammer and the whirring sound of his drill. Everything seemed perfect until...

CRASH!

Grandpa Joe accidentally knocked over his jar of screws. Hundreds of tiny screws scattered across the concrete floor like silver raindrops.

"Oh no!" Grandpa Joe groaned, his face turning red. "Now I'll never finish the music box in time! This is so frustrating!"

Polly watched as Grandpa's hands began to shake with anger. She had never seen him so upset before. Her heart felt heavy seeing her friend so sad.

"Breathe, breathe!" Polly chirped softly, remembering what Grandpa had taught her when she got scared during thunderstorms.

Grandpa Joe looked up at his feathered friend and took a deep breath. "You're right, Polly. Getting angry won't pick up these screws."

But Polly had an idea! She spread her colorful wings wide and flew down to the floor. With her sharp beak, she began picking up screws one by one, placing them in neat little piles.

"Polly, what are you doing?" asked Grandpa Joe, kneeling down beside her.

"Helping! Helping!" Polly sang proudly, her tail feathers wiggling with determination.

Soon, Grandpa Joe was smiling again. He gently picked up screws alongside Polly, and together they made quick work of the mess. The workshop filled with the soft sounds of their teamwork - clink, clink, clink as the screws dropped back into the jar.

"You know what, Polly?" said Grandpa Joe, scratching behind her head. "Your kindness helped me feel better. When we help each other, everything becomes easier."

Polly puffed out her chest proudly. "Friends help friends!"

With all the screws collected, Grandpa Joe was able to finish fixing the music box. When he wound it up, it played the most beautiful tinkling melody that made Polly dance on her perch.

"Thank you for showing me compassion today," said Grandpa Joe. "You saw I was upset and chose to help instead of flying away. That's what real friends do."

Polly tilted her head thoughtfully. "Compassion means caring?"

"Exactly!" Grandpa Joe smiled. "Compassion means noticing when someone needs help and choosing to be kind, even when it's not easy."

Just then, Mrs. Chen arrived to pick up the music box. When she saw how beautifully it worked, her eyes sparkled with joy.

"My granddaughter will love this! Thank you so much!" she said.

As the sun set through the garage window, Polly snuggled against Grandpa Joe's shoulder. She felt warm and happy inside, knowing that her small act of kindness had made such a big difference.

"Tomorrow we'll build something new together," whispered Grandpa Joe.

"Together!" agreed Polly sleepily, already dreaming of their next workshop adventure.

The End.
