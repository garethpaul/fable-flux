# Ollie's Workshop Adventure

Ollie the otter loved to play with his dad's shiny tools in the big garage workshop. The hammers went "tap-tap-tap," the screwdrivers made "click-click" sounds, and the saw made a fun "whirr" noise.

One sunny morning, Ollie's dad said, "I need to fix Mrs. Rabbit's broken chair today. Can you help me get ready?"

"Yes, yes!" squeaked Ollie, clapping his little paws together. But instead of helping, he started playing with the colorful paintbrushes. He dipped them in water and painted wiggly lines on the workbench.

"Ollie," Dad said gently, "we need to prepare first. Let's gather our tools."

But Ollie was having too much fun! He grabbed the measuring tape and pulled it out long like a stretchy snake. "Look, Dad! It goes zip-zip-zip!"

Dad smiled but looked worried. "Son, if we don't prepare properly, we might not be able to help Mrs. Rabbit."

Ollie kept playing. He stacked the wooden blocks high, high, high until they tumbled down with a loud "CRASH!" Wood pieces scattered everywhere across the smooth concrete floor.

Just then, Mrs. Rabbit knocked on the garage door. "Knock-knock-knock!"

"Oh no!" gasped Dad. "We're not ready!" He looked around at the messy workshop. Paint was dripping, tools were scattered, and wood blocks covered the floor.

Mrs. Rabbit hobbled in with her broken chair. One leg was wobbly and loose. "Can you fix this today?" she asked hopefully.

Dad's face turned red. "I'm so sorry, Mrs. Rabbit. We... we're not prepared. We need more time."

Mrs. Rabbit's ears drooped sadly. "Oh dear. I really needed to sit down. My back hurts from standing."

Ollie saw Mrs. Rabbit's sad face and felt a tight, uncomfortable feeling in his tummy. He realized his playing had caused a big problem.

"I'm sorry, Mrs. Rabbit," Ollie said in a small voice. "I didn't help Dad get ready. It's my fault."

Mrs. Rabbit patted Ollie's head gently. "Thank you for saying sorry, little one."

Ollie stood up tall and spoke clearly. "Dad, I want to help fix this! Can we prepare together right now?"

Dad smiled proudly. "Yes, we can!"

Ollie and Dad worked as a team. First, they cleaned up the paint and put the brushes away. Then they picked up every single wood block - one, two, three, four, five! Next, they gathered the right tools: a hammer, screws, and wood glue that smelled like pine trees.

"Now we're prepared!" announced Ollie.

Together, they fixed Mrs. Rabbit's chair. Dad held it steady while Ollie carefully handed him each tool. The hammer went "tap-tap," and the screws went "twist-twist."

"All done!" said Dad.

Mrs. Rabbit sat down and wiggled happily. "Perfect! Thank you both so much!"

She gave Ollie a warm hug that smelled like fresh carrots. "You did wonderful work preparing and helping."

Ollie beamed with pride. "I learned that preparing first makes everything work better!"

From that day on, whenever Dad had a project, Ollie always helped prepare first. They would gather tools, clean the workspace, and plan together. And Mrs. Rabbit's chair stayed strong and sturdy for many, many years.

"Being prepared helps us help others," Ollie would tell his friends. And it always made him feel proud and happy inside.

The End.
