# Bubbles, Colors, and the Clever Dolphin

Splash was a playful dolphin who loved to click, whistle, and make tiny bubble rings with his nose. One sunny morning, Splash peeked into a bright, friendly bathroom. The floor tiles were cool and smooth. The towels were soft and smelled like clean cotton. The bathtub was full of warm, wiggly water. “Hello, bathroom!” Splash chirped. “Let’s make today a day of ideas!”

He swam in with a gentle whoosh. The water felt cozy and calm. Plip-plop went the faucet. Drip-drop went the shower. “Listen,” Splash said, closing his eyes. “The bathroom is singing.” He clicked a little rhythm and tapped the side of the tub with his fin. Tap-tap, click-click. “Music!”

On the shelf, there were colorful bath crayons, a cup, and a rubber duck. The duck squeaked, “Squeak!” Splash giggled. “Nice to meet you, Duck. I’m Splash. Would you like to make art with me?”

“Squeak!” said Duck. That meant yes.

Splash lifted a red bath crayon with his fin. “I can draw!” he cheered. He carefully drew a big round sun on the smooth tub wall. The crayon felt waxy. The color felt bright and happy. Next, he used blue to draw a wavy ocean. He used green for tall seaweed and purple for a silly octopus with a smile.

“I made my ocean home,” Splash said. “But I want to learn something new today.” He looked around and took a deep, curious breath. “What else can I try?”

He saw the cup and scooped water. He poured it slowly. “Glug, glug.” The cup made a tiny waterfall. “What happens if I pour fast?” Splash poured quickly. “Whoosh!” The water splashed higher. “I am testing,” Splash said. “Fast pouring makes big splashes. Slow pouring makes soft splashes.” He nodded. “I used my thinking head.”

Splash drew dots on the wall: one, two, three. “I know numbers,” he said. “Numbers help me count. Counting helps me plan.” He counted his bath toys: “One duck, two green sea sponges, three crayons.” He smiled. “I can use what I know to make new ideas.”

He blew a bubble ring. It floated up, up, up, and popped. “What if I make a bubble train?” Splash tried again. He blew one bubble, then a second, then a third. “Choo-choo!” he laughed. The bubbles felt tickly on his nose. “I solved a little problem. I learned to blow slow and steady.”

The bathroom mirror shimmered with tiny water drops. Splash swished his tail and looked. He saw himself and also the room. “I see many colors,” he said. “Blue towels, yellow soap, white sink, brown basket. So many different colors. All different, all special.” He smiled at Duck. “We are different too. I am a dolphin. You are a duck. We both make sounds. We both like the water. Different and the same!”

“Squeak!” Duck agreed.

Splash tried something new. He made a story picture on the wall. He drew a big circle and said, “This is the world.” He added tiny shapes—triangles for mountains, dots for houses, swirls for wind. He drew a little dolphin and a little duck, side by side. He whispered, “Friends can come from many places.”

He dipped a sponge into warm water and dabbed the wall. The colors blended like a rainbow mist. “Ooo,” he said softly. “The sponge makes soft clouds.” He patted again and watched the colors swish and swirl. “Art can change. I can change my art.”

Splash heard the shower drip. Drip. Drip. “I wonder why it drips,” he thought. He checked the knob. It was not tight. He turned it gently. “Click.” The drip stopped. “I used careful thinking,” he said. “I tried one small step, and it worked.” He looked proud and calm.

“Let’s make a song,” Splash said. He tapped the tub—tap-tap. He shook the cup—shaka-shaka. He squeaked with Duck—squeak-squeak. He hummed low and then high. “This is our bathroom band,” he giggled. “Music is art too. We can make it together.”

Soon the tub wall was a bright gallery: suns, clouds, fish, mountains, and friendly faces. Splash looked at his work. “What do I feel?” he asked himself. He placed a fin on his chest. “I feel happy, brave, and full of ideas. Art shows my feelings.”

He rinsed a corner of the wall with the warm shower. The colors slid away like rainbow rain. “I can clean and care for my space,” he said. “I can make art again tomorrow.” He wiped the edge of the tub and neatly stacked the crayons, the cup, and the sponges. “All tidy!”

Duck bobbed in the water. “Squeak?”

“Yes,” Splash said, “I learned that I can use my voice, my colors, my ideas. I can try, test, and try again. I can listen, count, and look closely. Creative play helps me think and grow. And it is fun!” He nuzzled Duck. “Your squeak is part of our art too.”

Splash took one last glide around the tub, leaving tiny ripples that sparkled in the light. He whispered to the bathroom, “Thank you for the music, the colors, and the quiet thinking time.”

He waved his fin. “Friends, remember: Your ideas matter. Use your colors, your songs, your smart steps. Be kind, be curious, and make something new every day.”

The bathroom felt warm and peaceful. Splash felt cozy and proud. He clicked a happy goodbye and dove out with a soft swoosh, ready for tomorrow’s adventure.

The End.
