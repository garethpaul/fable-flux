# Mimi Mouse's Plan in the Home Office

Mimi was a kind little mouse who lived in a cozy home office. The home office was full of clicky keys, soft papers, and a big spinning chair. Every morning, the sun made bright stripes on the floor, and the room smelled like warm tea and pencils. Mimi loved the soft tap-tap of the keyboard and the gentle hum of the computer. She liked to help keep the office neat and happy.

But one day, there was a problem.

“Oh no,” said Mimi, twitching her whiskers. “The papers are everywhere!” Papers were scattered like fallen leaves. The sticky notes were stuck to the floor, the desk, and even the plant. Pencils rolled under the chair with a clink-clink. The big calendar had slipped and was hiding behind a box.

Mimi took a deep breath. She was small, but she was brave and kind. “I will help,” she said. “I will use a smart plan.”

Mimi remembered something her grandma used to say: “When something is big, make it small. Use steps. Use a little dance!”

“A dance?” Mimi squeaked. Her eyes sparkled. “Yes! A tidy-up dance! That can be my strategy!” She smiled, because a strategy is a plan to solve a problem.

First, Mimi looked and listened. She stood very still. She heard the quiet hum of the computer. She felt the cool floor under her tiny paws. She smelled the lemony cleaner on the desk. She looked at the mess and made a map in her mind.

“Step one,” she said to herself. “Pick up the papers. One pile at a time.”

Mimi put on a little music from the radio in the corner. The music was soft and bouncy. “Tippy-toe, tippy-toe,” she sang. She made a paper pile dance. She slid a paper with a swoosh. She twirled with another paper. “Paper pile goes here,” she said, placing them in a neat stack. “I am using my strategy!”

“Step two,” she said. “Find the sticky notes.” The sticky notes were like tiny, colorful leaves. Mimi made a sticky-note shuffle. Shuffle-shuffle, pat-pat! She peeled them off the floor gently. “Come with me,” she told the notes. “Let’s stick you back on your board.” The notes made soft pop sounds as they pressed onto the board. Pop! Pop! Pop!

“Step three,” said Mimi. “Get the pencils.” The pencils were rolling under the chair like little logs. Mimi made a pencil scoot dance. Scoot-scoot, slide-slide! She used a ruler like a bridge. “This is my tool,” she said, feeling proud. “I can use tools in my plan.” The pencils clicked together in a cup. Click-click!

Just then, the big chair spun with a whirr. Mimi hopped back. “Oh!” she squeaked. “The chair is fast.” She put a sticky note on the floor to mark a safe path. “This is my safe spot,” she said. “A good plan keeps me safe.”

“Step four,” said Mimi. “Find the calendar.” The calendar was hiding behind a box. Mimi sniffed. Sniff-sniff. “I smell paper.” She listened. Crinkle-crinkle. “I hear you!” She tugged one corner. Tug-tug. The calendar slid out with a whoosh. Mimi hugged it with her little arms. “You belong on the wall.”

Mimi looked up at the wall. It was very high. “Hmm,” she said, tapping her paw. “I need a new part of my plan.” She looked around the room and made a discovery. The books on the shelf were like steps! “A book ladder!” she cheered.

Mimi checked each step with her paw. “Safe?” Tap. “Safe.” Tap. “Safe.” She climbed slowly. Tap, step, pause. “I can go slow,” she told herself. “Slow is smart.” At the top, she reached the calendar hook. She hung the calendar carefully. Click! It fit just right.

Mimi climbed down and took a big breath. The room felt calm. The sunshine looked brighter. The air smelled like lemon and paper again.

Just then, the human came in. “Wow!” the human said, eyes wide. “The office looks wonderful! Did you help, little friend?”

Mimi smiled and did a happy wiggle. “I used a strategy,” she said. “I made a plan with steps. I danced and I discovered new ways to help!”

“What was your plan?” asked the human, kneeling to listen.

Mimi told the steps, counting on her tiny fingers. “One: papers in a pile. Two: sticky notes on the board. Three: pencils in the cup with a tool. Four: calendar on the wall with a book ladder.” She giggled. “And I had a safe spot!”

The human clapped softly. Clap-clap. “That is a smart plan,” they said. “You used your eyes, your ears, your nose, and your paws. You went slow. You stayed safe. You turned a big job into small steps. That is good strategy.”

Mimi felt warm and proud. The music still played softly. She did a little joy dance. Twirl, step, clap! “Strategy helps me,” she sang. “Dance makes it fun. Discovery makes me brave!”

That night, the stars twinkled through the window. The office hummed softly. Mimi curled up by the pencil cup and smiled.

The lesson was clear: When a problem feels big, make a plan. Use small steps. Stay safe. Try tools. Have a little dance. With strategy, you can solve problems, and with discovery and joy, you can have fun along the way.

The End.
