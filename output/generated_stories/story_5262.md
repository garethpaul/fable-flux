# Penny the Parrot's Helpful Heart

In a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls and fluffy white curtains, lived a bright green parrot named Penny. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds in the morning sunlight that streamed through the window. Penny loved to create beautiful things with whatever she could find.

One sunny morning, Penny heard soft sniffling sounds coming from the corner of the room. She fluffed her colorful tail feathers and flew over to investigate.

"Hello there," chirped Penny gently. "Are you okay?"

A small gray mouse named Max peeked out from behind a pile of old magazines. His tiny whiskers twitched nervously. "Oh! I'm sorry for being in your room," squeaked Max. "I was looking for materials to fix my nest, but I don't have anything good to use."

Penny tilted her bright orange head thoughtfully. Many animals might have been scared of a mouse in their room, but Penny believed in being kind to everyone, no matter how different they were.

"Don't worry, little friend," said Penny warmly. "What happened to your nest?"

Max's small black eyes filled with tears. "The wind blew it apart last night. All my babies are cold, and I can't find the right things to rebuild it. Everything I find is either too big or falls apart."

Penny's heart felt warm and fuzzy. She wanted to help! She looked around the cozy bedroom with fresh eyes. The soft carpet felt squishy under her orange claws as she walked around, thinking hard.

"I have an idea!" squawked Penny excitedly. "Instead of throwing away these old things, we can use them to make something new and wonderful!"

Penny showed Max the pile of magazines, some cotton balls from a craft project, and colorful yarn scraps. "We can reuse all of these to build you the coziest nest ever!"

Max's whiskers perked up. "Really? You would help me?"

"Of course!" chirped Penny. "Helping friends makes my heart sing!"

Together, they got to work. Penny used her strong beak to tear the magazines into soft strips. The paper made gentle rustling sounds as she worked. Max gathered the fluffy cotton balls, which felt like tiny clouds between his paws.

"This yarn is so pretty!" squeaked Max as he wound the colorful scraps into a circle.

"And look how soft these cotton balls are," said Penny, nuzzling them with her beak. "Your babies will be so warm!"

They wove everything together carefully. Penny's creative mind helped them make the most beautiful nest, with magazine strips for structure, cotton for softness, and yarn for extra warmth. The finished nest felt cozy and smelled like fresh paper and flowers.

"Oh, Penny!" cried Max joyfully. "This is the most wonderful nest ever! My babies will love it!"

As they worked together, Penny felt a special happiness in her chest. "You know what, Max? Helping you made me feel even better than making things just for myself."

Max smiled his biggest mouse smile. "Thank you for being so kind to me, even though I'm different from you. You're the most helpful friend ever!"

From that day on, Penny and Max became the best of friends. Penny learned that helping others and reusing old things to make something new brought the greatest joy of all. And whenever someone needed help in their cozy bedroom, Penny was always ready to lend a wing.

"Helping others," Penny would say, "makes the whole world brighter!"

The End.
