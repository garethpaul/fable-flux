# Maple the Mouse Learns to Listen

High up in the old oak tree lived a kind little mouse named Maple. Her treehouse was cozy and warm, with tiny windows that let in golden sunlight and soft cushions made from flower petals.

Every morning, Maple would wake up feeling excited to play. She loved to run around her treehouse, squeaking loudly and bouncing from room to room. "Wheee!" she would shout, jumping on her acorn bed. "This is so much fun!"

But lately, Maple had been feeling tired and grumpy. Her tummy hurt, and she couldn't remember where she put her favorite cheese crackers. She kept bumping into things and felt all mixed up inside.

One sunny morning, Maple's wise grandmother, Nana Rose, climbed up to visit. Nana Rose had soft gray fur and kind, twinkling eyes.

"Good morning, dear Maple," said Nana Rose gently. "How are you feeling today?"

"Oh, Nana!" Maple sighed. "I feel all jumbled up. I can't find anything, my tummy feels funny, and I'm so tired!"

Nana Rose nodded thoughtfully. "I think I know what might help. Would you like to learn something special with me?"

"Yes, please!" said Maple, her whiskers twitching with curiosity.

"Let's sit quietly together," said Nana Rose, patting a soft moss cushion. "Close your eyes and listen to your body. What do you hear?"

Maple closed her eyes. At first, all she could hear were the birds chirping outside and the wind rustling the leaves. But then she noticed something else.

"I can hear my heart beating!" she whispered excitedly.

"Very good," smiled Nana Rose. "Now, what does your tummy tell you?"

Maple placed her tiny paws on her belly and listened carefully. "It's making little rumbling sounds. I think... I think it's hungry!"

"When did you last eat a good meal?" asked Nana Rose.

Maple thought hard. "I had some cookie crumbs yesterday, but I was too busy playing to eat my vegetables and cheese."

Nana Rose nodded. "Your body is trying to tell you something important. It needs healthy food to feel strong and happy. Shall we make a nutritious breakfast together?"

"Yes!" squeaked Maple.

Together, they prepared a colorful meal with fresh berries, crunchy nuts, and creamy cheese. As Maple ate slowly and mindfully, she noticed how much better she felt.

"Nana, this tastes so good! And my tummy feels much happier," said Maple.

"Learning to listen to our bodies is very important," explained Nana Rose. "When we pay attention, we can take better care of ourselves."

After breakfast, Maple practiced her new skill. When she felt thirsty, she drank fresh water from a leaf cup. When she felt tired, she rested on her soft bed instead of bouncing around.

"Nana, I feel so much better when I listen to what my body needs!" said Maple proudly.

"You're learning to be mindful, dear one," said Nana Rose, giving her a warm hug. "This means paying attention to how you feel and taking good care of yourself."

From that day on, Maple made sure to eat healthy meals, drink plenty of water, and rest when she felt tired. She still loved to play and have fun, but now she knew how to listen to her body's important messages.

Whenever her friends felt grumpy or tired, Maple would share her special learning with them. "Let's sit quietly and listen to what our bodies are telling us," she would say kindly.

And they all lived happily and healthily ever after in their cozy treehouse home.

The End.
