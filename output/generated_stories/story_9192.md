# Leo's Big Garden Day

Leo was a brave little lion cub with golden fur and bright green eyes. He lived in a cozy apartment with his mama, and they had a beautiful balcony garden full of colorful flowers and green plants.

One sunny morning, Mama Lion gave Leo a special job. "Leo, our garden needs help today. Can you water all the plants while I make breakfast?"

Leo's chest puffed up with pride. "Yes, Mama! I can do it all by myself!"

Mama handed him a small red watering can. "Remember, each plant needs just the right amount of water. Not too much, not too little."

Leo nodded and walked onto the sunny balcony. The morning air smelled fresh and sweet. Birds chirped happily in the nearby trees. He looked at all the plants - there were red tomatoes, purple flowers, green herbs, and yellow sunflowers.

"This looks easy!" Leo said to himself. He started with the tomato plants, pouring water on the leaves. The water dripped everywhere.

"Hmm," Leo thought. "Maybe I should water the soil instead." He tried again, but this time he poured too much. The water overflowed from the pot.

Leo felt frustrated. His little paws were getting muddy, and the job seemed harder than he thought. "Maybe I should ask Mama for help," he whispered.

But then Leo remembered what Mama always said: "Brave lions keep trying, even when things get tough."

Leo took a deep breath. He watched carefully this time. He noticed how the other plants looked. Some had dry soil, others looked just right. He smelled the earth - the dry pots smelled dusty, while the healthy ones smelled rich and dark.

"I need to look and listen to what each plant needs," Leo said.

Slowly and carefully, Leo watered each plant. He gave the thirsty sunflowers extra water because their soil was very dry. He gave the herbs just a little sprinkle because their soil was still damp. He talked to each plant as he worked.

"Here you go, little tomato plant. Drink up!"

"You're looking beautiful today, purple flowers!"

As Leo worked, he felt proud of himself. His golden fur sparkled in the sunshine. His green eyes focused on each task. He was learning to pay attention and take his time.

When Mama Lion came outside with breakfast, she gasped with delight. "Leo! You did such a wonderful job! Each plant has exactly what it needs."

Leo beamed with pride. "It was hard at first, Mama. I wanted to give up. But I kept trying, and I learned to listen to what each plant needed."

Mama hugged her brave little cub. "That's what makes you so special, Leo. You didn't give up, and you learned something new. Completing tasks makes us stronger and more confident."

Leo looked at his beautiful, happy plants. The tomatoes seemed redder, the flowers brighter, and the herbs smelled more fragrant. He had done it all by himself!

"Tomorrow, can I help with more garden jobs?" Leo asked excitedly.

"Of course!" Mama smiled. "There's always more to learn and do in our garden."

Leo felt warm and happy inside. He had learned that completing tasks wasn't always easy, but it felt wonderful when you finished. He was proud to be a helpful, brave little lion who could take care of things all by himself.

The End.
