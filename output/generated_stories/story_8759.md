# Buzzy Beaver and the Splishy-Splash Plan

Buzzy Beaver was very busy. He was busy all day, building with blocks, drawing with crayons, and helping his friends. At night, he was busy too. He liked to splash in the bathtub. Splish! Splash! Swirl!

The bathroom smelled like warm soap and minty toothpaste. The light was soft and yellow. The floor felt cool and smooth under Buzzy’s little feet. The towels were fluffy and smelled like sunshine.

But there was one big problem.

Buzzy made big messes.

He squeezed too much toothpaste. Squish! It plopped on the sink. He splashed too much water. Splish! It puddled on the floor. He left toys everywhere. Clunk, clatter, clink!

Mama Beaver peeked in. “Oh, Buzzy,” she said gently. “I see toothpaste on the mirror and water on the rug.”

Buzzy’s cheeks felt warm. His tail drooped. “I didn’t mean to,” he whispered. “I was just… busy.”

Mama smiled kindly. “I know. You are a busy beaver. But we can improve. We can make a simple bathroom plan.”

Buzzy blinked. “A plan?”

“A routine,” Mama said, tapping a paw to her chin. “Steps to help you slow down and take care.”

Buzzy took a deep breath. In through his nose. Out through his mouth. He liked the minty smell. “I want to try.”

Mama nodded. “Let’s make the Splishy-Splash Plan together.”

They got a little card and a marker. Buzzy loved helping. He felt proud, like a volunteer helper.

“Step one,” Mama said. “Ready body.”

Buzzy tried it. He stood tall and still. He hugged his tail. He whispered, “Ready body.” His shoulders relaxed.

“Step two,” Mama said. “Tiny toothpaste dot.”

Buzzy squeezed gently. Plip! A tiny dot. “Like a pea!” he giggled.

“Step three,” Mama said. “Slow brush—top, bottom, front, back. Count to ten.”

Buzzy moved slowly. One… two… three… The brush felt tickly. The bubbles tasted minty. He tried to keep the foam in his mouth. He wiggled and then paused. He remembered his ready body. He felt proud.

“Step four,” Mama said. “Gentle water—on, rinse, off.”

Buzzy turned the tap. Drip, drip, not too fast. He rinsed his brush. He turned the tap off. Click.

“Step five,” Mama said. “Towel time—pat, pat, pat.”

Buzzy patted his paws. The towel was soft and warm. He liked the gentle sound. Pat, pat, pat.

“Step six,” Mama said. “Toy home and tidy check.”

Buzzy put bath toys in a little basket. Plop, plop. He wiped a tiny splash with his towel corner. Swipe! He smiled. The sink was shiny. The floor was dry. It felt good.

The next night, Buzzy was very excited to splash again. He jumped into the tub. Slosh! Water waved over the side. Uh-oh.

He looked at Mama. Mama raised her eyebrows but didn’t scold. “What does our plan say?” she asked softly.

Buzzy took a deep breath. In and out. “Ready body,” he whispered. He sat still for a moment. The water got calm. He smiled. “Tiny toothpaste dot,” he said when it was time to brush. Plip! He counted slowly while brushing. One… two… three… The bubbles felt fizzy. He wanted to spit fast, but he waited and finished counting. He felt brave and strong.

“Gentle water,” he said. Drip, rinse, click. “Towel time,” he sang. Pat, pat, pat. “Toy home and tidy check,” he finished. Plop, swipe!

Mama clapped softly. “Look at this improvement, Buzzy! You used your plan. You took care. You used self-control.”

Buzzy’s heart felt warm. “I did it,” he said. His smile was big and bright.

The next morning, Buzzy saw a drip at the sink. Little sister Beebee had brushed and left a tiny puddle. Beebee’s eyes were worried. “Oops,” she said.

Buzzy remembered how he felt. He puffed his chest. “I can help,” he said. “I’m a bathroom volunteer!”

He showed Beebee the card. “Step one, ready body,” he said kindly. They breathed together. In and out. “Step two, tiny toothpaste dot.” Plip! Beebee giggled. “Step three, slow brush. Count with me.” They counted together. “Step four, gentle water. Step five, towel time. Step six, toy home and tidy check.”

They worked side by side. Swipe, pat, plop. The bathroom looked neat and cozy. It smelled minty and fresh.

Mama peeked in and smiled. “Buzzy, you improved. You helped yourself, and you helped others. That is wonderful.”

That night, Buzzy looked at the shiny sink and the tidy basket. He felt calm inside. He wasn’t just busy. He was careful and kind. He had a routine.

Buzzy whispered his plan like a song:
“Ready body,
Tiny dot,
Slow brush count,
Gentle water—stop!
Towel time,
Toys away,
Tidy check—hip hooray!”

He hugged Mama. “I like our bathroom plan,” he said. “It helps me slow down. It helps me be my best.”

Mama hugged back. “And when we practice, we improve.”

Buzzy nodded. “I can do it every day.”

And he did.

The End.
