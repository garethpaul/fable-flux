# Polly's Colorful Morning

In a cozy bedroom with soft yellow walls and fluffy white curtains, lived a bright green parrot named Polly. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds in the morning sunlight that streamed through the window. Polly loved to create beautiful things, but today she felt very sad.

"Squawk! I'm sorry, I'm sorry!" Polly chirped quietly, hiding her head under her colorful wing. Yesterday, she had been so excited about making art that she had splashed paint everywhere - on the walls, on the carpet, and even on her friend Emma's favorite book.

Emma, a kind little girl with curly brown hair, heard Polly's sad sounds. She walked over to the birdcage and gently opened the door. "Good morning, Polly. You sound upset. What's wrong?"

Polly's bright orange beak trembled. "I made a big mess yesterday with my paints. I was trying to make something beautiful, but I ruined everything instead. I'm scared you don't want me to paint anymore."

Emma's eyes were warm and understanding. "Oh, Polly! I was a little sad about my book, but I'm not angry with you. You just need to learn how to be creative in a safe way."

"But how?" Polly asked, tilting her feathered head to one side.

Emma smiled and clapped her hands together. "Let's start with our morning routine first. When we take care of ourselves, we think more clearly!"

Together, they began their day. Emma gave Polly fresh, crunchy seeds and sweet apple slices. "Good nutrition helps our brains work better," Emma explained as Polly munched happily. The seeds made satisfying little cracking sounds, and the apple was so juicy and refreshing.

After breakfast, Emma brought out something special - a large sheet of paper and some washable paints in bright containers. "These are safe paints, and this paper will catch any drips," she said.

Polly's eyes grew wide with excitement. "Really? I can still create art?"

"Of course! Being creative is wonderful, Polly. We just need to be thoughtful about how we do it."

With courage building in her heart, Polly dipped her feet carefully in the red paint. Step by step, she walked across the paper, leaving perfect little footprints. Then she tried blue, making beautiful patterns that looked like flowers dancing in the wind.

"This feels amazing!" Polly squawked joyfully. "The paint is cool and squishy between my toes!"

Emma laughed as she watched Polly create a masterpiece. "You're being so creative and careful at the same time!"

When they finished, Polly looked at her colorful creation with pride. "Emma, I'm truly sorry about yesterday. Thank you for showing me how to express myself safely."

"Apologies help us learn and grow," Emma said, hanging Polly's artwork on the wall. "And look how beautiful this is!"

From that day forward, Polly followed their creative routine every morning. She ate her nutritious breakfast, set up her safe art space, and let her imagination soar. Her bedroom became filled with wonderful paintings that made everyone smile.

Polly learned that being creative doesn't mean being careless. With courage, good habits, and thoughtful preparation, she could express herself beautifully while keeping everyone happy and safe.

The End.
