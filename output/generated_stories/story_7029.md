# Pippin's Rooftop Art Adventure

Pippin the penguin waddled up the snowy stairs to the tall building's rooftop. His orange flippers carried a big box of colorful chalk. The morning sun made the white rooftop sparkle like diamonds.

"What a perfect day to make art!" Pippin said happily, his breath making little puff clouds in the cold air.

Soon, his friends arrived. Ruby the rabbit hopped up with paintbrushes. Max the mouse scurried over with paper and crayons. Luna the lamb climbed up with glue and glitter.

"Wow! Look at all our art supplies!" said Ruby, her pink nose twitching with excitement.

"Can I use the chalk first?" asked Max, reaching for Pippin's box.

"I want the paintbrushes!" Luna called out.

Everyone started grabbing different supplies at the same time. The chalk scattered across the rooftop. Paint spilled on the ground. Papers flew in the wind.

"Oh no!" Pippin gasped. "Our art supplies are everywhere!"

The friends stopped and looked at the mess. The rooftop was covered with scattered supplies, but nobody could make any art.

Pippin thought for a moment. His little penguin brain had an idea. "Friends, what if we take turns? That way everyone gets to use everything!"

"Taking turns?" asked Ruby. "How does that work?"

"Well," Pippin explained, "first one person uses the supplies for a little while. Then they share with the next friend. Everyone gets a chance!"

Max's whiskers twitched. "That sounds fair!"

"And," Pippin added, "we should each be responsible for cleaning up our own supplies when we're done."

Luna nodded her fluffy head. "I like that idea!"

Pippin went first. He drew a big, happy sun with yellow chalk. The chalk felt smooth and cool in his flippers. "Your turn, Ruby!" he said, carefully putting the chalk back in the box.

Ruby painted beautiful red flowers with the brushes. The paint smelled fresh and looked so bright against the white rooftop. When she finished, she washed her brushes in a water bucket. "Max, you're next!"

Max drew a funny mouse family with his crayons. He colored them brown and gray, pressing gently to make soft lines. He put his crayons away neatly. "Luna's turn!"

Luna made sparkly stars with glue and glitter. The glitter felt like tiny, magical sand between her hooves. She cleaned up every sparkle when she was done.

"Now let's all create something together!" suggested Pippin.

The four friends worked side by side. Pippin drew wavy ocean lines with blue chalk. Ruby painted colorful fish swimming in the waves. Max added a bright yellow sun with his crayons. Luna sprinkled silver glitter to make the water sparkle.

"Look what we made!" Ruby exclaimed.

Their rooftop artwork was beautiful! The ocean scene stretched across the whole space, with each friend's special touch making it magical.

"This is the best art ever!" said Max, dancing on his tiny paws.

"Taking turns made it so much better," Luna agreed. "We all got to use everything!"

Pippin smiled his biggest penguin smile. "And being responsible for our supplies kept everything neat and tidy!"

As the sun set, painting the sky orange and pink, the friends cleaned up together. They put all the supplies away carefully.

"Tomorrow, let's come back and make more art!" suggested Pippin.

"Yes!" his friends cheered.

Pippin learned that sharing supplies, taking turns, and being responsible made creating art even more fun. When everyone works together, the most wonderful things can happen!

The End.
