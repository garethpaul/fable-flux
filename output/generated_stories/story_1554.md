# Melody's Garden Helper Adventure

In a beautiful balcony garden filled with bright flowers and green plants, lived a small yellow bird named Melody. She had the sweetest voice in the whole neighborhood, and she loved to sing happy songs every morning.

"Tweet, tweet, tra-la-la!" Melody sang as the warm sunshine touched her soft feathers. The flowers smelled like honey, and the leaves rustled gently in the breeze.

One sunny morning, Melody noticed something strange. Mrs. Rose, the kind lady who lived in the apartment, was looking very worried as she watered her plants.

"Oh dear," Mrs. Rose sighed. "My poor tomato plant looks so droopy. I don't know what's wrong."

Melody tilted her little head and looked at the sad tomato plant. Its leaves were turning yellow, and it looked very thirsty even though Mrs. Rose had just watered it.

"Tweet, tweet! I want to help!" chirped Melody. But Mrs. Rose couldn't understand bird language, so she went back inside.

Melody flew down to look more closely. She hopped around the plant, her tiny claws clicking on the wooden planter. The soil smelled earthy, but something seemed different about this plant compared to the others.

"I wonder what's wrong," Melody said to herself. "Maybe I can figure it out!"

First, Melody tried singing to the plant. "La-la-la, grow big and strong!" But the plant still looked sad.

Next, she tried flapping her wings to give it more air. Whoosh, whoosh went her wings! But that didn't help either.

Melody didn't give up. She was determined to help! She flew around the garden, looking at all the other healthy plants. The roses were bright red, the daisies were cheerful white, and the herbs smelled wonderful.

Then Melody noticed something important. All the healthy plants had dark, rich soil, but the tomato plant's soil looked very light and sandy.

"Aha!" tweeted Melody excitedly. "I think I know the problem!"

Melody flew to the corner of the balcony where Mrs. Rose kept a bag of dark, rich soil. It was too heavy for a little bird to carry, but Melody had an idea.

She began to sing a very special song - not just any song, but the loudest, most beautiful song she had ever sung. "Tweet-tweet-TWEET! Help-help-HELP! Come-come-COME!"

Mrs. Rose heard the unusual singing and came back outside. "What's all this noise about, little bird?"

Melody flew to the soil bag, then to the tomato plant, then back to the soil bag. She did this over and over again, chirping loudly.

"Are you trying to tell me something?" asked Mrs. Rose, watching carefully.

Melody nodded her little head and pointed her beak at the soil bag, then at the sad plant.

Mrs. Rose looked at the tomato plant's soil and gasped. "Oh my! You're right, little bird! This soil is too sandy. The plant needs richer soil to grow strong!"

Mrs. Rose added the dark, rich soil around the tomato plant. Within just a few days, the plant stood up tall and proud, with bright green leaves and tiny yellow flowers.

"Thank you, clever little bird!" said Mrs. Rose. "You didn't give up, and you saved my plant!"

Melody sang her happiest song ever. "Tweet, tweet, tra-la-la!" She had learned that when you keep trying and don't give up, you can solve problems and help others.

From that day on, Melody became the official garden helper, always watching over the plants and singing her beautiful songs.

The End.
