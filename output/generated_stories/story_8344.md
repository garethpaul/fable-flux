# Polly's Special Art Box

In a sunny backyard filled with colorful flowers and buzzing bees, lived a bright green parrot named Polly. Polly loved to make beautiful things with her wings and beak. She had a special wooden box where she kept all her crafting treasures.

"Squawk! Time to make art!" Polly chirped happily every morning as the warm sunshine tickled her feathers.

Polly's box was full of wonderful things she had found around the yard. There were smooth pebbles, pretty leaves that had fallen from trees, soft flower petals, and shiny bottle caps that people had dropped. She never took anything that was still growing or that belonged to someone else.

One sunny Tuesday, Polly was making a beautiful picture with her treasures when her friend Benny the bunny hopped over.

"Wow, Polly! That's so pretty!" said Benny, his nose twitching with excitement. "Can I use some of your things to make art too?"

Polly's wings fluttered nervously. She loved her special treasures and didn't want to share them all. "These are mine," she said quietly, pulling her box closer.

Benny's ears drooped. "Oh, okay," he said sadly and hopped away.

Polly felt funny in her tummy. She wanted to keep her treasures safe, but she also felt sad seeing Benny look disappointed.

Just then, Rosie the raccoon waddled over. "Polly, I saw Benny looking sad. What happened?" she asked, her tiny paws clasped together.

"He wanted to use my craft things, but they're special to me," Polly explained, her colorful feathers ruffling in the gentle breeze.

Rosie nodded wisely. "I understand. Your things are important to you. But maybe there's a way to help Benny and keep your treasures safe too?"

Polly tilted her head, thinking hard. Suddenly, her eyes sparkled with an idea!

"Squawk! I know!" she chirped excitedly. "I can teach Benny how to find his own treasures!"

Polly flew to find Benny, who was sitting under a shady tree looking glum.

"Benny! Want to go on a treasure hunt with me?" Polly asked cheerfully.

Benny's ears perked up. "Really?"

"Yes! I'll show you how to find amazing things for crafting, just like I do!"

Together, they explored the sunny backyard. Polly taught Benny to look for fallen leaves with interesting shapes, smooth stones by the garden path, and colorful flower petals that had naturally dropped to the ground.

"Remember," Polly explained as they searched, "we only take things that have already fallen. We never pick flowers that are still growing or take things that don't belong to us."

"This leaf feels so soft!" Benny said, gently touching a golden leaf with his paw.

"And smell this flower petal," Polly suggested. "It smells like sunshine!"

Soon, Benny had his own collection of crafting treasures. They sat together in the warm grass, making beautiful art side by side.

"Thank you for teaching me, Polly," Benny said happily. "Now I have my own special things, and you still have yours!"

Polly felt proud and warm inside. "Sharing what I know feels even better than sharing my things," she realized.

From that day on, Polly became the backyard's craft teacher. She showed all her friends how to find their own treasures while taking care of nature. Everyone had their own special collections, and they all created wonderful art together.

"The best part of crafting," Polly would say, "is making beautiful things while keeping our world beautiful too!"

The End.
