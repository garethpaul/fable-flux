# Benny Beaver Bakes and Waits

Benny was a busy beaver with bright eyes and two big, helpful teeth. Today, Benny stood on a little stool in a warm kitchen. The sun made soft squares on the floor. The air smelled like cinnamon and sweet apples. The oven hummed, “Mmm.” Pots clinked. A wooden spoon rested in a big mixing bowl.

“I want to bake apple muffins!” Benny said, bouncing on his toes. “I want to stir! I want to pour! I want to taste!”

Mama Beaver smiled and tied on a tiny apron for Benny. “We can do it together,” she said. “Baking is fun. We will take turns and show respect.”

Benny tilted his head. “Respect? What is respect, Mama?”

Mama tapped the wooden spoon. “Respect is being kind and careful with people’s feelings and with things we use. We listen. We wait. We share.”

Benny nodded slowly. “I can try.”

Mama set three things on the counter: a bag of flour, a jug of milk, and a bowl of shiny red apple pieces. “First choice,” Mama said. “What do we add first?”

Benny’s paws reached fast. He wanted to grab everything. His tail swished with excitement. But he stopped and took a slow breath. The kitchen smelled like calm cinnamon.

“I will think,” Benny said softly. “We need dry and wet and apples. Maybe we add flour first, so the bowl is ready.”

Mama smiled. “Good thinking. You used your beaver brain.”

Benny grinned. “My busy beaver brain!”

“Who will pour?” Mama asked.

Benny wanted to shout, “Me!” Instead, he remembered respect. “We can take turns,” he said. “You pour half. I pour half.”

“Wonderful plan,” Mama said.

They counted together. “One... two...” The flour fell like snow. It puffed up and made a soft cloud. Benny giggled. “It tickles my nose!” He sneezed. “Achoo!”

“Bless you,” Mama said, laughing. “Now milk.”

Benny held the jug with both paws. It was heavy and cold. “I can do it,” he whispered. He poured a little. “Glug, glug, glug.” A white ribbon of milk slid into the bowl.

“Nice and slow,” Mama said. “Thank you for being careful.”

Benny looked at the apple pieces. They were shiny and sweet. He wanted to pop one into his mouth. His tummy rumbled. “Can I taste one?” he asked.

“That is respectful to ask,” Mama said. “Yes, one taste.”

Benny chewed. The apple was crisp. “Crunch!” He sighed happily. “Yum.” Then he added the rest to the bowl.

“Now we stir,” Mama said.

Benny grabbed the spoon, then paused. “Mama, do you want a turn first?”

Mama blinked in surprise. “That is very kind. Let’s make a plan. I stir ten times. You stir ten times.”

They counted and stirred together. “One, two, three...” The batter made squishy sounds. The wooden spoon went “swish, swish.” Warm cinnamon and apple smells filled the kitchen.

When the batter was smooth, Benny licked his lip. “Now we put batter in the cups.” He looked at the tray. So many tiny cups! “How do we share this job?”

“Let’s use our thinking,” Mama said. “What is a fair plan?”

Benny looked closely. “There are twelve cups. You can fill six. I can fill six.”

“Smart solution,” Mama said.

Benny scooped slowly. Plop! Plop! Batter slid into the cups. Some were too full. Some were not full enough. Benny frowned. “Oh no.”

“It’s okay,” Mama said gently. “We can fix it. Respect also means being patient with mistakes.”

Benny took a deep breath. “We can use the spoon to move some batter from the big one to the small one.” He did it carefully. “There. Better.”

Mama nodded. “You solved a problem. That is critical thinking.”

Benny’s tail thumped happily. “Thunk, thunk.”

They slid the tray into the warm oven. “Tick, tick,” went the timer. “Now we wait,” Mama said.

Waiting was hard. Benny wiggled. He wanted to open the oven. He wanted to peek. He wanted to say, “Is it done? Is it done? Is it done?” But he remembered respect. The muffins needed time. The oven needed to be safe.

“I can respect the time,” Benny said. “I can listen for the timer.”

Mama smiled. “Would you like a turn to set the table while we wait?”

“Yes!” Benny put out napkins. He counted plates. “One, two.” He set two little cups. He placed a tiny honey pot in the middle. He stood back and looked. “It looks nice.”

Ding! The timer sang. Mama opened the oven. A warm, sweet smell filled the kitchen. The muffins were golden and puffy.

Benny’s eyes were wide. “Wow.” He waited for Mama to use the mitts. He waited for the tray to cool. His paws itched to grab, but he kept them back. “Respect is waiting,” he whispered.

“Now we can taste,” Mama said.

Benny chose one muffin and gave one to Mama. “Your turn first,” he said.

Mama blew on her muffin. “Thank you. That is very respectful.”

Benny took a small bite. The muffin was soft and cozy. Cinnamon danced on his tongue. “Mmm,” he hummed.

Mama took a bite too. “Benny, I am proud of you. Today you showed respect. You took turns. You used your busy beaver brain to solve problems.”

Benny smiled and hugged Mama. “Respect feels good,” he said. “It makes baking fun.”

Mama hugged back. “Respect makes everything better.”

Benny looked at the tray. “Tomorrow we can bake again. We can think. We can share. We can wait. We can be kind.”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Every day can be a respectful adventure.”

Benny nodded, happy and warm. The kitchen hummed softly. The muffins steamed gently. Their little table looked just right.

“Thank you, Mama,” Benny said.

“Thank you, Benny,” Mama said.

They clinked their cups. “Cheers to respect,” they said together, and they both smiled.

The End.
