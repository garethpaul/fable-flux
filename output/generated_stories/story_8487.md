# Fawn’s Nighttime Neighbor Club

In a cozy bedroom, a gentle deer fawn named Dottie curled up on a soft quilt. The room smelled like clean sheets and warm cocoa. Moonlight painted silver stripes on the floor. Dottie’s ears twitched at the soft tick-tock of a little clock.

“I love bedtime,” Dottie whispered. “It is quiet and safe.”

Then—tap tap. A tiny sound came from the window. Dottie lifted her head. “Hello?” she called.

A shy voice answered, “It’s me, Pip the mouse. May I come in?”

Dottie tiptoed to the window and opened it a little. Cool night air brushed her nose. Pip peeked in, whiskers twitching. “I’m cold,” Pip said. “My leaf house blew away.”

Dottie’s heart felt warm. “You can come in,” she said. “We can share.” She put a small pillow by the window. “We include all neighbors here.”

Pip climbed in and sighed with relief. “Thank you,” he said. “You are kind.”

Dottie nodded. “We help each other. That’s our bedroom rule.”

They were almost asleep when—thump, thump—came another sound from the closet. Dottie’s fur prickled. “Who’s there?” she asked, voice small but brave.

The closet door creaked, and a sleepy raccoon peeked out. “I’m Rocco,” he said. “I got lost in the dark. I followed the smell of clean blankets and… I hid in here. I’m sorry I didn’t ask first.”

Dottie took a slow breath. She felt a little scared, but she also felt strong. “Thank you for telling the truth,” she said gently. “Honesty helps us feel safe.”

Pip nodded. “Yes. We can work together.”

Dottie looked around the cozy bedroom. There was one bed, one quilt, and one small rug. Now there were three friends. “We need a plan,” she said. “Let’s make a Nighttime Neighbor Club. Our club will include everyone, keep the room tidy, and help our town be safe.”

“What is a club?” Pip squeaked.

“It’s a team,” said Dottie. “We agree on rules. We help each other. That is civic responsibility. We care for our home and for others.”

Rocco rubbed his eyes. “That sounds good. I want to do my part.”

Dottie smiled. “First, we will make space.” She folded a corner of the quilt into a cozy nest. “Pip can sleep here.” She rolled a soft sweater into a pillow. “Rocco, you can sleep on the rug. I will share my extra blanket.”

Pip sniffed the blanket. It smelled like sunshine. “Mmm.”

Rocco stretched. “This feels nice. Thank you.”

Tap tap again. Dottie opened the door and found a ladybug on the floor. “I lost my way,” the ladybug buzzed. “I am very small.”

Dottie crouched low. “You are welcome,” she said. “We include big and small.” She placed a clean tissue near the flower pot, making a tiny bed. “Here is a cozy spot.”

Soon the room felt full and brave. The clock ticked. The night hummed. Then Dottie saw something. The night-lamp blinked. Pop! The light went out. The room grew dim. Pip squeaked. Rocco bumped the dresser. The ladybug buzzed in a circle.

“It’s okay,” said Dottie. “We will solve this together.” She took a deep breath and listened to the soft night sounds. “We need light. Let’s be resourceful.”

“What is ‘resourceful’?” asked Pip.

“It means we can find smart ways to fix things,” Dottie said. “We can use what we have.”

Rocco raised his paw. “I saw a flashlight under the bed,” he whispered, remembering his hiding spot. “I didn’t take it. I just saw it.”

“Thank you for being honest,” Dottie said.

Pip scurried under the bed. “I feel something!” He pulled out a small yellow flashlight. Dottie clicked it, but nothing happened.

“The batteries are sleepy,” Rocco said.

Dottie looked around. She found a string of glow-in-the-dark stars in a drawer, a clean jar, and a shiny ribbon. “We can make a glow jar,” she said. “It will be soft and safe.”

They worked together. Pip stuck the stars on the inside of the jar. Rocco tied the ribbon around it with careful paws. Dottie held the jar up to the moonlight. The stars drank in the glow.

“One, two, three,” Dottie counted. “Now let’s turn off the moonlight with the curtain.” When she closed the curtain, the little stars in the jar glowed gently. The room filled with a calm, friendly light.

“Oooh,” said Pip.

“Wow,” said Rocco.

“Cozy,” buzzed the ladybug.

Dottie placed the glow jar in the center of the room. “Our Nighttime Neighbor Club did this,” she said. “We included everyone. We told the truth. We worked together. We cared for our space. That is how we help our home and our town.”

They all settled into their spots. Pip yawned a tiny yawn. Rocco tucked his tail. The ladybug folded her dotted wings.

“Dottie,” Pip whispered, “thank you for including me.”

“Me too,” Rocco said. “I will help return the jar to the shelf in the morning, and I will help sweep. That is my job for our club.”

The ladybug chimed, “I will fly to the window each night and check if anyone needs help finding the door.”

Dottie smiled. Her heart felt big and soft. “I am glad we are together,” she said. “When we include everyone, we make more light.”

The room was quiet again, warm and safe. The glow jar shone gently. The clock tick-tocked. Dottie closed her eyes and whispered, “Our club cares. We tell the truth. We help our home.”

And everyone slept, wrapped in kindness and a soft, steady glow.

The End.
