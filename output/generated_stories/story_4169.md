# Tessa Tortoise’s Nursery Routine

In a warm, comfortable nursery, a wise tortoise named Tessa lived on a soft blue rug. The rug felt fuzzy under her feet, and the room smelled like fresh soap and orange peels. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. Toys rested in tidy bins. Books smiled from a little shelf.

Tessa blinked slowly and smiled. “Good morning, little room,” she said. Her voice was gentle, like a lullaby.

“Good morning, Tessa!” chirped Pip the plush penguin from the pillow pile.

Tessa stretched her legs and wiggled her toes. “Today is my Daily Adventure,” she said. “I will learn healthy habits and complete my tasks. Slow and steady.”

She picked up a small list on a yellow card. It said:
- Brush
- Wash
- Eat
- Play
- Tidy
- Rest

Tessa nodded. “One by one.”

First, Tessa went to the tiny sink. The water was warm and whispered, shhhh. She squeezed a pea-sized spot of toothpaste on her small brush. “Brush, brush, brush,” she hummed. “Tiny circles, gentle and kind.” The mint smelled cool and fresh. She spat and rinsed. “Mmm! My mouth feels clean,” she said, smiling big.

Pip waddled closer and giggled. “Do my beak, too?”

“You can pretend,” Tessa chuckled. “We learn by doing.”

Next, she washed her hands. Pump, pump, went the soap. Bubbles smelled like lemons. “Front and back, between my fingers,” Tessa sang. The bubbles popped, pop pop, like tiny kisses. She rinsed and dried on a fluffy towel. “Clean hands help me stay healthy,” she said.

“Healthy!” echoed Pip, flapping his soft wings.

Tessa peeked at the yellow card. “Eat.” She opened the snack box. Inside were apple slices, crunchy carrots, and a small cup of yogurt. “Colorful foods help my body grow,” she said. She took slow bites. Crunch, crunch went the carrots. The apples were sweet and juicy. The yogurt was cool and creamy. “I sip water, too,” she said, sipping. “Water helps me think and play.”

Pip patted his tummy. “Happy tummy!”

“Happy tummies help happy days,” Tessa agreed.

The sunbeam moved across the rug. “Play time,” she read. Tessa set out a simple puzzle with big, bright shapes. “Triangle, circle, square,” she said, touching each one. She tried the triangle in the square hole. It didn’t fit.

Pip frowned. “Oh no.”

“It’s okay,” Tessa said kindly. “We learn by trying.” She slowed down. “Look with my eyes. Think with my mind.” She turned the triangle and found the triangle hole. Snap! In it went. “Slow and steady helps me learn,” Tessa said, feeling proud.

Then Tessa built a tower with soft blocks. One, two, three, four. The tower wobbled. She took a deep breath in, slow like a turtle, and placed the fifth block carefully. “I can do hard things, one step at a time,” she whispered. The tower stood tall!

“Yay!” Pip cheered. “You did it!”

Tessa clapped. “We did it together.”

She checked the yellow card. “Tidy.” Tessa hummed a tidy song. “Blocks in the bin, shapes on the shelf.” The blocks made a gentle thump. The puzzle pieces slid with a soft shhh. She folded the play mat. She lined up books—one, two, three—in a neat row.

Pip scooted a stray crayon closer. “This one!”

“Thank you, helper,” said Tessa. “When we finish our play, we tidy our space. It keeps us safe and calm.”

Now the nursery looked peaceful again. The air smelled clean. The rug felt smooth and warm.

“Rest,” Tessa read. She put on her tiny sleep cap and climbed into a cozy basket with a soft blanket. She took three turtle breaths. In… and out. In… and out. In… and out.

Pip whispered, “What did we learn today?”

Tessa smiled, eyes half-closed. “We learned that healthy habits help our bodies and minds. We brush and wash to stay clean. We eat colorful foods and drink water to feel strong. We play and try new things to learn. We tidy up to finish our tasks. We rest to be ready for more.”

Pip snuggled in beside her. “Slow and steady?”

“Slow and steady,” Tessa said. “One by one, we can do each task. We can be kind to ourselves. We can keep going.”

The nursery grew quiet. A gentle breeze carried the soft smell of lavender from the curtains. The clock ticked, tick-tock, tick-tock, like a friendly heartbeat.

Before sleep, Tessa whispered, “I am proud of my day.”

Pip whispered, “I am proud, too.”

Outside, the sun slid lower, painting the room with a cozy golden glow. The blue rug hugged the floor. The toy bins waited, tidy and content. Tessa drifted to sleep with a happy heart, ready for tomorrow’s little adventure.

And the yellow card? It rested on the shelf with a tiny checkmark by each word.

Brush. Wash. Eat. Play. Tidy. Rest.

Slow and steady, task by task, Tessa the wise tortoise had a healthy, happy day.

The End.
