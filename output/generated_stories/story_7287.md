# Leo the Lion Cub and the Colorful Plate Adventure

Leo was a brave little lion cub with golden fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, he sat at the big wooden table in his family's cozy dining room, staring at his breakfast plate with a worried frown.

"I don't want to eat this green stuff," Leo said, pushing the broccoli around with his fork. "It looks yucky!"

Mama Lion sat down beside him, her warm paw gently touching his shoulder. "Leo, sweetheart, what's wrong with trying new foods?"

"Green foods are scary," Leo whispered. "I only like chicken nuggets and cookies."

Just then, Leo's tummy made a loud rumbling sound. "Grrrrowl!" He felt tired and weak, not like the brave little lion he wanted to be.

Grandpa Lion walked into the dining room, carrying a basket full of colorful vegetables from their garden. "Good morning, Leo! Want to help me sort these rainbow vegetables?"

Leo's ears perked up. "Rainbow vegetables?"

"Yes!" Grandpa Lion smiled, setting down red tomatoes, orange carrots, yellow peppers, green broccoli, and purple eggplant. "Each color gives your body different superpowers!"

Leo's eyes grew wide. "Superpowers?"

"The red tomatoes help your heart stay strong," Grandpa explained, letting Leo touch the smooth, round tomato. "Orange carrots help your eyes see better in the dark, like a real lion hunter!"

Leo picked up a crunchy orange carrot and took a tiny bite. "Mmm! It's sweet!"

"The yellow peppers make your body fight off germs," Grandpa continued. "Green broccoli builds strong muscles for running and jumping. And purple eggplant helps your brain think smart thoughts!"

"Can I really get superpowers from eating these?" Leo asked, his tail swishing with excitement.

"Let's find out!" Mama Lion said. "How about we make a game? We'll create the most colorful plate ever!"

Leo jumped up from his chair and started moving around the dining room. "I want to help! I want to be strong like a real lion!"

Together, they washed the vegetables in the sink. Leo loved the cool water splashing on his paws and the fresh, earthy smell of the garden vegetables. He helped arrange them on his plate like a beautiful rainbow.

"Now, let's take one bite of each color," Mama Lion suggested.

Leo started with the orange carrot - crunch! Then the red tomato - so juicy! The yellow pepper was crisp and sweet. He took a brave bite of the green broccoli and discovered it tasted like tiny trees. Finally, he tried the purple eggplant, which was soft and mild.

"I feel the superpowers working!" Leo announced, flexing his little muscles and running in circles around the dining room.

After finishing his rainbow breakfast, Leo felt energetic and strong. "Mama, can we share our extra vegetables with our neighbors? Mrs. Rabbit looked hungry yesterday."

"What a wonderful idea, Leo!" Mama Lion beamed. "That's very kind of you to think of others."

They packed a basket with vegetables from their garden to share with their forest friends. Leo felt proud that he was helping others while also taking care of his own body.

From that day on, Leo always made sure his plate had lots of different colors. He learned that eating healthy foods wasn't just good for him - it helped him have energy to help others and take care of the earth by eating foods that grew in their own garden.

"Being brave means trying new things," Leo told his reflection in the dining room window, "especially when they help me grow big and strong!"

The End.
