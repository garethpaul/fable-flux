# Sunny Seeds and Slow Moments

In a sunny backyard, a resourceful squirrel named Sprig woke up to warm light on his fur. The grass smelled sweet. Birds chirped, “Cheep, cheep!” The breeze felt soft on Sprig’s whiskers.

“Today I will plant my sunflower seeds,” Sprig said, patting his small pouch. “Grandma Willow taught me to plant with care.”

Sprig hopped to a cozy spot near a little fence. The dirt was cool and crumbly. He pressed his tiny paws into it. “Hello, dirt,” he whispered. “I will be gentle.”

He took a deep breath in. He let a slow breath out. “Planting time,” he said.

Sprig dug small holes. Plop, plop, plop went the seeds. He covered them with soil and patted them softly. “Sleep tight, little seeds,” he said. “Grow big and bright.”

A blue butterfly danced by. A ladybug tickled his paw. Sprig giggled. “Friends, you can watch the garden too!”

Then Sprig’s tummy rumbled. “I want sunflowers now,” he said. He looked at the dirt. It looked the same. “Why aren’t you growing?”

Sprig frowned. He tapped his foot. He rubbed his tail. He stared at the soil. It did not change.

His friend, a small robin, hopped near. “Tweet, tweet. What’s wrong, Sprig?”

“I want flowers. I want them right now,” Sprig said. His voice wobbled. “I worked hard.”

The robin tilted her head. “Grandma Willow shared a tradition,” Sprig said softly. “She sings to seeds and waits. She says, ‘Patience is a soft blanket for tiny things.’ But waiting is hard.”

The robin chirped, “Let’s try the tradition.”

Sprig nodded. He closed his eyes. He put one paw on his heart and one paw on the ground. He felt his heart go thump-thump. He felt the cool earth under his paw.

“Breathing in,” Sprig whispered. “Breathing out.” He hummed a gentle tune Grandma taught him. “Hmmm, hmmm, grow slow and strong.”

He opened his eyes. The dirt still looked the same.

“I don’t see anything,” Sprig said, shoulders drooping.

“Waiting takes time,” the robin said kindly. “Can we do kind things while we wait?”

Sprig thought. He remembered Grandma’s words: “Use waiting time for kindness.” He looked around the sunny yard. The birdbath was low. The water shone like tiny stars.

“I can fill the birdbath,” Sprig said. He carried a small leaf-cup to the hose. Drip, drip, drip. The water was cool on his paws. He poured with care. The robin took a happy sip.

“Thank you,” she chirped.

Sprig smiled. He felt warm inside. He went back to his seeds. He listened. He heard bees buzzing by the apple tree. Bzzzz. He smelled the sweet apples and the fresh grass.

He waited. He breathed in. He breathed out.

A little caterpillar inched along the fence. “Hello,” Sprig said. “Do you need help?”

“I’m slow,” said the caterpillar. “That is my way.”

Sprig nodded. “I am learning slow too.”

He moved a leaf so the caterpillar could climb easily. “Thank you,” the caterpillar said.

Sprig went back to the garden bed. He wanted to poke the dirt, but he remembered Grandma’s voice. “Patience is gentle.”

He whispered, “I will be gentle.”

The sun moved across the sky. The warm light touched Sprig’s ears. He felt the tickle of grass on his toes. He heard the soft whoosh of wind. He sang again, “Hmmm, hmmm, grow slow and strong.”

Just then, Sprig’s neighbor, a gray rabbit named Loppy, hopped over. “Hi, Sprig,” said Loppy. “What are you doing?”

“I planted sunflower seeds,” Sprig said. “I am waiting with patience. I am also doing kind things while I wait.”

Loppy smiled. “Can I help?”

“Yes,” said Sprig. “We can make a small fence so paws stay off the bed.” They placed tiny sticks around the soil. Tap, tap, tap. Sprig shared Grandma’s tradition. “We sing to the seeds and we breathe slow.”

Loppy hummed too. Together they breathed in. They breathed out. They felt calm.

Sprig looked at the soil again. He noticed a tiny green tip peeking up. “Oh!” he squeaked. “Look, look! A sprout!”

“It’s so small,” Loppy said.

“It is doing its slow work,” Sprig said, eyes bright. “My patience helped me see it.”

Sprig did not shout or jump on the bed. He knelt close and whispered, “Welcome, little sprout.”

The robin fluttered her wings. “Your kindness and calm helped the time pass.”

Sprig nodded. “I learned something. When I wait with patience, I feel peaceful. When I am kind and mindful, I can enjoy the waiting time.”

He touched his heart and took one more slow breath. He looked around at his friends, the birdbath, the caterpillar, the sunny backyard, and the tiny sprout.

“Growing takes time,” Sprig said. “I can be patient.”

Days later, yellow petals opened like little suns. Sprig shared seeds with Loppy and the robin. “This is our tradition,” he said. “We plant, we wait with patience, we are kind, and we enjoy each slow moment.”

They all hummed Sprig’s song in the warm light. “Hmmm, hmmm, grow slow and strong.”

Sprig smiled. “Patience helped me, and patience can help you too.”

The End.
