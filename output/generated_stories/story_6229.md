# Skip, Stretch, and Study, Squirrel!

In a quiet study corner, by a little desk and a tall shelf of books, lived a resourceful squirrel named Sprig. The study corner was cozy. A small lamp made a warm, yellow glow. The chair felt soft like a cloud. The pencil cup smelled like fresh wood. Sprig loved the rustle of paper and the squeak of clean erasers.

Sprig liked to learn. He liked to draw shapes and practice letters. But today, his body felt wiggly. His tail twitched and swished. His feet tapped, tap-tap-tap, on the floor.

“I want to move!” Sprig said. “But I also want to study.”

Sprig looked at his paper. The lines looked long. The letters looked squished. His nose wrinkled. His cheeks puffed.

“I can’t!” he squeaked, and his tail thumped. His tiny hands clenched into fists. A warm grumpy feeling bubbled up in his belly, like a pot about to boil.

Sprig took a big breath in. He smelled the lemony scent of the table wipes. He let the breath out slowly, like blowing on a dandelion. But the grumpy feeling was still there.

“I need a plan,” said Sprig. “I am a resourceful squirrel!”

He remembered something his friend Finch had chirped: “When your body is busy, let it move in a safe way.”

Sprig nodded. “Yes. I can move and then study.”

He made a little sign and taped it on the lamp. The sign said, “Move, then Learn.” He smiled. The letters looked nicer already.

“First, a wiggle break,” he told himself.

Sprig stood up on the soft mat by the desk. He spread his arms wide. “Hello, arms!” he giggled. He stretched them up, up, up to the lamp’s warm light. He felt his back grow long. He wiggled his fingers. They tingled like tiny bells.

Next, Sprig did five big jumps. “Boing! Boing! Boing! Boing! Boing!” His feet landed with soft thumps. The room echoed with happy sounds. The smell of dust shook up from the rug—achoo! Sprig laughed.

He marched in place. “Left, right! Left, right!” His tail swished like a flag. He twisted side to side—swish, swish—like tall grass in the wind.

Sprig stopped and put a tiny paw on his chest. His heart went thump-thump, fast and proud. His face felt warm. The grumpy feeling had melted, like butter on toast.

“Now calm,” he said. He took another big breath in through his nose for four counts. “One, two, three, four.” He let it out for four. “One, two, three, four.” His shoulders softened. His paws relaxed.

Sprig hopped back into the chair. He picked up his pencil. “Hello, letters,” he said. He traced a big S. “S is for Sprig. S is for Stretch!” He smiled. The S looked smooth. He drew three circles. “Round and round,” he hummed.

After a few minutes, the wiggly feeling came back like a tiny drum. Tap. Tap. Tap.

Sprig did not thump or puff. He noticed. He nodded. “Body wants to move again. That’s okay.”

He made a new plan. “We will have Movement Time, Work Time, and Clean Time.”

He drew little pictures on sticky notes:
- A bouncing ball for Movement Time.
- A pencil for Work Time.
- A soap bubble for Clean Time.

“Let’s try,” he said.

Movement Time! Sprig played a quiet game. He tiptoed to the door and back, heel-to-toe, heel-to-toe. The floor felt cool and smooth. He hopped over an invisible river—hop! He did star shapes with his body. “Shine!” he giggled.

Work Time! Sprig sat and wrote his name. He counted acorns on his page. “One, two, three, four.” He drew a tiny map of the study corner. He added the chair, the lamp, and the big book on the shelf.

Then Sprig remembered hygiene. “Clean Time,” he sang.

He pushed in the chair gently. He put the pencil back in the cup. He picked up a soft cloth. “Wipe, wipe,” he said. The desk smelled lemony again. He washed his paws at the little sink by the door. The water felt cool and splashy. “Swish, swish,” he scrubbed with soap. Bubbles popped like tiny stars. “Bye-bye, germs!” he giggled. He dried his paws on a clean towel. “All fresh.”

Just then, Sprig saw a tricky picture in his book. It looked zig-zaggy and hard. The warm grumpy feeling peeked back in.

Sprig paused. He put a paw on his belly. “Hello, feeling,” he whispered. “You can be here. I will move and breathe.”

He stood up and did three slow stretches. Up like a tall tree. Out like a wide bridge. Down to touch his toes—hello, toes! He counted breathing again. “In for four. Out for four.” His belly felt soft.

Now he looked at the picture. “I can try,” he said.

He traced the zig. He traced the zag. He went slow. He smiled. “I did it!”

“Movement helps me learn,” Sprig said out loud. “Exercise gives my body happy energy. Breathing keeps my feelings calm. Cleaning keeps my space and paws healthy.”

He stuck his sticky notes on the edge of the desk. He wiggled his tail in a happy dance. The study corner felt bright. The lamp hummed. The desk shone. Sprig’s heart felt light.

Sprig whispered to the room, “Move, then learn. Breathe, then try. Clean, then smile.”

He packed away his paper. He took one last deep breath. The lemon scent tickled his nose.

“I’m proud,” he said. “I can make a plan when things are hard.”

The End.
