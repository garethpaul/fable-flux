# Miri Mouse Makes a Rainbow Map

Miri was a kind little mouse who lived in a playful living room. The room was full of soft pillows, tickly rugs, and boxes of bright crayons. The sun peeked in through the window, making little squares of warm light on the floor.

Miri stretched her tiny paws. “Today, I will make something special,” she squeaked. “I will make a Rainbow Map! It can help friends find cozy spots to read and play.”

Her tail twitched with excitement. She pulled out a big piece of paper. It felt smooth under her paws. She sniffed the crayon box. “Mmm,” she said, “it smells like fruit and fun.”

Her friend Pip, a small, shy puppy, padded over. His ears were floppy and soft. “What is a Rainbow Map?” Pip asked softly.

“It is a picture of our living room,” Miri said. “With colors for feelings and places. Red for dancing. Orange for building blocks. Yellow for reading. Green for quiet time. Blue for music. Purple for nap corner.”

Pip wagged his tail. “That sounds happy.”

“It will be,” said Miri. “Will you help me?”

Pip nodded. “I can bring pillows. I can carry small things.”

Miri smiled. “Thank you. Let’s start.”

Miri pressed a red crayon to the paper. Scritch, scritch. The sound was gentle. The color was bright and warm. “This red spot is for dancing,” she said. “Stomp, twirl, clap!”

Pip clapped his paws. “I like dancing,” he said, bouncing a little.

Next, Miri picked up orange. It felt waxy and smooth. “This is the block corner,” she said. “We can build tall and wide.” She drew tiny squares and triangles. Tap, tap went her paw on the paper.

Then she reached for yellow. “This is our reading light,” she whispered, drawing a sun with rays. “Warm and calm.” The sun looked friendly.

Miri paused. She looked at the big paper. “It is a lot to draw,” she said. “I will keep going. One color at a time.”

Pip brought her a sip of water in a tiny cup. “You can do it,” he said. “You are brave and kind.”

Miri smiled again and took a sip. “Thank you, helper.”

She held the green crayon. “This is the quiet corner,” she said, drawing a little leaf and a soft rug. “Here we take deep breaths. In and out.” Miri breathed in and out. Pip did too. The room felt calm.

When she picked up the blue crayon, it snapped! Crack!

Miri’s whiskers drooped. “Oh no,” she said. “Now I cannot draw the music spot.”

Pip sniffed the broken blue. “We can still use it,” he said. “We can wrap a tiny cloth around it so it won’t hurt your paw.”

Together, they wrapped the blue crayon in a little square of soft cloth. Miri tried again. Scritch, scritch. It worked!

She drew a little drum and a wavy song line. “Blue is for music,” she said. “Tap, tap, hum, hum.”

Then came purple. Miri reached into the box. She frowned. “I cannot find purple.”

Pip looked under a pillow. “I can’t find it either.”

Miri sat very still. She wiggled her nose. “What can we do?” she asked.

Pip’s tail swished. “We can make purple,” he said. “We can try red and blue together.”

Miri’s eyes lit up. “Yes! We can try. We can mix.”

She took a bit of red and a bit of blue and rubbed them together on the paper. The colors blended. A soft purple appeared. “We made purple!” Miri squeaked.

“Purple for nap corner,” Pip said, curling up for a tiny test nap. He yawned. “It feels snuggly.”

Miri looked at the paper again. The Rainbow Map was almost done. She added little pawprints to show paths from place to place. Pitter-patter, pitter-patter went her tiny paws in paint. She drew a big heart in the middle.

“There,” she said. “But we still need signs for our friends. Words can help.”

Pip nodded. “I will hold the paper still.”

Miri wrote simple labels: Dance, Blocks, Read, Quiet, Music, Nap. Her writing was careful and clear. The room smelled like crayons and warm sunshine. It sounded like gentle hums and tiny claps.

Just then, Squeak the baby mouse peeked out from behind a cushion. “Play?” Squeak asked.

Miri held up the Rainbow Map. “Yes,” she said. “This shows where and how. Do you want to try?”

Squeak pointed to yellow. “Read.”

Miri smiled. “Great choice.” She led Squeak to the reading light. Pip brought a pillow. They sat together. Miri opened a small picture book. The pages rustled. Squeak cuddled close. Pip rested his head on Miri’s lap.

After story time, they all danced on the red spot. Then they built a tiny bridge in the orange corner. They took deep breaths in the green space. They tapped a rhythm in the blue spot. At last, Squeak curled up in the purple nap corner, cozy and safe.

Pip whispered, “Your map helped us. We finished it, even when it was hard.”

Miri felt warm inside. “We kept trying,” she said. “We used our ideas. We helped each other.”

She looked at the Rainbow Map on the wall. It glowed with color. It was their art. It was their plan.

Lesson: When we use our creativity, keep trying, and help friends, we can make something beautiful and finish our tasks. We can share our ideas, and our ideas can help everyone play, rest, and learn.

The End.
