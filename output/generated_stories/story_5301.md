# Benny's Musical Thank You

Benny the beaver loved to build things with his strong teeth and busy paws. But today was special - it was his first day in the musical practice room at the forest school!

"Wow!" said Benny, looking around the bright room. Shiny instruments sparkled on shelves. A big piano sat in the corner, and colorful drums were lined up near the window. The room smelled like fresh wood polish and sounded quiet and peaceful.

Mrs. Robin, the music teacher, flew in with a cheerful chirp. "Welcome, Benny! Today you'll learn to play music with your new friends."

Soon, other young animals arrived. There was Lily the lamb with her soft white wool, Max the mouse with his tiny whiskers twitching, and Zoe the zebra with her beautiful black and white stripes.

"I want the big drum!" Benny said loudly, rushing toward the biggest, shiniest drum. His flat tail slapped the floor with excitement.

"Actually," said Mrs. Robin gently, "we take turns choosing instruments. Lily was here first yesterday, so she gets to pick first today."

Benny's ears drooped. That didn't seem fair! He was bigger and stronger than little Lily.

Lily chose a small, silver triangle that made the prettiest tinkling sound. *Ting, ting, ting!*

Max picked tiny maracas that rattled like autumn leaves. *Shake, shake, shake!*

Zoe selected a tambourine that jingled like happy bells. *Jingle, jangle, ring!*

"Now Benny," smiled Mrs. Robin, "what would you like?"

Only a small wooden flute was left. Benny looked at it sadly. It wasn't big or loud like he wanted.

"I don't want that tiny thing," Benny grumbled, crossing his furry arms.

Mrs. Robin sat beside him. "Benny, let's try something. Close your eyes and listen."

Benny closed his eyes tight. He heard Lily's triangle singing sweetly, Max's maracas dancing with rhythm, and Zoe's tambourine adding sparkly sounds.

"What do you hear?" asked Mrs. Robin.

"It sounds... beautiful," Benny whispered, opening his eyes wide.

"Each instrument is special," explained Mrs. Robin. "Even the smallest flute can make the most wonderful melodies."

Benny picked up the wooden flute. When he blew gently, it made a warm, singing sound that reminded him of wind through the trees. *Toot, toot, toooo!*

"That's lovely!" said Lily, clapping her hooves.

"You're really good at that!" squeaked Max.

"It sounds like a bird singing!" neighed Zoe happily.

Benny felt warm and happy inside. His friends liked his music!

"Let's all play together," suggested Mrs. Robin.

The friends made the most wonderful music. Benny's flute sang the melody while the others kept the beat. The practice room filled with joyful sounds that made everyone smile and dance.

"Thank you, Mrs. Robin, for teaching me that every instrument is special," said Benny. "And thank you, friends, for being so kind about my flute playing."

"Thank you for making such pretty music, Benny!" said Lily.

"And thank you for sharing practice time fairly," added Max.

As they cleaned up, Benny realized something important. Being grateful for what he had made him much happier than wanting what others had. Every instrument was different, just like every friend was different, and that made their music wonderful.

"Tomorrow, we'll switch instruments!" announced Mrs. Robin.

"Hooray!" cheered all the friends together.

Benny's tail wiggled with joy. He couldn't wait to try new instruments and make more beautiful music with his grateful heart.

The End.
