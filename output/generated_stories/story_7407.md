# Fern the Fawn Finds Fairness

Fern was a gentle deer fawn with soft, speckled fur and bright, curious eyes. Today, Fern was visiting Mama’s home office. The room smelled like warm paper and pine pencils. The keyboard made tiny tap-tap sounds. The window let in a golden square of sunshine that felt cozy on Fern’s back.

On the floor, there were three baskets: one with crayons, one with sticky notes, and one with shiny paper clips that glimmered like little fish. Mama said, “You may help, Fern. Please sort the crayons by color. That is your job.” Fern nodded. “I can do it,” she said softly. She liked helping. She liked doing the right thing.

Fern hummed while she worked, plop-plop, red crayon here, blue crayon there. The crayon wax felt smooth and smelled sweet. Fern smiled. Doing a fair job felt good.

Suddenly, the door clicked. In trotted Bristle, the neighbor porcupine, with prickles that shivered like tiny drums. “Hi, Fern!” Bristle said. “Let’s use those shiny paper clips to build a bridge!” He reached his paws toward the clip basket.

Fern blinked. “Oh, Bristle,” she said. “Mama gave me a job. The paper clips are for Mama’s papers. I am sorting crayons.”

Bristle frowned. “But I want them now.” He scooped a pawful anyway. The clips jingled and fell like metal raindrops.

Fern’s belly felt tight. This did not seem fair. The clips were not hers or Bristle’s. They were Mama’s. Fern took a slow breath, just like Mama taught her. In, out. Self-care helps calm my body, she thought.

Then Fern spoke in a gentle voice. “Bristle, we need to be fair. Fair means everyone’s things are safe, and everyone gets a turn. These clips are not for playing.”

Bristle puffed his cheeks. “But my bridge!” His quills rustled, ticka-ticka-tick, like dry leaves.

Fern remembered another rule: Use kind words. Ask for help if you need it. She tried again. “Let’s find a safe and fair way. We can use sticky notes for a bridge. They are for practicing. And we can ask Mama if we can use just two clips later.”

Bristle crossed his paws. “Hmph.”

Just then, the printer whirred, whirrr-bzz, and a paper slid out with Mama’s big words. Mama turned in her chair. “I hear tricky feelings,” she said. “What happened?”

Fern stood tall, even though her legs were wobbly. “Mama, Bristle took the clips. I want to be fair and safe. The clips are for your work. I asked to use sticky notes instead.”

Mama nodded. “Thank you for telling the truth with kind words. That is justice. Justice means we choose what is right and fair. It keeps people and things safe.”

Bristle’s quills drooped a little. “I didn’t think about fair,” he said. “I just wanted a bridge.”

Mama smiled softly. “Wanting is okay. But we must keep things safe and use them the right way. Let’s make a plan.”

Fern’s ears perked. She liked plans. Plans help solve problems, she thought.

Mama said, “Plan: Fern finishes sorting crayons. Bristle may use sticky notes to build a practice bridge on the mat. After I finish my paper, I will lend two clips. We will count them in and count them back. That is fair and safe.”

Bristle looked at the sticky notes. They were bright like lemon and peach. “Okay,” he said. “I can wait.” He handed the clips back. They chimed, ting-ting, as they landed in the basket.

Fern felt warm and proud. “Thank you, Bristle. Let’s build together.” She finished the reds, blues, and greens. Her sorting made a rainbow line. It looked neat and happy.

On the mat, the two friends folded sticky notes into little ramps. “Zoom!” said Fern as a tiny eraser rolled down. Bristle giggled. “Bridge!” His quills wiggled with joy.

Soon, Mama clapped softly. “My paper is done. Time for two clips.” She held up two fingers. “We will use them safely, and we will return them. That is the rule.”

Bristle took one clip. Fern took one clip. They clipped the sticky-note ramps together. The bridge held! The eraser rolled across with a soft shhh. Both friends cheered.

When playtime was over, Fern said, “Time to return the clips.” She and Bristle counted, “One, two,” and dropped them back. The basket made a happy clink.

Mama smiled. “You solved a problem with justice and care. You kept things safe. You used kind voices. You made a fair plan.”

Bristle nodded. “I learned to wait and to ask. That feels good.”

Fern’s belly felt calm and light. “Justice is fair and kind,” she said. “It helps everyone.”

Mama gave them each a sip of cool water. “And we take care of ourselves, too. Bodies need rest and water after big feelings.” The water felt crisp, like tiny bells, and made Fern’s tongue tingle.

They cleaned up together. Crayons in a rainbow row. Sticky notes in a stack. Clips counted and safe. The home office looked neat, and the sun made the desk shine.

Bristle waved goodbye. “See you, Fern!” His quills made a soft swish as he went out.

Fern curled by the window square of sun. She felt proud, safe, and sleepy. She whispered, “Be fair. Be kind. Be safe.” The words felt like a lullaby.

The End.
