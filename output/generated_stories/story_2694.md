# Tiko the Tortoise Builds at the Table

Tiko the wise tortoise lived in a cozy house with a sunny dining room. The table was smooth like a lake, and the chairs made soft squeaks when they moved. Morning light made warm squares on the floor. It smelled like toast and sweet jam.

“Good morning, table,” said Tiko in a gentle voice. He patted the wooden table with his tiny claws. Tap, tap. “Today, we build.”

Tiko had a big idea. He wanted to build a tower. He rolled his shell slowly to the shelf and brought back blocks, cups, and napkin rings. The blocks were bright red, blue, and yellow. The cups were light and wobbly. The napkin rings felt cool and round. He set everything on the table. Clink. Clack.

His friends came to visit. Miri the mouse peeked over the edge. “Squeak! Can I help?” she asked, whiskers twitching.

“Of course,” said Tiko, smiling. “We build together. We use kind words. We solve problems peacefully.”

Pip the parrot fluttered to a chair back. “Squawk! Build, build!” he sang. His feathers shimmered green and gold.

“Let’s start with a strong base,” Tiko said. “A strong base helps the tower stand tall.” He placed two big blocks side by side. Thunk. “We go slow. Slow is smart.”

Miri set a napkin ring on top. “Round and round,” she giggled.

Pip added a blue cup. “Squawk!” The cup wobbled. The tower leaned.

Tiko put a steady paw on the table. “Let’s pause,” he said softly. “Look. Listen. How does our tower feel?”

They listened. The dining room was quiet, with little sounds: the tick of a clock, the purr of the fridge, the tiny cup wobble—wibble, wobble.

“It feels wobbly,” Miri said.

“Yes,” Tiko nodded. “We can fix it. We can build better.” He slid a flat block under the cup. “Strong under soft.”

The tower stood taller. “Hooray!” Pip sang.

Then a little bump—bump! Pip’s tail touched the tower. The cups shook. The blocks rattled. The whole tower tumbled down. Clatter, clink, clonk!

Miri’s eyes went big. “Oh no!”

Pip drooped. “I broke it,” he squawked softly.

Tiko took a slow breath. In…out. He smiled kindly. “It fell, yes. That is okay. We can try again. That is a growth mindset. We learn. We keep going.”

Pip blinked. “We keep going?” he asked.

“We keep going,” said Tiko. “And we tell the truth.” He looked at Pip with warm eyes.

Pip raised a wing. “I bumped it. I’m sorry,” he said.

“Thank you for being honest,” Tiko said. “That is integrity. Now, let’s help each other.”

They picked up the blocks. Tap. Picked up the cups. Clink. Miri stacked the napkin rings. Ring, ring, ring.

“Plan,” Tiko said. “We plan before we build. A plan is a picture in your mind. First, big blocks. Then flat pieces. Then light things on top.”

“Slow and steady,” Miri sang.

“Slow and steady,” Pip echoed.

They tried again. Tiko pressed two big blocks flat. Miri slid a flat block like a little bridge. Pip gently placed a cup. No wobble this time. The tower felt strong.

Pip fluffed his feathers. “I will flap very slowly,” he said. “Soft wings.” He moved like a cloud. Whoosh—very quiet.

The tower grew. Red. Yellow. Blue. Cup. Ring. Block. Higher and higher. The dining room light made the colors glow. The table felt warm under their paws and claws. Tiko’s heart felt warm too.

From the doorway, Rolo the rabbit peeked in. “Can I build?” he asked, ears tall.

“Yes,” said Tiko. “We have space. We share.”

Rolo hopped to the table and reached for the top. His paw bumped a cup. Wobble!

“Freeze like a statue,” Tiko said calmly. Everyone froze. The wobble slowed…stopped.

Tiko smiled. “Good job. When things wobble, we pause, we breathe, we fix.” He moved a napkin ring to the middle and pressed down gently. “Now add the last piece, Rolo.”

Rolo placed a tiny spoon at the very top. Ting! The tower stood straight and tall, taller than a juice box, taller than Rolo’s ears.

“We did it!” Miri cheered.

Pip sang a happy song. “Build, build, we can build! Slow and kind, we use our mind!”

They all looked at the tower. Tiko felt proud, but he also felt peaceful. “We built more than a tower,” he said. “We built trust. We built kindness. We built calm.”

Miri hugged Tiko’s shell. “You are a wise builder.”

Tiko chuckled. “You are wise builders too. We listened. We told the truth. We helped. We tried again.”

The clock ticked. The fridge hummed. Sunlight warmed their faces. Tiko carefully took a picture with his mind. “Click,” he said softly. “I will remember this.”

They cleaned up together. Blocks into the box—thunk. Cups into the tray—clink. Napkin rings in a stack—ring, ring.

Pip asked, “Can we build tomorrow?”

Tiko nodded. “Yes. We can build every day—towers, ideas, and good habits. When things fall, we breathe, we fix, we try again.”

Miri smiled. “Building is fun.”

Tiko patted the table. “Building is how we grow.”

The End.
