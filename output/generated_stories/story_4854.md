# Pebble the Penguin’s Kind Colors

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a wiggly waddle. Pebble loved colors. Red like strawberries, blue like the ocean, and green like big leafy trees. Today, Pebble flapped into the creative art room, humming a happy tune.

The art room smelled like fresh paint and clean paper. Sunlight came in through the windows and made shiny shapes on the floor. There were soft brushes, jars of paint, bins of paper scraps, and baskets of buttons and bottle caps to reuse.

Pebble clapped little flippers. “I will make a big bright picture!” Pebble said. “A picture of our blue ocean home!”

Pebble picked up a soft brush and dipped it in blue paint. Swish, swish. The paper sang with blue waves. Pebble added white dots for bubbles. Tap, tap. Pebble giggled.

Just then, two friends waddled in. Seal, with a warm smile, carried a water cup. Puffin, with colorful beak, carried a basket of saved bottle caps.

“Hi, Pebble!” said Seal. “We came to make art too.”

“Hello!” said Puffin. “Let’s make the ocean together.”

Pebble nodded. “Yes! Let’s all paint!”

Pebble’s brush danced. But Pebble painted very fast, and little splashes fell. A drop of blue plopped onto Puffin’s wing.

“Oh!” Puffin said softly. “That is a little messy.”

Pebble didn’t notice. Pebble was excited. “Look at the waves!” Pebble said.

Seal filled the water cup at the sink. Drip, drip, drip. Seal walked back, careful and slow. Pebble rushed by, bumping Seal. The cup tipped, and water spilled onto the floor.

Splash! The water made a small puddle.

Seal blinked. “Oops,” said Seal. “The floor is slippery now.”

Pebble stopped and frowned. “I want to make art,” Pebble said. “I want it fast.”

Puffin set down the basket of bottle caps. The caps clicked and clacked. “We can use these to make fish,” Puffin said. “We saved them so they won’t go into the ocean. That helps the Earth.”

Pebble looked at the caps. Pebble cared about the ocean. Pebble loved to help. Pebble took a deep breath. The air smelled like lemons from the clean floor soap.

“I hear you,” Pebble said. “I was going too fast. I did not notice the mess.”

Seal smiled kindly. “We can clean together,” Seal said. “We can understand what each friend needs.”

Pebble nodded. “I want to understand,” Pebble said. “Puffin, you need clean wings. Seal, you need a dry floor. I need to go slow.”

“Good plan,” said Puffin, cheerful again.

They found soft towels in a tidy drawer. Swish, swish, they cleaned the water. Pebble dabbed the blue spot off Puffin’s wing with gentle taps. “I am sorry,” Pebble said. “Are you okay?”

“I feel better,” Puffin said with a bright smile. “Thank you for understanding.”

Seal put a sign by the sink: “Walk slow. Wet floor.” Pebble traced the letters with a flipper. The sign felt bumpy. “This will help others,” Pebble said.

Now the room felt calm again. Pebble listened. The brushes made soft sounds. The sun felt warm on Pebble’s back. Pebble felt ready to try again, slower this time.

“Let’s make an ocean picture together,” Pebble said. “We can reuse things so we don’t hurt the sea.”

Puffin poured a little glue. “We can make fish from these caps,” Puffin said.

Seal lined up scraps of green paper. “These can be seaweed,” Seal said.

Pebble painted smooth blue waves, taking time. “Swish,” Pebble whispered. “Slow and steady.”

Puffin pressed yellow caps for little fish. Click, click. Seal made seaweed from long green strips. Rustle, rustle. Pebble added white paint for foam on the waves. Tap, tap. They all smiled.

Soon, the picture looked like a happy ocean. Fish swam. Seaweed swayed. The waves sparkled.

Pebble looked around. The table had a few paint drops. The floor had some paper bits. “Let’s make the room happy too,” Pebble said. “We can clean up so others can make art after us.”

“Yes,” said Seal. “Helping others is kind.”

“Yes,” said Puffin. “And keeping things clean helps the Earth.”

They wiped the table with a soft cloth. They put bottle caps back in the basket. They put paper bits in the recycle bin. The room smelled fresh and looked neat. Pebble felt proud and peaceful.

A little otter peeked in the door. “Can I paint next?” the otter asked.

“Yes!” said Pebble. “We kept the room ready for you.”

The otter saw the sign by the sink. “Walk slow. Wet floor,” the otter read. “Thank you for this,” the otter said. “Now I know to be careful.”

Pebble’s heart felt warm. “We learned to understand,” Pebble said. “We listened and helped. We took care of our room and our ocean.”

Puffin nodded. “When we understand each other, we make good choices.”

Seal added, “When we help others and care for our Earth, everyone is safer and happier.”

Pebble looked at the ocean picture. “Our art shows our hearts,” Pebble said.

They all hugged with happy squeaks. The sun slid a golden stripe across the clean floor. The brushes rested. The room was ready for more kind art.

Pebble whispered, “Slow, kind, and clean.” Pebble smiled a big penguin smile.

And that day, Pebble kept painting with care, listening to friends, and loving the blue, blue sea.

The End.
