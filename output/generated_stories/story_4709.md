# Polly's Amazing Attic Adventure

High up in the quiet attic of an old house lived a bright green parrot named Polly. Her colorful feathers sparkled red, blue, and yellow in the dusty sunbeams that peeked through the small window. The attic was peaceful and cozy, filled with old boxes, forgotten treasures, and cobwebs that danced in the gentle breeze.

Every morning, Polly's tummy would rumble loudly. "Squawk! I'm so hungry!" she would say, flapping her wings. For weeks, she had been waiting for someone to bring her food and fresh water. But the family who lived in the house below had moved away, and no one came to visit the attic anymore.

One particularly quiet morning, Polly felt very sad and worried. "What will I do?" she chirped softly. "I can't just wait here forever. My water bowl is almost empty, and I haven't eaten in two days!"

As she looked around the dusty attic, something caught her bright black eye. There, in the corner, sat an old wooden box overflowing with colorful items. Polly hopped closer, her tiny claws clicking on the wooden floor.

"Oh my!" she squawked excitedly. Inside the box were shiny bottle caps, pieces of string, colorful buttons, and even some old nuts that had rolled under a blanket years ago.

Polly's creative mind began to spin with ideas. "I can use these things to help myself!" she said proudly. First, she carefully arranged the bottle caps under the leaky spot in the roof where rainwater dripped. *Plink, plink, plink* went the water drops, filling up her new water dishes.

"That's much better!" Polly chirped, taking a refreshing drink.

Next, she noticed the window was stuck shut, making the attic stuffy. Using her strong beak and clever thinking, Polly tied the pieces of string together to make a long rope. She looped it around the window latch and pulled with all her might.

*Creeeeak!* The window opened wide, letting in fresh, cool air that ruffled her beautiful feathers.

"Ahh, that feels wonderful!" she sighed happily.

For food, Polly discovered she could crack open the old nuts with her powerful beak. She also learned to catch the tiny insects that flew through the open window. "I'm getting quite good at taking care of myself!" she said proudly.

As days passed, Polly became more and more confident. She learned to collect rainwater in different containers, organize her food supplies, and even build a comfortable nest from the soft materials she found.

"You know what?" Polly said to herself one sunny morning, preening her bright feathers. "I don't need to wait for someone else to take care of me. I can do it all by myself!"

She had learned to be self-reliant, using what she had instead of wasting things, and being independent and strong. The quiet attic had become her perfect home, and she had become the perfect caretaker for herself.

From that day forward, Polly felt proud and happy. She had discovered that she could solve her own problems and take care of herself beautifully.

"Squawk! I did it all by myself!" she sang joyfully, spreading her colorful wings wide in the warm sunlight.

The End.
