# Rusty’s Music Room Mission

Rusty was a helpful robot dog. His ears were shiny like bells. His nose was a soft button that went boop when he sniffed. He lived in a musical practice room where children came to sing and play.

The room smelled like wood and lemon polish. Sunlight made the floor warm. Drums sat in a row like little round tables. A big window showed the outside yard, green and bright.

“Good morning, Rusty!” said Mia, a small girl with curly hair. She hugged Rusty’s smooth metal neck.

“Woof-beep!” Rusty wagged his tail. His tail made a happy whirr-whirr sound.

Mia pointed to a pile of wooden sticks and colorful scarves. “We need a music stand. Our old one is wobbly. Can we build one?”

Rusty blinked his light-up eyes. “Yes. We can build and help,” he said in his gentle robot voice. “We can also go outside to test our music. Outdoor play is good for our bodies.”

Mia clapped. “Outside! I like to move and breathe fresh air.”

Rusty sniffed. Boop. “First, we plan. Planning helps us build safely.” He tapped the floor with a tiny metal paw. Tick-tick. “We will make a simple stand: a base, a tall stick, and a top tray for music.”

Mia spread the wooden sticks. “These can be the base!”

Rusty nodded. “Good idea. You are learning to build.” He handed her a soft band to tie the pieces. “We will help each other.”

They worked together. The wood felt smooth. The scarf was soft like a cloud. Rusty held the sticks steady. Mia tied a big cross shape. Rusty added a long stick in the middle. Click! It fit. On top, they used a flat board for the tray. Tap-tap-tap, Rusty pressed the edges snug with his careful paw tool.

Mia smiled. “We built it! It stands!”

Rusty gently wiggled the stand. It did not wobble. “Strong and safe,” he said. “Now, music!”

Mia picked up a small drum. Rusty chose tiny jingle bells. They tried a song. Ding-ding! Boom-boom! The music filled the room like warm soup fills a bowl.

Just then, they heard a faint voice from outside the window. “Help, please!” It was Lenny, the neighbor boy. He waved from the yard. “The garden stage fell down. We need a place to play music outside.”

Rusty looked at Mia. “Helping others is kind,” he said. “Outdoor activity helps us grow strong and calm. Shall we go?”

Mia nodded. “Yes! Let’s help!”

They carried the new stand carefully. The door opened with a soft creak. Outside, the air felt cool and fresh on their cheeks. The grass smelled sweet. Birds sang chip-chip-chirp. Rusty’s paws hummed on the path. Whirr-click, whirr-click.

In the yard, Lenny pointed to a little stage made of crates. “It fell,” he said sadly. “We want to sing outside.”

Rusty wagged. “We can build together. We will learn and move our bodies. Teamwork!”

Mia, Lenny, and Rusty gathered crates, flat boards, and a big picnic blanket. Rusty said, “First, we check the ground.” He patted the grass. “Flat is best. It keeps us safe.”

They pushed the crates to a smooth spot. Thump, thump. Rusty showed them how to line up the crates like blocks. “One, two, three,” Mia counted. Counting made her feel calm and smart.

Lenny brought a board. It was rough, so Rusty sanded the edges with a soft buzzing tool. Bzz-bzz. “Smooth now,” Rusty said. “Touch with your hand.” Lenny touched. “It’s safe!”

Together they lifted the board onto the crates. Lift, breathe, set down. Rusty reminded them, “Bend your knees. Use your legs. That keeps your body strong.”

They spread the picnic blanket on top. The stage looked bright and cozy. The wind picked up a corner of the blanket. Flutter-flutter. Mia giggled and smoothed it down.

Rusty placed the new music stand on the stage. It stood tall and steady. “We built it. We helped. We learned,” Rusty said, his eyes twinkling.

Mia beat the drum. Boom-boom. Lenny shook maracas. Shaka-shaka. Rusty rang his bells. Ding-ding. The music sounded happy in the open air. A warm breeze touched their faces. The sun felt like a soft hug.

“Let’s try a movement song,” Mia said. Rusty wagged. “Yes. Outdoor activity is fun!”

Rusty sang in his friendly robot tone:
“Step and stretch,
Bend and sway,
Breathe in fresh air,
Move and play!”

They marched around the stage. Step-step-step. They stretched their arms up to the sky. They bent like trees in the wind. They laughed and felt strong.

Lenny smiled big. “Thank you, Rusty. You helped us. You taught us to build and to be safe. And you showed us it feels good to move outside.”

Rusty’s tail whirred. “Woof-beep! Helping others makes hearts shine. Building with friends teaches us patience. Playing outside helps our bodies and our minds.”

Mia hugged Rusty. “I learned to plan, to count, and to breathe. I feel brave.”

Rusty looked at the garden, the stage, and the happy friends. “Today’s lesson,” he said, “is: Go outside, help someone, and build something simple and safe. Your body learns. Your heart smiles.”

They played one more song for the birds and the breeze. The birds chirped back like tiny singers. The sun began to lower, painting the sky peach and gold.

“See you tomorrow,” Lenny said, waving.

Back in the musical practice room, Mia put the extra sticks away. Rusty set his bells on the shelf. The room glowed softly.

“Good work today,” Mia whispered.

Rusty curled beside the drum. “Good work together,” he replied.

The End.
