# Penny Penguin’s Practice Song

Penny was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a bouncy waddle. She loved music. One snowy morning, she slipped into the musical practice room at the community center. The room smelled like clean wood and lemon polish. Shiny drums sat in a row. A piano waited with white and black keys. Colorful scarves hung on hooks, fluttering like little flags. Penny clapped her flippers. “Today I will learn a new song!” she chirped.

Penny had a big goal. She wanted to play a gentle song on the xylophone and sing along. She placed the xylophone on a soft blue mat. The bars felt cool and smooth. She took a deep breath. “Ding, ding, dong,” she sang, tapping the bars with tiny sticks.

Clunk! The sticks slipped. The sound was loud and messy. Penny blinked. “Oops,” she said. She tried again. “Ding, di—clank!” Her beak drooped.

Just then, the door creaked. A little mouse named Max peeked inside. Max wore tiny red ear muffs. “Hi, Penny,” he squeaked. “I want to practice, too, but my hands are too small for the big drum.”

Penny smiled. “Hello, Max! I’m learning a new song. I’m having trouble. The notes go clank.”

Max nodded. “I have trouble, too. The drum goes BOOM and it scares me.”

Penny looked at Max’s small hands. She looked at the big drum. She listened to the quiet room. She had an idea. “Let’s set a goal together,” she said. “Goal one: Be kind to ourselves. Goal two: Take small steps. Goal three: Help each other.”

Max’s whiskers twitched. “I like those goals.”

Penny scooted over and patted the mat. “Sit with me. We can take turns. I will try slow taps. You can try a soft drum.” She pointed to a smaller hand drum on the shelf. “This one is gentle.”

Max smiled. He picked up the small drum. It felt warm and smooth. He tapped it very, very softly. “Pum, pum,” it whispered.

Penny held her sticks again. “Slow and soft,” she told herself. Tap. Tap. “Ding… ding…” The notes rang like tiny stars. She closed her eyes and listened. The room felt calm and sweet.

Max whispered, “Good job, Penny.”

Penny’s heart felt warm. Kind words helped. “Thank you,” she said. “Your soft drum is lovely.”

They tried together. Penny tapped, “Ding… ding… dong.” Max answered, “Pum… pum.” Their sounds danced together like snowflakes. Penny grinned. “We are making music!”

The door opened again. A young seal named Sunny slid in, bumping a music stand. Clatter! Sunny frowned. “I can’t hold the shaker. It keeps slipping.” Her flippers were wet and slippery.

Penny waddled over with a towel from the hook. It smelled like soap. “Here, Sunny,” Penny said kindly. “Let’s dry your flippers.” She dabbed them gently. “We can help. Our goal is to be kind and keep trying.”

Sunny smiled a little. “Thank you. I feel better.”

Penny looked at the shaker. It was smooth and round. She thought for a moment. “Problem: The shaker slips. Solution: Wrap it.” She tied a soft scarf around the shaker. Sunny gripped it. Shake-shake! It made a happy rustle-rattle sound.

Sunny giggled. “It works!”

Penny clapped. “We solved the problem together.”

Soon the room filled with soft sounds. Penny’s xylophone went, “Ding, ding, dong.” Max’s drum went, “Pum, pum.” Sunny’s shaker went, “Shhh-rattle, shhh-rattle.” They listened to each other. They breathed slowly. They smiled.

But when Penny tried the whole song, she got stuck at the middle part. “Oh no,” she said. “I always make a clank here.” Her tummy felt tight.

Max patted her flipper. “Let’s be kind. Take a tiny break.”

Sunny nodded. “We can hum the tune together first.”

Penny closed her eyes. She hummed, “Mmm, mmm, mmm.” Max hummed, “Mmm.” Sunny hummed, “Mmm.” The hums blended like warm cocoa. Penny felt calm again.

Penny spoke her plan out loud. “Step one: Hum the middle part. Step two: Tap it slow. Step three: Add the words.” She tried step one. Mmm, mmm. Then step two. Tap… tap… tap. The notes came clear and sweet. Then step three. She sang softly, “Kind hearts, kind hands, we help our friends.”

Max and Sunny joined in. “Kind hearts, kind hands, we help our friends.” The music floated like a friendly breeze.

Penny finished the song with a gentle, “Ding!” The room was quiet for a heartbeat. Then everyone cheered.

“You did it!” said Max.

“You met your goal!” said Sunny.

Penny’s cheeks felt fizzy with joy. “We did it together,” she said. “Your kind words and smart ideas helped me. Kindness makes us strong. Small steps help us learn. When we have a problem, we can stop, think, and try again.”

They put the instruments away. The wood smelled warm. The scarves felt soft as they folded them. Penny held the door for her friends. “Thank you for practicing with me,” she said.

Max waved. “Thank you for helping me find the gentle drum.”

Sunny beamed. “Thank you for the scarf trick.”

Penny waddled out, humming her song. “Kind hearts, kind hands,” she sang. Her friends hummed along. Their voices blended, friendly and bright. The snowy air outside felt fresh and cool, and their hearts felt happy and brave.

The clear lesson stayed with them: Be kind, set small goals, solve problems together, and keep trying. With kindness, the music never stops.

The End.
