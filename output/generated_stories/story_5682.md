# Polly's Perfect Painting Day

Polly the parrot loved to paint beautiful pictures in Daddy's home office. Her bright green feathers sparkled in the morning sunlight. Red and blue paint tubes sat neatly on the wooden desk.

"Good morning, Polly!" said Daddy. "Ready to make art today?"

Polly flapped her colorful wings excitedly. "Yes! I want to paint a rainbow right now!"

She dipped her brush in the red paint. Splash! Red drops fell on her yellow beak. She tried to paint quickly, but the brush slipped. Paint splattered on her beautiful feathers.

"Oh no!" Polly squawked. "I'm all messy!"

Daddy looked up from his computer. "Polly, let's clean your beak and feathers first. Then we can paint together."

"But I want to paint NOW!" Polly said impatiently. She stamped her tiny orange feet. "Cleaning takes too long!"

Daddy smiled kindly. "When we clean ourselves first, our art turns out much better. Let me show you."

He brought a soft, warm washcloth. "Close your eyes, Polly."

Polly felt the gentle cloth on her beak. It tickled a little! The warm water felt nice and soothing. Daddy carefully cleaned each colorful feather.

"There," Daddy said. "Now you're clean and ready to create!"

Polly looked in the small mirror. Her feathers shined brightly again. Her beak was spotless and smooth.

"I feel so much better!" she chirped happily.

This time, Polly took her time. She held the brush carefully with her claw. Slowly, she painted a red stripe across the white paper. Then orange. Then yellow.

"Wait," Daddy said gently. "Let the red paint dry first. If we're patient, the colors won't mix together."

Polly watched the red paint. It looked wet and shiny. She counted to ten slowly. "One... two... three..."

While waiting, she cleaned her brush in the water cup. The water turned pink, then clear again.

"Now the red is dry!" Daddy said. "See how it looks perfect?"

Polly carefully painted the orange stripe next to the red. She waited again. The orange dried beautifully too.

"This is fun!" Polly said. "Being patient makes my rainbow so pretty!"

She painted yellow, then green, then blue, then purple. Each time, she waited patiently for the paint to dry. She kept her feathers clean by wiping her claws on the paper towel.

"Look, Daddy!" Polly sang proudly. "My rainbow is perfect!"

The colors were bright and clear. Each stripe stayed in its own space. No muddy colors anywhere!

"You did wonderful work, Polly," Daddy said. "You were patient and kept yourself clean. That's how we make our best art."

Polly felt proud and happy. Her tummy felt warm and fuzzy inside. She had learned something important today.

"Tomorrow, can we paint flowers?" Polly asked.

"Of course," Daddy said. "And we'll remember to be patient and clean, right?"

"Right!" Polly chirped. "Patience and cleanliness make everything better!"

She carefully put the paint brushes in the water cup to clean them. Then she washed her claws in the little sink. Being clean felt wonderful.

That night, Polly dreamed of beautiful rainbows and colorful flowers. She couldn't wait to paint again tomorrow, taking her time and staying clean.

The End.
