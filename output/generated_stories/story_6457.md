# Tiko the Tortoise and the Colorful Choices

In a bright creative art room, the sun made gentle squares on the floor. Paints lined the shelves like a rainbow: red, yellow, blue, green, and even sparkly gold. Paper rustled. Brushes rested in little cups. The room smelled like clean paper and sweet glue.

Tiko the wise tortoise peeked out from his smooth shell. He wore a tiny blue apron. It had a small paint splash from yesterday. Tiko’s eyes were warm and kind. He liked to think before he spoke. He liked to try things on his own. Today was “Art from Our World Day.”

“Good morning,” Tiko said softly. “I will make my own picture today.” He took a deep breath. “I can do it by myself.”

Around Tiko, friends gathered. Lila the bunny wiggled her ears. Kofi the kitten flicked his tail. Mei the mouse held a bundle of colorful paper. The teacher put on happy music from many places. Drums went boom-boom. A flute sang la-la. Tiko felt the rhythm in his shell.

“I want to paint my family’s special pattern,” Tiko said. He touched a small square of cloth from his grandma. It had shapes like tiny steps and dancing triangles. The cloth was soft and smelled like sunshine. “Grandma says each shape tells a story.”

“I like the dots!” said Lila, pointing to the cloth.

“I like the lines!” said Kofi, swishing his tail.

“I like the bright colors,” said Mei, eyes shining.

Tiko nodded. “I will make my own pattern. I will try first by myself. Then I can share.” His voice was calm and sure.

He set out a big piece of paper. He chose red and yellow. He held a short brush. “Slow and steady,” Tiko whispered. Swish-swish went the brush. He made small steps of red, then tiny dancing triangles of yellow. His shell felt warm with pride.

Kofi reached for the same brush. “Can I use that now?” he asked.

Tiko looked up. He wanted to keep going. He also wanted to be kind. He took a breath. “I am not finished yet,” Tiko said. “I need two more lines. Then I can share. Would you like the green brush while you wait?”

Kofi thought. He smiled. “Okay! I can start with green.” He took the green brush and began to paint a swirly river. He purred, “Thank you, Tiko.”

Lila hopped closer. She pointed to the sparkly gold paint. “I want gold dots,” she said. Her nose twitched.

Tiko’s eyes twinkled. “I will use gold for the sunny parts,” he said. “You can use it after me. Can we trade? You may use my red now.”

Lila took the red and started making round strawberries. “Deal!” she chirped.

Mei held up her paper. “I am making lanterns,” she said softly. “They glow like moons.” She had no brush. “Tiko, can I borrow your small brush after Kofi?”

Tiko checked his plan. He looked at his picture. Two more lines, then gold dots. He could do it. He could be patient and kind. “Yes, Mei,” he said. “I will bring it to you when I finish my lines.”

The music changed. Drums tapped. A guitar strummed. Tiko dipped the brush and painted one careful line. Swish. He painted the next line. Swish. He smiled. “I did it,” he said to himself. “I did it by myself.” His heart felt big.

He handed the brush to Mei. “Here you go,” he said.

“Thank you,” Mei whispered. “I will be gentle.”

Now Tiko touched the gold paint. It was thick and shiny. He made tiny dots like little suns. Tap-tap-tap. The dots sparkled. The room smelled like sweet glue and a little bit like oranges from someone’s snack. Tiko felt happy and strong.

Kofi looked over. “Your steps and triangles look like a dance,” he said.

“They tell my family’s story,” Tiko replied. “In my grandma’s village, people wear cloth with shapes. The shapes mean love, work, and rest.”

Lila held up her strawberry dots. “In my family,” she said, “we put red berries on cake for New Year.”

Mei smiled at her paper lanterns. “My lanterns mean light and hope,” she said.

Tiko listened. He liked hearing about his friends. He liked sharing stories. He liked learning.

Soon, there was one last bottle on the shelf: blue paint. Everyone wanted it. The friends stopped and looked at each other.

“I want blue for the river,” said Kofi.

“I want blue for sky,” said Lila.

“I want blue for cool lantern shadows,” said Mei.

Tiko took a slow breath. “We can make a plan,” he said. “Let’s take turns. We can count together. Each friend gets to count to five while painting. Then we pass the blue.”

Kofi’s tail flicked. “That is fair,” he said.

“Fair and fun,” said Lila.

“I like counting,” said Mei.

They lined up. Tiko set the blue bottle in the middle. “I can pour a little for each turn,” he said. “I can do this job.” He opened the lid carefully and poured a small circle of blue into a tiny dish. He handed it to Kofi.

Together they counted, “One, two, three, four, five!” Kofi passed it to Lila. “One, two, three, four, five!” Lila passed it to Mei. “One, two, three, four, five!” Then it was Tiko’s turn. He made a soft blue border. “One, two, three, four, five!” The blue moved around the room like a friendly wave.

When they finished, the art room was full of color. Papers dried on the racks. The teacher clapped softly. “What beautiful art,” she said. “And what beautiful teamwork.”

Tiko held his picture. He felt calm and proud. He had made his own pattern. He had shared. He had helped make a fair plan. He had listened and learned about his friends’ special things.

Tiko smiled. “Today I learned I can do things by myself,” he said. “I can also share and make a plan with friends. We can all make art and tell our stories.”

The friends nodded. The room glowed with colors and happy hearts.

The End.
