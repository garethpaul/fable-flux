# Whiskers Gets Ready

The morning sun streamed through the big window in the playful living room. Whiskers the clever cat stretched his orange and white paws on the soft, colorful rug. Today was special - his best friend Emma was coming to play!

"Meow!" Whiskers said happily. He looked around the living room. Toys were scattered everywhere. His favorite feather wand lay under the coffee table. Puzzle pieces dotted the floor like tiny rainbows.

Whiskers heard footsteps upstairs. "Emma will be here soon," called Mom from the kitchen. "Are you ready, Whiskers?"

Whiskers tilted his furry head. Ready? He felt ready to play! His tail swished back and forth with excitement. But then he looked around again. The room was quite messy.

"Hmm," Whiskers purred thoughtfully. He remembered last week when Emma came over. They couldn't find the red ball anywhere! It was hiding under a pile of books. They spent so much time looking that they barely had time to play catch.

Whiskers' green eyes sparkled with an idea. "I should get ready!" he meowed to himself.

First, Whiskers gathered all the puzzle pieces. His pink nose sniffed each one carefully. "These smell like fun," he purred. He put them in the bright yellow box where they belonged. Click, click, click went the pieces as they dropped in.

Next, Whiskers found his toy mice. One was fuzzy and gray. Another was smooth and blue. The third was soft and squeaked when he touched it. "Squeak!" it said. Whiskers giggled and placed them neatly in their basket.

"What about the feather wand?" Whiskers wondered aloud. He crawled under the coffee table. It was dark and cozy underneath. The feather wand was there, along with a lost sock! Whiskers pulled both out with his paws.

Just then, the doorbell rang! Ding-dong!

"Oh no!" Whiskers meowed. "Emma's here and I'm not finished!"

Whiskers felt worried. His whiskers twitched nervously. Should he quickly shove everything under the couch? That would be fast and easy.

But Whiskers was an honest cat. He knew hiding the mess wasn't really cleaning. Emma might trip on hidden toys. That wouldn't be safe or fun.

"I need to tell the truth," Whiskers decided bravely.

He ran to the door where Mom was greeting Emma. "Meow, meow!" Whiskers said, pointing to the living room with his paw.

"What is it, Whiskers?" asked Emma, kneeling down. She had curly brown hair and kind eyes.

Whiskers led Emma to the living room. "Meow," he said honestly, showing her the toys still scattered about.

Emma's face lit up with understanding. "Oh! You were getting ready for me to come play! That's so thoughtful, Whiskers!"

"Would you like help finishing?" Emma asked sweetly.

Whiskers purred loudly. "Meow!" he said gratefully.

Together, they put away the remaining toys. Emma sorted the blocks by color while Whiskers pushed his ball into the toy chest with his nose. They worked as a team, talking and laughing.

"Red blocks here," Emma said cheerfully. "Blue blocks there!"

"Meow!" agreed Whiskers, learning the color words Emma used.

When they finished, the living room looked wonderful. Everything had its place. The soft rug was clear for playing. Sunlight danced on the clean floor.

"Now we're ready for the best playtime ever!" Emma declared.

And they were! They played catch with the red ball, built tall towers with blocks, and chased the feather wand around the room. Because everything was organized, they found all their toys easily.

That evening, as Emma waved goodbye, Whiskers felt proud and happy. He had learned something important: preparing ahead of time made everything more fun! And being honest when he needed help made him feel good inside.

"Tomorrow I'll get ready early again," Whiskers purred contentedly, curling up on his favorite cushion.

The End.
