# Koala Kiki’s Kind Dance

Koala Kiki was a calm koala with soft gray fur and a gentle smile. Every day, Kiki visited the musical practice room. The room smelled like fresh wood and lemon polish. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Drums, maracas, and a shiny piano waited quietly. A big mirror smiled back at Kiki.

“Hello, room,” Kiki whispered, patting a drum. “Today, I will dance.”

Kiki loved to sway and tap. She liked moving slow, like leaves in a soft breeze. But today, the room looked messy. Colorful scarves lay in heaps. Drumsticks rolled on the floor. Glittery tap shoes were in a jumble.

Kiki took a deep breath. “I can do my chores first,” she said, calm and kind. “Cleaning helps me and helps my friends.”

Kiki picked up the scarves. They were silky and cool in her paws. Swish, swish, swish went the scarves into a neat basket. She gathered the drumsticks. Tap, tap, tap, they clicked together like little friends. She lined up the shoes by size. Big, medium, small. The room began to shine.

Just then, Kiki heard a tiny sniffle behind the piano. “Hello?” Kiki asked softly. “Are you okay?”

A little mouse peeked out. She had pink ears and a ribbon. “I’m Mimi,” the mouse whispered. “I want to dance, but I feel scared. My feet go fast. I trip. I am not calm.”

Kiki sat on the soft mat. “It’s okay to feel scared,” she said. “I can be calm for both of us. Would you like to dance with me?”

Mimi nodded, her whiskers trembling. Kiki smiled. “First, we warm up.” She turned the music knob to a gentle song. The piano made quiet, twinkly sounds. Ding, ding, ding. Kiki lifted her arms. “Reach to the sky,” she sang. “Wiggle your fingers. Wiggle your toes.”

Mimi copied. Stretch, stretch. Wiggle, wiggle. Her ribbon bobbed like a tiny flag. She giggled. “This feels nice.”

Kiki nodded. “Now, we try a dance step. I like slow steps. You like fast ones. Let’s try both.” Kiki showed a slow sway. Sway left. Sway right. Her fur brushed the air. Mimi showed a quick tip-tap. Tip-tap, tip-tap! Her tiny feet went pitter-patter on the floor.

Kiki clapped softly. “Your fast feet are wonderful,” she said. “Can we mix our steps?”

Mimi blinked. “Mix them? Like soup?”

“Yes,” Kiki laughed. “A dance soup! Slow sways and quick taps together.”

They tried. Kiki swayed. Mimi tip-tapped around her. Slow-slow, quick-quick-quick. The music felt warm and sweet, like honey tea. The mirror showed two friends smiling.

Soon, the door creaked. In came Benny the bunny, holding a broom. “I’m here to sweep,” he said. “But I don’t dance.”

Kiki waved. “Everyone can dance,” she said kindly. “Would you like to sweep to the beat?”

Benny’s ears twitched. “I can try.” Swish, swish, went the broom in time with the music. Benny’s sweeping made a soft whooshy sound. It matched Mimi’s tip-taps and Kiki’s sways.

The door opened again. Lila the lizard peeked in with a rag. “I’m here to dust,” she said shyly. “My tail wiggles when I move.”

“That is perfect for dance,” Kiki said. “Wiggle your tail to the rhythm.”

Lila dusted the piano keys. Wipe, wipe, wipe. The keys went plink-plonk when she touched them gently. Her tail made a happy wiggle-wiggle. The room sparkled, and the music sparkled too.

Mimi suddenly stumbled. “Oh!” she squeaked. She fell onto a soft scarf. Her eyes filled with tears. “I can’t do it,” she sniffed.

Kiki sat beside her and put a calm paw on Mimi’s shoulder. “You can,” she whispered. “Falling is part of learning. Take a breath with me.”

They breathed in together. One, two, three. They breathed out together. One, two, three. Mimi’s whiskers settled.

“Let’s try again,” Kiki said. “We will go slow first, then fast. I am here. Benny and Lila are here too.”

Benny nodded. “I’ll sweep softly so you can hear the beat.” Swish, swish.

Lila smiled. “I’ll tap one key to help.” Plink… plink… plink.

Kiki counted. “One, two, sway. One, two, sway.” Mimi listened and stepped gently. Then Kiki said, “Now fast!” Mimi tip-tapped, bright and brave. Tip-tap, tip-tap! She twirled once, and did not fall.

“I did it!” Mimi cheered.

“You did,” Kiki said, her voice warm like sunshine. “You were open to trying new steps. We all helped each other. We cleaned together, and we danced together.”

The music ended in a soft ding. The room was tidy and glowing. Scarves were folded. Shoes were lined up. The broom rested. The piano smiled again.

“Thank you,” Mimi told Kiki. “You were calm. You showed me to try slow and fast. I learned I can dance my way.”

“And I learned sweeping can dance too,” Benny said, laughing.

“My tail is a good dancer!” Lila giggled.

Kiki hugged her friends gently. “Dance is for everyone,” she said. “When we listen, when we help, when we try new things, our hearts dance too.”

They bowed to the mirror. The room felt safe and kind. The air smelled like lemon and a little like happy sweat. Their feet were warm. Their smiles were big.

Kiki turned off the music and whispered, “Thank you, dance. Thank you, friends.” She placed a small sign on the wall: Be calm. Be kind. Try new steps. Clean up as you go.

They all nodded. Then they skipped out for a snack, humming a sweet, steady tune.

The clear lesson: Dance can be slow or fast. Try new things, help with chores, and be kind to friends.

The End.
