# Benny Beaver's Big Game Plan

Benny Beaver sat at his little wooden desk in his cozy home office. Sunlight streamed through the window, making his brown fur shine like chocolate. He tapped his big front teeth thoughtfully as he looked at the calendar on the wall.

"Oh my!" Benny squeaked. "The Forest Olympics are next week, and I haven't signed up for anything yet!"

Benny's whiskers twitched with worry. All his friends had been practicing for months. Rosie Rabbit was hopping hurdles in her backyard. Tommy Turtle was swimming laps in the pond every morning. Even little Pip the mouse was lifting tiny acorns to build his muscles.

"What can I do?" Benny wondered aloud. His fluffy tail slapped the floor with a soft *thump*.

Then Benny's eyes grew bright. "Wait! I don't have to give up. I can still try!" He jumped up from his chair so fast it spun around three times.

Benny grabbed his favorite red pencil and a piece of bark paper. "First, I need to make a plan," he said, his voice full of determination. The pencil felt smooth in his paws as he wrote in big, wobbly letters: "BENNY'S SPORTS PLAN."

He thought hard, his nose wiggling. "What am I good at? I'm great at building things with my teeth. I'm strong from carrying logs. And I can swim really well!"

*Scratch, scratch, scratch* went his pencil as he wrote down ideas. "Log rolling! That's perfect for me!"

Benny felt his heart beating fast with excitement. Even though he was starting late, he wasn't going to let that stop him. "I'll practice every day until the Olympics!" he declared.

The next morning, Benny woke up extra early. The air smelled fresh and dewy as he waddled down to the river. His webbed feet made little *splash* sounds in the shallow water.

"Okay, Benny," he said to himself. "You can do this!" He climbed onto a floating log and tried to balance. *SPLASH!* Down he went into the cool water.

"Brrr!" Benny shivered, but he climbed right back up. "Try again!"

Day after day, Benny practiced. His muscles got stronger. His balance got better. When he fell off the log, he just laughed and tried again. "Practice makes progress!" he would say.

Rosie Rabbit hopped by one morning. "Wow, Benny! You're getting really good at that!"

"Thanks!" Benny beamed, his wet fur sparkling in the sunshine. "I decided I wasn't going to give up just because I started late."

On the day of the Forest Olympics, Benny's tummy felt like it had butterflies dancing inside. The other log-rolling animals looked very experienced. But Benny took a deep breath and stepped up to his log.

"I practiced hard, and I'm going to do my best!" he whispered to himself.

When the whistle blew, Benny rolled his log with smooth, steady movements. His feet danced on the bark, and his tail helped him balance perfectly. The crowd cheered as he stayed on his log longer than anyone expected!

Even though he didn't win first place, Benny felt proud and happy. He had learned something very important: when you take initiative and don't give up, you can achieve amazing things.

"I'm so glad I decided to try," Benny told his friends afterward. "Taking that first step was the best decision I ever made!"

The End.
