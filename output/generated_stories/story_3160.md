# Melody's Fair Share Adventure

In a cozy kitchen pantry filled with jars of honey and bags of seeds, lived a little yellow bird named Melody. She had the most beautiful voice in the whole house and loved to sing sweet songs every morning.

"Tweet-tweet-tra-la-la!" Melody sang as the sun peeked through the pantry window. The golden light made all the glass jars sparkle like jewels.

But today, something was different. Melody heard tiny squeaking sounds coming from behind the flour bag.

"Help! Help!" squeaked a small voice.

Melody fluttered her bright yellow wings and hopped closer. Behind the big white bag, she found three baby mice - Pip, Pop, and Peanut. They looked very hungry and sad.

"What's wrong, little friends?" Melody chirped gently.

"We haven't eaten in two days," said Pip, the smallest mouse. "The big jar of seeds is too high for us to reach, and we're too little to climb up."

Melody looked up at the tall shelf where a large jar of sunflower seeds sat. It would be easy for her to fly up and get some seeds for herself, but she noticed something that made her heart feel heavy.

"That's not fair," Melody said, tilting her head. "Everyone should have food to eat and grow strong."

The brave little bird knew what she had to do. Even though she was small, she had strong wings from all her flying exercises around the house.

"Don't worry!" Melody sang. "I'll help you get some seeds."

She flapped her wings hard - flap, flap, flap - and flew up to the high shelf. The exercise made her feel warm and strong. With her tiny beak, she carefully pushed some seeds through the jar's loose lid.

"Catch them!" she called down to the mice.

The seeds rained down like tiny treasures. Pip, Pop, and Peanut scurried around, catching the falling seeds in their little paws.

"More! More!" they squeaked happily.

Melody made several trips, flying up and down, up and down. Her wings got a wonderful workout, and her heart felt full of joy. She made sure each mouse got exactly the same amount of seeds - that was the fair thing to do.

"Now everyone has enough!" Melody sang proudly.

The three little mice munched on their seeds, their tiny bellies growing round and happy.

"Thank you, Melody!" said Pop. "You shared fairly with all of us."

"And you were so brave to help us," added Peanut.

Melody felt proud and strong. All that flying had been great exercise for her wings, and helping her friends made her feel wonderful inside.

From that day on, Melody made it her special job to help any small creatures who couldn't reach the food. She learned that being fair meant making sure everyone got what they needed, and being brave meant helping others even when it took extra work.

Every morning, she would sing her happy song and do her flying exercises around the pantry, keeping her wings strong so she could always help her friends.

"When we share fairly and help each other," Melody sang, "everyone feels happy and healthy!"

And in the cozy pantry, surrounded by the sweet smell of honey and the gentle rustling of seed bags, all the little creatures lived together in harmony.

The End.
