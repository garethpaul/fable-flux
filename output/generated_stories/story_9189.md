# Leo’s Thank-You Stars

Morning light peeked into the study corner. The little lamp made a warm glow. Papers, crayons, and glue sticks were on the small desk. A brave little lion cub named Leo sat on his cushion. His tail swished, swish-swish, against the soft rug.

“Today I will make thank-you cards,” Leo said. His voice was bright and brave. “I want to say thank you to everyone who helps me.”

He picked up a crayon. It smelled waxy and sweet. He pressed it to the paper. Scritch, scritch. A big heart appeared. Leo smiled. “This card is for Grandma. She reads me stories.”

Mama Lion peeked in. “Good morning, Leo. What a lovely plan.”

Leo puffed his chest. “I am brave. I can make many cards!”

Mama smiled. “Remember, little steps help big goals. And remember to be grateful for help.”

Leo nodded. He reached for the glue stick. It felt cool and smooth. He opened it. Twist, twist. The glue smelled a little like cherries.

“I will make stars,” he said. “Golden thank-you stars!”

He cut shiny paper, snip-snip-snip. Pieces fluttered down like tiny leaves. He pressed a star on the card. Pat, pat. It stuck.

Then he looked at the other papers. The desk was getting messy. Crayons rolled—tap, tap, tap. Scissors slid to the edge. A stack of cards leaned like a wobbly tower.

Leo’s ears drooped. “There are so many,” he whispered. “So many stars. So many cards.”

He looked at his list:
- Grandma
- Teacher Owl
- Neighbor Tortoise
- Baby Sister

“I can’t do them all,” he said softly. His brave chest felt small. His paws felt heavy.

Mama’s paws made a soft shhhh on the rug. “How are you feeling, Leo?”

Leo blinked. “I feel stuck. I want to make perfect cards. But it is too much. Maybe I should stop.”

Mama sat beside him. “It’s okay to feel that way. Let’s accept those feelings. Hello, big feelings. You may sit with us. We can still finish, one little step at a time.”

Leo took a deep breath. In…out. He felt the warm air in his nose. He felt the chair under his legs. “One little step,” he repeated.

Mama pointed to a small basket. “Let’s sort your tools. Crayons in the cup. Scissors in the tray. Glue on the mat.”

Leo nodded. Tap. The crayons went into the cup. Click. The scissors went into the tray. Thump. The glue sat on the mat. The desk looked kinder now.

“Thank you, Mama,” Leo said. His voice was soft and warm. “I feel calmer.”

“I’m glad,” Mama said. “I will sit with you while you craft.”

Leo picked up a blue crayon. Scritch, scritch. He drew a tiny book for Grandma. He stuck a star. Pat, pat. “Grandma cooks soup when I feel sick. Thank you, Grandma,” he practiced.

Next card. Leo whispered, “One little step.” He drew a little chalkboard for Teacher Owl. He added two shiny stars. “Teacher Owl shows me new words. Thank you, Teacher Owl.”

A small knock came at the doorway. Knock, knock. Neighbor Tortoise poked in his head. “Hello, little lion,” he said slowly. “I brought you a leaf bookmark.”

The bookmark smelled like fresh grass. It was soft and smooth. “Wow,” Leo said. “Thank you!” He felt warmth in his chest, like sunshine.

Neighbor Tortoise smiled. “I am grateful for your roars of hello every morning.” He waved and left.

Leo held the bookmark and grinned. “Mama, saying thank you feels good. It feels like a warm hug inside.”

Mama nodded. “Gratitude is like a gentle light. It helps us see the good.”

Leo finished the card for Neighbor Tortoise. He drew a tiny leaf and added a star that shimmered. “Thank you for the bookmark,” he whispered as he wrote slowly.

Baby Sister toddled in, pat-pat-pat. She giggled and wobbled. “Roar!” she said, but it sounded like “Raa!”

Leo laughed. “This last card is for you.” He drew a little rattle and a smiling face. He let Baby Sister press the star. Squish! It went a little crooked.

Leo looked at the crooked star. He felt a tiny pinch. “It’s not perfect,” he said.

Mama touched his paw. “We can accept little wobbles. They show love too.”

Leo took another breath. The cherry smell of glue, the crayon wax, the soft rug under his paws—all felt cozy. “I accept it,” he said. “This star is special.”

He lined up all the cards. The golden stars sparkled in the lamp glow. His tail swished again, swish-swish. “I did it,” he said. “I finished my task. One little step at a time.”

Mama hugged him. “I am proud of your brave heart and your grateful words.”

Leo delivered each card. Grandma hugged him tight. Teacher Owl hooted happily. Neighbor Tortoise smiled wide. Baby Sister clapped her tiny paws.

Back in the study corner, Leo cleaned up. Crayons in the cup. Paper in the bin. Glue cap on—click. He looked around. Neat and calm.

“I feel thankful,” Leo said. “Thankful for help. Thankful for friends. Thankful for my study corner.”

He placed one last star above his desk. It shone softly. “This star reminds me to say thank you, to accept help, and to finish my tasks, little by little.”

He smiled a big lion smile. “Gratitude makes my heart brave.”

The End.
