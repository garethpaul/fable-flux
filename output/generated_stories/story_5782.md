# Leo the Lion Cub’s Tidy-Time Roar

The morning sun peeked through the curtains of the comfortable nursery. Soft rugs felt like clouds under paws. Shelves held bright blocks, fuzzy blankets, and picture books that smelled like paper and lemons. In the middle of the room sat Leo, a brave little lion cub, with a golden mane like a warm sunrise.

“I am brave,” Leo said, puffing his chest. “I go on adventures!”

Today’s adventure was in the nursery. Toys were everywhere—rattles clinked under the crib, puzzle pieces hid behind a pillow, and crayons made a rainbow on the floor.

“Roar!” Leo laughed, racing his toy cars. His new friend, Bella the Bunny, hopped in. Bella had soft gray fur and long ears that wiggled like little flags.

“Hi, Leo,” Bella said quietly. “Can we play tea party?”

Leo grinned. “Yes! I’ll pour the pretend tea.”

They clinked tiny cups. The pretend tea smelled like strawberries in Leo’s mind. “Mmm,” Leo said, “it smells so sweet!”

Bella smiled. “I like sweet smells. And I like putting toys away when we finish. My paws like neat.”

Leo wrinkled his nose. “Chores are boring. I’m too brave for chores!”

Just then, a gentle voice floated in. “Tidy time, little ones,” said Nana, the caregiver, her slippers whispering on the rug. She wore a soft sweater that felt like a hug. “When play is done, chores are fun. We care for our space.”

Leo’s tail swished. “But I want to keep playing,” he said. He zoomed a car across the rug—and crash! It bumped the block tower. Blocks tumbled like little drums. One block rolled into Bella’s teacup and splashed water from her real sippy cup. A small puddle spread across the rug, cool and wet, soaking Bella’s toes.

Bella’s ears drooped. “Oh,” she said softly. She looked at her wet paws and the messy floor.

Leo blinked. His brave heart felt heavy. The room smelled like a tiny rain cloud on the rug. He saw Bella’s sad eyes and felt a twist in his tummy.

Nana knelt down. “What happened?” she asked kindly.

Leo stared at his paws. “I zoomed too fast. I made a mess,” he said. His voice was small. He looked at Bella. “I’m sorry,” he whispered. “I didn’t listen about tidy time.”

Bella’s ears lifted just a little. “Thank you for saying sorry,” she said. “My paws are cold.”

Nana smiled gentle and warm. “Saying sorry is brave,” she said, handing Leo a soft towel. “And cleaning up is brave too. Chores show love.”

Leo held the towel. It felt warm from Nana’s hands. “I can help,” he said. He patted the puddle. Squish, squish. The towel drank the water. “Good towel,” Leo giggled.

Bella brought a small bucket. “I can pick up the blocks,” she said. “I like counting.”

Leo nodded. “I can count too! One, two, three…” He scooped blocks, their wood cool and smooth. Click, clack, into the bucket they went. Bella’s fast, careful paws made soft tapping sounds.

Nana hummed a tidy song. “Pick and place, pick and place, we make a happy, tidy space.”

Leo joined in, singing in a growly voice. “Pick and place!” His tail swished to the beat.

When the blocks were done, Leo looked around. Crayons on the floor looked like sleepy snakes. “I’ll put you in your bed,” he told them, sliding them into a tin that went ping when it closed. He wiped the table with a clean cloth. Swish, swish. The room smelled like lemon again.

Bella arranged the tea set on a tray. “I line things up in rows,” she said. “It makes me calm.”

“I like big roars and fast zooms,” Leo said, smiling. “You like soft hops and neat lines.”

Bella nodded. “We are different.”

Leo’s eyes sparkled. “And that is good,” he said. “Different helps us! Your neat helps my zoom. My brave helps your quiet.”

Nana clapped softly. “You celebrated your differences. That is wonderful.”

Leo stood tall. “I learned brave is not just loud roars. Brave is saying sorry. Brave is doing chores. Brave is caring for friends.”

Bella giggled. “And brave can be putting the last puzzle piece back!” She held up the piece. It felt smooth and a little warm from her paws.

“May I?” Leo asked.

“Yes,” Bella said.

Leo pressed the piece in. Click! It fit perfectly. He felt a bright, warm glow in his chest. The nursery looked cozy and calm. The rug felt dry again, like a soft meadow.

“We did it!” Leo cheered.

Nana brought a small sticker sheet. “For your brave hearts and helpful paws,” she said, giving Leo a star and Bella a moon.

Leo placed the star on his paw. “Shiny!” he said. “I like tidy time. It makes room for more fun.”

Bella’s ears wiggled. “And it keeps paws warm.”

Leo turned to Bella. “I am sorry again for the splash,” he said clearly. “Next time, I will clean as I go.”

Bella smiled bright. “Thank you. Next time, I will remind you gently.”

They bumped paws—tap, tap—like tiny drumbeats.

Nana winked. “Now you may play again. Your nursery is ready.”

Leo took a slow breath. The room smelled fresh and felt happy. “Adventure time,” he said softly, “and tidy time after.”

They set up a new game together—slow hops, careful zooms, and a promise to pick and place.

The End.
