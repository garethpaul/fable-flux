# Momo the Monkey's Caring Day

In a warm, cozy nursery filled with soft toys and colorful books, lived a brave little monkey named Momo. The nursery smelled like lavender and sunshine, with fluffy clouds painted on the ceiling and a rocking chair that creaked gently by the window.

One morning, Momo swung from his favorite rope when he heard a tiny sniffle coming from the corner. "Achoo! Achoo!" came the sound again.

Momo's big brown eyes looked around until he spotted Bella the bunny sitting sadly behind a pile of building blocks. Her nose was red, and her ears drooped down.

"Hi Bella," said Momo softly, climbing down from his rope. "Are you feeling okay?"

Bella looked up with watery eyes. "I don't feel good, Momo. My nose is stuffy, and my head hurts. But I don't want to bother anyone."

Momo's heart felt heavy seeing his friend so sad. He remembered what his mama always told him: "When someone needs help, a brave monkey speaks up and cares."

"Bella, it's important to tell someone when you don't feel well," Momo said gently. "Let me help you feel better!"

First, Momo brought Bella the softest blanket from the reading corner. It was blue and fluffy like a cloud. "Here, this will keep you warm and cozy," he said, wrapping it around her shoulders.

"Thank you, Momo," whispered Bella, already feeling a little better.

Next, Momo swung over to the play kitchen and pretended to make some special "get-well tea" in a tiny purple teacup. "This magical tea will help your stuffy nose," he said with a big smile, pretending to pour warm, invisible tea.

Bella giggled softly and pretended to sip the tea. "Mmm, it tastes like honey and sunshine!"

But Momo knew the most important thing to do. He gently took Bella's paw and said, "We need to tell Teacher Sarah that you're not feeling well. She can help you feel even better."

"But what if I'm being silly?" asked Bella, her ears still drooping.

"Feeling sick is never silly," said Momo bravely. "Taking care of ourselves and asking for help is very smart!"

Together, they walked to Teacher Sarah, who was reading by the window. The wooden floor felt smooth under their feet, and the morning light made everything glow warmly.

"Teacher Sarah," said Momo clearly, "Bella isn't feeling well. She has a stuffy nose and her head hurts."

Teacher Sarah smiled kindly and knelt down. "Thank you for telling me, Momo. And Bella, I'm so glad you have such a caring friend. Let's call your mama and get you some medicine to help you feel better."

As Teacher Sarah made the phone call, she praised Momo. "You did exactly the right thing by helping Bella and telling me. That's what good friends do."

Soon, Bella's mama arrived with tissues and medicine. Bella felt much better knowing she was being taken care of.

"Thank you, Momo," said Bella, giving him a gentle hug. "You helped me remember that it's okay to ask for help when I don't feel good."

Momo beamed with pride. "That's what friends are for! Taking care of each other makes everyone feel better."

From that day on, all the nursery friends remembered Momo's lesson: when someone doesn't feel well, speak up, show you care, and help them get the care they need.

The End.
