# Sunny Song in the Home Office

Sunny the singing bird lived in a cozy home office. The room smelled like fresh paper and lemon cleaner. Sunlight warmed the desk. A soft hum came from the computer. Tick-tock went the little clock. Tap-tap went the raindrops on the window.

“Good morning!” Sunny chirped, his feathers puffing with joy. “Today I sing, I learn, and I care for me!”

He hopped to the pencil cup and peered inside. “Bright colors! But pencils are pointy,” Sunny said. “Pointy means poke. I will not touch.” He fluffed his wings and felt proud. “Safe choice!”

On the desk, a sticky note fluttered. The fan made a gentle whir. “Whir, whir,” Sunny sang. “Spinny things are not for little wings.” He stood back. He could feel a cool breeze on his cheeks. It felt nice, but he kept a safe space. “I watch. I do not touch. Safe wings!”

Sunny heard a cheerful ding from the computer. “Hello, screen,” he chirped. “I look, but I sit far.” He perched on a soft mouse pad and blinked. The screen was bright and blue. “Blue like the sky!” he sang. “But screens can tire eyes. Blink, rest, blink.” He closed his eyes for a tiny moment, then opened them. “Ahh! That feels good.”

Sunny’s tummy rumbled. “Grumble, grumble,” he giggled. “Snack time!” He hopped to a little bowl with apple slices and tiny seeds. “Crunch, crunch,” he pecked. The apple was sweet and juicy. “Healthy snack helps me sing,” he chirped. He took a sip of cool water. “Sip, sip.” He dabbed his beak on a napkin. “Clean beak, clean desk!”

In the corner, a power cord curled like a snake. Sunny tilted his head. “Hello, string?” He hopped closer, then stopped. “Nope! Not a toy.” He remembered a rule. “Cords are not for pecking. I stay away.” He backed up and hummed a proud little tune.

Just then, the chair rolled a tiny bit. “Whoosh!” Sunny felt a rush of air on his feathers. “Moving chair!” he chirped. “I keep my toes safe.” He waited until the chair was still. Then he crossed the floor with careful steps. “Look, listen, wait,” he sang. “Safe steps, no rush.”

A new smell drifted from the doorway. Warm and yummy. “Mmm,” Sunny said. “Smells like toast.” A friendly voice called, “Hi, Sunny!” It was Mia, the girl who worked in the home office with her mama. Mia had gentle eyes and clean hands.

“I washed my hands,” Mia said, wiggling her fingers. “Want to sing while I eat my toast?”

“Yes!” Sunny chirped. “First, safe check!” He looked around with Mia. They checked the fan. Off. They checked the cords. Tucked. They checked the chair. Locked. They smiled.

Mia put a little mat on the desk. “Snack space,” she said. “We sit when we eat.”

Sunny nodded. “Sitting helps me nibble, not wiggle.”

Mia took a bite. “Crunch. Whole wheat,” she said. “It helps me grow strong.” She offered Sunny a tiny sunflower seed. “For you.”

“Thank you,” Sunny sang. He tasted the seed. “Nutty and nice!” He felt full and happy.

Mia whispered, “What about the markers?”

Sunny looked at the markers lined up in a cup. Red, blue, green, yellow. “Pretty,” he chirped. “But caps stay on. Markers are for paper, not feathers, not beaks.”

Mia giggled. “Right. We keep them closed when we’re done.” She clicked the caps. “Click. Click. Click.”

The clock ticked. The rain slowed. Light made tiny rainbows on the window. Sunny wanted to fly to the window and sing his biggest song. He spread his wings and felt the air. But he paused. “Windows can be hard,” he said softly. “Tap can hurt my head.” He looked at Mia.

Mia nodded. “I’ll open the curtain and tilt the window a little. We stay back, and we sing from here.”

Sunny perched on a safe spot by a soft plant. The leaves smelled green and fresh. He took a deep breath. “I am ready,” he said. He sang a bright, happy song. “La-la-la, safe and sound. Look, listen, safe all around.”

Mia clapped gently. “Sunny, you are smart and safe.”

Sunny chirped, “I ask. I look. I wait. I make healthy choices. That keeps me safe.”

They cleaned up together. Mia wiped the desk. Sunny picked up a tiny paper scrap with his beak and dropped it in the bin. “Tidy desk, tidy mind,” he sang.

The fan stayed off. The cords stayed tucked. The chair stayed still. The screen went to sleep with a soft glow. Sunny yawned a little bird yawn.

“Good work today,” Mia said.

“Good safe day,” Sunny replied. He felt calm and proud. His feathers were smooth, his tummy was happy, and his heart felt big.

Sunny curled into his cozy nest by the plant. He whispered his last song, soft and sweet. “Safe choices, healthy snacks, open mind, kind acts.”

The room was quiet and warm. Sunny closed his eyes with a smile.

The End.
