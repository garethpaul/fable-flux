# Tweet’s Task Song

In a sunny, playful living room, a little blue singing bird named Tweet hopped along a soft, squishy rug. The rug felt warm on Tweet’s tiny feet. Sunbeams made the room sparkle, and the air smelled like fresh oranges. Toys made friendly clinks and clacks as Tweet moved.

“Good morning!” sang Tweet in a bright, happy voice. “La-la-la! It’s a new day!”

Tweet’s friend, Mia, sat on the couch with a big smile. She held a colorful picture book with children from all around the world. There were children waving in many languages. “Hello, Tweet,” Mia said. “Today, we have little jobs to do. Then we can sing and play.”

Tweet fluffed his feathers. “Jobs? Let’s make a plan! I love a plan. La-la-plan!” He opened his wings wide like a list. “First, make the bed. Second, put toys away. Third, snack and water. Fourth, call our friend Aki on the video to say hello! Then playtime!”

Mia clapped softly. “That’s a good list. Let’s try to finish each task.”

Tweet hopped to the cozy blanket on the couch. It was wrinkly like waves. He pulled the corners gently with his beak. Swish-smooth. Swish-smooth. “Nice and neat,” Tweet sang. “Tidy and sweet.” The blanket smelled like clean laundry, and that made Tweet feel proud.

Mia smiled. “First task, done!”

Tweet sang a little tune. “Check it off, check it off!” He tapped the air with his wing like a checkmark.

Next, they looked at the toys on the rug. Blocks, cars, and a tiny drum waited. A toy panda looked up with a painted grin.

“Let’s sort,” Mia said. “Blocks in the blue bin. Cars in the red bin. Drum on the shelf.” She pointed and nodded.

Tweet liked sorting. “Colors! Shapes! We can do it!” he chirped. Click-clack went the blocks into the bin. Vroom-vroom went the cars as they rolled home to the red bin. The tiny drum went thump on the shelf. Tap-tap—perfect.

Tweet wiggled with joy. “Second task, done!” He made the checkmark in the air again. “We are finishers!”

Mia laughed. “Yes. We finish our tasks. That helps our home. That helps each other.”

Tweet stopped and listened. His tummy made a small bubble sound. “Gurgle?” he asked, giggling.

“That means it’s snack time,” Mia said. “Healthy fuel so we can do more.”

They went to the little table by the window. Mia washed her hands with warm water and soap. Tweet dipped his feet in a tiny dish of water and shook them dry. “Wash, wash, splash,” he sang. “Clean hands help us stay well.”

Mia cut crunchy apple slices. Crunch! Crunch! The apple was sweet and juicy. Tweet sipped cool water from a tiny cup and hummed, “Sip, sip, ahhh.” The water felt fresh and kind.

“Healthy habits help us work and play,” Mia said.

Tweet nodded. “Snack task, done!” He felt calm and strong.

Now it was time to call their friend Aki, who lived far away. The tablet screen glowed. Beep-beep. Then a smiling face appeared. Aki waved. Behind Aki was a window with tall trees and bright birds.

“Hello, Aki!” Tweet sang. “We made our bed, put toys away, and had a healthy snack!”

Aki clapped. “Great job, friends! We also clean up our toys here. We take care of our space. It helps our family, and it helps the Earth.” Aki held up a little basket. “We sort paper and plastic for recycling.”

Tweet’s eyes sparkled. “We can do that too!” He looked around and saw an empty paper bag and a plastic bottle from yesterday’s craft. Together, Mia and Tweet put them in the right bins.

“We are helpers in our home and in our world,” Mia said softly.

Tweet sang a gentle line, “Little tasks, big heart, we do our part.”

Aki smiled. “When we all help, the world smiles back.” Aki taught them how to say hello in another language. “Hola!” Aki said.

“Hola!” Mia and Tweet repeated, giggling. The word felt warm in their mouths, like sunshine.

“Thank you, Aki,” Tweet chirped. “We finished our list!”

“Yay!” Aki cheered. “Time to play!”

They waved goodbye. Click went the call. The living room felt bright and peaceful. The rug was clear. The blanket was smooth. The bins were full and tidy. Tweet took a deep breath. The room smelled like apples and soap. It felt good.

Mia stood and stretched. “We finished our tasks. Now we can play with happy hearts.”

Tweet fluttered up and twirled down like a blue leaf. “Playtime song!” he sang. He tapped the tiny drum. Thump-thump. He sang a soft melody that sounded like giggles. Mia shook a rattle. Sha-sha-sha. Together they made music that danced through the room.

Tweet felt proud, safe, and light. “When we plan our tasks and finish them, we have more time for fun,” he sang. “We care for our bodies, our home, and our world.”

Mia hugged Tweet gently. “That’s the lesson,” she said. “We can do little jobs, make healthy choices, and be kind friends to people everywhere.”

Tweet puffed his feathers and whispered, “We did it.”

“Yes,” Mia said with a smile. “And we can do it again tomorrow.”

The End.
