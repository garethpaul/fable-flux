# Ruby Rabbit's Clean Adventure

Ruby Rabbit loved her cozy burrow, but she had one big problem. She didn't like taking baths! The bathroom felt cold and scary with its white tiles and shiny faucet.

Every evening, Mama Rabbit would call, "Ruby, time for your bath!"

And every evening, Ruby would hide behind the fluffy towels. "I don't want to!" she would say. "I'm already clean enough!"

But Ruby wasn't clean at all. Her soft brown fur was muddy from playing outside. Her little paws were dirty from digging in the garden. Even her pink nose had smudges on it.

One morning, Ruby woke up feeling itchy and uncomfortable. Her fur felt sticky and gross. When she looked in the bathroom mirror, she gasped.

"Oh my!" Ruby said. "I look terrible! My beautiful fur is all matted and dirty."

Ruby's friend Penny Possum was coming over to play today. Ruby felt embarrassed. How could she play with her friend looking so messy?

Ruby hopped into the bathroom and stared at the big white bathtub. It looked like a scary cave! The soap smelled funny, and the water made splashing sounds that frightened her.

"I'm scared," Ruby whispered to herself. "But I need to get clean for my friend."

Ruby took a deep breath and turned on the warm water. *Splash, splash* went the water as it filled the tub. She dipped one tiny paw in. 

"Oh!" she said with surprise. "It's warm and nice!"

Slowly, Ruby climbed into the tub. The warm water felt like a cozy hug around her furry body. She picked up the lavender soap - it smelled like pretty flowers!

Ruby began washing her paws. The soap made fluffy white bubbles that tickled her nose. "This is fun!" she giggled, blowing bubbles into the air.

She scrubbed behind her long ears and washed her twitchy nose. The dirt swirled away down the drain like chocolate milk. Ruby felt lighter and happier with each splash.

When Mama Rabbit peeked in, she smiled. "Ruby! You're taking a bath all by yourself!"

"Yes, Mama!" Ruby said proudly. "The water is warm like sunshine, and the soap smells like a garden!"

Ruby washed her fluffy tail and cleaned between her toes. She even shampooed her soft fur until it was silky smooth. When she was done, she felt absolutely wonderful.

Mama Rabbit wrapped Ruby in a big, soft towel. "I'm so proud of you for being brave," Mama said, giving her a warm hug.

"I'm sorry I was scared before," Ruby said. "I should have listened to you about taking baths."

"It's okay, little bunny," Mama said kindly. "We all learn new things when we're ready."

When Penny Possum arrived, she couldn't believe her eyes. "Ruby! You look so clean and beautiful! Your fur is so shiny!"

Ruby felt proud and happy. "Thank you! I learned that baths aren't scary - they're wonderful! I feel so fresh and clean."

From that day on, Ruby looked forward to bath time. She loved the warm water, the sweet-smelling soap, and the fluffy bubbles. Most of all, she loved feeling clean and healthy.

Ruby learned that being clean helped her feel good about herself and showed respect for her friends and family. She was grateful to Mama Rabbit for being patient with her, and she forgave herself for being scared.

"Taking baths is the best!" Ruby would tell all her friends. "It makes you feel amazing inside and out!"

The End.
