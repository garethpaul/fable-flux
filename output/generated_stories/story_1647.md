# Bubbles, Brushes, and the Busy Beaver

Benny was a busy beaver with bright, shiny teeth and a warm, wiggly smile. He loved to make things. He loved to build. He loved to paint, too. One rainy morning, Benny padded into the bathroom. Drip, drop, drip—the faucet sang a tiny song. The mirror was foggy and soft like a cloud. The bath smelled like sweet soap and minty toothpaste.

“I have an idea!” Benny said, clapping his little paws. “Art time!”

He set out a small towel, a cup of water, and a little box of safe bath paints. He put on his tiny apron with blue stars. Swish, swish went the water in the cup. Plop, plop went the paint on the edge of the tub. It made Benny giggle.

But when Benny looked around, he frowned a little. The bathroom felt big and quiet. The walls were plain and white. He wanted to make a bright, happy picture, but he did not know where to start.

“This is a problem,” Benny whispered. “I want to make art, but I feel stuck.”

He took a deep breath. He listened to the drip, drop, drip. He rubbed the fog on the mirror with his paw. Squeak, squeak! A shiny circle appeared on the glass. Benny smiled. “I can start small,” he said softly. “A circle!”

He dipped his brush into blue paint. Swirl, swirl. He made a big blue circle on the foggy mirror. Then he added two tiny dots and a smile. “Hello, Blue Bubble Face!” said Benny. “You look happy.”

The circle smiled back at him in the shiny glass. Benny’s worries felt lighter, like bubbles popping.

Benny heard little footsteps. Pat, pat, pat. His friends waddled in: Dot the duckling with soft yellow feathers, Pip the puppy with floppy ears, and Mia the mouse with a pink nose. They peeked into the bathroom.

“What are you doing?” Pip asked, tail wagging.

“I am making art,” Benny said. “I was stuck, but I started with a circle. Now I feel brave.”

Dot tipped her head. “Can we help?”

“Yes,” Benny said with a big beaver grin. “Let’s make bath art together! We can each make one shape. Then we can put the shapes together.”

“That is a good plan,” Mia squeaked. “You have a leader voice, Benny.”

Benny felt warm in his chest. He liked helping friends. He liked making a plan. “Okay,” he said. “I will guide us. First, we pick colors. Next, we paint shapes. Then, we tell a story with our art.”

The friends cheered. “Hooray!”

Benny passed out little brushes. “Dot, you pick a color.”

“I pick yellow!” Dot quacked. She dipped her brush—swish—and painted sunshine dots on the tub wall. Tap, tap, tap. Yellow polka dots danced up and down.

“Pip, your turn,” Benny said kindly. “What shape will you make?”

“I love stars,” Pip barked. He dipped into the white paint—plop—and made tiny stars on the tile. “Twinkle, twinkle!” Pip sang. The tile felt cool under his paws.

“Mia?” Benny asked.

“I will make a pink swirl,” Mia squeaked. She made a swirl that looked like a cinnamon roll. “Mmm,” she giggled. “It looks yummy.”

Benny nodded. “Now I will make water waves.” He painted soft blue waves along the rim of the tub. Swish, swish, swoosh. The bathroom smelled extra soapy and clean as the warm air fogged the mirror again.

They stepped back. The mirror had a smiling face. The wall had sunshine dots and stars. The tub had gentle waves. The art looked happy, but something felt unfinished.

“We need a big idea,” Benny said. “What story are we telling?”

Dot flapped. “A boat!”

Pip wagged. “With friends!”

Mia squeaked. “And music!”

Benny clapped. “Yes! Let’s make a music boat on the bath waves.”

Benny drew a brown boat with a flat tail shape—just like a beaver’s tail. He added a little flag. Dot painted a sunny sail with yellow stripes. Pip dotted white star buttons on the flag. Mia swirled a pink music note above the boat. Ping! The note looked like it was singing.

Benny stepped back and took a deep breath. “Friends,” he said, “this is beautiful. We solved our problem by starting small, making a plan, and working together.”

Suddenly, splish! A tiny drip from the faucet slid down the tub and smudged one corner of the boat. Pip’s ears drooped. “Oh no,” he whimpered. “It’s ruined.”

Benny shook his head gently. “It’s okay,” he said. “Art can change. Art can grow. We can fix it.”

He dabbed the smudge with the towel—pat, pat—and turned it into a little fish jumping near the boat. “Look!” Benny beamed. “A new friend in our picture.”

Dot laughed. “Happy fish!”

Mia smiled. “Mistakes can make magic.”

Benny nodded. “Yes. When we try, and when we lead with kind words, we can make something wonderful.”

They all hummed a little tune while they added tiny bubbles around the fish. Pop, pop, pop. The bathroom felt warm and bright. The minty smell mixed with the sweet soap smell. The friends felt proud.

Benny put his paw to his heart. “I learned that art is fun,” he said. “Start small. Make a plan. Lead with kindness. Share with friends. Turn mistakes into new ideas.”

Dot, Pip, and Mia cheered. “We did it together!”

Benny helped wash the paints away with warm water. The colors swirled down the drain—whoosh!—like a rainbow ribbon. The mirror was clear again. The tiles were clean. Their smiles stayed.

“Tomorrow,” Benny said, “we can make a new boat, or a tree, or a castle! Our ideas can grow every day.”

“Yes!” his friends shouted.

They dried their paws and tails. They felt calm and happy. The bathroom was quiet again, but their hearts were full of bright art.

The End.
