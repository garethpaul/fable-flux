# Gigi the Giraffe and the Fair Pillow Game

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe who loved bedtime. Her cozy bedroom was soft and warm. The night light glowed like a tiny moon. The curtains were blue and silky. Her quilt smelled like clean sunshine. Gigi’s long neck rested on a fluffy pillow. She wiggled her ears and smiled.

“I love bedtime games,” Gigi whispered.

Every night, Gigi played a quiet game with her stuffed friends. There was Bink the bunny, soft and velvety. There was Dot the ladybug, small and shiny. There was Pompom the lion, fuzzy and brave. They took turns putting a sticker star on a chart. When someone learned a new skill, they got a star. Tonight’s skill was zipping pajamas all by yourself.

Gigi held up the shiny star stickers. “We will be fair,” she said. “Fair means everyone gets the same chance. Fair means we take turns.”

Bink bounced. “I want a star! I zipped fast!”

Dot squeaked, “I zipped too!”

Pompom puffed, “I tried, but my paws are clumsy.”

Gigi nodded. “Trying is important. We learn with practice.”

Gigi opened her pajama zipper. Zzzzip. The sound was smooth. She tried to zip it back up. Her long neck made it tricky. The zipper felt cold on her chin. Her hooves were big and a little clumsy too.

“Hmm,” Gigi said. “This is hard for me.”

She took a breath. She remembered the rule: take turns and tell the truth. Integrity means being honest and kind. Gigi smiled and said, “I will try again.”

Zzzip. She pulled the zipper halfway. It stuck. Gigi felt her cheeks get warm. She wanted to rush. She wanted to say, “I did it!” But that would not be true.

Gigi pressed her hoof to her chest. “I want to be fair,” she whispered. “I will say what is true.”

Bink tilted his head. “Do you want help?”

Gigi thought. She wanted help. But it was her turn. Asking for help is brave. It is self-advocacy. That means using your voice to say what you need. Gigi lifted her head and spoke clearly.

“I need help, please. The zipper is stuck.”

Pompom padded closer. “I can try,” he said softly.

Gigi smiled. “Thank you. Please hold the bottom.”

Pompom held the zipper bottom steady. Gigi pulled slowly. Zzzip. The zipper moved smooth and quiet. Gigi’s tummy felt lighter. She grinned.

“I did it with help!” she said.

Dot clapped her tiny legs. “That was good asking!”

Gigi placed the star chart on her quilt. She looked at the names. Bink, Dot, Pompom, Gigi. Her heart beat steady like a drum. She wanted a star. She wanted to be first. But she also wanted to be fair.

“Let’s share the turns,” Gigi said. “Who zipped by themselves?”

Bink raised a paw. “I did.”

“Did you tell the truth?” Gigi asked, smiling.

“Yes,” said Bink. “I zipped, then unzipped, then zipped again.”

Gigi placed a gold star by Bink’s name. “Good job. You practiced your skill.”

Dot wiggled. “I did it too.”

Gigi asked, “Did you check the bottom clip?”

Dot nodded. “I clicked and zipped. Click, zzzip!”

Gigi placed a star by Dot’s name. “You used the steps.”

Pompom’s ears drooped. “I tried. I slipped.”

Gigi touched Pompom’s fuzzy paw. “Trying is brave. Do you want to practice again now?”

Pompom blinked. “Yes, please.”

Gigi showed him the steps. “Hold the bottom. Click. Pull slow. Breathe.”

Pompom took a deep breath. He held the bottom. Click. Then zzzip. His eyes went wide. “I did it!”

Gigi cheered. “That was amazing! You built your skill with practice.” She placed a star by Pompom’s name.

Gigi looked at her own name. She had needed help. She had used her voice. She had told the truth. Was a star fair for her?

She said aloud, “I did not zip alone. I will not take a star for that. I will wait until I can do it by myself.”

Bink patted her quilt. “But you asked for help kindly.”

Dot nodded. “That is a skill too.”

Gigi smiled. “It is. Self-advocacy is a skill. But the star today is for zipping alone. We will be fair.”

Gigi picked a small heart sticker from the sheet. “I can put a heart by my name for asking for help.” She placed the red heart next to Gigi.

Her room felt calm and sweet. The night light glowed warm. The curtains swished softly. Gigi’s chest felt proud and peaceful. She had been fair. She had told the truth. She had used her voice.

She tried the zipper again. Click. Zzzip. Slowly, gently, carefully. It went all the way up. Her eyes shined.

“I did it!” Gigi said, laughing.

Her friends clapped. Gigi placed a gold star by her name. “Now this is fair,” she said. “We all had the same rule. We all had the same chance. We all tried. We helped. We told the truth.”

Gigi cuddled under her quilt. “Fairness means sharing turns, telling the truth, and using our voices to ask for what we need,” she whispered. “When we do that, everyone feels safe and happy.”

Her room was quiet. The tiny moon light glowed. Gigi closed her eyes with a soft smile.

The End.
