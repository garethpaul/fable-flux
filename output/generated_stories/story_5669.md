# Benny Beaver's Table Trouble

Benny the busy beaver pattered into the dining room with his little wooden toolbox. His tail thumped softly against the floor. Tap, thump, tap. The air smelled like warm pancakes and maple syrup. Sunlight made bright squares on the shiny table.

“I will set the table all by myself,” Benny said. His voice sounded brave. His whiskers twitched with excitement. “I can do big jobs.”

He opened the drawer and took out napkins. The drawer squeaked. He smiled. “Hello, napkins,” he giggled. “Please be neat.” He placed one napkin at each chair. But the napkins slid and crumpled.

“Oh no,” Benny whispered. His ears drooped. “They won’t stay flat.”

He felt a wiggle of worry in his tummy. He wanted to stomp his tail. He wanted to say, “This is too hard!” He took a slow breath instead. In, out. In, out. “I can try again,” he said softly.

Benny smoothed the napkins with his little paws. They still wrinkled. He looked at the long table. He looked at his toolbox. Then Benny had a sparkly idea.

“I can solve this,” he said. “I will make napkin rocks!”

He opened his toolbox and pulled out four tiny wooden blocks he had made from sticks. They were smooth and smelled like fresh wood. He set one block at each corner of a napkin. The napkin stayed! It looked crisp and pretty.

Benny’s eyes got bright. “It worked!” he cheered. “I am a thinking beaver.”

He moved to the spoons. He liked the shiny spoons that glowed like little moons. Clink, clink, clink, they sang as he picked them up. He tried to place them straight. But his paw bumped the spoon pile. The spoons slid and clattered to the floor.

Clang! Clang! Clang!

Benny’s heart thumped fast. He felt heat in his cheeks. “I made a big loud mess,” he said. He wanted to hide under the table. He held his tail with his paws and hugged it. He took another slow breath. In, out. In, out. The sound of his breath was like a soft wind in the forest.

“It’s okay to make a mistake,” he told himself. “I can fix it.”

He picked up each spoon. “Hello, spoon. Thank you for waiting,” he said to each one. He wiped them with a clean cloth. He lined them up on the table so they would not slide.

Next came cups. The cups felt cool in his paws. He set them by the napkins. One cup wobbled on a tiny crumb. Benny stopped. He saw the crumb sparkle in the sun. “Oh!” he said. “The table needs a clean dance.”

He got a damp cloth. Swish, swish, swish. He cleaned in gentle circles. The table smelled like lemon. It felt smooth like a river stone.

Benny smiled. “Now the cups will sit still.”

He stepped back to look. The table looked neat, but a little plain. Benny’s whiskers wiggled. “I can add art,” he said. “I am good at making things.”

He gathered pine cones from a basket by the door. He put one pine cone on each napkin rock. He folded the napkins into little boats. He made a tiny paper sign that said, “Welcome.” He drew a happy sun on it with a yellow crayon.

He placed the sign in the middle of the table next to a small jar of wildflowers. The flowers smelled sweet, like honey and rain. The colors popped—yellow, purple, and white.

Just then, Mama Beaver walked in. Her paws were warm from cooking. “Oh my,” she said, eyes wide. “Benny, what a beautiful table!”

Benny felt his chest puff up, like a little drum. “I had problems,” he said. “The napkins slipped. The spoons fell. A cup wobbled. I felt mad. But I breathed. I thought. I tried new ideas. I made napkin rocks! I cleaned the table. I made art!”

Mama Beaver hugged him. “You solved each problem, step by step,” she said. “You used your calm, your brain, and your heart. I am proud of you.”

Benny’s tail thumped, but gently. Tap, tap. He looked at his work. It looked lovely. It sounded quiet and calm. It smelled like lemon, flowers, and pancakes. He felt tall inside, even though he was small.

Papa Beaver came in and sniffed the sweet air. “Wow,” he said. “Thank you, Benny. This table makes me feel happy.”

“You’re welcome,” Benny said. He placed the last fork with care. “I like helping. I can solve problems.”

They all sat down together. The chairs whispered across the floor. The pancakes steamed and sizzled on plates. Mama poured milk. Benny took a sip. Cold milk, warm pancakes, happy tummies.

Benny looked at his napkin boat. “Bon voyage,” he whispered, and giggled.

Mama smiled. “What did you learn today, our busy beaver?”

Benny tapped his chin. “When there is a problem, I can stop, breathe, and think. I can try new ideas. I can make things beautiful while I fix things. That makes me proud, and it helps everyone.”

Mama and Papa nodded. “That is a wonderful lesson,” Papa said.

Benny’s eyes shone. “I am a problem solver,” he said softly, and took another happy bite.

The End.
