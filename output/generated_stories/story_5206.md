# Milo Mouse's Busy Day Helper

Milo Mouse lived in a cozy corner of Daddy's home office. His tiny whiskers twitched as he watched Daddy type on the big computer. Click-click-click went the keys. Ring-ring went the phone. Beep-beep went the printer.

"So many sounds!" squeaked Milo. His little heart beat fast. The office felt very busy today.

Daddy rubbed his forehead. "Oh my," he sighed. "So much work to do. The computer is slow. The phone keeps ringing. I feel overwhelmed."

Milo's pink ears drooped. When Daddy felt worried, Milo felt worried too. But then Milo remembered something important. Grandma Mouse always said, "When things feel too busy, take three deep breaths."

Milo sat up straight in his corner. He took a deep breath through his tiny nose. "One," he whispered. He smelled the warm coffee on Daddy's desk. Another breath. "Two." He felt his little chest go up and down. One more breath. "Three." His whiskers stopped twitching.

"That feels better," Milo said softly. Now he could think clearly.

Milo noticed Daddy's pencils had rolled off the desk. Papers were scattered everywhere. The trash can was full and tippy.

"I can help!" thought Milo. Even though he was small, he could do small things to make the office calmer.

First, Milo carefully pushed the pencils back onto the desk with his nose. Then he organized the papers into neat little piles with his tiny paws. He even pushed some crumpled papers into the trash can.

Ring-ring! The phone rang again. Daddy looked tired.

Milo had an idea. He scurried to the windowsill and opened the window just a crack. Fresh air floated in, carrying the sweet smell of flowers from outside. Gentle sunlight made the office feel warmer and brighter.

"That's much better," Daddy said, taking a deep breath. "The fresh air helps me think."

When the phone rang again, Daddy answered calmly. "Hello, this is Tom. How can I help you today?" His voice sounded peaceful now.

Milo smiled. By staying calm himself, he had helped Daddy feel calm too.

Later, when a neighbor called asking for help with her computer, Daddy said, "Of course! I'll come over after I finish this project. We should always help our neighbors when we can."

Milo's chest puffed with pride. Daddy was being a good community helper, just like Milo had been a good office helper.

As the afternoon grew quiet, Daddy noticed his tidy desk. "How did everything get so organized?" he wondered aloud. He looked around and spotted Milo in the corner.

"Thank you, little friend," Daddy whispered. "You helped make our office a peaceful place today."

Milo's whiskers wiggled happily. He had learned something wonderful: when things feel busy and loud, taking deep breaths helps you stay calm. And when you stay calm, you can help others feel calm too. Even the smallest mouse can make a big difference by being kind and helpful.

That night, Milo curled up in his cozy corner. The office was quiet now. Through the window, he could see stars twinkling peacefully in the dark sky. Tomorrow would bring new sounds and new chances to help, but Milo wasn't worried anymore. He knew how to stay calm and be a good helper.

The End.
