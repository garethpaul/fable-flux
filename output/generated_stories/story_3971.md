# Benny Beaver's Wellness Corner

Benny Beaver had been working all morning long. *Chop, chop, chop!* went his strong teeth as he cut branches for his dam. His little paws were tired, and his back felt achy from bending over so much.

"Oh my," Benny said, rubbing his furry tummy. "I haven't eaten breakfast, and I forgot to drink water too!"

Benny's wise Grandpa Beaver noticed him looking droopy. "Benny, my boy, come sit with me in our family study corner," Grandpa called gently.

The study corner was Benny's favorite cozy spot. Soft moss covered the ground like a green carpet, and smooth river stones made perfect seats. Sunlight danced through the willow branches above, making pretty patterns on the water.

"Grandpa, I want to finish my dam today," Benny said, his voice sounding tired. "But I feel all wobbly and weak."

Grandpa Beaver's whiskers twitched with a knowing smile. "Ah, little one, let me share something my grandfather taught me long ago. Would you like to hear the Beaver Wellness Song?"

Benny's ears perked up. "A song? Yes, please!"

Grandpa began to sing in his warm, rumbly voice:

"*First we eat our breakfast treats,
Juicy bark and water sweet.
Then we stretch up to the sky,
Like the tall trees growing high.
Rest our bodies, rest our minds,
That's how wellness we will find!*"

"That's a wonderful song, Grandpa!" Benny clapped his small paws together. "But what does wellness mean?"

"Wellness means taking good care of your body and feelings," Grandpa explained. "Just like we take care of our river home, we must take care of ourselves too."

Grandpa showed Benny how to stretch his arms up high like tree branches, then bend down to touch his toes like diving for fish. "Feel how good that feels in your muscles?" Grandpa asked.

"Yes! It's like my body is waking up!" Benny giggled.

Next, Grandpa brought fresh cattail roots and cool, clean water. "Our beaver family has always started each day with good food and fresh water," he said. "This tradition keeps us strong and healthy."

Benny munched the crunchy roots. *Crunch, crunch!* They tasted sweet and earthy. The cool water felt refreshing as it flowed down his throat. "Mmm, this tastes so good! I feel better already."

"Now," said Grandpa, "it's important to speak up when you need help. Can you tell me how you're feeling?"

Benny thought carefully. "I feel much better now! My tummy is happy, and my body feels strong again. Thank you for teaching me, Grandpa."

"You did something very brave by telling me you felt tired," Grandpa said proudly. "That's called self-advocacy - speaking up for what you need."

Benny hugged his grandfather tight. "Will you teach me the Wellness Song every day?"

"Of course! And remember, taking care of yourself helps you take better care of our beautiful river home too. When beavers are healthy and happy, we can build the best dams to help all the forest animals."

From that day on, Benny started each morning in the study corner with Grandpa, singing their special song, eating well, stretching, and sharing how they felt. Soon, Benny was the strongest, happiest little beaver in the whole river!

The End.
