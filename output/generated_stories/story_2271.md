# Ellie's Big Helper Heart

In a cozy nursery with soft blue walls and fluffy white clouds painted on the ceiling, a gentle elephant calf named Ellie sat on her favorite rainbow rug. Her gray skin was warm and smooth, and her little trunk wiggled when she was thinking hard.

Today, Ellie heard something that made her ears perk up. From across the nursery came sniffling sounds. *Sniff, sniff, sniff.*

"What's that sound?" Ellie wondered aloud. She stood up on her four sturdy legs and followed the sad noise with her trunk leading the way.

Behind the tall bookshelf, Ellie found two of her friends having a big problem. Benny the bear was holding tight to a soft yellow blanket, while Lily the lamb was pulling on the other end.

"It's MY blanket!" Benny said, his voice shaky. "I had it first!"

"No, it's MINE!" Lily cried, tears rolling down her fluffy white cheeks. "I need it for my nap!"

The blanket stretched between them like a tug-of-war rope. Both friends looked very upset, and their faces were red from pulling so hard.

Ellie's kind heart felt heavy seeing her friends so sad. "Oh my," she said gently. "This looks like a big problem."

She walked closer with careful, quiet steps. Her trunk reached out and touched the blanket softly.

"Wait, friends," Ellie said in her sweetest voice. "Fighting won't help anyone feel better. Can you tell me what happened?"

Benny wiped his nose with his paw. "I was playing with my blanket when Lily just took it!"

Lily stamped her little hoof. "But I was cold and needed something warm for story time!"

Ellie nodded thoughtfully. Her trunk curled up as she thought of a solution. "I have an idea! What if we work together to solve this?"

She looked around the nursery with her bright, curious eyes. Near the window, she spotted something perfect.

"Look!" Ellie pointed with her trunk. "There's another soft blanket on the reading chair. And see those cozy pillows on the shelf?"

Ellie walked over and gently picked up the blue blanket with her trunk. It smelled like lavender and felt just as soft as the yellow one.

"Benny, you can keep your yellow blanket," Ellie said kindly. "And Lily, would you like this blue one? It's just as warm and snuggly."

Lily's eyes grew wide. "It's so pretty! And it feels so soft!"

But Ellie wasn't finished helping. "And what if we all share the big pillows? We could make the coziest reading corner together!"

Soon, all three friends were arranging pillows and blankets in a perfect circle. They worked as a team, sharing and helping each other. The nursery filled with happy giggles instead of sad tears.

"Thank you, Ellie," Benny said, hugging his friend's trunk. "You helped us remember that sharing feels better than fighting."

Lily nodded, snuggling into her blue blanket. "And now we can all be comfortable together!"

As they settled down for story time, Ellie felt warm and happy inside. Her trunk wiggled with joy. She had learned something very important: when friends have problems, working together to find solutions makes everyone feel better.

"Helping others feels wonderful," Ellie whispered to herself, closing her eyes for the best nap ever.

The End.
