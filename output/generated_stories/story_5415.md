# Leo the Lion Cub and the Pantry Plan

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, Leo’s tummy rumbled like a tiny drum. “Gr-r-rumble,” it said. Leo padded to the kitchen and peeked into the pantry. The door smelled like warm wood. Inside, there were neat rows of jars and boxes. He saw crunchy carrots in a basket, a jar of oats, and a shiny tin of cookies.

Leo licked his lips. “Cookies smell sweet,” he whispered. His nose twitched. The oats smelled toasty. The carrots smelled earthy and fresh.

Mama Lion came in, humming a happy tune. “Good morning, Leo,” she said. “What are you looking for?”

Leo took a deep breath. He stood tall on his little paws. “Mama, I feel hungry,” he said clearly. “I want a snack. I want to choose a healthy one. Can you help me?”

Mama smiled. “That is strong self-advocacy, Leo. You used your brave voice to say what you need.”

Leo nodded. “I am little, but I can ask.”

They looked at the shelves together. “We have choices,” Mama said. “We can have carrots and hummus, or oats with banana, or a cookie after a healthy snack.”

Leo’s tummy rumbled again. “I want oats with banana, please,” he said. “And maybe a cookie later.”

“Good plan,” Mama said. “First, we wash our paws.” The sink water was cool and splashy. “Sploosh, splish.” Leo giggled.

In the pantry, Leo reached for the oats, but the jar was high. He paused. “I can’t reach,” he said. “I need help.”

Mama nodded. “Asking for help is smart.” She brought the jar down. “Now, can you scoop?”

Leo held the scoop. “Scoop, plop,” went the oats into the bowl. He felt proud. He peeled a banana. “Peel, peel,” and the sweet smell floated up. He sliced soft pieces with a safe, small knife while Mama watched.

“Can I pour the milk?” Leo asked.

“Let’s do it together,” Mama said. “Slow and steady.” The milk flowed like a tiny white river. “You are patient,” Mama said. “Patience keeps messes small.”

Leo stirred. “Swirl, swirl.” He tasted a spoonful. “Mmm! Warm and sweet.”

Mama set a small plate nearby. “After your oats, you may have one cookie.”

Leo took a breath. “I will wait,” he said. He looked at the cookie tin. His paw wiggled. He wanted one now. But he remembered his plan. He sang softly, “Oats first, cookie later. I can wait, I can wait.”

“Leo,” Mama said, “thank you for being responsible.”

They sat at the table by the window. The sun made golden squares on the floor. Leo ate his oats, bite by bite. “I feel strong,” he said. “My tummy feels happy.”

After breakfast, Leo helped put things away. He noticed the carrots were in a plastic bag. He saw a cloth bag hanging on a hook. “Mama,” Leo said, using his brave voice again, “can we use the cloth bag for carrots? It is better for the Earth.”

Mama’s eyes shone. “Yes, Leo. That shows care for our world.” Together, they tipped the carrots into the cloth bag. “Rustle, pat.” The carrots looked cozy.

Leo also found a little box for food scraps. “This can be our compost,” he said. “Peels can go here. The garden can eat, too!”

“What a thoughtful idea,” Mama said. “You are caring for your body and the Earth.”

Later that day, Leo and Mama went back to the pantry to choose a snack for a picnic. Leo saw bright apples, crunchy crackers, and the cookie tin. He placed a paw on his chest. “I will use my brave voice,” he said to himself. Then he spoke up. “Mama, I would like an apple and some crackers, please. And one cookie after. I want to keep my body strong.”

Mama nodded proudly. “That is self-advocacy, patience, nutrition, and responsibility all together.”

Leo washed the apple until it shone. “Shhh, splash,” went the water. He dried it with a towel. He put the core in the compost box. “For the garden,” he said.

At the picnic, a breeze tickled Leo’s whiskers. Birds chirped, “Cheep, cheep.” Leo ate his apple slices first. Crunch! Crunch! He waited, humming, for his cookie. When he finished, he asked, “May I have my cookie now?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “You kept your plan.”

Leo took a tiny bite. “Nibble.” He smiled. “I did it, Mama. I spoke up, I waited, I chose good food, and I helped the Earth.”

Mama hugged him. “Leo, you are brave and kind.”

Leo’s heart felt as warm as sunshine. He looked at the pantry door when they got home. He smiled at the cloth bag, the tidy shelves, and the little compost box. “This is our pantry plan,” he said. “I can ask for what I need. I can be patient. I can choose foods that help me grow. I can care for our world.”

He padded to his cozy bed and snuggled down. “I am little,” he whispered, “but my voice is strong.”

The End.
