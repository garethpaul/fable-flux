# Pippa the Calm, Creative Parrot

Pippa was a bright green parrot with splashes of blue and gold on her wings. She lived in a cozy home office with her person, Sam. The home office smelled like lemon soap and fresh paper. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. The computer hummed softly, “hmmmmm,” like a sleepy bee.

On the desk sat a big to-do list. It said, “Make a poster about saving water!” The poster had to be ready today. Sam smiled and said, “Pippa, I need to finish this poster. It’s about conservation. That means we take care of things like water, paper, and power.”

“Squawk! Save water, save paper!” Pippa chirped. She loved making art. She loved helping, too. But there was a problem. The desk was messy! Papers were everywhere. Crayons rolled on the floor. The recycling bin was empty. The trash can was full. The printer went “zzzt, zzzt,” and tiny bits of paper sprinkled down.

“Oh dear,” Sam said. “I feel a little rushed.”

Pippa fluffed her feathers. Her heart beat fast. She wanted to help. She wanted to make the poster right away. She picked up three crayons at once and dropped them with a clack-clack. She flapped and squawked, “Hurry! Hurry!”

Sam took a slow breath. In. Out. In. Out. Sam said, “Pippa, let’s be calm. Calm helps us think. Calm helps us finish.”

Pippa listened. The room was quiet except for the soft hum and the tick-tick of the little clock. She felt the warm sun on her wings. She took a slow, deep parrot breath. In. Out. Her feathers settled. “Calm,” she whispered.

“Great,” said Sam. “We have a problem. The office is messy, and we need a clean space to make the poster. Let’s solve it together.”

Pippa nodded. “Teamwork!”

They made a simple plan:
- First, pick up the rolling crayons.
- Next, sort paper into “recycle” and “use again.”
- Then, turn off things we don’t need.
- Last, make the poster.

Pippa loved the plan. She picked up a red crayon with her beak. “Red!” She put it in a little cup. She picked up blue. “Blue!” She put it in the cup. The crayons made soft sliding sounds, shhh, shhh.

Sam found a stack of paper with only one side printed. “Look, Pippa,” Sam said. “We can use these again for drawing. That saves paper.”

“Save paper!” Pippa copied. She tapped the stack with her curved beak. Tap, tap.

They sorted paper together. “Recycle,” said Sam, dropping scraps into the blue bin. Scritch, scritch. “Use again,” said Pippa, patting the clean-side sheets. Pat, pat. Pippa discovered a little paper leaf shape on the floor. She tucked it into the “use again” pile. “Leaf art later,” she chirped.

The printer light blinked. “Are we using that right now?” Sam asked.

Pippa shook her head and pressed the sleepy button. Click. The humming stopped. The room felt even calmer.

Sam smiled. “That saves power, too.”

“Save power,” Pippa said proudly.

Now the desk was clean. The sun on the desk felt warm and bright. Pippa and Sam washed their hands with a little squirt of soap. Bubbles smelled like lemons. “Clean hands for clean art,” Sam said.

Time to make the poster!

Pippa dipped a brush in blue paint. Swish. She made a big blue circle. “Water!” she said. Sam drew a smiling faucet with a tiny drop. Drip. Drip. Sam said, “We turn off the tap when we brush our teeth.”

Pippa added a picture of a little bird using a tiny cup for rinsing. “One cup,” she said. “Not a big splash.” She dotted soft splashes with her brush. Dot, dot, dot.

They added simple words in big letters:
- Save Water
- Save Paper
- Save Power
- Teamwork!
- Stay Calm

Pippa drew a sun in the corner to show brightness without many lights. She glued the paper leaf to make a tree. The glue smelled sweet. Squish. Press. She patted it flat with her soft-feathered wing. “Tree!” she chirped. “Trees like water.”

Sam laughed gently. “Yes, trees like water. And we like calm.”

Pippa felt happy. But then a tiny crayon rolled to the edge. It wobbled. It fell—clack! Pippa’s heart jumped. She almost flapped fast again. Then she remembered. In. Out. In. Out. She took a calm breath. “It’s okay,” she said softly. She picked up the crayon and put it back.

“Great calm,” said Sam. “You solved a small problem with a calm heart.”

They looked at the poster together. It was bright and clear. It smelled like paper and paint. The colors glowed in the sun. Pippa’s eyes shone. “We did it!” she sang. “We finished our task!”

Sam taped the poster by the desk. Rip, press. “Now, every day, we can see our plan. We can be calm, we can finish our work, and we can take care of our world.”

Pippa puffed with pride. “Calm. Complete. Con-serve,” she said slowly.

Sam nodded. “Conservation means we take care of water, trees, and power. Calm helps us remember. Teamwork helps us do it.”

Pippa tapped the list with her beak. Tap. “Task done!” she said. She did a tiny happy hop. Her feet made a soft pat on the desk. Pat, pat.

They cleaned up together. Brushes in water—swish. Cups stacked—clink. Crayons in the cup—shhh. Sam turned off the light by the window because the sun was bright enough. Click.

Pippa sat in the warm sun square. She felt peaceful and proud. “When I feel rushy,” she whispered, “I breathe. I go slow. I can finish.”

Sam patted her head. “That’s the lesson, Pippa. Calm helps us think. Calm helps us finish. Calm helps us care for our world.”

Pippa closed her eyes and smiled. “Calm and kind,” she said. “Teamwork, every time.”

The End.
