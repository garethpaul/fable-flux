# Ruby Rabbit's Colorful Mistake

Ruby Rabbit hopped into Grandma's cozy guest room, her fluffy tail wiggling with excitement. The room smelled like lavender and sunshine streaming through the lace curtains. On the small wooden table sat a beautiful set of watercolor paints that sparkled like jewels.

"Oh my!" Ruby gasped, her pink nose twitching. "These colors are so pretty!"

Ruby had always wanted to paint, but she had never tried before. She picked up a soft brush and dipped it in the bright blue paint. "This looks easy," she said to herself. "I'll paint the most beautiful picture ever!"

She started painting on the white paper, but the blue paint dripped and ran everywhere. "Oops!" Ruby squeaked. She tried to fix it with yellow paint, but now she had green splotches. The red paint mixed with everything, making muddy brown spots.

"This is too hard!" Ruby cried, her ears drooping. "I'm not good at painting. I should give up."

Just then, Grandma Bunny hopped into the room. She saw Ruby's sad face and the messy painting. "What's wrong, little one?" Grandma asked gently.

Ruby's whiskers trembled. "I wanted to make you a beautiful painting, but I messed it all up. I'm not an artist. I can't do it."

Grandma sat down beside Ruby and looked at the colorful paper. "Tell me, Ruby, is this your very first time painting?"

Ruby nodded slowly. "Yes, Grandma."

"Well," Grandma smiled, "did you think you could hop perfectly on your very first try?"

Ruby giggled softly. "No, I fell down lots of times!"

"That's right," Grandma said, touching Ruby's paw. "Learning new things takes practice. Every artist starts with messy paintings. The secret is to keep trying and have fun!"

Grandma showed Ruby how to hold the brush gently, like holding a flower. "Let's try together," she said. "And remember, there are no mistakes in art, only happy discoveries."

Ruby felt brave again. She dipped her brush in purple paint and made a wobbly circle. "It's not perfect," she said honestly.

"But look how the purple shimmers in the light!" Grandma pointed out. "And see how it makes you feel happy?"

Ruby painted another circle, this time in orange. It was still wobbly, but she smiled. "It looks like the sun!"

For the next hour, Ruby painted with Grandma's encouragement. She made swirls and dots, lines and shapes. Some were neat, some were messy, but all were made with joy.

"I'm getting better!" Ruby exclaimed, painting a green tree that looked more like a lollipop.

"Yes, you are!" Grandma agreed. "Because you didn't give up. You kept practicing with a happy heart."

When Ruby finished, she looked at her painting. It wasn't perfect, but it was full of bright colors and showed how hard she had tried. "Grandma, I want to tell you the truth. I thought painting would be easy, but it's harder than I expected. But I'm glad I kept trying!"

Grandma hugged Ruby tight. "I'm proud of you for being honest and for not giving up. This painting is beautiful because you made it with love and determination."

Ruby hung her painting on the guest room wall. Every time she looked at it, she remembered that learning new things takes time, practice makes you better, and it's always good to tell the truth about how you feel.

From that day on, Ruby painted every week, and each painting got a little better than the last.

The End.
