# Squeaky the Squirrel Starts the Car Day

Squeaky the squirrel lived in a big oak tree near a cozy house. He was small, fluffy, and very curious. He liked to help. He liked to move. He liked to try. Today, Squeaky saw something shiny in the driveway. It was the family car! The car was blue like the sky and warm from the sun. The windows sparkled. The tires smelled like rubber. The seats looked soft like marshmallows.

Squeaky’s whiskers wiggled. “I can help,” he said. “I can be a helper today.”

Mama, Papa, and little Maya were getting ready to go to the park. They packed a ball and a snack. They sang a happy song. “Park day! Park day! Let’s move and play!”

Squeaky watched from a branch. He wanted to help the family go faster. He wanted to show initiative. That means trying to do a good thing without being asked. Squeaky took a deep breath. He scampered down the tree. His tiny feet went pat-pat-pat on the driveway. His tail swished. His heart thumped, thump-thump, with excitement.

Squeaky peeked inside the car. The door was open. He smelled new car smell and a little orange juice. He heard the soft tick-tick of the car clock. “Hello, car,” Squeaky whispered. “I want to help. I will get things ready!”

He hopped onto the seat. The seat felt squishy under his paws. He straightened a seatbelt. Click! He put a small blanket neatly on Maya’s seat. Smooth. He moved the ball so it would not roll. Roll-stop. He picked up a little wrapper and set it in the trash bag. Rustle.

Squeaky felt proud. “I am doing my best,” he said. “I am using my body to move and help.”

Then Squeaky saw the keys. The keys were shiny and jingle-jangly. He touched them. Jingle-jingle! “Maybe I can start the car,” he said. “Then the family can go fast, fast!”

His little paw reached toward the keys. His whiskers tickled. His tummy fluttered. But then Squeaky stopped. He remembered a good rule. “If I do not know, I ask. If it is not mine, I wait.” He felt a small pinch in his heart. He wanted to help, but he also wanted to be safe and honest.

Just then, Maya came to the car. She had two pigtails and bright shoes. “Oh! A squirrel!” she giggled. “Hi, fluffy friend.”

Squeaky stood up tall. “Hello, Maya,” he said softly. “I tried to help get the car ready. I put the blanket neat. I picked up trash. I moved the ball. I thought about the keys. I almost touched them. I wanted to start the car. But I didn’t. The keys are not mine. I don’t know how. I stopped.”

Maya smiled big. “Thank you for helping,” she said. “That was kind. And thank you for telling the truth. That is honesty.”

Mama and Papa came too. Mama leaned down. She smelled like apples. “You took initiative,” Mama said. “You tried to help without being asked. You used your body to move with care. And you were honest about the keys. That is brave and smart.”

Papa nodded. He patted the seat. “Some jobs are for grown-ups,” he said. “Starting the car is for me. But there are many jobs you can do.”

Squeaky’s ears perked up. “I can do more?”

“Yes!” said Maya. “Let’s make a plan. You can check the bag. Do we have water? Do we have snacks?”

Squeaky jumped to the bag. He peeked inside. Crunchy crackers. A water bottle that went slosh-slosh. A napkin that felt soft. “We have water. We have snacks. We have a napkin,” he said proudly.

“Great,” said Mama. “You can also help buckle Teddy. Teddy needs to be safe.” Teddy was Maya’s stuffed bear. Teddy felt fuzzy and warm. Squeaky hugged Teddy and put him next to Maya. Click went the tiny belt around Teddy’s tummy.

Squeaky’s tail twitched with joy. “I like to help,” he said. “I like to move. I like to tell the truth.”

Papa sat in the driver’s seat. The seat went creak. He put the keys in. Vroom! The car hummed like a big cat. Squeaky felt the gentle rumble under his paws. The air smelled like cool breeze from the vents. “We are ready,” Papa said.

Maya clapped. “Thank you, Squeaky. You had initiative! You made the car tidy and safe. And you told us the truth.” She held out her hand. Squeaky touched it with his tiny paw. Tap.

As the car rolled down the driveway, Squeaky looked out the window. The sun was bright. The trees swished. Squeaky felt calm and proud. “Today I learned,” he said, “that helping starts with small moves. I can see a need. I can try. I can ask when I’m not sure. I can be honest, even when it’s hard.”

They reached the park. The grass smelled sweet. The swings squeaked. The ball went bounce-bounce-bounce. Squeaky ran in happy circles. His paws went pitter-patter. He laughed his little squirrel laugh. He had taken action. He had told the truth. He had been safe.

Mama waved from the car. “Thank you, helper!” she called. “Your initiative helped us move today.”

Squeaky waved back. His heart felt big and warm. He whispered, “When I see a way to help, I will try. I will move my body with care. I will be honest every time.”

The End.
