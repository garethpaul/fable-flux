# Tilly the Tortoise Finds a Fair Way

The family car hummed like a sleepy bee. The seats were soft and warm in the sun. The air smelled like orange snacks and a little like crayons. Daddy drove. Mommy sang a gentle song. In the back seat, two little voices began to rise like kettles.

“I want the window seat!” cried Mia.

“No, I want the window seat!” said Max.

In the middle, buckled in snug and safe, sat Tilly the Tortoise. Tilly was small and green, with a shell as smooth as a shiny pebble. Her eyes were kind. She spoke slowly and softly, like a lullaby.

Tilly blinked and listened. She always listened first.

Mia crossed her arms. “I like to see the trees,” she said. “They swoosh, swoosh, swoosh!”

Max stamped his feet a little. “I like to see trucks,” he said. “They honk! Beep-beep!”

The car bumped over a tiny pothole. Bump. The seat belts clicked. Click. Daddy looked in the mirror. “Please use calm voices,” he said.

Tilly took a deep breath. In. Out. She smelled the oranges and the cool air from the little fan. She touched her shell for courage. “Friends,” Tilly said, “I hear you. You both want the window seat.”

Mia sniffled. “Yes.”

Max huffed. “Yes.”

Tilly nodded. “Let’s talk. Let’s listen. We can find a fair way.”

Mia and Max looked at Tilly. Her voice was warm like a blanket.

Tilly asked, “Mia, tell us more. Why do you want the window today?”

Mia wiped a tear. “I like to see birds fly. It makes me happy.”

Tilly turned to Max. “Max, tell us more. Why do you want the window today?”

Max tugged his shoe. “I like to count big wheels. One, two, three! It makes me laugh.”

Tilly smiled. “Thank you for telling me. I listened. I hear Mia likes birds and trees. I hear Max likes trucks and wheels.”

Mommy handed back a small timer shaped like an egg. It ticked softly. Tick-tick-tick.

Tilly tapped the timer. “I have an idea. We can share time. We can take turns. Mia gets the window for ten minutes. Then—ding!—it is Max’s turn for ten minutes. We can switch again if we need to.”

Mia looked at the egg. “Ten minutes is not too long.”

Max wiggled his toes. “Ten minutes is okay.”

Tilly nodded. “This is called negotiation. It is a big word. It means we talk, we listen, and we find a fair plan.”

Mia and Max looked at each other. Their voices got softer.

Mia said, “Max, after my turn, you can count wheels.”

Max said, “Mia, during my turn, I can show you the biggest truck.”

Tilly smiled. “That is kind.”

Daddy smiled too. “Great plan, Tilly.”

Mommy said, “I’m proud of your listening.”

The egg timer began. Tick-tick-tick. Mia sat by the window first. The glass felt cool on her fingers. She pressed her nose close. “Look! A red bird!” she whispered. “Tweet!”

Max leaned over just a bit. “I see it! Tweet-tweet!” He kept his body safe and stayed buckled.

Tilly listened to the gentle hum of the car. Hummm. She smelled fresh air when Daddy cracked the window. A soft breeze kissed their cheeks. Whoosh. Tilly’s shell felt warm in a sun spot.

Ding! The egg timer sang.

Now it was Max’s turn. They switched seats safely when the car stopped at a park gate. The seat buckles clicked again. Click. Max sat by the window. His eyes were bright. “Truck!” he said. “Big wheels! One, two, three!” He laughed. Ha-ha!

Mia leaned over just a bit. “I see it too! Beep-beep!”

Tilly clapped her little feet. “You are both good at taking turns. You listened. You spoke kindly. You made a plan.”

The car rolled on. The road smelled like rain. Pitter-patter drops began on the window. Tap-tap-tap. Max and Mia watched the raindrops race. “Go, drop, go!” they giggled.

After a while, the egg went ding again, and they switched once more. Each time, they used calm voices. Each time, they smiled.

When they reached Grandma’s house, the car stopped with a soft squeak. The door opened. Fresh bread smell floated in. Mmm. Everyone unbuckled.

Mia turned to Max. “Thank you for the window time.”

Max nodded. “Thank you for the bird time.”

Tilly’s eyes shone. “What did we learn?”

Mia stood tall. “We learned to listen first.”

Max grinned. “We learned to talk about what we want.”

Tilly added, “And we learned to make a fair plan together. That is negotiation. It helps us keep peace.”

Daddy said, “I liked the calm car.”

Mommy said, “I liked your kind words.”

Tilly tucked her head happily. “When we listen and take turns, everyone feels good.”

Mia and Max held hands. “We can do this again,” they said together.

Tilly nodded. “Yes. We can negotiate anytime.”

They all went inside for warm bread and hugs. The house felt cozy. The rain made a soft song on the roof. And in their hearts, everything felt peaceful and bright.

The End.
