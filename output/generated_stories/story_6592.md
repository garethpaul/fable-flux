# Benny Beaver's Wobbly Morning

Benny Beaver woke up on his peaceful front porch feeling very excited. The morning sun was warm and golden, and the birds were singing their happy songs. Today was the day he wanted to learn something new - how to stack his wooden blocks into a tall, tall tower!

"Good morning, world!" Benny said with a big yawn. He stretched his little paws and wiggled his flat tail. "Today I'm going to build the biggest tower ever!"

Benny had ten colorful wooden blocks sitting in a neat pile beside his favorite rocking chair. He picked up the first red block with his tiny paws. It felt smooth and cool in his hands.

"This looks easy!" Benny said confidently. He placed the red block down on the wooden porch floor. Then he picked up a blue block and tried to put it on top.

CRASH! The blue block tumbled right off and rolled across the porch.

"Hmm," Benny said, scratching his furry head. "That didn't work."

He tried again. This time he picked up a yellow block and very carefully tried to balance it on the red one. But his little paws were shaking just a tiny bit.

CRASH! Down went the yellow block too.

Benny's bottom lip started to quiver. "Why can't I do this? It looks so easy when Mama does it!"

Just then, Mama Beaver came out onto the porch carrying a cup of warm honey tea. The sweet smell made Benny feel a little better.

"What's wrong, my little builder?" Mama asked, sitting down in the rocking chair.

"I can't stack the blocks!" Benny said sadly. "They keep falling down, and my paws feel all wobbly."

Mama Beaver smiled gently. "Oh, Benny, do you know how I learned to build our dam? I practiced every single day. At first, my paws were wobbly too!"

"Really?" Benny's eyes grew wide.

"Really! Let me show you a special trick," Mama said. "First, take three deep breaths with me. In... and out... In... and out... In... and out."

Benny breathed deeply. The fresh morning air smelled like flowers and grass. He felt calmer already.

"Now," Mama continued, "try moving slowly. Place your paws flat on the block, then gently let go when it feels steady."

Benny picked up the red block again. This time, he moved his paws slowly and carefully. He placed it down and counted to three before letting go. The block stayed put!

"I did it!" Benny cheered.

"Now try the next one," Mama encouraged.

Benny picked up the blue block. Slowly, slowly, he placed it on top of the red one. He held his breath and counted to three again. It stayed!

"Wonderful!" Mama clapped. "Keep practicing, and soon it will feel easy."

Every morning after that, Benny practiced on his peaceful porch. Some days he could stack three blocks. Other days he could stack five! Each day, his paws got steadier and his coordination got better.

After two weeks of practice, Benny built a tower with all ten blocks! It reached almost as high as his whiskers.

"Look, Mama! I did it!" Benny danced around his colorful tower.

"I'm so proud of you, Benny," Mama said, giving him a warm hug. "You learned that practice makes everything possible!"

From that day on, Benny practiced something new every morning on his porch. And he always remembered: when something feels hard, take deep breaths, move slowly, and practice every day!

The End.
