# Busy Beaver Helps in the Art Room

In a bright creative art room, a busy beaver named Benny swished his wide tail. The room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Paints sparkled in little cups. Brushes stood tall like tiny trees.

“Good morning, friends!” said Ms. Dot, the art teacher. She wore a polka-dot apron. “Today we make a big rainbow poster for the hallway. Who would like to help?”

Benny’s paws tingled. He loved to build and help. His heart went thump-thump with excitement. “I can help!” Benny said. “I volunteer!”

Ms. Dot smiled. “Thank you, Benny. Volunteering means choosing to help. That is kind and brave.”

Benny stood a little taller. His fur felt warm and proud. He took a deep breath. The paint smelled sweet and a little tickly in his nose.

“First,” said Ms. Dot, “let’s follow directions. Step one: put on smocks. Step two: put out paper. Step three: share the brushes.”

Benny listened. He nodded. “Smocks, paper, brushes,” he whispered to remember.

Benny slid into a blue smock. It felt soft and a little slippery. He carefully rolled out a long sheet of paper. “Shooosh,” it whispered as it unrolled across the table. He set brushes in a line like a neat beaver dam.

“Great job,” said Ms. Dot. “Now, friends, you can paint!”

The room filled with happy sounds. “Swish-swish,” went the brushes. “Plop-plop,” went the paint. Children giggled. Colors danced.

Benny saw his friend Mia holding a heavy paint cup. It wobbled. “Oh!” said Mia. “It’s heavy.”

“I can help,” said Benny. “I volunteer to carry it.” He held the cup with two paws. The paint felt cool as a tiny drop landed on his smock. “I got it, Mia.”

“Thank you,” Mia said. She smiled bright like yellow paint.

Soon, a new friend came in. His name was Raj. He held his hands close to his chest. He looked at the paint, then at the brushes. His eyes were wide.

Benny walked over slowly. “Hi, I’m Benny. I like to help. Do you want a brush?”

Raj shook his head. “I’m not sure. I don’t like sticky paint.”

Benny nodded. “It can feel sticky. That is okay.” He pointed to the crayons. “Would you like to draw the rainbow stripes with crayons? You can try. You can also say no if you don’t want to. Your choice.”

Raj’s shoulders relaxed. “I can try crayons,” he said softly.

Benny brought a box of crayons. “I volunteer to sit with you,” said Benny. “We can draw together.”

“Thank you,” said Raj. He picked a red crayon. “Scratch, scratch,” it went on the paper.

Across the room, a tiny voice called, “Benny?” It was Lulu, the mouse. Her brush had too much paint. It dripped like raindrops. “I need help.”

Benny looked at Raj. “I will be right back,” he said. “Is that okay?”

Raj smiled. “Okay. I’m going to draw orange.”

Benny hurried to Lulu. “I can help,” he said. He took a deep breath and remembered Ms. Dot’s directions. “Wipe the brush on the cup,” he said. He showed Lulu how. “Tap-tap, wipe-wipe.”

Lulu tried. “Tap-tap, wipe-wipe!” The drips stopped. “Thank you, Benny!”

“You did it!” said Benny. His tail thumped happily on the floor. Thump. Thump.

Then a big splash sound! “Oh no!” cried Max. A splash of blue paint landed on the table edge. It made a puddle by Mia’s sleeve.

Benny’s heart jumped. He felt a little scared. His paws shook. “I can be brave,” he whispered. “I can help.”

He went to Ms. Dot. “Ms. Dot, there is a spill. I volunteer to clean.”

Ms. Dot nodded. “Thank you for telling me and for being brave. Please follow directions. Step one: get a cloth. Step two: wipe from the outside in. Step three: wash hands.”

Benny took a cloth. It was soft and warm. He wiped slowly, outside to inside, like Ms. Dot said. “Wipe-wipe, circle-circle.” The blue puddle got smaller and smaller. “We did it,” he said to Max and Mia.

Max smiled. “Thanks, Benny.”

Mia patted her sleeve. “All clean!”

Benny washed his paws. The water was cool and splashy. “I like helping,” he said. “Volunteering feels good.”

Raj called, “Benny, look!” He had drawn red and orange and yellow stripes. They were bright and neat.

“Wow,” said Benny. “You were open to trying crayons. That was brave too.” Benny kept a little space between them so Raj felt comfy. “Do you want to add green, or do you want me to?”

Raj thought. “Can you do green? I like watching.”

“I can,” said Benny. “Thanks for telling me your boundary.” He dipped a brush into green paint. Swish-swish. He made a gentle green stripe next to Raj’s lines.

Soon the rainbow was almost done. Ms. Dot clapped. “Friends, this is beautiful! Thank you for volunteering and for following directions. You were brave and open-minded. You also used your words to share your comfort.”

Benny’s chest felt big and light. He looked around. He saw smiles. He heard happy giggles. He smelled the sweet paint and the waxy crayons. He felt proud.

Ms. Dot hung the poster in the hallway. The rainbow shined. A little sign said: “Made with Helping Hands.”

Benny waved his paw. “Any time someone needs help,” he said, “I can volunteer. I can listen. I can be brave. I can be kind. And I can ask, ‘Is this okay?’”

Everyone cheered. “Hooray for helpers!”

Benny’s tail thumped one more time. Thump. Thump. He took a deep breath and smiled.

The End.
