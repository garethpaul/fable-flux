# Whiskers’ Sparkly Bath Plan

Whiskers was a clever cat with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, Whiskers padded into the bathroom. The tiles felt cool under his paws. He sniffed the warm air and listened. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. Plink, plink, plink went drops into the white tub.

“Mmm,” said Whiskers. “It is bath day. Clean fur feels nice. Clean paws feel safe. But how can I make bath time easy and fun?”

Whiskers sat down and wrapped his tail around his feet. He took a slow breath. He liked to think things through.

“Step one,” he meowed softly. “Gather what I need.” He looked around with his bright eyes. He saw a soft blue towel. He saw a gentle brush. He saw kitty shampoo that smelled like sweet apples. He saw a little cup for rinsing. He lined them all up neatly.

Whiskers smiled. “Everything in order. That keeps me calm.”

He turned the silver handle and watched the water run. He put his paw in the stream. “Too hot,” he said, pulling back. He turned the handle a tiny bit. He tested again. “Too cold.” He turned it just a little more. He tested one more time. The water felt warm like a hug.

“Just right,” he purred. “I used small steps and careful checks.”

The bathroom filled with a warm, apple-fresh smell. Steam made the mirror a little foggy. Whiskers drew a smiley face in the mist with his paw. He giggled a tiny cat giggle.

Just then, his friend Dot, a tiny red ladybug, landed on the soap dish. “Hi, Whiskers!” Dot buzzed. “What are you doing?”

“I am taking care of me,” said Whiskers. “It is bath day.”

Dot peered into the tub. “Baths make me nervous,” she whispered. “Water is big, and I am small.”

Whiskers nodded kindly. “I understand. We can be brave and be safe. I will show you my plan.”

He held up one paw. “First, I check the water. Second, I keep my towel close. Third, I go slowly. If I worry, I take a breath.”

Dot took a tiny breath. “I can do that.”

Whiskers stepped into the tub one paw at a time. He counted softly. “One, two, three, four.” The warm water touched his fur. It felt like sunshine and cuddle time.

He dipped his brush and made little circles. Swish, swish, swish. Bubbles grew like tiny clouds. Pop, pop went the bubbles at his whiskers. Whiskers smiled and sang, “Wash my ears, wash my face, washing keeps me strong and safe.”

Dot watched from the soap dish. “You look calm,” she said.

“I am,” said Whiskers. “I am caring for me. Clean fur helps me feel cozy. Clean paws help me not slip.”

Then Whiskers heard a soft sniffle. He turned his head. On the edge of the sink sat Pip, a fluffy, shy puppy with droopy ears. Pip’s paws were muddy, and he looked worried.

“Hello, Pip,” said Whiskers gently. “What is wrong?”

“I got muddy,” Pip said softly. “Baths scare me. The water splashes my nose.”

Whiskers blinked kindly. “I can help. We can use a smart plan. We will be gentle and go slow.”

Pip’s tail thumped once. “Really?”

“Yes,” said Whiskers. “First, let us check the water.” He turned the handle a little and let Pip touch the stream with a paw. “How does it feel?”

Pip’s eyes widened. “Warm! Not too hot. Not too cold.”

“Just right,” said Whiskers. “Next, we keep a dry cloth for your nose.” He folded a small cloth and set it by Pip. “If a drop splashes, you can dab it. That keeps you comfy.”

Pip smiled a tiny smile. “Thank you.”

Whiskers finished rinsing himself with the little cup. “Cup time,” he said. Splash, splash. He covered his own nose with his paw. “See? We can protect the parts that feel tickly.”

Dot buzzed closer. “I can help too! I will count and cheer.”

Whiskers stepped out of the tub and wrapped himself in the soft blue towel. Rub, rub, rub. He felt dry and warm. His fur smelled like apples. He brushed gently. “Smooth and neat,” he purred. “I feel bright and ready.”

Now it was Pip’s turn. Pip trembled, but Whiskers stood by him.

“We will go step by step,” said Whiskers. “Step one: deep breath.”

Pip breathed in and out. “Okay.”

“Step two: paw test.” Pip touched the water again and nodded.

“Step three: slow paws into the tub,” said Whiskers. “One, two, three, four.”

Pip stepped in. The water hugged his fur. “It’s warm,” he said in surprise.

Dot counted, “One circle, two circles,” as Whiskers made gentle brush swirls on Pip’s back. Swish, swish. The bubbles smelled like fresh apples. Pip’s ears perked up. He wagged his tail. He even laughed when a bubble sat on his nose. Pop!

“If a splash comes, dab,” said Whiskers, handing the cloth. Pip dabbed his nose and grinned. “That helps!”

“Last step: rinse with the cup,” said Whiskers. “Eyes closed.” Splash, splash. It was slow and easy. Pip wagged again.

“You did it!” Dot cheered.

Pip stepped out, and Whiskers wrapped him in a towel. Rub, rub. Pip’s fur fluffed up, soft and clean.

“Thank you, Whiskers,” Pip said. “You shared your plan. You were gentle and kind.”

Whiskers purred. “Taking care of myself helps me help others. I feel calm and strong. You can feel that way too.”

Dot spun in the air. “Today we learned a smart way: make a plan, go slow, and be kind.”

Whiskers nodded. “Self-care is doing what helps our bodies and hearts. Clean fur, warm towels, calm breaths, and helping friends. That is love.”

Pip’s tail wagged fast. “I will use the plan next time!”

Whiskers looked at the tidy bathroom, the neat tools, and his clean friends. The faucet went quiet. The room smelled sweet. Everyone felt safe and cozy.

“Bath day can be a good day,” said Whiskers.

“Yes,” said Dot and Pip together. “With care, clear steps, and sharing.”

Whiskers smiled and purred, “That is our lesson.”

The End.
