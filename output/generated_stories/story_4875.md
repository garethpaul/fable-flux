# Rollo Raccoon’s Fair Share Song

Rollo the raccoon loved music. His striped tail swished like a metronome. His whiskers twitched to the beat. In his cozy bedroom, soft yellow light glowed from a tiny lamp shaped like a moon. His bed had a quilt with bright squares. His drum was a gentle hand drum. His little xylophone shone with rainbow bars. A warm, sweet smell of hot cocoa drifted from the kitchen.

Tonight was Band Night with Rollo’s friends. They met in his cozy bedroom to practice for a small show for their neighbors. Rollo tapped his drum. Ting! He tapped his xylophone. Plink, plink! He hummed. Mmm-mmm-mmm!

His friends arrived with happy feet and big smiles. Bella Bunny carried a shaker. “It goes shake-shake!” she giggled.

Pip the Puppy had a small bell. “Ding!” he barked.

Mina Mouse rolled in a tiny keyboard. “Beep-beep-beep,” she squeaked.

“Welcome!” said Rollo. “Let’s make music! We will play a song for our street tomorrow. We will make our neighbors smile.”

They all liked the idea. It was community service. They wanted to help make people feel happy and calm.

Rollo felt proud. He loved being the leader. He thumped his drum louder. BOOM. He tapped his xylophone faster. PLINK-PLINK-PLINK.

Bella raised a paw. “Rollo, can I try the xylophone, please?”

Rollo hugged the mallets. “Um… it is mine,” he said. “I need it.”

Pip wagged his tail. “Can I do a solo with the bell?”

Rollo shook his head. “I think I will do the bell and the drum and the xylophone. I want it to be perfect.”

Mina’s whiskers drooped. “Can we take turns?” she asked softly.

Rollo frowned. His bedroom was his space. His instruments were his tools. He wanted the song to be just how he imagined. He tapped the drum again. BOOM. He tapped the xylophone. PLINK.

But the room felt tight. The cozy bedroom was quiet now, except for the little hum of the lamp. The sweet cocoa smell did not feel so warm anymore. Bella stared at the floor. Pip’s tail slowed. Mina hugged her keyboard.

Rollo’s chest felt heavy. He saw his friends’ faces. They looked sad. He remembered the show was for the neighbors, not just for him. He remembered the word fair.

He whispered to himself, “Fair means everyone gets a turn. Fair means everyone is heard.”

Rollo took a deep breath. He listened to his breath. In and out. The drum felt lighter in his paws. He used his thinking brain. He could fix this problem.

“Friends,” Rollo said, “I am sorry. I was not being fair.”

Bella looked up. “You weren’t?”

Rollo shook his head. “No. I wanted to do everything. That is not fair. I want to share. I want to be kind and responsible.”

Pip’s ears perked. “What can we do?”

Rollo tapped his chin. He used critical thinking. “Let’s make a plan. We will take turns. We will make a chart. We will count to five for each turn.”

He took a big piece of paper from his desk. He drew four boxes with a thick crayon. He wrote names: Rollo, Bella, Pip, Mina. He drew little pictures: a drum, a shaker, a bell, a keyboard, and a rainbow xylophone.

“We will switch after five counts,” Rollo said. “We will listen to each other. We will make a Fair Share Song.”

Bella grinned. “I like that!”

Mina clapped her tiny paws. “Me too!”

Pip’s tail went thump-thump on the quilt.

Rollo handed Bella the xylophone mallets. “You first,” he said. “I will count.”

He counted slow and clear. “One… two… three… four… five!”

Bella played. Plink, plonk, plink! The sound was bright like sunshine on glass. Everyone smiled.

“Now Pip,” said Rollo, passing the bell. “Ding, ding!” Pip made a happy chime.

Rollo gave Mina the drum. BOOM… boom… boom. It felt like soft footsteps on the bed.

When it was Rollo’s turn again, he took the shaker. Shake-shake-sha-shake! He laughed. “This is fun!”

They kept switching. They listened. They waited. They made room for each other’s ideas. They used kind words.

“Can we make a soft part?” asked Mina.

“Yes,” said Rollo. “Soft like a whisper.” They played quietly. The room felt like a hug.

“Can we make a loud part?” asked Pip.

“Yes,” said Rollo. “Loud like a cheer.” They played louder, but not too loud. Rollo reminded them, “We are in my bedroom. We can be loud and still be respectful.”

Bella added a new beat. Pip added a jingle. Mina added a beep. Rollo added a tap. Their Fair Share Song grew and grew. It was full of everyone’s sound.

When they finished, they took a bow to the lamp and the quilt and the moon outside the window. The cozy bedroom glowed again. The sweet cocoa smell felt warm again.

Rollo smiled. “Tomorrow, we will play this song for our neighbors. It will help our community. We were fair. We shared. We were responsible and kind.”

Bella said, “We made it together!”

Pip said, “We took turns!”

Mina said, “We listened!”

Rollo said, “That is fairness. Fair means everyone gets a turn and a voice. Fair makes music better. Fair makes friends feel good.”

They sipped cocoa and hummed the tune. Mmm-mmm-mmm. Their hearts felt light. Their tails, ears, and whiskers relaxed.

The next day, their street clapped and smiled. The Fair Share Song made people sway and grin. Rollo felt proud, but not just of the music. He felt proud of being fair.

And every Band Night after, the chart came out, the turns were counted, and the cozy bedroom filled with happy sounds and happy friends.

The End.
