# Sunny’s Song in the Warm Kitchen

Every morning, a little singing bird named Sunny fluttered to the warm kitchen window. The kitchen smelled like cinnamon toast and sweet oranges. The sun made soft golden squares on the floor. The kettle hummed, “Mmm,” and the clock went tick-tock, tick-tock.

“Good morning!” chirped Sunny. “It’s time for my morning song!”

Sunny had a routine. First, Sunny stretched tiny wings—up, up, and round. Next, Sunny sipped a tiny dish of water, tap-tap-tapping with a dainty beak. Then Sunny warmed up with little notes, “La-la, dee-dee, doo.” Sunny loved the routine. It made Sunny feel ready.

“Are you ready to sing?” asked the wooden spoon, clacking against a bowl.

“Almost!” said Sunny. “Preparation helps my song feel steady and strong!”

The oven gave a soft whoosh, letting out a cozy puff of warm air. “Mmm, I smell toast,” Sunny said, eyes twinkling. The butter on the counter was so soft it made tiny shiny puddles. The orange slices looked like little suns.

Sunny hopped closer. “Today I will sing a new song,” Sunny said. “I will sing about big, brave skies!”

“But Sunny,” whispered the tea bag, “your song book says today is ‘gentle morning song.’”

Sunny tilted a feathery head. “I can be open to new ideas,” Sunny chirped. “But I also need to prepare.”

The clock ticked in agreement: tick-tock, tick-tock.

Sunny took a deep breath. “Step one: Stretch.” Wings stretched—up, down, round and round. “Step two: Sip water.” Sip, sip. “Step three: Warm up.” “La, la, la,” sang Sunny softly. The warm kitchen seemed to hum along. The kettle shhhhed. The oven whooshed. The toast popped—pop!

Sunny peeked at the song book. The pages smelled like paper and a little like flour, because it lived on the counter. “Gentle morning song,” Sunny read. “But I feel brave today. Can I try something new?”

“Routine helps,” said the clock. “But being open can help, too.”

Sunny nodded. “I can prepare for gentle. I can prepare for brave. I can do both!”

So Sunny made a little plan.

“Plan step one,” said Sunny, tapping the page. “Start gentle, like soft butter.”

“Plan step two,” said the spoon. “Add a brave note, like crunchy toast.”

“Plan step three,” said the orange slices, gleaming. “End with a sunny smile.”

Sunny giggled. “Good plan!”

Sunny practiced the gentle part. “Doo-doo, la-la,” soft and sweet like warm milk. The sound floated around the kitchen like a soft scarf. Then Sunny practiced the brave part. “DEE-DAH!”—bright and strong like sunshine on a window.

The first brave note came out squeaky.

“Oops!” Sunny chirped. “I need more preparation.” Sunny took another sip of water and tried again, slower. “Dee … dah.” Better.

Just then, a little mouse named Pip peeked from behind the bread box. “Hi, Sunny,” squeaked Pip. “I had a busy night. I forgot to plan my morning snack. Now I feel all wiggly and rushed.”

Sunny fluttered down. “Preparation helps me feel calm,” Sunny said. “Would you like to make a tiny routine?”

Pip nodded, whiskers twitching. “Yes, please.”

“First,” said Sunny, “let’s make space.” Sunny and Pip moved the spoon and the jam jar. The counter felt smooth and cool.

“Second, we choose,” Sunny said. “Toast or orange first?”

Pip sniffed. “Orange first. It smells happy.”

“Third, we count,” Sunny said. “One slice for Pip. One sip of water. Then toast.”

Pip tried it. “One slice … one sip … then toast.” Pip’s eyes relaxed. “I feel ready!”

Sunny chirped, “Preparation is like a little hug for your day.”

Pip smiled. “Can I listen to your song?”

“Yes!” said Sunny. “I will follow my plan.”

The kitchen grew very quiet. The warm air felt like a soft blanket. Sunny began with the gentle notes, floating like steam. “Doo-doo, la-la.” The clock ticked softly in time. The kettle sighed. Pip hugged his tiny tail.

Then came the brave part. Sunny stood tall, chest puffed. “Dee … dah!” Strong, shiny notes filled the room. The brave sound didn’t push. It smiled. It was bright, but kind.

Finally, Sunny ended with a sunny smile of sound: “Luuu-laaa.” The notes curled around the butter, the toast, the tea, and the orange slices. The whole kitchen felt glad.

Pip clapped tiny paws. “That was perfect!”

Sunny beamed. “Preparation helped me try something new. My routine made me ready. And being open-minded let me add brave notes.”

The spoon clinked happily. The oven gave a warm hum. The orange slices glowed. The warm kitchen felt like a friend.

Pip nibbled toast and said, “Tomorrow I will prepare my snack plan. First slice, then sip, then toast. I can do it!”

Sunny nodded. “And I will check my song book, warm up, and stay open to new ideas. Routine helps me start. Preparation helps me feel safe. Being open helps me grow.”

Sunny looked at the golden squares on the floor. “Thank you, warm kitchen,” Sunny sang. “Thank you, little plan.”

Then Sunny and Pip hummed together, soft and brave, gentle and bright. The day felt ready, and so did they.

The End.
