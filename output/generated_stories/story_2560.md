# Koala Kai Cleans the Shed

Koala Kai was a calm koala with soft, gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived near a little garden shed that smelled like fresh soil and lemon soap. Every morning, Kai stretched, took a deep breath, and said, “I am ready to help.”

One sunny day, Kai peeked inside the garden shed. “Oh,” he whispered. The shelves were messy. A broom lay on the floor. A box of cans sat open. A snack wrapper crinkled under his paw. It was noisy and cluttered. The shed felt crowded and not very safe.

Kai put his paw on his heart. “I care for my place,” he said. “I can help.” This was civic responsibility—taking care of the shared space so everyone could use it.

“Good morning, shed,” Kai sang softly. His voice was gentle like a lullaby. “Let’s tidy and sort.”

He made a simple plan:
- Wash hands
- Tidy and sort
- Recycle and reuse
- Snack and rest

First, Kai went to the little sink. The water was cool and splashy. “Swish, swish,” he sang as he washed his paws with soap. “Clean paws help me stay healthy.” He dried them on a clean towel. They felt warm and fluffy.

Next, he picked up the broom. “Sweep, sweep,” went the bristles. Dust swirled like tiny clouds and then settled. Kai wore a small mask to keep dust out of his nose. “Healthy habits are smart,” he hummed.

As he swept, he found things to sort. “This is trash,” Kai said, picking up the crinkly wrapper. “This is recycling,” he said, tapping the shiny can with a gentle clink. He found a small plastic bottle, a paper box, and a broken leaf rake.

“Let’s make homes for each thing,” Kai decided. He lined up three bins. He made simple labels with pictures: a banana peel for compost, a can for recycling, and a little trash bag for waste.

“Clink, clink,” went the cans into recycling. “Rustle, rustle,” went the paper. “Plop,” went the apple core into compost. Kai smiled. “Recycling saves resources. Compost feeds the garden. Trash stays small.”

Just then, Little Bee buzzed in from the garden. “Bzz, bzz! What are you doing, Kai?”

“I’m caring for our shed,” said Kai. “Everyone uses this place. It should be clean and safe.”

“Bzz, can I help?” asked Little Bee.

“Yes,” said Kai. “You can hold the labels.”

Little Bee held the picture labels while Kai placed them carefully. The shed began to look friendly again.

Next, Sparrow flew to the window. “Chirp! I see a wobbly box.”

“Good eye,” said Kai. “Let’s make it steady.” He pushed the heavy box to a low shelf. “We keep heavy things low. That is safe for everyone.”

Kai opened the snack tin. He found one sticky treat wrapper inside. “Oops,” he said kindly. “We toss wrappers into trash or, if they’re clean paper, into recycling. We keep bugs and mice away.”

“Bzz, thank you for teaching,” said Little Bee.

“You are welcome,” Kai replied. “We learn together.”

Kai checked the broken rake. The handle was cracked. He touched it gently. “Can we fix it?” he asked. He wrapped the handle with tape and placed it in a “Fix It” corner. “We try to repair before we throw away,” he explained. “That helps the Earth.”

A soft breeze came through the door. Kai smelled rosemary and damp wood. The shed felt calm and cool now. Shelves were neat. Bins were labeled. The floor was clear.

Kai clapped softly. “Time for a healthy break.” He washed his paws again—swish, swish—then poured a cup of cool water and ate a crunchy carrot stick. “Crunch!” He shared sweet apple slices with Sparrow and offered a small sip of water to Little Bee in a tiny leaf cup.

“Thank you,” chirped Sparrow.

“Bzz, yum,” buzzed Little Bee.

Kai posted a little sign near the door. It had a picture list:
- Wash hands
- Put things back
- Sort recycling
- Keep heavy things low
- Share and help

“This is our shed routine,” said Kai. “A routine helps us remember to care.”

From outside, Tortoise trundled in. “I like this tidy shed,” he said slowly. “I feel safe here.”

Kai’s cheeks felt warm with pride. “We did it together,” he said. “When we care for shared places, we help our whole neighborhood.”

Little Bee buzzed a happy tune. Sparrow chirped along. Tortoise nodded to the beat. Kai hummed, “Sweep, sort, share, and smile.”

The sun dipped a little, and golden light filled the shed. Kai looked around one more time. He closed the bins, put the broom on its hook, and took a deep, calm breath. The shed smelled like lemon soap, wood, and a little bit of apple.

“We will keep this up every day,” Kai said. “Small jobs, big help.”

Everyone agreed.

“See you tomorrow,” Kai told the shed.

“Chirp, bzz, nod,” sang his friends as they headed out.

Kai felt peaceful. He had used healthy habits. He had recycled and reused. He had cared for a shared place. He had shown civic responsibility with kindness and calm.

And that felt just right.

The End.
