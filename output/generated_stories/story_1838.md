# Benny Beaver’s Pancake Plan

In a warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon and sunshine, a busy beaver named Benny wore a tiny apron. The apron had little blue bubbles on it. Pop! Pop! The bubbles were just pictures, but Benny liked to pretend he could hear them. He patted the apron and smiled.

“Today is Pancake Day,” Benny said. His voice sounded happy and bright. “My goal is to make a perfect pancake stack. Tall and tasty!”

Benny wiggled his flat tail. Tap, tap. He opened a recipe book with soft, sticky pages. He took a deep breath. The kitchen smelled like vanilla, warm wood, and sweet berries in a bowl. The stove hummed a low song. The mixing bowl felt cool and smooth in his paws.

Benny was busy. He was always busy. He grabbed the flour, the milk, the eggs, and the whisk. He reached for everything at once.

“Oh!” Benny said as the whisk clinked to the floor. “Oops.”

He grabbed again. The egg wobbled. “Oh!” The egg slipped. Splish! It landed in the bowl, shell and all.

Benny frowned. His tail drooped. “I want a perfect stack, but I can’t do everything at the same time.”

He turned off the stove with a soft click. He took a deep breath. In-and-out. In-and-out. The warm kitchen felt safe. The sun made a bright square on the floor. A fly buzzed by the window. Bzzzz.

“I need a plan,” Benny said. “I need coordination.” He said the word slowly, tasting each piece. “Co-or-di-na-tion. That means we work in order, and we work together.” He tapped his head gently. “My paws and eyes and tail can be a team.”

Benny set a goal. “First, I will clean. Next, I will mix. Then, I will cook. Last, I will stack.” He held up one paw-finger for each step. “Four steps. I can do four steps.”

He looked at the messy egg shell in the bowl. “I will talk kindly to myself,” he said softly. “It’s okay to start again.” He smiled a little smile. “Peaceful words help.”

Benny cleaned the bowl. Swish, swish went the sponge. Drip, drip went the water. He dried it with a soft towel. Pat, pat.

“Now I mix,” he said. He set the flour, milk, and eggs in a line. “We go in order,” he told them. “Flour first.” Plooof! The flour puffed like a little white cloud. “Milk next.” Glug, glug. “Then eggs.” Crack! Crack! He tapped them gently on the bowl edge and dropped in the eggs without shells.

Benny held the whisk. He whispered, “Paws, eyes, and whisk, let’s work together.” He watched the bowl. He held the bowl steady with one paw. He whisked in circles with the other. Swirl, swirl, swirl. The batter became smooth and creamy. It smelled like vanilla dreams.

“Good job, team,” Benny said to himself.

He turned on the stove. Click! Wooooo, went the little flame. He poured a small puddle of batter into the warm pan. Ssssss. The sound was like a soft snake. Tiny bubbles popped on top. Pop, pop, pop.

Benny wanted to flip it fast. His tail twitched. But he stopped. “We wait for bubbles on the edges,” he said calmly. “Co-or-di-na-tion means timing, too.” He watched. He counted softly, “One, two, three…” The edges turned golden.

“Now,” he said. He slid the spatula under the pancake. He kept his paw steady. He breathed. Flip! The pancake turned over and landed with a gentle plop. The kitchen smelled warm and sweet.

He made another pancake, and another. He kept his plan. Mix, pour, wait, flip. He hummed a little song. “We work together, we take our time. Paws and whisk and happy mind.”

Knock, knock! Someone tapped at the back door.

Benny opened it. His friend Pippa, a chipmunk with bright eyes, peeked in. “Hi, Benny! It smells yummy!”

“It’s Pancake Day,” Benny said. “I have a goal. A tall, tasty stack.”

Pippa clapped. “Can I help?”

“Yes,” Benny said. “Let’s use peaceful communication. I will say what I need. You will listen. Then you can tell me what you need.”

Pippa nodded. “Okay!”

Benny pointed to the berries. “Pippa, please wash the berries and pat them dry.”

Pippa saluted with a giggle. “Yes, Chef Benny!” She ran water over the berries. Tinkle, tinkle. Then she patted them with a towel. Squish? No, just soft and juicy.

“Thank you,” Benny said. “I feel calm when we work in order.”

Pippa smiled. “I feel helpful.”

Together, they kept the rhythm. Benny poured batter. Pippa set plates. Benny flipped. Pippa topped each pancake with three berries. “One, two, three,” she counted. “We are a good team.”

They had a small problem when the last pancake stuck to the pan. “Uh-oh,” said Pippa.

“It’s okay,” said Benny. “We can be resourceful. Let’s add a tiny pat of butter.” Sizzle. “Now we wait.” He counted again. “One, two, three…” Flip! The pancake came free.

At last, the stack stood tall and golden. The berries made a red and blue crown on top. A little river of honey flowed down the sides. The warm kitchen felt extra cozy. The sun on the floor looked like a golden napkin.

Benny and Pippa sat at the table. The chairs creaked softly. “We did it,” Benny said. He felt proud in his chest. It felt like a warm light.

Pippa took a bite. “Mmm! Perfect.”

Benny nodded. “We had a goal. We made a plan. We used coordination. We took turns and talked kindly. We solved problems with calm words.”

Pippa tapped her spoon. “Teamwork tastes sweet.”

Benny laughed. “Let’s eat!”

They shared the pancakes. They giggled when honey dripped. They said thank you and please. When they were done, they cleaned up together. Swish, clink, pat. The kitchen shone.

Benny hung up his blue-bubble apron. “Today I learned that coordination helps me reach a goal,” he said. “When my body, my words, and my friends work together, I can do big things.”

Pippa nodded. “Me too.”

The warm kitchen hummed softly. The goal was met, the stack was gone, and their hearts felt full.

The End.
