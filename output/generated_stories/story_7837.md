# Bruno Bear Cub’s Guest Room Puzzle

Bruno was a friendly bear cub with soft, warm fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny afternoon, he padded into Grandma Maple’s house. He sniffed the air. It smelled like cinnamon cookies and clean linen. Today, Bruno would help set up the guest room for a special visitor.

“Bruno,” Grandma Maple said with a smile, “Auntie Robin is staying tonight. Can you help me make the guest room cozy?”

“Yes!” Bruno said, clapping his paws. “I want to help.”

They walked to the guest room. The room felt cool and quiet. Sunlight made little squares on the wooden floor. A fluffy blue rug waited beside the bed. Fresh sheets lay folded in a neat stack. A small, round lamp sat on the table, but it was a little wobbly.

Bruno set his ear near the window and heard birds chirp. He touched the soft sheets. “They feel like clouds,” he said.

Grandma Maple chuckled. “First, let’s make the bed. Then we will fix the wobbly lamp. Finally, we’ll find a spot for Auntie Robin’s book.”

Bruno nodded. “We can do it together.”

They unfolded the sheet. It was wide and floppy. The sheet fell over Bruno’s head. “Oh!” he giggled. “It’s dark and tickly.”

“Let’s try again,” said Grandma. “We can cooperate. That means we work as a team.”

“How do we do that?” Bruno asked.

“We can talk, listen, and help,” Grandma said gently. “You hold one corner, and I will hold the other. We lift at the same time.”

Bruno held his corner tight. “Ready?” he asked.

“Ready,” Grandma said.

“One, two, three!” they lifted and shook the sheet. It floated like a white cloud and settled softly on the bed. Bruno smoothed the edges. His paws made little whoosh sounds. “We did it!” he cheered.

“Teamwork makes it easier,” Grandma said. “Now, the blanket.”

They spread the blanket together. Bruno felt proud and warm inside. He liked helping.

Next was the wobbly lamp. It made a tiny tap-tap on the table. “Why is it wobbly?” Bruno wondered.

“Let’s use our thinking,” Grandma said. “We can look, listen, and test.”

Bruno bent low. He looked closely. “I see a little screw,” he said. “It is loose.”

“Good discovery,” Grandma said. “Do we have a tool?”

Bruno thought. He touched his chin. “The drawer!” he said. He opened the drawer slowly. It squeaked. Inside was a small, friendly-looking screwdriver. Bruno held it carefully. “May I try?” he asked.

“Thank you for asking,” Grandma said. “Yes, you may. I will hold the lamp steady.”

Bruno turned the screwdriver, righty-tighty. The screw snugged in with a soft click. The lamp stood straight. Bruno pressed the switch. Click. The lamp glowed warm and yellow, like honey. “We fixed it together,” he said.

“You used careful thinking,” Grandma said. “And you were gentle with the lamp. That shows respect.”

Bruno’s ears perked up. “Respect means I take care of things and people,” he said.

“Exactly,” Grandma smiled.

Now they needed a place for Auntie Robin’s book. Bruno looked around. The table had a seashell dish and a small plant. The plant had tiny, sweet leaves that smelled like lemon. The windowsill had sunshine, but it was high. The bottom shelf of the little bookshelf had space, but it also had a puzzle box.

Bruno asked, “Where should we put the book so Auntie Robin can reach it easily?”

Grandma said, “Let’s think about the visitor. What would be kind and helpful for her?”

Bruno looked at the table. “The lamp is here. Light for reading,” he said. He looked at the plant. “The plant needs space to breathe.” He looked at the shelf. “The shelf is low. That might be easy, but it is far from the lamp.”

Bruno took a deep breath. He listened to his body feel calm. “Grandma, may we move the seashell dish to the shelf? Then the book can sit by the lamp.”

“That is a thoughtful plan,” Grandma said. “Let’s cooperate again. I’ll lift the dish. You move the plant a little.”

Bruno used gentle paws. The leaves brushed his fur and smelled fresh. They placed the seashell dish on the shelf next to the puzzle box. The shells made a soft clink-clink sound. Bruno set the plant to the side and made sure it had room and light. Then he placed Auntie Robin’s book by the lamp. It looked just right.

They stepped back. The bed was smooth. The lamp was steady. The book waited by the warm light. The room felt cozy and welcoming.

“Bruno,” Grandma said, “how do you feel?”

Bruno’s eyes shone. “I feel proud. We worked together. We talked, listened, and helped. We respected the things in the room. We used our thinking to solve little problems.”

Grandma hugged him. Her sweater felt soft and smelled like cinnamon. “That is cooperation,” she said. “When we cooperate, we make good things happen.”

Just then, they heard the door. Auntie Robin called, “Hello!”

Bruno and Grandma led her to the guest room. Auntie Robin smiled wide. “Oh! The bed looks so comfy. The lamp is perfect. My book is right here!”

Bruno said, “We made it cozy for you. We worked as a team.”

Auntie Robin patted Bruno’s head. “Thank you for your care and respect.”

Bruno’s heart felt warm, like the lamp’s glow. He whispered, “Teamwork makes the room feel happy.”

And everyone agreed.

The End.
