# Miko’s Music Room Discovery

Miko was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and soft, fuzzy fur. He wore a tiny red scarf that made him feel brave. Today, Miko stepped into the musical practice room. The room smelled like fresh wood and clean soap. Sunlight made little dust sparkles dance in the air.

“Hello, room!” Miko said with a grin. “I’m ready to discover!”

Inside the room were instruments from all over the world. There was a shiny drum from Africa that went boom-boom. There was a wooden flute from South America that sang sweetly. There was a long sitar from India that hummed like a warm bee. There were maracas, a triangle, and a big piano with white and black teeth.

Miko reached for the drum and then paused. On a low table, he saw a small sink, soap, and a sign with pictures. The sign showed hands with bubbles, and a smiley face.

Miko tilted his head. “What is this? A clue?”

A gentle voice came from the doorway. It was Ms. Lina, the music teacher. “Hello, Miko! Today is a discovery day. We will discover sounds, and we will discover how to care for our hands and our world.”

“Our world?” Miko asked, eyes wide.

“Yes,” said Ms. Lina. “These instruments are from many places. We show love to the world when we take care of our things and our bodies. First discovery: clean hands help us play better.”

Miko puffed his chest. “I am brave enough to try!”

He climbed on a step stool and turned on the water. The water was warm. It felt like a tiny rain on his fingers. He pumped soap. Squish! The soap smelled like lemons. “Bubbles!” Miko giggled. He rubbed his palms. He scrubbed the backs of his hands. He wiggled his thumbs. He sang as he scrubbed, “Scrub, scrub, scrub, clean hands for me. One, two, three, play happily!”

Ms. Lina clapped. “Great job, Miko. Rinse and dry.”

Miko rinsed the bubbles away. The water made a soft splish-splash. He patted his hands with a soft towel. “Ready!” he said.

“Second discovery,” Ms. Lina said. She pointed to the drum. “This drum is called a djembe. It comes from Africa. It talks with your hands.”

“It talks?” Miko asked.

“Listen,” said Ms. Lina. She tapped the drum. Boom, boom, BOOM. It sounded like a brave heart.

Miko put his clean hands on the drum. Tap-tap, boom-boom. The sound filled his chest. “I feel strong!” he said.

“Music helps us feel,” Ms. Lina smiled. “And clean hands help keep the drum’s skin nice. That is hygiene. Hygiene means we keep things clean and healthy.”

Miko nodded. “Hygiene helps music!”

They went to the flute. “This flute is from South America,” Ms. Lina said. “It sings like wind.”

Miko held the flute gently. He took a small breath. He blew. The flute made a soft, airy tweet. It tickled his lip. “Hee-hee! It sounds like a little bird.”

“Your careful hands keep the flute clean,” Ms. Lina said. “That shows love for people far away who made it.”

Next, they sat near the sitar. It glowed with rich wood. Miko plucked a string. Twanggg. It was long and warm. He put his ear near. The sound buzzed like a bee in a sunny garden.

“I can hear the world,” Miko whispered.

“You are discovering,” Ms. Lina said. “You are brave, kind, and careful.”

Miko found maracas with bright colors. He shook them gently. Chika-chika! He moved his shoulders. He wiggled his tail. He laughed. “My body likes this!”

Ms. Lina nodded. “Music travels. It travels from country to country. People share sounds and stories. We can learn from everyone.”

Miko looked around. “So many places. So many friends,” he said softly.

He noticed a small smudge on the piano keys. “Oh!” Miko said. “Another clue!” He got a soft cloth from the shelf. He wiped gently. Wipe, wipe. The keys shone. He sat and pressed one key. Ding. Then two. Ding-dong. Then three. Ding-dong-ding. He made a tiny song.

“I discovered a clean key song,” Miko said proudly.

Ms. Lina smiled. “You discovered with your eyes, your ears, and your hands. And you used hygiene to care for the instruments. That is smart and kind.”

Miko hopped down. He walked back to the sink. “After playing, we clean again,” he said. He washed his hands with lemon soap. Squish. Bubbles danced. He dried them well.

“Why again?” Ms. Lina asked gently.

“So I keep me clean, and I keep the room clean too,” Miko said. “That helps everyone. People here, and people far away who share music.”

“That is global awareness,” Ms. Lina said. “You see the big world in a small room.”

Miko felt warm and proud. He hugged his red scarf. “Today, I discovered sounds. I discovered clean hands. I discovered our big world.”

Ms. Lina knelt and looked into his bright eyes. “What did you learn, brave Miko?”

Miko thought. He smiled. “I learned to look, listen, and care. I learned that clean hands make happy music. I learned that our world is full of friends.”

“Beautiful,” Ms. Lina said.

Miko waved to the instruments. “Thank you, drum. Thank you, flute. Thank you, sitar. Thank you, piano. I will keep you clean. I will play with care.”

The room felt sunny and soft. The dust sparkles danced again, like tiny stars. Miko’s heart went boom-boom, like the drum, brave and kind.

He took a deep breath. The lemon smell made him giggle. “I’m ready for more discoveries tomorrow,” he said.

The End.
