# Poppy the Creative Parrot Plans and Shares

Poppy was a bright green parrot with rainbow tail feathers. She loved to say, “Squawk! I can make, I can do!” Poppy’s favorite place was the study corner. It was a cozy spot with a small table, soft floor cushions, a little shelf of books, and a sunny window. The study corner smelled like crayons and fresh paper. When the sun came in, it made warm, golden squares on the floor.

One morning, Poppy flapped to the study corner and said, “Today I will make a picture book! Squawk!” She set out crayons, paper scraps, a glue stick, and a cup for old crayons to save, not throw away. Poppy liked to save and reuse. “We care for our things. We care for our world,” she chirped.

Poppy started to draw a tree. “Green, green leaves,” she sang softly. She felt calm. She took slow breaths and pressed the crayon gently. Scratch, scratch. Then she drew a blue river. “Swish, swish,” she whispered, moving the crayon in smooth lines.

Just then, her friends peeked in. Miko the mouse, Tia the turtle, and Bibi the bunny liked the study corner too.

“What are you making?” asked Miko.

“A picture book!” said Poppy. “It will show how to care for nature. Squawk!”

“I want to help!” said Bibi.

“Me too,” said Tia.

Poppy puffed her feathers. She felt excited, but also a little worried. She only had one glue stick and a few crayons. If they all grabbed at once, things might break. Poppy wanted to be fair and gentle. She wanted to use tools carefully.

Poppy took a slow breath. “We can do this together,” she said. “First, let’s plan. Then, we can take turns.”

Miko’s whiskers twitched. “Plan? How?”

Poppy tapped her beak. “We will choose jobs. That is called decision-making. We will use inside voices and gentle hands. That is self-regulation. We will reuse paper scraps and old crayons. That is conservation. Squawk!”

Everyone nodded.

Poppy drew a little chart on a scrap. “Job one: drawing. Job two: cutting. Job three: gluing. Job four: checking and saving.”

“I can cut slow and safe,” said Tia. “Snip, snip.”

“I can draw,” said Bibi, hopping happily.

“I can glue,” said Miko.

“And I will check and save,” said Poppy. “I will place tiny crayon bits in the reuse cup. We will use just what we need.”

They began. Bibi drew soft clouds. “Puffy and white,” she said, making round shapes. Tia snipped leaves from green paper scraps. Snip, snip. The scissors made a tiny clicking sound. Miko dabbed glue in small dots. “Dot, dot, not a lot,” he sang. Poppy checked the table. She kept the crayons in a neat line. She saved the little pieces. She felt proud of their careful work.

But soon, a problem popped up. Miko squeezed the glue too hard. “Oh no!” he cried. A big blob plopped onto the page. It was sticky and wet. The paper wrinkled.

Bibi’s ears drooped. “Our book is ruined,” she said.

Poppy felt her heart thump fast. She wanted to shout, “Squawk!” But she remembered self-regulation. She put a wing on her chest and took three slow breaths. In, out. In, out. In, out. She felt calmer.

“It’s okay,” Poppy said softly. “Mistakes help us learn. We can solve this.”

“How?” asked Miko, sniffling.

Poppy looked around. She had saved a dry cloth. She had also saved a piece of clean scrap paper. “We will use tools to fix it,” she said. “Press the cloth to soak up the extra glue. Then lay the scrap on top and smooth it.”

Everyone helped. Press, press. Smooth, smooth. The blob was covered, and the page was strong again.

Miko smiled. “Thank you. I will squeeze gently next time. Dot, dot, not a lot.”

Poppy nodded. “Great self-control.”

Later, another problem came. Bibi and Tia both reached for the same green crayon. Their paws bumped. “I need it!” they both said.

Poppy raised a wing like a traffic sign. “Stop. Let’s use our plan. We can take turns. Bibi, count to five while Tia colors. Then switch.”

They tried it. “One, two, three, four, five,” counted Bibi. Tia passed the crayon with a smile. “Your turn.” The coloring went faster and felt kinder. Cooperation felt good.

By the end of the morning, the study corner table was full of bright pages. Trees, rivers, birds, and a little sign that said, “Please reuse. Please be gentle.” The room smelled like crayons and paper, and the sun made the pages shine.

Poppy fluffed her feathers. “We did it! Our picture book is ready.”

They read it together. Poppy turned each page. “Care for nature. Share tools. Take turns. Use just what you need. Breathe when upset. Make a plan. Solve problems.”

Miko clapped his tiny paws. “We learned so many skills!”

Tia nodded slowly. “We worked together.”

Bibi hugged Poppy. “You are a creative parrot and a kind leader.”

Poppy smiled. “We all are helpers. We can build skills every day. Squawk!”

They cleaned up the study corner. Poppy put tiny crayon bits in the reuse cup. Miko clicked the glue stick closed. Tia stacked the scraps. Bibi wiped the table. The room looked neat and calm. The sun was warm, and everyone felt proud inside.

Poppy looked at her friends. “What did we learn today?” she asked.

Miko said, “Plan first.”

Tia said, “Take turns.”

Bibi said, “Use just what you need.”

Poppy added, “Breathe, be gentle, and try again.”

They all smiled. “We can do it together!”

The End.
