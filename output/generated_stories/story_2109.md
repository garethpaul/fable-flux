# Joey's Special Bath Time Words

Every evening, when the sun painted the sky orange, it was bath time for Joey the kangaroo joey. He loved splashing in the warm, bubbly water. He loved his yellow rubber duck. He loved making soap mountains with his tiny paws. But tonight felt different.

"Bath time, Joey!" called Mama Kangaroo from the bathroom.

Joey bounced excitedly down the hallway. His soft gray fur bounced with each hop. The bathroom smelled like lavender soap and felt warm and steamy.

But when Joey reached the doorway, he heard something unusual. Mama was singing, but not in English like always. The words sounded different - musical and flowing.

"Mama, what are those funny words?" Joey asked, tilting his head.

Mama smiled warmly. "These are words from my childhood, little one. My mama sang them to me when I was your age. They're in our family's special language."

Joey wrinkled his nose. "But I don't understand them. They sound weird."

Mama's ears drooped a little. "They're not weird, Joey. They're beautiful words that tell stories about our family. Would you like to learn?"

"No thank you," Joey said quickly. "I only want English words. Those are the real words."

Mama looked sad, but she helped Joey into the warm bath anyway. The water felt nice, but something didn't feel right. Mama wasn't singing her happy songs anymore. She quietly washed Joey's fur without the special words.

The bathroom felt too quiet. Even Joey's rubber duck seemed lonely floating in the silent bubbles.

"Mama," Joey said softly, "why aren't you singing?"

"I thought you didn't want to hear my special words," Mama said gently.

Joey felt a funny feeling in his tummy. Not a good funny - a sad funny. "But... but I like when you sing. The bathroom sounds empty without your voice."

Mama's eyes sparkled. "Would you like to try learning just one word?"

Joey nodded slowly. "Maybe one tiny word."

"This word means 'love,'" Mama said, singing a gentle sound that felt like a warm hug.

Joey tried to copy the sound. It felt strange on his tongue, but also special. "What does it mean again?"

"Love," Mama repeated. "Like how much I love you."

"Can you teach me the word for 'happy'?" Joey asked, splashing excitedly.

Mama's whole face lit up. She sang another beautiful word, and Joey tried to copy it. The sounds felt like music in his mouth.

"These words are like secret treasures!" Joey giggled. "Can we sing together?"

"Of course, my little joey," Mama said. "We can mix your English words with our family words. That way, bath time has the best of both!"

So Mama sang a special song. She used English words that Joey knew, mixed with the flowing family words that sounded like gentle water. Joey sang along, learning new sounds and remembering that different didn't mean wrong.

The bathroom filled with their happy voices. Steam swirled around them like musical notes. Joey's rubber duck seemed to dance in the bubbles.

"Mama," Joey said as she wrapped him in his soft blue towel, "tomorrow can you teach me more family words?"

"I would love that," Mama said, nuzzling his clean fur. "Languages are gifts we share with people we love."

Joey learned that words from different places could all be beautiful. Some words felt familiar, like warm hugs. Other words felt new and exciting, like discovering hidden treasures. But all words were special when shared with love.

From that night on, bath time became even more magical, filled with songs in two languages and twice as much joy.

The End.
