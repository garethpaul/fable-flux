# Nutkin's Big Decision

In a quiet, dusty attic above Mrs. Chen's house, there lived a clever little squirrel named Nutkin. The attic was filled with old boxes, forgotten treasures, and warm sunbeams that streamed through a small round window.

Nutkin had fluffy gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved exploring the attic's cozy corners and collecting shiny buttons and colorful ribbons.

One chilly morning, Nutkin discovered something wonderful. Hidden behind a stack of old books was a large jar filled with the most beautiful acorns he had ever seen!

"Wow!" Nutkin squeaked with excitement. "These acorns are perfect!"

But as Nutkin reached for the jar, he noticed something important. His tiny paws were covered in dust and dirt from exploring the attic all morning.

"Hmm," Nutkin said, looking at his messy paws. "Should I grab these acorns right now, or should I clean my paws first?"

Nutkin really wanted those acorns. His tummy was rumbling, and they looked so tasty. But he remembered what his mama had taught him about keeping clean.

"Dirty paws can make food yucky," Nutkin said to himself. "I need to make a good decision."

Nutkin scampered over to a small puddle of rainwater that had collected near the window. He carefully washed his paws, scrubbing away all the dust and dirt.

"There!" he said, wiggling his clean, pink paws. "Much better!"

Just as Nutkin returned to the acorn jar, he heard a tiny voice.

"Please help me," squeaked the voice.

Nutkin looked around and saw a little mouse family huddled in the corner. They looked cold and hungry.

"We haven't eaten in two days," said Mama Mouse sadly. "The cold weather makes it hard to find food."

Nutkin looked at the jar of acorns, then at the hungry mouse family. He had another big decision to make.

"I could keep all these acorns for myself," Nutkin thought. "But these mice really need help."

Nutkin's kind heart knew what to do. He carefully opened the jar and counted out half of the acorns.

"Here," Nutkin said warmly, offering the acorns to the mouse family. "These will help keep you warm and full."

"Oh, thank you!" cried the little mice. "You're so generous!"

As Nutkin shared the acorns, something magical happened. The mice began telling wonderful stories about their adventures. They sang sweet songs and taught Nutkin fun games.

"This is even better than eating alone," Nutkin realized happily.

Soon, other attic creatures joined them. A friendly spider brought dewdrops to drink, and a wise old owl shared riddles and jokes.

The quiet attic filled with laughter and friendship. Nutkin felt warm and happy inside, much warmer than a full belly could ever make him feel.

As the sun set through the round window, Nutkin reflected on his day.

"I made two good decisions today," he said proudly. "I cleaned my paws before eating, and I shared with friends who needed help."

The mouse family snuggled close to Nutkin. "Thank you for thinking carefully and being so kind," whispered Mama Mouse.

That night, Nutkin fell asleep surrounded by new friends, his heart full of joy. He had learned that making thoughtful decisions – about cleanliness and sharing – could lead to the most wonderful surprises.

From that day forward, Nutkin always remembered to think carefully before acting, keep himself clean, and share with others in need.

The End.
