# Bubbles Learns to Ask and Learn

In a cozy, comfortable nursery, soft rugs lay like clouds on the floor. Sunlight made little squares on the walls. Paints, crayons, and big paper sat on a low table. A gentle hum from the air purifier sounded like a whispering seashell.

Splash! A playful dolphin named Bubbles peeked out from a big round water tank by the window. The tank glowed blue. Tiny bubbles rose and tickled Bubbles’ nose. “Hee-hee!” giggled Bubbles. “Today I will make art!”

Ms. Star, the kind caregiver, smiled. “Good morning, Bubbles. Good morning, friends. Today we will paint our feelings and learn new things. We will use our minds and our manners.”

Bubbles flicked his tail. “I know everything about water,” he said proudly. “I can do it all!”

Ella the elephant plush sat on a shelf. Toby the turtle toy rested nearby. Children in soft slippers sat in a circle. They held brushes and took deep breaths.

“First,” said Ms. Star, “we breathe. In like a flower. Out like a candle.” She breathed in slowly, then out. The room felt calm and safe.

Bubbles tried. In—cool air smelled like clean soap. Out—soft and slow. His chest felt light.

“Now painting,” said Ms. Star. “We share. We ask. We listen.”

Bubbles saw a shiny silver brush. He loved shiny things. “Mine!” he chirped, and he splashed his flipper to reach it. Water sprinkled the table. Drip, drop, drip!

“Hey,” said Mia, a little girl with curly hair. “My paper is wet.”

Toby the turtle toy bumped gently against Bubbles’ tank. “Respect,” he seemed to say.

Bubbles’ cheeks felt warm. “Oh,” he said, “I did not mean to splash you.”

Ms. Star knelt by the tank. “Bubbles, when we want something, we use our words. We ask. We learn the right way. That is knowledge. Knowledge helps us make good choices.”

Bubbles blinked. “I like learning,” he said. “How do I ask?”

“Try this,” said Ms. Star. “You can say, ‘May I use the silver brush when you are done?’”

Bubbles practiced. “May I use the silver brush when you are done?”

Mia smiled. “Yes, Bubbles. I will finish this sun. Then it’s your turn.”

Bubbles felt a warm glow inside, like sunshine on water. He waited. He watched Mia make yellow circles. He breathed in and out. The room smelled like sweet crayons and oranges from snack time.

While he waited, Bubbles made tiny bubbles that went “blip, blip.” He looked around the nursery. There were books with bright pictures. One book had sea animals. Another showed how to wash hands. Posters said, “We listen,” and “We take turns.”

“Here you go,” Mia said. She placed the silver brush by the tank. “Your turn.”

“Thank you,” said Bubbles. “I will be careful.” He dipped the brush in blue. He painted soft waves on thick paper taped near his tank. Swish, swish. The brush felt smooth. The paper sounded like a quiet scratch.

“I want to paint a rainbow,” said Bubbles. He reached for red paint. His flipper bumped the cup. Wiggle—wiggle—tip! Red paint wobbled.

“Careful,” said Ms. Star, but the cup began to fall.

Bubbles froze. His heart thumped. “Oh no!”

Ms. Star held up a hand. “What do we do when we feel worried?”

“Breathe,” whispered Bubbles. “In like a flower. Out like a candle.” He took a slow breath. Then he used his flipper to steady the cup. Plop! It settled back down. No spill.

Bubbles smiled. “I did it!”

“You used mindfulness,” said Ms. Star. “You noticed your feelings. You used your breath. You solved the problem.”

Bubbles dipped into red. He painted a curve. Then orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple. His rainbow arched over the waves. It looked happy.

He saw Ella the elephant plush with a little paint dot on her ear. “Oops,” said Bubbles. “I made a mess.”

“Healthy habits,” said Ms. Star kindly. She handed Bubbles a soft cloth. “We clean up. We wash hands and tools.”

Bubbles wiped the dot from Ella’s ear. He washed the brush in a cup of water. Swirl, swirl, clear again. He even wiped tiny splashes from the table. It smelled fresh.

“Bubbles,” Mia said, “can you show me how to make waves like yours?”

Bubbles beamed. “Yes! First, look at the picture in the sea book.” He pointed with his fin. “See how waves go up and down? Then swish your brush like this.”

Mia tried. Swish, swish. “I did it!”

“You learned,” said Bubbles. “I like sharing what I know.”

“And I like learning from you,” said Mia. “Now I’ll share. Do you want to know how to paint a sun with little rays?”

“Yes, please,” said Bubbles.

Mia showed him how to dot the center and flick tiny lines around it. Tap, tap, flick, flick. The sun looked bright and warm.

Bubbles made a small sign for their art: “We Ask. We Breathe. We Share. We Care.” He drew gentle pictures next to each word so everyone could understand.

At snack time, they washed hands with warm water and soap. Rub, rub, rinse. Their hands smelled like lemons. They ate apple slices and drank water. Crunch, sip. Bubbles munched seaweed snacks in his tank. “Yum!”

Ms. Star clapped softly. “Friends, today you used knowledge, mindfulness, art, healthy habits, and respect. You asked questions. You listened. You taught each other.”

Bubbles felt proud, but also calm. He looked at the rainbow and the sun and the waves. “I learned to ask,” he said. “I learned to breathe. I learned to share what I know. Learning helps me help others.”

Mia nodded. “And it makes art more fun!”

Everyone smiled. The nursery felt warm and bright, like a big hug.

The End.
