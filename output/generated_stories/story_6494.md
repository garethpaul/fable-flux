# Melody's Big Discovery

Melody was a bright yellow canary who lived in a cozy cage near the window of Daddy's home office. Every morning, she loved to sing her beautiful songs - "Tweet-tweet-trill! La-la-tweet!" Her voice was as sweet as honey and as bright as sunshine.

But one Monday morning, something was different. Daddy sat at his big wooden desk with a serious face, talking into a small black box.

"Good morning, everyone. Let's begin our meeting," Daddy said in his quiet work voice.

Melody didn't understand. Where was her morning greeting? Where was Daddy's smile? She felt confused and a little sad. So she did what always made her feel better - she sang her loudest, happiest song.

"TWEET-TWEET-TRILL! LA-LA-LA-TWEET!"

"Shh, Melody," Daddy whispered, looking worried. "Not right now, please."

Melody's little heart felt heavy. Why couldn't she sing? Singing was what birds do! She tried again, even louder this time.

"TWEET-TRILL-TWEET! TRILL-LA-LA!"

"Melody, please be quiet," Daddy said, his voice sounding frustrated. He covered the black box with his hand.

Now Melody felt angry. Her feathers ruffled up like a tiny golden puffball. She wanted to sing SO badly! Her chest felt tight and hot. She hopped from perch to perch, making the cage rattle - *clink, clink, clink.*

But then Melody noticed something. She tilted her little head and listened carefully. Daddy was talking, but other tiny voices were coming from the black box too! She heard a lady's voice say, "Can everyone hear me clearly?"

Melody's curiosity grew bigger than her anger. What was that magical talking box? She listened more closely, her bright black eyes watching Daddy's every move.

After a while, Daddy said, "Thank you, everyone. Talk to you tomorrow." He closed the black box and turned to Melody with tired eyes.

"I'm sorry, little friend," Daddy said softly. "I know you love to sing. But when I have work meetings, I need it to be quiet so everyone can hear each other talk."

Melody chirped quietly, "Tweet?" She was still curious about the talking box.

Daddy smiled for the first time that morning. "That's my computer, Melody. I talk to my work friends through it. Just like how you talk to me with your beautiful songs."

Melody's anger melted away like ice cream in the sun. She understood now! Daddy needed quiet time to talk to his friends, just like she needed time to sing to hers.

"Tweet-tweet?" Melody asked gently.

"You want to know when you can sing?" Daddy chuckled. "How about this - when my computer is closed, you can sing all you want. When it's open for meetings, could you sing very softly or wait until I'm done?"

Melody thought about this. She could see Daddy's computer from her cage. When it was closed, she could sing loudly. When it was open, she could sing quietly or find other fun things to do - like playing with her mirror or organizing her seeds.

"Tweet!" Melody chirped happily. That sounded fair!

The next morning, Daddy opened his computer for another meeting. Melody saw this and instead of singing loudly, she whispered the tiniest, sweetest "tweet" and then played quietly with her favorite bell.

When Daddy closed his computer, he smiled big. "Thank you for being so thoughtful, Melody! Now it's singing time!"

"TWEET-TWEET-TRILL! LA-LA-LA-TWEET!" Melody sang joyfully, and Daddy clapped his hands.

From that day on, Melody and Daddy worked together perfectly. Melody learned that compromise means finding a way for everyone to be happy, and that was the most beautiful song of all.

The End.
