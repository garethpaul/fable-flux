# Ruby Rabbit’s Safe Science Day

Ruby was a small, determined rabbit with bright eyes and soft, gray fur. She had a big dream. “I want to make bubble lights,” she said one sunny morning. “Glow, glow, glow!” Her ears stood tall with excitement.

Ruby lived with her family in a cozy house. Under the house was a special place: the basement lab. It smelled a little like soap and lemons. The floor felt cool under Ruby’s paws. There were shelves with jars, a small sink, a humming air cleaner, and a big table with a soft, blue mat. On the wall, there was a poster with pictures: goggles, gloves, and a broom. It said, “Safety First!”

Ruby hopped down the steps, thump, thump, thump. “Today I will make bubble lights,” she told herself. “I have a goal. I will plan. I will be careful.”

She stopped at the top step and took a deep breath. The air felt cool and a little dusty. “Step one,” she whispered. “Ask for help.” Ruby called, “Mom? I’m going to the lab!”

Mom Rabbit peeked down the stairs. “What’s your plan, Ruby?”

“I want glowing bubbles,” Ruby said. “I will use safe soap, water, and a tiny bit of glow paint that is kid-safe. I will wear goggles. I will set a timer. I will clean up.”

Mom smiled. “I hear your plan. Good goal. What is the safety rule?”

Ruby stood straight. “No hot, no sharp, no mixing unknown things. Goggles on. Gloves on. Vent on. Ask if I’m not sure.”

Mom nodded. “I trust you. I’ll be upstairs if you need me. Call me if you have a question.”

Ruby hopped into the lab. She pressed the vent button. The machine went whirr, like a soft wind. She slipped on small, clear goggles. Click! She put on little blue gloves. Snap! The gloves felt smooth and a bit squeaky. Ruby giggled.

On the table, Ruby set her items: a wide bowl, a spoon, a cup of water, gentle soap, and safe glow paint made for kids. She put a big towel on the floor in case of drips.

Ruby spoke to herself, “Step two, measure.” She poured water. Swish, swish. She added soap. Plop, plop. Her whisk made little foam. “Step three, add a tiny bit of glow.” She squeezed one small drop. Drip!

She bent close and sniffed. It smelled like clean soap. The glow paint had no smell. “Safe and gentle,” she said. “Good.”

Ruby dipped a bubble wand. She blew a slow breath. Whooo. A bubble grew and floated. It was green and glowy like a soft firefly. “Oh!” Ruby clapped. The bubble made a tiny pop. Pop!

Ruby made more bubbles. Pop, pop, pop! The lights were low, so the glow looked bright. In the quiet room, the bubbles sounded like rain drops. She felt happy. Her heart went thump in a fast, excited way.

She tried a big breath. Whoooooo. A giant bubble wobbled like a jelly moon. “Come on, bubble,” Ruby whispered. It bumped the wall. Pop! A shiny drip slid down. “Oops,” said Ruby. She grabbed a cloth and wiped it up. “Rule: clean as you go.”

Ruby wanted rainbow bubbles. “Can I mix blue and green?” She paused. Her whisk hovered. She remembered the rule: no mixing unknown things. She checked the labels. Both paints said “kid-safe” and “mixable.” Ruby thought, “When I am not sure, I ask.” She hopped to the stairs. “Mom? Is it okay to mix the blue and green paint?”

Mom came down, her feet soft on the steps. “Let’s read the label together,” she said. They read the words slowly. “Mixable. Non-toxic.” Mom nodded. “You checked. You asked. That is safe and smart.”

Ruby smiled. “Thanks, Mom!” She mixed one tiny drop of blue into her green bowl. The water turned sea-color. She stirred slowly. Swish. She smelled again. Still just soap.

She dipped the wand and blew. The bubble shimmered green-blue, like a little ocean world. “Wow,” Ruby said. “It looks like a tiny planet.”

She set a small timer. Beep, beep. “Work time, then break time,” she said. “That keeps me focused.”

On her break, Ruby sipped cool water and ate a crunchy carrot. Crunch, crunch. She rested her eyes. The vent hummed. She felt calm and proud.

Back to work, she tried a new idea. She used a paper cone to make bubble prints on dark paper. Plop, lift, plop. Round circles shone softly. “Art bubbles!” Ruby laughed. “I am creative and safe.”

Suddenly, her glove caught the edge of the bowl. It tipped. Glowy water splashed on the mat. Splish! Ruby’s ears jumped. “Oh no.”

She took a breath. “Stay calm. Follow the plan.” She turned off her timer. She slid the bowl upright. She held it steady. “Mom! I spilled! I am okay!”

Mom’s voice floated down. “Are you hurt?”

“No. Goggles on. Gloves on. No sharp things. Just a spill.”

“Great job telling me,” Mom said. “What is your next step?”

“Clean up,” Ruby said. She grabbed the towel and pressed it down. Squish. She wiped the table, too. She put the wet towel in a bin. She washed her gloves in the sink. Sssshh, water ran. She dried them with a cloth. Pat, pat.

Mom came to check. The floor was dry. The bowls were safe. “Ruby, you stayed calm. You followed safety steps. That is independence with care.”

Ruby’s whiskers twitched with pride. “I met my goal,” she said. “Glow bubbles, glow art, and safe clean-up.”

Mom hugged her gently. Ruby felt warm and loved. “What did you learn today?” Mom asked.

Ruby thought. “Set a goal. Make a plan. Wear safety gear. Ask when I’m not sure. Clean as I go. Take breaks. Be calm if there’s a spill.”

Mom smiled. “Wonderful.”

Ruby looked at her shining bubble art. “I can be creative and safe,” she said. “I can try new things and take care.”

She turned off the vent. Click. She took off her goggles and gloves. Click, snap. She washed her hands with warm water. It felt silky and smelled like lemons. She dried them soft and warm on a towel.

Ruby hopped up the stairs, thump, thump. The lab was clean. Her heart felt light. She had done it all by herself, with smart choices and a little help when needed.

“Tomorrow,” Ruby said, “I will plan a new project. Safety first, fun always!”

The End.
