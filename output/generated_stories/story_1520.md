# Grace the Giraffe's Big Mistake

Grace was a very tall, thoughtful giraffe who loved helping her animal friends. Every Sunday, all the forest animals gathered in the big dining room at the Community Center for their special friendship dinner.

"I want to help set the table today!" Grace said excitedly, her long neck swaying as she walked into the warm, cozy dining room. The wooden tables smelled like fresh polish, and colorful flowers sat in pretty vases.

Mrs. Rabbit was already there, carefully placing shiny forks and knives. "That would be wonderful, Grace! Could you please put the tall water glasses on each table?"

Grace felt so proud to have such an important job. She stretched her long neck high to reach the glass cabinet. "I can do this perfectly!" she thought.

But as Grace carried the first tray of sparkling glasses, her long legs got tangled up. CRASH! The glasses fell to the floor with a loud, tinkling sound. Water splashed everywhere, and broken glass scattered across the smooth wooden floor.

"Oh no!" Grace cried, her brown eyes filling with tears. "I ruined everything!"

Little Bunny hopped over, his white fur now spotted with water. "My favorite bow tie is all wet!" he said sadly.

Mrs. Rabbit's whiskers twitched with worry. "Grace, you need to be more careful! Now we don't have enough glasses for everyone."

Grace's long neck drooped low. She felt terrible. Her stomach felt heavy, like she had swallowed a big rock. "I'm so sorry," she whispered. "I just wanted to help."

The other animals started to arrive. Squirrel chattered angrily when he saw the mess. "Who did this? Now our dinner is ruined!"

Grace wanted to hide behind the big wooden chairs, but she knew that wasn't right. Taking a deep breath, she stood up tall.

"I did it," Grace said in a shaky voice. "I was trying to help, but I wasn't careful enough. I broke the glasses and got everyone wet. I'm really, really sorry."

The dining room became very quiet. Grace could hear her heart beating fast like a drum.

Then Mrs. Rabbit's face softened. "Grace, thank you for telling the truth. That was very brave and responsible."

Little Bunny looked up at Grace with his big, kind eyes. "I was mad about my bow tie, but I know you didn't mean to do it. I forgive you, Grace."

One by one, the other animals nodded. "We forgive you too," they said.

Grace felt the heavy feeling in her stomach start to go away. "How can I make it better?" she asked.

"Let's work together!" said Mrs. Rabbit. "Grace, you can use your height to get the paper cups from the high shelf. Bunny, you can dry the floor. Squirrel, you can sweep up the glass pieces safely."

Everyone worked as a team. Grace was extra careful this time, moving slowly and watching her feet. Soon the dining room looked perfect again, filled with the happy sounds of friends talking and laughing.

As they sat down to eat their delicious vegetable soup, Mrs. Rabbit smiled. "Grace, mistakes happen to everyone. What matters most is that you took responsibility and that we all forgave each other."

Grace's heart felt warm and light. "Thank you for forgiving me," she said. "Next time, I'll ask for help when I need it."

From that day on, Grace remembered that everyone makes mistakes, and forgiveness makes everything better.

The End.
