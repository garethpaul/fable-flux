# Ruby's Big Concert Day

The morning sun warmed the peaceful front porch where Ruby Robin lived in her cozy nest. She fluffed her bright red feathers and opened her tiny beak. But instead of her usual sweet song, only a worried chirp came out.

"Oh no!" Ruby whispered to herself. "Today is the Big Spring Concert, and I'm not ready!"

All the birds in the neighborhood were supposed to sing together. Ruby had been so busy playing and chasing butterflies that she forgot to practice her special song. Her little heart beat fast like a tiny drum.

Mrs. Sparrow landed on the porch railing with a gentle *tap-tap* of her claws. "Good morning, Ruby! Are you excited for the concert?"

Ruby's head drooped low. "I'm scared, Mrs. Sparrow. I didn't practice my song. What if I mess up in front of everyone?"

Mrs. Sparrow's wise eyes twinkled. "Oh my! That does sound worrying. But you know what? We still have time before the concert starts. Would you like some help getting ready?"

Ruby's bright eyes looked up hopefully. "Really? You would help me?"

"Of course, dear! Preparation is like building a strong nest - we do it step by step," Mrs. Sparrow said kindly. "First, let's practice your song together."

Ruby took a deep breath. The sweet smell of morning flowers filled her tiny lungs. She began to sing: "Spring is here, spring is here, flowers bloom and skies are clear!"

"Beautiful!" chirped Mrs. Sparrow. "Now let's practice it three more times so your voice feels strong and confident."

As they practiced, more bird friends arrived. Blue Jay brought some fresh berries to keep Ruby's voice sweet. Cardinal showed her how to stand tall and proud. Even tiny Wren helped by keeping the beat with her wings.

"I feel much better now," Ruby said, her voice growing stronger with each song. "But what if I still get nervous during the concert?"

Mrs. Sparrow smiled warmly. "It's okay to feel nervous sometimes. That just means something is important to you. When you feel worried, just remember all the practice we did together."

Soon, birds from all around the neighborhood gathered on the front porch. The wooden boards creaked softly under their tiny feet. Ruby looked at all her friends who had helped her prepare.

"I'm ready now," Ruby announced, her red chest puffed out with pride.

When it was Ruby's turn to sing, she remembered Mrs. Sparrow's words. She took a deep breath, smelled the fresh spring air, and began her song. Her voice rang out clear and beautiful: "Spring is here, spring is here, flowers bloom and skies are clear!"

All the birds joined in, creating the most wonderful harmony. Ruby felt warm and happy inside, like sunshine filling her whole body.

After the concert, Ruby hugged Mrs. Sparrow with her wing. "Thank you for helping me prepare! I learned that when I practice and ask for help, I can do anything!"

Mrs. Sparrow nodded proudly. "That's right, Ruby. Preparation and working together make everything better. You were very brave to ask for help when you needed it."

As the sun set over the peaceful porch, Ruby sang one more happy song. She had learned that preparing ahead of time and cooperating with friends made even scary things feel possible.

The End.
