# Dippy the Dolphin’s Colorful Game Plan

Dippy was a playful dolphin who loved to splash paint with her fins. Today, Dippy swam into the creative art room, where walls looked like rainbows and the floor felt soft like a sponge. The room smelled like fresh paper and sweet crayons. There were brushes, glitter, clay, and big, bright posters of kids playing sports.

“Hello, colors!” Dippy chirped, flipping her tail. “I’m ready to make art!”

Coach Canvas, a friendly teacher with paint on his apron, waved. “Hi, Dippy! Today we have a special plan. We’ll make art about our favorite sports. Then we’ll have a tiny, gentle game. We must use our time well. First art, then game, then clean-up.”

Dippy clapped her fins. “Fun! I can do it all!”

Her friends were already there. Mei wore a purple ribbon in her hair and loved badminton. Jamal had bright green sneakers and loved soccer. Lila used a shiny hearing aid that sparkled like a star and loved yoga. Mateo rolled in with a sporty wheelchair and loved basketball.

Dippy smiled at everyone. “We are all different. We like different sports. We make different art. That is beautiful!”

Mei giggled. “Let’s show our favorites!”

Coach Canvas drew a big clock on the board. “We have three parts. Ten minutes for planning our art. Ten minutes for making it. Ten minutes for a gentle game. When the bell rings—ding!—we change to the next part.”

Dippy nodded fast. “Easy peasy!”

The bell went ding!

Dippy planned quickly. “I will paint dolphin water polo! Splashy fun!”

But the paint pots looked so shiny. The glitter jar winked. The clay felt cool and squishy. Dippy dipped a fin in blue. “Ooh!” She swirled. “Ahh!” She tapped glitter. “Wow!” She poked clay. “Boing!”

Mei whispered, “Dippy, plan first.”

“I am planning,” Dippy said. “I plan to try everything!” She giggled and made tiny blue dots.

Ding!

“Time to make!” Coach Canvas smiled.

Jamal started cutting green paper for a soccer field. Lila stretched and drew calm poses. Mateo glued orange circles like basketballs. Mei painted a racket and a birdie with soft wings.

Dippy tried to do it all. She dripped paint, sprinkled glitter, rolled clay, and blew bubbles in a water cup. “I love it! I love it all!”

But the page got messy. The colors mixed to brown. The glitter clumped. The clay stuck to her fin. Dippy’s smile faded. “Oh no. My art looks muddled.”

Lila came close and spoke gently. “It’s okay to try. Maybe choose one idea.”

Mei nodded. “I like your blue swirls.”

Mateo grinned. “Your splashes look like fast water for water polo.”

Jamal held up his soccer field. “Different sports, different styles. That’s cool. You can make yours simple and strong.”

Dippy took a deep breath. She listened to the soft hum of the lights and the quiet scratch of brushes. She felt her heart go thump-thump. “I will pick one. I will make blue waves and a bright ball. That is my plan.”

She wiped her fin. She chose blue and white and a little yellow. Swish-swish went her fin. Plip-plop went the paint. Dippy made a big, happy wave and a round, sunny ball. She added a tiny net with thin lines.

Ding!

“Art time is done,” said Coach Canvas. “Now a gentle game!”

The tiny game was friendly and safe. Each person shared their sport in a small way. Jamal tapped a soft foam ball into a box goal. “Goal!” he cheered. Lila showed a slow, calm stretch. “Breathe in, breathe out,” she said. Mei bounced a birdie with her hand like a scoop. “Tap-tap!” Mateo rolled and shot a soft ball into a hoop on the wall. “Swoosh!”

Dippy pushed a light ball across a little tub of water with her nose. “Boop!” She laughed. “Water polo is splashy!”

They clapped for each other. The room sounded happy—clap, clap, clap!

Ding!

“Clean-up time,” said Coach Canvas. “We can do it together.”

Dippy wanted to play more, but she remembered the plan. “First clean, then more fun later,” she told herself. She put brushes in a cup, wiped the table, and closed the glitter jar. Everyone helped, each in their own way. The room smelled like soap and oranges.

Coach Canvas gathered them. “Look at all the art! Each one is different. Each one is special.”

Dippy looked around. Mei’s birdie had soft feathers. Jamal’s field was bright and green. Lila’s yoga shapes were calm and kind. Mateo’s orange circles bounced off the page. Dippy’s wave was bold and blue.

“We are all different,” Dippy said. “We like different sports and make different art. That makes our room colorful!”

“And we used our time,” Mei added. “Plan, make, game, clean.”

Jamal smiled. “I feel proud.”

Lila nodded. “I feel peaceful.”

Mateo beamed. “I feel strong.”

Dippy felt warm inside. “I feel proud, peaceful, and strong too. I can choose one thing, do my best, and cheer for others.”

Coach Canvas gave a thumbs-up. “That’s the lesson. Our differences make us shine. When we use time wisely, we can do what we love and help our friends.”

Dippy hugged her painting. “I love our different selves.” She tapped the clock gently. “And I love our plan.”

They all said together, “Different is beautiful. Time helps us try. Sports make us move. We believe in ourselves!”

The End.
