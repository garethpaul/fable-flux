# Ollie the Otter’s Rooftop Rainbow

Ollie the otter liked to wiggle, giggle, and splash. One sunny morning, Ollie climbed the stairs all the way up to a wide, flat rooftop. The roof felt warm under his paws. He could smell fresh bread from the bakery below. He could hear buses rumble and birds tweet-tweet in the bright blue sky.

Ollie looked around. “Wow!” he said. “So high! So sky!” He waved to a small cloud drifting by.

On the roof sat some friendly neighbors: Mina the mouse, Pip the pigeon, and Gus the gentle old tortoise. They all liked to visit the rooftop garden. Little pots held mint and tiny tomatoes. A soft breeze made the leaves shiver-shiver.

“Hi, friends!” Ollie said, doing a happy wiggle. “Let’s make a big rainbow banner for the neighborhood! We can hang it on the fence so everyone below can see it and smile.”

Mina’s whiskers twitched. “A rainbow banner?” she squeaked. “That sounds big.”

Pip puffed his chest. “I can fly high to help!”

Gus smiled slowly. “A good goal,” he said. “A rainbow to share is a kind idea.”

Ollie clapped his paws. “Our goal is to make a bright rainbow banner before lunchtime. We will work as a team. Teamwork means we help each other.”

They looked at the supplies. There was a long white sheet, some colorful paints, a roll of tape, and string. But the paints were in tall bottles with tight caps.

Ollie tugged at a cap. “Hmm. So tight!” He grunted. “I can’t open it by myself.” He felt a little frown.

Mina scurried up. “Let me try!” She wrapped her tiny paws around the cap and squeaked, “Twist, twist!” But it didn’t move.

Gus came closer. “We can do it together. Ollie holds. Mina twists. I steady the bottle.” His voice was calm, like a slow drum.

They all counted. “One, two, three!” Ollie held the bottle. Gus kept it still. Mina twisted with all her might. Pop! The cap came off. They laughed with surprise.

“Teamwork!” Ollie cheered. “We did it together.”

They opened all the colors and set the sheet on the warm roof. Pip dipped a feather into red paint. “I’ll make the red stripe,” he cooed.

Ollie dipped his paws in orange paint. It felt cool and slippery. “Ooo! Squishy! Squelch!” He made handprints across the sheet.

Mina used a tiny brush for yellow. “Swish, swish,” she said, her nose wiggling.

Gus used a sponge for green. “Press, lift. Press, lift,” he said, keeping the line neat.

The breeze smelled like mint from the little pots. The paint smelled sharp and tickly. They could feel the sun on their backs. The colors looked bright and happy.

“Next is blue,” said Ollie, “and then purple!”

Pip fluttered, but his tail brushed the edge of the paint tray. Splat! A big blue blob fell on the roof. Pip’s eyes grew wide. “Oh no,” he cooed. “I made a mess.”

Ollie looked at Pip. Pip looked sad and small. Ollie took a deep breath. “It’s okay,” he said softly. “We all make mistakes. We can help. That’s compassion.”

Mina ran to get a cloth. Gus brought water in a little cup. Ollie dabbed gently. “Dab, dab, dab,” he said. “We clean together.”

Pip’s feathers relaxed. “Thank you,” he whispered. “I feel better.”

“Now,” said Gus, “back to our goal. We can try again with care.”

They lined up. Pip painted the blue stripe with slow, careful strokes. Ollie added purple with a wide, wavy brush. “Wiggle-waggle,” he laughed.

At last, the rainbow was bright and full. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple shone under the sun. But the wind picked up. Whoosh! The sheet flapped like a big, soft flag.

“We need to hang it strong,” said Mina. “The wind is big.”

Ollie nodded. “Let’s make a plan.” He drew a quick picture with his paw in a dusty spot: four corners, four friends. “Pip holds the top and ties the string. Mina tapes the sides. I hold the bottom. Gus checks the knots.”

“Good plan,” said Gus.

They moved into place. Pip flapped up and tied one corner, then the other. Mina pressed tape, smoothing it flat. Ollie held the bottom steady. Gus tested every knot with a firm, gentle tug.

The wind blew again. Whoosh! The banner stayed. It rippled like a rainbow river in the sky.

Down on the street, people looked up. “Ooo!” “Ahh!” They smiled and waved. A child pointed and clapped. The bakery lady held up a loaf and called, “Beautiful!”

Ollie’s heart felt warm and full. “We did it!” he said. “Our teamwork made our goal come true.”

Pip puffed with pride. “And when I made a mess, you helped me.”

Mina nodded. “That is kind. That is compassion.”

Gus smiled, his eyes crinkling. “With a clear goal, kind hearts, and teamwork, we can do big things.”

They sat together on the warm rooftop, eating little mint leaves and tiny tomatoes from the garden. The rainbow banner waved and waved, making colors dance on the walls below. The breeze sang soft, and the city hummed.

Ollie leaned back and sighed, happy. “Today we learned something bright,” he said. “Teamwork helps us reach our goals. Being kind makes our team strong.”

Everyone nodded. They felt proud, calm, and close.

The End.
