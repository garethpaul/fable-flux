# Joey's Big Car Adventure

Little Joey the kangaroo was bouncing excitedly in his car seat. Today was special - his family was driving to visit Grandma Roo for her birthday! The sun was shining bright through the car windows, making everything feel warm and happy.

"Are we there yet? Are we there yet?" Joey asked, his little paws tapping on his seat. He could smell Mama's fresh eucalyptus cookies in the basket beside him, and they smelled so yummy!

"Not yet, sweetie," said Mama Roo, looking back with kind eyes. "We still have a long way to go. Remember what we talked about? This is a good time to practice being patient."

Joey's ears drooped a little. Being patient was hard! He wanted to see Grandma Roo RIGHT NOW. His body felt wiggly, and his heart was beating fast with excitement.

Papa Roo smiled in the rearview mirror. "Joey, would you like to hear about the special tradition we're bringing to Grandma today?"

"Yes, yes!" Joey bounced, but then he remembered Mama's words about being patient. He took a deep breath like she had taught him. "I mean... yes, please, Papa."

"Good job using your calm voice," Papa said proudly. "We're bringing Grandma the same eucalyptus cookies that her grandma used to make for her when she was little, just like you!"

Joey's eyes grew wide. "Grandma was little like me?"

"Oh yes," Mama laughed, her voice soft and musical. "And she used to bounce in car seats too, waiting to visit HER grandma."

As they drove past green fields and blue lakes, Joey saw a family of wallabies having a picnic. One little wallaby was crying while his big sister hugged him gently.

"Why is that little wallaby sad?" Joey asked, pressing his nose to the cool window.

"Maybe he's feeling frustrated about something," Mama suggested. "How do you think his sister is helping him?"

Joey watched carefully. "She's giving him a hug! And... and she's talking to him nicely, not being mean."

"That's right," Papa nodded. "She's showing empathy - that means she cares about how he feels."

Joey thought about this. Sometimes when he felt upset, hugs helped too. "If I was there, I would share my cookies with him to make him feel better."

"That's very kind, Joey," Mama smiled. "You have such a caring heart."

The car ride continued, and Joey started feeling wiggly again. His legs wanted to kick, and his voice wanted to be loud. But then he remembered the little wallaby and how the big sister stayed calm to help.

"Mama," Joey said quietly, "my body feels jumpy. Can you help me?"

"Of course, sweetheart. Let's count to ten together and take deep breaths." Mama began counting slowly, and Joey breathed in the sweet smell of the cookies with each number.

"One... two... three..." By the time they reached ten, Joey felt much calmer.

"Look!" Papa called out. "There's Grandma's house!"

Joey could see the cozy burrow with colorful flowers all around. Grandma Roo was waving from her front porch, her gray fur shining in the sunlight.

"I did it!" Joey said proudly. "I was patient the whole way!"

"You sure were," Mama said, unbuckling his seat belt. "And now we get to share our family tradition with Grandma and show her how much we love her."

Joey hopped out carefully, holding the cookie basket. He had learned that good things come to those who wait, and that helping others feel better makes you feel good too.

The End.
