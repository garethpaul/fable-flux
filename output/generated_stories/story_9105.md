# Ollie Otter’s Wiggle Pancake Dance

The warm kitchen smelled like cinnamon and butter. Sunlight made the floor look golden. Pitter-pat, pitter-pat went little wet feet. Ollie the playful otter slid in on his tummy and giggled.

“Good morning, Mama!” Ollie squeaked. “What are we making?”

Mama Otter stood by the stove. She wore a bright red apron. Sizzle, sizzle went the pan. “We are making pancake dots,” Mama said. “Tiny circles for tiny bites.”

Ollie clapped. “Can I help? I want to pour! I want to flip! I want to dance!”

Mama smiled. “You can help, Ollie. But we have to follow directions. Step by step.”

Ollie bounced on his toes. He wiggled his whiskers. “I can follow! I can!”

Mama set a big bowl on the table. Tap, tap went the spoon. “First, we wash our paws.”

Ollie ran to the sink. Splash, splash. He rubbed soap between his fingers. The water felt warm and tickly. “All clean!” he sang.

“Great job,” said Mama. “Next, we mix the batter. I will read the steps. You can do the actions.”

Ollie stood tall on a stool. “I am ready.”

Mama looked at a card with pictures. “Step one: pour the milk.”

Ollie held the little cup tight. “Slow and gentle,” he whispered. Drip, drip, drip. Into the bowl it went.

“Step two: add flour.”

“Like snow!” Ollie said. He scooped soft white powder. Puff! A tiny cloud floated up. Ollie sneezed. “Ah-choo!” He giggled.

Mama laughed. “Bless you, little whiskers. Step three: crack the egg.”

Ollie took a breath. Crack! He opened the egg slow. Plop went the yolk. “I did it!” he cheered.

“Step four: stir, stir, stir.”

Ollie moved the spoon around and around. Swish, swish. The batter got smooth and creamy. His arms felt tired. “It’s hard,” he huffed.

“You can do hard things,” Mama said gently. “Take a slow breath in. Blow a slow breath out.”

Ollie breathed in. He blew out like a soft wind. “I feel better,” he said. He stirred three more times. “All done!”

Mama winked. “Now we cook. Watch first, then try.”

Ollie watched Mama pour tiny circles on the hot pan. Sizzle, sizzle. He sniffed. “Mmm. It smells sweet.”

Mama said, “When we see little bubbles pop, it’s time to flip.”

Ollie nodded. Bubble, pop. Bubble, pop. “Now?” he asked.

“Now,” Mama said.

Ollie slid the spatula under a pancake dot. He flipped. Flop! It landed a little squished. “Oh no,” he said. His whiskers drooped. “I messed up.”

Mama touched his paw. “It’s okay to feel upset. Let’s try our calm steps.”

Ollie’s eyes were shiny. “What are the calm steps?”

“Step one: pause,” Mama said. They both froze like statues.

“Step two: take a belly breath.” They put paws on tummies. In... out...

“Step three: try again, slow and gentle.”

Ollie sniffled and nodded. He tried again. Slide, lift, flip. Flop! This time the pancake dot was round.

“I did it!” Ollie grinned. “I followed the steps!”

Mama clapped. “Yes! Following directions helps our bodies and our feelings.”

Ollie’s tail swished. He had an idea. “Mama, can we make a pancake dance? A dance with steps to follow?”

Mama smiled big. “What a fun idea. Let’s make a Wiggle Pancake Dance.”

They wiped the table. The warm kitchen hummed. The sun made little light spots on the floor. Ollie and Mama stood side by side.

Mama said, “I will say the steps. You follow.”

“Okay!” said Ollie.

Mama called, “Step one: wiggle whiskers!”

Ollie wiggled his whiskers fast. “Tee-hee!”

“Step two: pancake flip.” They both made gentle flipping moves with their paws.

“Step three: bubble pop.” They hopped, pop-pop!

“Step four: belly breath.” In... out...

Ollie laughed. “Again, again!”

They did the Wiggle Pancake Dance two times, then three times, then four. Each time, Ollie listened and followed the order. He felt proud and steady.

Ding! The timer beeped. The pancake dots were ready. Mama put them on a warm plate. She drizzled a little maple syrup. The syrup smelled like trees and sunshine.

Ollie took a bite. Soft, warm, sweet. “Yummy,” he said with a happy sigh. “The dance made my body calm. The steps helped me flip. Following directions helps everything!”

Mama hugged him. “Yes, my clever otter. When we listen, watch, and follow steps, we learn. And when we feel frustrated, we pause, breathe, and try again.”

Ollie nodded. He licked a bit of syrup from his paw. “Tomorrow, can we follow directions to make fruit faces?”

Mama chuckled. “Yes. And we can make a new dance, too.”

Ollie looked at the shiny pan, the sunny floor, and the little pancake dots. His heart felt warm like the kitchen. “I’m ready to follow,” he said. “Step by step.”

The End.
