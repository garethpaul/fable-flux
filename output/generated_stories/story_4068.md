# Tiko’s Treehouse Tea Party

Tiko was a shy turtle with a smooth green shell. He lived by a big tree with a cozy treehouse nestled high in the branches. The treehouse smelled like warm wood and sweet fruit. Wind chimes tinkled softly. Birds chirped, “Tweet, tweet!” and leaves whispered, “Shhh.”

One sunny morning, Tiko looked up at the door sign. It said, “Welcome, friends!” Tiko took a deep breath. “I will host a tea party,” he said quietly. “But… how?” His voice was small, like a tiny bell.

Tiko’s friends were from many places. Laila the lamb loved mint tea. Bao the bunny liked jasmine tea. Kofi the kitten liked spiced tea with cinnamon. Mia the mouse liked sweet fruit tea. Tiko wanted everyone to feel happy and seen. He wanted to show care for their favorite tastes from home.

Tiko wiggled his toes. “I feel shy,” he whispered. “But I can think step by step. I can make a plan.”

He pulled out a big paper and a red crayon. “Step one,” he said, drawing a square. “Ask what each friend likes.” He walked to the window and called softly, “Laila, Bao, Kofi, Mia—what tea do you like today?”

Laila bleated, “Mint, please!” The leaves smelled fresh when Tiko rubbed them. Bao bounced and said, “Jasmine!” The tiny white flowers were soft like snow. Kofi purred, “Spiced tea!” and the cinnamon stick smelled warm and cozy. Mia squeaked, “Fruit tea!” and the strawberries looked shiny and red.

“Step two,” Tiko said, making another square. “Count cups.” He counted slowly, tapping each cup with his gentle turtle claw. “One, two, three, four… and one for me makes five.” He smiled a small smile. His belly felt less fluttery.

“Step three,” he said. “Make a safe, friendly space.” Tiko swept the floor. Swish, swish! He brushed crumbs away. He set soft cushions in a circle. He opened the window a little, and a cool breeze smelled like apples and pine.

“Step four,” Tiko said. “Match tea to friend.” He set mint by a green cup for Laila. He put jasmine by a white cup for Bao. He placed spiced tea by a brown cup for Kofi. He set fruit tea by a red cup for Mia. He put warm water in the kettle. It hummed, “Mmmmmm,” then bubbled, “Blub, blub!”

Knock, knock! His friends climbed the ladder to the treehouse. Tiko’s cheeks felt warm. “Hello,” he said softly. “I made a plan. Please come in.”

Laila sniffed the air. “It smells like fresh leaves!” Bao’s ears twitched. “I hear chimes!” Kofi’s tail curled. “I smell cinnamon.” Mia clapped. “Look at the bright cups!”

But then—oh no! The red crayon rolled and marked the paper where Tiko had drawn the steps. The boxes looked messy. Tiko’s heart went thump-thump. “What if I mix the cups?” he worried. “What if I forget?”

He took a slow breath. “I can think. I can choose. I can ask.” He remembered his plan.

Tiko said, “Let’s check.” He spoke kindly and clearly. “Laila, does mint go with green?” Laila smiled. “Yes, green like mint leaves!” Tiko nodded and put the green cup by her.

“Bao,” Tiko asked, “does jasmine go with white?” Bao sniffed the jasmine and said, “Yes, white like the flowers.” Tiko set the white cup by Bao.

“Kofi,” Tiko asked, “does spiced tea go with brown?” Kofi purred, “Yes, like cinnamon sticks.” Tiko placed the brown cup by Kofi.

“Mia,” Tiko asked, “does fruit tea go with red?” Mia giggled. “Yes, like strawberries!” Tiko set the red cup by Mia.

“See?” Tiko told himself. “I used clues. I asked. I checked.” His shy feeling softened, like a blanket.

They poured hot water. Steam curled up like tiny clouds. Everyone waited a moment. The room smelled like gardens and kitchens around the world.

Laila sipped. “Mint tea is cool and bright.” Bao breathed in. “Jasmine tea is soft and sweet.” Kofi took a taste. “Spiced tea is warm and brave.” Mia smiled. “Fruit tea is juicy and fun.”

Tiko tried each scent and listened to their happy sounds. “Your teas tell stories,” he said. “They tell where you come from and what you love.”

Bao nodded. “In my home, we share jasmine tea to show care.” Laila said, “We drink mint with family.” Kofi added, “Spiced tea is for big celebrations.” Mia said, “Fruit tea is for picnics!”

Tiko’s shell felt lighter. “Thank you for sharing,” he said. “I learned about each of you.”

Then Mia pointed. “Oh! We have five cups but only four teas.” Tiko blinked. “My cup!” He smiled. “Step five,” he said, drawing one more square. “Choose one to try.” He thought. “I want to learn more, so I will try a little of each.”

He poured tiny sips into his cup—mint, jasmine, spice, and fruit. He tasted slowly. “Cool, then soft, then warm, then sweet,” he said. “Together, they make a new taste. It feels like friends.”

Everyone cheered. The wind chimes sang, and the leaves clapped.

Tiko said, “When I felt shy, I made a plan. I asked questions. I used clues. I checked my steps. That helped me choose.”

Laila bleated, “That is smart thinking!” Bao said, “That is good deciding!” Kofi purred, “That is brave.” Mia squeaked, “That is kind.”

They ate little cookies that crunched. Crick, crack! They told funny stories. They shared words from home. Tiko learned to say hello in four ways. He practiced softly and smiled big.

When the sun began to set, the treehouse glowed golden. Tiko walked his friends to the ladder. “Thank you,” he said. “Our different teas made the party special.”

The friends waved. “Goodbye!” “Zaijian!” “Kwaheri!” “Adiós!” The air filled with happy sounds.

Tiko looked at his plan paper. It had boxes and crayon marks. He patted it gently. “Logical thinking helps me,” he said. “I can make steps. I can ask. I can decide. I can celebrate our different ways.”

He turned the sign on the door. It said, “Come again.” Tiko’s shy heart felt brave and warm.

The End.
