# Penny Panda's Dancing Garden

Penny Panda loved to dance. Every morning, she would wiggle her fluffy black and white body and spin around her cozy bedroom. But today was different. Today, the warm sunshine was streaming through her window, and the birds were singing their happy songs outside.

"What a beautiful day!" said Penny, looking out at the sunny backyard. "I think I'll practice my dancing outside today!"

Penny waddled out to the green grass. The sun felt warm on her soft fur, and a gentle breeze tickled her nose. She could smell the sweet flowers and hear the buzzing of busy bees.

"This is perfect!" Penny said with a big smile. She started to dance, spinning and twirling across the yard. But as she spun faster and faster, her big panda feet began to stomp hard on the ground.

THUMP! THUMP! THUMP!

"Oh no!" cried a tiny voice.

Penny stopped dancing and looked around. "Who said that?"

A little earthworm poked his head up from the soil. "It's me, Wiggly! Your dancing is shaking my underground home. My family and I can't rest!"

Penny's heart felt sad. She didn't want to hurt anyone. "I'm so sorry, Wiggly. I just love dancing outside in the sunshine. But I don't want to bother you and your family."

Penny sat down on the soft grass and thought very hard. The warm sun made her feel sleepy, but she wanted to find a way to dance AND keep her new friend happy.

"I have an idea!" said Penny, clapping her paws together. "What if I practice my gentle dances? I can move softly like the butterflies!"

Wiggly wiggled up a little higher. "That sounds nice, but what about your big spinning dances?"

Penny looked around the sunny backyard. She saw the wooden deck near the house. "I could do my big dances on the deck! The wood won't shake your home underground."

"That's a wonderful idea!" said Wiggly. "And maybe I can watch you dance. I've never seen a panda dance before!"

Penny felt excited again. She carefully walked to the deck, feeling the warm wooden boards under her feet. She began to dance, spinning and jumping on the deck. The wood felt smooth and didn't shake the ground at all.

Then she came back to the grass and practiced her soft, gentle moves. She swayed like the tall grass in the breeze and stepped lightly like raindrops falling on leaves.

"This is beautiful!" said Wiggly. "You dance like nature itself!"

More friends came to watch. A red robin landed on the fence, and a fluffy squirrel climbed down from the oak tree. Even some colorful butterflies fluttered nearby.

"Dancing outside is even more fun with friends!" laughed Penny.

As the afternoon sun began to set, Penny felt happy and tired. She had learned that dancing outside was wonderful, but caring for her garden friends was important too.

"Thank you for teaching me to be gentle with your home, Wiggly," said Penny.

"And thank you for sharing your beautiful dancing with us!" replied Wiggly.

From that day on, Penny danced outside every sunny day. She did her big, energetic dances on the deck and her soft, gentle dances on the grass. The backyard became full of friends who loved to watch her dance in harmony with nature.

The End.
