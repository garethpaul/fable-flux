# Rico Raccoon’s Dinnertime Drum

Rico was a small, musical raccoon with bright eyes and gentle paws. He loved sounds. He loved tap-tap-tapping on bowls, clink-clink-clinking on spoons, and hum-hum-humming little songs. Today, Rico stood in the dining room. The table was long and shiny. The sun made warm squares on the floor. Rico sniffed. He smelled sweet apples, warm bread, and bubbly soup. His whiskers twitched with joy.

“I will play a song for dinner,” Rico said. “A kind song. A steady song. A song for everyone.”

On the table, there were cups from many places. A blue cup with stars from the night sky. A red cup with a tiny dragon from a faraway land. A green cup with a leaf from a rainforest. A yellow cup with a sun from a sandy beach. Rico smiled. “People everywhere eat together,” he said softly. “Around the world, we share meals. We share music, too.”

Rico lifted two wooden spoons. Tap-tap. Tap-tap. He tried a simple beat. But the spoons slipped. Clatter! The spoons fell. A napkin fluttered to the floor like a quiet bird.

Rico’s ears drooped. “Oh no,” he whispered.

He took a deep breath. “Try again,” he told himself. “Be patient. Be steady.” He picked up the spoons. Tap-tap. Tap-tap-tap. This time, the beat was too fast. Clink! One spoon bumped a glass. The glass wobbled and wiggled.

“I’m sorry,” Rico said quickly, patting the glass with a soft paw. “I did not mean to scare you.” Rico looked at the glass. He looked at the napkin. “I can fix it.” He lifted the napkin and folded it slowly. Fold, press, smooth. He set it back on the table. He smiled a little. “I can make mistakes and make them better.”

Rico tried again. He sat on a sturdy chair with his feet flat. He counted in a whisper, “One, two, slow and true.” Tap… tap… tap… The sound was gentle, like rain. The wooden spoons made a warm, woody click. The bowls sang a low, round tone. The forks made tiny stars of sound, tinkle-tink.

Rico closed his eyes. He imagined children in many homes around the world. He pictured a family in a snowy place, eating hot soup and humming. He pictured a family in a sunny place, sharing rice and smiling. He pictured a family by the sea, tasting fish and clapping their hands. “We are different,” he said, “and we are the same. We all eat. We all listen. We all learn.”

He opened his eyes and tried a new pattern. Tap on the bowl. Tap on the table edge. Tap on the spoon. Tap-tap, pause. Tap-tap, pause. But then—thump! His tail bumped a chair leg. The chair scooted. A fork slid off the table and pinged on the floor.

Rico’s heart went thump-thump. He felt hot in his cheeks. “I’m sorry,” he said to the fork. He picked it up carefully. “I will be gentle.” He placed the fork back and straightened the placemat. Smooth, smooth. He took another deep breath. In. Out. In. Out. He pressed his paw to his chest. “I can keep going,” he whispered. “I can be calm. I can be kind. I can try again.”

Rico thought of completing his song, like finishing a puzzle. He did not want to stop in the middle. He wanted to be independent and responsible. “I can do this,” he told himself.

He made a plan:
- Sit still.
- Hold spoons low.
- Count slow.
- Stop if something wobbles.
- Say sorry if needed.
- Try again.

Rico began. Tap… tap… tap… tap… The sound moved like footsteps. He added a soft hum. Mmm-mmm-mmm. He tapped the blue star cup—ding! He tapped the red dragon cup—dong! He tapped the green leaf cup—ding! He tapped the yellow sun cup—dong! The cups made a gentle, happy pattern.

He whispered, “This beat is for friends in cold places. This beat is for friends in hot places. This beat is for friends who eat rice, bread, soup, and noodles. We all can listen. We all can share.”

The dining room filled with warm sound. The table seemed to smile. The sunlight felt cozy on Rico’s fur. The smells of apples and soup floated around him like hugs. Rico finished with a soft roll on the wooden spoons: trr-rr-rr-rrp! Then a tiny tap. Tap!

Rico set the spoons down. He looked around. Nothing had fallen. Nothing wobbled. His tail sat still, like a fluffy comma. He had completed his song.

Rico stood tall. “I did it,” he said. His voice was calm and proud. “I kept trying. I was careful. I said sorry. I finished my task.”

The door opened, and friends peeked in. “We heard your music,” said Mia. “It sounded like faraway places and home at the same time.”

Rico smiled. “Thank you,” he said. “I wanted a song for everyone.”

Mia clapped softly. “Dinner time!” she said. “Will you play again tomorrow?”

Rico nodded. “Yes,” he said. “I will practice. I will be patient. I will keep going, even when it is hard.”

They sat at the table. Rico passed the bread. He used gentle paws. “Please,” he said. “Thank you.” He tasted the soup. Slurp. “Mmm,” he smiled. “Warm and kind.”

Mia tapped her cup and whispered, “From here to there, we all care.”

Rico’s eyes shone. “Together,” he said. “All around the world.”

They ate, they talked, and they listened to the quiet, happy beat of being kind and trying again.

The lesson was clear: Keep trying, be gentle, say sorry, and finish what you start. With patience and practice, you can do great things—right at your own table.

The End.
