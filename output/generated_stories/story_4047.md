# Sunny’s Song and the Tidy Treehouse

High in a leafy tree, there was a cozy wooden treehouse. The floor felt smooth like warm toast. The walls smelled like fresh pine. A little round window let in sunny light. This was Sunny’s home.

Sunny was a singing bird with bright yellow feathers and a tiny blue scarf. Sunny loved to sing. Sunny sang in the morning. Sunny sang at noon. Sunny sang before bed. “La-la-laaa!” Sunny would sing, hopping from branch to branch. Music made Sunny’s heart feel big and happy.

One day, Sunny had a big plan. “I will have a music show tonight!” Sunny chirped. “I will sing. My friends will clap. We will all feel the music.”

Sunny flapped to a little shelf. “Where is my bell?” Sunny peeped. A silver bell should have gone ding-ding. But the shelf was a jumble. Paint sticks, buttons, leaves, and string were bent and mixed. No bell.

Sunny checked under a feather pillow. Rustle, rustle. “Where is my drum?” Sunny peeped again. The small drum wasn’t there. Sunny’s tail drooped.

Sunny looked at the floor. It was covered with seed shells, tiny papers, and a purple ribbon. “Oh no,” Sunny whispered, tummy feeling twisty. “I want to make music. But I cannot find my things.”

Sunny tried anyway. “La-la-laaa!” Sunny sang, but a squeaky toy squeaked underfoot. “Eek!” Sunny hopped back and bumped a cup. The cup toppled and spilled beads that rattled like rain. Clink, clatter, plink!

Sunny’s chest felt tight. A hot tear filled one eye. “I feel upset,” Sunny said softly. “I feel frustrated. I want my music show to be ready.”

Down below, a gentle wind carried voices. “Sunny? Are you there?” called Pip the squirrel. “We came to help practice!”

Up scurried Pip with a fluffy tail, and slow, steady Tulla the turtle. Behind them came Bree the rabbit, ears twitching to the beat of the breeze.

“You look wobbly, Sunny,” said Bree. “Is something wrong?”

Sunny took a deep breath in… and out. “My treehouse is messy,” Sunny said. “I cannot find my bell or my drum. I feel sad. I feel stuck.”

Pip nodded. “Thank you for telling us how you feel.”

Tulla smiled. “We can help. We can prepare together.”

Sunny blinked. “Prepare?”

“Get ready,” said Tulla. “Organize. Then we can make music.”

Sunny’s eyes brightened. “Yes! Let’s make it tidy so the music can shine.”

They made a plan. Bree said, “We will make three baskets.” Pip held up a red basket. “Music things here.” Pip placed a small whistle inside. It went whooo.

Tulla held up a blue basket. “Craft things here.” Tulla added the purple ribbon, smooth and cool.

Sunny held up a yellow basket. “Trash here.” Sunny brushed seed shells into it. Tap-tap-tap.

They sang a little sorting song. Sunny made up the tune. “Sort, sort, sort—put it in the right spot!” Bree clapped soft paws. Pip tapped the floor with a nut. Tulla hummed low like a cello.

Sunny picked up a shiny spoon. “Music or craft?” Sunny asked.

“Music!” said Pip. “We can tap it like a bell.” Pip tapped: ting-ting!

Sunny giggled. “That sounds nice.”

They worked in slow, happy steps. The room began to smell less dusty and more like pine again. Sunlight made little sparkles on the clean wood. The red basket filled with instruments: the spoon, a small shaker, two sticks, a harmonica, and—“My bell!” Sunny chirped. It went ding-ding-ding, bright as stars.

“In the blue basket,” said Tulla, placing tape and buttons inside. “We will use these later.”

The yellow basket got full of tiny bits. Bree tied up the bag. “All tidy!” Bree said, breathing out with a smile.

Sunny’s shoulders felt light. “Thank you,” Sunny said. “I felt frustrated before. Now I feel calm.”

Pip grinned. “When we organize, we can find our music.”

“Now we can prepare the show,” said Tulla. “Let’s practice.”

Sunny set the instruments in a neat row on the shelf. “Bell on the left. Drum in the middle. Sticks on the right.” Sunny nodded. “I can see everything.”

They practiced together. Sunny sang, soft and sweet: “La-la-laaa, the sun is bright.” Bree added a quiet clap, pat-pat. Pip shook the shaker: shaka-shaka. Tulla tapped the sticks: tik-tik-tik.

“Let’s try a stop,” said Sunny. “When my wing goes up, we stop. When my wing goes down, we play.”

Sunny raised a wing. Everyone stopped. The treehouse grew still. Sunny lowered a wing. Music rose again like a smile. They all laughed.

“That’s cooperation,” said Tulla. “We listen. We work together.”

Evening came, warm and golden. Friends gathered below the tree. Fireflies blinked like tiny lights. The air smelled sweet, like clover.

Sunny stepped to the window. “Welcome to our music show!” Sunny chirped. “We prepared. We organized. We are ready to share our song.”

They played their tune. Ding from the bell. Shaka from the shaker. Tik from the sticks. Clap from Bree. And Sunny’s voice, clear and happy: “La-la-laaa! Music helps our hearts feel brave.”

The friends clapped and cheered. Sunny bowed. Sunny’s eyes shone. “Thank you,” Sunny said. “Today I learned: when I feel big feelings, I can breathe and say how I feel. When I get ready and keep my things in their place, I can make music more easily. And when we cooperate, our song is stronger.”

Pip nodded. “Organization is a friend to music.”

Tulla added, “Preparation helps us feel calm.”

Bree hugged Sunny. “And sharing music feels wonderful.”

Sunny looked at the neat shelf and the happy friends. Sunny’s heart felt full as a drum. “We did it together,” Sunny sang softly. “La-la-laaa.”

The End.
