# Pebble’s Balcony Garden Picnic

On a sunny morning, a kind mouse named Pebble peeked out from a tiny hole by a balcony garden. The garden was full of bright green leaves, red tomatoes like little balls, and purple flowers that smelled sweet. Bees hummed softly. A light breeze tickled Pebble’s whiskers.

“I love our balcony,” Pebble squeaked. “It smells like sunshine and mint!”

Pebble lived in a cozy corner with a soft cotton nest and a shiny button for a table. Pebble liked to help. Pebble also liked to share. But today, Pebble had a small worry.

Pebble found a basket of fresh veggies left near the pots. It was from the neighbors who lived in the building. They were all different families. Pebble saw little notes and tiny flags on the basket. One note said, “From Ana and Abuela—tomates!” Another said, “From Samir—mint and cucumbers.” Another had a picture of a mango and said, “From Priya’s Papa—sweet chutney.” Pebble sniffed. The chutney smelled spicy and sweet. The tomatoes smelled sunny. The mint smelled cool and green.

Pebble’s tummy rumbled. “I could eat all the tomatoes,” Pebble said. Then Pebble stopped. Pebble felt a tug in their heart. “But… the basket is for everyone. Sharing is caring.”

Just then, a tiny sparrow landed on the railing with a flutter. “Chirp! Hi, Pebble! I’m Suri. Is there a picnic?” Suri’s feathers looked soft like clouds.

“I think there can be,” Pebble said, ears perking up. “But we need to share so it’s fair.”

A ladybug crawled over a basil leaf. “I’m Dot,” she said in a teeny voice. “I love sweet things.”

Pebble had an idea. Pebble took initiative. “Let’s make a sharing table! We can invite friends. We can taste a little from each family. We can say thank you.”

Suri chirped. Dot waved her tiny red wings. Pebble got to work. Pebble pushed the shiny button table to the middle. Pebble spread a clean leaf as a cloth. Pebble cut the tomatoes into tiny pieces with a little shell. Chop, chop. The juice smelled warm and bright.

Pebble sniffed the mint. “So cool!” Pebble tapped it in a cup of water. The water smelled fresh. “Mint tea,” Pebble said proudly.

Pebble opened the chutney jar. “Oh! Spicy-sweet!” Pebble dipped a cucumber slice. Crunch! Pebble smiled. “Yum.”

Soon, more balcony friends came. A snail named Moon slid over, slow and smooth. “I brought wet lettuce,” Moon said, eyes twinkling. A butterfly named Lale fluttered down, her wings like little flags of many colors. “I can fan the table,” she said, whoosh-whoosh.

Pebble made little signs with chalk. One said, “Tomatoes—de Ana y Abuela.” Pebble practiced saying, “Gracias!” Another sign said, “Mint—shukran, Samir!” Pebble practiced, “Shukran!” Another said, “Chutney—dhanyavaad!” Pebble smiled, practicing, “Dhanyavaad.”

Suri tilted her head. “Why do you say different words?”

Pebble’s eyes shone. “We all come from different places. We have different words and foods. But we can share. We can learn. That is kind.”

They all sat. The breeze carried basil and soil smells. The sun warmed their backs like a soft hug. The city below made gentle hums. Pebble held up a tiny cherry tomato. “Let’s each take a little. We share, so everyone gets some.”

Dot took a tiny nibble. “Mmm! Sweet and bright!”

Moon crunched the cucumber. “Cool and juicy,” he said slowly.

Suri dipped a seed in chutney. “Spicy! But good,” she chirped, flapping with surprise, then laughing.

Just then, Pebble saw something. A big pigeon landed by the basket. The pigeon looked worried and hungry. His feathers were ruffled. “I am Pico,” he cooed softly. “My nest is new. I did not find food.”

Pebble felt the tummy-rumble memory. Pebble smiled. “You can join us, Pico.” Pebble made space. Pebble cut another tiny piece. Pebble poured mint water into a small bottle cap. “Here you go.”

Pico blinked. “For me?” he asked. “You share?”

Pebble nodded. “We share with friends. We share with new friends too.”

Everyone tasted a little. Everyone said the new words together. “Gracias! Shukran! Dhanyavaad!” Their voices sounded like a happy song.

The balcony garden felt full of smiles. The purple flowers waved. The bees hummed along.

When the food was almost gone, Pebble checked the basket. There was one last tomato. Pebble looked at it. Pebble looked at the friends. Pebble took a breath. “I wanted this,” Pebble said honestly. “But I will share it too.” Pebble cut the tomato into four tiny pieces. Pebble gave one to Pico. Pebble kept one for later. “Saving and sharing can go together,” Pebble said.

Pico puffed his chest. “I will bring seeds next time,” he promised. “From my friend, Lolo.”

Suri chirped, “I can bring crumbs!”

Moon said, “I will bring dew drops.”

Dot giggled, “I will bring smiles.”

They all laughed. Pebble’s heart felt light, like a leaf on the breeze.

Pebble stood on the shiny button and spoke. “Today we learned: Sharing helps everyone. When we share, we try new things. We say thank you in many ways. We take initiative to help. We make a big family, even on a small balcony.”

The sun began to set, painting the sky orange and pink. The garden smelled sleepy and sweet. Pebble wrapped the basket and left a note. “Thank you. We shared. We learned. We are grateful.”

Pebble curled up in the cozy nest. Pebble whispered, “Gracias. Shukran. Dhanyavaad.” Pebble smiled and closed their eyes, feeling warm and proud.

The End.
