# Tessa Turtle’s Tiny Green Plan

In a quiet study corner, where the lamp made a warm, soft glow, Tessa the shy turtle peeked from her shell. Her shell smelled like clean rain. Her little study corner had a small desk, a cup of bright crayons, and a stack of picture books. The chair creaked gently when she moved.

Tessa liked the study corner because it felt safe. It was calm. It was quiet. Today, she held a book called “Helping Our Earth.” The cover showed a happy tree and a smiling sun. Tessa touched the page with her small, careful claw. The paper felt smooth.

Tap, tap. Her friend Pip the sparrow landed on the window ledge. “Good morning, Tessa! What are you learning?” chirped Pip.

Tessa spoke softly. “I am reading about caring for our Earth. It says to pick up litter, save water, and plant seeds. But I feel small. I feel shy.”

Pip tilted his head. “Small is okay. Small can be strong. What is your goal today?”

Tessa took a deep breath. She looked at her supplies: a tiny metal pail, two seed packets, a little watering can, and a list she drew with crayon. The list had four big stars:
- Listen and learn
- Make a small plan
- Do one green thing
- Share the good news

“My goal is to do one green thing,” Tessa whispered.

Rustle, rustle. Nana Owl shuffled in, her feathers soft and smelling like dry leaves. She wore round glasses that reflected the lamp light. “Hoo-hoo, Tessa dear,” said Nana Owl kindly. “What does your book say? May I listen?”

Tessa nodded and read in a tiny voice, “Step one: Listen to nature. Nature tells us what it needs.”

Nana Owl nodded slowly. “Let’s listen together.”

They turned off the music and sat very still. The study corner became extra quiet. Tessa heard the slow drip of the faucet. Drip… drip… drip. She heard wind whisper at the window. She heard Pip’s tiny toes click on the sill.

Tessa blinked. “I hear the sink,” she said. “It is dripping. That wastes water.”

“That is good listening,” Nana Owl said. “What will you do?”

Tessa slid off her chair. Her feet made soft shuff-shuff sounds on the rug. She reached up and turned the faucet handle gently until the drip stopped. Silence. Then Pip chirped, bright and happy.

Tessa smiled a little. “I did one green thing.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “What next on your list?”

Tessa opened another page. The book showed pictures of plants and clean parks. “It says to pick up litter with a buddy and to plant seeds.”

She looked at the study corner. Under the desk, a paper wrapper hid like a crinkly snake. “Oh!” Tessa said. She took her pail and, with care, picked up the wrapper using her tiny tongs. Crinkle, plink! It fell into the pail.

Nana Owl tapped her beak lightly. “Good work. Do you know the right bin for that?”

Tessa thought. She looked at her knowledge cards. One card had a blue circle: recycle. One card had a green leaf: compost. One card had a gray square: trash. She touched the wrapper. “It is not food. It is not cardboard. It is shiny. Trash,” she said.

“Wise choice,” said Nana Owl.

Tessa felt a warm glow under her shell. “Two green things,” she whispered.

Pip fluttered down to the desk. “Seeds?” he asked, hopping.

Tessa opened the seed packet. It smelled like dry earth. She wanted to plant them today, but she felt a little worried. “I am shy to go outside,” she said. “What if others see me? What if I do it wrong?”

Nana Owl’s eyes were kind. “You can plant right here in a cup to start. Small is smart.”

Tessa nodded. She filled a tiny cup with soil. The soil felt cool and crumbly. She poked a gentle hole with her claw. Pip watched quietly. Tessa placed one seed in the hole. “Sleep well, little seed,” she whispered. She covered it with a soft pinch of soil.

She poured a tiny drink from the watering can. Drip, drip—just enough. The soil smelled like fresh rain. Tessa drew a label with her crayon: “Bean.”

“Now what does your list say?” Nana Owl asked.

Tessa looked at the last star: Share the good news. She took a breath. “I can tell my friends,” she said, her voice a little bigger. She opened her window and called, “Friends! I did small green things today. I stopped a drip. I picked up litter. I planted a seed.”

Benny Bunny peeped from the hallway. “Wow, Tessa! Can I learn too?” he asked.

Tessa stood a little taller. “Yes,” she said. “We can listen to nature. We can make small plans. We can help every day.”

Nana Owl smiled. Pip sang a sweet, bright song. The study corner felt full of hope. Tessa wrote her new goal on a fresh card: “One green thing each day.” She taped it above her desk. The tape made a tiny rip sound—rip!—and the card hung like a friendly flag.

She patted her cup. “Grow slow, little bean. I will care for you,” she said.

Tessa tucked her book away and took one more look at the quiet faucet, the clean floor, and the neat little plant cup. Her shy heart felt strong.

“I can help the Earth,” Tessa whispered, smiling.

The End.
