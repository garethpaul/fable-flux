# Koko the Koala and the Missing Cookies

Koko the koala loved helping Mama in the big, cozy pantry. The pantry smelled like warm cinnamon and sweet honey. Tall shelves reached up to the ceiling, filled with colorful jars, boxes, and bags of yummy food.

"Koko," called Mama from the kitchen, "please bring me the jar of chocolate chips from the pantry!"

Koko's fuzzy ears perked up. "Okay, Mama!" he said in his gentle voice.

As Koko climbed up to the middle shelf, something caught his eye. There was a big glass jar filled with his favorite cookies - the ones with rainbow sprinkles on top! His tummy rumbled loudly.

"Grumble, grumble!" went his belly.

Koko looked around. No one was watching. The cookies looked so delicious, sitting there like little treasures. He could take just one, and no one would ever know.

His paw reached toward the cookie jar. But then Koko stopped and thought.

"Wait," he said to himself. "Mama didn't say I could have a cookie. These cookies are for the whole family to share."

Koko's heart felt heavy. He really, really wanted a cookie. But he knew taking one without asking would be wrong.

Instead, Koko grabbed the chocolate chips and climbed down carefully. His little claws made soft clicking sounds on the wooden shelves.

"Here are your chocolate chips, Mama!" Koko said, handing her the bag.

"Thank you, sweetie," Mama smiled. "You're such a good helper."

Koko fidgeted with his fluffy tail. "Mama, I saw the rainbow cookies in the pantry. They looked really yummy, and my tummy was hungry. I wanted to take one, but I didn't because you didn't say I could."

Mama's eyes grew warm and proud. She bent down and gave Koko the biggest, softest hug.

"Oh, Koko! I'm so proud of you for telling the truth. That shows you have integrity - that means doing the right thing even when no one is watching."

"What's integrity?" asked Koko, tilting his fuzzy head.

"Integrity means being honest and doing what's right, even when it's hard," Mama explained. "You showed integrity by not taking the cookie and by telling me the truth."

Mama continued, "And you know what? Those cookies are special. We're saving them for Grandma Koala's birthday party tomorrow. By not eating one, you helped us conserve them - that means saving them for when we really need them."

Koko's eyes grew wide. "I helped save them for Grandma?"

"Yes! And because you were honest, I have something for you." Mama reached into the cupboard and pulled out a fresh eucalyptus leaf cookie she had just baked. "This one is just for you, for being so truthful and helpful."

The cookie was warm and smelled like Koko's favorite eucalyptus trees. It tasted even better than he had imagined because he had earned it by doing the right thing.

"Being honest feels good in my heart," Koko said, patting his chest with his paw.

"That's right," Mama said. "When we have integrity, we feel proud and happy inside. And now you know something important - it's always better to ask before taking something, and telling the truth is always the right choice."

From that day on, whenever Koko felt tempted to do something he wasn't sure about, he remembered how good it felt to be honest. He learned that integrity, conservation, and knowledge all worked together to help him make good choices.

The End.
