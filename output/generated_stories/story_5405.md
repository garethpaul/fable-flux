# Benny the Beaver's Big Heart

Benny the Beaver loved his cozy living room. It had soft, colorful cushions scattered everywhere, wooden blocks that smelled like fresh pine, and a big window that let in warm, golden sunshine. Benny was always busy building things with his strong teeth and tiny paws.

"Tap, tap, tap!" went Benny's teeth as he chewed on a stick. He was making a special fort out of branches and leaves he had collected from outside. The living room floor was covered with his building materials - smooth river rocks, bendable twigs, and crunchy autumn leaves that made rustling sounds when he walked on them.

But as Benny worked, he noticed something troubling. Through his big window, he could see his forest friends looking sad and cold. Little Rabbit was shivering without a warm burrow. Squirrel was searching everywhere for nuts, her fluffy tail drooping. And Bird was trying to build a nest, but she didn't have enough twigs.

"Oh my," said Benny, his brown eyes growing wide with worry. "My friends need help, but I'm so busy with my own fort."

Benny looked at his half-finished fort, then back at his friends outside. His whiskers twitched as he thought hard. The fort was going to be the most amazing one ever! But his friends looked so sad.

Just then, Little Rabbit hopped up to the window. "Benny," she called softly, "do you have any extra branches? I'm trying to make my burrow warmer before winter comes."

Benny's heart felt heavy. He wanted to help, but giving away his branches meant his fort wouldn't be as big and wonderful as he had planned.

"I... I need these for my fort," Benny said quietly, feeling his tummy twist with guilt.

As the day went on, Squirrel and Bird also came asking for help. Each time, Benny said no because he was too busy with his own project. But something funny happened - the more he worked on his fort alone, the less fun it became. The living room felt empty and quiet, even with all his rustling leaves and tapping sounds.

That evening, Benny sat in his beautiful fort, but he felt lonely and sad. "This isn't fun at all," he sighed, his voice echoing in the empty space.

Suddenly, Benny had a wonderful idea! His eyes sparkled like the stars beginning to twinkle outside.

"I know what to do!" he exclaimed, clapping his small paws together.

Benny gathered up half of his carefully collected branches, his smoothest rocks, and his crunchiest leaves. Then he hurried outside to find his friends.

"Little Rabbit!" he called. "Here are some warm branches for your burrow!"

"Squirrel! These acorns I found can be your winter snacks!"

"Bird! These perfect twigs will make your nest extra cozy!"

His friends' faces lit up with joy. "Thank you, Benny!" they all cheered. "You're so kind!"

But the best surprise came next. "Can we help you finish your fort?" asked Little Rabbit.

"We'd love to build together!" added Squirrel and Bird.

Soon, all four friends were in Benny's living room, working together. They built the most amazing fort ever - even better than Benny's original plan! The room filled with happy chatter, giggles, and the wonderful sounds of friendship.

As they all snuggled in the cozy fort that night, Benny's heart felt warm and full. "Sharing makes everything better," he whispered contentedly.

And from that day on, Benny learned that being generous and caring for others made him happier than keeping everything for himself.

The End.
