# Kiki Koala and the Guest Room Puzzle

Kiki was a calm koala with soft gray fur and warm brown eyes. She loved quiet mornings and gentle hugs. One sunny day, Kiki’s friend, Auntie Roo, came to visit. Auntie Roo would sleep in the little guest room with the bright yellow curtains and the cozy blue rug.

“Kiki,” said Mama, smiling, “can you help make the guest room ready?”

“Yes!” said Kiki. Her voice was soft, but brave. She took a deep breath. She liked to breathe in slowly and breathe out slowly. It made her body feel steady, like a tree.

Kiki padded to the guest room. It smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. A small lamp sat on the table. The window was open a crack, and the air felt cool and kind on Kiki’s nose. Kiki clapped her paws together. “Time to tidy!”

But something was not right.

The night-light glowed, click-click, then flicked off. The lamp blinked, blink-blink, and went dark. The window made a tiny moan, eeee, as it wiggled in the breeze. Auntie Roo peeked in. “Oh my,” she said softly. “This room is a little tricky.”

Kiki felt a flutter in her tummy. “I want Auntie Roo to feel safe,” she said. “I will think, think, think.”

She sat on the rug and took a calming breath. “When we have a puzzle,” she whispered, “we look and listen.”

First, Kiki used her eyes. She saw the lamp cord hiding under the bed, bent and twisty. She saw the night-light in the wall, a little loose. She saw the window not closed tight.

Second, Kiki used her ears. She listened. The window said eeee. The lamp made a tiny tap. The night-light hummed, hmmmmm.

Third, Kiki used her paws. She touched the cord gently. “Careful paws,” she told herself. “Ask for help with plugs.” She called, “Mama, can you help with the plug, please?”

Mama came with a kind smile. “Good noticing, Kiki. That is safe thinking.”

Kiki pointed with one paw. “The cord is bent. The night-light wiggles. The window is wobbly.”

Mama nodded. “Let’s solve one thing at a time.”

Kiki felt brave. “First, we fix the window,” she said. She pressed the window down and clicked the latch. The breeze became a soft whisper, shhh, and the moan went away. “Better,” Kiki said. She smiled and felt proud.

“Second, the lamp,” Mama said. “I will unplug and plug it in again.” She gently straightened the cord and clicked the switch. The lamp glowed warm and golden. The light made a friendly pool on the rug.

“Third, the night-light,” Kiki said. “May I try to push it in with one finger while you watch?”

“Yes,” said Mama. “Slow and gentle.”

Kiki pressed. Click. The night-light settled snug. It shined a small, steady star. Kiki’s tummy felt calm, like a quiet pond.

Auntie Roo clapped softly. “Kiki, you used your eyes, your ears, and your paws. You looked, listened, and asked for help. That is smart thinking.”

Kiki’s cheeks felt warm with happy. “Thank you. I used steps.”

But then Kiki saw something else. A basket of toys sat in the corner. It was tippy-toppy and might fall. “Oh!” she said. “We should make it safe.”

She lifted the toys and placed them in a neat row: the soft bear, the tiny truck, and the puzzle with big pieces. She scooted the basket to a flat spot. The room felt tidy. It smelled like lemon and a little like Auntie Roo’s rose hand cream as she unpacked.

“Kiki,” said Auntie Roo, “I brought a surprise.” She pulled out a small stone with a painted koala face. “This is a courage stone. When I feel wobbly, I hold it and breathe.”

Kiki took the smooth, cool stone. It felt nice in her paw. “I can be calm and brave,” she whispered.

Just then, Kiki frowned. “I remember,” she said slowly. “Yesterday I played in here. I rushed. I pushed the lamp under the bed. I forgot to fix it.” She looked at Auntie Roo. “I am sorry. I made the room tricky.”

Auntie Roo’s eyes were gentle. “Thank you for telling the truth. That is courage.”

Kiki stood tall. “I will make amends. I will be careful next time. I will use steps: look, listen, and ask.”

Mama hugged Kiki. Her fur smelled like eucalyptus tea. “That is wellness,” Mama said. “You cared for the room, for safety, and for your feelings.”

Auntie Roo lay down on the bed. The sheets were crisp. The lamp shined warm. The night-light glowed steady. “This guest room feels peaceful,” she said with a sigh.

Kiki sat in the soft chair and held the courage stone. “Today I learned to be a thinker,” she said. “When there is a puzzle, I can be calm. I can use steps. I can ask for help. I can make things right.”

Auntie Roo smiled. “That is a wonderful lesson, Kiki.”

Kiki nodded. “Big problems get small when we think.”

They all listened to the quiet hum of the safe, cozy room. Kiki’s heart felt light. She handed Auntie Roo the courage stone to share. “We are brave together,” she said.

The End.
