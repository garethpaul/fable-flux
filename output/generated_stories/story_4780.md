# Daisy's Daily Helper Garden

Little Daisy the deer fawn loved playing in the sunny backyard. Her soft brown spots sparkled in the warm sunlight, and her tiny hooves made gentle tap-tap sounds on the grass.

But Daisy had a big problem. Every day felt mixed up and confusing! Sometimes she forgot to eat breakfast. Sometimes she played all day and forgot to help Mama Deer with chores. Sometimes she felt too tired to play with her forest friends.

"Oh dear," sighed Daisy, sitting under the big apple tree. "I wish every day could feel happy and organized like Mama's days."

Just then, her friend Benny the bunny hopped over. "Hi Daisy! Why do you look sad?"

"I keep forgetting important things," Daisy explained. "My days feel all jumbled up like puzzle pieces that don't fit."

Benny's whiskers twitched thoughtfully. "My family has a special helper board! We made it together, and it shows us what to do each day. Want to make one for you?"

Daisy's ears perked up. "Really? That sounds wonderful!"

Soon, more friends arrived. Penny the squirrel brought colorful leaves. Tommy the turtle carried smooth pebbles in his shell. Sara the sparrow flew down with pretty flower petals.

"Let's work together!" chirped Sara. "We'll make the most beautiful routine helper ever!"

They found a flat piece of bark under the apple tree. Benny showed them how to arrange everything in order.

"First, put a yellow flower for morning time," suggested Penny, placing a bright sunflower petal at the top.

"Then a green leaf for breakfast time!" added Tommy, carefully setting down a mint leaf.

Daisy felt excited as they worked. The warm sun made everything smell fresh and earthy. She could hear birds singing and feel the soft grass tickling her hooves.

"What comes after breakfast?" asked Daisy.

"Play time!" laughed Benny, adding a bouncy acorn.

They continued decorating their special board. A blue pebble meant helping time. A red leaf showed lunch time. A brown stick meant rest time. And a purple flower meant family time.

"Now you'll remember everything!" said Sara, fluttering her wings happily.

The next morning, Daisy bounded to her routine helper board. The yellow flower reminded her it was morning time. She stretched her legs and felt the cool dew on the grass.

"Time for breakfast!" she said, seeing the green leaf. She munched sweet clover while the morning sun warmed her back.

When she saw the bouncy acorn, Daisy knew it was play time. She called to her friends, "Come play hide-and-seek!"

The blue pebble reminded her to help Mama Deer gather berries. Working together made the job fun and quick.

At lunch time, the red leaf told her to eat juicy apples. During rest time, the brown stick reminded her to nap in the shade.

Finally, the purple flower meant family time. Daisy snuggled with Mama Deer and shared stories about her wonderful day.

"My routine helper board makes everything so much easier!" Daisy told her mama. "And my friends helped me make it. Now every day feels organized and happy!"

Mama Deer nuzzled Daisy gently. "When we follow a routine and work together with friends, everything becomes more joyful."

From that day on, Daisy's routine helper board guided her through each happy, organized day. She never forgot important things again, and she always remembered to include her caring friends in her daily adventures.

The End.
