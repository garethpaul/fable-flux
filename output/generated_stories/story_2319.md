# Pippin's Colorful Lesson

Pippin the penguin waddled into the bright art room at Rainbow School. His black and white feathers gleamed under the warm lights. Colorful paintings hung on every wall, and tables were covered with paintbrushes, crayons, and paper.

"Good morning, everyone!" chirped Miss Daisy, the art teacher. "Today we're making friendship pictures!"

Pippin's eyes sparkled with excitement. He loved making art! He spotted a big box of brand-new crayons sitting on the center table. The crayons were so shiny and perfect - red like strawberries, blue like the ocean, yellow like sunshine.

"Mine!" thought Pippin. He quickly waddled over and wrapped his flippers around the whole box.

"I need these crayons," Pippin announced loudly. "I'm going to make the most beautiful picture ever!"

Bella the bunny hopped over with her paper. "Can I please use the red crayon, Pippin? I want to color my heart."

"No!" said Pippin, hugging the box tighter. "I'm using ALL of them!"

Tommy the turtle slowly walked up. "May I borrow the green one? Just for my tree?"

"No way!" Pippin turned his back. "These are MY crayons now!"

Soon, all the other animals sat quietly at their tables. They only had pencils to draw with. Their papers looked gray and sad without any colors.

Pippin started his picture. He drew a big rainbow with the red crayon. Then he switched to orange. The orange felt waxy and smooth. But when he looked around the room, something felt wrong.

His friends looked unhappy. Bella's bunny drawing had no pink nose. Tommy's tree had no green leaves. Sara the squirrel's flower had no yellow petals.

The art room felt quiet and sad instead of fun and cheerful.

Pippin's tummy felt funny - like he had eaten too many fish. He looked at his colorful rainbow, then at his friends' gray pictures.

"This doesn't feel good," Pippin whispered to himself.

Miss Daisy walked over and sat beside Pippin. "How are you feeling about your picture?" she asked gently.

"It's pretty," said Pippin slowly. "But... but my friends look sad. And the room feels sad too."

"What do you think might help?" asked Miss Daisy.

Pippin looked at the big box of crayons. He had only used three colors. There were so many more - purple, pink, brown, and black. Enough for everyone!

Pippin took a deep breath. He waddled to the middle of the room with the crayon box.

"Friends," he called out. "I'm sorry I kept all the crayons. That wasn't kind. I want to share them with everyone!"

Bella's ears perked up. Tommy smiled his slow, happy smile.

"Really?" asked Sara. "We can all use them?"

"Yes!" said Pippin. "Art is more fun when we share!"

He placed the box in the center of the table. "Let's all make beautiful pictures together!"

Soon the art room buzzed with happy chatter. Friends shared crayons and helped each other. Bella colored her heart bright red. Tommy made his tree the greenest green. Sara's flower bloomed with yellow petals.

"Thank you for sharing, Pippin!" said Bella.

"Your rainbow looks wonderful!" said Tommy.

Pippin felt warm and happy inside. His picture was beautiful, but seeing his friends' colorful artwork made him feel even better.

When art time ended, Miss Daisy hung all their pictures on the wall. The room looked like a rainbow garden!

"Sharing makes everything more beautiful," Pippin said, admiring their colorful display.

From that day on, Pippin always remembered: the best art happens when friends share together.

The End.
