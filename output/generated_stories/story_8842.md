# Joey Jumps to a Sparkly Clean

Sunlight peeked through a tiny round window in the quiet attic. Dust danced in the beams like little stars. A joyful kangaroo joey named Kip tipped his ears and sniffed. "Achoo!" he squeaked. "This attic smells dusty."

Kip’s soft paws brushed a stack of boxes. The cardboard felt bumpy. He heard a gentle creak as the floorboards said hello. In the corner, a small radio sat next to a basket of old brushes and cloths. Kip’s tail thumped with a happy rhythm. "I love to dance," he said, bouncing once. "But first, we need clean space!"

A tiny mouse popped her head out from behind a hatbox. "Squeak! I’m Mimi," she said. "I like dancing too."

Kip smiled. "Hi, Mimi! I’m Kip. Let’s make the attic fresh and shiny. Clean, then dance!"

Mimi sniffed the air. "Squeak… it is a little stinky."

Kip nodded. He spoke in a gentle, strong voice. "We can fix it. Clean hands. Clean space. Then we can move and breathe easy."

Mimi looked at her paws. "My paws feel sticky," she said softly.

Kip pointed to a small sink at the top of the attic stairs. "Let’s wash. I can show you."

They walked together. The sink was cool and silver. Water trickled like tiny bells. "Turn on the water," Kip said. "Wet your paws. One, two!"

Mimi dipped her paws. "It’s cold!" she giggled.

"It wakes us up," said Kip. He pumped a little soap. It smelled like lemons. "Rub, rub, rub," he sang. "Front, back, in between. Rub, rub, rub, now we are clean!" He showed Mimi how to scrub each finger and the thumbs too. He counted slowly. "One… two… three… four… five… six… seven… eight… nine… ten."

Mimi copied him. "Rub, rub!" She rinsed. The water washed away the gray. "My paws feel slippery and then squeaky. I like it!"

They dried with a soft towel. It was fluffy and warm. "Now let’s clean the attic," Kip said. "We can do it together."

They made a plan. Kip spoke kindly but clearly. "I will sweep. You can wipe. If we get tired, we rest. If we need help, we ask."

Mimi nodded. "Yes! We can do it."

Kip tied a little scarf over his nose to keep dust away. Mimi tied a tiny ribbon. Kip held the broom and swish-swish went the bristles. Dust skittered like tiny clouds. Mimi sprayed a gentle cleaner on a cloth. It smelled like oranges. She wiped the radio. "Wipe, wipe," she said, making little circles. The radio looked shiny and proud.

"Listen," said Kip. "Do you hear the squeak of the clean cloth?"

"Squee-eeak," Mimi laughed. "That means it’s clean!"

They hummed as they worked. "Brush and wipe, sweep and shine, make it fresh and make it fine," they sang.

Kip saw an old box with a wobbly lid. Inside were feather boas and sparkly hats. "Ooo!" he squealed. "Dance hats!"

Mimi reached for a hat with a long feather. It tickled her whiskers. "Eep! It tickles!" she giggled. She put a small hat on her head and stood tall.

Kip looked at the floor. "There is a sticky spot," he said. "We need warm water." He paused and took a breath. "Mimi, I need help carrying the bucket. It is safer with two."

Mimi nodded. "I can help. We can hold it together."

They carried the bucket slowly. Drip, drip went the water. They set it down. Kip dipped the mop and swished. Mimi held the bucket steady. "We are a team," she said.

Kip smiled. "Yes. We are strong and safe when we work together."

Soon the attic smelled sweet and light. The sunbeams felt warm on their faces. The floor looked smooth and bright.

"Now we dance!" Kip said. He clicked the radio on. Soft music filled the room like a friendly hug. Kip bounced lightly. Thump, thump went his feet, soft and steady. Mimi twirled like a leaf. The feather boa whooshed.

"Wait," Kip said. "Before we dance, we wash hands again. We touched dust."

Mimi blinked. "Again?"

Kip nodded. "Yes. Clean before, clean after. That keeps us healthy."

Back to the sink they went. "Wet, soap, rub, rinse, dry," Kip sang. "Front, back, in between, make those paws and hands so clean!" They counted to ten again. The lemon smell made them smile.

Then they danced with joy. Kip hopped high and landed softly. Mimi made tiny tip-taps. "Tap, tap!" The radio hummed. The clean floor felt smooth and safe under their feet. Breathe in—fresh. Breathe out—happy.

When the song ended, they gave each other a gentle high-five. "We did it," Mimi said. "We cleaned. We washed. We danced."

Kip nodded. "We used kind words. We asked for help. We worked as a team. We kept our hands and our space clean. I feel proud."

Mimi’s eyes shone. "Me too. Clean helps us feel good and play better."

Kip hung the cloth to dry. He turned off the radio. The attic was quiet again, but it was a bright, friendly quiet.

Kip looked at Mimi. "Tomorrow, we can dance again," he said. "And we will wash our hands again too!"

"Yes," Mimi squeaked. "Clean and dance. Together."

The End.
