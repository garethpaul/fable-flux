# Sunny Sings in the Home Office

Sunny was a small, bright bird with a voice like a bell. She lived in a cozy cage by the big window of a home office. The office smelled like warm paper and lemon cleaner. The sunlight made the keyboard shine, and the clock went tick-tick-tick. Sunny loved the gentle breeze that slipped through the curtain. She loved to sing.

“Tweet-a-dee! Tweet-a-doo!” Sunny sang, fluffing her soft yellow feathers. Her song was sweet and sparkly, like tiny bubbles in the air.

Across the room sat Sam, who did his work on the computer. Sam wore soft slippers that went shuffle-shuffle on the carpet. His coffee made a little steam cloud that smelled like toast. Sam smiled at Sunny’s music. “Good morning, Sunny,” he said. “Your song makes the morning bright.”

“Tweet-a-dee! Good morning!” sang Sunny.

But one day, a big blue button on Sam’s screen blinked red. He put on his headphones and whispered, “Important meeting.” His voice was gentle, but his eyebrows bent like little tents. The clock ticked a little louder in the quiet.

Sunny felt excited and wanted to sing her new song. It went up and down like a slide. “Twee-ee-eet! La-la-la!” She hopped on her perch and spread her wings. She did not know about the meeting. She just wanted to share her joy.

Sam tried to talk to the people on the screen. “Hello every—” Sunny sang a bright trill. Sam pressed the mute button and whispered, “Oh dear.” He looked at Sunny. “Sunny, I love your song, but I need quiet for a little while.”

Sunny tilted her head. She didn’t understand the big meeting. She only heard her own happy heart. She sang again, even louder. “TWEE-EE-EEET!”

Sam’s shoulders slumped. The people on the screen frowned. One person said, “I hear a bird.” Another person giggled.

Sam took off his headphones and walked to Sunny’s cage. He knelt down so they were eye to eye. He spoke slowly, kindly, and clearly. “Sunny, I need to talk to my friends right now. It is important. Your song is wonderful. Your voice is special. But I can’t hear them. Can we make a plan?”

Sunny blinked her round black eyes. “Plan?” she chirped softly. The word felt new and shiny.

“Yes,” said Sam. He pointed to a little picture chart he drew on a sticky note. He drew a sun, a music note, and a quiet moon. “Sing-time when the sun is here,” he said, tapping the sun and music note. “Quiet-time when the moon is here,” he said, tapping the moon.

Sunny leaned closer. The sticky note smelled sweet and papery. She touched it with her beak. “Sing-time. Quiet-time,” she copied, in a tiny whisper. She liked how the words sounded.

Sam nodded. “And we can use a signal,” he said. He picked up a soft blue scarf from the chair. “When I put the scarf on the desk, it means quiet-time. When I take it off, it’s sing-time!” He smiled. “We can work together.”

Sunny looked at the scarf. It was silky and cool, like a calm pond. She chirped a curious little “peep.” She wanted to help. She wanted to be safe and kind.

Just then, the screen blinked again. Sam set the blue scarf on the desk. He pointed to the scarf, then to the moon picture. He put a finger to his lips and whispered, “Quiet-time.”

Sunny took a deep breath. She folded her wings. She hummed a tiny, gentle hum, like a bee in a flower. Then she sat very still. The clock tick-tick-ticked. The room smelled like lemons and paper. Sunny watched the autumn leaves outside float and twirl.

Sam spoke clearly to his friends. He smiled more. The people on the screen smiled back. One person showed a drawing. Another person said, “Good idea, Sam.” The meeting felt smooth, like a path without bumps.

When the meeting ended, Sam lifted the blue scarf off the desk and waved it like a little flag. He pointed to the sun and music note. “Sing-time!”

Sunny puffed up with pride. “Sing-time!” she cheered. She burst into her happy song, but this time she added soft parts and loud parts, like a story. “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo, soft-soft, loud-loud, just for you!”

Sam clapped. “Sunny, your song is amazing. Thank you for listening. Thank you for helping.” He opened the cage door and offered his finger. Sunny hopped on and felt his warm, steady hand. She felt safe.

Sam said, “Your voice is different and special. My work is different and special too. When we talk and make a plan, we can both do our best.”

Sunny nodded and sang a new line, gentle and bright. “Talk-talk, plan-plan, friends can understand!” She nuzzled Sam’s thumb with her beak. It felt smooth, like a small pebble.

They made more picture cards: a snack apple, a play ball, and a rest cloud. They made signals for each time. They celebrated their plan with a little dance. Sam wiggled his slippers. Sunny flapped her wings in tiny swooshes. They laughed together.

From that day on, the home office was a happy place. Sometimes it was quiet like a calm lake. Sometimes it was musical like a parade. Sunny felt proud to use her voice at the right times. Sam felt thankful for Sunny’s bright songs.

They had learned that communicating—talking, listening, and making simple signals—helps everyone. It keeps the home safe and calm. It lets all voices be heard and loved.

Sunny sang her soft bedtime song as the sun went down. “We talk, we plan, we understand.” The words floated like feathers in the warm, lemony air.

The End.
