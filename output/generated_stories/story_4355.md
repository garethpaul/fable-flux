# Porch Pals and the Patience Game

On a peaceful front porch, a gentle deer fawn named Fern took a soft step onto the wooden boards. Tap, tap, tap went Fern’s tiny hooves. The porch smelled like warm sunshine and sweet apples from the tree nearby. A breeze whispered through the leaves. Chirp, chirp, chirp sang a little bird. Fern’s ears twitched with every sound.

“I like this calm place,” Fern said. “It feels safe.”

Fern came each morning to look and learn. She looked at the clouds. She learned how the breeze felt on her fur. She listened to the soft creak of the rocking chair. Creak, creak. The porch was a place to breathe and think.

One sunny day, Fern saw a small rabbit hopping toward the porch. Hop, hop, hop. The rabbit’s nose wiggled fast, and its ears were long and pink inside.

“Hello,” said Fern in a gentle voice. “I am Fern. I like this porch. Would you like to sit with me?”

The rabbit blinked. “I am Pip,” the rabbit said. “I feel shy. Is it okay if I sit over there?” Pip pointed to the shady corner.

“Yes,” said Fern. “You can sit where you feel safe.”

Fern and Pip sat quietly. They listened to the wind chimes go ting-a-ling. They smelled the flowers by the steps. Sweet and soft. They watched a butterfly float past, wings like tiny flags.

After a while, Fern said, “Would you like to play a game? We can play the Patience Game. We wait and we count. We listen for sounds.”

Pip’s whiskers twitched. “I can try,” said Pip. “But sometimes I want to rush.”

“That’s okay,” said Fern. “We can try together.”

They began to count the sounds. “One,” said Fern, as a bee buzzed by. Bzzz. “Two,” said Pip, as a leaf fell and brushed the step. Shhh. “Three,” said Fern, as the rocking chair creaked again. Creak.

Pip hopped up and down. “I heard a four! I heard a four!” Pip squeaked when the screen door clicked. But Pip forgot to wait their turn.

Fern took a calm breath. In, and out. “It is your turn,” Fern said kindly. “You heard number four. Good listening!”

Pip beamed. “Thank you.” Pip took a deep breath, too. In, and out.

They kept playing. The porch felt happy, like a soft hug.

Then a gust of wind blew the flower pot on the rail. Clatter! The pot tipped and soil spilled. Pip jumped, eyes wide. “Oh no! I made a mess,” Pip said, even though the wind had done it. Pip’s ears drooped.

Fern stood very still. “It’s okay,” Fern said softly. “We can fix it together.”

Pip sniffled. “I don’t know how.”

“We can be helpers,” said Fern. “Friends help. We can be calm. We can be strong and try again.”

Fern nudged a little hand broom with a hoof. “I will sweep slowly,” said Fern. “You can hold the little dustpan.”

Pip wiped their nose and nodded. “Okay,” Pip said in a small voice.

Sweep, sweep, sweep went Fern. The soil made a soft, sandy sound. Pip held the dustpan steady, small paws shaking at first, then still. Together, they moved the soil back to the pot. They patted it down with careful paws and hooves.

“There,” said Fern. “We did it together.”

Pip’s ears lifted. “We did fix it! Thank you for helping me,” Pip said.

“You helped me, too,” said Fern. “You held the dustpan just right.”

The little bird on the rail chirped, as if clapping. Chirp-chirp-chirp! The wind chimes sang again, but softer now. Ting-a-ling-ling, like a thank-you song.

Pip smiled. “Fern, you were calm. You did not get mad.”

Fern smiled back. “Sometimes I feel worried, too,” said Fern. “But I take a breath. I ask a friend. Friends listen. Friends understand.”

Pip nodded. “I want to be a friend like that.”

Fern’s tail flicked happily. “We can practice,” Fern said. “Let’s try the Patience Game again. This time, if we rush, we stop and breathe.”

They sat side by side. The porch felt cozy and bright. They listened.

“Do you want the next number?” Fern asked.

Pip giggled. “Yes, please.”

They counted the squeak of a squirrel’s chatter. “Four,” said Pip. They counted the whoosh of the breeze. “Five,” said Fern. They counted the plink of a drip from the watering can. “Six,” they said together.

Pip said, “I like playing with you. I feel brave with you.”

“I feel brave with you, too,” said Fern. “We can be brave and gentle. We can try again when things are hard.”

The sun slid lower, making golden stripes on the porch floor. It warmed Fern’s fur and Pip’s paws. The air smelled like apples again. The rocking chair whispered a soft good job. Creak, creak.

Pip stood and stretched. “Fern, can we meet tomorrow?” Pip asked.

“Yes,” said Fern. “Friends come back. Friends care.”

Pip grinned. “I will bring a tiny carrot to share.”

“I will bring an apple slice,” said Fern. “We will listen. We will play. We will help each other.”

They touched noses—boop!—and laughed.

That evening, as fireflies blinked like little stars, Fern thought about the day. Fern felt warm inside. Friendship felt like the porch: safe, calm, and kind. With a friend, Fern could listen, understand, and try again.

Lesson: Friends listen and help each other. We stay calm, we try again, and we are kind. That is how friendship grows.

The End.
