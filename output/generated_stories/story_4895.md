# Gigi's Big Voice

Gigi the giraffe loved playing in the cozy living room with her family. The soft carpet felt nice under her hooves, and colorful toys were scattered everywhere like a rainbow. But today, something was bothering her.

"Gigi, can you put away all these blocks?" called Mama from the kitchen. The wooden blocks were piled high near the window where the warm sunshine streamed in.

Gigi looked at the tall tower of blocks. They reached way up high, but even higher were the toy shelves where they belonged. Gigi stretched her long neck as far as she could, but her hooves couldn't quite reach the top shelf.

"I'll try," Gigi said quietly, standing on her tippy-hooves. She could smell the fresh lavender scent from the room diffuser as she stretched and stretched. But it was no use - the shelf was still too high.

Papa Giraffe walked by, his heavy footsteps making the floorboards creak softly. "Gigi, are you done with those blocks yet?"

Gigi's cheeks felt warm. She wanted to help, but she felt too shy to say the shelf was too high. "Almost," she whispered, even though she hadn't put away a single block.

Her little brother Benny bounced into the room on his toy horse. "Look at me, Gigi! I'm flying!" The toy horse's wheels squeaked happily across the smooth floor.

Gigi watched Benny play and felt sad. She wanted to play too, but first she needed to finish her chore. The blocks sat there like a colorful mountain, waiting.

Then Gigi remembered what her teacher Ms. Owl had said: "When you need help, use your voice. It's okay to ask!"

Gigi took a deep breath that filled her long neck with air. She walked to the kitchen where Mama was washing dishes. The soapy water smelled like lemons and made pretty bubbles that popped softly.

"Mama," Gigi said, her voice a little shaky. "I want to put away the blocks, but the shelf is too high for me. Can you help me, please?"

Mama turned around with a big smile. Her eyes sparkled like stars. "Oh, sweetie! Of course I can help. Thank you for telling me."

Together, they walked back to the living room. Mama lifted Gigi up so she could reach the high shelf. Gigi carefully placed each block in its special spot - red ones here, blue ones there, yellow ones in between.

"I did it!" Gigi said proudly. The shelf looked neat and tidy, and she felt happy inside like sunshine.

"You sure did," Mama said, giving her a gentle hug that felt warm and safe. "And you did something even more important - you used your voice to ask for help when you needed it."

Papa came over and patted Gigi's head softly. "We're proud of you for speaking up, Gigi. Asking for help shows you're brave and smart."

Benny rode over on his squeaky toy horse. "Now can we all play together?" he asked with a giggle.

"Yes!" Gigi laughed, feeling light and free. The living room felt even more cozy now, filled with love and understanding.

As they played together on the soft carpet, Gigi learned that using her voice to ask for help wasn't scary at all. It was actually the best way to solve problems and feel good about herself.

The End.
