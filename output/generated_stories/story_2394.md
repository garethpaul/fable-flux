# Finn Leads the Bubble Brigade

Finn was a curious fox kit with bright eyes and soft, orange fur. One rainy afternoon, Finn padded into the bathroom. Drip, drip, drip went the rain on the window. The bathroom smelled like soap and mint. The floor tiles felt cool on Finn’s paws.

Mama Fox called from the hallway, “Finn, it’s bath time! Please wash your paws, brush your teeth, and hang up your towel when you’re done.”

Finn’s ears perked up. “Okay, Mama!” he chirped. He liked the shiny faucet and the mirror that made two Finns. He liked the bubbles most of all.

Finn climbed onto his little blue step stool. He turned the faucet. “Swish,” went the water. Warm steam puffed up like tiny clouds. Finn giggled. “Hello, clouds!”

Just then, three bath toy friends peeked from the tub. There was Bubbles the whale, Tiny Turtle, and Captain Duck. Finn waved. “Hi, team!”

Captain Duck bobbed and said, “Quack! We’re ready for a bubble parade!”

Tiny Turtle blinked. “I’m a little scared. The water looks big.”

Bubbles the whale hummed, “We can do it together.”

Finn felt his tail swish. His tummy felt fizzy like soda pop. He wanted everyone to have fun and to stay safe, too. He remembered Mama’s words: wash, brush, and hang the towel.

“I can be the leader,” Finn said softly. He stood tall on the stool. “Leaders listen. Leaders help. Leaders follow directions.” He took a deep breath. “We will do things in order. That keeps us safe.”

Finn turned the faucet to a tiny trickle. “First, wash paws.” He rubbed his paws with a small dollop of soap. Squish, squish, squish. The soap smelled like oranges. “Mmm, smells happy,” Finn said.

Captain Duck quacked, “Quack! Big water next!”

Finn shook his head kindly. “We wait our turn, Captain. Tiny Turtle is nervous. Let’s be gentle.”

Tiny Turtle peeked out. “Thank you, Finn.”

Finn nodded. “I will fill the tub slowly.” He watched the water rise. Glub, glub. He checked the water with his paw. “Warm, not hot.” He remembered Mama’s rule: “Use the blue dot.” He turned the handle to the blue dot.

Bubbles the whale sang, “Whooooooo!”

Captain Duck tried to splash. Splash! Water sprinkled the mirror. Finn held up a paw. “Freeze, please.” He used a calm voice. “We don’t splash yet. We listen first.”

Captain Duck wobbled and squeaked, “I want big waves.”

Finn smiled. “We can have big waves after we wash and brush. We can make a wave dance at the end. Is that okay?”

Captain Duck thought and then nodded. “Quack-okay.”

Finn filled a small cup and poured it over Bubbles the whale. Trickle, trickle. “See, Tiny Turtle?” Finn said. “Small water feels nice.”

Tiny Turtle slid into the tub. “It’s warm like a hug.”

Finn washed behind his ears. He scrubbed his paws and tail. He showed his friends how to wash gently. “Scrub, scrub, scrub. Rinse, rinse, rinse.”

Then Finn climbed down to brush his teeth. He squeezed a pea of minty paste on his brush. The mint smelled bright, like snow. “Brush, brush, brush,” he sang. “Shine, shine, shine.” He looked at his friends. “Leaders show and tell. Let’s count to ten.” They counted together. “One, two, three…” Up and down, round and round, little bubbles tingled on Finn’s tongue.

Suddenly, Captain Duck slipped toward the drain. “Quack! Help!”

Finn’s heart jumped. He wanted to grab fast. But he remembered: pause, think, solve. He turned off the water with a click. He placed the drain cover snug. “You are safe, Captain,” Finn said. He used a washcloth to lift Captain Duck back. “We stay calm. We use our plan.”

Bubbles the whale hummed with relief. “Leaders keep everyone calm.”

Tiny Turtle smiled. “Thank you for listening to me when I was scared.”

Finn’s chest felt warm and proud. “Thank you for telling me. Leaders listen to feelings.”

Mama Fox peeked in the door. “I hear good washing. How’s my bubble brigade?”

Finn grinned, toothpaste foam on his whiskers. “We’re following directions! We washed, we brushed, and we kept the water safe. We made a plan together.”

Mama Fox clapped softly. “That is good leadership, Finn. You listened, you were gentle, and you kept your team safe.”

Finn nodded. “Now we do the wave dance?” he asked his friends.

Captain Duck quacked, “Yes! Small waves!”

Finn filled the cup with just a little water. He swished it side to side. Slosh, slosh, swish! Tiny Turtle giggled. Bubbles the whale hummed a happy tune. The mirror sparkled. The bathroom smelled like mint and oranges. The rain outside whispered, tap-tap-tap.

When it was time to finish, Finn turned off the water. Click. He patted his fur dry with a soft towel. “Last step,” he said. “Hang up the towel.” He stretched on his toes and hung it neatly on the hook.

Mama Fox smiled. “Leader Finn, mission complete.”

Finn felt calm and strong. He had used a gentle voice. He had followed directions. He had listened to worries. He had solved a problem with peaceful choices.

He whispered to Captain Duck, Tiny Turtle, and Bubbles the whale, “Good team.”

The End.
