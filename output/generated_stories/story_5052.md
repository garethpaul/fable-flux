# Benny Bunny Takes a Breath

Benny Bunny had long ears, soft paws, and a big, bouncy hop. He was a determined rabbit who loved to help. One sunny morning, Benny hopped into the warm kitchen. The air smelled like cinnamon and apples. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. The kettle hummed a gentle song, and the oven clicked as it warmed.

“I will make carrot-apple muffins!” Benny said, puffing out his chest. “They will be yummy and healthy.” He wiggled his nose at a shiny bowl, a wooden spoon, and a basket of carrots and apples.

Mama Bunny peeked in. “That sounds helpful,” she said with a smile. “I will be nearby if you need me.”

Benny washed his paws with soap and warm water. The bubbles were soft and slippery. “Clean paws help healthy tummies,” he said proudly, rubbing his paws dry.

He lined up the carrots. Tap, tap, tap went the grater. Scritch, scritch went the apples. Orange and white curls fell into the bowl. “This smells sweet and fresh,” Benny said. He felt happy.

Benny cracked an egg. Plop! It slipped in. He scooped flour. Poof! A tiny cloud dusted his whiskers. He stirred carefully. Squish, squish, squish. The batter was thick and sticky.

Benny reached for the muffin pan. It wobbled. “Oh!” The pan slid. Batter dripped onto the warm oven door and sizzled. Ssssss. Benny’s ears drooped. His tail sagged.

“I wanted it perfect,” he whispered. His nose wiggled fast. He felt hot in his cheeks. His tummy felt tight. “I can’t do it,” he said, louder now. “This is too hard!”

Mama Bunny stepped closer. She kept her voice soft. “I see a frustrated face,” she said. “It is okay to feel that. Let’s help your feelings first.”

Benny blinked. A tear rolled down his fur. “How?” he asked.

“Try our calm plan,” Mama said. “Pause. Put your paws on your belly. Take three slow breaths.”

Benny put his warm paws on his soft middle. In through the nose—sniff. Out through the mouth—whoooo. One. Two. Three. He felt his shoulders drop. The tight tummy got softer.

Mama Bunny nodded. “Name the feeling.”

“I feel frustrated,” Benny said. The word felt big, but it fit. “I wanted it to work right away.”

“That is a strong feeling,” Mama said. “Now choose a helpful action. We can clean up and try again.”

Benny’s ears perked up. He sniffed the kitchen. It still smelled like cinnamon and apples. The sunlight still made golden squares. The oven still hummed. “I can help,” he said. “I can keep going.”

Together they wiped the oven door. Swipe, swipe. They washed the sticky bowl. Splash, splash. Benny breathed slow when he felt the tight tummy try to come back. He said, “Frustration comes, and I can breathe.”

“Healthy habits help us try again,” Mama said. “Clean space. Calm body. Careful steps.”

Benny smiled a little. He set the muffin pan flat on the counter. He held it with one paw and scooped batter with the other. Plop, plop, plop, neat little circles filled the cups.

He looked at the oven and remembered the sizzle. His nose twitched, but he took a slow breath. “I feel ready,” he said. “Mama, can you help me put them in?”

“Yes,” Mama said. She opened the oven with a mitt. Warm air whooshed out, smelling sweet. Benny watched, paws behind his back, as Mama slid in the pan.

While the muffins baked, Benny cleaned the table. He hummed a hop-hop tune. He stacked the bowls. He washed the spoon. He felt proud to be helpful.

Tick, tick, tick went the timer. Ding! The muffins were golden and puffed. Steam curled up like tiny clouds.

Benny waited for them to cool. He wanted to grab one right away, but he remembered calm steps. He blew gently. Fffff. He counted to five. One, two, three, four, five.

He took a bite. The muffin was warm and cozy. It tasted like sweet apples and crunchy carrots. “Mmm!” Benny’s eyes sparkled. “I did it!”

Mama Bunny hugged him. “You did it with calm breaths, helpful hands, and a healthy plan.”

Benny nodded. “When I feel frustrated, I can pause. I can breathe three times. I can name the feeling. I can choose a helpful action.”

He looked at the clean counter and the empty batter bowl. He looked at the golden muffins. He felt strong and steady inside.

Benny hopped to the door and called, “Neighbors, we have muffins!” He shared with Mr. Mole and Little Squirrel. “I made these by trying again,” he told them. “Breathing helped me.”

Mr. Mole smiled. “That is a tasty lesson.”

Little Squirrel giggled. “Crunchy carrots!”

Benny took another slow breath. The warm kitchen felt safe and bright. He learned that frustration can visit, but calm can stay. With healthy habits and helpful hands, he could try, try, try again.

The End.
