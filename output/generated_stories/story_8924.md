# Sunny Seeds on the Balcony

Pip was a small, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail. He lived near a cozy apartment with a sunny balcony garden. The balcony smelled like mint and tomatoes. The leaves rustled softly in the warm morning breeze.

Every day, Pip visited the balcony to check on his plant friends. He liked to pat the soil with his tiny paws. He listened to the bees buzz and the birds sing, tweet-tweet! He loved the sound of water dripping from the watering can, plip-plop.

“Good morning, garden,” Pip chirped. “Let’s have a good day!”

One day, dark clouds rolled in. The wind hissed. Whooosh! A big storm came fast. Rain pounded the balcony, splish-splash, splish-splash! The wind pushed the little pots. A tray of seeds slid to the edge.

“Oh no!” Pip gasped. He grabbed the tray, but the wind tugged and tugged. Pip’s paws slipped. “I can’t hold it alone!”

Just then, a ladybug named Dot landed on Pip’s ear. “I’m here,” Dot buzzed softly. “We can try together.”

Pip nodded. “Teamwork!” he said. He tucked his tail under the tray to stop it from sliding. His tail felt cold and wet, but he held on tight.

“Push, Dot!” Pip called.

Dot pushed with her tiny legs. She was small, but she was brave. Pip pushed too. The tray moved slowly, scrape, scrape, back from the edge.

“Almost there,” Dot grunted.

A sparrow named Peep hopped onto the railing. He shook off the rain. “Need help?” Peep chirped.

“Yes, please!” Pip said. “We need to save the seeds.”

Peep flapped his wings, fwoosh fwoosh, to block the wind. The air felt kinder right away. With the wind softer, Pip and Dot slid the tray all the way to the safe corner.

“We did it!” Dot cheered.

But then, the rain poured even harder. A small tomato plant tipped over with a thump. Its soil spilled like dark cocoa. The plant’s leaves drooped.

Pip’s heart thumped. He felt a little scared. His paws were muddy. His whiskers dripped. He wanted to hide, but he took a deep breath. In-out. In-out. “I can try again,” he told himself. “I won’t give up.”

“Let’s help the tomato,” Pip said. “We can be gentle.”

He lifted the plant with careful paws. The stem was soft. The tiny fruits were green and smooth like marbles. Dot found a sturdy stick near the mint pot.

“Use this!” Dot buzzed.

“Great idea,” Pip said. Peep held a big leaf over Pip like a small umbrella. Drip, drip, the rain tapped the leaf instead of Pip’s head.

Pip set the tomato back in the pot. He scooped soil, pat-pat, around the roots. Dot placed the stick next to the stem. Pip tied it with a soft string he found by the watering can. The tomato plant stood tall again.

“Is it okay?” Peep asked.

Pip touched a leaf. The plant smelled green and fresh. “It’s better now,” he said with a smile.

The storm slowed. The clouds thinned. A pale rainbow peeked over the buildings. The balcony felt calm and clean. The air was cool and smelled like rain and basil.

Pip looked around. Some pots were still tilted. A little strawberry plant had lost a flower. A small snail peeked out from under a pot, eyes wiggling.

“Hello,” the snail said shyly. “I was scared.”

“It was scary,” Pip agreed. “But we kept trying. And we helped each other.”

Pip, Dot, and Peep worked together. They nudged pots upright. They brushed soil back into place. They picked fallen leaves and said, “Thank you, plants,” for growing so well.

Soon the balcony looked neat and bright. The seeds were safe. The tomato stood tall. The mint smelled mintier than ever. The snail smiled a slow, happy smile.

“You were very brave,” Dot told Pip.

Pip shook water from his whiskers. “Sometimes I felt small,” he said. “Sometimes I slipped. But I tried again. That’s being strong inside.”

Peep chirped, “That’s resilience!”

Pip liked the new word. He repeated it softly, “Re-si-li-ence. It means I keep going, even when it’s hard.”

They all sat in a patch of warm sun. Pip shared a crunchy sunflower seed with Dot and Peep. Crunch, crunch. Their tummies felt full, and their hearts felt full, too.

Pip patted the tomato pot one more time. “We did good today,” he said. “We helped others. We worked as a team. We did not give up.”

Dot buzzed, “And the garden is happy.”

Peep sang a sweet note, “Tweet!”

Pip looked at his friends and the bright, tidy balcony. He felt proud and peaceful. “Next time there’s a storm,” he said, “we’ll be ready to try, try, try again.”

The sun slid across the floor, making warm golden stripes. The leaves whispered, shhh, shhh, like a lullaby. Pip curled his tail and smiled.

“We are strong together,” he whispered.

The End.
