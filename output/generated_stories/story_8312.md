# Leo's Colorful Garden Adventure

Leo the lion cub loved spending time in Mama's home office. The sunny room had big windows that looked out at their beautiful garden. Today, Leo noticed something that made his little heart sad.

"Mama," Leo said, tugging on her paw as she worked at her desk. "The vegetables in our garden look droopy and tired. Some are brown and yucky!"

Mama Lion looked up from her computer and smiled. "Oh my! Let's go see what's happening, little one."

They walked outside together. Leo's nose wrinkled at a strange smell. "Eww! What's that stinky smell, Mama?"

"It smells like someone threw trash near our garden," Mama said, pointing to some plastic bottles and food wrappers scattered by the fence. "Trash can hurt our plants and make them sick."

Leo's golden eyes grew wide. "That's not fair! Our vegetables work so hard to grow big and strong to help us be healthy!"

"You're absolutely right, Leo," Mama said proudly. "Vegetables give us vitamins and minerals to help our bodies grow. Carrots help our eyes see better, and spinach makes our muscles strong like yours!"

Leo flexed his tiny muscles and roared softly. "I want to help our vegetable friends!"

Together, they cleaned up all the trash. Leo carefully picked up each piece with his little paws. "This bottle can be recycled," he said, remembering what Mama taught him. "And this wrapper goes in the trash can where it belongs."

After cleaning, they watered the plants with fresh, cool water. Leo giggled as the water droplets sparkled in the sunshine like tiny diamonds.

"Look, Mama! The tomatoes are getting redder already!" Leo pointed excitedly.

"Plants are amazing," Mama agreed. "When we take care of them, they take care of us by growing nutritious food."

The next day, Leo invited his friend Zara the zebra to see their garden. Zara looked different from Leo with her black and white stripes, but Leo thought she was wonderful just the way she was.

"Wow!" Zara said, clapping her hooves. "Your vegetables look so healthy and colorful!"

"We cleaned up the trash that was making them sick," Leo explained proudly. "Now they can grow big and strong!"

"Can I help too?" Zara asked hopefully.

"Of course!" Leo smiled. "Everyone deserves to have healthy food and a clean place to live."

They spent the morning planting new seeds together. Leo showed Zara how to make little holes in the soft, dark soil that smelled like rain. They planted rainbow carrots, leafy green lettuce, and bright yellow bell peppers.

"These vegetables will have so many beautiful colors!" Zara exclaimed.

"And each color gives us different vitamins," Leo added wisely. "Orange carrots, green spinach, red tomatoes - eating lots of colors helps our bodies stay strong!"

When Mama called them for lunch, they washed their paws and sat down to a meal full of fresh vegetables from their garden. The tomatoes tasted sweet and juicy, and the lettuce was crisp and fresh.

"Thank you for helping our garden, Leo," Mama said, giving him a warm hug. "You showed that taking care of our environment helps everyone - plants, animals, and us!"

Leo beamed with pride. He learned that being brave meant standing up for what's right, even for plants that couldn't speak for themselves. Taking care of the earth and eating healthy foods made everyone stronger and happier.

The End.
