# Toby Turtle's Musical Picnic

Toby Turtle hid behind the big flour jar in the pantry. His green shell felt safe in the dark, quiet space. Today, his family was going to the park for a musical picnic. But Toby felt scared.

"I don't want to go," Toby whispered to himself. His little head peeked out just a tiny bit. "There will be so many animals there. They'll all be singing and dancing. I'm too shy."

Mama Turtle's voice echoed from the kitchen. "Toby! Time to get ready for our picnic!"

Toby pulled his head deeper into his shell. The pantry smelled like sweet cookies and warm bread. It felt much safer than the big, open park.

"Toby?" Papa Turtle's gentle voice came closer. "Are you in the pantry again?"

Papa's kind eyes appeared around the flour jar. "What's wrong, little one?"

"The musical picnic sounds scary," Toby said softly. "Everyone will be making music. I don't know any songs. What if they laugh at me?"

Papa sat down next to Toby. The wooden pantry floor creaked softly. "Would it help if we practiced first? Preparation makes everything easier."

Toby's eyes grew curious. "Practice here?"

"Of course!" Papa smiled. He picked up two wooden spoons from a nearby shelf. "Let's make some music right now!"

Papa tapped the spoons gently on an empty tin can. *Tap, tap, tap!* The sound was soft and friendly.

"That's pretty," Toby said, his head coming out a little more.

"Your turn," Papa handed Toby the spoons. "Just try."

Toby held the spoons carefully. He tapped them once. *Tap!* Then again. *Tap, tap!* The sound made him smile.

"I did it!" Toby giggled. "It sounds like raindrops!"

"Wonderful!" Papa clapped. "Now let's try humming."

Papa hummed a simple tune. "Hmm, hmm, hmm." It sounded like a gentle breeze.

Toby tried humming too. His voice was quiet but sweet. "Hmm, hmm."

"Beautiful!" Papa said. "You're already making music!"

They practiced together in the cozy pantry. Toby tapped rhythms on different containers. Empty boxes made deep sounds. Small jars made high, tinkling notes. The pantry became their own little concert hall.

"I know a song now!" Toby said proudly. He felt his confidence growing like a warm feeling in his chest.

"Are you ready for the picnic?" Papa asked.

Toby thought for a moment. The pantry still felt safe and comfortable. But now he had something special to share. "Yes! I want to show everyone my music!"

At the park, Toby felt nervous again when he saw all the animals. But he remembered his practice time. He had prepared!

A friendly rabbit approached. "Hi! I'm Ruby. Want to make music with us?"

Toby's shell felt tight, but he remembered his spoons. "I know how to make rain sounds," he said quietly.

"Show us!" Ruby said excitedly.

Toby tapped his spoons softly. *Tap, tap, tap!* Just like in the pantry.

"That's amazing!" said a cheerful squirrel. "It really sounds like rain!"

Soon, all the animals were making music together. Toby's rain sounds mixed with Ruby's humming and the squirrel's leaf-rustling. The music filled the sunny park with joy.

"I'm so glad I prepared," Toby thought happily. His shell felt comfortable now, not tight with worry.

When it was time to go home, Toby didn't want to leave. "Can we come back next week?" he asked.

"Of course," Mama said, hugging him gently. "You were so brave today."

That night, Toby visited the pantry one more time. But now it felt different. It wasn't just a hiding place anymore. It was where he had learned that preparing and practicing could help him feel confident anywhere.

"Thank you, pantry," Toby whispered. "You helped me get ready for my big adventure."

The End.
