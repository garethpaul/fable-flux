# Benny Beaver’s Big Share Day

Benny the busy beaver had a cozy home office by the river. His desk was made of smooth sticks. His chair was soft and squeaky. Sunlight painted golden stripes on the floor. The room smelled like fresh wood and crayons.

“Tap, tap, tap,” went Benny’s little paws on the keyboard. “Squeak, squeak,” went his chair. Today, Benny wanted to make a big story book with pictures. He wanted it to be the best story book ever.

Benny had a jar of bright crayons—red, blue, green, and glittery gold. He also had a stack of thick paper and a glue stick that smelled like sweet apples. He puffed his cheeks and smiled. “I will write and draw all by myself,” he said.

Just then, there was a gentle knock. Tap, tap.

“Come in,” Benny called.

His friends peeked in: Dot the duck with soft, fluffy feathers, and Tilly the turtle with a shiny, cool shell. They carried a small basket.

“Hi, Benny!” quacked Dot. “We brought snacks!”

“Crunchy carrot sticks,” said Tilly, holding one up. “Crunch, crunch.”

Benny wiggled his tail. “Hi! I’m busy. I’m making a story book. It will have pictures and a brave hero.”

Dot peeked at the crayons. “Ooh! Can we help?”

Tilly smiled slowly. “I can make a page about a river.”

Benny hugged his crayons close. He felt a tiny pinch in his tummy. “Um… I want to do it all myself,” he said. “My story. My art.”

Dot’s smile got small. Tilly’s head dipped a little. The room felt quiet. The sunlight stripes looked thinner.

Dot set the basket on the desk. “We can eat, and then we can go,” she quacked softly.

Benny nodded, but his heart felt heavy. He looked at the crayons. He looked at his friends. He saw Dot’s feathers ruffle. He saw Tilly’s slow blink. He heard the river outside go shhh, shhh, like a whisper.

Benny picked up a blue crayon. He drew a wavy river. But the line felt wobbly. He tried red for a sunset, but his paw paused.

He listened to his feelings. His tummy pinch grew. He remembered a time when Tilly shared her shiny stone, and a time when Dot shared her umbrella in the rain. Sharing made him feel warm then.

Benny took a deep breath. He liked that warm feeling.

“Wait,” he said. “I changed my mind.” His voice was bright again. “Sharing makes stories bigger. Sharing makes art brighter. Will you please help me?”

Dot’s smile grew wide. “Quack-yes!”

Tilly’s eyes sparkled. “I would like that.”

Benny scooted his chair. “Let’s make this story together.” He tapped the desk. “We can share crayons, paper, and ideas.”

They washed their paws and flippers. They crunched carrots together. Crunch, crunch. The room smelled yummy and friendly.

Benny handed Dot the glittery gold crayon. “You can use the gold.”

Dot gasped. “For me?”

“For us,” Benny said. “We are sharing.”

Tilly opened the basket. “I brought star stickers.” She slid them to the middle. “We can share these, too.”

Benny grinned. “Thank you! Let’s begin.”

They planned the story. Benny asked, “Who is the hero?”

Dot fluffed her feathers. “A brave beaver!”

Tilly tapped her shell. “With helpful friends.”

Benny giggled. “Perfect.”

They drew together. Benny drew a beaver with bright eyes. Dot drew a glowing sun with the gold crayon. “Shine, shine,” she sang.

Tilly drew a calm river. “Whoosh,” she whispered, moving her crayon slow and steady.

They added stickers. Pop, pop, pop! Little stars landed on the page. They glued leaves for trees. The glue smelled like apples again.

Benny wrote simple words under the pictures. “Beaver shares. Friends share. We make art. We tell stories.”

They took turns telling lines of the tale.

Benny said, “Once there was a busy beaver.”

Dot said, “He learned to share his crayons.”

Tilly said, “And his heart felt light.”

They clapped. Clap, clap, clap!

Benny noticed something. The sun looked brighter because Dot drew it. The river looked smoother because Tilly drew it. His beaver looked happier because he was not alone.

He whispered, “Sharing makes more magic.”

Dot nodded. “When we share, we all shine.”

Tilly added, “Sharing turns ‘mine’ into ‘ours.’”

Benny felt warm and proud. The sunlight stripes looked wide again. The room buzzed with happy energy. He could hear the soft rustle of paper and the gentle shhh of the river. The crayons felt smooth in his paw. The gold crayon left sparkly dust on his fingers. He didn’t mind at all.

They made a cover page together. Benny wrote the title: Our Big Book. He drew a heart. Dot added a star. Tilly added a leaf.

Benny looked at his friends. “Thank you for helping. Thank you for sharing.”

Dot quacked, “Thank you for sharing, too!”

Tilly smiled. “Sharing makes friends closer.”

They set the book on the desk to dry. Benny passed around the last carrot sticks. “Crunch for us all,” he said.

Crunch, crunch, crunch.

Benny took a deep breath. His tummy felt calm, not pinchy. His heart felt as warm as the sun. He learned something big in his home office today: Sharing makes stories better, art brighter, and friends closer.

And from that day on, Benny always kept a space at his desk and a crayon to share.

The End.
