# Tiko the Turtle and the Treehouse Clock

Tiko was a shy turtle with a smooth, green shell. He lived near a tall tree with a cozy treehouse. The treehouse smelled like pine and sunshine. Inside, there were soft pillows, a tiny drum, a bright paper lantern, and a little clock that went tick-tock, tick-tock.

Today was a busy day. Tiko and his friends planned a Sharing Songs Party in the treehouse. Each friend would share a song from their home. There would be claps, taps, and gentle humming. Tiko loved songs, but he moved slowly and felt shy when many friends watched him.

Tiko took a deep breath. “I can do it,” he whispered. He looked at the clock. The big hand pointed up. The little hand pointed to two. “Party starts at three,” Tiko said. “I have time. I need to get ready.”

He made a list on a little leaf with a stubby pencil:
- Sweep the floor
- Hang the lantern
- Set the snack bowls
- Practice my tiny drum song

Tiko smiled at his list. The treehouse was quiet. A soft breeze kissed his cheeks. Leaves whispered. Birds chirped sweet notes.

“First, sweep the floor,” he said. The broom was taller than Tiko. It smelled like straw. Swish, swish. Dust danced in the sunbeams. Tiko watched the sparkly dust. It was pretty.

Tick-tock, tick-tock.

Tiko peeked at the clock. “Still time,” he said.

He saw a shiny pebble by the window. “Oh! Shiny!” He rolled it with his foot. Roll, roll. The pebble made a tiny click on the floor. Tiko smiled and rolled it again.

Tick-tock, tick-tock.

He looked up. “Oh no! I stopped sweeping!” He blushed and tucked his head a little into his shell. “It’s okay,” he told himself. “Back to the list.”

Swish, swish. The floor was clean. Tiko put the broom away.

“Next, hang the lantern,” he said. The paper lantern was red and gold. It had little painted fish that looked like they were swimming. It smelled like paper and paste. Tiko stood on a step stool. His legs felt wobbly.

“I can ask for help,” Tiko whispered. But he was shy.

Just then, his friend Ami the squirrel peeked in. “Hi, Tiko!” Ami chattered. Her tail brushed the doorway like a feather. “Need a hand?”

Tiko nodded. “Yes, please.”

Ami held the stool. Tiko hung the lantern. They stepped back. The lantern glowed warm and bright.

Tick-tock, tick-tock.

Tiko checked the clock. “Half past two,” he said. “We are doing well.” His cheeks felt warm, but he smiled. He crossed off two items on his leaf list.

“Next, snack bowls,” Tiko said. He set bowls of sweet apple slices, crunchy carrots, and soft bread. The apples smelled like flowers. The bread smelled toasty.

“Can I help?” asked Leo the rabbit, hopping in with gentle feet. He wore a small blue scarf from his grandma.

“Yes, please,” said Tiko. “Put the cups by the water jug.”

Leo lined up cups in a neat row. “We’re a good team,” he said.

Tick-tock, tick-tock.

Tiko and Leo looked at the clock. “Almost three,” said Tiko. His stomach fluttered. “One more thing. Practice my tiny drum song.”

Tiko picked up his drum. It was small and round. The skin felt smooth. He tapped softly. Tap-tap, tap-tap. His shell vibrated with each beat. He had a special rhythm from his family. It sounded like waves: tap-tap, pause, tap-tap-tap.

Ami clapped to the beat. Leo hummed along. “Your song is beautiful,” Ami said. “It sounds like the river.”

Tiko took another breath. “Thank you,” he said. He checked his list. Everything was done. He felt proud. He felt ready.

Friends arrived. Nia the bird brought a bright scarf from her aunt. It had tiny bells that jingled. Jae the raccoon brought a drum from his cousin. It was painted with stars. The treehouse filled with happy voices. There were soft hellos and warm smiles.

Tiko rang a small bell. “Welcome,” he said. His voice was quiet, but steady. “We will share songs from our homes. We will take turns. We will listen and cheer.”

Ami went first. She sang a fast clicking song that sounded like acorns. Then Leo sang a gentle song his grandma taught him. It made everyone sway. Nia shook her scarf, and the tiny bells chimed. Jae played his star drum with a bright, bouncy beat.

Finally, Tiko held his tiny drum. He glanced at the clock. “Right on time,” he whispered. Tap-tap, pause, tap-tap-tap. The rhythm rolled like water over stones. The lantern glowed. The room smelled like apples and warm wood. Everyone listened. Some closed their eyes. When Tiko finished, his friends cheered softly so he wouldn’t feel shy.

“You did it!” Ami said.

“I did it,” Tiko said, surprised and happy.

They shared snacks. They talked about their songs. Each song was special. Each song came from a different home. Tiko felt the treehouse was full of love and learning.

Ami looked at the list on the leaf. “This list helped,” she said.

Tiko nodded. “The list and the clock helped me use my time. When I got distracted, I looked back at the list. When I felt slow, my friends helped. We did it together.”

As the sun dipped low, the lantern still glowed. Tiko put a tiny check next to each task. He tucked the leaf into his shell as a memory.

Today, Tiko learned that making a plan, watching the time, and asking friends for help made big jobs feel small. He learned that everyone’s songs matter. With time, teamwork, and care, shy hearts can shine.

The End.
