# Pippa Penguin and the Big Bathroom Helper Day

Pippa was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a yellow scarf. She loved to sing little songs. Today, Pippa waddled into the big, warm bathroom at the community center. The floor tiles were cool on her feet. Drip, drip went a shiny faucet. Splash, splash went the bubbles in a tub. The bathroom smelled like minty soap and orange spray.

Pippa looked around and smiled. “Today is Helper Day!” she chirped. “I want to help my friends and make our bathroom shiny and safe.”

But when Pippa peeked into the stalls, she saw a problem. There were paper towels on the floor. A soap bottle was empty. The faucet was dripping, drip-drip-drip. The mirror had tiny water spots like stars.

Pippa took a brave deep breath. “I can do this,” she said. “Helping is kind. Helping is brave.”

Then she heard a squeak. “I’m scared of the hand dryer,” whispered Momo Mouse, peeking from behind a trash bin. “It goes whoooooosh!”

“It is loud,” said Pippa gently. She patted Momo with a soft wing. “But we can make a plan together. We can decide what to do. We can help each other.”

Momo’s whiskers twitched. “What’s the plan?”

Pippa thought. She tapped her beak. She listened to the drip, drip. “First, we make the bathroom clean. That helps everyone. Then we fix the drip so we don’t waste water. Then we can take turns with the hand dryer, and we can make it less scary.”

“Okay,” said Momo, standing a little taller.

Pippa waddled to the sink. She turned the handle and listened. Drip. She turned it snug. The drip stopped. “Good decision!” Pippa said. “Water is precious.” She smiled at Momo. “One problem solved.”

Next, Pippa picked up paper towels with a little grabber stick. Swipe, swoop, swish! Momo held the bag open. “We are a good team,” said Momo.

“We are being helpers,” said Pippa. “This is community service. We help our whole penguin-and-mouse family!”

They wiped the mirror, round and round, until it shone. Pippa spritzed orange spray. The bathroom smelled bright and happy. “Shiny!” cheered Momo.

Just then, Tilly Turtle poked her head in. “I slipped on a splash yesterday,” she said. “I am worried.”

“We are fixing that,” said Pippa. “Look, the floor is dry now.” She placed a yellow “Wet Floor” sign while she finished drying a corner. “Safety first!”

Tilly smiled. “Thank you. Can I help?”

“Yes!” said Pippa. “Can you fill the soap bottle?” Tilly carefully poured pink soap. Glug, glug, glug. The bottle was full. Pippa pressed the pump. Squish! “Perfect.”

Then the hand dryer hummed. Whoooooosh! Momo squeaked and jumped.

Pippa put a wing around Momo. “Let’s use brave breaths. In… out…” They breathed slow. “We can make a deal with the dryer,” Pippa said. “We can negotiate.”

“What does that mean?” asked Momo.

“It means we make a plan that is fair,” said Pippa. “We will take turns. We will count. We will choose a signal.”

Momo nodded. “Okay.”

They talked kindly with Tilly. “We will turn the dryer on for three counts,” said Pippa. “If Momo wants to stop, Momo can wave a tail. Then we stop. Then we try again.”

“That sounds fair,” said Tilly.

Pippa pressed the button. Whoooooosh! The air felt warm, like a summer hug. Pippa counted, “One… two… three!” She turned it off. Momo did not wave. Momo wiggled fingers. “It was loud, but I am okay,” Momo said.

“Brave mouse!” said Pippa. “Want to try again?”

Momo nodded. “Yes. This time, I will stand a little farther.” Pippa and Tilly stepped back with Momo. The sound felt softer. They tried again. “One… two… three!” Off. Momo smiled. “I did it!”

“You used brave breaths and good decisions,” said Pippa. “We made a plan together. That is smart and kind.”

Soon, more friends came in. Benny Bear needed soap. “There is plenty,” said Pippa. “Please pump once. Then rub, rub, rub, and rinse.” She showed healthy habits. “Scrub your paws, tops and bottoms, and between the claws. Sing a short song!” Pippa sang, “Bubbles here, bubbles there,” and everyone giggled and scrubbed.

Lulu Lion cub asked, “Who cleaned this? It looks so nice.”

“We did,” said Pippa. “We are helpers for our community. We take care of shared places.”

“Can I help too?” asked Lulu.

“Yes,” said Pippa. “You can check the paper towels.” Lulu refilled the dispenser. Click, click.

When they were done, the bathroom was bright and safe. The mirror was clear. The floor was dry. The faucet was quiet. The soap was full. The hand dryer felt friendly.

Pippa waddled to the door and put up a little sign: “Helper Day—Thank you for keeping our bathroom clean!” She turned to her friends. “We were brave. We made good choices. We used kind words. We helped our whole community.”

Momo took Pippa’s wing. “I’m not scared of the whoosh now. I can take a brave breath and ask for a fair plan.”

Tilly nodded. “And I won’t slip. We all helped.”

Pippa’s eyes sparkled. “Helping feels good inside. When we care for places we share, everyone smiles.”

They all smiled. The bathroom smelled like mint and orange and happy helpers.

The End.
