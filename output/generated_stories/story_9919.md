# Spark the Robot Dog and the Cozy Closet Plan

Spark was a helpful robot dog with shiny silver ears and a soft, waggy tail that went whirr-whirr when he was happy. He had bright blue eyes that blinked with tiny lights. Spark lived with Maya, who loved to make forts and read books.

One rainy afternoon, Maya said, “Spark, let’s make a secret hideaway in the closet!” The closet was small and quiet. It smelled like clean towels and a little bit like lavender. It felt cozy, like a hug.

Spark wagged. Whirr-whirr. “Woof-beep! Hideaway time!”

They carried blankets and pillows to the closet. The hallway light made a warm glow. Tap-tap, went Spark’s metal paws on the floor. Maya stacked soft pillows, and Spark pulled a blanket with his gentle robot teeth.

Inside the closet, it was dark and snug. Maya clicked on a little lamp. A golden circle of light filled the space. “Perfect,” Maya whispered. “Let’s read.”

But when Maya reached for her favorite picture book—uh-oh—it was stuck behind a big box of shoes. The box was heavy. It made a thump when Maya tried to move it. The book’s bright red cover peeked out, but wouldn’t budge.

“I can’t get it,” Maya said. Her eyebrows squished together. Her cheeks felt hot. Her hands squeezed into fists. “This is not fair!” She huffed. “I want it now!”

Spark’s ears twitched. He heard her frustration. He blinked his lights and sat down. Whirr. “Woof-beep. Let’s think.”

Maya crossed her arms. “I just want the book.” A little tear poked at her eye. The closet felt smaller now.

Spark took a slow, pretend breath. “In… out,” he said gently. He wagged. “We can calm. We can solve.”

Maya looked at Spark’s friendly face. She breathed with him. “In… out.” Her shoulders softened. “Okay,” she said. “Let’s try.”

Spark tapped his paw. Tap-tap. “Step one: Name the problem.”

Maya sniffled and nodded. “The book is stuck behind the heavy shoe box.”

“Step two: Think of ideas,” Spark said. His tail went whirr-whirr. “Idea one: I push the box.”

Maya tried to push with Spark. The box scraped the floor. Scrrrr. It moved a little, but not enough.

“Idea two,” Spark said. “We ask for help.”

Maya peered out of the closet. “Mama is in a meeting,” she whispered. “We have to wait if we ask her now.”

Spark blinked. “Waiting can be hard.”

Maya nodded. “Yes. Waiting makes me grumpy.”

“Idea three,” Spark said. “We make a trade with the box.”

Maya giggled a little. “How do we trade with a box?”

“Negotiation,” Spark said, wagging. “We give the box room. The box gives the book back.”

Maya tilted her head. “We can move something else to make space?”

“Smart idea,” Spark said. “Step three: Choose the best idea.”

Maya looked around. There was a small basket of scarves beside the box. The basket was light. “We can move the scarves,” she said.

Spark nodded. “Let’s do it together.”

They lifted the scarf basket out. The scarves felt silky and smooth. They whispered shhhh as they slid over the edge. Now there was a little space in front of the shoe box.

“Try again?” Maya asked.

“Team push,” Spark said.

They pushed slowly. Scrrrr. The box moved more this time. Spark’s motor hummed. Maya’s hands pressed steady. “We did it!” Maya said. The red book slipped free with a soft fwip.

Maya hugged Spark. His metal fur was cool and smooth. “Thank you, Spark. We solved it!”

Spark wagged. Whirr-whirr. “You calmed down. You made a plan. You worked with me. That is big-smart.”

Maya smiled. “I was mad, but I breathed. Then we used steps.”

Spark nodded. “Step one: Name the problem. Step two: Think of ideas. Step three: Choose the best. Step four: Try it slow. Step five: Ask for help if needed.”

Maya held up the book. “Let’s read in our cozy closet.”

They settled on the pillows. The lamp made the pages glow. Rain tapped softly on the window outside. Tap-tap-tap. The closet smelled warm and safe. Maya’s voice was calm now. She read the story, and Spark listened, ears perked, tail humming.

After the story, Maya said, “Spark, what if Mama needs the shoe box later? We moved it.”

Spark blinked. “We can make a fair trade. We can put a note.”

Maya grabbed a sticky note and a crayon. She drew a smiling box and wrote, with careful letters, “We moved this a little. Love, Maya.” She stuck it on the box. It made a tiny pap sound.

“That is good negotiation,” Spark said. “We told the plan. We were kind.”

Maya nodded. “We did not just take. We shared our idea.”

Spark lay his head on Maya’s knee. “When we feel stuck, we can calm, think, and talk.”

Maya patted his shiny head. “And we can be gentle with our feelings.”

They snuggled into the soft blankets. The closet felt just right again. Maya felt proud. Spark’s lights glowed soft and blue. The rain sang outside, like a quiet drum.

“I can solve problems,” Maya whispered.

“Woof-beep,” Spark replied. “Yes, you can.”

The End.
