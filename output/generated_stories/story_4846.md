# Leo's Treehouse Adventure

High up in a big oak tree, there lived a brave little lion cub named Leo. His treehouse was cozy and warm, with soft yellow cushions and windows that sparkled in the sunshine. Leo loved looking out at the forest below, watching the birds fly and the squirrels play.

One sunny morning, Leo heard a tiny voice crying, "Help! Help!" He poked his fluffy golden head out the window and saw a little blue bird sitting on a branch. Her wing was tangled in a piece of plastic string.

"Oh no!" said Leo. "Don't worry, little bird. I'll help you!"

Leo climbed down from his treehouse, his paws gripping the rough bark carefully. The bird looked scared when he came closer.

"Please don't hurt me," chirped the bird softly.

"I would never hurt you," Leo said gently. "My name is Leo. What's yours?"

"I'm Bella," said the bird. "I was building my nest when this yucky string got wrapped around my wing. I can't fly!"

Leo could see that Bella was frightened. He sat down a little distance away so she wouldn't feel scared. "Bella, I want to help you, but I need to come close to untangle the string. Is that okay with you?"

Bella thought for a moment. "Yes, but please be very gentle."

"I promise," said Leo. He moved slowly and carefully, using his small claws to gently loosen the plastic string. "This string doesn't belong in our forest," he said. "It's trash that can hurt animals like you."

As Leo worked, he talked softly to keep Bella calm. "There, almost got it... just a little more..." Finally, the string came free!

Bella flapped her wing happily. "Thank you, Leo! You're so kind!"

But Leo noticed something else. The forest floor was covered with more plastic bottles, wrappers, and strings. "Look at all this trash, Bella. It's making our beautiful forest dirty and dangerous."

"You're right," said Bella sadly. "Just yesterday, my friend got his foot stuck in a can."

Leo's eyes lit up with an idea. "What if we work together to clean up our forest? But only if you want to help."

"I'd love to help!" chirped Bella excitedly.

Leo climbed back up to his treehouse and brought down a big basket. Together, they spent the morning picking up trash. Leo collected the bigger pieces while Bella flew around pointing out smaller bits with her sharp eyes.

"Look how beautiful our forest looks now!" said Bella, perching on Leo's shoulder.

Leo smiled proudly. The forest floor was clean, and the flowers seemed to shine brighter. "We make a great team, Bella. Thank you for letting me help you, and thank you for helping me clean our home."

"Thank you for being so gentle and asking permission first," said Bella. "It made me feel safe."

From that day on, Leo and Bella became the best of friends. They met every morning to keep their forest clean and help any animals who needed it. Leo learned that helping others feels wonderful, especially when you're kind and respectful.

And whenever Leo saw trash in the forest, he remembered to clean it up right away, because he knew that taking care of their environment meant taking care of all their forest friends.

The End.
