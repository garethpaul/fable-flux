# The Pantry Picnic

Little Lio was a brave lion cub with soft golden fur and bright, curious eyes. One rainy morning, he padded into the pantry. The pantry was cool and cozy. It smelled like cinnamon and apples. Shelves stood tall, holding foods from many places.

Lio’s tummy rumbled. “I want a snack now,” he said. He looked up at a jar of peanut butter. He looked at a bag of rice. He saw beans, oats, and a shiny can of peaches. He saw noodles from Asia, spicy beans from Mexico, olive oil from Spain, and dates from Africa. The labels had little flags and happy pictures.

Lio tried to jump. “Up I go!” he roared softly. His paws slipped on the smooth floor. Clunk. He landed on his bottom. “Ow.” His whiskers drooped.

Just then, his friends peeped in. Tari the turtle wore a tiny blue scarf. Pippa the parrot fluttered her green wings. Nuru the mouse wrinkled his pink nose.

“What’s wrong, Lio?” asked Tari in a calm, slow voice.

“I want a snack now,” said Lio. “But I can’t reach. I am brave. I want to do it fast.”

Pippa tilted her head. “Fast is not always best,” she chirped. “Let’s use patience.”

Nuru squeaked, “We can make a plan. We can work as a team.”

Lio huffed. He wanted to roar. But he took a long, slow breath. In. Out. He remembered what Grandma Lion said: “Patience is brave waiting with a plan.”

“Okay,” Lio said. “Let’s wait. Let’s plan.”

They looked around. The pantry shelves were tall like trees. The jars shone like little suns. The rain tapped on the window, tap-tap-tap.

“What snack should we make?” asked Pippa.

“Something strong and kind,” said Tari. “Food that helps us grow.”

“We can share,” said Nuru. “A global pantry picnic!”

Lio’s eyes sparkled. “Yes! We can taste foods from many places. We can learn and be kind to the world.”

Tari smiled. “First, we ask for help, and we wait our turn. That is patience.”

They made a list:
- Oats from Scotland for warm bellies
- Beans from Mexico for strong muscles
- Peaches from a farm nearby for sweet smiles
- Noodles from Asia for long energy
- Olive oil from Spain for healthy hearts
- Dates from Africa for chewy joy

Nuru pointed to a low shelf. “We can reach oats and beans here. Safe and easy.”

“Patience step one,” said Pippa. “Use what we can reach.”

They each carried a small piece. Lio took the oats. Tari nudged the bean bag slowly. Pippa held the light box of noodles in her claws. Nuru rolled the can of peaches carefully. Clink, clink, clink.

Lio looked up at the olive oil and dates on a high shelf. His paws itched to jump again. He felt the hurry rise up like a growl. He stopped. He placed his paw over his heart. In. Out. Patience.

“I will wait,” he said. “We can ask Big Bear for help.”

They called, “Big Bear!” Big Bear worked in the kitchen. He had gentle paws and a warm laugh.

Big Bear opened the door. The smell of soup followed him, cozy and good. “Hello, little friends,” he said. “What do you need?”

“We are making a global pantry picnic,” said Lio. “We chose healthy foods. We are working as a team. We are being patient. Can you help us reach the high shelf?”

Big Bear beamed. “What a smart, kind plan. Yes, I can help.” He reached up and handed down the olive oil and dates.

“Thank you,” said all the friends together.

They set up a little mat on the pantry floor. Rain still tap-tapped. It sounded like a drum. The light was soft and golden.

Pippa read the labels. “Oats give us fiber. That helps tummies.”

“Beans give us protein,” said Tari. “Strong shells and strong paws!”

“Peaches give vitamins,” squeaked Nuru. “They help eyes and smiles.”

“Olive oil is heart-healthy,” said Big Bear. “Dates are natural sweets.”

Lio licked his nose. “We will taste a little of each,” he said. “We will share. We will say thank you to the farmers around the world.”

They made simple bowls. Warm oats with peach slices. Beans and noodles with a tiny drop of olive oil. A date cut in four to share. They stirred, sniffed, and listened. The oats were creamy. The peaches were juicy. The beans were soft. The noodles were slippery. The date was sticky and sweet. Mmm!

“This is a kind meal,” said Pippa. “Kind to our bodies and kind to the world.”

Lio felt his tummy fill. He also felt something new. Calm. Happy. Proud. “Waiting was hard,” he said. “But patience made it better. We made a plan. We asked for help. We shared.”

Big Bear nodded. “Patience is brave,” he said. “And you were all brave today.”

Lio smiled and wrapped his tail around his paws. “Next time I feel hurry in my chest,” he said, “I will breathe, wait, and make a plan.”

Tari patted Lio’s paw. “That is the lesson.”

Pippa chirped, “We can take turns, share food, and thank the world.”

Nuru squeaked, “Teamwork makes waiting sweet!”

The rain slowed to a quiet drip. The pantry felt warm and safe. Lio looked at his friends and the foods from many places. His heart felt big.

“Thank you, patience,” Lio whispered.

The End.
