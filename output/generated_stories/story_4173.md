# Tita Tortoise Tidies the Kitchen

In a warm kitchen, the air smelled like cinnamon toast and sweet apples. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. A wise tortoise named Tita moved slowly and softly. Her shell was smooth and green, and her eyes were kind.

“Good morning, kitchen,” Tita said. “Today I will tidy and recycle.” Her voice was calm and happy.

Tita looked around. There were empty yogurt cups, a clean glass jar, a cardboard box from cereal, and some crinkly plastic. There were also soft paper towels and a banana peel in a little bowl.

“Tidy time,” Tita whispered. “One task at a time. I can do it.”

She slid to the sink. Drip, drip, drip. The water sounded like a tiny song. Tita washed the yogurt cups. Swish, swish. “Clean for the blue bin,” she said. “Blue bin is for recycling.” She looked at the bin with the blue lid. It smiled back at her in her mind.

A small mouse peeked in from the doorway. “Hi, Tita,” squeaked the mouse. “What are you doing?”

“I am recycling,” said Tita. “Recycling means we use things again. We do not waste. We help the Earth.”

The mouse sniffed the air. “It smells warm and nice,” he said. “Like toast!”

“Yes,” said Tita, smiling. “The kitchen is cozy. Will you help me complete my tasks?”

“Yes!” said the mouse.

Tita made a little song. “Sort, sort, sort. One, two, three. Clean and kind for Earth and me.”

She held up the glass jar. It caught the sun and sparkled. “Glass goes to the blue bin,” she said. “Plink!” The jar went into the bin with a soft sound.

She picked up the cardboard box. It felt light and papery. She folded it flat. “Cardboard also goes to the blue bin,” she said. “Fold and flatten.” She pressed it gently. “Crunch.” Into the bin it went.

The mouse touched the crinkly plastic. “What about this?” he asked.

Tita nodded. “Plastic can be tricky,” she said. “This thin plastic goes in the trash. But we choose less next time. That is smart.”

The mouse nodded. “Less plastic next time,” he repeated.

Tita pointed to the banana peel and the soft paper towels. “These can go to compost,” she said. “Compost is for old food and paper. It makes new soil. The soil helps plants. We help life grow.”

They opened a small green bin. It smelled like orange peels and fresh dirt. The mouse wrinkled his nose, then smiled. “It smells like a garden,” he said.

“Yes,” said Tita. “A garden is inside the bin, making food for the Earth.”

They worked together. Tita kept her task list in her mind. “Step one: clean. Step two: sort. Step three: put away.” She checked each step with a slow nod.

When the kitchen was tidy, the counters felt smooth under Tita’s feet. The floor looked shiny. The blue bin was not too full, and the green bin was happy in the corner.

The mouse clapped his tiny paws. “We did it! We finished our tasks!”

Tita smiled. “Yes. We completed our tasks by doing a little at a time. Slow and steady is good.”

Then Tita had an idea. She held up an extra clean yogurt cup and some paper from a paper bag. “Let’s be creative,” she said. “We can use these again right now.”

“How?” asked the mouse, eyes wide.

“We can make a crayon cup,” said Tita. She wrapped the cup with the paper bag and tied it with a bit of clean string from the drawer. She drew a little leaf with a green crayon. “Now the crayons have a home.”

The mouse giggled. “I want to make something too!” He took the clean glass jar and put in dry pasta shapes. “Shake, shake,” he said. The jar made a soft rattle. “It is music!”

Tita laughed, a warm, gentle laugh. “That is creative. We recycled by making new things. We also saved the Earth a tiny bit.”

They sat at the table. The table felt warm from the sun. Tita poured water into two small cups. “Sip,” she said. The water was cool and fresh.

The mouse looked proud. “I learned so much,” he said. “Blue bin for recycling. Green bin for compost. Trash only for what cannot be used again. Clean first. Sort. Put away. Then be creative!”

Tita nodded. “You are a good helper. When we recycle, we care for the Earth. When we complete our tasks, we feel calm and happy. When we are creative, we make joy.”

The kitchen hummed with quiet. Somewhere a timer ticked. Tick, tick, tick. The air still smelled like cinnamon, with a tiny hint of orange peel from the compost bin.

“Thank you, wise Tita,” said the mouse.

“Thank you, helpful mouse,” said Tita. “Let’s keep making good choices, one small step at a time.”

They put the new crayon cup on the shelf. They put the pasta shaker next to it. The blue bin and the green bin stood ready. The kitchen was cozy, clean, and bright.

Tita looked around and breathed in. “Today we learned that recycling helps the Earth, completing tasks feels good, and creativity makes new fun from old things,” she said. “We can do this every day.”

The mouse smiled. “Every day,” he echoed.

The End.
