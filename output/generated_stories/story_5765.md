# Rocky Raccoon Hums and Builds

Rocky the musical raccoon loved songs. He wore a tiny blue scarf and kept a little wooden drum by his side. Today, Rocky sat in a comfortable nursery with soft yellow walls, a big window, and squishy rugs that felt like clouds under his paws. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Blocks, books, and plush toys waited like quiet friends.

Rocky tapped his drum with gentle paws. Tap-tap, tap-tap. He hummed a happy tune. “Hmm-hmm, la-la,” he sang. The sound was soft and warm, like a blanket.

His friends were in the nursery too. Mia the mouse stacked colorful blocks. Benji the bunny sorted soft, fuzzy balls by color. Tiko the turtle wiggled slowly toward a basket of scarves.

“I will build a music tower,” Rocky said with a smile. “A tower that hums!”

Mia’s ears perked up. “I like that,” she squeaked. “But I am building a tall castle.”

Benji twitched his nose. “I want a round bunny fort,” he said.

Tiko blinked. “I just want a cozy corner,” he whispered.

Rocky felt excited. He tapped his drum a little faster. Tap-tap-tap! He hummed louder. “La-la-LA!”

Mia looked up. “Rocky,” she said, “your drum is too loud. My hands feel wiggly.”

Benji put down a fuzzy ball. “My ears feel full,” he said.

Tiko pulled his head into his shell. “Too big sound,” he murmured.

Rocky stopped. He felt a tight, twisty feeling in his tummy. “I did not want to bother you,” he said softly. “I just wanted to play and build.”

He put a paw on his chest. He took a slow breath in and out. In…and out. He remembered what Teacher Owl had taught: “When things feel tricky, breathe in, breathe out, and notice.” Rocky closed his eyes for a moment.

“I hear my breath,” Rocky whispered. “It sounds like a soft shhh.” He opened his eyes. The room felt calmer.

“I can be mindful,” he said. “Mindful means I notice my feelings and your feelings too.”

Mia nodded. “I feel better when it’s quiet,” she said.

Benji nodded too. “I can listen when sounds are soft.”

Rocky hugged his drum. “I like music,” he said in a clear, kind voice. “Music helps me feel happy. I will keep my drum, but I can play gently.” He tapped with one finger. Tip… tip. The sound was tiny, like raindrops on leaves.

“That’s better,” Mia said with a smile.

Rocky smiled back. He felt brave for using his voice kindly. “Can we build together?” he asked. “Maybe a music castle-fort-corner?”

Mia giggled. “Yes! Let’s try.”

“Okay!” said Benji. “I’ll bring the fuzzy balls.”

Tiko peeked out. “I can lay the scarves,” he said.

Rocky took another slow breath. He looked around. He listened. He noticed Mia’s careful hands, Benji’s twitchy nose, Tiko’s slow smile. He noticed his own calm heart beat. Thump… thump.

“First,” Rocky said, “let’s make a quiet base.” He set soft blocks on the rug. They made a gentle pat sound. Pat… pat. He hummed a low, soft note. “Hmmmm.”

Mia stacked blocks in a straight line. “Tall and steady,” she said. “One, two, three.”

Benji placed fuzzy balls around the base like little pillows. “Round and cozy,” he said.

Tiko spread the scarves on top like a bright tent. The scarves felt smooth and cool. “Shady and soft,” he said.

Rocky lightly tapped his drum once. Tip. “When the drum goes tip,” he said, “we pause and breathe.”

Mia giggled. “A breathing game!”

Benji bounced, then stopped. “Tip means breathe,” he said.

They worked and played. Tip. They breathed in and out together. The room felt peaceful. The sunlight felt warm on their fur. The scarves smelled like clean cotton. The blocks were cool and smooth under their paws.

Soon the music castle-fort-corner was tall, round, and cozy. It had a soft scarf roof, block walls, and a fuzzy ball path. It looked like a rainbow nest.

Rocky’s tummy felt warm and happy now. “We did it,” he said. “We built it together.”

Just then, two new friends came in. Gigi the goat and Pip the parakeet looked at the tower. Gigi bleated, “Can I climb?”

Pip chirped, “Can I sing loud?”

Rocky put a paw on his chest again. He breathed in and out. He remembered to be mindful. “We can all play,” he said kindly and firmly. “But we must keep our bodies and voices gentle in the nursery.”

Gigi nodded. “I can step softly,” she said.

Pip nodded too. “I can sing softly.” He chirped like a tiny bell. Ting-ting.

Mia smiled. “We can make a gentle song.”

Benji clapped with soft paws. “We can hop quietly.”

Tiko waved a scarf slowly. “We can swish-swish.”

Rocky began a calm, happy tune. “Hmm-hmm,” he hummed. Everyone joined in. The tower stood steady. No one’s ears hurt. Everyone’s hearts felt calm.

Rocky’s eyes shone. “Today I learned that being mindful helps me and helps my friends,” he said. “I can notice my feelings. I can notice your feelings. I can use a gentle voice and ask for what I need.”

Mia said, “I learned to say, ‘Too loud, please.’ That is kind and strong.”

Benji said, “I learned to listen.”

Tiko said, “I learned to go slow.”

Gigi said, “I learned soft steps.”

Pip chirped, “I learned soft songs.”

They all took one more breath together. In…and out. The nursery felt safe, warm, and full of friends.

The End.
