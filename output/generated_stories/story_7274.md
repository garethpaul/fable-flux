# Benny Bunny’s Beat

In a sunny school, down the hall with the bright blue doors, there was a musical practice room. The room smelled like clean wood and orange polish. Soft lights made the floor shine. Drums sat in a neat row. Tiny bells twinkled on a shelf. A big mirror stretched along one wall.

Benny Bunny, a determined rabbit with floppy ears and quick feet, hopped inside. Thump, thump, thump went his paws on the floor. He peered around with curious eyes.

“I want to dance today,” Benny told himself. “I want to learn a new move!”

He set down his little backpack. Inside was a water bottle, a crunchy carrot, and a banana. “Snack for energy,” he said proudly. “Dancers need good food.”

Benny looked at the mirror. He took a deep breath. “I can do it,” he whispered. “One, two, three!”

He tapped his toe. Tap. He swished his paw. Swish. He jumped a tiny jump. Boing!

The drum in the corner looked at him, almost like it was smiling. Benny picked up a small stick and gave the drum a gentle pat. Dum. Dum-dum. The sound was warm and round. It made his whiskers wiggle.

“I like that beat,” Benny said. “Let’s try a hop with the dum-dum!”

He hopped to the dum. He turned to the dum-dum. It felt good. His ears flopped in time. He giggled. “I’m a dancing bunny!”

Suddenly, he heard a clink. A row of jingle bells leaned to the side. “Oh no,” Benny said. “They might fall.”

He didn’t wait for a grown-up. He showed initiative. He set down the stick, scooted over, and carefully stood the bells back up. Jingle-jingle, they sang softly. Benny smiled. “I can help with little chores,” he said. “Keeping the room neat helps me dance.”

He grabbed a soft cloth from a basket and wiped a small dusty spot on the floor. Wipe, wipe. “Safe floor, happy feet,” he said.

Benny took a sip of water. Glug-glug. He nibbled his carrot. Crunch, crunch. “Yum,” he said. “Good fuel helps me move.”

“Okay, feet,” he told himself. “Let’s learn a new step.” He watched his reflection. His nose twitched with curiosity. “What if I make a star with my steps?”

He tapped front. Tap. He tapped side. Tap. He tapped back. Tap. Then he hopped to the middle. Boing! “That looks like a star!” he said, clapping. “Star Step!”

He tried again. Front, side, back, boing! The bells chimed. The drum hummed. Benny’s paws made soft pat-pat sounds on the floor. He felt his heart beat in a happy rhythm.

Then he slipped. Oops! His paw slid a little. He wobbled. “Oh!” He felt a tiny sting of worry in his tummy. “Maybe dancing is too hard for me.”

He sat down, ears drooping. He looked at the mirror. He saw a small rabbit with a brave face.

Benny took a slow breath in. Smell of orange polish. He let the breath out. “I am determined,” he said softly. “I can try again. Little steps help.”

He tied his shoelaces tighter. Loop, swoop, pull. He stood up slowly. He checked the floor with his paw. Smooth. Safe.

“Let’s break it down,” he told himself. “First, just the tap.” Tap. “Good! Now the side.” Tap. “Good! Now the back.” Tap. “Good! Now the hop.” Boing!

He put them together like blocks. Front, side, back, boing! He smiled. “I did it!”

Benny added the drum. Dum for front. Dum for side. Dum for back. Dum-dum for boing! “I like the sound,” he said. “It helps me keep time.”

He tried with the bells. Jingle for the hop! His feet felt light. His ears swished like ribbons. The mirror bunny grinned back.

The door opened a crack. Miss Melody, the music teacher, peeked in. She smelled like lemon tea. “Hello, Benny,” she said softly. “I saw the sign on the schedule. Are you practicing by yourself?”

Benny nodded. “Yes! I made a Star Step. I cleaned a spot, too. And I ate my carrot for energy.”

Miss Melody clapped. Clap, clap. “What wonderful initiative! Show me your Star Step.”

Benny stood tall. “One, two, three, Star Step with me!” he sang.

Front, side, back, boing! Dum, dum, dum, jingle! He landed with steady paws. His whiskers wiggled with pride.

Miss Melody smiled. “Beautiful work. You listened to the beat. You kept the room tidy. And you chose healthy fuel. That’s what dancers do.”

Benny’s chest felt warm and bright. “I kept trying,” he said. “Even when I slipped.”

“That is how we learn,” said Miss Melody. “Would you like to end with a cool-down?”

Benny nodded. They stretched slow. Arms up. Ears long. Sway side to side like tall grass. The room was quiet, except for soft breaths and a friendly clock tick-tick.

When they finished, Benny put the sticks and bells back in place. He wiped a tiny spill from his water. Wipe, wipe. He zipped his backpack and tucked in a shiny red apple. “Snack for later,” he said. “Good nutrition helps me grow.”

Miss Melody gave him a thumbs-up. “See you tomorrow, Benny.”

Benny hopped to the door. Thump, thump. He looked back at the mirror and waved. “Thank you, room,” he whispered. “I learned my Star Step today.”

He felt proud, calm, and ready for the next beat.

The End.
