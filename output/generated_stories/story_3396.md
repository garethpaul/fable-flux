# Roo's Ready-Set-Story Day

Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a bouncy tail. One sunny morning, Roo hopped into the home office where Mama worked. The room felt cozy and busy. The soft hum of the computer sounded like a bee. Papers made a gentle crinkle. Sunlight made warm squares on the carpet.

Roo sniffed the air. “It smells like crayons and tea,” Roo said, wrinkling a tiny nose.

Mama smiled. “It is Story Day on the screen tonight,” she said. “We will tell a story to the family. Are you ready, Roo?”

Roo wiggled. “I want to! I will hop and talk and—oh! I don’t know what to say.”

Roo’s ears drooped. A little worry fluttered in Roo’s tummy like a moth.

Mama scooted her chair and patted the soft rug. “We can get ready. We can build our storytelling skills, step by step. Small steps make strong stories.”

Roo sat and hugged a fuzzy pencil. “How do we start?”

“First, we prepare,” Mama said. “We think of a beginning, a middle, and an end. Let’s use our senses to help.”

Roo nodded. “I can do that!”

They opened a small notebook. The pages smelled like fresh paper. Mama drew three boxes.

“Box one is the beginning,” said Mama. “What do you see or hear at the start?”

Roo looked around. “I see the shiny screen. I hear the hum. I feel the warm carpet. I taste a tiny crumb from my snack.” Roo giggled.

“Great details,” Mama said. She wrote Roo’s words. Scribble, scribble went the pencil.

“Box two is the middle,” said Mama. “What happens next?”

Roo thought. “Maybe the joey is shy. The joey takes a breath. The joey practices a big voice!”

“Wonderful. That is skill-building,” Mama said. “Practice makes brave.”

“Box three is the end,” Mama said. “How does it finish?”

Roo bounced. “The joey tells the story! The family claps. The joey feels proud.”

“Perfect,” Mama said. “Now we practice the parts. We will build our skills like blocks.”

They made a little plan on sticky notes:
- Warm up the voice.
- Use hands to show big and small.
- Look at the camera.
- Speak slow and clear.

Roo tried the voice warm-up. “La-la-la,” Roo sang softly, then louder. The notes felt tickly in Roo’s throat.

“Use your hands,” Mama said.

Roo spread little paws wide. “This is a BIG moon,” Roo said, and then pinched fingers. “This is a tiny star.”

“Look at the camera,” Mama said.

Roo looked at the small circle on the screen. “Hello, circle,” Roo whispered.

“Speak slow and clear,” Mama said. “Let’s try a sentence.”

Roo took a deep breath. The breath felt cool going in, warm going out. “Once upon a time, a joey found a story,” Roo said slowly.

“Beautiful,” Mama said. “You are preparing.”

But later, when the sun made long lines on the floor, Roo’s tummy fluttered again. “What if I forget?” Roo asked.

Mama nodded. “That is a problem. Let’s find a solution. We can make cue cards with tiny pictures.”

They drew a moon for the beginning, a heart for the middle, and a star for the end. The markers smelled sweet and made soft squeaks.

Roo held the cards. “Moon, heart, star,” Roo said. “I can remember.”

Evening came. The room looked golden. The computer chimed. “Ding!” The family faces popped up on the screen. Uncle waved. Grandma blew a kiss. “Are you ready, Roo?” they asked.

Roo’s paws felt warm. Roo looked at the moon card. “I am ready,” Roo said. “I prepared. I practiced. I can do it.”

Roo began. “Once upon a time, in a cozy home office, a little joey heard a hum and saw a shiny screen.” Roo showed BIG with wide paws and tiny with pinchy fingers. Roo looked at the camera circle and spoke slow and clear.

Roo peeked at the heart card. “The joey felt shy,” Roo said. “But the joey took a breath. In… and out…” Roo breathed so everyone could hear a soft whoosh. “And the joey practiced, and the joey grew brave.”

Roo held up the star card. “At the end, the joey told the story. The family clapped and smiled. The joey felt proud and warm inside.”

Grandma clapped. Uncle cheered. Mama gave Roo a hug that smelled like tea and soap. “You did it! How do you feel?”

Roo’s eyes shone. “I feel proud. I used my skills. I prepared. I told my story.”

Mama nodded. “That is the lesson. When we prepare and practice, our skills grow. We can solve problems. We can share our voices.”

Roo tucked the cue cards into the notebook. “Moon, heart, star,” Roo said. “Next time, I will be ready again.”

Roo bounced once, twice, three happy hops. The home office hummed softly, like it was clapping too.

The End.
