# Hops of Calm in the Garage

Pip was a joyful kangaroo joey. He had bright eyes, strong legs, and a happy hop. One sunny morning, Pip bounced into Grandpa Roo’s garage workshop. The garage smelled like fresh wood and orange oil. Tiny dust sparkles danced in the sunbeams. Tools hung on the wall like shiny toys. There were soft brushes, smooth sandpaper, and a big red toolbox that went “click” when it closed.

“I want to help!” Pip said, tail swishing. “I can hop and carry!”

Grandpa Roo smiled. “You are my strong helper. We will make a birdhouse today.”

Pip clapped his little paws. “Tweet home! Tweet home!”

Grandpa Roo gave Pip a small blue hammer. “Tap, tap, gentle,” he said. “We use calm hands.”

Pip took a deep breath. He felt the cool handle. He heard the soft hum of a little fan. Tap, tap… tap—oops! Pip’s tail bumped the red toolbox. It fell open with a loud CLANG. A box of screws spilled like silver rain. They rolled everywhere—ping, ping, ping!

Pip’s eyes went wide. “Oh no!” He felt hot tears coming. His chest felt tight. “I made a mess!”

Grandpa Roo looked at the floor. Screws were under the bench, by the broom, and near the paint cans. Pip’s ears drooped.

“I’m sorry,” Pip whispered. “I didn’t mean to.”

Grandpa Roo put a gentle paw on Pip’s shoulder. “Thank you for saying sorry,” he said. “Accidents happen. We can fix this together.”

Pip sniffed. “But I feel wiggly and jumpy. I don’t feel calm.”

Grandpa Roo nodded. “Let’s try a calm hop. Ready?”

Pip blinked. “A calm hop?”

“Yes,” said Grandpa Roo. “We breathe in, we breathe out, and we hop slow. In… out… hop.”

Pip tried. He breathed in through his nose. He smelled the sweet wood and a lemony cloth. He breathed out through his mouth. He felt the cool air. Hop. Slow and soft. His knees bent gently. His tail moved steady, like a soft broom.

“In… out… hop,” Grandpa Roo said. “Calm body, calm mind.”

Pip felt his heart slow down. The tears went away. “I can do calm hops,” he said with a small smile.

“Good,” said Grandpa Roo. “Now we pick up the screws. This is exercise for our paws and legs.”

They made a game. “Silver Star Hunt!” Pip said. He squatted down and stretched, reaching under the bench. “Stretch and grab!” He felt the cool, smooth screws tickle his paw. Grandpa Roo used a magnet stick that went click-click as it picked up little screws. They both laughed at the funny sound.

“Listen,” said Grandpa Roo. “Ping, ping—little sounds. We can hear them to find the last ones.”

Pip tilted his head. “I hear a ping by the paint!” He hopped, but slow and steady. “In… out… hop.” He found three more screws. “Got them!”

Soon the floor was clean. Pip felt proud. “We fixed the mess!”

Grandpa Roo nodded. “We fixed it together. And Pip, I forgive you.”

Pip looked up. “Forgive?”

“Forgive means I am not mad,” Grandpa Roo said softly. “I know you didn’t mean it. I let the mad feeling go. I keep the love.”

Pip’s chest felt warm and light. “You keep the love,” he repeated. He hugged Grandpa Roo. “Thank you.”

“Now,” Grandpa Roo said, tapping his nose, “shall we try the birdhouse again?”

Pip held the hammer with calm paws. “Tap, tap, gentle,” he said. He breathed in, breathed out, and tapped. The wood felt smooth. The nail went in straight. Grandpa Roo held the pieces. They worked as a team. Pip counted slow taps. “One, two, three.” Tap, tap, tap.

They painted the roof sunny yellow. Pip dabbed with a soft brush. “Swish, swish!” The paint smelled a little sweet. They added a blue door and a tiny perch. Pip wiped a little drip with a rag. “Clean and neat!”

At last, they held up the birdhouse. It glowed in the light. Pip’s eyes sparkled. “We did it!”

Grandpa Roo grinned. “Yes. We used calm, we used exercise, and we used forgiveness.”

Pip nodded. “When I make a mistake, I can say sorry. I can breathe. I can do calm hops. I can fix it. And I can forgive myself, too.”

Grandpa Roo hugged him. “That is a big lesson for a small joey.”

They carried the birdhouse outside and hung it on a low branch. Birds chirped, “Tweet! Tweet!” The air was warm and soft. Pip did a happy hop—still calm, still steady.

“In… out… hop,” he said, smiling. “I keep the love.”

The End.
