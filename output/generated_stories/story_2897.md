# Joey’s Tidy Treasure Day

Joey the kangaroo joey bounced into the comfortable nursery with a happy thump-thump, thump-thump. The room felt warm and cozy. Sunlight danced on the soft rug. The air smelled like crayons and clean cotton. Joey’s ears perked up. “I’m ready for a big day!” he chirped.

Teacher Mira smiled. She wore a bright scarf with little stars. Her scarf came from a faraway place, and she loved to tell stories about it. “Good morning, Joey,” she said gently. “Today we will play, sort, and make art. We will learn how to recycle.”

Joey’s tail wiggled. “Re…cy…cle?” he sounded out the word slowly.

“Recycle means we give old things a new life,” said Teacher Mira. “We don’t throw everything away. We sort. We save. We make something new.”

On a low table stood three bins with big, colorful pictures. The blue bin showed a bottle and said, “Plastic.” The green bin showed a can and said, “Metal.” The yellow bin showed a box and said, “Paper.” The bins looked friendly, like they were smiling.

Joey bounced closer. He felt the smooth table with his tiny paws. “They feel cool and shiny,” he said.

Teacher Mira nodded. “Yes. Let’s listen and look and touch. That helps us think.” She tapped her head and smiled. “Logical thinking!”

Joey’s friends gathered. Amina wore a red dress from Nigeria with gold swirls. Kenji had a shirt with a tiny crane from Japan. Sofía wore bracelets that jingled softly from Mexico. “We all help together,” said Amina.

First, Teacher Mira poured a basket of clean, safe items onto the rug: a paper tube, a shiny can, a crinkly water bottle, a soft box, and a magazine. They made gentle sounds—crinkle, clink, rustle.

“Let’s sort,” said Teacher Mira. “We can use our eyes, ears, and hands. We can ask, ‘Is it paper, plastic, or metal?’”

Joey picked up the crinkly bottle. It made a “crr-rrr” sound. “Plastic?” he asked.

“Yes!” said Kenji. Together, they dropped it in the blue bin. Plink!

Amina tapped the shiny can. “Clink, clink. Metal!” She placed it in the green bin. Clink!

Sofía hugged the soft box. “Paper,” she sang, and slid it into the yellow bin. Swish!

Joey found the paper tube. “Paper?” He looked at Teacher Mira.

“You used your eyes and hands,” said Teacher Mira. “Good thinking. Paper!”

They sorted and sorted until the basket was empty. The bins looked neat and proud. Joey breathed in. The room smelled like paper and warm sunshine. He felt calm and happy.

“Now we make art,” said Teacher Mira. “We use our treasures.” She spread glue that smelled sweet, safe paint that smelled like fruit, and bright stickers. “Art helps us see beauty in old things.”

Amina painted a bright sunset. “This is like the sunsets my grandma sees,” she said. “Orange and gold.”

Kenji folded a paper crane wing from the magazine. “In my home, cranes mean hope,” he said softly.

Sofía made a shaker from the can with little beans inside. “It goes cha-cha-cha!” She danced. Joey giggled.

Joey thought and thought. “I will make a tidy treasure bin for my toys,” he said. “I like to be organized.” He glued the paper tube to the box like a handle. He added the bottle cap as a button. He painted a happy face. He stuck on a yellow star.

Teacher Mira clapped. “What a clever plan, Joey. You recycled and you made something useful. That is organization and art!”

Joey pressed the cap button and whispered, “Beep!” He pretended his bin talked. “I help toys find home,” it said in a tiny voice.

Everyone laughed. The nursery hummed with gentle sounds—brush, swish, tap, cha-cha-cha. The colors were bright, and the children’s smiles were brighter. Joey felt warm, like a hug.

Soon it was clean-up time. Joey’s friends helped sort again. Paper scraps into yellow. Plastic lids into blue. Metal bits into green. The room became neat and calm. Joey’s heart felt neat and calm too.

Teacher Mira gathered the children on the rug. “What did we learn today?” she asked.

Joey raised his paw. “We sort and save. We give old things a new life. We recycle!”

Amina added, “We share ideas from our homes.”

Kenji said, “We listen and think.”

Sofía shook her shaker softly. “We make art and keep things tidy.”

Teacher Mira smiled. “Yes. When we recycle, we help the Earth. We help each other. We use our minds, our hearts, and our hands.”

Joey hugged his tidy treasure bin. “I will use this every day,” he said. “I will keep my toys in the right place. I will sort my paper, plastic, and metal. I can do it!”

“You can do it,” said his friends.

Outside, a breeze brushed the window. Inside, the nursery felt safe and bright. Joey’s feet went thump-thump as he did a little hop. “Recycling is kind,” he said. “Recycling is fun!”

“Recycling is for everyone,” Teacher Mira said, wrapping her bright scarf around her shoulders. The stars on it seemed to sparkle.

Joey looked around at his friends from many places, at the colorful art, and at the neat bins. His smile grew big and warm. He felt proud.

And every time Joey put something in the right bin, it sounded like a tiny cheer—plink, clink, swish—good job!

The End.
