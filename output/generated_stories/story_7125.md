# Robo-Pup’s Big Nursery Day

In a soft, sunny nursery, the curtains were peach and the rug felt fluffy like a cloud. Crayons sat in a rainbow row. Paper smelled like fresh books. Blocks clicked gently in their basket. In the corner, a helpful robot dog named Robo-Pup blinked his friendly blue eyes.

“Beep-boop! Good morning!” Robo-Pup wagged his metal tail. It went tink-tink against the floor. His ears whirred with a happy sound. “Today, I try by myself. I can do it!”

A little child named Mira hugged Robo-Pup. “You can do it,” she said. “I can try too.”

Robo-Pup looked at the art table. It was low and smooth. The chairs were small and cozy. A big paper was waiting. The crayons smelled waxy and warm. Paint brushes looked like little soft brooms.

“I will make art,” Robo-Pup said. His voice was calm and bright. “I can pick a color on my own.”

He reached with one paw. Whirr-click. The paw opened and closed. He aimed for the blue crayon. Oops! He bumped the yellow one instead. It rolled, rolly-rolly, across the table.

Robo-Pup took a slow breath. “I can try again.” He steadied his paw. Tap, tap. He looked with both eyes. Click! He picked up the blue crayon at last.

“You did it!” Mira cheered. “That’s good coordination.”

“Co-or-di-na-tion,” Robo-Pup repeated, smiling. “That means my paw and eyes work together.”

He drew a wobbly circle. Scratch-scratch went the crayon. The paper felt bumpy under his paw. He added dots and lines. He made a little house with a bright door. He made a shiny sun and a tiny blue bird.

“I am making my picture by myself,” he said. “I can choose what I like.”

Mira clapped. “What will you add next?”

Robo-Pup listened to the room. The clock went tick-tock. The window made a soft whoosh. Far away, a lullaby hummed. He felt brave and peaceful. “I will write a word,” he said. “I like words.”

He pressed his paw gently. “D-O-G,” he sounded out. “Dog.” He wagged. “Language is fun.”

Mira pointed to the bird. “Can you label ‘bird’?”

Robo-Pup nodded. “B-I-R-D.” His letters were a little crooked. That was okay. He was learning. “I can try. I can practice.”

Next, they moved to the block corner. The blocks were smooth and wooden. They smelled like clean trees. Robo-Pup picked up a square block. He set it down, clack. He put a long block on top, clack. He added a triangle roof, tap.

The tower leaned a little. Robo-Pup whispered, “Slow and steady.” He used both paws. He looked closely. He set the next block with care. The tower stood tall like a brave soldier.

Mira grinned. “You did that on your own!”

“I listened to my body,” Robo-Pup said. “I used balance. That is independence. I try first. I ask for help when I need it.”

A soft bell chimed. It was clean-up time. The room smelled like soap and crayons. The sun made warm squares on the floor. Robo-Pup looked around. The blocks needed to go in the basket. The crayons needed to go in their tray. The paper needed to stack in a neat pile.

“I can do it,” Robo-Pup said. He hummed a tidy tune. “Pick up, put away, one by one, hooray!”

Clack went the blocks into the basket. Plink-plink went the crayons. The paper made a gentle shhh as it slid into a neat stack.

Mira held the dustpan. “I’ll hold. You sweep.”

Robo-Pup picked up the tiny broom with careful paws. Swish, swish. He swept little color crumbs into the dustpan. He smiled. “Teamwork helps my independence. I do my part.”

When the nursery was neat, they sat on the soft rug. It felt like warm marshmallows. Robo-Pup showed his art. The sun was bright. The house was cozy. The bird had tiny wings. The letters DOG and BIRD looked proud.

“Tell me about your art,” Mira said.

Robo-Pup spoke slowly. “This is my house of calm. The sun is my warm feeling. The bird is my brave try. The words help me say what I made.”

Mira nodded. “You used your words. You used your hands. You used your heart.”

Robo-Pup wagged. “I tried by myself. When I made a mistake, I tried again. I chose colors. I built a tower. I cleaned up, too. That is independence.”

They hung the picture on the wall with a gentle press. Stick. It looked colorful and happy.

Robo-Pup said, “I learned three big things today.” He raised one paw. “One: I can try first.” He raised another paw. “Two: I can use my eyes and paws together.” He tapped his chest. “Three: I can use words to share my art.”

Mira hugged Robo-Pup. “I’m proud of you.”

Robo-Pup hummed a proud note. His blue eyes shone like little stars. “I am proud of me, too.”

The nursery grew quiet. The light turned golden. The rug felt extra soft. Robo-Pup curled up with a gentle whirr. “Tomorrow, I will try something new,” he whispered. “Today, I did it. I was independent.”

Mira smiled and whispered back, “Yes. You can do it.”

The End.
