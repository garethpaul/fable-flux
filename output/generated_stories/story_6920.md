# Splash the Dolphin's Big Adventure

Splash the dolphin lived in a magical snow globe on a dusty shelf in Grandma's quiet attic. The attic smelled like old books and lavender sachets. Sunbeams danced through the small round window, making everything sparkle.

Every day, Splash swam in circles inside his glass home. He could see amazing treasures all around the attic - colorful quilts, wooden toys, and shiny music boxes. But he felt lonely in his small space.

"I wish I could explore the attic and make friends," Splash said to himself. His voice echoed softly in the glass globe.

One morning, Splash noticed something exciting. A family of toy mice lived in an old dollhouse nearby. They were building a tiny playground with blocks and string.

"Hello!" Splash called out, pressing his nose against the glass. "I'm Splash! Can I help you build?"

The mice looked up and waved. "We'd love your help!" squeaked Pip, the smallest mouse. "But how can you get out of there?"

Splash felt frustrated. He wanted to help so badly! Then he remembered what Grandma always told her grandchildren: "When you have a problem, make a plan!"

Splash swam in a thinking circle. "I need to make a plan," he said aloud. "First, I need to figure out how to get out. Then, I need to think about how to move around the attic. Finally, I need to plan how to help my new friends."

He looked carefully at his globe. The top had a small cork stopper that was loose from many years in the attic. The warm sunlight had made it shrink just a little.

"I can push against the cork!" Splash realized. "But I need to be gentle so I don't break anything."

Splash took a deep breath and made his plan. He would swim up fast, push the cork with his nose, and wiggle out carefully. Then he would call for help from his new friends.

"Here I go!" Splash announced. He swam down to the bottom of his globe, then zoomed up as fast as he could. 

*Pop!* The cork flew out and landed softly on a pile of quilts.

"Hooray!" cheered the mice. "You did it!"

But now Splash had another problem. He was a dolphin, and dolphins need water to swim. The attic floor was dry and dusty.

"Oh no," Splash worried. "How can I move around without water?"

Then he had another brilliant idea. "I need to speak up for myself and ask for help," he said. "That's called self-advocacy!"

"Dear mice friends," Splash called out politely, "I need your help. Could you please bring me some water in a bucket? Then I can swim around the attic and help you build!"

The mice loved this plan. They worked together to drag a small watering can from Grandma's plant corner. Soon, Splash was happily swimming in his new portable pool.

"Now I can help you build!" Splash said excitedly. With his strong tail, he could move blocks and tie knots. The mice climbed on his back to reach high places.

Together, they built the most wonderful playground the attic had ever seen. It had swings made from thread, slides made from old books, and a merry-go-round made from a spinning top.

As the sun began to set, Splash felt proud and happy. "Making a plan really works!" he said. "First I planned how to get out, then I planned how to move around, and then I asked for help when I needed it."

"You're the best planner we know," said Pip. "And the best friend too!"

From that day on, Splash and the mice had many more adventures in the quiet attic. And Splash always remembered to make a plan first, speak up for what he needed, and work together with his friends.

The End.
