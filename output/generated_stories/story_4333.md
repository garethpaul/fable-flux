# Gigi’s Porch Puzzle

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe. She loved to sit on her peaceful front porch in the soft morning light. The porch smelled like warm hay and sweet flowers. Birds chirped. A gentle breeze tickled Gigi’s long eyelashes.

Gigi liked to think. She would sway her long neck and say, “Hmm.” Thinking felt good, like a hug for her brain.

One sunny day, Gigi poured a cup of cool mint tea. She sat on a large, comfy cushion. Her porch had a small table with bright buttons, smooth pebbles, and a little bell that went ting-ting. She smiled. “Today, I will solve a porch puzzle,” she said.

Her friends came by the path to say hello. First came Benny the bunny with soft gray fur. His ears wiggled. “Hi, Gigi! What are you doing?”

“I am thinking,” Gigi said gently. “Would you like to help?”

Benny nodded. “I like to hop and help!”

Next came Tula the turtle with a shiny green shell. She moved slowly-slowly. “Good morning,” Tula whispered. “I have time to think.”

Then came Zara the zebra with bold black-and-white stripes. She trotted and laughed, “I love puzzles!”

Gigi clapped her hooves softly. “Wonderful! Here is the porch puzzle. My basket of porch snacks is missing.” She pointed with her nose to an empty spot on the table. “It was here. Now it is gone.”

“Oh no!” said Benny. “Did it run away?”

“Baskets don’t run,” Tula said kindly.

“Let’s use steps,” Gigi said, smiling. “We can think in order. Step 1: What do we know?”

“We know the basket was here,” said Zara, touching the table. “And now it is not.”

Step 2: “What do we see?” Gigi asked, bowing her long neck low.

Benny squinted. “I see a trail of crumbs.” Little cracker dots led off the porch.

Tula nodded. “I smell fruit. Sweet and juicy.”

Zara flicked her tail. “I hear a faint rustle. Like leaves.”

“Good clues,” said Gigi. “Step 3: What do we ask?”

Benny’s ears stood straight. “Who took it?”

Tula blinked slowly. “Where did it go?”

Zara tilted her head. “Why would it move?”

Gigi tapped the little bell. Ting-ting. “Let’s follow the crumbs.”

They walked from the peaceful porch. The sun felt warm on their backs. The grass felt cool on their hooves, paws, and feet. The crumb trail curved around a flower pot and under a shady bush.

Benny whispered, “I see tiny paw prints.” He pointed to soft marks in the dirt.

“Let’s use our different strengths,” Gigi said. “Benny, you hop ahead and peek. Tula, you look closely at the ground. Zara, you listen and watch the bushes. I will ask gentle questions.”

Benny hopped and peered under the bush. “Hello?” he said softly.

A small voice squeaked, “Eep!” Out came two little field mice. One had a white spot on her ear. The other wore a tiny blue leaf like a hat. They held a crumb together.

Gigi knelt to be low and kind. “Hello, friends. I am Gigi. We are solving a porch puzzle. Our basket is gone. Did you see it?”

The mouse with the white spot nodded. “We saw a big basket roll off the table. The wind pushed it. It bumped down the steps.”

The other mouse said, “We were hungry. We took a few crumbs. We are sorry.”

Gigi’s eyes were warm. “Thank you for telling the truth. That is helpful. We can share snacks. And we can find the basket.”

Zara pricked her ears. “I hear it!” There was a soft thump-thump behind the bush.

They went around the bush together. There was the basket, upside down by the fence. A napkin fluttered like a little flag.

“Step 4: Try a plan,” Gigi said. “How do we get it back?”

Benny said, “I can tug the napkin.”

Tula said, “I can push slowly with my shell.”

Zara said, “I can steady the side with my nose.”

Gigi smiled. “I can lift with my gentle giraffe chin.”

“One, two, three,” said Tula.

They worked as a team. Tug, push, steady, lift. The basket flipped over and landed safely. Inside were crackers, apple slices, and carrot sticks.

The mice looked shy. “May we have a little?” they squeaked.

Gigi nodded. “Yes. We will share. But next time, please ask.”

“We will,” said the mice.

They sat together on the peaceful front porch. The bell went ting-ting in the breeze. They crunched crackers. They munched apples. The mice nibbled tiny bites. Everyone smiled.

Benny said, “We solved the puzzle!”

Zara giggled. “We used our ears, eyes, noses, and brains.”

Tula added, “We asked questions and took turns.”

Gigi took a sip of mint tea. “This was good logical thinking. We used steps: know, see, ask, try. We worked with our different strengths. We were kind. And we shared.”

The mice chimed, “Thinking helps. Friends help, too.”

Gigi looked at her friends. The sun made soft golden patches on the porch. She felt proud and calm. “When a problem comes,” she said, “we can pause, look for clues, make a plan, and work together.”

Everyone nodded. The breeze whispered yes.

The End.
