# Bouncy Roo and the Backyard Map

Bouncy Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey. He loved to hop, hop, hop in the sunny backyard. The grass felt cool on his toes. The sun felt warm on his ears. The air smelled like sweet flowers. Birds sang, “Cheep-cheep!” and a breeze whispered, “Whoosh.”

One bright morning, Bouncy Roo found a big cardboard box. On the box was a picture of a round world. “Oh!” said Bouncy Roo. “What is this circle with blue and green?”

Mama Roo smiled. “That is a map of our world,” she said. “Blue is water. Green and brown are land.”

Bouncy Roo’s eyes grew wide. “Our world?” he asked. “Is it bigger than our backyard?”

“Oh yes,” said Mama Roo. “There are many places far away. People live there. They eat different foods. They speak different words. But we all share one big world.”

Bouncy Roo felt a bubble of curiosity in his tummy. “I want to say hello,” he said. “But how can I say hello to everyone?”

Mama Roo patted his head. “You can learn,” she said. “Start small. Listen carefully. Ask kindly. Try on your own. You can do it.”

Bouncy Roo nodded. He wanted to try. He took out crayons. He drew a big round circle on the box flap. He colored blue for water and green for land. He drew a little star for “home.” He whispered, “This is my backyard. But there is more.”

Just then, a butterfly with sunny yellow wings floated by. “Hello!” said Bouncy Roo.

The butterfly fluttered near his nose. Its wings went, “Fwip-fwip.” Bouncy Roo giggled. “Do you live far away?” he asked.

The butterfly landed on the map. “Butterflies fly many places,” said Mama Roo. “Some travel across fields and forests.”

Bouncy Roo breathed in. He smelled the flowers again. “If I want to say hello to friends far away, I must learn to listen and share,” he said softly.

He had an idea. “I will make a Hello Stand,” he said. “I will learn hello in different ways!”

He dragged a little table to the sunny spot. He put the map on it. He found a bell that went “ding!” He made a sign with big letters: “HELLO!”

“Good plan,” said Mama Roo. “Try to find out how friends say hello in different places. You can ask neighbors. You can look at a small book. You can remember and practice.”

Bouncy Roo clapped his paws. He opened a picture book Mama kept by the window. The pages showed children smiling in many places. Under each picture were words.

Bouncy Roo read slowly with Mama. “Hola,” he said. “That is hello in Spanish.”

“Bonjour,” Mama read. “That is hello in French.”

“Jambo,” Bouncy Roo said, tracing the letters. “That is hello in Swahili.”

“Namaste,” Mama added. “That is hello in Hindi.”

Bouncy Roo bounced in joy. “I can try! I can practice!” He sounded out each word. “Ho-la. Bon-jour. Jam-bo. Na-ma-ste.”

He wrote the words on little cards. He placed them on the table. He drew happy faces next to each one.

Soon, friends came by the fence. Tilly the turtle peeked in. A robin sat on the fence post. Mr. Kim from next door waved.

Bouncy Roo rang the bell. “Ding! Welcome to my Hello Stand!” he said. “We all live in one world. We can talk and share.”

Mr. Kim smiled. “Annyeong,” he said. “That is hello in Korean.”

Bouncy Roo grinned. “Ann-yeong,” he repeated slowly. “Thank you!” He wrote it on a new card.

Tilly the turtle blinked. “I am slow,” said Tilly. “Can you teach me one hello?”

Bouncy Roo nodded. He spoke clearly and kindly. “Hola,” he said to Tilly. “It means hello.”

Tilly tried. “Ho-la,” she said. Her voice was soft like a leaf. She smiled. “I did it!”

The robin chirped, “Cheer-up! Cheer-up!” Bouncy Roo laughed. “That is bird hello!”

A little breeze carried new smells—fresh-cut grass, sweet apples from a basket, and a bit of soil from the garden. Bouncy Roo felt brave. He was learning. He was sharing. He was doing it himself, with careful listening and kind words.

A small problem popped up. Two kids from down the street came over. “We don’t know how to say hello like that,” one said. “We feel shy.”

Bouncy Roo placed his paw on his heart. “It is okay to feel shy,” he said. “We can try together. We can practice. We can help each other.”

He showed them the cards. He pointed at the smiling faces. He sounded out the words slowly. They tried. They giggled. They made little mistakes. They tried again.

“Bonjour,” one child said.

“Namaste,” said the other, pressing palms together.

“You did it!” Bouncy Roo cheered. They all clapped. The bell went “ding-ding!”

Mama Roo watched with warm eyes. “Bouncy Roo,” she said, “you found a way to say hello to the world from our sunny backyard.”

Bouncy Roo hugged the map. The paper felt smooth under his paws. “We all share one big world,” he said. “We can talk. We can listen. We can help. I can try by myself, and I can ask for help too.”

As the sun began to dip, the sky turned peach and gold. The air was cool on Bouncy Roo’s cheeks. He rang the bell one last time. “Hello, world!” he called. “Hola! Bonjour! Jambo! Namaste! Annyeong!”

Everyone waved. The butterfly fluttered. The robin sang. Tilly tucked in her head with a happy sigh.

Bouncy Roo felt calm and proud. He had learned new words. He had shared them. He had helped friends feel brave. He had explored the big world, right from his sunny backyard.

The clear lesson glowed in his heart: The world is big and shared by everyone. When we listen, learn words, and speak kindly, we can be friends with people near and far. We can try on our own and help each other too.

The End.
