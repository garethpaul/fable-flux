# Birdie Builds a Workout

Birdie was a small singing bird with bright blue feathers and a sunny yellow beak. Birdie loved to sing sweet songs like, “La-la-la!” Birdie also loved to visit the basement lab. The lab was cool and quiet, with a soft hum from a tiny fan. It smelled like clean soap and warm wood. Shelves held boxes and bright blocks. A big table stood in the middle, covered with tools that went click and clack.

One day, Birdie flapped down the stairs and peeped, “La-la! I want to build a tall tower today!” Birdie’s wings buzzed with excitement.

But when Birdie looked around, the lab was messy. Blocks were in a heap on the floor. Little wheels rolled under the table. Ribbons of tape stuck to the chair. Birdie blinked. “Oh no,” Birdie said softly. “I can’t find my red blocks. I can’t build my tower.”

Birdie felt a little droop in the wings. The problem felt big. Birdie took a deep breath. The air was cool in the beak. “I need a plan,” Birdie said. “A smart plan!”

Birdie remembered what the teacher once said: “Use a strategy. Make a plan. Move your body. Use your brain.” Birdie chirped, “I can do that!”

Step one, Birdie decided, was to get the body moving. “Exercise first! When I move, I feel strong and ready,” Birdie sang.

Birdie set a timer that went beep-beep. “Time for a lab workout!” Birdie said with a happy wiggle.

- “Wing Stretches!” Birdie spread wings wide, wide, wide. The feathers brushed the cool air. “Ahh,” Birdie sighed. Stretching felt good.
- “Tiptoe March!” Birdie marched in place, tap-tap-tap on the smooth floor. Knees up, wings out. “March, march, march!”
- “Bunny Hops!” Birdie bent and hopped lightly, boing, boing, boing! The hops made the heart beat faster. Birdie giggled.

“Exercise makes me ready,” Birdie said, puffing a tiny bit. “Now I can think clearly.”

Step two, Birdie made a strategy. Birdie sang, “Sort, stack, and store. That is how I’ll find more!”

Birdie got three bins. One bin was red, one was blue, and one was yellow. “Colors help me organize,” Birdie chirped.

- “Blocks in the red bin!”
- “Wheels in the blue bin!”
- “Tape and tools in the yellow bin!”

Birdie picked up a block. “Clack!” It went into the red bin. A wheel rolled away. “Come back, little wheel!” Birdie scooted low, did a gentle stretch under the table, and reached. “Got it!” The wheel went into the blue bin with a soft plink.

“Moving is helping me clean,” Birdie said. “Exercise makes me strong and steady.”

Birdie made a game. “Clean-up steps!” Birdie sang.
- “Ten tiptoe steps to the shelf,” tip-tip-tip.
- “Five star stretches to reach high,” stretch-stretch-stretch.
- “Three slow breaths to rest,” in, out, in.

The lab started to look neat. The table felt smooth when Birdie brushed a wing over it. The floor looked bright and open.

Then Birdie found the red blocks! They were under a fluffy cloth. “Hooray!” Birdie trilled. “Now I can build.”

Step three, Birdie began to build the tower. “First a wide base,” Birdie said. Plop. Plop. Two big blocks on the bottom. “Strong feet, like me when I stand tall.”

Birdie used a strategy. “Two blocks, then one. Two blocks, then one.” The pattern made the tower steady. Birdie tapped each block gently, “Tap-tap,” to make it sit flat. The tower grew taller and taller. Birdie stood on tiptoe. Birdie did a careful reach with a long exhale. “I can do it,” Birdie whispered.

Clink! The last block fit on top. The tower did not wobble. It stood tall and proud, like a tree in the sun.

“I did it!” Birdie sang, voice clear like a bell. “La-la-la! Exercise, strategy, and building!”

Birdie stepped back to admire the work. The red bin, the blue bin, and the yellow bin sat on the shelf, easy to reach. The lab hummed softly, and the air felt fresh. Birdie’s heart felt warm.

A tiny mouse friend peeked from a corner. “Squeak! Birdie, your tower is amazing! How did you do it?”

Birdie smiled. “I had a problem. The lab was messy. I felt stuck. I made a plan. I moved my body to get ready. I sorted my things to get organized. Then I built slowly with a pattern. Exercise helped me feel strong and calm.”

The mouse clapped tiny paws. “That is a good plan. Can I try the workout too?”

“Yes!” said Birdie. “Let’s do Wing Stretches, Tiptoe March, and Bunny Hops. Then we can build a bridge!”

They did the little workout together. Stretch, march, hop! They laughed and breathed and felt steady. Then they used the bins. They found tiny blocks and built a small bridge right next to the tower.

Birdie sang a soft song that filled the lab like warm light. “When I move, I can think. When I plan, I can build. When I clean, I can find. I am strong. I am kind.”

The mouse squeaked, “We can do big things with small steps!”

Birdie nodded. “That is our lesson,” Birdie said. “Exercise helps our bodies and our brains. Strategy helps us solve problems. Building is fun when we stay organized.”

They looked at the tower and the bridge. They felt proud. The lab was tidy, bright, and ready for the next day.

The End.
