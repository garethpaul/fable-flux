# The Song in the Closet

In a quiet house, there was a small closet hideaway. It was cozy and soft. The walls were lined with coats that felt like fuzzy blankets. A tiny lamp made a warm, golden glow. Buttons, scarves, and old shoes made gentle smells of soap and leather. It was a perfect place for stories and songs.

One day, a singing bird named Lulla fluttered inside. Lulla was bright blue with a soft yellow belly. Her feathers felt like silk, and her voice sounded like bells in the morning. She loved to sing. She sang when she woke up. She sang when she ate seeds. She even sang when she sat very still.

Lulla peeked around the closet. “Hello?” she chirped. “Is anyone here?”

From a little fort made of pillows, a mouse squeaked, “I’m here! I’m Pip.”

From a box of scarves, a turtle whispered, “I’m Shelly.”

From behind a raincoat, a spider said, “I’m Dot.”

Lulla smiled. “I like friends,” she sang. “May I sing with you?”

Pip wrinkled his nose. “Um, we are having story time. It’s very quiet.”

Shelly pulled the scarf closer. “Stories are better with soft voices,” she said.

Dot hung from a tiny thread. “Yes, very soft,” she added.

Lulla’s wings drooped. Her chest felt tight. “Oh,” she said in a small voice. “I understand.”

She tucked herself into a corner on a comfy boot. She tried to hum very quietly. But a little song slipped out. Her voice filled the closet with a shiny sound. It made the lamp glow a bit brighter.

Pip covered his ears. “Too loud,” he squeaked.

Shelly pulled her head into her shell. “Too much singing,” she said.

Dot shook. “This is not our way,” she whispered.

Lulla stopped. Tears pricked her eyes. “Maybe I don’t belong,” she said. Her feathers felt heavy. The closet did not feel cozy anymore.

The little lamp flickered. The coats swished softly. The closet felt the sadness. It felt the quiet too.

Pip peeked at Lulla. He saw her shiny tears. He heard the quiet part of her song, the part that sounded like a lullaby. His heart felt warm and wiggly.

Pip took a breath. “Lulla,” he said, “I didn’t like the loud part. But I liked the tiny part. It felt like a hug.”

Shelly slowly put out her head. “Sometimes I need quiet,” she said. “My shell is my quiet place. But I also like kind sounds.”

Dot swayed on her silk thread. “I’m small,” she said. “Big sounds can scare me. But I don’t want you to feel left out.”

Lulla sniffled. “I want to be with you,” she said softly. “Singing is my way to share.”

Pip’s whiskers twitched. “Maybe we can have both,” he said. “Story time and song time.”

Shelly nodded. “We can make a plan.”

Dot clapped her tiny legs. “A plan is kind,” she said.

They all sat in a little circle on a fuzzy coat. The lamp made golden circles on the floor.

Pip said, “We can use a gentle bell.” He picked up a button and tapped it on a shoe. Ting! “When we hear the bell once, it is story time. We use soft voices.”

Shelly smiled. “When we hear the bell twice,” she said, “it is song time. We can sing. We can try soft songs, and we can try happy loud songs, but not too loud.”

Dot added, “And if someone feels scared or left out, we can say, ‘How can we help?’”

Lulla’s eyes shone. “I can sing the softest song I know,” she said. “And I can sing a happy song with small claps.”

Pip rang the button once. Ting! They told a story about a moon that wore a scarf. Their voices were soft and sweet.

Then Pip rang the button twice. Ting, ting! Lulla took a deep breath. She sang like a feather floating. Her song smelled like warm tea and sounded like sunrise. Shelly tapped her shell in a soft beat. Dot made a tiny twang on her silk. Pip clapped softly with his paws.

Lulla sang a happy part too. She watched her friends. If Dot covered her eyes, Lulla sang lower. If Shelly smiled, Lulla sang a little brighter. If Pip’s tail wiggled fast, Lulla added a gentle trill. Everyone felt safe. Everyone felt seen.

When the song ended, the closet felt like a hug. The lamp shone steady. The coats sighed. The old shoes smelled like adventure.

Pip said, “I liked song time.”

Shelly said, “I liked our plan.”

Dot said, “I liked being included.”

Lulla’s heart felt light and warm. “I liked being with you,” she sang.

They rang the bell once more. Ting! It was time for a bedtime story. They curled up in the cozy closet hideaway. Lulla hummed very softly, like a blanket for the ears. Dot spun a tiny star in her web. Shelly tucked everyone close. Pip told a gentle story.

In the warm glow, they all felt together. Different, and together.

The lesson was clear: We can include everyone. We can make space for quiet and for song. We can listen. We can ask, “How can we help?” When we do, we all belong.

The End.
