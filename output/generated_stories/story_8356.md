# Koa the Calm Koala and the Sound Puzzle

Koa the calm koala lived in a big house with a special place downstairs. It was a basement lab. The lab was cozy, not scary. Lights were soft and warm. The floor was smooth and cool. Shelves held jars with bright buttons, soft yarns, shiny spoons, and little flags from many countries. The air smelled like fresh paper and lemon soap.

Koa loved to think. Koa loved to solve puzzles. Today, Koa had a new plan. “I will make a song box,” Koa said in a gentle voice. “When we press a button, the box will play sounds from around the world.” Koa’s furry ears twitched with joy.

Koa opened a wooden box. Inside were three big buttons: red, blue, and green. There were also cards with little pictures. One card showed waves and a shell. One card showed drums. One card showed bells and snow.

Koa wiggled a soft nose and said, “I will match each button to a sound. I will use clues. I will think step by step.” Koa’s heart felt slow and peaceful.

Just then, friends came to visit. Nina wore a bright scarf from India. Kenji had a small bell from Japan. Amara had a tiny drum from Ghana. They all stepped into the basement lab. Their feet made little tap-tap sounds on the floor.

“What are you making?” asked Nina.

“A song box!” said Koa. “We will learn about our big world. We will use our ears. We will use our minds.”

Koa placed the three picture cards on the table. “We have a problem,” Koa said. “The buttons are not matched to the sounds yet.”

Kenji rang his bell. Ting-ting! The sound was light and clear like little stars.

Amara tapped her drum. Dum-dum! The sound was round and deep like a heartbeat.

Nina cupped her hands by her mouth. “I hear the ocean in my seashell,” she said. “Shhh-whoosh!” It sounded like soft wind and waves.

Koa smiled. “We have three sounds and three buttons. Let’s think. Let’s look. Let’s listen. We can solve this with steps.”

Step 1: Name the sounds.
Koa held up a finger. “Sound one is the bell. Sound two is the drum. Sound three is the sea.”

Everyone nodded. Ting-ting. Dum-dum. Shhh-whoosh.

Step 2: Look for clues.
Koa pointed to the cards. “Bells and snow,” Koa said softly, pointing at the bell card. Little snowflakes on the card shone like tiny dots. “Drums and warm sun,” Koa said, pointing at the drum card. The drum card had a bright yellow sun. “Waves and shells,” Koa whispered, touching the sea card. The sea card had blue swirls.

Step 3: Try a match.
Koa pressed the red button. It made a bright beep. “Red feels warm like the sun. Red goes with the drum?” Koa wondered.

Amara smiled. “Yes! Dum-dum feels warm and brave.”

Koa pressed the blue button. It made a soft beep. “Blue feels like water. Blue goes with the sea?” Koa asked.

Nina put her ear to the shell. “Shhh-whoosh. Blue is the sea to me,” she said.

Koa pressed the green button. It made a calm beep. “Green feels cool like a pine tree. Green goes with bells in snow?” Koa asked.

Kenji nodded. “In winter festivals, we ring bells. Snow is cool. Trees are green.”

Step 4: Test it out.
Koa clipped little wires with tiny clicks. Red to drum. Blue to sea. Green to bells. Koa’s paws were soft and careful. “We are not in a rush,” Koa said. “We think. We test.”

“Ready?” Koa asked.

“Ready!” said the friends.

Koa pressed the red button. Dum-dum! The drum sound filled the lab. The floor hummed gently.

Koa pressed the blue button. Shhh-whoosh! The sea sound made everyone close their eyes and smile.

Koa pressed the green button. Ting-ting! The bell sound sparkled in the air.

“We did it!” said Nina.

“We solved the puzzle,” said Kenji.

“We used steps,” said Amara.

Koa clapped softly. “We used our minds and our hearts. We listened. We looked. We tried. We tested. We did not give up.”

They all danced a tiny dance. Their feet made happy tip-tap sounds. Their hands made soft clap-clap sounds. The basement lab felt bright and alive.

Koa took out small paper and crayons. “Let’s draw what we learned,” Koa said. They drew a red sun for drums, a blue wave for the sea, and a green tree with little bells. Each friend wrote one word: listen, think, test.

Nina said, “In India, we dance to drums at weddings.”

Kenji said, “In Japan, bells ring for new year.”

Amara said, “In Ghana, drums talk. They call people to come.”

Koa’s eyes were warm. “Our world is big. Our sounds are many. We can learn from each other.”

They put their drawings on the wall. The lemon soap smell mixed with a cozy, warm feeling. Koa pressed all three buttons again. Dum-dum. Shhh-whoosh. Ting-ting. The sounds made a gentle, friendly song.

Koa took a deep breath. “The problem was the buttons. The solution was to use steps. Name, look, try, test.” Koa smiled. “When we think in order, we solve problems. When we listen to friends, we learn about our world.”

The friends waved. “Bye, Koa!”

“Bye,” said Koa. “Stay calm. Think step by step. The world is full of wonderful sounds.”

The End.
