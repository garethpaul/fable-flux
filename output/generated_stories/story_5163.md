# Soft Steps in the Attic

The gentle elephant calf had a name. Everyone called him Nuzo. Nuzo had big kind eyes, soft gray ears, and the quietest steps an elephant could make. One sunny afternoon, Nuzo climbed the creaky wooden stairs to a quiet attic.

The attic smelled like old lavender and warm dust. Sunbeams danced through a small round window. Tiny dust sparkles floated like stars. Boxes sat in neat rows. A blue trunk waited by a stack of books. The floor felt smooth under Nuzo’s padded feet.

“Hello?” Nuzo whispered. His voice sounded soft and round in the still air.

“Hello!” squeaked a tiny voice. A little mouse peeked from behind a basket of buttons. The mouse wore a bright red thread like a scarf. “I’m Pip. This is our quiet attic.”

“Our?” Nuzo asked in a gentle way.

Pip nodded. “I share it with friends. We like quiet play. We like soft voices. We like taking turns.” Pip sniffed the air. “You smell like sunshine and leaves.”

Nuzo smiled. “I like soft things too,” he said. “May I play here?”

Pip’s whiskers twitched. “Yes. When we play, we ask first. We listen to ‘yes’ and we listen to ‘no.’ We keep our bodies safe and our hearts comfy.”

Nuzo’s big ears flapped happily. “I can do that.”

Just then, a sleepy cat named Tulla stretched on a folded quilt. A bright green beetle, Beenie, clicked tiny feet on a wooden train track. A shy bird, Dot, tucked her beak under her wing on a hat stand.

“Hi,” Nuzo said softly. “I’m Nuzo.”

Tulla purred, “We like quiet. We like space.”

Nuzo nodded. “I will be careful with my big body.”

Pip scampered to a box. “We have treasures! Buttons, scarves, and a bell that sings.”

Nuzo’s eyes grew wide. He really wanted to see the bell. It was shiny and golden. He lifted his trunk. “Can I ring the bell?”

Pip smiled. “Yes, ring it one time, softly.”

Nuzo wrapped his trunk around the bell. He paused. He looked at Pip. Pip nodded. “Go ahead.”

Ding! The bell sang a tiny, sweet note. The sound floated like sugar in tea. Dot peeped happily. Tulla’s tail swished. Beenie clicked, “Nice.”

Nuzo felt warm inside. Asking felt good.

Then Nuzo saw the soft quilt under Tulla. It looked squishy and perfect for a nap. Nuzo stepped closer. “That quilt looks cozy,” he said. “May I sit on the corner?”

Tulla lifted her head. “Not right now,” she purred. “This is my quiet spot. I need it.”

Nuzo’s feet stopped. His heart pinged with a small “oh.” He wanted the quilt. But he remembered Pip’s words: We listen to ‘no’ and keep our bodies safe and our hearts comfy.

“Okay,” Nuzo said in a calm voice. “Thank you for telling me.” He stepped back. His chest felt a little tight, then soft again. Tulla relaxed and purred louder.

Pip handed Nuzo a blue scarf. “You can sit on this,” Pip said. “It’s a soft spot too.”

“Thank you,” Nuzo said. He made a little nest with the scarf. It smelled like soap and rain. He settled his big belly carefully. The floor groaned just a tiny bit. “I like my soft spot,” he said.

Beenie tapped the train track. “I built a bridge,” Beenie clicked. “Do you want to help push the train?”

Nuzo’s eyes lit up. “Yes! Can I touch the train?”

“Yes,” Beenie said. “Two fingers on the roof, slow and steady.”

Nuzo used the tip of his trunk like two fingers. He moved the little wooden train. Click-clack, click-clack. The sound was gentle and happy. Beenie smiled. “Good listening.”

Dot hopped down from the hat stand. She held a shiny feather. “My feather is special,” she peeped. “Look, but no touching today. That keeps me comfy.”

Nuzo leaned close. He looked with his big kind eyes. The feather shone blue and green. “It is beautiful,” he whispered. “Thank you for sharing with my eyes.”

Dot’s feathers fluffed with joy. “Thank you for asking.”

Soon, Pip brought a tiny cup and a tiny plate. “Snack time,” Pip said. “We have apple slices.”

Nuzo’s tummy rumbled. “May I have an apple slice?”

“Yes,” Pip said. “One slice at a time.”

Nuzo took one slice. The apple was cool and crisp. It tasted sweet like a hug. The sound of his munch was small and tidy.

After snack, Tulla stretched. “I am done with my quiet spot,” she said. “Would you like to sit on the quilt now?”

Nuzo’s ears perked up. “Yes, please!”

Tulla smiled. “Thank you for waiting when I said no. That made me feel safe.”

Nuzo stepped onto the quilt with careful feet. It felt fluffy and warm. “Thank you for saying yes now,” he said.

Pip clapped tiny paws. “Today we learned: We ask before we touch. We listen to yes. We listen to no. When we listen, everyone feels safe, included, and happy.”

Nuzo’s heart felt big and light. He looked at his friends. “I like asking,” he said. “I like listening. It makes this quiet attic feel like a kind place.”

The sunbeam slid across the floor. The dust stars danced slowly. Nuzo wrapped the blue scarf like a ribbon around his trunk and giggled. Pip giggled too. Tulla purred a cozy song. Beenie clicked a tiny beat. Dot peeped a soft tune.

Nuzo whispered, “Soft steps, soft voices, soft hearts.”

Everyone nodded. In the quiet attic, they played, they asked, they listened, and they cared for each other. That was their gentle magic.

The End.
