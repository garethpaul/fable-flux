# Ready, Set, Rabbit!

Bunny Blue was a small, determined rabbit with bright eyes and soft, gray fur. He lived near a cozy garden shed that smelled like fresh soil, wooden sticks, and minty soap. The shed had a squeaky door, a round window, and neat shelves with pots, twine, and shiny tools that gleamed in the sun.

One sunny morning, Bunny Blue bounced to the shed and wiggled his nose. “Today is picnic day!” he said. “I will make a carrot-and-clover picnic for my friends. I must be ready.”

Bunny Blue felt excited and a little nervous. “I want it to be perfect,” he whispered. His ears stood tall. “I will prepare. I will plan.”

He opened the squeaky door. Squeeeak! Inside, it was cool and dusty. Rays of light made tiny sparkles in the air. Bunny set out a small blanket on the floor. He wrote a list on a leaf with a purple berry:

“Bunny Blue’s Picnic Plan:
- Wash the carrots.
- Pick fresh clover.
- Pack napkins.
- Bring cups.
- Check the weather.
- Invite friends.”

“Good plan,” said Bunny Blue. He smiled and hummed a little song. “Plan, plan, plan. Prepare as much as I can.”

He hopped to the carrot bin and brushed dirt off with a soft cloth. Swish, swish. “Clean,” he said. He put the carrots in a basket.

He looked for clover, and found a tin labeled CLOVER. He opened it and sniffed. “Mmm, smells sweet.” He added the clover to the basket.

He reached for napkins, but the shelf was high. He stood on his toes. His paws slid. “Oh!” he squeaked. The napkins wobbled. He stopped and took a breath. “Wait,” Bunny said. “I will use a safe tool.” He looked around and found a small step stool. “This is why we prepare,” he told himself as he climbed slowly. Step, step. He reached the napkins and put them in the basket.

Bunny Blue heard patter-patter on the window. He looked up. “Rain?” He hopped to the door and peered outside. A gray cloud drifted in. “I will check the weather,” he said, tapping his list. He found a little radio on the shelf. Click. A calm voice said, “A light rain is coming this afternoon.”

Bunny Blue thought and nodded. “I need a plan for rain.” He looked around. He found a big umbrella and a stack of clean, dry cups with lids. “Perfect.”

Just then, his friends arrived. Squirrel Sunny chattered at the door. “Hi, Bunny! I brought crunchy nuts!” Bird Bella fluttered in. “Tweet! I brought a small song.” Tortoise Tito smiled slowly. “I brought time,” he said in a warm voice. “I am always on time.”

Bunny Blue held up his list. “I made a plan. We will have our picnic in the shed if it rains. We will share the umbrella if we go out. And we have napkins, cups, and clean carrots.”

Sunny clapped. “You are so ready!” Bella sang a happy note. Tito nodded. “Preparation is smart.”

They started to set the picnic. But when Bunny reached for the cup lids, the box was empty. “Oh no,” he said softly. “I thought I had enough.” His ears drooped.

A drop of rain tapped the window. Tap. Tap. Tap.

Bunny took a deep breath. “It’s okay,” he said. “I can solve this.” He looked around the shed. He saw small jars with twisty tops. He picked one up and smiled. “We can use jars for juice.”

“Good problem-solving,” said Tito.

“We can also share,” said Sunny. “I can sip first, then you,” she told Bella. Bella chirped, “Yes! We can take turns.”

Bunny thought again. “Let’s make a plan to share. We will use kind words. We will say, ‘Your turn,’ and ‘Thank you.’”

The rain grew soft and steady. Pitter-patter, pitter-patter. It sounded like a gentle drum. The shed smelled like rain and warm wood. It felt cozy.

They poured carrot-apple juice into the jars. “Mmm,” said Bella. “Sweet!” They crunched carrots. Crunch, crunch. They nibbled clover salad. Nibble-nibble. They used napkins and kept things tidy.

Soon, Mouse Mina peeked in, looking shy and wet. “I heard music,” she squeaked. “But I have no snack.”

Bunny’s heart felt warm. “You can share ours,” he said kindly. “We have enough when we plan and share.”

Mina smiled. “Thank you.” She wrung the rain from her tiny scarf. Drip, drip.

There was one small blanket, and more friends than space. Bunny saw this and paused. “We need to compromise,” he said. “We can move the basket and make room.”

Tito gently slid the basket aside. Sunny scooted over. Bella fluffed her feathers to be smaller. Bunny folded the blanket in a new way. “Now we all fit,” he said.

They ate and laughed. Bella sang a soft rain song. Sunny tapped a gentle beat with a wooden spoon. Tito hummed low. Mina giggled. Bunny’s eyes shone. He felt proud.

When the rain stopped, the sun peeked out and the shed glowed golden. Bunny checked his list again. Every box had a tiny berry checkmark.

He said, “Today I learned something big. When we prepare, we feel calm. When a problem comes, we can solve it. When we are kind and we compromise, everyone can be happy.”

His friends nodded. “Yes!” they said together.

Bunny Blue packed up neatly, put tools back on their shelves, and dried the umbrella. He smiled at his friends. “Thank you for helping.”

“Thank you for planning,” said Tito.

“Thank you for sharing,” said Mina.

“Thank you for being kind,” sang Bella.

Sunny added, “You were ready, steady, and kind!”

Bunny Blue wiggled his nose. “Ready, set, rabbit!”

They all laughed as the garden sparkled after the rain.

The End.
