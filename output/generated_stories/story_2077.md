# Dori Dolphin’s Cozy Bedtime Cleanup

In a cozy bedroom with star stickers on the ceiling, a playful dolphin named Dori flopped on a soft blue bed. The bed felt squishy, like warm sea foam. A little fan made a gentle whoosh-whoosh sound. The room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap.

“Splish-splash!” Dori giggled, swishing her tail on the blankets. “Tonight is story night!”

Mama Dolphin peeked in. “Yes, Dori. We have time for story, song, and sleep,” she said kindly. “But first, we need a quick cleanup. We take care of our room like we take care of the ocean.”

Dori looked around. Toys were everywhere—blocks, books, and a toy bus. A shiny shell, a drum, and a tiny world map lay on the rug. Dori’s tummy felt wiggly. “Oh no. That is a lot,” she whispered.

Mama smiled and pointed to a round clock. “Tick-tock, time to rock! We have three steps. Step one: five minutes to sort. Step two: five minutes to put away. Step three: one minute to rest. We can do it together.”

Dori took a deep breath. She sniffed the lemony air and felt brave. “Okay! I can try.”

“Let’s solve this like a puzzle,” Mama said. “Problem-solving is our superpower.”

Dori nodded. “Super Dori!”

They set the timer. Beep! Dori picked up a book. The cover showed children from many places—some wore bright hats, some wore scarves, some had beads. “Look, Mama!” Dori said. “So many friends from many lands.”

“Yes,” Mama said. “We all share Earth. We all share water. We all take care.”

Dori smiled. “We take turns, too!” She held up a blue block. “My turn to sort blocks!”

Mama picked up soft toys. “My turn to put plushies on the shelf.” Her voice was calm and musical. “After that, we switch.”

Dori moved the blocks into a basket. Plunk, plunk. The sound was happy. She smelled a little wood from the basket. “This is fun!”

“Tick-tock,” said the clock.

Dori found the shiny shell. “Oh! This shell is special.” She listened. The shell whispered shhhh like a quiet wave. “I love it.”

Mama nodded. “We can keep treasures. But we keep them safe and tidy so they don’t get lost or broken.”

Dori placed the shell in a tiny bowl. “Safe and tidy,” she said.

Beep! The timer chimed. “Step one is done,” Mama said. “Now step two. Your turn to put away the books. My turn to place the blocks.”

Dori lifted the books. “Too many,” she sighed.

Mama tapped the cover with the children. “Let’s try a small plan. Put away three books. Then take a tiny breath. Then three more.”

Dori counted, “One, two, three.” The books slid on the shelf with a soft thump. She took a tiny breath in—sniff—and out—whooo. “I can do it!”

They worked and switched turns. Dori put away books. Mama stacked blocks. Dori folded a little blanket. Mama plopped plushies on the bed. The fan went whoosh-whoosh. The clock went tick-tock. The rug felt cozy under Dori’s fins.

Then Dori found some paper and a crinkly bottle. She frowned. “This bottle is empty. It is not a toy.”

“You found a new job!” Mama said. “We sort our waste. That is called sustainability. It means we take care of Earth by reusing, recycling, and not wasting.”

Dori’s eyes sparkled. “I can do that. Paper goes to paper bin. Bottle goes to recycle bin. We keep our home clean like the ocean clean!”

“Yes,” Mama said. “We turn off lights when we leave, save water, and reuse boxes, too.”

Dori looked at the lamp. Click. She turned it a little dim. “Soft light for bedtime,” she said proudly. “We save energy.”

Beep! The timer chimed again. “Step three: one minute rest,” Mama said, sitting on the bed. “Let’s cuddle.”

Dori curled up. The blanket felt warm like sun on the sea. Mama hummed a gentle tune. Dori’s eyelids felt heavy, but she had one more thought.

“Mama,” she whispered, “when we share and take turns, cleaning is fast. When we plan our time, it is easy. When we sort and recycle, Earth is happy.”

Mama kissed Dori’s head. “You solved a big problem with a small plan. You used time like a helper. You cared for our room and our world.”

Dori grinned. “I am Super Dori, the Earth helper!”

“Tomorrow,” Mama said, “we will read the book about children from many countries. We will learn how they help Earth, too.”

Dori yawned. “We all share the water. We all share the sky. We all take turns to care.”

The room was tidy. The bins were sorted. The lamp was dim. The fan went whoosh-whoosh, like soft waves. Dori’s heart felt calm and bright.

“Good night, Super Dori,” Mama whispered. “Thank you for taking care.”

“Good night,” Dori murmured. “Plan, sort, and share. That’s how we show love.”

The End.
