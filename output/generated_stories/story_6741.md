# Kiki the Calm Koala’s Dinner of Feelings

Kiki the calm koala sat at the big wooden table in the dining room. The room smelled warm and cozy, like toast and honey. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. The table was set with colorful plates, tiny forks, and napkins folded like little tents.

Kiki’s friends were coming for a dinner party. There was Amina the rabbit with long soft ears, Jun the panda with bright curious eyes, and Leo the lion cub with a fluffy, tickly mane. Each friend was bringing a dish from home.

Kiki placed a paw on her chest and took a slow breath in and out. “I feel calm,” Kiki whispered. “I am ready to listen and share.”

Knock, knock! Amina hopped in first. “I brought carrot couscous,” she said, giggling. “It is soft and a little sweet.” The bowl smelled like cinnamon and sunshine.

Jun waddled in and set down dumplings that puffed like little pillows. “These are jiaozi,” Jun said. “They are warm and friendly.” Steam curled up, and Kiki could hear the dumplings whisper, shhh, like tiny clouds.

Leo trotted in with a bright bowl. “Mango salad!” he roared softly. “It’s tangy and juicy.” The mango shone like little suns.

Kiki clapped gently. “Thank you, friends. Let’s wash paws and sit together.”

They sat at the table. Plates clinked. The napkins felt soft like clouds. Kiki smiled and said, “We can taste, and we can say how we feel. All feelings are okay.”

Amina scooped couscous. “I feel excited!” she said. “It’s like sparkles in my tummy.”

Jun tried a dumpling and nodded. “I feel cozy, like a hug.”

Leo poked a dumpling with his fork. “I feel…unsure,” he said. “It looks new to me.” His ears drooped just a little.

Kiki noticed. She put a paw on her heart. “Thank you for telling us, Leo,” she said in a soft voice. “Unsure is a real feeling. Would you like a tiny taste?”

Leo took a tiny bite. He chewed slowly. “Hmm,” he said. “I feel curious now. It’s chewy and warm.”

Amina tasted the mango salad. Her nose wrinkled. “Oh! This is very tangy. I feel surprised.” She took a breath in and out. “I can try one more bite.” She smiled. “Now I feel brave.”

Kiki tasted everything. The couscous was fluffy, the dumplings were soft, and the mango was juicy. “I feel thankful,” Kiki said. “My mouth is dancing.”

Suddenly, a little spoon clattered on the floor. Clink! Leo’s eyes got wide. “I feel embarrassed,” he whispered. His cheeks felt hot.

Kiki bent down, picked up the spoon, and gave it back. “Thank you for telling us, Leo,” she said. “Spoons fall. Hearts can be gentle. Let’s take a breath together.” They all breathed in and out. The room felt calm again, like a quiet hug.

Jun said, “Sometimes I feel shy when I bring food from home. Will you like it? Will you not?” Jun’s eyes looked soft.

Amina nodded. “Sometimes I feel proud. Sometimes I feel worried, too.”

Kiki folded her paws. “Our foods come from our homes. Our homes give us stories. When we taste, we learn. When we listen, we care.”

Leo tried another dumpling. “I like your story, Jun,” he said. “Now I feel happy and full.”

They each shared one more feeling:
- Amina said, “I feel loved.”
- Jun said, “I feel seen.”
- Leo said, “I feel brave.”
- Kiki said, “I feel peaceful.”

Then Kiki brought out a small bowl of eucalyptus tea. The steam smelled minty and sweet. “This is from my home,” Kiki said. “It tastes like a gentle forest.” They sipped. The warm tea made their tummies feel cozy.

Kiki put three little cards on the table. Each card had a picture: a happy sun, a cloud with raindrops, and a star. “Point to how you feel now,” Kiki said.

Amina touched the sun. “Happy,” she said.

Jun touched the star. “Proud,” he smiled.

Leo touched the cloud and then the sun. “I feel a little cloudy and a lot sunny,” he said. “Both can be true.”

Kiki beamed. “Yes! Feelings can change, like the sky. We can notice them, name them, and be kind.”

They cleared the plates together. Clink, swish, pat. The napkins smelled like orange peels. The floor felt cool under their toes. Kiki hummed a soft tune that sounded like leaves in the wind.

Before they left, each friend taught a word:
- Amina said, “In my house we say shukran to say thank you.”
- Jun said, “We say xiexie.”
- Leo said, “We say thanks.”

Kiki placed a paw on her heart. “Shukran. Xiexie. Thanks,” she said. “I learned your words and your foods. I learned your feelings too.”

At the door, they hugged. Leo said, “When I feel unsure, I can take a tiny taste and a big breath.”

Jun said, “When I feel shy, I can share my story.”

Amina said, “When I feel surprised, I can try again.”

Kiki smiled. “When we listen to our feelings and to each other, we grow brave and kind.”

The dining room grew quiet and warm. The golden squares of sunlight stretched like sleepy cats. Kiki took one last calm breath and whispered, “Today, my heart learned.”

The End.
