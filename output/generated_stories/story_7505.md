# Koko's Kind Words Garden

Koko the koala loved spending quiet time in the old garden shed behind his house. The wooden walls smelled like fresh earth and sunshine. Today, he was organizing colorful seed packets when he heard loud voices outside.

"That's MY watering can!" shouted Benny the bunny.

"No, it's MINE!" yelled Penny the pig. "I saw it first!"

Koko's fuzzy ears twitched. His friends sounded upset. He took a deep breath, feeling the cool air fill his lungs, and stepped outside with gentle steps.

"Hello, friends," Koko said in his soft, calm voice. "I can hear you're both feeling frustrated. What's happening?"

Benny's nose was twitching fast. "Penny took my watering can! I need it for my carrots!"

Penny's face was red and puffy. "But I need it for my mud puddle garden! Benny is being mean!"

Koko sat down slowly on a tree stump, making himself smaller so his friends wouldn't feel scared. "It sounds like you both really need water for your special gardens. That must feel worrying."

"Yes!" both animals said together, then glared at each other again.

Koko smiled gently. "When I feel worried, I like to take three deep breaths. Want to try with me?" He breathed in slowly through his black nose. "In... and out... In... and out... In... and out."

Benny and Penny copied him. Their shoulders relaxed a little.

"Much better," Koko said. "Now, Benny, can you tell Penny how you feel using kind words?"

Benny thought for a moment. "Penny, I feel sad when you take things without asking. My carrots are very thirsty."

"Good listening words, Benny," Koko praised. "Penny, how do you feel?"

Penny's voice was quieter now. "I feel sad too. I was excited about my garden, and I forgot to ask nicely. I'm sorry, Benny."

Koko's eyes sparkled. "Beautiful words, Penny! You know what? I have an idea. Follow me to the shed."

Inside the cozy shed, Koko pointed to a shelf. "Look up there!"

On the high shelf sat three more watering cans - one red, one blue, and one yellow with flower stickers.

"Wow!" gasped Benny. "There are more!"

"My grandmother left these here for anyone who needed them," Koko explained. "She always said gardens grow better when friends share and use kind words."

Penny clapped her hooves. "Can I have the yellow one? The flowers are so pretty!"

"Of course," said Benny. "And I'll take the red one. It matches my fur!"

Koko felt warm and happy inside. "When we use peaceful words and listen to each other's feelings, problems become much easier to solve."

The three friends spent the afternoon watering their gardens together. Benny shared his crunchy carrots, Penny showed them how to make perfect mud pies, and Koko taught them the names of different flowers in the shed.

"Thank you for helping us use kind words," Penny said, giving Koko a gentle hug.

"Yeah!" agreed Benny. "Talking nicely feels much better than yelling."

As the sun set, painting the sky pink and orange, Koko smiled. His garden shed had become a place where friends learned that gentle words and listening hearts could solve any problem.

"Remember," Koko said softly, "when we speak with kindness and listen with care, everyone feels better, and friendships grow stronger - just like flowers in a garden."

The End.
