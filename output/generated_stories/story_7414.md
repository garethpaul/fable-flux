# Rocco’s Rest-and-Respect Rhythm

Rocco the raccoon loved music. He wore tiny headphones and a bright blue scarf. He had a small drum, a shiny triangle, and a gentle ukulele. His favorite place to play was the home office where his family worked. The office smelled like fresh paper and warm tea. The computer hummed a soft “hmmmmm.” Sunlight made little squares on the rug.

One morning, Rocco tip-tapped into the office. “Good morning!” he sang. “I will play a happy song!”

Mama sat at the desk, typing. “Good morning, Rocco,” she said. “I have a big call soon. I need quiet. After the call, we can play together.”

Rocco’s ears perked. “Quiet now. Play later,” he repeated. He wanted to do the right thing. But his paws wiggled. His tail twitched. The drum called to him. Tap tap! Tap tap!

“Just one tiny beat,” he whispered. Tap—tap—tap.

The computer went “beep!” Mama held up one finger. “Wait, please.”

Rocco took a deep breath. He pressed his paw to his chest. “In… out…” he whispered. The breath felt cool in his nose and warm in his belly. He remembered: Self-care helps my body and my brain. He hugged his tail. He sipped water from his little cup. Sip, sip. The water was cool and sweet.

Then the mail slot clinked. A paper slid onto the floor. Rocco gasped. “Mail music!” he chirped. He picked up the paper, but some mail got stuck under the office door. He poked his drumstick toward the gap.

“Stop,” he told himself. “Is this safe?” He looked at the door. He remembered the safety rules: No sticks under doors. No fingers under doors. Ask for help.

He pulled the drumstick back. “Mama, I need help,” he called. “The mail is stuck.”

Mama smiled and came over. “Thank you for asking. That was safe.” She bent down and gently pulled the letter out.

Rocco felt proud. “Safe and smart,” he said.

Just then, his cousin Rico the raccoon popped into the office. Rico wore a red cap and a grin. “Let’s make a band!” Rico cheered. He shook maracas—chicka-chicka! “We will play super loud!”

Rocco looked at Mama. She put a note on the desk that said, “Call at 10.” She whispered, “Quiet, please.”

Rico rolled his eyes. “But I want to play now.” He stomped one foot. Chicka-chicka echoed around the room.

Rocco felt a little mad inside. He wanted to play too. His tummy felt tight, like a knot. He remembered another self-care step: Name the feeling. Take a break.

“I feel angry,” Rocco said softly. “I feel jumpy. I want music, but Mama needs quiet.”

Rico crossed his arms. “I want music!”

Rocco nodded. “I hear you. Music is fun.” He put a paw on his heart. “Let’s solve this like helpers. We can use our words. We can make a plan.”

“Hmm,” Rico said.

Rocco looked around. The office chair was tall and spinny. The pencils smelled like cedar. The plant leaves were shiny and green. He had an idea.

“Rico,” Rocco said, “we can do a quiet rhythm. Soft like clouds.” He picked up his triangle and touched it very gently. Ting…ting… The sound was tiny and sweet.

Rico tried his maracas very, very softly. Chick… chick…

Rocco smiled. “Good! And we can use a timer. When the call is done, we can play big and loud in the living room, not the home office. That is safer for jumping and for ears.”

Rico’s eyes lit up. “A timer?”

Rocco set a little kitchen timer for ten minutes. Tick-tick-tick. “When it rings, we will check with Mama. That is fair. That is justice—everyone has a turn and a time.”

“Justice?” Rico asked.

“Justice means making fair choices,” Rocco explained in simple words. “We keep people safe. We listen. We share space.”

Rico nodded slowly. “Okay. Quiet clouds now. Big band later.”

They made cloud music. Ting… chick… Rocco hummed softly. Mmm-mmm. He felt calm again. His shoulders relaxed. He took another deep breath in and out. He smiled at Mama. Mama gave a thumbs-up.

Tick-tick-tick. The timer rang. Ding!

Mama finished her call. “Thank you, band,” she said. “You were safe and kind. I am proud of you.”

Rocco’s whiskers wiggled. He looked at Rico. “We did it! Let’s move our big music to the living room.”

They carried the drum and maracas out of the home office. They checked the floor for cords. They stepped carefully. Safety first! They put the instruments on a soft rug.

Rico laughed. “Ready for the big band?”

“Wait,” Rocco said. He remembered one more self-care step. “I need a rest and a snack. My body needs fuel.”

They washed their paws with warm water. Swish, swish. They dried them on a towel. Soft and fluffy. They ate apple slices. Crunch! They drank water. Sip, sip.

Rocco stretched his arms to the sky. “I feel strong and calm,” he said.

Rico nodded. “Me too. Let’s play!”

They made happy music—tap-tap-tap! Chicka-chicka! Strum-strum!—in the living room, not the home office. They watched each other’s faces and took turns. If a sound got too loud, Rocco raised his paw. They lowered the volume together. They smiled and laughed.

At the end, Rocco said, “Today I learned: Self-care helps me make good choices. When I feel big feelings, I can breathe, drink water, and ask for help. I can solve problems with words. I can be safe and fair.”

Mama clapped. “That is a beautiful rhythm, Rocco. Rest-and-respect!”

Rocco bowed. “Rest-and-Respect Rhythm,” he said. “That is our song.”

The End.
