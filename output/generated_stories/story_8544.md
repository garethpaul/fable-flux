# Kori the Calm Koala Cleans the Basement

Kori the calm koala liked quiet hugs and eucalyptus tea. One rainy day, Kori visited friends in a busy basement under a big house. The basement hummed and whirred. Fans spun. A washer chugged. Boxes made little mountains. The air smelled like soap and dust. Drip, drip, drip went a leaky pipe in the corner.

Kori blinked slowly and smiled. “Hello, basement,” Kori whispered. “I am calm and careful.”

Milo the mouse zipped by. “Squeak! Everything is noisy and messy!” he cried, twitching his whiskers.

Bea the beetle rolled a tiny bottle cap. “Clink, clink! I found shiny things!” she said.

Pip the pigeon flapped. “Coo! There is trash, and the bin is full,” he cooed, peeking into a wobbly trash can.

Kori’s round ears flicked. The basement floor had paper bits, old cans, and a broken toy truck. The busy basement was too busy.

Milo sighed. “I am small. I can’t help,” he said, looking down.

Bea clicked her legs. “I am just a bug. I can’t help,” she murmured.

Pip drooped his wings. “I am only a pigeon. I can’t help,” he cooed.

Kori placed a soft paw on a box. “We can help,” Kori said calmly. “We can care for our home. We can care for Earth. We are each important.”

Milo’s eyes grew bright. “Really? Even me?”

“Yes,” Kori said. “Every small help makes a big change.”

Drip, drip, drip went the pipe. Chug, chug went the washer. Kori took a deep breath. “Let’s make a plan,” Kori said. “Problem first. Then a calm solution.”

“What is the problem?” asked Bea.

Kori pointed gently. “The basement is messy. The trash is mixed. The bin is full. The pipe is dripping. It makes the room feel loud and wild. We feel small.”

Milo nodded. “Yes. That is the problem.”

Kori smiled. “Here is the solution. We will sort. We will save. We will fix. We will do it together. We will be kind to ourselves while we work.”

Bea tilted her head. “Sort? Save? Fix?”

“Sort means put things in the right place,” Kori said. “Trash here. Recycle there. Toys in a box to share.”

“Save?” Pip asked.

“Save means use less and keep good things,” Kori said. “Save water. Save energy. Turn off what we don’t use.”

“Fix?” Milo squeaked.

“Fix means make broken things better,” Kori said. “We will tell a grown-up about the drip.”

They all nodded. Kori drew three big circles on a clean box with a crayon: TRASH, RECYCLE, TOYS. The crayon smelled waxy and sweet. Kori handed out little cloth gloves. “Soft and safe,” Kori said.

Milo zipped to pick up paper bits. “Paper goes to recycle!” he cheered, tail swishing.

Bea rolled the tiny bottle cap. “Metal goes to recycle!” she clicked, proud and shiny.

Pip nudged the broken toy truck. “This can be fixed or shared,” he cooed, placing it in the TOYS box.

Kori wiped a spill with a rag. “Wipe, wipe,” Kori hummed. “We keep our space clean.”

The fan hummed. The washer finished and beeped. Kori reached up with a careful paw and pressed the button. “When not in use, we turn it off,” Kori said gently. “That saves energy.”

Milo looked at the leaky pipe. “Drip, drip,” he said. “It makes me worry.”

Kori nodded. “You noticed the drip. That is smart,” Kori said. “You are brave and helpful.” Kori hung a small bucket under the drip. “We will tell a grown-up. This bucket will catch the drips. That saves water.”

Milo’s whiskers twitched with pride. “I helped!”

Bea found a soft leaf sticker on a box. “Look! A green leaf,” she said. “It means we care.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “Coo! My wings are good for lifting light things,” he said, carrying empty boxes to a shelf.

They worked and sang a little cleaning song.

“Kori is calm, and we can be too.
Sort, save, and fix is what we do.
We help our world, we help our place.
We help ourselves with a kind, brave face.”

Soon the floor looked clear. The basement smelled like soap and fresh air. The hum was soft, not loud. The trash bin stood tall but not too full. The recycle box was ready to go. The toy box had treasures to share.

Kori clapped softly. “Look at what we did,” Kori said. “We cared for the basement. We cared for Earth. We cared for ourselves. We did it together.”

Milo puffed his chest. “I am small, but I am strong,” he said.

Bea clicked with joy. “I am just right the way I am.”

Pip flapped happily. “I am helpful and kind.”

Kori smiled, warm and wide. “You are each important,” Kori said. “You saw a problem. You made a plan. You found a solution. That is smart. That is brave. That is enough.”

A grown-up came down the stairs. “Thank you, helpers!” they said. “I will fix the pipe now.” The grown-up turned a wrench. Drip, drip turned into silence.

The basement felt peaceful. The friends sipped warm tea from tiny cups. The cups smelled like lemon. The friends felt proud and cozy.

Kori looked around. “When we care for our place, we care for our planet,” Kori said. “And when we try our best, we feel good inside.”

Milo, Bea, and Pip nodded. “We can help again tomorrow,” they chimed.

“Yes,” Kori said softly. “Calm hearts can do big things.”

The End.
