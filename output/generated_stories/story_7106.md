# Beep’s Big Bedtime Plan

In a cozy bedroom, soft and warm like a hug, a helpful robot dog named Beep blinked his bright blue eyes. Beep’s metal ears went tck-tck as they turned, and his tail made a happy whirr-whirr.

“Good morning, Beep!” said Lina, rubbing her sleepy eyes. She was small, with curly hair and a big smile. Sunlight peeped through the curtains. The room smelled like clean blankets and crayons.

Beep wagged his tail. “Good morning, Lina. Today we practice being independent,” he said in a gentle, beepy voice. “That means trying to do things by yourself.”

Lina nodded. “I can try!”

Beep’s nose light blinked. “First, we prepare.” He opened his little chest door with a click. Inside were picture cards. He placed them on the floor: a sock picture, a toothbrush picture, a toy box picture, and a green recycle bin picture.

“Let’s make a plan,” Beep said. “A plan helps our day feel smooth.”

Lina clapped. “Plan, plan!”

“Step one: get dressed,” Beep said. “Step two: brush teeth. Step three: clean and recycle. Step four: quiet time and boundaries. Step five: bedtime cuddle and story.”

Lina picked up her rainbow socks. “I can do step one!”

She sat on the soft rug that felt like a cloud. She pushed her toes into the socks. “One sock… two sock!” She giggled. Her sock went on sideways. “Oh!”

“You’re doing great,” Beep encouraged. “Try again. You can do it.”

Lina tugged and twisted. The sock slid on snug. “I did it by myself!” she cheered.

Beep’s tail went whirr-whirr. “Independent win!”

“Step two?” Lina asked.

“To the sink,” Beep said. They walked across the cozy bedroom to the little bathroom door. The mirror was shiny. The toothpaste smelled minty.

“I squeeze,” Lina said, holding the tube.

“Please ask first,” Beep reminded kindly. “Personal boundaries mean we ask before touching someone else or their things. That keeps everyone safe and happy.”

Lina nodded. “May I squeeze, Beep?”

“Yes,” Beep said. “Thank you for asking.”

Lina squeezed a tiny dot. “Pea-size!” she said proudly. She brushed—swish, swish, swish—little circles. Bubbles tasted cool and minty. She spit and rinsed. “All done!”

Beep beeped softly. “Step three: clean and recycle.”

Back in the cozy bedroom, toys were everywhere. Blocks, books, and a shiny juice box from snack time sat on the floor.

Beep showed two bins. One had a toy picture. One had a green triangle sign. “Toys go in the toy bin. Paper, plastic, and metal go into the recycling bin.”

Lina picked up the juice box. It was empty and crinkly. “Recycle?” she asked.

“Good thinking,” said Beep. “Let’s check. Is it empty? Is it clean?”

Lina peeked. “Empty! I’ll put it in the green bin.”

Clink! The box dropped in.

She gathered blocks. “Blocks in toy bin,” she said. The blocks made a soft clatter as she stacked them neatly. She smoothed her blanket. The room looked calm. It felt nice.

Beep nodded. “You prepared a clean space. That makes play and rest easier.”

Lina smiled. “I can do things!”

A soft knock came at the door. Lina’s big brother, Max, peeked in. “Hi, Lina, can I play cars?”

Lina hugged her favorite bear. She took a tiny breath. “Um… not right now,” she said. “I want quiet time.”

Max nodded. “Okay. Thanks for telling me.” He waved and closed the door gently.

Beep wagged. “That was a healthy boundary. You used your words.”

Lina felt proud and calm. Quiet time felt soft and peaceful. She listened to the tick-tick of the clock and the gentle hum-hum of Beep’s motor.

Beep placed the last picture card: a moon and a star. “Step four is quiet time. Step five is bedtime.”

“Story, please,” Lina said.

“First,” Beep said, “let’s prepare for tomorrow. Preparation helps you be independent.” He opened his chest again and pulled out a tiny checklist with pictures: pajamas, tomorrow’s clothes, backpack.

Lina chose a comfy pajama set. The fabric felt warm. She put it on by herself, one sleeve, then the other. She placed tomorrow’s shirt and pants on a chair. She put her small backpack by the door.

“I’m ready,” she said.

Beep dimmed his eye lights. The lamp glowed like a sleepy firefly. The room smelled like lavender lotion. Lina climbed into bed. The sheets whispered shhh as she snuggled in.

“Beep,” Lina whispered, “can you tuck me?”

Beep paused. “May I touch your blanket?” he asked.

“Yes,” Lina said.

Beep tucked the blanket gently under her chin. “Thank you for saying yes.”

“Beep, I did so many things by myself,” Lina murmured, cozy and happy.

“You did,” said Beep. “You dressed yourself, brushed with care, recycled, cleaned up, set a boundary, and prepared for tomorrow.”

Lina’s eyes grew heavy. “I feel big,” she whispered.

“You are growing,” Beep said softly. “Independence means trying first, asking for help when you need it, and being kind to others and to yourself.”

Lina yawned. “Goodnight, Beep.”

“Goodnight, Lina.” Beep’s tail made a tiny whirr, like a lullaby. “Tomorrow, you will try again. You can do many things.”

Lina smiled and drifted to sleep, safe and proud in her cozy bedroom, with her helpful robot dog by her side.

The End.
