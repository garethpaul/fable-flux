# Finny’s Garden Shed Plan

Finny was a curious fox kit with bright eyes and a twitchy nose. One sunny morning, he peeked into the garden shed behind the big oak tree. The shed smelled like pine wood and fresh grass. Sunbeams danced in the dusty air. Finny’s whiskers wiggled.

“I want to plant a tiny garden,” Finny whispered. “That is my goal.”

He felt excited. He also felt a little wiggly inside. The shed was full of tools and boxes. A rake leaned on the wall. A soft, old straw hat hung on a hook. Small clay pots sat in a row like little cups.

Finny took a deep breath. “Step one,” he said, holding up one paw. “Make a plan.”

He looked around. On a shelf, he found a small notepad with a pencil. He could just hold the pencil between his tiny paws. He drew a big circle and a smiley sun. Then he printed his plan in simple steps:

1. Find seeds.
2. Find tools.
3. Get water.
4. Plant seeds.
5. Take care every day.

“Good plan,” Finny said. His tail swished.

Just then, a ladybug landed on his nose. “Hello!” Finny giggled. The ladybug tickled. “I’m making a garden. Want to watch?”

The ladybug opened her tiny red wings and buzzed to a clay pot.

“Step two,” Finny said happily. “Find seeds.”

He searched a wooden box. Swish, swish went the sound of packets sliding. He smelled paper and earth. He found a packet with a picture of green leaves. “Lettuce!” he read slowly. “And carrots!” he found another. Finny clapped his paws. “Yum.”

“Step three: Find tools,” Finny told himself. He spotted a small trowel with a shiny scoop. He tested it in the air. It felt cool and smooth. He also found tiny gloves that smelled like clean cotton. He tugged them on. They were a little big, but they stayed.

“Step four: Get water,” Finny said. He saw a little blue watering can. When he lifted it, it sloshed. Swish-slosh, swish-slosh. He smiled.

Finny carried the pots outside the shed. The ground was warm under his paws. A breeze smelled like clover. Birds chirped, “Cheep, cheep,” from the fence.

But when Finny looked at the ground, he paused. “Uh-oh,” he said. “Where should the pots go?”

He remembered his plan. He looked up. The sun was bright on one side. “Plants like sun,” Finny said. He placed the pots where the sun touched. He patted the soil inside each pot. It felt soft and crumbly, like chocolate cake crumbs.

“Dig, dig,” he sang, using the trowel. He made little holes. He opened the seed packets and peeked inside. The seeds were tiny, like dots and dashes.

“Hello, little seeds,” Finny whispered. “I have a goal. I will help you grow.”

He dropped a few seeds into each hole. “Not too many,” he said gently. “Give them space.” He covered them with soft soil and pressed with his paw. “Tuck-tuck,” he murmured.

Now he lifted the blue watering can. “Sip, not splash,” he told himself. He tilted the can and watched the water drizzle. Drip, drop, drip, drop. The soil turned darker and smelled even more like earth. The ladybug landed on a pot rim and watched with tiny black eyes.

Finny felt proud. He checked his notepad again.

1. Find seeds. “Check!”
2. Find tools. “Check!”
3. Get water. “Check!”
4. Plant seeds. “Check!”
5. Take care every day. He circled this one. “This is the big step,” he said. “This is my daily goal.”

He thought for a moment. “How will I remember?” He looked back into the shed and saw the old straw hat. He placed it on a peg near the door, low enough for him to reach.

“When I see the hat, I will water,” Finny decided. “The hat will be my reminder.”

The next morning, Finny trotted to the shed. He smelled dew and heard a faraway bee hum. He saw the straw hat and smiled. “Time to water!” Drip, drop, drip, drop. He whispered, “Good morning, seeds.”

Every day, Finny did the same. He checked the soil. He pulled tiny weeds with careful paws. He gently touched the top of the soil: cool and damp or warm and dry? If it felt dry, he gave a sip. If it felt damp, he waited.

One day, a small green loop pushed up. “A sprout!” Finny gasped. His heart felt big. “Hello, little one.” Then more loops came, like tiny green question marks.

Finny danced in a circle. The ladybug flew up, up, and then landed on his ear. “We did it!” Finny laughed.

He looked at his notepad. All steps had check marks. He added one more line: 6. Celebrate and keep caring.

Days passed. The sprouts grew leaves. Finny tasted one tiny leaf with a careful nibble. It was fresh and sweet. He felt proud, calm, and happy.

He stood by the garden shed and spoke softly. “I set a goal. I made a plan. I took small steps every day. I prepared my tools and water. I watched and waited. And now my garden grows.”

The wind rustled the leaves. The birds sang. The ladybug lifted her wings and buzzed in the sun.

Finny hugged the blue watering can. “I can reach goals,” he said. “I can plan, prepare, and do a little each day.”

He smiled at the tiny garden, shining in the light. “Tomorrow, I will come back and care again.”

The End.
