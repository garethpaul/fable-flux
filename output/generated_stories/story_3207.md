# Panda Ping’s Laundry Day Parade

Panda Ping was a patient panda with soft black ears and a round, warm belly. Today was laundry day. Ping padded into the laundry room, where the washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” like a sleepy bee. The room smelled like clean soap and warm towels. Sunlight sprinkled little squares on the floor.

Ping smiled. “Today, I will wash everyone’s team scarves,” he said. The scarves were colorful and special, from many friends. Each scarf came from a different place, with a different story.

Ping picked up a red scarf with tiny golden stars. “This is Mei’s scarf from China,” he said softly. He traced the stars with his paw. “Mei says hello as ‘Nǐ hǎo.’ Nǐ hǎo,” Ping practiced, slow and kind. He liked learning new words.

He lifted a blue scarf with white snowflakes. “This is from Leo, who speaks Spanish.” Ping practiced, “Hola.” He giggled when the sound tickled his tongue.

Next was a green scarf with bright yellow suns. “This is Ayo’s scarf from Nigeria,” Ping said. “He taught me ‘Bawo ni?’ It means, ‘How are you?’” Ping looked at the scarf and whispered, “Bawo ni?” The scarf felt smooth and warm, like sunshine.

There was also a purple scarf with tiny music notes from France. Ping tried, “Bonjour.” The word felt like a small hop. “Bon-jour!” He gave a happy hop.

Ping lined up the scarves and read the tags carefully. “Gentle wash. Cold water,” he said. He took a deep breath. “I will be patient. I will do it right.”

Just then, Ping’s friends peeked in. Mei the kitten bounced in with easy steps. “Nǐ hǎo, Ping!” she sang.

“Hola, Ping!” said Leo the lamb, shaking his fluffy wool.

“Bawo ni, Ping?” called Ayo the puppy, his tail wagging fast.

“Bonjour, Ping,” said Colette the chick, peeping softly.

Ping’s heart felt cozy. “Hello, friends! Today is Laundry Day Parade,” he said. “We will wash your scarves with care. Then we will hang them like flags!”

The washing machine door clicked open with a soft “clack.” Ping put in the scarves, one by one. He poured in soap that smelled like fresh rain. “Teamwork time,” Ping said. “Mei, please press gentle wash.”

Mei tapped the button. Beep! The machine began to spin. “Whirr, swish, shush,” it sang. Everyone listened. The sounds were like waves.

While they waited, Ping said, “Let’s share words from our homes.” He made a small circle with his paws. “One by one.”

Mei smiled. “In Chinese, thank you is ‘Xièxiè.’” She bowed a little.

Ping bowed back. “Xièxiè.”

Leo bleated happily. “In Spanish, friend is ‘Amigo.’”

“Amigo,” Ping said, and touched Leo’s warm wool.

Ayo wagged. “In Yoruba, please is ‘Jọwọ.’”

“Jọwọ,” Ping repeated. The word felt soft, like a pillow.

Colette peeped. “In French, good is ‘Bon.’ Like a bon day!”

“Bon,” Ping said, and everyone giggled.

The machine slowed. “Whirr… shhhh… click.” Ping opened the door. Warm mist puffed out, and the scarves smelled like clean clouds. “Mmm,” said everyone.

Ping lifted the red scarf. It was tangled with the blue one. He stopped. “I will be patient,” he whispered. Carefully, he wiggled them apart. “Gentle, gentle.” He felt proud when they came free.

They carried the damp scarves to a line by the sunny window. The clothespins felt like little birds in Ping’s paws. “Teamwork!” Ping said.

- Mei pinned the red scarf and said, “Nǐ hǎo” to the sun.
- Leo clipped the blue scarf and said, “Hola” to the breeze.
- Ayo hung the green scarf and said, “Bawo ni” to the room.
- Colette placed the purple scarf and said, “Bonjour” to the light.

Ping stood back. The scarves fluttered like happy flags. “Listen,” he said. The room was full of soft flaps and tiny taps, the whisper of drying fabric. The colors danced—red, blue, green, purple—like a friendly rainbow.

Then Ping noticed a small spot on Ayo’s scarf. “Oh,” he said. “A tiny stain.” He did not rush. He brought a little bowl of water and a drop of soap. “Pat, pat, circle,” he said, moving slow. Ayo watched with big eyes. “You are careful,” Ayo said.

Ping smiled. “Care means love,” he answered. Together, they rinsed the spot. “Clean!” said Ayo, wagging faster.

When the scarves were dry, the friends tried them on. Mei twirled. Leo bounced. Ayo did a happy wiggle. Colette fluffed her feathers. Ping put each scarf around each friend gently, like a hug.

They stood in a row. Ping raised his paw. “Laundry Day Parade!” he called.

They marched around the laundry room. Their feet went “step, step.” The scarves swished, “fwoosh.” They took turns leading. Each friend called a kind word in their language.

“Nǐ hǎo!” called Mei.

“Hola!” called Leo.

“Bawo ni!” called Ayo.

“Bonjour!” called Colette.

Ping listened and clapped his paws. “All these words are welcome,” he said. “All these colors are welcome. We are a team.”

The friends circled Ping and hugged him. He felt warm, like fresh towels. “Thank you, Ping,” they said.

Ping looked at the bright scarves and his smiling friends. “Today I learned that every scarf has a story,” he said. “Every language is a song. When we are patient and work together, we can care for each other.”

Everyone nodded. Mei whispered, “Xièxiè.”

Leo said, “Gracias.”

Ayo said, “O se.”

Colette peeped, “Merci.”

Ping’s eyes shone. “You are my friends. Amigo. Ami. Péjú. Péngyǒu,” he tried, giggling as the words danced.

They all laughed. The laundry room felt big and bright, full of clean smells, soft sounds, and kind hearts. The scarves fluttered, a parade of friendship.

The End.
