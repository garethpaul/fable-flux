# Pip's Special Space

Pip was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright black eyes. She lived in a cozy corner of Mr. Green's garden shed. The shed smelled like fresh wood and dried flowers. Pip loved her tiny home behind the flower pots.

Every morning, Pip would peek out from her corner. She could see garden tools hanging on the walls. Bags of seeds sat on wooden shelves. Sunlight streamed through the dusty window, making everything glow.

Pip had made her corner very special. She had a soft nest made of cotton and leaves. She kept her favorite acorns in a small tin box. Her corner felt safe and warm.

One day, a family of field mice moved into the shed. There was Papa Mouse, Mama Mouse, and three little baby mice. They were very loud and messy.

"Hello!" said Papa Mouse. "This shed is perfect for all of us!"

The mice family spread out everywhere. They scattered seeds all over the floor. The baby mice played tag around Pip's corner. They knocked over her acorn box.

"Excuse me," said Pip politely. "This corner is my special space."

But the mice family didn't listen. They were too busy exploring. One baby mouse even tried to sleep in Pip's cozy nest!

Pip felt worried. Her whiskers twitched nervously. "What should I do?" she wondered. "I want to be kind, but I need my own space too."

Pip thought hard. She remembered what her grandmother had taught her: "It's okay to say no when someone crosses your boundaries."

The next morning, Pip took a deep breath. She stood up tall and spoke clearly.

"I'm happy you're here," Pip said to the mouse family. "But I need to keep my corner for myself. It's my special space where I feel safe."

Papa Mouse looked surprised. "Oh! We didn't know this was your home."

"Yes," said Pip bravely. "I've lived here for a long time. This corner has my nest and my things."

Mama Mouse nodded kindly. "We understand. Everyone needs their own special space."

Pip smiled. "But I have an idea! The shed is big. We can share it together. You can have that side, and I'll keep this corner."

The baby mice clapped their tiny paws. "That sounds fair!"

Working together, they organized the shed. Pip showed them the best spots for nests. She shared her knowledge about where Mr. Green kept the bird seeds. The mice family helped clean up the scattered seeds.

"Thank you for teaching us about boundaries," said Papa Mouse. "In our old home, we all shared everything. But now we know it's important to respect each other's special spaces."

Pip felt proud and happy. Her corner was safe again, and she had new friends too. The shed felt peaceful with everyone respecting each other.

From that day on, all the mice lived happily together. They played games in the middle of the shed. They shared meals by the window. But they always respected each other's special spaces.

When bedtime came, each mouse family went to their own cozy corner. Pip snuggled into her soft nest, feeling safe and content.

"Speaking up for myself was the right thing to do," Pip whispered to herself. "Good boundaries make good friendships."

The garden shed was quiet and peaceful. Everyone felt happy and respected. And Pip learned that being kind doesn't mean giving up what's important to you.

The End.
