# Squeaky's Fair Share Day

In a cozy closet behind the winter coats lived a kind little mouse named Squeaky. Her hideaway was warm and soft, filled with scraps of fabric and shiny buttons she had collected. The closet smelled like lavender sachets and old leather shoes.

One sunny morning, Squeaky heard tiny voices arguing outside her door. She peeked out and saw three baby mice sitting by a pile of colorful cereal pieces.

"I want all the red ones!" squeaked the first baby mouse, grabbing with his tiny paws.

"No fair! I saw them first!" cried the second baby mouse, her whiskers twitching.

"But I'm the biggest!" said the third baby mouse, pushing the others away.

Squeaky's heart felt heavy watching them fight. She took a deep breath, counting slowly to three like her mama had taught her. "One... two... three," she whispered softly to herself, feeling calmer.

"Hello, little ones," Squeaky said gently, stepping out of her hideaway. "What seems to be the problem?"

The baby mice all started talking at once, their voices getting louder and squeakier.

"Stop, stop," Squeaky said with a warm smile. "Let's talk one at a time. That's much fairer."

She sat down on her soft tail and patted the ground. "Come sit with me."

The first baby mouse sniffled. "We found these yummy cereal pieces, but we can't agree how to share them."

Squeaky nodded thoughtfully. "Sharing can be tricky sometimes. But I have an idea that might help. Would you like to hear it?"

All three baby mice nodded eagerly, their dark eyes sparkling.

"When I was little like you," Squeaky began, "my mama taught me a special way to share fairly. We can count all the pieces together, then divide them equally. That means everyone gets the same amount."

"But what if there are extra pieces?" asked the second baby mouse.

Squeaky's whiskers twitched with pride. "Great question! If there are extras, we can take turns choosing who gets them. Today, tomorrow, and the next day."

The baby mice looked at each other, then back at Squeaky. "Will you help us?" they asked together.

"Of course!" Squeaky said cheerfully.

Together, they counted the cereal pieces. "One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine!" they counted out loud. The closet filled with their happy voices.

"Nine pieces for three mice," Squeaky said. "That means each mouse gets three pieces, with no extras left over. Perfect!"

The baby mice clapped their tiny paws. "That is fair!" they squeaked.

As they divided the cereal, Squeaky felt proud and confident. She had helped solve the problem calmly and fairly. The sweet smell of the cereal mixed with the lavender scent of her closet home.

"Thank you, Squeaky!" said all three baby mice. "You're so smart and kind!"

"You're very welcome," Squeaky replied. "Remember, when we share fairly, everyone feels happy. And when we stay calm and think together, we can solve any problem."

The baby mice munched their cereal happily, and Squeaky returned to her cozy hideaway with a warm feeling in her heart. She had learned something important too - helping others made her feel confident and proud.

From that day on, whenever the baby mice had trouble sharing, they came to Squeaky's closet hideaway for help. And Squeaky was always ready with a calm voice, a fair solution, and a gentle smile.

The End.
