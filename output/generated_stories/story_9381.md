# Pebble’s Closet Club

Pebble the cheerful penguin loved to waddle, wiggle, and sing. “Waddle-waddle, wiggle-wiggle, peep!” she chirped. One rainy day, Pebble found a cozy closet hideaway. It smelled like clean soap and pine. Soft coats brushed her wings. On the floor lay a fluffy rug like fresh snow.

“This is perfect,” Pebble said. “A secret club!” She clapped her little flippers.

Two friends peeked in. Tilly the turtle slowly smiled. “Can I come in?” she asked.

“Me too?” asked Buzz the busy bee, his wings making a gentle bzz-bzz.

“Yes!” said Pebble. “We can all share. Come feel the soft sleeves and hear the rain tap-tap on the door.”

They sat together. Pebble listened to the rain. Tilly touched a fuzzy scarf. Buzz sniffed the air. “Mmm, it smells like lemon soap,” Buzz said.

Pebble made a sign with a crayon: “Closet Club.” She hummed, “Waddle-waddle, closet club!” Tilly giggled. Buzz buzzed a harmony.

Soon, a little problem grew. Pebble wanted to line up shiny buttons she found in a tin. Tilly wanted the rug for a slow-and-steady nap. Buzz wanted to fly loops in the tiny space.

Buzz zoomed in circles. “Loop! Loop! Bzz!”

Tilly yawned. “Too busy,” she said. “I need quiet.”

Pebble set the buttons in a row. “Red, blue, green.” But the rug rippled, and the buttons rolled. “Oh no!”

Buzz swooped low and whooshed the buttons out of their line. Tilly tucked into her shell, bumping the tin. Clink-clank!

Pebble’s smile drooped. “Friends, I feel jumbled inside. The closet is cozy, but we are bumping and bumping.”

Tilly nodded slowly. “I feel squished.”

Buzz hovered. “I feel zoomy!”

Pebble took a deep breath. She listened to the gentle rain. Tap-tap-tap. An idea glimmered like a tiny star. “Let’s use a plan,” she said. “We can talk. We can share. We can take turns.”

Tilly peeked out. “A plan?”

Buzz tilted his head. “A sharing plan?”

Pebble made three simple rules and said them in a song:
- “We use kind words.”
- “We take turns.”
- “We make space for each friend.”

She looked at Tilly. “What do you need?”

Tilly patted the rug. “I need a quiet corner.”

Pebble smiled. “Good idea.” She moved two long coats to make a snug nook. Swish-swish. “This is your nap corner.”

Tilly sighed with happy relief. “Thank you.”

Pebble asked Buzz, “What do you need?”

Buzz buzzed softly. “I need flying space.”

Pebble pointed to the higher shelf area where hangers made a little arch. “This can be your flying loop. We will keep it clear.”

Buzz zipped up. “Loop loop!” His wings hummed, but not too loud now.

Pebble looked at her tin. “I need a flat place for my button game.” She spread a scarf on a shoebox lid. Smooth-smooth. “This will be my game table.”

Pebble clapped. “Now we need a turn timer.” She placed a tiny bell in the center. “When the bell rings, we switch.”

Tilly smiled. “I can nap first for five raindrops.”

Buzz nodded. “Then I fly for five raindrops.”

“And I’ll play buttons for five raindrops,” Pebble said.

They listened. Drip, drop, drip, drop, drip. Pebble rang the bell. Ding!

Tilly curled into her nook. “Mmm. Cozy.” Buzz hovered gently, making quiet little eee-eee notes. Pebble lined up buttons: “One, two, three.” The closet felt calm and happy.

After five raindrops, ding! Buzz took his turn to fly higher loops. Pebble cheered, “Go, Buzz, go!” Tilly watched with a rested smile.

Another ding! Pebble shared her button game. “Let’s sort by color together,” she said. “Red here, blue here, green here.”

Buzz landed and nudged a green button. “Green!” Tilly slid a red one slowly. “Red.” Pebble giggled. “Teamwork!”

Soon the closet was a true club for all. The coats made soft whooshes. The rug felt warm. The air smelled lemony and safe. Pebble felt her smile return, big and bright.

But then a new problem came. The door clicked. Click! It was a little stuck.

Buzz buzzed, “Uh-oh.”

Tilly blinked. “We need a strategy.”

Pebble nodded. “Friends, let’s try together.” She spoke kindly. “Buzz, please check the top. Tilly, please nudge the bottom. I will push the handle.”

“Ready?” Pebble asked.

“Ready,” said Tilly.

“Ready!” buzzed Buzz.

“Push, nudge, lift!” Pebble counted, “One, two, three!” They moved together. The door opened with a soft creak. Creeeak!

“We did it!” Pebble cheered.

They stepped out, then back in, because the closet hideaway was still their happy place. Pebble rang the bell. Ding! “Closet Club friends,” she said, “What did we learn?”

Tilly said, “We use kind words.”

Buzz added, “We make a plan!”

Pebble finished, “We share and take turns. That is friendship.”

They hugged—wing, shell, and tiny buzzing body. The rain slowed to a gentle patter. Pebble sang, “Waddle-waddle, friends together,” and her friends joined in.

The Closet Club glowed with warm smiles and calm hearts. They had solved problems with talking, taking turns, and working as a team. And every rainy day after, they used their special plan, so everyone felt safe, heard, and happy.

The End.
