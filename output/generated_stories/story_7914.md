# Melody's Big Art Show

In a cozy living room filled with colorful cushions and toy boxes, lived a little yellow bird named Melody. She had the sweetest voice and loved to sing happy songs all day long. The living room was her favorite place because it had big, sunny windows and soft carpets perfect for dancing.

One morning, Melody woke up with a wonderful idea. "Tweet, tweet! I want to make beautiful art for all my friends!" she chirped excitedly, fluffing her bright yellow feathers.

Melody flew to the toy box and found some crayons scattered on the bottom. "Oh my!" she said, tilting her tiny head. "These crayons are all mixed up and some don't have their tips anymore. How can I make pretty pictures with broken crayons?"

She tried to draw with a crayon that had no point. The colors came out thick and bumpy. "This doesn't look right," Melody sighed, her little wings drooping.

Just then, her friend Oliver the teddy bear waddled over. "What's wrong, Melody?" he asked in his warm, fuzzy voice.

"I want to make art for everyone, but my crayons are all messy and broken," Melody explained, showing him the scattered supplies.

Oliver smiled kindly. "You know what, Melody? Before we start any big project, we need to prepare! That means getting everything ready first."

"Prepare?" Melody asked, her bright black eyes sparkling with curiosity. "What does that mean?"

"It means we organize our supplies and make sure everything is ready before we begin," Oliver explained. "Let's work together!"

First, they sorted all the crayons by color. Melody sang a little rainbow song: "Red and orange, yellow too, green and blue and purple too!" as they made neat piles on the soft carpet.

Next, Oliver helped sharpen the crayon tips using a special sharpener they found in the art box. "Now they have nice points for drawing details!" Melody chirped happily.

Then they found clean paper and spread it out on the low coffee table. They even got a cup of water for cleaning their hands and some tissues for wiping.

"Now everything is ready!" Oliver announced.

Melody felt so excited and organized. She began to draw the most beautiful pictures - a sunny garden with red flowers, a blue sky with fluffy white clouds, and a rainbow with every color perfectly bright.

"Tweet, tweet! Look how much better my art looks when I prepare first!" Melody sang joyfully, dancing on her tiny orange feet.

Her friends - Oliver, Luna the stuffed rabbit, and Max the toy dinosaur - all gathered around to admire her colorful artwork.

"Your pictures are wonderful, Melody!" Luna said, clapping her soft paws.

"And you learned something very important," Max added with a big smile. "When we prepare and organize our things first, everything works so much better!"

Melody felt proud and happy. She had learned that taking time to get ready made her art time more fun and successful. From that day on, whenever Melody wanted to create something special, she always prepared first.

"Preparation makes everything better!" she sang in her sweetest voice, and all her friends joined in the happy song.

The End.
