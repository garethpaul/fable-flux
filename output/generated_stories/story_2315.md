# Pebble the Penguin Says “I’m Sorry”

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a happy waddle. Pebble lived with Nana on the tenth floor, where the wind was breezy and the sun felt warm. Outside their door, there was a tiny balcony garden. It had little pots of mint, tomatoes, and tiny strawberries. The garden smelled fresh and sweet, like morning rain.

One sunny morning, Pebble waddled onto the balcony. “Good morning, garden!” Pebble chirped. The mint leaves looked shiny, and a ladybug sat on a leaf, taking a tiny rest.

Nana smiled. “Pebble, we water, we wait, and we watch. That’s how plants grow.”

Pebble nodded. “Water, wait, watch,” Pebble repeated, tapping wings together.

Pebble loved to help. Pebble loved to move fast, too. “Zoom!” Pebble giggled and slid across the balcony on a small mat like a tiny ice slide.

But the mat bumped a pot. “Uh-oh!” Pebble cried.

The strawberry plant tipped. Dirt spilled. A green leaf bent. The ladybug flew away.

Pebble’s tummy felt twisty. Pebble’s cheeks felt hot. Pebble looked at the messy dirt. Pebble wanted to hide behind the watering can.

Nana came close. “Pebble, are you okay?”

Pebble looked down at penguin feet. “I made a mess,” Pebble whispered. “I broke the plant.”

Nana knelt and spoke softly. “Let’s take a breath. In through the nose, out through the beak.”

Pebble sniffed in. Pebble puffed out. “In… out…” Pebble felt the air move slow and cool. The twisty feeling got a little softer.

Nana nodded. “Now, what is the kind thing to do?”

Pebble blinked. Pebble knew. Pebble took another breath. Pebble waddled closer to the plant and to Nana. Pebble said, with a small but brave voice, “I’m sorry. I bumped the pot. I wasn’t careful.”

Nana’s eyes were gentle. “Thank you for your careful words, Pebble. Saying ‘I’m sorry’ is a strong, kind thing.”

Pebble’s chest felt lighter, like a bubble floating up. “Can I help fix it?” Pebble asked.

“Yes,” said Nana. “We can fix things when we can. That is healthy and kind.”

Pebble got a little scoop. Pebble felt the cool, crumbly dirt on penguin flippers. Pebble listened to the soft scratch of the scoop and the quiet rustle of leaves. Pebble and Nana lifted the plant. They tucked the roots back into the pot, like cozy toes back into a sock.

“Thank you, plant,” Pebble whispered. “We will be gentle.”

They gave the plant a tiny sip of water. Drip, drip, drip. Pebble watched the shiny drops slide into the soil. Pebble noticed the sun on the leaves and the small, sweet smell.

Nana smiled. “You used your senses. You looked, you listened, you smelled. That is called being mindful.”

Pebble tried the new word. “Mind-ful,” Pebble said slowly, nodding.

A little white flower peeked from the strawberry plant. Pebble pointed. “Look! A flower!”

“That is a lovely discovery,” said Nana. “A flower means the plant is trying again.”

Pebble felt proud and calm. Pebble thought about moving fast. Pebble also thought about being careful. “I can move slow on the balcony,” Pebble said. “Slow is safe for plants.”

“That’s a healthy habit,” Nana said. “Slow feet, steady wings, and water, wait, watch.”

Pebble practiced. Pebble stepped heel, toe, heel, toe, very slow. The pots did not wobble. The mint did not shake. The ladybug came back and landed on Pebble’s flipper. It tickled!

“Hello, tiny friend,” Pebble giggled.

Then Pebble looked at Nana. “Nana, when I bump or spill, I can say, ‘I’m sorry,’ and I can help fix it. I can breathe to calm my tummy. I can use slow feet.”

Nana hugged Pebble. Pebble felt warm and safe. “Yes,” Nana said. “That is a smart plan.”

Later, Pebble helped wipe the balcony. Pebble washed penguin flippers with soap and water. The bubbles smelled like lemon. Swish, swish, swish. Clean flippers were another healthy habit.

The sun began to set, painting the sky pink and gold. Pebble and Nana sipped cool water and ate one tiny, ripe strawberry. It tasted sweet and juicy.

Pebble looked at the plant and spoke softly. “Thank you for growing. I will be gentle.”

Nana asked, “Pebble, what did you learn today?”

Pebble thought. Pebble felt the soft breeze and heard a dove coo. Pebble said, “I learned to say ‘I’m sorry’ when I make a mistake. I learned to take deep breaths to help my body feel calm. I learned to move slow and be careful in our garden. I learned that fixing and helping feels good.”

Nana nodded. “Beautiful lessons.”

Pebble smiled big. “Water, wait, watch,” Pebble said again. “And say sorry, breathe, and be gentle.”

The strawberry leaves rustled like they were clapping. The ladybug opened tiny wings. Pebble waved. The balcony garden felt peaceful and happy.

Pebble cuddled close to Nana. “Tomorrow,” Pebble whispered, “I will be mindful again.”

“And I will be proud again,” said Nana.

They watched the soft colors fade and the first star wink hello. Pebble’s eyes grew sleepy. Pebble felt calm, kind, and brave.

The garden was neat. The plant was safe. Pebble’s heart felt light.

The End.
