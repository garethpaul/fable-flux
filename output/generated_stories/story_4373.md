# Fern's Fair Share Adventure

Little Fern the deer fawn peeked through the big kitchen window with her bright brown eyes. The warm morning sun made the kitchen smell like fresh pancakes and sweet honey. Her soft nose twitched as she watched Mama Bear making breakfast inside.

"Oh my!" said Fern softly. "That smells so yummy!"

Mama Bear noticed the gentle fawn at her window and smiled. "Good morning, little Fern! Would you like to come in for breakfast?"

Fern's heart felt warm and happy. She carefully stepped through the open kitchen door, her small hooves clicking gently on the wooden floor. The kitchen was cozy and bright, with sunbeams dancing on the yellow walls.

"I'm making pancakes for all my forest friends," said Mama Bear, flipping a golden pancake in her big pan. "Rabbit, Squirrel, and Owl are coming too!"

Soon, Rabbit hopped in through the door, followed by chattery Squirrel and wise old Owl. They all sat around the round wooden table, their eyes sparkling with excitement.

Mama Bear brought a tall stack of fluffy pancakes to the table. "Now, who would like some?" she asked kindly.

Rabbit's ears perked up. "Me first! Me first!" he called out, reaching for the biggest pancake.

Squirrel's tail twitched. "No, I'm the hungriest!" she chattered, grabbing for two pancakes at once.

Owl ruffled his feathers. "I should go first because I'm the oldest," he hooted importantly.

Fern watched quietly as her friends argued. Her gentle heart felt sad seeing everyone upset. She took a deep breath, staying calm and thinking carefully.

"Wait, friends," said Fern in her soft, sweet voice. "What if we share fairly instead?"

The kitchen became quiet. All eyes turned to the thoughtful little fawn.

"How do we do that?" asked Rabbit, tilting his head curiously.

Fern's eyes sparkled with a wonderful idea. "Let's count how many pancakes there are, and how many friends we have. Then we can divide them equally!"

Mama Bear clapped her paws together. "What a smart and fair idea, Fern!"

Together, they counted. "One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight pancakes," said Fern slowly. "And one, two, three, four friends. That means we each get two pancakes!"

"That's perfectly fair!" hooted Owl, impressed by Fern's thinking.

Fern carefully placed two pancakes on each plate. The warm pancakes smelled like vanilla and butter, making everyone's tummies rumble happily.

"Thank you, Fern," said Rabbit, taking a big, syrupy bite. "These taste even better when we share fairly!"

"You're right!" agreed Squirrel, her cheeks full of fluffy pancake. "Sharing makes everything more delicious!"

As they ate together, the kitchen filled with happy chatter and warm laughter. The morning sun streamed through the windows, making their breakfast feel extra special.

"Fern," said Mama Bear proudly, "your fair sharing made everyone happy. When we're calm and think of others, we find the best solutions."

Fern's heart felt as warm as the sunny kitchen. She had learned that being fair meant making sure everyone got their equal share, and that staying calm helped her think of creative ways to solve problems.

After breakfast, all the friends decided to explore the forest together, their bellies full and their hearts happy from sharing fairly.

The End.
