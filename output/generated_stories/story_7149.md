# Benny Beaver's Kitchen Helper Day

Benny Beaver loved his warm, cozy kitchen. The yellow walls were bright like sunshine, and the smell of fresh cookies always filled the air. Every morning, Benny would put on his little blue apron and get ready to cook.

"Today I'm going to make pancakes, muffins, AND soup!" Benny said excitedly, clapping his small paws together. He loved to cook, but sometimes he tried to do too many things at once.

Just as Benny started mixing pancake batter, he heard a gentle knock on his kitchen door.

"Hello, Benny!" called his friend Rosie Rabbit. "I smell something yummy!"

Benny looked up from his big mixing bowl. White flour was sprinkled all over his whiskers. "Hi, Rosie! I'm making lots of food today. Want to help?"

Rosie hopped inside, her nose twitching at all the wonderful smells. "I'd love to help! What can I do?"

Benny looked around his kitchen. Ingredients were scattered everywhere. Mixing bowls covered the counter. The oven timer was beeping loudly.

"Oh no!" Benny squeaked. "My muffins are ready, but I haven't finished the pancakes yet, and I haven't even started the soup!"

Rosie saw that her friend looked worried. His little beaver tail was twitching nervously. "Don't worry, Benny. We can work together!"

"But I wanted to do everything myself," Benny said sadly. "I'm supposed to be a good cook."

Rosie smiled warmly. "Good cooks know when to ask for help. That's what makes them really good!"

Benny thought about this. Maybe Rosie was right. "Okay," he said slowly. "Could you help me take the muffins out of the oven? They smell like blueberries and sunshine!"

"Of course!" Rosie carefully pulled the golden muffins from the warm oven. They smelled absolutely delicious.

While Rosie let the muffins cool, Benny finished mixing his fluffy pancake batter. "This is easier when we work together," he admitted.

"What about the soup?" Rosie asked, washing her paws in the warm, soapy water.

Benny looked at his clock. "Maybe we could make soup tomorrow instead. We already have yummy muffins and we're making pancakes!"

"That's a great idea!" Rosie agreed. "Sometimes it's better to do fewer things really well."

Soon, the kitchen filled with the sizzling sound of pancakes cooking in the pan. Benny flipped them high in the air, and they landed perfectly back in the pan with a soft plop.

"You're amazing at flipping pancakes!" Rosie cheered.

When everything was ready, Benny and Rosie sat at the little wooden table. The warm muffins were sweet and fluffy. The golden pancakes were perfectly round and delicious.

"Thank you for helping me, Rosie," Benny said, his mouth full of pancake. "I learned something important today."

"What's that?" Rosie asked, taking a bite of blueberry muffin.

"I learned that asking friends for help makes everything more fun. And sometimes it's better to do fewer things really well instead of trying to do everything at once."

Rosie nodded happily. "And I learned that helping friends feels really good!"

From that day on, whenever Benny cooked in his warm kitchen, he remembered to ask for help when he needed it. And he always made sure to help his friends too, because that's what good friends do.

The End.
