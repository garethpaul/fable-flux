# Pip Paints the Balcony

Pip was a bright, creative parrot. His feathers shone red, green, and gold. He lived with Mia on a sunny balcony garden. The air smelled like mint and sweet tomatoes. Tiny wind chimes sang tinkly songs. Bees hummed softly near the flowers.

Pip loved to make art. He dipped a small brush in safe water paint. Tap, tap, tap went the brush on a flat stone. Swoosh, swoosh went the colors. “I am making a rainbow path,” Pip chirped. “It will be cheerful!”

Mia smiled and watered the plants. The water made a gentle hiss. Drops sparkled like stars. “That looks lovely, Pip,” she said. “You are very creative.”

Pip puffed his chest. He had an idea. “I will paint a big garden sign!” he squawked. “It will say, ‘Welcome!’” He flapped with joy. He took the biggest brush. He set a clean board on a low stool.

Just then, Nib the squirrel scurried along the railing. His tail was bushy and twitchy. His nose wiggled at the smell of fresh strawberries. “Hello, Pip!” Nib chattered. “What are you making?”

“A sign,” Pip said proudly. “A beautiful sign for our garden.”

Nib leaned close. “Can I help?” he asked. His brown eyes were shiny and hopeful.

Pip felt a little worried. “I like to make art my way,” he said. “Maybe you can watch.”

Nib’s ears drooped. He nodded and sat by a pot of basil. He watched Pip paint loops and letters. The sun warmed their feathers and fur. A soft breeze carried the scent of soil.

Suddenly, a gust of wind whooshed across the balcony. The board wobbled. The big brush slid. Splash! Red paint splattered onto the sign. Drip! Drip! A bold red line ran right through the word “Welcome.”

“Oh no!” Pip cried. “My sign! It is ruined!” His heart thumped. His feathers felt prickly. He flapped hard.

Nib leaped up. “I’ll fix it!” he said. He grabbed a small cloth to dab the paint. But his paws were quick and shaky. Swipe! He smudged the red into the yellow. Now it looked brown and messy.

Pip squawked. “Nib, stop!” His voice was sharp like a crack. “You made it worse!”

Nib froze. His paws trembled. “I’m sorry,” he whispered. “I wanted to help.” His tail drooped low. He backed away to the corner by the mint.

The balcony went quiet. Even the chimes seemed shy.

Pip stared at the messy sign. His chest felt hot with angry sparks. He wanted to puff and shout. But then he heard a little sniff. He saw Nib’s eyes shine with tears. Pip took a long slow breath. In and out. In and out. The angry sparks softened.

Pip looked at the sign again. He felt his tummy twist. “I shouted,” he thought. “Nib tried to help. He made a mistake, but he cared.”

Pip took a step toward Nib. “Nib,” he said softly. “I was upset. I used a sharp voice. I am sorry.” He bent his head. “I forgive you for the mess. Can we try again together?”

Nib blinked. “You forgive me?” he asked.

“Yes,” Pip said. “We can fix it. We can share the job. We can use a new idea.”

Nib’s tail lifted a little. “I can bring leaves!” he said. “We can make leaf prints.”

Pip smiled. “Great idea. That shows initiative! Let’s do it.”

They got to work. Pip washed the brush in a cup. Swish, swish. Nib gathered soft basil leaves and big strawberry leaves. Plop, plop went the leaves in green and yellow paint. Tap went Pip’s claws as he held the board steady.

“Press and lift,” Pip said. “Like this.” He pressed a leaf and lifted it. A pretty green shape bloomed on the board.

“I can do that!” Nib chirped. He pressed a yellow leaf next to the green one. Together they made a sunny pattern. They added tiny red dots that looked like berry seeds. The mess became a garden of shapes.

Mia peeked over. “Oh my,” she said. “What a beautiful sign! It looks like the whole garden is saying ‘Welcome.’”

Pip’s heart felt light. “We made it together,” he chirped. “We shared the work.”

Nib grinned. “Thank you for forgiving me, Pip,” he said. “I will be more careful next time.”

Pip nodded. “And I will use kind words when I feel upset,” he said. He looked at the sign. The red streak now was a twisty path for a painted ladybug. “See? Mistakes can become new ideas.”

They hung the sign on the railing. The wind chimes sang. The leaves rustled. The mint and basil smelled fresh and bright. Bees buzzed a happy tune.

Pip fluffed his feathers. “Welcome,” he read. “Welcome, friends. In this garden, we share. We help. We forgive.”

Nib tapped the board gently. “And we try again,” he added.

They both smiled. The balcony felt warm and kind, like a hug. Pip and Nib sat together, sipping cool water and nibbling a strawberry. The sweet juice tickled their tongues.

“Together is better,” Pip said.

“Together is kinder,” Nib said.

The End.
