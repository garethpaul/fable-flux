# Bubbles in the Laundry Room

Flip the playful dolphin loved to play with bubbles. One sunny morning, Flip wriggled through a magic puddle and—splash!—popped into a bright laundry room. The room smelled like clean soap and warm towels. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer sang, “Thump, thump.” Little dust motes danced in the light like tiny stars.

“Wow! So many bubbles!” Flip giggled. He flapped his fins and swished his tail. “Hi, machines! I’m Flip!”

The washing machine blinked its little lights. “Hello,” it hummed. “I’m Washy.”

The dryer spun slowly and said, “I’m Dryo. I’m warm and cozy.”

A basket of socks wobbled nearby. One small sock peeked out and squeaked, “I’m Dot. I’m lost from my sock friend.”

Flip’s eyes grew wide. “Lost? Oh no! We can help. We can work as a team!”

Dot sniffled. “I feel mad and sad. My friend is missing.”

Flip floated closer and spoke softly. “I hear your feelings, Dot. It’s okay to feel mad. Let’s take a deep breath together.”

In and out they breathed. Flip said, “Smell the soap… blow the bubbles.” Dot breathed in the clean, lemony smell and blew out gently. “Phew,” Dot said, feeling a little better.

Washy hummed, “I saw socks swim in my soapy water.”

Dryo added, “I tumbled socks in my warm drum.”

Flip clapped his fins. “Team meeting! We have a job. Find Dot’s friend. We will use teamwork.”

They made a plan. Flip would look under shelves. Washy would peek in the drum. Dryo would check the lint screen. Dot would call softly, “Friend? Friend?”

Flip slid across cool tiles. “It’s slippery!” he laughed. His fin brushed a puddle. The water was cold and tickly. He lifted a towel. “Hello? Not there.” He sniffed. “Mmm, it smells like sunshine.”

Dot hopped to the laundry basket. “Friend?” The basket rustled. Only a tiny button blinked back.

Washy sloshed. “I have suds, but no sock,” it hummed.

Dryo puffed warm air. “No sock here. Just fuzzy lint.”

Flip felt a tight squeeze in his belly. “Oh no,” he said. His tail flicked too fast and splashed water. “I feel mad. I want to shout.”

Dot looked worried. “Please don’t shout.”

Flip paused. He put a fin on his chest. “I feel mad because we did not find the sock yet. My body feels hot. My tail is swishy. I will calm down now.” He took a big breath. “Smell the soap… blow the bubbles.” He blew a soft stream, and tiny rainbow bubbles floated up. Pop, pop, pop. “Ah,” Flip sighed. “I feel better.”

Washy beeped. “Thank you for saying your feelings.”

Dryo hummed, “That helps the team.”

Flip smiled. “We can try something new. Let’s make a sock-finder. An… in-no-va-tion!” He stretched out the word like taffy. “We will use what we have.”

They looked around. There was a long string. There were clothespins. There was a flashlight shaped like a little star.

Flip said, “Here is the plan. Dot, you wear the flashlight like a necklace. Washy, you swish and make a gentle sound. Dryo, you puff warm, cozy air. I will pull the string with clothespins and listen.”

“Teamwork!” everyone cheered.

Dot clicked on the star light. “Twinkle, twinkle,” it shined.

Washy hummed softly, “Whirr, whirr,” like a calm song.

Dryo puffed, “Whoosh,” like a warm hug.

Flip moved slowly, clipping clothespins to the string and tapping under shelves. Tap, tap. “Friend? We are here,” Flip called.

From behind a tall bottle of soap came a teeny voice. “I’m here! I’m here!” A striped sock peeked out, shy and stuck. “I slid and got scared. I feel embarrassed.”

Dot wiggled with joy. “Friend!” Dot’s little toe shook. “It’s okay to feel that way. You are safe.”

Flip swam gently over. “We will help you.” He clipped a clothespin to the striped sock and pulled the string. Pull, pull. The sock slid free. “Whee!”

The striped sock laughed. “Thank you, team.”

Dot and the striped sock hugged with soft cotton hugs. “I am happy,” Dot said. “I feel warm inside.”

Flip grinned. “We did it together. We listened. We breathed. We tried something new. That is teamwork.”

Washy beeped a happy song. Dryo spun a victory tumble. The laundry room smelled even more like fresh lemon and warm bread. Bubbles bounced in the light.

Flip turned to the socks. “Do you want to help sort the laundry?”

“Yes!” said Dot. “Teamwork time!”

They made simple jobs:
- Flip matched colors.
- Dot checked tiny toes.
- The striped sock counted, “One, two, three,” in a bright voice.
- Washy and Dryo kept humming and puffing.

When a sock got stuck again, Flip reminded, “Let’s use calm breaths.” Everyone breathed together. In… out… Slow and gentle. Feelings felt softer.

Soon the basket was neat. Pairs of socks smiled, toe to toe. The room felt peaceful and proud.

Flip stretched his fins. “I learned something today,” he said. “Teamwork means we help each other. We share our feelings. We calm our bodies. We try ideas together. That is how we fix big problems.”

Dot nodded. “Teamwork feels safe.”

Dryo added, “Teamwork feels warm.”

Washy hummed, “Teamwork feels clean and happy.”

Flip giggled. “And bubbly!”

A new puddle shimmered like a glassy door. Flip waved. “Good-bye, team! Thank you for being brave and kind.”

“Good-bye, Flip!” everyone called. “Come play again!”

Flip slid through the puddle and—splash!—he was gone, leaving tiny rainbow bubbles that popped with soft, happy sounds.

The End.
