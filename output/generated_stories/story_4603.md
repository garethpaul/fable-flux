# Leo the Lion Cub's Big Workshop Day

Leo the lion cub peeked into Grandpa Bear's garage workshop. The smell of wood shavings tickled his nose, and he heard the gentle *tap-tap-tap* of a hammer.

"Good morning, Grandpa Bear!" Leo called out, his little voice echoing in the big space.

"Well, hello there, Leo!" Grandpa Bear smiled, setting down his tools. "What brings you to my workshop today?"

Leo's ears drooped a little. "I want to help you build something, but... I'm just a little cub. I don't think I'm big enough or smart enough to use real tools."

Grandpa Bear's eyes twinkled. "Oh, Leo! Being small doesn't mean you can't do big things. Would you like to help me finish this special birdhouse?"

Leo's golden eyes grew wide. "Really? You think I can do it?"

"I know you can!" Grandpa Bear said warmly. "But first, let's make sure you have energy for our project. Did you eat a good breakfast?"

Leo shook his head. "I was too excited to eat. My tummy feels a bit rumbly now."

"A good builder needs fuel!" Grandpa Bear chuckled. He pulled out a lunch box with apple slices, cheese cubes, and whole grain crackers. "These foods will help your body and brain work their best."

Leo munched happily, feeling the sweet apples and creamy cheese fill his empty tummy. "Mmm! Now I feel stronger!"

"Perfect! Now, let's find the right job for you," Grandpa Bear said. He showed Leo some smooth pieces of sandpaper. "Can you help make this wood nice and smooth? Just rub gently, like you're petting a friendly cat."

Leo took the sandpaper in his small paws. At first, he felt worried. "What if I mess it up?"

"Leo," Grandpa Bear said gently, "it's okay to feel nervous about new things. But you know what? You're brave, you're careful, and you're trying your best. That's what matters most."

Leo began to rub the sandpaper against the wood. *Swish, swish, swish.* The rough wood became smooth under his paws. "Look, Grandpa Bear! I'm really doing it!"

"You certainly are! You're doing a wonderful job," Grandpa Bear praised.

As they worked together, Leo felt proud and confident. When he needed help reaching a high spot, he used his voice clearly: "Grandpa Bear, could you please help me reach up there?"

"Of course! I'm so glad you asked for help when you needed it," Grandpa Bear replied.

After an hour of careful work, they stepped back to admire the beautiful birdhouse. It was painted bright yellow with a red roof, smooth as silk from Leo's sandpaper work.

"We make a great team!" Leo beamed, his chest puffed out with pride.

"We do indeed," Grandpa Bear agreed. "You showed me today that you can complete important tasks, speak up for yourself, and work hard. Being small doesn't make you any less capable."

Leo hugged Grandpa Bear tight. "I felt scared at first, but I'm really proud of what we built together!"

"And you should be proud, Leo. Remember, believing in yourself is the first step to doing amazing things."

As they hung the birdhouse outside, Leo knew he would always remember this special day when he learned that being small didn't mean he couldn't do big, important things.

The End.
