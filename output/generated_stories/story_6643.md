# Benny Beaver's Garden Helper Day

Benny the beaver loved visiting Grandma's balcony garden. The bright red tomatoes hung like little balloons. The green lettuce leaves felt soft and cool. The yellow sunflowers reached up to the sky.

"Good morning, Benny!" said Grandma, watering her plants. "Look how thirsty my garden is today."

Benny watched the water drip-drop from the watering can. His big orange teeth showed when he smiled. "I want to help!" he said, clapping his flat tail.

But when Grandma went inside to make breakfast, Benny saw a big problem. The water was running off the balcony! It splashed down to the ground below instead of staying with the plants.

"Oh no!" Benny said. "The plants need that water!"

Benny put his paw to his furry chin. He thought and thought. Beavers were very good at solving water problems!

"I know!" Benny said excitedly. "I can build something to catch the water!"

Benny looked around the balcony. He found some empty yogurt containers. He found a big plastic tray. He found some old towels.

First, Benny placed the containers under the dripping water. Plink! Plink! The drops made happy sounds as they fell into the containers.

"This water can be used again!" Benny said proudly.

Next, Benny put the plastic tray under the plant pots. Now when Grandma watered the plants, the extra water would stay in the tray instead of running away.

But Benny wasn't finished yet. He rolled up the old towels and put them around the edges of the balcony. The towels would soak up any water that tried to escape.

"There!" said Benny, stepping back to look at his work. "Now every drop of water will help the plants grow!"

When Grandma came back outside, she gasped. "Benny! What wonderful work you've done!"

"I saw the water running away," Benny explained. "Plants need water to grow big and strong. So I made a way to save every drop!"

Grandma hugged Benny tight. "You took initiative to solve a problem all by yourself! And you helped us use water wisely too."

Together, they watered the garden with Benny's new water-saving system. The tomatoes seemed to shine brighter. The lettuce leaves looked greener. The sunflowers stood taller.

"Look, Grandma!" Benny pointed to the containers. "We caught so much water! We can use it tomorrow too!"

A little bird landed on the balcony railing. "Tweet! Tweet! Thank you for saving water for all of us!" the bird sang.

Benny felt warm and happy inside. He had seen a problem and fixed it all by himself. He had helped the plants and saved water too.

"When we take care of our garden," said Grandma, "our garden takes care of us."

Benny nodded, his tail patting the ground happily. "And when we see something that needs fixing, we can try to fix it ourselves!"

From that day on, Benny always looked for ways to help. He learned that taking initiative meant seeing problems and working to solve them. And the best part was helping others and taking care of the world around him.

The balcony garden grew more beautiful every day, thanks to one busy beaver who wasn't afraid to try something new.

The End.
