# Song Feathers in the Practice Room

Tweet was a small singing bird with bright blue feathers and a warm, golden beak. Every morning, Tweet fluttered into the musical practice room. The room was cozy and full of friendly sounds. There were drums that went boom-boom, bells that chimed ting-ting, and a big round drum that hummed like thunder. Soft light slipped through the window and made little dust sparkles dance in the air.

Tweet loved to sing. “La-la-la!” Tweet sang, hopping from a drum to a shiny piano bench. The air smelled like wood and a little bit like lemon polish. It felt safe. It felt happy.

One day, as Tweet warmed up, the door creaked open. In came friends, each with their own special music. Mei held a small red drum. “Ni hao, Tweet!” she said with a smile. Malik carried a sweet, long flute. “Salaam, Tweet,” he whispered kindly. Rosa had tiny clacking castanets. “Hola, Tweet!” she sang. Nani carried a ukulele with flowers painted on it. “Aloha,” she hummed. Liam rolled in a big Irish bodhrán drum. “Hi!” he said, cheeks pink and bright.

Tweet blinked. So many sounds! So many smiles! “Hello, friends!” Tweet chirped. “Will you share your songs with me?”

Mei patted her drum with gentle hands. “This is a lion dance beat,” she said. Boom, ba-boom! Boom, ba-boom! The beat was round and strong, like big footsteps. Tweet felt the sound in her chest. “It sounds brave,” Tweet said. “I like it!”

Malik lifted his flute. He blew a soft, swirly tune. Hoo, hoo, hooooo. The notes curled like smoke and floated like feathers. “My grandpa taught me,” Malik said. “He told me stories about the desert and the moon.” Tweet closed her eyes and could almost see a silver moon in a dark, quiet sky.

Rosa clicked her castanets. Click-clack, clickety-clack! “This is for dancing with happy feet,” she laughed. Her shoulders wiggled. Her shoes tapped. Tweet giggled and tapped along with tiny bird toes.

Nani strummed the ukulele. Plink, plonk, pling! The sound was sunny and warm, like sand and sea. “My tutu taught me a song,” Nani said. “It tells a story about waves saying hello to the shore.” Tweet’s heart felt soft and calm.

Liam thumped the bodhrán. Dum-dum de-dum! The rhythm was like rain on a roof and ponies on a hill. “My uncle plays this at family dances,” Liam said. “We clap and laugh together.” Tweet’s wings tingled with joy.

Tweet looked at all the friends. “Your music is so different,” Tweet said, “and so beautiful.” Tweet felt excited but also a little unsure. “I only know la-la-la. Is that enough?”

Mei smiled. “We can learn together.”

Malik nodded. “We can listen.”

Rosa twirled. “We can share.”

Nani strummed. “We can tell stories.”

Liam tapped. “We can make a song with everyone!”

Tweet took a deep breath. The room smelled like wood, lemon, and a little bit of hot chocolate from a thermos on the shelf. The floor felt smooth under Tweet’s feet. Tweet opened her beak. “La-la-la,” Tweet sang softly. Then louder. “La-la-laaa!”

“Let’s try this,” Mei said. She tapped boom, ba-boom. Malik floated in with hoo, hoo, hooooo. Rosa added clickety-clack. Nani sprinkled plink, plonk, pling. Liam rolled dum-dum de-dum. Tweet’s la-la-la danced in the middle.

At first the sounds bumped into each other. Oops! Clack-boom-hoo! Tweet giggled. “It sounds like a busy train!” Everybody laughed.

“Let’s listen,” Malik said. They tried again, slower. Boom, pause. Hoo, gentle. Click, click. Plink, plonk. Dum, de-dum. Tweet waited, then sang. “La-la-la,” she breathed, matching the gentle beat.

The room changed. It felt like a big blanket of sound. It felt like a story. Mei’s drum was the brave footsteps. Malik’s flute was the moon. Rosa’s clicks were happy feet. Nani’s ukulele was warm sun. Liam’s drum was soft rain. Tweet’s voice was the wind that carried it all.

Tweet thought about the stories. “Your music tells who you are,” Tweet said. “It tells about family and places and love.”

Mei nodded. “We share to care.”

Rosa grinned. “We listen to learn.”

Nani swayed. “We sing to remember.”

Liam tapped. “We play to bring joy.”

They played their song again. The boom, hoo, click, plink, and dum blended with Tweet’s la-la-la. The sound filled the practice room and slipped out the window. Outside, a squirrel stopped to listen. A cat tilted its head. Even the leaves in the tree shivered with happiness.

When the last note faded, the room felt warm and quiet. Tweet’s feathers puffed with pride. “Thank you for your songs,” Tweet said. “Now I know more. I feel close to you.”

Mei bowed. Malik smiled. Rosa clapped. Nani hugged her ukulele. Liam gave a thumbs-up.

Tweet chirped, “Today I learned that every culture has music with a story. When we listen and share, we understand and care.”

Everyone said together, “We are different. We are friends. Our music fits!”

Tweet hopped onto the piano bench and looked at the shining keys. “Tomorrow,” Tweet said, “let’s learn a new song. You can teach me a word from your home.”

“Yes!” said Mei.

“Inshallah!” said Malik.

“Sí!” said Rosa.

“Ae!” said Nani.

“Grand!” said Liam.

Tweet sang one last soft note. “La-la-la,” like a goodnight smile.

The musical practice room glowed with happy, gentle light. The friends packed their instruments with care. They waved and went home with warm hearts and humming tunes.

Tweet tucked her head under her wing. The lesson fluttered close, like a cozy blanket: Different songs, different stories, all welcome here.

The End.
