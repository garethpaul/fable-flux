# Benny Bear Learns to Set the Table

Benny Bear was a fluffy brown cub who lived in a cozy house with his mama and papa. One sunny morning, Mama Bear called from the kitchen, "Benny, breakfast is almost ready! Can you help set the table?"

Benny's ears perked up. He had watched Mama and Papa set the table many times, but he had never tried it himself. "I can do it!" he said, bouncing on his paws with excitement.

He waddled into the dining room where the big wooden table stood empty. The morning sunlight streamed through the window, making everything warm and bright. Benny looked at the tall cabinet where the dishes lived. They seemed so high up!

"Let me get the step stool first," Benny said to himself, remembering how Papa always prepared before starting a big job. He pushed the little wooden stool across the floor with a soft scraping sound until it was next to the cabinet.

Climbing up carefully, Benny reached for the plates. They were heavier than he expected! His little paws wobbled as he carried the first plate to the table. *Clink!* He set it down a bit too hard.

"Oops," Benny giggled. "I need to be more gentle."

He went back for the second plate, moving slower this time. His arms were getting a little tired from all the reaching and carrying, but it felt good to move his body and help his family.

When Benny tried to place the forks next to the plates, they kept sliding around. "This is harder than it looks," he huffed, feeling frustrated. One fork fell to the floor with a tiny *ting!*

"Need some help, little cub?" asked Papa Bear, appearing in the doorway with a warm smile.

"I want to do it myself," Benny said, his bottom lip trembling a little. "But the forks won't stay where I put them!"

Papa Bear sat down next to Benny. "Learning new skills takes practice," he said gently. "Even I had to learn how to set the table when I was a cub. Would you like me to show you a trick?"

Benny nodded eagerly.

"First, let's take three deep breaths together when we feel frustrated," Papa said. They breathed in the sweet smell of pancakes from the kitchen and breathed out slowly. Benny felt much calmer.

"Now," Papa continued, "place the fork on the left side of the plate, about one finger-width away. Like this." He showed Benny how to line up the fork carefully.

Benny tried again, using his little finger to measure the distance. "One finger-width," he repeated, concentrating hard. This time, the fork stayed put!

"I did it!" Benny cheered, doing a little dance that made his fluffy tail wiggle.

Together, they finished setting the table. Benny placed each cup carefully, lined up the napkins, and even helped Papa fold them neatly. His paws were getting stronger with each movement, and his confidence grew with every success.

When Mama Bear brought in the golden pancakes, she gasped with delight. "What a beautiful table! Benny, you did such a wonderful job!"

Benny's chest puffed out with pride. "I learned that when something is hard, I can take deep breaths, ask for help, and keep practicing. And moving my body to help makes me feel strong!"

As the family sat down to eat, Benny looked at the perfectly set table and smiled. He had learned that building new skills takes patience, practice, and preparation – and it felt amazing to help his family.

The End.
