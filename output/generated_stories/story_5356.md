# Songbird in the Dining Room

Early in the morning, a little singing bird named Pip fluttered into the dining room through a sunny open window. Pip’s feathers were soft and bright, and his voice was sweet like honey. He perched on the back of a sturdy wooden chair and sang, “Chee-chee-cherp! Good morning!”

The dining room smelled like warm toast and orange juice. Sunlight made the shiny spoons sparkle. A little ball sat on the rug near the table, and a tiny hoop made of yarn hung on a chair leg. Today was a play day.

“Good morning, Pip!” said Mia, a cheerful child with big curious eyes. “Do you want to play ball hoop with me?”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “Chee-chee, yes! But I am a bird. I have wings, not hands.”

Mia smiled and thought. “We can use gentle rules. You can tap the ball with your beak or your feet.”

Pip tilted his head. He liked when friends made a plan together. “Let’s try,” he said.

They set the hoop on the floor. Mia showed Pip how to roll the soft ball. “We say ‘Ready, roll!’ Then we take turns.”

“Ready, roll!” chirped Pip, and he gave a tiny tap. The ball rolled slowly, bumping the rug. Pip listened to the soft thump-thump of the ball and the whisper of the rug fibers. It felt calm and fun.

Mia rolled next. “Ready, roll!” The ball went through the yarn hoop. “I did it!” she laughed.

Pip trilled a happy tune. “Chee-chee-chee! Great job, friend!”

Then Mia said, “Now you try to score.” She moved the hoop closer to Pip so he wouldn’t have to hop too far.

Pip took a breath. He loved to sing when he was brave. “La-la-lee,” he sang softly, and tapped the ball with his beak. The ball wobbled, then whoosh! It slipped through the hoop.

“I did it!” Pip chirped. He flapped his wings and sang a bright, proud song.

Mia clapped. “We are a team! High f— oh! Do you like high fives?”

Pip paused. Suddenly he felt a little shy. A flying hand felt big and fast. “Hmm,” he said, “I don’t like big fast hands. It makes my feathers tickle too much.”

Mia pulled her hand back and smiled gently. “Thank you for telling me. We can do a wing wave instead.” She wiggled her fingers like little wings.

Pip’s eyes sparkled. “Yes, a wing wave!” He waved a wing. It felt safe and kind.

They kept playing, taking turns. Sometimes the ball missed the hoop and made a soft bump. Sometimes it rolled perfectly, and they both cheered. Pip learned to breathe slow when he felt excited. Mia learned to ask, “Is this okay?” before trying new moves, like a gentle nudge or a closer hoop.

Soon, Pip heard a tiny groan. “My tummy says it is snack time,” Mia giggled.

The dining room table held a plate of apple slices and a cup of cool water. The air smelled crisp and sweet. “Would you like a snack, Pip?” Mia asked.

Pip thought carefully. “Apples are big for me. Could I have tiny bites?”

Mia nodded. “Yes.” She cut a small apple piece into even smaller bits. “Is this size okay?”

Pip pecked a bit and chirped, “Perfect! Thank you for asking.”

After snack, they wanted to try a new game. Mia looked at the dining chairs. “We can make a gentle obstacle path. But we must keep it safe.”

Pip fluttered to the back of a chair and hummed. “How do we make it safe?”

Mia touched her chin, thinking. “We leave space for walking. We ask before moving chairs. We watch our feet and our wings.”

Pip liked this thoughtful plan. They asked a grown-up, who said, “You may make a small path, and you must keep the floor clear.” Mia and Pip said, “Yes!”

They set two chairs apart to make a tunnel. The sunlight made a warm golden stripe across the floor. Pip flew low and slow through the tunnel. “Wheee,” he sang. Mia crawled under, moving gently, and Pip cheered, “You did it!”

Then Mia reached toward Pip to help him hop. She stopped and asked, “Pip, may I hold the chair for you, or do you want to do it yourself?”

Pip felt proud. “I want to do it myself. But please watch me.”

“I will,” said Mia. She stood nearby, ready but not touching. Pip hopped, hopped, and made it to the seat. He sang a soft victory note.

They rested on the rug, listening to the clock tick-tock and the distant hum of a fridge. The dining room felt cozy and safe. Mia said, “Pip, I like being your friend.”

Pip’s song was gentle and warm. “I like being your friend, too. You listen. You ask. You cheer.”

Mia smiled. “You sing. You share. You tell me what is okay and what is not.”

Pip nodded. “That is friendship. We think together. We respect each other’s space. We help, but we always ask.”

Mia held up her hand, slow and small. “Wing wave?”

“Wing wave!” Pip chirped. They waved to each other and laughed.

Pip flew to the window. The sun was higher now. “I will come back tomorrow,” he sang. “We can play again and make new plans.”

“See you tomorrow, friend,” said Mia. “We are a team.”

As Pip fluttered out, the dining room still smelled like apples and sunshine. Their laughter stayed like a warm echo.

The clear lesson is this: Good friends listen, ask before touching, think of safe plans, and cheer for each other. With kind words and careful choices, friendship grows strong every day.

The End.
