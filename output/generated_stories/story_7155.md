# Benny Beaver's Big Art Project

Benny Beaver loved to build things. His big orange teeth were perfect for chewing wood. His flat tail made a great tool for patting down mud. In the creative art room at Forest Friends School, Benny always worked alone.

Today, the art room smelled like crayons and glue. Colorful papers covered every table. Paint brushes stood in tall jars like tiny trees.

"Time for our group project!" announced Ms. Robin, their teacher. "We're building a sports stadium for our toy animals!"

Benny's eyes lit up. Building was his favorite thing! He grabbed some cardboard boxes and wooden blocks. He started stacking them high, higher, highest.

"I can do this myself," Benny said proudly. His busy paws moved quickly, placing block after block.

Nearby, Luna the rabbit was cutting colorful flags. "These will make the stadium pretty!" she said.

At another table, Sam the squirrel was drawing tiny soccer balls and basketballs. "Sports decorations!" he chirped happily.

Benny looked at his pile of blocks. It kept falling down. *Crash!* Down went his tower. *Crash!* Down it went again.

"This is hard," Benny muttered. His whiskers twitched with worry.

Luna hopped over. "Hi Benny! Your building looks tricky. Want some help?"

"No thank you," Benny said quickly. "Beavers are the best builders. I can do it alone."

But the blocks kept tumbling down. Benny's tail slapped the floor in frustration. *Slap! Slap!*

Sam scurried over with his drawings. "Maybe we could work together? I made these sports pictures for decorating."

"I don't need decorations," Benny said. "I just need to build higher!"

He tried again. And again. Each time, the blocks fell with a loud *CRASH!*

Other friends gathered around. There was Maya the mouse with tiny screws and bolts. Carlos the cardinal had brought soft cotton for stadium seats. Zoe the zebra carried strips of black and white paper for making goal posts.

"We could all help each other," suggested Maya in her squeaky voice.

Benny's shoulders sagged. His building wasn't working. Everyone else's projects looked so colorful and fun. His pile of blocks just looked... messy.

"I... I don't know how to make it pretty like yours," Benny admitted quietly.

"But you're so good at building the strong parts!" said Luna. "We need someone who knows about making things sturdy."

"Really?" Benny's ears perked up.

"Yes!" said Sam. "And we need someone to organize all our different pieces."

Benny looked around at all his friends. Luna's flags were beautiful. Sam's sports pictures were perfect. Maya's tiny screws could hold things together. Carlos's cotton looked so soft and cozy. Zoe's goal posts were exactly the right size.

"Maybe... maybe we could all work together?" Benny said slowly.

"Yes!" everyone cheered.

Benny used his building skills to make strong walls. Luna added her colorful flags on top. Sam glued his sports pictures all around. Maya used her tiny screws to make everything extra sturdy. Carlos stuffed his cotton into little stadium seats. Zoe placed her goal posts at each end.

Working together, they finished in half the time! Their stadium was strong AND beautiful AND fun.

"Look what we made!" Benny said, his tail wiggling with joy.

Ms. Robin flew over. "Wonderful teamwork, everyone! Benny, your building skills made this stadium so strong. And everyone's different talents made it special."

Benny smiled big. "I learned that building with friends is even better than building alone. Everyone has different talents that help make things amazing!"

From that day on, Benny always invited his friends to help with his projects. And he loved helping with theirs too. The art room became filled with even more wonderful creations, made by friends working together.

The End.
