# Melody's Morning Song

In a cozy nursery with soft yellow walls and a rocking chair by the window, lived a little singing bird named Melody. Her feathers were bright blue like the morning sky, and she had the sweetest voice in the whole house.

Every morning, Melody would perch on the windowsill and sing beautiful songs to wake up baby Sophie. "Tweet-tweet-trill! Good morning, little one!" she would chirp cheerfully.

But one morning, something was different. Melody felt tired and her throat was scratchy. When she tried to sing, only a quiet "croak" came out instead of her lovely melody.

"Oh no!" Melody whispered, touching her throat with her tiny wing. "What happened to my beautiful voice?"

Sophie's mommy was changing the baby's diaper nearby. She heard Melody's worried sounds and looked over with kind eyes.

"Poor little bird," Mommy said gently. "I think you forgot to take care of yourself yesterday."

Melody thought back to the day before. She had sung all morning without stopping. She hadn't drunk any water from her little dish. She hadn't eaten the healthy seeds Mommy left for her. She had even stayed up late singing to the moon instead of getting her sleep.

"I was so busy singing that I forgot to eat and drink," Melody realized sadly. "And I didn't get enough sleep either."

Mommy smiled warmly. "Just like people, little birds need healthy habits to feel their best. Would you like me to help you?"

Melody nodded eagerly, her small head bobbing up and down.

First, Mommy filled Melody's water dish with fresh, cool water. "Drink slowly," she said. "Your throat needs to feel better."

Melody dipped her beak into the clear water. It felt so refreshing! She could feel the coolness going down her throat. "Ahh," she sighed happily.

Next, Mommy sprinkled nutritious seeds into Melody's food bowl. There were sunflower seeds, millet, and tiny pieces of apple. "Healthy food gives you energy to sing," Mommy explained.

Melody pecked at the seeds one by one. They were crunchy and delicious. She felt stronger with each bite.

"Now," said Mommy, "you need to rest your voice and take a nap. Even singing birds need quiet time."

Melody flew to her cozy nest in the corner of the nursery. The soft cotton padding felt like a warm hug. She closed her eyes and listened to the gentle sounds around her - baby Sophie's quiet breathing, the soft tick-tock of the clock, and the whisper of wind through the curtains.

When Melody woke up an hour later, she felt wonderful! Her throat didn't hurt anymore, and she felt full of energy.

"Let me try singing now," she said to herself.

Melody took a deep breath and opened her beak. "Tweet-tweet-trill! La-la-la!" Her beautiful voice was back, clearer and sweeter than ever!

Baby Sophie giggled and clapped her tiny hands. Mommy smiled from the rocking chair where she was reading.

"Thank you for teaching me about healthy habits," Melody sang gratefully. "I promise to drink water, eat good food, and get plenty of sleep every day!"

From that day on, Melody remembered to take care of herself. She drank water throughout the day, ate her healthy seeds, and got plenty of rest. Her songs became even more beautiful because she felt so good.

And every morning, her joyful voice filled the nursery with the sweetest melodies, bringing smiles to everyone who heard them.

The End.
