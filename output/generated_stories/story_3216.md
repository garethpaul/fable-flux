# Penny Panda's Morning Moves

Penny Panda woke up in her cozy bedroom. The soft morning light came through her window. Her fluffy blanket felt warm and snuggly. But Penny felt something strange in her body.

"My arms feel wiggly," said Penny. She stretched one arm up high. Then she stretched the other arm. "My legs feel jiggly too!"

Penny sat up in her bed. She looked around her bedroom. Her stuffed animals sat quietly on the shelf. Her books were neat and tidy. Everything was still and quiet.

"I need to move!" said Penny. She stood up on her soft carpet. The fuzzy rug felt good under her feet.

Penny tried marching in place. Left foot, right foot, left foot, right foot. "This feels better," she said. "But I need more space."

Penny looked around her bedroom. She used her thinking skills. The space between her bed and her toy box was perfect!

"I can make a movement path," said Penny. She moved her pillow to one side. She pushed her toy basket to make more room.

First, Penny tried jumping. Hop, hop, hop! Her feet made soft sounds on the carpet. "This is fun!" she said.

Next, Penny tried spinning. She held her arms out wide. Around and around she went. The room looked like it was dancing with her!

Then Penny heard a small voice. "Can I try too?" It was her little stuffed bear, Benny.

"Of course!" said Penny. She picked up Benny. "We can move together!"

Penny held Benny while she did gentle stretches. She reached up to touch the ceiling. She bent down to touch her toes. Benny came along for every move.

"What should we try next?" asked Penny.

Penny thought carefully. She looked at her bedroom again. She saw her colorful scarves in the dress-up box.

"I have an idea!" said Penny. She got three scarves. One red, one blue, one yellow. "We can wave these while we move!"

Penny held the scarves high. She moved her arms like gentle waves. The scarves danced in the air like colorful clouds.

"This looks beautiful," said Benny.

Soon, Penny heard footsteps. Her little brother Pip peeked into her room.

"What are you doing?" asked Pip.

"We're having a movement party," said Penny. "Do you want to join us?"

Pip's eyes got big and happy. "Yes, please!"

Penny became the leader. She showed Pip how to march. She taught him how to stretch. She gave him a scarf to wave.

"Follow me," said Penny. She led a parade around her bedroom. Pip followed behind her. Even Benny joined the fun parade.

They marched around the bed. They tiptoed past the bookshelf. They danced by the window where the sunshine came in.

"I feel so much better now," said Penny. Her wiggly, jiggly feeling was gone. Her body felt happy and calm.

"Moving makes me feel good too," said Pip.

Penny smiled. She had solved her problem by thinking and trying different movements. She had also helped her brother feel good.

"Every morning, we can have movement time," said Penny. "Our bodies need to move to feel their best."

From that day on, Penny's cozy bedroom became a special place. It was quiet for sleeping and reading. But it was also perfect for moving and playing.

Penny learned that being a good leader means helping others feel good too. And moving her body every day made her feel strong and happy.

The End.
