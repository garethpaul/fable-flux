# Penny Panda's Kitchen Adventure

Penny Panda loved spending time in her warm kitchen. The morning sun streamed through the yellow curtains, making everything glow like golden honey. The sweet smell of fresh bread filled the air, and Penny could hear birds chirping happily outside.

"Good morning, kitchen!" Penny said cheerfully, patting her round belly. She was getting ready to make her favorite breakfast - bamboo pancakes with berry syrup.

As Penny reached for her mixing bowl, she heard a tiny voice.

"Help! Help me, please!"

Penny stopped and listened carefully with her fuzzy ears. "Hello? Who's there?" she asked gently.

"Down here! By the sugar jar!" squeaked the little voice.

Penny looked down and saw a very small mouse stuck under a heavy wooden spoon. The mouse's whiskers were trembling, and his tiny eyes looked scared.

"Oh my!" said Penny softly. "You look frightened, little friend. Let me help you."

But as Penny bent down, the mouse squeaked loudly, "No! Stay away! You're too big! You might step on me!"

Penny felt her heart beating faster. She wanted to help, but the mouse seemed so afraid. She took a deep breath, just like her mama had taught her, and spoke in her calmest, gentlest voice.

"Little mouse, I can see you're scared. I'm scared too - scared that you might be hurt. But I want to help you feel safe. May I move very slowly and carefully?"

The mouse's tiny whiskers stopped trembling. "You... you're scared too?"

"Yes," Penny nodded, sitting down slowly on the warm kitchen floor. "Sometimes being big makes me nervous about helping small friends. But when we talk about our feelings, we can work together."

"I'm stuck and I can't get out," the mouse whispered. "I was looking for crumbs, and the spoon fell on my tail."

Penny felt brave knowing she could help. "Would it be okay if I lift the spoon very, very slowly? You can tell me if you want me to stop."

"Okay," said the mouse, his voice a little stronger now. "But please be gentle."

Penny used just two fingers to carefully lift the wooden spoon. She moved as slowly as a cloud drifting across the sky. "How does that feel?" she asked.

"Better! A little more, please," said the mouse.

With one more gentle movement, the mouse was free! He wiggled his tail and did a happy little dance on the warm kitchen tiles.

"Thank you, Penny Panda!" the mouse said, his eyes now sparkling with joy. "You listened to my feelings and helped me feel safe."

Penny's heart felt warm and fuzzy. "Thank you for telling me how you felt, little mouse. When we use gentle words and listen to each other, we can solve problems together."

The mouse smiled. "My name is Max. Would you like to share some crumbs with me?"

"I'd love to, Max!" Penny giggled. "How about we make those bamboo pancakes together? There will be plenty of yummy crumbs for both of us."

As they cooked together in the sunny kitchen, Penny learned that using peaceful words and listening carefully made even the smallest friends feel brave and safe.

The End.
