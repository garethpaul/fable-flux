# Momo the Monkey and the Mountain of Laundry

Momo was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and gentle hands. One sunny morning, Momo tiptoed into the laundry room. The room smelled like lemons and warm soap. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer clicked, “Clack, clack.” A big mountain of clothes sat on the floor, wobbling like jelly.

“Oh my,” Momo whispered. “This is a big job.” Momo took a deep breath in. “Smell the lemons,” he said softly. He breathed out. “Blow out a candle.” Momo liked to use calm breaths when he felt unsure.

Just then, Momo heard a small cry. “Help!” squeaked a tiny voice.

Momo’s ears perked up. He looked closer and saw a little sock peeking out of the laundry pile. The sock had a smile stitched on its toe and a tiny blue stripe around its top.

“I’m Squeaky Sock,” said the sock, wobbling. “My best friend, Stripey Sock, is missing. I’m scared to go in the big, loud washer alone.”

Momo put his hand on his heart. “It’s okay to feel scared,” he said. “Let’s be brave together.”

Squeaky Sock sniffled. “Thank you.”

Momo knelt on the soft rug and listened. The washer hummed. The dryer clicked. Water dripped, “plip, plip.” He felt the cool tile under his feet. He looked at the mountain of clothes. “We can do this step by step,” he said. “First, we sort. Then, we wash. Then, we dry. Then, we fold. We can find Stripey Sock along the way.”

Momo made three piles on the floor. “Lights here. Darks here. Towels here,” he said.

Squeaky Sock wiggled. “I go with lights!”

Momo smiled. “Good choice.”

As Momo sorted, he found other laundry friends. A red shirt said, “Careful, I can leak color!” Momo put it in the darks. A fluffy towel whispered, “I like warm hugs.” Momo put it in the towel pile. A tiny mitten sighed, “I miss my pair.” Momo patted the mitten kindly. “We will look for your friend too.”

Suddenly, the mountain shook and spilled. A grumpy stain on a white shirt frowned. “I’m sticky,” it groaned.

Momo looked with kind eyes. “I hear you,” he said. “Let’s help.” He dabbed a bit of soapy water on the stain and rubbed gently. “Soft circles,” he said, making tiny swirls with his fingers. The shirt brightened. “Ahh,” the shirt said. “Thank you.”

Squeaky Sock watched with wide eyes. “You help everyone.”

Momo nodded. “When we are kind, we all feel better.”

Momo checked the washer. He touched the buttons carefully. “We use the gentle cycle,” he said. “Gentle, like a hug.” He measured soap slowly. The soap smelled sweet, like fresh rain. “Not too much,” he said. “Just right.”

He put the lights in, and Squeaky Sock held his breath. “I’m scared,” Squeaky whispered.

Momo held Squeaky’s toe. “Let’s do three calm breaths together.” They breathed in. “Smell the lemons.” They breathed out. “Blow out a candle.” One, two, three. Squeaky Sock’s wobbles slowed.

“Ready?” Momo asked.

“Ready,” said Squeaky, a little braver.

Momo pressed start. “Whirr, whirr,” sang the washer. Momo and Squeaky watched the clothes swish and swoosh like fish in a pond. Bubbles danced on the glass. Momo hummed a soft tune. “Swish and clean, kind and keen.”

When it was done, Momo gently moved the wet clothes to the dryer. “Tumble time,” he said. The dryer went “Clack, clack, warm and cozy.”

They waited. Momo poured two cups of water. “Sip, sip,” he said. “Water helps our bodies.” Squeaky Sock slurped happily. They stretched their arms up. “Reach for the sky,” Momo said. “Wiggle fingers.” They shook their hands out and smiled.

The dryer beeped. Momo opened the door. Warm air puffed out like a soft hug. “Mmm, toasty,” said the fluffy towel.

Momo folded the shirts into neat squares. He rolled the towels like sleepy snails. He paired pairs: two mittens, two gloves, two socks—wait.

“Stripey Sock?” Squeaky called. “Stripey?”

Momo looked under the hamper. He checked behind the basket. He listened. “Plip, plip,” went the sink. “Clack,” went the dryer door. “I’m here,” came a faint, shy voice.

Momo gently lifted a small white sheet. Under it lay a sock with two blue stripes and a tiny tear near the heel.

“Stripey!” Squeaky cheered. “I missed you!”

Stripey Sock looked down. “I got torn,” he said softly. “I feel sad.”

Momo’s heart felt warm. He knelt beside Stripey. “It’s okay to feel sad,” he said. “We can help you.” He found a little sewing kit in a drawer. The needle was shiny, and the thread was soft and blue.

“Will it hurt?” Stripey asked.

Momo smiled kindly. “I will be gentle.” He took another deep breath. “Smell the lemons.” He blew out. “Blow out a candle.” Then he stitched slowly, in tiny loops. “In… out… in… out.” Stripey held Squeaky’s edge. Squeaky hummed a calm tune. Soon the tear was closed.

Stripey wiggled his toe. “It feels better,” he said.

Squeaky hugged Stripey. “I am so happy you’re okay.”

Momo placed them side by side. “Together again,” he said.

They finished folding. The room was tidy. The air smelled fresh and clean. The washer was quiet. The dryer rested. The mountain of laundry was now a neat, happy hill.

Squeaky Sock looked up at Momo. “You were so brave and kind.”

Momo smiled. “Being brave means helping, even when things feel big. Being kind means listening, caring, and taking gentle steps.”

Stripey Sock added, “And taking calm breaths helps our hearts.”

Momo nodded. “Yes. Today we learned compassion, calm breaths for our minds, and healthy habits like sorting, washing, and drinking water.”

Squeaky and Stripey cheered, “Hooray for kindness!”

Momo patted his heart. “Let’s remember: We can help, we can breathe, and we can care.”

The End.
