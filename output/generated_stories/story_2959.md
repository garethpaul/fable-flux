# Roo’s Gentle Breaths in the Attic

Thump-thump! Roo, a joyful kangaroo joey, hopped up the wooden stairs to the quiet attic. The attic smelled like warm dust and cinnamon. Sunlight made soft squares on the floor. A tall window showed tiny dancing dust sparkles.

“Look at all the boxes!” Roo said. “Let’s make art!” Roo’s tail swished with happy wiggles.

On an old trunk, Roo found crayons, big paper, and a shiny box of stickers. The stickers were stars, moons, and little hearts. “Ooo,” Roo whispered. “So pretty.”

Roo’s friend, Pip the koala, climbed up, yawned, and rubbed sleepy eyes. “I want to make a rocket,” Pip said in a slow, cozy voice.

Roo clapped. “I want to make a rainbow!” Roo grabbed the red crayon. It felt smooth and warm. Scritch, scritch went the crayon on the paper. The attic was quiet, but Roo could hear birds cheeping outside and the wind saying shhh through the window.

Pip reached for the shiny sticker box. “I’ll put stars on my rocket,” Pip said.

Roo’s heart jumped. Roo loved stars. Roo really, really wanted the stars too. Roo’s tummy felt tight, like a knot. Roo’s cheeks got hot. A little storm cloud puffed in Roo’s chest.

“That’s mine!” Roo shouted, grabbing the star box. “I saw it first!”

Pip pulled it back. “No, me!”

The box slipped. Thump! It hit the floor. Stickers flew like little snowflakes. One star stuck to Roo’s nose. Another stuck to Pip’s ear. Roo’s fists clenched. Pip’s lip wobbled.

“I am MAD!” Roo yelled. The word sounded loud in the soft attic.

Pip scooted back, eyes round. The attic felt too small. The dust sparkles didn’t dance now. Roo’s heart pounded—boom, boom, boom—like a drum.

Roo remembered something Mama had said: “When big mad feelings come, stop, breathe, and use gentle words.” Mama had shown Roo how to take balloon breaths.

Roo put paws on the chest. “Stop,” Roo whispered. “Balloon breaths.”

Roo breathed in slowly, filling the belly like a big red balloon. In... and out... whoosh. In... and out... whoosh. Roo felt the tight knot loosen. The storm cloud got smaller. Pip watched, ears twitching.

“Can I try?” Pip asked softly.

Roo nodded. “In... and out... like a balloon.” They breathed together. In... out... in... out. The attic felt calm again. The dust sparkles swirled like tiny dancers.

Roo looked at Pip. “I felt mad,” Roo said honestly. “I wanted the stars so much. I grabbed. That was not kind.”

Pip touched the star on Roo’s nose and giggled. “You look like a shiny kangaroo.” Pip’s voice was gentle. “I felt mad too. I wanted them first.”

Roo blinked. “We both felt mad.”

Pip nodded. “We can take turns.”

Roo’s ears perked. “Yes! Taking turns helps.”

Roo picked up the shiny box, held it with both paws, and said, “I am sorry I grabbed. You can use the stars first. I can wait.” Roo’s tail curled close. Waiting felt hard, but Roo breathed again. In... out...

Pip smiled. “Thank you, Roo. After I do my rocket, it’s your turn.”

They began again. Scritch-scritch went Pip’s gray crayon making a tall rocket. Peel, press, peel, press went the stickers. Pip placed stars around the rocket. “Zoom,” Pip whispered. Roo watched and hummed a little song to help waiting feel soft.

Soon, Pip handed back the box. “Your turn!”

Roo’s grin was big and bright. “Thank you for waiting with me.” Roo peeled a golden star and placed it on the rainbow. Peel, press. Peel, press. The rainbow looked like it was twinkling. Roo added a moon sticker and some hearts. The hearts made Roo think of being kind.

They taped their pictures to the slanted wall. Pip’s rocket zoomed through starry space. Roo’s rainbow sparkled like candy.

Roo and Pip held paws. “We did it,” Roo said. “We made art. We used balloon breaths. We took turns.”

“And we told the truth about our feelings,” Pip added. “That helped.”

Roo nodded. “When I felt mad, I stopped, breathed, and used gentle words. That made the mad get smaller.”

The attic felt friendly again. The wind said shhh in a happy way. The sun squares felt warm on their fur. Roo’s tummy felt light, not tight.

Mama’s voice floated up the stairs. “Roo? Pip? How is it going?”

Roo called, “We’re good! We are sharing and taking turns!”

Mama came up and saw the art. “Beautiful,” she said. “I see stars, a rocket, and a rainbow. I also see calm breaths and honest hearts.”

Roo bounced once, softly. “When we got mad, we did balloon breaths. Then we used gentle words. We waited our turns.”

Mama smiled. “That is wonderful anger-management. You were tolerant and honest. You listened to each other.”

Roo hugged Pip. Pip hugged back. Their fur felt soft and snuggly. The attic seemed to smile.

Roo whispered, “Next time I feel mad, I will stop, breathe, and speak gently.”

Pip whispered back, “And I will, too.”

Together they looked at their pictures and felt proud. The stars on Roo’s nose and Pip’s ear still sparkled. They laughed and laughed, and the attic was quiet and happy.

The End.
