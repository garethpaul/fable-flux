# Bubbles Builds a Colorful Castle

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with shiny gray skin and bright, curious eyes. One rainy day, Bubbles visited the creative art room at the community center. The room smelled like crayons and glue. Soft music tinkled like tiny bells. Paints sparkled in jars. Brushes sat like little soldiers in a cup. The floor felt cool and smooth under Bubbles’s flippers.

"Welcome, Bubbles!" said Miss Rosa, the art teacher. She had paint on her apron and a warm, kind smile. "Today we build with boxes and make art!"

Bubbles giggled and splashed his tail in a little bucket of clean water. "I want to build a castle!" he chirped.

Around the room, children of all kinds were busy. Mei hummed while she glued colorful paper squares. Jamal stacked tall boxes, higher and higher. Priya rolled clay into tiny snakes. Mateo made round windows with safety scissors. Everyone’s hands were different sizes. Everyone’s skin and hair were different colors and shapes. The room was full of happy voices and gentle clinks and taps.

Bubbles found some boxes. Big boxes, small boxes, and a box that smelled like oranges. He stacked them, pat… pat… pat. He added a paper flag that fluttered when the fan whispered by. "Ta-da!" Bubbles said. He stepped back.

But the boxes leaned. They wobbled. Wobble… wobble… crash! The castle fell with a soft whoosh and a papery flop.

Bubbles’s cheeks felt warm. His heart made a tiny thump. "Oh no," he whispered. "I can’t do it."

Miss Rosa knelt beside him. "What do we say when something falls?" she asked gently.

Bubbles took a deep breath. He listened to the music. He smelled the lemony soap on the table. "We try again," he said, very soft.

"Yes," Miss Rosa smiled. "We try, and we learn. That is how we improve."

Mei peeked over. "I can share some sticky tape," she said. "It helps things hold hands."

Jamal grinned. "I can show you how to make a strong bottom," he added. "I call it a wide base."

Priya waved a clay snake. "I make little clay donuts," she said. "They stop sliding."

Mateo held up a cardboard tube. "This can be a tower. It’s round and strong."

Bubbles felt a tiny bubble of hope. "Thank you," he said. His tail made a happy swish.

They all worked together. Jamal pressed two big boxes side by side. "Wide base," he said. Pat, pat. Priya set little clay donuts like puffy wheels under the boxes. "No slide," she said. Squish, squish.

Bubbles tried again. He added a box on top, very gently. He used Mei’s sticky tape. Rip, press, smooth. The box stayed. "It stayed!" Bubbles laughed. His eyes shone.

Miss Rosa nodded. "You are improving already. You are using new steps."

Bubbles picked up Mateo’s tube and tapped it lightly. "Tower here?" he asked.

"Yes! But listen," Mateo said. He wiggled the tube. "It rolls. Tape it to a square first."

Bubbles did. The tube did not roll away. He felt proud. His chest felt warm and light, like a bubble floating up.

He added windows made of shiny foil. He drew swirly waves with blue crayon. The crayon made a crunchy sound on the cardboard. Mei glued on paper flowers—pink, purple, yellow. They smelled faintly sweet. Jamal helped make a little bridge. Priya rolled more clay donuts and giggled when one looked like a tiny bagel.

Bubbles stopped and looked. The castle was not crooked now. It stood strong. It was colorful and different, with tall round towers, short square rooms, and a zigzag roof. It did not look like any castle in a book. It looked like their castle.

A small penguin named Pip waddled over. Pip had never built before. "My flippers are different," Pip said softly. "I don’t know if I can."

Bubbles smiled. He remembered the whoosh and flop. He remembered the warm feeling of trying again. "Different is good," he told Pip. "We all do things in our own way. We can improve together."

Bubbles showed Pip how to press tape, slow and gentle. Pip tapped with his flippers. Tap, tap. The tape stuck. Pip giggled. "I did it!"

"See?" Bubbles said. "Step by step."

Miss Rosa rang a tiny bell. "Artists, please share what you learned."

Bubbles took a breath. "My castle fell," he said. "I felt sad. But my friends helped me, and I tried again. I learned to make a wide base. I learned to go slow. I improved! Now I feel proud."

Mei said, "I learned that tape is like a hug."

Jamal said, "I learned big on bottom, small on top."

Priya said, "I learned clay donuts are strong."

Mateo said, "I learned round can be strong when it stands on square."

Pip said, "I learned I can do it with my flippers."

They all clapped. The claps sounded like soft rain. Bubbles’s fins tingled with joy.

Miss Rosa smiled. "We build with boxes, and we build ourselves, too. Every try makes us stronger."

Bubbles touched the flag on top. It fluttered and whispered. He looked at his friends—different faces, different hands, different skills. Together, they had built something beautiful, and Bubbles had built his self-esteem.

"Next time," Bubbles said, "I will try something new again."

"Together," said his friends.

The End.
