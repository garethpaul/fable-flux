# Miko’s Clean Hands Story

Miko was a courageous little monkey who loved his cozy bedroom. His blanket was soft like clouds. His pillow smelled like fresh bananas. On his small desk sat crayons, paper, and a bright blue pencil. Miko loved to write stories and draw pictures.

One rainy afternoon, Miko bounced onto his bed. “Today,” he said, “I will write a big, brave story!” He wiggled his toes and grabbed his blue pencil.

Then he saw it. Crumbs on his sheets. Sticky smudges on his hands. A smear of banana on his cheek. “Oh no!” Miko frowned. “My hands are icky and sticky!”

He tried to write. “Once upon a—” The pencil slipped. A sticky handprint smeared across the page. Miko’s eyebrows scrunched. His tail twitched. His ears felt hot. “This is not working!” he shouted.

His favorite stuffed friend, Coco the bear, watched from the pillow. Miko hugged Coco tight. “I’m mad,” he said, his voice shaky. “I want to write, but my hands are yucky.”

Coco was quiet, but Miko heard a tiny voice inside his heart, like Coco was whispering, “Breathe, Miko. Slow breaths.”

Miko took a big breath in. His chest rose up like a balloon. He let the air out slowly. “Whooo.” He tried again. In... and out. The angry feeling began to melt, little by little, like ice in the sun.

Tap, tap. Mama Monkey peeked into the cozy bedroom. Warm light from the hallway made gentle shadows on the walls. “I heard a loud voice,” Mama said softly. “Do you need help, brave writer?”

Miko blinked. “Mama, I want to write my story. But my hands are sticky, and I keep making a mess. I feel angry.”

Mama nodded with compassion. She sat beside Miko and patted his hand. “Feelings are okay,” she said. “Anger comes to visit sometimes. We can listen to it and make a good plan.”

Miko sniffled. His nose smelled sweet banana and a little soap from the hallway. “What plan?”

Mama smiled. “Step one: calm body. You already took slow breaths. That’s brave. Step two: clean hands. Clean hands help clean writing. Step three: try again.”

Miko’s eyes lit up. “I can do that!”

He hopped off the bed and padded to the small bathroom next to his room. The floor felt cool under his feet. He turned on the water. It made a soft whoosh and warm steam kissed his face. “Hello, water,” Miko giggled.

He pumped soap into his palm. Squish! The soap smelled like oranges. Tiny bubbles tickled his fingers. “Scrub, scrub, scrub,” he sang. “Palms and backs, between fingers, around thumbs. Scrub, scrub, scrub!” He counted slowly, “One... two... three...” all the way to twenty. He rinsed. The water turned the bubbles into shiny rivers.

Miko shook his hands. Drip, drop! Then he dried them on a clean towel. Rub, rub. His hands felt smooth and fresh. He looked in the mirror and wiped the banana smudge from his cheek with a warm, damp cloth. “All clean!” he cheered.

Back in his cozy bedroom, everything felt brighter. The blanket felt fluffy again. The blue pencil felt sturdy in his clean fingers. Miko smiled at Coco. “Thank you for waiting.”

He opened a new page. The paper smelled like wood and sunshine. He began to write big, careful letters. “M-I-K-O.” He drew a star. He wrote his story: “Once upon a time, a brave monkey found a clean, calm way.”

He read it aloud to Mama. His voice was clear and happy. “The monkey had sticky hands. He felt mad. He took breaths. He washed with soap and water. Then he discovered he could write his best story!”

Mama clapped softly. “You solved the problem. That’s discovery! And you took care of your feelings with kind breaths. That’s anger management. I’m proud of you.”

Miko looked at Coco and whispered, “Thank you for being patient.” He gave his stuffed friend a gentle squeeze. “That’s compassion,” Mama added with a wink.

Miko added one more line to his story. “Clean hands help clean writing.” He drew bubbles, water drops, and a smiling sink. He traced each letter slowly. The pencil made a satisfying scratch-scratch on the page.

Later, Miko made a little sign and taped it near his desk. It said, “Breathe. Wash. Write.” He decorated it with orange soap bubbles and a tiny blue pencil. The sign made his cozy bedroom feel even more special.

That night, as the rain tapped the window like soft drums, Miko snuggled under his warm blanket. His room smelled fresh and cozy. He whispered, “I am brave. I am kind. I keep my hands clean.” Coco cuddled close.

Miko closed his eyes with a happy sigh. He had learned something big and bright: when he cared for his body and his feelings, he could do his best work. Clean hands, calm heart, clear writing.

The End.
