# Ollie Otter’s Cozy Care Time

Ollie the playful otter loved his comfortable nursery. The nursery had soft star blankets, big squishy pillows, a tiny bookshelf, and a round rug with rainbow fish. Warm light glowed like morning sunshine. The room smelled like fresh soap and a little like oranges.

“Good morning, Ollie!” sang Miss Maple, the gentle teacher. She had kind eyes and a smile like a warm hug. “Today we learn about caring for ourselves.”

Ollie rolled on the rug. “Play time! Splash time!” He wiggled like a fish. His whiskers tickled his cheeks.

Miss Maple nodded. “Yes, play is important. But first, self-care. We wash, we brush, we rest. Then we play.”

Ollie’s tail swished. “Do I have to?” he asked. He wanted to build block towers and make them go clink, clank!

Miss Maple kneeled beside him. “Let’s make a plan together,” she said. “We can compromise. That means we both get a little of what we want.”

Ollie blinked. “Com-pro-mise?”

“It’s a friendly share-plan,” Miss Maple said. “How about this: we do quick care steps, then a fun game.”

Ollie thought. He loved games. “Okay,” he said, “but can the game have bubbles?”

“Yes,” Miss Maple laughed. “Bubbles it is.”

They went to the sink. The water felt warm on Ollie’s paws. Shhh, shhh went the tap. “Soap time,” said Miss Maple. The soap smelled like oranges. Squish, squish went the foam. Ollie rubbed front paws, back paws, and in between.

“Why do we wash?” Ollie asked.

“Good question!” said Miss Maple. “Washing keeps us healthy. It gets rid of tiny germs we can’t see.”

“Tiny germs?” Ollie peered at his paw. He couldn’t see any. He giggled. “They must be very tiny.”

“Very tiny,” Miss Maple agreed. “But washing sends them away.”

Next came face wiping. The cloth was soft and warm. “Ahh,” Ollie sighed. “This feels nice.” He patted his whiskers dry. They went boing!

They moved to the cozy mirror. “Brushing time,” said Miss Maple, handing him a small, blue toothbrush.

“Blue like the river!” Ollie said. He put a pea-sized dot of toothpaste on the brush. It smelled like mint leaves. “Tingle taste!” He brushed in little circles. Swish-swish, top and bottom. He counted softly, “One, two, three, four…” Miss Maple counted with him until ten.

“Why do we brush?” Ollie asked, bubbles on his lip.

“To keep our teeth strong,” Miss Maple said. “We brush away sugar bugs. It helps our smiles stay bright.”

Ollie grinned at the mirror. “Bright!”

After brushing, Ollie’s tummy made a quiet grr-blup.

“Snack time,” Miss Maple said. She set out crunchy apple slices and cool water. “Crisp!” Ollie said as he bit. Crunch! The apple tasted sweet and juicy. “Why apples?” he asked.

“Apples help our bodies,” Miss Maple said. “They give us energy for play.”

Ollie wiggled. “Energy!” He chewed happily.

Then Miss Maple pointed to a soft corner with pillows and a star blanket. “Now a short rest. Just three calm breaths.”

Ollie looked at the block tower. He wanted to play. But he remembered the share-plan. Compromise. He nodded and snuggled into the blanket. It felt warm and gentle. He took a slow breath in. He smelled a hint of oranges again. He let the breath out. Whooo. Three times.

“How do you feel?” asked Miss Maple.

“Calm,” Ollie said. His body felt light and comfy, like floating in a quiet pond.

Miss Maple clapped softly. “You learned so much! You cared for your paws, your teeth, your tummy, and your feelings.”

“Now bubble game?” Ollie asked.

“Bubble game!” Miss Maple agreed. She dipped a wand and blew. Whooosh! Big bubbles floated like jellyfish. Tiny bubbles twinkled like stars. Ollie clapped his paws. Pop! Pop! Pop! The bubbles felt cool and tickly.

As they played, a friend, Tilly Turtle, peeked in. “Can I play?” she asked in a shy voice.

Ollie looked at the bubble wand. There was only one. He held it close, then stopped. He remembered the share-plan.

“We can take turns,” he said. “I blow, you pop. Then you blow, I pop.” He smiled. “That is compromise!”

Tilly smiled back. “Thank you, Ollie.”

Miss Maple nodded, proud. “You used your knowledge and your kind heart.”

They took turns. Ollie blew a long, slow breath. Tilly popped bubbles with soft taps. Then Tilly blew. Ollie popped with gentle pats. Pop! Pop! Laughter filled the nursery like music.

When the bubbles were gone, Ollie built a small block tower. Tilly added a roof. They made a cozy house for a tiny toy fish. The blocks felt smooth and warm in their paws. The tower did not fall. It stood tall and steady.

Ollie stretched and yawned a happy yawn. “Self-care helped me feel good,” he said. “Wash, brush, snack, rest, and then play.”

Miss Maple gave him a thumbs-up. “You learned the self-care steps. You practiced compromise. You asked good questions. That is how we grow.”

Ollie patted his chest. “I can care for me,” he said. “And I can care for friends.”

The warm light glowed. The nursery felt safe and bright. Ollie felt clean, calm, and strong. He hugged his star blanket and smiled a big, bright smile.

Today was a good day.

The End.
