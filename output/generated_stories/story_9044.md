# Polly's Big World Plan

Polly the parrot sat on her perch in the guest room. Her bright green feathers shimmered in the morning light. She looked out the window at the big, wide world.

"I want to help everyone feel happy," Polly chirped to herself. Her orange beak clicked as she thought hard.

The guest room was cozy and warm. Soft pillows sat on the bed. A colorful world map hung on the wall. Polly loved looking at all the different countries.

"But how can one little parrot help the whole world?" she wondered aloud.

Polly fluffed her red tail feathers. She had an idea! She hopped over to the desk where crayons and paper waited.

"I'll make a plan!" she squawked excitedly.

First, Polly drew a big circle. "This is the world," she said, using a blue crayon. Then she added green spots for land.

"Everyone in the world needs kindness," Polly thought. She drew little hearts all over her picture.

Next, Polly picked up a yellow crayon. She drew smiling faces of children from different places. Some had different skin colors. Some wore different clothes.

"We're all the same inside," Polly chirped happily. "We all want friends!"

Polly heard footsteps in the hallway. Her friend Emma peeked into the guest room.

"Hi, Polly! What are you making?" Emma asked sweetly.

"I'm planning how to help the whole world!" Polly explained proudly. Her feathers danced as she got excited.

Emma sat down beside the desk. The room smelled like fresh flowers from the garden outside.

"Tell me about your plan," Emma said with a warm smile.

Polly pointed to her colorful drawing with her wing. "First, we use kind words. No yelling or mean talk. Just gentle, peaceful words."

"That sounds wonderful," Emma nodded.

"Second," Polly continued, "we share with others. If someone is hungry, we share food. If someone is sad, we share hugs!"

Polly hopped excitedly on her perch. "And third, we learn about different places and people. The more we know, the more we can help!"

Emma clapped her hands softly. "Your plan is amazing, Polly!"

"But I'm just one small parrot," Polly sighed. Her bright eyes looked worried.

Emma gently stroked Polly's soft feathers. "Even small actions can make big changes. You can start right here!"

Polly tilted her colorful head. "How?"

"You can teach other children your plan," Emma suggested. "When they visit, you can show them your drawing and share your ideas."

Polly's eyes sparkled like tiny stars. "Yes! And I can learn new words in different languages!"

"¡Hola! Bonjour! Guten Tag!" Polly practiced cheerfully.

Emma giggled. The sound filled the cozy guest room with joy.

"I can also send letters to children in other countries," Polly planned excitedly. "We can be pen pals!"

Polly picked up a green crayon and added more to her picture. She drew children holding hands around the world.

"Look, Emma! When we all work together with kindness, the whole world becomes brighter!"

The afternoon sun streamed through the window, making Polly's feathers glow like a rainbow.

"You're right, Polly," Emma said warmly. "Every person can help make the world more peaceful and happy."

Polly carefully hung her drawing on the wall next to the world map. Now she had both a picture of the world as it is, and her dream of how it could be.

"Tomorrow, I'll start teaching my plan to everyone who visits," Polly announced proudly.

As the day ended, Polly felt happy and hopeful. She learned that even the smallest parrot could help make the world a better place through kindness, sharing, and peaceful communication.

The End.
