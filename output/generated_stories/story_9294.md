# Gigi the Giraffe and the Fair Treehouse Rules

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with soft, sandy fur and gentle eyes. She lived near a big, friendly tree. High in the branches was a cozy treehouse. The treehouse smelled like fresh wood and warm sunshine. The walls were covered with colorful pictures and little paper stars.

One bright morning, Gigi climbed the ladder carefully. “Step, step, step,” she said. Her long neck peeked through the door. “Hello?” she called.

Her friends were inside. Tiko the tiny turtle tapped a drum. Buzz the bee buzzed a happy tune. Mimi the monkey swung from a rope, giggling. “Welcome, Gigi!” Mimi cheered.

Gigi smiled. “I brought paper and crayons,” she said. “Let’s draw and write!”

They sat on soft cushions. Gigi felt the cool breeze tickle her ears. “I love being here,” she sighed.

Soon, a loud thump shook the treehouse. Bump! Bump! Bump! Bruno the bear climbed up fast. He pushed open the door. “I want the drum! Move!” Bruno grabbed Tiko’s drum.

Tiko’s eyes filled with tears. “But I was playing,” he whispered.

Buzz buzzed nervously. Mimi hid behind a curtain. Gigi’s heart felt heavy. This did not feel fair. It did not feel safe.

Gigi took a deep breath. “Bruno,” she said softly, “we share here. We take turns. That is fair.”

Bruno snorted. “I’m big. I want it now.”

Gigi blinked. She was thoughtful and brave. She wanted justice—what is right and fair for everyone. She wanted safety too.

Gigi said, “Friends, let’s pause. Let’s make treehouse rules. We can write them so everyone knows.”

Tiko sniffled. “Will rules help?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Gigi. “Rules help keep us safe. Rules help make things fair.”

Mimi peeked out. “Can we make them fun?”

Gigi smiled. “Yes! Let’s be creative.”

She spread the paper. The crayons smelled waxy and bright. Gigi wrote slowly, with big letters, so everyone could see. “TREEHOUSE RULES,” she read aloud. “Let’s think together.”

Buzz hovered near. “Rule one?” he buzzed.

Gigi tapped her chin. “One: We take turns.”

“Good,” said Tiko, patting the drum.

Mimi added, “Two: We use gentle hands and gentle voices.”

Buzz sang, “Three: We listen when someone says stop.”

Gigi kept writing. Her neck made a soft swish as she leaned over the paper. “Four: We keep the ladder safe—one at a time.”

“Five,” said Tiko shyly, “We clean up together.”

Gigi wrote each rule in bright colors. Then she drew little pictures: a drum with two happy faces, a ladder with one careful paw, a broom with smiling bristles. She felt proud. She was using her writing to help.

Bruno frowned. “I don’t like rules.”

Gigi looked at him kindly. “Rules help everyone. They help you too, Bruno. When it is your turn, no one will push you. That is justice. That is fair.”

Bruno looked at the drum, then at Tiko. He shuffled his big feet. “But I wanted to play now.”

Gigi nodded. “I understand. Waiting is hard. We can make a turn timer.” She placed three smooth pebbles on the floor. “Each pebble is a turn. Tiko plays one pebble song. Then it is your turn. Then it is Mimi’s. See? Everyone gets a turn.”

Bruno scratched his head. “Hmm.”

Gigi handed Bruno a blue crayon. “Would you like to add a rule?”

Bruno stared at the crayon. His voice went small. “Six: We ask before we take.”

Gigi wrote it down and drew a paw and a question mark. “That is a smart, kind rule,” she said. “Thank you.”

Bruno’s shoulders relaxed. He took a deep breath. “Okay,” he said. “I will wait.”

Tiko smiled and tapped a soft, bouncy beat. Thum-thum-thum. The drum sounded warm and friendly. One pebble song later, Tiko placed a pebble in a little bowl. “Your turn, Bruno,” he said.

Bruno sat carefully. He tapped gently. boom-boom, tap. “This feels nice,” he whispered.

Mimi clapped. Buzz zipped and hummed along. Gigi’s heart felt light. The treehouse felt safe again. The air smelled like crayons and honey. Sunlight made golden dots on the floor.

Later, they played a new game. Mimi said, “Let’s act out our rules!” She pretended to wait at the ladder. Gigi cheered, “Safe ladder!” Tiko showed gentle hands with the drum. Buzz practiced asking, “May I try?” Everyone laughed and learned.

At the end of the day, Gigi taped the rules to the wall. The paper fluttered softly. “We did this together,” she said. “We were persistent. We kept trying until it was fair for everyone.”

Bruno nodded. “I felt mad at first. But the rules helped. I like fair.”

Gigi smiled. “Justice means everyone is treated right. Justice means we share, we listen, and we keep each other safe.”

The sun began to set. The treehouse glowed warm and orange. They cleaned up together. Step by step, they climbed down the ladder, one at a time.

Gigi looked up at the rules through the open door. She felt proud and calm. “Today we used our words. We used our writing. We used our hearts,” she said.

All the friends said together, “Fair is for everyone!”

The End.
