# Leo's Pantry Adventure

Leo was a brave little lion cub with golden fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived with his mama in a cozy house with a big pantry full of yummy food. The pantry smelled like cookies, apples, and warm bread.

One sunny morning, Leo's tummy rumbled loudly. "GRRRROWL!" it went.

"Mama, I'm hungry!" Leo called out, but Mama was busy washing dishes in the kitchen. The water was splashing, and she couldn't hear him.

Leo's tummy rumbled again. He looked at the pantry door and thought, "I'm brave! I can get my own snack!"

He pushed open the heavy pantry door with his little paws. Inside, the shelves were tall and full of colorful boxes and jars. Leo saw his favorite honey crackers on the very top shelf, way up high.

"I can reach those!" Leo said confidently. He climbed up on a wobbly stool and stretched his paws as high as he could. But the box was still too far away.

Leo jumped and jumped, making the stool rock back and forth. "Almost... got... it!" he puffed.

CRASH! The stool tipped over, and Leo tumbled down. Boxes of cereal fell everywhere, making a terrible mess. Rice scattered across the floor like tiny white pebbles. A jar of jam rolled under the shelf.

Leo sat in the middle of the mess, feeling very sorry for himself. His knee hurt a little, and now he was even hungrier than before.

"Oh no," he whispered, looking at all the spilled food. "Mama will be so upset with me."

Just then, Mama Lion appeared in the doorway. She saw the mess and Leo's sad face covered in cereal dust.

"Leo, what happened here?" Mama asked gently.

Leo's eyes filled with tears. "I was hungry, and I wanted to get crackers by myself. But I made a big mess instead. I'm sorry, Mama. I should have waited for you to help me."

Mama Lion sat down next to Leo and gave him a warm hug. "Thank you for telling me the truth, little one. I forgive you. But next time, please ask for help when you need it."

"I will, Mama. I promise," Leo sniffled. "But now all the food is ruined, and we don't have anything to eat."

Mama Lion smiled and looked around the pantry. "Let's see what we can do together."

They worked side by side, cleaning up the mess. Leo was patient as they picked up each piece of cereal. As they cleaned, Mama showed Leo all the good food that wasn't spilled.

"Look, Leo! The apples are still perfect," Mama said, holding up a shiny red apple.

"And the bread is safe too!" Leo noticed, pointing to a loaf on a low shelf.

"And here are some crackers that didn't fall," Mama added.

As they continued cleaning, Leo realized something wonderful. "Mama, we still have so much good food! I thought everything was ruined, but look how much we have!"

"That's right, Leo. Even when things go wrong, we can always find something to be grateful for," Mama said wisely.

They made a delicious snack together with apple slices, crackers, and a little bit of honey. As they ate, Leo felt warm and happy inside.

"Thank you, Mama, for helping me and forgiving me," Leo said. "And thank you for all this yummy food. I'm grateful for everything we have."

"You're welcome, my brave little cub," Mama said, nuzzling his fuzzy head. "Remember, being grateful makes even the simplest things feel special."

From that day on, Leo always said "thank you" for his meals and asked for help when he needed it. He learned that gratitude and patience made everything taste better.

The End.
