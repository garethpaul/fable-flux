# Whiskers and the Listening Ears

In a cozy little house, there lived a clever orange cat named Whiskers. Whiskers had bright green eyes and the softest fur you could imagine. His favorite place in the whole house was a special closet hideaway behind the winter coats, where he kept all his treasures.

One sunny morning, Whiskers was in his closet making a beautiful card for his friend Bunny's birthday. He had colorful papers, sparkly stickers, and his special safety scissors. "This will be the best card ever!" he purred happily.

"Whiskers, breakfast time!" called Mama from downstairs.

But Whiskers was so focused on cutting out a perfect heart shape that he didn't hear her. The scissors made a satisfying *snip-snip* sound as he worked.

"Whiskers! Your tuna is getting cold!" Mama called again, her voice a little louder.

Still, Whiskers kept cutting and gluing. The glue made a *squish* sound as he pressed the heart onto the card. "Almost done," he whispered to himself.

When Mama came upstairs and found him in the closet, she looked worried. "Whiskers, I've been calling you for ten minutes! Your breakfast is all cold now."

Whiskers felt his whiskers twitch with frustration. "But Mama, I was making something special! I didn't want to stop!" His tail started to swish back and forth, which always happened when he felt upset.

Mama sat down next to him in the cozy closet. "I can see you're working very hard, sweetie. But when we don't listen, we miss important things. What if there had been an emergency?"

Whiskers felt his ears droop. He hadn't thought about that. "I'm sorry, Mama. I just wanted to finish my card."

"Your card is beautiful," Mama said, touching the sparkly heart. "But let's think of a way to keep crafting AND listen at the same time. What ideas do you have?"

Whiskers put his paw on his chin, thinking hard. That's what he always did when he needed to solve a problem. "Maybe... maybe I could keep my ears up while I work? Like this?" He demonstrated by perking up his ears and turning them toward the door.

"That's a wonderful idea!" Mama smiled. "What else could help?"

"Oh! I could work with my closet door open so I can hear better!" Whiskers said, his eyes lighting up. "And maybe I could answer when you call, even if I'm not done yet?"

"Those are very smart solutions," Mama said proudly. "You're such a good problem-solver, Whiskers."

They warmed up his tuna together, and Whiskers practiced his new listening skills. When Mama called him for lunch later, he immediately said, "Coming, Mama!" even though he was in the middle of adding glitter to Bunny's card.

At lunch, he told Mama all about his card. "I listened with my ears AND finished my project! It worked!"

"You learned something very important today," Mama said. "Good listening helps us stay safe and shows we care about others. And the best part? You figured out how to do both things - craft AND listen!"

That afternoon, when Bunny came over for her birthday surprise, she loved her sparkly card. "You're such a good friend, Whiskers!"

Whiskers purred loudly. He felt proud that he had learned to be a better listener. From that day on, he always kept his ears open and his heart ready to hear the people he loved.

The End.
