# Ollie Otter’s Closet Camp

Ollie was a playful otter with shiny whiskers and a bright blue scarf. He loved to make cozy forts. Today, Ollie crawled into his favorite spot—his closet hideaway. It was hush-quiet inside. The soft coats felt like sleepy clouds. The floor smelled a little like cedar and a little like fresh soap.

“Hello, Closet Camp!” Ollie giggled. He clicked his little teeth with joy and wiggled his tail. “Today, we explore!”

Ollie turned on his tiny lantern. The warm light made gentle circles on the walls. He listened. Drip, drip—he could hear the bathroom faucet far away. Rustle, rustle—he heard the sleeves of a long coat brushing his ear. His whiskers tingled with adventure.

“Ollie?” called a friendly voice outside. It was Mia, his next-door friend. Mia had curly hair and bright pink glasses. She loved to draw and to ask questions. “Can I come in?”

“Yes!” said Ollie. “Welcome to Closet Camp! We have secret maps and snacks and… oh!” He bumped a small pile beside his basket. Paper rolled out like a sleepy snake. There were crinkly bottles and boxes, too.

Mia crawled inside and pushed up her glasses. “What is all this?”

Ollie sniffed. The pile smelled like old juice and cardboard. “I was saving things to make crafts,” he said. “But now it looks messy.” His ears drooped. “I don’t like mess.”

Mia smiled. “We can clean it up! We can sort it. My teacher says, ‘We reduce, reuse, recycle!’”

Ollie tilted his head. “Recycle?”

“It means we help old things become new things,” Mia said. “We put them in the right bins.”

Ollie clapped his paws. “A sorting game! I love games.”

They scooted closer. Ollie held up a plastic bottle. “This one is smooth and shiny.”

“Plastic,” said Mia. “That goes in the blue bin.”

Ollie placed it in a little blue basket he used for socks. Plink! The sound was happy.

Next, Mia lifted a cereal box. “This one is cardboard. It goes in the green bin.”

“Green bin!” Ollie echoed. He loved the crunchy sound the box made—crunch, crunch—as he flattened it with his paws. “I can practice flattening! Tap-tap, press-press.” He smiled. “Practicing makes me better.”

Mia nodded. “Practice helps us learn.” She picked up a shiny can. “Metal!”

Ollie sniffed the can and wrinkled his nose. “It smells like beans.” He giggled. “Pee-ew!” He looked around. “We should wash it. Hygiene helps us stay healthy.”

Mia clapped. “Yes! Clean, clean.”

They tiptoed to the bathroom. The water was cool and splashy. Swish, swish—Ollie rinsed the can. Mia washed the bottle. They sang a little song:

“Wash, wash, wash away,
Sticky bits and smells today.
Clean and bright, it’s okay,
Recycling the tidy way!”

Back in the closet, it felt fresher. The air was light, like a little breeze. Ollie’s whiskers wiggled with pride.

“Look,” said Mia. “We have three bins: blue for plastic, green for cardboard, yellow for metal.”

Ollie pointed to a glittery ribbon. “What about this?”

Mia thought. “Hmm. That one we can reuse. It can be a craft.”

“Reuse!” Ollie tied the ribbon on his blue basket like a bow. “Fancy bin!”

They kept sorting. Paper went whoosh. Cans went clink. Bottles went plink. Ollie practiced each step: look, name, sort. He said it like a little poem. “Look, name, sort. Look, name, sort.” Each time, he felt braver and smarter.

“Sometimes I get mixed up,” Ollie said softly. “I am different. I need more time.”

Mia smiled warm and kind. “Different is good. We all learn in our own way.” She put a hand on his paw. “You told me when you needed help. That is great communication.”

Ollie’s heart felt bubbly like soda. “Thank you, Mia.” He took a deep breath. “I can do this.”

Soon, the pile was gone. The closet hideaway looked neat and cozy again. The coats whispered, “Thank you.” The lantern light seemed brighter.

Mia stretched. “Time to wash our paws.”

“Hygiene!” said Ollie. They washed with soap. The bubbles smelled like lemon. Squish-squash, rub-rub, rinse-rinse. Their paws felt clean and soft.

Back in the closet, Ollie spread a small blanket. “Now we celebrate. Snack time!”

Mia laughed. “What’s the snack?”

“Apple slices,” said Ollie. Crunch! The apple was sweet and juicy. “We can put the core in the compost,” he added, proud of his big word.

Mia drew a little map of their bins with a purple crayon. “This is our Recycling Map. We can practice every day.”

Ollie taped the map to the wall. “Closet Camp Rules,” he announced. “One: We reduce, reuse, recycle. Two: We wash sticky things. Three: We practice. Four: We talk and help each other. Five: We celebrate our differences!”

Mia cheered. “Hooray!”

Ollie looked around his tidy, happy hideaway. His chest felt warm. “Today we learned to help the Earth,” he said. “Small otters can do big help.”

Mia nodded. “Yes. We did it together.”

Ollie snuggled into the blanket. “I feel proud, and my closet feels calm.” He smiled. “Tomorrow, we can teach others.”

“Deal,” said Mia. They bumped fists. Tap!

The End.
