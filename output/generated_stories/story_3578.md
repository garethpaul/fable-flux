# Miko's Big Guest Room Adventure

Miko was a brave little monkey with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. Today was special because his grandparents were coming to visit! Mama had asked him to help make the guest room perfect for their arrival.

"Miko, can you help me get the guest room ready?" Mama called from down the hall.

"Yes, Mama! I'm coming!" Miko swung his long tail happily as he scampered to the cozy guest room.

The room smelled like fresh lavender from the clean sheets. Sunlight streamed through the big window, making everything glow warmly. But when Miko looked around, he saw a big problem.

"Oh no!" Miko gasped. The bed was too high for him to reach! The pillows needed fluffing, and the blanket was all wrinkled. How could a little monkey like him fix such a tall bed?

Miko scratched his head and thought hard. "I won't give up," he said to himself. "There must be a way!"

First, he tried jumping. "One, two, three... jump!" But even his highest monkey jump couldn't reach the pillows. He landed softly on the carpet with a gentle thump.

"Hmm," Miko said, tapping his chin with his finger. "What else can I try?"

He looked around the room with his curious eyes. There was a sturdy wooden chair by the window! Miko carefully pushed the chair across the soft carpet. It made a quiet scraping sound as it moved.

"This might work!" he said excitedly.

Miko climbed onto the chair, but it still wasn't quite tall enough. He needed to think of something else. Then he spotted some thick books on the dresser.

"Books are for learning, and they can help me reach too!" Miko said cleverly.

He carefully stacked two big books on the chair. The books felt heavy in his small hands, but he was determined. Now he could reach the bed perfectly!

Miko fluffed the pillows until they were puffy and soft. "These feel like fluffy clouds!" he giggled. He smoothed the wrinkled blanket with his tiny hands until it looked neat and cozy.

But there was still one more problem. The room needed fresh flowers from the garden, and they were in a tall vase on the high dresser.

"I need to be extra careful," Miko thought. He remembered what Mama always said about taking turns and asking for help when needed.

"Mama!" he called. "Can you help me with the tall vase? I want to take turns - I'll move it, and you can help me reach!"

Mama came in and smiled when she saw how resourceful Miko had been. "Look at what a clever monkey you are! You used the chair and books to solve your problem!"

Together, they carefully moved the beautiful flowers to the bedside table. The sweet smell of roses filled the room.

"The guest room looks perfect!" Mama said, giving Miko a warm hug.

When Grandma and Grandpa arrived, they were amazed. "This room is so welcoming!" Grandpa said.

"Miko made it special all by himself," Mama explained proudly.

Miko beamed with happiness. He had learned that when you face a problem, you don't give up. You look around, think creatively, and use what you have. Sometimes you need to ask for help, and that's okay too!

"Being resourceful means using my brain and what's around me," Miko said wisely. "And never giving up when things seem hard!"

That night, as Grandma and Grandpa slept peacefully in their cozy room, Miko felt proud of his big accomplishment.

The End.
