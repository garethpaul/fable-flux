# Ruby Rabbit's Guest Room Surprise

Ruby Rabbit hopped excitedly into the cozy guest room. Her soft brown ears twitched with joy as she looked around at the pretty blue walls and fluffy white bed. Today was special - Grandma Bunny was coming to visit!

"I want to make this room perfect for Grandma!" Ruby said to herself, her pink nose wiggling with determination. She had so many wonderful ideas swirling in her head like colorful butterflies.

Ruby began pulling out her art supplies from a big wooden box. Crayons rolled across the smooth wooden floor with little clicking sounds. She grabbed sheets of bright paper and started drawing beautiful flowers and hearts.

"These will look amazing on the walls!" Ruby giggled, reaching up high to tape her artwork everywhere she could reach.

Just then, her little brother Benny Bunny peeked his gray head through the doorway. His whiskers drooped sadly as he watched Ruby decorating.

"Can I help, Ruby?" Benny asked in a small voice. "I want to make it nice for Grandma too."

Ruby paused, holding a purple crayon in her paw. She looked at Benny's hopeful brown eyes. "No, Benny. This is MY special project. You might mess it up," she said, turning back to her drawing.

Benny's ears fell down, and he shuffled away with quiet little footsteps on the carpet.

Ruby continued working hard, but something didn't feel right in her fluffy chest. The room looked pretty, but it felt a bit lonely. She could hear Benny playing sadly by himself in the hallway.

When Mama Rabbit came to check on Ruby's progress, she noticed Ruby's worried expression.

"The room looks lovely, sweetheart," Mama said, her voice warm like honey. "But you look troubled. What's wrong?"

Ruby's ears drooped. "I hurt Benny's feelings. He wanted to help, but I said no. Now I feel bad inside."

Mama sat down on the soft bed and patted the spot next to her. "How do you think Benny felt when you didn't let him help?"

Ruby thought carefully, her little paws fidgeting. "Probably sad and left out. Like when no one picks me for games."

"That's very thoughtful of you to realize that," Mama smiled. "What could you do to make things better?"

Ruby's eyes lit up like tiny stars. "I could ask Benny to help! We could work together!"

Ruby hopped quickly to find Benny, who was sitting quietly in the corner with his favorite stuffed carrot.

"Benny!" Ruby called softly. "I'm sorry I hurt your feelings. Would you like to help me finish decorating for Grandma? I really need your help!"

Benny's face brightened like sunshine after rain. "Really? You want my help?"

"Yes! You're so good at making things beautiful," Ruby said, giving him a warm hug.

Together, they worked as a team. Benny drew wonderful pictures of carrots and lettuce while Ruby made colorful welcome signs. They hung streamers that danced gently in the breeze from the window. The room filled with their happy laughter and the sweet smell of fresh flowers they picked from the garden.

When Grandma Bunny arrived, she gasped with delight. "Oh my! This guest room is absolutely magical! You both worked so hard together!"

Ruby and Benny beamed with pride, their arms around each other.

"Working together made it even more special," Ruby said, learning that sharing and including others makes everything better.

The End.
