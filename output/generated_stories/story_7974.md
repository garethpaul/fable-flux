# Rosie Rabbit Learns to Listen and Sort

Rosie was a determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. One rainy day, she hopped down the wooden steps to a busy basement. The basement hummed and buzzed. The washing machine whirred. The heater clicked. Boxes rustled. It smelled like fresh soap and old cardboard.

Rosie’s ears wiggled. “Today I will help!” she said. “I will recycle all the things!”

Mama Bunny came down with a warm smile. “That is kind, Rosie,” Mama said. “Let’s learn how to sort.”

Rosie’s feet thumped with excitement. “I can do it! I am determined!”

On a table sat three big bins. Blue. Green. Yellow. A sign over them read: “Paper. Plastic. Metal.”

Mama Bunny pointed. “We listen and look. We read signs. We ask questions. That is how we learn. Can you say, ‘listen, look, learn’?”

Rosie stood tall. “Listen, look, learn!”

Mama tapped the blue bin. “Paper goes here. It is for things like boxes, newspapers, and clean paper.”

She tapped the green bin. “Plastic goes here. It is for bottles and clean food tubs.”

She tapped the yellow bin. “Metal goes here. It is for cans and lids.”

Rosie nodded so hard her whiskers wiggled. “I am ready!”

Rosie hopped to a crinkly box. “Box!” She sniffed. It smelled like cereal. “Paper?” She looked at the blue bin. Her paw lifted.

But then Rosie saw a shiny can. “Ooo, shiny!” She picked it up. “Metal!” She lifted her other paw.

Rosie tried to carry both at once. Oops! The can clinked. The box flopped. A plastic bottle rolled away. Clunk, clank, clatter!

Rosie’s ears drooped. “Oh no. It is too busy. I don’t know what to do first.”

Mama took a gentle breath. “It is okay. Busy places can make big feelings. Let’s slow down. We can use a plan. We can use our senses.”

Rosie breathed in. The basement smelled like lemons. She breathed out. The heater made a soft whoosh.

Mama said, “First, we listen.” She cupped her ear. “Listen to the words on the signs. Blue says paper. Green says plastic. Yellow says metal.”

Rosie giggled. “Signs do not talk!”

Mama smiled. “We read them or picture them in our minds. That is listening with our eyes. Next, we look. We look at each item. Is it paper? Is it plastic? Is it metal?”

Rosie nodded. “I can do that.”

“Last,” Mama said, “we learn by trying. If we do not know, we ask.”

Rosie held the cereal box again. She pressed it. It felt soft and bendy. “Paper,” she said, and gently put it in the blue bin. Plop.

She picked up the shiny can. It felt hard and cool. “Metal,” she said, and placed it in the yellow bin. Clink.

Rosie found the rolling bottle. It felt smooth. She could see through it. “Plastic!” Into the green bin it went. Thunk.

The basement did not feel so loud now. Rosie hummed with the heater. “Listen, look, learn,” she whispered.

Soon Rosie found a paper with sticky jam. She paused. “Paper?” She wrinkled her nose. “But it is messy.”

Mama nodded. “Good question. Messy paper cannot be recycled. We can throw it away or clean it if we can. Asking is smart.”

Rosie’s eyes were bright. “I will ask next time too.”

She found a big cardboard box. “Paper!” She flattened it with her gentle paws. Crunch, crunch. Into the blue bin it slid.

She found a plastic cup with a lid. “Plastic cup in green. Metal lid in yellow,” she said. Pop. Clink. She smiled big.

A little brother bunny hopped down the steps. “Rosie, can I help?” he asked in a tiny voice.

Rosie looked at the signs. She listened in her mind. She remembered Mama’s plan. “Yes,” she said softly. “First, we listen. Then we look. Then we learn.” She showed him the bins with her paw. “Blue is paper. Green is plastic. Yellow is metal.”

Her brother repeated, “Listen, look, learn.” He beamed.

Together they sorted more things. The washing machine swished. The bins filled with neat, tidy sounds—plop, thunk, clink. The busy basement began to feel calm and happy.

At last, Mama clapped. “You did it! You listened. You looked. You learned. You worked together. You helped the Earth by recycling.”

Rosie’s heart felt warm. She stood tall again. “I am a determined rabbit,” she said. “When I listen, I can learn new things. When I learn, I can help.”

Mama hugged both bunnies. “Education is all around us,” she said. “We can learn in school, and we can learn at home. We learn by asking, by trying, and by listening.”

Rosie wiggled her nose. “Education helps us take care of our world. Recycling is one way. Listening is another way.”

She took her brother’s paw. “Let’s make a chart for the bins,” she said. “We can draw pictures so we remember.”

They got paper and crayons. Scratch, scratch. Rosie drew a box and a newspaper for the blue bin. She drew a bottle for the green bin. She drew a can for the yellow bin. They taped the pictures above the bins. The tape made a tiny rip sound—rrrip!

Rosie looked at their work. “Listen, look, learn,” she said again, smiling. “I can do hard things when I listen.”

The End.
