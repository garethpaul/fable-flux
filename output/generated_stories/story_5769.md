# Rusty's Rooftop Rescue

High up on a tall building's rooftop, a shiny blue robot dog named Rusty was playing with his favorite bouncy ball. His metal tail wagged happily as he watched the red ball bounce *boing, boing, boing* across the smooth concrete.

"Woof! This is so much fun!" Rusty barked cheerfully, his LED eyes glowing bright green with joy.

Suddenly, the wind picked up with a loud *whoooosh!* Rusty's ball bounced too high and rolled right to the edge of the rooftop. As Rusty ran to catch it, he heard something that made his circuits buzz with worry.

"Chirp! Chirp! Help!" came a tiny, scared voice.

Rusty's ears perked up. He looked down and saw a small baby bird with fluffy gray feathers sitting in a nest near the roof's edge. The little bird was shivering in the cold wind.

"Oh no!" Rusty said, his voice gentle and kind. "Are you okay, little friend?"

"I'm so cold and scared," chirped the baby bird. "My mama flew away to find food, but this big wind is making me so chilly! I don't know what to do!"

Rusty's heart-light began to glow warm and yellow. He wanted to help, but he wasn't sure how. "Maybe I should wait for someone else to help," he thought at first.

But then Rusty remembered what his creator had taught him: "When someone needs help, don't wait for others. Take the first step yourself!"

"I can help you!" Rusty said with confidence, his tail wagging determinedly. "I have an idea!"

Rusty looked around the rooftop with his smart robot eyes. He spotted some old newspapers fluttering in the wind, a small cardboard box, and some soft leaves that had blown up from the trees below.

"I know what to do!" Rusty barked excitedly.

First, Rusty carefully picked up the newspapers with his gentle robot mouth and placed them around the nest to block the cold wind. *Crinkle, crinkle* went the papers as they formed a protective wall.

"That's better!" chirped the baby bird, feeling less wind.

But Rusty wasn't finished. He gathered the soft, dry leaves in his mouth and gently placed them in the cardboard box. Then, very carefully, he moved the box close to the nest.

"This will be like a cozy blanket!" Rusty explained, using his warm exhaust vent to blow gentle, warm air over the leaves. The leaves became toasty and comfortable.

"Oh, thank you!" sang the baby bird, snuggling into the warm leaves. "You're so kind and smart!"

Just then, Mama Bird returned with a worm in her beak. When she saw what Rusty had done, her eyes sparkled with gratitude.

"Thank you for taking care of my baby," Mama Bird chirped sweetly. "You didn't have to help, but you chose to. That shows you have a brave and caring heart!"

Rusty's chest glowed with pride and happiness. "I'm glad I could help," he said. "I learned that when I see someone who needs help, I shouldn't wait. I can take action right away!"

The baby bird was now warm and safe, and Mama Bird was back home. Rusty had solved the problem all by himself by taking initiative and being compassionate.

As the sun set, painting the sky orange and pink, Rusty picked up his bouncy ball and smiled. He felt proud that he had the confidence to help someone in need.

"Taking the first step to help others feels even better than playing with my favorite ball!" Rusty said happily.

The End.
