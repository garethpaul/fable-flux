# Sunny the Squirrel’s Cozy-Bedroom Art Show

Sunny the Squirrel lived in a cozy bedroom at the top of a little house. The bedroom smelled like clean blankets and pine cones. A soft lamp made a warm, golden glow. Sunny’s tail was fluffy, and her eyes were bright. She loved to make things with her tiny paws.

One morning, Sunny woke up to birds singing outside the window. “Chirp, chirp!” they sang. Sunny stretched. “Today, I feel like making art,” she said. Her whiskers twitched with excitement.

On her small desk sat a cup of safe, washable paints, a jar of clean water, and paper made from old, recycled scraps. Sunny had collected the scraps neatly. She had followed the directions on the bin: “Paper only.” She was proud. “Using things again is good for the Earth,” she said softly, patting the recycle symbol on the side.

Sunny’s friend, a little bluebird named Pip, landed on the windowsill. “Good morning, Sunny!” Pip chirped. “What are you making today?”

“I want to paint the forest,” Sunny said. “I want to show how it sounds and smells! But I’m not sure I can do it.”

Pip tilted his head. “You are resourceful. You find smart ways to do things. You can try.”

Sunny took a deep breath. She remembered Mama’s safety rules that were taped right above the desk:
- Ask for help if you need it.
- Use a mat.
- Wipe spills right away.
- Put lids on when you’re done.

Sunny said, “I will follow the directions.” She laid down a big, washable mat. She put on a little apron with acorn buttons. She set a soft cloth next to the water jar.

First, Sunny listened. The bedroom was quiet and cozy. She could still hear the birds. “Chirp, chirp.” She painted tiny music notes. Then she sniffed. The air smelled like pine and warm blankets. She dabbed green for pine and yellow for warm light. “Splash, splash,” went her brush in the water. The sound made her smile.

Pip cheered. “I can almost hear the forest in your picture!”

Sunny tried something new. She used a dry pine needle she had found on the floor. “I will reuse this safely,” she said, wiping it clean first. She dipped the needle tip into brown paint and drew tiny tree bark lines. Scratch, scratch. It looked real!

But then, oh no! The water jar wobbled. “Careful,” said Pip. The jar tipped. “Plop!” Water splashed on the paper. Sunny’s heart thumped. “Oh dear!” she said. She felt scared. “My art!”

Sunny remembered the rule: Wipe spills right away. She pressed the cloth on the water. Pat, pat. The spill stopped. Sunny took a brave breath. “I can fix this,” she whispered. “I am resourceful.”

She looked at the splash. It made a soft, round shape. Sunny smiled. “This can be a pond!” She added blue paint. She touched the pond with a corner of a paper towel to make little ripples. The pond looked shiny, like the real one in the park.

Pip clapped his wings. “You turned a spill into a pond! That is creative!”

Sunny felt warm and proud. Her tail swished happily. “Thank you, Pip.”

Next, Sunny thought about caring for the Earth. She did not want to waste paint. She mixed small drops to make new colors. Swirl, swirl. She made leafy green, cozy brown, and sunset orange. She used her recycled paper scraps to test colors. When she was done with a color, she put the lid back on the paint. Click. “That saves it for later,” she said.

Sunny added smells and sounds with colors and shapes. Curvy lines for wind. Soft dots for pine. Yellow swishes for sunlight on blankets. She even made a tiny trail of acorn prints using her pinky toe. “Tiny, tiny,” she giggled.

At last, she leaned back. “My art is done.” The painting looked full of feelings, like a hug. The cozy bedroom felt even cozier with the warm lamp shining on the picture.

Pip peered closer. “It’s beautiful,” he said. “I can hear the chirps and feel the warm light.”

Sunny nodded. “I followed directions. I cared for the Earth. And I used my own ideas. That took courage.”

There was one more thing to do. Sunny cleaned up. She washed her brush in fresh water. Swish, swish. She dried it on the cloth. She stacked leftover scraps in the recycle bin. She wiped the mat. She closed the paints. Click, click. The room smelled clean again.

Then Sunny hung her painting with a safe, sticky tab above her bed. She made a small sign from another scrap: “Sunny’s Cozy-Bedroom Art Show.”

Mama Squirrel peeked in. “Oh, Sunny,” Mama said softly. “I can see your brave heart in your art. I can see the forest, the light, and your joy.”

Sunny’s chest felt big and bright. “I did it,” she said. “When I make art, I show my ideas. I can take care of the Earth, follow directions, and be myself.”

Pip sang a happy note. “Tweet!”

Sunny smiled at her painting. The golden lamp glowed. The blankets felt soft. The room sounded like quiet and song together.

Lesson learned: When you follow directions, care for the Earth, and use your own ideas, you can make something special. Your creative heart is strong, and you can turn little mistakes into new magic.

The End.
