# Bubbles Cleans the Music Room

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with bright, curious eyes. He loved to click and squeak little songs. Today, Bubbles swam into the musical practice room at the sea school. The room was full of sounds. Tap-tap on drums. Ting-ting on little bells. Whoosh from soft sea fans that moved the water. It smelled like clean sea salt and shiny shells.

“Hello, music!” Bubbles clicked. He wiggled his tail and made tiny bubbles that danced up, up, up.

Ms. Coral, the music teacher, waved a fin. “Good morning, Bubbles! Today we practice a song together. We need good listening and good coordination. That means our bodies and our sounds work together.”

Bubbles nodded. “I can do that!” he said.

Bubbles swam to a row of shell drums. But he stopped. Something was wrong. A crinkly plastic bag floated near the drums. A bent paper cup wobbled by the bells. A long string of ribbon twirled like a skinny eel. There were bits and pieces that did not belong.

Bubbles felt a pinch in his tummy. “Oh no,” he whispered. “The room is messy. The music will be messy too.”

At that moment, a tiny crab named Pip peeked out from behind a cymbal shell. “I tripped on that ribbon,” Pip said in a squeaky voice. “It made me scared.”

Bubbles looked around. He heard the soft scrape of cups against the floor. He saw the bag flap-flap in the water. He felt the ribbon tickle his fin. The mess made the room feel too busy, too noisy.

Ms. Coral glided over. “We can make music after we make our room safe,” she said. “Who can help?”

Bubbles raised his fin high. “I can help! We can recycle. We can sort and make new uses.”

Pip clacked his little claws. “I can help too!”

Bubbles took a big, calm breath. “First, we look. Then we sort,” he said. “Let’s work together.” He made a gentle beat with his tail: swish, swish. Pip tapped his claws in time: tap, tap.

“Listening. Moving. Working together,” Ms. Coral hummed.

Bubbles found three big baskets. He read the pictures on them. “This one has a bottle picture. This one has a paper picture. This one has a food picture,” he said. He used simple words for Pip, and for himself too. “Plastic here. Paper here. Food waste there.”

The bag went swish in the water. Bubbles reached slowly. “I’ll grab it carefully,” he said. He wrapped his smooth snout around the bag and dropped it into the plastic basket. “Plop!”

“Good job!” cheered Pip.

The bent cup floated by. Pip scuttled under it and popped it up with his shell. “I found paper!” Pip giggled, and the cup went into the paper basket. “Plink!”

Bubbles spotted a shiny bottle cap. It twirled like a coin. “Plastic,” he said, and in it went. “Clink!”

They kept moving in a steady rhythm. Swish, tap. Swish, tap. Their bodies and sounds worked together. Bubbles felt proud. He saw the room become clear. He heard the music of cleaning—a happy little beat.

“Ribbon?” Pip asked, holding the long blue string.

Bubbles thought. He looked at Ms. Coral. “Can we reuse this?” he asked.

Ms. Coral smiled. “Yes! Reusing is part of recycling too. What could it be?”

Bubbles brightened. “A drum stick ribbon! It can help us keep the beat together.”

He tied the ribbon gently around two small mallets. He hummed. Pip hummed too. The ribbon made the mallets stay together, like good friends. “We saved it,” Bubbles said. “We helped the room. We helped our music.”

Soon the baskets were full. The room was clear and calm. The shell drums shone. The bells sparkled. The water felt easy and light.

Ms. Coral clapped her fins. “Wonderful work! You were helpful. You recycled. You moved in a careful rhythm. Now, let’s play our song.”

Bubbles and Pip took their places. Bubbles held the ribboned mallets. Pip sat by a tiny triangle shell. Ms. Coral counted, “One, two, ready, play!”

Bubbles tapped the shell drum: tum-tum, tum-tum. Pip tinged the triangle: ting, ting. They listened. They watched. They kept the beat together. Their bodies felt steady. Their hearts felt happy. The room filled with sweet, clear music.

When the song ended, everyone smiled.

“I learned something,” Bubbles said softly. “When we recycle, we care for our room. We care for our friends. We make better music too.”

Pip nodded. “I feel safe now. I like helping.”

Ms. Coral added, “Recycling and reusing keep our space clean. Helping hands—and fins and claws—make big jobs small. And good coordination helps us move and play as one.”

Bubbles grinned and made a ring of bubbles that floated up like tiny moons. “Tomorrow,” he said, “I will bring a clean jar for tiny bells. We can reuse it!”

“That is a great idea,” said Ms. Coral.

Bubbles felt warm and proud. He listened to the quiet, neat room. He smelled the fresh sea salt. He touched the smooth drum. He knew this: small helpers can make a big difference, one sorted piece at a time.

The End.
