# Otter’s Backyard Dance Parade

In a sunny backyard, a playful otter named Ollie splashed in a blue kiddie pool. The water felt cool and tickly on his paws. The grass smelled sweet, and a soft breeze rustled the leaves of a lemon tree. Birds chirped a peep-peep song.

“I love to dance!” Ollie giggled. He wiggled his whiskers, swished his tail, and tapped his paws. Tap-tap, swish-swish! He spun in a circle and laughed. “Let’s have a backyard dance parade!”

Ollie clapped his paws and looked around the yard. A row of potted flowers lined the fence. A red wagon sat under the shade. A picnic blanket was spread out for snacks. Ollie had an idea. “I will invite my friends,” he said. “We will dance together!”

First, Bibi the bluebird fluttered down. “Hello, Ollie!” she sang. “What’s that sound?”

“It’s my dance beat! Tap-tap, swish-swish! Do you want to dance with me?” Ollie asked.

“Yes!” chirped Bibi. She hopped on the fence and flapped her wings. Flap-flap, hop-hop! “This is my bird dance,” she said.

Next, Tilly the turtle peeked from under a bush. Her shell was shiny like a smooth stone. “What’s going on?” Tilly asked in a slow, warm voice.

“A dance parade!” Ollie smiled. “Do you want to dance?”

“I move slow,” Tilly said. “But I can try.” She took a deep breath. Slide-step, pause. Slide-step, pause. “This is my turtle dance.”

Then, Max the squirrel zipped across the grass. “I heard music!” he chattered. He bounced to the wagon and twitched his tail. “I have a fast dance!” Bounce-bounce, zip-zoom!

Ollie’s eyes shone. “We all dance different,” he said. “That is fun!”

But when they tried to dance together, it felt messy. Ollie tapped and spun. Bibi hopped and flapped. Tilly slid and paused. Max zipped and bounced. Their feet bumped. Their tails tangled. The wagon squeaked. “Oh no!” Ollie cried, almost tipping into the pool. “This is not a parade. This is a puddle of wiggles!”

Bibi fluttered up with a worried peep. “How can we dance together?”

Max chattered, “We need to go fast!”

Tilly shook her head gently. “I need to go slow,” she said.

Ollie’s whiskers drooped. He felt a little twist in his tummy. The backyard was still sunny. The lemons still smelled bright. But the dance felt tangled. “I want it to work,” he whispered.

Ollie sat on the picnic blanket. He closed his eyes and took a big, slow breath. In, out. The breeze kissed his nose. He listened. Tap-tap from his paws. Flap-flap from Bibi. Slide-step from Tilly. Zip-zoom from Max. Each sound was special. Each friend was special.

“I have a plan,” Ollie said, sitting up tall. “We can take turns. We can be patient. We can make a dance map!”

“A dance map?” asked Bibi.

“Yes,” said Ollie. He rolled out a long strip of paper from the craft box by the porch. He drew big shapes with a purple crayon. “Circle for Ollie,” he said, drawing a round shape. “Feather for Bibi.” He drew a little feather. “Shell for Tilly.” He drew a smooth shell. “Nut for Max.” He drew a tiny nut.

“We will line up our shapes,” Ollie explained. “We will dance in order. First my circle, then Bibi’s feather, then Tilly’s shell, then Max’s nut. We will count to four. One, two, three, four. Then we start again!”

Tilly smiled. “That is organized,” she said.

Max hopped in place. “I can wait for my turn,” he added, “but it’s hard!”

“We can practice,” Bibi chirped. “Practice makes patience.”

They stood in a row by the wagon. Ollie tapped his paws gently on the wood. Tap-tap. “Ready?” he asked.

“Ready!” his friends said.

Ollie began. “One, two, three, four!” On circle, Ollie wiggled and spun. His tail swished like water. On feather, Bibi hopped and flapped, soft as whisper wind. On shell, Tilly slide-stepped, smooth and steady. On nut, Max bounced and zipped, quick and bright.

They did it again. One, two, three, four. Circle, feather, shell, nut. The backyard filled with rhythm. The lemons shone. The pool rippled. The grass felt springy under their feet. Everyone smiled.

“Listen to the sounds,” Ollie called. “Tap-tap, flap-flap, slide-step, zip-zoom!”

They tried a new pattern. “Let’s do shell first!” Tilly said. So they changed the dance map. Shell, circle, feather, nut. They waited, they watched, they took turns. They discovered new moves. Bibi added a twirl. Max added a hop-stop. Ollie added a splashy clap. Tilly added a tiny spin that made her giggle.

Neighbors peeked over the fence and clapped. “What a dance parade!” someone cheered.

Ollie bowed. His whiskers wiggled with joy. “We did it together,” he said. “We were patient. We were organized. We danced in turns. And we celebrated our differences!”

Tilly nodded. “Slow can shine.”

Max grinned. “Fast can wait.”

Bibi sang, “Different dances make a beautiful song!”

Ollie looked at his friends. His heart felt warm like the sun on the grass. “Dance helps us listen, learn, and love our friends,” he said.

They ended with a big group pose by the lemon tree. Tap-tap, flap-flap, slide-step, zip-zoom—pause! Then everyone laughed and hugged.

The End.
