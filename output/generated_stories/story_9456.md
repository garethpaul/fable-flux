# Polly's Big Question

In a cozy home office filled with books and colorful papers, lived a bright green parrot named Polly. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds in the morning sunlight that streamed through the big window. Polly loved to watch her human friend, Sarah, work at her desk every day.

"Click, click, tap, tap," went Sarah's fingers on the keyboard. Polly tilted her head, listening to the rhythmic sounds. Her curious eyes watched the glowing screen with moving letters and pictures.

"What are you doing, Sarah?" chirped Polly, fluffing her colorful tail feathers.

"I'm working on the computer, Polly," Sarah said with a smile. "I'm writing stories for children."

Polly's bright black eyes grew wide with wonder. "But how do the letters get inside that flat box? Where do they come from?"

Sarah chuckled softly. "That's a great question, Polly! Would you like to learn?"

Polly bobbed her head up and down excitedly. "Yes, yes! I want to know everything!"

But as the days passed, Polly noticed something puzzling. Sometimes the computer screen went completely black, and Sarah would frown. "Oh no, not again," Sarah would sigh.

"What's wrong?" Polly asked, concerned for her friend.

"The computer isn't working properly. I can't figure out why it keeps turning off," Sarah explained, looking worried.

Polly's curiosity sparked like a little flame. She wanted to help! She flew closer to the desk and looked carefully at everything around the computer. She noticed the thick black cord that snaked from the computer to the wall.

"Sarah, what's that long, dark rope?" Polly asked, pointing with her bright orange beak.

"That's the power cord, Polly. It gives the computer electricity so it can work."

Polly followed the cord with her eyes. She hopped along the desk, then fluttered to the floor. The cord went behind a tall bookshelf, but something looked strange. Part of the cord was bent and twisted, and it wasn't pushed all the way into the wall socket.

"Sarah! Sarah! Come look!" Polly called excitedly, dancing on her little feet.

Sarah came over and knelt down beside Polly. "What did you find, my clever friend?"

"The rope isn't in the wall properly!" Polly announced proudly, pointing with her wing.

Sarah looked closely and gasped. "Polly, you're absolutely right! The plug is loose. That's why the computer keeps turning off!" She gently pushed the plug firmly into the socket until it clicked into place.

They returned to the desk, and Sarah pressed the computer's power button. The screen lit up brightly with cheerful colors, and the familiar humming sound returned.

"It works! It works!" Polly squawked happily, doing a little dance on her perch.

"Thank you, Polly! Your curiosity and careful looking solved our problem," Sarah said, gently stroking Polly's soft green feathers. "You asked good questions and used your eyes to find the answer."

Polly puffed out her chest with pride. "I like asking questions! They help me understand things better."

"That's right," Sarah agreed. "When we're curious and ask questions, we learn new things and can even help solve problems. Your wondering mind saved the day!"

From that day forward, Polly continued to ask questions about everything she saw. She learned about plants, books, music, and so much more. And whenever Sarah had a problem, Polly would look carefully and ask helpful questions.

"Curiosity is like having a superpower," Polly would tell herself happily each morning. "It helps me understand the world!"

The End.
