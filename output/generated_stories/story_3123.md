# The Brave Build of Cleo the Clever Cat

Cleo the clever cat lived in a sunny house with a bright, busy garage workshop. The workshop smelled like fresh wood and orange soap. It sounded like soft tapping and little whirs. Shelves held jars of shiny screws that jingled like tiny bells. A blue toolbox sat on a bench, and a yellow measuring tape curled like a sleepy snake.

One morning, Cleo twitched her whiskers and looked at a wobbly wagon by the workbench. The wagon belonged to Max, the small neighbor who loved to wave and laugh. But the wagon was broken. One wheel wiggled and squeaked, “Eee-ee!” Max looked sad.

“I wish my wagon could carry my teddy,” Max said, peeking into the garage. “But it tips, and I’m scared it will fall.”

Cleo’s ears perked up. “I can help,” she purred. “We can fix and build. We can be brave.”

Max shuffled his feet. “I feel a little scared.”

Cleo nodded. “Feeling scared is okay,” she said softly. “Bravery means we try, even when we feel a wiggle in our tummy.”

Cleo padded to the wagon. She sniffed the wooden side. It smelled dusty. She tapped the loose wheel. Tap-tap. “Let’s think,” she said. “First, we look. Then, we plan. Then, we build.”

Max’s eyes grew wide. “Look. Plan. Build,” he repeated.

Cleo pulled open the blue toolbox. Clink! Clank! Tools glimmered. “We will need a wrench, some screws, and a little oil,” Cleo said. “And we will need our brave hearts.”

Max took a tiny breath. “Okay,” he said. “I will try.”

They began the plan. Cleo gently spun the wobbly wheel. “It is loose,” she said. “We need to make it tight.”

“How?” asked Max.

Cleo smiled. “Step by step.” She held up one paw. “Step one: Hold the wagon steady.” She held up another paw. “Step two: Turn the nut with the wrench.” She flicked her tail. “Step three: Test the wheel.”

Max stood tall. “I can hold the wagon,” he said. He placed his small hands on the wooden side. His fingers felt the smooth wood and a little bump where paint had chipped.

Cleo picked up the wrench. “Lefty loosey, righty tighty,” she sang. Her paw turned the wrench. Skrrrk. Skrrrk. The nut moved. Cleo dropped in a shiny washer. “This will help,” she said. “It’s a smart, tiny helper.”

Max smiled. “A tiny helper! I like that.”

Cleo turned the wrench again. Righty tighty. Skrrrk. Skrrrk. The wheel felt stronger. “Now, we test,” Cleo said.

Max looked at the garage door. Sunlight made a bright path on the floor. “What if it tips?” he whispered.

Cleo touched her nose to Max’s hand. “We will test slowly,” she purred. “Being brave does not mean rushing. It means careful steps.”

They rolled the wagon a little. Squeak. Roll. Squeak. Roll. It did not tip, but the squeak was still loud.

Cleo tilted her head. “We fixed one problem. Now we find the next,” she said. “Where is the squeak?”

Max listened. He closed his eyes. He heard the soft hum of the fridge. He heard birds outside. He heard the squeak. “It comes from here,” he said, pointing at the axle.

Cleo beamed. “Good listening. That is smart thinking.” She took the little oil can. Drip. Drip. Drip. The oil smelled like lemons. She turned the wheel. The squeak went away. “Problem solved,” Cleo said.

Suddenly, a new sound came—Tap-tap-tap—at the open garage door. A small, shy mouse peeked in. “H-hi,” said the mouse. “My name is Millie. I am scared of loud noises, but I saw the wagon. It looks nice.”

Max blinked. “Hi, Millie,” he said. He held his teddy close. “Do you want to watch?”

Millie nodded slowly. “I can be brave too,” she whispered.

Cleo’s eyes were warm. “Everyone can be brave in their own way,” she said. “You can stand by the soft cloths. It is quiet there.”

Millie scurried to a little pile of folded rags. She watched with bright eyes.

Cleo and Max added a ribbon to the handle. They checked each screw. Tight. Tight. Tight. Cleo measured with the yellow tape. “All even,” she said. “Our plan worked.”

“Let’s try a big test,” Max said. His voice shook a little, but he stood tall.

“You can do it,” Millie squeaked.

Max placed Teddy in the wagon. Cleo held one side. Max pulled the handle. The wagon rolled across the bright path, out to the sunny driveway. The wheels hummed, smooth and quiet. No squeak. No tip.

Max laughed. “We did it! I was brave!”

Cleo purred loud, like a tiny engine. “Yes. You looked, you planned, you built, and you were brave.”

Millie clapped her tiny paws. “I was brave to watch,” she said.

Cleo nodded. “Bravery can be big or small. It is trying, even when you feel a little scared.”

Max hugged Teddy, then looked at Millie. “Do you want a turn?” he asked.

Millie smiled. “Yes, please.”

Max pulled the wagon gently. Millie rode, ears twitching with joy. The sun felt warm. The air smelled like lemons and wood. Everyone laughed.

Cleo stretched and felt proud. “We fixed the wagon,” she said. “We used our heads and our hearts.”

Max nodded. “I learned: Look, plan, build. Be brave. Ask for help. And it’s okay to be different and still be part of the team.”

Cleo winked. “That is a clever lesson.”

The End.
