# Panda Poppy Prepares

Poppy was a patient panda with soft black ears and a warm, gentle smile. One sunny morning, her tummy rumbled quietly. Rrrr-rrrumble. Poppy patted her belly and laughed.

“I would like a snack,” Poppy said. “I will make panda pancakes!”

Poppy walked to the pantry. The pantry was cool and cozy. It smelled like flour and a little like sweet vanilla. On the shelves were jars and boxes and baskets, all lined up. Some were high, some were low. The light made tiny dust sparkles dance in the air.

Poppy reached for the pancake mix. “I am ready!” she said.

But when she opened the box, little puffs of flour floated out. “Oh!” Poppy blinked. Inside, the mix was clumpy. The sugar jar was sticky. A banana had a brown, mushy spot. A tiny ant waved hello and marched away.

Poppy felt her tummy rumble again. Rrrr-rrrumble.

“I want pancakes now,” she whispered. Then she took a deep breath. In… and out. Poppy was a patient panda. She remembered something Mama had said.

“Preparation helps us do things well,” Mama Panda’s voice echoed in her heart. “We get ready first. Then the job is easier and safer.”

Poppy nodded. “I will prepare,” she said. “I will be responsible and take initiative.”

She tied on her little yellow apron. The ties felt smooth, and the bow sat snug on her back. She put on her tiny chef hat too. It felt soft and puffy.

“First, clean,” said Poppy. She turned on the sink. Swish, swish! The water was cool on her paws. She used soap. Bubble, bubble, bubble! The bubbles tickled her nose. “Wash, wash, wash,” she sang. “Clean paws help me cook.”

Poppy dried her paws with a fresh towel. Pat, pat, pat.

“Next, tidy,” she said. She looked around the pantry. “I can do this.”

Poppy wiped the sticky sugar jar with a wet cloth. Wipe, wipe, wipe. It felt smooth again. She threw the mushy banana in the compost bin. Plop. She checked dates on boxes. “Good. Good. Oops! This one is old,” she said, and put the old box in the trash.

She brushed a few crumbs into a pan. Tap, tap, tap. A little ant waved again. “Goodbye, tiny ant. Please stay outside the pantry,” Poppy said kindly. She closed the little crack where the ants came in with a safe cover Mama kept on the shelf. Click.

“Now, organize,” Poppy said. She put flour on the left, sugar in the middle, and salt on the right. She made a little label with a picture for each: a cloud for flour, a candy dot for sugar, and a tiny shaker for salt. “This will help me next time,” she said, smiling.

Poppy’s tummy made a softer sound now. Rumble… but calm.

She set out everything she needed before cooking. “Bowls? Check. Spoon? Check. Pan? Check. Eggs? Check. Milk? Check.” She placed each item on the counter. Clink, clink, clink.

Poppy paused. “Did I forget something?” She looked at her clean paws. She looked at the tidy shelf. She remembered one more step. “Hair away,” she said, tucking her fur gently under her hat. “Safe and clean.”

Just then, her friend, Little Mouse, peeked in. “Squeak! Are you making pancakes, Poppy?”

“Yes,” said Poppy. “But first, I prepared the pantry. I cleaned, I organized, and I set things out.”

Little Mouse sniffed the air. “It smells fresh,” she squeaked. “You took initiative!”

Poppy beamed. “Thank you.”

They began to cook. Poppy poured the mix into the bowl. Glug. She cracked an egg. Crack! She added milk. Swish! She stirred slowly. Circle, circle, circle. The batter looked smooth and creamy.

Poppy heated the pan. Ssssss! She waited patiently until the pan was ready. “I am patient,” she said. “I can wait.” When tiny bubbles popped on top of the pancake, she flipped it gently. Flop!

Little Mouse clapped. “You did it!”

Soon, there was a stack of warm, golden pancakes. Steam twirled up like little ribbons. Poppy added slices of a fresh banana. She poured a small drizzle of honey. Drip, drop.

They sat at the table. Poppy placed napkins and cups of water. “Prepared,” she said with a nod.

They took bites. Mmmm. The pancakes were fluffy and sweet. The banana was soft. The honey was light. Poppy’s heart felt cozy.

Mama Panda walked in and looked around. “Oh my,” she said. “The pantry is clean and tidy. The food is ready. You were very responsible, Poppy.”

Poppy’s ears wiggled. “I prepared first,” she said. “It made cooking easy and safe.”

Mama Panda hugged her. “Preparation is a kind of love,” Mama said. “It helps you, and it helps others.”

Poppy shared pancakes with Mama and Little Mouse. They laughed and chatted. “Next time,” said Poppy, “I will check the pantry before I am hungry. I will make a list. I will keep my tools in the same place.”

Little Mouse squeaked, “That is good planning!”

Poppy nodded. “Preparation, responsibility, and initiative make me proud,” she said.

Her tummy was happy. Her pantry was happy. Her heart was happy too.

The lesson was clear: When we prepare, we make good things happen.

The End.
