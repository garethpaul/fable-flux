# Leo Learns to Wait His Turn

In a sunny backyard with warm golden light, soft grass, and the sweet smell of oranges, a brave little lion cub named Leo wiggled his paws. He was excited. Today was Game Day.

On the fence hung a bright banner that said, “Play and Share!” Children and families from many places came to play. There were balls, a jump rope, tiny cones, and chalk lines on the ground. Laughter bubbled like music. The breeze felt gentle on Leo’s whiskers.

“I want to kick the ball now!” Leo said, his tail twitching.

“Hello!” said Asha, a girl with a red ribbon in her dark hair. “I play soccer with my family. We take turns. Want to try with me?”

“Me too!” said Mateo, who wore a blue cap. “I bring a small ball from my abuelita.”

“And me,” said Lin, holding a jump rope. “I like to count and cheer.”

Leo’s eyes shone. “Let’s play!”

Asha placed the ball on the grass. “First, we line up. We wait. We take turns to kick to the cone.”

Leo’s ears drooped. “Wait? But I’m ready now!”

Asha smiled kindly. “I know. Waiting is hard. But patience helps everyone have fun.”

Lin spun the rope and sang softly, “Slow and steady, we will go. Wait your turn, and watch the show.”

Leo bounced on his paws. He sniffed the sunny air. He heard birds chirp and felt the smooth ball with his paw. The line moved slowly. Mateo kicked. Asha kicked. Lin kicked. Leo felt his tummy wiggle like jelly.

“I can’t wait,” he whispered. “I want my turn right now.”

Asha knelt beside him. “Leo, can you try a patience trick?”

“A trick?” Leo blinked.

“Yes,” Asha said. “Breathe in like you smell a flower. Breathe out like you blow a bubble.”

Leo sniffed the orange tree. Sweet! He blew out gently, like sending a bubble to the sky. In and out. In and out. His shoulders softened.

Mateo nodded. “My abuelita says, ‘Despacio, despacio,’ which means ‘slowly, slowly.’ It helps me wait.”

Lin added, “My nai nai taps her knee and counts one-two-three.”

Leo tapped his paw. “One… two… three.” He felt calmer.

Asha pointed to the players. “While we wait, we cheer. Watch how they kick. We can learn.”

Leo watched. Mateo swung his foot. Thump! Asha stepped back, then kicked. Whoosh! Lin looked at the cone and aimed. Tap!

Leo’s eyes grew wide. He saw different styles. He heard claps and cheers in many voices. “Good kick!” “Bien!” “Hao!”

He smiled. “I can cheer too.” He called, “Great job, Lin!”

Lin grinned. “Thanks, Leo!”

At last, it was almost Leo’s turn. But a tiny toddler waddled up, holding a little ball. She looked nervous. Her dad said softly, “First time.”

Leo looked at the line. He looked at the toddler’s small hands. He felt a warm feeling in his chest.

He took a breath. Flower… bubble. Then he said, “You can go before me.”

The toddler’s face lit up. She rolled the ball with a giggle. Everyone clapped. “Yay!”

Asha whispered, “That was very kind, Leo. You are very patient.”

Finally, it was Leo’s turn. The grass felt cool. The cone waited. The sun made the ball shine like a tiny moon. Leo remembered what he learned.

“Step back. Aim. Slow and steady,” he said.

He breathed in and out. He counted, “One… two… three.” He swung his paw. Thump! The ball rolled straight, straight, straight—bonk!—right into the cone.

Cheers burst like popcorn. “Great kick!” “Buen trabajo!” “Tai bang le!”

Leo’s heart jumped with joy. His whiskers tickled. “I did it!”

Asha high-fived him. “Your patience helped you play your best.”

Mateo grinned. “And you shared your turn. That is a big win!”

Lin turned the jump rope. “Break time! Want to try jump rope together?”

They made a big circle. Families taught little songs from their homes. Asha showed a soccer trick from her auntie. Mateo taught a clap rhythm from his abuelita. Lin counted in Mandarin, “Yi, er, san!” Leo shared his lion cub stretch—paws wide, big breath, brave smile.

The sun felt cozy on their backs. The orange tree swished. The backyard hummed with happy voices.

Leo looked around at his friends. Different words, different games, one big team. He felt proud and calm.

He said, “Today I learned a patience trick: smell the flower, blow the bubble, count one-two-three, and cheer for others. Waiting lets everyone play. When I wait, I learn. When I wait, I have fun.”

Asha nodded. “Patience makes space for friends.”

Mateo added, “Patience feels like a strong heart.”

Lin said, “Patience helps us play fair.”

Leo’s tail swished like a golden ribbon. “I’m a brave little lion cub, and I can be patient.”

The sun slowly dipped lower, painting the sky peach and pink. They packed the balls and ropes, waved goodbye in many voices, and promised to play again.

Leo took one last deep breath. Flower. Bubble. He smiled.

The End.
