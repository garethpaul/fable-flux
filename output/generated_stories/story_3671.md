# Leo Learns to Share

The family car hummed softly as it rolled down the road. The seats were warm from the sun, and the air smelled like oranges from Mama’s snack bag. A brave little lion cub named Leo buckled into his seat. His soft paws held his favorite blue car book. The pages felt smooth. The pictures were bright and shiny.

“I want the window,” Leo said, pressing his nose on the cool glass. “I want to see the trucks!”

His little sister, Lila, a tiny lion cub with big curious eyes, sat beside him. “I want to see too,” Lila whispered. Her tail twitched with hope.

“No,” Leo grumbled. His paws tightened on the book. “It’s my window. MY book. MY turn.”

Papa Lion’s paws held the steering wheel. He looked in the mirror. “Deep breaths, cubs,” he said in a calm voice. “We can all enjoy the ride.”

Mama Lion passed a juicy orange slice to each cub. “Sweet and sunny,” she said. Leo licked the juice. It tasted like a sunny day. He felt a little better.

But Lila reached for the blue book. “Can I see the picture with the big red bus?” she asked.

Leo’s fur bristled. His face grew hot. His tummy felt tight. He hugged the book close. “No! Mine!” he roared, but it was a small, cubby roar.

Mama Lion took a slow breath. “Leo,” she said softly, “use your words. Tell us how you feel.”

Leo scrunched his nose. His cheeks puffed. “I feel mad,” he said. “I waited for the car. I want the book. I want the window. I want everything!”

The car hummed. The tires made a shh-shh sound. Papa Lion nodded. “Thank you for telling us,” he said. “When big feelings come, we can do three calm steps. Ready?”

Leo blinked. “Three steps?”

Papa smiled. “One: Paw on tummy. Two: Breathe in like you smell a flower. Three: Breathe out like you blow out a candle.”

Leo placed his paw on his tummy. In he breathed—sniff, sniff—like smelling a flower. Out he blew—whooo—like a candle. His tummy softened. His face cooled. The car felt cozy again.

Lila watched and copied. Paw on tummy. Sniff a flower. Blow a candle. Her little whiskers wiggled.

Mama Lion smiled. “Good job, cubs.”

Leo looked at Lila. She looked back, hopeful. He remembered when Lila shared her blanket with him when he felt cold last night. The blanket was soft and warm. It made him happy. He looked at his blue book. He loved it. But he loved Lila too.

“Sharing is caring,” he whispered to himself. “Sharing helps us both.”

He took another flower breath. He held the book out. “Lila, we can share,” he said. “I will look first to the red bus page. Then it’s your turn.”

Lila’s eyes sparkled. “Thank you, Leo!” she squeaked. She clapped her tiny paws.

Leo turned the pages. Swish, swish. The pictures smelled like new paper. “Here is the big red bus,” he said. “Vroom! Beep! Beep!” He made silly bus sounds. Lila giggled. Her tail flicked happily.

“Your turn,” Leo said. He passed the book gently to Lila. His paws felt light. His chest felt proud. Sharing didn’t make the book smaller. It made the fun bigger.

Lila traced the bus with her paw. “Vroom! Vroom!” she said. Then she peeked at the window. “Do you want the window now, Leo?” she asked. “We can share that too.”

Leo nodded. “Let’s count trucks together,” he said.

They took turns leaning toward the cool glass. “One big truck,” Leo said.

“Two green trucks,” Lila added.

“Three tiny trucks,” they said together, giggling.

The car smelled like oranges and crayons. The hum of the road was like a soft song. The clouds outside looked like fluffy sheep. Inside, the cubs felt calm and kind.

Papa Lion gave a happy rumble. “I see two great sharers,” he said.

Mama Lion passed a small toy car back. “You can share this too,” she said. “Maybe the car can visit the red bus page.”

Leo and Lila took turns rolling the toy car over the book. Click-rrr, click-rrr. They made gentle rules. “My turn, then your turn.” “Soft paws.” “Kind words.”

When the car stopped at a red light, Leo looked at Lila. “I felt mad before,” he said. “My tummy was tight. But I did our three steps. Now I feel calm.”

Lila nodded. “I felt sad before. I wanted a turn. But you shared. Now I feel happy.”

Leo smiled a brave, bright smile. “Sharing is brave,” he said. “It means I can be strong and kind.”

Papa Lion drove on. The green light glowed. The car rolled forward. The cubs hummed together, “Share, breathe, care,” like a little song. Their voices were soft and sweet.

At last, they reached the park. The sun shined. The air smelled like grass. Leo hopped out and held the book for Lila to climb down. “After the park,” Leo said, “we can read again. We can take turns.”

Lila grinned. “Yes! Sharing makes more fun.”

Mama Lion patted both cubs. “Today you learned a big lesson,” she said. “When we feel angry, we can breathe. When we can, we share. Sharing shows love and helps everyone.”

Leo puffed his chest. He felt brave, calm, and kind. He took Lila’s paw, and together they ran toward the swings, ready to share the sky.

The End.
