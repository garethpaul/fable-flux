# Rocky Raccoon’s Kitchen Concert

Rocky was a musical raccoon who loved to sing, tap, and play. He lived near a warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon toast and honey tea. The floor felt cozy under his paws. The window let in golden sunlight, and the kettle hummed a soft, happy tune.

One morning, Rocky said, “Today I will have a kitchen concert!” He picked up a wooden spoon and tapped a pot. Ting, ting! He shook a little jar of rice. Shaka-shaka! He blew across a bottle. Whooo!

But when he looked around, Rocky blinked. Bowls were on the chair. Spoons were under the table. Flour dust made white footprints on the floor. The music stopped. His whiskers drooped.

“Oh no,” Rocky whispered. “I can’t find my favorite bell. I can’t find my drum. Where did my things go?”

The kettle answered with a low shhh. The warm air smelled sweet and safe, but Rocky’s tummy felt twisty. He looked under a napkin. He looked behind a loaf of bread. He looked in a big bowl.

Clonk!

“Ouch,” said Rocky, rubbing his ear. “This is hard. I feel mixed-up.”

Just then, a tiny mouse named Mina peeked in. She wore a soft blue ribbon. “Hi, Rocky,” she squeaked. “I heard ting-ting. Is it concert time?”

“I want it to be,” said Rocky, his voice small. “But the kitchen is messy. I can’t find anything. I feel sad.”

Mina’s eyes were kind. “I’m sorry you feel sad,” she said. “That does feel hard.” She sniffed the air. “Cinnamon smells nice. Let’s take a slow breath together.”

Rocky nodded. They breathed in warm cinnamon and breathed out worry. In and out. The twisty feeling in Rocky’s tummy got smaller.

Mina said, “May I help? Maybe we can get ready together.”

Rocky’s ears perked up. “Okay. Thank you.”

Mina tapped her whiskers. “Let’s make a plan. We can put like things together. Spoons with spoons. Bowls with bowls. Music with music.”

Rocky tilted his head. “Like a song?”

“Yes,” Mina smiled. “A tidy song! First verse: ‘Sort and sing!’”

Rocky grinned. “Sort and sing!”

They sang softly as they worked.

“Sort the spoons, clink-clink-clink,” sang Rocky.

“Stack the bowls, wink-wink-wink,” sang Mina.

They made homes for each thing. Spoons in a tall jar. Bowls on a lower shelf. Pots in a neat line. Rocky made a music basket too. He put in his wooden spoon, his rice shaker, and his bottle flute.

The warm kitchen felt brighter. The floor looked clean. Rocky found his missing bell under a towel. Ding!

“My bell!” Rocky cheered. “Thank you, Mina!”

Mina smiled. “You did great, Rocky. You were brave to ask for help.”

Rocky’s whiskers wiggled. “I felt upset. I thought, ‘I can’t do it.’ But you listened to me. That helped my heart.”

Mina nodded. “When things are messy, it can make our minds feel messy. When we organize, our hearts can rest.”

Rocky thought about that. He hummed a gentle note. “I like that. Organizing is care for my heart.”

Mina pointed to a small basket by the door. “What if we make a ‘put-away basket’ for later? If we find a lost thing, we put it there. Then, after music, we put it in its home.”

Rocky’s eyes shone. “That is open-minded and smart!”

They added a tiny sign: “Put-Away Basket.” Rocky drew a smiley face on it.

Now it was time. The kitchen glowed with soft light. The kettle sang, the bread smelled warm, and the air felt calm. Rocky stood tall.

“Welcome to the Kitchen Concert!” he said. “Today we play the Tidy Song.”

Mina clapped her tiny paws. Rocky tapped his spoon. Ting-ting. He shook his rice shaker. Shaka-shaka. He rang his bell. Ding-ding! The sounds were bright and clear. With everything in its place, Rocky could find each instrument fast. The music was smooth and happy.

Between songs, Rocky said, “I learned a big thing today. When we put things where they belong, we can find them. It helps our minds and our music. It helps our hearts too.”

Mina added, “And when a friend feels mixed-up, we can listen, breathe together, and offer help.”

Rocky looked around the warm kitchen. It felt safe, kind, and neat. He felt proud. He felt calm.

After the concert, they used the put-away basket. Rocky took a stray napkin to the drawer. Mina rolled a lemon back to its bowl. It was quick and easy, like a short, sweet song.

Rocky smiled at Mina. “Thank you for your compassion.”

Mina smiled back. “Thank you for being open to a new plan.”

Rocky hugged his music basket. “I will keep my things organized every day. Sort, sing, and smile!”

They laughed. The kettle went shhh, like a happy sigh.

And the kitchen stayed warm, tidy, and full of music.

The End.
