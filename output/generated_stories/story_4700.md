# Jumping Joey’s Study Corner Journey

In a cozy study corner, a joyful kangaroo joey named Joey wiggled his soft ears. The room smelled like fresh paper and warm crayons. Sunlight made bright squares on the carpet. Joey’s little desk had a cup of colored pencils, a globe that spun with a soft whirr, and a big water bottle that went glug-glug when he took sips.

“Good morning, Study Corner!” Joey chirped. His tail thumped gently on the rug. “Today, I want to learn about the world.”

Mama Roo peeked in with a smile. “Hello, my bounce star,” she said. “Start with a stretch and a sip.”

Joey stood tall, stretched his arms up, and wiggled his paws. “Stretch, stretch,” he sang. Then he took a big sip—glug-glug! The cool water felt good. “I’m ready!”

He tapped the globe. It spun, hum-hum-hum. The colors looked like a rainbow puzzle. Joey stopped it with his paw. “Where are friends?” he wondered.

His tablet pinged with a soft ding! Joey’s study corner had a screen for a special circle time. Friends from all around the world were there. Their faces popped up in little squares.

“Hi, Joey!” said Amara, waving. She wore bright beads in her hair.

“Hello!” said Kenji, holding a tiny origami crane.

“G’day!” chirped Mia, hugging a plush koala.

“Salam!” called Samir, with a big smile.

Joey’s heart felt warm. “We are far, but we are together,” he said.

Amara giggled. “We can share and care, even from far away.”

Kenji nodded. “We can learn how to be good helpers.”

Samir added, “We can be kind to people and the planet.”

Joey’s nose twitched. He smelled bananas from his snack bowl. “First,” he said, “healthy snack!” He washed his paws with warm, soapy water at the little sink in the corner. Swish, swish. The bubbles felt slippery. “Clean paws help keep us well,” he said.

Mia clapped. “Healthy habits help us help others too!”

They all munched their snacks. Crunch, munch, yum. Joey took a sip of water. Glug-glug. He felt strong and ready to learn.

Mama Roo set down a small basket. Inside were stamps with friendly pictures: a heart, a leaf, a hand, and a smile. “You can stamp when you do a global citizen action,” she said.

Joey’s eyes sparkled. “Global citizen means we care for everyone,” he said slowly. “Even if we live in different places.”

“And we learn from our differences,” said Amara. “Different is special.”

“We share what we have,” said Kenji.

“We listen to each other,” said Samir.

“We protect the Earth,” added Mia.

Joey opened his notebook. The paper felt smooth. “Let’s make a kindness plan,” he said.

He drew pictures with big, bold lines. A heart for love. A hand passing a book. A leaf on a clean tree. He held up the page. “I will share my books,” he said. “I will save water. I will listen and learn.”

His friends cheered. “Yes!” “Great idea!” “Hooray!”

Joey looked at the globe again. “Today, we can read stories from different places,” he said. “We can say hello in many ways.”

They tried together. “Hello!” “Konnichiwa!” “Jambo!” “Hola!” “Bonjour!” The words felt bouncy on Joey’s tongue. He smiled wide. “All the hellos sound like happy songs,” he said.

Then the screen went quiet. The internet was slow. The picture froze. Joey’s belly felt twisty. “Oh no,” he whispered. “I can’t hear my friends.”

He took a deep breath, in and out, slow like the ocean. He remembered his healthy habits. “First, calm,” he said softly. He sipped water. Glug-glug.

Mama Roo pointed to a small list on the wall: Pause. Breathe. Try again. Ask for help.

Joey nodded. “I will try again.” He checked the plug with gentle paws. Click. He tapped the refresh button. Ting. He asked, “Mama, can you help me?”

Mama Roo adjusted the little box with blinking lights. “Let’s see.” The lights blinked green, green, green.

The screen came back. “We’re here!” said Mia.

Joey laughed. “Thank you for waiting. Good teammates wait and help.”

Kenji smiled. “You solved it step by step.”

Amara said, “That’s what global citizens do. We keep trying and help each other.”

Samir held up a drawing of a big blue Earth. “Look,” he said. “A home for all of us.”

Joey felt the warm glow in his chest. “Our big home,” he whispered.

They each shared one small promise:
- Amara: “I will share my crayons.”
- Kenji: “I will pick up litter at the park.”
- Mia: “I will say hello to new friends.”
- Samir: “I will turn off lights to save energy.”
- Joey: “I will read stories from many places and listen with my heart.”

Joey took his heart stamp and pressed it on his notebook. Thump. A bright red heart. He stamped the leaf. Thump. Then the hand. Thump. And the smile. Thump.

The study corner smelled like crayons and soap. The globe hummed softly. Joey’s tail made a happy thup-thup on the rug. “Today, we learned about the world,” he said. “We learned to care, share, and listen.”

Mama Roo hugged him. Her fur felt warm and safe. “You are a kind joey,” she said.

Joey looked at the screen and the spinning globe. “No matter where we live,” he said, “we can be friends. We can help our big home.”

He closed his notebook with a gentle pat. “I’m a global citizen,” Joey said, “and I’m ready to help—one small hop at a time.”

The End.
