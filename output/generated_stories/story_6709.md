# Fern's Big Feelings Workshop

Little Fern the fawn had soft brown spots and the biggest, brightest eyes in the whole forest. She loved to explore and discover new things every day. One sunny morning, while playing near the edge of the woods, she spotted something amazing - a cozy garage workshop with its big door wide open.

"Wow!" Fern whispered to herself, her ears twitching with excitement. "Look at all those shiny tools and colorful things!"

She tiptoed inside, her small hooves clicking softly on the concrete floor. The workshop smelled like wood shavings and paint. There were hammers hanging on hooks, paintbrushes in jars, and the most wonderful pile of wooden blocks she had ever seen.

"I want to build something RIGHT NOW!" Fern said, bouncing up and down. Her heart was beating fast with excitement, and she felt like she might burst with all her big feelings.

But when Fern tried to stack the blocks, they kept tumbling down. CRASH! The blocks scattered everywhere, making a loud noise that echoed in the garage.

"This is too hard!" Fern cried, stamping her little hooves. Her face felt hot, and her breathing became quick and shallow. "I want to build the tallest tower ever, but nothing is working!"

Just then, she heard a gentle voice. "Hello there, little one. I'm Mr. Oak, and this is my workshop. Are you feeling frustrated?"

Fern looked up to see a kind old man with twinkling eyes and paint-splattered overalls. She nodded, feeling tears in her eyes.

"Sometimes when we have big feelings, our bodies feel all jumbled up inside," Mr. Oak said softly. "Would you like to learn a special trick that helps me when I feel overwhelmed?"

Fern nodded again, curious despite her frustration.

"Let's try the 'Slow and Steady' breathing," Mr. Oak suggested. "Put one hoof on your chest and one on your tummy. Now, breathe in slowly like you're smelling a beautiful flower... and breathe out slowly like you're blowing on hot soup."

Fern tried it. In... and out... In... and out. She could feel her heart slowing down and her muscles relaxing.

"That feels better," she said with surprise. "My tummy isn't all tight anymore!"

"Wonderful!" Mr. Oak smiled. "Now, would you like to tell me what you were trying to build?"

"I wanted to make the most amazing tower," Fern explained, "but I got so excited that I couldn't think straight."

"Ah," Mr. Oak nodded wisely. "Big feelings can make it hard to focus. How about we work together? I'll help you start with just three blocks, and we can add more slowly."

Working side by side, Fern learned to pause and breathe whenever she felt her excitement getting too big. Mr. Oak showed her how to ask for help when she needed it, and how to celebrate each small success.

"Look!" Fern exclaimed as they placed the final block on their tower. "We did it together! And I stayed calm the whole time!"

"You certainly did," Mr. Oak said proudly. "You learned to notice your big feelings and use your breathing to help yourself feel better. That's called self-regulation, and it's a very grown-up skill."

As the sun began to set, Fern thanked Mr. Oak for teaching her such an important lesson. She skipped back to the forest, excited to practice her new breathing trick and to tell her family about her wonderful adventure.

From that day forward, whenever Fern felt big emotions bubbling up inside, she remembered to pause, breathe slowly, and ask for help when she needed it.

The End.
