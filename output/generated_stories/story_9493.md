# Melody's Honest Song

In a cozy nursery with soft yellow walls and fluffy white curtains, lived a little singing bird named Melody. Her feathers sparkled like golden sunshine, and her voice was as sweet as honey. Every morning, she perched on the windowsill and sang beautiful songs to wake up baby Emma.

"Tweet-tweet-trill! Good morning, sunshine!" Melody chirped happily, watching Emma stretch and giggle in her crib.

One Tuesday morning, Melody noticed something different. The bird feeder outside the window was completely empty! Usually, Emma's mommy filled it with yummy seeds every day. Melody's tummy rumbled loudly.

"Oh my," she whispered to herself. "I'm so hungry, but there are no seeds left."

Just then, Melody spotted something shiny on Emma's changing table. It was a small bag of crackers that had fallen from the diaper bag. The crackers smelled delicious, and Melody's hungry tummy growled even louder.

"No one would notice if I took just one tiny cracker," Melody thought, fluttering closer to the bag.

But as she reached out with her little orange beak, Melody stopped. She remembered what her mama bird had taught her: "Always be honest, little one. Taking things that don't belong to you is never right, even when you're hungry."

Melody pulled her beak back. "Those crackers belong to baby Emma," she said firmly to herself. "I need to find my own food the right way."

Instead of taking the crackers, Melody decided to be creative and self-reliant. She flew around the nursery, looking carefully with her bright black eyes. Behind the rocking chair, she discovered some dropped birdseed from when Emma's daddy had refilled the feeder last week.

"Perfect!" Melody chirped with joy, pecking at the scattered seeds. They tasted wonderful because she had found them honestly.

Just then, Emma's mommy walked into the nursery. "Oh my goodness!" she exclaimed, noticing the empty bird feeder outside. "Poor Melody must be so hungry. I completely forgot to fill the feeder yesterday!"

Mommy quickly filled the feeder with fresh, colorful seeds. Melody watched through the window, her heart feeling warm and happy.

"Thank you for being patient, sweet Melody," Mommy said, looking right at the little bird. "I know you could have taken Emma's crackers, but you didn't. That shows you have a good, honest heart."

Melody's chest puffed up with pride. She had made the right choice! Flying to the full feeder, she enjoyed the most delicious breakfast ever. The seeds tasted extra special because she had waited for them honestly.

After eating, Melody sang her most beautiful song yet. "Tweet-trill-tweet! Being honest feels so sweet!" Her melody filled the nursery with joy, making baby Emma clap her tiny hands and giggle.

From that day on, whenever Melody felt tempted to take something that wasn't hers, she remembered how good it felt to be honest. She learned that being truthful and patient always led to better things than taking shortcuts.

Emma's family made sure the bird feeder was always full, and Melody continued to wake them each morning with her honest, happy songs. The nursery remained a place of trust, love, and beautiful music.

The End.
