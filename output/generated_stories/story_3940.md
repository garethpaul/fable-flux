# Penny Panda's Rainbow Garden Art

Penny Panda loved her cozy balcony garden. Every morning, she would water her bright red tomatoes, yellow sunflowers, and green lettuce plants. The morning sun felt warm on her fuzzy black and white fur.

"Good morning, little plants!" Penny said softly. "You're growing so big and strong!"

But today, Penny noticed something different. Her neighbor's balcony looked sad and empty. The concrete was gray and plain.

"I wish I could make it more beautiful," Penny thought, scratching her round ear. "But how?"

Penny sat down on her small wooden stool and looked around her garden. She saw empty yogurt containers, old tin cans, and cardboard boxes from her recent shopping trip.

"Wait!" Penny clapped her paws together. "I can use these to make art!"

First, Penny carefully cleaned the yogurt containers with soapy water. The bubbles tickled her paws and smelled like fresh lemons.

"These will make perfect planters," she said, drying them with a soft towel.

Next, Penny found her washable paints - bright red, sunny yellow, ocean blue, and grass green. She dipped her paw in the red paint.

"This feels cool and squishy!" she giggled, making paw prints on the yogurt container.

Penny painted beautiful flowers on each container. She used yellow for the sun, blue for the sky, and green for the stems. The colors looked like a rainbow!

"Now for the tin cans," Penny said patiently. She painted them with stripes and dots, making each one special and unique.

The cardboard boxes became colorful signs. Penny painted big, simple letters that said "LOVE," "GROW," and "SHARE."

While the paint dried, Penny filled each container with rich, dark soil. The earth felt soft and smelled like rain.

"Perfect!" she said, planting small herb seeds in each painted pot. "Basil, mint, and parsley will grow here."

When everything was ready, Penny carefully carried her colorful garden art to her neighbor's balcony. She arranged the painted planters in a beautiful row and hung the cheerful signs on the railing.

Just then, her neighbor Mrs. Rabbit came outside.

"Oh my!" Mrs. Rabbit gasped, her pink nose twitching with surprise. "What beautiful art! Did you make this, Penny?"

"Yes!" Penny beamed proudly. "I used things that would have been thrown away. Now they're helping plants grow and making everything pretty!"

Mrs. Rabbit's eyes sparkled with joy. "This is wonderful! You turned trash into treasure and made art that helps the Earth too!"

"Would you like to help me take care of the plants?" Penny asked hopefully.

"I would love to!" Mrs. Rabbit replied. "We can watch them grow together!"

From that day on, Penny and Mrs. Rabbit tended the colorful garden art together. The herbs grew tall and green, filling the air with sweet smells. Other neighbors stopped by to admire the beautiful painted planters and asked Penny to teach them how to make their own.

Penny learned that art could be made from anything, even things that might be thrown away. She discovered that being patient and creative could bring joy to others and help take care of the Earth at the same time.

"Art is everywhere," Penny whispered to her plants as she watered them. "You just need to look with your heart."

The End.
