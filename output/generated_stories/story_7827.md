# Benny Beaver Helps in the Guest Room

Benny was a busy beaver with bright teeth and a warm, wiggly tail. Today, Mama Beaver said, “Benny, Grandma is visiting. Let’s tidy the guest room.”

Benny’s eyes shined. “I can help! I’m a busy beaver!” he chirped.

They walked to the guest room. The door creaked softly, like a tiny song. The room smelled like fresh soap and sunshine from the open window. The curtains swished. The bed was fluffy, the blankets wrinkly, and there were soft towels in a basket.

Mama said, “We have chores to do. Chores help our home. Chores show we care.”

Benny nodded. “I care! What can I do?”

Mama smiled. “Let’s move our bodies and get it done! First, we shake the sheets.”

Benny grabbed one corner. The sheet felt cool and smooth. “Ready, set, shake!” he said. Whoosh! The sheet flew up like a white cloud and floated down. Benny giggled. “It’s like a sky!”

“Good shaking,” said Mama. “Now we tuck the corners.” Benny’s small paws pushed, pat, pat, pat. He took a deep breath. The room smelled clean and cozy. He felt proud.

Mama pointed to the floor. “Let’s sweep.” The broom made a soft swish-swish. Benny’s tail swished too. He danced from one side to the other, stepping, sliding, and tiptoeing. “I’m a sweeping dancer!” he laughed.

“Careful steps help,” Mama said. “Chores can be fun and safe.”

Benny found a tiny button under the chair. He picked it up. “Mama, I found this!” he said.

Mama looked. “Thank you for telling me. That shows integrity. Integrity means doing the right thing, even when it’s small.”

Benny held the button. It felt smooth and round. He thought. “Doing right feels good,” he said, and set the button on the table.

Next, they fluffed the pillows. Poof! Poof! Benny’s paws went squish-squish. Feathers did not fly, but the pillows grew tall and bouncy. Benny jumped once, just a little. Boing! “Oops,” he said. “No jumping on the bed.”

Mama smiled. “You remembered the rules. That’s integrity too.”

Benny stood straight. “I can follow rules.”

“Let’s dust now,” said Mama. She gave Benny a soft cloth. It was warm from the sun. Benny wiped the lamp. He wiped the picture frame. He wiped the window sill. Wipe, wipe, wipe. The dust smelled like old wood and a little like pine.

He noticed the toy truck he had left on the floor last time. He stopped and frowned. “I forgot to put this away.”

Mama waited. “What will you do?”

“I will make it right,” Benny said. He picked up the truck. He put it in the toy basket. His heart felt light, like a bubble. “Integrity!” he said proudly.

“Integrity,” Mama echoed. “And chore complete!”

They carried the towels to the bathroom. Benny walked carefully, step-step, so the towels stayed folded. “I like moving my feet for a job,” he said. “It makes me strong.”

Back in the guest room, the sun made the bedspread look like a shiny river. Benny stroked it. Smooth! He straightened the edge until it lined up just right.

Mama held up a small plant. “Would you like to water the plant?”

“Yes!” The tiny watering can felt cool in Benny’s paws. He tipped it gently. Drip, drip. The soil smelled earthy and happy. “The plant is thirsty. I’m helping.”

“You’re helping a lot,” said Mama.

Just then, Benny saw a sock peeking from under the bed. He crouched low and wiggled his tail. “I see you, sock!” He stretched, reached, and scooted. Scritch-scratch went his little claws on the floor. He pulled the sock out. It was dusty. He could hide it. He could kick it back. He looked at Mama. Mama was folding pillowcases.

Benny took a breath. He remembered integrity. He stood up tall. “I found a sock,” he said. “I will put it in the laundry.”

Mama looked proud. “That’s the right choice. Thank you.”

Benny carried the sock to the basket. Plop! He smiled so wide his cheeks felt round and warm.

They looked around the room. The bed was neat and smooth. The floor was clean. The pillows looked like marshmallows. The air smelled fresh. The guest room felt calm, like a hug.

Mama hugged Benny. “You worked hard. You moved your body. You did your chores. You showed integrity.”

Benny hugged back. “Chores help our home. Moving makes me strong. Doing right feels good.”

Just then, there was a soft knock. Knock, knock. Grandma peeked in with twinkly eyes. “Oh my,” she said. “What a lovely room!”

Benny’s tail did a happy thump-thump. “Welcome, Grandma! We made it nice for you.”

Grandma kissed Benny’s head. “Thank you, Busy Beaver. I can rest here. You are kind and honest.”

Benny’s heart glowed like a little lamp. He whispered, “I’m proud of me.”

Mama said, “Our lesson today?”

Benny grinned. “Do your chores, move your body, and show integrity—do the right thing, even when it’s small.”

They all smiled. The guest room was ready, and so was Benny.

The End.
