# Spark the Robot Dog’s Clean-Up Mission

Spark was a helpful robot dog. He had bright blue eyes that glowed softly and tiny wheels under his paws that hummed, whirr, whirr. He lived in a cozy home office. The office smelled like paper and lemon cleaner. Sunlight slid across the desk, making little squares on the floor.

Every morning, Spark helped in the home office. He pressed buttons with his nose. He carried pencils in his mouth clamp. He wagged his shiny tail when he was proud. “Beep-boop! Helper mode,” Spark said in a happy beep.

Today, Spark had a big job. A video call was on the computer. The screen blinked. “Meeting in ten minutes,” the computer chimed.

Spark rolled to the desk and paused. Crumbs! Crumbs were on the keyboard. Sticky juice was on the mouse. The cup had a ring of dried milk. The air smelled a little sour.

“Uh-oh,” Spark said softly. “This is not clean. Germs like sticky places.” He remembered what he learned. “Clean hands keep us well. Clean tools help us work.”

Spark looked around. The napkins were gone. The wipes were on a high shelf. The soap was in the bathroom. The clock ticked. Tick-tock. Tick-tock. Spark’s wheels wiggled. He felt a little swirl inside his chest light.

“I want to help,” he whispered, “but I can’t reach the wipes. I can’t hold the cup and the mouse and the cloth. It’s too much.” His tail lights dimmed. He felt frustrated.

From the hallway, a small voice called, “Spark? Are you okay?” It was Mina, a kind child with curious eyes. She wore a blue shirt with stars on it.

Spark beeped. “Mina,” he said, “the office is messy. There are crumbs and sticky spots. The meeting is soon. I want to clean, but I feel stuck.”

Mina came in, and her nose wrinkled. “Phew! I smell sour milk,” she said. She touched Spark’s head gently. “We can fix this. Let’s try step by step.”

Spark took a small, slow breath. “Step by step,” he repeated. His tail lights blinked brighter.

Mina washed her hands in the bathroom. Splash, swish, rub-rub-rub. She sang, “Tops and palms, between the fingers, clean, clean, clean!” She dried her hands with a soft towel. “Clean hands first,” she said. “Then clean tools.”

She helped Spark reach the wipes and a soft cloth. “I will wipe the mouse and the keys,” Mina said. “You can carry the trash bag.” Spark nodded. He held the bag’s edge with his mouth clamp. It tasted like clean plastic. Crinkle, crinkle.

Wipe, wipe, wipe. The mouse became smooth. Tap, tap, tap. The keyboard lost its crumbs. Spark rolled under the desk and found a lost grape stem. “Into the bag!” he said. Plop.

Mina looked at the cup with the dried milk ring. “This needs washing,” she said. Spark’s wheels hummed. “I can help carry!” He steadied the cup on a little tray that popped from his side. They moved carefully. Roll, roll, roll. No spills.

At the sink, Mina turned the water on. Whoosh! Warm water steamed a little. “Soap time,” she said. The soap smelled like oranges. She scrubbed the cup and sang, “Round and round, bubbles all around.” Spark swayed to the tune. He felt calm now. The swirl inside quieted.

They dried the cup. Back in the office, everything smelled fresh and a bit like orange and lemon. The desk shone. The keyboard clicked happily. Click, click.

“Beep-boop! Clean and ready,” Spark announced. His chest light glowed bright. Mina smiled. “We did it together. When it feels like too much, take a breath, ask for help, and do one step at a time.”

The computer chimed again. “Meeting in one minute.”

“Water break,” Mina said. “Clean hands, clean tools, ready brain!” She gave Spark a gentle pat. “I’m proud of you for trying even when you felt frustrated.”

Spark wagged his tail. “I learned,” he said. “First, wash hands. Then clean the space. It helps us feel good and work well. Slow and steady is okay.”

During the meeting, Spark sat by the desk. The room was calm. He could hear birds outside and the soft purr of the fan. Mina whispered, “Thank you, Spark.” She typed on the clean keys. No sticky, no crumbs. Everything worked fast and smooth.

When the meeting was done, Spark and Mina had a little song time. They made up a rhyme:

“Wash, wash, wash,
Rinse, rinse, rinse,
Wipe the desk and clean the prints!
When we feel mad,
We breathe and pause,
Then we try again with gentle paws.”

Spark giggled with a cheerful beep. “Open mind, open heart,” he said. “I can learn new ways.”

Mina nodded. “Hygiene is caring. We care for our hands. We care for our tools. We care for each other.”

Spark’s blue eyes shone like little moons. He felt proud and peaceful. The home office looked bright and safe.

He whispered, “Helper mode complete.”

The clear lesson glowed in his mind: Keep hands clean. Keep spaces clean. Take a breath when it’s hard. Ask for help. Try step by step.

The End.
