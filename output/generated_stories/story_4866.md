# Rocco’s Fair Bedtime Band

Rocco was a musical raccoon with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved music more than anything. In his cozy bedroom, a small lamp glowed like a little moon. His bed was fluffy, his rug felt warm under his paws, and his shelves were full of friendly instruments—tiny drums, a shiny triangle, a wooden recorder, and his favorite, a red toy guitar.

“Strum-strum!” Rocco sang, brushing the guitar strings. The sound was twangy and happy. He wiggled his ringed tail. “I want to play a bedtime concert!”

Mama Raccoon peeked in. “It’s almost sleep time, Rocco,” she said softly. “Your little sister, Rina, is tired. She needs quiet to fall asleep.”

Rina yawned from her crib in the corner. “Night-night,” she murmured, rubbing her eyes. The room smelled like clean blankets and warm cocoa from snack time.

Rocco’s ears drooped. “But I practiced all day,” he whispered. “I want to play my new song.” His paws itched to strum. His heart thumped with music.

Mama knelt beside him. “I know you worked hard. That is very responsible,” she said. “But we also need to be fair. Fair means everyone gets what they need. Rina needs rest. You need to play. How can we be fair to both?”

Rocco frowned and thought. His whiskers twitched. “Maybe I can play fast?” he said. “Fast songs are short!”

Mama shook her head kindly. “Fast can still be loud.”

Rocco tried again. “Maybe I can play outside?”

Mama looked at the dark window. “It is late and cold. Not safe.”

Rocco’s tummy fluttered. He didn’t want to give up. He wanted to keep trying. He wanted to be fair and responsible. He took a deep breath. “I will find a quiet way,” he said. “I will persist. I will not stop until I find it.”

He tried tapping the tiny drum with one finger. Tap… tap… tap… It was softer, but Rina’s eyes blinked open. “Too loud,” she mumbled.

He tried the recorder. He blew very gently. Fooo… Fooo… The soft notes still made Rina squirm. “No whistle,” she said, covering her ears.

Rocco’s tail drooped again. “Hmm,” he hummed. He looked around his cozy bedroom. The lamp made a warm circle of light. Shadows danced on the walls. The curtains whispered when a breeze came. He listened to the quiet sounds: Mama’s slow breathing, Rina’s sleepy sigh, the clock’s gentle tick-tock.

“Music can be in anything,” Rocco said to himself. “Even in quiet.”

He saw the shiny triangle and the soft scarf hanging on his bedpost. “Maybe…” He wrapped the scarf around the triangle to make it softer. He held it gently and tapped with the little stick. Ting… ting… The sound was a tiny sparkle, like a star far away.

Rina smiled in her crib. “Star song,” she whispered.

Rocco smiled too. “Star song,” he whispered back. He tapped a steady, tiny beat. Ting… ting… ting… He felt proud. He was being careful. He was being responsible with his music.

He added a whisper voice, so light it was almost like the wind. “Good night, little lights,” he sang. “Shine so soft and bright.”

Mama nodded. “That is very fair, Rocco,” she said. “You found a way to share the space. Rina gets quiet. You get to play. You kept trying. That is persistence.”

Rocco’s eyes sparkled. He wanted to make it even more fair. “Rina,” he said softly, “do you want a turn?” He held out the little stick.

Rina shook her head, sleepy. “You play,” she said.

Rocco thought again. He wanted to give her something. He looked at his red toy guitar. He plucked one string with one claw, very, very gently. Plink. It sounded like a raindrop. He smiled. “One raindrop for you,” he said. Plink.

Rina giggled, then yawned. “Plink,” she whispered, and her eyes began to close.

Rocco made a quiet bedtime band all by himself. Ting… plink… ting… plink… The sounds were soft as feathers. The room felt warm and safe. The curtains swished. The clock tick-tocked like a sleepy drum. Mama hummed along, low and gentle.

Rina’s breathing slowed. Her tiny paws relaxed. She fell asleep with a smile.

Rocco put down the stick. He wrapped the scarf neatly back on the bedpost. He set the triangle on the shelf and placed the guitar in its little stand. He looked at his cozy bedroom and felt proud in his heart.

Mama gave him a soft hug. “You were fair to Rina,” she said. “You were responsible with your things and your sounds. You kept trying until you found a good way. That is persistence. I am proud of you.”

Rocco hugged Mama back. His tail swished happily. “I learned something,” he said. “Fair means we share care. I can have music, and Rina can have rest. I can find quiet ways.”

He turned off the lamp. The room became a gentle gray cave. He climbed into his fluffy bed and pulled up his soft blanket. The pillow felt cool and smooth. He closed his eyes and whispered, “Good night, little lights.”

In the quiet, he could still hear the music of the room—the tiny tick-tock, the faraway breeze, and the sweet sound of being fair.

The End.
