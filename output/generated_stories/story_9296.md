# The Fair Dance in the Garden Shed

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with a long, gentle neck and big, kind eyes. Gigi loved to think. She thought about clouds, she thought about colors, and she thought about what was fair and right. One sunny morning, Gigi peeked into the little garden shed at the edge of the yard.

The shed smelled like sweet soil and fresh wood. Sunbeams danced in dusty air. There were pots stacked in a neat row, a broom against the wall, and a shiny metal watering can by the door. Gigi’s ears twitched as she heard music—soft taps and claps—from inside.

“Tap, tap, clap! Tap, tap, clap!” went the sound.

Gigi stretched her long neck and looked in. Inside the garden shed, many small animals were getting ready for the Happy Day Dance. There was Pip the mouse, Tiko the turtle, Bibi the bluebird, and Lula the lamb. Everyone wanted a turn to dance. Everyone wanted to practice.

But—oh no!—the space was small. The floor was tiny. Some animals stood in a crowd, and some sat on a box. Pip squeaked, “I’m first! I’m fast!” He zipped to the middle.

Bibi fluffed her wings. “I am first. I can fly,” she chirped.

Lula stamped her hoof. “I have a ribbon,” she said. “I am first.”

Tiko blinked slowly. “I was here early,” he said. “Maybe I am first.”

Voices got louder. Feet and paws shuffled. The watering can clinked. The broom rattled. Gigi felt her heart thump. She cared about fairness. She cared about justice. Justice means everyone gets a fair chance. Justice means we take turns and we listen.

Gigi took a deep breath. She smelled mint leaves in a pot. She heard the wind whisper outside. She stepped inside and lowered her head to fit.

“Friends,” Gigi said in a warm voice, “I see a problem.”

Pip stopped. Bibi paused. Lula and Tiko looked up.

Gigi tilted her head. “We all want to dance. But there is not much room. It is not fair if only one voice is heard. It is not fair if only one body moves. Let’s make a plan.”

Pip frowned. “A plan?”

Gigi nodded. “A fairness plan. A plan for justice. We will take turns. We will share. We will listen.”

“How?” asked Bibi.

Gigi looked around the shed. She saw chalk on a shelf. She saw a string on a nail. She saw the watering can, so shiny. She smiled. “We can be creative,” she said. “Watch.”

Gigi used her teeth to lift the chalk. She drew a big circle on the floor. Scratch, scratch, scratch. The chalk felt dusty and soft. The circle made a stage.

Then Gigi took the string and tied it to the handle of the watering can. “Ding,” she said, tapping the can gently with her hoof. It made a bright bell sound. “Ding!”

“When I make the ‘ding’ sound,” Gigi explained, “one dancer stops, and the next dancer starts. Each dancer gets two dings of time. That is fair.”

“Two dings?” Tiko asked.

Gigi smiled. “Yes. I will count: one ding to start, one ding to stop. Everyone gets two dings. Pip, would you like to go first?”

Pip’s whiskers twitched. He looked at the others. “Is that fair?” he asked.

Bibi chirped, “Yes, if we all get two dings.”

Lula nodded. “That feels fair.”

Tiko smiled slowly. “Justice,” he said.

Gigi lifted the can. “Ding!” she rang.

Pip hopped into the chalk circle. He danced tiny, funny hops. His tail swished. His paws patted the floor: pat-pat-pat! Everyone clapped. Gigi lifted the can again. “Ding!”

“Thank you, Pip,” said Gigi. “Bibi, your turn.”

“Ding!” Bibi fluttered into the circle. She flapped and spun. The air whooshed softly under her wings. She sang a sweet note. “Ding!” Gigi rang, and Bibi hopped out, smiling.

“Lula?” Gigi asked.

“Ding!” Lula pranced with her ribbon. Swish, swish, swish. Her hooves clicked: clack, clack. She bowed as the final “Ding!” sounded.

“Tiko?” Gigi’s eyes were kind.

Tiko moved slowly into the circle. He swayed left. He swayed right. His shell made a soft rub sound on the floor. He smiled big. “Ding!”

They all cheered. “Hooray!”

Gigi looked around. “Would anyone like another turn?” she asked. “If we all agree, we can keep going. We will follow the same fair plan.”

“Yes!” everyone said.

They took turns again and again. The shed was full of happy sounds: claps, taps, swishes, whooshes, and bright little dings. The dusty sunbeams felt warm on their faces. Gigi’s heart felt warm too. She had been a leader. She had used her mind. She had used creativity to bring justice to the dance.

At the end, Pip said, “Gigi, thank you. You listened to us.”

Bibi added, “You made a plan that was fair.”

Lula nuzzled Gigi’s leg. “You helped everyone shine.”

Tiko nodded. “Justice is sharing time and space.”

Gigi lowered her head and smiled. “Justice means we make room for all,” she said softly. “Justice means we take turns, we use kind words, and we listen. When we are fair, everyone can dance.”

They hung the string-and-can by the door. They drew a little heart by the chalk circle. The garden shed felt calm and bright. The friends knew what to do next time.

As they left, the wind smelled like fresh grass and the sky looked soft and blue. Gigi took a slow breath. She felt proud. She felt peaceful. And she thought, “Today, we made justice with creativity and care.”

The End.
