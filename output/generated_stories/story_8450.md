# Ollie's Dancing Garden Day

Ollie the otter loved his cozy balcony garden. Every morning, he would peek through the glass door and see his colorful flowers waving hello in the sunshine. Today felt extra special because his friends were coming to visit!

"Good morning, little plants!" Ollie chirped as he stepped outside. The warm sun tickled his whiskers, and he could smell the sweet flowers blooming everywhere.

But oh my! Ollie's garden looked very messy. His watering can was tipped over, leaves were scattered on the floor, and his gardening gloves were covered in yesterday's dirt.

"Hmm," Ollie scratched his fuzzy head. "I want my garden to look beautiful for my friends, but I have so many chores to do!"

Just then, Ollie had a wonderful idea. "I know! I'll make my chores into a dance!"

First, Ollie needed to clean his dirty paws. He wiggled and splashed at the little sink, singing, "Scrub-a-dub-dub, wash away the mud! Clean paws are happy paws!" The soap smelled like fresh mint, and his paws felt squeaky clean.

Next, he picked up his watering can and filled it with cool water. "Tip and pour, tip and pour," Ollie sang as he danced from plant to plant. The water made gentle splashing sounds, and the flowers seemed to smile as they got their morning drink.

"Sweep, sweep, sweep!" Ollie twirled with his little broom, gathering all the fallen leaves. His tail swished back and forth as he moved in circles, making the work feel like a fun game.

Soon, Ollie heard footsteps on the stairs. His friends were here! First came Maya, a little girl with beautiful dark skin and bouncy curls. Then came Sam, a boy with freckles and bright red hair. Last was Zara, who used a colorful wheelchair that sparkled in the sunlight.

"Wow, Ollie!" said Maya, clapping her hands. "Your garden looks amazing!"

"How did you clean everything so fast?" asked Sam, looking around at the tidy space.

Ollie giggled and did a little spin. "I turned my chores into a dance! Want to try?"

"Yes!" all three friends shouted together.

Ollie showed them his cleaning dance. Maya swayed as she helped water the flowers. Sam hopped and skipped while gathering more leaves. Zara spun her wheelchair in graceful circles as she helped organize the gardening tools.

"This is the best chore dance ever!" laughed Zara.

As they worked together, the garden became even more beautiful. The flowers stood tall and proud, the leaves were all swept away, and everything sparkled in the sunshine.

"You know what?" said Ollie, looking at his clean, happy garden and his wonderful friends. "Chores aren't boring when you make them fun! And they're even better when you do them with friends!"

Maya nodded. "At home, I dance when I put away my toys too!"

"And I sing when I brush my teeth!" added Sam.

"I race when I organize my books!" said Zara.

They all laughed and danced together in the beautiful, clean garden. The flowers swayed in the breeze as if they were dancing too, and the whole balcony felt magical.

From that day on, Ollie always remembered that chores could be fun, especially when you use your imagination and share them with good friends.

The End.
