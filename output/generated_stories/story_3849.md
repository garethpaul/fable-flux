# Joey's Big Heart

In a sunny backyard filled with green grass and colorful flowers, lived a little kangaroo joey named Bounce. Bounce loved hopping around in the warm sunshine, feeling the soft earth under his tiny paws.

Every morning, Bounce would hop to his favorite spot under the big apple tree. "Hop, hop, hop!" he would say happily as he bounced around the yard. The sweet smell of apples made his tummy rumble.

One bright morning, Bounce found something wonderful - a basket full of juicy red apples had fallen from the tree! His eyes grew wide with excitement. "Oh wow! So many yummy apples!" he said, counting them carefully. "One, two, three... ten whole apples!"

Bounce picked up an apple and took a big, crunchy bite. "Mmm! Sweet and delicious!" The juice dripped down his chin as he smiled. He was about to eat another when he heard a tiny voice.

"Excuse me," squeaked a small voice. Bounce looked down and saw a little field mouse with tears in her eyes. "I'm so hungry, and I smell those wonderful apples. Could you please share just one small piece?"

Bounce hugged his basket tighter. "But these are MY apples," he said. "I found them first!" The little mouse's whiskers drooped sadly as she walked away.

A few minutes later, a friendly rabbit hopped over. "Those apples look amazing!" said the rabbit. "I haven't eaten anything today. Would you share one with me?"

Again, Bounce shook his head. "No way! I need all ten apples for myself!" The rabbit's ears drooped as he hopped away slowly.

Soon, a baby bird chirped from above. "Tweet, tweet! Those apples smell so good! Could I have just a tiny piece? I'm learning to find food, but it's very hard."

"Absolutely not!" Bounce said firmly, turning his back on the little bird.

As the day went on, Bounce sat alone with his basket of apples. He ate one, then another, then another. But something felt wrong in his tummy - and it wasn't from eating too much. He felt sad and lonely.

Bounce looked around the empty yard. Where were all his friends? He remembered their sad faces when he said no. His heart felt heavy like a rock.

"I don't feel happy anymore," Bounce said to himself. "Even with all these apples, something is missing."

Just then, Bounce heard his mama's gentle voice in his memory: "The happiest hearts are the ones that share, little one."

Suddenly, Bounce understood! He hopped up quickly. "I know what to do!"

Bounce found the little mouse first. "I'm sorry I didn't share," he said kindly. "Would you like this apple? And maybe we could be friends?"

The mouse's eyes sparkled with joy. "Thank you so much! You're very kind!"

Next, Bounce found the rabbit and the baby bird. "I have apples for both of you too," he said warmly. "I'm sorry I wasn't nice before."

Soon, all the animals were sitting together under the apple tree, munching happily and laughing together. Bounce's heart felt warm and full - much better than his tummy had felt with all those apples!

"Sharing makes everything taste better!" Bounce giggled, taking a bite of his apple while surrounded by his new friends.

From that day on, Bounce always remembered that the best feeling came not from keeping everything for himself, but from sharing with others and making friends.

The End.
