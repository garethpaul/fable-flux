# Joey's Rooftop Adventure

High up on a sunny rooftop, a little kangaroo joey named Bounce was having the most wonderful time. The warm sun tickled his soft brown fur, and the gentle breeze made his ears wiggle. Bounce loved visiting the rooftop because he could see the whole neighborhood from up there.

"Boing, boing, boing!" Bounce hopped around the rooftop, chasing colorful butterflies and watching puffy white clouds drift by. The rooftop felt like his very own playground in the sky.

But then Bounce heard a familiar voice calling from below. "Bounce! Time to come down for lunch, sweetie!"

It was Mama Kangaroo, and her voice sounded a little worried.

"Just five more minutes, Mama!" Bounce called back, not stopping his happy hopping. The butterflies were so pretty, and the clouds looked like cotton candy. He didn't want to leave yet.

Bounce kept playing, jumping from one side of the rooftop to the other. The sun felt so nice and warm on his back. He watched a red bird fly by and tried to hop as high as the bird could fly.

"Bounce, please come down now!" Mama called again, and this time she sounded more worried. "Lunch is getting cold!"

"Almost done, Mama!" Bounce replied, but he kept playing. He found a shiny pebble and started pushing it around with his nose. It made a fun clicking sound on the rooftop.

After a while, Bounce started to feel funny in his tummy. It was making little rumbling sounds - grumble, grumble, grumble. He realized he was very hungry! And suddenly, he felt sad because he could hear Mama calling his name again, and now she sounded upset.

Bounce stopped hopping and sat down on the warm rooftop. He thought about how Mama had made his favorite lunch, and how she was probably worried about him. His ears drooped a little as he realized he hadn't been listening very well.

"Oh no," Bounce said to himself. "I should have come down when Mama first called me. She was trying to take care of me, and I wasn't being respectful."

Bounce hopped to the edge of the rooftop and called down clearly, "Mama, I'm sorry! I'm coming down right now!"

"Thank you, Bounce!" Mama called back, and he could hear the relief in her voice. "I was getting worried about you up there."

Bounce carefully hopped down from the rooftop, using the safe path that Mama had shown him. When he reached the ground, Mama gave him the biggest, warmest hug.

"I'm sorry I didn't come when you called, Mama," Bounce said, snuggling into her soft pouch. "I was having so much fun that I forgot about lunch time."

Mama smiled and gently stroked his ears. "I understand, little one. Playing is wonderful, but it's important to listen when it's time to stop. When I call you, it means I need you to come because I love you and want to take care of you."

"Next time, I'll come right away when you call," Bounce promised. "And maybe I can ask if I can play for just a little longer instead of just saying I need more time."

"That's a great idea!" Mama said. "Good communication helps us both understand each other better."

They went inside together, and Mama served Bounce his favorite lunch - fresh green leaves and sweet berries. As Bounce munched happily, he felt proud that he had learned something important about listening, being respectful, and managing his playtime.

From that day on, whenever Bounce was playing and Mama called, he would stop and listen right away. And sometimes, when he asked nicely, Mama would give him a few extra minutes to finish his game.

The End.
