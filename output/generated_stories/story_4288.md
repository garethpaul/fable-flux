# Splash's Big Feelings

Splash the dolphin loved to swim and play, but today was different. Today, Splash was visiting his friend Danny's garage workshop to help build something very special - a ramp for the neighborhood sports day!

"Welcome to my workshop, Splash!" said Danny, opening the big garage door. The workshop smelled like fresh wood and paint. Tools hung neatly on the walls, and colorful pieces of wood were stacked everywhere.

Splash wiggled excitedly in the special water tank Danny had set up. "This looks amazing! What are we building?"

"A ramp for everyone to use during sports day," Danny explained. "Kids in wheelchairs, kids who walk, kids who hop - everyone can use it to play together!"

Splash clapped his flippers. "That's wonderful! I want to help!"

Danny handed Splash a paintbrush with a special long handle. "You can paint this board blue, like the ocean!"

Splash dipped the brush in the paint and started painting. But the brush felt slippery in his flipper. SPLASH! The paint bucket tipped over, spilling blue paint everywhere.

"Oh no!" Splash's face turned red. His heart started beating fast. "I ruined everything!" he shouted, slapping his tail hard against the water. SPLASH! SPLASH!

"It's okay, Splash," Danny said gently. "Accidents happen."

But Splash felt angry bubbles growing in his tummy. "I'm terrible at this! I can't do anything right!" He splashed more water, getting paint and water all over the clean workshop.

Danny sat down next to Splash's tank. "I can see you're feeling really angry. That's okay. Everyone feels angry sometimes."

"But I made a big mess!" Splash huffed, his breathing fast and shallow.

"Let's try something together," Danny said softly. "When I feel angry, I take deep breaths. Want to try? Breathe in slowly... and out slowly."

Splash took a big breath in through his blowhole. "Whoooosh." Then he breathed out slowly. "Ahhhhhh."

"Good! Let's count to five together while we breathe," Danny suggested.

"One... two... three... four... five," they counted together, breathing slowly.

Splash felt the angry bubbles in his tummy getting smaller. "I feel a little better," he said quietly.

"That's great! Now, what if we work together to clean up and try again?" Danny asked.

Splash nodded. "Can you help me hold the brush steady?"

"Of course! Teamwork makes everything easier."

Together, they cleaned up the spilled paint. Danny held the brush steady while Splash guided it. Soon, they were painting beautiful blue stripes on the ramp.

"Look!" said Splash proudly. "We're making something that will help everyone play sports together!"

"That's right," Danny smiled. "Just like how we worked together when things got difficult."

As they painted, other neighborhood kids arrived - Maria in her wheelchair, Sam who used crutches, and little Emma who loved to run. They all helped paint different colored stripes.

"This ramp is going to be perfect for everyone!" said Maria.

"And it's so colorful!" added Sam.

When they finished, Splash felt proud and happy. "I learned something important today," he said. "When I feel angry, I can take deep breaths and ask for help instead of splashing around."

"That's right," Danny said. "Big feelings are normal, but we can learn to handle them in good ways."

The next day at sports day, kids of all abilities used the beautiful rainbow ramp to play together. Splash watched happily from his special pool, knowing that working through his angry feelings had helped create something wonderful for everyone.

The End.
