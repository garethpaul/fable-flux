# Bubbles in the Attic

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a big, happy smile. One sunny afternoon, Bubbles flapped his fins and swished his tail all the way up the stairs to a quiet attic. The attic smelled like warm wood and old books. Dust sparkled in a beam of light, like tiny stars. The floor was soft with old rugs, and the room was calm and still.

“Hello, attic!” Bubbles chirped. “I’m ready to play!”

He found a big trunk. The lid creaked, “Creeeeak,” when he opened it. Inside were colorful scarves, tiny drums, a paint set, and a box of paper fans with pretty patterns from many places around the world. Some had flowers, some had waves, and some had tiny birds. Bubbles clapped his fins. “So many things! So many sounds! So many colors!”

His friend Mina the mouse peeked from behind a hat. “Squeak! I like the drums,” she said.

Omar the owl hooted softly from a beam. “Hoo-hoo, I like the books.”

Lulu the ladybug landed on Bubbles’s fin. “I like the scarves,” she buzzed.

Bubbles smiled. “Let’s make an art show! We can make music, paint pictures, and dance. We can share things from different places. We can say thank you for our friends.”

Everyone cheered. The attic filled with happy sounds. Tap-tap went the drums. Swish-swish went the scarves. Hoo-hoo went Omar. Bubbles dipped a brush into bright blue paint. He painted wavy water. He hummed a splashy song.

Then, oops! Mina bumped the drum. It rolled and bumped the paint. The blue paint tipped. Splash! It spilled on Bubbles’s picture. Blue paint ran like a little river over the paper and onto the rug. It dripped on Bubbles’s tail. Cold! Sticky!

Bubbles felt his face get hot. His chest felt tight. His tail went stiff. “Oh no! My picture!” he cried. His voice got loud and sharp. “Mina, you ruined it!”

Mina’s tiny ears drooped. “I’m sorry,” she squeaked. “I didn’t mean to.”

Omar blinked. “Hoo… let’s slow down,” he said gently.

But Bubbles was mad. His tummy felt like a storm. He wanted to splash and shout. His tail flicked. The attic felt too loud, too messy, too much.

Then Bubbles remembered something his grandma taught him. “When big feelings come,” she’d said, “stop, drop, and breathe.”

Stop. Drop your shoulders. Breathe.

Bubbles closed his eyes. He put a fin on his chest. He took a slow breath in. One… two… three. He let it out like a soft bubble. Blub… blub… blub. He did it again. In… one… two… three. Out… blub… blub… blub.

He felt his chest loosen. His face cooled. The storm inside grew smaller. He opened his eyes. He saw Mina’s worried face, Omar’s kind eyes, and Lulu holding up a tiny paper fan, fanning the spilled paint. The fan had little cherry blossoms from far away. It made a soft whoosh-whoosh sound.

Bubbles spoke softly. “I was very mad. My body felt tight. I wanted to yell. I’m breathing now. I can use my words. Mina, I felt upset when the paint spilled. I worked hard on my picture.”

Mina nodded. “I understand,” she said. “I am sorry. I can help.”

Omar ruffled his feathers. “That was good breathing, Bubbles. Big feelings can be big. But you can be bigger.”

Lulu fluttered. “We can make it new! We can make it special!”

Bubbles looked at the spill. The blue paint made swirls like ocean waves. He wiggled his tail. “Maybe this is okay,” he said. “Maybe it can be art.”

Mina smiled a tiny smile. “A wave dance picture?”

Bubbles grinned. “Yes! A wave dance picture. And we can add more. We can use colors from our friends’ homes. We can make a world art picture.”

They worked together. Mina tapped the drum slowly. Boom… boom… like a calm heartbeat. Bubbles spread the blue paint with a scarf. Swish… swish. Omar read a poem from an old book, soft and steady. Lulu dotted the paper with bright red from cherry-blossom pinks. They used orange like warm sand, green like forest trees, and gold like sun tea. They added tiny birds, little boats, and smiling faces.

Bubbles showed Mina how to wipe the rug with a cloth. “It’s okay to make a mess,” he said, “and it’s good to clean up together.”

Mina’s ears perked up. “Thank you for forgiving me.”

Bubbles nodded. “Thank you for saying sorry.”

When they were done, the attic felt warm and happy again. The picture looked like a big blue ocean with friends from many places dancing on the waves. It was full of color, sound, and care.

They had a little art show. Omar announced, “Hoo-hoo! Presenting: The Friendship Waves!” Lulu held up a fan with tiny stars and fanned the picture. Whoosh! The paint dried. Mina offered a small bow. Bubbles took a deep breath and smiled wide.

“I learned something today,” Bubbles said. “When I feel mad, I can stop, drop my shoulders, and breathe. I can use calm words. I can forgive. I can turn a mistake into art.”

Mina added, “And I can say sorry and help fix things.”

Omar hooted, “We can share ideas from many places and be grateful.”

Lulu buzzed, “Thank you for friends!”

Bubbles wrapped a scarf around everyone in a gentle group hug. “Thank you,” he said. “I’m glad we are together.”

The attic was quiet again, but it felt full—full of color, kindness, and a calm, steady beat. Boom… boom. Swish… swish. Blub… blub.

The End.
