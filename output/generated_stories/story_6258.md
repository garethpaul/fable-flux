# Kira's Kitchen Note

Kira the koala sat at the big wooden table in her warm kitchen. The morning sun streamed through the yellow curtains, making everything glow like honey. She could smell Mama's fresh banana bread cooling on the counter.

"Kira, I'm going to water the garden," called Mama from the doorway. "Please don't touch the banana bread until it cools down completely."

"Okay, Mama!" Kira replied, swinging her fuzzy gray legs from the tall chair.

But as Mama's footsteps faded away, the sweet smell of banana bread filled Kira's nose. Her tummy rumbled loudly. "Just one tiny piece won't hurt," she whispered to herself.

Kira climbed onto the counter and broke off a small piece of the warm bread. It was so soft and yummy! Before she knew it, she had eaten three big pieces, leaving a messy gap in the loaf.

"Oh no!" Kira gasped, covering her mouth with her paws. "What have I done?"

She heard Mama's footsteps coming back. Kira quickly jumped down and pretended to color at the table, her heart beating fast like a little drum.

"Hello, sweetie," Mama said, walking to the counter. "How's your coloring— Oh my! What happened to the banana bread?"

Kira's ears drooped. She wanted to tell the truth, but she was scared. "Maybe... maybe a bird flew in and ate it?" she said quietly, not looking up from her crayons.

Mama's eyebrows went up. "A bird? In our kitchen?"

Kira felt her cheeks grow hot. The lie made her tummy feel all twisty and uncomfortable, much worse than when she was hungry.

"Actually, Mama," Kira said in a very small voice, "I ate the bread. I'm sorry I lied. I was scared you'd be upset with me."

Mama sat down next to Kira and gave her a gentle hug. "Thank you for telling me the truth, little one. I'm proud of you for being honest. But I'm a little sad that you didn't listen when I asked you to wait."

Kira's eyes filled with tears. "I feel really bad, Mama. How can I make it better?"

Mama smiled softly. "Well, what do you think would help?"

Kira thought hard, tapping her crayon against her chin. "I could write you a sorry note! And maybe help you make new banana bread?"

"That sounds perfect," Mama said, squeezing Kira's paw.

Kira got out her special writing paper with the rainbow borders. She drew a big heart and carefully wrote in her best letters: "DEAR MAMA, I AM SORRY I ATE THE BREAD AND TOLD A LIE. I LOVE YOU. FROM KIRA."

She drew pictures of bananas and hearts all around the words, using her favorite purple crayon.

"This is beautiful, Kira," Mama said, reading the note. "Your writing is getting so good! And I can see how sorry you feel."

Together, they mixed flour and mashed bananas for a new loaf. Kira helped measure and stir, being extra careful not to spill.

"Mama," Kira said as they put the new bread in the oven, "telling the truth felt scary at first, but now I feel much better inside."

"That's because honesty makes our hearts feel light and happy," Mama explained. "And writing that note was a wonderful way to show you care."

Kira hugged Mama tight. "Next time I'll ask before taking food. And I'll always tell the truth, even when it's hard."

"I know you will, my sweet koala," Mama said, kissing the top of Kira's fuzzy head.

The End.
