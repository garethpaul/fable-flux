# Mimi Mouse Finds Her Strong Yes and No

In a quiet study corner, where the sunlight made warm squares on the floor, a kind mouse named Mimi kept her little books, her tiny pencil, and her shiny silver bell. The bell made a soft ding-ding sound. The study corner smelled like paper and lemon tea, and Mimi liked the way the chair cushion felt squishy under her paws.

Mimi loved music and learning. She tapped her toes and hummed as she traced letters. “La-la, step by step,” she sang. “I can do it, and I can rest.”

One busy morning, Mimi spread out her paper. “I will practice my letters and my song,” she said. “First work, then a wiggle break.” She took a deep breath in, smelling the lemon tea. Her whiskers tickled. She smiled.

Just then, her friend Buzzy, a bright yellow bee, zipped in. “Mimi! Let’s play marching band!” Buzzy buzzed a happy tune. “Zzz-oom, zzz-oom!”

Mimi’s heart bounced. She loved music. But she looked at her paper. She had a plan. She had a choice to make.

Mimi put a paw on her chest. “I feel excited,” she said softly. “But I also need to finish my letters.” She looked at Buzzy and used her warm voice. “Buzzy, I like playing with you. Right now I am working. I will play marching band after I finish two pages and take a little rest.”

Buzzy hovered. “Now?” he asked, wings whirring.

“Not now,” Mimi said kindly but clearly. “I will play in a little while. You can come back when the big hand on the clock points up.” She pointed to the ticking clock. Tick-tock, tick-tock.

Buzzy buzzed in a circle. “Okay! I’ll come back when it points up!” He zoomed out, making a soft wind on Mimi’s ear. Mimi giggled and felt proud. She had said no, and she had said it kindly. Her no kept her plan safe.

Mimi traced her first page. “Round and up,” she whispered. “Round and up.” The pencil made a scritch-scratch sound. She hummed her practice song, “La-la, step by step.” When her paw felt tired, she stopped. She shook her paw. She took a sip of warm lemon tea. Ahh. “I am taking care of me,” she said. “This is self-care.”

Then her neighbor, Pip the puppy, padded in with a drum. Thump-thump. “Mimi, do you want to drum loud loud loud?” Pip wagged so hard his ears flopped.

Mimi liked Pip’s drum. The drum felt bouncy and deep. She also liked the quiet of her study corner. She had one more page to finish.

Mimi stood tall, even though she was small. “Pip, the drum sounds fun. I am not ready now. I will be ready after I finish one more page and take a wiggle break. You can drum softly, or wait and drum loud with me later.”

Pip put his head to the side. “Soft?” he asked.

“Yes. Soft like a heartbeat.” Mimi tapped the table gently. Thump... thump...

Pip smiled. “Soft drum,” he said, and tapped very gently. Mimi smiled back and finished her letters. She felt calm and strong. Saying what she needed helped her and her friends.

When the big hand on the clock pointed up, Mimi stood and rang her bell. Ding-ding! “Wiggle break!” she sang. She stretched her paws to the sky. She rolled her shoulders. She wiggled her nose. She felt her body wake up and feel happy.

Buzzy zipped back in. “It’s time! Marching band now?” he buzzed.

“Yes!” Mimi said. “Thank you for waiting.” She looked at Pip. “And thank you for drumming softly. Now we can play.” She took a deep breath. “Let’s make a plan together.” Mimi liked making choices. It helped everyone.

She pointed to the floor. “We will march two times around the study corner. Then we will play one song. Then we will clean up and have quiet time.”

Buzzy buzzed a happy yes. Pip wagged a yes. Mimi felt ready. She counted, “One, two, march!” The three friends stepped around the warm sun squares. The drum made a steady thump. Buzzy’s wings made a zzz-zzz. Mimi shook her bell, ding-ding, and sang, “La-la, step by step!”

They marched once. They marched twice. Then Mimi held up her paw. “Stop, please.” Her voice was clear and kind.

They stopped. “Song time!” Mimi said. “Soft at first, then big, then soft again.” She led the music with her bell. The sounds filled the study corner like bubbles. Ding-ding, thump-thump, zzz-zzz. It felt joyful. It felt just right.

When the song was done, Mimi put her paw on her chest again. “I feel happy,” she said. “I did my work. I had my rest. I said no when I needed to. I said yes when it fit my plan. I took care of me and had fun with friends.”

Buzzy nodded. “Your words helped me know what to do,” he said.

Pip licked Mimi’s paw. “Your plan helped me wait,” he said.

Mimi smiled. “Being kind includes being clear,” she said. “I can be small and still be strong. I can say, ‘No, not now,’ and I can say, ‘Yes, later.’ That is being assertive. That is making good choices. That is caring for me and for you.”

They cleaned up together—drum on the shelf, bell in the box, pencil in the cup. The study corner felt neat again. The lemon tea smelled sweet. The cushion was squishy and soft.

Mimi took one more deep breath and hummed, “La-la, step by step.” She felt proud of her clear voice and her gentle heart.

Today, Mimi learned: I can speak up for myself with kind words. I can keep trying, take breaks, and make a plan. When I use my strong yes and no, everyone knows what to do, and we can all feel good.

The End.
