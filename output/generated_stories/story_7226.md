# Oliver Owl's Calm Corner

Oliver was a wise old owl with soft, fluffy feathers and big, round eyes that sparkled like stars. He lived in a cozy guest room at the top of a tall oak tree. The room had a comfy bed with a patchwork quilt, a wooden desk by the window, and shelves filled with colorful books.

One windy afternoon, Oliver heard loud crying coming from downstairs. "Hoo-hoo-hoo!" he called softly as he fluttered down to see what was wrong.

Little Bunny was sitting on the floor, tears rolling down her furry cheeks. Her toy blocks were scattered everywhere, and her drawing paper was crumpled up in a ball.

"What's troubling you, little one?" Oliver asked gently, his voice as soft as a whisper.

"I can't make anything right!" Bunny sobbed. "My block tower keeps falling down, and my drawing looks all messy! Everything is going wrong!"

Oliver's wise eyes twinkled. "Oh my, that does sound frustrating. Would you like to come to my special guest room? I have something that might help."

Bunny wiped her nose and nodded. Together, they climbed the spiral staircase to Oliver's peaceful room.

"Welcome to my Calm Corner," Oliver said, spreading his wings wide. "When I feel upset or worried, I come here to find my peace."

The room smelled like lavender and felt as quiet as a gentle breeze. Sunlight streamed through the window, making rainbow patterns on the wall.

"First," Oliver said, settling onto a soft cushion, "let's take three deep breaths together. In through your nose... and out through your mouth. Feel your shoulders relax."

Bunny took the deep breaths. "That feels nice," she said, her voice already calmer.

"Now," Oliver continued, "before we create something beautiful, we need to prepare. Would you like to help me organize these art supplies?"

Together, they sorted crayons by color, stacked paper neatly, and arranged blocks by size. Bunny's breathing became slower and steadier as they worked.

"Preparing helps our minds feel ready," Oliver explained. "When everything has its place, we can focus on creating."

"I feel much better now," Bunny said, running her paws over the smooth, organized supplies.

"Wonderful! Now, let's create something together. But this time, we'll go slowly and enjoy each step."

Oliver showed Bunny how to stack blocks carefully, one at a time, breathing calmly between each piece. When the tower wobbled, instead of getting upset, they simply tried again.

"Look!" Bunny exclaimed as their tower grew tall and steady. "We did it!"

Next, they drew pictures together. Oliver taught Bunny to hold her crayon gently and move it slowly across the paper. "There's no rush," he said. "Creating is about enjoying the process."

Bunny drew a beautiful rainbow with careful, colorful strokes. "This is my best drawing ever!" she said proudly.

As the sun began to set, Oliver wrapped his soft wing around Bunny. "Remember, little one, when you feel overwhelmed, take deep breaths, prepare your space, and create with a calm heart. Magic happens when we slow down."

Bunny hugged Oliver tight. "Thank you for teaching me about being calm. Can I visit your Calm Corner again?"

"Anytime you need it," Oliver smiled. "And now you can create your own calm corner wherever you are."

From that day on, whenever Bunny felt frustrated, she remembered Oliver's lessons. She would breathe deeply, prepare carefully, and create with patience and joy.

The End.
