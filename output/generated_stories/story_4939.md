# Nutkin's Mindful Morning

In a cozy quiet attic above Mrs. Chen's house, a little gray squirrel named Nutkin was getting ready for his day. The morning sunlight streamed through the dusty window, making golden patterns on the wooden floor.

"What a beautiful day!" Nutkin said, stretching his fluffy tail. But as he looked around his attic home, he felt overwhelmed. His acorn collection was scattered everywhere, his nest was messy, and he had so many things to do.

Nutkin's heart started beating fast. "Oh no! I need to organize my acorns, fix my nest, find more food, and clean up! How will I do it all?" He began running around in circles, his little paws moving quickly but not really accomplishing anything.

Just then, Nutkin heard a gentle voice. "Slow down, little friend."

He looked up to see Wise Owl perched on a beam above him. Her big golden eyes were kind and calm.

"But I have so much to do!" Nutkin squeaked. "I don't know where to start!"

Wise Owl fluttered down gracefully. "Let me teach you something special, Nutkin. It's called being mindful. First, let's take three deep breaths together."

Nutkin sat still and breathed in slowly through his tiny nose. The air smelled like old wood and sunshine. He breathed out slowly, feeling his shoulders relax.

"Good," said Wise Owl. "Now, close your eyes and listen. What do you hear?"

Nutkin listened carefully. "I hear birds singing outside. I hear the wind in the trees. I hear my own heartbeat getting slower."

"Perfect! Now open your eyes and look around. Don't think about what you need to do. Just notice what you see."

Nutkin looked around his attic home with fresh eyes. He saw dust dancing in the sunbeams. He noticed how the old trunk in the corner had interesting scratches that looked like tiny maps. He saw that his scattered acorns made pretty patterns on the floor.

"I feel calmer now," Nutkin said with wonder.

"That's mindfulness," explained Wise Owl. "When we slow down and pay attention to right now, we feel peaceful. And when we're peaceful, we can think clearly."

Nutkin nodded. "So what should I do about all my work?"

"Let's make a plan together," said Wise Owl. "What's the most important thing to do first?"

Nutkin thought carefully. "I think organizing my acorns, because I need to know how much food I have for winter."

"Excellent thinking! And how could you make it fun?"

Nutkin's eyes sparkled with creativity. "I could sort them by size and make patterns! Big ones here, medium ones there, small ones in a circle!"

For the next hour, Nutkin worked mindfully. When he felt rushed, he would stop, take three deep breaths, and notice something beautiful around him. He arranged his acorns in creative spirals and made his nest cozy with soft feathers he found.

"Look!" Nutkin said proudly, showing Wise Owl his organized home. "I did it all, and I had fun too!"

"You learned something very important today," said Wise Owl. "When we slow down, breathe deeply, and focus on one thing at a time, we can do anything. And we can enjoy doing it!"

From that day on, whenever Nutkin felt overwhelmed, he remembered to be mindful. He would breathe, listen, look around, and make a plan. His days became much happier and more peaceful.

The End.
