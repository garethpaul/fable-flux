# Ella’s Backyard Painting Pause

In a sunny backyard, with soft green grass and warm light, lived a gentle elephant calf named Ella. The air smelled like oranges from a nearby tree. Bees hummed, and a little breeze tickled the leaves.

“I want to paint a picture!” Ella trumpeted softly. She dipped her tiny trunk into bright, cool paint. Plop! Blue dripped onto the paper. Swoosh! Red made a shiny line. “I will make a rainbow, a sun, and my favorite word,” she said with a smile.

She loved words. She liked how they sounded and felt. “Hello,” she practiced, letting it roll out gently. “Hola,” she tried, giggling at the new shape in her mouth. “Bonjour,” she whispered, and the word sounded like a little song.

Ella painted and talked to herself in happy tones. “Blue. Azul. Bleu,” she said, tapping each color with her trunk. The paint smelled a little sweet and a little like earth. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” A butterfly fluttered by like a tiny kite.

But after a while, Ella’s shoulders drooped. Her trunk felt heavy and sticky. Her tummy made a small grumble. Her picture looked messy, not like the rainbow she wanted. A yellow blob slid down the page.

“Oh,” Ella said, “my rainbow is running away.”

She tried to fix it. Drip! Drop! Splish! More paint, more lines, more blobs. The colors mixed into brown. Ella’s eyes felt prickly, and her ears sagged. “Why is this so hard?” she asked. Her heart thumped fast. “I want it to be beautiful.”

Her friend, a tiny ladybug named Dot, landed on her ear. “Hi, Ella,” Dot buzzed in a small, friendly voice. “You look tired.”

“I am,” said Ella. “I want to make art and say my new words. But my trunk is wobbly, and my tummy is growly. I feel grumpy.”

Dot nodded. “Maybe it’s time for a pause.”

“A pause?” Ella blinked her soft eyelashes. “But I must finish. I have to get it right.”

Dot sat on Ella’s paint jar. “Self-care time,” she said. “When we care for ourselves, we feel better. Then our art and our words feel better too.”

Ella looked at her messy paper. She sighed. “I think I need help to feel calm.”

“Let’s make a plan,” said Dot. “Step one: breathe. Step two: sip. Step three: stretch. Step four: try again.”

Ella listened. She put down her brush with a gentle plop.

Step one: Breathe. Ella closed her eyes. She filled her belly like a balloon. In… two… three. Out… two… three. The warm air smelled like oranges again. Her chest softened. Her trunk felt lighter. “Ahh,” she said.

Step two: Sip. Ella poured cool water into a little cup. She sipped slowly. Sip… sip… sip. The water felt fresh and kind on her tongue. “Thank you, water,” she whispered.

Step three: Stretch. Ella stretched her legs. She rolled her shoulders. She wiggled her toes in the grass. The grass felt tickly and safe. “Mmm,” she hummed. “That feels good.”

Dot clapped her tiny hands. “Now your heart is ready.”

Ella smiled. “I feel calmer. I can try again.”

She rinsed her trunk in a bowl. The water turned swirly purple. She got a clean sheet of paper that smelled like new books. She dabbed red. She dabbed yellow. “Red,” she said softly. “Rojo. Rouge.” She smiled at the music in each word.

She painted a big sun with gentle strokes. She let the paint dry for a moment. “Pause,” she told herself. “Breath, sip, stretch.” She did one slow breath. In… out. The sun stayed bright and round.

Next, she made a rainbow. She sang as she worked. “Blue, azul, bleu. Green, verde, vert.” The colors curved like a hug. She added dots, tiny like raindrops. Tap, tap, tap. The brush sounded like soft patters on the paper.

When her tummy rumbled again, she did another small sip and a banana bite from her snack bowl. “Snack break,” she giggled. “Merci, banana!”

Dot grinned. “You are caring for yourself and your art.”

Ella wrote her favorite word at the bottom. “Love,” she printed carefully. Then she tried in other ways. “Amor.” “Amour.” Each word felt warm and round. She touched the paper gently, so the letters would stay neat.

The wind whispered, and the sun made her picture glow. Ella’s ears perked up. Her heart felt steady and proud. “I did it,” she said.

Dot twirled. “How does your body feel now?”

“Soft and strong,” Ella said. “My head is clear. My trunk is steady. My heart is happy.”

Dot nodded. “That’s the magic of self-care.”

Ella looked at her first messy page. She smiled at it, too. “This page helped me learn,” she said. “It’s okay to pause. It’s okay to try again.”

She held up her finished painting. The rainbow, the sun, and the loving words shone bright. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” The butterfly fluttered close as if to see.

Ella turned to the backyard and said, “Hello! Hola! Bonjour!” The words floated out like friendly bubbles. She waved her trunk.

Then she whispered to herself, “I can grow. I can care for me. I can make art with love.”

Dot buzzed happily. “Yes, yes, yes!”

Ella giggled. “Next time I feel wobbly, I will breathe, sip, stretch, and try again.”

The backyard felt extra sunny, and Ella felt peaceful and proud. She placed her painting to dry and gave herself a gentle trunk hug.

The End.
