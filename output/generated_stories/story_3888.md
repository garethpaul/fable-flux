# Bubbles the Dolphin at Dinner Time

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with bright, curious eyes. Today, Bubbles splashed into a very different place: a cozy dining room! The room smelled like warm bread and sweet fruit. The table was set with shiny spoons, soft napkins, and colorful cups. The chairs were big and wooden, and the light was gentle and golden.

Bubbles wore a small blue bib. “Squeee!” Bubbles chirped happily. “I want to help!”

On the table sat a big bowl of salad, a plate of crunchy carrots, and a little dish of dipping sauce. Bubbles wiggled a fin. “I can pass the carrots!” Bubbles said.

Tippy, a tiny turtle with a shy smile, peeked from a booster seat. “I can carry the napkins,” Tippy whispered.

Pip, a fluffy penguin, waddled in with a pitcher of water and ice. “Teamwork!” Pip said. The ice clinked, and the pitcher felt cold and heavy.

Everyone got ready to set the table together. But as Bubbles reached for the carrots, the bowl slipped. Plop! A carrot fell and made a soft orange spot on the tablecloth. Then another carrot rolled onto the chair. Plink!

Bubbles’ cheeks felt hot. “No, no, no!” Bubbles squeaked. “I wanted it perfect!”

Inside Bubbles’ chest, the feeling got big and tight, like a knot. The room felt too bright. The sounds felt too loud. The smell of salad felt too strong.

“I’m mad!” Bubbles shouted. The shout felt prickly. Bubbles’ fins flapped fast, and water droplets sprinkled the air.

Tippy pulled into his shell a little. “I don’t like loud,” he said softly.

Pip held the pitcher steady. “It’s okay to feel mad,” Pip said. “Let’s use calm tools.”

Bubbles frowned. “I don’t know how.”

Pip waddled closer, but not too close. He remembered personal boundaries. “Can I stand here?” Pip asked, pointing to a spot near the table.

“Yes,” Bubbles said, taking a slow breath. “That spot is okay.”

“Thank you,” Pip said. “When I feel mad, I do my penguin breath. Want to try?”

“What is penguin breath?” Bubbles sniffled.

Pip showed. “Smell the cold ice… in through the nose.” He breathed in slowly. “Blow the warm air… out through the mouth.” He blew out gently. “Want to try with me and Tippy?”

Tippy peeped out and nodded. “I can count,” Tippy said. “One… two… three.”

Bubbles tried. In… smelling the cool ice. Out… blowing warm, slow air. The tight knot inside started to loosen. The room felt softer.

Bubbles took another breath. In. Out. The prickly feeling got smaller.

“I still feel a little mad,” Bubbles said, “but I can talk now.”

Pip smiled. “Talking is a calm tool too.”

Bubbles looked at the carrots. “I felt mad because I wanted to help, and I dropped them,” Bubbles said. “I got scared you would be mad at me.”

Tippy put a napkin near the carrot spot. “I’m not mad,” he said. “Mistakes happen.”

Pip nodded. “We can fix it together. That is teamwork.”

Bubbles’ fins slowed. “Can you help me pass the carrots?” Bubbles asked.

“Yes,” Pip said. “I will hold the bowl with two flippers.”

“And I will hold the napkins,” Tippy added, “so the table stays clean.”

They worked as a team. Pip held the bowl steady. Bubbles used a gentle fin. Tippy placed napkins under the bowl. The carrots stayed put. No plop. No plink.

“Thank you for asking before coming close,” Bubbles said to Pip. “That felt good.”

“Thank you for telling me where to stand,” Pip said. “That helps me know your boundaries.”

They kept going. Bubbles poured water very slowly. The ice went clink, clink. A few drops splashed. Bubbles felt a tiny tickle of worry, like a little wave. Bubbles did one penguin breath. In. Out. The worry wave went away.

Dinner smelled so yummy now. Warm bread. Crispy carrots. Sweet fruit. Everyone sat down.

“Wait,” Tippy said. “We forgot the dipping sauce.”

Bubbles blinked. “I can get it.” Bubbles slid the dish carefully, one small push at a time. It wobbled but did not spill.

“You did it,” Pip cheered.

Bubbles smiled. “I kept trying,” Bubbles said. “I used my calm tools.”

They ate together. Crunch, munch, sip. The food tasted fresh and friendly. The dining room felt safe and happy.

After dinner, Bubbles looked at the clean tablecloth and the neat plates. “Today I learned something,” Bubbles said. “Big feelings can visit. I can breathe, use my words, and ask for help. I can set boundaries and include friends. We can fix mistakes together.”

Tippy tapped the table gently. “We are a team,” he said.

Pip waddled in place. “When we get mad, we can pause, breathe, and try again. That is persistence,” he said.

Bubbles’ eyes shone. “Yes,” Bubbles said. “Calm hearts help happy dinners.”

They all giggled, and the golden light in the dining room felt warm and kind.

The End.
