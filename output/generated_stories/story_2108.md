# Ellie's Dancing Treehouse

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved her special treehouse. It had bright yellow walls, soft cushions, and a big window that let in warm sunshine. But today, Ellie felt frustrated and stompy.

"Thump, thump, THUMP!" went Ellie's feet as she danced around the treehouse. She spun and twirled, making the whole tree shake.

"Ellie, sweetie," called Mama Elephant from below. "The treehouse is wobbling! What's happening up there?"

But Ellie didn't answer. She was too busy being upset. She wanted to show her friends her new dance, but every time she tried yesterday, they had walked away. Now she felt sad and angry all mixed together.

"STOMP, STOMP, STOMP!" Ellie's dancing got louder and wilder.

Papa Elephant climbed up the rope ladder, his kind eyes twinkling. "Oh my! This treehouse is really rocking! Are you having big feelings, little one?"

Ellie stopped dancing and plopped down on a purple cushion. Her trunk drooped sadly. "Papa, I tried to show Ruby Rabbit and Benny Bear my special dance, but they didn't want to see it. They just hopped and walked away!"

Papa sat down beside her, making the treehouse creak gently. "That sounds frustrating. Tell me, how did you ask them to watch your dance?"

Ellie's ears flapped as she thought. "Well... I was so excited that I just started dancing really, really fast! And I was spinning and jumping and making lots of noise!"

"Ah," Papa nodded wisely. "And what were Ruby and Benny doing when you started dancing?"

"Ruby was reading a book under the apple tree, and Benny was building a tower with his blocks," Ellie remembered.

Papa's trunk gently touched Ellie's. "I think I know what happened. When we want to share something special, we need to use our words first. Did you ask if they wanted to see your dance?"

Ellie's eyes grew wide. "No... I just started dancing. I didn't ask!"

"That's right," Papa said softly. "When friends are busy doing quiet things, we need to speak gently and ask permission. Would you like to practice?"

Ellie nodded eagerly. She took three deep breaths, just like Papa taught her when she had big feelings. "Papa, would you like to see my special dance?"

"I would love to see your dance, Ellie!" Papa smiled.

This time, Ellie danced slowly and gracefully. She swayed like the trees in the breeze, stepped lightly like raindrops, and moved her trunk like a gentle wave. The treehouse barely moved at all.

"That was beautiful!" Papa clapped. "You used your body in such a controlled way. How did that feel?"

"It felt good! And peaceful," Ellie giggled. "I think my friends might like this better too."

The next day, Ellie climbed down from her treehouse and found Ruby reading under the apple tree. "Hi Ruby," Ellie said quietly. "When you finish your chapter, would you like to see a dance I made up?"

Ruby's ears perked up. "I'd love to! Let me just finish this page."

When Ruby was ready, Ellie performed her gentle dance. Ruby clapped and asked to learn the moves too! Then they found Benny, and Ellie asked politely if he'd like to join them after he finished his block tower.

Soon all three friends were dancing together in the soft grass, moving like trees and raindrops and gentle waves.

That evening, back in her cozy treehouse, Ellie smiled as she looked out the window. She had learned that using kind words and listening to others made everything better. And her gentle dance was even more beautiful when shared with friends who were ready to watch.

The End.
