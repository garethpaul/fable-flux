# Nutmeg Solves the Lab Light

In a cozy house, down creaky stairs, there was a basement lab. The air felt cool and smelled like clean soap and a little like crayons. Beep-beep went a tiny timer. Drip-drop went a slow faucet. Little lights blinked like stars.

A resourceful squirrel named Nutmeg lived nearby. Nutmeg had bright eyes, a fluffy tail, and quick paws. Nutmeg loved to help. Nutmeg loved to think.

One morning, Nutmeg heard a thunk. The lab light flickered. Then it went out! The basement felt dim and gray.

“Oh no,” said Nutmeg softly. “It is dark. But I can be calm. I can think.” Nutmeg took a slow breath in. One, two, three. Then a slow breath out. One, two, three.

Nutmeg clicked on a small flashlight. Click! A warm circle of light glowed on the floor. The light showed a note on the table. The note said, “Please water the green seed, then check the light.”

Nutmeg nodded. “First task, then next task,” Nutmeg said. “I can do things in order.”

Nutmeg looked around. The lab had jars, labels, and soft humming machines. A small pot sat by the window well, with a tiny green sprout. The soil looked dry and felt dusty on Nutmeg’s paw.

“I will water the green seed,” said Nutmeg. “But just the right amount.” Nutmeg saw three cups: big, medium, and tiny. Each cup had a picture. The big cup had a picture of a tree. The medium cup had a picture of a flower. The tiny cup had a picture of a seed.

Nutmeg tapped a paw to think. “The seed is very small,” said Nutmeg. “A tiny seed needs a tiny sip.” Nutmeg chose the tiny cup. Drip, drip. The soil turned dark and cool. The sprout looked happy and shiny.

“Task one is done,” Nutmeg said with a smile. “I kept the rule to be gentle and true.”

Now Nutmeg looked at the light. The switch was up. It should be on. But the bulb was dark. Nutmeg did not guess fast. Nutmeg used steps.

Step one: Look and listen. Nutmeg tilted an ear. The timer still went beep-beep. The hum went mmmmmm. Power was working.

Step two: Check the cord. Nutmeg followed the cord like a path. Around a stool, under a shelf, and—oh!—to a loose plug.

Nutmeg touched the plug. “I will be safe,” said Nutmeg. “I will be mindful.” Nutmeg made sure paws were dry. Nutmeg turned off the switch. Click. Then Nutmeg pushed the plug in all the way. Snug. Safe.

Nutmeg turned the switch back on. Click. But the light still did not glow.

“Hm,” said Nutmeg. “If not the plug, maybe the bulb.” Nutmeg looked up. The bulb was dusty. Dust made it look cloudy like a little moon.

Nutmeg found a soft cloth. Swish, swish. The dust floated away like feathers. Nutmeg tried the switch again. Click. Still dark.

Nutmeg did not pout. Nutmeg breathed in. One, two, three. Out. One, two, three. “I can try another step,” said Nutmeg. “I can ask for help. Asking is smart.”

Nutmeg called up the stairs, “Hello? Is anyone there?”

Mrs. Owl peeked down with kind gold eyes. “Whoo-hoo? Oh, Nutmeg! Thank you for watching the lab.”

“The light will not turn on,” said Nutmeg. “I checked the plug. I cleaned the bulb. I was safe. May I try a new bulb?”

“That is honest and wise,” said Mrs. Owl. “You told me what you did. That shows integrity. Yes, try a new bulb.”

Nutmeg found a box marked “bulbs.” The box had a label that said “Lab Light.” Nutmeg read each word slowly. L-a-b L-i-g-h-t. “Match the label to the lamp,” Nutmeg said. “Then the task is done right.”

Nutmeg turned off the switch. Click. Nutmeg twisted the old bulb, lefty-loosey. Then Nutmeg twisted in the new bulb, righty-tighty. Safe and snug.

“Ready?” asked Nutmeg. “Ready,” said Mrs. Owl.

Click.

The lamp glowed warm and bright. The lab looked sunny and kind. The sprout sparkled with tiny drops.

“Hooray!” said Nutmeg. “The light is on.”

Mrs. Owl clapped her wings. “Well done. You used critical thinking. You made a plan. You checked one thing at a time. You were honest about what you did. And you finished your tasks.”

Nutmeg’s tail swished happily. “First I watered the seed with the tiny cup. Then I checked the plug. Then I cleaned the bulb. Then I asked for help. Then I used the right bulb. Step by step!”

Mrs. Owl smiled. “That is how we solve problems. We think. We stay calm. We do things in order. We tell the truth. That is integrity.”

Nutmeg felt proud and peaceful. The lab now hummed softly. The warm light made the room feel safe and cozy. It smelled like fresh cloth and wet soil.

“I will write a note,” said Nutmeg. Nutmeg wrote, “I watered the seed. I fixed the light with a new bulb. - Nutmeg.” Nutmeg set the note by the sprout.

Before leaving, Nutmeg looked once more. The timer went beep-beep. The sprout was green and strong. The lamp glowed bright. All tasks were complete.

Nutmeg whispered, “Think, be honest, and finish what you start.” Nutmeg patted the tiny sprout. “Grow well, little friend.”

Nutmeg scampered up the stairs, feeling light and brave.

The End.
