# Pip's Big Welcome

Pip the parrot lived in a cozy guest room with yellow walls and soft blue curtains. Her feathers were bright green and red, and she loved to paint pictures with her special paintbrush held in her beak. Today, something exciting was happening!

"Squawk! Someone new is coming!" Pip chirped as she heard footsteps in the hallway.

The door opened, and in walked a little girl with dark hair and sad eyes. She carried a small suitcase and looked around the room quietly.

"Hello," said Mama from the doorway. "Pip, this is Yuki. She's visiting us from far away. Her family had to leave their home, and she'll be staying with us for a while."

Yuki sat on the bed and didn't say anything. She looked scared and lonely.

Pip tilted her colorful head. She wanted to help, but she felt nervous too. "What if she doesn't like me?" Pip thought. "What if we can't understand each other?"

But then Pip remembered what Grandpa Parrot always said: "When someone needs a friend, be brave and kind."

Pip took a deep breath and flew down to the desk. She picked up her paintbrush and started painting a picture. Swish, swish went the brush. She painted a big, bright rainbow with all her favorite colors.

"Pretty!" Yuki said softly, pointing at the painting.

Pip's heart felt warm and happy. "You like it?" she squawked gently.

Yuki nodded and smiled a tiny smile. Then she opened her suitcase and pulled out a small wooden doll with a beautiful blue dress.

"This is from my home," Yuki whispered, showing Pip the doll.

Pip looked at the doll carefully. It was different from any toy she'd seen before, but it was beautiful. "Wow! It's so special!" Pip chirped.

Yuki's smile grew bigger. She made the doll dance on the bed, and Pip flapped her wings happily.

"Can you teach me about your home?" Pip asked bravely. "I want to learn!"

Yuki's eyes lit up. She started telling Pip about her country, where there were tall mountains covered in snow and cherry trees with pink flowers. She showed Pip how to say "hello" in her language and taught her a little song.

Pip listened carefully and tried to repeat the words. "Kon-ni-chi-wa!" she squawked, and Yuki giggled.

Then Pip had an idea. "Let's paint together!" she said. "You can show me how to paint the mountains from your home, and I'll show you how to paint the ocean from mine!"

They spent the whole afternoon painting and sharing stories. Pip painted blue waves and sandy beaches, while Yuki painted snowy peaks and cherry blossoms. The room filled with the sweet smell of paint and the sound of happy chatter.

When Mama came to check on them, she found both friends sitting together, surrounded by beautiful paintings from around the world.

"Look, Mama!" Pip called out proudly. "Yuki taught me about her home, and I taught her about mine! We're friends from different places, but we're the same inside!"

Yuki hugged her wooden doll and smiled at Pip. "Thank you for being kind to me," she said.

Pip puffed out her chest proudly. She had been brave enough to reach out to someone new, and now she had learned so many wonderful things about the world.

"Everyone has special stories to share," Pip realized. "When we listen and learn from each other, we make the world more beautiful!"

That night, as the stars twinkled outside the guest room window, Pip and Yuki fell asleep as new friends, dreaming of all the amazing places in their big, wonderful world.

The End.
