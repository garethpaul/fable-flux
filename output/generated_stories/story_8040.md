# Otter Twirls and Tries Again

In a bright musical practice room, a playful otter named Ollie wiggled his whiskers. The room smelled like warm wood and lemon polish. Little lights twinkled on the mirror wall. A drum sat in the corner. A big piano waited with shiny black and white keys. The floor felt smooth under Ollie’s paws.

“I’m ready to dance!” Ollie chirped. He bounced, tail swishing. His fur was soft and shiny, and his small feet made gentle tap-tap sounds on the floor.

Miss Melody, the kind teacher, smiled and clapped softly. “Hello, Ollie. Today we practice three dance steps: slide, twirl, and bow.”

Ollie’s eyes sparkled. “Slide, twirl, bow! I can do it!” He puffed his cheeks with a happy breath.

Miss Melody played a sweet tune on the piano. Plink-plonk, plink-plonk. The notes were like raindrops. Ollie slid one paw forward. Swish. He twirled. Whoosh! He bowed. Ta-da!

“Great start,” said Miss Melody. “Let’s try it again. Slowly. Slide… twirl… bow.”

Ollie tried faster. Swish—whoosh—bonk! He bumped a music stand. It wobbled and made a tinny ring. Ollie’s whiskers drooped.

“I can’t do it!” he huffed. “My feet feel mixed up.”

Miss Melody stopped the song. “It’s okay to feel upset. Let’s take a belly breath.”

Ollie nodded. He put his paws on his tummy. Inhale in… one… two. Exhale out… one… two. His body felt softer, like a warm blanket.

From the corner, a tiny mouse named Mimi peeked out. She wore small dance shoes. “Hi, Ollie,” squeaked Mimi. “I like your twirl.”

Ollie’s ears perked. “Thanks, Mimi. But I got frustrated. I bumped the stand.”

Miss Melody tapped the piano keys gently. “Feelings are helpers. They tell us we need a pause. When we pause, we can try again.”

Ollie nodded. “I can try again.” He looked at the stand and whispered, “I’m sorry, music stand.” He turned to Miss Melody and Mimi. “I’m sorry I got loud. I felt upset.”

“Thank you for your apology,” Miss Melody said kindly. “That shows care.”

Mimi scampered closer. “We can dance together. We can count. One, two, slide. Three, four, twirl. Five, six, bow.”

Ollie smiled. “Counting helps.” His tail thumped a soft thump-thump on the floor.

Miss Melody played a slow, steady tune. Plink… plonk… plink… plonk. The air felt calm, like a quiet river.

“Ready?” asked Mimi. “One, two—slide.”

Ollie slid. Swish. His paws made a soft whisper sound.

“Three, four—twirl,” said Mimi.

Ollie twirled. Whoosh! He felt the air brush his fur. He saw the lights sparkle in the mirror.

“Five, six—bow,” Mimi squeaked.

Ollie bowed. Ta-da! His heart felt proud and warm. He grinned so big his whiskers curled.

“You did it,” Miss Melody cheered. “Let’s clap for trying and for kindness.” Clap, clap, clap.

Ollie clapped too. “Thank you, Mimi. Thank you, Miss Melody.” He looked at the drum in the corner. “Can we use the drum to help the beat?”

Miss Melody nodded. “Great idea.”

Mimi tapped the drum with tiny sticks. Dum… dum… dum. The beat felt like a friendly heartbeat.

“Let’s add arms,” said Miss Melody. “When you slide, your arms float. When you twirl, your arms open like a hug. When you bow, your arms rest by your sides.”

Ollie took a belly breath. In… out. The drum went dum… dum… dum. The piano sang plink… plonk.

“Slide,” Mimi counted. Ollie’s arms floated like leaves. Swish.

“Twirl,” Mimi counted. Ollie opened his arms wide, like a big hug. Whoosh.

“Bow,” Mimi counted. Ollie rested his arms and dipped. Ta-da.

He laughed softly. “This feels good. My body is dancing with the music.”

Then Ollie tried too fast again. His feet tangled. He wobbled. “Oops!” He almost fell onto his tail. He felt a tiny spark of frustration. His face scrunched.

He paused. He put paws on his tummy. In… one, two. Out… one, two. He whispered, “It’s okay to be learning. I can slow down.” He looked at Mimi. “Can we count again?”

“Of course,” Mimi said. “We help each other.”

They tried again with slow beats. Dum… dum… dum. Swish. Whoosh. Ta-da. The room smelled like lemon and sounded like a happy song. Ollie’s worries melted like ice in warm sun.

At the end, Miss Melody rang a small bell. Ting! “Wonderful dancing today. What did we learn?”

Ollie raised a paw. “I learned to dance with slide, twirl, and bow. I learned to breathe when I feel frustrated. I learned to say ‘I’m sorry’ when I bump or get too loud. And I learned to be kind and ask for help.”

Mimi nodded. “And we learned to count together and go slow.”

Miss Melody smiled. “Dance is for joy. Dance is for trying again. Dance is for kindness.”

Ollie did one last gentle twirl. The lights sparkled. The drum hummed. His heart felt steady and glad.

“I can dance,” he whispered, proud and calm. “I can try again.”

The End.
