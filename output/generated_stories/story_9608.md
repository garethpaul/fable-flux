# Koala Kori’s Cozy Clean-Up Quest

Kori the calm koala woke up in a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls and a window that let in warm, golden morning light. The room smelled like clean eucalyptus leaves and fresh laundry. A fluffy rug tickled Kori’s toes as Kori stretched and yawned.

“Good morning, room,” Kori whispered in a gentle voice. “Today, I will practice being calm and persistent.”

Kori’s bookshelf was full of picture books, puzzles, and a small basket of toy cars. The bed had a green blanket with leaf patterns. Kori loved this cozy place. But today the toy shelf looked a little messy. A puzzle had pieces everywhere. A tiny drum sat on top of a stack of books. A soft bunny toy peeked out from under the bed.

Kori took a deep breath in and breathed out slowly. “I can do this,” Kori said. “I will clean, I will care for my room, and I will keep trying.”

Kori picked up a puzzle piece with a picture of a tree. It felt smooth and cool. “This piece goes somewhere,” Kori said, tapping the wooden board. Kori tried one spot. It didn’t fit. Kori did not frown. Kori tried another spot. Still no. Kori smiled softly and patted the piece. “I will try again.”

Kori wiggled the piece, listened to the tiny click as it slid in just right, and clapped. “Yes! Persistence!”

A gentle knock came at the door. “Kori? Can I play?” asked Pip, the friendly possum neighbor.

“Come in,” Kori said calmly.

Pip peeked in with bright eyes. “Wow, your room smells like leaves! Oooh, can we play with the drum?”

“Yes,” Kori said, “We can play. But let’s take turns. And first, I’m cleaning to care for my room and our planet. When we take care of our things, we don’t waste. That helps the Earth.”

Pip nodded. “I’ll help!”

Kori and Pip picked up books. They stacked them by size, small to big. The books made a soft thump as they settled on the shelf. They rolled toy cars into a basket. The wheels whirred and clicked. Kori found a crinkly paper wrapper under the bed.

“This goes in the recycle bin,” Kori said. “We can turn old paper into new paper.”

Pip smiled. “That’s good for the Earth.”

They kept cleaning. Kori hummed a little song: “Slow and steady, one by one, calm and kind, until it’s done.” The song sounded like a warm hug. Pip began to hum too.

Then Kori saw the tiny drum on top of the books. “Let’s put the drum on the music shelf,” Kori said. “And then we can play.”

Pip wiggled with excitement. “My turn first!”

Kori took a breath. “We will take turns,” Kori said gently. “Let’s count to three. Then it’s my turn, then your turn, and we keep going.”

They placed the drum on the bed. Kori tapped lightly. Tap-tap. The sound was soft, like raindrops on leaves.

“Now your turn,” Kori said, passing the drum with both hands. Pip tapped a little faster. Tap-tap-tap! The drum sounded like happy feet.

“Nice,” Kori said. “Let’s keep the drum soft so we are kind to ears.”

They traded the drum back and forth, counting, “One, two, three,” taking turns without fuss. Kori’s calm voice made the room feel peaceful. Pip’s smile grew huge.

After a while, Pip spotted the puzzle with a missing piece. “Can I help?”

“Yes,” Kori said, “Let’s find the last piece. It might be hiding.”

They looked under the pillow. They looked behind the chair. Pip checked the bunny toy’s pocket. No piece. Kori brushed crumbs off the rug into a tiny dustpan. “We can keep trying,” Kori said. “Persistence means we don’t give up. We try again and again, calmly.”

Pip flopped on the rug. “But it’s hard.”

Kori nodded. “It is hard. Let’s breathe together.” They breathed in, smelling the clean blanket. They breathed out, hearing the soft whoosh. “Now let’s try a new idea,” Kori said. “We will look slowly and carefully.”

Kori crawled to the bookshelf and looked at the floor. A little corner of wood peeked out under the green blanket. “I see it!” Kori cheered softly.

Pip grabbed it and held it up. “We did it!”

They placed the piece together. It clicked. The tree picture looked whole and bright. Kori and Pip clapped and giggled.

Kori picked up the recycle bin. “Let’s take this to the big bin later,” Kori said. “Caring for our room is caring for the Earth.”

Pip nodded. “And we took turns!”

“And we kept trying,” Kori said. “We were calm. We were persistent.”

The room now looked tidy and cozy. The blue walls seemed brighter. The rug felt fluffier under their knees. The air felt light, like a little breeze from the open window.

Pip stood up. “Can we read a book now?”

“Yes,” Kori said. “We can read one book, taking turns to turn the pages.” Kori chose a story about trees and animals. Pip turned a page gently. Swish. Kori read in a soft, steady voice.

When the story was done, Pip gave Kori a hug. “Thank you, Kori. I learned to keep trying, to be calm, to take turns, and to care for our things.”

Kori hugged back. “Me too,” Kori said. “Small steps, calm breaths, and try again.”

As the sun made a square of warm light on the floor, Kori whispered, “Today’s quest is complete.”

The clear lesson: When we are calm, take turns, care for our space, and keep trying, we can finish hard things and help the Earth too.

The End.
