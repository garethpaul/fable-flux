# Marni Mouse Leads a Treehouse Dance

Marni was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright whiskers that tickled when she giggled. She lived in a cozy treehouse high in a friendly oak. The treehouse smelled like warm wood and sweet apples. Sunlight peeked through the window and made tiny dust sparkles dance in the air.

One sunny morning, Marni woke up and stretched. “Squeak! Today feels like a dancing day,” she said, patting her round, pink ears. She opened her door. A gentle breeze kissed her nose. Leaves whispered shhh shhh. Marni smiled. “I will dance! And I will invite my friends,” she said.

She hung a little sign on the rail: Dance Time at Marni’s Treehouse! All friends welcome!

Soon there was a soft tap-tap on the ladder. Tessa Turtle peeked up. “I move slowly,” Tessa said, “but I want to dance.”

“Come in!” said Marni. “We can all dance in our own way.”

Next came Benny Bunny, who hopped-hopped with quick feet. “I like big jumps!” he said.

Then Pip the squirrel scurried up with a nut in each paw. “I can shake-shake-shake!” Pip chirped.

Marni clapped her tiny paws. “Wonderful! Let’s begin.”

The floor was smooth and warm. A little drum sat by the window, and a tiny bell hung from the ceiling. Marni tapped the drum: bum-bum, bum-bum. She rang the bell: ting!

“First,” Marni said, “we feel the beat. Tap your toes.” She tapped. Tap, tap. Everyone listened. The beat felt like a heartbeat. Bum-bum. Bum-bum. Marni’s whiskers twitched with joy.

Benny tried to jump right away. Boing! He bumped a cushion. “Oops,” he said, ears drooping.

“It’s okay,” said Marni kindly. “We can try again. In dance, we listen, we feel, and we try. We help each other.”

Tessa frowned a little. “I am slow. I can’t jump like Benny.”

Marni took her paw. “That’s all right. Slow can be beautiful. You can glide.” She showed Tessa how to slide a foot. Sliiiide. Sliiiide.

Tessa smiled. “I can glide.”

Pip shook the nuts. Click-clack, click-clack. “I can make music!”

Marni nodded. “You are our rhythm helper, Pip.”

Marni tapped the drum again. “Let’s make a dance together. I will lead, and I will listen. A good leader is kind and helps friends shine.”

She looked around. “Step one: Tap your toes. Tap-tap.” They all tapped. Tessa tapped gently. Benny tapped fast. Tap-tap-tap! Pip clicked the nuts to the beat. Click-clack.

“Step two: Arms out like tree branches.” Marni stretched wide. The warm air hugged their fur and shells. The sunlight felt like a soft blanket.

“Step three: Show your own move!” said Marni. “Benny, you go first.”

Benny took a breath. “I can do a small hop.” Boing. He did a careful, small jump. “I did it!”

“Great!” said Marni. “Small hops are smart hops.”

“Tessa?” Marni asked.

Tessa lifted one foot and glided in a slow circle. Sliiiide. Her shell shone in the sun. “I feel calm,” she said.

“It’s beautiful,” Marni said. “Slow can be strong.”

“Pip?” Marni smiled.

Pip shook the nuts up, down, side to side. Click-clack, clickety-click! “I make the music dance!”

Marni’s heart felt warm. “Now my move,” she said. She twirled gently. Whoosh. Her whiskers tickled her cheeks. She giggled. “That’s my twirl.”

They put the moves together. Tap-tap, arms out, hop, glide, shake, twirl. They danced again. Tap-tap, arms out, hop, glide, shake, twirl. The floor creaked a happy creak. The leaves whispered. The bell went ting at the end.

But then, the drum rolled off the stool. Thump! It scared Pip. “Oh!” Pip squeaked. The nuts fell. Clatter!

Benny’s ears drooped again. “Maybe I can’t dance.”

Marni took a slow breath. In through the nose. Out through the mouth. “It’s all right. Sometimes things fall. We can pause. We can be kind to ourselves.”

She picked up the drum and set it steady. She handed Pip the nuts. “You are safe. You are doing great.”

Marni looked at Benny. “Let’s try a new idea. We can count together. One, two, three.” She nodded. “We go slow first. One, two, three.” They moved with the count. Tap on one. Arms on two. Hop or glide or shake on three. Twirl when they felt ready.

Benny tried a tiny hop on three. Boing. He landed on his feet. His nose wiggled. “I did it!”

“You did!” said Marni. “See? Dance is for everyone. We practice. We listen. We care.”

Soon, the dance felt like a happy hug. Tap-tap. Arms out. Hop. Glide. Shake. Twirl. Laughter filled the treehouse like bubbles. The air smelled sweet, like apples and leaves. The light made soft gold on the floor.

At the end, Marni rang the bell. Ting! She bowed. Everyone bowed back.

“Thank you for dancing,” Marni said. “Today we learned three big things. One, dance starts with listening to the beat. Two, kindness helps us try again. Three, a good leader helps friends use their own special moves.”

Tessa smiled. “I can glide with pride.”

Benny grinned. “Small hops are still hops.”

Pip wiggled his tail. “Click-clack is music!”

Marni’s heart felt big. “When we dance with kindness,” she said, “we all shine together.”

They hugged. The leaves whispered a soft, happy shhh. The sun gave one last warm kiss through the window. Dance Day at Marni’s Treehouse was done, and everyone felt brave, kind, and full of music.

The End.
