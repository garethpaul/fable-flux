# Oliver the Owl's Big Problem

In a sunny backyard filled with colorful flowers and buzzing bees, lived Oliver the Owl in a tall oak tree. Oliver had soft gray feathers and big, round eyes that sparkled like stars. Even though owls usually sleep during the day, Oliver loved watching the busy backyard come alive in the sunshine.

One bright morning, Oliver heard tiny voices crying for help. "Help! Help!" squeaked the voices from below.

Oliver looked down and saw three little field mice trapped under a heavy flower pot that had fallen over. The mice were scared and couldn't push the pot away by themselves.

"Don't worry, little friends!" hooted Oliver kindly. "I'll help you!"

Oliver flew down and tried to lift the pot with his talons, but it was too heavy even for him. He pushed with his wings, but the pot wouldn't budge.

"Oh no," thought Oliver, feeling worried. "What can I do?"

The little mice looked up at him with hopeful eyes. "Please don't give up, Oliver!" said the smallest mouse.

Oliver sat quietly and thought very carefully. He looked around the sunny backyard, noticing everything he could see. There were smooth stones by the garden path, a long stick near the fence, and a small bucket by the water spigot.

"Wait a minute," Oliver said, his eyes growing bright. "I have an idea! But I need to think about this step by step."

First, Oliver picked up the long stick in his beak. He placed one end under the edge of the flower pot. Then he found a smooth, round stone and carefully positioned it under the stick, close to the pot.

"What are you doing?" asked one of the mice curiously.

"I'm making a lever!" explained Oliver. "The stick will help me lift the pot, and the stone will be my helper. Watch and see!"

Oliver pressed down on the other end of the stick with his strong talons. Slowly, slowly, the heavy flower pot began to lift up!

"It's working!" cheered the mice excitedly.

"Quick! Run out while I hold it up!" called Oliver.

The three little mice scurried out safely, their tiny paws pattering on the warm ground. Oliver gently let the pot down and wiped his feathers proudly.

"How did you know to do that?" asked the mice, gathering around Oliver's feet.

Oliver smiled warmly. "When I had a big problem, I stopped and thought carefully. I looked at everything around me and asked myself, 'How can these things help me?' Sometimes the best solutions come when we think step by step and don't give up."

The smallest mouse hugged Oliver's feather. "Thank you for being so smart and kind! You saved us!"

"And thank you for being patient with me while I figured it out," Oliver replied. "Good thinking takes time, but it's always worth it."

From that day on, Oliver and the mice became the best of friends. Whenever anyone in the backyard had a problem, they would all work together, thinking carefully and trying different ideas until they found a solution.

As the sun shone down on the happy backyard, Oliver felt proud that he had used his mind to help others. He learned that being brave sometimes means taking time to think, and the best answers come to those who look carefully and never give up.

The End.
