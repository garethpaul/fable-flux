# Panda Poppy’s Bathroom Timer

Panda Poppy woke up with a big yawn. Stretch. Wiggle. Smile. Today felt shiny and new. The bathroom was bright and warm. The light made tiny sparkles on the mirror. The fluffy bathmat felt soft under Poppy’s toes.

“Good morning, toothbrush,” Poppy said. “Good morning, bubbles.” Poppy was a patient panda. Poppy liked to take time. But today, Poppy also had a plan.

Poppy’s plan was on a small chart taped to the wall. It had little pictures:
- Brush teeth
- Wash face
- Comb fur
- Tidy towels
- Music time!

Next to the chart was a colorful sand timer. Swish, swish, swish, the sand slid down. “This is my time helper,” Poppy said. “It helps me know what to do now, and what to do next.”

Poppy touched the cool faucet. Drip, drip. “First, brush teeth.” Poppy turned the timer. Swish. The sand began to fall. “Two minutes,” Poppy said softly. “I can do two minutes.”

Poppy held the minty toothpaste. It smelled fresh, like a leaf after rain. “Squeeze, tiny pea,” Poppy whispered. Squish! A wiggly line came out, a little too big. “Oops.” Poppy smiled. “I forgive myself. I will try again.” Poppy wiped the extra and made a tiny pea. “There. Better.”

Brush, brush, brush. Tiny circles. Poppy hummed a gentle song. Bubbles tickled Poppy’s tongue. “Giggle,” Poppy said, spitting carefully. “Mint snow!” The timer sand kept swishing. Poppy watched, but kept brushing until the sand reached the bottom.

“Ding—well, no ding,” Poppy laughed. “But the sand says stop.” Poppy rinsed, feeling clean and proud.

Next on the chart: wash face. Poppy turned the timer again. Swish. The water felt cool and splashy. Poppy patted cheeks with a soft towel. “Plop, plop,” went the drops. “I can do this before the sand is gone,” Poppy said. Poppy took time, but not too much time. It felt just right.

Then, comb fur. Poppy’s comb was purple and smooth. “Hello, comb,” Poppy said. “Let’s make my fur flat and cozy.” Comb, comb, swoosh. A tiny curl popped up. “Boing!” Poppy giggled. “You are silly.” Poppy gently smoothed it down. The timer sand was halfway. “I am doing well,” Poppy said, feeling calm.

Suddenly, Poppy saw the bath crayons. Red, blue, yellow. So bright! Poppy loved to draw. “I could make a quick sun,” Poppy whispered. Poppy looked at the timer. The sand was almost done.

“I want to draw,” Poppy said, “but I want to follow my plan.” Poppy took a breath. In. Out. “I can wait. I can finish first.” Poppy smiled at the crayons. “I will draw when the chart is done.” The crayons waited, quiet and shiny.

Next: tidy towels. Poppy turned the timer. Swish. Poppy folded the small towel. Fold, press, pat. It smelled like lemons. Poppy hung the big towel on the hook. “Up you go,” Poppy said. Poppy picked up a little toy boat from the tub. Drip, drip. “Boat goes in the basket.” Poppy felt proud. The bathroom looked neat and calm. The sand slipped to the bottom.

Now: music time! Poppy clapped. “I finished my chart!” Poppy looked at the crayons. “And now I can draw.” Poppy turned the timer again. “I will draw until the sand is gone. Then I will stop. That is my plan.”

Poppy drew a bright sun with a smiling face. Circle, circle. Poppy drew a blue wave for the toy boat. Swish, swoosh. Poppy drew a tall bamboo with little leaves. Tap, tap, tap, went the crayon on the tub wall. The bathroom smelled a little like soap and a little like crayons. It felt happy.

Just then, a tiny smear of blue got on the mirror. “Oh!” Poppy blinked. “I did not mean to do that.” Poppy felt a little twist in the tummy. Then Poppy took a deep breath. “I forgive myself. I can fix it.”

Poppy got a small cloth and a drop of warm water. Wipe, wipe. The blue smear was gone. “All better,” Poppy said. Poppy put the cloth back in the laundry basket. Organized and neat.

Swish, swish… the last grains of sand fell. “Time is up for drawing,” Poppy said. Poppy looked at the sunny picture. “I want more,” Poppy felt. The feeling was big. Poppy put a paw on the heart. “I can draw again later. I did what I planned. I can be patient.”

Poppy cleaned the crayons and put them in a little cup. Red, blue, yellow. “Thank you, crayons. See you after lunch,” Poppy said. Poppy checked the bathroom. Towels tidy. Toys in the basket. Comb and toothbrush in their spots. Chart done. Timer quiet.

Poppy smiled in the mirror. “I used my time helper. I followed my plan. I was patient and kind to myself. I cleaned up, too.”

From the hallway, a friendly voice called, “Poppy, are you ready?”

“Yes!” Poppy called back. “I am ready on time!” Poppy felt light and proud and calm.

Poppy gave the chart a soft pat. “Plan, timer, tidy,” Poppy said. “This is how I take care of my time.”

Poppy waved to the bathroom. “Thank you for a good morning.”

And off Poppy went, paws soft on the floor, heart happy, mind clear.

The End.
