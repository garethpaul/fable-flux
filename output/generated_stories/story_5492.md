# Tiko the Wise Tortoise in the Closet Hideaway

Tiko was a wise tortoise with a smooth, green shell that felt cool like a leaf. He moved slowly, step… step… step, and he liked to think before he talked. One rainy afternoon, pitter-patter drops tapped on the window. Tiko lived with Mia and Max, two curious twins, who were looking for a place to play.

“Let’s make a secret hideaway!” Mia whispered.

“In the closet!” Max said, pointing to the big, cozy closet with soft blankets and boxes.

Tiko blinked and nodded. “A hideaway can be wonderful,” he said in his gentle voice. “We must make a plan.” He loved helping, and he loved good plans.

They opened the closet door. It smelled like clean cotton and warm wood. It felt snug and quiet inside. Mia pulled a blue blanket. Max tugged a red pillow. Tiko moved along the floor, step… step… step, guiding with his calm eyes.

“I want the top shelf,” Mia said.

“I want the top shelf too!” Max said.

They both grabbed the ladder.

Tiko looked up. The top shelf was small. It could not fit both children safely. “Hmm,” Tiko said softly. “We have a little problem.”

Mia folded her arms. Max frowned. The hideaway was not feeling cozy now.

Tiko took a breath and smiled. “When we have a problem, we can use kind words. We can listen. We can make a plan together. That is called negotiation.”

“Ne-go-shi-a-tion?” Mia asked.

“It means we talk, we listen, and we find a fair way,” Tiko said. “Let’s sit in a circle.”

They sat on the soft rug. Tiko tapped his shell gently. “First, we take turns. Mia, you share what you want.”

“I want the top shelf,” Mia said. “I like to see out.”

“Thank you,” Tiko said. “Max, your turn.”

“I want the top shelf too,” Max said. “I like to reach the light.”

“Thank you,” Tiko nodded. “Now we think of ideas. We move our bodies to try ideas.” He smiled and began to show them. “Idea one: take turns by time. Mia on top for five minutes. Max on top for five minutes.”

Mia wiggled. “Five minutes is short!”

Max shook his head. “I don’t like waiting.”

Tiko nodded. “Okay. Idea two: different spots. The top shelf is small. The floor is big and soft. We can make two cozy places.”

Mia peered in. “But I still want to see out.”

Max looked at the light switch. “And I still want the light!”

Tiko scratched his chin. “Idea three: We add something to each spot. We can make the floor spot bright with a little flashlight. We can make the top shelf a peek spot with a tiny window in the blanket.”

Mia and Max looked at each other.

Tiko smiled again. “We can volunteer to help each other. Volunteering means we choose to help. I will volunteer to carry things. I am slow but strong.”

“I can volunteer to tape the blanket window,” Mia said.

“I can volunteer to hold the flashlight,” Max said.

They all got to work. Tiko moved, step… step… step, pushing the flashlight with his nose. Mia taped a small square in the blanket. Snip, stick, smooth. Max clicked the flashlight. Click! A warm glow filled the lower spot. The closet felt cozy again.

“Let’s try our new plan,” Tiko said. “Mia, would you like the top peek spot first?”

“Yes,” Mia said, smiling. She climbed carefully. The blanket window made a tiny square of light. She could see a little bit of the room. “I can peek!”

“Max,” Tiko said gently, “your floor spot is bright. How does it feel?”

Max lay on the red pillow. The flashlight made stars on the wall. “It feels like a campout!”

They both giggled. The rain still went pitter-patter, but inside the closet, it sounded like a soft drum.

After a while, Mia called down, “Max, would you like a turn up here? I can come down.”

Max smiled. “Yes! Thank you.” He climbed up carefully. Mia lay on the pillow and held the flashlight, making star shapes dance. Tiko watched with warm eyes.

“You both used kind words,” Tiko said. “You listened. You made a plan. You tried ideas with your bodies. That is good negotiation.”

Mia and Max nodded. “Thank you, Tiko.”

Tiko tucked into his shell and then peeked out. “I learned too. I learned that when we move and try ideas, our bodies help our brains. Step by step.”

The twins took turns a few more times. They even added a little bell for a turn timer. Ding! Time to switch. They laughed and switched spots, easy and fair. The closet hideaway felt safe, soft, and bright. It smelled like cotton and a tiny bit like tape. It sounded like giggles and little dings.

When the rain stopped, the sun made a golden line on the floor.

Mia patted Tiko’s shell. “We solved our problem.”

Max nodded. “We made a plan together.”

Tiko smiled. “Yes. The lesson is: When we have a problem, we can use negotiation. We can talk, listen, and make a fair plan. We can move, try, and help each other. That is wise.”

They curled up in their cozy places one last time, calm and happy.

The End.
