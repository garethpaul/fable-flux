# Rosie Rabbit's Thank You Garden

The morning sun warmed the peaceful front porch where Rosie Rabbit sat with her little watering can. She looked at the empty flower boxes with a frown.

"Oh no!" Rosie said, tapping her fluffy white foot. "My beautiful flowers are all gone!"

Yesterday, strong winds had blown away all her pretty petals. The flower boxes looked sad and empty. Rosie's ears drooped down as she stared at the bare dirt.

"What am I going to do?" she whispered to herself. "My porch looks so plain now."

Grandpa Rabbit hopped onto the porch, carrying a basket of fresh carrots. He smelled like sunshine and garden soil.

"Good morning, Rosie," he said with a warm smile. "Why do you look so sad on this beautiful day?"

"My flowers are all gone, Grandpa," Rosie sighed. "The wind took them away. Now I have nothing pretty to look at."

Grandpa Rabbit set down his basket and sat beside Rosie. "Let me tell you a secret, little one. The most beautiful gardens grow from grateful hearts."

"What do you mean?" Rosie asked, tilting her head.

"Well," Grandpa said, "instead of being sad about what's gone, let's be thankful for what we still have. What good things do you see right now?"

Rosie looked around slowly. "I see... the warm sunshine on my fur. That feels nice."

"Yes! What else?" Grandpa encouraged.

"I hear birds singing in the trees. Their songs sound happy," Rosie said, her ears perking up a little.

"Wonderful! Keep looking."

Rosie stood up and sniffed the air. "I smell your fresh carrots. They smell crunchy and sweet!"

"That's right! And what about your watering can?"

Rosie picked up her little green watering can. "It's still here! And it's perfect for watering new seeds."

Grandpa clapped his paws together. "Now you're thinking like a grateful gardener! When we notice all the good things around us, we feel happier and stronger."

"Can we plant new flowers together?" Rosie asked, bouncing on her toes.

"Of course! But first, let's make this a special routine. Every morning, we'll say three things we're thankful for."

Rosie nodded eagerly. "I'm thankful for sunshine, bird songs, and... you, Grandpa!"

They worked together all morning. Grandpa showed Rosie how to coordinate her movements - one paw to hold the seed packet, one paw to sprinkle seeds, then both paws to pat the soil gently.

"Coordination means working together," Grandpa explained. "Your paws work together, and we work together too!"

As they planted, Rosie carefully watered each seed. "I'm taking care of the earth by giving these seeds a good home," she said proudly.

"That's environmental care," Grandpa smiled. "We help nature, and nature helps us."

Days passed, and Rosie kept her grateful routine. Every morning on the peaceful porch, she would say thank you for three things. She watered her seeds with coordination and care.

Soon, tiny green sprouts peeked through the soil. Then colorful flowers bloomed - red, yellow, and purple!

"Look, Grandpa!" Rosie called excitedly. "My thank you garden is growing!"

The porch was more beautiful than ever. Rosie had learned that gratitude made everything better - even problems could become wonderful new beginnings.

"Remember, Rosie," Grandpa said, "grateful hearts grow the most beautiful gardens of all."

Rosie hugged her grandpa tight, feeling thankful for everything around her.

The End.
