# Poppy Panda Shares from the Heart

Poppy Panda lived in a cozy treehouse that smelled like sweet bamboo and pine. Sunlight peeked through the leafy roof and made tiny golden dots on the wooden floor. Poppy was a patient panda who loved to sit on a soft pillow and listen to the wind whisper, shhh, shhh.

One bright morning, Poppy opened a big tin of crunchy bamboo biscuits. “Mmm,” Poppy hummed, taking a deep breath. “They smell toasty and yummy.” The biscuits made a tiny crackle sound when Poppy lifted the lid. Poppy’s tummy gave a gentle rumble.

Just then, friends arrived. Tilly Tortoise tapped up the steps, slow and steady. Bibi Bird fluttered in, her feathers swishing. Max Monkey swung to the window and plopped inside, giggling. “Hi, Poppy!” they chirped and chattered.

Poppy smiled. “Welcome to my treehouse! I have biscuits.”

Max peeked at the tin. “Can I have one now? Please!” His eyes were big and round.

Tilly nodded. “They smell nice.”

Bibi fluffed her wings. “Tweet! I would like a tiny one.”

Poppy felt excited. The biscuits were Poppy’s favorite. Poppy wanted to eat them all, right now. But then Poppy remembered: be patient, be kind. Poppy took a slow panda breath in, and a slow panda breath out. “I can wait,” Poppy told self softly. “I can be generous.”

Poppy said, “Let’s make a biscuit plan so everyone gets a fair share.”

Max bounced. “A plan?”

“Yes,” Poppy said, patting the floor. “We will sit in a circle. We will use careful hands. We will take turns. We will use kind voices.”

Tilly smiled. “That shows respect.”

Bibi chirped, “Tweet! That feels safe.”

Poppy opened the tin. Crunch! The sound was happy. The smell was warm and sweet. “First, we each choose one biscuit,” Poppy said. “Then we taste together.”

Max reached fast. His fingers zoomed to the biggest biscuit. The biscuit tumbled and almost fell. Poppy noticed a swirl in her belly. The swirl felt like a small storm. Poppy paused. Poppy put a paw on her tummy. “Slow breath in,” Poppy whispered. “Slow breath out.” The swirl calmed.

Poppy spoke gently. “Max, please slow down. We are taking turns. We use careful hands.”

Max blinked. “Oh. I forgot.” He pulled his hand back. “Sorry, Poppy.”

“Thank you for listening,” Poppy said. “That shows respect.”

They tried again. Poppy passed the tin to Tilly. “You may choose first today.”

Tilly picked a small round biscuit. “Thank you,” she said.

Bibi chose a tiny crumbly heart. “Tweet! Thank you!”

Max chose a star biscuit. He held it with both hands. “I waited,” he said proudly.

Poppy picked a square biscuit. “Good waiting,” Poppy said. Poppy counted, “One, two, three—taste!”

Crunch, crunch, crunch. The biscuits were delicious. The treehouse filled with happy munching sounds.

Soon, there were only three biscuits left. Max looked at the tin. “I want another star,” he said. He reached again, a little quick.

Poppy felt a tug in her chest. “I want one, too,” Poppy thought. “But I can be generous.” Poppy took a patient breath. The wind outside went shhh, shhh, helping Poppy calm.

Poppy said, “Friends, let’s check in. Is anyone still hungry?”

Tilly patted her shell. “I am a little hungry.”

Bibi peeped, “Tweet! I am a tiny bit hungry.”

Max raised his hand. “Me too!”

Poppy nodded. “We have three biscuits and four friends. Hmmm.” Poppy tapped a paw softly. “I have an idea. We can share. We can break the biscuits.”

Poppy broke one biscuit into four even pieces. Snap. “We each get a piece,” Poppy said. “That is fair.”

They took turns breaking the other biscuits. Snap. Snap. The pieces made a neat circle. Everyone had the same amount. Max smiled. “Sharing feels good.”

Tilly nodded. “This is generous.”

Bibi chirped, “We are kind and calm.”

Poppy tasted a small piece. Sweet crumbs tickled Poppy’s tongue. Poppy felt warm and proud. “Being generous helps everyone,” Poppy thought. “It helps me, too.”

After snack, Max wanted the shiny red paintbrush for a craft. Bibi wanted it, too. Their voices rose like little sirens. “Mine!” “No, mine!”

Poppy felt that tummy swirl again. Poppy remembered the plan. “Slow breath in. Slow breath out.” Poppy said, “Friends, we can use our plan for taking turns. We can set a timer leaf.”

Poppy took a big leaf from the shelf and set it by the brush. “When I say go, Max paints for the time it takes the leaf to slide to the floor.” Poppy gently dropped the leaf, letting it float and land. “Then Bibi’s turn.”

Max watched the leaf float down. “Okay, I can wait.”

Bibi nodded. “Tweet, I can wait, too.”

They took turns. The paintbrush swished, swish, swish. The colors looked bright like candy apples and sunny lemons. Everyone smiled.

When the craft was done, Bibi flapped happily. “Thank you for the plan.”

Tilly said, “Poppy, you were calm and kind.”

Max high-fived Poppy. “You shared your biscuits and the brush. That was generous!”

Poppy’s heart felt big and light. The treehouse seemed extra cozy. The wind whispered, shhh, shhh. Poppy said, “Today we learned three things: Be generous. Respect others. And use calm breaths to help our bodies listen.”

Poppy gave each friend a gentle hug. “I love to share with you.”

“We love to share with you, too!” they all said.

They cleaned up together, humming a happy tune. The tin clicked closed. The paintbrush sparkled clean. The sun made golden dots dance on the floor as the friends waved goodbye.

Poppy sat on the soft pillow and smiled. “Sharing from the heart makes our treehouse a kind place,” Poppy whispered. Poppy took one more slow, peaceful breath.

The End.
