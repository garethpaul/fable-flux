# Ollie Otter’s Rooftop Parade

Ollie was a playful otter with soft, shiny fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny afternoon, Ollie climbed a safe stairway to the rooftop of his apartment building. The roof felt warm on his paws. A gentle breeze tickled his whiskers. He could smell flowers from the garden below and hear birds chirp, “Tweet, tweet!”

“Wow,” Ollie said, wiggling with joy. “The rooftop is a big open place. It feels like the sky is close!”

Neighbors came up too. The rooftop was a friendly place where everyone shared. There was Ms. Amina with her colorful kites. There was Mr. Chen with his bamboo flute. Maria had maracas that went shake-shake-shake. Jamal brought a drum that went boom-boom. Luna twirled ribbons that swished in the air.

Ollie clapped his paws. “Let’s have a rooftop parade!”

“Great idea,” Maria said, shaking her maracas. “We can share our music.”

“We can dance together,” Jamal added, tapping his drum. “Boom-boom, tap-tap!”

Luna smiled. “I can teach ribbon twirls!”

Ollie felt big excitement bubble in his tummy. He loved to play. He loved to splash and dash and zoom. He wanted to lead the parade. He wanted it to be the best parade ever.

“We start here,” Ollie said, pointing to a chalk line. He drew a swirly path with blue chalk. “We march, we play, we cheer!”

Everyone nodded. The sun shone bright. The breeze smelled like fresh bread from the bakery below. Pigeons cooed, “Coo, coo.”

Ollie picked up a shiny whistle. “Ready?” he asked. “One, two, three!”

He blew the whistle. Tweet!

The parade began. Mr. Chen played soft notes on his flute. The notes floated like feathers. Maria’s maracas went shake-shake. Jamal’s drum went boom-boom. Luna’s ribbon made a rainbow swirl. Ms. Amina’s kite tugged the string and fluttered, flap-flap.

Ollie ran to the front, bouncing high. He spun in a happy circle. But when he spun, he bumped into Luna’s ribbon. The ribbon tangled with the kite string. Uh-oh! The ribbon tugged the kite. The kite tugged Jamal’s drum strap. The drum strap tugged Maria’s maracas bag. The maracas spilled and rolled. One maraca rolled close to the roof edge.

Everyone gasped. “Oh no!”

Ollie froze. His tummy felt tight. His whiskers drooped. “I… I didn’t mean to,” he whispered.

Luna pulled her ribbon back. Ms. Amina grabbed her kite string. Jamal caught his drum. Maria reached for the rolling maraca, but it kept rolling, rolling…

“I can get it,” Maria said, stepping forward.

Ollie saw the edge. He felt a worried shiver. He took a deep breath. “Stop,” he said, loud and clear. “That is not safe. We need a plan.”

Maria paused and nodded. “You are right. Safe is smart.”

Ollie held up his paw. “I made the mess. I need to fix it. But I need help. Can we do it together?”

Jamal smiled. “Teamwork!”

They each had an idea. Mr. Chen said, “We can use my long flute case like a scoop.” Luna said, “My ribbon can help pull it closer.” Ms. Amina said, “I can guide from over here.” Maria said, “I will wait and catch it.” Jamal said, “I can hold the back of Ollie’s vest so he stays safe.”

Ollie looked at everyone. He felt warm and brave. “Thank you,” he said. “Let’s try.”

They moved slowly. Jamal held Ollie’s vest, strong and steady. Luna slid her ribbon along the floor, swish-swish, and looped it around the maraca. Mr. Chen used the flute case like a gentle scoop. Ms. Amina called out, “A little left. A little right. Good, good!” Maria waited with open hands.

The maraca wobbled, then rolled into the flute case. Mr. Chen lifted it. Maria caught it with a smile. “Got it!”

Everyone cheered. The pigeons flapped, as if clapping. Ollie’s tight tummy loosened. He sighed, “Phew.”

Ollie turned to the group. His voice was small but clear. “I am sorry. I ran too fast. I did not look. I bumped the ribbon. I made a tangle and a scare.”

Luna touched his paw. “Thank you for saying sorry.”

Ms. Amina nodded. “You also spoke up to keep us safe. That was smart.”

Jamal grinned. “And we solved it together.”

Ollie’s whiskers lifted. He felt proud and calm. “Next, we can make parade rules,” he said. “Slow feet near the edge. Eyes open. Ask before spinning. We take turns leading.”

Everyone agreed. They said the rules together. Slow feet. Eyes open. Ask before spinning. Take turns leading.

“Also,” Maria added, “we can share our different music and dance. Each turn can be from a different friend.”

“That is a great idea,” said Mr. Chen. “I will start with a soft song.”

He played a tune from his home country. It sounded like wind in tall grass. Then Ms. Amina flew a kite pattern her grandma taught her. It made a dancing shadow. Maria shook a lively beat she learned from her family. Jamal added a deep drum rhythm. Luna twirled a ribbon dance she learned from her aunt.

Ollie listened. He watched. He felt the warm sun and the cool breeze. He could hear, see, and feel the joy of everyone’s special ways. When it was his turn, he did a small, careful otter wiggle. He asked, “Is it okay if I spin now?”

“Yes,” they laughed. “Spin!”

Ollie spun, slow and safe. No bumps this time.

The rooftop parade was beautiful. It was full of music, color, and kind hearts working together.

Ollie smiled. “Teamwork makes our parade safe and fun. Saying sorry helps hearts feel better. Speaking up keeps us safe.”

Everyone cheered, “Teamwork!”

The End.
