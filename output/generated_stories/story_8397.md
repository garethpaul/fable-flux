# Rocco’s Sparkly Song

Rocco was a musical raccoon with bright, curious eyes and little paws that loved to tap rhythms. He lived near a cozy house with a big garage workshop. Inside the workshop were tools that hummed, jars that clinked, and boxes that smelled like wood and lemon soap. Rocco called it his music studio.

One sunny morning, Rocco padded in and sniffed the warm, dusty air. “Good morning, drums! Good morning, broom!” he sang. He wore his tiny red bandana and carried a small spoon to tap on things.

Clink, tap, ding! The spoon made happy sounds on a metal bucket. “I love this song,” Rocco giggled. He rubbed his nose and felt a tickle. “Achoo!” He looked at his paws. They were smudged with oil and dust.

Rocco’s friend, a little wind-up robot named Zip-Zap, rolled out from under a shelf. “Beep-beep! Hello, Rocco,” buzzed Zip-Zap. “Are you ready to practice your big concert song?”

“Yes!” said Rocco. “But first, I want to explore.” His whiskers wiggled. “I am curious!”

Rocco climbed onto a soft pile of rags. They smelled like lemon and clean soap. He patted a wooden box. Thunk-thunk! He shook a jar of screws. Rattle-rattle! He touched a sticky roll of tape. “Ew!” he squeaked, making a face. “My paws feel yucky.”

Zip-Zap blinked. “Beep. Yucky paws make messy notes.”

Rocco tried to play his tiny harmonica. He placed it to his mouth and blew. Instead of a clear toot, he tasted dusty metal. “Bleh!” He stuck out his tongue. “This tastes like old pennies.”

From the corner, the old radio crackled. The radio voice was gentle. “Good morning, makers! Remember: wash up before you work and wash up when you’re done.”

Rocco’s ears perked. “Wash up?” He looked at his paws again. There were little black smudges on his fur and a smudge on his cheek, too. He looked in a shiny saw blade and saw his reflection. “Oh,” he whispered, “I am dirty.”

Zip-Zap spun in a circle. “Self-care time!” he chimed.

“What is self-care?” asked Rocco.

“Beep-beep. Self-care means taking care of you,” said Zip-Zap. “Clean paws, rested body, happy heart.”

Rocco nodded. “I want clean paws and a happy heart.” He sniffed the air. He could smell soap and warm water from a sink at the back of the workshop. He skipped over, leaving little dusty paw prints behind.

At the sink, there was a pump of foamy soap and a soft towel. Rocco pressed the pump. Squish! A cloud of foam puffed into his paws. It felt slippery and cool. “Hello, bubbles,” he giggled. He rubbed his paws together. Swish, swish, swish! The bubbles made tiny crackling sounds. He washed between his fingers. He scrubbed his thumbs. He even scrubbed his wrists.

“Sing your washing song,” said Zip-Zap.

Rocco sang in a bright voice, “Top and bottom, front and back, scrub-a-dub and never slack!” He counted to twenty, tapping his foot to keep the beat. The warm water whooshed over his fur. The dirt slid away. The water sounded like a quiet rain. Rocco’s nose twitched. Now he smelled lemon and clean air.

He dried his paws on the soft towel. Pat-pat-pat. The towel felt cozy. He looked in the shiny saw again. He could see his stripes and his bright eyes. No more smudges! He smiled. “I feel fresh!”

“Beep-beep. Fresh paws make bright music,” said Zip-Zap.

Rocco went back to his instruments. He picked up the harmonica again. He took a deep breath and blew. Toot-le-dee! The sound was clear and sweet. It filled the workshop like sunshine. He tapped the spoon on the bucket. Ding-ding-dong! He added a shaker he made from an old jar with beans inside. Rattle-shake! Rattle-shake!

Rocco felt happy. “My music is shiny,” he said. He lifted his bandana and wiped his forehead. “I will keep my body clean, so my songs can sparkle.”

He looked around the workshop. There were crumbs from his snack, a trail of dusty paw prints, and a drip of sticky tape on the floor. Rocco frowned a little. “I made a mess when I was curious.”

“Curiosity is good,” said Zip-Zap. “Beep. Curiosity helps us learn. And cleaning up is part of self-care, too.”

Rocco nodded. “I can be curious and clean.” He found the little broom and the dustpan. Swish, sweep, swish. He brushed the crumbs away. He wiped the sticky spot with a damp cloth. Wipe-wipe-wipe. He even put the screws back in the jar. Rattle-plink.

While he cleaned, he sang, “Care for my body, care for my space, clean hands, clean tools, a happy place!”

When the floor was tidy, Rocco felt proud. He stood tall. His tail swished. “I am ready for my concert.”

Zip-Zap flashed his lights. “Three, two, one… music!”

Rocco played his harmonica, tapped his spoon, and shook his bean jar. The sounds blended like a friendly choir. Toot-le-dee, ding-dong, rattle-shake! He smiled so big his cheeks felt warm. The workshop smelled fresh and sounded bright.

When the song ended, Rocco bowed. “Thank you, thank you.” He patted his chest. “Today I learned something important. When I take care of me—wash my paws, clean my space, and rest when I need to—my music is better, and my heart feels calm.”

Zip-Zap whirred softly. “Beep-beep. Self-care is a daily adventure.”

Rocco giggled. “Tomorrow, I will explore again. I will be curious and careful. I will wash, I will clean, and I will shine.”

The End.
