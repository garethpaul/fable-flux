# Tali the Giraffe Plans a Dinner

Tali the giraffe had a long neck, soft spots, and very kind eyes. She was thoughtful. She loved to think and plan. One sunny evening, Tali stood in the dining room and sniffed the air. It smelled like warm bread, sweet tomatoes, and a tiny sprinkle of pepper. The wooden table felt smooth under her hooves. The curtains were yellow and made soft swishy sounds in the breeze.

Tonight was special. Grandma Giraffe was coming for dinner. She would share an old family tradition: Story Soup Night. Everyone would eat soup, and everyone would share a story.

Tali looked at the table. She wanted the dinner to be just right. She wanted to make Grandma proud. But the soup pot was empty. The bread was small. The napkins were all different colors. Tali took a deep breath.

“I can do this,” Tali said to herself. “I will use what I have. I will make a plan.”

She got a pencil and a big piece of paper. She wrote her plan in big, simple steps:

1. Count what we have.
2. Use it in a smart way.
3. Ask for help.
4. Make it cozy.
5. Share stories.

Tali smiled. “This is resourceful planning,” she said. The word felt big and strong in her mouth. She liked it.

First, she counted. She counted six bowls. “One, two, three, four, five, six.” She counted four spoons. “Oh no,” she said gently. “We need two more spoons.” She counted three carrots, two tomatoes, one onion, and a tiny bunch of noodles. She looked in the bread basket. “One little loaf,” she said, tapping it softly.

Tali did not feel worried. She felt ready.

“Step two,” she said. “Use it in a smart way.” She broke the bread into six small slices. She put each slice in a warm napkin. The napkins were blue, green, yellow, pink, orange, and purple.

“They do not match,” Tali said, tilting her head. Then she smiled. “They are rainbow napkins! That is fun.”

She moved to the stove. She peeled the onion. It smelled sharp and made her eyes sting a little. “Hello, onion,” she giggled, blinking. She chopped the carrots and tomatoes into tiny pieces so they could go farther. Plink, plonk, plink went the little pieces into the shiny pot. She added water and a pinch of salt. She stirred with care. Swish, swish, swish.

Grandma Giraffe peeked in. “Mmm,” Grandma said. “That smells cozy.”

Tali lifted her head. “Grandma, I am being resourceful. We only have a few things, but I am using them well.”

Grandma smiled. “That is our family way,” she said. “When I was little, we made Story Soup with only a handful of noodles. We planned. We shared. We always had enough.”

Tali’s ears perked up. “That is our tradition,” she said. “We can share it tonight.”

She looked back at her list. “Step three: ask for help.”

She called, “Mama, could you bring two spoons from the picnic basket?”

Mama Giraffe came in with a clink and a smile. “Two spoons, coming right up!”

“Papa,” Tali said, “can you fill the water pitchers? Cold water makes the soup taste even better.”

Papa filled the pitchers. The water glug-glug-glugged and sounded fresh.

“Step four: make it cozy,” Tali said. She put a small candle in the middle of the table. She set the rainbow napkins with the warm bread on each bowl. She put a tiny flower in a jar. She turned on soft music. The dining room felt warm, bright, and safe.

Soon the soup bubbled gently. The smell was sweet and savory. Tali added the noodles very slowly and stirred again. Swish, swish. She tasted a tiny spoonful. “Yum,” she said. “It needs a little more pepper.” She sprinkled just a pinch. Achoo! She laughed.

Everyone came to the table—Grandma, Mama, Papa, and Tali. They sat together. The bowls were full, and the bread was warm. The rainbow napkins made everyone smile.

Grandma lifted her spoon. “Tonight we share our Story Soup tradition,” she said. “We take small things, plan with care, and make them enough for all.”

Tali nodded. “Step five: share stories.”

Mama spoke first. “When I was little, I learned to draw with short pencils. I planned where each line would go. My small pencils made big pictures.”

Papa told a story about fixing a wobbly chair with string and patience. “I made a plan, tried, and tried again,” he said.

Tali’s turn came. Her voice was soft but strong. “Tonight I made a plan. I counted. I used what we had. I asked for help. I made it cozy. We shared our tradition. We had enough.”

Grandma touched Tali’s hoof. “You were very resourceful, dear.”

Tali felt proud. Her heart felt warm, like the soup. She tasted another spoonful. It was simple and good. The bread was crunchy on the outside and soft inside. The dining room glowed. The breeze made the curtains whisper.

Tali looked at her list one last time. She drew a big happy star at the bottom.

She said, “When we plan and share, small things can be big enough.”

Everyone nodded. They ate, laughed, and told more stories until the candle made tiny, sleepy flickers.

The End.
