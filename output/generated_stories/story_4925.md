# Benny Bear's Bathroom Truth

Benny was a fluffy brown bear cub who loved playing with his toy boats in the bathtub. Every evening, his mama would call, "Bath time, Benny!" and he would splash and giggle in the warm, bubbly water.

But tonight was different. Benny had been playing outside all day, and his paws were muddy. His fur was sticky with tree sap, and he had grass stains on his tummy. When Mama Bear called for bath time, Benny looked at himself in the bathroom mirror.

"Oh no," he whispered to his reflection. "I'm really, really dirty today!"

Mama Bear knocked gently on the bathroom door. "Benny, sweetie, are you washing up?"

Benny looked at the warm water filling the tub. He dipped one paw in and felt how nice and cozy it was. But then he had a silly idea. Maybe he could just splash around quickly and tell Mama he was all clean!

"Yes, Mama!" Benny called out. He splashed loudly in the water for just two minutes, making lots of noise. Then he hopped out, shook his fur, and wrapped himself in his soft yellow towel.

When he opened the bathroom door, Mama Bear smiled. "All clean, my little cub?"

Benny's tummy felt funny and twisty. He looked down at his paws - they were still muddy! His fur still had sticky sap in it. But he nodded anyway. "Yes, Mama. All clean!"

The next morning, Benny woke up feeling itchy and uncomfortable. The dried mud made his paws feel rough, and the sticky sap pulled at his fur when he moved. When he went to play with his best friend Rosie Rabbit, she wrinkled her little pink nose.

"Benny, you smell like old leaves and dirt," Rosie said gently. "Are you feeling okay?"

Benny's ears drooped. He felt embarrassed and uncomfortable. All day, the sticky, dirty feeling bothered him. He couldn't enjoy playing tag because his fur felt yucky. He couldn't concentrate on building block towers because he kept scratching.

That evening, when Mama Bear called for bath time again, Benny sat on the bathroom floor feeling sad.

"Mama," he called softly. "Can you come here?"

Mama Bear opened the door and saw her little cub looking unhappy. "What's wrong, Benny?"

Benny took a deep breath. His heart beat fast, but he knew what he needed to do. "Mama, I need to tell you the truth. Yesterday, I didn't really take a good bath. I just splashed for a tiny bit and said I was clean. But I wasn't clean at all!"

Mama Bear sat down beside Benny and gave him a gentle hug. "Thank you for telling me the truth, sweetheart. That was very brave and honest of you."

"I feel yucky and uncomfortable," Benny admitted. "And Rosie said I smell bad. I think being honest about my bath would have been much better."

Mama Bear nodded wisely. "When we're honest, even about little things like bath time, we feel good inside and outside. Shall we take a really good bath now?"

Benny nodded eagerly. This time, he used soap to scrub his muddy paws until they were clean and soft. He shampooed his fur until it was fluffy and sweet-smelling again. He even cleaned behind his ears!

When he finished, Benny felt wonderful. His fur was soft, he smelled like lavender bubbles, and best of all, his heart felt light and happy.

"Being honest feels so much better than pretending," Benny said, hugging Mama Bear.

"That's right, my wise little cub," Mama Bear smiled. "Honesty always makes everything better."

The End.
