# Pixel Paws and the Rainbow Snack Plan

In a cozy bedroom with soft blankets and a twinkly night-light, a helpful robot dog named Pixel Paws wagged his shiny tail. His nose-light blinked blue, and his little metal ears whirred softly. The room smelled like clean sheets and warm apples. Crayons and paper rested on a low table. A gentle hum from Pixel Paws made the room feel safe.

Mia sat on her bed, hugging a pillow. “I don’t like the green snack,” she whispered. Her voice was small. Her tummy made a little rumble. “Mama says the green snack gives me strong muscles. But I don’t like it. I feel bad.”

Pixel Paws tilted his head and beeped kindly. “Beep-boop, I hear you, Mia. You feel worried about the green snack.” His voice was warm and calm. “Let’s talk about it.”

Mia nodded. “I want to be strong. But the green snack tastes funny. I wish it would say sorry to my tongue.”

Pixel Paws blinked his nose-light. “Your feelings matter,” he said. “You can say what you need. That is brave. We can find a way.”

Mia sat up a little taller. “Can I tell Mama I don’t like it?”

“Yes,” said Pixel Paws. “That is self-advocacy. You can use kind words and a brave voice.”

Just then, Mama peeked into the cozy bedroom with a tray. “Snack time,” she said. The tray held a little cup of green peas, a slice of orange carrot, and a red strawberry shaped like a heart. The colors looked bright and happy.

Mia’s nose wrinkled. She looked at the peas. “Mama,” she said, “I don’t like the green snack. It tastes too squishy.”

Mama sat beside her and stroked her hair. “Thank you for telling me, Mia,” Mama said gently. “I accept your feelings. Your body is yours. Peas are healthy, but we can try something else green. We can keep trying, little by little.”

Pixel Paws wagged. “Beep-beep! Teamwork time,” he chirped. “Let’s make a Rainbow Snack Plan!”

He clicked his paw and a tiny light shone on the paper. “First,” he said, “we accept how Mia feels. That is step one.” He drew a big heart with a green crayon. “Second, we try different shapes and sounds. That is persistence. We keep going, a little each day.”

Mia smiled. “Can we make a green smiley face?”

“Yes!” said Mama. She opened a small lunch box. “I have green cucumber circles. Crunchy, not squishy.”

Pixel Paws placed a small plate on Mia’s blanket. “Listen,” he said softly, “crunch-crunch!” He tapped a cucumber slice with his paw. “Hear the sound?”

Mia sniffed the cucumber. It smelled cool like rain. She touched it. It felt smooth and cold. “It makes a funny little squeak,” she giggled.

“You can lick it first,” Mama said. “Just a tiny try.”

Mia licked. “Hmm.” She thought. “It’s not bad. It’s crunchy like a little drum!”

Pixel Paws made a drum beat with his paw. “Boom-tap, crunch-crunch!” He wagged. “Creative expression helps our feelings. Let’s make food art!”

Together, they made a happy face on the plate: cucumber circles for eyes, a carrot smile, and a pea for a nose. Pixel Paws clicked a photo. “Artist Mia!” he beeped.

Mia took a teeny bite of cucumber. Crunch. She smiled. “I did it!” Then she took a teeny bite of carrot. Crunch-crunch. “I like orange crunch.”

Pixel Paws looked at the peas. “The green peas are shy,” he said, whispering. “Maybe they want to be part of a game.”

Mia nodded. “Let’s tuck them in a blanket!” Mama brought a little spoon of soft yogurt. They rolled two peas in a tiny yogurt “blanket” and placed them on top of the cucumber “pillow.”

Mia tried one tiny pea. She closed her eyes. “I’m a brave taster,” she said. “It’s still a little squishy. But I tried.”

Mama hugged her. “I am proud of your brave try. I accept that you don’t love peas today. We can try again another day, or try a different green.”

Pixel Paws wagged. “That is persistence,” he said. “Little tries make big skills.”

Mia nodded. “Can we make a Rainbow Snack Chart?” she asked. “I can put stickers when I try.”

“Yes,” Mama said. She brought a sheet of star stickers. Together they drew a rainbow: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple. Mia put a star near green. “I tried a tiny bite,” she said proudly. “I listened to my body and my words.”

Pixel Paws curled at Mia’s feet, warm and gentle. “Beep,” he said. “Acceptance means we love ourself and others, even when we like different things. Nutrition means we give our body colors and crunch. Persistence means we keep trying, small and kind.”

Mia nibbled a strawberry heart. Sweet juice tickled her tongue. “I feel happy,” she said. “I can say what I need. I can try new things, a little bit.”

Mama tucked the blankets. The night-light glowed like a tiny moon. Pixel Paws hummed a sleepy tune. Mia whispered, “Thank you, Pixel. Thank you, Mama. I am brave. I am kind. I am me.”

Pixel Paws’ nose-light blinked softly. “And that is perfect,” he said.

The End.
