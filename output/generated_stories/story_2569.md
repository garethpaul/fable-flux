# Dottie the Dolphin Visits the Garden Shed

Dottie was a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a curious smile. One sunny morning, a gentle wave carried Dottie up a winding stream that flowed right to a little garden. There stood a small, cozy garden shed that smelled like pine wood and fresh dirt. Bees hummed nearby. The air felt warm. The shed door was open just a crack.

“Hello?” Dottie called, splashing her tail in a puddle left by the sprinkler. “Is anyone home?”

Milo the mouse peeked from a watering can. “Eep! A dolphin? In the garden?”

“Yes!” Dottie giggled. “I’m visiting today. I want to help. I want to learn. And I want to play!”

From the shed, Rosie the rabbit hopped out. She wore tiny garden gloves. “We are fixing tools,” Rosie said. “But some tools are too big for my paws.”

Dottie looked inside the shed. It was full of smells and sounds. She smelled mint, soil, and wooden handles. She heard drip, drip, drip from a leaky hose. She saw a wheelbarrow, a broken rake, a stack of flower pots, and a shiny red button on a little machine.

“I can help,” said Dottie. She swished her tail and made a little splash. “But I am a dolphin. I need water. And I need to know what to do.” Her voice was kind but clear.

Rosie nodded. “That is good self-advocacy, Dottie. You are saying what you need.”

Milo skittered to a bucket and tapped it. “We can bring water to you!”

Together, they filled a low tub with cool water from the hose. Dottie slid in with a happy wiggle. “Ahh,” she sighed. The water felt smooth and safe.

Now Dottie could help. She peeked at the shiny red button. “What does this do?”

“That is our tiny tool cleaner,” Rosie said. “It spins and scrubs. But it wobbles, and I get scared to start it.”

Dottie looked at the wobbly legs of the machine. She felt a flutter in her tummy. She took a slow breath. “I feel a little nervous too,” she said. “I want to try, but I need help. Can we make a plan?”

Milo’s whiskers twitched. “A plan! Yes!”

They made a simple plan, step by step.

- Step one: Make the machine steady.
- Step two: Tell each other what we need.
- Step three: Start it together and stop if anyone feels unsafe.

Dottie looked around. “We need something to keep the legs still,” she said. “What can we use?”

Rosie pointed to a stack of rubber bands, a soft mat, and two bricks by the door. Dottie’s eyes lit up. “Innovation time!” she sang.

With careful moves, they placed the soft mat under the machine. Milo rolled the bricks, one by one, like tiny pushing dances. Dottie held steady with her tail pressing a board. “We are coordinating,” Dottie said. “We move together.”

Rosie smiled. “I will be the talker,” she said. “I will say start and stop. That is my job.”

“I will watch the legs,” said Dottie.

“I will watch the lights,” said Milo.

“Ready?” Rosie asked. Her voice was warm. “If anyone feels worried, say ‘Pause, please.’ That is self-advocacy.”

Dottie lifted her fin. “Pause, please,” she practiced. The words felt strong and safe. Milo practiced too. “Pause, please!”

Rosie tapped the red button. The cleaner hummed to life. Whirr, whirr. The shed filled with a gentle buzz. The machine stayed steady on the mat. The air smelled like soap and metal.

Dottie watched the legs. “So far so good,” she said.

Milo watched the lights. “Green means go,” he whispered.

Rosie held up a dirty trowel. “Here we go,” she said, placing it in the cleaner. Swish, swish. Bubbles danced. Dottie clapped her fins. “It’s working!”

Then the hose dripped faster. Drip, drip, drip. A small stream tickled the floor. Dottie felt the water move. Her tummy fluttered again. “Pause, please,” she said in a clear voice.

Rosie stopped the button. Click. The hum faded. “Thank you for saying what you need,” Rosie said. “What do you need, Dottie?”

“I need the floor to be less slippery,” Dottie said. “And I need time to feel calm.”

Milo nodded and placed a towel on the wet spot. Rosie patted Dottie’s fin. “You did a brave thing. You spoke up.”

Dottie took a slow breath and smiled. “I feel ready now.”

They started again. Whirr, whirr. Together, they cleaned the trowel, a tiny shovel, and even a muddy spoon. Each time, they checked in.

“Are you okay?” asked Rosie.

“Yes,” said Dottie. “I feel safe.”

“Yes,” said Milo. “Green light!”

Soon, the tools were shiny and clean. The shed smelled fresh and a little soapy. Sunbeams danced on the walls.

Rosie hopped with joy. “We did it!”

Dottie splashed a happy splash. “We did it together. We made a plan. We tried a new idea. And we spoke up when we needed to.”

Milo wrapped a ribbon around the toolbox. “Team Daisy Garden!” he squeaked.

Dottie’s heart felt warm. “Today I learned that my voice matters,” she said. “When I say what I need, my friends can help. That is self-advocacy. When we share ideas, that is innovation. And when we move together, that is coordination.”

Rosie hugged Dottie’s fin. “Your brave words helped all of us.”

Dottie looked at the soft mat, the steady bricks, and the clean tools. The garden buzzed with happy sounds. She felt proud, calm, and ready for more adventures.

The End.
