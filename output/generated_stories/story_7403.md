# Rollo Raccoon’s Quiet Song

Rollo was a musical raccoon. He wore tiny blue headphones and a sparkly scarf. He loved to make songs with his small drum, his jingling bell, and his gentle ukulele. Today, Rollo sat in the family’s study corner. The lamp hummed softly. The rug felt fluffy under his paws. Crayons smelled waxy and sweet. Books rested in neat rows, like quiet friends.

“I will make a soft song,” Rollo whispered. He tapped his drum with one paw. Tap-tap. He strummed his ukulele. Plink-plonk. He shook his bell. Jing-jing.

Across the room, Mia sat at the table. She was drawing a big sun. The sun was bright and round. “I like your music,” Mia said with a smile. “But I need quiet to draw the tiny lines.”

Rollo nodded. “I can play softly,” he said. He tucked his tail in and played slow. Tap… plink… jing…

Just then, Max rushed in. Max was Mia’s little cousin. His shoes thumped on the floor. Thump-thump! He saw Rollo’s drum. His eyes got wide. “I want it! Mine!” Max cried. He grabbed the drum and hit it hard.

BOOM-BOOM-BOOM!

Mia jumped. Her crayon slipped. A zigzag line cut across her sun. “Oh no!” she gasped. Her eyes filled with tears.

Rollo’s ears drooped. His chest felt tight. The loud sound made his heart race. The lamp seemed too bright. The room felt messy and hot.

Rollo took a deep breath. In… out. He remembered a safety rule: Stop, look, and listen.

He put one paw up. “Stop,” he said calmly. “We need safe hands. No grabbing.”

Max clutched the drum. “But I want it now,” he pouted. His face looked stormy.

Rollo nodded slowly. “I hear you,” he said. “You want a turn. We can share. But we must be safe. We must be gentle. And Mia needs quiet in the study corner.”

Mia dabbed her eyes. “I worked hard,” she whispered. “The sun got a zigzag.”

Rollo turned to Mia. “That’s not fair,” he said softly. “We will fix it.”

He turned back to Max. “When we grab and bang, someone gets upset. That is not just. Justice means fair turns and safe choices.”

Max looked down at his shoes. He shuffled his feet. “I didn’t mean to scare you,” he muttered.

Rollo nodded again. “Thank you for saying that,” he said. “Let’s make a plan.”

He held up three furry fingers.

“Plan:
- First, we use gentle voices in the study corner.
- Second, we ask before we take.
- Third, we take turns with a timer.”

Mia sniffled. “Can we fix my sun?” she asked.

“Yes,” Rollo said. “I will play a quiet steady beat to help you feel calm.” He turned the drum to its soft side. Tap… tap… tap… like raindrops on a window.

Mia took a deep breath. In… out. She picked a yellow crayon. She traced over the zigzag and turned it into a happy sunbeam. “There,” she said. She smiled again. “Thank you, Rollo.”

Max watched. His shoulders loosened. “Can I have a turn after the timer?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Mia and Rollo together.

Rollo set a small sand timer on the table. The sand glittered like sugar. “When the sand is gone,” he said, “it’s your turn to play the drum gently. I will show you a quiet beat.”

Max nodded. “Okay. Safe hands. Gentle sounds.”

While the sand slipped down, Rollo taught a hand signal. He touched his lips for “quiet.” He opened his paw for “stop.” He pointed to his ear for “listen.” They practiced together. Quiet. Stop. Listen. It felt like a secret safety dance.

The lamp hummed. The rug hugged their feet. The study corner felt calm again. Rollo played a whisper song. Plink… plink… tap… Mia colored the sun with tiny, careful lines. Max waited, watching the last grains fall—shhh.

“Your turn,” Rollo said when the sand finished. He passed the drum with two paws. “Thank you for waiting.”

Max held the drum close to his chest. “I will be gentle,” he said. He tapped softly. Tap… tap… Smile.

Mia giggled. “That’s nice!”

Rollo grinned. “See? Safety sounds sweet.”

They made a “Study Corner Song” together:
“Stop, look, listen,
Safe hands, kind hearts.
Ask first, take turns,
That’s how our music starts.”

They sang it quietly and swayed. The study corner glowed warm and happy. Justice felt like sharing. Safety felt like calm. Conflict turned into a plan.

Rollo packed his bell and ukulele. “We learned today,” he said. “Safety first. Ask first. Take turns. Use gentle voices. Fix what we can.”

Mia held up her bright sun. “We made it better,” she said.

Max patted the drum softly. “I can wait,” he said. “I can be safe.”

Rollo’s tail made a soft whoosh on the rug. His heart felt light, like a song on a breeze.

The End.
