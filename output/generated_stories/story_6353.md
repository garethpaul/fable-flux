# Benny Bear's Garden Helper Day

Benny Bear was a fluffy brown cub who loved spending time on his family's sunny balcony garden. The morning sun felt warm on his fuzzy ears as he watered the bright red tomatoes and yellow marigolds with his little green watering can.

"Good morning, plants!" Benny said cheerfully, watching the water sparkle in the sunlight. "I hope you grow big and strong today!"

As Benny was putting his watering can away, he heard a tiny voice crying. "Help! Help!"

Benny looked around carefully. There, between two flower pots, was a small earthworm wiggling on the hot, dry concrete.

"Oh no!" said Benny. "Little worm, you look very sad. What's wrong?"

The earthworm sniffled. "I was trying to find some good soil to help the plants grow, but I got lost on this hard ground. The sun is so hot, and I can't find my way to the soft, cool dirt!"

Benny's kind heart felt worried for his new friend. "Don't cry, little worm. I want to help you! But I'm not sure how."

Benny thought hard, tapping his furry paw on his chin. Then he remembered something Mama Bear had taught him. "When you don't know something, it's okay to ask for help!"

"Mama!" called Benny. "Can you help me help someone else?"

Mama Bear came onto the balcony, wiping her paws on her apron. "What's happening, my sweet cub?"

Benny pointed to the earthworm. "This little friend needs to get to the soil, but I don't know the best way to help."

Mama Bear smiled proudly. "What a caring heart you have, Benny! Let's think together. What do earthworms need?"

"Cool, soft dirt!" said Benny excitedly.

"That's right! And look," Mama Bear pointed to their compost bin made from an old yogurt container. "We have perfect soil right here from our food scraps!"

Benny carefully picked up the earthworm with a small stick and gently placed him in the rich, dark compost soil. The earthworm wiggled happily.

"Thank you so much, Benny Bear!" said the earthworm. "This soil smells like banana peels and coffee grounds - my favorite! Now I can help make the soil even better for your plants."

"You're very welcome!" said Benny, feeling warm and happy inside. "I'm glad I could help you find a good home."

Mama Bear hugged Benny tight. "I'm so proud of you for noticing someone who needed help and asking for guidance when you weren't sure what to do."

Over the next few days, Benny watched as his plants grew bigger and greener. The earthworm was working hard in the compost, turning old food scraps into rich soil.

"Look, Mama!" said Benny, pointing to a new tomato bud. "The plants are growing so well!"

"Yes," said Mama Bear. "When we help others, everyone benefits. The earthworm is happy, our plants are healthy, and you learned that asking for help makes you a better helper too."

Benny beamed with pride as he watered his thriving garden. He had learned that helping others felt wonderful, that it was smart to ask questions when he didn't know something, and that even small acts of kindness could make a big difference.

"Thank you, little earthworm friend, for teaching me how good it feels to help!" Benny whispered to the soil.

From that day on, Benny always looked for ways to help others in his garden and everywhere he went.

The End.
