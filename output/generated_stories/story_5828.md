# Ruby's Closet Club

Ruby was a fluffy white rabbit with soft pink ears. She had made a special hideaway in the big closet under the stairs. It was her favorite place to think and play.

Ruby had filled her closet with colorful pillows, her favorite books, and a small lamp that made everything glow warm and cozy. She loved the way it smelled like lavender from her grandmother's sachets.

One sunny morning, Ruby heard tiny voices coming from her closet. She hopped over and peeked inside. Three little field mice were sitting on her pillows!

"Hey!" Ruby said loudly. "This is MY hideaway! You can't be here!"

The mice looked scared. The smallest one started to cry. "We're sorry," squeaked the middle mouse. "We just needed somewhere safe and warm."

Ruby felt her ears get hot with anger. She wanted to stomp her big feet and tell them to leave right away. But then she took a deep breath, just like Mama Rabbit had taught her.

"Why do you need somewhere safe?" Ruby asked more gently.

The biggest mouse spoke up. "Our home got flooded when the sprinkler broke outside. We have nowhere to go until it dries out."

Ruby's heart felt softer. She remembered when her own burrow got too muddy last spring. It had been scary and cold.

"I was being mean," Ruby said honestly. "I got angry because I thought you were trying to take my special place. But you just needed help."

The little mouse stopped crying. "We don't want to take your hideaway. We just need help."

Ruby's whiskers twitched as she thought. She was good at solving problems. "I have an idea! My closet is big enough for all of us. We can share it until your home is ready."

The mice's eyes grew wide with hope. "Really? You would share with us?"

"Yes," Ruby said with a warm smile. "But we need some rules so everyone feels happy and safe."

Ruby gathered everyone in a circle on the soft pillows. The closet felt even more cozy with friends inside.

"First rule," Ruby said, counting on her paws. "We take turns choosing what to do. Second rule: we use kind words, even when we feel upset. Third rule: we clean up together."

The mice nodded eagerly. "Those are good rules," said the biggest mouse. "Thank you for being so kind, Ruby."

For the next three days, Ruby's closet became the most wonderful place. They read stories together, shared snacks, and played quiet games. Ruby learned that the mice knew amazing songs. The mice learned that Ruby was great at telling funny jokes.

When it was time for the mice to return to their dry home, they all felt sad to say goodbye.

"Ruby," said the smallest mouse, "you taught us that sharing makes everything better. You're a great leader."

Ruby's ears perked up with pride. "You taught me something too. I learned that being different doesn't mean we can't be friends. Mice and rabbits can have fun together!"

From that day on, Ruby kept her closet door open. Sometimes the mice would visit for story time. Sometimes other woodland friends would stop by. Ruby discovered that sharing her special place made it even more special.

Every time Ruby felt angry or wanted to keep something all to herself, she remembered her mouse friends. She learned that tolerance and kindness always made her heart feel bigger and warmer than being mean ever could.

The End.
