# Hops on the Porch

The sun was warm and soft on the peaceful front porch. The wooden boards felt smooth under little feet. A joyful kangaroo joey named Hops sat on a bright blue cushion. Hops wiggled his ears and sniffed the fresh morning air. He could smell toast, jam, and a little bit of lemon from the pot of tea.

“Good morning, world!” Hops said, bouncing just a tiny bounce. “I’m ready for a porch adventure.”

Mama Roo brought a plate. “Toast for you, Hops,” she said, smiling. “And a small spoon of jam.”

“Thank you, Mama,” Hops said. “Can we listen to the porch radio?”

Mama Roo turned the little radio on. The radio clicked and buzzed, then played a friendly voice. “Hello from around the world,” said the voice. “Today, children everywhere are saying hello in many languages.”

Hops sat up tall. His tail thumped once. “Around the world?” he whispered. The wooden porch felt cool through his fur. He heard a bird sing, “Cheep, cheep,” from the fence. A breeze tickled his whiskers.

The radio said, “Bonjour means hello in French.” Hops tried it. “Bon-jour!” He giggled. The sound felt bouncy and sweet, like bubbles.

The radio said, “Hola means hello in Spanish.” Hops said, “Oh-la!” He laughed at his own voice. He liked learning words. It felt like opening little doors.

Mama Roo nodded. “Learning words helps us be good neighbors,” she said. “Even with neighbors far away. We share our world.”

Hops looked at the porch steps. A small ant was walking with a crumb. Another ant followed. “We all share,” Hops said. “Even ants.” He leaned closer and listened to the tiny crunch.

Just then, a little girl from next door peeked over the fence. Her name was Mina. She had bright eyes and a bright yellow hat. “Hi, Hops!” she called. “I made a card for my grandma in another country. But the wind blew it. It slid under your porch.”

Hops jumped to his feet. “Oh no!” he said. “Let’s help.”

They looked through the porch slats. A corner of a pink card peeked out. Hops felt brave and helpful. “I can reach,” he said. He stretched his small paws between the boards. He touched the card and pulled.

Rip.

The card tore a little bit at the corner.

Mina’s mouth made a small O. Hops’ tummy felt heavy. “I’m sorry, Mina,” Hops said right away. His ears drooped. “I didn’t mean to rip your special card.”

Mina looked sad but took a breath. “Thank you for trying,” she said softly. “I want to send it to Grandma so she knows I love her. She lives far away.”

Mama Roo sat beside them. “This is a chance to help,” she said. “Helping others is a big part of being a citizen of our world. We care for neighbors near and far.”

Hops nodded. “I want to help,” he said. “We can fix the card.”

He ran inside and came back with stickers, tape, and a blue crayon. Hops smelled the paper smell and the sweet sticker smell. “Let’s add a happy sun,” he said.

Mina smiled a little. “Yes,” she said. “And a heart.”

They taped the torn corner very gently. Hops pressed the tape smooth. Mina drew a little red heart. Hops drew a bright sun with rays like soft noodles. The card looked cheerful again.

Hops remembered the radio. “We can write hello in many languages,” he said. “That shows love to the big whole world.”

Mina’s eyes sparkled. “Let’s do it!”

Hops wrote, “Hello!” Mina wrote, “Hola!” Hops wrote, “Bonjour!” with his tongue poking out in careful work. They added “Namaste” and “Jambo” with Mama Roo’s help, saying each word slowly together. The porch felt full of happy sounds. Wind chimes tinkled. The leaves whispered. The radio hummed a gentle tune.

Mina held the card to her chest. “Now Grandma will know people everywhere care,” she said.

Hops took a deep breath. His chest felt light again. “Mina,” he said, “I’m sorry I ripped your card. Thank you for letting me help fix it.”

Mina nodded. “I forgive you,” she said. “You said sorry. You helped me. That is kind.”

Mama Roo poured tiny cups of warm lemon tea. The steam curled up and smelled bright. They clinked cups together, very carefully. Hops licked a drop from his lip. “Zing!” he said, giggling.

The radio voice came back. “Remember,” it said, “we are all part of one big world. When we help, listen, and share, we are good global citizens.”

Hops looked at the sky. A cloud drifted like a soft sheep. “I want to be a good global citizen,” he said. “I can learn words, help friends, and say sorry when I make a mistake.”

“And fix what we can,” Mama Roo added.

“And send love,” Mina said, holding her card.

They waved as Mina went home to mail the card. Hops sat back on his blue cushion. The porch boards were warm now, and they felt friendly. The bird on the fence sang again. Hops wiggled his ears and smiled wide.

“Today’s porch adventure was small,” Hops said, “but it reached far.”

Mama Roo hugged him. “That is how the world grows kinder,” she said. “One small porch, one small joey, one big heart.”

Hops closed his eyes for a moment and listened to the breeze, the chime, and the faraway hum of the world. He felt proud and peaceful.

The End.
