# Rooftop Hops and Calm Breaths

Sunny Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey. Sunny had soft brown fur, bright eyes, and springy feet that loved to hop. One morning, Sunny climbed the stairs with little thump-thump sounds and went up to the rooftop. The roof garden smelled like fresh mint and warm sun. A gentle breeze tickled Sunny’s whiskers.

“I have a big goal,” Sunny said, patting a small paper badge on their chest that read, “I Can Do It.” “Today, I will hop across the rooftop from the flower box to the green bench. Hop, hop, hop!”

Sunny looked at the flower box. It was bright and red, with tiny yellow blooms. Far away, the green bench shone like a friendly frog. The space between looked long. Sunny’s tummy fluttered, like a tiny bird flapping.

“I can do this,” Sunny whispered. “I will be calm. I will use my own steps.”

Sunny bent knees. Boing! Sunny hopped once. The rooftop felt warm and a little bouncy under their feet. A little cricket sang, “Chirp, chirp,” in the corner. Somewhere, a dog barked down on the street, “Woof!” The world felt big. Sunny paused.

“I feel wiggly,” Sunny said. “When I feel wiggly, I breathe.” Sunny put a paw on their belly. “In… out… slow and soft.” The breeze puffed Sunny’s ears. The sun kissed Sunny’s nose. The flutter in the tummy grew smaller.

Sunny made a plan, right there on the roof. “Step one,” Sunny said, holding up one tiny finger, “I will make little hops.” “Step two, I will keep my eyes on the green bench.” “Step three, when I feel wiggly, I will pause and breathe.” Sunny smiled. “Step four, I will try again.”

Boing. Little hop. Boing. Little hop. Sunny counted, “One. Two. Three.” The petals on the red flowers brushed Sunny’s tail. “Hee-hee, that tickles!” Sunny giggled. The giggle made Sunny feel lighter.

Halfway there, Sunny stopped. The green bench looked closer, but Sunny’s legs felt tired. A small worry crept in like a gray cloud. “What if I cannot do it?” Sunny’s tail drooped.

Sunny listened. Chirp, chirp. Woof! A bird flew by and called, “Cheer-up!” The kind sounds reminded Sunny of their goal. Sunny put both paws on the warm roof. “I can help myself,” Sunny said in a brave, small voice. “I can calm my body. I can choose my next hop.”

Sunny closed eyes for a moment and took three soft breaths. “In… out. In… out. In… out.” Sunny opened eyes and saw the bench. The green paint looked shiny. The bench seemed to smile.

From a nearby chimney, a friendly pigeon waddled closer. “Coo?” the pigeon asked.

Sunny waved. “Hi! I am hopping to the bench.”

“Coo-coo,” said the pigeon. “Slow and steady.”

Sunny nodded. “Yes. Slow and steady is my plan.” Sunny felt proud for making a plan. Sunny felt strong for choosing calm.

“Ready,” Sunny told their own feet. “Little hops.” Boing. Boing. Boing. The breeze whispered. The sun warmed Sunny’s back. The sound of the city hummed below, like a soft drum.

Sunny came to a puddle left by last night’s rain. It was shiny like a mirror. Sunny peered in and saw a tiny joey face. “Hello, helper me,” Sunny said to the reflection. “We can do this.”

Sunny bent knees and hopped over the puddle with a gentle splash. Tiny drops kissed Sunny’s fur. “That was fun!” Sunny grinned, tail swishing.

At the last stretch, Sunny felt the flutter try to return. “This is the far part,” Sunny said. “I will use my steps.” Sunny breathed in slow. Sunny breathed out slow. Sunny kept eyes on the green bench. Sunny chose three small hops.

“Hop one,” Sunny whispered. Boing.

“Hop two.” Boing.

“Hop three!” Boing!

Sunny landed beside the bench with a soft thump. Sunny touched the smooth green seat. “I did it!” Sunny cheered. The pigeon flapped and cooed. The cricket chirped a happy song. Sunny’s heart felt big and bright, like the sun.

Sunny climbed onto the bench and sat tall. The rooftop garden smelled sweet. The air tasted like mint and sunlight. Sunny swung small feet and patted the paper badge. “I set a goal. I made a plan. I stayed calm. I moved my own body. I helped myself.”

Down below, the dog barked again, but it sounded friendly now. Sunny waved at the sky. “Thank you, calm breaths. Thank you, careful hops. Thank you, me.”

The pigeon nodded. “Coo-coo. You did the work.”

Sunny smiled. “Yes. When I feel wiggly, I can pause. I can breathe. I can choose small steps. I can try again. I can rely on me.”

Sunny stood and did a tiny happy dance on the bench. Tap, tap, tap went little feet. The green paint felt cool. The breeze ruffled Sunny’s ears like a hug. Sunny looked back at the red flower box and then at the long space crossed with small, brave hops.

“I will make a new goal tomorrow,” Sunny said. “Maybe I will hop to the wind chime and listen to its song.”

Sunny hopped down, calm and proud, ready for a sip of cool water and a rest.

The lesson was clear in Sunny’s heart: Stay calm, make a plan, take small steps, and trust yourself. With self-reliance and gentle movement, big goals can be reached, one little hop at a time.

The End.
