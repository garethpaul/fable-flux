# Ollie the Otter’s Sunny Backyard Challenge

Ollie was a playful otter with shiny whiskers and a wiggle-waggle tail. He lived by a small pond next to a sunny backyard. The grass was warm. The air smelled like fresh flowers. Birds chirped, “Chee-chee!” Bees hummed, “Bzzz!” The day felt bright and happy.

Ollie popped up from the water and clapped his little paws. “Today I will learn a new trick!” he said. “I will build a splash course and slide, all by myself.”

In the sunny backyard, a sprinkler sprayed tiny rainbows. A plastic tub, some soft pool noodles, and three buckets lay in the grass. A big cardboard sign said, “Backyard Splash Course!” There were simple steps drawn on the sign with little pictures.

Ollie read the first picture. “Step 1: Put the pool noodles in a line.” He smiled. “Easy-peasy!”

He pushed one noodle. Squeak! He pushed another. Thump! The noodles rolled and wobbled. “Hee-hee! Wiggly noodles!” Ollie giggled. He liked to make silly shapes. He made a noodle circle. He made a noodle zigzag. He made a noodle hat and put it on his head.

Just then, a small turtle named Tessa peeked from behind a flower pot. “Hi, Ollie,” she said in a tiny voice. “Can I watch?”

“Sure!” said Ollie. “I’m building my splash course. I can do it all by myself.” His chest puffed up. He wanted to feel proud.

On the fence, a bluebird named Bree fluttered her wings. “Tweet! I can help read the steps,” Bree offered. “I’m good at directions.”

Ollie paused. He liked Bree and Tessa. But he wanted to show he could do it. “Thank you,” he said, “but I think I’ve got it.”

He skipped to the next picture on the sign. “Step 2: Put the tub at the end.” Ollie tugged the tub. Scrape, scrape. It was a little heavy. He pulled with all his might. His paws got wet and slippery. The tub slid and bumped into a bucket. Clonk!

Tessa blinked. “Do you want a push?”

Ollie shook his head. “I can do it,” he said, but his voice got small. His tail drooped a little.

He looked at the next picture. “Step 3: Fill the buckets and pour water on the slide.” Ollie filled a bucket. Splash! Water got on his fur. It felt cool and tickly. He tried to pour the bucket slowly, but it sloshed too fast. Whoosh! The water missed the noodle slide and soaked the grass.

Bree chirped softly. “Tweet-tweet. It’s okay, Ollie. New things take practice. Want me to read the steps out loud while you pour?”

Ollie’s whiskers drooped. He felt a little frustrated. His tummy did a tiny twist. “Maybe I do need help,” he whispered.

Tessa smiled. “We can be a team.” She moved slowly, steady as a rock. “I can hold the tub still.”

Bree tilted her head. “I can read the directions, one by one. We can follow them together.”

Ollie took a deep breath. The backyard smelled like mint leaves. The sun warmed his back. “Okay,” he said. “Let’s try as a team.”

Bree read, “Step 1: Put the pool noodles in a line.” Ollie and Tessa lined them up straight. Squeak, squeak. “Step 2: Put the tub at the end.” Tessa braced her shell. Ollie pushed. The tub slid into place. Thunk! “Step 3: Fill the buckets and pour slowly.” Bree counted, “One, two, three.” Ollie tipped the buckets just a little. The water trickled down the noodles, making a shiny, slippery slide. Drip, dribble, swoosh!

“Good job!” Tessa cheered.

Bree fluffed her feathers. “Tweet! Step 4: Test the slide with a small toy first.”

Ollie placed a tiny rubber duck at the top. “Ready, ducky?” he giggled. He touched it with one paw. The duck glided down. Wheee! It plopped into the tub with a happy plip.

Ollie’s eyes sparkled. “It works!”

“Step 5: Take turns,” Bree read. “And be kind.”

Tessa blinked slowly. “You go first, Ollie. It’s your idea.”

Ollie shook his head. “We made it together. Let’s cheer for each other.” He looked at his friends. “Teamwork means we all shine.”

Tessa went first. She slid gently on her tummy, gliding like a leaf on water. “Wheee,” she said, very softly. Bree took a tiny splash with her feet and fluttered, “Tweet-wheee!”

Then it was Ollie’s turn. He put his belly on the noodles. He counted, “One, two, three!” He pushed off. The water was cool and slippery. Whoosh! He zipped down and landed in the tub with a splash. Splish-splash! Water sparkled in the sun. Ollie grinned so big his whiskers wiggled.

“I did it!” he said. “We did it!”

Bree nodded. “You listened to the directions. You worked with friends. That’s smart and kind.”

Tessa added, “And you didn’t give up.”

Ollie’s heart felt big and warm. “I feel proud,” he said. “My body is small, but my try is big.”

He looked at the sign again. He traced the pictures with a wet paw. “Following directions helped me. Teamwork helped me. And trying again helped me believe in me.”

Bree chirped, “That’s self-esteem, Ollie! Feeling proud when you try, learn, and do your best.”

Ollie smiled at his friends. “I am strong. I am kind. I can learn new skills.”

The sun shone brighter. The sprinkler made a little rainbow. The backyard buzzed and chirped and laughed. Ollie, Tessa, and Bree took turns on the splash course, cheering for each slide.

They cleaned up together when playtime was done. Buckets stacked. Noodles piled. Tub tipped. The grass smelled sweet and clean.

Ollie patted his heart. “Today I learned I can do hard things when I listen, when I’m patient, and when I work with friends. I like me.”

His friends smiled. “We like you, too,” they said.

The End.
