# Koala Kiki Speaks Up

Kiki was a calm koala who loved soft breezes and warm tea. She sat on her peaceful front porch every morning. The porch smelled like eucalyptus leaves and sweet bread. Wind chimes sang tinkly songs. The sun felt cozy on Kiki’s fur.

Today was Craft Day on the porch. Kiki set out yarn, paper, stickers, and safe, blunt craft scissors. “I will make kind cards,” Kiki said. “Cards that say, ‘I hear you’ and ‘Please and thank you.’”

Soon friends arrived. Benny Bunny hopped, thump-thump. Tessa Turtle shuffled, tap-tap. Momo Magpie swooped down with a shiny button in her beak. “Hello!” they all called.

“Welcome to my porch,” said Kiki with a gentle smile. “We will craft and share old family ways. My gran taught me to braid kindness into every craft.”

Benny reached for the scissors fast. Snip! Snip! The sound was sharp. Paper flew. Kiki blinked. The scissors were special. They were a gift from Gran. Kiki felt a tight flutter in her tummy. She took a deep breath. In for four, out for four. The wind chime tinkled. Calm washed over her.

Kiki practiced self-advocacy. She spoke up for herself, in a clear and kind voice. “Benny,” she said, placing a soft paw on the table, “those scissors are special to me. Please ask before you use them.”

Benny froze. His ears drooped. “Oh! I forgot,” he said. “I’m sorry, Kiki.”

“Thank you for listening,” said Kiki. “You may use them after I finish this card. I can share the green scissors now.” She slid a green pair toward Benny.

Benny smiled. “Okay! I can wait.” He picked up the green scissors. Snip-snip, softer this time.

Tessa Turtle reached for the yarn ball. It rolled off the table. Plop. The yarn bounced across the porch and into a tiny puddle. “Oh no,” Tessa whispered. Her eyes got wet.

Kiki’s ears heard the small sniff. She scooped up the yarn and patted it dry with a towel. “Accidents happen,” she said. “We can still use this yarn for a rainbow braid. My gran taught me a tradition: when yarn gets splashed, we braid it with sunshine.” Kiki held up yellow yarn. “This is our sunshine.”

Momo Magpie set down the shiny button. “I brought this from my nest,” he chirped. “In my family, we add one bright thing to every craft. It means, ‘We share light.’”

Kiki beamed. “Thank you, Momo. We will add your bright button.”

They crafted together. The porch smelled like glue and oranges from Kiki’s tea. Paper rustled. Yarn brushed their paws and wings, soft as clouds. Kiki showed a simple braid. “Over, under, over, under,” she said, humming. “Breathe in, breathe out.”

Soon, the table got crowded. Elbows bumped. Momo’s feathers ruffled. Benny’s whiskers twitched. Tessa’s shell tapped the cup. Klink.

Kiki felt a squeeze in her chest. The space was too tight. She wanted room to move her paws. She remembered to speak up again. Calm voice. Clear words.

“Friends,” Kiki said, “I need a little more space. I feel squished. Please move your crafts two paw steps away from mine.”

Benny hopped back-two steps. Tap-tap. “Like this?”

“Yes, thank you,” said Kiki.

Tessa slid her mat. “Is this better?”

“Much better,” Kiki said with a smile.

Momo fluffed his feathers. “I can craft on the railing. I like the breeze!”

Kiki’s body felt light again. She sipped warm tea and sighed, “Ahhh.” The wind chime sang a happy ting-ting.

They kept crafting and talking about family traditions. Tessa said, “My grandma hums while she works. It helps her feel brave.” Tessa hummed a gentle tune. Hum-hum-hum.

Benny said, “My uncle says, ‘Ask first, then use.’ He taught me to wait my turn.” Benny looked at Kiki and grinned.

Momo said, “My aunt says, ‘If there’s a fuss, make a plan, not a peck.’ We use peaceful words.”

Kiki clapped softly. “These are wonderful traditions. Let’s put them on our cards.”

They made Kindness Cards with simple messages:
- Please ask first.
- I need space.
- I can help.
- Let’s make a plan.
- I hear you.

Kiki carefully glued Momo’s shiny button to the card that said, “I hear you.” The button gleamed in the sun.

Then a small problem popped up. Benny wanted the rainbow braid. Tessa wanted it too. Their eyes grew worried.

Kiki saw the worry and took a breath. She modeled self-advocacy again. “I hear both of you,” she said. “I need the porch to feel peaceful. Let’s use our cards to solve this.”

Benny held up “Ask first.” Tessa held up “Let’s make a plan.”

Benny said, “Tessa, may I use the braid first for my card?”

Tessa nodded. “Yes. Then it can go on mine.”

Kiki smiled. “What a peaceful solution. Thank you for asking. Thank you for sharing.”

At last, the crafts were done. The porch looked bright and cozy, with cards lined up like little flags. The air smelled like orange tea and dry yarn. The wind chime gave a happy song.

Kiki looked at her friends. “Today we learned to speak up kindly,” she said. “We asked. We waited. We made space. We helped. We shared our family traditions.”

Benny, Tessa, and Momo nodded. “We can do this at home too,” they said.

Kiki hugged her friends with a warm, gentle squeeze. “When we use our voices, we take good care of ourselves and each other.”

They sipped tea, nibbled sweet bread, and watched the sun go down, soft and gold.

The End.
