# Monkey Max Finds His Calm

Monkey Max loved the sunny backyard. The grass felt soft under his toes. The warm sun tickled his fur. Flowers smelled sweet, like honey. Birds sang, “Tweet-tweet!” and a little breeze whispered, “Whoosh.”

Max was a courageous monkey. He liked climbing the low tree, watering the garden, and washing his shiny red bucket. Today, Max had a big plan. He would build a leaf-and-stick fort and then wash his hands before snack time. He felt proud and excited.

“Hi, sun! Hi, birds!” Max waved. “I can do this myself.”

He picked up sticks. He gathered big green leaves. He set down his shiny red bucket. Clink, clink, clink went the sticks. Rustle, rustle went the leaves. Max hummed a happy tune.

Then—uh-oh! The wind puffed. Whoosh! The leaves tumbled down. His stick wall fell over.

Max’s belly felt tight. His cheeks felt hot. “My fort!” he cried. “It broke!”

He stomped one foot. He wanted to yell. He wanted to throw a stick. His eyes felt watery.

Max put a hand on his chest. He remembered something Nana Monkey always said, “Feel it, name it, tame it.” Max whispered, “My feelings matter.”

He sat on the soft grass. He closed his eyes for a moment. He took a slow breath in. One banana breath in. He blew out slowly. One banana breath out.

“In… and out,” Max said softly. “I feel mad and sad.”

The sun warmed his fur. The breeze cooled his face. He listened to the birds. Tweet-tweet. His belly loosened a little. The tight feeling got smaller.

Max opened his eyes. “I can be brave on the inside too,” he told himself. “I can take care of me.”

He looked at the sticks and leaves. “The wind knocked it down. That is what wind does,” he said. “I can try again.”

Max smiled a tiny smile. “New plan,” he said. He moved closer to the low tree where it was less windy. He pressed the sticks deeper into the dirt. Poke, poke. He tucked the leaves between the sticks. Tuck, tuck.

“Good job, Max,” he whispered. “You are calm and strong.”

A little ant crawled by. “Hello, Ant,” Max giggled. “I am building a fort. I had big feelings. I took banana breaths. Now I feel better.”

The ant wiggled its feelers and marched on. Max kept working. His fort stood taller. The wind came again, but softer here. Whoosh. The leaves rustled but stayed put.

Max clapped. “I did it!”

He felt proud in his heart. He felt steady in his belly. He felt brave in his brain.

Just then, a muddy puddle by the garden splashed. Plop! Mud dotted Max’s hands and arms. Squish-squish. “Oh no,” Max laughed. “I am dirty!”

Max remembered another rule. “Clean hands, healthy body,” Nana said. Max liked taking care of himself.

He picked up his shiny red bucket. He filled it with water from the little hose. Splish-splash. The water felt cool on his fingers. He took a tiny bit of soap. Bubble-bubble! He rubbed his hands. Palm to palm. Between his fingers. Around his thumbs. Over his wrists. He sang, “Scrub-a-dub, scrub-a-dub, wash, wash, wash!”

The bubbles smelled like lemons. The sun made them sparkle. Pop, pop, pop went the tiny bubbles.

Max rinsed his hands in the bucket. Swish. He shook them dry. Drip, drip. He used a small towel. Pat, pat.

“Clean and calm,” Max said with a grin. “I can help myself.”

He sat beside his leaf-and-stick fort and ate his snack. Crunch went the apple. Sweet juice dribbled on his chin. He wiped it with his towel. Birds sang. The breeze hummed. Max felt peaceful.

A butterfly floated by. “Hello, Butterfly,” Max said. “Today I learned about feelings. When I felt mad and sad, I took banana breaths. I said how I felt. I made a new plan. I built my fort. I washed my hands. I took care of me.”

The butterfly fluttered. Max waved and took another crunchy bite.

Soon Nana Monkey peeked over the fence. “How’s my courageous Max?” she asked.

Max pointed to the fort. “I had a problem. The wind knocked it down. I felt big feelings. I breathed in and out. I moved my fort. I kept trying. I cleaned my hands, too!”

Nana smiled. “You noticed your feelings. You calmed your body. You solved your problem. And you practiced good hygiene. That is brave.”

Max’s heart felt warm, like a sunbeam. He hugged Nana. “Feel it, name it, tame it,” he said proudly.

Nana nodded. “Yes. And remember: calm bodies make smart choices.”

Max looked at his fort, his clean hands, and his shiny red bucket. He took one more banana breath. In… and out. He whispered, “I can do hard things. I can take care of me.”

The sun glowed. The breeze whooshed softly. The birds sang good-bye.

Max waved to the day. “Thank you, feelings,” he said. “Thank you, calm.”

The End.
