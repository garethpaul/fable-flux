# Splash the Dolphin Speaks Up

Splash the dolphin was visiting his grandma's house for the first time. Grandma Dolphin had a special guest room just for him! The room had a big, round pool with soft blue water. It had colorful sea plants and smooth rocks to play with.

But there was one big problem. The water was too cold!

Splash dipped his fin in the water. "Brrr!" he said, pulling it back quickly. The water felt like ice cubes! His teeth chattered. "Ch-ch-ch-ch!"

Splash tried swimming anyway. He jumped in with a big SPLASH! But the cold water made him shiver all over. His whole body went "shake, shake, shake!"

"This is too cold," Splash said to himself. "But maybe I should just be quiet. Maybe dolphins are supposed to like cold water."

So Splash tried to play. He picked up a smooth rock, but his fins were too cold to hold it. The rock went PLOP back into the water. He tried to swim through the sea plants, but he was shivering too much to swim straight.

Splash felt sad and cold. His tummy felt funny too - like butterflies were swimming inside it.

Just then, Grandma Dolphin peeked into the guest room. "How are you doing, little one?" she asked with a warm smile.

Splash opened his mouth to say "Fine," but then he stopped. He remembered what Mama Dolphin always told him: "Use your voice, Splash. Tell others what you need."

Splash took a deep breath. "Grandma," he said in his clearest voice, "the water is too cold for me. I'm shivering and I can't play. Could you please help me make it warmer?"

Grandma Dolphin's eyes grew wide. "Oh my stars!" she said. "I'm so sorry, dear one! I forgot to turn on the water heater. Thank you for telling me!"

Grandma Dolphin swam to a special button on the wall. She pressed it with her fin. "This will warm up your water," she said kindly. "You were very brave to speak up!"

Soon, warm water began flowing into the pool. Splash could feel it getting warmer and warmer. The water felt like a cozy hug!

"Ahhhh," said Splash, relaxing his whole body. "This feels perfect!"

Now Splash could play! He swam in happy circles. He tossed the smooth rocks. He played hide-and-seek behind the sea plants. The warm water felt wonderful on his skin.

"Splash," said Grandma Dolphin, "I'm proud of you for telling me what you needed. Speaking up was the right thing to do!"

Splash's chest felt warm and happy - not just from the water, but from being brave. "I was scared to ask," he said. "But I'm glad I did!"

"Sometimes grown-ups don't know what you need," said Grandma Dolphin. "When you use your voice and ask for help, you take good care of yourself. That's what big dolphins do!"

Splash smiled his biggest smile. He had learned something important: when something isn't right, it's good to speak up! Asking for help isn't being difficult - it's being smart and brave.

From that day on, whenever Splash needed something, he remembered to use his voice. And his visits to Grandma's guest room were always perfectly warm and wonderful!

The End.
