# Gigi the Giraffe Says “I’m Sorry”

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with long, gentle eyelashes and warm brown spots. She lived in a bright home with a cozy home office. The home office had a soft blue rug, a little desk with crayons, and a big window that let in sunshine. The air smelled like paper and crayons and a little bit like cinnamon tea.

Gigi liked to help. She also liked to make plans. One sunny morning, Gigi clacked her hooves softly on the floor and said, “Today is list day!” Her tail swished. She made a list on a sticky note. “Draw a rainbow. Build a block tower. Call Grandma. Share the tablet with my friend, Sam.”

Sam the squirrel was coming over. Sam had quick little paws and a happy chatter. When he arrived, Gigi heard a tap-tap-tap at the door. “Hi, Gigi!” Sam chirped. His nose wiggled. He smelled the cinnamon tea and smiled.

“Hi, Sam! Come to the home office. We can take turns with my tablet,” Gigi said. She remembered the last line on her list.

On the desk, the tablet was in a soft case. It was shiny and made a quiet click when Gigi pressed the button. A friendly ping sounded. Gigi’s eyes sparkled. She loved the drawing game. Her long neck bent to see better.

Sam climbed onto a little stool. “Can I go first?” he asked in a small voice.

Gigi felt a wiggle of wanting in her tummy. She really, really wanted to go first. She reached out her hoof. “I’ll just do a tiny turn,” she said. The screen lit up with bright colors. Gigi tapped red, then orange. “I’ll make a rainbow!”

Sam’s little paws folded in his lap. He waited. He swung his tail. His eyes were patient at first, but then they drooped. “Is it my turn now?” he asked.

“Almost,” Gigi said. She added yellow, then green. The colors made soft swishy sounds as she drew. Her ears heard the happy chime of the game. She felt cozy and excited. She forgot the time. She forgot she had said they would take turns.

Sam’s paws fidgeted. He tried to smile, but his whiskers drooped. “I wanted to draw a tree,” he whispered.

Gigi looked up. She saw Sam’s droopy whiskers. She heard his quiet voice. Her own tummy felt tight. Her heart made a slow thump. She remembered her list: Share the tablet with my friend, Sam.

“Oh,” Gigi said softly. “I did not take turns. I said ‘almost’ again and again. That was not fair.”

Sam blinked. He looked at his feet.

Gigi took a deep breath. The air smelled like cinnamon and crayons. She felt brave and kind inside. She put the tablet down. The case made a soft thud on the desk. Gigi looked at Sam and said, “I’m sorry. I did not take turns. I will make it right.”

Sam’s ears perked a little. “How?” he asked.

“I will give you a long turn,” Gigi said. “You can go first now. I will wait. I will watch. When you are done, you can tell me it’s my turn. That is taking turns. That is honest and kind.”

Sam’s tail swished. “Okay,” he said. His voice sounded lighter.

Gigi pulled the stool closer for Sam. She even made a cozy waiting spot on the rug for herself. She held a soft pillow. It felt squishy and cool under her chin. She hummed a tiny song while she waited. Hummm-hummm. Waiting felt a little hard, but it also felt right.

Sam tapped the screen. The game chimed. He drew a big green tree. Swish, swish. He added a brown trunk. Thock, thock. He made red apples. Pop, pop, pop. He glanced at Gigi. “Do you like it?” he asked.

Gigi smiled. “I love it. You are doing great.” Her words were warm. She meant them. Being honest felt good.

Sam drew a blue bird on a branch. He giggled. Then he looked at Gigi. “Your turn,” he said. He handed her the tablet with both paws.

Gigi’s heart felt light. “Thank you,” she said. “Thank you for waiting before, and thank you for letting me fix my mistake.” She drew a rainbow above the tree, but this time she asked, “Should we add a sun together?”

“Yes!” Sam said. They touched the screen together. Ding! A bright yellow sun smiled in the corner. The home office glowed with happy light. They both laughed.

Later, Gigi called Grandma. “Grandma,” Gigi said, “I forgot to take turns. I made Sam sad. I said, ‘I’m sorry,’ and I made it right. We shared. We told the truth. We took turns.”

Grandma’s voice was warm like tea. “That is integrity,” Grandma said. “That means doing the right thing, even when it is hard. You made amends. You fixed your mistake. I am proud of you, my thoughtful giraffe.”

Gigi’s cheeks felt warm and happy. “I will remember,” she said.

That night, Gigi added a new sticky note to her desk. It said, “Take turns. Say sorry. Make it right.” She looked at Sam and smiled. Sam smiled back. Their picture on the tablet showed a tree, a rainbow, and a sun, all shining together.

The End.
