# Ollie Otter's Porch Problem

On a sunny morning, Ollie the otter wiggled his way up to Mrs. Chen's peaceful front porch. The wooden boards felt warm under his furry belly, and he could smell the sweet flowers blooming nearby.

"What a perfect place for a nap!" Ollie said, stretching out his paws.

But just as he closed his eyes, he heard a tiny voice crying. "Help! Help!"

Ollie's whiskers twitched as he looked around. "Who's there?" he called softly.

"Down here!" squeaked the voice.

Ollie peered over the edge of the porch and saw a little mouse stuck in a flower pot. The pot was too tall and smooth for the mouse to climb out.

"Oh my!" said Ollie. "How did you get in there?"

"I was looking for seeds to eat," sniffled the mouse. "But I fell in and now I can't get out. I've been trying all morning!"

Ollie scratched his head with his paw. He wanted to help, but the flower pot was heavy and the mouse was very small. "Hmm," he thought out loud. "Let me think about this."

First, Ollie tried reaching down with his paw, but his arm wasn't long enough. "That didn't work," he said. "Let me try something else."

Next, he looked for a stick to help pull the mouse up. He found a small twig, but it was too short and weak. "This won't work either," Ollie said, not giving up.

Then Ollie noticed Mrs. Chen's watering can sitting nearby. His eyes lit up! "I have an idea!" he said excitedly.

"What is it?" asked the mouse hopefully.

"I'm going to tip this watering can very carefully so you can climb up the water like a ramp!" Ollie explained.

The little otter used his strong paws to slowly tilt the watering can. A gentle stream of cool water flowed into the flower pot. The water level rose higher and higher until the mouse could easily climb out.

"Hooray!" cheered the mouse, shaking the water droplets from his tiny fur. "You saved me! Thank you so much, Ollie!"

"You're very welcome," said Ollie, his whiskers twitching with happiness. "I'm so glad I could help."

The mouse's stomach rumbled loudly. "Oh dear, I'm still very hungry, but I'm afraid to look for food alone."

Ollie's kind heart felt warm. "Would you like me to help you find some safe seeds? I know where Mrs. Chen keeps the bird feeder."

"Really? You'd do that for me?" asked the mouse, his eyes sparkling.

"Of course! Friends help friends," said Ollie with a big smile.

Together, they found plenty of seeds that had fallen from the bird feeder onto the soft grass. The mouse ate happily while Ollie watched for any danger.

"You know," said the mouse between bites, "I learned something today. When I have a problem, it's okay to ask for help."

"And I learned that sometimes the best solution isn't the first idea you try," said Ollie. "It's important to keep thinking and trying new ways."

As the warm sun climbed higher in the sky, both friends felt proud and happy. They had solved a big problem together with kindness and clever thinking.

From that day on, whenever the mouse needed help or Ollie wanted to share an idea, they would meet on Mrs. Chen's peaceful front porch – the place where their friendship began.

The End.
