# Buzzy Beaver’s Bathroom Plan

Buzzy the busy beaver woke up with a wiggle in his tail and a rumble in his tummy. “I want breakfast. Crunchy carrot sticks!” he said. He hopped off his bed and skittered to the bathroom. The cool tiles felt smooth under his paws. Drip, drip, drip went the sink. Minty smells floated in the air.

Buzzy grabbed his toothbrush. “Brush fast! Eat fast! Go, go, go!” he chattered. He squeezed the toothpaste. It squished out with a soft ploop. A big blob oozed onto the counter. “Oops,” said Buzzy, eyes wide. The toothpaste was sticky and shiny.

His friend Dot the duck waddled in. “Good morning,” quacked Dot, fluffing her soft feathers. “I’m ready to brush too.”

Buzzy clutched the brush. “I will brush first. I am quick. I am busy!”

Dot tilted her head. “We can take turns. My beak needs bubbles too.”

Buzzy felt his heart thump-thump. He wanted to hurry. His tummy rumbled again. “Carrot sticks!” he squeaked. He reached for the sink at the same time as Dot. The soap slipped. The cup tumbled. Clink! Water splashed his whiskers. Cold drops ran down his nose.

Dot stepped back. “Let’s slow down,” she said softly. “We can make a plan.”

“A plan?” Buzzy rubbed his nose. He liked to build plans for dams, not brushing. But the bathroom floor was wet, and his paws felt slidey. He didn’t like the messy blob.

Dot pointed to a bright towel. “We can wipe the counter. We can breathe. We can take turns. Then we eat.”

Buzzy took a little breath. In, out. His chest felt a tiny bit softer. “Okay. A plan.” He wiped the counter with the towel. Rub, rub. The minty smell grew fresh again. “Step one, clean up,” he said.

Dot smiled. “Step two, slow breaths.” She showed him. “Smell the flowers.” She sniffed in. “Blow the bubbles.” She puffed out.

Buzzy copied. “Smell the flowers. Blow the bubbles.” He felt the air whoosh past his teeth. His shoulders dropped. His tail stopped swishing so fast.

“Step three,” Dot said, “take turns.”

Buzzy nodded. “You first,” he said. His voice was calm now. He set the toothbrush in a little cup and sat on the bath mat. It felt warm and soft. He listened to the gentle swoosh of the fan. Whoooosh.

Dot brushed. Swish, swish, swish. “Front teeth, back teeth,” she sang. Minty bubbles sparkled on her beak. She spit—ptoo!—into the sink and rinsed. “All done,” she said. She passed the brush to Buzzy. “Your turn.”

Buzzy squeezed a tiny pea-sized dot of paste. “Just a dot,” he said. “I can control the squeeze.” The toothpaste made a soft hiss as it came out. He put the brush in his mouth. “Swish, swish, swish,” he sang. “Little circles.” The mint tasted tingly. The sound of the bristles was gentle, like soft rain. His whiskers quivered. He spit—ptoo!—and rinsed. “All done,” he said proudly.

Dot clapped her wings. “You did it!”

Buzzy felt warm and happy. “I slowed down. I used my breaths. I took turns.” He patted his belly. It rumbled again. “Now carrots?”

“Almost,” said Dot. “Step four: preparation. We wash our paws.” She turned on the water. Buzzy felt the warm stream. He rubbed the soap. Squish, squish. Bubbles popped under his paws. Pop, pop, pop! He rinsed and dried on the fluffy towel.

Buzzy smiled. “Now we’re ready.”

They went to the kitchen. Buzzy crunched a carrot stick. Crunch! It was sweet and juicy. “Mmm,” he said. “Carrots help me grow strong.”

Dot nibbled a slice of apple. “Crunch, crunch,” she said. “Good for energy.”

Buzzy thought about the bathroom. He remembered the spill and his quick feelings. He remembered the plan. He looked at Dot. “Thank you for helping me slow down.”

Dot nuzzled his shoulder. “Friends help friends. We can practice self-control together.”

Later that day, their friend Pip the porcupine shuffled by the bathroom door. “I need to wash my paws,” Pip said shyly. “But you go first.”

Buzzy shook his head kindly. “We can take turns. Let’s make a plan.” He held up his paw. “Step one, clean up. Step two, smell the flowers, blow the bubbles. Step three, take turns. Step four, prepare and then enjoy our snack.”

Pip smiled. “That sounds calm.”

They followed the steps together. The bathroom stayed neat. The water sounded like a small song. Everyone felt peaceful.

Buzzy’s tail gave a happy thump. “When I feel rushy,” he said, “I can use my plan. I can breathe. I can wait. I can share.”

Dot and Pip cheered, “Team calm!”

Buzzy munched another carrot later. He took a small bite. He chewed slowly. Crunch, crunch, crunch. He tasted the sweetness. He felt full and proud. “Going slow helps me do things well,” he said.

That night, Buzzy brushed again. He used a tiny dot of toothpaste. He waited his turn. He breathed in flowers and blew bubbles. The bathroom smelled minty and clean. The floor was dry. His heart was quiet and steady.

Buzzy whispered to the mirror, “I can be busy and calm.” He waved to his friends. “See you tomorrow!”

The End.
