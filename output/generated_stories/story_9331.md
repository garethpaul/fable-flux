# Fawn’s Turn in the Busy Basement

In a cozy house, the basement was busy and bright. Lights hummed softly. A fan whirred. Boxes made little cardboard mountains. A soft rug felt warm on tiny hooves. A gentle deer fawn named Fawn stood at the top of the stairs and sniffed the air. It smelled like fresh soap, wood, and a little bit like crayons.

“I hear clink, clink,” Fawn whispered.

“Come on down,” called a friendly voice.

It was Owl, perched on a shelf. Mouse zipped by with a toy truck. Squirrel stacked blocks into a tall, wobbly tower. Cat stretched on the rug, swish, swish went the tail. The basement was full of sounds and friends.

“We are having basement club!” said Owl. “We have three stations today.”

Owl pointed with a wing. “Block Building. Art Table. Yoga Corner.”

Fawn’s ears perked up. “I want to do blocks! I want to build a tall tree!”

Squirrel hugged the blocks. “Me too! My turn!” Squirrel’s eyes were big and excited.

Mouse squeaked, “I want a turn to roll the truck up the block ramp!”

Owl blinked slowly. “We can all have turns. Let’s plan.”

“Plan?” Fawn asked. The word felt new and nice, like a soft blanket.

Owl nodded. “Planning helps us take turns. It helps us feel calm and strong.”

Fawn felt a little flutter in the tummy. What if my turn never comes? What if I forget my idea? The fan’s whirr sounded louder for a moment.

Cat padded over. “Breathe with me,” Cat purred. “In… and out.” Fawn breathed in. The basement smelled like crayons again. Fawn breathed out. The flutter felt smaller.

Owl set a sand timer on a box. “We will use this. When the sand is gone, we switch. Everyone gets a turn.”

“Okay,” said Squirrel, tapping the timer. “I start at blocks.”

“I will start at the art table,” Mouse said, holding a blue crayon. “Scribble, scribble!”

“I can start at yoga,” Cat stretched. “Stretch, reach, breathe.”

Fawn looked at the blocks again. “I will wait for my turn,” Fawn said, trying to stand tall. “I will plan my tree.”

“What do you need for your tree?” asked Owl.

“Green blocks for leaves,” said Fawn. “Brown blocks for the trunk. A tiny nest on top.”

Owl smiled. “Good plan. Let’s draw it.”

At the art table, Fawn held a fat green crayon. The paper felt smooth. “Swish, swish,” went the crayon. Fawn drew a tall tree with a little nest. “I made a plan!”

“Beautiful,” Mouse squeaked. “I will draw a blue ramp for my truck.”

At Yoga Corner, Cat and Owl stretched. “Mountain pose,” said Cat. Fawn tried it too. Hooves pressed into the rug. Fawn felt strong, like a little mountain inside. “I can wait,” Fawn told the mountain feeling.

The sand in the timer slid down, soft whisper, soft hush. “Switch,” Owl called.

Squirrel hopped to Yoga Corner. Mouse scurried to blocks. Fawn’s heart went thump. “Is it my turn?” Fawn asked.

Mouse nodded. “After me. I am building the ramp, then I will switch. Look!” Mouse rolled the truck up. “Vroom!”

Fawn watched. “Good ramp,” Fawn said. It felt kind to say it. Fawn’s turn would come. The plan was ready. The mountain feeling was still there.

The sand fell again. “Switch,” said Owl.

Mouse held out the truck. “Your turn, Fawn,” Mouse squeaked. “Want some green blocks?”

“Yes, please,” said Fawn. The blocks felt cool and smooth. Stack. Stack. Stack. Fawn placed brown blocks for the trunk. “Steady,” Fawn whispered. “I can do it.” Fawn added green blocks for leaves. Soft clack. Soft clack. On top, Fawn set a tiny nest with a little cotton ball.

Squirrel clapped. “It looks like a real tree!”

Cat purred. “Strong and gentle.”

Fawn smiled. The smile felt warm, like sunshine on fur. “Thank you for waiting for my turn,” Fawn said. “Thank you for helping me plan.”

“Your plan worked,” Owl said. “And you took turns. How do you feel?”

“I feel proud,” Fawn said. “And calm. And tall like my tree.”

They all switched again. Now Squirrel added a red apple block. Mouse parked the truck under the tree. Cat did a quiet pose called “Tree Pose.” Everyone giggled.

At snack time, Owl brought water and apple slices. “Wellness break,” Owl said. “Drink and rest.”

Fawn sipped cool water. “It tastes like tiny cold stars,” Fawn said. The apple slice was crisp. Crunch!

Owl asked, “What did we learn today?”

Fawn thought. The fan hummed. The light buzzed. The rug felt cozy.

“We learned to take turns,” Fawn said. “We made a plan. We waited. We helped. And we all got a turn.”

“And how did that feel?” Cat asked.

“Fair,” Squirrel said.

“Safe,” Mouse squeaked.

“Strong,” Fawn said, standing in mountain pose again.

Owl nodded. “Taking turns helps us all feel good. Planning helps us feel ready. Breathing helps us feel calm.”

Fawn nuzzled the block tree. “Tomorrow, I can wait again. I can plan again. I can take turns.”

“Yes,” said Owl. “And we can make more trees.”

Everyone laughed. The busy basement felt bright and friendly, like a happy song.

The End.
