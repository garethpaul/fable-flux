# Tiko the Wise Tortoise Rides Along

Tiko the wise tortoise had a smooth green shell and kind, curious eyes. He moved slowly, but he thought carefully. One sunny morning, Tiko climbed into a cozy family car with Mia, her little brother Ben, and their mom. The car smelled like orange slices and clean seats. The sunshine warmed the windows, and soft air whooshed from the vents.

“Seat belts on,” Mom said, clicking her buckle. Click! Click! Tiko did his best to buckle, too. He tugged the strap over his shell. “Safety first,” he said in a low, steady voice.

Mia wiggled. “I want to listen to my princess songs,” she said, her eyes bright.

“No!” said Ben, shaking his head. “I want to hear dinosaur roars!” He stomped his feet. Thump, thump.

The car hummed onto the road. The tires made a soft “rumble-rumble.” Birds chirped outside. Inside, the air felt tight, like a balloon too full. Mia crossed her arms. Ben puffed his cheeks. Mom looked into the mirror. “We have time for both,” she said. “Let’s ask Tiko for help.”

Tiko smiled and took a slow breath. In… and out. “I hear two good ideas,” he said gently. “Princess songs and dinosaur sounds. But we only have one speaker. Hm.”

“I want mine first,” Mia said quickly.

“No! Mine first!” Ben said, louder. His face turned red.

Tiko blinked slowly. “Let us try something,” he said. “First, let us listen with our ears to each other.”

Mia huffed. “But I already know what Ben likes.”

“Listening is respect,” said Tiko. His voice was soft, like warm tea. “Respect means we care about others. We look, we listen, and we think about feelings.”

Mom nodded. “Good idea, Tiko. Mia, Ben, take turns telling us what you like.”

Ben pointed to his toy dino. “I like the big loud ROAR,” he said. “It makes me feel brave.” He made a roar that tickled everyone’s ears. “Raaaawr!”

Mia giggled a little. “I like songs with twirls and sparkles,” she said. She swished her fingers in the air. “They make me feel happy and floaty, like a feather.”

Tiko nodded. “Thank you for sharing. When we listen, we learn. That is being open-minded.”

The car rolled past tall green trees. Sun dots danced on the dashboard. The warm air smelled like pine. Tiko tapped his chin. “Now we try to find a way that helps both of you,” he said. “That is called a compromise.”

“What’s a comp-… com-pro…?” Ben stumbled over the word.

“Com-pro-mise,” said Tiko slowly. “It means we both give a little, and we both get a little.”

Mia looked at Ben. Ben looked at Mia. They weren’t sure.

“Let us make a plan,” said Tiko. He held up one short, wrinkly finger. “Plan one: Princess songs first, then dinosaur sounds.” He held up a second finger. “Plan two: Dinosaur sounds first, then princess songs.” He raised a third finger. “Plan three: One song, then one roar, taking turns.”

Mia’s mouth made a tiny o. “I like plan three,” she said.

Ben scrunched his nose. “But… what if I have to wait?”

Tiko smiled. “Waiting is hard,” he said. “But waiting shows respect, too. While we wait, we can clap for the other person’s choice. That makes the wait feel kind.”

Mom switched on the music gently. “We can also use the timer,” she said, holding up her phone. “One minute for a song, one minute for a roar.”

Mia nodded. “Okay. I can clap for Ben.”

Ben nodded back. “I can clap for Mia.”

Tiko clapped his slow tortoise clap. Pat… pat… pat. “Shall we begin?”

The first minute began. Mia’s princess song filled the car with soft bells and a friendly voice. “La-la-la,” sang Mia, smiling. Ben clapped in time. Clap, clap, clap. The music felt like glittery soap bubbles popping in the air.

The timer ting! “Now me!” Ben said, but he didn’t shout. He took a breath like Tiko. In… and out. “Please?” he added.

“Of course,” said Mom, tapping the button. Dinosaur roars filled the car. “Roooar!” “Grrrr!” “Stomp! Stomp!” The sounds shook like thunder. Mia covered her ears for a moment, then lowered her hands and clapped along. Clap-clap! She pretended to stomp, too, and laughed.

Tiko watched them kindly. “You are respecting each other,” he said. “You are open-minded and trying new sounds. And you made a good compromise.”

The timer ting! Back to the princess song. Then ting! Back to the dinosaur roars. The car felt light now, like a kite in the sky. The sun warmed Tiko’s shell. The air from the vents felt cool on Mia’s cheeks. Ben’s feet stopped thumping and tapped in time instead.

Soon they reached a red light. The car slowed. Mia peeked at Ben. “Ben, do you want to try one of my songs with drums?” she asked. “It’s not too twirly.”

Ben grinned. “Okay! And do you want to try my dino song with soft roars? They’re not too loud.”

Tiko smiled wider. “You are negotiating. That means talking together to find what works for everyone.”

Mom pressed play. The new songs were a mix: gentle princess beats with little drums, and dinosaur songs with quiet growls. Mia and Ben both swayed. They were both happy. The car was calm. The world outside moved by like a painting.

At last, they pulled into the driveway. Click! Click! Seat belts came off. Mia hugged Ben. “Thank you for waiting and clapping for me.”

Ben hugged back. “Thank you for listening to my roars.”

Tiko nodded. “Today you learned a big lesson,” he said. “Respect means we listen, we care, and we share. When we are open-minded and we compromise, everyone feels good.”

Mia and Ben said together, “We can do that again next time!”

Tiko’s eyes twinkled. “Slow and wise wins the ride,” he said, with a little chuckle.

The End.
