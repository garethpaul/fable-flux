# Penny Panda's Puzzle Problem

In a cozy bedroom filled with soft pillows and warm sunlight, lived a patient panda named Penny. Her bedroom was her favorite place in the whole world, with fluffy blankets that smelled like lavender and toys scattered on the soft carpet.

One sunny morning, Penny woke up feeling excited. "Today is puzzle day!" she said to herself, stretching her black and white paws. She loved working on puzzles because they made her think and feel proud when she finished them.

Penny walked over to her toy box and pulled out a beautiful new puzzle. The box showed a picture of a rainbow over a green meadow with flowers. "This looks wonderful!" she said, her dark eyes sparkling with joy.

She sat cross-legged on her soft rug and opened the box. Out tumbled dozens of colorful puzzle pieces. But when Penny looked closer, her smile turned into a worried frown. "Oh my," she whispered. "There are so many pieces! This looks much harder than my other puzzles."

Penny felt her tummy get a little tight with worry. The pieces seemed to jumble together in a confusing pile. Some pieces were mostly blue, others were green, and some had tiny flower patterns. "I don't know where to start," she said, feeling overwhelmed.

For a moment, Penny wanted to put the puzzle away. But then she took a deep breath, just like her mama had taught her. "When I feel worried, I need to think step by step," she reminded herself.

Penny sat quietly and looked at the puzzle box again. "The picture shows me what it should look like when it's done," she said thoughtfully. "Maybe I can use this to help me!"

First, she decided to find all the edge pieces. "The edges are straight on one side," she said, carefully looking at each piece. She picked up pieces one by one, feeling their smooth edges with her paws. "This one is straight! And this one too!"

Soon, she had a small pile of edge pieces. "Now I feel better," Penny said, her worry starting to melt away. "I have a plan!"

Next, she looked at the colors. "All the blue pieces might be the sky," she reasoned. "And the green pieces could be the grass." She sorted the pieces into different color groups, making neat little piles on her rug.

As Penny worked, she felt calm and focused. When she couldn't find where a piece belonged, she didn't get upset. Instead, she would say, "That's okay. I'll try a different piece and come back to this one later."

Piece by piece, the puzzle began to take shape. The blue sky appeared at the top, then the bright rainbow colors, and finally the green meadow with tiny flowers. Each time Penny placed a piece correctly, she felt a warm, happy feeling in her chest.

"I did it!" Penny exclaimed as she placed the very last piece. The completed puzzle was even more beautiful than the picture on the box. She felt so proud and happy that she did a little dance on her soft carpet.

Penny's mama peeked into the room. "Wow, Penny! You solved that big puzzle all by yourself!"

"I felt worried at first," Penny shared honestly. "But I took deep breaths, made a plan, and took it step by step. When I felt stuck, I stayed patient and tried again."

"That's wonderful problem-solving," her mama said, giving her a warm hug that smelled like cookies.

From that day on, whenever Penny faced a problem, she remembered her puzzle lesson: take a deep breath, make a plan, and be patient with yourself.

The End.
