# Tiko the Tortoise and the Sparkly Sink

Tiko was a wise tortoise with a smooth, shiny shell. One sunny morning, Tiko waddled into the bathroom. The tiles felt cool under his little feet. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. “Good morning, bathroom,” Tiko said softly. “Today, I will finish my chores.”

Tiko had a list, neatly written on a sticky note. He read it slowly. “Brush teeth. Wash paws. Wipe sink. Hang towel.” He smiled. “One by one, I can do it.”

He peeked at the mirror. It was a little foggy and sprinkled with tiny water dots. “I will start by brushing my teeth,” he said. He squeezed a pea-sized dot of toothpaste. “Just a little,” he told himself. Minty smell tickled his nose. “Brrr! So fresh!” He used small circles. “Round and round, front and back,” he hummed. The brush made soft swish-swish sounds.

Tiko paused and looked at the sticky note. “Did I finish? Not yet! Spit, rinse, and put the brush away.” He did each step. “Done,” he said, placing the brush in the cup. He put a tiny check mark on his list. His eyes shone. “I can do it when I go slow.”

Next came washing paws. He turned the faucet. Cold water splashed. “Ooh! That’s chilly,” he giggled, then warmed it a little. He rubbed soap, and bubbles grew like little clouds. “Hello, bubble friends,” Tiko said. “Where are you from?” A bubble popped. “We travel all around the world on water,” he imagined it whisper. Tiko thought about children in faraway places washing hands too. “We all clean up. We all care,” he said kindly.

He sang a short song, “Tops and bottoms, all around, clean, clean, clean is a happy sound.” The water felt smooth. The soap smelled like lemons. Pop, pop, pop went the bubbles. He rinsed and turned off the faucet. “Click,” went the handle. He shook his paws, then dried them on the towel. Another check mark on the list. “Step by step,” Tiko said. “I finish my tasks.”

Now Tiko looked at the sink. There were toothpaste dots and splashy spots. He took a soft cloth. “Wipe sink,” he read. “I will make it sparkle.” But first, he had an idea. “I can use my creativity!”

Tiko made a tiny cleaning game. He gave each spot a name. “This one is Dot from Mexico. This one is Splish from Kenya. This one is Spark from Japan.” He smiled. “Dot, Splish, and Spark, it’s time to go home to the drain!” He hummed a steady rhythm, “Wipe, wipe, round we go, make it shiny, make it glow.”

As he wiped, he imagined the sink as a little sea. “We are all helpers,” he told the spots. “We all share water on Earth. We keep it clean together.” He spoke gently, like a teacher. The cloth felt soft. The sink felt cool and smooth. Soon, the spots were gone. The sink shone like a white moon. “Hello, sparkle!” Tiko laughed.

He checked his list again. “Hang towel,” he read. He looked at the towel on the floor. “Oh dear,” he said. “A towel on the floor can’t dry well.” He lifted it carefully. “Up you go, friend.” He hung it on the hook. He made a final check mark. All the boxes were full. Tiko took a deep breath. He felt calm and proud.

Just then, he heard a tiny voice. It was his rubber duck, Dashi, floating in the tub. “Quack? Are we done?” Dashi asked.

“Yes, Dashi,” Tiko said. “We finished every task.”

“Quack! How did you do it?” Dashi asked.

“I made a plan,” Tiko said, tapping the list. “I did one small step at a time. I used care. I used creativity. I made a game. I also thought about friends far away who clean and save water too.”

Dashi bobbed. “Quack! That sounds smart.”

Tiko nodded. “When we all do our tasks, our homes stay nice. When we use just the water we need, our world smiles. We all share this big, blue planet.”

Dashi looked at the shiny sink and the neat towel. “Quack! It looks beautiful. And it smells like lemons!”

Tiko took one last look around. The faucet was off. The mirror was clear. The sink was sparkly. The towel was tidy. He put the sticky note on the mirror and drew a happy star. “I did it,” he whispered.

He felt warm inside. “Finishing tasks helps me and helps others,” he said. “Little steps make big changes.”

Dashi splashed softly. “Quack! What will we do tomorrow?”

Tiko smiled. “Tomorrow, we will do our tasks again. We can make another game. We can sing a new song. We can help our home and our world, one tiny step at a time.”

He turned to the door, ready for a snack and a story. “Thank you, bathroom,” Tiko said. “See you later.”

The End.
