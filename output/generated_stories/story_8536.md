# Bibi Sings in the Pantry

Bibi was a bright blue singing bird with a soft, warm belly and tiny silver claws. One rainy morning, she fluttered through a small open window and landed in a pantry. The pantry smelled like cinnamon, oranges, and fresh bread. It was cozy and quiet. Shelves stood like tall trees, holding jars of beans, oats, and shiny nuts.

“Tweet, tweet! La-la-la!” sang Bibi, shaking a few crumbs from a bread basket. Her song sounded like sunshine and sparkles. She liked the echo in the pantry. It made her notes bounce and twirl.

From inside a jar, a little voice whispered, “Excuse me?”

Bibi stopped and tilted her head. “Hello?” she chirped.

A tiny ant peeked out of a jar of rice. “I’m Anto. Your song is pretty,” he said, “but it is very loud in here. It makes the jars rattle.”

Bibi blinked. “Oh! I didn’t know. I love to sing. Singing makes me happy.”

Anto smiled. “That is wonderful. I like to line up the rice grains. That makes me happy. Maybe we can both be happy?”

Bibi thought about this. “Yes,” she said, “but how?”

She flew to a wooden spoon and perched gently. The spoon smelled like warm soup. She hummed a softer note. “Hmm-hmm-hmm.” The jars stayed still.

Anto nodded. “Much better. Thank you for listening.”

Bibi felt warm inside. Listening felt nice. She looked around the pantry and saw a roll of paper towels, a basket of onions, and a stack of metal lids like shiny coins. She wanted to sing more, but she also wanted to be kind.

Just then, the door opened. A child named Mia stepped inside. She had big curious eyes and tiny slippers that whispered on the floor. “Oh!” Mia said. “A bird! In our pantry!”

Bibi fluttered up to a top shelf and tucked her wings in. “Hello,” she chirped softly.

Mia smiled. “Hi, little bird. Are you okay? You can rest here, but please don’t peck the bags. Mommy keeps food safe in here.”

Bibi looked down at the sacks of flour and sugar. “I don’t want to break anything,” she said. “I only want to sing.”

Mia nodded. “I like songs. But we need quiet sometimes, too. If we all talk, no one can hear.”

Bibi thought again. “I can sing soft,” she offered. “Like a hush song.”

Anto waved a tiny antenna. “And I can carry crumbs to the compost bowl,” he said. “That keeps the pantry clean.”

Mia clapped softly. “What a good team! We can make a plan.”

They all sat together—Bibi on the spoon, Anto by the rice jar, Mia on a small step stool. The pantry light was golden and gentle.

Mia said, “First, we set boundaries. That means we say what is okay and what is not okay.”

Bibi fluffed her feathers. “My boundary: I want to sing every day.”

Anto lifted a rice grain. “My boundary: I need the jars to be still so my line does not fall.”

Mia pointed to the shelves. “Our house boundary: We keep food safe and we don’t waste.”

Bibi nodded. “So I will sing softly inside the pantry. Loud songs can be outside.”

Anto nodded. “And I will not explore open boxes without asking. I will use only crumbs from the floor.”

Mia smiled. “Great! Now, creativity time. Let’s make music that is soft and kind.”

Bibi looked at the metal lids. “They shine like moons,” she whispered. She tapped one with her beak—ting! The sound was tiny and sweet.

Mia gently drummed the wooden spoon on her knee—tok, tok, tok—like raindrops. Anto tapped a rice grain on the side of his jar—tick, tick, tick.

Bibi sang a soft melody. “La, la, la,” she breathed, like a feather floating.

Together they made a pantry band. The sounds were gentle and calm. They made a rhythm like a heartbeat. The onions made a rustly whisper. The paper bag crinkled like a soft wave.

Bibi smiled with her whole body. “This feels safe,” she said. “And it is fun!”

Anto giggled. “My line is still perfect!”

Mia nodded. “And nothing is messy.”

They played three songs. Then Mia said, “Now it is cleanup time. Part of caring for the earth is making less waste.”

Anto saluted. “I will carry tiny crumbs to the compost bowl in the kitchen.”

Bibi looked at a loose feather on the shelf. “My feather can go to the garden. It can help make soil.”

Mia gathered the paper bits and lids and put them back. She wiped a small spill of oats. “We reuse and recycle,” she said. “That is good care.”

Bibi fluttered near the window. The rain had turned into a silver mist. “May I go outside to sing my big song?” she asked.

Mia opened the window wide. “Yes. Thank you for asking. That is respectful.”

Bibi lifted into the soft air. She took a deep breath. “Laaaaa!” Her big song flew across the wet garden and made the puddles smile.

Before she flew away, Bibi peeked back in. “Thank you, friends,” she chirped. “Today I learned to listen, to set boundaries, and to be creative in a gentle way.”

Anto waved. “And we learned to speak up kindly.”

Mia waved too. “And to care for our home and our earth.”

Bibi blew a tiny kiss of song. “Tweet-goodbye!”

The pantry felt peaceful. The jars did not rattle. The floor was clean. And in the garden, Bibi’s big song was happy and free.

The End.
