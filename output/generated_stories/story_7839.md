# Oliver Owl's Special Visitors

Oliver the wise old owl lived in a cozy guest room at the top of Grandma Rose's house. His big, round eyes sparkled with kindness, and his soft brown feathers were speckled with white spots like tiny stars.

One sunny morning, Oliver heard excited voices downstairs. "Hoot, hoot! What's all that happy chatter?" he wondered, fluffing his feathers with curiosity.

Soon, footsteps came up the wooden stairs - *thump, thump, thump* - and the guest room door opened wide. In walked Grandma Rose with three children Oliver had never seen before.

"Oliver," said Grandma Rose with a warm smile, "meet our special guests! This is Maya, who speaks Spanish and English. This is Jin, who just moved here from Japan. And this is Zara, who uses a wheelchair to get around."

Oliver's eyes grew even bigger with excitement. "Hoot, hoot! Welcome to my room!" he called out cheerfully.

Maya had long, dark hair and wore a bright red dress. She waved and said, "¡Hola, Oliver! That means 'hello' in Spanish!"

Jin had straight black hair and kind eyes. He bowed politely and said, "Konnichiwa, Oliver-san! That's how we say hello in Japanese!"

Zara had beautiful curly hair and the biggest smile. Her wheelchair was purple - her favorite color! "Hi there, Oliver! I love your spotted feathers!"

Oliver was fascinated. He had never met children who spoke different languages or used a wheelchair before. His wise mind started thinking of questions.

"Maya," Oliver asked thoughtfully, "can you teach me more Spanish words?"

Maya clapped her hands with joy. "Sí! That means 'yes'! You are a búho - that's 'owl' in Spanish!"

"And Jin," Oliver continued, "what's it like in Japan?"

Jin's face lit up. "In Japan, we have beautiful cherry blossom trees called sakura. They're pink and white, just like the colors in your feathers!"

Oliver turned to Zara with gentle curiosity. "Zara, your wheelchair looks so cool! How does it help you?"

Zara giggled and spun her wheels. "It helps me move around quickly! Watch this!" She zoomed in a small circle, making everyone laugh. "I can go really fast, and it never gets tired like legs do!"

As the afternoon went on, the children played together in the guest room. Maya taught everyone to count in Spanish: "Uno, dos, tres!" Jin showed them how to fold paper into beautiful origami birds. Zara demonstrated how she could reach high shelves by raising her wheelchair seat.

Oliver watched with wonder as the children learned from each other. Maya helped Jin practice English words, Jin taught Zara to make paper cranes, and Zara showed Maya how to do wheelies in her chair.

"You know what?" Oliver said, spreading his wise wings wide. "Each of you is special in your own wonderful way! Maya speaks two languages, Jin knows about different countries, and Zara shows us that there are many ways to move around the world."

The children gathered around Oliver, their faces glowing with happiness.

"We're all different," said Maya.

"But we're all friends!" added Jin.

"And that makes everything more fun!" finished Zara.

Oliver nodded his wise head. "Hoot, hoot! You've taught this old owl something very important today. Our differences make us special, and when we share what makes us unique, we all learn and grow together!"

From that day on, Oliver's guest room became a place where children from everywhere felt welcome, knowing that being different was something to celebrate.

The End.
