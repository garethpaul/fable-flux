# Leo Chooses the Colors

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright curious eyes. Today, Leo padded into the creative art room. The room smelled like sweet glue and fresh paper. Sunlight splashed across the tables. Brushes, crayons, and paints waited in neat little cups. The walls were full of colorful art.

“Wow,” whispered Leo, his whiskers twitching. “So many colors!”

Mrs. Zebra, the art teacher, smiled with kind eyes. “Good morning, Leo. Today we will make a big class mural. We will paint a friendly jungle. You can choose what you paint.”

Leo’s ears perked up. He wanted to make something special. He wanted to help his friends. But what should he choose?

His friend Tia the turtle scooted over. “I will paint the river,” Tia said. “Blue, swish, swish!” She moved her brush in a gentle wave.

Benny the bunny hopped by. “I will paint carrots!” he giggled. “Orange, dot, dot!”

Leo looked at the brushes. Big. Small. Fat. Thin. He looked at the paint. Red, yellow, blue, green, purple, orange. So many choices. His heart did a little thump. He wanted to be brave, but choosing felt hard.

Mrs. Zebra came close and spoke softly. “When we make a choice, we can take small steps. We can prepare. We can think. We can try. That is good decision-making.”

Leo took a deep breath. He felt the cool air fill his chest. “Small steps,” he said to himself.

He looked at the empty space on the mural. There was a sunny spot near the top. There was a grassy place at the bottom. There was a bare corner with nothing but sky.

“What should I paint?” Leo asked.

“Listen,” said Mrs. Zebra. “What does the picture need? What do your friends need? What do you want to learn?”

Leo closed his eyes and listened. He heard Tia’s brush go swish, swish. He heard Benny’s tapping, dot, dot. He smelled the lemony wipes and the leafy scent from the paper trees on the wall. He felt his paw pads press on the floor. He opened his eyes.

“I can choose,” he said. “I can prepare.”

First, Leo wiped his table. Wipe, wipe. Next, he put on a little apron. Tie, tie. Then he picked two brushes: one big for the sky, one small for tiny details. Tap, tap. He set out three colors: yellow for sun, green for leaves, and brown for tree trunks.

“I will make a tall tree with a cozy nest,” said Leo. “Birds need a home.”

“That is a thoughtful choice,” Mrs. Zebra said. “You decided by looking, listening, and preparing.”

Leo touched the big brush to yellow paint. It felt cool and slippery. He lifted the brush to the paper. Swirl, swirl. A bright sun grew in the corner. Warm and happy.

He chose the green next. He made leaves like little handprints. Pat, pat, pat. Then he picked up the small brush. He dipped it in brown and drew a strong trunk. Straight and steady.

“Oops,” Leo said when his paw bumped the cup. A tiny drip fell onto the sky. Plop.

Leo felt his tummy twist. He wanted to hide under the table. But he remembered: be brave, make a plan, fix what you can.

“I can fix it,” Leo said. He dabbed the drip with a soft cloth. Dab, dab. It faded like a cloud. He smiled. “Better!”

Benny peered over. “I like your tree,” Benny said. “Can I add a carrot patch under it?”

Leo thought. The space was small. The tree needed roots. He had to decide. He took a breath and looked again.

“Yes,” Leo said, “but let’s make a tiny patch here.” He pointed to a corner. “Then the tree can have roots here.”

“Great plan!” Benny said. They both grinned.

Tia rolled up slowly. “I can paint a little stream by the roots,” she said. “The tree will drink.”

Leo nodded. “That is helpful.”

Mrs. Zebra clapped her hooves softly. “You are using kind words and smart choices. You asked, you looked, and you decided.”

Leo added a small round nest in the branches. He used the tiny brush and gentle strokes. He placed two blue eggs inside. He listened to his feelings. He felt calm and proud.

A little chick peeked from behind a paint jar. “Cheep?” it squeaked in the picture in Leo’s mind. Leo laughed. “You will have a home,” he whispered to the painted nest.

At last, the mural was full. The river flowed. The carrots grew. The tree stood tall with a sunny smile. The room hummed with happy sounds. Brushes clinked. Paper rustled. Friends giggled.

Mrs. Zebra gathered everyone. “Look at our work,” she said. “Leo, tell us about your choices.”

Leo held his head high. “I looked and listened,” he said. “I prepared my space. I picked tools and colors. I made a plan. When there was a drip, I fixed it. I asked my friends, and we decided together. That helped our picture.”

Everyone cheered. Leo’s fur felt warm. He felt brave and ready for new choices.

Mrs. Zebra nodded. “Today we learned: good decisions come from looking, listening, and preparing. We can be brave and kind. We can try, and we can fix little mistakes.”

Leo smiled at the bright mural. “I can choose, and I can learn,” he said.

The End.
