# Leo the Curious Helper

In the bright dining room, morning light glittered on the shiny spoons. The table smelled like warm toast and sweet oranges. A brave little lion cub named Leo padded in on soft paws. His golden fur tickled his nose, and he gave a tiny sneeze. Achoo!

Leo’s tummy gave a rumble. “Grrr-rrr-rowl,” it said. He giggled. “Breakfast time!”

But the table was messy. Napkins were crumpled like little clouds. Forks and spoons were mixed up like a puzzle. A tall bowl of fruit wobbled on the edge, ready to topple.

Leo’s whiskers twitched with curiosity. He loved to find out how things worked. He peered up at the fruit bowl. “How do I make this safe?” he wondered. “What goes where? Why is everything jumbled?”

Mama Lion entered, carrying a pitcher of milk. The milk sloshed with a soft swish. “Good morning, Leo,” she sang. “Hmm, the table looks very silly today.”

Leo took a deep breath. He felt a brave spark inside. “Mama,” he said, tail swishing, “can I help? I want to learn. I want to try.”

Mama smiled. “That is kind and brave, Leo. You can be our helper. But the table is high. What is your plan?”

Leo looked around. He sniffed the buttery toast, heard the clock tick-tock, and felt the cool floor under his paws. His ears perked. “I have an idea!” he said. “I will start on the chairs. Then I will reach the table. I will ask for what I need.”

He dragged a chair carefully, scrape-scrape, not too loud. The chair wobbled. Leo stopped. “Hmm,” he said. “What if I ask for help?” He raised his paw. “Mama, can you hold the chair while I climb?”

Mama put a big paw on the chair. “We can do it together,” she said.

Leo climbed up. His paws felt the smooth wood. He peeked at the fruit bowl. Red apples, green grapes, and a sunny banana smiled at him with sweet smells. “Yum,” he whispered, licking his lips.

He reached out. The bowl wiggled. “Uh-oh,” Leo said. “This might spill.”

Papa Lion came in, humming a happy tune. He carried a basket of whole-grain rolls. The rolls were warm, and steam puffed like little clouds. “Good morning, team,” Papa said. “What’s the plan?”

Leo puffed his chest. “I am the helper. I want to put things in the right places. But the bowl is wobbly.”

Papa nodded. “Good noticing, Leo. Where do you think the bowl will be safest?”

Leo looked. He saw a flat place in the middle of the table. He saw a napkin bunched up under one side. “Oh! The napkin is making the bowl tilt,” he said.

Mama smiled. “That is curious thinking. What will you try?”

“I will move the napkin,” Leo said. He touched it slowly. He felt the soft cloth. He slid it out. The bowl sat flat. No wobble. “I did it!”

Leo’s heart felt warm. He looked at the forks and spoons. “Mama, which side do forks go on?” he asked.

Mama tapped her chin. “What do you think?”

Leo thought. He looked at his paws. Left paw, right paw. He remembered a song. “Forks on the left. Spoons on the right. Knives sleep next to the plate, not too tight.” He grinned. “I will try.”

He placed each fork on the left with a gentle clink. He put each spoon on the right. He set the knives with their sharp sides turned safely in. “Safety first,” he said proudly.

Papa set down the basket. “We also need colors on the table,” he said. “What fruits should we choose for a strong, healthy breakfast?”

Leo sniffed the apples. Crunchy. He touched the grapes. Cool. He peeled the banana with careful claws. Soft and sweet. “Can we make a rainbow plate?” he asked. “Red apple, green grapes, yellow banana, and maybe orange slices?”

Mama clapped softly. “That is smart nutrition, Leo. A rainbow helps our bodies grow.”

Leo nodded. “I am curious. What do these foods do?”

Papa poured milk. “Milk helps bones. Fruit gives vitamins. Rolls give energy.” He winked. “And curious minds help us learn.”

Leo placed a tiny apple slice on each plate. He felt proud to volunteer for the job. “May I pass the grapes?” he asked.

Mama said, “Let’s trade. I will pass the milk. You pass the grapes.”

Leo tilted the little bowl carefully. “Would you like a few grapes, Mama?” he asked.

“Yes, please,” Mama said.

“Me too,” Papa added.

Leo giggled. “This is like a game. We take turns. We ask. We listen.” He paused. “And we keep the table safe.”

They sat together. Leo’s chair was cozy. The toast crunched. The orange slices burst with juice. “Mmm,” Leo said. “My tummy is happy.”

Mama touched Leo’s ear. “You were curious. You asked questions. You made a plan.”

Papa rumbled, “You took initiative. You volunteered to help. You solved the wobbly bowl problem. And you made healthy choices.”

Leo’s eyes sparkled. “I learned that asking is smart. I learned that trying is brave. I learned that helping makes breakfast better.”

They clinked cups of milk. “To curious minds,” Papa cheered.

“To helpful paws,” Mama added.

“And to rainbow plates,” Leo laughed.

The End.
