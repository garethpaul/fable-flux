# Ruby Rabbit's Bubble Story Time

Ruby Rabbit loved to tell stories. She told stories to her teddy bear, to the flowers in the garden, and even to her breakfast carrots. But there was one place Ruby had never tried telling stories - the bathroom during bath time.

"Bath time, Ruby!" called Mama Rabbit from downstairs. Ruby's ears drooped. She loved being clean, but bath time felt so quiet and boring. The white tiles were cold, and the water just splashed without any fun.

Ruby hopped slowly up the stairs. Her fluffy feet made soft thumping sounds on each step. When she reached the bathroom, she saw the big white bathtub waiting for her.

"I don't want to take a bath," Ruby said to herself. "It's no fun at all."

But then Ruby had a wonderful idea! Her whiskers twitched with excitement. "What if I tell stories in the bathtub?" she wondered aloud.

Ruby turned on the warm water. It made a gentle rushing sound as it filled the tub. She added some bubble soap that smelled like sweet lavender. Soon, fluffy white bubbles danced on top of the water.

"Perfect!" Ruby giggled. She climbed into the warm, bubbly water. It felt cozy and soft around her fur.

Ruby picked up a handful of bubbles. "Once upon a time," she began, "there was a brave bubble princess who lived in a magical foam castle!" She shaped the bubbles into a tall tower with her paws.

As Ruby told her story, something amazing happened. The bath time that used to feel long and boring now felt fun and exciting! She made bubble characters - a wise bubble owl, a silly bubble frog, and a kind bubble elephant.

"The bubble princess wanted to share her magic with everyone," Ruby continued, moving the bubbles around like little actors. "So she gave bubble gifts to all the animals in the forest!"

Ruby was having so much fun that she almost forgot to wash behind her ears! She giggled as she used her washcloth to scrub her paws while telling her story.

Just then, Baby Bunny peeked into the bathroom. Baby Bunny was Ruby's little brother, and he was scared of bath time.

"Ruby, what are you doing?" Baby Bunny asked with curious, wide eyes.

"I'm telling bubble stories!" Ruby said happily. "Want to hear one?"

Baby Bunny nodded and sat on the fluffy bathroom rug. Ruby told him all about the bubble princess and her magical adventures. Baby Bunny laughed and clapped his tiny paws.

"Can I have a bubble story bath too?" Baby Bunny asked.

"Of course!" Ruby said generously. "I'll teach you how to make bubble characters, and we can tell stories together!"

When Mama Rabbit came to check on them, she found both little rabbits clean, happy, and excited about bath time.

"Ruby, you've discovered something wonderful," Mama Rabbit said with a warm smile. "Stories can make any time special, even bath time!"

From that day on, Ruby and Baby Bunny looked forward to their bubble story baths. Ruby learned that taking care of herself could be fun when she used her imagination. She also learned that sharing her stories with others made them even more magical.

And whenever other little animals were scared of bath time, Ruby would invite them over for bubble story time, teaching them that self-care and storytelling go perfectly together.

The End.
