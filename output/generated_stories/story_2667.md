# Roary’s Wiggle Break

Roary was a brave little lion cub with a soft golden mane and bright button eyes. He lived with his family in a cozy home where the living room was also a home office. There was a big desk, a humming laptop, and a spinny chair that went “whooosh!” if you turned it fast. The air smelled like warm paper and cinnamon tea.

One sunny morning, Mama worked at the desk, tapping keys. “Tap-tap-tap,” went the keyboard. Roary wanted to draw a big rainbow map. He spread out crayons and paper on the rug. He held a blue crayon tight.

But the crayon wouldn’t make a smooth line. It went “scritch-scratch,” and the blue river looked bumpy. Roary’s whiskers twitched. His tail flicked. His tummy felt tight like a knot.

“I can’t do it!” Roary growled, a small growl that felt big inside. He scrunched his face. “Grrr.”

Mama looked up. “I hear a brave growl,” she said gently. “Sometimes our feelings get big. Would you like a Wiggle Break?”

Roary blinked. “What’s a Wiggle Break?”

“It’s movement to help our bodies and minds,” Mama smiled. “Exercise helps when we feel stuck. Want to try?”

Roary took a slow breath. He put down the crayon. “Okay. I want to be brave.”

Mama stood up and stretched her arms to the ceiling. “Let’s make space.” She slid the chair to the side. The floor felt cool on Roary’s paws.

“First,” said Mama, “we wake up with Star Jumps. Feet out, hands up. Ready?”

Roary nodded. He bent his knees and jumped. “Boing!” His paws tapped the rug. “I am a star!” he laughed. His heart went thump-thump. He felt warm, like sunshine on fur.

“Now Lion Breaths,” Mama said. “Breathe in through your nose. Breathe out with a big, gentle ‘haaa,’ like a kind lion.”

Roary breathed in. He breathed out, “Haaaa.” His shoulders relaxed. His tummy knot loosened.

“Let’s visit the world with movement,” Mama said. “Our neighbors all around the world move in fun ways.”

Roary’s ears perked up. “Around the world?”

Mama nodded. “We can try dances and stretches people enjoy in different places.”

She played soft music on her phone. The home office filled with gentle notes.

“First, a Salsa wiggle,” Mama said. “People in places like Cuba and Colombia love to dance salsa.” She stepped side to side, hips swaying. Roary giggled and copied. “Wiggle-wiggle, step-step.” The rug tickled his toes. He felt bouncy and brave.

“Now, a Bollywood hands dance,” Mama said, wiggling her fingers like sparkles. “In India, people dance with big feelings.” Roary lifted his paws and opened and closed his fingers. “Twinkle-twinkle!” he laughed. His tail swished with the beat.

“Next is Tai Chi slow flow,” Mama whispered. “In China, people practice slow movements for calm.” They moved their arms like clouds floating. Roary’s breath was soft. He felt like a drifting leaf.

“Kick-kick like soccer in Brazil!” Mama said. Roary tapped an imaginary ball with careful toes. “Tap, tap, goal!” He cheered softly so he didn’t scare the laptop. His legs felt strong.

They finished with a big stretch. “Reach up, reach down,” Mama sang. Roary touched his toes. His nose brushed the rug. It smelled like clean cotton.

“How do you feel now?” Mama asked.

Roary placed a paw on his chest. Thump-thump. It felt steady. “I feel calm and strong. The knot is gone.” He smiled. “I am brave.”

“Want to try your map again?” Mama asked.

Roary nodded. He picked up the blue crayon. He took one Lion Breath in and one Lion Breath out. The crayon made a smooth “shhhh” across the paper. He drew a long blue river. He drew a red bridge, a green park, and tiny orange houses. He drew kids dancing in a circle, paws and hands together.

He whispered, “Thank you, Wiggle Break.”

Mama sat back at the desk. “When we move, our bodies help our brains. Exercise is a friend.”

Roary kept drawing. He made a tiny flag with a heart. He added a little lion cub on the map, doing star jumps next to the park. His paws worked with the music still humming softly.

Soon, Roary held up his rainbow map. “Mama, look!”

Mama clapped softly. “Beautiful! You kept going. You were brave. You used your breath. You moved your body. You tried again.”

Roary’s eyes sparkled. “When I feel grrr, I can move and breathe. Then I can try.”

Mama nodded. “Yes. When we feel frustrated, we can:
- Stop and notice the feeling.
- Take Lion Breaths.
- Do a Wiggle Break.
- Try again with a calm heart.”

Roary hugged Mama’s leg. The spinny chair whispered, “whooosh,” as it turned a little. The laptop hummed, “mmm.” The cinnamon tea smelled sweet. Everything felt cozy and bright.

Roary hung his map on the wall by the desk. “Now our home office is a travel place,” he said proudly. “We can visit the world with our feet!”

Mama smiled. “And with our brave hearts.”

Roary stood tall on his back paws and did one last star jump. “I am Roary the Brave,” he said. “I move my body. I calm my mind. I try again.”

He picked up a yellow crayon. “Now I add sunshine.”

The room glowed in his imagination, warm and golden, as the little lion cub drew and hummed and felt strong.

The End.
