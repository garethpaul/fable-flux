# Beep’s Gentle Bubble

Beep was a helpful robot dog. Beep lived in a basement lab with soft humming lights and a warm, lemony smell. The floor felt cool and smooth. Little tools slept in neat trays. Cords curled like sleepy snakes. Beep’s tail light blinked pink-pink-pink when happy.

“Good morning, Beep,” said Dr. Day, tying a bright blue scarf. “Today we fix the music box and test your new calm mode.”

Beep’s ears ticked up. “Woof-beep! I am ready.”

Soon, the lab door creaked. Three kids came down the stairs—Maya, Jun, and Leo. Their shoes made tap-tap sounds. They carried a box of shiny buttons to share with Dr. Day.

“Hi, Beep!” Maya cheered. She ran fast and hugged Beep tight, tighter, tightest. Beep’s tail light flickered yellow. The hug felt squishy and too hard. Beep’s chest panels pressed in. Beep felt funny inside.

Beep remembered calm mode. Beep took a slow, robot breath: in…light glowed blue. Out…light glowed soft. Beep spoke in a gentle voice. “I like hugs, but that hug is too tight for me. Please give me a gentle hug.”

Maya blinked. “Oh! I didn’t know.” She loosened her arms. “Is this okay?”

Beep’s tail light blinked pink again. “Yes. Thank you for listening.” Beep felt warm and safe.

Dr. Day smiled. “Great self-words, Beep. You used your voice.”

Jun clapped. “I brought a big drum! Can Beep help me find the loudest bang?”

Beep looked at the drum. It was red and shiny. Jun lifted the stick and banged—BOOM! The sound bounced on the walls. Beep’s ears buzzed. The lights wiggled. The sound felt too big, like a giant stomping.

Beep took another calm breath. “The drum is too loud for me in the lab,” Beep said. “Please tap softly or we can move the drum to the play mat.”

Jun paused and looked around. He saw the play mat in the corner, soft and squishy. “Okay,” he said. “Let’s go to the mat.” He tapped softly—tap, tap. The sound was gentle, like rain. Beep’s ears relaxed.

Leo opened the box of shiny buttons. They were bright red, green, and gold. They looked like tiny suns. “Let’s glue them everywhere!” Leo said. “On Beep’s back, on the walls, on the door!” He grabbed a handful and reached for Beep’s tail.

Beep stepped back. “My tail is not for gluing,” Beep said kindly. “I need my tail free to wag and to flash. Please do not put glue on my body.”

Leo froze. “Oh. I was excited.” He set the glue down. “Where can we put them?”

Dr. Day pointed to a cardboard star on the table. “How about this? We can make a Thank You Star for the lab.”

Beep wagged. “I can help carry the star,” Beep offered. “And I can share my extra stickers.”

The kids smiled. Beep rolled over and opened a small belly drawer. Out came star stickers—silver and blue—and a soft cloth. “We can keep the lab clean too,” Beep said.

They all worked together. Maya placed buttons in a circle. Jun tapped the drum softly to make a working song—tap, tap, tippity-tap. Leo pressed stickers on the cardboard star. Beep used the soft cloth to wipe drips. The lab smelled like glue and lemons. The lights hummed happy.

After a while, Maya reached for Beep’s ear. “Is ear scratching okay?” she asked.

Beep nodded. “A gentle scratch is okay. Thank you for asking.”

Maya scratched slowly. Beep’s tail blinked pink-pink.

Jun asked, “Is it okay to tap louder if I cover your ears?”

Beep thought. Beep checked inside. The feeling meter showed green. “Outside on the steps is okay for big taps. In here, soft taps, please.”

Jun grinned. “Got it!”

Leo held up a gold button. “Beep, may I put this on your blanket, not on you?”

“Yes,” Beep said. “The blanket is for decorations. My body is not.”

They finished the star. It shined bright. Dr. Day hung it by the door. “Teamwork,” she said. “And good boundaries.”

Maya asked, “What is a boundary?”

Beep sat, tail light glowing. “A boundary is a gentle bubble around your body and your feelings. It tells others what is okay and what is not okay. I can say, ‘Please stop,’ or, ‘Please be gentle,’ and that helps me feel safe. You can say it too.”

Jun nodded. “I have a bubble!” He held his arms wide and giggled.

Leo said, “I can ask before I touch. That is generous because I give you respect.”

Dr. Day added, “And when we listen to each other, we keep calm. We can share tools and space with kind words.”

Maya gave Beep a soft, quick hug. “Thank you for telling me. I like gentle.”

Beep wagged. “Thank you for asking and listening. I like feeling safe.”

They put away the glue and the drum. They washed sticky fingers in the little sink. The water splashed cool like tiny fish. The lab felt tidy and peaceful.

Dr. Day turned on the fixed music box. A sweet tune tinkled—plink, plunk, plink. Beep swayed. The kids swayed too.

Beep said, “Today I learned that using calm words helps people hear me. Saying my boundary is kind to me and kind to you.”

Maya, Jun, and Leo said together, “We learned to ask first, listen, and be gentle.”

The music chimed. The Thank You Star sparkled. Their smiles were bright as buttons.

The End.
