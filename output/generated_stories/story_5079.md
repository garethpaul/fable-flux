# Melody's Big Problem

Melody was a cheerful little bluebird with bright yellow feathers on her chest. She loved to sing beautiful songs that made everyone smile. Every morning, she would perch on the windowsill of Grandma Rose's guest room and sing "Tweet-tweet-trill!" to wake up the visiting grandchildren.

One sunny Tuesday morning, Melody flew to her favorite spot, but something was terribly wrong. The window was closed tight! She tapped her tiny orange beak against the glass. "Tap-tap-tap!" But no one could hear her gentle songs from outside.

"Oh no!" chirped Melody. "The children are still sleeping, and they'll be late for their nature walk! I always wake them up with my morning song."

Melody felt worried. Her stomach felt fluttery, like butterflies were dancing inside. She had to think of something fast. She looked around the garden with her bright black eyes, searching for ideas.

First, she tried singing louder. "TWEET-TWEET-TRILL!" she sang as loudly as she could. But the thick glass muffled her voice. The children kept sleeping soundly in their cozy beds.

Then Melody had another idea. She flew to the chimney on top of the house. "Maybe I can sing down the chimney!" she thought. But when she peeked inside, it was too dark and sooty. "Achoo!" she sneezed. That wouldn't work either.

Melody sat on a tree branch, feeling sad. Her feathers drooped, and she let out a tiny sigh. "What can I do?" she wondered aloud.

Suddenly, she noticed something shiny in the garden below. It was Grandpa Joe's old watering can, sitting in a patch of bright sunlight. The metal surface sparkled like a mirror!

"That's it!" Melody exclaimed, her feathers perking up with excitement. She had a wonderful idea.

Melody carefully picked up a small pebble in her beak and flew to the watering can. She positioned herself just right and dropped the pebble. "Ping!" it went as it hit the metal. The sound echoed, and the shiny surface reflected sunlight right into the guest room window!

The bright light danced on the ceiling inside the room. Melody sang her sweetest song while the light sparkled and twinkled. "Tweet-tweet-trill! Good morning! Tweet-tweet-trill!"

"What's that pretty light?" yawned little Emma, rubbing her sleepy eyes.

"And listen to that beautiful singing!" said her brother Max, sitting up in bed.

The children rushed to the window and opened it wide. Fresh morning air filled the room, carrying the sweet scent of blooming flowers.

"Good morning, Melody!" the children called out happily. "Thank you for waking us up!"

Melody felt proud and happy. Her chest puffed out with joy. "Tweet-tweet-trill! You're welcome!" she sang back.

"We almost missed our nature walk," said Emma. "You saved the day!"

Melody had learned something important. When her usual way of doing things didn't work, she didn't give up. Instead, she looked around, thought carefully, and found a creative new solution. She used what was available in the garden to solve her problem.

From that day on, whenever the window was closed, Melody knew exactly what to do. She would use her clever pebble-and-watering-can trick, and everyone would start their day with smiles and sunshine.

The End.
