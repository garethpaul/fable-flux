# Benny Bear's Swimming Lesson

Benny was a small brown bear cub with soft, fluffy fur and bright black eyes. He lived with Mama Bear in a cozy house near the forest. Today was a very special day - it was time for Benny's first swimming lesson!

But Benny felt scared. His tummy had butterflies dancing inside. His paws felt sweaty and shaky.

"Mama, I don't want to go swimming," Benny whispered. He hid behind the bathroom door, peeking out with worried eyes.

Mama Bear knelt down beside him. "What's wrong, little one?" she asked gently, stroking his fur.

"The water looks so deep and splashy," Benny said. "What if I can't swim? What if I sink like a rock?" His voice got smaller and smaller.

Mama Bear smiled warmly. "Oh, sweet Benny. Everyone feels scared when they try something new. Even Mama felt nervous during her first swim!"

"Really?" Benny's ears perked up a little.

"Really! Come here," Mama said, leading him to the bathroom. She turned on the faucet. Warm water splashed into the big white bathtub. Steam rose up like fluffy clouds. "Let's practice being brave together."

Benny watched the water swirl around. It made gentle splashing sounds - not scary at all! He dipped one toe in. "It's warm!" he giggled.

"That's right," Mama said. "Now try putting your whole paw in."

Benny slowly put his paw in the water. It felt nice and cozy, like a warm hug. He wiggled his toes and watched the water ripple. "This is fun!" he said, feeling a little braver.

"You're being so courageous," Mama praised. "Courage means doing something even when you feel scared."

Next, Benny sat on the edge of the tub. He splashed gently with both paws. Water droplets sparkled in the bathroom light. He laughed as the water tickled his fur.

"I'm getting brave!" Benny announced proudly. His chest puffed out like a superhero.

Mama filled a small cup with water. "Want to practice putting your face near the water?" she asked.

Benny took a deep breath. His heart beat fast, but he felt stronger now. He leaned down and let the water touch his nose. It felt cool and fresh. "I did it!" he cheered.

"You're amazing, Benny! You're learning that being scared is okay. The important thing is trying anyway."

Benny nodded wisely. "My feelings are telling me something, but I can still be brave!"

Soon it was time to go to the real swimming pool. Benny felt those butterflies again, but now he knew what to do.

At the pool, Coach Otter smiled at Benny. "Ready for your lesson, little bear?"

Benny looked at the blue water. It was bigger than his bathtub, but he remembered practicing. He remembered feeling brave.

"Yes!" Benny said loudly. "I feel scared AND brave at the same time!"

Coach Otter laughed. "That's the best kind of brave there is!"

Benny stepped into the shallow water. It felt just like his practice bath - warm and friendly. Soon he was splashing and kicking, learning to float like a happy otter.

"Look, Mama!" Benny called out. "I'm swimming! I was scared, but I tried anyway!"

Mama Bear clapped her paws. "You learned the most important lesson of all, Benny. Courage isn't about not feeling scared. Courage is feeling scared and doing it anyway!"

Benny beamed with pride. He had discovered that his feelings were okay, and he could be brave no matter what. From that day on, whenever Benny felt scared about trying new things, he remembered his bathroom practice and knew he could be courageous.

The End.
