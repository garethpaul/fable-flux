# Rosie Rabbit's Healthy Road Trip

Rosie Rabbit bounced excitedly in her car seat as her family packed for their big road trip. "Are we ready to go?" she asked, her fluffy ears twitching with excitement.

"Almost, sweetie," said Mama Rabbit, loading the last bag into the trunk. "We just need to pack our snacks for the long drive."

Rosie watched as Papa Rabbit grabbed a big bag of candy and some sugary drinks. "Yummy!" she squealed, clapping her little paws together.

But as they drove down the sunny highway, Rosie began to feel funny. Her tummy started to rumble and grumble. The sweet candy made her feel jittery, and then very sleepy. "Mama, my tummy doesn't feel good," she whimpered, holding her round belly.

"Oh dear," said Mama Rabbit, looking back at Rosie's pale face. "I think all that sugar isn't making you feel very well."

Papa Rabbit pulled into a rest stop with a small market. "Let's see if we can find some better snacks," he said kindly.

Rosie felt wobbly as she walked into the bright, clean store. She took a deep breath and thought carefully. "I want to feel better," she said with determination. "But I don't know what foods will help."

"Let's look together," suggested Mama Rabbit. "What colors do you see in the fresh food section?"

Rosie's brown eyes grew wide as she looked around. "I see orange carrots! And red apples! And green celery!" she said, pointing with her paw.

"Those are perfect!" said Papa Rabbit. "Foods with bright colors have special vitamins that help our bodies feel strong and happy."

Rosie carefully picked out crunchy carrots that smelled fresh and earthy. She chose shiny red apples that felt smooth and cool in her paws. She found a small container of creamy yogurt that looked white and thick.

"Now, let's organize our snacks," said Mama Rabbit. She showed Rosie how to arrange everything in a special cooler. "Carrots here, apples there, and yogurt in the cold spot."

Back in the car, Rosie munched on a sweet, crunchy carrot stick. "Mmm, this tastes so good!" she said. The fresh flavor made her mouth happy, and her tummy felt calm and peaceful.

"How do you feel now?" asked Papa Rabbit, smiling in the rearview mirror.

Rosie took a moment to think about her body. She breathed slowly and noticed how she felt inside. "My tummy feels happy and quiet," she said thoughtfully. "And I have energy to play I-Spy!"

As they continued their journey, Rosie enjoyed her healthy snacks. Each bite of crisp apple made her feel alert and cheerful. The cool yogurt was creamy and satisfying.

"I learned something important today," Rosie announced as they watched the sunset paint the sky orange and pink. "When I eat colorful, fresh foods, my body feels strong and happy. And when I organize my snacks, I can find them easily!"

"That's right, sweetheart," said Mama Rabbit. "Good food helps us feel our best, especially on long trips."

Rosie smiled and patted her content tummy. She had solved her problem by choosing nutritious foods and staying organized. Now she felt ready for any adventure that came her way.

The End.
