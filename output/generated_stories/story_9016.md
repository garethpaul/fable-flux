# Oliver Owl's Special Hideaway

Oliver the owl had a secret hideaway in the big closet behind the winter coats. It was cozy and dark, just how owls like it. He had soft pillows, his favorite books, and a little lamp that made everything glow warmly.

Every morning, Oliver would hoot "Good morning!" to all the forest animals from his closet window. The squirrels would chatter back, and the rabbits would wiggle their noses hello. This was Oliver's special routine, and it made everyone happy.

But one Tuesday morning, Oliver felt grumpy. His feathers were ruffled, and he didn't want to say good morning to anyone.

"Hmph!" Oliver huffed. He stayed quiet in his hideaway.

Down below, little Bunny waited by the big oak tree. "Where is Oliver's morning hoot?" she wondered, twitching her soft ears.

Squirrel scampered up to the closet window. "Oliver? Are you okay?" he called with his squeaky voice.

"Go away!" Oliver hooted loudly. "I don't want to talk today!"

The forest grew very quiet. No cheerful morning sounds. No happy chatter. Even the birds stopped singing their sweet songs.

Oliver sat in his dark closet feeling sorry for himself. But as the day went on, something felt wrong. The silence was too loud. His hideaway felt lonely instead of cozy.

"What have I done?" Oliver whispered to himself. His big yellow eyes grew sad.

He peeked out his closet window and saw Bunny sitting alone. She looked worried. Squirrel was gathering nuts by himself, not chattering happily like usual.

Oliver's heart felt heavy like a stone. "I was mean to my friends," he said softly. "I hurt their feelings because I felt grumpy."

The wise old owl knew what he had to do. He fluffed his brown and white feathers and flew down from his closet hideaway.

"Bunny! Squirrel!" Oliver called gently. "Please come here."

His friends looked up with hopeful eyes.

"I'm very sorry," Oliver said, bowing his head low. "I was grumpy this morning, but that was no reason to be mean to you. You were just being kind, and I was unkind back."

Bunny's nose twitched. "We were worried about you, Oliver."

"I know," Oliver said. "You are good friends, and I should have told you I was feeling grumpy instead of being mean. Can you forgive me?"

Squirrel's fluffy tail wiggled. "Of course! We all have grumpy days sometimes."

Oliver smiled with his whole heart. "Tomorrow, I promise to start our morning routine with kindness, even if I feel grumpy. And if I do feel grumpy, I'll tell you nicely instead of being mean."

The next morning, Oliver hooted his cheerful "Good morning!" from his closet window. But this time, he added something special.

"Good morning, dear friends! Thank you for being so kind and forgiving!"

Bunny hopped with joy. Squirrel chattered happily. All the forest animals felt warm and loved.

From that day on, Oliver remembered that being a good leader meant saying sorry when he made mistakes. His closet hideaway became even more special because it was where he learned that making amends with friends makes everyone's heart feel light and happy.

And whenever Oliver felt grumpy, he would remember to use kind words and ask for help instead of pushing his friends away.

The End.
