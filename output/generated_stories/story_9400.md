# Bella Bird's Big Concert

High up on a red brick rooftop, a little yellow bird named Bella loved to sing. Every morning, she would stretch her tiny wings and practice her beautiful songs. "La-la-la!" she would trill, and all the other birds would stop to listen.

One sunny day, Bella heard exciting news. "There's going to be a big concert tomorrow!" chirped her friend Robin. "All the birds in the neighborhood can perform their best songs!"

Bella's heart fluttered with joy. "Oh, I want to sing too!" she said, bouncing on her little orange feet. But then she looked around at the other birds practicing. They all seemed so ready, while Bella felt nervous butterflies in her tummy.

"I'll just wing it," Bella thought to herself. "Singing is easy for me. I don't need to prepare."

The next morning arrived with golden sunshine and a gentle breeze. Bella could hear the other birds warming up their voices with scales and exercises. "Do-re-mi-fa-so!" sang Robin. "Tweet-tweet-trill!" practiced Blue Jay.

But Bella just preened her feathers and ate a few seeds. "I'll be fine," she told herself, even though her voice felt a little scratchy from not practicing.

When it was time for the concert, all the birds gathered on the big oak tree. Families of squirrels, rabbits, and even some friendly cats came to listen. Bella's turn came, and she hopped onto the performance branch.

She opened her beak to sing her favorite song, but... "Croak!" came out instead of her usual sweet melody. Her throat felt dry and tight. The notes came out wobbly and weak. Bella's cheeks turned pink with embarrassment.

"Oh no," she whispered, her voice barely working. "I should have prepared better."

Bella flew back to her rooftop feeling sad and disappointed. She had let herself down by not getting ready properly.

Old Owl, who was very wise, landed gently beside her. "What happened, little Bella?" he asked kindly.

Bella took a deep breath and decided to be honest. "I didn't practice or prepare," she admitted, her voice small and sorry. "I thought I didn't need to because singing usually comes easily to me. But my voice wasn't ready, and I felt scared."

Owl nodded thoughtfully. "Being honest about what happened is very brave, Bella. Now, what do you think you could do differently?"

Bella thought carefully. "I could practice every day, drink lots of water to keep my throat healthy, and maybe do warm-up exercises like the other birds?"

"Those are wonderful ideas!" said Owl. "Preparation helps us do our best, even when we're naturally good at something."

From that day on, Bella made a special routine. Every morning, she would drink fresh dewdrops, do her vocal warm-ups, and practice her songs. She learned that taking care of her voice was just as important as having talent.

A month later, there was another concert. This time, Bella was ready. Her voice rang out clear and beautiful across the rooftop. "La-la-la-la!" she sang, and everyone cheered.

After her performance, little Robin asked, "Bella, how did you sound so amazing?"

Bella smiled proudly. "I prepared! I practiced every day, kept my voice healthy, and got ready properly. It feels so much better to be prepared than to worry!"

And from that day forward, Bella always remembered that preparation and healthy habits helped her shine her brightest.

The End.
