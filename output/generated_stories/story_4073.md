# Tiko Turtle Finds Time

Tiko was a shy turtle with a soft green shell. He liked quiet places. Today he sat in the musical practice room at school. The room smelled like lemon polish and wood. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. Instruments rested on stands. There were shiny drums, a tall bass, and a tiny flute. A colorful poster showed folk music from many lands.

Tiko hugged his music book. He could hear a clock go tick-tock, tick-tock. The sound made his tummy feel wiggly.

“I want to get better at my drum,” Tiko whispered. “But I also want to go outside and feel the wind.”

His teacher, Ms. Lila, clapped softly. “Hello, friends,” she said. “Today we learn a song from many places. We will tap a West African rhythm, hum a Japanese melody, and sway to a Caribbean beat.”

The room filled with gentle sounds. Tap tap. Hum hum. Swish sway. It felt like a warm hug of music from around the world.

Tiko loved the soft hum. He closed his eyes. But then he looked at the window. He could see bright leaves dancing outside. He wanted to go out and touch the cool grass.

His friend, Milo the chipmunk, bounced up. “After class, let’s race to the oak tree!” Milo squeaked.

Tiko’s heart thumped. “I want to,” he said. “But I also need practice. I am slow. I am shy. I need time.”

Ms. Lila heard them. She smiled kind eyes. “Time is like a friend,” she said. “We can plan it. We can share it. We can make it work.”

“How?” asked Tiko. He pulled his head in a little. His shell felt safe. His cheeks felt warm.

Ms. Lila brought out a big sand timer. The sand sparkled like tiny stars. “We can use a plan,” she said. “We can practice, then play outside. We can make time for both.”

Tiko peeked at the timer. “Will there be enough?” he asked.

“We will make enough,” said Ms. Lila. “Let’s try.”

She drew a simple chart on the board with three boxes.

Box one said: Warm-up taps — 5 minutes.
Box two said: World song practice — 10 minutes.
Box three said: Outside rhythm walk — 10 minutes.

“What is a rhythm walk?” Tiko asked.

“We take our music outside,” Ms. Lila said. “We listen to wind, leaves, and birds. We tap the beat with our feet. That is our compromise. We practice and we go outdoors.”

Tiko’s eyes grew bright. “I like that plan,” he said.

“Me too!” cheeped Milo.

They flipped the timer. Soft sand began to fall. Tick-tock, went the clock. Shhhh, went the sand.

“Warm-up taps,” said Ms. Lila.

Tiko held a small hand drum. He tapped gently. Tap… tap… tap. The drum felt smooth. It smelled like clean leather. The sound was soft like rain.

“Good steady beat,” Ms. Lila said. “You are keeping time.”

Tiko smiled a tiny smile.

“World song practice,” said Ms. Lila when the sand was done. She flipped the timer again.

They learned names of rhythms and songs with care and respect. “This rhythm is called ‘kuku’ from West Africa,” she said. “We honor the people who made it.” Tap-ta-ta, tap-ta. “This melody is from Japan,” she hummed. “We bow to the music’s home.” La-la-la. “This beat is from the Caribbean,” she swayed. “We thank the islands.” Bum-bum, swish.

Tiko listened and tried. His shy voice hummed. His slow hands tapped. Sometimes he missed a beat. He felt a pinch of worry.

“It’s okay,” Ms. Lila said softly. “Breathe. Count. One, two, three, four. Time can help.”

Tiko breathed in. He counted. One, two, three, four. His hands found the beat again. Tap-ta-ta, tap-ta.

The sand slid down. “Outside rhythm walk,” said Ms. Lila. “Bring your drum.”

They opened the door. Fresh air kissed Tiko’s face. The grass smelled sweet. The sky was bright and blue. Leaves whispered, swish, swish. Birds chirped peep-peep.

“Listen,” Ms. Lila said. “What do you hear?”

“I hear wind,” Tiko said. “Swish.” He tapped a soft swish on his drum.

“I hear my steps,” Milo said. “Pat-pat.” He patted his cheeks and laughed.

Tiko stood tall. “I can use a plan. I can practice. I can also play. That is our compromise.”

They stopped by the oak tree. Ms. Lila turned the timer one more time. “One last try,” she said.

Tiko counted. One, two, three, four. Tap-ta-ta, tap-ta. La-la-la. Bum-bum, swish. The wind joined. The leaves danced. Tiko’s shy heart felt brave. He was on time. He was in the song.

Milo clapped. “You did it, Tiko!”

Tiko blushed and smiled wide. “The plan helped,” he said. “Time is my friend. I can share it. I can make it work.”

Back in the practice room, Tiko drew his own little chart on a sticky note.

Practice taps — 5.
World song — 10.
Outside rhythm walk — 10.

He put the note in his music book. He patted his drum. “I will use my plan every day,” he whispered.

Ms. Lila nodded. “You found your time. You honored the music. You made a good compromise. Well done.”

Tiko took a deep breath. He felt calm and proud. He looked at the window and did not worry. He had a plan.

The lesson: When we make a simple plan and share our time, we can practice, play outside, and honor others’ music too.

The End.
