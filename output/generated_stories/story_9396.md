# Ollie's Secret Hideaway

Ollie the otter loved his special hideaway in the big closet behind Mama's winter coats. The soft, fuzzy coats made perfect walls for his secret fort. He could smell the sweet lavender sachets Mama kept there, and the wooden hangers made gentle clicking sounds when he moved around.

"This is the best hideaway ever!" Ollie whispered to himself as he arranged his favorite toys in a circle. His red ball, his squeaky fish, and his colorful blocks made his hideaway feel just right.

Every morning, Ollie would slip into his closet fort to play. But today was different. Today, his tummy made a loud "GRUMBLE!" sound.

"Oh no," Ollie said, touching his fuzzy belly. "I'm so hungry, but I don't want to leave my hideaway to eat breakfast."

Ollie tried to ignore his hungry tummy, but it kept making louder and louder sounds. "GRUMBLE! RUMBLE! GROWL!"

His friend Bella Bunny hopped by the closet. "Ollie? Are you in there? I hear funny noises!"

"It's just my tummy," Ollie called out sadly. "I'm hungry, but I want to keep playing in my fort."

"Why don't you eat breakfast first?" Bella asked, her soft ears twitching with concern.

"But then I'll have to leave my hideaway," Ollie sighed. "And what if I forget something important for my games?"

Bella's pink nose wiggled as she thought. "Maybe you could prepare differently tomorrow?"

"What do you mean?" Ollie asked, peeking his whiskered face out from behind the coats.

"Well," Bella said gently, "what if you ate a good breakfast BEFORE coming to your hideaway? And what if you brought some healthy snacks with you?"

Ollie's dark eyes grew wide. "That's a great idea! But I feel bad that I didn't think of that myself. I was so excited to play that I forgot to take care of my body first."

The next morning, Ollie took a deep breath and did something brave. He went to Mama Otter first thing.

"Mama," he said, his voice a little shaky, "I'm sorry I skipped breakfast yesterday. I was so excited about my hideaway that I forgot to eat. Can you help me prepare better?"

Mama Otter's whiskers twitched with a warm smile. "Of course, my brave little otter! I'm proud of you for apologizing and asking for help."

Together, they prepared a wonderful breakfast of fish cakes and berries. Ollie's tummy felt happy and full. Then Mama helped him pack a small basket with healthy snacks: apple slices, cheese cubes, and a water bottle.

"Now you're prepared for a whole day of playing!" Mama said, giving Ollie a fuzzy hug.

Ollie carried his snack basket to his closet hideaway. The familiar smell of lavender welcomed him, and the soft coats felt even cozier now that his tummy wasn't grumbling.

"Bella!" he called excitedly. "Come see my prepared hideaway!"

Bella hopped over and gasped with delight. "Wow, Ollie! You have everything you need!"

They played happily all morning. When Ollie felt hungry, he munched on his apple slices. When he felt thirsty, he sipped his water. His body felt strong and happy, which made playing even more fun.

"Preparing ahead made everything better," Ollie told Bella as they shared some cheese cubes. "I learned that taking care of my body first helps me play better and longer!"

From that day on, Ollie always prepared for his hideaway adventures by eating well and bringing healthy snacks. His closet fort became an even more magical place because he felt good inside and out.

The End.
