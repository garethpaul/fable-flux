# Buzzy Beaver’s Bubble Plan

Buzzy the busy beaver loved to move, wiggle, and work. He stacked sticks, swished his tail, and splashed in puddles. One morning, Buzzy scurried into the bright, echo-y bathroom. Drip, drip went the faucet. The floor felt cool on his paws, and the air smelled like minty soap.

“Oh! Bubbles!” Buzzy chirped, peeking at a shiny bottle. “I want a super bubble bath right now!”

He turned the faucet. Whoosh! Warm water rushed into the tub. Steam rose up and tickled his whiskers. Buzzy squeezed the bubble bottle—squish, squish. “More bubbles! More fun!” he sang, shaking his tail. The bubbles puffed up like little white clouds.

Buzzy climbed onto the rug and hopped in the tub—splash! Water sloshed over the side. “Whee!” he giggled. He swam and kicked. He did tail-wiggle jumps. He made a hat out of foam. But the water kept climbing higher and higher.

Glub. Glub. The bubbles stacked up to his chin.

Buzzy blinked. His heart thumped fast. “Uh-oh,” he whispered. “Too many bubbles.”

The faucet sang on and on. Whoooooosh.

Buzzy’s belly wobbled. His paws felt slippery. “I want to keep playing,” he told himself, “but I need to slow down.” He took a big belly breath. In through his nose. Out through his mouth. “I can make a plan,” he said softly. “I can use control.”

Just then, Mama Beaver called from the hall, “Buzzy? Are you okay?”

Buzzy looked at the silver drain chain and the shiny faucet handle. He looked at the towel. He remembered how Mama said, “When we prepare, we feel safe. When we pause, we choose.”

“I’m okay, Mama!” Buzzy called back. “I’m using my plan!”

He put one paw on his chest and hummed, “Bzzzz,” like a gentle bee. The hum made his chest feel warm and steady. He counted, “One… two… three.” Then he reached with slow, careful paws. Turn. Turn. Creak. He turned the faucet off. The whoosh stopped. The bathroom got quiet, except for tiny drip-drop sounds.

“That’s better,” Buzzy said. He peeked at the drain. “Now I need to make room.” He pulled the silver chain. Swirl, swirl. The water spun like a little merry-go-round. The bubbles slowly sank.

Buzzy smiled. “I did it! I used my calm.” He lifted his head and took another breath. “Now I can clean and play safely.”

Mama Beaver opened the door just a crack. “I hear quiet,” she said. “What happened, helper?”

Buzzy grinned. “I wanted the biggest bubble bath ever. But it got too big. I paused. I breathed. I turned off the water and opened the drain. I made a plan!”

Mama nodded. “That is self-regulation,” she said softly. “You noticed your body. You used your breath. You chose what to do. That is being strong and smart.”

Buzzy’s eyes sparkled. “Strong and smart,” he repeated. “Can I make a bathroom plan chart?”

“Yes,” said Mama. “Let’s prepare together.”

They made a simple chart with pictures: a towel, a bath mat, the soap, the faucet, and a smiling beaver taking deep breaths. Buzzy stuck the chart on the wall with a silly slap. Smack!

“Now I’m ready,” he said. He put the towel near the tub. He laid the bath mat flat so it wouldn’t slip. He lined up his scrub brush and his toothbrush. He wiggled with happy energy.

“Before I splash,” Buzzy told himself, “I do my calm steps.” He touched his nose, took a slow breath, and buzzed a gentle “Bzzzz.”

He did little exercise moves to warm up his body: “Three tail taps,” tap-tap-tap on the mat. “Two knee bends,” bend-bend. “One big stretch,” arms up high. He giggled. “My body is ready. My brain is ready.”

Then Buzzy climbed in carefully. Swoosh, splash. This time he turned the faucet on just a tiny bit. Drip, drip, drip. He counted to five and then turned it off. He squeezed a small dot of bubbles—just enough to smell sweet and see foam. He scrubbed his shoulders. He washed his paws. He hummed while he cleaned behind his ears. The warm water felt soft. The bubbles tickled his nose.

“I am calm and curious,” Buzzy said. “I can explore with care.” He dipped a cup in and poured slowly. He watched the water make rain. Pitter-patter. He listened to the plink of drops in the tub. He felt proud.

When he finished, he pulled the drain again. Swirl. He dried his fur with his towel—rub, rub, rub. He put the bubble bottle back. He hung the towel neat and straight. He looked at his chart and gave it a thumbs-up.

Mama came back in and smiled. “Your bathroom is tidy. Your body is clean. Your heart is calm.”

Buzzy puffed out his chest. “I did my plan,” he said. “Pause, breathe, choose. Prepare, then play. Exercise to get ready!”

Mama hugged him. “What a busy, brave beaver you are.”

Buzzy hugged back. “Next time I feel too wiggly,” he said, “I will buzz, breathe, and choose.”

He tapped the chart, put his paws on his belly, and hummed, “Bzzzz.” His body felt peaceful, and his mind felt bright.

The End.
