# Whiskers and the Rainbow Plan

Whiskers was a clever cat with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. Today, Whiskers padded into the creative art room. The room smelled like fresh paper and sweet glue. Sunlight warmed the colorful rugs. Paints, brushes, and shiny beads glowed on the shelves.

“Hello, art friends!” Whiskers purred. Children from many places smiled and waved. Maya wore a bright headband with tiny mirrors. Jun had star stickers on his sleeves. Amina wore a bold, patterned scarf. Ben rolled paint like a little steam train. Everyone felt welcome.

Ms. Lila clapped gently. “Today we make a Friendship Rainbow,” she said. “We will follow directions, take turns, and keep our space tidy. When we feel stuck, we can take a calm breath and try again.”

Whiskers’ whiskers twitched. “I love rainbows!” he meowed. He grabbed a red brush. Splot! The paint was thick and shiny. He wanted to make the biggest stripe.

“First,” Ms. Lila said kindly, “we draw a simple pencil curve. Then we paint inside the lines.”

Whiskers’ ears perked. But his paws felt bouncy. “I can do it fast!” he said. He dipped, swish, swish, swirled—and painted outside the line. The red stripe looked wiggly and messy. A drip rolled down. Plop!

Whiskers’ tail drooped. “Oh no. It’s not perfect.”

Jun whispered, “It’s okay, Whiskers. We can fix it.”

Whiskers felt a hot, tight feeling in his tummy. His paws felt too busy. “I’m frustrated,” he said in a small voice.

Ms. Lila knelt beside him. “Thank you for telling us. When we feel frustrated, we can pause. Let’s try the Rainbow Plan.”

“What’s the Rainbow Plan?” asked Whiskers.

“Three steps,” said Ms. Lila. She held up her fingers. “One: Stop and breathe. Two: Ask for help or look at the directions. Three: Try again slowly.”

Whiskers took a slow breath in. He smelled lemony wipes and wet paint. He breathed out. “Shhhhhh.” His body felt softer.

“Let’s look at the directions,” Ms. Lila said. She pointed to a simple chart: Draw line. Paint inside. Wipe drips. Put brush back.

Whiskers nodded. “I can try again.”

Maya brought a small cloth. “Here, we can blot the drip. My aunt taught me to press, not rub.” She dabbed gently. “In my culture, we make mirror art. We go slow so the mirrors shine.” She smiled. “Going slow can be special.”

“Thank you,” said Whiskers. He drew a new curve with a pencil. Scratch, scratch. He painted inside the line. Swish, swish. This time the red stripe looked neat. His tail lifted with hope.

Next came orange. Jun said, “I will hold the cup so it doesn’t tip.” Ben said, “I’ll pass the brush when you’re ready.” Amina hummed a soft tune. “In my family, we sing while we work. It helps us move together.”

Whiskers dipped the orange brush. “Stop, breathe, check, then paint,” he whispered. He painted a smooth orange stripe. The room sounded like soft brushing and happy humming.

Soon, yellow, green, blue, and purple glowed. The rainbow curves looked bright and cozy. Little beads in bowls shivered with tiny clinks. “Let’s add patterns from our cultures,” Amina said. “Dots, triangles, waves.”

Whiskers wanted to glue all the beads at once. His paws wiggled again. “So many choices!” The tight feeling came back. He felt his breath get fast.

Ms. Lila smiled. “Rainbow Plan?”

Whiskers put a paw on his chest. “Stop.” He took a slow breath in. “Breathe.” He looked at the chart. “Ask for help.”

“What pattern should I try?” he asked.

Amina showed a gentle wave pattern. “We call this a river line.”

Maya placed tiny mirrors in a sun shape. “I can show you how to press, not push.”

Jun lined up star stickers. “One at a time. We can count.”

Whiskers counted with them. “One, two, three.” Press. Click. The bead made a soft tick sound. His paws stayed calm. His tummy felt fine.

Soon the rainbow sparkled with mirrors, stars, dots, and waves. It looked like a hug from everyone’s ideas. The table stayed tidy. Brushes sat in their cups. Caps clicked on the glue. The floor was clear. Whiskers liked that everything had a place.

Ms. Lila clapped again. “You followed directions. You stayed organized. You used the Rainbow Plan to cope with frustration. And you listened to each other.”

Whiskers purred. “I learned to stop, breathe, ask, and try again. Going slow helps my art shine.”

Maya nodded. “And we shared our styles.”

Jun grinned. “We took turns.”

Amina hummed. “We included everyone.”

They hung the Friendship Rainbow on the wall. Sunlight kissed the mirrors. The colors glowed warm and bright. Whiskers’ heart felt warm too.

“Friends,” said Ms. Lila, “what is our lesson today?”

Whiskers lifted his chin. “When I feel frustrated, I can pause and breathe. I can follow directions and keep my space tidy. With friends and patience, I can try again.”

Everyone cheered. The room smelled like clean water now and sounded like happy purrs and soft claps.

Whiskers wrapped his tail around his paws. “I am a clever cat,” he said, “and a calm one too.”

The End.
