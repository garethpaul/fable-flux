# Beep-Beep Bird Chooses

Chirpy was a small, bright blue bird who loved to sing. His feathers were soft like clouds, and his voice was sweet like honey. One sunny morning, Chirpy sat on the open window of a family car. The car smelled like oranges from a little air freshener. The seats were warm and squishy. A gentle breeze tickled Chirpy’s wings.

“Beep-beep!” sang Chirpy. “We are going! We are going!”

Inside the car sat Mama, Dad, and little Maya in her car seat. Maya’s shoes had tiny stars that sparkled. She clapped when Chirpy sang.

“Hi, Chirpy!” said Maya. “Come sing with us! We are going to the park!”

Chirpy hopped inside and perched on the soft headrest. “Sing and ride, sing and ride!” he chirped. He loved the hum of the engine and the whoosh of the road. It made a nice beat for his songs.

Soon, the car stopped at a light. Mama gave Maya two snacks to choose from. One was a crunchy apple slice in a little cup. The other was a crinkly bag of extra-salty chips.

“Maya, you can pick one,” said Mama. “What is your choice?”

Maya looked at the apple. It smelled fresh and sweet. She looked at the chips. The bag crinkled loudly. Chirpy’s eyes grew wide.

“I like crunchy,” Chirpy sang. “Crunchy, munchy, yummy! Pick the chips!”

Maya smiled at Chirpy. “Chips!” she said. She grabbed the bag and shook it. Crinkle, crinkle. Chirpy bounced and sang louder. “Chips, chips, chips!”

The light turned green. The car rolled on. Maya munched. Crunch, crunch, crunch. The salt made her thirsty. “Water, please,” Maya said. Dad passed a water bottle to Mama. Mama helped Maya take a sip.

Soon, the bag was empty. Maya frowned. “My tummy feels funny,” she said softly. Her face looked a little pale.

Mama glanced back. “Oh, honey. Maybe too many salty chips in the car made your tummy unhappy.”

Chirpy’s song wobbled. He looked at the crinkly empty bag. He looked at Maya’s sad eyes. “Oh,” he peeped. His feathers felt heavy. He wanted to help, not hurt.

The car turned into a shady rest stop with trees and birds singing. Cheep-cheep. The air smelled like pine and sunshine. Dad opened a window wider. Fresh air flowed in. Whoosh.

“Maya,” Mama said, “let’s rest. Would you like a small bite of apple now? It is gentle on tummies.”

Maya nodded. Crunch. The apple was cool, juicy, and sweet. Chirpy listened to the crunch. It sounded calm, not crinkly or loud.

Chirpy fluttered closer to Maya’s seat. “Maya,” he said in a tiny, careful voice, “I am sorry. I sang for chips. I did not think about your tummy. I did not make a good choice song.”

Maya looked at Chirpy and patted his soft feathers. “It’s okay,” she said. “You can help me next time.”

Chirpy’s heart felt warm. He took a deep breath. He listened to the trees. He listened to his own small voice. He wanted to make a better choice.

“New song,” Chirpy decided. “Think, choose, feel good.”

He sang, soft and bright:
“Stop and think,
What do I need?
A happy tummy,
A gentle feed.
Sip some water,
Crunch an apple,
Make a choice
That helps me travel.”

Mama smiled. “That is a thoughtful song, Chirpy.”

After a little rest, Maya felt better. The car started again. Vroom, vroom. The seat belts clicked. Click, click. The windows let in a cool breeze that smelled like grass.

At the next light, Dad said, “We have a long ride. Maya, would you like a game or a calm song?”

Maya looked at Chirpy. He waited, quiet and kind. “I will choose a calm song,” she said. “Please, Chirpy.”

Chirpy nodded. He made his voice soft like a blanket. He sang about flowers, clouds, and gentle rain. Pitter-patter, pitter-patter. Maya hummed along. Her tummy felt comfy now. She took small sips of water. She held one more apple slice for later.

Then they saw the park up ahead. The swings looked like friendly smiles. The sky was big and blue. When the car parked, Maya unbuckled with Mama’s help. She took a deep breath and stretched. “I feel good,” she said. She looked at Chirpy. “Thank you for the new song.”

Chirpy puffed his feathers with pride. “I am learning,” he said. “First, I say sorry. Then I stop and think. Then I make a choice that helps.”

Mama knelt down and hugged Maya. “That is decision-making,” she said. “When we stop and think, we choose what keeps us healthy and happy.”

Chirpy flew in a small happy circle. “Decision-making!” he sang. “Think, choose, feel good!”

They all laughed. The breeze smelled like fresh cut grass. A faraway bell went ding-dong. Chirpy started a final tune, bright and clear:
“When you are in the car,
Or near or far,
Stop and think,
Before you pick a snack or a song.
Choose what helps you feel strong.
Say sorry when you need to,
Then try again, that’s what we do.”

Maya clapped. “Let’s play!” she said. They ran to the swings, ready for fun, with calm tummies, kind hearts, and a new song to guide their choices.

The End.
