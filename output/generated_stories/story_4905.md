# Benny Bear's Big Game Day

Benny Bear was a fluffy brown cub who loved to play soccer with his friends. His soft fur was always a little messy from rolling around in the grass, and his bright brown eyes sparkled when he smiled.

One sunny Saturday morning, Benny woke up excited. "Today is the big soccer game!" he said, stretching his paws high in the air. The game started at ten o'clock, and Mama Bear had told him to be ready by nine-thirty.

Benny looked at the big clock on his wall. The short hand pointed to eight, and the long hand pointed to twelve. "Eight o'clock!" he said proudly. He had learned to tell time just last week.

But instead of getting ready, Benny's curious nose caught a wonderful smell. "Mmm, pancakes!" He ran to the kitchen where Mama Bear was flipping golden, fluffy pancakes.

"Good morning, sleepyhead," Mama Bear said with a warm smile. "Remember, you need to brush your teeth, wash your face, and get dressed for your soccer game."

"I have lots of time!" Benny said, gobbling up three syrupy pancakes. They tasted so sweet and warm.

After breakfast, Benny found his favorite toy truck in the living room. "Just a quick game," he thought. He made vrooming sounds as he pushed the bright red truck around the carpet. The truck's wheels made a satisfying clicking sound on the wooden floor.

Tick-tock, tick-tock went the clock.

Suddenly, Benny heard Mama Bear calling. "Benny! It's nine o'clock! You need to get ready now!"

Benny's eyes grew wide. "Oh no!" He had been playing for a whole hour! He rushed to the bathroom, his paws patting quickly on the tile floor.

In the bathroom, Benny looked at himself in the mirror. His fur was still messy from sleeping, and he could see bits of pancake around his mouth. The bathroom smelled fresh and clean, like the mint soap Mama Bear used.

"I need to hurry!" Benny said. He grabbed his toothbrush and squeezed on some strawberry toothpaste. As he brushed, he thought about his soccer team counting on him.

But then Benny stopped. He looked at his reflection again. His teeth weren't very clean, and he had only brushed for a few seconds.

"Mama always says to brush for two whole minutes," Benny said to himself. "And I should wash behind my ears too."

Benny felt worried. If he did everything properly, he might be late. If he rushed, he wouldn't be clean for his team. What should he do?

Then Benny remembered what his soccer coach always said: "Good players are honest players."

Benny took a deep breath. He decided to be honest with himself and do things the right way, even if it took longer. He brushed his teeth for the full two minutes, counting "One Mississippi, two Mississippi..." The minty foam felt cool and tingly in his mouth.

He washed his face with warm, soapy water and scrubbed behind his ears until they felt squeaky clean. Then he combed his fur until it was neat and fluffy.

When Benny ran downstairs, Mama Bear was waiting with his soccer uniform. "I'm sorry I took so long," Benny said honestly. "I got distracted, but I made sure to clean up properly."

Mama Bear hugged him tight. "I'm proud of you for being honest, Benny. And look - we still have ten minutes before we need to leave!"

At the soccer game, Benny played his best ever. He felt confident and fresh, and his teammates said he looked great.

"Next time," Benny told himself, "I'll watch the clock and start getting ready earlier. That way I can do everything right AND have time left over!"

The End.
