# Leo’s Listening Plan

The brave little lion cub was named Leo. Leo lived in a playful living room with bright pillows, a soft blue rug, and a bouncy couch that went boing when he hopped. Sunlight made warm stripes on the floor. Toys made friendly clinks, and a music box sat on the shelf, shiny and silver.

One afternoon, Leo’s friend, a tiny drum shaped like a turtle, went thump-thump when he tapped it. “I want to make a song!” Leo roared softly. His roar sounded like purr-rumble, not too loud. He pulled out a rattly shaker, a jingly tambourine, and a little xylophone with rainbow bars.

Leo tried to play all the sounds at once. “Rattle! Jingle! Boom! Ping!” The room got very noisy. The couch bounced. The pillows tumbled. Leo’s ears twitched.

“I can’t hear my song,” Leo said. His tail drooped. “Maybe I’m not good at music.”

Just then, Mama Lion came in with a kind smile. She smelled like warm cookies. “What’s the matter, brave cub?”

“I want to make music,” Leo said. “But it sounds messy.”

Mama sat on the rug. “When something is tricky, we can use a plan. That’s called strategy,” she said slowly. “A plan helps our brain. Would you like to try a music plan?”

Leo blinked. “A plan? Like a map?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Step by step. We listen, we choose, and we try.”

Leo’s ears perked up. “I can do that!”

Mama clapped softly. “First step: Quiet. Let’s listen.”

They both sat still. The room hummed. Tick-tock went the clock. The window breeze whispered shhh. Leo breathed in. He smelled crayons and cookies.

“I hear the clock,” Leo whispered. “Tick. Tock.”

“Good listening,” Mama said. “Second step: Choose one sound.”

Leo touched the turtle drum. “Thump?” He tapped once. Thump. It sounded like a slow heart.

“Nice and steady,” Mama said. “Third step: Add one more sound.”

Leo picked up the shaker. He shook it gently. “Shhh-shhh,” it said. He tried thump, shhh, thump, shhh. He nodded. “It feels like walking!”

“Fourth step: Try a pattern,” Mama said. “A pattern is a repeat.”

Leo smiled. “Thump, thump, shhh. Thump, thump, shhh.” He tapped twice, then shook. The pattern made his paws warm and happy.

“Fifth step: Check how it sounds,” Mama said. “Do we like it?”

Leo listened with his whole face. He felt the beat in his belly. “I like it,” he said. “It’s my song.”

Mama beamed. “Sixth step: Add your voice.”

Leo took a brave breath. “La-la… ROAR!” He giggled. His roar was friendly, like rolling thunder far away. He sang again, softer. “La-la, roar,” he said to the beat. Thump, thump, shhh. La-la, roar.

The living room seemed to dance. The sun stripes looked like golden piano keys. The couch went boing-bop when Leo moved, but he kept his pattern steady. He felt strong and clever.

“I made music!” Leo said. His tail swished big happy swishes.

“You used strategy,” Mama said. “You listened, chose, and tried. You checked and added your voice. That’s a great plan.”

Leo stood taller. “I am good at music. I just needed a plan.” His whiskers tingled with pride.

He wanted to share. “Mama, will you play too?”

Mama picked up the jingly tambourine. “I will follow your pattern,” she said.

Leo tapped. Thump, thump, shhh. Mama jingled on the shhh. Jingle! The sounds fit like puzzle pieces. Leo laughed. “It’s a music team!”

Then Leo saw the xylophone. It gleamed like a rainbow. He paused and took a breath. “Strategy,” he told himself. “One change at a time.”

He tried one soft ping on the red bar at the end of his pattern. Thump, thump, shhh… ping. He listened. It was sweet, like a tiny bell. He tried again. Thump, thump, shhh… ping. It felt just right.

Mama nodded. “You checked your plan and made a gentle change. That is smart.”

Leo felt brave all over. “I can make a plan for other things too,” he said. “If my tower falls, I can build slow. If my puzzle is hard, I can look for corner pieces. If I feel wiggly, I can breathe and count.”

Mama hugged him. Her fur was soft and warm. “Yes, my clever cub. Strategy helps us do hard things. And doing hard things helps us believe in ourselves.”

Leo finished his song with a proud little roar. “La-la, roar!” The room glowed. The last ping rang like a star.

He bowed to his toys. “Thank you, band.” The turtle drum seemed to smile. The shaker whispered shhh like a secret friend.

Leo looked at Mama. “I did it,” he said.

“You did,” Mama said. “You made a plan, used your ears, and believed in you.”

Leo puffed his chest. “I am a brave little lion cub. I can make music with my plan.”

And from that day, whenever things felt noisy or hard, Leo would stop, listen, and make a little plan. His songs grew bright, his steps grew steady, and his heart grew strong.

The lesson: When something is tricky, use a simple plan—listen, choose, try, and check. With strategy and a brave heart, you can make your own music and feel proud.

The End.
