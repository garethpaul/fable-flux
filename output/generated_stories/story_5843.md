# Miko the Mouse and the Gentle Story

In a sunny garage workshop, tools hung like shiny moons on the wall. There was the soft smell of wood and a warm hum from a little fan. Sawdust made tiny golden clouds in the light. In a small cardboard house by the toolbox lived Miko, a kind mouse with bright eyes and soft whiskers.

Every morning, Miko swept his tiny floor with a feather. He loved to help keep the workshop neat. He also loved stories. He made up stories when he worked. He told them to the screws and nails in their jars.

“Good morning,” Miko squeaked. “Today’s story is about a brave little bolt who learns to wait.”

Clink, clink, went the jars, as if they liked listening.

One day, as Miko pushed a crumb into his lunch tin, he heard a loud clatter. Bang! Clang! A wooden car project on the workbench shook and a wheel rolled off the edge. It bumped onto the floor and stopped by Miko’s feet.

“Oh no,” Miko said, picking up the smooth wheel. “Who is upset?”

From behind a paint can popped Trixie, a tiny toy robot with a loose arm. Her button eyes flashed. “I am! I am mad!” she buzzed. “The glue is sticky and slow. I wanted the car to be done now. I pulled the wheel, and it fell!”

Miko placed a gentle paw on the wheel. He could feel Trixie’s angry buzz in the air, like a bee. “I hear your feelings,” he said softly. “It is hard to wait.”

“I hate waiting!” Trixie shouted. She stomped her little metal foot. Tap! Tap! Tap!

Miko took a deep breath. He remembered his own wiggly feelings when he had to wait for crumbs to cool. “Let’s sit,” he said. “Let’s listen to a story. Stories can help feelings calm down.”

Trixie crossed her tiny arms, but she sat. Miko climbed onto a spool of thread. He set the wheel between them like a round table.

He spoke in a warm, slow voice. “Once, in a busy workshop, there lived a little spark named Zip. Zip wanted to light up a lamp right away. ‘Now, now, now!’ Zip crackled. The switch said, ‘Click, not yet. The wire needs to rest.’ Zip felt hot with mad. Zip wanted to sizzle. But a friend mouse—small and kind—said, ‘Let’s count our breaths. In… one. Out… one. In… two. Out… two.’ Zip counted, and the hot feeling cooled. The lamp glowed, soft and steady.”

Trixie’s eyes dimmed a bit. “Did counting really help Zip?” she asked, her voice quieter.

Miko nodded. “Yes. When we breathe slow, we tell our bodies, ‘It is okay.’ Would you like to try with me?”

Trixie looked at the rolling wheel. “Okay,” she said.

Together they breathed. In… one. Out… one. In… two. Out… two. Miko listened to the fan hum and smelled the sweet wood. Trixie’s metal shoulders loosened. Her angry buzz faded to a tiny purr.

“How do you feel now?” Miko asked.

“Better,” Trixie said. “Not so hot. Not so buzzy.”

Miko smiled. “That is the magic of a gentle story and a slow breath.”

He looked at the wheel. “Now we have a job. We need to fix what fell. Fixing is part of being responsible.”

Trixie nodded. “I made the mess. I can help fix it.”

They climbed the wood stool together. Miko tugged a small cloth. “First,” he said, “we clean the glue spot.” He dabbed-dabbed with the cloth. “Second, we wait so it can dry a little.” He giggled. “Waiting again!”

Trixie gave a tiny laugh. “We can tell another story while we wait.”

“Yes,” said Miko. “A waiting story.”

He began. “Once there was a wooden car who dreamed of rolling far. The car felt wobbly and worried. A friend said, ‘Let’s learn. Let’s listen. Let’s take turns.’ They watched how glue held things together. They learned that strong things take time.”

They watched the drop of glue shine like a tiny star. The workshop smelled sweet and sharp. The fan hummed. Somewhere, a clock ticked like a soft drum.

Miko touched the glue. “Almost ready,” he said. He pressed the wheel on, gentle and true. “Now we count to five while I hold it. One… two… three… four… five.”

Trixie held Miko’s tail for balance. “I am helping,” she whispered.

“You are,” said Miko. “You are being careful. You are being responsible.”

When Miko let go, the wheel stayed. The car sat straight and proud. Trixie’s eyes lit up bright and happy. “We did it!”

“We did,” Miko said. “And we did it with a story, slow breaths, and careful hands.”

Trixie looked down at her own loose arm. “Maybe I can wait for my arm too,” she said. “I will not pull or stomp. I will ask for help and breathe.”

Miko nodded. “We can fix it after snack. First, we clean up.” He swept tiny glue strings into a bin. Trixie put the cloth back. They made the space neat again.

“Thank you for your story,” Trixie said. “It made my mad feel smaller.”

“Stories can hold our feelings,” Miko said. “They can teach our hearts how to be calm and kind.”

The fan hummed. The wood smelled warm. The jars went clink, clink, like applause. The little car gleamed on the workbench, ready for a gentle roll another day.

Trixie waved. “Tell me another story tomorrow?”

Miko smiled and tucked the feather broom away. “Yes. Tomorrow we will learn more. We will breathe. We will help. We will tell stories that guide us.”

And in the sunny garage workshop, the kind mouse and his friend felt safe, smart, and proud.

The End.
