# Shelly's Treehouse Adventure

The warm sun shone through the leaves as Shelly the turtle slowly climbed up the rope ladder to the treehouse. Her little green feet moved one step at a time. The wooden boards creaked softly under her shell.

"Welcome to our treehouse!" called Rabbit from above. "Come play with us!"

Shelly peeked her head through the treehouse door. Inside, Rabbit and Squirrel were building a tall tower with colorful blocks. The blocks made clicking sounds as they stacked them higher and higher.

"Hi," whispered Shelly very quietly. Her heart beat fast like a little drum. The treehouse felt big and new. She wanted to play, but she also wanted to hide in her shell.

"Want to help us build?" asked Squirrel, holding out a red block. "We're making the tallest tower ever!"

Shelly's tummy felt fluttery. She took a deep breath, just like Mama Turtle taught her. "In through your nose, out through your mouth," she whispered to herself. The breathing made her feel a little calmer.

"I... I might knock it down," Shelly said softly. "My shell is big and bumpy."

"That's okay!" said Rabbit with a big smile. "We can always build it again!"

Shelly thought for a moment. She really wanted to play, but she felt scared. Then she had an idea. "Maybe I could help in a different way?"

She looked around the treehouse with her bright eyes. The afternoon sun made everything warm and golden. She noticed that the blocks were all mixed up in a big pile.

"I could sort the blocks by color!" Shelly said, feeling proud of her idea. "Then you could find the ones you need easier."

"That's a great idea!" said Squirrel, clapping her tiny paws together.

Shelly started sorting. She made neat piles of red blocks, blue blocks, yellow blocks, and green blocks. Her careful turtle movements were perfect for this job. She didn't rush. She took her time.

"Wow, Shelly!" said Rabbit. "Now we can build even better!"

As they worked together, Shelly felt her worried feelings getting smaller. When she felt nervous, she remembered to breathe slowly. When the other animals moved too fast, she asked them to slow down a little.

"Could we take turns?" Shelly asked when Squirrel was moving very quickly. "I need a little more time."

"Of course!" said Squirrel. "Good idea!"

Soon they had built the most amazing tower. It had red blocks on the bottom, then blue, then yellow, with green blocks on top like leaves.

"We did it together!" cheered Rabbit.

Shelly smiled her biggest smile. "I was scared at first," she said. "But I took deep breaths and found my own way to help."

"You were so smart to sort the blocks," said Squirrel. "And brave to tell us what you needed."

As the sun started to set, painting the sky pink and orange, the three friends sat in their treehouse. Shelly felt proud and happy. She had learned that feeling scared was okay, and that taking deep breaths helped her feel better.

"Can we play again tomorrow?" asked Shelly.

"Yes!" said Rabbit and Squirrel together.

Shelly climbed down the rope ladder slowly and carefully. She couldn't wait to tell Mama Turtle about her wonderful day. She had been resourceful, worked with friends, and learned to manage her big feelings all by herself.

The End.
