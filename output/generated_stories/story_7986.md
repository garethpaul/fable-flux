# Timmy Turtle Tells the Truth

In a quiet house on a rainy day, there was a basement lab. It smelled like soap and lemons. Lights blinked, buttons clicked, and little fans went whirr-whirr. Timmy Turtle lived there with his friend, Dr. Dot. Timmy was a shy turtle with a soft green shell and careful steps. He liked to listen more than talk.

“Good morning, Timmy,” said Dr. Dot with a smile. She wore big round glasses and blue gloves. “Today we will test the Bubble-Maker 3000!”

Timmy’s eyes grew wide. “B-bubbles?” he whispered. He loved bubbles. They made soft pops that tickled his ears.

Dr. Dot pointed to a shiny table. On it sat a round machine with a silver dial and a red lever. Next to it were three jars: Sweet Soap, Rainbow Color, and Gentle Air. “We must be careful,” said Dr. Dot. “We listen. We take turns. We tell the truth about what we do. That helps us stay safe.”

Timmy nodded. He listened to the drip-drip of water and the hum of the lights. He felt brave enough to help. Dr. Dot went to the chalkboard to draw. “I’ll write the plan,” she said. “Do not touch the lever yet.”

Timmy watched the lever. It looked so fun, so shiny, so red. He felt a wiggle in his toes. He wanted to see one tiny bubble.

He whispered to himself, “Just a teeny push?” Timmy looked at the stairs. He looked at the chalkboard. He did not want to wait. He slowly reached out… tap.

The Bubble-Maker 3000 hissed. A big bluup sound filled the air. A bubble grew and grew! It was as big as a pumpkin. It smelled like lemons and candy. “Oh!” Timmy squeaked. The bubble floated, brushed his nose, and popped with a soft plip!

Dr. Dot turned around. “Timmy? Did you touch the lever?”

Timmy’s heart went thump, thump. His face felt warm. He wanted to hide in his shell. He thought, Maybe I can say no. Maybe I can say it was the wind. But he remembered Dr. Dot’s words: We tell the truth to stay safe. Timmy took a small breath.

“I… I touched it,” he said in a tiny voice. “I am sorry. I did not wait.”

Dr. Dot walked over and knelt beside him. She listened to Timmy’s shaky breath. She looked into his eyes. “Thank you for being honest,” she said softly. “That was brave. Are you okay?”

Timmy nodded. “I am okay.”

“The machine is okay too,” said Dr. Dot. “But we need to talk and make a plan. That’s called negotiation. We can find a way that feels good for both of us.”

Timmy blinked. “Ne-go-sha-tion?”

“It means we listen to each other,” said Dr. Dot. “We share what we want. We make a fair plan.”

Timmy rubbed his shell. “I wanted to see a bubble. I did not want to wait. But I do want to be safe.”

“I want you to learn and have fun,” said Dr. Dot. “I also want to keep the lab safe. What if we make a deal?” She held up one finger. “One, we only pull the lever when we both say yes.” She held up a second finger. “Two, we take turns pressing buttons.” A third finger. “Three, if we feel wiggly and want to rush, we say it out loud. Then we breathe together.”

Timmy thought. He listened to the tiny ticking clock. He liked the plan. “I can do that,” he said. “Can I have the first turn if I wait and ask?”

Dr. Dot smiled. “That is a good ask. Yes, you may have the first turn after we check the jars.”

They checked the jars together. “Sweet Soap, one spoon,” Timmy read slowly. He dipped the spoon. “Rainbow Color, five drops,” said Dr. Dot. Drip, drip, drip, drip, drip. “Gentle Air, medium,” they said together, and Timmy turned the silver dial to M. It clicked nicely.

Dr. Dot looked at Timmy. “Ready?”

Timmy put his hand on the red lever. He looked at Dr. Dot. “Do we both say yes?”

“Yes,” said Dr. Dot.

“Yes,” whispered Timmy, feeling brave.

He pulled. Whirr! A stream of tiny bubbles floated up. Pink, yellow, green! They smelled sweet and clean. The bubbles kissed Timmy’s cheeks with soft pops. He laughed, a small laugh that grew and grew. “Pop! Pop! Pop!” he sang.

They took turns. Dr. Dot pressed the blue button, and the bubbles made gentle chimes, ting-ting. Timmy pressed the green button, and the bubbles turned into silly shapes: a star, a turtle, a hat! They both clapped.

After a while, they cleaned up. Timmy wiped the table. Dr. Dot washed the spoons. The lab felt calm. The air smelled like lemon and happy bubbles.

Dr. Dot sat with Timmy. “Timmy, I am proud of you,” she said. “You told the truth. You listened. You helped make a fair plan.”

Timmy touched his chest. “When I told the truth, my tummy felt better,” he said. “I like our plan. I like taking turns.”

Dr. Dot nodded. “Honesty helps us trust each other. Listening helps us understand. Negotiation helps us both feel good.”

Timmy smiled a shy, warm smile. “Next time, I will wait and ask. We can say yes together.”

“Deal,” said Dr. Dot, and they bumped fists very gently—tap.

The End.
