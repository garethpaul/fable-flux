# The Sharing Shelves in the Treehouse

In a cozy treehouse, high in a big green tree, lived a kind mouse named Mima. The treehouse smelled like warm wood and pine. Sunlight made little dots on the floor. The wind whispered, “Shhh,” through the leaves.

Mima loved her home. She had tiny cups, soft blankets, and bright buttons. She kept everything neat. “A place for everything, and everything in its place,” Mima said with a smile.

Today was a special day. It was Share Day, a tradition in the tree. Every week, friends came to the treehouse to share stories and small things. Mima tapped a tiny bell. Ding, ding! “Time to get ready!” she said.

She opened a box of colorful ribbons. “These are for bows,” she said. She sniffed cinnamon cookies baking in her little oven. Yum! She hummed a happy song.

Knock, knock! Friends arrived. Pip the chipmunk brought crunchy acorns. Lila the ladybug brought shiny stickers. Tiko the turtle came slowly, carrying a little drum. “Hello!” everyone said. Their voices sounded friendly and bright.

Mima clapped her paws. “Welcome to Share Day! Let’s put our things on the sharing shelves.” She had made labels: Snacks, Music, Craft, and Stories. The labels were big and clear.

Pip peeked at the shelves. “Where do acorns go?” he asked.

“Snacks,” Mima said, pointing. “Right here.” Pip smiled and placed the acorns in a neat pile.

Lila’s stickers stuck to her tiny legs. “Oh no!” she giggled.

Mima knelt and helped gently. “Craft shelf for stickers,” she said. The stickers went in a little basket. “Thank you for sharing, Lila.”

Tiko lifted his drum. “Boom… boom,” he played softly.

“Music shelf,” Mima said. She tapped the label. Tiko smiled. “I like the labels,” he said. “They help me know where to put things.”

Soon, the treehouse felt busy and happy. The cookies smelled sweet. The acorns made a soft crunch. The drum beat went bump-bum-bump. Sunlight warmed everyone.

Mima said, “Today, we share more than things. We share our traditions. I will show you my family ribbon dance.” She tied a blue ribbon on her tail. “My grandma taught me,” she said.

Pip’s eyes were round. “Can we learn too?”

“Yes,” Mima nodded. “Sharing is caring.”

Just then, a little bird, Nori, flew in. Her feathers were ruffled, and her tummy growled. “Tweet… I am hungry,” she said. “I lost my seed bag.”

Mima blinked. She looked at the Snacks shelf. There was only one cinnamon cookie left, the special one with a heart shape. Mima had saved it for herself. She had planned to eat it after the dance.

Her paws felt warm. She thought of the sweet taste. She looked at Nori’s worried eyes. Mima took a breath. “A kind mouse shares,” she whispered to herself.

She picked up the heart cookie and walked to Nori. “Here,” Mima said softly. “This is for you.”

Nori chirped with joy. “For me?”

“Yes,” Mima said. “We share here.” She broke the cookie in half and gave the bigger piece to Nori. Nori nibbled. “Crunch. Mmm. Thank you,” she sang. “You are very kind.”

Pip clapped. Lila smiled. Tiko tapped the drum. “Boom for generosity!” he said.

Mima felt warm inside. Sharing felt good. She still had a small piece to taste. She chewed and smiled.

Then Mima had an idea. “Let’s make a Sharing Plate,” she said. She found a big leaf and wrote Sharing Plate on it. “We can put extra snacks here for anyone who needs them.”

Everyone nodded. Pip added acorns. Lila added a tiny honey candy. Tiko put two raisins from his pocket. “Boom for teamwork,” he whispered.

“Now the ribbon dance,” Mima said. She handed out ribbons—red for Pip, yellow for Lila, green for Tiko, and a little gold ribbon for Nori. “My grandma said, ‘We dance, we share, we care.’”

Mima showed the steps. “Step, swish, turn,” she said. The ribbons whispered, swish-swish, through the air. Pip giggled when his ribbon tickled his nose. Lila spun slowly. Tiko’s drum kept a friendly beat. Nori fluttered and chirped a happy tune.

They danced in a circle. Their ribbons made a rainbow. The treehouse felt full of love. Mima’s whiskers twitched with joy.

After the dance, they cleaned up together. Pip put cups on the shelf. Lila stacked napkins. Tiko rolled up the ribbons. Nori dusted with a soft feather. The labels helped. Everything went back to its place.

Mima rang the tiny bell again. Ding, ding! “Thank you for helping. Thank you for sharing. Today we learned that being generous makes hearts happy,” she said.

Nori chirped, “When I have seeds, I will bring some for the Sharing Plate.”

Pip said, “I will share my acorns with anyone who is hungry.”

Lila said, “I will share stickers when a friend is sad.”

Tiko said, “I will share my beat so friends can dance.”

Mima smiled. “And I will share my traditions and my time,” she said. “When we are generous and work together, our treehouse stays warm, tidy, and full of joy.”

The sun began to set. The leaves glowed golden. The tree whispered, “Good job.” The friends hugged. Their hearts felt light and full.

Mima looked at the Sharing Plate and the tidy shelves. She felt proud and peaceful. “A place for everything,” she said, “and room in our hearts to share.”

The End.
