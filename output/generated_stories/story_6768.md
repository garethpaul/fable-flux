# Nutkin's Neat and Tidy Bathroom Adventure

Nutkin the squirrel lived in a cozy tree house with his family. Every morning, he loved to wash his fluffy tail and brush his tiny teeth in their special bathroom. But today, something was very wrong!

"Oh my acorns!" squeaked Nutkin as he opened the bathroom door. Towels were scattered on the floor like colorful leaves. Toothbrushes rolled around the sink. Soap bottles stood in wobbly towers, ready to tumble down.

His little sister Hazel peeked around the corner. "Sorry, Nutkin," she whispered. "I was playing bathroom restaurant and forgot to clean up."

Nutkin's whiskers twitched as he looked at the messy room. The white tiles felt cold under his paws, and he could smell the sweet lavender soap mixed with minty toothpaste. "Don't worry, Hazel," he said kindly. "Let's work together to make this bathroom sparkle!"

"But how?" asked Hazel, her big brown eyes looking worried.

Nutkin's bushy tail swished with excitement. "We'll build an organization system! First, let's gather everything that's the same."

The two squirrels began their mission. Nutkin showed Hazel how to collect all the towels in one pile. "Feel how soft and fluffy they are," he said as they folded each towel carefully. "Towels go on the towel rack, where they can dry and stay clean for everyone."

Next, they gathered all the toothbrushes. "Each toothbrush has its own special spot in the holder," explained Nutkin. "When we put them back where they belong, no one has to search for their toothbrush."

Hazel giggled as she placed her tiny pink toothbrush next to Nutkin's blue one. "It's like giving each toothbrush its own little home!"

"Exactly!" cheered Nutkin. "Now let's organize the soap bottles."

They lined up the bottles by size - big shampoo bottles in the back, smaller soap bottles in front. The bathroom counter began to look neat and tidy. Nutkin could see their reflection clearly in the mirror now that it wasn't splashed with soap.

"This looks so much better," said Hazel, clapping her tiny paws together.

But Nutkin wasn't finished yet. "Let's build something special to help us stay organized," he suggested. From his acorn collection, he took out a small wooden box. "This can be our bathroom treasure box for small things like hair clips and nail files."

Together, they decorated the box with smooth pebbles they found outside. The pebbles felt cool and bumpy under their paws. Soon, they had created a beautiful storage box that looked like a tiny castle.

"Now everyone in our family will know where to find things," said Nutkin proudly. "And when we're done using something, we can put it right back where it belongs."

Hazel hugged her big brother. "Thank you for helping me clean up and not being angry about my mess."

"That's what families do," smiled Nutkin. "We help each other and take care of our shared spaces."

From that day on, their bathroom stayed neat and organized. Every morning, Nutkin and Hazel would check their special systems and make sure everything was in its proper place. Their parents were so proud of how the two little squirrels learned to work together and respect their family's shared bathroom.

The bathroom sparkled like sunshine on water, and everyone could find exactly what they needed, exactly when they needed it.

The End.
