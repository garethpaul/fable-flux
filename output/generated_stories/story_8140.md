# Koala Kori and the Pantry Plan

Koala Kori was calm and cozy. Kori loved soft leaves, warm hugs, and quiet thinking. One rainy day, Kori padded into the pantry. The pantry smelled like sweet oats and crunchy crackers. It was cool and a little dusty. The rain went pitter-patter on the window.

Kori felt a gentle tummy rumble. “Hmm,” Kori said softly. “I would like a snack.”

On the shelf, Kori saw a big round jar. Inside were bright, golden crackers. The jar’s lid was very tight. Kori reached up with soft, gray paws. Twist, twist. The lid did not move.

Kori took a calm breath. “I can try again,” Kori whispered. “I can improve. I can learn.”

Kori tried once more. Twist, twist. The lid squeaked but stayed stuck. Kori’s ears drooped for a moment. Kori placed a paw on the heart and said, “It’s okay to try many times. I am brave.”

Kori looked around the pantry. There was a wooden spoon, a small cloth, some boxes, and a little step stool. The air smelled like cinnamon and grain. The floor felt cool under Kori’s feet.

“I need a plan,” Kori said. “I can think. I can be careful.”

Kori set the jar on the table. “First, I will make the jar not slippery.” Kori wrapped the small cloth around the lid. The cloth felt rough and grippy. “That is improvement number one!”

Kori tried again. Twist, twist. The lid moved a tiny bit. “Oh!” Kori smiled. “It moved. Small steps count.”

But the lid still would not open. Kori did not cry. Kori took another calm breath. “I can keep trying.”

Kori climbed onto the little step stool. Now Kori was taller and could hold the jar steady. “This is improvement number two,” Kori said. “A steady base helps.”

Twist, twist. The lid squealed. “Eeeek!” It still did not come off.

Kori wiggled a little but stayed calm. “What else can I do? I can craft a tool!”

Kori looked at the wooden spoon. Tap, tap. “No, I will not hit the jar,” Kori said kindly. “I will be gentle. I will protect the jar.”

Kori looked at a rubber band on a bag of oats. “Yes!” Kori slipped the band around the lid, over the cloth. The rubber felt stretchy and snug. “This is improvement number three.”

Kori held the jar, took a slow breath in and out, and tried again. Twist… twist… POP! The lid came off! The crackers smelled warm and toasty.

Kori laughed softly. “I did it. I used my brain, and I was brave, and I improved my plan.”

Just then, little Mouse Mina peeked into the pantry. Mina’s whiskers twitched. “Hi, Kori,” Mina squeaked. “I heard a pop! Are you okay?”

“I am okay,” Kori said. “I had a problem. I made a plan. I tried, changed, and tried again.”

Mina looked shy. “I can’t open jars,” Mina said. “I am small.”

Kori knelt and smiled. “It is okay to be small. We are all different. We accept that. We can help each other.”

Kori gave Mina a cracker. Crunch, crunch. It was salty and crisp. “Thank you,” Mina said. “How did you do it?”

Kori showed the steps. “First, I made the lid grippy with a cloth. Then I stood on the stool to hold the jar steady. Then I used a rubber band to make it even more grippy. I improved my plan each time.”

Mina’s eyes shone. “That is smart problem-solving!”

Kori nodded. “Yes. That is called critical thinking. We look. We listen. We try. We change. We try again.”

Mina looked at a jam jar. “Could we try together?” she asked.

Kori felt a warm, brave glow. “Yes, we can.” Kori placed the cloth around the jam lid. Mina held the jar steady at the bottom. Kori slipped a rubber band over the cloth.

“Ready?” Kori asked.

“Ready,” Mina said.

They both took calm breaths. “In… out…” said Kori.

Twist… twist… POP! The jam lid came off too! The pantry filled with the sweet smell of berries. Mina giggled. “We did it!”

Kori clapped softly. “We improved together. You were brave to try.”

Mina smiled. “And you were gentle and patient. You taught me.”

Kori poured a tiny bit of jam on a cracker. Mina tasted it. “Mmm! Sweet!” Kori tasted too. “Mmm! Yum!”

They cleaned up the cloth, the band, and the spoon. They put the lids back gently. Kori said, “We keep things neat. That is part of being careful.”

The rain outside slowed to a soft drip. The pantry felt warm and safe. Kori and Mina sat side by side, munching and smiling.

Kori said, “Today we learned: When a problem is hard, we can be calm. We can be brave. We can think. We can improve our plan, step by step.”

Mina nodded. “Small steps make big success.”

Kori hugged Mina. “And we accept ourselves and each other, just as we are.”

They waved goodbye to the pantry shelves and the jars that now seemed friendly. Then they padded out, full of courage and crunchy snacks.

The End.
