# Beep-Boop Barks for Kind Words

In a comfortable nursery with soft blue walls and a sunny round window, a little robot dog named Beep-Boop lived on a cozy charging mat. Beep-Boop had shiny silver ears that wiggled, a glowing nose that blinked softly, and gentle wheels that hummed like bumblebees. The nursery smelled like clean soap and warm blankets. A tiny fan went whirr-whirr, and a music mobile tinkled like little stars.

Every morning, the children came to play. There was Mia with her curly hair, Max with his striped shirt, and Noor with her bright smile. They loved to build towers, draw rainbows, and feed the big stuffed bear pretend soup. Beep-Boop helped with everything. He fetched blocks, held crayons in his gentle mouth, and wagged his metal tail with a cheerful click-clack.

One sunny morning, the children wanted to build the tallest tower ever. Beep-Boop rolled over with a basket of soft, colorful blocks. “Beep! Blocks ready!” he said in his friendly robot voice.

Mia took a red block. Max reached for a blue one. Noor picked up a green block. They stacked slowly. Tap. Tap. Tap. The tower grew taller and taller. Beep-Boop steadied the base with his little paw. His nose light blinked, blink-blink, as he watched.

Suddenly, Max grabbed the very special gold block. Mia had wanted that block too. Her eyebrows scrunched up. “Hey, I wanted that,” she said. Her voice got louder.

Max hugged the block to his chest. “Mine!” he shouted. His face got hot and pink.

Noor stepped back. The tower wobbled. The room felt tight and prickly. Beep-Boop’s ears wiggled. He could hear the bump-bump of upset feelings. Inside his chest, a tiny soft light glowed. It was his Calm Light.

Beep-Boop took a small breath in. “Beeeep.” He breathed out slowly. “Boooop.” He had a peaceful plan.

He rolled between Mia and Max and sat very still. His voice was soft and warm. “Let’s use kind words. We can talk and listen. Beep-Boop can help.”

Mia crossed her arms. “Max took it,” she said, a little tear in her eye.

Max stomped his foot. “I want the shiny block!” His fists were tight.

Beep-Boop’s nose glowed like a night light. “Let’s try calm bodies,” he said. “Hands to lap. Breathe with me. In… beeeep. Out… boooop.”

Mia put her hands on her knees and breathed. In… beeeep. Out… boooop. Max did it too. His shoulders softened. Noor sat close and patted the big stuffed bear. The nursery felt softer again. The fan whispered. The mobile sang its tiny song.

“Good breathing,” said Beep-Boop. “Now, we use peaceful words. We take turns talking. We listen with our eyes and ears.” He tapped his ear gently. “Max, Mia, who would like to go first?”

Mia raised her hand. “I feel sad,” she said. “I wanted the gold block for the top. I wanted to make it sparkle.”

Max looked at Mia. He listened. He could see her little tear. “I feel mad,” he said. “I picked it up first. I wanted to be the one to make it tall.”

Beep-Boop nodded. “Thank you for telling your feelings. That is peaceful communication. Now we find a way to share.”

Noor whispered, “We could take turns!”

Beep-Boop wagged. “Yes. We can cooperate. We can make amends. Amends means we fix the hurt feelings.”

Max’s face softened. He looked at the shiny gold block in his hands. He took a breath. “I’m sorry for shouting, Mia,” he said. “That was not kind.”

Mia’s eyebrows un-scrunched. “I’m sorry I grabbed,” she said. “That was not kind either.”

Beep-Boop’s tail went click-clack! “You both made amends. Thank you.”

Mia said, “Max, you can put the gold block on first. Then I can put the star sticker on top.”

Max smiled. “Okay. And Noor can count for us!”

Noor clapped. “One, two, three!”

Max placed the gold block gently. Tap. The tower stood strong. Mia placed a glittery star sticker on top. Noor cheered. Beep-Boop steadied the base, his wheels humming softly. The tower sparkled in the sunlight.

“Look!” said Mia. “We did it!”

Max nodded. “We worked together.”

Noor hugged the bear. “We used kind words.”

Beep-Boop sat proudly, his Calm Light glowing warm. “When we talk and listen, we find a way,” he said. “Peaceful words help our hearts feel safe.”

They kept building and laughing. Beep-Boop helped pass blocks. When someone wanted the same piece, they used the Beep-Boop way:

- Breathe in… beeeep. Breathe out… boooop.
- Say how you feel with kind words.
- Listen with eyes and ears.
- Make a plan to share and take turns.
- Say sorry and fix it if someone feels hurt.

Soon, the tower was as tall as the big stuffed bear. The nursery felt calm and happy. The air smelled like crayons and warm blankets. The music mobile twinkled like tiny bells. Beep-Boop’s ears wiggled with joy.

At clean-up time, Max picked up the basket. “I’ll carry,” he said.

Mia gathered blocks. “I’ll sort by color.”

Noor brought the star stickers. “I’ll put them away.”

Beep-Boop rolled to each child. “Thank you for cooperating,” he said. “Thank you for peaceful communication.”

Mia patted Beep-Boop’s shiny head. “You helped us breathe.”

Max scratched behind Beep-Boop’s ear. “You helped us listen.”

Noor gave Beep-Boop a gentle hug. “You helped us make amends.”

Beep-Boop’s nose blinked happily. “Beep-Boop loves kind teams,” he said.

Together, they looked up at the tall, sparkling tower one more time. It stood strong because they had built it with calm bodies, kind words, and caring hearts.

And from that day on, whenever a problem popped up in the cozy nursery, the children remembered the Beep-Boop way: breathe, talk, listen, and share. With peaceful communication, every day could be a gentle, happy day.

The End.
