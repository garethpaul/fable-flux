# Miko the Brave Little Helper

Miko was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright button eyes. He lived in a creative art room filled with colors and giggles. Paintbrushes stood like little trees in jars. Crayons rested in a rainbow box. Glitter twinkled on a shelf like tiny stars. The room smelled like fresh paper and sweet glue.

Every morning, Miko peeked out from a cozy tin cup. “Good morning, art room,” he squeaked. He loved to help. He loved to listen. And he loved to make art with the children.

One sunny day, the teacher, Miss Lila, clapped her hands. “Art time!” she sang. The children cheered. They painted big, bright pictures. Swish, swish went the brushes. Tap, tap went the glue cups. Snip, snip went the safety scissors. The room was busy and happy.

Miko helped by passing tiny paper stars. “Here you go,” he said softly. He listened to the children’s ideas.

“I want a red sun!” said Jonah.

“A red sun is brave,” Miko said, nodding.

“I want a purple tree,” said Maya.

“A purple tree is playful,” said Miko.

When art time was over, Miss Lila rang a little bell. “Chores time,” she said. “We clean up together.”

Miko liked chores. He liked the soft swoosh of the broom and the click of lids on glue. But today, something felt different. A tall stack of paint cups wobbled. Drip, drip, drip—blue paint slid down the side. The glitter jar was open, and the tiny stars sparkled on the floor. It looked like a big, shiny mess.

Miko’s whiskers trembled. “Oh dear,” he whispered. “That is a lot.”

Miss Lila smiled kindly. “We can do it,” she said. “We just need courage, careful hands, and good listening.”

Miko took a deep breath. He put his paw on his chest. Thump-thump went his little heart. “I can be brave,” he squeaked. “I can help.”

He scurried to the paint cups. “Maya, may I hold the stack while you wipe?” he asked.

“Yes, please,” Maya said.

Miko listened to Maya count. “One, two, three.” Together, they made the cups steady. The blue drips stopped. Plop! The last drop fell into the sink. Miko smiled. He felt warm and proud.

Then Miko saw the glitter on the floor. It shone like a tiny galaxy. He grabbed a small brush and a dustpan. “Jonah, will you help me sweep?” he asked.

“Okay!” Jonah said. “Sweep, sweep,” they sang together. The glitter made a soft, scratchy sound as it slid into the pan. It looked like a little moon in a tiny boat. Miko giggled.

Just then, Miko bumped a box of crayons. Clatter! The crayons rolled onto the table. One crayon fell into a puddle of glue. “Oh no,” said Miko. His ears drooped. “I made a mess.”

Miss Lila came close and knelt down. “It’s okay to make a mistake, Miko,” she said gently. “What do we say when we make a mistake?”

Miko looked up with brave eyes. “I say, ‘I’m sorry.’” He turned to the children. “I’m sorry I knocked the crayons.”

Jonah nodded. “Thank you for saying sorry,” he said.

Maya smiled. “We can fix it,” she said.

Miko took a cloth and wiped the gluey crayon. Squish, squish went the cloth. “All clean,” he said. He pressed the crayons back into the rainbow box. Click! The box closed.

The room felt calmer. The air smelled like orange soap now. The tables were shiny and smooth. Miss Lila rang the bell again. “Wonderful work, helpers,” she said. “You used courage. You did your chores. You listened. And you said sorry when you needed to. That is brave.”

Miko’s chest felt big and light. “I was scared at first,” he said. “But I took a breath. I asked for help. I listened. I did it!”

Miss Lila gave Miko a tiny gold sticker shaped like a star. “For courageous cleaning and kind words,” she said.

Miko stuck the star on his tin cup home. It sparkled. He could almost hear it sing, ting!

Before nap time, Miko curled up inside the cup. The room was quiet. He closed his eyes and remembered the swish of brushes, the sweep of glitter, the soft squish of cloth, and the happy click of clean boxes. He felt safe. He felt strong.

He whispered, “Being brave means trying, even when I feel small. Being brave means doing my chores. Being brave means saying, ‘I’m sorry,’ and listening.”

Miss Lila turned off the light. “Goodnight, little helpers,” she said.

“Goodnight,” Miko squeaked, smiling. “Tomorrow I will be brave again.”

The End.
