# Gigi's Bathroom Story Time

Gigi the giraffe loved her tall bathroom mirror. Every morning, she would look at herself and practice telling stories. Her long neck made her the perfect height to see everything clearly.

"Once upon a time," Gigi said to her reflection, "there was a brave little mouse who..."

*CRASH!*

Gigi's little brother Benny had knocked over her special soap dispenser. Pink soap splashed everywhere on the white tiles.

"BENNY!" Gigi shouted, her voice echoing in the bathroom. "You broke my favorite soap! Now look at this mess!"

Benny's eyes filled with tears. "I'm sorry, Gigi. I just wanted to wash my hooves like a big giraffe."

Gigi felt her anger growing hot in her chest. She wanted to stomp her hooves and yell more. But then she remembered something important - her stories always taught her lessons.

"Wait," Gigi said, taking a deep breath. The cool bathroom air filled her lungs. "Let me tell us both a story."

She sat down carefully on the bathroom floor, her long legs folding gracefully. Benny sat beside her, still sniffling.

"Once upon a time," Gigi began in a gentle voice, "there lived a big sister elephant named Ella. One day, her little brother accidentally broke her favorite water bucket."

Benny wiped his nose and listened carefully.

"Ella felt very angry," Gigi continued. "Her trunk got all hot and steamy. But then Ella remembered that accidents happen. Her little brother didn't mean to break the bucket. He was just trying to help."

"What did Ella do?" Benny asked, his voice small and curious.

"Well," Gigi said, her anger melting away like soap bubbles, "Ella took three deep breaths. She counted to ten. Then she helped her brother clean up the water. Together, they made the mess disappear!"

Gigi looked at the pink soap on the floor. It actually looked kind of pretty, like flower petals scattered around.

"And you know what the best part was?" Gigi asked.

"What?" Benny whispered.

"Ella's little brother learned how to be more careful. And Ella learned that being kind was more important than being right."

Benny smiled a tiny smile. "Can we clean up together, like Ella and her brother?"

"Yes!" Gigi said, standing up tall. "But first, let me finish the story."

She grabbed some soft towels from the bathroom cabinet. They smelled like lavender and sunshine.

"Ella and her brother became the best cleaning team in the whole jungle. They helped all their neighbors keep their homes tidy. They learned that working together made everything better."

As Gigi told the story, she and Benny wiped up every drop of soap. The bathroom tiles sparkled like stars.

"There!" Gigi said, admiring their work. "Our bathroom looks perfect."

Benny hugged his big sister's long leg. "Thank you for the story, Gigi. And thank you for not staying mad at me."

Gigi bent her neck down to nuzzle Benny's head. "Stories help me remember what's really important. Being angry doesn't fix anything. But being kind and working together fixes everything!"

From that day on, whenever Gigi felt angry, she would tell herself a story. And whenever there was a mess to clean, she and Benny did it together, just like the elephants in her tale.

"Every problem," Gigi would say, looking in her tall bathroom mirror, "is just a story waiting for a happy ending."

The End.
