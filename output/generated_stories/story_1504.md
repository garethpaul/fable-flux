# Ellie’s Big Imagination Plan

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a tiny, swishy tail. She lived in a cozy home where the playful living room had a rainbow rug, a basket of blocks, and a shelf of picture books. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. The room smelled like fresh crayons and sweet banana bread.

“Today, I’ll make a pretend city!” Ellie trumpeted softly, her trunk wiggling with joy. She set down plush animals: a giraffe, a lion, a rabbit, and a turtle. “Everyone can play.”

Ellie’s friend Milo, a small monkey with bright eyes, bounced onto the couch. “I want to build a tower! The biggest tower ever!”

“Great idea,” Ellie said. “I’ll make a road with pillows. Vroom, vroom!”

They began to play. Ellie stacked soft pillows into a curvy road. Milo grabbed blocks—clack, clack, clack. The lion plush fell on its side with a gentle thump. The rabbit leaned against a book. The turtle looked very brave.

Soon, blocks were everywhere. Crayons rolled under the coffee table. A sock slid off the couch like a sleepy snake.

Ellie stopped and looked around. Her ears drooped. “Oh,” she said in a tiny voice. “It’s a mess. My road is bumpy. My city feels squished.”

Milo kept stacking. “Tall tower!” he cheered.

Ellie felt a little squeeze in her chest. She wanted to keep imagining, but the messy room made it hard to see her city. She took a slow breath, like her mama taught her. In, out. In, out.

“I have a problem,” Ellie said. “I can’t see the city in my mind. The toys are all mixed up.”

Milo blinked. “But towers are fun.”

“They are,” Ellie said kindly. “And roads are fun. Maybe we can make a plan. A plan to help our imagination.”

Milo tilted his head. “A plan?”

Ellie nodded. “Yes. We can talk and choose together. That’s called negotiating. We can listen, and we can share ideas. Then our play will be even better.”

Milo hugged his knees. “Okay. I like sharing ideas.”

Ellie smiled. Her trunk swayed. “First, let’s invite everyone to join.” She lifted the plush giraffe. “Giraffe likes tall things. Lion likes quiet naps. Rabbit loves colors. Turtle moves slow and steady.”

Milo giggled. “We can include them all!”

Ellie’s eyes sparkled. “Let’s use our imagination to give each friend a job. That will help us organize.”

She set the giraffe near the block basket. “Giraffe is tower helper.” She placed the lion near a pillow. “Lion watches the road. Quietly.” She tucked rabbit near the crayons. “Rabbit matches colors.” She set turtle by a small box. “Turtle puts teeny pieces in the box. Slow and steady.”

Milo bounced less and listened more. “I can build my tower next to Giraffe. Then it won’t fall on the road.”

“And I can make a road next to Lion,” Ellie said. “So Lion can nap and watch at the same time.”

They began to tidy. Ellie used her trunk to place blocks in a line. Click, click. Milo scooped crayons into a cup. Swish, swish. They sang a little song.

“Sort, sort, sort,
We’re a helpful team!
Clear the floor,
So we can dream!”

The room started to look neat. The rainbow rug peeked out again. The pillows made a smooth, curvy road. The air felt calm and happy.

Ellie placed a blue block. “Milo, do you want to make two small towers and one tall tower?”

Milo thought. “Hmm. I really want one super tall tower. But we also need space for your road.” He scratched his head. “How about one tall tower in the corner, and two tiny towers by the shelf? Then your road can go in the middle.”

Ellie beamed. “That’s a great plan. Thank you for thinking of both.”

Milo grinned. “We negotiated!”

They built and built. The road went whoosh around the couch. The tower stood straight and strong. The tiny towers looked like baby trees. Rabbit matched red blocks with red crayons. Giraffe nodded from the basket. Lion yawned. Turtle slowly pushed a toy car box to the side.

Ellie closed her eyes and pictured her city. She could see it so clearly now. “I can imagine everything,” she whispered. “I can hear tiny cars. I can smell crayons. I can feel the soft rug.”

Milo tickled his own tail and laughed. “I can imagine too! My tower is a rocket. Three, two, one!”

Ellie raised her trunk like a trumpet. “Vroom! Zoom!” They made gentle rocket sounds and soft car noises. The whole room felt like a big, friendly world.

After they finished, Ellie patted the rainbow rug. “We kept the room tidy with our plan. We were kind to each other and to our friends. We included everyone. We listened and shared.”

Milo nodded. “And we used our imaginations!”

Ellie’s ears wiggled. “When we organize, our minds feel open. When we are kind, our play feels happy. When we talk and plan, we can solve problems together.”

Milo gave Ellie a high-five on her trunk. “Best city ever.”

Ellie smiled. “Best team ever.”

The End.
