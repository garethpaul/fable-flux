# Kiki the Calm Koala’s Home Office Day

Kiki was a calm koala with soft gray fur and a gentle smile. She lived in a cozy home with a small home office. The office had a big wooden desk, a comfy blue chair, a bright yellow lamp, and a window that showed the sunny sky. Kiki liked the quiet hum of her little fan and the click-click sound of her keyboard. Click, click. Hum, hum. It felt peaceful.

One morning, Kiki stretched her arms and rubbed her sleepy eyes. “Good morning, home office,” she said in her soft voice. She sat in her chair and looked at her list. “Today I will draw a picture, send a message, and make a tiny paper boat,” she said. Kiki liked to plan her day.

She picked up her green crayon. Scritch-scratch went the crayon on the paper. “I will draw a big sun,” she said. But the green crayon made a green sun. Kiki paused. “Hmm. My yellow crayon is missing.”

She looked in the cup. She looked in the drawer. She looked under the blue chair. No yellow crayon. Kiki felt a little pinch in her tummy. “I wanted a yellow sun,” she whispered.

Kiki took a slow breath in. She let a slow breath out. “It’s okay,” she told herself. “I can be calm. I can accept this.” She smiled a tiny smile. “A green sun can be special.”

Tap, tap. Someone knocked on the door. It was Pip, the little parrot from next door. Pip had bright feathers and a squeaky voice. “Hi, Kiki! What are you doing?” Pip asked, peeking in.

“I’m drawing,” said Kiki. “My yellow crayon is lost. I have a green sun now.”

Pip tilted his head. “Green suns are funny,” he chirped.

Kiki nodded. “Yes, and funny can be good. I accept my green sun.” She held up the picture. The green sun smiled with a friendly face. Pip giggled.

Kiki moved to the keyboard. Click, click. “Now I will send a happy message to my friend,” she said. But when she clicked, the screen did not glow. It stayed dark like night. The lamp was bright. The fan was humming. But the screen was quiet.

Kiki felt her tummy pinch again. “Oh no,” she said. “I wanted to send a message.” She took a slow breath in. She let a slow breath out. “It’s okay. I can accept this. I can try another way.”

She looked around the home office. She saw paper. She saw crayons. She saw sticky notes. “I can write a note,” she said. “I can deliver it myself.” That was her resourceful idea.

Kiki wrote with her blue crayon: “Hello, friend! I hope you have a sunny day.” She drew a green sun with a big smile. She folded the paper and put on a tiny heart sticker. Pat, pat.

Pip flapped his wings. “I can help! I can take the note,” he said.

“Thank you, Pip,” said Kiki. “You are kind. I can also do it myself if needed. I can be self-reliant.”

Pip nodded. “We can both help,” he chirped.

Kiki stood up and heard a little creak from her chair. Creak. She put on her small blue backpack. She slipped the note inside. The backpack felt snug. “First, I will finish my paper boat,” she said.

On the desk was a square paper. Fold, fold, smooth, smooth. Kiki’s paws were careful and steady. Soon she had a tiny paper boat. “Hello, little boat,” she whispered. “You are ready.”

But when she tried to pick up the boat, a tiny drop of water from her cup fell on it. Drip. The boat sagged. “Oh no,” Kiki said. “My boat is soggy.”

Her tummy pinched again. She took another slow breath in. She let a slow breath out. “It’s okay. I accept this. Paper can get wet. I can make another boat, and I can move my cup.”

She moved her cup far away. She used a new piece of paper. Fold, fold, smooth, smooth. Soon, a fresh boat sat on the desk, safe and dry. Kiki smiled wider now. “Thank you, calm breaths,” she said.

Pip chirped, “Kiki, you are good at fixing problems!”

“Thank you,” said Kiki. “I try to be calm and kind. I accept what I cannot change. I try new ideas when I can.”

Pip hopped. “Let’s deliver the note!”

Kiki opened the window. Warm air came in, smelling like sunshine and flowers. The leaves outside made a soft swish-swish sound. Kiki and Pip listened. It felt nice.

Kiki handed Pip the note. “Please take this to my friend in the blue house,” she said.

“I will!” Pip said. He flapped, flap, flapped and flew away with a happy “Cheep-cheep!”

Kiki watched, feeling proud. She sat back down. The screen was still dark. The green sun on her paper smiled at her. The little paper boat stood tall and dry.

Later, Pip came back. “Your friend says thank you!” he said. “She liked the green sun.”

Kiki’s heart felt warm. “I am glad,” she said. “Today I learned I can accept changes. I can be calm. I can help myself. I can find new ways.”

She looked around her home office. Click, click went the keys as she typed a note to herself: “Be calm. Accept things. Try new ideas.” She taped it on the wall.

Kiki took one more slow breath and smiled. “Even a green sun can make a bright day,” she said.

The End.
