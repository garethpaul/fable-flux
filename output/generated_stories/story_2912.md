# Pebble's Polite Pause

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a wiggly waddle. Pebble loved to build and draw. One sunny day, Pebble climbed the ladder to a cozy treehouse. The wood was warm from the sun, and the breeze smelled like pine and pancakes.

Inside the treehouse, Pebble spread out crayons, colorful paper, and shiny buttons. “I will make a hoppy-happy art wall!” Pebble chirped. Tap tap tap went the tiny hammer. Scritch scritch scritch went the crayons. Pebble hummed a bouncy tune. “Da-da-dum, art is fun!”

A squirrel named Squeaks poked his head through the window. “Hi, Pebble! What are you making?” he asked, his nose twitching.

“I’m making rainbow fish with button bubbles,” Pebble said, pointing to a big blue fish.

Squeaks scurried inside. He smelled like nuts and leaves. “Can I help?” he asked.

Pebble smiled. “Yes, you can glue the bubbles! I like help.” Pebble handed Squeaks some glue.

Soon, the door creaked again. “Hello!” sang Bella Bluebird, fluttering in. “I brought feathers!”

“Oh! Thank you, Bella,” Pebble said. “Feathers can be fins!”

They all giggled as they worked. The treehouse filled with soft feather tickles, gluey smells, and happy sounds. Pebble was cheerful and busy. Pebble felt proud.

Then Squeaks reached for Pebble’s favorite shiny button. Pebble loved that button. It looked like a tiny moon. Squeaks grabbed it and said, “I will glue this right here!” His paw moved fast.

Pebble’s tummy felt tight. Pebble’s smile dimmed. Pebble wanted to say stop, but felt small. The button pressed near the edge of the fish.

Bella hopped closer and pecked at Pebble’s drawing. “I can change the fish color!” she chirped, picking up Pebble’s crayon and scribbling big swirls.

Pebble’s cheeks felt hot, like a warm cookie. Pebble pulled the crayon back, then let go. “Um…” Pebble’s voice was tiny. The art didn’t look like Pebble’s idea anymore. Pebble’s heart went thump, thump.

Squeaks leaned close and hugged Pebble without asking. “We are super helpers!” he squeaked, squeezing tight.

Pebble’s flippers felt squished. Pebble didn’t feel good. Pebble looked down at the floor. “I… I don’t like this,” Pebble whispered.

Pebble took a big, brave breath. Pebble thought, I can be kind and also say what I need. It is okay to say stop. That is my boundary.

Pebble stood tall on little penguin feet. “Friends,” Pebble said, voice soft but steady, “I like your help. But this is my art idea. Please ask before you touch my art. And please ask before you hug me. My body and my art are mine.”

Squeaks blinked. “Oh,” he said, ears drooping. “I didn’t know. I didn’t ask.”

Bella fluttered back. “I am sorry,” she chirped. “I should have asked.”

Pebble nodded. “Thank you for listening. You can still help! We can use a plan. I will draw the fish. You can add bubbles and feathers when I say ready. And if you want to hug, you can ask, ‘May I give you a hug?’ I will say yes or no.”

Squeaks lifted a paw. “Pebble, may I give you a hug?”

Pebble smiled. “Not right now. I want space.” Pebble spread flippers wide to show a bubble of space. “This is my space.”

Squeaks stepped back and smiled too. “Okay! I respect your space.”

Bella chirped, “May I add one soft feather when you say ready?”

“Yes,” Pebble said. “When I say ready.”

They tried the plan. Pebble drew careful blue lines. Scritch scritch. Pebble said, “Ready for bubbles!” Squeaks gently glued two shiny buttons. Plop. Plop.

Pebble said, “Ready for one feather!” Bella added a tiny, soft feather fin. Fluff.

The art looked just right. Pebble felt light, like a bubble floating. The treehouse felt calm and happy again. The piney breeze whispered, and the sun made golden dots on the floor.

“Thank you for asking,” Pebble said. “I feel safe and brave.”

Squeaks bounced. “I learned something! I can ask first. I can wait for yes or no.”

Bella nodded. “I learned too. Your art is your idea. My body is my own, and your body is yours.”

Pebble smiled wide. “We can be kind and still have our space.”

They made a sign together with bright letters. Pebble wrote, “Please ask before touching.” Squeaks drew a bubble of space around three smiling friends. Bella added a little heart.

They taped the sign by the door. Tap tap. The treehouse smelled like glue and pine and pancakes again. Pebble felt proud and peaceful.

At the end of the day, Pebble climbed down the ladder and waved. “Bye, friends! Thank you for respecting my boundaries.”

“Bye!” chirped Bella.

“Bye!” squeaked Squeaks.

Pebble waddled home, humming. The sky turned pink and gold. Pebble whispered, “I was brave. I used my words. I kept my body and my art safe.” Pebble’s heart felt warm and cozy.

Lesson: It is kind and brave to say what you need. Your body is yours. Your space and your ideas matter. Ask first. Listen to yes or no. Friends respect boundaries and still have lots of fun.

The End.
