# Ruby Rabbit Writes It Right

Ruby was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, busy paws. She sat in her cozy home office. The room smelled like fresh paper and clean soap. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Her tiny desk had smooth pencils, big crayons, and a cup of cool water. A gentle fan hummed, whirr, whirr.

“Today,” said Ruby, twitching her nose, “I will write my story!” She wiggled in her chair and smiled. Writing made her heart feel big and bright.

Ruby picked up a pencil. “Tap, tap,” she said. “My story will be about carrots and clouds!” She pressed the pencil to the paper, but then she sneezed. “Ah-choo!”

Her paws felt sticky. She looked at her hands. “Oh! I forgot to wash after my snack.” She remembered the rule: clean paws for clean pages.

“Pause and wash,” Ruby told herself. She hopped to the sink in the corner of her office. The water was warm. She sang, “Bubbles, bubbles, scrub-a-dub,” while soap made soft foam. She counted to twenty in a gentle voice. “One, two, three...” The water smelled like lemons. She dried her paws with a fluffy towel. “Now my hands are happy,” she said.

Back at her desk, Ruby wrote, “Once there was a bunny who loved to write.” The pencil made a soft scratchy sound. She smiled. Her ears wiggled with joy.

Knock, knock. It was her little brother, Pip. Pip peered in with big curious eyes. “Ruby, can I use your crayons?”

Ruby looked at her paper. She loved Pip, but she wanted to keep writing. “I am working right now,” she said kindly. “I can share when I take a break.”

Pip nodded. “Okay!” He tiptoed out, but the door bumped a cup. Uh-oh! The water wobbled. Splash! A tiny drop landed on Ruby’s page and made a little gray spot.

Ruby’s tummy felt tight. “Oh no,” she whispered. She looked at the spot. She felt a little angry, a little sad. She took a slow breath. In. Out. The fan hummed. Whirr, whirr.

Pip peeked back in. “I am sorry, Ruby,” he said in a small voice. “I bumped it. I did not mean to.”

Ruby put a paw on her heart. She remembered peaceful words. She used her calm voice. “Thank you for telling me the truth, Pip. I feel upset because my page is wet. I want to keep my story nice and clean. Can you please help me fix it?”

Pip nodded fast. “Yes! What can I do?”

Ruby thought carefully. Logical-thinking time! “First, we will dab the spot,” she said. She grabbed a clean tissue. Dab, dab. “Next, we will move the cup away from the papers.” Pip slid the cup to a safe corner. “Then, we will put a coaster under it.” Pip placed a coaster down—thunk!

The spot got lighter. The page still had a mark, but it was okay. Ruby smiled a little. “We solved it.”

Pip looked at the crayons. “Can I draw now?”

Ruby checked her plan. “I will take a break in five minutes,” she said. “When the timer beeps, I will share.”

She set a small timer. Beep in five minutes. Click. Ruby kept writing. “The bunny washed her paws and kept her paper clean.” She added a smell. “The air was lemon-fresh.” She added a sound. “The fan went whirr, whirr.”

The timer went beep-beep. Ruby stood up. “Break time!” She passed the crayons to Pip. “Please use the paper tray, and keep drinks on the coaster.”

Pip saluted. “Yes, Captain Clean!” They giggled.

Ruby stretched. She sipped water and put it back on the coaster. She washed her paws again after touching the cup. “Bubbles, bubbles, scrub-a-dub.” She dried them. Her heart felt calm.

Back at her desk, she noticed something else. On the corner of the page was a neat sentence she did not remember writing: “The carrot rocket zoomed to the moon.” Ruby blinked. “Pip, did you write this?”

Pip’s ears drooped. “Yes. I wanted to help. I am sorry. I should have asked.”

Ruby nodded. Honesty time. “Thank you for telling the truth. We ask before we write on someone’s page. This is my story. But you can write your own!”

Pip’s face lit up. “My own?”

“Yes!” said Ruby. “I will help you start. First, wash your paws. Then get your paper. Then choose your idea.” Logical steps.

Pip washed. Swish, swish. Soap, rinse, dry. Ruby gave him a clean sheet. They sat side by side. Ruby whispered, “Tap, tap. We can do it.”

Ruby wrote: “The bunny flew a carrot rocket.” Pip wrote: “Zoom!” The pencils danced. Scratch, scratch.

When they finished, Ruby read her story out loud. “Once there was a bunny who loved to write. She kept her paws clean, used kind words, told the truth, and fixed small problems with smart steps. The end.”

Pip clapped. “I like your story!”

Ruby smiled. “I like yours too.” She hugged him. The room smelled like paper and a little lemon. The fan hummed. Whirr, whirr.

Ruby whispered, “Writing is work, but I can do it. Clean paws, honest words, calm voice, and smart steps help me write it right.”

They taped their stories on the wall. The pages fluttered softly like little flags. Ruby felt proud and peaceful.

The clear lesson was bright in her mind: Be clean, be honest, speak kindly, think in steps, and keep writing.

The End.
