# Rocky's Rooftop Rest

The bright morning sun warmed the red brick rooftop where Rocky Raccoon lived. Rocky loved making music on his rooftop home. He had collected shiny bottle caps, old tin cans, and wooden sticks. *Tap-tap-ting!* went his homemade drums.

Every day, Rocky played music from sunrise to sunset. His little paws moved fast across his instruments. *Bang-clang-ring!* The sounds echoed across the city below.

But today, Rocky felt very tired. His striped tail drooped low. His dark eyes felt heavy and sleepy.

"I must keep playing," Rocky said to himself. "The birds expect my morning concert. The squirrels love my afternoon show."

Rocky picked up his drumsticks with shaky paws. *Tap... tap...* His music sounded slow and quiet. Not happy and bouncy like usual.

A wise old pigeon named Penny landed nearby. Her gray feathers sparkled in the sunlight.

"Good morning, Rocky," cooed Penny gently. "Your music sounds different today."

"I'm just tired," Rocky sighed. "But I can't stop playing. Everyone counts on my music."

Penny tilted her head thoughtfully. "Rocky, when did you last take a break?"

Rocky's whiskers twitched as he thought. "I... I can't remember. Maybe last week?"

"Oh my!" Penny flapped her wings softly. "Even the best musicians need rest. Your body is telling you something important."

Rocky looked down at his tired paws. They felt sore from so much drumming. His tummy rumbled loudly - he had forgotten to eat breakfast!

"But what about my concerts?" Rocky worried.

"True friends will understand," Penny said kindly. "Taking care of yourself helps you take care of others too."

Rocky set down his drumsticks. For the first time in days, he really listened to his body. His muscles felt tight and achy. His throat was dry and scratchy.

"You're right, Penny. I need to rest."

Rocky found a sunny spot on his rooftop. He stretched his arms up high, then touched his toes. The gentle exercise felt wonderful! Next, he drank cool water from his rain bucket. The water tasted fresh and clean.

Then Rocky curled up in his cozy nest made of soft leaves and old newspapers. The afternoon breeze felt gentle on his fur. His eyes grew heavy as he listened to the peaceful city sounds below.

When Rocky woke up, the sun was setting. His body felt strong and refreshed. His paws didn't hurt anymore. His tummy had stopped rumbling.

"How do you feel?" asked Penny, who had been watching over him.

"Amazing!" Rocky stretched happily. "I forgot how good it feels to rest."

Rocky picked up his drumsticks again. This time, his music rang out clear and joyful. *Ting-tang-ring!* The sounds danced across the rooftop like happy butterflies.

The birds chirped along from nearby trees. The squirrels gathered to listen and dance. Everyone loved Rocky's music even more because he had taken care of himself first.

"Thank you, Penny," Rocky said gratefully. "I learned something important today."

"What's that?" Penny asked with a smile.

"Taking breaks isn't being lazy - it's being smart! When I rest, I can make even better music for my friends."

From that day on, Rocky made sure to rest every afternoon. He drank water when thirsty and ate when hungry. He stretched his muscles and took deep breaths of fresh air.

Rocky's rooftop concerts became the most beautiful music in the whole city. And Rocky felt happy and healthy every single day.

The End.
