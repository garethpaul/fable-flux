# Ruby Rabbit’s Ready Plan

Ruby was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She liked to hop, hop, hop all around the house. One rainy morning, Ruby found a special place—the closet hideaway. It was cozy and smelled like clean laundry. The coats felt fuzzy. The scarves felt silky. Shoes sat in a neat row like quiet soldiers.

“Hello, closet!” Ruby whispered. “You are my hideaway.”

She pulled in a small blanket, a tin flashlight, and a tiny notebook. “I want to make a plan,” she said. “Plans help me be ready.”

Ruby’s ears perked up when she heard Mama Rabbit call, “Ruby, it’s rainy outside. Let’s play indoors.”

“In the closet hideaway!” Ruby answered. She felt proud. She was making a plan all by herself.

Ruby opened her tiny notebook and drew pictures. She drew a cup for water. She drew a carrot snack. She drew the flashlight. She drew a clock. She drew a little heart too.

“My Ready Plan,” she said, tapping the page. “If it gets dark, I will use the flashlight. If I get thirsty, I will drink water. If I get hungry, I will have a carrot. If I feel scared, I will take a deep breath and count to three.”

She practiced. “One… two… three.” Her nose twitched. Her whiskers tickled. She smiled.

Just then, the light in the hallway flickered. The house hummed and then… click. The lights went out. The closet became extra cozy and dark.

Ruby’s heart thumped, thump-thump, like a tiny drum. “It’s very dark,” she said in a small voice. She breathed in. She breathed out. “I have a plan. I am ready.”

She turned on her little flashlight. A soft circle of light shone on the coats. The red coat looked like a friendly mountain. The blue scarf looked like a quiet river.

Mama called, “Ruby? Are you okay?”

Ruby took a brave breath. “I’m okay! I am in my closet hideaway. I have my light,” she called back clearly. She was assertive—she used a strong, kind voice.

Mama’s voice sounded warm. “Good job, Ruby. I’m getting candles. I’ll be right here.”

Ruby looked around. “What else do I need?” she asked herself. She checked her notebook. “Water.” She didn’t have water. She checked her snack. “No carrot yet.”

“I can be resourceful,” Ruby said. She peeked out of the closet. The hallway was dark and quiet. The rain went pat-pat-pat on the window. The air smelled like wet grass and soap.

Ruby held her flashlight and hopped to the kitchen. She kept her body small and careful. “Hop. Stop. Listen,” she said softly. She opened a low cabinet and found her bunny cup. She filled it with water. Swish. She took one sip. Cool and fresh.

She looked in the crisper. “Carrots!” she cheered. She picked a small carrot and a napkin. “Thank you, carrots,” she whispered.

Back in the closet, Ruby tucked the cup in a safe corner. She wrapped the carrot in the napkin. She looked at her notebook and drew a smiley face next to the cup and the carrot.

“Now my plan is ready,” she said. She felt calm and strong. Her ears relaxed.

Soon, her little brother, Pip, peeked into the closet. “Ruby,” Pip squeaked, “I got scared and ran. I bumped your blanket. I’m sorry.”

Ruby saw the blanket was a little wrinkly. She remembered being scared too. She looked at Pip’s worried face. She took a kind breath.

“It’s okay, Pip,” Ruby said. “Thank you for telling me. I accept your apology.” She patted the blanket and smoothed it. “Would you like to see my Ready Plan?”

Pip nodded, his eyes big.

Ruby showed him the pictures. “This is our light. This is our water. This is our carrot. This is our counting.”

Pip whispered, “Can we count together?”

“Yes,” said Ruby. “Let’s practice. Ready? One… two… three.” They both breathed in and out. Pip’s whiskers settled.

Mama came by with a gentle glow of candles. The room smelled like warm vanilla. “What a smart hideaway,” Mama said. “I see water, a snack, and a light. You prepared so well, Ruby. You were brave and resourceful.”

Ruby’s chest felt warm and proud. “I made a plan before I needed it,” she said. “It helped me. It helped Pip too.”

The rain slowed. The lights flicked back on—click! The coats shone bright colors again. The blue scarf looked like a happy river now.

Ruby turned off her flashlight and wrote one more picture in her notebook—a little bunny holding a heart. She hugged Pip.

“I learned something,” Ruby said. “Preparation makes me ready. Using a strong, kind voice helps me say what I need. Being resourceful helps me solve problems. And when we make a mistake, we can say sorry and fix it.”

Pip smiled. “I want a Ready Plan too!”

“I’ll help you,” Ruby said. “We can make one together.”

They hopped out of the closet hideaway to draw more pictures, feeling safe, smart, and loved.

The End.
