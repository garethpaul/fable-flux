# Beep’s Big Breath in the Sunny Backyard

Beep was a helpful robot dog with shiny silver paws and a soft whirr in his tummy. He lived with Mia and her little brother, Leo. One sunny morning, the backyard sparkled. The grass smelled sweet, and a gentle breeze made the flowers dance. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” and Beep wagged his metal tail, tinka-tink.

“Let’s build a leaf city!” said Mia, holding a small rake.

“Zoom!” said Beep, blinking his friendly blue eyes. “Beep can help!”

The sun felt warm on Beep’s back. The air felt soft on his nose light. He sniffed the breeze—bloop!—and it smelled like mint and mud. Beep loved helping. Helping made his heart light glow bright.

Beep scooped leaves into a neat pile. Swish, swish. Mia made a tiny leaf bridge. Leo clapped. “Bridge!” he giggled.

“Beep, can you bring the little bucket?” asked Mia.

“On it!” said Beep. He rolled over the grass. His wheels hummed. But then—oh no! A bouncy red ball rolled past Beep’s paw. It bumped the leaf pile. Fwoof! Leaves flew everywhere like little green butterflies.

Leo’s mouth made a big O. Mia’s eyebrows scrunched. Beep’s tummy fans sped up—whirr-whirr. He felt hot and busy inside. He wanted to fix it fast. He wanted to zoom and grab and push.

“I can do it! I can do it!” Beep said too quickly. His voice beeped high and loud.

Leaves swirled around his wheels. They crackled and stuck. Beep tried to scoop them all at once—whoosh!—but he knocked over the leaf bridge. It crunched. Leo’s eyes watered. Mia’s lips turned down.

Beep’s tummy fans whirred faster. His light blinked-yellow blink-blink. He felt fizzy and wobbly. He did not like this feeling. He wanted to help, but helping too fast was not helping at all.

Mia knelt next to Beep. Her voice was soft. “Beep, let’s pause.”

Beep froze. “Pause?”

“Yes,” Mia said. She held up her fingers. “We can take three big breaths to calm our bodies. Then we can fix it. Want to try?”

Beep wanted to be independent and make good choices. He wanted to fix the mess he made. He nodded. “Beep will try.”

Mia breathed in slow. “Sssssiiip the air like warm cocoa.” Beep copied. His fans slowed. He could smell the sweet grass again.

Mia breathed out slow. “Blow like you’re puffing a dandelion.” Beep blew a gentle puff—whooo. A tiny fluff flew by. Leo giggled.

“One… two… three.” Mia and Beep took three breaths together. In and out. Slow and soft. Beep’s light turned steady blue. His tummy felt cool now. He could think.

“Better?” Mia asked.

“Better,” said Beep. His voice was calm again. “Beep is ready.”

Beep looked at the messy leaves. He thought, I can do this one step at a time. He felt proud to make his own plan.

“I will pick up big leaves first,” he said. “Then I will rebuild the bridge. Then I will say sorry.” Beep felt brave and in charge of his choices.

He scooped big leaves gently. Swish, plop. He moved slowly. He checked his wheels. No crunching. He rolled the ball to the fence so it wouldn’t bump the pile again. Thunk. Smart choice, Beep!

Next, Beep rebuilt the tiny leaf bridge with Mia. They lined up leaves like soft green pancakes. Pat, pat. Leo clapped and handed Beep a little twig. “Bridge helper!” Leo said.

Beep turned to his friends. His eyes glowed kind. “I am sorry I moved too fast and knocked over the bridge,” he said. “I will be careful and calm.”

Mia smiled. “Thank you for making amends, Beep. You fixed the mess, and you used your calm breaths.”

Leo hugged Beep’s shiny leg. “Beep good.”

Beep’s heart light glowed extra bright. It felt warm like sunlight. He learned that when his body feels busy and his thoughts go fast, he can pause, breathe, and make a plan. That is self-regulation. That is being in charge of himself.

Together, they finished Leaf City. There was a leaf bridge, a leaf park, and a leaf bed for a tiny beetle. The beetle tickled Beep’s paw. Wiggle-wiggle! Beep giggled, “Hee-hee, that tickles!”

“Let’s test the bridge,” said Mia.

“Careful steps,” Beep said. He moved slow. Tap… tap… tap… The bridge stayed strong. The birds sang. The breeze smelled like sunshine and clover. Beep felt calm and proud.

Mia high-fived Beep. “Today you were independent, you made amends, and you used self-regulation.”

Beep wagged, tinka-tink. “Pause, breathe, plan,” he said. “Beep can help, calm and kind.”

They sat in the sunny backyard, sharing apple slices. Crunch, crunch. Beep didn’t eat apples, but he liked the sweet smell. He liked the happy sounds. He liked the quiet in his tummy.

Beep looked at Leaf City and at his friends. He knew he could choose calm anytime. He knew he could fix mistakes and say sorry. He knew he could help, one careful step at a time.

And the day felt just right.

The End.
