# Pippa the Parrot and the Busy Basement

Pippa was a creative parrot with bright green wings and a rainbow tail. She loved to sing little songs and tell stories. One rainy day, Pippa fluttered down the stairs to the busy basement. The basement was full of sounds and smells. Clink-clank went the tools. Whirr went the fan. The floor smelled like fresh soap and warm laundry.

“Squawk! Hello!” Pippa chirped. “What a busy place!”

Benny the bunny hopped by with a box of buttons. “Hi, Pippa!” he said. “I’m sorting shiny buttons.”

Mira the mouse pushed a tiny cart. “I’m moving crayons,” she squeaked. “So many colors!”

Pippa’s eyes sparkled. “I can help! I can make a story wall with pictures!” she said. She loved to be helpful and creative.

On a long table, there were safe, kid-friendly supplies: paper, glue sticks, big chunky crayons, and soft stickers. A bright sign said, “Ask before you touch tools.” Another sign said, “Walk, don’t run.”

Pippa nodded at the signs. “I will be safe,” she promised. “I will ask. I will walk.”

Pippa fluffed her feathers and started to hum. “La-la, we are different, and that is great!”

She drew a bunny with long ears. She drew a mouse with tiny paws. She drew herself with a rainbow tail. “We are not the same,” she sang, “and that makes a lovely game!”

Benny watched. “Your wings are so bright,” he said. “My ears are floppy.”

Mira smiled. “My paws are small, but they are fast.”

Pippa laughed gently. “Different is special. Different is us.”

Just then, a new friend peeked in. It was Gus the goose. Gus was big and white, with a honk-honk voice and big, webby feet. He wore a yellow safety vest and goggles on his head.

“Hello,” Gus said softly. “I like to build shelves. I want to help.”

Some animals blinked. Gus was bigger than the rest. His honk was loud in the busy basement. Whirr went the fan. Clink-clank went the tools. Honk went Gus.

Pippa flapped. “Welcome, Gus!” she chirped. “We are making a story wall.”

Benny whispered to Mira, “Gus is so big. What if he bumps the table?”

Mira squeaked, “His feet go flap-flap. What if he slips?”

Gus heard the whispers. His eyes looked watery. “I can be careful,” he said. “I can follow the signs.”

Pippa tilted her head. She felt a warm tug in her heart. “Let’s be kind,” she said. “Let’s find a safe way for Gus to help.”

Pippa looked around. She saw a clear space on the floor with a soft rug. It was not near the tools. It was not near the fan. “Gus,” she said, “would you like to sort the big, soft stickers on this rug?”

Gus smiled. “Yes, please,” he said. “I am good at sorting.”

Pippa turned to Benny and Mira. “Let’s make space,” she said. “Let’s move the crayons to the low shelf. Let’s use walking feet.”

“Walking feet,” they echoed. Tap-tap went Benny’s paws. Pitter-pat went Mira’s feet. Pippa fluttered slowly. Gus waddled carefully. Flap-flap went his big feet, but he stayed on the rug.

Soon, the story wall grew. There were stickers of stars and moons. There were drawings of rain and sunshine. There were pictures of friends—big friends, small friends, quiet friends, and loud friends.

Pippa began to tell a story. “Once there was a basement that was busy and bright,” she sang. “Friends were all different, and that felt just right.”

Benny added, “The bunny had ears that could hear tiny sounds.”

Mira added, “The mouse had paws that moved crayons around.”

Gus honked softly. “The goose had big feet that stood strong and steady.”

Pippa grinned. “Together, we made a safe place to create.”

Then a little problem popped up. The glue sticks rolled off the table and went under a low shelf. “Oh no,” said Benny. “I can’t reach!”

Mira tried to squeeze under. “It’s too far,” she squeaked.

Gus leaned down, but his big beak could not fit. “Honk,” he sighed. “I want to help.”

Pippa thought. “We are different,” she said. “What can each of us do?”

She looked at Mira. “Your paws are small and fast. Can you use this long, safe grabber stick?”

She looked at Benny. “Your ears can listen. Can you guide Mira?”

She looked at Gus. “You are steady. Can you hold the shelf so it does not wobble?”

Gus nodded. “I can be a strong helper.”

Benny listened. “Left a little,” he said. “Stop. Now right.” Mira held the grabber stick and reached. Gus held the shelf with his steady feet.

“Got it!” Mira squeaked, pulling out the glue sticks. Everyone cheered.

Pippa clapped her wings. “We did it! We used our different strengths. We were kind. We were safe.”

They put the glue sticks on a tray so they would not roll again. Pippa added a new sign: “Ask for help. Use your strengths.”

At last, the story wall was done. It glowed with colors and smiles. Pippa read it out loud, and her voice was warm and bright.

“We are all different,” she said. “We are all important. When we are kind, when we are careful, we can make something beautiful—together.”

Gus stood tall. “Thank you for helping me help,” he said.

Benny and Mira hugged Pippa. The busy basement still went clink-clank and whirr, but now it also went giggle-giggle and clap-clap.

Pippa fluffed her rainbow tail. “Different is special,” she sang. “Different is us.”

The friends nodded. “And kindness and safety help everyone,” they said.

The End.
