# Leo’s Balcony Garden Schedule

On a sunny morning, a brave little lion cub named Leo padded onto the balcony garden. The air smelled like mint and sweet tomatoes. Bees hummed. Wind chimes sang a tiny song. The balcony had green pots, a small watering can, and a cozy mat for resting paws.

Leo wiggled his whiskers. “I have so many jobs today!” he said. “I will water the plants, feed my fish in the tiny bowl, and help Grandma Lion with her tea.” He puffed his chest. “I can do it all!”

His friend, Miri the snail, peeked from a clay pot. “Good morning, Leo,” she said slowly. “What will you do first?”

Leo looked all around. The sun was warm. The tomatoes looked thirsty. The fish made little bubbles. Grandma Lion called from inside, “Tea in ten minutes, little star!”

Leo gulped. That was a lot! His tummy felt wiggly. “I will do everything now,” he said. He grabbed the watering can and ran. Water splashed. His paw bumped a pot. The tiny bowl wobbled. “Oh no!”

“Slow,” said Miri, blinking. “One thing at a time.”

Leo sat on his mat. He took a deep breath. In, out. In, out. “You’re right,” he said. “I need a plan.” He found a chalk and a small board. “Morning plan,” he wrote, with big, wobbly letters. “One: water plants. Two: feed fish. Three: help Grandma.”

Miri smiled. “That is smart time plan.”

Leo smiled back. His tail flicked with brave pride. He checked the sky. “The sun is hot. Plants first!”

He poured water gently. Drip, drip. The soil drank it up. The mint leaves smelled cool and green. “Thank you,” whispered the basil, swishing in the breeze.

Next, he stood by the fish bowl. “Snack time, Tiny Fin!” he said. He shook the food slowly. Plip, plip. Little flakes floated down. Tiny Fin wiggled and made happy bubbles. “Done!” Leo said.

Grandma Lion called, “Two minutes, Leo!”

Leo looked at his chalk board. He had time! He padded inside and carried the tea tray very carefully. The cups tinkled like bells. Back on the balcony, the steam smelled like lemon and honey. “Tea is ready, Grandma,” he said.

Grandma Lion smiled. “You used your time well, little star.”

Leo felt strong and calm. “My plan helped,” he said. “I did one job, then the next.”

Just then, a flutter of wings rushed in. A young pigeon named Pip landed on the railing. His feathers were messy. “I’m late! I’m late!” Pip cooed. “I promised to help build a nest, but I saw shiny things and forgot the time.” Pip’s eyes were big and worried.

Leo sat down beside him. “It’s okay, Pip,” he said kindly. “Sometimes we forget. You can try again. We can make a quick plan.”

Pip shuffled his feet. “But the other pigeons might be mad.”

Leo nodded. “Maybe. But you can say, ‘I’m sorry. I will do better.’ That is forgiveness.” He looked at his chalk board. “We can share my plan. First, drink water. Second, gather soft straw. Third, fly back.”

Pip took a deep breath. “I can do that.” He sipped from a tiny saucer of water. “Thank you, Leo.”

Miri the snail peered up. “Also, be kind to slow friends,” she said softly. “I am slow. But I try.”

Pip looked at Miri. “I’m sorry I almost bumped your pot,” he said. “I was in a rush.”

“I forgive you,” said Miri. “Please go slow.”

Pip smiled. “Thank you for your tolerance and forgiveness.” He fluffed his feathers. “I will be careful.”

They all worked together for a moment. Leo tied a small ribbon around a bundle of soft straw. Pip practiced flying to the railing and back, counting slowly, “One, two, three.” Leo counted with him. “One step, two step, three step.”

Suddenly, a tiny accident happened. Pip’s wing brushed the chalk board. It fell and smudged the plan. Chalk dust puffed like little clouds.

“Oh!” gasped Pip. “I messed up your plan!”

Leo blinked, then looked at Pip’s worried eyes. He felt a little pinch in his chest—then he breathed in and out. “It’s okay,” Leo said gently. “Accidents happen. I forgive you.” He smiled. “We can make a new plan together.”

They set the board upright. Leo wrote slowly, with Pip watching: “New plan: Help Pip fly. Then clean up chalk. Then rest.” Miri added a tiny leaf sticker to the corner. It looked cheerful and neat.

Pip took off with his straw bundle. “I will be on time!” he called. “Thank you, friends!”

Leo and Miri cleaned the chalk dust. Swish, swish. The balcony smelled like mint again. The wind chimes sang. Grandma Lion brought a plate of warm, cinnamon biscuits. “You all did well,” she said. “You were patient with each other. You forgave. And you used your time with care.”

Leo munched a biscuit. Crunch, sweet, cozy. He felt proud and calm. “A plan helps my day,” he said. “First this, then that. One thing at a time.”

Miri nodded. “And hearts feel soft when we forgive.”

Leo looked at the garden, at the shining fish bowl, and at the sky where Pip flew home. He touched his chalk board and smiled.

“Today,” he said, “we learned to use our time, to be gentle with friends who are different or late, and to forgive mistakes. That makes a brave heart.”

The End.
