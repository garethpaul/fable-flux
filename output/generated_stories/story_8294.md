# Danni the Dolphin’s Brave Bedtime

Danni was a playful dolphin who loved to splash, sing, and giggle. Tonight, Danni was in a cozy bedroom with sea-blue walls, soft shell pillows, and a warm, snuggly blanket shaped like a wave. The night-lamp glowed like a tiny moon, and the room smelled like clean soap and salty air.

“Time for our bedtime swim,” sang Mama Dolphin, brushing Danni’s small fins with a soft, tickly brush. “Brush, brush, swish, swish!”

Danni wiggled. “Mama, I want to try brushing by myself!”

Mama smiled. “That is brave, Danni. You can take the first turn.”

Danni took the brush. “Swish… swish…” The gentle bristles made a scratchy-shoosh sound. Danni’s fins felt tingly and clean. “I can do it!”

“You are taking initiative,” Mama said. “That means you try first with courage.”

Danni beamed. “Courage feels warm in my tummy.”

After brushing, Danni and Mama washed up. The water in the little bowl felt cool like tiny raindrops. Splash, pat, dry! Danni took a deep breath and said, “I am brave. I am clean. I am ready!”

They sat on the soft bed. The blanket rustled like a quiet wave. Outside, the night made a hush-hush sound. Danni hugged Bubble, a small pillow shaped like a smiley fish.

“Story time?” Danni asked.

“Story time,” Mama said. “But let’s take turns reading.”

“I can start!” Danni chirped. Then Danni’s voice got very small. “Mama… what if I make a mistake?”

Mama touched Danni’s cheek. “Courage means trying, even if you feel a little scared. We try, we learn, and we improve.”

Danni nodded. “Okay. I will try.”

Danni opened the book. The pages felt smooth. The paper smelled like lemons and ocean wind. Danni read slowly. “Once… there was a little… shell.” Danni paused. “Is that right?”

“Great try!” said Mama. “Your turn was wonderful. Now my turn.”

They took turns, page by page. Danni listened, then read, then listened again. It felt fair and friendly, like a gentle game. When Danni stumbled, Mama waited and smiled. Danni tried again. Each try felt easier, like swimming in soft water.

After the story, Mama said, “Time to tidy toys.”

Danni looked at the floor. The block boat, the music shell, and the rainbow rings were everywhere. Danni’s belly fluttered. “It looks so big. I feel small.”

“Courage,” whispered Mama. “We can make a plan. You pick up the rings. I’ll gather the blocks. We will take turns putting them in the basket.”

Danni took a deep breath. “I can do the first ring.” Plink! It dropped in the basket. “Now you!”

Mama dropped a block. Plonk!

They giggled. Plink, plonk, plink, plonk. The sounds made a happy little song. The room began to look neat. Danni’s worry swam away.

“We did it!” Danni clapped. The basket was full. The floor felt clear and smooth under Danni’s tail.

“Clean room, clean fins, clean heart,” Mama said. “Healthy habits help us rest.”

Danni yawned. “My eyes feel sleepy like soft clouds.”

“Before sleep,” Mama said, “would you like to practice brave breathing?”

“Yes, please,” Danni whispered.

They lay under the wave blanket. The fabric felt cool and silky. The lamp glowed warm and buttery. Mama said, “Smell the ocean for three… one, two, three. Blow tiny bubbles for three… one, two, three.”

Danni breathed in. The air smelled cozy, like soap and sea. Danni breathed out, making a teeny “boo-boo-boop” sound. “I can do brave breathing.”

“You showed courage many times tonight,” Mama said. “You brushed by yourself. You read and took turns. You cleaned with a plan. You started, you tried, and you improved.”

Danni’s heart felt big and floaty. “Mama, I was scared a little, but I tried.”

“That is what courage is,” Mama whispered. “Feeling a little scared and trying anyway.”

Danni thought for a moment. “Tomorrow, I will try to sing the bedtime song first.”

Mama smiled. “That’s initiative, my bright dolphin.”

Danni hummed a soft tune. The room made a gentle shhh like the sea. The blanket felt like a friendly hug. Bubble the pillow was squishy and cool on Danni’s cheek.

“Good night, Mama,” Danni murmured. “Thank you for taking turns with me.”

“Good night, Danni,” Mama said. “I am proud of your brave heart.”

Danni closed sleepy eyes. The cozy bedroom glowed softly. The tidy toys rested. The basket sat quiet. Danni’s breath went in and out, smooth like waves.

In the hush, Danni whispered one more time, “I can be brave. I can take turns. I can take care of me.”

And Danni drifted into sweet, splashy dreams, ready to try again tomorrow, growing a little stronger and a little braver each day.

The End.
