# Ollie Otter and the Fair Toy Box

Ollie the playful otter loved to wiggle, splash, and learn new things. Today, Ollie climbed a small wooden ladder to a quiet attic. The attic smelled like dry wood and warm dust. Sunlight made soft golden squares on the floor. Tiny motes floated and twinkled like little stars.

“Ooo,” said Ollie, whiskers twitching. “This is a calm place.”

In the corner, Ollie found a big toy box. It was painted blue with yellow dots. The lid squeaked. Inside, there were blocks, a soft drum, a buttony robot that went beep, and a pretty red scarf.

Ollie clapped. “I can build! I can play!”

Just then, light paws pattered. Miri the mouse peeked out from behind a trunk. “Squeak! Hello,” said Miri. “I like to drum.”

Pip the pigeon fluttered down from a beam. “Coo! I like the robot.”

And Tavi the turtle slowly stepped onto the rug. “Hello,” said Tavi. “I like the scarf.”

Ollie smiled. “We can all play.” Ollie’s tail swished with joy.

They each reached into the box. But something felt not quite right. The robot had a loose arm. The drum’s stick was missing. The scarf was tangled in a knot. The blocks were mixed with tiny pieces, and some were stuck together with tape.

Ollie’s whiskers drooped. “Oh no. This is not fair. We cannot play well.”

Miri’s ears bent. “I can’t drum if there is no stick.”

Pip flapped. “I can’t press the robot button. The arm keeps falling.”

Tavi frowned kindly. “My scarf is stuck. I cannot twirl.”

Ollie took a deep breath. The attic stayed quiet and warm. Ollie’s paws felt the smooth wood floor. “We want to play. We want it to be fair. Fair means everyone gets a good chance.”

Miri whispered, “What can we do?”

Ollie said, “We can use our voices. We can fix. We can share. That is justice. Justice means we make things right for everyone.”

Pip tilted his head. “How?”

Ollie thought. “Step one: Look and listen. Step two: Make a plan. Step three: Work together and speak kindly.”

They looked. Ollie gently tapped the robot. “Click, click.” The little screws were loose.

Miri softly beat the drum with a paw. “Thump… thump.” It sounded dull without a stick.

Tavi tried to pull the scarf. “Tug, tug.” It was tight and twisty.

Pip poked the blocks. “They are stuck.”

Ollie nodded. “Here is our plan. We will fix the toys so we all get a fair turn. That is justice.”

Miri stood tall. “I can find a drum stick. Maybe a pencil? Or a small dowel?”

Pip cooed, “I can find twine to tie the robot arm.”

Tavi said, “I can take my time and un-knot the scarf.”

Ollie smiled. “I will clean the blocks. I will peel the tape off.”

They began. The attic air was quiet and full of tiny sounds.

Miri tip-tapped around boxes. “Tap, tap.” She found a short wooden stick. “Squeak! A stick!”

Pip pecked at a little tool tin. “Coo, coo.” He found a bit of twine and a tiny screw. “Coo-hoo!”

Tavi placed the scarf in his lap. “Slow… and… gentle.” He breathed slow. “In… out.” The knot loosened.

Ollie used careful paws. “Peel… peel.” The tape came off the blocks with a soft “shhrrp.” He washed them with a damp cloth he found. “Wipe, wipe.” They looked bright and new.

Soon, the toys were ready. The robot’s arm stayed on. The drum went “thump-thump!” The scarf flowed like a red river. The blocks clicked together smoothly.

But then, Pip leaned toward the robot. Miri reached for the drum. Tavi hugged the scarf. Ollie picked up the blocks. Everyone wanted to play at the same time. It felt jumpy again.

Ollie put a paw on his heart. “Wait. We fixed the toys. Now we make turns. That is fair. That is justice.”

Miri nodded. “How do we make turns?”

Ollie smiled. “We use peaceful words. We say what we need. We listen. We make a plan.”

Ollie drew a circle in the dust with a block. “This is our turn circle.”

- First turn: Miri on the drum for one song.
- Second turn: Pip with the robot for ten beeps.
- Third turn: Tavi with the scarf for one slow twirl.
- Fourth turn: Ollie builds one tall tower.

“Then we swap,” said Ollie. “We will all try everything.”

Miri took a breath. “I need a turn on the drum.” She held the stick. “Is that okay?”

Pip cooed, “Yes. I will wait for my beeps.”

Tavi said, “I will twirl after the beeps.”

Ollie added, “I will build last. I will cheer for you.”

Miri drummed. “Thump, thump, thum-pa!” It sounded bright. Everyone smiled.

Pip pressed the robot. “Beep-beep-beep-beep-beep-beep-beep-beep-beep-beep!” He counted ten and stopped. “Coo! My turn is done.”

Tavi held the scarf and twirled slowly. “Whoosh.” The soft cloth brushed his shell. He beamed.

Ollie stacked blocks. “Click, click, click.” He made a tall tower. “I did it!”

They clapped and cheered for each other. It felt warm and fair. The attic glowed in the sunlight.

At last, they sat in a little circle. Ollie said, “Today we learned about justice. Justice means making things fair. We fixed the toys. We spoke kindly. We took turns. We gave everyone a good chance.”

Miri squeaked, “We used our voices.”

Pip cooed, “We solved problems.”

Tavi smiled, “We built skills.”

Ollie hugged his friends. “When we see something not fair, we can help make it right.”

The friends put the toys back in the box, neat and ready for the next day. The attic was still and happy.

Ollie whispered, “Fair is kind. Fair is fun. Justice is for everyone.”

The End.
