# Bubbles Discovers a Cozy Guest Room

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a happy tail flip. One sunny day, Bubbles swam up a magical bubble ramp and whoosh—plopped gently onto a soft rug in a cozy guest room.

“Ooh!” said Bubbles. “What is this place? It smells like clean sheets and lemon tea.”

The guest room was quiet and sunny. A little window let in warm light. The bed had a quilt that felt smooth and cool. There were shelves with baskets, and a small table with crayons, paper, and glue sticks. A tiny fan hummed, whirr, whirr. Bubbles could hear a clock tick-tock, tick-tock.

“I want to discover,” said Bubbles. “I want to learn something new today.”

Bubbles set a goal. “First, I will look with my eyes. Then, I will touch gently. Then, I will make something. I can do it step by step!”

Bubbles peeked at a basket. Inside were buttons, yarn, and shining stickers. Another basket had paper shapes—stars, circles, and fish. Bubbles giggled. “Fish! Like me!”

Just then, a soft voice came from the doorway. It was Nia, a kind kid with curly hair. “Hi, I’m Nia. This is our guest room. People rest here.”

Bubbles smiled. “Hi, Nia! I am Bubbles. I like to play and learn. May I explore?”

“Yes,” said Nia. “We explore with gentle hands. We share. We ask first.”

“I will ask,” said Bubbles. “May I look in this basket?”

“Yes,” said Nia. “Let’s discover together.”

They opened the lid. It creaked a tiny creak. Inside were seashells, smooth and cool. Bubbles held one to a cheek. “It is cold and silky.”

Nia put a shell to an ear. “I hear whoosh sounds. Like the sea.”

Bubbles listened. Whoosh-whoosh, like little waves. Bubbles felt happy and calm.

On the table, Nia set out paper, glue, and crayons. “Let’s craft a picture. We can make a guest-room welcome sign.”

Bubbles clapped fins. “I love crafting. My goal is to make a sign that says Welcome!”

They began to work. Bubbles used a blue crayon to draw a big wave. Swish, swish. Nia drew a cozy bed with a smiling pillow. They glued a shell in the corner. The glue was sticky-sweet and smelled like fruit. Bubbles wrinkled a nose and giggled. “It’s sticky!” The paper felt bumpy where the shell sat.

Bubbles said, “Nia, I am different. I am a dolphin, and I am in a guest room. Is that okay?”

Nia nodded. “Yes. We welcome all friends. Different is okay. We are kind and patient.”

Bubbles felt warm inside. “Thank you. I will be kind, too.”

They kept crafting. Bubbles tried to cut a star shape but snip, snip—it looked more like a squiggle. Bubbles frowned. “Oh no. I wanted a star.”

Nia smiled. “We can try again. We have time. We can set a small goal.”

“What is a small goal?” asked Bubbles.

“It means one little step,” said Nia. “First, we draw a star. Then, we cut slow.”

Bubbles breathed in, slow and steady. “I will try again.” Bubbles drew a star, then snipped along the lines. Snip, snip, snip. “I did it!” The star was neat and bright.

“Great work,” said Nia. “You learned by trying.”

They added stickers—sparkly dots, shiny hearts, and a little dolphin. Bubbles giggled. “That one looks like me!”

Just then, a cat with white paws tiptoed in. The cat’s name was Moon. Moon sniffed the shells and purred, rrrr-rrrr. Moon batted a button. It rolled, click-click, under the bed.

Bubbles blinked. “Oh! The button rolled away.”

Nia said, “We can look under the bed.” She lay down and peered into the soft shadow. “I see it! But I also see dust bunnies. Let’s use the feather duster.”

Bubbles helped by holding the dust pan with a fin. Swoosh-swoosh. The air smelled like feathers and a tiny bit dusty. They found the button and laughed.

“Thank you, Moon,” said Bubbles. “You helped us discover a hidden place.”

They placed the button back and finished the sign. It said, in big, friendly letters: Welcome!

Bubbles and Nia hung the sign on the guest room door. It looked bright and cheerful in the sunlight. The room felt extra cozy, like a hug.

Bubbles stretched a fin. “Today I learned three things. One: Discovery is fun when we look, listen, and ask. Two: We can set small goals and try again. Three: We are kind to friends, even when they are different.”

Nia smiled. “We learned together. That is the best way.”

Moon purred and rubbed against Bubbles’ fin. It felt soft and tickly. Bubbles giggled. “I like this guest room. It is a place for rest, for crafting, and for welcome.”

The clock went tick-tock, tick-tock. The fan hummed, whirr, whirr. The quilt shone in the warm light. Bubbles felt calm and proud.

“I discovered new things,” said Bubbles. “I learned. I made something special. That was my goal, and I did it.”

Nia gave a thumbs up. “Great job, Bubbles.”

Bubbles waved a happy fin. “I will remember: Look. Listen. Ask. Try. Be kind.”

Then the magical bubble ramp shimmered again. Bubbles floated back toward the sea, carrying the bright feeling of the guest room in a bubbly heart. “Goodbye, friends! I will visit again.”

“Goodbye, Bubbles!” called Nia. “Our door says Welcome.”

The End.
