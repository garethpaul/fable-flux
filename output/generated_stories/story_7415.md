# Otter Ollie Buckles Up

Otter Ollie was a playful otter with bright eyes and a happy wiggle. Today, Ollie climbed into the family car with Mama Otter, Papa Otter, and Baby Dot. The sun was warm, and the seat felt smooth and cool. The car smelled like oranges and a little like wet fur from Ollie’s splashy morning bath.

“Click, click,” said Papa, buckling his belt.

“Click!” said Mama, helping Baby Dot with her tiny seat.

Ollie bounced on the seat. “I want to play with my red ball!” he giggled, patting the soft, bouncy ball in his paws.

“First, self-care,” Mama said kindly. “We do what helps our bodies stay safe and calm.”

“What is self-care?” Ollie asked, tilting his head.

“Self-care means taking care of your body and feelings,” Mama said. “We buckle up, sit snug, and take deep breaths.”

Ollie nodded, but his tail swished. He wanted to wiggle, not buckle. He pulled the seat belt across his tummy, but then he peeked out the window and forgot. The belt slipped back.

“Vroom,” hummed the car as Papa started the engine. The seat hummed softly. The trees waved hello. Ollie hugged his red ball and bounced. The ball thumped against the window.

“Please sit still, Ollie,” Papa said in a calm voice. “Buckle up. That keeps you safe.”

Ollie pouted. “But I want to play now.”

Baby Dot squeaked, “Click! Safe!” and giggled.

The car rolled forward. A little bump in the road went thump-thump. Ollie bumped too. His tummy felt flip-floppy. “Oh,” he said quietly. He didn’t like the wobbly feeling.

Mama reached back with gentle eyes. “Ollie, your body is telling you something. What do you need?”

Ollie put a paw on his tummy. It felt fluttery. His heart felt quick. “I feel wiggly and a little worried,” he whispered.

“That’s okay,” Mama said. “We can solve this together. Step one: stop and notice. Step two: breath. Step three: buckle.”

Papa pulled the car to the side for a moment. The engine hummed kindly. A bird chirped outside, “Cheep, cheep!”

“Let’s do it together,” Mama said. “Breathe in like smelling a flower.” She held her paw to her nose and sniffed. “And out like blowing a bubble.” She puffed her cheeks and blew.

Ollie sniffed. He smelled the orange. He blew a pretend bubble. “Pop!” he giggled. They did it again. In, out. In, out. The fluttery feeling got smaller.

“Good self-care,” Mama smiled. “Now, buckle.”

Ollie pulled the belt across his chest. “Click!” It made a happy sound. The strap felt snug like a hug. His body felt strong and steady.

“I did it!” he beamed.

Papa smiled in the mirror. “Thank you, Ollie. That keeps everyone safe.”

The car went vroom again. The road made a soft whoosh. Ollie stroked his red ball softly on his lap. His paws felt the rubber bumps. It felt nice.

After a while, Ollie saw a bright truck. It honked, “Honk!” Ollie flinched. Baby Dot squeaked. Ollie wanted to shout, but he remembered. Self-care helps bodies and feelings. He took one flower breath and one bubble breath. In, out. He felt calm again.

Baby Dot reached for the ball. “Mine?” she asked.

Ollie hugged his ball tighter. “No! My ball!” His cheeks got hot. His tail twitched.

Mama said softly, “We have a problem. Two otters want one ball. Let’s use kind words.”

Ollie’s tummy squeezed. He wanted to keep the ball, but he also loved Baby Dot. He took a breath. “I feel mad,” he said. “I want my ball.”

Mama nodded. “Thank you for sharing your feelings. Baby Dot, can you wait?”

Baby Dot blinked and pouted. “Ball.”

Papa said, “We can make a plan.”

Ollie thought. He tapped the ball. Tap, tap. He looked at Baby Dot’s small paws. He listened to the hum of the car. He smelled orange again. He felt calmer.

“I have an idea,” Ollie said. “We can take turns. I will hold the ball until we count to ten. Then Baby Dot can hold it until we count to ten.”

Baby Dot clapped. “Ten!”

Mama smiled. “That is great conflict-resolution.”

They counted together. “One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten!” Ollie passed the ball to Baby Dot. She hugged it and giggled. They counted again. Ten! Baby Dot passed it back. Sharing felt warm and proud.

Soon, the car stopped at the park. Papa turned off the engine. The car grew quiet. The birds sang sweetly. The sun felt warm on the windows.

“Before we get out,” Mama said, “what do we do?”

Ollie grinned. “Self-care! We wait. Papa opens the door. We look. We step out safely.”

Papa opened the door and looked for other cars. “All clear.” Ollie unbuckled, “Click,” and slid out with careful paws. The ground felt firm. The air smelled like grass.

Mama gave Ollie a hug. “Today you took care of your body and your feelings. You buckled up, you breathed, and you shared.”

Ollie’s whiskers twitched with joy. “Self-care helps me be safe and kind,” he said.

“Yes,” Papa said. “That is the lesson.”

Ollie laughed and held Baby Dot’s paw. “Let’s play. And we can take turns!”

The End.
