# Ollie Owl’s Kitchen Art Plan

The warm kitchen glowed like a hug. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. A pot of oatmeal bubbled softly, saying blip, blip. Cinnamon smelled sweet in the air.

On a wooden stool sat Ollie the wise old owl. His feathers were soft and speckled. He wore tiny round glasses on his beak. He tapped a pencil on a notepad and hooted, “Hoo-hoo! Today, we make a big art poster!”

Pip the kitten padded in, tail swishing. “Art? Me-ow! I like paint!”

Mimi the mouse peeked from a teacup. “I can glue sparkles,” she squeaked.

Ollie nodded. “We will plan first,” he said in a warm, gentle voice. “Planning helps us know what to do. Planning makes things smooth.”

He pointed to his notepad. “Step one: choose a picture. Step two: gather tools. Step three: make a spot to work. Step four: clean up. Hoo-hoo!”

Pip bounced. “Let’s paint now!”

Ollie smiled kindly. “We will, Pip. First, we plan.” He wrote big, simple words and drew little pictures: a sun for “picture,” a brush for “tools,” a table for “spot,” and a broom for “clean.”

Mimi sniffed the cinnamon air. “Mmm. I feel cozy. I choose a picture of the sun.”

“Good choice,” Ollie said. “The sun is bright and happy.”

They looked around the warm kitchen. The table felt smooth. The oven hummed. The oatmeal smelled like cookies. Outside, birds chirped. It was a good day for art.

“Tools time,” said Ollie. He opened a drawer. “Do we have paper, paint, brushes, tape, and rags?”

Pip wiggled his whiskers. “I see paper! I see blue paint and yellow paint! I see a fat brush and a tiny brush!”

Mimi tugged at a roll of tape. “I have tape! Sticky-sticky!”

Ollie set everything on the table in a neat line. He put an old newspaper under the paper. “This is our work spot,” he said. “It will keep the table safe.”

Pip’s eyes grew big. “Can I start now? Please?”

“Yes,” said Ollie. “But let’s talk about the plan for the picture. First, a yellow circle. Then sun rays. Then a happy face if we like.”

“Okay!” Pip dipped the fat brush into yellow paint. Swoosh! The brush made a big circle. The paint was cool and squishy, and it smelled a little like soap.

Mimi dabbed sparkles gently. “Twinkle, twinkle,” she sang.

Ollie watched with a soft smile. “Slow and steady. Hoo-hoo. Planning helps our art shine.”

Pip felt excited. He wanted to add blue clouds. He looked left and right. Ollie’s glasses glinted. The notepad sat on the counter. Pip whispered, “I will just add clouds now. I won’t tell. It’s a surprise.”

He dipped the tiny brush into blue paint. Drip! A big drop fell—plop—right on the table where there was no newspaper. A blue spot spread like a puddle.

“Oh no,” Pip gasped. His ears went flat. His paws felt sticky. The warm kitchen felt a little too warm.

Mimi blinked. “Pip, did you drip?”

Pip looked at Ollie’s wise eyes. He took a small breath. “Yes. I did. I didn’t follow the plan. I didn’t tell you. I made a mess. I am sorry.”

Ollie tilted his head and hooted softly. “Thank you for telling the truth, Pip,” he said. “Honesty is brave. Now we can fix it.”

He pointed to the list. “Step four: clean up. We planned for spills.” He handed Pip a damp rag. “Wipe slow.”

Pip wiped the blue spot. Swish, swish. The table felt smooth again. His tummy unknot. “I feel better,” he said.

Mimi nodded. “Honesty helps. Planning helps, too.”

Ollie added a new line to the notepad: “Step five: check the plan.” He smiled. “Sometimes we forget. That is okay. We can check and try again.”

They went back to the picture. The yellow sun dried shiny. Pip painted soft blue clouds on the paper this time, right where the newspaper covered the table. Mimi glued a tiny orange border. The glue smelled like apples. Ollie drew gentle sun rays: zip, zip, zip. The kitchen felt peaceful and bright.

“Done,” Pip purred.

“Beautiful,” Mimi squeaked.

Ollie held up the poster. The sun smiled. The clouds puffed like marshmallows. Sparkles winked. “Teamwork,” said Ollie. “Planning, art, and honesty made this.”

They cleaned the brushes with warm water. Swish, swish, swish. They put caps on the paint with little clicks. They folded the newspaper and wiped the table one more time. The oatmeal was ready. Ollie scooped bowls and sprinkled cinnamon. Steam rose in soft curls.

They sat together in the warm kitchen, spoons tapping lightly. Pip licked a cinnamon dot from his nose and giggled.

Ollie tapped the notepad. “What did we learn today?”

Pip raised a paw. “Plan first, then paint.”

Mimi wiggled her whiskers. “Tell the truth if there’s a spill.”

Ollie nodded. “Yes. Planning helps us know what to do. Art is more fun when we plan. Honesty helps us fix mistakes. Hoo-hoo!”

They hung the sun poster on the fridge. It glowed like a little morning inside the kitchen.

The End.
