# Robo-Pup Listens and Wins

In a cozy study corner, with a sunny window and a soft blue rug, lived a helpful robot dog named Robo-Pup. His metal ears could wiggle, his nose light could blink, and his tail made a happy click-click sound. The study corner smelled like crayons and fresh paper. A tiny fan hummed a gentle “whirr.”

Robo-Pup loved to help. He loved lists, too. He had a bright yellow task card that said:
1. Listen to Coach’s rules.
2. Finish homework.
3. Practice sports moves.

“Woof-beep!” he said, tapping the card with his paw.

Today was special. The kids in the house were getting ready for Sports Day at the park later. But first, they had to finish their worksheet in the study corner. Coach Lina would call to tell the rules for Sports Day. Robo-Pup’s job was to listen and help everyone complete tasks.

Maya and Sam sat at the little desk. Pencil tips made scratch-scratch sounds. Robo-Pup sat tall, ears up. The tablet buzzed. “Bzzz!” Coach Lina’s face popped up.

“Hi, team!” Coach Lina smiled. “For Sports Day, we must be fair. That means justice—everyone gets the same turn. We also must listen. Here are the rules. Rule one: Stop and listen when the whistle blows. Rule two: Take turns. Rule three: Finish what you start.”

Robo-Pup’s tail clicked. “Woof-beep! I will listen.” He leaned in close, his ear lights glowing.

“Remember,” Coach Lina said kindly. “If you feel too wiggly, pause, take a belly breath, count to three, and then try again. That’s called self-regulation.”

Robo-Pup repeated, “Pause. Breathe. Count. Try again.” He looked at Maya and Sam. “We can do it!”

But just then, a soccer ball rolled into the study corner from the hallway. It bumped Robo-Pup’s paw. “Thump!” Robo-Pup loved soccer. He wanted to run and kick. His motors buzzed. His tail went click-click-click-click faster.

Maya giggled. “Let’s kick it!”

Sam stood up. The chair made a squeak. “Just one kick!”

The tablet was still talking. Coach Lina said, “If you don’t listen, you might miss important instructions.”

Robo-Pup felt tug-tug inside. He looked at the ball. He looked at the tablet. He wanted to play. But the yellow task card blinked softly: 1. Listen to Coach’s rules.

Robo-Pup took a small step back. He pressed his paw to his chest. “Pause,” he whispered. He took a slow belly breath. “In…” His tiny fan whirred. “Out…” He counted softly, “One, two, three.”

His motors calmed. The buzz quieted. He sat again, ears up. “I am listening,” he said.

Maya and Sam saw Robo-Pup. They copied him. They put the ball by the shelf. They took a breath together. “In… out…” They counted, “One, two, three.” Then they sat.

Coach Lina finished. “Great listeners! See you at the park. Remember justice: everyone gets the same number of turns. And complete your homework before you play.”

The call ended. The room was calm. The sun made warm squares on the rug. Robo-Pup wagged. “We listened! Now we complete tasks.”

Maya smiled. “Let’s finish our worksheet.”

Sam nodded. “Then sports!”

They worked. Pencils went scratch-scratch. Erasers made soft shhh sounds. Robo-Pup read the questions out loud in a gentle beep-voice. “Circle the red ball.” Maya circled it. “Count to five.” Sam counted, “One, two, three, four, five!”

Robo-Pup checked the yellow card. “Step two: Finish homework.” He stamped a tiny paw print sticker. “Done!”

“Time for practice,” said Maya.

Robo-Pup brought foam cones. He lined them up on the rug. “We will practice stopping when we hear the whistle.” He played a little whistle sound: “Tweet!”

Maya ran. Sam ran. Robo-Pup’s tail clicked. “Tweet!” Everyone froze like statues. They giggled. Robo-Pup said, “Good listening!”

They tried taking turns. “Maya dribbles first,” said Robo-Pup. “Sam counts to ten. Then switch.” Maya dribbled the soft ball, “tap-tap-tap,” while Sam counted, “One, two, three…” At ten, Robo-Pup said, “Switch!” Sam dribbled while Maya counted. Robo-Pup watched with bright eyes. “This is justice,” he explained. “Same turns for everyone.”

Soon it was time for Sports Day at the park. But the study corner fun made them ready. At the park, kids lined up. The coach blew the whistle. “Tweet!”

Everyone stopped and listened. Robo-Pup stood next to Maya and Sam. He took a tiny breath. His ear lights glowed. He remembered: Pause. Breathe. Count. Try again.

The relay race began. Maya ran, then passed the baton. Sam ran next. Some kids shouted. One boy tried to take an extra turn. Robo-Pup trotted over gently. “Woof-beep. Justice means fair turns,” he said kindly. “You will get your turn too.” The boy nodded. “Okay,” he said. He waited, and he smiled when his turn came.

At the end, Coach Lina clapped. “Great listening! You finished tasks, took turns, and stayed calm.”

Robo-Pup wagged. The sun felt warm. The grass smelled sweet. Maya hugged Robo-Pup’s smooth metal neck. “You helped us listen,” she said.

Sam patted Robo-Pup’s head. “And we had fun!”

Robo-Pup blinked his nose light. “When we listen, we learn. When we are fair, everyone can play. When we finish what we start, we feel proud.”

They all cheered. The study corner, the park, the list—everything felt bright and good.

The clear lesson:
- Listen first.
- Be fair and take turns.
- Pause, breathe, and finish your tasks.

Robo-Pup’s tail made a happy click-click. “Woof-beep! We did it!”

The End.
