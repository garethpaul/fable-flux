# Squeaky’s Bathroom Build

Squeaky the squirrel loved to explore. One sunny morning, he scampered into the big, bright bathroom. The tiles felt cool under his tiny paws. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet, singing a soft song. The air smelled like bubbly soap and minty toothpaste.

“Oh!” said Squeaky, his eyes wide. “So many shiny things to discover!”

He peeked into a drawer. “Clink!” went the comb. He sniffed a fluffy towel. “Mmm, it smells like lemon!” He watched bubbles slide down the shower wall. “They look like little moons!” he giggled.

Squeaky’s heart felt busy and happy. He loved finding out how things worked. On the counter, he saw cups, brushes, and a little basket. On the floor, he saw toy boats from bath time. Some things were messy. Some things were tipped over.

Squeaky tilted his head. “If I discover where everything belongs, I can help,” he said. “And maybe I can build something, too!”

Just then, Hedgie the hedgehog waddled in, carrying a tiny rubber duck. “Quack-quack!” he squeaked for fun.

“Hi, Hedgie!” Squeaky chirped. “Let’s explore together.”

Hedgie sniffed. “It smells fresh in here. What are you doing?”

“I’m making discoveries,” said Squeaky. “Look! This basket can hold brushes. This cup can hold toothbrushes. And those toy boats need a harbor!”

“A harbor?” asked Hedgie.

“Yes,” said Squeaky, tail twitching with ideas. “Let’s build a boat harbor to keep the toys safe and tidy.”

Hedgie clapped his little paws. “That’s fun! But what is fair? My duck needs a spot too.”

Squeaky nodded. “Fairness means everyone gets a place. We can share the space.”

They looked around. The bathtub made a gentle “plop” as a drop fell. The mirror shone. The towel felt soft as a cloud.

Squeaky began to organize. He made a plan, saying the steps out loud.

“Step one: Discovery! We find out what we have,” he said, pointing. “Brushes, cups, towels, boats, and a duck.”

“Step two: Sorting,” added Hedgie. “Things that go together stay together.”

“Step three: Building,” said Squeaky. His eyes sparkled. “We build the harbor!”

They negotiated kindly. Squeaky asked, “Hedgie, where do you want the duck’s spot?”

Hedgie thought. “Near the boats, but not too crowded.”

Squeaky nodded. “Good idea. How about the corner by the drain? The duck can rest there.”

“That is fair,” said Hedgie, smiling. “The boats can share the shelf ledge.”

They both worked. Squeaky slid the toy boats onto the low edge of the tub. “Slide, slide,” went the plastic. He lined them up from little to big.

Hedgie placed the rubber duck in the corner. “Quack,” the duck squeaked when he pressed it. Hedgie giggled. “It likes its new home!”

Squeaky lifted the little basket. “Brushes go here.” He tapped the basket’s side. “Tap, tap.” He put toothbrushes in the cup. “Stand tall,” he whispered to them. He folded the lemony towel and hung it straight. “There. Soft and neat.”

They stepped back and looked. The bathroom felt calm. The boats were in a row, the duck had a spot, and the counter was tidy.

“Do we need signs?” asked Hedgie.

“Yes!” Squeaky said. “Let’s build signs!” He found sticky notes and a crayon in a nearby drawer. He drew simple pictures: a boat, a duck, a brush, a cup. He stuck the picture notes above each place.

“Now everyone will know,” Squeaky said. “This is organized.”

Hedgie’s eyes shone. “We discovered, we sorted, and we built! We also made it fair.”

They heard soft steps. Bunny hopped in, ears wiggling. “Wow,” Bunny whispered. “It looks so tidy! But I want to play with the duck. Is that okay?”

Squeaky smiled. “Yes. We can share. Let’s make a rule.”

He spoke in a gentle voice. “Rule: If we play, we put things back. We take turns so it’s fair.”

Bunny nodded. “I can do that.”

Hedgie said, “I would like three quacks with the duck, then you can have three quacks, Bunny.”

“That’s a good negotiation,” Squeaky said. “Three quacks each!”

The duck went “Quack, quack, quack” for Hedgie. Then Bunny had three quacks. They laughed and clapped.

After playing, Bunny placed the duck back in the corner. “I followed the rule,” Bunny said, proud.

Squeaky felt warm inside. He listened. The bathroom was quiet and clean. He sniffed the lemon towel. He touched the smooth cup. He watched the boats rest in their harbor.

“We did it,” he said softly. “We discovered how things work. We built a harbor. We made it organized and fair.”

Hedgie nodded. “We worked together. We used kind words.”

Bunny added, “We made a plan and kept it.”

Squeaky turned to his friends. “What did we learn?”

Hedgie said, “Discovery helps us see what we have.”

Bunny said, “Building can solve problems.”

Squeaky said, “And organization makes things easy to find. Fairness and negotiation help us share.”

They smiled at one another. The faucet stopped dripping, as if it was listening and pleased.

Squeaky fluffed his tail. “Every day can be an adventure,” he said. “Even in a bathroom!”

They all giggled. Then they washed their paws, dried them on the lemon towel, and waved goodbye to the tidy harbor.

The bathroom felt bright and calm. Squeaky’s heart felt bright and calm, too. He knew he could discover, build, and organize again tomorrow.

The End.
