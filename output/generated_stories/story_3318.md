# Daisy's Mindful Meal

Little Daisy the deer fawn stood in Grandma Rabbit's cozy dining room, her soft brown eyes wide with worry. The wooden table was set with pretty blue plates and shiny spoons, but something was very wrong.

"Oh no!" Daisy whispered, looking at the mess she had made. Orange juice dripped from the table onto the soft carpet below. The pitcher lay on its side, and sticky puddles covered everything.

Daisy's heart beat fast like a drum. "Thump-thump, thump-thump!" She had been so excited to help set the table that she forgot to be careful. Now Grandma Rabbit's special lunch was ruined!

"I'm so sorry, Grandma Rabbit!" Daisy called out, her voice shaky. "I made a big mess!"

Grandma Rabbit hopped into the dining room, her fluffy white ears bouncing. When she saw the orange juice everywhere, she took a deep, slow breath. "Oh my," she said gently.

Daisy's eyes filled with tears. "I was trying to help, but I moved too fast. I'm sorry, I'm sorry!"

Grandma Rabbit sat down next to Daisy and patted her soft fur. "Sweet Daisy, accidents happen. But let's take a moment to breathe together, okay?"

"Breathe?" Daisy sniffled.

"Yes, dear. When we feel upset or worried, we can use our breath to help us feel calm. Watch me." Grandma Rabbit closed her eyes and took a slow breath in through her nose. "Smell the fresh air," she said softly. Then she breathed out slowly through her mouth. "And let the worry float away."

Daisy tried it too. She breathed in slowly, smelling the sweet flowers from the garden outside. Then she breathed out, feeling a little calmer. "That feels nice," she said.

"Good! Now, let's think mindfully about what we can do," said Grandma Rabbit. "Being mindful means paying attention to right now, and thinking before we act."

Daisy looked around the dining room. She noticed how the sun made golden squares on the floor through the window. She heard birds singing outside. She felt the soft carpet under her hooves.

"I can help clean up!" Daisy said, standing up straight. "I want to volunteer to make it better."

"What a wonderful idea," smiled Grandma Rabbit. "Let's work together."

Daisy moved slowly and carefully this time, thinking about each step. She got paper towels from the kitchen while Grandma Rabbit got the mop. Together, they wiped up every sticky drop.

"Now I'll be more mindful," Daisy said as she gently placed new napkins on the table. "I'll move slowly and think about what I'm doing."

When they finished cleaning, the dining room sparkled even more than before! Daisy carefully poured fresh orange juice into a new pitcher, holding it with both hooves and moving like a graceful dancer.

"Thank you for forgiving me and teaching me about mindfulness," Daisy said, giving Grandma Rabbit a gentle hug.

"You're very welcome, dear. Remember, when we slow down and pay attention, we can do amazing things. And when we make mistakes, we can always make them better with kindness and hard work."

As they sat down to eat their lunch, Daisy took another mindful breath. She noticed how good the soup smelled, how warm the bowl felt in her hooves, and how happy she felt inside. From that day on, Daisy always remembered to breathe, think, and move mindfully.

The End.
