# Benny Bunny and the Sticky Keys

Benny Bunny was a determined rabbit. His ears stood tall when he had a plan. Today, he hopped into Mama’s home office. The room smelled like paper and pencils. The window was open, and a friendly breeze tickled his whiskers. On the desk sat a shiny keyboard and a colorful stack of sticky notes.

“I will make a thank-you card for Grandma,” Benny said. “I will type the words, then draw a picture!”

Tap-tap, he touched the keys with his soft paws. “Clack, clack,” went the keyboard. But then it made a funny sound. “Squish… squish?” Benny wrinkled his nose. The keys felt sticky. They made his paw pads damp.

“Hmm,” Benny hummed. He pressed another key. “Squish!” A sweet, fruity smell floated up. It smelled like strawberry jam.

Benny’s eyes grew wide. “Oh no,” he whispered. “The keys are messy. My paws are messy too!”

Mama Bunny peeked in with a warm smile. She wore round glasses and held a mug of tea. “Working hard, Benny?” she asked.

“Yes, Mama,” Benny said. He wiggled his sticky paws. “But the keys are squishy. My paws feel yucky. I can’t type my thank-you words.”

Mama’s whiskers twitched. “We can fix this,” she said. “What’s your plan, my determined bunny?”

Benny took a deep breath. He put his paw on his chest. Thump-thump went his brave heart. “Step one,” he said slowly, “I will stop touching the keys.” He lifted his paws carefully. “Step two, I will get clean. Clean paws make clean keys. Step three, I will clean the keyboard, gently. Step four, I will try again.”

Mama nodded. “That is smart, Benny. Hygiene helps our tools and our bodies.”

Benny hopped to the bathroom. The light was bright and warm. He turned the faucet. “Swish!” Cool water ran like a tiny river. He squeezed a pea-sized drop of soap. “Plop!” It smelled like lemons. He rubbed his paws together—front, back, between the little toes. “Bubbles!” Benny giggled. The foam looked like little clouds. He sang, “Scrub, scrub, rub-a-dub,” until his paws squeaked clean.

He rinsed. “Swish!” He shook his paws. “Drip-drip.” Then he dried them on a fluffy towel. “Soft,” he said, rubbing his cheeks with a smile.

Back in the home office, Mama held up a soft cloth and a small bottle. “This is safe cleaner,” she said. “We will keep the keyboard off while we clean.”

Benny nodded. “Step three,” he whispered. He watched carefully. Mama sprayed the cloth, not the keys—“psst”—then wiped gently. Benny used a cotton swab for the little spaces. “Tick-tick,” went the tiny stick. He worked slow and steady. His ears wiggled with focus.

“This is taking a long time,” Benny said, feeling a tiny frown.

“You are being patient,” Mama said. “That is resilience. You keep going, even when it is slow.”

Benny breathed in and out. In smelled like lemon. Out felt calm. “I can do it,” he said. “I am a determined rabbit.”

At last, the keys looked shiny again. No more squish. No more jam. Benny’s nose twitched with pride.

“Step four,” he said, hopping to the chair. He set a clean sticky note by the keyboard, just in case. He pressed a key. “Click!” Not squish. He smiled. He pressed another. “Click-click!” The keyboard sounded happy.

Benny typed, saying each word out loud. “Thank you, Grandma, for the cozy scarf.” He sounded out the letters. “T…h… Thank!” His ears perked with each click. He loved the way words felt in his mouth and looked on the screen. “Words are like little hugs,” he said.

“Beautiful,” Mama whispered.

Benny printed the words and set the paper on the desk. Now it was art time. He brought out crayons and a clean sheet. He drew a big bunny with a bright scarf—zigzags of red and gold. “Scratch-scratch,” went the crayons. He added stars and a warm sun. The paper smelled like wax and fun.

“May I glue the picture under my words?” he asked.

“Yes,” Mama said, “but first—clean hands.” She held up a gentle wipe. Benny cleaned his paws again. “Swipe, swipe.” He spread the glue—“smear”—and pressed the picture. “Pat-pat.” No mess, no smudges.

Benny held up his card. “I did it!” His eyes shone. His chest felt light and proud.

Mama clapped softly. “What did you learn, Benny?”

Benny thought. He looked at his clean paws, the clean keys, and his lovely card. “I learned that clean hands help me do my work. I learned to make a plan and follow steps. I kept trying, even when it was slow. Hygiene helps my art. Hygiene helps my words. Hygiene helps me!”

Mama smiled. “That is a big lesson for a small rabbit.”

Benny placed the card in an envelope. He drew a tiny lemon on the flap, just for fun. The home office felt fresh and bright. The keyboard went “click-click,” like a happy song.

“Next time,” Benny said, “I will wash my paws before I type. Clean before, clean after.”

“That is smart and kind,” Mama said.

Benny’s ears stood tall again. He was ready for his next idea, his next word, his next drawing—clean and calm and full of care.

The End.
