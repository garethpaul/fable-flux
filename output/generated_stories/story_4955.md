# Benny Beaver's Big Art Plan

Benny Beaver sat at his little wooden desk in his cozy home office. Sunlight streamed through the window, making his brown fur sparkle. He wiggled his flat tail with excitement as he looked at all his art supplies scattered everywhere.

"Oh my!" said Benny, scratching his head with his tiny paw. "I want to make something special for Grandma Beaver's birthday tomorrow, but I don't know where to start!"

Colorful crayons rolled across his desk. Paint brushes were mixed up with pencils. Glue sticks hid under piles of paper. Everything was such a mess!

Benny's whiskers twitched as he thought hard. "Maybe I should make a plan first," he said to himself. "That's what Mama always tells me to do!"

He found a clean piece of white paper and drew a big circle at the top. "This is for my ideas," he said, speaking out loud like he always did when he was thinking.

Inside the circle, Benny drew a heart. "Grandma loves hearts," he whispered, remembering how she always drew hearts on his lunch notes.

Next to the heart, he drew a tree. "And she loves our family tree by the pond where we all live!"

Then Benny made another circle below the first one. "This is for what I need," he announced proudly. He wrote in his best letters: "PAPER, PAINT, BRUSHES."

"Now for my plan!" Benny drew three boxes in a row. In the first box, he drew himself cleaning up. In the second box, he drew himself painting. In the third box, he drew himself giving the gift to Grandma.

"First, I'll clean up my workspace," Benny said, feeling much better now that he had a plan. "Then I'll paint my picture. Then I'll surprise Grandma!"

Benny began putting his crayons in the red cup, his pencils in the blue cup, and his paintbrushes in the yellow cup. The sweet smell of new crayons filled the air as he organized everything neatly.

"This feels so much better!" he chirped happily.

Next, Benny took out his watercolor paints. The colors looked like little rainbows in their tiny squares. He dipped his brush in water and watched the blue paint swirl and dance on his paper.

Carefully, he painted a big tree with a brown trunk and green leaves. Around the tree, he painted all his family members as little beavers - Mama, Papa, his sister Bella, and Grandma with her special purple hat.

"Perfect!" Benny clapped his paws together when he finished.

The next morning, Benny wrapped his painting in newspaper and tied it with a yellow ribbon he found in Mama's sewing box.

At Grandma's birthday party, everyone gathered around as she opened Benny's gift. Her eyes sparkled with joy when she saw the family tree painting.

"Oh, Benny!" Grandma hugged him tight. "This is the most beautiful gift ever! I can see you planned this very carefully."

"I did, Grandma!" Benny beamed. "I made a plan with circles and boxes, and it helped me make something really special for you!"

Grandma hung the painting right above her fireplace where everyone could see it. "This will remind me every day how much our family loves each other," she said.

Benny felt warm and proud inside. Planning ahead had helped him create the perfect gift and make Grandma so happy. From that day on, whenever Benny wanted to make something special, he always started with a good plan.

The End.
