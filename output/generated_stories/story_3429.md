# Koala’s Porch Poems

Kiki the koala was calm and cozy on the peaceful front porch. The sun was warm. The porch boards felt smooth under her soft paws. A gentle breeze smelled like lemon tea and eucalyptus leaves. Birds chirped sweet little songs.

Kiki took a deep breath. “Today, I will write,” she whispered. She had a small blue notebook and a short, stubby pencil. The pencil felt smooth and cool in her paw. She liked the scratchy sound it made on the paper. Scratch, scratch!

Her friend Pip the parrot fluttered down to the porch rail. “Good morning, Kiki!” chirped Pip. “What are you doing?”

“I am writing a poem,” said Kiki in her calm voice. “Writing helps me share my thoughts. Writing helps me slow down and see.”

Pip tilted his head. “Can I help?”

“Yes,” said Kiki, smiling. “Look and listen with me. We will write what we see, hear, smell, and feel.”

Kiki looked out from the porch. “I see the bright, round sun,” she said.

Scratch, scratch. She wrote, “Sun is a bright hug.”

Pip listened. “I hear birds singing. Tweet, tweet, tweet!” he chirped.

Scratch, scratch. Kiki wrote, “Bird songs tickle the air.”

Kiki closed her eyes for a moment. “I smell lemon tea,” she said softly.

Scratch, scratch. “Tea smells warm and kind.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “I feel the breeze. Whoosh!” He wiggled. “It makes me want to move!”

Kiki nodded. “Writing is about senses. We write what we notice. Let’s take a wiggle break. Moving helps our brains think.”

They stood up on the porch. Kiki stretched her arms to the sky. “Reach up high,” she said.

Pip reached his wings up. “Reach, reach!”

Kiki bent slowly to touch her toes. “Hello, toes,” she giggled.

Pip pecked gently at his claws. “Hello, claws!”

They stepped side to side. Step, step. Tap, tap. The porch made a friendly thump under their feet. Kiki felt her heart beat strong and steady. “Exercise helps me feel awake,” she said. “Now I can write more.”

A little mouse named Momo peeked from a flower pot. “Did I hear music?” asked Momo. “I brought my tiny drum.” He held up a small round drum and a short stick.

Kiki’s eyes lit up. “Music can help our writing,” she said. “Let’s make a steady beat. A beat can guide our words.”

Momo tapped the drum. Dum… dum… dum.

Pip sang softly. “La, la, la.”

Kiki listened to the beat. She wrote with the rhythm. Scratch, scratch, dum, dum.

“Breeze says shhhh, porch says tap,
Feet go step, hearts go clap.”

Pip clapped his wings. “That sounds nice!”

Kiki smiled. “Music helps my words dance.”

A cloud moved over the sun. The light became soft and gray. The air felt cool and smelled like wet leaves. A tiny raindrop fell on the porch rail. Plip!

Pip blinked. “Rain!”

Momo tapped a faster beat. Dum-dum-dum-dum!

Kiki stayed calm. “Let’s write the rain, too,” she said. She held out her paw and felt a cool drop. “The drop is cold and small.”

Scratch, scratch. “Rain taps tiny hello songs.”

The rain began to patter gently on the roof. Pit-pat, pit-pat. The sound was cozy. Kiki took another deep breath. “Writing helps me feel the world,” she said. “When I write, I notice more. I remember more. I understand my feelings.”

Pip nodded. “I feel happy and calm.”

Momo swayed to the drum. “I feel brave and busy.”

Kiki looked at her notebook. It had neat lines of words. Some lines were short. Some were long. All were true. “Would you like to hear my porch poem?” she asked.

“Yes, please!” said Pip and Momo together.

Kiki read in her soft, slow voice:

“Sun is a bright hug,
Bird songs tickle the air.
Tea smells warm and kind,
Breeze whispers in my hair.

Porch boards go tap-tap,
Feet step, hearts clap along.
Rain taps tiny hello,
We answer with our song.

I write what I notice,
I write what I feel.
Words make my world clear,
Words help my heart heal.”

Pip’s eyes shone. “That is beautiful.”

Momo beamed. “Your words march and dance!”

Kiki hugged her notebook. “Thank you. Writing is for everyone. We can all write. We can write with pictures, or letters, or sounds. We can write about what we see, hear, smell, taste, and feel. We can take movement breaks. We can add music. These help our words grow.”

The rain stopped. The sun peeked out again. A sweet smell rose from the wet porch. The birds began to sing brighter songs. Pip flapped in a happy circle. Momo tapped a final dum!

Kiki smiled and put her pencil down. “Today we learned to write with our senses, to move our bodies to wake up our brains, and to let music help our words find a beat. When we look closely, listen gently, and practice, our writing becomes strong.”

Pip chirped, “Let’s write again tomorrow!”

“Yes,” said Kiki. “Every day can be a writing day.”

They sat together on the peaceful front porch, calm and glad, ready for more words, more songs, and more little steps.

The End.
