# Toshi the Tortoise Rides Safe

The family car hummed softly like a sleepy bee. The seats were warm and comfy, and the air smelled like orange slices from a snack bag. On the back seat sat Toshi, a wise tortoise with a shiny green shell and kind, careful eyes.

“Click,” went Toshi’s seat belt. “Safe and snug,” he said in his slow, gentle voice.

Maya and Leo sat beside him, swinging their feet. “We’re going to the park!” Leo cheered.

“We can play car games,” Maya said. She wiggled and reached for Toshi’s shell. “Your shell is so smooth!”

Toshi smiled, but he held up a small, careful paw. “I like gentle hands, Maya. Please ask first.”

Maya blinked. “Can I touch your shell, Toshi?”

“Yes,” Toshi said, nodding. “One gentle pat is okay.” Maya patted once, soft as a feather. Toshi’s eyes crinkled. “Thank you for asking. That keeps my body safe.”

The car rolled on. Trees swooshed by the window. “Who wants music?” Dad asked from the front.

“Me!” said Leo. The song began with a happy beat. Maya started to dance in her seat.

“Boing, boing!” Leo bounced up and reached for his seat belt buckle. “I want to stand!”

Toshi’s eyes grew wide. He took a breath, slow and calm. “Leo,” he said kindly, “please keep your buckle clicked. Seat belts keep bodies safe.”

Leo frowned. “But I want to dance.”

Toshi nodded. “Me too. Let’s dance with our fingers!” He wiggled his claws. “Tap-tap-tap.” He bobbed his head in tiny moves that stayed in his seat. Maya giggled and copied: “Tap-tap-tap!”

Leo looked at his buckle, then at Toshi’s smile. “Okay.” He clicked the buckle tight again. “Tap-tap-tap!” The three danced with small, safe moves. Dad peeked in the mirror and smiled.

The car stopped at a light. A bright red ball rolled under Maya’s feet. She leaned down to grab it and bumped into Toshi.

“Oof!” said Toshi. The ball rolled back under the seat. Maya frowned. “I didn’t mean to, Toshi.”

Toshi patted her hand. “I know. Accidents happen. But in the car, we keep our hands and feet in our own space. That helps everyone stay safe.”

Maya nodded. “I will keep my feet in my space.”

The light turned green. The engine purred. Outside, birds chirped, and sunshine painted warm spots on the seat. Leo looked sleepy, but then he saw a picture book in the pocket. He pulled it out and opened it wide, right in Toshi’s face.

“Look!” Leo said. “A big rocket!”

Toshi blinked at the page that covered his eyes. “Leo, I can’t see. That doesn’t feel good for me.”

“Oh.” Leo lowered the book. “Sorry.”

Toshi smiled softly. “Thank you for moving the book. Next time, please ask, ‘Toshi, can I show you?’ Then I can say yes or no.”

Leo tried again. “Toshi, can I show you the rocket?”

“Yes,” said Toshi. “Hold it a little lower.” Leo held it lower, and Toshi looked. “Wow! Whoosh!” They both made rocket sounds. “Whoooooosh!”

Soon the car turned onto a bumpy road. “Bump, bump,” went the tires. The ball rolled out again and bumped Leo’s leg. “Ouch!” he said, making a grumpy face. “I don’t like that ball.”

Toshi took another slow breath. “We have a problem,” he said in his calm voice. “The ball is loose, and it bumps us. That is not safe.”

“What can we do?” Maya asked.

Toshi thought. He listened to the engine hum and felt the seat belt snug. “Let’s make a plan,” he said. “We can use peaceful words. We can ask for help.”

“Dad,” Maya called, “we have a rolling ball problem.”

Dad’s voice was warm. “Thank you for telling me safely. I will stop soon.”

When the car reached a safe place, Dad pulled over. The car got quiet. “Click,” went the buckles as Dad turned around. “What’s the plan?”

Toshi lifted his paw. “Please put the ball in the bag, so it can’t roll. That will keep our bodies safe.”

“That’s a wise plan,” Dad said. He tucked the ball into the snack bag and zipped it. “All set.”

“Thank you,” Leo said. “No more bumps.”

Before they started again, Maya looked at Toshi. “Toshi, can I hold your paw?”

Toshi smiled. “Thank you for asking. Yes, you can hold it for five breaths.”

They held paws and took five slow breaths together. In and out. In and out. The car felt calm and cozy.

As they drove, a siren wailed far away. “Woo-woo.” Toshi’s ears perked up. “I hear a siren,” he said. “Let’s stay quiet so Dad can listen.”

They all grew quiet. Dad pulled over and let the loud fire truck pass. “Great listening,” Dad said. “That keeps helpers safe too.”

At last, they reached the park. “We did it!” Maya cheered.

Toshi nodded. “We kept our seat belts clicked. We kept our hands and feet in our own space. We asked before touching. We used peaceful words and made a plan. That is riding safe.”

Leo took Toshi’s paw again. “Thank you, wise Toshi,” he said. “I learned to ask, to listen, and to keep my body safe.”

Toshi’s eyes sparkled. “And when we keep our bodies safe, we help others feel safe too.”

They all stepped out, stretched in the sunshine, and smiled. The park smelled like grass and fresh air. It was a good day for safe fun.

The End.
