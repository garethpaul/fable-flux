# Jump-Jump Joey and the Laundry Clock

Joey was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a bouncy tail. He loved to jump, jump, jump! One sunny morning, Joey hopped into the laundry room. It smelled warm and soapy, like bubbles and clean towels. The washing machine hummed a gentle song, whirr-rrr, whirr-rrr, and the dryer went tumble-tumble-tumble.

“Today, I will help,” Joey said. “I can be fast like a rocket!”

Mama Roo smiled and patted a fluffy towel. “Helping is wonderful, Joey. We will sort, wash, and fold. We will also use time wisely.”

“Time wisely?” Joey tilted his head. “What does that mean?”

Mama pointed to a round kitchen timer shaped like a big red tomato. It ticked softly, tick-tick-tick. “We will make a plan. A plan helps us stay calm and finish our jobs. When we know what to do and when to do it, we feel good inside.”

Joey’s ears perked up. “A plan! I like plans. Can we make one?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “First, we sort for five minutes. Then, we start the wash. While the washer works, we do a little practice—jumping softly and counting breaths to stay calm. After that, we fold when the dryer is done.”

Joey clapped his tiny paws. “Okay! Five minutes to sort!”

Mama twisted the timer. Tick-tick-tick. “Ready, set, sort!”

Joey sniffed the basket. It smelled like grass and sunshine. He pulled out a blue sock. “Blue goes with blue.” He found a red shirt. “Red goes with red.” He found a soft white towel and rubbed it on his cheek. “So cozy!” The timer ticked. Joey felt excited. He wanted to go fast, but the sock pile was messy. Socks slid and flopped.

“I can’t find the other blue sock,” Joey said, his tail twitching.

“Let’s be calm,” Mama said softly. “Take a belly breath and count to three.”

Joey put his paws on his belly. “Breathe in… one, two, three. Breathe out… one, two, three.” He felt lighter, like a slow bounce on a soft bed.

He looked again. “Aha!” The other blue sock was under a striped shirt. Joey placed the socks together. “I did it!”

Ding! The timer rang. “Sorting time is done,” Mama said. “Great work.”

They started the wash. Joey poured in soap. It smelled like sweet lemon. He pressed the button, and the machine went swish-swish-swish. The round window showed floating clothes, like fish swimming in a pond.

“What do we do now?” Joey asked.

“We practice calm waiting,” Mama said. “The washer takes ten minutes. Let’s set the timer. While we wait, we can do soft jumps and count our breaths. Practice helps our bodies learn.”

Joey nodded. “I will jump gently.” He bent his knees and did quiet hops on the mat. Boing… boing… boing. “One breath in… one breath out,” he whispered. The laundry room felt snug. The light was warm. The air smelled clean and sweet. Joey felt peaceful and proud.

After a few minutes, Joey’s paws got wiggly. He wanted to peek at the washer again. “Is it done yet?”

Mama pointed to the tomato timer. “Let’s check the timer, not the washer. The timer tells us when. That’s time-management, Joey. The timer helps us use time well, so we don’t need to worry.”

Joey nodded. “Timer, please tell me.” Tick-tick-tick.

He practiced more. He lined up clothespins by size—small, medium, big. He sang a tiny song. “Tick-tock, clothes rock, calm breaths on the clock.” He smiled. He was using time with care.

Ding! The washer finished. Joey and Mama moved the wet clothes to the dryer. The clothes were cold and heavy. “Oof! This towel is like a sleepy cloud,” Joey giggled.

Mama set the timer again. “Drying takes ten minutes. What can we do while we wait?”

Joey thought. “We can practice folding with my little blanket.”

“Good idea,” Mama said. “Practice helps us learn.” She showed Joey slow folds. “Corner to corner, smooth and gentle.”

Joey tried. His blanket looked like a wrinkly pancake. He felt a tiny frown coming.

Mama touched his paw. “Let’s be calm. Try again. Corners kiss, hands smooth.”

Joey took a belly breath. In… out… He lifted one corner, then the other. Smooth, smooth. The blanket looked better. “I did it!” he cried.

Ding! The dryer sang a happy tune. Warm air whooshed out, smelling like sunshine tea. Joey and Mama folded towels, shirts, and socks. Joey matched pairs. “Two socks together are friends,” he said.

They made a neat stack. The laundry room looked tidy. Joey felt cozy and proud.

Mama smiled. “You used time wisely today. You made a plan, you stayed calm, and you practiced. How do you feel?”

Joey puffed his chest. “I feel strong and steady. The timer helped me wait. Practice helped me fold. Calm breaths helped my heart.”

Mama hugged him. “That is time-management. We choose what to do first, next, and later, and we keep our bodies calm while we wait.”

Joey wiggled his tail. “Tomorrow, can we use the timer to clean my toys?”

Mama laughed softly. “Yes, we can.”

Joey looked at the tomato timer. “Thank you, timer,” he said. “Tick-tock, we rock!”

And with a happy hop, Joey carried the warm towels down the hall, ready for the next kind and calm adventure.

The End.
