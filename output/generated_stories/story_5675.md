# Paints at the Dinner Table

A gentle deer fawn named Dapple tiptoed into the bright dining room. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. The table smelled like fresh wood and oatmeal. Dapple’s hooves went tap-tap on the floor as she peeked at a big sheet of paper, paints, and soft brushes set neatly on the table.

Mama Deer smiled. “Good morning, Dapple. Today we can paint before breakfast.”

Dapple’s eyes grew wide. “Paint? In the dining room?” Her little tail flicked with joy.

“Yes,” said Mama, “but we will be patient and careful. We take deep breaths. We keep the table tidy. We use our feelings to make pictures.”

Dapple climbed onto a cushioned chair. The chair felt bouncy and soft. She dipped a brush into blue paint. The paint was cool and slippery. “I want to paint the sky,” Dapple whispered.

“Wonderful,” said Mama. “What does your sky feel like?”

Dapple closed her eyes and took a slow, gentle breath. “My sky feels swishy and happy.” She swirled the brush in slow circles. Blue rings bloomed on the paper. Dapple giggled. “Swish, swish!”

Mama laid out carrot sticks on a small plate. Crunchy smells filled the air. “Remember,” Mama said, “creative-expression means sharing your feelings in your own way.”

Dapple nodded. “I can share with colors!”

She tried yellow. “Sunshine!” She dab-dab-dabbed little dots. Then she added green “trees,” but the brush slipped. Splot! A big green blob landed on the white tablecloth.

Dapple froze. Her ears drooped. “Oh no.” She looked at the blob. It was sticky and shiny. “I made a mess.”

Mama took a soft cloth and breathed in, then out. “Let’s be patient,” she said. “Mistakes happen. We can fix it together.”

“I’m sorry,” Dapple whispered. Her voice was small.

“I forgive you,” Mama said kindly. “Can you forgive yourself too?”

Dapple took another slow breath. In… out… The green blob was still there, but her tummy felt less twisty. “I forgive me,” she said, a little braver.

“Good,” Mama said. “Let’s use a damp cloth.” They dabbed the spill. Dab, dab, dab. The cloth felt warm and wet. The blob faded into a little green cloud.

Dapple peered at the cloud. “It looks like a tiny forest puff,” she said. “Maybe it can be part of my picture!”

“That is creative thinking,” Mama said. “You turned a mistake into art.”

Dapple smiled. She dipped into soft brown. “I will paint a friendly tree for the forest puff.” She drew a wiggly trunk. The brush tickled the paper. “He says hello,” Dapple giggled.

“What else does your picture want to say?” asked Mama.

Dapple pressed her hoof gently to her chest. “It wants to say, ‘I am calm. I am kind. I can try again.’” She painted a little heart in the sky. “This is my patient heart.”

They paused to nibble crunchy carrot sticks. Crunch, crunch. The sound was happy and neat. Dapple sipped cool water. The glass clinked softly. “I like painting with snacks,” she said.

“Me too,” Mama laughed. “It is good to care for our bodies while we create.”

Dapple picked a tiny brush for dots. Tap-tap-tap went the brush, like little raindrops. “Listen,” she said, “it sounds like rain.”

“It does,” said Mama. “What does the rain feel like?”

“Soft,” Dapple said. She touched her cheek. “Like a kiss.”

Soon, Dapple wanted to add purple swirls. She dipped too fast. Plop! A drop landed on her nose. It felt cool and tickly.

Dapple wrinkled her nose and giggled. “I am a purple-nose fawn!”

Mama handed her a napkin. “Do you want help?”

“Yes, please,” Dapple said. She waited while Mama gently wiped. The napkin was cozy and dry. “Thank you.”

“You were patient,” Mama said. “You asked for help. That keeps our bodies and our art safe.”

Dapple looked at her picture. Blue sky, yellow dots, green forest puff, friendly tree, purple swirls, and a heart. “It shows my feelings,” she said. “It shows my morning.”

“Would you like to add words?” Mama asked. “We can use our voices too.”

Dapple nodded. She sang softly, “Swish, swish, tap-tap, plop, plop. Try again, don’t stop.” Her voice was sweet and wavy.

Mama clapped softly. “Beautiful. That is creative-expression with sound.”

Dapple took one more breath and added a smiling sun. She signed her name with careful strokes. “All done,” she said.

They cleaned the brushes together. Swoosh went the water. The soap smelled like lemons. The tablecloth had a faint green cloud, but it looked like part of the art now.

Dapple hugged Mama. Her fur felt warm. “Thank you for forgiving me,” she said.

“Thank you for being patient and kind to yourself,” Mama replied. “What did you learn today?”

Dapple thought. “I learned that art can show feelings. I learned to slow down and breathe. I learned that mistakes can become something new. And I learned to say, ‘I’m sorry,’ and ‘I forgive you.’”

Mama smiled. “That is a wonderful morning lesson.”

Dapple beamed. “I will paint again tomorrow.”

“Yes,” said Mama. “We will make space for your creative heart.”

Dapple looked at her picture one more time. It seemed to glow in the sun. “My art is my voice,” she whispered, happy and calm.

The End.
