# Tilly Turtle’s Long Ride Plan

Tilly was a shy little turtle with a smooth green shell. She liked quiet places and soft sounds. Today, Tilly was in the family car. The seats were warm and squishy. The air smelled like oranges from a snack. The car hummed, “Mmmmmm,” like a sleepy bee.

Tilly peeped from her shell. “It is loud,” she whispered to herself. The road made whoosh noises. The wipers went swish-swish on the window. Her heart thumped, “bump, bump, bump.”

Mom buckled Tilly’s seat safe and snug. “We’re going to Grandma’s,” Mom said with a smile. “It’s a long ride, sweet turtle.”

Tilly wanted to be brave, but she felt small. “I don’t like long,” she said softly. Her belly felt fluttery. She tucked her head back inside her shell.

Dad looked in the mirror. “We can make a plan,” he said in a gentle voice. “A plan can help.”

Tilly peeked out again. “A plan?” she asked.

“We’ll do little steps,” Mom said. “Step by step.”

Tilly nodded slowly. She liked small steps. Small steps felt safe.

“What is step one?” Tilly asked.

“Step one: take a slow breath,” Dad said. He breathed in and out. “Smell the oranges.” The car smelled sweet and sunny. Tilly breathed in. In, out. In, out. Her belly felt softer.

“Step two,” Mom said, “pick a job for the ride.”

“A job?” Tilly blinked.

“Your job is to be our Map Helper,” Mom said. She handed Tilly a simple picture map with bright colors. “You can look for things we drew. A red truck. A big tree. A blue sign.”

Tilly’s eyes grew wide. “I can do that.”

The car hummed along. The road went rumble-rumble. Tilly held the map with her small front feet. Her shell warmed in the sun stripe on her seat.

“I see a red truck!” Tilly said. Her voice was small, but proud.

“Great spotting,” Dad said.

“I see a big tree,” Tilly said next. The leaves looked like green hands waving hello.

“Wonderful,” Mom said. “Step three: ask for help when you need it.”

Tilly nodded. But a little while later, the car felt too wiggly. It bumped-bumped over a crack. Tilly pulled into her shell again.

“I feel wobbly,” she said.

“That’s okay,” Mom said softly. “What could help?”

Tilly thought. “Can we make the car quieter?” she asked.

“We can try,” Dad said. He turned the music down low. The song became a soft hum. Mom tucked a small blanket around Tilly like a gentle cloud.

“Step four,” Mom whispered, “use your cozy things.”

Tilly held her tiny stuffed leaf. She rubbed its fuzzy edge. It felt smooth and safe. She peeked out. The window showed gray clouds and silver rain dots. Swish-swish went the wipers. Tap-tap went the rain.

“Rain sounds like little feet,” Tilly said, curious now.

“It does,” Dad said, smiling.

They stopped at a light. The car was still. Tilly’s heart felt steady. “What is step five?” she asked.

“Step five: take turns with time,” Mom said. “We will drive for three songs. Then we will pause and stretch.”

Tilly liked that. She could count songs. One song, two songs, three songs. After three, they rolled into a quiet rest stop. The air outside was cool. It smelled like wet grass.

Tilly stretched her little legs. She touched her shell. “Good job, shell,” she whispered. “We’re doing it.”

Back in the car, the sky brightened. A soft glow came through the windows. Tilly held her map again. She saw a blue sign with a big G.

“G for Grandma!” Tilly said, braver now.

“Almost there,” Dad said.

The car turned onto a small road. Birds chirped. The engine purred. Tilly sat a little taller. She looked out without hiding. She could see the red flowers by the fence. She could hear the gentle crunch of gravel under the tires.

But then—uh-oh—the car slowed down. A long line of cars sat in front of them. A worker in a bright vest held a stop sign.

“We have to wait,” Mom said.

Tilly felt a little squeeze in her tummy. Waiting was hard. “What is our plan for waiting?” she asked, remembering step three.

Dad smiled. “Good asking. Step six: do a peace game.”

“What is a peace game?” Tilly asked.

“We look for kind things,” Mom said. “One kind sound. One kind color. One kind thought.”

Tilly listened. She heard a bird sing, “tweet-tweet.” “Kind sound,” she said.

She looked. She saw a soft yellow flower by the road. “Kind color,” she said.

She thought. “I can be patient,” she told herself. “I can try again and again.”

The worker turned the sign. The line moved. The car rolled forward. Tilly clapped her little feet together. “We did it!”

Soon, the car turned into Grandma’s driveway. The engine went quiet. The door opened. Warm baked-bread smell floated in. Grandma waved with happy eyes.

“Tilly Turtle!” Grandma sang. “You made it!”

Tilly smiled big. “We had a plan,” she said. “I took small steps. I kept trying. I asked for help. I used my cozy things. I waited with peace.”

Grandma hugged Tilly gently. “That is persistence,” she said. “That means you keep going, little by little.”

Tilly felt proud and calm. The long ride had seemed big. But the plan made it small and friendly. She learned that peaceful steps and patient trying could help her finish a hard thing.

That day, Tilly showed Grandma the map and the steps. Grandma nodded. “We can use your plan for many things,” she said. “Small steps, soft breaths, and kind waiting.”

Tilly nuzzled her stuffed leaf and smiled. She was still shy. But she knew how to keep going.

The End.
