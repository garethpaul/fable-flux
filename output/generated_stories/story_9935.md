# Joey's Big Sorry

Little Joey the kangaroo bounced excitedly on Grandma Roo's peaceful front porch. The morning sun felt warm on his soft brown fur, and the sweet smell of honeysuckle flowers danced in the air.

"Boing, boing, boing!" Joey hopped around the wooden porch, his tiny paws patting the smooth boards. He loved visiting Grandma Roo's cozy house with its creaky rocking chair and colorful flower boxes.

Suddenly, Joey spotted something shiny in Grandma's garden. "Ooh, what's that?" he wondered aloud, bouncing closer to investigate.

It was a beautiful blue watering can, sitting next to a patch of bright yellow sunflowers. Without thinking, Joey grabbed the handle with both paws and started to play.

"Wheee!" he giggled, swinging the watering can around and around. Water splashed everywhere - on the flowers, on the porch, and all over Grandma's freshly hung laundry!

"Oh my!" came a gentle voice. Grandma Roo appeared at the door, her kind eyes looking at the wet mess. "Joey, dear, what happened here?"

Joey's ears drooped. His tummy felt funny and twisty. He looked at the dripping clothes and the puddles on the porch. "I... I was just playing," he said in a small voice.

Grandma Roo sat down on her rocking chair and patted her lap. "Come here, little one."

Joey hopped over slowly, his head hanging low. "I made a big mess, didn't I, Grandma?"

"Yes, you did," Grandma Roo said softly, stroking Joey's ears. "But everyone makes mistakes sometimes. Do you know what we do when we make mistakes?"

Joey thought hard, his nose twitching. "Um... hide?"

Grandma Roo chuckled gently. "No, sweetheart. When we make mistakes, we say 'I'm sorry' and we try to help fix things."

"I'm sorry, Grandma," Joey said, looking up with big, honest eyes. "I didn't mean to make such a mess. I just wanted to play with the pretty watering can."

"Thank you for saying sorry, Joey. That takes courage," Grandma Roo smiled. "Now, would you like to help me clean up? We can work together."

Joey's ears perked up. "Yes! I want to help!"

Together, they gathered the wet clothes and hung them back on the line. Joey carefully carried small items while Grandma Roo handled the bigger pieces. The warm breeze would dry them in no time.

"You know, Joey," Grandma Roo said as they worked, "helping others feel better after we've made mistakes is one of the kindest things we can do. It shows we care about people all around the world."

"Even people far away?" Joey asked, amazed.

"Even people far away," Grandma Roo nodded. "Being kind and saying sorry helps make the whole world a happier place."

As they finished cleaning, Joey felt much better. His tummy wasn't twisty anymore, and his heart felt light and happy.

"Grandma, next time I want to play with something, I'll ask first," Joey promised, giving her a big, bouncy hug.

"That's my smart little joey," Grandma Roo laughed, hugging him back. "Now, how about we use that watering can together to give the flowers a proper drink?"

"Yes, please!" Joey bounced with joy.

As they watered the sunflowers together, Joey learned that saying sorry wasn't scary at all. It actually felt wonderful to make things right and help others feel better.

The End.
