# Fawn's Big Sorry

In a cozy nursery filled with soft blankets and colorful toys, lived a gentle deer fawn named Daisy. The nursery smelled like warm milk and lavender, and sunlight streamed through the pretty yellow curtains.

Daisy loved playing with all the baby animals in the nursery. There were fluffy bunny babies, tiny bear cubs, and chirping baby birds. Miss Robin, the kind caregiver, always said, "Remember, little ones, we must be gentle with our friends and follow our nursery rules."

One sunny morning, Daisy felt very excited and bouncy. She wanted to play with the new toy train that sat on the wooden shelf.

"I want the train! I want it now!" Daisy said, stamping her little hooves.

"Wait your turn, Daisy," said Miss Robin gently. "Little Bear is playing with it first."

But Daisy didn't listen. She jumped up high with her strong legs and knocked the train right out of Little Bear's paws. CRASH! The train fell to the soft carpet, and one of its wheels broke off.

Little Bear's eyes filled with tears. "My train!" he cried. "It's broken!"

Daisy looked at the broken toy and felt a heavy, yucky feeling in her tummy. She saw Little Bear crying and noticed how sad all the other baby animals looked. Even Miss Robin shook her head with disappointment.

"Oh no," whispered Daisy. "What did I do?"

Miss Robin knelt down beside Daisy. "How do you think Little Bear feels right now?" she asked softly.

Daisy's ears drooped low. "He feels... sad. And hurt. Because I broke his toy." Her voice was very quiet. "I feel bad in my tummy too."

"That feeling is called being sorry," Miss Robin explained. "When we hurt someone, we feel sorry. But feeling sorry isn't enough. What can you do to make things better?"

Daisy thought hard, her little tail twitching. She looked at crying Little Bear, then at the broken train, then back at Miss Robin.

"I... I could say sorry?" Daisy asked.

"That's a good start," Miss Robin nodded. "What else?"

Daisy walked slowly to Little Bear. "I'm very sorry I broke your train," she said, her voice shaking a little. "I didn't listen to Miss Robin, and I hurt your feelings."

Little Bear sniffled but looked at Daisy.

Then Daisy had an idea. "Miss Robin, can we fix the train together? I want to help make it better."

Miss Robin smiled. "That's a wonderful way to make amends, Daisy."

Daisy and Little Bear sat together on the soft rug. With Miss Robin's help, they carefully glued the wheel back on the train. Daisy let Little Bear hold the glue while she held the wheel steady.

"There!" said Daisy happily. "It's fixed!"

Little Bear smiled big. "Thank you for helping fix it, Daisy. And thank you for saying sorry."

"From now on, I'll listen to the rules and wait my turn," Daisy promised. "Being a good friend is more important than getting toys first."

Miss Robin hugged them both. "Daisy, you showed real leadership today by making things right. When we make mistakes, we can always make amends by saying sorry and helping fix what we broke."

All the baby animals clapped their paws and wings. Daisy felt warm and happy inside, much better than that yucky feeling from before.

From that day on, Daisy always remembered to follow directions and think about her friends' feelings first.

The End.
