# Kippy’s Clever Dinner Dance

Kippy was a joyful kangaroo joey. His tail wiggled when he was happy, and today it wiggled a lot. It was dinner time in the bright dining room. The round table was smooth and warm. The window let in soft golden light. Kippy could smell sweet carrots and warm corn. His tummy said, “Rumble, rumble!”

Mama Roo set down plates. “Dinner is almost ready,” she sang.

Kippy saw a problem. The table was big, and the plates were far away. The napkins were in a stack. The cups were tall and tippy. Kippy wanted to help, but his little paws were busy holding a spoon.

“I want to help! I want to help!” Kippy bounced. His feet went thump-thump on the soft rug.

Mama smiled. “We can take turns,” she said. “Taking turns helps us share. But we also have a little problem to solve. How can we set the table faster without spilling?”

Kippy looked around. He heard the tiny tick-tick of the clock. He smelled the buttery corn. He felt the smooth spoon in his paw. His eyes got bright. “We can make a helper!” he chirped.

“A helper?” asked Mama.

“Yes! An idea helper. An invention!” Kippy whispered, excited. His tail swished. “We can build something to move the napkins and cups.”

Mama nodded. “That sounds like innovation—using new ideas to solve a problem.”

Kippy’s ears perked. He liked that big word. “In-no-vay-shun,” he said slowly. “A new idea helper!”

Kippy studied the dining room. On a chair sat a soft scarf. On the sideboard, there were empty paper rolls. In a basket, there were clothespins. Kippy tapped his chin. “Hmm. What can they be?” He closed one eye, then the other. He listened to his thinking. It sounded like a gentle hum.

“I know!” Kippy clapped. “We can make a table train. It can carry napkins and cups around the table.”

Mama’s eyes twinkled. “Let’s try.”

Kippy and Mama took turns. Kippy picked a paper roll. “My turn,” he said. He slid the scarf through the roll like a tunnel. “Now your turn,” he giggled.

Mama tied one end of the scarf to a wooden spoon. “Choo-choo!” she said.

Kippy laughed. He clipped clothespins along the scarf. “These are car hooks,” he explained. He clipped a napkin to each pin. “Look! They can ride!”

Mama clapped softly. “What about the cups?”

Kippy thought again. He felt the cool cup in his paw and the soft scarf in his other paw. “Cups spill,” he said. “We need cup seats.” He found small rubber bands in the drawer. He looped one around a cup and hooked it under a clothespin. The cup sat snug like a baby in a seat.

“Smart thinking,” Mama said. “Let’s see how your invention works.”

Kippy took the wooden spoon handle and gently pulled the scarf. The “table train” slid around the edge of the round table. Swish. Swish. The napkins fluttered like little flags. The cups hummed a tiny tune against the clothespins. No spills!

“Whoa,” Kippy breathed. “It goes!”

He stopped. “Your turn, Mama,” he said.

Mama pulled the spoon slowly. “Choo-choo,” she whispered. The train visited each seat. Kippy un-clipped a napkin. “Plop.” He set it by a plate. He unhooked a cup. “Clink.” He set it by a fork. They went seat by seat, taking turns pulling and placing.

Kippy felt proud. His chest felt warm. His tail tapped-tapped the chair leg. “We are working together!” he said.

Just then, the scarf snagged on the chair. “Uh-oh,” said Kippy. The train stopped. The clock went tick-tick. The carrots smelled extra sweet now.

Kippy took a deep breath. “We can fix it,” he said. “Problem-solving!” He lifted the scarf and saw the clothespin was turned sideways. He turned it back and smoothed the scarf. “Try again,” he said.

Mama pulled gently. The train moved. The cups stayed steady. “You did it,” she cheered.

Soon, every seat had a napkin and a cup. The table looked happy, like it was smiling. Kippy wiggled. “Our invention works!”

Mama brought the bowl of carrots and the dish of corn. She set them down with a soft thump. “Dinner is served. Thank you, Engineer Kippy.”

Kippy sat tall. “In-no-vay-shun helped us. We used new ideas. We took turns. We solved a problem.”

They ate. The carrots were sweet and soft. The corn popped gently between Kippy’s teeth. The room felt cozy and bright. Kippy’s tummy was glad. His heart felt big.

After dinner, Kippy folded the scarf. He patted the wooden spoon. “Good job, table train,” he said. “We can use you again tomorrow.”

Mama hugged Kippy. “When we notice a problem and try new ideas, we are innovators. When we take turns, we are kind. That makes our home work like a team.”

Kippy nodded. “I can be brave and try ideas,” he said. “I can wait and take turns. That helps everyone.”

He smiled and his tail wiggled one more happy wiggle. “Innovation is fun,” he whispered.

The End.
