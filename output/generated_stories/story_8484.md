# Bun-Bun’s Guest Room Discovery

Bun-Bun was a small, determined rabbit with soft white fur and long, twitchy ears. One sunny morning, Mama Rabbit said, “Bun-Bun, Grandma is coming to stay. We will make the guest room cozy.”

Bun-Bun’s whiskers wiggled. “Guest room? What is a guest room?” he asked, hopping in place. He loved to learn. He loved to ask questions.

Mama smiled. “A guest room is a room for visitors. It should be clean, cozy, and full of welcome.”

Bun-Bun’s eyes grew bright. “I want to help! I want to see! I am curious and careful.” He hopped down the hall and peeked into the guest room. The curtains were pale yellow. The bed was soft and smelled like sunshine. A quilt lay folded at the foot of the bed. The quilt had tiny red tulips, green leaves, and little blue stars. There was a shelf with books, a small rocking chair, and a window that made square patches of light on the floor.

Bun-Bun took a deep sniff. “It smells like soap and lavender,” he said. “The dust is tickly!” He sneezed, “Achoo!” and giggled.

Mama brought a small basket. “Here are clean towels. Here is a dust cloth. Here is a list. We will work together.”

Bun-Bun looked at the list. The words were big and clear. It said: Dust, Make Bed, Put Towels, Place Quilt, Set Books, Welcome Note, Share Tradition.

Bun-Bun traced the words with his paw. “What is ‘Share Tradition’?” he asked.

Mama touched the quilt. “Grandma made this quilt. Our family’s tradition is to tell the story of the quilt to our guests. We share where it came from and why it is special. That is tradition-sharing.”

Bun-Bun’s ears stood tall. “I want to share the story! I will learn it. I am curious!”

He started to dust the shelf. The dust tickled his nose again. “Achoo!” He laughed and said, “Dusting is silly. But I will do it.” He wiped slowly. He hummed a little song. Wipe, wipe, wipe. The shelf looked shiny.

Next, he pulled the quilt up to the pillows. But the quilt hung crooked. One blue star was sideways. Bun-Bun frowned. “Hmm. Something is not right.” He tugged the corner. The quilt bunched up. He tugged again. It bunched more.

“I can solve this,” he said softly, patting the bunched spot. “I will try a new way.” He smoothed one side, then the other. He used both paws. He took a deep breath. “Slow and gentle,” he told himself. At last, the quilt lay flat. The blue star smiled up at him.

Mama nodded. “Good problem-solving, Bun-Bun.”

“Thank you,” said Bun-Bun. He placed clean towels in a neat stack. He set a small vase of clover on the table. The clover smelled sweet and green. He lined up three books: Good Night, Forest Friends; Grandma’s Quilt Stories; and How to Be a Good Neighbor.

“Why that last one?” Bun-Bun asked.

Mama said, “When guests come, we show civic-responsibility. We take care of our home and our community. We welcome people and help them feel safe. That is being a good neighbor.”

Bun-Bun nodded slowly. “We are helpers,” he said. “I like to help.”

He drew a welcome note with a big heart and his name. He stuck it on the pillow. “Welcome, Grandma! Love, Bun-Bun.”

Then he sat in the rocking chair and practiced the quilt story. He held the edge of the quilt and whispered, “Grandma sewed this with kind hands. The red tulips mean love. The green leaves mean growing. The blue stars mean brave nights. This is our family’s gift.”

He rocked back and forth. Creak, creak. The chair felt smooth. The room felt warm and bright. His tummy fluttered with happy nerves.

Later, the doorbell chimed. Ding-dong! Bun-Bun’s paws tapped the floor, tap-tap-tap. Grandma Rabbit stepped in with a soft shawl and a smile that crinkled her eyes.

“Welcome, Grandma!” Bun-Bun cried, hopping. “Come see your room!”

They walked down the hall. The guest room smelled like lavender and clover. Light made the quilt glow. Grandma gasped. “Oh, my! This is so cozy.”

Bun-Bun stood tall. “I helped. I dusted. I made the bed. I set the books. I left a note. I learned the quilt story. May I share our tradition?”

Grandma sat on the bed and patted the spot beside her. “Please do,” she said.

Bun-Bun took a big breath. “This quilt was made by you. The red tulips mean love. The green leaves mean growing. The blue stars mean brave nights. This is our family’s gift.” He looked up, eyes shining. “We are good neighbors, too. We keep our guest room clean and calm. We welcome you with care.”

Grandma’s eyes sparkled. “You are a thoughtful, determined rabbit. Your curiosity helped you learn. Your care helped you serve. I feel so loved.”

Bun-Bun felt warm from nose to tail. “I asked questions. I tried new ways. I kept things tidy. That is organization. That is being a helper,” he said proudly.

They read a book together. The pages rustled. The clover scent drifted. The rocking chair went creak, creak. Grandma tucked the quilt under her paws. “Perfect,” she murmured.

That night, as stars blinked outside, Bun-Bun whispered, “Curiosity helps me learn. Helping makes our home and our town better. Sharing our traditions keeps love alive.”

Mama kissed his head. “Yes,” she said. “Ask, learn, help, and share.”

Bun-Bun smiled. “I will.”

The End.
