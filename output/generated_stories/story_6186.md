# Panda Poppy in the Quiet Attic

Poppy was a patient panda. She had soft black-and-white fur and big gentle eyes. One sunny afternoon, Poppy climbed the creaky stairs to the quiet attic. Creak, creak, creak. She moved slowly. She listened. The attic smelled like old paper and lemon polish. Dust danced in the light like tiny stars.

“I am here to help,” Poppy whispered. “I will be calm. I will be brave.”

The attic was full of boxes and old things. There were hats, buttons, ribbons, and a tiny wooden drum. There were also brushes, paints, and a big roll of paper. Poppy’s ears perked up. She loved to make art. She loved to be careful and kind.

Then Poppy heard a soft tap-tap-tap. Tap. Tap. Tap. She held her breath. “Hello?” she called. Her voice was quiet and calm. “Is someone up here?”

A little voice squeaked, “I’m sorry!” Something rustled behind a box. A small gray mouse peeked out. The mouse’s whiskers trembled. “I am Miri,” she said. “I did not mean to make a mess.”

Poppy looked around. A paint jar had tipped over. Red paint made a shiny puddle. A brush rolled on the floor. A paper hat was soggy and red. Poppy felt a tiny twist in her tummy. She liked things neat. She liked things safe.

Poppy took a deep breath. In. Out. “It’s okay, Miri,” she said in a warm voice. “Thank you for telling me. You are brave to say sorry.”

Miri blinked. “I wanted to paint a picture for my friend,” she whispered. “I wanted it to be a surprise. But the jar was heavy. I did not ask for help.”

Poppy nodded. “I understand. You were excited to make art. That is kind. And it is important to ask for help with heavy things.” She smiled gently. “We can fix this together.”

Miri’s ears stood up. “Together?”

“Yes,” said Poppy. “We will clean. We will be safe. Then we will make your picture. We can be creative and careful.”

Poppy found a cloth and a small bucket. Swish, swish, swish. She wiped the red paint. Miri dabbed tiny drops with a soft napkin. Dab, dab, dab. They hummed a little tune. Hum-hum-hummm. The room smelled like fresh soap now. The floor felt smooth again.

Poppy showed Miri how to twist the lids tight. “This is responsible,” she said. “It keeps the paint inside.”

Miri repeated, “Re-spon-si-ble.” She smiled.

They set out a big sheet of paper. Poppy asked, “What do you want to show in your picture?”

Miri thought. “I want to show understanding,” she said. “I want my friend to know I care.”

Poppy’s eyes shone. “We can paint a listening ear, a gentle heart, and two friends sharing.”

Miri tilted her head. “Can we add a brave star too?”

“Yes,” Poppy said. “Bravery is part of caring.”

They dipped brushes into bright colors. Swish went the blue. Swoop went the yellow. Dot, dot, dot went the white stars. Miri painted a mouse with open ears. Poppy painted a panda with a calm smile. Together they painted two friends holding a tiny broom and a tiny bucket, cleaning up. They laughed softly. The attic felt warm and happy.

Suddenly, a little gust of wind slid in from the window. Whoosh. The paper fluttered. A jar wobbled. Wobble, wobble. Poppy put out her paw. She spoke clearly, “Stop, please.” Her voice was firm but kind. She closed the window latch. Click. “That was me being assertive,” Poppy explained. “I used my strong voice to keep us safe.”

Miri nodded. “Assertive means I can say what I need,” she said. “I can say, ‘Please help,’ or ‘Please stop.’”

“Yes,” Poppy said. “That helps everyone understand.”

They added a golden sun in the corner of the page. The paint smelled a little sweet. The brush felt soft in their paws. Poppy said, “When we listen and speak kindly, we show understanding. When we ask for help, we show responsibility. When we make art, we show creative expression. And when we say what we need, we show bravery and assertiveness.”

Miri’s eyes glittered. “I feel brave,” she said. “I asked for help. I helped clean. I can take care of things.”

Poppy nodded. “I am proud of you.”

They let the painting dry. They cleaned the brushes and put the lids back on. Twist, twist. Click, click. The attic was quiet again. The dust sparkled in the sunlight. It looked like the room was smiling.

Miri hugged Poppy’s paw. “Thank you for understanding,” she said.

Poppy hugged back. “Thank you for telling me the truth. We solved the problem together.”

They held up the finished picture. Two friends smiled up from the page. Above them, in bright letters, it read: “We Listen. We Care. We Are Brave.”

Poppy and Miri carried the painting downstairs, step by careful step. Creak, creak, creak. Poppy felt calm and strong. Miri felt light and proud.

The problem was fixed. The art was made. The lesson was clear: when we understand each other, and use brave, kind words, we can solve problems and make something beautiful.

The End.
