# Nutkin's Kind Heart

Nutkin the squirrel lived in a cozy oak tree next to the Miller family's house. His fluffy gray tail twitched with excitement as he watched snow begin to fall outside. Winter was coming, and he had worked hard all autumn gathering acorns and nuts for the cold months ahead.

One chilly morning, Nutkin heard soft crying sounds coming from the guest room window. He scampered along the tree branch and peeked inside. There, huddled in the corner, was a tiny field mouse named Pip. The little mouse was shivering and looked very hungry.

"Oh my!" squeaked Nutkin. "Are you okay, little friend?"

Pip looked up with big, watery eyes. "I'm so cold and hungry," she whispered. "I couldn't find enough food before winter came. The big cats outside scared me, so I hid in here, but I don't belong in this house."

Nutkin's whiskers twitched thoughtfully. He had plenty of food stored away, but he had worked so hard to collect it all. What if winter lasted longer than usual? What if he ran out?

But looking at poor Pip, Nutkin felt his heart grow warm despite the cold air. "Wait right there," he said kindly. "I'll be back soon!"

Nutkin scurried down to his tree hollow and gathered some of his finest acorns and hazelnuts. The nuts felt smooth and cool in his tiny paws. He could smell their rich, earthy scent. These were some of his very best ones!

He climbed back up and squeezed through a small gap in the window frame. "Here you go, Pip," he said, placing the nuts gently beside her. "These will help you feel better."

Pip's eyes grew wide with wonder. "But these are yours! You worked so hard to gather them. I can't take your food!"

"Food tastes much better when it's shared with a friend," Nutkin said with a warm smile. "Besides, I have plenty more. My mama always taught me that helping others makes our hearts feel full too."

As Pip nibbled on the sweet nuts, color returned to her cheeks. "These taste wonderful!" she said gratefully. "You're so kind, Nutkin. But I still don't know where I'll live. This guest room isn't really my home."

Nutkin's tail fluffed up with another idea. "I know the perfect place! There's a warm space under the garden shed where Mrs. Miller puts out seeds for the birds. You could make a cozy home there, and there's always food nearby!"

Together, they carefully made their way outside. Nutkin showed Pip the safe, dry space under the shed. It was protected from wind and rain, with soft leaves perfect for nesting.

"This is perfect!" Pip exclaimed, her whiskers twitching happily. "Thank you so much, Nutkin. You've been so generous and kind to me, even though we just met."

From that day forward, Nutkin and Pip became the very best of friends. Every morning, Nutkin would visit Pip and share breakfast with her. Sometimes other small animals would join them too. Nutkin learned that when he shared his food and showed kindness to others, he felt happier than when he kept everything to himself.

As spring arrived and new nuts began to grow, Nutkin realized something wonderful: his generous heart had made him the richest squirrel in the whole neighborhood, not because of what he had, but because of the friends he'd made by caring for others.

The End.
