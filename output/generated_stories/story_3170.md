# Melody's Safe Pantry Adventure

Melody was a bright yellow canary who loved to sing. She lived in a cozy cage near the kitchen window. Every morning, she would chirp happy songs while watching the family get ready for their day.

"Tweet-tweet-tweet! Good morning, sunshine!" Melody sang as golden light streamed through the window.

One day, the pantry door was left open. Melody had never seen inside before. She could smell wonderful things - sweet honey, fresh bread, and crunchy seeds.

"Oh my! What's in there?" Melody wondered. Her cage door was open too for her morning flight time.

Melody flew toward the pantry. Inside, she saw tall shelves filled with colorful boxes and jars. Everything sparkled in the dim light.

"This looks like a treasure cave!" she chirped excitedly.

But as Melody flew deeper into the pantry, she noticed something important. There were many things that could be dangerous for a little bird like her.

She saw a bottle with a bright red warning label. "That looks scary," she said, staying far away.

There was a box of soap that smelled very strong. "Achoo! That makes my nose tickle in a bad way," Melody said, flying higher.

Then she spotted a bag of rice that had spilled on the floor. "Those tiny pieces could make me choke," she realized.

Melody remembered what her bird family had taught her: "Always be careful in new places. Look for dangers before you explore."

She decided to be very smart and safe. Instead of landing on dangerous things, Melody perched on a high shelf with bird-safe items.

"I can explore safely from up here," she sang proudly.

From her safe spot, Melody could see a small bag of sunflower seeds. "Those are perfect for birds like me!" she chirped.

She also noticed that some food containers were left open. "I should tell the family about this," Melody thought. "Open food can attract bugs and mice. That's not good for anyone!"

Melody flew back to the kitchen and began singing a special warning song. "Tweet-tweet-danger! Tweet-tweet-help!"

Mom heard Melody's urgent song and came to see what was wrong.

"What is it, Melody?" Mom asked gently.

Melody flew toward the pantry and back again, singing loudly.

"Oh! You're trying to tell me about the pantry!" Mom said. "You're such a smart bird!"

Mom looked in the pantry and saw the spilled rice and open containers. "Thank you for warning me, Melody. You helped keep our home safe and clean."

Together, they cleaned up the spilled rice. Mom sealed the open containers to keep the food fresh and pest-free.

"You know what, Melody?" Mom said. "You can have a few of these sunflower seeds as a reward for being so helpful and safe."

Melody ate her treats happily. She felt proud that she had been careful and had helped her family too.

From that day on, Melody made it her special job to check the pantry during her morning flights. She always stayed on safe, high perches and sang warnings if she saw anything that needed attention.

"Being safe and helpful makes me the happiest bird in the world!" Melody sang every morning.

And the whole family loved their clever, caring canary who helped keep their home safe and tidy.

The End.
