# Benny Bunny Says Thank You

Benny Bunny was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright button eyes. He loved to hop, hop, hop! But today was different. Today, Benny had a big problem to solve in a very busy place—the bathroom.

The bathroom was warm and steamy from the shower Mama Bunny took. The mirror was foggy, and Benny could draw happy faces with his paw. The soap smelled like oranges. “Mmm,” Benny sniffed. “So clean!”

Benny looked at his toothbrush. It was blue with a little carrot picture. “Today, I will brush all by myself,” he said. “I can do it!”

He hopped up on his step stool. “Step. Step.” He reached for the toothpaste. “Squeeze… oh!” A big, squiggly blob plopped onto the sink. “Oops,” Benny whispered. His little whiskers drooped. The blob looked like a toothpaste mountain!

Benny felt a wibble in his tummy. He wanted to hide. He looked at the towel. He looked at the trash can. “Maybe I can wipe it away,” he said. But then he stopped. He remembered Mama’s words: “We tell the truth. We are honest, even when it is hard.”

Benny took a deep breath. He could taste the minty air. “I will be honest,” he said.

“Mama?” Benny called. “I need help.”

Mama Bunny peeked in with a warm smile. “What’s the matter, my determined little hopper?”

Benny pointed to the toothpaste mountain. “I squeezed too much. I made a mess. I am sorry, Mama.”

Mama Bunny knelt down. She was not mad. She hugged Benny, and he felt her soft fur and smelled her sweet tea smell. “Thank you for telling the truth,” she said. “That was very brave and honest.”

Benny’s ears lifted. “It was brave?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “You asked for help. That is brave. And honest.” She handed Benny a cloth. “Let’s clean together.”

They wiped the sink with little circles. “Swish, swish.” The water made a cheerful drip-drop sound. The soap bubbles popped like tiny stars. “Pop! Pop!”

When the sink was clean, Mama showed Benny how to make a tiny toothpaste dot. “Like a pea,” she said. “Just a dot.”

“A pea dot,” Benny repeated, giggling.

“Now brush up and down, round and round,” Mama sang. “Two little front teeth, two little back teeth!”

Benny brushed. The minty taste was cool and tingly. “It feels like snow on my tongue,” he said.

When he finished, he smiled at his shiny teeth in the mirror. “I did it!”

Mama touched his ear. “You took care of your body. That is self-care. I am proud of you.”

Benny’s heart felt warm, like a cozy blanket. He wanted to do something kind. He looked around the bathroom and had an idea. “Mama, can I help the toothbrushes?” he asked.

“The toothbrushes?” Mama laughed softly.

“Yes! I can share,” Benny said. He lined up the family toothbrushes—Mama’s green one, Papa’s brown one, and Baby Bun’s yellow one—on a clean towel. He made sure each one had a tiny pea dot of toothpaste for bedtime later. “A gift for my family,” he said. “So they can brush with a smile!”

“That is generous,” Mama said. “You are sharing your time and helping your family.”

Benny felt proud, but gentle proud, like a soft pat. He thought about Grandma, who loved stories. “I want to tell her what I learned,” he said. “Story time?”

Mama nodded. “Let’s hear your story.”

Benny hopped to the mirror and looked at his reflection. He spoke in a storyteller voice, clear and small. “Once upon a bathroom, a determined rabbit tried to brush. He squeezed too much. The sink said, ‘Glop!’ He felt wobbly. But he told the truth. He said, ‘I am sorry.’ His mama helped. They cleaned. He learned a pea dot. He shared toothpaste for everyone. He felt grateful. The end.”

Mama clapped softly. “What are you grateful for, Benny?”

Benny pressed his paws together and thought. He listened to the drip-drop, smelled the orange soap, and felt the warm air. “I am grateful for you helping me,” he said. “I am grateful for my toothbrush friend. I am grateful for water, and soap, and towels, and my step stool. They help me take care of me.”

Mama’s eyes twinkled. “That’s a beautiful list.”

Benny added one more. “I am grateful for my brave heart.”

“That makes me grateful too,” Mama said. “Thank you for being honest and generous.”

Benny hugged Mama’s leg. “Thank you for loving me.”

That night, when it was time to brush again, Papa Bunny smiled at the tiny pea dots. “Who left these little gifts?” he asked.

Benny hopped. “I did! I wanted to share.”

Papa rubbed Benny’s ears. “What a kind and thoughtful rabbit.”

Benny brushed his teeth, round and round, up and down. The bathroom felt friendly. The mirror was clear. The soap smelled bright and sweet. Benny whispered, “Thank you,” to each helpful thing—the water, the towel, the light, the tiny pea dot. He felt full of happy sparkles.

He went to bed with a clean mouth, an honest heart, and a grateful smile. He knew that problems could be solved with truth, help, and kindness. And he knew that saying thank you made the whole world feel warm.

The End.
