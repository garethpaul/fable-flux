# Benny the Beaver's Quiet Discovery

High up in the quiet attic of an old farmhouse, Benny the beaver was very busy. *Tap, tap, tap!* His big orange teeth chewed on wooden beams all day long. The dusty sunlight streamed through the small window, making everything look golden and warm.

"I'm the best builder in the whole world!" Benny said proudly, his fluffy tail slapping against the wooden floor. *Thump, thump!* "Look at all the wood chips I can make!"

But down below in the house, something was wrong. Benny heard a tiny sound. *Sniff, sniff.* Was someone crying?

"That's not my problem," Benny said, continuing his loud chewing. "I'm too busy building!" *Crunch, crunch, crunch!*

The crying got a little louder. Benny's round ears twitched. He stopped chewing for just a moment and listened carefully. The attic felt very quiet and still.

"Hello?" called a small, shaky voice from downstairs. "Is someone up there making all that noise?"

Benny peeked his brown furry head through a crack in the attic floor. Below, he saw a little girl sitting on her bed, holding a teddy bear tight. Her eyes were red and watery.

"My name is Emma," the little girl said, looking up. "I can't sleep because of all the loud sounds. I'm scared and sad because we just moved to this new house."

Benny felt something funny in his chest - like a warm, gentle feeling. He had never thought about how his loud building might make others feel.

"Oh," Benny whispered softly. "I didn't know I was keeping you awake. I'm sorry."

Emma wiped her nose with her sleeve. "I miss my old room. Everything here feels different and scary."

Benny's heart felt heavy. He remembered when he first came to this attic - how lonely and scared he had felt too. The feeling in his chest grew warmer and stronger.

"I know how you feel," Benny said gently, climbing down from the attic. His wet nose twitched as he smelled the new paint and unfamiliar scents. "When I first moved here, I felt scared too."

Emma's eyes grew wide. "Really? But you seem so confident and busy!"

"I was scared," Benny nodded. "So I started building and chewing to feel better. But I didn't think about how loud I was being."

Benny sat quietly next to Emma's bed. "What if I help you feel better about your new home? And I promise to be much quieter when you need to sleep."

Emma smiled for the first time. "Would you really do that?"

"Of course!" Benny's tail wiggled happily. "Tomorrow, I'll build you a special little wooden toy - very quietly! And maybe I can show you all the cozy corners of this house."

"Thank you, Benny," Emma said, hugging her teddy bear. "You're very kind."

From that day on, Benny learned to listen with his heart. He built quietly during Emma's nap time and bedtime. When Emma felt lonely, Benny would tell her funny stories about his building adventures.

Benny discovered that thinking about others' feelings made him feel even better than building the tallest wood chip pile. He was still a busy beaver, but now he was also a caring friend.

"Being busy is good," Benny said to himself each morning, "but being kind is even better."

The End.
