# Ellie's World Helper Mission

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved her special closet hideaway. It was cozy and warm, with soft blankets and her favorite stuffed animals. The closet smelled like lavender from Mama's clothes, and sunlight peeked through the crack under the door.

One morning, Ellie sat in her hideaway feeling sad. She had heard Mama and Papa talking about children around the world who needed help. Some didn't have clean water, others needed books to learn, and some were hungry.

"I'm just a little elephant," Ellie whispered to her teddy bear. "How can I help children so far away?"

Her teddy bear seemed to smile back at her, as if saying, "You can do more than you think!"

Ellie's trunk drooped as she thought hard. Then she remembered something Papa had taught her: "Every big job starts with one small step."

"That's it!" Ellie trumpeted softly. "I'll make a plan!"

She gathered her crayons and paper from the closet shelf. The crayons felt smooth and waxy in her trunk as she began to draw. First, she drew a picture of children holding hands around the world. The paper crinkled as she worked.

"Step one," Ellie said aloud, "learn about other countries." She drew a big book with colorful flags.

"Step two," she continued, her little trunk moving the crayon carefully, "collect things to help." She drew toys, books, and coins.

"Step three," Ellie added with growing excitement, "ask grown-ups to help me send them!"

Just then, Mama's gentle voice called from outside the closet. "Ellie, sweetie, what are you doing in there?"

"I'm making a world helper plan!" Ellie called back, her voice full of pride.

Mama opened the closet door, and warm light spilled in. Her kind eyes sparkled when she saw Ellie's drawings.

"Oh my!" Mama said, sitting down beside Ellie. "Tell me about your plan."

Ellie's ears flapped with excitement as she explained each step. "And then we can send care packages to children who need them! Even though I'm little, I can still help!"

Mama's trunk gently touched Ellie's forehead. "You know what, little one? You're absolutely right. Being a good world citizen means caring about others everywhere, not just in our own backyard."

"Really?" Ellie's eyes grew wide and bright.

"Really," Mama nodded. "And I know just how we can start. Grandma Elephant runs a charity that sends school supplies to children around the world. We can help her!"

Ellie jumped up and down, making the closet floor creak with joy. "Can we start today? Can we, can we?"

"We certainly can," Mama laughed. "First, let's gather some of your books and toys that you've outgrown. Then we'll visit Grandma Elephant together."

Ellie carefully folded her plan and tucked it in her pocket. As she and Mama began collecting items from her toy box, Ellie felt warm and happy inside.

"Mama," Ellie said thoughtfully, "I learned something important today."

"What's that, sweetheart?"

"Even little elephants can make a big difference in the world when they care about others and work hard to help!"

Mama smiled proudly. "That's the most important lesson of all, Ellie. You're becoming a wonderful world citizen."

From that day on, Ellie's closet hideaway became her planning headquarters for helping children around the world. And every time she completed a helper mission, she felt her heart grow a little bigger with love for all the children everywhere.

The End.
