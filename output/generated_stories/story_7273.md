# Bunny Beats and Tidy Feet

Thump-thump! Little Rilla Rabbit hopped into the musical practice room with a bright smile. Rilla was a determined rabbit. She loved dance. She loved music. She loved to learn. Today, she wanted to practice a new twirl.

The room smelled like lemon polish. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Drums sat in a row. Shiny cymbals blinked. A small piano waited quietly. But the floor was messy. Scarves were crumpled. Sticks and shakers were under the chairs. A squeaky tambourine rolled in circles.

Rilla wiggled her nose. “I want to dance now!” she said.

Her friend, Mr. Tempo the Turtle, peeked in. He wore tiny glasses and a kind smile. “Hello, Rilla,” he said softly. “Are you ready to practice your twirl and tap?”

“Yes!” said Rilla. She stomped her feet. Tap-tap!

Mr. Tempo looked around. “Hmm,” he hummed. “The room is not ready. Our first job is our chores.”

Rilla’s ears drooped. “Chores? But I want music now.”

Mr. Tempo nodded. “Chores help the music come out to play. A tidy room is a happy room. And a happy room helps your dance feet. We can make it fun.”

Rilla took a deep breath. She was determined. “Okay,” she said. “Let’s try.”

Mr. Tempo clapped a slow beat. Clap… clap… clap. “We will tidy to the music,” he said. “Chore Beat!”

Rilla giggled. “Chore Beat!”

They started with the scarves. Swish, swish! Rilla folded the blue one like a small cloud. She folded the red one like a warm sunset. “Fold, fold, fold,” she sang. Her paws felt soft fabric. It smelled like fresh soap.

“Good folding,” said Mr. Tempo. “Listen to the rhythm. Fold on the beat.”

Clap… clap… clap.

Rilla matched the sound. Fold… fold… fold. Her ears perked up. “This is fun!” she said.

Next, they picked up the shakers. Rilla shook one. Chika-chika! The beads made a happy sound. She put the shakers in a basket. “Shake, then away,” she sang. “Shake, then away!”

Mr. Tempo smiled. “You made a tidy song.”

Rilla hopped to the tambourine. It rolled away. “Come back, circle friend!” she laughed, chasing it. Her feet pattered. Pitter-pat! She caught it and dusted it with a soft cloth. Dust sparkled in the sun like tiny stars.

“Now the drumsticks,” said Mr. Tempo. Tap, tap, tap, he set them neatly in a cup. Rilla followed. Tap, tap, tap. “It sounds like little raindrops,” she said.

Soon the floor was clear. The room looked bright. The piano smiled with shiny keys. The air felt calm and happy.

Rilla stood tall. “We did it! The chores are done.”

Mr. Tempo nodded. “How do you feel?”

Rilla wiggled her toes. “I feel proud. My body feels ready. My mind feels ready too.”

Mr. Tempo said, “That’s the magic of chores. They help us care, they help us share, and they help us prepare.”

Rilla clapped. “Care, share, prepare!”

“Now,” said Mr. Tempo, “let the music begin.”

He played three gentle notes on the piano. Plink, plonk, plink. The sound was sweet like honey. Rilla’s feet started to move. She made a small hop, then a bigger hop. Hop… hop!

“Add a twirl,” said Mr. Tempo.

Rilla twirled. Swirl! Her ears flew like ribbons. She added a tap-tap with her toes. Tap-tap! The floor was smooth under her feet. The air smelled like lemons and a little like rain from the open window.

“Try the Chore Beat Twirl,” said Mr. Tempo. “Fold, fold, fold—twirl!”

Rilla laughed. She danced her tidy dance:
- Fold, fold, fold—twirl!
- Shake, then away—tap-tap!
- Tap, tap, tap—big smile!

The music grew. Plink, plonk, plink-plonk! Mr. Tempo added a soft drum. Boom… boom… boom. Rilla swirled and hopped and tapped. Her heart felt light and brave.

She paused and listened. Her breath went in and out. In… out… in… out. “Mr. Tempo,” she said, “doing chores made space for my dance. I did my best because the room felt good.”

Mr. Tempo nodded kindly. “Yes, Rilla. Chores are care in action. When we clean up, we show love for our things, our friends, and ourselves. Music and dance grow well in a clean space.”

Rilla placed her paw on her chest. “I can do my chores every day. I can make a Chore Beat at home too!”

“What will your Chore Beat be?” asked Mr. Tempo.

Rilla thought. “Make my bed—pat, pat, pat. Put toys away—plop! Wipe the table—swish! Then I can dance and play.”

Mr. Tempo clapped. “Wonderful plan.”

Rilla did one last big twirl. Swirl-whoosh! She landed with a soft tap. Tap!

“I did it,” she said. “I learned my new twirl. And I learned that chores help me dance.”

Mr. Tempo bowed. “You did it, determined Rilla.”

Rilla smiled wide. “Care, share, prepare,” she whispered. The room felt warm and bright. The music felt close, like a friendly hug.

The End.
