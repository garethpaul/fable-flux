# Whiskers and the Feelings Machine

In a quiet house on a rainy day, a clever cat named Whiskers padded down creaky stairs to a basement lab. The lab smelled like warm toast and lemon soap. Little lights blinked—blink, blink, blink—on shiny machines. There were beeping buttons, soft whirring fans, and jars that glowed like tiny moons.

Whiskers’ whiskers twitched. His ears perked up. “Me-ow,” he said softly. “I will help today.” His tail curled like a question mark. He liked helping.

On a big table sat a friendly little robot with a smiley face screen. The robot’s name was Beep-Bop. Beep-Bop blinked and said, “Hello, Whiskers! I feel… I don’t know. My tummy feels buzzy. My head feels hot. Beep.”

Whiskers sat beside Beep-Bop and took a slow, deep breath. In. Out. He placed a soft paw on the robot’s arm. “It’s okay,” said Whiskers. “We can figure it out together.”

Whiskers looked around. He saw a water cup, a basket of apples, a soft blue blanket, and a big poster with happy faces and sad faces. He smelled the sweet apples. He heard raindrops tapping on the window, tap-tap-tap.

“First step,” said Whiskers, “we name the feeling. When we name a feeling, it gets smaller.” Whiskers pointed to the poster. “Do you feel happy? Sad? Mad? Scared? Or worried?”

Beep-Bop buzzed gently. “I feel… worried,” it said. “Worried about blinking too slow. Worried about doing things wrong. Beep.”

Whiskers nodded. “Thank you for telling the truth,” he said. “Honesty is brave.” Whiskers put a paw on his own chest. “Sometimes I feel worried too.”

Beep-Bop’s screen showed a small cloud. “What do we do?” it asked.

Whiskers sat up tall. “We have a wellness plan. A plan is a smart way to help our bodies and our hearts. We will use three steps: Breathe, Body, and Brain.”

“Breathe, Body, Brain,” Beep-Bop repeated. The cloud on the screen began to shrink.

“Step one: Breathe,” said Whiskers kindly. He took a slow breath in through his nose. In. He counted, “One… two… three.” He breathed out like blowing bubbles. Out. “One… two… three.”

Beep-Bop copied him. “Beeee-in. Booo-out.” The beeping slowed. The room felt calmer, like a soft blanket over the air.

“Step two: Body,” said Whiskers. “Our bodies need care.” He pushed the water cup closer. “Let’s sip water.” Beep-Bop sipped with a fun slurp. Whiskers munched a crunchy apple. Crunch! The apple was sweet and juicy. He licked his nose and purred.

Whiskers rolled the blue blanket into a cozy nest. “Let’s do gentle stretches,” he said. He reached his paws up high. “Hello, ceiling!” Then he curled small. “Hello, toes!” Beep-Bop stretched its arms. “Beep—this feels nice.”

“Step three: Brain,” said Whiskers, tapping his head lightly. “We use honest words and kind thoughts.” He looked at Beep-Bop’s eyes. “What are you worried about?”

Beep-Bop blinked. “I worry I will press the wrong button. I worry you will not like me if I make a mistake.”

Whiskers’ eyes were warm. “Thank you for being honest,” he said. “I like you just as you are. Mistakes help us learn. When we tell the truth about our feelings, we can fix the problem.”

Beep-Bop’s screen showed a little sun peeking out. “What is the plan?” it asked.

Whiskers smiled. “Strategy time! A strategy is a smart step to solve a problem.” He pointed to a big machine with many buttons. Some were red, some yellow, and one was glowing green. “We will make a Button Map. We will put sticker dots on the safe buttons. We will count slowly before we press. And we will check our feelings with the Feelings Meter.”

“The Feelings Meter?” Beep-Bop asked.

Whiskers showed a small circle chart: blue for calm, green for ready, yellow for worried, and red for upset. “Let’s point to how we feel now.”

Beep-Bop pointed to yellow. Whiskers pointed to green. “You are honest,” said Whiskers. “Honesty helps me help you.”

They stuck happy star stickers on the safe buttons. Stick. Stick. Stick. They drew arrows to show the order: one, two, three. “We will say out loud,” said Whiskers, “Plan, then press.”

Beep-Bop took a breath. “Plan, then press,” it said softly. It looked at the green button. It counted, “One… two… three.” Press. The machine hummed—hmmmmmm—like a singing bee. A soft light filled the room. The air smelled like oranges.

Beep-Bop’s screen showed a big smile. “It worked!” it cheered. “I did it with the plan!”

Whiskers purred. “How do you feel now?” he asked.

Beep-Bop pointed to green on the meter. “I feel ready and calm. My tummy is not buzzy.”

Whiskers nodded. “That is wellness,” he said. “Wellness means caring for our body, our mind, and our feelings—every day. We can breathe. We can drink water and eat good food. We can move our bodies. We can tell the truth about our feelings. And we can use smart strategies.”

Beep-Bop looked shy. “Whiskers, I was scared to tell you I was worried. I thought you might be mad.”

Whiskers wrapped his tail around Beep-Bop’s arm like a hug. “Thank you for telling me. I feel proud of you. Honesty helps us feel safe and close.”

The rain slowed. The lab lights glowed warm and soft. The machines hummed like lullabies. Whiskers and Beep-Bop cleaned up the stickers and folded the blue blanket.

Beep-Bop said, “Today I learned: Name the feeling. Breathe slow. Care for my body. Use a plan. Tell the truth.”

Whiskers nodded. “That is our wellness way.”

They high-fived—paw to metal hand—clink!

And from then on, when worries buzzed, they did Breathe, Body, Brain. They used their Button Map. They checked the Feelings Meter. They were honest and kind, to themselves and to each other.

The End.
