# Splash the Dolphin's Special Gift

Splash the dolphin lived in a magical world where sea creatures could visit land whenever they wanted. One sunny morning, Splash swam up to visit his friend Emma, who was sitting quietly on her peaceful front porch.

"Good morning, Emma!" Splash called out cheerfully, his gray skin glistening in the warm sunlight. "What are you doing?"

Emma looked up from her lap with a sad face. "Oh, hi Splash. I'm trying to make a birthday card for my grandma, but I can't think of anything special to put on it."

Splash noticed the colorful papers, crayons, and stickers scattered around Emma's feet on the wooden porch. He could smell the sweet scent of the morning flowers blooming nearby and hear the gentle chirping of birds in the trees.

"May I help you?" Splash asked kindly, settling himself comfortably next to the porch steps. "Sometimes when I slow down and really think about someone I love, special ideas come to me."

Emma's eyes brightened. "Really? How do you do that?"

"Well," Splash said thoughtfully, "first, I close my eyes and take three deep breaths. Then I think about all the wonderful things that make that person special."

Emma closed her eyes and breathed in slowly. "I can smell grandma's cookies baking," she whispered.

"That's perfect!" Splash encouraged. "What else do you remember about your grandma?"

"She always gives the best hugs," Emma said, opening her eyes with a smile. "And she teaches me how to garden. Oh! And she sings silly songs that make me laugh."

Splash clapped his flippers together excitedly. "Those are beautiful memories! Now, what if we made a card that shows all those special things?"

Emma picked up a piece of blue paper. "We could draw a cookie here!"

"And maybe some flowers for the garden," Splash suggested, watching as Emma carefully drew with her yellow crayon.

As they worked together, Splash helped Emma think of kind words to write inside. "When we take time to really think about someone," Splash explained, "we can create something that comes straight from our hearts."

Emma drew little hearts around the edges and wrote "I LOVE YOU GRANDMA" in her best letters. Splash showed her how to make the letters extra special by coloring them in rainbow colors.

"This is the most beautiful card I've ever seen," Splash said warmly. "Your grandma will feel so loved when she sees how much thought and care you put into making it."

Emma held up the finished card, her face glowing with pride. The card was covered with drawings of cookies, flowers, hearts, and even a little dolphin that looked just like Splash.

"Thank you for helping me, Splash," Emma said, giving her friend a gentle hug. "You taught me that when I slow down and think with my heart, I can make something really special."

Splash smiled his biggest dolphin smile. "That's right, Emma. Being kind means taking time to think about what would make others happy. And the most wonderful gifts are the ones we make with love."

As Splash swam away, Emma sat peacefully on her porch, holding her grandmother's card and feeling proud of the kindness in her heart.

The End.
