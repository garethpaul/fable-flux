# Polly's Laundry Day Helper

Polly the parrot loved bright colors and shiny things. Her feathers were green and blue, and she could say many words. Today, she heard the washing machine going "whoosh-whoosh" in the laundry room.

"What's that sound?" Polly asked herself, flapping her colorful wings.

She flew into the warm laundry room where Mom was folding clothes. The room smelled like fresh soap and sunshine. Polly saw piles of colorful shirts, soft towels, and tiny socks everywhere.

"Hello, Polly!" said Mom with a smile. "I have so much laundry to fold today. Would you like to help me?"

"Help! Help!" squawked Polly excitedly. She loved helping with anything colorful.

Mom showed Polly how to match the socks. "Put the blue sock with the blue sock, and the red sock with the red sock," she explained gently.

Polly tried her best. She picked up a yellow sock with her beak and looked for its match. But it was tricky! The sock kept slipping, and she accidentally dropped it in the wrong pile.

"Oops!" said Polly, feeling sad. "Sorry, sorry!"

"It's okay, Polly," said Mom kindly. "Everyone makes mistakes when they're learning. Let's try again together."

Mom held one sock while Polly carefully picked up its match. "We make a great team!" said Mom. "You find the colors, and I'll help you place them."

Polly felt happy again. Working together was much easier! She found a tiny pink sock and chirped, "Pink! Pink!" Mom helped her put it with its partner.

Then Dad came in carrying a basket of wet clothes. "Oh no," he said, looking tired. "The dryer is full, and I have nowhere to hang these clothes."

Polly had a creative idea! "Help! Help!" she squawked, flying to the empty clothesline.

"Polly wants to help hang clothes too!" said Mom.

Dad smiled. "What a smart bird! But I don't think she can hold the clothespins."

Polly felt a little hurt. She wanted to help so badly! But then she had another idea. She could pass the clothespins to Dad!

"Watch this!" chirped Polly. She carefully picked up a clothespin in her beak and flew it over to Dad.

"Wow, Polly! You're amazing!" said Dad, taking the clothespin. "This is so helpful!"

Now all three of them worked together. Mom sorted the clothes by color, Polly delivered clothespins, and Dad hung everything up. The laundry room was full of happy sounds - the gentle "drip-drop" of water, the "snap" of clothespins, and Polly's cheerful chirping.

"Red shirt! Blue towel! Yellow socks!" Polly announced each item proudly.

When they accidentally dropped some clothes, they all helped pick them up together. When Polly got tired, Mom and Dad encouraged her to keep trying.

"Teamwork makes everything easier and more fun," said Dad, patting Polly's soft feathers.

Soon, all the clothes were clean, folded, and hung up to dry. The laundry room looked neat and tidy. It smelled wonderful, like fresh flowers and clean cotton.

"Thank you, Polly," said Mom, giving her a gentle hug. "We couldn't have done this without you. You're the best laundry helper ever!"

"Team! Team!" squawked Polly happily, spreading her beautiful wings wide.

From that day on, Polly always helped with laundry day. She learned that working together, practicing new things, and forgiving mistakes made every job easier and much more fun.

The End.
