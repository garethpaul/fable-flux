# Miko Mouse Tells the Truth

In a sunny garden, a small wooden shed sat by a row of bright marigolds. Inside the shed, it smelled like fresh soil and sweet straw. Tools hung on hooks. A green watering can rested by the door. Little Miko Mouse lived there, in a cozy nest of leaves. Miko was a kind mouse with bright eyes and soft gray fur.

Each morning, Miko squeaked, “Good morning, garden!” and stretched his tiny paws. He had a plan for the day. He would water the flowers, sweep the shed floor, and bring a shiny berry to share with his friend, Bella Beetle. He looked at the sun peeking over the fence. “I will do my jobs before snack time,” Miko said. He tapped a pebble clock he had made. “First water, then sweep, then share.”

Miko pushed the green watering can. It made a little shhh sound as it slid over the wood. “Heavy!” he puffed, but he smiled. Outside, the air was warm. Birds chirped. The dirt smelled like rain. Miko tipped the can and gave each flower a drink. Drip, drip, drip.

Just then, he saw something shiny in the corner of the shed. It was Mr. Robin’s red button. Mr. Robin loved that button. He had found it long ago and kept it in a small tin on a shelf. “Oh!” Miko said. “It must have fallen.”

Miko reached up, stood on his tippy toes, and tried to put it back. His paw slipped. CLANG! A small trowel fell from the hook. A clay pot wobbled. “No, no—” Miko tried to stop it, but the pot tipped over and broke with a crack.

Miko’s heart thumped. His ears got hot. “Oh no,” he squeaked. “I broke the pot!”

He felt a big bubble of mad feelings rise inside. “Why are my paws so slippery?” he said. His tail flicked. He wanted to stomp. He wanted to shout. He grabbed his whiskers and took a big breath in. Then he let it out slow. “Hoo… shhh.” He did it again. “Hoo… shhh.” His belly felt softer. His face cooled.

“I can choose,” Miko whispered. “I can tell the truth, or I can hide.” He looked at the door. He looked at the broken pot. He remembered the pebble clock. It was time to sweep next. He decided.

“I will be honest,” Miko said. “I will clean up, and I will tell Mr. Robin.”

He got his tiny broom and dustpan. Sweep, sweep, sweep—little bits of clay slid into the pan. He hummed to help his feelings stay calm. “Sweep and breathe, sweep and breathe.” He set the red button safely on the shelf, in the tin.

Just then, Bella Beetle buzzed by. “Hi, Miko! Want to race leaves?” she asked. She waved a shiny green leg.

“I want to,” said Miko. “But I need to finish my jobs. I broke a pot. I am cleaning now. After I tell Mr. Robin and finish sweeping, I can play.”

Bella nodded. “Good choice! I will find a fast leaf,” she said, and she zipped away to the marigolds.

Miko finished sweeping, then placed the clay pieces in a little basket. He felt a tiny twist in his tummy. “What if Mr. Robin is mad?” he whispered. He took another slow breath. “Honest words are brave words.”

Mr. Robin hopped to the shed door. His chest looked like a bright red apple. “Good morning, Miko!” he chirped. “What a tidy floor!”

Miko held the basket. “Mr. Robin,” he said in a small but steady voice, “I tried to put your button back. I slipped, and the pot fell. I am sorry. I cleaned up, and I will help fix it.”

Mr. Robin blinked, then tilted his head. “Thank you for telling me the truth,” he said gently. “Accidents happen. Honesty helps us make it right.” He pecked the clay pieces. “We can use these in the garden path. Crunchy, pretty steps!”

Miko’s whiskers perked up. “Really?”

“Really,” Mr. Robin said. “And next time, ask for help with high shelves.”

Miko nodded. “I will.”

They worked together outside. The sun warmed their backs. The clay pieces felt cool and bumpy in Miko’s paws. They pressed them into the dirt path. Crunch, press, crunch. The path looked like a red-and-brown mosaic. Bella Beetle cheered, “It sparkles!”

Miko checked his pebble clock. “Watered? Yes. Swept? Yes. Share a berry? Yes!” He ran to his leaf pantry and found a bright, juicy berry. It smelled sweet and felt smooth. He brought it out. “Bella, would you like to share?”

“Yes, please!” Bella buzzed. They each took a tiny nibble. “Mmm!”

Mr. Robin fluffed his feathers. “Miko, I am proud of you,” he said. “You made a careful plan, you calmed your mad feelings, and you told the truth.”

Miko’s heart felt light. “Honesty helps,” he said. “It makes things better.”

The sun climbed higher. The marigolds danced in a soft breeze. Miko and Bella raced two crunchy leaves down the little path. They laughed as the leaves went flip, flip, flip. When the race was done, Miko waved. “Playtime was fun. Now it’s rest time,” he said, patting his pebble clock.

He curled up in his leaf nest. The shed smelled safe and clean. He closed his eyes and smiled. “I told the truth. I made good choices. I feel brave.”

Outside, the garden hummed with happy sounds. Inside, Miko Mouse dreamed of shiny buttons, crunchy paths, and kind, honest hearts.

The End.
