# Milo's Big Fix-Up Day

In a cozy house, high up in the quiet attic, lived a little monkey named Milo. The attic was his special play space, filled with old boxes, soft blankets, and dusty sunbeams that danced through the small window.

One sunny morning, Milo woke up feeling very excited. "Today I'm going to make the BEST craft ever!" he chattered to himself, swinging from beam to beam.

Milo gathered papers, crayons, glue, and scissors. He cut and pasted and colored with great big swooshes. "Red here! Blue there! More glue everywhere!" he sang happily.

But oh my! Milo was having so much fun that he forgot to be careful. Scraps of paper flew around like confetti. Glue dripped on the wooden floor with sticky *plop, plop* sounds. Crayon bits rolled under the old furniture.

"Almost done!" Milo said, stepping back to look at his beautiful paper butterfly. But when he looked around, his mouth made a little "O" of surprise.

The quiet attic was a big mess! Paper pieces covered the floor like fallen leaves. The glue had made puddles that caught dust and dirt. Even his favorite blanket had crayon marks on it.

Just then, Milo heard *thump, thump, thump* - footsteps coming up the attic stairs.

"Oh no," whispered Milo, his tummy feeling twisty. "Grandma Rose is coming!"

The attic door creaked open, and Grandma Rose peeked in. She had kind eyes and silver hair, but when she saw the mess, her face looked sad.

"Milo, sweetie, what happened here?" she asked gently.

Milo's cheeks turned pink. He wanted to hide, but he knew that wouldn't help. "I made a craft, Grandma Rose, but I made a big mess too. I'm sorry," he said in a small voice.

Grandma Rose sat down carefully on a clean spot. "Thank you for telling the truth, Milo. But this mess isn't good for our attic home or for the earth. All this waste could hurt our environment."

Milo's eyes grew wide. "I didn't mean to hurt anything! Can I fix it?"

"Of course you can," smiled Grandma Rose. "Making amends means making things right again. Would you like to learn how?"

"Yes, please!" Milo nodded eagerly.

Grandma Rose helped Milo make a plan. "First, let's pick up all the paper scraps. We can put them in the recycling bin to help the earth."

Together, they gathered every piece of paper. Milo's little hands worked quickly - *rustle, rustle* went the papers as they filled the recycling bag.

"Now let's clean the glue spots with warm, soapy water," said Grandma Rose.

Milo scrubbed gently with a soft cloth. The water smelled like lavender soap and made everything fresh and clean again.

"What about my blanket?" asked Milo, touching the crayon marks.

"We can wash it together," said Grandma Rose. "And next time, we can put newspaper down first to protect our things."

When they finished cleaning, the attic looked beautiful again. The afternoon sun made everything glow golden and peaceful.

"I'm sorry I made such a mess, Grandma Rose," said Milo, giving her a big hug.

"You made it right again, and that's what matters most," she said, hugging him back. "You were very brave to tell the truth and work hard to fix things."

From that day on, Milo always prepared his craft space first and cleaned up as he went. He learned that taking care of his environment and making amends when he made mistakes felt even better than making the most beautiful crafts.

The End.
