# Gigi the Giraffe’s Cozy Closet Choice

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with big, gentle eyes and a long, warm neck. She loved to think before she did things. Today, rain tapped-tapped on the windows. Drip, drop, drip! Gigi’s favorite place on rainy days was the closet hideaway.

The closet hideaway was soft and cozy. Pillows made a squishy nest on the floor. A small lamp glowed like a golden moon. Scarves hung like rainbow vines. The air smelled like clean cotton and cinnamon. It was perfect for quiet fun.

Gigi peeked in. “Hello, closet,” she whispered. “I have big choices to make today.”

Her friends were already there. Pip the penguin waddled in tiny steps. “Let’s play a slide game with the scarves!” Pip said. “It will be so fast!”

Mimi the mouse squeaked, “Let’s make a snack party now! I brought crunchy crackers.”

Bash the bunny bounced. “Let’s build a fort! A tall, tall fort!”

Gigi blinked and took a slow breath. “Hmm,” she said. “We have many ideas. I want to make a good choice. Let’s listen to each friend first.”

Everyone grew quiet. The lamp hummed softly. Rain pattered a gentle beat.

Pip said, “Scarves are fun! I want to slide on them.” He wiggled his little feet.

Mimi said, “Snack party! My tummy is rumbling.” She rubbed her belly. Rumble, rumble.

Bash said, “Fort! I want to jump and build and hide.” He thumped his feet, thump-thump.

Gigi nodded. “Thank you for sharing. I hear all of you.” She touched a scarf with her soft nose. “Scarves can be slippery. Is it safe to slide in the closet?”

Pip tilted his head. “Maybe not,” he said. “We could fall.”

Gigi smiled. “That’s a good thought. We are being responsible.”

She looked at Mimi. “Snacks sound yummy. Are we allowed to eat in the closet?”

Mimi’s whiskers twitched. “Hmm. The rule says, ‘Snacks stay at the table.’”

Gigi nodded again. “We will follow the rule. We are making careful choices.”

She turned to Bash. “A fort sounds fun. Can we build without hurting the lamp or the scarves?”

Bash looked up at the golden lamp. “We can be gentle. We can ask for help.”

Gigi took another slow breath. “Let’s decide together. We want safe, kind, and fun. We want everyone included.”

She made a little plan in her mind. Plans helped her feel calm.

Gigi said, “I have an idea. First, we build a gentle fort with pillows and a blanket. We keep the lamp safe. After we play, we clean up. Then we all go to the table for a snack party. Later, we can have a scarf parade, not sliding—just dancing. How does that sound?”

Pip clapped his flippers. “Yes! A scarf parade is safe.”

Mimi squeaked, “Yay! Snack at the table!”

Bash bounced softly. “Fort time!”

They worked together. Gigi used her long neck to lift a light blanket. Bash stacked pillows with careful, quiet thumps. Pip carried tiny cushions, step-step. Mimi smoothed the sides with her tiny paws. The fort grew snug and round. The lamp glowed like a friendly star behind it.

“Listen,” Gigi said. “We move slow. We keep the lamp safe.”

“Yes,” everyone whispered. The fort was ready.

Inside, it was warm and hush-hush. They could hear the rain sing. Pip told a silly story in a soft voice. Mimi giggled. Bash made shadow puppets on the blanket wall. Gigi felt proud. Her heart felt like warm cocoa.

After a while, Gigi said, “It’s time to clean up.” She smiled. “We take care of our space.”

They put pillows back, pat-pat. They folded the blanket, nice and neat. The closet hideaway looked tidy again.

“Snack time!” Mimi cheered.

At the table, crackers went crunch-crunch. Pip shared his apple slices. They tasted sweet and cool. Bash passed napkins. Gigi poured tiny cups of water. Everyone said, “Thank you.”

When they finished, Gigi said, “Now, scarf parade!”

Back in the closet, music played softly from a little toy radio. Gigi tied a blue scarf like a ribbon. Pip wore a green one like a cape. Mimi had a tiny yellow bow. Bash twirled a pink scarf in the air.

They danced slow and careful. Swish, swish went the scarves. Tap, tap went their feet. They laughed and took turns leading the parade. “Your turn, Pip!” “Now you, Mimi!” No one was left out. Everyone felt seen and happy.

At last, Gigi took a deep breath. The room smelled like cotton and a little bit like apples. Her voice was calm. “Friends, today we made good choices. We listened. We were safe. We followed rules. We included everyone. That was responsible.”

Pip said, “You helped us think.”

Mimi said, “You helped us share.”

Bash said, “You helped us decide.”

Gigi smiled. “We all helped. When we listen and think, we make good choices together.”

Outside, the rain slowed to a soft drip. The lamp made the closet glow like a hug. Gigi felt happy and proud. She knew that next time, they could make good choices too.

The End.
