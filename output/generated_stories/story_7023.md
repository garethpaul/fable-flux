# Daisy's Big Day in the Treehouse

Little Daisy was the smallest deer fawn in the whole forest. Her soft brown fur had pretty white spots that looked like tiny flowers. Every morning, she loved to visit the big oak tree where her friend Rabbit had built a wonderful treehouse.

"Good morning, Rabbit!" Daisy called up to the treehouse. The wooden house sat high in the branches, with a rope ladder hanging down.

But today, something was different. No one answered.

Daisy looked up at the treehouse windows. "Rabbit? Are you there?" she called again. Still no answer.

She walked around the big tree trunk, her small hooves making soft sounds on the fallen leaves. The morning air smelled like pine and fresh grass, but Daisy felt worried. Where was her friend?

Then she heard a tiny voice from way up high. "Help! Help me!"

Daisy looked up and saw Rabbit's little paw waving from the treehouse window. "I'm sick today, Daisy," Rabbit called down weakly. "I can't come down to play, and I'm so thirsty. But I'm too tired to get water."

Daisy's heart felt sad for her friend. She wanted to help, but the treehouse was so high up! The rope ladder looked very long and scary.

"Don't worry, Rabbit!" Daisy called up bravely. "I'll help you!"

But how? Daisy had never climbed the rope ladder before. She was always too scared, and Rabbit usually came down to play with her instead.

Daisy took a deep breath. The rope felt rough under her small hooves. "I can do this," she whispered to herself. "Rabbit needs me."

Step by step, Daisy climbed up. The ladder swayed a little in the gentle breeze. When she looked down, the ground seemed very far away. Her legs felt shaky.

"You're doing great, Daisy!" Rabbit called from above. "Just a little more!"

Daisy kept climbing. She thought about how kind Rabbit had always been to her. When she was scared of thunder, Rabbit sang songs to make her feel better. When she couldn't reach the sweet apples, Rabbit helped her get them.

Finally, Daisy's head popped up through the treehouse door. "I made it!" she said, feeling proud and excited.

The treehouse was cozy inside, with soft cushions and books everywhere. Rabbit was lying on his bed, looking tired but happy to see her.

"You climbed all by yourself!" Rabbit said with a smile. "I'm so proud of you!"

Daisy found Rabbit's water bowl and filled it from the rain bucket by the window. The water was cool and fresh. She brought it to her friend carefully.

"Thank you, Daisy," Rabbit said after drinking. "You're such a good friend. And look what you did today – you climbed up here all by yourself!"

Daisy's chest felt warm with happiness. "I was scared at first," she admitted. "But I knew you needed help, so I tried anyway."

"That's what being brave means," Rabbit said. "Being scared but doing the right thing anyway."

Daisy spent the whole day taking care of Rabbit. She brought him soup, read him stories, and sang the same songs he used to sing for her when she was scared.

When evening came, Daisy climbed down the ladder. It didn't seem scary anymore. She felt strong and proud.

"I can take care of myself AND help my friends," Daisy said to herself as she walked home through the forest.

From that day on, Daisy visited the treehouse often, climbing up and down the rope ladder like it was easy. She had learned that she was braver and stronger than she ever knew.

The End.
