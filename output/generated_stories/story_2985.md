# Bubbles the Dolphin Makes a Smart Plan

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash and sing. One sunny afternoon, Bubbles visited a creative art room. The room smelled like fresh paper and crayons. Paints shimmered in little cups like rainbow puddles. Soft music hummed, and the floor felt cool and smooth.

“Hello!” chirped Bubbles, wiggling his fins. “I want to make a big, big ocean picture!”

Miss Maple, the kind art teacher, smiled. “Welcome, Bubbles. In our art room, we learn with our eyes, ears, hands, and hearts. Today, we use calm breaths and smart steps.”

Bubbles nodded fast. He saw glitter, brushes, stickers, and stacks of paper. He felt so excited that little splashes of water danced from his tail.

“I will use everything!” Bubbles giggled. He grabbed glue. He grabbed glitter. He grabbed three brushes at once. “Whee!”

Miss Maple held up a gentle hand. “Slow down, Bubbles. Let’s think before we begin.”

But Bubbles twirled and squeezed glue in a big squiggly swirl. He sprinkled glitter like shiny snow. He dipped all the brushes into all the paints. The colors mixed and turned brown. The glitter stuck to his nose. “Oops,” he said softly.

Across the room, friends were making art too. Mia was coloring a sun with bright yellow. Teddy was making a paper boat. Lina was painting a calm blue sky. They looked up when Bubbles bumped the table. A cup of blue paint tipped—plop! A puddle spread like a tiny lake.

Lina gasped. “My sky!”

Bubbles froze. His heart thumped, thump-thump. “I’m sorry,” he whispered. His fins drooped. The room felt too quiet.

Miss Maple came close and spoke in a warm voice. “It’s okay to make mistakes. This is a learning room. Let’s solve the problem together.”

Bubbles took a deep breath. In and out. He listened to the soft music again. He looked at Lina’s picture. The blue paint had splashed near the edge.

“We can dab it,” said Teddy, holding up a sponge.

“We can add clouds,” said Mia, pointing to white paper.

Bubbles blinked. “I can help,” he said. “I want to help.”

Miss Maple placed a small towel in Bubbles’s fin. “First, we pause. Then, we plan. This is called using our minds. Education helps us make good choices.”

Bubbles nodded. “Pause. Plan.”

He pressed the towel gently on the spill. “Dab, dab.” The towel soaked up the extra blue. Mia cut soft, puffy clouds from the white paper. Lina smiled a little. “I like clouds,” she said.

“Thank you for helping me,” Lina added.

Bubbles felt warm and glad. “Thank you for letting me help,” he said. He turned back to his messy table and looked carefully. “I want to try again. What is my plan?”

Miss Maple tapped the steps card on the wall. It showed simple pictures:
1. Breathe.
2. Think.
3. Choose tools.
4. Create slowly.
5. Clean up.

Bubbles read the pictures. He took a deep breath. He thought about the ocean he loved. “I choose blue, green, and white,” he said. “One brush. One paper. A little glue.”

He dipped one brush in calm blue paint. Swish, swish. He painted gentle waves. He added soft green seaweed. He pressed tiny white paper circles for bubbles. Pop, pop. He listened to the quiet swish of his brush and felt proud of his careful fin.

Mia watched and smiled. “Your waves look smooth.”

Teddy pointed. “Your bubbles look bouncy!”

Bubbles giggled. “They do!”

Then he heard a sniffle. In the corner, a little crab named Chip held a broken crayon. “My red crayon snapped,” Chip said. His eyes were wet.

Bubbles remembered the steps. “Pause. Plan.” He looked at Miss Maple. “May I help Chip?”

Miss Maple nodded. “Helping others is a wonderful way to learn.”

Bubbles found a crayon wrapper and some tape. “We can wrap it,” he said. He showed Chip how to press the pieces together and roll the tape. “There. It’s shorter, but strong.”

Chip tried the crayon. Scritch, scritch. “Thank you,” Chip said. “I can finish my heart now.”

Bubbles felt gratitude bloom like a warm bubble in his chest. “Thank you for letting me help,” he said again. “I learn when I help.”

Soon it was sharing time. The room smelled like clean water and lemons as everyone wiped tables. The music made tiny bell sounds. The walls sparkled with art.

Lina held up her sky with fluffy clouds. “Bubbles helped me fix my picture. I am grateful,” she said.

Chip held up his red heart. “Bubbles showed me a smart trick.”

Bubbles held up his ocean. “I learned to pause, plan, and create slowly. Education helps me make good choices. Helping others feels good. I am thankful for my friends and teacher.”

Miss Maple clapped softly. “You used your mind and your heart. That is true learning.”

Bubbles smiled big. His fins felt calm and strong. He whispered to himself, “Breathe. Think. Choose. Create. Clean.” The words sounded like a little song.

Everyone cheered. The creative art room felt bright and cozy. Bubbles did a tiny, careful splash. “I did it,” he said. “We did it—together.”

The End.
