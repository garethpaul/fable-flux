# Splash's First Paintbrush

Splash the dolphin wiggled excitedly in her special water tank in the art room. The room smelled like fresh crayons and paint. Colorful pictures hung on every wall like a rainbow.

"Today we're going to paint!" said Ms. Rose, the art teacher. She had kind eyes and paint-splattered apron.

Splash watched the other sea friends dip their brushes in bright paint. Ollie the octopus used all eight arms to paint. Shelly the turtle painted slowly and carefully. But Splash had never painted before.

"I don't know how," Splash said sadly. Her smooth gray skin felt warm from worry. "What if I can't do it?"

Ms. Rose smiled and brought a special big brush to Splash's tank. "Everyone starts somewhere, dear. Would you like to try?"

Splash looked at the brush. It seemed so big! The paint looked so messy! Her heart beat fast like ocean waves.

"What if I make mistakes?" Splash asked.

"Mistakes help us learn," said Ms. Rose gently. "Even the most beautiful paintings start with one small brush stroke."

Splash took a deep breath. She grabbed the brush with her flipper. It felt smooth and soft. She dipped it in bright blue paint that reminded her of the ocean.

"Here I go!" Splash said bravely.

She touched the brush to the white paper. A beautiful blue line appeared! It wobbled and wiggled, just like Splash when she swam.

"I did it!" Splash squeaked with joy.

She tried again. This time she made a circle. Then another line. The paint felt cool and wet on the brush. Soon she was making swirls and dots all over her paper.

"Look at my painting!" Splash called to her friends.

Ollie swam over. "Wow! Those blue swirls look like ocean waves!"

Shelly nodded slowly. "And those dots look like bubbles!"

Splash felt proud and happy. Her painting wasn't perfect, but it was hers. She had tried something new and discovered she could do it!

"Can I try green paint now?" Splash asked excitedly.

"Of course!" laughed Ms. Rose. "Artists love to try new colors."

Splash dipped her brush in green paint. She painted seaweed and fish. With yellow, she painted a bright sun. Each new color made her more excited to keep painting.

Soon, Splash's paper was full of beautiful ocean scenes. Blues and greens swirled together. Yellow fish swam between painted seaweed.

"I love painting!" Splash announced happily. "At first I was scared, but now I know I can learn new things!"

Ms. Rose clapped her hands. "You were very brave to try, Splash. And look how much you learned!"

Splash looked at her colorful painting. She remembered feeling scared at the beginning. But she had been curious and tried anyway. When she made wobbly lines, she didn't give up. She kept trying until she got better.

"Tomorrow can we paint again?" Splash asked hopefully.

"Every day if you'd like," said Ms. Rose. "Artists practice to get better and better."

Splash splashed happily in her tank. She had learned that trying new things could be fun. Even when something seemed hard, she could be brave and give it a try. And if she made mistakes, that was okay too - mistakes helped her learn!

The art room filled with the sounds of happy sea friends painting and learning together.

The End.
