# Finn's Turn to Shine

Little Finn the fox kit loved exploring. His fluffy red tail twitched with excitement as he discovered something wonderful behind the big apple tree. There, tucked away in the corner of the garden, stood an old wooden shed with its door slightly open.

"What's in there?" Finn whispered to himself, his black nose twitching as he caught the scent of old wood and dusty tools.

Finn peeked inside and gasped. The shed was full of amazing things! Garden tools hung on hooks, colorful flower pots sat on shelves, and in the center was something that made his amber eyes sparkle—a shiny red wheelbarrow.

"Wow!" Finn squeaked. He had never seen anything so wonderful. The wheelbarrow had a bright yellow handle and made a soft squeaking sound when he touched it.

Just then, he heard small voices coming from deeper in the shed.

"My turn! My turn!" chirped Rosie the robin.

"But I was here first!" squeaked Pip the mouse.

"No fair! You've been using it forever!" added Benny the bunny.

Finn crept closer and saw his three friends gathered around another wheelbarrow—this one was blue with silver handles. They were all trying to climb in at the same time, pushing and squabbling.

"What's going on?" Finn asked gently.

Rosie fluffed her feathers. "We all want to ride in the wheelbarrow, but everyone wants to go at the same time!"

"It's not fair!" Pip stamped his tiny paw. "Benny got to go first yesterday!"

Finn's ears perked up as an idea formed. "Wait! I found another wheelbarrow right over there. But maybe we can make this more fun for everyone."

He pointed to the red wheelbarrow with his fluffy tail. "What if we take turns? Everyone gets a chance, and it's fair for all of us."

Benny's ears drooped. "But taking turns is hard. I want to go now!"

"I know," Finn said kindly. "But taking turns means everyone gets to have fun. Watch!"

Finn climbed into the red wheelbarrow first. "I'll count to ten, then it's Rosie's turn." He counted slowly: "One... two... three..." The wheelbarrow felt smooth and cool under his paws.

When he reached ten, Finn hopped out. "Your turn, Rosie!"

Rosie's eyes lit up. She flew into the wheelbarrow and chirped happily as she counted to ten. The metal felt warm from the sunshine streaming through the shed window.

"Now me!" squeaked Pip, and he scurried in for his turn.

Finally, Benny got his turn in the blue wheelbarrow. "This is actually more fun!" he realized. "I get to watch everyone else have fun too, and then I know my turn is coming!"

Soon, all four friends were taking turns in both wheelbarrows, counting together and cheering for each other. The shed filled with happy laughter and the gentle squeaking of wheels.

"Taking turns means everyone gets to play," Finn said, his tail wagging. "And it's fair because we all get the same amount of time."

"Plus, we can practice counting!" added Rosie.

"And we can help each other," said Pip, helping Benny climb out of the wheelbarrow.

As the sun began to set, painting the shed walls golden, the four friends sat together on a pile of soft garden sacks.

"Thank you, Finn," said Benny. "Taking turns made everything better."

Finn smiled, his whiskers twitching with happiness. "When we're fair and take turns, everyone has more fun. And we all learned we can do things by ourselves too!"

The friends agreed to meet at the shed tomorrow for more wheelbarrow adventures, taking turns and playing fairly together.

The End.
