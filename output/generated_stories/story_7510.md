# Koda's Quiet Corner

Koda the koala loved his special place in the old garden shed. It smelled like warm wood and fresh flowers from the garden outside. The morning sun peeked through the dusty windows, making golden spots dance on the wooden floor.

"Good morning, little shed," Koda said softly, patting the wall with his fuzzy paw. "What shall we do today?"

Koda had made this shed his wellness corner. He kept soft cushions in one spot, some books in another, and his favorite breathing stones in a small basket. The stones were smooth and cool, perfect for holding when he needed to feel calm.

Suddenly, he heard sniffling sounds outside. Through the window, Koda saw Ruby the rabbit sitting by the tomato plants. Her ears drooped down, and her nose was red.

"Oh my," whispered Koda. He felt his heart do a little flip-flop. When someone was sad, Koda sometimes felt sad too. He took a deep breath, just like his mama taught him.

Koda picked up one of his smooth stones and held it tight. "What should I do?" he asked himself quietly. He closed his eyes and listened to his feelings. His heart said, "Help Ruby feel better."

He walked outside, his feet making soft crunching sounds on the garden path. "Hello, Ruby," he said gently. "I see you're feeling sad. Would you like to talk about it?"

Ruby looked up with watery eyes. "Oh, Koda! I was trying to pick carrots for lunch, but they're too hard to pull out. I got frustrated and said mean words to the carrots. Now I feel bad."

Koda nodded slowly. "It sounds like you had big feelings," he said. "Sometimes when things are hard, our feelings get big too. That's okay."

"But I was mean to the carrots," Ruby sniffled.

"Would you like to come to my quiet corner in the shed?" Koda asked. "We can figure this out together."

Ruby wiped her nose and nodded. They walked to the cozy shed together.

Inside, Koda showed Ruby how to sit on the soft cushions. "First, let's take three deep breaths," he said. "Breathe in the good feelings, breathe out the worried feelings."

They breathed together. Koda could smell the lavender from his mama's garden and hear the gentle buzzing of bees outside.

"How do you feel now?" Koda asked.

"A little better," Ruby said, her voice calmer.

"When I have big feelings, I hold my worry stones," Koda explained, offering Ruby a smooth blue stone. "Then I think about what I can do to make things better."

Ruby held the stone and thought. "Maybe I could say sorry to the carrots and ask for help pulling them?"

"That sounds like a wonderful idea," Koda smiled. "And next time you feel frustrated, you can take deep breaths first, just like we did."

They walked back to the garden together. Ruby gently touched the carrot tops and whispered, "I'm sorry I was grumpy with you." Then she asked Koda to help her pull the carrots.

Working together, they gathered enough carrots for lunch. Ruby's ears perked up, and she was smiling again.

"Thank you, Koda," Ruby said. "Your quiet corner helped me feel better and make a good choice."

Koda felt warm and happy inside. "Taking care of our feelings helps us take care of each other," he said wisely.

As the sun climbed higher, Koda returned to his special shed, grateful for his peaceful place and the chance to help a friend feel better.

The End.
