# Joey Roo and the Cozy Closet Club

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a bouncy tail. One rainy morning, the house was quiet and soft. Pitter-pat, pitter-pat went the rain on the window. Joey sniffed the warm smell of clean laundry and padded over to the hallway closet. It was his favorite hideaway.

He opened the door. “Ooo,” Joey whispered. “It’s cozy!” The closet smelled like fresh towels and a tiny bit like Daddy’s peppermint soap. Inside were soft blankets, a basket of socks, and a flashlight. Joey’s ears perked up. “I have an idea,” he said. “I can make a Closet Club!”

Joey wiggled in and spread a blanket. He lined up the socks like little pillows. “Welcome to the Cozy Closet Club,” he announced to himself. “We will read, we will stretch, and we will take turns.” His heart felt brave and warm. He was taking initiative—starting something good all by himself.

Joey peeked out of the closet and called, “Mama! May I make a club in the closet? I will keep it tidy.”

Mama hopped over with a smile. “What a wonderful idea, Joey. That shows initiative. You are starting something helpful and kind.”

Joey beamed. “Yes! I will make a plan.” He held a crayon and a small notepad. “Rule one: Kind words. Rule two: Healthy habits. Rule three: Take turns. Rule four: Be brave and try.”

He taped the little list to the inside wall. The tape made a tiny rip sound—rrrip! Joey liked that sound. He placed a bowl of water and apple slices at the closet door. “Healthy snack,” he said. He put a basket for toys and another for books. “Tidy, tidy,” hummed Joey.

Soon, his friend Kiki Koala knocked on the doorframe. Tap tap. “Can I join the club?” she asked, peeking in with shy eyes.

“Yes!” said Joey. “You may join. First, we wash hands.” He held up a clean towel. “Let’s go.” Joey led the way to the sink. The water whooshed, warm and friendly. Joey sang, “Scrub-a-dub, tops and thumbs.” Kiki giggled and scrubbed, too. They dried their hands on a fluffy towel. It smelled like sunshine.

Back in the closet hideaway, the rain still tapped the window. Joey clicked the flashlight on and off. Click! Click! “Story time,” he said. “We take turns holding the light.”

“I feel shy,” Kiki whispered. “What if I drop it?”

Joey took a brave breath. “I can show you,” he said. “I will go first.” He held the flashlight steady and shined it on a book. The pages rustled like soft leaves. “Your turn,” Joey said, smiling. He passed the light with two careful paws. “You can try. I believe in you.”

Kiki held the flashlight. Her paws trembled a little. “I’m scared,” she said.

“It’s okay to feel scared,” Joey said gently. “Courage is trying, even when we feel scared.”

Kiki nodded and held the light still. The beam made a round, golden circle on the page. “I did it!” she said, grinning.

“You did it!” Joey cheered. “That was brave.”

They read a silly story about a hat that sneezed. “Achoo!” Joey acted it out. Kiki laughed so hard her ears wiggled. Then Joey sat up straight. “Stretch break!” he announced. “Healthy bodies need to move.” He led a slow, gentle stretch. “Reach high like a tall tree,” he said. They reached up. “Now roll shoulders like tiny wheels.” Roll, roll. Their muscles felt warm and happy.

After the stretch, Joey looked at the tidy baskets. A few socks were on the floor. “I can fix that,” he said, picking them up. “I started the club, so I will help care for it.” He felt proud. Taking initiative meant helping without being asked.

Kiki asked, “Can I choose a game?”

“Yes,” said Joey. “We take turns. Your turn to choose.”

Kiki chose a whisper game. “We whisper a brave thing we can try,” she said.

Joey leaned close. The closet felt safe and soft. He could hear the rain and smell the apples. “I can try new veggies,” he whispered. “Crunchy carrots!”

Kiki whispered, “I can say hello to new friends.”

“Good brave tries,” Joey said. “Let’s have a snack.” They sat at the doorway and munched crisp apple slices. Crunch, crunch. They drank cool water. Sip, sip. Joey wiped his mouth with the clean towel. “Healthy habits feel good,” he said.

Soon, their friend Benji Bilby waddled by. “What’s in there?” Benji asked.

“Our Closet Club!” Joey said. “We have rules. We use kind words. We wash hands. We take turns. We try brave things. Do you want to join?”

Benji nodded. “Yes, please.”

Joey smiled. “I’ll show you how.” He taught Benji the hand-washing song and showed him the tidy baskets. He let Benji choose the next activity. “Your turn,” Joey said. Benji picked a quiet drawing time. They drew smiley suns and bouncy tails.

When drawing was done, Joey looked around. The closet was still neat. The towel was folded. The bowls were empty and clean. Joey felt a happy zing in his chest. He had started something kind and kept it going.

Mama peeked in. “What a wonderful club,” she said. “I see clean hands, healthy snacks, and brave friends taking turns.”

Joey hopped a little. “I had an idea, and I started it. That’s initiative!”

Mama hugged him. “Yes, Joey. You took initiative. You were kind, healthy, and brave.”

Joey looked at his friends. “Tomorrow, we can meet again,” he said. “We can try a new veggie and a new game.”

Kiki and Benji cheered. “Closet Club forever!”

Joey felt cozy and proud. The rain was soft, the closet was warm, and his heart was brave. He learned that starting something good, helping others, and keeping healthy habits can make a small space feel big with joy.

The End.
