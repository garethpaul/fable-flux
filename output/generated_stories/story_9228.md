# Fern the Fox’s Green Art Day

Fern was a curious fox kit with bright eyes and soft, orange fur. One sunny morning, Fern padded into the creative art room at the forest school. The room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight painted warm squares on the floor. Brushes sat like tiny trees in cups, and jars of buttons and ribbons sparkled like treasure.

“Today,” said Teacher Owl, her feathers smooth and gray, “we make art that helps the Earth.”

Fern’s ears perked up. “Helps the Earth? With art?” she asked.

“Yes,” Teacher Owl hooted. “We will use scraps and old things. We will make something new. That is called re-use.”

Fern wiggled her tail. “I want to try!”

Fern looked around. There were piles of paper scraps, empty boxes, bottle caps, cardboard rolls, and old fabric squares. But there were also crumpled papers on the floor and a tipped-over bin. Some ribbons were tangled like spaghetti, and glue sticks had no caps.

“Oh no,” Fern whispered. “This room looks messy. If we make a mess, where will the scraps go? Will they hurt the Earth?”

Fern’s tummy felt twisty. She wanted to help the Earth, not harm it.

Teacher Owl saw Fern’s worried face. “What is your plan, Fern?” she asked kindly.

“A plan?” Fern blinked. “I can make a plan?”

“Yes,” said Teacher Owl. “That is called a strategy. When we have a plan, we can do a big job, little by little.”

Fern stood tall on her small paws. “Okay! First, I will sort.” She sniffed the air. It smelled like paper and paint and a little like apples from snack time. “Paper goes here,” she said, placing a box on the left. “Plastic caps go here,” she said, tapping a tray in the middle. “Fabric and ribbons go here,” she added, spreading a soft cloth on the right.

“Good start,” said Teacher Owl.

Fern started picking up the scraps. “Flip, flop, flap,” went the fabric squares. “Clink, clink,” went the bottle caps. The paper made a friendly “shhhh” as she stacked it. Her paws felt busy and warm. A soft breeze came in the window and tickled her whiskers.

But then the cardboard tower wobbled. “Uh-oh!” Fern squeaked. The boxes tumbled with a “crash!” Fern’s ears drooped. She felt a little sad.

“Try again,” Teacher Owl said gently. “That is called resilience. We keep trying.”

Fern took a deep breath. “I can do it,” she told herself. “I will make the tower strong.” She put big boxes on the bottom and small boxes on top. She used a ribbon to tie the stack in a neat bow. “There!” she said. The tower stayed still. Fern smiled.

Now it was time to make art. Fern wanted to make a fox forest. She gathered green paper scraps, brown cardboard rolls, and the shiny bottle caps. She made trees from the rolls and green triangles. She made a sparkly river from blue ribbon. She made tiny animals from buttons and bits of fabric. “Hello, button bunny!” she giggled. “Hello, fabric bird!”

Fern reached for a glue stick. “Oh no,” she said. “No caps! The glue is dry.” Her whiskers drooped again.

“What can you do?” asked Teacher Owl.

Fern thought. Then her eyes lit up. “I can make paste!” She mixed a little flour and water from the snack table in a small bowl. She stirred slowly, “swish, swish.” The paste was smooth and cool. “It works!” she cheered, sticking a tree to the paper. The room smelled a bit like bread dough, and Fern felt proud.

Other kits peeked over. “Wow, Fern!” said Mole, blinking. “Your trees look tall.”

“They are re-used trees,” Fern said. “We are helping the Earth by using old things in a new way.”

Squirrel clapped. “Can I help?”

“Yes,” Fern said kindly. “You can place the clouds.” She gave Squirrel soft cotton fluff. “Let’s keep our scraps sorted so nothing gets lost,” she added.

Soon, the art room filled with happy sounds: “snip, snip,” “tap, tap,” “swish, swish.” The sun warmed Fern’s back as she worked. She felt calm and brave. When a ribbon knot got tight, she took a slow breath and tried again. When a cap rolled away, she chased it, laughed, and put it in the right tray.

At last, their picture was done. The fox forest glowed with green trees, a blue ribbon river, and friendly button animals. Fern’s heart felt full and light.

Teacher Owl looked closely. “What did we learn today?” she asked.

Fern raised her paw. “We learned to care for the Earth,” she said. “We used scraps instead of new things. We sorted our trash. We kept the room clean so nothing hurts the Earth.”

“And how did you do it?” asked Teacher Owl.

“We made a plan,” Fern said. “We used strategy. When things fell down, we tried again. That is resilience. We also shared and helped each other. That is kind.”

Teacher Owl smiled. “You discovered that art can be green,” she hooted. “Small paws can do big help.”

Fern looked at her friends and the bright picture. She felt proud, strong, and happy. “I can care for the Earth every day,” she said. “I can turn off lights. I can refill my water bottle. I can sort recycling. I can make new art from old things!”

The class cheered. The room felt fresh and warm, like sunshine on leaves. Fern hugged her friends with her fluffy tail.

“Let’s hang our art by the window,” Fern said. “So everyone can see how we helped the Earth.”

They hung it high. The ribbon river shimmered. The button bunny winked. Fern’s eyes sparkled.

“We did it,” she whispered. “Little by little, with a good plan.”

The End.
