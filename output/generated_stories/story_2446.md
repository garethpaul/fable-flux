# Rocco’s Bedroom Ball Game

Rocco the musical raccoon loved songs and sounds. His cozy bedroom felt warm and soft, with a snuggly blanket mountain on the bed, a little lamp that glowed like honey, and a basket of colorful socks. His drum made a gentle thump. His tiny ukulele went twang-twang. Rain tapped the window, pitter-patter-pat, like a tiny crowd clapping.

“Today, I want to play sports,” Rocco said. “I want to move my body! I want to cheer!” He bounced on his paws and shook his tail. “But the rain is big and splashy. I can’t go outside.”

Rocco sat on the rug and hummed a thinking song. “Hmm-mm-hmm.” He tapped his drum, thump-thump, like a heartbeat. “I can make a game in here! I can build a game.”

He looked around the cozy bedroom. Pillows. Blocks. A laundry basket. Socks. “Yes!” he said. “I can build a safe sports game.”

Rocco put the laundry basket by the bed. He stacked soft pillows to make a little wall so nothing would bonk the lamp. He lined up blocks to make a friendly path. He rolled up socks into soft balls. “This is our field,” he said. “This is our goal. This is a gentle game.”

Just then, his friend, Mina the mouse, peeked in. “Squeak! It is rainy. What are you doing, Rocco?”

“I am building a bedroom ball game,” said Rocco. “It is a sport we can play inside. We will be kind and careful. We will move our bodies and take turns.”

Mina’s whiskers wiggled. “That sounds fun! Can I help?”

“Yes,” said Rocco. “Let’s be a team.” He handed Mina a rolled sock. “Listen to the beat. We play to the beat. Thump, thump, toss!”

Rocco tapped the drum soft and steady. Thump. Thump. Mina tossed the sock ball. It whooshed through the air. Plop! It landed in the basket. Rocco cheered, “Go, team!”

“My turn!” said Rocco. He wiggled his toes, breathed in and out, and tossed. The sock ball bumped a pillow and slid into the basket. Plop! They laughed. The room felt bright, even with the rain.

Rocco sang, “We toss with care, we toss with care, we play inside and show we care.” Mina clapped her tiny paws. “I like your song,” she said.

Then, a problem came. Their friend Bruno the bear cub came stomping in. “I want to play, too!” Bruno roared. His paws were big. His steps were loud. Boom-boom. He grabbed a sock ball and threw it hard. Zoom! The sock ball flew past the pillows and knocked over the block path. Clatter!

Mina squeaked and hid behind the drum. Rocco’s ears drooped. “Oh no,” he said. “Our game is not safe now. We need help.”

Bruno’s face fell. “I didn’t mean to scare anyone,” he said softly. “I just wanted to play sports.”

Rocco took a deep breath and placed a gentle paw on Bruno’s arm. “It’s okay,” he said kindly. “We can fix it together. We are a team. We build it again.”

Rocco hummed a calm tune. “Fix-it song, fix-it song, we can fix it, sing along.” Mina peeked out and smiled. “We can rebuild!” she said.

They worked together. Rocco stacked the blocks back on the friendly path. Mina made the pillow wall taller. Bruno held the laundry basket steady with his strong paws. “Thank you for helping,” said Rocco. “You are strong and kind.”

Bruno nodded. “I can use gentle paws,” he said. He practiced with a slow toss. Swish. The sock ball landed softly. Plop.

“Nice! That was safe and smart,” said Mina.

Rocco smiled. “Let’s set the rules for our bedroom sport.”

He sang the rules in a bouncy tune:
“Soft toss, kind heart,
Take turns, that’s the start.
Move your body, cheer and clap,
Build it safe, no crash, no zap!”

They all giggled. Rocco tapped the drum. Thump, thump. “Bruno, you go first,” he said.

Bruno tossed gently. Plop! A score! Mina tossed next. Plop! Another score! Rocco tossed last. The sock ball rolled around the rim and fell in. Plop! “Teamwork!” shouted Rocco.

They played again and again, taking turns, cheering, and helping. When a block tipped over, Bruno said, “I can fix it.” When Mina felt shy, Rocco sang a soft brave song. When Rocco dropped a sock, Mina handed it back with a smile. “We are kind,” she said.

The room was full of happy sounds: thump-thump, plop-plop, clap-clap, and rain tap-tap-tapping outside. Rocco’s heart felt warm. “We can play sports inside,” he said. “We can move, we can build, and we can be kind.”

At the end, they sat on the blanket mountain, breathing slowly. In, out. In, out. Rocco strummed the ukulele. Twang-twang. “What did we learn?” he asked.

Bruno said, “In sports, we use gentle bodies and teamwork.”

Mina said, “We build safe spaces and help friends.”

Rocco said, “We are kind and we try again.”

They all said together, “Sports are fun when we are safe, creative, and kind!”

The End.
