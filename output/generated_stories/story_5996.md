# Panda Poppy’s Study Corner Shuffle

Panda Poppy was a patient panda with soft, black-and-white fur and a big, gentle smile. She loved her cozy study corner. It had a small desk, a comfy cushion, bright crayons, and a cup with a friendly blue toothbrush. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor, and the air smelled like fresh bamboo and minty soap.

“Good morning, study corner,” Poppy whispered. “Today I learn and dance!”

Poppy’s tummy gave a tiny rumble. “First, wellness,” she said, patting her belly. She poured a little bowl of crunchy bamboo bites and sliced sweet apple. Crunch, crunch! The apple was juicy and cool on her tongue. She sipped warm water from her cup. “Ahh,” she said, feeling calm.

After breakfast, Poppy remembered hygiene. She held up her toothbrush like a tiny magic wand. “Brush, brush, sparkle time,” she sang. Swish-swish went the minty foam. “Top teeth, bottom teeth, little panda smile!” She rinsed, wiped her mouth with a soft cloth, and washed her paws with bubbly soap. The bubbles smelled like lemons. Pop! Pop! Pop! The bubbles tickled her nose. Poppy giggled.

“Clean paws, clean teeth, happy heart,” Poppy said. She felt fresh and ready.

On her desk was today’s plan: Learn letters, stretch body, dance break, then tidy up. Poppy tapped her nose. “I can be patient. I can keep trying.”

She opened her alphabet book. The letter of the day was P. “P is for Panda Poppy,” she said, pointing with her paw. She traced the big P with a blue crayon. “P is for patience, too!” She tried to make the P nice and round. Her first P was a little wobbly.

“It’s okay,” Poppy told herself. “I can try again.” She took a slow, deep breath in, then out. “Slow and steady.” She tried again. Swoop! The next P looked better. Her heart felt warm and proud.

“Time to move my body,” she said. She stood up on her cushion and reached her paws to the sky. “Hello, sunshine!” The sunlight felt like a warm blanket on her fur. She rolled her shoulders, wiggled her toes, and stretched her back. “Stretching helps my body feel strong.”

Then Poppy pressed play on her little music box. A happy beat filled the study corner. Thump-thump, tinkle-tinkle! Poppy smiled. “Dance break!”

She stepped side to side. Step, step. She clapped her paws. Clap, clap! She tried a gentle spin. Swish! She wobbled a little and bumped her crayon cup. A few crayons rolled—tick-tick—onto the floor.

Poppy paused. She felt a tiny pinch in her tummy. “Oops,” she whispered. “I can be patient. I can fix it.” She knelt down and picked up each crayon, one by one, placing them back with care. “Green, blue, red, yellow,” she counted softly. Her breath slowed. Her body felt calm again.

“Let’s try the spin again,” Poppy said. “I will keep trying.” She bent her knees, held her balance, and turned slowly. Swish. She did it! “Hooray!” She giggled and tapped her paws in a happy rhythm. Tap-tap-tap!

After dancing, Poppy felt her cheeks warm and rosy. “Water time,” she said. She took a sip. Cool, cool, cool. The water felt like a tiny river inside, making her feel good. She patted her chest. “Wellness means listening to my body.”

Poppy went back to her book. She drew a big P and a little p. “P is for practice,” she said. She drew a pineapple with spiky hair. She drew a toothbrush with sparkles. She drew a tiny panda dancing.

When she finished, she noticed a few crumbs on her desk. “Hygiene helps me stay healthy,” she said. She got a small cloth and gently wiped the crumbs. Swipe, swipe. She washed her paws again. Bubbles kissed her fur. Pop! Pop! “Clean again,” she said happily.

Just then, Poppy’s friend, a little paper bird named Peep, fluttered down from the bookshelf. “Tweet! What are you doing, Poppy?” Peep chirped.

“I’m learning and moving in my study corner,” Poppy said. “I am practicing patience, too.”

Peep tilted his tiny head. “Can I dance?”

“Yes!” Poppy laughed. “Let’s do the Panda-Patience Dance! Step, step, clap, spin, smile!” They danced together. Step, step—clap! Slow spin—swish! Peep fluttered. Poppy giggled. The room felt bright and bouncy.

Peep landed on the desk. “Tweet! I’m thirsty.”

“Me too,” said Poppy. They both took a water break. Sip, sip. Poppy wiped the rim of her cup and put it back neatly. She loved how tidy her study corner looked.

Poppy closed her book and smiled at Peep. “Today I ate healthy food, brushed my teeth, washed my paws, stretched, danced, and practiced my letters. I was patient when things were wobbly. I kept trying.”

Peep chirped, “Tweet! That is wellness!”

Poppy nodded. “Wellness means caring for my body and my feelings. Hygiene keeps me clean. Dance helps me move and be happy. Patience helps me learn.”

She gave the study corner a gentle hug with her eyes. The sunlight was soft now, like a sleepy song.

“I did my best,” Poppy said quietly. “And I feel good.”

Peep fluttered up to the shelf. “Tweet! See you tomorrow!”

“See you tomorrow,” Poppy whispered.

The End.
