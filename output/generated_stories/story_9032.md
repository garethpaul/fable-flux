# Tessa Turtle Leads the Way

In the quiet guest room, a shy turtle named Tessa peeked out from her smooth green shell. The guest room smelled like clean soap and lemon. Soft blankets were folded on the bed. Sunlight made a cozy square on the rug. Tessa liked the soft square best. It felt warm on her toes.

Today, Tessa had a plan. She wanted to build a Cozy Corner for story time. She wanted to invite everyone to sit and read together. But Tessa’s voice was small. Her cheeks felt warm when she spoke. “I can try,” she whispered. “I can be brave.”

She slowly pushed a pillow. Thump. She slid a blanket. Swish. She placed a basket of picture books. Rustle. The guest room looked friendly now.

Then the door creaked open. “Hi!” chirped Mina Mouse. “Is this a reading fort?”

“It can be,” Tessa said shyly. Her words were soft like a hush. “I… I’m making a Cozy Corner.”

More friends came to visit the guest room. Bobo Bunny bounced in, his ears flopping. “I brought carrots to share!” he said. Zuzu, a tiny bird with bright eyes, fluttered to the curtain rod. “I have a song,” she chirped. And Malik, a gentle kid from far away, peeked in with a smile. “I can tell a story from my home,” he said. “We used to read under a mango tree.”

Tessa’s heart made a small brave beat. Friends were here. She had made a plan. Maybe she could lead. She took a deep breath. She felt the soft air in her nose. She let it out slowly.

“Welcome,” Tessa said. “Let’s make the Cozy Corner together.” She looked around the guest room. “We can share the pillows. We can take turns. We can listen.”

Bobo wiggled. “I want the big pillow!” he said.

Mina Mouse hugged a blanket. “I want it first!” she squeaked.

Zuzu flapped her wings. “My song now!” she chirped.

Tessa’s tummy fluttered. The room felt busy. She wanted everyone to be happy. She remembered her plan. Leaders help. Leaders listen. Leaders share.

“Friends,” Tessa said softly. She practiced making strong words. “Let’s talk.” She sat tall, even though she was small. “We can make a plan together.”

Bobo stopped bouncing. Mina stopped squeaking. Zuzu perched quietly. Malik nodded and sat cross-legged on the rug.

Tessa pointed to the basket of picture books. “We can take turns. First, stories. Then, songs. Then, snacks.” She smiled. “We can share the big pillow by counting. One, two, three. Then we switch.” She looked at each friend. “Does that sound fair?”

Mina’s whiskers twitched. “Can I use the blanket first if I give it to Zuzu next?” she asked.

Zuzu chirped, “Yes! And I can sing softly during the pictures.”

Bobo’s ears drooped, then perked up. “I can wait two turns,” he said. “I’ll practice sitting still.”

Malik raised his hand. “In my home, we say, ‘My turn, your turn, our turn.’ We can try that.”

Tessa clapped her tiny feet. “That is a good idea. My turn, your turn, our turn.”

They began. Malik told a mango tree story. His voice was warm like the sun square on the rug. The guest room felt like traveling far away. Tessa listened. She held the basket and passed the first book to Mina.

“Thank you,” Mina said. “Your turn next, Tessa.”

Tessa blinked. “Me?” Her heart thumped. She wanted to hide in her shell. But she remembered: practice makes brave. She took a small breath. “I can try.” She opened a book with bright pictures. “Once there was a tiny seed,” she read in a gentle voice. Zuzu hummed softly. Bobo hugged a small pillow and waited. Everyone listened.

When Tessa finished, she looked up. “How did I do?” she asked.

“You led us kindly,” Malik said.

“You used fair words,” Mina added.

“You shared the turns,” Zuzu chirped.

“And you were brave,” Bobo said.

They all said, “My turn, your turn, our turn,” and switched places. Zuzu sang a sweet song like rain. Bobo passed crunchy carrot sticks around. Tessa made sure everyone had some. The carrots smelled fresh and tasted crisp. Crunch, munch. The guest room felt happy and safe.

Soon it was time to clean up. Tessa led again. “Let’s work together,” she said. “Mina, you fold the blanket. Zuzu, gather books. Bobo, stack pillows. Malik, help me sweep crumbs.”

They moved in a gentle rhythm. Swish, stack, flap, tap. The room became neat. The lemon smell came back. The sunlight square stretched longer on the rug.

When they finished, Tessa smiled. Her shy feelings were still there, like a small whisper, but her brave feelings were bigger now, like a warm hug.

Malik held out his hand. “Leader Tessa, thank you,” he said.

Tessa shook his hand. “Thank you, team,” she said. “We shared. We took turns. We listened. We practiced being kind, even when we wanted the same things.”

Zuzu chirped, “That is good leadership.”

Bobo nodded. “And good snacks!”

Mina giggled and tucked the blanket into the basket. “Let’s do it again tomorrow,” she said.

Tessa tucked into her shell just a little, cozy and proud. “Yes,” she whispered. “Tomorrow, we can lead together.”

The End.
