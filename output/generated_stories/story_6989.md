# Whiskers Solves the Pencil Problem

Whiskers was a clever cat with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved the cozy study corner by the window. The corner smelled like paper and cedar wood. Sunbeams made warm stripes on the carpet. On the small desk were crayons, pencils, and a big, smooth notebook. Whiskers’ tail flicked with excitement.

“Today, I will draw a sunny garden,” Whiskers purred. He placed a shiny yellow pencil beside his paw. He liked the smooth feel of the wood and the pointy tip. “Just right,” he said.

Tap-tap. Two little friends peeked in: Dot the ladybug and Pip the mouse. They loved the study corner too.

“Hi, Whiskers!” Pip squeaked. “Can I borrow your yellow pencil? I want to draw cheese.”

Dot fluttered over the desk. “I need it too. I want to draw sunshine spots!”

Whiskers blinked. He had only one yellow pencil.

“My friends both want my pencil,” he whispered to himself. “Hmm.” His whiskers twitched. “I can solve this.”

Pip reached for the pencil. Dot reached too. Their tiny hands touched, and their faces scrunched up.

“I saw it first!” said Pip.

“I asked first!” said Dot.

Whiskers’ ears tilted back at the sharp voices. He felt a tight ball in his tummy. The sunny room didn’t feel sunny anymore.

He took a deep breath. In, out. The cedar smell helped him calm down. “We have a problem,” he said kindly. “Let’s fix it together.”

Dot buzzed her wings. “I am mad,” she said softly.

Pip shuffled his feet. “Me too.”

Whiskers nodded. “It’s okay to feel mad. Let’s use thinking steps.”

He lifted his paw and tapped the desk, tap-tap-tap, like counting.

“Step one,” said Whiskers. “We listen.” He looked at Dot. “Dot, you talk first. Pip and I will listen.”

Dot took a tiny breath. “I want to draw sunshine spots for my flower picture. Yellow is perfect.”

“Thank you,” said Whiskers. He turned to Pip. “Your turn.”

Pip nodded. “I want to draw cheese for my kitchen picture. Yellow is perfect too.”

Whiskers’ eyes shone. “Good listening, everyone.”

“Step two,” he said, tapping again. “We think of ideas.”

He placed three smooth buttons on the desk: a red one, a blue one, and a green one. They clicked softly as they landed. “Idea one,” he said, pointing to the red button. “We take turns. We can use a timer.”

Pip tilted his head. “How long?”

Whiskers smiled. He set the small sand timer on the desk. The golden sand glowed in the sun. “One turn is when the sand is all down.”

“Idea two,” said Whiskers, pointing to the blue button. “We draw together. We can share the yellow pencil, passing it back and forth for each line.”

Dot wiggled with interest.

“Idea three,” he said, patting the green button. “We find another way. Maybe we mix colors. Yellow can come from other crayons. Let’s test.”

Whiskers pulled out a white crayon and an orange crayon. He pressed them together on a scrap paper with gentle strokes. Scritch-scratch. The colors blended into a bright new shade.

Pip’s eyes widened. “It looks yellow!”

Dot giggled. “It’s sunshine-y!”

Whiskers purred. “Now we choose. What idea is kind and fair?”

Dot looked at Pip. Pip looked at Dot. Their faces softened.

“I like taking turns,” said Pip.

“I like drawing together,” said Dot. She paused. “Maybe we can do both. We take turns with the yellow pencil, and when we don’t have it, we mix colors to make more yellow.”

Whiskers felt the tight ball in his tummy melt away. He nodded. “That is generous and smart.”

He flipped open the big notebook. The paper felt thick and smooth. “Let’s make a plan.”

- Pip uses the yellow pencil while the sand falls.
- Dot mixes white and orange while she waits.
- When the sand is done, they switch.

The sand began to fall. Whoosh, soft and golden. Pip drew a cheese with tiny holes. Scritch-scratch. He passed the pencil to Dot with a smile. “Your turn.”

“Thank you,” said Dot. She drew sunshine spots on big flowers. Her wings hummed happily.

Whiskers drew a garden path with a gray pencil. He listened to the gentle sounds: the sand whispering, the crayons scritching, the soft purr in his chest. The study corner felt sunny again.

Soon, the page was full: a yellow cheese on a little table, bright sunshine on tall flowers, and a stone path where friends could walk together. Pip pointed. “Look! Our pictures fit!”

Dot clapped her tiny hands. “We shared. We were kind.”

Whiskers smiled and wrapped his tail around them both. “We listened. We thought. We chose a fair way. That is conflict-resolution.”

Pip nodded. “And you were generous, Whiskers.”

Dot added, “And clever!”

Whiskers blushed under his gray fur. “We were all clever. We solved it together.”

He wrote a small note at the bottom of the page: “When we have a problem, we can listen, think of ideas, and choose a kind plan.”

The study corner glowed in the warm light. The room smelled sweet like paper and cedar. Their hearts felt light.

“Friends,” said Whiskers, “let’s always share and solve together.”

“Yes!” Pip and Dot cheered.

The End.
