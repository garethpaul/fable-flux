# Pebble the Penguin's Plan

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with a bright orange beak and soft, smooth feathers. Pebble waddled into the comfortable nursery with a happy “tap-tap” of little feet on the wooden floor. The room smelled like warm crayons and freshly washed blankets. The big window let in gentle light, and a soft rug felt cozy under Pebble’s feet.

“Good morning!” Pebble chirped. “I’m ready to play!”

On the wall was a colorful picture chart. It showed Pebble’s tasks for the morning: 
1. Put blocks away.
2. Water the plant.
3. Finish the puzzle.
4. Read a book.

Ms. Lark, the kind teacher, knelt beside Pebble. “Today, we will practice completing tasks,” she said with a smile. “We will plan, and we will follow directions. Ready?”

Pebble puffed up with pride. “Ready!”

Pebble’s belly felt bubbly with excitement. So many fun things! Pebble ran to the big basket of rainbow blocks. “Look at the red block! Look at the blue!” Pebble giggled and tossed blocks in the air. They clacked and clinked as they landed. The sound was loud and silly.

Ms. Lark held up the chart. “Pebble, what is step one?”

Pebble read the picture of a tidy shelf. “Put blocks away,” Pebble said.

Pebble looked at the blocks on the floor. There were many. Pebble’s smile wiggled a little. “That’s a lot.”

Ms. Lark’s voice was gentle. “We can make a plan. A plan helps us finish. Let’s do it together.”

Pebble nodded. “A plan!”

Ms. Lark pointed to three small baskets. “We will sort by color. Red blocks go here. Blue blocks go here. Yellow blocks go here. We start at the rug corner.”

Pebble took a deep breath. The rug smelled like clean cotton. “I can do that.”

Pebble picked up a red block. “Red goes in the red basket,” Pebble said softly. The block made a soft thump. Pebble picked up a blue block. “Blue goes in the blue basket.” Thump. Yellow. “Yellow goes in the yellow basket.” Thump.

“Tap-tap, thump-thump,” went Pebble’s feet and the baskets. Pebble smiled again. “This plan is working!” Pebble said.

Ms. Lark clapped once. “You are following directions. You are completing your first task.”

Soon, the floor was clear. Pebble patted the rug. “All clean!”

“How do you feel?” Ms. Lark asked.

“I feel proud,” Pebble said, chest warm and happy. “What is next?”

Ms. Lark pointed to the chart. “Water the plant. The directions say: fill the small watering can, pour slowly, and stop when the soil is damp.”

Pebble waddled to the sink. The water made a soft hiss. Pebble held the tiny watering can. It was cool and smooth. “Fill slowly,” Pebble whispered. Pebble watched the water rise and then turned off the tap. Pebble carried the can carefully to the green plant by the window.

“Pour slowly,” Pebble said. The water made a sweet trickle sound. The soil turned dark and smelled like rain. Pebble stopped. “The soil is damp,” Pebble said. “Done!”

Ms. Lark smiled. “Great job following directions.”

Pebble looked at the chart again. “Finish the puzzle!”

The puzzle was a picture of a snowy hill and a shiny red sled. Pebble sat on the soft rug and touched each piece. “Edge pieces first,” Pebble planned. Pebble found straight sides and clicked them together. Click, click. Pebble hummed a little song. Pebble tried a middle piece and frowned. “It doesn’t fit.” Pebble looked closely. “Oh! I see the red sled corner.” Pebble tried again. Click! Pebble clapped. “I kept trying. I am almost done.”

At last, the final piece slid into place with a neat little click. Pebble’s eyes sparkled. “Puzzle complete!”

“What is the last task?” Ms. Lark asked, tapping the chart lightly.

“Read a book,” Pebble said. Pebble chose a book with a shiny blue cover. Pebble cuddled into a fluffy beanbag. The beanbag went whoosh and felt like a cloud. Pebble read the pictures and the simple words out loud. “Penguin goes up the hill. Penguin sleds down. Whee!”

Ms. Lark sat nearby and listened. The room was calm and warm. Pebble turned the last page and closed the book with a soft flap.

“I did it,” Pebble said, beak wide in a big smile. “I finished all my tasks!”

Ms. Lark held up the chart one more time. Every picture now had a tiny star sticker. “How did you do it, Pebble?”

Pebble tapped a wing to the chart. “I made a plan. I followed directions. I did one step, then the next step, and I didn’t give up.”

Ms. Lark nodded. “Yes. Planning helps us know what to do. Following directions helps us do it safely and well. Completing tasks makes us feel proud.”

Pebble stood tall. “I can plan. I can follow directions. I can finish my tasks!”

The light from the window felt warm on Pebble’s feathers. The nursery smelled like clean books and sweet plants. Pebble gave a happy little hop. “What shall we plan next?”

Ms. Lark laughed softly. “Snack time! First, wash hands. Then, sit. Then, enjoy.”

Pebble grinned. “I know how to do that!”

And Pebble did.

The End.
