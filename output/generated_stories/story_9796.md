# Bubbles, Songs, and Sunny Paws

On a bright, warm morning, a playful otter named Otti splashed in a little blue plastic pool in the sunny backyard. The grass tickled Otti’s paws. The air smelled like oranges and soap bubbles. Birds sang, “Tweet, tweet!” and a gentle breeze whispered, “Whoosh.”

Otti loved to play. “Swish, swish! Splash, splash!” Otti giggled, zooming around the pool. But soon, Otti’s paws were sticky from sweet mango slices, and muddy specks dotted Otti’s whiskers.

“Uh-oh,” said Otti, touching the sticky paws. “My paws feel icky. I want to eat more mango, but my paws need a clean.”

Just then, friends came to play. Mei arrived with a bright red kite. “Hi, Otti!” she said. She wore a blue hat and smelled like jasmine soap. Sam came with a drum that went “dum, dum.” He wore a green shirt and a big smile. A tiny bell jingled on Asha’s braid, and she carried a yellow bucket and a purple towel. “We can play pretend picnic!” Asha said.

“I want to eat more mango,” Otti said, “but my paws are sticky and dirty.”

Mei nodded. “My grandma says, ‘Clean hands, happy tummy.’ We wash before we eat.”

Sam tapped his drum softly. “My dad sings a hand-washing song. We sing it until the bubbles rinse away.”

Asha held up a little bottle of soap. “My aunt taught me to scrub between fingers and thumbs. Like this!” She rubbed her hands and made soft foam clouds.

Otti looked at the hose and the soap on the garden table. The soap smelled like lemons. The towel was fluffy and warm in the sun. Still, Otti wrinkled a nose. “Washing is so long,” Otti said. “I want to play now.”

Mei smiled. “We can make it fun! We can follow directions like a game.”

Sam thumped a happy beat. “Dum, dum-dum! Step one, turn on the water!”

“Okay,” said Otti. “Step one!” The hose hissed, and cool water tickled Otti’s paws. “It’s cold!” Otti giggled.

“Step two,” Asha said gently, “wet your paws all over.” She showed Otti how to turn and wiggle each paw. Otti felt the water drip, drip, drip.

“Step three,” Mei said, squeezing a tiny dot of soap into Otti’s paws. “This soap is from my home. It smells like lemons. Some people use jasmine soap too. So many lovely smells!” She took a deep breath. “Ahh.”

Otti sniffed. The lemon smell was bright and happy. “I like this soap,” Otti said.

Sam tapped the drum again, slower. “Step four, scrub and sing.” He sang, “Bubbles up, bubbles down, scrub the tops, scrub around!”

Asha joined in, “Between the fingers, round the thumbs, count to twenty with the drums!”

Mei counted with calm, careful words. “One, two, three, four...” Otti rubbed all over, making soft white bubbles. The bubbles looked like mini clouds. They popped, pop-pop! “Five, six, seven, eight...” Otti scrubbed between fingers, around thumbs, and over wrists. “Nine, ten, eleven, twelve...” The bubbles smelled sweet and clean. “Thirteen, fourteen, fifteen, sixteen...” Otti felt proud. “Seventeen, eighteen, nineteen, twenty!”

“Step five,” said Asha. “Rinse, rinse, rinse until the water feels smooth.” Otti held paws under the water. The bubbles swirled away, like tiny boats floating down a river.

“Step six,” said Mei. “Dry with the towel.” The towel was soft and cozy. Otti rubbed, rub-rub, until the paws were warm and dry.

Otti looked at the clean paws. They shone in the sun. “My paws are happy!” Otti cheered. “I followed every step!”

Sam smiled. “You did great. When we follow directions, we take care of our bodies.”

Mei nodded. “In my family, we sing as we wash. In Sam’s family, they tap a drum. In Asha’s family, they count slowly. Different ways, same good care.”

Asha held out a small box. “I brought roti and cucumber sticks,” she said. “We can share. But first, we wash our hands too.” She winked.

They all washed together, each using their special way: Mei sang a gentle tune, Sam tapped a steady beat, and Asha counted in a soft voice. Otti joined with happy splashes. The backyard filled with music, numbers, and the fresh smell of soap.

After washing, they sat on a sunny blanket. Otti tasted mango. “Mmm,” said Otti. “Sweet and juicy!” The clean paws did not feel sticky now. The kite fluttered above, the drum hummed low, and the bell on Asha’s braid jingled like a tiny star.

Otti looked at friends and smiled. “Today I learned washing is caring. I can follow steps and keep my paws clean. We can use songs, drums, or counting. Different ways, same clean!”

Mei clapped. “Clean hands, happy tummy.”

Sam thumped the drum. “Healthy and strong!”

Asha rang her bell. “We did it together.”

Otti stood tall on the blanket and said, “I will wash before I eat, after I play, and after I potty. I will scrub, rinse, and dry, every time.”

The sun warmed their faces. The breeze said, “Whoosh,” and the birds sang, “Tweet, tweet!” Otti felt proud, safe, and loved.

The End.
