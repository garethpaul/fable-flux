# Bubbles, Splash, and "I’m Sorry"

Ollie the playful otter loved bath time. The bathroom was warm and steamy, and it smelled like sweet orange soap. Tiny water drops dotted the mirror like little stars. Ollie’s favorite part was the bubbles. He liked to blow them. He liked to stack them. He liked to pop them with his whiskery nose.

“Splish, splash!” sang Ollie as he swished his tail in the tub. “Pop, pop!” went the bubbles. The water felt cozy on his fur. The rubber duck squeaked, “Squee!” when he hugged it.

Mama Otter smiled. “Ollie, this is joyful,” she said. “Bath time can be fun and helpful. We get clean, and we take care of our space. After we play, we do our chores.”

Ollie nodded. “I can help,” he said. He liked to be helpful. He liked to do his part. But sometimes, when he felt excited, he forgot.

Mama set a small towel on the floor. “This towel is for wet feet,” she said. “We keep the floor dry so no one slips. We put the toys away too.”

Ollie blew a giant bubble. It floated up like a glassy moon. His eyes grew wide. “Oooo,” he said softly. “Look, Mama!”

“That is a beautiful bubble,” said Mama. “Now, remember our plan.”

But then Ollie saw something new. It was the big, shiny bottle of blueberry bubble bath. It sat on the sink, glowing blue like the ocean. It smelled like sweet muffins and summer. Ollie reached out with a wet paw.

“Mmmmm! Berries!” he giggled. He gave the bottle a little squeeze.

Glurp!

Blue goop oozed out. It slid down the bottle and plopped onto the floor. Sploot! Another squeeze. Glorp! The blue goop made a slippery, shiny puddle.

“Ollie, stop, please,” Mama said gently. “That is too much.”

But Ollie wanted to see the bubbles grow. He tipped the bottle more.

Whoosh!

Suddenly, blue bubbles bubbled everywhere—under the sink, around the rug, and near the toilet. The floor looked like a blueberry cloud. The room smelled super sweet. Ollie laughed. “It’s a bubble snowstorm!” He splashed his paws on the floor. Splat! Splat!

Then Ollie’s foot slipped. “Woah!” He wobbled and grabbed the tub. Mama held him steady.

“Ollie,” Mama said, her voice soft but serious. “This is not safe. The floor is very slippery. And now we have a big mess.”

Ollie’s belly felt squishy, like a sad sponge. His ears drooped. He looked at the blue puddles. He looked at Mama. Her whiskers were wet, and her eyes were tired.

“I forgot,” Ollie whispered. “I wanted more bubbles. I did not listen. I made a mess.”

Mama took a breath. “Thank you for telling the truth,” she said. “That was honest. I feel upset about the mess. But I love you. We can fix this. We can be safe. And we can practice forgiveness.”

Ollie’s eyes filled with little tears. “I’m sorry, Mama,” he said. “Will you forgive me?”

Mama knelt down. She hugged him close. Her fur was warm and soft. “I forgive you, Ollie,” she said. “Forgiveness means we let go of the upset. We choose to help and do better next time.”

Ollie felt lighter, like a bubble floating up. “I want to help,” he said. “What are our chores?”

Mama smiled. “First, we make the floor safe. Then we clean up the blue. Then we put toys away.”

They worked together. Mama placed big towels on the floor. Fwump! Ollie pressed the towels with his small paws. Squish, squish. The towels soaked up the slippery puddles. The room smelled less strong now.

“Good pressing,” said Mama. “You are being careful and helpful.”

Ollie nodded. “Careful and helpful,” he repeated.

Next, they rinsed the blue soap with warm water. The water sounded like a soft rain. Pitter-pat. Ollie used a little scrub brush. “Scrub-a-dub, scrub-a-dub,” he sang. He watched the blue swirl away down the drain. Swish.

“Now toys,” said Mama. “Rubber duck, boat, and cup go in the basket.”

Ollie picked up the duck. “Squee!” it said. He gave it a gentle shake and set it in the basket. He stacked the cup and the boat too.

“Ollie, you are doing your chores,” Mama said. “That is helpful to our home.”

Ollie looked around. The floor was dry. The towels were in a neat pile. The bottle of bubble bath sat safely on a high shelf now. The bathroom felt calm again, like a quiet pond.

“I feel proud,” Ollie said, patting his chest. “I made a mess, but I fixed it. I said sorry, and you forgave me.”

Mama nodded. “Yes. Mistakes happen. We can learn. We can say, ‘I’m sorry.’ We can forgive. And we can help.”

Ollie thought for a moment. “Next time,” he said, “I will ask before I squish the bottle. I will use just a little.”

“That is a wise choice,” said Mama. “And we will keep a towel on the floor for wet feet.”

Ollie smiled. “Bath time is still fun. And chores make it safe.”

Mama gave him a gentle nose boop. “Exactly.”

They hugged again. The rubber duck squeaked a happy “Squee!” The bathroom smelled fresh and clean. Ollie’s fur felt fluffy and warm.

Ollie whispered, “Thank you for forgiving me, Mama.”

Mama whispered back, “Thank you for helping, Ollie.”

They turned off the light and padded out, paws soft on the dry floor, hearts light like bubbles.

The End.
