# Rusty's Fair Share Adventure

Rusty was a little orange fox kit with bright amber eyes. He loved exploring the old garden shed behind Grandma Maple's house. The shed smelled like fresh wood and dried flowers. Sunlight peeked through the dusty windows.

Today, Rusty found something wonderful. A big basket of shiny red apples sat on the wooden table. His tummy rumbled loudly.

"Oh my! These look so yummy," Rusty said, licking his lips.

Just then, he heard tiny voices outside. Peek, peek, peek! Three little field mice were tapping on the shed door.

"Please, Rusty!" squeaked the first mouse. "We're so hungry!"

"We smelled the sweet apples," said the second mouse.

"Can you share some with us?" asked the third mouse politely.

Rusty's ears twitched. He looked at the big basket. Then he looked at the three small mice. His tummy rumbled again.

"But I found them first," Rusty said. "And I'm really hungry too."

The mice's whiskers drooped sadly. They turned to walk away with empty bellies.

Rusty felt something funny in his chest. It wasn't hunger. It was a warm, uncomfortable feeling. He watched the mice's tiny paws shuffle in the dirt.

"Wait!" Rusty called out. "Come back!"

The mice turned around hopefully.

Rusty thought hard. The basket had many apples. More than his little fox tummy could eat. He counted them carefully with his paw. One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight!

"I have an idea," Rusty said with a smile. "Let's share them fairly!"

He picked up the first apple. "This one is for Mouse One."

He picked up the second apple. "This one is for Mouse Two."

He picked up the third apple. "This one is for Mouse Three."

Then he picked up the fourth apple. "And this one is for me!"

"But there are more apples," squeaked Mouse One.

"Yes!" said Rusty. "Let's each take one more. That's fair!"

So each friend got two apples. The mice's eyes sparkled with joy.

"Thank you, Rusty!" they cheered together. "You're so kind!"

Rusty felt warm and happy inside. Much better than when he wanted all the apples for himself.

"Now we all have enough," Rusty said, crunching his sweet apple.

The mice munched happily too. The shed filled with the sounds of friends enjoying their snack together.

"Sharing is much more fun than eating alone," said Mouse Two.

"And it's fair when everyone gets the same," added Mouse Three.

Rusty nodded. His tummy was full, and his heart felt even fuller. He had learned something important today.

From that day on, whenever Rusty found good things in the garden shed, he always thought about his friends first. He would count carefully and make sure everyone got a fair share.

The mice became Rusty's best friends. They would explore the shed together, looking for treasures and always sharing what they found.

Grandma Maple smiled when she saw them through the window. "What good friends," she said softly. "They know that sharing fairly makes everyone happy."

And Rusty learned that the best feeling in the world wasn't having everything for yourself. It was making sure everyone had enough.

The End.
