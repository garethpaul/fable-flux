# Milo the Monkey's Workshop Friends

Milo was a brave little monkey who loved to fix things. He had soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes that sparkled when he discovered something new. Every day, he would swing down to Mr. Garcia's garage workshop to help fix cars and bicycles.

The garage smelled like oil and metal, and tools hung neatly on the walls. Milo loved the sounds of hammers tapping and wrenches turning. "Click, clank, ping!" went the tools as everyone worked together.

One sunny morning, Milo arrived at the workshop and saw something that made his heart sad. Three new animals had come to help - a quiet turtle named Shelly, a mouse named Pip who used a tiny wheelchair, and a bird named Sky who couldn't fly very well.

But the other workshop animals weren't being very nice.

"Turtles are too slow!" said Rex the raccoon, not letting Shelly help with the screws.

"Mice are too small!" huffed Benny the bear, pushing Pip away from the toolbox.

"Birds should fly, not fix things!" squawked Carla the crow, taking the paintbrush away from Sky.

Milo watched as Shelly, Pip, and Sky sat sadly in the corner. Their eyes looked wet with tears, and their shoulders drooped down low.

"This isn't fair!" thought Milo. His monkey brain started working hard. "Everyone should get to help. Everyone has special talents!"

Milo swung over to Mr. Garcia. "Mr. Garcia," he said in his squeaky monkey voice, "I have an idea to make our workshop even better!"

Mr. Garcia smiled and nodded. "Tell me, Milo!"

Milo gathered everyone around a broken red bicycle. "This bike needs lots of different help," he announced. "And I know just the right animals for each job!"

He turned to Shelly the turtle. "Shelly, you're so careful and steady. Can you help tighten these tiny screws? They need someone who won't rush."

Shelly's face lit up like sunshine. "I can do that!" she said, and her steady flippers worked perfectly with the small screwdriver.

Next, Milo looked at Pip the mouse. "Pip, you're the only one small enough to reach inside this tight space to fix the chain!"

Pip rolled his wheelchair closer and reached his tiny paws into the bike frame. "I found the problem!" he squeaked happily.

Finally, Milo turned to Sky the bird. "Sky, your feathers are perfect for cleaning all the dust out of these hard-to-reach places!"

Sky fluffed up with pride and used her soft feathers like the best cleaning brush ever.

As they worked together, something magical happened. Rex, Benny, and Carla watched in amazement.

"Wow," whispered Rex. "Shelly really is the best at careful work."

"And Pip can reach places I never could!" said Benny.

"Sky makes everything so clean and shiny!" added Carla.

Soon, everyone was working together. The garage filled with happy chatter and the sweet sounds of teamwork. "Ting, tap, whoosh!" went their tools as they fixed the bicycle together.

When they finished, the red bicycle looked brand new and sparkled in the sunlight streaming through the garage window.

Mr. Garcia clapped his hands. "This is the best work we've ever done! When everyone helps with their special talents, we can fix anything!"

From that day on, the workshop became the happiest place in town. Milo had learned something very important: everyone has special gifts, and when we include everyone, we can do amazing things together.

"Different is wonderful!" Milo would say, swinging happily from the garage rafters.

The End.
