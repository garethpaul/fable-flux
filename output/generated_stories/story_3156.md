# Kiki the Calm Koala’s Rooftop Routine

Every morning, Kiki the calm koala woke up to the soft glow of the sun. She lived on a cozy rooftop garden filled with tiny trees, bright flowers, and little veggie pots. The city hummed below like a quiet song. Kiki stretched her furry arms and sniffed the cool air. It smelled like wet leaves and sweet flowers.

“Good morning, garden,” Kiki whispered. Her voice was soft and kind. “Let’s have a clean, green day.”

Kiki loved her routine. Routine means doing helpful things the same way every day. First, she picked up her small green bucket. “Check, check,” she said, tapping the bucket. “Let’s keep our home happy.”

Kiki walked to the edge of the rooftop and waved at the sky. A gentle breeze brushed her ears. “Hello, wind,” she said. “Thank you for the fresh air.”

Just then, her friend Pip the pigeon fluttered down. “Coo! Coo!” Pip said. “Kiki, your rooftop smells so nice!”

“Thank you, Pip,” Kiki replied. “I water the plants. I sort the trash. I use my green bucket. That helps the Earth.”

Pip tilted his head. “What is sorting the trash?”

Kiki smiled. “I can show you.” She walked to three small bins. One was blue, one was green, and one was gray. She touched each bin gently.

“Blue is for recycling,” Kiki said. “We put clean paper, cans, and bottles here. They can be made new again.”

“Green is for compost,” she continued. “We put food scraps and leaves here. They turn into rich, dark soil that helps plants grow.”

“Gray is for trash,” Kiki finished. “This is for things that cannot be reused or made into soil.”

Pip blinked. “Coo! That’s smart.”

Kiki nodded. “It’s our rooftop routine. It helps the Earth breathe.”

They heard a little clink. A plastic bottle rolled by the flower bed. “Oh dear,” Kiki said. She picked it up and washed it with a tiny splash from her water can. Drip, drip. The water made a soft sound. “Now it’s clean. It goes into the blue bin.”

Pip pecked at a banana peel near the tomato pot. “What about this?”

“That belongs in the green bin,” Kiki said. She lifted the lid. Inside, the compost smelled like warm dirt and oranges. “The peel will become food for the soil.”

Kiki and Pip worked together. They picked up a crinkly paper cup, a shiny can, and a handful of dry leaves. Kiki showed Pip where each one went. “Blue, green, gray,” she said like a song. “Recycle, compost, trash.”

Pip flapped his wings happily. “I can help every day!”

“That is kind,” Kiki said. “Helping is part of our routine.”

Next, Kiki watered the plants. The watering can was painted with little suns. “Sip, sip,” she said, giving each plant just enough. The marigolds smelled peppery and bright. The mint leaves smelled cool and sweet. The soil felt damp and soft under her paws.

“Plants need water and care,” Kiki explained. “When we take care of plants, they clean the air for us.”

Pip took a deep breath. “Mmm. Fresh!”

Kiki smiled. “We also save water. I use rain from the rain barrel.” She tapped the barrel. It went thump-thump. “We do not waste. That helps the Earth too.”

They heard a rustle by the gate. A tiny mouse named Meli peeked in. “Hello,” squeaked Meli. “Can I rest here? The street is noisy.”

“Of course,” Kiki said, calm and warm. “Our rooftop is a safe, clean place.”

Meli climbed onto a sun-warm stone. “It smells like grass,” she sighed. “How do you keep it so nice?”

Kiki pointed to the bins and the barrel. “We have a plan. We clean a little each day. We sort our things. We use what we have. We share.” She patted the mint. “We grow. We care. We learn.”

Pip puffed his chest. “I learned blue, green, and gray!”

Kiki clapped softly. “Yes, Pip. You learned to love the Earth.”

They sat together and listened. Bees buzzed like tiny violins. The city hummed like a big drum. A cloud drifted by, cool and soft like cotton.

Then, a gust of wind whooshed over the roof and scattered a few wrappers. One wrapper landed on a sunflower. The flower drooped.

Kiki took a calm breath. In, out. “We can fix this,” she said. She picked up the wrappers gently. “Thank you for telling me, sunflower.” She blew a little kiss to the flower and placed the wrappers in the gray bin. She tucked a small stick under the sunflower to help it stand tall again.

Meli smiled. “You stayed calm.”

Kiki nodded. “When we are calm, we can help.”

She turned to her friends. “Let’s make a promise. Every morning, we will follow our rooftop routine. We will sort, save, and share. We will care for our plants. We will keep our home clean.”

Pip saluted with a wing. “I promise!”

Meli put a tiny paw on her heart. “I promise!”

Kiki felt warm and proud. “When we care for our small place,” she said, “we help the big Earth too.”

They finished with a happy dance. Pip twirled, Meli wiggled, and Kiki swayed, her soft ears bouncing. The sun shone brighter, and the mint leaves sparkled with tiny drops.

Kiki looked at her friends. “Today, we learned that caring for the Earth is a simple routine. We sort our things. We save water. We grow plants. We clean up. Little steps make a big difference.”

Pip cooed, “Little steps!”

Meli squeaked, “Big difference!”

Kiki hugged them both. “Thank you for helping,” she said. “Let’s do it again tomorrow.”

The End.
