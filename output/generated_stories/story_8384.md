# Benny Bear's Workshop Dance

Benny Bear was a fluffy brown cub who loved to explore. Today, he wandered into Grandpa Hiro's garage workshop. The workshop smelled like wood chips and had tools hanging on every wall.

"Wow!" said Benny, looking around with wide eyes. "What are all these shiny things?"

Grandpa Hiro smiled warmly. He was fixing a colorful wooden toy train. "These are my tools, little Benny. I use them to build and fix things. But first, I always do my morning exercises to keep my hands and body ready for work."

"Exercises?" Benny tilted his fuzzy head. "What kind of exercises?"

"Come, I'll show you!" Grandpa Hiro stood up and stretched his arms high above his head. "In Japan, where I grew up, we learned that taking care of our bodies helps us work better. Let's start with our fingers!"

Grandpa Hiro wiggled his fingers like they were dancing raindrops. "Wiggle, wiggle, wiggle! This helps my fingers stay nimble for holding small screws."

Benny giggled and wiggled his paws. "This is fun! My paws feel tingly!"

"Now let's try arm circles," said Grandpa Hiro, moving his arms in big, slow circles. "This keeps my shoulders loose for reaching high shelves."

Benny tried to copy, but his left paw went one way while his right paw went another. He wobbled and almost fell over! "Oops! This is harder than it looks!"

"That's perfectly okay," laughed Grandpa Hiro gently. "Coordination takes practice. Watch me again, and we'll go very slowly."

Together, they moved their arms like gentle windmills. Benny concentrated hard, sticking out his pink tongue. Slowly, his paws began moving together.

"I'm doing it!" Benny cheered. "Look at me go!"

"Excellent! Now let's try marching in place," said Grandpa Hiro. "Lift your right paw, then your left paw. This helps our whole body work together."

Step, step, step! Benny marched around the workshop, feeling proud and strong. The wooden floor creaked pleasantly under his paws.

"Why do you do exercises every day?" asked Benny, still marching.

"Taking care of our bodies is very important," explained Grandpa Hiro. "When my hands and arms are strong and coordinated, I can build beautiful things safely. Plus, it makes me feel happy and energetic!"

Benny nodded seriously. "Like brushing my teeth and combing my fur?"

"Exactly! Self-care means taking care of our whole selves - our bodies, our minds, and our hearts."

For their final exercise, Grandpa Hiro taught Benny a special balance pose. "Stand on one paw and stretch the other paw forward, like you're reaching for honey!"

Benny wobbled and giggled, but he didn't give up. After several tries, he balanced for three whole seconds!

"I did it! I did it!" Benny danced around happily.

"You certainly did," said Grandpa Hiro proudly. "Now my hands feel ready to work, and you've learned something new!"

As they finished their exercises, Benny felt warm and energized. His whole body felt awake and coordinated.

"Can we do exercises together every morning?" asked Benny hopefully.

"I would love that," said Grandpa Hiro, giving Benny a gentle hug. "Remember, when we take care of our bodies and practice coordination, we can do amazing things!"

Benny grinned widely, already excited for tomorrow's exercise time in the workshop.

The End.
