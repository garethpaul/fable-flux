# Nutmeg Plans the Day

In a comfortable nursery with soft yellow walls and a round blue rug, a little squirrel named Nutmeg lived in a cozy wicker basket by the window. The nursery smelled like warm soap and fresh crayons. Sunlight made tiny dust sparkles float in the air.

Nutmeg was a resourceful squirrel. She liked to solve little problems all by herself. She kept a small tin box with pictures, stickers, and a paper list. On the list were simple pictures: a sun, a toothbrush, a cup, blocks, a leaf, and a moon. This was Nutmeg’s plan for the day.

“Good morning, Nutmeg,” whispered the wind through the curtains. They tickled her whiskers. Nutmeg stretched her tiny paws. “Good morning,” she chittered. “Today, I will try to do things by myself.”

She tapped her tin box. Tap, tap. “First,” she said, pointing to the sun picture, “I wake up and make my bed.” She patted her little blanket smooth. Pat, pat. She fluffed her pillow. Puff! It smelled like clean cotton. Nutmeg smiled. “I did it!”

Next was the toothbrush picture. Nutmeg hopped to the low sink step. “Slow and steady,” she told herself. She squeezed a tiny dot of minty paste. “Just a pea!” she giggled. She brushed in tiny circles. Swish, swish. When the foam felt too big, Nutmeg paused and took a slow breath. In…and out. “I can slow down,” she whispered. She spit, rinsed, and wiped her whiskers. “Fresh!” The mint felt tingly and cool.

Nutmeg looked at her list. A cup picture! She scampered to her snack shelf. “I will pour some water.” She held the cup with two careful paws. Glug, glug. A little splash landed on the counter. Drip. Nutmeg froze. Her tail went poof. She wanted to call for help, but then she remembered her plan.

“I can fix small spills,” she said. She took a cloth, dabbed the drops, and wrung it out. Squeeze. “All clean.” Her tail relaxed. She took a sip. “Ahh.”

On the rug, a basket of blocks waited. The block picture meant playtime. “I will build a cozy tower,” said Nutmeg. Clack, clack. She stacked red, then blue, then yellow. The tower wobbled. Her heart beat fast. It fell. Clatter!

Nutmeg felt hot in her cheeks. She wanted to stomp. She scrunched her nose. Then she remembered another plan: “When I feel big feelings, I can stop, hug myself, and breathe.” She wrapped her arms around her chest. Squeeze. “In…two…three. Out…two…three.” Her body felt softer. “I can try again.”

She placed the blocks carefully. Flat ones on the bottom, small ones on top. “Plan, then do,” she sang in a tiny voice. Soon the tower stood, tall and proud. She added a soft scarf as a flag. It brushed her paw like a cloud. “I did it by planning and calming,” she said.

A little bell on the door jingled. “Hello, Nutmeg!” called Mia, the gentle caregiver, from the hallway. Nutmeg waved. “Hi, Mia! I am practicing doing things by myself.”

Mia smiled. “I see your tidy bed, your shiny teeth, and your tall tower. You are very resourceful.”

Nutmeg’s tummy rumbled. “Snack time,” she said, pointing to the cup picture again and a tiny drawing of an apple she added herself. “I planned a crunchy apple.” She picked up an apple slice from a little plate and took small bites. Crunch, crunch. Sweet juice dripped on her fur. She dabbed it with a napkin. “I can keep myself clean,” she said proudly.

Later, Nutmeg saw the leaf picture on her list. It meant “Care for the plant.” In the sunny corner sat a small green plant with shiny leaves. Nutmeg checked the soil with one gentle paw. “Dry,” she said. She measured a little water in a tiny watering can. Drip…drip…stop. “Just enough.” The leaves shivered happily. “You’re welcome,” Nutmeg whispered.

After story time with Mia, Nutmeg felt wiggly. Her tail swished too fast. She wanted to jump on the couch. She paused. “I can choose a calm way to move,” she told herself. She rolled out her little mat. “Stretch time.” Reach up high like a tree. Wiggle like a worm. Curl like a pebble. Her body felt quiet again. “I can be the boss of my body,” she said with a grin.

Soon, the moon picture peeked from her list. “Bedtime,” Nutmeg said softly. She put away her blocks. Clack, clack into the basket. She set her cup in the wash bin. She folded her scarf flag. Fold, fold. Each little sound made the room feel tidy and calm.

Mia came to the doorway. “You had a full day, Nutmeg.”

Nutmeg showed her list. “I made a plan. I followed my plan. When things were hard, I breathed, and I tried again. I can do many things by myself.”

Mia knelt and looked into Nutmeg’s bright eyes. “You are careful, you plan, and you take care of your feelings. That is self-reliance.”

Nutmeg snuggled into her basket. The pillow smelled like cotton and a tiny hint of apple. The nursery was quiet, with only the soft tick-tick of the clock and the whisper of the curtains. Nutmeg tucked the list and tin box under her paw.

“I can trust myself,” she murmured. “I can plan my day. I can calm my body. I can try again.” Her eyes fluttered closed.

In the warm, gentle light, Nutmeg’s tail curled like a comma. She felt proud, safe, and sleepy.

And the little list waited for a new sun, a new day, and a new plan.

The End.
