# Benny the Beaver Learns to Take Turns

Benny the Beaver was always busy, busy, busy! He loved to build things with his strong teeth and flat tail. But this morning, Benny had a big problem.

"I need to brush my teeth!" Benny said, rushing into the bathroom. But when he got there, he saw his little sister Bella already at the sink.

"Move, Bella!" Benny said loudly. "I'm bigger and I need to use the sink RIGHT NOW!"

Bella's eyes filled with tears. "But Benny, I was here first. I'm brushing my teeth too."

Benny felt his body getting hot and wiggly. His tail started slapping the floor - THUMP, THUMP, THUMP! When beavers get upset, their bodies tell them by making them feel all squirmy inside.

"I don't want to wait!" Benny shouted. His voice echoed in the small bathroom, making it sound even louder.

Mama Beaver heard the noise and came to see what was happening. She saw Benny's red face and his tail going thump-thump on the floor.

"Oh my," said Mama gently. "Benny, your body is telling you that you're feeling frustrated, isn't it?"

Benny nodded. His chest felt tight and his paws were clenched.

"Let's try something special," Mama said. "Put your paws on your tummy and take three deep breaths with me. In through your nose... and out through your mouth."

Benny breathed in slowly. The air felt cool in his nose. Then he breathed out, making a soft "whoosh" sound. He did this three times.

"How does your body feel now?" asked Mama.

"A little better," Benny said. His tail had stopped thumping.

"Good! Now, let's look at this problem. Bella was at the sink first, and you both need to brush your teeth. What could we do?"

Benny thought hard. His body felt calmer now, so his brain could think better.

"Maybe... maybe we could take turns?" Benny suggested.

"What a wonderful idea!" said Mama. "And you know what? You and Bella are different sizes, so you might need different amounts of time. That's okay!"

Bella smiled. "Benny, I'm almost done. Just two more minutes!"

While he waited, Benny discovered something fun. He could practice his breathing and count to ten. He also noticed how the bathroom smelled like minty toothpaste and how the morning sun made pretty patterns on the wall.

"Your turn, Benny!" Bella said cheerfully.

"Thank you for sharing the sink," Benny said. "I'm sorry I was loud before."

"That's okay," Bella giggled. "Your tail makes funny sounds when you're upset!"

Benny laughed too. "Maybe next time we can brush our teeth together if we both stand on the step stool."

"Great idea!" said Bella. "We can share the space!"

As Benny brushed his teeth, he felt proud. He had learned that when his body felt hot and wiggly, he could take deep breaths to help himself feel better. He also learned that waiting and taking turns wasn't so bad - especially when he used that time to notice nice things around him.

"Mama," Benny said after spitting out his toothpaste, "I learned that Bella and I are different, but we can still share and be kind to each other."

"Yes," said Mama, giving him a warm hug. "And you learned how to help your body feel calm when you're frustrated. That's called self-regulation, and it's a very grown-up skill!"

From that day on, whenever Benny felt his body getting upset, he remembered to breathe deeply and think of good solutions. And he and Bella became the best tooth-brushing team ever!

The End.
