# Melody's Fair Song

High up on a red brick rooftop, a little yellow bird named Melody loved to sing. Every morning, she would fluff her bright feathers and open her tiny beak to share beautiful songs with the whole neighborhood.

"Tweet-tweet-trill!" Melody sang happily as the warm sun tickled her wings. Her voice was so sweet that flowers seemed to dance and children smiled as they walked to school below.

But one cloudy Tuesday, Melody noticed something that made her heart feel heavy. A big gray crow named Gruff had taken all the breadcrumbs that kind Mrs. Peterson left on her windowsill for the birds to share.

"Those are for everyone!" chirped Melody, watching three hungry sparrows look sadly at the empty windowsill.

Gruff puffed out his chest. "I'm bigger, so I get it all!" he cawed loudly. "That's just how it is, little bird!"

The sparrows' bellies rumbled with hunger. One baby sparrow started to cry tiny peeping tears. Melody felt her feathers ruffle with worry. This wasn't fair at all!

"But sharing is the right thing to do," Melody said gently. "Mrs. Peterson left those crumbs for all of us."

"I don't care!" Gruff squawked, scattering more crumbs into his own pile.

Melody thought hard, her little head tilting left and right. Fighting wouldn't help anyone. She needed to find a peaceful way to make things fair again.

Suddenly, Melody had a wonderful idea! She knew something that might help Gruff understand.

"Gruff," she called sweetly, "would you like to hear a special song? It's about friendship and sharing."

Gruff stopped eating and looked curious. He did love music, even though he pretended to be tough.

Melody took a deep breath and began to sing the most beautiful song about kindness. Her voice was like golden honey flowing through the air. She sang about how good it feels to share, how friends help each other, and how everyone deserves to eat when they're hungry.

As Melody sang, something magical happened. Gruff's hard heart began to soften. The music helped him remember when he was a baby crow and other birds had shared their food with him.

"That... that was beautiful," Gruff said quietly, his voice much gentler now. He looked at the hungry sparrows and then at his big pile of breadcrumbs.

"You know what, Melody? You're right. Sharing IS the right thing to do."

Gruff carefully divided the breadcrumbs into four equal piles – one for each sparrow and one for himself. "I'm sorry I was greedy," he said. "Will you forgive me?"

The sparrows chirped with joy and gratitude. "Thank you, Gruff! Thank you, Melody!"

From that day forward, all the birds shared Mrs. Peterson's breadcrumbs fairly. Gruff even learned to sing along with Melody's morning songs, though his voice was much deeper and croakier.

"You taught me something important, Melody," Gruff said one sunny morning. "Being fair and kind feels much better than being selfish."

Melody's heart felt as warm as sunshine. "Learning about fairness makes everyone happier," she chirped. "And music helped us solve our problem peacefully!"

Mrs. Peterson smiled from her window, watching all the birds eat together happily. She left extra breadcrumbs that day, enough for everyone.

The End.
