# Tall Necks, Tiny World

In a sunny, comfortable nursery, soft rugs hugged the floor and rainbow blocks made gentle clacks. The air smelled like warm crayons and orange slices. A small fan hummed a sleepy tune. In the middle sat Gigi, a thoughtful giraffe with big kind eyes and a long, careful neck.

Gigi loved to think before she spoke. She loved to listen too. Today was Sharing Circle Day. The teacher, Miss Nia, rang a tiny bell that sounded like a twinkle. “Friends,” she said, “we will share a little about our homes and traditions from around the world. We will take turns.”

Gigi’s heart fluttered with happy thumps. “I can learn about the big world,” she whispered, folding her knees gently. “I can share too.”

Miss Nia held up a soft globe ball. It was blue like the sky and green like leaves. “When you hold the globe,” she said, “it’s your turn.”

First, little Ayo the lion cub caught the globe. His paws were fuzzy and careful. “In my family,” Ayo said, “we sing a welcome song before dinner. We clap, clap, clap.” He clapped three times. Clap! Clap! Clap! The sound felt bright. Everyone smiled.

Gigi swayed her long neck, listening. “Clapping before dinner. I like that,” she thought.

Next, Mei the panda held the globe. “My grandma makes moon dumplings,” she said. “We look up at the round moon and say thank you.” She sniffed the air, remembering. “They smell warm and sweet.” Gigi imagined the sweet smell and her tummy made a tiny polite rumble.

Then, Sami the camel took a turn. “We draw henna swirls on hands for a big celebration,” he said, tracing a swirl in the air. “It tickles!” Everyone giggled. Gigi moved her hoof so softly that she made a swirl on the rug with her toe. She felt calm and curious.

Gigi wanted to speak, but she held still. “We take turns,” she reminded herself. “I will wait.”

Miss Nia smiled and passed the globe to Zuzu the zebra. Zuzu started, “My family dances a stompy dance.” She stomp-stomp-stomped! The floor gave a tiny thump-thump. The room smelled like crayons and fresh soap. The fan kept singing its soft song.

At last, the globe rolled to Gigi. It touched her hoof. She felt a little rush of warmth. “It’s my turn,” she said with a gentle smile. “In my family, we share a tall-neck stretch every morning. We greet the sun and say, ‘Hello, world!’ We listen to birds. We think good thoughts for friends far away.” Gigi stretched her neck slowly, up, up, up. The class stretched too. Up felt good.

Gigi looked around. “I learned something,” she added. “Our world is big, but we can feel close when we listen and share.”

Ayo raised his paw. “Can we all try the welcome song and the tall-neck stretch together?” he asked.

Miss Nia nodded. “Yes, but one at a time. We will take turns and honor each tradition.”

First, they did Ayo’s claps. Clap! Clap! Clap! The room buzzed with happy sound. Next, they tried Mei’s moon thank-you. “Thank you, moon,” they whispered. It felt soft and kind. After that, Sami drew pretend henna swirls in the air. It tickled noses and made everyone laugh. Then, Zuzu led the stomp-stomp dance. The rug cushioned their feet, thump-thump, safe and steady.

When it was time for Gigi’s stretch, she moved with care. “Slow and gentle,” she said. “Breathe in the orange slice smell.” They all breathed in. The scent was bright and sweet. “Say, ‘Hello, world!’” said Gigi. Their voices floated like bubbles. “Hello, world!”

Just then, the snack bell chimed. The table was full of little bowls: orange slices, crunchy crackers, and tiny cups of cool water. Ayo reached first, then paused. He pulled his paw back. “We should take turns,” he said. “Gigi was patient. I can be patient too.”

Gigi’s cheeks felt warm with pride. “Thank you, Ayo,” she said. “I will wait with you.”

They made a line. Each friend took a bowl and said one kind word they learned today. “Welcome,” said Ayo. “Thank you,” said Mei. “Celebrate,” said Sami. “Dance,” said Zuzu. “Hello, world,” said Gigi.

As they munched, Miss Nia brought out a small book. On the cover was a picture of the globe. “This is our World Book,” she said. “When we learn a new tradition, we draw it. We tell the truth about what we learned. That is called integrity. It means we share honestly and kindly.”

Gigi drew a little sun and a long, smiling neck. She wrote, with careful letters, “We stretch and say hello to the world.” Mei drew a round moon. Ayo drew clapping hands. Sami drew swirls. Zuzu drew stomping feet.

The fan hummed. The crayons made soft scratch-scratch sounds. The room felt cozy and bright.

Gigi looked at the globe ball and then at her friends. “Our world is big,” she said, “and our nursery is small. But our hearts can reach far when we share and tell the truth.”

Miss Nia nodded. “Yes,” she said. “Today we learned about global awareness, taking turns, and integrity. We learned to honor each other’s traditions.”

Gigi felt tall on the inside and outside. She took a gentle sip of water and smiled. “Hello, world,” she whispered, “I am learning about you.”

The End.
