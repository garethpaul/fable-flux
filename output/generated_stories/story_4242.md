# Brave Benny's Bubble Trouble

Benny Bunny was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a cozy burrow with a shiny, clean bathroom. The bathroom smelled like minty soap, and the tiles felt cool under Benny’s feet. He liked to hop in and say, “Time to wash and shine!”

One sunny morning, Benny ran into the bathroom. His whiskers twitched with excitement. “Today I will make the biggest, best bubble bath,” he said. He turned the water on. The faucet whooshed, and warm water splashed into the tub. Steam puffed up like a tiny cloud. Benny squeezed the soap bottle. “Squish, squish.” He squeezed again. “Squish, squish!” Big white bubbles piled up like fluffy snow.

Benny grinned. “Look at all the bubbles! I’m so clean already!” He dipped one paw in. The water was warm and cozy. He made a bubble hat. He made a bubble beard. He giggled. “I am Bubble Bunny!”

Then Benny heard a soft sniffle from the hallway. He paused. The faucet still rushed. “Who’s there?” he called.

In came Tilly Tortoise, Benny’s neighbor. She moved slowly and carried a tiny towel. Her shell had dusty spots. “Hi, Benny,” Tilly said in a small voice. “I was hoping for a little water to wash my shell. It’s dusty from the garden. But the water outside is off today. The pump is taking a nap.”

Benny looked at his tub. He looked at the bubbles. He felt proud of his big bath. “I worked hard,” he whispered. His ears drooped. He also looked at Tilly’s kind eyes and dusty shell.

Benny thought and thought. “I want to help,” he said. “But I also want my bath.” He took a deep breath. “I can find a way!”

Problem: Benny wanted a big bubble bath. Tilly needed water. And the faucet kept running and running. Benny remembered what Mama Bunny said: “Use just what you need. Save water for friends and flowers.”

Benny turned the faucet off. The rushing stopped. The bathroom got quiet. Plip. Plop. Little drops slid down the faucet like silver beads.

Tilly smiled. “Thank you for turning it off.”

Benny puffed his chest. “I can share.” He hopped to the cabinet and took a small cup. He scooped some warm water from the tub. “Here you go, Tilly. We can use this cup to wash your shell.” He passed the cup carefully.

Tilly held the cup and giggled. “It’s warm! And it smells like mint.” Benny grabbed a soft cloth and put just a tiny drop of soap on it. “One dot,” he said. “That’s enough.”

Together they worked. Benny dipped the cloth, and Tilly turned slowly so he could reach each dusty spot. Swish, swish. Wipe, wipe. Bubbles popped like tiny kisses. Pop…pop…pop! Tilly hummed a happy tune. The bathroom felt cozy and calm.

Benny noticed the tub was still half-full. “I don’t need more soap,” he said. “I already have bubbles.” He smiled at Tilly. “After we wash your shell, I can still take a bath—with just this water.”

Tilly nodded. “You are very generous, Benny. You shared your water and your time.”

Benny’s ears perked up. “Generous means giving to help others,” he said. “I like that.”

They finished cleaning Tilly’s shell. It shone like a tiny moon. Tilly looked in the mirror and beamed. “Shiny! I feel fresh.”

Benny filled the cup again and handed it to Tilly. “Here’s some to drink later for your plant.” He pointed to a little fern in a pot by the window. The fern’s leaves were a bit droopy. “We can save the last cup for the fern.”

Tilly clapped her small hands. “A share for me, and a share for the plant! That is kind to the Earth.”

Benny nodded. “We can care for ourselves and the planet too.” He carried the cup to the fern with Tilly. Together they poured slowly. Glug…glug…glug. The fern lifted its leaves like a tiny dancer.

“Thank you, Benny,” said Tilly. “You gave me water, and you saved water.”

Benny hopped back to the tub. He slipped in and relaxed. The water felt like a warm hug. He used a small washcloth and gentle strokes. “Scrub, scrub,” he sang softly. “Wash my ears, wash my nose. Wash my paws and wiggly toes.” He used just a little soap and rinsed carefully with the cup. Splash, splash. His fur smelled clean and fresh.

When he finished, Benny pulled the plug. He watched the water swirl down, then put a bucket under the faucet and turned it on just a little—drip…drip…drip—so he could catch water for later plant drinks. He turned it off again. “We can save some for tomorrow,” he told Tilly.

Tilly waved. “Today you were brave, smart, and generous.”

Benny blushed under his fur. “I learned that sharing feels good. I can take care of me, help a friend, and care for our Earth—all at the same time!”

They bumped elbows like pals. The fern rustled a happy whisper. The bathroom smelled minty and clean, and everything felt calm and bright.

Lesson: Be generous. Use only what you need. Share with friends and nature.

The End.
