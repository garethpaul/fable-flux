# Benny Bunny’s Kitchen Plan

The sun made the warm kitchen glow. The floor felt cozy on Benny Bunny’s little feet. The air smelled like cinnamon toast and sweet carrots. Pots sat on the stove, and a round timer tick-tick-ticked on the counter. Benny Bunny’s whiskers twitched with excitement.

“I want to make carrot pancakes all by myself,” Benny said. His ears stood tall. “I am a determined rabbit!”

Mama Bunny smiled and wiped her paws on a soft towel. “You can do it, Benny,” she said. “We will use a plan. A plan is a strategy. A strategy helps when things feel hard.”

Benny nodded. “I like a plan.”

Mama pointed to a big paper on the fridge with simple pictures. “Step 1: Wash paws. Step 2: Gather tools. Step 3: Mix slowly. Step 4: Cook safely. Step 5: Taste and clean up.”

Benny hopped to the sink. The water was warm and splashy. “Swish, swish,” went the soap bubbles. “I am washing my paws,” he sang. “Clean paws are healthy paws.” He shook his paws dry and giggled when tiny drops went plip-plop on his whiskers.

“Good job,” said Mama. “Now, tools.”

Benny looked around. There was a big bowl, a small whisk, a measuring cup, and a spatula. He reached for the whisk, but it clattered to the floor. “Clink!”

“Oh no,” Benny said, his ears drooping.

“It’s okay,” Mama said. “We breathe. In… and out…” She took a slow breath. Benny copied. “In… and out…” His shoulders felt softer.

“I can try again,” Benny said. He picked up the whisk and set it gently next to the bowl. “I am using a strategy.”

“Step 3: Mix slowly,” said Mama.

Benny poured in milk. He added carrot shreds that smelled fresh and sweet. He sprinkled cinnamon that made the air cozy and warm. He grabbed the whisk and stirred fast. Splish! The batter jumped out of the bowl and splattered on the counter.

Benny’s nose scrunched. “I can’t do it,” he said. “This is hard!”

Mama knelt down. “Sometimes we feel frustrated,” she said softly. “Frustration is a big feeling. When it comes, we can use a plan to help.”

“What plan?” Benny sniffled.

“First, pause. Then, breathe. Then, try a new way,” Mama said. “That’s our strategy.”

Benny squeezed his paws. He closed his eyes. “Pause.” He took a slow, deep breath. “In… and out…” He opened his eyes. “Try a new way,” he said. “Mix slowly.” He moved the whisk in small circles. Swish… swish… The batter stayed in the bowl. His ears perked up.

“I did it!” Benny grinned. “Slow circles help.”

“Step 4: Cook safely,” Mama said. She turned on the stove with a soft click. The pan warmed and made a gentle sizzle sound when a tiny drop of batter touched it. “Sizzle,” sang the pan.

Benny held the spoon. He wanted to put a big scoop in the pan. “Big pancakes!” he said.

“Small scoops first,” Mama said. “Small scoops are easier to flip. That is another strategy.”

Benny nodded. Plop. Plop. Plop. Little round pancakes puffed up. The kitchen smelled like sweet carrots and cinnamon. Steam rose like little clouds.

Benny tried to flip a pancake. The spatula slipped. The pancake folded in half. “Oh,” he said, his whiskers drooping again.

“Pause,” Benny whispered to himself. He took a breath. “In… and out…” He watched Mama hold the spatula under the pancake, then wiggle-wiggle, then flip. “Wiggle-wiggle, flip!” sang Mama.

Benny copied. Wiggle-wiggle… flip! The pancake turned over and landed flat. “I did it!” he laughed, hopping in a little circle. His feet made soft thump-thump sounds on the warm floor.

Soon a plate had a stack of small, golden pancakes. Benny drizzled a little honey and added blueberries that popped with juice. He took a bite. “Mmm!” His eyes went wide. “They taste like carrot sunshine!”

“How do you feel?” Mama asked.

“Proud,” Benny said. “And hungry.” He chewed slowly. “Chew, chew,” he said. “Chewing slow is healthy.”

After eating, Benny looked at the messy counter. “Step 5: Clean up,” he said. He wiped the counter with a warm soapy cloth. Squeak-squeak went the sponge. He put the tools in the sink. Clink-clink went the dishes. He washed and dried them with Mama. The kitchen smelled clean and cozy again.

Mama hugged him. Her apron felt soft. “You used a strategy when you felt frustrated,” she said. “You paused, breathed, and tried a new way. You also used small steps, mixed slowly, and cooked safely. That is smart and healthy.”

Benny’s ears wiggled with joy. “When something is hard, I can make a plan,” he said. “I can pause, breathe, and try a new way. I can use small steps. I can take care of my body with clean paws, slow chewing, and helping clean up.”

He patted his belly. “And carrot pancakes give me bunny power,” he giggled.

Mama smiled. “You are a determined rabbit, Benny. Your plan helped you do your best.”

Benny looked at the picture steps on the fridge. They made him feel calm. He took one more tiny bite and savored the warm, sweet taste. He looked at the sunny window and the happy, tidy kitchen.

“I like plans,” he said. “Plans help me grow.”

The End.
