# Tiko the Tortoise and the Gentle Words

Morning light peeked into the garden shed. Dust danced in the sunshine. The air smelled like soil and fresh wood. Shelves held pots and seeds. A little broom leaned in the corner. In the soft straw bed, Tiko the tortoise blinked his kind eyes and stretched his legs.

“Good morning, shed,” Tiko said in a calm, warm voice. “Let us have a gentle day.”

Tiko was a wise tortoise. He moved slowly, but he listened quickly. He loved words. He loved how “thank you” felt like a hug. He loved how “please” sounded like a soft bell. He even liked to say hello in many ways. “Hello,” he would say in English. “Hola,” he would say in Spanish. “Jambo,” he would whisper with a smile. The words tickled his tongue. They made his heart feel big.

Outside the shed, the garden was busy. Bees buzzed. Leaves rustled. The watering can dripped, “plip, plip.” Tiko opened the little shed door and peeked out.

“Help! Help!” squeaked Mip the mouse. She held a tiny paper sign. “Garden Clean-Up Day!” the sign said.

Mip’s whiskers shook. “The kids from the street will come to see the garden,” she said. “We want it to look nice. But we are not working together.” Her nose twitched. “Cricket clicks too loud. Beetle blocks the path. Worm won’t stop wiggling the soil into piles.”

Tiko nodded slowly. “Let us use gentle words,” he said. “Gentle words help gentle bodies.”

They walked back into the garden shed to make a plan. The shed felt cool and safe. Tiko took a small chalkboard from a shelf. He drew three big hearts with a piece of white chalk. Scratch, scratch.

“Heart one,” Tiko said. “We listen.” He tapped his ear. “Heart two. We speak kindly.” He patted his mouth. “Heart three. We find a way to help together.” He put his front feet together like a little bridge.

Mip breathed in. She breathed out. “I can try,” she said.

They went outside. Cricket clicked on a rake. “Click-click! Click-click!” Beetle stood in the path with a heavy pot. Worm made hills and valleys in the soft dirt.

Tiko lifted his head. “Friends,” he said, “may we stop for a moment? I would like to listen.”

Cricket’s legs slowed. “I just want to help fast,” Cricket said. “Click-click.”

“Thank you for telling me,” Tiko said. “Your fast helps us.”

Beetle hummed. “This pot is heavy,” Beetle said. “I am tired. I am blocking the way.”

“Thank you for telling me,” Tiko said. “Your strong helps us.”

Worm wiggled and peeped, “I make good soil! But I make bumps too.”

“Thank you for telling me,” Tiko said. “Your rich soil helps us.”

Mip squeaked, “I feel worried. I want the garden to look nice.”

Tiko took a slow breath. “We listened,” he said gently. “Now we speak kindly. Let us use simple words.”

He showed them the chalkboard hearts. “Please,” he said, and he smiled. “Thank you. Excuse me. May I help you?”

Tiko looked at Beetle. “Beetle, may I help you carry the pot, please?”

Beetle’s eyes brightened. “Yes, please,” Beetle said. Together, slow and steady, they moved the pot. Thump… pause… thump.

Mip turned to Cricket. “Cricket, may we make soft music while we work?” she asked. “Click softly, please.”

Cricket bowed his head. “Click,” he tried, very gentle. The sound was sweet, like rain on leaves.

Worm peeped at Tiko. “Excuse me, may I smooth the soil after I wiggle?”

Tiko nodded. “Yes. Wiggle, then smooth,” he said. “I will use the little rake.” He showed Worm how to pull the rake in long, calm lines. Swish… swish.

They worked and talked. They said “please” and “thank you.” They said “excuse me” when they needed to pass. They even shared hello in new ways. “Hola,” Cricket clicked softly. “Bonjour,” Beetle buzzed. “Ni hao,” Mip chirped. Tiko chuckled and said, “Hello to all.”

The garden began to shine. The path was clear. The pots were lined up like soldiers at rest. The soil lay smooth like a soft brown blanket. The air smelled clean and happy. The sun warmed their backs, and a light breeze kissed their faces.

Just then, the children from the street came to the gate with their teacher. Their shoes made little crunch sounds on the path. “Oh!” said one child. “It looks beautiful!” Another child said, “I love the gentle sounds.”

The teacher smiled at Tiko and the helpers. “Thank you for your community service,” she said. “You worked as a team.”

Tiko bowed. “We used peaceful words,” he said. “Gentle words make gentle hands.”

One small child stepped forward. “How do you say thank you in another language?” she asked.

Tiko’s eyes twinkled. “Gracias,” he said softly. “Merci. Asante.” The children repeated the words, giggling as the sounds rolled off their tongues.

Mip stood tall. “I learned to ask with ‘please,’” she said.

Cricket clicked softly. “I learned to listen,” he said.

Beetle hummed. “I learned to ask for help,” he said.

Worm wiggled proudly. “I learned to smooth,” he said.

Tiko felt the warm sun on his shell. His heart felt full. “Today,” he said, “we cared for the garden. We cared for each other. When we speak with kindness, our work feels light. Our bodies feel well. Our hearts feel safe.”

The children clapped. The garden shed door stood open, cool and welcoming. Tiko looked at his chalkboard hearts and smiled.

“Let us keep these words,” he said. “Please. Thank you. Excuse me. May I help?” He paused and added, “And hello, in every way.”

Everyone nodded. The garden rested, tidy and calm. The breeze whispered through the leaves, like a soft “well done.”

The End.
