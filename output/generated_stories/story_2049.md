# Mango’s Kitchen Promise

In a warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon and sweet mango, a courageous monkey named Mango tied on a small red apron. The kettle sang a soft whistle. Pots clinked. Sunlight made gold squares on the floor.

“I have a big goal,” Mango said, patting his chest. “I want to make a friendly snack for everyone. I want to help and lead. I want to learn new things too!”

Mango looked at a big recipe card on the counter. It had bright pictures: bananas, rice, milk, a tiny jar of cardamom, and a plate of crunchy plantain chips. There were notes from different families who shared their treats.

“This is a kindness snack,” Mango read slowly. “A little from here, a little from there. We can taste and share.”

Mango felt excited, but also a little worried. His tail twitched. “What if I make a mess? What if it tastes funny?”

He took a deep breath and touched his heart. “I can do it. I will make small steps. First step: wash hands.”

Swish, swish went the warm water. The soap smelled like oranges. “Step one, done!” Mango smiled.

He set small bowls on the counter. “Step two: gather ingredients.” He counted with his fingers. “One banana, two scoops of rice, a splash of milk, a sprinkle of carda… carda… cardamom!” He giggled at the tricky word.

Knock, knock! Friends peeked in. A tiny turtle named Tika rolled in with a basket. A bird named Bina fluttered to a stool.

“It smells so cozy,” said Bina. “What are you making?”

“A kindness snack,” said Mango. “My goal is to make a snack we can all share. I will lead the steps. Will you help me?”

“Yes!” said Tika. “I can stir slowly.”

“I can taste and sing,” chirped Bina.

Mango nodded. “Good team! Step three: mash the banana.” Plop! The banana slid into a bowl. “Tika, you stir. Gentle circles,” Mango guided.

“Gentle circles,” Tika repeated, moving the spoon slowly. The sound was squishy and soft, like a sandy wave.

Bina sniffed the tiny jar. “What is this?”

“Cardamom,” said Mango. “Some friends use it in sweet rice. It smells warm, like hugs.”

Bina breathed in. “Mmm. It smells like a happy blanket.”

Mango added a pinch. “Step four: add rice and milk.” The rice made a tiny rain sound. The milk went swish and made the mixture creamy.

When Mango stirred, a little splashed. “Oops!” he squeaked. He felt his cheeks get warm. He wanted to hide, but he remembered his goal. “It’s okay,” he said softly to himself. “I can improve. I will slow down.”

“That’s good leadership,” said Tika. “You are calm.”

Mango smiled. “Thank you. Step five: taste and adjust.” He gave Bina a tiny spoon.

Bina tasted. “It is sweet, but it needs a little crunch.”

Mango clapped. “Great idea! We can add plantain chips. Some families love crunchy plantains.”

He crushed a few chips. Crackle, crackle! They sprinkled over the top like golden leaves.

Mango listened to the kettle. “Step six: safe and steady.” He turned the stove off and let the warm pot rest. “We wait and breathe.” He led a slow breath in, and a slow breath out. Everyone followed.

“Waiting helps my feelings,” Mango said. “I feel brave again.”

Ding! The timer sang a tiny song. Mango stirred and served the snack into small bowls. The creamy rice and banana smelled like cozy mornings. The cardamom scent curled in the air. The crunchy chips made a happy sound.

Before they ate, Mango said, “Let’s honor where our snack comes from. Thank you to the families who taught us about cardamom rice. Thank you for plantains. Thank you for bananas. Thank you for milk and rice. We appreciate every place and every person.”

They all nodded. “Thank you,” they whispered.

They tasted. “Mmm!” said Tika. “Soft and crunchy together!”

“It tastes like a warm song,” chirped Bina.

Mango felt proud. His tail swished in a happy wave. “We did it. My goal was to make a friendly snack and to lead with care. I made small steps. I listened. I stayed calm. I learned from others. That helped me improve.”

Bina looked at the recipe card. “Can we write our steps for next time?”

“Yes,” said Mango. He found a big pencil. Together they wrote:
- Set a clear goal.
- Make small steps.
- Ask friends to help.
- Listen and taste.
- Stay calm and fix mistakes.
- Thank and share.

Mango hung the list on the wall. “This is our kitchen promise.”

They washed the bowls. The water was warm, and the bubbles smelled like lemons. Mango hummed. “I feel strong inside,” he said. “When we make a goal, and we take small steps, we can do big things.”

Tika nodded. “You led us with kindness.”

“And you taught us to breathe,” added Bina.

Mango looked around the warm kitchen. Sunlight danced on the clean counter. “Tomorrow,” he said, “my new goal is to try a different spice. We will learn more and share more.”

Everyone cheered. “We can do it, step by step!”

The End.
