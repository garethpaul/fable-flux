# Pebble the Penguin Builds a Song

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a bright red scarf. Pebble waddled into the musical practice room with a happy hum. The room was cozy and cool. Soft lights glowed. Shiny drums twinkled. A big piano waited like a friendly whale. Bouncy cushions made quiet thumps when Pebble sat. It smelled like clean wood and oranges from a little diffuser.

Pebble clapped. Clap-clap! “Today I will build a song,” Pebble said. “I will build it strong, just like building a tower.”

Pebble loved building towers with blocks. Pebble also loved music. Pebble wanted to build a song from little music pieces. But Pebble did not know how to start.

Pebble took a deep breath. In… out… “Healthy breath helps me think,” Pebble said softly.

Coach Seal slid in with a friendly wave. “Hello, Pebble! What are you building today?”

“A song,” said Pebble. “But my song is tiny. I want it to be tall and steady.”

Coach Seal nodded. “Songs are built, just like towers. We build them step by step. First a base, then middle parts, then a top. Let’s use tools: quiet, breath, and try-again.”

Pebble’s feathers tingled. “I can try!”

Pebble tapped the drum. Tap… tap-tap. It sounded like little raindrops. “This is my base,” Pebble said. Pebble tapped again, steady and slow. Tap… tap… tap…

Then Pebble tried the piano. Plink-plonk! Plink-plonk-plink! The sounds stacked on the drum taps. Pebble swayed. “It is growing!”

But then Pebble pressed too many keys. Clang! Wrong notes spilled out like a messy pile. Pebble jumped. Pebble’s cheeks felt hot. “Oh no. I broke my song.”

Coach Seal shook his head with a warm smile. “Not broken. Just wiggly. Buildings can wobble. We make them steady again. Let’s pause. Wiggle your flippers. Take a sip of water.”

Pebble sipped cool water from a blue bottle. Swish, gulp. Pebble took another deep breath. In… out… The room felt calm again. Pebble’s tummy soft, Pebble’s heart slow.

“I can try again,” Pebble said, a little braver.

Pebble tapped the drum, steady like a heartbeat. Tap… tap… tap… Pebble counted, “One, two, three, four.” Pebble pressed two piano keys that sounded sweet together. Plink… plink… The sound felt like silver snowflakes.

Pebble smiled. “This is my middle.”

Coach Seal clapped softly. “Strong base. Good middle. What about a top?”

Pebble thought of towers and roofs. “A top could sparkle,” Pebble whispered. Pebble picked up little jingle bells. Shake-sha! Shake-sha! Pebble added the bells only at the end of each count. Tap… tap… tap… shake-sha!

But again, Pebble got excited. Pebble shook the bells too fast. Shake-sha-sha-sha—everything got noisy. The song tipped and tumbled like blocks falling. Pebble’s eyes filled with tears. “I can’t do it.”

Coach Seal sat close. “Pebble, look at what you built. You built a base. You built a middle. That is strong work. Building takes time. Your song wants gentle hands and brave tries.”

Pebble sniffed. The bells felt cool and smooth. Pebble rubbed them and listened to the tiny chime. “I am a builder,” Pebble whispered. “I can be gentle. I can be brave.”

Pebble did a healthy reset. Pebble stretched flippers up, then down. Pebble rolled shoulders. Pebble counted to four with slow breaths. One… two… three… four. Pebble felt steady.

Pebble started again. Tap… tap… tap… Pebble set the base. Plink… plink… Pebble added the middle. Shake… pause… sha. Pebble laid one bell sound like a shiny roof tile on top of the steady beats.

Pebble listened. The room felt warm and happy. The notes sparkled but did not wobble. Coach Seal closed his eyes and swayed. “Yes, Pebble. Your song stands tall.”

Pebble giggled. “I built it! I built my song!”

Coach Seal said, “Tell me how you built it.”

Pebble held up three flippers—well, two flippers and a tiny tail wiggle. “Step one: I made a strong base. Step two: I added a simple middle. Step three: I put on a small, shiny top. I used quiet, breath, water, and try-again.”

Coach Seal nodded. “You also used kind words to yourself. That grows your inside muscles—self-esteem.”

Pebble stood a little taller. “I am proud of me.”

“Play it once more,” said Coach Seal.

Pebble played. Tap… tap… tap… plink… plink… shake… sha. The sounds stacked like smooth stones. Pebble felt the drum in the belly, the piano in the fingers, the bells tingling in the ears. The song felt like a sturdy tower with a bright flag.

Pebble bowed. “Thank you, music room,” Pebble said. “Thank you, body. Thank you, breath.”

Coach Seal smiled. “What will you build next?”

Pebble grinned. “Tomorrow, I will build a longer song. I will add rest breaks and water sips. Healthy habits help builders grow.”

Pebble packed the bells, wiped the keys, and washed flippers with warm soapy water. Swish, bubble, rinse. Pebble put the drum away carefully, one careful step at a time.

As Pebble waddled out, the little diffuser puffed another orange cloud. The air smelled bright and brave. Pebble hummed the tall, steady song, and the song hummed back in Pebble’s happy heart.

Lesson: We can build big things by making a strong base, adding simple parts, and finishing with gentle tops. With healthy habits, kind self-talk, and try-again hearts, our songs—and our selves—stand tall.

The End.
