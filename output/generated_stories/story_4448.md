# Paints, Feathers, and Friendly Words

In a bright creative art room, a creative parrot named Pico fluffed his green and blue feathers. Sunlight sprinkled the tables, making the paint jars sparkle like tiny rainbows. The room smelled like fresh paper and sweet, safe paint. Brushes stood in cups like little trees.

“Squawk! Today I will paint a giant sky!” Pico chirped. His eyes shone. He dipped a brush into soft blue paint. Swish, swish. The brush made a quiet whispering sound across the big paper.

Pico’s friends were painting too. Tilly the turtle tapped a sponge into warm yellow. Bo the bunny dotted pink circles. The room hummed with happy sounds—tap, swish, plop, giggle.

Pico stretched his wings wide to reach a corner. His bright tail brushed the table. Oops! His feathered wing bumped a jar. The jar wobbled. “Uh-oh,” Pico breathed.

The jar tumbled. Plop! Blue paint splashed like a puddle sky. It splashed on Tilly’s yellow sun. It splashed on Bo’s pink dots. It splashed on Pico’s own feet. Drip, drip, drip.

Tilly blinked. “My sun!” she said softly. Bo’s nose twitched. “My dots,” he whispered. Pico’s heart felt tight, like a knot. His cheeks felt hot. He wanted to hide under the easel.

“I didn’t mean to,” Pico murmured. His voice was small. He looked at the messy blue splashes. He looked at his friends’ faces. They looked surprised and a little sad.

The art teacher, Miss Lark, walked over. Her shoes made soft steps on the floor. She knelt down and spoke in a calm, warm voice. “Let’s take a breath,” she said. “In… and out.” Everyone breathed together. In… and out. The air smelled like lemon soap and paint.

Pico swallowed. “I am sorry,” he said. “I bumped the jar. I made a big mess.”

Tilly looked at her paper. “I feel sad,” she said. “I liked my yellow sun.”

Bo nodded. “I feel upset,” he said. “My dots are all splashy.”

Pico listened. He kept his wings still. “Thank you for telling me,” he said. “How can I help?”

Miss Lark smiled a little. “That was kind, Pico,” she said. “When we have a problem, we can use peaceful words. We can say how we feel. We can ask how to fix it.”

Pico puffed a tiny sigh. “I can get towels,” he said. He flapped to the sink and brought soft towels. He dabbed gently. Dab, dab, dab. The blue lightened. Tilly dabbed too. Bo dabbed with his ears tucked back.

“I can share my blue,” Pico offered. “Maybe your sun can have a sky friend.”

Tilly tilted her head. “A sun needs a sky,” she said. “Maybe my sun can peek from a cloud.” Her voice sounded a little brighter.

Bo wiggled his whiskers. “My dots can be balloons,” he said. “Blue can be the string!” He smiled a small smile.

Pico’s tight feeling loosened. “I can help draw clouds,” he said. He dipped a brush in fluffy white. Puff, puff, puff. Soft clouds grew around Tilly’s sun. Bo added long blue lines to his balloons. Swoop, swoop. The lines swung like strings in a breeze.

Miss Lark brought a clean paper for Pico. “What will you make now?” she asked.

Pico looked at his blue feet and laughed. “I will make a sky with footprints,” he said. He stepped into a tray—squish! The paint felt cool and slippery. He walked across the paper. Step, step, step. Feather prints and toe prints made a funny path.

He looked at Tilly. “May I add a little yellow?” he asked.

Tilly nodded. “Yes,” she said. “Please add a sun friend to the corner.”

Pico dipped gently. “I will be careful,” he promised.

He looked at Bo. “May I add a tiny bird balloon?”

Bo giggled. “Yes!” he said. “Make it say tweet!”

Pico painted a small bird balloon with a curly blue string. “Tweet!” he chirped, just for fun. Everyone laughed. The room felt warm again.

When the paintings were done, they lined them up to dry. Tilly’s sun peeked from pillowy clouds. Bo’s pink balloons floated on strong blue strings. Pico’s sky was full of little footprints and one very funny bird balloon saying, “Tweet!”

Miss Lark clapped softly. “Look at these beautiful ideas,” she said. “You had a problem. You used calm breaths. You used peaceful words. You listened. You helped. And you forgave.”

Tilly turned to Pico. “I forgive you,” she said kindly. “Thank you for fixing it with me.”

Bo nodded. “I forgive you too,” he said. “Thank you for asking how to help.”

Pico’s feathers felt light again. “Thank you,” he said. “I learned something. When I make a mistake, I can say I’m sorry. I can listen. I can help. We can make new art together.”

The art room filled with happy sounds again—swish, tap, flutter, giggle. Pico washed his feet in the sink. The water was warm and bubbly. He watched the blue swirl away like a tiny river.

He looked at his friends and smiled. “Friends, feathers, and kind words,” he said. “That is the best art.”

Miss Lark nodded. “That is the best lesson,” she said.

The End.
