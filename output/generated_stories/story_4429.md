# Ollie the Otter Helps His Friends

Ollie the otter loved playing in the sunny backyard. The warm grass tickled his paws, and the bright sunshine made his brown fur sparkle. He had a small blue kiddie pool where he splashed and swam all day long.

"Splash, splash!" went Ollie as he dove into his pool. Water droplets flew everywhere, making tiny rainbows in the sunlight.

One morning, Ollie heard sad crying sounds coming from the flower garden. He peeked over and saw his friend Bella the bunny sitting by the wilted flowers.

"What's wrong, Bella?" asked Ollie, shaking water from his whiskers.

"Oh, Ollie!" sniffled Bella. "All our beautiful flowers are dying. We haven't had rain for many days, and I don't know how to water them all by myself."

Ollie looked at the droopy sunflowers and sad roses. Their petals looked thirsty and their leaves hung down low. Then he looked at his pool full of fresh, clean water.

"I want to help!" said Ollie. "But I don't know how to carry water from my pool to your flowers."

Bella wiped her eyes. "It's okay, Ollie. You're just a little otter. This job is too big."

Ollie felt sad. He wanted to help his friend, but he didn't know what to do. He sat by his pool, thinking hard. The water felt cool and refreshing on his paws.

Suddenly, Ollie had a wonderful idea! He remembered how he could hold his breath underwater and swim really well. Maybe his swimming skills could help!

"Bella! Bella!" called Ollie excitedly. "I have an idea!"

Ollie dove into his pool and filled his mouth with water. Then he waddled quickly to the flower garden and gently sprayed the water onto the thirsty sunflowers.

"That's amazing!" said Bella, clapping her paws. "You're like a walking watering can!"

Ollie felt proud and happy. He made trip after trip, carrying water in his mouth from his pool to each flower. His little legs worked hard, and soon he was breathing heavily.

"Are you tired?" asked Bella. "You don't have to help if it's too hard."

"I want to help my friends," puffed Ollie. "Working together makes everything better!"

Soon, other backyard friends came to see what was happening. Chip the chipmunk brought tiny acorns to catch extra water. Ruby the robin flew overhead, cheering Ollie on with happy chirps.

"You're so brave and helpful, Ollie!" sang Ruby.

After an hour of hard work, all the flowers stood tall and proud again. Their petals were bright and colorful, and they smelled sweet in the warm air.

"Thank you so much, Ollie," said Bella, giving him a big hug. "You used your special swimming skills to help our whole community!"

Ollie's chest puffed up with pride. He had never felt so good about himself. "I learned that even little otters can do big things when they help others!"

From that day on, Ollie became the official backyard gardener. Every morning, he would fill his mouth with water and visit all his friends' plants. The sunny backyard became the most beautiful garden in the neighborhood.

"Helping others makes me feel happy inside," Ollie would tell anyone who would listen. "When we work together and care for each other, amazing things happen!"

The End.
