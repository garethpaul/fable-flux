# Rascal's Sorry Song

The morning sun peeked through the bathroom window. Rascal the raccoon stood by the sink. His fluffy gray tail drooped down low. Water dripped everywhere from his messy paws.

"Oh no!" squeaked Penny the pink mouse from her tiny house behind the toilet. "My home is getting wet!"

Rascal had been washing his breakfast berries. The purple juice made pretty swirls in the white sink. But splash, splash, splash! Water flew all around the bathroom floor.

"Sorry, Penny," Rascal said quickly. He kept washing his berries. More water splashed out.

"Please be more careful!" called Oliver the orange goldfish from his bowl on the counter. "The water is making everything slippery!"

But Rascal was feeling frustrated. His paws were sticky and purple. The berries needed lots of washing. He scrubbed harder and harder.

SPLASH! A big wave of water hit Penny's little pink house.

"My bed is soaking wet now!" Penny cried. Her tiny voice sounded very sad.

Rascal stopped washing. He looked around the bathroom. Water covered the floor like a shiny lake. Penny's house was dripping. Oliver's fishbowl had water spots all over it.

His friends looked upset and worried.

"I... I made a big mess," Rascal whispered. His black mask around his eyes felt heavy with shame.

He sat down on the wet floor. His bottom felt cold and damp. What could he do to make things better?

Then Rascal remembered something special about himself. He loved making music! Maybe he could sing an apology song.

He cleared his throat and began to hum. The sound echoed softly in the bathroom. "Hmm, hmm, hmm."

"I'm sorry, sorry, truly sorry," Rascal sang in his gentle voice. "I made a mess and got you wet. I should have listened, I forget."

Penny poked her pink nose out of her house. Her whiskers twitched with curiosity.

Rascal kept singing. "Let me help you, let me try. I'll clean up and make things dry."

Oliver swam closer to the edge of his bowl. His orange fins moved gently in the water.

"Your song sounds beautiful," Penny said softly. "But my house is still very wet."

"And the floor is still slippery," Oliver added kindly.

Rascal nodded. Words and songs were nice, but actions mattered more.

He grabbed the fluffy blue towel from the rack. First, he gently dried Penny's little house. He was very careful with her tiny furniture.

"Thank you," Penny smiled. "That feels much better."

Next, Rascal got down on his hands and knees. He wiped up every drop of water from the bathroom floor. The tiles sparkled clean again.

Then he took a soft cloth and carefully cleaned Oliver's fishbowl. The glass became crystal clear.

"Wow!" Oliver bubbled happily. "I can see everything perfectly now!"

Rascal looked at his friends. They were smiling at him with warm, forgiving eyes.

"I learned something important today," Rascal said thoughtfully. "When I make mistakes, saying sorry is good. But doing something to fix the problem is even better."

"We all make mistakes," Penny said gently. "What matters is that you listened to us and made things right."

"Plus, your sorry song was really pretty!" Oliver laughed, making tiny bubbles in his bowl.

Rascal felt warm and happy inside. His friends had different colors and lived in different places. But they all cared about each other.

From that day on, whenever Rascal washed his berries, he remembered to be extra careful. And sometimes, just for fun, he would sing happy songs while he cleaned.

The bathroom became a place filled with friendship, music, and kindness.

The End.
